copula:/ˈkɒpjʊlə/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpjələ/ (US)| n. |系动词；连接词|A copula is a word used to link the subject of a verb to a subject complement, such as the word *is* in the sentence "The sky *is* blue."|The verb "to be" is the most common copula in English.  天空是蓝色的。(The sky *is* blue.) |Synonyms: linking verb, connective|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms for a grammatical function)|noun

copulas:/ˈkɒpjʊləz/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpjələz/ (US)| n. |系动词（复数）；连接词（复数）|Plural form of "copula," referring to multiple linking verbs or connectives.|The copulas in these sentences are all forms of "to be." 这些句子中的系动词都是“to be”的不同形式。|Synonyms: linking verbs, connectives|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms for a grammatical function)|noun
