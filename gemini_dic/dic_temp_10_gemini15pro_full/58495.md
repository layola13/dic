dueled: /ˈdjuːəld/ (US), /ˈdʒuːəld/ (UK)| v.|决斗(ed)| engaged in a formal combat with someone. The two rivals dueled for her affections.  两位情敌为了赢得她的爱而决斗。|近义词：sparred, jousted|反义词: cooperated, collaborated|verb (past tense)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French dueler, from duel (noun), ultimately from Latin duellum ("war," literally "two," related to duo).  Originally referred to judicial combat between two individuals.
