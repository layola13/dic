A person with gutsers is someone who is brave and determined.

gutsers:/ˈɡʌtsərz/
noun
内脏；肠子 (Informal, sometimes humorous or slightly vulgar)
This refers to a person's internal organs, particularly the intestines.  It's used informally and can be humorous or slightly vulgar.
He felt like his gutsers were churning after the rollercoaster ride. 坐过山车后，他感觉五脏六腑都在翻腾。


Etymology:  Derived from "guts" (courage, intestines), with the suffix "-er" (often used to form agent nouns, but here indicating a person possessing the quality).  "Guts" itself comes from Old English "guttas."

Synonym: courage, bravery, nerve, grit, determination, fortitude, pluck (for the sense of bravery)
Synonym: intestines, bowels, innards (for the informal/vulgar sense)

Antonym: cowardice, timidity, fearfulness (for the sense of bravery)
