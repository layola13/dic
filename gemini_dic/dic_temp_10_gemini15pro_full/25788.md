bureaucrat: /ˈbjʊərəkræt/ (US) /ˈbjʊərəkræt/ (UK)| n. | 官僚 | An official in a government department, in particular one perceived as being concerned with procedural correctness at the expense of people's needs. |  The bureaucrats are delaying the approval process. 这些官僚正在拖延审批过程。|Synonyms: official, administrator, civil servant, public servant| Antonyms: nonconformist, maverick, rebel | noun

bureaucrat: /ˌbjʊərəˈkrætɪk/ (US) /ˌbjʊərəˈkrætɪk/ (UK)| adj. | 官僚主义的 | Relating to or characteristic of a bureaucracy or bureaucrat; excessively concerned with procedure. | The bureaucratic process made it difficult to get anything done.  官僚主义的程序使得很难完成任何事情。 | Synonyms: administrative, procedural, official, governmental | Antonyms: flexible, adaptable, dynamic | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *bureaucrate*, from *bureau* (desk, office) + *-crate* (from Greek *kratēs*, meaning "ruler" or "member of a body").
