biases:/ˈbaɪəsiz/ /ˈbaɪəsɪz/| n.|偏见，偏向，偏好|A particular tendency, trend, inclination, feeling, or opinion, especially one that is preconceived or unreasoned.|The judge was accused of having biases against the defendant. 法官被指控对被告有偏见。|Synonyms: prejudices, preconceptions, leanings|Antonyms: impartiality, objectivity, fairness|noun

bias:/ˈbaɪəs/ /ˈbaɪəs/| n.|偏见，偏向，偏好|A particular tendency, trend, inclination, feeling, or opinion, especially one that is preconceived or unreasoned.|He has a bias against modern art. 他对现代艺术有偏见。|Synonyms: prejudice, preconception, leaning|Antonyms: impartiality, objectivity, fairness|noun
bias:/ˈbaɪəs/ /ˈbaɪəs/| v.|使有偏见，偏向|To influence in an unfair way; prejudice.|The media's coverage of the event was biased. 媒体对该事件的报道有偏见。|Synonyms: prejudice, influence, sway|Antonyms: be objective, be fair|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *biais "slant, oblique," probably from Provençal biais "crooked, askew," of unknown origin.
