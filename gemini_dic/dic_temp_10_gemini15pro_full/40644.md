conventionalizes:/kənˈvɛnʃənəˌlaɪzɪz/
|v.|使习俗化，使惯例化|To make something conventional; to cause something to conform to established conventions or customs.|The author conventionalizes the plot by using a predictable love triangle.|近义词: standardize, normalize, formalize|反义词: revolutionize, innovate, individualize|词性:动词

Etymology:
From convention + -al + -ize.  "Convention" comes from the Latin "conventio," meaning "a coming together, meeting, agreement."
