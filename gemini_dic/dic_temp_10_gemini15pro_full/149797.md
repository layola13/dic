paleichthyes:/ˌpeɪliˈɪkθiːz/ | n. |古鱼类|An extinct paraphyletic group of jawed fishes that lived from the Silurian to the Mesozoic Era, characterized by cartilage skeletons and placoid scales.  They are the ancestors of modern bony fish (Osteichthyes) and cartilaginous fish (Chondrichthyes).| The paleichthyes, though extinct, hold a key place in vertebrate evolution. 古鱼类虽然已经灭绝，但在脊椎动物进化中占有关键地位。|  The study of paleichthyes fossils provides insights into the development of jaws in vertebrates. 对古鱼类化石的研究为了解脊椎动物颌骨的发育提供了线索。|近义词: ancient fish, fossil fish |反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it's a taxonomic group) |词性: noun
