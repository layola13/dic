non-Malthusian:/ˌnɒn mælˈθuːziən/ | adj. n. | 非马尔萨斯主义的；非马尔萨斯主义者 |  Relating to the theory that population growth will not outstrip the Earth's resources, contrary to the predictions of Thomas Robert Malthus.  |  Many economists today are non-Malthusian and believe technology can solve resource scarcity. 许多当今的经济学家是非马尔萨斯主义者，他们相信科技能够解决资源短缺问题。 | Synonyms:  cornucopian, optimistic (regarding resource availability) | Antonyms: Malthusian, pessimistic (regarding resource availability) | adjective


non-Malthusian:/ˌnɒn mælˈθuːziən/ | n. | 非马尔萨斯主义者 | A person who does not subscribe to the theories of Thomas Robert Malthus regarding population growth exceeding resource availability. |  The non-Malthusian argued that innovation would prevent mass starvation.  这位非马尔萨斯主义者认为创新将阻止大规模的饥荒。 | Synonyms: cornucopian, optimist (regarding resource availability)  | Antonyms: Malthusian, pessimist (regarding resource availability) | noun
