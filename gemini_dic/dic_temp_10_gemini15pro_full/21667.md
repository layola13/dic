blustering:/ˈblʌstərɪŋ/| adj.| 咆哮的；狂吹的；大吵大闹的 | characterized by strong winds and typically rain, snow, or sleet. Loudly boastful or threatening, typically with little effect. | The blustering wind rattled the windows.  狂风拍打着窗户。| 近义词：boisterous, stormy, tumultuous, ranting, raving | 反义词：calm, quiet, gentle, peaceful | 词性: adjective

词源: From the verb "bluster," which likely originated as an imitative word, perhaps influenced by Middle Low German “blusteren” (to blow hard).
