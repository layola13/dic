luminary:/ˈluːmɪnɛri/ | n. |杰出人物，权威，发光体| A person who inspires or influences others, especially one prominent in a particular sphere.  A natural light-giving body, especially the sun or moon. | He was a luminary in the field of astrophysics. 他是天体物理学领域的杰出人物。 The moon is a luminary of the night sky. 月亮是夜空中的一盏明灯。 | synonyms: leading light, star, notable, dignitary | antonyms: unknown, nonentity | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *luminarie*, from Old French *luminaire* (modern French *luminaire*), from Latin *luminare* (“to light up, illuminate”), from *lumen* (“light”).
