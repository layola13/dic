horrify:/ˈhɔːrɪfaɪ/ (US) /ˈhɒrɪfaɪ/ (UK)| vt.|使恐惧，使震惊|To fill with horror; shock greatly.|The violent scenes in the movie horrified many viewers. 电影中的暴力场面使许多观众感到恐惧。|近义词:terrify, frighten, shock, appall|反义词:comfort, soothe, please|verb

horrifying:/ˈhɔːrɪfaɪɪŋ/ (US) /ˈhɒrɪfaɪɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|令人恐惧的，骇人听闻的|Causing horror.|The news of the earthquake was horrifying. 地震的消息令人恐惧。|近义词:terrible, dreadful, frightful, shocking|反义词:comforting, pleasing, delightful|adjective

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *horrifyen, from Old French horrifier, from Latin horrificāre (“to make rough or bristly, to make terrible”), from horrificus (“causing horror”), from horrēre (“to bristle, to shudder”), from horrōr (“bristling hair, shuddering, horror”).
