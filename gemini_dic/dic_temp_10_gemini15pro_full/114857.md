mammock:/ˈmæmək/| n. |碎片，碎块；一口；少量|a shapeless piece, scrap, fragment; a bite; a small amount|The explosion reduced the building to a mammock of rubble. 爆炸把大楼变成了一堆瓦砾。|Synonyms: fragment, scrap, piece, bit, morsel|Antonyms: whole, entirety|noun

Etymology->
From Middle English *mammoke, from Old Norse *mamka (“to chew”), from Proto-Germanic *mamjaną (“to chew, mumble”), from Proto-Indo-European *mems- (“to chew, bite”). Cognate with Danish mamme (“to mumble, chew”), Swedish mumsa (“to munch, mumble”), Icelandic mamla (“to mumble”).
