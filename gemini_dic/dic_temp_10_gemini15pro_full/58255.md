drumlin:/ˈdrʌmlɪn/| n. | 鼓丘；蛋形丘 |A smooth, elongated hill, typically formed beneath a glacier and aligned in the direction of ice flow.|The landscape was dotted with drumlins, evidence of the ancient glacier's passage.| 近义词:hill, mound | 反义词:valley, depression | 词性:名词

Etymology: From Irish *droimnín*, diminutive of *drom* (“ridge, back”).
