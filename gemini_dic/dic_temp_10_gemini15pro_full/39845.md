constable:/ˈkɒnstəbl/ (UK), /ˈkɑːnstəbl/ (US)| n.|警察；警官；治安官|A police officer of the lowest rank.|The constable arrested the thief. 警官逮捕了小偷。|Synonyms: officer, policeman, policewoman|Antonyms: criminal, offender|noun

constables:/ˈkɒnstəblz/ (UK), /ˈkɑːnstəblz/ (US)| n.|警察；警官；治安官（复数）|Plural form of constable, referring to multiple police officers of the lowest rank.|The constables were patrolling the streets.  警官们在街上巡逻。|Synonyms: officers, policemen, policewomen|Antonyms: criminals, offenders|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French conestable, from Latin comes stabuli (“count of the stable”), originally a high-ranking official in the Roman Empire and later in medieval Europe, responsible for managing horses and stables. The term evolved to denote a high-ranking military officer and eventually a peace officer.
