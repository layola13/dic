levered:/ˈliːvərd/ /ˈlɛvərd/| adj.|杠杆化的，举债经营的|Using borrowed capital as a funding source; operating with leverage.  A company is levered if it has debt. |Example 1: The company's levered returns were impressive, but the high debt levels also posed a significant risk. 这家公司的杠杆回报令人印象深刻，但高债务水平也带来了重大风险。

Example 2:  Levered buyouts are common in the private equity world. 杠杆收购在私募股权领域很常见。|近义词：geared, leveraged|反义词:unlevered, ungeared|词性:形容词

lever:/ˈliːvər/ /ˈlɛvər/| n. vt.|杠杆; 用杠杆撬动|A rigid bar resting on a pivot, used to help move a heavy or firmly fixed load with one end when pressure is applied to the other.|Example 1:  Use a lever to lift the heavy rock. 用杠杆撬起这块沉重的石头。

Example 2: He levered the door open with a crowbar. 他用撬棍撬开了门。|近义词: crowbar, pry bar (as nouns); pry, prize (as verbs)|反义词: N/A|词性:名词,动词


词源: levered 来自于名词 lever，lever 源于古法语 levier，最终源于拉丁语 levare，意思是“举起，抬起”。 -ed 后缀将名词转化为形容词，表示“具有...的特征”。
