catabaptist:/ˌkætəˈbæptɪst/ | n. | 再洗礼派教徒；重浸礼派教徒 | A member of a radical Protestant sect that arose in Europe in the 16th century, advocating adult baptism and rejecting infant baptism. | Example: The catabaptists were persecuted for their beliefs. 再洗礼派教徒因其信仰而受到迫害。| Example:  Many Catabaptists fled Europe to escape religious persecution. 许多再洗礼派教徒逃离欧洲以逃避宗教迫害。 | Synonym: Anabaptist | Antonym: N/A | Noun
