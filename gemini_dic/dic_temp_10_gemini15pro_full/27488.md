campanularian:/ˌkæmpənˈjʊləriən/ | n. adj. | 钟形水母；钟形水母类的 | A small, transparent, bell-shaped marine animal, a type of hydroid.  It is characterized by its bell-shaped body and branching colonies. | Example: The researchers observed the feeding behavior of the campanularian. 研究人员观察了钟形水母的摄食行为。| Example:  The campanularian colony attached itself to the seaweed. 钟形水母群体附着在海藻上。 | Synonym: hydroid, hydrozoan | Antonym: None | Noun, Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Modern Latin *campanularia, from Latin campanula (“small bell”), diminutive of campana (“bell”).  Ultimately from Latin campana (“of Campania”, a region in Italy), as bells were first widely used there.
