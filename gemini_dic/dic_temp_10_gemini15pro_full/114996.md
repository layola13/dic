mandarinate:/ˈmændərɪneɪt/| v. |用官话讲话；像官僚一样说话；发号施令|to speak in an authoritative or pompous manner, like a mandarin (a high-ranking official)|He mandarinated about the importance of following procedures. 他用官腔强调了遵守程序的重要性。|The CEO mandarinated to his employees about the new company policy. 首席执行官向员工们就公司的新政策发号施令。|近义词：pontificate, declaim, dictate, preach|反义词：listen, consult, cooperate|词性:动词

mandarin:/ˈmændərɪn/| n. adj.| 官话；柑橘；中国官吏|1. (n.) a high-ranking official in the Chinese empire. 2. (n.) the standard literary and official form of Chinese based on the Beijing dialect. 3. (adj.) of or relating to mandarins or the Mandarin language. 4. (n.) a small citrus fruit similar to a tangerine.| The emperor consulted his mandarins. 皇帝咨询了他的官员们。|He speaks Mandarin fluently. 他说一口流利的普通话。|She peeled a mandarin. 她剥了一个柑橘。|近义词：(for n. 1) official, bureaucrat; (for n. 2) Putonghua; (for n. 3) official; (for n. 4) tangerine, clementine|反义词：(for n. 3) informal, colloquial|词性:名词，形容词
