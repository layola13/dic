esqr:/ˈɛsˌkwʌɪər/ | n. | Esquire | A title of respect, historically given to men of gentle birth, especially one who held land but had no higher hereditary title. Now used more broadly as a courtesy title, often in legal contexts. | Example: John Smith, Esqr. | 近义词: Mr., gentleman | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Old French *esquier*, from Latin *scūtārius* "shield bearer," from *scūtum* "shield."
