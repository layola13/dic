heterogamy: /ˌhɛtərˈɒɡəmi/ (UK) /ˌhɛtərˈɑːɡəmi/ (US)| n. |异配生殖，异婚| Reproduction involving the union of two dissimilar gametes (e.g., dissimilar in size, form, or motility) or of individuals of dissimilar sexual type.  The state of having different modes of sexual reproduction within a species or population. In sociology, marriage between people of different social groups.|

例句：Heterogamy is common in many species of algae. 许多藻类都存在异配生殖现象。

例句：The study examined the effects of heterogamy on social mobility. 这项研究调查了异婚对社会流动性的影响。


近义词：anisogamy (specifically referring to gametes of different sizes),  intermarriage (in a sociological context)|
反义词：homogamy, isogamy|
词性：noun|

词源：From hetero- (different) + -gamy (marriage, union, referring to gametes).  Ultimately from Greek heteros (other, different) + gamos (marriage).|
