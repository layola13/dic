commonplace:/ˈkɒmənpleɪs/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmənpleɪs/ (US)| adj. n.| 平凡的，普通的；寻常的事物 | Something that is commonplace is ordinary and not interesting.  |  Wearing jeans is commonplace in most Western countries.  在大多数西方国家，穿牛仔裤是很平常的事。 | synonyms: ordinary, usual, common, everyday, routine, run-of-the-mill, pedestrian, unexceptional, unremarkable, uninspired, mundane, humdrum, banal, trite, clichéd | antonyms: unusual, remarkable, extraordinary, exceptional, original | adjective

commonplace:/ˈkɒmənpleɪs/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmənpleɪs/ (US)| n. | 平凡的事物，寻常的事 | A commonplace is an ordinary remark or observation, especially one that expresses a popular belief. |  He regarded violence as a commonplace of big-city life. 他认为暴力是大城市生活中司空见惯的事情。 | synonyms: cliché, truism, platitude, banality | antonyms: novelty, rarity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: comun place, denoting a rhetorical topic, from Old French lieu commun, from Latin locus communis ‘shared place’.
