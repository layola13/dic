fast-tracks:/ˈfæstˌtræks/ |v. (fast-tracked, fast-tracking)|快速处理，加速完成|To expedite a process, especially by using special procedures to bypass normal routines.|The company fast-tracked the development of the new drug to get it to market quickly.  公司快速推进新药的研发，以便尽快将其推向市场。|Example 2: The government fast-tracked the visa application for the Olympic athletes. 政府为奥运健儿快速办理了签证申请。|Synonyms: expedite, accelerate, hasten, rush|Antonyms: delay, hinder, obstruct|verb

fast track:/ˈfæst træk/ |n.|快速通道，快速晋升|A route or method which provides for more rapid progress than usual.|He's on the fast track to becoming a partner in the firm. 他正快速晋升，即将成为公司的合伙人。|Example 2:  The fast track program allows students to graduate early. 快速通道项目允许学生提前毕业。|Synonyms: express route, shortcut, rapid advancement|Antonyms: slow lane, normal route|noun
