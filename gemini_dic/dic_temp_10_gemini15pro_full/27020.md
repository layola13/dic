calcus: /ˈkælkjʊs/ | n. |结石; 积垢; 牙垢 | A hard mass formed in the kidneys, gallbladder, or urinary bladder, consisting of mineral salts or other substances. Also, a similar deposit on the teeth or elsewhere in the body. |  Kidney stones can be incredibly painful. 肾结石会非常痛。 The dentist removed the calculus from my teeth. 牙医从我的牙齿上清除了牙垢。 | 近义词: stone, deposit, tartar (for teeth) | 反义词: None | 词性: noun

词源: From Latin *calculus* "small pebble," diminutive of *calx* "limestone, heel."  The original meaning referred to pebbles used for counting, hence the mathematical meaning also developed.
