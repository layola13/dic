一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Ich esse jeden Tag Obst. (I eat fruit every day.)
Er isst gern Fleisch. (He likes to eat meat.)

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
This tense doesn't exist in German in the same way as in English.  The present tense is often used to express ongoing actions.  A construction like "Ich bin am Essen" (I am at the eating) is possible but less common.
Ich esse gerade zu Mittag. (I am currently eating lunch.)
Sie isst gerade ein Stück Kuchen.  (She is currently eating a piece of cake.)

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
Ich habe einen Apfel gegessen. (I have eaten an apple.)
Wir haben im Restaurant gegessen. (We have eaten in the restaurant.)

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
This tense is not directly translated into German.  The perfect tense is often used instead.
Ich habe den ganzen Tag gegessen. (I have been eating all day.)
Er hat die ganze Zeit Schokolade gegessen. (He has been eating chocolate all the time.)

一般过去时 (Simple Past):
Ich aß gestern Abend Pizza. (I ate pizza last night.)
Sie aßen zusammen zu Mittag. (They ate lunch together.)

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
Similar to the present continuous, this tense is not directly translated.  The preterite (simple past) is often used.
Ich aß gerade, als das Telefon klingelte. (I was eating when the phone rang.)
Sie aß gerade Kuchen, als er hereinkam. (She was eating cake when he came in.)

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
Ich hatte schon gegessen, bevor sie ankam. (I had already eaten before she arrived.)
Sie hatten im Restaurant gegessen, bevor sie ins Kino gingen. (They had eaten in the restaurant before they went to the cinema.)

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
Again, this tense doesn't have a direct equivalent.  The past perfect is often used instead.
Ich hatte den ganzen Tag gegessen, bevor ich ins Bett ging. (I had been eating all day before I went to bed.)
Er hatte stundenlang gegessen, bevor er satt war. (He had been eating for hours before he was full.)


一般将来时 (Simple Future):
Ich werde morgen frühstücken. (I will have breakfast tomorrow.)
Wir werden im Restaurant essen. (We will eat in the restaurant.)

将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
This tense doesn't have a direct German equivalent. The future tense is often used instead.
Ich werde morgen Abend essen. (I will be eating tomorrow evening.)
Sie werden die ganze Woche essen. (They will be eating all week.)

将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
Ich werde bis morgen Abend gegessen haben. (I will have eaten by tomorrow evening.)
Wir werden bis dahin alles aufgegessen haben. (We will have eaten everything by then.)

将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
This tense has no direct German equivalent. The future perfect is often used instead.
Bis morgen werde ich den ganzen Tag gegessen haben. (By tomorrow I will have been eating all day.)
Bis nächste Woche wird er die ganze Zeit gegessen haben. (By next week he will have been eating the whole time.)

虚拟语气:
Wenn ich Hunger hätte, würde ich essen. (If I were hungry, I would eat.)
Ich wünschte, ich könnte mehr essen. (I wish I could eat more.)

被动语态:
Das Essen wird serviert. (The food is being served.)
Die Pizza wurde gegessen. (The pizza was eaten.)

疑问句:
Was isst du? (What are you eating?)
Hast du schon gegessen? (Have you eaten yet?)

否定句:
Ich esse nichts. (I am not eating anything.)
Ich habe noch nicht gegessen. (I haven't eaten yet.)

注意事项:  "essen" is a regular verb in German.  The past participle is "gegessen," and it is used with the auxiliary verbs "haben" (to have) in the perfect tenses.  The imperative forms are "iss!" (singular) and "esst!" (plural).
