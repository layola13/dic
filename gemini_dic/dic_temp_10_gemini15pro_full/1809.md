acrimony: /ˈækrɪməni/ /ˈækrɪmoʊni/ | n. | 尖刻；刻薄；激烈 | bitterness or ill feeling. | The acrimony of the divorce proceedings surprised everyone. 离婚诉讼的激烈程度令所有人惊讶。

The political debate was marked by acrimony. 这场政治辩论的特点是尖刻。| 近义词: bitterness, rancor, animosity, hostility, venom, vitriol | 反义词: goodwill, amity, civility, cordiality, harmony | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *acrimonia* ("sharpness, pungency of taste or smell; bitterness of speech or temper"), from *acris* ("sharp, pointed, pungent")


USAGE->
The acrimony between the two families has lasted for generations. = 这两个家族之间的积怨已经持续了几代人。

There was an undercurrent of acrimony in her voice. = 她的声音里带着一丝尖刻。


PHRASE->
an acrimony of tone = 语气尖刻
the acrimony of the debate = 辩论的激烈程度
the acrimony of the divorce = 离婚的激烈程度
filled with acrimony = 充满尖刻
an acrimony of feeling = 激烈的感情
