conquerors: /ˈkɒŋkərərz/ (UK) /ˈkɑːŋkərərz/ (US)
n.
征服者，胜利者
Those who overcome and subdue by force of arms, or by superior power or skill.
The conquerors claimed the spoils of war. 征服者们宣称拥有战利品。
The Roman conquerors built roads and cities across their vast empire. 罗马征服者在他们广阔的帝国中修建了道路和城市。

近义词: victors, winners, champions, vanquishers
反义词: losers, defeated, vanquished, subjugated
词性: 名词 (plural)

词源: From Old French conquerour, from Latin conqueror, from conquirere "to seek out, procure, win, gain, acquire, conquer," from com- "together" (see com-) + quaerere "to seek, gain, ask" (see query (v.)). The -or suffix is from Latin -or.
