antimechanism:/ˌæntiˈmɛkənɪzəm/
|n.|反机械论; 反机械主义|A philosophical doctrine that opposes mechanism, especially the application of mechanistic principles to living organisms or the explanation of human behavior.|The biologist argued against antimechanism, emphasizing the importance of physical and chemical processes in life.|近义词:vitalism, holism|反义词:mechanism, reductionism|词性:名词

Etymology:
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to (from Greek anti-)
mechanism (noun): a system of parts working together in a machine; a natural or established process by which something takes place or is brought about. (From Greek mēkhanē 'machine')
