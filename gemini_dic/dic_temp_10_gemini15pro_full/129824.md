non-Hebrew:/nɒnˈhiːbruː/ | adj. | 非希伯来语的，非犹太人的 | Not of or relating to the Hebrews or their language; Gentile. |  Many non-Hebrew scholars study the Old Testament. 许多非希伯来学者研究旧约圣经。| synonyms: Gentile, non-Jewish | antonyms: Hebrew, Jewish | adjective

Etymology:  "non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "Hebrew" (referring to the Hebrews or their language). The prefix "non-" comes from Latin "non" meaning "not".  "Hebrew" comes from Late Latin "Hebraeus", from Greek "Hebraios", ultimately from Hebrew "Ivri" (meaning "one from across the river").
