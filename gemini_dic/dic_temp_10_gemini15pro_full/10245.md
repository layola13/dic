apothegm: /ˈæpəθɛm/ | n. | 格言；箴言；警句 | A concise and witty saying, often expressing a general truth or principle. |  Example 1:  "Know thyself" is a famous apothegm.  “认识你自己”是一句著名的格言。

Example 2:  She sprinkled her conversation with apothegms gleaned from ancient philosophers.  她在谈话中穿插着从古代哲学家那里收集来的格言。 | 近义词:  maxim, adage, proverb, saw, dictum | 反义词:  platitude, cliché | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *apothegma, from Greek *apóphthĕgma "a thing declared," from *apóphthĕggomai "I speak one's mind plainly, declare," from *apó "off, away from" + *phthĕggomai "I speak."
