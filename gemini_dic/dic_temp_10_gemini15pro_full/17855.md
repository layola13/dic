beefeater:/ˈbiːfiːtər/| n. |伦敦塔卫士；(旧时)食牛肉者|A ceremonial guardian of the Tower of London, historically a Yeoman Warder.  Originally, perhaps, a servant in a royal or noble household. |例句：The beefeater gave a tour of the Tower of London.  伦敦塔卫士带领游客参观了伦敦塔。

例句：Tourists love to have their pictures taken with the beefeaters. 游客们喜欢与伦敦塔卫士合影。|近义词：Yeoman Warder, guard|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From beef +‎ eater.  Originally, perhaps, a servant in a royal or noble household, or specifically a yeoman of the guard. The current meaning, specifically referring to the Yeoman Warders of the Tower of London, may derive from their historic allowance of rations of beef.
