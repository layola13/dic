encyclopedically: /ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpiːdɪkli/ | adv. | 百科全书式地，全面地 |  In a way that resembles an encyclopedia, covering a wide range of information comprehensively. | He described the event encyclopedically, leaving out no detail. 他对这件事进行了百科全书式的描述，没有遗漏任何细节。 | comprehensively, exhaustively, thoroughly | superficially, briefly, cursorily | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From encyclopedia + -ically.  "Encyclopedia" comes from Late Latin encyclopaedia,  from the Greek enkyklios paideia "general education," from enkyklios "circular, recurring" (from en- "in" + kyklos "circle") + paideia "education, rearing of a child."
