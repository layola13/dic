abetment:/əˈbeɪtmənt/| n. |教唆；煽动；幇助|The act of encouraging or assisting someone to commit a crime or other wrongful act.|He was charged with aiding and abetment in the robbery. 他被指控协助和教唆抢劫。|Synonyms: encouragement, assistance, support, incitement|Antonyms: discouragement, dissuasion, prevention, hindrance|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Anglo-French abeter, from a- (expressing direction or addition) + beter (to bait). Ultimately from the same Germanic root as "bait".

USAGE->
The abetment of the crime led to further charges. =  对犯罪的教唆导致了进一步的指控。
The lawyer argued that his client's actions did not constitute abetment. = 律师辩称，其当事人的行为不构成教唆。

PHRASE->
abetment of suicide = 教唆自杀
abetment to murder = 教唆谋杀
charged with abetment = 被控教唆
found guilty of abetment = 被判教唆罪成立
abetment of a crime = 教唆犯罪
