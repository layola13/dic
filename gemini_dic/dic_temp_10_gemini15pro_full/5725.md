alveolarity:/ˌælviəˈlærɪti/ | n. | 肺泡性；齿槽音性 | The quality of being produced with the tongue close to or touching the alveolar ridge (the ridge just behind the upper teeth).  The sounds /t/, /d/, /n/, /s/, /z/, /l/ are alveolar consonants in English. |  The alveolarity of the /t/ sound is crucial for its correct pronunciation. /t/音的肺泡性对其正确发音至关重要。 |  Synonyms:  alveolar articulation | Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but could be contrasted with other places of articulation like bilabiality or velarity) | noun
