inter-libeled:/ˌɪntərˈlaɪbəld/ | adj. |互相诽谤的|Accusing each other of libel; defaming one another.  This word is not commonly used and might be considered non-standard.  It's constructed using the prefix "inter-" meaning "between" or "mutually" and the past participle of the verb "libel".|The two rival politicians inter-libeled each other throughout the campaign.  这两个敌对的政治家在整个竞选过程中互相诽谤。|Near synonyms: mutually defamed, reciprocally slandered |Antonyms: mutually praised, reciprocally commended|adjective
