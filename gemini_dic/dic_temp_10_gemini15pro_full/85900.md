headhunted:/ˈhɛdˌhʌntɪd/
adjective
猎头的；被猎头挖走的
Having been approached by a headhunter with an offer of a job.  Being sought out by a recruiter or executive search firm for a specific position.
He was headhunted by a rival company. 他被一家竞争对手公司挖走了。
Synonyms:recruited, poached, sought after
Antonyms:unemployed, overlooked, rejected
词性:adjective


headhunt:/ˈhɛdˌhʌnt/
verb
猎头；物色人才
To try to persuade someone to leave their job and go to work for another company.  The practice of identifying and attracting suitable candidates for senior positions in organizations.
They're headhunting managers from rival companies.  他们正在从竞争对手公司挖经理。
Synonyms:recruit, poach, scout
Antonyms:dismiss, fire, lay off
词性:verb


headhunter:/ˈhɛdˌhʌntər/
noun
猎头
A person who tries to persuade someone to leave their job and go to work for another company.  A recruiter specializing in placing high-level executives or professionals.
She works as a headhunter for a large multinational corporation. 她是一家大型跨国公司的猎头。
Synonyms:recruiter, executive searcher, talent scout
Antonyms:employee, candidate, applicant
词性:noun
