ace up their sleeve:/eɪs ʌp ðɛər sliːv/| idiom | 藏在袖子里的王牌; 秘而不宣的优势; 锦囊妙计 | An idiom referring to a secret advantage or plan that someone can use when necessary. It originates from card games where a hidden ace guarantees victory. | "He had an ace up his sleeve – he knew the chairman of the board." 他胸有成竹——他认识董事会的董事长。

"She kept her ace up her sleeve until the final round of negotiations." 她把她的王牌留到最后一轮谈判才用。 | 近义词: secret weapon, hidden advantage, trump card | 反义词: disadvantage, weakness | 词性: idiom


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He always has an ace up his sleeve. = 他总是留有后手。
She never reveals what ace she has up her sleeve. = 她从不揭露她有什么锦囊妙计。

一般过去时:
He had an ace up his sleeve which secured him the victory. = 他胸有成竹，这确保了他的胜利。
She wouldn't have won if she hadn't had an ace up her sleeve. = 如果她没有锦囊妙计，她是不会赢的。

现在完成时:
They've had an ace up their sleeve all along. = 他们一直留有后手。
She has always had an ace up her sleeve in business dealings. =  她在商业交易中总是有锦囊妙计。


过去完成时:
He had had an ace up his sleeve, but he didn't need to use it. = 他本来留有后手，但不需要用。
She had had an ace up her sleeve that she'd been saving for the right moment. = 她一直留有后手，就为了在合适的时机使用。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish I had an ace up my sleeve in this situation. = 我希望在这种情况下我有一个锦囊妙计。

词源:
This idiom originates from card games, particularly poker, where having a hidden ace guarantees a winning hand. The “sleeve” refers to the practice of hiding cards up one's sleeve to cheat. Over time, the phrase evolved to describe any hidden advantage or plan, regardless of its ethical implications.
