discipleship:/dɪˈsaɪpəlʃɪp/| n.|门徒身份，门徒训练，弟子身份|The state or process of being a disciple; the action of following and learning from a religious or philosophical teacher.|She dedicated her life to Christian discipleship. 她将自己的一生奉献给了基督教门徒训练。|近义词：apprenticeship, following, learning, tutelage|反义词：leadership, mentorship|词性:名词

Etymology: From disciple + -ship.  "Disciple" comes from Old French disciple and directly from Latin discipulus "pupil, student," from discere "to learn." The suffix "-ship" forms abstract nouns of state, condition, or quality.
