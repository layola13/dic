consorters:/kənˈsɔːrtərz/
n.
合作者，结伴者，共谋者
People who associate with others, especially for some common purpose or in some illegal activity.
例句：The consorters planned the robbery meticulously.  这些共谋者精心策划了抢劫。

例句：The police arrested the thief and his consorters. 警察逮捕了小偷和他的同伙。

近义词：accomplices, associates, partners, collaborators, confederates
反义词：opponents, adversaries, enemies
词性: 名词(plural)

consort:/kənˈsɔːrt/
n.
配偶（尤指皇室成员）,伙伴,同伴
A spouse, especially of a reigning monarch; a companion or partner.
例句：The prince consort attended the state dinner. 王子 consort 出席了国宴。

例句：She chose her closest friend as her consort in the business venture. 她选择了她最亲密的朋友作为她在商业冒险中的伙伴。

近义词：spouse, partner, companion, associate
反义词：opponent, enemy
词性: 名词

consort:/kənˈsɔːrt/
v.
交往，结交；陪伴
To associate with; to keep company.
例句：He consorts with known criminals. 他与已知的罪犯来往。

例句：She consorted with artists and musicians. 她与艺术家和音乐家交往。

近义词：associate, fraternize, mingle, socialize
反义词：avoid, shun
词性: 动词

词源:
From Old French *consorter* "to share, divide," from Latin *consors* "sharing, partaking, partner," from *com-* "together" (see com-) + *sors* "lot, fate, share" (see sort (n.)). Meaning "spouse of a monarch" is from mid-15c. Related: *Consorted*; *consorting*.
