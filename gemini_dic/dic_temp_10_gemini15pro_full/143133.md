outcast:/ˈaʊtkæst/| n. |被抛弃者，被驱逐者；流浪者|a person who has been rejected by society or a social group|例句：He felt like an outcast after his divorce.  他离婚后感觉像个被社会抛弃的人。

例句：The outcast wandered the streets, searching for food and shelter.  那个流浪者在街上游荡，寻找食物和住所。|近义词：pariah, exile, reject, leper|反义词:insider, accepted member|词性:名词

outcast:/ˈaʊtkæst/| adj. |被抛弃的，被驱逐的|rejected and despised by society|例句：She lived an outcast existence in the remote mountains.  她在偏远的山区过着被抛弃的生活。

例句：He was treated as an outcast by his former friends.  他被以前的朋友们当作弃儿对待。|近义词：rejected, shunned, exiled, ostracized|反义词:accepted, welcomed|词性:形容词

词源:From Old Norse *út-kasta (“to cast out”), from út (“out”) + kasta (“to cast”).
