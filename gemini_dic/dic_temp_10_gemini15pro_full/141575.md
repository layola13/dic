opposite: /ˈɒpəzɪt/ (UK) /ˈɑːpəzɪt/ (US) | adj. n. prep. adv.| 相反的，对面的；对立面；在…对面 |  Something that is opposite is completely different from something else. | The car was parked opposite the bank. 这辆车停在银行对面。| synonyms: contrasting, contrary, reverse, inverse | antonyms: similar, same, alike | adjective

opposite: /ˈɒpəzɪt/ (UK) /ˈɑːpəzɪt/ (US) | n.| 对立面，对立物 |  A person or thing that is as different as possible from another. | My brother and I are opposites in every way. 我和我哥哥在各方面都截然不同。 | synonyms: antithesis, contrary, reverse | antonyms: counterpart, equivalent | noun

opposite: /ˈɒpəzɪt/ (UK) /ˈɑːpəzɪt/ (US) | prep.| 在…对面 |  Facing something, on the other side. | The post office is opposite the bank. 邮局在银行对面。 | synonyms: across from, facing, vis-à-vis | antonyms: behind, beside | preposition

opposite: /ˈɒpəzɪt/ (UK) /ˈɑːpəzɪt/ (US) | adv.| 在对面，对过 |  In a position facing something, on the other side. | He lives opposite. 他住在对面。 | synonyms: across, facing | antonyms: behind, beside | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *opposit (Modern French opposite), from Latin oppositus, past participle of opponere (“to place against, oppose”), from ob (“against”) + ponere (“to put, place”).
