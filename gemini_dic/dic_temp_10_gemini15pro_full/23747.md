bravado:/brəˈvɑːdoʊ/ (US) /brəˈvɑːdəʊ/ (UK)
n.|虚张声势，逞能|A show of bravery or defiance, often intended to conceal doubts or insecurities.|He displayed a false bravado, trying to hide his fear.|近义词: swagger, bluster, braggadocio|反义词: timidity, humility, meekness|词性: 名词

bravados:/brəˈvɑːdoʊz/ (US) /brəˈvɑːdəʊz/ (UK)
n.|虚张声势，逞能（复数）|Plural form of bravado, referring to multiple instances of boastful or swaggering behavior.|His bravados were easily seen through by his peers.|近义词: boasts, swaggering, bluster|反义词: acts of humility, displays of meekness|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From Italian *bravata* meaning "bragging, boasting," from *bravo* meaning "wild, savage," ultimately of Germanic origin.
