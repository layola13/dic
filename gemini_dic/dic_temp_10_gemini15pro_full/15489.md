badly-off:/ˈbædli ˈɒf/ (UK), /ˈbædli ˈɔːf/ (US)| adj. |贫困的，境况不好的|Poor; not having enough money for basic needs.|Many families in the area are badly-off and struggle to afford food. 这个地区的很多家庭都很贫困，难以负担食物。| The charity helps badly-off families at Christmas.  这个慈善机构在圣诞节帮助贫困家庭。|近义词：poor, impoverished, needy, disadvantaged, hard up, strapped for cash|反义词：rich, wealthy, affluent, well-off, prosperous|词性:形容词

词源:  This term is a compound adjective formed from "badly" (an adverb meaning "in a bad way") and "off" (an adjective or adverb with various meanings, including "away," "removed," and in this context, "in a poor financial state").  The combination signifies being in a bad financial situation.
