dius: /ˈdiːəs/ | n. | 神 | (In Roman mythology) a god, especially Jupiter. |  Julius Caesar claimed descent from Venus and, through her, from the Trojan prince Aeneas and ultimately from the goddess Dius. | 近义词: deity, divinity | 反义词: mortal | 词性: 名词

词源: From Latin *diuus (“divine”), from Proto-Italic *deiwos, from Proto-Indo-European *deywós (“god, celestial, bright”). Cognate with Old English tīw (“Tiw, god of war and the sky”), Old High German Zīo (“Tiw”), Old Norse Týr (“Tyr, god of war and justice”).
