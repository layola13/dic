crustacean:/krʌˈsteɪʃən/| n. |甲壳类动物|An arthropod of the large, mainly aquatic group Crustacea, such as a crab, lobster, shrimp, or barnacle.|例句：Lobsters are crustaceans. 龙虾是甲壳类动物。

例句：Many crustaceans are scavengers. 许多甲壳类动物是食腐动物。|近义词：arthropod, shellfish |反义词: (No direct antonym for a biological classification) |词性: noun

crustated:/ˈkrʌsteɪtɪd/ | adj. |有壳的，外壳坚硬的|Having a crust or shell; covered with a hard outer layer.|例句：The crustated remains of the ancient trilobite were found in the rock.  在这块岩石中发现了古代三叶虫的有壳遗骸。

例句：The artist created a crustated sculpture using layers of plaster.  这位艺术家用多层石膏创作了一个外壳坚硬的雕塑。|近义词：crusted, shelled, encased |反义词: soft, shell-less |词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *crustatus*, past participle of *crustare* "to encrust," from *crusta* "crust, shell."
