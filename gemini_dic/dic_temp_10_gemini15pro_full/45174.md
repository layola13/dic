cuffs:/kʌfs/| n. |手铐，袖口|A restraint, typically a pair of metal rings connected by a short chain, used to restrain someone's wrists; the folded-back edge of a sleeve or trouser leg.|The police officer placed the cuffs on the suspect. 警察给嫌疑人戴上了手铐。|Synonyms: shackles, manacles, restraints, handcuffs (specifically for wrists), sleeve ends, trouser cuffs|Antonyms: freedom, liberation|Noun

cuffs:/kʌfs/| n. |袖口，裤口翻边|The folded-back edge of a sleeve or trouser leg.|He rolled up his shirt cuffs. 他卷起了衬衫袖口。|Synonyms: sleeve ends, trouser cuffs, turn-ups (for trousers)|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but could consider "unrolled sleeves/trousers")|Noun
