denialist:/dɪˈnaɪəlɪst/
n.
否认者；抵赖者；拒绝接受现实的人
A person who refuses to admit the truth or reality of something, especially something unpleasant or inconvenient.
例句：Climate change denialists refuse to accept the scientific consensus. 气候变化否认者拒绝接受科学共识。

例句：He's a Holocaust denialist, spreading dangerous misinformation. 他是大屠杀否认者，散布危险的错误信息。

近义词：denier, skeptic, contrarian
反义词：accepter, believer, realist
词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From denial + -ist.  "Denial" comes from the Old French word "denier" meaning "to deny" which itself comes from the Latin "denegare". The suffix "-ist" forms agent nouns and nouns denoting adherents of doctrines, systems, etc.
