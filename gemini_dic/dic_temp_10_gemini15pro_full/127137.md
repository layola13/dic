natron:/ˈneɪtrɒn/ (US) /ˈneɪtrən/ (UK)| n.|天然碱；泡碱|A naturally occurring mixture of hydrous sodium carbonate, sodium bicarbonate, sodium chloride, and sodium sulfate. It was used by the ancient Egyptians for mummification and other purposes.|The Egyptians used natron to dry out the bodies during mummification.|Synonyms: soda ash, alkali|Antonyms: acid|Noun

natron:/ˈneɪtrɒn/ (US) /ˈneɪtrən/ (UK)| n.|泡碱，苏打|A naturally occurring alkaline salt, used especially in ancient Egypt in the process of mummification. |Natron was a key ingredient in the mummification process.|Synonyms:  trona, soda ash|Antonyms: acid|Noun
