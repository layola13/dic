basilects:/ˈbæsɪlekts/ | n. | 地方方言；基层方言 |  A regional or social dialect spoken by a subordinate group and characterized by divergence from the standard language. It often includes features considered non-standard or ungrammatical by speakers of the dominant language variety.| The basilects of the rural areas were barely comprehensible to city dwellers. 农村地区的地方方言对于城市居民来说几乎难以理解。 | 近义词: dialect, vernacular | 反义词: acrolect | 词性: 名词

词源: From Ancient Greek βάσις (básis, “base”) + λέκτος (léktos, “spoken”).  Coined by William A. Stewart in the 1960s.
