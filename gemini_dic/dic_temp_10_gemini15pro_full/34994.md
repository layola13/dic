cloistering:/ˈklɔɪstərɪŋ/ | n. | 修道院生活, 隔离, 与世隔绝 | The act of secluding oneself or others, especially in a monastery or convent; the state of being secluded. |  The writer sought cloistering in a remote cabin to finish his novel.  作家为了完成他的小说，在一个偏僻的小屋里过着与世隔绝的生活。| 近义词: seclusion, confinement, isolation, retreat | 反义词: exposure, freedom, socializing, worldliness | 词性: 名词

cloister:/ˈklɔɪstər/ | n. vt.| 修道院, 回廊；隐居；禁闭 | A covered walk in a monastery or college, typically with a wall on one side and a colonnade open to a quadrangle on the other; to seclude or confine. | The monks walked in the cloister every morning. 僧侣们每天早晨在修道院的回廊里散步。 | 近义词: monastery, convent, abbey, sanctuary | 反义词: open space, public area | 词性: 名词, 动词
