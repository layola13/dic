dissociated:/dɪˈsoʊʃieɪtɪd/ | adj. | 分离的，游离的，分裂的 | Separated, disconnected, or lacking connection; having become separate or independent; (Chemistry) having undergone dissociation into constituent molecules or ions. | The dissociated fragments of the meteorite scattered across the desert. 陨石分离的碎片散落在沙漠各处。| The patient felt dissociated from reality. 病人感觉与现实脱节。| synonyms: detached, separate, unconnected, aloof, withdrawn | antonyms: connected, associated, united, integrated | adjective

dissociate:/dɪˈsoʊʃieɪt/ | v. |  分离，游离，分裂 | To separate or become separated; to disconnect something from something else; (Chemistry) (of a molecule) to split into separate atoms, ions, or radicals. | He tried to dissociate himself from the scandal. 他试图把自己与丑闻撇清关系。| The compound dissociates in water.  这种化合物在水中会分解。| synonyms: separate, disconnect, detach, disunite, sever | antonyms: connect, associate, unite, integrate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dissociatus, past participle of dissociare "to disunite," from dis- "apart" + sociare "to associate," from socius "companion."
