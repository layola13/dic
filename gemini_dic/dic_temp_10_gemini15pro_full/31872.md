cheerleaders:/ˈtʃɪrˌliːdərz/| n.|啦啦队员|A person who leads cheers, especially one who leads organized cheering at sporting events.|The cheerleaders rallied the crowd with their energetic performance. 啦啦队员们用充满活力的表演激励了观众。|The cheerleaders encouraged the team with their cheers and stunts. 啦啦队员们用她们的欢呼和特技鼓励了球队。|近义词：supporters, fans|反义词：critics, detractors|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From cheer + leader.  "Cheer" comes from Old French "chiere" meaning "face, countenance," later evolving to mean "joyful reception." "Leader" comes from Old English "lædere" meaning "one who leads."
