dandyise:/ˈdændɪˌaɪz/| vt.|打扮成花花公子；使像花花公子|To make or become a dandy; to give a dandyish appearance to.|He dandified himself before going out. 他出门前把自己打扮得像个花花公子。|近义词：spruce up, embellish, adorn|反义词：simplify, unadorn|词性:动词

dandyish:/ˈdændɪɪʃ/| adj.|花花公子似的；衣着讲究的|Characteristic of a dandy; foppish.|He had a dandyish way of dressing. 他的穿着打扮很有花花公子的风格。|近义词：foppish, dapper, stylish|反义词：slovenly, unkempt|词性:形容词

词源: From dandy + -ize/-ise.  "Dandy" itself originated in the late 18th century, of uncertain origin, possibly a pet-form of Andrew.
