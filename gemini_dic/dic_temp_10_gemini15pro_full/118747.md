metalepsy: /ˌmɛtəˈlɛpsi/ | n. | 转喻; 超越时空的叙述 | A figure of speech in which a word or phrase from one domain of experience is used to describe another, often creating a surprising or unexpected connection.  It can also refer to a narrative technique where the boundaries between the fictional world and the real world are blurred, such as a character addressing the reader directly. |  Example 1: "The pen is mightier than the sword." (This is a metalepsis, where "pen" stands for written words and "sword" stands for military force.)  Example 2: The author suddenly appeared in his own story, disrupting the narrative and speaking directly to the reader. | Near synonyms:  transumption, metonymy | Antonyms:  literalism | noun
