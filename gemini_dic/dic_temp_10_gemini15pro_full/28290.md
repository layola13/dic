capturing:/ˈkæptʃərɪŋ/ | adj. | 俘获的；迷人的 |  The act of taking someone or something into one's possession or control, often by force. Also, capable of arousing and holding the attention. | The photographer was capturing the beauty of the sunset. 摄影师正在捕捉日落的美丽。| Synonyms: seizing, arresting, fascinating, enthralling | Antonyms: releasing, freeing, repelling, boring | Word type: Present Participle of the verb "capture"

capture:/ˈkæptʃər/ | n. vt. | 捕获，俘虏；夺得 | The act of taking something or someone by force or skill.  | The police made a daring capture of the escaped convict. 警方大胆地抓获了逃犯。 | Synonyms: seizure, arrest, acquisition | Antonyms: release, liberation, loss | Word type: Noun, Verb
