crosshairs:/ˈkrɒsˌheəz/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌheəz/ (UK)| n. |十字线，瞄准线|A pair of fine wires or lines crossing each other in the focus of an optical instrument, such as a telescope or gunsight, used as a reference point for aiming.|The sniper had the target in his crosshairs.|Synonyms: sights, reticle|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

crosshair:/ˈkrɒsˌheə(r)/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌheə(r)/ (UK)| n. |十字线，瞄准线|One of the two intersecting lines or wires in a crosshairs.|A single crosshair was slightly damaged.|Synonyms: sight line, reticle line|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
