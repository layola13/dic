muckerisms:/ˈmʌkərɪzəmz/| n. |粗俗言语，粗鲁行为|Words or behavior characteristic of a mucker; vulgarity.|He was known for his muckerisms, often using foul language in polite company.|近义词：vulgarities, obscenities, crudities|反义词：politeness, refinement, decorum|词性:noun

词源:  Derived from "mucker," a term originally referring to someone who worked with muck (dirt, manure, etc.), and later extended to mean a rough, vulgar person.  The suffix "-isms" indicates characteristic words or practices.
