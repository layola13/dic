antitheses:/ænˈtɪθɪsiːz/
n.
对照，对立面，反题
the juxtaposition of contrasting ideas, often in parallel structure
His sermon was full of startling antitheses. 他的布道充满了惊人的对立。
近义词: contrasts, opposites, contradictions
反义词: similarities, parallels, agreements
词性: 名词(plural)

antithesis:/ænˈtɪθɪsɪs/
n.
对照，对立面，反题
a person or thing that is the direct opposite of someone or something else
Love is the antithesis of selfishness. 爱是自私的对立面。
近义词: opposite, contrast, reverse
反义词: similarity, parallel, resemblance
词性: 名词(singular)


词源: From Late Latin antithesis, from Ancient Greek ἀντίθεσις (antíthesis, “opposition, contrast”), from ἀντιτίθημι (antitíthēmi, “I oppose, I set against”), from ἀντί (antí, “against”) + τίθημι (títhēmi, “I put, I place”).
