clasm: /klæzm/ /klɑːzm/
n.|扣子，钩子；紧握，拥抱|A fastening device, typically metal, used to hold two things together.  A firm hold or grip.|She fastened the clasp of her necklace. 她扣上了项链的扣子。|近义词：fastener, buckle, clip, grip, embrace|反义词：release, unfasten|词性:名词

clasp: /klæsp/ /klɑːsp/
vt.|扣住，握紧；拥抱|To fasten something with a clasp. To hold someone or something firmly in your hands or arms.|He clasped her hand tightly. 他紧紧地握住她的手。|近义词：grasp, grip, embrace, hug|反义词：release, unfasten|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *claspe, *claspen, from Old Norse *klaspa (“to fasten, buckle, button”), from Proto-Germanic *klapsōną (“to clap, flap”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)klep- (“to stick, adhere”).  Cognate with Dutch klampen (“to clamp, grasp”), German klammern (“to cling, clasp”), Swedish klämma (“to pinch, squeeze”). The noun is from the verb.
