cawing:/ˈkɔːɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈkɔːɪŋ/ (US)| n. v.| 乌鸦的叫声,  发出鸦叫声|A harsh, raucous cry, like that of a crow; the act of making such a cry. |The cawing of the crows filled the air. 乌鸦的叫声充满了空气。|The crows were cawing loudly in the trees. 乌鸦在树上大声鸣叫。|近义词：croaking, squawking|反义词: chirping, tweeting|词性:名词, 动词

Etymology:  From Middle English *cawen, from Old Norse *ká, from Proto-Germanic *kawō (“jackdaw, crow”), from Proto-Indo-European *keh₂w- (“jackdaw, crow”). Cognate with Swedish kaja (“jackdaw”), German kauen (“to chew”). The verb is a derivation from the noun.
