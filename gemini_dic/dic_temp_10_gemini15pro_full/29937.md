cave:/keɪv/| n. vi.|洞穴，窑洞；塌陷，倒塌|A large hole in the side of a hill or mountain, or one that is underground. |例句：The explorers discovered a hidden cave in the mountains. 探险家们在山中发现了一个隐藏的洞穴。

|例句：The roof of the mine caved in, trapping several workers. 矿井的顶部塌陷，困住了几名工人。|近义词：cavern, grotto, hole, cavity|反义词: mound, hill|名词|
cave:/keɪv/| vi.|塌陷，倒塌|To collapse or fall in. |例句：The pressure of the snow caused the roof to cave.  雪的压力导致屋顶塌陷。

|例句：He caved under the pressure and confessed to the crime. 他在压力下屈服并承认了罪行。|近义词：collapse, crumble, give way|反义词: hold up, support|动词|
