clearances:/ˈklɪərənsɪz/
n.
清除，清理
The removal of something unwanted or unnecessary.
The city scheduled clearances of the dilapidated buildings.  这个城市安排了对破旧建筑的清理。
clearing, removal, purge
accumulation, retention

n.
许可，批准
Official permission or authorization for something.
He received security clearance to access the confidential files. 他获得了安全许可，可以访问机密文件。
authorization, permission, approval
prohibition, denial

n.
间隙，净空
The space between two objects or surfaces.
The bridge has a clearance of 16 feet. 这座桥的净空为16英尺。
space, gap, allowance
obstruction, blockage

n.
清仓，甩卖
The sale of goods at reduced prices to get rid of them.
The store is having a clearance sale on all winter clothing. 这家商店正在对所有冬季服装进行清仓甩卖。
sale, closeout, liquidation


ETYMOLOGY->
From "clear" (verb) + "-ance" (suffix forming nouns of action or process).  "Clear" ultimately comes from the Old French "cler" meaning "bright, shining," which in turn derives from the Latin "clarus."
