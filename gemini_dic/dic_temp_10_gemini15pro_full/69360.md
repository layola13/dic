federationist:/ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪst/ | n. adj. |联邦主义者；拥护联邦制的人（或组织）；联邦制的 |A person who advocates or supports federation, especially a political system in which states or regions delegate certain powers to a central government while retaining other powers. |例句：The federationists argued for a strong central government. 联邦主义者主张建立一个强大的中央政府。

例句：She was a staunch federationist, believing in the power of unity. 她是一位坚定的联邦主义者，相信团结的力量。|近义词：federalist, unionist |反义词：separatist, secessionist |词性: noun, adjective

federationist:/ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪst/ | adj. |联邦制的 |Relating to or advocating federation. |例句：A federationist system of government. 联邦制政府体系。

例句：Federationist principles guided the formation of the new nation. 联邦制原则指导了新国家的组建。|近义词：federal |反义词：confederal, unitary |词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From federation + -ist.  Federation comes from Late Latin foederatio "league, covenant," from Latin foederare "to make a treaty, league," from foedus "treaty, league, covenant," related to fides "faith, trust."  The suffix -ist denotes "one who adheres to or advocates a doctrine, system, or practice."
