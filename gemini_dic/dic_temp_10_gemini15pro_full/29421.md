catafalque: /ˈkætəfɔːlk/ /ˈkætəfɑːlk/ | n. | 棺架；灵柩台 |A decorated platform, often raised, on which a coffin or a body in an open coffin rests in state or lies in repose before burial or cremation. | The king's body lay in state on a magnificent catafalque in the cathedral. 国王的遗体安放在大教堂里一个华丽的棺架上供人瞻仰。

The catafalque was draped in black velvet. 棺架上覆盖着黑色天鹅绒。 | synonyms: bier, hearse | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *catafalco*, from Old Italian *cadafalco*, possibly from Vulgar Latin *catafalicum* ("scaffold"), from Late Latin *cataphalicum*, from Greek *κατάφαλκος* (*kataphalikos*, "scaffold; platform").
