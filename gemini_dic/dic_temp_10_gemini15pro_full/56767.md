dopamines:/ˈdoʊpəˌmiːnz/| n. |多巴胺|A neurotransmitter associated with feelings of pleasure and reward, as well as motivation and motor control.  It plays a crucial role in various brain functions, including mood, sleep, learning, and attention.  A deficiency in dopamine can lead to Parkinson's disease, while excessive levels can contribute to schizophrenia and other mental disorders.|The release of dopamines makes you feel good.|近义词: neurotransmitter|反义词: (No direct antonym for a specific neurochemical)|词性: noun
