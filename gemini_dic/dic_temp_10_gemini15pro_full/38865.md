confederationism:/kənˌfedəˈreɪʃənɪzəm/
n.
邦联主义，联邦主义
The doctrine or principle of forming or supporting a confederation.  A system of government in which sovereign states delegate certain powers to a central government for specific purposes.
The Canadian political system is often described as an example of confederationism. 加拿大政治体系常被描述为邦联主义的例子。
Confederationism emerged as a compromise between complete independence and total integration. 邦联主义是完全独立和完全融合之间的一种妥协。
近义词:federalism, unionism
反义词:centralism, unitary system
词性:noun

confederationist:/kənˌfedəˈreɪʃənɪst/
n.
邦联主义者
A supporter of confederationism.  A person who advocates for a confederation-style government.
He was a staunch confederationist who believed in strong regional autonomy. 他是一个坚定的邦联主义者，相信强大的区域自治。
The confederationists argued for a system that preserved the sovereignty of individual states. 邦联主义者主张建立一个维护各州主权的制度。
近义词:federalist
反义词:centralist, unitarian
词性:noun

confederate:/kənˈfedərət/
adj.
结盟的，同盟的
Joined in a confederation or alliance.  United in a league or conspiracy.
The confederate states agreed to cooperate on defense and trade.  结盟的各州同意在国防和贸易方面进行合作。
The confederate armies fought against the Union during the American Civil War.  同盟军在美国内战期间与联邦军作战。
近义词:allied, united, federated
反义词:independent, separate
词性:adjective

confederate:/kənˈfedərət/
n.
同盟者，盟友
A person or group united with others for a common purpose, especially in a conspiracy or rebellion. An accomplice.
He was a confederate in the plot to overthrow the government. 他是推翻政府阴谋的同谋。
The bank robbers had several confederates who helped them escape. 银行劫匪有几个同伙帮助他们逃跑。
近义词:ally, accomplice, associate
反义词:enemy, opponent
词性:noun

confederate:/ˈkənˌfedərˌeɪt/
v.
结盟，联合
To unite in a league, alliance, or conspiracy.  To join together for a common purpose.
The smaller nations confederated to form a stronger economic bloc.  这些小国联合起来组成了一个更强大的经济集团。
Several tribes confederated to resist the invading army.  几个部落联合起来抵抗入侵的军队。
近义词:ally, unite, join forces
反义词:separate, divide
词性:verb


Etymology:
From Late Latin *confederatio, from Latin con- ‘together with’ + foederatio (see federation).
