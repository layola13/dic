electromagnets:/ɪˌlektroʊˈmæɡnɪts/ | n. |电磁铁|A magnet consisting of a coil of insulated wire wrapped around a core of ferromagnetic material, such as soft iron, that produces a magnetic field when an electric current is passed through the wire. |Electromagnets are used in many devices, such as electric motors and generators. 电磁铁用于许多设备，例如电动机和发电机。|Synonyms: solenoid, electromagnet coil |Antonyms: permanent magnet | Noun
