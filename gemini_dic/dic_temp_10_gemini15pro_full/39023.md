conflagrant:/kənˈfleɪɡrənt/
adjective
燃烧的；容易着火的；煽动性的
involved in, or relating to, a large fire that causes a lot of damage; tending to make people become angry or excited enough to fight
例句：The conflagrant warehouse sent plumes of smoke into the sky. 燃烧的仓库向天空喷出滚滚浓烟。

例句：The politician's conflagrant rhetoric incited violence in the streets.  这位政客煽动性的言论在街头引发了暴力事件。
近义词：burning, fiery, inflammatory, incendiary
反义词：extinguishing, nonflammable, peaceful, calming
词性: adjective


conflagration:/ˌkɒnfləˈɡreɪʃn/
noun
大火灾；武装冲突
a large and destructive fire; a conflict or war
例句：The conflagration destroyed several city blocks. 大火摧毁了几个街区。

例句：The small border dispute escalated into a full-blown conflagration.  小小的边境争端升级为全面战争。
近义词：fire, blaze, inferno, war, conflict
反义词：peace, truce, calm
词性: noun


词源:
From Latin conflagrāns, present participle of conflagrāre (“to burn completely”), from con- (“together”) + flagrāre (“to burn, blaze”).
