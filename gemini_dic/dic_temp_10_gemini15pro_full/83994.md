half-defiantly:/ˌhɑːf dɪˈfaɪəntli/ | adv. | 半挑衅地，略带反抗地 |  In a manner that shows some resistance or disobedience, but not completely or aggressively. It suggests a subtle or partial act of defiance. | She tilted her chin half-defiantly, refusing to back down from her position. 她略带挑衅地扬起下巴，拒绝放弃自己的立场。|  He half-defiantly muttered under his breath as he reluctantly followed orders. 他不情愿地服从命令，同时小声地咕哝着，略带反抗。 | Near synonyms:  somewhat defiantly, slightly defiantly, partially defiantly | Near antonyms:  obediently, submissively, meekly | Word type: Adverb

Etymology:
* "half-" (Old English healf) meaning "one of two equal parts"
* "defiantly" from "defy" (Old French defier, from Latin dis- "apart" + fidere "to trust") + "-ant" (suffix forming adjectives) + "-ly" (suffix forming adverbs)
