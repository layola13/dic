crucially:/ˈkruːʃəli/| adv.|至关重要地，关键地|In a crucial way; decisively.  It indicates something is of great importance and has a significant impact on the outcome of a situation.|例句：Crucially, the evidence supports our hypothesis. 关键的是，证据支持我们的假设。|近义词：importantly, significantly, vitally, essentially|反义词:insignificantly, unimportantly|词性:副词
词源: From Late Latin *crucialis, from Latin crux "cross, torture," figuratively "a difficulty, critical moment."  The sense evolution likely stems from the idea of a crossroad representing a decisive turning point.
