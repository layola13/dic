mutinousnesses:/ˌmjuːtɪnəsnɪsɪz/
|n.|叛乱，反抗，不服从|The mutinousnesses of the sailors threatened the voyage.|水手们的反抗威胁着这次航行。

|反义词：obedience, submission, compliance|

mutinous:/ˈmjuːtɪnəs/
|adj.|叛乱的，反抗的，不服从的|The mutinous crew seized control of the ship.|叛变的船员控制了这艘船。
|近义词：rebellious, revolutionary, insurgent|
|反义词：obedient, submissive, compliant|
|词性：形容词|

mutiny:/ˈmjuːtɪni/
|n.|叛乱，反抗|The mutiny was quickly suppressed by the captain.|叛乱很快就被船长镇压了。
|v.|叛乱，反抗|The soldiers mutinied against their commanding officer.|士兵们反抗他们的指挥官。
|近义词：(n.) rebellion, revolt, insurrection; (v.) rebel, revolt|
|反义词：(n. & v.) obey, submit|
|词性：名词，动词|


词源:
From Middle French *mutin, from Old French mutin (“rebellious, riotous; mutineer”), from Italian mutino (“rebellious; mutineer”), from muta (“change, alteration; riot, mutiny”), probably from Latin *movita, from movēre (“to move”).
