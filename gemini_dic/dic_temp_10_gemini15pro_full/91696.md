hyperconformity:/ˌhaɪpərˈkənˈfɔːrməti/ |n.|过度从众，超从众性|The tendency to conform to a group's behavior or beliefs to an excessive degree, often exceeding the group's actual norms.|The study showed a surprising level of hyperconformity among the participants, who agreed with obviously incorrect statements made by the majority. 这项研究显示，参与者中存在令人惊讶的过度从众现象，他们同意大多数人提出的明显不正确的陈述。|近义词: overconformity,  blind obedience, groupthink|反义词: nonconformity, individualism, independence|词性: noun

Etymology:
hyper- (prefix): from Greek, meaning "over," "above," "beyond"
conformity: from Late Middle English, from Old French conformite, from Latin conformitatem, from conformis "similar, like," from com- "together" + formis "form"
