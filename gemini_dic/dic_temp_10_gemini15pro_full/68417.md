falsifiability:/ˌfɔːlsɪfaɪəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可证伪性 | The inherent possibility that a statement, hypothesis, or theory can be proven false.  It is a key concept in the philosophy of science, associated with Karl Popper. |  The scientist stressed the importance of falsifiability in distinguishing science from pseudoscience. 科学家强调了可证伪性在区分科学与伪科学中的重要性。|  The falsifiability of his theory was questioned by his peers.  他的理论的可证伪性受到了同行的质疑。 | testability, refutability | verifiability, confirmability | noun
