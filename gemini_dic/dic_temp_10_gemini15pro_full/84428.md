hallucinating:/həˈluːsɪneɪtɪŋ/
v.| 幻觉；出现幻觉| experiencing hallucinations; seeming to see or hear things that are not really there|  He was hallucinating from the fever. 他因为发烧而产生幻觉。|近义词:tripping, imagining, fantasizing|反义词: perceiving, realizing|词性:Verb


hallucinate:/həˈluːsɪneɪt/
v.| 产生幻觉|to see or hear things that are not really there, especially because of illness or drugs| The patient began to hallucinate after taking the medication. 病人在服药后开始出现幻觉。|近义词:trip, imagine, fantasize|反义词: perceive, realize|词性:Verb


hallucination:/həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃn/
n.| 幻觉|a sensory experience of something that does not exist outside the mind, caused by various physical and mental disorders, or by reaction to certain toxic substances, or by extreme exhaustion, solitude, etc.|  He was having hallucinations brought on by lack of sleep. 他因为缺乏睡眠而产生幻觉。|近义词:delusion, illusion, mirage|反义词:reality, perception|词性:Noun

词源: Late 16th century: from Latin hallucinat- ‘wandered in mind’, from the verb hallucinari, from alucinari ‘to wander in mind’.
