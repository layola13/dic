earl:/ɜːl/| n.|伯爵；|A British nobleman ranking above a viscount and below a marquess.|例句：The Earl of Essex was a favorite of Queen Elizabeth I.  埃塞克斯伯爵是伊丽莎白一世女王的宠臣。

例句：He was made an earl for his services to the crown. 他因效忠王室而被封为伯爵。|近义词：count, nobleman|反义词: commoner|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *eorl (unattested), from Old Norse jarl "chieftain, warrior," of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Proto-Germanic *erlaz "man, warrior."  Cognate with Danish and Swedish jarl.  Replaced Old English ealdorman.
