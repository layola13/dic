dispellers:/dɪˈspelərz/
| n. |驱散者；消除者|Someone or something that dispels (drives away) something unpleasant, such as doubts, fears, or misconceptions.|The new evidence acted as a dispeller of the rumors.|近义词：remover, eliminator, erradicator|反义词：creator, instigator, promoter|词性: noun (plural)


Etymology:
From dispel + -er.  Dispel comes from the Old French *despeler, from des- "away" + peler "to push, drive."
