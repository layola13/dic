I'm:/aɪm/ | contraction | 我是 | Contraction of "I am".  It combines the subject pronoun "I" with the present tense form of the verb "to be". | I'm going to the store. 我要去商店。 | I am |  (No direct antonym for a contraction) | contraction

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
I'm happy to see you. 我很高兴见到你。
I'm a student at this university. 我是这所大学的学生。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
I'm watching a movie right now. 我现在正在看电影。
I'm learning how to cook Italian food. 我正在学习如何做意大利菜。

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
(I'm is not typically used in present perfect)

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
(I'm is not typically used in present perfect continuous)


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
(I'm is not used in the past tense.  Use "I was")


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
(I'm is not used in the past tense.  Use "I was")


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
(I'm is not used in the past tense. Use "I had been")


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
(I'm is not used in the past tense. Use "I had been")


一般将来时 (Simple Future):
(While grammatically correct, “I’m” is rarely used in future tense on its own.  Usually "I'll be..." or "I'm going to..." is used.)


将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
I'm meeting friends for dinner tomorrow evening. 明天晚上我和朋友们一起吃晚饭。
I'm going to be traveling next week. 我下周要去旅行。


将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
(I'm is not typically used in future perfect)


将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
(I'm is not typically used in future perfect continuous)


虚拟语气:
If I'm not mistaken, that's my car. 如果我没弄错的话，那是我的车。
I wish I'm on a beach right now. 我希望我现在在沙滩上。


被动语态:
(I'm is not used in the passive voice)


疑问句:
(I'm is rarely used at the beginning of a question, except in informal contexts like "I'm right, aren't I?")


否定句:
I'm not going to the party. 我不打算去参加聚会。
I'm not feeling well today. 我今天感觉不舒服。



PHRASE->
I'm good. = 我很好。
I'm sorry. = 我很抱歉。
I'm telling you. = 我跟你说/我告诉你。
I'm off. = 我走了。
I'm on it. = 我正在处理/ 我来做。


词源:  "I'm" is a contraction formed from "I am". "I" comes from Old English "ic", and "am" comes from Old English "eam", both tracing back to Proto-Germanic and ultimately Proto-Indo-European roots.
