neotectonics:/ˌnɪoʊtɛkˈtɒnɪks/ /ˌniːoʊtɛkˈtɑːnɪks/
n.
新构造运动；新构造学
The study of the relatively recent tectonic activity, typically from the Miocene epoch (about 23 million years ago) to the present.  It focuses on the movements and deformations of the Earth's crust that are currently taking place or have occurred in the recent geological past.
The Himalayas are a prime example of neotectonics at work. 喜马拉雅山脉是新构造运动的典型例子。
Neotectonic activity has shaped the landscape of the region. 新构造活动塑造了该地区的地貌。
tectonics, recent tectonics
ancient tectonics
noun
