dodecaphonism: /ˌdoʊˌdekəˈfoʊnɪzəm/ | n. | 十二音体系；十二音列音乐；十二音技法 | A method of musical composition devised by Arnold Schoenberg in which all twelve notes of the chromatic scale are treated as equally important and none is repeated within a specific section until all the others have been sounded. | Schoenberg's music is characterized by dodecaphonism.  勋伯格的音乐以十二音体系为特征。|  Serialism, twelve-tone music | Tonal music | noun
