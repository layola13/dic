harlequinade:/ˌhɑːrlɪkɪˈneɪd/ |n.|滑稽剧；闹剧；丑角表演|A play or entertainment in the style of a harlequinade, typically including mime, acrobatics, and slapstick comedy. | The circus featured a lively harlequinade that had the children roaring with laughter.  马戏团上演了一场热闹的滑稽剧，逗得孩子们哈哈大笑。|Synonyms: burlesque, farce, slapstick, buffoonery |Antonyms: tragedy, drama, seriousness|noun

harlequinade:/ˌhɑːrlɪkɪˈneɪd/ |adj.|滑稽的；闹剧的|Resembling or characteristic of a harlequinade; comical and boisterous. |  His harlequinade antics amused the crowd. 他滑稽的动作逗乐了人群。|Synonyms: comical, farcical, slapstick, ludicrous|Antonyms: serious, dramatic, tragic|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *harlequinade*, from Italian *arlequinata*, from *Arlecchino* "Harlequin," a stock comic character in Italian commedia dell'arte.
