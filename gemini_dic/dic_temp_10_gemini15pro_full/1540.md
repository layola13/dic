ached:/eɪkt/ | v. | 疼痛，酸痛 |  To feel a continuous dull pain. | My back ached after a long day of gardening.  我的背在长时间的园艺工作后感到疼痛。

My head ached terribly. 我的头疼得厉害。 | 近义词：hurt, pained, sore, throbbed | 反义词：soothed, relieved | 词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *acan* (to ache), of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *aken* and German *acken*.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
My head aches when I'm stressed. = 当我感到压力时，我的头就会痛。
His knee aches after running. = 他跑步后膝盖疼。

一般过去时:
My muscles ached after the workout. = 锻炼后我的肌肉酸痛。
Her feet ached from standing all day. =  站了一整天，她的脚很痛。

现在完成时:
My stomach has ached all morning. = 我的胃一上午都疼。
His back has ached ever since he lifted that heavy box. = 自从他搬了那个沉重的箱子后，他的背就一直疼。


过去完成时:
His legs had ached for days before he went to the doctor. =  他的腿痛了好几天才去看医生。
Her head had ached all night before she finally took some medicine. = 她的头疼了一整夜，最后才吃了一些药。

将来时:
If I walk that far, my feet will ache. = 如果我走那么远，我的脚会疼。
I'm sure my back will ache tomorrow after all this gardening. = 我相信明天做了这么多园艺工作后，我的背会痛。

虚拟语气:
If my head didn't ache, I would go to the party. = 如果我的头不痛，我就去参加聚会。
I wish my back wouldn't ache so much. = 我希望我的背不要这么痛。

被动语态: (Ached is rarely used in the passive voice.)

疑问句:
Does your stomach ache? = 你的胃痛吗？
Where does it ache? = 哪里痛？

否定句:
My head doesn't ache anymore. = 我的头不再痛了。
My legs didn't ache during the hike. =  徒步旅行时我的腿不痛。


PHRASE->
(Ached is not typically used in set phrases.  It usually stands alone or is modified by adverbs.)
My head ached badly. = 我的头很痛.
My muscles ached terribly. = 我的肌肉非常酸痛.
My feet ached constantly. = 我的脚一直痛.
My back ached dully. = 我的背隐隐作痛.
My stomach ached sharply. = 我的胃一阵剧痛.
