elite: /ɪˈliːt/ (US), /eɪˈliːt/ (UK)| n. adj.|精英；精英的|A select group that is superior in terms of ability or qualities to the rest of a group or society.|The elite of the entertainment industry attended the awards ceremony. 娱乐界的精英出席了颁奖典礼。|近义词: best, cream, pick, choice, upper crust|反义词: dregs, masses, riffraff|词性: 名词，形容词

elite: /ɪˈliːt/ (US), /eɪˈliːt/ (UK)| adj.|精英的|Superior in terms of ability or qualities to the rest of a group or society.|Only elite athletes qualify for the Olympics. 只有精英运动员才能参加奥运会。|近义词: select, exclusive, superior|反义词: ordinary, average, inferior|词性: 形容词

词源: From French élite, from Old French eslite "chosen," past participle of elire "elect," from Latin eligere "choose out, select," from ex- "out" + legere "to choose, gather, read."
