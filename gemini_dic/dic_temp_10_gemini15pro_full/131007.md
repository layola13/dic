nonattributively:/ˌnɒnəˈtrɪbjətɪvli/ | adv. | 非限定地，非修饰地 |  In a way that does not function as an attributive adjective.  An attributive adjective modifies a noun directly, appearing before the noun it describes. A nonattributive adjective, in contrast, appears elsewhere in the sentence, such as after a linking verb. |  "Present" can be used nonattributively, as in "He is present." | Synonyms: predicatively | Antonyms: attributively | Part of Speech: adverb
