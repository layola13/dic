|n.|强制性；强有力；有效性|The forcibleness of her argument convinced the jury. 她的论证有力地说服了陪审团。|forcefulness, potency, vigor|gentleness, weakness, feebleness|noun

Etymology:
From forcible + -ness.  Forcible comes from Old French *forcible (modern French forçable), from Late Latin forcibilis "strong, powerful," from Latin fortis "strong" + -ibilis, suffix forming adjectives meaning "able to be."  The suffix -ness forms nouns denoting a state or quality.
