medulla:/məˈdʌlə/ (US), /mɪˈdʌlə/ (UK)| n. | 骨髓；髓质；延髓 | The soft inner part of something, such as bone marrow or the pith of a plant stem.  Specifically, in anatomy, it refers to the inner region of an organ or tissue, especially the marrow of bone, the renal medulla, or the medulla oblongata (the lowest part of the brainstem). | The medulla oblongata controls vital functions such as breathing and heart rate. 延髓控制呼吸和心率等重要功能。|  Bone marrow produces blood cells. 骨髓产生血细胞。 | synonyms: marrow, pith, core, center | antonyms: cortex, surface, exterior | noun

medulla:/məˈdʌlə/ (US), /mɪˈdʌlə/ (UK)| n. | 骨髓；髓质；延髓 |  (Botany) The central, spongy tissue in the stems of some plants. | The medulla of the sunflower stem is soft and easily damaged. 向日葵茎的髓质柔软，容易受损。 | synonyms: pith, core | antonyms: cortex, rind | noun
