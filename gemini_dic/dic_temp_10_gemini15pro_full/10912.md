aramaism:/ˌærəˈmeɪɪzəm/ | n. | 阿拉姆语 | An ancient Semitic language of the Middle East, once widely spoken from Egypt to Afghanistan. It was the everyday language of Jesus and his disciples. |  Example 1: Aramaic was the lingua franca of the Near East for centuries.  阿拉姆语几个世纪以来一直是近东地区的通用语言。

Example 2:  Scholars are still deciphering ancient Aramaic inscriptions.  学者们仍在破译古代阿拉姆语铭文。 |Synonyms: Syriac (specifically, Eastern Aramaic), Chaldean (specifically, Northeastern Neo-Aramaic) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a language) | noun
