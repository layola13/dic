layoff:/ˈleɪˌɔːf/ (US), /ˈleɪɒf/ (UK)| n.|裁员，解雇；临时解雇；停工期|A layoff is a temporary or permanent discharge of a worker or workers.|The company announced layoffs affecting 100 employees. 公司宣布裁员100人。|Example:  Many workers face layoffs in the current economic climate. 当前经济环境下，许多工人面临失业。|Synonyms: redundancy, dismissal, discharge, furlough|Antonyms: hiring, recruitment|noun


lay off:/ˌleɪ ˈɔːf/ (US), /ˌleɪ ˈɒf/ (UK)| vt.|解雇，裁掉；停止；戒除|To lay someone off is to temporarily or permanently discharge them from their job.|The factory had to lay off a third of its workers. 工厂不得不解雇三分之一的工人。|Example: He was laid off from his job due to budget cuts. 由于预算削减，他被解雇了。|Synonyms: dismiss, discharge, fire, let go|Antonyms: hire, employ, recruit|verb
