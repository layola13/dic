non-interventionism:/ˌnɒnɪntəˈvɛnʃənɪzəm/ | n. |不干涉主义|The policy or doctrine of not intervening in the affairs of other nations, especially by force. |  Example:  The country's foreign policy was based on non-interventionism.  这个国家的外交政策基于不干涉主义。| Example:  Some historians argue that non-interventionism allowed the conflict to escalate. 一些历史学家认为不干涉主义导致了冲突的升级。| 近义词:neutrality, noninterference | 反义词: interventionism, interference | 词性: noun

non-interventionist:/ˌnɒnɪntəˈvɛnʃənɪst/ | n. adj. |不干涉主义者；不干涉主义的|A person who advocates or believes in non-interventionism; relating to or characterized by non-interventionism. | Example: He was a staunch non-interventionist and opposed the war. 他是一个坚定的不干涉主义者，反对这场战争。| Example:  The senator's non-interventionist stance was unpopular with many voters.  参议员的不干涉主义立场不受许多选民的欢迎。| 近义词: isolationist | 反义词: interventionist | 词性: noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + interventionism.  "Interventionism" comes from the French word "intervention"  meaning the act or instance of intervening.  "Intervening" comes from the Latin "intervenire" meaning "to come between."
