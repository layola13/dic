disenfranchisement:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
n.
剥夺权利；褫夺公权
The act of depriving someone of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.
Many people were concerned about the disenfranchisement of minority voters. 很多人担心少数族裔选民的权利被剥夺。
disenfranchisement:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
n.
剥夺权利；丧失权利
The state of being deprived of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.
The disenfranchisement of ex-offenders is a controversial issue.  对有前科的人剥夺公民权是一个有争议的问题。
disenfranchisement:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
n.
剥夺权利
Deprivation of power or influence.
The group felt a sense of disenfranchisement when their concerns were ignored. 当他们的担忧被忽视时，这个群体感到了一种被剥夺权利的感觉。


近义词:deprivation, disqualification, exclusion
反义词:enfranchisement, empowerment
