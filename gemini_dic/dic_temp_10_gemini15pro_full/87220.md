hermit:/ˈhɜːmɪt/ |n.|隐士；遁世者|A person who lives in solitude, especially for religious reasons. |The hermit lived in a cave in the mountains. 这位隐士住在山里的一个洞穴里。|Example: He lived as a hermit for many years, seeking spiritual enlightenment. 他过着隐士般的生活很多年，寻求精神上的启迪。|Synonyms: recluse, solitary, anchorite|Antonyms: socialite, extrovert|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *ermite*, from Late Latin *eremita*, from Greek *erēmítēs* "desert-dweller," from *erēmos* "desert, lonely place," from *erēmos* "lonely, desolate."
