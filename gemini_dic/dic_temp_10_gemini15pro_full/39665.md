conservator:/kənˈsɜːrvətər/ | n. | 保护者，管理者，馆长 | A person responsible for the preservation and management of something, especially a museum, art collection, or natural resource. | The conservator carefully restored the ancient painting.  这位管理员小心翼翼地修复了这幅古画。 | synonyms: curator, guardian, keeper, custodian | antonyms: destroyer, vandal | noun


conservatorial: /kənˌsɜːrvəˈtɔːriəl/ | adj. | 保护的，管理的，馆长的 | Relating to or characteristic of a conservator or their work. |  She has a conservatorial approach to art history. 她对艺术史采取了一种保护性的研究方法。 | synonyms: protective, preservative, curatorial | antonyms: destructive, neglectful | adjective


conservatorship: /kənˈsɜːrvətərˌʃɪp/ | n. | 保护，管理，监护 | The position or office of a conservator; the state of being under the care of a conservator. | The museum is under the conservatorship of the city council.  这家博物馆由市议会监管。| synonyms: guardianship, custodianship, curatorship | antonyms: neglect, abandonment | noun
