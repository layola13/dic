multiflagellated:/ˌmʌltiˈflædʒəˌleɪtɪd/ | adj. | 多鞭毛的 | Having multiple flagella (whip-like appendages used for locomotion). | Example: Multiflagellated bacteria are common in aquatic environments. 多鞭毛细菌在水生环境中很常见。 | Example: The multiflagellated protozoan moved swiftly through the water.  这种多鞭毛原生动物在水中快速移动。 | Synonym: polyflagellate | Antonym: uniflagellate, aflagellate | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From multi- (many) + flagellum (whip) + -ated (having).  "Multi-" comes from Latin "multus" meaning "much" or "many". "Flagellum" comes from Latin "flagellum" meaning "whip" or "scourge". "-ated" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "having" or "provided with".
