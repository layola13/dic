counter-salient:/ˈkaʊntər ˈseɪliənt/ |n.|突出部；反突出部|A counter-salient is a bulge or outward projection in a military line or fortification, typically surrounded on three sides by enemy territory. It is the opposite of a salient, which is an inward projection into enemy territory.|The enemy attacked the counter-salient with heavy artillery. 敌人用重型火炮攻击了突出部。|The general ordered his troops to defend the counter-salient at all costs. 将军命令他的部队不惜一切代价保卫突出部。|synonyms: bulge, projection, protrusion|antonyms: salient, re-entrant, indentation|noun
