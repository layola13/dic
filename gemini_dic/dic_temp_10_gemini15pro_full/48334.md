decriminalising: /ˌdiːˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪzɪŋ/
decriminalising| v.| 非刑事化，使合法化 |The act of making something that was previously a crime legal or no longer subject to criminal penalties. |Many countries are considering decriminalising the possession of small amounts of marijuana. 许多国家正在考虑将持有少量大麻非刑事化。|近义词：legalizing, legitimizing|反义词：criminalizing, prohibiting|词性: 动名词 (present participle)

decriminalise: /ˌdiːˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪz/
decriminalise| vt.| 非刑事化，使合法化 |To make something that was previously a crime legal or no longer subject to criminal penalties. |Portugal decriminalised all drugs in 2001. 葡萄牙在2001年将所有毒品非刑事化。|近义词：legalize, legitimize|反义词：criminalize, prohibit|词性: 动词

decriminalised: /ˌdiːˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪzd/
decriminalized| adj.| 非刑事化的 |Something that is no longer considered a crime. |Possession of small amounts of cannabis has been decriminalised in some states. 在一些州，持有少量大麻已被非刑事化。|近义词：legal, legitimate|反义词：criminal, prohibited|词性: 形容词 (past participle)


词源:  de- (prefix meaning "reverse" or "remove") + criminalize.  The word "criminalize" comes from "criminal" + "-ize" (suffix meaning "to make").  Ultimately derived from Latin "crimin-" (accusation, crime).
