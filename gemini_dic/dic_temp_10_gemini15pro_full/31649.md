chattel:/ˈtʃætəl/| n.|动产，奴隶|A chattel is an item of personal property which is movable, as distinct from real property (land or buildings).  It can also refer to a slave, though this usage is now archaic and offensive.|The farmer sold all his chattels, including his livestock and farming equipment. 农民卖掉了他的所有动产，包括牲畜和农具。| The slaves were considered mere chattels. 奴隶被视为单纯的动产。 |synonyms: property, possession, goods, effects, belongings (for inanimate objects); slave, bondsman, bondswoman (archaic and offensive) |antonyms: real estate, land, property (for inanimate objects); freeman, freewoman (archaic) |noun
