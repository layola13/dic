词源： "mowyer" comes from the verb "mow," meaning to cut down grass.  "Mow" has Old English origins (mawan), related to the Latin "metere" (to reap, harvest). The "-yer" suffix denotes a person who performs the action of the verb, similar to "lawyer" or "sawyer."
