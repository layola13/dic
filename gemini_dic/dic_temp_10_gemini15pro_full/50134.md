denudation:/ˌdiːnjuːˈdeɪʃən/ /ˌdɛnjuˈdeɪʃən/
n.
剥蚀；剥夺；脱衣服
the process of stripping something of its covering, especially land of vegetation; the laying bare of rock or the process by which the earth is laid bare by the removal of its superficial parts.

The denudation of the forest caused severe soil erosion.
森林的剥蚀造成了严重的水土流失。

The denudation of the mountainside revealed layers of ancient rock.
山坡的剥蚀揭示了古代岩石的层层叠叠。


近义词：erosion, stripping, deforestation, uncovering
反义词：afforestation, reforestation, covering, accretion

词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin dēnūdātiō, from Latin dēnūdātus, past participle of dēnūdāre (“to lay bare”), from dē (“from, off, down”) + nūdāre (“to strip bare”), from nūdus (“naked”).
