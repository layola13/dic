eupione:/ˈjuːpiːoʊn/ | n. |尤皮油，石蜡油|A colorless, odorless, oily liquid hydrocarbon, C20H42, extracted from the heavier portions of petroleum and used as a lubricant and illuminant.| Eupione was used in lamps before the advent of electricity. 尤皮油在电灯出现之前用于油灯。|Synonyms: paraffin oil, mineral oil|Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, but could consider substances like water or alcohol which are not lubricants or illuminants)|noun
