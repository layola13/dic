nobble:/ˈnɒbəl/ (British), /ˈnɑːbəl/ (American)| vt.|暗中破坏，搞垮；买通，收买；偷偷取得|To disable or incapacitate, especially a horse in a race, by unfair means; to get hold of something secretly or deceptively.|They nobbled the favourite to win the race. 他们暗中破坏了最有希望赢得比赛的马。|Synonyms: sabotage, disable, thwart, fix, bribe, subvert|Antonyms: aid, assist, help, support|verb

nobble:/ˈnɒbəl/ (British), /ˈnɑːbəl/ (American)| n.|暗中破坏，搞垮；买通，收买；偷偷取得的行为|The act of disabling or incapacitating, especially a horse in a race, by unfair means; the act of getting hold of something secretly or deceptively.|The police suspected a nobble in the horse race. 警方怀疑赛马比赛中有人搞鬼。|Synonyms: sabotage, bribery, subversion|Antonyms: assistance, support|noun
