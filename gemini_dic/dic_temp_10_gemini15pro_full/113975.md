magian:/ˈmeɪdʒən/| n. adj.|魔术师，法师；有魔力的|A magian is a person skilled in magic, often associated with ancient Persia. As an adjective, it describes something related to magi or magic. |例句：The magian performed a dazzling feat of illusion.  魔术师表演了一个令人眼花缭乱的幻象。

|例句：The magian powers of the sorcerer were legendary.  巫师的魔法力量是传奇性的。|近义词：magician, sorcerer, wizard|反义词:mundane, ordinary|词性:noun, adjective
Etymology->
From Late Latin *magianus, from Greek μάγος (mágos, “magus, sorcerer”). Ultimately from Old Persian *maguš (whence also Avestan 𐬨𐬀𐬔𐬎𐬱𐬙𐬀‎ (maguš)).
