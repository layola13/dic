distractable:/dɪˈstræktəbl/ | adj. | 易分心的，易分散注意力的 | Easily diverted from one's original focus of attention; easily distracted. |  Example 1: He's very distractable and finds it hard to concentrate on his studies. 他很容易分心，很难集中精力学习。

Example 2: The open office environment proved too distractable for her to get any real work done.  开放式办公环境对她来说太容易分心了，以至于无法完成任何实际工作。| 近义词: distractible, easily distracted, inattentive, unfocused | 反义词: focused, attentive, concentrated, undistracted | 词性: 形容词

distracted:/dɪˈstræktɪd/ | adj. | 心烦意乱的，注意力分散的 | Having one's attention diverted; unable to concentrate. | Example 1: She seemed distracted and didn't hear what I said. 她似乎心不在焉，没听到我说的话。

Example 2:  He drove home in a distracted state, worried about his sick child. 他心烦意乱地开车回家，担心他生病的孩子。 | 近义词: preoccupied, abstracted, inattentive | 反义词: attentive, focused, concentrated | 词性: 形容词

distract:/dɪˈstrækt/ | v. | 使分心，分散(注意力) | Prevent (someone) from giving full attention to something. | Example 1:  The noise from the street distracted me from my reading. 街上的噪音使我无法专心读书。

Example 2: Don't distract her while she's driving. 她开车时别打扰她。| 近义词: divert, sidetrack, disturb | 反义词: focus, concentrate | 词性: 动词

distraction:/dɪˈstrækʃn/ | n. | 分心的事物，娱乐 | A thing that prevents someone from giving full attention to something else. | Example 1:  The constant distractions of city life made it hard to relax.  都市生活中不断的干扰让人很难放松。

Example 2:  Television can be a distraction from your studies.  电视会分散你学习的注意力。| 近义词: diversion, interruption, disturbance | 反义词: focus, concentration | 词性: 名词
