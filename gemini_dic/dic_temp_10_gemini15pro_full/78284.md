germest:/ˈdʒɜːrmɪst/ |n.|细菌学家; 微生物学家|A scientist who studies germs (bacteria, viruses, etc.). |Example: The germest discovered a new strain of bacteria. 细菌学家发现了一种新的细菌菌株。
Example: The germest is studying the spread of infectious diseases.  细菌学家正在研究传染病的传播。|Synonym: microbiologist, bacteriologist |Antonym: N/A |noun

germicide:/ˈdʒɜːrmɪsaɪd/ |n. |杀菌剂|A substance that kills germs (bacteria, viruses, etc.). |Example:  We used a germicide to clean the wound. 我们用杀菌剂清洗伤口。
Example: Many household cleaners contain germicides. 许多家用清洁剂含有杀菌剂。|Synonym: disinfectant, antiseptic |Antonym: N/A |noun

germinal:/ˈdʒɜːrmɪnəl/ |adj.|胚芽的; 初期的; 萌芽期的|Relating to a germ or seed; in the earliest stage of development. |Example: The germinal stage of an idea is crucial for its future success. 一个想法的萌芽阶段对其未来的成功至关重要。
Example: The germinal cells of a plant develop into seeds. 植物的胚芽细胞发育成种子。|Synonym: nascent, embryonic, incipient |Antonym: mature, developed |adjective

germinate:/ˈdʒɜːrmɪneɪt/ |v. |发芽; 萌芽; 开始发展|To begin to grow or develop; to sprout. |Example: The seeds began to germinate after a few days. 几天后种子开始发芽。
Example: Her idea germinated into a successful business. 她的想法发展成了一项成功的业务。|Synonym: sprout, bud, develop |Antonym: wither, die |verb

germ:/dʒɜːrm/ |n.|细菌; 病菌; 微生物;  萌芽; 起源|A microorganism, especially one that causes disease; a beginning or origin. |Example: Wash your hands to avoid spreading germs. 洗手以避免传播细菌。
Example: The germ of an idea began to form in his mind.  一个想法的萌芽开始在他的脑海中形成。|Synonym: microbe, bacterium, virus, seed, origin |Antonym: N/A |noun
