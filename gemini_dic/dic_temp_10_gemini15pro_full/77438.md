gawkier: /ˈɡɔːkiər/ (US), /ˈɡɔːkɪə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更笨拙的，更尴尬的|Comparative form of gawky: more gawky.  Describes someone who is more socially awkward, clumsy, or ungainly than someone else.|He became even gawkier as a teenager, bumping into things and tripping over his own feet. 他十几岁时变得更加笨拙，撞到东西，还被自己的脚绊倒。|Example:  His dancing was gawkier than a newborn giraffe. 他的舞姿比刚出生的长颈鹿还要笨拙。|Synonyms: clumsier, more awkward, more ungainly, more ungraceful|Antonyms: more graceful, more elegant, more coordinated, smoother|词性: 形容词

gawk: /ɡɔːk/| n. vi.|呆子，傻瓜；盯着看|1.(n.) a clumsy or awkward person. 2.(v.) to stare openly and stupidly.|He gawked at the celebrity like a lovesick teenager. 他像一个害了相思病的少年一样盯着那个名人。|Synonyms: (n.) klutz, oaf, lummox; (v.) stare, gape, ogle|Antonyms: (n.) sophisticate, graceful person; (v.) glance, avert one's eyes|词性: 名词，动词
