matrix's:/ˈmeɪtrɪksɪz/| n. |矩阵的|The possessive form of "matrix," indicating ownership or association. It refers to something belonging to or associated with a matrix.|The matrix's elements are all real numbers. 矩阵的元素都是实数。|近义词: matrix (in some contexts when referring to a specific, implied matrix) |反义词: None (possessive forms don't typically have antonyms)|词性:Possessive noun


Etymology:
From Latin "matrix" (womb, source, list), from "mater" (mother). The mathematical sense developed later, referring to a rectangular array of numbers. The 's is the standard English possessive suffix.
