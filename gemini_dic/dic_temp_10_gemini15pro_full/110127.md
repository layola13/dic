lidge:/lɪdʒ/ | n. | 壁架，岩架 | A narrow horizontal shelf or ledge, especially one that projects from a cliff face. |  The climber rested on a small lidge before continuing his ascent. 登山者在继续攀登之前在一个小壁架上休息。| Synonyms: shelf, ledge, projection | Antonyms: chasm, abyss | noun

Etymology:  From Middle English ligge, legge (“ledge, shelf”), from Old Norse *liggja (“something laid down, shelf”), from leggja (“to lay, to lie”), from Proto-Germanic *lagjaną (“to lay, to put”), from Proto-Indo-European *legh- (“to lay, to lie down”).  Cognate with Swedish lägga (“to lay, to put”), Danish lægge (“to lay, to put”), Icelandic leggja (“to lay, to put”).
