enarch:/ˈiːnɑːrk/ | n. |地方行政长官|A prefect or governor of a province, especially in ancient Greece or the Byzantine Empire.  A high-ranking official in the French civil service, especially a graduate of the École Nationale d'Administration. |例句：The enarch was responsible for collecting taxes in his province.  这位地方行政长官负责在他所在的省份征税。

例句：Graduates of the ENA, known as enarchs, often hold high positions in French government and industry.  从法国国立行政学院毕业的被称为“地方行政长官”的人，通常在法国政府和工业界担任要职。|近义词：prefect, governor, administrator, official |反义词: subordinate, civilian (in some contexts) |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *énarque*, from Ancient Greek ἔναρχος (*énarchos*) "in office, in command," from ἐν (*en*) "in" + ἄρχω (*archō*) "to rule."
