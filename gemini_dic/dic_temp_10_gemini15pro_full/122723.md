mohock:/ˈmoʊhɔːk/| n.|莫霍克人，流氓，小混混|A member of a gang of violent young men, especially in 18th-century London, noted for acts of vandalism and rowdy behavior.  Originally referring to a Native American tribe, the term later became associated with street gangs. |例句：The mohocks terrorized the streets of London at night.  莫霍克族在夜间恐吓伦敦的街道。

例句：He was known as a mohock for his wild and destructive behavior. 他因其狂野和破坏性的行为而被称为莫霍克人。|近义词：ruffian, hooligan, thug, vandal|反义词:law-abiding citizen|词性:noun
