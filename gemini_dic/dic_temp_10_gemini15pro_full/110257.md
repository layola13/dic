life-threatening:/ˈlaɪfˌθretnɪŋ/| adj.|危及生命的，威胁生命的|Potentially causing death; extremely dangerous.|The patient had a life-threatening illness. 患者患有危及生命的疾病。|The snake's venom is life-threatening. 这条蛇的毒液足以致命。|近义词：fatal, mortal, perilous, dangerous|反义词：safe, harmless, benign|词性:形容词
词源:  Compound of 'life' and 'threatening'. 'Threatening' comes from the verb 'threaten', which has roots in Old English and Old Norse, ultimately meaning "to force, compel, or constrain."
