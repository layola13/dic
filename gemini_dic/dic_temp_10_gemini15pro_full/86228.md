heathlike:/ˈhiːθlaɪk/
adjective
像石楠荒原的; 荒凉的
Resembling or characteristic of a heath; desolate.
The landscape was heathlike, with low-growing shrubs and rocky terrain.  这片景色像石楠荒原，长着低矮的灌木和岩石地形。
The windswept, heathlike moors stretched for miles.  被风吹过的、像石楠荒原的荒野绵延数英里。
近义词: barren, desolate, moorland
反义词: fertile, lush, verdant
词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From heath + -like.  Heath (meaning an area of open uncultivated land) comes from Old English *hǣþ.  The suffix -like is a common English suffix used to form adjectives indicating resemblance.
