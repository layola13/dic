aasar:/ˈɑːsɑːr/| n.|烦恼，怨恨; (尤指印度教)宗教的愤怒|A feeling of annoyance or resentment, especially religious indignation (Hinduism).|He felt aasar at being ignored. 他因被忽视而感到恼火。|Synonyms: annoyance, resentment, irritation, vexation, pique, displeasure, indignation, umbrage|Antonyms: pleasure, delight, happiness, contentment|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Hindi and Urdu  آثار (āsār, “traces, vestiges, effects”) from Arabic آثار (ʾāṯār, “traces, vestiges”).  Ultimately from the root  أَثَرَ ('aṯara, “to leave a trace”).
