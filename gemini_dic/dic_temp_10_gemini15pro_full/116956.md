mazourka: /məˈzɜːrkə/ /məˈzʊərkə/ | n. | 马祖卡舞；马祖卡舞曲 | A lively Polish folk dance in triple time, characterized by a strong accent on the second or third beat.  It also refers to the music for this dance. |  Example 1: The orchestra played a lively mazourka, and the dancers whirled around the floor.  管弦乐队演奏了一曲欢快的马祖卡舞曲，舞者们在舞池中旋转。

Example 2:  She learned to dance the mazourka from her grandmother. 她跟祖母学会了跳马祖卡舞。| 近义词: dance, polka, waltz | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for a specific dance) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Polish *mazurek*, meaning "inhabitant of Mazovia". Mazovia is a region in central Poland.
