disjection:/dɪsˈdʒɛkʃən/
n.
沮丧，灰心，颓丧
The news of his failure sent him into a state of disjection.  他失败的消息使他陷入了沮丧之中。
He felt a deep sense of disjection after losing his job. 失业后，他感到深深的沮丧。
近义词: dejection, despondency, depression, discouragement
反义词: elation, joy, happiness, cheerfulness


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *disjectiōn*, from Latin disjectus, past participle of disicere (“to scatter, disperse”), from dis- (“apart, asunder”) + jacere (“to throw”).
