Fatimids: /ˈfætɪmɪdz/ | n. | 法蒂玛王朝 |  A member of a Muslim dynasty that traced its descent from Fatima, daughter of Muhammad, and that built an empire in North Africa, Egypt, and Syria between the 10th and 12th centuries. | The Fatimids established Cairo as their capital. 法蒂玛王朝定都开罗。| The Fatimids were known for their patronage of the arts and sciences. 法蒂玛王朝以其对艺术和科学的赞助而闻名。| synonyms:  Fatimid caliphate, Fatimid dynasty | antonyms: N/A | noun
