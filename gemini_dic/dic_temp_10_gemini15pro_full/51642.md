deviancy:/ˈdiːviənsi/ | n. | 偏差，偏离，越轨行为 | The act of departing from an accepted standard. | His deviancy from the company’s policy resulted in his dismissal. 他偏离公司政策的行为导致他被解雇。 |  His deviancy shocked the conservative community. 他的越轨行为震惊了保守的社区。 | 近义词: aberration, abnormality, perversion, nonconformity | 反义词: conformity, normalcy, standard | 词性: 名词

deviant:/ˈdiːviənt/ | adj. n. | 偏离的，异常的；偏差者 | Departing from an accepted standard. |  The study focused on deviant behavior in adolescents. 这项研究的重点是青少年的偏差行为。 | He was considered a deviant by his peers. 他被同龄人视为异类。 | 近义词: abnormal, atypical, irregular, unconventional | 反义词: normal, typical, conventional, standard  | 词性: 形容词，名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *deviantia, from devians, present participle of deviare "to turn aside," from de- "from, away" + via "way, road."
