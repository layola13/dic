abetting: /əˈbɛtɪŋ/
verb
教唆；怂恿；煽动
The act of encouraging or assisting someone to do something wrong or illegal.
He was charged with aiding and abetting the robbers. 他被指控协助和教唆抢劫犯。

She was accused of abetting the escape of a prisoner. 她被指控教唆囚犯越狱。

近义词:  encouraging, assisting, aiding, inciting, instigating
反义词:  deterring, discouraging, hindering, preventing
词性:  动名词 (gerund) -  abetting 是动词abet的动名词形式

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French abeter "to bait, incite," from a- "to" + beter "to bait."  The original sense was of urging dogs to fight.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
His behavior abets violence. = 他的行为助长了暴力。
The company's policy abets tax evasion. = 该公司的政策助长了逃税。


一般过去时:
He abetted the thief in his escape. = 他帮助小偷逃跑。
The lawyer abetted his client in committing perjury. = 律师教唆他的客户犯伪证罪。


现在完成时:
The government has been abetting terrorism by funding certain groups. = 政府一直通过资助某些团体来纵容恐怖主义。
He has been abetting his friend's gambling addiction. = 他一直纵容他朋友的赌瘾。


过去完成时:
She had been abetting the fraud for years before she was caught. = 她参与诈骗多年才被抓获。
They had abetted the rebels in their fight against the government. = 他们曾教唆叛军对抗政府。


将来时:
Anyone caught abetting the crime will be prosecuted. = 任何被发现教唆犯罪的人都会被起诉。
The new law will make it more difficult to abet money laundering. = 新法律将使洗钱更加困难。


虚拟语气:
If I were to abet such behavior, I would be complicit in the crime. = 如果我教唆这种行为，我将成为犯罪的同谋。
I wish I hadn't abetted his bad habits. = 我希望我没有纵容他的坏习惯。


被动语态:
The crime was abetted by several individuals. = 这起犯罪是由几个人教唆的。
The escape was abetted by a prison guard. =  越狱是由一名狱警协助的。


疑问句:
Is he abetting the enemy? = 他是在帮助敌人吗？
Are they abetting the spread of misinformation? = 他们在煽动传播错误信息吗？


否定句:
I am not abetting his criminal activities. = 我没有教唆他的犯罪活动。
She did not abet the theft. = 她没有教唆盗窃。
