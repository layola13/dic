hyper-inflation:/ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪʃn/ | n. |恶性通货膨胀|A period of rapid and excessive price increases in an economy, typically exceeding 50% per month.|例句：The country experienced a period of hyper-inflation after the war. 战后该国经历了一段恶性通货膨胀时期。

|例句：Hyper-inflation can destroy an economy. 恶性通货膨胀可以摧毁一个经济体。|近义词：runaway inflation, galloping inflation|反义词: deflation, disinflation|词性: noun


hyper-inflation:/ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪʃn/ | n. |超级通货膨胀|A period of very rapid and excessive price increases in an economy.|例句：Hyper-inflation made everyday goods unaffordable.  超级通货膨胀使得日常用品变得难以负担。

|例句：The government struggled to control hyper-inflation. 政府努力控制恶性通货膨胀。|近义词：excessive inflation, uncontrolled inflation|反义词: price stability, deflation|词性: noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- +‎ inflation,  "hyper-" comes from Greek "ὑπέρ (hupér)" meaning "over, above, beyond". "Inflation" comes from Late Latin "inflātiōn-" meaning "a swelling, inflation,"  from Latin "inflāre" meaning "to blow into, inflate."
