pan-Turanian:/ˌpæn tjʊˈreɪniən/
adjective
泛突厥的，泛图兰的
Relating to or denoting a hypothetical family of languages comprising Turkic, Mongolian, Tungusic, Korean, and Japanese, or a hypothetical racial group speaking these languages.  Considered outdated and inaccurate by modern linguists and anthropologists.
The pan-Turanian theory, popular in the 19th century,  posited a common origin for a wide range of Asian languages.  泛突厥理论在19世纪很流行，它假设了广泛的亚洲语言的共同起源。
Some 19th-century scholars promoted the idea of a pan-Turanian civilization stretching across Eurasia. 一些19世纪的学者提倡了一个横跨欧亚大陆的泛突厥文明的观点。
Synonym: (Outdated and inaccurate) Altaic
Antonym:  None (The concept itself is now largely rejected)
词性: adjective
