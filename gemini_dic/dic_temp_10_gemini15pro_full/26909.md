calamary:/ˈkæləməri/ (US) /ˈkæləməːri/ (UK)| n.|鱿鱼；乌贼|A cephalopod mollusk with ten tentacles, of which two are longer than the others, and a long, tapered body. Calamari are often eaten as food, especially in Mediterranean cuisine.|We had fried calamari for appetizer. 我们前菜吃了炸鱿鱼。|近义词：squid, cuttlefish|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider land animals as a broad opposite)|noun

calamary:/ˈkæləməri/ (US) /ˈkæləməːri/ (UK)| n.|（尤指作为食物的）鱿鱼肉|The flesh of a calamary, eaten as food.|The calamari was tender and delicious. 鱿鱼肉鲜嫩可口。|近义词：squid meat|反义词: (No direct antonym)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *calamaro*, meaning "ink pot," from Late Latin *calamarium*, also meaning "ink pot," due to the squid's ink sac. This ultimately derives from Latin *calamus*, meaning "reed" or "pen," as reeds were used for writing.
