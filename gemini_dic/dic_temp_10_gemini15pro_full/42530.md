countermanding:/ˌkaʊntərˈmændɪŋ/ | adj. |  撤销的，取消的 |  Revoking or cancelling a previous command or order. | The general issued a countermanding order, stopping the troops from advancing. 将军发布了撤销命令，阻止部队前进。 |  revoking, rescinding, canceling, overriding | confirming, upholding, ratifying | adjective

countermand:/ˌkaʊntərˈmænd/ | v. | 撤销，取消 | To revoke or cancel (a previous command or order). |  The king countermanded his earlier decree. 国王撤销了他早先的法令. | revoke, rescind, cancel, override | confirm, uphold, ratify | verb

countermand:/ˈkaʊntərˌmænd/ | n. | 撤销命令，取消命令 | An order that revokes or cancels a previous command. | The countermand arrived just in time to prevent the disaster.  取消命令及时到达，阻止了灾难的发生。 | revocation, cancellation, repeal | confirmation, ratification | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *contremander*, from contre- (“against”) + mander (“to order”). Ultimately from Latin *contramandare*, from contra (“against”) + mandare (“to entrust, order”).
