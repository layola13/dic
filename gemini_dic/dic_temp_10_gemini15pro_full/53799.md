ETYMOLOGY->
From the French *déconcerter*, from *dé-* (expressing negation) + *concerter* (to arrange, agree upon), ultimately from Latin *concertare* (to contend, dispute).  The shift in meaning from "to upset plans" to "to unsettle emotionally" occurred in English.
