emblazoners:/ɪmˈbleɪzənərz/
|n.|纹章绘制者，纹章装饰者
|People who emblazon, or adorn with heraldic devices. Those skilled in creating and applying coats of arms and other insignia.|
|The emblazoners worked diligently to create the intricate design for the royal banner.|纹章绘制者们辛勤工作，为皇家旗帜创作了复杂的设计。

|The family hired emblazoners to decorate their ancestral home with their newly granted coat of arms.|这家人聘请了纹章绘制者用他们新获得的盾徽来装饰他们的祖屋。
|近义词:heraldists, decorators|
|反义词:None|
|词性:Noun (plural)|

emblazon:/ɪmˈbleɪzən/
|v.|（以纹章）装饰；颂扬；使鲜明
|1. To adorn or decorate with heraldic devices, such as a coat of arms. 2. To celebrate or extol publicly. 3. To make vivid or prominent.|
|They emblazoned the shield with a golden lion.|他们在盾牌上饰以金色的狮子。

|His name was emblazoned across the headlines.|他的名字赫然出现在头条新闻上。
|近义词: adorn, decorate, celebrate, extol, highlight|
|反义词:obscure, conceal|
|词性:Verb|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *emblazoner (modern French blasonner), from blason "shield, coat of arms," from Frankish *blaso "a blowing, blast (of a horn)," related to Old High German blāsan "to blow."  The original sense was "to blow a trumpet to announce the arrival of a knight and describe his coat of arms."
