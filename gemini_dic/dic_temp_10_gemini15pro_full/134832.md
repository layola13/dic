nonmischievousness:/ˌnɒnmɪsˈtʃiːvəsnəs/
n.
无恶意；不淘气；温顺
lack of mischievousness; the state of being not mischievous; innocence.
Her nonmischievousness was evident in her gentle demeanor. 她的温顺从她温柔的举止中显而易见。
近义词: innocence, harmlessness, good behavior, docility
反义词: mischievousness, naughtiness, malice, bad behavior
词性: noun

Etymology:
From non- (not) + mischievous + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing a state or quality).  "Mischievous" comes from Old French "meschever" (to come to grief, meet with misfortune), from "meschief" (misfortune, bad luck), ultimately from Latin "minus" (less) and "capum" (head).
