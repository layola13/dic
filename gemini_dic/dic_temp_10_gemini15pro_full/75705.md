Etymology:
full-bloodedness is derived from "full-blooded" + "-ness".
"Full-blooded" comes from "full" + "blooded".
"Full" means complete or total.
"Blooded" refers to having a particular type of blood or ancestry.
"-ness" is a suffix that forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
