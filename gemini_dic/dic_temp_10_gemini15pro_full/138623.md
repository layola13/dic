nuclein:/ˈnjuːkliɪn/ | n. | 核素，核蛋白 | A complex organic substance present in the nuclei of cells, now known to be deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) or a related substance. |  Friedrich Miescher discovered nuclein in 1869.  弗里德里希·米歇尔在1869年发现了核素。 |  The nuclein extracted from the cells contained phosphorus. 从细胞中提取的核素含有磷。 | synonyms: DNA, chromatin | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical substance like nuclein exists) | noun
