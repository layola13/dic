intangibilities:/ɪnˌtæn(d)ʒɪˈbɪlɪtiz/
|n.|无形资产；无形的东西|qualities that cannot be physically touched, but are still valuable, such as reputation, goodwill, and employee morale.|The company's intangible assets, such as its brand recognition and customer loyalty, are crucial to its success. 该公司的无形资产，例如品牌认知度和客户忠诚度，对其成功至关重要。|synonyms: non-physical assets, abstract assets|antonyms: tangible assets, physical assets|noun
intangibility:/ɪnˌtæn(d)ʒɪˈbɪlɪti/
|n.|无形性；难以理解|the state of being intangible; difficult to grasp or define.|The intangibility of certain services makes it hard to assess their value.某些服务的无形性使得难以评估其价值。|synonyms: impalpability, abstractness|antonyms: tangibility, concreteness|noun
intangible:/ɪnˈtæn(d)ʒɪbəl/
|adj.|无形的；难以捉摸的|unable to be touched or grasped; not having physical presence.|The benefits of a good education are often intangible, but they are very real. 良好教育的益处通常是无形的，但它们非常真实。|synonyms: impalpable, abstract, ethereal|antonyms: tangible, concrete, physical|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *intangibilis, from in- "not" + tangibilis "tangible," from tangere "to touch."
