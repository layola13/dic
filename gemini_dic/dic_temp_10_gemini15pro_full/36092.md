coemption:/koʊˈɛmpʃən/
n.
共同购买; 共买权
The act or right of purchasing the whole of a thing, especially the right of a husband in Roman law to purchase his wife's property.
In ancient Rome, coemption was a form of marriage involving a symbolic sale of the bride to the groom.  在古罗马，共买是一种婚姻形式，包括象征性地将新娘卖给新郎。
Coemption gave the husband complete control over his wife's property. 共买权赋予丈夫对其妻子财产的完全控制权。
Synonyms: acquisition, purchase
Antonyms: sale, disposal
