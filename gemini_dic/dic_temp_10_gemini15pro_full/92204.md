hypersuspiciously:/ˌhaɪpər səˈspɪʃəsli/ | adv. | 过分怀疑地，极度猜疑地 |  Characterized by excessive suspicion; in a way that shows extreme distrust.  | He looked at her hypersuspiciously, convinced she was hiding something. 他极其怀疑地看着她，确信她在隐瞒什么。 | hypersuspicious, distrustful, wary, skeptical | trusting, gullible, naive | adverb

Etymology:
From "hyper-" (Greek: ὑπέρ, hupér, "over, above") + "suspicious" (from Latin suspiciosus "mistrustful," from suspicere "to look up at, admire, mistrust," from sub "up from below" + specere "to look at").
