inchamber:/ɪnˈtʃeɪmbər/ | adj. |  在议院内; 秘密地; 私下地 | Done or held privately or secretly, as by judges or legislators in private chambers.  |  The judge inchambered the jury to deliberate. 法官让陪审团进入密室进行商议。 | 近义词: private, secret, confidential, closed-door | 反义词: public, open | 词性: 形容词

词源: From "in" + "chamber." "Chamber" originally referred to a room, especially a private room or bedroom. The prefix "in" adds the sense of location or action within the chamber.
