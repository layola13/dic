call-in-sick:/ˌkɔːl ɪn ˈsɪk/ | adj. | 打电话请病假 | To telephone one's employer to inform them that one is too ill to work. |  Example: I had to call in sick this morning because I woke up with a terrible headache. 我今天早上不得不打电话请病假，因为我醒来时头痛得厉害。| 近义词: report sick, phone in sick | 反义词: come to work, go to work | 词性: Adjective (used predicatively)


call-in-sick:/ˌkɔːl ɪn ˈsɪk/ | n. | 打电话请病假（的行为）| The act of calling one's employer to report illness and inability to work. | Example:  The company has seen a rise in call-ins-sick this winter.  今年冬天，这家公司打电话请病假的次数有所增加。| 近义词: sick leave notification, absence report | 反义词: attendance, presence | 词性: Noun


**词源:**

"Call in" meaning to communicate by telephone dates back to the early 20th century.  "Sick" meaning ill has been in use for much longer, originating from Old English "seoc."  The combination "call in sick" became common in the mid-20th century.
