halter:/ˈhɔːltər/ (US) /ˈhɔːltə(r)/ (UK)| n. vt.|笼头，缰绳；束缚；系带；无带背心|A rope or strap placed around the head of a horse or other animal, used for leading or tying it. Also, a top garment that ties behind the neck, leaving the back and shoulders bare. |The farmer led the horse by the halter.  农民用缰绳牵着马。
She wore a brightly colored halter top. 她穿着一件颜色鲜艳的露肩背心。|近义词：bridle, headstall (for animals); tank top, tube top (for garment)|反义词: N/A|词性: noun, verb

halter:/ˈhɔːltər/ (US) /ˈhɔːltə(r)/ (UK)| vt.|用缰绳拴住；阻止，抑制|To put a halter on (an animal). Also, to bring to an abrupt stop; check.|He haltered the horse and led it to the stable. 他给马套上笼头，把它牵到马厩里。
The sudden rain haltered their progress. 突如其来的大雨阻止了他们的前进。|近义词：restrain, curb|反义词: release, free|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *halter, from Old English *hælfter, from Proto-Germanic *halftra- (meaning "that which holds"), from Proto-Indo-European *kelH- (meaning "to strike, cut"). The garment sense developed in the 20th century.
