d-day:/ˈdiː deɪ/
n.
D-Day; D-day
(with initial capital) June 6, 1944, the day of the Allied invasion of Normandy in World War II.
例句：The D-Day landings were a turning point in the war.  诺曼底登陆是战争的转折点。

例句：He remembers D-Day vividly. 他对诺曼底登陆日记忆犹新。

近义词：invasion day, landing day
反义词: None (D-Day is a specific historical event, it doesn't have a direct antonym.)
词性: noun

d-day:/ˈdiː deɪ/
n.
d-day; D-day
(usually with small initial letter) a day scheduled for a major undertaking or operation.
例句：The d-day for the product launch has been set. 产品发布的日期已经确定。

例句：We're just waiting for d-day to arrive. 我们只是在等待决战日的到来。

近义词：deadline, target date, appointed day
反义词: None (A general d-day doesn't have a direct antonym)
词性: noun
