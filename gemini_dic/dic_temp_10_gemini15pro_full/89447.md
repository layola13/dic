honer:/ˈɒnər/ (British English), /ˈɑːnər/ (American English)| n. vt.|荣誉，尊敬；给予荣誉|A feeling of pride and pleasure in something you have achieved, or a quality that deserves to be admired. To regard or treat with honor; respect. |例句：They fought for the honor of their country. 他们为国家的荣誉而战。

He was honored for his bravery. 他因勇敢而受到表彰。


近义词：respect, esteem, reverence, dignity, glory|反义词:dishonor, shame, disgrace|词性:名词，动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English honour, honor, honur, from Old French honor, honour, honur, honnour (“honor, respect, privilege, dignity, courtesy, chastity”), from Latin honor, honōs (“honor, esteem, reputation, public office, ornament, grace, beauty”), from Proto-Italic *honōs, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰen- (“to favor, revere”).
