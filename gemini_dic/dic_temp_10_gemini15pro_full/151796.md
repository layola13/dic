passacaglia:/ˌpæsəˈkɑːljə/ /ˌpæsəˈkæljə/| n. |帕萨卡利亚舞曲；帕萨卡利亚|A musical form consisting of variations on a short theme in triple time, typically in a minor key, played at a slow tempo. It is often used in instrumental music, particularly for organ or harpsichord, and occasionally in vocal music.|Bach's Passacaglia and Fugue in C minor is a masterpiece of the form. 巴赫的《c小调帕萨卡利亚与赋格》是这种形式的杰作。

The final movement of Brahms's Fourth Symphony is a passacaglia.  勃拉姆斯的第四交响曲的末乐章是一首帕萨卡利亚。|近义词：chaconne, ground bass|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *passacaglia*, from Spanish *pasacalle* (“street song”), from *pasar* (“to pass, walk”) + *calle* (“street”).
