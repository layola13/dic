hyperthyroidism:/ˌhaɪpərˈθaɪrɔɪdɪzəm/| n.|甲状腺功能亢进症|A condition in which the thyroid gland produces too much thyroid hormone.|She was diagnosed with hyperthyroidism and had to take medication to regulate her hormone levels. 她被诊断出患有甲状腺功能亢进症，必须服药来调节激素水平。|近义词:overactive thyroid, thyrotoxicosis|反义词:hypothyroidism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- + thyroid + -ism.  "Hyper-" is a prefix meaning "over, excessive," from Greek *hyper*, comparative of *hypér* "over, above." "Thyroid" refers to the thyroid gland, from Greek *thyreoeidḗs* "shield-shaped," from *thyreós* "shield" + -eidḗs "shaped like." "-ism" is a suffix indicating a condition or state.
