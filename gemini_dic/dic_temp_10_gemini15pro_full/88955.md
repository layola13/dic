holes:/hoʊlz/ (US) /həʊlz/ (UK)| n.|洞，孔，漏洞|An opening through something; a hollow place in a solid body|例句：There are several holes in my socks. 我的袜子上有几个洞。|近义词：cavities, openings, gaps|反义词: solids, fills|noun

holes:/hoʊlz/ (US) /həʊlz/ (UK)| n.|（高尔夫球的）球洞|A small hollow in the ground on a golf course|例句：He got the ball in the hole in one stroke. 他一杆进洞。|近义词：pits, cups|反义词: mounds, bumps|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hol, from Proto-Germanic *hulaz, from Proto-Indo-European *kel- (to cover, conceal). Related to hell.
