high-pressures:/ˌhaɪ ˈprɛʃərz/| n.|高压，高压环境| situations characterized by intense stress or demands|  The high-pressures of the job led to burnout. 工作的巨大压力导致了倦怠。|Near Synonyms: intense demands, high stress, heavy burden|Opposite: low-pressures, relaxed atmosphere| Noun

high-pressure:/ˌhaɪ ˈprɛʃər/| adj.|高压的，高压力的| characterized by intense stress or demands| He works in a high-pressure environment. 他在一个高压的环境中工作。|Near Synonyms: stressful, demanding, intense|Opposite: low-pressure, relaxed| Adjective

Etymology:  "high" (Old English *hēah) + "pressure" (from Latin pressūra, from pressus, past participle of premere "to press"). The combination describes a state of elevated or intense force.
