mainlining:/ˈmeɪnˌlaɪnɪŋ/
|n.| (毒品)直接注射；沉迷；全神贯注 |injecting a drug directly into a vein; being completely absorbed in something| He was arrested for mainlining heroin. 他因直接注射海洛因被捕。|Synonyms: injecting, shooting up (drugs); absorbing, immersing, engrossing |Antonyms: abstaining, quitting (drugs); neglecting, ignoring |noun


mainlining:/ˈmeɪnˌlaɪnɪŋ/
|v.| (毒品)直接注射；沉迷；全神贯注 |to inject a drug directly into a vein; to be completely absorbed in something| He was mainlining video games all weekend. 他整个周末都沉迷于电子游戏。|Synonyms: injecting, shooting up (drugs); absorbing, immersing, engrossing |Antonyms: abstaining, quitting (drugs); neglecting, ignoring |verb

Etymology: "mainline" comes from the idea of injecting a drug into a major vein, providing the most direct route to the bloodstream. The "-ing" suffix turns it into a noun (the act of doing so) or a verb (to do so).
