decivilize:/diːˈsɪvɪlaɪz/| vt.|使野蛮化；使丧失文明；使腐化|To cause someone or something to lose the qualities or behaviour considered civilized.|Colonialism often decivilized indigenous populations, destroying their traditional social structures. 殖民主义常常使土著居民野蛮化，破坏他们的传统社会结构。

The constant exposure to violence has decivilized him.  持续接触暴力使他变得野蛮。|近义词：barbarize, brutalize, corrupt|反义词：civilize, refine, cultivate|词性：动词

decivilized:/diːˈsɪvɪlaɪzd/| adj.|野蛮的；未开化的；腐化的|Lacking the qualities or behaviour considered civilized.|The decivilized behaviour of the rioters shocked the nation. 暴徒不文明的行为震惊了全国。

They lived in a decivilized state, without laws or social order. 他们生活在一个野蛮的状态，没有法律和社会秩序。|近义词：barbaric, brutal, savage|反义词：civilized, cultured, refined|词性：形容词

decivilization:/diːˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n.|野蛮化；腐化；堕落|The process of becoming decivilized.|The decivilization of society is a worrying trend. 社会的野蛮化是一个令人担忧的趋势。

The war led to a rapid decivilization of the region. 战争导致该地区迅速走向野蛮化。|近义词：barbarization, brutalization, corruption|反义词：civilization, refinement, cultivation|词性：名词
