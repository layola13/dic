agitpropists:/ˈædʒɪtprɒpɪsts/ | n. |宣传鼓动者|People who spread political propaganda, especially for a communist cause.  They typically use art, literature, and other forms of media to influence public opinion.|The agitpropists distributed leaflets encouraging workers to join the strike.  宣传鼓动者散发传单鼓励工人参加罢工。|The play was a blatant piece of agitprop, designed to stir up revolutionary fervor.  这出戏是露骨的政治宣传，旨在激起革命热情。|synonyms: propagandists, activists, campaigners|antonyms:  moderates, conservatives|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Russian агитпроп (agitprop), a portmanteau of агитация (agitaciya, “agitation”) and пропаганда (propaganda, “propaganda”). Ultimately from Latin agitare (“to set in motion, drive, stir up”) and propagare (“to propagate”).
