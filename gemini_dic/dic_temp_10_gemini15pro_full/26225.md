butteries:/ˈbʌtəriz/ | n. |奶油面包卷; (苏格兰)大学食堂 |Small, round, usually currant-studded rolls, especially popular in Scotland.  Also refers to the buttery, a student cafeteria in some Scottish universities. | We enjoyed fresh butteries with our tea. 我们享用了新鲜的奶油面包卷配茶。|Synonyms: rolls, scones (for the food); cafeteria, canteen (for the place) | Antonyms: None (for the food); None (for the place)| noun


butteries:/ˈbʌtəriz/ | n. |(古英语)酒窖; (旧时)负责酒窖的仆人 |Historically, a buttery was a storeroom for alcoholic drinks, especially in a monastery or college.  It can also refer to the servant in charge of this room. | The buttery kept the wine cellar locked. 酒窖管理员把酒窖锁上了。|Synonyms: cellar, pantry (for the room); steward, butler (for the person) |Antonyms: None | noun
