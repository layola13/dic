deputising:/ˈdɛpjʊtaɪzɪŋ/
deputise:/ˈdɛpjʊtaɪz/ | v. | 代理；代表；替…执行任务 | to act or speak for another person, especially at work |  The vice-president is deputising for the president while he is abroad. 副总统在总统出国期间代理总统职务。| synonyms: representing, substituting, acting for | antonyms:  leading, heading | verb

deputised:/ˈdɛpjʊtaɪzd/ | v. |  代理过；代表过；替…执行过任务 | past tense of deputise | I deputised for my manager last week while she was on vacation.  上周经理休假时我代理了她的工作。| synonyms: represented, substituted, acted for | antonyms: led, headed | verb

deputy:/ˈdɛpjʊti/ | n. | 副手；代理人；代表 | a person who is appointed to act for or represent another |  He's the deputy head of the school. 他是学校的副校长。| synonyms: assistant, substitute, representative | antonyms: leader, chief, head | noun


Etymology: From the Old French word "deputé," past participle of "deputer" meaning "to appoint," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "deputare" meaning "to assign, allot, or select."
