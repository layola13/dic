overtenaciously: /ˌoʊvərˌtəˈneɪʃəsli/ | adv. | 过于顽强地，过于执着地 |  In a manner that is excessively persistent or stubborn; with too much tenacity.  Holding on or continuing something with unreasonable firmness. | He overtenaciously clung to his outdated beliefs, refusing to consider any new evidence. 他过于顽固地坚持着他过时的信念，拒绝考虑任何新的证据。|  persistently, stubbornly, doggedly, relentlessly |  halfheartedly, apathetically | adverb

tenacious: /təˈneɪʃəs/ | adj. | 顽强的，执着的 |  Holding fast; characterized by keeping a firm hold (often followed by *of*).  Persistent, resolute.  Not easily letting go or giving up. |  She has a tenacious grip on the company's finances. 她对公司的财务状况有着牢固的控制。| persistent, resolute, determined, firm | weak, yielding, irresolute | adjective

tenacity: /təˈnæsəti/ | n. | 顽强，韧性，执着 | The quality or fact of being able to grip something firmly; grip. The quality or state of being tenacious; persistence. |  Her tenacity in the face of adversity was admirable.  她在逆境中的韧性令人钦佩。| persistence, determination, resolve, firmness | weakness, irresolution, apathy | noun
