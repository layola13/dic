maverick:/ˈmævərɪk/ (US) /ˈmævərɪk/ (UK)| n. adj.|特立独行的人；持不同意见者；标新立异的；不墨守成规的|An independent individual who does not go along with a group or party; something unconventional or unorthodox.|例句：He was a maverick in the field of architecture, always pushing the boundaries of design. 他是建筑界的特立独行的人，总是挑战设计的界限。|例句：She's known for her maverick approach to problem-solving. 她以其不墨守成规的解决问题的方法而闻名。|近义词：nonconformist, rebel, individualist, dissenter, unorthodox, unconventional|反义词：conformist, traditionalist, orthodox, conventional|词性:名词,形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Samuel Maverick (1782-1870), a Texas rancher who refused to brand his cattle.  His unbranded cattle were then called "mavericks". The term later broadened to describe anyone who acts independently or takes an unconventional stand.
