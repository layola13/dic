overshadow:/ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊ/ /ˌəʊvəˈʃædəʊ/| vt.|使失色，使相形见绌，蒙上阴影|to cause someone or something to seem less important or less happy|Her new book is likely to be overshadowed by her previous bestseller. 她的新书可能会被她以前的畅销书比下去。|近义词：eclipse, obscure, dwarf|反义词：highlight, emphasize|词性:动词

overshadow:/ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊ/ /ˌəʊvəˈʃædəʊ/| vt.|（不愉快的事情）给…蒙上阴影|If an unpleasant event or feeling overshadows something, it makes it seem less important or enjoyable by comparison.|News of the accident overshadowed the wedding celebrations.  这起事故的消息给婚礼的庆祝活动蒙上了一层阴影。|近义词：cloud, darken|反义词：brighten|词性:动词
