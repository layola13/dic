colluvies:/kəˈluːviːz/
noun
崩积物；冲积物；混合物
A heterogeneous mixture of material, especially rock fragments and soil, deposited by the action of gravity, water, or both.
The colluvies at the base of the cliff was a mix of earth and shattered rock.  悬崖底部的崩积物是泥土和碎石的混合物。

The heavy rains caused colluvies to slide down the mountainside.  暴雨导致崩积物沿着山坡滑落。

synonym
debris, rubble, accumulation, deposit
antonym
bedrock

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *colluviēs*  "a washing together," from *colluō* "I wash together," from *com-* (together) + *luō* "I wash."
