counterculturalists:/ˌkaʊntərˈkʌltʃərəlɪsts/
|n.|反文化者|People who reject or oppose the dominant values and norms of society, often advocating for alternative lifestyles and beliefs. |The 1960s saw the rise of many counterculturalists who challenged traditional social norms.  20世纪60年代见证了许多挑战传统社会规范的反文化者的兴起。|Synonyms: nonconformists, rebels, dissenters, bohemians|Antonyms: conformists, traditionalists|noun

countercultural:/ˌkaʊntərˈkʌltʃərəl/
|adj.|反文化的|Relating to or characteristic of a counterculture. |The countercultural movement of the 1960s embraced peace, love, and individual expression.  20世纪60年代的反文化运动拥护和平、爱和个人表达。|Synonyms: unconventional, unorthodox, alternative|Antonyms: conventional, orthodox, mainstream|adjective

counterculture:/ˈkaʊntərˌkʌltʃər/
|n.|反文化|A way of life and set of attitudes opposed to or at variance with the prevailing social norm. |The counterculture of the 1960s questioned authority and embraced experimentation. 20世纪60年代的反文化质疑权威，拥抱实验。|Synonyms: subculture, underground|Antonyms: mainstream culture, dominant culture|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"counter-" (prefix): from Old French "contre" and Latin "contra," meaning "against"
"culture": from Latin "cultura," meaning "cultivation" or "growing."
