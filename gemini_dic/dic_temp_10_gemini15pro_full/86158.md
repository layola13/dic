heartquake:/ˈhɑːrtˌkweɪk/| n.|心震，内心震颤|A sudden feeling of strong emotion, especially love or sadness.|例句：Seeing her again after all these years gave me a heartquake. 多年后再次见到她，我的内心一阵震颤。

例句：The news of his death sent heartquakes through the community. 他去世的消息让整个社区都为之震动。|近义词：pang, flutter, thrill, tremor|反义词:apathy, indifference|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"heartquake" is a combination of "heart" and "quake".  "Heart" refers to the emotional center of a person. "Quake" means to tremble or shake, usually from instability. Therefore, "heartquake" literally means a shaking or trembling of the heart due to strong emotion.  It is a relatively modern word, likely first appearing in the late 19th or early 20th century.
