first-floor:/ˈfɜːrst flɔːr/ (US) /ˈfɜːst flɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. adj.|一楼，底层|The first floor in American English refers to the ground floor, while in British English it refers to the floor above the ground floor.  In American English, the floor above the ground floor is called the second floor.  In British English, this floor is called the first floor.|例句：My apartment is on the first floor. (US) 我的公寓在一楼（地面层）。

例句：Her office is on the first floor. (UK) 她的办公室在一楼（地面上一层）。|近义词：(US) ground floor, (UK) first floor (in its US sense)|反义词: top floor|词性: noun, adjective
