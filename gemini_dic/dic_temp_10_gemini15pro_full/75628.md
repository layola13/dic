fugitively:/ˈfjuːdʒətɪvli/| adv.|逃亡地，短暂地|in a way that suggests someone is trying to avoid being noticed or caught.  Like someone running away from the law or responsibility.|He glanced fugitively around the room. 他迅速地环顾了一下房间。|Synonyms: furtively, stealthily, secretly, clandestinely, surreptitiously|Antonyms: openly, publicly, brazenly|Word Type: adverb

fugitive:/ˈfjuːdʒətɪv/| n. adj.|逃亡者，逃犯；短暂的，易逝的|n. a person who has escaped from a place or is in hiding, especially from the police. adj. lasting for a very short time.|n. The fugitive was apprehended near the border. 逃犯在边境附近被捕。 adj.  a fugitive glimpse of a rare bird. 匆匆一瞥稀有鸟类。|Synonyms: (n.) runaway, escapee, deserter; (adj.) fleeting, ephemeral, transient, evanescent|Antonyms: (n.) resident, citizen; (adj.) permanent, lasting, enduring|Word Type: noun, adjective
