going-over:/ˈɡəʊɪŋ ˈəʊvə(r)/| n.| 检查；复习；斥责 |A thorough examination or review of something; a reprimand.|例句：The car needed a going-over before the long trip. 这辆车在长途旅行前需要彻底检查一下。

例句：He gave the report a quick going-over before submitting it. 他在提交报告之前快速地检查了一遍。|近义词：inspection, review, examination, check, scrutiny, reprimand, scolding |反义词: neglect, disregard|词性:noun


going-over:/ˈɡəʊɪŋ ˈəʊvə(r)/| n.| 经历；遭遇 |An experience, especially a difficult or unpleasant one.|例句：She had a real going-over at the dentist's office. 她在牙医诊所里真不好受。

例句：The team got a going-over in the last game.  这个队在上一场比赛中惨败。|近义词：ordeal, experience, trial|反义词:  breeze, cinch|词性:noun
