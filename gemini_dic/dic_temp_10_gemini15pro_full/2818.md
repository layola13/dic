adventitia: /ˌædvenˈtɪʃə/
n.
外膜
The outermost connective tissue covering of an organ or vessel.
The surgeon carefully dissected the adventitia from the artery.  外科医生小心地从动脉上剥离外膜。
The inflammation extended to the adventitia of the blood vessel. 炎症延伸到血管的外膜。
synonyms: tunica externa, outer coat
antonyms: intima,  lumen (Not direct opposites, but represent opposite layers/spaces within a vessel)
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *adventitius* (“coming from abroad”), from *adveniō* (“I arrive”), from *ad* (“to”) + *veniō* (“I come”).  The term refers to the outermost layer, as if "coming from outside" the organ itself.
