ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *funeralles*, from Anglo-Norman *funerailles*, from Late Latin *fūnerālia*, neuter plural of *fūnerālis* (“of or belonging to a funeral, funereal”), from Latin *fūnus* (“funeral rites, burial, corpse”).
