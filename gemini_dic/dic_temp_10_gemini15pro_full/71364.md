flagellant:/ˈflædʒələnt/ | n. adj. | 鞭笞者；苦行者；自虐者 |  A person who whips themselves, especially as a religious discipline; also used to describe someone who seems to enjoy suffering or self-punishment. | The monk, a devout flagellant, believed his suffering brought him closer to God.  这位僧侣，一个虔诚的鞭笞者，相信他的苦难使他更接近上帝。| Near Synonyms: ascetic, masochist, self-punisher | Antonyms: hedonist, sybarite | Noun, Adjective


flagellant:/ˈflædʒələnt/ | adj. | 鞭笞的；苦行的 | Relating to flagellation, or whipping. | His flagellant practices were considered extreme even by the standards of his time. 他的鞭笞行为即使以他那个时代的标准来看也被认为是极端的。| Near Synonyms: ascetic, self-abnegating | Antonyms: indulgent, hedonistic | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *flagellantem, present participle of flagellare (“to whip”), from Latin flagellum (“whip, scourge”).
