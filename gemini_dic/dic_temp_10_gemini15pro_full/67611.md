extraversion:/ˈɛkstrəvɜːʃən/ (US) /ˈɛkstrəvɜːʒən/ (UK)| n. |外向性；外倾性|The act, state, or habit of being predominantly concerned with and obtaining gratification from what is outside the self.|She possesses a high degree of extraversion and enjoys socializing.|Synonyms: outgoingness, sociability, gregariousness|Antonyms: introversion, shyness, reserve|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin extra- (outside) + vertere (to turn)  + -ion (forming nouns of action or condition). Coined by Carl Jung.
