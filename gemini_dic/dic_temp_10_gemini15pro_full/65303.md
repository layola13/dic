eudemonist:/ˌjuːdɪˈmɒnɪst/ | n. | 快乐主义者; 享乐主义者 |  A person who believes in and strives for eudaemonia (human flourishing, happiness, well-being).  |  The eudemonist sought happiness not in fleeting pleasures but in a life of virtue and purpose.  快乐主义者追求的幸福并非转瞬即逝的快乐，而是在充满美德和目标的生活中。| Near synonyms: hedonist (focuses on pleasure), Epicurean. | Antonym: ascetic. | Noun

eudaemonistic:/ˌjuːdɪˈmɒnɪˈstɪk/ | adj. | 快乐主义的; 享乐主义的 |  Relating to or characteristic of eudaemonism. | He followed a eudaemonistic philosophy, prioritizing personal growth and fulfillment. 他遵循快乐主义哲学，优先考虑个人成长和成就。 | Near synonyms: hedonistic, Epicurean. | Antonym: ascetic. | Adjective

eudaemonism:/ˌjuːdɪˈmɒnɪzəm/ | n. | 快乐主义; 享乐主义 | A system of ethics that bases human happiness on rational pursuits. | Eudaemonism emphasizes living a life of virtue, reason, and purpose. 快乐主义强调过一种充满美德、理性和目标的生活。| Near synonyms: hedonism, Epicureanism. | Antonym: asceticism. | Noun
