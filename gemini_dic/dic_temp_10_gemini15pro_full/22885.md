botcher:/ˈbɒtʃə(r)/ (US) /ˈbɑːtʃər/
botcher: n.|拙匠，笨手笨脚的人|A person who does something badly; a bungler. |He's a real botcher in the kitchen, always burning the food. 他在厨房里真是个笨手笨脚的人，总是把食物烧焦。|Synonyms: bungler, incompetent, duffer, klutz|Antonyms: expert, professional, adept|Noun

botcher:/ˈbɒtʃə(r)/ (US) /ˈbɑːtʃər/
botcher: vt.|笨手笨脚地做，搞砸|To do (something) badly; to ruin or spoil through clumsiness or incompetence.|He completely botched the repair job and now the car is worse than before. 他把修理工作完全搞砸了，现在车子比以前更糟了。|Synonyms: bungle, mess up, spoil, ruin|Antonyms: perfect, master, accomplish|Verb
