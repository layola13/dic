casuistical:/ˌkæzjuˈɪstɪkəl/| adj.| 诡辩的；决疑法的；逐例推理的| Relating to casuistry; resolving moral problems by the application of general principles of right and wrong to particular instances.  Characterized by or involving casuistry, especially in relation to moral questions; overly concerned with subtle distinctions or arguments, often seen as excessively refined or hair-splitting.|例句：His casuistical arguments seemed designed to obscure rather than clarify the issue. 他的诡辩论证似乎是为了混淆视听而不是澄清问题。

例句：The lawyer's casuistical defense of his client was met with skepticism by the jury. 律师对当事人诡辩式的辩护遭到了陪审团的质疑。|近义词：sophistical, specious, quibbling, hairsplitting|反义词：straightforward, direct, honest, candid|词性: adjective
