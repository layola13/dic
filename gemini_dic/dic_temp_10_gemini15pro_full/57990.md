drivelled:/ˈdrɪvəld/| v.|胡说八道，说废话，流口水|to talk foolishly or incoherently; to let saliva flow from the mouth.|He drivelled on about his latest conspiracy theory. 他胡扯着他最新的阴谋论。|synonyms: babble, ramble, drool|antonyms: articulate, speak clearly, be concise|verb

drivel:/ˈdrɪvəl/| n.|胡言乱语，废话，口水|foolish or meaningless talk; saliva.|His speech was pure drivel. 他的演讲纯粹是废话。|synonyms: nonsense, gibberish, balderdash|antonyms: sense, wisdom, coherence|noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *drevelen*, frequentative of *dreven* (“to drive, to push”), from Old Norse *drífa* (“to drive”).  The sense of "to talk nonsense" arose from the idea of letting words flow uncontrolled like saliva.  The noun form is derived from the verb.
