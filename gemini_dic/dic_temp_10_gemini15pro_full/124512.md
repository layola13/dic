mounds:/maʊndz/
|n.|土堆，堆；大量|A rounded mass projecting above a level surface. A large quantity or number of something.|例句：The archaeologists excavated the ancient burial mounds.  考古学家挖掘了古代的墓葬堆。

例句：She had mounds of paperwork to do. 她有一大堆文书工作要做。|近义词：heap, pile, stack, mass|反义词:valley, hollow|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mund, from Proto-Germanic *mundaz (“hand, protection”), from Proto-Indo-European *man- (“hand”).  The sense evolved from "protecting hand" to "something that protects" hence a fortification, and then to a rounded hill.
