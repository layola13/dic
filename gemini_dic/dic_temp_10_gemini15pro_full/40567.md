contumacy: /ˈkɒntjʊməsi/ (US) /kənˈtjuːməsi/ (UK)| n. | 顽抗，抗命，不服从 |  stubborn refusal to obey or comply with authority, especially a court order or summons. |  His contumacy in refusing to appear before the judge resulted in a further charge of contempt of court. 他拒不出庭的顽抗行为导致他被进一步指控藐视法庭。| synonyms: defiance, disobedience, insubordination, recalcitrance | antonyms: obedience, compliance, submission | noun

contumacious: /ˌkɒntjʊˈmeɪʃəs/ (US) /ˌkɒntjuːˈmeɪʃəs/ (UK)| adj. | 顽固的，不服从的 |  stubbornly disobedient; rebellious. | The contumacious witness refused to answer the lawyer's questions.  这个顽固的证人拒绝回答律师的问题。| synonyms: defiant, rebellious, insubordinate, recalcitrant | antonyms: obedient, compliant, submissive | adjective
