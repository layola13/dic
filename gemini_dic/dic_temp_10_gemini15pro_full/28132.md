capitol:/ˈkæpɪtl/| n. | 国会大厦；州议会大厦 |A building that houses a state legislature.|  The capitol building is impressive with its grand dome. 国会大厦以其宏伟的圆顶给人留下深刻印象。

The governor gave a speech at the state capitol. 州长在州议会大厦发表了讲话。| synonym:  legislature building, statehouse | antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "private residence") | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *capitol*, from Old French *capitol*, from Latin *Capitōlium*, the temple of Jupiter Optimus Maximus on the Capitoline Hill in Rome; ultimately from Latin *caput* (“head”).  The spelling distinction between *Capitol* (the building) and *capital* (the city, money, etc.) arose in the 18th century.
