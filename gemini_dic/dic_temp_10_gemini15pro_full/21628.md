bluring:/ˈblɜːrɪŋ/| adj. | 模糊的，朦胧的 |  The act of making something unclear or less distinct, often visually. It can also refer to a mental state of confusion or lack of clarity. | The camera's rapid movement caused a blurring of the image. 相机的快速移动导致图像模糊。| synonyms:  obfuscating, smearing, blurring, indistinct, hazy | antonyms:  sharpening, focusing, clarifying, distinct, clear | adjective

bluring:/ˈblɜːrɪŋ/| n. | 模糊，朦胧 | The state of being blurred or made unclear. | The blurring of the lines between work and personal life can be stressful. 工作和个人生活界限的模糊会让人感到压力。| synonyms:  obfuscation, smearing, indistinctness, haziness | antonyms:  sharpness, focus, clarity, distinctness, clearness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "blur," which comes from Middle English *blouren*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *blyrja* "to blink, wink."
