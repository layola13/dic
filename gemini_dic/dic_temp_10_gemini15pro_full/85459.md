hasian:/haːsiːən/| n.|亲爱的，甜心，宝贝|A term of endearment used primarily in the Batak languages (spoken in North Sumatra, Indonesia). It translates roughly to "darling," "sweetheart," or "beloved." It can be used between romantic partners, family members, or close friends.|例句：Hasian, mau makan apa? 亲爱的，想吃什么？|近义词：darling, sweetheart, beloved, honey, love|反义词: (No direct antonym, but the absence of affection could be implied.)|词性:noun

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Hasian ku, ho do na uli lagu. = My darling, you are the one who sings beautifully.
Hasian, rap mangan hita. = Honey, let's eat together.

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Hasian, lagi mangula au. = Darling, I'm working.
Hasian, lagi masak ibana. = Sweetheart, she's cooking.

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
Hasian, nunga sahat au. = Honey, I've arrived.
Hasian, nunga borhat ibana tu Jakarta. = Darling, he has gone to Jakarta.


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
(This tense is less common in Batak languages, a simpler form would usually be used.)
Hasian, nungnga leleng au paima-ima ho. = Darling, I've been waiting for you for a long time.  (More natural in Batak:  Hasian, leleng au paima-ima ho.)
Hasian, nungnga leleng ibana marsahap. = Honey, she has been studying for a long time. (More natural in Batak:  Hasian, leleng ibana marsahap.)

一般过去时 (Simple Past):
Hasian, ro au dohonon tu ho. = Darling, I came to tell you.
Hasian, mangan hita nasobok. = Sweetheart, we ate delicious food yesterday.

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
Hasian, lagi mangan au tingki i. = Honey, I was eating at that time.
Hasian, lagi modom ibana tingki ro au. = Darling, she was sleeping when I arrived.

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
Hasian, nunga borhat ibana andorang so ro au. = Darling, he had left before I arrived.
Hasian, nunga mangan ibana andorang so huboto i. = Sweetheart, she had eaten before I knew it.

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
(Similarly to Present Perfect Continuous, a simpler past tense is more commonly used in Batak.)
Hasian, nungnga leleng ibana mangula andorang so mulak. = Darling, he had been working for a long time before returning. (More natural: Hasian, leleng ibana mangula andorang so mulak.)
Hasian, nungnga leleng au paima-ima ho andorang so ro ho. = Honey, I had been waiting for you a long time before you arrived. (More natural: Hasian, leleng au paima-ima ho andorang so ro ho.)


一般将来时 (Simple Future):
Hasian, laho ma au marsahap. = Darling, I will go to study.
Hasian, mulak do ibana tu huta. = Sweetheart, he will return to the village.

将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
Hasian, lagi marsahap ma au annon. = Honey, I will be studying tomorrow.
Hasian, lagi mangula ma ibana annon. = Darling, she will be working tomorrow.

将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
Hasian, nunga sae ma au marsahap annon. = Darling, I will have finished studying tomorrow.
Hasian, nunga sahat ma ibana tu Medan annon. = Sweetheart, he will have arrived in Medan tomorrow.


将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
(Again, a simpler future tense would likely be used.)
Hasian, nungnga leleng ma au marsahap dung sae annon. = Darling, I will have been studying for a long time after finishing tomorrow. (More natural: Hasian, leleng ma au marsahap dung sae annon.)
Hasian, nungnga leleng ma ibana mangula dung sae annon. = Honey, she will have been working for a long time after finishing tomorrow. (More natural: Hasian, leleng ibana mangula dung sae annon.)
