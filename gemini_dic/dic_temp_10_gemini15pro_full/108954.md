leerily:/ˈlɪərɪli/| adv.| 警惕地；怀疑地；不信任地| in a wary and suspicious manner, as if expecting something bad to happen|  He looked at her leerily, not quite trusting her sudden change of heart. 他警惕地看着她，不太相信她突然改变了主意。|  The dog leerily approached the stranger, growling softly.  那条狗小心翼翼地靠近陌生人，轻轻地咆哮着。| 近义词: suspiciously, distrustfully, warily, cautiously | 反义词: trustingly, confidently, openly | 词性: 副词

词源: From "leery" + "-ly." "Leery" comes from the 19th-century American English slang "leary," meaning "knowing," "alert," or "suspicious," perhaps from British dialect "leery" ("sly," "cunning").
