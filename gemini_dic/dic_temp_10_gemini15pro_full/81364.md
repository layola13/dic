grapple:/ˈɡræpəl/| n. v.|抓住；格斗；抓斗|A struggle or contest in which two opponents grapple with each other.|The two wrestlers grappled with each other. 两个摔跤选手扭打在一起。|Synonyms: wrestle, contend, struggle|Antonyms: release, surrender|noun

grapple:/ˈɡræpəl/| v.|抓住；格斗；抓斗|To seize firmly.|He grappled the rope and pulled himself up. 他紧紧抓住绳子把自己拉了上来。|Synonyms: grasp, seize, clutch|Antonyms: release, let go|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *graper or *grappiller, frequentative of craper ("to grasp, claw"), ultimately from Frankish *krappon ("to hook, seize"). Related to Dutch grabbelen and German grappeln.
