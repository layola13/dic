governabilities:/ˌɡʌvərnəˈbɪlətiz/| n. |可治理性，可统治性|The quality or state of being governable; the extent to which something can be governed effectively.|The country's governabilities has improved significantly since the new regime took over. 这个国家的可治理性自从新政权上任以来显著提高。|Synonyms: manageability, controllability, tractability|Antonyms: ungovernability, unruliness, intractability|Noun

governance:/ˈɡʌvərnəns/| n. |治理，管理，统治|The action or manner of governing or controlling something.|The company's governance structure needs to be reformed. 该公司的治理结构需要改革。|Synonyms: administration, management, rule|Antonyms: misrule, anarchy, chaos|Noun

governable:/ˈɡʌvərnəbəl/| adj. |可治理的，可统治的|Capable of being governed; controllable.|The newly formed democracy is proving more governable than expected.  新成立的民主国家比预期的更易于治理。|Synonyms: manageable, controllable, tractable|Antonyms: ungovernable, unruly, intractable|Adjective

govern:/ˈɡʌvərn/| v. |统治，管理，支配|To control and direct the public business of a country, city, group of people, etc.|The prime minister governs the country. 首相治理国家。|Synonyms: rule, administer, manage|Antonyms:  misrule|Verb
