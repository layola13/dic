brutaler:/bruˈtɑːlər/ | adj. | 更野蛮的，更残酷的 |  Comparative form of brutal: more brutal.  It indicates a higher degree of brutality than the base form "brutal". | The dictator's regime became even brutaler after the attempted coup.  独裁者的政权在未遂政变后变得更加残酷。| The winter this year was brutaler than last year. 今年的冬天比去年更残酷。 |  more savage, fiercer, harsher, crueler | kinder, gentler, more humane | adjective

brutal:/ˈbruːtl/ | adj. | 野蛮的，残酷的 |  Savagely violent.  It describes something or someone characterized by extreme cruelty or violence. | The brutal attack left several people injured.  野蛮的袭击导致数人受伤。| The dictator was known for his brutal treatment of political prisoners.  这位独裁者以其对政治犯的残酷虐待而闻名。 | savage, cruel, vicious, barbaric, inhumane | kind, gentle, humane, compassionate | adjective

Etymology:
From the French word "brutal," which in turn comes from the Late Latin word "brutalis," meaning "of beasts," derived from "brutus," meaning "dull, senseless, irrational, brutish." Ultimately, these words trace back to the Proto-Indo-European root "*gwer-," meaning "heavy, clumsy."
