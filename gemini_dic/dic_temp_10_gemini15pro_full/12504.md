asperse:/əˈspɜːrs/ /əˈspərs/ | vt. | 诽谤，诋毁，中伤 | To falsely or maliciously attack the reputation of someone; to slander or defame. | The politician's rivals aspersed his character with false accusations.  政客的对手用虚假的指控诋毁他的人格。

| Example:  His opponents aspersed his motives. 他的对手诋毁他的动机。| Synonyms: slander, defame, vilify, calumniate, denigrate | Antonyms: praise, commend, laud, extol | verb

Etymology: From Latin *aspersus*, past participle of *aspergere* "to sprinkle, scatter," from *ad* "to, toward" + *spargere* "to scatter." Originally, the word referred to the ritual sprinkling of holy water, but it later acquired the figurative meaning of "to sprinkle with blame or criticism."
