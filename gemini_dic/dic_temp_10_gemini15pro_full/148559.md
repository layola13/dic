oversystematicnesses:/ˌoʊvərˌsɪstəmætɪknɪsɪz/
|n.|过度系统化|The tendency to apply regular grammatical rules to irregular words, often seen in children's language development.  This refers to instances where a child applies a regular grammatical rule to an irregular word, such as saying "goed" instead of "went" or "mouses" instead of "mice".|Example: The child's oversystematicnesses led him to say "bringed" instead of "brought." 这个孩子的过度系统化让他说“bringed”而不是“brought”。
Example:  Oversystematicnesses are a normal part of language acquisition. 过度系统化是语言习得的正常组成部分。|近义词:overregularization|反义词: (No direct antonym, but perhaps) correct usage, standard grammar|词性: Noun
