eucopolysaccharides:/ˌjuːkoʊˌpɒliˈsækəraɪdz/ | n. |优多糖|A type of polysaccharide, primarily found in the cell walls of fungi and algae, characterized by containing glucose and other sugars like xylose, mannose, and galactose. They often have branching structures and play important roles in maintaining cell wall integrity and protecting the organism. | Chitin and glucan are examples of eucopolysaccharides found in fungal cell walls.  甲壳素和葡聚糖是在真菌细胞壁中发现的优多糖的例子。| Synonyms:  glycans, complex carbohydrates | Antonyms:  None | noun
