antanaclasis:/ˌæntənəˈklæsɪs/
|n.|回环词格，双关，同词异义|A rhetorical device or figure of speech involving the repetition of a word or phrase but with a shift in meaning.|The first time he kissed her, she kissed him back. The second time he kissed her, she kissed someone else back.  (kiss 第一次指亲吻这个动作，第二次指背叛)|近义词：pun, paronomasia|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *antanaclasis*, from Ancient Greek ἀντανάκλασις (antanáklasis, “reflection back, bending back, grammatical figure of speech”), from ἀντί (antí, “against, opposite”) + ἀνά (aná, “up, back”) + κλάσις (klásis, “breaking”).
