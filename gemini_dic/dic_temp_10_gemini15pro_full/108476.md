lay oneself out:/leɪ wʌnˈsɛlf aʊt/
|phrasal verb|
|全力以赴；竭尽全力；认真努力；刻意打扮|
|To exert oneself fully; to make a special effort.  It can also mean to dress smartly.|
|He laid himself out to impress the boss. 他竭尽全力给老板留下好印象。|
|go all out, strive, endeavor, exert oneself, make every effort|
|neglect, ignore, do the bare minimum|
|verb phrase|


lay oneself out:/leɪ wʌnˈsɛlf aʊt/
|phrasal verb|
|精心打扮；盛装打扮|
|To dress elegantly or smartly, often for a special occasion.|
|She really laid herself out for the party. 她为参加聚会精心打扮了一番。|
|dress up, get dolled up, put on the ritz|
|dress down, dress casually|
|verb phrase|
