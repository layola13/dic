friability:/fraɪəˈbɪləti/
n.
易碎性，脆性
The quality of being easily crumbled or reduced to powder.
The friability of the soil made it difficult to cultivate. 土壤的易碎性使其难以耕种。
Synonyms: crumbliness, fragility, brittleness
Antonyms: toughness, strength, durability
noun

friable:/ˈfraɪəbl/
adj.
易碎的，脆的
Easily crumbled or reduced to powder.
The dry, friable soil blew away in the wind.  干燥易碎的土壤被风吹走了。
Synonyms: crumbly, fragile, brittle
Antonyms: tough, strong, durable
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *friabilis, from Latin friare "to rub, crumble," of uncertain origin.
