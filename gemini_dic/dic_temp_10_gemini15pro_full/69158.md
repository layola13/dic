fawned:/fɔːnd/| v. | 奉承；讨好；（小鹿等）偎依 | to seek favor or attention by flattery and obsequious behavior; (of a deer) to show affection by nuzzling | The courtiers fawned over the king. 朝臣们对国王阿谀奉承。| The young deer fawned on its mother. 小鹿依偎着它的母亲。| synonyms: flatter, grovel, kowtow, toady, cringe | antonyms: ignore, disdain, defy | verb

fawn:/fɔːn/| n. | 幼鹿 | a young deer in its first year | The fawn hid in the tall grass. 小鹿藏在高高的草丛里。 | synonyms:  yearling, calf (of a deer) | antonyms: (no direct antonym, could use adult deer) | noun

fawn:/fɔːn/| adj. | 浅黄褐色的 | a light yellowish-brown color | She wore a fawn-colored dress. 她穿着一件浅黄褐色的连衣裙。| synonyms: beige, tan, light brown | antonyms: dark, black | adjective
