contrived:/kənˈtraɪvd/ | adj. |人为的，做作的，不自然的 | Appearing artificial, forced, or unnatural; obviously planned or calculated; lacking spontaneity. |  The dialogue in the movie felt contrived and unconvincing.  电影中的对话感觉很做作， unconvincing. | Synonyms: artificial, forced, unnatural, affected, staged,  | Antonyms: natural, genuine, spontaneous, authentic, unforced | adjective

contrived:/kənˈtraɪvd/ | adj. |不自然的，牵强的 |  Lacking in naturalness or spontaneity; labored or strained. |  Her smile seemed contrived, as if she were forcing it.  她的笑容看起来很牵强，好像她是强颜欢笑。 | Synonyms: labored, strained, artificial,  | Antonyms: natural, effortless, spontaneous | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contriviātus, past participle of contriviāre (“to rub together, wear down”), from Latin com- (“together”) + triviālis (“belonging to the crossroads, ordinary”).
