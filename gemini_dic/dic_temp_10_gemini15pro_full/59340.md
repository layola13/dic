earlily: /ˈɜːrlili/ | adv. | 早；提早 | In a time relatively early.  | She arrived earlily for the meeting to prepare. 她早早地来到会议现场做准备。 | The farmer planted the seeds earlily this year hoping for an early harvest. 今年农民早早地播下了种子，希望能早点收成。 | 近义词: early, prematurely, soon | 反义词: late, tardily | 词性: 副词

词源:  From "early" + "-ly." "Early" comes from Old English "ærlice," meaning "early."  The suffix "-ly" transforms adjectives into adverbs.

注意事项:

"Earlily" while grammatically correct, feels somewhat awkward and is less common than simply using "early."  It is generally better to use "early" as an adverb. For instance, "She arrived early" is preferred over "She arrived earlily."  While the provided examples are grammatically correct, using "early" in place of "earlily" will often sound more natural.
