leases: /liːsiz/
n.|租赁，租约|A contract by which one party conveys land, property, services, etc. to another for a specified time, usually in return for a periodic payment.|The company holds leases on several downtown buildings.  这家公司持有几栋市中心建筑的租约。|agreements, rentals|purchases, sales|noun

leases: /ˈliːsiz/
v.|出租，租赁|To grant the temporary possession or use of (lands, tenements, etc.) to another, usually in consideration of rent.|He leases his apartment to students. 他把他的公寓租给学生。|rents, lets|buys, purchases|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *lesser, from Latin *laxare "to loosen, open, slacken," frequentative of laxus "loose."  The noun sense developed from the verb.
