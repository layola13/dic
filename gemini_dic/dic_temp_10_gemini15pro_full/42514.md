counterintuitively:/ˌkaʊntərɪnˈtuːɪtɪvli/ | adv. | 反直觉地 | In a way that seems to contradict one's intuition or expectations; not intuitively obvious. |  While it seems counterintuitive, adding salt to ice can actually help it melt faster. 虽然看起来反直觉，但在冰里加盐实际上可以帮助它融化得更快。 | Surprisingly, unexpectedly, paradoxically | Intuitively, obviously, predictably | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- (meaning "against" or "opposite") + intuitive (meaning "perceived by intuition") + -ly (adverbial suffix).  The word "intuitive" ultimately derives from the Latin word "intuēri," meaning "to look at attentively."
