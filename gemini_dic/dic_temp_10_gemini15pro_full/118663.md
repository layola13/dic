meta-ethical:/ˌmɛtəˈɛθɪkəl/
|adj.|元伦理学的|Relating to the branch of ethics that deals with the nature of moral judgment, language, and reasoning, rather than with specific moral issues.|Meta-ethical discussions often revolve around the meaning of moral terms like "good" and "bad." 元伦理学的讨论经常围绕着诸如“好”和“坏”之类的道德术语的含义展开。|
|近义词:none|
|反义词:none|
|词性:adjective|


ETYMOLOGY->
From meta- + ethical.  "Meta-" is a prefix of Greek origin meaning "after," "beyond," "along with," "among," "behind," or "higher." In this context, it signifies a level of analysis that goes beyond specific ethical questions to examine the foundations of ethics itself.
