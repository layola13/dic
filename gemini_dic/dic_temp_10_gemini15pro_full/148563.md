oversystematizing: /ˌoʊvərˈsɪstəmətˌaɪzɪŋ/ | v. (present participle) | 过度系统化；过度组织化 |  The act of creating excessive or unnecessarily complex systems, often to the point of inefficiency or counterproductivity. It involves applying rigid structures and rules to situations where a more flexible approach would be beneficial. |  He was oversystematizing his work to the point that it was taking longer than necessary. 他把工作过度系统化，以至于花费了比必要更长的时间。

He's so obsessed with productivity that he ends up oversystematizing everything. 他对效率如此痴迷，以至于最终把一切都过度系统化了。 | 近义词: overorganizing, overstructuring | 反义词: simplifying, streamlining | 词性: verb (present participle)
