case-worker:/ˈkeɪsˌwɜːrkər/ (US), /ˈkeɪsˌwɜːkə/ (UK)| n. |个案工作者，社会工作者|A person employed by a social welfare agency, etc., to provide social services to individuals or families.|The case-worker helped the family find suitable housing.|Synonyms: social worker, counselor, advisor|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "case" + "worker". "Case" in this context refers to an individual or family needing social services. "Worker" indicates someone employed to perform a specific job.  The term emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries with the development of professional social work.
