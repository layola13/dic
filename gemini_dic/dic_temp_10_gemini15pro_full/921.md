abuts:/əˈbʌts/| v.|邻接，毗邻，|to be next to or touching something  |例句：My property abuts onto a field. 我的房产紧邻一片田野。

The park abuts the river. 公园与河流毗邻。|近义词：adjoins, borders, touches, meets|反义词: separates, divides|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French abouter, "to join end to end, to place end to end," from a- "to" + bout "end."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
His land abuts mine. = 他的土地与我的土地相邻。
The building abuts the street. = 这栋建筑物紧邻街道。


一般过去时:
The old house abutted the forest. = 那栋老房子紧挨着森林。
Their property abutted a public park. = 他们的房产毗邻一个公园。


现在完成时:
The two properties have abutted each other for centuries. = 这两处房产已经相邻几个世纪了。
The fence has abutted the road since it was built. = 自建成以来，篱笆一直紧挨着道路。


过去完成时:
The shed had abutted the neighbor's fence before it was moved. = 棚屋在搬走之前一直紧挨着邻居的篱笆。
The two kingdoms had abutted each other for generations before the war. = 在战争爆发前，这两个王国已经毗邻了几代人。


将来时:
The new shopping center will abut the highway. = 新的购物中心将紧邻高速公路。
The proposed extension will abut the existing building. = 提议的扩建部分将与现有建筑物相连。


There are no common passive or interrogative usages for the verb "abuts". It primarily describes a state of being.


PHRASE->
land that abuts a river = 紧邻河流的土地

a garden that abuts a forest = 紧邻森林的花园
a wall that abuts a building = 紧挨着建筑物的墙
a fence that abuts a road = 紧邻道路的围栏
