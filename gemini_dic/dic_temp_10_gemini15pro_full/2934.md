advocateship: /ˈædvəkəsiˌʃɪp/
n.
拥护；辩护；倡导
The act of pleading for, supporting, or recommending; active espousal.
She is known for her advocateship of women's rights. 她以倡导女权而闻名。
The organization's advocateship for environmental protection has led to significant policy changes.  该组织对环境保护的倡导导致了重大的政策变化。

近义词: advocacy, support, championship, promotion
反义词: opposition, resistance, antagonism
词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From advocate + -ship.  "Advocate" comes from the Latin "advocatus," past participle of "advocare" (to summon, to call to one's aid), from "ad-" (to) + "vocare" (to call).  "-ship" is a suffix forming nouns denoting state, condition, office, skill, etc.
