mortifiers:/ˈmɔːrtɪfaɪərz/| n.|克制者；禁欲者；苦行者|People who practice mortification, especially of the flesh, are called mortifiers. They often abstain from worldly pleasures and endure hardship as a spiritual discipline.|The monks, true mortifiers, lived on a diet of bread and water.|近义词：ascetics, abstainers|反义词：hedonists, libertines|noun

mortify:/ˈmɔːrtɪfaɪ/| vt.|使…感羞愧；使…受辱；克制；抑制（尤指肉体上的欲望）|To cause (someone) to feel embarrassed, ashamed, or humiliated; to subdue or deaden (especially the flesh) by abstinence or discipline.|It mortified me to have to ask my parents for money.|His extravagance mortified his family.|近义词：humiliate, shame, embarrass, subdue, repress|反义词：exalt, gratify, indulge|verb
