clamor:/ˈklæmər/ (US) /ˈklæmə(r)/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.|喧闹，吵闹；大声要求|A loud and confused noise, especially that of people shouting vehemently.  To make a vehement protest or demand.|The clamor of the crowd grew louder as the team scored.  人群的欢呼声随着队伍得分而越来越大。|The citizens clamored for change. 市民强烈要求变革。|synonyms: uproar, outcry, hubbub, din, commotion|antonyms: silence, quiet, hush, stillness|noun, verb
