buccaneer: /ˌbʌkəˈnɪr/ | n. |海盗，冒险家|A pirate, especially one operating in the Caribbean during the 17th century.  Originally, buccaneers were French hunters of wild pigs and cattle in the West Indies, but the term was later applied to the pirates who preyed on Spanish shipping in the Caribbean. |例句：The buccaneer sailed the seven seas, plundering Spanish galleons.  这个海盗航行七海，掠夺西班牙大帆船。

例句：He lived a buccaneer's life, full of adventure and danger. 他过着海盗般的生活，充满冒险和危险。|近义词：pirate, corsair, privateer, freebooter, rover |反义词: lawman, sailor (non-piratical)| noun
