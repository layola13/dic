conqueror:/ˈkɒŋkərə(r) (UK), /ˈkɑːŋkərər (US)| n. |征服者，胜利者|A person who conquers a place or people, especially by military force.|例句：William the Conqueror became king of England in 1066. 威廉一世于1066年成为英格兰国王。

例句：The conquerors plundered the city after the battle. 征服者在战斗后洗劫了这座城市。

近义词：victor, champion, winner, vanquisher|反义词：defeated, loser, vanquished|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French conquerour (masculine), conquerresse (feminine), from Late Latin conqueror, from Latin conquirere "to acquire, gain, win, procure," from com- "together" + quaerere "to seek, gain."
