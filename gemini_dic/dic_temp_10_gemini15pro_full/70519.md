filet:/fɪˈleɪ/ (US), /ˈfɪlɪt/ (UK)| n. |鱼片，肉片，菲力牛排|A boneless piece of meat or fish, especially one cut from the tenderloin of beef.|例句：He ordered the filet mignon. 他点了菲力牛排。|例句：The chef carefully deboned the fish to create perfect filets.  厨师小心翼翼地将鱼去骨，做成完美的鱼片。|近义词：tenderloin, fillet, steak (for beef)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


filet:/fɪˈleɪ/ (US), /ˈfɪlɪt/ (UK)| v. |把…切成片|To cut (meat or fish) into a filet.|例句：She fileted the salmon for dinner. 她把鲑鱼切成片做晚餐。|例句：He carefully fileted the chicken breast. 他小心翼翼地把鸡胸肉切成片。|近义词：slice, cut|反义词: N/A|词性:verb


词源: From the Old French word *filet*, meaning "thread," ultimately from the Latin *filum*.  The sense of a thin slice of meat arose from the resemblance to a thread.
