brittleness:/ˈbrɪtlnəs/| n.|脆性，易碎性|The quality of being easily broken or cracked. |The brittleness of the glass made it shatter into tiny pieces. 玻璃的脆性使它碎成了小片。|Synonyms: fragility, frangibility, breakability|Antonyms: toughness, resilience, flexibility|noun


brittleness:/ˈbrɪtlnəs/| n.|脆弱性，不稳定性|The quality of being easily damaged or destroyed, either physically or emotionally. |The brittleness of the peace agreement was evident. 和平协议的脆弱性显而易见。|Synonyms: vulnerability, instability, precariousness|Antonyms: strength, stability, robustness|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *brittilnesse, equivalent to brittle +‎ -ness.  From Old English *brytelnes (“brittleness”), equivalent to brytel (“brittle”) +‎ -nes (“-ness”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *brutiz (“brittle”).  Cognate with Dutch broosheid (“brittleness”).
