metallicities: /ˌmɛtəˈlɪsɪtiz/ | n. | 金属丰度；金属性 | The abundance of elements present in a star or other astronomical object, excluding hydrogen and helium.  It is often expressed as the ratio of the abundance of iron to hydrogen, compared to the same ratio in the Sun. | The metallicities of stars in globular clusters are generally lower than those in the disk of the galaxy. 球状星团中恒星的金属性通常低于星系盘中恒星的金属性。| synonym: metal abundances | antonym: (No direct antonym, but low metallicity could be considered the opposite of high metallicity) | noun
