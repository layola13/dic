institutionalize: /ˌɪnstɪˈtuːʃənəlaɪz/ | vt. | 使制度化；使机构化；把…送进机构 | To establish something as a convention or norm in an organization or culture; to place someone in an institution, such as a hospital or orphanage. |  The company decided to institutionalize the new safety procedures. 公司决定将新的安全程序制度化。|  His family had to institutionalize him after his mental breakdown.  他的家人在他精神崩溃后不得不把他送进精神病院。| synonyms: systematize, organize, hospitalize, commit | antonyms: personalize, individualize | verb

institutionalize: /ˌɪnstɪˈtuːʃənəlaɪz/ | v. |  变得刻板；变得僵化 |  To become rigid and inflexible, often due to established procedures or routines. | The creative process had become institutionalized, stifling innovation.  创作过程变得刻板，扼杀了创新。| After years in prison, he was institutionalized and struggled to adapt to life outside. 在监狱待了很多年后，他变得僵化，难以适应外面的生活。| synonyms: ossify, rigidify | antonyms: adapt, evolve | verb
