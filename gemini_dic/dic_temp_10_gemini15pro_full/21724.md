boarest:/ˈbɔːrɪst/ |adj.|最无聊的|superlative adjective of bore; most tedious and uninteresting.|The boarest lecture I've ever attended was about the history of tax law. 我参加过的最无聊的讲座是关于税法的历史。|Synonyms: tedious, dull, monotonous, tiresome|Antonyms: interesting, exciting, stimulating|adjective

boast:/boʊst/ |v. n.|吹嘘，自夸；拥有（值得自豪的东西）|
(v.) talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction about one's achievements, possessions, or abilities.
(n.) an act of boasting.
| (v.) He boasted about his new car. 他吹嘘他的新车。(n.) His boast about his new car was annoying.  他吹嘘他的新车很烦人。|Synonyms: brag, vaunt, crow (v.); brag, vaunt (n.)|Antonyms: downplay, minimize (v.); modesty, humility (n.)|verb, noun

PHRASE->
boast of  拥有（值得自豪的东西）The town boasts of a beautiful lake.  这个小镇拥有一个美丽的湖泊。
boast about 吹嘘，夸耀 He boasted about his new car. 他吹嘘他的新车。
make a boast of 吹嘘，夸耀  He made a boast of his wealth. 他夸耀他的财富。
empty boast  空洞的吹嘘  His promises were just empty boasts. 他的承诺只是空洞的吹嘘。
idle boast  无意义的吹嘘  It was an idle boast, he never intended to do it.  这只是一个无意义的吹嘘，他从未打算这样做。


注意：boarest 是 boast 的最高级形式，主要用作形容词，表示“最无聊的”。而 boast 本身主要用作动词或名词，表示“吹嘘”或“自夸”。
