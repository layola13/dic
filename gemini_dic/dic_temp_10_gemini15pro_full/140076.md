of-few-words:/əv fjuː wɜːdz/
adjective
寡言少语的，简洁的
Someone who is of few words does not speak very much.
He's a man of few words, but he's very kind. 他是个寡言少语的人，但他很善良。
近义词:laconic, reticent, taciturn, concise, brief
反义词:talkative, loquacious, verbose, garrulous, prolix
词性:adjective

词源:  This phrase is a straightforward combination of the preposition "of" indicating possession or characteristic and the adjective "few" modifying the noun "words." It literally means "having few words."  Its usage dates back to at least the 16th century.
