canonlike:/ˈkænənˌlaɪk/ | adj. | 像经典的，符合经典标准的 | Resembling or characteristic of a canon, especially in terms of authority, authenticity, or traditional acceptance.  |  The professor's canonlike pronouncements on the subject left no room for debate. 教授关于这个主题的权威性发言不容置疑。 | Synonyms: authoritative, orthodox, traditional, established, accepted, standard | Antonyms: unorthodox, unconventional, heterodox, heretical, apocryphal | adjective

Etymology:  From *canon* + *-like*. *Canon* comes from Late Latin *canon* "rule, standard," from Greek *kanōn* "straight rod, rule, standard." *-like* is an Old English suffix meaning "having the qualities of."
