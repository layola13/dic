criticise: /ˈkrɪtɪsaɪz/ (UK) /ˈkrɪtəˌsaɪz/ (US)| vt. |批评，批判；评论；挑剔| To express disapproval of someone or something; to judge something and give an opinion about it, especially a negative opinion. |例句：He criticised her handling of the situation. 他批评了她对局势的处理。

例句：The film was criticised for its violence. 这部电影因其暴力内容而受到批评。

近义词：blame, condemn, denounce, censure, reprehend, castigate|反义词: praise, commend, laud, extol|词性: 动词


criticism: /ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/ (UK) /ˈkrɪtəˌsɪzəm/ (US)| n. |批评，批判；评论；挑剔| The act of expressing disapproval of someone or something; an analysis and judgment of the merits and faults of a literary or artistic work. |例句：The government's economic policy came under heavy criticism. 政府的经济政策受到了猛烈批评。

例句：Literary criticism can be a fascinating subject. 文学评论可以是一个引人入胜的主题。

近义词：condemnation, denunciation, censure, reprehension, castigation|反义词: praise, commendation, applause|词性: 名词


critic: /ˈkrɪtɪk/| n. |批评家，评论家；爱挑剔的人| A person who judges the merits of literary, artistic, or musical works, especially one who does so professionally.  A person who tends to find fault or judge with severity. |例句：He is a renowned film critic. 他是一位著名的电影评论家。

例句：She's her own worst critic. 她对自己最苛刻。

近义词：reviewer, analyst, judge|反义词: supporter, admirer, fan|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *critiquer*, from Late Latin *criticāre* "to judge, criticize," from Greek *kritikós* "able to judge, critical," from *krínein* "to separate, decide, judge."
