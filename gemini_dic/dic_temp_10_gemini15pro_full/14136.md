authorism: /ˈɔːθərɪzəm/ | n. | 权威主义；独裁主义 | The belief in or practice of government “from above,” in which authority is exercised regardless of popular consent. |  The country was ruled by authoritarianism for decades.  这个国家被独裁主义统治了几十年。 | Example: The rise of authoritarianism in the region is a cause for concern. 该地区权威主义的兴起令人担忧。 | Synonyms: dictatorship, totalitarianism, despotism, tyranny | Antonyms: democracy, liberalism, freedom, self-government | noun


authorism: /ˈɔːθərɪzəm/ | n. | 权威主义；专制主义 | The enforcement or advocacy of strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom. |  He demonstrated his authoritarianism by making all the decisions without consulting his team. 他通过不咨询团队就做出所有决定来展示他的专制主义。 | Example: Her authoritarianism in the workplace created a tense atmosphere. 她在工作场所的专制主义制造了紧张的气氛。 | Synonyms: absolutism, dogmatism, illiberalism | Antonyms: democracy, liberalism, freedom, self-government | noun
