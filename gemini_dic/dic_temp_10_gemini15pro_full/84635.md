hand-me-down:/ˌhænd mi ˈdaʊn/| n. adj.|旧衣服，别人用过的东西|Something, typically clothing, that has been passed down from another person, usually an older sibling.  A hand-me-down can also refer to something, like a tradition or belief, that has been passed down through generations. |My younger brother always wore hand-me-down clothes from me. 我的弟弟总是穿我穿过的旧衣服。|The old bicycle was a hand-me-down from my cousin.  这辆旧自行车是我表哥传给我的。|Synonyms: second-hand, used, pre-owned|Antonyms: new, brand-new|noun, adjective
