horsewomanship:/ˈhɔːrswʊmənʃɪp/| n. |骑术；马术|The art or skill of riding and managing horses.  The ability to ride and control a horse effectively.|She demonstrated excellent horsemanship in the competition. 她在比赛中展现了出色的骑术。|近义词：equitation, riding skill|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider lack of skill, ineptitude)|名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "horsewoman" + "-ship."  "Horsewoman" dates back to the late 14th century, meaning "a woman who rides a horse."  "-ship" is a suffix used to form nouns denoting skill, state, or condition.
