abrogative: /ˈæbrəɡətɪv/
adjective
废除的，撤销的
Having the power or effect of abolishing or repealing something.
The government issued an abrogative decree, rendering the previous law null and void. 政府发布了一项废除法令，使先前的法律无效。

The new policy is seen as abrogative of the earlier agreement. 新政策被视为废除了早先的协议。

synonym:  annulling, revoking, repealing, rescinding, nullifying
antonym:  ratifying, confirming, establishing, upholding, enacting
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *abrogativus, from Latin abrogatus (past participle of abrogare “to repeal”), from ab- “away” + rogare “to ask, propose a law”.


USAGE->
The abrogative power of the legislature allows them to repeal outdated laws. 立法机关的废除权允许他们废除过时的法律。

The court's decision had an abrogative effect on the previous ruling. 法院的判决对先前的裁决具有废除效力。

The abrogative clause in the contract allowed either party to terminate the agreement. 合同中的废除条款允许任何一方终止协议。

His abrogative actions effectively ended the treaty. 他的废除行为有效地终止了条约。


PHRASE->
abrogative power = 废除权
abrogative act = 废除行为
abrogative effect = 废除效力
abrogative clause = 废除条款
abrogative decision = 废除决定
