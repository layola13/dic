brood:/bruːd/
noun
一窝;一孵的幼雏;（尤指）一窝小鸡
A family of young birds or other animals, especially a single hatch of chicks.
The hen proudly paraded her brood of chicks. 母鸡骄傲地带着她的一窝小鸡走来走去。

noun
（某一地方或类型的）一批（人）
A group of people or things of a particular kind, especially one regarded as unpleasant.
A brood of young actors eager to make their mark. 一群渴望成名的年轻演员。

verb
沉思;忧思;（尤指）郁郁不乐地想
Think deeply about something that makes one unhappy, especially for a long time.
She brooded over the injustice of the situation. 她对情况的不公正感到郁郁寡欢。

verb
（鸟）孵蛋
(of a bird) sit on (eggs) to hatch them.
The female bird broods the eggs for about three weeks.  雌鸟孵蛋大约三周。


近义词: contemplate, ponder, ruminate, meditate (for verb); clutch, offspring (for noun)
反义词: celebrate, rejoice (for verb)
词性: noun, verb

词源：Old English brōd, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch broed and German Brut, from an Indo-European root shared by breed.
