parasympathetic:/ˌpærəsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk/| adj.|副交感的|Relating to the part of the autonomic nervous system that counterbalances the action of the sympathetic nerves. It conserves energy as it slows the heart rate, increases intestinal and gland activity, and relaxes sphincter muscles.|The parasympathetic nervous system helps the body to relax and digest food.|近义词:cholinergic|反义词:sympathetic|词性:adjective

Etymology:
From para- + sympathetic.  Para- comes from Greek, meaning "beside," "alongside of," "against," "contrary to," "irregular," "amiss." Sympathetic also has Greek roots, from sym- "together" + pathos "feeling."  The term describes the nerves' working "alongside" the sympathetic nervous system.
