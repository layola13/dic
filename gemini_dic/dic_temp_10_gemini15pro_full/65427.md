euphrasy:/ˈjuːfrəsi/ | n. |眼亮草；小米草；(尤指眼疾的)慰藉，舒缓|A plant formerly used as a remedy for eye diseases.  Figuratively, anything that clears the vision. |  She sought euphrasy for her tired eyes, both literally with herbal remedies and figuratively with a calming meditation. 她为疲惫的眼睛寻求慰藉，既用草药进行字面上的治疗，也用冥想进行象征意义上的舒缓。 | 近义词: eyebright, clarity, solace | 反义词: affliction, discomfort | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Middle English *eufrasie*, from Old French *eufraise*, from Latin *euphrasia*, from Ancient Greek *εὐφρασία* (*euphrasía*) "delight, gladness, good cheer, mirth", from *εὔφρων* (*eúphrōn*) "joyful, cheerful", from *εὖ* (*eu*) "well" + *φρήν* (*phrḗn*) "mind, heart, spirit".  The plant was believed to improve eyesight and thus bring joy.
