clerestory:/ˈklɪərstɔːri/ (US), /ˈklɪəstɔːri/ (UK)| n.|高侧窗，透光高窗|A high section of wall containing windows that admits light to the central part of a building, especially a church.|The clerestory windows filled the cathedral with a warm, ethereal light. 高侧窗使大教堂充满了温暖而空灵的光线。|Synonym: high window, skylight|Antonym: low window|noun


clerestory:/ˈklɪərstɔːri/ (US), /ˈklɪəstɔːri/ (UK)| adj.|高侧窗的|Having a clerestory.|The clerestory design allows for natural ventilation. 高侧窗设计允许自然通风。|Synonym: high-windowed|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "windowless")|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clerestorie*, from Old French *cler-estorie*  "clear story," referring to the upper level of a church nave having windows that admit light. The "clear" element likely refers to the unobstructed light.
