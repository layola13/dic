inlander:/ˈɪnləndər/ | n. | 内地人；内陆居民 | A person who lives in the interior of a country, rather than on the coast. |  He was an inlander, unfamiliar with the ways of the sea. 他是个内地人，对海洋生活方式不熟悉。

| Example 1: The inlander was amazed by the vastness of the ocean.  内地人对海洋的浩瀚感到惊奇。

| Example 2:  Inlanders often find the coastal climate too humid. 内地人通常觉得沿海气候太潮湿。

| Synonym: native, resident, inhabitant | Antonym: coaster, foreigner | noun
