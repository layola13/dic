loudspeaker: /ˈlaʊdˌspiːkər/ (US), /ˈlaʊdˌspiːkə(r)/ (UK)| n. |扬声器；喇叭|A device that converts electrical signals into sound waves, typically housed in a cabinet and used to amplify music or speech.|The band's loudspeakers were so loud they could be heard for miles. 乐队的扬声器声音很大，几英里外都能听到。|Synonyms: speaker, amplifier, sound system|Antonyms: microphone (input device, opposite function)|noun

loudspeakers: /ˈlaʊdˌspiːkərz/ (US), /ˈlaʊdˌspiːkəz/ (UK)| n. |扬声器（复数）；喇叭（复数）|Plural form of loudspeaker, referring to multiple devices that convert electrical signals into sound waves.|We need to buy new loudspeakers for the auditorium. 我们需要为礼堂购买新的扬声器。|Synonyms: speakers, amplifiers, sound systems|Antonyms: microphones (input devices, opposite function)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "loud" + "speaker". "Loud" comes from Old English "hlūd," meaning "noisy, clamorous." "Speaker" comes from Middle English "spekeren," meaning "to speak."
