agrappes:/əˈɡraps/ | adj. |成串的，簇生的 |Bunches of grapes, usually still attached to the stems.  From the French "à grappes," meaning "with bunches."  Used primarily in viticulture and winemaking to describe grapes harvested and/or fermented in whole clusters, as opposed to destemmed berries. |The wine was made using whole agrappes, resulting in a distinctive flavor profile. 这款葡萄酒是用整串葡萄酿造的，因此具有独特的风味。|Synonyms: clustered, bunched |Antonyms: destemmed, single |Adjective
