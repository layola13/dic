gimmicking: /ˈɡɪmɪkɪŋ/
| n. |  耍花招；弄虚作假 |  The act of using gimmicks, often to deceive or manipulate.  | The magician's performance was all gimmicking and no real magic.  魔术师的表演全是耍花招，没有真本事。 | synonyms: trickery, deception, manipulation | antonyms: honesty, authenticity | noun

gimmick: /ˈɡɪmɪk/
| n. | 花招；噱头；小玩意儿 | A trick or device intended to attract attention, publicity, or business. |  The free toy was just a marketing gimmick to get people to buy the cereal.  免费玩具只是一个营销噱头，目的是让人们购买麦片。| synonyms: trick, device, gadget, ploy | antonyms:  genuineness, sincerity  | noun
| v. |  用小玩意装饰；耍花招 | To equip or embellish with gimmicks. |  They gimmicked the car up with all sorts of flashing lights. 他们用各种各样的闪光灯装饰汽车。 | synonyms: trick out, rig, manipulate | antonyms: simplify, streamline  | verb
