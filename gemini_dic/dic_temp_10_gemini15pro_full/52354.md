dictatorially:/ˌdɪktəˈtɔːriəli/ | adv. |独裁地，专横地 | In a dictatorial manner; like a dictator.  Characterized by giving orders and expecting obedience without question. | He ruled dictatorially, brooking no dissent. 他独裁统治，不容异议。|Synonyms: autocratically, tyrannically, despotically, imperiously | Antonyms: democratically, cooperatively, liberally | Part of Speech: Adverb

dictatorial:/ˌdɪktəˈtɔːriəl/ | adj. |独裁的，专横的 | Of or characteristic of a dictator; characterized by absolute authority or power.  |  His dictatorial style alienated his colleagues.  他专横的作风疏远了他的同事。| Synonyms: autocratic, tyrannical, despotic, imperious | Antonyms: democratic, cooperative, liberal | Part of Speech: Adjective

dictator:/ˈdɪkteɪtər/ | n. |独裁者 | A ruler with total power over a country, typically one who has obtained power by force. | The dictator ruled with an iron fist.  这个独裁者实行铁腕统治。| Synonyms: autocrat, tyrant, despot | Antonyms: democrat | Part of Speech: Noun

dictate:/dɪkˈteɪt/ | v. |口述；命令；支配 | 1. Say or read aloud (words to be typed, written down, or recorded). 2. Lay down authoritatively; prescribe. |  1. She dictated the letter to her secretary. 她把信口述给秘书。 2.  The weather dictated our plans. 天气决定了我们的计划。| Synonyms: command, order, decree, prescribe | Antonyms: suggest, propose, request | Part of Speech: Verb

dictation:/dɪkˈteɪʃən/ | n. |口述；听写 | The action of saying words aloud to be typed, written down, or recorded. | The students had a dictation test. 学生们进行了听写测试。| Synonyms: transcription | Antonyms: N/A | Part of Speech: Noun


Etymology: From Latin *dictator*, agent noun from *dictare* "to say repeatedly, prescribe," frequentative of *dicere* "to say, speak."
