metaphor:/ˈmetəfɔːr/ (US), /ˈmetəfə(r)/ (UK)| n. | 比喻，隐喻 | A figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable. |  例句： "Life is a journey" is a common metaphor.  人生是一段旅程，是一个常见的比喻。

例句：The snow is a white blanket. 雪像一张白色的毯子。

近义词：simile, analogy, figure of speech | 反义词: literalism | 词性: noun

PHRASE->
mixed metaphor  =  混杂的比喻
dead metaphor =  死喻 (失去比喻意义的词语，例如 "kick the bucket")
extended metaphor = 延伸的比喻
root metaphor =  根本比喻
conceptual metaphor =  概念比喻


词源: From Middle English *metaphore, from Old French metaphore, from Latin metaphora, from Ancient Greek μεταφορά (metaphorá, “a transfer”), from μεταφέρω (metaphérō, “I transfer, I carry over”), from μετά (metá, “after, over”) + φέρω (phérō, “I bear, I carry”).
