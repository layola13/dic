cavalierisms:/ˌkævəˈlɪərɪzəmz/| n. |傲慢的态度；轻蔑的言行；随便的处理|An arrogant disregard for others; offhand or disdainful treatment or behavior.|He dismissed her concerns with a wave of his hand and a series of cavalierisms.|近义词：arrogance, disdain, haughtiness, superciliousness|反义词：humility, modesty, respect|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cavalierisme*, from *cavalier* ("knight, horseman"), from Late Latin *caballarius* ("horseman"), from *caballus* ("horse").  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*ḱabalos* ("pale; dappled horse"). The sense of "offhand or disdainful treatment" developed from the perceived arrogance of aristocratic horsemen.
