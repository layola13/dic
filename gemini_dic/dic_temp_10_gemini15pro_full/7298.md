angsty: /ˈæŋ(k)sti/ | adj. | 焦虑的，苦闷的 | Experiencing or expressing angst, typically in an exaggerated or melodramatic way. Characterized by anxiety, apprehension, and emotional turmoil, often associated with adolescence or young adulthood. |  Example: The teenager wrote angsty poetry about unrequited love.  这个青少年写了一些关于单恋的焦虑诗歌。

Example:  The movie is full of angsty teenagers struggling to find their place in the world.  这部电影充满了苦闷的青少年，他们挣扎着寻找自己在世界上的位置。 | Near synonyms: anxious, worried, apprehensive, uneasy, troubled, tormented, brooding | Opposite: calm, serene, peaceful, content | Word Class: Adjective

angst: /æŋst/ | n. | 焦虑，苦闷 | A feeling of deep anxiety or dread, typically an existential one. A feeling of persistent worry about something. | Example:  Teenage angst is a common theme in literature and film. 青春期的焦虑是文学和电影中的常见主题。

Example: He suffered from a deep sense of angst about the future. 他对未来充满了深深的焦虑。| Near synonyms: anxiety, dread, worry, apprehension, unease | Opposite: calmness, serenity, peace, contentment | Word Class: Noun
