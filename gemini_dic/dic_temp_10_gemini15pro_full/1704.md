acorns:/ˈeɪkɔːrnz/ (US) /ˈeɪkɔːnz/ (UK)| n.|橡子；橡实|The oak tree produces acorns. 橡树结橡子。|例句：Squirrels bury acorns for the winter. 松鼠把橡子埋起来过冬。

The children collected acorns in the park. 孩子们在公园里收集橡子。|近义词：nut, seed|反义词:|名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *æcern, from Proto-Germanic *akraną (“acorn, fruit, berry”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eǵ- (“fruit, berry”). Cognate with Old Norse akarn, Old High German ackarn, Old Saxon akarn, Dutch aker, German Ecker.

USAGE->
Plural Form:
The squirrels gathered acorns for the winter. 松鼠们收集橡子过冬。
We found many acorns under the oak tree. 我们在橡树下发现了许多橡子。

As Part of a Larger Quantity:
Acorns provide food for many animals. 橡子为许多动物提供食物。
The forest floor was covered with acorns. 森林地面上覆盖着橡子。

With Adjectives:
The ripe acorns fell from the tree. 成熟的橡子从树上掉下来。
We found some small acorns under the bush. 我们在灌木丛下发现了一些小橡子。

In a Metaphorical Sense:
From small acorns, mighty oak trees grow.  千里之行，始于足下（字面意思：从小的橡子，长成参天橡树）。
The idea was just an acorn, but it grew into a successful business. 这个想法最初只是一个橡子，但后来发展成了一家成功的企业。
