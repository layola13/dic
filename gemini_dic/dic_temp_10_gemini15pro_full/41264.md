cord:/kɔːrd/ (British), /kɔːrd/ (American)| n.|绳索，线，帘线；灯芯绒裤子|A length of thick string or thin rope, composed of several strands twisted together.  Also, a ribbed fabric, typically cotton, with a pile surface formed by cut tufts.|The climbers used a strong cord to secure themselves.|近义词：rope, string, twine, cable, line|反义词: N/A|noun

cord:/kɔːrd/ (British), /kɔːrd/ (American)| v.|用绳子捆绑|To tie or bind with cord.|He corded the wood for the fire.|近义词：tie, bind, fasten|反义词: untie, loosen|verb
