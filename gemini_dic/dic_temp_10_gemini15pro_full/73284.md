forebodingly:/fɔːrˈboʊdɪŋli/ | adv. |不祥地，预兆不祥地 | in a way that suggests that something bad is going to happen |  The music played forebodingly as the protagonist entered the haunted house.  音乐不祥地响起，主角走进了鬼屋。 | synonym: ominously, menacingly, threateningly | antonym: reassuringly, hopefully, cheerfully | 词性: 副词

foreboding:/fɔːrˈboʊdɪŋ/ | n. | 预感，预兆 | a feeling that something bad will happen | He had a foreboding that the journey would be fraught with danger. 他有一种预感，这次旅程将充满危险。 | synonym: premonition, presentiment | antonym:  assurance, hope | 词性: 名词

forebode:/fɔːrˈboʊd/ | v. | 预示，预兆 | to be a sign that something bad will happen |  Dark clouds forebode a storm. 乌云预示着暴风雨即将来临。 | synonym:  portend, presage, augur | antonym: promise, assure | 词性: 动词

词源:  From Middle English *forboden, from Old English forbōdian (“to announce beforehand, foretell, predict, portend, decree, command”), from for- (“before, for”) + bōdian (“to proclaim, announce, predict”).
