chromotype: /ˈkroʊməˌtaɪp/ | n. | 染色体型，染色体组型 | A chromotype is the particular combination of chromosomes in an individual or species, often visualized as a karyotype. |  Example:  The chromotype of a Down syndrome patient reveals an extra copy of chromosome 21.  唐氏综合症患者的染色体型显示21号染色体多了一个副本。

Example:  Scientists are studying the chromotypes of various cancer cells to understand their genetic basis.  科学家正在研究各种癌细胞的染色体型，以了解它们的遗传基础。| 近义词: karyotype | 反义词:  (No direct antonym) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *chrōma* (color) + *typos* (type).  The term refers to the banding patterns produced by staining chromosomes, which are used to identify and classify them.
