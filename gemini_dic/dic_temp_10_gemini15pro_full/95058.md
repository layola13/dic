inadequately:/ɪnˈædɪkwətli/
adverb
不足地，不充分地
Not enough; insufficiently.  Failing to reach an expected or required level.
The building was inadequately heated, leaving the tenants shivering in the winter. 这座建筑供暖不足，让租户们在冬天瑟瑟发抖。
The situation was inadequately explained, leading to further confusion.  情况解释得不够充分，导致了进一步的混乱。
Synonyms: insufficiently, poorly, improperly, deficiently, unsatisfactorily
Antonyms: sufficiently, adequately, properly, competently, effectively
词性: 副词

Etymology: From in- (not) + adequate (sufficient) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Adequate" comes from Latin "adaequatus," past participle of "adaequare" (to make equal), from "ad-" (to) + "aequus" (equal).
