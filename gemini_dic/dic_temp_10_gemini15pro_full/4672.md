alemannic: /ˌæləˈmænɪk/ | adj. n.| 阿勒曼尼的，阿勒曼尼人；阿勒曼尼语 | Relating to the Alemanni, a Germanic people who lived in the region of the Upper Rhine in the 3rd century AD. Also refers to their language or dialects descended from it. |  The Alemannic dialects are spoken in parts of Switzerland, Germany, France, and Austria. 阿勒曼尼方言在瑞士、德国、法国和奥地利的部分地区使用。|  The museum has an exhibit on Alemannic art and culture.  博物馆有一个关于阿勒曼尼艺术和文化的展览。 | synonyms: Swabian, Alsatian (for certain dialects) | antonyms:  N/A | adjective, noun


Etymology:  From Late Latin *Alamannus, from Proto-Germanic *Alamanniz ("all men," perhaps referring to a mixed tribal group).
