descriptionist:/dɪˈskrɪpʃənɪst/ | adj. n. |描述主义的；描述主义者 | Relating to or characteristic of descriptivism, a nonjudgmental approach to language that focuses on how it is actually used, rather than how it should be used. |  Example: A descriptionist would accept "irregardless" as a word because people use it, even though it's considered incorrect by prescriptivists. 描述主义者会接受“irregardless”作为一个单词，因为人们使用它，即使它被规定主义者认为是不正确的。

| Example:  The descriptionist approach to grammar focuses on observing and recording actual language use. 描述主义的语法方法侧重于观察和记录实际的语言使用。 | Near synonyms:  non-prescriptive, liberal,  | Antonyms: prescriptive, traditionalist | adjective, noun
