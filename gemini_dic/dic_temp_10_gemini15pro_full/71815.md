fleecing:/ˈfliːsɪŋ/| n. |敲诈，诈骗|The act of cheating someone out of money or other valuable possessions.|They were accused of fleecing investors out of millions of dollars. 他们被指控诈骗投资者数百万美元。|近义词：swindling, defrauding, cheating, exploiting|反义词: donating, giving|noun

fleeced:/fliːst/ | adj. |被敲诈的，被骗的|Having been cheated or swindled out of money or possessions.|The tourists felt fleeced after paying exorbitant prices for souvenirs.  游客们在为纪念品支付了过高的价格后感到被敲诈了。|近义词：cheated, swindled, defrauded|反义词:  protected, safeguarded|adjective

fleece:/fliːs/ | v. |敲诈，欺骗|To cheat someone out of money or other valuable possessions.|The con artist fleeced the elderly woman out of her life savings.  骗子骗走了老妇人的毕生积蓄。|近义词：swindle, defraud, cheat, exploit|反义词: donate, give|verb

词源: From Old English *flȳs, from Proto-Germanic *flausą (“fleece”). Cognate with Old Frisian flūs (“fleece”), Dutch vlies (“fleece”), German Vlies (“fleece”).
