eachwhere: /ˈiːtʃweər/ | adv. | 各处，到处 | Not a standard English word.  It seems to be a blend of "everywhere" and "each."  It is not commonly used and might be considered dialectical or non-standard. |  (Not a standard usage, so no genuine example exists. A contrived example could be: "Eachwhere I looked, there were flowers," intending to mean "Everywhere I looked, there were flowers.") | 近义词: everywhere, throughout | 反义词: nowhere | 词性: adverb


词源:  A blend of "each" and "everywhere."  "Each" comes from Old English "ǣlc." "Everywhere" comes from Old English "ǣghwǣr."
