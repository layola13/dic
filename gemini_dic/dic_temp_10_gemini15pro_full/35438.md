co-regency:/ˌkəʊˈrɪːdʒənsi/
|n.|共同摄政；共治；联合执政|A period of joint rule by two or more monarchs.|The co-regency of William and Mary marked a turning point in English history.|joint reign, dual monarchy|sole reign|noun
Etymology: From 'co-' (together) + 'regency' (rule during the absence or incapacity of a monarch), ultimately from Latin 'regere' (to rule).
