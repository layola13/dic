haematoin:/ˌhiːməˈtoʊɪn/ /ˌheməˈtoʊɪn/| n. |血红素，高铁血红素|A brownish-black pigment formed by oxidation of heme and containing iron in the ferric state.  It is the form in which iron is stored in tissues.  |The malaria parasite converts heme into haematoin, which is non-toxic. 疟原虫将血红素转化为无毒的血红素。|Synonyms: ferriheme, hematin|Antonyms: N/A|noun

haematin:/ˈhiːmətɪn/ /ˈhemətɪn/| n. |血红素，正铁血红素|A bluish-black or brownish-black derivative of hemoglobin formed by oxidation.  It is used medicinally to treat porphyria. |Haematin is administered intravenously to treat acute attacks of porphyria. 血红素静脉注射用于治疗急性卟啉症发作。|Synonyms: ferriheme, haematoin|Antonyms: N/A|noun
