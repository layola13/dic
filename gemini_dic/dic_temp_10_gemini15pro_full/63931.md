epipodiale:/ɛpɪˈpoʊdiˌeɪl/ | n. | 附肢骨; 跗跖骨; 腕掌骨 | A bone in the distal portion of the vertebrate limb, specifically those of the wrist (carpus) and ankle (tarsus).  It refers to the bones between the more proximal limb bones (radius/ulna and tibia/fibula respectively) and the metapodials (hand and foot bones).  | The epipodiale of the horse is particularly susceptible to injury. 马的附肢骨特别容易受伤。|  The epipodiale plays a crucial role in the flexibility of the wrist. 附肢骨在腕部的灵活性中起着至关重要的作用。 | carpal, tarsal, metapodial | proximal | noun
