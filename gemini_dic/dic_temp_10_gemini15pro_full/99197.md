interclasp: /ˌɪntərˈklæsp/ | v. n. |互相紧握，扣住，交握 | To link or join together closely, especially by clasping or intertwining. | The lovers interclasped their hands as they walked along the beach. 恋人们沿着海滩散步时互相紧握着手。 |  The vines interclasped, forming a dense canopy. 藤蔓互相交织，形成了茂密的树冠。| 近义词: interlock, intertwine, entwine, clasp | 反义词: separate, unclasp, disentangle | 词性: 动词, 名词

词源:  From inter- + clasp. "Inter-" is a prefix meaning "between," "among," "mutually," or "reciprocally." "Clasp" comes from Middle English claspen, from Old English *clyppan, of Germanic origin.
