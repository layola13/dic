antistrophe:/ænˈtɪstrəfi/ | n. | 反strophe; 回旋歌; | A part of a Greek choral ode answering a previous strophe, sung by the chorus when returning from left to right.  In rhetoric, it's the repetition of the same word or phrase at the end of successive clauses. |  Example 1: The antistrophe in Sophocles' play echoed the themes of the strophe.  索福克勒斯戏剧中的回旋歌呼应了前strophe的主题。

Example 2: The speaker used antistrophe, repeating the phrase "never again" to emphasize his point. 演讲者使用了回旋歌，重复“永不再”这个短语来强调他的观点。 |  Synonym:  epistrophe | Antonym: strophe | noun
