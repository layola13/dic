call-ups:/ˈkɔːl ʌps/ (US), /ˈkɔːl ʌps/ (UK)| n.|征召，召集，调遣|An instance of calling someone up, especially for military service or to join a sports team.|The team had several call-ups from the minor leagues due to injuries.  该队由于伤病从低级别联赛中征召了几名球员。|Synonyms: summons, drafting, mobilization|Antonyms: demobilization, discharge|noun

call-ups:/ˈkɔːl ʌps/ (US), /ˈkɔːl ʌps/ (UK)| n.|晋升，提升|An instance of being promoted or elevated to a higher position or level.|He received a call-up to the senior management team. 他被提升到高级管理团队。|Synonyms: promotion, elevation, advancement|Antonyms: demotion, downgrade|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrasal verb "call up," composed of the verb "call" (Old English *ceallian, from Proto-Germanic *kallōną) and the adverb/preposition "up" (Old English up, uppe, from Proto-Germanic *upp).  The noun form "call-up" emerged later, likely in the 19th century.
