buccan:/ˈbʌkən/| n.|海盗，掠夺者|A pirate, especially one operating in the Caribbean during the 17th century. |例句：The buccans plundered Spanish ships for gold.  这些海盗掠夺西班牙船只上的黄金。

例句：Stories of buccans and buried treasure are popular. 关于海盗和埋藏宝藏的故事很受欢迎。|近义词：pirate, corsair, freebooter, privateer|反义词: sailor, mariner (普通水手,非海盗)|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *boucanier*, "one who smokes meat," from boucan, "wooden frame for smoking meat," borrowed from Tupi *moka'en*.  The term originally referred to French hunters in the West Indies who cured meat on such frames. These hunters later turned to piracy, giving the word its modern meaning.
