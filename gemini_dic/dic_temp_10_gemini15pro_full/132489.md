nondescript:/ˌnɒndɪˈskrɪpt/ | adj. n. | 平凡的，不引人注目的；难以形容的人或物 | Lacking distinctive or remarkable features; dull and uninteresting.  Can also refer to a person or thing that is difficult to describe because of a lack of noteworthy characteristics. | The building was a nondescript gray block. 这栋建筑是一个不起眼的灰色街区。 | The witness could only give a nondescript description of the thief. 目击者只能对小偷进行模糊的描述。 | synonyms: ordinary, unremarkable, commonplace, characterless, featureless, undistinguished | antonyms: distinctive, remarkable, striking, memorable, unusual, unique | adjective/noun

nondescripts:/ˌnɒndɪˈskrɪpts/ | n. | 平凡的人或物 | People or things that are nondescript. | The street was lined with nondescripts. 街道两旁都是些不起眼的东西。 |  The guests were a collection of nondescripts. 客人们是一群不起眼的人。| synonyms: nobodies, unknowns | antonyms: VIPs, celebrities, notables | noun
