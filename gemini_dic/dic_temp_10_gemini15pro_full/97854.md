in league:/ɪn liːɡ/
adj. |勾结的，沆瀣一气的| secretly working with someone else, especially for some dishonest purpose |The two companies were suspected of being in league to fix prices.  这两家公司被怀疑串通操纵价格。| in cahoots, colluding, conspiring |against, opposed


Etymology:
From Middle English *in lige, from Old French en lige "in allegiance, under obligation," from lige "liege, loyal, bound by oath," of Germanic origin. The sense shifted from "bound by oath" to "allied" and then to the modern sense of "secretly allied for a dishonest purpose."
