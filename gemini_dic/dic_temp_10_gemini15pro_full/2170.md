ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English 'nædre wyrt', literally 'snake plant'.  'Nædre' (snake) + 'wyrt' (plant, herb).  The name likely arose from the belief that the plant could be used to treat snakebites, or from its appearance (perhaps the flower spike resembling a snake's tongue).
