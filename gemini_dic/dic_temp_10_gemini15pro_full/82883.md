gulper:/ˈɡʌlpər/ | n. |吞食者；大嘴鱼；暴食者|A fish with a large mouth and expandable stomach, capable of swallowing prey larger than itself.  Also, someone who eats or drinks quickly or in large amounts.| The deep-sea gulper can swallow prey much larger than itself. 深海大嘴鱼可以吞下比自己大得多的猎物。| synonyms: glutton, gorger, devourer | antonyms: nibbler | noun


gulper:/ˈɡʌlpər/ | adj. | (非正式) 巨大的，庞大的| (Informal) Extremely large.  |  He took a gulper breath before diving into the cold water. 他在跳入冰冷的水中之前深吸了一口气。| synonyms: enormous, huge, gigantic | antonyms: tiny, small, minuscule | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "gulp" (to swallow large amounts quickly), which comes from the Middle Dutch "gulpen".
