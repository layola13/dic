cleat: /kliːt/ | n. |防滑钉，夹板，系缆桩 |A metal or plastic projection on the sole of a shoe or boot, used to provide extra grip, especially on soft or slippery surfaces. Also, a piece of wood or metal with two projecting horns used for fastening ropes or cables. |The football player's cleats dug into the muddy field.  足球运动员的防滑钉扎进了泥泞的场地。|  The sailor secured the rope to the cleat on the dock. 水手把绳子系在码头的系缆桩上。|Synonyms: spike, stud, crampon | Antonyms: smooth | noun


cleat: /kliːt/ | v. |用夹板固定，系住(缆绳) | To fasten with a cleat. |He cleated the rope to the deck. 他把绳子用夹板固定在甲板上。|She cleated her cycling shoes before the race.  她在比赛前系好自行车鞋。|Synonyms: secure, fasten, tie |Antonyms: unfasten, release | verb
