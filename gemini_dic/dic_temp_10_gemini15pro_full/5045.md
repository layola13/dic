all-of-a-sudden:/ɔːl əv ə ˈsʌdn/| adv.|突然地，出乎意料地|Suddenly, without warning.  It describes something happening unexpectedly and quickly.|例句：All of a sudden, the lights went out. 突然，灯灭了。|近义词：suddenly, unexpectedly, abruptly|反义词:gradually, slowly|词性:副词

Etymology:  "All of a sudden" is a phrase that evolved over time.  "Sudden" comes from the Old French word "soudain," which itself derives from the Latin "subitaneus," meaning "coming quickly or stealthily."  The phrase "all of a sudden" adds emphasis to the unexpected nature of the event.
