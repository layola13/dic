forensic:/fəˈrɛnzɪk/ | adj. | 法医的，法庭的，适于法庭的 | relating to or denoting the application of scientific methods and techniques to the investigation of crime. | 例句1：Forensic evidence was used to convict the murderer. 法医证据被用来给凶手定罪。

例句2：He works in the forensic science department. 他在法医科学部门工作。 | 近义词：legal, judicial, medicolegal | 反义词：N/A | 词性: adjective

forensics:/fəˈrɛnzɪks/ | n. | 法医学，辩论术 | scientific tests or techniques used in connection with the detection of crime.  Also: the art or study of formal debate. | 例句1：Forensics played a crucial role in solving the mystery. 法医学在解开谜团方面发挥了至关重要的作用。

例句2：She's studying forensics at university. 她在大学学习法医学。 | 近义词：criminology | 反义词：N/A | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *forensis* "of the forum, public," from *forum* "marketplace, forum." Originally referred to legal proceedings conducted in public.  The modern sense, related to scientific analysis of evidence for legal purposes, emerged in the 18th century.
