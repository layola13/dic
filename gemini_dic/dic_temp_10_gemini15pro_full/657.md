abrogate:/ˈæbrəɡeɪt/| vt.|废除，取消|To formally put an end to (a law, agreement, or custom).|The treaty abrogates all previous agreements.  该条约废除所有以前的协议。

The new law abrogates the old one. 新法律废除了旧法律。|近义词：repeal, revoke, annul, nullify, abolish|反义词：enact, establish, institute, ratify|词性：动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government abrogates the law. = 政府废除该法律。
He abrogates his responsibility as a father. = 他放弃了作为父亲的责任。

一般过去时:
The king abrogated the treaty. = 国王废除了条约。
They abrogated their contract last year. = 他们去年解除了合同。

现在完成时:
The company has abrogated the agreement. = 公司已经废除了协议。
Parliament has abrogated several outdated laws. = 议会已经废除了几项过时的法律。

过去完成时:
The council had abrogated the bylaw before the protests began. = 在抗议开始之前，委员会已经废除了该附则。
They had abrogated the contract before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期之前废除了合同。

将来时:
The committee will abrogate the rule next month. = 委员会下个月将废除该规则。
They will abrogate the treaty if the other side doesn't comply. = 如果对方不遵守，他们将废除条约。

虚拟语气:
If they abrogated the law, it would cause chaos. = 如果他们废除法律，将会造成混乱。
I wish they would abrogate this ridiculous policy. = 我希望他们能废除这项荒谬的政策。

被动语态:
The law was abrogated by the government. = 该法律被政府废除。
The agreement has been abrogated. = 协议已被废除。
