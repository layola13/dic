charlatanism:/ˈʃɑːrlətənɪzəm/| n. |江湖骗术；招摇撞骗|The practice of pretending to have more skill or knowledge than one possesses. |His claims of being able to cure any disease were pure charlatanism. 他声称能治好任何疾病，纯属江湖骗术。|Synonyms: quackery, fraud, deception, imposture|Antonyms: genuineness, authenticity, honesty|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *charlatanisme*, from Italian *ciarlatanismo*, from *ciarlatano* ("charlatan"), from *ciarlare* ("to chatter"), of imitative origin.
