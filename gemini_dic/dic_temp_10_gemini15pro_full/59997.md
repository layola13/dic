editorialising:/ˌedɪˈtɔːriəlaɪzɪŋ/
| n. |评论化；发表社论式意见|The act of expressing personal opinions or biases, often in a way that promotes a particular point of view, within a supposedly objective piece of writing or reporting.|He was accused of editorialising in his news reports.|Synonyms: commenting, opining, moralizing, sermonizing|Antonyms: reporting, stating, recounting|noun

editorialise:/ˌedɪˈtɔːriəlaɪz/
| v. |评论化；发表社论式意见|To express personal opinions or biases, often in a way that promotes a particular point of view, within a supposedly objective piece of writing or reporting.|The reporter was careful not to editorialise in the article.|Synonyms: comment, opine, moralize, sermonize|Antonyms: report, state, recount|verb
