digest:/daɪˈdʒɛst/ /dɪˈdʒɛst/| v. n.|消化；领悟；摘要|To break down food in the stomach and intestines so that the body can use it.  To think about something carefully in order to understand it.|例句：It took me a while to digest all the information. 我花了一段时间才消化所有信息。|近义词：absorb, assimilate, comprehend|反义词: misunderstand, reject|词性:动词

digest:/ˈdaɪdʒɛst/| n.|摘要；文摘|A summary of a longer piece of writing.|例句：The monthly digest summarizes important articles.  月刊文摘总结了重要的文章。|近义词：summary, abstract, compendium|反义词: expansion, elaboration|词性:名词

词源: Late Middle English: from Latin digest- ‘separated, arranged’, from the verb digerere, from dis- ‘apart’ + gerere ‘carry’.  The original sense was ‘to distribute or arrange methodically’, later ‘to absorb (food)’ and ‘to assimilate (information) mentally’.
