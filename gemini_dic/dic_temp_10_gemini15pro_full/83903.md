half-bald:/ˌhæf ˈbɔːld/ | adj. | 半秃的，部分秃头的 | Partially bald; having lost some but not all of one's hair. | He's starting to go half-bald.  他开始变得半秃了。 |  My uncle is half-bald and wears a hat. 我叔叔半秃，戴着帽子。 | 近义词: partly bald, thinning | 反义词: thick-haired, hirsute | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
"half" + "bald"

"half" (Old English healf) meaning "one of two equal parts."
"bald" (Old English beald) originally meant "shining, white," later coming to refer to a white or bare patch on the head, and eventually to hairlessness in general.
