doler:/doˈler/| v. | 疼痛，悲伤 | To feel physical or emotional pain.  This verb is primarily used in Spanish and Portuguese. |  Me duele la cabeza. (My head hurts.) / Me duele mucho su partida. (Their departure pains me a lot.) | 近义词: ache, hurt, grieve, sorrow | 反义词: soothe, comfort | 词性: verb
Etymology: From Latin *dolēre, present active infinitive of doleō (“I grieve, feel pain”), from Proto-Italic *dolēō, from Proto-Indo-European *dōl-ē- (“to feel pain”).

(While "doler" doesn't form phrases in the same way English verbs do, here are some common constructions using the verb):

¿Te duele? = Does it hurt?

¿Dónde te duele? = Where does it hurt?

Me duele mucho. = It hurts a lot.

No me duele nada. = It doesn't hurt at all.

Ya no me duele. = It doesn't hurt anymore.
