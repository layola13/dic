mooching:/muːtʃɪŋ/| n. vi.|揩油，蹭吃蹭喝，闲逛|The act of spending time in a place or with someone, often without a clear purpose, and sometimes getting food or other things without paying for them.  It can also refer to borrowing small amounts of money frequently without repaying.|He's always mooching around the kitchen, hoping someone will offer him food. 他总是晃荡在厨房附近，希望有人能给他点吃的。|Synonyms: scrounging, sponging, loitering, hanging around|Antonyms: working, earning, providing, contributing|verb, noun

mooch:/muːtʃ/| vi. vt.|揩油，蹭吃蹭喝，闲逛|To get something (like food or money) from someone else without paying or working for it; to wander or loiter.|Can I mooch a cigarette off you?  我可以蹭你一根烟吗？|Synonyms: scrounge, sponge, loiter, hang around|Antonyms: work, earn, provide, contribute|verb
