disincentivizing: /dɪsɪnˈsɛntɪvaɪzɪŋ/ | v. (present participle) | 使失去动力；抑制；劝退 |  The act of discouraging someone from doing something, typically by making it less attractive or rewarding. |  High taxes can be disincentivizing to investors. 高税收可能会抑制投资者的积极性。 |  deterring, discouraging, inhibiting |  encouraging, incentivizing, motivating | verb

disincentive: /ˌdɪsɪnˈsɛntɪv/ | n. | 阻碍因素；抑制因素；劝退因素 | Something that discourages a particular action or behaviour. |  The fear of punishment acts as a disincentive to crime.  对惩罚的恐惧是抑制犯罪的一个因素。 | deterrent, obstacle, impediment |  incentive, encouragement, motivation | noun
