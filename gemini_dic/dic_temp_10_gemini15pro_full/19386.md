bicameralism:/ˌbaɪˈkæmərəlɪzəm/| n.|两院制，二院制|The principle or system of having two legislative chambers or houses, like a Senate and a House of Representatives.|The United States operates under bicameralism, with the Senate and the House of Representatives.|Synonyms: two-chamber system|Antonyms: unicameralism|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From bi- (two) + camera (chamber) + -ism (doctrine, system)
