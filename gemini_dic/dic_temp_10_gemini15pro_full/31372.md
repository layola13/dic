charbroiling:/ˈtʃɑːrˌbrɔɪlɪŋ/
|n.|炭火烧烤|The act of grilling food over a charcoal fire, giving it a distinctive smoky flavor.  It involves cooking food quickly over high heat, searing the outside while leaving the inside tender.|
|例句：Charbroiling gives the steak a wonderful smoky flavor. 炭火烧烤赋予牛排美妙的烟熏味。|
|例句：We enjoyed the charbroiling of burgers and hot dogs at the picnic. 我们在野餐时享用了炭火烤制的汉堡和热狗。|
|近义词：grilling, barbecuing|
|反义词：boiling, frying|
|词性：名词|


charbroil:/ˈtʃɑːrˌbrɔɪl/
|v.|炭火烧烤|To cook food by grilling it over a charcoal fire. This method imparts a smoky flavor and creates attractive grill marks.|
|例句：I'm going to charbroil some chicken for dinner. 我打算用炭火烤些鸡肉做晚餐。|
|例句：They charbroiled the vegetables to perfection. 他们把蔬菜烤得恰到好处。|
|近义词：grill, barbecue|
|反义词：boil, fry|
|词性：动词|


charbroiled:/ˈtʃɑːrˌbrɔɪld/
|adj.|炭火烧烤的| Cooked over a charcoal fire.  Describes food that has been prepared using the charbroiling method.|
|例句：The charbroiled steak was juicy and flavorful. 炭火烤的牛排多汁又美味。|
|例句：We ordered a charbroiled burger with all the fixings. 我们点了一个配料齐全的炭火烤汉堡。|
|近义词：grilled, barbecued|
|反义词：boiled, fried|
|词性：形容词|



词源：
"char" + "broil".  "Char" comes from Old North French "charrer" meaning "to turn to charcoal."  "Broil" comes from Old French "bruillir"  meaning "to roast on the embers."
