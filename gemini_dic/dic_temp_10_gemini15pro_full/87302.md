hermitisms:/ˈhɜːrmɪtɪzəmz/ | n. |隐居生活; 隐士的言行; 遁世思想|The practice of living as a hermit; the beliefs or pronouncements of a hermit.|He retreated into the mountains, embracing hermitisms and solitude. 他隐居到山里，拥抱隐居生活和孤独。|  Hermitisms offered him a refuge from the complexities of the world. 隐居生活为他提供了一个远离尘世喧嚣的避难所。|synonyms: seclusion, solitude, isolation|antonyms: sociability, gregariousness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *heremitismus, from Latin eremita (“hermit”), from Ancient Greek ἐρημίτης (erēmítēs, “desert-dweller”), from ἔρημος (érēmos, “desert, lonely”).
