concession's: /kənˈseʃənz/ | n. |让步，妥协，优惠，特许权|Something granted or yielded, often in response to demands; a thing conceded.  The act of conceding or yielding, as a right, a privilege, or a point or fact in an argument.|

例句：The company made several concessions during the negotiations.  公司在谈判过程中做出了几项让步。

例句：The government granted tax concessions to encourage investment. 政府给予税收优惠以鼓励投资。


近义词：compromise, allowance, adjustment, grant, privilege|
反义词：insistence, resistance, denial|
词性：名词 (Noun)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *consessioun, from Old French concession, from Latin concessio (“a yielding, granting”), from concedere (“to yield, concede”), from com- (“together”) + cedere (“to go, yield”).
