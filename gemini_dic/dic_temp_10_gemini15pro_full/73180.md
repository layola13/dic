forbiddingnesses:/fərˈbɪdɪŋnəsəz/| n. |令人生畏的特质；令人却步的特点；森严；险恶|The quality of being forbidding; a forbidding appearance or atmosphere.  The plural form of "forbiddingness".|The forbiddingnesses of the castle walls kept most people away.|近义词：severity, austerity, grimness, inhospitability|反义词：welcomingness, friendliness, warmth, accessibility|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From forbidding + -ness.  Forbidding comes from the Old English verb *forbeodan*, meaning "to prohibit."  The suffix *-ness* is used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
