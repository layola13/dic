floats:/floʊts/ (US) /fləʊts/ (UK)| n. v.|漂浮物，浮标，浮子；漂浮，传播|Something that floats on a liquid surface; the act of floating or remaining suspended in a liquid or gas. |The child's toy boat floats in the bathtub.  孩子的玩具船漂浮在浴缸里。|The smell of baking bread floats through the house. 烤面包的香味飘满了整个房子。|近义词：drifts, glides, sails|反义词:sinks, submerges|词性:noun, verb
float:/floʊt/ (US) /fləʊt/ (UK)| v.|漂浮，浮动；提出，提议|To rest or move on or near the surface of a liquid without sinking; to suggest an idea or plan. |The balloon floated gently in the breeze. 气球在微风中轻轻飘动。|She floated the idea of a picnic. 她提议去野餐。|近义词：drift, hover|反义词:sink, plunge|词性:verb
