electrifying:/ɪˈlɛktrɪfaɪɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人兴奋的；激动人心的；震撼的 |  Causing a sudden thrill of excitement; thrilling.  | Her performance was electrifying. 她的表演令人振奋。| synonyms: thrilling, exciting, stimulating, exhilarating, invigorating | antonyms: boring, dull, tedious, monotonous, vapid | adjective

electrifying:/ɪˈlɛktrɪfaɪɪŋ/ | adj. | 通电的；充电的 |  Supplied with or transmitting electric current. |  The third rail is electrifying.  第三轨带电。| synonyms: powered, charged, energized | antonyms: unpowered, discharged, de-energized | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From electrify + -ing.  Electrify comes from the French *electrifier*, from electric + -fier (from Latin -ficare,  meaning "to make, to do").  Ultimately from Latin electrum "amber" and Greek ēlektron.
