nobles:/ˈnoʊblz/| n.|贵族；高尚的人；|People of noble birth often felt a responsibility to those less fortunate. 出身高贵的人常常觉得有责任帮助那些不幸的人。|The nobles lived in grand castles and enjoyed lavish lifestyles. 贵族们住在宏伟的城堡里，过着奢华的生活。|近义词：aristocrats, lords, peerage|反义词：commoners, peasants|词性：名词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *nobles* (plural of *noble*), from Latin *nobilis* "well-known, famous, high-born, excellent," literally "knowable," from *noscere* "to come to know."  The semantic development was likely from "well-known" to "renowned" to "high-ranking" and "noble."
