horror:/ˈhɔːrər/ (US) /ˈhɒrə(r)/ (UK)| n. | 恐怖，恐惧；令人恐怖的事物 | An intense feeling of fear, shock, and revulsion.  A thing that causes such a feeling. |  She stared in horror at the scene unfolding before her. 她惊恐地看着眼前发生的这一幕。 The horror of the war stayed with him for years. 战争的恐怖在他心中留存多年。 |  fear, dread, terror, fright |  calm, peace, tranquility | noun

horror:/ˈhɔːrər/ (US) /ˈhɒrə(r)/ (UK)| adj. (informal) |  可怕的，令人恐惧的 | Used to emphasize the bad quality of something. | The traffic was absolutely horror this morning. 今早的交通真是糟透了。(informal usage)  It was a horror movie, full of jump scares. 这是一部恐怖电影，充满了吓人的场景。 |  terrible, awful, dreadful, frightful | wonderful, pleasant, delightful | adjective
