coulda:/ˈkʊdə/| contraction|可能已经，本来可以|Contraction of "could have," used informally.  Expresses possibility in the past or a missed opportunity. |例句：I coulda gone to the party, but I was tired. 我本来可以去参加聚会的，但我太累了。|近义词：could have, might have|反义词:couldn't have|modal verb (contraction)
例句：You coulda told me you were coming! 你本来可以告诉我你要来的！

I coulda done it, but I didn't have time. = 我本来可以做的，但我没有时间。
He coulda been a doctor, but he chose to be a teacher. = 他本来可以做医生的，但他选择做老师。

Not applicable. "Coulda" refers to the past.

虚拟语气:
If I had known, I coulda helped. = 如果我知道的话，我本来可以帮忙的。
I wish I coulda been there. = 我真希望我当时在那里。


被动语态:
The problem coulda been solved. = 这个问题本来是可以解决的。
It coulda been done differently. =  这本来可以做得不一样的。


疑问句:
Coulda you helped me with this? = 你本来可以帮我一下这个吗？ (informal)
Coulda she done anything differently? = 她本来可以做些什么不同的事情吗？ (informal)


否定句:
I couldn't have done it any better.  = 我本来做不了比这更好的了。（注意：否定形式用couldn't have）
She couldn't have known. = 她本来不可能知道的。


PHRASE->
Not commonly used in set phrases. Its informal nature makes it less suitable for fixed expressions.


注意事项:
Coulda is very informal and should not be used in formal writing or speech.  It's a contraction that represents spoken English, not written.  Using "could have" is always preferred in formal contexts.
