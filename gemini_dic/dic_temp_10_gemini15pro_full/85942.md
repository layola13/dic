headmaster:/ˈhedˌmæstər/ (US) /ˈhedˈmɑːstər/ (UK)| n.| 校长|The principal of a private school, especially a British one, typically for boys. |The headmaster addressed the students at the assembly. 校长在集会上向学生们讲话。|synonyms: principal, head teacher| antonyms: (No direct antonym for a job title)| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hed maister*, equivalent to *head* + *master*.  The term reflects the historical tradition of male leadership in education, particularly in private schools.
