footprint:/ˈfʊtprɪnt/| n.|足迹，脚印；影响，痕迹|the mark made by a foot or shoe on a surface; the effect that a person or organization has on the environment|例句：The archaeologists found footprints of dinosaurs. 考古学家发现了恐龙的足迹。

例句：The company is trying to reduce its carbon footprint.  这家公司正在努力减少其碳足迹。|近义词：trace, mark, imprint, impression|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

footprint:/ˈfʊtprɪnt/| n.|（建筑物、设备等的）占地面积|the amount of space on a surface that something covers|例句：The footprint of the new building is much smaller than the old one. 新建筑的占地面积比旧的小得多。

例句：The server has a small footprint, making it ideal for small businesses. 这台服务器占地面积小，非常适合小型企业。|近义词：area, size, dimensions|反义词: N/A|词性:noun
