brutalists:/ˈbruːtəlɪsts/
n.|野兽派艺术家，野兽派建筑师|Adherents of Brutalism, an architectural style characterized by the use of raw concrete and modular elements.|The brutalists of the 1960s favored massive, imposing structures.|近义词:architects, designers|反义词:traditionalists, classicists|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *brutaliste*, from *brut* (“raw, crude”) (ultimately from Latin *brūtus* (“dull, senseless, irrational, brute”)) + *-iste* (suffix forming agent nouns).
