arrogantly: /ˈærəɡəntli/ | adv. | 傲慢地，自大地 | In a way that shows a feeling of superiority and contempt for others. | He arrogantly dismissed their concerns. 他傲慢地驳回了他们的担忧。|She tossed her hair arrogantly and walked away. 她傲慢地甩了甩头发，走开了。| 近义词: haughtily, disdainfully, superciliously, presumptuously | 反义词: humbly, modestly, respectfully | 词性: 副词

arrogant: /ˈærəɡənt/ | adj. | 傲慢的，自大的 | Having or revealing an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities. | He was an arrogant young man. 他是一个傲慢的年轻人。| She has an arrogant disregard for other people's opinions. 她傲慢地无视别人的意见。 | 近义词: haughty, disdainful, supercilious, presumptuous | 反义词: humble, modest, respectful | 词性: 形容词

arrogancy: /ˈærəɡənsi/ | n. | 傲慢，自大 | The quality of being arrogant. | His arrogance alienated his colleagues. 他的傲慢疏远了他的同事。|Her arrogance knew no bounds. 她的傲慢无止境。| 近义词: haughtiness, disdain, superciliousness, presumption | 反义词: humility, modesty, respect | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Late Latin *arrogant-, from Latin arrogant- ‘claiming for oneself’, present participle of arrogare, from ad- ‘to’ + rogare ‘ask’.
