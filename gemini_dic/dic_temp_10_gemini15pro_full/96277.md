indicator:/ˈɪndɪkeɪtər/ | n. | 指示器，指标，标志 |Something that shows or suggests the state or level of something. | The economic indicators suggest a recession is coming. 经济指标表明经济衰退即将到来。| synonym: sign, signal, measure, gauge | antonym:  concealment,  obscuration | noun

indicator:/ˈɪndɪkeɪtər/ | n. | 指示剂 | A substance that changes color to show the presence or absence of a particular chemical. | Litmus paper is an indicator of acidity. 石蕊试纸是酸度指示剂。 | synonym: reagent, marker | antonym: N/A | noun

indicators:/ˈɪndɪkeɪtərz/ | n. | 指示器（复数），指标（复数），标志（复数）| Plural form of "indicator". |  Several indicators suggest the company is in financial trouble.  几项指标表明该公司财务状况不佳。| synonym: signs, signals, measures, gauges | antonym: concealments, obscurations | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *indicatorem, nominative of *indicator, from Latin indicatus, past participle of indicare "to point out, show, indicate," from in- "in, on" + dicare "to proclaim, dedicate," from PIE root *deik- "to show," also "to pronounce solemnly."
