consuetudinary: /ˌkɒn.s(j)uːˈɛ.tʃʊ.dɪ.nər.i/ /ˌkɑːn.suːˈɛ.tʃuː.dɪ.ˌnɛr.i/
adjective
习惯的；惯例的；习俗上的
Relating to or established by custom; customary.
The consuetudinary laws of the tribe dictated their social interactions.  部落的习惯法规定了他们的社会交往。
New members of the club are expected to follow the consuetudinary practices.  俱乐部的新成员应该遵循惯例。
近义词: customary, traditional, usual, conventional, habitual
反义词: unconventional, unusual, atypical, unorthodox
词性: adjective

consuetudinary: /ˌkɒn.s(j)uːˈɛ.tʃʊ.dɪ.nər.i/ /ˌkɑːn.suːˈɛ.tʃuː.dɪ.ˌnɛr.i/
noun
惯例；习俗；仪式书
A book containing the forms and ceremonies used in a religious or other rite.
The consuetudinary of the monastery outlined the daily rituals of the monks.  修道院的仪式书概述了僧侣们的日常仪式。
Researchers studied the ancient consuetudinary to understand the historical practices.  研究人员研究了古代仪式书，以了解历史习俗。
近义词: custom, ritual, tradition, practice, ceremony
反义词: innovation, change, novelty
词性: noun


词源: From Late Latin *consuetudinarius, from Latin consuetudo (“custom, habit”), from consuescere (“to accustom”), from con- (“together, with”) + suescere (“to be accustomed, become used to”).
