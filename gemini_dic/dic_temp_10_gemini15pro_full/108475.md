lay-on-the-line:/ˌleɪ ɒn ðə ˈlaɪn/
lay-on-the-line: idiom
lay-on-the-line: 中文翻译：坦白地说，直截了当地说
lay-on-the-line: 英文解析：To be completely honest and direct; to reveal something frankly, often something unpleasant or risky.  To speak honestly and frankly about one's feelings or intentions.
lay-on-the-line: 例句：I laid it on the line with him – either he improved his performance or he was fired. 我跟他直截了当地说了——要么他改进他的表现，要么他被解雇。

lay-on-the-line: 例句：She laid her feelings on the line and told him she loved him. 她坦率地表达了自己的感受，告诉他她爱他。
lay-on-the-line: 近义词：be frank, be honest, be upfront, tell it like it is, come clean, level with
lay-on-the-line: 反义词：be evasive, beat around the bush, be indirect, conceal, hide
lay-on-the-line: 词性: idiom (idiomatic phrase)
