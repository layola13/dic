narcotically:/nɑːrˈkɑːtɪkli/| adv.|麻醉地，昏昏沉沉地|In a way that relates to or produces narcosis (a state of stupor, drowsiness, or unconsciousness).|She spoke slowly and narcotically, as if heavily medicated. 她说话缓慢而麻木，像是吃了很多药。|The drug acted narcotically, inducing a deep sleep. 这种药物起到了麻醉作用，使人沉睡。|近义词：drowsily, sleepily, lethargically|反义词：alertly, energetically, wakefully|词性:副词

narcotic:/nɑːrˈkɑːtɪk/| n. adj.|麻醉剂，麻醉的|n. A drug or other substance affecting mood or behavior and sold for nonmedical purposes, especially an illegal one.|adj. Relating to or denoting narcotics or their effects.|n. Heroin is a highly addictive narcotic. 海洛因是一种高度成瘾的麻醉剂。|adj. The narcotic effect of the drug was immediate. 药物的麻醉效果是立竿见影的。|近义词：(n.) opiate, analgesic, painkiller (adj.) soporific, anesthetic|反义词：(adj.) stimulating, invigorating|词性:名词，形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *narcoticus, from Greek narkōtikos "benumbing, narcotic," from narkoun "to benumb," from narkē "numbness, stupor."
