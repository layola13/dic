fanaticize:/fəˈnætɪsaɪz/
verb
使狂热，使着迷
to make someone become a fanatic; to cause someone to become excessively enthusiastic or zealously devoted to something
The cult leader fanaticized his followers with promises of eternal life.  邪教领袖用永生的承诺使他的追随者变得狂热。
The media can sometimes fanaticize public opinion about minor issues.  媒体有时会使公众对一些小问题的意见变得狂热。
近义词: obsess, infatuate, indoctrinate
反义词: moderate, temper, calm
词性: v.

Etymology:  From fanatic + -ize.  Fanatic comes from Late Latin fanaticus "inspired by a deity, frenzied," from Latin fanum "temple."
