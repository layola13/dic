conceptualised:/kənˈsɛptʃuəlaɪzd/ | v. | 使概念化，将…概念化 | To form a concept or idea of something; to think of something in terms of its general characteristics or essential features. | The artist conceptualised the sculpture as a symbol of hope.  这位艺术家将这座雕塑概念化为希望的象征。| conceptualize, formulate, devise |  - | verb
Etymology: From conceptualize + -ed (past participle suffix).  Conceptualize comes from concept + -ize (verb-forming suffix). Concept ultimately derives from Latin conceptus ("a conceiving, thing conceived"), past participle of concipere ("to take in, conceive").
