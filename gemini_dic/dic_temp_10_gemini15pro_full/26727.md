cadgers:/ˈkædʒərz/ (UK) /ˈkædʒərz/ (US)| n.|乞丐; 寄生虫; 白吃白喝的人|People who get things from others without paying or working for them, often by persuading them to feel sorry for them.|The street was full of cadgers begging for money.  街上到处都是乞丐在乞讨钱财。
He's a cadger, always borrowing money and never paying it back. 他是个白吃白喝的人，总是借钱不还。|近义词：beggar, scrounger, freeloader, sponger|反义词：giver, philanthropist, benefactor|词性:名词

cadger:/ˈkædʒər/ (UK) /ˈkædʒər/ (US)| v.|乞讨; 寄生; 白吃白喝|To ask for or get something from someone without paying or working for it, often by persuading them to feel sorry for you.|He cadged a cigarette from a stranger. 他向一个陌生人要了根烟。
She's always cadging lifts to work. 她总是蹭车去上班。|近义词：beg, scrounge, freeload, sponge|反义词：give, donate, contribute|词性:动词
