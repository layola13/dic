allurers:/əˈlʊrərz/
|n.|诱惑者，吸引者|People who tempt or attract others, often by offering something desirable or exciting.|The allurers of the city nightlife drew her in.  城市的夜生活诱惑着她。|近义词:tempters, charmers, seducers, enticers|反义词:repellers, deterrents|词性:名词

词源:
From the verb "allure," which comes from Old French "alurer" (to lure, entice), from the prefix "a-" (to) + "lure" (bait, decoy). Ultimately from Germanic origin.
