decayers:/dɪˈkeɪərz/
n.
腐朽者，衰败者
Those who cause or undergo decay; things that decay.
The decayers of society are those who promote violence and corruption.  社会的腐败者是那些宣扬暴力和腐败的人。
Old, abandoned buildings are often home to decayers like fungi and insects.  古老的废弃建筑通常是真菌和昆虫等腐朽者的家园。
近义词:decomposers, deteriorators, putrefiers
反义词:builders, creators, preservers
词性:名词(plural)


decay:/dɪˈkeɪ/
v.
腐烂，腐朽
To rot or decompose; to decline in health, strength, or vigor.
The fruit began to decay after being left out in the sun. 水果在太阳下暴晒后开始腐烂。
The old wooden bridge slowly decayed over time.  古老的木桥随着时间的推移慢慢腐烂。
近义词:rot, decompose, deteriorate
反义词:grow, flourish, thrive
词性:动词

n.

The decay of the old building was evident in its crumbling walls.  这栋老建筑的腐烂在其摇摇欲坠的墙壁上显而易见。
Tooth decay can be prevented with proper dental hygiene.  可以通过适当的牙齿卫生来预防蛀牙。
近义词:rot, decomposition, deterioration
反义词:growth, development, improvement
词性:名词
