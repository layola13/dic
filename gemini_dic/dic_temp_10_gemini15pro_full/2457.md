adjuring:/əˈdʒʊərɪŋ/
verb
恳求；告诫；郑重要求
To adjure someone is to earnestly urge or request them to do something, often under oath or with great seriousness.  It can also mean to command solemnly, often under a threat of penalty.
The judge adjured the witness to tell the truth. 法官告诫证人要说实话。

The king adjured his followers to remain loyal. 国王恳求他的追随者保持忠诚。

近义词: beseech, implore, entreat, exhort, urge, command
反义词: dissuade, discourage
词性: verb (动词)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French adjurer, from Latin adjūrāre (“to swear to”), from ad- (“to”) + jūrāre (“to swear”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *yewes- (“law”).
