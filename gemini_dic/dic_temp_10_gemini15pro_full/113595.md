macedonianism: /ˌmæsɪˈdoʊniənɪzəm/ | n. | 马其顿主义 | The advocacy or practice of Macedonianism, which is a nationalist ideology that asserts the existence of a distinct Macedonian ethnicity and language, primarily in North Macedonia and neighboring regions. This ideology is often seen as irredentist by neighboring countries like Greece and Bulgaria, who consider parts of Macedonian identity and history as intrinsically linked to their own. |  The museum's exhibits seemed to promote a form of Macedonianism, focusing solely on a separate Macedonian narrative and ignoring regional interconnectedness. |  nationalism, irredentism |  internationalism, cosmopolitanism | noun
