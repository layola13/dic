interdiffusion:/ˌɪntər dɪˈfjuːʒən/ | n. |相互扩散，互扩散 | The process by which atoms or molecules of different substances mix together as a result of their random thermal motion.  This often occurs in solids, particularly at elevated temperatures. |  The interdiffusion of nickel and chromium is crucial in the formation of certain alloys. 镍和铬的相互扩散对于某些合金的形成至关重要。

High temperatures promote the interdiffusion of elements in the material.  高温促进了材料中元素的相互扩散。| 近义词：diffusion, intermingling | 反义词: segregation | 词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From inter- + diffusion.  "Inter-" is a Latin prefix meaning "between" or "among." "Diffusion" comes from the Latin "diffundere," meaning "to pour out" or "to spread."
