cowardice:/ˈkaʊərdɪs/| n. |怯懦，胆怯|Lack of courage to face danger, difficulty, opposition, pain, etc.|His cowardice prevented him from speaking up. 他的怯懦使他不敢说出自己的想法。|The soldier was court-martialed for cowardice in the face of the enemy.  这名士兵因在敌人面前胆怯而被送上军事法庭。|近义词：timidity, fearfulness, pusillanimity|反义词：bravery, courage, valor|词性: noun

cowardly:/ˈkaʊərdli/| adj. |胆小的，懦弱的|Lacking courage; behaving in a timid or fearful way.|It was a cowardly act to abandon his friends. 抛弃他的朋友是一个懦弱的行为。|He made a cowardly retreat from the battle. 他从战场上懦弱地撤退了。|近义词：timid, fearful, craven|反义词：brave, courageous, valiant|词性: adjective

coward:/ˈkaʊərd/| n. |懦夫，胆小鬼|A person who lacks courage to face danger, difficulty, opposition, pain, etc.|He was branded a coward for refusing to fight. 他因为拒绝战斗而被称为懦夫。|Only a coward would hit a woman. 只有懦夫才会打女人。|近义词：craven, poltroon, dastard|反义词：hero, brave, courageous|词性: noun
