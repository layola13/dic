aerologic: /ˌɛəroʊˈlɒdʒɪk/ | adj. | 航空逻辑的，航空逻辑学的 | Relating to or based on the principles of aerologic, which is the study of the free atmosphere by means of instruments carried aloft by balloons, kites, airplanes, etc. | Aerologic data is crucial for weather forecasting.  航空逻辑数据对于天气预报至关重要。 |  Aerological observations help understand atmospheric conditions. 航空逻辑观测有助于了解大气状况。 | 近义词: atmospheric, meteorological | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | 词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From aero- + logic. Aero- comes from Greek ἀήρ (aēr, “air”).  Logic comes from Greek λογική (logikḗ, “reasoning, rationale”), from λόγος (lógos, “word, reason, speech”).

USAGE->
Aerologic is typically used as an adjective to describe data, observations, studies, or other things related to the study of the free atmosphere using instruments carried aloft. Here are a few examples of its usage in different grammatical contexts:

Attributive Adjective:

Aerologic data is essential for understanding weather patterns. 航空逻辑数据对于理解天气模式至关重要。
Aerologic balloons are used to collect data from the upper atmosphere. 航空逻辑气球用于收集高层大气的数据。


Predicative Adjective:
The study of the atmosphere is aerologic.  对大气的研究是航空逻辑学的。
The methods used were strictly aerologic. 使用的方法严格来说是航空逻辑学的。
