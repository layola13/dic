in-a-sense:/ɪn ə sɛns/| adv.|在某种意义上，在一定程度上|In some way, to a certain extent.  It means that something is partly or somewhat true, but not entirely or completely true.|例句：In a sense, he is right, but he doesn't see the whole picture. 在某种意义上，他是对的，但他没有看到全局。|近义词：sort of, kind of, in a way, to some extent, partly|反义词:absolutely, completely, totally|词性:副词
Etymology: From "in" + "a" + "sense".  "Sense" comes from Old French "sens" meaning "feeling, meaning, direction," ultimately from Latin "sensus" "feeling, perception."
