miserablenesses:/ˈmɪzərəbəlˌnɛsɪz/
noun
悲惨；痛苦；不幸
The state of being miserable; extreme unhappiness or discomfort.
Her miserablenesses were evident in her gaunt face and hollow eyes. 她的痛苦从她憔悴的面容和空洞的眼神中显而易见。
近义词: misery, wretchedness, unhappiness, distress
反义词: happiness, joy, bliss, contentment
词性: noun

Etymology: From miserable + -ness.  Miserable comes from Middle French *miserable, from Late Latin miserabilis "pitiable, wretched," from Latin miserari "to pity," from miser "wretched, unfortunate."
