hypocritely:/ˌhɪpəˈkrɪtɪkli/
adverb
虚伪地，伪善地
In a way that suggests one has beliefs or principles that one does not really possess.
She hypocritely condemned the violence, while secretly funding the aggressors. 她虚伪地谴责暴力，却暗中资助侵略者。
He hypocritely preached about honesty while cheating on his taxes.  他虚伪地宣扬诚实，却在偷税漏税。
近义词: disingenuously, falsely, deceptively
反义词: sincerely, genuinely, honestly
词性: adverb

词源: From hypocrite + -ly.  From Middle French hypocrite, from Late Latin hypocrita, from Ancient Greek ὑποκριτής (hupokritḗs, “actor, pretender”), from ὑποκρίνομαι (hupokrínomai, “I answer, interpret, play a part”).
