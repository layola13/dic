coruscatingly:/ˌkɔːrəˈskeɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 闪耀地，闪烁地 |  In a way that is brilliantly and strikingly witty or lively.  It describes something that shines or flashes brilliantly, often used metaphorically to describe wit or intelligence. |  Her coruscatingly witty remarks captivated the audience. 她妙语连珠的评论吸引了全场观众。 | Synonyms: brilliantly, dazzlingly, sparklingly, glitteringly | Antonyms: dully, dimly, faintly | Part of Speech: Adverb

Etymology:
From the Latin verb "coruscare," meaning "to flash" or "glitter."  The "-ing" suffix creates the present participle, "coruscating," which functions as an adjective. The "-ly" suffix transforms the adjective into the adverb "coruscatingly."
