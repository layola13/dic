middling-meat:/ˈmɪdlɪŋ miːt/| n.|中等肉，普通肉|Meat of average quality, neither the best nor the worst cuts. It is often used in ground meat or processed meats.|The sausages were made with middling-meat.|近义词: average meat, ordinary meat|反义词: prime meat, choice meat|词性:名词

Etymology:
middling (adjective) + meat (noun)

"middling" comes from Middle English "midlen," meaning "situated in the middle."  It evolved from Old English "middel."

"meat" comes from Old English "mete," meaning "food."  It's related to Old High German "maz," meaning "food, meal."
