chopfallen:/ˈtʃɑːpfɔːlən/ | adj. | 沮丧的，垂头丧气的 | Dejected; downcast.  Having a drooping appearance, especially of the lower jaw.  |  He looked chopfallen after hearing the bad news. 他听到坏消息后看起来很沮丧。 | Synonyms: dejected, downcast, crestfallen, discouraged | Antonyms: cheerful, happy, elated, jubilant | word property: adjective

Etymology:
From "chop" (jaw) + "fallen".  Originally referred to a horse whose lower jaw hung open, a sign of illness. Later applied to people with a similar facial expression indicating sadness or disappointment.
