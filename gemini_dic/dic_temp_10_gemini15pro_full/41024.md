cope:/koʊp/ (US) /kəʊp/ (UK)| vi. |处理，应付|to deal successfully with a difficult situation|例句：He coped admirably with the stress of the job. 他出色地应对了工作的压力。|例句：She's coping well with her illness. 她很好地应对了自己的疾病。|近义词：manage, handle, deal with, contend with|反义词:succumb, fail, falter|动词

cope:/koʊp/ (US) /kəʊp/ (UK)| n. |斗篷，披肩 (尤指教士或主教穿的)|a long cloak, especially one worn by a priest or bishop|例句：The bishop wore a magnificent cope embroidered with gold. 主教穿着一件绣金的华丽斗篷。|例句：The priest's cope was a deep shade of purple.  神父的斗篷是深紫色的。|近义词：cloak, mantle, cape|反义词: N/A|名词

词源:
From Old French *couper, from Latin colaphus “a blow with the fist,” from Greek kolaphos. Originally meant "to strike or meet in combat." The meaning shifted to "deal with successfully" in the 19th century.
