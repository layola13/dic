nodded-off:/ˈnɒdɪd ɒf/ (UK) /ˈnɑːdɪd ɔːf/ (US)| v. | 打瞌睡，睡着 | Fall asleep briefly, usually unintentionally. | Example: I nodded off during the lecture. 我在讲座期间打瞌睡了。 | Example:  He nodded off in front of the television. 他在电视机前睡着了。 | 近义词: dozed off, drifted off, dropped off | 反义词: woke up, became alert | 词性: 动词

nodded:/ˈnɒdɪd/ (UK) /ˈnɑːdɪd/ (US)| v. | 点头 | Moved one's head up and down, typically as a sign of agreement or acknowledgment. | Example:  She nodded in agreement. 她点头表示同意。 | Example: He nodded to acknowledge her presence. 他点头示意注意到她的存在。 | 近义词: agreed, acknowledged, concurred | 反义词: shook one's head, disagreed | 词性: 动词

off:/ɒf/ (UK) /ɔːf/ (US) | adv., prep., adj. | 离开，关闭，休息 | Away from a place or position; no longer operating or attached; not at work or not functioning. | Example: The light is off. 灯关了。 | Example:  He took the day off. 他休假一天。 | 近义词: away, detached, inactive | 反义词: on, attached, active | 词性: 副词，介词，形容词

词源: "Nodded off" is a phrasal verb formed from the verb "nod" (Old English *hnoddian, related to Dutch knikken "to nod") and the adverb "off" (Old English of).  The combination creates the meaning of nodding one's head as one falls asleep.
