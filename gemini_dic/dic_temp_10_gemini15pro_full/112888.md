luddite:/ˈlʌdaɪt/| n.| 勒德分子；反对技术进步的人|A member of any of the bands of English workers who destroyed machinery, especially in cotton and woolen mills, that they believed was threatening their jobs (1811–16); a person opposed to increased industrialization or new technology.|例句：Some people think that those who resist new technologies are modern-day Luddites.  有些人认为抵制新技术的人是现代的勒德分子。

例句：He was accused of being a Luddite for refusing to use email. 他因为拒绝使用电子邮件而被指责为勒德分子。|近义词：reactionary, conservative, anti-progress|反义词:progressive, innovator, technophile|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ned Ludd, the name of a Leicestershire worker who supposedly destroyed stocking frames in 1779; the name was subsequently adopted by other protesters and became a generic term.  源自莱斯特郡工人奈德·勒德的名字，据说他在1779年破坏了织袜机；这个名字后来被其他抗议者采用，并成为一个通用术语。
