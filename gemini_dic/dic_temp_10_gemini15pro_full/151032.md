parapatry:/ˌpærəˈpætri/ | n. |异域相邻|The distribution of species or populations in which they are geographically adjacent but do not overlap.  A situation where two related species or populations occupy adjacent but non-overlapping geographical areas, often separated by a narrow zone of contact. |The two species of squirrels exhibit parapatry, separated by the river. 这两个松鼠物种呈现异域相邻分布，被河流隔开。|Nearopatry, parapatric distribution|Sympatry|noun
