catastrophically:/ˌkætəˈstrɒfɪkli/ | adv. |灾难性地，悲惨地| In a manner that involves or results in a catastrophe.  A sudden, widespread disaster.|例句：The dam failed catastrophically, flooding the entire valley.  大坝灾难性地决堤，淹没了整个山谷。|例句：The company's stock price fell catastrophically after the scandal.  丑闻曝光后，该公司股价暴跌。|近义词：disastrously, ruinously, calamitously|反义词:  fortunately, luckily, successfully |词性: 副词

catastrophe:/kəˈtæstrəfi/ | n. |灾难，灾祸| A sudden, widespread disaster.|例句：The earthquake was a catastrophe for the region.  这场地震对该地区来说是一场灾难。|例句：The oil spill was an environmental catastrophe.  石油泄漏是一场环境灾难。|近义词：disaster, calamity, tragedy|反义词:  triumph, success, blessing |词性: 名词
