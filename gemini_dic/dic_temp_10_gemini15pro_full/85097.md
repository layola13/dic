hardboiled:/ˌhɑːrdˈbɔɪld/| adj.| 冷酷的，强硬的，煮熟的(鸡蛋)| (of a person) tough, unsentimental, and cynical, especially when dealing with crime or violence. Also (of an egg) boiled until both the white and yolk are solid. |例句：He's a hardboiled detective who's seen it all. 他是一个饱经世故、冷酷无情的侦探。

例句：I like my eggs hardboiled. 我喜欢吃煮老的鸡蛋。|近义词：tough, callous, cynical, unemotional |反义词:soft-hearted, sentimental, compassionate |词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From hard + boiled.  The literal sense "boiled until hard" dates to the late 18th century.  The figurative sense of "toughened, callous" emerged in the late 19th/early 20th century, particularly in American slang, often associated with detective fiction.
