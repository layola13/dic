crucification: /ˌkruːsɪˈfɪkʃən/ | n. | 钉死于十字架；极大的痛苦 | The act of putting someone to death by nailing them to a cross.  A very difficult or painful experience. |  His crucifixion was a pivotal moment in Christian history. 他的被钉死在十字架上是基督教历史上的关键时刻。

The loss of her child was a crucifixion for her.  失去孩子对她来说是极大的痛苦。 | 近义词: execution, torment, ordeal | 反义词: salvation, liberation | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *crucifixio, from Latin crucifixus, past participle of crucifigere “to crucify,” from crux “cross” + figere “to fix, fasten.”
