mercantilists:/ˈmɜːrkəntɪlɪsts/
noun
重商主义者
Advocates or practitioners of mercantilism, an economic theory prevalent in Europe from the 16th to the 18th centuries that promoted governmental regulation of a nation's economy for the purpose of augmenting state power at the expense of rival national powers.  It was characterized by the belief that national strength could be maximized by limiting imports via tariffs and maximizing exports.
The mercantilists believed that a nation's wealth was best measured by the amount of gold and silver it possessed. 重商主义者认为一个国家的财富最好用它拥有的金银数量来衡量。

European mercantilists frequently pushed for colonization as a way to gain access to raw materials and create new markets for finished goods. 欧洲重商主义者经常推动殖民化，以此作为获取原材料和为制成品创造新市场的一种方式。

近义词:protectionists, nationalists (in an economic sense)
反义词:free traders, laissez-faire economists
词性:n.
