gymnolaemata:/ˌdʒɪmnəˈliːmətə/ | n. | 裸唇纲 | A class of Bryozoa (moss animals), characterized by a circular lophophore and the lack of an operculum.  They are primarily marine, forming colonies that encrust rocks, shells, and other surfaces. |  The gymnolaemata are a diverse group of marine invertebrates. 裸唇纲是海洋无脊椎动物的一个多样化群体。| Gymnolaemata colonies can be found in a variety of marine habitats.  裸唇纲群体可以在各种海洋栖息地中找到。 |  Bryozoa, Cheilostomata |  N/A | noun
