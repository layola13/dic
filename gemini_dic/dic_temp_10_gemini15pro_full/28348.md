carapato:/kərəˈpɑːtoʊ/ (Brazilian Portuguese), /ˌkærəˈpætoʊ/ (anglicized)| n.|蜱; 壁蝨; (尤指) 牛蝨|A small arachnid that lives as a parasite on mammals and birds, sucking their blood.  It is similar to a tick or mite. |The dog was covered in carapatos after walking through the tall grass.  狗在穿过高高的草丛后身上爬满了蜱虫。|Synonyms: tick, mite|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

carapato:/kərəˈpɑːtoʊ/ (Brazilian Portuguese), /ˌkærəˈpætoʊ/ (anglicized)| n.| (比喻) 寄生虫; 赖皮狗|Someone who is overly dependent on others, especially financially. (Figurative usage, primarily in Brazilian Portuguese)|He's a real carapato, always borrowing money and never paying it back. 他真是个寄生虫，总是借钱，从不还。|Synonyms: leech, parasite, freeloader|Antonyms: benefactor, patron|Noun
