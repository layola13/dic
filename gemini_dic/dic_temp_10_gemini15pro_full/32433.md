chimer:/ˈkaɪmər/ (US), /ˈkaɪmɪə/ (UK)| n.|嵌合体；虚构的怪物|A fire-breathing female monster with a lion's head, a goat's body, and a serpent's tail, composed of incongruous parts.|The chimera in Greek mythology was a fearsome creature.|Synonyms: hybrid, monster, composite|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "purebred," "natural")|Noun

chimeric:/kaɪˈmɛrɪk/ (US), /kaɪˈmɛrɪk/ (UK) | adj.|空想的；幻想的；嵌合的|Produced by or as if by a wildly fanciful or unrealistic process; highly improbable; given to unrealistic fantasies; relating to or being a chimera.|His plans to build a castle on the moon were chimeric.|Synonyms: fanciful, unrealistic, imaginary, utopian|Antonyms: realistic, practical, achievable, down-to-earth|Adjective
