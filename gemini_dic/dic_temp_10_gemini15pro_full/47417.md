deadlock:/ˈdedlɒk/ (UK) /ˈdɛdlɑːk/ (US)| n. v.|僵局，死锁|A situation, typically one involving opposing parties, in which no progress can be made.|The negotiations reached a deadlock. 谈判陷入了僵局。|近义词：standoff, stalemate, impasse, gridlock|反义词：agreement, breakthrough, compromise|noun
deadlock:/ˈdedlɒk/ (UK) /ˈdɛdlɑːk/ (US)| v.|使陷入僵局，停顿|To cause a situation in which no progress can be made.|The talks were deadlocked by disagreements over funding.  谈判因资金分歧而陷入僵局。|近义词：stall, block, hinder|反义词：facilitate, expedite, resolve|verb
