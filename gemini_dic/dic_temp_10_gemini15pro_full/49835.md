demonstrators:/ˈdemənˌstreɪtərz/ | n. | 示威者，游行者 | People who participate in a public demonstration to express their opinions or support for a cause. | Thousands of demonstrators marched through the streets. 数千名示威者走上街头游行。 | protestors, marchers,  protesters | supporters, allies | noun
demonstrator:/ˈdemənˌstreɪtər/ | n. | 示范者，演示者| A person who demonstrates something, typically a product or skill. | The demonstrator showed us how to use the new software.  演示者向我们展示了如何使用这款新软件。 | presenter, exhibitor | observer | noun

demonstrate: /ˈdemənˌstreɪt/ | v. | 证明，表明；示范，演示；示威，游行 | To show or prove something clearly; to give a practical exhibition and explanation of how something works; to take part in a public demonstration. |  He demonstrated his skill by solving the complex puzzle. 他通过解开复杂的谜题展示了他的技能。 | show, prove, exhibit, display | conceal, hide | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *demonstrator, from Latin demonstratus, past participle of demonstrare "to point out, show," from de- "fully" + monstrare "to show," from monstrum "divine portent, monster, sign, omen."
