contradictorinesses: /ˌkɒntrəˈdɪktərɪnəsɪz/ | n. |矛盾性；前后矛盾；自相矛盾|The contradictorinesses in his statement made it difficult to believe him. | His testimony was filled with contradictorinesses, making it hard to determine the truth. | synonyms: inconsistencies, discrepancies, incongruities, paradoxes | antonyms: consistencies, harmonies, agreements, coherences | noun

Etymology:
From contradictory + -ness + -es.  "Contradictory" comes from Late Latin *contradictorius, from Latin contradicere "to speak against," from contra "against" + dicere "to say."  "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, denoting a state, quality, or instance of being. "-es" is a plural suffix.
