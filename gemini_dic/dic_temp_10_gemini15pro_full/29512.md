catastrophe:/kəˈtæstrəfi/ | n. |灾难，灾祸|A sudden event causing very great trouble or destruction.|The earthquake was a catastrophe that devastated the city. 地震是一场毁灭城市的灾难。|近义词：disaster, calamity, tragedy|反义词：triumph, success|名词

catastrophes:/kəˈtæstrəfiz/ | n. |灾难，灾祸（复数）|Plural form of catastrophe.|The region has suffered a series of catastrophes in recent years. 该地区近年来遭受了一系列灾难。|近义词：disasters, calamities, tragedies|反义词：triumphs, successes|名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *catastropha, from Ancient Greek καταστροφή (katastrophḗ, “overturning, sudden turn, overthrow, ruin”), from καταστρέφω (katastréphō, “I overturn”), from κατά (katá, “down”) + στρέφω (stréphō, “I turn”).
