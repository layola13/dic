bound:/baʊnd/ | adj. | 受约束的，有义务的；一定的，必然的；准备前往...的；跳跃的 | Certain to do or experience something; going or intending to go to a particular place; tied with rope, fastened; leaping |  The company is bound by the terms of the contract.  这家公司受合同条款的约束。|  She is bound to succeed. 她一定会成功。| synonyms: obliged, obligated, committed, destined, sure, certain | antonyms: free, unbound, uncommitted, unlikely | adjective

bound:/baʊnd/ | v. | 跳跃；弹回；形成...的边界；限制 | To move quickly with large jumping movements; to spring back; to form the boundary of; to restrict | The ball bounded down the stairs. 球沿着楼梯弹跳下来。 |  The country is bounded on the north by the river.  这个国家北面以这条河为界。| synonyms: leap, jump, spring, bounce, delimit, restrict | antonyms: crawl, creep, trudge | verb

bound:/baʊnd/ | n. | 跳跃；界限，范围 | A jump or leap; a limit or boundary | He cleared the fence in a single bound. 他一跃就越过了篱笆。 |  There are no bounds to his ambition. 他的野心没有界限。| synonyms: leap, jump, spring, limit, boundary, extent | antonyms: limitlessness | noun
