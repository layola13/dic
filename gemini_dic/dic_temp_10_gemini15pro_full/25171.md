buckyball: /ˈbʌkiˌbɔːl/ | n. | 巴基球 | A buckyball, or Buckminsterfullerene, is a spherical molecule composed of 60 carbon atoms (C60) arranged in a pattern resembling a soccer ball.  It is a member of the fullerene family. | Buckyballs have potential applications in nanotechnology. 巴基球在纳米技术中具有潜在的应用。 | Buckyballs are named after Buckminster Fuller, the architect who designed geodesic domes. 巴基球以设计测地穹顶的建筑师巴克敏斯特·富勒的名字命名。 | synonym: fullerene | antonym: N/A | noun
