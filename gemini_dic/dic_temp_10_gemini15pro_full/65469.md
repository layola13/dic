eurocommunism:/ˌjʊrəʊˈkɒmjunɪzəm/ | n. | 欧洲共产主义 | A revised form of communism that rejected the Soviet model and sought to achieve socialism through democratic means within the existing political framework of Western Europe. | Eurocommunism gained prominence in the 1970s, particularly in Italy and France. 欧洲共产主义在20世纪70年代声名鹊起，尤其是在意大利和法国。 |  Synonyms: democratic communism, Western communism | Antonyms: Stalinism, Soviet communism | noun
