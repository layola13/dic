cozied-up:/ˈkoʊ.zid ʌp/| adj.|依偎在一起的，亲密的|Having made oneself warm and comfortable, often in close proximity to another person or thing.  It implies a sense of intimacy and relaxation.|例句：The couple cozied up by the fire on a cold winter night.  这对夫妇在一个寒冷的冬夜依偎在火炉旁。

例句：The cat cozied up to its owner on the sofa.  猫在沙发上依偎着它的主人。|近义词：snuggled up, cuddled up, nestled|反义词:distant, apart|词性:形容词

词源: From "cozy" + "up". "Cozy" originates from the Scots word "cosie," meaning "snug, comfortable."  "Up" in this context intensifies the meaning of cozy and suggests a drawing together.
