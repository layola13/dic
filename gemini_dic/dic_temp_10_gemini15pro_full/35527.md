coagulate:/koʊˈæɡjʊleɪt/| v. | 凝结，凝固 | to change from a liquid to a thick, almost solid state |  The blood coagulated quickly after the cut.  血液在割伤后迅速凝结。| synonyms: clot, thicken, congeal | antonyms: liquefy, thin, dissolve | verb

coagulates:/koʊˈæɡjʊleɪts/| v. | （第三人称单数）凝结，凝固 |  Heats coagulates the egg white. 热使蛋清凝固。 | synonyms: clots, thickens, congeals | antonyms: liquefies, thins, dissolves | verb

coagulation:/koʊˌæɡjʊˈleɪʃn/| n. | 凝结，凝固 | the process of a liquid changing to a solid or semi-solid state |  The doctor is checking the coagulation time of his blood. 医生正在检查他的血液凝固时间。| synonyms: clotting, thickening, congealing | antonyms: liquefaction, thinning, dissolution | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin coagulātus, past participle of coagulāre (“to curdle, congeal”), from co- (“together”) + agere (“to drive, do”).
