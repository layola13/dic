manhole:/ˈmænhoʊl/ (US) /ˈmænhəʊl/ (UK)| n.|人孔，检查井，下水道入口|A covered opening in a street or pavement, providing access to underground utilities such as sewers, electricity cables, etc.|例句：The workmen opened the manhole to inspect the sewer.  工人们打开人孔检查下水道。

例句：A child's toy fell down the manhole. 一个小孩的玩具掉进了人孔里。|近义词：inspection chamber, access hole, utility hole|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From man + hole.  "Man" in this context refers to something made or used by humans, not specifically a male person.  The term originated in the late 19th century.
