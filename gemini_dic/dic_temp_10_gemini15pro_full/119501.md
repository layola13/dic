microphoning:/ˈmaɪkrəˌfoʊnɪŋ/| n.|话筒效应，啸叫|The unwanted amplification of sound caused by a microphone picking up sound from a loudspeaker or monitor, creating a feedback loop.  This often results in a high-pitched squeal.|The band had to stop playing because of terrible microphoning.|近义词：feedback, howling|反义词:silence|词性:名词
Etymology:  From "microphone" + "-ing" (present participle suffix, forming a noun denoting an action or process).  "Microphone" itself comes from "micro-" (small) + "phone" (sound).
