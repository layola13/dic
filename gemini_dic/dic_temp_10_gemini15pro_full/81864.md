gridlock:/ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk/ (US) /ˈɡrɪdˌlɑːk/ (UK)| n.  v.|交通堵塞，僵局|A situation where streets are so blocked by traffic that vehicles cannot move.|例句：The accident caused a gridlock on the highway.  事故导致高速公路交通堵塞。|近义词：traffic jam, standstill, deadlock|反义词: free flow, open road|词性:名词, 动词

gridlock:/ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk/ (US) /ˈɡrɪdˌlɑːk/ (UK)| v. |(使)陷入僵局，(使)交通堵塞|To cause a traffic jam or standstill.|例句：The protest gridlocked the city center. 抗议活动导致市中心交通瘫痪。|近义词：block, congest, paralyze|反义词: expedite, clear|词性:动词
