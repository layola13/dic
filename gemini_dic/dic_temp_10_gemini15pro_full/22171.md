bombastry:/ˈbɒmbæstri/ (US) /ˈbɒmbræstri/ (UK)| n. |夸夸其谈；浮夸的言辞|High-sounding language with little meaning, used to impress people.|His speech was full of bombastry, but lacked substance. 他的演讲充满了夸夸其谈，但缺乏实质内容。|例句：The politician's bombastry failed to impress the voters.  这位政客的夸夸其谈未能给选民留下深刻印象。

例句：The CEO's bombastry during the press conference alienated many potential investors. 首席执行官在新闻发布会上的夸夸其谈疏远了许多潜在投资者。


近义词：pomposity, grandiloquence, rhetoric, fustian|反义词:simplicity, conciseness, modesty, understatement|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: from Old French *bombast, from bombex ‘cotton wool, padding’ (via Italian bombacino ‘padded doublet’), from Latin bombyx, from Greek bombux, from an oriental word. Originally applied to cotton wool used as padding, hence inflated or pretentious language.
