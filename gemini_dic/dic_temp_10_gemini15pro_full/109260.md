lemma:/ˈlɛmə/| n.|词元，词目，引理|(linguistics) The canonical form of a set of words.  A lemma is a word that represents a set of forms. For example, the lemma "run" represents "runs," "running," and "ran."|The lemma "go" represents many different forms of the verb.|Synonyms: headword, base form|Antonyms: inflection, variant|noun

lemma:/ˈlɛmə/| n.|引理，辅助定理|(mathematics) A subsidiary proposition proved in order to simplify the proof of a required theorem.|The lemma helped to prove the main theorem.|Synonyms: proposition, theorem (minor)|Antonyms: theorem (major)|noun
