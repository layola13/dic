chequer:/ˈtʃɛkər/ (US) /ˈtʃɛkə/ (UK)| n. v.|棋盘，方格图案；格仔布；出纳员；收银员|A board for playing checkers or chess, marked with squares of alternating colors. Also refers to a pattern of squares, often in contrasting colors. As a verb, it means to mark with a checkered pattern. |The chessboard has a distinct chequer pattern.  棋盘有明显的方格图案。|Synonyms: checkerboard, grid, lattice, plaid|Antonyms: solid, plain|noun

chequer:/ˈtʃɛkər/ (US) /ˈtʃɛkə/ (UK)| v.|使成方格图案；使多样化|To vary or diversify; to mark with squares or a checkered pattern. |Her life was chequered with both joy and sorrow. 她的生活充满了喜乐和悲伤。|Synonyms: variegate, diversify, mottle|Antonyms: unify, homogenize|verb
