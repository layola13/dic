dripstone:/ˈdrɪpˌstoʊn/| n.|滴石,钟乳石|A deposit of calcium carbonate formed by the dripping of water, such as a stalactite or stalagmite.|The cave was filled with fascinating dripstone formations.|近义词：stalactite, stalagmite|反义词:|名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From drip + stone.  "Drip" comes from Old Norse "dryppa" meaning "to drop." "Stone" comes from Old English "stan" meaning "stone, rock."
