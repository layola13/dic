convoys:/ˈkɒnvɔɪz/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnvɔɪz/ (US)| n. |护航队，车队，船队|A group of vehicles or ships traveling together, typically one or more of which are armed, for mutual protection.|The army convoy moved slowly through the mountains. 陆军车队缓慢地穿过山区。|The navy provided a convoy for the merchant ships. 海军为商船提供了护航。|近义词：escort, fleet, procession|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

convoy:/ˈkɒnvɔɪ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnvɔɪ/ (US)| vt. |护航，护送|To accompany and protect (a group of vehicles, ships, etc.) on a journey.|Two destroyers convoyed the troopship. 两艘驱逐舰护送着运兵船。|The police convoyed the president's car through the city. 警察护送总统的汽车穿过城市。|近义词：escort, guard, protect|反义词: N/A|词性:verb
