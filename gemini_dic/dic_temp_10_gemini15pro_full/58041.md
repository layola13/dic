drolled:/droʊld/| v.|  逗乐，开玩笑 | to make jokes or say funny things, especially in a dry or sarcastic way | He drolled about the absurdity of the situation. 他打趣着这情况的荒谬。| synonyms: joked, jested, quipped | antonyms: lamented, mourned | verb

droll:/droʊl/| adj.| 滑稽的，古怪有趣的 | amusing in an odd or whimsical way |  He had a droll sense of humor. 他有一种古怪的幽默感。| synonyms: amusing, comical, funny, whimsical, quirky | antonyms: serious, somber, dull | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *droler*, from Middle Dutch *drolle*, meaning "buffoon" or "jester".  The meaning shifted from referring to a comical person to describing something comical.
