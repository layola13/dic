heteroclitic:/ˌhɛtərəˈklɪtɪk/ | adj. | 異形變化的，不規則變化的 |  (of a word) having inflections that follow more than one paradigm or deviate from the normal pattern of inflection.  A heteroclitic noun or adjective is one that exhibits a combination of inflectional patterns, often borrowing forms from different declensions or classes. |  The word "ox" (singular) and "oxen" (plural) is a classic example of a heteroclitic noun in English. |  irregular, anomalous, aberrant | regular, normal | adjective
