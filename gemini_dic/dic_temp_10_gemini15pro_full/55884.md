divisive:/dɪˈvaɪsɪv/| adj.|分裂的，引起分歧的|tending to cause disagreement or hostility between people.|The issue of abortion is highly divisive in the United States.  在美国，堕胎问题极具争议性。|例句：The politician's divisive rhetoric fueled tensions between the two groups.  这位政客的分裂性言论加剧了两组之间的紧张关系。|近义词：disruptive, discordant, polarizing|反义词：unifying, uniting, harmonizing|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Late Latin *dīvīsus, past participle of dīvidere “to divide,” from dis- “apart” + -videre “to separate.”
