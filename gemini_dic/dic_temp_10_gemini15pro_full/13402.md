atomism:/ˈætəmɪzəm/ | n. |原子论；原子主义|The theory that all matter is composed of tiny indivisible particles called atoms, and that all phenomena are explicable in terms of the configurations and motions of these atoms.  Also, the theory that society is made up of individuals pursuing their own self-interests, without necessary regard for the well-being of others. | Democritus was a proponent of ancient atomism. 德谟克利特是古代原子论的支持者。

The rise of individualism and atomism in modern society is a topic of much debate.  现代社会个人主义和原子主义的兴起是一个备受争议的话题。| 近义词: individualism, reductionism | 反义词: holism, collectivism | 词性: noun

Etymology: From French *atomisme*, from *atome* (“atom”) + *-isme* (from Latin *-ismus*, from Ancient Greek *-ισμός* (-ismós)). Ultimately from Ancient Greek *ἄτομος* (átomos, “indivisible”).
