bloviation:/ˌbloʊviˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 长篇大论；夸夸其谈 | lengthy and pompous speech or writing | The politician's speech was nothing but two hours of bloviation.  这位政客的演讲不过是两个小时的夸夸其谈。|  His bloviations on the economy put everyone to sleep. 他关于经济的长篇大论让每个人都昏昏欲睡。 |近义词：bombast, rhetoric, verbosity, rant |反义词: conciseness, brevity |词性: noun

bloviate:/ˈbloʊvieɪt/ | v. |  夸夸其谈；长篇大论 | to speak or write verbosely and pompously | He bloviated for hours about his accomplishments. 他滔滔不绝地讲了几个小时他的成就。| The CEO bloviated about the company's future, but offered few specifics. 首席执行官大谈公司的未来，但几乎没有提供具体细节。| 近义词：rant, rave, spout, pontificate |反义词:  be concise, be brief |词性: verb
