correlator:/ˈkɒrəleɪtə(r)/ (US) /ˈkɔːrəleɪtər/ (UK)| n.|相关器，关联器|A device or mathematical function that compares two signals or sets of data to determine the degree to which they are related or correlated.  It measures the statistical relationship between two or more random variables or sets of data.|The correlator showed a strong relationship between temperature and ice cream sales.|Synonyms: comparator, analyzer|Antonyms: N/A|noun

correlate:/ˈkɒrəleɪt/ (US) /ˈkɔːrəleɪt/ (UK)| v. |使…相关联，互相关联|To establish a mutual or reciprocal relationship between; to bring into a common relation.|Researchers tried to correlate smoking with lung cancer.|Synonyms: connect, relate, link|Antonyms: disconnect, separate|verb

correlation:/ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃn/ (US) /ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃn/ (UK)| n.|相关性，关联|A mutual relationship or connection between two or more things.|There is a strong correlation between education and income.|Synonyms: relationship, connection, link|Antonyms: independence, disconnection|noun
