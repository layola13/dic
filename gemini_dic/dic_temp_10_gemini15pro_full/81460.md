gratuities: /ɡrəˈtjuːɪtiz/ /ɡrəˈtuːɪtiz/
n.
小费，赏钱，赠品
A sum of money customarily given by a client or customer to a worker such as a waiter or bellhop; a tip.
例句：The tour price includes gratuities for the hotel staff. 旅行费用包含给酒店员工的小费。
例句：He left a generous gratuity for the waiter. 他给服务员留了一笔丰厚的小费。
近义词：tips, gifts, donations, perks
反义词：deductions, withholdings
词性：名词

词源：
From Late Middle English: from Old French gratuite, from Latin gratuitus ‘done freely, spontaneous’, from gratia ‘grace, favor’.
