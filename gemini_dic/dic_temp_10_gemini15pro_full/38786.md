conductibility:/kənˌdʌktɪˈbɪlɪti/ | n. | 传导性，导电性 | The property of a substance to transmit heat, electricity, or sound. | Copper has high conductibility. 铜具有高导电性。| The conductibility of this new material is being tested.  这种新材料的导电性正在测试中。 | synonyms: conductivity, conductance | antonyms: resistance, insulation | noun

conductive:/kənˈdʌktɪv/ | adj. | 传导的，导电的 | Having the property of transmitting heat, electricity, or sound. | Copper is a highly conductive metal. 铜是一种高导电性的金属。|  This material is conductive and therefore suitable for electrical wiring. 这种材料是导电的，因此适用于电线。 | synonyms: transmitting, conducting | antonyms: resistant, insulating | adjective

conductance:/kənˈdʌktəns/ | n. | 电导 | The ability of a substance to conduct electricity. | The conductance of a wire depends on its material and dimensions. 导线的电导取决于其材料和尺寸。| Measuring the conductance of a solution can help determine its ion concentration. 测量溶液的电导有助于确定其离子浓度。| synonyms: conductivity | antonyms: resistance | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *conductibilitas, from Latin conductus (“a leading, conducting”) + -ibilitas.  Conductus is the past participle of conducere, from com- (“together”) + ducere (“to lead”).
