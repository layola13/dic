housewifey: /ˈhaʊswʌɪfi/ | adj. | 家庭主妇般的；有家庭主妇特质的 | Resembling or characteristic of a housewife, especially in a traditional or stereotypical way.  Often implies a focus on domestic duties, a concern for neatness and order, and perhaps a slightly old-fashioned or conventional outlook. |  She had a very housewifey approach to decorating, prioritizing practicality and comfort. 她对装修采取了非常家庭主妇式的方法，优先考虑实用性和舒适性。

She always keeps a spotless house – she’s so housewifey! 她总是把房子打扫得一尘不染——她真是个家庭主妇！ | 近义词: domestic, homey | 反义词: career-oriented, unconventional | 词性: 形容词

词源:  Derived from "housewife" + "-y" (a suffix forming adjectives indicating resemblance or characteristic of). "Housewife" itself comes from Old English "hūs" (house) + "wīf" (woman).
