conditionalism:/kənˈdɪʃənəlɪzəm/
n.
条件主义
The doctrine that the salvation of individuals is conditional on their faith, repentance, and obedience.
Example: Conditionalism is a theological viewpoint that contrasts with the belief in eternal torment.  条件主义是一种神学观点，与永恒的折磨的信仰形成对比。

Example:  The preacher's sermon focused on the concept of conditionalism and its implications for believers. 牧师的布道集中在条件主义的概念及其对信徒的影响。
Synonym: conditionality
Antonym: unconditionalism
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "conditional" + "-ism."  "Conditional" comes from the Late Latin "conditionalis,"  meaning "pertaining to a condition," from "conditio" (condition). "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine, etc.
