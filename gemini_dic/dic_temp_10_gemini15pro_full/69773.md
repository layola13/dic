fermata:/ferˈmɑːtə/ /fərˈmɑːdə/
|n.|延长记号，休止符|A symbol (𝄐) placed over a note or rest indicating that it is to be held for longer than its normal time value, the length of the extension being at the performer's discretion.  A pause or hold.|The conductor indicated a fermata over the final chord.|近义词: pause, hold, rest|反义词: continuation|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From Italian *fermata*, literally "stopped," feminine past participle of *fermare* "to stop," from Late Latin *firmare* "to strengthen, make firm, fix," from Latin *firmus* "firm, strong, steady."
