antistatism:/ˌæntiˈstætɪzəm/
n.|反国家主义；反国家干预主义|The philosophy that opposes state intervention, especially in economic affairs.  It emphasizes individual liberty and free markets.|He expressed strong antistatism, arguing that government intervention stifled innovation.|近义词: libertarianism, laissez-faire, individualism|反义词: statism, collectivism|词性: noun

antistatisms:/ˌæntiˈstætɪzəmz/
n.|（反国家主义的）多种观点或思潮|Different philosophies or movements that oppose state intervention, especially in economic affairs.|The conference explored various antistatisms, from classical liberalism to anarcho-capitalism.|近义词: libertarianisms, individualisms|反义词: statisms, collectivisms|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- (against) + statism (the principle or policy of concentrating extensive economic, political, and related controls in the state).  "Statism" ultimately derives from the Latin word "status" (state, condition).
