fawn:/fɔːn/ (British), /fɔːn/ (American)| n. | 幼鹿 | A young deer, especially one less than a year old. |例句：The fawn hid in the tall grass. 小鹿躲在高高的草丛里。|例句：The mother deer protected her fawn from predators. 母鹿保护她的小鹿免受捕食者的伤害。|近义词：calf, yearling |反义词: adult deer (成年鹿)
fawn:/fɔːn/ (British), /fɔːn/ (American)| vi. | 奉承；讨好 | To seek favor or attention by flattery and obsequious behavior. |例句：The courtiers fawned over the king, hoping for favors. 朝臣们巴结国王，希望得到好处。|例句：She fawned on her boss in an attempt to get a promotion. 她讨好老板，试图升职。|近义词：flatter, grovel, kowtow |反义词: criticize, defy
