accumbency: /əˈkʌmbənsi/
n.
在职; 卧位
The state of holding an office.  The act of lying down.
例句：
The president's accumbency was marked by economic growth.  总统的任期以经济增长为标志。

The dog's accumbency on the rug was a sign of contentment. 狗躺在毯子上是一种满足的迹象。

近义词：incumbency, tenure, office
反义词：vacancy
词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *accumbentia, from accumbere “to lie down,” from ad- "to, towards" + -cumbere "to lie down."

USAGE->
During his accumbency, the company saw record profits. = 在他的任期内，公司利润创了纪录。
The accumbency of the new mayor brought about significant changes. = 新市长的就职带来了重大的变化。

The accumbency of the patient was monitored closely. = 病人的卧位受到严密监控。
The doctor advised a period of accumbency after the surgery. = 医生建议手术后卧床一段时间。
