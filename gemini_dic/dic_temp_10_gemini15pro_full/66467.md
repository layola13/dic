exequious:/ɪkˈsiːkwiəs/| adj.|谄媚的；奴颜婢膝的；过分殷勤的|Excessively eager to please or obey; servile; fawning.  Characterized by or showing servile obedience.|例句：His exequious manner toward the CEO was embarrassing to witness. 他对首席执行官奴颜婢膝的态度让人尴尬。

例句：The exequious waiter hovered around their table, anticipating their every need.  那个谄媚的侍者在他们的桌子旁徘徊，预料着他们的每一个需求。|近义词：servile, obsequious, fawning, sycophantic|反义词：assertive, independent, forthright|词性:adjective
ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *exsequiosus*, from exsequi (“to follow, pursue, execute, perform”), from ex- (“out”) + sequi (“to follow”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *sekʷ- (“to follow”).
