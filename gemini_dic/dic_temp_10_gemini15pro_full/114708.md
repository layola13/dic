malleableness:/ˌmæliəˈbɪlnəs/ |n.| 柔软性；延展性；可塑性；顺从|The malleableness of gold makes it ideal for jewelry.  黄金的延展性使它成为制作珠宝的理想材料。|例句：The artist admired the malleableness of the clay. 艺术家很欣赏黏土的可塑性。

例句：The malleableness of his political opinions made him an unreliable ally.  他政治观点的可塑性使他成为一个不可靠的盟友。

|近义词：ductility, plasticity, flexibility, suppleness, pliability|反义词：rigidity, stiffness, inflexibility, unyieldingness|词性：名词

malleable:/ˈmæliəbl/ |adj.| 可锻的；可塑的；易受影响的|Gold is a malleable metal. 黄金是一种可锻金属。|例句：He has a malleable personality, easily influenced by others. 他性格容易受影响，容易被别人左右。

例句：The young student's mind was malleable and open to new ideas.  年轻学生的头脑具有可塑性，乐于接受新思想。|近义词：ductile, plastic, flexible, supple, pliable|反义词：rigid, stiff, inflexible, unyielding|词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *malleable*, from Late Latin malleabilis "that can be hammered," from Latin malleare "to hammer," from malleus "hammer."
