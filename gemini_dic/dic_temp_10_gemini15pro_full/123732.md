moonshine:/ˈmuːnʃaɪn/ | n. | 私酿酒；月光 | Illicitly distilled or smuggled liquor; the light of the moon. |  The police raided a moonshine operation in the mountains. 警方突袭了山区的一个私酒酿造点。

The moonshine illuminated the path through the woods. 月光照亮了穿过树林的小路。 | 近义词：liquor, hooch, white lightning (for illicit alcohol); moonlight (for moon's light) | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, could be considered "darkness" for moon's light aspect) | 词性: noun


moonshine:/ˈmuːnʃaɪn/ | v. |  从事走私或私酿 | To engage in the illicit distilling or smuggling of liquor. | They were moonshining in the backwoods to avoid taxes. 他们在穷乡僻壤私酿酒以逃避税收。 | 近义词：bootleg | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From moon + shine. Originally referring to working by the light of the moon, especially to illicit activities, later specifically to distilling liquor at night to avoid detection.
