namby-pambyisms:/ˈnæmbi ˈpæmbiɪzəmz/
|n.|幼稚的言行，故作多情的行为，扭捏作态|Sentimental or weakly emotional behaviour or language, especially of an excessive or affected kind.|His writing is full of namby-pambyisms that make me cringe. 他的作品充满了让我 cringe 的幼稚言行。|The audience was irritated by the namby-pambyisms of the actors. 观众被演员们扭捏作态的表演激怒了。|近义词：sentimentality, mawkishness, effeminacy|反义词：masculinity, strength, robustness|词性:名词

namby-pamby:/ˈnæmbi ˈpæmbi/
|adj.|幼稚的，故作多情的，扭捏作态的|Weak, sentimental, and ineffectual.|He's got such a namby-pamby attitude, it's infuriating. 他那种扭捏的态度真让人恼火。|She gave a namby-pamby speech that bored everyone. 她做了一个无聊的、故作多情的演讲。|近义词：sentimental, mawkish, effeminate|反义词：masculine, strong, robust|词性:形容词

namby-pamby:/ˈnæmbi ˈpæmbi/
|n.|一个幼稚的人，一个故作多情的人|A weak, sentimental, or ineffectual person.|Don't be such a namby-pamby! 别这么扭捏！|He's a namby-pamby and can't handle any pressure. 他是个软弱的人，承受不了任何压力。|近义词：weakling, milksop|反义词：strongman, tough guy|词性:名词


词源:  From Ambrose Philips (c. 1674 – 1749), an English poet ridiculed by Henry Carey and others for the supposed childishness of his verses addressed to children.  Originally a nickname "Namby Pamby" based on a childish pronunciation of "Ambrose."
