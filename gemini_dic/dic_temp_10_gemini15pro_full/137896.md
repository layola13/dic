nonvitiation:/nɒnˌvɪʃiˈeɪʃən/
n.
non-invalidation;  the act of not vitiating or making something invalid.
The nonvitiation of the contract ensured the deal proceeded as planned.  合同的有效性确保了交易按计划进行。
The lawyer argued for the nonvitiation of the will. 律师辩称遗嘱有效。
近义词: validation, confirmation, ratification
反义词: vitiation, invalidation, annulment
词性: noun


Etymology:
From Latin *nonvitiatio, from non- "not" + vitiatio "a spoiling, impairing,"  from vitiatus, past participle of vitiare "to spoil, injure, make faulty," from vitium "fault, defect."
