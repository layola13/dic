anomie: /ˈænəmi/ (US), /ˈænəmi/ (UK)| n. |失范，混乱，社会规范缺失|A social condition marked by a breakdown of social norms and values, often resulting in alienation and a lack of purpose.  A state of normlessness; a lack of regulation in society.|The rapid social changes led to a state of anomie.  快速的社会变革导致了失范状态。|Synonyms: lawlessness, chaos, disorder, normlessness|Antonyms: order, stability, social cohesion|noun

anomie: /ˈænəmi/ (US), /ˈænəmi/ (UK)| n. |失范，混乱，社会规范缺失|A personal state of mind characterized by a lack of ethical or social standards.|He felt a sense of anomie after losing his job and his family.  失去工作和家庭后，他感到一种失范感。|Synonyms: alienation, isolation, rootlessness|Antonyms: belonging, connection, integration|noun

anomalies: /əˈnɒməliz/ (UK), /əˈnɑːməliz/ (US)| n. |异常，反常，不规则|Something that deviates from what is standard, normal, or expected.  The plural form of anomaly.|The scientists were puzzled by the anomalies in the data. 科学家们对数据中的异常现象感到困惑。|Synonyms: irregularities, deviations, aberrations, exceptions|Antonyms: norms, standards, regularities|noun
