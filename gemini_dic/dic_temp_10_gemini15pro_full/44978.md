crystallised:/ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd/ (UK) /ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪzd/ (US)| adj. | 结晶的；明确的；固定的 |  Having become fixed and definite in form or structure; (of sugar) having formed crystals. | The honey had crystallised in the jar.  蜂蜜在罐子里结晶了。|  His vague ideas crystallised into a concrete plan. 他模糊的想法变成了一个具体的计划。 | synonyms: solidified, hardened, concretized, set, established | antonyms: fluid, amorphous, vague, uncertain | adjective

crystallize:/ˈkrɪstəlaɪz/ (UK) /ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪz/ (US)| v. | 结晶；使明确；使固定 |  To cause to form crystals or assume crystalline form; to make or become definite and clear.  | The cold temperatures caused the solution to crystallize. 低温使溶液结晶。 |  The discussion helped to crystallize his thoughts. 讨论帮助他理清了思路。 | synonyms: solidify, harden, concretize, coalesce,  consolidate | antonyms: dissolve, melt, liquefy | verb
