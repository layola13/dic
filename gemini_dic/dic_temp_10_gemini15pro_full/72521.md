flushed: /flʌʃt/ | adj. |  脸红的；激动的；兴奋的；冲水的 | Reddened in the face, typically as a result of strong emotion, heat, or illness; also, excited or exhilarated. |  例句：Her face was flushed with anger. 她的脸因愤怒而涨得通红。

例句：He was flushed with excitement after winning the race.  他赢得比赛后激动得满脸通红。

近义词：reddened, ruddy, rosy, blushing, thrilled, excited | 反义词：pale, ashen, calm, composed | 词性：形容词

flushed: /flʌʃt/ | adj. | 齐平的；与…平的 |  Level with another surface. | 例句：The tiles are flushed with the wall.  瓷砖与墙壁齐平。

例句：Make sure the door is flushed with the frame. 确保门与门框齐平。

近义词：level, even | 反义词：uneven, protruding | 词性：形容词

flushed: /flʌʃt/ | v. |  冲洗；清除；使（脸）发红 | To cleanse or empty (something) with a sudden flow of water; to cause (someone's face) to become red. | 例句：I flushed the toilet. 我冲了厕所。

例句：Embarrassment flushed her cheeks. 尴尬使她脸红了。

近义词：rinse, clean, redden | 反义词：retain, hold | 词性：动词


词源: From Middle English *fluschen, from Old French flux, ultimately from Latin fluere ("to flow").
