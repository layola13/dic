blighty: /ˈblaɪti/| adj. (old-fashioned, informal)|轻微受伤的；（尤指士兵在战争时期）轻伤的|slightly injured or unwell (especially of soldiers during World War I).|例句：He got a blighty wound in the leg. 他的腿受了轻伤。|例句：He was lucky to get away with only a blighty one. 他很幸运，只受了点轻伤。|近义词：slightly injured, lightly wounded|反义词:seriously injured, badly wounded|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Hindi bilāyatī "foreign, European," from Persian vilāyat "province, foreign land," from Arabic wilāya(t) "authority, governorship, province."  Originally referred to India under British rule, later specifically to England itself from the perspective of British soldiers abroad.
