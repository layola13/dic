mercurial:/mərˈkjʊəriəl/ | adj. | 善变的，反复无常的；水银的 | Characterized by rapid and unpredictable changeableness of mood; relating to or containing mercury. | Her mercurial temperament made it difficult to predict her reactions. 她善变的脾气让人很难预测她的反应。| The thermometer contained mercurial liquid. 温度计里装的是水银液体。 | synonyms: volatile, erratic, fickle, capricious, temperamental | antonyms: stable, steady, constant, predictable | adjective

mercurial:/mərˈkjʊəriəl/ | n. | 汞制剂 | A medicine or other substance containing mercury. |  The doctor prescribed a mercurial for the skin condition. 医生开了一种汞制剂来治疗皮肤病。| synonyms: quicksilver, hydrargyrum | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mercuriālis* "of or pertaining to Mercury," from Latin *Mercurius* "the god Mercury," also the name of the planet and the metal.  The association with quick and changeable moods comes from the perceived swiftness of the planet Mercury and the fluidity of the metal.
