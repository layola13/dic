obsolescence:/ˌɒbsəˈlɛsəns/ /ˌɑːbsəˈlɛsəns/ | n. | 过时，陈旧，淘汰 | the process of becoming obsolete or outdated because of advances in technology, changes in fashion, etc. |  The rapid obsolescence of electronic devices is a major environmental concern. 电子设备的快速淘汰是一个主要的环境问题。|  Planned obsolescence is a strategy used by some companies to ensure that consumers buy new products regularly. 计划性报废是一些公司使用的策略，以确保消费者定期购买新产品。| synonyms: outdatedness, antiquity, superannuation | antonyms: modernity, currency, relevance | noun

obsolescent:/ˌɒbsəˈlɛsənt/ /ˌɑːbsəˈlɛsənt/ | adj. | 即将过时的，逐渐被淘汰的 | becoming obsolete or outdated  | Many of the older machines are now obsolescent. 许多老机器现在已经过时了。 | Typewriters are becoming increasingly obsolescent in the digital age. 在数字时代，打字机正变得越来越过时。 | synonyms: outdated, outmoded, old-fashioned | antonyms: modern, current, up-to-date | adjective
