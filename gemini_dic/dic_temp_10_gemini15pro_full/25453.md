bulldogging:/ˈbʊlˌdɒɡɪŋ/ (US) /ˈbʊlˌdɔːɡɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |摔牛; 顽强地坚持; |The act of wrestling a steer to the ground by seizing its horns and twisting its neck, or by grabbing its nose and twisting its head.  Also refers to tenacious persistence.|He showed his bulldogging spirit by refusing to give up despite the setbacks. 他展现了顽强的精神，即使遇到挫折也拒绝放弃。|Synonyms: wrestling, grappling, tenacity, persistence, perseverance|Antonyms: surrender, quitting, yielding|noun

bulldogging:/ˈbʊlˌdɒɡɪŋ/ (US) /ˈbʊlˌdɔːɡɪŋ/ (UK)| v. |摔牛;  顽强地坚持|To wrestle a steer to the ground; to persist tenaciously.|He bulldogged his way through the crowd to get to the front. 他顽强地挤过人群到达最前面。|Synonyms: wrestle, grapple, persist, persevere|Antonyms: surrender, quit, yield|verb
