词源: Late Middle English: from Old French complexion, from late Latin complexio(n-) ‘combination, combination of humours’, from complex- ‘woven together’ (see complex). Originally in the sense ‘combination of the four humours of the body, supposed to determine a person’s temperament and physical constitution’.
