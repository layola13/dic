disorient: /dɪsˈɔːriənt/ (UK) /dɪsˈɔːriənt/ (US)
| vt. | 使迷失方向，使迷惑 | To cause (someone) to lose their sense of direction or their bearings. | The dense fog disoriented the hikers. 浓雾使徒步旅行者迷失了方向。 | synonyms: confuse, bewilder, perplex | antonyms: orient, guide, direct | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- + orientate, from Late Latin orientatus, past participle of orientare "to set toward the east," from Latin orientem (nominative oriens) "the east, rising sun," present participle of oriri "to rise."
