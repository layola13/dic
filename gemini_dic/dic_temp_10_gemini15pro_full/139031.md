oaklike: /ˈoʊklaɪk/
adjective
像橡树的；橡木似的
Resembling or characteristic of an oak tree, especially in strength, durability, or appearance.
The table was made of oaklike hardwood.  这张桌子是用类似橡木的硬木做的。
The old man had an oaklike strength.  这位老人有着像橡树一样的力量。
近义词: sturdy, robust, strong, durable
反义词: weak, fragile, flimsy
词性: adjective

词源: oak + like
oak: From Old English *āc (akin to Old Saxon ēk, Old Norse eik, Old Frisian ēk, Dutch eek, Old High German eih, German Eiche), from Proto-Germanic *aikō (akin to Latin aesculus).
like: From Old English gelīc (“like, similar, equal, resembling, probable, likely”), from Proto-Germanic *galīkaz (“having the same form”), from Proto-Indo-European *(o)lēi- (“to bend, twist”). Cognate with Dutch gelijk (“equal, like, similar”), German gleich (“equal, like, same, level, straight”), Old Norse líkr (“like”), Gothic 𐌻𐌴𐌹𐌺𐍃 (leiks, “like, similar”).
