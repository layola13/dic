neeld:/niːld/ | n. | 针 | A small, slender, pointed piece of metal with a hole at one end for thread, used in sewing. | She threaded the neeld and began to sew. 她穿好针线，开始缝纫。|Synonyms: needle, pin | Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a physical object.  Could consider "hook" as a contrasting tool.) | noun

neeld:/niːld/ | v. | (usually with "to")  迫使；强迫;  (obsolete) 刺；戳 | To force or compel someone to do something.  (Archaic) To prick or sting.  |  He was needled into accepting the offer. 他被迫接受了这份提议. (Archaic usage): The thorns needled his skin. 刺扎了他的皮肤. | Synonyms: goad, prod, coerce, pressure | Antonyms: dissuade, deter | verb
