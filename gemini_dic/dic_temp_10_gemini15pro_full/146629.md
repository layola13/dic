overhang:/ˈəʊvəhæŋ/ (British), /ˌoʊvərˈhæŋ/ (American)| n. v.|突出部分，悬垂物；逼近的威胁|A part of something that sticks out over something else.  A threat that appears likely to happen soon.|The balcony overhangs the street below.  阳台突出到下面的街道上。|synonyms: projection, protrusion, extension, ledge, shelf, canopy, eave, brow, jut, precipice|antonyms:  recess, indentation, alcove|noun

overhang:/ˈəʊvəhæŋ/ (British), /ˌoʊvərˈhæŋ/ (American)| v.|突出，悬垂；逼近，威胁|To stick out or project over something else. To loom over; to threaten.|Dark clouds overhung the city.  乌云笼罩着城市。|synonyms: project, protrude, jut, extend, loom, impend, menace|antonyms: recede, withdraw|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *oferhangian, from ofer- (over) + hangian (to hang).
