epoch's:/ˈiːpɒks/ /ˈɛpɑːks/ | n. | 时代的，纪元的 |  Possessive form of "epoch," meaning belonging to or associated with a particular period of time marked by significant events or changes. |  The epoch's defining moment was the invention of the printing press.  这个时代的决定性时刻是印刷机的发明。 | synonym: era's, age's | antonym: N/A | Possessive Noun

Etymology: From the Late Latin *epoca*, from the Greek *epokhē* meaning "pause, fixed point of time."
