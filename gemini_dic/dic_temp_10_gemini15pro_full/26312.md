buxomnesses:/ˈbʌksəmˌnɪsɪz/
n.
丰满， voluptuousness
The painting celebrated the buxomnesses of the female form. 这幅画赞美了女性丰满的体态。
The buxomnesses of the models were emphasized by their tight dresses. 模特们的紧身裙突出了她们的丰腴身材。
Near Synonyms: full-figuredness, ampleness, voluptuousness, curvaceousness
Antonyms: slenderness, thinness, slimness
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From buxom + -ness.  Buxom itself comes from Middle English *buxom, from Old English *būhsum (“bent, pliable, obedient”), from būgan (“to bend, bow”) + -sum (“-some”).  The semantic shift from "obedient" to "healthy and lively" and then to "full-bosomed" occurred in Middle English.
