deconstructor:/ˌdiːkənˈstrʌktər/ | n. |解构者，解构主义者|  Someone who deconstructs something, especially a text or work of art, typically by exposing its hidden internal assumptions and contradictions. | The literary deconstructor argued that the author's intent was irrelevant to the meaning of the text.  这位文学解构主义者认为，作者的意图与文本的意义无关。 | 近义词: analyst, interpreter | 反义词: constructor, builder | 词性: noun

deconstructors:/ˌdiːkənˈstrʌktərz/ | n. |解构器，解构函数（尤指计算机科学中）| In computer science, a destructor is a special method within a class that is automatically called when an object of that class is destroyed or goes out of scope. It is used to release resources, such as memory, that were allocated by the object during its lifetime.  | The destructor ensures that dynamically allocated memory is freed when the object is no longer needed. 析构函数确保在不再需要对象时释放动态分配的内存。 | 近义词: finalizer (in some contexts) | 反义词: constructor | 词性: noun
