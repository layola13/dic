coercers:/koʊˈɜːrsərz/
|n.|胁迫者，强迫者|People who coerce others; those who compel someone to do something against their will by using force, threats, or intimidation.|The coercers threatened the villagers into giving up their land. 胁迫者威胁村民放弃他们的土地。|Synonyms: bullies, intimidators, oppressors, extorters|Antonyms: protectors, defenders, supporters|Noun

coercer:/koʊˈɜːrsər/
|n.|胁迫者，强迫者|A person who coerces someone; someone who compels someone to do something against their will by using force, threats, or intimidation.|The coercer was eventually arrested and charged with extortion.  这个胁迫者最终被逮捕并被指控敲诈勒索。|Synonyms: bully, intimidator, oppressor, extorter|Antonyms: protector, defender, supporter|Noun

词源:
From Latin *coerciō, *coerciōnis, from coercere "to restrain, confine, enclose, encircle, hold together, collect; to repress, curb, check," from com- "together" (see com-) + arcēre "to enclose, confine, contain, ward off" (see arktos).
