accordion:/əˈkɔːrdiən/ | n. | 手风琴 | A portable musical instrument with bellows and a keyboard, producing sound by forcing air through reeds. | He plays the accordion beautifully. 他手风琴弹得优美。 | Synonyms: squeezebox | Antonyms: None | Noun

accordion:/əˈkɔːrdiən/ | adj. | 可折叠的，伸缩的 |  Folding or creased like the bellows of an accordion. |  The photographer used an accordion-style lens. 摄影师使用了可折叠式镜头。| Synonyms: foldable, collapsible, expanding, contracting | Antonyms: rigid, fixed | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Akkordion*, from *Akkord* ("chord," musical harmony), itself from Italian *accordo*.  Ultimately from Latin *accordāre* ("to bring into agreement, to tune"). The instrument was named for its ability to play chords.
