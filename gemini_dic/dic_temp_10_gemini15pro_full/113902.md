madrigalian: /ˌmædrɪˈɡæliən/ | adj. n. | 牧歌的；牧歌体；牧歌式歌曲 | Relating to, written in, or characteristic of the style of a madrigal (a part-song for several voices, especially one of the Renaissance period).  Also, a madrigal itself. |  

Example 1: The composer's madrigalian style evokes a sense of pastoral peace.  作曲家的牧歌风格唤起了一种田园般的宁静感。

Example 2:  She performed a beautiful madrigalian at the concert. 她在音乐会上演唱了一首优美的牧歌。


Synonym:  part-song,  choral, a cappella (when unaccompanied) | 反义词: (No direct opposite, perhaps "non-madrigalian" or music of a contrasting style like "operatic" or "symphonic" could be considered) | 词性: adjective, noun

Etymology: From Italian "madrigale,"  ultimately of uncertain origin, possibly from Late Latin *matricale "rustic song," from mater "mother."
