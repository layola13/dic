metacism:/ˈmɛtəˌsɪzəm/| n.|浊音化，m的浊音化|A change undergone by a sound, especially a stop consonant, whereby it becomes a fricative, as when /t/ becomes /s/ or /θ/.  Specifically, the change of a Greek or other Indo-European *b, *d, *g, *gw to /m/ in certain positions.|The metacism of *b to /m/ is a common feature in several Indo-European languages.|Synonym: lenition|Antonym: fortition|noun
