burlesque:/bərˈlɛsk/ | n. adj. | 滑稽模仿；滑稽戏；讽刺作品 | A theatrical entertainment of broad and earthy humor, typically including songs and striptease.  A literary or dramatic work that makes fun of something by means of exaggerated imitation. |  例句：The burlesque show was full of bawdy jokes and slapstick humor. 滑稽戏充满了粗俗的笑话和闹剧式的幽默。

例句：Her novel is a burlesque of Victorian society. 她的小说是对维多利亚时代社会的一部讽刺作品。| 近义词：parody, satire, lampoon, caricature, travesty | 反义词：serious drama, tragedy | 词性：名词，形容词

burlesque:/bərˈlɛsk/ | adj. | 滑稽的；讽刺的；模仿的 |  Amusingly exaggerated or imitative. | 例句：He gave a burlesque performance of the opera. 他滑稽地模仿了这部歌剧。

例句：The play was a burlesque imitation of a Shakespearean tragedy.  这出戏是对莎士比亚悲剧的滑稽模仿。 | 近义词：comic, humorous, satirical, mocking | 反义词：serious, respectful | 词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *burlesco*, from *burla* "joke, mockery," of uncertain origin.
