biting:/ˈbaɪtɪŋ/| adj.| 刺骨的；辛辣的；尖刻的|Causing a sharply painful or stinging sensation, either physically or emotionally.  Can also refer to a sharp or incisive quality in speech or writing.|The biting wind made my eyes water. 刺骨的寒风使我眼睛直流泪。|Synonyms: sharp, stinging, piercing, pungent, acrid, caustic, trenchant|Antonyms: mild, gentle, soothing, bland|adjective

biting:/ˈbaɪtɪŋ/| n.| 咬；叮|The act of gripping or cutting something with the teeth.|The dog's biting caused a serious injury. 狗的咬伤造成了严重的伤害。|Synonyms: gnawing, nipping|Antonyms: releasing|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bityng*, present participle of *biten* (“to bite”), from Old English *bītan* (“to bite”), from Proto-Germanic *bītaną* (“to bite”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰeyd- (“to split, cleave, bite”).  Cognate with Dutch *bijten*, German *beißen*, Gothic *𐌱𐌴𐌹𐍄𐌰𐌽* (*beitan*).
