canceling:/ˈkænsəlɪŋ/ | v. | 取消，撤销 |The act of stopping or discontinuing something that was planned or scheduled. |  They are canceling the concert due to the storm. 他们因为暴风雨取消了音乐会。| synonyms: abandoning, revoking, withdrawing, rescinding | antonyms: confirming, approving, establishing, proceeding | verb (present participle)

canceling:/ˈkænsəlɪŋ/ | adj. | 取消的 |  Relating to or involving the act of canceling something. | The canceling flight caused a lot of inconvenience. 取消的航班造成了很多不便。| synonyms: null, void, abolished | antonyms: valid, effective, confirmed | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old North French *canceller, from Medieval Latin cancellare "to cross out with lines, delete, make void," from Latin cancelli "lattice work," diminutive of cancer "lattice." The sense evolution is from drawing lines across a writing to obliterate it.
