machiavellianism: /ˌmækiəˈveliənɪzəm/ | n. | 马基雅维利主义；权谋术；不择手段 |  The employment of cunning and duplicity in statecraft or in general conduct, named after Niccolò Machiavelli.  | His machiavellianism helped him climb the corporate ladder quickly, but it also made him many enemies. 他运用马基雅维利主义的手段很快爬上了公司的阶梯，但也树敌众多。 | 近义词: cunning, duplicity, ruthlessness, expediency, cynicism | 反义词: honesty, integrity, scrupulousness | 词性: noun

machiavellian: /ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | adj. | 马基雅维利式的；狡猾的；不择手段的 |  Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career. |  He employed machiavellian tactics to win the election. 他采用了马基雅维利式的策略赢得了选举。 | 近义词: cunning, devious, unscrupulous, artful, wily | 反义词: honest, principled, straightforward | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Niccolò Machiavelli (1469-1527), Italian Renaissance political philosopher and historian, author of The Prince.  The term "Machiavellianism" arose from interpretations, often misinterpretations, of his work.
