adorer:/əˈdɔːrər/ (US) /əˈdɔːrə(r)/ (UK)| n.|崇拜者，爱慕者|Someone who adores; a devoted admirer or lover.  A person who feels great admiration or love for someone or something.|She has many secret adorers. 她有很多秘密的崇拜者。

He is a great adorer of art. 他是一位艺术的狂热爱好者。|近义词：fan, devotee, admirer, lover|反义词：critic, detractor, hater|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *adoreor*, from Latin *adorator*, agent noun from *adorare* "to adore".

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He adores his wife. = 他很爱他的妻子。
She is an adorer of classical music. = 她是一位古典音乐的爱好者。

一般过去时:
He adored his grandmother. = 他很爱他的祖母。
She adored the paintings in the museum. = 她非常喜欢博物馆里的画。

现在完成时:
He has always adored his children. = 他一直很爱他的孩子们。
She has adored that singer since she was a teenager. = 从她十几岁起，她就一直很喜欢那个歌手。

过去完成时:
He had adored her for years before they finally met. = 在他们最终见面之前，他已经爱慕她多年了。
She had adored that book since she was a child. = 从孩提时代起，她就一直很喜欢那本书。

将来时:
He will always adore his family. = 他将永远爱他的家人。
She will adore the new puppy. = 她会很喜欢那只新小狗。

虚拟语气:
If I were an adorer of opera, I would go to see more performances. = 如果我是歌剧的爱好者，我会去看更多的演出。
I wish I were an adorer of spicy food. = 我希望我喜欢吃辣的食物。

被动语态:
The author is adored by her fans. = 这位作家受到粉丝们的喜爱。
The painting was adored by critics. = 这幅画受到了评论家的喜爱。

疑问句:

Who are your adorers? = 谁是你的崇拜者？

否定句:

She doesn't adore him anymore. = 她不再爱他了。
