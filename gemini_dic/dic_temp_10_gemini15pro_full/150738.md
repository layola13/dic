parachute:/ˈpærəʃuːt/| n. vi. vt.|降落伞；跳伞|A cloth canopy which fills with air and allows a person or heavy object attached to it to descend slowly when dropped from an aircraft; to jump from an aircraft using a parachute.|He parachuted out of the burning plane. 他跳伞离开了燃烧的飞机。|chute|None|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *parachutiste*, from parachute + -iste.  Parachute comes from the Italian para- (protecting against) + French chute (fall).
