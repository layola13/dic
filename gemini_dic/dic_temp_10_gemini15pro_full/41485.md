corns:/kɔːrnz/| n.|玉米; 鸡眼|Kernels of corn, especially dried and used as food (in British English, the word 'maize' is more commonly used for this).  A small, hard area of skin that develops on a toe or foot, usually caused by pressure or friction.|例句：The farmer grew corns in his field. 农民在他的田里种玉米。

例句：She had a painful corn on her little toe. 她的脚小趾上有一个疼痛的鸡眼。|近义词：(for maize) maize, grain; (for callus) callus, bunion|反义词: None|词性: Noun


corns:/kɔːrnz/| v. (usually passive)|用玉米喂养; 腌制|To preserve and flavor (meat or other food) in a brine made with salt and spices, especially saltpeter.|例句：The beef was corned for several days before being cooked. 牛肉在烹煮前用盐腌了好几天。

例句：The chickens were corned with cracked corn. 小鸡们被喂食碎玉米。|近义词：(for preserve) preserve, cure, pickle|反义词: None|词性: Verb
