feynesses:/ˈfeɪnɪsnɪsɪz/| n. | 优雅；精致；精妙；巧妙 |  The quality of being feigned; artificiality; pretense.  A display of insincere or affected refinement. Plural of feyness. |  Her feynesses were obvious to everyone, a thinly veiled attempt to impress. 她矫揉造作的样子每个人都看得出来，只是为了给人留下深刻印象而做的拙劣掩饰。| 近义词：pretenses, affectations, artificiality, insincerity | 反义词：genuineness, sincerity, naturalness | 词性: noun

Etymology: From *feyness*, derived from *fey* (giving an impression of vague unworldliness and otherworldliness) + *-ness* (suffix forming nouns expressing state, condition, or quality). Ultimately from Middle English *fay*, from Old French *fae*, from Vulgar Latin *fata*, from Latin *fātum* (fate, destiny).
