frocked:/frɒkt/| adj.|穿着礼服的，穿着教士袍服的|Dressed in a frock or a long robe, typically used to describe clergy or those in formal attire.|The newly frocked priest gave his first sermon.  新任命的穿着教士袍服的牧师做了第一次布道。|Synonyms: robed, gowned, vested|Antonyms: unfrocked, defrocked|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *froken, from Old English *froc "frock, garment, coat of mail," of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Old French froc "monk's habit," and ultimately from Late Latin froccus.  The sense of "wearing a frock" developed later.
