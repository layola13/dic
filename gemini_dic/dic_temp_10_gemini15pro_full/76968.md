garbled:/ˈɡɑːrbəld/ | adj. | 混乱的，含糊不清的，失真的 |  (of a message, sound, or transmission) distorted or confused; unclear.  From "garble," meaning to mix up or confuse, ultimately from Arabic "gharbala," meaning to sift. | The message was so garbled, I couldn't understand a word. 这条消息太混乱了，我一个字也听不懂。 | synonyms: distorted, jumbled, scrambled, incoherent, unintelligible | antonyms: clear, distinct, coherent, intelligible | adjective


garble: /ˈɡɑːrbl/ | vt. |  篡改，歪曲，断章取义; (of a message, sound, or transmission) distort or confuse (a message, sound, or transmission), typically by suppressing or adding parts. |  He deliberately garbled the facts to make himself look good. 他故意歪曲事实，让自己看起来不错。 | synonyms: distort, misrepresent, falsify, twist, manipulate | antonyms: clarify, explain, elucidate | verb
