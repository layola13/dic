boatmanship: /ˈbəʊtmənʃɪp/ (UK) /ˈboʊtmənʃɪp/ (US)
n.
操船技巧；驾船技术
The skill in handling a boat.
His boatmanship was superb, navigating the treacherous rapids with ease. 他的操船技术高超，轻松地驾驭了险峻的急流。
近义词: seamanship, navigation, sailing skill
反义词: incompetence, inexperience
词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From boatman + -ship. Boatman from boat + man.  Boat from Old English *bāt (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *baitaz, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰeydʰ- (“to split, cleave”). -ship from Old English -scipe (suffix forming abstract nouns).
