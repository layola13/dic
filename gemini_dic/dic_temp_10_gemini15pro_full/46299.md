d-glucoses:/diːˈɡluːkoʊsiːz/| n. |D-葡萄糖（复数）|Plural form of d-glucose, referring to multiple molecules of the right-handed isomer of glucose.  D-glucose is a simple sugar (monosaccharide) and is the most common form of glucose used by living organisms.|
The solution contained several d-glucoses.  该溶液包含几种D-葡萄糖。
|Synonyms:  dextrorotatory glucose (more formal), dextrose|Antonyms: l-glucose|Noun
