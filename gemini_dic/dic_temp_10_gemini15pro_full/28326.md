carageen:/ˈkærəɡiːn/| n.|角叉菜，鹿角菜；爱尔兰苔藓|A type of red seaweed (Chondrus crispus) that grows along the rocky parts of the Atlantic coasts of Europe and North America. It is used as a thickening agent in food and other products.|Carageen is commonly used in making desserts. 角叉菜通常用于制作甜点。|Synonyms: Irish moss, chondrus crispus|Antonyms: None|Noun

carrageen:/ˈkærəɡiːn/| n.|角叉菜胶|A gelatinous substance extracted from carageen seaweed, used as a thickener, stabilizer, and gelling agent in food and other products.|Carrageenan is used as a stabilizer in ice cream. 角叉菜胶用作冰淇淋中的稳定剂。|Synonyms: carrageenan, Irish moss extract|Antonyms: None|Noun
