admissibilities: /ədˌmɪsəˈbɪlətiz/ | n. |可受理性，可接纳性|The admissibilities of evidence in court are strictly regulated.  在法庭上，证据的可采纳性受到严格的监管。|The judge questioned the admissibilities of the witness's testimony. 法官质疑证人证词的可采纳性。| 近义词：acceptability, allowability | 反义词: inadmissibility, unacceptability | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "admissible" + "-ities" (suffix forming plural nouns). "Admissible" comes from Late Middle English: from Old French admissible, from late Latin admissibilis, from Latin admittere ‘admit.’

There are fewer distinct grammatical usages for "admissibilities" as it's a plural noun.  Here are some examples in different contexts:

The admissibilities of the documents were challenged by the defense lawyer. = 文件的可采纳性受到了辩护律师的质疑。
