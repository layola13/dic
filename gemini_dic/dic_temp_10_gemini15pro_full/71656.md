flattering:/ˈflætərɪŋ/| adj.|奉承的；讨好的；使显得更漂亮的|Making someone look or feel better than usual, often in a way that is not sincere.|That dress is very flattering on you. 那件衣服你穿真漂亮。|近义词：complimentary, praising, enhancing|反义词：unflattering, critical|词性:形容词
词源: From the verb "flatter," from Old French "flater" (to stroke, caress, flatter), from Latin "flatus" (a blowing, puff of wind).  The original sense was related to stroking or caressing, which developed into the metaphorical meaning of pleasing someone with insincere praise.
