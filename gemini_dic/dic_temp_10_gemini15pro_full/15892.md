ballot:/ˈbælət/ (US) /ˈbælɒt/ (UK)| n. v.|选票；投票；投票表决|A piece of paper used for voting, typically marked secretly.  A process of voting.|例句：
The election was decided by ballot.  选举通过投票方式决定。
He cast his ballot for the Republican candidate. 他投票支持共和党候选人。|近义词：vote, poll, election|反义词：None (No direct antonym for the concept of balloting itself)|noun, verb

ballot:/ˈbælət/ (US) /ˈbælɒt/ (UK)| v.|投票；投票表决|To vote, typically in secret.|例句：
The union members will ballot on the new contract next week. 工会成员下周将对新合同进行投票表决。
They balloted to choose a new leader. 他们投票选出一位新领导人。|近义词：vote, poll, elect|反义词：None (No direct antonym for the action of balloting)|verb
