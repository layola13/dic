cullis:/ˈkʌlɪs/| n.|滤器，格栅，闸门|A strainer or filter, especially one of perforated metal or wire; a portcullis.|The chef strained the soup through a fine-meshed cullis.|近义词:strainer, filter, sieve, colander|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "blockage" in a functional sense)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *coliz* "colander, strainer," derived from *couler* "to strain, flow," from Latin *colare* "to filter, strain."  The word also came to mean "portcullis" due to the resemblance of the grating of a portcullis to a strainer.
