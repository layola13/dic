continuous /kənˈtɪnjuəs/ | adj. | 连续的，持续的 | Forming an unbroken whole; without interruption. | The continuous noise from the construction site was unbearable.  建筑工地持续不断的噪音令人难以忍受。| continuous flow of traffic 车流不息 | synonyms: uninterrupted, constant, incessant, ceaseless | antonyms: intermittent, sporadic, discontinuous | adjective

continuous /kənˈtɪnjuəs/ | adj. | 延伸的，广阔的 | Extending or prolonged in space or time. | The forest is a continuous expanse of green. 这片森林是一片连绵不断的绿色。 | a continuous line of trees 一排连绵不断的树 | synonyms: extensive, vast, widespread | antonyms: limited, restricted, confined | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *continuosus, from Latin continuus (“holding together, uninterrupted”), from continēre (“to hold together, contain”), from com- (“together”) + tenēre (“to hold”).
