ebonicses:/ɛˈbɒnɪksiːz, iːˈbɒnɪksiːz/| n.|黑人英语（的多种形式）|AAVE (African American Vernacular English) in its various forms.  It is considered by some to be a dialect of English and by others a distinct language.  The term is sometimes considered controversial due to the debate surrounding its linguistic classification and social implications.| Example 1: Linguists have studied various ebonicses across different regions of the United States.  语言学家研究了美国不同地区各种形式的黑人英语。

Example 2:  The debate about whether ebonicses are dialects or languages continues. 关于黑人英语是方言还是语言的争论仍在继续。|Synonym: African American Vernacular English (AAVE), Black English|Antonym: Standard American English|Noun
