misericorde:/ˌmɪzərɪˈkɔːrd/ | n. |怜悯；慈悲；（中世纪）短剑 |A dagger used by a knight to deliver the death stroke to a fallen opponent.  A small knife used in medieval times to deliver a coup de grâce.  An act of mercy or compassion. |He granted the defeated knight a misericorde. 他给予了战败的骑士一个痛快。The knight used his misericorde to end the suffering of the wounded soldier. 骑士用他的短剑结束受伤士兵的痛苦.  She showed the condemned man misericorde and pleaded for his life. 她对这个被判刑的人表示怜悯，并恳求饶他一命。 | 近义词：mercy, compassion, pity, clemency | 反义词：cruelty, ruthlessness, mercilessness |词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *misericorde* "mercy, pity, compassion," from Late Latin *misericordia*, from Latin *misericors* "merciful, compassionate," from *miseri* (genitive of *miser* "wretched, unfortunate") + *cor* "heart."
