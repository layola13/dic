biuret:/ˌbaɪjʊˈrɛt/ | n. |缩二脲|A compound formed by heating urea.  It is used in a chemical test for proteins.|The biuret test turned purple, indicating the presence of protein.|Synonyms: carbamylurea|Antonyms: None|noun

biuret reaction:/ˌbaɪjʊˈrɛt riˈækʃən/ | n. |缩二脲反应|A chemical test used to detect the presence of peptide bonds, and thus proteins, using biuret reagent.|The biuret reaction is a common laboratory test for proteins.|Synonyms: biuret test|Antonyms: None|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From bi- (two) + urea, as it is formed from two urea molecules.
