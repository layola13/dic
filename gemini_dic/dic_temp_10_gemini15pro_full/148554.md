overswings:/ˌoʊvərˈswɪŋz/ | n. | 过度波动；过度调整；过冲 | An excessive movement in one direction followed by a movement in the opposite direction, typically in an economic or social context.  The price of oil experienced significant overswings before stabilizing. | 例句：The pendulum overswung, hitting the wall. 钟摆过度摆动，撞到了墙。

例句：The stock market overswings are making investors nervous. 股市的过度波动让投资者感到不安。 | 近义词：fluctuations, oscillations, excesses | 反义词：stability, equilibrium | 词性: 名词

overswing:/ˌoʊvərˈswɪŋ/ | v. | 过度波动；过度调整；过冲 | To move or swing excessively in one direction and then the other. | 例句：The car overswung on the corner and nearly hit a pedestrian. 汽车在拐角处过度转向，差点撞到一个行人。

例句：The economy tends to overswing before finding a new balance. 经济往往会在找到新的平衡点之前出现过度波动。 | 近义词：overcorrect, overreact, overshoot | 反义词：stabilize, moderate | 词性: 动词
