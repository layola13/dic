monologuing: /məˈnɒləɡɪŋ/ (UK) /məˈnɑːləɡɪŋ/ (US)
| v. | 独白；自言自语 | Delivering a monologue, speaking to oneself or thinking aloud regardless of any hearers, especially in a dramatic or theatrical setting.  | He was monologuing about his childhood again. 他又在自言自语地讲述他的童年了。 | 近义词：soliloquizing, ranting, rambling | 反义词：dialoguing, conversing | 词性: Verb (present participle)

monologue: /ˈmɒnəlɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈmɑːnəlɑːɡ/ (US)
| n. | 独白；自言自语 | A long speech by one actor in a play or movie, or as part of a theatrical or broadcast program.  |  The play opened with a powerful monologue. 这部戏剧以一段强有力的独白开场。| 近义词：soliloquy, speech, address | 反义词：dialogue, conversation | 词性: Noun

词源: From French *monologuer*, from mono- 'single' + Greek logos 'speech, discourse'.
