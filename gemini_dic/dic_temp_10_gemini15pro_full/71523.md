flapjack:/ˈflæpˌdʒæk/ (US) /ˈflæpˌdʒæk/ (UK)| n.|煎饼；燕麦饼|A pancake or a type of baked bar made of rolled oats, butter, sugar, and syrup.|例句：She made a batch of delicious flapjacks for breakfast. 她做了一批美味的煎饼当早餐。

例句：He grabbed a flapjack from the bakery on his way to work. 他在上班路上从面包店买了一块燕麦饼。|近义词：pancake, oatcake, granola bar|反义词: None|词性: Noun


flapjack:/ˈflæpˌdʒæk/ (US) /ˈflæpˌdʒæk/ (UK)| n.| (British informal) 骗子; 谎话|A dishonest person; a lie.|例句：He's a flapjack, don't believe a word he says. 他是个骗子，别相信他说的任何一句话。

例句：That story is a complete flapjack!  那个故事完全是谎话！|近义词：liar, fraud, fib|反义词: None|词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
The origin of "flapjack" for pancake is uncertain, but likely comes from the sound or action of flipping the pancake in the pan. The British slang usage for "liar" or "lie" is thought to be a rhyming slang derivation of "flapmouth" (someone who talks too much or boasts).
