insularize:/ˈɪnsələrˌaɪz/ | v. | 使孤立；使隔绝；使狭隘 | To make someone or something isolated or detached, as if living on an island; to cause someone or something to become narrow-minded or uninterested in outside influences. | The community's refusal to embrace new technologies has insularized them. 这个社区拒绝接受新技术，使得他们变得封闭。

The island's remote location insularized its inhabitants, leading to the development of a unique culture.  这个岛屿偏僻的地理位置使其居民与世隔绝，从而发展出一种独特的文化。 | 近义词: isolate, seclude, alienate | 反义词: integrate, connect, broaden | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From insula (Latin) meaning "island" + -ize (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or cause to be").
