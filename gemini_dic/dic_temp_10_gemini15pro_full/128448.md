newsboy:/ˈnuːzbɔɪ/ (US), /ˈnjuːzbɔɪ/ (UK)| n.| 报童，卖报的男孩 |A boy who sells or delivers newspapers.| The newsboy shouted the headlines as he ran down the street. 报童一边沿街跑，一边大声喊着报纸的头条新闻。|Synonyms: paperboy, news vendor|Antonyms: N/A|noun

newsboy:/ˈnuːzbɔɪ/ (US), /ˈnjuːzbɔɪ/ (UK)| adj.|报童款式的（尤指帽子）|Resembling or characteristic of a newsboy, especially of a style of cap.|She wore a newsboy cap and a tweed jacket. 她戴着一顶报童帽，穿着一件粗花呢夹克。|Synonyms: N/A|Antonyms: N/A|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From news + boy.  The term arose in the late 19th and early 20th centuries when young boys commonly sold newspapers on street corners.
