clauses:/ˈklɔːzɪz/| n.|条款，从句|A clause is a group of words that contains a subject and a predicate.  It can be independent (forming a complete sentence) or dependent (relying on another clause for its meaning).|The contract includes a clause about liability.  合同包含一项关于责任的条款。|terms, provisions, stipulations|phrases, words|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *clause*, from Old French *clause* (meaning "close, end, conclusion of a sentence, article of a treaty"), from Latin *clausa*, feminine past participle of *claudere* (meaning "to close, shut, conclude").
