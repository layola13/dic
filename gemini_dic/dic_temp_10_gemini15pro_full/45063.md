cubhood:/ˈkʌbhʊd/| n.|幼崽期，幼兽期|The period of being a cub; the time when an animal is a cub.  This refers to the time in an animal's life when it is still dependent on its parents and developing into adulthood.|The lion cub spent its cubhood learning to hunt with its mother. 这只小狮子在幼崽期跟妈妈学习捕猎。|Synonyms: infancy, youth, early childhood|Antonyms: adulthood, maturity|noun

cubhood:/ˈkʌbhʊd/| n.|幼年时期，童年时期 (比喻)|Figuratively, it can refer to the early childhood of a human.  It evokes a sense of innocence and playfulness.|He remembered his cubhood fondly, a time of carefree exploration. 他深情地回忆起自己的童年，那是一段无忧无虑的探索时光。|Synonyms: childhood, infancy, early years|Antonyms: adulthood, maturity, later years|noun
