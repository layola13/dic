dipole:/ˈdaɪpoʊl/ (US), /ˈdaɪpəʊl/ (UK)| n.| 偶极子；双极子|A pair of equal and opposite charges or magnetic poles separated by a small distance.|The water molecule is a dipole. 水分子是一个偶极子。|synonyms: doublet|antonyms: monopole|noun

dipole moment:/ˈdaɪpoʊl ˈmoʊmənt/ (US), /ˈdaɪpəʊl ˈməʊmənt/ (UK)| n.| 偶极矩|A measure of the polarity of a chemical bond or molecule, calculated as the product of the charge separation and the bond length.|The dipole moment of water is high. 水的偶极矩很高。|synonyms: electric dipole moment|antonyms: N/A|noun
