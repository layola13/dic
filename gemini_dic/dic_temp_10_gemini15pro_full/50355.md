depoliticized:/diːpəˈlɪtɪsaɪzd/
adjective
去政治化的；非政治化的
Removed from political influence or considerations.  Stripped of political character.
The organization aimed to create a depoliticized environment for discussion.  该组织旨在创建一个去政治化的讨论环境。
The report offered a depoliticized analysis of the economic situation.  该报告对经济形势进行了非政治化的分析。
近义词:neutral, non-political, objective
反义词:politicized, partisan
词性:adjective

depoliticize:/diːpəˈlɪtɪsaɪz/
verb
去政治化；使非政治化
To remove from political influence or considerations. To make something less political.
The government attempted to depoliticize the education system. 政府试图使教育系统去政治化。
They tried to depoliticize the issue by focusing on its economic aspects. 他们试图通过关注其经济方面来淡化该问题的政治色彩。
近义词:neutralize
反义词:politicize
词性:verb

depoliticization:/diːpəˌlɪtɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən/
noun
去政治化
The act or process of removing something from political influence or considerations.
The depoliticization of the civil service was a key reform. 公务员制度的去政治化是一项关键的改革。
There has been a growing call for the depoliticization of the judiciary.  越来越多的人呼吁司法机构去政治化。
近义词:neutralization
反义词:politicization
词性:noun


词源:  "de-" (prefix meaning "remove" or "reverse") + "politicize" (from "political") + "-ed/-ize/-ation" (suffixes indicating the verb/adjective/noun forms).  Ultimately from the Greek "politikos" (of citizens or the state).
