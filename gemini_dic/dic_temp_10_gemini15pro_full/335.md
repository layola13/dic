abhorred:/əbˈhɔːrd/ /əbˈhɔːrd/| v. |憎恶，厌恶，痛恨|regarded with disgust and hatred.|She abhorred cruelty to animals. 她痛恨虐待动物。| He abhorred the idea of leaving his family. 他厌恶离开家人的想法。 | synonyms: detest, loathe, despise, abominate | antonyms: love, adore, cherish | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *abhorren*, from Old French *abhorrer*, from Latin *abhorrēre* (“to shrink back from, shudder at, have an aversion to”), from *ab* (“away from”) + *horrēre* (“to bristle, shudder, tremble”).

USAGE->
Simple Present Tense:
He abhors violence. = 他憎恶暴力。
She abhors any form of discrimination. = 她厌恶任何形式的歧视。

Simple Past Tense:
They abhorred the dictator's policies. = 他们憎恶独裁者的政策。
She abhorred the thought of betraying her friend. = 她厌恶背叛朋友的想法。

Present Perfect Tense:
I have always abhorred dishonesty. = 我一直憎恶不诚实。
He has abhorred spiders since he was a child. = 他从小就讨厌蜘蛛。

Past Perfect Tense:
She had abhorred the idea of moving to a new city, but eventually she came to enjoy it. = 她曾经厌恶搬到一个新城市的想法，但最终她开始享受它。
They had abhorred the war for years before it finally ended. = 在战争最终结束之前，他们已经憎恨了它很多年。

Future Tense:
I will always abhor injustice. = 我将永远憎恶不公正。
She will abhor the consequences of her actions. = 她会憎恶自己行为的后果。

Subjunctive Mood:
If I abhorred lying, I would tell the truth. = 如果我憎恶撒谎，我会说出真相。
I wish I didn't abhor public speaking. = 我希望我不讨厌公开演讲。

Passive Voice (Rarely used with "abhor"):
The idea was abhorred by many. = 这个想法被许多人憎恶。
Such behavior is abhorred by society. = 这种行为被社会所憎恶。

Interrogative Form:
Do you abhor the smell of cigarettes? = 你讨厌香烟的味道吗？
Did she abhor the idea of working there? = 她讨厌在那里工作的想法吗？

Negative Form:
I do not abhor all insects. = 我并不讨厌所有昆虫。
She did not abhor the changes. = 她并不讨厌这些变化。
