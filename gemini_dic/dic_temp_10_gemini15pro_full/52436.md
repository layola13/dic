didynamian: /ˌdaɪdaɪˈneɪmiən/ | adj. | 二强的，双体雄蕊的 |  Having two pairs of stamens, one pair long and the other short, as in the flowers of the mint family (Lamiaceae).  This term is used in botany to describe a specific type of flower structure. | Example: The mint flower is didynamous. 薄荷花是二强的。 Example: Didynamous stamens are a characteristic feature of many plants in the Lamiaceae family. 二强雄蕊是唇形科许多植物的一个特征。 | Synonym: tetradynamous (similar concept but with six stamens) | Antonym:  N/A (Lack of a direct opposite term) | adjective
