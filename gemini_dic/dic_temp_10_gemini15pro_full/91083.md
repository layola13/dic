huttoning: /ˈhʌtənɪŋ/ | n. | 赫托宁式耕作; 防御性耕作 | A system of plowing and cultivating that leaves large amounts of crop residue on the soil surface to reduce erosion. | Huttoning helps to retain soil moisture and prevent erosion. 赫托宁式耕作有助于保持土壤水分和防止侵蚀。 | Example: The farmer practiced huttoning to improve soil health.  这位农民采用赫托宁式耕作来改善土壤健康。| Synonym: conservation tillage, no-till farming | Antonym: conventional tillage | noun
