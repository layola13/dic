chain:/tʃeɪn/| n. v.|链条，一系列，连锁；束缚，拴住|A series of connected links or rings, typically made of metal; a sequence of connected things or events.|The dog was fastened to a post with a heavy chain.  这条狗被一条粗重的链子拴在柱子上。|synonyms: series, sequence, succession, progression, string, concatenation |antonyms: disconnection, separation, individual, single|noun
chain:/tʃeɪn/| v.|用链条拴住，束缚，限制|To fasten or secure with a chain; to restrict the freedom of.|He chained his bicycle to the railings. 他把自行车锁在栏杆上。|synonyms: bind, fetter, shackle, manacle, restrain, confine |antonyms: free, liberate, release, unchain|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *chaine*, from Latin *catena* "chain, fetter," from *cat-,* possibly related to the PIE root *ked-* "to bind."
