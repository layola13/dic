nomadism:/ˈnəʊmædɪzəm/ (US) /ˈnoʊmædɪzəm/|n.|游牧；流浪|The state or practice of living as a nomad; a way of life in which people do not live continually in the same place but move cyclically or periodically.|The Tuareg people of the Sahara are known for their nomadism.|近义词：wandering, roaming, migration|反义词:sedentarism, settlement|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *nomadisme*, from *nomade* "nomad," from Late Latin *Nomas*, from Greek *nomas* (genitive *nomados*) "roaming, wandering, pasturing," from *nemein* "to pasture, distribute, allot."
