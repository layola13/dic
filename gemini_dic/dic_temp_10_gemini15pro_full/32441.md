chimera:/kaɪˈmɪərə/ /kɪˈmɪərə/
n.
嵌合体；虚构的怪物；幻想
A fire-breathing female monster with a lion's head, a goat's body, and a serpent's tail.  An illusion or fabrication of the mind; especially : an unrealizable dream. Something that exists only in the imagination and is not possible in reality.
例句：The chimera of economic stability proved elusive.  经济稳定的幻想证明是难以捉摸的。

例句：She chased chimeras of fame and fortune. 她追逐着名利双收的幻想。

近义词：illusion, fantasy, delusion, dream, figment, phantom
反义词：reality, actuality, truth
词性：noun

chimera:/kaɪˈmɪərə/ /kɪˈmɪərə/
n.
(遗传学)嵌合体
(genetics) An organism containing a mixture of genetically different tissues, formed by processes such as fusion of early embryos, grafting, or mutation.
例句：The researchers created a plant chimera by grafting different species together. 研究人员通过嫁接不同的物种创造了一种植物嵌合体。

例句：The patient was found to be a chimera, possessing two distinct sets of DNA. 发现该病人是一个嵌合体，拥有两组不同的DNA。


近义词：mosaic
反义词：(None specific -  perhaps 'homogeneous organism')
词性：noun


词源：Late Middle English: from Old French chimere, from Latin chimaera ‘she-goat’, from Greek khimaira, literally ‘year-old goat’.
