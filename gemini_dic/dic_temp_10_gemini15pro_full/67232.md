expropriationist: /ɛksˌproʊpriˈeɪʃənɪst/
n.
征收者；强占者；没收者
Someone who advocates or practices expropriation, especially of private property by the state.
The expropriationist policies of the new government caused widespread unrest among landowners.  新政府的征收政策在土地所有者中引起了广泛的不安。
近义词: confiscator, seizer, appropriator
反义词: donor, giver, contributor
词性: noun


expropriate: /ɛksˈproʊprieɪt/
v.
征用，没收；强占
(especially of the government) to take property from its owner for public use or benefit.
The government expropriated the land for the construction of a new highway. 政府征用了这块土地来修建一条新的高速公路。
近义词: seize, confiscate, commandeer
反义词: return, give back, restore
词性: verb


expropriation: /ɛksˌproʊpriˈeɪʃən/
n.
征用；没收；强占
The act of expropriating something, especially the seizure of property by the state.
The expropriation of the farmlands led to a decline in agricultural production.  农田的征用导致农业产量下降。
近义词: confiscation, seizure, appropriation
反义词: restitution, return, restoration
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin ex- (out of, from) + proprius (one's own).
