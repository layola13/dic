garrotters:/ˈɡærətərz/
| n. |勒索凶手；扼杀者|A person who garrottes someone, especially a robber who strangles their victim.  勒死某人的人，尤指勒死受害者的强盗。|The garrotters were apprehended by the police. 警察逮捕了勒索凶手。|
| n. |使用勒索的人|Someone who uses a garrotte. 使用勒索的人|The garrotters attacked the wealthy merchant in the dark alley. 勒索凶手在黑暗的小巷里袭击了那位富商。|
|近义词：stranglers, assassins, murderers, thugs|
|反义词:protector, rescuer|
|词性:noun (plural)|


garrotter:/ɡəˈrɒtə(r)/
| n. |勒索；绞杀|A cord or wire with handles at each end, used for strangling someone. 一种两端带柄的绳索或金属丝，用于勒死人。|The garrotter was found hidden in the assailant's pocket. 勒索被发现藏在袭击者的口袋里。|
| vt. |勒死；扼杀；压制|To strangle (someone) with a garrotte; to throttle. 用勒索勒死（某人）；扼杀。|He was garrotted with a piano wire. 他被钢琴丝勒死了。|
|近义词:strangle, choke, throttle, suffocate|
|反义词:resuscitate, revive|
|词性:noun, verb|



ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *garrote* "cudgel, strangling stick," from Old Provençal *garrot* "stick, club," of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Vulgar Latin *garra* "claw."
