caitiff:/ˈkeɪtɪf/| n. adj.| 懦夫，卑鄙的人；可怜的，卑劣的 |A cowardly and despicable person; wretched, base. |The caitiff cowered before the king, begging for mercy.  这个懦夫畏缩在国王面前乞求怜悯。|近义词: coward, villain, wretch, miscreant|反义词: hero, brave, noble|词性: 名词, 形容词
Etymology: From Old French *caitif*, from Latin *captīvus* "captive," past participle of *capere* "to take, seize."  The semantic shift from "captive" to "wretched" and then to "base, cowardly" occurred in French.
