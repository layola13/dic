leftier: /ˈlɛf.ti.ər/ (US), /ˈleftiə(r)/ (UK)| adj.| 更左的，更左倾的| further to the left, either physically or politically |  例句：The party's policies are becoming leftier. 该党的政策越来越左倾。|近义词：more left-wing, more radical, more progressive|反义词:rightist, more conservative, more right-wing|词性:形容词
Etymology: From 'left' + '-ier' (comparative suffix). 'Left' originally referred to the weaker hand (most people are right-handed), and later acquired political connotations associated with groups advocating for social and political change. The '-ier' suffix is used to form the comparative degree of adjectives.
