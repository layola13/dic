frontbencher:/ˈfrʌntˌbɛntʃər/ | n. |前座议员；前排议员|A member of Parliament who holds a significant position in their party and sits on the front benches of the House of Commons or House of Lords.  A frontbencher is usually a minister, shadow minister, or other senior party official. | The frontbencher delivered a passionate speech on the new policy. 前座议员就新政策发表了热情洋溢的讲话。| synonym:  senior politician, leading member, party leader | antonym: backbencher | noun
