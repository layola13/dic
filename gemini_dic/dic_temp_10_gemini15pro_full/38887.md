conferred:/kənˈfɜːrd/ /kənˈfɝːd/| v. |授予，商讨，商议 |To grant or bestow (a title, degree, benefit, or right). To discuss something with someone else; consult; have discussions;|The university conferred an honorary degree on the renowned scientist. 大学授予这位著名科学家荣誉学位。|They conferred about the next step in the project.  他们商讨了项目的下一步。|Synonyms: award, bestow, grant, discuss, deliberate, consult|Antonyms: revoke, rescind, withhold, refuse|verb

conferred:/kənˈfɜːrd/ /kənˈfɝːd/| adj. |被授予的，协商的 (This usage is less common, usually appearing as part of a larger phrase like "conferred status") |Granted or bestowed, especially in a formal ceremony. Discussed or deliberated upon.|He enjoyed his newly conferred status as team captain. 他很享受他新获得的队长身份。|The conferred decision was accepted by all parties. 协商后的决定得到了各方的接受。|Synonyms: awarded, bestowed, granted, discussed|Antonyms: revoked, rescinded, withheld, refused|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *conferre*, from *com-* "together" + *ferre* "to bear, carry".
