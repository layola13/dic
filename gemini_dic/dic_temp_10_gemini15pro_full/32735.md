chloroleucite:/ˌklɔːroʊˈluːsaɪt/| n. |氯白榴石|A rare feldspathoid mineral, essentially a potassium-bearing analogue of leucite containing chlorine.  It forms in potassium-rich, silica-poor volcanic rocks.|Chloroleucite was found in the volcanic ejecta. 氯白榴石在火山喷出物中被发现。|Synonym: none (very specific mineral)|Antonym: none (mineralogy doesn't typically use antonyms in this way)|noun
