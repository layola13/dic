heathenized:/ˈhiːðənaɪzd/
| adj. |异教化的，皈依异教的|Converted to heathenism; made heathen. Having adopted the customs or beliefs of heathens. |The Roman Empire gradually heathenized the conquered territories.| 近义词:paganized, barbarized| 反义词: Christianized, civilized| 词性:形容词

词源:
From heathen + -ize.  "Heathen" comes from Old English *hæðen "inhabitant of uncultivated land,"  later "one who does not acknowledge the God of the Bible."  "-ize" is a suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become (like)," from Greek -izein, also from French -iser.
