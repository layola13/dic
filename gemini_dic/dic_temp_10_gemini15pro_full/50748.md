derringers:/ˈdɛrɪndʒərz/
| n. |德林杰手枪；袖珍手枪|A small, usually single-shot pistol with a large bore, named after Henry Deringer, a 19th-century Philadelphia gunsmith.  It's typically easily concealable due to its small size.|He kept a pair of derringers tucked into his boots. 他把一对德林杰手枪塞进靴子里。|Synonyms: pocket pistol, palm pistol|Antonyms: rifle, shotgun|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the name of Henry Deringer (1786–1868), an American gunsmith.  While Deringer himself spelled his name with one "r," the genericized term for the type of pistol he created is commonly spelled with two "r"s – "derringer."
