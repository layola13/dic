actualist: /ˈæktʃuəlɪst/
n.|现实主义者；实际主义者|A person who believes in dealing with things as they actually exist, rather than on theories or beliefs.|The actualist focused on practical solutions, not abstract ideals. 现实主义者专注于实际的解决方案，而不是抽象的理想。|
|近义词: pragmatist, realist|反义词: idealist, theorist|词性: 名词

actualist: /ˈæktʃuəlɪst/
adj.|现实主义的；实际主义的|Based on dealing with things as they actually exist, rather than on theories or beliefs.|He took an actualist approach to the problem. 他对这个问题采取了现实主义的方法。|
|近义词: pragmatic, realistic|反义词: idealistic, theoretical|词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin actualis "acting, effective," from actus "act, deed" (from the past participle stem of agere "to do, drive, lead, act").  The "-ist" suffix denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or belief.
