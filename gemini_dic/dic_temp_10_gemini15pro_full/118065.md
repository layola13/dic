mender:/ˈmɛndər/ | n. | 修理工；修补者 | A person who repairs something, especially clothes or shoes. |  The mender skillfully repaired the tear in my coat. 修理工巧妙地修补了我外套上的裂口。 | Synonyms: repairer, fixer, patcher | Antonyms: breaker, damager | noun

mender:/ˈmɛndər/ | v. | 修理；修补 | To repair something, especially something made of cloth. | She mended the hole in her sock. 她补好了袜子上的洞。 | Synonyms: repair, fix, patch | Antonyms: break, damage | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *menden, from Old English *mendan, from Proto-Germanic *manþijaną (“to improve, repair”), from Proto-Indo-European *mendʰ- (“to improve, make good, compensate, atone”). Cognate with Old Norse menda (“to improve, make good”), Old High German bentida (“better”), Old Frisian menda (“to repair”), Dutch menden (“to mend”), German menden (“to mend”).
