neandertals:/niˈændərˌtɔːlz/, /niˈændərˌθɔːlz/ | n. | 尼安德特人 | An extinct species of human that was widely distributed in ice-age Europe between circa 120,000–40,000 years ago, with a receding forehead and prominent brow ridges. |  Neandertals were skilled hunters and toolmakers. 尼安德特人是熟练的猎人和工具制造者。 |  Neandertal DNA can be found in modern humans.  尼安德特人的DNA可以在现代人类身上找到。 | synonyms:  Neanderthal Man,  caveman,  early man | antonyms:  modern human, Homo sapiens | noun


neanderthal:/niˈændərˌtɔːl/, /niˈændərˌθɔːl/ | adj. | 尼安德特人的；原始的 | Relating to or denoting Neanderthal Man.  |  Neanderthal tools have been found in caves throughout Europe.  尼安德特人的工具在整个欧洲的洞穴中都有发现。 |  His Neanderthal views on women are offensive. 他对女性的原始观点令人反感。 | synonyms:  primitive,  ancient,  prehistoric | antonyms:  modern,  contemporary,  advanced | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Neander Thal (Neander Valley), Germany, where the first recognized fossil was found.  The 'thal' was later respelled 'tal'.
