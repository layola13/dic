Launceston: /ˈlɔːns(t)ən/ | n. | 朗塞斯顿 | A city in northern Tasmania, Australia, and a former city in Cornwall, England. |  We visited Launceston during our trip to Tasmania. 我们在塔斯马尼亚之旅期间参观了朗塞斯顿。|  Synonyms: town, city | Antonyms: countryside, rural area | Noun

Etymology:

The Tasmanian Launceston is named after Launceston, Cornwall, the birthplace of Lieutenant-Governor George Arthur.  The Cornish Launceston derives its name from the Cornish language, meaning "church enclosure of St Stephen".
