escapologist:/ɪˈskeɪpɒlədʒɪst/ /ɪˈskeɪpɑːlədʒɪst/ | n. | 脫逃術表演者；逃脫大師 | A performer who specializes in escaping from restraints, such as handcuffs, ropes, or locked containers. |  Harry Houdini was a famous escapologist. 哈利·胡迪尼是一位著名的脫逃術表演者。

escapologist:| n. | 逃脫困境的人 | Someone who is skilled at getting out of difficult situations. | He's a real escapologist when it comes to avoiding deadlines.  他很擅长逃避截止日期，是个真正的逃脱大师。

escapologist:| n. |逃避現實的人| Someone who avoids facing reality or difficult situations. | She's an escapologist, always burying her head in the sand when problems arise. 她是一个逃避现实的人，总是在问题出现时把头埋在沙子里。

ETYMOLOGY->
From escape + -ologist.  "Escape" comes from Old French "eschaper," ultimately from Latin "excappāre" meaning "to slip out of one's cape."  "-ologist" is a suffix denoting a person who studies or practices a particular subject.
