oversparingness:/ˌəʊvərˈspɛərɪŋnəs/| n. |过度节俭，过度吝啬|The state of being excessively sparing or frugal, often to the point of deprivation or hardship.  It implies an unwillingness to spend money even on necessities or reasonable comforts.|His oversparingness led him to wear worn-out clothes and eat meager meals, even though he could afford better.|近义词：parsimony, stinginess, frugality, miserliness|反义词：generosity, extravagance, lavishness|词性:noun

oversparing:/ˌəʊvərˈspɛərɪŋ/| adj. |过度节俭的，过度吝啬的|Excessively sparing or frugal, often to the point of deprivation or hardship.|Her oversparing habits made it difficult for her to enjoy life's simple pleasures.|近义词：parsimonious, stingy, frugal, miserly|反义词：generous, extravagant, lavish|词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "sparing" (adjective meaning "reluctant to spend or use") + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality).  "Sparing" comes from Old English "sparian," meaning "to refrain from harming or destroying."
