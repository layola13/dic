blackfriars: /ˈblækfraɪəz/ | n. |黑衣修士；黑衣修士区|A place name, often referring to a district in London formerly occupied by a Dominican priory. It can also refer to members of the Dominican order. |例句：I visited Blackfriars Bridge in London. 我参观了伦敦的黑衣修士桥。

例句：The Blackfriars Theatre was a famous London playhouse. 黑衣修士剧院是伦敦著名的剧院。|近义词：Dominicans |反义词: N/A |词性: 名词

Etymology: From "black" + "friars," referring to the black cloaks worn by the Dominican order.  "Friar" comes from Old French "frere," meaning "brother," ultimately from Latin "frater."
