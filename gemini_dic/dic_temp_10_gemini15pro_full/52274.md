dichastic:/dɪˈkæstɪk/| adj.|二歧的，双歧的，分叉的|Relating to, characterized by, or exhibiting dichotomy; branching into two parts.  Often used in statistics and probability to describe processes with two possible outcomes at each stage.|The dichastic nature of the experiment allowed for only two possible results: success or failure. 这个实验的二歧性质只允许两种可能的结果：成功或失败。|近义词：bifurcate, forked, branching|反义词：unified, single, combined|词性:形容词

Etymology: From Greek *dikhastós* "divided in two," from *dikházein* "to divide in two, cleave," from *díkha* "in two, asunder."
