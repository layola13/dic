gypsywort:/ˈdʒɪpsiwɜːrt/| n. |吉普赛草，野芝麻|A perennial herb (Lycopus europaeus) of the mint family, with small white flowers and whorled leaves, found in damp habitats. It was traditionally used medicinally by Romani people (Gypsies).|Gypsywort grows along the riverbank. 吉普赛草生长在河岸边。|Gypsywort is a common sight in wetlands. 吉普赛草在湿地很常见。|近义词：bugleweed, water horehound|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


Etymology:
From gypsy + wort.  "Wort" is an Old English word meaning "plant" or "herb," often used in the names of plants with perceived medicinal properties. The "gypsy" part of the name likely refers to the traditional use of the plant by Romani people ("Gypsies").
