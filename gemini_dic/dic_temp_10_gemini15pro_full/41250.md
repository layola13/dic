corbeled:/ˈkɔːr.bəld/ (US), /ˈkɔː.bəld/ (UK)| adj.| 搁置的，用托架支撑的|A corbeled structure is one that is built using corbels, which are architectural supports projecting from a wall. They allow for wider spans or openings without the need for continuous support from below.|The castle's upper floors were supported by a series of elaborately carved corbeled arches. 城堡的上层由一系列精心雕刻的托架拱门支撑。 The ancient aqueduct featured a series of corbeled arches that spanned the valley. 古老的渡槽有一系列跨越山谷的托架拱门。|近义词：bracketed, cantilevered|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "supported from below")|词性: adjective

corbel:/ˈkɔːr.bəl/ (US), /ˈkɔː.bəl/ (UK)| n.| 托架，突出的支撑物|A corbel is a piece of stone, wood, brick, etc., jutting out from a wall to support a weight above, such as a beam, arch, or vault.|The corbels supporting the balcony were intricately decorated with gargoyles. 支撑阳台的托架上装饰着复杂的滴水嘴兽。  The roof was supported by a row of sturdy oak corbels. 屋顶由一排坚固的橡木托架支撑。|近义词：bracket, support|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性: noun

词源: From Old French *corbel (unattested), diminutive of corb "raven," from Latin corvus. The architectural feature is so named because of a resemblance to a raven's beak.
