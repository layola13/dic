acquiesces:/ˌækwiˈɛsɪz/| v.|默许，勉强同意|To accept something reluctantly but without protest.  To give in or comply silently or passively.|She acquiesced to their demands.|近义词:consent, comply, agree, accede|反义词:object, resist, oppose|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *acquiescere*, from *ad-* ("to, towards") + *quiescere* ("to rest, be quiet").

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He acquiesces to her requests. = 他默许了她的请求。
She often acquiesces without voicing her concerns. = 她经常默不作声地表示同意，没有表达她的担忧。

一般过去时:
The manager acquiesced to the employees' demands for a raise. = 经理默许了员工加薪的要求。
Despite his reservations, he acquiesced to the plan. = 尽管有所保留，他还是同意了这个计划。

现在完成时:
The government has acquiesced to the demands of the protesters. = 政府已经默许了抗议者的要求。
She has acquiesced to his wishes throughout their marriage. = 在他们的婚姻生活中，她一直默许他的愿望。

过去完成时:
He had acquiesced to their demands before realizing the full implications. = 他在意识到全部含义之前就已经默许了他们的要求。
They had acquiesced to the changes even though they were unhappy about them. = 即使他们对这些变化不满意，他们还是默许了。


将来时:
If the price is right, I will acquiesce to the deal. = 如果价格合适，我会同意这笔交易。
He will acquiesce to their demands if they threaten him. = 如果他们威胁他，他会默许他们的要求。

虚拟语气:
If he were to acquiesce, the project could move forward. = 如果他默许，项目就可以向前推进。
I wish he would acquiesce to our proposal. = 我希望他会同意我们的提议。


被动语态:
His demands were acquiesced to by the management. = 他的要求得到了管理层的默许。
The changes were acquiesced to without much discussion. = 这些变化没有经过太多讨论就被默许了。


疑问句:
Why did she acquiesce so readily? = 她为什么这么轻易就默许了？
Will he acquiesce to our terms? = 他会同意我们的条件吗？

否定句:
He did not acquiesce to their demands. = 他没有默许他们的要求。
She will not acquiesce to such an unreasonable request. = 她不会默许这样一个不合理的要求。


PHRASE->
acquiesce in (something) = 默许(某事)
acquiesce to (something/someone) = 默许(某事/某人)
acquiesce without protest = 毫无异议地默许
tacitly acquiesce = 默认
reluctantly acquiesce = 不情愿地默许
