administrator: /ədˈmɪnɪstreɪtər/ (US) /ədˈmɪnɪstreɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 管理员，管理者|A person who manages or directs affairs; especially, one who administers an estate.  A person responsible for running a business, organization, or website.|The network administrator has changed the passwords. 网络管理员更改了密码。|Synonyms: manager, director, executive, superintendent, supervisor|Antonyms: subordinate, employee|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *administrator, from Latin administrare "to manage, direct," from ad- "to" + ministrare "to serve, minister."

USAGE->
Direct Object:
The company hired a new administrator. = 公司聘请了一位新的管理员。
She works as an administrator for a non-profit organization. = 她在一个非营利组织担任管理员。

Indirect Object:
The board granted the administrator more power. = 董事会授予了管理员更大的权力。
They gave the administrator a list of tasks. = 他们给了管理员一个任务清单。

Subject:
The administrator oversees the daily operations. = 管理员负责监督日常运营。
An administrator is responsible for managing resources. = 管理员负责管理资源。

Prepositional Phrase:
The decision rests with the administrator. = 这项决定取决于管理员。
Under the new administrator, the company thrived. = 在新任管理员的领导下，公司蓬勃发展。

Possessive:
The administrator's decision was final. = 管理员的决定是最终的。
The administrator's office is on the second floor. = 管理员的办公室在二楼。
