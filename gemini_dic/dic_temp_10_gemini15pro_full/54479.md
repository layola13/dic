disinterestedly:/dɪsˈɪntrɪstɪdli/
adverb
无私地；公正地；不感兴趣地
In a disinterested manner; without bias or self-interest; impartially.
He disinterestedly judged the art competition, even though his daughter was a participant.  他公正地评判了艺术比赛，即使他的女儿是参赛者之一。
She listened disinterestedly to the lecture, her mind already on the weekend. 她毫无兴趣地听着讲座，心里想着周末的安排。
近义词:impartially, neutrally, objectively, unbiasedly
反义词: selfishly, biasedly, partially
词性:adverb

disinterest:/dɪsˈɪntrɪst/
noun
无私；公正；不感兴趣
Lack of interest; indifference.  The state of not being influenced by personal involvement in something; impartiality.
He showed a complete disinterest in the political debate. 他对政治辩论完全不感兴趣。
The judge's disinterest in the case was questioned due to his previous relationship with the defendant.  法官在此案中的公正性受到了质疑，因为他此前与被告有关系。
近义词:indifference, apathy, impartiality, neutrality
反义词:interest, bias, partiality
词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- + interested.  Originally meant "unbiased," but now often confused with "uninterested."
