imagination:/ɪˌmædʒɪˈneɪʃn/ | n. | 想象力，想象；空想，幻想 | The faculty or action of forming new ideas, or images or concepts of external objects not present to the senses. |  She has a vivid imagination. 她有着丰富的想象力。

|  He used his imagination to write the story. 他运用他的想象力来创作故事。


| Synonyms:  fantasy, creativity, ingenuity, vision, inventiveness | Antonyms:  reality, actuality | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English:  imaginacioun, from Old French imaginacion, from Latin imaginatio(n-), from imaginari ‘picture to oneself’.
