conceitedness:/kənˈsiːtɪdnəs/| n.|自负，骄傲自大|The state of being excessively proud and self-satisfied; vanity.|His conceitedness alienated his friends. 他的自负疏远了他的朋友们。|近义词：vanity, arrogance, pride, egotism, self-importance|反义词：humility, modesty|词性:名词
Etymology:  From conceited + -ness. Conceited from conceit (n.) "favorable opinion of oneself," from conceive (v.) + -ed. Originally meaning "something conceived in the mind, thought, idea." The sense shifted to "high opinion of oneself" in the 16th century.
