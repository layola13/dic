pantographies:/ˌpæntəˈɡræfiz/
n.
缩放仪，缩放刻写器，缩放刻写法
A pantograph is a device that can be used to copy a drawing or plan at a different scale.  It consists of a framework of hinged and sliding rods, which can be moved to trace an image.  The movement is then reproduced by a pen or other marker at a fixed point on the framework, creating a copy of the original image, enlarged or reduced.
The architect used pantographies to scale his blueprints. 建筑师使用缩放仪来缩放他的蓝图。
Synonyms: pantograph
Antonyms: None
noun


pantography:/pænˈtɒɡrəfi/
n.
缩放刻写术，缩放刻写法
Pantography is the art or process of copying a drawing or plan at a different scale using a pantograph.
He studied pantography in order to reproduce old maps. 他学习了缩放刻写术以便复制旧地图。
Synonyms: pantograph, scaling
Antonyms: None
noun
