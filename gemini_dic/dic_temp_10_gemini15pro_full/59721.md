eclipse: /ɪˈklɪps/ (US) /ɪˈklɪps/ (UK)| v. n.|使黯淡；使失色；遮蔽；月食；日食|to deprive (someone or something) of significance, power, or prominence;  to obscure or block out (light)|The moon eclipsed the sun. 月亮遮住了太阳。|近义词：overshadow, obscure, surpass, outshine|反义词:highlight, brighten|词性:动词/名词

eclipse: /ɪˈklɪps/ (US) /ɪˈklɪps/ (UK)| adj.|黯然的；失色的；被遮蔽的|being overshadowed or obscured|His reputation was eclipsed by his brother's fame. 他的名声被他兄弟的名声所掩盖。|近义词：overshadowed, obscured|反义词:prominent, highlighted|词性:形容词

词源：Late Middle English: from Old French eclipse or Latin eclipsis, from Greek ekleipsis, from ekleipein ‘fail to appear, be eclipsed’, from ek ‘out’ + leipein ‘leave’.
