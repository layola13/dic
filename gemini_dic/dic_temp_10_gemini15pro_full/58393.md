dubbing:/ˈdʌbɪŋ/ | n. | 配音；复制；拷备 | the process of replacing the original dialogue of a film or television programme with a translation spoken in another language | Example: The dubbing of foreign films is a common practice. 外国电影的配音是一种常见的做法。

Example:  He works in dubbing, adding sound effects to movies. 他从事配音工作，为电影添加音效。 | 近义词：voice-over, looping, synchronization | 反义词：original sound, subtitling | 词性:noun


dub:/dʌb/ | vt. | 配音；授予称号；轻拍；打击 | to give someone or something a particular name or title, often humorously; to add (sound effects or music) to a film or broadcast; to strike lightly | Example: The media dubbed him "The King of Pop." 媒体称他为“流行音乐之王”。

Example: They dubbed the movie into French. 他们将这部电影配音成法语。 | 近义词：name, call, entitle, label, tap | 反义词：strip, remove | 词性:verb


词源: From Old French *dobber, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch dobben and German tupfen "to dip, tap." Originally referring to a light blow or tap, the meaning extended to the process of adding sound to film, as if "tapping" in the new audio.
