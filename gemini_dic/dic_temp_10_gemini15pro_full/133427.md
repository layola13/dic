ETYMOLOGY->
non- (prefix):  Meaning "not," from Old French non- and directly from Latin non "not," cognate with Greek ne- "not," Old English ne "not, no."
farcical (adjective): From farce (n.), from Old French farce "stuffing, forcemeat (in cookery), foolish jest (in theatre)," from farcir "to stuff," from Latin farcire "to cram, stuff."  Originally referring to comic interludes inserted into religious plays.
-ity (suffix):  A suffix forming abstract nouns of quality or condition, from French -ité and directly from Latin -itas, -itatis.
