disputations:/ˌdɪspjuˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 辩论，争论 |  Formal discussions about a subject where different opinions are expressed; debates.  | The philosophical disputations went on for hours. 哲学辩论持续了几个小时。| The medieval university was a center of theological disputations. 中世纪的大学是神学争论的中心。 | synonyms: debates, arguments, controversies, discussions | antonyms: agreements, accords, harmonies | noun

disputation:/ˌdɪspjuˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 辩论，争论 |  A formal debate or argument. | He engaged in a heated disputation with his professor. 他和他的教授进行了激烈的辩论。| The disputation over the new policy lasted for weeks. 关于新政策的辩论持续了数周。 | synonyms: debate, argument, controversy, discussion | antonyms: agreement, accord, harmony | noun


Etymology:
From Late Middle English *disputacioun*, from Old French *disputation*, from Latin *disputātiō*, from *disputāre* "to discuss, examine, argue," from *dis-* "apart, asunder" + *putāre* "to think, consider, reckon, suppose."
