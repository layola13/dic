contradictorily:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪktərɪli/ /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪktərɪli/| adv.|矛盾地；自相矛盾地；反驳地|In a way that contradicts something else; inconsistently.|He contradictorily claimed to love her yet treated her with disdain. 他自相矛盾地声称爱她，却又轻蔑地对待她。|Synonyms: inconsistently, paradoxically, incompatibly, incongruously, conflictingly|Antonyms: consistently, harmoniously, compatibly, congruously|Adverb

contradict:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkt/ /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkt/| vt.|反驳；否认；与…矛盾；与…抵触|deny the truth of (a statement) by asserting the opposite.|The facts contradict his testimony. 事实与他的证词相矛盾。|Synonyms: deny, refute, dispute, challenge, gainsay|Antonyms: confirm, support, corroborate, verify|Verb

contradiction:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkʃn/ /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkʃən/| n.|矛盾；否认；反驳|a combination of statements, ideas, or features of a situation that are opposed to one another.|His behavior is a contradiction of his words. 他的行为与他的言辞相矛盾。|Synonyms: inconsistency, paradox, conflict, discrepancy, incongruity|Antonyms: consistency, harmony, agreement, conformity|Noun
