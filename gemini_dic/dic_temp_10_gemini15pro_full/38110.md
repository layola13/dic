compounds:/ˈkɒmpaʊndz/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmpaʊndz/ (US)| n. |化合物，混合物；复合词；用围墙围起来的场地，院子|A thing that is composed of two or more separate elements; a mixture.|The chemist analyzed the compounds in the sample. 化学家分析了样品中的化合物。|近义词：mixture, blend, composite, complex|反义词：element, component|词性: noun

compounds:/kəmˈpaʊnd/ (UK) /kəmˈpaʊnd/ (US)| vt. |使加重，使恶化；混合，合成；和解，妥协|Make (something bad) worse; combine or mix (ingredients or constituents); settle (a debt or other matter) by mutual agreement or compromise.|His financial problems were compounded by the loss of his job. 他的经济问题因失去工作而雪上加霜。|近义词：aggravate, worsen, exacerbate, combine, mix|反义词：alleviate, improve, separate|词性: verb

compounds:/ˈkɒmpaʊnd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmpaʊnd/ (US)| adj. |复合的，合成的|Made up or consisting of several parts or elements.|He suffered a compound fracture of his leg. 他的腿 sufrió una fractura compuesta. |近义词：composite, complex|反义词：simple, single|词性: adjective
