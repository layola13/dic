freight:/freɪt/| n. vt.|货物，运费；运送|Goods carried by a large vehicle, ship, train, or aircraft; the charge for carrying these goods.|The ship carries both passengers and freight. 这艘船既载客也载货。|近义词：cargo, shipment, load|反义词: N/A|词性:名词, 动词


freight:/freɪt/| vt.|装货于，运送|Load (a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle) with goods for transportation.|The train was freighted with coal. 火车装载着煤炭。|近义词：ship, transport, haul|反义词:unload, discharge|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *fraite, *fret, from Old French fret (“freight, ship's cargo, ship's hire, earnings”), from Frankish *fraht (“earnings, reward”), from Proto-Germanic *frahtą (“profit, gain, reward”). Cognate with Old High German frāta (“profit, advantage”), Old English freōtu (“comfort, help, refuge”).  The verb is derived from the noun.
