paralogize:/ˈpærəlɒdʒaɪz/ | v. | 推理论述；诡辩；用似是而非的论据来辩论 | To reason falsely or to draw conclusions from faulty premises. | He paralogized his way through the debate, confusing the audience with his specious arguments. 他在辩论中诡辩，用似是而非的论点迷惑了观众。| synonyms: equivocate, prevaricate, quibble | antonyms: reason logically, argue cogently | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *paralogizari, from Greek paralogizesthai "to reason falsely," from para- "beside, amiss" + logizesthai "to reason," from logos "word, reason."
