disinclination:/ˌdɪsɪnklɪˈneɪʃn/
|n.|
|不愿，不情愿|
|A lack of willingness or desire to do something; reluctance.|
|He showed a strong disinclination to get involved in the argument. 他明显不愿卷入这场争论。|

|近义词：reluctance, unwillingness, aversion, dislike|
|反义词：inclination, willingness, eagerness, desire|
|词性：名词|



disinclinations:/ˌdɪsɪnklɪˈneɪʃənz/
|n.|
|不愿，不情愿（复数）|
|Multiple instances of a lack of willingness or desire to do something.|
|She has strong disinclinations towards housework and cooking. 她对家务和烹饪非常不情愿。|

|近义词：reluctances, unwillingnesses, aversions, dislikes|
|反义词：inclinations, willingnesses, eagernesses, desires|
|词性：名词|
