nonsubstantiation:/ˌnɒnsəbˌstænʃiˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 未能证实;  缺乏实质证据 | The report criticized the government's nonsubstantiation of its claims. |  The lack of evidence led to the nonsubstantiation of the rumor. | 近义词: lack of proof, unsubstantiated, unproven | 反义词: proof, substantiation, verification | 词性: 名词

Etymology: non- (not) + substantiation (the act of providing evidence to support a claim) from Late Latin substantiāre “give substance to,” from Latin substantia “substance.”
