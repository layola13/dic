discomfit:/dɪsˈkʌmfɪt/
verb
使窘迫；使不安；挫败
To make someone feel uneasy, embarrassed, or disconcerted.  To thwart or defeat plans.
The unexpected question discomfited her.  这个突如其来的问题让她感到窘迫。
The army's advance was discomfited by the enemy's surprise attack.  军队的推进被敌人的突然袭击挫败了。
近义词: disconcert, embarrass, fluster, ruffle, perturb, thwart, frustrate
反义词: comfort, reassure, encourage, assist
词性: verb


discomfiture:/dɪsˈkʌmfɪtʃər/
noun
窘迫；不安；挫败
A feeling of unease, embarrassment, or discomposure.  The state of being disconcerted or thwarted.
She couldn't hide her discomfiture at his unexpected arrival. 她无法掩饰他对意外到来的不安。
The team's discomfiture was evident after their crushing defeat.  球队惨败后，他们的沮丧显而易见。
近义词: embarrassment, unease, discomposure, frustration
反义词: composure, comfort, ease, satisfaction
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French desconfit (past participle of desconfire), from des- "not, opposite of" + conficere "to do, perform, complete, finish," from com- "together" + facere "to make, do."
