overspeculation: /ˌoʊvərˌspɛkjʊˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 过度投机，过度推测 | Excessive speculation, often based on insufficient or unreliable information, leading to inflated expectations or risky investments. | The overspeculation in the housing market led to a crash. 房地产市场的过度投机导致了崩盘。| The dot-com bubble was fueled by overspeculation. 网络泡沫是由过度投机推动的。 | 近义词：overtrading, excessive speculation | 反义词: underspeculation, cautious investment | 词性: noun


Etymology:
From over- + speculation, from Late Latin speculationem (nominative speculatio) "act of looking at, contemplation, espionage," noun of action from past-participle stem of speculari "to look at, watch, observe," from specula "watchtower, lookout post," from specere "to look at."
