entwining:/ɪnˈtwaɪnɪŋ/
verb|缠绕，交织|The vines were entwining around the branches. 藤蔓缠绕在树枝上。|例句：The ivy was entwining itself around the oak tree.  常春藤缠绕在橡树上。|近义词：intertwining, twisting, weaving, braiding|反义词：untangling, separating|v.

entwining:/ɪnˈtwaɪnɪŋ/
adjective|缠绕的，交织的|The entwining branches created a shady canopy. 交织的树枝形成了一个阴凉的树冠。|例句：The entwining serpents symbolized duality.  交缠的蛇象征着二元性。|近义词：intertwined, twisted, woven, braided|反义词：untangled, separated|adj.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *entwynynge, from Old English *intwīnan, from in- + twīnan (“to twine”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *twīnaną (“to twine, twist”). Cognate with Dutch twijnen (“to twine”), German zwijnen (“to twine”).
