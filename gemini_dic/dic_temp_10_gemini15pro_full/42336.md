counteract:/ˌkaʊntərˈækt/ | v. | 抵消，对抗，中和 | to act against something in order to reduce its force or neutralize it |  Regular exercise can counteract the effects of a sedentary job. 经常锻炼可以抵消久坐工作带来的负面影响。

The government implemented policies to counteract inflation. 政府实施了对抗通货膨胀的政策。 | 近义词: neutralize, offset, counterbalance, oppose | 反义词: enhance, augment, reinforce, strengthen | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- (against) + act (to do).  The prefix "counter-" comes from the Old French "contre-" and ultimately from the Latin "contra" meaning "against."  The word "act" comes from the Latin "actus" meaning "a doing, a driving, an impulse."
