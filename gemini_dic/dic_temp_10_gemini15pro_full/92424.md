hypomanic:/ˌhaɪpəˈmænɪk/| adj.|轻躁狂的|A mild form of mania, characterized by elevated mood, increased energy, and racing thoughts, but without the severe symptoms of full-blown mania, such as psychosis or a complete break from reality.  It is a mood state that is less severe than full mania. |

例句：
He experienced a hypomanic episode last year, marked by increased energy and impulsive spending.
他去年经历了一次轻躁狂发作，表现为精力充沛和冲动消费。


例句：
Her hypomanic periods are characterized by intense creativity and productivity, followed by periods of depression.
她的轻躁狂时期以强烈的创造力和生产力为特征，随后是抑郁时期。


近义词：elated, exuberant, energetic|
反义词：depressed, lethargic|
词性：adjective|

ETYMOLOGY->
From hypo- (Greek: 'under, beneath, slightly') + manic (from mania).  Meaning "slightly manic."
