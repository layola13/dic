exaltedness:/ɪɡˈzɔːltɪdnəs/| n. |崇高，高尚，显赫|The state of being exalted; elevated in rank, character, or dignity. |Her exaltedness was evident in her regal bearing. 她的高贵气质在她庄重的举止中显露无遗。|Synonyms: nobility, grandeur, majesty, loftiness, sublimity |Antonyms: baseness, meanness, lowliness, insignificance|noun


exalted:/ɪɡˈzɔːltɪd/| adj. |崇高的，高贵的，兴奋的|Raised to a high rank, position, or dignity; of high moral or intellectual value; elevated in style or tone. |He held an exalted position in the company. 他在公司担任要职。  She was in an exalted mood after winning the lottery.  中了彩票后，她欣喜若狂。|Synonyms: noble, lofty, elevated, dignified, majestic |Antonyms: lowly, base, humble, insignificant|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *exaltednes*, from *exalted* + *-nes*.  *Exalted* comes from Middle English *exalten*, from Old French *exalter*, from Latin *exaltare*, from *ex-* ("out, upward") + *altus* ("high").
