monostrophic:/ˌmɒnəˈstrɒfɪk/ | adj. | 单系的，单基因的 | Relating to or characterized by monostrophy, a condition in which an organism relies on a single food source or host. |  The parasitic wasp is monostrophic, relying solely on the eggs of a specific species of spider for its larval development.  这种寄生蜂是单系的，它的幼虫发育完全依赖于一种特定蜘蛛的卵。| Example 2:  Monostrophic pollinators are highly specialized and vulnerable to changes in the population of their host plant. 单系传粉者高度专业化，容易受到其寄主植物种群变化的影响。 |  Synonyms: specialized, specific, host-specific | Antonyms: generalist, polyphagous | adjective
