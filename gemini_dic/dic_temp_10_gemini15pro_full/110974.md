liquidated:/ˈlɪkwɪˌdeɪtɪd/
adjective
清算的；已偿付的；已清盘的
Converted into cash; paid off.
例句：The company's assets were liquidated to pay off its debts. 该公司的资产被清算以偿还债务。

例句：His liquidated assets amounted to only a few thousand dollars. 他变现的资产只有几千美元。

近义词：settled, paid off, cleared, dissolved
反义词：outstanding, unpaid, due, owing
词性：adjective

liquidate:/ˈlɪkwɪdeɪt/
verb
清算；清偿；偿付；肃清；清除；杀掉；消灭
To eliminate or settle by paying off debts, selling assets, or killing someone.
例句：The company was liquidated after it went bankrupt.  这家公司在破产后被清算了。

例句：The government ordered to liquidate the terrorist group. 政府下令消灭恐怖组织。


近义词：eliminate, dissolve, abolish, eradicate, kill, destroy
反义词：establish, found, create, build
词性：verb

liquidation:/ˌlɪkwɪˈdeɪʃn/
noun
清算；清偿；偿付；清除；消灭
The process of converting assets into cash or of settling a debt.
例句：The company went into liquidation after failing to secure new funding.  这家公司在未能获得新的资金后进入清算程序。

例句：The liquidation of the enemy troops was swift and decisive. 对敌军的清除迅速而果断。


近义词：dissolution, elimination, eradication
反义词：establishment, foundation, creation
词性：noun
