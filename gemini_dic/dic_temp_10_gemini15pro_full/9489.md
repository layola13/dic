antiromanticisms: /ˌæntiroʊˈmæntɪsɪzəmz/ | n. | 反浪漫主义 |  The principles or practices of antiromanticism, characterized by a rejection of idealized or sentimentalized notions of love, nature, or art. It emphasizes realism, skepticism, and a focus on the practical or mundane aspects of life. |  His antiromanticisms were evident in his stark, unadorned prose. 他的反浪漫主义体现在他朴实无华的散文中。|  cynicism, realism, pragmatism | romanticism, idealism, sentimentalism | noun

Etymology:
From anti- + romanticism + -s (plural suffix). "Anti-" is a prefix of Greek origin meaning "against," "opposite of," or "opposed to." "Romanticism" comes from the French word "romantisme," referring to a movement in the arts and literature that emphasized emotion, imagination, and individualism.
