anarch:/ˈænɑːrk/| n.|无政府主义者|a person who believes in or tries to bring about anarchy.|例句：The anarch threw a Molotov cocktail at the police. 这个无政府主义者向警察投掷了燃烧瓶。|例句：She was labeled an anarch for her radical views.  由于她激进的观点，她被贴上了无政府主义者的标签。|近义词：rebel, insurgent, revolutionary|反义词：conformist, traditionalist|词性:noun
anarchy:/ˈænərki/| n.|无政府状态|a state of disorder due to absence or nonrecognition of authority.|例句：The country descended into anarchy after the coup. 政变后，这个国家陷入了无政府状态。|例句：The classroom was in a state of anarchy when the teacher left. 老师离开后，教室里一片混乱。|近义词：chaos, disorder, lawlessness|反义词：order, control, government|词性:noun
anarchic:/əˈnɑːrkɪk/| adj.|无政府的|characterized by a lack of order and control.|例句：The anarchic protests led to widespread violence. 无政府主义的抗议活动导致了大范围的暴力事件。|例句：The children's play was anarchic and full of energy. 孩子们的游戏无拘无束，充满了活力。|近义词：chaotic, lawless, unruly|反义词：orderly, controlled, organized|词性:adjective
anarchism:/ˈænərkɪzəm/| n.|无政府主义|belief in the abolition of all government and the organization of society on a voluntary, cooperative basis without recourse to force or compulsion.|例句：Anarchism is a complex political philosophy. 无政府主义是一种复杂的政治哲学。|例句：He studied the history of anarchism in 19th-century Europe. 他研究了19世纪欧洲无政府主义的历史。|近义词：nihilism (sometimes), libertarianism (sometimes)|反义词：authoritarianism, totalitarianism|词性:noun
