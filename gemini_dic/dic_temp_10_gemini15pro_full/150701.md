parabled:/ˈpærəbld/ | adj. | 以寓言形式表达的；寓言化的 | Expressed or told in the form of a parable; allegorical. | The story is parabled to illustrate the importance of forgiveness.  这个故事以寓言的形式来说明宽恕的重要性。| Example:  His life was a parabled journey, full of symbolic events. 他的人生是一段寓言般的旅程，充满了象征性的事件。| Synonym: allegorical, symbolic, metaphorical | Antonym: literal, factual | word property: adjective

parabled:/ˈpærəbld/ | v. (past participle) |  (已)用寓言表达 | Expressed or told using a parable. | The moral lessons were parabled through a series of interconnected short stories.  这些道德教训通过一系列相互关联的短篇故事以寓言的形式表达出来。 | Example: The complex themes of love and loss are parabled in the film's narrative.  电影的叙事以寓言的形式表达了爱与失去的复杂主题。 | Synonym: allegorized, symbolized | Antonym: stated directly, explicitly explained | word property: verb (past participle)

Etymology: From "parable" + "-ed." "Parable" comes from Old French "parable" and ultimately from Greek "parabole," meaning "comparison, illustration."
