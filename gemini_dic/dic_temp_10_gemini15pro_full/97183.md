infixations:/ɪnˌfɪkˈseɪʃənz/| n. |插入词缀；中缀|The process of inserting a morpheme (an affix) inside a word stem.  This is less common than prefixation or suffixation.|Example:  "Absogoddamlutely!" uses the infix "goddam" within "absolutely".  This is often done for emphasis or humorous effect.|近义词:interfix, tmesis|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From in- + fix + -ation.  "In-" indicates "within" or "inside." "Fix" comes from Latin "fixus," meaning "fastened" or "attached." "-ation" is a suffix forming nouns of action or process.
