derailing:/dɪˈreɪlɪŋ/
n.|脱轨，出轨|The derailing of the train caused several injuries. 火车的脱轨造成数人受伤。|
v.|使脱轨，使出轨|The protesters tried to derail the government's plans. 抗议者试图阻挠政府的计划。|
adj.|脱轨的，出轨的，偏离主题的|The conversation went off on a derailing tangent.  谈话偏离了主题。|
近义词:diverting, obstructing, hindering|
反义词:supporting, furthering, focusing|
词性:名词, 动词, 形容词|
英文解析:The act of causing a train or other vehicle to leave its tracks. Can also be used figuratively to describe something going off course or being disrupted.|
例句:The investigation into the derailing is ongoing. 对脱轨事件的调查正在进行中。|
词源:From the verb "derail," which comes from the French "dérailler," meaning "to go off the rails."|
