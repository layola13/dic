neckweed:/ˈnɛkˌwid/| n. |颈草; 毒芹; |A type of poisonous plant, specifically referring to several species of the genus *Veronica*, particularly *Veronica peregrina*. It can also sometimes refer to other plants with similar growth habits or uses in folk medicine, though these usages are less precise.  These plants are generally considered weeds.|The farmer tried to eradicate the neckweed from his fields.  农民试图从他的田地里清除颈草。|Synonyms: purslane speedwell, American speedwell|Antonyms: N/A|noun

Etymology: "Neck" likely refers to the plant's slender stem, and "weed" indicates its undesirable nature as an invasive plant. The genus name *Veronica* honors Saint Veronica.
