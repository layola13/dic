frankpledge:/ˈfræŋkˌpledʒ/| n.|十户联保，互保制度|A system in medieval England in which a group of ten families, called a tithing, pledged to be responsible for the good behavior of each member and to ensure that any member accused of a crime would appear in court.|The frankpledge system was a cornerstone of social order in Anglo-Saxon England.|Synonym: mutual guarantee, collective responsibility|Antonym: individual responsibility|noun
