carefuller:/kɛərˈfʊlər/ |adj.| 更仔细的 (non-standard) | This word is a non-standard form of "more careful". While grammatically incorrect, it sometimes appears in informal speech or writing, especially among children. It attempts to create a comparative form of "careful" by adding "-er", similar to regular adjectives.  However, "careful" is a two-syllable adjective ending in -ful, and typically forms the comparative using "more" before the adjective. | He tried to be carefuller this time. 他这次试图更小心. (non-standard) | Near Synonyms: more careful, more cautious, more meticulous | Antonyms: less careful, less cautious, more reckless |adjective

more careful:/mɔːr ˈkɛərfəl/| adj.|更仔细的，更小心的| The comparative form of "careful", used to indicate a greater degree of care or caution. |She is more careful with her work than he is. 她比他更细心工作。|Near Synonyms: more cautious, more meticulous, more attentive| Antonyms: less careful, less cautious, more reckless|adjective
