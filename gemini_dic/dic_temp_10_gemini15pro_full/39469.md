connive:/kəˈnaɪv/| vi.|密谋，纵容，默许|secretly allow (something considered immoral, illegal, wrong, or harmful) to occur.|例句：The government connived with the company to pollute the river. 政府与该公司串通一气污染了河流。|例句：She connived at her husband's infidelities. 她默许了她丈夫的不忠。|近义词：conspire, collude, plot, scheme|反义词：expose, oppose, prevent|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *connivēre*, present active infinitive of *conniveō* (“I close the eyes, wink, overlook, pretend not to see”), from *com-* (“with, together”) + *niveō* (“I wink, blink”).
