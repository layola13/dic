dauphinate:/ˈdɔːfɪneɪt/ | v. | 立储；立为皇太子 | To invest with the title or dignity of Dauphin (the eldest son of the King of France). |  The king dauphinated his eldest son at a grand ceremony. 国王在一个盛大的仪式上立长子为皇太子。|  invest, designate, appoint | disinherit, dethrone | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *dauphiner*, from *Dauphin*, the title of the heir apparent to the French throne, originally the feudal title of the counts of Viennois, from Old French *dalfin*, from Late Latin *dalphinus*, from Latin *delphinus* "dolphin".  The counts of Viennois bore a dolphin on their coat of arms.
