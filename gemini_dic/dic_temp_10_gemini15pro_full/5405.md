almanzors:/ˌælˈmænzɔːrz/ | n. | 阿尔曼佐尔 |  A historical term referring to rulers, specifically caliphs of Cordoba, Spain, during the 10th and 11th centuries who took the title "al-Mansur" (meaning "victorious" in Arabic). The term is often used in a more general sense to refer to powerful or influential Muslim rulers of that era. |  The Almanzors of Cordoba presided over a golden age of learning and culture. 科尔多瓦的阿尔曼佐尔王朝引领了一个学习和文化的黄金时代。 |  caliphs, rulers, emirs |  - | noun
