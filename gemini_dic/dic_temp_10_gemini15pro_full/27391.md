cameloid:/ˈkæməlɔɪd/| n. adj.|骆驼科动物；骆驼科的|A cameloid is a member of the Camelidae family, which includes camels, llamas, alpacas, vicuñas, and guanacos.  These animals are known for their humps (or lack thereof in some species), long necks, and ability to thrive in harsh environments.|The Bactrian camel is a well-known cameloid.  双峰驼是一种著名的骆驼科动物。

The llama is a cameloid native to South America.  美洲驼是一种原产于南美洲的骆驼科动物。|近义词：camel, llama, alpaca (depending on context)|反义词: N/A|词性: noun, adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From New Latin *Cameloidea, from Latin camelus (“camel”) + -oidea (a taxonomic suffix). Ultimately from Greek κάμηλος (kámēlos, “camel”).
