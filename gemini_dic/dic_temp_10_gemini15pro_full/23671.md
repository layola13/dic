brank:/bræŋk/
n.
辔头，嚼子；(旧时)一种惩罚性器具，用于约束或惩罚饶舌妇人
A scold's bridle; an instrument formerly used for punishing scolds.
The brank was placed on the woman's head as a punishment for gossiping.  这个饶舌妇人被戴上辔头以示惩罚。
Near Synonyms: scold's bridle, bridle, gag
Antonyms: freedom of speech, liberty
Noun


brank:/bræŋk/
v.
用嚼子勒住；用辔头控制；压制(言论)
To bridle; to restrain with a brank.
The king branked the outspoken critic. 国王压制了这位直言不讳的批评家。
Near Synonyms: bridle, restrain, curb, silence, suppress
Antonyms: unleash, liberate, free
Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *branc, from Proto-Germanic *brangkaz (“necklace, collar”), related to Old High German pranga (“fetter, chain”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰrengʰ- (“to bind”).
