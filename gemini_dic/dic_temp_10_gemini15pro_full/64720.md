escheat:/ɪsˈtʃiːt/
n.| 财产归公；无人继承的财产 |The property escheated to the state because there were no heirs.| 因为没有继承人，财产归国家所有。
escheat:/ɪsˈtʃiːt/
v.| (财产)归公；被没收 |His land escheated to the crown when he died without an heir.| 他死后无继承人，土地归王室所有。


escheat:/ɪsˈtʃiːt/
Etymology: From Old French *escheoir "to fall to, devolve," from Latin *excadere "to fall out, fall away, lapse, cease," from ex- "out" + cadere "to fall."

escheat:/ɪsˈtʃiːt/
Synonyms: forfeit, confiscate, revert
escheat:/ɪsˈtʃiːt/
Antonyms: inherit, acquire, retain
escheat:/ɪsˈtʃiːt/
Word Type: noun, verb
