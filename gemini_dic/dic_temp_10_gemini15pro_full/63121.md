enslaved:/ɪnˈsleɪvd/| adj.|被奴役的，受控制的|Made a slave or forced to work as a slave; controlled or dominated by something.|例句：Many Africans were enslaved and brought to America. 许多非洲人被奴役并带到美洲。

例句：He felt enslaved by his job. 他感觉被工作束缚了。|近义词：captive, subjugated, oppressed, bound, in bondage,  |反义词:free, liberated, independent|词性:形容词

enslaved:/ɪnˈsleɪvd/| v.|奴役，使沦为奴隶|To make someone a slave.|例句：The conquerors enslaved the native population. 征服者奴役了当地居民。

例句：Her beauty enslaved him. 她的美貌使他倾倒。|近义词：subjugate, oppress, dominate,  |反义词:liberate, free, emancipate|词性:动词

词源: From en- + slave + -ed.  "En-" is a prefix meaning "to put into" or "to cause to be."  "Slave" comes from the Medieval Latin word "sclavus," referring to the Slavic peoples who were often enslaved.
