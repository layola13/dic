encapsulation:/ɪnˌkæpsjʊˈleɪʃən/ | n. |封装，囊括|The act of enclosing something in a capsule or the state of being enclosed.  The bundling of data with the methods that operate on that data, or the restricting of direct access to some of an object's components.|Example 1: The encapsulation of the drug ensures its slow release into the bloodstream.  药物的封装确保其缓慢释放到血液中。

Example 2:  Object-oriented programming uses encapsulation to protect data integrity. 面向对象编程使用封装来保护数据完整性。|Synonyms: enclosure, containment, packaging|Antonyms: exposure, release, unpacking|noun
