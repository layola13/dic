bribed:/braɪbd/
verb
贿赂了的，被收买的
past participle of bribe: having given or offered a bribe to.
He was bribed to keep silent. 他被贿赂保持沉默。
近义词: corrupted, bought, fixed
反义词: honest, incorruptible
词性: past participle


bribe:/braɪb/
noun
贿赂
money or a favor given or promised in order to influence a person's judgment or conduct.
The official was accused of accepting a bribe.  这名官员被指控收受贿赂。
近义词: payoff, kickback, inducement
反义词: reward, gift
词性: noun

bribe:/braɪb/
verb
贿赂
persuade (someone) to act in one's favor, typically illegally or dishonestly, by a gift of money or other inducement.
They bribed the guards to let them escape. 他们贿赂警卫让他们逃跑。
近义词: buy off, corrupt, grease someone's palm
反义词: dissuade, deter
词性: verb


词源:
From Middle English *briben, from Old French briber (“to beg, to steal”), of uncertain origin. Possibly from a Germanic source akin to Old English brēf (“brief, letter, document”), from Latin brevis (“short”).
