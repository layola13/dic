candidating:/ˈkændɪdeɪtɪŋ/
n.|参选，候选|The act of being a candidate, especially in an election.  The process of applying for or seeking a position. |He's tired of all the candidating and just wants a job.| 近义词: applying, running (for office), campaigning| 反义词: withdrawing, resigning|词性: noun (gerund)


candidating:/ˈkændɪdeɪtɪŋ/
v.|参选，申请|Acting as a candidate, putting oneself forward for a position. |She's been candidating for months, attending numerous interviews.|近义词: applying, running (for office), vying| 反义词: withdrawing, resigning|词性: verb (present participle)

词源：From the Latin "candidatus," meaning "clothed in white," referring to the white togas worn by Roman office seekers.  The word evolved to "candidate," and "candidating" is the gerund or present participle form of the verb "to candidate," although less common than the noun form "candidacy."
