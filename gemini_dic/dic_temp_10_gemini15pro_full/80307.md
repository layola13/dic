gold-plated:/ˈɡoʊld ˈpleɪtɪd/ (US) /ˈɡəʊld ˈpleɪtɪd/ (UK) | adj. |镀金的；虚有其表的；华而不实的|Covered with a thin layer of gold; superficially attractive or impressive but lacking real value.| The company offered a gold-plated pension plan to its executives.  这家公司向其高管提供了一个优厚的养老金计划。|Synonyms: gilded, ornate, luxurious, lavish|Antonyms: plain, simple, unadorned, austere|adjective

gold-plated:/ˈɡoʊld ˈpleɪtɪd/ (US) /ˈɡəʊld ˈpleɪtɪd/ (UK) | n. |镀金制品|An object that is plated with gold.|  She inherited a collection of gold-plated silverware. 她继承了一套镀金银器。|Synonyms: gilt, gold-leaf|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "gold" + "plated," the past participle of the verb "plate," meaning to cover with a thin layer of metal.  "Plate" comes from Old French "plat" meaning "flat," ultimately from Greek "platys" meaning "broad, flat."
