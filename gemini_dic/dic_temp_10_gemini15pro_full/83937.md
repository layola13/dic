half-climbing:/ˌhæf ˈklaɪmɪŋ/ | adj. | 半攀缘的；攀缘性不强的 | Partially climbing; exhibiting climbing habits but not fully adapted for climbing. | The half-climbing rose bush sprawled across the fence. 半攀缘的玫瑰丛蔓延在篱笆上。| The half-climbing vine needed support to reach the roof. 半攀缘的藤蔓需要支撑才能到达屋顶。 | semi-climbing, scrambling | non-climbing | adjective

Etymology:
"half-" (Old English *healfe, from Proto-Germanic *halbaz) + "climbing" (present participle of "climb," from Old English climban)
