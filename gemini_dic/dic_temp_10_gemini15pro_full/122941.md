momentous:/moʊˈmentəs/ /məˈmentəs/| adj.|重大的，重要的|Of great importance or significance, especially in its bearing on the future.|例句：The signing of the peace treaty was a momentous occasion. 签署和平条约是一个重大事件。

例句：Her graduation was a momentous day for her and her family. 她的毕业对她和她的家人来说都是一个重要的日子。

|近义词：significant, important, historic, crucial, pivotal|反义词：trivial, insignificant, unimportant|词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *momentaneus "of the moment," from Latin momentaneus "lasting but a moment," from momentum "movement, moving power" (see momentum). The sense of "important, weighty" developed in English c. 1775.
