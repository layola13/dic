novatianism: /ˌnoʊveɪˈʃiənɪzəm/ | n. | 诺瓦天主义 |  A schismatic movement within the early Christian Church (3rd century) led by Novatian, a Roman presbyter.  It held that the Church should not readmit those who had lapsed from the faith during persecution, particularly those who had offered sacrifice to Roman gods. |  Novatianism caused significant divisions within the Christian community.  诺瓦天主义造成了基督教社区内部的重大分裂。 |  The rigorist views of Novatianism contrasted sharply with the more lenient policies of other church leaders.  诺瓦天主义的严苛观点与其他教会领袖更为宽容的政策形成了鲜明对比。 |近义词: rigorism, puritanism | 反义词: leniency, laxity | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Novatian, the name of the Roman presbyter who led the movement, + -ism.
