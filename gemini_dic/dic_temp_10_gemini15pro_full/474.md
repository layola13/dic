abnegation: /ˌæbnɪˈɡeɪʃən/
n.
放弃；克制；拒绝；自制
the act of renouncing or rejecting something.
例句：His abnegation of worldly pleasures surprised his friends.  他放弃世俗享乐让他的朋友们感到惊讶。

例句：The athlete's abnegation of junk food is essential to his training regimen.  这位运动员戒掉垃圾食品对他训练计划至关重要。

近义词：renunciation, rejection, sacrifice, self-denial, forgoing
反义词：indulgence, acceptance, embrace
词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin "abnegātiōn-", from "abnegātiō", from "abnegāre" (to deny, renounce), from "ab-" (from, away from) + "negāre" (to deny).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*h₂neḱ-" (to reach, obtain).

USAGE->
She practiced abnegation of her desires to focus on her studies. = 她为了专注于学习而克制自己的欲望。
The monk's life was one of abnegation and devotion to God. =  僧侣的生活是克己奉神的。


PHRASE->
the abnegation of self =  自我克制
an act of abnegation =  一种克己的行为
practice abnegation =  实行克己
abnegation of rights = 放弃权利
abnegation of responsibility = 推卸责任
