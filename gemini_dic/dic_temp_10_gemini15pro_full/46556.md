dallies:/ˈdæliz/| n.|嬉戏，玩耍，调情|A playful, often flirtatious, exchange or activity; a frivolous pastime or amusement.  A delay or waste of time.|She spent her afternoons in dallies with the young gentlemen of the town. 她每天下午都和镇上的年轻绅士们嬉戏玩耍。|Synonyms: flirtations, trifling, toying, delaying, procrastination|Antonyms: work, labor, seriousness, haste, diligence|noun

dallies:/ˈdæliz/| v.|嬉戏，玩耍，调情，延误，浪费时间|(third-person singular simple present dallies, present participle dallying, simple past and past participle dallied) To engage in playful, often flirtatious, behavior; to trifle or toy. To delay or waste time; to loiter or linger.|He dallied with the idea of going to the party, but ultimately decided to stay home. 他考虑过去参加聚会，但最终决定待在家里。|Synonyms: flirt, trifle, toy, delay, linger|Antonyms: hasten, hurry, rush, expedite, work|verb
