hyposplenism:/haɪpoʊˈspliːnɪzəm/ /ˈhaɪpəʊspliːnɪzəm/
noun
脾功能减退
Decreased or absent splenic function.  This can be due to a variety of causes, including surgical removal of the spleen, certain diseases affecting the spleen, or congenital absence of the spleen.
Hyposplenism can increase the risk of serious infections.  People with hyposplenism should receive vaccinations against encapsulated bacteria.
近义词:asplenia (in cases of complete absence of splenic function)
反义词: hypersplenism
