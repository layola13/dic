hydriodide:/ˌhaɪdrɪˈaɪdaɪd/
noun
氢碘化物; 碘化氢盐
A binary compound of hydrogen and iodine.  Specifically, it often refers to the ionic compounds containing the iodide anion (I⁻).
Potassium hydriodide is used in the preparation of organic compounds. 碘化钾用于制备有机化合物。
synonyms: iodide (when referring to the salt)
antonyms: None (specific antonyms are context-dependent, e.g., an oxidizing agent might be considered an antonym in a redox reaction)
