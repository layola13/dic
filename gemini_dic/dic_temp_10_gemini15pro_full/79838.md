glyphograph:/ˈɡlɪfəɡræf/| n.|雕版印刷; 浮雕印刷品|A glyphograph is a process of printing from a relief surface, similar to a woodcut, but made on a softer material.|The museum displayed a collection of rare glyphographs from the 19th century.  博物馆展出了一批19世纪的珍稀雕版印刷品。|Synonyms: woodcut, engraving, relief print|Antonyms: intaglio, etching|noun


glyphograph:/ˈɡlɪfəɡræf/| n.|雕刻;雕刻作品|A glyphograph can also refer to the carved or engraved image itself.|The glyphograph depicted a scene from ancient mythology. 这幅雕刻描绘了古代神话中的一个场景。|Synonyms: carving, engraving, inscription|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could consider "blank surface")|noun
