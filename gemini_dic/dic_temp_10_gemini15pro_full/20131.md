bipartition:/ˌbaɪpɑːrˈtɪʃən/| n.|二分法，一分為二|The bipartition of the country led to years of conflict. 这个国家的二分导致了多年的冲突。|例句：The bipartition of Germany after World War II created East and West Germany. 二战后德国的二分形成了东德和西德。

例句：The algorithm performs a bipartition of the graph into two sets of vertices.  该算法将图形二分为两组顶点。

近义词：division, separation, dichotomy|反义词:union, merger|词性:noun

bipartite:/baɪˈpɑːrtaɪt/| adj.|二分的，由两部分组成的|A bipartite graph is a graph whose vertices can be divided into two disjoint sets. 二部图是一种其顶点可以被分成两个不相交集合的图。|例句：The agreement was a bipartite contract between the company and its employees. 该协议是公司与其员工之间的两方合同。

例句：The bird has a bipartite beak, with a distinct upper and lower mandible. 这只鸟有一个二分的喙，有明显的上下颚。

近义词：two-part, dual, double|反义词:unified, single|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *bipartitus*, from bi- (“two”) + partitus (“divided”), past participle of partiri (“to divide”).
