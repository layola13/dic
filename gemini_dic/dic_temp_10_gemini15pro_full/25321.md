bugles:/ˈbjuːɡlz/ (US), /ˈbjuːɡlz/ (UK)| n. |军号；喇叭；（尤指狩猎时用的）号角；玻璃珠 |A brass instrument like a small trumpet, used for giving signals in the army, or (especially in the past) for hunting. Also, small glass beads used for decoration. |The bugles sounded, signaling the start of the battle. 军号响起，标志着战斗开始。|Synonyms: trumpet, horn, cornet|Antonyms: silence, quiet|noun

bugles:/ˈbjuːɡlz/ (US), /ˈbjuːɡlz/ (UK)| n. |圆锥形脆玉米片|A brand of corn snack shaped like a small cone. |He grabbed a bag of Bugles from the snack aisle. 他从零食区抓了一袋 Bugles。|Synonyms: corn snacks, chips |Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps healthy snacks)|noun
