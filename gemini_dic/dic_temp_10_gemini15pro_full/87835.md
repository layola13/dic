hidage:/ˈhaɪdɪdʒ/| n.| 租税，地税（按面积征收）|A tax formerly levied in England based on the number of hides of land owned.|The hidage was a significant source of revenue for the crown during the Anglo-Saxon period.  盎格鲁-撒克逊时期，地税是王室的重要收入来源。|近义词：land tax, property tax|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly tax exemption)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hīdāġe, from hīd (hide of land) + -āġe (payment, tax). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *hīdiz (piece of land).
