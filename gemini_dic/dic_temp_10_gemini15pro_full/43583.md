cream:/kriːm/| n. adj. v.|奶油，乳脂；精华；面霜；奶油色的|A thick white or pale yellow fatty liquid that rises to the top of milk, used in cooking or eaten as a dessert; the best part of something.|She added a dollop of cream to her coffee. 她往咖啡里加了一块奶油。|近义词：butter, lotion, creme, elite|反义词：skim milk, dregs|noun

cream:/kriːm/| n. adj. v.|奶油色的；乳白色的|Having the pale yellowish-white color of cream.|She wore a cream-colored dress. 她穿着一件奶油色的连衣裙。|近义词：ivory, off-white|反义词：black, dark|adjective

cream:/kriːm/| n. adj. v.|提取精华；彻底击败；使变成糊状|To remove the best part of something; to beat thoroughly, especially in a game or competition; to make something into a creamy consistency.|The company creams off the best graduates. 这家公司招揽了最优秀的毕业生。|近义词：skim, best, thrash, pulverize|反义词：lose, worsen, solidify|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *creme (unattested), from Latin crēmor "thick juice, broth," related to crēma "thick substance, dregs," from Proto-Indo-European *krei- "to sieve, distinguish, separate."
