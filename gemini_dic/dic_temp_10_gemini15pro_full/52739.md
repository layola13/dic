diktat:/dɪkˈtɑːt/ /ˈdɪktæt/| n. |独裁命令，强制规定|An order or decree imposed by someone in power without popular consent. |The company president issued a diktat that no one could leave before 6 pm. 公司总裁发布了一项强制规定，任何人不得在下午6点之前离开。

The government imposed a diktat banning public gatherings. 政府下令禁止公众集会。

|近义词：decree, edict, mandate, order, fiat|反义词：request, plea, petition|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Diktat*, from Latin *dictatum* ("something dictated"), neuter past participle of *dictare* ("to dictate"), frequentative of *dicere* ("to say"). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *deyk-*.
