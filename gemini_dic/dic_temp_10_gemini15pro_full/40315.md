contortion:/kənˈtɔːrʃn/ /kənˈtɔːrʃən/| n. |扭弯，扭曲；弯曲的形状|a twisting or bending of something out of its normal shape; a distorted shape or position|The contortions of the acrobat were amazing. 杂技演员的扭转动作令人惊叹。|Synonyms: twist, bending, deformation, writhing|Antonyms: straightening, unbending|noun

contortions:/kənˈtɔːrʃnz/ /kənˈtɔːrʃənz/| n. |（尤指身体的）扭动，扭曲，弯曲|violent twisting or bending movements, especially of the body|The contortions of his face showed he was in pain. 他脸上扭曲的表情表明他很痛苦。|Synonyms: writhings, gyrations, convulsions|Antonyms: stillness, immobility|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contortionem, accusative of contortio (“a twisting”), from Latin contortus, past participle of contorquēre (“to twist violently, wrench, distort”), from con- (“together, thoroughly”) + torquēre (“to twist”).
