bolted:/ˈboʊltɪd/ (US) /ˈbəʊltɪd/ (UK)| v. adj.|拴住的，用螺栓固定的；狼吞虎咽的|fastened with a bolt; having run away suddenly; (of food) eaten quickly|The door was bolted shut. 门被紧紧地拴住了。|近义词：fastened, secured, locked, bolted down, gobbled|反义词:unbolted, loose, open|词性:动词(过去式和过去分词)；形容词

bolted:/ˈboʊltɪd/ (US) /ˈbəʊltɪd/ (UK)| adj.|狼吞虎咽的|eaten quickly|He bolted his breakfast and rushed out the door. 他狼吞虎咽地吃完早餐，冲出了门。|近义词：gobbled, devoured, wolfed down|反义词:savored, nibbled|词性:形容词

词源：From the verb "bolt," meaning "to fasten with a bolt," from Old English *bolt "short, thick arrow," related to Old Norse bolti "bolt, beam." The sense of "to eat quickly" arose from the idea of swallowing food quickly, like shooting a bolt.
