ne'er-do-wells: /ˌneərduːˈwɛlz/ | n. | 无赖，游手好闲的人 | A person who is habitually idle, irresponsible, and unreliable. |  The town was plagued by ne'er-do-wells who loitered in the streets and caused trouble. 这个小镇饱受街头游荡、惹是生非的无赖的困扰。

| 近义词: good-for-nothings, loafers, idlers, scoundrels | 反义词:  hard workers, responsible citizens | 词性: noun


ne'er-do-well: /ˌneərduːˈwɛl/ | adj. |  不务正业的，游手好闲的 | Characterized by idleness, irresponsibility, and unreliability. |  His ne'er-do-well attitude prevented him from holding down a steady job. 他不务正业的态度使他无法保住一份稳定的工作。

| 近义词: shiftless, worthless, indolent | 反义词:  diligent, industrious, responsible | 词性: adjective


词源:  "ne'er" is a contraction of "never," and "do-well" refers to someone who prospers or succeeds.  Therefore, a "ne'er-do-well" is literally someone who never does well or prospers.
