cauterism:/ˈkɔːtərɪzəm/
n.
烧灼术，烙术
The application of a hot iron or caustic substance to destroy tissue.
The doctor performed cauterism to stop the bleeding. 医生进行了烧灼术来止血。

cauterization:/ˌkɔːtərɪˈzeɪʃn/
n.
烧灼，烙术
The act of cauterizing.
Cauterization was used to seal the wound. 烧灼被用来封闭伤口。

cauterize:/ˈkɔːtəraɪz/
vt.
烧灼，烙
To burn or sear (tissue) with a hot iron or caustic substance.
The surgeon cauterized the blood vessel. 外科医生烧灼了血管。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cauterismus, from Greek kautēriasmos, from kautēriazō ("I burn or brand with a hot iron"), from kautērion ("branding iron"), from kaio ("I burn").

SYNONYMS->
(n.) cauterization, sear, branding
(v.) burn, sear, brand

ANTONYMS->
(v.) heal, soothe, cool

PARTS OF SPEECH->
cauterism: noun
cauterization: noun
cauterize: verb
