cataclysm:/ˈkætəklɪzəm/| n.|灾难，大灾变|A cataclysm is a sudden and violent event, often a natural disaster, that causes widespread destruction and upheaval.|The earthquake was a cataclysm that destroyed entire cities.  地震是一场摧毁了整座城市的灾难。|catastrophe, disaster, calamity|boon, blessing|noun
cataclysms:/ˈkætəklɪzəmz/| n.|灾难，大灾变（复数）|Plural form of cataclysm, referring to multiple sudden and violent events causing widespread destruction.|The series of hurricanes and earthquakes felt like a chain of cataclysms.  这一系列的飓风和地震感觉就像一连串的灾难。|catastrophes, disasters, calamities|boons, blessings|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cataclysme*, from Late Latin *cataclysmus*, from Greek *kataklysmos* "deluge, flood," from *kataklyzein* "to inundate, deluge," from *kata-* "down" + *klyzein* "to wash, dash over."
