astragalomancy: /əˈstræɡələˌmænsi/ | n. | 占卜 (通过掷骰子或踝骨) | Divination by casting dice or knuckle-bones, especially the ankle bones of sheep or goats.  |  Example: Astragalomancy was a common practice in ancient Greece.  古代希腊盛行占卜。

Example:  He consulted an oracle who practiced astragalomancy. 他咨询了一位练习占卜的神谕。 | Synonym: divination, sortilege, cleromancy | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps 'scientific analysis' or 'rational thought') | noun

From Greek *astragalos* (ankle bone, die) + *-manteia* (divination).
