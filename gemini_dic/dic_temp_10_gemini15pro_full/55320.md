dissonance:/ˈdɪsənəns/ (US), /ˈdɪsənəns/ (UK)| n.|不和谐，不一致，冲突|A lack of harmony or agreement between things; a combination of sounds that is unpleasant or harsh.|The dissonance between her words and actions confused everyone. 她言行不一，让每个人都很困惑。|近义词：discord, conflict, clash, inconsistency, incongruity|反义词：harmony, concord, agreement, consistency|词性:名词

dissonant:/ˈdɪsənənt/ (US), /ˈdɪsənənt/ (UK)| adj.|不和谐的，不一致的|Not in harmony; disagreeing; incongruous.|The dissonant chords created a sense of unease. 不和谐的和弦营造了一种不安的感觉。|近义词：discordant, clashing, conflicting, inharmonious|反义词：harmonious, consonant, agreeable, concordant|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin dissonantia "dissonance," from dissonantem (nominative dissonans) "discordant," present participle of dissonare "to sound discordantly," from dis- "apart, away" + sonare "to sound."
