insinuatingly:/ɪnˈsɪnjuˌeɪtɪŋli/ | adv. |  insinuatingly | in a way that suggests something bad or unpleasant indirectly | He smiled insinuatingly, making her feel uncomfortable. 他带着暗示性地笑了，让她感到不舒服。|近义词：suggestively, slyly, subtly |反义词：directly, frankly, openly |词性:副词
insinuate:/ɪnˈsɪnjuˌeɪt/ | v. | 暗示；旁敲侧击地说 | to suggest something bad or unpleasant indirectly | He insinuated that she was lying. 他暗示她在撒谎。|近义词：imply, suggest, hint |反义词：state, declare, assert |词性:动词
insinuation:/ɪnˌsɪnjuˈeɪʃn/ | n. | 暗示 | an indirect suggestion about something bad or unpleasant | His insinuation that she was dishonest angered her. 他暗示她不诚实的言论激怒了她。|近义词：implication, suggestion, hint |反义词：statement, declaration, assertion |词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin insinuatus, past participle of insinuare "to introduce by windings and turnings, wind or worm one's way into," figuratively "to ingratiate oneself," from in- "into" + sinuare "to curve, bend," from sinus "curve, fold."
