desensitized:/diːˈsɛnsɪtaɪzd/
adjective
脱敏的，迟钝的
Made less sensitive.  Reduced or eliminated emotional responsiveness to something, usually something unpleasant or shocking.
After years of working in the emergency room, the doctor had become desensitized to the sight of blood.  多年在急诊室工作后，这位医生对看到血已经麻木了。
近义词:numb, indifferent, hardened
反义词:sensitive, responsive, susceptible
词性:adjective

desensitize:/diːˈsɛnsɪtaɪz/
verb
使脱敏，使迟钝
To make someone less sensitive to something. To reduce or eliminate emotional responsiveness to something, usually something unpleasant or shocking.
Exposure therapy aims to desensitize people to their phobias. 暴露疗法旨在使人们对恐惧症脱敏。
近义词:numb, dull, harden
反义词:sensitize, sharpen
词性:verb

词源:  From de- + sensitize,  from  sensitive + -ize.  Ultimately from Latin sentire "to feel."
