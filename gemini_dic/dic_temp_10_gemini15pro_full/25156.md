buckraming:/ˈbʌkrəmɪŋ/| n.|粗硬亚麻布的支撑；虚张声势；装腔作势|The act of supporting or stiffening something with buckram, often figuratively to describe an artificial or exaggerated demeanor.  It can also refer to the buckram itself.|The play felt stiff and artificial, full of buckraming and overacting.  这齣戏感觉僵硬且不自然，充满了虚张声势和夸张的表演。|近义词：stiffening, bolstering, posturing, affectation|反义词：naturalness, genuineness, sincerity|noun

buckram:/ˈbʌkrəm/| n. adj.|粗硬亚麻布；用粗硬亚麻布支撑的；僵硬的|A coarse, stiff cloth made of linen or cotton, heavily sized with glue.  As an adjective, it describes something stiffened with this cloth or something that feels stiff and unyielding like it.|She wore a buckram dress that rustled when she moved. 她穿着一件走动时沙沙作响的粗硬亚麻布连衣裙。|近义词：(n.) stiff cloth, canvas; (adj.) stiff, rigid|反义词：(adj.) flexible, supple|noun, adjective
