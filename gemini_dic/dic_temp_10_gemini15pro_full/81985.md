gringa:/ˈɡriŋɡə/ (US), /ˈɡrɪŋɡə/ (UK)| n.|外国女人（尤指白人女性，在拉丁美洲国家使用，有时带有贬义）|A foreign woman, especially a white woman, in Latin America. Often used with derogatory connotations.|例句：The gringa stuck out in the small Mexican village.  这个外国女人在墨西哥小村庄里很显眼。

例句：He called her a gringa, which she found offensive. 他叫她外国女人，她觉得很冒犯。|近义词：foreigner, outsider|反义词:local, native|词性:noun

Etymology: From Spanish *gringa*, feminine of *gringo*, ultimately from Greek *grékos* "Greek".  The term originally referred to any foreigner who spoke a language other than Spanish, but eventually came to be associated primarily with English speakers and then, more specifically, with white, North American women.
