faction:/ˈfækʃən/| n.|派系，派别，内讧|A dissenting group within a larger one, especially in politics.|The party split into warring factions.  该党分裂成互相争斗的派系。|synonyms: clique,  group, sect, wing, bloc, caucus, coalition, lobby|antonyms: unity, harmony|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French, from Latin *factio(n-), from facere ‘do’.
