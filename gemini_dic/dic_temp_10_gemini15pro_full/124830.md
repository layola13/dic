muckers: /ˈmʌkərz/
noun
伙伴；朋友；同事（常用复数）
Close friends or companions, especially those who work together or share a common interest.
例句：
He went down the pub with his muckers after work.  他下班后和他的伙伴们去了酒吧。

The muckers on the building site always have a laugh together.  建筑工地的工友们总是笑得很开心。


近义词：mates, pals, buddies, friends, colleagues
反义词：enemies, rivals
词性：n. (noun)


muckers: /ˈmʌkərz/
verb (often used with "about/around")
胡闹；捣乱；闲逛
To mess around, play carelessly, or waste time.
例句：
Stop muckering about and get your homework done!  别再胡闹了，快去做作业！

They were muckering around in the garage all afternoon.  他们整个下午都在车库里瞎混。


近义词：mess around, fool around, play around, potter about
反义词：work diligently, focus
词性：v. (verb)


词源:
Likely from the earlier word "mucker," meaning someone who works with muck or dirt, particularly in stables or mines. Over time, it evolved to refer to anyone working in a messy or challenging environment, and eventually broadened to include close companions in general.  可能来源于早期单词“mucker”，意思是与污垢或泥土打交道的人，尤其是在马厩或矿井中。随着时间的推移，它演变成指在凌乱或具有挑战性的环境中工作的任何人，并最终扩展到包括一般的亲密伙伴。
