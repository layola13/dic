claptrap:/ˈklæptræp/| n.|废话，空话，哗众取宠的言辞|Pretentious or insincere language intended to impress others; nonsense.|The politician's speech was full of claptrap about freedom and democracy.  这位政客的演讲充满了关于自由和民主的空洞 rhetoric.|近义词：nonsense, drivel, balderdash, twaddle, hogwash|反义词：sense, truth, fact|noun
Etymology: From 'clap' (meaning to applaud) + 'trap' (meaning a device or trick). Originally referred to a trick to elicit applause.
词性: noun
