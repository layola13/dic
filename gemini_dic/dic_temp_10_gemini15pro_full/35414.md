co-ordinated:/kəˈɔːrdɪneɪtɪd/ | adj. | 协调的，配合的 | Arranged or organized in a harmonious and efficient way.  Working together smoothly and efficiently. |  The dancers' movements were perfectly co-ordinated. 舞蹈演员的动作完美协调。 |  The co-ordinated attack took the enemy by surprise. 协调一致的进攻使敌人措手不及。 |  synchronized, harmonized, integrated, organized |  disorganized, chaotic, uncoordinated | adjective

co-ordinate:/kəʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt/ | v. | 协调，配合 | To bring into a common action, movement, or condition : harmonize.  To cause to work or act together smoothly and efficiently. |  We need to co-ordinate our efforts to achieve our goals. 我们需要协调我们的努力来实现我们的目标。 | She co-ordinated the marketing campaign with the product launch. 她将营销活动与产品发布协调起来。 |  organize, synchronize, harmonize, integrate |  disorganize, separate | verb

co-ordination:/kəʊˌɔːrdɪˈneɪʃn/ | n. | 协调，配合 | The organization of the different elements of a complex body or activity so as to enable them to work together effectively. | The project requires close co-ordination between the various departments. 该项目需要各部门之间的密切协调。 |  He showed excellent hand-eye co-ordination. 他表现出了极佳的手眼协调能力。 |  collaboration, cooperation, organization, synchronization |  disorganization, chaos | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: from medieval Latin coordinatus, past participle of coordinare, from co- ‘together’ + ordinare ‘arrange’.
