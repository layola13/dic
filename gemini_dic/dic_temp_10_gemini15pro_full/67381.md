extenuate:/ɪkˈstɛnjuˌeɪt/| v. | 减轻；为…辩解；使人原谅 |  To lessen the seriousness of (an offense) by giving excuses or serving as mitigating circumstances. |  His youth extenuates his crime to some degree.  他的年轻在某种程度上减轻了他的罪行。 |  mitigate, excuse, palliate, lessen |  aggravate, worsen, exacerbate | verb
extenuating:/ɪkˈstɛnjuˌeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. |  情有可原的；使罪过显得轻微的 | Serving to extenuate; lessening the seriousness of something. | There were extenuating circumstances that caused her to miss the deadline. 她错过截止日期是有情可原的。| mitigating, excusing | aggravating | adjective
extenuation:/ɪkˌstɛnjuˈeɪʃən/ | n. |  减轻；辩解；情有可原 |  The act of attempting to lessen the seriousness of by providing partial excuses. |  The jury found no extenuation for the defendant's actions. 陪审团认为被告的行为没有任何情有可原之处。 | mitigation, excuse, palliation | aggravation | noun
