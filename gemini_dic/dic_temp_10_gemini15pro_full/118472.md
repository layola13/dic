mesiad: /ˈmiːsiæd/ | n. |  史诗;  英雄叙事诗;  长诗; | A long narrative poem celebrating the great deeds of a hero or heroes, typically derived from oral tradition. | Example:  Milton's "Paradise Lost" is considered a mesiad.  弥尔顿的《失乐园》被认为是一部史诗。

Example: The Aeneid is a Roman mesiad.  《埃涅阿斯纪》是一部罗马史诗。 | Synonym: epic, saga, epopee | Antonym:  ditty,  limerick | noun


Etymology:  From Greek "mesos" (middle) + "aeidein" (to sing).  Originally referred to a type of poem with a dactylic hexameter meter, often dealing with heroic themes.  源自希腊语 "mesos"（中间） + "aeidein"（歌唱）。最初指的是一种用扬抑六步格写成的诗，通常处理英雄主题。
