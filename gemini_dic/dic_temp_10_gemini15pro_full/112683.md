lowkey:/ˌloʊˈkiː/| adj. adv.|低调的，低调地|Not intended to attract a lot of attention; restrained or understated.  Relatively or comparatively low in intensity.|例句：They had a lowkey wedding ceremony with only close family and friends. 他们举行了一个低调的婚礼，只有亲朋好友参加。|例句：She wanted to keep the party lowkey. 她想让派对保持低调。|近义词：subtle, understated, quiet, restrained, discreet|反义词：flashy, ostentatious, flamboyant|词性:形容词, 副词

词源:  A combination of "low" and "key," originating in hip-hop culture.  "Key" in this context refers to musical key, where a lower key is generally more subdued.
