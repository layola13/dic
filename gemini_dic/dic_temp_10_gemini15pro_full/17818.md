bedsores:/ˈbedˌsɔːrz/ (US), /ˈbedˌsɔːz/ (UK)| n. |褥疮；压疮|Sores that develop on areas of the skin that are under pressure from lying in bed or sitting in a chair for long periods. | The elderly patient developed bedsores from being bedridden for so long.  这位卧床不起的老人因长期卧床而长了褥疮。|Synonyms: pressure sores, decubitus ulcers|Antonyms: healthy skin|Noun

bedsores:/ˈbedˌsɔːrz/ (US), /ˈbedˌsɔːz/ (UK)| n. |褥疮；压疮|Sores that develop due to prolonged pressure on the skin, typically in bedridden or immobile individuals. |Regular turning and repositioning of the patient is essential to prevent bedsores. 定期为病人翻身和调整体位对预防褥疮至关重要。|Synonyms: pressure ulcers, decubitus ulcers|Antonyms: healthy skin|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "bed" + "sore".  "Bed" refers to the place where the sores often develop, and "sore" refers to a painful or damaged area of skin.
