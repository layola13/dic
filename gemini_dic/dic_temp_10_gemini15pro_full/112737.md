lpg:/ˌel piː 'dʒiː/| n.|液化石油气|Liquefied Petroleum Gas, a flammable mixture of hydrocarbon gases used as fuel.|We use LPG for cooking. 我们用液化石油气做饭。|propane, butane|natural gas|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
LPG is an initialism, formed from the initial letters of Liquefied Petroleum Gas.  "Liquefied" comes from the Latin "liquefacere" meaning "to make liquid." "Petroleum" comes from the Latin "petra" (rock) and "oleum" (oil). "Gas" is from the Greek "khaos," meaning void.
