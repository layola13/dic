overpreoccupy: /ˌoʊvərpriˈɒkjupaɪ/ (UK) /ˌoʊvərpriˈɑːkjupaɪ/ (US)| v. |过度关注，过分担忧|To occupy the mind of (someone) too much; to cause (someone) to worry or think too much about something.|He was overpreoccupied with his work and neglected his family. 他过度专注于工作而忽略了家人。|The upcoming exam overpreoccupied her so much that she couldn't sleep. 即将到来的考试让她过度担忧，以至于无法入睡。|近义词: obsess, fixate, worry excessively|反义词: neglect, ignore, disregard|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (excessive) + "preoccupy" (to occupy beforehand).  "Preoccupy" comes from the Latin "praeoccupare," from "prae-" (before) + "occupare" (to seize, occupy).
