nfc:/ˌen ef ˈsiː/ | n. |近场通信|Near-field communication (NFC) is a set of communication protocols that enables two electronic devices, one of which is usually a portable device such as a smartphone, to establish communication by bringing them within 4 cm (1.6 in) of each other.|I use NFC to pay for my groceries. 我用NFC支付我的杂货。|近义词: RFID, Bluetooth (in some applications) |反义词:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps "long-range communication") |词性: noun
