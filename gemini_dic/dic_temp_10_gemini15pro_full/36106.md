coerce:/koʊˈɜːrs/ (US) /kəʊˈɜːs/ (UK)| v.|强制,胁迫|To persuade (an unwilling person) to do something by using force or threats.|The government coerced the rebels into laying down their arms. 政府胁迫叛军放下武器。|Synonyms: force, compel, pressure, intimidate|Antonyms: persuade, encourage|verb

coerce:/koʊˈɜːrs/ (US) /kəʊˈɜːs/ (UK)| v.|强迫,迫使|To make someone do something they don't want to do by using threats or force.|He was coerced into signing the confession. 他被迫签署了供词。|Synonyms: compel, force, pressure|Antonyms: allow, permit|verb
