/ˈɛktəˌproʊktə/ | n. | 外肛动物 | Aquatic invertebrate animals, characterized by a lophophore (a crown of ciliated tentacles around the mouth) used for feeding, and a U-shaped digestive tract. They typically form colonies, and their anus is located outside the ring of tentacles. |  Ectoprocts are commonly found encrusting rocks and other surfaces in marine environments. 外肛动物通常在海洋环境中包裹岩石和其他表面。| Bryozoa, Polyzoa |  None | noun
