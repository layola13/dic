hematotherma:/ˌhiːmətəˈθɜːrmə/ | n. | 温血动物 | An animal whose body temperature is regulated, at least in part, by its metabolic processes.  It is a combination of "hemo" (blood) and "therm" (heat). |  Example: Birds and mammals are hematotherms, maintaining a relatively constant internal body temperature. 鸟类和哺乳动物是温血动物，保持相对恒定的体温。| Example:  Unlike reptiles, hematotherms can remain active in cold environments. 与爬行动物不同，恒温动物可以在寒冷的环境中保持活跃。 | Synonym: endotherm, warm-blooded animal | Antonym: poikilotherm, cold-blooded animal | Noun
