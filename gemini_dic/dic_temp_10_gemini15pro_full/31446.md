chark:/tʃɑːrk/| v. n.| 烧焦，炭化，炭|To burn (something) so that it becomes black and brittle.  As a noun, it refers to the charred remains.|例句：The wood charked in the intense heat. 木头在高温下烧焦了。

|例句：The steak was charked on the outside but still rare inside. 牛排外面烧焦了，但里面还是生的。|近义词：char, scorch, sear|反义词: extinguish, douse|词性:verb, noun
Etymology: From Middle English *charken, likely a frequentative of charren, from Old English cearcian (“to creak, crackle”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *karkōną (“to crackle, creak”).  Related to Old High German kerran (“to lament”), Old Norse kerra (“to creak, groan”).
