ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *oposite, from Latin oppositus (“placed against, opposed”), past participle of oppōnō (“I place against, oppose”). Equivalent to ob- (“against”) + positus (“placed, situated”), perfect passive participle of pōnō (“I put, place”).
