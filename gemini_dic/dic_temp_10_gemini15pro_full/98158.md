inscapes:/ˈɪnskeɪps/| n.|内景；心灵景象|A term coined by Gerard Manley Hopkins to describe the unique inner nature of a thing, as opposed to its outer appearance. This inner nature is revealed through the distinctive design, pattern, or characteristics that give it its individual identity.  It often involves intense sensory perception and emotional response.|Example: The poet captured the inscapes of the natural world in vivid detail. 诗人生动地捕捉了自然世界的内在景象。

Example:  Hopkins believed that recognizing the inscapes of things could lead to a deeper understanding of God's creation. 霍普金斯认为，认识事物的内在景象可以使人更深刻地理解上帝的创造。|近义词：inner landscape, inner world, soul, spirit, essence|反义词:exterior, outside, surface|词性:noun
