broodily:/ˈbruːdɪli/| adv.|闷闷不乐地，沉思地|In a manner showing deep unhappiness or thoughtfulness, often with resentment or anger.|He stared broodily out the window, thinking about what he'd lost.|近义词：glumly, moodily, sullenly, thoughtfully|反义词：cheerfully, happily|词性:副词

词源: From brood (v.) + -ly (adv. suffix).  "Brood" comes from Old English "brōd" meaning "a young bird," later expanding to mean "the young of animals" and then "to sit on eggs." The sense of brooding as "meditating moodily" developed in the 16th century.
