mischaracterizes:/ˌmɪskærəktəˈraɪzɪz/
|v.|错误地描述，歪曲|To describe (someone or something) inaccurately or unfairly.|The media mischaracterizes the situation, portraying it as more violent than it actually was. 媒体歪曲了局势，将其描绘得比实际情况更暴力。|misrepresents, distorts, falsifies|accurately represents, portrays truthfully|verb
ETYMOLOGY->
mis- (prefix): from Old French mes- "wrongly, badly," from Latin minus "less"
characterize (verb): from Late Latin characterizare "to mark with a character," from Latin character "a distinctive mark" + -ize (suffix)
