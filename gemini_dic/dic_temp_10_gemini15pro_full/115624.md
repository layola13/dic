marcher:/ˈmɑːrtʃər/ (US) /ˈmɑːtʃə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 行军者，游行者；进行曲爱好者 |A person who marches, especially in a protest or demonstration, or as part of a military formation. |例句：The marchers carried banners and chanted slogans. 游行者们举着横幅，高呼口号。

例句：The band played a rousing march for the marchers. 乐队为游行者们演奏了一首激昂的进行曲。|近义词：protester, demonstrator, parader, hiker, walker|反义词: bystander, spectator|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "marchier" (to walk, to tread), from "marche" (boundary, frontier, march), from Frankish "*marka" (boundary), from Proto-Germanic "*markō" (boundary, borderland). Related to English "mark". Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*mereg-" (edge, boundary).
