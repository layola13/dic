harrier:/ˈhæriər/ | n. | 鹞; 骚扰者 | A slender, narrow-winged hawk that hunts small animals, often by hovering low over open ground.  A person who persistently annoys or bothers someone. | The harrier hovered over the field, searching for prey.  那只鹞在田野上盘旋，寻找猎物。 The debt collectors were relentless harriers.  讨债人是不懈的骚扰者。| 近义词: hawk, raptor, tormentor, persecutor | 反义词: protector, defender | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *harier "to harry, ravage," from Frankish *herion "to devastate, plunder," from Proto-Germanic *harjaz "army."  The sense of "a kind of hawk" arose from the bird's hunting habits.
