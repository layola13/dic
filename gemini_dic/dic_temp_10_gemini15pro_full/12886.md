assumed: /əˈsuːmd/ | adj. | 假定的，设想的，装出来的 | supposed to be the case on the basis of probability or evidence but without proof |  He assumed an air of indifference. 他装出一副漠不关心的样子。 | synonyms: supposed, presumed, pretended, affected | antonyms: actual, real, genuine | adjective

assumed: /əˈsuːmd/ | v. | 假定，设想，承担，呈现 | take or begin to have (power or responsibility); suppose to be the case, without proof. | He assumed the presidency. 他就任总统职位。| synonyms:  presume, suppose, take on, adopt | antonyms: relinquish, abandon | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin assumptus, past participle of assumere "to take up, adopt, take to oneself," from Latin ad- "to" + sumere "to take," from sub- "under" + emere "to take, buy, obtain"
