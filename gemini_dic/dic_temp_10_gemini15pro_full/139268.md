obliquation:/ˌɒblɪˈɡeɪʃn/ | n. | 义务，责任，债务 | An obligation is something a person is morally or legally bound to do. |  I have an obligation to help my family. 我有义务帮助我的家人。

| Example:  She felt a strong obliquation to help the homeless. 她感到有强烈的义务帮助无家可归的人。| Synonyms: duty, responsibility, commitment | Antonyms: exemption, freedom, irresponsibility | noun


oblique:/əˈbliːk/ | adj. | 斜的，倾斜的；间接的，不直截了当的 |  Neither parallel nor at a right angle to a specified or implied line; slanting. Not explicit or direct in addressing a point. | The painting hung at an oblique angle. 这幅画挂得歪歪斜斜的。

| Example: He made an oblique reference to his past. 他拐弯抹角地提到了他的过去。 | Synonyms: slanted, inclined; indirect, evasive | Antonyms: straight, direct; explicit, forthright | adjective
