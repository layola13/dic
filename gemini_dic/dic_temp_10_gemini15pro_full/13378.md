atmospherics:/ˌætməsˈfɛrɪks/ | n. | 气氛，环境，（尤指）商业气氛，大气干扰 | The atmospherics in the restaurant were romantic.  这家餐厅的氛围很浪漫。 | English explanation: The prevailing mood or tone of a place or situation, especially one that affects the feelings of people present;  also, disturbances in radio reception caused by atmospheric conditions. | Example: The positive atmospherics at the conference boosted morale. 会议上积极的气氛鼓舞了士气。 | Synonym: ambiance, atmosphere, aura, vibe | Antonym:  -- (no direct antonym, could be considered as "bad vibes" or "negative energy" depending on context) | Noun


atmosphericses:  This word is not standard English. "Atmospherics" is already plural in its typical usage as a noun referring to atmospheric conditions affecting radio reception.  When referring to the general atmosphere or ambiance, "atmospherics" is generally treated as a mass noun and does not take a plural form.  Therefore, "atmosphericses" is grammatically incorrect.
