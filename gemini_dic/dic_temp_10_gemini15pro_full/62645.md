enfranchisers:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzərz/
n.
授予公民权的人；赋予选举权的人
People (or a legal body) who grant the right to vote, or otherwise bestow a privilege or right.
The 19th Amendment's enfranchisers opened up voting to millions of women.  第19修正案的赋予选举权者为数百万妇女打开了投票的大门。
The city council acted as enfranchisers, granting businesses the right to operate within the city limits. 市议会作为授权者，授予企业在市区范围内经营的权利。
Synonyms: empowerers, liberators, grantors
Antonyms: disenfranchisers, oppressors, restrictors
词性: noun
