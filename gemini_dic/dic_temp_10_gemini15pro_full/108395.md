lawgiver:/ˈlɔːɡɪvər/| n.|立法者，法律制定者|A person who creates or gives laws.|Moses is considered a great lawgiver. 摩西被认为是一位伟大的立法者.|Synonyms: legislator, lawmaker|Antonyms: lawbreaker, criminal|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lagu* "law" + *giefan* "to give."  The word "law" ultimately comes from Proto-Germanic *lagō* "something laid down, that which is fixed or set,"  and "give" comes from Proto-Germanic *geban*.
