limestones:/ˈlaɪmˌstoʊnz/| n. |石灰岩，石灰石|A sedimentary rock consisting mainly of calcium carbonate, CaCO3, formed by the deposition of the shells of marine organisms.|The White Cliffs of Dover are made of limestone. 多佛白崖是由石灰岩构成的。|Synonyms: calcite, chalk|Antonyms: granite, basalt|noun

limestone:/ˈlaɪmˌstoʊn/| n. |石灰岩，石灰石|A sedimentary rock consisting mainly of calcium carbonate, CaCO3, formed by the deposition of the shells of marine organisms.|Limestone is used in the production of cement. 石灰石用于生产水泥。|Synonyms: calcite, chalk|Antonyms: granite, basalt|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *līm-stān (attested only as līm-stenc "lime cement"), from līm "lime" + stān "stone".
