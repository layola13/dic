circumventive:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈvɛntɪv/ /ˌsɝːkəmˈvɛntɪv/| adj.|迂回的，规避的|Avoiding or getting around something, especially by clever or indirect means.  Employing a trick or tactic to avoid something. |例句：He found a circumventive way to avoid paying taxes. 他找到了一个规避纳税的迂回方法。

例句：Her circumventive strategy allowed her to bypass the bureaucracy. 她的迂回策略让她绕过了官僚机构。|近义词：evasive, indirect, oblique, devious|反义词：direct, straightforward, forthright|词性:adjective

词源:From Late Latin *circumventivus, from Latin circumventus, past participle of circumvenire (“to surround, encompass, circumvent”), from circum (“around”) + venire (“to come”).
