bipolarizing:/ˌbaɪˈpoʊləˌraɪzɪŋ/| adj.|两极化的，造成分裂的|Causing something to divide into two sharply contrasting groups or sets of opinions.|The issue of gun control is increasingly bipolarizing American society. 枪支管制问题正日益加剧美国社会的两极分化。|近义词：divisive, polarizing|反义词：unifying, uniting|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From bi- (two) + polar (having two poles) + -ize (to cause to become) + -ing (present participle suffix).  The word "polarize" itself comes from the idea of light waves being oriented in a specific plane, and by extension, to cause opinions or attitudes to become sharply divided.
