adversative: /ədˈvɜːsətɪv/ /ədˈvɜːrsətɪv/
adjective
表示相反、对立的；转折的
Expressing opposition or contrast; serving to indicate a contrast.
He used adversative conjunctions like "but" and "however" to connect the two clauses. 他使用像“but”和“however”这样的转折连词来连接这两个从句。

The lawyer presented adversative evidence that challenged the witness's testimony. 律师提出了与证人证词相反的证据。

近义词: contrasting, opposing, contradictory
反义词: supporting, confirming, similar
词性: adjective

adversative: /ədˈvɜːsətɪv/ /ədˈvɜːrsətɪv/
noun
反义词，转折词
A word or phrase that expresses opposition or contrast.
"But" is a common adversative. “But”是一个常见的反义词/转折词。

The use of adversatives helps to create a more complex and nuanced argument. 使用反义词/转折词有助于创建一个更复杂和细致的论点。


近义词: conjunction, connective
反义词:  N/A (Nouns don't typically have antonyms in the same way adjectives do)
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *adversativus, from Latin adversari (“to oppose”) + -ivus.

USAGE->
As an adjective:

The adversative conjunction highlighted the contrast between the two ideas.  转折连词突出了两种观点之间的对比。

His adversative stance on the issue made negotiation difficult. 他在这个问题上对立的立场使得谈判变得困难。


As a noun:

The writer used several adversatives to connect different parts of the text. 作者使用了几个转折词来连接文本的不同部分。

Understanding the function of adversatives is crucial for comprehending complex sentences. 理解转折词的功能对于理解复杂句子至关重要。
