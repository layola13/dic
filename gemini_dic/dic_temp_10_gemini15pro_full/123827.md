moraine:/məˈreɪn/ | n. | 冰碛 |  A mass of rocks and sediment carried down and deposited by a glacier, typically as ridges at its edges or extremity. | The hikers struggled across the rugged moraine.  徒步旅行者艰难地穿过崎岖的冰碛。| synonyms:  glacial drift, glacial till, ice debris | antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "bedrock" in certain contexts) | noun

moraine:/məˈreɪn/ | n. | 冰碛石 |  A rock or fragment of rock transported by a glacier. | The geologist examined the moraine for signs of ancient life. 地质学家检查冰碛石，寻找古代生命的迹象。| synonyms:  erratic, glacial boulder | antonyms: (No direct antonym) | noun
