hypernormalities:/ˌhaɪpərˌnɔːrˈmælɪtiz/ | n. | 超常态 |  A state of seeming normality that is achieved by excessive regulation or control, often masking underlying instability or dysfunction.  It suggests a situation that appears stable and normal on the surface, but is actually fragile and unsustainable due to the rigid and extreme measures used to maintain it. |  The country's hypernormality was shattered by the sudden economic collapse. 这个国家的超常态被突如其来的经济崩溃打破了。

The strict social controls created a sense of hypernormality, but dissent simmered beneath the surface. 严格的社会控制造成了一种超常态的感觉，但在表面之下，异议正在酝酿。 | 近义词: artificiality, facade, semblance | 反义词: instability, chaos, disorder | 词性: 名词

词源:  "Hyper-" (Greek: over, excessive) + "normality"
