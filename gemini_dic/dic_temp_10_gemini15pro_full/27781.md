cannibalizing:/ˈkænɪbəˌlaɪzɪŋ/
v.
蚕食，内部竞争
reducing the sales of one product by introducing a similar, competing product; taking parts from one machine to repair another.
The company is cannibalizing its own market by offering two competing versions of the same product.  这家公司通过提供同一产品的两个竞争版本来蚕食自己的市场。
The mechanic cannibalized parts from an old engine to fix the broken-down truck.  机修工从一台旧发动机上拆下零件来修理那辆抛锚的卡车。
synonyms:undermining, eroding, consuming, stripping
antonyms:supporting, bolstering, supplementing
verb

cannibalization:/ˌkænɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
n.
蚕食，内部竞争
the act of cannibalizing.
The cannibalization of sales by the new product line was an unforeseen consequence. 新产品线对销售的蚕食是一个无法预见的后果。
The cannibalization of the old equipment allowed them to keep the newer machines running. 旧设备的拆解让他们能够保持新机器的运转。
synonyms:erosion, attrition, depletion
antonyms:growth, augmentation, enhancement
noun
