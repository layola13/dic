energize:/ˈenərˌdʒaɪz/ (US), /ˈenədʒaɪz/ (UK)| v. |使充满活力，使活跃；激励；供能|To give energy or vigor to; to invigorate.|The coach's speech energized the team. 教练的讲话激励了球队。|Synonyms: invigorate, stimulate, revitalize, animate|Antonyms: enervate, weaken, exhaust, drain|verb

energize:/ˈenərˌdʒaɪz/ (US), /ˈenədʒaɪz/ (UK)| v. |使通电|To supply with electrical energy.|The power plant energizes the entire city. 发电厂为整个城市供电。|Synonyms: power, electrify|Antonyms: de-energize, disconnect|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *energizare, from Ancient Greek ἐνέργεια (enérgeia, “activity, operation”), from ἐν (en, “in, at”) + ἔργον (érgon, “work”).
