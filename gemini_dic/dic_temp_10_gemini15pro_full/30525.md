cephalata:/ˌsɛfəˈleɪtə/
| n. | 头足类动物 |Cephalata refers to a group of mollusks characterized by a prominent head and tentacles, including octopuses, squids, and cuttlefish.  It is derived from the Greek "kephalē" meaning "head".|Cephalata, such as the giant squid, are fascinating creatures of the deep sea. 巨大的鱿鱼，一种头足类动物，是深海迷人的生物。|Synonyms: cephalopods|Antonyms: N/A|noun
