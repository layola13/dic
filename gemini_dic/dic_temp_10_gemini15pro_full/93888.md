immortalised:/ɪˈmɔːtəlaɪzd/| v. |使不朽，使永垂不朽|To give lasting fame to someone or something, especially by writing about them or portraying them in a work of art.|Shakespeare's works have immortalised him in the eyes of the world. 莎士比亚的作品使他在世人眼中永垂不朽。|The events of that day were immortalised in a famous poem. 那一天的事件在一首著名的诗中被不朽化了。|Synonyms: perpetuate, memorialize, commemorate|Antonyms: forget, obliterate|verb

immortalised:/ɪˈmɔːtəlaɪzd/| adj. |不朽的，永生的|Made immortal; given lasting fame.|His immortalised image graces the walls of the museum. 他不朽的形象装点了博物馆的墙壁。|Her immortalised spirit lives on in her music. 她不朽的精神在她的音乐中永存。|Synonyms: eternal, undying, everlasting|Antonyms: mortal, perishable, transient|adjective
