carouser:/kəˈraʊzər/| n.|狂欢作乐的人，寻欢作乐的人|A person who engages in noisy, drunken revelry.|例句：The carousers kept the whole neighborhood awake all night. 那些狂欢作乐的人让整个街区整夜不得安宁。

例句：He was known as a bit of a carouser in his younger days. 他年轻时有点寻欢作乐的名声。|近义词：reveler, partygoer, merrymaker, roisterer|反义词:teetotaler, ascetic|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *carouser*, from *carous*, "a drinking feast," from German *gar aus*, literally "quite out," referring to emptying a glass.  The sense evolved from "a drinking party" to "a person who participates in drinking parties."
