malign:/məˈlaɪn/| v.  |诽谤，污蔑；中伤|to say bad things about someone publicly and falsely, in order to damage their reputation|The politician was maligned by the press.  这位政治家被媒体诽谤。|Synonyms: slander, defame, libel, vilify, smear, disparage|Antonyms: praise, commend, laud, extol|verb

malign:/məˈlaɪn/| adj.  |恶性的；有害的|causing harm; harmful|The tumor was malign. 肿瘤是恶性的。|Synonyms: harmful, evil, malevolent, malicious, detrimental, pernicious|Antonyms: benign, beneficial, harmless|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *malignier (attested in the derivative malignité "malignancy"), from Latin malignāri "to do or speak ill of, slander, envy," from malignus "ill-disposed, wicked," from male "badly" + -gnus related to genus "birth, race, kind."
