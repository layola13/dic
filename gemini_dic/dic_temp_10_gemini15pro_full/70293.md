fidget:/ˈfɪdʒɪt/| n. vi. |烦躁不安，坐立不安;  fidgety adj. |A quick, small movement, typically a repeated one, caused by nervousness or impatience.  To make small, restless movements, typically because of nervousness or boredom.|He kept fidgeting with his pen during the meeting.  会议期间他不停地摆弄他的钢笔。|近义词：wriggle, squirm, twitch|反义词:still, calm|词性:noun, verb

fidgety:/ˈfɪdʒɪti/| adj. |烦躁不安的，坐立不安的|Inclined to fidget; restless or uneasy.|The children were getting fidgety waiting for the show to start. 孩子们在等待演出开始时变得烦躁不安。|近义词：restless, uneasy, impatient|反义词:calm, patient|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *figeten, frequentative of *fike, *fikete (“to move restlessly, hasten”), of unknown origin.  Possibly related to Norwegian dialectal fikla (“to feel, grope, fumble”).
