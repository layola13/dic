crowflower: /ˈkroʊˌflaʊər/ | n. | 野蔷薇，黑莓 | A wild rose, especially the dog rose (Rosa canina).  It is sometimes used more generally to refer to various species of wild roses and blackberries. |例句： The hedgerows were filled with fragrant crowflowers.  篱笆上开满了芬芳的野蔷薇。

例句：She picked a bouquet of crowflowers on her walk. 她在散步时摘了一束野蔷薇。 |近义词：dog rose, wild rose, blackberry (depending on context) |反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps cultivated rose) |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *croweflower*, likely from the resemblance of the flower's thorns to a crow's foot, or possibly because crows were known to eat the fruit.  The element "crow" may also refer to the black color of the fruit in some varieties.
