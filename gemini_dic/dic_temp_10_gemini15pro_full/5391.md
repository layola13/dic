alma:/ˈælmə/ | n. | 母校；灵魂；苹果 |  From Latin, meaning "nourishing, fostering." In some contexts, it can refer to the soul or animating principle. In Spanish, it means "apple." | She often reminisced about her alma mater. 她经常回忆起她的母校。|  The poet wrote about the alma, the essence of being. 诗人写了关于灵魂，存在的本质。 | synonyms: university, college, school, spirit, essence | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for alma mater or alma. For the "soul" meaning, perhaps "body" or "corpse" could be considered a loose antonym.) | noun
