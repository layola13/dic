fairy: /ˈfeəri/ (UK) /ˈferi/ (US)| n.| 仙女，小精灵|A mythical being of folk tales, typically having magical powers, often in the form of a very small person with wings.|She believes in fairies. 她相信有仙女。|The tooth fairy left me a dollar. 牙仙给我留了一美元。|近义词：elf, sprite, pixie|反义词:giant, monster|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *fairie*, from Old French *faerie* ("enchantment, fairyland"), from *fae* ("fairy"), from Vulgar Latin *fata* ("fate"), from Latin *fatum* ("that which has been spoken; destiny, fate").
