nontangiblenesses:/ˌnɒntænˈdʒɪblənəsɪz/ | n. | 无形资产，无形性 |  The plural form of "nontangibleness," referring to the quality of being intangible; difficult or impossible to define or measure. | The company's nontangiblenesses, such as its brand reputation and customer loyalty, are crucial to its success. 该公司的无形资产，例如品牌声誉和客户忠诚度，对其成功至关重要。| The report highlighted the importance of the company's nontangiblenesses, including its skilled workforce and innovative culture.  报告强调了公司无形资产的重要性，包括其熟练的员工队伍和创新文化。 | synonyms: intangibles, impalpabilities | antonyms: tangibles, concreteness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + tangible + -ness + -es.  "Tangible" comes from Late Latin "tangibilis" meaning "that can be touched,"  from Latin "tangere" meaning "to touch."  The suffix "-ness" forms nouns denoting a state or quality. The suffix "-es" creates the plural form.
