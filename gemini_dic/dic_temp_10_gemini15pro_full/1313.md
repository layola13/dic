accredit:/əˈkrɛdɪt/ | vt. | 授权；委派；正式认可；相信 | To officially recognize, authorize, or give credit to someone or something. |  The agency was accredited to inspect factories.  该机构被授权检查工厂。

The university is accredited by the government. 这所大学得到了政府的认可。 | 近义词：authorize, certify, license, recognize | 反义词：discredit, disapprove | 词性：动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The school accredits teachers to teach specific subjects. = 学校授权教师教授特定科目。
This organization accredits hospitals that meet certain standards. = 该组织认可符合特定标准的医院。

一般过去时:
The government accredited the new ambassador last week. = 政府上周正式任命了新的大使。
They accredited the discovery to a team of scientists. = 他们将这一发现归功于一个科学家团队。

现在完成时:
The university has accredited several new online courses. = 这所大学已经认可了几门新的在线课程。
The board has accredited him as a qualified surgeon. = 董事会已经认可他为合格的外科医生。

过去完成时:
The agency had accredited the program before the scandal broke. =  丑闻爆发前，该机构已经认可了这个项目。
They had accredited his success to hard work and dedication. = 他们将他过去的成功归功于努力工作和奉献精神。

将来时:
The committee will accredit the new laboratory next month. = 委员会将于下月认可新的实验室。
They will accredit her with completing the project successfully. = 他们会认为是她成功完成了这个项目。

被动语态:
The hospital is accredited by the Joint Commission. = 这家医院获得了联合委员会的认证。
New teachers are accredited after a probationary period. = 新教师在试用期后获得认证。


PHRASE->
accredit somebody with something = 把…归于某人;认为某人…
accredit something to somebody = 把…归于某人;认为某人…
accredit somebody to do something = 授权某人做某事
be accredited by = 被…认可/认证
be accredited to = 被派驻到...; 被授权到...
