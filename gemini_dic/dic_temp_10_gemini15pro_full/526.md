abominableness:/əˌbɒmɪnəblˈnɛs/| n.|可憎，令人厌恶|The abominableness of the crime shocked the nation. 这起罪行的可憎震惊了全国。 The sheer abominableness of his behavior made her leave. 他行为的极度恶劣让她离开了。|近义词：loathsomeness, odiousness, repulsiveness, vileness, hatefulness|反义词：pleasantness, agreeableness, attractiveness, beauty|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *abominablenesse*, from Old French *abominablete*, from Late Latin *abōminābilis* (“detestable, loathsome”), from Latin *abōminor* (“to deprecate as ominous, abhor, detest”), from *ab-* (“from, away from”) + *ōmen* (“omen, portent”).

USAGE->
The noun "abominableness" is typically used to describe something extremely unpleasant or morally reprehensible. It's less common in everyday conversation and more frequently found in formal or literary contexts.

Example 1:  The abominableness of war is undeniable. = 战争的可憎是不可否认的。

Example 2:  He spoke of the abominableness of slavery. = 他谈到了奴隶制的可恶。

PHRASE->
Due to its low frequency of use, "abominableness" doesn't have common set phrases associated with it.  It is generally used directly with articles (the, an) followed by a prepositional phrase (of) to explain what is abominable.
