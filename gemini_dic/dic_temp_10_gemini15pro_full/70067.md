feudalisms:/ˈfjuːdəˌlɪzəmz/
n.
封建制度（复数）
The social system of feudalism.  A social system that existed in Europe during the Middle Ages in which people worked and fought for nobles who gave them protection and the use of land in return.
The decline of feudalisms marked a turning point in European history.  封建制度的衰落标志着欧洲历史的一个转折点。
Medieval Europe was characterized by feudalisms. 中世纪的欧洲以封建制度为特征。
近义词:feudal systems, feudal society
反义词: democracy, republic
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *feodalism, from feodal + -isme. From Medieval Latin feudalis, from feudum (“fief”) + -alis.
