acerbity: /əˈsɜːrbəti/ /əˈsɜːrbɪti/
noun
酸涩；尖刻；辛辣
a state of being harsh or severe in temper or expression; sharpness of temper
Her acerbity can be off-putting to some people. 她尖刻的言辞可能会让一些人反感。

The critic's acerbity was evident in his scathing review. 批评家尖刻的评论显而易见。

近义词: bitterness, sourness, tartness, acrimony, harshness, astringency
反义词: sweetness, mildness, gentleness, amenity
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *acerbitas, from Latin acerbus (“sour, bitter, harsh”), from acer (“sharp, pointed, keen”).

USAGE->
The acerbity of the unripe fruit made her wince. = 未成熟水果的酸涩让她皱起了眉头。

The wine had a pleasant acerbity that complemented the cheese. = 这款酒有一种令人愉悦的酸涩，与奶酪相得益彰。

PHRASE->
the acerbity of her tone = 她语气中的尖刻
the acerbity of the lemon = 柠檬的酸味
the acerbity of the criticism = 批评的尖刻
the acerbity of the winter air = 冬日空气的刺骨
the acerbity of his wit = 他机智的辛辣
