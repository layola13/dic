confrontational:/ˌkɒnfrʌnˈteɪʃənl/ | adj. | 对抗性的，挑衅的 | Tending to deal with situations in an aggressive way; challenging or provocative. |  He has a confrontational style of management. 他有一种对抗性的管理风格。| The lawyer took a confrontational approach during the cross-examination.  律师在盘问时采取了对抗性的方式。|近义词: aggressive, antagonistic, belligerent, hostile, provocative |反义词: conciliatory, cooperative, passive, diplomatic |词性:形容词

confrontation: /ˌkɒnfrʌnˈteɪʃn/ | n. | 对抗，冲突 | A hostile or argumentative meeting or situation between opposing parties. | The two groups had a confrontation outside the building.  这两个团体在大楼外发生了冲突。| There were violent confrontations between police and protesters. 警察和抗议者之间发生了暴力冲突。|近义词: clash, conflict, encounter, showdown |反义词: agreement, compromise, harmony, peace |词性:名词

confront: /kənˈfrʌnt/ | v. | 面对，对抗 | To face up to and deal with (a problem or difficult situation). To meet (someone) face to face, especially in hostility or defiance. |  She decided to confront her fears. 她决定面对她的恐惧。| He was confronted by an angry mob.  他面对着一群愤怒的暴徒。|近义词: face, challenge, oppose, encounter |反义词: avoid, evade, ignore, retreat |词性:动词
