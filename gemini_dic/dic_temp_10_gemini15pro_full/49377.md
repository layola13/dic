delirium:/dɪˈlɪriəm/ /dəˈlɪriəm/ | n. | 谵妄，神志不清 | A state of mental confusion, often accompanied by hallucinations and incoherent speech. | The high fever brought on a state of delirium. 高烧引起谵妄状态。

delirium:/dɪˈlɪriəm/ /dəˈlɪriəm/ | n. | 狂喜，极度兴奋 | A state of wild excitement or ecstasy. | They were in a delirium of joy after winning the championship. 赢得冠军后，他们欣喜若狂。

delirium tremens:/dɪˈlɪriəm ˈtriːmɛnz/ | n. | 震颤谵妄 | A severe form of alcohol withdrawal marked by delirium, hallucinations, and tremors. | He experienced delirium tremens after he stopped drinking abruptly. 他突然戒酒后经历了震颤谵妄。

Etymology: From Latin dēlīrium "going out of the furrow (of reason)," from dē- "off, away" + līra "furrow, ridge between furrows."
