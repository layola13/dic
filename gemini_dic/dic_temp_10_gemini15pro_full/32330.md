child-proofed: /ˈtʃaɪldˌpruːft/ | adj. | 防儿童开启的，对儿童安全的 | Designed to prevent children from opening or using something potentially dangerous. |  The medicine bottle is child-proofed.  药瓶是防儿童开启的。|  The kitchen cabinets are child-proofed to protect our toddler. 厨房橱柜安装了儿童安全锁以保护我们的小孩。 | child-resistant, tamper-proof | accessible | adjective

child-proof: /ˈtʃaɪldˌpruːf/ | v. |  使…防儿童开启，使…对儿童安全 | To make something safe for children by preventing them from opening or using it. | We need to child-proof the house before the baby starts crawling. 我们需要在婴儿开始爬行之前做好房屋的儿童安全措施。| They child-proofed the electrical outlets with plastic covers. 他们用塑料盖子保护电源插座，防止儿童触电。 | secure, protect | endanger | verb


Etymology:
child (Old English cild) + proof (from Anglo-French preove, from Latin probare "to test, approve") + -ed (past participle suffix)
