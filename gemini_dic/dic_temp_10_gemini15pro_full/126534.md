nabobishly:/ˈneɪbɒbɪʃli/
adverb|以巨富姿态地，奢华地|In a manner characteristic of a nabob; luxuriously or ostentatiously.|He entertained nabobishly, throwing lavish parties and showering his guests with expensive gifts. 他以巨富的姿态招待客人，举办奢华的派对，并向客人赠送昂贵的礼物。|luxuriously, ostentatiously, lavishly, extravagantly|frugally, simply, modestly|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From nabob + -ishly.  Nabob comes from Hindi "nawāb," from Arabic "nuwwāb," plural of "nā'ib" (viceroy, deputy).  The suffix "-ishly" forms adverbs from adjectives, indicating manner or resemblance.
