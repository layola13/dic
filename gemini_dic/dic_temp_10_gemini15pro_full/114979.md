mancipation:/mænˈsɪˈpeɪʃən/ |n.|解放，解脱|The act of freeing someone from slavery or other forms of control.|The mancipation of the slaves was a turning point in American history. 美国奴隶的解放是美国历史上的一个转折点。| 近义词：liberation, freedom, release|反义词： enslavement, captivity, oppression|词性：名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *mancipatio*, from Latin *mancipare* "to transfer ownership," from *manus* "hand" + *capere* "to take." Originally referred to the formal transfer of ownership in Roman law, later broadened to mean liberation from slavery or any kind of control.
