gothic: /ˈɡɒθɪk/ (British) /ˈɡɑːθɪk/ (American)| n.|哥特式建筑；哥特式字体；哥特小说 |A style of architecture prevalent in western Europe from the 12th to the 16th centuries; a style of literature characterized by a gloomy atmosphere and grotesque or fantastic incidents; a plain, angular typeface without serifs. |She enjoys reading Gothic novels. 她喜欢读哥特小说。|近义词： (for architecture) medieval architecture, (for literature) horror fiction |反义词： (for architecture) modern architecture, (for literature) realistic fiction |词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *gothique*, from Italian *gotico*, ultimately from *Goth* (referring to the East Germanic tribe), with the sense developing from association with the destruction of classical Roman architecture and perceived barbarity of the Goths.
