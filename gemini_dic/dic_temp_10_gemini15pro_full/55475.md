distract:/dɪˈstrækt/| vt.|转移(注意力); 使分心|to draw away the attention of; divert|The loud noise distracted me from my work. 喧闹的声音使我无法专心工作。|synonyms: divert, sidetrack, disturb|antonyms: focus, concentrate|verb

distracted:/dɪˈstræktɪd/| adj.|心烦意乱的; 注意力分散的|having one's attention diverted|He seemed distracted and didn't hear what I said. 他似乎心不在焉，没听到我说的话。|synonyms: preoccupied, inattentive, absent-minded|antonyms: focused, attentive, concentrated|adjective

distracting:/dɪˈstræktɪŋ/| adj.|分散注意力的; 令人分心的|tending to distract; disturbing|The constant notifications on my phone are very distracting. 我手机上不断弹出的通知非常令人分心。|synonyms: disturbing, diverting, intrusive|antonyms: focusing, calming, soothing|adjective

distraction:/dɪˈstrækʃn/| n.|分心的事; 使人分心的事物|something that distracts; a diversion|The TV was a constant distraction while I was trying to study. 我想学习的时候，电视一直让我分心。|synonyms: diversion, disturbance, interruption|antonyms: focus, concentration, attention|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *distractus, past participle of distrahere (“to pull apart, divide, distract”), from dis- (“apart”) + trahere (“to draw, pull”).
