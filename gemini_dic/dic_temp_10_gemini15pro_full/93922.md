immunised:/ˈɪmjʊnaɪzd/ | adj. |免疫的，已接种疫苗的 |Made resistant to infection, typically by inoculation. | Example: The child was immunised against measles.  这个孩子接种了麻疹疫苗。| Example:  They were immunised against polio. 他们接种了脊髓灰质炎疫苗。| Synonyms: vaccinated, inoculated, protected | Antonyms: vulnerable, susceptible, unprotected | Word Type: Adjective

immunize:/ˈɪmjʊnaɪz/ | v. |使免疫，接种疫苗 | To make someone immune to infection, typically by inoculation. | Example:  The doctor immunized the children against diphtheria. 医生为孩子们接种了白喉疫苗。 | Example:  They immunize all the cattle against anthrax. 他们给所有牛接种炭疽疫苗。 | Synonyms: vaccinate, inoculate, protect | Antonyms: expose, endanger | Word Type: Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *immunis* "exempt, free, not paying a share," from *in-* "not" (see in- (1)) + *munis* "performing services, obligated," related to *moenia* "walls, fortifications" (see moenia). Originally in reference to exemption from public service or charges. The medical sense of "exempt from disease" is first recorded 1750.
