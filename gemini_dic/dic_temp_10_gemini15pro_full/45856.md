cut-it-out:/ˌkʌt ɪt ˈaʊt/ | phrasal verb | 停止；住手；别闹了 | Used to tell someone to stop doing something annoying or childish.  It's an informal expression. It can also express disbelief. | "Cut it out! You're not really going to jump off the roof, are you?"  别闹了！你不会真的要从屋顶跳下去吧？| 近义词: stop it, knock it off, quit it | 反义词: continue, keep going | 词性: 短语动词

词源:  "Cut it out" originated in the late 19th century. "Cut" has long been used figuratively to mean "stop" or "discontinue," and "it" refers to the annoying behavior. "Out" emphasizes the complete cessation of the action.
