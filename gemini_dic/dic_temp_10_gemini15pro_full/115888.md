marmalade:/ˈmɑːrməleɪd/
n.
橘子酱，果酱
A preserve made from citrus fruit, especially bitter oranges, prepared by boiling the pulp and rind with sugar.
She spread marmalade on her toast. 她把橘子酱涂在吐司上。
近义词：jam, preserve, conserve
反义词:(None -  No direct opposite for a food item)
词性:noun

marmalade:/ˈmɑːməleɪd/
n.
(比喻)混乱，困境
(Figurative) A messy or confused situation.
The traffic was a complete marmalade. 交通一片混乱。
近义词：mess, muddle, confusion
反义词：order, organization
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Portuguese *marmelada "quince jam," from marmelo "quince," from Latin melimelum (apple grafted on a quince), from Greek melimēlon, from meli "honey" + mēlon "apple."  The shift in meaning from quince to citrus probably occurred in the 17th century in England.
