off-licence: /ˈɒf ˌlaɪsns/ (UK), /ˈɔːf ˌlaɪsns/ (US)| n. | 酒类专卖店；持有销售（酒类）许可证|A shop licensed to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption off the premises.| I bought a bottle of wine from the off-licence. 我从酒类专卖店买了一瓶葡萄酒。|Synonym: liquor store, bottle shop (Australian, New Zealand)|Antonym: pub, bar|Noun

off-license: /ˈɒf ˌlaɪsns/ (UK), /ˈɔːf ˌlaɪsns/ (US)| n. |（在指定场所外销售酒类的）许可证|A licence to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption off the premises.| The shop applied for an off-license. 这家商店申请了酒类销售许可证。|Synonym: liquor licence|Antonym: on-licence|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "off" (meaning away from) + "licence" (permission to do something).  The term refers to the licence to sell alcohol for consumption *off* the premises, as opposed to an *on*-licence which permits consumption on the premises.
