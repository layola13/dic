nervous: /ˈnɜːrvəs/ (US) /ˈnɜːvəs/ (UK)| adj.|紧张的，不安的|Easily agitated or apprehensive; worried or uneasy.|例句：I'm nervous about my exam. 我对我的考试感到紧张。|例句：He was nervous about speaking in public. 他对在公众场合讲话感到紧张。|近义词：anxious, apprehensive, uneasy, tense|反义词：calm, relaxed, composed|词性:形容词

nervously: /ˈnɜːrvəsli/ (US) /ˈnɜːvəsli/ (UK)| adv.|紧张地，不安地|In a nervous manner; with anxiety or apprehension.|例句：She nervously tapped her foot. 她紧张地敲着脚。|例句：He nervously cleared his throat before speaking.  他说话前紧张地清了清嗓子。|近义词：anxiously, apprehensively, uneasily|反义词：calmly, relaxedly, composedly|词性:副词

nervousness: /ˈnɜːrvəsnəs/ (US) /ˈnɜːvəsnəs/ (UK)| n.|紧张，不安|The state of being nervous.|例句：Her nervousness was apparent to everyone. 她的紧张显而易见。|例句：The nervousness in the room was palpable. 房间里的紧张气氛是显而易见的。|近义词：anxiety, apprehension, unease, tension|反义词：calmness, relaxation, composure|词性:名词

词源: From Latin *nervosus* "sinewy, vigorous," from nervus "sinew, tendon, nerve." The sense evolution in Latin was from "strong" to "excitable" to "sensitive" to "timid."
