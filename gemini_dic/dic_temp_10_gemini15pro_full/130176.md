non-rival:/ˌnɒnˈraɪvl/ | adj. | 非竞争性的，非对抗性的 |  A good is non-rivalrous if one person's consumption of it does not diminish another person's consumption of it.  This means that the good can be used by multiple people simultaneously without reducing its availability or benefit to others. |  Street lighting is a non-rival good – its use by one person doesn't diminish its availability for others. 街灯是非竞争性物品——一个人使用它并不会减少其他人对它的可用性。

 Public parks are often considered non-rivalrous, unless overcrowding becomes an issue.  公共公园通常被认为是非竞争性的，除非出现过度拥挤的问题。 | 近义词：non-excludable, public | 反义词：rivalrous, private | 词性：形容词

Etymology: "non-" (Latin prefix meaning "not") + "rival" (from Old French "rival," meaning "one who uses the same stream," from Latin "rivalis," meaning "of a brook," hence "competitor")
