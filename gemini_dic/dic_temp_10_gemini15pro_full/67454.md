extispicious: /ɛkˈstɪspɪʃəs/
adjective
吉兆的，预示成功的
Indicating good fortune; auspicious.  Derived from Latin "extispicium," the practice of divination by examining the entrails of sacrificed animals.
The Roman general considered the extispicious signs a good omen for the battle. 罗马将军认为这些吉兆预示着这场战斗的胜利。
