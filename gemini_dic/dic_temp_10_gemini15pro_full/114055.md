magnetism:/ˈmæɡnɪˌtɪzəm/| n. |磁性，磁力，吸引力|The property of attracting certain metals, or the ability to create this property.  The force exerted by magnets when they attract or repel each other.  The power to attract or charm.|例句：The magnetism of the Earth protects us from harmful radiation. 地球的磁性保护我们免受有害辐射。

例句：He has a natural magnetism that draws people to him. 他有一种天生的吸引力，让人们被他吸引。|近义词：attraction, pull, allure, charisma|反义词：repulsion|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *magnetismus*, from Latin magnes (“magnet, lodestone”) (from Ancient Greek μάγνης (mágnēs, “lodestone, magnet”), perhaps from Magnesia (Μαγνησία), a region of Thessaly in Greece where lodestone was found) +‎ -ismus (from Ancient Greek -ισμός (-ismós)).
