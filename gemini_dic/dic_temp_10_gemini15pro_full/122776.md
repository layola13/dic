mol:/mɒl/ (UK) /moʊl/ (US)| n. |摩尔，物质的量|A unit of amount of substance in the International System of Units (SI), defined as the amount of a substance that contains as many elementary entities (e.g., atoms, molecules, ions, electrons) as there are atoms in 12 grams of pure carbon-12.|One mole of carbon-12 atoms has a mass of exactly 12 grams. 12克纯碳12原子正好是一摩尔。|synonyms: gram-molecule|antonyms: N/A|noun
