coddler:/ˈkɒdlə(r)/ (US: /ˈkɑːdlər/)| n. |娇惯者，溺爱者；煮蛋器|A person who treats someone with excessive indulgence; a kitchen utensil used for cooking eggs.|She's such a coddler with her children, they're never told no. 她太溺爱她的孩子们了，他们从来没被拒绝过。|He used a coddler to prepare soft-boiled eggs for breakfast. 他用煮蛋器准备早餐的溏心蛋。|Synonyms: pamperer, spoiler, overindulger|Antonyms: disciplinarian, strict parent|noun


coddle:/ˈkɒd(ə)l/ (US: /ˈkɑːdl/)| vt. |溺爱，娇惯；细心照料|To treat someone with excessive indulgence and tenderness; to care for solicitously.|She coddles her dog like a baby. 她像对待婴儿一样溺爱她的狗。|The gardener coddled the delicate seedlings. 园丁细心照料着娇嫩的幼苗。|Synonyms: pamper, spoil, indulge, baby|Antonyms: neglect, ignore, discipline|verb
