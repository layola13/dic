bikari: /bɪˈkɑːri/ | n. | 托钵僧；乞丐 | A mendicant, especially a religious mendicant. |  He lived as a bikari, wandering from village to village. 他过着托钵僧的生活，从一个村庄流浪到另一个村庄。| Synonym: beggar, mendicant, pauper | Antonym: rich man, philanthropist | noun

Etymology: From Hindi बिकारी (bikārī), from Sanskrit विकारिन् (vikārin), meaning "changed, altered, transformed."  This likely refers to the transformed state of one who renounces worldly possessions.
