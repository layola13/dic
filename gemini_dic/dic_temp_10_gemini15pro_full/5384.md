ally:/ˈælaɪ/ /əˈlaɪ/| n. |同盟;盟友;支持者|A person, group, or nation that is associated with another for mutual benefit or a common objective.|例句：The US is a close ally of the UK. 美国是英国的亲密盟友。|例句：He found an ally in the fight against corruption. 他在反腐败斗争中找到了一个盟友。|近义词：friend, partner, supporter, confederate|反义词：enemy, opponent, foe|词性:名词

ally:/əˈlaɪ/| v. |结盟;联合|To combine or unite a resource or commodity with (another) for mutual benefit.|例句：Several smaller parties allied themselves with the socialists to form a coalition government.  几个小党与社会党联合组成了联合政府。|例句：He allied himself with the reformers. 他与改革派结盟。|近义词：join forces, unite, combine, collaborate|反义词：separate, divide, oppose|词性:动词
