corpuscle:/ˈkɔːrpəsəl/ (US) /ˈkɔːpʌsəl/ (UK)| n. |微粒；小体；血球|A minute particle of matter.  A cell, especially a red or white blood cell.|Red blood corpuscles carry oxygen throughout the body. 红血球将氧气输送到全身。|Synonyms: particle, speck, granule, molecule, atom|Antonyms: aggregate, mass|noun

corpuscle:/ˈkɔːrpəsəl/ (US) /ˈkɔːpʌsəl/ (UK)| n. |语素|A basic meaningful unit in a language. (Less common usage)|The linguist analyzed the corpuscles of the ancient language. 语言学家分析了古代语言的语素。|Synonyms: morpheme, element, unit|Antonyms: N/A|noun
