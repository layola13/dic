flute:/fluːt/| n.|长笛；笛子；凹槽|A musical instrument of the woodwind family, typically a slender tube closed at one end, across a hole in which the breath is blown, and having a row of finger-holes or keys.|She plays the flute beautifully. 她长笛吹得很好。|Synonyms: recorder, pipe, fife|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a musical instrument.  One might consider "silence" in a metaphorical sense.)|noun

flute:/fluːt/| v.|吹奏长笛；用长笛声表达；使成槽形|To play the flute; to make a sound like a flute; to make grooves or flutes in something.|The bird fluted its melodic song. 这只鸟用笛子般的声音歌唱。|Synonyms: pipe, whistle, trill|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for this verb form. One might consider "mute" in a metaphorical sense.)|verb
