bermed:/bɜːmd/ | adj. | 有堤防的，有护堤的 | Furnished with a berm; embanked. | The bermed road provided extra safety for drivers. 有护堤的道路为驾驶员提供了额外的安全保障。 | The newly constructed highway is bermed to prevent soil erosion. 新建的公路筑有护堤，以防止水土流失。 |  近义词: embanked, diked | 反义词: unbermed, unprotected | adjective

berm:/bɜːrm/ | n. |  (道路、铁路、运河等边缘的)斜坡，护堤，堤防；(尤指战壕边缘的)矮墙，土堤 | A raised bank or path, especially at the edge of a road, canal, or moat; a ledge or shoulder, as along the top of a parapet. |  The berm along the highway helps to prevent accidents. 公路边的护堤有助于防止事故发生。 |  Soldiers built a berm to protect themselves from enemy fire. 士兵们筑起土堤来保护自己免受敌人的炮火。| 近义词: bank, embankment, dike, levee, rampart | 反义词: ditch, trench | noun
