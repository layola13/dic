caballero:/ˌkɑːbəˈjɛroʊ/ /ˌkabəˈʎeɾo/| n.|绅士，骑士，先生|A Spanish word meaning gentleman, knight, or sir.  Used as a polite term of address for a man.|例句：Buenos días, caballero.  Good morning, sir.|近义词：gentleman, sir, lord|反义词:villain, knave|noun

caballero:/ˌkɑːbəˈjɛroʊ/ /ˌkabəˈʎeɾo/| n.|骑士，武士|A mounted soldier serving a king or other feudal superior.|例句：The caballero rode his valiant steed into battle.  骑士骑着他的勇猛的战马投入战斗。|近义词：knight, warrior|反义词:peasant, commoner|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Spanish *cavallero, from Late Latin caballārius (“horseman”), from Latin caballus (“horse”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱabʰállos (“pale, dappled”).
