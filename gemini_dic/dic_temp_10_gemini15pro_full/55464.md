distort:/dɪˈstɔːrt/ (US) /dɪˈstɔːt/ (UK)| v. | 扭曲，歪曲 | to twist out of shape; to give a false or misleading account of; to change the form of something, especially in a way that makes it inaccurate | The loud music distorted the sound of her voice. 喧闹的音乐使她的声音失真了。 | synonyms: misrepresent, twist, warp, falsify, contort | antonyms: clarify, represent truthfully, purify, refine | verb

distortion:/dɪˈstɔːrʃn/ (US) /dɪˈstɔːʃn/ (UK)| n. | 扭曲，变形，失真 | the act of distorting something or the state of being distorted | The newspaper's distortion of the facts angered many readers.  报纸歪曲事实激怒了许多读者。 | synonyms: misrepresentation, perversion, falsification, contortion | antonyms: accuracy, truth, clarity, precision | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *distorquere*  (to twist different ways), from *dis-* (apart, in different directions) + *torquere* (to twist).
