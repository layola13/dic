distress:/dɪˈstrɛs/| n. vt.| 痛苦，忧虑，窘迫，危难，使痛苦，使忧虑 |Extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain. Trouble; misfortune.|例句：He expressed his distress at the situation. 他对这种情况表示 distress。|近义词：anxiety, anguish, sorrow, grief, pain, suffering, agony, torment, misery, hardship, trouble, difficulty, adversity, crisis, emergency|反义词：comfort, happiness, joy, peace, relief, tranquility, well-being|词性:名词,动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French destresse (“hardship, oppression, grief”), from Vulgar Latin *districtia, from Latin districtus (“pressed together, compressed, narrow, difficult”), past participle of distringō (“I draw asunder, pull different ways, distract, perplex”), from dis- (“apart, asunder”) + stringō (“I draw tight, bind”).
