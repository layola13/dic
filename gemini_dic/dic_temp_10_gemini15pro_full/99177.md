intercircles:/ɪntərˈsɜːrkəlz/ | n. | 内切圆群，互切圆|A set of circles that are tangent to each other.  Specifically, each circle in the set is tangent to every other circle in the set.|The diagram showed a complex pattern of intercircles within a larger circle.| 近义词: tangent circles, mutually tangent circles | 反义词: N/A | Noun

Etymology:
"inter-" (Latin prefix meaning "between, among") + "circle" (from Old French "cercle," ultimately from Latin "circulus," diminutive of "circus" meaning "ring, circular course").
