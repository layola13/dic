disenchanting:/ˌdɪsɪnˈtʃæntɪŋ/ |adj.| 令人失望的；使人清醒的|Causing someone to stop believing in something or someone that they previously believed in or admired; disillusioning.|The trip was a disenchanting experience.  这次旅行令人失望。|Synonyms: disillusioning, disappointing, disheartening, discouraging|Antonyms: enchanting, captivating, charming|adjective

disenchant:/ˌdɪsɪnˈtʃænt/ |v.| 使不再抱幻想；使清醒|To free from illusion or false belief; disillusion.|The harsh realities of life soon disenchanted her.  生活的残酷现实很快让她清醒了。|Synonyms: disillusion, undeceive|Antonyms: enchant, charm|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desenchanter, from des- (expressing negation) + enchanter (“to enchant”), from Latin incantare (“to chant, to cast a spell”), from in- + cantare (“to sing”).
