augustuss: /ɔːˈɡʌstəs/ | n. | 奥古斯都 |  A male given name, derived from the Roman emperor Augustus Caesar. It means "venerable" or "magnificent". | Augustus was a wise ruler. 奥古斯都是一位英明的统治者。 |  Caesar, emperor | - | proper noun
Etymology: From Latin Augustus,  originally a title conferred on Octavian, the first Roman emperor, meaning "consecrated, venerable".  Ultimately from Latin augere "to increase".
