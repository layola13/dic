grandstanding:/ˈɡrændˌstændɪŋ/
n.|哗众取宠；炫耀；作秀|The act of behaving in a showy or ostentatious way in order to attract attention or applause. |He was accused of political grandstanding. 他被指责玩弄政治作秀。|近义词：showboating, showing off, attention-seeking|反义词：modesty, humility|词性:名词

grandstand:/ˈɡrændˌstænd/
n.|大看台|A large covered stand for spectators, typically at a racecourse or stadium. |The crowd cheered from the grandstand.  人群从大看台上欢呼。|近义词：stand, bleachers, terrace|反义词：field, track|词性:名词

grandstand:/ˈɡrændˌstænd/
v.|哗众取宠；作秀|To behave in a showy or ostentatious way in order to attract attention or applause. |The senator was grandstanding for the cameras.  这位参议员对着摄像机哗众取宠。|近义词：show off, showboat, play to the gallery|反义词：be humble, be modest|词性:动词
