couloir:/ˈkuːlwɑːr/ (US), /kʊˈlwɑː(r)/ (UK)| n. |狭窄陡峭的雪道；（尤指政治）走廊，秘密通道|A steep, narrow gully on a mountainside, often filled with snow or ice. Also, a passage or corridor, especially in political contexts, suggesting private or backroom dealings.|The skiers carefully navigated the treacherous couloir. 滑雪者小心翼翼地穿过危险的狭窄雪道。| The deal was brokered in the political couloirs of Washington. 这笔交易是在华盛顿的政治秘密通道中促成的。|Synonyms: gully, chute, channel, corridor, passage|Antonyms: plain, flat, open space|noun
