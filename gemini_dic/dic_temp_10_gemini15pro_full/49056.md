dehorter:/deɔʁte/| v. |驱逐出境，驱逐，劝退|To remove someone from a place, often forcefully or by persuasion, especially from a country.  Originally referring to removing parasites from the body.|The government dehorter the illegal immigrants. 政府驱逐了非法移民。|近义词：expel, deport, evict, dissuade|反义词:admit, welcome|词性:verb
词源: From French *déhorter*, from *de-* (out of) + *horter* (to urge, exhort). Ultimately from Latin *hortārī* (to encourage, urge).  The original meaning was to expel parasites from the body.
