chancels:/ˈtʃænsəlz/
n.
圣坛围栏，圣所
The part of a church near the altar, reserved for the clergy and choir, and typically separated from the nave by steps or a screen.
The choir sang beautifully from the chancels.  唱诗班在圣坛围栏里优美地歌唱。
近义词:sanctuary, altar area
反义词:nave
词性:名词

n.
圣坛围栏，圣所
The part of a church near the altar, reserved for the clergy and choir, and typically separated from the nave by steps or a screen.
The priest stood in the chancel during the service.  牧师在仪式中站在圣坛围栏里。

反义词:nave
词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *cancel, from Late Latin cancellus "lattice," diminutive of cancer "lattice," from Proto-Italic *karkros, from Proto-Indo-European *korgʰros.
