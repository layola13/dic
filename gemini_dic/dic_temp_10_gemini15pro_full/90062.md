hostage:/ˈhɒstɪdʒ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːstɪdʒ/ (US)| n.|人质|A person held by someone who demands that certain things be done before the person is freed.|The terrorists were holding several hostages.|近义词：captive, prisoner|反义词: captor, kidnapper|词性: noun

Etymology: From Old French *ostage, from Medieval Latin *ostaticum, from Latin obsidaticum, from obses, obsidis “hostage,” from obsidere “to besiege, blockade,” from ob- “against” + sedere “to sit.”
