implant:/ɪmˈplɑːnt/ (US), /ɪmˈplɑːnt/ (UK)| n. vt.|植入物，植入|A thing implanted in something else, especially a piece of tissue, prosthetic device, or other object inserted surgically into the body.  |The surgeon implanted a pacemaker in the patient's chest.  外科医生在病人的胸腔植入了一台起搏器。|近义词：insert, embed, graft|反义词:remove, extract|noun

implant:/ɪmˈplɑːnt/ (US), /ɪmˈplɑːnt/ (UK)| vt.|植入，灌输|Insert or fix (tissue or an artificial object) in a person's body, especially by surgery.  |Dentists can implant artificial teeth. 牙医可以植入人造牙齿。|近义词：insert, embed, instill|反义词:remove, extract|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin implantare, from in- ‘in’ + plantare ‘to plant’.
