demagogged:/ˈdɛməɡɒɡd/ |v. |煽动，蛊惑 (past participle)|To have been subjected to the methods of a demagogue; to have been manipulated by inflammatory appeals to emotion and prejudice.|The populace was demagogged into supporting the war.|近义词:manipulated, exploited, inflamed|反义词:reasoned, educated, enlightened|词性:动词(过去分词)

ETYMOLOGY->
From demagogue + -ed.  Demagogue comes from the Greek dēmagōgos, meaning "a popular leader," from dēmos "people" + agōgos "leading."
