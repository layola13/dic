drooped:/druːpt/ | v. | 下垂，低垂，萎蔫 | To bend or hang downward; to become limp or weak. | The flowers drooped in the heat. 花朵在高温下低垂。|  The tired dog's head drooped.  疲惫的狗的头低垂着。| synonyms: sagged, wilted, slumped | antonyms: perked up, revived, straightened | verb

drooping:/ˈdruːpɪŋ/ | adj. | 下垂的，低垂的，萎蔫的 | Hanging or bending downward; limp or weak. | The drooping branches of the willow tree swept the ground. 垂柳低垂的枝条扫过地面。 |  She had drooping eyelids from lack of sleep. 她因为缺乏睡眠而眼皮下垂。| synonyms: sagging, wilting, slumped | antonyms: erect, upright, perked up | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *drupa, "to droop," related to Old English *dreopan, "to drip."  The connection likely stems from the image of something dripping or hanging downward like a drop of water.
