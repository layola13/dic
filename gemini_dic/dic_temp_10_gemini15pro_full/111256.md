litigative: /ˈlɪtɪɡeɪtɪv/| adj.|诉讼的，好诉讼的|inclined to engage in lawsuits; readily resorting to litigation.|例句：The company has a reputation for being litigative.  这家公司以好诉讼而闻名。

例句：She has a litigative personality. 她性格好诉讼。|近义词：contentious, quarrelsome, disputatious|反义词：conciliatory, compromising, amicable|词性: adjective

词源: From Latin *litigātīvus*, from litigātus, past participle of litigāre (“to go to law, dispute judicially”), from lis (“lawsuit”) + agere (“to do, drive, act”).
