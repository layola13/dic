blue-chip:/ˈbluː tʃɪp/| adj. n.|蓝筹股；一流的，稳健的|A blue-chip stock is the stock of a large, well-established and financially sound company that has operated for many years.  A blue-chip company is known to weather downturns and operate profitably in most economic climates.  The term is also more generally used to describe something considered to be of high quality and reliable. |Example:  Investing in blue-chip stocks is generally considered a safe bet. 投资蓝筹股通常被认为是安全的赌注。 They hired a blue-chip lawyer to handle the case. 他们聘请了一位一流的律师来处理这个案件。|近义词：top-rated, first-class, reliable, established, prestigious|反义词:speculative, risky, volatile|词性:形容词, 名词


Etymology: From the blue chips used in poker, which are traditionally the highest-valued chips. 源于扑克游戏中使用的蓝色筹码，传统上是价值最高的筹码。
