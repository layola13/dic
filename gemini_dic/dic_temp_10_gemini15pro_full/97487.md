ingenuous:/ɪnˈdʒɛnjuəs/ | adj. | 天真的，坦率的，淳朴的 | Showing innocence or childlike simplicity; frank and straightforward; artless. | She had an ingenuous trust in everyone. 她对每个人都有一种天真的信任。 |  The politician's ingenuous explanation of the scandal fooled no one.  这位政客对丑闻的坦率解释，谁也没信。 | 近义词: naive, innocent, artless, guileless, candid, frank | 反义词: cunning, deceitful, sophisticated, worldly, artful | 词性: adjective

词源: Late Middle English: from Latin ingenuus ‘native, freeborn, noble, frank’, from in- ‘in’ + gignere ‘beget’. The original sense was ‘noble, honorable’, later ‘frank, open’, whence ‘naive, artless’.
