felsen:/ˈfɛlzən/| n.|岩石，峭壁|A large mass of rock, typically one that projects up or out of the ground.|The climbers scaled the sheer felsen.|rocks, cliffs, crags|plains, valleys|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From German Felsen, from Old High German felisa, from Proto-Germanic *felisaz, from Proto-Indo-European *pl̥th₂ós (“flat, broad”). Cognate with Old English feld (“field”).  The semantic shift from "flat" to "cliff" likely occurred through an intermediate meaning of "flat rock surface".
