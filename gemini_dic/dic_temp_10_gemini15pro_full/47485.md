dean:/diːn/| n.|院长，系主任，教务长|A dean is a senior official in a college or university who is responsible for a specific academic area or group of students.  The dean of students handles student affairs.|The dean of the medical school addressed the graduating class.|Synonyms: principal, head, director|Antonyms: student, subordinate|noun

dean:/diːn/| n.|（尤指英国国教主教座堂的）主任牧师|In the Church of England, a dean is a senior clergyman, especially one in charge of a cathedral.|The dean presided over the service.|Synonyms: canon, prebendary, vicar|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps parishioner or curate)|noun
