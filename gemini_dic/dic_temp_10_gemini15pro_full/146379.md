overfloridness:/ˌoʊvərˈflɔːrɪdnɪs/
|n.| 过分华丽; 过分修饰; 辞藻过于华丽|The overfloridness of his prose made it difficult to read.  他文章的辞藻过于华丽，让人读起来很费劲。|
|English Explanation:  The state or quality of being excessively ornate or flowery in style, especially of language. It implies an overabundance of elaborate figures of speech, adjectives, and other rhetorical devices, often to the point of being excessive and detracting from the clarity and impact of the writing or speech.|
|例句：The overfloridness of the wedding decorations was overwhelming. 婚礼装饰过于华丽，让人感到有些眼花缭乱。|
|例句：His overfloridness of speech made him sound pretentious. 他讲话辞藻过于华丽，让他听起来很做作。|
|近义词：ornateness, elaborateness, flamboyance, floridity|
|反义词：simplicity, plainness, conciseness, austerity|
|词性: noun|
