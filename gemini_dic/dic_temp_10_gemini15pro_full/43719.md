credulity:/krɪˈdʒuːləti/| n. |轻信，易受骗|a tendency to be too ready to believe that something is true|例句：His credulity made him an easy target for con men. 他的轻信使他很容易成为骗子的目标。

例句：The advertisement played on people's credulity.  这则广告利用了人们的轻信。|近义词：gullibility, naivety, innocence|反义词:skepticism, cynicism, disbelief|词性:noun

credulous:/ˈkrɛdʒələs/| adj. |轻信的，易受骗的|having or showing too great a readiness to believe things|例句：He was credulous enough to believe her. 他很容易就相信了她。

例句：A credulous person is easily fooled. 轻信的人容易被愚弄。|近义词：gullible, naive, trusting|反义词:skeptical, cynical, unbelieving|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *crēdulus* (believing), from *crēdere* (to believe).
