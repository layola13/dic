abides:/əˈbaɪdz/| v.| 忍受，容忍；遵守；居留，逗留 |To tolerate or put up with something or someone. To conform to or follow rules, a decision, etc. To remain or continue in a particular condition or place. |She abides by the rules of the game. 她遵守游戏规则。

He abides in the countryside. 他居住在乡下。|近义词：tolerate, endure, obey, reside, dwell, stay |反义词：disobey, defy, leave |词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *abiden*, from Old English *ābīdan* (“to await, remain, continue, endure, experience”), from *ā-* (from Proto-Germanic *aiw-* (“ever, always”)) + *bīdan* (“to bide, wait, remain”), from Proto-Germanic *bidaną* (“to wait, abide”). Cognate with Old Frisian *abida* (“to wait, expect, experience”), Old Saxon *abidan* (“to remain, persevere”), Old High German *irbitan* (“to remain”), German *erbeiden* (“to await, abide”), Gothic *������������������������* (beidan, “to expect, wait”). Related to bide.
