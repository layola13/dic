liquid:/ˈlɪkwɪd/| adj. n.|液体的；液态的；流体的；清澈的；流畅的；现金；流音|A substance that flows freely but is of constant volume, having a consistency like that of water or oil.|例句：Water is a liquid. 水是液体。

liquid:/ˈlɪkwɪd/| adj. n.|液体的；液态的；流体的；清澈的；流畅的；现金；流音|Assets held in cash or easily converted into cash.|例句：The company has $5 million in liquid assets. 这家公司拥有500万美元的流动资产。

liquid:/ˈlɪkwɪd/| adj. n.|液体的；液态的；流体的；清澈的；流畅的；现金；流音|(of a consonant) pronounced with the tongue in such a position as to allow the free passage of breath.|例句：The letter 'l' is a liquid. 字母“l”是一个流音。

liquid:/ˈlɪkwɪd/| adj. n.|液体的；液态的；流体的；清澈的；流畅的；现金；流音|(of eyes, a gaze, etc.) soft and gentle; melting.|例句：She gazed at him with liquid eyes. 她用温柔的眼神凝视着他。



近义词：fluid, watery, molten, runny, smooth, flowing|反义词:solid, gaseous, frozen, thick|词性:adjective, noun


词源: From Middle French *liquide*, from Latin *liquidus* (“liquid, flowing, clear”), from *liquēre*, *liquō* (“to be fluid, melt, dissolve”).
