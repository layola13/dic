antisegregation:/ˌæntiˌseɡrɪˈɡeɪʃən/ | adj. | 反种族隔离的 | Opposing or designed to prevent segregation, especially racial segregation. |  The antisegregation movement gained momentum in the 1960s.  反种族隔离运动在20世纪60年代获得了动力。| synonyms: integrationist, desegregationist | antonyms: segregationist | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to. From Greek anti-.
segregation (noun): the action or state of setting someone or something apart from other people or things or being set apart. From Latin segregatus, past participle of segregare 'to set apart'.
