fasciculus:/fəˈsɪkjʊləs/ (US), /fəˈsɪkjʊləs/ (UK)| n.|小束，分册，丛|A small bundle, cluster, or collection of things, often referring to nerve or muscle fibers or a part of a book published in installments.|The doctor identified the fasciculus of nerves responsible for the patient's loss of sensation.|Synonym: bundle, cluster, tract, fascicle|Antonym: dispersion, scattering|noun

fasciculus:/fəˈsɪkjʊləs/ (US), /fəˈsɪkjʊləs/ (UK)| n.|分册，合订本的一部分|A separately published installment of a book, often later combined with others to form a complete volume.|The fasciculus contained the first two chapters of the novel.|Synonym: installment, part, section|Antonym: whole, complete|noun
