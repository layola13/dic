dismantle:/dɪsˈmæntl/ /dɪsˈmɑːntl/ | vt. | 拆除，拆卸 | To take apart a machine or structure so that it is in separate pieces. | The mechanic dismantled the engine to find the problem. 机械师拆卸了发动机以找出问题所在。|  The old bridge was dismantled piece by piece.  这座旧桥被一块一块地拆除了。 | take apart, disassemble, break down | assemble, construct | verb

dismantled: /dɪsˈmæntld/ /dɪsˈmɑːntld/ | adj. | 被拆除的，被拆卸的 |  Taken apart; disassembled. | The dismantled clock lay in pieces on the table. 被拆卸的钟表零件散落在桌子上。 | The workers left the dismantled scaffolding on the ground. 工人们把拆卸下来的脚手架留在地上。 | disassembled, taken apart, broken down | assembled, constructed | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desmanteler*, from des- (prefix indicating reversal) + mantel (cloak, mantle).  The original meaning was to strip of a cloak or mantle, figuratively to deprive of authority or dignity. The meaning shifted to taking something apart, like stripping a building of its covering.
