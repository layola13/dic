antiliberalisms:/ˌæntaɪˈlɪbərəlɪzəmz/
|n.|反自由主义|Doctrines or practices opposed to liberal principles, encompassing various forms of authoritarianism, conservatism, or radicalism that reject individual rights, free markets, or democratic governance.|The rise of antiliberalisms in the 20th century led to various totalitarian regimes.|近义词:authoritarianism, illiberalism, conservatism, totalitarianism|反义词:liberalism, progressivism|词性:名词

Etymology:
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to (from Greek anti-)
liberal (adjective/noun): favoring individual liberty, free trade, etc. (from Latin liberalis)
-ism (suffix): denoting a doctrine, belief, or practice
