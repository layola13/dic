edulcorative:/ɪˈdʌlkəˌreɪtɪv/ | adj. | 使甜的；使悦耳的；矫饰的；奉承的 | Making something more pleasant or agreeable, often excessively so.  Implies artificial sweetening or refinement, often to disguise something unpleasant. | The politician delivered an edulcorative speech, full of empty promises and flattery.  这位政客发表了一通甜言蜜语的演讲，充满了空洞的承诺和奉承。|  His edulcorative version of the story omitted all the unpleasant details. 他那经过润色的故事版本省略了所有令人不快的细节。 | 近义词:sweetening, flattering, palliative, sugary, saccharine | 反义词:bitter, harsh, acrimonious, unpalatable | 词性: adjective

Etymology:
From Late Latin *ēdūlcōrāre, from Latin ēdūlcōrātus, past participle of ēdūlcōrāre (“to sweeten, purify”), from ē- (variant of ex-) + dūlcōrāre (“to sweeten”), from dulcis (“sweet”).
