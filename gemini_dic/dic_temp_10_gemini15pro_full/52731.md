dike:/daɪk/ | n. |堤坝，沟渠|A long wall or bank built to prevent flooding from the sea or a river.|The Netherlands is famous for its dikes that hold back the sea. 荷兰以其抵御海水入侵的堤坝而闻名。|

dike:/daɪk/ | v. |筑堤，开沟|To build a dike.|They diked the land to protect it from floods. 他们为这片土地筑堤以防洪水。|

Etymology: From Middle Dutch *dīc, from Old English dīc "ditch, dike, trench," from Proto-Germanic *dīkaz "dike, ditch," from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeygʷʰ- "to form, build."

Synonyms: levee, embankment, barrier, dam

Antonyms: breach, gap, opening
