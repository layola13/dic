counterbalance:/ˈkaʊntərbæləns/ /ˈkaʊntərˌbæləns/| n. vt.|平衡，抵消；平衡物，抵消物|A counterbalance is something that has an equal but opposite effect to something else, and so creates a balance.|The rise in oil prices is counterbalanced by the fall in the value of the dollar. 石油价格上涨被美元贬值所抵消。|Synonyms: offset, compensate, balance, neutralize|Antonyms: enhance, amplify, exacerbate, worsen|noun/verb


counterbalances:/ˌkaʊntərˈbælənsɪz/ /ˌkaʊntərˈbælənsəz/| v.|平衡，抵消|Third-party forces counterbalance the two main parties. 第三方力量与两大主要政党抗衡。|Synonyms: offsets, compensates, balances, neutralizes|Antonyms: enhances, amplifies, exacerbates, worsens|verb
