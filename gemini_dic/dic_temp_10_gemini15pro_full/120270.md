miniard:/ˈmɪnjərd/| adj. n.|娇小的，优雅的；娇小玲珑的人|A miniard is something or someone small and delicately formed.  It can refer to a person, especially a woman, or an object.|She was a miniard woman with delicate features. 她是一位身材娇小，五官精致的女子。|The miniard figurine was exquisitely carved. 这个小巧的雕像雕刻精美。|近义词：petite, dainty, diminutive, small, delicate|反义词：large, hulking, massive|词性: adjective, noun
词源: From Middle French *miniard, from Old French mignard (“dainty, delicate, pretty, charming, lovable”), from Germanic *minnja (“love, affection”) (compare German Minne (“love”)). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *men- (“to think; to have an opinion”).
