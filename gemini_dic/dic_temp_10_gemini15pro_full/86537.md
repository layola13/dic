held-on:/held ˈɒn/ (British) /held ˈɑːn/ (American)| adj.|持续的，坚持的|An adjective used to describe something that has been continued or maintained, often with effort.  Derived from the verb "hold on," which means to grip something tightly or to persevere.|The team put up a held-on fight against their rivals.  团队与对手进行了一场顽强的战斗。|The company's held-on success is due to its innovative marketing strategies.  公司持续的成功归功于其创新的营销策略。|Near Synonyms: sustained, continued, persistent, enduring|Antonyms: brief, temporary, fleeting|Adjective
