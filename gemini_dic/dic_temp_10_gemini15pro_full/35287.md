cluttery:/ˈklʌtəri/ (US), /ˈklʌtəri/ (UK)| adj.| 凌乱的，杂乱的|Filled with or covered in clutter; messy and disorganized.|Her desk was always cluttery, piled high with papers and books. 她的桌子总是乱七八糟的，堆满了文件和书籍。|The attic was a dark and cluttery space. 阁楼是一个黑暗而杂乱的空间。|近义词：messy, disordered, untidy, chaotic|反义词：tidy, neat, organized, orderly|词性: 形容词

clutter:/ˈklʌtər/ (US), /ˈklʌtər/ (UK)| n. vt.| 杂乱，混乱；使凌乱|A collection of things lying about in an untidy state. To fill or cover in a disorderly way.|The room was filled with clutter. 房间里堆满了杂物。|Too many pictures cluttered the wall.  太多的照片把墙弄得很乱。|近义词：mess, disorder, chaos|反义词：tidiness, order, neatness|词性: 名词, 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Middle English word *clutter*, meaning "to make a rattling noise," likely of imitative origin, later evolving to mean "a confused noise" and then "a confused mass of things."
