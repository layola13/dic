copular:/ˈkɒpjʊlə(r)/ (US) /ˈkɑːpjələr/ (UK)| adj.|系动的，连系的|A copular verb connects the subject of a sentence to a subject complement, which identifies or describes the subject.  It does not show action.|She *is* a doctor. (connects "she" with "doctor") The soup *tasted* salty. (connects "soup" with "salty")|近义词: linking|反义词: (No direct antonym, as it describes a grammatical function)|词性: adjective

copula:/ˈkɒpjʊlə/ (US) /ˈkɑːpjʊlə/ (UK)| n.|系词|A copula is a word used to link the subject of a sentence with a predicate (a subject complement).  The most common copula in English is "be". Other examples include "become," "seem," "appear," etc.|The word "is" is a *copula*.|近义词: linking verb|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性: noun

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
The food *smells* delicious.  食物闻起来很香。

He *seems* tired. 他看起来很累。


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
The milk *is turning* sour. 牛奶正在变酸。

The situation *is becoming* more complicated.  情况变得越来越复杂了。


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
The cake *has become* dry. 蛋糕已经变干了。

He *has seemed* anxious all day. 他一整天都显得很焦虑。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
The weather *has been getting* colder. 天气越来越冷了。

He *has been appearing* more confident lately. 他最近看起来越来越自信了。


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
The music *sounded* beautiful. 音乐听起来很美妙。

She *felt* happy. 她感到很高兴。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
The soup *was tasting* bland. 汤尝起来很淡。

He *was looking* pale. 他看起来很苍白。


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
The apple *had gone* bad. 苹果已经坏了。

She *had become* a renowned scientist. 她已成为一位著名的科学家。


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
The room *had been getting* warmer. 房间一直在变暖。

The situation *had been becoming* more and more tense.  局势越来越紧张。


一般将来时 (Simple Future):
The concert *will sound* amazing. 音乐会听起来会很棒。

He *will become* a lawyer. 他将成为一名律师。


将来进行时 (Future Continuous):

The house *will have looked* better after the renovations.  装修后，房子看起来会更好。


将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
