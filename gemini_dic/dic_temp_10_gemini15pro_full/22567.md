booted:/ˈbuːtɪd/| adj.|穿着靴子的|Wearing boots.  Origin: From the noun "boot" (referring to a type of footwear) + the suffix "-ed," which forms adjectives indicating having or wearing something.  |Example: He wore a booted foot into the stirrup. 他穿着靴子把脚踩进马镫里。|Example: The booted soldiers marched through the mud. 穿着靴子的士兵们在泥泞中行军。|近义词：shod (especially with shoes),|反义词:barefoot, unshod|adjective
