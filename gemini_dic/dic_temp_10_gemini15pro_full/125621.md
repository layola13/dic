multungulate:/ˌmʌltʌŋˈɡjʊlət/ | adj. n. | 多趾的，多趾动物 | Having more than the usual number of toes; an animal with more than the usual number of toes. |  Example:  The multungulate animals, like hippos and rhinos, are fascinating to study.  多趾动物，如河马和犀牛，是引人入胜的研究对象。|Synonym: polydactyl | Antonym:  (no direct antonym, consider "artiodactyl" or "perissodactyl" for specific toe numbers) | Adjective, Noun

Etymology: From Latin *multus* ("many") + *ungula* ("hoof, claw, nail") + -ate (suffix forming adjectives).
