estuance:/ˈestʃuːəns/| n.|河口；江口|The wide estuance of the River Plate. 拉普拉塔河的宽阔河口。|近义词：estuary, mouth, inlet|反义词:upstream|名词
etymology: From Anglo-French *estuance, from Old French estuer "to be very hot, boil, seethe", from Latin aestuare "to surge, boil, be agitated," from aestus "heat, boiling, surge." The sense shifted from "boiling, surging" to "tidal rise and fall," then to "place of tidal rise and fall."
