mantlet:/ˈmæntlɪt/| n.|防盾，护墙，女式披风|A protective covering, screen, or shield, especially one forming part of a fortification or protecting a gun emplacement; also, a short cloak or cape worn by women.|The soldiers hid behind the mantlet as they advanced. 士兵们躲在防盾后面前进。|近义词:shield, screen, covering, cloak, cape|反义词:(For fortification sense) opening, breach; (For clothing sense) none|noun

mantle:/ˈmæntl/| n. vt.|斗篷，覆盖物；地幔|1. (n.) A loose sleeveless garment worn over other clothes. 2. (n.) Something that covers, envelops, or conceals. 3. (vt.) To cover, envelop, or conceal.|1. The king wore a velvet mantle. 国王穿着一件天鹅绒斗篷。 2. A mantle of snow covered the fields. 田野上覆盖着一层雪。 3.  Silence mantled the room. 房间里一片寂静。|近义词:cloak, cape, covering, blanket, shroud|反义词:uncover, reveal|noun, verb
