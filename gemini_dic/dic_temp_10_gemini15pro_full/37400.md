commercialise:/kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪz/ (UK) /kəˈmɜːrʃəlaɪz/ (US)| v. | 商业化，商品化 | To develop something so that it can be sold or used to make a profit. |  Many artists struggle to commercialise their work. 许多艺术家难以将他们的作品商业化。

The company commercialised the new technology quickly. 这家公司迅速将新技术商业化。| 近义词：monetize, marketize | 反义词：decommercialize | 词性: 动词

commercialisation:/kəˌmɜːʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (UK) /kəˌmɜːrʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (US)| n. | 商业化，商品化 | The process of developing something so that it can be sold or used to make a profit. | The commercialisation of Christmas has become excessive. 圣诞节的商业化已经变得过度了。

The increasing commercialisation of education is a worrying trend. 教育日益商业化是一个令人担忧的趋势。| 近义词：monetization, marketization | 反义词：decommercialization | 词性: 名词
