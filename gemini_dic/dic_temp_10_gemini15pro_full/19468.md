bid-fair:/bɪd ˈfeər/| v.|似乎；预示；有希望|To seem likely; to give promise of.|例句：The company bid fair to become the market leader. 这家公司似乎有望成为市场领导者。|例句：The weather bids fair for a picnic. 天气看来适合野餐。|近义词：seem likely, appear likely, promise, be likely to|反义词：be unlikely to, seem unlikely|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *biden feire*, from Old English *bīdan* (to wait, remain, experience) + *fæger* (fair, beautiful, pleasant). The original sense was "to meet fairly", later evolving to "to seem likely".
