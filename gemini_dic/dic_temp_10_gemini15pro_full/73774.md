formalist:/ˈfɔːrməlɪst/ | n. adj. | 形式主义者；拘泥形式的人 | Someone who adheres rigidly to prescribed forms or rules, often at the expense of content or meaning. | He was a strict formalist, more concerned with the rules of grammar than the meaning of the poem. 他是一个严格的墨守成规的人，更关心语法的规则而不是诗歌的意义。|  近义词：pedant, stickler, doctrinaire | 反义词：freethinker, innovator, nonconformist | 词性: 名词, 形容词

formalist:/ˈfɔːrməlɪst/ | adj. | 形式主义的；拘泥形式的 | Relating to or characteristic of formalism. |  Her formalist approach to literature ignored the historical context of the works. 她对文学的刻板方法忽略了作品的历史背景。| 近义词：rigid, inflexible, conventional | 反义词：flexible, innovative, creative | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *formaliste*, from *formal* + *-iste*. Ultimately from Latin *formalis*.
