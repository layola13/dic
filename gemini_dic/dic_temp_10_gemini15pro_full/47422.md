deadness:/ˈdɛdnəs/| n. |死气沉沉，麻木，沉闷|lack of activity, life, or feeling; the state of being dead or inactive. |The deadness of the economy is worrying. 经济的死气沉沉令人担忧。|Synonyms: inactivity, lifelessness, dullness, apathy, torpor|Antonyms: liveliness, vibrancy, activity, energy|noun
deadness:/ˈdɛdnəs/| n. |(声音)沉闷，无生气|lack of resonance or vibrancy in a sound. |The deadness of the drum made it sound flat. 鼓的声音沉闷，听起来很平淡。|Synonyms: flatness, hollowness, muffled|Antonyms: resonance, vibrancy, richness|noun
deadness:/ˈdɛdnəs/| n. |(颜色)暗淡，无光泽|lack of brightness or vividness in a color. |The deadness of the paint made the room feel gloomy. 油漆的暗淡使房间感觉很阴郁。|Synonyms: dullness, flatness, drabness|Antonyms: brightness, vividness, brilliance|noun
