nonprolixity:/ˌnɒnprəˈlɪksɪti/
noun
简洁，简明
The quality or state of being concise and not overly lengthy or wordy.
His nonprolixity was appreciated in the meeting; he got straight to the point.  他在会议上的简洁性受到赞赏；他直奔主题。
The lawyer's nonprolixity ensured the jury understood the complex case.  律师的简洁性确保了陪审团理解了这个复杂的案件。
conciseness, brevity, succinctness
prolixity, verbosity, wordiness
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + prolixity (wordiness).  Prolixity comes from Latin prolixus "extended, long," from pro- "forth" + liqui "to flow."
