bogglish:/ˈbɒɡlɪʃ/| adj.| 令人困惑的，难以理解的 | causing confusion or bewilderment; puzzling. | The instructions were so bogglish that nobody knew what to do.  说明书太令人困惑了，以至于没有人知道该怎么做。|近义词：confusing, perplexing, baffling, bewildering |反义词: clear, understandable, lucid|词性:形容词


bogglish:/ˈbɒɡlɪʃ/| v. | 困惑，使糊涂 | to confuse or bewilder someone. | The complex jargon bogglished the new employees. 复杂的术语让新员工感到困惑。|近义词：confuse, perplex, baffle, bewilder |反义词: clarify, enlighten|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
Likely a blend of "bog" (suggesting something unclear or murky) and "gobbledygook" (meaning unintelligible jargon). There is no definitive etymology, and the word is not considered standard English.
