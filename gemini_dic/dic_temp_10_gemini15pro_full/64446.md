erie:/ˈɪəri/ | adj. | 怪异的，阴森的，神秘可怕的 | Strange and mysterious, inspiring a sense of unease or fear. |  The abandoned house had an eerie silence.  那栋废弃的房子有一种阴森的寂静。| synonyms: uncanny, creepy, spooky, weird | antonyms: normal, ordinary, comforting | adjective

词源:  Likely from Middle English *eri, from Old English earg "cowardly," related to Old Norse argr "effeminate, cowardly." The meaning shifted from "cowardly" to "inspiring fear" over time.
