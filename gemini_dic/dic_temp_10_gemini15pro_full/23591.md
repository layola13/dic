brakes: /breɪks/
n. |刹车，制动器|A device for slowing or stopping a moving vehicle, typically by applying friction to the wheels.|The driver slammed on the brakes to avoid hitting the deer. 司机猛踩刹车以避免撞到鹿。|
近义词:stops, inhibitors
反义词:accelerators
词性:名词

brakes: /breɪks/
v. |刹车，减速|To slow or stop a moving vehicle using a brake.|He braked hard at the intersection. 他在十字路口急刹车。|
近义词:slows, decelerates
反义词:accelerates
词性:动词


词源: From Middle English *braken*, from Old English *brecan* ("to break"), of Germanic origin.  Related to Dutch *breken*, German *brechen*.  The sense of "to check, restrain" developed from the notion of "breaking" the speed or momentum.
