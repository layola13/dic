allegiances: /əˈliːdʒənsiz/ | n. | 忠诚；忠贞；效忠 |  the loyalty that a citizen owes to their country (or subjects to their sovereign), or more generally the duty that one owes to a person, group, or cause. |  His allegiances are divided between his family and his friends.  他的忠诚在他的家庭和朋友之间摇摆不定。 | synonyms: loyalty, fidelity, devotion, commitment, adherence | antonyms: disloyalty, treachery, betrayal, treason | noun

词源:
From Old French *alegeance (Modern French allégeance), from alege "liege, free from all servitude except to the sovereign," from Latin allegiatus, past participle of allegiare "to bind by oath," from ad- "to" + ligare "to bind."
