confect:/kənˈfɛkt/ /ˈkɑːnfɛkt/| v. n.|制作（糖果、蜜饯等）；糖果；蜜饯|To make or prepare something, especially something sweet or elaborate. Also, a sweet, especially one made of sugar or chocolate.|例句：The company confects a variety of delicious chocolates. 这家公司制作各种美味的巧克力。|例句：She bought a box of assorted confects. 她买了一盒什锦糖果。|近义词：create, make, prepare, produce, sweet, candy|反义词:destroy, ruin|词性:verb, noun

confection:/kənˈfɛkʃən/ /ˈkɑːnfɛkʃən/| n.|糖果；甜食；（精美的）作品|A dish or delicacy made with sweet ingredients, or something carefully created or constructed.|例句：The bakery displayed a dazzling array of confections. 面包店展示了一系列令人眼花缭乱的甜点。|例句：His latest novel is a literary confection. 他的最新小说是一部文学佳作。|近义词：sweet, candy, treat, delicacy|反义词:|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *confectus, past participle of conficere "to put together, complete, accomplish, carry out," from com- "together" (see com-) + facere "to do, make" (from PIE root *dhe- "to set, put").  The noun meaning "sweetmeat" is from 1570s.
