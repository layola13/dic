extuberate:/ɪkˈstʃuːbəreɪt/ | v. |  使欢欣鼓舞；使兴高采烈 | To make someone joyful and elated; to fill with or express great joy or triumph. |  She was extuberated by the news of her promotion. 她听到升职的消息欣喜若狂。 | 近义词：exhilarate, elate, rejoice | 反义词：depress, sadden | 词性: 动词

extuberant:/ɪɡˈzjuːbərənt/ | adj. |  充满活力的；热情洋溢的；兴高采烈的 | Filled with or characterized by exuberance; overflowing with enthusiasm and excitement. | The children were exuberant after their trip to the zoo. 孩子们去完动物园后兴高采烈。 | 近义词：enthusiastic, lively, energetic | 反义词：depressed, subdued | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From Latin *exuberant- (stem of exuberans), present participle of exuberare "to be abundant, overflow," from ex- "out" + uber "udder, fertile, copious"
