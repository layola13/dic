ills:/ɪlz/ | n. | 弊端，缺点 |  bad qualities or features | The ills of modern society are numerous. 现代社会的弊端不胜枚举。 | synonyms: defects, flaws, shortcomings, drawbacks | antonyms: virtues, merits, strengths | noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *illes*, plural of *ille* (“evil, sickness, misfortune”), from Old English *yfel* (“evil, bad, ill, wicked, vicious”), from Proto-Germanic *ubilaz* (“evil”), from Proto-Indo-European *up-elo-* (“bad, evil”). Cognate with Scots *ill* (“bad, evil, sick”), West Frisian *yl* (“bad”), Dutch *euvel* (“bad, wrong”), German *übel* (“evil, bad, ill”), Danish *ond*, *ilde* (“bad, evil”), Swedish *illa*, *ond* (“badly, evil”), Icelandic *illr* (“bad, evil”).
