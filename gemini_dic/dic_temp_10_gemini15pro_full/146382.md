Etymology:

"Over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *ubar, meaning "above, over, beyond") + "flour" (from Old French flor, flour, ultimately from Latin flos, floris, meaning "flower," as flour is the "flower" or best part of the grain) + "-ed" (past participle suffix).
