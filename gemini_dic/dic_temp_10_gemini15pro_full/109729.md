levels:/ˈlɛvlz/ (US) /ˈlɛvəlz/ (UK)| n.|水平，等级，级别|A position on a scale of amount, quantity, extent, or quality.|The company operates at several levels of management.  这家公司在多个管理层级运营。|synonyms: ranks, tiers, grades, stages, degrees|antonyms: none|noun

levels:/ˈlɛvlz/ (US) /ˈlɛvəlz/ (UK)| v.|使平坦，使相等，摧毁，坦白承认|To make something flat or even; to destroy something completely; to admit something frankly.|They leveled the old building to make way for a new shopping center.  他们夷平了旧建筑，以便为新的购物中心腾出空间。|synonyms: flatten, raze, demolish, confess|antonyms: raise, build, construct, conceal|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *læfel, from Proto-Germanic *laibaz, meaning "something left, remainder." The original meaning referred to a horizontal beam or board, which later evolved to mean a flat surface and eventually to the abstract concept of a level or degree.
