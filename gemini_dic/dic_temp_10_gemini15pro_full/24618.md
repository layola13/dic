broke-down:/ˈbroʊkˈdaʊn/ (US) /ˈbrəʊkˈdaʊn/ (UK)| adj.| 故障的；垮掉的；破旧的；分解的；情绪崩溃的|  Literally, broken down into parts, no longer functioning. Figuratively, emotionally or mentally distressed.  |The car broke down on the highway. 这辆车在高速公路上抛锚了。|近义词：malfunctioning, faulty, ruined, dilapidated, distressed, overwhelmed |反义词:functioning, working, intact, sound, composed, calm|词性:形容词

broke-down:/ˈbroʊkˈdaʊn/ (US) /ˈbrəʊkˈdaʊn/ (UK)| n.| 故障；崩溃；分类；分解|  An instance of something breaking down, either mechanically or emotionally. |There was a breakdown in communication between the two parties. 双方沟通出现故障。|近义词：failure, malfunction, collapse, decomposition|反义词:success, function, repair, composition|词性:名词
