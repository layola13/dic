nonpapist:/ˌnɒnˈpeɪpɪst/ | n. adj. |非天主教徒，非罗马天主教徒|A person who is not a Roman Catholic, especially one who opposed the Catholic Church during a period of strong anti-Catholic sentiment.|例句：In 17th-century England, nonpapists were often subject to discrimination. 17世纪的英格兰，非天主教徒经常受到歧视。|例句：He was raised a nonpapist in a predominantly Protestant community. 他在一个以新教徒为主的社区长大，并非天主教徒。|近义词：Protestant, Dissenter|反义词：Papist, Catholic|词性:名词，形容词

Etymology:
From non- + papist.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "papist" refers to a Roman Catholic (often used disparagingly).
