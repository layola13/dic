palsgrave:/ˈpælzɡreɪv/| n.|宫廷教师；朋友；伙伴|A historical title for a tutor to a royal child, or a close friend or companion. |He served as palsgrave to the young prince. 他担任了年轻王子的宫廷教师。|近义词：tutor, friend, companion|反义词:enemy, foe|noun


palsgrave:/ˈpælzɡreɪv/| n.|伯爵 Palatine|A count palatine, a high-ranking noble in the Holy Roman Empire. |The palsgrave held significant power in the region. 伯爵帕拉蒂尼在该地区拥有相当大的权力。|近义词：count, noble|反义词:commoner, peasant|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *palsgrave*, from German *Pfalzgraf*, from *Pfalz* ("palace") + *Graf* ("count"). Ultimately from Latin *palatium* ("palace").
