concrement:/ˈkɒŋkrɪmənt/ (US) /ˈkɑːnkrɪmənt/| n. |结石，凝结物|A solidified mass, especially a stone formed in a body cavity, such as a kidney stone or gallstone.|The doctor found a concrement in the patient's bladder. 医生在病人的膀胱里发现了一块结石。|Synonyms: calculus, stone|Antonyms: solution, liquid|noun

concretion:/kənˈkriːʃn/ (US) /kənˈkriːʃn/| n. |凝结; 凝结物|The process of becoming hard or solid; a hard solid mass formed by the local accumulation of matter, especially within the body or within a mass of sediment.|The concretion of minerals formed a beautiful geode. 矿物质的凝结形成了美丽的晶洞。|Synonyms: solidification, coagulation, mass|Antonyms: dissolution, liquefaction|noun
