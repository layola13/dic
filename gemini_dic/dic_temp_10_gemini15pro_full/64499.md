erodibility:/ɪˌroʊdəˈbɪləti/| n. |可蚀性，易蚀性|The quality of being susceptible to erosion. The ease with which a soil or rock can be eroded by wind, water, or other natural agents.|The erodibility of the soil was a major factor in the landslide.  土壤的可蚀性是滑坡的主要因素。|Synonyms: vulnerability, susceptibility, fragility|Antonyms: resistance, stability, impermeability|noun
