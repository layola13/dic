misphrases:/ˌmɪsˈfreɪzɪz/| v. |措辞不当，表达错误|To express something inaccurately or clumsily, using the wrong words or phrasing.| He often misphrases things, which can lead to misunderstandings. 他经常措辞不当，这会导致误解。| misstates, misrepresents| expresses correctly, articulates clearly| verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (wrongly) + phrase (to express in words).  "Mis-" is a prefix of Germanic origin meaning "wrong," "bad," or "astray." "Phrase" comes from the Late Latin phrasis, from Greek phrasis, meaning "diction, style of speech."
