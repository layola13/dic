numbing:/ˈnʌmɪŋ/ (US), /ˈnʌmɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|麻木的；使人麻木的|Causing loss of feeling or responsiveness; making something less painful or less intense.|The dentist gave me a numbing injection before he started the procedure. 牙医在开始手术前给我打了麻醉针。|The shock of the news was numbing.  这令人震惊的消息使人麻木。|Synonyms: deadening, desensitizing, anesthetic, benumbing|Antonyms: stimulating, exciting, sensitizing|adjective


numbing:/ˈnʌmɪŋ/ (US), /ˈnʌmɪŋ/ (UK)| n.|麻木|The state of being numb.|The numbing effect of the medication wore off after a few hours. 药物的麻木效果几个小时后就消失了。|She felt a strange numbing in her fingers. 她感到手指一阵奇怪的麻木。|Synonyms: numbness, deadness, insensibility|Antonyms: sensation, feeling, sensitivity|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From numb + -ing.  The word "numb" comes from the Old English "numen," meaning "taken, seized, captured," likely related to the idea of being deprived of sensation.
