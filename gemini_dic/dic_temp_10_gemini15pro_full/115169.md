maniacs:/ˈmeɪniæks/ | n. |疯子；狂热者；癖好者|A person exhibiting extreme symptoms of wild behavior, especially one with uncontrollable obsession or enthusiasm for something.|Those maniacs were driving way too fast. 那些疯子开车太快了。|近义词：lunatics, fanatics, obsessives|反义词：sane people, moderates|词性: 名词

词源: From Late Latin *maniacus*, from Latin maniacus (“affected with mania”), from Ancient Greek μανιακός (maniakós, “mad, raving, frenzied”), from μανία (manía, “madness, frenzy”).
