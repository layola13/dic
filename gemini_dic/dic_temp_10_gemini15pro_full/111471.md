load-shedding:/ˈləʊd ʃɛdɪŋ/| n. |停电，拉闸限电|The deliberate shutdown of electric power in a part of a power-distribution system, generally to prevent the failure of the entire system when the demand strains the capacity of the system.|Due to the extreme heat, the city is experiencing rolling load-shedding. 由于极端高温，该市正在经历轮流停电。|Power outage, blackout, brownout|Continuous power supply|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
load (n.) + shedding (n.)
"load" refers to the amount of power being used.
"shedding" refers to the act of getting rid of something.
