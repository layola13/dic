gesticulation:/dʒɛstɪkjʊˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 手势; 姿势; | An expressive gesture made in showing strong feeling or excitement. |  She spoke with great animation, her hands gesticulating wildly. 她说话很激动，双手不停地比划着。|Synonyms: gesture, motion, sign, signal | Antonyms: stillness, immobility | noun

gesticulation:/dʒɛstɪkjʊˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 比划; | The act of making gestures. |  His gesticulations became more frantic as he tried to explain. 他越是想解释清楚，他的手势就越疯狂。 | Synonyms: gesturing, signaling, signing, motioning| Antonyms: stillness, immobility | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin gesticulātiō, from Latin gesticulor (“I make gestures”), from gesticulus (“a mimic gesture”), diminutive of gestus (“gesture, posture”).
