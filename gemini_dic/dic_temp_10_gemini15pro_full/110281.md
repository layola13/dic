ligand:/ˈlɪɡənd/| n.|配体|An ion or molecule that binds to a central metal atom to form a coordination complex.  A ligand donates at least one electron pair to the central metal atom or ion.|The heme group in hemoglobin is a ligand that binds to iron.|Synonyms: complexing agent, chelating agent|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but one could consider "free ion/molecule" as a contrasting concept)|noun
