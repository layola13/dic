outbow:/ˌaʊtˈboʊ/
verb
超过，胜过（尤指在礼节方面）
to surpass or exceed, especially in courtesy or politeness
He outbowed everyone at the reception, greeting each guest with a deep, elaborate bow. 他在招待会上胜过所有人，向每位客人深深地鞠躬致意。

outbow:/ˌaʊtˈboʊ/
verb
（船）比…更能顶风航行
(of a ship) to sail closer to the wind than (another ship)
The smaller yacht outbowed the larger one in the strong winds. 在强风中，较小的游艇比较大的游艇更能顶风航行。


Etymology:
From 'out-' (meaning surpassing or exceeding) + 'bow' (the act of bending the head or upper body as a sign of respect or greeting). The nautical meaning derives from the ability of a ship to "bow" or lean into the wind more effectively than another.

Synonyms:
outdo, surpass, excel, exceed (in courtesy or performance), outmaneuver (nautical)

Antonyms:
be outdone, be surpassed, lag behind (in performance), be outsailed (nautical)

Word Type:
Verb
