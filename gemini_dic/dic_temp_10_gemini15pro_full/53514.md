disassociation:/dɪsəˌsoʊʃiˈeɪʃən/ /dɪsəˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən/
n.|脱离，分离，分裂|The act of separating or the state of being separated.  A disconnection or detachment.|She experienced a period of disassociation following the trauma.|近义词：separation, detachment, disconnection, alienation|反义词：association, connection, union|词性:名词

dissociation:/dɪˌsoʊʃiˈeɪʃən/ /dɪˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən/
n.|解离，精神分裂|A defense mechanism, pathologic or otherwise, in which normally integrated mental processes, such as memory, consciousness, perception, or identity, split off from each other.|He suffered from dissociation and had multiple personalities.|近义词：splitting, detachment, compartmentalization|反义词：integration, unification|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dissociationem, from dissociare "to disunite, separate," from dis- "apart" + sociare "to associate," from socius "companion."
