heirloom:/ˈɛərˌluːm/ (US), /ˈeə(r)ˌluːm/ (UK)| n. |传家宝；祖传之物|A valuable object that has belonged to a family for several generations.|My grandmother left me a beautiful necklace as an heirloom. 我的祖母留给我一条漂亮的项链作为传家宝。|
heirloom:/ˈɛərˌluːm/ (US), /ˈeə(r)ˌluːm/ (UK)| n. |（尤指品种优良的）祖传植物，老品种（植物）|A plant variety that has been passed down through generations, often with desirable characteristics.|These heirloom tomatoes have a much richer flavor than the ones from the supermarket.  这些祖传番茄比超市买的番茄味道浓郁得多。|
heirloom:/ˈɛərˌluːm/ (US), /ˈeə(r)ˌluːm/ (UK)|adj.(attributive) |祖传的; 传家的|Something that is an heirloom.|These heirloom seeds have been passed down in my family for generations. 这些传家宝种子在我家已经传承好几代了。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *erelome, from Old English *ērfe-lōma (“heirloom, inheritance”), equivalent to heir +‎ -loom.  The second element is related to loan. Originally, an heirloom was a piece of furniture or other item specifically mentioned in a will.
