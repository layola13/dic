monopolise:/məˈnɒpəlaɪz/ (UK) /məˈˈnɑːpəlaɪz/ (US)| v. |垄断，独占|To take exclusive control or possession of something, so that others are prevented from using or sharing it.|The company has monopolised the market for years.  这家公司多年来垄断了市场。

Microsoft was accused of monopolising the software market. 微软被指控垄断了软件市场。

|近义词：dominate, control, corner, hog|反义词: share, distribute|词性:动词


monopolization:/məˌnɒpəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ (UK) /məˌnɑːpəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ (US)| n. |垄断，独占|The act of monopolising something.|The government is concerned about the monopolisation of the media. 政府担心媒体被垄断。

The monopolisation of resources by a few companies led to public outcry. 少数公司对资源的垄断引起了公众的强烈抗议。

|近义词：dominance, control|反义词: sharing, distribution|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *monopoliser*, from *monopole* "monopoly," from Greek *monopōlion*, from *monos* "alone, single" + *pōlein* "to sell."
