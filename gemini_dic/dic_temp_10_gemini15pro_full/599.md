abox:/əˈbɒks/| adv.| 在盒子里；装在盒子里；在船上；上船 | in a box; on board a ship | The toys were all abox. 玩具都装在盒子里了。|近义词：boxed, encased |反义词: unpacked, unboxed |副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From a (preposition) + box (noun).  The preposition "a" in this context is an archaic form meaning "on" or "in".  It survives in a few words like "abed" (in bed) and "ashore" (on shore).  Thus, "abox" literally means "in a box."

注意事项:
This word is archaic and rarely used in modern English.  "Boxed" or "in a box" are more common and preferred alternatives.  The nautical sense of "on board ship" is also archaic. "Aboard" is the modern equivalent.
