nobble:/ˈnɒbl/ (British) /ˈnɑːbl/ (American)| vt.|暗中破坏，搞垮，收买；偷偷猎取（兔子等）|To disable or incapacitate by underhand means; to get hold of something illicitly.  Also, in British English, to catch rabbits illegally.|He nobbled the horse before the race so it wouldn't win. 他在比赛前搞垮了那匹马，使它赢不了。|近义词：sabotage, disable, fix, rig, nobble (especially British)|反义词:repair, enable, assist|词性:动词

nobbles:/ˈnɒblz/ (British) /ˈnɑːblz/ (American)| vt.|暗中破坏，搞垮，收买；偷偷猎取（兔子等）（第三人称单数）|Third-person singular present tense form of "nobble".|He often nobbles the competition. 他经常暗中破坏竞争对手。|近义词：sabotages, disables, fixes, rigs|反义词:repairs, enables, assists|词性:动词

词源:
"Nobble" likely originates from the Romany word "naklo" meaning "nose," referring to snaring rabbits by the nose.  Over time, it broadened to mean capturing or disabling something by underhand means.
