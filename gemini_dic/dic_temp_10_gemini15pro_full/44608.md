crowded:/ˈkraʊdɪd/ | adj. | 拥挤的，挤满的 | Filled with a large number of people or things close together. | The bus was so crowded I could barely breathe. 公交车太挤了，我几乎喘不过气来。| The market was crowded with shoppers. 市场里挤满了购物者。 | packed, jammed, congested, overflowing | empty, deserted, sparse, uncrowded | adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *crowden, from Old English crūdan (“to press, crowd, hasten”), from Proto-Germanic *kreutaną (“to press, crowd”), from Proto-Indo-European *grewd- (“to press”). Related to crowd (n.).
