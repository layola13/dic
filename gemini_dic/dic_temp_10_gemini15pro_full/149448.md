padded cell: /ˈpædɪd sɛl/ | n. | 软壁囚室，保护室 | A room in a psychiatric hospital or prison with padded walls, designed to prevent patients from injuring themselves. |  The patient was placed in a padded cell after becoming violently agitated.  病人变得狂躁不安后，被安置在软壁囚室里。 | synonyms: padded room, rubber room | antonyms: normal cell, regular room | noun

padded cells: /ˈpædɪd sɛlz/ | n. | 软壁囚室，保护室（复数） | Rooms in a psychiatric hospital or prison with padded walls, designed to prevent patients from injuring themselves. | Several padded cells were available in the emergency ward. 急诊室里有几个软壁囚室。| synonyms: padded rooms, rubber rooms | antonyms: normal cells, regular rooms | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "padded" (past participle of "pad," meaning to fill or cover with soft material) + "cell" (a small room, especially in a prison or monastery).  The term reflects the practice of padding the walls of these rooms to protect inmates prone to self-harm.
