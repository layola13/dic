baton:/bəˈtɒn/ (UK), /bəˈtɑːn/ (US)| n. |指挥棒；警棍；接力棒|A stick used by a conductor, a police officer, or in a relay race.|The conductor raised his baton and the orchestra began to play. 指挥举起了指挥棒，乐队开始演奏。|The police officer used his baton to subdue the suspect. 警察用警棍制服了嫌疑人。|synonyms: stick, rod, staff, club (for police baton)|antonyms: (No direct antonym for all senses.  Perhaps "instrument" for conductor's baton, as it's not a baton but serves a similar purpose.)|noun


baton:/bəˈtɒn/ (UK), /bəˈtɑːn/ (US)| vt. |用警棍打|To hit someone with a baton.|The protester was batoned by the police. 抗议者被警察用警棍打了。|(This usage is less common and often considered violent.)|synonyms: club, bludgeon|antonyms: (No direct antonym)|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *baston*, meaning "stick," ultimately from Late Latin *bastum*.
