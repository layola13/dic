co-operator:/koʊˈɑːpəreɪtər/ | n. |合作者，合作社社员|A person who cooperates with another or others, especially in a business or other organized activity.|Example 1: The co-operators in the project shared the profits equally. 项目的合作者们平分了利润。

Example 2:  All the co-operators in the housing cooperative were responsible for the upkeep of the building.  所有住房合作社的成员都有责任维护建筑物的保养。|近义词：collaborator, partner, associate|反义词：competitor, opponent, rival|词性: noun

co-operate:/koʊˈɑːpəreɪt/ | v. |合作，协作|To work or act together for a common purpose or benefit.|Example 1: The two companies co-operated on the development of the new software. 两家公司合作开发了这款新软件。

Example 2:  The witnesses refused to co-operate with the police investigation.  目击者拒绝配合警方调查。|近义词：collaborate, work together, team up|反义词：compete, oppose, obstruct|词性: verb

词源: From the Latin "co-" (together) + "operari" (to work).
