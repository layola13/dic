fluctuation:/ˌflʌktʃuˈeɪʃn/| n. |波动；起伏；变动|irregular rising and falling in number or amount; a variation.|例句：The fluctuations in the stock market made investors nervous. 股票市场的波动让投资者感到紧张。

例句：Temperature fluctuations can affect the growth of plants. 温度波动会影响植物的生长。|近义词：variation, oscillation, swing, instability|反义词：stability, constancy, steadiness|词性：名词

fluctuate:/ˈflʌktʃueɪt/| v. |波动；起伏；变动|to rise and fall irregularly in number or amount.|例句：The price of oil has been fluctuating wildly in recent months.  近几个月来，石油价格一直在剧烈波动。

例句：Her mood fluctuates between happiness and despair. 她的情绪在快乐和绝望之间波动。|近义词：vary, oscillate, swing, waver|反义词：stabilize, remain constant|词性：动词
