devolutionary:/ˌdiːvəˈluːʃəneri/ |adj.|权力下放的；退化的；发展的|Relating to devolution, the transfer of power from a central government to regional or local governments; also, relating to degeneration or backward evolution.|The UK experienced a devolutionary process in the late 20th century, granting more power to Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland.|近义词:decentralizing, delegating|反义词:centralizing, unitary|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "devolution" + "-ary".  "Devolution" comes from Late Latin "devolūtiōn-", "devolūtiō" ("a rolling down"), from the past participle stem of "devolvere" ("to roll down"), from "de-" ("down") + "volvere" ("to roll").
