bereave: /bɪˈriːv/ /bəˈriːv/ | vt. | 使丧失（亲人）; 剥夺 | To deprive someone of a relative or close friend through death, especially by death.  It can also mean to deprive someone of something valued. | His family was bereaved by his sudden death. 他的家人因他的突然离世而悲痛欲绝。


The flood bereaved them of their home and all their possessions. 洪水使他们失去了家园和所有财产。| synonyms: deprive, strip, rob, dispossess | antonyms: provide, give, endow | verb

bereaved: /bɪˈriːvd/ /bəˈriːvd/ | adj. | 丧失亲友的 | Deprived of a close relation or friend through their death. |  The bereaved family received many condolences.  这个痛失亲人的家庭收到了许多慰问。 | synonyms: grieving, mourning, sorrowful | antonyms: joyful, happy, celebrating | adjective

bereavement: /bɪˈriːvmənt/ /bəˈriːvmənt/ | n. | 丧失亲友 |  The state of being deprived of a close relation or friend through their death. | She experienced a deep sense of bereavement after her husband's death. 她丈夫去世后，她经历了深深的悲痛。| synonyms: loss, grief, mourning | antonyms: gain,  joy | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *berēafian, *bereafian (to deprive, rob), from be- (thoroughly, completely) + rēafian (to rob, plunder).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *raubōną (to rob).  Related to German rauben (to rob).
