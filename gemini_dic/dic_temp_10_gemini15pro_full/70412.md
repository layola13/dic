fight-with-windmills:/faɪt wɪð ˈwɪndmɪlz/ |idiomatic expression| 与风车作战；做无谓的斗争；|To fight with windmills means to attack imaginary enemies or to pursue hopeless or unrealistic causes. It refers to a futile struggle against something that cannot be overcome. This expression comes from the novel "Don Quixote" by Miguel de Cervantes, where the main character, Don Quixote, mistakes windmills for giants and attacks them.|He's always fighting with windmills, getting upset about things that don't really matter. 他总是与风车作战，为一些无关紧要的事情生气。

Don Quixote, in his madness, was convinced he was fighting with windmills, not giants.  在他疯狂的状态下，堂吉诃德确信他是在与风车作战，而不是巨人。| 近义词: tilt at windmills,  chase rainbows, pursue a lost cause | 反义词: face reality, be practical, address real problems| noun phrase
