feudaloid:/ˈfjuːdəlɔɪd/ | adj. | 封建主义的，类似封建制度的 | Resembling or characteristic of feudalism, but not strictly feudal.  A social or economic system that resembles feudalism. |  The plantation system in the antebellum South was described as feudaloid, with the plantation owner acting as lord and the enslaved people as serfs.  南方战前种植园制度被描述为类似封建制度，种植园主扮演领主，奴隶扮演农奴。 |  The company's hierarchical structure was criticized as being feudaloid. 这家公司的等级结构被批评为类似封建制度。 | Near synonyms:  feudalistic, quasi-feudal | Antonym: democratic, egalitarian | Word type: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From feudal + -oid.  "Feudal" traces back to Medieval Latin "feudalis," related to "feudum" (fief).  "-oid" is a suffix meaning "resembling" or "having the form of."
