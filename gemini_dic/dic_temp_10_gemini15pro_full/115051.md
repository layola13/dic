mandrel:/ˈmændrəl/ (US), /ˈmændrəl/ (UK)| n.| 心轴，芯棒，顶针|A shaft or spindle, usually cylindrical, on which work is mounted for turning, shaping, or other machining operations; also used for holding materials during processing such as bending, molding, or curing.|The potter used a mandrel to shape the clay vase.|Synonyms: arbor, spindle, shaft, axle|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, perhaps cavity, mold in some contexts)|noun

mandrel:/ˈmændrəl/ (US), /ˈmændrəl/ (UK)| n.| 卷筒|A cylindrical form on which materials such as paper, film, or tape are wound.|The cable was neatly wound around the mandrel.|Synonyms: spool, reel, bobbin|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms)|noun
