calk:/kɔːk/| n. |嵌缝；填隙料；马蹄防滑钉|A substance used to fill cracks and gaps, especially in wooden ships. Also, a sharp point fixed to a shoe to prevent slipping. |Example 1:  The shipwright used calk to seal the seams of the hull.  造船工人用填隙料封住船体的接缝。

Example 2:  He hammered the calks into his boots before climbing the icy slope. 他在上冰坡之前把防滑钉钉进靴子里。|近义词：sealant, filler, caulk, cleat|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性:noun


calk:/kɔːk/| vt. |填塞；嵌缝；装钉马蹄防滑钉|To fill or close seams or crevices with calk. Also, to fix sharp points to a shoe to prevent slipping.|Example 1: He calked the windows to keep out the draft. 他给窗户嵌缝以防风。

Example 2: The farrier calked the horse's shoes for better grip on the icy roads.  蹄铁匠为马蹄装上防滑钉以便在结冰的道路上更好地抓地。|近义词：seal, fill, caulk|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性:verb


词源: From Middle English *calken, *cauken, from Old North French *cauquer, calquer (“to press, tread”), from Latin calcare (“to tread, press”), from calc- (“heel”).  Related to heel.
