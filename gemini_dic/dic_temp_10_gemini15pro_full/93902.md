immortalizing:/ɪˈmɔːrtəˌlaɪzɪŋ/
verb
使不朽；使永垂不朽
The artist immortalized her in a painting. 这位艺术家在一幅画中使她永垂不朽。
immortalize: /ɪˈmɔːrtəˌlaɪz/ | v. | 使不朽；使永垂不朽 |  The photograph immortalized the moment. 这张照片使这一刻成为永恒。 | perpetuate, commemorate, eternalize | forget, obliterate | verb

immortalized:/ɪˈmɔːrtəˌlaɪzd/
adjective
被使不朽的；被使永垂不朽的
The actress was immortalized in the Hollywood Walk of Fame.  这位女演员的名字被刻在好莱坞星光大道上，流芳百世。
immortalized: /ɪˈmɔːrtəˌlaɪzd/ | adj. | 被使不朽的；被使永垂不朽的 | The poet's works were immortalized in numerous anthologies. 这位诗人的作品被收录在众多选集中，使其不朽。 | enshrined, commemorated, celebrated | forgotten, obscure | adjective


Etymology:
From Late Middle English *immortalisen, *immortalizen, from Late Latin immortalizāre, from Latin immortālis (“immortal”) + -izāre, equivalent to immortal + -ize.
