heathen:/ˈhiːðən/ | n. adj. |异教徒，野蛮人；不信教的，野蛮的 | A person who does not belong to a widely held religion (especially one who is not a Christian, Jew, or Muslim) as regarded by those who do.  |  例句：Early missionaries attempted to convert the heathens. 早期传教士试图改变异教徒的信仰。| 近义词：pagan, infidel, gentile | 反义词：believer, Christian | 词性: 名词, 形容词
heathen:/ˈhiːðən/ | adj. |不信教的，野蛮的 |  Uncivilized; irreligious. | 例句：Their heathen ways appalled the missionaries. 他们的野蛮行径使传教士感到震惊。 | 近义词：uncivilized, barbaric, pagan | 反义词：civilized, religious | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hæþen (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *haithanaz (“dwelling on the heath”), from *haithiz (“heath, uncultivated land”). Cognate with Old Saxon hēthin, Old Norse heiðinn, Old Frisian hethen, Dutch heiden, German Heide (“heath”).  Originally referred to people living in rural, uncultivated areas, later acquiring the meaning of "non-Christian" as Christianity spread from urban centers.
