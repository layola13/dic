ambulatory:/ˈæmbjʊləˌtɔːri/ (US), /ˈæmbjʊlətri/ (UK)| adj. n.|步行的，流动的；门诊病人；流动诊所|able to walk; relating to or adapted for walking; of or relating to patients who are not confined to bed; a place for walking, especially an aisle around the apse or a cloister of a church or monastery|例句：The patient is now ambulatory. 病人现在可以走动了。|近义词：mobile, walking, peripatetic|反义词:bedridden, immobile|词性:adjective, noun

ambulatory:/ˈæmbjʊləˌtɔːri/ (US), /ˈæmbjʊlətri/ (UK)| n.|门诊病人；流动诊所|a person who is able to walk; a place for walking, especially an aisle around the apse or a cloister of a church or monastery|例句：The hospital has a new ambulatory wing. 医院有一个新的门诊部。|近义词：outpatient, walker|反义词:inpatient|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *ambulatorius, from Latin ambulat-, ambulare "to walk" + -orius.
