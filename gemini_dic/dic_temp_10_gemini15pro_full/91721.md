hypercriticism:/ˌhaɪpərˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 过度批评；吹毛求疵 |  Excessive or overzealous criticism; the act of criticizing someone or something too harshly or severely. | The author faced hypercriticism for a minor historical inaccuracy in his novel.  这位作者因为小说中一个小小的历史错误而面临过度批评。|  Example: The teacher's hypercriticism discouraged the students from participating in class discussions. 老师的过度批评让学生们不敢参与课堂讨论。 | Synonyms: nitpicking, carping, faultfinding, caviling | Antonyms: praise, commendation, approval | Noun
