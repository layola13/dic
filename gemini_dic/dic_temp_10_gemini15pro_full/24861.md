brownstone: /ˈbraʊnˌstoʊn/ (US) /ˈbraʊnˌstəʊn/ (UK)
noun
褐砂石; 褐砂石建筑
A reddish-brown sandstone used for building. Also, a house or row house fronted with this stone, especially common in New York City.
The neighborhood is lined with elegant brownstones. 这个街区林立着优雅的褐砂石建筑。
synonyms: townhouse, row house
antonyms: (No direct antonyms for the material itself, possibly "modern building" for the architectural style)
noun

brownstone: /ˈbraʊnˌstoʊn/ (US) /ˈbraʊnˌstəʊn/ (UK)
adjective
褐砂石的
Made of or faced with brownstone.
They lived in a brownstone apartment. 他们住在一套褐砂石公寓里。
synonyms: sandstone
antonyms: (No direct antonym, potentially "brick," "wooden," or other building material adjectives)
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From brown + stone.  The term became particularly associated with a specific architectural style popular in the 19th and early 20th centuries, especially in eastern US cities like New York.
