non-denial:/ˌnɒn dɪˈnaɪəl/ | n. |不否认;间接承认|An act of not denying something, which can be interpreted as an implicit admission of guilt or responsibility.  It often involves refusing to confirm or deny something, especially in situations where a denial would be expected if the accusation were false.|The government's non-denial of the rumors fueled further speculation. 政府对传闻的不否认进一步加剧了猜测。|Synonyms: implicit admission, tacit acknowledgment, evasion|Antonyms: denial, refutation, contradiction|noun
