acrisia:/əˈkrɪziə/| n.|茫然，无判断力|A state of indecision or inability to make judgments, especially in moral matters; lack of a sense of crisis or urgency.|He displayed acrisia in the face of danger, seemingly unaware of the gravity of the situation. 他在危险面前表现出茫然，似乎没有意识到情况的严重性。

The prolonged acrisia of the government led to further economic decline. 政府长期以来的无判断力导致了经济的进一步衰退。|近义词：indecision, apathy, indifference|反义词： decisiveness, resolve, determination|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἀκρισία (akrisía, “lack of judgment”), from ἀ- (a-, “without”) + κρίσις (krísis, “judgment”).
