back-pedal:/ˈbækˌpɛdəl/ (US), /ˈbækˌpɛd(ə)l/ (UK)| v. |后退；改变主意；收回前言|To reverse one's previous position on something; to retreat from a commitment or opinion.| The politician had to back-pedal on his earlier statement after it caused public outcry. 这位政治家不得不在他早先的声明引起公众强烈抗议后收回前言。|
back-pedal:/ˈbækˌpɛdəl/ (US), /ˈbækˌpɛd(ə)l/ (UK)| v. |后退；改变主意；收回前言|To literally pedal backwards on a bicycle.|He back-pedaled furiously to avoid hitting the dog. 他拼命地向后踩踏板以避免撞到那只狗。|
back-pedal:/ˈbækˌpɛdəl/ (US), /ˈbækˌpɛd(ə)l/ (UK)| n. |后退；改变主意；收回前言|The act of reversing one's position or retracting a statement. (Noun usage is less common)|His quick back-pedal saved him from further embarrassment.  他迅速的改口使他免于进一步的尴尬。|近义词：retreat, withdraw, recant, reverse, backtrack|反义词：advance, proceed, persist, maintain|词性: Verb (primarily), Noun (less common)


USAGE->

词源:  "Back-pedal" originates from the action of pedaling backwards on a bicycle, which naturally leads to a reversal of direction. This physical action was then metaphorically applied to situations where someone reverses their opinion or position.
