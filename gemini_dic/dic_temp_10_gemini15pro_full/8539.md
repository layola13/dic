anticeremonialists:/ˌænˌtɪˌserɪˈmoʊniəlɪsts/
|n.|反仪式主义者|People who oppose or reject ceremonies, rituals, or formal customs, often viewing them as unnecessary, ostentatious, or meaningless.  They often advocate for simplicity and informality.|The anticeremonialists boycotted the elaborate coronation, preferring a simple affirmation of the new leader.|近义词:nonconformists, iconoclasts, dissenters|反义词:traditionalists, ritualists, formalists|Noun
