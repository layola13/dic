abolishers:/əˈbɒlɪʃəz/
n.
废除者，取消者
People who abolish something; those who advocate for the abolishment of a system, practice, or institution.
The abolitionists worked tirelessly to end slavery. 废奴主义者孜孜不倦地致力于结束奴隶制。

The abolitioners of the death penalty argue that it is cruel and unusual punishment. 死刑废除者认为死刑是残酷和不寻常的惩罚。

近义词: reformers, advocates, campaigners
反义词: supporters, proponents, defenders
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "abolish," which comes from the Old French "aboliss-," the past participle stem of "abolir," meaning "to abolish, annul, destroy," from Latin "abolere," from "ab-" (from, away from) + "olere" (to grow, be accustomed; to be obliged, owe).  The "-er" suffix denotes a person who performs the action of the verb.
