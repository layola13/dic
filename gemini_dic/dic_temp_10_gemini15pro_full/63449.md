entrenches:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃɪz/ | v. | 巩固，加强；使处于牢固地位；根深蒂固；挖壕沟防护 |  to establish (an attitude, habit, or belief) so firmly that change is very difficult or unlikely.  | The government's policies have become further entrenched in recent years. 政府的政策近年来得到了进一步巩固。| Synonyms: fortify, strengthen, reinforce, secure | Antonyms: weaken, undermine, erode | verb

entrenched:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃt/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；牢固的 | firmly established and difficult or unlikely to change; ingrained. |  These attitudes are deeply entrenched in our society. 这些态度在我们社会中根深蒂固。 | Synonyms: ingrained, established, fixed, rooted | Antonyms:  weak, unstable, superficial | adjective
