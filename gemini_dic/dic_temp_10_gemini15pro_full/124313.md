motherjumper:/ˈmʌðərˌdʒʌmpər/| n. |胆大妄为的人；冒失鬼；令人吃惊的人（或事物）|A person who takes surprising or unexpected risks; something startling or astonishing.  It's primarily used in informal contexts and can sometimes have a mildly negative connotation, suggesting recklessness or foolishness.|例句：That motherjumper just climbed the radio tower!  那个胆大包天的家伙竟然爬上了广播塔！

例句：The speed of that new car is a real motherjumper. 那辆新车的速度真是惊人。|近义词：daredevil, risk-taker, madcap, hotshot, firecracker|反义词:coward, timid person|词性:noun


Etymology:
The origin of "motherjumper" is uncertain, but it's likely a combination of "mother" used as an intensifier and "jumper," referring to someone who jumps or takes leaps, both literally and figuratively.  It may also be related to the older term "jumper," meaning a loose-fitting jacket or dress, suggesting someone who jumps into things quickly without much thought.
