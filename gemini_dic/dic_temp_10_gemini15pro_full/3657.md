agglomerate:/əˈɡlɒməreɪt/ (UK) /əˈɡlɑːməreɪt/ (US)| v.  n. adj.|聚集，凝聚，结块|To gather into a mass or ball; to collect into a cluster or group.  As a noun, a mass or collection of things. As an adjective, collected or formed into a mass.|The small stones agglomerated into a large boulder over time. 小石头随着时间的推移聚集成了一个大圆石。|Synonyms: accumulate, amass, aggregate, coalesce|Antonyms: disperse, scatter, separate|verb

agglomerate:/əˈɡlɒməreɪt/ (UK) /əˈɡlɑːməreɪt/ (US)| n.|聚集体，团块，凝聚物|A mass or collection of things; a cluster.|The agglomerate of dust bunnies under the bed was impressive. 床下聚集的灰尘团令人印象深刻。|Synonyms: cluster, mass, collection, aggregation|Antonyms: scattering, dispersion|noun

agglomerate:/əˈɡlɒməreɪt/ (UK) /əˈɡlɑːməreɪt/ (US)| adj.|聚集的，凝聚的|Collected or formed into a mass.|The agglomerate rock formation was formed by volcanic activity.  火山活动形成了聚集的岩层。|Synonyms: clustered, amassed, aggregated|Antonyms: dispersed, scattered|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin agglomeratus, past participle of agglomerare "to wind into a ball," from ad- "to" + glomerare "to form into a ball," from glomus "ball."
