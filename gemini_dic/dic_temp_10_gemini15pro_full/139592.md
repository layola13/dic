obvoluted: /ˈɒbvəˌluːtɪd/ (UK) /ˈɑːbvəˌluːtɪd/ (US) | adj. | 复杂的，费解的，迂回的 |  Made complex or difficult to understand; excessively convoluted.  Derived from the Latin "obvolutus," past participle of "obvolvere" (to wrap around, envelop). The prefix "ob-" intensifies the meaning, suggesting a thorough wrapping or covering that obscures the underlying simplicity. | The professor's explanation of the theory was so obvoluted that few students understood it. 教授对这个理论的解释如此复杂，以至于很少有学生理解它。

The legal document was filled with obvoluted language, making it difficult for the average person to comprehend.  法律文件充满了复杂的语言，使普通人难以理解。
