convulsing:/kənˈvʌlsɪŋ/ | v. | 抽搐，痉挛 |  Undergoing a series of involuntary muscular contractions, typically producing violent, uncontrolled movements of the body. | The patient was convulsing on the floor. 病人在地板上抽搐。|  spasming, twitching, fitting, seizing | relaxing, calming | verb

convulse:/kənˈvʌls/ | v. | (使)抽搐，痉挛 | To cause a sudden, violent, irregular movement of a limb or of the body, typically due to a medical condition. |  The seizure convulsed his entire body. 癫痫发作使他全身抽搐。 | shake, tremble, shudder, quake | soothe, calm | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *convulsus*, past participle of *convellere* "to pull violently, tear away," from *com-* "together" (see com-) + *vellere* "to pluck, pull" (see vulsion).
