词源:  "Nonfallaciousness" comes from "non-" (not) + "fallacious" (containing a fallacy, or a mistaken belief) + "-ness" (a suffix forming nouns from adjectives). "Fallacious" derives from the Latin "fallacia" (deceit, trick).

用法说明: This word is a formal term used primarily in philosophical and logical contexts to describe the state of being free from logical fallacies or errors in reasoning.  It is less common in everyday speech.
