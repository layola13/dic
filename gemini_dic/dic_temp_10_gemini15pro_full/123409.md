monophyletic:/ˌmɒnəfaɪˈlɛtɪk/ /ˌmɑːnəfaɪˈlɛtɪk/| adj.|单系的|Descended from a common evolutionary ancestor or ancestral group, especially one not shared with any other group.|Birds are a monophyletic group because they all share a common ancestor that is not the ancestor of any other extant group.|近义词：holophyletic|反义词：paraphyletic, polyphyletic|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek μόνος (mónos, “alone, single, only”) + φυλή (phylḗ, “tribe, clan, race”) + -ic.
