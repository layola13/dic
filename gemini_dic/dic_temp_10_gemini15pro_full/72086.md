floatier:/ˈfloʊ.ti.ər/ (US), /ˈfləʊ.ti.ər/ (UK)| adj.|更轻浮的，更漂浮的|Comparative form of floaty: more floaty.  Describes something that has more of a tendency to float or drift, often used figuratively to describe something light, airy, or insubstantial.|The new dress is floatier than the old one. 这件新裙子比旧的更轻飘飘的。|The music was floatier and more dreamlike.  这音乐更加飘渺梦幻。|近义词：lighter, airier, more buoyant, more ethereal|反义词：heavier, denser, more substantial|词性:adjective
Etymology: From float + -y + -er (comparative suffix).  Ultimately from Old English *flotian, from Proto-Germanic *flotan.
