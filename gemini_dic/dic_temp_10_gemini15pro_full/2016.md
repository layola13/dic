actuary: /ˈækʃʊəriː/ (UK) /ˈækʃəˌweri/ (US)| n.|精算师|A person who compiles and analyzes statistics and uses them to calculate insurance risks and premiums.|The actuary calculated the likelihood of a major earthquake in the region.  该精算师计算了该地区发生大地震的可能性。

My friend is an actuary for a large insurance company. 我的朋友是一家大型保险公司的一名精算师。|近义词: statistician, analyst|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *actuarius* "a clerk, recorder, bookkeeper," from Latin *actus* "an act, public record, legal proceeding."  Originally referred to a clerk who recorded the acts or proceedings of a court.  The modern meaning developed from the role of calculating payments related to contingencies.
