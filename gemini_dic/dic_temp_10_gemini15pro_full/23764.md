bravoes:/ˈbrɑːvoʊz/ | n. |喝彩；叫好；暴徒；亡命之徒 |  A shout of "bravo," especially one used to express approval of a performance. Also, a group of daring or reckless people, especially hired assassins.  | The bravoes shouted their approval at the end of the opera. / The bravoes were hired to eliminate the rival gang leader.  | 近义词:  cheers, applause, assassins, thugs | 反义词: boos, disapproval, law-abiding citizens | 词性: noun

bravoes:/ˈbrɑːvoʊz/ | n. |喝彩；叫好；暴徒；亡命之徒 |  Plural form of "bravo." Historically, it referred to hired assassins, especially in Italy.  More commonly, it refers to shouts of "bravo!" expressing approval. | The audience showered the performers with bravoes. /  Stories of bravoes and their daring exploits filled the taverns. | 近义词:  accolades, plaudits, hired guns | 反义词: jeers, criticisms, peaceful citizens | 词性: noun
