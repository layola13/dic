outdwelling:/ˌaʊtˈdwɛlɪŋ/
n.|外住；在外居住|The practice of living outside of a particular community or area, often while maintaining ties to it.  Often used in the context of diaspora or migration.|Many young people from rural areas choose outdwelling, seeking better opportunities in cities.|近义词：emigration, migration, relocation|反义词: immigration, repatriation|词性:名词

Etymology:
"out-" (Old English *ūt, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *ūt, *ūz) + "dwelling" (from dwell (v.), from Old English dwellan "to hinder, delay, lead astray, deceive; go astray, err").  The prefix "out-" here carries the sense of "outside" or "away from".
