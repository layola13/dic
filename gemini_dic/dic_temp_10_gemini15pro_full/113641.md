machine-gun:/məˈʃiːn ɡʌn/ /məˈʃiːn ɡʌn/| n. |机枪|An automatic gun that fires bullets rapidly and continuously.|The soldiers used a machine-gun to defend their position. 士兵们用机枪保卫他们的阵地。|近义词：automatic rifle, submachine gun|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "knife" or "sword" as contrasting weapons)|noun

machine-gun:/məˈʃiːn ɡʌn/ /məˈʃiːn ɡʌn/| v. |用机枪扫射|To shoot with a machine gun.|The enemy machine-gunned the village. 敌人用机枪扫射了村庄。|近义词：strafe, riddle (with bullets)|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "protect" or "defend")|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "machine" + "gun".  "Machine" ultimately comes from the Greek "mēkhanē" meaning "contrivance, machine." "Gun" likely originates from the name "Gunnhildr," a Scandinavian female name associated with war.
