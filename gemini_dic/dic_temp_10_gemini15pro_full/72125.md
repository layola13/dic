flood:/flʌd/| n. v.|洪水，泛滥，大量|An overflowing of a large amount of water beyond its normal confines, especially over what is normally dry land.  A large number or amount.|
The town was devastated by the flood.  这个城镇被洪水摧毁了。
The office was flooded with applications. 办公室收到了大量的申请。|近义词：deluge, inundation, torrent|反义词:drought, trickle|词性:名词,动词

flood:/flʌd/| v.|淹没，涌入，充满|To cover or become covered with water, especially in a way that causes problems. To arrive or go somewhere in large numbers or amounts.|
The river flooded the valley. 河水淹没了山谷。
Memories flooded back. 记忆涌上心头。|近义词：inundate, overwhelm, engulf|反义词:drain, empty|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *flōd (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *flōduz, from Proto-Indo-European *plew-, *plōw-, meaning "to flow, swim, float." Cognates include German Flut, Dutch vloed, and Latin pluvia ("rain").
