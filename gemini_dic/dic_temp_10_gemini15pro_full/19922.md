biochar:/ˈbaɪoʊtʃɑːr/
n.
生物炭
A charcoal-like material produced from biomass (organic matter) through pyrolysis, a process of heating in the absence of oxygen.  It is used as a soil amendment to improve soil health and sequester carbon.
Biochar can improve soil water retention. 生物炭可以提高土壤的保水性。
Biochar helps to sequester carbon in the soil. 生物炭有助于将碳封存在土壤中。
Synonyms: charcoal, biocoal
Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but perhaps: compost, fertilizer as alternative soil amendments)
noun
