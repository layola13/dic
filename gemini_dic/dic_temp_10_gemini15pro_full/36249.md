cognomen:/ˈkɑːɡˌnoʊmən/ | /ˈkɒɡˌnəʊmən/ | n. | 称号，别名 | A surname; a name added to the original name, especially as a nickname or a name that expresses a characteristic. | Gaius Julius Caesar's cognomen was Caesar. 盖乌斯·尤利乌斯·凯撒的别名是凯撒。

Julius Caesar adopted Octavian, giving him his cognomen. 尤利乌斯·凯撒收养了屋大维，并将他的别名赐给了他。

近义词: surname, nickname, moniker, epithet, appellation

反义词: (No direct antonym for a name)

词性: noun


词源:  From Latin cognōmen (meaning "surname, family name, additional name"), formed from co- (meaning "with, together") and nōmen (meaning "name").
