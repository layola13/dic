concubinacy: /kɒnˈkjuːbɪnɪsi/ /kɑːnˈkjuːbɪnəsi/
n.|妾侍关系，姘居|The state of being a concubine; the practice of keeping a concubine.  A cohabitation of a man and woman without legal or formal marriage. |In ancient Rome, concubinacy was a recognized relationship, though not as legally binding as marriage.|近义词:cohabitation, common-law marriage|反义词:marriage, monogamy|词性:noun

concubine: /ˈkɒŋkjubʌɪn/ /ˈkɑːŋkjubɑɪn/
n.|妾，姘妇|A woman who lives with a man but has lower status than his wife or wives.|The emperor had many concubines.|近义词:mistress, paramour|反义词:wife|词性:noun
