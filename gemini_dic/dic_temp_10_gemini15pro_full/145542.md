overcommercializing: /ˌoʊvər kəˈmɜːrʃəˌlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 过度商业化 |  Exploiting something excessively for profit, often at the expense of quality or value. |  The city center is becoming overcommercialized, losing its charm with all the chain stores. 城市中心正在变得过度商业化，到处都是连锁店，失去了它的魅力。| overexploiting, overselling | underselling, preserving | Verb

overcommercialized: /ˌoʊvər kəˈmɜːrʃəˌlaɪzd/ | adj. | 过度商业化的 | Having been exploited excessively for profit, often resulting in a loss of quality or value. | The once quaint seaside town is now overcommercialized and crowded.  这个曾经古雅的海滨小镇现在过度商业化，人满为患。| overexploited, oversold |  understated, authentic | Adjective


Etymology: "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "commercialize" (from "commerce" + "-ize," meaning "to make commercial").  "Commerce" comes from Old French "commerce," ultimately from Latin "commercium" ("trade, traffic").
