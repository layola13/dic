mischaracterized: /ˌmɪsˈkærəktəraɪzd/
|v.|被错误描述的，被歪曲的|to describe incorrectly or unfairly|The media mischaracterized her comments, making her seem insensitive. 媒体歪曲了她的评论，让她看起来麻木不仁。|misrepresented, distorted, falsified|accurately portrayed, faithfully represented|verb

Etymology:
mis- (prefix): from Old English, meaning "wrongly," "badly," "unfavorably"
characterize (verb): from Late Latin characterizare "to mark with a character," from Greek charakterizein, from charakter "mark, distinctive quality"
-ed (suffix): past participle marker
