liripipe:/ˈlɪrɪpaɪp/| n.|里拉管,长垂布|A long streamer or tail of a hood, especially one attached to the chaperon worn by academics in the Middle Ages.|The scholar adjusted his liripipe before addressing the crowd. 学者在向众人讲话前调整了一下他的里拉管。|Synonyms: streamer, tail, pendant, lappet|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, consider words like "cap," "hood" without a liripipe) |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *liripip, from Old French *liripipion.  Ultimate origin uncertain, possibly related to a Germanic word meaning "to lisp" or "to stammer," perhaps in a humorous allusion to the way the long tail of the hood might flap about.
