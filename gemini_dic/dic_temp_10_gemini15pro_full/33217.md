chronicling: /ˈkrɒnɪklɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkrɒnɪklɪŋ/ (UK)
n.|记录; 编年史|The act of recording events in chronological order. |His latest book is a chronicling of the civil rights movement.|Synonyms: recording, documenting, registering|Antonyms: ignoring, neglecting|noun

chronicling: /ˈkrɒnɪklɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkrɒnɪklɪŋ/ (UK)
v.|记录; 编入编年史|Present participle of "chronicle," meaning to record events in chronological order.|She is chronicling the lives of ordinary people during wartime. |Synonyms: recording, documenting, registering|Antonyms: ignoring, neglecting|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *chroniclen*, from Old French *chronique*, from Late Latin *chronica*, from Greek *khronika* (plural of *khronikon*, neuter of *khronikos* "of time"), from *khronos* "time".
