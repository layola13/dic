admirations:/ˌædməˈreɪʃənz/
noun
钦佩；赞赏；羡慕；令人赞赏的人或物
feelings of respect and approval; things that are admired
She expressed her admiration for his work. 她表达了她对他的工作的钦佩。
His courage and skill earned him the admiration of his colleagues. 他的勇气和技能赢得了同事们的钦佩。
synonyms: respect, approval, esteem, regard, appreciation
antonyms: disdain, contempt, scorn, disapproval
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin admirationem, from Latin admirari "to wonder at," from ad- "to" + mirari "to wonder," from mirus "wonderful, astonishing, strange, causing wonder."

USAGE->
Her admirations include the works of Jane Austen and the music of Mozart. = 她所钦佩的事物包括简·奥斯汀的作品和莫扎特的音乐。
He spoke of his admirations for the great explorers of the past. = 他谈到他对过去伟大探险家们的钦佩。

The garden was the admiration of all the neighbors. = 这个花园是所有邻居都羡慕的对象。
Her paintings were the admiration of the art world. = 她的画作是艺术界的瑰宝。

He received many expressions of admiration for his bravery. = 他因勇敢而受到了许多赞赏。
The project earned the team the admiration of their clients. = 这个项目为团队赢得了客户的赞赏。
