gobbetly:/ˈɡɒbɪtli/ (British English) | adv. | 一块一块地; 零碎地 | In a piecemeal or fragmented way; bit by bit. |  He ate his dinner gobbetly, picking at the food. 他一块一块地吃着晚餐，挑挑拣拣。|  She told the story gobbetly, jumping from one event to another.  她断断续续地讲述了这个故事，从一个事件跳到另一个事件。 | piecemeal, fragmentarily, bit by bit, intermittently | continuously, smoothly, fluidly | adverb

Etymology:
From "gobbet" (a lump or piece) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).  "Gobbet" comes from Old French "gobet," a diminutive of "gobe" (mouthful).  Ultimately from Frankish "*gob," related to Old High German "chuppo" (lump).
