agnosia:/æɡˈnoʊʒə/ /æɡˈnəʊziə/| n. |失认症|Loss of the ability to recognize objects, persons, sounds, shapes, or smells while the specific sense is not defective nor is there any significant memory loss. It is usually caused by brain damage.|The stroke patient experienced agnosia and could no longer recognize familiar faces.  这位中风病人患上了失认症，再也认不出熟悉的面孔了。|近义词:inability to recognize, sensory deficit|反义词:recognition, perception|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἀγνωσία (agnōsía, “ignorance”), from ἀ- (a-, “not”) + γνῶσις (gnôsis, “knowledge”).
