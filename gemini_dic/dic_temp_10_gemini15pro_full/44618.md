crowdsources:/ˈkraʊdsɔːrsɪz/
|v.|众包；群体外包|To obtain (information or input into a particular task or project) by enlisting the services of a number of people, either paid or unpaid, typically via the Internet.|The company crowdsources its product development.|近义词: crowdsource, outsource, distribute|反义词: insource|词性: verb

crowdsourcing:/ˈkraʊdsɔːrsɪŋ/
|n.|众包|The practice of obtaining information or input into a particular task or project by enlisting the services of a number of people, either paid or unpaid, typically via the Internet.|Crowdsourcing can be a cost-effective way to gather data.|近义词: open innovation, collaborative innovation, distributed problem-solving|反义词: traditional sourcing, in-house development|词性: noun

crowdsourced:/ˈkraʊdsɔːrst/
|adj.|众包的|Obtained by crowdsourcing.|The crowdsourced data was used to create a map of the city.|近义词: collaboratively developed, publicly sourced|反义词: internally developed, privately sourced|词性: adjective
