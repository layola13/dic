daisies:/ˈdeɪziz/|n.|雏菊|A small European plant with a yellow center and white petals, often considered a weed. Also refers to similar plants of the same family.|She picked a bouquet of daisies from the field. 她从田野里摘了一束雏菊。|近义词:flower, bloom|反义词:weed (in some contexts)|词性:noun

daisy:/ˈdeɪzi/|n.|极好的事物；一流的东西|Something excellent or of first-rate quality (informal).|That new car is a real daisy! 那辆新车真是一流！|近义词:excellent, first-rate, superb|反义词:awful, terrible|词性:noun

PHRASE->
pushing up daisies: 死翘翘了；入土为安了 He's been pushing up daisies for years. 他已经去世多年了。
fresh as a daisy: 精神饱满  Despite the long journey, she looked as fresh as a daisy. 尽管旅途漫长，她看起来仍然精神饱满。
ups-a-daisy:  用来鼓励小孩站起来的用语  Ups-a-daisy!  There you go!  真棒！你起来了！
daisy chain: 雏菊花环 She made a daisy chain to wear in her hair. 她做了一个雏菊花环戴在头发上。
a daisy cutter (baseball):  棒球术语，指贴地滚地球 The pitcher threw a daisy cutter that the batter grounded out on. 投手投了一个贴地滚地球，击球手出局了。


词源：来源于古英语 dægeseage,  意思是“day's eye”，因为雏菊在日出时开放，日落时闭合。
