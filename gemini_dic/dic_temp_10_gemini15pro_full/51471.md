detoured:/ˌdiːˈtʊərd/ /ˌdɪˈtʊərd/| adj. v.|绕道的，迂回的；被绕道的|diverted from a direct course or route; having been made to take a different route.|例句：The road was detoured due to construction. 由于施工，道路绕行了。|例句：We were detoured through the back roads. 我们被绕道走了偏僻小路。|近义词：rerouted, diverted, indirect|反义词：direct, straight|词性:形容词/动词

detour:/ˈdiːtʊər/ /ˈdɪtʊr/| n. v.|绕道，迂回；绕行|a deviation from a direct course or route; to cause someone or something to deviate from a direct course or route.|例句：We had to make a detour because of the accident. 因为事故，我们不得不绕道而行。|例句：The police detoured traffic around the accident site. 警察让车辆绕过事故现场。|近义词：bypass, diversion, roundabout|反义词：direct route, straight path|词性:名词/动词
