mislearns:/ˌmɪsˈlɜːrnz/
|v.|错误地学习，学错|To learn something incorrectly, forming an inaccurate or incomplete understanding.|She mislearned the capital of France and thought it was Lyon.|近义词: misunderstand, misinterpret, misapprehend|反义词: learn, master|词性:动词

Etymology:
"mis-" (Old English *mis-, from Proto-Germanic *missa-, meaning "wrongly, badly, astray") + "learn" (Old English leornian, from Proto-Germanic *liznojan, meaning "to learn, study, follow a track").
