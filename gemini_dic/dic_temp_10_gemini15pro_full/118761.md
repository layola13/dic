metallicity:/mɛtəˈlɪsɪti/| n.|金屬豐度；金屬量|The metallicity of a star is a measure of the proportion of its mass made up of elements heavier than hydrogen and helium.|The Sun has a metallicity of about 0.02, meaning that 2% of its mass is made up of elements heavier than hydrogen and helium. 太阳的金屬豐度約為 0.02，這意味著其質量的 2% 由比氫和氦重的元素組成。|Near synonyms: metal abundance, chemical composition|Antonym: (No direct antonym, but low metallicity could be considered the opposite of high metallicity)|noun
