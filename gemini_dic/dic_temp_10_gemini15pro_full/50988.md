desirefulness:/dɪˌzaɪərəblˈnɛs/ |n.|  desirability; the quality of being worthy of desire. | The desirefulness of a quiet life in the countryside appealed to her. 她向往宁静的乡村生活。| 近义词: desirability, attractiveness, appeal | 反义词: undesirability, unattractiveness, repulsion | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From desirable + -ness.  Desirable comes from the Old French *desirable (modern French désirable), from Late Latin desiderabilis "worthy of being desired," from Latin desiderare "to long for, wish for," from de- "from" (see de-) + siderari "to consider, look at closely," originally "to observe the stars," from sidus (genitive sideris) "star, constellation."
