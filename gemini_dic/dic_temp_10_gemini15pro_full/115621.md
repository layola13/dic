march:/mɑːrtʃ/ (US) /mɑːtʃ/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.|行军，三月，进行曲|A steady, rhythmic walk, especially of soldiers; the third month of the year; a piece of music with a strong regular rhythm, typically for a military band or procession.|The soldiers marched for days. 士兵们行军了几天。|近义词：walk, parade, procession, advance, progression (n.);  stride, troop, parade (v.)|反义词: retreat, withdraw (v.)|词性:名词，动词

march:/mɑːrtʃ/ (US) /mɑːtʃ/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.|进行，进展|To walk in a steady, rhythmic way, especially in a formal group; to make steady progress.|The project is marching forward. 项目正在稳步推进。|近义词：progress, advance|反义词: retreat, regress|词性:名词，动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "marcher" (to walk, trample), from Frankish "*markōn" (horse), from Proto-Germanic "*markō" (horse).  The month's name comes from Latin "Martius,"  named after Mars, the Roman god of war.
