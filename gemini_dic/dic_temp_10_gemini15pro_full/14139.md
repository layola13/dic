authoritarianisms: /əˌθɔːrɪˈtɛəriənɪzəmz/
|n.|专制主义；独裁主义；权力主义|A system of government or other social organization characterized by strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom, marked by absolute or blind obedience to authority, as against individual liberty and related social and political rights.|Many countries in the 20th century suffered under brutal authoritarianisms. 许多国家在20世纪遭受了残酷的专制主义统治。| The rise of authoritarianisms around the world is a worrying trend. 全球专制主义的兴起是一个令人担忧的趋势。|近义词: dictatorships, tyrannies, despotisms|反义词: democracies, libertarianisms|词性: 名词 (plural)

Etymology:
From authoritarian + -ism (suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine).  Authoritarian is derived from authority, from Old French autorité and Latin auctoritas.
