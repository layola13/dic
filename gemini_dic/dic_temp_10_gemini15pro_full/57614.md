draught:/drɑːft/ /dræft/| n.| 气流，通风；汇票； draught beer 生啤；草稿，草图|A current of air (usually cold air) in a room or other enclosed space.  Also refers to a preliminary version of a piece of writing; a dose of liquid medicine; the depth of water needed for a ship to float; the act of pulling a load; and (chiefly British) a serving of beer drawn from a cask.|例句：There's a terrible draught in this room.  这个房间里有股可怕的冷风。

例句：He showed me the first draught of his novel.  他给我看了他的小说的初稿。|近义词：draft, breeze, current, gust, sketch, outline, dose|反义词：stillness|词性：名词


draught:/drɑːft/ /dræft/| adj. |(啤酒)散装的，从桶里打的| (of beer) served from a cask rather than from a bottle or can.|例句：I prefer draught beer to bottled beer. 我喜欢散装啤酒胜过瓶装啤酒。

例句：The pub is known for its excellent draught Guinness. 这家酒吧以其出色的散装吉尼斯黑啤而闻名。|近义词：draught, on tap|反义词：bottled, canned|词性：形容词


词源：Middle English: from Old English *droht and Old Norse drott ‘drawing, pull’, of Germanic origin and related to drag.
