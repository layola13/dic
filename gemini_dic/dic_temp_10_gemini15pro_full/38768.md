condottiere:/ˌkɒndɒˈtjɛːri/ /ˌkɑːndəˈtjɛːri/| n.|雇佣兵首领；意大利雇佣兵|A leader of a band of mercenaries, especially in Italy in the 14th and 15th centuries.| Cesare Borgia was a notorious condottiere who used ruthless tactics to gain power. 切萨雷·博尔吉亚是一位臭名昭著的雇佣兵首领，他使用残酷的策略来获取权力。|The condottieri often switched sides depending on who offered them the best payment.  雇佣兵首领经常根据谁给他们最好的报酬而改变立场。|近义词：mercenary captain, soldier of fortune|反义词: loyal soldier, patriot|词性:noun

condottieri:/ˌkɒndɒˈtjɛːri/ /ˌkɑːndəˈtjɛːri/| n.|雇佣兵首领；意大利雇佣兵（condottiere的复数形式）|The plural form of condottiere, referring to multiple leaders of mercenary bands.| The condottieri were often more loyal to their purse than to any particular cause. 雇佣兵首领们通常对他们的钱袋比对任何特定的事业更忠诚。|Many Italian city-states relied on condottieri for their defense. 许多意大利城邦依靠雇佣兵首领进行防御。|近义词：mercenary captains, soldiers of fortune|反义词: loyal soldiers, patriots|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian condottiero, from condotto 'conduct, leadership', from Latin conductus, past participle of conducere 'to lead together, hire'.
