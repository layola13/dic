feudalists:/ˈfjuːdəlɪsts/
noun
封建主；封建主义者
People who support or benefit from feudalism, a social system in which land is held in exchange for service or labour.
The feudalists held vast estates and demanded loyalty from their vassals.  封建主们拥有广阔的土地，并要求他们的附庸效忠。
The king relied on the support of powerful feudalists to maintain his rule.  国王依靠强大的封建领主的支持来维持他的统治。
近义词：lords, nobles, aristocracy
反义词：peasants, serfs, commoners
词性：n. (noun)


feudalistic:/ˌfjuːdəˈlɪstɪk/
adjective
封建的；封建主义的
Relating to or characteristic of feudalism.
The feudalistic system dominated medieval Europe. 封建制度统治着中世纪的欧洲。
The novel portrays the injustices of a feudalistic society. 这部小说描绘了一个封建社会的不公正。
近义词：hierarchical, aristocratic
反义词：democratic, egalitarian
词性：adj. (adjective)


ETYMOLOGY->
From *feudal* + *-ist*.  *Feudal* comes from Medieval Latin *feudalis*, from *feudum* "fief." The *-ist* suffix denotes a person who adheres to or practices a particular doctrine or system.
