mammalia:/məˈmeɪliə/ | n. | 哺乳动物 | Mammals are a class of vertebrate animals characterized by the possession of mammary glands which in females produce milk for nourishing (nursing) their young, a neocortex (a region of the brain), fur or hair, and three middle ear bones.  |例句：Blue whales are the largest mammalia. 蓝鲸是最大的哺乳动物。

例句：Humans also belong to the class Mammalia. 人类也属于哺乳动物纲。|近义词：mammals |反义词: (无明确的反义词，可以考虑其他动物类别，例如：reptiles 爬行动物, amphibians 两栖动物) |词性:名词

Etymology:  From New Latin *Mammalia,  from Modern Latin mammalis (“of the breast”), from Latin mamma (“breast, udder”). Coined by Carl Linnaeus in 1758.
