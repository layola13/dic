USAGE->
Simple Present:
What do you do for a living? = 你做什么工作？
What does he like to eat? = 他喜欢吃什么？

Present Continuous:
What are you watching on TV? = 你在电视上看什么？
What is she cooking for dinner? = 她晚饭在做什么？

Present Perfect:
What have you learned recently? = 你最近学了什么？
What has he done to his hair? = 他对他的头发做了什么？

Present Perfect Continuous:
What have you been working on lately? = 你最近一直在做什么？
What has she been reading all day? = 她一整天都在读什么？

Simple Past:
What did you do yesterday? = 你昨天做了什么？
What did she say to you? = 她对你说了什么？

Past Continuous:
What were you doing when the phone rang? = 电话响的时候你在做什么？
What was she thinking about when I saw her? = 我看到她的时候她在想什么？

Past Perfect:
What had you done before you left? = 你离开之前做了什么？
What had she eaten before she got sick? = 她生病之前吃了什么？

Past Perfect Continuous:
What had you been doing before I interrupted you? = 我打断你之前你一直在做什么？
What had she been studying before she changed her major? = 她换专业之前一直在学什么？

Simple Future:
What will you do next weekend? = 你下周末要做什么？
What will the weather be like tomorrow? = 明天天气会怎么样？

Future Continuous:
What will you be doing this time next week? = 下周这个时候你会在做什么？
What will she be studying next semester? = 她下学期会学什么？

Future Perfect:
What will you have accomplished by the end of the year? = 到年底你会完成什么？
What will she have learned by the time she graduates? = 到她毕业的时候她会学到什么？

Future Perfect Continuous:
What will you have been doing for five years by 2028? = 到2028年你将已经做这件事五年了？
What will she have been studying by the time she gets her PhD? = 到她获得博士学位时，她将已经学习什么专业多久了？

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hwæt (which also gave rise to "who," "how," "why"), ultimately from Proto-Germanic *hwat, from Proto-Indo-European *kwo-.  Relates to words in other Indo-European languages meaning "who," "what," or "which."
