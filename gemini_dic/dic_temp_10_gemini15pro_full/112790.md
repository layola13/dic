lubricate:/ˈluːbrɪkeɪt/ | v. | 润滑；使…顺畅 | To apply a substance such as oil or grease to (a machine or engine) to reduce friction and allow smooth movement. |  The mechanic lubricated the engine. 机械师给发动机上了润滑油。|  The host lubricated the conversation with jokes. 主持人用笑话让谈话更顺畅。| synonyms: oil, grease, smooth | antonyms:  dry, seize, stick | verb

lubrication:/ˌluːbrɪˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 润滑；润滑作用 | The action of applying a substance such as oil or grease to (a machine or engine) to reduce friction and allow smooth movement. | Regular lubrication is essential for the proper functioning of the engine.  定期润滑对发动机的正常运转至关重要。| The lubrication of social interactions with polite conversation.  用礼貌的谈话润滑社交互动。| synonyms: oiling, greasing | antonyms: drying | noun

lubricant:/ˈluːbrɪkənt/ | n. | 润滑剂；润滑油 | A substance such as oil or grease used to reduce friction and allow smooth movement. |  The mechanic used a high-quality lubricant on the gears. 机械师在齿轮上使用了高质量的润滑剂。 |  Kind words can be a social lubricant.  友善的话语可以成为社交润滑剂。 | synonyms: oil, grease | antonyms: desiccant | noun
