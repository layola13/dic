belaying:/bɪˈleɪɪŋ/
|n.|保护, 确保；支撑, 固定|The act of securing a climber with a rope.  This involves controlling the rope through a device to limit the distance of a fall.|He checked his belaying device before starting the climb. 他在开始攀登前检查了他的保护装置。|近义词: securing, safeguarding, roping|反义词: releasing, freeing|词性:名词, 动名词
belay:/bɪˈleɪ/
|v.|保护, 确保；支撑, 固定|To secure a climber with a rope, controlling it through a device.|She belayed her partner as he ascended the rock face. 她在她同伴攀登岩壁时为他确保安全。|近义词: secure, safeguard, rope|反义词: release, free|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bilaien*, from *be-* + *lay* "to arrange."  Originally meaning "to besiege, surround," it later evolved to mean "to fasten" or "to make fast," specifically a rope.  The modern climbing sense emerged in the 19th century.
