disciplinarity:/ˌdɪsəplɪˈnærɪti/ | n. |学科性，规训性|The state of being disciplinary or the quality of pertaining to a particular discipline.  It refers to the ways in which knowledge is produced, validated, and disseminated within specific fields of study.|The increasing disciplinarity of academic research has led to greater specialization. 学术研究日益增长的学科性导致了更大的专业化。|近义词: specialization, compartmentalization |反义词: interdisciplinarity, multidisciplinarity |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *disciplinarité*, from *disciplinaire* ("disciplinary") + *-ité* (suffix forming abstract nouns).  Ultimately from Latin *disciplīna* ("instruction, knowledge").
