excerpt:/ˈeksɜːrpt/ /ˈɛksɜːrpt/ | n. | 摘录，节选 | A passage or quotation taken or selected from a book, film, or piece of music. |  Example 1:  The professor read an excerpt from Shakespeare's Hamlet. 教授朗读了莎士比亚《哈姆雷特》的节选。

Example 2: This excerpt from her new novel is quite intriguing. 她新小说的这段摘录非常引人入胜。 | synonyms: extract, passage, selection, quotation, fragment, snippet | antonyms: whole, entirety, complete work | noun


excerpt:/ɪkˈsɜːrpt/ /ɛkˈsɜːrpt/ | vt. | 摘录，节选 | To take a passage or segment from a longer work. | Example 1:  I excerpted a few key paragraphs from the report. 我从报告中摘录了几个关键段落。

Example 2:  The journalist excerpted a section of the interview for her article.  这位记者摘录了采访的一部分内容用于她的文章。 | synonyms: extract, select, quote, cite, lift | antonyms: insert, include, add | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin excerptus, past participle of excerpere "to pick out," from ex- "out" + carpere "to pluck, harvest."
