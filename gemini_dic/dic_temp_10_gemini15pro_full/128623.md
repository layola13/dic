nickeled-and-dimed:/ˈnɪkəld ænd daɪmd/| adj.|被榨取每一分钱的，斤斤计较的|Refers to a situation where someone is charged for many small, often unnecessary, things, resulting in a significant overall cost.  Derived from the small denominations of nickel and dime coins.|The airline's baggage fees and extra charges for everything left me feeling nickeled and dimed.|近义词：exploited, overcharged, ripped off|反义词：generous, charitable|词性:形容词

nickel:/ˈnɪkəl/| n.|镍, 五分镍币|A silvery-white metallic chemical element and a coin of the US and Canada worth five cents.|I paid a nickel for the gum.|近义词：(for the coin) five cents|反义词: (no direct antonym)|词性:名词

dime:/daɪm/| n.|一角硬币|A coin of the US and Canada worth ten cents.|Can I borrow a dime?|近义词：(for the coin) ten cents|反义词: (no direct antonym)|词性:名词
