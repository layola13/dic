obligement:/əˈblaɪdʒmənt/ |n.|义务，责任，恩惠|A feeling of being indebted or grateful because of a favor or service; something that someone is morally or legally bound to do.| I felt a deep sense of obligement to him for his help. 我对他提供的帮助深感感激。|Synonyms: obligation, duty, debt, responsibility, favor|Antonyms: disobligation, irresponsibility, right|noun

obligement:/əˈblaɪdʒmənt/ |n.|强制，强迫|The act of obliging or compelling someone to do something.|The government imposed an obligement on all citizens to carry identification. 政府强制所有公民携带身份证。|Synonyms: compulsion, coercion, constraint|Antonyms: freedom, liberty, option|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French obligement, from obliger "to oblige," from Latin obligare "to bind, tie up," from ob- "toward" + ligare "to bind."
