demised:/dɪˈmaɪzd/
v.|转让的；遗赠的；出租的|Transferred or leased by means of a demise.  The term often appears in legal documents related to property.|The property was demised to the tenant for a period of 99 years.|近义词：leased, transferred, bequeathed, conveyed|反义词：retained, withheld
词性:v. (past participle)

demised:/dɪˈmaɪzd/
adj.|已故的| Deceased; dead. Used primarily in formal or legal contexts.|The demised king's wishes were carried out by his son.|近义词：deceased, late, departed|反义词：living, extant
词性:adj.

demised:/dɪˈmaɪzd/
n.|死亡；财产转让|Death, especially of a royal person or distinguished individual. Also, the transfer of an estate by lease or will.|The demise of the king led to a period of instability. / The demise of the property to the heir was clearly outlined in the will.|近义词：death, decease, transfer, conveyance|反义词：birth, life, retention
词性:n.
