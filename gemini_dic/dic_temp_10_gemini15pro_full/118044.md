menagerie:/məˈnæʒəri/ |n.|动物园；收藏的珍奇动物|A collection of wild or foreign animals, especially for exhibition. |The duke kept a private menagerie on his estate. 公爵在他的庄园里养了一个私人动物园。|Synonyms: zoo, collection, assortment|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps wilderness)|Noun

词源: From French *ménagerie*, from *ménager* "to manage, handle, husband," from Latin *mansionaticum* "dwelling, residence," from *mansio* "a staying, dwelling."  Originally referred to a household, then to the management of a household or farm, including the animals. Later narrowed to refer specifically to a collection of animals kept for display.
