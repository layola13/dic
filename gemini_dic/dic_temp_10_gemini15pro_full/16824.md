bases:/ˈbeɪsiːz/ (US), /ˈbeɪsiːz/ (UK)| n.|基础; 基地;  碱|The plural of "basis".  It refers to the underlying support or foundation of something, a center of operations, or (in chemistry) substances that accept protons.|

例句：
The bases of the sculpture were made of marble.
雕塑的底座是大理石做的。

The company has several military bases overseas.
这家公司在海外有多个军事基地。

Strong bases can neutralize acids.
强碱可以中和酸。


近义词：foundations, grounds, headquarters, alkalis|反义词:acids (in chemistry)|词性:noun


base:/ˈbeɪs/ (US), /ˈbeɪs/ (UK)| n. vt. adj.|基础; 基地;  卑鄙的; 以...作基础|A single "basis". Refers to the bottom support of something, a center of operations, or (in chemistry) a substance that accepts protons. As a verb, it means to establish a base or headquarters. As an adjective, it means without moral principles.|

例句：
The base of the lamp is unstable.
灯的底座不稳。

He based his argument on solid evidence.
他的论点基于确凿的证据。

That was a base and cowardly act.
那是一个卑鄙懦弱的行为。


近义词：foundation, ground, headquarters, alkali, despicable (adj.)|反义词:acid (n., in chemistry), noble (adj.)|词性:noun, verb, adjective
