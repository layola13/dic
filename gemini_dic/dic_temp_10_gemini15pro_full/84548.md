ETYMOLOGY->
From "hammer" (n.) + "away" (adv.). "Hammer" originates from Old English "hamer," from Proto-Germanic "*hamaraz," ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*kō(m)-," meaning "to strike." "Away" comes from Old English "onweg," meaning "on the way," from "on" (on) + "weg" (way).
