confessionals: /kənˈfɛʃənəlz/ | n. | 忏悔室；告解室；忏悔录 |  A place, especially a small private booth in a church, where a person can confess their sins to a priest. Also refers to things of the nature of a confession, such as a piece of writing or a speech. |  The priest listened patiently in the confessional.  神父在忏悔室里耐心地听着。| synonyms: confession boxes, confession booths | antonyms:  None (The concept doesn't have a direct opposite.) | noun

confessional: /kənˈfɛʃənəl/ | adj. |  忏悔的；告解的 | Relating to confession or the act of confessing. |  He adopted a confessional tone in his autobiography.  他在自传中采取了一种忏悔的语气。| synonyms: penitential, contrite | antonyms: unrepentant, unapologetic | adjective
