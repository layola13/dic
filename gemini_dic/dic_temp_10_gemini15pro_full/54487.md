disinter:/ˌdɪsɪnˈtɜːr/ (US), /ˌdɪsɪnˈtɜː/ (UK)| v. |掘出；发掘；使重现|To dig up (something, especially a corpse) from the earth; to unearth; to bring back from obscurity or oblivion. |例句：The archaeologists disinterred a Viking ship from the burial mound. 考古学家从墓冢中挖掘出一艘维京船。

例句：Old family feuds were disinterred during the property dispute. 在财产纠纷中，旧的家族恩怨被重新提起。|近义词：exhume, unearth, uncover, resurrect|反义词：bury, inter, conceal|词性：动词

disinterred:/ˌdɪsɪnˈtɜːrd/ (US), /ˌdɪsɪnˈtɜːd/ (UK)| adj. |被挖掘出的；被重新提起的|Having been dug up from the earth; having been brought back from obscurity or oblivion. |例句：The disinterred bones were carefully examined by the forensic team.  被挖掘出的骨头经过法医小组的仔细检查。

例句：The disinterred documents revealed a long-forgotten scandal. 被重新提起的文档揭露了一桩早已被遗忘的丑闻。|近义词：exhumed, unearthed, resurrected|反义词：buried, interred, concealed|词性：形容词
