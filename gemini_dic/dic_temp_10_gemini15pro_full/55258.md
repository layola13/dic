dissimule:/dɪˈsɪmjʊleɪt/ /ˈdɪsɪˌmjuːl/| v.|掩饰，假装，隐藏|To disguise or conceal one's true motives, feelings, or intentions.|He dissimulated his anger with a forced smile. 他强颜欢笑，掩饰内心的愤怒。|She dissimulated her fear by pretending to be confident. 她假装自信来掩饰她的恐惧。|近义词：conceal, disguise, mask, feign|反义词：reveal, expose, disclose|词性:动词
dissimulation:/ˌdɪsɪmjʊˈleɪʃn/ /ˌdɪsɪˌmjuːˈleɪʃn/| n.|掩饰，假装|The act of dissembling; concealment of one's true motives, feelings, or character.|Her dissimulation was so perfect that no one suspected her true intentions. 她的掩饰天衣无缝，以至于没有人怀疑她的真实意图。|The politician's dissimulation about his involvement in the scandal was ultimately exposed.  这位政客对卷入丑闻的掩饰最终被揭露。|近义词：deception, pretense, hypocrisy|反义词：honesty, candor, openness|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dissimulāre, from Latin dissimilis (“unlike, dissimilar”) + simulāre (“to imitate, copy, pretend”), from similis (“like, similar”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *(t)sem- (“one; as one, together with”).
