Etymology:
From "over" + "speculative" + "-ness" + "-es"

* "Over-" (prefix):  Meaning "excessive" or "too much."
* "Speculative" (adjective): From Latin "speculativus,"  meaning "contemplative," from "speculatus," past participle of "speculari," meaning "to look at, observe, contemplate."
* "-ness" (suffix): Forms nouns from adjectives, denoting a state or quality.
* "-es" (suffix):  Forms the plural of nouns ending in "-ness."
