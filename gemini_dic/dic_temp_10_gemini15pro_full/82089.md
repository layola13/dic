groaning:/ˈɡroʊnɪŋ/ (US) /ˈɡrəʊnɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. n.| 呻吟的，抱怨的；呻吟声|A low, guttural sound expressing pain, discomfort, or disapproval. Also, the act of making such a sound. |The groaning of the old floorboards underfoot was unnerving.  地板下旧地板的呻吟声令人不安。|近义词：moaning, complaining, grumbling|反义词：cheering, rejoicing|词性: adjective, noun
groan:/ɡroʊn/ (US) /ɡrəʊn/ (UK)| v. n.| 呻吟，抱怨；呻吟声|To make a low, guttural sound expressing pain, discomfort, or disapproval. Also, the sound itself. |He groaned in pain as he lifted the heavy box. 他抬着沉重的箱子时痛苦地呻吟着。|近义词：moan, complain, grumble|反义词：cheer, rejoice|词性: verb, noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *grōnen*, from Old English *grānian* (“to groan, lament”), from Proto-Germanic *grōnaną* (“to groan”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵrēh₁- (“to roar, shout”). Cognate with Dutch *gronen* (“to groan”), German *greinen* (“to weep, whine”), Old Norse *grōna* (“to sniffle, whimper”).
