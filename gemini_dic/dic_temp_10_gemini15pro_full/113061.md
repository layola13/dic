lumpily:/ˈlʌmpɪli/| adv.|块状地，疙疙瘩瘩地|in a lumpy manner; with lumps.|The mashed potatoes were lumpily made and not very appetizing. 土豆泥做得疙疙瘩瘩的，不太开胃。|Synonyms: unevenly, roughly, clumsily|Antonyms: smoothly, evenly, uniformly|词性:副词

lumpily:/ˈlʌmpɪli/| adv.|笨拙地，粗糙地|in a clumsy or awkward manner.|He walked lumpily down the stairs, still half asleep. 他笨手笨脚地走下楼梯，仍然睡眼惺忪。|Synonyms: awkwardly, clumsily, heavily|Antonyms: gracefully, smoothly, deftly|词性:副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From lump + -ily.  "Lump" comes from Middle English *lumpe, of uncertain origin, possibly from a Scandinavian source.  "-ily" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives ending in -y.
