edification:/ˌedɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/
n.|教化，启迪，陶冶|The process of improving someone's mind or character by providing moral instruction or intellectual stimulation.|Reading good literature can be a source of edification. 阅读优秀的文学作品可以陶冶情操。|instruction, enlightenment, improvement|corruption, degradation|noun

edify:/ˈedɪfaɪ/
v.|教化，启迪，陶冶|To improve someone's mind or character by providing moral instruction or intellectual stimulation.|The teacher hoped to edify her students through literature. 老师希望通过文学来启迪她的学生。|instruct, enlighten, improve|corrupt, degrade|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: via Old French from late Latin aedificatio(n-), from aedificare ‘to build’ (see edify).
