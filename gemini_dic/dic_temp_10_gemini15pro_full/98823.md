intellectualnesses:/ˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəlnəsɪz/ | n. |  智力，才智（罕用，通常用intellect或intellectualism）| The quality of being intellectual; intellect.  This word is rarely used and considered awkward. It's better to use "intellect" or "intellectualism." |  His intellectualnesses surprised everyone. (Awkward usage) 他的才智让每个人都感到惊讶。（用法不自然）| 近义词: intellect, intellectualism, intelligence, brainpower | 反义词: ignorance, stupidity | 词性: 名词


词源: From intellectual + -ness.  "Intellectual" comes from Late Latin intellectualis, from intellectus "understanding," past participle of intelligere "to understand." "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns meaning "state or quality of being."
