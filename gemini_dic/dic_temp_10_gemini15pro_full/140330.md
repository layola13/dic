ogygian:/oʊˈdʒɪdʒiən/ | adj. | 巨大的；极古老的；洪荒时代的 | Of or relating to a vast period of time; extremely old; primeval. |  The ogygian redwood forests evoke a sense of awe and wonder.  巨大的红杉林唤起人们敬畏和惊叹之情。 |  ancient, primeval, primordial, archaic | modern, recent, contemporary | adjective

Etymology:
From Greek *Ōgygēs* (Ὠγύγης), a mythical king of Thebes during a great flood, + -ian (suffix forming adjectives).  The flood of Ogyges was a legendary deluge in ancient Greek mythology that predated the more famous flood of Deucalion.
