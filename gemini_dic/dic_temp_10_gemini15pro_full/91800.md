hyperfederalists:/ˌhaɪpərˈfɛdərəlɪsts/ | n. | 极端联邦主义者 | Supporters of a strong central government and advocates for greater federal power over states' rights, particularly during the early years of the United States. | The hyperfederalists believed a powerful national government was essential for the stability and success of the new nation. | 近义词: centralists, nationalists | 反义词: antifederalists, states' rights advocates | 词性: 名词

词源: "hyper-" (Greek: over, excessive) + "federalist" (from "federal," ultimately from Latin "foedus," meaning "treaty, covenant").
