crepuscule: /krɪˈpʌskjuːl/ /krəˈpʌskjʊl/ | n. |暮色；黄昏；朦胧状态|The period of twilight; dusk.  A state of dimness or obscurity.|例句：The crepuscule settled over the valley, painting the sky in hues of orange and purple.  暮色笼罩着山谷，将天空染成橙色和紫色。

例句：He emerged from the library into the crepuscule, blinking in the fading light. 他从图书馆里出来，走进暮色中，在逐渐消失的光线下眨着眼睛。|近义词：twilight, dusk, sundown, evening|反义词:dawn, daybreak, sunrise|词性:名词

Etymology:
From French *crépuscule*, from Latin *crepusculum* ("twilight, dusk"), diminutive of *creper* ("dark, dusky"). Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European *krep-/*, meaning "dark".
