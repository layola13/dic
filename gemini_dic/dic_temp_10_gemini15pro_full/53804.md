disconcert:/ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrt/ | v. | 使不安；使惊慌；使困惑 | To disturb the composure of; unsettle:  | His silence disconcerted her. 他的沉默让她感到不安。|  Synonyms:  unsettle, disturb, ruffle, perturb, discompose, confuse | Antonyms:  calm, soothe, reassure, comfort | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *desconcerter*, from des- (expressing reversal) + concerter (to arrange, agree upon).  Ultimately from Latin *conserere* (to join or knit together).
