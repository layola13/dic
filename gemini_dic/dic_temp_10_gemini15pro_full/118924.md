metempiricism:/ˌmɛtəmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/
|n.|元经验主义，超经验主义|A philosophical position that claims that there are aspects of reality that are beyond the reach of empirical investigation, but are still knowable through other means, such as intuition, revelation, or a priori reasoning.|Metempiricism suggests that some truths are beyond the scope of science.|近义词:transcendentalism, metaphysics|反义词:empiricism, positivism|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek *μετά* (metá, “beyond, after, among”) + *ἐμπειρία* (empeiría, “experience”) + *-ισμός* (-ismós, abstract noun suffix).
