co-optive:/koʊˈɑːptɪv/
adjective
吸收的，拉拢的，采纳的
Tending or seeking to co-opt.  Involving the adoption of outside elements or influences.
The co-optive power of the group was evident in its ability to absorb new members with differing viewpoints.  这个团体吸收不同观点新成员的能力，显示出其强大的拉拢力。

The company's co-optive strategy involved incorporating customer feedback into product development. 该公司的采纳策略包括将客户反馈纳入产品开发。
近义词:  assimilative, incorporative, integrating
反义词:  exclusive, rejecting, alienating
词性: adjective

co-opt:/koʊˈɑːpt/
verb
吸收，拉拢，采纳
To appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members. To adopt (an idea or policy) for one's own use.
The committee co-opted several new members to broaden its expertise. 委员会吸收了几名新成员以扩展其专业知识。

The opposition party co-opted the government's proposals on education reform. 反对党采纳了政府的教育改革方案。
近义词:  appropriate, adopt, embrace
反义词:  reject, exclude, oppose
词性: verb


词源:
From Latin co-optāre, from co- ‘together’ + optāre ‘choose, desire’.
