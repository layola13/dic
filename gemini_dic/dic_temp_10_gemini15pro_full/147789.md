overprosperousness:/ˌōvərprɒˈspɛrəsnəs/ | n. | 过度繁荣，过分兴旺 | A state of excessive prosperity, often implying an unsustainable or unhealthy level of wealth. | The overprosperousness of the Roaring Twenties ultimately led to the Great Depression.  二十年代的过度繁荣最终导致了大萧条。| 近义词：affluence, opulence, exuberance | 反义词：poverty, impoverishment, depression | 词性：名词

Etymology:
From over- + prosperous + -ness.  "Over-" signifies "excessive" or "too much." "Prosperous" comes from the Latin "prosperus," meaning "successful" or "fortunate." "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives.
