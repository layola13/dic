crusader:/kruːˈseɪdər/| n. |十字军战士；拥护者；改革者|A person who campaigns vigorously for political, social, or religious change; a participant in one of the religious wars between Christians and Muslims in the 11th, 12th, and 13th centuries.|例句：Richard the Lionheart was a famous crusader.  狮心王理查是一位著名的十字军战士。

例句：He's a crusader for educational reform. 他是一位教育改革的拥护者。|近义词：campaigner, advocate, reformer, champion|反义词：opponent, antagonist|词性:noun

crusades:/kruːˈseɪdz/| n. |十字军东征；斗争，运动|A medieval military expedition, one of a series made by Europeans to recover the Holy Land from the Muslims in the 11th, 12th, and 13th centuries; a vigorous campaign for political, social, or religious change.|例句：The Crusades lasted for several centuries. 十字军东征持续了几个世纪。

例句：She's on a crusade against smoking. 她正在进行一场反对吸烟的运动。|近义词：campaign, struggle, fight|反义词：surrender, concession|词性:noun
