disfavours:/dɪsˈfeɪvəz/
| n. |不喜欢，不赞成；冷遇；不利条件|Disfavour is a feeling of disapproval or dislike. It can also refer to a situation that puts someone at a disadvantage.|His proposal met with disfavour from the committee. 他的提议遭到委员会的反对。|近义词：dislike, disapproval, objection, prejudice, bias|反义词：favour, approval, support, preference
|词性:名词


disfavour:/dɪsˈfeɪvə(r)/
| vt. |不喜欢，不赞成；冷落|To disfavour something is to regard it with disapproval or dislike. To disfavour someone is to treat them with prejudice or neglect.|The coach disfavoured the younger players. 教练不喜欢年轻球员。|近义词：dislike, oppose, reject|反义词：favour, support, prefer
|词性:动词

词源:
From dis- + favour.  The prefix "dis-" adds a negative meaning. "Favour" comes from Old French "favor" and ultimately from Latin "favor" meaning "goodwill, kindness, approval."
