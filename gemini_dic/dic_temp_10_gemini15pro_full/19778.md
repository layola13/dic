billow:/ˈbɪloʊ/ (US), /ˈbɪləʊ/ (UK)| n. v.|巨浪，波涛汹涌；（像波浪般）翻滚，飘扬|A large wave or a mass of something that swells and rolls like a wave. billow:/ˈbɪloʊ/ (US), /ˈbɪləʊ/ (UK)| v.|（像波浪般）翻滚，飘扬|To swell or roll outward like a wave.|The sails billowed in the wind.  风帆在风中 billow billow。|近义词：surge, swell, roll, waft|反义词:flatten, recede|词性:verb

词源: From Old Norse *bylgja, from Proto-Germanic *bulgijō, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰelǵʰ- (“to swell”). Cognate with Old English bylge (“billow, wave”), Old Saxon bulgia, Old High German bulga (“leather bag, sack”), German Balg (“bellows, bag”), Dutch balg (“skin, hide, bellows”), Gothic 𐌱𐌰𐌻𐌲𐍃 (balgs, “leather bottle, wineskin”).
