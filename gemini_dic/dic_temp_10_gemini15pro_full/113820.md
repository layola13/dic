madcap:/ˈmædˌkæp/ | adj. n. | 疯狂的，鲁莽的；鲁莽的人 |  (adj.) Impulsive and reckless; (n.) A person who acts impulsively and recklessly.  Characterized by wild or reckless behavior.  Originates from "mad" (insane) and "cap" (head).  | He's always been a bit of a madcap, jumping into things without thinking. 他总是有点鲁莽，做事不经思考。 |  She embarked on a madcap scheme to win the lottery. 她开始了一个疯狂的计划来赢得彩票。 | 近义词: reckless, impulsive, harebrained, wild, foolhardy | 反义词: cautious, careful, sensible, prudent | 词性: adjective, noun
