aulnager:/ˈɔːlnədʒər/ | n. | 检查员；量布员 | An official appointed to inspect and measure woolen cloth. | The aulnager ensured the quality of the cloth before it was sold. 量布员在布匹出售前确保其质量。| The aulnager stamped the cloth to certify its length. 量布员在布匹上盖章以证明其长度。|  Synonym: inspector, examiner | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "manufacturer" in a loose sense) | noun

Etymology: From Old French "aulnage" (measurement of cloth by the ell), ultimately from Latin "ulna" (ell, forearm).
