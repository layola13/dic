disenchanter:/ˌdɪsɪnˈtʃæntər/ | n. |  使人清醒的人/事物； disillusioner | Someone or something that disenchants someone makes them stop believing in something or someone that they previously admired or respected. | The Vietnam War was a major disenchanter for many Americans. 越南战争使许多美国人不再抱有幻想。 | 近义词: disillusioner, undeceiver | 反义词: enchanter, charmer | 词性: noun

disenchant:/ˌdɪsɪnˈtʃænt/ | vt. | 使幻想破灭；使不再抱有幻想 | To disenchant someone means to make them stop believing in something or someone that they previously admired or respected. |  The experience disenchanted her with politics. 这次经历让她对政治不再抱有幻想。 | 近义词: disillusion, undeceive | 反义词: enchant, charm | 词性: verb
