ecphrastic:/ɛkˈfræstɪk/
adjective
描述艺术作品的；生动的
Relating to or involving the verbal description of a work of visual art, especially a work of art.  Vividly describing or evoking a visual scene or work of art.
例句：The poem is an ecphrastic response to a painting by Monet. 这首诗是对莫奈一幅画的描述性回应。

例句：Her ecphrastic prose vividly brought the ancient sculpture to life. 她生动的描述性散文将古代雕塑栩栩如生地展现出来。
近义词：descriptive, vivid, evocative
反义词：nondescript, dull, unimaginative
词性：adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *ekphrastikos* "descriptive," from *ekphrazein* "to describe vividly, proclaim," from *ek-* "out" + *phrazein* "to tell, declare."
