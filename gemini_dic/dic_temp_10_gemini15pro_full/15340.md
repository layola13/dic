backstaff: /ˈbækˌstæf/ | n. | 背杖，反照象限仪 | An instrument formerly used for taking the altitude of the sun by observing the shadow cast by the vane on a graduated staff.  It allowed observation of the sun without the observer having to look directly at it, thus avoiding eye damage. | Sailors used the backstaff to determine their latitude.  水手们使用背杖来确定他们的纬度。 | Synonyms: quadrant, astrolabe, sextant | Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, perhaps modern GPS) | noun

backstaff: /ˈbækˌstæf/ | n. | (古)梯子 | A staff placed against the back of a cart or wagon. | The farmer used a backstaff to climb onto the hay wagon. 农民用梯子爬上干草车。| Synonyms: ladder, ramp | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym) | noun
