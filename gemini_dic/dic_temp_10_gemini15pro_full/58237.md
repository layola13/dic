druidism:/ˈdruːɪdɪzəm/ /ˈdruːɪˌdɪzəm/
n.|德鲁伊教|A religion practiced by the ancient Celts of Gaul, Britain, and Ireland, characterized by belief in the power of nature, particularly trees, and involving rituals performed by priests called druids.|Modern druidism draws inspiration from the ancient Celtic religion.|现代德鲁伊教从古代凯尔特宗教中汲取灵感。|paganism, Wicca|Christianity, Islam|noun
