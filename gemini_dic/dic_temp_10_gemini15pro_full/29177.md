cashed-in:/kæʃt ˈɪn/ | v. |兑现，变现；利用（机会、优势）；（因伤病或年老）放弃，退出；套现；|He cashed in his chips after the company went public.  他公司上市后就套现了股票。 |Synonyms: capitalize on, exploit, redeem, liquidate |Antonyms: invest, save, hold| verb

cashed-in:/kæʃt ˈɪn/ | adj. | 兑现的，变现的；利用了的；放弃的，退出的 | Feeling cashed-in, the athlete decided to retire. 感到力不从心，这位运动员决定退役。| Synonyms: spent, exhausted, finished | Antonyms: active, engaged, invested | adjective

Etymology:  "cash in" originates from the practice of exchanging gambling chips for cash.  The metaphorical meaning of taking advantage of a situation or opportunity developed later.
