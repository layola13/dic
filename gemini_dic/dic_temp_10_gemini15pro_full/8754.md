antidiuretic:/ˌæntiˌdaɪjʊˈrɛtɪk/| adj. n.|抗利尿的，抗利尿剂|Preventing or reducing the excretion of urine.|The doctor prescribed an antidiuretic medication to help control his frequent urination. 医生给他开了抗利尿药来控制他频繁的排尿。|近义词：antidiuretic hormone (ADH), vasopressin|反义词:diuretic|词性:adjective, noun

antidiuretic:/ˌæntiˌdaɪjʊˈrɛtɪk/| n.|抗利尿激素|A hormone that helps control the balance of water in the body by reducing the amount of urine produced.|The antidiuretic hormone plays a crucial role in regulating fluid balance. 抗利尿激素在调节体液平衡中起着至关重要的作用。|近义词：vasopressin, ADH|反义词:diuretic|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + diuretic.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposite," derived from Greek.  "Diuretic" comes from the Greek "diouretikos," meaning "promoting urine," from "dia," meaning "through," and "ourein," meaning "to urinate."
