imagisms:/ˈɪmədʒɪzəmz/
n.
意象主义
A movement in early 20th-century Anglo-American poetry that favored precision of imagery and clear, sharp language.
The poet's work was a clear example of imagisms, focusing on vivid descriptions of nature. 这位诗人的作品是意象主义的鲜明例证，侧重于对自然的生动描写。
近义词:modernism, symbolism
反义词:romanticism
词性:noun

Etymology:  From *image* + *-ism*.  The term was coined by Ezra Pound around 1912.
