ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin lymphaticus (“inspired, frenzied”), from Latin lympha (“water, water nymph”), from Ancient Greek νύμφη (númphē, “nymph, bride, young woman”).  The sense shifted to "lymph" due to the clear, watery appearance of the fluid.
