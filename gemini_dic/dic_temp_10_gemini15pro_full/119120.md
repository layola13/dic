metropolitanate:/ˌmɛtrəˈpɒlɪtənɪt/ /ˌmɛtrəˈpɑːlɪtənɪt/
n.
the office or see of a metropolitan bishop; the province or jurisdiction of a metropolitan
大都市教区；大主教的职位或教座；大都市的管辖范围
The metropolitanate of Canterbury covers a large area of Southern England.  坎特伯雷大都市教区覆盖了英格兰南部的大片地区。
The historian studied the history of the medieval metropolitanate. 这位历史学家研究了中世纪大都市教区的历史。
近义词: archdiocese, province
反义词: diocese, parish
词性: noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *metropolitanatus, from Late Latin metropolitanus (“metropolitan”), from Ancient Greek μητροπολίτης (mētropolítēs, “metropolitan bishop”), from μήτηρ (mḗtēr, “mother”) + πόλις (pólis, “city”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *méh₂tēr (“mother”) and *ptólis (“city, fortified place”).
