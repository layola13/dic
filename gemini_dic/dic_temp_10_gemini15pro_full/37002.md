columbine: /ˈkɒləmbaɪn/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləmbaɪn/ (US)| n.|耏斗菜；鸽子|A plant of the buttercup family, bearing showy flowers with spurred petals, mostly native to North America. Also, a pale purple color, like that of some columbine flowers.  It can also symbolize folly or foolishness, derived from the jester's cap, which sometimes resembled the flower.|The columbine bloomed in vibrant shades of purple and red. 耏斗菜开出了鲜艳的紫色和红色花朵。|Synonyms: aquilegia (botanical name), granny's bonnet|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

columbine: /ˈkɒləmbaɪn/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləmbaɪn/ (US)| adj.|鸽子似的；淡紫色的|Resembling a dove or having a pale purple color.|Her columbine dress flowed gracefully as she danced.  她那淡紫色的裙子在她跳舞时优雅地飘动。|Synonyms: dove-like, lavender, lilac|Antonyms: N/A|Adjective
