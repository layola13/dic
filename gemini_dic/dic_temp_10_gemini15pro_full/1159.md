acclaim's: /əˈkleɪmz/ | n. |  赞扬，称赞 |  Possessive form of "acclaim," meaning the praise or applause given to someone or something.  It indicates that the acclaim *belongs* to someone or something. | The film garnered the acclaim's of critics worldwide. = 这部电影赢得了世界各地评论家的赞扬。

The book received the acclaim's of both readers and professionals. = 这本书受到了读者和专业人士的赞扬。 | 近义词: praise, applause, commendation, recognition | 反义词: criticism, condemnation, disapproval | 词性: 名词 (possessive)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *aclamer* (to cry out, proclaim), from Latin *acclamare* (to shout at, applaud), from *ad-* (to) + *clamare* (to cry out).  The "-'s" is the possessive suffix.

USAGE->
Because "acclaim's" is a possessive form, its usage revolves around indicating ownership or association of the acclaim. It's less common than the base word "acclaim."

Example 1: The author basked in the acclaim's warmth. = 作者沉浸在赞扬的温暖中。
Example 2: The acclaim's impact on her career was significant. = 赞扬对她的职业生涯产生了重大影响。
