overconservatively: /ˌoʊvər kənˈsɜːrvətɪvli/ | adv. | 过于保守地 | In a way that is excessively cautious and traditional, avoiding change or innovation. |  He invested his money overconservatively, missing out on potential gains. 他过于保守地投资，错失了潜在的收益。| The government approached the issue overconservatively, fearing public backlash. 政府过于保守地处理这个问题，担心公众的强烈反对。 | 近义词： cautiously, timidly, prudently (excessively) | 反义词： aggressively, boldly, radically | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
over- (prefix) + conservatively (adverb)
"over-" denotes "excessively" or "too much"
"conservatively" derives from "conservative," which ultimately comes from the Latin "conservare" (to preserve).
