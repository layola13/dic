demobilized:/diːˈmoʊbəˌlaɪzd/ |adj.|复员的，遣散的|Discharged from military service.  Returned to civilian life after serving in the armed forces.  No longer mobilized or ready for war.|例句：The demobilized soldiers returned home to their families. 复员的士兵回到了家中与家人团聚。

例句：Many factories were demobilized after the war and converted to peacetime production.  战争结束后，许多工厂复员，转为和平时期的生产。|近义词：discharged, mustered out, released|反义词：mobilized, enlisted, drafted|词性：形容词

demobilize:/diːˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz/ |v.|复员，遣散|To release from military service. To disband (troops or an army).|例句：The government plans to demobilize thousands of soldiers next year. 政府计划明年遣散数千名士兵。

例句：After the war ended, the army was quickly demobilized. 战争结束后，军队迅速复员。|近义词：discharge, disband, muster out|反义词：mobilize, enlist, conscript, draft|词性：动词

词源: From de- + mobilize, from French mobiliser, from mobile ‘movable’.  Ultimately from Latin mobilis.
