arbitrating: /ˈɑːrbɪtreɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 仲裁；公断 |  The act of settling a dispute between two parties by a third party chosen by them. |  The union and management are arbitrating their differences. 工会和管理层正在仲裁他们的分歧。 |  mediating, adjudicating, umpiring | negotiating, litigating | verb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *arbitratus, past participle of arbitrari (“to judge, examine, give judgment, witness”), from arbiter (“judge, umpire, witness”).
