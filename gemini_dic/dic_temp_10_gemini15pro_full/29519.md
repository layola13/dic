catastrophic:/ˌkætəˈstrɒfɪk/ /ˌkætəˈstrɑːfɪk/| adj.|灾难性的，毁灭性的|Involving or causing sudden great damage or suffering.|The earthquake was catastrophic, destroying entire cities. 地震是灾难性的，摧毁了整座城市。|近义词：disastrous, calamitous, ruinous|反义词：beneficial, advantageous|词性:形容词
词源: From Late Latin catastrophē, from Ancient Greek καταστροφή (katastrophḗ, “overturning, sudden turn, overthrow, ruin, disastrous end”), from καταστρέφω (katastréphō, “I overturn”), from κατά (katá, “down”) + στρέφω (stréphō, “I turn”).
