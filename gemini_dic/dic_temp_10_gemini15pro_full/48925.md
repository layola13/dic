deftest:/ˈdɛftɪst/ | adj. | 灵巧的，熟练的，最灵巧的 |  Superlative form of 'deft,' meaning exceptionally skillful and adroit, particularly with the hands. |  He was the deftest pianist I had ever seen. 他是我见过的最灵巧的钢琴家。 |  skilled, adept, adroit, proficient | clumsy, awkward, inept | adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *defte*, from Old English *gedæfte*, past participle of *gedæftan* (“to make fit”), from *gedæftan* (“to be fit, suitable”), from Proto-Germanic *gadabjaną* (“to be fitting”).  Related to German *gedeihen* (“to thrive”). The superlative suffix *-est* comes from Old English *-est*.
