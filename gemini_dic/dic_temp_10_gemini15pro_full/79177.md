glamour:/ˈɡlæmər/ (US) /ˈɡlæmə(r)/ (UK)| n.|魅力，诱惑力，迷人的美|A mysterious or fascinating quality that makes something or someone seem attractive, exciting, or special.|She was captivated by the glamour of Hollywood. 她被好莱坞的魅力所吸引。|近义词：charm, allure, magic, enchantment|反义词：drabness, dullness|noun

glamorous:/ˈɡlæmərəs/ (US) /ˈɡlæmərəs/ (UK)| adj.|富有魅力的，迷人的|Having or associated with glamour; full of charm and excitement.|She led a glamorous life, attending parties and traveling the world. 她过着光鲜亮丽的生活，参加派对，周游世界。|近义词：charming, alluring, attractive, fascinating|反义词：drab, dull, ordinary, plain|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Scottish *gramarye*, meaning "magic, enchantment, spell," itself from Old French *gramaire* "grammar, learning," ultimately from Latin *grammatica*. The change in meaning is thought to have originated from the association of learning with magic and occult practices.
