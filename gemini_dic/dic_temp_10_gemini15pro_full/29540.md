catch-cold:/ˈkætʃ koʊld/ (US) /ˈkætʃ kəʊld/ (UK)| n.| 感冒|The act of becoming infected with a common cold.  It's considered informal and more commonly represented by the phrase "catch a cold".|He caught a cold after being out in the rain all day. 他在雨中待了一整天后感冒了。|近义词: get a cold, come down with a cold|反义词: recover from a cold|词性: noun (phrase)


catch:/kætʃ/ (US) /kætʃ/ (UK)| v.| 抓住，感染|To capture or seize something, or to contract a disease.|She caught the ball. 她接住了球。 He caught a cold. 他感冒了。|近义词: grab, seize, contract|反义词: miss, release, recover|词性: verb

cold:/koʊld/ (US) /kəʊld/ (UK)| n. adj.| 感冒，寒冷的|A common viral infection of the nose and throat; having a low temperature.|I have a cold. 我感冒了。 It's cold outside. 外面很冷。|近义词: chill, illness, frigid|反义词: warm, hot, healthy|词性: noun, adjective
