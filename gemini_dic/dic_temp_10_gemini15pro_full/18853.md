beseechers:/bɪˈsiːtʃərz/
|n.|恳求者，请求者|People making earnest requests or petitions; those who beseech.|The beseechers knelt before the king, begging for mercy. 恳求者跪在国王面前，乞求怜悯。|近义词：supplicants, petitioners, pleaders|反义词：demanders, commanders|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From the verb "beseech," which comes from Middle English *bisechen*, from Old English *besēcan* "to seek, implore, ask for earnestly," from *be-* (intensive prefix) + *sēcan* "to seek."  The *-ers* suffix indicates a person who performs the action of the verb.
