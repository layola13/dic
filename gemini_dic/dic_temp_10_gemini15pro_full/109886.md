liage:/ˈliːɪdʒ/| n.|效忠，臣服；臣属关系|The knight pledged his liage to the king.|骑士向国王宣誓效忠。|近义词：allegiance, loyalty, fealty|反义词：treachery, betrayal|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *liege, from Frankish *leode, “freeman, vassal”  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *leudiz. Cognate with Dutch lieden (“people”), German Leute (“people”). The original sense was "free," but the word developed the sense of "one bound by oath to a superior," hence "vassal."
