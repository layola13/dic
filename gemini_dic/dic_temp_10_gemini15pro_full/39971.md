consubstantialist:/ˌkɒnsəbˈstænʃɪəlɪst/ /ˌkɑːnsəbˈstænʃɪəlɪst/| n. | 同体论者；同质论者 | A believer in consubstantiation, the doctrine that the substance of the body and blood of Christ are present together with the substance of the bread and wine in the Eucharist. |  Martin Luther was a consubstantialist, believing that the bread and wine coexist with the body and blood of Christ. 马丁·路德是一位同体论者，他相信面包和葡萄酒与基督的身体和血液共存。| synonym: coreligionist, fellow believer | antonym: heretic, dissenter | noun

Sharing similar qualities or characteristics.| The Nicene Creed declares that Jesus is consubstantial with the Father. 尼西亚信经宣称耶稣与圣父同体。 |  Their arguments, while superficially different, were consubstantial in their core logic. 他们的论点虽然表面上不同，但在核心逻辑上是相同的。| synonym: coessential, connatural | antonym: different, dissimilar | adjective
