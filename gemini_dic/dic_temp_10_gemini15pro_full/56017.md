do-out-of:/duː aʊt əv/ | phrasal verb | 骗取; 剥夺; 使丧失 | To cheat someone out of something, especially money; to deprive someone of something. | He was done out of his inheritance by his greedy relatives. 他被贪婪的亲戚骗走了遗产。| synonyms: cheat, defraud, swindle, deprive, rob | antonyms: give, bestow, award | verb

do-out-of:/duː aʊt əv/ | phrasal verb |  使精疲力竭; 使筋疲力尽 | To exhaust or tire someone completely. | The marathon did me out of all my energy. 马拉松比赛耗尽了我所有的精力。 | synonyms: exhaust, tire, wear out, drain | antonyms: energize, invigorate, refresh | verb
