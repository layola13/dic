moisturize: /ˈmɔɪstʃəraɪz/ (US) /ˈmɔɪstʃəraɪz/ (UK)| v. |滋润，保湿|To make something less dry, especially skin, by putting a cream or lotion on it.| I always moisturize my skin after showering. 我洗澡后总是会给皮肤保湿。|Synonyms: hydrate, condition, soften|Antonyms: dry, dehydrate|Verb

moisturizer: /ˈmɔɪstʃəˌraɪzər/ (US) /ˈmɔɪstʃəˌraɪzə(r)/ (UK)| n. |保湿霜，润肤霜|A cream or lotion that you put on your skin to make it less dry.|This moisturizer leaves my skin feeling soft and smooth.  这款保湿霜让我的皮肤感觉柔软光滑。|Synonyms: lotion, cream, balm|Antonyms: desiccant, drying agent|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From moist + -ize.  Moist ultimately comes from Latin *musteus, from mustum "new wine."  The suffix -ize comes from Greek -ίζειν (-izein).
