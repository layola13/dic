novelty:/ˈnɒvəlti/ (UK) /ˈnɑːvəlti/ (US)| n.|新奇; 新奇的事物; 新颖小巧而廉价的物品|Something new or unusual.  A small, cheap, unusual object, often given as a present.|例句：The novelty of the new toy soon wore off.  新玩具的新鲜劲儿很快就过去了。|例句：He bought a few novelties at the airport. 他在机场买了几件新奇的小玩意儿。|近义词：innovation, originality, freshness, newness|反义词：familiarity, staleness, oldness|词性:noun

novelty:/ˈnɒvəlti/ (UK) /ˈnɑːvəlti/ (US)| adj.|新奇的; 新颖的 |New and unusual or different.|例句：The novelty of the situation excited him.  新奇的状况让他兴奋不已。|例句：She enjoys the novelty of foreign travel. 她喜欢外国旅行的新鲜感。|近义词：innovative, original, fresh, new|反义词：familiar, stale, old|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *novelté, from Latin novitas (“newness”), from novus (“new”).
