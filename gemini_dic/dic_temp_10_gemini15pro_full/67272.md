exquisitism:/ɛksˈkwɪzɪtɪzəm/| n. |精致主义；讲究；唯美主义|The pursuit of extreme refinement and delicacy in matters of taste, especially in art, literature, or food.|Her apartment was a testament to her exquisitism, filled with rare antiques and delicate porcelain. 她的公寓证明了她的精致主义，里面摆满了稀有的古董和精致的瓷器。|近义词：refinement, elegance, sophistication, fastidiousness|反义词：crudeness, coarseness, vulgarity|词性:noun

exquisite:/ɪkˈskwɪzɪt/| adj. |精致的；精美的；剧烈的；强烈的|Extremely beautiful and delicate, or intensely felt.|She wore an exquisite diamond necklace. 她戴着一条精致的钻石项链。  The pain was exquisite. 疼痛剧烈。|近义词：beautiful, delicate, refined, intense|反义词：coarse, crude, rough, mild|词性:adjective
