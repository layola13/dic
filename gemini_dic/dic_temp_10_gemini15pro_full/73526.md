foreshow:/fɔːrˈʃoʊ/ (US) /fɔːˈʃəʊ/ (UK)| v.|预示，预兆|To show or indicate beforehand; portend.|Dark clouds foreshow a storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨。|The sudden drop in sales foreshowed the company's eventual bankruptcy. 销售额的骤降预示着该公司最终的破产。|近义词：presage, portend, foretell, predict, augur|反义词：conceal, hide|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *foreshewen*, from Old English *foresċēawian* (“to see beforehand, provide, foresee”), from *fore-* (“before”) + *sċēawian* (“to look at, see, view, inspect, consider”).  Cognate with Dutch *verschouwen* (“to inspect”), German *verschauen* (“to look at intently, spoil by looking at”).
