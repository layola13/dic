flyboat:/ˈflaɪˌboʊt/| n. |平底货船，快速帆船|A large, flat-bottomed Dutch sailing vessel formerly used in coastal and inland waterways.|The flyboat, with its shallow draft, was ideal for navigating the canals of Holland.|Synonym: barge, lighter|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "ocean liner" as a contrasting vessel type)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Dutch *vlieboot*, literally "fly boat," likely referring to its speed and maneuverability, not literal flight.  The word entered English in the 17th century.
