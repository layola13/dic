anachronize:/əˈnækrənaɪz/ | v. | 使…时代错误；把…置于错误的时代 | To place (something) in the wrong historical period; to represent or treat (something) as if it belonged to the wrong historical period. |  The film anachronizes the events by portraying characters using cell phones in the 1920s. 这部电影通过描绘1920年代的角色使用手机而使事件时代错误。

The author anachronized the story by including details of modern technology. 作者通过加入现代技术的细节，使故事时代错误。| 近义词: misdate, misplace, mistime | 反义词: contextualize, date correctly | 词性: 动词

anachronism:/əˈnækrənɪzəm/ | n. | 时代错误；年代误植；落伍的人或物 | Something (such as a word, an object, or an event) that is chronologically out of place; especially : one that is placed in a period to which it does not belong.  | The movie is full of anachronisms, like knights wearing wristwatches. 这部电影充满了时代错误，比如骑士戴着手表。

His quaint manners seemed like an anachronism in the modern world. 他古怪的举止在现代社会中似乎显得格格不入。| 近义词: misdating, solecism | 反义词: contemporaneity | 词性: 名词

anachronistic:/əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪk/ | adj. | 时代错误的 | Belonging to a period other than that being portrayed or dealt with; chronologically out of place.  |  It would be anachronistic to depict a medieval king using a computer. 描绘一位中世纪国王使用电脑将是时代错误的。

The play's use of modern slang felt anachronistic. 这部戏使用现代俚语让人感觉时代错误。 | 近义词: outdated, old-fashioned, antiquated | 反义词: contemporary, modern, current | 词性: 形容词
