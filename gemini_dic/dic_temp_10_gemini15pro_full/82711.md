/ɡuːɪd/ /ɡwɪd/| n.|全局唯一标识符 (Globally Unique Identifier)|A GUID (Globally Unique Identifier) is a 128-bit integer number used to identify resources.  It is designed to be unique across all computers and networks, making it useful for identifying objects or information in distributed systems.|Example: Each record in a database can be assigned a GUID to uniquely identify it.  每个数据库中的记录都可以分配一个 GUID 来唯一标识它。

Example:  GUIDs are used in software development to avoid naming conflicts. GUID 在软件开发中用于避免命名冲突。

|Synonym: UUID (Universally Unique Identifier)|Antonym: N/A|Noun
