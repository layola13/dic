electrocardiographic:/ɪˌlɛktroʊˌkɑːrdiəˈɡræfɪk/ |adj.|心电图的|Relating to electrocardiography; obtained or recorded by electrocardiography.  It describes something related to the recording or interpretation of the electrical activity of the heart.|The electrocardiographic findings suggested a previous heart attack.|Synonyms: ECG, EKG|Antonyms: None|adjective

electrocardiograph:/ɪˌlɛktroʊˈkɑːrdiəɡræf/ |n.|心电图机|A machine for recording the electrical activity of the heart.|The doctor used an electrocardiograph to check the patient's heart rhythm.|Synonyms: EKG machine, ECG machine|Antonyms: None|noun


electrocardiogram:/ɪˌlɛktroʊˈkɑːrdiəɡræm/ |n.|心电图|A record or display of a person's heartbeat produced by electrocardiography.|The electrocardiogram showed an irregular heartbeat.|Synonyms: EKG, ECG|Antonyms: None|noun
