cDNA:/ˌsiː diː ɛn ˈeɪ/ |n.| 互补DNA | Complementary DNA, a DNA molecule made in vitro using mRNA as a template and the enzyme reverse transcriptase. cDNA libraries are frequently used to clone eukaryotic genes in prokaryotes. |Scientists used cDNA to study gene expression. 科学家使用cDNA来研究基因表达。|  near synonyms: copy DNA, complementary DNA | antonyms: genomic DNA | noun
