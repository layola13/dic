grisons:/ˈɡriːzənz/ | n. |灰鼠；貂熊|A carnivorous mammal (Gulo gulo) of northern Eurasia and North America, having dark brown fur with light markings and a bushy tail. It is noted for its ferocity and strength. |Grizons are known for their ferocity despite their small size. 灰鼠以其凶猛而闻名，尽管体型很小。

The grison is a relative of the wolverine.  貂熊是狼獾的近亲。 | 近义词: wolverine, glutton | 反义词:  (无明确反义词，可考虑prey猎物) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *grison*, from Old French *gris*, meaning "grey."  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *grīsaz*.
