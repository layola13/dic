overture: /ˈoʊvərˌtʃər/ (US) /ˈəʊvərtʃə(r)/ (UK)| n. |序曲；提议；主动姿态|An introduction to something more substantial, such as a musical piece, a long poem, or a relationship/negotiation.  An initial proposal or offer.|
例句：The overture to the opera was magnificent. 歌剧的序曲很壮丽。

例句：His overture of friendship was warmly received. 他主动示好的举动受到了热烈欢迎。

近义词：prelude, introduction, proposal, offer, advance|
反义词：conclusion, ending, rejection, refusal|
词性: noun


overtured: /oʊvərˈtʃərd/ (US) /əʊvərˈtʃərd/ (UK) | v. (past tense and past participle of overture) |提议；主动建议；以…作为开场|Made an initial proposal or offer; introduced with an overture. |
例句：He overtured a peace agreement between the warring factions. 他提议交战各方达成和平协议。

例句：The concert was overtured by a short film about the composer.  这场音乐会以一部关于作曲家的短片作为开场。

近义词：proposed, suggested, initiated, opened, introduced|
反义词：concluded, finished, rejected, refused|
词性: verb


词源: From French *ouverture*, from *ouvrir* "to open", from Latin *aperire*.
