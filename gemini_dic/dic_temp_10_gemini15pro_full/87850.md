hideboundnesses:/ˈhaɪdˌbaʊndnɪsɪz/
noun
迂腐；顽固；保守；墨守成规
The state of being hidebound, characterized by unwillingness or inability to change because of tradition or convention.  A rigid adherence to outmoded ideas or ways of doing things.
His hideboundnesses prevented him from accepting new ideas. 他的迂腐使他无法接受新思想。
近义词: inflexibility, rigidity, conservatism, dogmatism
反义词: flexibility, open-mindedness, adaptability, progressiveness
词性:n.


Etymology:
From "hidebound" + "-ness."  "Hidebound" literally means having a hide (skin) that is so close to the bones that it cannot move freely.  This physical sense extended metaphorically to describe a person's character or thinking.
