claptrap:/ˈklæptræp/| n.|空洞的言辞；哗众取宠的语句；花言巧语；骗人的玩意儿|A claptrap is a remark or idea that sounds impressive but is actually foolish or meaningless.  It can also refer to a trick or device designed to attract attention.|The politician's speech was full of claptraps, promising everything but delivering nothing. 这位政客的演讲充满了空洞的言辞，承诺了一切，但什么也没兑现。

The weight-loss product was just a claptrap, and people who bought it lost no weight.  这款减肥产品只是一个骗人的玩意，购买它的人并没有减肥。|近义词：nonsense, drivel, balderdash, gimmick, trickery|反义词：sense, wisdom, truth|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "clap" (meaning to strike together with a sharp sound, as in clapping hands) + "trap" (meaning a device for catching animals).  Originally, it referred to a device used in theaters to create a sudden loud noise, often used to get applause. Over time, it evolved to mean language intended to elicit applause or approval, even if undeserved.
