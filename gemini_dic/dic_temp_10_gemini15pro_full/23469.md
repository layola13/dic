braggery: /ˈbræɡəri/ | n. | 吹牛，自夸 |  Boastful or arrogant behavior; excessive pride and self-satisfaction. | He was known for his braggery and often exaggerated his accomplishments. 他以吹牛而闻名，经常夸大自己的成就。 |  She couldn't stand his constant braggery about his new car. 她受不了他不停地吹嘘他的新车。 | 近义词：boasting, bragging, vainglory, swaggering | 反义词：humility, modesty | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  From the verb "brag" + "-ery" (a suffix forming nouns denoting a condition or behavior).  "Brag" comes from Middle English *braggen*, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old Norse *braka* "to crash, crack."
