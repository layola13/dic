partisan: /ˈpɑːrtɪzæn/ (US) /ˈpɑːtɪzan/ (UK)| n. adj.| 游击队员，党羽，偏袒者，拥护者|A strong supporter of a party, cause, or person, especially one who shows a biased, emotional allegiance.| The crowd was made up of partisan Clinton supporters. | 近义词: supporter, advocate, adherent, devotee, backer, follower, champion, zealot | 反义词: opponent, adversary, critic, detractor, foe | 词性: 名词，形容词

partisan: /ˈpɑːrtɪzæn/ (US) /ˈpɑːtɪzan/ (UK)| adj.| 偏袒的，盲目拥护的|Prejudiced in favor of a particular cause.| He made a highly partisan speech. | 近义词: biased, prejudiced, one-sided, sectarian, factional | 反义词: impartial, unbiased, neutral, objective | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From French *partisan*, from Italian *partito* "party, faction," from *partire* "to divide, depart, share," from Latin *partiri* "to divide," related to *pars* "part."
