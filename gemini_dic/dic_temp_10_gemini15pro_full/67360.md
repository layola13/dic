extensionalism:/ɪkˈstɛnʃənəlɪzəm/| n.|外延主义|The doctrine that the meaning of a term is determined solely by its extension, that is, by the set of things to which it applies.  It contrasts with intensionalism.|Example 1: Extensionalism holds that two predicates with the same extension are synonymous, even if their definitions differ. 外延主义认为，即使两个谓词的定义不同，只要它们的外延相同，它们就是同义的。

Example 2: The philosopher argued for extensionalism, claiming that the meaning of "red" is simply the set of all red things. 这位哲学家支持外延主义，声称“红色”的含义就是所有红色事物的集合。|近义词:denotationalism|反义词:intensionalism|词性:noun
