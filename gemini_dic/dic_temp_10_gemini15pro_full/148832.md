overvivid:/ˌoʊvərˈvɪvɪd/| adj.| 过于鲜艳的，过于生动的| Excessively vivid; too bright or intense, often to the point of being unrealistic or disturbing.  It describes something that is so vivid that it is overwhelming or unpleasant.| The overvivid colors in the painting gave me a headache. 这幅画中过于鲜艳的颜色让我头疼。| synonyms:  garish, gaudy, lurid, flamboyant, excessive, intense | antonyms:  dull, muted, subdued, pale, faint, understated | adjective

Etymology:
"over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "vivid" (from Latin "vivus" meaning "alive, lively")
