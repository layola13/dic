contumaciousness:/ˌkɒntjʊˈmeɪʃəsnəs/ /ˌkɑːntjʊˈmeɪʃəsnəs/
n.
顽抗; 抗命; 故意违抗;
The quality or state of being contumacious; stubborn or willful disobedience, especially to a court order or summons.
His contumaciousness resulted in a jail sentence for contempt of court.  他的顽抗导致他因藐视法庭而被判入狱。
近义词: defiance, rebelliousness, insubordination, obstinacy
反义词: obedience, compliance, submission, deference
词性: noun

contumacious:/ˌkɒntjʊˈmeɪʃəs/ /ˌkɑːntjʊˈmeɪʃəs/
adj.
顽抗的; 抗命的; 故意违抗的;
Stubbornly or willfully disobedient to authority, especially to a court order or summons.
The judge held the defendant in contempt for his contumacious behavior. 法官因被告的抗命行为而判其藐视法庭。
近义词: defiant, rebellious, insubordinate, obstinate
反义词: obedient, compliant, submissive, deferential
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contumacia, from Latin contumax (“very stubborn, rebellious”), from con- (“with, together”) + tumēre (“to swell, be swollen”).  The -ousness suffix forms abstract nouns from adjectives.
