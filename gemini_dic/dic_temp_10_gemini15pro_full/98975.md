inter-pollinates:/ˌɪntərˈpɒləˌneɪts/ | v. |异花授粉；相互授粉|Causes pollen from one plant to fertilize another plant of a different variety.|The gardener inter-pollinates his roses to create new hybrids. 园丁将他的玫瑰异花授粉以创造新的杂交品种。|The bees inter-pollinated the apple trees and the pear trees. 蜜蜂使苹果树和梨树异花授粉。|近义词：cross-pollinate, hybridize|反义词: self-pollinate|词性:动词

inter-pollinating:/ˌɪntərˈpɒləˌneɪtɪŋ/ | adj. |异花授粉的|Relating to or involving the transfer of pollen from one plant to another of a different variety.|The inter-pollinating nature of these plants leads to a diverse range of offspring. 这些植物的异花授粉特性导致了后代的多样性。|The bees were busy inter-pollinating the flowers in the garden. 蜜蜂忙着在花园里给花朵异花授粉。|近义词：cross-pollinating|反义词: self-pollinating|词性:形容词

inter-pollination:/ˌɪntərˌpɒləˈneɪʃən/ | n. |异花授粉|The transfer of pollen from one plant to another of a different variety.|Inter-pollination is essential for the production of many fruits and vegetables. 异花授粉对于许多水果和蔬菜的生产至关重要。|The inter-pollination of different species of flowers can create new varieties. 不同种类花的异花授粉可以创造新品种。|近义词：cross-pollination, hybridization|反义词: self-pollination|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From inter- + pollinate.  "Inter-" is a Latin prefix meaning "between," "among," "mutually," "reciprocally." "Pollinate" comes from the Latin "pollen" (fine flour, dust), ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European root *pel- (dust, fine powder).
