deliria:/dɪˈlɪəriə/| n.|谵妄，神志不清|A state of mental confusion, often accompanied by hallucinations and disordered speech, typically caused by fever or illness.|The patient experienced deliria due to high fever. 患者因高烧而出现谵妄。|Near synonyms: confusion, hallucinations, disorientation, incoherence, madness|Antonyms: lucidity, clarity, coherence, sanity|Noun


delirium:/dɪˈlɪəriəm/| n.|谵妄，神志不清|A state of mental confusion, often accompanied by hallucinations and disordered speech, typically caused by fever or illness.|The patient experienced delirium due to high fever. 患者因高烧而出现谵妄。|Near synonyms: confusion, hallucinations, disorientation, incoherence, madness|Antonyms: lucidity, clarity, coherence, sanity|Noun

词源: From Latin *dēlīrium*, from *dēlīrāre* "to be crazy, rave," from *dē-* "from, out of" + *līra* "furrow, track."  The original sense was of being "out of the furrow" when ploughing, hence "off track" mentally.
