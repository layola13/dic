baubles:/ˈbɔːblz/ (US), /ˈbɔːbəlz/ (UK)| n. |小饰品；廉价珠宝；小玩意儿|Small, inexpensive, and showy ornaments or jewelry, often considered frivolous.  They are typically used for decoration or play.|The Christmas tree glittered with colorful baubles.  圣诞树上闪耀着五彩缤纷的小饰品。|Synonyms: trinkets, ornaments, decorations, gewgaws|Antonyms: valuables, treasures|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *baudekin*, from Old French *baudequin*, from *Baldacco* (Italian), from *Baghdad* (Arabic). Originally referred to a rich embroidered silk fabric from Baghdad, later coming to mean showy ornaments generally.
