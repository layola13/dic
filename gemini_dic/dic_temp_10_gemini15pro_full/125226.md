multicultural:/ˌmʌltiˈkʌltʃərəl/| adj.|多元文化的|Relating to or containing several cultural or ethnic groups within a society.|例句：The city is becoming increasingly multicultural. 这座城市正变得越来越多元文化。|例句：Multicultural societies are often enriched by the diverse traditions and perspectives of their residents. 多元文化社会通常因其居民的各种传统和观点而变得更加丰富。|近义词：diverse, cosmopolitan, pluralistic|反义词：homogeneous, monocultural|词性:形容词

Etymology->
From multi- + cultural.  Multi- is a combining form meaning “many,” “multiple,” attached to adjectives, nouns, and verbs, ultimately from Latin multus "much, many." Cultural is derived from culture, ultimately from Latin cultura "a cultivating, agriculture," figuratively "care, culture, an honoring," from past participle stem of colere "to till."
