eudemonistic:/ˌjuːdɪˈmɒnɪstɪk/ | adj. | 快乐主义的；幸福主义的 | Relating to or based on the belief that happiness or well-being is the chief good. |  Eudemonistic philosophy emphasizes the importance of living a fulfilling life. 快乐主义哲学强调过一种充实的生活的重要性。| Synonyms: hedonistic, utilitarian | Antonyms: ascetic, stoic | adjective

eudemonism:/ˌjuːdɪˈmɒnɪzəm/ | n. | 快乐主义；幸福主义 | The ethical theory that happiness or well-being is the chief good. | Eudemonism is a philosophy that prioritizes personal fulfillment. 快乐主义是一种优先考虑个人成就的哲学。| Synonyms: hedonism, utilitarianism | Antonyms: asceticism, stoicism | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *eudaimōnistēs* (one devoted to happiness), from *eudaimōn* (happy, fortunate) + *-istēs* (agent suffix).  The Greek *eu-* means "good" and *daimōn* means "spirit" or "guardian deity."
