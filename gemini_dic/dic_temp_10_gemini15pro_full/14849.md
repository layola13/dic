axman:/ˈæksmən/| n.|伐木工人；用斧子砍人的人|A person who cuts down trees with an axe; a person who attacks someone with an axe. |The axman swung his axe with powerful strokes. 伐木工人有力地挥动着斧头。/  The police were searching for the axman who had committed the gruesome murders. 警方正在搜捕犯下可怕谋杀案的持斧歹徒。|近义词：lumberjack, woodcutter (for a person who cuts down trees), attacker, assailant (for a person who attacks someone with an axe)|反义词: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From axe + man.  "Axe" comes from Old English *æx, from Proto-Germanic *aksi, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂egʷsi- "axe." "Man" comes from Old English mann, from Proto-Germanic *mann-, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *man- "man, human being."
