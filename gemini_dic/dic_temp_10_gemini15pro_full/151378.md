parleyer:/ˈpɑːrliər/ | n. |谈判者，会谈者|A person who parleys, especially one who is appointed to hold a discussion or conference with an enemy, usually concerning terms, truce, or the like.|The parleyer approached the enemy lines under a white flag.  谈判者在白旗下靠近敌军防线。|Synonyms: negotiator, mediator, envoy, emissary|Antonyms: combatant, aggressor|noun

parley:/ˈpɑːrli/ | n. vi. |会谈，谈判|A discussion or conference, especially one between enemies over terms of a truce or other matters.|The two sides agreed to a parley to discuss a ceasefire. 双方同意进行会谈，讨论停火事宜。|Synonyms: negotiation, conference, discussion, meeting|Antonyms: fight, battle, conflict|noun, intransitive verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *parleier*, from *parler* "to speak," from Late Latin *parabolare* "to speak (in parables)," from *parabola* "parable," from Greek *parabole* "comparison, parable," from *paraballein* "to throw alongside, compare," from *para-* "beside" + *ballein* "to throw."
