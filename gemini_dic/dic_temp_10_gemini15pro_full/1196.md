accoast: /əˈkoʊst/
verb
搭讪；上前和…说话；恳求
To approach and speak to (someone) often in a challenging or aggressive way.
例句：
A stranger accosted me on the street and asked for money. = 一个陌生人在街上搭讪我，问我讨钱。

He was accosted by a group of beggars. = 他被一群乞丐缠住了。

近义词：approach, address, greet, hail, solicit, buttonhole
反义词：avoid, shun, ignore
词性：v.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French acoster (to come alongside), from à (to) + coste (coast, side).  Originally a nautical term meaning "to come alongside," it later broadened to mean "to approach and speak to."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often accosts tourists to sell them souvenirs. = 他经常搭讪游客向他们兜售纪念品。
She accosts strangers for directions whenever she gets lost. = 每当她迷路时，她都会向陌生人问路。


一般过去时:
I was accosted by a panhandler while walking home. = 我走在回家的路上时被一个乞丐缠住了。
The reporter accosted the mayor for a statement. = 记者上前向市长询问声明。


现在完成时:
Several people have accosted me about donating to their charity. = 有几个人跟我搭讪，要我向他们的慈善机构捐款。
He has accosted many potential clients at the conference. = 他在会议上搭讪了许多潜在客户。


过去完成时:
The police officer had accosted the suspect before he could flee. = 警察在嫌疑人逃跑之前就把他拦住了。
She had accosted several shopkeepers before finding what she was looking for. = 在找到她要找的东西之前，她已经问过好几个店主了。


将来时:
I will accost him about the missing files tomorrow. = 我明天会向他询问丢失的文件的事情。
If he continues to behave suspiciously, security will accost him. = 如果他继续表现得可疑，保安会上前盘问他。


虚拟语气:
If I were to accost her, she might think I'm rude. = 如果我去搭讪她，她可能会觉得我很粗鲁。
I wish I hadn't accosted that stranger; he turned out to be rather unpleasant. = 我希望我没有搭讪那个陌生人；他原来是个很不友好的人。


被动语态:
He was accosted by security guards. = 他被保安拦住了。
She was accosted for her autograph. = 她被拦住索要签名。


疑问句:
Why did you accost that woman? = 你为什么搭讪那个女人？
Has anyone accosted you about the missing jewelry? = 有没有人问过你关于丢失珠宝的事？


否定句:
I didn't accost him, he approached me. = 我没有搭讪他，是他来找我的。
She never accosts strangers in dark alleys. = 她从不在黑暗的小巷里搭讪陌生人。
