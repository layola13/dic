farand:/ˈfærənd/ | n. | Traveling show; entertainment; festivity | A traveling show or company of actors, especially in Scotland; also, merrymaking, festivity. | The farand came to town with their colorful wagons and lively music.  巡回演出团带着他们色彩鲜艳的马车和活泼的音乐来到了城镇。| Synonym: troupe, entertainment, festivity | Antonym: boredom, monotony | noun

Etymology: From Middle English *farende, present participle of faren, from Old English faran (“to journey, travel, go, proceed”). Related to Old High German faran (“to travel, journey”), Old Norse fara (“to go, travel, behave”). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *faraną (“to go, travel”).
