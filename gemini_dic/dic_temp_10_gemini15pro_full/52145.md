diaphaneity:/ˌdaɪəfəˈniːɪti/ | n. | 透明度；透明性 | The quality of being diaphanous; transparency.  Allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be distinctly seen. | The diaphaneity of the fabric made it perfect for summer dresses.  这种织物的透明度使它非常适合做夏装。 | 近义词: transparency, translucence, pellucidity | 反义词: opacity, opaqueness | 词性: noun

diaphanous:/daɪˈæfənəs/ | adj. | 透明的；薄如蝉翼的 |  (especially of fabric) light, delicate, and translucent. |  She wore a diaphanous white dress. 她穿着一件透明的白色连衣裙。 | 近义词: sheer, translucent, transparent | 反义词: opaque, thick | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek διαφανής (diaphanḗs, “transparent”), from διά (diá, “through”) + φαίνω (phaínō, “to show, to shine”).
