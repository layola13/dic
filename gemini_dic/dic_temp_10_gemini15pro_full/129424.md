nominator:/ˈnɒmɪneɪtər/ (US) /ˈnɒmɪneɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n.|提名者；任命者；（分数的）分子|A person who nominates someone or something, especially for a prize, position or honor. In mathematics, the top number in a fraction.|例句：The nominator of the Nobel Peace Prize remains anonymous. 诺贝尔和平奖的提名者仍然匿名。|例句：He was the nominator for the new CEO. 他是新任首席执行官的提名者。|近义词：proposer, sponsor, appointer|反义词: nominee|词性:名词

nominator:/ˈnɒmɪneɪtər/ (US) /ˈnɒmɪneɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n.|（数学）分子|The top number in a fraction. It indicates how many parts of the whole are being considered.|例句：In the fraction 3/4, 3 is the nominator. 在分数3/4中，3是分子。|例句：The nominator is larger than the denominator in an improper fraction. 在假分数中，分子大于分母。|近义词: numerator|反义词: denominator|词性:名词
