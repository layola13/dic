fiends:/fiːndz/| n.|恶魔; 邪恶的人; 瘾君子; 狂热爱好者|An evil spirit or demon; a wicked or cruel person;  a person who is addicted to something; an enthusiast.|She described the attackers as fiends. 她把袭击者描述为恶魔。|近义词：devils, demons, villains, addicts, fanatics|反义词：angels, saints, heroes|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fēond (attested as only gefēon), from Proto-Germanic *fiyandaz (“hater, enemy”), from Proto-Indo-European *pi-yont- (“hating”). Cognate with Old Saxon fīund, Old Frisian fiand, Dutch vijand, Old High German fīant (German Feind), Old Norse fjandi, Gothic fijands. Related to fiend (n.).
