miascite:/ˈmaɪəˌsaɪt/ /ˈmiːəˌsaɪt/| n. |霞石正长岩|A plutonic rock composed essentially of alkali feldspar and nepheline, sometimes with a small amount of biotite, pyroxene, or amphibole. |The miascite was identified by its distinct mineral composition.  这种霞石正长岩通过其独特的矿物成分被识别出来。| The quarry yielded a large quantity of miascite. 这个采石场出产大量的霞石正长岩。|Synonyms:  nepheline syenite, foid syenite (less precise)|Antonyms:  N/A (lack of a direct opposite for a specific rock type)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Mount Mias, Ilmen Mountains, Ural Mountains, Russia, where it was first described.
