drudge:/drʌdʒ/
n.|苦工；单调乏味的工作|a person who does tedious, menial, or unpleasant work.|She felt like a drudge, constantly cleaning and cooking.|近义词: toiler, laborer, menial, servant|反义词:  idler, loafer|词性: 名词

drudge:/drʌdʒ/
v.|做苦工；做单调乏味的工作|to work hard in a tedious or monotonous way.|He drudged away at his homework for hours.|近义词: toil, slave, grind|反义词: relax, idle|词性: 动词

drudges:/drʌdʒɪz/
n.|苦工们；做单调乏味工作的人们|plural form of drudge|The drudges in the factory worked long hours for little pay.|近义词: toilers, laborers, menials, servants|反义词: idlers, loafers|词性: 名词复数


词源:  From Middle English *druggen, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *drjúga (“to toil, work hard”).
