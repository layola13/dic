bulb:/bʌlb/ | n. |灯泡；球茎；球状物|A glass container, typically pear-shaped, that encloses a filament which produces light when heated by an electric current; A rounded underground storage organ present in some plants, notably onions and lilies.|例句：The bulb in the lamp burned out. 灯泡烧坏了。|例句：The tulip bulbs should be planted in the fall. 郁金香球茎应该在秋天种植。|近义词：globe, lamp, tuber, corm |反义词: - |词性:noun

bulb:/bʌlb/ | n. |球状物|Something shaped like a bulb, especially a rounded swelling.|例句：The doctor checked the bulb of my eye. 医生检查了我的眼球。 |例句：The end of his nose was red and bulbous. 他的鼻尖又红又肿。|近义词：swelling, protuberance |反义词: - |词性:noun
