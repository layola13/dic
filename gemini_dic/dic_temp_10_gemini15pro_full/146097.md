ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + effusive + -ness + -es.  "Effusive" comes from the Latin "effusus," past participle of "effundere," meaning "to pour out."  "Over-" signifies an excess.  "-ness" forms a noun denoting a state or quality. "-es" creates the plural form.
