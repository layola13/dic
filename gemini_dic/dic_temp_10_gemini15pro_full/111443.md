**Suffix -ller:**

/-lər/ | suffix | -者，-员 | Used to form nouns indicating a person or thing that performs a particular action (often derived from verbs).  It is related to the "-ler" suffix, and the choice between them is often arbitrary, influenced by etymology and established usage. |  Examples: controller (控制者),  scroller (滚动条),  bestseller (畅销书) | Similar suffixes: -er, -or | N/A | suffix


**Word: what**

/wɒt/ (UK) /wʌt/ (US) | pronoun, determiner, adverb, interjection | 什么, 多么 | Used to ask for information about something; used to express surprise or disbelief; used to emphasize something. |

Examples:

What is your name? = 你叫什么名字？
What do you want? = 你想要什么？

What a beautiful day! = 多么美好的一天！
What are you doing? = 你在做什么？
