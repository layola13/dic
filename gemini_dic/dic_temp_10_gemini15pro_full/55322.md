dissonancy: /ˈdɪsənənsi/ (US), /ˈdɪsənənsi/ (UK)| n. |不和谐，不协调，刺耳|A lack of harmony or agreement between things; a combination of sounds that is unpleasant or harsh.|The dissonancy between her words and her actions was obvious. 她言行之间的不一致很明显。|The painting's dissonancy of color was jarring. 这幅画颜色不协调，很不舒服。|近义词：discord, conflict, clash, incongruity|反义词：harmony, consonance, agreement, concord|词性: 名词

dissonant: /ˈdɪsənənt/ (US), /ˈdɪsənənt/ (UK)| adj. |不和谐的，不协调的，刺耳的|Lacking harmony or agreement; sounding harsh and unpleasant.|The dissonant chords created a sense of unease. 不和谐的和弦造成了一种不安的感觉。|His dissonant views on the project caused friction within the team. 他对这个项目不和谐的观点引起了团队内部的摩擦。|近义词：discordant, inharmonious, clashing, conflicting|反义词：harmonious, consonant, agreeable, concordant|词性: 形容词
