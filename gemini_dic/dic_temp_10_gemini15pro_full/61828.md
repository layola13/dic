empiricists:/ɪmˈpɪrɪsɪsts/
| n. |经验主义者|People who believe that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience and observation, rather than from innate ideas or intuition.| Empiricists emphasize the role of evidence in forming beliefs. 经验主义者强调证据在形成信仰中的作用。|
|近义词：experimentalists, positivists|
|反义词：rationalists, idealists|
|词性：noun (plural)|


empiricist:/ɪmˈpɪrɪsɪst/
| n. |经验主义者|A person who supports the theory that all knowledge is based on experience derived from the senses.|John Locke was a prominent empiricist.  约翰·洛克是一位杰出的经验主义者。|
|近义词：experimentalist, positivist|
|反义词：rationalist, idealist|
|词性：noun (singular)|
