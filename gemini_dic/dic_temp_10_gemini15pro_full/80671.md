gore: /ɡɔːr/ (UK) /ɡɔːr/ (US) | n. v. | 血污，血块；(描写暴力的)血腥场面；三角形布 | Blood, especially clotted blood; bloodshed, violence; a triangular piece of cloth |  The movie was full of gore. 这部电影充满了血腥场面。| 近义词: blood, bloodshed, carnage | 反义词: peace, tranquility | 词性: 名词, 动词


gore: /ɡɔːr/ (UK) /ɡɔːr/ (US) | v. | (用角)顶伤，戳伤 | To pierce or wound with a horn or tusk | The bull gored the matador. 公牛用角顶伤了斗牛士。| 近义词: pierce, stab, impale | 反义词: heal | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *gor "filth, dung," related to Old Norse gor "gore, cow dung."  The meaning shifted to "clotted blood" probably by association with the bloody mess of butchering. The verb sense "to pierce" comes from a different, though possibly related, Old English word gar "spear."
