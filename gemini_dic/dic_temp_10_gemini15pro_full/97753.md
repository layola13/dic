initiate:/ɪˈnɪʃieɪt (US) / ɪˈnɪʃɪeɪt (UK)| v. n. adj.| 开始，创始；启蒙；引入；接纳；新加入的成员 | To begin or cause something to begin. To formally admit someone into an organization or group, often with a special ceremony.  A person who has recently been introduced to a skill or activity, or who has recently joined a group. |例句：The government initiated a new program to combat poverty. 政府启动了一项新的扶贫计划。|近义词：begin, start, commence, inaugurate, introduce, induct |反义词:terminate, end, conclude, finish|词性:verb

initiate:/ɪˈnɪʃieɪt (US) / ɪˈnɪʃɪeɪt (UK)| n. |新加入的成员|A person who has recently joined a group or organization. |例句：The initiates were given a warm welcome by the senior members. 新成员受到了资深成员的热烈欢迎。|近义词：newcomer, beginner, novice |反义词: veteran, expert|词性:noun

initiate:/ɪˈnɪʃieɪt (US) / ɪˈnɪʃɪeɪt (UK)| adj. |新加入的；启蒙的|Having been recently introduced to something. |例句：He's an initiate member of the club. 他是俱乐部的新成员。|近义词：introductory, preliminary |反义词: advanced, experienced|词性:adjective


词源: From Latin *initiatus*, past participle of *initiare* "to begin, originate," from *initium* "beginning."
