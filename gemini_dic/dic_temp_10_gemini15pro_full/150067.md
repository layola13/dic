palstave: /ˈpɔːlˌsteɪv/ | n. | 铜斧;  掌形斧 | A type of bronze axe with a blade that flares towards the cutting edge and flanges along the sides, often with a loop at the side for hafting. | Example: The archaeologist unearthed a well-preserved palstave from the Bronze Age burial site. 考古学家从青铜时代的墓葬中挖掘出一把保存完好的掌形斧。

Example: The palstave, unlike a flat axe, had flanges to secure it to its handle.  掌形斧不像扁平斧，它有凸缘来固定斧柄。| Near Synonyms: axe, hatchet, celt | Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a specific tool) perhaps: peace symbol, plowshare | Noun
