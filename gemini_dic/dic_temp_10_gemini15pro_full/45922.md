cutting:/ˈkʌtɪŋ/| n. adj.|切割；剪切；插枝；开凿；(电影胶片等的)剪辑；(尤指从报刊上)剪下的东西；伤人的；尖刻的；寒冷刺骨的|A piece or part that is cut from something larger; the act of cutting something.|例句：The cuttings from the roses have started to grow. 玫瑰的插枝已经开始生长了。|近义词：slice, snip, trim, incision, excision, piercing, sharp, biting, raw|反义词:blunt, joining, mending|词性:noun

cutting:/ˈkʌtɪŋ/| adj.|切割的；剪切的；伤人的；尖刻的；寒冷刺骨的|Having a sharp edge or point; capable of cutting; hurtful; critical; (of wind or cold) bitterly sharp.|例句：The cutting wind made her eyes water. 刺骨的寒风使她双眼流泪。|近义词：sharp, piercing, biting, raw, incisive, trenchant|反义词:blunt, kind, gentle|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *kutting, *kytinge, from Old English *cytting, verbal noun from cuttan (“to cut”). Cognate with West Frisian kitting (“cutting”), Dutch kitting (“notch”), German Kittung (“cement, putty”).
