bankruptcies: /ˈbæŋkrʌptsiz/ | n. | 破产; 倒闭（bankruptcy 的复数） | The number of bankruptcies has increased significantly this year. 今年的破产数量显著增加。|  The economic downturn led to a wave of bankruptcies across the country. 经济衰退导致全国范围内的一波破产潮。 | 近义词：insolvencies, failures | 反义词：solvencies, successes | 词性: 名词

bankruptcy: /ˈbæŋkrʌptsi/ | n. | 破产; 倒闭 | The company filed for bankruptcy after years of losses.  这家公司在连年亏损后申请破产。 | He was forced into bankruptcy after his business failed.  他的生意失败后，他被迫破产。| 近义词：insolvency, failure | 反义词：solvency, success | 词性: 名词

词源: From Old French *banqueroute, from Italian banca rotta, literally "broken bench," referring to the benches used by money changers in medieval markets. When a money changer failed, his bench was broken to symbolize his insolvency.
