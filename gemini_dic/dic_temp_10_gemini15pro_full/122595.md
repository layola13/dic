modernist:/ˈmɒdənɪst/ (British), /ˈmɑːdərˌnɪst/ (American)| n. adj.|现代主义者；现代主义的|A person who embraces modernism, especially in art, literature, or architecture; relating to or characteristic of modernism.|Example (n.):  The architect was a staunch modernist, rejecting traditional styles.  这位建筑师是一位坚定的现代主义者，拒绝传统风格。

Example (adj.): The modernist building stood out among the older structures. 这座现代主义建筑在古老的建筑中显得格外突出。|近义词：contemporary, avant-garde, innovative|反义词：traditional, conventional, classic|词性: noun, adjective

modernism:/ˈmɒdəˌnɪzəm/ (British), /ˈmɑːdərˌnɪzəm/ (American)| n.|现代主义|A movement in the arts and other fields that rejected traditional styles and embraced new forms of expression, typically characterized by experimentation and a rejection of established norms.|Example:  Modernism in literature often features fragmented narratives and stream-of-consciousness writing. 现代主义文学通常以片段化的叙事和意识流写作手法为特色。

Example: The museum's exhibit showcased the evolution of modernism in painting. 博物馆的展览展示了绘画中现代主义的演变。|近义词：avant-gardism, innovation|反义词：traditionalism, classicism|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From modern + -ist. "Modern" comes from Late Latin *modernus, from Latin modo "just now."
