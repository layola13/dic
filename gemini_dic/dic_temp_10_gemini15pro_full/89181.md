homeostatically:/ˌhəʊmɪəʊˈstætɪkli/ | adv. |体内平衡地| In a way that maintains homeostasis, the tendency of a system to maintain internal stability. | The body temperature is homeostatically regulated.  体温是由体内平衡机制调节的。|  The human body works homeostatically to maintain a stable internal environment. 人体以体内平衡的方式运作以维持稳定的内部环境。 | 近义词: stably,  | 反义词: erratically,  dysfunctionally | 词性: 副词

homeostasis: /ˌhəʊmɪəʊˈsteɪsɪs/ | n. |体内平衡，稳态 | The tendency toward a relatively stable equilibrium between interdependent elements, especially as maintained by physiological processes. | The body maintains homeostasis through a complex system of feedback mechanisms. 身体通过一个复杂的反馈机制系统来维持体内平衡。|  Homeostasis is essential for the survival of all living organisms. 体内平衡对所有生物的生存至关重要。 | 近义词: equilibrium, stability, balance | 反义词: imbalance, instability | 词性: 名词

static: /ˈstætɪk/ | adj. n. | 静态的；静电；静电干扰 | Lacking in movement, action, or change, especially in a way viewed as undesirable or uninteresting. | The population has remained static for the past decade.  过去十年人口一直保持不变。 |  The picture was full of static. 画面充满了静电干扰。| 近义词: still, stationary, unchanging | 反义词: dynamic, moving, changing | 词性: 形容词，名词


词源:  homeo- (similar) + -stasis (standing still)  "homeo" 源自希腊语 "homoios"，意为“相似的”； "stasis" 源自希腊语 "stasis"，意为“站立、静止”。
