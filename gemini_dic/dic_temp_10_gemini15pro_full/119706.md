midrash:/ˈmɪdrɑːʃ/ (US), /ˈmɪdræʃ/ (UK)| n.|米德拉什，圣经注释|An ancient Jewish interpretation of the Hebrew scriptures, often involving imaginative storytelling and legal interpretations.|例句：The midrash offers a fascinating interpretation of the biblical story.  米德拉什对圣经故事提供了引人入胜的解释。

例句：Rabbis often used midrash to explain complex legal concepts. 拉比经常使用米德拉什来解释复杂的法律概念。|近义词：commentary, exegesis, interpretation|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


midrashim: /mɪˈdrɑːʃɪm/ (US), /mɪˈdræʃɪm/ (UK)| n.|米德拉什（复数）|The plural form of midrash, referring to multiple Jewish interpretations of scripture.|例句：The midrashim offer diverse perspectives on the meaning of the Torah. 这些米德拉什对托拉的含义提供了不同的视角。

例句：Many midrashim explore the ethical dimensions of biblical stories. 许多米德拉什探讨了圣经故事的伦理维度。|近义词：commentaries, exegeses, interpretations|反义词: N/A|词性: noun (plural)
