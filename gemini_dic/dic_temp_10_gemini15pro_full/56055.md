docetic:/dəˈsɛtɪk/
adj.
幻影说，幻影论的
Relating to or characterized by Docetism, a heresy denying the full humanity of Jesus Christ, asserting that his body was a phantom or appearance and that he only seemed to suffer.
Docetic views were common in the early centuries of Christianity.  幻影说观点在基督教早期很普遍。
Early Christian thinkers rejected docetic interpretations of the Gospels. 早期的基督教思想家拒绝了对福音书的幻影式解读。
近义词:illusory, phantom, unreal
反义词:real, actual, tangible
词性:adjective

词源: From Greek *dokein* "to seem" + *-ikos* adjectival suffix, via Late Latin *doceticus*.
