over-the-counter:/ˌoʊvər ðə ˈkaʊntər/| adj. | 非处方药的；场外交易的 | Available without a prescription; traded directly between two parties, rather than through an exchange. | You can buy aspirin over the counter. 你可以不用处方买阿司匹林。|Synonyms: nonprescription, readily available | Antonyms: prescription-only, restricted | adjective
over-the-counter:/ˌoʊvər ðə ˈkaʊntər/| n. | 场外交易市场 | A market for securities not listed on a formal exchange. | Many small companies trade their stock over the counter. 许多小公司在场外交易市场交易他们的股票。|Synonyms: OTC market | Antonyms: stock exchange | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrase "over the counter," literally referring to goods sold directly to a customer over a shop counter, as opposed to requiring a prescription or special order. The term was later extended to financial transactions conducted directly between parties.
