cross-pollinates: /ˌkrɒs ˈpɒləˌneɪts/ (UK) /ˌkrɔːs ˈpɑːləˌneɪts/ (US)| v. |异花授粉，交叉授粉| To fertilize a plant with pollen from another plant.  The process also applies metaphorically to the exchange of ideas or influences between different groups or disciplines.| Bees cross-pollinate apple blossoms. 蜜蜂给苹果花异花授粉。|Synonyms:  cross-fertilize, interbreed (metaphorically)| Antonyms: self-pollinate| verb

cross-pollination: /ˌkrɒs ˌpɒləˈneɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌkrɔːs ˌpɑːləˈneɪʃn/ (US)| n. |异花授粉，交叉授粉| The transfer of pollen from one plant to another, or the mixing of ideas or influences.| Cross-pollination is essential for many fruit trees. 异花授粉对于许多果树至关重要。|Synonyms: cross-fertilization, hybridization (in plants)| Antonyms: self-pollination| noun
