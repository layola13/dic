emancipationist:/ɪˌmænsɪˈpeɪʃənɪst/
n.
解放主义者；废奴主义者
A person who advocates for emancipation, especially the abolition of slavery.
The emancipationist movement gained momentum in the 19th century.  19世纪，废奴运动势头强劲。
Frederick Douglass was a prominent emancipationist.  弗雷德里克·道格拉斯是一位杰出的废奴主义者。
近义词:abolitionist, liberator, reformer
反义词:slaveholder, enslaver, oppressor
词性:noun

emancipate:/ɪˈmænsɪpeɪt/
v.
解放；释放
To free from restraint, control, or power of another; to liberate.
Lincoln's Emancipation Proclamation emancipated enslaved people in Confederate states.  林肯的《解放宣言》解放了南方邦联的奴隶。
The organization works to emancipate women from oppressive social norms.  该组织致力于将妇女从压迫性的社会规范中解放出来。
近义词:liberate, free, release
反义词:enslave, imprison, confine
词性:verb

emancipation:/ɪˌmænsɪˈpeɪʃən/
n.
解放；释放
The act or process of being set free from legal, social, or political restrictions; liberation.
The emancipation of slaves was a turning point in American history.  奴隶的解放是美国历史上的一个转折点。
She fought for the emancipation of women in her country.  她为本国妇女的解放而斗争。
近义词:liberation, freedom, release
反义词:enslavement, oppression, captivity
词性:noun
