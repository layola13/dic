hinters:/ˈhɪntərz/| n.|暗示者；提示者|Someone who gives hints or clues.  A person who drops hints. |The hinters subtly suggested the answer without giving it away directly.  暗示者巧妙地暗示了答案，但没有直接说出来。|Synonyms: prompters, cuers, insinuators|Antonyms: deceivers, misleaders|Noun

Etymology->
From "hint" + "-er" (agent suffix). "Hint" comes from Old English "hentan" meaning "to seize, grasp."  The meaning evolved to "to allude to indirectly" in the 16th century.
