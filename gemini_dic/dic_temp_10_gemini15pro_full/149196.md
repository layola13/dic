oxygenators:/ˈɒksɪdʒəneɪtəz/ (US) /ˈɑːksɪdʒəneɪtərz/ (UK)| n. |氧合器|A device that adds oxygen to the blood, used during heart surgery or other procedures where the heart or lungs are not functioning normally.  It performs the function of the lungs. |The surgeon carefully monitored the oxygenators during the bypass surgery.|Synonyms: oxygenator machine, heart-lung machine (partially), extracorporeal membrane oxygenation (ECMO) machine|Antonyms:  None (The absence of an oxygenator wouldn't be a specific device, but rather a reliance on natural oxygenation.)|noun

oxygenator:/ˈɒksɪdʒəneɪtə(r)/ (US) /ˈɑːksɪdʒəneɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n. |氧合器|Singular form of oxygenators. |The oxygenator was essential for keeping the patient alive during the operation.|Synonyms: oxygenator machine, heart-lung machine (partially), extracorporeal membrane oxygenation (ECMO) machine|Antonyms: None|noun
