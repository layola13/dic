funky:/ˈfʌŋki/ | adj. | 时髦的，节奏强烈的，有霉味的 | Having a strong, distinctive, and typically rhythmic quality, especially in music; also, having a musty or unpleasant smell. |  例句：The band played a funky tune that got everyone dancing. 乐队演奏了一首时髦的曲子，让每个人都跳了起来。

例句：The old basement had a funky smell.  旧地下室有一股霉味。

近义词：groovy, hip, cool, stylish, rhythmic, musty | 反义词：bland, dull, stale, odorless | 词性: 形容词

funky:/ˈfʌŋki/ | n. | 芬克音乐 | A style of music, usually dance music, with a strong rhythmic feel, usually played on electric instruments. | 例句：He's really into funky and soul music. 他真的很喜欢放克和灵魂乐。

例句：The DJ played a set of classic funky tracks. DJ播放了一组经典的放克音乐曲目。

近义词：funk | 反义词： (No direct antonym, perhaps 'easy listening') | 词性: 名词
