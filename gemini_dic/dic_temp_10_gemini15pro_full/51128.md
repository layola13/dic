despiteful:/dɪˈspaɪtfʊl/| adj.|充满恶意,怀恨在心|Showing malicious ill will and a desire to hurt; malevolent.|He gave her a despiteful look. 他恶狠狠地瞪了她一眼。|近义词:malicious, spiteful, venomous, vindictive|反义词:benevolent, kind, compassionate|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Middle English *dispitous, *despitous, from Anglo-Norman *despitus, from Old French despitous, from despit (“spite, contempt”), from Late Latin despectus, past participle of despicere (“to look down on, despise”), from Latin dē (“down, from”) + specere (“to look”).
