disconnectedness:/ˌdɪskəˈnɛktɪdnəs/
|n.|
|不连贯性，脱节，孤立|
|The disconnectedness of the curriculum made it difficult for students to see the relevance of what they were learning. 课程的不连贯性使得学生难以看出他们所学知识的相关性。|
|例句：
There's a sense of disconnectedness between the government and the people. 政府和人民之间存在一种脱节感。

The report highlights the growing disconnectedness of young people from traditional social structures. 这份报告强调了年轻人与传统社会结构日益脱节的现象。|
|近义词：isolation, detachment, alienation, separation|
|反义词：connection, cohesion, unity, integration|
|词性: 名词|


disconnected:/ˌdɪskəˈnɛktɪd/
|adj.|
|不连贯的，脱节的，孤立的|
|The plot felt disconnected, jumping from one scene to another without a clear narrative thread.  情节感觉不连贯，从一个场景跳到另一个场景，没有清晰的叙事线索。|
|例句：
He seemed emotionally disconnected from his family. 他似乎在情感上与家人疏离。

The museum's exhibits felt disconnected and lacked a unifying theme.  博物馆的展品感觉脱节，缺乏一个统一的主题。|
|近义词：incoherent, fragmented, disjointed, rambling|
|反义词：connected, coherent, unified, integrated|
|词性: 形容词|


disconnect:/ˌdɪskəˈnɛkt/
|v.|
|断开，分离，切断|
|Please disconnect the cable from the wall socket before cleaning the appliance. 在清洁设备之前，请断开电缆与墙壁插座的连接。|
|例句：
The phone company threatened to disconnect our service if we didn't pay the bill. 电话公司威胁说如果我们不付账单就断开我们的服务。

He felt a growing need to disconnect from social media and reconnect with the real world. 他感到越来越需要断开与社交媒体的联系，重新与现实世界联系起来。|
|近义词：detach, separate, sever, unplug|
|反义词：connect, join, link, attach|
|词性: 动词|


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation or reversal) + connect + -ed + -ness.  "Connect" ultimately comes from the Latin "connectere," meaning "to bind together."
