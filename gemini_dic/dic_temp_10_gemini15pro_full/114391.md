make-a-virtue-of-necessity:/ˌmeɪk ə ˈvɜːrtʃuː əv nəˈsesɪti/ | idiom |  把不利条件变成有利条件；化被动为主动；善处逆境 | To pretend that something unpleasant is in fact desirable or beneficial.  This is often done when one has no choice but to accept a difficult situation. |  Since the shops were closed, we made a virtue of necessity and had a picnic in the park. 既然商店都关门了，我们只好将计就计，在公园里野餐。 |  make the best of a bad bargain, turn a disadvantage to one's advantage | grin and bear it, complain | phrase
