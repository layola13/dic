depredations:/ˌdeprəˈdeɪʃənz/ | n. |掠夺，破坏，蹂躏|Acts of destruction and plundering, often by an invading or hostile force.  The act of preying upon or plundering. | The depredations of the invaders left the city in ruins. 入侵者的掠夺使这座城市沦为废墟。 | synonyms:  pillaging, looting, plundering, ravaging, sacking, despoiling, despoliation | antonyms: preservation, protection, safeguarding | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dēpraedātiōn-, from Latin dēpraedārī (to plunder, rob), from dē- (from, away from) + praedārī (to plunder), from praeda (booty).
