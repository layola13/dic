chessil:/ˈtʃɛsɪl/
n.
Chesil Beach; a pebble beach
A bank or accumulation of pebbles or shingle on the seashore, especially one forming a beach. Specifically, the Chesil Beach, a long shingle beach on the coast of Dorset, England.
The Chesil Beach stretches for 18 miles along the Dorset coast.  切西尔海滩沿着多塞特海岸绵延18英里。
The constant movement of the waves has shaped the chessil into a unique formation.  海浪的不断运动将卵石滩塑造成独特的形状。
Synonyms: shingle beach, pebble bank, strand
Antonyms: cliff, headland
Etymology: From Old English *ceosol, *cisel ‘gravel’, related to Old High German kisil ‘gravel, sand’
