antirealist:/ˌæntiˈriːəlɪst/ | n. |反实在论者| Someone who believes that reality is not independent of our minds but is shaped by our beliefs, perceptions, and social constructs. |例句：The antirealist argued that scientific theories don't describe the world as it is, but rather as we perceive it.  反实在论者认为，科学理论描述的不是世界的本来面目，而是我们感知到的世界。

例句：Some antirealists believe that moral truths are subjective and depend on individual or cultural values. 一些反实在论者认为，道德真理是主观的，取决于个人或文化价值观。

|近义词：idealist, subjectivist, constructivist|反义词：realist|词性：noun


antirealists:/ˌæntiˈriːəlɪsts/ | n. |反实在论者（复数）| Plural form of "antirealist." |例句：The antirealists presented a compelling argument against the objective existence of the external world. 反实在论者提出了一个令人信服的论点，反对外部世界的客观存在。


例句：Many antirealists challenge the traditional view of scientific progress. 许多反实在论者挑战传统的科学进步观。

|近义词：idealists, subjectivists, constructivists|反义词：realists|词性：noun


词源:
From anti- + realist.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposed to." "Realist" comes from the Latin "res" meaning "thing" and refers to someone who believes in the independent existence of reality.
