parrots:/ˈpærəts/ (US), /ˈpærɒts/ (UK)| n.|鹦鹉|A tropical bird with a curved beak and brightly coloured feathers. Parrots are known for their ability to mimic sounds, including human speech. |例句：The parrots in the rainforest were squawking loudly. 雨林里的鹦鹉大声地叫着。

例句：My neighbor has two parrots that can talk. 我的邻居养了两只会说话的鹦鹉。|近义词：parakeet, cockatoo|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *paret*, alteration (influenced by *Pierre*) of Old French *perrot*, of uncertain origin. Possibly from Italian *pierotto* (“little Peter”), a pet name for the bird because of St. Peter's connection with the keys to heaven.
