cravat:/krəˈvæt/| n.|领巾，围巾，领结|A piece of cloth worn around the neck, especially by men, as a tie or part of a uniform.|He wore a silk cravat with his suit. 他穿西装时戴了一条丝绸领巾。|Synonyms: neckcloth, scarf, tie|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but consider "open collar")|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French cravate, from Cravate (Croat), referring to the scarves worn by Croatian mercenaries in the 17th century.
