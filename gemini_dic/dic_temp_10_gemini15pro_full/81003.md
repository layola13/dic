gradient: /ˈɡreɪdiənt/ (US), /ˈɡreɪdiənt/ (UK)| n. |梯度，坡度，倾斜度|An inclined surface; a slope; the rate of change of a quantity (such as temperature, pressure, or concentration) with respect to distance or another variable.|The cyclist struggled up the steep gradient.  骑自行车的人艰难地爬上陡坡。|Synonyms: slope, incline, grade, slant|Antonyms: level, flat|noun

gradient: /ˈɡreɪdiənt/ (US), /ˈɡreɪdiənt/ (UK)| adj. |倾斜的，渐变的|Changing gradually in degree, intensity, or color.|The painting featured a gradient background from light blue to deep indigo.  这幅画的背景是从浅蓝色到深靛蓝的渐变。|Synonyms: graded, gradual, progressive|Antonyms: abrupt, sudden, sharp|adjective
