glibnesses:/ˈɡlɪbnɪsɪz/| n. |油嘴滑舌，花言巧语|The quality of being glib; excessive fluency of speech or writing that suggests insincerity or superficiality.|His glibnesses about the economy made voters wary. 他对经济的花言巧语让选民们感到警惕。|近义词：smoothness, slickness, fluency, volubility|反义词: sincerity, earnestness, genuineness|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From glib + -ness.  Glib itself comes from Middle Low German or Middle Dutch *glibberich "slippery," related to gleiten "to glide."  The original sense of "slippery" evolved to "smooth-talking" and then to the negative connotation of insincere fluency.
