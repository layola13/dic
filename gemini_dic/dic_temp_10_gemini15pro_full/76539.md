gallanting:/ˈɡæləntɪŋ/ | n. |殷勤款待；献殷勤；护送女士 |The act of escorting or attending to a woman in a courteous and attentive manner. | He spent the evening gallanting the young ladies. 他晚上都在殷勤款待年轻女士们。| 近义词: courting, wooing, escorting | 反义词: neglecting, ignoring | 词性: noun (gerund or present participle)

gallant:/ˈɡælənt/ | adj. | 英勇的；勇敢的；献殷勤的 | Brave, heroic, chivalrous; showing courtesy and attentiveness, especially to women. | He made a gallant attempt to rescue the child from the burning building. 他英勇地试图从燃烧的建筑物中救出孩子。|近义词: brave, courageous, chivalrous, courteous | 反义词: cowardly, timid, rude | 词性: adjective


词源: From Old French "galant," meaning "courteous," "valiant," ultimately from Frankish *galant, from Proto-Germanic *ga- "together" + *leujan "to leap, jump."  The original sense was of a leaping, spirited horse, then a dashing young man.
