glossily:/ˈɡlɒsɪli/ (US) /ˈɡlɑːsɪli/ (UK)| adv.|光滑地，光亮地|Having a smooth, shiny surface or appearance; superficially attractive or plausible.|The magazine was printed on glossily paper. 这本杂志印在光滑的纸上。|近义词：smoothly, shiningly, slickly|反义词：dully, roughly, mattely|词性:副词
Etymology: From gloss + -ily.  "Gloss" comes from the Old French "glose" and ultimately from the Late Latin "glossa," meaning "explanation of a difficult word." The suffix "-ily" forms adverbs from adjectives.
