divagation:/ˌdaɪvəˈɡeɪʃən/ | n. | 漫游，离题，偏离 |A wandering away from the main subject or path; a digression. | The speaker's frequent divagations made it hard to follow his point. 演讲者频繁的离题使人难以理解他的观点。 |  The professor's lecture was filled with amusing divagations. 教授的讲座充满了有趣的跑题。 | 近义词: digression, deviation, excursion, tangent | 反义词: adherence, focus, concentration | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *divagationem, accusative singular of divagatio (“a wandering about”), from Latin divagari (“to wander about, digress”), from di- (“apart, in different directions”) + vagari (“to wander, roam”).
