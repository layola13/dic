mocked:/mɑːkt/ (US) /mɒkt/ (UK)| v. adj.| 嘲笑，模仿；被嘲笑的| To tease or laugh at someone in a scornful or contemptuous manner. Also, to imitate someone or something in a way that makes fun of them. |  He mocked her accent relentlessly. 他不停地嘲笑她的口音。
The comedian mocked the president's speech.  那个喜剧演员模仿了总统的演讲，对其进行了嘲讽。 | 近义词：ridiculed, teased, mimicked, imitated, scoffed at, derided | 反义词： praised, admired, respected, complimented |词性：动词，形容词

mocked:/mɑːkt/ (US) /mɒkt/ (UK)| adj.| 被嘲笑的，假的|Something that is mocked is treated with ridicule or contempt.  It can also mean something that is not real or genuine, but is made to look like something else. | The mocked-up spaceship looked surprisingly realistic.  这个模拟的宇宙飞船看起来惊人地逼真。
He felt mocked by the empty promises. 他感觉被那些空洞的承诺嘲弄了。| 近义词： ridiculed, derided, sham, fake, imitation| 反义词： genuine, real, authentic, respected |词性：形容词
