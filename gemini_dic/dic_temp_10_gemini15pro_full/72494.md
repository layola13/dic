fluorescently: /flʊəˈresəntli/ /flɔːˈresəntli/
| adv. | 荧光地；发荧光地 | Emitting or reflecting light by fluorescence; in a way that is bright and glowing. | The bacteria were fluorescently labeled. 这些细菌被荧光标记。| brightly, vividly, luminously | dimly, faintly | adverb

fluorescent: /flʊəˈresənt/ /flɔːˈresənt/
| adj. | 荧光的；发荧光的 |  (of a substance) having or showing fluorescence. |  She wore a fluorescent pink jacket. 她穿着一件荧光粉色的夹克。 | bright, vivid, luminous | dull, dark | adjective

fluoresce: /flʊəˈres/ /flɔːˈres/
| v. | 发荧光 |  (of a substance) emit light by fluorescence. | Certain minerals fluoresce under ultraviolet light.  某些矿物质在紫外线下会发出荧光。 | glow, shine, radiate | darken, dim | verb

fluorescence: /flʊəˈresəns/ /flɔːˈresəns/
| n. | 荧光 |  the emission of light by a substance that has absorbed light or other electromagnetic radiation. | The fluorescence of the dye allowed researchers to track the cells.  这种染料的荧光使研究人员能够追踪细胞。 | luminescence, glow, radiance | darkness, dullness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fluorescent*, from *fluorescer* "to be fluorescent," from Latin *fluere* "to flow" + *-escent*, a suffix forming adjectives meaning "beginning to be" or "slightly."
