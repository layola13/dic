contradiction:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkʃn/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkʃn/ (US)| n.|矛盾，反驳，否认|A combination of statements, ideas, or features of a situation that are opposed to one another.|He claimed that there was a contradiction between the government's ideas and its actual policy. 他声称政府的理念与其具体政策之间存在矛盾。|synonyms:inconsistency, disparity, discrepancy, incongruity, conflict, clash, difference, variance, disagreement, opposition|antonyms:agreement, consistency, harmony|noun

contradict:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkt/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkt/ (US)| v.|反驳，否认，与…矛盾|To deny the truth of (a statement), especially by asserting the opposite.|The witness contradicted the policeman's testimony. 目击者的证词与警察的证词相矛盾。|synonyms:deny, refute, dispute, challenge, oppose, counter, gainsay|antonyms:confirm, support, corroborate|verb
