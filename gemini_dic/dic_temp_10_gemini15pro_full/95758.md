inconspicuousness:/ˌɪnkənˈspɪkjuəsnəs/| n. |不显眼，不起眼|The state or quality of not being easily noticed or attracting attention.|Her inconspicuousness in the crowd made it easy for her to observe unnoticed. 她不引人注目，让她很容易在人群中不被注意地观察。|近义词：unobtrusiveness, inconspicuousness, unnoticeableness |反义词：conspicuousness, prominence, noticeability|noun


inconspicuous:/ˌɪnkənˈspɪkjuəs/| adj. |不显眼的，不引人注目的|Not easily seen or noticed; not attracting attention.|The detective wore inconspicuous clothing so he could blend in. 侦探穿着不显眼的衣服以便融入环境。|近义词：unobtrusive, unnoticeable, subtle |反义词：conspicuous, prominent, noticeable|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *inconspicuus, from in- "not" + conspicuus "visible, striking," from conspicere "to look at, observe," from com- "together" + specere "to look."
