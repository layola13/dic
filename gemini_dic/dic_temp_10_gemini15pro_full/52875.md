diminishments:/dɪˈmɪnɪʃmənts/
|n.|减少，缩小，降低|The company suffered significant diminishments in sales this quarter.  这家公司本季度销售额大幅下降。|
|
|English Explanation:  A decrease in size, importance, or intensity; a reduction.  Usually used in the plural form to refer to multiple instances of decreasing.|
|
|例句：The recent diminishments in staff have affected productivity. 最近员工人数的减少影响了生产力。|
|
|例句：Budget diminishments forced the school to cut several programs. 预算减少迫使学校削减了几个项目。|
|
|近义词：reductions, decreases, declines, curtailments|
|
|反义词：increases, expansions, augmentations|
|
|词性: noun|
|
|词源：From the verb "diminish," which comes from Old French "diminuer," from Latin "diminuere" (to lessen), from "dis-" (apart, away) + "minuere" (to make small).|
