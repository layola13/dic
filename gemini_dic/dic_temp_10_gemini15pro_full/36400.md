coistrel:/ˈkɔɪstrəl/| n.|懦夫，胆小鬼；无赖；下贱的人|A cowardly, craven person; a knave; a base fellow.|He was called a coistrel for refusing to fight. 他因为拒绝战斗而被称为懦夫。|Synonyms: coward, craven, poltroon, dastard|Antonyms: hero, brave, courageous|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *coistre*, a groom (from Latin *coxa*, hip, thigh), perhaps referring to a groom's position when assisting a knight to mount his horse. The meaning later shifted to refer to a base or cowardly person.
