objectifying: /əbˈdʒɛktɪfaɪɪŋ/ | adj. | 使...物化，将...视为客体 | Treating a person as an object, especially sexually, without regard to their feelings or personality. |  The advertisement was criticized for objectifying women. 这则广告因物化女性而受到批评。|  dehumanizing, degrading, depersonalizing | humanizing, respecting, valuing | adjective

objectify: /əbˈdʒɛktɪfaɪ/ | v. | 使...物化，将...视为客体 | To treat someone as an object, especially sexually, without regard to their feelings or personality. | He objectified women, seeing them only as sexual conquests. 他物化女性，只把她们视为性征服的对象。 | dehumanize, degrade, depersonalize | humanize, respect, value | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *objectivificare, from Latin objectus (“thrown before, presented, set against”) + -ificare (“-fying”).  Essentially, turning someone into an "object" to be acted upon rather than a subject with agency.
