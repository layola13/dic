hydrocephalous:/ˌhaɪdrəˈsɛfələs/| adj.|脑积水的|A condition in which fluid accumulates in the brain, typically in young children, enlarging the head and sometimes causing brain damage.  Characterized by an abnormal accumulation of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) within the ventricles of the brain.|The child was diagnosed with hydrocephalous shortly after birth.|Synonyms: water on the brain|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but a healthy brain without excess fluid could be considered an opposite state)|adjective
