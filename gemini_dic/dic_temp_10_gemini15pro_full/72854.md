folklores:/ˈfəʊklɔːz/ | n. | 民间传说；民俗 | The body of expressive culture shared by a particular group of people, encompassing the traditions common to that culture, including tales, legends, myths, proverbs, folk songs, dances, games, jokes, and customs. |  Many folklores have been passed down through generations. 许多民间传说世代相传。| synonyms: traditions, customs, legends, myths | antonyms: facts, history | noun

folklore:/ˈfəʊklɔː(r)/ | n. | 民间传说；民俗 | Traditional beliefs, customs, and stories of a community, passed through the generations by word of mouth. |  Irish folklore is rich with tales of fairies and leprechauns. 爱尔兰民间传说中充满了仙女和小妖精的故事。| synonyms: traditions, customs, legends, myths | antonyms: facts, history | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From folk + lore.  "Folk" meaning "people" (Old English folc) and "lore" meaning "learning, teaching, instruction" (Old English lār).
