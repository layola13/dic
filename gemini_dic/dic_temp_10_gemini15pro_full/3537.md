against one's grain:/əˈɡɛnst wʌnz ɡreɪn/| idiom |违背意愿；格格不入|Going against the grain means doing something that is contrary to one's natural inclinations or preferences. It feels unnatural or uncomfortable.| Asking for help goes against the grain for him, he's always been fiercely independent.  请求帮助违背了他的意愿，他一直非常独立。| Working on weekends goes against my grain. 周末工作违背我的意愿。 |akin to, contrary to, in opposition to| with one's grain, agreeable to, in accordance with |idiom
