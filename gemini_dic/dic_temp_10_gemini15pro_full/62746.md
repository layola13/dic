engloom:/ɪnˈɡluːm/ | v. | 使忧郁，使沮丧；笼罩，包围 | To cast a gloom over; to darken or obscure; to envelop in darkness or gloom. | The news of the defeat engloomed the entire nation. 败北的消息使整个国家陷入了沮丧。|例句：A sense of foreboding engloomed her. 一种不祥的预感使她忧心忡忡。|近义词：depress, sadden, overshadow, shroud |反义词：brighten, cheer, illuminate |词性:动词

engloom:/ɪnˈɡluːm/ | n. | 忧郁，沮丧；昏暗，阴暗 | A state of gloom or depression; darkness or shadow.  |  The engloom of the forest was unsettling. 森林的昏暗令人不安。 | 例句：The engloom after the storm was palpable. 暴风雨过后，阴郁的气氛清晰可感。 | 近义词：gloom, darkness, depression, melancholy |反义词：brightness, light, cheerfulness, joy | 词性:名词
