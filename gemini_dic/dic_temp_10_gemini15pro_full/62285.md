end-run: /ˈɛnd rʌn/ (US) /ˈɛnd rʌn/ (UK)
| n.| 迂回战术；绕过障碍；变通方法 | An action that avoids dealing with rules, established procedures, or restrictions, often to achieve something more quickly or effectively. | The company tried an end-run around environmental regulations by building its factory in another country.  这家公司试图通过在另一个国家建厂来规避环境法规。| synonyms: bypass, workaround, circumvent, sidestep | antonyms:  confront, face, address | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From American football, where an end-run is a play in which a running back carries the ball around the end of the line of scrimmage.  This sporting term was then extended metaphorically to other contexts.
