madrigal:/ˈmædrɪɡəl/ | n. | 牧歌; 合唱曲; 情歌; | A part-song for several voices, especially one of the Renaissance period, typically arranged in elaborate counterpoint and without instrumental accompaniment.  A short lyrical poem, especially of a pastoral or amorous character. | Example 1:  The choir sang a beautiful madrigal by Byrd.  合唱团演唱了一首伯德创作的优美牧歌。

Example 2:  He wrote her a madrigal expressing his love. 他给她写了一首情歌表达他的爱意。 | 近义词: song, ballad, ditty, carol, part-song | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps cacophony if referring to the sound) | 词性: noun
