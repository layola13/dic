cloistered:/ˈklɔɪstərd/ (US) /ˈklɔɪstəd/ (UK)| adj.|隐居的，与世隔绝的；修道院的|Shut away from the world; secluded, especially in a monastery or convent.  Referring to a place or way of life that is sheltered from the outside world, often associated with religious orders.|The writer led a cloistered life, rarely leaving his secluded cabin in the woods.  这位作家过着隐居的生活，很少离开他在树林里僻静的小屋。|近义词：secluded, isolated, sheltered, monastic, withdrawn|反义词：worldly, public, open, exposed|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Middle English *cloistren*, from Anglo-French *cloistre*, from Late Latin *claustrum* ("cloister, enclosure, monastery"), from Latin *claudere* ("to close, shut").
