flatline:/ˈflætlaɪn/| v. n.|变平；失去活力；死亡（尤指心跳停止）；|To become level or static, especially after a period of growth or progress.  (As a noun) A state of inactivity or stagnation. In medical contexts, it refers to a state where the heart stops beating and a straight line appears on a heart monitor. |The company's profits flatlined after several years of growth. 公司的利润在几年的增长后停滞不前了。

The patient's heart flatlined, but doctors were able to revive him. 病人的心跳停止了，但是医生们成功地使他苏醒。|近义词：stagnate, plateau, deteriorate (v.); standstill, slump (n.)|反义词:increase, improve, rise (v.); growth, surge (n.)|词性:verb, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "flat line," referring to the straight horizontal line on a heart monitor indicating no heartbeat.  "Flat" meaning level and "line" meaning a continuous mark.
