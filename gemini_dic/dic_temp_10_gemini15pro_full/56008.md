do-goodings:/ˈduːˌɡʊdɪŋz/
|n.|
|做好事的人; 爱管闲事的人; 不切实际的理想主义者|
|People who try to do good, often in a way that is seen as interfering or unwelcome.  It often carries a slightly negative or ironic connotation, suggesting that their efforts are misguided, naive, or self-righteous.|
|The village was full of do-goodings trying to 'improve' the lives of the locals.|村子里到处都是些想“改善”当地人生活的好事者。

|The do-goodings were handing out blankets to the homeless.|那些爱管闲事的人在向无家可归的人分发毯子。
|近义词: philanthropists, humanitarians, reformers, altruists, social workers|
|反义词: malefactors, wrongdoers, evildoers|
|词性: noun (usually plural)|
|词源: From "do-gooder" + "-ing" (pluralizing suffix), with "do-gooder" attested from the late 19th century. Ultimately from the verb "do" and the adjective "good".|
