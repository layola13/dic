anglos:/ˈæŋɡloʊz/
n.
盎格鲁-撒克逊人；英国人；非拉美裔白人
People of English or Anglo-Saxon descent.  Specifically, in the Americas, white people of non-Latino heritage.
例句：
The anglos controlled much of the land in the early 20th century. 盎格鲁-撒克逊人在20世纪早期控制着大部分土地。

The term "anglos" is sometimes used in the Southwest to refer to white Americans. 在美国西南部，“anglos”一词有时指美国白人。

近义词：English, British, Americans (in some contexts), whites (in some contexts)
反义词：Non-anglos, Hispanics (in some contexts)
词性：名词(plural)

词源: Shortened from Anglo-Saxon.  Ultimately from Latin Angli, referring to the Angles, one of the Germanic peoples who settled in Britain.
