clutching:/ˈklʌtʃɪŋ/ /ˈklʌtʃɪŋ/| adj. | 抓紧的，紧握的 | Holding tightly onto something, often out of fear or anxiety. |  She was clutching her purse nervously. 她紧张地抓着她的钱包。|  grasping, gripping, holding tight | releasing, loosening | adjective

clutching:/ˈklʌtʃɪŋ/ /ˈklʌtʃɪŋ/| n. | 抓紧，紧握 | The act of holding tightly onto something. | The child's clutching of his mother's hand was reassuring.  孩子紧紧抓住母亲的手，这让人感到安心。| grasp, grip | release | noun

词源: From the verb "clutch," which comes from the Old English word *clyccan*, meaning "to bend, hook."
