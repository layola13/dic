diminutive:/dɪˈmɪnjətɪv/| adj. n.|小的，微小的；爱称|A diminutive person or thing is very small.  A diminutive word, name, or form of a word is one that suggests smallness, affection, or familiarity. |She has diminutive hands for an adult. 她成年人的手却很小。| synonyms: small, tiny, miniature, minute, petite, compact, concise, brief | antonyms: large, huge, enormous, gigantic |adjective

diminutive:/dɪˈmɪnjətɪv/| n.|指小词，昵称|A diminutive word, name, or form of a word is one that suggests smallness, affection, or familiarity.|"Mikey" is a diminutive of "Michael." Mikey是Michael的昵称。| synonyms: nickname, pet name, sobriquet | antonyms: full name, formal name |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dīminūtīvus, from Latin dīminūtus (past participle of dīminuere “to lessen”), from dē- “from” + minuere “to lessen.”
