deformable:/dɪˈfɔːrməbl/ | adj. | 可变形的，可形变的 | Capable of being deformed or changed in shape. |  The clay is deformable in the hands of the sculptor.  这块黏土在雕塑家手中可以变形。| synonyms: pliable, malleable, ductile, plastic | antonyms: rigid, inflexible, stiff, unyielding | adjective

deform:/dɪˈfɔːrm/ | v. | 使变形，扭曲 | To spoil the natural shape of something. | The intense heat deformed the metal.  高温使金属变形。 | synonyms: distort, contort, warp, twist | antonyms: straighten, rectify, fix, repair | verb

deformation:/ˌdiːfɔːrˈmeɪʃn/ | n. | 变形，畸形 | The act or process of deforming or the state of being deformed. | The deformation of the rock was caused by tectonic pressure. 岩石的变形是由构造压力引起的。 | synonyms: distortion, contortion, warping | antonyms: correction, rectification | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dēformābilis, from Latin dēformāre (“to deform”) + -bilis (“-able”).  Dēformāre is from dē- (“from, away”) + formāre (“to form, shape”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁- (“to put, place”).
