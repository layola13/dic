parallaxes:/ˈpærəlæksɪz/
n.
视差(复数)
The apparent displacement of an observed object due to a change in the position of the observer.
Astronomers use parallaxes to measure the distances to nearby stars. 天文学家利用视差来测量附近恒星的距离。
Parallaxes are crucial for determining distances in astronomy. 视差对于确定天文学中的距离至关重要。
近义词:displacements
反义词:(无)
词性:名词

反义词:(无)

词源:
From Late Latin *parallaxus, from Ancient Greek παράλλαξις (parállaxis, “alternation, mutual inclination”), from παραλλάσσω (parallássō, “to alternate, pass by one another”), from παρά (pará, “beside, alongside”) + ἀλλάσσω (allássō, “to alter, change”).
