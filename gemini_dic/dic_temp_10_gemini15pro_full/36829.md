colonise: /ˈkɒlənaɪz/ (British) /ˈkɑːlənaɪz/ (American)| vt.|殖民，拓殖；移植，定居|To establish a colony or colonies in (a territory).  To settle in or establish control over (an area) and send people to live there.|The British colonised Australia in the 18th century.  英国人在18世纪殖民了澳大利亚。|Synonyms: settle, populate, occupy, inhabit|Antonyms: abandon, desert, evacuate|Verb

colonise: /ˈkɒlənaɪz/ (British) /ˈkɑːlənaɪz/ (American)| vt.|（动植物）繁衍，遍布|To spread into and establish a presence in (a place or environment).|The weeds have colonised the entire garden.  杂草已经蔓延到整个花园。|Synonyms: spread, proliferate, overrun, infest|Antonyms: eradicate, exterminate|Verb
