gallows: /ˈɡæləʊz/ (British) /ˈɡæloʊz/ (American)| n. |绞刑架; 绞台 |A wooden frame, typically with two upright posts and a crossbeam, used for hanging condemned criminals. |The condemned man was led to the gallows.  那个被判刑的人被带上了绞刑架。| The prisoner mounted the gallows. 囚犯走上绞刑架。|近义词: scaffold |反义词:  (无直接反义词，可考虑 reprieve - 缓刑)|名词

词源:
From Middle English *galwes, from Old English *galga, gealga (“gallows”), from Proto-Germanic *galgō (“gallows, cross”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰelgʰ- (“pole, stick”). Cognate with West Frisian galge (“gallows”), Dutch galg (“gallows”), German Galgen (“gallows”), Danish galge (“gallows”), Swedish galge (“gallows”).
