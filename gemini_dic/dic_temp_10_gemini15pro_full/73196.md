force-feeding:/fɔːrsˈfiːdɪŋ/ | n. | 强制喂食 |The act of forcing someone or something to eat, typically against their will.  |Doctors resorted to force-feeding the hunger striker. 医生们对绝食抗议者采取了强制喂食。

The farmer was force-feeding the geese to produce foie gras.  农民强制喂食鹅以生产鹅肝。| Synonyms: gavage, compulsory feeding | Antonyms: voluntary eating, self-feeding | noun


force-feed:/fɔːrsˈfiːd/ | vt. | 强制喂食 | To make someone or something eat, typically against their will. | They force-fed the prisoners. 他们强制喂食囚犯。

She had to force-feed her sick cat to get it to take its medicine. 她不得不强行喂她生病的猫吃药。| Synonyms: stuff, cram, gorge | Antonyms: starve, deprive of food | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From force + feed.  Force (from Old French "force," ultimately from Latin "fortis" meaning "strong") and feed (from Old English "fēdan" meaning "to feed").
