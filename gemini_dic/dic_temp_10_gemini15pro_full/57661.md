drawbridges:/ˈdrɔːbrɪdʒɪz/
noun
吊桥
A bridge that can be raised or lowered to allow ships to pass underneath or to prevent access.
The drawbridges were raised to allow the tall ship to pass. 吊桥升起来了，好让高大的船只通过。

The medieval castle was protected by a moat and several drawbridges. 中世纪城堡由护城河和几座吊桥保护。

近义词:bascule bridge, swing bridge
反义词:(没有直接的反义词，可以考虑 fixed bridge 固定桥梁)
词性:n


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *drawbrigge*, from Old English *dragan* (to draw, pull) + *brycg* (bridge).  The word literally means a "bridge that is drawn" (pulled up).
