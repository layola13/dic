cheapish:/ˈtʃiːpɪʃ/| adj.|略微便宜的，有点便宜的|Somewhat cheap; relatively inexpensive.  It implies that something is inexpensive but perhaps not of the highest quality. |This restaurant is cheapish, but the food is surprisingly good. 这家餐馆有点便宜，但食物出奇的好。|The fabric feels a bit cheapish.  这种布料感觉有点廉价。|近义词：inexpensive, low-priced, reasonable, budget-friendly|反义词：expensive, costly, pricey|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From 'cheap' + '-ish'. The suffix '-ish' is used to form adjectives meaning "somewhat" or "approximately".  'Cheap' itself has Old English origins, from 'cēap' meaning "bargain, purchase, price."
