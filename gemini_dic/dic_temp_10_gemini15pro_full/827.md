abstinence: /ˈæbstɪnəns/
n.
节制；禁欲；戒绝
The practice of refraining from indulging in something, typically alcohol or sex.

He practiced total abstinence from alcohol.
他完全戒酒。

The doctor recommended abstinence from smoking.
医生建议戒烟。

近义词: self-restraint, self-denial, temperance, sobriety, moderation
反义词: indulgence, excess, intemperance
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin abstinentia (“act of holding back”), from abstinēns, present participle of abstinēre (“to hold back”), from abs (“from, away”) + tenēre (“to hold”).

USAGE->
The benefits of abstinence are numerous.  节制的好处很多。

Abstinence from certain foods can improve your health.  戒除某些食物可以改善你的健康。

He vowed abstinence from all forms of vice. 他发誓戒除一切形式的恶习。

Her abstinence from social media has improved her focus. 她戒除社交媒体后，注意力得到了改善。


Total abstinence is often difficult to maintain. 完全的节制往往难以维持。

The group promotes abstinence as a way to prevent teenage pregnancies.  该组织提倡禁欲作为预防青少年怀孕的一种方法。
