antirealist:/ˌæntiˈriːəlɪst/ | n. adj. |反实在论者，反实在论的 | Someone who believes that reality is not independent of our minds but is shaped by our beliefs, perceptions, and social constructs.  A philosophical position that denies the objective existence of some aspect of reality. |  例句：The antirealist argued that scientific theories are just useful tools, not descriptions of a mind-independent world. 反实在论者认为，科学理论只是有用的工具，而不是对一个独立于心灵的世界进行的描述。

例句：He took an antirealist stance on the existence of moral truths. 他对道德真理的存在采取了反实在论的立场。 | 近义词：idealist, subjectivist, constructivist | 反义词：realist | 词性：名词, 形容词
