arithmometer:/ˌærɪθˈmɒmɪtər/ (US) /ˌærɪθˈmɒmɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n. |计算器；四则运算器|A mechanical calculating device, typically one with a set of rotating numbered wheels.|The arithmometer, invented by Thomas de Colmar, was a significant step in the history of computing.|Synonym: calculator, adding machine|Antonym: abacus (arguably, as it's a different type of calculating device)|Noun
