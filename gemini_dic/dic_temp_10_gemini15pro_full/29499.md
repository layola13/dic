cataractous:/ˌkætəˈræktəs/| adj.|患白内障的；瀑布似的；(洪水)奔腾的|Affected with cataracts; resembling a waterfall; involving a large rush of water.|例句：His cataractous eyes made it difficult for him to see. 他的白内障眼睛使他很难看清东西。

例句：The river flowed in a cataractous rush towards the sea.  这条河奔腾着流向大海。|近义词：opaque, cloudy (for eyes); torrential, cascading (for water)|反义词：clear, transparent (for eyes); calm, still (for water)|词性:形容词

Etymology: From Late Latin *cataractosus, from Latin cataracta (“waterfall, floodgate, portcullis”) + -osus (suffix forming adjectives meaning "full of," "abounding in," "prone to," "like").  The Latin cataracta is from Ancient Greek κατάρρακτος (katárraktos, “down-rushing, precipitous”), from καταρράσσω (katarrássō, “I dash down”), from κατά (katá, “down”) + ῥάσσω (rhássō, “I dash, smash”).
