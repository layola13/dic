alembroth: /ˌæləmˈbrɒθ/ | n. | 汞盐，氯化汞氨，盐酸汞氨 | A double chloride of mercury and ammonium, used in alchemy and early chemistry.  A substance used as a solvent in alchemy, supposedly capable of dissolving gold. | The alchemist claimed his alembroth could dissolve any metal. 炼金术士声称他的汞盐可以溶解任何金属。 |  Synonyms:  sal sapientiae (salt of wisdom), white precipitate | Antonyms: None | noun

alembroth: /ˌæləmˈbrɒθ/ | n. |  炼金药 | A medicinal preparation, often associated with Paracelsus.  A supposed universal solvent sought by alchemists. |  Paracelsus prescribed alembroth for various ailments.  帕拉塞尔苏斯用汞盐来治疗各种疾病。 | Synonyms: elixir, panacea | Antonyms: poison, toxin | noun
