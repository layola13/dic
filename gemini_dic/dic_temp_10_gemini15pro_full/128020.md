nepotism: /ˈnepətɪzəm/ | n. | 任人唯亲；裙带关系 | The practice among those with power or influence of favoring relatives or friends, especially by giving them jobs. | Nepotism is rampant in this company; most of the management are related to the owner. 这家公司裙带关系盛行；大多数管理人员都与老板有亲戚关系。 | synonyms: cronyism, favoritism | antonyms: meritocracy | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *népotisme*, from Italian *nepotismo*, from *nipote* "nephew," from Latin *nepotem* (nominative *nepos*) "grandson, nephew."  The term originated in the Middle Ages, referring to the practice of popes appointing their nephews (often illegitimate sons) to positions of power within the Catholic Church.
