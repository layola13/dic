coliseum:/ˌkɑːləˈsiːəm/ | n. |竞技场，体育馆|A large amphitheater or stadium.  A place for public entertainment, especially games or contests.|The Los Angeles Lakers play their home games at the Staples Center, a modern coliseum. 洛杉矶湖人队在斯台普斯中心（一个现代化的竞技场）进行他们的主场比赛。|The gladiators fought in the coliseum.  角斗士在竞技场中战斗。|Synonyms: stadium, arena, amphitheater|Antonyms:  theater, opera house|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Colosseum*,  originally referring specifically to the Flavian Amphitheater in Rome,  perhaps because of its colossal size or a colossal statue of Nero nearby.
