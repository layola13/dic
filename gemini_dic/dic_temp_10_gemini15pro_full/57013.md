double-thinker:/ˈdʌbəlˌθɪŋkər/| n.|双重思想者，两面派|A person who accepts two contradictory beliefs as correct, often as a result of political indoctrination.  This involves the ability to hold two opposing ideas in one's mind simultaneously, and accept both of them.|The party required its members to be double-thinkers, capable of believing whatever the government told them, even if it contradicted reality.|Synonym: hypocrite, conformist|Antonym: freethinker, dissident|noun
