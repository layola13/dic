disallegiance:/ˌdɪsəˈliːdʒəns/
n.|不忠; 背叛; 叛逆|The act of refusing to support or obey a government or leader.|His disallegiance to the king led to his imprisonment. 他对国王的不忠导致了他的监禁。|近义词：treachery, betrayal, disloyalty, sedition, rebellion|反义词：loyalty, allegiance, fidelity|词性:名词

Etymology:
From dis- + allegiance.  "Dis-" is a Latin prefix meaning "apart," "asunder," "away," expressing negation, reversal, or undoing. "Allegiance" comes from Old French "alegeance," meaning "loyalty" or "obligation," derived from "lige," meaning "liege," ultimately from Frankish "*leudes," meaning "people."
