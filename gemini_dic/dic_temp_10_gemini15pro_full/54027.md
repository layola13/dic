discursivenesses:/dɪsˈkɜːrsɪvnɪsɪz/
|n.| 冗长；离题；散漫|The state or quality of being discursive; rambling or digressive speech or writing.|His writing suffers from excessive discursivenesses, making it hard to follow the main point.|近义词: prolixity, rambling, digression, wordiness|反义词: conciseness, brevity, succinctness|词性: 名词

词源:  From discursive + -ness + -es (plural).  Discursive comes from Late Latin *discursīvus, from Latin discursus ("a running about"), past participle of discurrere ("to run about, run to and fro, digress"), from dis- ("apart, in different directions") + currere ("to run").
