/flæɡz/ | n. | 旗帜；标志；旗帜 | a piece of cloth, usually rectangular and attached to a pole at one edge, that has a design that shows it represents a country or a group | The American flag flew proudly in the wind. 美国国旗在风中骄傲地飘扬。| banner, standard, pennant | - | noun


/flæɡz/ | v. |  标记；衰退；变弱 | 1. mark (an item) for attention or treatment in a specified way. 2. become tired, weaker, or less enthusiastic. |  I've flagged the emails that need a reply. 我已经标记了需要回复的电子邮件。| mark, label, indicate | strengthen, revive | verb



ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *flagge*, from Old Norse *flaga* (“to flap, flutter”), from Proto-Germanic *flagōną* (“to fly, flutter”), from Proto-Indo-European *pleg- (“to strike”).
