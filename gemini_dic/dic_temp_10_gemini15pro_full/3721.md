aggrieve: /əˈɡriːv/ | vt. |  使委屈；使悲痛；侵害 | To cause grief or injury to someone, especially by causing unfair treatment. |  例句：She was deeply aggrieved by the accusations. 她被这些指控深深地伤害了。

|例句：The new tax laws aggrieved many citizens. 新的税法使许多公民感到委屈。| 近义词：offend, distress, upset, wrong, injure | 反义词：comfort, console, soothe, please | 词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French agrever, from a- (prefix indicating intensification) + grever "to burden, grieve," from Latin gravare "to burden," related to gravis "heavy."
