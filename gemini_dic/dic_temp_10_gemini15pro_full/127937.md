neonomian:/ˌniːoʊˈnoʊmiən/| n. adj.|新律法主义者，新律法主义的|A person who advocates or believes in neonomianism, a doctrine that emphasized the importance of the moral law in the Christian life, particularly after justification.|John was accused of being a neonomian by his more traditional brethren.  约翰被他那些更传统的弟兄指责为新律法主义者。|The preacher's neonomian views were controversial within the church.  这位牧师的新律法主义观点在教会内部引起了争议。|近义词:legalist|反义词:antinomian|词性:名词，形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *neos* (new) + *nomos* (law) + -ian (suffix forming nouns and adjectives).
