aeries: /ˈɛəriːz/ | n. | 鹰巢，雕巢；高处的住所 | A nest of a bird of prey, especially an eagle, typically built high up on a cliff or in a tree; a lofty dwelling place. |  The climbers spotted aeries of golden eagles high on the cliffs.  登山者在高高的悬崖上发现了金雕的巢穴。

The ancient castle, perched atop the mountain, seemed like an aerie. 这座古老的城堡坐落在山顶上，看起来像一个鹰巢。 | 近义词: nests, eyries, dwellings | 反义词: ground, lowlands | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *eirye*, from Old French *eire*, from Latin *aria* (“nest of a bird of prey”), probably of Germanic origin. Related to German *Horst*.
