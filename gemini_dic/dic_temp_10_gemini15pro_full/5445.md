almsfolk: /ˈɑːmzfoʊk/ | n. | 受施者，接受救济的人 | People who receive alms; recipients of charity. | The almsfolk gathered outside the church, hoping for a warm meal. 受施者聚集在教堂外，希望能得到一顿热饭。|  The almsfolk gratefully accepted the donations. 受施者感激地接受了捐赠。 | Synonyms: beggars, paupers, the needy, the indigent | Antonyms: philanthropists, donors, benefactors | Noun

Etymology:
From Old English ælmesfolc, from ælmesse (alms) + folc (folk).  The word "alms" ultimately derives from the Greek word "eleēmosynē" meaning "pity, mercy, alms."
