boards:/bɔːrdz/ | n. |木板，板子；董事会；伙食；舞台|A flat piece of wood; a group of people who manage a company or organization; meals provided regularly; the stage in a theatre. |The house was built of wooden boards.|近义词：panels, planks, committee, council, food, stage|反义词：  (For "boards" as in wood planks) none; (For "board" as in a group of people) individuals |noun


boards:/bɔːrdz/ | v. |上（船、火车、飞机等）；用板盖上；提供膳宿|To get onto a ship, train, or aircraft; to cover with boards; to provide with regular meals and lodging.|We boarded the plane at 9 am.|近义词：embark, mount, cover, lodge, accommodate|反义词：disembark, alight|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English "bord," meaning "plank, table, side of a ship," from Proto-Germanic "*burdam."  The meaning expanded over time to include various other flat surfaces and then to groups of people meeting around a table (a board).
