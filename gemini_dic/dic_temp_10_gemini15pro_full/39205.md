congestion:/kənˈdʒɛstʃən/ | n. | 拥塞；堵塞；充血 | An excessive accumulation of something, typically people or vehicles, leading to obstruction or overcrowding. |  The traffic congestion in the city is terrible during rush hour. 城市在高峰时段交通拥堵严重。

The congestion in his chest made it difficult to breathe.  他胸部的充血使他呼吸困难。 | 近义词: overcrowding, blockage, gridlock | 反义词: flow, clearance, emptiness | 词性: 名词

congest:/kənˈdʒɛst/ | vt. | 使拥挤；阻塞；充血 | To cause an excessive accumulation in, leading to obstruction or overcrowding. |  The narrow roads were congested with traffic. 狭窄的道路被车辆堵塞了。

His sinuses were congested due to a cold. 他的鼻窦因感冒而充血。 | 近义词: overcrowd, block, clog | 反义词: clear, unclog, empty | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *congerere*, "to bring together," from *com-* ("together") + *gerere* ("to carry, bear").  The noun form, "congestion," arose later.
