headroom:/ˈhɛdruːm/ (US), /ˈhɛdruːm/ (UK)| n.|净空；顶部空间；余量；安全裕度|The amount of clear space between the top of someone's head and the ceiling or other overhead object. Also refers to the margin of safety, tolerance, or flexibility in a system or plan.|The car has plenty of headroom. 这辆车头部空间很宽敞。|Synonyms: clearance, space, leeway, margin, allowance|Antonyms: confinement, restriction|noun

headroom:/ˈhɛdruːm/ (US), /ˈhɛdruːm/ (UK)| n.|净空；顶部空间；余量；安全裕度|Figuratively, the amount of resources, time, or money available beyond what is strictly necessary, allowing for unexpected problems or changes.|We built some headroom into the budget in case costs increase.  我们在预算中预留了一些余量，以防成本增加。|Synonyms: buffer, cushion, reserve, slack|Antonyms: deficit, shortage|noun
