coronavirus: /kəˈrəʊnəvaɪrəs/ (UK) /kəˈroʊnəvaɪrəs/ (US)| n. |冠状病毒|A type of virus that can cause diseases in humans and animals.  The name comes from the Latin word "corona" meaning crown, due to the crown-like spikes on the surface of the virus.|The novel coronavirus has spread rapidly across the globe.  新型冠状病毒已在全球迅速传播。|Synonyms: virus, germ, pathogen|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a virus itself, perhaps consider "antibody" or "cure")|Noun
