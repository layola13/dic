haloing:/ˈheɪloʊɪŋ/| n.|晕轮效应；光晕效应|A cognitive bias in which an observer's overall impression of a person, company, brand, or product influences the observer's feelings and thoughts about that entity's character or properties.  It can affect the evaluation of multiple objects or entities.|Example: The haloing effect made it difficult for the manager to see the employee's flaws. 晕轮效应使得经理很难看到员工的缺点。

Example:  The company benefited from the haloing effect of its popular product.  该公司因其受欢迎的产品而受益于光晕效应。|近义词：bias, prejudice, distorted perception|反义词：objective assessment|词性:名词
