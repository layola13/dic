inquietude's:/ˌɪnˈkwaɪəˌtuːdz/| n. |不安，焦虑，担忧|The possessive form of "inquietude," referring to the anxiety or restlessness belonging to someone or something.|Her inquietude's source was the upcoming exam. 她的焦虑来源于即将到来的考试。|Near synonyms: anxiety's, uneasiness's, worry's, apprehension's, solicitude's|Antonyms: calmness's, tranquility's, serenity's, peacefulness's|Noun (possessive form)

Etymology:
From French *inquiétude*, from Late Latin *inquietudo*, from Latin *inquietus* (“uneasy, restless”), from *in-* (“not”) + *quietus* (“quiet, at rest”).
