contemplate:/ˈkɒntəmpleɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːntəmpleɪt/ (US)| v. |沉思；仔细考虑；思忖；凝视；注视|To think profoundly and at length; to look at or view with continued attention; to consider thoroughly; to intend or have in view|She contemplated the painting for a long time, lost in its beauty. 她长时间地凝视着这幅画，沉浸在它的美丽之中。|He contemplated the consequences of his actions. 他仔细考虑了他行为的后果。|近义词：consider, ponder, meditate, reflect, muse, study, gaze|反义词：ignore, disregard, neglect|词性:动词

contemplated:/ˈkɒntəmpleɪtɪd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːntəmpleɪtɪd/ (US)| v. |沉思的；仔细考虑过的；思忖过的|Past tense and past participle of "contemplate"|The contemplated merger was called off at the last minute.  计划中的合并被最后一刻取消了。|The architect contemplated the design for weeks before finalizing it. 建筑师在最终确定设计之前，对设计进行了数周的思考。|近义词：considered, pondered, meditated, reflected|反义词：ignored, disregarded, neglected|词性:动词(过去式/过去分词)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin contemplatus, past participle of contemplari "to gaze attentively, observe, contemplate," from con- "together" + templum "area for observation, temple." Originally referred to the practice of augurs observing the heavens from a consecrated space (templum).
