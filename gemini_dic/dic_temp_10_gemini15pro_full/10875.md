arachnidans: /əˈræknɪdənz/ | n. | 蜘蛛纲动物 |  A class (Arachnida) of chelicerate arthropods that includes spiders, scorpions, mites, and ticks. They have four pairs of legs, no antennae, and a body divided into two parts. | Example 1: Arachnidans, such as spiders and scorpions, are often feared.  蜘蛛纲动物，例如蜘蛛和蝎子，经常让人害怕。

Example 2: Many arachnidans are venomous. 许多蜘蛛纲动物是有毒的。 | Synonym: arachnids | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a biological classification) | noun
