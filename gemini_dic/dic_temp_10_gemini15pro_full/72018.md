ETYMOLOGY->
"Flip-flop" originates from the sound the sandals make when walking. The use of "flip-flop" to describe a change in position or opinion is more recent, likely drawing on the image of the sandals quickly changing direction.  "Flip-flapping" is the present participle form of "flip-flop" when used as a verb, or a noun derived from this verb form.
