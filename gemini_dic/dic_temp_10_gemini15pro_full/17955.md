beflatter: /bɪˈflætər/ | v. | 奉承；谄媚；使显得更漂亮 | To praise someone excessively or insincerely, often to gain favor; to make someone appear more attractive than they actually are. |  He beflattered her with compliments, hoping to get a promotion. 他奉承她，希望得到晋升。 |  The soft lighting beflattered her complexion. 柔和的灯光使她的肤色显得更漂亮。 | synonyms: compliment, flatter, adulate, sweet-talk | antonyms: criticize, disparage, belittle | verb

Etymology:  "be-" (prefix meaning "thoroughly" or "completely") + "flatter" (from Old French "flater," from Latin "flatus," meaning "a blowing, puff of wind, breath"). The original sense was to blow gently on something, later evolving to the figurative sense of inflating someone's ego with praise.
