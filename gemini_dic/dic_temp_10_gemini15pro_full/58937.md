dusky:/ˈdʌski/ (US), /ˈdʌski/ (UK)| adj.|昏暗的，朦胧的；(肤色)黝黑的|Somewhat dark; dim; shadowy. Also, having dark skin.|例句：The room was dusky, lit only by a single candle. 房间里很昏暗，只有一支蜡烛照明。|例句：He had a dusky complexion. 他肤色黝黑。|近义词：dim, shadowy, dark, swarthy|反义词：bright, light, fair|词性:形容词

dusky:/ˈdʌski/ (US), /ˈdʌski/ (UK)| n.|黄昏，暮色|The time of day immediately following sunset.|例句：The birds returned to their nests at dusky. 鸟儿在黄昏时分回到了它们的巢穴。|例句：The dusky sky hinted at the approaching night. 昏暗的天空预示着夜幕降临。|近义词：twilight, dusk, evening|反义词：dawn, daybreak|词性:名词 (rare)
