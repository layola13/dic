freewheeling:/ˌfriːˈwiːlɪŋ/ | adj. | 自由自在的，无拘无束的 |Characterized by a carefree lack of restraint or convention. |He has a freewheeling style of management. 他有一种自由放任的管理风格。|近义词: carefree, uninhibited, unconventional, spontaneous | 反义词: restrained, inhibited, conventional, cautious | adjective

词源:

From freewheel + -ing + -ness.  Freewheel itself comes from free + wheel.  The original sense related to a bicycle mechanism that allows the pedals to remain stationary while the bicycle is in motion.  This mechanical sense was then extended metaphorically to describe a carefree, unrestrained manner.

用法说明:

Freewheelingness is a noun that describes the state of being freewheeling. It often implies a lack of concern for rules or conventions, and a willingness to improvise or take risks.  It can be seen as both a positive and negative trait, depending on the context.
