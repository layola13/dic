off-the-grid:/ˌɔːf ðə ˈɡrɪd/ | adj. | 离网的；自给自足的；不依赖公共设施的 | Not connected to public utilities such as electricity, water, or sewage systems.  Living independently and self-sufficiently. |  They live an off-the-grid lifestyle in a remote cabin. 他们在偏远的小屋里过着离网生活。|  independent, self-sufficient, autonomous | connected, dependent, mainstream | adjective

Etymology:
"grid" originally referred to a network of electrical power lines.  "Off-the-grid" extended metaphorically to encompass other utilities and eventually a general sense of disconnection from mainstream systems and infrastructure.  The term gained popularity with the rise of environmentalism and self-sufficiency movements.
