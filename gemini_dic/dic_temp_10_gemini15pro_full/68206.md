fair-trading:/ˌfer ˈtreɪdɪŋ/ | adj. n.|公平交易，公平贸易的|Trade in which fair prices are paid to producers in developing countries.|例句：Fair-trading practices ensure that farmers receive a decent wage. 公平交易行为确保农民获得体面的工资。|例句：She is a strong advocate for fair-trading organizations. 她是一位公平贸易组织的坚定拥护者。|近义词：ethical trading, responsible trade|反义词：exploitative trade, unfair trade|词性:形容词,名词

fair:/fer/ | adj. |公平的，美丽的，晴朗的|Pleasing in appearance; attractive; free from bias, dishonesty, or injustice; light in color; ample.|例句：The judge made a fair decision. 法官做出了公正的决定。|例句：She had long fair hair. 她有一头漂亮的金发。|近义词：just, equitable, beautiful|反义词：unfair, unjust, biased|词性:形容词

trading:/ˈtreɪdɪŋ/ | n. |贸易，交易|The activity or process of buying and selling goods or services.|例句：The company is involved in international trading. 这家公司从事国际贸易。|例句：Online trading has become increasingly popular. 在线交易变得越来越流行。|近义词：commerce, exchange, dealing|反义词：N/A|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
"fair" comes from Old English "fæger" meaning "beautiful, pleasing, fitting, proper."
"trading" comes from the verb "trade," which originates from Middle Low German "trade" meaning "track, course."
