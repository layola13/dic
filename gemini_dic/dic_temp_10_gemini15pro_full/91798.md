hyperfederalist:/ˌhaɪpərˈfɛdərəlɪst/ | n. |极端联邦主义者；超级联邦主义者|A person who advocates for a strong central government and limited state power, often to an extreme degree. |例句：The hyperfederalists believed that a powerful national government was essential for the stability of the new nation. 极端联邦主义者认为，强大的中央政府对于新国家的稳定至关重要。

例句：He was accused of being a hyperfederalist for his staunch support of national policies over state regulations.  他因坚决支持国家政策而非州法规而被指责为极端联邦主义者。|近义词：centralist, nationalist|反义词：states' rights advocate, antifederalist|词性: noun
