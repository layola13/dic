deigns:/deɪnz/
verb
屈尊，俯就
to do something that one considers to be beneath one's dignity
The queen deigned to speak to the commoner. 女王屈尊与平民说话。
近义词: condescends, stoops, consents
反义词: refuses, declines, disdains
词性: verb

deigns:/deɪnz/
词源: From Old French deigner, from Latin dignari "to deem worthy," from dignus "worthy."
用法说明:  "Deign" is a transitive verb, meaning it takes a direct object. It often implies a sense of superiority and condescension on the part of the subject. It is typically used in formal or literary contexts.
