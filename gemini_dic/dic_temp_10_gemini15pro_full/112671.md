lowery:/ˈlaʊəri/ (US), /ˈlaʊəri/ (UK)| n. |阴暗处；隐蔽处；树丛；藏身处|A place of concealment or shelter, especially among trees or bushes.  A dark or shady place.|The fox disappeared into the lowery.|近义词：thicket, undergrowth, covert, copse|反义词: open ground, clearing|noun

Etymology->
From Middle English *lower, from Old French *loviere "place frequented by wolves," from loup "wolf" (from Latin lupus).  The sense shifted from "wolf's den" to a general sense of "hidden or secluded place".
