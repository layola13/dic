blabbering:/ˈblæbərɪŋ/ | n. | 喋喋不休，胡言乱语 | The act of talking foolishly or excessively. |  He was punished for his constant blabbering in class. 他因为在课堂上不断胡言乱语而受到惩罚。| synonyms: chattering, prattling, babbling | antonyms: silence, reticence | noun
blabbering:/ˈblæbərɪŋ/ | adj. | 喋喋不休的，胡言乱语的 | Talking foolishly or excessively. |  The blabbering child wouldn't stop talking about his new toy.  这个喋喋不休的孩子不停地谈论着他的新玩具。 | synonyms: talkative, garrulous, loquacious | antonyms: quiet, reserved | adjective

Etymology: From the verb "blabber," which is of imitative origin, suggesting the sound of rapid, indistinct speech.  Likely related to Middle Dutch  "blabberen" and German "blabbern."
