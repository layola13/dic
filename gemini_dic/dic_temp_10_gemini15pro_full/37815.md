compensating:/ˈkɒmpənseɪtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmpənseɪtɪŋ/ (US)| adj.|补偿的，赔偿的，补足的|reducing or offsetting the unwelcome effects of something|例句：He received a compensating payment for the damage to his car. 他因车辆损坏获得了一笔赔偿金。

例句：She developed a compensating mechanism for her shyness by becoming very outgoing online. 她通过在网上变得非常外向来弥补她的害羞。|近义词：offsetting, counterbalancing, balancing,  remedial, corrective|反义词：exacerbating, aggravating, worsening|词性: adjective

compensate:/ˈkɒmpənseɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmpənseɪt/ (US)| v.|补偿，赔偿，弥补|give (someone) something, typically money, in recognition of loss, suffering, or injury; reduce or counteract (something unwelcome or unpleasant) by exerting an opposite force or effect|例句：The company compensated her for the injury. 公司赔偿了她受伤的损失。

例句：Nothing can compensate for the loss of a child.  没有什么能弥补失去孩子的痛苦。|近义词：recompense, repay, reimburse, redress, make amends for|反义词：fail to reward, penalize|词性: verb

compensation:/ˌkɒmpənˈseɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌkɑːmpənˈseɪʃn/ (US)| n.|补偿，赔偿金，弥补|something, typically money, awarded to someone as a recompense for loss, injury, or suffering.|例句：She received compensation for her injuries. 她因受伤获得了赔偿。

例句：He demanded compensation for the damage to his property. 他要求赔偿他的财产损失。|近义词：recompense, payment, remuneration, restitution, indemnity|反义词：penalty, forfeiture|词性: noun
