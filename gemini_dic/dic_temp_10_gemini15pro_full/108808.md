leatherneck: /ˈleðərˌnek/ | n. | 皮革脖子；美国海军陆战队士兵 | A member of the U.S. Marine Corps. This nickname comes from the high leather collars that were part of the Marine uniform in the late 18th and early 19th centuries. These collars were intended to protect the neck from sword cuts. |  He's a proud leatherneck who served in Afghanistan. 他是一名在阿富汗服役的自豪的海军陆战队员。 | synonym: Marine, soldier | antonym: civilian | noun
