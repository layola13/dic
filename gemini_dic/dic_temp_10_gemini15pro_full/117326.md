meddlesomely:/ˈmɛdəlsəmli/| adv.|爱管闲事地，好干预地|In a way that is too eager to become involved in other people's affairs. |She meddlesomely rearranged the furniture in her daughter's apartment. 她爱管闲事地重新布置了她女儿公寓里的家具。|He meddlesomely offered advice on a subject he knew nothing about. 他对一个他一无所知的话题爱管闲事地提出建议。|近义词: interfering, intrusive, officious|反义词: respectful, discreet, unobtrusive|词性:副词

Etymology:
From Middle English *medelsum, from Old English medel + -sum.  Medel means "middle, intervening," related to German mittel. The suffix -sum forms adjectives meaning “characterized by” or “inclined to.”  The adverbial suffix -ly was added later.
