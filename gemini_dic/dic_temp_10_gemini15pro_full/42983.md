covetise: /ˈkʌvɪtɪs/ | n. | 贪婪，觊觎 | An excessive or inordinate desire to possess something, typically something belonging to someone else. |  He looked at his neighbour's new car with undisguised covetise. 他毫不掩饰地觊觎着邻居的新车。 |  The king's covetise for power led him to betray his allies. 国王对权力的贪婪导致他背叛了他的盟友。 | 近义词: greed, avarice, cupidity | 反义词: generosity, altruism | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *coveitise*, from *coveiter* "to covet," from Latin *cupiditas* "desire, longing, passion," from *cupidus* "eager, passionate," from *cupere* "to desire."
