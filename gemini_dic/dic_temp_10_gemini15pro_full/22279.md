boneshaker: /ˈboʊnˌʃeɪkər/ | n. | 骨头震动器，老式自行车 | An early type of bicycle with a large front wheel and a small rear wheel, known for its rough ride.  |  例句：Riding a boneshaker over cobblestone streets was quite an adventure.  骑着骨头震动器穿过鹅卵石街道真是一次冒险。

例句：Boneshakers were popular in the late 19th century.  19世纪晚期，骨头震动器很流行。 | 近义词：bicycle, velocipede, penny-farthing | 反义词: modern bicycle, car | 词性: 名词

boneshaker: /ˈboʊnˌʃeɪkər/ | adj. |  颠簸的，震动剧烈的 |  Describing something that causes a lot of shaking or vibration, typically a vehicle. | 例句：The old truck was a real boneshaker, rattling my teeth with every bump. 这辆旧卡车真是个骨头震动器，每颠簸一下都震得我牙齿咯咯响。

例句：The ride in the horse-drawn carriage was boneshaker, especially on the uneven road.  乘坐马车颠簸得很厉害，尤其是在不平坦的道路上。| 近义词：bumpy, jarring, rough | 反义词: smooth, comfortable | 词性: 形容词
