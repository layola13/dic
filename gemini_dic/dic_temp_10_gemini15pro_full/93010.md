idiosyncratic:/ˌɪdiəsɪŋˈkrætɪk/ | adj. | 特有的，独特的，古怪的 |  Relating to a characteristic or quality that is peculiar to a person or thing;  quirky or eccentric. | He has an idiosyncratic way of tying his shoelaces. 他系鞋带的方式很独特。 |  peculiar, distinctive, eccentric, quirky, unusual, individual | conventional, ordinary, typical, normal | adjective

idiosyncrasy:/ˌɪdiəˈsɪŋkrəsi/ | n. | 特质，癖好，古怪 | A mode of behavior or way of thought peculiar to an individual. | One of her idiosyncrasies is collecting vintage hats.  她其中一个癖好是收集古董帽子。 | peculiarity, eccentricity, quirk, mannerism, trait | normality, conventionality | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *idiosyncrasia, from Greek idiosynkrasia, from idios ("one's own, private, peculiar") + syn ("together, with") + krasis ("mixture, temperament").
