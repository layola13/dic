engarrison:/ɪnˈɡærɪsən/ | v. |驻防，守卫，安置驻军| To provide (a place) with a body of troops for defense; to station troops in. | The commander decided to engarrison the fort with 500 soldiers.  司令决定在堡垒中驻扎500名士兵。|近义词：fortify, garrison, defend, protect, secure |反义词:demilitarize, abandon |词性:动词

engarrison:/ɪnˈɡærɪsən/ | n. |驻军，守备部队| A body of troops stationed in a fortified place. | The engarrison held the fort against the enemy attack.  驻军守住了堡垒，抵御了敌人的进攻。|近义词：garrison, troops, soldiers |反义词:civilians |词性:名词

词源：
From en- + garrison.  来自前缀 en- (使…进入某种状态) + garrison (驻军).  Garrison 本身来自古法语 garison,  最终源自法兰克语 *warjan (保卫).
