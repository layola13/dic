lousewort: /ˈlaʊswɜːrt/ (US), /ˈlaʊswɜːt/ (UK)
n.
虱草；山萝蔔
A flowering plant of the genus *Pedicularis*, typically having toothed leaves and brightly coloured flowers.  These plants are often partially parasitic on the roots of other plants.
Lousewort is a common sight in alpine meadows. 虱草在高山草甸很常见。
Synonyms: wood betony, pedicularis
Antonyms: N/A (no direct antonym for a plant name)
Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *louswort*, from Old English *lūs* (“louse”) + *wyrt* (“plant, herb”).  The name comes from the belief that these plants caused lice in livestock that grazed on them.
