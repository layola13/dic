agape:/əˈɡɑːpeɪ/ | adj. | 张着嘴的，目瞪口呆的；(基督教)博爱的，无私的 |  Wide open, especially with surprise or wonder; (in Christian theology) denoting love that is spiritual and selfless, as distinct from erotic love or emotional affection. | He stood agape, staring at the spectacle. 他目瞪口呆地站着，注视着眼前的景象。 |  She felt an agape love for all humanity. 她对全人类都充满着博爱。 | synonyms: openmouthed, astonished, amazed, awestruck; altruistic, charitable, benevolent, philanthropic | antonyms: closed, shut, unmoved, indifferent; selfish, egotistical, self-centered | adjective

agape:/ˈæɡəpiː/ | n. | (基督教)博爱，无私的爱 | (in Christian theology) the highest form of love, charity; the love of God for man and of man for God. |  Agape is considered the purest form of love. 博爱被认为是最纯洁的爱。 | The pastor spoke of the importance of agape in the Christian faith. 牧师谈到了博爱在基督教信仰中的重要性。 | synonyms: charity, love, benevolence, compassion | antonyms: hatred, malice, selfishness, indifference | noun
