fined:/faɪnd/ | v. | 罚款 | to punish someone by making them pay a sum of money, especially for breaking a law or rule | The company was fined $1 million for polluting the river.  这家公司因污染河流被罚款100万美元。| synonyms: penalized, charged | antonyms: rewarded, compensated | verb

fined:/faɪnd/ | adj. | 精制的；精美的 | made pure or free from impurities |  She wore a dress of finely woven silk. 她穿着一件精致的丝绸连衣裙。 | synonyms: refined, purified, exquisite | antonyms: coarse, crude, unrefined | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *finen, from Old French finer (“to end, finish, conclude, pay, settle a debt”), from Latin fīnīre, present active infinitive of fīniō (“I limit, bound, set bounds to; end, finish, terminate; complete, perfect; determine, define, fix; pay, discharge, settle; expiate, atone for”), from fīnis (“end, limit, boundary; highest point, peak; goal, purpose; death”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰeyh₂- (“to bend, curve”).
