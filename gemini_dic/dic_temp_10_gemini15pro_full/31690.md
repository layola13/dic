chausses:/ˈʃɔːsəz/| n.|紧身裤，长袜，软甲|Close-fitting breeches or hose covering the legs and reaching to the waist, worn in the 14th and 15th centuries.  Also, armor for the legs.|The knight donned his chausses before mounting his horse. 骑士在上马前穿上了他的软甲护腿。|Near synonyms: hose, breeches, leggings|Antonym: None (archaic term, no direct opposite)|Noun

Etymology->
From Old French "chausses", plural of "chausse" meaning "hose, stocking," from Vulgar Latin *calcia, from Latin "calceus" meaning "shoe, sock."
