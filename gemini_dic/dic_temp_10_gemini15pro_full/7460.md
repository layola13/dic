anisospore:/ˌænɪˈsɒspɔːr/ |n.|异形孢子|A spore that is noticeably different in size or shape from other spores of the same species, especially in ferns that produce both megaspores and microspores.|The fern produces anisospores, leading to the development of separate male and female gametophytes.|Synonym: heterospore|Antonym: isospore|noun
