hypocaust: /ˈhaɪpəˌkɔːst/ | n. | 地暖系统，火炕 | An ancient Roman system of central heating, in which hot air and smoke from a furnace circulated through empty spaces under the floors and sometimes in the walls. | The Roman villa featured a sophisticated hypocaust system that kept the rooms warm even in winter. 罗马别墅拥有先进的地暖系统，即使在冬天也能保持房间温暖。

The hypocaust was a significant advancement in Roman engineering.  地暖系统是罗马工程学的一项重大进步。 | Synonyms: underfloor heating, central heating | Antonyms: None | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *hypocaustum*, from Greek *hypokaúston* (ὑπόκαυστον), neuter of *hypokaústos* (ὑπόκαυστος) "heated from below," from *hypo-* (ὑπό) "under" + *kaiein* (καίειν) "to burn."
