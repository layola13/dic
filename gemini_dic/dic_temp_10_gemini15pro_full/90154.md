hotfoot:/ˈhɑːtfʊt/ (US) /ˈhɒtfʊt/ (UK)| adv. n. v.|匆忙地，迅速地；热脚；匆忙赶往|To go somewhere quickly, often because of something urgent or exciting.|He hotfooted it back to the office when he heard the news. 他听到消息后急忙赶回办公室。|近义词：hurry, rush, hasten, dash|反义词：dawdle, linger|词性: adverb, noun, verb

hotfoot:/ˈhɑːtfʊt/ (US) /ˈhɒtfʊt/ (UK)| n.|匆忙，急忙|A hurried trip or journey.|He made a hotfoot to the store for more snacks. 他急忙赶到商店去买更多零食。|近义词：rush, dash, hurry|反义词：leisurely walk, slow journey|词性: noun

hotfoot:/ˈhɑːtfʊt/ (US) /ˈhɒtfʊt/ (UK)| v.|匆忙赶往|To go somewhere quickly and hurriedly.|He hotfooted it out the door when the fire alarm went off. 火警响起时，他冲出门外。|近义词：hurry, rush, hasten|反义词：dawdle, linger|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the earlier phrase "hot foot it," meaning to run or dance quickly as if one's feet were being burned.  The "hot" refers to the sensation of burning, and "foot" refers to the act of running.
