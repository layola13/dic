nonprosperously: /ˌnɑːnˈprɑːspərəsli/ | adv. | 不兴旺地，不顺利地 | Not prosperously; in a way that lacks success or economic well-being. |  The business venture ended nonprosperously, leaving them in debt.  这家企业最终以失败告终，让他们负债累累。| Synonym: unsuccessfully, unfortunately, unluckily | Antonym: prosperously, successfully, fortunately | Part of Speech: Adverb

词源:

non- (prefix):  Latin origin, meaning "not."
prosperous (adjective): From Latin *prospĕrus "according to one's hopes, favorable, fortunate," from pro- "forward" + spes "hope."
-ly (suffix): Forms adverbs from adjectives.
