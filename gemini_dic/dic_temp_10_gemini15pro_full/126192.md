muzzlers: /ˈmʌzlərz/ | n. |  口罩；枪口套；止吠器 | A device, typically made of straps or wire, fastened over the snout of an animal, typically a dog, to prevent it from biting or barking. Also, figuratively, anything that suppresses speech or expression. | The dogs were fitted with muzzlers before entering the crowded park. 公园里人很多，所以在进去之前给狗戴上了口罩。| Synonyms: gag, silencer, suppressor | Antonyms: amplifier, megaphone | Noun
