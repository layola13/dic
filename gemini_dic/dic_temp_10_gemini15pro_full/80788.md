gotcha:/ˈɡɒtʃə/ (US) /ˈɡɒtʃə/ (UK)| interj. n.| 逮到你了！；我明白了；知道了|A colloquial expression that combines "got you." It can be used to express triumph at having caught someone doing something wrong, understanding something, or as a playful acknowledgment of being caught or outsmarted.|Gotcha! I knew you were hiding the cookies! 逮到你了！我就知道你把饼干藏起来了！|Synonyms: I understand, I see, I get it, aha, caught you|Antonyms: I don't understand|interjection


gotcha:/ˈɡɒtʃə/ (US) /ˈɡɒtʃə/ (UK)| n.|圈套；陷阱|An unexpected problem or tricky situation, often deliberately created to catch someone out.|The quiz had a few gotchas that tripped up even the best students. 测验中有一些陷阱，即使是最好的学生也被难住了。|Synonyms: trap, pitfall, snare, catch|Antonyms: advantage, benefit|noun
