cuttlefish: /ˈkʌtəlˌfɪʃ/ | n. | 墨鱼；乌贼 | A marine mollusk of the order Sepiida, characterized by its internal shell (cuttlebone), eight arms, two tentacles, and the ability to change color and squirt ink. | The cuttlefish quickly changed color to blend in with the coral.  墨鱼迅速变色以融入珊瑚中。| synonyms: cuttle, sepia | antonyms: None | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cutelfissh*, from Old English *cudele* (“cuttlefish”) (akin to Old Norse *koddi* (“cushion, pillow”)), + *fish*.  The first element is ultimately from Latin *cuticula* (“little skin”).
