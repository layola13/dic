emulsify:/ɪˈmʌlsɪfaɪ/ | v. | 乳化 | To combine two liquids that don't usually mix, like oil and water, into a stable mixture. |  The egg yolks emulsify the oil and vinegar in the mayonnaise. 蛋黄使蛋黄酱中的油和醋乳化。 | synonyms: blend, mix, homogenize, combine | antonyms: separate, divide | verb

emulsification:/ɪˌmʌlsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 乳化（作用）| The process of combining two immiscible liquids into a stable mixture. | The emulsification of the fats is essential for proper digestion. 脂肪的乳化对于正常的消化至关重要。| synonyms: blending, mixing, homogenization, combination | antonyms: separation, division | noun

emulsion:/ɪˈmʌlʃən/ | n. | 乳状液 | A mixture of two immiscible liquids, one dispersed as droplets in the other. | Milk is an emulsion of fat in water. 牛奶是脂肪在水中的乳状液。| synonyms: suspension, colloid, mixture | antonyms: solution, separation | noun

emulge:/ɪˈmʌldʒ/ | v. | 乳化 | To make an emulsion; to combine two immiscible liquids into a stable mixture. |  Lecithin helps to emulge the ingredients in the cake batter. 卵磷脂有助于乳化蛋糕糊中的成分。 | synonyms: emulsify, blend, mix, homogenize, combine | antonyms: separate, divide | verb
