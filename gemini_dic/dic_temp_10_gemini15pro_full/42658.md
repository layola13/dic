counterstep:/ˈkaʊntərˌstɛp/| n. v.|反击，应对措施；跳反步舞|An action taken to oppose or counteract another action; a step in a dance, especially a waltz, taken in the opposite direction to the natural one.|例句：The government took countersteps to prevent the spread of the virus. 政府采取应对措施防止病毒传播。|例句：He executed a perfect counterstep in the waltz. 他在华尔兹舞中完美地跳了一个反步。|近义词：countermeasure, countermove, reaction, response|反义词:inaction, passivity|词性:名词，动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + step.  "Counter-"  is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposite," originating from the Old French "contre-" and ultimately from the Latin "contra."  "Step" comes from Old English "stæpe," meaning "a pace, a stride."
