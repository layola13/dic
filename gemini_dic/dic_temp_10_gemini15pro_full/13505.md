attacks:/əˈtæks/ (US), /əˈtæks/ (UK)| n. v.|攻击，袭击；抨击；疾病发作|An act of attacking someone or something physically or verbally; a sudden onset of illness or other unpleasant experience.|The enemy launched several attacks on the city. 敌人对这座城市发动了几次攻击。|近义词：assaults, onslaughts, criticisms, seizures|反义词：defenses, protections, praises|词性:名词复数; 动词第三人称单数形式

attacks:/əˈtæks/ (US), /əˈtæks/ (UK)| v.|攻击，袭击；抨击；疾病发作|To begin fighting or hurting someone or something; to criticize someone or something strongly; (of a disease or medical condition) to suddenly begin to affect someone.|The dog attacked the mailman. 狗袭击了邮递员。|近义词：assaults, onslaughts, criticizes, seizes|反义词：defends, protects, praises|词性:动词第三人称单数形式

Etymology: From Old French *atachier "to attach, fasten; to attack," from *estachier "to stake, fix," from a- "to" + estache "stake," from Frankish *stakka "stake," from Proto-Germanic *stakon "stake."
