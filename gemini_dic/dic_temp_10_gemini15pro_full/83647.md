haemophiliac: /ˌhiːməˈfɪliæk/ (UK), /ˌhiməˈfɪliæk/ (US) | n. | 血友病患者 | A person with haemophilia, a medical condition in which the ability of the blood to clot is severely reduced, causing the sufferer to bleed severely from even slight injuries. |  Example: The haemophiliac had to be careful to avoid any cuts or bruises. 血友病患者必须小心避免任何割伤或擦伤。

Example:  The hospital has a special clinic for haemophiliacs. 这家医院有一个专门治疗血友病患者的诊所。 |  Synonym: bleeder | Antonym: (No direct antonym for a person, but the antonym for the condition is "normal blood clotting") | Noun

haemophilic: /ˌhiːməˈfɪlɪk/ (UK), /ˌhiməˈfɪlɪk/ (US)| adj. | 血友病的 | Relating to or affected with haemophilia. | Example: Haemophilic patients require regular treatment. 血友病患者需要定期治疗。

Example: The doctor diagnosed the child with a haemophilic disorder. 医生诊断这个孩子患有血友病。 | Synonym: bleeder's | Antonym: (No direct antonym, but could be considered "non-haemophilic") | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek haîma "blood" + philia "loving," implying an affinity for bleeding due to the inability to clot.
