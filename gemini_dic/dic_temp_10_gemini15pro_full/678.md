abscesses: /ˈæbsɛsiːz/
noun
脓肿
A localized collection of pus surrounded by inflamed tissue.  A painful swollen area on or in the body, containing pus.
The doctor lanced the abscesses on his back. = 医生切开了他背上的脓肿。

The infection caused multiple abscesses to form. = 感染导致形成了多个脓肿。

近义词: boils, sores, ulcers, infections
反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider) healthy tissue, clear skin
词性: Noun (plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin abscessus, past participle of abscedere (“to go away, depart”), from ab- (“away from”) + -scendere (“to climb, ascend”). Originally referred to the process of pus "going away" or separating from the healthy tissue.
