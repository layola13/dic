llanero: /ljɑːˈnɛroʊ/ /jɑːˈnɛroʊ/
noun
委内瑞拉或哥伦比亚平原的牛仔；草原居民
A Venezuelan or Colombian plainsman, especially a cattle herder.
The llanero expertly lassoed the stray calf.  那名牛仔熟练地用套索套住了走失的小牛。
The llaneros of the Venezuelan plains are known for their horsemanship.  委内瑞拉平原的牛仔以其精湛的骑术而闻名。
synonyms: cowboy, herdsman, gaucho
antonyms: city dweller


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *llanero*, from *llano* ("plain") + *-ero* (suffix forming nouns denoting people associated with a place or activity). Ultimately from Latin *planus* ("flat, level").
