anglophobia:/ˈæŋɡləʊˌfəʊbiə/ | n. | 厌英症，恐英症 | An extreme dislike or fear of England, English people, or English culture. |  He suffered from anglophobia after a series of unpleasant encounters with English tourists. 他在与英国游客发生了一系列不愉快的遭遇后患上了恐英症。| 近义词: xenophobia (general fear of foreigners) | 反义词: anglophilia (love of England/English culture) | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Anglo- (referring to England or English) + -phobia (fear or aversion).  "Anglo-" ultimately derives from the Angles, one of the Germanic peoples who settled in Britain. "-phobia" comes from the Greek word "phobos" meaning "fear" or "panic".
