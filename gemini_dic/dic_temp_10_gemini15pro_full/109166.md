legitimating: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmeɪtɪŋ/
verb.
使合法化；赋予合法性
The process of making something legitimate.  The act of giving legal or official approval to something.
例句：The government is legitimating the use of force. 政府正在将使用武力合法化。

例句：The election results legitimated the new government's claim to power. 选举结果使新政府的权力主张合法化。
近义词：legalizing, validating, authorizing, justifying
反义词：invalidating, delegitimizing, condemning
词性：Verb (present participle)


legitimate: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət/
adjective.
合法的；正当的；合理的
Conforming to the law or to rules. Justifiable; reasonable.
例句：The government is a legitimate authority. 政府是一个合法机构。

例句：He had a legitimate reason for being late. 他迟到是有正当理由的。
近义词：lawful, legal, valid, justifiable, reasonable
反义词：illegal, unlawful, invalid, unreasonable
词性：Adjective


legitimacy: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi/
noun.
合法性；正当性；合理性
The state or quality of being legitimate. Conformity to the law or to rules.
例句：The legitimacy of the government is being questioned. 政府的合法性正受到质疑。

例句：The study raises questions about the legitimacy of using animals in research. 这项研究对在研究中使用动物的合法性提出了质疑。
近义词：lawfulness, legality, validity, justifiability
反义词：illegality, invalidity
词性：Noun


legitimately: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmətli/
adverb.
合法地；正当地；合理地
In a legitimate manner; lawfully.
例句：He was legitimately elected as president. 他是合法当选的总统。

例句：The company is legitimately operating in this country. 这家公司在这个国家合法经营。
近义词：lawfully, legally, validly, justifiably
反义词：illegally, unlawfully, invalidly
词性：Adverb


词源：From Late Latin legitimatus, past participle of legitimare “to make lawful,” from Latin legitimus “lawful,” from lex (genitive legis) “law.”
