dead-to-rights:/ˌdɛd tə ˈraɪts/| adj.|证据确凿的，无可抵赖的|Caught red-handed; having irrefutable evidence of guilt.  Having no chance of escape or justification.|The police caught the burglar dead-to-rights with the stolen jewels in his bag. 警察在窃贼的包里发现了被盗的珠宝，人赃并获。|Synonyms: bang to rights, red-handed, caught in the act, guilty as sin|Antonyms: innocent, blameless|adjective


dead-to-rights:/ˌdɛd tə ˈraɪts/| adv.|确凿无疑地|Without any doubt; definitely.  In a way that leaves no room for doubt or escape.|He knew, dead-to-rights, that she was lying. 他确凿无疑地知道她在撒谎。|Synonyms: definitely, undoubtedly, absolutely, certainly|Antonyms: possibly, maybe, perhaps|adverb
