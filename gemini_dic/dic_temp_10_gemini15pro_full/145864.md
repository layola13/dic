overderisively: /ˌəʊvərˈdɪrɪsɪvli/ | adv. | 过分嘲笑地，过分轻蔑地 |  In a way that shows excessive derision or mockery; with exaggerated scorn or contempt.  | He laughed overderisively at her suggestion, making her feel small. 他过分嘲笑她的建议，让她感到渺小。 |  He overderisively dismissed her concerns as childish whims. 他极度轻蔑地将她的担忧斥为孩子气的念头。 | 近义词: scornfully, mockingly, contemptuously | 反义词: respectfully, admiringly | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From "over" (excessively) + "derisively" (in a mocking or scornful manner). "Derisive" comes from the Late Latin "dērīsīvus," from "dērīdēre" (to mock, deride), from "de-" (down, away) + "rīdēre" (to laugh).
