aeon: /ˈiːən/ (US) /ˈiːɒn/ (UK)| n. |永世，万古，极长的时期|An aeon is an indefinitely long period of time, often used in geology or mythology to refer to an age or epoch.|The dinosaurs lived millions of aeons ago. 恐龙生活在数百万年前。

Over aeons, the river carved a deep canyon. 经过漫长的岁月，河流雕刻出一个深深的峡谷。

|近义词：eternity, age, epoch, era|反义词:instant, moment|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek αἰών (aiṓn, “lifetime, age, eternity”).

USAGE->
Indefinite Article:
An aeon ago, there was a great civilization here. =  很久以前，这里有一个伟大的文明。
It took an aeon for the computer to process the data. =  这台计算机花了很长时间才处理完数据。

Definite Article:
The aeon of dinosaurs ended with a catastrophic event. = 恐龙时代以一场灾难性事件结束。
The current aeon is the Phanerozoic. = 当前的纪元是显生宙。

Prepositional Phrases:
For aeons, the stars have shone in the night sky. =  亿万年来，星星在夜空中闪耀。
Over aeons, the continents have drifted apart. =  在漫长的岁月中，大陆漂移开来。

Plural Form:
The universe has existed for countless aeons. = 宇宙已经存在了无数个时代。
Through the aeons, life has evolved and adapted. =  历经漫长的岁月，生命不断进化和适应。


PHRASE->
eons ago = 很久以前
for eons = 长期以来
over eons = 经过漫长的岁月
