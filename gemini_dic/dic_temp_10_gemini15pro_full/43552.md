craze:/kreɪz/| n.|狂热，风尚；短暂的流行|An intense but short-lived enthusiasm for something.|There was a craze for yo-yos in the 1990s. |20世纪90年代溜溜球非常流行。|近义词：fad, mania, trend|反义词：apathy, indifference|名词

craze:/kreɪz/| vt.|使发狂，使精神错乱|To drive insane; to make someone crazy.|The pressure of the job crazed him. |工作的压力使他发疯了。|近义词： madden, derange|反义词： calm, soothe|动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *krassa "to bruise, crack," from Proto-Germanic *krat- "to scratch, scrape."  The noun sense emerged in the 16th century, initially referring to a flaw or crack, later evolving to mean a mental disorder and then a short-lived enthusiasm.
