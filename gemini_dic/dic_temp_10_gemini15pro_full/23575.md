brait:/breɪt/| adj.|明亮的，闪亮的；聪明的，伶俐的；欢快的，愉快的|Bright describes something that emits or reflects a lot of light, or someone who is intelligent and quick-witted, or something cheerful and happy.|The sun is very bright today. 今天阳光明媚。|近义词：shining, brilliant, intelligent, clever, cheerful, merry|反义词：dull, dim, stupid, gloomy|词性:形容词
词源：From Middle English *bryght, from Old English *beorht (attested as byrht, berht, beorht), from Proto-Germanic *berhtaz, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰerǵʰ- (“to shine, white, bright”). Cognate with Scots bricht (“bright”), West Frisian bjirken (“birch”), Dutch berk (“birch”), German Birke (“birch”), Swedish björk (“birch”), Icelandic birki (“birch”). The Germanic word is a derivative of the same root as *bʰerǵʰ- (“high”), whence also English burg, borough.
