calmant: /ˈkɑːmənt/ (US), /ˈkɑːlmənt/ (UK) | n. adj. |镇静剂；使平静的|A medication or other agent that has a soothing or calming effect.  Also used as an adjective to describe something that has this effect.|例句：The doctor prescribed a calmant to help her sleep. 医生给她开了镇静剂帮助她睡眠。

例句：The soft music had a calmant effect on the baby. 轻柔的音乐对婴儿有镇静作用。|近义词：sedative, tranquilizer, soothing, relaxing|反义词：stimulant, irritant, agitating|词性: noun, adjective

词源: From French calmant, present participle of calmer (“to calm”), from Late Latin calmare (“to quiet, still”), from Latin calēre (“to be warm”).
