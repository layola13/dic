overgeneralize: /ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪz/ | v. | 过度概括，以偏概全 | To draw a conclusion or make a statement about a group of people or things based on limited information or experience.  It involves applying a rule or observation to a wider range than is justified. | Children often overgeneralize grammatical rules, like saying "goed" instead of "went". 孩子们经常过度概括语法规则，比如用“goed”代替“went”。 |  He overgeneralized from his bad experience with one product and now refuses to buy anything from that brand. 他因一次不愉快的产品体验而以偏概全，现在拒绝购买该品牌的所有产品。 |  oversimplify, generalize, stereotype | specialize, particularize | verb
