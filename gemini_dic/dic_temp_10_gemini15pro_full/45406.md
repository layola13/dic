cupola:/ˈkjuːpələ/ (US) /ˈkjuːpələ/ (UK)| n.|穹顶，圆屋顶；小圆顶|A small dome, especially a rounded roof on a circular drum, crowning a roof or turret. |The cupola atop the courthouse provided a panoramic view of the city.  法院大楼顶上的穹顶可以欣赏到城市的全景。|Synonyms: dome, rotunda, turret|Antonyms: base, foundation|noun

cupola:/ˈkjuːpələ/ (US) /ˈkjuːpələ/ (UK)| n.|冲天炉|A dome-shaped furnace for melting metals. |The molten iron was poured from the cupola. 熔化的铁水从冲天炉中倒出。|Synonyms: furnace, smelter|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *cupola* "small cask, dome," diminutive of *cupa* "tub, cask," from Latin *cupa* "tub, cask, vat."  The metallurgical sense arose from the resemblance of the furnace to a dome.
