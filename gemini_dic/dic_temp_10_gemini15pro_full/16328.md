barbadoses:/bɑːrˈbeɪdoʊsiːz/| n. |巴巴多斯（旧称）|An older, now less common, plural form of Barbados.  It refers to the island nation of Barbados itself (more commonly used in the singular form "Barbados").|例句：The Barbadoses were a British colony until 1966. 巴巴多斯是英国的殖民地，直到1966年。

词源: From the Portuguese name "Os Barbados", meaning "the bearded ones," possibly referring to the fig trees with aerial roots that look like beards, or to the bearded Carib Indians who inhabited the island.
