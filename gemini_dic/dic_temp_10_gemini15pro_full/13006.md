astor:/ˈæstər/| n.|阿斯特; 海狸皮; |A person who traps or trades in furs, especially beaver pelts.  Historically, a wealthy and influential person involved in the North American fur trade. Now, can be used more generally to refer to someone who deals in valuable commodities. |John Astor was a prominent fur trader in the early 19th century.  约翰·阿斯特是19世纪早期一位著名的毛皮商人。|Synonyms: fur trader, merchant, tycoon|Antonyms: customer, buyer|noun
