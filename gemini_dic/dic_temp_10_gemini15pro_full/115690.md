marginalised:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəlaɪzd/ | adj. |边缘化的，被排斥的| Treated as insignificant or peripheral.|例句：Marginalised communities often lack access to essential services. 边缘化社区通常缺乏获得基本服务的机会。

例句：Indigenous peoples have historically been marginalised in many societies.  在许多社会中，土著人民历来被边缘化。|近义词：excluded, peripheral, disadvantaged, underprivileged, alienated, ostracized |反义词: central, included, mainstream, integrated, privileged |词性:形容词

margin:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪn/ | n. | 边缘，利润，余地| The edge or border of something. The difference between the cost of buying something and the price it is sold for.|例句：Please write your notes in the margin. 请在页边空白处写笔记。

例句：The company operates on very narrow margins. 这家公司的利润率很低。|近义词：edge, border, rim, profit, leeway |反义词: center, middle, loss |词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Latin word "marginalis," meaning "belonging to the margin."  The word "margin" itself comes from the Latin "margo," meaning "edge" or "border."
