double-crossers:/ˈdʌbəlˌkrɒs əz/ (US) /ˈdʌbəlˌkrɒs əz/ (UK)| n. |背叛者，出卖者|Someone who betrays or cheats someone who trusts them, especially by switching loyalties or breaking a promise.|He felt betrayed by his former friends, calling them a pack of double-crossers.|Synonyms: traitor, betrayer, deceiver, backstabber|Antonyms: loyalist, ally, friend|Noun

double-cross:/ˈdʌbəlˌkrɒs/ (US) /ˈdʌbəlˌkrɒs/ (UK)| v. |欺骗，背叛|To deceive or betray someone who trusts you, especially by switching loyalties or breaking a promise.|He double-crossed his partners and took all the money.|Synonyms: betray, deceive, cheat, backstab|Antonyms: support, assist, help|Verb
