choragus:/ˈkɔːrəɡəs/| n.|合唱队领队；赞助人；(古希腊)戏剧比赛的富人赞助者|A wealthy citizen in ancient Greece who financed and supervised the training and costuming of a dramatic chorus for public performances.  The choragus bore the expenses of theatrical productions and competitions.|The choragus spared no expense in costuming the chorus for the play.  这位合唱队领队不惜重金为戏剧的合唱队置办服装。|Synonyms: sponsor, backer, patron, financier|Antonyms:  None (The concept of an antonym for a historical role is difficult to define).|Noun
