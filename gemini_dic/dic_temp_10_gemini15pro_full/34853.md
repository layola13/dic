clinker:/ˈklɪŋkər/ | n. | 熔渣，熟料，砖块，（尤指劣质的）砖 |A hard mass of fused material, especially brick or fused refuse. Also, a very hard brick, especially one of inferior quality. | The clinkers from the furnace were dumped in the landfill.  熔炉里的熔渣被倾倒在垃圾填埋场。

clinker:/ˈklɪŋkər/ | n. | （尤指劣质的）砖 | A very hard brick, especially one of inferior quality. |  The builder used cheap clinkers, and the wall quickly crumbled.  建筑工人使用了廉价的劣质砖，墙很快就倒塌了。

clinker:/ˈklɪŋkər/ | n. | （因烘烤过度而变硬的）饼干，蛋糕 |A biscuit or cake that has become hard through overbaking.|  The cookies were left in the oven too long and turned into clinkers.  饼干在烤箱里放得太久了，变成了硬块。

clinker:/ˈklɪŋkər/ | n. | （马蹄铁上的）防滑钉 |A projection on a horseshoe to prevent slipping. | The horse was fitted with clinkers for better traction on the icy road. 马蹄上装了防滑钉，以便在结冰的道路上更好地牵引。

clinker:/ˈklɪŋkər/ | n. |  错误，失误 | A mistake or blunder (informal). | The quarterback threw a real clinker that cost them the game.  四分卫投出了一个真正的失误，让他们输掉了比赛。
