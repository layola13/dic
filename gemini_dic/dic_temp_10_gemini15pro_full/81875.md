grievance:/ˈɡriːvəns/| n.|委屈，不满，抱怨，申诉|a real or imagined wrong or other cause for complaint or protest; a feeling of resentment over something believed to be wrong or unfair.|例句：He has a grievance against his former employer. 他对他的前雇主心怀不满。|例句：The employees filed a grievance with the management. 员工向管理层提出了申诉。|近义词：complaint, injustice, resentment, beef, grouse|反义词：satisfaction, contentment, pleasure|词性: noun

词源: From Old French *grevance "difficulty, hardship, discomfort," noun use of present participle of grever "to burden, annoy, aggrieve," from Latin gravare "to burden," from gravis "heavy."
