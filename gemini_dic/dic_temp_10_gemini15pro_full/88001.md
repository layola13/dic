high-rolling:/ˈhaɪˌroʊlɪŋ/ | adj. | 豪赌的；挥金如土的；高风险高回报的 | Engaging in or involving large bets or extravagant spending; characterized by high stakes and potential for large gains or losses. | He lived a high-rolling lifestyle, frequenting casinos and expensive restaurants. 他过着挥金如土的生活，经常出入赌场和高档餐厅。 |  The high-rolling investors were looking for quick profits. 这些豪赌的投资者正在寻求快速获利。 | 近义词: extravagant, lavish, big-spending,  | 反义词: frugal, thrifty, economical | 词性: 形容词

词源:  "High" (meaning large or elevated) + "rolling" (referring to the rolling of dice in gambling).  The term originated in the gambling world, where "high rollers" were those who placed large bets.  Over time, the meaning expanded to encompass any kind of extravagant spending or high-stakes activity.
