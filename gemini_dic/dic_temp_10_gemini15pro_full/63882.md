epilogue:/ˈɛpɪlɒɡ/ (UK), /ˈɛpɪlɔːɡ/ (US)| n.|后记，结语；尾声|A section or speech at the end of a book or play that serves as a comment on or a conclusion to what has happened.|The epilogue explained what happened to the characters after the main story ended. 后记解释了主要故事结束后人物的命运。|Example 1: The author added an epilogue to her novel to tie up loose ends. 作者在小说中添加了后记，以整理未解决的情节。

Example 2: The play's epilogue showed the main characters twenty years later. 该剧的尾声展示了二十年后的主要人物。|synonyms: afterword, postscript, coda|antonyms: prologue, preface, introduction|noun
