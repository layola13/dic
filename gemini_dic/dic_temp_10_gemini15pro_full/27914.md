canterburys:/ˈkæntərbəriz/ | n. |坎特伯雷故事集; 坎特伯雷人|  A collection of stories written by Geoffrey Chaucer, presented as a storytelling contest by a group of pilgrims journeying to Canterbury Cathedral.  Also can refer to the pilgrims themselves.|  I'm reading The Canterbury Tales, and the Wife of Bath's prologue is hilarious. 我正在读《坎特伯雷故事集》，巴斯妇人的序言非常滑稽。| synonym: Chaucer's tales, pilgrim's stories | antonym: N/A | noun
