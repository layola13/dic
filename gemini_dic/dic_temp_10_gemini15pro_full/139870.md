oculinacea:/ˌɒkjʊlɪˈneɪsiə/ | n. | 有孔虫目 | An order of foraminifera, single-celled organisms with shells, characterized by a chambered test (shell) often arranged in a spiral. |  Foraminifera of the order Oculinacea are found in marine environments worldwide. 有孔虫目有孔虫发现于世界各地的海洋环境中。| 近义词: foraminiferan (广义的) | 反义词:  无 (由于是生物分类，没有直接的反义词) | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *oculinaceus*  (having eyes), from *oculus* (eye) + *-inaceus* (suffix forming adjectives). The name refers to the pore-like openings (apertures) in the shells of these organisms.
