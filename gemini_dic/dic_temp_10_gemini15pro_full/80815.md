goths:/ɡɒθs/ (UK) /ɡɑːθs/ (US)| n.|哥特人；哥特族|People who follow the goth subculture, characterized by dark clothing, music, and aesthetics, often associated with a fascination with death and the macabre.|例句：The goths gathered at the cemetery for a photoshoot.  哥特族聚集在墓地拍照。

例句：Many goths enjoy listening to bands like Bauhaus and The Cure.  许多哥特族喜欢听像包豪斯和治疗乐队这样的乐队。|近义词：darklings, gloomsters (informal)|反义词:preppy (preppy is a distinct subculture, not a direct opposite but represents a contrasting style)|词性:名词(plural)


goths:/ɡɒθs/ (UK) /ɡɑːθs/ (US)| adj.(informal, less common)|哥特式的；哥特风格的|Relating to the goth subculture.|例句：She had a goth look with black lipstick and ripped fishnets. 她化着哥特式的妆容，涂着黑色口红，穿着破洞渔网袜。

例句：He decorated his room in a goth style, with dark velvet curtains and skulls. 他把房间装饰成哥特风格，挂着深色天鹅绒窗帘和骷髅头。|近义词：dark, gloomy, morbid|反义词:bright, cheerful, preppy|词性:形容词(plural, informal, less common)
