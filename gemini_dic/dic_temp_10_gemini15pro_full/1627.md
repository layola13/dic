acidulent:/əˈsɪdjʊlənt/| adj.|酸的，尖刻的，刻薄的|Slightly sour or sharp in taste or manner.  Characterized by a biting or tart quality, often used figuratively to describe critical or sarcastic language.|Her acidulent wit made her a popular, if sometimes feared, guest at parties. 她的尖酸刻薄的机智使她成为聚会上受欢迎的客人，尽管有时也令人害怕。

The critic's acidulent review of the play left the actors feeling demoralized.  评论家对这部戏的刻薄评论让演员们感到 demoralized。|近义词：tart, sour, sharp, biting, acrid, caustic, mordant|反义词：sweet, bland, mild, gentle, kind|词性:形容词

USAGE->
Attributive Use (作定语):

He delivered an acidulent critique of the government's policies. = 他对政府的政策进行了尖刻的批评。
The comedian's acidulent humor delighted some audience members but offended others. =  喜剧演员尖刻的幽默让一些观众高兴，却冒犯了其他人。


Predicative Use (作表语):

Her tone was distinctly acidulent. = 她的语气明显很刻薄。
His remarks about her performance were surprisingly acidulent. = 他对她表演的评价出奇地刻薄。
