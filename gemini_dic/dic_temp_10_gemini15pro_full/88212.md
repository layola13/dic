Hindenburg: /ˈhɪndənbɜːrɡ/ | n. | 兴登堡 |  Refers to Paul von Hindenburg, a German general and president, or the Hindenburg disaster, the infamous airship explosion. | The Hindenburg disaster marked the end of the airship era. 兴登堡灾难标志着飞艇时代的终结。|  The name Hindenburg is synonymous with disaster. 兴登堡这个名字是灾难的同义词。| synonym: Zeppelin (for the airship), catastrophe (for the disaster) | antonym: success, triumph | noun


Hindenburg: /ˈhɪndənbɜːrɡ/ | adj. | 兴登堡式的 |  Used attributively to describe something reminiscent of the Hindenburg disaster, usually implying impending doom or failure. | The project had a Hindenburg-like quality to it, doomed from the start.  这个项目有一种兴登堡式的特质，从一开始就注定要失败。|  His career took a Hindenburg turn after the scandal.  丑闻曝光后，他的事业急转直下，如同兴登堡飞艇般坠落。| synonym: disastrous, catastrophic | antonym: successful, promising | adjective
