convenery: /ˈkɒnvənəri/ (British English) /ˈkɑːnvənɛri/ (American English) | n. | 修道院；女修道院 | A building or buildings occupied by a community of nuns living under religious vows. |  The young woman decided to dedicate her life to God and joined the convenery.  这位年轻女子决定献身于上帝，加入了修道院。|  The convenery gardens were renowned for their beauty and tranquility. 修道院的花园以其美丽和宁静而闻名。 | Synonyms: nunnery, abbey, cloister, monastery (for monks) | Antonyms:  secular world, outside world | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *conventrie*, from Late Latin *conventaria*, from Latin *conventus* ("assembly, meeting, company"), past participle of *convenire* ("to come together, assemble").  The word originally referred to a monastic community, and later to the buildings housing such a community.
