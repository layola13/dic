freestone:/ˈfriːˌstoʊn/| n.| 自由石, 砂岩 |A kind of stone, such as sandstone or limestone, that does not split easily along bedding planes and can therefore be easily cut and shaped in any direction.| The sculptor preferred freestone for his intricate designs because it was easier to carve.  这位雕塑家喜欢用砂岩来进行他复杂的创作，因为它更容易雕刻。|Synonyms: sandstone, limestone, ashlar|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "unworkable stone" could be considered)|noun


freestone:/ˈfriːˌstoʊn/| adj.| (of a fruit) having a stone that does not cling to the flesh; (of a peach, etc) having a stone to which the flesh does not cling|A freestone peach is much easier to eat.  离核桃子吃起来容易得多。|Synonyms: clingstone (opposite)|Antonyms: clingstone|adjective
