oenanthol:/iːˈnænθɒl/ (US) /iːˈnænθɔːl/ (UK)| n. |庚醛; 正庚醛|An aldehyde, CH3(CH2)5CHO, with a strong, disagreeable odor, used in organic synthesis and in flavors and perfumes.|Oenanthol is used in the production of certain fragrances. 庚醛用于生产某些香料。|Synonyms: heptaldehyde, n-heptaldehyde|Antonyms: None|Noun

oenanthol:/iːˈnænθɒl/ (US) /iːˈnænθɔːl/ (UK)| n. |烯醇|An enol is an alkene with a hydroxyl group attached to one of the carbon atoms composing the double bond.|While oenanthol primarily refers to the aldehyde, the term can sometimes be confused with an enol due to the similar suffix.  虽然oenanthol主要指的是醛，但由于后缀相似，该术语有时会被与烯醇混淆。|Synonyms: alkenol|Antonyms: None|Noun
