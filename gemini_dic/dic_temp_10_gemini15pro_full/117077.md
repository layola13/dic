meadowed:/ˈmedəʊd/ (UK) /ˈmedoud/ (US)| adj.|草地覆盖的，像草地的|Covered with meadows; resembling a meadow.|The valley was meadowed and lush. 山谷里长满了草地，郁郁葱葱。|The rolling hills were meadowed and dotted with sheep.  起伏的山丘上覆盖着草地，点缀着绵羊。|近义词：grassy, verdant|反义词: barren, arid|词性:形容词
Etymology: From meadow + -ed.  "Meadow" comes from Old English *mǣdwe (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *mēdwō, from Proto-Indo-European *mēdʰu-, meaning "honey, mead."  The suffix "-ed" forms adjectives from nouns, meaning "having the qualities of" or "covered with."
