disarrayment:/ˌdɪsəˈreɪmənt/ |n.|混乱，紊乱|A state of disorder or confusion. |The room was in a state of disarrayment after the party. 房间在派对后一片混乱。|The office was in complete disarrayment after the burglary. 办公室在盗窃案发生后一片狼藉。|近义词：disorder, chaos, confusion, mess|反义词：order, tidiness, organization|词性: 名词

disarray:/ˌdɪsəˈreɪ/ |n. |混乱，紊乱|A state of disorder or confusion. |The sudden change of plan threw their schedule into disarray.  计划的突然改变打乱了他们的日程安排。|After the earthquake, the city was in disarray. 地震过后，这座城市一片混乱。|近义词：disorder, chaos, confusion, mess|反义词：order, tidiness, organization|词性: 名词


词源:  From dis- + array + -ment.  Dis- 表示否定或相反，array 表示排列，-ment 是名词后缀，表示状态或结果。因此，disarrayment 的字面意思是“排列的反面”，即混乱的状态。
