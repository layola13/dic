lazarly: /ˈleɪzərli/ | adv. |  像麻风病人一样地；贫困潦倒地 | Resembling or suggestive of a leper, especially in being diseased, disfigured, or outcast; extremely poor. | He wandered the streets lazarly, begging for scraps of food. 他像麻风病人一样在街上游荡，乞讨残羹剩饭。| 近义词: leprous, poverty-stricken, destitute | 反义词: healthy, wealthy, affluent | 词性: 副词

词源: From Middle English *lazarli, from Old French lasdre, from Medieval Latin lazarus, from Late Latin Lazarus, from Greek Λάζαρος (Lazaros), from Hebrew אֶלְעָזָר (ʼElʻāzār, “God has helped”).  The word gained its association with leprosy from the parable of Lazarus the beggar in the Gospel of Luke.
