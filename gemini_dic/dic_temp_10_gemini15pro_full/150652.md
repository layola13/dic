pappiform:/ˈpæpɪfɔːrm/ | adj. | 似冠毛的，具冠毛状附属物的 | Resembling a pappus; having a pappus-like appendage.  A pappus is a modified calyx (the outer part of a flower) composed of bristles, hairs, or scales, often serving to aid in the dispersal of seeds by wind. | The pappiform seeds floated easily on the breeze. 这些似冠毛的种子在微风中轻松飘浮。| Near Synonyms: comose, plumose | Antonyms: glabrous, smooth | Word Origin: From Latin *pappus* (pappus) + *-formis* (-form). | word type: adjective
