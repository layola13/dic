entoderm:/ˈɛndəˌdɜːrm/ (US), /ˈɛntəʊˌdɜːm/ (UK)| n. |内胚层|The innermost of the three primary germ layers of an embryo, developing into the lining of the digestive tract and associated structures.|The entoderm gives rise to the lining of the gut. 内胚层会发育为肠道内壁。|Synonym:  endoderm, hypoblast|Antonym: ectoderm|noun
