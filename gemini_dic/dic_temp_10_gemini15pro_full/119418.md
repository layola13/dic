microform: /ˈmaɪkrəʊfɔːm/ (British) /ˈmaɪkroʊfɔːrm/ (American)
|n.|缩微胶片；缩微品|A photographic image, greatly reduced in size, stored on film, such as microfilm or microfiche.|Libraries often store newspapers and old documents on microform.|Synonyms: microfilm, microfiche, microimage|Antonyms: enlargement, macroform|noun
