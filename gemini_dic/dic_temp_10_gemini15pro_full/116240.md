mass-market:/ˈmæs ˌmɑːrkɪt/ /ˈmæs ˌmɑːrkət/| n.|大众市场|The market for goods that are produced in large quantities and sold at low prices to a large number of people.|例句：The company is trying to break into the mass market. 这家公司正试图打入大众市场。|近义词：consumer market, general public|反义词：niche market, luxury market|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
"mass market" emerged in the mid-20th century, combining "mass," meaning a large quantity or number (from Old French "masse," ultimately from Latin "massa"), and "market," referring to a place or system for buying and selling (from Old Norse "markaðr").  The term reflects the rise of mass production and consumption in the modern era.
