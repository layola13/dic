constitution:/ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃn/ (UK), /ˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃn/ (US)| n.| 宪法；体质；构成；建立|The fundamental principles and established precedents that constitute the legal basis of a polity, organization or other type of entity, and commonly determine how that entity is to be governed.|The United States Constitution is the supreme law of the land.|synonyms: charter, laws, framework|antonyms: lawlessness, anarchy|noun

constitute:/ˈkɒnstɪtjuːt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːnstɪtuːt/ (US)| vt.| 构成；组成；建立；任命|To be (a part) of a whole.|Twelve months constitute a year.|synonyms: compose, form, establish|antonyms: destroy, dissolve, ruin|verb
