beggars:/ˈbeɡərz/| n. |乞丐；乞讨者|People who ask for money or food because they are poor or homeless.|The beggars on the street held out their hands for spare change. 街上的乞丐伸出手来讨要零钱。|synonyms: mendicants, panhandlers, supplicants|antonyms: philanthropists, donors|noun

beggar:/ˈbeɡər/| n. |乞丐；乞讨者|A person who lives by asking for money or food.|The beggar sat on the corner, hoping for some kindness. 乞丐坐在街角，希望能得到一些好意。|synonyms: mendicant, panhandler, supplicant|antonyms: philanthropist, donor|noun

beggar:/ˈbeɡər/| vt. |使贫穷；使无法描述|To make someone poor or to exhaust the resources of something.|The war beggared the nation. 战争使这个国家变得贫穷。|synonyms: impoverish, bankrupt, exhaust|antonyms: enrich, enhance|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *beggere*, from Old North French *begar*, from uncertain origin, possibly related to *Beguine* (a member of a lay religious order).
