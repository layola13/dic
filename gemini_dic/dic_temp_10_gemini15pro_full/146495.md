overgarnishes:/ˌoʊvərˈɡɑːrnɪʃɪz/
|n.| 过度装饰; 过多的装饰物|An excessive amount of garnishes on a dish, often to the point of detracting from the main food item.|The chef overgarnishes every dish, making them look cluttered and unappetizing.  厨师在每道菜上都放了过多的装饰，使它们看起来杂乱无味。|overdecoration, excessive garnish|undergarnish|noun

Etymology:
"Over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi) meaning "above, beyond, excessively" + "garnish" (Old French garniss-, stem of garnir, from Frankish *warnjan "to provide, protect, furnish")
