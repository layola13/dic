counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/ | adj. n. |反事实的；反事实的陈述；虚拟的| relating to or expressing what has not happened or is not the case |例句：Counterfactual history is a fascinating but ultimately fruitless exercise. 反事实历史引人入胜，但最终徒劳无功。|近义词：hypothetical, conjectural, speculative, what-if |反义词：factual, real, actual |词性:形容词,名词

counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/ | n. |反事实的陈述；虚拟的| a statement that describes something that did not happen or is not the case |例句：It's interesting to consider counterfactuals, like what if the South had won the Civil War? 思考反事实的情况很有趣，例如如果南方赢得了内战会怎样？|近义词：hypothesis, conjecture, speculation |反义词：fact, reality |词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + factual.  "Counter-" signifies "against" or "opposite". "Factual" pertains to facts or reality. Thus, "counterfactual" literally means "against the facts".
