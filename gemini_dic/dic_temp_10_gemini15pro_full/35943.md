coddle:/ˈkɒdl/ (UK), /ˈkɑːdl/ (US)| v. |溺爱；娇惯；悉心照料|To treat someone with excessive care and tenderness, often to the point of spoiling them.  To cook something gently in liquid just below boiling point.|例句：She coddles her dog like a child. 她像对待孩子一样溺爱她的狗。

例句：The eggs were coddled to perfection. 鸡蛋被煮得恰到好处。|近义词：pamper, indulge, spoil, baby, mollycoddle|反义词：neglect, ignore, discipline|词性:动词

coddle:/ˈkɒdl/ (UK), /ˈkɑːdl/ (US)| n. |娇生惯养的人；被娇惯的东西|A person who is coddled or something that is treated with excessive care.|例句：He was a bit of a coddle, always complaining about the slightest discomfort.  他有点娇气，总是抱怨一点点不舒服。

例句：The delicate plant was a real coddle and needed constant attention. 这株娇嫩的植物真是个娇宝贝，需要持续的关注。|近义词：weakling, milksop|反义词：tough guy, survivor|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "caudle," meaning a warm drink, often given to invalids.  The sense of "treating tenderly" developed from the association of giving caudle to someone in need of care.  Ultimately from Latin "calidus" meaning "warm."
