formidably:/ˈfɔːrmɪdəbli/ | adv. | 令人敬畏地，可怕地，难对付地 | Inspiring fear or respect through being impressively large, powerful, intense, or capable. |  She is a formidably intelligent woman. 她是一位非常聪明的女性。 | The task ahead seemed formidably difficult.  面前的任务看起来极其艰巨。 | 近义词: impressively, powerfully, overwhelmingly, daunting | 反义词: weakly, insignificantly, easily | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *formidable*, from Old French *formidable*, from Latin *formidabilis* (“causing fear, dreadful, terrible”), from *formido* (“fear, dread, terror”).
