bunodonta:/ˌbjuːnoʊˈdɒntə/ | n. |丘齿类动物|A group of extinct mammals characterized by rounded cusps on their molar teeth, adapted for crushing rather than shearing food.  They are considered an ancestor to artiodactyls (even-toed ungulates).|The bunodonta thrived during the Eocene epoch.| 近义词:  (No direct synonyms, as it's a taxonomic classification.
