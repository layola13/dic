devastated:/ˈdevəsteɪtɪd/
adjective
极度悲伤的，感到震惊的；被摧毁的
extremely upset and shocked; destroyed
例句：She was devastated by the news of her husband's death. 她被丈夫去世的消息震惊了。

例句：The town was devastated by the earthquake. 这个城镇被地震摧毁了。

近义词：shattered, heartbroken, ruined, destroyed
反义词：elated, joyful, intact, unharmed
词性：adjective


devastating:/ˈdevəsteɪtɪŋ/
adjective
毁灭性的；令人震惊的
causing a lot of damage or destruction; extremely shocking
例句：The hurricane had a devastating effect on the coastal towns.  飓风对沿海城镇造成了毁灭性的影响。

例句：The news of the accident was devastating. 事故的消息令人震惊。

近义词：destructive, catastrophic, shocking, overwhelming
反义词：beneficial, helpful, constructive, insignificant
词性：adjective


devastate:/ˈdevəsteɪt/
verb
摧毁，毁灭；使极度悲伤
to destroy or damage something very badly; to cause someone to feel extremely upset
例句：The fire devastated the forest. 大火摧毁了森林。

例句：He was devastated by the loss of his job. 他因失去工作而极度悲伤。

近义词：destroy, ruin, wreck, shatter
反义词：build, create, construct, repair
词性：verb
