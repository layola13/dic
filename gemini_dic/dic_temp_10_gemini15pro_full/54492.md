disintricate:/dɪsˈɪntrɪkət/ |v. |解开；使脱离；理清|To free from entanglement; disentangle. |例句：The detective worked tirelessly to disintricate the complex web of lies.  侦探不知疲倦地工作，以解开复杂的谎言网络。

例句：It's difficult to disintricate fact from fiction in this case.  在这个案件中很难区分事实和虚构。|近义词：untangle, unravel, disentangle, extricate |反义词：entangle, complicate, confuse|词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- + intricate (complicated, entangled).  "Dis-" is a prefix indicating reversal or negation.  "Intricate" comes from Latin "intricatus," past participle of "intricare" (to entangle), from "in-" (in, into) + "tricae" (tricks, perplexities).
