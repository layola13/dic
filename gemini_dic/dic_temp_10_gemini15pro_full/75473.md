frustrate:/ˈfrʌstreɪt/ (US), /frʌˈstreɪt/ (UK)| vt. adj.|使沮丧，挫败；受挫的，失败的|To prevent (a plan or attempted action) from progressing, succeeding, or being fulfilled; feeling or expressing distress and annoyance resulting from an inability to change or achieve something.|例句：The rain frustrated our plans for a picnic.  下雨挫败了我们的野餐计划。|近义词：thwart, foil, hinder, disappoint, upset|反义词：aid, assist, help, encourage, satisfy|词性：动词 (vt.)

frustrate:/ˈfrʌstreɪtɪd/ (US), /frʌˈstreɪtɪd/ (UK)| adj.|沮丧的，失意的，挫败的|Feeling or expressing distress and annoyance resulting from an inability to change or achieve something.|例句：I'm feeling very frustrated with my lack of progress. 我对缺乏进展感到非常沮丧。|近义词：disappointed, discouraged, disheartened|反义词：satisfied, content, happy|词性：形容词 (adj.)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin frustratus, past participle of frustrare “to deceive, disappoint, frustrate,” from frustra “in vain, uselessly.”
