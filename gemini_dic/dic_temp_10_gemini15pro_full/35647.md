coaxation:/koʊˈækʃən/
n.
哄骗；劝诱；甜言蜜语
The act of coaxing or persuading someone to do something, often by flattery or gentle urging.
He used coaxation and bribery to get what he wanted. 他用哄骗和贿赂来得到他想要的东西。
persuasion, inducement, cajolery, wheedling
coercion, force, compulsion

Etymology:
From the verb "coax," which comes from the Middle English "cokes," meaning "simpleton, fool."  The original sense was to treat someone like a fool in order to get them to do something. Over time, the meaning shifted to persuading someone gently or by flattery.
