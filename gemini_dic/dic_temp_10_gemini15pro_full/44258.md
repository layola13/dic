croisade:/kruːˈseɪd/| n.| 十字军东征；运动；改革运动 |A medieval military expedition, one of a series made by Europeans to recover the Holy Land from the Muslims.  A vigorous concerted effort for a cause or against a perceived evil. |例句：The First Crusade began in 1096. 第一次十字军东征始于1096年。

例句：He launched a crusade against smoking. 他发起了一场反对吸烟的运动。|近义词：campaign, movement, drive|反义词：retreat, surrender|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *croisade* (marked with a cross), from *croisier* (to mark with a cross), from Latin *crux* (cross).  The original crusades were religious wars sanctioned by the Papacy, hence the association with the cross.
