hammer:/ˈhæmər/ (US) /ˈhæmə(r)/ (UK)| n. vt.|锤子；敲打|A tool with a heavy metal head mounted at right angles to a handle, used for driving in nails, breaking rocks, etc.|He used a hammer to drive the nail into the wall. 他用锤子把钉子钉进墙里。|synonyms: mallet, sledgehammer|antonyms: (No direct antonym for a tool.  Perhaps "chisel" as a contrasting tool)|noun, verb

hammer:/ˈhæmər/ (US) /ˈhæmə(r)/ (UK)| vt.|敲打；锤击|To hit or strike with a hammer or similar object.|She hammered the dent out of the car. 她把车上的凹痕敲了出来。|synonyms: pound, bash, strike|antonyms: caress, stroke|verb

PHRASE->
hammer home (a point):  反复强调（一个观点）  The speaker hammered home the importance of voting. 演讲者反复强调了投票的重要性。
hammer out (a deal/agreement):  达成（协议）  They finally hammered out a deal after hours of negotiation. 经过数小时的谈判，他们终于达成了协议。
hammer away at (something):  坚持不懈地做（某事）  He hammered away at the problem until he found a solution. 他坚持不懈地解决这个问题，直到找到解决方案。
go under the hammer: 被拍卖  The antique vase went under the hammer for $5,000.  这个古董花瓶以5000美元的价格被拍卖。
hammer and tongs:  全力以赴地; 争吵激烈地 They argued hammer and tongs for hours. 他们争吵了好几个小时。
