full-bloodednesses:/ˌfʊlˈblʌdɪdnɪsɪz/| n. |纯正血统，纯种|The state or quality of being full-blooded; pure breeding. |His claim of full-bloodednesses was disputed.  他声称的纯正血统受到了质疑。| The full-bloodednesses of the Arabian horses was evident in their speed and grace. 阿拉伯马的纯正血统在其速度和优雅中显而易见。|近义词：purity, thoroughbred|反义词: hybridity, impurity|词性:noun

Etymology:
From full-blooded + -ness.  "Full-blooded" itself comes from "full" + "blood" + "-ed." "Full" means complete or total. "Blood" refers to lineage or ancestry. "-ed" is a suffix used to form adjectives. "-ness" is a suffix used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
