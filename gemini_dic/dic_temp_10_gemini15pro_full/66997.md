expiate:/ˈekspiːeɪt/| v. |赎罪，弥补|To atone for; make amends or reparation for (guilt or wrongdoing).|He expiated his crime by serving a long prison sentence. 他服了很长时间的刑期来赎他的罪。|近义词：atone, redeem, redress, make amends for|反义词：commit, sin|词性:动词

expiation:/ˌekspiˈeɪʃn/| n. |赎罪，弥补|The act of making amends or reparation for guilt or wrongdoing; atonement.|His expiation for the crime was accepted by the victims' families.  受害者家属接受了他对罪行的赎罪。|近义词：atonement, redemption, redress|反义词：crime, sin|词性:名词

expiatory:/ˈekspiətɔːri/| adj. |赎罪的，补偿的|Serving to expiate or atone for guilt or wrongdoing.|He performed an expiatory ritual to cleanse himself of sin. 他进行了一个赎罪仪式来洗清自己的罪孽。|近义词：atonement, redemptive|反义词：sinful|词性:形容词
