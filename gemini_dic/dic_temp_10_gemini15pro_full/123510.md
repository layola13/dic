monothetically:/ˌmɒnəˈθɛtɪkli/ | adv. | 单一地，单调地 | Characterized by proceeding from a single premise or principle; considering only one aspect of a topic at a time.  Focusing on one thing at a time, often in a tedious or repetitive way. |  The textbook monothetically presented the history of the war, focusing solely on military strategy and ignoring the social and economic factors. 这本教科书单调地讲述了战争的历史，只关注军事战略而忽略了社会和经济因素。

 The lecturer monothetically droned on about the intricacies of tax law, losing the attention of most of the students. 演讲者单调地讲述着税法的复杂性，失去了大多数学生的注意力。 | 近义词: singly, individually, separately, one by one, tediously, repetitively | 反义词: comprehensively, multifacetedly, diversely | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From Greek *monos* (single, alone) + *thesis* (a placing, proposition) + *-ikos* (adjectival suffix) + *-ly* (adverbial suffix).
