nationalised:/ˈnæʃ(ə)nəlaɪzd/ /ˈnæʃnəˌlaɪzd/
adjective
国有化的
Converted to state ownership.
例句：The nationalised industries were inefficient. 国有化产业效率低下。

例句：The government nationalised the railways in 1948. 政府在1948年将铁路国有化。

近义词：state-owned, public
反义词：privatized, private
词性：adjective


nationalise:/ˈnæʃ(ə)nəlaɪz/ /ˈnæʃnəˌlaɪz/
verb
国有化
Transfer (a major branch of industry or commerce) from private to state ownership or control.
例句：The government nationalised the coal industry. 政府将煤炭工业国有化。

例句：Many industries were nationalised after the war. 战后许多产业被国有化。

近义词：state-control, socialize
反义词：privatize, denationalize
词性：verb


nationalisation:/ˌnæʃ(ə)nəlaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n/ /ˌnæʃnələˌzeɪʃn/
noun
国有化
The process of transferring a major branch of industry or commerce from private to state ownership or control.
例句：The nationalisation of the banks was a controversial move. 银行国有化是一个有争议的举动。

例句：The government's policy of nationalisation proved to be unpopular. 政府的国有化政策被证明不受欢迎。
近义词：state ownership, socialization
反义词：privatization, denationalization
词性：noun


词源：
From the verb "nationalize," derived from "national" + "-ize."  "National" comes from the Late Latin "nationalis," meaning "pertaining to birth," from Latin "natio" (birth, race, nation).  The suffix "-ize" forms verbs meaning "to make or cause to be," from Greek "-izein."
