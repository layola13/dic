biretta:/bɪˈrɛtə/| n.|四方帽，博士帽|A square cap with three or four ridges on top, worn by Roman Catholic clergy.|The cardinal donned his scarlet biretta.|Synonym:cap, headwear|Antonym:None|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *biretta*, diminutive of *berretta*, from Late Latin *birrus* (a hooded cloak). Ultimately from Gaulish.
