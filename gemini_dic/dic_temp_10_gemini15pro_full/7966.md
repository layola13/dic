antherozooid:/ˌænθərəˈzoʊɪd/| n. |游动精子|A motile male gamete (sex cell) of algae, fungi, bryophytes (mosses, liverworts, and hornworts), pteridophytes (ferns), and some gymnosperms.  It swims through water to fertilize the female gamete.|The antherozooids of the fern swim towards the archegonia.|Synonym: spermatozoid|Antonym: egg cell|Noun
