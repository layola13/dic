demure:/dɪˈmjʊr/ (US), /dɪˈmjɔːr/ (UK)| adj.|端庄的；娴静的；严肃的；假装正经的|Reserved, modest, and shy (typically used of a woman).  Suggesting a quiet, serious, and often slightly shy personality, sometimes in a way that seems deliberate.|She gave him a demure smile. 她给了他一个端庄的微笑。|近义词：modest, shy, reserved, bashful, coy|反义词：bold, immodest, forward, brash|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Old French *demourer "to linger, stay, remain," from Latin demorari "to delay, linger," from de- "down, completely" + morari "to delay."  The meaning shifted from "staying in place" to "serious" and then to "reserved and modest."
