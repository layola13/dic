accustomations: /əˌkʌstəˈmeɪʃənz/
n.
习惯，适应; 适应性改变
The process of becoming accustomed to something or the state of being accustomed.  Adaptations or adjustments made to become familiar with or comfortable in a new situation or environment.
She made several accustomations to her new life in the city. 她对在城市的新生活做了几次适应。
New students need time to make accustomations to college life. 新生需要时间来适应大学生活。

近义词: adaptations, adjustments, habituations
反义词: unaccustomedness

词性: noun (plural)

词源:  From Late Middle English *accustomacioun, from Old French acostumer (“to accustom”), from a- (“to”) + coustume (“custom, habit”).  The word "custom" itself comes from Old French costume, ultimately from Latin consuetudo ("habit, usage").
