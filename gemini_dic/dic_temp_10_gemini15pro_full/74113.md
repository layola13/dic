foul:/faʊl/
adjective
犯规的，邪恶的，污秽的，令人不快的
Morally bad; wicked; evil.  Offensive to the senses, especially through having a disgusting smell or taste or being dirty.
The foul odor of garbage filled the alley.  垃圾的恶臭弥漫着小巷。
synonyms:dirty, polluted, filthy, disgusting, sickening, nauseating, foul-smelling, stinking, fetid, putrid, rotten, rank, noxious, toxic, poisonous, mephitic, pestilential, loathsome, abhorrent, repulsive, revolting, offensive, objectionable, unpleasant, disagreeable, nasty, awful, terrible, dreadful, appalling, atrocious
antonyms:fair, clean, pleasant
词性:形容词

foul:/faʊl/
noun
犯规
An unfair or invalid stroke or piece of play, especially one involving interference with an opponent.
The referee called a foul on the player. 裁判判罚该球员犯规。
synonyms:infraction, infringement, breach, violation, transgression, offense, misdemeanor, misdeed, crime, sin, wrong, wrongdoing
antonyms:fair play
词性:名词

foul:/faʊl/
verb
犯规，弄脏
(Of a sports player) commit a foul against (an opponent). Make something dirty; pollute.
He fouled the other player by tripping him. 他绊倒了另一名球员，犯规了。
synonyms:dirty, pollute, contaminate, taint, defile, befoul, soil, sully, smear, besmirch, stain, tarnish, blacken, corrupt
antonyms:clean
词性:名词

foul:/faʊl/
adverb
犯规地，不正当地
In a foul manner.
The boxer fought foul, hitting below the belt.  拳击手犯规打架，打在腰带以下。
synonyms:unfairly, dishonestly, illegally, improperly, wrongly
antonyms:fairly
词性:副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fūl, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch vuil and German faul ‘rotten, lazy’.
