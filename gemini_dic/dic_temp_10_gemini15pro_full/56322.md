dogmatic:/dɒɡˈmætɪk/ /dɑːɡˈmætɪk/| adj.|教条的，武断的|Inclined to lay down principles as incontrovertibly true.|He has rather dogmatic views on art.|近义词:opinionated, doctrinaire, authoritarian, peremptory|反义词:open-minded, flexible, receptive|词性:形容词

dogmatic:/dɒɡˈmætɪkli/ /dɑːɡˈmætɪkli/| adv.|教条地，武断地|In a dogmatic manner.|She spoke dogmatically about the need for change.|近义词:authoritatively, peremptorily|反义词:flexibly, receptively|词性:副词


dogmatism:/ˈdɒɡmətɪzəm/ /ˈdɑːɡmətɪzəm/| n.|教条主义，武断|The tendency to lay down principles as undeniably true.|The dogmatism of the party leader alienated many voters.|近义词:doctrinairism, authoritarianism|反义词:open-mindedness, flexibility|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dogmaticus, from Ancient Greek δογματικός (dogmatikós, “of tenets”), from δόγμα (dógma, “opinion, tenet”).
