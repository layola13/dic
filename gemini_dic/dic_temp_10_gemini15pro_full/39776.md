consonance:/ˈkɒnsənəns/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsənəns/ (US)| n.|一致，和谐，和音，辅音韵|Agreement or compatibility between opinions or actions.  The repetition of consonant sounds, especially at the end of words or stressed syllables, without the similar correspondence of vowels.|The committee worked in consonance with the wishes of the majority. 委员会根据大多数人的意愿开展工作。  "The furrow followed free" uses consonance.|近义词：harmony, agreement, accord, concord, unity|反义词：dissonance, discord, disagreement, conflict|词性:名词

consonant:/ˈkɒnsənənt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsənənt/ (US)| adj.|一致的，和谐的，辅音的|Being in agreement or harmony; free from elements making for discord.  A speech sound that is not a vowel.|Her views are consonant with mine. 她的观点与我的一致。 He’s learning to pronounce consonants.|近义词：harmonious, agreeable, compatible, consistent|反义词：dissonant, discordant, incompatible, inconsistent|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French consonance, or from Latin consonantia, from consonant- ‘sounding together’, from the verb consonare, from con- ‘together’ + sonare ‘to sound’.
