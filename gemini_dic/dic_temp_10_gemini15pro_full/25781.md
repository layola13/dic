bureau:/ˈbjʊəroʊ (US) / /ˈbɪəroʊ (UK)/
|n.|局；办公室；办事处；(带抽屉的)写字台|A bureau is an office or organization that provides information on a particular subject, or a department of a government organization. It can also refer to a writing desk with drawers.|The FBI is a federal bureau. (联邦调查局是一个联邦机构。)  She kept her important papers in the bureau. (她把重要文件放在写字台里。)
|近义词:office, agency, department, desk|反义词: (No clear antonym)|词性:noun

bureau:/ˈbjʊəroʊ (US) / /ˈbɪəroʊ (UK)/
|n.|局；办公室；办事处；(带抽屉的)写字台|A specialized administrative unit; a chest of drawers for clothing, often with a mirror.|The travel bureau booked our flights. (旅行社帮我们订了机票。) The information bureau can provide you with maps of the city. (问讯处可以为你提供城市地图。)
|近义词:office, agency, department, desk|反义词: (No clear antonym)|词性:noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *burel* (coarse woolen cloth), from Old French *bure*, from Late Latin *burra* (dark brown cloth). The meaning shifted from the cloth covering a writing table to the table itself.
