hedgeborn:/ˈhedʒbɔːrn/| adj.|篱笆生的，出身卑微的|Of low birth or origin; rustic; mean.  Born or raised near hedges, implying a rural and possibly lowly upbringing.|The hedgeborn lad, though lacking formal education, possessed a keen wit and a kind heart.|Synonyms: lowborn, baseborn, humble, rustic, peasant, common|Antonyms: noble, aristocratic, highborn, privileged|Etymology:  From 'hedge' + 'born'.  'Hedge' refers to a boundary formed by shrubs or bushes, often associated with rural areas and less affluent dwellings.  'Born' indicates origin or birth.|Word type: Adjective
