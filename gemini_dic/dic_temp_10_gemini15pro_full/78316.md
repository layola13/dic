gerontocratic: /ˌdʒɛrənˈtɒkrætɪk/ | adj. | 老年政治的；由老人统治的 | Relating to or characterized by government by old people. | The country's gerontocratic leadership was resistant to change.  这个国家的老年政治领导层抵制变革。 | Example 2: Some argue that the company's board is too gerontocratic and needs younger members with fresh perspectives.  有些人认为该公司的董事会过于老龄化，需要更年轻的成员带来新的视角。 | Synonyms: geriatric, elder-ruled, patriarchal (sometimes) | Antonyms: youthful, young-led, progressive | adjective

gerontocracy: /ˌdʒɛrənˈtɒkrəsi/ | n. | 老人统治；老人政治 | A state, society, or group governed by old people. |  The gerontocracy in the village meant that the younger generation had little say in local matters. 村里的老人统治意味着年轻一代在地方事务中几乎没有发言权。 | Example 2:  Critics argue that the country is becoming a gerontocracy, with older politicians clinging to power. 批评家认为，这个国家正在变成一个老人政治国家，老政客们 clinging to power. | Synonyms: gerontocracy, elder rule | Antonyms: youth leadership | noun
