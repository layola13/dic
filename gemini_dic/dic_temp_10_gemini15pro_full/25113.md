bucephalus:/bjuːˈsɛfələs/| n. |布西发拉斯|The name of Alexander the Great's horse, renowned for its fierceness and loyalty.|Alexander tamed Bucephalus, a wild stallion no one else could ride. 亚历山大大帝驯服了布西发拉斯，一匹无人能骑的野马。| Bucephalus carried Alexander into many battles. 布西发拉斯载着亚历山大参加了许多战役。|synonyms: steed, charger, warhorse|antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek Βουκέφαλος (Bouképhalos), meaning "ox-head," possibly referring to a marking on the horse's forehead.
