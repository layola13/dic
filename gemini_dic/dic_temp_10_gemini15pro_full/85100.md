hardcoding:/ˈhɑːrdˌkoʊdɪŋ/| n.|硬编码|The practice of embedding data directly into the source code of a program or other executable object, rather than obtaining that data from external sources or generating it at runtime.|Example:  Hardcoding the server's IP address makes the application less flexible.  将服务器的IP地址硬编码会降低应用程序的灵活性。

Example:  Avoid hardcoding values that might change in the future. 避免对将来可能更改的值进行硬编码。|近义词: hardwiring|反义词: softcoding, data-driven|词性: noun
