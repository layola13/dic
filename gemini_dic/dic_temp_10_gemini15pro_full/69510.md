fell:/fɛl/
verb
砍伐，砍倒; 击倒，打倒;  (数量) 下降，减少
to cut down a tree; to knock someone down; to decrease in number or amount
The lumberjack felled the giant redwood.  伐木工人砍倒了那棵巨大的红杉树。
He felled his opponent with a single punch. 他一拳就把对手打倒了。
Sales fell sharply in the second quarter. 销售额在第二季度急剧下降。
近义词:cut down, knock down, drop, decline
反义词:rise, increase, plant
词性:v.


fell:/fɛl/
adjective
凶猛的，残酷的; 邪恶的;  可怕的
fierce, cruel, evil, terrible
A fell beast terrorized the village. 一头凶猛的野兽恐吓着村庄。
She gave him a fell look. 她恶狠狠地瞪了他一眼。
The fell disease claimed many lives. 这种可怕的疾病夺去了许多人的生命。
近义词:fierce, cruel, savage, terrible
反义词:gentle, kind, benign
词性:adj.


fell:/fɛl/
noun
(尤指丘陵地带的) 高地，荒野
(especially in hilly areas) high moorland or open mountainside
The sheep grazed on the fell. 羊群在荒野上吃草。
We hiked across the windswept fell. 我们徒步穿越了风吹日晒的高地。
近义词:moor, upland, hillside
反义词:valley, lowland
词性:n.


词源: From Old English *feallan, from Proto-Germanic *fallaną, from Proto-Indo-European *phol- (“to fall”). Cognate with Dutch vallen, German fallen, and Latin fallere (“to deceive, trip”). The adjectival and nominal senses are from Old Norse fell.
