high-sounding:/ˌhaɪˈsaʊndɪŋ/ | adj. | 冠冕堂皇的；虚夸的；华而不实的 |  Sounding impressive but having little meaning; pompous.  |  He used a lot of high-sounding language, but I didn't understand a word. 他用了很多冠冕堂皇的语言，但我一个字也听不懂。 |  He delivered a high-sounding speech about the importance of education. 他发表了一篇关于教育重要性的冠冕堂皇的演讲。 |  pompous, pretentious, grandiose, bombastic, inflated |  simple, modest, unpretentious, down-to-earth | adjective

Etymology:
From 'high' (referring to elevated or lofty style) + 'sounding' (referring to the impression created by the words).  The combination suggests language that aims to impress but lacks substance.
