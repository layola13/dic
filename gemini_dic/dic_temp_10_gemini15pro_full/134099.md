nonimmanences:/ˌnɒnɪˈmænənsɪz/
n.
非内在性，非固有性
The state or quality of not being immanent; transcendence.  A philosophical concept referring to that which is not inherent or intrinsic to a particular being or entity, but rather exists independently of it.
Philosophers debated the nonimmanences of abstract concepts like justice and truth. 哲学家们争论了像正义和真理这样的抽象概念的非内在性。
transcendence, externality
immanence, inherence
