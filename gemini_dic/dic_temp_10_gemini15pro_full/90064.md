hostaged:/ˈhɒstɪdʒd/ (UK) /ˈhɑːstɪdʒd/ (US)| adj.|被扣为人质的；被劫持的|Held as a hostage; seized or held by force.|The passengers were hostaged by the hijackers. 乘客被劫机者扣为人质。|Synonyms: captive, imprisoned, seized, abducted|Antonyms: freed, liberated, released|Adjective


hostage:/ˈhɒstɪdʒ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːstɪdʒ/ (US)| n.|人质；抵押品|A person seized or held by force, especially to compel others to fulfill demands.|The terrorists took several hostages. 恐怖分子劫持了几个人质。|Synonyms: captive, prisoner|Antonyms: captor, kidnapper|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *ostage, from Latin obsidēs (hostage, surety), from obsidēre (to besiege, blockade).
