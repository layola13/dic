Moshe:/ˈmoʊʃeɪ/ /ˈmoʊʃə/ | n. | 摩西 | A Hebrew name, meaning "drawn out (of the water)." It is traditionally believed to derive from Egyptian *msy, "child of," but the biblical story connects it to the Hebrew verb *mashah,* "to draw out." Moshe is the name of the most important prophet in Judaism, who led the Israelites out of slavery in Egypt. | Example 1: Moshe is a common name in Israel.  摩西是以色列的常见名字。
