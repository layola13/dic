bludged:/blʌdʒd/ | adj. | 疲惫不堪的，筋疲力尽的 | Exhausted and worn out, often from overwork or physical exertion. |  I felt completely bludged after working a 12-hour shift. 我在工作了12个小时的班后感到完全筋疲力尽。

| Example: After hiking for hours, we were all feeling rather bludged.  徒步旅行数小时后，我们都感到相当疲惫。
| Synonym: exhausted, drained, spent, worn out, knackered (informal), beat (informal)
| Antonym: energetic, invigorated, refreshed
| Word Type: Adjective


bludge:/blʌdʒ/ | vi. vt. n.| 逃避工作；偷懒；(澳/新西兰俚语)骗取；敲诈；|To avoid work or responsibility; to shirk. (Australian/New Zealand slang) To obtain something by deception or trickery.  A person who bludges. | He's always trying to bludge off his responsibilities. 他总是试图逃避他的责任。

| Example: He was accused of bludging money from his friends.  他被指控从朋友那里骗钱。
| Synonym: shirk, malinger, dodge, loaf (vi.); swindle, cheat, defraud (vt.); loafer, shirker (n.)
| Antonym: work hard, graft (informal)
| Word Type: Verb, Noun
