emulsioning:/ɪˈmʌlʃənaɪŋ/ /ɪˈməlʃəniŋ/
|n.|乳化|The process of creating an emulsion, where two immiscible liquids (like oil and water) are mixed so that one is dispersed as small droplets within the other.|The chef was emulsioning oil and vinegar to make a vinaigrette.|homogenizing, blending, mixing|separating|noun

emulsioning:/ɪˈmʌlʃənaɪŋ/ /ɪˈməlʃəniŋ/
|adj.|乳化的|Describes something that is in the process of being emulsified or has the properties of an emulsion.|The emulsioning mixture slowly thickened.|emulsified, blending|separated|adjective

emulsioning:/ɪˈmʌlʃənaɪŋ/ /ɪˈməlʃəniŋ/
|v.|乳化|To combine two immiscible liquids (like oil and water) into an emulsion.|She carefully emulsioned the ingredients for the mayonnaise.|homogenize, blend, mix|separate|verb
