nonformalism:/nɒnˈfɔːməlɪzəm/ | n. |非形式主义|  The rejection of formalism, especially in art, literature, or philosophy, emphasizing content, function, or social context over strict adherence to traditional rules or forms. |  "The architect's nonformalism was evident in his unconventional designs, prioritizing functionality over strict adherence to classical styles."  Nonformalism in literature often focuses on the social and political messages conveyed in a text rather than its formal structure. | Synonyms: informality, antiformalism | Antonyms: formalism | noun
