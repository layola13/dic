grandfathered:/ˈɡrændˌfɑːðərd/| adj.| 祖父级的，受旧规则保护的|Allowed to continue to operate under an old rule or law even when new rules are in effect.|例句：The company was grandfathered in and allowed to continue using the old software. 这家公司被允许继续使用旧软件。|例句：His old car was grandfathered into the new emissions regulations. 他的旧车被纳入新的排放法规的豁免范围。|近义词：exempted, protected|反义词:liable, subject|词性:形容词
词源: From the practice of "grandfather clauses" in the American South after the Civil War, which exempted certain people (primarily white voters) from literacy tests and other requirements for voting if their grandfathers had been eligible to vote. This effectively disenfranchised African Americans.
