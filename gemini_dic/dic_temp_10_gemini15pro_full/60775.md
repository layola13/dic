electrom:/ɪˈlektrɒm/ |n.| 电子 |A hypothetical or theoretical unit of negative electricity, especially one formerly regarded as a fraction of an elementary charge.  It is now understood that the elementary charge is the charge of a single electron. |The concept of the electrom was important in early atomic theory.  电子在早期原子理论中是一个重要的概念。 | synonym: electron | antonym: positron (though not a perfect antonym as it represents an antiparticle) | noun
