cravenly:/ˈkreɪvənli/| adv.|怯懦地，胆怯地，卑鄙地|In a cowardly and abject manner, characterized by a lack of courage and a willingness to submit to others out of fear or self-interest.  It implies a shameful lack of spirit and a base or ignoble motivation.|He cravenly abandoned his friends in their time of need. 他在朋友需要的时候卑鄙地抛弃了他们。|近义词：cowardly, timidly, pusillanimously, abjectly|反义词：bravely, courageously, valiantly, heroically|词性:副词

craven:/ˈkreɪvən/| adj. n.|怯懦的，胆小的；懦夫|Cowardly; lacking courage. As a noun, it refers to a cowardly person.|His craven behavior disgusted everyone. 他的懦弱行为让每个人都感到厌恶。|近义词：cowardly, timid, pusillanimous|反义词：brave, courageous, valiant|词性:形容词/名词

词源: From Middle English *cravant, from Old French cravante (“defeated, crushed”), past participle of cravanter (“to crush, defeat”), from Old North French craventer, of uncertain origin. Perhaps related to crever (“to burst”).
