pagans:/ˈpeɪɡənz/| n.|异教徒|People who do not acknowledge the God of Christianity, Judaism, or Islam; followers of a polytheistic religion or those with no religious beliefs.|Many pagans celebrate the summer solstice. 许多异教徒庆祝夏至。|The ancient Romans considered the early Christians as pagans. 古罗马人认为早期的基督徒是异教徒。|近义词：heathens, infidels|反义词：believers, Christians, Muslims, Jews|词性:名词

词源:
From Middle English *pagen*, from Old French *paien*, from Late Latin *pāgānus* "villager, rustic; civilian," from Latin *pāgus* "country district, village." The semantic shift occurred because Christianity spread first in cities, leaving rural areas still practicing earlier religions.
