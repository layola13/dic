manicheanism:/ˌmænɪˈkiːɪzəm/
|n.|摩尼教；二元论；善恶二元论|A dualistic philosophy dividing the world between good and evil principles, often with the implication that these are in constant conflict.|His worldview is marked by a stark Manicheanism, seeing everything in terms of black and white. 他的世界观以鲜明的二元论为特征，将一切都视为非黑即白。|The author's Manicheanism prevents him from acknowledging the complexities of human nature. 作者的二元论使他无法认识到人性的复杂性。|Synonyms: dualism, dichotomy|Antonyms: holism, monism|Noun

manicheanisms:/ˌmænɪˈkiːɪzəmz/
|n.|摩尼教思想；二元论观点；善恶二元论思想（manicheanism的复数形式）|Instances or examples of Manichean thinking, where situations or issues are viewed in simplistic terms of good versus evil.|The political discourse is rife with Manicheanisms, portraying every issue as a battle between righteous us and the evil them. 政治话语充斥着二元论，将每个问题都描绘成正义的我们与邪恶的他们之间的斗争。|Her Manicheanisms about health, seeing every food as either "good" or "bad," led to an unhealthy relationship with eating. 她对健康的二元论观点，将每种食物都视为“好”或“坏”，导致了她与饮食的不健康关系。|Synonyms: dualisms, dichotomies|Antonyms: complexities, nuances|Noun
