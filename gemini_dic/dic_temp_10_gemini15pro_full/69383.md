feebling:/ˈfiːblɪŋ/ | n. | 虚弱的人；幼小动物 | a person or animal that is weak, especially because they are very young or very old |  The little feebling struggled to keep up with its mother. 小家伙努力跟上它妈妈。 | 近义词: weakling, invalid | 反义词: strongman, athlete | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From feeble + -ling.  "Feeble" comes from Old French "feble," ultimately from Latin "flebilis" (lamentable, causing tears), from "flere" (to weep). The suffix "-ling" forms diminutive nouns, often with a derogatory connotation.
