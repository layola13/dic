loathers:/ˈloːðərz/| n.|憎恨者；厌恶者|People who intensely dislike something or someone are called loathers.  |The loathers of the new policy protested vehemently.  新政策的反对者强烈抗议。|Near Synonyms: haters, despisers, abominators |Antonyms: lovers, admirers, devotees|Noun


loathe:/loʊð/| v.|憎恨；厌恶|To loathe something is to feel intense dislike or disgust for it. |I absolutely loathe spiders. 我非常讨厌蜘蛛。|Near Synonyms: hate, detest, despise, abhor |Antonyms: love, adore, cherish|Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lothen*, from Old English *lāðian* (“to be hateful, to be disgusted with, to loathe”), from Proto-Germanic *laþōną* (“to invite, to summon; to be hostile”), from Proto-Indo-European *lēdʰ- (“to be exhausted, weary; to be disgusted”). Cognate with Dutch *liên* (“to suffer, endure”), German *leiden* (“to suffer”), Old Norse *leiða* (“to loathe, disgust”). More at loth.
