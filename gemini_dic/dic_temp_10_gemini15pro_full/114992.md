mandalusians:/ˌmændəˈluːʒənz/ |n.|曼达卢西亚人；安达卢西亚人| People from or associated with al-Andalus, the Muslim-ruled parts of the Iberian Peninsula (modern Spain and Portugal) between the 8th and 15th centuries. | The Mandalusians were known for their advancements in art, science, and philosophy. 曼达卢西亚人以其在艺术、科学和哲学方面的进步而闻名。| The architecture of the Alhambra is a testament to the skill of the Mandalusians. 阿尔罕布拉宫的建筑证明了曼达卢西亚人的技艺。|Synonyms: Andalusians, Moors (sometimes used but can be considered outdated or inaccurate)| Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps Visigoths, the preceding rulers of the Iberian Peninsula)| Noun
