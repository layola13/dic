claustrophobes:/ˌklɔːstrəˈfoʊbz/ | n. | 幽闭恐惧症患者 | People who suffer from claustrophobia, a fear of enclosed or narrow spaces. |  Claustrophobes often avoid elevators and crowded rooms. 幽闭恐惧症患者通常会避开电梯和拥挤的房间。|  Near synonyms: sufferers of claustrophobia, people with claustrophobia | Antonym: (No direct antonym for the person, but for the condition)  | Noun

claustrophobia:/ˌklɔːstrəˈfoʊbiə/ | n. | 幽闭恐惧症 | An anxiety disorder characterized by an extreme or irrational fear of confined places. | She suffers from claustrophobia and can't travel on the subway. 她患有幽闭恐惧症，不能乘坐地铁。| Near synonyms: fear of enclosed spaces, confinement anxiety | Antonym: (No direct antonym, possibly "agoraphobia" which is a fear of open spaces, but they are not opposites in a strict sense) | Noun

claustrophobic:/ˌklɔːstrəˈfoʊbɪk/ | adj. | 幽闭恐惧症的；引起幽闭恐惧症的 | Relating to or suffering from claustrophobia; inducing claustrophobia. | The small, windowless room felt claustrophobic.  这间没有窗户的小房间让人感到幽闭恐惧。 | Near synonyms: confining, constricting, restrictive | Antonym: open, spacious | Adjective
