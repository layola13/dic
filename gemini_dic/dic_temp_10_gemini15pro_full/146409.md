overfocussed: /ˌoʊvərˈfoʊkəst/ (US), /ˌəʊvəˈfəʊkəst/ (UK)| adj.| 过于专注的，过度集中的|  Giving excessive attention to one particular aspect or area, often to the detriment of others.  It implies an inability to shift focus or see the bigger picture.| He was so overfocussed on the details that he missed the main point. 他过于专注于细节，以至于错过了重点。|近义词：obsessed, fixated, preoccupied|反义词: distracted, inattentive|词性: adjective

Etymology:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "focussed" (past participle of "focus," from Latin "focus" meaning "hearth, fireplace"). The word reflects the idea of concentrating too intensely, like a fire burning too hot in one spot.
