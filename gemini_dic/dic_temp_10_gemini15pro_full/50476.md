depressibility:/dɪˌprɛsəˈbɪləti/
n.|易沮丧，易抑郁；可压下性|The quality of being easily depressed or dejected; the capacity of something to be pressed down.|Her depressibility made it difficult for her to cope with stress. 她的易沮丧让她难以应对压力。|近义词：dejection, despondency, melancholy|反义词：cheerfulness, buoyancy, resilience|词性:名词
词源: From Late Latin *depressabilis*, from Latin depress- (past participle stem of deprimere ‘press down’) + -ibilis (forming adjectives).
