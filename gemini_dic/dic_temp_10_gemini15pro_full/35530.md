coagulation:/koʊˌæɡjʊˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 凝固，凝结 |The process by which a liquid, especially blood, changes to a solid or semi-solid state.| Blood coagulation is essential to stop bleeding. 血液凝固对于止血至关重要。|  The coagulation of the milk is the first step in cheese making. 牛奶的凝结是制作奶酪的第一步。|synonyms: clotting, thickening, congealing | antonyms: thinning, liquefaction | noun

coagulations: /koʊˌæɡjʊˈleɪʃənz/ | n. | 凝固，凝结（复数）| Multiple instances of the process by which a liquid changes to a solid or semi-solid state. | The lab tested various coagulations of the protein. 实验室测试了蛋白质的各种凝结物。| The doctor observed several coagulations in the patient's blood sample. 医生在病人的血液样本中观察到 several 凝结物。 | synonyms: clots, thickenings | antonyms: dilutions, liquefactions | noun
