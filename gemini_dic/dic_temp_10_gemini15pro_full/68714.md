farfetched:/ˌfɑːrˈfetʃt/ | adj. | 牵强的，不自然的，难以置信的 | improbable; strained; not naturally pertinent or relevant |  His explanation was a bit farfetched. 他的解释有点牵强。|  implausible, improbable, unlikely, unconvincing |  realistic, believable, plausible, credible | adjective

Etymology->
From far + fetch + -ed.  "Fetch" here means "to derive, deduce," related to the sense of "to go and bring."  So "far-fetched" literally means "brought from far," implying something that is strained or improbable.
