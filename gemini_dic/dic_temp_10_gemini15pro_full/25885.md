burlesque:/bərˈlɛsk/| n.  vt. adj.|滑稽模仿；讽刺作品；滑稽的；|A burlesque is a literary, dramatic or musical work intended to cause laughter by caricaturing something serious or by ludicrous treatment of a trivial subject. | The play was a burlesque of Victorian melodrama.  这出戏是对维多利亚时代情节剧的滑稽模仿。 | synonyms: parody, satire, lampoon, caricature, travesty | antonyms: serious, respectful, reverent | noun
burlesque:/bərˈlɛsk/| n.  vt. adj.|滑稽模仿；讽刺作品；滑稽的；|To burlesque something is to create a humorous imitation of it. |  The comedian burlesqued the president's speech.  喜剧演员滑稽地模仿了总统的演讲。 | synonyms: parody, satirize, mock, mimic | antonyms: honor, respect, revere | verb
burlesque:/bərˈlɛsk/| n.  vt. adj.|滑稽模仿；讽刺作品；滑稽的；|Something that is burlesque is comically exaggerated or mocking. | The burlesque show featured bawdy songs and comic skits. 滑稽表演以粗俗的歌曲和滑稽的小品为特色。 | synonyms: comical, ludicrous, farcical, absurd | antonyms: serious, solemn, dignified | adjective
