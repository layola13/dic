bawcock:/ˈbɔːkɒk/| n.|雄赳赳的男子，好汉，（古语）小伙子|A fine fellow; a swaggering fellow.  A term of endearment.|例句：He struts around like a bawcock, chest puffed out. 他像个好汉一样昂首阔步，挺着胸脯。

例句： "Thou art a bawcock," quoth the old knight to his squire.  "你是个好小子，"老骑士对他侍从说。|近义词：chap, fellow, lad, rooster|反义词：coward, weakling|词性:noun

词源:
From Middle English *baukok, from Old North French *bel cok (“handsome rooster”), equivalent to beau (“beautiful”) +‎ cock (“rooster”). Cognate with Walloon bê cok (“fine fellow”).
