ludwigite:/ˈlʌdwɪɡaɪt/| n. |路德维希特|A blackish mineral consisting of magnesium and iron borate, occurring in metamorphic limestone.  It is named after the Austrian chemist Ernst Ludwig (1842-1915).|Ludwigite is often found in association with magnetite. 路德维希特经常与磁铁矿伴生。|Synonym: none (highly specific mineral name)|Antonym: none (highly specific mineral name)|noun
