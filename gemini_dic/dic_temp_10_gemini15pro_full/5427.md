almirahs:/ælˈmɪrəz/
|n.|衣橱，衣柜|A large wardrobe or cupboard, typically one with shelves and doors, used for storing clothes or other items.  Origin: from Portuguese *almira*, from Arabic *al-mir'āh* "the mirror," originally referring to a mirrored cupboard.|We stored our winter blankets in the almirahs.|Synonyms: wardrobes, cupboards, closets|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "open shelves" or "drawers")
