downshifting:/ˈdaʊnˌʃɪftɪŋ/ | n. | 放慢生活节奏；降档；减速 |  The act of simplifying one's lifestyle, often by working less, moving to a less expensive area, or reducing material possessions, in order to increase personal satisfaction and reduce stress. |  Many people are choosing downshifting in order to spend more time with their families.  许多人选择放慢生活节奏以便有更多时间陪伴家人。| synonyms: scaling back, simplifying, decelerating | antonyms: upshifting, accelerating, escalating | noun

downshift:/ˈdaʊnˌʃɪft/ | v. |  降低档位；放慢生活节奏 | To change to a lower gear in a vehicle or to simplify one's lifestyle. | He downshifted as he approached the curve. 他在接近弯道时降低了档位。 She downshifted from her high-powered job to spend more time with her children. 她从高压力的工作中放慢了节奏，以便有更多时间陪伴孩子。 | synonyms: decelerate, slow down, scale back | antonyms: accelerate, speed up, escalate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "down" + "shift,"  "shift" originating from Old English *sciftan, of Germanic origin. "Down" is also of Old English origin.  The term gained popularity in the 1990s to describe the lifestyle trend.
