lock-out: /ˈlɒkaʊt/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkaʊt/ (US)| n.|停工；闭厂； lockout |A lockout is a work stoppage initiated by the management of a company during a labor dispute.  Management literally locks its doors to the workers to force them to accept a particular contract.|The company initiated a lockout after the union rejected their final offer. 公司在工会拒绝他们的最终提议后，启动了停工。|The lockout lasted for three weeks, causing significant financial hardship for the workers. 停工持续了三周，给工人们造成了严重的经济困难。|synonyms: shutdown, closure|antonyms:  reopening,  work resumption|noun
