escaloped:/ɪˈskæləpt/ /ɛˈskɑːləpt/| adj.|扇贝形的，(指食物)蘸面包屑烤制的|A dish (especially of meat or fish) baked in a sauce with breadcrumbs on top.|例句：We had escalloped potatoes for dinner. 我们晚饭吃了烤土豆片。|例句：She made escalloped oysters for the party. 她为聚会做了烤牡蛎。|近义词：scalloped, au gratin|反义词: (无直接反义词)|词性:形容词

escalope:/ˈeskəlɒp/ /ˈeskəˌloʊp/| n.|薄肉片|A thin slice of meat, especially veal, that is often coated in breadcrumbs and fried.|例句：He ordered veal escalope with lemon sauce. 他点了一份柠檬汁小牛肉片。|例句：The chef prepared the chicken escalopes perfectly. 厨师完美地烹制了鸡肉片。|近义词：cutlet, fillet|反义词: (无直接反义词)|词性:名词

词源:
From French *escalope*, from Old North French *escalope* (a shell, husk, pod), from Frankish *skalja* (shell, scale), from Proto-Germanic *skaljō* (shell, scale).  The culinary sense ("thin slice of meat") arose from the resemblance of the dish to a shell.
