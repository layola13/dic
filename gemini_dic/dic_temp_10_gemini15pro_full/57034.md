doublethink:/ˈdʌbəlθɪŋk/
n.|双重思想|The act of simultaneously accepting two mutually contradictory beliefs as correct, often induced by propaganda or manipulation.  The power of holding two contradictory beliefs in one's mind simultaneously, and accepting both of them.|He engaged in doublethink, believing both that the Party was infallible and that he could secretly rebel against it.|近义词:doublespeak, hypocrisy, cognitive dissonance|反义词:clarity, consistency, logical thinking|词性:名词
