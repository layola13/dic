aim:/eɪm/ | n. vi. vt. | 目标，目的；瞄准；致力 | An aim is something you hope to achieve. |  He took aim and fired. 他瞄准开火。 | synonyms: goal, objective, target, purpose | antonyms: discouragement, indifference | noun

aim:/eɪm/ | n. vi. vt. | 目标，目的；瞄准；致力 | To aim is to point or direct something at a target. |  She aimed to become a doctor. 她的目标是成为一名医生。 | synonyms: aspire, strive, endeavor | antonyms: wander, stray | verb

aim:/eɪm/ | n. vi. vt. | 目标，目的；瞄准；致力 | To aim is to intend or plan to achieve something. | The new program aims to reduce unemployment. 新项目旨在降低失业率。 | synonyms: intend, plan, design | antonyms: avoid, neglect | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *aimer (Modern French esmer), from Latin aestimō (“I estimate, appraise”), from aestimus (“estimated value”).
