foreshadowing: /fɔːrˈʃædəʊɪŋ/ (British) /fɔːrˈʃædoʊɪŋ/ (American)
|n.|预示，伏笔|An indication of something that might happen in the future; a hint or clue about what will happen later.|The dark clouds foreshadowed a storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨。|synonyms: prefigure, portend, augur, forebode|antonyms: conceal, hide, mask|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From fore- + shadow + -ing.  Fore- means "before" or "in front of."  Shadow in this context means "to show an indistinct image of something."  Thus, foreshadowing literally means "showing an image of something before it happens."
