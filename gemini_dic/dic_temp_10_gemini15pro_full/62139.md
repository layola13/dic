enclave:/ˈenkleɪv/ (US), /ˈɒŋkleɪv/ (UK)| n.|飞地; 孤立区; 被包围的领土|A portion of territory within or surrounded by a larger territory whose inhabitants are culturally or ethnically distinct.|The Spanish-speaking enclave in Miami is vibrant and colorful.  迈阿密的西班牙语飞地充满活力和色彩。

The Vatican City is an enclave within Rome. 梵蒂冈城是罗马境内的一块飞地。|近义词：territory, region, district, island, pocket|反义词：mainland, surrounding area|词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *enclave*, from *enclaver* "to enclose," from *en-* "in" + *clavis* "key" (source also of French *clef*, Spanish *clave*).  The original sense was of something locked in.
