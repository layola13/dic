heterodactylous:/ˌhɛtərəˈdæktɪləs/| adj.|异趾的，不同趾的|Having the digits or toes of unequal length or arranged differently than usual, typically referring to birds with three toes pointing forward and one pointing backward.|Example: The heterodactylous arrangement of the woodpecker's toes allows it to cling to tree trunks effectively.  啄木鸟异趾的排列使它能够有效地附着在树干上。

Example:  While most birds are anisodactylous, woodpeckers are notably heterodactylous.  虽然大多数鸟类是常态足的，但啄木鸟是明显的异趾的。|近义词:anisodactylous (in a different sense, as it describes a different toe arrangement), zygodactylous|反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it describes a specific condition.  Perhaps 'homodactylous' could be coined, but it's not a standard term.)|词性:adjective
