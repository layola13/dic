distinguisher:/dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃər/ | n. | 区别者，识别者，辨别者 | Something or someone that distinguishes or makes distinctions. | The small red dot on its head is the main distinguisher between this species and others.  它头部的小红点是区分该物种与其他物种的主要标志。|  近义词: differentiator, identifier, marker | 反义词: confuser | 词性: 名词

distinguish:/dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ/ | v. | 区别，辨别，识别；使…显得突出 |  To recognize or treat (someone or something) as different. |  I can distinguish between the twins. 我能区分这对双胞胎。 | 近义词: differentiate, discern, separate | 反义词: confuse, confound | 词性: 动词

distinguishing:/dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃɪŋ/ | adj. |  区别的，有特色的，显著的 | Serving to mark as different. |  His distinguishing feature is his height.  他的显著特征是他的身高。 | 近义词: characteristic, distinctive, differentiating | 反义词: common, ordinary | 词性: 形容词


词源:

From Late Latin *distinguere, from Latin distinguere (“to separate, divide, distinguish, adorn, embellish”), from dis- (“apart, asunder, in different directions”) + stinguere (“to quench, extinguish, prick, incite”).
