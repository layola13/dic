motivelessness:/ˈməʊtɪvləsnɪs/ /ˈmoʊtɪvləsnɪs/| n. |缺乏动力，无动力的状态|The state of lacking motivation or a driving force.|His motivelessness made it difficult for him to complete even simple tasks. 他的缺乏动力使他难以完成即使是简单的任务。|apathy, inertia, listlessness|motivation, drive, ambition|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From motive + -less + -ness.  "Motive" comes from Old French motif, ultimately from Latin motus, past participle of movēre "to move."  "-less" is a suffix meaning "without," from Old English -lēas. "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, from Old English -nes(s).
