fauns:/fɔːnz/
n.
森林之神，半羊人
Mythological creatures that are part human and part goat, often associated with woodlands and fields.
The fauns frolicked in the meadow.  森林之神在草地上嬉戏。

The painting depicted a group of fauns playing pipes.  这幅画描绘了一群吹着排箫的森林之神。

Synonyms: satyrs, sylvan
Antonyms: None.
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Faunus*, the Roman god of the wild, equivalent to the Greek god Pan.  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*dʰeh₁u̯-no-\* "god".
