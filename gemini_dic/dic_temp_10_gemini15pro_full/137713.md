nonutopians:/ˌnɒnjuːˈtəʊpiənz/
| n. |非乌托邦主义者；非理想主义者|People who do not believe in or subscribe to utopian ideals; those who are realistic or pragmatic, often skeptical of the possibility of achieving a perfect society.|The nonutopians argued that the proposed social reforms were impractical and unsustainable.|近义词：realists, pragmatists, skeptics|反义词:utopians, idealists|词性:Noun


Etymology:
"Non-" (prefix): from Latin "non" meaning "not."
"Utopia": coined by Sir Thomas More in 1516, from Greek "ou" (not) + "topos" (place), meaning "no place" or "nowhere."  It refers to an imagined perfect society.
"-ian" (suffix):  used to form nouns indicating a person belonging to or associated with a particular place, belief, or movement.
