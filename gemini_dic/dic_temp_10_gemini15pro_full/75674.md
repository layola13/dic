fulfilled /fʊlˈfɪld/ | adj. |  满足的，实现的 | Satisfied, having achieved something desired or promised. |  She felt fulfilled after completing the marathon. 她跑完马拉松后感到很满足。| satisfied, gratified, content | unfulfilled, dissatisfied | adjective

fulfilled /fʊlˈfɪld/ | v. | 实现，履行 | To carry out or bring to completion; to satisfy a requirement or condition. | The company fulfilled its promise to deliver the product on time.  公司履行了按时交付产品的承诺。| accomplish, achieve, execute | fail, neglect | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fulfillen*, from Old English *fullfyllan* ("to fill full, fulfill, complete"), equivalent to *full* + *fill*. Cognate with Dutch *vervullen* ("to fulfill"), German *erfüllen* ("to fulfill").  The sense of "satisfy" arose in the late 19th century.
