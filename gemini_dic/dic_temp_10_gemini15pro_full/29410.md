catacomb:/ˈkætəˌkuːm/ | n. |地下墓穴，陵墓|An underground cemetery, especially one consisting of tunnels and chambers.|例句：The catacombs of Rome are a fascinating glimpse into the city's history. 罗马的地下墓穴为了解这座城市的历史提供了一个迷人的视角。

例句：They explored the dark and mysterious catacombs beneath the city. 他们探索了城市下方黑暗而神秘的地下墓穴。|近义词：crypt, vault, tomb, mausoleum|反义词:  (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑用地上墓地cemetery, graveyard)|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *catacumbae (plural), perhaps originally the name of a particular Roman cemetery, of uncertain origin. Possibly from Greek κατά (kata, "down") + κύμβη (kymbe, "cavity, bowl").
