blindside:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪd/| vt. n.|使措手不及，出其不意；攻其不备；盲侧|To surprise someone, usually with bad news or an unpleasant experience; The side of something that cannot be seen easily.|例句：The recession blindsided many small businesses. 经济衰退使许多小企业措手不及。

例句：The news of his resignation completely blindsided us. 他辞职的消息完全让我们措手不及。

近义词：surprise, ambush, catch off guard|反义词：forewarn, anticipate|词性：动词，名词


blindside:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪd/| n.|盲侧，视野之外|The side of something that cannot be seen easily.|例句：The cyclist was hit by a car that came from his blindside.  骑自行车的人被一辆从他盲侧驶来的汽车撞了。

例句：He checked his blindside before changing lanes. 他在变道前检查了盲区。

近义词：blind spot|反义词：field of vision|词性：名词


词源:  From "blind" + "side," referring literally to the side on which one is blind or cannot see.  The figurative sense of attacking or surprising someone from an unexpected direction developed later.
