paralysed:/ˈpærəlaɪzd/ (British) /ˈpærəˌlaɪzd/ (American)| adj.|瘫痪的，麻痹的；不知所措的|unable to move or act; powerless|例句：He was paralysed from the waist down after the accident.  事故发生后，他腰部以下瘫痪了。

例句：Fear paralysed her.  恐惧让她不知所措。|近义词：immobilized, crippled, incapacitated, numb, powerless|反义词：mobile, active, able-bodied, capable|词性:形容词

paralyze:/ˈpærəlaɪz/ (British) /ˈpærəˌlaɪz/ (American)| vt.|使瘫痪，使麻痹；使丧失作用；使惊呆|to make someone unable to move or act; to make something unable to function; to make someone feel unable to do anything|例句：The venom of the snake can paralyze its prey.  蛇的毒液可以使猎物瘫痪。

例句：The strike paralyzed the entire transportation system.  罢工使整个交通系统瘫痪。|近义词：disable, cripple, incapacitate, immobilize, stun|反义词：enable, empower, activate|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *paralysatus, past participle of paralysare "to paralyze," from Greek paralytikos "affected with paralysis," from paralyein "to disable, loosen at the side, paralyze," from para- "beside, alongside" + lyein "to loosen, dissolve."
