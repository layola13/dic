marmot:/ˈmɑːrmət/ (US), /ˈmɑːmɒt/ (UK)| n. |土旱鼠；旱獭|A large, stout-bodied ground squirrel with a short bushy tail, found in mountainous areas of the northern hemisphere. | The marmot whistled a warning to its family as the hawk circled overhead. 土拨鼠在老鹰盘旋头顶时向它的家人吹着口哨发出警告。

|Synonyms: groundhog, woodchuck (especially in North America)|Antonyms: N/A|Noun


marmot:/ˈmɑːrmət/ (US), /ˈmɑːmɒt/ (UK)| n. |(比喻)冬眠者；嗜睡的人|A person who sleeps a lot, especially during the winter. |He's such a marmot in the winter; he just wants to hibernate until spring. 他冬天就像只土旱鼠，只想冬眠到春天。

|Synonyms: sleepyhead, dormouse|Antonyms: early bird|Noun
