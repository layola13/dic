microscopical: /ˌmaɪkrəˈskɒpɪkəl/ (UK) /ˌmaɪkrəˈskɑːpɪkl/ (US) | adj. | 微观的；极小的 |  So small as to be visible only with a microscope; relating to a microscope or microscopy. | The microscopical examination revealed the presence of bacteria.  显微镜检查显示存在细菌。| microscopic, minute, tiny | macroscopic, huge, large | adjective

microscopically: /ˌmaɪkrəˈskɒpɪkəli/ (UK) /ˌmaɪkrəˈskɑːpɪkli/ (US) | adv. | 用显微镜；微观地 | In a way that relates to or requires the use of a microscope; extremely small. |  The sample was microscopically analyzed.  样品用显微镜进行了分析。| minutely | macroscopically | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *microscopique* and directly from Modern Latin *microscopicus*, from *micro-* + *-scopicus* (from Greek *skopein* "to look at").
