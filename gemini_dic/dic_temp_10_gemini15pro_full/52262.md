dicast:/ˈdaɪkæst/| n.|陪审员，法官|A juror or judge in ancient Greece, especially in Athens.|The dicast listened carefully to the arguments presented by both sides. 陪审员仔细聆听了双方提出的论点。|近义词：juror, judge|反义词:defendant, accused|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek δικαστής (dikastḗs, “judge”), from δικάζω (dikázō, “I judge”), from δίκη (díkē, “justice, right, lawsuit”).
