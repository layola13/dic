ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *holden, past participle of holden, from Old English *hālden, past participle of healdan (“to hold, keep, guard, observe, rule, possess, maintain”), from Proto-Germanic *haldaną (“to hold, keep, tend”), from Proto-Indo-European *kel- (“to incline, bend, hold”).  Cognate with Dutch houden, German halten, and Gothic haldan.  The use of "holden" as an adjective is less common than the past participle form "held," but it persists in certain contexts, especially legal or formal ones.  As a surname, it is derived from a place name meaning "deep valley."
