词源:

From Hebrew אֲרִיאֵל ('ari'el, literally “lion of God”), composed of אֲרִי ('ari, “lion”) + אֵל ('el, “God”).  Borrowed into English from the Hebrew Bible as a place name and the name of an angel.  Later used by Shakespeare as the name of an air spirit in The Tempest, from which the modern usage as a generic term for air spirits derives.
