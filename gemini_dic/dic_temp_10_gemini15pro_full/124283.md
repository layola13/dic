mothballing:/ˈmɔːθˌbɔːlɪŋ/ | n. |封存；搁置；暂停使用|The act of putting something into storage or preserving it for later use, often referring to equipment, projects, or ideas.  The process involves protecting the item from damage or deterioration while it is not in use.|例句：The government is considering mothballing the nuclear power plant. 政府正在考虑封存核电站。


例句：The project was mothballed due to lack of funding.  由于缺乏资金，该项目被搁置了。


近义词：preservation, storage, suspension, shelving, inactivation |反义词: reactivation, recommissioning, restarting |词性:noun


mothball:/ˈmɔːθˌbɔːl/ | v. |封存；搁置；暂停使用|To put something into storage or preserve it for later use. |例句：They mothballed the ship for the winter. 他们把船封存起来过冬。


例句：The company mothballed the factory because of falling demand. 由于需求下降，公司封存了工厂。


近义词：preserve, store, suspend, shelve, inactivate |反义词: reactivate, recommission, restart |词性:verb
