balistraria:/ˌbælɪˈstreəriə/ | n. | 弩炮射击孔，箭楼 | An opening or loophole in a fortified wall or parapet, through which crossbows or other projectile weapons could be fired. |  The castle's balistrariae offered excellent defensive positions for archers. 城堡的弩炮射击孔为弓箭手提供了绝佳的防御阵地。| synonym: loophole, embrasure, crenel | antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "solid wall" in a loose sense) | noun

balistraria:/ˌbælɪˈstreəriə/ | n. | 箭狭槽 | A narrow slit in a wall for shooting arrows. | The archers rained arrows down on the attackers through the balistrariae.  弓箭手们透过箭狭槽向进攻者们射箭。 | synonym: arrow slit, loophole | antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "solid wall" in a loose sense) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *balistraria, from Latin balista (a type of catapult), ultimately from Greek βαλλίστρα (ballistra), from βάλλειν (ballein, “to throw”).
