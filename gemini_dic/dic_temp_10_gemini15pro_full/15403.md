backwoodsman: /ˈbækˌwʊdzmən/ | n. | 森林居民；居住在偏远地区的人；粗俗的人 | A person who lives in a remote, wooded area, often characterized as being uneducated or unrefined. |  He was a true backwoodsman, living off the land and rarely venturing into town. 他是一个真正的森林居民，靠土地为生，很少进城。

|  The old backwoodsman had a wealth of knowledge about the local flora and fauna.  这位老森林居民对当地的动植物了如指掌。 |  Synonyms: frontiersman, pioneer, hillbilly, rustic | Antonyms: city dweller, sophisticate | noun
