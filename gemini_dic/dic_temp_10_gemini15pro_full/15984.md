balustrade: /ˌbæləˈstreɪd/ | n. | 栏杆，扶手 | A railing supported by balusters, especially an ornamental parapet on a balcony, bridge, or terrace. |  The marble balustrade gleamed in the sunlight. 大理石栏杆在阳光下闪闪发光。 | synonyms: railing, banister, parapet | antonyms: none | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *balustrade*, from Italian *balaustrata*, from *balaustro* "baluster," perhaps ultimately from Greek *balaustion* "flower of the wild pomegranate," because of a resemblance in shape.
