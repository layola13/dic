monochord:/ˈmɒnəkɔːd/ /ˈmɑːnəkɔːrd/ | n. |单弦琴，单弦音箱|A single-stringed musical instrument used for acoustical experiments and demonstrations, especially in ancient Greece, consisting of a single string stretched over a sound box with a movable bridge that could be used to divide the string into different lengths and thus produce different notes.  It was also used for tuning other instruments. |The scientist used a monochord to demonstrate the principles of acoustics. 科学家使用单弦琴演示声学原理。|
The musician tuned his lute using a monochord.  这位音乐家用单弦琴给他的鲁特琴调音。|
Synonyms: sonometer | Antonyms: polychord (hypothetical) | Noun
