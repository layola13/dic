oversentimentalizing: /ˌoʊvərˌsɛntɪˈmɛntəˌlaɪzɪŋ/
|v. (present participle)|过度伤感，过分感伤|The act of being excessively sentimental; dwelling on or exaggerating emotions, especially sadness or tenderness.|She was over-sentimentalizing the death of her goldfish, crying for days and writing poems about it.|近义词：overdramatizing, wallowing, exaggerating|反义词：understating, downplaying|词性: Verb (present participle)

oversentimentalize: /ˌoʊvərˌsɛntɪˈmɛntəˌlaɪz/
|v.|过度伤感，过分感伤|To be excessively sentimental; to dwell on or exaggerate emotions, especially sadness or tenderness.|He tends to oversentimentalize his relationships, leading to unrealistic expectations.|近义词：overdramatize, wallow, exaggerate|反义词：understate, downplay|词性: Verb


词源:  "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "sentimentalize" (from "sentiment" + "-ize").  "Sentiment" comes from French "sentiment" ultimately from Latin "sentire" (to feel).
