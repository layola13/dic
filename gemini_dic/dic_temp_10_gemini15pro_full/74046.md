fossa:/ˈfɒsə/ (UK) /ˈfɑːsə/ (US)| n.|窝，穴，凹陷；（解剖学）窝，小窝|A fossa is a shallow depression or hollow, typically in a bone or other anatomical structure. It can also refer to a ditch or moat, particularly in fortifications.  The word is also used in zoology to refer to a specific type of carnivorous mammal native to Madagascar.|The humerus has a radial fossa.|近义词：cavity, depression, pit, trench, ditch|反义词:ridge, protuberance|noun
