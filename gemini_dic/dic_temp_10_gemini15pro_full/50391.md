depose:/dɪˈpoʊz/ /dɪˈpəʊz/| vt.| 免职；罢免；作证；宣誓作证| To remove someone from a position of power, especially a monarch or other ruler; to testify or give evidence, especially under oath. | The king was deposed by his own son. 国王被他自己的儿子废黜了。 | synonyms: oust, overthrow, remove, testify, declare | antonyms: install, reinstate, appoint | verb

depose:/dɪˈpoʊz/ /dɪˈpəʊz/| vt.| 存放；放置|To lay down, place, or deposit something. (This meaning is less common.) | He deposed his bag on the floor. 他把包放在了地板上。 | synonyms: place, put, lay, deposit | antonyms: remove, take | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *deposer*, from Latin *dēpōnere* (“to lay down, put down, deposit, entrust”), from *dē* (“down, away from”) + *pōnere* (“to put, place”).
