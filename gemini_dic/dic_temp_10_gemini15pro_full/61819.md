empires:/ˈempaɪərz/| n.|帝国（复数）|A group of nations or peoples ruled by a single supreme authority, usually an emperor or empress.|The Roman and British empires were vast and powerful.|近义词：kingdoms, realms, dominions|反义词:colonies, dependencies|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *empire* (12c., modern French *empire*), from Latin *imperium* "command, control, supreme power; dominion, sovereignty, empire," from *imperare* "to command," from *in* "in" + *parare* "to order, prepare."
