dooms:/duːmz/| v. |注定失败，注定毁灭| condemns to ruination or death. |The scandal doomed his political career.  这桩丑闻毁了他的政治生涯。|近义词：condemns, sentences, destines|反义词:saves, rescues| verb (third-person singular simple present dooms, present participle dooming, simple past and past participle doomed)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dōm ("judgment, law, statute, decree, sentence, doom"), from Proto-Germanic *dōmaz ("judgment"), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁- ("to put, place, do"). Cognate with Old Saxon dōm ("judgment, decree"), Old Frisian dōm ("choice, judgment, renown"), Old High German tuom ("judgment"), Old Norse dómr ("judgment, dignity"), Gothic 𐌳𐍉𐌼𐍃 (dōms, “judgment”).
