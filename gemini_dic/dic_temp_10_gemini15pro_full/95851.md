incorrigiblenesses: /ɪnˈkɔːrɪdʒɪblənɪsɪz/
n.
不可救药， incorrigibility
The incorrigiblenesses of his behavior frustrated his parents. 他不可救药的行为让他的父母感到沮丧。
incorrigibility, hopelessness, irredeemability
reformability, corrigibility
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *incorrigible*, from Late Latin *incorrigibilis*, from Latin *in-* (not) + *corrigibilis* (corrigible, capable of being corrected), from *corrigere* (to correct), from *com-* (together) + *regere* (to guide, to rule). The suffix *-ness* forms nouns of quality or state from adjectives.
