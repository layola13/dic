bretwalda:/ˈbrɛtwɑːldə/ | n. | 霸主，最高统治者 | An Anglo-Saxon term, historically applied to a king who held a position of overlordship over other Anglo-Saxon kings.  A ruler of one of the Anglo-Saxon kingdoms in England who held a recognized position of overlordship over kings of other kingdoms. | Example: Offa of Mercia is considered one of the most powerful bretwaldas.  奥法是麦西亚王国最强大的统治者之一，被认为是一位霸主。

Example: The title of bretwalda was not hereditary and depended on the individual king's power and influence.  霸主称号并非世袭，而是取决于国王个人的权力和影响力。| 近义词: overlord, high king, paramount ruler | 反义词: subject, vassal | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old English *Brytenwealda (ruler of Britain), composed of Bryten (Britain) + wealda (ruler, wielder of power).
