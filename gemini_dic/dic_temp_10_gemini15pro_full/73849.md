formulae's:/ˈfɔːrmjʊliːz/ |n.|公式的|The possessive form of "formulae," meaning belonging to or associated with formulas.  "Formulae" itself is an older, less common plural of "formula." |The formulae's complexity made it difficult to understand. 这个公式的复杂性使它难以理解。|synonyms: formulas'|antonyms: N/A|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin formula, from Latin fōrma ("form, shape, rule, pattern"). The plural "formulae" retains the original Latin plural.  "Formulae's" adds the English possessive 's.
