ill-disposedness:/ˌɪl dɪˈspoʊzdnəs/
|n.|恶意；不友好；不情愿|The ill-disposedness of the critics towards the new play was evident in their scathing reviews.  评论家对这出新戏的恶意在他们尖刻的评论中显而易见。|
|English Explanation: A noun referring to a state of being unwilling or unfavorably inclined; hostility; malevolence.|
|Example 1:  His ill-disposedness towards his neighbors made it difficult for him to integrate into the community. 他对邻居的恶意让他很难融入社区。|
|Example 2:  The king's ill-disposedness to reform led to widespread unrest. 国王不愿改革导致了广泛的动荡。|
|Synonyms: hostility, animosity, antagonism, malevolence, unfriendliness, antipathy|
|Antonyms: benevolence, goodwill, friendliness, kindness, amity|


ETYMOLOGY->
From ill- + disposed + -ness.  "Ill" signifies "bad" or "unfavorable."  "Disposed" means "inclined" or "having a tendency towards."  "-ness" is a suffix that forms nouns denoting a state or quality.
