look-down-on: /ˌlʊk ˈdaʊn ɒn/ (UK) /ˌlʊk ˈdaʊn ɑːn/ (US)| phrasal verb | 轻视，瞧不起 | To consider someone or something inferior; to treat with disdain. | He looks down on anyone who hasn't had a university education. 他瞧不起任何没有受过大学教育的人。| synonyms: despise, disdain, scorn, condescend to | antonyms: admire, respect, look up to | verb phrase


ETYMOLOGY->
From the literal sense of looking down from a higher position, implying superiority. The metaphorical usage developed from this physical act.
