firlot:/ˈfɜːrlɒt/ (US) /ˈfɪrlət/ (UK)| n. |福乐(苏格兰旧容量单位)|A former Scottish unit of dry measure, varying in quantity for different commodities but usually equal to about 21.96 liters for oats, barley, malt, and meal, and about 31.98 liters for wheat, rye, beans, and peas.  It was equivalent to four pecks.|The farmer measured out a firlot of barley for the livestock.|近义词:boll, peck|反义词: (No direct antonym for a unit of measurement)|noun
