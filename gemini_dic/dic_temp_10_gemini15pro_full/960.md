academism: /əˈkædɪˌsɪzəm/
n.
学院派；学院主义；学术主义
A doctrine or style in art that emphasizes the importance of academic standards, especially in representing form, and often ignores the emotional or imaginative element.  The adherence to formal rules and traditional methods at the expense of originality and creativity.
His paintings were criticized for their dry academism. 他的绘画因其枯燥的学院主义而受到批评。

The museum's collection reflects a certain academism in its focus on 19th-century portraiture.  这家博物馆的藏品专注于19世纪的肖像画，反映出某种学院主义。

近义词: conventionalism, formalism, traditionalism
反义词: modernism, avant-garde, originality
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *académisme*, from *Académie* (French Academy) + *-isme* (-ism).  Ultimately from Greek *Akadēmeia* (Academy), the name of Plato's school of philosophy, named after a hero Akademos.
