parents: /ˈperənts/ (UK) /ˈpɛrənts/ (US)| n. |父母|The people who are responsible for the birth and upbringing of a child.|The school encourages parents to be involved in their children's education. 学校鼓励家长参与孩子的教育。|
parents: /ˈperənts/ (UK) /ˈpɛrənts/ (US)| n. |根源，起源| (formal, often used in the plural) Something from which something else originates or develops.|Modern Italian has its parents in Latin. 现代意大利语起源于拉丁语。|


Etymology: From Old French *parent, present participle of paroir "to appear, be visible", from Latin parere "to bring forth, give birth to, produce; obey; appear, be visible".  The original sense was "one who begets or brings forth", then generalized to "father or mother".

Synonyms:  mother and father, folks (informal), guardians (when parents are absent or deceased)

Antonyms: children, offspring
