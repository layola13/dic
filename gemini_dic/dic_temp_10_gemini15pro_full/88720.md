hobiler:/ˈhɒbɪlər/ (British English) /ˈhɑːbɪlər/ (American English)| n.| 轻骑兵；轻装步兵 |A hobiler was a light cavalryman or soldier, typically armed with a light spear or lance, and sometimes a sword or other sidearm. They were often used for scouting, raiding, and skirmishing. | The hobilers harassed the enemy flanks. 轻骑兵骚扰敌人的侧翼。| synonyms: light horseman, scout, skirmisher | antonyms: heavy cavalry, infantry | noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *hobelar*, from Anglo-Norman *hobelour*, from Old French *hobelart*, possibly from *hober*, meaning "to jump" or "to move quickly," referring to their agile movements on horseback.
