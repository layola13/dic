duncishly:/ˈdʌnʃɪʃli/
adverb|笨拙地，愚蠢地|In a duncish manner; clumsily or stupidly.|He duncishly stumbled over his words, unable to articulate his thoughts clearly. 他笨拙地结结巴巴，无法清楚地表达他的想法。|Synonyms: awkwardly, clumsily, stupidly, foolishly|Antonyms: gracefully, skillfully, intelligently, wisely|词性: 副词

Etymology:
From *dunce* (a stupid person) + *-ish* (forming adjectives) + *-ly* (forming adverbs).  The word "dunce" ultimately derives from the name of John Duns Scotus, a 13th-century philosopher whose followers' views were later ridiculed.
