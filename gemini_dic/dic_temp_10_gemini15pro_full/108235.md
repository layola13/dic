laughing-gases:/ˈlɑːfɪŋ ˌɡæsɪz/ | n. | 笑气 |  Nitrous oxide (N₂O), a colorless, sweet-smelling gas used as an anesthetic and sometimes recreationally. It can cause mild euphoria and hallucinations. Also known as laughing gas. | The dentist used laughing-gases to help calm the patient. 牙医使用笑气来帮助病人镇静。|  The recreational use of laughing-gases is dangerous.  娱乐性使用笑气很危险。 |Synonyms: nitrous oxide, dinitrogen monoxide | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a chemical compound, but perhaps "analgesic antagonist" in a medical context) | noun
