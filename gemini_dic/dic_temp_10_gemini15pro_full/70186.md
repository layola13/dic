fibrin:/ˈfaɪbrɪn/| n. |纤维蛋白|An insoluble protein formed from fibrinogen during the clotting of blood. It forms a fibrous mesh that impedes the flow of blood.|例句：Fibrin plays a vital role in blood clotting. 纤维蛋白在血液凝固中起着至关重要的作用。

例句：The wound was sealed with a mesh of fibrin. 伤口被纤维蛋白网封住了。|近义词：coagulant |反义词: None|词性: noun

fibrinogen:/faɪˈbrɪnədʒən/ | n. |纤维蛋白原|A soluble protein present in blood plasma, from which fibrin is produced by the action of the enzyme thrombin.|例句：Fibrinogen levels are often checked before surgery. 手术前通常会检查纤维蛋白原水平。

例句：Fibrinogen is converted to fibrin during blood coagulation. 在血液凝固过程中，纤维蛋白原转化为纤维蛋白。|近义词：clotting factor |反义词: None|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fibrine*, from Latin *fibra* ("fiber, filament") + *-in* (suffix forming nouns of chemical substances).
