aestheticist: /ɛsˈθɛtɪsɪst/ /iːsˈθɛtɪsɪst/
| n. |唯美主义者；审美家|A person who is devoted to aesthetic principles and the pursuit of beauty, especially in art. | Oscar Wilde was a famous aestheticist. 奥斯卡·王尔德是一位著名的唯美主义者。 |Synonyms: connoisseur, art lover, aesthete | Antonyms: philistine, barbarian | Noun

aestheticist: /ɛsˈθɛtɪsɪst/ /iːsˈθɛtɪsɪst/
| adj. |唯美主义的；审美的|Relating to or characterized by aesthetic principles or the pursuit of beauty. |  He had a very aestheticist approach to interior design. 他对室内设计有一种非常唯美主义的态度。 | Synonyms: artistic, tasteful, refined | Antonyms: unrefined, tasteless, vulgar | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the word "aesthetic" + "-ist" (suffix forming nouns denoting persons who practice or are concerned with something). "Aesthetic" comes from the Greek "aisthētikos" meaning "of sense perception," from "aisthēta" meaning "perceptible things."
