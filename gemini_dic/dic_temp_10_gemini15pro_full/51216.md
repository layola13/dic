destitution:/ˌdɛstɪˈtjuːʃən/ | n. | 赤贫；穷困潦倒 |  The state of being without the basic necessities of life, such as food, clothing, and shelter.  | Many families were reduced to destitution by the war.  战争使许多家庭陷入了赤贫。 | synonyms: poverty, indigence, penury, want, deprivation | antonyms: wealth, affluence, prosperity | noun

destitute:/ˈdɛstɪtjuːt/ | adj. | 赤贫的；穷困潦倒的 | Without the basic necessities of life; lacking something needed or desirable. |  The earthquake left many people destitute. 地震使许多人一贫如洗。| synonyms: impoverished, penniless, indigent, needy | antonyms: wealthy, affluent, prosperous | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *destitutus*, past participle of *destituere*  "to abandon, forsake," from *de-* "down, away" + *statuere* "to set, place."
