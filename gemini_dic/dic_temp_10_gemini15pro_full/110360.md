lightships:/ˈlaɪtʃɪps/ | n. |灯船|A ship equipped with a bright light, anchored at a specific location to warn other ships of navigational hazards.|例句：The lightship marked the entrance to the harbor. 灯船标志着港口的入口。

例句：Lightships were crucial before modern navigation systems. 在现代导航系统出现之前，灯船至关重要。|近义词：beacon, lightship, buoy |反义词: N/A |词性: noun

Etymology: From light + ship.  The word "light" comes from Old English "lēoht," and "ship" comes from Old English "scip."
