infiltrate:/ˈɪnfɪltreɪt/| v. |渗透，潜入|To enter or gain access to (an organization, place, etc.) surreptitiously and gradually, especially in order to acquire secret information or cause damage.|例句：The rebels infiltrated the government. 叛军渗透了政府。|例句：The company was infiltrated by spies.  公司被间谍渗透了。|近义词：penetrate, permeate, invade|反义词：exfiltrate, withdraw|词性:动词

infiltration:/ˌɪnfɪlˈtreɪʃn/| n. |渗透，潜入|The action of entering or gaining access to an organization, place, etc., surreptitiously and gradually, especially in order to acquire secret information or cause damage.|例句：The infiltration of the enemy agents was successful. 敌方特工的渗透是成功的。|例句：The report revealed the infiltration of organized crime into legitimate businesses. 报告揭露了有组织犯罪对合法企业的渗透。|近义词：penetration, permeation, invasion|反义词：exfiltration, withdrawal|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin infiltratus, past participle of infiltrare (“to filter in”), from in- (“in, into”) + filtrare (“to filter”).
