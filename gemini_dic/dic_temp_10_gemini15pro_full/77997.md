gentleman:/ˈdʒɛntlmən/ | n. |绅士，先生|A man of good social position, especially one of wealth and leisure; a courteous or honorable man.|例句：He behaved like a true gentleman. 他表现得像个真正的绅士。|例句：The gentlemen were all dressed in formal attire. 先生们都穿着正式服装。|近义词：man, sir, fellow, guy|反义词：cad, lout, boor|Noun

gentlemanly:/ˌdʒɛntlmənli/ | adj. |绅士的，有礼貌的|Behaving in a way that is traditionally considered polite and well-mannered, especially towards women.|例句：He opened the door for her with a gentlemanly gesture. 他以绅士的姿态为她开门。|例句：His gentlemanly conduct earned him the respect of his peers. 他绅士般的行为赢得了同行的尊重。|近义词：courteous, polite, respectful, chivalrous|反义词：rude, impolite, ungentlemanly, boorish|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French gentilhomme, from gentil "noble, gentle" (from Latin gentilis "of the same clan") + homme "man" (from Latin homō).
