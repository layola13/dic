legislatrix: /ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪtrɪks/
noun
女立法者; 女性立法委员
A female legislator; a woman who makes laws.
例句：The newly elected legislatrix championed environmental protection.  新当选的女立法委员倡导环境保护。

例句：The legislatrix proposed a bill to increase funding for education.  这位女立法委员提议一项增加教育经费的法案。

近义词：lawmaker, legislator, congresswoman (if in Congress)
反义词： N/A (No direct antonym for a person making laws)
词性：noun

Etymology:  From Latin *legislatrix*, feminine form of *legislator*, from *legislat-* (past participle stem of *legislare* "to legislate") + *-tor* (agent suffix).  *Legislare* is from *lex* "law" + *-lare* (frequentative suffix).
