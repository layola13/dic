daintily:/ˈdeɪntɪli/| adv.|精致地；优雅地；美味地|In a refined and delicate manner; characterized by grace and elegance, often in movement or action; sometimes also implying a small or delicate appetite.|She daintily picked up the small cake with her fingertips. 她用指尖优雅地拿起小蛋糕。|Synonyms: gracefully, elegantly, delicately, exquisitely, fastidiously|Antonyms: clumsily, awkwardly, crudely, roughly|Word Type: Adverb

daintily:/ˈdeɪntɪli/| adv.|讲究地；挑剔地|Fastidiously; with careful attention to detail, often implying a delicate or selective appetite.|He ate daintily, savoring each small bite. 他吃得很讲究，细细品味每一小口。|Synonyms: fastidiously, meticulously, carefully, selectively|Antonyms: voraciously, greedily, indiscriminately|Word Type: Adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dayntely, *daynteliche, from daynte (“pleasant, delightful, delicious”) +‎ -ly.  Ultimately from Old French daintie (“delicacy, pleasure”), from Latin dignitas (“worthiness, dignity”).
