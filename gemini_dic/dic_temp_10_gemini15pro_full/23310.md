boycott: /ˈbɔɪkɒt/ (UK) /ˈbɔɪkɑːt/ (US)
|n. vt.|抵制，联合抵制|An organized refusal to buy or use something, or to deal with a person or group, as a way of protesting. |Many people boycotted the company's products due to their unethical labor practices. 许多人因为该公司不道德的劳动行为而抵制其产品。|
| | |The union called on its members to boycott the meeting. 工会号召其成员抵制此次会议。|
| | |Synonyms: ban, embargo, prohibit, ostracize|
| | |Antonyms: support, patronize, endorse|
| | |noun, verb|
