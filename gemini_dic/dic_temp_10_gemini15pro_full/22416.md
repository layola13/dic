book-learned: /ˈbʊk lɜːrnd/ | adj. | 书本知识的，纸上谈兵的 | Learned from books rather than from practical experience; academic. | He was book-learned but lacked practical experience. 他满腹经纶，但缺乏实践经验。|  She had a lot of book-learned knowledge about farming, but had never actually set foot on a farm.  她有很多关于农业的书本知识，但实际上从未踏足过农场。 |近义词: academic, theoretical, scholarly | 反义词: practical, experienced, hands-on | 词性: adjective

Etymology:  Compound of "book" and "learned," the past participle of "learn."  The word "book" traces back to Old English "bōc,"  meaning "book, writing, document," from Proto-Germanic "*bokiz."  "Learn" comes from Old English "leornian," meaning "to learn, study, read," from Proto-Germanic "*liznōjaną."
