an-arm-and-a-leg:/ən ˌɑːrm ənd ə ˈleɡ/ | idiom |代价高昂；非常昂贵 |  This idiom means something is very expensive. It implies that you would have to give up a significant part of yourself (like an arm and a leg) to afford it. | That new car cost him an arm and a leg. 那辆新车花了他一大笔钱。 |  expensive, costly, exorbitant, pricey | cheap, inexpensive, affordable | idiom
近义词: a king's ransom, a small fortune
反义词: a bargain, a steal
