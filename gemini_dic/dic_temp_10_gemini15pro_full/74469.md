frankers:/ˈfræŋkərz/| n.|法郎（复数）;  坦率的人（复数）|A currency unit of several countries, especially Switzerland and France. /  People who are honest and direct in expressing their opinions and feelings.|The shop accepted Swiss frankers. 这家商店接受瑞士法郎。/ The frankers in the group voiced their concerns immediately.  小组中坦率的人立即表达了他们的担忧。|近义词：(currency)franc, (person)candid, straightforward, upfront|反义词:(person)dishonest, deceptive, evasive|词性:noun


frankers (currency):

词源: From Old French *franc, short for franc d'argent "free of silver", referring to a coin introduced in 1360.

用法说明:  Primarily used in the plural to refer to the currency of Switzerland and formerly of France.  Can sometimes be encountered as a singular "franker" but "franc" is the standard singular form.


frankers (person):

词源: From Frank, referring to a member of the Germanic tribes that conquered Gaul (France) in the 6th century. The word "frank" came to mean "free" and then "honest" or "outspoken."

用法说明: A less common usage, generally informal. Refers to people who are open and direct in their communication.  "Frank" is the more commonly used singular form.
