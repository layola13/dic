muskets:/ˈmʌskɪts/| n.|火枪，滑膛枪|A firearm with a long barrel, typically smoothbore, fired from the shoulder and using a matchlock, wheellock, or flintlock mechanism to ignite the charge.  Early firearms, used before rifles became common.|The soldiers were armed with muskets and bayonets. 士兵们配备了火枪和刺刀。|Synonyms: firearm, gun, rifle (in general usage)|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "modern firearm")|Noun
