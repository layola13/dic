cotised:/ˈkəʊtaɪzd/
adjective
并列的，相邻的 (Heraldry)
Placed or borne side-by-side, especially of narrow charges placed on either side of a wider one.
The shield is argent, a fess cotised gules. 盾牌是银色的，有一条红色横条纹，两侧各有一条较窄的红色条纹。
Synonyms:  flanked, bordered, accompanied
Antonyms: separated, isolated
词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *cotisé, past participle of cotiser "to tax, assess," from cote "quota, assessment," from late Latin quota (pars) "(proportional) part," from Latin quot "how many."  The heraldic sense developed from the idea of "side assessments" added to the main charge.
