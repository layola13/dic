dichroscope:/ˈdaɪkrəˌskoʊp/| n.|二色镜|A pocket instrument used in mineralogy for examining the dichroism of crystals, consisting of a cleaved and polished crystal of Iceland spar in a tube having a square hole at one end and a lens at the other.|Geologists use a dichroscope to examine the different colors exhibited by certain gemstones when viewed from different angles.|Synonyms: dichroiscope|Antonyms: N/A|noun
