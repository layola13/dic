boondoggler:/ˈbuːndɑːɡlər/ | n. | 闲混者，不务正业的人；浪费公款的人 | Someone who engages in trivial or useless work, especially in a government job, often to appear busy or to justify their position.  This often implies wasting public funds. | The senator denounced his opponent as a boondoggler who wasted taxpayer money on frivolous projects.  参议员谴责他的对手是一个浪费纳税人钱财在无聊项目上的闲混者。

The report exposed several boondogglers in the department who spent their days surfing the internet. 报告揭露了部门里几个整天在网上闲逛的闲混者。 |  Synonyms: idler, loafer, slacker, time-waster, shirker | Antonyms: hard worker, diligent worker | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From the word "boondoggle," which originated in the 1930s in the United States.  It referred to a type of braided leather lanyard made by Boy Scouts.  Later, it came to mean wasteful or unnecessary work, likely due to the perception that making these lanyards was a frivolous activity.
