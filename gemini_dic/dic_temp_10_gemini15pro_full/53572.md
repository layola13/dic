disbelievingly:/ˌdɪsbɪˈliːvɪŋli/| adv.|不相信地，怀疑地|In a manner that shows a lack of belief or trust.|She looked at him disbelievingly. 她怀疑地看着他。|Synonyms: skeptically, doubtfully, incredulously|Antonyms: trustingly, believingly|adverb

disbelieve:/ˌdɪsbɪˈliːv/| v.|不相信，怀疑|To not believe; to have doubts about the truth of something.|I disbelieve his story. 我不相信他的故事。|Synonyms: doubt, question, mistrust|Antonyms: believe, trust, accept|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + believe + -ing (present participle) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  The root "believe" comes from Old English "belēfan" meaning "to have faith in."
