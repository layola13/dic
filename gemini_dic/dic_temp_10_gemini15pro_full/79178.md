glamoured:/ˈɡlæmərd/ | adj. | 着迷的，被施魔法的 | Affected by glamour; charmed or fascinated. | She was completely glamoured by the Hollywood lifestyle. 她完全被好莱坞的生活方式迷住了。 |  She was glamoured by his charisma and charm. 她被他的人格魅力所吸引。 | Near Synonyms: enchanted, captivated, fascinated, beguiled, charmed | Antonyms: disenchanted, disillusioned, unimpressed | Word Class: Adjective

glamor:/ˈɡlæmər/ (American English) glamour:/ˈɡlæmə(r)/ (British English) | n. | 魅力，魔力，吸引力 | An attractive or exciting quality that makes certain people or things seem appealing or special. | The city has lost some of its glamour in recent years. 这座城市近年来失去了一些魅力。| Old Hollywood movies had a certain glamour. 老好莱坞电影有一种独特的魅力。| Near Synonyms: charm, allure, magic, fascination, charisma | Antonyms: dullness, ordinariness, plainness | Word Class: Noun
