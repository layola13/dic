articulated:/ɑːrˈtɪkjʊleɪtɪd/
articulated| adj.|清晰表达的，口齿伶俐的；有关节的| expressed clearly and fluently; having joints|  The professor delivered a well-articulated lecture on the history of philosophy. 教授就哲学史发表了一场精彩的演讲。|Synonyms: eloquent, fluent, coherent, lucid, expressive| Antonyms: inarticulate, mumbled, unclear, garbled|adjective

articulate:/ɑːrˈtɪkjʊleɪt/
articulate| v.|清晰地表达；连接| to express (an idea or feeling) fluently and coherently; to form a joint| She struggled to articulate her complex emotions. 她努力表达她复杂的情感。 | Synonyms: express, voice, utter, pronounce, enunciate | Antonyms: mumble, slur, mutter|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin articulatus, past participle of articulare “to utter distinctly, joint,” from Latin articulus “small joint, division, moment,” diminutive of artus “joint, limb.”
