as fit as a fiddle:/æz ˈfɪt æz ə ˈfɪdəl/| adj.|非常健康；精神饱满|Meaning "in perfect health," this idiom compares someone's well-being to a fiddle that is perfectly tuned and ready to play.|例句：My grandmother is 90 years old and as fit as a fiddle. 我的祖母90岁了，身体还非常硬朗。

例句：He's been training hard and is as fit as a fiddle for the marathon.  他一直刻苦训练，为马拉松做好了充分的准备，身体状态极佳。|近义词：healthy, well, strong, robust|反义词:sick, unwell, weak, frail|词性:adjective
ETYMOLOGY->
The origin of "as fit as a fiddle" is uncertain.  "Fit" originally meant "suitable, proper," evolving to mean "in good condition." "Fiddle" refers to a violin. A well-tuned fiddle is ready to play, suggesting peak condition.  The phrase likely emerged in the 16th or 17th century, though its exact origin is unknown.
