brahmanism: /ˈbrɑːmənɪzəm/ | n. | 婆罗门教 | A historical term for the religious tradition of Brahmanical Hinduism, based on the Vedas and centered on the priestly caste of Brahmins. It emphasizes ritual purity, sacrifice, and the authority of the Brahmins. | Brahmanism was the dominant religion in India before the rise of Buddhism. 婆罗门教是佛教兴起之前印度的主要宗教。| Hinduism, Vedic religion | Buddhism, Jainism | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Brahman (priest, member of the highest Hindu caste) + -ism. Ultimately from the Sanskrit word "bráhman" (prayer, sacred knowledge).
