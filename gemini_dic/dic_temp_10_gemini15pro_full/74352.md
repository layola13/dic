ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *fragraunt*, from Old French *fragrant*, from Latin *fragrāns*, present participle of *fragrāre* (“to emit a sweet smell, to be fragrant”), from *fragrum* (“strawberry”) (of uncertain origin) + *-āre* (verbal suffix forming verbs of the first conjugation).
