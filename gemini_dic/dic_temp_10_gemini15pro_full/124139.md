mortification: /ˌmɔːrtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ /ˌmɔːrtəfɪˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 羞辱；屈辱；苦修；坏疽 | A feeling of shame or humiliation; the practice of denying oneself pleasures or comforts, especially for religious reasons; the death of body tissue, usually due to infection or lack of blood supply. |  She felt a deep sense of mortification when she tripped and fell in front of everyone. 她当着所有人的面绊倒了，感到非常羞愧。| synonyms: humiliation, shame, embarrassment, chagrin, abasement | antonyms: pride, honor, glory | noun

mortify: /ˈmɔːrtɪfaɪ/ /ˈmɔːrtəˌfaɪ/ | vt. | 使感羞辱；克制；抑制；苦修；使…坏死 | To cause (someone) to feel very embarrassed or ashamed; to subdue (the body or its needs and desires) by self-denial or discipline, often for religious reasons; to cause (part of the body) to undergo necrosis. | Her son's behavior mortified her in front of their guests. 她儿子的行为让她在客人面前感到很丢脸。 | synonyms: humiliate, shame, embarrass, chagrin, subdue, suppress | antonyms:  honor, praise, extol | verb
