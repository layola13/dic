contemplation:/ˌkɒntəmˈpleɪʃn/ (UK), /ˌkɑːntəmˈpleɪʃn/ (US)| n.|沉思，冥想；注视，凝视|The act of thinking deeply about something; prolonged observation.|She sat in quiet contemplation of the sunset. 她静静地坐着凝视着日落。|近义词：meditation, reflection, thought, consideration|反义词：inattention, disregard, negligence|词性：名词

contemplate:/ˈkɒntəmpleɪt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːntəmˌpleɪt/ (US)| v.|沉思，冥想；注视，凝视；打算，计划|To think deeply about something; to look at thoughtfully; to have in mind as a possibility.|He contemplated the meaning of life. 他沉思人生的意义。  I'm contemplating a career change. 我正在考虑换个工作。|近义词：ponder, consider, meditate, mull over|反义词：ignore, disregard, neglect|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin contemplātiō ("act of contemplating"), from Latin contemplātus, past participle of contemplārī ("to contemplate, observe, survey a place"), from con- ("together") + templārī ("to frequent a temple"), from templum ("temple").
