chuffing: /ˈtʃʌfɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈtʃʌfɪŋ/ (US)| adj. | 非常高兴的，非常满意的；恼火的，生气的 | Expressing pleasure, satisfaction, or annoyance. |
例句：
He was absolutely chuffed with his new car.  他對他的新車非常满意。

She was chuffed to bits to win the competition. 她对赢得比赛感到非常高兴。


近义词：pleased, delighted, happy, satisfied; annoyed, irritated, angry |
反义词：displeased, unhappy, dissatisfied; calm, content |
词性：形容词


chuff: /tʃʌf/ (UK) /tʃʌf/ (US)| n. |  噗噗声，喘息声；傻瓜，笨蛋 | A dull, explosive sound, as of an engine exhausting steam; a fool or idiot. |
例句：
The old train chugged along, emitting puffs of chuff. 老火车吭哧吭哧地行驶着，喷出一股股的蒸汽。

He's a bit of a chuff, isn't he?  他有点傻，不是吗？


近义词：(for sound) puff, chug; (for person) idiot, fool |
反义词：(for person) genius, smart person |
词性：名词

chuff: /tʃʌf/ (UK) /tʃʌf/ (US)| v. |  发出噗噗声，喘息；使高兴，使满意 | To make a dull, explosive sound; to please, to satisfy. |
例句：
The engine chuffed as it pulled away from the station.  火车从车站开出时发出噗噗的声音。

His praise really chuffed me.  他的赞扬真的让我很高兴。


近义词：(for sound) puff, chug; (for feeling) please, delight |
反义词：(for feeling) displease, upset |
词性：动词


词源:  The origin of "chuff" is uncertain. One theory connects it to the Old English word *cyff, meaning "tub," possibly referring to the sound of a steam engine. The meaning "pleased" is thought to be a later development, perhaps influenced by the idea of a steam engine puffing contentedly.
