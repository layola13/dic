in-your-face:/ɪn jər ˈfeɪs/| adj.|咄咄逼人的；直截了当的；不顾及他人感受的|Characterized by being bold, assertive, and often confrontational, without regard for others' feelings. It can refer to a style, attitude, or behavior.|Her in-your-face attitude made it difficult for her to make friends. 她咄咄逼人的态度让她很难交到朋友。

His in-your-face style of music is not for everyone. 他直截了当的音乐风格并不适合所有人。|近义词：aggressive, assertive, bold, confrontational, provocative|反义词：subtle, understated, reserved, timid, shy|词性:形容词

Etymology: The phrase likely originated in the late 20th century, possibly from the image of someone aggressively thrusting something into another person's face.  It became popularized in the 1990s, particularly in association with certain music genres and subcultures.
