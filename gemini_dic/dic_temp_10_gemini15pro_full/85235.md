harlot:/ˈhɑːrlət/ | n. |妓女，娼妓 |A woman who engages in sexual intercourse for payment. |例句：The harlot solicited men on the street corner.  那个妓女在街角拉客。

例句：In the novel, the harlot eventually found redemption. 在小说中，那个妓女最终得到了救赎。

近义词：prostitute, whore, hooker |反义词:  (No direct antonym, could consider "chaste woman," "virtuous woman," but these are not perfect opposites.) |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *harlot* "vagabond, rascal, beggar," of uncertain origin. Possibly from Frankish *haral* "fellow, knave" or from a Germanic source related to Old High German *herilot*, meaning "bastard, vagabond." The word's meaning shifted over time to specifically refer to a female prostitute.
