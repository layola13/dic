Etymology: From insidious + -ness.  "Insidious" comes from the Latin "insidiosus," meaning "lying in wait," from "insidiae" ("ambush, treachery"), ultimately from "in-" ("in, on") + "sedere" ("to sit").  The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
