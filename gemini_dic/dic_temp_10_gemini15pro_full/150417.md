pansied:/ˈpæn.zid/ | adj. |  懦弱的，没有勇气的；像三色堇的 | Resembling or suggesting a pansy, especially in having a delicate or effeminate appearance; weak or timid. | He called his opponent a "pansied liberal." 他称他的对手为“懦弱的自由主义者”。|  He was too pansied to stand up to the bully. 他太胆小了，不敢反抗恶霸。 | 近义词: timid, cowardly, effeminate, unmanly | 反义词: brave, courageous, masculine | 词性: 形容词

词源: Derived from "pansy," which itself comes from the French "pensée," meaning "thought," referring to the flower's contemplative appearance.  The use of "pansy" to denote effeminacy or weakness emerged in the early 20th century.
