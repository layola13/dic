bribing:/ˈbraɪbɪŋ/
n.|贿赂; 行贿;|The act of giving or offering a bribe.|He was accused of bribing a government official. 他被指控贿赂政府官员。|近义词：corruption, graft, payola|反义词： honesty, integrity|词性: 名词 (gerund form of the verb "bribe")

bribe:/braɪb/
vt.|贿赂; 向…行贿;|To persuade someone to act in one's favor, typically illegally or dishonestly, by a gift of money or other inducement.|He bribed the guard to let him in. 他贿赂警卫让他进去。|近义词： corrupt, buy off|反义词： refuse a bribe|词性: 动词

词源：From Old French briber "to beg, ask alms, steal," from a Germanic source related to Old English brēowan "to brew," originally "to boil, cook." The sense shifted from "begging" to "offering a small gift" and then to "offering a gift to corrupt."
