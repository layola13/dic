bokeh:/ˈboʊkeɪ/ (US), /ˈbəʊkeɪ/ (UK)| n. |散景，焦外成像|The aesthetic quality of the blur produced in the out-of-focus parts of an image produced by a lens. |The photographer skillfully used bokeh to create a dreamy atmosphere in the portrait. 摄影师巧妙地运用散景，为人像照片营造出梦幻般的氛围。|近义词：blur, out-of-focus blur|反义词:sharpness, focus|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Japanese ぼけ (boke), meaning "blur" or "haze."  The term entered English photographic terminology in the late 1990s.
