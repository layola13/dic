over-filters:/ˈoʊvərˈfɪltərz/| v. |过度过滤|To apply excessive filtering, often in the context of photo editing or information processing, resulting in an unnatural or distorted outcome.|She over-filters her photos so much they look plastic. 她过度美化照片，以至于看起来像塑料做的。|The algorithm over-filters the data, excluding valuable information.  这个算法过度过滤数据，排除了有价值的信息。|over-processes, over-edits|under-filters|verb
over-filtered:/ˈoʊvərˈfɪltərd/| adj. |过度过滤的|Having been subjected to excessive filtering, typically referring to images or data.|The over-filtered photos lacked detail and looked artificial. 这些过度过滤的照片缺乏细节，看起来很假。|The report was based on over-filtered data, leading to inaccurate conclusions.  这份报告基于过度过滤的数据，导致结论不准确。|over-processed, over-edited|natural, unfiltered|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"Over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi) meaning "above, beyond, excessively".
"Filter" (from Old French filtre, from Medieval Latin filtrum, of Germanic origin) meaning "a porous device for removing impurities or unwanted elements".
