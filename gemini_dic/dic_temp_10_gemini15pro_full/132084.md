noncredibleness:/ˌnɒnˌkrɛdəˈbɪlnəs/ | n. | 不可信，不可置信 | The noncredibleness of his story was apparent to everyone. |  His testimony was marked by its noncredibleness. | 近义词:unbelievability, implausibility, improbability | 反义词:credibility, believability, plausibility | 词性:名词

Etymology:  From non- + credible + -ness.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Credible" comes from the Latin "credibilis," meaning "worthy of belief."  "-ness" is a suffix that forms nouns denoting a state or quality.
