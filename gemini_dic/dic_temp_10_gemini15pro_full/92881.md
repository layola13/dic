idealist:/aɪˈdiːəlɪst/ /ɪˈdiːəlɪst/
n.|理想主义者；空想家|A person who is guided more by ideals than by practical considerations.|例句：He's a hopeless idealist, always chasing rainbows. 他是个无可救药的理想主义者，总是在追逐彩虹。

例句：She's an idealist and believes that world peace is possible. 她是一位理想主义者，相信世界和平是可能的。
|近义词：visionary, dreamer, romantic, utopian|反义词：realist, pragmatist, cynic|词性:noun


idealistic:/ˌaɪdiːəˈlɪstɪk/ /ˌɪdiːəˈlɪstɪk/
adj.|理想主义的；空想的|Characterized by idealism; unrealistically aiming for perfection.|例句：She has a very idealistic view of marriage. 她对婚姻抱有非常理想化的看法。

例句：His idealistic plans were never put into practice.  他理想化的计划从未付诸实践。
|近义词：utopian, quixotic, romantic|反义词：realistic, pragmatic, cynical|词性:adjective


ideally:/aɪˈdiːəli/ /ɪˈdiːəli/
adv.|理想地；完美地|In an ideal manner; perfectly.|例句：Ideally, we'd like to find a house with a garden. 理想情况下，我们想找一个带花园的房子。

例句：Ideally, everyone would have access to healthcare. 理想情况下，每个人都应该有机会获得医疗保健。
|近义词：perfectly, optimally|反义词：realistically, practically|词性:adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *idéaliste*, from idéal (“ideal”) + -iste (“-ist”).  The French word idéal comes from Late Latin ideālis, from Latin idea (“idea, form, mental image”), from Ancient Greek ἰδέα (idéa, “form, pattern, idea”), from the verb ἰδεῖν (ideîn, “to see”).
