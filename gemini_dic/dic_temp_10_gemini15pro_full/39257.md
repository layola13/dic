congregationist: /ˌkɑːŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənɪst/ | n. adj. | 公理会教友；公理会的 | A member of a Congregational church, or relating to Congregationalism. |  She's a devout congregationist and attends services every Sunday. 她是一位虔敬的公理会教友，每个星期天都去做礼拜。| The congregationist church is known for its democratic structure. 公理会教堂以其民主的结构而闻名。 | 近义词: Congregational, church member, parishioner | 反义词: None (no direct opposite for a religious affiliation) | 词性: Noun, Adjective

Etymology:
From congregation + -ist.  "Congregation" comes from Late Latin *congregationem (nominative congregatio), from Latin congregare "to flock together," from com- "together" (see com-) + greg-, stem of grex "flock, herd."  The suffix "-ist" denotes a member or adherent of something.
