miscovet: /ˌmɪsˈkʌvɪt/
verb
垂涎；贪图；觊觎
To desire or covet (something, especially something that belongs to another person) wrongly or excessively.
He miscoveted his neighbor's wife. 他垂涎邻居的妻子。
近义词: covet, crave, desire, yearn for
反义词: despise, disdain, abhor
词性: verb

词源:
From mis- + covet.  "Mis-" is a prefix indicating something wrong or bad. "Covet" comes from Old French "coveiter," ultimately from Latin "cupiditas," meaning "desire," related to "cupere" - "to desire."
