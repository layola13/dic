discomposed:/ˌdɪskəmˈpəʊzd/ | /ˌdɪskəmˈpoʊzd/ | adj. |  不安的，扰乱的，心烦意乱的 | Disturbed or unsettled in mind or composure; ruffled. |  The unexpected news left her discomposed.  这突如其来的消息让她心烦意乱。 | Synonyms:  perturbed, ruffled, agitated, disconcerted, flustered | Antonyms:  calm, composed, serene, tranquil | word property: adjective

discomposed:/ˌdɪskəmˈpəʊzd/ | /ˌdɪskəmˈpoʊzd/ | v. | 使不安，扰乱，使心烦意乱  | To disturb or agitate the composure of; ruffle. | The loud noise discomposed the speaker.  巨大的噪音使演讲者心烦意乱。| Synonyms:  unsettle, disturb, agitate, disconcert, fluster | Antonyms:  calm, soothe, compose, tranquilize | word property: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation, reversal) + compose (to make up, constitute, or form; to put together; to arrange in proper or orderly form). Ultimately from Latin componere (to put together).
