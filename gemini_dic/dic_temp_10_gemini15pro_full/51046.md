desolated:/ˈdesəleɪtɪd/| adj.|荒凉的，孤寂的，凄凉的|Made unfit for habitation or use; laid waste. Reduced to a ruinous condition; deserted. Feeling or showing great unhappiness or loneliness.|The earthquake left the city desolated. 地震使这座城市变得荒凉。|近义词：barren, bleak, deserted, forlorn, devastated|反义词：populated, cheerful, lively|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Latin *desolatus*, past participle of *desolare* ("to abandon, forsake, lay waste"), from *de-* ("from, away") + *solus* ("alone").
