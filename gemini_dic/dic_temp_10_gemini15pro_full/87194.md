here-goes:/ˌhɪər ˈɡoʊz/
interjection
开始吧；瞧；开始了
An expression used to indicate that one is about to begin something, often something risky or challenging.
Here goes! Wish me luck! 开始吧！祝我好运！
近义词: here we go, off we go, let's do this
反义词: wait, stop
词性: interjection

词源：
"Here goes" is a shortened form of "here it goes" or "here he/she/it goes."  It originated in the late 19th century.  "Here" indicates the present moment or location, and "goes" signifies the start of an action or event.
