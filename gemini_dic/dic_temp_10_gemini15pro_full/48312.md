decrepitude:/dɪˈkrɛpɪtjuːd/ | n. | 衰老；老朽；破旧 |The decrepitude of the building was evident in its crumbling facade. 这座建筑的破败从它摇摇欲坠的正面就可以看出来。|例句：The decrepitude of the old car made it unreliable. 这辆老爷车的破旧使它变得不可靠。|近义词：decay, deterioration, dilapidation, feebleness, infirmity |反义词：strength, vigor, robustness, soundness |词性：名词

decrepit:/dɪˈkrɛpɪt/ | adj. | 衰老的；破旧的；老朽的 |The decrepit bridge was deemed unsafe. 这座破旧的桥被认为不安全。|例句：The decrepit chair collapsed under his weight.  那把破旧的椅子在他的重压下垮塌了。|近义词：frail, feeble, infirm, dilapidated, ramshackle |反义词：strong, robust, sturdy, sound |词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *decrepitus, from Latin decrepitus (“worn out, enfeebled by age”), past participle of decrepare (“to creak, rattle”), from de- (“down, from”) + crepare (“to crack, creak, rattle”).
