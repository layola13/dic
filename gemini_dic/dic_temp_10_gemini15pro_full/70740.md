fine-down:/faɪnˈdaʊn/ | v. | 精细化，细化，精加工 | To refine or improve something by making small adjustments or additions. |  The sculptor fine-downed the statue's features to achieve a lifelike appearance. 雕塑家细化了雕像的面部特征，使其栩栩如生。

The engineer fine-downed the design of the engine to improve its efficiency. 工程师细化了发动机设计以提高效率。 | 近义词: refine, improve, perfect, polish | 反义词: coarsen, roughen | 词性: 动词

词源:  "Fine" (from Old French *fin, ultimately from Latin finis "end, boundary, limit") meaning of high quality, delicate, or subtle.  "Down" (from Old English dūn "downwards, from a higher to a lower position") suggesting a movement towards greater precision or detail.  The combination implies a process of bringing something to a more refined or precise state.
