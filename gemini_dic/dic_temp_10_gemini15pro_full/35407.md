co-opted:/ˌkoʊˈɑːptɪd/ | adj. | 被吸纳的；被任命的 |  Appointed to a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members.  |  The community leader was co-opted onto the board of directors. 社区领导被吸纳进了董事会。|  The non-voting members were co-opted to the committee to provide expert advice.  非投票成员被任命到委员会提供专家意见。 | 近义词: appointed, elected, chosen, selected | 反义词: rejected, dismissed, excluded | 词性:  形容词

co-opt:/ˌkoʊˈɑːpt/ | vt. | 吸纳；任命；借用；挪用 |  To appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members; to adopt (an idea or practice) for one's own use. | The council co-opted several new members. 理事会吸纳了几名新成员。 |  The advertising campaign co-opted the language of environmentalism. 这场广告活动借用了环保主义的语言。| 近义词: appropriate, borrow, adopt, appoint | 反义词: reject, refuse, decline | 词性: 动词


词源: From Latin "co-optare," from "co-" (together) + "optare" (to choose, desire).
