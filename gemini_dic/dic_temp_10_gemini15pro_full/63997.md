epistolary:/ɪˈpɪstəˌleri/ | adj. | 书信体的，书信形式的 | Relating to or suitable for letters; written in the form of a series of letters. | Example 1:  The novel is written in an epistolary style, using letters and diary entries to tell the story. 这部小说是以书信体写成的，用信件和日记来讲述故事。

Example 2:  His epistolary novel captured the hearts of readers with its intimate portrayal of the characters. 他的书信体小说以其对人物的亲密刻画赢得了读者的心。 | 近义词: letter-based, correspondence-based | 反义词: non-epistolary | 词性: 形容词

epistle:/ɪˈpɪsəl/ | n. | 书信，（尤指正式的或严肃的）信 | A letter, especially a formal or didactic one; a poem or other literary work in the form of a letter. | Example 1:  The apostle Paul wrote epistles to various early Christian communities. 使徒保罗写信给各个早期基督教社区。

Example 2:  She penned a lengthy epistle to her estranged sister, hoping for reconciliation. 她给疏远的妹妹写了一封长信，希望和解。| 近义词: letter, missive, communication | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | 词性: 名词
