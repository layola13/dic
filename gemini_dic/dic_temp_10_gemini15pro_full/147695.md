overpraising: /ˌoʊvərˈpreɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 过度赞扬；过分称赞 | Praising someone or something too much, beyond what is deserved or warranted. |  He was overpraising his son's mediocre achievements. 他过度赞扬了他儿子平庸的成就. | synonyms: exaggerating, gushing, flattering, eulogizing | antonyms: criticizing, belittling, disparaging | verb (present participle)

overpraise: /ˌoʊvərˈpreɪz/ | v. | 过度赞扬；过分称赞 | To praise someone or something too much, beyond what is deserved or warranted. |  She tends to overpraise her employees, which can lead to complacency. 她倾向于过度赞扬她的员工，这会导致自满. | synonyms: exaggerate, gush, flatter, eulogize | antonyms: criticize, belittle, disparage | verb


Etymology: "over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi, meaning "above, over") + "praise" (from Old French preisier, ultimately from Latin pretium "price, value, reward").
