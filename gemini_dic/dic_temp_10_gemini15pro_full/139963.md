odontophora:/ˌoʊdɒnˈtɒfərə/ | n. | 牙形石 |  A small, tooth-shaped fossil of uncertain affinity, abundant in Paleozoic and Triassic marine rocks.  They are now considered to be the remains of an extinct group of jawless vertebrates. | Example: Odontophora are useful index fossils for dating Paleozoic strata. 牙形石是确定古生代地层年代的有用标准化石。 | Example: The discovery of new odontophora fossils has shed light on the evolution of early vertebrates. 新牙形石化石的发现为了解早期脊椎动物的进化提供了线索。 | 近义词: conodont elements (更常用的术语) | 反义词:  无 | noun
