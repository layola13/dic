bagging:/ˈbæɡɪŋ/| n.|装袋；装进袋子里的东西；（非正式）解雇；（非正式）猎获物|The act of putting things into bags, or the items that have been bagged.  It can also informally refer to being fired from a job, or the animals caught while hunting.|例句：The bagging of the groceries took longer than expected.  把食品杂货装袋比预期的要久。

例句：The hunter proudly displayed his bagging. 猎人自豪地展示了他的猎物。|近义词：sacking, packing, dismissing (informal), catch (informal)|反义词:unpacking, hiring (informal), releasing (informal)|词性:noun


bagging:/ˈbæɡɪŋ/| adj.|（统计学，机器学习）装袋法的|Relating to the statistical method of bagging, which involves creating multiple datasets from the original data by random sampling with replacement, and then using these datasets to train multiple models and aggregate their predictions.|例句：Bagging is a powerful ensemble learning technique. 装袋法是一种强大的集成学习技术。

例句：The bagging classifier improved the accuracy of the predictions. 装袋分类器提高了预测的准确性。|近义词：bootstrap aggregating|反义词:N/A|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "bag" + "-ing" suffix, indicating the action or result of the verb. "Bag" itself comes from Old Norse "baggi."
