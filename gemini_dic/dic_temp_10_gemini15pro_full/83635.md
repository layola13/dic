haemoglobinaemia:/ˌhiːməˌɡləʊbɪˈniːmiə/| n. |血红蛋白血症|The presence of hemoglobin in the blood plasma, resulting from the destruction of red blood cells.|The patient presented with haemoglobinaemia after the transfusion reaction.| 近义词: hemoglobinemia | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, consider normal blood condition) | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From Ancient Greek *haîma* (αἷμα) "blood" + *glóbulos* (γλόβυλος) "small round object" + *-īn- (-ίνη, adjectival suffix) + *-aemia* (-αιμία, "blood condition").
