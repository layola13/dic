pandarus:/ˈpændərəs/| n.|潘达鲁斯，皮条客，拉皮条的人|A go-between in love affairs, especially one who facilitates an illicit or clandestine relationship; a pimp.  Origin: From the character Pandarus in Chaucer's *Troilus and Criseyde* and Shakespeare's *Troilus and Cressida*, who acts as a go-between for the lovers.|Example: He played the pandarus, introducing the wealthy businessman to a young aspiring actress. 他扮演了皮条客的角色，把这位富商介绍给了一位年轻的有抱负的女演员。|Example:  Modern usage often sees "pandarus" used metaphorically, referring to someone who encourages or facilitates something harmful or immoral.  现代用法经常将“pandarus”用于比喻，指鼓励或促成有害或不道德事情的人。|近义词：pimp, procurer, go-between, intermediary|反义词:obstacle, impediment|词性:noun
