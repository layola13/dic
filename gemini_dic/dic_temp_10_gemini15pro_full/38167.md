compromisers:/ˈkɒmprəˌmaɪzəz/ (US) /ˈkɑːmprəˌmaɪzərz/
n.
妥协者，折中者
People who are willing to compromise to reach an agreement or solution.
The compromisers in the group helped to find a solution that everyone could accept.  小组中的妥协者帮助找到了一个每个人都能接受的解决方案。
synonyms: mediators, negotiators, conciliators
antonyms: hardliners, diehards, intransigents
noun


compromise:/ˈkɒmprəˌmaɪz/ (US) /ˈkɑːmprəˌmaɪz/
v.
妥协，折衷
To settle a dispute by mutual concession.
They compromised by agreeing to meet halfway. 他们妥协了，同意各退一步。
synonyms: concede, negotiate, bargain
antonyms: resist, oppose, stand firm
verb


compromise:/ˈkɒmprəˌmaɪz/ (US) /ˈkɑːmprəˌmaɪz/
n.
妥协，折衷
An agreement or a settlement of a dispute that is reached by each side making concessions.
The compromise involved both parties giving up something they wanted.  这个妥协涉及双方都放弃了一些他们想要的东西。
synonyms: agreement, settlement, bargain
antonyms: disagreement, deadlock, impasse
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin compromissum, past participle of compromittere "to promise mutually," from com- "together" + promittere "to promise."
