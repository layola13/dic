gothics:/ˈɡɒθɪks/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːθɪks/ (US)| n. adj.|哥特式风格（的）；哥特体（的）；哥特式小说（的）；哥特人（的）| Relating to the Goths, their language, or their culture; relating to a style of architecture prevalent in western Europe from the 12th to the 16th centuries, characterized by pointed arches, rib vaults, and flying buttresses; relating to a style of literature characterized by a gloomy or grotesque atmosphere and elements of horror and the supernatural.| The cathedral is a masterpiece of Gothic architecture. 这座大教堂是哥特式建筑的杰作。 | Synonyms:  dark, gloomy, medieval, macabre | Antonyms: light, bright, cheerful, modern | noun, adjective
