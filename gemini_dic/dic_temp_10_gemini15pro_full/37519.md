common market:/ˈkɒmən ˈmɑːkɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmən ˈmɑːrkɪt/ (US)| n. |共同市场|A group of countries that have agreed to trade freely with each other, often with a common external tariff.|The European Economic Community was a common market.|Synonyms: trading bloc, customs union, free trade area|Antonyms: closed market, protected market|noun

common markets:/ˈkɒmən ˈmɑːkɪts/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmən ˈmɑːrkɪts/ (US)| n. |共同市场（复数）|Multiple groups of countries that have agreed to trade freely with each other, often with a common external tariff.|Several common markets emerged in different regions of the world during the 20th century.|Synonyms: trading blocs, customs unions, free trade areas|Antonyms: closed markets, protected markets|noun
