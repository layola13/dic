counter-balance:/ˈkaʊntərˌbæləns/ /ˈkaʊntərˌbæləns/ | v. | 平衡，抵消；抗衡 | To offset or compensate for something. | The government's spending on education is counter-balanced by cuts in other areas. 政府在教育上的支出被其他领域的削减所抵消。 | synonyms: offset, compensate, counterpoise, counteract | antonyms:  intensify, aggravate, exacerbate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + balance.  "Counter-" comes from the Old French "contre-" meaning "against". "Balance" comes from Old French "balance" (scales), from Late Latin "bilanx" (having two scales), from Latin "bis" (twice) + "lanx" (plate, dish, scale pan).
