fuddy-duddy:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi/| n. |老顽固，古板守旧的人|A person who is old-fashioned and fussy, and disapproves of anything new or different.|例句：My grandfather is a bit of a fuddy-duddy and doesn't approve of my tattoos. 我的祖父有点老顽固，不赞成我的纹身。

例句：The fuddy-duddies at the club refused to allow women to join.  俱乐部里的那些老顽固拒绝允许女性加入。|近义词：conservative, traditionalist, stick-in-the-mud, old-fashioned, diehard|反义词:modern, progressive, liberal, innovator, radical|词性:名词

fuddy-duddy:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi/| adj. |老顽固的，古板的|Old-fashioned and fussy; disapproving of anything new or different.|例句：He has very fuddy-duddy ideas about how children should be raised. 他对如何抚养孩子有非常老套的想法。

例句：She thought the new art exhibit was too modern and a bit fuddy-duddy. 她认为新的艺术展览太现代了，有点古板。|近义词：conservative, traditionalist, stick-in-the-mud, old-fashioned, diehard|反义词:modern, progressive, liberal, innovator, radical|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
Originated in the early 20th century, possibly as a reduplication of "fuddy," which is of unknown origin but may be related to "fuss" or "fidget." The repetition emphasizes the sense of fussiness and disapproval.
