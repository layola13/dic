concretization:/kɒŋˌkriːtaɪˈzeɪʃn/ /kɑːŋˌkriːtɪˈzeɪʃn/| n.|具体化，实体化|The concretization of his ideas into a working model took several months.  他将他的想法具体化成一个工作模型花了几个月的时间。|The concretization of the abstract concept of love can be seen in acts of kindness.  抽象的爱之概念的具体化可以从善举中体现出来。|近义词：realization, materialization, embodiment|反义词：abstraction, generalization|词性: 名词

concretise:/ˈkɒŋkriːtaɪz/ /ˈkɑːŋkriːtaɪz/| vt.|使具体化，使实体化|She concretised her plans by drawing up a schedule. 她通过制定时间表使她的计划具体化。|The artist concretised his vision in a stunning sculpture.  这位艺术家将他的愿景具体化在一座令人惊叹的雕塑中。|近义词：realize, materialize, embody|反义词：abstract, generalize|词性: 动词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *concrētiō, -ōnis, from Latin concrētus (past participle of concrēscō (“grow together, solidify”)), from con- (“together”) + crēscō (“grow”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱer- (“to grow”).
