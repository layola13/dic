emancipator: /ɪˈmænsɪpeɪtər/ | n. | 解放者 | A person who frees someone from slavery or oppression. |  Abraham Lincoln is considered an emancipator of the slaves. 亚伯拉罕·林肯被认为是奴隶的解放者。

| Example: Nelson Mandela was an emancipator of his people.  纳尔逊·曼德拉是他的人民的解放者。 | Synonym: liberator, freer, deliverer | Antonym: oppressor, enslaver | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Late Latin *emancipator, from Latin emancipare (“to set free from paternal authority; to transfer; to sell”), from ex (“out of, from”) + mancipare (“to transfer ownership”), from manus (“hand”) + capere (“to take”).
