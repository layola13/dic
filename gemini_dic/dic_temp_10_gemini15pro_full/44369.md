cross-correlation: /ˌkrɒs kɒrəˈleɪʃən/ (UK) /ˌkrɔːs kɔːrəˈleɪʃən/ (US)| n. |互相关，交叉相关| A measure of similarity between two signals as a function of the time lag applied to one of them. It is used to find occurrences of a known signal within a noisy one.| The cross-correlation between the two signals showed a strong peak, indicating a high degree of similarity.|Synonyms: cross-covariance | Antonyms: N/A | Noun
