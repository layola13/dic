discern:/dɪˈsɜːrn/ (US), /dɪˈsɜːn/ (UK)| v. |辨别，识别；察觉，发觉|To perceive or recognize something, often with difficulty.  To distinguish something from something else.|例句：I could discern a faint light in the distance. 我能辨认出远处微弱的光。|例句：It was hard to discern his true motives. 很难看出他真正的动机。|近义词：perceive, detect, distinguish, recognize|反义词：overlook, miss, ignore|词性:动词

discernible:/dɪˈsɜːnəbl/ (US), /dɪˈsɜːnəbl/ (UK)| adj. |可辨别的，可识别的|Capable of being discerned; perceptible.|例句：There was a discernible improvement in her health. 她的健康状况有了明显的改善。|例句：The differences between the two versions are barely discernible. 两个版本之间的差异几乎难以察觉。|近义词：perceptible, noticeable, detectable, apparent|反义词：imperceptible, unnoticeable, invisible|词性:形容词

discernment:/dɪˈsɜːnmənt/ (US), /dɪˈsɜːnmənt/ (UK)| n. |辨别力，识别力；洞察力|The ability to judge well; insight; acuteness of judgment and understanding.|例句：He showed great discernment in choosing his friends. 他在选择朋友方面表现出极佳的洞察力。|例句：She possesses remarkable discernment in matters of art. 她在艺术方面具有非凡的鉴赏力。|近义词：judgment, insight, perception, wisdom|反义词：naiveté, ignorance, blindness|词性:名词

词源:  From Late Latin *discernere, from Latin discernere ("to separate, divide, distinguish, decide"), from dis- ("apart") + cernere ("to sift, separate, distinguish, perceive").
