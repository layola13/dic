nonconvertibility:/ˌnɑːnkənˌvɜːrtəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 不可兑换性 | The inability to exchange one currency for another, or one asset for another, usually due to government regulations or market restrictions. | The nonconvertibility of the currency made it difficult for businesses to operate. 该货币的不可兑换性使企业难以经营。| Synonyms: inconvertibility, non-exchangeability | Antonyms: convertibility, exchangeability | noun


Etymology:  From "non-" (not) + "convertible" (able to be exchanged) + "-ity" (forming a noun denoting a state or quality).  Ultimately derived from Latin "convertere" (to turn around).
