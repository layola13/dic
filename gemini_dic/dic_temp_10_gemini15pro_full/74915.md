fretful:/ˈfrɛtfəl/ | adj. | 烦躁的，焦躁的 | Feeling or expressing distress or irritation.  Characterized by excessive worrying and complaining. | The baby was fretful because it was teething.  婴儿因为正在长牙而烦躁不安。| The fretful child kept whining and complaining. 这个焦躁的孩子不停地哭闹抱怨。 | irritable, peevish, fractious, restless, agitated | calm, peaceful, contented, serene | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fretful, from Old English *fretan (to gnaw, devour) + -ful.  The original sense was "gnawed by anxiety," reflecting the physical sensations associated with worry.
