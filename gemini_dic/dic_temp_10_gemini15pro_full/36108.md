coerce:/koʊˈɜːrs/ (US), /kəʊˈɜːs/ (UK)| v. |强迫，胁迫|To persuade someone forcefully to do something that they are unwilling to do.|The government coerced him into signing the confession. 政府胁迫他签署了供认书.|Synonyms: force, compel, pressure, intimidate, bully|Antonyms: persuade, encourage, allow, permit|verb

coercer:/koʊˈɜːrsər/ (US), /kəʊˈɜːsər/ (UK)| n. |强迫者，胁迫者|A person who coerces someone.|The coercer threatened to reveal his secrets if he didn't comply.  胁迫者威胁说，如果他不服从，就揭露他的秘密。|Synonyms: bully, intimidator, oppressor|Antonyms: helper, supporter, encourager|noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *coerciō, *coerciōnis "constraint," from Latin coercere "to restrain, confine, enclose, repress," from com- "together" + arcēre "to keep off, ward off, prevent."
