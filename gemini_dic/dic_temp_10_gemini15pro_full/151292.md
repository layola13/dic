parhelion:/ˌpɑːrˈhiːliən/ |n.|幻日,假日|A bright spot of light that sometimes appears on either side of the sun, caused by the refraction of sunlight by ice crystals in the atmosphere.|I saw a parhelion while driving this morning. 我今天早上开车时看到了一个幻日。|Synonyms: sundog, mock sun|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *parēlion*, from Ancient Greek παρήλιον (parḗlion, “beside the sun”), from παρά (pará, “beside”) + ἥλιος (hḗlios, “sun”).
