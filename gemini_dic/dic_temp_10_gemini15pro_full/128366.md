neutrophil: /ˈnjuːtrəfɪl/ (US), /ˈnjuːtrəfɪl/ (UK)| n. |嗜中性粒细胞|A type of white blood cell that is an important part of the immune system and helps the body fight infection by ingesting microorganisms and releasing enzymes that kill them.  It is characterized by granules that stain readily with neutral dyes.|Neutrophils are the most abundant type of white blood cell in the body and play a crucial role in fighting bacterial infections.|近义词: polymorphonuclear leukocyte (PMN), granulocyte |反义词:  (No direct antonym, but a deficiency is called neutropenia) |词性:noun


neutrophil: /ˈnjuːtrəfɪl/ (US), /ˈnjuːtrəfɪl/ (UK)| adj. |嗜中性的| Relating to or staining readily with neutral dyes.  (This usage is less common than the noun form.) | The neutrophil granules were clearly visible under the microscope. |近义词:  N/A (as adjective) |反义词:  N/A (as adjective) |词性:adjective
