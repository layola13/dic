catalyst:/ˈkætəlɪst/| n.|催化剂；促进因素|A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change. A person or thing that precipitates an event.|The president's speech acted as a catalyst for change. 总统的演讲促成了变革。|synonyms: stimulus, impetus, trigger|antonyms: inhibitor, deterrent|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *katalysis* "dissolution," from *kataluein* "to dissolve," from *kata-* "down" + *luein* "to loosen."
