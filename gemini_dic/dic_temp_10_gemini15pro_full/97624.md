inhauler:/ˈɪnˌhɔːlər/| n.|拉入器；内拉索；内拉绳|A rope or device used to haul something inwards, especially a sail on a boat.|The sailor used the inhauler to trim the spinnaker.|Synonyms: inhaul, sheet (in sailing context)|Antonyms: outhauler|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From 'in' + 'hauler',  'hauler' derived from the verb 'haul' meaning to pull or drag with force.  'Haul' comes from the Old Norse word 'hala' meaning "to pull, drag."
