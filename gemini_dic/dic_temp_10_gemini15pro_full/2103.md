adamine: /ˈædəmiːn/
noun
アダミン (日语),  adamite (英语)
A rare mineral consisting of a basic zinc arsenate, occurring as glassy yellow, violet, or pink crystals. It is named after French mineralogist Gilbert-Joseph Adam (1795-1881).
Adamite is often found in oxidized zinc deposits and is popular among mineral collectors for its vibrant colors and distinct crystal formations.  Some specimens of adamine exhibit fluorescence under UV light.
Adamine crystals can be found in Mapimí, Durango, Mexico.
