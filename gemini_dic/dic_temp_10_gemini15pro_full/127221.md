nauplius: /ˈnɔːpliəs/ | n. | 无节幼体 |  The free-swimming larva of certain crustaceans, such as barnacles and copepods, having an unsegmented body and three pairs of appendages.  It is the earliest larval stage and undergoes several molts before reaching adulthood. | The nauplius larva is characteristic of many crustaceans.  无节幼体是许多甲壳类动物的特征。

The pond water teemed with nauplii.  池塘里充满了无节幼体。| 近义词: larva | 反义词: adult | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Modern Latin *nauplius, from Ancient Greek ναύπλιος (naúplios, “kind of shellfish, supposed to sail in its shell”), from ναῦς (naûs, “ship”) + πλέω (pléō, “to sail”).
