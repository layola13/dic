noncombatant:/ˌnɒnˈkɒmbətənt/ /ˌnɑːnˈkɑːmbætənt/| n. adj.|非战斗人员，非战斗性的|A person who is not involved in fighting during a war, such as a civilian or a medical worker.  Also describes something that is not related to fighting.|例句：The hospital was full of noncombatants injured in the crossfire. 医院里挤满了在交火中受伤的非战斗人员。|近义词：civilian, bystander, neutral|反义词：combatant, soldier, belligerent|词性:名词/形容词
noncombatant:| adj.|非战斗性的|Not involved in fighting during a war. |例句：The Red Cross provides noncombatant aid in war zones. 红十字会在战区提供非战斗性援助。|近义词：peaceful, nonviolent, unarmed|反义词：combatant, military, warring|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + combatant.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "combatant" comes from the Old French "combatant," present participle of "combattre" meaning "to fight." Ultimately derived from the Latin "combattere," meaning "to fight against."
