ingurgitation:/ɪnˌɡɜːrdʒɪˈteɪʃən/
|n.|吞咽；狼吞虎咽；暴饮暴食|The act of swallowing greedily or excessively; the state of being gorged or overfilled.|He felt ill after the ingurgitation of a large meal. 他在狼吞虎咽地吃完一顿大餐后感到不舒服。|近义词：gorging, guzzling, devouring|反义词: regurgitation, vomiting|词性: noun

ingurgitate:/ɪnˈɡɜːrdʒɪteɪt/
|v.|吞咽；狼吞虎咽；暴饮暴食|To swallow greedily or excessively; to gorge.|The python ingurgitated the whole rabbit. 蟒蛇吞下了整只兔子。|近义词：gorge, guzzle, devour|反义词: regurgitate, vomit|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin ingurgitātiōn- (stem of ingurgitātiō), from Latin ingurgitāt(us), past participle of ingurgitāre ‘to engulf, swallow down,’ from in- ‘in, into’ + gurgitāre ‘to engulf’ (from gurges, gurgit- ‘whirlpool’).
