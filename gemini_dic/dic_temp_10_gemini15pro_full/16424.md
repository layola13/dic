bardship: /ˈbɑːrdʃɪp/ | n. | 诗人身份；诗人的职业；诗人的生活 | The condition or profession of being a bard; the life of a poet, especially its hardships and difficulties. |  He bore the bardship with grace, never complaining about the lack of recognition. 他优雅地承受着诗人的艰辛，从不抱怨缺乏认可。

|  Bardship in the Romantic era was often romanticized, despite the poverty many poets faced. 浪漫主义时代的诗人生活常常被浪漫化，尽管许多诗人面临贫困。 | 近义词：poet's life, poetic career | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, potentially "prose writer's life" but not commonly used) | 词性: noun
