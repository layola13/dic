modulation:/ˌmɑːdʒəˈleɪʃən/ | /ˌmɒdʒəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 调制；调节；变调 | the act of modulating; a change in the sound of something, especially a person's voice | The violinist impressed the audience with his subtle modulations of tone. 小提琴家用他微妙的音调变化给观众留下了深刻的印象。 | synonyms: adjustment, variation, modification | antonyms: uniformity, constancy | noun

modulation:/ˌmɑːdʒəˈleɪʃən/ | /ˌmɒdʒəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 调制 | the process of varying the characteristics of a carrier wave, such as its amplitude or frequency, in order to transmit information |  Frequency modulation is used in FM radio broadcasting. 调频广播使用频率调制。 | synonyms: conversion, transformation | antonyms: demodulation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *modulatio, from Latin modulatus, past participle of modulari (“to regulate, measure rhythmically, tune”), from modulus (“small measure, standard”).
