cuckooflower:/ˈkʊkuːˌflaʊər/| n. |布谷鸟花，杜鹃花，十字花科的一种植物|A flowering plant of the mustard family, Cardamine pratensis, with pale lilac or white flowers. It is found in damp meadows and blooms in spring, around the time the cuckoo first calls.|The cuckooflower is blooming in the meadow. 布谷鸟花在草地上盛开。|Synonyms: lady's-smock, milkmaids|Antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Old English "cucuflōr," where "cucu" refers to the cuckoo bird and "flōr" means flower.  The name likely comes from the fact that the plant blooms around the same time the cuckoo bird first calls in spring.
