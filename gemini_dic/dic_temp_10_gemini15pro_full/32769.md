chock-full:/ˈtʃɒkˈfʊl/ (US) /ˈtʃɒkˈfʊl/ (UK)| adj.|充满的，塞满的|Completely full; crammed.|The pantry was chock-full of canned goods. 食品储藏室里塞满了罐头食品。|
The train was chock-full of commuters. 火车里挤满了通勤者。

|Synonyms: brimming, crammed, overflowing, packed, replete|Antonyms: empty, vacant|adjective|

词源:
"Chock" comes from the Middle English "chokken," meaning "to choke or block."  "Full" has its origins in Old English "full."  The combined term "chock-full" emphasizes the fullness to the point of being blocked or choked with contents.
