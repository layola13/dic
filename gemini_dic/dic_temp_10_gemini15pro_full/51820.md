dewfall:/ˈdjuːfɔːl/| n.|露水；露水的降落|Moisture condensed from the atmosphere, especially at night, and deposited in the form of small drops upon any cool surface.|The heavy dewfall made the grass sparkle in the morning sun. 晨露使草地在早晨的阳光下闪闪发光。|Synonyms: dew, condensation|Antonyms: evaporation, dryness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *deufal, from dew + fall. Equivalent to dew + fall.
