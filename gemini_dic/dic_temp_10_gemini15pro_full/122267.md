mitigated:/ˈmɪtɪɡeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 减轻的，缓和的 | lessened or reduced in severity, seriousness, or painfulness | The new medicine mitigated the patient's suffering.  这种新药减轻了病人的痛苦。| synonyms: alleviated, lessened, reduced, eased, diminished | antonyms: aggravated, worsened, exacerbated, intensified | adjective

mitigate:/ˈmɪtɪɡeɪt/ | v. | 减轻，缓和 | make (something bad) less severe, serious, or painful |  The government took measures to mitigate the effects of the recession. 政府采取措施减轻经济衰退的影响。| synonyms: alleviate, lessen, reduce, ease, diminish | antonyms: aggravate, worsen, exacerbate, intensify | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin mitigatus, past participle of mitigare "to soften, make mild," from mitis "mild, soft" + -igare (frequentative suffix related to agere "to do, drive, lead").
