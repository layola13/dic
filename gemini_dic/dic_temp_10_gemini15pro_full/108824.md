leaven: /ˈlɛvən/ (US), /ˈlɛvən/ (UK)| n. vt.| 酵母，发酵剂；影响，激励；使发酵，使变化|Something, typically yeast, that is added to dough to make it rise.  Also, an element, influence, or agent that produces fermentation or pervasive change. |例句：The baker added leaven to the dough. 面包师在面团里加了酵母。

leaven: /ˈlɛvən/ (US), /ˈlɛvən/ (UK)| vt.| 使发酵，使变化|To cause (dough or bread) to ferment and rise by adding leaven. Also, to permeate and transform by a leavening element. |例句：She leavened the heavy dough with yeast.  她用酵母使厚重的面团发酵。

例句：Humor leavens her serious novels.  幽默使她严肃的小说变得轻松活泼。|近义词：ferment, raise, transform, inspire|反义词：suppress, hinder|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *leven*, from Old English *lēafen*, from Proto-Germanic *laubiz*, from Proto-Indo-European *leb- (“to stick, adhere”). Cognate with Old Norse *leyfr* (“yeast”), Old Frisian *lēven*, Old High German *leibīn* (“loaf”), German *Laib* (“loaf”), Gothic *𐌷𐌻𐌰𐌹𐍆𐍃* (hlaifs, “loaf”).
