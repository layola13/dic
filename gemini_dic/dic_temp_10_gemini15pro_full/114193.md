maiden:/ˈmeɪdn/| n. adj.|少女，处女；未婚女子；初次的，首航的|A young unmarried woman; being or involving the first attempt or act of its kind.|例句：The ship is on her maiden voyage. 这艘船是首航。|近义词：girl, virgin, inaugural, first|反义词: married woman, experienced|词性:名词，形容词

maiden:/ˈmeɪdn/| adj.|初次的，首航的|Being or involving the first attempt or act of its kind.|例句：His maiden speech in Parliament was a resounding success. 他在议会的首次演讲取得了巨大的成功。|近义词：inaugural, first|反义词: experienced|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mægden (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *magwizōn (“girl, virgin”), from Proto-Indo-European *maghu- (“child, young person”). Cognate with Old Frisian maide, Old Saxon magath, Old High German magad (German Magd), Old Norse mey (Icelandic mey), Gothic 𐌼𐌰𐌲𐌰𐌸𐍃 (magaþs). More at may.
