downshifter:/ˈdaʊnˌʃɪftər/| n.|降档者；放慢生活节奏的人|A person who chooses to leave a demanding career or lifestyle for a less pressured and less highly paid but more fulfilling one.|例句：He became a downshifter, leaving his high-powered job in the city for a simpler life in the countryside. 他成为了一个降档者，离开了他在城市的高压工作，去乡下过更简单的生活。

例句：Many downshifters find greater satisfaction in their work and personal lives. 许多降档者在工作和个人生活中找到了更大的满足感。|近义词：minimalist, simple liver|反义词：careerist, workaholic|词性:noun

downshift:/ˈdaʊnˌʃɪft/| v.|放慢生活节奏；降低档位|To change to a lower gear in a vehicle; to adopt a simpler, less stressful lifestyle.|例句：She decided to downshift and move to a smaller town. 她决定放慢生活节奏，搬到一个小镇去。

例句：He downshifted his career to spend more time with his family. 他降低了职业要求，以便花更多时间陪伴家人。|近义词：simplify, decelerate|反义词：accelerate, escalate|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "down" + "shift,"  referring to changing to a lower gear, later applied metaphorically to simplifying one's lifestyle. "Down" implies a reduction or decrease, and "shift" indicates a change.
