fidgeters:/ˈfɪdʒɪtərz/| n. |指尖陀螺玩家；坐立不安的人；烦躁不安的人|People who fidget, often habitually or nervously.  Those who have trouble staying still or quiet, especially in situations where it's expected. |The teacher had trouble keeping the fidgeters focused during the long lecture. 老师很难让这些坐立不安的学生在冗长的讲座中集中注意力。|近义词：restless people, squirmy people,  fussers|反义词:calm people, composed people|词性:Noun (plural)

fidgety:/ˈfɪdʒɪti/| adj. |烦躁的；不安的；坐立不安的|Restless and fidgeting; unable to stay still. Characterized by nervous or restless movements.|The child was fidgety and couldn't sit still during dinner.  这个孩子很烦躁，晚饭时坐不住。|近义词：restless, antsy, squirmy|反义词:calm, still, patient|词性:Adjective

fidget:/ˈfɪdʒɪt/| n. vi. |烦躁；坐立不安|A quick, small movement, typically a repeated one, caused by nervousness or impatience. To make small, restless movements, especially of the hands and feet.|Her constant fidgeting with her pen annoyed me. 她不停地摆弄钢笔让我很烦。  He fidgeted nervously in his chair. 他在椅子上紧张地坐立不安。|近义词：fuss, twitch, wiggle|反义词:stillness, calmness|词性:Noun, Verb
