Dejima:/ˈdɛʒɪmə/ | n. | 出岛；[地名] 出岛 | A small artificial island, formerly in Nagasaki harbor, Japan, and for two centuries (1641–1859) the single port in Japan open to the Dutch and, through them, to the West. |  The Dutch traders were confined to Dejima, a small, fan-shaped artificial island in Nagasaki Bay. 荷兰商人被限制在长崎湾一个小的扇形人工岛出岛上。| Synonym: islet, island, trading post | Antonym: mainland, continent | Noun
