gairfowl:/ˈɡɛːrfɔːl/| n.|大海雀|A large, flightless North Atlantic seabird that became extinct in the mid-19th century.  It was hunted to extinction for its meat, eggs, and feathers.|The last known pair of gairfowl were killed in Iceland in 1844.  最后 diketahui的一对大海雀于1844年在冰岛被杀死。|近义词:great auk, penguin (in older usage)|反义词:N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Scots *gairfowll, from Old Norse *geirfugl, from geirr "spear, wedge" (referring to the bird's beak) + fugl "bird".
