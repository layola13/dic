cave-in:/ˈkeɪvɪn/ (US), /ˈkeɪvɪn/ (UK)| n. vi.|坍塌，塌方；屈服，让步|A cave-in is the sudden collapse of a roof or underground structure. It can also refer to yielding to pressure or demands.|The miners were trapped after the cave-in. 矿工们在塌方后被困住了。|
The government finally caved in to public pressure. 政府最终屈服于公众压力。|近义词：collapse, crumble, surrender, yield|反义词: resist, withstand|名词, 动词
