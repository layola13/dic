all-for-the-best:/ˌɔːl fɔːr ðə ˈbest/
adjective
一切都会好起来的；终究是好事
Used to say that although a situation seems bad or unpleasant, it is probably the best thing that could have happened.
例句：It's sad that he lost his job, but maybe it's all for the best. 他失去工作很令人难过，但也许这是最好的结果。

例句：Even though it was hard at the time, breaking up with her turned out to be all for the best. 尽管当时很艰难，但和她分手结果证明是最好的。
近义词：beneficial, advantageous, fortunate
反义词：disastrous, unfortunate, unfavorable
词性: adjective

词源: This phrase is idiomatic and doesn't have a clear etymological breakdown.  It likely evolved from the general concept of optimism and the belief that things will work out in the end.
