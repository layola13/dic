ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *betera (unattested), comparative of *bōt (“remedy, improvement, profit”), from Proto-Germanic *bōtiz (“advantage, improvement”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰed- (“good”). Cognate with Old Frisian betera, Old Saxon betero, Old Dutch betera, Old High German bezziro, Old Norse betri, Gothic 𐌱𐌰𐍄𐌹𐌶𐌰 (batiza).
