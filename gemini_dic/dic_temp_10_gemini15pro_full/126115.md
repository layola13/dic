mutedly:/ˈmjuːtɪdli/ | adv. | 轻声地；沉闷地；柔和地 | In a quiet and subdued manner, often indicating restraint or suppression of feeling. |  She spoke mutedly, her voice barely above a whisper. 她轻声说话，声音几乎听不见。| Synonyms: softly, quietly, subduedly, faintly, low, hushedly | Antonyms: loudly, boisterously, clamorously | 词性: 副词

mute:/mjuːt/ | adj. v. n. | 沉默的；无声的；减弱…的声音 |
1. adj. Silent; refraining from speech or utterance.
2. v. To reduce the sound of; to soften or silence.
3. n. A device used to soften or muffle the sound of a musical instrument.
词源: From Old French *mut (Modern French muet), from Latin mutus "silent, dumb," from PIE root *meu- "to bind, to tie."  The original sense likely referred to being "tongue-tied."
