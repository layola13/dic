ballistically:/bəˈlɪstɪkli/| adv.|弹道式地，惯性地|Relating to the flight of projectiles, especially bullets, shells, or missiles; propelled by the force of an explosion or other rapid release of energy. It can also be used figuratively to describe something moving very fast or forcefully.|The bullet travelled ballistically for 500 meters. 子弹弹道式地飞行了500米。|The car sped ballistically down the highway. 汽车飞快地沿着高速公路疾驰。|近义词：projectively, forcefully, rapidly, swiftly|反义词：slowly, gradually|词性：副词

Etymology:
From "ballistic" + "-ally." "Ballistic" comes from Greek "ballein" (to throw) via Late Latin "ballista" (a type of catapult).
