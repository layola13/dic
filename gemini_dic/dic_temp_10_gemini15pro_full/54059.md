disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/ /dɪsˈdeɪn/| v. n.|鄙视，蔑视|To regard or treat with contempt; consider beneath oneself.|She disdained to answer his rude question. 她不屑于回答他粗鲁的问题.|Synonyms: scorn, despise, contemn|Antonyms: admire, respect, esteem|verb

disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/ /dɪsˈdeɪn/| n.|鄙视，蔑视|A feeling of contempt for something or someone regarded as unworthy or inferior.|He looked at the cheap jewelry with disdain. 他轻蔑地看着那些廉价珠宝。|Synonyms: scorn, contempt, derision|Antonyms: admiration, respect, esteem|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desdeigner*, from *des-* (expressing negation) + *deigner* "to deign, condescend," from Latin *dignari* "to consider worthy," from *dignus* "worthy."
