contrariously:/kənˈtrɛəriəsli/ | adv. | 矛盾地，相反地，反常地 | In a contrary manner; in opposition; perversely.  | He contrariously decided to go left when everyone else went right. 他反常地决定往左走，而其他人则往右走。 |  He contrariously refused the offer despite its obvious benefits. 尽管这份工作好处明显，他还是执拗地拒绝了。| 近义词: perversely, oppositely, contradictorily | 反义词: agreeably, compliantly, obediently | 词性: 副词

contrary:/ˈkɒntrəri/ | adj. n. | 相反的，对立的；相反的事物 | Opposite in nature, direction, or tendency.  A fact or condition incompatible with another. | The car was travelling in the contrary direction. 汽车行驶方向相反。|  The contrary view is that he was innocent. 相反的观点是他无罪的。| 近义词: opposite, opposing, conflicting | 反义词: similar, alike, same | 词性: 形容词，名词


词源:
From Middle English contrarie, from Old French contraire, from Latin contrārius (“opposite, opposed”), from contrā (“against, opposite”).
