ballyhoo:/ˈbælihuː/| n. v. |喧闹，大肆宣传；大肆宣传|A noisy or elaborate attempt to attract attention to something, especially to promote or sell it. |The circus came to town with lots of ballyhoo. 马戏团带着大量的宣传来到城镇。|The company launched a new product with much ballyhoo.  公司大肆宣传推出了一款新产品。|近义词：hype, publicity, promotion, fanfare|反义词: understatement, silence|词性:名词,动词
ETYMOLOGY->
From earlier ballyhoo (1861) "a low, noisy entertainment; a racket," American English, of uncertain origin. Perhaps from the Irish exclamation "bailligh hoo," meaning "Be quiet!" said ironically, or a similar Irish phrase. The verb is from 1889.
