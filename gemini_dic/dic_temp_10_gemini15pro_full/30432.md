centralized:/ˈsɛntrəlaɪzd/ | adj. |集中的，中央集权的 |Brought together into a single administrative unit or location.  |
The company centralized its operations in London.  该公司将其业务集中在伦敦。 |
The government implemented a centralized system of education. 政府实施了中央集权的教育体系。|
近义词: consolidated, unified, concentrated |
反义词: decentralized, dispersed, distributed |
词性: adjective

centralize:/ˈsɛntrəlaɪz/ | v. | 集中，中央集权 |To bring together into a single administrative unit or location. |
The company decided to centralize its marketing efforts. 该公司决定将其营销工作集中起来。 |
The new CEO plans to centralize control of the company. 新任首席执行官计划集中控制公司。 |
近义词: consolidate, unify, concentrate |
反义词: decentralize, disperse, distribute |
词性: verb

centralization:/ˌsɛntrələˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 集中化，中央集权 |The act or process of centralizing. |
The centralization of power led to increased efficiency. 权力集中化提高了效率。 |
The centralization of resources can sometimes hinder innovation. 资源集中化有时会阻碍创新。 |
近义词: consolidation, unification, concentration |
反义词: decentralization, dispersion, distribution |
词性: noun

词源: From the French *centraliser*, derived from *central* (meaning "central") + *-iser* (a suffix forming verbs). Ultimately from Latin *centralis*, from *centrum* ("center").
