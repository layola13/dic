misinformation:/ˌmɪsɪnfərˈmeɪʃn/ | n. | 误报，错误信息 | False or inaccurate information, especially that which is deliberately intended to deceive. |例句：The spread of misinformation online can have serious consequences. 网上错误信息的传播可能会造成严重后果。

例句：The government was accused of spreading misinformation about the war. 政府被指控散布关于战争的错误信息。| 近义词：disinformation, falsehood, untruth, lie | 反义词：truth, accuracy, fact | 词性: 名词


Etymology:
From mis- (meaning "wrong," "bad," or "incorrect") + information.  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old French "mes-" from a Germanic source akin to Old High German "missa-" (meaning "wrongly"). "Information" comes from Old French "informacion" from Latin "informatio" meaning "formation" or "conception" from "informare" "to give form to, instruct, educate."
