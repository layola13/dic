acculturated: /əˈkʌltʃəreɪtɪd/
adjective
适应文化的，融入文化的
Having assimilated to a different culture, typically the dominant one.
Many immigrants become acculturated to the customs of their new country. 许多移民逐渐适应了他们新国家的习俗。

The children of immigrants often become acculturated more quickly than their parents. 移民的孩子通常比他们的父母更快地融入当地文化。

近义词: assimilated, integrated, adapted
反义词: unacculturated, alienated

词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin ad- (“to”) + cultura (“cultivation”).  The word "culture" itself derives from the Latin "colere," meaning "to cultivate" or "to till."  Thus, "acculturated" literally means "cultivated towards" or "adapted to a culture."
