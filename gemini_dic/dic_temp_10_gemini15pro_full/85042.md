harbor:/ˈhɑːrbər/ (US) /ˈhɑːbə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.| 港口，庇护所，怀有(想法或感情)|A harbor is a sheltered place where ships can safely anchor.  A harbor can also refer to giving shelter or refuge, or to holding onto a feeling or thought. |The ships sailed into the harbor at dawn. 船只在黎明时分驶入港口。|synonyms: port, haven, shelter, entertain (for feelings)|antonyms: expose, expel|noun

harbor:/ˈhɑːrbər/ (US) /ˈhɑːbə(r)/ (UK)| v.|庇护，怀有(想法或感情)|To harbor someone or something means to give them shelter or refuge. To harbor a feeling or thought means to keep it in one's mind, often secretly. |She harbored a secret desire to become a writer. 她怀着想成为作家的秘密愿望。|synonyms: shelter, protect, conceal, entertain (for feelings)|antonyms: expose, expel|verb
