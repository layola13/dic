liver:/ˈlɪvər/| n. |肝脏；肝|A large lobed glandular organ in the abdomen of vertebrates, involved in many metabolic processes.|The liver is responsible for filtering toxins from the blood.|近义词:None (as a body organ)|反义词:None|Noun

liver:/ˈlaɪvər/| adj. |肝色的，似肝的|Resembling the color or texture of liver.|He wore a liver-colored coat.|近义词:reddish-brown, maroon|反义词:None|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lifer, from Proto-Germanic *libraz.  Cognate with German Leber, Dutch lever. Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *lēp- (“to stick, adhere”).  The connection may be due to the liver's rich, sticky texture.
