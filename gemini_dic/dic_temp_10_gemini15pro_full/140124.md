ETYMOLOGY->
"off" (prep., adv.) Old English "of," from Proto-Germanic "*af" (source also of Old Norse "af," Old Frisian "of," Dutch "af," German "ab" "off, away, down, from"), from PIE root "*apo" (source also of Greek "apo," Latin "ab," Sanskrit "apa," Hittite "apa" "off, away").

"tackle" (n.) early 14c., "instrument of attack; weapons, gear, equipment," from Old North French "takel" "a fastening; equipment," from Middle Dutch "takel" (plural "takelen") "rope and pulley block; rigging of a ship," related to "takelen" "to rig, equip," from Proto-Germanic "*takila-" "tool, implement." The specific sense of "fishing gear" is from c. 1400. The sense in football is from 1894.
