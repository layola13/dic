doghouses: /ˈdɒɡˌhaʊzɪz/ (UK) /ˈdɔːɡˌhaʊzɪz/ (US)
|n.|狗窝|A small shelter for a dog, typically placed outdoors.|The dog curled up inside its doghouse. 狗蜷缩在它的狗窝里。|kennel, shelter|house (when referring to human habitation)|noun
doghouse: /ˈdɒɡˌhaʊs/ (UK) /ˈdɔːɡˌhaʊs/ (US)
|n.|困境，失宠|In a state of disfavor or trouble.|He's in the doghouse with his wife after forgetting their anniversary.  他忘记了结婚纪念日，现在妻子正生他的气。|trouble, hot water|favor|noun
Etymology:  Compound of "dog" and "house."  "Dog" comes from Old English "docga," of uncertain origin. "House" comes from Old English "hūs."
