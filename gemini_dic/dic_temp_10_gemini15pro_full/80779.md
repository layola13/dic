gossipy:/ˈɡɑːsəpi/ /ˈɡɒsɪpi/| adj.|爱说闲话的，八卦的|Fond of gossip; given to gossiping.  Inclined to spread rumors or talk about the private matters of others.|She has a gossipy neighbor who always knows everyone's business. 她有一个爱说闲话的邻居，总是知道每个人的事。|例句：He's a bit gossipy, so don't tell him anything confidential.  他有点八卦，所以不要告诉他任何机密的事情。|近义词：chatty, talkative, nosy|反义词：reserved, discreet|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From gossip + -y.  "Gossip" itself originates from Old English "godsibb," meaning "sponsor at baptism," later evolving to mean a close friend and eventually acquiring its modern connotation of idle chatter, often about others' private affairs.
