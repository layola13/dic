lay-it-on-the-line:/leɪ ɪt ɒn ðə laɪn/| idiom|坦白直言，说出真相|To be completely honest and direct; to speak frankly, even if it is unpleasant.|例句：I'm going to lay it on the line: your work isn't good enough. 我要坦白地说：你的工作不够好。|近义词：be frank, be honest, tell the truth, shoot straight, speak candidly, get something off one's chest, come clean|反义词：beat around the bush, be evasive, sugarcoat, equivocate|phrase

lay-it-on-the-line:/leɪ ɪt ɒn ðə laɪn/| idiom|摊牌，表明立场|To clearly state one's position or demands, often in a firm or demanding way.|例句：The boss laid it on the line: meet your sales targets or you're fired. 老板摊牌了：达到销售目标，否则你就被解雇了。|近义词：make one's position clear, issue an ultimatum, draw a line in the sand|反义词：be indecisive, hedge one's bets, prevaricate|phrase
