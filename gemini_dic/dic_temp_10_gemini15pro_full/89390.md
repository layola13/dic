homoplasy: /ˈhɒməˌpleɪzi/ /ˈhoʊməˌpleɪzi/ | n. | 同塑性，趋同 |  A character shared by a set of species but not present in their common ancestor.  A similar structure or function in two different species that has evolved independently and not through inheritance from a common ancestor. |  The wings of birds and bats are an example of homoplasy. 鸟类和蝙蝠的翅膀是同塑性的一个例子。 |  analogy, convergence | homology | noun
