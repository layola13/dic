barrage:/bəˈrɑːʒ/| v. n.|猛烈攻击，火力网|To bombard someone or something with something.  To subject (someone) to a rapid, concentrated outpouring, as of questions or criticisms.|The reporters barraged the president with questions about the scandal. 记者们就丑闻向总统提出了一连串问题。|The city was barraged with enemy fire. 城市遭到敌人的炮火猛烈攻击。|近义词：bombard, inundate, overwhelm, pepper|反义词: protect, shield|词性:动词,名词
词源: From French *barrage* "barrier, dam," from Old French *barre* "bar." Originally referred to a dam or barrier, then extended to a protective artillery fire, and finally to any overwhelming attack.
