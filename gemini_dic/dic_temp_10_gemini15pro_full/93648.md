imbricate:/ˈɪmbrɪkeɪt/ /ˈɪmbrɪkət/ | n. | 叠瓦状物 | An overlapping arrangement, like tiles on a roof. | The imbricate of the feathers provides excellent insulation. 羽毛的叠瓦状排列提供了极好的隔热效果。| overlap, shingle | separation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *imbricatus*, past participle of *imbricō* (“to cover with tiles”), from *imber* (“rain shower”) + *-icō* (suffix forming verbs).  The original sense was "to make like rain tiles" for waterproofing.
