deftnesses:/ˈdɛftnɪsɪz/
noun
灵巧；熟练；敏捷
skill, expertise, and quickness in movement or action.
The deftnesses of the surgeon's hands were remarkable.  外科医生双手的灵巧令人惊叹。
synonyms: dexterity, adroitness, agility, proficiency, skillfulness
antonyms: clumsiness, awkwardness, ineptness
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From deft + -ness. Deft comes from Middle English *deft, from Old English *gedæfte "mild, meek," related to Gothic *gadafts "fitting, suitable."  The sense shifted to "apt, skillful" in the 15th century.
