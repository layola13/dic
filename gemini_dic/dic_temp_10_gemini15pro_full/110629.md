limpin:/ˈlɪmpɪn/| adj.|跛行的，一瘸一拐的|Present participle of "limp," meaning walking with difficulty because of an injured leg or foot.|He was limpin' after he twisted his ankle. 他扭伤了脚踝，一瘸一拐地走着。|Synonyms:  hobbling, halting, stumbling|Antonyms:  walking, striding, running|verb (present participle)

limp:/lɪmp/| vi. n.|跛行，蹒跚；(船)缓慢费力地航行；跛行；无力|To walk with difficulty, typically because of a damaged or stiff leg or foot.  A way of walking impeded by injury or stiffness.|The dog limped back to the house. 那条狗一瘸一拐地回到了房子。|Synonyms: hobble, stagger|Antonyms: stride, run|verb, noun
