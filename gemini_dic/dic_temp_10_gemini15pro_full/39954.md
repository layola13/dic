constructivist: /kənˈstrʌktɪvɪst/ | n. adj. | 建构主义者；建构主义的 |  A constructivist is someone who believes that knowledge is constructed through experience and interaction with the world, rather than being passively received.  As an adjective, it describes theories, approaches, or methods based on this principle. |  Example: Piaget was a prominent constructivist in the field of developmental psychology. 皮亚杰是发展心理学领域杰出的建构主义者。|  Example: The teacher employed a constructivist approach to learning, encouraging students to discover principles for themselves. 老师采用建构主义的学习方法，鼓励学生自己探索规律。 | Synonyms: experiential, discovery-based | Antonyms: traditional, didactic | Noun, Adjective
