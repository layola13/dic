cue-in:/ˈkjuː ɪn/| n. |提示，暗示，信号|A signal (often musical) to begin something, especially a speech or performance; the words or actions that introduce something.|The orchestra waited for their cue-in to start playing.  乐队等待他们的提示开始演奏。|The host gave the comedian his cue-in to start his routine. 主持人给了喜剧演员开始表演的提示。| synonym: signal, prompt, cue, indication | antonym: interruption, cessation, stop | noun
cue-in:/ˈkjuː ɪn/| v. |提示，暗示|To give someone a cue or signal to begin something.|He cued-in the next speaker with a nod. 他点头示意下一位发言者开始。|The director cued-in the actors to begin the scene. 导演提示演员开始表演。|synonym: prompt, signal, cue |antonym: stop, interrupt |verb
