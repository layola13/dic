awoke:/əˈwəʊk/ (UK) /əˈwoʊk/ (US)| v. |醒来，唤醒|Past tense of awake.  The simple past of "awake" meaning to stop sleeping or to make someone stop sleeping.| I awoke at dawn. 我在黎明醒来。|  I awoke to the sound of birds singing. 我在鸟鸣声中醒来。 |近义词：wakened, roused, stirred|反义词：slept, slumbered|词性:动词

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
I awake early every morning. 我每天早上醒得很早。
He awakes to the sound of the alarm. 他被闹钟的声音吵醒。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
This tense is not typically used with "awake" as a verb. "Awake" usually describes a momentary action.  However, you could use it in a figurative sense:  He is awaking to the dangers of smoking. 他开始意识到吸烟的危害。
She is awaking to the realities of the situation. 她开始意识到情况的现实。

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
I have awoken several times during the night. 我晚上醒了好几次。
She has awoken to a new sense of purpose. 她对人生目标有了新的认识。

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
This tense is rarely used with "awake."

词源:
From Old English *awacan, from Proto-Germanic *wakjaną (“to wake, watch”), from Proto-Indo-European *wog- (“to be awake, watchful”). Cognate with Scots awauk (“to awake”), West Frisian wekker (“awake”), Dutch wakker (“awake”), German wacker (“brave, brisk”), Danish vågen (“awake”), Swedish vaken (“awake”), Icelandic vakna (“to wake up”).
