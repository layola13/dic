over-officious: /ˌoʊvər əˈfɪʃəs/ | adj. | 过于殷勤的，多管闲事的 | Excessively eager in offering unwanted services or advice; interfering.  Characterized by excessive and unnecessary zeal in performing duties, especially in a way that is perceived as bossy or intrusive. |  The over-officious waiter kept interrupting our conversation to offer suggestions.  那个过于殷勤的服务员不断打断我们的谈话，提出建议。|  The over-officious security guard wouldn't let me in even though I had my ID.  那个多管闲事的保安即使我带着身份证也不让我进去。|  officious, meddlesome, interfering, intrusive |  laid-back, relaxed, easygoing | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + officious.  "Officious" comes from the Latin "officiosus," meaning "dutiful, obliging," from "officium" meaning "duty, service." The prefix "over-" intensifies the meaning to indicate an excess of officiousness.
