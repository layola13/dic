obsessivenesses: /əbˈsɛsɪvnəsəz/ | n. |痴迷，着迷；困扰|The state of being obsessive; a preoccupation with something.| Her obsessivenesses with cleanliness made it difficult for anyone to live with her. 她对清洁的痴迷让任何人都难以和她一起生活。|Synonyms: fixations, preoccupations, compulsions |Antonyms: indifference, apathy| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From obsessive + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing state or quality).  Obsessive comes from the Late Latin obsessus, past participle of obsidēre "to besiege," from ob- "toward, against" + sedēre "to sit."
