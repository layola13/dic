anaesthetises:/ˌænɪsˈθɛtaɪzɪz/ /əˈnɛsθəˌtaɪzɪz/| vt.|麻醉|To make a person or a part of their body unable to feel pain, usually by giving them a drug before a medical operation.|The surgeon anaesthetised the patient before the operation. 外科医生在手术前给病人麻醉了。|Synonyms: numb, drug, sedate|Antonyms: stimulate, awaken|verb

anaesthetize:/əˈnɛsθəˌtaɪz/ /ˌænɪsˈθɛtaɪz/| vt.|麻醉|To make a person or a part of their body unable to feel pain, usually by giving them a drug before a medical operation.|The dentist anaesthetized the area around the tooth before starting the procedure. 牙医在开始手术前麻醉了牙齿周围的区域。|Synonyms: numb, drug, sedate|Antonyms: stimulate, awaken|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek ἀναισθησία (anaisthēsia, “lack of sensation”), from ἀν- (an-, “not, without”) + αἴσθησις (aisthēsis, “sensation”).
