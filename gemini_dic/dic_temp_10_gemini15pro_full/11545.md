aristarchian:/ˌærɪˈstɑːrkiən/ | adj. n. | 吹毛求疵的，过于严苛的；吹毛求疵的人 |  Relating to or characteristic of Aristarchus, a Greek grammarian and critic known for his rigorous standards; excessively critical; a person who is excessively critical. |  He gave an aristarchian analysis of the poem, finding fault with every line. 他对这首诗进行了吹毛求疵的分析，对每一行都挑剔。 | 近义词: hypercritical, fastidious, captious, censorious | 反义词: lenient, tolerant, forgiving | 词性: adjective, noun

词源: From Aristarchus (c. 217 – c. 143 BC), a Greek grammarian and critic of antiquity, known for his rigorous editing of Homer's works.  The term "aristarchian" came to be associated with his meticulous and sometimes overly critical approach to textual analysis.
