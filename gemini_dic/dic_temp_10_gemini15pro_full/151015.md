paranoid:/ˈpærənɔɪd/ | adj. | 偏执的，多疑的 | Exhibiting or characterized by paranoia, which is a mental condition marked by delusions of persecution, unwarranted jealousy, or exaggerated self-importance, typically elaborated into an organized system. It may be an aspect of chronic personality disorder, of drug abuse, or of a serious condition such as schizophrenia. | He's been acting paranoid lately, convinced that everyone is out to get him. 他最近表现得很偏执，确信每个人都想害他。

| He gave me a paranoid look, as if I were about to steal his wallet. 他给了我一个偏执的眼神，好像我要偷他的钱包似的。 | 近义词: suspicious, distrustful, wary, apprehensive | 反义词: trusting, confiding, naive | 词性: adjective

Etymology:
From Greek "para-" (beside, beyond, amiss) + "nous" (mind, intellect), meaning "deranged mind."
