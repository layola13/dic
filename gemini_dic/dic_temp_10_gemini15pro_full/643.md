apricot:/ˈeɪprɪkɒt/ (UK) /ˈæprɪkɑːt/ (US)| n.|杏; 杏子; 杏黄色|A small, round, soft fruit with yellow-orange flesh and a smooth stone.  It is similar to a peach or plum.|I ate a delicious apricot this morning. 我今天早上吃了一个美味的杏子。|Synonyms: nectarine, peach, plum|Antonyms: None (Fruits don't typically have antonyms)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *abricot (modern French abricot), from Arabic al-birqūq, ultimately from Late Greek praikókion (πραικόκιον), from Latin praecoquum "early-ripening," neuter of praecox, from prae- "before" + coquere "to cook, ripen."
