abrogator:/ˈæbrəɡeɪtər/| n.|废除者，取消者|A person who abrogates or repeals a law, treaty, or custom.|The king acted as abrogator of the old feudal laws. 国王废除了旧的封建法律。|Synonyms: abolisher, repealer, nullifier, revoker|Antonyms: ratifier, confirmer, upholder|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *abrogator*, from Latin abrogare (“to repeal”), from ab- (“away from”) + rogare (“to ask, propose a law”).

USAGE->
The abrogator of the treaty was heavily criticized.  该条约的废除者受到了严厉批评。

The president, acting as abrogator, annulled the outdated law. 总统作为废除者，废除了过时的法律。

The company's CEO was the abrogator of the previous policy. 公司首席执行官是先前政策的废除者。

The parliament voted to become the abrogator of the unpopular tax. 议会投票决定废除不受欢迎的税收。


PHRASE->
the abrogator of the law = 法律的废除者
the abrogator of the agreement = 协议的废除者
the abrogator of the contract = 合同的废除者
the abrogator of the custom = 习俗的废除者
act as abrogator = 担任废除者的角色
