deglaze:/ˌdiːˈɡleɪz/ | v. | 浇汁; 去釉 | To remove and dissolve the browned food residue from a pan by adding liquid, usually wine or stock, and heating.  This creates a flavorful sauce. |  After searing the steak, I deglazed the pan with red wine.  煎完牛排后，我用红酒给锅去釉。| synonyms: dissolve, loosen | antonyms: thicken, congeal | verb

deglazed:/ˌdiːˈɡleɪzd/ | adj. | 浇过汁的; 去过釉的 | Having had the browned food residue removed and dissolved from its surface by adding liquid and heating. |  The deglazed pan yielded a rich, savory sauce. 浇过汁的锅产生了一种浓郁可口的酱汁。 | synonyms: sauced, coated | antonyms: uncoated, dry | adjective
