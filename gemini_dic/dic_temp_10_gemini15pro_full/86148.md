heartland:/ˈhɑːrtlænd/ (US) /ˈhɑːtlænd/ (UK)| n.|中心地带，腹地；核心地区|The area considered central to a country or region, either geographically, culturally, or politically. It often refers to an area where traditional values are believed to be strongest.|The Midwest is often called the heartland of America. 美国中西部通常被称为美国的心脏地带。|synonyms: center, core, hub, nucleus|antonyms: periphery, outskirts, fringe|noun
