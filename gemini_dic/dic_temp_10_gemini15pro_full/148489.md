overstridently:/ˌoʊvərˈstraɪdəntli/
|adv.| 过于自信地，过于断然地|Speaking or behaving in a way that shows too much confidence and determination, often in a way that is annoying or offensive.|He overstridently proclaimed his innocence. 他过于断然地宣称自己无罪。|例句：She overstridently asserted her opinions in the meeting, alienating several colleagues.  她在会议上过于武断地表达自己的观点，疏远了几位同事。

例句：The politician overstridently declared victory before all the votes had been counted.  这位政治家在所有选票统计出来之前就过于自信地宣布获胜了。|近义词：assertively, dogmatically, emphatically, vehemently|反义词：hesitantly, meekly, timidly|词性:副词
ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + strident + -ly.  "Strident" comes from Latin *stridēns*, present participle of stridēre "to make a harsh noise."  "Over-" indicates an excess or surpassing of a limit.  Therefore, "overstridently" describes an action performed with an excessive degree of harshness or forcefulness in sound or manner.
