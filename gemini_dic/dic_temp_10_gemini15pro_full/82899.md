gum up the works: /ɡʌm ʌp ðə wɜːrks/
verb phrase
把事情搞砸；阻碍工作；使机器停止运转
To cause something to stop working or to become less effective.
The computer system was gummed up by a virus.  电脑系统被病毒搞瘫痪了。
Bureaucracy can really gum up the works. 官僚主义真的会把事情搞砸。
近义词: mess up, foul up, screw up, disrupt, obstruct, hinder
反义词: facilitate, expedite, smooth, help
词性: verb phrase

gum:/ɡʌm/
noun
口香糖；树胶
A sticky substance that is used for chewing or for sticking things together.
She chews gum all day. 她整天嚼口香糖。
The gum from the tree is used to make glue.  这种树的树胶用来制作胶水。
近义词: chewing gum, resin, adhesive
反义词: (For chewing gum) None, (For adhesive) solvent
词性: noun

up:/ʌp/
adverb, preposition, adjective
向上；起来；增加
Moving or pointing towards a higher place; increasing in amount, level, or degree.
Prices are going up.  价格在上涨。
He climbed up the ladder. 他爬上了梯子。
近义词: upward, higher, above, increase
反义词: down, lower, below, decrease
词性: adverb, preposition, adjective

the:/ðə/ /ðiː/
definite article
定冠词
Used before nouns to refer to particular things or people that have already been mentioned or are easily identified.

近义词: None (as it's a grammatical article)
反义词: None (as it's a grammatical article)

works:/wɜːrks/
noun
工厂；作品；运作方式
A place where something is made or repaired; the moving parts of a machine; the way something functions.
He got a job at the steel works. 他在钢铁厂找到了一份工作。
The inner works of a clock are intricate.  钟表的内部结构很复杂。
近义词: factory, plant, machinery, mechanism, operation
反义词: (For factory) None, (For machinery) idleness
词性: noun

词源：
"Gum up the works" originated in the late 19th century, likely related to machinery. The "gum" refers to sticky substances that could clog or jam the moving parts of a machine, thus hindering its operation. This mechanical sense was later extended metaphorically to any process or activity.
