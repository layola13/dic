interlibels: /ˌɪntərˈlaɪbəlz/ | n. |相互诽谤|  Mutual or reciprocal libels; defamatory statements made by two or more parties against each other. |  The two politicians engaged in a series of interlibels, each accusing the other of corruption.  两位政客进行了一系列相互诽谤，互相指责对方腐败。 | 近义词：cross-libels, mutual accusations | 反义词：praise, commendation | 词性: noun

词源:  "inter-" (Latin, meaning "between, among") + "libel" (from Old French "libelle," meaning "little book," which later came to mean a written defamatory statement).
