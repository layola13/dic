ETYMOLOGY->
"all of a heap" is a phrase that originated in the late 16th century.  "Heap" refers to a pile or mound.  The phrase "all of a heap" literally means "all in a pile," suggesting a state of disorder or collapse.  The sense of emotional or physical collapse likely developed later.
