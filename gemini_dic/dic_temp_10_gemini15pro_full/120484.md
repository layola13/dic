minters:/ˈmɪntərz/| n.|造币者；创造者；新词创造者|People who create or produce something new, particularly coins or new words.  It's the plural form of "minter."|The Royal Mint employs skilled minters. 皇家铸币厂雇佣了熟练的造币工人。

|近义词：creators, producers, inventors, coiners|反义词: destroyers, demolisher|noun

mints:/mɪnts/| n.|薄荷；薄荷糖；巨款；造币厂|A type of aromatic herb; a candy flavored with mint; a large sum of money; a place where coins are made.  It's also the third-person singular present tense form of the verb "to mint."|She always carries mints in her purse. 她钱包里总是带着薄荷糖。

|近义词：(for candy) sweets, confectionery; (for money) fortune, wealth; (for factory) mint, coin factory|反义词: (for factory) N/A|noun, verb

mint:/mɪnt/| n. v. adj.|薄荷；造币厂；巨款；铸造；完美的；崭新的|As a noun: a type of aromatic herb, a place where coins are made, or a large sum of money. As a verb: to manufacture (coins). As an adjective: in perfect condition; brand new.|He bought a mint condition vintage car. 他买了一辆完美的古董车。

|近义词：(n.) spearmint, peppermint, fortune, wealth, mint, coin factory; (v.) coin, produce, create; (adj.) pristine, immaculate, brand-new|反义词: (adj.) worn, used, damaged|noun, verb, adjective
