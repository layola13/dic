hypernormalness's:/ˌhaɪpərˈnɔːrməlnəsɪz/ | n. | 超常态 |  A state of simulated or exaggerated normality, often concealing underlying instability or abnormality. A term coined by journalist Peter Pomerantsev to describe the political and social environment of Russia under Vladimir Putin.  It refers to a condition where everything appears superficially normal, but underlying this facade is a deep sense of unease and a distorted perception of reality. |  The glossy facade of the city couldn't hide the hypernormalness's grip on its citizens. 这座城市光鲜亮丽的外表无法掩盖超常态对其市民的控制。

The dictator's carefully crafted image of stability only amplified the nation's sense of hypernormalness's. 独裁者精心打造的稳定形象，只会加剧这个国家超常态的感觉。 | Near synonyms: artificiality, facade, illusion, pretense | Near antonyms: authenticity, genuineness, reality, normalcy | noun
