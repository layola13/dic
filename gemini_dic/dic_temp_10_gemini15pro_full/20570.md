blackbirded:/ˈblækˌbɜːrdɪd/ | v. | 诱骗；挖墙脚；强迫加入 | To entice or coerce someone into a situation, especially employment, by dubious means.  Often implies secrecy or deception. |  They blackbirded the talented engineer from our company with a huge salary offer. 他们用高薪诱惑那位才华横溢的工程师离开我们公司。| synonyms: poached, headhunted, lured, kidnapped, shanghai'd | antonyms: released, freed | verb

blackbirded:/ˈblækˌbɜːrdɪd/ | adj. | 被诱骗的；被挖墙脚的 | Having been enticed or coerced into a situation, especially employment, by dubious means. |  The blackbirded employees were unhappy in their new roles. 被挖走的员工对他们的新职位并不满意。 | synonyms: poached, headhunted, lured | antonyms:  untempted | adjective
