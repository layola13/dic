calymene:/ˈkælɪˌmiːn/
n.
卡利米尼三叶虫
A genus of extinct trilobites of the order Phacopida, common in the Ordovician and Silurian periods, characterized by a distinctively furrowed exoskeleton that allowed it to roll into a ball.
Calymene trilobites are frequently found fossilized in rocks from the Silurian period.  卡利米尼三叶虫的化石经常在志留纪岩石中被发现。
Calymene are known for their ability to enroll, protecting themselves from predators. 卡利米尼三叶虫以其能够卷曲成球状来保护自己免受捕食者的攻击而闻名。
