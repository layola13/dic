diapy:/ˈdaɪəpaɪ/| n.|底辟|A piercing fold of strata resulting from the squeezing of underlying plastic material into overlying brittle material.  A dome-like structure formed by the upward movement of relatively mobile material, such as salt or shale, which pierces and deforms the overlying rock layers.|The diapir formed a prominent hill in the otherwise flat landscape.|近义词:dome, intrusion|反义词:(No direct antonym, consider subsidence)|词性:noun
