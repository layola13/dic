calm:/kɑːm/ | adj.  n.  v. | 平静的，镇静的；平静，镇定；使平静，使镇定 |Free from excitement, anxiety, or disturbance; tranquil. |The sea was calm after the storm.  暴风雨过后，大海平静下来了。|  近义词: peaceful, tranquil, serene, quiet | 反义词: agitated, turbulent, frantic, chaotic | 词性: 形容词

calm:/kɑːm/ | n. | 平静，镇定 |The state of being free from excitement, anxiety, or disturbance. |There was a strange calm before the battle.  战斗前有一种奇怪的平静。| 近义词: peace, tranquility, serenity, quietness | 反义词: agitation, turbulence, frenzy, chaos | 词性: 名词

calm:/kɑːm/ | v. | 使平静，使镇定 |Make (someone) tranquil and quiet; soothe. |She calmed the frightened child. 她使受惊的孩子平静下来。| 近义词: soothe, pacify, tranquilize, quiet | 反义词: agitate, excite, provoke, inflame | 词性: 动词


词源: From Middle French calme, from Italian calma “calm, stillness of the sea,” from Late Latin cauma “heat of the sun at midday; midday rest taken to avoid the heat,” from Greek kauma, neuter of kaūma “burning heat, fever heat,” from kaiein “to burn.”
