neocons:/ˈniːoʊkɑːnz/ |n. |新保守主义者|A person who advocates a return to traditional moral values, a strong national defense, and an assertive foreign policy, often with a focus on the promotion of democracy and American interests abroad.  It emerged in the United States in the 1970s and 1980s.|The rise of the neocons began in the 1970s. 新保守主义的兴起始于20世纪70年代。

The neocons advocated for the Iraq War. 新保守主义者主张伊拉克战争。| 近义词：hawks, interventionists |反义词：doves, non-interventionists| 词性: 名词 (plural)
