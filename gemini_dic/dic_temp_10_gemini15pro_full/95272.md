incapacitate:/ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪteɪt/ | vt. | 使无能力；使丧失能力；使不适合 | To deprive of strength or ability; to disable. | The accident incapacitated him from working.  这起事故使他无法工作。|  disable, cripple, impair | enable, empower | verb

incapacitate:/ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪteɪt/ | vt. | 使无行为能力；使失去资格；使不胜任 | To make legally incapable or ineligible. | His mental illness incapacitated him from managing his own affairs. 他的精神疾病使他无法管理自己的事务。| disqualify, unfit | qualify, enable | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *incapacitatus, past participle of incapacitare "to make unfit," from in- "not" + capacitatus, past participle of capacitare "to make capable," from Latin capax "able to hold," from capere "to take, seize."
