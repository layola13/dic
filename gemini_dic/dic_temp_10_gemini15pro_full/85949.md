n.

反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps student/pupil)

headmistress: /ˌhɛdˈmɪstrɪs/
n.
女校长，女舍监
A woman who is the head teacher of a school, especially a private school for girls.
Etymology:  "head" + "mistress"
"Head" meaning chief or leading.
"Mistress" from Old French "maistresse," meaning a woman in a position of authority or control.  Originally, "mistress" was the female equivalent of "master," but its meaning has evolved over time.  In this context, it retains the sense of authority and leadership.
