farsighted:/ˈfɑːrˈsaɪtɪd/ (US) /ˈfɑːˈsaɪtɪd/ (UK)| adj.|有远见的；远视的|Able to see things at a distance clearly but having difficulty seeing things close up.  Figuratively, it means having the ability to anticipate future trends or consequences.|例句：She's a farsighted leader who always plans for the future. 她是一位有远见的领导者，总是为未来做计划。

例句：He needs glasses because he's farsighted. 他需要眼镜，因为他有远视。|近义词：prescient, visionary, long-sighted|反义词：shortsighted, myopic, nearsighted|词性:adjective


farsightedness:/ˌfɑːrˈsaɪtɪdnəs/ (US) /ˌfɑːˈsaɪtɪdnəs/ (UK)| n.|远视；远见|The condition of being farsighted; the ability to anticipate future needs or events.|例句：Her farsightedness allowed her to make investments that paid off handsomely. 她的远见卓识使她能够进行回报丰厚的投资。

例句：Farsightedness can be corrected with glasses or contact lenses. 远视可以通过眼镜或隐形眼镜来矫正。|近义词：foresight, vision, prescience|反义词：shortsightedness, myopia|词性:noun
