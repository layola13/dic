bandmaster:/ˈbændˌmæstər/ | n. | 乐队指挥，军乐队指挥 | A person who conducts a band, especially a military or brass band. | The bandmaster raised his baton and the music began. 乐队指挥举起指挥棒，音乐开始了。|  The bandmaster led the orchestra through a complex piece. 乐队指挥带领管弦乐队演奏了一首复杂的曲子。 | synonyms: conductor, director, leader | antonyms: musician, player | noun

Etymology:
band + master

band (n.):  From Old French bande (“band, company”), from Frankish *band (“sign, proclamation, ban”), from Proto-Germanic *bandą (“something binding”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰendʰ- (“to bind”). Cognate with Old High German bant (“ban, excommunication”), Old English bend (“bond, fetter”).

master (n.): From Old French maistre, from Latin magister (“master, chief, director, teacher”), from magis (“more”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *meg- (“great”).
