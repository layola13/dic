boxwood:/ˈbɒkswʊd/ (UK) /ˈbɑːkswʊd/ (US)| n. |黄杨木；黄杨树|An evergreen shrub or small tree with small, glossy leaves, often used for hedges.  A hard, close-grained wood from this plant, used for carving and making musical instruments.|The gardener trimmed the boxwood hedge. 园丁修剪了黄杨树篱。|Synonyms: box,  |Antonyms: N/A|noun


boxwood:/ˈbɒkswʊd/ (UK) /ˈbɑːkswʊd/ (US)| adj. |黄杨木制的|Made from the wood of the boxwood tree.|He carved a delicate bird from boxwood. 他用黄杨木雕刻了一只精致的鸟。|Synonyms: box|Antonyms: N/A|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *boxwudu (attested only as box, referring to containers made of the wood), from box (referring to the tree) + wudu "wood."  Box (the tree name) is from Latin buxus, from Ancient Greek πύξος (púxos).
