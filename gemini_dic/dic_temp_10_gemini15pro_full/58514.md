duer:/ˈduːər/ (US), /ˈdʒuːər/ (UK)| n.| 做事的人，执行者; 欠债的人; 职责，义务 (Scottish, archaic)|A doer is someone who acts rather than merely thinks or talks.  In older Scottish usage, it can also mean someone who owes a debt or a duty. |例句：He's a doer, not a talker. 他是一个实干家，而不是一个空谈家。

例句：She proved herself a duer by completing the project ahead of schedule. 她通过提前完成项目证明了自己是一个实干家。|近义词：actor, performer, worker, agent|反义词:slacker, idler, procrastinator|词性:名词

词源: From Middle English *doere, from Old English *dēore, from Proto-Germanic *dōjaz. Cognate with Dutch doener (“doer”), German Dauer (“duration”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁- (“to put, place, do”).
