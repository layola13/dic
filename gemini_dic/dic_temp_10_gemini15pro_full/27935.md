cantillate: /ˈkæntɪleɪt/ | v. |吟诵，咏唱|to chant or intone, especially a liturgical text, in a musical or rhythmical monotone|The priest cantillated the prayers. 神父吟诵着祷告。|Synonyms: chant, intone, recite, sing|Antonyms: whisper, mumble|verb
cantillation: /ˌkæntɪˈleɪʃn/ | n. |吟诵，咏唱|the act of cantillating; a chanted or intoned passage, especially in a liturgical service|The cantor's cantillation of the psalm was beautiful.  领唱者对诗篇的吟诵很优美。|Synonyms: chanting, intonation, recitation|Antonyms: silence, whispering|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin cantillātiō, from Latin cantillāre “to sing, chant,” frequentative of canere “to sing.”
