break-ins:/ˈbreɪkɪnz/| n. |非法闯入，入室盗窃|An act of entering a building illegally, typically to commit a crime such as theft.|There have been a series of break-ins in the neighborhood recently. 最近附近发生了一系列入室盗窃事件。|近义词：burglaries, robberies, intrusions|反义词:security, safety|名词

break-in:/ˈbreɪkɪn/| n. |非法闯入，入室盗窃; 磨合期|An act of entering a building illegally, typically to commit a crime such as theft; a period of adjustment or training.|The police are investigating a break-in at the local museum. 警方正在调查当地博物馆的一起入室盗窃案. |The new shoes needed a break-in period before they were comfortable. 新鞋需要一段磨合期才能穿得舒服。|近义词：burglary, robbery, intrusion, initiation|反义词:security, safety|名词

词源:  From the phrasal verb "break in," where "break" means to force entry and "in" indicates direction.
