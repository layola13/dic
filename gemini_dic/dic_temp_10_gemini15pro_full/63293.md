enthusiasm:/ɪnˈθuːziæzəm/| n. |热情，热忱|A feeling of excitement and interest in something.|She has a great enthusiasm for learning new languages. 她对学习新语言充满了热情。|例句：The project was greeted with enthusiasm. 这个项目受到了热烈的欢迎。|近义词：passion, zeal, fervor, eagerness|反义词：apathy, indifference, coldness|词性:名词

enthusiast:/ɪnˈθuːziæst/| n. |热衷者，爱好者|A person who is very interested in and involved with a particular activity or subject.|He's a real computer enthusiast. 他是一位真正的电脑爱好者。|例句：She's an enthusiast for modern art.  她是现代艺术的爱好者。|近义词：fan, aficionado, devotee|反义词：critic, detractor|词性:名词

enthusiastic:/ɪnˌθuːziˈæstɪk/| adj. |热情的，热心的|Having or showing intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval.|They gave her an enthusiastic welcome. 他们热情地欢迎她。|例句：The audience was enthusiastic. 观众很热情。|近义词：eager, ardent, keen, avid|反义词：apathetic, indifferent, unenthusiastic|词性:形容词

enthusiastically:/ɪnˌθuːziˈæstɪkli/| adv. |热情地，热心地|In a way that shows intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval.|The crowd cheered enthusiastically.  人群热情地欢呼。|例句：She spoke enthusiastically about her new job. 她热情地谈论她的新工作。|近义词：eagerly, ardently, keenly, avidly|反义词：apathetically, indifferently, unenthusiastically|词性:副词

词源:  From French *enthousiasme*, from Late Latin *entusiasmus*, from Greek *enthousiasmos* "divine inspiration, possession by a god," from *entheos* "inspired, possessed," from *en* "in" + *theos* "god."
