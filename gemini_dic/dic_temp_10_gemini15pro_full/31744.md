cheapskating:/ˈtʃiːpskeɪtɪŋ/
adjective
吝啬的，爱占小便宜的
Reluctant to spend money, especially on things that are considered necessary or appropriate.
He's always cheapskating and never wants to split the bill evenly. 他总是爱占小便宜，从来不想平分账单。
My cheapskating boss refused to pay for new office furniture. 我那吝啬的老板拒绝为新办公家具买单。
stingy, miserly, parsimonious
generous, extravagant
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
cheapskate (n.) 1912, American English, from cheap (adj.) + skate (n.). The image perhaps is of someone so stingy that they would even skate on something cheap rather than spend money on proper equipment.  The verb cheapskate is attested by 1934.
