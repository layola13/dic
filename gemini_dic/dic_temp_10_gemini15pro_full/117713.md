melancholics: /ˌmɛlənˈkɑːlɪks/ | n. | 忧郁的人；忧郁症患者 | People who suffer from or are prone to melancholy; depressed or sad individuals. | Many artists and poets throughout history have been described as melancholics. 许多历史上艺术家和诗人都被描述为忧郁的人。 |  The melancholics sat staring out the window at the rainy day. 那些忧郁的人坐在窗边凝视着雨天。| 近义词: depressives, pessimists | 反义词: optimists, cheerful people | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Late Latin *melancholicus, from Ancient Greek μελαγχολικός (melankholikós, “atrabilious, melancholic”), from μέλας (mélas, “black, dark”) + χολή (kholḗ, “bile”).  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholy.
