earthshock:/ˈɜːrθʃɒk/ (US) /ˈɜːθʃɒk/ (UK)| n. |地冲击，地颤，地震|A sudden, violent shaking of the earth's surface; an earthquake.|The earthshock destroyed several buildings in the city. 地震摧毁了城里几座建筑物。|例句：The earthshock was felt as far away as 100 miles.  地震远在100英里外都能感觉到。|近义词：earthquake, tremor, temblor|反义词:stability, stillness|词性:noun
Etymology: earth + shock.  "Earth" from Old English "eorþe," meaning "ground, soil, dry land." "Shock" from Old French "choquer," meaning "to strike against."
