dorn:/dɔːrn/| n.|刺; 荆棘; 尖锐物|A sharp-pointed projection, as a thorn of a plant or the point of a weapon.  Something that causes persistent annoyance or pain.|He pricked his finger on a dorn while pruning the roses. 他在修剪玫瑰时被一根刺扎破了手指。|Synonyms: thorn, prickle, spine, splinter|Antonyms: bluntness, smoothness|noun

dorn:/dɔːrn/| n.|秘密; 隐藏的事物 (古语, 罕用)|A secret or hidden thing.|The dorn of their past remained unspoken.  他们过去的秘密从未被提起。|Synonyms: secret, mystery, enigma|Antonyms: openness, clarity|noun
