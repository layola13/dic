obdurate:/ˈɒbdjʊrət/ (UK) /ˈɑːbdərət/ (US)| adj.|顽固的，执拗的，冷酷无情的|Stubbornly persistent in wrongdoing, hardened in feelings; resistant to persuasion or softening influences.|The obdurate king refused to listen to his advisors.  固执的国王拒绝听取他的顾问的意见。|
The judge remained obdurate despite the defendant's pleas.  尽管被告恳求，法官仍然铁石心肠。|Synonyms: stubborn, inflexible, unyielding, adamant, intractable, obstinate|Antonyms: compliant, yielding, amenable, tractable, flexible|adjective


obdurating:/ˈɒbdjʊreɪtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈɑːbdəreɪtɪŋ/ (US)| adj.|使顽固的，使冷酷的|Making or tending to make somebody hardened in feelings; resistant to persuasion or softening influences.|His obdurating response only made the situation worse. 他强硬的回应只会让情况变得更糟。|The obdurating experience of war left him emotionally scarred. 战争的残酷经历给他留下了情感上的伤疤。|Synonyms: hardening, toughening, unyielding|Antonyms: softening, yielding, relenting|adjective


obdurately:/ˈɒbdjʊrətli/ (UK) /ˈɑːbdəreɪtli/ (US)| adv.|顽固地，执拗地，冷酷无情地|In a stubborn and unyielding manner.|He obdurately refused to admit he was wrong. 他固执地拒绝承认自己错了。|She obdurately clung to her beliefs despite the evidence against them. 尽管有证据反对，她仍然固执地坚持自己的信仰。|Synonyms: stubbornly, inflexibly, unyieldingly, adamantly|Antonyms: compliantly, yieldingly, amenably|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *obdūrātus*, past participle of *obdūrāre* (“to harden, to make hard”), from *ob-* (“against, towards, in the way”) + *dūrāre* (“to harden, to last, to endure”), from *dūrus* (“hard”).
