heathclad:/ˈhiːθˌklæd/| adj.|石楠覆盖的，覆盖着石楠的|Covered with heath; clad in heath.  The term evokes imagery of a landscape dominated by low-growing, evergreen shrubs.|The hikers traversed the heathclad hills.  徒步旅行者穿越了石楠覆盖的山丘。|近义词：heath-covered|反义词:bare, exposed|词性:形容词
Etymology:  "heath" (Old English *hāþ) + "clad" (Old English geclædd, past participle of clæþan "to clothe").
