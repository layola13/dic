choke:/tʃoʊk/ | v. | 窒息，噎住；使窒息；抑制；堵塞 | to have difficulty breathing because something is blocking your throat, or to make someone have this difficulty |  He choked on a piece of bread. 他被一块面包噎住了。| synonyms: suffocate, strangle, stifle | antonyms: breathe, inhale, release | verb

choke:/tʃoʊk/ | n. | 窒息；阻风门 | the action of choking or being choked | The smoke made him choke. 烟让他窒息。 | synonyms: gag, strangulation | antonyms: breath, respiration | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cēocian (attested only in the past participle acocod), from Proto-Germanic *keukōną (compare West Frisian tsukje “to choke, to gasp,” Dutch kwijnen “to languish, to pine away,” German keuchen “to gasp, to pant”).
