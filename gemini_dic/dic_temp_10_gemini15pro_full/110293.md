liegeance:/ˈliːdʒəns/ | n. | 忠诚，效忠 |  The feudal system demanded liegeance from vassals to their lords. |  Example 1:  The knight swore liegeance to the king. 骑士向国王宣誓效忠。

Example 2:  She pledged her liegeance to the cause. 她宣誓效忠于这项事业。 |  Synonyms: loyalty, fidelity, allegiance, devotion | Antonyms: treachery, disloyalty, betrayal | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *liegeance*, from lige "liege," ultimately from Frankish *letig* "free," from Proto-Germanic *leudigaz* "belonging to the people."  The semantic shift from "free" to "bound by oath" likely occurred through the intermediate sense of "one who is free but owes service."
