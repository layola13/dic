palpi: /ˈpælpaɪ/ (plural), /ˈpæl.pəs/ (singular)| n. |触须；触肢|A sensory appendage near the mouth of an invertebrate, used for touch, taste, or smell.  They are often paired.|The butterfly used its palpi to taste the nectar of the flower. 蝴蝶用它的触须品尝花蜜。|Synonyms: feelers, antennae|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical organ)|Noun

palpus: /ˈpæl.pəs/ (singular)| n. |触须；触肢|Singular form of palpi. A sensory appendage near the mouth of an invertebrate, used for touch, taste, or smell.|The spider's palpus helped it sense vibrations in its web. 蜘蛛的触肢帮助它感知到蛛网上的震动。|Synonyms: feeler, antenna|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical organ)|Noun
