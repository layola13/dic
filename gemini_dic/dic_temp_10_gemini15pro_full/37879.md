complacently:/kəmˈpleɪsntlɪ/
adverb
自满地，沾沾自喜地
in a self-satisfied way, often unaware of possible dangers or deficiencies
He smiled complacently, clearly satisfied with his performance. 他自满地微笑着，显然对自己的表现很满意。
Synonyms: smugly, self-satisfactorily, conceitedly
Antonyms: anxiously, apprehensively, humbly
词性: 副词

complacent:/kəmˈpleɪsnt/
adjective
自满的，得意的
showing smug or uncritical satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements
The team became complacent after winning a few games.  这个队赢了几场比赛后就变得自满了。
Synonyms: smug, self-satisfied, conceited
Antonyms: anxious, apprehensive, humble
词性: 形容词

complacency:/kəmˈpleɪsnsi/
noun
自满，得意
a feeling of smug or uncritical satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements
Their complacency led to their downfall. 他们的自满导致了他们的垮台。
Synonyms: smugness, self-satisfaction, conceit
Antonyms: anxiety, apprehension, humility
词性: 名词

词源: From Latin complacentem (nominative complacens) "pleasing," present participle of complacēre "to be very pleasing," from com- "with" + placēre "to please."
