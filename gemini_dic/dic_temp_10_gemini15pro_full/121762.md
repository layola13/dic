misquotation:/ˌmɪskwoʊˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 错误引用；误引 | An inaccurate or incomplete repetition of someone's words. |  He was furious about the misquotation of his remarks in the newspaper. 他对报纸上错误引用他的话感到愤怒。

misquotation:/ˌmɪskwoʊˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 引用错误 | The act of quoting someone incorrectly. | The article was full of misquotations and factual errors. 这篇文章充满了引用错误和事实错误。


ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (wrongly) + quotation (the act of quoting).  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old English "mis-" meaning "wrongly, badly, amiss."  "Quotation" comes from Late Middle English "quotacioun," from Late Latin "quotatio," from Latin "quoto" (I quote).

SYNONYMS->
distortion, misrepresentation, falsification, perversion

ANTONYMS->
accurate quotation, correct citation, faithful representation
