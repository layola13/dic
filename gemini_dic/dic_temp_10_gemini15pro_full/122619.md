modestly:/ˈmɑːdɪstli/ | adv. | 谦虚地；适度地 | In a manner that avoids drawing attention to oneself; unpretentiously. | She spoke modestly about her achievements. 她谦虚地谈论她的成就。| humbly, unassumingly | boastfully, arrogantly | adverb

modest:/ˈmɑːdɪst/ | adj. | 谦虚的；适度的；不太大的；端庄的 | Having or showing a moderate or humble estimate of one's merits, importance, etc.; not large in size or amount; decent and reserved in manner or appearance. | He's very modest about his success. 他对自己的成功非常谦虚。| humble, unpretentious | arrogant, immodest | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *modeste*, from Latin modestus ("moderate, keeping due measure"), from modus ("measure, manner").
