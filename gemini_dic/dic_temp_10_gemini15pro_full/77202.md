gassing-up:/ˈɡæsɪŋ ʌp/ | n./v. | 加油；吹牛；兴奋；鼓动 | The act of filling a vehicle's fuel tank with gasoline. Also, figuratively, becoming excited or energized, or making someone else feel that way. |  I stopped at the gas station for a quick gassing-up before the long drive. 我在长途驾驶前在加油站快速加了油。|  He was gassing-up the crowd before the game. 他在比赛前鼓动人群。| 近义词: refueling, filling up, hyping up, energizing | 反义词: deflating, discouraging | 词性: noun/verb

gas:/ɡæs/ | n./v. | 气体；汽油；煤气；毒气；空谈；加油 | A substance or matter in a state in which it will expand freely to fill the whole of a container, having no fixed shape (unlike a solid) and no fixed volume (unlike a liquid).  Also, gasoline, or to fill with gasoline. | I need to put some gas in the car. 我需要给汽车加点汽油。 | The balloon was filled with helium gas. 气球里充满了氦气。 | 近义词: fuel, petrol, vapor, flatulence | 反义词: solid, liquid | 词性: noun/verb

up:/ʌp/ | adv./prep./adj./n./v. | 向上；起来；在上面；增加；结束 |  Expressing movement from a lower to a higher position, an increase in amount, or completion. | Prices are up this year. 今年价格上涨了。 |  Time's up! 时间到了！ | 近义词: above, higher, increased, finished | 反义词: down, below, decreased | 词性: adverb/preposition/adjective/noun/verb
