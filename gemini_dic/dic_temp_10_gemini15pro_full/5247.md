allochroic:/ˌæləˈkroʊɪk/ | adj. | 多色的，变色的 | Exhibiting a change of color; relating to allochroism.  A mineral that exhibits allochroism changes color when viewed in transmitted light of different wavelengths. | The alexandrite is a famous example of an allochroic gemstone, appearing green in daylight and red under incandescent light.  This allochroic mineral displays different colors depending on the angle of observation. | Synonyms: pleochroic, dichroic (in some cases) | Antonyms: monochromatic, homochromatic | Adjective
