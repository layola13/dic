borax:/ˈbɔːræks/ (US) /ˈbɔːræks/ (UK)| n.|硼砂; 粗制硼酸钠|A white crystalline compound, hydrated sodium borate, used as a flux, in making glass, as a cleansing agent, and as a preservative.|Borax is often used in laundry detergents. 硼砂常用于洗衣粉中。

|Synonyms: sodium borate, sodium tetraborate decahydrate|Antonyms: None (Borax is a specific chemical compound, it doesn't have a direct opposite.)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *boras*, from Old French *boras, borax*, from Medieval Latin *borax*, from Arabic *būrāq* (“borax”), perhaps from Persian *būrah* (“borax, nitre”).
