impurely:/ɪmˈpjʊərli/| adv.|不纯洁地，不纯正地|In a way that is not pure; containing unwanted or unpleasant substances.  It describes actions or states of being that are tainted, contaminated, or morally flawed.|He acted impurely, motivated by greed. 他行为不纯，受贪婪驱使。|Synonyms: indecently, improperly, unchastely, wickedly|Antonyms: purely, chastely, virtuously|词性: 副词

Etymology:
From im- (not) + pure + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Pure" comes from Latin "purus" meaning "clean, clear."
