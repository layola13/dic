hyperacuity:/ˌhaɪpərəˈkjuːɪti/ | n. | 超常敏锐度；超常视力 |  The ability to perceive very small differences in spatial or temporal stimuli, exceeding the limits predicted by the spacing of receptors in the sensory system. This implies some kind of neural enhancement or interpolation of the sensory input.  | Human vision exhibits hyperacuity, allowing us to see details finer than the spacing of the photoreceptors in our eyes. 人类的视觉展现出超常敏锐度，使我们能够看到比眼睛中感光细胞间距更精细的细节。| near synonyms: super-resolution, enhanced resolution, heightened sensitivity | antonyms: hypoacuity, reduced sensitivity | noun
