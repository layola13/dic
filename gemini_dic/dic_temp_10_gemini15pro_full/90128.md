hot-wire:/ˈhɑːtˌwaɪər/ /ˈhɒtˌwaɪə(r)/| v.|启动（汽车）,使兴奋,使激动|to start a car engine by connecting wires directly to the starter motor, bypassing the ignition system; to thrill or excite someone|Example 1: The thieves hot-wired the car and sped off.  小偷们用电线启动了汽车，然后飞驰而去。

Example 2: The fast-paced action sequences really hot-wired the audience.  快节奏的动作场面让观众兴奋不已。|近义词：bypass, short-circuit, energize, thrill, excite|反义词:deactivate, calm, bore|词性:动词

hot-wires:/ˈhɑːtˌwaɪərz/ /ˈhɒtˌwaɪəz/| n. (plural)|启动线|wires used to hot-wire a car|Example 1: The police found hot-wires in the suspect's backpack.  警察在嫌疑人的背包里发现了启动线。

Example 2: He used a pair of hot-wires to start the abandoned car.  他用一对启动线启动了那辆废弃的汽车。|近义词：jumper cables (in some contexts)|反义词: N/A|词性:名词

词源:  "Hot-wire" originated in the early 20th century, referring to the practice of starting a car engine by using a wire heated by an external source to ignite the fuel mixture. Later, the term expanded metaphorically to describe anything that produces excitement or thrills.
