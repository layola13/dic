dodginess:/ˈdɒdʒɪnɪs/ /ˈdɑːdʒinɪs/| n. |躲闪，狡猾，可疑|The dodginess of his explanation made me suspicious. 他的解释闪烁其词，让我起了疑心。|例句：The dodginess of the deal made me back out. 这笔交易的可疑性让我退出了。

例句：There was a certain dodginess about his character that I couldn't quite put my finger on. 他的性格有点狡猾，我说不上来具体是什么。|近义词：shiftiness, evasiveness, shadiness, slipperiness|反义词：forthrightness, honesty, openness|词性:名词


dodgy:/ˈdɒdʒi/ /ˈdɑːdʒi/| adj. |躲闪的，狡猾的，可疑的|The meat smelled a bit dodgy, so I didn't eat it. 肉闻起来有点不对劲，所以我没吃。|例句：The dodgy wiring in the old house was a fire hazard. 老房子里不牢靠的电线是火灾隐患。

例句：He gave me a dodgy excuse for being late. 他给我找了个站不住脚的迟到借口。|近义词：suspicious, questionable, unreliable, risky|反义词：reliable, trustworthy, dependable, safe|词性:形容词



ETYMOLOGY->
From "dodge" + "-iness."  "Dodge" originates from the Middle English word "doggen," meaning "to move suddenly or shift."  This evolved to mean "to evade or avoid," and the noun "dodginess" followed, referring to the quality of being evasive, shifty, or unreliable.
