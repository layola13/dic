citizenry: /ˈsɪtɪzənri/ /ˈsɪtɪzənri/
n.
公民；国民；市民
The body of citizens of a state or nation.  All the citizens of a place considered as a group.
The mayor addressed the citizenry about the new park. 市长向市民发表了关于新公园的讲话。
The citizenry voted overwhelmingly for the new law.  全体公民以压倒性多数投票赞成新法律。

近义词: citizens, population, inhabitants, residents
反义词: non-citizens, foreigners, aliens
词性: noun

Etymology:
From citizen + -ry.  "Citizen" comes from Old French "citeain," ultimately from Latin "civis" (citizen). The suffix "-ry" denotes a group or collective body.
