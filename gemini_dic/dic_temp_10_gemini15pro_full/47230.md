dazzle:/ˈdæzl/ (US) /ˈdæzəl/ (UK)| v. n.|使目眩，使眼花缭乱；使倾倒，使赞叹|To impress greatly; overwhelm with brilliance or skill.  To lose clear vision, especially from looking at bright light. |The fireworks dazzled the children. 焰火使孩子们眼花缭乱。|synonyms: amaze, astonish, blind,  impress, overpower |antonyms: bore, disappoint, tire |verb

dazzle:/ˈdæzl/ (US) /ˈdæzəl/ (UK)| n.|耀眼的光；令人眼花缭乱的东西|A sudden, overwhelming feeling of admiration or wonder.  A bright, confusing light or display.|The dazzle of the headlights made it hard to see.  耀眼的前灯使人难以看清。|synonyms: brilliance, flash, glare, glitter |antonyms: darkness, dullness |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dasen, from Old Norse *dasa (“to be dazed”), from Proto-Germanic *dasaną (“to become tired, exhausted”).  Cognate with Swedish dasa (“to be idle, doze”), Danish dase (“to doze, be idle”).
