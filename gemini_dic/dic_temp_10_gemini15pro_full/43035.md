cowgirls: /ˈkaʊɡɜːrlz/ (US), /ˈkaʊɡɜːlz/ (UK)| n. |女牛仔|A woman or girl who herds and tends cattle, especially on horseback, typically in the western United States.|The cowgirls rode their horses across the dusty plains. 女牛仔们骑着马穿过尘土飞扬的平原。|Example:  She dreamed of being a cowgirl and riding horses all day. 她梦想成为一名女牛仔，整天骑马。|Synonyms:  ranch hand, stockwoman, cowhand|Antonyms:  cowboy (traditionally male), city dweller|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "cow" + "girl." "Cow" comes from Old English *cū (female bovine animal), from Proto-Germanic *kōuz. "Girl" comes from Middle English girle, of uncertain origin.
