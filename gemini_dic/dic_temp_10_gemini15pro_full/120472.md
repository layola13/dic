minsky: /ˈmɪnski/ | n. |  明斯基 | Hyman Minsky (1919-1996), an American economist known for his work on financial instability and economic cycles, particularly his "Financial Instability Hypothesis."  |  Minsky argued that stability breeds instability. |  Many economists rediscovered Minsky's work after the 2008 financial crisis. |  Synonym: none (as it's a proper noun referring to a specific person) | Antonym: none | proper noun

minsky moment: /ˈmɪnski ˈmoʊmənt/ | n. | 明斯基时刻 | A sudden collapse of asset values which marks the end of the growth phase in Minsky's credit cycle or business cycle. It is typically preceded by a long period of prosperity and increasing debt levels. | The 2008 subprime mortgage crisis is often considered a Minsky moment. |  The dot-com bubble burst in 2000 could be seen as a Minsky moment. | Synonym: credit crunch, financial crisis | Antonym: economic boom | noun phrase
