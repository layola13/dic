maranatha:/ˌmærəˈnæθə/ | interj. n.|主来吧；愿主来！| An Aramaic phrase meaning "Our Lord has come" or "Our Lord, come!"  It's used as an expression of Christian hope for the Second Coming of Christ. |Example 1:  The early Christians used "Maranatha" as a prayer and a greeting. 早期基督徒用“马拉拿撒”作为祷告和问候语。

Example 2:  "Maranatha!" he whispered, full of hope for the future. 他低声说道：“马拉拿撒！”，心中充满了对未来的希望。| 近义词: Come, Lord Jesus,  Amen | 反义词: N/A | 词性: interjection, noun


Etymology:
From Aramaic *ܡܪܢ ܐܬܐ (*maran 'athā) meaning "our Lord has come" or  ܡܪܢ ܐܬܐ (*maran 'thā), an imperative meaning "Our Lord, come!".
