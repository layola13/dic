equivocator: /ɪˈkwɪvəˌkeɪtər/ | n. | 模棱两可的人；含糊其辞的人；诡辩者 |  A person who uses ambiguous language, often with the intent to deceive or mislead. |  The politician was an equivocator, never giving a straight answer to a difficult question.  这个政客是个含糊其辞的人，从不直接回答难题。 | synonym: prevaricator, deceiver, dissembler | antonym: truth-teller, forthright person | noun

equivocate: /ɪˈkwɪvəˌkeɪt/ | v. |  使用模棱两可的语言；含糊其辞；诡辩 | To use ambiguous language so as to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself. | He equivocated when asked about his involvement in the scandal. 当被问及他是否卷入丑闻时，他含糊其辞。 | synonym: prevaricate, hedge, fudge | antonym: be forthright, be direct | verb

equivocation: /ɪˌkwɪvəˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 模棱两可的话；含糊其辞；诡辩 | The use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself. | Her equivocation made it impossible to know her true feelings. 她的含糊其辞使得人们无法了解她的真实感受。 | synonym: prevarication, ambiguity, evasion | antonym: directness, clarity, honesty | noun
