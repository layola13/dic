midwifing:/ˈmɪdˌwaɪfɪŋ/
n.|助产; 接生;|The act of assisting women in childbirth.  The process of helping something new come into existence.|She was trained in traditional midwifing. 她接受过传统的助产训练。|synonyms: birthing, assisting in childbirth|antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps hindering, obstructing in the context of development)|noun


midwife:/ˈmɪdˌwaɪf/
n. vt.|助产士; 接生婆; 促成…的形成的人;|A person, typically a woman, who is trained to assist women in childbirth.  A person who helps to produce or bring about something.|The midwife helped deliver the baby. 助产士帮助接生了婴儿.  She midwifed the peace agreement. 她促成了和平协议的达成。|synonyms: accoucheur, birth attendant|antonyms: (No direct antonym)|noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *midwyf*, from Old English *midwif*, from *mid* (“with”) + *wif* (“woman”).  Literally, "with-woman".  The meaning evolved from simply being with a woman during childbirth to actively assisting in the process.
