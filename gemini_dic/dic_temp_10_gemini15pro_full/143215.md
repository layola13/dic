outcrying:/ˈaʊtˌkraɪɪŋ/
noun
大声疾呼，强烈抗议
a strong expression of public disapproval or anger
The outcrying over the new tax law was deafening. 新税法的强烈抗议震耳欲聋。
近义词: protest, clamor, objection, outcry
反义词: approval, support, acceptance
词性: noun

outcry:/ˈaʊtkraɪ/
noun
大声疾呼，强烈抗议
a strong expression of public disapproval or anger
There was a public outcry against the proposed cuts in education. 公众强烈抗议提议的教育削减。
近义词: protest, clamor, objection, outcrying
反义词: approval, support, acceptance
词性: noun

词源: From Middle English *outcri*, from Old Norse *útkrá*, from *út* (out) + *krá* (crow).  Essentially, "to crow outwards/loudly."  The "-ing" form, "outcrying," acts as a gerund or present participle, emphasizing the act of protesting.
