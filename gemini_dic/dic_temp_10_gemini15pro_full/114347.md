majino: /mɑːˈdʒiːnoʊ/ |  n. |  マジノ (Maginot) |  Refers to the Maginot Line, a line of concrete fortifications, obstacles, and weapon installations built by France in the 1930s to deter invasion by Germany.  It is often used metaphorically to refer to a defensive barrier or strategy that proves ineffective. | The company's cybersecurity system proved to be a Maginot Line, easily bypassed by the hackers.  该公司网络安全系统被证明是马其诺防线，很容易被黑客绕过。|  barrier, fortification, defense | delusion, fallacy | noun
