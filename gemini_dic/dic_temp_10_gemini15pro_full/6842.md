anathematism:/əˈnæθəmətɪzəm/
n.
诅咒，咒逐，革出教门
The act of anathematizing; a solemn curse; excommunication.
The Pope pronounced an anathematism against the heretics. 教皇宣布对异教徒施以咒逐。
近义词：curse, excommunication, denunciation, condemnation
反义词：blessing, benediction, commendation
词性：名词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *anathematismus*, from Late Greek *ἀναθεματισμός (anathematismos)*, from *ἀναθεματίζειν (anathematizein)* "to curse, to excommunicate," from *ἀνάθεμα (anathema)* "a thing devoted to evil, a curse," from *ἀνατίθημι (anatithemi)* "I set up, dedicate," from *ἀνά (ana)* "up" + *τίθημι (tithemi)* "I put, place."
