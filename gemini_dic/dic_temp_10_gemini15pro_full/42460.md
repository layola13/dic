counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/ | adj. n. |反事实的，反事实的陈述| relating to or expressing what has not happened or is not the case | Historians sometimes use counterfactual history to explore what might have been. 历史学家有时会用反事实的历史来探讨可能发生的事情。|Counterfactual thinking can be helpful in learning from mistakes. 反事实思考有助于从错误中学习。|近义词: hypothetical, speculative, conjectural |反义词: factual, real, actual |词性:形容词, 名词

counterfactuals:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəlz/ | n. |反事实的陈述（复数）|  plural form of counterfactual; statements about what might have been|  He often dwells on counterfactuals, wondering what would have happened if he'd made different choices. 他经常沉湎于反事实的思考，想知道如果他做了不同的选择会发生什么。|  The book explores various counterfactuals about the outcome of the war. 这本书探讨了关于战争结果的各种反事实假设。| 近义词:hypotheticals, speculations, conjectures |反义词: facts, realities, actualities | 词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + factual. "Counter-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposite," derived from the Latin word "contra." "Factual" comes from the Latin word "factum," meaning "deed" or "fact." Thus, "counterfactual" literally means "against the facts."
