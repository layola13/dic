hatch:/hætʃ/ | n. v. | 舱口，孵化 | An opening or a cover for an opening, as in a deck, floor, or roof.  To emerge from an egg; to cause (young) to emerge from an egg. | The chick hatched from the egg. 小鸡从蛋里孵化出来。  They hatched a plan to escape. 他们策划了一个逃跑计划。 | synonyms: brood, incubate, breed, plot, scheme | antonyms: destroy, kill, stifle | noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hæċċ, *haċċ, from Proto-Germanic *hakō ("hook, latch"), from Proto-Indo-European *keg- ("to bend, hook").
