intemperatenesses:/ɪnˈtɛmpərɪtnəsnɪsɪz/
n.
lack of moderation or restraint, especially in the indulgence of passions or appetites; excessive indulgence.
放纵；无节制；过度
His intemperatenesses ultimately led to his downfall. 他的放纵最终导致了他的垮台。
Synonyms: excesses, immoderations, extravagances
Antonyms: moderation, restraint, temperance
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *intemperateness*, from *intemperat* + *-ness*. *Intemperat* from Latin *intemperant-*, *intemperans*, present participle of *intemperare* (“to be immoderate”), from *in-* (“not”) + *temperare* (“to moderate, temper, regulate”), from *tempus* (“time, season”).
