gallnut:/ˈɡɔːlnʌt/ | n. | 瘤；五倍子；没食子 | A hard, round outgrowth on the leaves or twigs of certain oak trees, caused by the larvae of gall wasps. Used in tanning and dyeing and for making ink. |  Oak gallnuts are used in traditional medicine. 橡树五倍子用于传统医学。| synonym: gall, oak apple | antonym: (No direct antonym) | noun
