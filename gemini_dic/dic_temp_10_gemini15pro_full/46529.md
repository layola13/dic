dala-dala:/ˌdɑːlɑːˈdɑːlɑː/| n.|达拉达拉（一种东非的公共交通工具）|A dala-dala is a kind of public transport found in East Africa. It is usually a minivan or pickup truck that is converted to carry passengers.  These are often privately owned and operate on fixed routes, picking up and dropping off passengers along the way.|I took a dala-dala from Dar es Salaam to Bagamoyo.|近义词：matatu (Kenya), trotro (Ghana), minibus taxi (South Africa)|反义词:private car, taxi|词性:noun

dala-dala:/ˌdɑːlɑːˈdɑːlɑː/| n.|达拉达拉（一种东非的公共交通工具）|Dala-dalas are a common and affordable way to travel around East African cities and towns. They are known for being crowded and sometimes unsafe, but they offer a vital transportation service for many people.|The dala-dala was packed with people, but it was the quickest way to get across town.|近义词：bus, public transport|反义词:private car|词性:noun
