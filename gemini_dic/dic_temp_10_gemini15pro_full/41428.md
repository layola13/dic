cornerstone:/ˈkɔːrnərˌstoʊn/ (US) /ˈkɔːnəstəʊn/ (UK)| n.|基石，奠基石；基础，根本|Something that is very important and forms the main part or basis of something else.  A stone that forms the base of a corner of a building, often laid in a special ceremony.|The signing of the treaty was the cornerstone of peace between the two nations.  该条约的签署是两国和平的基石。|synonyms: foundation, base, bedrock, underpinning, linchpin|antonyms: apex, peak, summit|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cornerston*, from Old English *cornērstān*, equivalent to *corner* + *stone*.  Cognate with Dutch *hoeksteen*, German *Eckstein*.
