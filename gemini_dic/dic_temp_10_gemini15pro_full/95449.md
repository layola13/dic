incision: /ɪnˈsɪʒən/ (US) /ɪnˈsɪʒn/ (UK)| n.| 雕刻，刻痕 |An act or instance of incising; a cut, gash, or notch.| The archaeologist examined the incisions on the ancient stone. 考古学家检查了古代石头上的刻痕。| synonyms: carving, engraving, etching | antonyms: smoothing, polishing, filling | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *incisionem (nominative incisio), noun of action from past participle stem of incidere "to cut into," from in- "into" + caedere "to cut."
