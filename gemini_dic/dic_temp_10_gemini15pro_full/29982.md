cavilled:/ˈkævəld/ | v. | 吹毛求疵，挑剔 | To raise trivial and unnecessary objections; to find fault without good reason. |  He cavilled at the wording of the contract, delaying the agreement.  他挑剔合同的措辞，拖延了协议的达成。 |  The critic cavilled at minor details in the performance.  评论家对表演中的细枝末节吹毛求疵。 |  nitpick, carp, quibble |  praise, commend | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cavillārī, frequentative of Latin cavillārī (“to jest, joke, mock”), from cavilla (“jest, scoff”), perhaps ultimately from cavus (“hollow”).
