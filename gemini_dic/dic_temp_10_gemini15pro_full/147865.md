overrationalized: /ˌoʊvərˈræʃənəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 过度理性化的，过分合理化的 | Explained or justified excessively using rational arguments, often to the point of ignoring or downplaying emotional or intuitive factors. |  The company's overrationalized decision-making process stifled creativity and led to a decline in innovation. 该公司过度理性化的决策过程扼杀了创造力，导致创新衰退。

| Her overrationalized explanation for her behavior seemed contrived and unconvincing. 她对她行为的过度理性化的解释显得做作且 unconvincing。 | 近义词: over-analyzed, over-intellectualized | 反义词: intuitive, instinctive, emotional | 词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + rationalized, past participle of rationalize.
"Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much."
"Rationalize" comes from Late Latin "rationalizare," meaning "to make rational," from Latin "rationalis" meaning "of or belonging to reason."
