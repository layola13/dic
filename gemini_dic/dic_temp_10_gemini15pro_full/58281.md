drunkly: /ˈdrʌŋkli/ | adv. | 醉醺醺地，醉态可掬地 | In a manner of someone who is drunk; showing the effects of having consumed too much alcohol. | He stumbled drunkly into the room. 他醉醺醺地跌跌撞撞走进房间。| She laughed drunkly and slurred her words. 她醉醺醺地笑着，说话含糊不清。 | 近义词: drunkenly, tipsily | 反义词: soberly | 词性: 副词


Etymology:
From drunk + -ly.  "Drunk" comes from Old English "druncen," past participle of "drincan" (to drink), from Proto-Germanic "*drenkanan," from Proto-Indo-European "*dʰrénǵʰeti" (to make muddy, to disturb). The suffix "-ly" is a common adverbial suffix in English.
