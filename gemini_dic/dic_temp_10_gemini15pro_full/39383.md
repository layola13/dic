conjunctiveness:/kənˌdʒʌŋkˈtɪvnəs/| n.|连接性，结合性|The quality of being conjunctive; the state of being joined together.  The tendency to combine or connect different ideas or elements.|The conjunctiveness of the argument was impressive, seamlessly weaving together various philosophical threads.  The artist's work displayed a remarkable conjunctiveness of form and color.|近义词：unity, coherence, connection, cohesion|反义词：disunity, incoherence, disconnection, separation|词性:noun
Etymology: From "conjunctive" + "-ness".  "Conjunctive" comes from Late Latin *conjunctivus*, from Latin conjunctus (past participle of conjungere, "to join together"), from com- ("together") + jungere ("to join").
