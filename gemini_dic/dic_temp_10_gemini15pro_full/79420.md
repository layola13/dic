glissando:/ɡlɪˈsændəʊ/ (UK), /ɡlɪˈsɑːndoʊ/ (US)| n.|滑音；滑奏|A continuous slide upwards or downwards between two notes.|The pianist finished the piece with a dramatic glissando.|Synonyms: slide, sweep, portamento|Antonyms: staccato|noun

glissando:/ɡlɪˈsændəʊ/ (UK), /ɡlɪˈsɑːndoʊ/ (US)| adv.|滑奏地|In a gliding manner.|She played the passage glissando.|Synonyms: smoothly, slidingly|Antonyms: abruptly, jerkily|adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *glissando*, present participle of *glissare* "to glide," from French *glisser*. Ultimately from Frankish *glisjan*.
