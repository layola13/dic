crippling:/ˈkrɪplɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人震惊的；造成严重后果的；严重的；使人衰弱的 | causing a severe and almost insuperable problem | The country's crippling debt is preventing further investment.  这个国家沉重的债务阻碍了进一步的投资。 | Example 2:  A crippling blow to their hopes. 对他们的希望来说是毁灭性的打击。 | synonyms: devastating, damaging, destructive, disabling | antonyms:  helpful, beneficial | adjective


cripple:/ˈkrɪpəl/ |  vt.  n. | 使跛；使残废；严重削弱；跛子；残废人|  to cause (someone) to become unable to move or walk properly |  The accident crippled him for life.  这起事故使他终身残疾。|  Example 2:  Lack of funding has crippled the project.  缺乏资金使该项目陷入瘫痪。 | synonyms: disable, incapacitate, impair, lame | antonyms:  heal, cure, strengthen | verb, noun
