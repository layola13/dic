domino:/ˈdɒmɪnəʊ/ (UK) /ˈdɑːmɪnoʊ/ (US)| n. |多米诺骨牌；多米诺骨牌游戏；(事件)骨牌效应；披风斗篷；面具；|A flat rectangular block, typically one of a set of 28, used in a game.  Also refers to the game itself, and metaphorically to a chain reaction of events.|He knocked over the first domino, starting a chain reaction. 他碰倒了第一张多米诺骨牌，引发了连锁反应。|Synonyms: tile, piece, block|Antonyms: (For the game) None; (For the effect) stability, standstill|Noun

dominoes:/ˈdɒmɪnəʊz/ (UK) /ˈdɑːmɪnoʊz/ (US)| n. |多米诺骨牌游戏；(事件)骨牌效应|The game played with dominoes, or a series of events that happen as a consequence of one initiating event.|We played dominoes all afternoon. 我们整个下午都在玩多米诺骨牌。|Synonyms: domino game, chain reaction|Antonyms: (For the game) None; (For the effect) stability, standstill|Noun
