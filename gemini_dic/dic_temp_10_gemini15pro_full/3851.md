noun
古希腊集市的（所有格）
Possessive form of "agorae," the plural of "agora."  An agora was a central public space in ancient Greek city-states.  The agorae's function was primarily as a marketplace, but it also served as a social and political gathering place.
The agorae's bustling atmosphere was a center of commerce and debate.  古希腊集市的喧嚣气氛是商业和辩论的中心。
The agorae's stones still echo with the voices of the past. 古希腊集市的石头仍然回响着过去的声音。
Synonym: marketplaces'
Antonym: (No direct antonym, but consider) private residences'
