cyrenaic: /ˌsaɪrəˈneɪɪk/ | adj. n. |  昔兰尼派的，享乐主义的；昔兰尼学派信徒 | Relating to Cyrenaicism, a school of philosophy that held that pleasure is the highest good.  A follower of Cyrenaicism. |  例句：He lived a rather cyrenaic lifestyle, indulging in every pleasure. 他过着一种相当享乐主义的生活方式，沉溺于各种享乐。

例句：The cyrenaic philosophy emphasizes the pursuit of immediate gratification. 昔兰尼哲学强调追求即时满足。 | 近义词：hedonistic, epicurean | 反义词：ascetic, stoic | 词性: adjective, noun

cyrenaic: /ˌsaɪrəˈneɪɪk/ | n. | 昔兰尼学派信徒 | A follower of Cyrenaicism. | 例句：The cyrenaics believed that pleasure was the ultimate goal in life. 昔兰尼学派信徒认为快乐是人生的终极目标。

例句：He was known as a cyrenaic, always seeking out new and exciting experiences. 他被称为一个享乐主义者，总是寻求新的和令人兴奋的经历。| 近义词：hedonist, epicurean | 反义词：ascetic, stoic | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek "Kyrēnaikos," relating to Cyrene, a Greek colony in North Africa, where the school of philosophy originated.  From "Kyrēnē" + "-ikos."
