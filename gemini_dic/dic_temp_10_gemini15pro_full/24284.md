brigandine:/ˈbrɪɡənˌdaɪn/| n.|锁子甲，一种中世纪的盔甲|A brigandine is a form of body armor from the Middle Ages consisting of small metal plates riveted to a fabric or leather backing.|The knight wore a brigandine under his surcoat for added protection.  骑士在外衣下穿了一件锁子甲以加强保护。|Synonyms: armor, coat of mail, hauberk|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "civilian clothing")|Noun
