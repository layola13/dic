milkwort:/ˈmɪlkˌwɜːrt/ (US), /ˈmɪlkˌwɜːt/ (UK)| n. |远志; 牛奶草|A low-growing plant with small, usually blue, purple, pink, or white flowers, several species of which are traditionally believed to increase milk flow in nursing mothers.  It belongs to the family Polygalaceae.|The farmer planted milkwort in the pasture, hoping it would benefit his cows.  农民在牧场里种了远志，希望它能对他的奶牛有益。|近义词: snakeroot (some species) |反义词: N/A| noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *milkwort*, from Old English *meolcwurt*, from *meolc* (“milk”) + *wyrt* (“plant, herb, root”).  So called from the traditional belief that it promotes lactation in cattle.
