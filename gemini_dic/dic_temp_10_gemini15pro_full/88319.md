hippie:/ˈhɪpi/ | n. |嬉皮士；嬉皮族|A person, typically young, of the counterculture of the 1960s, associated with a rejection of conventional values and the taking of hallucinogenic drugs.|Example: The hippie wore flowers in her hair and preached peace and love.  那个嬉皮士在头发上戴着花，宣扬和平与爱。|Example:  Many hippies gathered at Woodstock in 1969. 许多嬉皮士在1969年聚集在伍德斯托克。|Synonym: bohemian, flower child|Antonym: conformist, traditionalist|noun

hippier:/ˈhɪpiər/ | adj. (comparative) |更嬉皮的；更像嬉皮士的|More characteristic of a hippie.|Example: He's got a much hippier vibe than his brother. 他比他哥哥更有嬉皮士的氛围。|Example: Her clothes are hippier than mine. 她的衣服比我的更嬉皮。|Synonym: more bohemian, more unconventional|Antonym: more conventional, more traditional|adjective

词源:
From *hip*, meaning "aware, in the know," + *-ie* (diminutive suffix).  The term originally referred to beatniks who migrated to San Francisco's Haight-Ashbury district.  Later, it evolved to encompass the broader counterculture movement.
