hookworm:/ˈhʊkwɜːrm/ | n. | 钩虫 | A parasitic worm with hooks or suckers, usually living in the intestines of humans or animals. | The doctor diagnosed the patient with a hookworm infection. 医生诊断该病人感染了钩虫。 |  The hookworm burrows into the intestinal wall and feeds on blood. 钩虫钻入肠壁并吸食血液。| 近义词: parasite | 反义词:  (无直接反义词) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From hook + worm.  "Hook" referring to the hook-like structures the worm uses to attach to the host's intestines.  "Worm" referring to its worm-like appearance.
