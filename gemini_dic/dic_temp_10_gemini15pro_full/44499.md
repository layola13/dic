crosshair: /ˈkrɒsˌheə(r)/ (UK) /ˈkrɔːsˌher/ (US)| n. |十字线，瞄准线|Two intersecting lines or wires in the focus of an optical instrument, such as a telescope or a gunsight, that form an aiming point. |The sniper carefully aligned the crosshairs on his target. 狙击手仔细地将十字线对准目标。

The hunter adjusted the crosshairs of his rifle scope. 猎人调整了步枪瞄准镜的十字线。|近义词：reticle, graticule|反义词: None|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From cross + hair, referring to the thin intersecting lines, often made of hair or wire, used in optical instruments.
