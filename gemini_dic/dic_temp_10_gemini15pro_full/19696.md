bilharzias: /ˌbɪlhɑːrˈzaɪəsɪs/ /ˌbɪlhɑːrˈziːəsɪs/ | n. | 血吸虫病 | A parasitic disease caused by flatworms of the genus Schistosoma, contracted through contact with contaminated fresh water.  It is common in many tropical and subtropical countries and can cause serious damage to the liver, intestines, lungs, and bladder. |  Bilharzias is a major public health problem in Africa. 血吸虫病是非洲的一个主要公共卫生问题。|  Many children in the village suffer from bilharzias. 这个村子里的许多孩子都患有血吸虫病。| synonyms: schistosomiasis | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a disease.  One might consider "health" or "wellness" as a contrasting state.) | noun
