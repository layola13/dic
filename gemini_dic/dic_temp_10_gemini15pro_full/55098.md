disrest:/dɪsˈrɛst/
noun
不安;骚动;动乱
a state of dissatisfaction, disturbance, and agitation, typically involving public demonstrations or disorder.
例句：The economic downturn led to widespread social unrest. 经济衰退导致了广泛的社会动荡。

例句：Political unrest is growing in the region. 该地区的政治动荡日益加剧。

近义词：turmoil, ferment, agitation, disturbance, upheaval, chaos, disorder
反义词：peace, calm, tranquility, order, stability
词性:n.

Etymology:
From dis- + rest (n.), from Old French reste (“remainder, rest”), from Latin restō (“I remain”).  The prefix "dis-" denotes negation or reversal, so "disrest" literally signifies a lack of rest or tranquility.
