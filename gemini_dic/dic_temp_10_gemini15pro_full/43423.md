crape:/kreɪp/| n.|绉纱，绉布；黑纱，丧章|A thin, light fabric with a crinkled surface, typically used for dresses or decorations. Also, a piece of black crepe worn as a sign of mourning.|She wore a beautiful crape dress to the party. 她穿着一件漂亮的绉纱连衣裙去参加聚会。|The door was adorned with black crepe. 门上装饰着黑纱。|近义词：chiffon, gauze|反义词:|名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *crepe, from Old Northern French crespe (“curled”), from Old French crespe, past participle of cresper (“to curl”), from Vulgar Latin *crispāre, from Latin crispus (“curled, wrinkled”).
