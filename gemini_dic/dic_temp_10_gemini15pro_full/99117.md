intercedent:/ˌɪntərˈsiːd(ə)nt/| n. adj.|调解人，仲裁者；调解的，仲裁的|A person who intervenes on behalf of another, especially with a view to reconciling differences or obtaining a favor.  Intervening on behalf of another.|例句：The lawyer acted as an intercedent between the two warring parties. 律师充当了交战双方之间的调解人。

例句：Her intercedent role helped to resolve the conflict peacefully. 她作为调解人的角色帮助和平解决了冲突。|近义词：mediator, arbitrator, negotiator, intermediary|反义词：opponent, antagonist|词性: noun, adjective

intercede:/ˌɪntərˈsiːd/| v. |调解，仲裁；求情，说情|To intervene on behalf of another, especially with a view to reconciling differences or obtaining a favor. |例句：She interceded with the authorities on behalf of the refugees. 她为难民向当局求情。

例句：He interceded in the dispute between his two friends. 他调解了两个朋友之间的争端。|近义词：mediate, arbitrate, negotiate, intervene|反义词：ignore, neglect|词性: verb
