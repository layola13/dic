ditransitivity:/ˌdaɪtrænˈsɪtɪvɪti/ | n. | 双及物性；双宾语性 | The property of a verb that takes both a direct object and an indirect object. |  He gave her the book. (The verb "give" exhibits ditransitivity because it takes both "her" (indirect object) and "the book" (direct object).) | 近义词: divalency, bitransitivity | 反义词: intransitivity, monotransitivity | 词性: noun

词源: From di- (“two”) + transitive +‎ -ity.
