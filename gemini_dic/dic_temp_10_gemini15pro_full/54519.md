disjunctive:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋktɪv/ | adj. | 分离的；析取的 | Serving to disconnect; separating. In logic, designating a relation between two propositions that is true if and only if at least one of the propositions is true. | The lawyer presented disjunctive arguments, offering several independent reasons why his client should be acquitted.  律师提出了析取论证，提供了几个独立的理由说明他的客户应该被无罪释放。 | synonyms: alternative, contrasting, separating, dividing | antonyms: conjunctive, connecting, uniting | adjective

disjunction:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋkʃən/ | n. | 分离；析取 | The act of disjoining or the state of being disjoined; separation. A compound proposition that is true if and only if at least one of its component propositions is true. | There is a clear disjunction between the two schools of thought.  这两种思想流派之间有明显的区别。 | synonyms: separation, division, disconnection, break, split | antonyms: conjunction, connection, union, combination | noun
