defoliators:/diːˈfoʊliˌeɪtərz/
noun
落叶剂；食叶动物；落叶昆虫
An agent, such as a chemical spray or an insect, that causes the premature falling of leaves.  Something, such as an insect, that eats leaves.
例句：The defoliators stripped the trees bare in a matter of weeks.  这些落叶剂在几周内就把树木的叶子剥光了。

例句：Caterpillars are common defoliators in many gardens.  毛毛虫是许多花园里常见的食叶动物。
近义词：leaf-eaters, defoliating agents, defoliants
反义词: N/A
词性: noun


defoliate:/diːˈfoʊliˌeɪt/
verb
使落叶；除去…的叶子
To cause the premature falling of leaves, especially by the use of chemicals.
例句：The trees were defoliated by the strong winds.  强风使树木落叶。

例句：Farmers sometimes defoliate crops to make harvesting easier.  农民有时会使作物落叶以便于收割。
近义词：strip, denude, bare
反义词: N/A
词性: verb


defoliation:/diːˌfoʊliˈeɪʃən/
noun
落叶；去叶
The act or process of defoliating; the state of being defoliated.
例句：Defoliation can be caused by insects, disease, or chemicals.  落叶可能是由昆虫、疾病或化学物质引起的。

例句：The defoliation of the trees made the forest floor visible.  树木落叶后，森林的地面变得清晰可见。
近义词：leaf fall, leaf drop
反义词: N/A
词性: noun
