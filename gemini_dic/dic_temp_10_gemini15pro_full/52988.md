dinkly:/ˈdɪŋkli/| adj.|小的; 微不足道的; 无足轻重的|Small and unimportant; insignificant.  Often used to describe something cute or charmingly small.  It can also have a slightly negative connotation, suggesting something is trivial or not worth bothering with.|例句：She lives in a dinkly little apartment in the city. 她住在城里一套小小的公寓里。

例句：He dismissed my concerns as dinkly little problems. 他认为我的担忧都是些微不足道的小问题。|近义词：small, tiny, insignificant, trivial, petite, miniature|反义词：large, significant, important, substantial|词性:形容词

词源:Likely a playful, possibly onomatopoeic formation, perhaps influenced by words like "dinky" (small and insignificant) and "twinkly" (sparkling or shining).  Its precise origin is uncertain.
