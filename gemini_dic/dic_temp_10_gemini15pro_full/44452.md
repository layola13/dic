cross-subsidy:/ˌkrɒs ˈsʌbsɪdi/ | n. | 交叉补贴 |  The practice of charging higher prices for one product or service in order to subsidize the cost of another, often related, product or service.  This allows the company to offer the subsidized product or service at a lower price than it would otherwise be able to. |  The airline uses cross-subsidies, with its profitable international routes supporting its less popular domestic ones.  这家航空公司采用交叉补贴，其盈利的国际航线支撑着不太受欢迎的国内航线。| Example:  Cross-subsidization between urban and rural customers is common in telecommunications.  在电信领域，城市和农村客户之间的交叉补贴很常见。 | Synonyms: internal subsidy,  price discrimination | Antonyms: direct pricing,  market pricing | noun
