distress:/dɪˈstrɛs/| n.|痛苦，忧虑，悲伤|Extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain.|例句：Her distress was evident in her tear-stained face.  她泪流满面的脸上显露出痛苦。|近义词：anguish, suffering, pain, grief, misery|反义词：comfort, happiness, joy, relief, peace|词性:名词

distress:/dɪˈstrɛs/| vt.|使痛苦，使忧虑，使悲伤|Cause (someone) anxiety, sorrow, or pain.|例句：The news of the accident distressed her greatly. 事故的消息使她非常痛苦。|近义词：upset, worry, trouble, bother, perturb|反义词：comfort, soothe, reassure, calm, please|词性:动词

词源：From Old French destresse (“hardship, oppression, grief”), from Vulgar Latin *districtia, from Latin districtus (“drawn tight, restricted”), past participle of distringō (“I draw tight”).  The verb is a back-formation from the adjective.
