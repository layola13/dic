hesitate:/ˈhezɪteɪt/| v. |犹豫，迟疑|To pause before saying or doing something, especially because you are nervous or not certain.|She hesitated before answering the question. 她犹豫了一下才回答问题。|Synonyms: pause, waver, falter|Antonyms: decide, resolve|verb

hesitating:/ˈhezɪteɪtɪŋ/| adj. |犹豫的，迟疑的|Showing or feeling hesitation.|He gave a hesitating glance at the door. 他迟疑地瞥了一眼门口。|Synonyms: uncertain, hesitant, tentative|Antonyms: decisive, resolute|adjective

hesitation:/ˌhezɪˈteɪʃn/| n. |犹豫，迟疑|The act of hesitating.|Without hesitation, he jumped into the river to save the child. 他毫不犹豫地跳进河里救孩子。|Synonyms: pause, delay, indecision|Antonyms: decisiveness, resolution|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *haesitatus*, past participle of *haesitare* "to stick fast, remain fixed, stammer," frequentative of *haerere* "to stick, cling, adhere."
