conventionalism:/kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪzəm/ | n. |因循守旧，墨守成规; 习俗主义; 约定俗成 |The adherence to conventional practices or attitudes, especially in art or literature.  The theory that fundamental principles of a certain kind are based on agreements in a society, rather than on objective reality.|  His art is a rebellion against conventionalism. 他的艺术是对传统主义的反抗。| synonyms: conformity, orthodoxy, traditionalism | antonyms: unconventionality, originality, innovation | noun


conventionalism:/kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪzəm/ | n. |约定俗成（哲学）| In philosophy, the view that some principles, such as those of logic, mathematics, or ethics, are based on social agreement or convention rather than on objective truth or natural law. | Some philosophers argue that morality is a form of conventionalism. 一些哲学家认为道德是一种约定俗成。| synonyms: relativism, subjectivism | antonyms: objectivism, realism | noun
