calculative:/ˈkælkjʊlətɪv/ /ˈkælkjəleɪtɪv/| adj.|精于算计的，斤斤计较的|Tending to make careful calculations, especially about the likely gain or loss involved in doing something.|She made a calculative move to invest in the booming market. 她精明地投资了蓬勃发展的市场。|近义词：scheming, shrewd, cunning, tactical|反义词：impulsive, spontaneous, unthinking, naive|词性:形容词

Etymology->
From Late Latin *calculativus*, from Latin calculatus (“calculated”), past participle of calculare (“to calculate”), from calculus (“pebble, counter used in reckoning”), diminutive of calx (“limestone, heel”).
