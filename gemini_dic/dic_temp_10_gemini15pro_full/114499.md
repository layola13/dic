malacozoa:/ˌmæləˈkəʊzə/ | n. |软体动物|A large phylum of invertebrate animals (as snails, clams, and squids) that have a soft unsegmented body lacking internal skeleton and commonly protected by a calcareous shell.|Malacozoa are a diverse group, including slugs, snails, and octopuses.| 近义词: mollusca, mollusks | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for a taxonomic phylum) | noun
