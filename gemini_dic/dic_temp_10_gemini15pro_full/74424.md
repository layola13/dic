francic: /ˈfræŋkɪk/ | adj. | 法兰克人的；法兰克语的 | Relating to the Franks, a Germanic people who conquered Gaul (modern-day France) in the 5th century, or their language. | The Francic law code was influential in medieval Europe.  法兰克法律对中世纪欧洲很有影响。|  The Francic language evolved into Old French. 法兰克语演变成了古法语。| 近义词: Frankish | 反义词: N/A | 词性: adjective

Etymology: From Late Latin *francicus, from Francus (“Frank”). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *frankō (“javelin, spear”).
