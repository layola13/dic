hypermodernists:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɒdənɪsts/ | n. | 超现代主义者 |  Adherents of hypermodernism, a cultural and artistic movement characterized by its embrace of technology, speed, and the fragmentation of experience. It often reflects a sense of alienation and disillusionment with traditional values.  | The hypermodernists rejected traditional artistic forms. 超现代主义者拒绝传统的艺术形式。|  The museum's exhibition explored the works of various hypermodernists, showcasing their fascination with technology and urban life.  博物馆的展览探索了各种超现代主义者的作品，展示了他们对科技和都市生活的迷恋。 | 近义词: avant-gardists, futurists | 反义词: traditionalists, conservatives | 词性: noun

Etymology: "Hyper-" (Greek: ὑπέρ, hupér, "over, beyond") + "modern" (from Late Latin modernus, "recent, contemporary") + "-ist" (suffix denoting a follower or adherent of a doctrine or belief)
