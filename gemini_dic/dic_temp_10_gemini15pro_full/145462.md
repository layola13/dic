overcivilly:/oʊvərˈsɪvɪlli/
adv.|过于文明地，过分客气地|Excessively polite or civilized, often to the point of being unnatural or insincere.|He overcivilly offered her his seat, bowing deeply.|近义词: excessively politely, too formally|反义词: rudely, impolitely|词性:副词
词源: "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "civilly" (adverb form of "civil," from Latin "civilis" meaning "relating to citizens").
