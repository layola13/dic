litter:/ˈlɪtər/ (US), /ˈlɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.|垃圾，杂物；一窝（幼崽）；（动物的）褥草；轿子|Rubbish such as paper, cans, and bottles, that is left lying in an open or public place.  To make (a place) untidy with rubbish or discarded objects.  A group of young animals born at the same time to the same mother.  Material strewn to absorb the urine and feces of animals.|

例句：
The streets were covered with litter.  街道上到处都是垃圾。

The park was littered with empty cans and bottles. 公园里到处都是空罐子和瓶子。

She littered the floor with her clothes. 她把衣服扔得满地都是。

The cat gave birth to a litter of five kittens.  这只猫生了一窝五只小猫。

Spread some fresh litter in the hamster cage. 在仓鼠笼子里铺一些新鲜的褥草。

近义词：rubbish, trash, garbage, debris, mess, clutter (n.); scatter, strew, spread (v.)
反义词：tidiness, cleanliness, order (n.)
词性：名词，动词

litter:/ˈlɪtər/ (US), /ˈlɪtə(r)/ (UK)| v.|乱扔垃圾|To scatter about carelessly or randomly|

例句：
Don't litter!  不要乱扔垃圾！

People who litter should be fined. 乱扔垃圾的人应该被罚款。


近义词：scatter, strew, spread
反义词：collect, gather, tidy
词性：动词
