ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *drayman*, from *dray* (a heavy cart or sled) + *man*.  The word *dray* likely comes from Old English *dræge* meaning "a drawing" or "something dragged," from the verb *dragan* "to draw" or "to drag." Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *draganą*.
