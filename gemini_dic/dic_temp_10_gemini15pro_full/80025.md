go-to-bat-for:/ɡoʊ tə bæt fɔːr/ | phrasal verb | 替...辩护，支持，为...出力 | To support or defend someone, especially in a difficult situation. |  The union rep went to bat for the workers who were being unfairly treated. 工会代表为受到不公平待遇的工人辩护。| 近义词: champion, back up | 反义词: denounce, condemn | 词性: 动词短语


ETYMOLOGY->
Originates from the baseball analogy of a batter "going to bat" for their team.  Going to bat implies taking a turn at hitting, and by extension, taking action and making an effort on someone's behalf.
