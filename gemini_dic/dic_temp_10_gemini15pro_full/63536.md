envassal:/ɪnˈvæsəl/ | vt. |  使成为附庸；征服；奴役 | To make someone a vassal; to subjugate or enslave. | The king envassaled the neighboring tribes, forcing them to pay tribute. 国王征服了邻近的部落，强迫他们纳贡。 | The conquering army envassaled the defeated nation, demanding absolute loyalty. 征服军奴役了战败的国家，要求绝对的忠诚。 | 近义词: subjugate, conquer, enslave | 反义词: liberate, free | 词性: 动词

词源:  From en- + vassal.  "Vassal" comes from Old French "vassal," ultimately from Medieval Latin "vassallus," meaning "servant, dependent."  The prefix "en-" adds the sense of "making into" or "putting into the condition of."
