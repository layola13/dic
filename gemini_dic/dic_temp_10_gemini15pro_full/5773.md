amalgamator:/əˈmælɡəmeɪtər/
n.|合并者；混合者；融合者|A person or thing that amalgamates or unites things, especially companies or organizations.|The company is an amalgamator of small businesses.  这家公司是一个小型企业的合并者。|consolidator, merger, unifier|separator, divider|noun

amalgamate:/əˈmælɡəmeɪt/
v.|合并；混合；融合|To combine or unite to form one organization or structure.|Several small companies amalgamated to form a larger corporation.  几家小公司合并成了一家大公司。|combine, merge, unite|separate, divide|verb

amalgamation:/əˌmælɡəˈmeɪʃn/
n.|合并；混合；融合|The action, process, or result of combining or uniting.|The amalgamation of the two companies created a powerful new force in the market. 两家公司的合并创造了市场上的一股强大的新力量。|combination, merger, union|separation, division|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: from late Latin *amalgamator, from Latin *amalgamare,  ultimately from Greek *malagma 'soft mass', related to malakos 'soft'.  The initial a- may have arisen by analogy with words such as adulterate.
