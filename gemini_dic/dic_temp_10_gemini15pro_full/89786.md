horizontalism:/ˌhɔːrɪˈzɒntəlɪzəm/ /ˌhɔːrɪˈzɑːntəlɪzəm/| n. |水平主义，横向主义|The principle of organizing a movement or a political system based on decentralized, non-hierarchical participation, often emphasizing grassroots democracy and self-organization.  It stresses equality and collaboration among participants rather than top-down control.|The Occupy movement embraced horizontalism in its organizational structure.|Synonyms: decentralization, grassroots democracy, self-organization, participatory democracy|Antonyms: hierarchy, verticalism, centralism, authoritarianism|noun
