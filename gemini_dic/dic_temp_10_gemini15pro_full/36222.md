cognateness:/ˈkɒɡneɪtnəs/ (US) /ˈkɑːɡneɪtnəs/| n. |同源性，同源关系|The state or quality of being cognate; relationship by descent from a common ancestor.|English and German share a high degree of cognateness due to their shared Germanic roots. 英语和德语由于共同的日耳曼语族渊源而具有高度的同源性。|近义词：kinship, relatedness, affinity|反义词：unrelatedness, dissimilarity|词性:noun

cognate:/ˈkɒɡneɪt/ (US) /ˈkɑːɡneɪt/| adj. n. |同源的，同源词| (adj.) Related by birth; descended from a common ancestor. (n.) A word related to another word in a different language by descent from a common ancestral language.|The English word "father" and the German word "Vater" are cognate. 英语单词“father”和德语单词“Vater”是同源词。|近义词：(adj.) related, kindred, analogous (n.) relative, counterpart|反义词：(adj.) unrelated, dissimilar|词性:adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cognatus, from Latin cognatus ("related by blood"), past participle of cognasci ("to be born with"), from com- ("together") + nasci, gnasci ("to be born").
