cauterize: /ˈkɔːtəraɪz/ (US) /ˈkɔːtəraɪz/ (UK)| v. | 烧灼；烙 |To burn (a wound or snakebite, etc.) with a hot iron or caustic substance in order to stop bleeding or prevent infection.|The doctor cauterized the wound to stop the bleeding. 医生烧灼伤口以止血。|Synonyms: sear, burn, scorch|Antonyms: heal, soothe|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cauterizare, from Latin cauterizāre, from Greek kautēriázein, from kautḗrion "branding iron," from kaíein "to burn."
