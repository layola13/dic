dying:/ˈdaɪɪŋ/ | adj. | 垂死的，临终的；渴望的，极想的 |  Near death; expiring. Also, extremely desirous. |  The dying man whispered his last words.  垂死的人低声说出他的遗言。 | synonyms: expiring, moribund, perishing, failing, fading, weakening, declining, sinking, ebbing, deteriorating; informal: on one's last legs, at death's door, on the way out, kicking the bucket; literary: giving up the ghost  | antonyms: living, thriving, flourishing, growing, strengthening | adjective

dying:/ˈdaɪɪŋ/ | n. | 死亡，临终 |  The act or process of ceasing to live. | His dying was peaceful. 他的死亡很安详。 | synonyms: death, demise, passing, expiration, end, quietus | antonyms: birth, life | noun

ETYMOLOGY-> From Old English *dēgend, present participle of dēgan "to die," from Proto-Germanic *dagijaną "to become wet, become slow," from PIE root *dʰegʷʰ- "to burn, be hot; to perish."
