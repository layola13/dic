bridie:/ˈbraɪdi/| n.|肉馅饼; 新娘 (苏格兰方言)|A savory Scottish pastry filled with meat, onions, and other ingredients.  In some Scottish dialects, it can also mean "bride".|例句：I enjoyed a delicious bridie for lunch. 我午餐吃了一个美味的肉馅饼。

例句：The bridie looked beautiful in her white dress.  新娘穿着白色礼服看起来很漂亮。|近义词：pie, pasty (for the food meaning); bride (for the Scottish dialect meaning)|反义词:groom (only applicable for the dialect meaning)|词性:noun

词源:From Scots, probably from a personal name, Bride, a short form of Bridget.  The connection with weddings is likely due to the tradition of serving these pastries at weddings.
