omnibusses:/ˈɒmnɪbʌsɪz/ (UK), /ˈɑːmnɪbʌsɪz/ (US)| n. |公共汽车（复数）|A large motor vehicle carrying passengers by road, especially one serving as part of a regular public transport system.  Originally horse-drawn.|The omnibusses were crowded during rush hour. 公共汽车在高峰时间很拥挤。|近义词：buses, coaches|反义词: N/A|名词

omnibus:/ˈɒmnɪbəs/ (UK), /ˈɑːmnɪbəs/ (US)| adj. |总括的，综合的|Containing or including many items.|The omnibus bill covered several different topics.  这项综合法案涵盖了几个不同的主题。|近义词：comprehensive, inclusive|反义词: limited, specific|形容词


词源:
From Latin *omnibus* "for all," dative plural of *omnis* "all." Originally applied to a horse-drawn public vehicle in Nantes, France, the name of which, *Voitures Omnibus*, was taken from the shop of a hatter named Omnès, in front of which the vehicles stopped.
