passive-aggression:/ˈpæsɪv əˈɡrɛʃən/ | n. | 被动攻击 | A type of behavior or personality trait characterized by indirect resistance to the demands of others and an avoidance of direct confrontation, often expressed through sullenness, procrastination, stubbornness, or deliberate inefficiency.  |  Example: Her passive-aggression made it difficult to work with her, as she would constantly miss deadlines without explanation. 她被动攻击的行为让她很难合作，因为她总是无故错过截止日期。

Example: He showed his passive-aggression by agreeing to help, then doing a poor job intentionally. 他表面上同意帮忙，然后故意做得不好，以此表现他的被动攻击。 | 近义词: indirect aggression, passive resistance, sullenness | 反义词: directness, assertiveness, open communication | 词性: noun

passive-aggressive:/ˈpæsɪv əˈɡrɛsɪv/ | adj. | 被动攻击的 |  Characterized by passive-aggression.  | Example:  He gave me a passive-aggressive response when I asked him to help. 当我让他帮忙时，他给了我一个被动攻击的回应。

Example: Her passive-aggressive behavior is creating tension within the team. 她被动攻击的行为在团队中制造了紧张气氛。 | 近义词: resentful, sullen, obstructive | 反义词: assertive, direct, cooperative | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From passive + aggression.  The term emerged in the mid-20th century, originally used in the context of soldiers' reactions to authority.
