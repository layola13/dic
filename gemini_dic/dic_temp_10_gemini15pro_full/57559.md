dramaticism:/drəˈmætɪsɪzəm/
n.
戏剧主义；戏剧性；戏剧化
A method of rhetorical criticism focused on identifying how a speaker or writer persuades an audience to accept a particular point of view.  It analyzes communication by considering the five key elements of the “dramatistic pentad”: act, scene, agent, agency, and purpose.
例句：The critic analyzed the speech using the principles of dramaticism.  这位评论家用戏剧主义的原理分析了演讲。

例句：Dramaticism helps us understand how political rhetoric can influence public opinion. 戏剧主义帮助我们理解政治修辞是如何影响公众舆论的。
近义词：dramatics, theatricality
反义词：understatement, restraint
词性：noun
