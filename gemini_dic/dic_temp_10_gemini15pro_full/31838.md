cheekinesses:/ˈtʃiːkɪnɪsɪz/
n.
厚脸皮，无礼，放肆
Instances of being cheeky; plural form of cheekiness.  A collection of impudent or disrespectful acts.
His cheekinesses finally led to his dismissal. 他的厚脸皮最终导致他被解雇。
The child's cheekinesses were sometimes amusing, sometimes annoying.  这孩子的放肆行为有时很有趣，有时很烦人。
近义词: impudence, audacity, insolence, nerve, gall
反义词: politeness, respect, deference, humility, modesty
词性: 名词 (plural)


cheekiness:/ˈtʃiːkɪnɪs/
n.
厚脸皮，无礼，放肆
The quality of being cheeky; impudence or disrespect.
He had the cheekiness to ask for a raise after only a week on the job. 他竟然厚着脸皮在只工作了一周后就要求加薪。
Her cheekiness charmed some people but irritated others. 她的厚脸皮吸引了一些人，但也激怒了其他人。
近义词: impudence, audacity, insolence, nerve, gall
反义词: politeness, respect, deference, humility, modesty
词性: 名词 (singular)


词源: From cheeky + -ness.  Cheeky itself comes from cheek (Old English cēace "jaw, jawbone") with the suffix -y indicating a characteristic.  The plural cheekinesses is formed by adding the standard plural suffix -es.
