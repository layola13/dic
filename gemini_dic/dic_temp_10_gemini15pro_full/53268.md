dirging:/ˈdɜːrdʒɪŋ/
verb
(以挽歌的形式)吟唱；哀悼；(尤指)缓慢而悲伤地移动
moving or speaking slowly and sadly, especially in a way that seems like a funeral song
The mourners were dirging through the streets. 送葬者在街上缓慢而悲伤地行进。
The wind dirged through the trees. 风在树林中呜咽，如同挽歌。
近义词: lamenting, mourning, grieving
反义词: rejoicing, celebrating
词性: v.

Etymology: From dirge (n.), from Middle English dirige, from Latin dirige (imperative of dirigere, "to direct"), referring to the opening words of Psalm 5:8, Dirige, Domine, Deus meus, in conspectu tuo viam meam ("Direct, O Lord my God, my way in thy sight"), used in the Office for the Dead.
