carpooled: /ˈkɑːrˌpuːld/ | v. |拼车，合伙用车|To travel to a destination with others in one car, typically to save fuel or reduce traffic congestion. | Example: We carpooled to work to save money on gas. 我们拼车上班以节省汽油钱。

Example: They carpooled to the concert to avoid parking hassles.  他们拼车去音乐会是为了避免停车麻烦。| 近义词:shared a ride, rode together | 反义词: drove alone | 词性: verb

carpool: /ˈkɑːrˌpuːl/ | n. |拼车，合伙用车|An arrangement in which a group of people travel together in a single car, especially to work or school. | Example:  We formed a carpool to reduce our carbon footprint. 我们组成了一个拼车小组以减少碳足迹。

Example:  Joining a carpool can save you a lot of money. 加入拼车可以为你节省很多钱。 | 近义词: ride-sharing arrangement, car-sharing group | 反义词: solo commute | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "car" + "pool,"  "pool" in this context meaning a collective resource or a combination for shared use.
