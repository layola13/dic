double-decker:/ˈdʌbəlˌdɛkər/ (US), /ˈdʌblˌdɛkə(r)/ (UK)| n. adj.|双层公共汽车；双层（的）|A bus or other vehicle with two decks, one on top of the other.  Also used as an adjective to describe something with two layers or levels.|例句：I took a double-decker bus tour of London. 我乘坐双层巴士游览了伦敦。|例句：The restaurant had a double-decker seating area. 这家餐厅有一个双层的用餐区。|近义词：double-tiered, two-story|反义词:single-decker|词性：名词，形容词


double-deckered:/ˌdʌbəlˈdɛkərd/ (US), /ˌdʌblˈdɛkəd/ (UK)| adj.|双层的|Having two decks or levels.|例句：We stayed in a double-deckered bus converted into a hotel room. 我们住在改装成酒店房间的双层巴士里。|例句：She ordered a double-deckered sandwich. 她点了一份双层三明治。|近义词：double-tiered, two-story|反义词:single-deckered|词性：形容词

词源:  "double" + "deck" + "-er". "Double" means two or twice. "Deck" refers to a platform or level. "-er" is a suffix used to form nouns indicating a person or thing that performs a particular action or has a particular characteristic.  The word first appeared in the late 19th century, referring specifically to buses.
