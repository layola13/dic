half-subdued:/ˌhɑːf səbˈdjuːd/ |adj.| 半抑制的；半压制的；半控制的 | Partially suppressed or restrained; not fully controlled or expressed. |  The crowd's cheers were half-subdued, a mixture of excitement and apprehension.  人群的欢呼声半是压抑的，夹杂着兴奋和担忧。

Her laughter was half-subdued, as if she were trying not to disturb anyone. 她的笑声半是压抑的，好像她不想打扰任何人。| 近义词: muted, restrained, suppressed, dampened | 反义词: unrestrained, uninhibited, boisterous, loud | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
"half-" from Old English "healf," meaning "one of two equal parts."
"subdued" from past participle of "subdue," from Old French "subduire," from Latin "subducere," meaning "to lead away, withdraw, remove."  The prefix "sub-" means "under" and "-ducere" means "to lead."
