dirndl: /ˈdɪrndl/ (US), /ˈdəːndl/ (UK)| n. |连衣裙，紧身胸衣连衣裙|A type of dress, typically associated with Bavarian or Austrian traditional clothing, featuring a close-fitting bodice, often low-cut and laced, a full skirt gathered at the waist, and usually an apron tied over the skirt. The dirndl's historical roots can be traced back to peasant clothing of the 18th and 19th centuries, but it has since evolved into a fashionable garment worn for festive occasions, particularly Oktoberfest. |She looked charming in her dirndl. 她穿着连衣裙看起来很迷人。|Synonym: folk dress, peasant dress |Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps evening gown, cocktail dress) |noun
