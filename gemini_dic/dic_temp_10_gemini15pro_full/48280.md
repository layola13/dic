decoy:/ˈdiːkɔɪ/ (US), /dɪˈkɔɪ/ (UK)| n. vt.|诱饵，圈套；引诱，诱骗|Something used to lure or entice someone or something into a trap or dangerous situation. A person or thing used to distract attention from another.|The hunter used a decoy to attract the ducks. 猎人用诱饵来吸引鸭子。|The police used a decoy car to catch the thieves. 警察用一辆诱饵车来抓捕小偷。|近义词：lure, bait, trap, enticement|反义词：warning, deterrent|n.
decoy:/ˈdiːkɔɪ/ (US), /dɪˈkɔɪ/ (UK)| vt.|引诱，诱骗|To lure or entice someone or something into a trap or dangerous situation.|The criminals tried to decoy the police away from the bank. 罪犯试图将警察引离银行。|They decoyed her into a dark alley. 他们把她诱骗到一条黑暗的小巷里。|近义词：lure, entice, tempt, trap|反义词：warn, deter, repel|v.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *de kooi ("the cage"), from de ("the") + kooi ("cage").  Originally referred to a cage or trap used for catching wildfowl.
