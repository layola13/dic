distractedness's:/dɪˈstræktɪdnɪsɪz/ | n. | 分心，心不在焉 | The state of being easily distracted; inability to concentrate.  The possessive form indicates that this distractedness belongs to someone or something. |  The student's distractedness's impact on their grades was significant.  这个学生心不在焉对他们成绩的影响很显著。| 近义词:inattention, absent-mindedness, preoccupation | 反义词:focus, concentration, attentiveness | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
From *distract* + *-ed* + *-ness* + *-'s*.
*Distract* comes from the Latin *distractus*, past participle of *distrahere*, meaning "to draw apart, divide, separate, distract."
*-ed* is a suffix forming the past participle of verbs.
*-ness* is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
*-'s* is the possessive marker.
