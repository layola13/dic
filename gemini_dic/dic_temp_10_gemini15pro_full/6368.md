amphibology:/ˌæmfiˈbɒlədʒi/ | n. |  歧义; 模棱两可;  |A statement or sentence that is open to more than one interpretation due to its grammatical structure. | The statement "I saw the man with the telescope" is an example of amphibology because it could mean either that I used a telescope to see the man or that I saw a man who had a telescope. |我看拿着望远镜的那个人（这句话可以理解为我用望远镜看那个人，也可以理解为我看到一个拿着望远镜的人).| 近义词: ambiguity, equivocation | 反义词: clarity, precision | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *amphibologia, from Ancient Greek ἀμφιβολία (amphibolía, “ambiguity”), from ἀμφίβολος (amphíbolos, “ambiguous, doubtful”), from ἀμφί (amphí, “on both sides”) + βάλλω (bállō, “I throw, cast”).
