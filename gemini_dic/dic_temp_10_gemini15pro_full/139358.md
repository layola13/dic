obscurely:/əbˈskjʊərli/
adv.
隐晦地，费解地，模糊不清地
in a way that is difficult to understand or see clearly
The report was written so obscurely that nobody could understand it.  这份报告写得晦涩难懂，没有人能理解。
近义词: vaguely, indistinctly, ambiguously, dimly
反义词: clearly, plainly, distinctly, explicitly
词性: 副词

obscure:/əbˈskjʊr/
adj.
昏暗的，朦胧的；晦涩的，难懂的；无名的，鲜为人知的
not discovered or known about; uncertain
His origins and parentage are obscure. 他的出身和父母不详。
近义词: unclear, uncertain, unknown, vague, ambiguous, hidden
反义词: clear, obvious, known, famous
词性: 形容词

obscure:/əbˈskjʊr/
v.
使…模糊不清，掩盖；隐藏；使…不为人知
keep from being seen; conceal
The true history has been obscured by legends about the event.  关于这件事的传说掩盖了真实的历史。
近义词: hide, conceal, cover, veil
反义词: reveal, expose, clarify
词性: 动词


词源: From Latin obscurus "dark, dim, covered," figuratively "hidden, indistinct, unknown," from ob "over" + -scurus "covered," related to the root of skiā "shadow," and perhaps to Sanskrit sku- "to cover."
