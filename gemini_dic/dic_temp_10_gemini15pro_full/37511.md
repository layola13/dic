commoditized:/kəˈmɒdəˌtaɪzd/
adjective
商品化的
Having become a commodity; (of a product) widely available and interchangeable with other similar products, distinguished primarily by price.
Generic drugs have commoditized the pharmaceutical industry. 仿制药使制药行业商品化了。
近义词: generic, standardized, undifferentiated, mass-produced
反义词: specialized, niche, custom, bespoke
词性: adjective

commoditize:/kəˈmɒdəˌtaɪz/
verb
使商品化
To make (something) into a commodity.
Cloud computing has commoditized server infrastructure. 云计算使服务器基础设施商品化了。
近义词: standardize, generalize, homogenize
反义词: specialize, customize, differentiate
词性: verb

Etymology: From commodity + -ize. Commodity comes from the Late Latin commoditas, meaning "advantage, convenience, profit," from Latin commodus "suitable, convenient."
