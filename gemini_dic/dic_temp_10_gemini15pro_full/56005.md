do-gooder:/ˈduːˌɡʊdər/| n.|做好事的人；爱管闲事的人|A person who tries to do good, especially one who is ostentatiously virtuous or interfering.|She's always been a do-gooder, volunteering at the homeless shelter and organizing charity drives.|近义词：altruist, philanthropist, humanitarian|反义词:malefactor, evildoer|词性:noun

Etymology: do + good + -er.  The suffix "-er" is used to form nouns denoting persons who perform the action of the verb.  In this case, "do good."  The term often carries a slightly negative connotation, implying excessive or self-righteous zeal.
