cuminol:/ˈkjuːmɪnɒl/ | n. |枯茗醇，异丙基苯甲醛|An aromatic aldehyde, isopropyl benzaldehyde, found in cumin oil and used in perfumery.|Cuminol contributes to the characteristic aroma of cumin. 枯茗醇是孜然特有香味的来源之一。|The perfumer carefully blended cuminol with other essences to create a unique fragrance. 调香师小心翼翼地将枯茗醇与其他香精混合，调制出独特的香味。|Synonyms:  isopropylbenzaldehyde, p-isopropylbenzaldehyde|Antonyms: None|Noun

Etymology: From cumin + -ol (indicating an alcohol or phenol, though cuminol is an aldehyde).  Ultimately from Latin *cuminum*, from Greek *kyminon*.
