failing:/ˈfeɪlɪŋ/| n. |缺点，不足；失败|a weakness or fault in someone or something|Her greatest failing is her impatience. 她最大的缺点就是不耐心。|近义词:shortcoming, weakness, defect, flaw|反义词:strength, virtue|noun

failing:/ˈfeɪlɪŋ/| prep. |如果没有；若非|in the absence of; without|Failing a miracle, we're going to lose this game.  如果没有奇迹发生，我们就要输掉这场比赛了。|近义词:without, lacking, in the absence of|反义词:with, having|preposition

failing:/ˈfeɪlɪŋ/| adj. |衰退的，衰弱的；失败的|becoming weaker; unsuccessful|The failing business eventually closed down.  这家衰败的企业最终倒闭了。|近义词:declining, weakening, faltering|反义词:thriving, successful, flourishing|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *failen*, from Old French *faillir*, from Vulgar Latin *fallire*, from Latin *fallere* "to deceive, trick, disappoint, escape the notice of."  The original sense of "failing" was closer to "deceiving" or "disappointing."
