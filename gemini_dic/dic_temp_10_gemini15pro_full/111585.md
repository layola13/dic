lobotomizations:/ˌloʊbəˈtɒmɪˌzeɪʃənz/
noun
脑叶切除术（复数）
The surgical severing of nerve fibers connecting the frontal lobes to the thalamus, formerly used to treat certain mental disorders.
Many lobotomizations were performed in the mid-20th century, often with devastating consequences.
许多脑叶切除术在20世纪中期进行，通常会造成毁灭性的后果。

lobotomizations:/ˌloʊbəˈtɒmɪˌzeɪʃənz/
noun
脑叶切除手术（复数）
The process of performing lobotomies, often resulting in significant personality changes and cognitive impairment.
Lobotomizations were a controversial practice with lasting negative impacts on many patients.
脑叶切除手术是一项有争议的做法，对许多患者造成了持久的负面影响。


近义词: psychosurgery, prefrontal leucotomy
反义词: (No direct antonym, but could consider "psychotherapy" as a contrasting treatment)
词性: noun (plural)


词源:  From lobotomy + -izations.  Lobotomy from Greek lobos "lobe" + -tomy "incision, cutting."
