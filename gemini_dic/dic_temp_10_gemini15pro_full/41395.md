cornbind:/ˈkɔːnbaɪnd/ | n. | 田旋花，菟丝子 | A twining plant (Convolvulus arvensis) with arrow-shaped leaves and white or pinkish flowers, a common weed of arable land.  Also refers to other similar plants, especially those of the genus Cuscuta (dodder), which are parasitic. | The cornbind choked the young corn plants. 田旋花扼杀了幼小的玉米植株。|  The farmer struggled to remove the cornbind from his fields. 农民努力清除田里的菟丝子。 | synonyms: bindweed, dodder, field bindweed | antonyms: N/A | noun


cornbind:/ˈkɔːnbaɪnd/ | v. | 缠绕，束缚 | To bind or tie together, especially with a rope or cord; to entwine around something. This usage is archaic or dialectal and primarily refers to the action of the plant itself. | The cornbind had cornbound the rose bush. 菟丝子缠绕着玫瑰丛。(古语或方言用法) | The weeds cornbind the crops, hindering their growth. 杂草缠绕着农作物，阻碍它们的生长。(古语或方言用法) | synonyms: entwine, bind, twine, constrict | antonyms: unbind, loosen, free | verb
