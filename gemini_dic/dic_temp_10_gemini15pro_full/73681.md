forgettable:/fərˈɡetəbl/ | adj. | 健忘的，容易被忘记的 |  Unlikely to be remembered; not memorable. | The movie had a forgettable plot. 这部电影的情节很容易被忘记。| synonyms: unmemorable, insignificant, bland | antonyms: memorable, unforgettable, noteworthy | adjective

forgettable:/fərˈɡetəbl/ | adj. | 可原谅的 |  Easy to forgive or overlook.  | It was a forgettable offense. 这是一个可原谅的过错。| synonyms: excusable, pardonable, minor | antonyms: unforgivable, inexcusable, major | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *forgiettol, from forgietan "to forget" + -ol, adjectival suffix.  The "for-" prefix denotes loss or destruction of the following element. The root "gietan" means "to get, obtain."  Therefore, "forgietan" literally means "to lose one's hold on something."
