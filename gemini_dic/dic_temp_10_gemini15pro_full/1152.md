accipiter: /ækˈsɪpɪtər/
noun
鹰，隼（尤指体型较小的鹰）
A bird of prey of the genus *Accipiter*, typically having short rounded wings and a long tail, adapted for fast flight through forests.
The Cooper's hawk is a common accipiter in North America.  库珀鹰是北美常见的鹰属鸟类。

The sharp-shinned hawk is another accipiter known for its agility.  尖尾鹰是另一种以敏捷著称的鹰属鸟类。

synonym
hawk, falcon, raptor
antonym
prey
词性
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *accipiter* (hawk), from *accipere* (to seize, grasp), from *ad-* (to) + *capere* (to take).  源自拉丁语 *accipiter*（鹰），来自 *accipere*（抓住，握住），来自 *ad-*（朝向）+ *capere*（拿）。
