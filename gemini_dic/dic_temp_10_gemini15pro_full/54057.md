disdainful:/dɪsˈdeɪnfəl/
adjective
轻蔑的，鄙夷的
Showing contempt or lack of respect.
She cast a disdainful glance at the cheap jewelry. 她轻蔑地瞥了一眼那些廉价的珠宝。
近义词: contemptuous, scornful, haughty
反义词: respectful, admiring, appreciative
词性: adjective

disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/
noun, verb
轻蔑，鄙视；鄙视，蔑视
(n.) a feeling of contempt for someone or something regarded as unworthy or inferior; (v.) consider to be unworthy of one's consideration.
(n.) He regarded their proposal with disdain. 他对他们的提议嗤之以鼻。
(v.) She disdained to answer their accusations. 她不屑于回应他们的指责。
近义词: contempt, scorn, arrogance (n.); spurn, reject (v.)
反义词: respect, admiration (n.); accept, embrace (v.)
词性: noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desdeigner*, from *des-* (expressing negation) + *deigner* "to deign," from Latin *dignari* "to deem worthy," from *dignus* "worthy."
