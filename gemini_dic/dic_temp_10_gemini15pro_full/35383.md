co-op:/ˈkoʊ ɑːp/ (US), /ˈkəʊ ɒp/ (UK)| n.|合作社，合作公寓|A business or organization owned and run jointly by its members, who share the profits or benefits. Also, an apartment building owned and managed by a cooperative corporation.|例句：We buy our groceries from the local food co-op. 我们从当地的食品合作社购买食品杂货。

例句：She lives in a co-op in Manhattan. 她住在曼哈顿的一套合作公寓里。|近义词：cooperative, collective|反义词:corporation, conglomerate|词性:名词

co-oped:/koʊˈɑːpt/ (US), /kəʊˈɒpt/ (UK)| v.|拉拢，接管，吸收|To adopt or use as one's own; to take over.|例句：The mayor co-opted several of his opponent's proposals. 市长采纳了对手的几项提议。

例句：The board co-opted several new members. 董事会吸收了几名新成员。|近义词：appropriate, commandeer, incorporate|反义词:reject, refuse|词性:动词
