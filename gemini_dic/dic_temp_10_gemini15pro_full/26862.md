cajoled:/kəˈdʒoʊld/| v. |哄骗，劝诱|To persuade someone to do something by using flattery or gentle urging.|He cajoled his parents into letting him go to the concert. 他哄骗父母让他去听音乐会。|Synonyms: coax, wheedle, persuade, entice|Antonyms: dissuade, discourage, deter|verb
Etymology: From French cajoler "to chatter, prattle, coax," perhaps from Old French gale "joy, pleasure."|
