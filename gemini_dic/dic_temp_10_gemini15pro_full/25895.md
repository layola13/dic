burl:/bɜːrl/| n.|树瘤，节疤；（尤指树根处的）瘤状突起|A burl is a rounded outgrowth on the trunk or branch of a tree, often containing irregular grain patterns.|The woodworker prized the burl for its unique and intricate patterns. 木工珍视树瘤的独特而复杂的纹路。|Synonyms: knot, outgrowth, lump|Antonyms: smoothness, evenness|noun


burl:/bɜːrl/| v.|去除树瘤，去节|To remove burls from wood.|He burled the piece of wood before starting to carve it. 他在开始雕刻之前先去掉了木块上的树瘤。|Synonyms: remove, excise, trim|Antonyms: attach, add, affix|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *burle, likely related to Old Norse *byrla (“swelling, boil”), from Proto-Germanic *burlaną (“to swell, boil”). Cognate with Swedish byrl (“a burl”), dialectal German Bürle (“pimple, boil”).
