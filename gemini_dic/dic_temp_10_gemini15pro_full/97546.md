ingratiatingly:/ɪnˈɡreɪʃɪeɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 讨好地，献媚地 | In a manner intended to gain favor or approval, often excessively or insincerely. | He smiled ingratiatingly at his boss, hoping to get a promotion. 他讨好地对老板笑着，希望能升职。|  She ingratiatingly offered to help with the project, even though she knew nothing about it. 她献媚地主动提出帮忙做这个项目，尽管她对此一无所知。 | 近义词: charmingly, flatteringly, obsequiously | 反义词: offensively, rudely, abrasively | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "ingratiate," which comes from the Latin "in" (into) + "gratia" (grace, favor).  The suffix "-ing" creates the present participle, and "-ly" transforms it into an adverb.
