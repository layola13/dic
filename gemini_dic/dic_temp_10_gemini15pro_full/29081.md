carving:/ˈkɑːrvɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkɑːvɪŋ/ (UK)| n. v.|雕刻；雕刻品；切（肉）|The act or process of producing a sculpture by cutting away material from a larger block.  A carved object, especially a piece of sculpture. The act of cutting slices of meat.|He spent hours perfecting the carving of the eagle.  他花了几个小时完善鹰的雕刻。| 近义词:sculpture, engraving, etching, whittling, slicing|反义词:casting, molding|词性:名词, 动词

carving:/ˈkɑːrvɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkɑːvɪŋ/ (UK)| v. |雕刻；切（肉）|To cut (a figure or design) into a surface. To cut (cooked meat) into slices for serving.|She carved a wooden duck. 她雕刻了一只木鸭。|近义词:sculpt, engrave, etch, whittle, slice|反义词:cast, mold|词性:动词
