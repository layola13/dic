idealizing:/aɪˈdiːəlaɪzɪŋ/
verb|理想化；将…理想化|The biographer is accused of idealizing his subject.  这位传记作家被指责将他的传主理想化了。|例句：He tends to idealize his childhood. 他倾向于将他的童年理想化。|近义词：romanticizing, glorifying|反义词：vilifying, denigrating|v.
idealized:/aɪˈdiːəlaɪzd/
adjective|理想化的|She has a very idealized view of marriage. 她对婚姻抱有非常理想化的看法。|例句：His paintings depict an idealized version of rural life. 他的画描绘了理想化的乡村生活。|近义词：romantic, unrealistic|反义词：realistic, pragmatic|adj.
idealization:/ˌaɪdiːələˈzeɪʃn/
noun|理想化|The idealization of the past is a common phenomenon.  对过去的理想化是一种普遍现象。|例句：His poems are full of idealization of nature. 他的诗歌充满了对自然的理想化。|近义词：romanticization, glorification|反义词：vilification, denigration|n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From ideal + -ize + -ing.  "Ideal" comes from Late Latin "idealis" meaning "existing in idea," from Latin "idea" (from Greek "idea"). "-ize" is a suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or cause to be" something. "-ing" is a suffix forming present participles and gerunds.
