gradualists:/ˈɡrædʒuəlɪsts/ | n. | 渐进主义者；渐变论者 | People who believe in gradualism, the theory that change happens slowly and incrementally, rather than suddenly or dramatically. | The gradualists argued for a slow, phased approach to economic reform. 渐进主义者主张逐步缓慢地进行经济改革。| The gradualists preferred a step-by-step implementation of the new policy. 渐进主义者倾向于逐步实施新政策。| synonyms: moderates, conservatives (in some contexts) | antonyms: radicals, revolutionaries | noun
