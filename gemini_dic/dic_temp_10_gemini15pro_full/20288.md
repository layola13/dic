biscuit:/ˈbɪskɪt/ (US) /ˈbɪskɪt/ (UK)| n. |饼干；(美式英语)小面包|A small, flat, dry cake, typically crisp and sweet. In American English, biscuit can also refer to a small, soft, quick bread similar to a scone.|例句：I like to dunk my biscuit in my tea. 我喜欢把饼干泡在茶里吃。

例句：She baked a batch of chocolate chip biscuits. 她烤了一批巧克力饼干。

近义词：cookie, cracker, scone (US), bun (UK)|反义词: None|词性: Noun

biscuit:/ˈbɪskɪt/ (US) /ˈbɪskɪt/ (UK)| n. |(美式英语)小面包|A small, soft, quick bread, often served with savory dishes. Similar to a UK scone.|例句：In the South, biscuits are often served with gravy. 在美国南方，小面包通常与肉汁一起食用。

例句：He ordered biscuits and sausage for breakfast. 他早餐点了小面包和香肠。

词源：From Middle French *bescuit, from Old French bescuit, from the phrase “twice cooked” (bis + cuit, past participle of cuire, to cook).  This refers to the original method of making biscuits, which involved baking them twice to make them dry and crisp.
