in-grainedness:/ɪnˈɡreɪndnəs/ | n. | 根深蒂固，深入人心 | The state of being deeply ingrained or firmly fixed; the quality of being ingrained. |  Racial prejudice is an in-grainedness that some people find hard to overcome.  种族偏见是一种有些人难以克服的根深蒂固的观念。 | 近义词：rootedness, fixedness | 反义词：mutability, flexibility | 词性: noun

词源:  From "in-grained" + "-ness". "In-grained" is derived from "in-" (meaning "in, into, within") + "grained" (from "grain," referring to the texture of wood, implying deep penetration).  The suffix "-ness" forms nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition.
