anaphylaxises:/ˌænəfɪˈlæksiːz/ | n. |过敏反应（复数）| A severe, potentially life-threatening allergic reaction.  It can occur within seconds or minutes of exposure to an allergen. Symptoms include difficulty breathing, swelling, hives, and a drop in blood pressure. | Example 1: The patient experienced multiple anaphylaxises after being stung by bees. 病人被蜜蜂蜇伤后经历了多次过敏性休克。

Example 2:  The doctor warned her about the possibility of anaphylaxises due to her severe peanut allergy. 医生警告她，由于她对花生严重过敏，可能会发生过敏性休克。| 近义词: anaphylactic reactions, anaphylactic shocks | 反义词: None (anaphylaxis describes a specific medical condition, it doesn't have a direct opposite) | 词性: Noun


Etymology:  From Modern Latin anaphylaxis, from Greek ana- (up, back, against) + phylaxis (protection).  The term implies a reversed or failed protection.
