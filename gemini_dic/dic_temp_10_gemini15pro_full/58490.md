due:/duː/ /djuː/
adj.|到期的，应付的|owed as a debt or an obligation; payable.|The rent is due at the end of the month. 月底房租到期。|近义词：owing, payable, outstanding|反义词：paid, overdue|词性:形容词

due:/duː/ /djuː/
adj.|预期的，预定的|expected or scheduled to happen or arrive at a particular time.|The train is due in five minutes. 火车预计五分钟后到达。|近义词：expected, scheduled, anticipated|反义词：unexpected, unscheduled|词性:形容词

due:/duː/ /djuː/
adj.|适当的，应有的|appropriate or fitting.|He received due praise for his work. 他因工作得到了应有的赞扬。|近义词：proper, appropriate, fitting|反义词：improper, inappropriate|词性:形容词

due:/duː/ /djuː/
adv.|正对着，直接地|(especially of the wind) directly.|The ship sailed due west. 船正西航行。|近义词：directly, straight|反义词：indirectly|词性:副词

due:/duː/ /djuː/
n.|应得之物，应付款|something that is owed or payable.|He received his due reward. 他得到了应得的报酬。|近义词：payment, fee, debt|反义词：credit|词性:名词


词源: From Old French *deu, past participle of devoir “to owe,” from Latin dēbēre.
