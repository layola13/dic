The word "namation" is not a recognized English word. It's likely a misspelling or a neologism (newly coined word).  Perhaps you meant "animation" or "nomination"?  I will provide information for both:

**Animation:**

/ˌænɪˈmeɪʃən/ | n. | 动画; 活跃; 生气 | The process of making animated films, or the films themselves.  |  例句：The animation in the new Pixar movie is stunning.  皮克斯新电影的动画效果令人惊叹。 |  例句：Her face was full of animation as she described her trip.  她描述旅行时，脸上充满了活力。 | 近义词: liveliness, vibrancy, cartoon | 反义词: inactivity, dullness | 词性: noun


**Nomination:**

/ˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. | 提名; 任命 | The act of officially suggesting someone or something for a position, job, award, etc. | 例句：He received a nomination for best actor. 他获得了最佳男主角的提名。 | 例句：Her nomination to the Supreme Court was controversial. 她被提名到最高法院是有争议的。| 近义词: appointment, designation, proposal | 反义词: rejection, withdrawal | 词性: noun


**USAGE (for "What")** ->

What /wɒt/ /hwɒt/ | pron. det. adv.| 什么; 多么 | Used to ask for information about something. | 例句：What is your name? 你叫什么名字？ | 例句：What do you want for dinner? 你晚餐想吃什么？ | 近义词: which, how | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | 词性: pronoun, determiner, adverb
