engrieve:/ɪnˈɡriːv/| vt.|使悲伤，使苦恼|To cause to feel grief or sorrow. |The news of her death engrived the whole family. 她去世的消息使全家人悲痛欲绝。|Synonyms: sadden, distress, upset, grieve|Antonyms: comfort, console, gladden|Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *engreven, from Old French engrever (“to burden, aggravate”), from en- + grever (“to burden, grieve”), from Late Latin gravare (“to burden, weigh down”), from Latin gravis (“heavy”).
