eavesdrop: /ˈiːvzdrɒp/ (British) /ˈiːvzdrɑːp/ (American)
| n.|屋檐水|water dripping from the eaves of a house|例句：The eavesdrop made a steady drip, drip, drip. 屋檐水滴答滴答地响个不停。

例句：Plants thrived under the constant eavesdrop from the roof.  植物在屋檐不断滴落的水的滋润下茁壮成长。
|近义词：dripping, eaves water|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


Etymology: From Middle English *evesdroppe*, from *eves* "eaves" + *droppe* "drop". Originally referred to the ground on which water drips from the eaves, and later to the act of standing there to listen secretly.
