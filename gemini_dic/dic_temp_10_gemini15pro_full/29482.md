cataphorically:/ˌkætəˈfɒrɪkli/| adv.|前指地|Referring to something that will be mentioned later in the text or conversation.  It uses a pronoun or other referring expression to point forward to a later explanation or specification.|Example 1:  This is what I believe:  honesty is the best policy. (Here, "this" cataphorically refers to the subsequent clause).|Example 2: Although I don't know him well, I think John is a good person.  (Here, the pronoun "him" refers cataphorically to "John")|近义词:forward-referring|反义词:anaphorically|词性:副词
