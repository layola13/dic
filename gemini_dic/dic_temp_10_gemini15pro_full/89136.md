homecomer:/ˈhoʊmkʌmər/ (US) /ˈhəʊmkʌmə(r)/ (UK)| n. | 回乡者，返乡者；校友返校参加聚会者 | A person who returns home, especially after a long absence, or an alumnus/alumna who returns to their school for a homecoming event. |  例句：The town welcomed the homecomers with open arms.  小镇张开双臂欢迎返乡者。 |  例句：Many homecomers attended the annual alumni reunion. 许多校友返校参加了年度校友聚会。 | 近义词：returnee, alumnus/alumna (in the context of school reunions) | 反义词: emigrant, exile | 词性: Noun

Etymology:  "home" + "comer" (agent suffix denoting someone who performs an action). "Home" comes from Old English "hām," meaning "dwelling, house, village." "Comer" comes from Old English "cuma," meaning "one who comes, arrives."
