chronographers: /krəˈnɒɡrəfərz/ | n. | 计时员，计时器 | A person who records time, especially in sports competitions; a device used for recording time accurately. | The chronographers meticulously recorded each athlete's lap time. 计时员一丝不苟地记录了每个运动员的单圈时间。 |  The chronographers at the Olympics use highly sophisticated equipment. 奥运会的计时员使用高度精密的设备。 | synonyms: timer, timekeeper, stopwatch | antonyms: None | noun

chronograph: /ˈkrɒnəɡrɑːf/ | n. | 计时器，计时表 | A timepiece or watch with a built-in stopwatch function. | He started the chronograph as the race began. 比赛开始时，他启动了计时器。 | This chronograph not only tells time but also measures speed.  这个计时器不仅可以计时，还可以测量速度。 | synonyms: stopwatch, timer, chronometer | antonyms: None | noun


Etymology:  From the Greek words "chronos" (time) and "graphein" (to write).
