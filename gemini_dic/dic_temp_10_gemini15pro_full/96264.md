indictable:/ɪnˈdaɪtəbl/| adj.|可起诉的，可控告的|Subject to being formally accused of a crime in a court of law.|例句：His offense is indictable. 他的罪行是可以被起诉的。|例句：The crime is indictable in the Crown Court.  这宗罪行可在刑事法庭提出起诉。|近义词：prosecutable, chargeable, accusable|反义词:unaccusable, unimpeachable|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *enditable, *inditable, from Anglo-Norman enditer, endicter, from Old French enditier, enditer (“to dictate, write, compose, accuse, indict”), from en- (“in, into”) + ditier (“to dictate”), from Latin dictare (“to say often, prescribe, dictate”), frequentative of dicere (“to say”).
