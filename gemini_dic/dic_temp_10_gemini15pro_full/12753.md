assientist:/əˈsɪstɪənt/ | adj. n. | 协助的；援助的；协助者；援助者 | Relating to or characteristic of a system of trade in which a country grants a monopoly to a trading company for the purpose of assisting in the development of its colonies, typically involving the transport of enslaved people.  A person involved in such a system. |  The assientist policies of the 18th century contributed to the growth of the slave trade. 18世纪的协助主义政策促成了奴隶贸易的增长。 | The Royal African Company was granted assientist rights in the British colonies. 皇家非洲公司被授予在英国殖民地的协助权。 | 近义词: auxiliary, supporting, helper, aide | 反义词: obstructive, hindering, opponent | 词性: adjective, noun

词源:
From French *assientiste*, from Spanish *asiento* (“contract, treaty, agreement, seat”), from Latin *assidere* (“to sit down to, sit near”), from *ad-* (“to, towards”) + *sedere* (“to sit”).  The specific meaning related to colonial trade monopolies developed in the 17th and 18th centuries.
