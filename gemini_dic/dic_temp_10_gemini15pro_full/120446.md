minnows:/ˈmɪnoʊz/
|n.|米诺鱼，小鱼，无足轻重的人|A small freshwater fish of the carp family, often used as bait.  Also used figuratively to refer to unimportant or insignificant people or things.|例句：The minnows darted among the reeds. 小鱼在芦苇丛中穿梭。

|例句：The company felt like minnows swimming with sharks in the competitive market. 在竞争激烈的市场中，这家公司感觉就像小鱼与鲨鱼共游。|近义词：tidlers, small fry, tiny fish|反义词：giants, whales (figuratively)|词性:名词

词源：
From Middle English *menowes, *menwes, from Old English myne, from Proto-Germanic *minwą (“small fish”), from Proto-Indo-European *mey- (“small”). Cognate with Dutch menne (“minnow”), German Elritze (“minnow”), Swedish mört (“roach”).
