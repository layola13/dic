feel-oneself:/fiːl wʌnˈsɛlf/|v.|感到自己处于某种状态；觉得舒适；觉得健康；感觉自在|To be aware of one's own existence, personality, or feelings; to feel well or healthy, especially after illness; to feel comfortable or at ease.|例句：After a good night's sleep, I really felt myself again. 经过一夜好眠，我感觉自己恢复了。|例句：She doesn't feel herself today and has decided to stay home. 她今天感觉不舒服，决定待在家里。|近义词：be oneself, be back to normal, feel comfortable, feel well|反义词：feel ill, feel uncomfortable, be out of sorts|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
"feel" comes from Old English "fēlan" (to perceive by touch, experience emotionally). "oneself" comes from Old English "ān" (one) and "self" (self).  The combined phrase emphasizes the subjective experience of one's own physical or mental state.
