nucleobases: /ˈnjuːkliəʊbeɪsɪz/ /ˈnuːkliəˌbeɪsiz/ | n. | 核碱基 |  Any of the five nitrogenous bases that are components of nucleotides, the basic structural units of nucleic acids (DNA and RNA). These are adenine (A), guanine (G), cytosine (C), thymine (T), and uracil (U). |  The sequence of nucleobases in DNA determines the genetic code.  核碱基在DNA中的序列决定了遗传密码。| synonym: nitrogenous bases | antonym:  (No direct antonym, as these are fundamental components.  Perhaps one could consider non-canonical bases as a contrasting concept, but not a true antonym.) | noun
