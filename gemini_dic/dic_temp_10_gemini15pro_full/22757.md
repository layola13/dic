borrow-trouble:/ˈbɒrəʊ ˈtrʌbəl/ (UK) /ˈbɑːroʊ ˈtrʌbəl/ (US)| idiom |自寻烦恼，杞人忧天|To worry unnecessarily or anticipate problems that may not occur.| He's always borrowing trouble, worrying about things that haven't even happened yet. 他总是自寻烦恼，担心一些甚至还没有发生的事情。|例句：Don't borrow trouble by thinking about the worst-case scenario. 不要杞人忧天，去想最坏的情况。|近义词：worry unnecessarily, fret, anticipate problems, meet trouble halfway|反义词：stay positive, be optimistic, look on the bright side| idiom

borrow:/ˈbɒrəʊ/ (UK) /ˈbɑːroʊ/ (US) |verb|借入，借用|To take and use something belonging to somebody else, with the intention of returning it.|Can I borrow your pen for a minute? 我可以借你的笔用一下吗？|近义词：loan, take temporarily|反义词：lend, give temporarily| verb

trouble:/ˈtrʌbəl/ |noun|麻烦，问题|Difficulty or problems.|We had a lot of trouble getting here. 我们到这里很费劲。|近义词：difficulty, problem, issue|反义词：ease, solution, peace| noun
