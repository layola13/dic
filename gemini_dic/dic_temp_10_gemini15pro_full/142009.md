organize: /ˈɔːrɡənaɪz/ (US) /ˈɔːɡənaɪz/ (UK)| v. |组织，安排|To arrange or put in order; to establish or form. |例句：She organized a surprise party for her friend. 她为她的朋友组织了一个惊喜派对。

例句：He needs to organize his thoughts before the presentation. 他需要在演讲前整理一下思路。

近义词：arrange, coordinate, structure, systematize|反义词:disorganize, disorder, mess up|词性:动词

organization: /ˌɔːrɡənəˈzeɪʃn/ (US) /ˌɔːɡənɪˈzeɪʃn/ (UK)| n. |组织，机构|The act or process of organizing or of being organized; a group of people who work together. |例句：The organization is dedicated to helping the homeless. 这个组织致力于帮助无家可归的人。

例句：The school has a strong parent organization. 这所学校有一个强大的家长组织。

近义词：group, association, institution, body|反义词:disorganization, chaos|词性:名词

organized: /ˈɔːrɡənaɪzd/ (US) /ˈɔːɡənaɪzd/ (UK)| adj. |有条理的，有组织的| Arranged in a systematic way; efficient and orderly. |例句：She is a very organized person. 她是一个非常有条理的人。

例句：The event was well organized and ran smoothly.  这场活动组织得很好，进行得很顺利。

近义词：orderly, structured, systematic, efficient|反义词:disorganized, messy, chaotic|词性:形容词

词源: From Old French *organiser, from Late Latin organizare "to arrange," from Latin organum "instrument, tool, organ (of the body)," from Greek organon "instrument, tool, organ."
