hypostrophe:/haɪˈpɒstrəfi/ | n. | 省略;省略法;呼唤 | Addressing an absent person or thing; a rhetorical figure of speech in which the speaker addresses a dead or absent person, or an abstraction or inanimate object. | Example 1: "O, Romeo, Romeo, wherefore art thou Romeo?" (Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet - Juliet addresses Romeo, who is not present.)
Example 2:  "Is this a dagger which I see before me, the handle toward my hand? Come, let me clutch thee." (Shakespeare's Macbeth - Macbeth addresses a dagger, an inanimate object.) | Near Synonyms: apostrophe, address, invocation | Antonyms:  N/A | noun
