equivocators:/ɪˈkwɪvəˌkeɪtərz/| n. |模棱两可的人；含糊其辞的人|People who use equivocal language, often to deceive or mislead.  Those who use ambiguous language, especially with the intent to conceal the truth or avoid commitment.|The politician was accused of being an equivocator, never giving a straight answer.| 近义词: prevaricators, deceivers, liars| 反义词: truth-tellers, honest people| 词性: noun

equivocate:/ɪˈkwɪvəˌkeɪt/| v. |含糊其辞；模棱两可地说|To use ambiguous language so as to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself.|He equivocated when asked about his involvement in the scandal.| 近义词: prevaricate, hedge, evade| 反义词: be forthright, be direct| 词性: verb

equivocal:/ɪˈkwɪvəkəl/| adj. |模棱两可的；含糊不清的|Open to more than one interpretation; ambiguous.|Her answer was equivocal, leaving everyone unsure of her true intentions.| 近义词: ambiguous, vague, uncertain| 反义词: clear, unambiguous, definite| 词性: adjective

equivocation:/ɪˌkwɪvəˈkeɪʃən/| n. |模棱两可；含糊其辞|The use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself.|His equivocation made it difficult to determine his stance on the issue.| 近义词: prevarication, evasion, ambiguity| 反义词: clarity, directness, honesty| 词性: noun
