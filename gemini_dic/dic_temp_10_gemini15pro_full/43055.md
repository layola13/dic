cowleeching:/ˈkaʊˌliːtʃɪŋ/
n.
放血疗法（用牛蛭）;  牛蛭吸血
The practice of using cow leeches for bloodletting.  This historical medical practice involved applying leeches, specifically those that commonly infested cattle, to a patient's skin to draw blood.
Cowleeching was a common practice in the 18th century. 18世纪，放血疗法是一种常见的做法。
Near synonyms: bloodletting, leeching, hirudotherapy
No clear antonym.
