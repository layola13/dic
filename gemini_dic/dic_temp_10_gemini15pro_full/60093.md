eelpout:/ˈiːlpaʊt/ | n. | 鳗鱼 pout, 撅嘴鱼, 黏鱼 |A type of fish resembling an eel, typically found in cold northern waters.  They are known for their elongated bodies, slimy skin, and ability to survive in freezing temperatures. |  The fishermen caught a large eelpout in the Arctic waters. 渔民在北极水域捕获了一条大黏鱼。| synonym: viviparous blenny, Ocean pout| antonym: None (No direct opposite for a specific fish species)| noun


eelpout:/ˈiːlˌpaʊt/ | v. | 撅嘴；板着脸 | To sulk or make a sullen expression, like a pouting child.  This meaning is less common than the noun form. | He eelpouted when he didn't get the toy he wanted. 他因为没有得到想要的玩具而撅嘴。 | synonym: pout, sulk, frown| antonym: smile, grin| verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From eel + pout, referring to the fish's resemblance to an eel and its somewhat down-turned mouth.
