cutose: /ˈkjuːtoʊs/ | n. | 角质素；壳质 | A complex carbohydrate, similar to cellulose, forming the outer layer of some invertebrates, especially insects and crustaceans. | The cutose of the beetle's shell protected it from predators. 甲壳虫壳上的角质素保护它免受捕食者的侵害。 | 近义词: chitin, exoskeleton | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps endoskeleton as a contrasting concept) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cutose*, from Latin *cutis* ("skin").
