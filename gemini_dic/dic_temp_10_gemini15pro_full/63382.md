entoplasm:/ˈɛndəʊˌplæzəm/ (US) /ˈɛntəʊˌplæzəm/ (UK)| n.|内质，细胞质|The semi-fluid, granular substance that makes up the bulk of the cytoplasm of a cell, exclusive of the nucleus and other organelles.|The biologist studied the entoplasm of the amoeba under a microscope. 生物学家在显微镜下研究了变形虫的内质。|Synonyms: cytoplasm, protoplasm|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, as it is a specific component of a cell)|noun
