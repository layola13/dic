archilochian: /ˌɑːrkɪˈloʊkiən/ | adj. |  阿基洛柯式的；辛辣讽刺的；粗俗下流的 | Relating to or characteristic of the Greek lyric poet Archilochus, especially in being savagely satirical or personally abusive; using simple language and colloquial rhythms. |  His archilochian wit was both admired and feared. 他的阿基洛柯式的机智既令人钦佩又令人恐惧。| Synonyms: satirical, biting, caustic, sardonic,  invective, abusive | Antonyms: complimentary, flattering, praising, laudatory | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Archilochus (Greek Ἀρχίλοχος), a Greek lyric poet known for his sharp wit and invective.  The suffix "-ian" denotes "relating to" or "characteristic of".
