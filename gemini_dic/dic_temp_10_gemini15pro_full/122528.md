modalist:/ˈmoʊdəlɪst/ | n. |形态论者，形态主义者| A person who believes in Modalism, a theological doctrine that emphasizes the unity of God and denies the distinct persons of the Trinity, instead viewing the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit as different modes or manifestations of the same one God. |  Modalists believe that God revealed himself as the Father in the Old Testament, as the Son in Jesus Christ, and as the Holy Spirit after Christ's ascension. | 近义词: Monarchianist, Sabellian | 反义词: Trinitarian | 词性: Noun
