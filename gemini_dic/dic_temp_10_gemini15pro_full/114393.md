make amends:/meɪk əˈmendz/
|Verb Phrase|
|补偿；赔罪；纠正错误|
|To do something good to show that you are sorry about something you have done.|
|He tried to make amends for his past mistakes by donating to charity. 他试图通过向慈善机构捐款来弥补过去的错误。|

|She made amends by apologizing sincerely and offering to help. 她通过真诚地道歉并主动提供帮助来弥补过失。|
|compensate, atone, rectify, redress|
|worsen, aggravate|
|Verb Phrase|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Anglo-French *amender, from Latin emendare "to correct, free from fault," from ex- "out" + mendum "fault." Originally meant "to correct, improve," the sense of "making amends" arose from the idea of correcting a wrong.
