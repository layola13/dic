moulting:/ˈmoʊltɪŋ/ (US), /ˈməʊltɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |蜕皮; 换毛|The process of shedding old feathers, hair, skin, or other outer covering, to be replaced by new growth.|The snake is moulting its skin. 这条蛇正在蜕皮。|Synonyms: shedding, molting|Antonyms: growing|noun

moulting:/ˈmoʊltɪŋ/ (US), /ˈməʊltɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |蜕皮的; 换毛的|Relating to or undergoing the process of moulting.|The moulting bird looked scruffy.  正在换羽的鸟看起来很邋遢。|Synonyms: shedding, molting|Antonyms: growing|adjective

moult:/moʊlt/ (US), /məʊlt/ (UK)| v. |蜕皮; 换毛|To shed old feathers, hair, skin, or other outer covering, to be replaced by new growth.|The birds moult their feathers in the autumn. 鸟类在秋季换羽。|Synonyms: shed, exuviate|Antonyms: grow|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mūtian, of uncertain origin.  Cognate with German mausen "to moult," Dutch muiten "to moult."
