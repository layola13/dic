convalescence: /ˌkɒnvəˈlɛsns/ (British) /ˌkɑːnvəˈlɛsns/ (American)| n. |康复期；恢复期|The period of gradual recovery of health and strength after illness or injury.  The process of becoming well again after an illness or injury.|例句：She is making a good convalescence after her operation. 她手术后恢复得很好。

例句：He spent a month in convalescence after his heart attack. 他心脏病发作后花了一个月的时间康复。|近义词：recovery, recuperation, rehabilitation|反义词: relapse, deterioration|词性:noun

convalescences: /ˌkɒnvəˈlɛsnsɪz/ (British) /ˌkɑːnvəˈlɛsnsɪz/ (American)| n. |康复期；恢复期（复数）|Plural form of convalescence, referring to multiple periods of recovery.|例句：The hospital specializes in convalescences after major surgeries. 这家医院专门负责大手术后的康复。

例句：Their convalescences were remarkably swift. 他们的康复速度非常快。|近义词：recoveries, recuperations, rehabilitations|反义词: relapses, deteriorations|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *convalescere, from Latin convalescere "to grow strong, regain health," from com- "together" (see com-) + valescere "to grow strong," inchoative of valere "to be strong, be well."
