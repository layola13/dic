compounded:/kəmˈpaʊndɪd/ /ˈkɑːmpaʊndɪd/| adj. v. |复合的，混合的；加重的，恶化的（动词过去分词）|Made worse by other factors; combined.  Resulting from the addition of interest to the principal of a loan or deposit, or interest to interest.|例句：His financial problems were compounded by the loss of his job. 他的经济问题因失去工作而雪上加霜。|近义词：aggravated, worsened, combined, mixed|反义词：simplified, alleviated|词性:形容词/动词

compounded:/kəmˈpaʊndɪd/ /ˈkɑːmpaʊndɪd/| v. |混合，组合；加重，恶化；和解，妥协（动词过去式）|To make something bad worse; to combine something.|例句：He compounded his mistake by lying about it. 他撒谎使他的错误更加严重。|近义词：combine, mix, aggravate, worsen|反义词：separate, alleviate, simplify|词性:动词

Etymology:  From Late Middle English *compounen, from Old French compondre, from Latin componere (“to put together”), from com- (“together”) + ponere (“to put, place”).
