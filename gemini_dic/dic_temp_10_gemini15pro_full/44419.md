cross-peen:/ˈkrɒsˌpiːn/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌpiːn/ (UK)| n. |交叉锤头，十字形锤头|A type of hammer head with a wedge-shaped peen at right angles to the hammer's axis, used for working metal.|The blacksmith used a cross-peen hammer to shape the iron. 铁匠用一把交叉锤头锤来塑形铁块。|Synonyms: cross-pein, peen hammer|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps ball-peen hammer as a contrasting tool)|noun


cross-peen:/ˈkrɒsˌpiːn/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌpiːn/ (UK)| adj. |交叉锤头的|Having a cross-peen head.|He used a cross-peen hammer to create a decorative texture on the metal. 他用一把交叉锤头的锤子在金属上创造出装饰性的纹理。|Synonyms: cross-pein|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From 'cross' + 'peen'.  'Peen' comes from the Middle English 'pene', likely related to the Dutch 'pen' and German 'Pfanne' (pan), referring to the pan-like shape of the original peen. 'Cross' indicates the perpendicular orientation of the peen to the hammer's axis.
