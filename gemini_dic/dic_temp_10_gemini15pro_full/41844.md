corybantiasm:/ˌkɔːrɪˈbæntiæzəm/| n.|狂乱症，精神错乱|A frenzied, ecstatic, and agitated state, originally associated with the rites of the Corybantes, priests of Cybele.|His corybantiasm was evident in his wild dancing and incoherent speech. 他的狂乱症在他疯狂的舞蹈和语无伦次的讲话中表现得淋漓尽致。|Synonyms: frenzy, delirium, mania|Antonyms: calmness, tranquility|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *corybantiasmus, from Ancient Greek κορυβαντισμός (korubantismos), from κορύβας (korúbas, “Corybant”). The Corybantes were ecstatic dancers in the worship of Cybele, the Phrygian goddess.
