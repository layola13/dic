overcurious:/ˌoʊvərˈkjʊəriəs/| adj.| 过分好奇的；多管闲事的|Excessively inquisitive; unduly curious about the affairs of others.| She gave him an overcurious look, which made him uncomfortable. 她过分好奇地看了他一眼，让他感到不舒服。| He was known for his overcurious nature, always poking his nose into other people's business. 他以其多管闲事的性格而闻名，总是爱管别人的闲事。| 近义词：inquisitive, nosy, prying, meddling | 反义词：indifferent, apathetic, uninterested | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From over- + curious.  "Over-" acts as an intensifier, meaning "excessively" or "too much." "Curious" comes from the Latin "curiosus," meaning "careful, inquisitive, diligent."
