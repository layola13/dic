mismatches:/ˌmɪsˈmætʃɪz/ | n. | 不匹配，失配，错配 |  A failure to correspond or match; a discrepancy. |  There were several mismatches between the two datasets. 这两个数据集之间有一些不匹配的地方。 | synonyms: discrepancies, inconsistencies, disparities, incongruities | antonyms: matches, agreements, consistencies, harmonies | noun

mismatch:/ˌmɪsˈmætʃ/ | n. | 不匹配，失配，错配 | A combination of things or people that do not go together well or are not suitable for each other. |  The game was a mismatch, with one team far superior to the other. 这场比赛实力悬殊，其中一支球队远胜于另一支。| synonyms:  incompatibility, disparity, incongruity | antonyms: match, compatibility, suitability | noun

mismatch: /ˌmɪsˈmætʃ/ | vt. | 使不匹配，错误地匹配 | To match (people or things) incorrectly or unsuitably. | The coach mismatched the players, resulting in a crushing defeat. 教练错误地安排了球员，导致惨败。 | synonyms: mispair, misjoin | antonyms: match, pair, join | verb
