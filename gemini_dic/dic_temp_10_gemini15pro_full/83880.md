half-alive:/ˌhæf əˈlaɪv/| adj.|半死不活的，奄奄一息的；缺乏活力的|Partially alive; lacking energy or enthusiasm.  Referring to a state between life and death, or to someone lacking vitality and spirit.|例句：He felt half-alive after working for three days straight. 他连续工作了三天，感觉半死不活的。|例句：The party was dull, and everyone seemed half-alive. 派对很无聊，每个人都看起来无精打采。|近义词：listless, lethargic, languid, spiritless|反义词：energetic, lively, vibrant|词性:形容词

half-alive:/ˌhæf əˈlaɪv/| n.|半死不活的人；缺乏活力的人|A person who is partially alive; a person lacking energy or enthusiasm.|例句：He dragged himself out of bed, feeling like a half-alive. 他拖着身子起床，感觉像个行尸走肉。|例句：Don't be such a half-alive, get up and do something! 别这么死气沉沉的，起来做点什么吧！|近义词：zombie, drone|反义词：dynamo, go-getter|词性:名词
