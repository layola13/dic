fatuousnesses:/fəˈtuːəsnɪsɪz/
| n. |愚蠢；愚昧；昏庸 |The fatuousnesses of the celebrity's pronouncements were widely mocked.  这位名人的声明之愚蠢被广泛嘲笑。|  The sheer fatuousnesses of his suggestion left everyone speechless. 他建议的纯粹愚蠢让每个人都哑口无言。|  foolishness, stupidity, silliness |  intelligence, wisdom, sense | noun


fatuous:/ˈfætʃuəs/
| adj. |愚蠢的；昏庸的；傻的|He made a fatuous remark about the weather. 他对天气做了一个愚蠢的评论。|  It was a fatuous question with no real purpose. 这是一个没有实际意义的愚蠢问题。 | silly, foolish, inane |  intelligent, sensible, wise | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *fatuus* "foolish, insipid, silly,"  related to *fari* "to speak," literally "inspired by a god, prophetic."  The sense evolution is perhaps from "oracular" to "silly, foolish" due to the perceived inanity of oracles.
