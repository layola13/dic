muddle-through:/ˈmʌd.əl θruː/ (UK) /ˈmʌd.əl θruː/ (US)| phrasal verb | 勉强完成；胡乱应付；得过且过 | To achieve something despite lack of organization, planning, or direction.  To cope with a difficult situation in a clumsy or confused way, eventually achieving a degree of success. |  He muddled through the exam, despite not having studied much. 他虽然没怎么学习，但还是勉强通过了考试。

|  The company muddled through the financial crisis, but it wasn't easy.  公司勉强渡过了金融危机，但这并不容易。 | 近义词:  cope, stumble through, get by, make do | 反义词:  excel, succeed brilliantly, plan meticulously | 词性:  phrasal verb

muddle:/ˈmʌd.əl/ (UK) /ˈmʌd.əl/ (US) | n. v. | 混乱；糊涂；弄乱 |  n. a state of disorder or confusion.  v. to bring into a disordered or confusing state. | The paperwork was in a muddle. 文件一片混乱。 | She muddled the figures and got the wrong answer. 她把数字弄混了，得出了错误的答案。 | 近义词: mess, confusion, jumble (n.), confuse, mix up (v.) | 反义词: order, tidiness (n.), organize, arrange (v.) | 词性: noun, verb

through:/θruː/ (UK) /θruː/ (US)| prep. adv. adj.| 通过；穿过；从头到尾；完成 | from one end or side of something to the other. |  He walked through the door. 他走过了门。 |  She slept through the whole movie. 她看电影时睡了一整场。 | 近义词:  via, by means of, from beginning to end | 反义词:  around, bypassing | 词性: preposition, adverb, adjective
