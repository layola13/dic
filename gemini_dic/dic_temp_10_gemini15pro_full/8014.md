anthracite:/ˈænθrəsaɪt/| n.|无烟煤|A hard, compact variety of coal that has a high luster. It has the highest carbon content, the fewest impurities, and the highest calorific content of all types of coal.|The blacksmith used anthracite in his forge because of its intense heat.|近义词:hard coal, black diamond|反义词:lignite, peat|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From French *anthracite*, from Ancient Greek *ἄνθραξ* (*ánthrax*) meaning "coal, charcoal".
