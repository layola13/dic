lie: /laɪ/
lie: v. |躺；位于；平放；展现；撒谎| to be in or assume a horizontal or resting position on a supporting surface; to be located somewhere; to be or remain in a specified state or condition; to present a certain appearance; to make an untrue statement deliberately |例句：The book lies on the table. 书在桌子上。|近义词：recline, rest, prevaricate|反义词：stand, rise, tell the truth|词性:动词

lie: /laɪ/
lie: n. |谎言| a false statement made with deliberate intent to deceive; an intentional untruth |例句：He told a lie. 他撒了个谎。|近义词：falsehood, fib, untruth|反义词：truth|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lēogan "to lie, tell a falsehood,"  and licgan "to lie down, recline," both from Proto-Germanic *leugan/*ligan.  The two words became confused in Middle English.  Related to Dutch liegen and German lügen (to lie, tell a falsehood) and liegen (to lie, recline).
