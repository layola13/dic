embodied:/ɪmˈbɒdid/ (UK) /ɪmˈbɑːdid/ (US)| adj.|体现的，具体化的，人格化的|Having a physical form; representing an idea or quality in a clear and obvious way; incorporated or represented in a concrete form.|She embodied grace and elegance in her movements. 她在举手投足间体现了优雅和高贵。|近义词：personified, incarnate, materialized, manifested|反义词：abstract, incorporeal|词性:形容词

embody:/ɪmˈbɒdi/ (UK) /ɪmˈbɑːdi/ (US)| vt.|体现，使具体化，包含|To give a physical form to; to represent an idea or quality in a clear and obvious way; to include or contain as a constituent part.|The statue embodies the spirit of the city. 这座雕像体现了这座城市的精神。|近义词：represent, express, incorporate, exemplify|反义词：exclude, omit|词性:动词

词源:
From Late Middle English *embodien, from Old French *embodier, from en- + bodī (“body”).
