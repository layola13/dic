mete-out:/miːt ˈaʊt/| v. |给予（通常指惩罚）|to give or order a particular punishment or make someone receive it|例句：The judge meted out a harsh sentence to the criminal. 法官对罪犯处以严厉的判决。

例句：They meted out rewards and punishments fairly. 他们公平地给予奖励和惩罚。|近义词：administer, dispense, dole out, inflict, impose|反义词: pardon, forgive|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *metan "to measure," from Proto-Germanic *metan, from Proto-Indo-European *me- "to measure." The original sense was "to measure out," which developed into the meaning of "to allot" or "to distribute," especially in the context of punishment or justice.
