hardened:/ˈhɑːrdnd/ (US) /ˈhɑːdnd/ (UK)| adj.|变硬的，坚硬的，坚强的，冷酷无情的|Made hard or harder; toughened; unfeeling; callous.|The soldiers were hardened by their experiences in war. 这些士兵因战争经历而变得坚强。|Synonyms: toughened, reinforced, seasoned, inured, callous, obdurate|Antonyms: soft, softened, tender, compassionate, sympathetic|adjective

hardened:/ˈhɑːrdnd/ (US) /ˈhɑːdnd/ (UK)| v.|（使）变硬，（使）坚强|Past participle of "harden".  To make or become hard or harder.|The concrete hardened quickly in the sun. 水泥在阳光下迅速变硬。|Synonyms: solidify, strengthen, toughen, set|Antonyms: soften, weaken|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *heardian, from Proto-Germanic *hardjaną (“to make hard”), from *harduz (“hard”). Cognate with Dutch harden (“to harden”), German härten (“to harden”), Swedish härda (“to harden”).
