biniodide:/baɪˈaɪəˌdaɪd/| n. |二碘化物|A compound containing two iodine atoms per molecule.  Specifically, it often refers to mercury(II) iodide (HgI₂), also known as red mercury iodide.|Mercury(II) iodide is a common example of a biniodide. 二碘化汞是二碘化物的一个常见例子。|diiodide| |noun
Etymology->
From bi- +‎ iodide
