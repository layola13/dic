coachmanship:/ˈkoʊtʃmənˌʃɪp/| n. |御马术；驾车技巧；指导有方|The skill of driving a horse-drawn carriage or coaching a team of horses; also, skill in guiding or directing.|His coachmanship was impeccable, guiding the team of horses through the narrow streets with ease. 他的驾车技术无可挑剔，轻松地驾驶马车穿过狭窄的街道。|近义词：driving skill, guidance, leadership|反义词:incompetence, mismanagement|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From coachman + -ship.  Coachman comes from coach + man. Coach ultimately derives from the Hungarian town of Kocs, where a type of horse-drawn carriage originated.
