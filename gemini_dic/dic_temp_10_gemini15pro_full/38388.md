conceptualism:/kənˈsɛptʃʊəlɪzəm/
n.|概念主义|A philosophy that emphasizes the importance of concepts or mental states in understanding the world. It asserts that knowledge and meaning are derived from our mental grasp of abstract concepts rather than from direct sensory experience.|Conceptualism in art emphasizes the idea behind the work rather than its aesthetic qualities. 概念主义艺术强调作品背后的理念而不是其审美特质。|Conceptual art, conceptual thinking|realism, empiricism|noun
Etymology->
From Late Latin *conceptuālis*, from Latin conceptus (“a conceiving, a thought”), past participle of concipiō (“conceive”), from con- (“together”) + capiō (“take, grasp”).
