The word "moner" is archaic and no longer in common usage.  It refers to a single-celled organism, specifically a prokaryote.  While technically correct, using "prokaryote" or "bacterium" is preferred in modern scientific contexts.

moner:/ˈmoʊnər/
| n. |单细胞生物，原核生物|A single-celled organism; a prokaryote.  This term is outdated.|Example: Monera were once classified as a kingdom including bacteria.  单细胞生物曾经被归类为一个包括细菌的界——原核生物界。|Synonym: prokaryote, bacterium|Antonym: eukaryote|Noun
