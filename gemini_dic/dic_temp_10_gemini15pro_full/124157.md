mortmain:/ˈmɔːrtmeɪn/| n.|土地的永佃权；不动产|Land held inalienably by an ecclesiastical or other corporation, especially for charitable purposes. The state of being permanently owned by a corporation or institution, especially a church or charity.|The property was held in mortmain by the abbey.|近义词:inalienability|反义词:alienability|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Old French *morte main* (“dead hand”), referring to the inability of the land held in mortmain to be sold or transferred.  The term reflects the idea that once property passed into the "dead hand" of a corporation like the Church, it was effectively removed from circulation.
