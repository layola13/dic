outlier:/ˈaʊtˌlaɪər/ | n. | 异常值，局外人 |  A data point that differs significantly from other observations.  A person or thing situated away or detached from the main body or system. | The data point representing $1 million in sales was an outlier, as all other sales were under $10,000.  这个代表一百万美元销售额的数据点是一个异常值，因为所有其他销售额都低于一万美元。| He felt like an outlier in the group, as he didn't share their interests. 他觉得自己在这个群体中格格不入，因为他和他们的兴趣不一样。 | synonym: anomaly, aberration, exception, outsider, maverick | antonym: norm, standard, insider, conformist | noun


onlier: /ˈɒnlaɪər/ | n. | 内围值，正常值 | A data point that lies within the expected range of values in a dataset, in contrast to an outlier. A person or thing belonging to the main body or system. | Most of the data points clustered around the mean, representing the onliers of the dataset.  大多数数据点聚集在平均值附近，代表数据集的正常值。 | As an onlier in the community, she felt comfortable and accepted. 作为社区的一份子，她感到舒适和被接纳。 | synonym: insider, conformist, norm, standard | antonym: outlier, anomaly, aberration, exception, maverick | noun
