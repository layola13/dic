impose:/ɪmˈpəʊz/ (British) /ɪmˈpoʊz/ (American)| v. |强加；征税；施加；利用；欺骗|to force something, such as a belief or way of living, on someone else|The government imposed a new tax on cigarettes. 政府对香烟征收了新的税。|近义词：levy, inflict, enforce, dictate|反义词：remove, lift, abolish|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *imposer, from Latin imponere (to place upon), from in- (in, on) + ponere (to place).
