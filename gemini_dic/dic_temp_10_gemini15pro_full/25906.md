burn oneself out:/bɜːrn wʌnˈsɛlf aʊt/| phrasal verb | 耗尽精力；筋疲力尽 | To become extremely tired or sick by working too hard for too long. |  He burned himself out working too many hours. 他因工作时间过长而筋疲力尽。| exhaust oneself, wear oneself out, overwork | relax, rest | verb phrase
词源:  "Burn" comes from Old English "bærnan," meaning "to kindle, set on fire." "Out" indicates complete consumption or depletion.  The combination creates the figurative sense of complete exhaustion.
