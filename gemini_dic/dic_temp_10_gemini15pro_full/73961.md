fortifications:/ˌfɔːrtɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/| n. |防御工事，筑城，防御工事|Defensive walls, ditches, and other structures built to protect a place from attack.|The city's fortifications were breached by the enemy.  这座城市的防御工事被敌人攻破了。|Synonyms: defenses, ramparts, bulwarks,  |Antonyms: vulnerabilities, weaknesses| noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French fortification, from Late Latin fortificationem, from Latin fortificare "to strengthen," from fortis "strong" + facere "to make."
