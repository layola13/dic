nosegay: /ˈnoʊzɡeɪ/ (US), /ˈnəʊzɡeɪ/ (UK)| n. |花束；小束花 |A small bunch of flowers, typically one that is sweet-scented. |She carried a nosegay of fragrant lilies. 她拿着一束芬芳的百合花。

The bride carried a delicate nosegay.  新娘拿着一束精致的小花束。

|近义词：bouquet, posy, tussie-mussie|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "scattered flowers")|词性: noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *nosegey, from nose + gay ("ornament").  Originally referred to a small bunch of flowers designed to be held to the nose to mask unpleasant smells.
