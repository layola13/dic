cleaver: /ˈkliːvər/ (US) /ˈkliːvə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 切肉刀；剁肉刀；精明强干的人|A heavy knife with a large square blade, used for chopping meat.  A person who is skilled and efficient.|She used a cleaver to chop the bones for the stock. 她用一把切肉刀剁碎骨头熬高汤。 He's a political cleaver. 他是一位精明强干的政治家。|近义词：butcher knife, chopper, meat cleaver; sharp, astute, shrewd|反义词:dullard|词性:noun


cleaver: /ˈkliːvər/ (US) /ˈkliːvə(r)/ (UK)| adj.| 聪明的；精明的；熟练的|Skilled and efficient.|He's a cleaver negotiator. 他是一位精明的谈判者。 She found a cleaver solution to the problem. 她找到了一个巧妙的解决问题的方法。|近义词：smart, intelligent, sharp, astute, shrewd|反义词:stupid, dull|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *clīfre, from Proto-Germanic *klībijaną (“to cleave, split”), from Proto-Indo-European *glei- (“to stick, adhere; to smear”).  Related to cleave (v.).
