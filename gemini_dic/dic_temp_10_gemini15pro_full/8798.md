antiempirically:/ˌæntiɪmˈpɪrɪkli/
| adv. | 反经验地，非经验地 | Based on methods that do not rely on or are opposed to empirical observation or experience.  | Some philosophers argue antiempirically that knowledge is innate. 一些哲学家反经验地论证知识是与生俱来的。 |
|  Rejecting the importance of sensory experience in gaining knowledge.  | He approached the problem antiempirically, relying on intuition and deduction rather than observation.  他反经验地处理这个问题，依靠直觉和演绎而不是观察。 |
| Synonyms:  nonempirically, a priori, theoretically | Antonyms: empirically, experimentally, practically | 词性: 副词
