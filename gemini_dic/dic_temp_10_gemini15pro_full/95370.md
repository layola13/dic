incession:/ɪnˈseʃən/| n.|前进，行进；割让，转让；开始，就任|A formal procession or ceremonial walk, often into a place of importance; the act of cutting or notching, especially surgically; the beginning of a new duty or office.|The procession of graduates into the auditorium was a stately sight. 毕业生进入礼堂的队伍景象庄严。|近义词：procession, advance, inauguration, commencement|反义词：retreat, withdrawal, abdication|词性:noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *incessio, from Latin incessus (“gait, walk, way of walking”), from incedere (“to walk, step, march”), from in- (“in, on”) + cedere (“to go, yield”).
