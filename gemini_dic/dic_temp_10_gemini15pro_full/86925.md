hemorrhage:/ˈhɛmərɪdʒ/ (US), /ˈhɛmərɪdʒ/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.|出血，大量流失|A rapid and profuse loss of blood; also, a rapid and profuse loss of something other than blood, such as employees or money.|The patient suffered a severe hemorrhage after the accident. 病人在事故后大出血。|Synonyms: bleeding, loss, outflow|Antonyms: clotting, coagulation|noun, verb

hemorrhage:/ˈhɛmərɪdʒ/ (US), /ˈhɛmərɪdʒ/ (UK)| vi. vt.|大量流失，大量减少|To lose or cause to lose something, such as employees or money, rapidly and in large quantities.|The company hemorrhaged money after the failed product launch. 公司在产品发布失败后损失惨重。|Synonyms: drain, deplete, lose|Antonyms: gain, accumulate|verb
