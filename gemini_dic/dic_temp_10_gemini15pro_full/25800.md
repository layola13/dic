burganet:/ˈbɜːrɡənɪt/| n. |轻盔，锁子甲兜帽|A light helmet, typically a mail coif.|The knight wore a burganet under his great helm.|Synonym: coif, mail hood|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "bareheaded")|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *burguinette*, diminutive of *burguignon*, meaning "Burgundian".  Burgundy was a region renowned for its metalworking, especially armor.
