metalepsis: /ˌmɛtəˈlɛpsɪs/ | n. | 转喻; 超越; 借代 | A figure of speech in which a word or phrase from one domain of experience is used in another, often by way of an implied comparison or analogy. It involves a "transference of meaning" and can create a surprising or unexpected effect.  |  The city never sleeps. (This is a metalepsis, as cities don't literally sleep. It refers to the activity that continues day and night.)  |  synonym: metonymy, synecdoche, metaphor  |  antonym:  (No direct antonym exists for a literary device) | noun
