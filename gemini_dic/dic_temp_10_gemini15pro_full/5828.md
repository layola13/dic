amateur:/ˈæmətər/ (US), /ˈæmətə(r)/ (UK)| n. adj.|业余爱好者；外行；业余的|A person who engages in a pursuit, especially a sport, on an unpaid rather than a professional basis.  Also used to describe something done by or characteristic of such a person.|例句：He's an amateur photographer. 他是一位业余摄影师。|近义词：nonprofessional, dilettante, novice|反义词：professional, expert|词性: noun, adjective

amateur:/ˈæmətər/ (US), /ˈæmətə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|业余的；外行的|Done by or characteristic of a nonprofessional.|例句：The acting was very amateur.  表演很业余。|近义词：unprofessional, inexpert|反义词：professional, expert|词性: adjective

词源:
From French *amateur*, from Latin *amator* "lover, devotee," from *amare* "to love."  Originally referred to someone who engages in an activity for the love of it, rather than for financial gain.
