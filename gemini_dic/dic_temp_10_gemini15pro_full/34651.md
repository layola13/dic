clepsydra: /ˈklɛpsɪdrə/ | n. | 水钟 | A device for measuring time by the regulated flow of water, used in ancient times.  An ancient time-keeping device in which time was measured by the regulated flow of liquid into or out of a container. |  The museum displays a working clepsydra.  博物馆展出了一座还能运作的水钟。| synonym: water clock | antonym:  digital clock | noun

clepsydra: /ˈklɛpsɪdrə/ | n. |  计时器 | Something that measures the passage of time, especially by the flow of a liquid, although the term can be applied more broadly. |  The dripping faucet acted as a makeshift clepsydra, marking the slow passage of the night.  滴水的水龙头成了一个临时计时器，标志着夜晚的缓慢流逝。| synonym: timer, chronometer | antonym: stopwatch | noun
