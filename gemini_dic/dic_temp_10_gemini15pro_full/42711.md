countertripping:/ˈkaʊntərˌtrɪpɪŋ/
n.|反制，反击，对抗|The act of taking an action in response to another action, often to neutralize or negate its effect.  Specifically, in a military or political context, it refers to actions taken to counteract an enemy's offensive or strategy.|The government employed countertripping measures to combat the spread of misinformation.|Synonyms: countermeasure, counteraction, retaliation|Antonyms: inaction, passivity|noun
