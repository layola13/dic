misled:/ˌmɪsˈled/ | v. | 误导，欺骗 | to cause someone to believe something that is not true |  I was misled by the advertisement and bought a faulty product. 我被广告误导了，买了一个有缺陷的产品。

The map misled us, and we ended up lost in the woods. 地图误导了我们，我们最后在树林里迷路了。 | 近义词：deceived, fooled, tricked | 反义词: guided, informed, enlightened | 词性: 动词 (past tense and past participle of mislead)
