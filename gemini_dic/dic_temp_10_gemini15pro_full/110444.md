Etymology:
From Middle English *likerous, from Old English *licera, *licor, from Proto-Germanic *likraz (“greedy, lustful”), from Proto-Indo-European *leik- (“to lick”). Cognate with Dutch lekker (“delicious”), German lecker (“delicious”).  The sense development is from "eager to lick" to "greedy" to "lustful".
