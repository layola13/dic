domesticate: /dəˈmɛstɪkeɪt/ (US) /dəˈmɛstɪkeɪt/ (UK)
|v. adj.|驯养，驯化；教化|To adapt (a plant or animal) to life in intimate association with and to the advantage of humans. To make (something) fit for domestic use or life.|例句：Humans domesticated the dog thousands of years ago.  人类几千年前就驯化了狗。|近义词：tame, train, cultivate|反义词：wild, feral|词性：动词
domesticate: /dəˈmɛstɪkeɪtɪd/ (US) /dəˈmɛstɪkeɪtɪd/ (UK)
|adj.|驯养的，驯化的|Adapted to life in intimate association with and to the advantage of humans.|例句：Domesticated animals are often dependent on humans for food and shelter.  驯养的动物通常依赖人类提供食物和住所。|近义词：tame, trained|反义词：wild, feral|词性：形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin domesticatus, past participle of domesticare "to tame, domesticate," from Latin domesticus "belonging to the house," from domus "house."
