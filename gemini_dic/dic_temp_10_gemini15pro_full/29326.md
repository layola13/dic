castigative: /ˈkæstɪˌɡeɪtɪv/
adjective
惩罚的，责骂的
Intended to correct or punish someone.
例句：He delivered a castigative speech about the team's poor performance. 他就团队糟糕的表现发表了一番惩罚性的讲话。

例句：Her tone was castigative, leaving no room for excuses. 她的语气是责备的，不容许任何借口。

近义词：reproachful, rebuking, censorious, admonishing, chiding
反义词：praising, laudatory, complimentary, encouraging
词性：adjective

castigate: /ˈkæstɪɡeɪt/
verb
严厉批评；谴责
To reprimand (someone) severely.
例句：The judge castigated the lawyer for his disrespectful behavior. 法官严厉谴责了律师的不尊重行为。

例句：He was castigated by his parents for failing his exams. 他因考试不及格而受到父母的严厉批评。

近义词：reprimand, rebuke, censure, admonish, chide, scold, upbraid
反义词：praise, commend, laud, applaud
词性：verb

词源:
From Latin castigatus, past participle of castigare (“to correct, chastise”), from castus (“pure”) + agere (“to drive, lead, do”).
