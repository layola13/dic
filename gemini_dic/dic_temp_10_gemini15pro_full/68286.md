falanges: /fəˈlændʒiːz/ (plural) /ˈfælæŋks/ (singular)
| n. |指骨;趾骨|Phalanges are the bones that make up the fingers and toes. They are long bones and are essential for grasping, manipulating objects, and walking.|The doctor examined the X-ray of the patient's hand and identified a fractured phalanx. 医生检查了病人手的X光片，发现一根指骨骨折。

The gorilla's phalanges are remarkably long and strong. 大猩猩的指骨非常长而强壮。 |近义词：digit bones|反义词: (No direct antonym for a bone) |词性: Noun


falalanx: /ˈfælæŋks/
| n. |方阵;密集队形|A phalanx is a close-knit body of troops or people, standing or moving in close formation.|The Roman legions advanced in a tight phalanx, their shields forming a protective wall. 罗马军团以紧密的方阵前进，他们的盾牌形成了一道防护墙。

Protesters linked arms and formed a human phalanx to block the street. 抗议者手挽着手，组成人墙挡住了街道。 |近义词：formation, array, legion|反义词:disorder, scattered group |词性: Noun


词源:
From Latin *phalangēs*, plural of *phalanx*, from Ancient Greek *φάλαγξ* (phálanx, “line of battle, log, roller”).
