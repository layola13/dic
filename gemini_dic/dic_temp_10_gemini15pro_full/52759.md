dilatator:/ˈdaɪleɪtər/ (US), /daɪˈleɪtə/ (UK)| n.|扩张器, 扩张肌|A device or muscle that causes dilation (widening or expansion) of an opening or tubular structure, such as a blood vessel or the pupil of the eye.|The surgeon used a dilatator to widen the opening of the wound.|Synonyms: expander, stretcher|Antonyms: constrictor, compressor|noun


dilate:/daɪˈleɪt/ (US), /daɪˈleɪt/ (UK)| v.| 扩张，扩大|To make or become wider, larger, or more open.|The ophthalmologist used eye drops to dilate the pupils.|Synonyms: expand, enlarge, widen|Antonyms: contract, constrict, shrink|verb
