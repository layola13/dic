abacuses: /ˈæbəkəsiːz/ (US), /ˈæbəkəsiːz/ (UK)| n. |算盘|A counting frame with rows of beads or counters slid along rods or wires, used for performing calculations.|The shopkeeper still uses abacuses to calculate the total cost. = 店主仍然使用算盘来计算总成本。

Abacuses were used for centuries before electronic calculators were invented. = 在电子计算器发明之前，算盘已经使用了几个世纪。|近义词：counting frame, calculator|反义词: calculator (in a modern context)|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *abacus*, borrowed from Greek *ἄβαξ* (ábax, “counting board, tablet for drawing”), probably from a Northwest Semitic source such as Phoenician *𐤀𐤁𐤒*‎ (ʾbq, “sand, dust”), referring to the use of sand for drawing or counting.
