minimelodrama:/ˌmɪniˈmɛləˌdrɑːmə/ |/ˌmɪniˈmɛləˌdrɑːmə/| n. |小型情节剧；小题大做|A short, exaggeratedly dramatic episode or situation, often trivial in nature.  A small-scale drama, often exaggerated or over-the-top.|

例句：
Their argument about the TV remote control turned into a minimelodrama.
他们关于电视遥控器的争论变成了一场小题大做。

例句：
He tends to make a minimelodrama out of every minor inconvenience.
他倾向于把每一个小麻烦都搞成一场小题大做。


近义词：fuss, overreaction, histrionics|
反义词: understatement, calmness|
词性: noun|

词源:
"Mini-" (prefix meaning small) + "melodrama" (from Greek "melos" meaning "song" + "drama").  The term combines the idea of a small-scale version of a melodrama, which traditionally involves exaggerated emotions and dramatic situations.
