neoplatonism:/ˌniːoʊˈpleɪtənɪzəm/ | n. | 新柏拉图主义 | A philosophical system that originated in the 3rd century AD, based on the teachings of Plato but incorporating elements of other philosophies, especially Aristotelianism and Stoicism. It emphasized the spiritual and mystical aspects of Plato's thought, positing a hierarchy of being emanating from a single, transcendent source known as "The One." |  Example: Neoplatonism influenced many medieval and Renaissance thinkers. 新柏拉图主义影响了许多中世纪和文艺复兴时期的思想家。

| Example:  Plotinus is considered the founder of Neoplatonism. 普罗提诺被认为是新柏拉图主义的创始人。| 近义词: idealism, mysticism | 反义词: materialism, empiricism | 词性: noun
