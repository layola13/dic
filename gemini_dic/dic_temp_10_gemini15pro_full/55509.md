distraughtness:/dɪˈstrɔːtnəs/| n.|心烦意乱，极度悲伤，精神错乱|The distraughtness of the parents after the loss of their child was palpable. 孩子去世后，父母的悲痛之情显而易见。|例句：Her distraughtness was evident in her trembling voice. 她的声音颤抖着，显得很烦乱。

Her distraughtness over the accident made it difficult for her to speak.  她对事故的悲痛让她难以开口。|近义词：agitation, distress, anguish, despair|反义词：calmness, composure, serenity|词性:noun

Etymology: From distraught + -ness.  Distraught itself comes from Middle English *distraught, past participle of distracten, from Old French destraught, past participle of destraire "to distract," from Latin distractus, past participle of distrahere "to pull apart, divide, distract," from dis- "apart" + trahere "to draw, pull."
