draconian:/drəˈkoʊniən/| adj.|严酷的，苛刻的|Excessively harsh and severe, especially of laws and punishments.  Derived from Draco, an Athenian law-maker of the 7th century BC, whose code of laws prescribed the death penalty for even minor offenses.|The government imposed draconian restrictions on freedom of speech. 政府对言论自由施加了严厉的限制。|Synonyms: harsh, severe, stringent, rigorous, oppressive|Antonyms: lenient, merciful, moderate, mild|adjective
