overvaluable:/ˌoʊvərˈvæljuəbl/ | adj. | 过于贵重的，估价过高的 |  Too highly valued; overpriced.  An item or asset is considered overvaluable when its perceived worth exceeds its actual market value or intrinsic value. This can happen due to speculation, hype, or sentimental attachment. | This antique chair is overvaluable; it's not worth that much. 这把古董椅子估价过高；它不值那么多钱。| The company's stock is overvaluable, making it a risky investment.  这家公司的股票估价过高，使其成为一项高风险投资。|近义词：overpriced, overestimated | 反义词: undervalued, underestimated | 词性: adjective

词源:  "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "valuable" (adjective meaning "having great worth").  The prefix "over-" intensifies the meaning of "valuable" to signify an excessive or inflated valuation.
