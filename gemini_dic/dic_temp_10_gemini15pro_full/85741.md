hawk:/hɔːk/
n.
鹰；鹰派人物；骗子；清嗓子
a bird of prey with broad rounded wings and a long tail, typically taking prey by surprise with a short swift dive; a person who advocates an aggressive or warlike policy, especially in foreign affairs; a swindler, cheat
例句：The hawk soared high above the mountains.  鹰在群山上空翱翔。

例句：He was known as a hawk in the Senate, always pushing for military intervention. 他在参议院中被认为是鹰派人物，总是推动军事干预。

近义词: falcon, eagle (for the bird); warmonger (for the political sense)
反义词: dove (for the political sense)
词性: Noun


hawk:/hɔːk/
v.
兜售；咳嗽；清嗓子；放鹰狩猎
to sell goods aggressively, especially by going from place to place; to cough or clear one's throat noisily.
例句：Street vendors hawked their wares to passersby.  街头小贩向路人兜售他们的商品。

例句：He hawked up some phlegm. 他咳出了一些痰。

近义词: peddle, vend, sell
反义词: buy, purchase
词性: Verb
