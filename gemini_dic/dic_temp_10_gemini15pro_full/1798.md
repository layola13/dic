acridest:/ˈækrɪdɪst/| adj.|最辛辣的，最刺激的|Superlative form of acrid: having an irritatingly strong and unpleasant taste or smell.| The acridest fumes came from the burning tires. 燃烧的轮胎冒出最刺鼻的浓烟。|近义词：bitterest, harshest, sharpest, most pungent|反义词:sweetest, mildest|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *ācer* (“sharp, pungent, bitter”), related to *ācris* (“sharp”) and *acus* (“needle”).

USAGE->
The word "acridest" is the superlative form of "acrid" and is used to describe the most intense degree of an unpleasant, sharp, or bitter taste or smell.  Because it's a superlative, its usage often involves comparisons.

Example 1: The acridest smell I've ever encountered was from the burning plastic.  我遇到过的最刺鼻的气味是燃烧塑料产生的。

Example 2: Of all the chilies, the habanero had the acridest flavor.  在所有辣椒中，哈瓦那辣椒的味道最辛辣。
