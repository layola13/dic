disappointment:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt/ | n. |失望，沮丧；令人失望的人或事|A feeling of sadness or displeasure caused by the non-fulfillment of one's hopes or expectations. |The cancellation of the trip was a big disappointment. 旅行取消了，真是太令人失望了。|近义词：frustration, letdown, dissatisfaction|反义词：satisfaction, fulfillment, joy|词性:名词

disappoint:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt/ | vt. |使失望，辜负|To fail to fulfill the hopes or expectations of. |I hate to disappoint you, but I can't come to the party. 我很不想让你失望，但我不能参加聚会了。|近义词：let down, frustrate, displease|反义词：satisfy, please, delight|词性:动词

词源:
From Middle French *desapointier, from des- (expressing negation) + apointier ("to appoint, arrange").
