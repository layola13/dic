manicheisms:/ˈmænɪkiːɪzəmz/ | n. |摩尼教主义；二元论；绝对的善恶观|A system of thought, especially in morals or religion, that divides things into absolute good and evil, light and darkness, without allowing for shades of grey or compromise.|His political rhetoric often falls into simplistic Manicheisms, portraying his opponents as purely evil. 他的政治言论经常陷入简单的二元论，将他的对手描绘成纯粹的邪恶。|Synonyms: dualism, absolutism |Antonyms: relativism, nuance |noun
