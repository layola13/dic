misgoverned:/ˌmɪsˈɡʌvənd/ | adj. | 管理不善的，治理不佳的 | Ruled or managed badly or wrongly. | The country was misgoverned for years, leading to widespread poverty. 这个国家多年来治理不善，导致普遍贫困。| The misgoverned city was plagued by crime and corruption. 这个管理不善的城市充满了犯罪和腐败。 |  Synonyms: mismanaged, misruled, ill-governed | Antonyms: well-governed, well-managed, well-ruled | adjective

Etymology:
From mis- + govern + -ed.  "Mis-" is a prefix of Old English and Germanic origin meaning "wrong," "bad," or "wrongly." "Govern" comes from the Old French "governer," ultimately from the Latin "gubernare" meaning "to steer, guide, or rule."  "-ed" is a suffix forming the past participle.
