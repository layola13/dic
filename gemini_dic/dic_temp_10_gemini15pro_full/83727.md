haiduck:/ˈhaɪdʌk/| n.|海盗，强盗，暴徒；(旧时)匈牙利或波兰步兵|A soldier, especially one acting as a bandit or mercenary.  Originally, a member of bands of outlaws infesting central and southeastern Europe in the 17th and 18th centuries.|The haiduks roamed the countryside, stealing from the rich and giving to the poor.  这些海盗在乡间游荡，劫富济贫。|Synonyms: bandit, brigand, outlaw, mercenary|Antonyms: lawman, soldier (in a regular army), police|noun


haiduck:/ˈhaɪdʌk/| n.|爪牙，走狗；帮凶|A ruthless subordinate or henchman.|He acted as the dictator's haiduck, carrying out his dirty work. 他充当独裁者的爪牙，为他干脏活。|Synonyms: henchman, minion, thug, goon|Antonyms:  protector, guardian, defender|noun
