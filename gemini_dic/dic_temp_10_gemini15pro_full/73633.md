forfeit:/ˈfɔːrfɪt/ (US) /ˈfɔːfɪt/ (UK)| n. vt. adj.|丧失，罚款；没收；罚金；被没收的|Something lost or given up as a penalty for a fault, mistake, or offense.  The act of losing or surrendering something as a penalty.|He had to forfeit his driver's license after the DUI.  他因酒驾而不得不吊销驾照。|Synonyms: lose, surrender, relinquish, sacrifice|Antonyms: gain, win, earn, acquire|noun

forfeit:/ˈfɔːrfɪt/ (US) /ˈfɔːfɪt/ (UK)|vt.|丧失，失去（尤指因犯错而失去）|To lose or be deprived of (property or a right or privilege) as a penalty for wrongdoing.|She forfeited her right to a trial by pleading guilty.  她因认罪而丧失了受审的权利。|Synonyms: lose, surrender, relinquish, sacrifice|Antonyms: gain, win, earn, acquire|verb

forfeit:/ˈfɔːrfɪt/ (US) /ˈfɔːfɪt/ (UK)|adj.|被没收的；丧失了的|Lost or surrendered as a penalty.|His driving license was forfeit after the accident.  事故发生后，他的驾驶执照被吊销了。|Synonyms: lost, surrendered, relinquished, sacrificed|Antonyms: gained, won, earned, acquired|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *forfet, from forfaire "to transgress, forfeit," from fors- "outside, beyond" (from Latin foris) + faire "to do" (from Latin facere).
