choleric: /ˈkɑːlərɪk/ (US), /kəˈlɛrɪk/ (UK)| adj.|易怒的，暴躁的|bad-tempered; easily angered|例句：His choleric temperament made him difficult to work with.  他暴躁的脾气使他很难与人共事。

例句：The choleric old man yelled at the children playing in his yard.  那个脾气暴躁的老人对着在他院子里玩耍的孩子们大喊大叫。

近义词：irascible, irritable, cantankerous, bad-tempered, short-tempered|反义词：calm, placid, even-tempered, affable|词性: 形容词

词源: Late Middle English: from Old French colerique, via Latin from Greek kholerikos, from kholē ‘bile’.  The bodily humors were formerly believed to determine temperament, and choler (yellow bile) was associated with anger.
