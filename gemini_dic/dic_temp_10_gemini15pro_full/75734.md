fullerene:/ˈfʊləˌriːn/| n. |富勒烯|A form of carbon having a large spheroidal molecule consisting of a hollow cage of sixty or more atoms.  It's named after Buckminster Fuller, the architect who designed geodesic domes that resemble the structure of fullerenes.|Scientists are exploring the potential uses of fullerenes in nanotechnology. 科学家们正在探索富勒烯在纳米技术中的潜在用途。| C60, buckyball| |noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From the name of Richard Buckminster Fuller (1895–1983), American architect + -ene (indicating an unsaturated hydrocarbon). Fuller designed geodesic domes which resemble the structure of fullerenes.
