ballyhooer:/ˈbæliˌhuːər/| n. |大声宣传者；大肆吹嘘者|Someone who promotes or publicizes something in a loud or exaggerated manner.|The ballyhooer stood on the corner shouting about the amazing deals inside the store. 大声宣传者站在街角叫喊着店内惊人的优惠。|近义词：promoter, publicist, barker, huckster|反义词：critic, detractor|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From ballyhoo (n.) "noisy advertising or propaganda," 1890s, American English, of unknown origin. Perhaps related to the circus exclamation "balley-ho!" used in Japan in the 1860s, perhaps from a mishearing of Japanese.
