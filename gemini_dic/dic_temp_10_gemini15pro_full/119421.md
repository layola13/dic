microfracturing: /ˌmaɪkroʊˈfræktʃərɪŋ/ | n. | 微骨折术 |  A surgical technique used to stimulate cartilage growth in joints by creating tiny fractures in the underlying bone. This allows blood and bone marrow to seep into the area, forming a clot that eventually develops into fibrocartilage, a type of repair tissue. |  Microfracturing is often used to treat cartilage defects in the knee. 微骨折术常用于治疗膝关节软骨缺损。|  Synonyms: subchondral drilling, marrow stimulation | Antonyms: cartilage regeneration (a broader term, microfracturing is a type of cartilage regeneration technique) | noun
