folkland:/ˈfoʊklænd/| n.|民众之土，公共土地；（尤指）盎格鲁-撒克逊时代英国国王的领地|Land held by folkright, especially the land of the English king in Anglo-Saxon times.|The king granted a portion of the folkland to the church.|近义词:common land, public land|反义词:private land|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English folcland ("land of the people"), from folc ("people, folk") + land ("land").  The first element is cognate with Dutch volk, German Volk, and Latin vulgus. The second element is cognate with Dutch land, German Land, and Latin fundus.
