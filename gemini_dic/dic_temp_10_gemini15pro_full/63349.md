entomb:/ɪnˈtuːm/ /ɛnˈtuːm/| v. |埋葬，安葬；使深陷于|To bury or enclose in a tomb or as if in a tomb.|The pharaohs were entombed in pyramids. 法老们被埋葬在金字塔里。|The avalanche entombed the skiers. 雪崩掩埋了滑雪者。|近义词：bury, inter, sepulcher, enshrine|反义词：exhume, unearth|词性:动词

entomb:/ɪnˈtuːm/ /ɛnˈtuːm/| n. |（通常指大型或重要的）坟墓，陵墓 (rare usage as a noun) | A tomb, usually large or important. |  His final entomb was in the family mausoleum. 他最终被安葬在家族陵墓中。 | This ancient crypt served as the entomb for generations of royalty.  这座古老的地窖是几代皇室的陵墓。|近义词：tomb, sepulcher, mausoleum|反义词：N/A|词性:名词 (rare)


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *entomber*, from en- (“in”) + tombe (“tomb”), from Latin tumba (“tomb, mound”).
