augurate: /ˈɔːɡjəreɪt/ | v. | 开创，预示 | to mark the beginning of something; to serve as an omen or promise of something | The new CEO's speech augurated a new era for the company.  新任首席执行官的讲话开启了公司的新纪元。|  The sighting of the rare bird augurated good luck.  看到这只珍稀的鸟预示着好运。 | synonyms: inaugurate, initiate, portend, foreshadow | antonyms: terminate, conclude, end | verb

auguration: /ˌɔːɡjəˈreɪʃn/ | n. |  开创，就职典礼，预兆 | the act of beginning something new, typically with a ceremony; an omen or sign | The inauguration of the president was a grand affair. 总统的就职典礼是一场盛大的活动。| The auguration of the new building project was marked by a groundbreaking ceremony. 新建项目的开工仪式标志着项目的启动。 | synonyms: inauguration, commencement, initiation, omen, portent | antonyms: termination, conclusion, end | noun
