break the ice: /breɪk ði aɪs/ | idiom (verb phrase) | 破冰；打破僵局；缓和气氛 | To do or say something to relieve tension or get conversation started, especially at the beginning of a meeting or party. |  At the start of the meeting, the manager told a joke to break the ice.  经理在会议开始时讲了个笑话来打破僵局。

| Example:  The game was a good way to break the ice among the new students.  这个游戏是让新生们互相认识的好方法。

| Synonyms:  get the ball rolling, start the conversation, make a start, ease the tension, warm up the atmosphere

| Antonyms:  stiffen up, clam up

| Part of Speech:  idiomatic phrase (verbal in function)
