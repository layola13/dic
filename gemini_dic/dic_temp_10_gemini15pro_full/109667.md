leucoline:/ˈluːkəˌliːn/, /ˈljuːkəˌlaɪn/ | n. |喹啉, 白线 |A colorless, oily liquid with a disagreeable odor, obtained from coal tar and bone oil. It is a heterocyclic aromatic organic compound. It is used in the manufacture of dyes, pharmaceuticals, and other chemicals.  Also called quinoline.|Leucoline is used in the synthesis of certain antimalarial drugs. 白线用于合成某些抗疟疾药物。|Synonyms: quinoline | Antonyms: None | Noun
