crossing: /ˈkrɒsɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈkrɔːsɪŋ/ (US)| n.|  穿越，渡过|The act of going across something such as a road, river, or border. | The crossing to the island took three hours. 到岛上的航程花了三个小时。| synonym: traversal, passage | antonym:  return, retreat | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *crossinge*, verbal noun from *crossen* (“to cross”), from Old Norse *krossa* (“to mark with a cross, bless”), from *kross* (“cross”).  Ultimately from Latin *crux* (“cross”).
