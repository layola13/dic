hoodwink:/hʊdˈwɪŋk/
| vt.|欺骗，蒙蔽|To deceive or trick someone. | He hoodwinked them into investing in his bogus scheme. 他欺骗他们投资他的虚假项目。|
| |deceive, trick, dupe, swindle, cheat| |verb|
hoodwink:/hʊdˈwɪŋk/
| n.|欺骗，蒙蔽 (较少用)|An act of deceiving or tricking someone. |The whole affair was a hoodwink designed to get us to invest. 整件事就是一个骗局，目的是让我们投资。|
| |deception, trickery, fraud, swindle| |noun|

Etymology: From the late 16th century.  Likely from *hood wink*, meaning to blindfold or cover someone's eyes with a hood, thus making them easy to deceive.  The original meaning referred to a game similar to blind man's bluff.
