nonfading:/ˌnɒnˈfeɪdɪŋ/ /ˌnɑːnˈfeɪdɪŋ/ | adj. | 不褪色的，持久的 | Not losing freshness or intensity of color; permanent. | The nonfading beauty of the ancient city captivated her. 这座古城的永恒之美深深吸引了她。 |  The artist used nonfading pigments to ensure the longevity of his work.  这位艺术家使用不褪色的颜料来确保作品的持久性。 | 近义词：permanent, lasting, enduring, indelible | 反义词：fading, temporary, ephemeral | 词性：形容词

Etymology:
non- + fading

"non-"  is a prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of".  It comes from Latin.

"fading" comes from the Middle English "faden,"  meaning "to lose freshness or color," from Old English "fægan"  meaning "to be glad or joyful." The sense shifted over time to describe the loss of vibrancy or intensity, as joy can fade.
