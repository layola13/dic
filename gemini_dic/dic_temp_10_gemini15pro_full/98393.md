insolubilities: /ɪnˌsɒljʊˈbɪlɪtiz/ | n. | 不溶性；不溶解性 | The state or quality of being insoluble; inability to dissolve.  |  The insolubilities of some compounds make them useful in specific applications. 某些化合物的不可溶性使它们在特定应用中很有用。 | The insolubilities of certain pesticides in water contribute to their persistence in the environment.  某些杀虫剂在水中的不溶性导致它们在环境中持续存在。 | 近义词：insolubility, undissolvedness | 反义词：solubilities, solubility | 词性: 名词

insolubility: /ɪnˌsɒljʊˈbɪləti/ | n. | 不溶性 | The inability of a substance to dissolve in a particular solvent. | The insolubility of oil in water is a well-known phenomenon. 油在水中不溶解是一个众所周知的现象。 |  Due to its insolubility, the compound precipitated out of the solution. 由于其不溶性，该化合物从溶液中沉淀出来。 | 近义词：insolubleness, undissolvedness | 反义词：solubility | 词性: 名词


词源:  From in- (not) + soluble (capable of being dissolved) + -ity (state or quality).  最终源自拉丁语 solvere (to loosen, dissolve).
