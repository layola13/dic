damned:/dæmd/ | adj. | 該死的，可惡的，非常的 | Used to express anger or annoyance, or to emphasize something.  From Old English *dēmd, past participle of dēman "to judge, deem," from Proto-Germanic *domjan "to form a judgment, think." | That damned dog barked all night! 那該死的狗叫了一整夜！| Synonyms: cursed, blasted, bloody (British) | Antonyms: blessed, sacred | adjective

damned:/dæmd/ | adv. | 非常，极其 | Used to emphasize something, especially something negative. |  It was damned difficult to find a parking space. 找到一個停車位真是太難了。 | Synonyms: extremely, very, awfully | Antonyms: slightly, a little | adverb
