cadastral:/kəˈdæstrəl/
adjective
地籍的
Relating to an official register of the quantity, value, and ownership of land, for taxation purposes.
The cadastral survey accurately mapped the boundaries of each property. 地籍测量准确地绘制了每个房产的边界。

cadastral:/kəˈdæstrəl/
adjective
地籍的
Relating to a public record, survey, or map of the value, extent, and ownership of land as a basis of taxation.
The government is updating the cadastral maps to reflect recent property transactions. 政府正在更新地籍地图以反映最近的房产交易。


cadastral:/kəˈdæstrəl/
adjective
地籍的
Of or relating to a cadastre.
Cadastral information is essential for property management and urban planning.  地籍信息对于物业管理和城市规划至关重要。




ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cadastral*, from Italian *catastrale*, from *catastro* (“register for a tax assessment”), from Late Latin *catastrum*, probably from Greek κατάστιχον (katástikhon, “line by line, in rows”), from κατά (katá, “down”) + στίχος (stíkhos, “row, line”).

SYNONYM->
land registry
survey


ANTONYM-> (No direct antonym for "cadastral".  The concept implies organization and record-keeping, so antonyms would relate to lack of those qualities, but not be direct opposites in meaning.)
unrecorded
unregistered
informal
