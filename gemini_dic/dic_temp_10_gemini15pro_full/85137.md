hardline:/ˈhɑːrdlaɪn/ | adj. n. |强硬的，强硬路线的；强硬派人士|Uncompromising and unyielding in one's views or policies.  A firm and uncompromising stance or position.|例句：The senator took a hardline stance on immigration.  这位参议员对移民问题采取了强硬立场。|例句：He is known for his hardline views on law and order. 他以在法律和秩序问题上的强硬观点而闻名。|近义词：inflexible, uncompromising, unyielding, firm, rigid|反义词：flexible, compromising, moderate, lenient|词性:形容词,名词
hard-liner:/ˈhɑːrdˌlaɪnər/ | n. |强硬派人士|A person who adheres rigidly to a set of principles, especially political ones.|例句：The hard-liners in the party refused to compromise. 党内的强硬派拒绝妥协。|例句：She's considered a hard-liner on environmental issues. 她被认为是环境问题上的强硬派。|近义词：extremist, zealot, diehard|反义词：moderate, centrist|词性:名词
