instincts:/ˈɪnstɪŋkts/| n.|本能，直觉|An innate, typically fixed pattern of behavior in animals in response to certain stimuli.|例句：Animals rely on their instincts to survive. 动物依靠本能生存。

例句：Maternal instincts told her to protect her young. 母性本能告诉她要保护她的孩子。|近义词：intuition, hunch, feeling, impulse|反义词：reasoning, logic|词性：名词

词源: From Late Latin instinctus "instigation, impulse," past participle of instinguere "to incite, impel," from in- "on, upon" + stinguere "to prick, incite."
