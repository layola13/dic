bruiter:/ˈbruːtər/ (US), /ˈbruːɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 散布谣言者;  传播者|A person who spreads rumors or reports, especially of an official nature.|The bruiter of this false news was never identified. 这个假消息的散布者身份从未被确认。|Synonyms: rumormonger, gossipmonger, scandalmonger,  propagandist, disseminator|Antonyms: suppressor, censor|noun

bruite:/bruːt/ (US), /bruːt/ (UK)| v.| 散布; 传播 (尤指消息或谣言)|To spread or report (something, especially news or a rumour).|The news of the royal engagement was bruited about the country. 皇家订婚的消息传遍了全国。|Synonyms:  spread, circulate, disseminate, promulgate, report, noise abroad, broadcast, rumour|Antonyms: suppress, conceal, withhold|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *bruire* "to make a noise, roar," from Old French *bruire* "to roar, make a noise," from Vulgar Latin *brugere*, perhaps of imitative origin.  The English verb "bruite" and the noun "bruiter" are derived from this French root.
