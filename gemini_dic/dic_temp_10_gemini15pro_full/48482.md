deep-sixing:/ˌdiːpˈsɪksɪŋ/ | v. |  销毁，抛弃，彻底放弃 | To dispose of or discard something completely, often secretly.  It originates from nautical slang, referring to discarding something so deep in the ocean (six fathoms) that it will never be recovered. |  They were accused of deep-sixing evidence that could have exonerated the defendant. 他们被指控销毁了可以证明被告无罪的证据。 | The company deep-sixed the project after it became clear it would be unprofitable.  这家公司在项目明显无利可图后彻底放弃了它。 | synonyms: discard, ditch, scrap, abandon, jettison | antonyms: retain, keep, preserve, salvage | verb
