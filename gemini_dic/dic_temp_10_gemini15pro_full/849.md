abstractional:/æbˈstrækʃənəl/| adj.|抽象的|Relating to abstraction; considered apart from concrete realities, specific objects, or actual instances.  Characterized by or involving the process of abstracting. |His paintings moved towards a more abstract and abstractionist style. 他的绘画风格变得更加抽象和抽象主义。 The process of learning to read involves abstractionist thought. 学习阅读的过程涉及抽象思维。|近义词：abstract, theoretical, conceptual, notional, philosophical, metaphysical|反义词：concrete, specific, tangible, real, practical|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *abstractionalis*, from Latin abstractionem (nominative abstractio) "a drawing away, separation," from abstractus, past participle of abstrahere "to draw away," from abs- "away" + trahere "to draw."  The English word was first used in the 15th century.

USAGE->
Attributive usage (before a noun):

Abstractional art is difficult for some people to understand. = 抽象艺术对某些人来说很难理解。
He employed abstractionist techniques in his design. = 他在设计中运用了抽象技巧。


Predicative usage (after a verb):
The concept is highly abstractionist. = 这个概念非常抽象。
Her thinking became increasingly abstractionist as she studied philosophy.  =  随着她学习哲学，她的思维变得越来越抽象。
