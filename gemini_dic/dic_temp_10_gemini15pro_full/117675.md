meiosis:/maɪˈoʊsɪs/| n. |减数分裂|A type of cell division that results in four daughter cells each with half the number of chromosomes of the parent cell, as in the production of gametes and plant spores.|Meiosis is essential for sexual reproduction.|Synonym: reduction division|Antonym: mitosis|noun

meioses:/maɪˈoʊsiːz/| n. |减数分裂（复数）|The plural form of meiosis, referring to multiple instances of this type of cell division.|Multiple meioses occur within the organism.|Synonym: reduction divisions (plural)|Antonym: mitoses (plural)|noun
