friskal:/ˈfrɪskəl/ | adj. | 活泼的，嬉戏的 | Playful and lively; full of energy and high spirits.  Relating to or resembling frisking. | The friskal lambs frolicked in the meadow. 活泼的小羊在草地上嬉戏。| Synonyms: playful, frolicsome, lively, spirited, energetic, exuberant, bouncy, frisky | Antonyms: lethargic, sluggish, listless, apathetic, subdued | word attribute:adjective


Etymology->
From Middle English *frisken, frequentative of frisken (“to frisk, dance, leap, skip, frolic”), from Old Norse *fríska (“to frisk”), from Proto-Germanic *friskaną (“to leap, jump, dance”). Cognate with Danish friske (“to frisk”), Swedish friska (“to refresh”), Icelandic friska (“to refresh”), Middle High German vrischen (“to rain, drizzle, sprinkle”). More at fresh.
