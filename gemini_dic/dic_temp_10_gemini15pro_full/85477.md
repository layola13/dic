hassidism: /ˈhæsɪdɪzəm/ (US), /ˈhæsɪdɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |哈西德主义|A Jewish religious revival movement characterized by mystical and ecstatic prayer, joy, and a close relationship between the rebbe (spiritual leader) and his followers. It originated in Eastern Europe in the 18th century.|Example 1: Hassidism emphasizes the importance of joy in religious practice. 哈西德主义强调在宗教实践中快乐的重要性。

Example 2: The rebbe plays a central role in Hassidism. 拉比在哈西德主义中扮演着核心角色。|Synonym: Hasidic Judaism|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but potentially opposing religious movements within Judaism could be considered)|noun
