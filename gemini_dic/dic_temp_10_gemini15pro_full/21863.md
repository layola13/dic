bodkin:/ˈbɑːdkɪn/ | n. | 锥子；发夹；(旧)匕首 | A sharp, pointed instrument, originally like a small dagger, used for piercing holes in leather or cloth; later, a blunt, thick needle for drawing tape or ribbon through hems; also, a hairpin. | She used a bodkin to thread the ribbon through the lace. 她用锥子把丝带穿过蕾丝。|Synonyms: awl, stiletto, dagger (archaic), needle (for sewing)| Antonyms: None (for the tool); loose (for the hairpin) | noun
