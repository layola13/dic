grovellers:/ˈɡrɒv(ə)ləz/ /ˈɡrɑːv(ə)lərz/| n.|卑躬屈膝的人；奴颜婢膝者|People who behave in a subservient or fawning manner, often to gain favor or avoid punishment.  They act in a way that shows excessive humility or deference.|The king was surrounded by grovellers, all vying for his attention. 国王身边围绕着一群卑躬屈膝的人，都争着想得到他的注意。

The dictator expected grovellers, not critics. 独裁者期望看到的是奴颜婢膝的人，而不是批评家。|近义词：sycophants, flatterers, yes-men, toadies|反义词：rebels, dissenters, critics|词性：名词(plural)

grovel:/ˈɡrɒv(ə)l/ /ˈɡrɑːvəl/| v.|匍匐；卑躬屈膝；奴颜婢膝|To behave in a subservient or fawning manner, often to gain favor or avoid punishment. It involves acting in a way that shows excessive humility or deference, often to an embarrassing degree.|He grovelled at the feet of the emperor, begging for mercy. 他匍匐在皇帝脚下，乞求宽恕。

She refused to grovel for forgiveness; she had done nothing wrong. 她拒绝低声下气地请求原谅；她没有做错任何事。|近义词：crawl, fawn, kowtow|反义词：defy, resist, stand up to|词性：动词
