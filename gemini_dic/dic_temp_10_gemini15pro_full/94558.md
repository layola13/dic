imposture:/ɪmˈpɒstʃə(r)/ (US: /ɪmˈpɑːstʃər/) | n. | 欺骗；冒名顶替；骗局 | The act of deceiving someone by pretending to be someone else. | He was eventually exposed as an imposture, a fraud who had no medical qualifications. 他最终被揭穿是一个骗子，一个没有医学资格的冒名顶替者。 |  He maintained the imposture for over a decade. 他维持了十多年的这种欺骗行为。 | 近义词：fraud, deception, sham, charade | 反义词：honesty, truthfulness | 词性: 名词

impostor:/ɪmˈpɒstə(r)/ (US: /ɪmˈpɑːstʃər/) | n. | 冒名顶替者；骗子 | A person who deceives others by pretending to be someone else. |  The impostor was eventually caught and brought to justice.  冒名顶替者最终被抓获并绳之以法。 | The police are looking for the impostor who claimed to be a police officer. 警察正在寻找那个自称是警察的骗子。 | 近义词：fraud, charlatan, fake | 反义词：genuine person | 词性: 名词


词源：
From Old French *imposture*, from Latin *impostūra* ("imposition, deception"), from *impositus*, past participle of *imponere* ("to impose, place upon"), from *in-* ("on, upon") + *ponere* ("to put, place").
