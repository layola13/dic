fauves:/foʊvz/| n.|野兽派画家；野兽派作品|Wild beasts; a term applied to a group of early 20th-century French artists whose works emphasized painterly qualities and strong color over the representational or realistic values retained by Impressionism.|The Fauves were known for their bold use of color. 野兽派以大胆运用色彩而闻名。|近义词：Expressionists, modernists|反义词:Impressionists|词性:noun

Etymology:
French, plural of fauve, literally, wild beast, from Old French, fallow deer, from Latin *falvus, yellowish
