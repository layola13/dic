high-wire:/ˈhaɪ waɪər/
adjective|高空钢丝的；惊险的；高风险的|performing on a high wire means performing on a tightrope or cable high above the ground.  Figuratively, it describes something risky or dangerous.|例句：The CEO was engaged in some high-wire financial dealings. 首席执行官正在进行一些高风险的金融交易。|近义词：risky, dangerous, perilous, precarious|反义词：safe, secure, low-risk|词性: adjective

high-wire:/ˈhaɪ waɪər/
noun|高空钢丝；高压线|a wire or cable high above the ground, typically used for acrobatic performances. It can also refer to a high-voltage power line.|例句：He walked the high-wire without a safety net. 他在没有安全网的情况下走钢丝。|近义词：tightrope, cable, power line|反义词：None|词性: noun


Etymology:
From "high" + "wire."  "High" meaning a large distance from the ground and "wire" referring to a metal cable.
