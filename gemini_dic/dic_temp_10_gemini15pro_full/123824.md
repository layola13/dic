morad:/məˈrɑːd/| n.| Ballad, poem, especially one expressing a moral.|A morad is a type of poem, often a ballad, that conveys a moral or a lesson.  It's a traditional form of storytelling in verse, particularly common in some cultures.|He recited a morad about the importance of honesty.|Synonym:  ballad, poem, fable, parable|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "nonsense verse")|noun
