bastardized:/ˈbæstərdaɪzd/ | adj. | 杂交的，劣化的，非正统的 | Changed from its original form, usually in a way that makes it worse or less pure.  Derived from "bastard," meaning illegitimate child, reflecting a sense of impurity or inferiority. |  The architect complained that the builder had bastardized his original design.  建筑师抱怨说，建筑商把他最初的设计改得面目全非。|  debased, corrupted, adulterated |  authentic, pure, genuine | adjective

bastardize:/ˈbæstərdaɪz/ | v. |  使…杂交，使…劣化，歪曲 | To change something from its original form, usually in a way that makes it worse or less pure. | The language has been bastardized by the introduction of foreign words.  这种语言因引入了外来词而变得不纯正。 | corrupt, debase, adulterate | purify, refine, improve | verb
