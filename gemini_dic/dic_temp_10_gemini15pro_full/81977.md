grinding:/ˈɡraɪndɪŋ/| adj. n. | 磨的，磨碎的；辛苦的；刺耳的；（尤指低沉的）摩擦声 |  (adj.) causing friction; harsh and grating; (n.) the act of reducing something to small particles or powder; a difficult or tedious task | The grinding poverty of the region is heartbreaking.  该地区的极端贫困令人心碎。| synonyms: abrasive, harsh, grueling, monotonous | antonyms: smooth, easy, pleasant | adjective

grinding:/ˈɡraɪndɪŋ/| n. | 磨碎；研磨；摩擦 | the action of crushing something into powder or small pieces | The grinding of the gears was deafening. 齿轮的摩擦声震耳欲聋。| synonyms: milling, crushing, pulverizing | antonyms: constructing, building | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *grindan, from Proto-Germanic *grindaną, from Proto-Indo-European *ghrendh- (“to rub, grind”). Cognate with Dutch grinden (“to grind”), German greinen (“to grin, weep”), Latin frendere (“to gnash”).
