glyphography:/ɡlɪˈfɒɡrəfi/
n.
雕刻印刷术；电刻术
A process of electrotyping, in which the design is produced in intaglio on a copper plate by etching, the lines being protected by a varnish and the plate afterwards coated with a deposit of metal.
Glyphography was a popular method of illustration in the 19th century.  雕刻印刷术在19世纪是一种流行的插图方法。
electrotyping, engraving
