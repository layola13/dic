defensiveness:/dɪˈfɛnsɪvnəs/| n. |防御性，守势|the quality or state of being defensive; inclined to defend oneself or one's actions, often excessively or inappropriately.|She reacted with defensiveness when asked about her spending habits. 她被问及消费习惯时，反应很 defensively。|Synonyms: guardedness, self-protection, sensitivity, wariness|Antonyms: openness, receptiveness, vulnerability|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From defensive + -ness.  "Defensive" comes from the Late Middle English "defensif," borrowed from Old French "defensif," ultimately from Latin "defendere" (to ward off, defend). The suffix "-ness" is a native English suffix forming nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition.
