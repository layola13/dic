non-programmable:/ˌnɒnˈprəʊɡræməbl/ (US) /ˌnɑːnˈproʊɡræməbl/| adj.|不可编程的|Not able to be programmed; fixed or permanent in function.  Referring to a device, especially electronic, that cannot have its function changed by a user.  |This old washing machine is non-programmable, you just turn the dial to the setting you need.|Synonyms: fixed, hardwired, preset, unchangeable|Antonyms: programmable, adaptable, configurable|adjective

non-programmable:/ˌnɒnˈprəʊɡræməbl/ (US) /ˌnɑːnˈproʊɡræməbl/| n.|不可编程设备|A device that is non-programmable.  (Less common usage than the adjective form)|The museum displayed several non-programmables from the early days of computing.|Synonyms: fixed device, hardwired device|Antonyms: programmable device, computer|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "programmable".  "Programmable" derives from "program" (meaning a set of instructions) + "-able" (suffix meaning "capable of being").  Ultimately from the Greek "programma" meaning "a written public notice".
