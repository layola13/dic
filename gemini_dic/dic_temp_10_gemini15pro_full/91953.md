hypermodern:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɒdən/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpərˈmɑːdərn/ (US)| adj.|超现代的，极端现代的|Characterized by an extreme or exaggerated form of modernism, especially in chess, marked by unconventional or provocative openings and strategies often involving early fianchettoing of bishops and control of the center from a distance.|The hypermodern architecture of the new museum contrasts sharply with the surrounding historical buildings. 这座新博物馆的超现代建筑与周围的历史建筑形成了鲜明的对比。|近义词：avant-garde, ultramodern, cutting-edge|反义词：traditional, conventional, classical|词性:形容词

hypermodern:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɒdən/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpərˈmɑːdərn/ (US)| n.|超现代主义|A style or movement characterized by an extreme or exaggerated form of modernism, especially in chess.|Hypermodernism in chess emphasizes controlling the center of the board from a distance rather than occupying it with pawns. 国际象棋中的超现代主义强调从远处控制棋盘中心，而不是用兵占据它。|近义词：avant-gardism, ultramodernism|反义词：traditionalism, classicism|词性:名词
