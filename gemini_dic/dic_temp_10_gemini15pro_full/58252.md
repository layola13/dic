drumlin:/ˈdrʌmlɪn/| n. | 鼓丘；蛋丘 | A smooth, oval-shaped hill of glacial drift, typically steeper on the side facing the glacier's advance and gentler on the down-ice side.  It is formed beneath a moving glacier and is composed of till. | The landscape was dotted with drumlins, evidence of the ancient ice sheet.  这片景观点缀着鼓丘，证明了古代冰盖的存在。| synonym:  glacial hill, drift hill | antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps valley or depression) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Irish *druimnín, diminutive of druim (“ridge, back”), from Proto-Celtic *dromos (“back”). Cognate with Welsh trum (“ridge”).  First attested in English in 1833.
