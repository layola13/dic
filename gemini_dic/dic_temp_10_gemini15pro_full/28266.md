captiousness:/ˈkæpʃəsnəs/
n.
吹毛求疵；挑剔
A disposition to find fault or raise petty objections.
His captiousness made it difficult to work with him. 他吹毛求疵，很难与他共事。
Synonyms: faultfinding, quibbling, carping, nitpicking, caviling
Antonyms: tolerance, acceptance, broad-mindedness
noun

captious:/ˈkæpʃəs/
adj.
吹毛求疵的；挑剔的
Tending to find fault or raise petty objections.
She asked captious questions, clearly trying to find flaws in his argument. 她问了一些挑剔的问题，显然是想在他的论点中找到 flaws。
Synonyms: faultfinding, quibbling, carping, nitpicking, caviling
Antonyms: tolerant, accepting, broad-minded
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *captiosus, from Latin captio (“a taking, catching”), from capere (“to take, seize”).
