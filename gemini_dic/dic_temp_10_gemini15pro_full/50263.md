depersonalization:/diːˌpɜːrsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ /diːˌpɜːrsənələˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |人格解体，失去个性|A state in which one's thoughts and feelings seem unreal or not to belong to oneself, or in which one loses all sense of identity.|She experienced episodes of depersonalization after the trauma. 她经历了创伤后的人格解体。|近义词：alienation, detachment, dissociation|反义词: realization, connection|词性: noun

depersonalize:/diːˈpɜːrsənəlaɪz/ /diːˈpɜːrsənələɪz/ | vt. |使失去个性，使人格解体|To make impersonal or anonymous; to deprive of individuality or personality.|Bureaucracy can depersonalize individuals. 官僚主义会使个人失去个性。|近义词：dehumanize, objectify|反义词: personalize, humanize|词性: verb

depersonalized:/diːˈpɜːrsənəlaɪzd/ /diːˈpɜːrsənələɪzd/ | adj. |失去个性的，非人性的|Lacking personal or individual characteristics; impersonal.|The hospital felt cold and depersonalized. 这家医院让人感觉冰冷且没有人情味。|近义词：impersonal, anonymous|反义词: personal, individualized|词性: adjective
