borrowed: /ˈbɒrəʊd/ (British) /ˈbɑːroʊd/ (American)
| adj. | 借来的，借用的 | Something that is borrowed is something you have taken from someone else with their permission and must return it later. |  I read a borrowed book. 我读了一本借来的书。|  loaned, rented | lent, owned | adjective


borrowed: /ˈbɒrəʊd/ (British) /ˈbɑːroʊd/ (American)
| v. | 借入，借用(borrow的过去式和过去分词) |  Past tense and past participle of borrow. | I borrowed a book from the library. 我从图书馆借了一本书。| loaned, rented | lent | verb

Etymology: From Middle English *borwen, from Old English *borgian, from Proto-Germanic *burgōną (“to borrow, lend, give as security”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰerǵʰ- (“high, elevated”). Cognate with Old Frisian borgia (“to borrow”), Middle Dutch borgen, borghen (“to borrow, lend, give as security”), Old High German borgen (“to borrow, lend, save, rescue”), German borgen (“to borrow, lend”), Old Norse bjarga (“to save, help, rescue”).
