insolubleness:/ɪnˈsɒljəb(ə)lnəs/ | n. | 不可溶性，不溶解性 | The insolubleness of the chemical compound made it difficult to work with.  这种化合物的不可溶性使其难以使用。| Example: The insolubleness of the problem frustrated the team.  这个问题的棘手性让团队感到沮丧。|Synonyms: insolubility, indissolubility, non-solubility | Antonyms: solubility, dissolvability | noun

insoluble:/ɪnˈsɒljəb(ə)l/ | adj. | 不可溶的，不溶解的；无法解决的 |  The scientist found the substance to be insoluble in water. 科学家发现这种物质不溶于水。 | Example:  They faced an apparently insoluble dilemma. 他们面临着一个显然无法解决的困境。 | Synonyms:  indissoluble, unsolvable, irresolvable | Antonyms: soluble, dissolvable, solvable | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *insoluble*, from Latin *insolubilis* ("that cannot be loosened, indissoluble"), from *in-* ("not") + *solubilis* ("that can be loosened, dissoluble"), from *solvō* ("I loosen, dissolve").  The ending *-ness* is a native English suffix forming abstract nouns from adjectives.
