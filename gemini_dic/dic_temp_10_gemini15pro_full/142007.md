organistship:/ˈɔːɡənɪstʃɪp/
n.
管风琴师的职位；管风琴师的技能
The position or skill of an organist.
He held the organistship at the local church for many years. 他在当地教堂担任管风琴师多年。
position, post, job, appointment
N/A
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From organist + -ship.  Organist comes from organ (the musical instrument) + -ist (denoting a person who plays or operates something).  Organ ultimately derives from the Greek *organon* meaning "instrument" or "tool". The suffix -ship denotes a state, condition, or quality of being.
