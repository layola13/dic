Irkutsk: /ɪrˈkuːtsk/ | n. | 伊尔库茨克 | A city in southeastern Siberia, Russia, situated on the Angara River near Lake Baikal. It is the administrative center of Irkutsk Oblast. | Irkutsk is a popular starting point for tours to Lake Baikal. 伊尔库茨克是前往贝加尔湖旅游的热门起点。| Near Antonyms:  Moscow, St. Petersburg | Far Antonyms: Listvyanka (a smaller town near Irkutsk) | noun

Etymology:
From Russian "Иркутск", likely derived from the name of the Irkut River, which flows into the Angara at Irkutsk. The name "Irkut" itself is thought to come from a Buryat word meaning "spinning" or "whirling," possibly referring to the swirling currents of the river.
