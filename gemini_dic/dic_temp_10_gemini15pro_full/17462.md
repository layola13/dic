bearbind:/ˈbeərˌbaɪnd/| n.|旋花属植物；黑牵牛;|A twining plant of the genus *Convolvulus*, especially the field bindweed (*C. arvensis*).  It's a fast-growing vine with white or pink trumpet-shaped flowers, often considered a weed due to its ability to smother other plants.|The bearbind had completely engulfed the rose bushes. 旋花已经完全吞没了玫瑰丛。|近义词：bindweed, morning glory|反义词: N/A|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bēarbind*, from *bēar* (bear) + *bind* (to bind), possibly because it was thought that bears used the plant to bind up wounds or because it was so strong it could bind a bear.
