foreboding:/fɔːrˈboʊdɪŋ/| n. adj.|预感，预兆;不祥的| (n.) A feeling that something bad will happen; an omen. (adj.) implying or seeming to imply that something bad is going to happen.| (n.) He had a foreboding that the journey would be disastrous. 他有一种预感，这次旅行将是一场灾难。(adj.) The foreboding music created a sense of suspense. 不祥的音乐营造了一种悬念感。|近义词：(n.) premonition, apprehension, misgiving (adj.) ominous, sinister|反义词：(n.) hope, optimism (adj.) promising, auspicious|名词，形容词

词源:  From Middle English *forboden, from Old English forbēodan (“to forbid, prohibit”), from for- (“for-, fore-”) + bēodan (“to command, offer”).  The sense shifted from "prohibition" to "foretelling evil" in the 16th century.
