curtsey: /ˈkɜːrsi/ (UK) /ˈkɜːrtsi/ (US)| n. vi.|行屈膝礼|A gesture of respect or greeting made by women and girls, consisting of a slight lowering of the body with a bending of the knees.|The little girl curtseyed to the queen. 小女孩向女王行屈膝礼。|近义词：bow (for women), genuflect|反义词: (No direct antonym for the physical action, but conceptually: disrespect)|noun, verb

curtseys: /ˈkɜːrsiz/ (UK) /ˈkɜːrtsiz/ (US)| n. vi.|行屈膝礼（复数）|Plural form of curtsey.  A gesture of respect or greeting made by women and girls, consisting of a slight lowering of the body with a bending of the knees.|All the bridesmaids curtseyed to the newly married couple. 所有的伴娘都向新婚夫妇行屈膝礼。|近义词：bows (for women), genuflects|反义词: (No direct antonym for the physical action, but conceptually: acts of disrespect)|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *curteis, from curteis "courteous," from cort "court."  Reflecting the elaborate manners of royal courts.
