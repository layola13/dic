arteriosclerosis:/ɑːrˌtɪriəʊskləˈrəʊsɪs/ |n.| 动脉硬化|A chronic disease characterized by thickening and hardening of the arteries, leading to reduced blood flow.|Example: Arteriosclerosis can lead to heart attacks and strokes. 动脉硬化会导致心脏病和中风。|Example:  His father suffered from arteriosclerosis. 他父亲患有动脉硬化。|Synonym: hardening of the arteries|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but the opposite condition would be healthy, elastic arteries) |Noun
