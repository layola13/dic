idealistically:/ˌaɪdiːəˈlɪstɪkli/
adv.
理想主义地，理想化地
In a manner characterized by idealism; based on or having high principles or ideals, often impractical.
He idealistically believed that peace could be achieved through diplomacy alone. 他理想地认为单凭外交就能实现和平。
Synonyms: naively, romantically, unrealistically, quixotically
Antonyms: pragmatically, realistically, cynically
词性: 副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From ideal + -istic + -ally.  "Ideal" comes from Late Latin "idealis" meaning "existing in idea," from "idea."  "-istic" forms adjectives meaning "relating to" or "characteristic of." "-ally" forms adverbs from adjectives.
