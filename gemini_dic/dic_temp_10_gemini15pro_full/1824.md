acrobatic: /ˌæk.rəˈbæt.ɪk/ (US) /ˌæk.rəˈbæt.ɪk/ (UK)| adj.|杂技的；特技的；灵活的|Requiring or involving great agility and skill, like that of an acrobat.  Relating to or characteristic of acrobatics. |The gymnast's acrobatic feats were breathtaking.  体操运动员的杂技表演令人叹为观止。|Synonyms: agile, nimble, deft, skillful, athletic|Antonyms: clumsy, awkward, stiff, inflexible|adjective

USAGE->
Attributive usage (修饰名词):
The acrobatic performance was amazing. = 杂技表演令人惊叹。
She has acrobatic skills. = 她有杂技技能。

Predicative usage (作表语):
His movements were incredibly acrobatic. = 他的动作非常灵活。
The dancer's style is very acrobatic. = 舞者的风格非常具有杂技性。

With "too" (与"too"连用):
The climb was too acrobatic for me. = 这次攀登对我来说太难了（需要太多杂技技巧）。
The routine was too acrobatic for beginners. =  这个套路对于初学者来说太难了（需要太多杂技技巧）。

With "enough" (与"enough"连用):
He wasn't acrobatic enough to perform the trick. = 他不够灵活，无法完成这个技巧。
She is acrobatic enough to join the circus. = 她足够灵活，可以加入马戏团。

In comparative and superlative forms (比较级和最高级):
He's more acrobatic than his brother. = 他比他哥哥更灵活。
She's the most acrobatic person I know. = 她是我认识的最灵活的人。
