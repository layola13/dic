parenthesis:/pəˈrɛnθəsɪs/ | n. | 括号，插入语，插曲 |A word, clause, or sentence inserted as an explanation or afterthought into a passage that is grammatically complete without it.  A remark or passage that departs from the theme of a discourse. | The author adds a parenthesis to explain a complex term. 作者添加了一个括号来解释一个复杂的术语。|  She paused, as if in parenthesis, to say she would return soon. 她停顿了一下，像是在插话，说她很快就会回来。| synonym: aside, interpolation, digression | antonym: main theme, core idea | noun

parentheses:/pəˈrɛnθəˌsiz/ | n. | 括号 | A pair of round brackets ( ) used to mark off a parenthetical word or phrase. |  Use parentheses to enclose any extra information. 使用括号将任何额外信息括起来。| The date of the meeting (January 15th) was confirmed. 会议日期（1月15日）已确认。 | synonym: brackets, round brackets | antonym: N/A | noun
