anticonstitutionalisms:/ˌæntiˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃənəlɪzəmz/
|n.|违宪主义，反宪政主义| Doctrines or principles opposed to the principles or spirit of a constitution, especially a nation's constitution.|The rise of anticonstitutionalisms in the 20th century threatened democracies around the world. 20世纪违宪主义的兴起威胁着世界各地的民主。| The group's anticonstitutionalisms led to their eventual downfall.  该组织的反宪政主义最终导致了他们的垮台。|近义词： unconstitutionality, illegality|反义词: constitutionality, legality|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to (from Greek ἀντί (anti))
constitution (noun): a body of fundamental principles or established precedents according to which a state or other organization is acknowledged to be governed. (from Latin constitutio, from constituere "to set up, establish")
-al (suffix): relating to
-ism (suffix): a doctrine, belief, or practice
