dismally:/ˈdɪzməli/| adv.|凄凉地，阴沉地，沮丧地|In a gloomy or depressing manner.  Characterized by or causing gloom or depression.|The rain fell dismally, mirroring my mood. 雨凄凉地下着，映照着我的心情。|gloomily, drearily, miserably, depressingly|cheerfully, brightly, happily|adverb
Etymology: From dismal + -ly.  Dismal comes from the Old French *dismal (attested in Anglo-Norman), from Late Latin dies mali "evil days," from Latin dies "day" + malus "bad, evil."
