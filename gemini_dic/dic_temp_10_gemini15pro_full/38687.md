condensative:/kənˈdɛnsətɪv/ | adj. | 冷凝的; 浓缩的 | Relating to or causing condensation; tending to condense.  | The condensative properties of the substance allowed it to quickly form droplets. 这种物质的冷凝特性使其能够快速形成液滴。|  The machine uses a condensative process to remove excess moisture from the air.  这台机器使用冷凝工艺去除空气中多余的水分。 | synonyms: condensing, concentrating | antonyms: evaporative, dispersive | adjective

condensation:/ˌkɒndenˈseɪʃn/ | n. | 冷凝; 浓缩; 凝结 | The process by which a gas or vapor changes to a liquid. Also: the product of this process. | Condensation formed on the cold window. 冷凝物在冰冷的窗户上形成。 | The condensation of the steam created water droplets. 蒸汽的凝结产生了水滴。 | synonyms: liquefaction, precipitation | antonyms: evaporation, vaporization | noun

condense:/kənˈdɛns/ | v. |  冷凝; 浓缩; 压缩 |  To change or cause to change from a gas or vapor to a liquid. Also: to make something shorter or more concise. |  The steam condensed into water. 蒸汽凝结成水。 | She condensed her report into a single page. 她把报告压缩成了一页。 | synonyms: compress, shorten, abridge | antonyms: expand, lengthen, dilute | verb
