erodes:/ɪˈroʊdz/ (US), /ɪˈrəʊdz/ (UK)| v.| 侵蚀，腐蚀| Gradually wears away or destroys something, often through natural forces like wind or water, but also through chemical or other processes. |例句：The coastline is slowly being eroded by the sea. 海岸线正被海水缓慢侵蚀。

例句：Acid rain erodes stone buildings. 酸雨腐蚀石材建筑。|近义词：corrodes, wears away, eats away, deteriorates, weakens|反义词：builds, strengthens, reinforces, restores|词性：动词

eroding:/ɪˈroʊdɪŋ/ (US), /ɪˈrəʊdɪŋ/ (UK)| v.| 侵蚀，腐蚀（现在分词）| The present participle form of "erode," describing the ongoing process of wearing away. |例句：The constant wind and rain are eroding the mountain.  持续的风雨正在侵蚀着山脉。

例句：His confidence was slowly eroding. 他的自信正在慢慢瓦解。|近义词：corroding, wearing down, eating away, deteriorating, weakening|反义词：building, strengthening, reinforcing, restoring|词性：动词 (现在分词)

erosion:/ɪˈroʊʒən/ (US), /ɪˈrəʊʒən/ (UK)| n.| 侵蚀，腐蚀| The process of being eroded or the result of such a process. |例句：Soil erosion is a major problem in some areas.  水土流失在某些地区是一个主要问题。

例句：The erosion of public trust is a serious concern. 公众信任的丧失是一个严重的问题。|近义词：corrosion, deterioration, wearing away, disintegration|反义词：building, strengthening, reinforcement, restoration|词性：名词
