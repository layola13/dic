cautel:/ˈkɔːtəl/| n.|谨慎; 警惕; 警告; (尤指法律上的)注意事项|A warning or caution; a provision or stipulation, especially in a legal document.|The lawyer inserted a cautel into the contract to protect her client's interests. 律师在合同中加入了一项警告条款以保护其客户的利益。|Synonyms: warning, caution, admonition, proviso, stipulation|Antonyms: recklessness, carelessness, imprudence|Noun

cautel:/ˈkɔːtəl/| adj.|谨慎的; 小心的|Exercising or marked by caution.|He was cautel in his approach to the negotiations, careful not to give too much away. 他在谈判中很谨慎，小心不要泄露太多。|Synonyms: cautious, wary, careful, circumspect|Antonyms: reckless, careless, imprudence|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *cautel*, from Latin *cautela* ("caution, precaution"), from *cautus* ("cautious"), past participle of *caveō* ("I beware, take heed").
