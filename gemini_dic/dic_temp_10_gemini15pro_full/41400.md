corncrake: /ˈkɔːrnkreɪk/ (UK) /ˈkɔːrnkreɪk/ (US)| n. |秧鸡；长脚秧鸡|A secretive bird of grasslands, with a distinctive, harsh, rasping call.|例句：The corncrake's call is a familiar sound in the meadows in summer. 夏天，草地上秧鸡的叫声很熟悉。

例句：Conservation efforts have been made to protect the declining corncrake population.  人们已经做出保护措施来挽救数量下降的秧鸡。|近义词：rail, crake|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *corncrake, likely imitative of the bird's call.  The "corn" element likely refers to the bird's habitat in grain fields.
