nilus:/ˈnaɪləs/| n. |尼罗河|The Nile is a major north-flowing river in northeastern Africa, and is generally regarded as the longest river in the world. |The ancient Egyptians depended on the annual flooding of the Nilus for agriculture. 古埃及人依靠尼罗河每年的泛滥来进行农业生产。|近义词: Nile River|反义词: (无，专有名词)|词性: 名词

Etymology: From Latin *Nilus*, from Greek *Νεῖλος (Neîlos)*, ultimately of uncertain origin, possibly from Egyptian or Semitic. Some theories connect it to a root meaning "river valley" or "blue".
