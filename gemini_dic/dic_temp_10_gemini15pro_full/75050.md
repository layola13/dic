frighted:/ˈfraɪtɪd/| adj.|受惊的，害怕的|A literary or archaic word meaning afraid or scared.  It emphasizes a sudden, intense fear.|例句：The frighted child hid behind his mother. 受惊的孩子躲在母亲身后。|近义词：scared, terrified, afraid, frightened|反义词：brave, unafraid, bold, courageous|词性:形容词|词源:From Middle English *frighten, from Old English *fyrhtan, causative of *forhtian (“to frighten”), from Proto-Germanic *furhtjaną (“to frighten”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰerǵʰ- (“high; to be excited, afraid”). Cognate with Dutch *vrijen (“to court”), German *fürchten (“to fear”).
