liad:/ˈlaɪæd/ | n. |谎言，虚假陈述|A statement that is known or intended to be untrue.| He told a liad to avoid getting into trouble. 他撒谎是为了避免惹上麻烦。|近义词: falsehood, fib, untruth, fabrication|反义词: truth, honesty|词性:名词

词源:
From Middle English *liȝe, from Old English lyge (“lie, falsehood”), from Proto-Germanic *lugiz (“lie, deceit”), from Proto-Indo-European *lewgʰ- (“to lie, deceive”). Cognate with Scots lee (“lie”), West Frisian ligen (“to lie”), Dutch liegen (“to lie”), German lügen (“to lie”), Danish lyve (“to lie”), Swedish ljuga (“to lie”), Icelandic ljúga (“to lie”).
