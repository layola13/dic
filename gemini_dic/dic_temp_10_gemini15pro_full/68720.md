faring:/ˈfɛərɪŋ/| n.|进展，遭遇，经历|the manner in which someone or something performs or progresses in a particular situation; experience, especially in relation to success or failure|例句：The company's faring in the current economic climate has been surprisingly good.  这家公司在当前经济环境下的表现出奇地好。

例句：His faring in the competition was much better than expected. 他在比赛中的表现比预期的要好得多。|近义词：progress, performance, experience, fortune|反义词: failure, misfortune|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *faren, from Old English *faran (“to fare, travel, go, journey, depart, die”), from Proto-Germanic *faraną (“to travel, go, fare”), from Proto-Indo-European *per- (“to try, risk, cross over”). Cognate with West Frisian farre (“to fare, travel”), Dutch varen (“to sail, travel, fare”), German fahren (“to drive, travel, go”), Danish fare (“to travel, go, depart”). Related to fear.
