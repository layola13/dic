coriolis:/ˌkɔːriˈoʊlɪs/ /ˌkɒrɪˈəʊlɪs/ | adj. n.|科里奥利（的）|Relating to or resulting from the Coriolis force (an inertial force that acts on objects moving within a rotating frame of reference).  It causes moving objects on Earth to be deflected to the right in the Northern Hemisphere and to the left in the Southern Hemisphere.|The Coriolis effect influences wind patterns and ocean currents. 科里奥利效应影响风向和洋流。|The pilot had to account for the Coriolis force when plotting the plane's course. 飞行员在规划飞机航线时必须考虑科里奥利力。|近义词: inertial (referring to the force), rotational |反义词: N/A |词性: adjective, noun (usually used attributively as in "Coriolis force" or "Coriolis effect")


Etymology:
Named after Gaspard-Gustave de Coriolis (1792-1843), a French scientist who mathematically described the effect in 1835.
