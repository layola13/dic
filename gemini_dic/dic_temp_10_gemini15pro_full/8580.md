anticlassicalists:/ˌæntiˈklæsɪsɪsts/
|n.|反古典主义者|People who oppose or reject the principles of classicism, especially in art, literature, or music.  Those who challenge the established norms and traditions of the classical period. |The anticlassicalists of the early 20th century sought to break free from the rigid structures of traditional forms.  20世纪初的反古典主义者试图摆脱传统形式的僵化结构。|Near synonyms: modernists, avant-gardists, iconoclasts|Antonyms: classicists, traditionalists|noun

anticlassical:/ˌæntiˈklæsɪkəl/
|adj.|反古典的|Opposed to or rejecting the principles of classicism.  Characterized by a departure from or rejection of classical styles, forms, or values.|The anticlassical movement in architecture embraced new materials and innovative designs. 反古典主义建筑运动采用了新材料和创新设计。|Near synonyms: nonclassical, unconventional, modernist|Antonyms: classical, traditional|adjective
