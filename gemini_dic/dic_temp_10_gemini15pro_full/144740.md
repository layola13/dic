over-aptnesses:/ˌoʊvərˈæptnəsəz/
noun
过度贴切; 过于恰当
The quality of being excessively or inappropriately apt.  Being too quick or eager to apply a particular concept or idea, often resulting in a strained or forced interpretation.
His analysis of the poem suffered from over-aptnesses, forcing symbolic meanings onto simple lines.  他对这首诗的分析过于贴切，强加了象征意义给简单的诗句。
Synonyms: strained interpretations, forced analogies, over-ingenuities
Antonyms:  inanities, vapidity, dullness
