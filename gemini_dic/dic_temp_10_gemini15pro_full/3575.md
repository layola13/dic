agaric:/ˈæɡərɪk, əˈɡærɪk/| n. |伞菌，蘑菇|A fungus with gills, typically having a stem and a cap, especially one that is edible.|例句：The chef sautéed the agaric with garlic and butter.  厨师用大蒜和黄油煎了蘑菇。

例句：The forest floor was covered in various types of agaric. 森林的地面上覆盖着各种各样的伞菌。|近义词：mushroom, toadstool|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

Etymology->
From Middle English *agarike*, from Old French *agaric*, from Latin *agaricum*, from Ancient Greek *ἀγαρικόν* (agarikón). Ultimately of uncertain origin, possibly from a place name in Sarmatia Asiatica.
