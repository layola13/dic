jerkier: /ˈdʒɜːrkɪər/ (US), /ˈdʒɜːkɪə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更急促的，更颠簸的，更抽搐的|Comparative adjective of jerky: more jerky.  Describes something that is characterized by abrupt, sudden movements or changes.|The ride in the old jeep was jerkier than I expected.  开着旧吉普车的旅程比我预料的更颠簸。|The animation was jerkier in the older version of the game.  旧版游戏中的动画比新版更不流畅。|近义词：abrupt, uneven, spasmodic, jolting|反义词：smooth, fluid, continuous|词性: 形容词

词源:  From the adjective "jerky" + "-er" (comparative suffix). "Jerky" originates from "jerk," a verb meaning "to move sharply and suddenly."  Ultimately from a imitative source.
