ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ċyrin (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *kurnijan, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ker- (“to turn, bend”). Cognate with Dutch kernen (“to churn”), German kirnen (“to churn”), Danish kerne (“to churn”). The noun is from the verb.  The sense of "customer turnover" is first recorded in 1979.
