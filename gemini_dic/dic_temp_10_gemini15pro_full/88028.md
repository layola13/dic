highballed:/ˈhaɪbɔːld/ | v. | 估价过高；要价过高；开价过高 | To overestimate or overquote, especially a price or cost. |  The contractor highballed the estimate for the renovations, and we ended up paying far more than we should have.  承包商对装修的估价过高，结果我们付的钱比应该付的多得多。

| Example:  They highballed the price of the car, hoping the customer wouldn't notice. 他们故意抬高了汽车的价格，希望顾客不会注意到。| Synonym: overcharge, overestimate, inflate | Antonym: undercharge, underestimate, lowball | verb

highball:/ˈhaɪbɔːl/ | n. |  (一种烈酒加苏打水或姜汁啤酒的)高杯酒 | A mixed alcoholic drink consisting of a spirit (such as whiskey) and a mixer (such as soda water or ginger ale) served in a tall glass. | I ordered a highball at the bar. 我在酒吧点了一杯高杯酒。 | Synonym: cocktail | Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "neat" or "straight up" for undiluted liquor) | noun
