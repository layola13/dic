cheep:/tʃiːp/| vi. n.|吱吱叫；唧唧声|A short, high-pitched sound, typically made by a young bird.|The chicks cheeped loudly for food. 小鸡们大声唧唧叫着要吃的。|近义词：chirp, peep, tweet|反义词:squawk, shriek|verb

cheep:/tʃiːp/| n.|吱吱声；唧唧声|The sound made by a young bird.|The faint cheep of the baby bird could be heard from the nest. 从鸟巢里可以听到雏鸟微弱的唧唧声。|近义词：chirp, peep|反义词:squawk, shriek|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *chepen, *chippen, from Old English *cēapian (“to bargain, buy”), from Proto-Germanic *kōpijaną (“to buy, bargain”), from Proto-Indo-European *keh₂p- (“to take, seize, hold”). Related to cheap.  The noun is from the verb.
