airs:/ɛərz/ | n. | 旋律; 歌曲; 样子; 神态; 架子 | A succession of notes forming a distinctive sequence; a tune. An impression of a quality or manner given by someone or something; an air of mystery. Affected manners intended to impress others.| She sang a simple airs. 她唱了一首简单的歌。 He had an airs of superiority. 他摆出一副高人一等的样子。| 近义词：tune, melody, manner, affectation | 反义词：naturalness, sincerity | 词性: noun

词源: From Old French *air, from Latin aer, from Ancient Greek ἀήρ (aḗr, “air, lower atmosphere”).  The musical sense developed from the idea of a melody being "light and airy".
