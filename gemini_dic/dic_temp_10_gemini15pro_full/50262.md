depersonalising:/diːˈpɜːrsənəlaɪzɪŋ/
adjective
使人失去个性化的；使人感觉不像个人的
Making someone feel less like an individual; divesting of personality or individuality.
The depersonalising effects of living in a big city can be isolating.  生活在大城市里使人失去个性化的影响可能会让人感到孤立。
Modern medicine can be depersonalising, treating patients as numbers rather than individuals. 现代医学可能会使人失去个性化，把病人当作数字而不是个人来对待。
近义词: dehumanising, alienating, objectifying
反义词: personalising, individualising
词性: adjective


depersonalise:/diːˈpɜːrsənəlaɪz/
verb
使失去个性；使感觉不像个人
To make someone feel less like an individual; to divest of personality or individuality.
The hospital's strict rules and sterile environment can depersonalise patients. 医院严格的规定和无菌的环境会使病人失去个性。
Bureaucracy can depersonalise the relationship between the government and its citizens. 官僚主义会使政府与其公民之间的关系变得没有人情味。
近义词: dehumanise, alienate, objectify
反义词: personalise, individualise
词性: verb


Etymology:
From de- + personal + -ise/-ize.  De- is a prefix meaning "remove" or "reverse". Personal relates to a particular person. -ise/-ize is a suffix used to form verbs meaning "to make" or "to become".
