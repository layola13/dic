good-ole-boy:/ˌɡʊd ˈoʊl ˈbɔɪ/ | n. | 老好人；老朋友（尤指美国南部白人男性）| A term used, often disparagingly, to refer to a white man of the southern U.S., especially one who is seen as being part of a traditional, conservative, and often exclusive social network. |  Example: He's a good-ole-boy who always gets his way. 他是个老好人，总是能随心所欲。

Example:  The good-ole-boy network controlled the local politics.  这个老好人关系网控制着当地的政治。| 近义词: crony, buddy, pal | 反义词: outsider, newcomer | 词性: noun
