enomotarch:/ˈiːnəmoʊtɑːrk/ | n. | 祭司长; 圣餐司祭 | A priest in charge of sacred laws, especially those concerning festivals.  A priest or official responsible for the proper observance of festivals. | The enomotarch oversaw the preparations for the annual harvest festival.  The enomotarch ensured that the rituals were performed correctly. | 近义词: hierophant, priest | 反义词: layman | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek *ἐνομοτάρχης* (ennomotárkhēs), from *ἔνομος* (énomos, “lawful”) + *ἄρχω* (árkhō, “to rule”).
