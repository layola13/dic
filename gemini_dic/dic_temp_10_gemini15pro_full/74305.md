fractious: /ˈfrækʃəs/ | adj. | 易怒的，暴躁的 | (typically of children) irritable and quarrelsome. | The fractious child refused to go to bed.  这个暴躁的孩子拒绝上床睡觉。 |  Synonyms: irritable, grumpy, bad-tempered, cantankerous, cross, peevish, petulant, querulous, churlish, irascible | Antonyms: affable, amiable, amicable, complaisant, good-natured, good-tempered, obliging | 词性: 形容词

fractious: /ˈfrækʃəs/ | adj. | (of a group or organization) difficult to control; unruly. |  The political party became increasingly fractious as the election approached.  随着选举临近，这个政党变得越来越难以控制。 | Synonyms: unruly, unmanageable, difficult, awkward, recalcitrant, refractory | Antonyms: docile, manageable, obedient | 词性: 形容词
