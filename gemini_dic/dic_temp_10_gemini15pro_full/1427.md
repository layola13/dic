ace-it:/ˈeɪs ɪt/
verb phrase
取得成功；出色地完成；彻底征服
To succeed, perform excellently, or conquer completely.
He aced it on the exam. 他在考试中取得了优异的成绩。
She aced her presentation and got the job. 她出色地完成了演讲，并得到了那份工作。

近义词:succeed, triumph, excel, conquer, master, nail it
反义词:fail, flunk, bomb, flop
词性:动词短语


ETYMOLOGY->
"Ace" originally referred to the highest playing card, signifying excellence.  The addition of "it" transforms the noun into a verb phrase, emphasizing the act of achieving something perfectly.  This usage likely emerged in informal American English in the mid-20th century.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He aces every test he takes. = 他每次考试都得高分。
She always aces her presentations. = 她每次演讲都表现出色。

一般过去时:
I aced the interview and got the job offer. = 我面试表现出色，并得到了工作机会。
They aced their final project. = 他们的期末项目完成得很出色。

现在完成时:
She has aced all her exams this semester. = 她这学期所有考试都得了高分。
He has aced every challenge he's faced. = 他成功地应对了每一个挑战。

过去完成时:
I had aced the exam before I even started studying for it. = 我甚至在开始复习之前就对考试胸有成竹。
They had aced the presentation, so they were confident about the outcome. = 他们出色地完成了演讲，因此对结果充满信心。

将来时:
I will ace this test tomorrow. = 我明天考试会考得很好。
She will ace her audition next week. = 她下周的试镜会表现出色。

虚拟语气:
If I were to ace this exam, I would get a scholarship. = 如果我能考好这次考试，我就能获得奖学金。
I wish I could ace every test I take. = 我希望我能考好每次考试。

被动语态:
The test was aced by the entire class. = 全班都考得很好。(被动语态在此处不自然)
The presentation was aced, impressing the clients. = 演讲进行得很出色，给客户留下了深刻印象。(被动语态在此处不自然)


疑问句:

How did she ace the presentation? = 她是怎么把演讲做得这么出色的？


否定句:

He didn't ace the interview, so he didn't get the job. = 他面试表现不佳，所以没有得到那份工作。
