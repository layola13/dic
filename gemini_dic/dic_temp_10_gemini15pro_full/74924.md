frett:/frɛt/| v.| 烦躁，焦躁，苦恼 | to worry or be anxious about something |  She was fretting about the upcoming exam. 她正为即将到来的考试而焦躁不安。| 近义词: worry, agonize, fuss | 反义词: relax, calm | 词性: 动词
frett:/frɛt/| n.| 金属丝，品丝 | a small bar of metal fixed across the fingerboard of a stringed instrument | The guitar's frets were worn down from years of playing.  这把吉他的品丝由于多年的演奏而磨损了。| 近义词: bar, wire | 反义词: (无直接反义词) | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fretan "gnaw, devour," from Proto-Germanic *fratanan "gnaw, rub," from PIE *ghrē- "to rub, grind."  The musical sense developed from the idea of "rubbing" the strings against the frets.
