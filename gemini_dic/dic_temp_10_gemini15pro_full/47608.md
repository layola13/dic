debaucher:/dɪˈbɔːtʃər/| n.|放荡者，堕落者|Someone who corrupts or seduces others into immoral or excessive indulgence.|He was known as a debaucher of young women. 他被称为年轻女性的堕落者。|Synonyms: libertine, profligate, rake|Antonyms: ascetic, puritan|noun
debauch:/dɪˈbɔːtʃ/| v.|使放荡，使堕落；败坏|To corrupt or seduce someone into immoral or excessive indulgence.|The emperor was debauched by power. 皇帝被权力腐蚀了。|Synonyms: corrupt, pervert, deprave|Antonyms: purify, elevate, uplift|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *débaucher* "to corrupt, lead astray," literally "to remove from the workshop," from *dé-* (expressing removal) + *bauche* "workshop."
