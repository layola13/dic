calcination:/ˌkælsɪˈneɪʃn/ | n. |煅烧，焙烧|The process of heating a substance to a high temperature but below the melting or fusing point, causing loss of moisture, volatile matter, or a chemical change.|The limestone underwent calcination in the kiln to produce lime. 石灰石在窑中煅烧以生产石灰。|近义词: roasting, firing |反义词: hydration |词性: 名词
