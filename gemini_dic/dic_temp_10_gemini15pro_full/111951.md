词源:  The word "lollypop" (or "lollipop") comes from Northern English dialects.  "Lolly" meant "tongue" and "pop" referred to a slapping or hitting sound.  So, essentially, it described licking a sweet treat.  It's thought to have become popularized in the late 18th century.
