Gryffindor: /ˈɡrɪfɪndɔːr/ (British) /ˈɡrɪfɪndɔːr/ (American)| n. |格兰芬多|One of the four houses of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry in the Harry Potter series, known for bravery, chivalry, daring, nerve, and determination.| Harry was sorted into Gryffindor. 哈利被分院到格兰芬多。|近义词: brave house, courageous house |反义词: Slytherin, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw |词性: noun

Etymology:
"Gryffindor" is likely derived from the French "griffon d'or," meaning "golden griffin."  The griffin is a mythical creature with the body of a lion and the head and wings of an eagle, symbolizing courage and strength.
