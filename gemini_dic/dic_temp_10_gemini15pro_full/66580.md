exhortation:/ˌɛksɔːrˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 劝告，忠告，勉励 | An earnest attempt to persuade someone to do something; a strong urging or encouragement. | The president delivered an exhortation to the nation to conserve energy. 总统向全国发表了节约能源的劝告。

The coach's pre-game exhortation inspired the team to victory. 教练在赛前的激励鼓舞了球队取得胜利。

| 近义词：encouragement, urging, admonition, advice, counsel | 反义词：discouragement, dissuasion | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *exhortation, from Late Latin *exhortātiō (“an encouraging”),  from Latin *exhortārī (“to encourage strongly”), from *ex- (“out of, from”) + *hortārī (“to encourage, urge”).
