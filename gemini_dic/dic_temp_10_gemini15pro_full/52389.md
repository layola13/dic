|n.|说教；教导主义|The author's didacticisms became tiresome after a while.  作者的说教过了一段时间就变得令人厌烦了。 |The novel is filled with heavy didacticisms about the importance of family. 这部小说充满了关于家庭重要性的沉重说教。|近义词:moralizing, preaching, instruction, edification|反义词:entertainment, amusement, fun|词性:名词

didacticism:/ˌdɪdəˈktɪsɪzəm/
|n.|说教；教导主义|The teacher's didacticism made the lessons boring. 老师的说教使得课程很无聊。|His didacticism alienated his audience. 他的说教疏远了他的听众。|近义词:moralizing, preaching, instruction, edification|反义词:entertainment, amusement, fun|词性:名词

didactic:/daɪˈdæktɪk/
|adj.|教诲的；说教的|The poem is overly didactic and lacks any real emotional depth. 这首诗过于教诲，缺乏任何真正的情感深度。 |The professor's lectures were didactic but engaging. 教授的讲座虽然教诲，但却引人入胜。|近义词:instructive, educational, pedagogic, moralistic|反义词:entertaining, amusing, diverting|词性:形容词
