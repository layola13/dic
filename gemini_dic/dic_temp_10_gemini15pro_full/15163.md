back-stabbing: /ˈbækˌstæbɪŋ/ | adj. n. | 背后捅刀子；暗箭伤人 | The act of criticizing someone unfairly behind their back.  It describes behavior that is treacherous and deceitful, often involving betrayal of trust. |  He lost his job because of office politics and back-stabbing. 他因为办公室政治和背后捅刀子而丢了工作。| Synonyms: treacherous, deceitful, two-faced, disloyal, perfidious | Antonyms: loyal, faithful, trustworthy, supportive | adjective, noun

back-stabbing: /ˈbækˌstæbɪŋ/ | n. | 背后捅刀子；暗箭伤人 |  The act of betraying someone's trust, especially by criticizing them behind their back. | The team was rife with back-stabbing and infighting.  团队中充斥着背后捅刀子和内讧。 | Synonyms: treachery, deceit, betrayal | Antonyms: loyalty, faithfulness, support | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From back (adv.) + stabbing (n.), from stab (v.).  The figurative sense of "treacherous attack" dates from the late 19th century.
