leafiest:/ˈliːfiɪst/ | adj. | 多叶的；枝繁叶茂的 | Having the most leaves; covered with many leaves. |  The leafiest tree in the park provided ample shade. 公园里枝叶最茂盛的树提供了充足的阴凉。| leafy, lush, verdant | bare, barren | adjective

Etymology:
From leaf + -y + -est.  "Leaf" comes from Old English *lēaf, from Proto-Germanic *laubiz, from Proto-Indo-European *leub-.  "-y" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "having the quality of," from Old English -ig.  "-est" is a superlative suffix.
