muggy:/ˈmʌɡi/ (US) /ˈmʌɡi/ (UK)| adj.|闷热的，潮湿的|Characterized by warm, moist, and oppressive air, often indicating an impending thunderstorm.|It was a muggy day, and everyone was sweating.  今天天气闷热，每个人都在流汗。|The muggy weather made it difficult to sleep. 闷热的天气让人难以入睡。|近义词：humid, sticky, sultry, close|反义词：dry, crisp, cool, fresh|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From mug (n.) + -y (suffix forming adjectives of quality, state, or resemblance). The original "mug" referred to a mist or drizzle, and "muggy" initially meant misty or drizzly. Over time, the meaning shifted to describe the warm, damp, and oppressive atmosphere that often accompanies such weather.
