palliative:/ˈpæliətɪv/| adj. n.|缓和的，治标不治本的；缓和剂|Relieving pain or alleviating a problem without dealing with the underlying cause.  |The doctor prescribed palliative care for the patient's chronic pain. 医生为病人开了慢性疼痛的舒缓治疗。|Synonyms: soothing, alleviating, mitigating, relieving, assuaging |Antonyms: curative, remedial, corrective|adjective, noun

palliative:/ˈpæliətɪv/| adj. n.|姑息的；权宜的|A temporary or superficial remedy or measure that does not address the root cause of a problem.  |The government offered a palliative solution to the economic crisis. 政府为经济危机提供了一个权宜之计。|Synonyms: temporary, stopgap, makeshift, expedient |Antonyms: permanent, lasting, long-term|adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *palliativus, from Latin palliatus (past participle of palliare “to cloak, conceal”), from pallium “cloak, coverlet.”
