grenadier:/ˌɡrɛnəˈdɪər/| n.|掷弹兵；榴弹兵；卫兵；|A soldier, originally one specially trained in throwing grenades.  A member of a regiment formerly composed of such soldiers. A tall, erect person.|The grenadiers stormed the enemy trench. 掷弹兵猛攻敌人的战壕。|Synonyms: soldier, trooper, guardsman|Antonyms: civilian|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *grenadier*, from *grenade* (grenade), from Old French *pome grenade* (pomegranate), from Latin *pomum granatum* (seeded apple).  The soldiers were so named because they originally threw grenades resembling pomegranates.
