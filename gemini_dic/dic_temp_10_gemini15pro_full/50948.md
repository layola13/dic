designates:/ˈdɛzɪɡneɪts/
verb
指定；指派；标明；命名
Officially give a specified status or role to. To mark or point out clearly.

The red flag designates the location of the lifeguard station.
红旗标明了救生员站的位置。


The president designated her as his successor.
总统指定她作为他的继任者。


Synonyms: appoint, nominate, assign, denote, indicate, identify, specify
Antonyms: dismiss, remove, withhold, obscure

verb
动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin designatus, past participle of designare (“to mark out, indicate, choose, appoint”), from de- (“out, from”) + signare (“to mark, seal”), from signum (“mark, sign, token”).
