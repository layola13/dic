concealedness:/kənˈsiːldnɪs/| n.|隐蔽性，隐藏状态|The concealedness of the weapon made it difficult to detect. 武器的隐蔽性使其难以被发现。|例句：The concealedness of the documents was crucial to the success of the mission.  这些文件的隐蔽性对任务的成功至关重要。

例句：The spy relied on the concealedness of his identity.  这个间谍依赖于他身份的隐蔽性。


|近义词：secrecy, hiddenness, camouflage, disguise|反义词: openness, visibility, exposure|词性:noun

conceal:/kənˈsiːl/| vt.|隐藏，掩盖|He tried to conceal his disappointment. 他试图掩盖他的失望。|例句：She concealed the gift behind her back. 她把礼物藏在背后。

例句：The dense foliage concealed the entrance to the cave. 茂密的树叶遮蔽了洞穴的入口。

|近义词：hide, cover, mask, obscure|反义词: reveal, expose, show, uncover|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *concelen, from Old French conceler, from Latin concēlāre (“to hide, conceal”), from con- (“completely”) + cēlāre (“to hide”).
