fleur-de-lis:/ˌflɜːr də ˈliː/ (US), /ˌflɜː(r) də ˈliː/ (UK)| n.|百合花饰; 鸢尾花(图案)|A stylized design of a lily or iris, often used as a symbol, especially associated with French royalty.  It has three petals, the central one upright and the outer two curving outwards. Its origins are debated, but it has been used in heraldry and other decorative arts for centuries.|The king's banner was emblazoned with golden fleurs-de-lis. 国王的旗帜上饰有金色的鸢尾花。|Synonyms:  lily symbol, iris emblem, heraldic lily|Antonyms: N/A|noun
