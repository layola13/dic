louting: /ˈlaʊtɪŋ/ | n. | 喧闹；大声叫喊；狂欢作乐 | The act of behaving in a noisy and boisterous way. |  The louting of the football fans disturbed the neighborhood. 足球迷的喧闹扰乱了附近的居民。| 近义词: carousing, revelry, boisterousness, rowdiness | 反义词: quiet, silence | 词性: 名词

louting: /ˈlaʊtɪŋ/ | v. | 鞠躬；弯腰 | To bend or bow the body or head. | The courtiers were louting before the king. 朝臣们在国王面前鞠躬。| 近义词: bowing, stooping | 反义词: standing | 词性: 动词

词源:  From Middle English *louten, from Old Norse *lúta (“to bend, stoop, bow”), from Proto-Germanic *lūtaną (“to bend, stoop”). Cognate with Old English lūtan (“to bow, bend, incline, fall, lie down, stoop”), Old Frisian lūta, Old Saxon lūtian, Middle Dutch lūten, Dutch luiten (“to bow”), Old High German lūzen, German leuchten (“to shine”). Related to Old English lot (“a lot, share”), from Proto-Germanic *hlautam (“lot, share”).
