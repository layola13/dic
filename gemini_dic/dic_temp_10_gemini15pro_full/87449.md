hesitatory:/ˈhezɪtətɔːri/ (US) /ˈhezɪtəri/ (UK)| adj.|犹豫的，迟疑的，踌躇的|Inclined to hesitate; tending to pause or waver in action, purpose, or speech because of uncertainty, indecision, or disinclination.|She gave a hesitatory smile. 她迟疑地笑了笑。|Synonyms: tentative, uncertain, reluctant, doubtful, indecisive|Antonyms: decisive, confident, resolute, assertive|adjective

hesitantly:/ˈhezɪtəntli/ (US) /ˈhezɪtəntli/ (UK)| adv.|犹豫地，迟疑地|In a hesitant manner; with hesitation.|He hesitantly agreed to help. 他犹豫地同意帮忙。|Synonyms: tentatively, uncertainly, reluctantly|Antonyms: decisively, confidently, resolutely|adverb

hesitation:/ˌhezɪˈteɪʃn/| n.|犹豫，迟疑，踌躇|The act of hesitating; a pause or wavering in action, purpose, or speech.|She answered without hesitation. 她毫不犹豫地回答。|Synonyms: pause, wavering, indecision, reluctance|Antonyms: decisiveness, resolution, certainty|noun
