canonizing:/ˈkænənaɪzɪŋ/
verb
册封为圣徒；使成为正典；使神圣化
The act of officially declaring someone a saint, especially by the Catholic Church. Also, the act of accepting something as being of high quality or importance.
The church is canonizing the late Mother Teresa. 教会正在册封已故的特蕾莎修女为圣徒。
Shakespeare's works are practically canonized in English literature. 莎士比亚的作品实际上已成为英国文学的经典。
近义词:glorifying, sanctifying, extolling, deifying
反义词:condemning, denouncing, vilifying
词性:v.
