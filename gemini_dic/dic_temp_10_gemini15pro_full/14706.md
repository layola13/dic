词源:  "awardability" is derived from "award" (verb and noun) + "-able" (suffix making adjectives) + "-ity" (suffix making nouns from adjectives).  "Award" itself comes from Old North French "eswarder" meaning "to judge, decide, assign."

英文解析: "Awardabilities" refers to the qualities or characteristics of something that make it deserving of an award or recognition. It can also refer to the potential for something to be awarded or granted. It's a less common word and often replaced with more straightforward terms like "merits" or "potential for awards."
