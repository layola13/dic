gloat:/ɡloʊt/| vi. |幸灾乐祸，沾沾自喜|dwell on one's own success or another's misfortune with smugness or malignant pleasure.|He gloated over his rival's failure. 他对竞争对手的失败幸灾乐祸。|She gloated about her new job. 她对自己的新工作沾沾自喜。|近义词：rejoice, relish, revel, crow, boast|反义词：commiserate, sympathize|词性:动词

gloated:/ˈɡloʊtɪd/ |v. (past tense and past participle of gloat) |幸灾乐祸过，沾沾自喜过|The past tense and past participle form of "gloat."|He had gloated over his enemy's downfall. 他曾因敌人的垮台而幸灾乐祸。 |She gloated about her high score on the test. 她对考试的高分沾沾自喜。 |近义词: rejoiced, reveled, relished|反义词: commiserated, sympathized|词性:动词

词源: From Middle English *gloten, from Old Norse *gláta (“to grin, smile scornfully”), from Proto-Germanic *glōaną (“to glow, shine”).
