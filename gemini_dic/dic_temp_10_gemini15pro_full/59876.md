ecumenists:/ɪˈkjuːmənɪsts/
|n.|基督教合一主义者|People who promote ecumenism, the principle or aim of promoting unity among the world's Christian churches.|例句：The ecumenists worked tirelessly to bridge the divides between different denominations. 基督教合一主义者孜孜不倦地致力于弥合不同教派之间的分歧。

例句：Many ecumenists believe that greater cooperation between churches is essential in the modern world. 许多基督教合一主义者认为，在现代世界，教会之间加强合作至关重要。
|近义词：unifiers, conciliators|反义词：sectarians, separatists|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *oecumenicus*, from Greek *oikoumenikos* "of the inhabited world, general, universal",  from *oikoumene* "the inhabited world," from *oikeō* "I inhabit," from *oikos* "house."  The word entered English in the mid-19th century.
