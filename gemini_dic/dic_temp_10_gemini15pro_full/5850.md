amazers:/əˈmeɪzərz/
|n. (plural)|令人惊叹的人或事物|People or things that cause amazement. |The acrobats were true amazers, captivating the entire audience.| 近义词:astonishers, wonders, marvels|反义词:disappointers, bore|词性:名词

词源:
From the verb "amaze" + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person or thing that performs the action of the verb).  "Amaze" comes from Old French "amaser" (to bewilder, confound), ultimately from Vulgar Latin *examasiare (to put out of one's mind), from Latin "ex" (out of) + "a-" (not, without) + "mens" (mind).
