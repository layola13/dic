bills:/bɪlz/| n.|账单，钞票，议案|A piece of paper money or a statement of money owed.|例句：The bills are piling up on my desk. 我的办公桌上堆满了账单。

例句：He paid the bills with a hundred-dollar bill. 他用一张一百美元的钞票付了账单。|近义词：invoices, checks, notes, statements (for bills as statements of money owed), currency, cash (for bills as paper money), legislation, acts (for bills as proposed laws)|反义词:payments, income|词性:名词

bills:/bɪlz/| n.|鸟嘴|The beak of a bird.|例句：The bird used its bills to crack open the seeds. 这只鸟用它的喙啄开了种子。

例句：The pelican has a large bills for scooping up fish. 鹈鹕有一个很大的喙用来舀鱼。|近义词：beak, nib|反义词:None|词性:名词
