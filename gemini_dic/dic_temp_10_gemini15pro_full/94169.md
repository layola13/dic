impenitentness:/ɪmˈpɛnɪtəntnəs/| n. |不悔悟，顽固不化|The state of being impenitent; lack of remorse or regret for wrongdoing.|His impenitentness made it difficult to forgive him. 他的顽固不化使得很难原谅他。|Synonyms: remorselessness, unrepentance, obduracy, hardness of heart|Antonyms: remorse, repentance, penitence, contrition|noun

impenitent:/ɪmˈpɛnɪtənt/| adj. |不悔悟的，顽固不化的|Not feeling sorry for having done something wrong.|He remained impenitent despite the evidence against him. 尽管有证据证明他有罪，但他仍然不知悔改。|Synonyms: remorseless, unrepentant, obdurate, uncontrite|Antonyms: remorseful, repentant, penitent, contrite|adjective


Etymology:
From Late Middle English *impenitentnes*, from impenitent + -ness.  Impenitent comes from Old French impenitent, from Latin *impoenitent-*, *impoenitens*,  from in- "not" + poenitent- "repenting."  Ultimately from Latin *paenitēre* "to repent."
