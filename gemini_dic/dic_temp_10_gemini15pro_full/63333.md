entitling:/ɪnˈtaɪtlɪŋ/
v.
赋予权利；给予资格
giving someone the right to have or do something
例句：The law entitles you to a full refund.  法律赋予你全额退款的权利。

例句：Being unemployed entitles you to receive benefits. 失业使你有资格领取救济金。

近义词：empowering, authorizing, qualifying
反义词：disqualifying, restricting
词性：动词 (Verb)


entitling:/ɪnˈtaɪtlɪŋ/
adj.
(通常指书籍、电影等)吸引人的，引人入胜的
(usually of a book, film, etc.) having a name that attracts your attention and makes you want to know more
例句：The book had a rather entitling title, which made me want to read it. 这本书的书名相当吸引人，让我想读一读。

例句：The documentary's entitling premise drew a large audience.  这部纪录片引人入胜的前提吸引了大量的观众。

近义词：appealing, captivating, intriguing
反义词：dull, boring, uninteresting
词性：形容词 (Adjective)
