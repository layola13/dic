abdicators: /ˈæbdɪˌkeɪtərz/
n.
退位者，放弃者
Those who formally renounce or relinquish a throne, right, claim, responsibility, or the like.
The abdicators lived a quiet life after leaving the throne.  退位者离开王位后过着平静的生活。
King Edward VIII and other abdicators in history have chosen love over duty.  爱德华八世国王和历史上的其他退位者选择了爱情而不是责任。
近义词: renouncers, relinquishers, forsakers
反义词: inheritors, successors, usurpers
词性: Noun (plural)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin abdicatus, past participle of abdicare (“to renounce, disown, disclaim, reject”), from ab- (“away from”) + dicare (“to proclaim, dedicate”).

USAGE->
The abdicators were interviewed about their decision. =  退位者就他们的决定接受了采访。
The abdicators' lives after leaving their positions were vastly different. = 退位者离开职位后的生活大相径庭。
