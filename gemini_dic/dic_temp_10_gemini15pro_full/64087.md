epoch:/ˈiːpɒk/ (US) /ˈɛpɒk/ (UK)| n. |时代，纪元，时期|A period of time in history or a person's life, typically one marked by notable events or particular characteristics.|例句：The invention of the printing press marked a new epoch in human history. 印刷机的发明标志着人类历史的新纪元。|例句：The Victorian epoch was a time of great social change. 维多利亚时代是一个社会巨大变革的时期。|近义词：era, age, period, time|反义词:instant|词性:名词

epochs:/ˈiːpɒks/ (US) /ˈɛpɒks/ (UK)| n. |时代，纪元，时期（epoch的复数形式）|Plural form of epoch, referring to multiple periods of time in history or a person's life, typically marked by notable events or particular characteristics.|例句：The geological record is divided into different epochs. 地质记录被划分为不同的时期。|例句：He recalled the great epochs of his life. 他回忆起他人生中的伟大时代。|近义词：eras, ages, periods, times|反义词:instants|词性:名词
