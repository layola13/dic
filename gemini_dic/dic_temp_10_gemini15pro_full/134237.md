noninfallibly:/ˌnɒnɪnˈfælɪbli/| adv.|并非绝对可靠地，并非万无一失地|Not infallibly; in a way that is not certain to be correct or accurate.  It implies that something is prone to error or mistakes.|The system, while sophisticated, does not noninfallibly predict future market trends.|近义词：imperfectly, faillibly, erringly|反义词:infallibly, certainly, accurately|词性:副词

词源:
non- (prefix):  Meaning "not," from Latin *non.
infallibly (adverb): From Late Latin infallibilis, from in- "not" + fallere "to deceive, err."
