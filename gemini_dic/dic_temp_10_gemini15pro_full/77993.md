gentle: /ˈdʒɛnt(ə)l/ (British) /ˈdʒɛntl/ (American)| adj.|温柔的；温和的；文雅的；轻柔的|Kind, soft, mild, not harsh or violent.  Characterized by a calm, peaceful, and tender nature.|She has a gentle touch with animals. 她对动物很温柔。|Synonyms: kind, tender, mild, soft, delicate, meek, calm, peaceful, soothing, lenient, moderate|Antonyms: harsh, rough, violent, aggressive, severe, forceful, strong, wild, turbulent|adjective

gentle: /ˈdʒɛnt(ə)l/ (British) /ˈdʒɛntl/ (American)| n.|蛆；饵|Maggot, especially one used as bait in fishing. (This meaning is much less common than the adjective form)|He used gentles to catch trout. 他用蛆钓鳟鱼。|Synonyms: maggot, bait|Antonyms: (Not applicable for this meaning)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gentil*, from Latin *gentilis* "of the same clan or family," from *gens* "clan, family, nation, race," related to *gignere* "to beget." The meaning shifted from "noble, of good family" to "courteous, refined" and then to "mild, soft."
