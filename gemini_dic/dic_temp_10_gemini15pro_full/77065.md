garnisher:/ˈɡɑːrnɪʃər/| n.|装饰者，加装饰菜的人|A person who garnishes food.|The garnisher added a sprig of parsley to the dish. 装饰者在菜上加了一枝欧芹。|近义词:decorator, embellisher|反义词:(None, as it refers to a person)|noun

garnishment:/ˈɡɑːrnɪʃmənt/| n.|装饰，装饰物，(尤指食物的)配菜; (工资的)扣押|Something used to garnish something else, especially food; the legal withholding of a portion of wages to pay a debt.|The garnishment on the salad made it look more appealing. 沙拉上的装饰物使它看起来更诱人。|The garnishment of his wages left him with very little to live on.  他的工资被扣押，几乎没有剩余的钱来维持生活。|近义词:decoration, embellishment, trimming (for food); attachment, seizure (for wages)|反义词:(None for food garnishment); release, repayment (for wage garnishment)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "garnir" (to furnish, equip, provide, garnish), from Frankish "*warnjan" (to provide, defend), from Proto-Germanic "*warnijaną" (to take care, beware). The meaning shifted from "to equip" to "to decorate" in the 15th century.
