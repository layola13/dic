bootlace: /ˈbuːtleɪs/ (UK) /ˈbuːtleɪs/ (US) | n. | 鞋带 | A string or cord used to fasten a boot or shoe. |  He tripped over his untied bootlace. 他被自己没系的鞋带绊倒了。| synonym: shoelace, laces | antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps buckle, strap for alternative fastening methods) | noun


bootlace: /ˈbuːtleɪs/ (UK) /ˈbuːtleɪs/ (US) | adj. | 鞋带似的; 微薄的, 勉强够的 | Resembling or relating to a bootlace; meager, barely sufficient. | He earned a bootlace living selling newspapers. 他靠卖报纸勉强维持生计。 | synonym: meager, scanty, slender, shoestring | antonym: ample, abundant, generous, substantial | adjective



ETYMOLOGY->
From "boot" + "lace." "Boot" comes from Old English "bōt" (a covering for the leg and foot), related to Old Norse "bót" (remedy, help). "Lace" comes from Old French "las," ultimately from Latin "laqueus" (noose, snare).
