papilionides:/pəˌpɪliˈɒnɪdiːz/ | n. |凤蝶科| A family of butterflies, commonly known as swallowtails or parnassians.  They are characterized by their large size, often brightly colored wings, and the presence of tail-like extensions on the hindwings of many species. | Example: The Papilionides are a diverse group of butterflies found worldwide. 凤蝶科是一个遍布世界各地的蝴蝶多样化群体。

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "papilio" (butterfly) + "-ides" (plural suffix denoting a family or group).  The word "papilio" itself is believed to be derived from the Latin verb "papillare" meaning "to flutter".
