overjudicious: /ˌōvərˌdʒuˈdɪʃəs/
|adj.|
|过于审慎的；过于明断的；吹毛求疵的|
|Characterized by excessive or inappropriate judiciousness; too critical or cautious.|
|He was so overjudicious in his choice of words that the speech became tedious.|他遣词造句过于谨慎，以至于演讲变得冗长乏味。
|
|The overjudicious editor made so many changes that the author's voice was lost.|过于明断的编辑做了太多修改，以至于作者的声音消失了。
|
|近义词: hypercritical, overscrupulous, fastidious|
|反义词: careless, negligent, rash|
|词性: 形容词|

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + judicious.  "Over-" acts as a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much."  "Judicious" comes from the Latin "iudicium" meaning "judgment," ultimately derived from "iudex" meaning "judge."
