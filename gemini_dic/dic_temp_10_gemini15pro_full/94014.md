impalsy: /ɪmˈpɔːlzi/ | n. | 瘫痪，麻痹；无力；无能为力 | Paralysis or weakness, especially in a part of the body.  |  The stroke caused impalsy in his left arm.  中风导致他左臂瘫痪。|  paralysis, weakness, debility | strength, power, ability | noun

Etymology:  From Late Latin *impalsus, past participle of impallēscere “to grow pale, lose strength,” from Latin in- “in, on” + pallēscere “to grow pale,” from pallēre “to be pale.”
