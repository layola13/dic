bicameralists:/ˌbaɪˈkæmərəlɪsts/| n. |两院制拥护者；支持两院制的人|People who advocate for or support a bicameral system of government, meaning a legislature with two separate chambers or houses, like the Senate and House of Representatives in the US. |The bicameralists argued that two houses would provide checks and balances and prevent hasty legislation.|Synonyms: supporters of bicameralism, advocates for two-chamber legislatures |Antonyms: unicameralists| noun
