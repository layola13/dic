overtax:/ˌoʊvərˈtæks/| vt.|使负担过重；课税过重；|To make excessive demands on (someone or something).|The long working hours overtaxed the employees. 长时间的工作让员工们负担过重。|Synonyms: overburden, overload, strain|Antonyms: undertax, ease, lighten|verb

overtax:/ˌoʊvərˈtæks/| vt.|过度使用；滥用|To use something too much or for too long, so that it becomes weak or damaged.|Don't overtax your eyesight by reading in dim light. 不要在昏暗的灯光下看书，以免过度使用视力。|Synonyms: overuse, strain, exhaust|Antonyms: underuse, conserve|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + tax.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much." "Tax" comes from Old French "taxer" meaning "to assess, estimate," ultimately from Latin "taxare" meaning "to handle, estimate, censure."
