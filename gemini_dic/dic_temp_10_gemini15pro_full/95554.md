incognitant:/ɪnˈkɒɡnɪtənt/ | adj. | 未知的，未被认识的；隐姓埋名的 | Not known or recognized; disguised; traveling under an assumed name or identity. | He traveled incognitant, using a false name and passport. 他隐姓埋名地旅行，使用假名和护照。|  The prince travelled incognitant through the town. 王子隐姓埋名地穿过城镇。| 近义词：anonymous, unknown, disguised, incognito | 反义词：known, recognized, famous | 词性: 形容词

incognitant:/ɪnˈkɒɡnɪtənt/ | n. | 隐姓埋名的人 | A person who is unknown or unrecognized; a person traveling incognito. | The hotel staff were instructed not to reveal the incognitant's true identity.  酒店工作人员被指示不要透露这位隐姓埋名者的真实身份。 |  The incognitant slipped into the back of the theater unnoticed.  这位隐姓埋名者不引人注目地溜进了剧院后排。 | 近义词：incognito | 反义词：celebrity | 词性: 名词


词源:

From Latin *incognitāns*, present participle of *incognōscere* (“not to know, not to recognize”), from *in-* (“not”) + *cognōscere* (“to get to know”).
