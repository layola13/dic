dreadfulnesses:/ˈdrɛdfʊlnəsɪz/
n.
可怕；恐怖；令人不快的事情
The extreme unpleasantness or awfulness of something.
The dreadfulnesses of war are well documented.  战争的恐怖被充分记载。
The museum exhibit detailed the dreadfulnesses of the Holocaust.  博物馆的展览详细描述了大屠杀的恐怖。
近义词: horrors, terrors, atrocities, awfulness
反义词: pleasantness, delights, joys
词性: 名词 (plural)

dreadfulness:/ˈdrɛdfʊlnəs/
n.
可怕；恐怖；令人不快
The state or quality of being dreadful.
The dreadfulness of the situation was overwhelming.  情况的可怕程度令人难以承受。
She spoke of the dreadfulness of solitary confinement. 她讲述了单独监禁的可怕之处。
近义词: awfulness, terribleness, frightfulness
反义词: pleasantness, wonderfulness, delightfulness
词性: 名词

dreadful:/ˈdrɛdfʊl/
adj.
可怕的；糟糕的；极坏的
Causing or involving great suffering, fear, or unhappiness; extremely bad or serious.
The weather was dreadful. 天气糟透了。
It was a dreadful accident. 这是一场可怕的事故。
近义词: terrible, awful, horrible, appalling
反义词: wonderful, excellent, great, pleasant
词性: 形容词

dread:/drɛd/
n.
恐惧；担忧；可怕的人（或事物）
Great fear and apprehension.
She lives in dread of her husband finding out. 她一直担心丈夫会发现真相。
He's the dread of all the teachers. 他是所有老师都害怕的人。
v.
害怕；担心；恐惧
Anticipate with great apprehension or fear.
I dread to think what would happen if he found out. 我不敢想象如果他发现了会发生什么。
She dreaded going to the dentist. 她害怕去看牙医。
近义词: fear, terror, apprehension (n.); fear, be afraid of (v.)
反义词: hope, anticipation, eagerness (n.); look forward to (v.)
词性: 名词，动词

词源:  From Middle English *dredful, from Old English *drēdfull, equivalent to dread +‎ -ful.
