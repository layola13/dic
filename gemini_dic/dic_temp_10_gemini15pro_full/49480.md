delusive:/dɪˈluːsɪv/ | adj. | 欺骗性的，虚假的，错觉的 | Giving a false or misleading impression; deceptive. |  He had delusional beliefs about his own importance. 他对他自己的重要性抱有错觉。 |  misleading, deceptive, fallacious, illusory, spurious | realistic, genuine, truthful, factual, valid | adjective

delusion:/dɪˈluːʒən/ | n. | 错觉，妄想 | An idiosyncratic belief or impression that is firmly maintained despite being contradicted by what is generally accepted as reality or rational argument, typically a symptom of mental disorder. | He suffered from delusions of grandeur. 他患有夸大妄想症。 | misconception, misbelief, fallacy, illusion, hallucination | reality, truth, fact, actuality, verity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dēlusīvus, from Latin dēlūs-, past participle stem of dēlūdere "to mock, deceive," from de- "down, completely" + lūdere "to play."
