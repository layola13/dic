acer:/ˈeɪsər/| n.|枫树; 槭树|A tree of the genus Acer, characterized by its usually five-lobed leaves and winged seeds.  Maple syrup is made from the sap of some North American species.|The acer in my yard turns a brilliant red in the fall. 我院子里的枫树在秋天变成鲜红色。|Synonym: maple|Antonym: N/A|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *acer* meaning "sharp, pointed," referring to the pointed lobes of the leaves.  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ḱr̥ós* meaning "sharp."
