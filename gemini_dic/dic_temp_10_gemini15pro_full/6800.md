anapodeictic:/ˌænəpəˈdaɪktɪk/| adj.|必然的，无可辩驳的|Clearly or demonstrably necessary; undeniable.  Relating to something that is necessarily true or that can be known with certainty.|The anapodeictic proof of the theorem convinced even the most skeptical mathematicians.|近义词：incontrovertible, undeniable, irrefutable, unquestionable|反义词：dubious, questionable, debatable, disputable|词性:形容词
词源: From Greek *anapodeiktos* "demonstrated, proved," from *anapo+deiknynai* "to show, demonstrate."
