cardinalities:/ˌkɑːrdɪˈnælɪtiz/| n.|基数; 基数性|The cardinalities of the two sets are different. 这两个集合的基数不同。|Cardinality refers to the size of a set, specifically the number of elements it contains.  It is a fundamental concept in set theory and is often used in mathematics, computer science, and database design to describe the size or "count" of things.|The database schema defines the cardinalities of the relationships between tables.  该数据库模式定义了表之间关系的基数。|size, count, magnitude| N/A |noun
