cogwheel:/ˈkɒɡwiːl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːɡwiːl/ (US)| n. |齿轮|A wheel with teeth around its edge, designed to mesh with the teeth of another wheel or part to transmit motion.|The cogwheels of the clock were intricate and fascinating. 时钟的齿轮复杂而迷人.|Synonyms: gear, gearwheel|Antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cogwhele*, equivalent to cog + wheel.  Cog itself likely comes from Old Norse *kaggi* meaning "cog, projection."
