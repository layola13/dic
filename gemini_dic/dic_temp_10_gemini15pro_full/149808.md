paleocarida:/ˌpeɪliːoʊˈkærɪdə/| n. |古甲壳类|An extinct subclass of crustaceans that existed from the Cambrian to the Permian periods, characterized by a bivalved carapace and a segmented body.  They are considered to be an important group in understanding the evolution of crustaceans.|Paleocarida fossils are crucial for understanding early crustacean evolution. 古甲壳类化石对于理解早期甲壳类动物的进化至关重要。| The paleocarida were a diverse group inhabiting various marine environments.  古甲壳类是一个多样化的群体，栖息在各种海洋环境中。|近义词: ancient crustaceans|反义词:  (No direct antonym, potentially modern crustaceans)|词性:noun
