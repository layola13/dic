cosmopolitan:/ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtən/ /ˌkɑːzməˈpɑːlɪtən/| n. adj.|世界主义者，世界性的|A cosmopolitan person is familiar with and at ease in many different countries and cultures.  A cosmopolitan place is full of people from many different countries and cultures.|She considers herself a cosmopolitan. 她认为自己是一个世界主义者。|Synonyms: worldly, sophisticated, cultured, international, global|Antonyms: provincial, parochial, insular, narrow-minded|noun

cosmopolitan:/ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtən/ /ˌkɑːzməˈpɑːlɪtən/| adj.|世界性的，世界主义的|Containing or having experience of people and things from many different parts of the world.|London is a very cosmopolitan city. 伦敦是一个非常国际化的城市。|Synonyms: worldly, sophisticated, cultured, international, global|Antonyms: provincial, parochial, insular, narrow-minded|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *kosmopolítēs* "citizen of the world," from *kosmos* "world, universe" + *polítēs* "citizen."
