monopolizer:/məˈnɑːpəˌlaɪzər/ /məˈnɒpəˌlaɪzər/| n. |垄断者，独占者|Someone who monopolizes something; a person who obtains exclusive possession of a commodity or service.| The company became a ruthless monopolizer, controlling nearly all aspects of the industry.  这家公司变成了一个无情的垄断者，控制着该行业的几乎所有方面。

The tech giant was accused of being a data monopolizer, limiting competition and stifling innovation.  这家科技巨头被指控是数据垄断者，限制竞争并扼杀创新。|近义词：hoarder, cornerer, controller |反义词: competitor, sharer |词性: noun

Etymology: From monopolize + -er.  From French monopoliser, from monopole (“monopoly”) + -iser, equivalent to English -ize.  Ultimately from Greek mónos (“alone, single”) + póleos (“for sale”).
