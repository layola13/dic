cloddily:/ˈklɑːdəli/ /ˈklɒdɪli/| adv.|笨拙地；土里土气地；粗俗地|In a cloddish or clumsy manner; awkwardly and without grace.  Characterized by a lack of refinement or sophistication.|He walked cloddily across the room, knocking over a lamp. 他笨手笨脚地走过房间，撞翻了一盏灯。|Synonyms: clumsily, awkwardly, gracelessly, gawkily, lumberingly|Antonyms: gracefully, elegantly, deftly, smoothly, adroitly|词性: 副词

cloddish:/ˈklɑːdɪʃ/ /ˈklɒdɪʃ/| adj.|笨拙的；土里土气的；粗俗的|Lacking grace or refinement; awkward and clumsy.  Unsophisticated or uncultured.|His cloddish manners embarrassed her. 他粗俗的举止让她感到尴尬。|Synonyms: clumsy, awkward, graceless, oafish, boorish|Antonyms: graceful, elegant, refined, sophisticated, urbane|词性: 形容词


词源:  From clod + -ish.  "Clod" originally referred to a lump of earth, and by extension came to mean a stupid or clumsy person.
