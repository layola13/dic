bitt:/bɪt/ | n. | 小块，少量，一小口 | A small piece or amount of something. |  She took a little bitt of the cake. 她拿了一小块蛋糕。| Synonyms: piece, morsel, fragment | Antonyms: lot, whole | noun

Etymology: From Middle English *bitte*, probably from Old Norse *bit*, related to *bita* "to bite".  Originally referring to a mouthful of food, the meaning broadened to refer to a small piece of anything.
