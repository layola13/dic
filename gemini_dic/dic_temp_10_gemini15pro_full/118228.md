mercaptide:/mərˈkæptaɪd/| n. |硫醇盐|A compound formed by replacing the hydrogen atom of a thiol group (-SH) with a metal ion, especially a heavy metal ion.|Silver mercaptide is used in some antibacterial preparations. 硫醇银用于一些抗菌制剂中。|Synonym: thiolate|Antonym: N/A|noun
