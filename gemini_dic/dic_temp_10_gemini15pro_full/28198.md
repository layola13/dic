capriciously:/kəˈprɪʃəsli/ | adv. | 善变地；反复无常地 | In a capricious manner; unpredictably; impulsively. |  She acted capriciously, changing her mind every few minutes. 她反复无常，每隔几分钟就改变主意。 | She smiled capriciously, leaving him wondering what she was thinking. 她反复无常地笑了笑，让他琢磨不透她在想什么。 | synonym: erratically, whimsically, impulsively, arbitrarily | antonym: consistently, steadily, predictably | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *capriciosus, from Italian capriccioso (“whimsical”), from capriccio (“sudden start”), from capo (“head”) + riccio (“hedgehog”).  The image is of a head with hair standing on end, like a hedgehog's spines, suggesting a sudden whim or change of mood.
