maunder:/ˈmɔːndər/ (British), /ˈmɑːndər/ (American)| v. n.| 闲逛；漫步；唠叨；抱怨|To talk in a rambling or incoherent way, often complaining; to wander aimlessly.|He maundered on about his childhood for hours. 他絮絮叨叨地讲了他几个小时的童年故事。|近义词：ramble, mutter, grumble, wander, meander|反义词：articulate, be concise, be direct|词性: 动词/名词

maunders (plural): /ˈmɔːndərz/ (British), /ˈmɑːndərz/ (American)| n.| 唠叨；抱怨|Rambling or incoherent talk, often complaining.|Her maunders about the weather were becoming tiresome. 她对天气的抱怨越来越令人厌烦。|近义词： ramblings, complaints, grumbles|反义词： praise, compliments|词性: 名词

词源:  From the obsolete verb *maund*, meaning "to beg," likely related to the Old French word *mendier*, meaning "to beg."  The shift in meaning from begging to aimless talking and complaining may be due to the perceived similarity between a beggar's rambling pleas and incoherent complaining.
