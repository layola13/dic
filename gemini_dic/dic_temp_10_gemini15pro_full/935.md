abyssal:/əˈbɪsl/| adj.|深渊的；深不可测的|Relating to or denoting the depths or bed of the ocean, especially between about 2,000 and 6,000 meters (6,500 and 20,000 feet) down.|The anglerfish is a bizarre-looking inhabitant of the abyssal zone.  琵琶鱼是居住在深渊地带的长相奇特的生物。

The submarine explored the abyssal plains of the Atlantic. 这艘潜水艇探索了大西洋的深海平原。|近义词：profound, deep, unfathomable|反义词：shallow, superficial|词性：形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *abyssal*, from Latin *abyssus* ("bottomless pit, immeasurable depth"), from Greek *ábyssos* ("bottomless").

USAGE->
Attributive Usage:
The abyssal depths are home to many strange creatures. = 深渊深处是许多奇异生物的家园。
Abyssal plains are vast, flat areas of the deep ocean floor. = 深海平原是广阔平坦的深海海底区域。

Predicative Usage (less common):
The darkness in the cave seemed abyssal. = 洞穴里的黑暗似乎深不可测。
The silence of the night felt abyssal and unsettling. = 夜晚的寂静让人感到深不可测和不安。


PHRASE->

abyssal fauna = 深海动物群
