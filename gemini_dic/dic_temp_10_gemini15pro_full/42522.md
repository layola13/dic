counterlathing:/ˈkaʊntərˌlæθɪŋ/ |n.|反向板条，挂瓦板条|A system of narrow strips of wood fixed to rafters or wall studs and upon which laths are nailed to provide an airspace between the laths and the wall or roof.  It creates an air cavity for insulation or ventilation.|The builder installed counterlathing before adding the exterior siding.  建筑工人在安装外墙板之前先安装了反向板条。|Counterlathing helps prevent moisture buildup in the walls. 反向板条有助于防止墙壁积聚水分。|Synonyms: furring strips, strapping|Antonyms: N/A|noun
