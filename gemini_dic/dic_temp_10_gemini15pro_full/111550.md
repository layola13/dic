lobbing:/ˈlɒbɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈlɑːbɪŋ/ (US)| n. v.|游说，疏通；高抛，吊高球|The act of trying to persuade a government official or politician to support a particular cause or policy.  Also, the act of throwing something in a high arc.|The company spent millions on lobbying to get the law changed. 这家公司花费数百万美元游说以修改法律。|Synonyms: persuading, influencing, canvassing, importuning|Antonyms:  n/a|noun/verb

lobbied:/ˈlɒbid/ (UK) /ˈlɑːbid/ (US)| v. |游说，疏通；(球)吊高|Past tense and past participle of "to lobby".|They lobbied hard for the new environmental regulations. 他们大力游说新的环境法规。|Synonyms: persuaded, influenced, canvassed, importuned|Antonyms: n/a|verb

lobs:/lɒbz/ (UK) /lɑːbz/ (US)| v. |高抛，吊高球|Third person singular present tense of "to lob".|He lobs the ball over the net. 他把球高高地抛过网。|Synonyms: throws, tosses, pitches|Antonyms: catches, receives|verb
