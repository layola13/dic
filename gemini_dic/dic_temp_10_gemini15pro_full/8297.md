anti-modern:/ˌæntiˈmɒdən/ /ˌæntiˈmɑːdərn/| adj.|反现代的，反对现代化的|Opposed to or rejecting modernism, especially in the arts, culture, or society.  Characterized by a preference for traditional values and practices.|He held a staunch anti-modern view of the world, preferring the simplicity of the past. 他对世界持有一种坚定的反现代观点，更喜欢过去的简单。|The architect's anti-modern design incorporated classical elements.  这位建筑师的反现代设计融合了古典元素。|近义词:traditional, conservative, reactionary|反义词:modern, contemporary, progressive|词性:adjective

anti-modernism:/ˌæntiˈmɒdəˌnɪzəm/ /ˌæntiˈmɑːdərnɪzəm/| n.|反现代主义|A philosophical movement that rejects modernism and its associated values, often advocating a return to traditional beliefs and practices.|Anti-modernism gained traction in the early 20th century as a reaction against rapid societal change. 反现代主义在20世纪初获得了关注，是对社会快速变化的一种反应。|His anti-modernism was reflected in his rejection of abstract art. 他的反现代主义体现在他对抽象艺术的排斥上。|近义词:traditionalism, conservatism|反义词:modernism, progressivism|词性:noun
