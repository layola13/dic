moderance:/ˈmɒdərəns/ | n. |节制，适度|The state or quality of being moderate; avoidance of extremes or excesses.|He preached moderation in all things. 他提倡凡事都要适度。|近义词: temperance, restraint, abstemiousness|反义词: excess, immoderation, intemperance|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *moderaunce*, from Old French *moderaunce*, from Late Latin *moderantia*, from Latin *moderant-*, present participle stem of *moderārī* (“to moderate”), from *modus* (“measure, limit, due proportion”) + *-ārī* (suffix forming denominative verbs).  Doublet of *moderation*.
