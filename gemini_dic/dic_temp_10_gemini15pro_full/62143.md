enclaving:/ɪnˈkleɪvɪŋ/
v. |包围；隔离；嵌入|The process of forming an enclave, surrounding a territory completely by another. |The government is accused of enclaving the minority group by restricting their movement and access to resources. 政府被指控通过限制少数群体的行动和获取资源来孤立他们。|例句：The artist created a sculpture by enclaving a piece of wood within a glass case. 艺术家通过将一块木头封入玻璃柜中创作了一个雕塑。|近义词：enclosing, surrounding, isolating, confining|反义词：integrating, uniting, connecting|词性：动词

enclave:/ˈɛnˌkleɪv/
n. |飞地；被包围的领土；孤立的地区|A portion of territory within or surrounded by a larger territory whose inhabitants are culturally or ethnically distinct.|例句：The small village remained a cultural enclave, preserving its traditions despite the changing world around it. 这个小村庄仍然是一个文化飞地，尽管周围的世界不断变化，它仍然保留着自己的传统。|近义词：island, pocket, territory|反义词：mainland, surroundings|词性：名词
