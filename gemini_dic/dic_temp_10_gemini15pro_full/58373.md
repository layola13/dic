dualistic:/duːəˈlɪstɪk/| adj.|二元的，二元论的|Characterized by or consisting of two distinct and opposed elements or principles, such as mind and matter, good and evil, or light and darkness.|Example 1:  The ancient philosophy embraced a dualistic worldview, seeing the universe as a struggle between good and evil. 古代哲学信奉二元世界观，将宇宙视为善与恶之间的斗争。

Example 2: Her dualistic approach to painting incorporated both abstract and realistic elements. 她的二元绘画方法融合了抽象和现实主义元素。|近义词：binary, dichotomous|反义词：monistic, unified|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Late Latin dualis + -istic.  "Dualis" meaning "containing two," from Latin duo "two."  The "-istic" suffix denotes a doctrine, system, or characteristic.
