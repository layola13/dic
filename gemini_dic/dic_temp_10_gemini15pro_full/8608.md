anticoagulant: /ˌæntikoʊˈæɡjələnt/ | n. adj. | 抗凝剂，抗凝的 | A substance that prevents blood from clotting. |
The doctor prescribed an anticoagulant to prevent blood clots after surgery. 医生开了抗凝剂以防止术后血液凝块。

This medication acts as an anticoagulant. 这种药物起到抗凝剂的作用。


| Synonym: blood thinner, antithrombotic | Antonym: coagulant, procoagulant |


Etymology:
From anti- (against) + coagulant (causing coagulation). Coagulant comes from Latin coagulāre "to curdle," from coagulum "rennet," from co- "together" + agere "to drive, lead, act."
