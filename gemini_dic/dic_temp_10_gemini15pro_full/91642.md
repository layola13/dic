hyperbole:/haɪˈpɜːrbəli/ | n. |夸张; 夸张法 |  An extravagant statement or claim not meant to be taken literally. |  Example: "I've told you a million times to clean your room" is a hyperbole.  我说过一百万次让你打扫你的房间（这是夸张的说法）。| Synonym: exaggeration, overstatement | Antonym: understatement, meiosis | noun

hyperbolic:/ˌhaɪpərˈbɒlɪk/ | adj. |夸张的; 夸张法的 | Relating to or characterized by hyperbole. | Example: His hyperbolic claims about the product's effectiveness were misleading. 他关于产品功效的夸张说法具有误导性。| Synonym: exaggerated, overstated | Antonym: understated, restrained | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hyperbola, from Greek ὑπερβολή (huperbolḗ, “excess, exaggeration, hyperbole”), from ὑπερβάλλειν (huperballein, “to exceed, throw beyond”), from ὑπέρ (hupér, “over, beyond”) + βάλλειν (ballein, “to throw”).
