conjure:/ˈkʌndʒər/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒə(r)/ (UK)| v. |变魔术；使…（像魔术般）出现；想起；恳求|To make something appear as if by magic, or to call upon or evoke a spirit or memory.|
The magician conjured a rabbit out of his hat. 魔术师从帽子里变出一只兔子。

She conjured up images of her childhood. 她想起了童年的景象。

He conjured them to help him. 他恳求他们帮助他。|近义词：summon, evoke, invoke, implore|反义词：banish, dispel|词性:动词

conjure up:/ˈkʌndʒər ʌp/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒə(r) ʌp/ (UK)| phrasal v. |使…（像魔术般）出现；想起|To bring something to mind, or to create or invent something.|
The smell of baking bread conjured up memories of my grandmother's kitchen. 烤面包的味道让我想起了祖母的厨房。

He conjured up a brilliant plan to save the company. 他想出了一个拯救公司的绝妙计划。|近义词：evoke, imagine, invent|反义词：forget, suppress|词性:动词词组


词源:
From Old French *conjurer* "to invoke (a devil or spirit) by oath, to implore," from Latin *conjurare* "to swear together, conspire," from *com-* "together" (see *com-) + *jurare* "to swear" (see *jury*). Meaning shifted in French to "to practice magic" (14c.), which was picked up in English.
