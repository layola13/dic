overbrutalizations: /ˌoʊvərˌbruːtələˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. | 过度残暴化；过度暴行 | The act of brutalizing excessively or to an extreme degree.  It refers to instances where violence or cruelty goes beyond what is considered necessary or justifiable, often involving repeated and excessive acts of brutality. | The overbrutalizations committed during the war left lasting scars on the population.  战争期间过度残暴的行为给人民留下了永久的伤疤。|  The reports documented numerous overbrutalizations of prisoners. 报告记录了对囚犯的大量过度暴行。 | 近义词: atrocities, barbarities, savagery | 反义词: kindness, compassion, humanity | 词性: 名词 (plural)


词源:

over- (prefix):  From Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *ubar, meaning "above," "over," "beyond."  Indicates excess or going beyond a limit.

brutalization (noun): From brutalize + -ation.

brutalize (verb): From brutal + -ize.

brutal (adjective): From French brutal, from Late Latin brutalis "of beasts," from Latin brutus "dull, stupid, irrational; senseless, brute, beast," from PIE root *gwer- "wild animal."
