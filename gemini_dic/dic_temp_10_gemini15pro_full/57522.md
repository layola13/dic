dragoonages:/drəˈɡuːnɪdʒɪz/
|n.|强制征兵；强迫服兵役；以武力胁迫|The act of dragooning, or the state of being dragooned; oppressive exaction.  The practice of forcing someone into military service.|The dragooning of young men into the army was met with resistance. 年轻男子被强征入伍遭到了抵抗。|近义词：conscription, impressment, forced enlistment|反义词：voluntary enlistment|词性:名词

dragooning:/drəˈɡuːnɪŋ/
|n.|强迫征兵；胁迫|The act of coercing someone into doing something, especially joining the military.|The dragooning of local farmers into the rebel army caused widespread resentment.  强迫当地农民加入叛军，引起了广泛的怨恨。|近义词：coercion, compulsion, enforcement|反义词：persuasion, volunteering|词性:名词

dragoon:/drəˈɡuːn/
|n.|龙骑兵；|A heavily armed cavalryman, originally one carrying a firearm called a dragon.|The dragoons charged across the battlefield. 龙骑兵冲过战场。|近义词：cavalryman, trooper, soldier|反义词：civilian|词性:名词
dragoon:/drəˈɡuːn/
|vt.|强迫；胁迫|To coerce or compel someone into doing something.|He was dragooned into helping with the move. 他被迫帮忙搬家。|近义词：coerce, compel, force, press, oblige|反义词：persuade, encourage|词性:动词
