brethren:/ˈbreðrən/| n.|兄弟们；同胞；教友|A group of male members of a religious group, or a group of people sharing a common interest or profession.  Used chiefly in religious or formal contexts.|The brethren gathered for their annual meeting.  教友们聚集在一起举行年会。|synonyms: brothers, comrades, fellows, colleagues|antonyms: adversaries, enemies|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *brōðru, plural of brōðor (“brother”), from Proto-Germanic *brōþēr, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰréh₂tēr. Cognate with Old Saxon brōðar, Old Frisian brother, Old High German bruodar (German Bruder), Old Norse bróðir, Gothic 𐌱𐍂𐍉𐌸𐌰𐍂 (brōþar), Latin frāter, Old Irish bráthair, Sanskrit भ्रातृ (bhrātṛ).  The archaic plural ending "-en" is similar to that found in "oxen" and "children."
