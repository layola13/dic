bulbs:/bʌlbz/
n.
灯泡；球茎；球状物
a rounded underground storage organ present in some plants, notably those of the lily family, consisting of a short stem surrounded by fleshy scale leaves or leaf bases modified for food storage.  Also, the glass part of an electric light containing a wire filament that glows when heated.
I need to buy some new light bulbs. 我需要买一些新的灯泡。
The daffodil bulbs need to be planted in the fall. 水仙花的球茎需要在秋天种植。

近义词: lamps, tubers, corms
反义词: (No direct antonym for a physical object like a bulb)
词性: noun (plural)


bulbs:/bʌlbz/
n.
灯泡；球茎；球状物
The rounded glass part of an electric light. Also, a plant structure like an onion.
Please change the light bulbs in the hallway. 请更换走廊里的灯泡。
The tulip bulbs are starting to sprout. 郁金香的球茎开始发芽了。
近义词: globes (for light bulbs), onions, tubers
反义词: (No direct antonym)
词性: noun (plural)


词源：From Old English *bulbe, from Proto-Germanic *bulbon, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *bhel- (to swell, bloom).
