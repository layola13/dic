inpaint:/ɪnˈpeɪnt/ | v. | 修补，（尤指）修复（绘画作品的）损坏部分 | to restore (a damaged painting or photograph) by repainting or reprinting a missing area | The museum curator carefully inpainted the damaged fresco.  博物馆馆长小心翼翼地修复了受损的壁画。| synonyms: restore, retouch, mend, repair | antonyms: damage, deface, impair | verb

inpaint:/ɪnˈpeɪnt/ | n. | 修补，修复（尤指对绘画作品）| the act of restoring a damaged painting or photograph by repainting or reprinting a missing area |  The inpaint was so seamless that it was impossible to tell where the damage had been. 修复得天衣无缝，根本看不出哪里曾经受损。| synonyms: restoration, retouching, mending, repair | antonyms: damage, defacement, impairment | noun

Etymology:  "in-" (in, into) + "paint" (to apply paint).  The term emerged with digital image processing, referring to algorithmically filling in missing or damaged parts of an image.  It later extended to traditional restoration techniques.
