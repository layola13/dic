ghast:/ɡæst/ | adj. n. | 惊恐的；恐怖的；(古)鬼，幽灵 | Filled with horror; terrified.  A ghost or specter (archaic). |  He had a ghast look on his face after seeing the accident.  事故发生后，他脸上露出惊恐的表情。 |The old castle was said to be haunted by ghasts.  据说那座古老的城堡里闹鬼。 | 近义词: ghostly, spectral, eerie, horrified, terrified | 反义词: calm, peaceful, unafraid | 词性: adjective, noun (archaic)


Etymology: From Middle English *gast, from Old English *gǣst (“ghost, spirit, demon”), from Proto-Germanic *gaistaz (“terror, supernatural being”). Cognate with Scots gaist (“ghost”), West Frisian geest (“ghost”), Dutch geest (“spirit, mind”), German Geist (“ghost, spirit, mind”), Icelandic geyst (“rage”).
