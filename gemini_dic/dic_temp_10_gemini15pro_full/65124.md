ethic: /ˈeθɪk/ (US) /ˈɛθɪk/ (UK) | n. | 道德，伦理；道德规范 | A system of moral principles that govern a person's behavior or the conducting of an activity. |  例句：Medical ethics prohibit doctors from taking bribes.  医学伦理禁止医生受贿。

例句：He has strong work ethics. 他有很强的工作道德。

近义词：moral, principle, value

反义词：immorality

词性: noun


ethic: /ˈeθɪk/ (US) /ˈɛθɪk/ (UK) | adj. | 伦理的，道德的 | Relating to moral principles or the branch of knowledge dealing with these. |  例句：It’s an ethic issue that deserves careful consideration. 这是一个值得仔细考虑的伦理问题。

例句：The ethic dimensions of the problem should not be ignored.  这个问题的伦理层面不应该被忽视。

近义词：moral

反义词：unethical, immoral

词性: adjective (less common)


USAGE->

词源: From Middle French ethique, from Late Latin ethica, from Greek ēthikē (tekhnē) '(the science of) morals,' feminine of ēthikos 'moral,' from ēthos 'custom, character.'
