diglossia: /daɪˈɡlɒsiə/ /daɪˈɡlɑːsiə/
n.
双语现象，双言制
A situation in which two distinct varieties of a language are used in different social contexts, one a "high" variety (H) used for formal situations and the other a "low" variety (L) used for everyday communication.
In many Arabic-speaking countries, diglossia exists between Modern Standard Arabic (MSA) and the local colloquial dialects. 在许多阿拉伯国家，现代标准阿拉伯语 (MSA) 和当地方言之间存在双语现象。
Diglossia is a common phenomenon in many societies around the world. 双语现象在世界许多社会中都很普遍。
synonyms: bilingualism, multilingualism
antonyms: monolingualism
noun
