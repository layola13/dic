bolshevism:/ˈbɒlʃɪvɪzəm/ /ˈboʊlʃəvɪzəm/| n.|布尔什维主义|A revolutionary Marxist political theory and practice, advocating violent overthrow of capitalism and establishment of a dictatorship of the proletariat, as implemented by the Bolsheviks in Russia.|The rise of bolshevism in Russia led to the creation of the Soviet Union.|近义词：communism, Marxism-Leninism|反义词：capitalism, liberalism|词性:noun

bolshevik:/ˈbɒlʃɪvɪk/ /ˈboʊlʃəvɪk/| n. adj.|布尔什维克，布尔什维克的|A member of the majority faction of the Russian Social Democratic Party, which seized power in the October Revolution of 1917.|Lenin was a key bolshevik leader.|近义词：communist, revolutionary|反义词：Menshevik, capitalist|词性:noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Russian большевик (bolʹševik, “member of the majority”), from большинство (bolʹšinstvo, “majority”), from больший (bolʹšij, “bigger”).  The Bolsheviks, led by Lenin, were originally the majority faction in the Russian Social Democratic Labour Party.
