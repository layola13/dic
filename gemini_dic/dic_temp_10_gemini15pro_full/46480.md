daimyor:/ˌdaɪˈmaɪər/ | n. | 大名 (daimyo) | A daimyo (大名, daimyō, literally "great name") was a powerful Japanese feudal lord who ruled most of Japan from their vast, hereditary land holdings. They were subordinate to the shogun and nominally to the emperor and the kuge. |  The daimyo controlled vast territories and commanded armies of samurai. | 近义词: feudal lord, magnate | 反义词: peasant, commoner | 词性: noun

词源: From Japanese 大名 (daimyō), from 大 (dai, “large, great”) + 名 (myō, “name”).
