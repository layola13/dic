chiasm: /ˈkaɪæzəm/ | n. | 交叉，交叉形式，(修辞)交错配列法 | An arrangement of two corresponding pairs of words in which the second pair is reversed, creating a pattern like ABBA.  A literary or rhetorical device in which words, grammatical constructions, or concepts are repeated in reverse order, in the same or a modified form. |  Example 1: "Ask not what your country can do for you; ask what you can do for your country" is a famous example of chiasm.  示例1：“不要问你的国家能为你做什么，问问你能为你的国家做什么”是著名的交错配列法的例子。

Example 2: The poem used chiasm to create a sense of balance and symmetry. 示例2：这首诗使用交错配列法来创造平衡和对称感。 |  Near synonyms: antimetabole, inversion | Antonyms: N/A | noun
