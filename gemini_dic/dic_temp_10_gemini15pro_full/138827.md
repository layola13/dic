nundinary: /ˈnʌndɪnɛri/ | adj. | 市场的；市集的；交易的；八日一次集市的 | Relating to a market or market day, especially one held every ninth day (counting inclusively) in ancient Rome. | The nundinary cycle influenced Roman social and political life.  八日一次集市的周期影响了罗马的社会和政治生活。| The farmer brought his produce to the nundinary market. 农民把他的产品带到八日集市。 |  Synonyms: market, commercial | Antonyms: noncommercial, private | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *nundinarius*, from *nundinae* "market day, the ninth day", from *novem* "nine" + *dies* "day". Originally, Roman market days were held every eight days, counting inclusively, so the ninth day from one market day would be the next market day.
