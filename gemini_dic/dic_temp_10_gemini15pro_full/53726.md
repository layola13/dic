discodactyl:/ˌdɪskoʊˈdæktɪl/| adj.|盘状指的，吸盘指的|Having disc-shaped adhesive pads on the fingers and toes, typically for climbing.  This term is primarily used in zoology to describe certain amphibians, particularly tree frogs.|The White's tree frog is discodactyl, enabling it to climb smooth surfaces. 白氏树蛙有吸盘指，使其能够攀爬光滑的表面。|Synonym: adhesive-toed, suction-toed|Antonym: non-adhesive, claw-toed|adjective

discodactylous:/ˌdɪskoʊˈdæktɪləs/| adj.|盘状指的，吸盘指的|Having disc-shaped adhesive pads on the fingers and toes, typically for climbing.  This term, similar to discodactyl, is also primarily used in zoology to describe certain amphibians, particularly tree frogs.|Many species of gecko are discodactylous. 许多种类的壁虎都有吸盘指。|Synonym: adhesive-toed, suction-toed|Antonym: non-adhesive, claw-toed|adjective
