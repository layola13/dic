advisor:/ədˈvaɪzər/ (US) /ədˈvaɪzə(r)/ (UK)| n.|顾问，指导者|Someone who gives advice, especially in a particular area of expertise.|My financial advisor helped me plan for retirement. 我的财务顾问帮我规划退休计划。

She is an advisor to the president. 她是总统的顾问。|近义词：consultant, counselor, mentor, guide|反义词: advisee, mentee|词性:noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
My advisor meets with me weekly. = 我的顾问每周都和我见面。
He works as a financial advisor. = 他是一名财务顾问。


一般过去时:
I spoke to my advisor yesterday about my career goals. = 我昨天和我的顾问谈了我的职业目标。
The advisor recommended a different investment strategy. = 顾问推荐了不同的投资策略。


现在完成时:
The advisor has given me some valuable advice. = 顾问给了我一些宝贵的建议。
She has been an advisor to the company for five years. = 她担任公司顾问已有五年了。


过去完成时:
The advisor had already left before I arrived. = 在我到达之前，顾问已经离开了。
I had spoken to my advisor several times before making a decision. = 在做决定之前，我和我的顾问谈过几次。


将来时:
I will meet with my advisor next week. = 我下周要和我的顾问见面。
The advisor will review my proposal tomorrow. = 顾问明天将审核我的提案。


虚拟语气:
If I had an advisor, I would ask for their opinion. = 如果我有一个顾问，我会征求他们的意见。
I wish I had an advisor to help me with this problem. = 我希望我有一个顾问来帮助我解决这个问题。


被动语态:
The students were advised to apply early. = 学生们被建议尽早申请。
The company is advised by a team of experts. = 该公司由一个专家团队提供咨询。


疑问句:
Who is your advisor? = 谁是你的顾问？
What advice did your advisor give you? = 你的顾问给了你什么建议？


否定句:
I don't have an advisor. = 我没有顾问。
She isn't an advisor for that company. = 她不是那家公司的顾问。
