frieze:/friːz/| n. |雕带，横饰带，带状装饰|A decorative band, usually horizontal, along a wall near the ceiling, or around the top of a building or piece of furniture.|The temple frieze depicts scenes from mythology.|近义词：band, border, decoration, ornament|反义词:plainness|noun

friezes:/friːziz/| n. |雕带，横饰带，带状装饰（复数）|Plural form of "frieze," referring to multiple decorative bands.|The friezes in the palace were covered in gold leaf.|近义词：bands, borders, decorations, ornaments|反义词:plainness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "frise" (curly or napped cloth), from Medieval Latin "frisium" (type of cloth), of Germanic origin.  Related to Old High German "friso" (curly). Originally referring to a type of cloth with a nap, the term later came to describe architectural ornaments resembling such cloth.
