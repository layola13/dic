nihilist: /ˈnaɪɪlɪst/ (US), /ˈnɪɪlɪst/ (UK)| n. |虚无主义者|A person who believes that life is meaningless and rejects all religious and moral principles.|例句：He became a nihilist after witnessing the horrors of war.  他目睹了战争的恐怖后变成了一个虚无主义者。

例句：The nihilist argued that all values are baseless and nothing is truly knowable.  这个虚无主义者认为所有的价值观都是没有根据的，没有什么东西是真正可知的。

|近义词：pessimist, cynic, skeptic|反义词:optimist, believer|词性:noun


nihilists: /ˈnaɪɪlɪsts/ (US), /ˈnɪɪlɪsts/ (UK)| n. |虚无主义者（复数）|Plural form of "nihilist," referring to multiple people who believe that life is meaningless.|例句：The nihilists rejected traditional morality and embraced chaos.  这些虚无主义者摒弃了传统道德，拥抱了混乱。

例句：Many nihilists find solace in art and creativity. 许多虚无主义者在艺术和创造中找到慰藉。

|近义词：pessimists, cynics, skeptics|反义词:optimists, believers|词性:noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From the Latin word "nihil" meaning "nothing."  It entered English in the mid-19th century.
