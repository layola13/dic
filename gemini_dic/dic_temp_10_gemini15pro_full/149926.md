palliard:/ˈpæliərd/| n.| 流氓，恶棍，懒汉|A person, typically a man, who is immoral, lazy, and avoids honest work. | He was known as a palliard and a scoundrel in the town. 他在这个小镇上被称为流氓和恶棍。|Synonyms: rogue, scoundrel, vagabond, rascal, good-for-nothing|Antonyms: gentleman, upright citizen|Noun


Etymology:
From Middle French *paillard* "lecher, fornicator," from Old French *paille* "straw" (from Latin *palea*), perhaps referring to straw mattresses used by vagrants or prostitutes, or from the notion of "threshing straw," implying idleness.
