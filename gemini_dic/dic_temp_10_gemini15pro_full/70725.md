finches:/ˈfɪntʃɪz/| n.|雀类；燕雀科鸣禽|Small, seed-eating songbirds with short, stout beaks.  These birds are common throughout the world.|例句：The finches were chirping merrily in the trees.  树上的雀鸟欢快地鸣叫着。

例句：Darwin studied the beaks of finches in the Galapagos Islands.  达尔文在加拉帕戈斯群岛研究了雀类的喙。|近义词：birds, songbirds|反义词: (No direct antonym for a type of bird)|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *finc, from Proto-Germanic *finkiz, from Proto-Indo-European *sping-.  Cognate with German Fink, Dutch vink.
