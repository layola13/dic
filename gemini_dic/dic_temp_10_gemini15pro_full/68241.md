fairyhood:/ˈfeərihʊd/| n.|仙女身份，仙境|The state or quality of being a fairy; the realm or world of fairies.|She longed for the magic and wonder of fairyhood. 她渴望仙境的神奇和美妙。|近义词：fairyland, enchantment|反义词：reality, mundane|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From fairy + -hood.  "Fairy" comes from Old French faerie (belief in fairies, magic, enchantment), from faie (fairy), from Vulgar Latin *fata, from Latin fatum (fate, destiny). "-hood" is an Old English suffix denoting a state, condition, or quality of being.
