hyperopia:/ˌhaɪpərˈoʊpiə/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈrəʊpiə/ (UK)| n. |远视|A condition in which visual images come to a focus behind the retina of the eye and vision is better for distant than for near objects.|She was diagnosed with hyperopia at a young age. 她很小的时候就被诊断出患有远视。|My grandmother needs glasses for her hyperopia. 我的祖母因为远视需要戴眼镜。|近义词:farsightedness, long-sightedness|反义词:myopia, nearsightedness, short-sightedness|词性:noun

Etymology: From Ancient Greek ὑπέρ (hupér, “over, above”) + ὤψ (ōps, “eye, face”) + -ia.
