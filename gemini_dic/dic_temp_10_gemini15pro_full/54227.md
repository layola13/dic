disfranchisement:/ˌdɪsˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
n.
剥夺公民权；剥夺选举权
The state of being deprived of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.
The disfranchisement of black voters was a key tactic of the Jim Crow South.  南方各州实行种族隔离政策的重要策略之一就是剥夺黑人选民的选举权。

Many suffragettes went to prison fighting for the disfranchisement of women to end. 许多妇女参政论者入狱是为了争取结束剥夺妇女选举权的状况。

disenfranchisement:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
n.
剥夺公民权；剥夺选举权
The act of depriving someone of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.
The new law led to the disenfranchisement of thousands of eligible voters. 新法律导致成千上万的合格选民被剥夺了选举权。

The group protested the disfranchisement of people with criminal records.  该团体抗议剥夺有犯罪记录者的公民权。


Synonym:
deprivation, disqualification
Antonym:
enfranchisement, empowerment
Etymology:
From dis- (expressing negation) + franchise (right or privilege) + -ment (forming nouns of action or process)
