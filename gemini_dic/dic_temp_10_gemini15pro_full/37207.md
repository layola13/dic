comet:/ˈkɒmɪt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːmɪt/ (US)| n.|彗星|A celestial body consisting of a nucleus of ice and dust and, when near the sun, a “tail” of gas and dust particles pointing away from the sun.|Halley's Comet is visible from Earth every 76 years. 哈雷彗星每76年从地球上可见。|近义词:meteoroid, asteroid|反义词:N/A|词性:名词

cometary:/ˈkɒmɪtəri/ (UK), /ˈkɑːməteri/ (US)| adj.|彗星的|Relating to or resembling a comet.|Cometary dust can cause meteor showers. 彗星尘埃会导致流星雨。|近义词:N/A|反义词:N/A|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French comete, from Latin cometes, from Greek komētēs (long-haired), from komē (hair).
