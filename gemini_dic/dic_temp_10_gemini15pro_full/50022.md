denominationalism:/dɪˌnɑːmɪˈneɪʃənəlɪzəm/
n.
宗派主义，教派主义
The condition of being divided into different denominations or sects, especially within Christianity; adherence to a particular denomination.
The rise of denominationalism in the 19th century led to the establishment of numerous independent churches. 19世纪宗派主义的兴起导致了许多独立教会的建立。
Competition between different denominations for converts can sometimes lead to conflict. 不同教派之间争夺皈依者的竞争有时会导致冲突。

sectarianism, factionalism
non-denominationalism, ecumenism
noun
