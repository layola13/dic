asters: /ˈæstərz/ | n. | 紫菀；翠菊 | Asters are daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and fall.  They come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, white, and blue. | The asters in her garden were a vibrant shade of purple. 她花园里的紫菀是鲜艳的紫色。|  The hillside was covered in a blanket of wild asters. 山坡上覆盖着一层野生的紫菀。| Synonyms: daisy, michaelmas daisy | Antonyms: N/A | Noun

Etymology: From the Latin word "aster," meaning "star," referring to the star-like shape of the flower.  Ultimately from the Greek word "astēr," also meaning "star."
