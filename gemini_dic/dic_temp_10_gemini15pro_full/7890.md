antefix:/ˈæntiˌfɪks/ | n. |  垂饰，檐口饰 | An ornamental block fixed to the edge of a roof, especially along the eaves, often carved with a design. |  The antefixes on the temple were decorated with intricate floral patterns.  寺庙的檐口饰雕刻着复杂的植物图案。 | Synonyms:  acroterion, ornament, decoration | Antonyms:  plainness, simplicity | noun

antefix:/ˈæntiˌfɪks/ | n. |  (古代)屋檐上的饰件 |  An upright ornament, often of terra cotta, placed along the edge of a roof in classical architecture. | The archaeologists unearthed several antefixes depicting mythical creatures. 考古学家挖掘出几件描绘神话生物的屋檐饰件。 | Synonyms:  acroter, eave ornament  | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, consider using terms like "bare roof edge" or "unadorned eaves") | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *antefixus*, past participle of *antefigere* ("to fasten before"), from *ante-* ("before") + *figere* ("to fasten, fix").
