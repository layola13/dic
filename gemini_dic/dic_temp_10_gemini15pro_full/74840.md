frenzy:/ˈfrɛnzi/ | n. | 狂暴，疯狂，狂热 | A state of uncontrolled excitement or wild behavior. |
The shoppers were in a frenzy on Black Friday. 顾客们在黑色星期五陷入了疯狂抢购。
He was in such a frenzy that he couldn't think straight. 他如此疯狂以至于无法清晰地思考。

|近义词：mania, hysteria, delirium, rage, fury |
|反义词：calm, composure, tranquility, peace |
|词性：noun |

frenzy:/ˈfrɛnzi/ | vt. | 使狂怒，使狂热 | To drive into a frenzy; to make frantic. |
The loud music frenzied the crowd. 喧闹的音乐使人群变得狂热。
The scent of blood frenzied the sharks. 血腥味使鲨鱼疯狂。

|近义词：enrage, infuriate, madden |
|反义词：calm, soothe, pacify |
|词性：verb |


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *phrenēsis, from Greek phrénēsis "inflammation of the brain," from phren "mind, diaphragm (as the seat of emotions)."
