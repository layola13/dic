barag: /ˈbæræɡ/ | n. |  争吵，争执；喧闹，骚动 | A noisy quarrel or fight; a commotion or uproar. |  The barag in the marketplace made it difficult to hear the town crier.  市场上的喧闹声使人很难听到市镇传令员的声音。| Synonyms: brawl, fracas, melee, rumpus, tumult | Antonyms: peace, quiet, tranquility | noun

Etymology:  Possibly from a Scandinavian source, related to Old Norse *barakka "to fight," or from a Middle Low German source. The word is now considered archaic or dialectal.
