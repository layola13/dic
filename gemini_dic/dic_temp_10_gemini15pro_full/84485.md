halting:/ˈhɔːltɪŋ/ (US), /ˈhɔːltɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|犹豫的，迟疑不决的，断断续续的|Stopping and starting often, not continuous or fluent.|Her speech was halting and uncertain due to her nervousness. 由于紧张，她的讲话断断续续，很不确定。|例句：His halting steps showed his uncertainty. 他犹豫的步伐显示出他的不确定。|近义词：hesitant, faltering, unsteady, hesitant, wavering|反义词：fluent, smooth, continuous, decisive, confident|词性:形容词

halting:/ˈhɔːltɪŋ/ (US), /ˈhɔːltɪŋ/ (UK)| n.|停止，暂停|The act of stopping or coming to a standstill.|The halting of the production line caused significant delays.  生产线的停工造成了严重的延误。|例句：The sudden halting of the train startled the passengers. 火车突然停止，吓了乘客一跳。|近义词：cessation, stop, pause, interruption|反义词：continuation, progression, advance|词性:名词

词源:  From Middle English *halten, from Old French haleter (“to pant, breathe heavily”), from Frankish *haltōn, of uncertain origin.  Related to Old High German halzōn (“to limp, hobble”).  The sense development is perhaps from the labored breathing of someone who is exhausted or injured.
