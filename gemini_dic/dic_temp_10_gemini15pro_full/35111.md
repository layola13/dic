cloud:/klaʊd/| n. v. |云，云状物；阴云；阴影；一大群|A visible mass of condensed water vapor floating in the atmosphere, typically high above the ground.  To make something unclear or less bright; to become gloomy or overcast.|The clouds drifted lazily across the sky. 云朵懒洋洋地飘过天空。|近义词：mist, haze, fog, nebula|反义词：sunshine, clarity, clearness|词性:名词,动词

cloud:/klaʊd/| v. |使模糊；使黯然；使蒙上阴影|To make something unclear or less bright; to become gloomy or overcast.|His face clouded over with anger. 他的脸上蒙上了一层怒气。|近义词：obscure, darken, dim|反义词：brighten, clarify, illuminate|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *clud, akin to clod.  The original meaning referred to a mass of rock or earth, later extended to masses of water vapor in the sky.
