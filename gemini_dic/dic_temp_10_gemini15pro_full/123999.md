morningstar:/ˈmɔːrnɪŋ stɑːr/ (US) /ˈmɔːnɪŋ stɑː(r)/ (UK)| n.| 启明星；金星（清晨出现时）；晨星|A bright planet, especially Venus, seen in the east before sunrise.|例句：The morningstar shone brightly in the pre-dawn sky. 启明星在黎明前的空中闪耀。|近义词：Venus, Phosphorus, Lucifer|反义词: evening star|noun

morningstar:/ˈmɔːrnɪŋ stɑːr/ (US) /ˈmɔːnɪŋ stɑː(r)/ (UK)| n.|  狼牙棒；钉头锤|A medieval club-like weapon with a spiked metal ball at the end of a chain or shaft.|例句：The knight wielded his morningstar with deadly force. 骑士挥舞着他的狼牙棒，威力无比。|近义词：mace, flail|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps shield or armor as defensive countermeasures)|noun
