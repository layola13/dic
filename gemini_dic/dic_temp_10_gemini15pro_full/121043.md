misconducting:/ˌmɪskənˈdʌktɪŋ/
adjective
行为不端的，管理不善的
Failing to conduct oneself properly or professionally, especially involving dishonesty or illegal behavior; managing something badly.
The company's CEO was accused of misconducting himself and misusing company funds.  该公司首席执行官被指控行为不端和滥用公司资金。
The investigation revealed that the police officer was misconducting himself while on duty. 调查显示，这名警官在值班期间行为不端。
近义词: wrongdoing, misbehaving, malfeasant, corrupt
反义词: ethical, honest, proper, lawful
词性: adjective

misconduct: /ˌmɪsˈkɒndʌkt/
noun
不端行为，失职
Unacceptable or improper behavior, especially by an employee or professional person.
The lawyer was accused of professional misconduct.  该律师被指控职业失当。
His misconduct led to his dismissal from the company. 他的不端行为导致他被公司解雇。
近义词: wrongdoing, misbehavior, malpractice
反义词: good conduct, propriety
词性: noun

misconduct: /ˌmɪsˈkɒndʌkt/
verb
行为不端，管理不善
To manage or conduct (oneself or one's affairs) badly or improperly.
He misconducted the company's finances, leading to significant losses. 他对公司财务管理不善，导致重大损失。
The teacher was accused of misconducting the exam by giving students unfair advantages.  该教师被指控在考试中行为不端，给予学生不公平的优势。
近义词: mismanage, misbehave
反义词: manage well, behave properly
词性: verb


词源:  From mis- + conduct.  "Mis-" is a prefix of Latin origin meaning "wrong" or "bad".  "Conduct" comes from the Latin "conductus", meaning "leading, guidance".
