anti-semitism:/ˌæntiˈsɛmɪtɪzəm/
|n.|反犹太主义|Hostility to or prejudice against Jewish people.|The politician's comments were condemned as blatant anti-Semitism.|近义词：racism, bigotry, xenophobia, discrimination|反义词：tolerance, acceptance|词性:名词

anti-semitisms:/ˌæntiˈsɛmɪtɪzəmz/
|n.|多种形式的反犹太主义|Various forms of hostility to or prejudice against Jewish people. This plural form highlights the diverse manifestations of anti-Jewish sentiment.|The report documented several anti-semitisms, including hate speech, vandalism, and physical assault.|近义词：bigotries, prejudices, discriminations|反义词：tolerances, acceptances|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From *anti-* (against) + *Semitism*, coined in 1879 by German agitator Wilhelm Marr to designate modern hostility toward Jews as a race rather than as a religious group.  *Semitism* comes ultimately from the Hebrew name *Shem* (שם), one of the sons of Noah.
