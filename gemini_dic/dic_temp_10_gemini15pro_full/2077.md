ad-lib:/ædˈlɪb/ | adv. adj. n. v. | 即兴的，即兴表演，即兴创作，即兴表演 | To improvise or perform something spontaneously, without preparation. | The comedian ad-libbed a joke about the current events.  这位喜剧演员即兴讲了一个关于时事的笑话。

The actor forgot his lines and had to ad-lib the rest of the scene.  这位演员忘记了台词，不得不即兴表演完剩下的场景。 | 近义词: improvise, extemporize | 反义词: rehearse, pre-plan | 词性: adverb, adjective, noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *ad libitum*  "at pleasure," from *ad* "to, at" + *libitum*, noun use of the neuter past participle of *libere* "to please."  Shortened in English to *ad lib*.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often ad-libs during his stand-up routine. = 他在单口相声表演中经常即兴发挥。
She ad-libs when she forgets her lines. = 她忘记台词时会即兴发挥。

一般过去时:
The musician ad-libbed a solo during the concert. = 音乐家在音乐会上即兴演奏了一段独奏。
He ad-libbed a response to the unexpected question. = 他对这个突如其来的问题即兴作答。

现在完成时:
The actors have ad-libbed several scenes in the play. = 演员们在这部戏中即兴表演了几个场景。
She has ad-libbed throughout her career. = 她在整个职业生涯中都在即兴表演。

过去完成时:
The comedian had ad-libbed a few jokes before the show started. = 喜剧演员在演出开始前即兴讲了几个笑话。
They had ad-libbed the entire scene because they lost the script. = 因为丢失了剧本，他们不得不即兴表演了整个场景。

将来时:

He will ad-lib a song for the encore. = 他将在返场时即兴演唱一首歌。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

Many of the lines were ad-libbed by the actors. = 许多台词都是由演员即兴创作的。

疑问句:

否定句:

She doesn't usually ad-lib during her performances. = 她通常在表演中不会即兴发挥。
