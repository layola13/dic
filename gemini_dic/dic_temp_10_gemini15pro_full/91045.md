hussar:/həˈzɑːr/
hussar:|n.|轻骑兵|A member of a class of light cavalry, originally from Hungary and Serbia, noted for their distinctive uniforms, often including a short, braided jacket called a dolman. They were prevalent in European armies from the 17th to the 20th centuries.|The dashing hussars charged across the plain. 轻骑兵冲过平原。|Synonyms: cavalryman, trooper, horseman|Antonyms: infantryman|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Hungarian *huszár (“hussar”), from Serbian *хусар, from Medieval Greek χοῦσαρος (choúsaros), of uncertain origin, possibly from Late Latin cursus (“course, running”).  Borrowed into English in the mid 16th century.
