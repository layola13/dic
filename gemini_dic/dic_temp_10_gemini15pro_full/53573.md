disbench:/dɪsˈbɛntʃ/ | n. | 取消资格，撤职，使停职；逐出教会 |  The act of removing someone from a position of authority or privilege, especially in a church or other organization. | The bishop disbenched the priest for misconduct. 主教因牧师行为不端而将其撤职。 | 近义词：dismiss, remove, oust, dethrone, defrock, unseat | 反义词：appoint, install, reinstate, elect | 词性: noun


disbench:/dɪsˈbɛntʃ/ | vt. | 取消…的资格，撤…的职，使…停职；逐出教会 | To remove someone from a position of authority or privilege, especially in a church or other organization. | The committee disbenched the chairman after the scandal. 丑闻之后，委员会撤销了主席的职务。 | 近义词：dismiss, remove, oust, dethrone, defrock, unseat | 反义词：appoint, install, reinstate, elect | 词性: verb


词源:  "dis-" (prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of") + "bench" (noun referring to a seat of authority, especially for judges or bishops).  The word originally referred to removing someone from a seat of authority.
