distaste:/dɪsˈteɪst/ | n. | 厌恶，反感 | A feeling of aversion or dislike. |  He looked at the food with distaste. 他厌恶地看着食物。

She expressed her distaste for his behavior. 她表达了对他行为的反感。 | 近义词: aversion, dislike, repugnance, revulsion | 反义词: liking, fondness, relish, appetite | 词性: 名词

distasteful:/dɪsˈteɪstfʊl/ | adj. | 令人不快的，讨厌的 | Unpleasant and offensive. | The idea of eating insects is distasteful to many people. 吃昆虫的想法让很多人感到厌恶。

He found the violence in the movie distasteful. 他觉得电影中的暴力令人反感。 | 近义词: unpleasant, offensive, disagreeable, objectionable | 反义词: pleasant, agreeable, appealing, attractive | 词性: 形容词


词源:  Late Middle English: from dis- (expressing negation) + taste.
