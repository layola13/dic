abjure:/əbˈdʒʊr/ /əˈbʒʊr/| vt.|正式放弃，公开宣布放弃|To abjure one's beliefs is to renounce them publicly. 公开宣布放弃自己的信仰。|He abjured his former allegiances. 他宣布放弃他以前的效忠对象。|近义词：renounce, relinquish, forsake, forswear|反义词：affirm, embrace, uphold, maintain|verb

abjure:/əbˈdʒʊr/ /əˈbʒʊr/| vt.|发誓放弃|She abjured all earthly pleasures. 她发誓放弃所有世俗的享乐。|He abjured his religion. 他放弃了他的宗教信仰。|近义词： renounce, forswear|反义词： embrace|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *abjuren*, from Anglo-Norman *abjurer*, from Old French *abjurer*, from Latin *abiūrāre* (“to deny on oath, forswear, renounce”), from *ab* (“from, away”) + *iūrāre* (“to swear”), from *iūs* (“law, right”).

USAGE->
Present Tense:
He abjures violence. = 他宣布放弃暴力。
She abjures her former lifestyle. = 她宣布放弃她以前的生活方式。

Past Tense:
He abjured his title. = 他放弃了他的头衔。
They abjured their allegiance to the king. = 他们宣布放弃对国王的效忠。

Present Perfect Tense:
He has abjured his claim to the throne. = 他已经放弃了对王位的要求。
She has abjured her former beliefs. = 她已经放弃了她以前的信仰。

Past Perfect Tense:
He had abjured his faith before joining the new sect. = 在加入新教派之前，他已经放弃了他的信仰。
She had abjured her citizenship before moving to another country. = 在移居到另一个国家之前，她已经放弃了她的公民身份。

Future Tense:
He will abjure his right to an attorney. = 他将放弃他聘请律师的权利。
She will abjure her inheritance. = 她将放弃她的继承权。

Subjunctive Mood:
If he were to abjure his oath, he would be punished. = 如果他违背誓言，他将受到惩罚。
I wish he would abjure his bad habits. = 我希望他能改掉他的坏习惯。

Passive Voice:
His former beliefs were abjured by him. = 他以前的信仰被他放弃了。
The treaty was abjured by the government. = 该条约已被政府废除。

Interrogative:
Why did he abjure his religion? = 他为什么放弃他的宗教信仰？
Will she abjure her rights? = 她会放弃她的权利吗？

Negative:
He did not abjure his responsibilities. = 他没有放弃他的责任。
She will not abjure her principles. = 她不会放弃她的原则。


PHRASE->
abjure a claim = 放弃索赔
abjure a right = 放弃权利
abjure a belief = 放弃信仰
abjure an oath = 违背誓言
abjure a lifestyle = 放弃一种生活方式
