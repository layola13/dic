gipsy: /ˈdʒɪpsi/ (UK) /ˈɡɪpsi/ (US)| n. |吉普赛人；流浪者；骗子|A member of a traveling people traditionally believed to have come from India, now living mostly in Europe and North America and noted for their brightly colored clothes and caravans and for their skill as fortune tellers and musicians. (Often considered offensive).|例句：The gipsy told her fortune. 吉普赛人给她算命。

例句：They lived a gipsy life, travelling from place to place. 他们过着吉普赛式的生活，四处漂泊。|近义词：Roma, Romani, traveler, nomad|反义词：settled person|词性：名词


gipsy: /ˈdʒɪpsi/ (UK) /ˈɡɪpsi/ (US)| adj. |吉普赛式的；流浪的|Resembling or characteristic of Gypsies, especially in being nomadic or colorful.|例句：She had a gipsy soul, always yearning to travel. 她有一颗流浪的心，总是渴望旅行。

例句：The caravan had a gipsy-like appearance. 这辆大篷车看起来像吉普赛人的。|近义词：nomadic, wandering, itinerant|反义词：settled, stationary|词性：形容词
