mooring:/ˈmʊərɪŋ/ (US), /ˈmɔːrɪŋ/ (UK)| n.| 停泊处；系泊设备；系泊|A place where a boat or ship is moored.  The ropes, chains, anchors, etc. used to moor a boat or ship.|The yacht was secured to its mooring.  游艇被固定在它的泊位上。|Synonyms: anchorage, berth, dock|Antonyms: unmooring, casting off|noun

mooring:/ˈmʊərɪŋ/ (US), /ˈmɔːrɪŋ/ (UK)| n.| 停泊；系泊（动名词）|The act of mooring a vessel.|The mooring of the ship took longer than expected. 船的停泊比预期的要长。|Synonyms: anchoring, berthing, docking|Antonyms: unmooring, casting off|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *morynge, from the verb *moren (“to moor”), from Old English *mōrian (“to moor”), of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old Norse *mæra (“to mark out, define limits”), from Proto-Germanic *mērōną (“to provide with a boundary, limit, measure”).
