anachronistically: /əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪkli/ | adv. | 过时地，时代错误地 | In a way that is chronologically misplaced. Placing something in the wrong historical time period. | The film anachronistically features a character using a cell phone in ancient Rome. 这部电影时代错误地描绘了一个在古罗马使用手机的角色。|  The author anachronistically placed a computer in Victorian England. 作者时代错误地将一台电脑置于维多利亚时代的英格兰。| 近义词:  incorrectly, inappropriately, unseasonably | 反义词:  chronologically, appropriately, correctly | 词性: 副词

anachronism: /əˈnækrənɪzəm/ | n. | 时代错误，时代错乱 | Something (such as a word, an object, or an event) that is mistakenly placed in a time where it does not belong in a story, movie, etc.  A thing belonging or appropriate to a period other than that in which it exists, especially a thing that is conspicuously old-fashioned. | The inclusion of a telephone in a film about ancient Egypt is a glaring anachronism. 在一部关于古埃及的电影中加入电话是一个明显的时代错误。 |  His quaint clothes were an anachronism in the modern city. 他古朴的服装在现代都市里显得格格不入。| 近义词:  misplacement, incongruity, solecism | 反义词:  chronological accuracy | 词性: 名词

anachronistic: /əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪk/ | adj. | 时代错误的，过时的 | Belonging to a period other than that being portrayed. |  It would be anachronistic to depict a medieval knight using a machine gun. 描绘一位中世纪骑士使用机关枪是时代错误的。 | The play's costumes were deliberately anachronistic. 这部戏的服装故意设计成时代错误的风格。| 近义词:  outdated, old-fashioned, antiquated | 反义词:  contemporary, modern, current | 词性: 形容词


词源: From the Greek *ανά* (aná, "against, back, up") + *χρόνος* (khrónos, "time") + *-ιστικός* (-istikós, adjectival suffix forming adjectives of characteristic or relation) + *-ly* (adverbial suffix).
