close-hauled:/ˌkloʊsˈhɔːld/| adj.|逆风航行；迎风行驶的|Sailing as close to the wind as possible.|The yacht was close-hauled, struggling against the strong headwind.  游艇逆风航行，与强劲的逆风搏斗。|close to the wind, beating|running, downwind|adjective
closehauled:/ˌkloʊsˈhɔːld/| adj.|迎风的；顶风的|Positioned or sailing close to the wind.|The captain ordered the crew to keep the ship closehauled. 船长命令船员保持船只顶风航行。|close to the wind, beating|running, downwind|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "close" + "hauled", where "hauled" refers to the sails being pulled in tight.  "Close" in this context means near to.  The term originates from nautical terminology, describing the positioning of a sailing vessel's sails in relation to the wind.
