diffracting:/dɪˈfræktɪŋ/ | adj. | 衍射的，绕射的 | Causing light or other waves to spread out as they pass through a narrow opening or across an edge. | The prism was diffracting the light into a rainbow of colors. 棱镜将光衍射成彩虹色。|  The diffracting grating separated the different wavelengths of light. 衍射光栅分离了不同波长的光。| synonyms: scattering, dispersing, spreading | antonyms: focusing, converging | adjective

diffract:/dɪˈfrækt/ | v. | 衍射，绕射 | (of light or other waves) to spread out as they pass through a narrow opening or across an edge. |  Light diffracts when it passes through a small slit. 光线通过狭缝时会发生衍射。 | The sound waves diffracted around the corner. 声波绕着拐角衍射。| synonyms: scatter, disperse, spread | antonyms: focus, converge | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *diffractus*, past participle of *diffringere* "to break apart," from *dis-* "apart" + *frangere* "to break."
