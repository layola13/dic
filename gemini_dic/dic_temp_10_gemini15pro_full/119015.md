methodistical:/ˌmɛθəˈdɪstɪkəl/| adj.|循规蹈矩的，墨守成规的；拘泥形式的|Marked by or relating to Methodism or its practices, especially in being excessively formal, rigid, or conventionally pious.  Also, generally, excessively systematic or rigid.|Her approach to gardening was almost methodistical, with every task performed according to a strict schedule.|近义词：rigid, inflexible, dogmatic, precise, meticulous|反义词：flexible, adaptable, pragmatic|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Methodist + -ical. Methodist itself comes from method + -ist. Method ultimately derives from Greek *méthodos* meaning “way of inquiry or teaching.”
