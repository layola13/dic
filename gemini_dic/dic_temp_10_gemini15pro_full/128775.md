night-and-day:/ˌnaɪt ən ˈdeɪ/ | adv. adj.| 日夜不停地，夜以继日地；持续不断地 | Continuously, without interruption, around the clock.  |  He worked night and day to finish the project. 他日夜不停地工作以完成这个项目。|近义词：constantly, incessantly, perpetually, around the clock |反义词: intermittently, occasionally |词性: 副词，形容词

night-and-day:/ˌnaɪt ən ˈdeɪ/ | adj.|  显著的，明显的 | Very noticeable; striking; stark. | There's a night-and-day difference between their performance and ours. 他们的表现和我们的表现有着显著的差异。|近义词：obvious, clear, distinct, marked |反义词: slight, subtle, insignificant |词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the literal sense of "during both night and day," emphasizing continuous activity.  The phrase has been in use since the late 14th century.  The hyphenated form became common in the 19th century.
