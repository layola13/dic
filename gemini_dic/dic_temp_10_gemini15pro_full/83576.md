haematin:/ˈhiːmətɪn/ /ˈhemətɪn/ | n. |血红素，高铁血红素|A dark bluish or brownish pigment containing iron, formed by oxidation of heme.  It is found in oxidized blood and in old blood clots.|The forensic scientist identified haematin in the bloodstain.  法医在血迹中发现了血红素。|Synonyms: ferriheme, oxidized heme|Antonyms: heme (in its reduced form)|noun

haematin:/ˈhiːmətɪn/ /ˈhemətɪn/ | n. |血晶素，血红质|A dark bluish or brownish pigment containing iron, derived from hemoglobin.  It is found in oxidized blood and in old blood clots.|The presence of haematin indicated the blood was old. 血液中存在血红质表明血液已经很老了。|Synonyms: hemin|Antonyms: hemoglobin (in its reduced, oxygen-carrying form)|noun
