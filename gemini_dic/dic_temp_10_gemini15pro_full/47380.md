dead-in-the-water:/ˌdɛd ɪn ðə ˈwɔːtər/| adj.|停滞不前的，毫无进展的，失败的|Unable to move or make progress; stalled or inactive, usually referring to a plan, project, or process that has ceased to function or advance.|例句：The project is dead in the water due to lack of funding.  这个项目由于缺乏资金而停滞不前。|近义词：stalled, stuck, grounded, inactive, defunct|反义词：progressing, advancing, thriving, flourishing|词性:形容词

dead-in-the-water:/ˌdɛd ɪn ðə ˈwɔːtər/| n.|停滞状态，僵局|A state of stagnation or stalemate.|例句：Negotiations are at a dead-in-the-water situation. 谈判陷入了僵局。|近义词：standstill, impasse, gridlock|反义词：progress, breakthrough, advancement|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
This idiom originates from nautical terminology. A ship that is "dead in the water" is unable to maneuver and is essentially adrift, making no progress. This image is then applied figuratively to any situation or endeavor that is similarly stalled or inactive.
