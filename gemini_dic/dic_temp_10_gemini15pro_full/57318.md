downtown: /ˌdaʊnˈtaʊn/ (US), /ˌdaʊnˈtaʊn/ (UK)| n. adj. adv.|市中心，市区的，往市中心|The heart of a city, especially its main business and commercial area.  Can also be used as an adjective to describe something located in or relating to the downtown area, or as an adverb indicating movement towards the downtown area.|例句：We went downtown for dinner last night. 昨晚我们去市中心吃晚饭。|近义词：city center, urban core, central business district|反义词:uptown, suburbs, outskirts|词性: noun, adjective, adverb

downtown: /ˌdaʊnˈtaʊn/ (US), /ˌdaʊnˈtaʊn/ (UK)| adj.|市中心的，市区的|Located in or relating to the downtown area.|例句：She works at a downtown law firm. 她在市中心的一家律师事务所工作。|近义词：city center, urban core, central business district|反义词:uptown, suburban, outlying|词性: adjective

downtown: /ˌdaʊnˈtaʊn/ (US), /ˌdaʊnˈtaʊn/ (UK)| adv.|往市中心|Towards the downtown area.|例句：We're heading downtown to catch a show. 我们正前往市中心去看演出。|近义词：to the city center, towards the urban core|反义词:uptown, to the suburbs, outwards|词性: adverb

词源： "down" + "town".  "Down" originally referred to the direction of river flow, so "downtown" often referred to the part of town situated downstream, which was frequently the older, more established, and commercial center of a city.
