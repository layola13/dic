capillary:/ˈkæpɪˌleri/ (US), /kəˈpɪləri/ (UK)| n. adj.|毛细管; 毛细血管; 微血管|A very thin tube, especially one of the smallest blood vessels in the body.|Blood flows from the arteries into tiny capillaries. 血液从动脉流入微小的毛细血管。|Synonyms: blood vessel, capillary tube, capillary vessel|Antonyms: artery, vein|noun

capillary:/ˈkæpɪˌleri/ (US), /kəˈpɪləri/ (UK)| adj.|毛细管的; 毛细作用的|Relating to or resembling a capillary or capillaries.|Capillary action causes the water to rise up the tube. 毛细作用使水上升到管子里。|Synonyms: fine, hair-like, minute|Antonyms: large, wide|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *capillaris, from Latin capillus (“hair”).
