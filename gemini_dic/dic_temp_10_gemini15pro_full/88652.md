hoarding:/ˈhɔːrdɪŋ/ (US) /ˈhɔːdɪŋ/ (UK)| n.|囤积；贮藏；（尤指广告）招贴板|The act of accumulating and storing things, especially excessively. A large board used to display advertisements.|She has a hoarding problem and her house is overflowing with junk. 她有囤积问题，她的房子里堆满了垃圾。|The company put up a new hoarding advertising their latest product. 公司竖起了一块新的广告牌，宣传他们的最新产品。|synonyms: accumulating, stockpiling, saving, amassing|antonyms: discarding, distributing, spending, sharing|noun

hoard:/hɔːrd/ (US) /hɔːd/ (UK)| n. v.|贮藏，囤积；贮藏物|A store of something valuable or useful, often kept secret or carefully guarded. To accumulate and store something valuable or useful.|He had a hoard of gold coins hidden in the attic. 他在阁楼里藏了一堆金币。|Squirrels hoard nuts for the winter. 松鼠为冬天储存坚果。|synonyms: stockpile, cache, store, reserve|antonyms: distribute, spend, share, scatter|noun, verb
