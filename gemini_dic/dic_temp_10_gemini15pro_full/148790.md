overvaliantnesses: /ˌəʊvərˈvæliəntnəsɪz/ | n. | 过分勇敢，鲁莽 |  Excessive or unwarranted displays of valor; rashness or foolhardiness.  | His overvaliantnesses ultimately led to his downfall. 他的过分勇敢最终导致了他的失败。 |  foolhardiness, recklessness, rashness | caution, prudence, circumspection | noun

Etymology:
From over- + valiant + -ness + -es.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively," "too much." "Valiant" comes from Old French "vaillant," ultimately from Latin "valere" meaning "to be strong" or "to be worth." "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality. "-es" is a plural suffix.
