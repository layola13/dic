chirpy:/ˈtʃɜːrpi/ (US), /ˈtʃɜːpi/ (UK)| adj.|活泼的，快活的|Describes someone who is cheerful and lively, often talking a lot in a friendly way.  It suggests a lighthearted and optimistic mood.|She was chirpy and full of energy this morning. 她今天早上精神抖擞，充满活力。|The birds were chirpy in the early morning sunshine.  清晨的阳光下，鸟儿们叽叽喳喳地叫着。|Synonyms: cheerful, merry, lively, upbeat, perky, jaunty|Antonyms: gloomy, depressed, sad, morose, sullen|Etymology:  From the verb "chirp," which imitates the sound of birds singing.  The word "chirpy" then developed to describe the happy and lively feeling associated with birdsong.|adjective
