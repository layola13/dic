mendaciously: /mɛnˈdeɪʃəsli/ | adv. | 欺骗地，虚伪地 | in a mendacious manner; characterized by deception or falsehood.  | He mendaciously claimed to have witnessed the event. 他谎称自己目睹了这件事。 |  Example: The politician mendaciously denied any involvement in the scandal.  这个政客虚伪地否认了参与这起丑闻。| 近义词: dishonestly, falsely, deceptively, untruthfully | 反义词: truthfully, honestly, sincerely | 词性: 副词

mendacious: /mɛnˈdeɪʃəs/ | adj. | 撒谎的，虚假的 | given to lying; habitually dishonest. | He is a mendacious person, so I don't believe anything he says. 他是个爱说谎的人，所以我不相信他说的任何话。 | Example: The mendacious rumors spread quickly through the town.  虚假的谣言迅速传遍了整个城镇。 | 近义词: lying, dishonest, deceitful, untruthful | 反义词: truthful, honest, sincere | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Latin *mendac-, from mendax "lying, deceitful," from menda "fault, defect, blemish."
