malacotoon:/ˌmæləˈkuːtuːn/| n.|桃子; 蜜桃|A peach, especially a clingstone peach. |例句：The malacotoon was ripe and juicy.  这颗桃子熟透多汁。

例句：He bit into the sweet malacotoon. 他咬了一口甜甜的桃子。|近义词：peach, nectarine|反义词: (No direct antonym for a fruit. Could consider unripe fruit, or a contrasting fruit like lemon)


词性: noun (名词)

词源: From French *malacoton*, from Italian *melacotone*, from Late Latin *malum cotonium* "quince," from Latin *malum* "apple" + *cotonium* (of uncertain origin, possibly related to the town of Kydonia on Crete).  The word originally referred to a quince, but later shifted to mean a peach.
