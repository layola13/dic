launch:/lɔːntʃ/ (UK), /lɑːntʃ/ (US)| v.|发射，启动，发行，下水|To send something out, such as a new product or spacecraft, into the market or space. Also, to put a boat into the water.|They launched a new satellite into orbit. 他们发射了一颗新卫星进入轨道。|synonyms: start, begin, initiate, release, inaugurate |antonyms:  terminate, end, conclude, withdraw|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *lanchier "to hurl, throw, brandish," from Frankish *lanca * "lance," from Proto-Germanic *lanko.
