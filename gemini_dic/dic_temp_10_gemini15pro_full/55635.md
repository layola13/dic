ditchwater:/ˈdɪtʃˌwɔːtər/| n.|脏水，污水；毫无价值的东西|Ditchwater is the stagnant, often muddy or dirty water found in a ditch. Figuratively, it refers to something dull, uninteresting, or of poor quality. |The coffee tasted like ditchwater. 这咖啡尝起来像脏水一样难喝。|近义词：stagnant water, foul water, muck, mire|反义词：fresh water, pure water|词性:名词

词源:  From ditch + water.  The word "ditch" comes from Old English "dīc," meaning "a trench dug in the earth."  "Water" comes from Old English "wæter."
