come-out-in-the-wash:/kʌm aʊt ɪn ðə wɒʃ/ | idiom |  最终会得到解决；真相大白；水落石出 | This idiom suggests that problems or difficulties will eventually be resolved or clarified, often without any specific action being taken. It implies that things will work themselves out in the end. | Don't worry too much about the disagreement; it'll all come out in the wash.  别太担心这次的争执，一切最终都会解决的。|Synonyms: sort itself out, work itself out, straighten itself out, come right in the end| Antonyms:  persist, fester, worsen, remain unresolved | idiom
