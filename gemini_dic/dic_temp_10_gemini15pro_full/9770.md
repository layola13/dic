antonomasy:/ˌænˈtɒnəməsi/ /ˌænˈtɑːnəməsi/ | n. |换称，代称|The substitution of a title, epithet, or descriptive phrase for a proper name, or the use of a proper name as a synonym for a general idea;  a name used as a substitute for another word or idea. |Example: "The Bard" for Shakespeare is an example of antonomasy.  “诗翁”指莎士比亚，这是换称的一个例子。

Example:  Calling a shrewd businessman "a Solomon" is an example of antonomasy. 把一个精明的商人称为“所罗门”是换称的一个例子。|Synonym: metonymy, appellative, denomination|Antonym: N/A|noun
