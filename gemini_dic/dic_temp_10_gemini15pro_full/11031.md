arcadianisms:/ɑːrˈkeɪdiənɪzəmz/
|n.|田园诗般的风格；田园主义；田园风味|The idealized, often unrealistic, representation of rural life and nature, suggesting a simple, innocent existence.|Her paintings were filled with arcadianisms, depicting shepherds and shepherdesses in idyllic landscapes.|近义词：pastoralism, bucolicism, idyll|反义词：realism, modernity|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Arcadian + -ism.  Arcadian refers to Arcadia, a region in Greece traditionally associated with pastoral simplicity and beauty.
