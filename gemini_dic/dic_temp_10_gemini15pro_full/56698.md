doomful: /ˈduːmfʊl/ | adj. | 厄运的；不祥的；预示灾难的 | Filled with a sense of doom or impending disaster; ominous. | The doomful prophecy foretold the end of the kingdom.  不祥的预言预示着王国的终结。|  The dark clouds gathered overhead, creating a doomful atmosphere.  乌云密布，营造出一种不祥的气氛。 | synonyms: ominous, portentous, foreboding, inauspicious | antonyms: auspicious, promising, hopeful | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From *doom* (n.) + *-ful*.  *Doom* comes from Old English *dōm* "judgment, law, statute; sentence, condemnation," ultimately from Proto-Germanic *dōmaz* "judgment."  *-ful* is a suffix meaning "full of," from Old English *-full*.
