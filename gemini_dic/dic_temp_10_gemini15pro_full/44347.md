cross-aisle: /ˈkrɒsˈaɪl/ (British) /ˈkrɔːsˈaɪl/ (American)| adj.|跨党派的，跨越党派界限的|Involving or relating to members of different political parties, especially in a cooperative way.  Reaching across the divisions between political parties.|The senator is known for his cross-aisle cooperation.  这位参议员以其跨党派合作而闻名。|The bill received cross-aisle support.  该法案获得了跨党派的支持。| bipartisan, nonpartisan | partisan| adjective

Etymology:
"cross-" + "aisle". "Cross-"  means "across" or "between". "Aisle" refers to the passage separating seating areas, particularly in legislative chambers, and by extension, the groups of legislators on either side. The term signifies bridging the gap between opposing sides.
