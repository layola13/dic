gentrified:/ˈdʒɛntrɪfaɪd/| adj.| 中产阶级化的，贵族化的|The process of renovating and improving a house or district so that it conforms to middle-class taste. This process often displaces lower-income residents due to rising costs.|The neighborhood has become increasingly gentrified in recent years, with trendy cafes and boutiques replacing the old mom-and-pop shops.  这个社区近年来变得越来越中产阶级化，时尚的咖啡馆和精品店取代了旧时的夫妻店。|Synonyms:  upscaled, renovated, improved, modernized|Antonyms:  run-down, dilapidated, neglected|adjective

gentrify:/ˈdʒɛntrɪfaɪ/| v.| 使中产阶级化，使贵族化|To renovate and improve (a house or district) so that it conforms to middle-class taste.|Developers are gentrifying the old industrial area, building luxury apartments and high-end retail spaces. 开发商正在将旧工业区中产阶级化，建造豪华公寓和高端零售空间。|Synonyms:  upscale, renovate, improve, modernize|Antonyms:  neglect, decay|verb

gentrification:/ˌdʒɛntrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/| n.| 中产阶级化，贵族化|The process of changing the character of a neighborhood through the influx of more affluent residents and businesses, often displacing current inhabitants.|Gentrification has become a contentious issue in many urban areas. 在许多城市地区，中产阶级化已成为一个有争议的问题。|Synonyms:  urban renewal,  upscaling, revitalization|Antonyms:  urban decay, decline|noun
