overelegant:/ˌoʊvərˈɛlɪɡənt/
adjective
过分优雅的；矫揉造作的
Excessively elegant; too elaborate or refined in style or taste.
Her overelegant prose felt artificial and insincere. 她过分优雅的散文显得矫揉造作，缺乏真诚。
The overelegant decor made the room feel stuffy and unwelcoming. 过于精致的装饰使房间感觉沉闷，不友好。
近义词:ostentatious, pretentious, flamboyant
反义词:simple, plain, understated
词性:adjective

词源:
From over- + elegant.  "Over-" acts as a prefix indicating "excessively" or "too much".  "Elegant" comes from the French "élégant," ultimately from the Latin "eligere" meaning "to choose" or "select," implying a refined and discerning taste.
