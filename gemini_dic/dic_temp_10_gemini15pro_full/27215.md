callowness:/ˈkæləʊnɪs/| n.|幼稚，不成熟|Lack of experience and worldly wisdom; immaturity.|His callowness was evident in his naive comments about politics.|近义词：immaturity, inexperience, naiveté, greenness|反义词：maturity, sophistication, experience, worldliness|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From callow + -ness.  Callow from Old English *calu "bald, bare," from Proto-Germanic *kalwaz "bald, bare."  The sense shifted from "bald" (like a young bird) to "immature."
