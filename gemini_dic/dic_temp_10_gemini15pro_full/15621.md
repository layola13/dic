bailout:/ˈbeɪlaʊt/| n.|紧急财政援助；保释；跳伞|An act of giving financial assistance to a failing business or economy to save it from collapse.  A rescue from a difficult situation.|The government provided a bailout to the struggling airline. 政府向陷入困境的航空公司提供了紧急财政援助。|Synonyms: rescue, lifeline, assistance|Antonyms: refusal, denial|noun


bail out:/beɪl aʊt/| v.|保释；帮助…摆脱困境；跳伞；舀出|To rescue someone or something from a difficult situation, often financial. To jump out of an aircraft with a parachute.|He bailed his friend out of jail. 他把他的朋友从监狱里保释出来。  The pilot had to bail out of the plane. 飞行员不得不跳伞逃生。|Synonyms: rescue, extricate, save|Antonyms: abandon, condemn|verb
