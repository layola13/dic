ouzel: /ˈuzəl/ (US), /ˈʌzəl/ (UK)| n. | 鸟，（尤指）欧亚鸲，水鸫 | A small, dark thrush, typically with a white crescent on the breast.  A bird of the thrush family, especially the Eurasian blackbird or any of several similar North American birds. |例句：The ouzel sang a melodious tune from its perch on the branch.  欧亚鸲在树枝上唱着优美的曲调。

例句：The American Dipper, also known as the Water Ouzel, dives into fast-flowing streams to catch aquatic insects.  美洲河乌，也被称为水鸫，潜入湍急的溪流中捕捉水生昆虫。|近义词：blackbird, thrush |反义词: N/A |词性: noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *osle, from Old English ōsle, from Proto-Germanic *amsalaz, from Proto-Indo-European *oms- (“thrush”). Cognate with Old High German amsala (German Amsel), Old Norse qǫsl.
