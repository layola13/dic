mascagnite:/mæˈskænjaɪt/ | n. | 马斯卡尼矿，硫酸铵矿 | A naturally occurring mineral form of ammonium sulfate, (NH4)2SO4. It is typically found as a white to yellowish powdery or crystalline efflorescence in volcanic fumaroles and around burning coal seams. |  Mascagnite is a common mineral found near active volcanoes. 马斯卡尼矿是一种常见于活火山附近的矿物。

Mascagnite can be used as a nitrogen fertilizer. 马斯卡尼矿可以用作氮肥。 | 近义词: ammonium sulfate | 反义词: None | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Named after Paolo Mascagni (1755-1815), an Italian anatomist and chemist who first described the mineral.
