commemorated: /kəˈmɛməreɪtɪd/ | v. (past tense) | 纪念了，庆祝了 | Past tense of "commemorate," meaning to remember and show respect for someone or something. | The town commemorated the founding fathers with a statue.  该镇为纪念开国元勋而立了一座雕像。| Synonyms: celebrated, observed, honored | Antonyms: forgot, ignored | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin commemoratus, past participle of commemorare "call to remembrance," from com- "together" + memorare "to mention, relate," from memor "mindful."
