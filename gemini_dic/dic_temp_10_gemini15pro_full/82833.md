gulch:/ɡʌltʃ/
gulches:/ˈɡʌltʃɪz/
| n. |深谷，峡谷；冲沟|A deep, narrow ravine, especially one formed by heavy rains or a flash flood. |The cowboys rode their horses through the steep gulches. 牛仔们骑着马穿过陡峭的峡谷。|近义词：ravine, canyon, gorge, gully|反义词：ridge, peak|词性:名词

gulch:/ɡʌltʃ/
gulches:/ˈɡʌltʃɪz/
| vt. |（非正式）吞没，吞噬|To swallow or engulf, often in a figurative sense. |The earthquake gulched entire villages. 地震吞没了整座村庄。|近义词：swallow, engulf, devour, consume|反义词：disgorge, expel|词性:动词


词源:
From American English, perhaps from a dialectal German word *gulch "throat, pit."  Related to German *gulgen "to gurgle."
