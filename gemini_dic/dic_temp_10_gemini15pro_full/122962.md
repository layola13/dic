monachal:/məˈnɑːkəl/| adj.|修道院的，僧侣的，禁欲的|Of or relating to monks or monasteries; characteristic of monks or monastic life, especially in its austerity and seclusion.|例句：He lived a monachal life devoted to prayer and study. 他过着一种致力于祈祷和学习的僧侣生活。

例句：The monachal atmosphere of the library was conducive to quiet contemplation. 图书馆的修道院氛围有利于安静的沉思。|近义词：ascetic, austere, cloistered, monastic|反义词：secular, worldly|词性：adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *monachalis*, from Latin monachus (“monk”), from Ancient Greek μοναχός (monakhos, “solitary, single”), from μόνος (monos, “alone, single”).
