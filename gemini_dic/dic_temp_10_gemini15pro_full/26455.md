byssus:/ˈbɪsəs/| n. |细丝，足丝，亚麻布|A fine, silky filament secreted by certain bivalve mollusks, especially mussels, used to attach themselves to rocks or other surfaces; also, the fabric made from these filaments.|The mussel anchored itself to the rock with its byssus.  贻贝用它的足丝把自己固定在岩石上。|Synonyms: beard, filament|Antonyms: N/A|noun

byssus:/ˈbɪsəs/| n. |（古）细麻布|A fine linen or cotton cloth used in ancient times.|The ancient Egyptians used byssus to wrap mummies. 古埃及人用细麻布包裹木乃伊。|Synonyms: linen, cotton|Antonyms: N/A|noun
