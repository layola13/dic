damnation:/dæmˈneɪʃən/
n.
诅咒，咒骂;  毁灭;  地狱;
The act of damning or the state of being damned. Eternal punishment in hell. A cause or source of ruin or misery.
He muttered a damnation on his enemies. 他低声诅咒他的敌人。
The constant noise was the damnation of the neighborhood.  持续不断的噪音是这个街区的祸根。
近义词: condemnation, curse, perdition, doom
反义词: salvation, redemption, blessing
词性: 名词


damnations:/dæmˈneɪʃənz/
n.
诅咒，咒骂（复数）
Plural form of damnation.  Multiple instances of condemning someone or something to hell or destruction.
His fiery speech was filled with damnations of the corrupt officials.  他慷慨激昂的演讲充满了对腐败官员的谴责。
The preacher warned against the various damnations that awaited sinners.  传教士警告说各种各样的诅咒正等待着罪人。
近义词: condemnations, curses
反义词: blessings, redemptions
词性: 名词


词源:
From Old French *damnation, from Late Latin damnationem (nominative damnatio), noun of action from past-participle stem of Latin damnare "to condemn, sentence, blame, reject," from damnum "damage, hurt, loss; penalty, fine," from Proto-Italic *dapno-, probably from PIE root *dap- "to distribute, allot, share."
