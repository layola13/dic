calotype: /ˈkælətaɪp/ | n. |卡罗式摄影法，卡罗版照片 | An early photographic process in which a negative image is formed on paper sensitized with silver iodide. This negative is then used to make positive prints. | Example 1: The museum exhibited a collection of rare calotypes from the 19th century.  博物馆展出了一批19世纪罕见的卡罗式照片。

Example 2:  Calotypes were an important step in the development of photography. 卡罗式摄影法是摄影发展的重要一步。 | Synonym: Talbotype | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but could consider later photographic processes like Daguerreotype as a contrasting process) | noun
