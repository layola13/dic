ETYMOLOGY->
From "gullible" + "-ities" (suffix forming plural nouns of abstract qualities).  "Gullible" comes from "gull" (verb meaning "to cheat or deceive") + "-ible" (suffix meaning "capable of being"). "Gull" likely originates from an imitative word suggesting the sound of a bird being easily caught.
