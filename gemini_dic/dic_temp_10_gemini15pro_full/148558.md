oversystematicness:/ˌoʊvərˌsɪstəˈmætɪknəs/| n. |过度系统化；过度规则化|The tendency to apply grammatical rules too rigidly, often resulting in incorrect forms.  This is common in children learning a language.|Example: The child's use of "goed" instead of "went" is an example of oversystematicness.  这个孩子用“goed”而不是“went”是过度规则化的一个例子。|Example: Oversystematicness in language acquisition can be seen in the application of regular verb endings to irregular verbs. 在语言习得中，过度系统化可以体现在将规则动词词尾应用于不规则动词上。|Synonyms: overregularization, hypercorrection|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider: irregularity,  deviation)|noun
