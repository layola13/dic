effeteness:/ɪˈfɛktnəs/| n. | 衰弱；虚弱；柔弱 |The state of being effete; weakness, lack of vigor, or decadence. |His effeteness made him unsuitable for the demanding job. 他的柔弱使他不适合这份要求很高的工作。| 近义词：weakness, feebleness, debility, impotence, fragility | 反义词：strength, vigor, robustness, power | 词性: 名词


effete:/ɪˈfiːt/| adj. | 衰弱的；虚弱的；缺乏活力的；（尤指男人）柔弱的；女人气的 |(of a person) affected, overrefined, and ineffectual; no longer capable of effective action.|(especially of a man) weak or effeminate. |  The once powerful empire had grown effete and decadent. 这个曾经强大的帝国已经变得衰弱和颓废。|The effete aristocracy seemed oblivious to the suffering of the poor.  柔弱的贵族似乎对穷人的苦难漠不关心。| 近义词：weak, feeble, debilitated, decadent, overrefined, ineffectual | 反义词：strong, vigorous, robust, powerful, effective | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *effetus*  "that has given birth, exhausted by bearing" (hence weakened), past participle of *effere* "to bear out, bring forth," from *ex-* "out" + *ferre* "to bear."
