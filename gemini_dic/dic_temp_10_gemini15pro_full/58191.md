drowsing: /ˈdraʊzɪŋ/ | adj. n. | 打瞌睡的，昏昏欲睡的；瞌睡 | being in a state between sleeping and waking; a short sleep, especially during the day |  He was found drowsing at his desk. 他被发现在办公桌上打瞌睡。 | Synonyms: dozing, nodding off, napping | Antonyms: awake, alert | adjective

drowsing: /ˈdraʊzɪŋ/ | n. |  瞌睡 | a short sleep, especially during the day |  I had a drowsing on the train. 我在火车上打了个盹。 | Synonyms: nap, doze, siesta | Antonyms: wakefulness, insomnia | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *drousen, from Old English *drūsian (“to become sluggish, grow weak”), from Proto-Germanic *drūsaną (“to sink, fall, become troubled”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰreus- (“to fall, to flow”). Cognate with Dutch droosen (“to doze, to mope”), German trübsen (“to sadden, to grieve”).
