commanded:/kəˈmændɪd/ |v. | 命令，指挥，统率|  give an authoritative order | The general commanded his troops to attack. 将军命令他的部队进攻。 | order, direct, instruct | disobey, refuse | verb


commanded:/kəˈmændɪd/ |adj. |  被指挥的，被控制的 | under the control or authority of someone | The commanded area was under strict surveillance. 被指挥的区域处于严密监视之下。 | controlled, governed | uncontrolled, independent | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *commaunden*, from Old French *commander*, from Latin *commandāre* (“to entrust, commit, commend, order”), from *com-* (“together”) + *mandāre* (“to entrust, order”).
