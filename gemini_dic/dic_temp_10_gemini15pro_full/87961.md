high-fived:/haɪˈfaɪvd/
verb
击掌庆祝
past tense of "high-five" meaning to celebrate something by slapping someone else's raised hand.
The team high-fived after winning the game.  团队赢得了比赛后击掌庆祝。
We high-fived each other after finishing the project.  我们完成项目后互相击掌庆祝。
Synonyms: celebrated, congratulated
Antonyms: mourned, commiserated
词性: 动词

high-five:/ˈhaɪ faɪv/
noun, verb
击掌；互相击掌
An act of slapping palms with another person, usually to express congratulations or joy.
We gave each other a high five after the meeting. 会议结束后我们互相击掌。
She high-fived her teammate after scoring the winning goal.  她在打进制胜球后与队友击掌。
Synonyms: hand slap, clap
Antonyms: N/A
词性: 名词，动词

ETYMOLOGY->
"high five" originated in the 1970s in American sports, specifically baseball and basketball. The exact origin is debated, but it's believed to have evolved from the "low five" and became popularized through its use in professional sports.
