empugn:/ɪmˈpjuːn/| vt.|质疑，攻击， impugn (someone's character, motives, etc.)|To challenge the honesty, integrity, or reputation of (someone or something).|
The lawyer impugned the witness's testimony. 律师质疑证人的证词。

The politician's integrity was impugned by his opponents.  这位政治家的正直受到了对手的攻击。

|近义词：challenge, question, dispute, attack, criticize|反义词：support, defend, uphold, endorse|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *impugnare, from Latin in- (“in, into, on, upon, against”) + pugnare (“to fight”).
