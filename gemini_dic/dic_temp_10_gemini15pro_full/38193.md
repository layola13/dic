compulsions:/kəmˈpʌlʃənz/| n.|强迫症，强迫行为|An irresistible urge to behave in a certain way, especially against one's conscious wishes.|He felt compulsions to wash his hands every few minutes. 他感到每隔几分钟就必须洗手。|synonyms:obsessions, urges, drives, impulses|antonyms:aversions, disinclinations|noun


compulsion:/kəmˈpʌlʃən/| n.|强制，强迫|The action or state of forcing or being forced to do something; constraint.|He acted under compulsion. 他是在强迫下行动的。|synonyms:coercion, constraint, duress, oppression|antonyms:freedom, liberty|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin compulsiō (“a driving or urging on”), from Latin compellō (“I drive or force together”), from com- (“together”) + pellō (“I drive, push”).
