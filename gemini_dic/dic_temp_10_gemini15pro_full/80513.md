good-ol-boys:/ˌɡʊd əʊl ˈbɔɪz/ | n. | 老好人；哥们儿；死党（常指南方白人男性，尤指拥有特权或保守观点的） | A group of men, especially in the southern US, who have known each other for a long time and who support each other, often seen as having conservative or prejudiced views and excluding those who are different. | Example 1: The good-ol-boys network at the country club made it difficult for outsiders to join.  乡村俱乐部的老好人网络让外来人很难加入。

Example 2:  She accused the local government of being run by a clique of good-ol-boys. 她指责当地政府被一群老好人控制。 |  Synonyms: cronies, buddies, clique, inner circle | Antonyms: outsiders, newcomers | Noun
