beggared:/ˈbɛɡərd/| v. |使贫穷，耗尽，使不足|To impoverish someone or something; to exhaust or deplete resources.|The war beggared the country, leaving its economy in ruins. 战争使这个国家变得贫穷，经济一片废墟。|Synonyms: impoverished, ruined, depleted, exhausted|Antonyms: enriched, prosperous|verb
beggared:/ˈbɛɡərd/| adj. |贫穷的，耗尽的|Impoverished; depleted; lacking.|He was left beggared after the stock market crash. 股市崩盘后，他变得一贫如洗。|Synonyms: impoverished, destitute, penniless|Antonyms: wealthy, affluent|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From beggar (n.) + -ed.  The verb "beggar" itself comes from Old French "begart,"  a derogatory term for a member of the Beghards, a lay religious order known for their poverty.  Over time, the word lost its religious connection and came to mean simply "a person who begs."
