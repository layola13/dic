canvass:/ˈkænvəs/ (US) /ˈkænvəs/ (UK)| n.|拉选票，游说；细查；讨论；兜售|The act of soliciting votes, opinions, or support; a thorough examination; a discussion.|例句：The canvass revealed widespread support for the proposal.  民意调查显示该提案得到了广泛支持。|近义词：solicitation, poll, survey, examination, discussion|反义词：--|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *canevas* "coarse cloth," from Late Latin *cannabis* "hemp." The political sense arose from the idea of sifting through something as through a canvas cloth.
