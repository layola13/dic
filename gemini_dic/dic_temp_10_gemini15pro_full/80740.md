goshawk:/ˈɡɒs.hɔːk/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːs.hɑːk/ (US)| n. |苍鹰|A large, short-winged hawk (Accipiter gentilis) noted for its swift, powerful flight and its ability to hunt birds and small mammals in woodlands.|The goshawk swooped down and caught a rabbit. 苍鹰俯冲下来，抓住了一只兔子。|近义词：hawk, raptor, falcon|反义词:prey|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *gōshafoc, from gōs "goose" + hafoc "hawk".  Literally "goose hawk," likely referring to its size and power making it capable of preying on geese and similarly sized birds.
