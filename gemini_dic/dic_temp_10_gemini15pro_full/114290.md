mainpernable:/mɛnˈpɜːrnəbəl/| adj.| 可保证的；可维持的；可担保的 |  Able to be maintained or upheld, especially financially; capable of being guaranteed.  Derived from French "mainpernable," from "mainpernor" (to guarantee, bail), ultimately from Frankish *manwarnjan (to warn, summon).  The word is rarely used in modern English. | The loan was considered mainpernable due to the valuable collateral offered.  这笔贷款被认为是可以担保的，因为提供了有价值的抵押品。|  Synonyms: securable, guaranteeable, bondable | Antonyms: unsecurable, unguaranteeable, unbondable | word property:adjective
