insinuating:/ɪnˈsɪnjuːeɪtɪŋ/
adjective
暗示的，含沙射影的，谄媚的
Suggesting or implying something bad or unpleasant in an indirect way; Having a slyly suggestive and unpleasant quality.
She made some insinuating remarks about his past. 她对他的过去做了一些含沙射影的评论。
Synonyms: suggestive, implying, hinting, intimate, innuendo, allusive
Antonyms: direct, forthright, frank, explicit
词性: 形容词

insinuate:/ɪnˈsɪnjuːeɪt/
verb
暗示，含沙射影地说，钻营
Suggest or hint (something bad or reprehensible) in an indirect and unpleasant way; maneuver oneself into (a position of favor or office) by subtle manipulation.
He insinuated that she was lying. 他暗示她在撒谎。
Synonyms: imply, suggest, hint, intimate, allusive
Antonyms: declare, state, assert, pronounce
词性: 动词

词源: From Latin insinuare "to wind or worm one's way into," from in- "into" + sinuare "to curve, bend," from sinus "curve, fold."
