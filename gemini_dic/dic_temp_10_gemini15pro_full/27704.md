candy-ass:/ˈkændiˌæs/| adj. n.|懦弱的，胆小的；胆小鬼，懦夫|A derogatory term for a weak or cowardly person, often implying effeminacy.|He called him a candy-ass for refusing to fight. 他因为他拒绝打架而叫他胆小鬼。|近义词：coward, wimp, sissy|反义词：brave, courageous, heroic|词性:形容词，名词

Etymology:
From "candy" (suggesting sweetness and weakness) + "ass" (a vulgar term for a person).  The term likely originated in the mid-20th century, possibly in military or prison slang.
