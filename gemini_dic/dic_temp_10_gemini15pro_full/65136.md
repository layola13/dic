ethidene: /ˈeθɪdiːn/ | n. |亚乙基|A bivalent radical, CH3CH=, derived from ethane by removal of two hydrogen atoms from the same carbon atom. It forms the basis for the naming of many organic compounds.|Example: Ethidene dichloride is a colorless liquid used as a solvent.  亚乙基二氯是一种无色液体，用作溶剂。|Example:  The ethidene group is present in several important chemical compounds. 亚乙基基团存在于几种重要的化合物中。|Synonym: - (No direct synonym, as it refers to a specific chemical structure) |Antonym: - (Not applicable to chemical radicals)|Noun
