dividend:/ˈdɪvɪdend/ (US), /ˈdɪvɪdənd/ (UK)| n.|股息；红利；回报；被除数|A sum of money paid regularly (typically quarterly) by a company to its shareholders out of its profits (or reserves).|Example 1: The company announced a dividend of 50 cents per share.  公司宣布每股派息50美分。

Example 2:  Investing in stocks that pay high dividends can provide a steady income stream. 投资支付高股息的股票可以提供稳定的收入来源。|Synonyms: payout, distribution, return, yield|Antonyms: loss, debt|noun
