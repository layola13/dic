admits:/ədˈmɪts/| v.|承认；准许进入；可容纳|To confess to be true or to be the case. To allow entry; to grant or afford. To have or be capable of space for.|He admitted his guilt. 他承认了他的罪行。

The ticket admits two people to the concert. 这张票允许两个人入场观看音乐会。
The theater admits 1000 people. 这个剧院可容纳1000人。|近义词：confesses, acknowledges, concedes, allows, permits|反义词：denies, rejects, refuses|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English admitten, from Latin admittere, from ad- (“to, toward”) + mittere (“to send, let go”).

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He admits his mistake. = 他承认他的错误。
She admits she needs help. = 她承认她需要帮助。

一般过去时:
They admitted their involvement in the crime. = 他们承认参与了犯罪。
He admitted he was wrong. = 他承认他错了。

现在完成时:
The company has admitted its responsibility. = 公司已经承认了它的责任。
She has admitted to feeling overwhelmed. = 她承认感到不知所措。

过去完成时:
He had admitted his guilt before the trial began. = 在审判开始前，他已经承认了罪行。
They had admitted their defeat before the game ended. = 比赛结束前，他们已经承认了失败。

将来时:
He will admit his mistake eventually. = 他最终会承认他的错误。
They will admit her to the hospital tomorrow. = 他们明天会让她住院。

虚拟语气:
If he admitted his fault, I would forgive him. = 如果他承认他的错误，我会原谅他。
I wish he would admit the truth. = 我希望他承认真相。

被动语态:
New evidence was admitted in court. = 新证据在法庭上被接受。
He was admitted to the university. = 他被大学录取了。

疑问句:
Does he admit his mistake? = 他承认他的错误吗？
Was he admitted to the hospital? = 他住院了吗？

否定句:
He doesn't admit his guilt. = 他不承认他的罪行。
She wasn't admitted to the club. = 她没有被俱乐部录取。

PHRASE->
admit to (doing something) = 承认（做某事）
admit someone to/into something = 允许某人进入某处/成为某组织的成员
admit defeat = 承认失败
admit of something = 容许某事；有…的可能
admit that = 承认…
