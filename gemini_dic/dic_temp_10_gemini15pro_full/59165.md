dyscrasia:/dɪsˈkreɪʒə/ /dɪsˈkreɪziə/ | n. | 体液不调；异常状态；紊乱 |  An abnormal or disordered state of the body or of a bodily part, especially of the body fluids (humors) or constitution, resulting from an imbalance of the four humors.  |  Example 1:  The doctor diagnosed a blood dyscrasia. 医生诊断出血液紊乱。

Example 2:  Metabolic dyscrasia can lead to various health problems. 代谢紊乱会导致各种健康问题。 |  Synonyms: disorder, imbalance, abnormality, ailment, malady | Antonyms:  balance, equilibrium, health, normalcy | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dyscrasīa, from Ancient Greek δυσκρασία (duskrasía, “bad temperament, ill-humour, intemperateness”), from δυσ- (dus-, “bad, difficult”) + κρᾶσις (krâsis, “mixture, blending”).
