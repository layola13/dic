euphuize:/ˈjuːfjuːaɪz/
verb
使…华丽；使…浮夸；用华丽的词藻修饰
To express in a highly elaborate and artificial style, characterized by excessive use of antithesis, alliteration, and far-fetched similes, often to the point of obscurity.
Shakespeare euphuized his language in some of his early plays.  莎士比亚在他的一些早期戏剧中使用了华丽的辞藻。
She euphuized her acceptance speech with literary allusions and metaphors.  她在获奖感言中使用了大量的文学典故和比喻，使其显得华丽。
近义词: embellish, adorn, elaborate, over-elaborate
反义词: simplify, streamline, understate
词性:v.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Euphues, the title character of John Lyly's Euphues: The Anatomy of Wit (1578), whose affected style gave rise to the term.  来自约翰·利利的《尤弗伊斯：智慧的解剖》（1578）中的主人公尤弗伊斯，他矫揉造作的风格产生了这个词。
