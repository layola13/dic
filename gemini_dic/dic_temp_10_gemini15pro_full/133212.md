nonexaggerations:/ˌnɒnɪɡˌzædʒəˈreɪʃənz/
|n.| 非夸大；没有夸大的说法|A statement or account that avoids exaggeration; the quality or state of being nonexaggerated.|Her account of the events was a refreshing example of nonexaggerations, sticking to the verifiable facts.|近义词: truths, facts, realities, understatements|反义词: exaggerations, hyperbole, overstatements|词性:名词

词源:
non- (prefix):  from Latin *non "not".
exaggeration (noun): from Late Latin exaggerationem (nominative exaggeratio) "a heaping up, exaggeration," noun of action from past-participle stem of exaggerare "to heap up, increase, aggravate," from ex- "out" (see ex-) + aggerare "to heap up," from agger "heap, mound"
