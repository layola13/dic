commando:/kəˈmændəʊ/ (UK), /kəˈmændoʊ/ (US)| n. |突击队员；特种兵|A soldier specially trained for carrying out raids.|The commandos stormed the enemy base. 特种兵突袭了敌军基地。|Synonyms: raider, special forces, elite troops|Antonyms: civilian, non-combatant|Noun


commando:/kəˈmændəʊ/ (UK), /kəˈmændoʊ/ (US)| adj. |突击队的；特种部队的|Relating to or characteristic of commandos.|A commando unit was deployed to the region. 一支突击队被部署到该地区。|Synonyms: special forces, elite|Antonyms: conventional, regular|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Afrikaans *kommando* "military unit, raiding party," from Dutch *kommando*, from Portuguese *comando*, from *comandar* "to command," ultimately from Latin *commandare*.
