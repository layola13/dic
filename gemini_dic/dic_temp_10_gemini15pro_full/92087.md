hyperrealist:/ˌhaɪpərˈriːəlɪst/ | n. adj. | 超现实主义者，超现实主义的 |  An artist or writer who depicts reality with extreme detail, often exceeding the precision of a photograph, especially in painting and sculpture.  Characterized by such depiction. | The hyperrealist paintings were so detailed they looked like photographs. 这些超现实主义绘画非常细致，看起来像照片一样。 |  photorealist, superrealist | surrealist, abstract | noun, adjective

hyperrealistic:/ˌhaɪpərˌriːəˈlɪstɪk/ | adj. | 超现实主义的 |  Marked by or adhering to the principles of hyperrealism. Extremely realistic; resembling a photograph. | The hyperrealistic sculpture captured every wrinkle and pore on the subject's face.  这座超现实主义雕塑捕捉到了对象脸上的每一条皱纹和毛孔。| photorealistic, superrealistic | surreal, abstract | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
hyper- (prefix): from Greek, meaning "over, beyond, excessive"
realist: from French réaliste, from Latin realis "real," from res "thing"
-ic (suffix): forming adjectives, meaning "having the character or form of," "related to," "made of"
