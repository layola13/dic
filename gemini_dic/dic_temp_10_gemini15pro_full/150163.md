pan-Turanianism:/ˌpænˌtʊrəˈneɪɪənɪzəm/| n.|泛突厥主义|A political and cultural movement of the late 19th and early 20th centuries that advocated the political and cultural unity of all Turkic, Mongolic, Siberian, and other peoples supposedly descended from the ancient Ural-Altaic population of Central Asia.|The rise of pan-Turanianism coincided with the decline of the Ottoman Empire. 泛突厥主义的兴起与奥斯曼帝国的衰落同时发生。| Pan-Turanism sought to unite various groups from Turkey to Japan. 泛突厥主义试图将从土耳其到日本的各个群体联合起来。|近义词:Pan-Turkism|反义词:None|词性:noun
