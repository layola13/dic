establishmentarian:/ɪˌstæblɪʃmɛnˈtɛəriən/
| adj. n.|建制派的，支持国教制度者；建制派人士|Relating to, or upholding, an establishment, especially the Church of England.  A person who supports the established church or institutions.| The politician was seen as an establishmentarian, favoring traditional policies. 这位政治家被视为建制派，支持传统政策。| The local establishmentarian group lobbied against any changes to the school's curriculum. 当地的建制派团体游说反对任何学校课程的改变。| 近义词：conventional, orthodox, traditionalist | 反义词：radical, reformer, revolutionary | 词性: adjective, noun
词源: From "establishment" + "-arian". "Establishment" traces back to Old French "establissement" and ultimately Latin "stabilis" (stable).  "-arian" is a suffix indicating adherence to a particular belief or system.
