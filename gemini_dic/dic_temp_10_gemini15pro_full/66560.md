exhibitionisms: /ˌɛksɪˈbɪʃənɪzəmz/
n.
暴露癖；炫耀症
A tendency to expose oneself indecently in public; a compulsive need to show off or draw attention to oneself.
His exhibitionisms led to his arrest. 他的暴露行为导致他被捕。

The singer's elaborate costumes and onstage antics were seen by some as mere exhibitionisms.  这位歌手精心设计的服装和舞台上的滑稽动作被一些人视为纯粹的炫耀。

近义词: showboating, ostentation, flamboyance
反义词: modesty, humility, reserve
词性: 名词 (plural)

词源: From exhibitionism + -s (plural marker).  Exhibitionism is derived from exhibition + -ism. Exhibition comes from the Latin "exhibitio," meaning "a showing forth."  The suffix "-ism" denotes a practice, doctrine, or characteristic.
