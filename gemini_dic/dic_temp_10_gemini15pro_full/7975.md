anthodium:/ænˈθoʊdɪəm/| n. |头状花序|A type of inflorescence characteristic of the Asteraceae (sunflower family), in which numerous small, sessile flowers are borne on a flattened receptacle, giving the appearance of a single flower.  The whole structure is surrounded by bracts forming an involucre.|The sunflower is a classic example of an anthodium.|Synonym: capitulum, flower head|Antonym: N/A|Noun

anthodium:/ænˈθoʊdiəm/| n. |头状花序|A flower head composed of many small florets arranged on a common receptacle, as in the daisy or sunflower.  It is surrounded by bracts that form an involucre, giving the appearance of a single large flower.|Dandelions and thistles also have anthodium inflorescences.|Synonym:  capitulum|Antonym: N/A|Noun
