crash (n.) + landing (n.)

crash (n.): From imitative origin, representing the sound of something breaking or colliding violently.

landing (n.): From land (v.) + -ing (suffix forming present participles and gerunds).

land (v.): From Old English "land," meaning "land, territory, estate." Ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*landą."
