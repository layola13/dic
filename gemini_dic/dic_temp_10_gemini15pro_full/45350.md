cumulostratus:/ˌkjuːmjʊləˈstrɑːtəs/ /ˌkjuːmjʊloʊˈstreɪtəs/
n.
积层云
A principal cloud type, gray or whitish, often with dark parts composed of a thick layer with patches that appear to be superimposed. It is intermediate between stratocumulus and nimbostratus.
Cumulostratus clouds often cover the entire sky. 积层云通常覆盖整个天空。
The cumulostratus thickened, turning into nimbostratus. 积层云变厚，变成了雨层云。
近义词:stratocumulus, nimbostratus
反义词: (No direct antonym for a cloud type)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin cumulus (“heap, pile”) + stratus (“spread out”). Coined by Luke Howard in 1803.
