over-reservedness:/ˌoʊvər rɪˈzɜːrvɪdnəs/
|n.| 过分拘谨，过分矜持|The state of being excessively reserved or shy; undue reticence.|Her over-reservedness made it difficult for her to make new friends. 她过分的拘谨让她很难结交新朋友。|reserve, shyness, reticence, modesty, inhibition|outgoingness, boldness, assertiveness, confidence|noun


Etymology:
"over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi "over, above, beyond") + "reservedness" ("reserved" (from French réserver "to reserve, keep back," from Latin reservare "to keep back, retain") + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being").
