chilling:/ˈtʃɪlɪŋ/| adj.|令人恐惧的；令人不寒而栗的；寒冷的|Causing a feeling of fear or unease; very frightening; cold.|The movie had a chilling climax. 这部电影的高潮令人毛骨悚然。|Synonyms: frightening, terrifying, eerie, spooky, cold|Antonyms: comforting, reassuring, warming, pleasant|adjective

chilling:/ˈtʃɪlɪŋ/| n.|冷却；变冷|The act of becoming or making something cold.|The chilling of the air conditioner was a welcome relief. 空调的制冷让人感到欣慰。|Synonyms: cooling, refrigeration|Antonyms: heating, warming|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "chill," which comes from Old English "ciele" (cold), from Proto-Germanic "*kaliz" (cold).  The "-ing" suffix transforms the verb into a present participle, which can function as an adjective.
