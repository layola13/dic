eclipsable:/ɪˈklɪpsəbl/
adjective
可食的；可遮蔽的；可掩蔽的
Capable of being eclipsed.
The moon is eclipsable by the earth. 月球可以被地球遮蔽。
近义词:obscured, concealable
反义词:visible, unconcealable
词性:adjective

eclipse:/ɪˈklɪps/
noun
日食，月食；黯然失色，衰落
An astronomical event that occurs when an astronomical object or spacecraft is temporarily obscured, by passing into the shadow of another body or by having another body pass between it and the viewer.  A loss of significance, power, or prominence in relation to another person or thing.
A total eclipse of the sun occurred. 发生了日全食。
近义词:occultation, covering
反义词:exposure
词性:noun
词源:From Old French e(s)clipse, from Latin eclipsis, from Ancient Greek ἔκλειψις (ékleipsis, “abandonment, failing, omission”), from ἐκλείπω (ekleípō, “I forsake, quit, make default”), from ἐκ (ek, “out”) + λείπω (leípō, “I leave”).
