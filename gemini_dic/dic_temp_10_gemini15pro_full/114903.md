mansard:/ˈmænsɑːrd/ | n. | 屋顶；阁楼 | A mansard roof is a roof with two slopes on each of its four sides, with the lower slope being steeper than the upper one. | The old house had a distinctive mansard roof. 这栋老房子有一个独特的孟莎式屋顶。| synonym: roof, attic | antonym: floor | noun

Etymology: From French *mansarde*, named after François Mansart (1598–1666), a French architect who popularized this roof style, though it was used earlier.
