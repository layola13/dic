foil:/fɔɪl/ | n. |箔,金属薄片| A thin sheet of metal, especially one of gold or silver, used for decoration or wrapping. | The chocolate was wrapped in gold foil. 巧克力用金箔包裹着。| synonyms: sheet, film, leaf | antonyms: thick, bulky | noun
foil:/fɔɪl/ | v. |挫败,阻止| Prevent (something considered wrong or undesirable) from succeeding. | The police foiled the bank robbery. 警察挫败了银行抢劫。| synonyms: thwart, frustrate, defeat | antonyms: assist, aid, support | verb
foil:/fɔɪl/ | n. |陪衬物,衬托| A person or thing that contrasts with and emphasizes the qualities of another. | The comedian's straight-faced partner acted as a perfect foil.  喜剧演员面无表情的搭档是一个完美的陪衬。| synonyms: contrast, complement | antonyms: counterpart, duplicate | noun
