garnet:/ˈɡɑːrnɪt/ (US) /ˈɡɑːnɪt/ (UK)| n. |石榴石；深红色|A hard, glassy silicate mineral that is often used as a gemstone and typically varies in color from dark red to brownish or yellowish red. |例句：She wore a garnet necklace. 她戴着一条石榴石项链。

例句：The museum displayed a collection of rare garnets.  博物馆展出了一系列珍稀的石榴石。|近义词：ruby, gemstone|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gernet*, from Old French *grenat* (compare modern French *grenat*), from Medieval Latin *granatum*, neuter of Latin *granatus* (“having many grains/seeds”), from *granum* (“grain, seed”). So called for resemblance in color and shape to the seeds of the pomegranate.
