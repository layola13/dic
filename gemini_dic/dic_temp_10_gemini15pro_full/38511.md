conciliation:/kənˌsɪliˈeɪʃn/
n.
和解，调解，安抚
The act of placating and overcoming distrust or hostility.
Example:  The union and management reached a conciliation after a long strike.  工会和管理层在长期罢工后达成了和解。

Example:  The government appointed a special envoy for conciliation between the warring factions. 政府任命了一名特使来调解交战各派。
近义词:reconciliation, appeasement, mediation, compromise
反义词:conflict, disagreement, hostility, antagonism
词性:名词


conciliations:/kənˌsɪliˈeɪʃnz/
n.
和解，调解（conciliation 的复数形式）
Plural form of conciliation, referring to multiple instances of placating and overcoming distrust or hostility.
Example: Several conciliations were attempted before the conflict escalated. 在冲突升级之前，曾尝试过几次和解。

Example: The community center offers free conciliations services for neighborhood disputes. 社区中心为邻里纠纷提供免费的调解服务。
近义词:reconciliations, appeasements, mediations, compromises
反义词:conflicts, disagreements, hostilities, antagonisms
词性:名词

词源:
From Late Middle English: from Old French conciliacion, or from late Latin conciliatio(n-), from the verb conciliare ‘bring together, win over’ (see conciliate).
