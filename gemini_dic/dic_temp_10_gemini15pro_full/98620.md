instrumentalism:/ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəlɪzəm/ | n. | 工具主义 | A doctrine that ideas are instruments of action and that their truth or value consists in their usefulness.  It emphasizes the practical function of theories and beliefs, judging their worth by their success in solving problems or achieving desired outcomes, rather than by their correspondence with reality. |  Many scientists adopt instrumentalism, valuing theories for their predictive power rather than their truth. 许多科学家采用工具主义，重视理论的预测能力而不是其真实性。

The government's approach to education was based on instrumentalism, focusing on skills needed for the job market. 政府的教育方法基于工具主义，侧重于就业市场所需的技能。 | 近义词: pragmatism, utilitarianism | 反义词: realism, idealism | 词性: noun
