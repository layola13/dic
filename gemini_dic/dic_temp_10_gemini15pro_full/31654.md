chatterbox:/ˈtʃætərˌbɒks/ (US) /ˈtʃætəbɒks/ (UK)| n. |喋喋不休的人，话匣子|A person who talks a lot.|例句：She's a real chatterbox and can talk for hours. 她真是个话匣子，能说上好几个小时。|例句：He's such a chatterbox, it's hard to get a word in edgewise. 他太爱说话了，很难插上一句话。|近义词：talkative person, blabbermouth|反义词：quiet person, reticent person|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From chatter (v.) + box (n.).  "Chatter" comes from the imitative verb "chatter" (of birds, teeth, etc.),  likely of Germanic origin.  "Box" in this context refers to a container or source, implying a container of chatter.
