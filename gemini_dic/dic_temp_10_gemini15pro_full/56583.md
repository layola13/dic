被动语态: 
“Don’ted” itself cannot be used in the passive voice because it is a non-standard form.  The correct form "didn't" is used with the passive voice as follows:

The work wasn't finished yesterday. = 这项工作昨天没有完成。
The cake wasn't eaten. = 蛋糕没有被吃掉。


疑问句:
Didn't you go to the party? = 你没去参加派对吗？
Didn't she finish her dinner? = 她没有吃完晚饭吗？


否定句:
I didn't not go to the party (Double negative, meaning I DID go). = 我去了派对。
She didn't not finish her dinner (Double negative, meaning she DID finish). = 她吃完了晚饭。



PHRASE->
While “don’ted” itself is not used in standard phrases, here are phrases using "didn't":

I didn't mean to... = 我不是故意的...
I didn't know that. = 我不知道。
I didn't see you there. = 我没看到你在那里。
I didn't hear you. = 我没听到你说。
I didn't get that. = 我没听懂。


注意事项:
“Don’ted” is considered incorrect grammar.  Avoid using it in formal writing or speech. Use "didn't" instead.
