half-mad:/ˌhæf ˈmæd/ | adj. | 半疯的；精神错乱的；狂热的 | Partially insane; deranged; exhibiting excessive enthusiasm or passion. | He had a half-mad gleam in his eyes. 他的眼睛里闪烁着疯狂的光芒。|  The fans were half-mad with excitement. 球迷们兴奋得近乎疯狂。 | 近义词：crazy, insane, demented, passionate, fanatical | 反义词：sane, rational, calm | 词性: 形容词

词源:  "half" (Old English healf) + "mad" (Old English gemæd).  "Half" indicates a partial state, while "mad" originally meant "foolish" or "senseless," evolving to its current meaning related to insanity.
