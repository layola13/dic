bournonite:/ˈbʊərnəˌnaɪt/| n. |车轮矿|A steel-gray, metallic sulfide mineral, PbCuSbS₃, crystallizing in the orthorhombic system. It is an ore of lead, copper, and antimony.|Bournonite is a relatively rare mineral, but can be found in some hydrothermal veins. 车轮矿是一种相对稀有的矿物，但在一些热液矿脉中可以找到。|synonyms: None (as it's a specific mineral name).|antonyms: None (as it's a specific mineral name).|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *bournonite*, named after Jacques Louis, Comte de Bournon (1751-1825), French crystallographer and mineralogist.
