astate:/əˈsteɪt/ | adj. | 惊恐的，惊慌的 | In a state of shock and fear; terrified. | He was astate at the news of his wife's accident. 他听到妻子出事的消息惊恐万分。 | Synonyms: terrified, horrified, aghast, shocked, frightened, alarmed | Antonyms: calm, composed, tranquil, serene | adjective

Etymology: From Old French *estat, past participle of ester "to stand," from Latin stare "to stand."  The emotional sense developed from the idea of being "stupefied" or "frozen in place" with fear.
