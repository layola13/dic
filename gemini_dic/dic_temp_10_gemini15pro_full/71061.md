first-world:/ˈfɜːrst wɜːrld/ | adj. | 第一世界的；发达国家的 | Relating to or characteristic of the wealthy, industrialized nations of the world. |  The cafe offered free wifi, a typical first-world amenity. 这家咖啡馆提供免费WiFi，这是典型的第一世界国家设施。

The politician's concerns seemed first-world compared to the struggles of the refugees. 与难民的挣扎相比，这位政客的担忧似乎是第一世界的问题。 | 近义词: developed, industrialized, affluent | 反义词: developing, third-world, underdeveloped | 词性: 形容词


词源:  This term originated during the Cold War, referring to the capitalist bloc of countries aligned with the United States, in contrast to the "Second World" (communist states) and the "Third World" (non-aligned and developing nations).
