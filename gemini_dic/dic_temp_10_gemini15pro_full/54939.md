dispossessing:/dɪspəˈzɛsɪŋ/
adjective
剥夺的；使丧失所有权的
Depriving someone of land, property, or other possessions.
The dispossessing of farmers from their land caused widespread unrest.  农民被剥夺土地引起了广泛的社会动荡。
The government's policy is dispossessing many low-income families of their homes. 政府的政策正在剥夺许多低收入家庭的住房。
近义词: expropriating, depriving, ousting
反义词: providing, granting, bestowing
词性: adjective

dispossess:/dɪspəˈzɛs/
verb
剥夺；使丧失所有权
To deprive someone of land, property, or other possessions.
The landlord dispossessed the tenants for not paying rent. 房东因租户未付房租而将其驱逐。
Many families were dispossessed of their homes after the hurricane. 许多家庭在飓风过后失去了家园。
近义词:evict, expel, oust
反义词:provide, grant, endow
词性: verb


词源:
From dis- (expressing negation or reversal) + possess (to have or own).  由dis-（表示否定或反转）+ possess（拥有）组成。
