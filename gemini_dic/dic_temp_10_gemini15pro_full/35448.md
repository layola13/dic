co-star:/ˈkoʊˌstɑːr/ (US) /ˈkəʊˌstɑː(r)/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.| 联合主演，合演|A performer who shares top billing with another performer.  A person who shares a leading role with another actor in a play, film, or television programme.|例句：She co-starred with Tom Hanks in the movie. 她和汤姆·汉克斯在电影中联合主演。|近义词：share the spotlight, co-lead|反义词: supporting actor, extra|词性:名词,动词

co-star:/ˈkoʊˌstɑːr/ (US) /ˈkəʊˌstɑː(r)/ (UK)| vi. vt.| 联合主演，合演|To perform together with another actor in a leading role.|例句：He co-starred in several films with Marilyn Monroe. 他与玛丽莲·梦露合演了几部电影。|近义词：act alongside, perform with|反义词: support, play a minor role|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->

From co- + star.  "Co-" is a prefix meaning "jointly," "mutually," or "together."  "Star" refers to a prominent performer, especially in film or theatre.
