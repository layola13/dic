farthing:/ˈfɑːrðɪŋ/ (UK), /ˈfɑːrθɪŋ/ (US)| n. | 四分之一便士（英国旧货币单位，等于四分之一便士）|A farthing was a unit of currency in the United Kingdom, equal to one quarter of a penny.|He wouldn't give a farthing for it. 他一分钱也不肯花在这个上面。|Synonyms: quarter-penny|Antonyms: pound, sovereign|noun

farthing:/ˈfɑːrðɪŋ/ (UK), /ˈfɑːrθɪŋ/ (US)| n. | 少量，微不足道的东西|A farthing can also refer to a very small amount or something of little value.|His opinion isn't worth a farthing. 他的意见一文不值。|Synonyms: trifle, pittance|Antonyms: fortune, lot|noun
