disinterest:/dɪsˈɪntrəst/| n.|冷漠，不感兴趣；公正|Lack of interest; impartiality; unbiasedness.  The judge showed complete disinterest in the outcome of the case, ensuring a fair trial.|例句：He showed a marked disinterest in politics. 他对政治明显地不感兴趣。

例句：The judge must act with complete disinterest. 法官必须完全公正地行事。|近义词：indifference, apathy, impartiality, neutrality|反义词：interest, enthusiasm, bias, partiality|词性:名词

disinterested:/dɪsˈɪntrəstɪd/| adj.|无私的，公正的；不感兴趣的|Not influenced by personal advantage; unbiased; having no interest in something.|例句：A disinterested observer is needed to judge the competition. 需要一位公正的观察员来评判比赛。

例句：He seemed disinterested in my proposal. 他似乎对我的提议不感兴趣。|近义词：impartial, unbiased, neutral, indifferent|反义词：interested, biased, partial, prejudiced|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + interest.  Originally meant "impartial, unbiased," later acquiring the sense of "lack of interest."
