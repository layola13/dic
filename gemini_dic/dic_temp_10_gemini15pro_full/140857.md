on-the-hook:/ˌɑːn ðə ˈhʊk/ | adj. | 钩住的，有责任的，有义务的 | Being in a difficult situation, having an obligation or commitment that is difficult to avoid. | Example: I'm on the hook for organizing the whole event. 我负责组织整个活动。| Example: He's on the hook for a lot of debt. 他背负着很多债务。| Synonyms: trapped, obligated, responsible, liable | Antonyms: free, uncommitted, off the hook | adjective


on-the-hook:/ˌɑːn ðə ˈhʊk/ | adj. | 接通电话的 | Connected on a telephone call. (Less common usage) | Example:  The operator said she had him on the hook. 接线员说她已经把他接通了。 | Example: I kept her on the hook for half an hour. 我让她在电话里等了半个小时。 | Synonyms: connected, on the line | Antonyms: disconnected, off the line | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
Originates from the image of a fish caught on a hook, unable to escape. This metaphor extends to situations where someone is trapped or obligated.  The telephone usage likely stems from early telephone switchboards where physically connecting a call involved placing a hook onto a line.
