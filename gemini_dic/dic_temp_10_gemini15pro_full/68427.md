falsifying:/ˈfɔːlsɪfaɪɪŋ/ | v. | 伪造；篡改；歪曲；证明…是假的 |  The act of making something false; altering something to deceive; misrepresenting something; proving something to be untrue. | He was accused of falsifying data in his research. 他被指控在他的研究中伪造数据。| Synonyms: misrepresenting, fabricating, forging, counterfeiting, distorting, doctoring | Antonyms: verifying, confirming, validating, authenticating | Verb

falsify:/ˈfɔːlsɪfaɪ/ | v. | 伪造；篡改；歪曲；证明…是假的 | To make something false; to alter something to deceive; to misrepresent something; to prove something to be untrue. |  She falsified her age on the application. 她在申请表上谎报了年龄。| Synonyms: misrepresent, fabricate, forge, counterfeit, distort, doctor | Antonyms: verify, confirm, validate, authenticate | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *falsificare, from Latin falsus (“false”) + -ficare (from facere (“to make”)).
