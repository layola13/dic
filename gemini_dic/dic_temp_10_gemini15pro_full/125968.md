词源:
From Middle English *musen, from Old French muser (“to dream, loiter, muse”), of uncertain origin. Perhaps related to muse (“snout”) (compare the expression “to hang one's muzzle”), referring to animals standing idly with their heads down, or from Vulgar Latin *mūsus (“muzzle”).
