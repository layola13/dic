fortune-hunting:/ˈfɔːrtʃənˌhʌntɪŋ/ | n. | 寻宝；猎财；追求财富 | The act of seeking wealth, often through marriage or inheritance, rather than through work or other productive activities. | He went to California during the gold rush, hoping to strike it rich through fortune-hunting. 他在淘金热时期去了加利福尼亚，希望能通过猎财致富。 | synonyms: gold-digging, treasure-hunting, prospecting | antonyms: earning, working, laboring | noun

fortune-huntings: /ˈfɔːrtʃənˌhʌntɪŋz/ | n. |  (通常用复数形式) 指多次或多人的寻宝/猎财行为 | Multiple instances of seeking wealth through opportunistic means. |  The Klondike Gold Rush saw countless fortune-huntings, with many prospectors returning home empty-handed. 克朗代克淘金热见证了无数次的寻宝活动，许多探矿者空手而归。 | synonyms:  gold rushes, treasure hunts | antonyms: investments, savings | noun
