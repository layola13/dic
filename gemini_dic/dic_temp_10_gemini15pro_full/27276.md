calumniate:/kəˈlʌmnieɪt/| v. |诽谤，造谣，中伤|to make false and malicious statements about someone in order to damage their reputation. |例句：He was calumniated by his political opponents. 他被他的政敌诽谤了。

例句：She was calumniated in the press. 她在媒体上被诽谤了。

近义词：slander, libel, defame, vilify, smear|反义词: praise, commend, laud|词性:动词

词源: From Latin *calumniārī*  "to accuse falsely, bring a false accusation," from *calumnia* "false accusation, trickery, chicanery," perhaps originally "verbal entrapment," related to *calvi* "to deceive," *callīdus* "cunning, sly."
