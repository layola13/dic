cirrocumulus:/ˌsɪroʊˈkjuːmjələs/| n. |卷积云|A type of high-altitude cloud characterized by small, white patches or ripples arranged in regular patterns, often resembling fish scales or a flock of sheep.  It is composed of ice crystals and indicates stable atmospheric conditions.|Look at the beautiful cirrocumulus clouds, they look like fish scales. 看看美丽的卷积云，它们看起来像鱼鳞。|The cirrocumulus clouds drifted across the sky, creating a mesmerizing display. 卷积云飘过天空，形成一幅迷人的景象。|近义词: mackerel sky (informal),|反义词: None|词性: Noun

Etymology:  From Latin *cirrus* ("curl of hair, wisp") + *cumulus* ("heap, pile").
