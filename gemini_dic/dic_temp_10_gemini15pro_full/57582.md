dramaturgically:/ˌdræməˈtɜːrdʒɪkli/ /ˌdrɑːməˈtɜːrdʒɪkli/
adv. |戏剧地，从戏剧构作角度| In a way that relates to the structure or composition of a dramatic work.  |The play was dramaturgically complex, weaving multiple timelines and perspectives. 这部戏在戏剧结构上很复杂，交织了多个时间线和视角。|  The director approached the scene dramaturgically, analyzing the characters' motivations and the underlying conflict. 导演从戏剧构作的角度处理这个场景，分析了人物的动机和潜在的冲突。| theatrically, dramatically | plainly, simply | adverb


Etymology:

From "dramaturgy" + "-ically".  "Dramaturgy" comes from the French "dramaturgie," ultimately from the Greek "δρᾶμα" (drama, "deed, act, play") + "-urgy" (from Greek "-ουργία," denoting "working," "activity," or "craft").
