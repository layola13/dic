mouse:/maʊs/ (plural mice /maɪs/) | n. |老鼠；鼠标；胆小羞怯的人|A small rodent with a pointed snout, small rounded ears, and a long, almost hairless tail. A hand-held device that controls the cursor on a computer screen.|The mouse scurried across the floor. 老鼠在地板上 scurried across the floor。|She moved the mouse to click on the icon. 她移动鼠标点击图标。|Synonyms: rodent, clicker (for computer mouse), timid person|Antonyms: lion (figuratively for a brave person), elephant (figuratively, for its size)|noun

mouse:/maʊz/ | v. |(usually with around/about) search stealthily; to hunt for something stealthily|To move around cautiously and quietly, especially when searching for something.|The cat was mousing around in the bushes. 猫在灌木丛中四处觅食。|He was mousing about for his glasses. 他正在四处寻找他的眼镜。|Synonyms: prowl, creep, sneak|Antonyms: stride, march, strut|verb
