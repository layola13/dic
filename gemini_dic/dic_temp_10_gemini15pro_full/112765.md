lubbard:/ˈlʌbərd/| n.|懒汉，笨蛋|A lazy, clumsy person.  A person who is idle and gluttonous.|He called his lazy roommate a lubbard.|Synonyms: loafer, idler, slug, sluggard, drone|Antonyms: hard worker, diligent person|noun

Etymology->
From Middle English *loberd*, from *lob* (lump) + *-ard* (pejorative suffix).  The original sense likely referred to a clumsy, lumpish person, later evolving to mean a lazy person.
