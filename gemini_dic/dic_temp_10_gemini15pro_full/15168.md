back-track: /ˈbækˌtræk/ (US) /ˈbæktræk/ (UK)| v. n.| 后退，折返；取消，撤回；| To return along the same path or route one has just taken; to reverse one's position or opinion. |  Example: We had to backtrack about a mile to find the turnoff. 我们不得不折返大约一英里才能找到岔路口。 | Synonyms: retreat, withdraw, reverse, retract | Antonyms: advance, proceed, continue | verb
