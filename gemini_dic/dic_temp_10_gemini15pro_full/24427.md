bristle:/ˈbrɪsəl/ (US), /ˈbrɪsl/ (UK)| n. v.|刚毛，鬃毛；竖起，发怒|A short, stiff hair, typically one of those on an animal's skin or a man's face.  To react angrily or defensively; to stand upright.|The cat's fur bristled when it saw the dog. 猫看到狗时毛都竖起来了。|近义词：hair, quill, spine, stand up, rise|反义词:smooth, lie down|词性:noun, verb
bristle:/ˈbrɪsəl/ (US), /ˈbrɪsl/ (UK)| v.|竖起，发怒|To react angrily or defensively; to stand upright.|He bristled at the suggestion that he was wrong.  他一听说是自己错了就勃然大怒。|近义词：react angrily, take offense|反义词:calm down, acquiesce|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bristel, *bristle, from Old English *byrst*, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *burstiz. Cognate with Dutch borstel (“brush”), German Borste (“bristle”).
