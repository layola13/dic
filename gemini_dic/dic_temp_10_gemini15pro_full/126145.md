mutinous:/ˈmjuːtɪnəs/ | adj. | 叛变的，反抗的，不服从的 | Refusing to obey the orders of a person in authority. | The mutinous sailors took control of the ship. 叛变的水手控制了这艘船。 | The mutinous soldiers refused to follow orders.  叛变的士兵拒绝服从命令。 | 近义词: rebellious, insubordinate, defiant, seditious | 反义词: obedient, loyal, compliant, submissive | 词性: adjective

mutiny:/ˈmjuːtəni/ | n. | 叛变，兵变 | An open rebellion against the proper authorities, especially by soldiers or sailors against their officers. | The mutiny was quickly suppressed by the captain. 叛乱很快就被船长镇压了。 | The sailors staged a mutiny against the cruel captain. 水手们发动了反对残忍船长的叛乱。 | 近义词: rebellion, revolt, uprising, insurrection | 反义词: obedience, loyalty, submission | 词性: noun


词源:
From Middle French *mutin* (“rebellious”), from Old French *mutin*, from the Italian *mutino*, which is of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Latin *movere* (“to move”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *(e)mey- (“to change, exchange, move”).
