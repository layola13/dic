crawler's:/ˈkrɔːlərz/| n.|爬行者(的), 匍匐动物(的)|Possession form of "crawler," referring to something belonging to or associated with a creature that crawls.|The crawler's legs were covered in mud. 爬行者的腿上沾满了泥。|近义词：creeping thing's, reptile's, insect's|反义词:flyer's, walker's, runner's|Noun, Possessive

Etymology:
From "crawl" + "-er" (agent suffix) + "-'s" (possessive suffix).  "Crawl" originates from Old Norse *krafla "to crawl, scratch."
