duomo:/ˈdwɔːmoʊ/ (US), /ˈdwəʊməʊ/ (UK)| n. |大教堂（尤指意大利的）|A cathedral, especially in Italy.  Specifically refers to the principal church of a diocese, often elaborately decorated and possessing historical significance.  The term derives directly from the Italian word "duomo," signifying "house" (Latin: domus), ultimately referring to the "house of God."|We visited the Duomo in Milan. 我们参观了米兰大教堂。| The Florence Duomo is renowned for its dome. 佛罗伦萨大教堂以其圆顶而闻名。|Synonyms: cathedral, basilica|Antonyms: chapel, shrine|noun
