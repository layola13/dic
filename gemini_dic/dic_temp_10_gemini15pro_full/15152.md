back-handed:/ˌbækˈhændɪd/ | adj. | 间接的，反讽的，虚情假意的；(网球等)反手的 | (of a remark) appearing complimentary but implying criticism or disapproval; delivered with the back of the hand. | He gave a back-handed compliment about her outfit. 他对她的服装给出了一个虚情假意的赞美。 | Synonyms: indirect, sarcastic, insincere, left-handed (for tennis) | Antonyms: direct, sincere, genuine, right-handed (for tennis) | adjective


back-handed:/ˌbækˈhændɪd/ | n. | 反手击球 | A stroke played with the back of the hand facing the direction of the stroke. | He hit a powerful backhanded down the line. 他沿着边线打出一个强有力的反手球。 | Synonyms: backhand | Antonyms: forehand | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "back" + "hand" + "-ed". Originally referring to a stroke in tennis or other racket sports made with the back of the hand facing the direction of the stroke. The figurative meaning of "indirect" or "insincere" developed later, perhaps from the idea of a blow delivered indirectly or deviously.
