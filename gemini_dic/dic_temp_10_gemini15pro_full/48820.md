defocalization:/diːfoʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 散焦，失焦，去中心化 | The act or process of defocusing something, or the state of being defocused. This can refer to a literal blurring of an image, or a metaphorical shift in attention or focus. | The photographer experimented with defocalization to create a dreamlike effect. 摄影师尝试散焦来营造梦幻般的效果。 | The defocalization of power in the company led to more autonomous teams. 公司权力下放导致团队更加自主。 |  近义词: blurring, diffusion, decentralization | 反义词: focusing, concentration, centralization | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From de- (prefix meaning "removal, reversal") + focalization (from focus).  The word "focus" ultimately comes from the Latin "focus" meaning "hearth, fireplace."
