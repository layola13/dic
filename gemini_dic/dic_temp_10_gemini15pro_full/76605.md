gallivanter:/ˌɡælɪˈvæntər/| n.|浪荡子，游荡者|A person who goes out frequently in search of pleasure or amusement, especially one who travels to many different places for this purpose.|He was known as a gallivanter, always off on some new adventure. 他以浪荡子闻名，总是去新的地方冒险。|Synonyms: gadabout, philanderer, playboy, adventurer, rover, wanderer|Antonyms: homebody, stay-at-home|Noun

gallivanting:/ˌɡælɪˈvæntɪŋ/| n. |游荡，闲逛|The act of gallivanting; going around from one place to another in search of pleasure or amusement.|She spent her summer gallivanting around Europe. 她夏天都在欧洲各地游荡。|Synonyms: gadding about, roaming, wandering, rambling|Antonyms: staying put, settling down|Noun

gallivant:/ˌɡælɪˈvænt/| v. |游荡，闲逛|To go around from one place to another in search of pleasure or amusement.|He's been gallivanting all over the world. 他一直在世界各地游荡。|Synonyms: gad about, roam, wander, ramble|Antonyms: stay put, settle down|Verb
