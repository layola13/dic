fickly:/ˈfɪk(ə)li/
adjective
反复无常地，易变地
Changing frequently, especially in mood or affection; capricious.
She acted fickly, one moment loving him, the next pushing him away. 她反复无常，一时爱他，一时又把他推开。
近义词:capricious, erratic, volatile, whimsical, mercurial
反义词:constant, steady, stable, resolute
词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From fickel + -ly. Fickle itself comes from Middle English fikel, probably from Old English *ficol, related to Old English befician "to deceive, cheat," of Germanic origin.
