absorbents:/əbˈzɔːrbənts/ /əbˈsɔːrbənts/
noun|吸收剂，吸收体|Materials that can soak up liquids or other substances.|The lab used highly absorbent cloths to clean up the spill. 实验室使用高吸水性布料来清理溢出的液体。
|Example:  Paper towels are common absorbents. 纸巾是常见的吸收剂。

Diapers contain super absorbent polymers. 尿布含有高吸水性聚合物。


|Synonyms:  absorber, sponge,  porous material|Antonyms:  impermeable, waterproof|词性: 名词(plural)

absorbant:/əbˈzɔːrbənt/ /əbˈsɔːrbənt/
adjective|吸收性的，能吸收的|Having the ability to absorb liquids or other substances.|Sponges are highly absorbent materials. 海绵是高吸水性材料。
|Example: The new towels are very absorbent. 新毛巾吸水性很好。

Charcoal is an absorbent material used in filters. 木炭是一种用于过滤器的吸附性材料。



|Synonyms:  permeable, porous, spongy|Antonyms:  impermeable, waterproof, non-absorbent|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *absorbēns*, present participle of absorbēre (“to swallow up”), from Latin ab (“from, away from”) + sorbēre (“to suck in, absorb”).

USAGE->
作为名词:
The chemist carefully selected the right absorbents for the experiment. = 化学家仔细地为实验选择了合适的吸收剂。
Various absorbents are used in cleaning up oil spills. = 清理石油泄漏使用各种吸收剂。

作为形容词:
An absorbent cloth is essential for cleaning up spills. = 吸水布对于清理溢出物至关重要。
The soil in this area is very absorbent. = 这个地区的土壤吸水性很强。
