fledge:/fleʤ/
verb
(使)长羽毛,(使)变得成熟
To develop feathers large enough for flight; to leave the nest after developing such feathers.  Also used metaphorically to describe reaching maturity or independence.
The young eagles fledged in late spring.  幼鹰在晚春长出了羽毛。
The students fledged and left the university to start their careers.  学生们羽翼丰满，离开了大学，开始了他们的职业生涯。
近义词:mature, develop, feather
反义词:immature, undeveloped
词性:v.


fledge:/fleʤ/
noun
初飞,羽毛丰满
The act of a young bird developing feathers and learning to fly; the stage at which a young bird has developed such feathers.  Can also refer to a young bird itself in this stage.
The fledgling robin hopped around the garden.  刚会飞的知更鸟在花园里跳来跳去。
Seeing the fledge of the young birds was a beautiful sight.  看到幼鸟羽毛丰满的景象真是美不胜收。
近义词:fledgling, chick
反义词:adult, mature
词性:n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *flycge, related to fly and flier.
