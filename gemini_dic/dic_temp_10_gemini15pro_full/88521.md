hit-and-miss:/ˌhɪt ən ˈmɪs/| adj.|时好时坏的；碰运气的；无计划的|Something that is hit-and-miss is sometimes successful and sometimes not, often because it is not carefully planned.|The restaurant's food is a bit hit-and-miss - sometimes it's great, other times it's awful. 这家餐馆的菜时好时坏——有时很棒，有时很糟糕。|近义词：random, haphazard, unpredictable, erratic|反义词：consistent, reliable, dependable|词性:形容词
hit-and-miss:/ˌhɪt ən ˈmɪs/| n.|碰运气；无计划|A hit-and-miss approach is one that is not carefully planned and therefore sometimes successful and sometimes not.|His investment strategy was rather hit-and-miss. 他的投资策略相当碰运气。|近义词：potluck, gamble, lottery|反义词：strategy, plan, system|词性:名词
