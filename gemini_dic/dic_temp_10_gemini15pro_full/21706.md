boarder:/ˈbɔːrdər/ (US) /ˈbɔːdə(r)/ (UK)| n.|寄宿生；付费膳宿者；登船者；边境居民|A person who rents a room and receives meals, typically in a private house; someone who boards a ship, train, or plane; someone who lives near a border.|例句：The boarder paid his rent weekly.  这位寄宿生每周支付房租。

例句：Boarders lined up to embark the cruise ship.  旅客们排队登上游轮。|近义词：lodger, resident, tenant, passenger |反义词: landlord, host |词性:noun

boarder:/ˈbɔːrdər/ (US) /ˈbɔːdə(r)/ (UK)| n.|边界；边境|The edge or boundary of something, especially a country or region.|例句：They crossed the boarder into Canada. 他们越过边界进入加拿大。

例句：The town lies on the boarder between two states.  这个小镇位于两个州的边界上。 |近义词：boundary, frontier, edge, limit |反义词: center, interior |词性:noun
