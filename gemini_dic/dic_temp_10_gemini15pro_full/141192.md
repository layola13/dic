oomiak: /ˈuːmiˌæk/ (US), /ˈuːmiæk/ (UK)| n. |尤米亚克, 一种爱斯基摩人的大皮船|An open Eskimo boat, often large, made of skins stretched over a wooden frame, and propelled by paddles.|The oomiak carried the women, children, and household goods while the men hunted from kayaks.|Synonym: umiak, skin boat|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps kayak as a smaller, contrasting vessel)|noun
