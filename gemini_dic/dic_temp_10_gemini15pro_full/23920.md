breakout:/ˈbreɪkaʊt/| n.|爆发，突围，越狱|A sudden escape from prison or confinement; a sudden increase or occurrence, typically of something unpleasant like a disease or war.|例句：The breakout from the prison was planned for months.  越狱计划策划了数月。

例句：There was a breakout of measles in the school. 学校爆发了麻疹。|近义词：escape, eruption, outbreak|反义词:containment, suppression|词性:noun
break out:/breɪk aʊt/| v.|爆发，突然发生|To suddenly start or erupt.|例句：War broke out in 1939. 1939年战争爆发了。

例句：A fire broke out in the kitchen. 厨房里发生了火灾。|近义词：erupt, burst forth, flare up|反义词:die down, subside|词性:verb
