buoyancy:/ˈbɔɪənsi/ | n. | 浮力，轻快，乐观 |The ability to float in water or air. Also, a feeling of cheerfulness and optimism. | The life jacket provided the necessary buoyancy to keep him afloat. 救生衣提供了必要的浮力让他漂浮在水面上。| synonyms: floatation, lightness, cheerfulness, optimism | antonyms: heaviness, depression, pessimism | noun

buoyant:/ˈbɔɪənt/ | adj. | 有浮力的，轻快的，乐观的 | Able to float easily; cheerful and optimistic. |  The buoyant economy led to increased consumer spending.  繁荣的经济导致消费者支出增加。| synonyms: floating, lighthearted, cheerful, optimistic | antonyms: heavy, depressed, pessimistic | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *boiant (present participle of boier "to float"), from Late Latin boiare, of uncertain origin, probably from a Germanic source akin to Old Norse búa "to dwell, live, reside."  The sense of "cheerful" developed from the idea of "floating" or "rising."
