constitutionalist:/ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəlɪst/ | /ˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃənəlɪst/ | n. | 宪政主义者；立宪主义者 | A person who strongly supports or advocates for a constitution, especially adherence to its principles. |  The constitutionalists argued against the expansion of executive power. 宪政主义者反对扩大行政权力。

constitutionalist:/ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəlɪst/ | /ˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃənəlɪst/ | adj. | 宪法的；拥护宪法的 | Relating to or advocating for a constitution. | The constitutionalist movement gained momentum after the king's abuses of power.  拥护宪法的运动在国王滥用权力后获得了动力。
