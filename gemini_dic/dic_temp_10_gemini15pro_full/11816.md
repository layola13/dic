arrayed:/əˈreɪd/| adj.|排列的，布置的，穿着盛装的|Dressed or arranged in a particular way, often impressively or formally.|例句：The troops were arrayed for battle. 部队已整装待发。|近义词：arranged, assembled, dressed, attired|反义词：disordered, scattered|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *araien, *arrayen, from Old French *areer, *araier (“to array, arrange, order, prepare, dress, adorn”), from Vulgar Latin *arredare, from Latin ad (“to”) + *redare (from re- (“again”) + dare (“to give”)).

There are not common established phrases using "arrayed" in the same way as simpler words.  It is more often used in formal or literary contexts. However, it can be used in constructions like:

1. arrayed in (穿着...): arrayed in a purple robe (身穿紫色长袍)
2. arrayed against (对抗...): arrayed against the enemy (对抗敌人)
