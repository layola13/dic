legatee:/ˈlɛɡəˌtiː/ /ləˈɡeɪti/
n.
遗产继承人
A person who receives a legacy (money or property) through a will.
My aunt named me as her legatee in her will. 我的姑姑在遗嘱中指定我为她的遗产继承人。
The legatee received a substantial sum of money.  这位遗产继承人得到了一大笔钱。

近义词:inheritor, beneficiary, heir
反义词:testator (遗嘱人)
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French legat, from Latin legatus (“ambassador, envoy, legate”), past participle of legare (“to send as an ambassador, bequeath, depute, appoint”), from lex, legis (“law”).  The meaning shifted from "papal legate" to "one to whom a legacy is bequeathed."
