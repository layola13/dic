concubinage:/kɒnˈkjuːbɪnɪdʒ/| n.|姘居，妾侍(制度)|The state of being a concubine; the practice of keeping a concubine.  The practice or state of cohabitation without legal or formal marriage.|In some cultures, concubinage was an accepted social institution. 在某些文化中，姘居是一种被社会接受的制度。|Synonyms: cohabitation, living together, common-law marriage|Antonyms: marriage, wedlock|noun

concubinate:/kɒnˈkjuːbɪneɪt/| v.|姘居，做妾|To live together as or with a concubine; to cohabit without being legally married.|He concubinates with several women. 他与几个女人姘居。|Synonyms: cohabit, live together|Antonyms: marry, wed|verb
