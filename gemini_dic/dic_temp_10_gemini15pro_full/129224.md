ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *nokke*, *nok*, from Old English *nocc* (“notch, corner, angle”), from Proto-Germanic *knakkaz* (“something bent, crooked”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kneh₂ǵ- (“to bend, turn”). Cognate with Middle Low German *knocke* (“knot, joint”), Dutch *nok* (“ridge, top, cog”), German *Knochen* (“bone”).
