hobbly:/ˈhɑːbli/ (US), /ˈhɒbli/ (UK)| adj.|跛行地，蹒跚地|Moving with difficulty, especially because of an injured leg or foot.  Characterized by a limping or awkward gait.|The old man walked hobbly down the street. 老人蹒跚地走在街上。|近义词：limping, halting, faltering, shuffling|反义词:nimbly, agilely|词性:adjective
词源:From hobble (v.) + -ly (adv. suffix). Ultimately from Middle English hobelen.
