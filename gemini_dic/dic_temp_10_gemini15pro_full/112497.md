louringly:/ˈlaʊərɪŋli/ | adv. | 阴沉地，虎视眈眈地 |  In a dark and threatening way, often suggesting something bad is about to happen. | The storm clouds gathered louringly above the town.  暴风云阴沉地聚集在小镇上空。|  The guard dog stared louringly at the stranger.  看门狗虎视眈眈地盯着陌生人。| synonyms: menacingly, threateningly, ominously | antonyms: brightly, cheerfully, amicably | adverb

Etymology:
From the verb 'lour' (meaning to frown or look angry) + '-ing' (present participle suffix) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix). 'Lour' itself originates from Middle English 'louren,' likely related to Old Norse 'lúra' (to lie in wait).
