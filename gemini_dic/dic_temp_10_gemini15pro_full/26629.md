cackle:/ˈkæk(ə)l/| vi. n.|咯咯地笑； cackle (n.) 咯咯声|To laugh in a loud, harsh way, often suggesting mockery or triumph. (v.)  A loud, harsh laugh. (n.)|The witch cackled gleefully as she stirred her potion.  女巫一边搅拌着她的药水，一边高兴地咯咯地笑着。|近义词：chuckle, guffaw, laugh|反义词:cry, sob, whimper|词性:verb, noun
Etymology:  From Middle English *kakelen, of imitative origin.  Related to Dutch kakelen (“to cackle”), German gackern (“to cackle”).
