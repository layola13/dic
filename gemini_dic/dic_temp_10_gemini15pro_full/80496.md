good-deal:/ˈɡʊd ˈdiːl/| n.|划算的交易；便宜货；大量|A bargain; something bought or sold for a lower price than its value.  A large amount or number of something.|例句：I got a good deal on this car. 我这辆车买得很划算。

例句：There's a good deal of work to be done.  有很多工作要做。|近义词：bargain, steal, lots, plenty|反义词：rip-off, raw deal, little, few|词性:名词

good-deal:/ˌɡʊdˈdiːl/ | adv. | 相当多地；大量地 | Considerably; to a significant extent. | 例句：I slept a good deal better last night. 我昨晚睡得好多了。

例句：He travels a good deal for work. 他因工作原因经常出差。 | 近义词：considerably, substantially, a lot, much | 反义词：slightly, a little, hardly | 词性:副词


词源:  "Good" comes from Old English "gōd." "Deal" comes from Old English "dǣl" meaning "part, portion, share."  The combined phrase "good deal" originally referred to a significant portion or share, evolving to mean a bargain or a large quantity.
