altar: /ˈɔːltər/ (US) /ˈɔːltə(r)/ (UK)| n. |祭坛，圣坛|a raised structure or place on which sacrifices are offered or incense is burned in religious worship. |The priest stood before the altar.  神父站在祭坛前。|The bride and groom exchanged vows at the altar. 新娘和新郎在圣坛前交换誓言。|近义词: shrine, sanctuary|反义词: None|词性: noun

词源: From Old French *alter, auter, from Latin altare "high altar," noun use of neuter of adjective altaris "of a high altar," from altus "high."

PHRASE->
lead to the altar:  带领走向圣坛（通常指结婚）
He led her to the altar. 他把她领到圣坛前（结婚）。
lay at the altar of:  为了...而牺牲
He laid his career at the altar of family. 他为了家庭牺牲了自己的事业。
worship at the altar of:  极度崇拜；把...奉为神明
Many people worship at the altar of money. 许多人把金钱奉为神明。
approach the altar:  走近祭坛
The bride approached the altar with her father.  新娘和她的父亲一起走向祭坛。
altar boy:  祭坛侍童
The altar boy helped the priest during the mass.  祭坛侍童在弥撒期间帮助神父。
