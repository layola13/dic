admonitions: /ˌædməˈnɪʃənz/ | n. | 劝告，警告，训诫 |  Authoritative counsel, advice, or warnings, especially with regard to conduct.  | The teacher's admonitions to the class to be quiet went unheeded. 老师告诫全班同学安静下来的警告被忽视了。

The parent offered admonitions about the dangers of smoking. 家长提出了关于吸烟危害的警告。 | 近义词: warnings, cautions, advice, counsel, reproofs | 反义词: praise, commendation, encouragement | 词性: 名词(plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin admonitionem (nominative admonitio) "a reminding, warning," noun of action from past-participle stem of admonere "to remind, advise, warn," from ad- "to" + monere "to warn."


USAGE->
There aren't many verb conjugations with "admonitions" as it's a noun.  However, it can be used in different sentence structures:

陈述句:
His admonitions were ignored. = 他的劝告被忽视了。
Her frequent admonitions about safety finally sank in. = 她经常关于安全的告诫最终被理解了。


疑问句:
Were his admonitions helpful? = 他的劝告有帮助吗？
Did you heed her admonitions? = 你听从了她的劝告吗？


否定句:
His admonitions were not taken seriously. = 他的劝告没有被认真对待。
The students didn't listen to the teacher's admonitions. = 学生们没有听老师的劝告。



PHRASE->
heed admonitions = 注意劝告
ignore admonitions = 忽视劝告
offer admonitions = 提供劝告
frequent admonitions = 频繁的劝告
stern admonitions = 严厉的劝告
