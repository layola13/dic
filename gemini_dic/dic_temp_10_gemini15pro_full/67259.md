expurgation:/ˌekspərˈɡeɪʃn/ | n. | 删除，删节，净化 | the removal of matter thought to be objectionable or unsuitable from a book or account | The editor made several expurgations to the novel before its publication. 编辑在小说出版前对它做了几处删节。 |  censorship, bowdlerization, purging | inclusion, addition | noun

expurgations: /ˌekspərˈɡeɪʃnz/ | n. | 删除，删节，净化（复数） |  plural form of expurgation, referring to multiple instances of removing objectionable content. |  Several expurgations were made to the text to make it suitable for children.  为了使文本适合儿童阅读，进行了几处删节。 | censorship, bowdlerization, purging | inclusion, addition | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *expurgationem,  from Latin expurgare "to cleanse, purify,"  from ex- "out" + purgare "to cleanse."
