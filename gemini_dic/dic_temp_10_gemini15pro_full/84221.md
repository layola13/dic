half-savage:/ˈhæf ˈsævɪdʒ/ | adj. | 半野蛮的；未开化的 | Partly or imperfectly civilized; barbarous in some degree. |  The explorer encountered a half-savage tribe deep in the jungle. 探险家在丛林深处遇到一个半野蛮的部落。

half-savage:/ˈhæf ˈsævɪdʒ/ | n. | 半野蛮人 | A person who is partly or imperfectly civilized. |  He lived as a half-savage, relying on hunting and foraging. 他过着半野蛮人的生活，依靠狩猎和觅食为生。


Etymology:
"half" (Old English healf) + "savage" (Middle English sauvage, from Old French sauvage, from Late Latin salvaticus "wild", from Latin silvaticus "of the woods", from silva "forest")

Synonyms:
uncivilized, barbarous, primitive, untamed, wild

Antonyms:
civilized, cultured, refined, sophisticated, tame
