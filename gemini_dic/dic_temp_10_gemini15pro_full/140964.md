one-upsmanship: /ˌwʌnˈʌpmənʃɪp/
one-upsmanship: n.
one-upsmanship: 超越他人，胜人一筹; 力争胜过他人
one-upsmanship: The practice of successively outdoing a competitor.  It describes the often subtle and indirect ways that people try to gain an advantage or appear superior to others.
one-upsmanship: He’s always trying to one-up me, but I’m not going to let him. 他总是想胜过我，但我不会让他得逞的。
one-upsmanship: She told a story about her travels, clearly as an act of one-upsmanship. 她讲述了她旅行的故事，显然是为了胜过别人。
one-upsmanship: one-upmanship, competitiveness, rivalry, vying, outdoing
one-upsmanship: cooperation, collaboration, modesty, humility
one-upsmanship: noun
