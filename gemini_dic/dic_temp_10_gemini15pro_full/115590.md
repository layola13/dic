marauding:/məˈrɔːdɪŋ/| adj.|掠夺的，抢劫的|Going about in search of things to steal or people to attack.  Characterized by plundering and pillaging.|例句：Marauding gangs terrorized the village. 掠夺成性的匪帮恐吓了整个村庄。

例句：The marauding army pillaged the conquered city.  这支四处劫掠的军队洗劫了被征服的城市。|近义词：pillaging, plundering, raiding, looting|反义词：protecting, defending|词性：形容词

marauder:/məˈrɔːdər/| n.|掠夺者，抢劫者|Someone who goes around searching for things to steal or people to attack.|例句：The marauders stole everything of value. 掠夺者偷走了所有值钱的东西。

例句：The villagers feared the marauders who roamed the countryside. 村民们害怕在乡间游荡的掠夺者。|近义词：raider, looter, plunderer, pillager|反义词：protector, defender|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *marauder*, "to raid," from *maraud*, "rogue," of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Old French *marrir*, "to grieve," from Latin *marcere*, "to wither, droop."  The sense shifted from "rogue" to "raider" in the 17th century.
