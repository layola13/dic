half-colored:/ˈhæfˈkʌlərd/
adjective
混血的；肤色较浅的
Having parents of different races, especially one white and one Black.  Historically, often used in a derogatory way to refer to people of mixed Black and white ancestry.
例句：In the 19th century, "half-colored" was a common, but offensive, term in the United States. 19世纪，“half-colored”在美国是一个常见但冒犯性的词语。

例句：She was born in a time when being half-colored carried a heavy social stigma.  她出生在一个混血身份背负沉重社会污名的时代。
近义词：mixed-race, biracial, multiracial
反义词: unmixed, pure-blooded (these are outdated and potentially offensive)
词性: adjective

half-colored:/ˈhæfˈkʌlərd/
adjective
部分着色的
Partially colored; not completely colored.  This usage is less common when referring to people and more often applies to objects.
例句：The artist presented a half-colored canvas, leaving the rest for the audience to complete. 艺术家展示了一幅半上色的画布，剩下的留给观众完成。

例句：The butterfly had half-colored wings, one side vibrant and the other pale. 这只蝴蝶的翅膀一半是彩色的，一面鲜艳，另一面苍白。
近义词：partially colored, partly colored
反义词: fully colored, completely colored
词性: adjective
