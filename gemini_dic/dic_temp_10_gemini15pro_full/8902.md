antigen:/ˈæn.tɪ.dʒən/ | n. |抗原| Any substance that causes your immune system to produce antibodies against it.  It may be a foreign substance from the environment, such as chemicals, bacteria, viruses, or pollen.  It may also be formed within the body, as with bacterial toxins or tissue cells.| The body produces antibodies to fight off the antigen.  身体产生抗体来对抗抗原。| synonyms: immunogen,  toxin | antonyms: antibody | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek *ἀντίγονος (antígonos) "produced against, born against".  From ἀντί (antí) "against"  + γίγνομαι (gígnomai) "to be born, to become"
