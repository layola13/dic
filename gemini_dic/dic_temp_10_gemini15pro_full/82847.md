gulfy:/ˈɡʌlfi/| adj.|贪婪的，狼吞虎咽的；像海湾一样大的|Having a large capacity or being insatiable, often in relation to food or drink; resembling a gulf in size or depth.|He took gulfy bites of the sandwich, clearly ravenous. 他狼吞虎咽地吃着三明治，显然是饿坏了。|近义词：greedy, ravenous, voracious|反义词:abstemious, moderate|词性:形容词
Etymology:  From "gulf" + "-y" (suffix forming adjectives). "Gulf" ultimately derives from the Greek word "kólpos," meaning "bosom, bay, gulf."
