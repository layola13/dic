entorganism:/ɛntɔːrɡənɪzəm/ | n. |内共生体；体内共生生物|An organism that lives within the body or cells of another organism, typically providing a benefit to the host.| Example: Mitochondria and chloroplasts are believed to have originated as bacterial entorganisms. 线粒体和叶绿体被认为起源于细菌内共生体。| Example:  The entorganism provided essential nutrients to its host.  内共生体为宿主提供必要的营养。|Synonyms: endosymbiont, symbiont| Antonyms: parasite (sometimes, depending on the relationship)|Noun
