magazined:/ˈmæɡəziːnd/
|v.| 已装填弹药的；已装填胶卷的 | filled with ammunition or film; often used in combination| The soldiers were magazined and ready for battle. 士兵们装填好了弹药，准备战斗。 | loaded, armed, charged, primed | empty, unloaded | verb

Etymology:

From magazine (n.) + -ed. The noun "magazine" originally referred to a storehouse or depot, especially for military supplies.  This sense extended to a chamber in a firearm holding cartridges, and from there to the verb meaning "to furnish with ammunition from a magazine."
