briskly:/ˈbrɪskli/| adv.|轻快地，活泼地，迅速地|In a quick and energetic way.|She walked briskly down the street. 她轻快地走在街上。|quickly, rapidly, swiftly, energetically|slowly, sluggishly|adverb
Etymology->
From brisk + -ly.  Brisk comes from Middle English *brisk, from Anglo-Norman *brusc, from Old French *brusque (compare modern French brusque), ultimately of uncertain origin. Perhaps related to Italian brusco "sour, tart" or to Gaulish *brusco.
