begrudging:/bɪˈɡrʌdʒɪŋ/| adj.|吝啬的，勉强的，不情愿的|Given reluctantly or resentfully.  Showing unwillingness to give or allow something.|He gave a begrudging apology. 他很不情愿地道歉了。|Synonyms: reluctant, unwilling, resentful, grudging, envious|Antonyms: willing, generous, eager, enthusiastic|adjective


begrudge:/bɪˈɡrʌdʒ/| vt.|嫉妒，羡慕；吝惜，舍不得给|To envy someone the possession or enjoyment of something. To give reluctantly or resentfully.|I don't begrudge her the success she's had. 我并不嫉妒她的成功。 He begrudges spending money on anything other than food.  除了食物，他什么钱都舍不得花。|Synonyms: envy, resent, grudge, covet|Antonyms: grant, allow, give freely|verb
