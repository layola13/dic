haquebut:/ˈhækˌbʌt/ | n. | 手铳，钩銃 | A type of portable firearm, a heavy musket, used from the 15th to the 17th centuries.  A forerunner of the musket. | The soldiers marched into battle, their haquebuts ready to fire. 士兵们带着准备开火的手铳进入战场。|  arquebus, handgonne, musket | rifle, cannon | noun
Etymology: From Middle French *hacquebute*, from Old French *haquebute*, ultimately from Middle Dutch *hakebusse*, from *hake* (hook) + *busse* (box, gun).
