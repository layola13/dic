dilettantism:/ˌdɪləˈtænˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 业余爱好；浅尝辄止；玩票 | The superficial, unserious, and amateurish pursuit of an art, especially the fine arts.  A dabbling or superficial interest in an art or a branch of knowledge. |  His dilettantism in painting was evident in his clumsy brushstrokes.  他在绘画方面的业余爱好从他笨拙的笔触中可见一斑。

His knowledge of philosophy was mere dilettantism. 他对哲学的了解仅仅是浅尝辄止。 | 近义词：amateurism, superficiality, dabbling | 反义词：professionalism, expertise, mastery | 词性: noun


dilettante:/ˌdɪləˈtænti/ | n. | 业余爱好者；浅尝辄止的人 | A person who cultivates an area of interest, such as the arts, without real commitment or knowledge.  Someone with a superficial and unserious interest in a subject. | He was a dilettante in music, attending concerts but never practicing an instrument. 他是一个音乐爱好者，经常参加音乐会，但从未练习过乐器。

She considered herself a dilettante artist, painting occasionally for pleasure. 她认为自己是一个业余艺术家，偶尔画画是为了娱乐。 | 近义词：amateur, dabbler, trifler | 反义词：professional, expert, master | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *dilettante*, from *dilettare* "to delight," from Latin *delectare* "to allure, delight."
