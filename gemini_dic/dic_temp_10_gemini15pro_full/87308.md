hermodactyl:/ˌhɜːrməˈdæktɪl/| n. |秋水仙 |A bulbous plant of the lily family (Colchicum autumnale), native to Eurasia, whose dried corms have been used medicinally since ancient times.  Also refers to the dried corm itself, historically used as a purgative and to treat gout. |The physician prescribed hermodactyl for the patient's gout. 医生给病人开了秋水仙治疗痛风。|Synonyms: meadow saffron, autumn crocus|Antonyms: N/A|noun
