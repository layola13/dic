lockup: /ˈlɒkʌp/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkʌp/ (US) | n. | 监禁；拘留所；锁闭 |  A place where people are locked in, especially a prison or jail; the act of locking something securely. |  The suspect was held in lockup overnight. 嫌疑人被关押了一夜。 The bank vault has a secure lockup system.  银行金库有一个安全的锁闭系统。 | 近义词: jail, prison, detention, confinement | 反义词: release, freedom | 词性: 名词

lockup: /ˈlɒkʌp/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkʌp/ (US) | n. | (尤指公司合并或股票发行前的)冻结期 | A period during which assets or shares cannot be traded. | There's a six-month lockup period after the company goes public. 公司上市后有六个月的锁定期。 Investors are subject to a lockup period of 90 days. 投资者需要遵守90天的冻结期。| 近义词: moratorium, freeze | 反义词: trading, exchange | 词性: 名词

词源:  From the verb "lock up," meaning to fasten or secure something with a lock.  "Lock" originates from Old English *loc, related to Old High German loh. "Up" intensifies the action of locking.
