mooring's:/ˈmʊərɪŋz/ |n.| 系泊设备；系泊处 |A mooring is a place where a boat or ship is secured, or the ropes, chains, or anchors used to secure it. | The ship was secured to its moorings.  这艘船被固定在它的系泊处。| synonyms: anchorages, berths, docks | antonyms:  unmooring, casting off | noun

mooring:/ˈmʊərɪŋ/ |n.| 系泊；停泊 |The act of securing a boat or ship. | The mooring was difficult due to the strong currents. 由于强劲的水流，系泊很困难。| synonyms: anchoring, berthing, docking | antonyms: unmooring, casting off | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *morynge, from Old English *mōrung, from the verb *mōrian (“to moor”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *mōrijaną (“to moor”), from Proto-Indo-European *mer- (“to bind, tie”).
