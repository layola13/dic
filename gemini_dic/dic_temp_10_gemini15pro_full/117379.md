mediatorship:/ˌmiːdiˈeɪtərʃɪp/
n.|调解；调停；斡旋|The act of mediating; arbitration.  The role or position of a mediator.|例句：The UN played a crucial role in the mediatorship between the warring factions. 联合国在交战派别之间的调解中发挥了关键作用。

例句：Her mediatorship was essential in resolving the conflict. 她的调解在解决冲突中至关重要。
|近义词：arbitration, conciliation, intervention, negotiation|反义词：aggravation, escalation, incitement|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *mediatourship*, from *mediatour* (“mediator”) + *-ship*.  *Mediatour* comes from Old French *mediateur*, from Latin *mediātor*, from *mediāre* (“to be in the middle, mediate”), from *medius* (“middle”).
