concentrate:/ˈkɒnsəntreɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsəntreɪt/ (US)| v. n.| 集中，浓缩，浓缩物 | To focus all one's attention on something; to bring or come together at a single point; a substance made by removing water or other diluting agent; |  I need to concentrate on my work. 我需要集中精力工作。| focus, direct, condense | distract, disperse | verb
concentrate:/ˈkɒnsəntreɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsəntreɪt/ (US)| n.| 浓缩物 | A substance made by removing water or other diluting agent; |  Orange juice concentrate is cheaper than fresh juice. 浓缩橙汁比鲜榨橙汁便宜。| solution, extract, essence | dilution | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin concentratus, past participle of concentrare "to bring to a center," from com- "together" + centrum "center."
