cutout:/ˈkʌtaʊt/ (US), /ˈkʌtaʊt/ (UK)| n.|剪纸；镂空；断电；(引擎)熄火；(美)硬纸板模型|A shape cut out of paper or other material. A piece of material removed by cutting, especially a section removed from a garment or photograph.  A device that interrupts an electrical circuit if excessive current flows.|She made a cutout of a star. 她剪了一个星星形状的剪纸。|近义词: silhouette, shape, stencil, template, model, figure|反义词: whole, complete|词性:noun

cutout:/ˈkʌtaʊt/ (US), /ˈkʌtaʊt/ (UK)| adj.|剪下的；镂空的|Made by cutting.|A cutout design. 一个镂空图案。|近义词: excised, removed|反义词: intact, whole|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "cut out" (phrasal verb), from "cut" + "out." "Cut" has origins in Proto-Germanic *kut-, and "out" comes from Old English ūt.
