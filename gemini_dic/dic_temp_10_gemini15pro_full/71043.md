first-class:/ˈfɜːst ˈklɑːs/ (US) /ˈfɜːst ˈklɑːs/ (UK)| adj. n.|一流的，顶级的；头等舱；|Of the highest quality; excellent. first-class:/ˈfɜːst ˈklɑːs/ (US) /ˈfɜːst ˈklɑːs/ (UK)| n.|头等舱，一流的|The highest quality or most luxurious class of accommodation, especially on a train, ship, or plane.|They flew first-class to London. 他们坐头等舱飞往伦敦。|Synonyms: premium class, luxury class|Antonyms: economy class, second class|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "first" (meaning foremost, highest in rank) + "class" (meaning a category or grade).  The term began to be used in the mid-19th century, particularly in relation to travel accommodations.
