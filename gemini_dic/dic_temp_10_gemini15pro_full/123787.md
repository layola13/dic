mootness:/ˈmuːtnɪs/| n. |无实际意义；可争辩性；未决状态|The mootness of the question makes further discussion unnecessary. 这个问题已无实际意义，无需继续讨论。|例句：The case was dismissed due to mootness.  这个案子由于失去时效而被驳回。

例句：The mootness of the issue rendered the debate pointless.  这个问题已无意义，使得辩论毫无意义。

近义词：irrelevance, immateriality, academicness|反义词:relevance, significance, importance|词性:名词

moot:/muːt/| adj. |无实际意义的；可争辩的；未决的|Whether the policy is effective is a moot point. 这项政策是否有效是一个有争议的问题。|例句：The question of his guilt is now moot, as he has confessed.  由于他已经认罪，他是否有罪的问题现在已经没有实际意义了。


例句：It's a moot point whether he would have won the election anyway.  无论如何他是否会赢得选举现在都已无关紧要。|近义词：debatable, arguable, disputable|反义词:undisputable, settled, resolved|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mōt (noun), (ge)mōtian (verb) meaning "meeting, assembly," related to "meet."  The original sense referred to a meeting or assembly for discussion and debate, especially of legal matters. Over time, the meaning shifted to describe matters debated at such meetings, and eventually to matters that are debatable or of no practical significance.
