bauble:/ˈbɔːbl/ (US) /ˈbɔːbəl/ (UK)| n. | 小饰品；廉价珠宝；华而不实的东西 | A small, showy trinket or decoration, often inexpensive and not of high quality.  It can also refer to something superficially attractive but ultimately worthless. | She wore a bracelet covered in glittering baubles. 她戴着一个镶满闪亮小饰品的手镯。

The politician's promises were just baubles to attract voters.  这位政客的承诺只是吸引选民的华而不实的东西。 | trinket, ornament, knick-knack, gewgaw | valuable, substantial, significant | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *baube*, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Latin *balbus* (stammering, babbling), suggesting something light and frivolous.
