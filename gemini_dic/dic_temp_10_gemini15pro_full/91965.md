hypermorality:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɔˈrælɪti/ | n. | 过度道德，超级道德 |  An excessive adherence to moral principles, often to the point of being self-righteous or judgmental.  It implies a rigid and inflexible application of moral rules, sometimes beyond what is reasonable or necessary. |  His hypermorality made him unbearable to be around; he constantly criticized everyone else's behavior. 他过度的道德感让他令人难以忍受；他不断地批评其他人的行为。 |  He viewed her strict adherence to the rules as hypermorality, bordering on fanaticism. 他认为她严格遵守规则是过度道德，近乎狂热。 |  rigidity, moralism, puritanism, sanctimony |  immorality, amorality, laxity | noun
