normal:/ˈnɔːrməl/ (US) /ˈnɔːm(ə)l/ (UK)| adj. n.|正常的，标准的；法线；常态|Conforming to a standard; usual, typical or expected.|例句：It's normal to feel tired after a long day.  长时间工作后感到疲倦是正常的。|近义词：usual, typical, standard, regular, ordinary|反义词：abnormal, unusual, atypical, irregular, exceptional|词性:形容词

normal:/ˈnɔːrməl/ (US) /ˈnɔːm(ə)l/ (UK)| n.|常态，标准；法线|The usual, typical or expected state or condition.|例句：Things quickly returned to normal after the storm.  暴风雨过后，一切很快恢复正常。|近义词：standard, average, norm|反义词：abnormality, exception|词性:名词

normals:/ˈnɔːrməlz/ (US) /ˈnɔːm(ə)lz/ (UK)| n.|标准，常态；法线（复数）|The usual, typical or expected states or conditions.|例句：The doctor said all my vitals were within normals. 医生说我所有的生命体征都正常。|近义词：standards, averages, norms|反义词：abnormalities, exceptions|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *normalis (“made according to a carpenter's square”), from Latin norma (“carpenter's square, rule, pattern”).
