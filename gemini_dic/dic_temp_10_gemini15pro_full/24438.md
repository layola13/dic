bristling:/ˈbrɪslɪŋ/ | adj. | 竖立的，发怒的，戒备的 | Covered with or characterized by bristles; showing anger or defensiveness. |  The cat, bristling with fear, backed away slowly.  这只猫吓得毛都竖起来了，慢慢地后退。| 近义词: prickly, spiky, indignant, furious | 反义词: smooth, calm, peaceful | 词性: adjective

bristle:/ˈbrɪsl/ | n. | 短而硬的毛；| A short, stiff hair. | The pig's bristles were thick and coarse. 猪的鬃毛又粗又硬。| 近义词: hair, quill | 反义词: -- | 词性: noun

bristle:/ˈbrɪsl/ | v. | （毛发）竖立；发怒；充满 | (of hair or fur) stand erect; react angrily or defensively; be full of. |  He bristled at the suggestion that he was wrong. 他对认为他错了的建议感到愤怒。| 近义词:  rise, stand up, bridle, fume | 反义词:  smooth, calm, relax | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bristelen, frequentative of brisen, from Old English *byrstlan, frequentative of berstan (“to burst”). Cognate with German bersten (“to burst”).  The original sense was "to stand stiffly erect, like bristles."
