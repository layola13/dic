eccentric:/ɪkˈsentrɪk/ /ekˈsentrɪk/ | adj. |古怪的，反常的；不同圆心的| deviating from the recognized or customary character, practice, etc.; irregular; odd; peculiar;  not having the same center|  例句：He was a kind but eccentric man. 他是一个善良但古怪的人。

例句：Her eccentric behaviour made her the talk of the town. 她古怪的行为使她成为全城的谈论话题。|近义词：peculiar, odd, strange, unusual, quirky, idiosyncratic|反义词：normal, conventional, ordinary, usual|词性: adjective

eccentricity:/ˌeksɛnˈtrɪsɪti/ /ˌeksenˈtrɪsədi/ | n. |古怪；反常；偏心距| the quality of being eccentric; deviation from the normal or customary;  the amount by which something is eccentric|例句：His eccentricity was well known. 他的古怪是众所周知的。

例句：The eccentricity of the Earth's orbit causes the seasons to change. 地球轨道的偏心率导致季节变化。|近义词：oddity, peculiarity, strangeness, quirkiness|反义词：normality, conventionality, ordinariness|词性: noun
