exorbitantly:/ɪɡˈzɔːrbɪtəntli/
|adv.|过高地；过分地；（价格）昂贵地|
In an exorbitant manner; excessively.  Charging an exorbitant price means charging a much higher price than is reasonable or usual.|
The hotel charged exorbitantly for phone calls. 这家旅馆的电话收费过高。
The car was exorbitantly priced. 这辆车的价格过高。|
近义词：excessively, unreasonably, outrageously|
反义词：reasonably, moderately, affordably|
词性: 副词

Etymology:
From Late Latin exorbitant-, exorbitans, present participle of exorbitare "to deviate, go out of the track," from ex- "out of" + orbita "track, rut," from orbis "circle, wheel, orbit." The original sense in English (early 15c.) was literal, "deviating from a circular path." The figurative sense of "exceeding proper or usual bounds" is by 1640s.
