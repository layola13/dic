backbencher:/ˈbækˌbɛntʃər/| n.|后座议员；普通议员|A member of Parliament who does not hold a government post and sits on the back benches.  指坐在后排席位，没有政府职务的议员。|例句：The backbencher voiced his concerns about the new policy.  这位后座议员表达了他对新政策的担忧。

例句：Backbenchers often have more freedom to speak their minds than ministers.  后座议员通常比部长们有更大的言论自由。|近义词：rank-and-file member, ordinary member|反义词：frontbencher, minister, government official|词性:noun
