implacablenesses:/ɪmˈplækəbəlˌnɛsɪz/| n. |不妥协，不宽容，无情|The implacability of her enemy frightened her.  (她敌人的无情让她感到害怕。)|例句：The implacabenesses of the dictator led to countless atrocities. (独裁者的不妥协导致了无数的暴行。)|近义词：relentlessness, inflexibility, ruthlessness|反义词：mercy, leniency, compassion|词性:名词
词源: From implacable + -ness. Implacable from Late Middle English: from Old French, or from late Latin implacabilis, from in- ‘not’ + placabilis ‘able to be appeased’ (from placare ‘to appease’).
