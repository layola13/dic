cephalanthium:/ˌsefəˈlænθiəm/ | n. | 头状花序 | A type of inflorescence (flower cluster) in which the flowers are densely packed together on a receptacle, forming a head-like structure.  Similar to a composite flower head but without the distinction between ray and disc florets. | The buttonbush (Cephalanthus occidentalis) is named for its distinctive cephlanthium.  | Synonym: flower head, capitulum | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a specific inflorescence type) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *kephalē* (head) + *anthos* (flower)
