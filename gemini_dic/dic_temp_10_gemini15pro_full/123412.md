monophysite:/ˌmɒnəˈfaɪsaɪt/ | n. |基督一性论者|A believer in Monophysitism, a doctrine that held that Christ had only one nature, divine, rather than two natures, divine and human. |  Example 1: The monophysites were condemned as heretical by the Council of Chalcedon.  基督一性论者被迦克墩会议判定为异端。

Example 2:  The emperor supported the monophysite bishops, causing further division within the church.  皇帝支持基督一性论的主教，导致教会内部进一步分裂。 |Synonym: Miaphysite | Antonym: Dyophysite | Noun
