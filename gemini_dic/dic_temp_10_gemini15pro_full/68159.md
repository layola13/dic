failsafe:/ˈfeɪlˌseɪf/| adj. n.|自动防故障的；故障保险装置|Designed to prevent malfunctioning or to minimize damage in the event of malfunction.  A system or plan that is designed to prevent failure or to protect against the effects of failure.|例句：The plane has a failsafe device that will deploy the parachute if the engine fails. 这架飞机有一个故障保险装置，如果引擎发生故障，会自动打开降落伞。

例句：The nuclear reactor has numerous failsafe mechanisms. 核反应堆有许多故障保险装置。|近义词：foolproof, reliable, secure|反义词：risky, unreliable, dangerous|词性:形容词, 名词

failsafe:/ˈfeɪlˌseɪf/| n.|故障保险装置|A device or system that is designed to prevent failure or to protect against the effects of failure.|例句：The engineers designed a failsafe for the braking system. 工程师们为制动系统设计了一个故障保险装置。

例句：The failsafe prevented a catastrophic system failure.  故障保护装置防止了灾难性的系统故障。|近义词：backup, safeguard, precaution|反义词：hazard, risk, danger|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "fail" + "safe".  First used as an adjective in the 1940s, later also as a noun.  The concept is of a system designed to "fail safe," meaning that if it fails, it does so in a way that causes minimal harm.
