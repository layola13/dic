gastrolith:/ˈɡæstrəˌlɪθ/ /ˈɡæstrəlɪθ/ | n. |胃石；砂囊石|A stone swallowed by an animal (especially a bird, reptile, or fish) and held in the gizzard or stomach to aid digestion by grinding food.|Birds sometimes swallow small stones, called gastroliths, to help them grind their food.|Synonym: gizzard stone |Antonym: N/A |Noun
