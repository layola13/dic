insolence: /ˈɪnsələns/ (US) /ˈɪnsəl(ə)ns/ (UK)| n. |傲慢，无礼，侮辱|Rude and disrespectful behavior. |Example:  The student's insolence towards the teacher was unacceptable.  这个学生对老师的无礼是不可接受的。

Example: He was punished for his insolence to the judge. 他因对法官的傲慢无礼而受到惩罚。


|近义词：rudeness, impudence, disrespect, arrogance, haughtiness|反义词: respect, courtesy, humility, deference|词性: noun


insolent: /ˈɪnsələnt/ (US) /ˈɪnsəl(ə)nt/ (UK)| adj. |傲慢的，无礼的，侮辱的|Showing a rude and arrogant lack of respect. |Example: She gave him an insolent stare. 她傲慢无礼地瞪着他。

Example: His insolent remarks angered everyone in the room. 他傲慢无礼的言论激怒了房间里的每一个人。


|近义词：rude, impudent, disrespectful, arrogant, haughty|反义词: respectful, courteous, humble, deferential|词性: adjective


词源:  From Late Latin *insolentia, from Latin insolēns (“unusual, unaccustomed; arrogant, insulting”), from in- (“not”) + solēns, present participle of solēre (“to be accustomed”).
