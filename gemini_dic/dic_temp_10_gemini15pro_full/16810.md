baselines:/ˈbeɪsˌlaɪnz/| n.|基线，基准，底线|A starting point or standard against which progress or comparisons can be made.  It can also refer to a minimum acceptable level or a line marking the starting point in a race or other competition.|The company uses last year's sales figures as a baseline for measuring growth this year.  该公司用去年的销售数据作为衡量今年增长的基准。|synonyms: benchmark, standard, starting point, foundation|antonyms: peak, apex, maximum|noun

baselines:/ˈbeɪsˌlaɪnz/| v.|建立基线，以…为基准|To establish a baseline or use something as a baseline.|The researchers baselined the participants' stress levels before the experiment.  研究人员在实验前确定了参与者的压力水平基线。|synonyms: benchmark, standardize|antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, but consider "deviate from" or "ignore")|verb
