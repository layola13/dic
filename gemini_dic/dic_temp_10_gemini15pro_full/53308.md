disable:/dɪˈseɪbl/ | vt. | 使丧失能力，使残疾，使无效 | To make someone unable to do something, especially by making them physically or mentally weak or injured; to stop something from working | The accident disabled him from walking.  这场事故使他无法行走。 | synonyms: incapacitate, impair, cripple | antonyms: enable, empower | verb

disable:/dɪˈseɪbl/ | adj. | 残疾的 | (of a person) having a physical or mental condition that limits their movements, senses, or activities. (This word is now often considered offensive; the preferred term is *person with a disability*) |  There are special parking spaces for disabled drivers. 有专门为残疾司机提供的停车位。| synonyms: handicapped, impaired | antonyms: able-bodied, enabled | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation, reversal) + able
