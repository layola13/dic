cassandra:/kəˈsændrə/
|n.|卡珊德拉；预言灾难但不被相信的人|A person who predicts disasters or negative outcomes, especially one who is disregarded or disbelieved.  The name comes from Greek mythology, referring to Cassandra, daughter of Priam, who was cursed to utter true prophecies but never to be believed.|She was a Cassandra, warning of the dangers of the new policy, but no one listened. 她就像卡珊德拉一样，警告新政策的危险性，但没有人听。|prophet of doom, doomsayer|optimist
