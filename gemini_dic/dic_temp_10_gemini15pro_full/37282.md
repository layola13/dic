commandeering:/ˌkɒmənˈdɪərɪŋ/ | n. | 征用，强征 |The act of officially taking possession or control of something, typically for military purposes. | The army commandeered all the vehicles in the town.  军队征用了镇上所有的车辆。| synonyms: seizing, confiscating, appropriating, expropriating | antonyms: returning, releasing, surrendering | noun

commandeer:/ˌkɒmənˈdɪər/ | vt. | 征用，强征 | To officially take possession or control of something, typically for military purposes. | The government commandeered the building for use as a temporary hospital. 政府征用了这栋楼作为临时医院。| synonyms: seize, confiscate, appropriate, expropriate | antonyms: return, release, surrender | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Dutch *kommanderen*, from commanderen "to command," from French commander.
