deconsecrate:/ˌdiːˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/| vt.| 撤销圣职；使不再神圣；| To deprive (a consecrated place or object) of its sacred character.  |The church was deconsecrated and turned into a concert hall. 教堂被撤销了圣职，变成了一个音乐厅。|The altar was deconsecrated before being removed. 祭坛在被移除之前被撤销了圣职。|近义词：desecrate, profane|反义词：consecrate, sanctify|词性：动词

Etymology:
From de- + consecrate, from Latin consecratus, past participle of consecrare "to dedicate, devote," from com- "together" + sacrare "to make sacred," from sacer "sacred."
