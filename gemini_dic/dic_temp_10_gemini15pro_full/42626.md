counterrevolutionaries:/ˌkaʊntərˌrɛvəˈluːʃənɛriz/
|n.|反革命分子，反革命者|People who oppose a revolution, especially those who try to reverse its effects after it has been successful.|The government ruthlessly suppressed the counterrevolutionaries.|Synonyms: reactionaries, insurgents, rebels|Antonyms: revolutionaries, rebels|noun

counterrevolutionary:/ˌkaʊntərˌrɛvəˈluːʃənɛri/
|adj.|反革命的|Opposing or seeking to reverse the effects of a revolution.|The government implemented counterrevolutionary policies.|Synonyms: reactionary, insurgent, rebellious|Antonyms: revolutionary, radical|adjective

counterrevolutionary:/ˌkaʊntərˌrɛvəˈluːʃənɛri/
|n.|反革命者|A person who opposes a revolution.|The counterrevolutionary was arrested and imprisoned.|Synonyms: reactionary, insurgent, rebel|Antonyms: revolutionary, rebel|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"counter-" (prefix meaning "against, opposite") + "revolution" + "-ary" (suffix forming adjectives and nouns)

"revolution" comes from Late Latin "revolutionem" (nominative revolutio), meaning "a revolving," from past participle stem of "revolvere" "to revolve," from "re-" "back, again" + "volvere" "to roll"
