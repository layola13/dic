fronton:/ˈfrʌntɒn/ (UK), /ˈfrʌntɑːn/ (US)| n.|前墙，（尤指巴斯克地区的）墙式回力球场|A fronton is the principal wall of a building, especially the decorated facade of a church. In Basque pelota, it is the main wall against which the ball is played. |The town square was dominated by the church's ornate fronton.  小镇广场上的教堂华丽的前墙占主导地位。


The pelota players hit the ball against the fronton with incredible force. 回力球运动员以惊人的力量将球击向前墙。|近义词：facade, wall (in the context of a building); court (in the context of pelota)|反义词:rear, back|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fronton*, from Old French *frontel*, diminutive of *front* (“forehead, front”), from Latin *frōns* (“forehead, front”).
