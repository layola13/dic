malingerer:/məˈlɪŋɡərər/| n.|装病者，逃避责任者|Someone who pretends to be ill or injured in order to avoid work or duty. | He was suspected of being a malingerer because he always seemed to be "sick" on Mondays. 他总是星期一“生病”，因此被怀疑是装病。|  The soldier was a malingerer, feigning illness to avoid combat.  这名士兵是个装病者，假装生病以逃避战斗。|Synonyms: shirker, slacker, dodger, skiver|Antonyms: hard worker, diligent worker|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *malingre* "sickly, ailing," perhaps of Germanic origin. The English word "malinger" (verb) appeared first, followed by "malingerer" (noun).
