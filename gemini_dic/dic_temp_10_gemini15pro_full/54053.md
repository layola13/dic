disdainfulness:/dɪsˈdeɪnfʊlnəs/
n.
轻蔑；傲慢；鄙夷
The state or quality of being disdainful; a feeling or show of contempt or scorn.
She looked at him with utter disdainfulness. 她极其轻蔑地看着他。
Near Synonyms: contempt, scorn, arrogance, haughtiness, superciliousness
Antonyms: respect, admiration, humility, modesty
Etymology: From disdainful + -ness. Disdainful from disdain + -ful. Disdain from Old French desdeignier, from des- (expressing negation) + deignier "to deign," from Latin dignari "to deem worthy."
