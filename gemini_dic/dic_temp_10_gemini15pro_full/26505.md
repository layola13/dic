cabal:/kəˈbɑːl/
| n. |阴谋；秘密结社；(尤指政治)阴谋集团|a secret political group, typically of powerful people united in some close design, usually to overthrow a government or to gain power|A cabal of disgruntled officers plotted a coup. 一群心怀不满的军官密谋发动政变。|近义词：plot, conspiracy, intrigue, machination|反义词：transparency, openness|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French cabale, from Medieval Latin cabala "Kabbalah," from Hebrew qabbalah "received (lore)." Originally referring to Jewish mysticism, the word later acquired its pejorative sense of secret plotting.
