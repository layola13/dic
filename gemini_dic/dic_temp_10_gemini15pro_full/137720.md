nonvacuously:/ˌnɒnvæˈkjuːəsli/
adverb|非空地，不平凡地|in a way that is not vacuous; meaningfully; not trivially.  Not in a way that is empty, lacking substance, or without significance.|The theorem applies nonvacuously to all finite sets.|近义词: meaningfully, significantly, substantially|反义词: vacuously, trivially, meaninglessly|词性: adverb

Etymology:
From non- + vacuous + -ly.  "Vacuous" comes from Latin "vacuus" meaning "empty".
