allotropism:/əˈlɒtrəpɪzəm/
n.
同素异形性
The existence of two or more different physical forms of a chemical element.
Carbon exhibits allotropism; its allotropes include diamond, graphite, and fullerenes.  碳表现出同素异形性；它的同素异形体包括金刚石、石墨和富勒烯。
Allotropism is a fascinating phenomenon observed in certain chemical elements. 同素异形性是在某些化学元素中观察到的迷人现象。
Synonym: allotropy
Antonym: N/A (This is a scientific phenomenon, not a quality with a direct opposite)
noun
