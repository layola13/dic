believable:/bɪˈliːvəbl/ | adj. | 可信的，可相信的 |  Able to be believed; credible. | Her story seemed believable at first, but later we found out she had lied. 她一开始的故事听起来可信，但后来我们发现她撒谎了。| credible, plausible, convincing | unbelievable, implausible, doubtful | adjective

Etymology: From 'believe' + '-able'.  'Believe' comes from Middle English *bileven*, from Old English *belīefan* "to have confidence in, trust," composed of *be-* "about, by; upon" + *lēof* "dear, pleasing."  The suffix '-able' comes from Old French *-able* and ultimately from Latin *-bilis*.
