mesmerically:/ˌmezməˈrɪkli/ | adv. | 催眠地，迷人地 | in a mesmerizing manner, captivatingly; so as to transfix or fascinate as if by hypnosis | Her voice was soft and mesmerizing, drawing him in mesmerically. 她嗓音轻柔迷人，像催眠般地吸引着他。 |  Synonyms: hypnotically, captivatingly, enchantingly, fascinatingly | Antonyms:  repulsively, dully, boringly | Word type: Adverb

Etymology:
From *mesmerize* + *-ically*. *Mesmerize* comes from the name of Franz Mesmer (1734–1815), an Austrian physician who developed a theory of animal magnetism, later called *mesmerism*. The suffix *-ically* forms adverbs from adjectives.
