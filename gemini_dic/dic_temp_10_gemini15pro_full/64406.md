ergative:/ˈɜːrɡətɪv/| adj. n.| 及物的，作格的；作格|A verb whose subject undergoes the action of the verb (as in 'The window broke') is called an ergative verb.  (一个动词的主语经历动词的动作（如“窗户破了”）被称为作格动词。) |The verb "break" can be used both transitively and ergatively.  (动词“打破”既可以用作及物动词，也可以用作作格动词。)|近义词：transitive|反义词: intransitive|词性: adjective, noun

ergative:/ˈɜːrɡətɪv/| n.| 作格|In Ergative-Absolutive languages, the subject of an intransitive verb has the same case marking as the object of a transitive verb, this is called the absolutive case. The subject of a transitive verb then has a different case, called the ergative case. (在作格-绝对格语言中，不及物动词的主语与及物动词的宾语具有相同的格标记，这被称为绝对格。及物动词的主语则有不同的格，称为作格。)|Basque is an example of a language with an ergative case system. (巴斯克语是具有作格格系统的语言的一个例子。)|近义词：agent|反义词: absolutive, patient|词性: noun
