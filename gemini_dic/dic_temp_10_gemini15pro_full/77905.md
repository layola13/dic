geniohyoid:/ˌdʒɪniːoʊˈhaɪɔɪd/
n.|颏舌骨肌|A narrow muscle running from the chin to the hyoid bone, involved in swallowing and depressing the mandible.|The geniohyoid muscle is active during swallowing. 颏舌骨肌在吞咽过程中很活跃。|synonyms: submental muscles (broad category)|antonyms: N/A (muscles don't typically have direct antonyms)|noun

geniohyoid:/ˌdʒɪniːoʊˈhaɪɔɪd/
adjective|颏舌骨的|Relating to the geniohyoid muscle.|Geniohyoid dysfunction can cause difficulty swallowing. 颏舌骨功能障碍会导致吞咽困难。|synonyms: submental (related to the area), suprahyoid (related to muscles above the hyoid bone)|antonyms: N/A|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From genio-  "chin" +  -hyoid "hyoid bone".  "Genio-" comes from the Greek  γένειον (geneion) "chin," and "-hyoid" from the Greek  ὑοειδής (hyoeidēs), meaning "shaped like the letter upsilon (υ)," referring to the hyoid bone.
