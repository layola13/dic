hubristic:/hjuːˈbrɪstɪk/ | adj. | 傲慢的，狂妄自大的 | Excessively proud or self-confident; arrogant. |  His hubristic attitude made him unpopular. 他傲慢的态度让他不受欢迎。| Example: The CEO's hubristic pronouncements alienated the board of directors.  首席执行官傲慢的声明疏远了董事会。| Synonyms: arrogant, haughty, conceited, presumptuous, overweening | Antonyms: humble, modest, unassuming | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *hybris* (ὕβρις) meaning "insolent pride, outrageous treatment," leading to *hybristes* (ὑβριστής) "insolent, wanton person."  Borrowed into English via Late Latin *hubris*. The "-tic" suffix transforms the noun into an adjective.
