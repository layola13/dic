gastropods:/ˈɡæstrəpɒdz/ (US) /ˈɡæstrəpɑːdz/ (UK)| n. |腹足纲动物；腹足类动物|A large class of mollusks, typically having a single spiral shell and a broad flat foot, such as snails and slugs.  This class includes snails, slugs, limpets, and sea hares. They are characterized by a single shell (often coiled), a muscular foot used for locomotion, and a head with tentacles.|Snails and slugs are common gastropods.  蜗牛和蛞蝓是常见的腹足纲动物。|Synonyms: univalves,  gasteropoda|Antonyms: None (Gastropods are a biological class, not a concept with an opposite)|noun
