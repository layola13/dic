lemures:/ˈlɛmjʊriːz/ | n. | 鬼魂，幽灵（尤指古罗马宗教中的）|  Spirits of the dead in Roman religion, often considered malevolent and needing to be appeased. | The Romans believed that lemures roamed the earth during Lemuria.  罗马人相信，在勒穆里亚节期间，鬼魂在地球上游荡。| The family made offerings to appease the lemures of their ancestors.  这家人供奉祭品以安抚他们祖先的亡灵。 | Synonyms: ghosts, specters, shades, phantoms | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "living beings" or "deities") | Noun
