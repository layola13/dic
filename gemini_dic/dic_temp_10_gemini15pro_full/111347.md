livelode:/ˈlaɪvlihʊd/| n. |生计，生活来源|A person's livelihood is the way they earn money in order to live. |例句：Fishing is the main livelihood of many people in the village.  捕鱼是村里许多人的主要生计。

例句：She lost her livelihood when the factory closed down. 工厂倒闭后，她失去了生计。|近义词：income, living, sustenance, means of support|反义词: unemployment, destitution|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *livelode, *liflode, from Old English līflād (“way of life, sustenance, leading of life”), from līf (“life”) + lād (“course, journey, way”).  Surface analysis would suggest līf (“life”) + hōd (“-hood; state, condition”).
