mastigopoda:/ˌmæstɪˈɡɒpədə/ | n. | 鞭毛足类 |A subclass of Sarcodina (protozoa) characterized by having one or more flagella for locomotion and often pseudopodia for feeding.  They are typically found in freshwater environments. | Example: Mastigopoda, such as *Cercomonas*, are often studied in introductory biology courses.  鞭毛足类，例如尾滴虫，经常在生物入门课程中被研究。 | Synonym: Flagellata (older, now mostly obsolete) | Antonym:  N/A | noun

mastigophoran:/ˌmæstɪˈɡɒfərən/ | adj. n. | 鞭毛的，鞭毛虫 |(adj.) Possessing one or more flagella; (n.) A mastigophoran organism, especially a protozoan. | Example (adj.): Mastigophoran protozoa are common in pond water.  鞭毛原生动物在池塘水中很常见。 Example (n.):  The *Trypanosoma*, a mastigophoran, causes sleeping sickness. 锥虫，一种鞭毛虫，导致昏睡病。| Synonym: flagellate | Antonym: N/A | adjective, noun
