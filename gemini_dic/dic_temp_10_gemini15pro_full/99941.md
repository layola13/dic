interns:/ˈɪntɜːrnz/ (US) /ˈɪntɜːnz/ (UK)| n.|实习生|A student or recent graduate who works for a period of time in order to gain experience in a particular field.|The company hired three interns for the summer.  这家公司夏天雇佣了三个实习生。|Synonyms: trainee, apprentice|Antonyms: professional, expert|noun


intern:/ˈɪntɜːrn/ (US) /ˈɪntɜːn/ (UK)| n.|实习生|A student or recent graduate who works for a period of time in order to gain experience in a particular field.|She's working as an intern at a law firm. 她在一家律师事务所实习。|Synonyms: trainee, apprentice|Antonyms: professional, expert|noun


intern:/ɪnˈtɜːrn/ (US) /ɪnˈtɜːn/ (UK)| vt.|拘留，扣押|To confine or impound especially during a war.|Many Japanese Americans were interned during World War II.  许多日裔美国人在二战期间被拘留。|Synonyms: detain, confine|Antonyms: release, liberate|verb
