leakier:/ˈliːkiər/| adj.|更容易漏的；更藏不住秘密的|more leaky; allowing more leaks or secrets to be revealed|The older boat was leakier than the new one. 这艘旧船比新船更容易漏水。|The politician became leakier as the scandal unfolded. 随着丑闻的曝光，这位政治家越来越藏不住秘密了。|近义词：more porous, less secure, less tight|反义词：tighter, less porous, more secure|词性：形容词

Etymology->
From leak + -y + -er (comparative suffix).  "Leak" comes from Middle English *leken, from Old Norse *leka "to drip, leak," related to Old English *hlec "covering, roof."  The suffix "-y" forms adjectives meaning "full of" or "characterized by." The suffix "-er" forms the comparative degree of adjectives.
