disinclose:/dɪsɪnˈkloʊz/ | v. | 揭露，泄露 | To reveal or disclose something previously hidden or secret.  |  He disinclosed the secret plans to his competitor. 他向他的竞争对手泄露了秘密计划。

The whistleblower disinclosed evidence of corruption within the company. 告密者揭露了公司内部腐败的证据。 | 近义词：reveal, disclose, divulge, expose | 反义词：conceal, hide, suppress | 词性：动词

词源：From dis- + enclose.  "Dis-"  prefix meaning "apart, asunder, away, utterly" or having a privative, negative, or reversing force. "Enclose" meaning to shut in on all sides.  Therefore, disinclose signifies opening up what was enclosed or secret.
