ETYMOLOGY->
"bare" (v.) - Old English *beran "to carry, bring forth," from Proto-Germanic *beranan.
"down" (adv.) - Old English adune "from a higher place to a lower one," from Proto-Germanic *of duno "off the hill."


"bare down" originally referred to the physical exertion of childbirth, specifically the act of pushing.  This meaning has broadened over time to encompass any situation requiring intense effort or concentration.
