overflexions: /ˌoʊvərˈflɛkʃənz/ | n. | 过度屈折; 过度变形 | Excessive inflection or bending, often used in linguistics to describe the use of more inflectional endings than is standard or necessary.  This term is more common in older linguistic texts. |  Overflexions were common in some early Germanic languages. 早期某些日耳曼语中过度屈折很常见。 |  The author's use of overflexions made the text difficult to understand. 作者过度使用屈折变化使得文本难以理解。 |  hyperinflection |  underinflection | noun
