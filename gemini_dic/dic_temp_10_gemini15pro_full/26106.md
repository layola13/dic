buskin:/ˈbʌskɪn/| n.|半高靴；悲剧；悲剧风格|A thick-soled boot or half-boot reaching to mid-calf, laced or strapped, worn by actors in Greek tragedy.  A style or genre of serious or tragic drama.|The actor wore buskins for his role in the Greek play.  演员穿着半高靴来扮演他在希腊戏剧中的角色。|近义词：boot, cothurnus|反义词: sock (in the context of theatrical footwear representing comedy)|noun

buskin:/ˈbʌskɪn/| n.|高贵的风格；悲壮的风格|Elevated or tragic style, especially in language.|His writing was marked by a certain buskin, reflecting the gravity of the subject matter. 他的写作以某种悲壮的风格为标志，反映了主题的严肃性。|近义词：loftiness, grandeur|反义词:levity, humor|noun
