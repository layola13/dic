fyrd:/fɜːrd/| n.| 民兵，征召兵 | An army of freemen organized in Anglo-Saxon England, called to defend their lands when necessary. |  The fyrd was called up to repel the Viking invaders.  征召兵被召集起来击退维京入侵者。| 近义词: militia, levy | 反义词: standing army | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fyrd, meaning "army, expedition, military force," related to Old Frisian *ferd "army," and Old High German *fart "journey, passage, military expedition."  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *fardiz, meaning "journey, expedition."
