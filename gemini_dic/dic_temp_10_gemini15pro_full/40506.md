contrives:/kənˈtraɪvz/ | v. | 设计；发明；图谋；设法做到 | to plan cleverly; to devise; to bring about, especially by scheming | He contrived a way to escape. 他设计了一个逃跑的方法。 | synonyms: devise, engineer, plan, scheme, plot, concoct | antonyms:  fail, neglect,  abandon | verb

contrives:/kənˈtraɪvz/ | v. |  策划；  | to manage to do something difficult |  She somehow contrived to get tickets for the sold-out concert. 她设法弄到了这场售罄音乐会的门票。| synonyms: manage, arrange, achieve, accomplish | antonyms: fail | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French controver (Modern French controuver), from Latin controversus "turned against, disputed, doubtful," from contra "against" + versus, past participle of vertere "to turn."  The original sense was "to investigate, discuss, debate," later "to invent, fabricate," then "to plan with ingenuity."
