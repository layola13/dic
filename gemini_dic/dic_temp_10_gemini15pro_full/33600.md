cimmerian:/sɪˈmɪəriən/| adj. n.|黑暗的，阴沉的；居住在黑暗中的人|Extremely dark; gloomy.  Relating to an ancient people of the Black Sea region, described by Homer as dwelling in perpetual darkness.  Figuratively, it refers to a place or state of gloom or obscurity.|The cimmerian darkness of the cave made it impossible to see.  洞穴里漆黑一片，什么也看不见。|Synonyms: dark, gloomy, murky, shadowy, obscure, stygian|Antonyms: bright, sunny, light, luminous|adjective

cimmerian:/sɪˈmɪəriən/| n.|居住在黑暗中的人|A member of an ancient people of the Black Sea region, described by Homer as dwelling in perpetual darkness.|The legend of the Cimmerians speaks of a people shrouded in mist and darkness.  关于辛梅里安人的传说讲述了一个被薄雾和黑暗笼罩的民族。|Synonyms: troglodyte, cave dweller|Antonyms: none|noun
