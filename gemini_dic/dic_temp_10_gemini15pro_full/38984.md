confirmabilities:/kənˌfɜːrməˈbɪlɪtiz/| n. |可证实性；可确认性|The confirmabilities of the experimental results are high, meaning they can be easily reproduced and verified by other researchers.  实验结果的可证实性很高，意味着它们可以很容易地被其他研究人员复制和验证。| The historian questioned the confirmabilities of the ancient scrolls, citing the lack of corroborating evidence.  历史学家质疑古代卷轴的可证实性，理由是缺乏确凿的证据。|近义词：verifiability, attestability, substantiation|反义词：unprovability,  unverifiability|词性: 名词

词源:  From "confirmable" + "-ity". "Confirmable" comes from the Late Latin "confirmabilis",  from "confirmare" (to strengthen, establish), from "con-" (together, with) + "firmare" (to make firm), from "firmus" (firm, strong).  "-ity" is a suffix forming abstract nouns denoting a state or quality.
