out-and-away:/ˌaʊt ən əˈweɪ/
adverb
远远地；无疑地；绝对地
By far; without question; definitely.
She is out-and-away the best student in the class. 她无疑是班上最好的学生。
近义词: by far, definitely, absolutely, easily
反义词: slightly, marginally, barely, hardly
词性: adverb

out-and-away:/ˌaʊt ən əˈweɪ/
adjective
无可争议的；绝对的
Undisputed; absolute.
It was an out-and-away victory for the home team.  这是一场主队无可争议的胜利。
近义词: decisive, conclusive, overwhelming, unquestionable
反义词: close, narrow, debatable, disputed
词性: adjective


词源:  The phrase "out and away" originated in the early 19th century. "Out" often implies surpassing or exceeding something, while "away" emphasizes the degree of difference.  The combination intensifies the meaning of being beyond comparison.
