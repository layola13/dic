ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hālig, from Proto-Germanic *hailagaz ("holy, sacred, healthy"), from Proto-Indo-European *kailo- ("whole, uninjured, of good omen"). Cognate with Old Frisian hālig, Old Saxon hēlag, Old High German heilig, Old Norse heilagr, Gothic 𐌷𐌰𐌹𐌻𐌰𐌲𐍃 (hailags). Related to whole and hail.  The sense of "morally and spiritually excellent" developed later.
