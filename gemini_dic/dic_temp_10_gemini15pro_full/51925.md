diabase: /ˈdaɪəbeɪs/ (US), /ˈdaɪəbeɪs/ (UK)| n. |辉绿岩|A dark, fine-grained igneous rock composed mostly of plagioclase feldspar and pyroxene, typically occurring in dikes and sills.  It is equivalent to dolerite.|The diabase dike cut through the surrounding sedimentary rock.|Synonym: dolerite, basalt (similar composition, different texture)|Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a rock type. Could consider contrasting rock types like granite or rhyolite based on texture or mineral composition.)|noun
