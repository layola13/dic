disability:/ˌdɪsəˈbɪləti/ /ˌdɪsəˈbɪlɪti/| n.|残疾，无力，无能|A physical or mental condition that limits a person's movements, senses, or activities.|例句：He has a learning disability. 他有学习障碍。|例句：The disability made it difficult for her to climb stairs. 残疾使她爬楼梯很困难。|近义词：impairment, handicap, incapacity|反义词：ability, capability|词性：名词

disability:/ˌdɪsəˈbɪləti/ /ˌdɪsəˈbɪlɪti/| n.|伤残|A disadvantage or restriction of activity caused by a contemporary social organization which takes no or little account of people who have physical impairments and thus excludes them from participation in the mainstream of social activities.|例句：Legislation has been enacted to protect the rights of people with disabilities.  已经制定了法律来保护残疾人的权利。|例句：The building's lack of ramps is a disability for wheelchair users.  该建筑缺少坡道对轮椅使用者来说是一种障碍。|近义词：handicap, impairment|反义词：ability, capability|词性：名词
