bellwether:/ˈbelˌweðər/ (US), /ˈbelˌweðə(r)/ (UK)| n.|领头羊；前导；风向标；征兆|a person or thing that leads or indicates a trend.  It originally referred to a sheep chosen to lead the flock, often with a bell around its neck.|例句：The company's stock is considered a bellwether for the entire industry. 该公司的股票被认为是整个行业的晴雨表。

例句：The success of this product will be a bellwether for the future of the company.  这个产品的成功将是公司未来的风向标。|近义词：leader, indicator, guide, barometer, harbinger|反义词：follower, laggard|词性：noun
