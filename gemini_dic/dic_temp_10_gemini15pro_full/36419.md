cokier:/ˈkʊkiər/| adj.|更喜欢吃饼干的；更像饼干的|Someone who is cokier is more fond of cookies or more like a cookie in some way.  This word is not standard English and is likely a neologism or a humorous/informal creation.|He's become cokier in his old age, always wanting a biscuit with his tea. 他年纪大了以后更爱吃饼干了，喝茶总要配块饼干。|近义词:cookie-loving, biscuit-loving|反义词: (No direct opposite, could be something like) indifferent to cookies|词性:adjective

词源: This word is derived from "cookie" with the comparative suffix "-er".  "Cookie" itself ultimately comes from the Dutch word "koekje," meaning "little cake."
