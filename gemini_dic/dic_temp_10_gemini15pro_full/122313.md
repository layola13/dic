mitty:/ˈmɪti/| n.| 沃尔特·米蒂; 空想家; 爱幻想的人|A person who spends more time daydreaming than paying attention to the real world; a daydreamer, especially one who fantasizes about a life of excitement and adventure.|He's a bit of a Mitty, always imagining himself as a hero.|近义词：daydreamer, fantasist, romantic, visionary|反义词: realist, pragmatist|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the fictional character "Walter Mitty" in James Thurber's short story "The Secret Life of Walter Mitty" (1939), who escapes his mundane life through elaborate daydreams.
