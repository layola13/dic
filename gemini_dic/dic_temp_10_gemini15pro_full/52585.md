dig-up:/ˌdɪɡ ˈʌp/ | phrasal verb | 挖出; 发现; 搜寻出 | To unearth something from the ground or to find something after searching. |  The archaeologists dug up an ancient Roman coin. 考古学家挖出了一枚古罗马硬币。

The reporter dug up some dirt on the politician. 记者挖出了这位政客的一些丑闻。 | 近义词：unearth, discover, excavate, exhume | 反义词: bury, conceal, hide | 词性: 动词短语

dig-up:/ˌdɪɡ ˈʌp/ | n. | (非正式)挖掘出来的东西; 发现物 | Something that has been dug up, often unexpectedly or after a search. | The dig-up at the construction site turned out to be an old well. 建筑工地挖出来的东西原来是一口古井。 | 近义词：discovery, find | 反义词: N/A | 词性: 名词 (较少用)
