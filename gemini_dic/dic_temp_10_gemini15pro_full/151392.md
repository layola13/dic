parliaments:/ˈpɑːrləmənts/ | n. |议会，国会|A parliament is the group of people who are elected to make and change the laws of a country.  A parliament is a legislative body of government. |The UK Parliament consists of the House of Commons and the House of Lords. 英国议会由下议院和上议院组成。

The Canadian parliament is in Ottawa. 加拿大议会位于渥太华。|Synonyms: legislature, congress, assembly, diet |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a governing body) | Noun

Etymology: From Old French *parlement* "speaking, talk," from parler "to speak," from Late Latin *parabolare* "to speak (in parables)," from Latin parabola "parable," from Greek parabole "comparison, illustration; parabola," literally "a throwing beside," from para- "beside" + ballein "to throw." The sense evolution is from "discussion, conference" to "judicial court, assembly."
