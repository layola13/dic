forceps:/ˈfɔːrseps/ (US), /ˈfɔːsɪps/ (UK)| n.|镊子；钳子|A hinged instrument used for grasping and holding objects, similar to tongs or pincers. Typically used in medical procedures, especially childbirth and surgery, but also in various other fields like laboratory work and jewelry making.|The surgeon used forceps to remove the splinter.  外科医生用镊子取出了碎片。

The biologist used forceps to handle the delicate specimen. 生物学家用镊子处理精细的标本。|synonyms: tongs, pincers, tweezers, pliers|antonyms: N/A (Forceps are a tool; they don't have a direct opposite.)|noun
