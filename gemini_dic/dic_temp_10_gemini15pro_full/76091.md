furrow:/ˈfɜːroʊ/ (US) /ˈfʌrəʊ/ (UK)| n. vt.|犁沟，皱纹|A long, narrow trench made in the ground by a plow, or a wrinkle on the skin, especially on the forehead.|The farmer plowed deep furrows in the field. 农民在田里犁出了深深的犁沟。|Synonyms: groove, trench, wrinkle, crease|Antonyms: smoothness, ridge|noun
furrow:/ˈfɜːroʊ/ (US) /ˈfʌrəʊ/ (UK)| vt.|犁，使起皱纹|To make furrows in; plow; to wrinkle.|He furrowed his brow in concentration. 他皱着眉头，聚精会神。|Synonyms: plow, wrinkle, crease|Antonyms: smooth, unwrinkle|verb
