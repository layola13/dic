monadology:/ˌmɒnəˈdɒlədʒi/ | n. | 单子论；单子学 |  The philosophical doctrine that the universe is constituted of an infinite number of simple and indivisible units called monads, each of which is a self-contained microcosm mirroring the universe. | Leibniz's Monadology is a complex and challenging work.  莱布尼茨的单子论是一部复杂而富有挑战性的著作。|  monism, pluralism | dualism | noun
