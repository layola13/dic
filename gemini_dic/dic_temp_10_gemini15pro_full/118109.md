mennonite:/ˈmɛnəˌnaɪt/ | n. | 门诺派教徒；门诺派信徒 | A member of a Protestant sect originating in the 16th century in the Netherlands and Switzerland, emphasizing adult baptism, pacifism, and simple living. |  Example: The Mennonites in the community are known for their pacifist beliefs.  社区里的门诺派教徒以他们的和平主义信仰而闻名。

Example:  My neighbor is a Mennonite and often brings us freshly baked bread. 我的邻居是门诺派教徒，经常给我们带来新鲜出炉的面包。 | 近义词:Anabaptist, Amish (sometimes) | 反义词: None (Specific antonyms are difficult to define due to the complexity of religious beliefs.) | 词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Menno Simons (c. 1496–1561), a Dutch Anabaptist religious leader + -ite.
