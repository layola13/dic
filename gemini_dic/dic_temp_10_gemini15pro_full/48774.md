deflates:/dɪˈfleɪts/ | v. | 放气；泄气；使缩小；贬低 | To release air or gas from something, making it smaller or less inflated; to reduce someone's confidence or make them feel less important. | The balloon slowly deflated.  气球慢慢地瘪了。|synonyms: collapse, shrink, diminish, depress | antonyms: inflate, expand, enlarge, boost | verb

deflates:/dɪˈfleɪts/ | v. | 使缩小；贬低 | To reduce someone's confidence or make them feel less important. |  His harsh criticism deflated her. 他严厉的批评使她泄气。|synonyms: discourage, demoralize, demean | antonyms: encourage, uplift, inspire | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *deflātus*, past participle of *deflāre*  "to blow away, deflate," from *de-* "down, away from" + *flāre* "to blow."
