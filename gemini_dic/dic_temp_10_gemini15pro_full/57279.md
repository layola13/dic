ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dūn, from Proto-Germanic *dūnaz (“hill, down”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰewh₂- (“to rise in a cloud of dust, smoke”). Cognate with Dutch duin (“dune”), German Düne (“dune”).  The meaning "tract of upland" is first attested in the late 14th century.


注意事项:
这个单词主要作为名词使用，表示丘陵、草地或低谷。 注意区分其单复数形式。 作为“厄运”解释时通常用复数形式 downs。
