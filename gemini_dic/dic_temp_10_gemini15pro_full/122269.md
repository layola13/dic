mitigate:/ˈmɪtɪɡeɪt/| v. | 减轻，缓和 | to make something less harmful, serious, or painful. |  The government is trying to mitigate the effects of the recession. 政府正在努力减轻经济衰退的影响。 | alleviate, lessen, reduce, diminish | worsen, aggravate, exacerbate | verb

mitigates:/ˈmɪtɪɡeɪts/| v. (third-person singular present) | 减轻，缓和（第三人称单数现在时） | He mitigates the risk by diversifying his investments. 他通过分散投资来降低风险。 |  Regular exercise mitigates the risk of heart disease. 经常锻炼可以减轻患心脏病的风险。 | alleviates, lessens, reduces, diminishes | worsens, aggravates, exacerbates | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *mitigatus*, past participle of *mitigare* (“to soften, make mild”), from *mitis* (“mild, soft”) + *-igare* (frequentative suffix).
