indiscretion:/ˌɪndɪˈskreʃən/| n. |轻率；不谨慎；言行失检|Lack of good judgment or caution in behavior or speech, especially when it involves revealing something confidential or embarrassing.|He committed an indiscretion when he revealed confidential information to the press. 他向媒体透露机密信息，犯了轻率之举。|Synonyms: imprudence, folly, recklessness, carelessness, slip, lapse, blunder, gaffe, faux pas|Antonyms: prudence, discretion, caution, circumspection|noun

indiscretions:/ˌɪndɪˈskreʃənz/| n. |轻率行为；不慎之举；言行失检|Plural form of indiscretion; instances of lacking good judgment or caution in behavior or speech.|His youthful indiscretions came back to haunt him later in life. 他年轻时的轻率行为在他晚年给他带来了困扰。|Synonyms: imprudences, follies, recklessnesses, carelessnesses, slips, lapses, blunders, gaffes, faux pas|Antonyms: prudences, discretions, cautions, circumspections|noun
