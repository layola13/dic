naturalise:/ˈnætʃrəlaɪz/ /ˈnætʃərəˌlaɪz/| v. | 使归化；移植；吸收；使入籍；使习惯于| To grant full citizenship to a foreigner. To introduce and establish (a plant or animal) in a new region or environment. To make something seem normal or acceptable. |Example 1:  He was naturalised as a British citizen last year.  他去年被归化为了英国公民。

Example 2: The plant has become naturalised in many parts of the world.  这种植物已在世界许多地方移植生长。| 近义词: acclimatize, adapt, assimilate, integrate | 反义词: expatriate, alienate|词性:动词


naturalise:/ˈnætʃrəlaɪz/ /ˈnætʃərəˌlaɪz/| adj. | 归化的；驯化的；引进的| Having acquired citizenship in a foreign country. Made suitable for a new environment. | Example 1:  A naturalised American citizen. 一个归化的美国公民。

Example 2:  Naturalised plants in the garden.  花园里移植的植物。 |近义词: acclimated, adapted, assimilated, indigenous (in some contexts) | 反义词: foreign, alien, exotic|词性:形容词


词源: From the Late Latin "naturalizare,"  meaning "to make natural," from "naturalis" (natural).
