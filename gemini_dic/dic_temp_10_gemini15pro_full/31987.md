chemotaxis:/ˌkɛmoʊˈtæksɪs/ /ˌkiːmoʊˈtæksɪs/| n. |趋化性；化学趋向性|The movement of an organism or cell in response to a chemical stimulus.  The chemicals can either attract (positive chemotaxis) or repel (negative chemotaxis) the organism/cell.| White blood cells exhibit chemotaxis towards sites of infection. 白细胞对感染部位表现出趋化性。|Synonyms: chemotropism |Antonyms: chemokinesis|noun
