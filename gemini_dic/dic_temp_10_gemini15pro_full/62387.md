endochrome:/ˈɛndəʊkrəʊm/ | n. | 内色素，细胞色素 | A coloring matter within a cell, especially within algae and other photosynthetic organisms, but excluding the green pigments within chloroplasts. | Example: The endochrome in diatoms is golden-brown. 硅藻的内色素是金棕色的。

Example:  Researchers studied the specific endochrome present in certain species of red algae. 研究人员研究了某些种类的红藻中存在的特定内色素。| 近义词: pigment, chromoplast | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, could consider terms related to absence of color, like achromic) | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From endo- (within) + -chrome (color).  Ultimately from Greek endon (within) and chroma (color).
