absolve:/əbˈzɒlv/, /əbˈsɑːlv/| vt.|赦免，免除，宣告…无罪|To declare (someone) free from guilt, obligation, or punishment.|The priest absolved him of his sins.  神父赦免了他的罪过。

The court absolved her of all responsibility for the accident. 法院免除了她对事故的所有责任。|近义词：exonerate, acquit, pardon, forgive|反义词：condemn, convict, incriminate|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *absolven*, from Old French *absoldre*, *assoldre*, from Latin *absolvere* (“to loosen from, detach, acquit, complete”), from *ab* (“from, away from”) + *solvere* (“to loosen, untie, release, solve”).

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The court absolves the defendant. = 法院宣布被告无罪。
The priest absolves sinners of their sins. = 神父赦免罪人的罪孽。

一般过去时:
The jury absolved the defendant of all charges. = 陪审团宣布被告所有罪名不成立。
The church absolved him from his vows. = 教会免除了他的誓言。

现在完成时:
The judge has absolved the witness from further testimony. = 法官已经免除了证人进一步作证的义务。
The committee has absolved him of any wrongdoing. = 委员会已经宣布他没有任何不当行为。

过去完成时:
The king had absolved the traitor before his execution. = 国王在处决叛徒之前赦免了他。
The pope had absolved the knight of his oaths. = 教皇已经免除了骑士的誓言。

将来时:
The judge will absolve the defendant if new evidence emerges. = 如果出现新的证据，法官将宣布被告无罪。
The committee will absolve her of responsibility once the investigation is complete. = 一旦调查完成，委员会将免除她的责任。


被动语态:
He was absolved of all blame. = 他被免除了一切责备。
She was absolved from her oath. = 她被免除了誓言。


PHRASE->
absolve from = 免除...的责任/义务
absolve of = 赦免...的罪过
be absolved from = 被免除...的责任/义务

absolve somebody from something = 免除某人某事
