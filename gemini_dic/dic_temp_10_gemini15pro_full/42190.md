coulomb:/ˈkuːlɒm/ (UK), /ˈkuːlɑːm/ (US)| n.|库仑|The SI unit of electric charge, equal to the quantity of electricity conveyed in one second by a current of one ampere.|The battery stores a charge of 150,000 coulombs.  电池储存了15万库仑的电荷。|Synonyms: unit of charge|Antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *coulomb*, named after Charles-Augustin de Coulomb (1736–1806), French physicist.
