graping:/ˈɡreɪpɪŋ/| v.|抓取，抓住，理解，领会|The child was graping the toy tightly.  孩子紧紧地抓着玩具。|English Explanation:  The act of seizing or holding something firmly, often with the hand or fingers. Can also metaphorically refer to understanding or comprehending something.|例句：He was graping for the rope as he fell. 他掉下去的时候紧紧抓住绳子。|例句：I'm still graping with the complex concepts of quantum physics. 我仍在努力理解量子物理学的复杂概念。|近义词：gripping, clutching, seizing, grasping, comprehending, understanding|反义词:releasing, letting go, misunderstanding|词性:动词

词源: From "grape" + "-ing" (present participle suffix).  While related to the fruit, the "graping" used here derives from the action of picking grapes, which involves seizing and holding.  The metaphorical meaning of understanding likely stems from the idea of grasping a concept.
