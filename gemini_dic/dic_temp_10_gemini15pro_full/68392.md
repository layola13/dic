fallownesses:/ˈfæləʊnɪsɪz/
n.
休耕地；闲置状态；荒芜
The state of being fallow; inactivity or neglect.
The fallownesses of the fields allowed the soil to regain its nutrients.  田地的休耕让土壤恢复了养分。
The artist lamented the fallownesses of his creative spirit.  艺术家哀叹他创作精神的荒芜。
近义词：inactivity, idleness, barrenness, sterility
反义词：activity, fertility, productivity
词性：名词

fallow:/ˈfæləʊ/
n.
休耕地
Land left unplanted for a period of time to improve its fertility.
The farmer left the field fallow for a year.  农民让这块地休耕了一年。
adj.
休耕的；闲置的；未开垦的
Left unplanted or uncultivated for a period in order to restore its fertility.
The fallow fields stretched as far as the eye could see.  休耕的田野一望无际。
vt.
使休耕；使闲置
To leave (land) unplanted for a period in order to restore its fertility.
The farmer fallowed the field to improve the soil.  农民让这块田休耕以改善土壤。
近义词：(n.)  unused land, (adj.)  uncultivated, dormant
反义词：(adj.)  cultivated, active
词性：名词，形容词，动词

词源：From Old English *fealh, from Proto-Germanic *falwaz (“pale, fallow, yellow”).  来自古英语 *fealh，源于原始日耳曼语 *falwaz（“苍白的，休耕的，黄色的”）。
