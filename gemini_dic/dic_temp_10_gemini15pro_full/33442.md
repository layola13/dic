ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ċyernan, from Proto-Germanic *kurnaną, from Proto-Indo-European *kʷer- (“to turn, twist”). Cognate with Scots churn (“to churn”), West Frisian kerne (“to churn”), Dutch kernen (“to churn, to pit (fruit)”), German kirnen (“to churn”), Danish kerne (“to churn”), Swedish kärna (“to churn”).
