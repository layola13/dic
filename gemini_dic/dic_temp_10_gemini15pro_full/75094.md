frilly:/ˈfrɪli/| adj.|褶边多的，饰有褶边的，花哨的|Characterized by or having many frills; ornate.  Often used to describe something overly decorated or fussy, sometimes with a negative connotation.|例句：She wore a frilly pink dress to the party. 她穿了一件粉红色的褶边连衣裙去参加聚会。|例句：The curtains were frilly and old-fashioned. 窗帘有很多褶边，显得过时。|近义词：lacy, ruffled, ornate, fussy|反义词：plain, simple, unadorned|词性:形容词
frilliness:/ˈfrɪlinəs/| n.|褶边，装饰，虚饰|The state or quality of being frilly; an excess of ornamentation.|例句：The frilliness of the dress was a bit much for a casual lunch. 这条裙子的褶边对于一顿便餐来说有点太多了。|例句：She disliked the frilliness of the Victorian furniture. 她不喜欢维多利亚时代家具的繁复装饰。|近义词：ornamentation, decoration, fussiness|反义词：simplicity, plainness|词性:名词

词源: From *frill* + *-y*.  The noun *frill* likely originates from a Low German source related to Middle Dutch *vrille*, meaning "twist, curl".
