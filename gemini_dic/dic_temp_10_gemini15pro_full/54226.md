disfranchise:/ˌdɪsˈfrænˌtʃaɪz/ | vt. | 剥夺公民权；剥夺选举权 | To deprive someone of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote. |  The new law disfranchised many poor people from voting. 新法律剥夺了许多穷人的投票权。

The government disfranchised people who had committed felonies. 政府剥夺了重罪犯的公民权。| 近义词：disenfranchise, deprive | 反义词：enfranchise | 词性：动词
