interattrition:/ˌɪntərəˈtrɪʃən/ | n. | 内部损耗，内部人员流失 |  The gradual reduction in the size of a group or organization caused by members leaving, but not being replaced from outside the group, especially in a company. | Example 1: The company experienced significant interattrition due to retirements and internal transfers, with no new hires to fill the gaps. 公司由于退休和内部调动经历了严重的内部人员流失，没有新员工来填补空缺。

Example 2:  High interattrition rates can lead to a loss of institutional knowledge and experience. 高内部人员流失率会导致机构知识和经验的丧失。 | 近义词：internal churn, internal turnover | 反义词： recruitment, hiring | 词性: noun

词源:  "inter-" (between, among) + "attrition" (gradual reduction in numbers).  Attrition itself comes from the Latin "atterere" (to rub against, wear away).
