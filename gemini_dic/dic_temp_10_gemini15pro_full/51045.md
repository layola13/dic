desolate:/ˈdesələt/ (US) /ˈdɛsələt/ (UK)| adj. | 荒凉的，孤寂的，凄凉的 | Making someone feel sad and alone; empty of people and in a state of ruin. | The war left the city desolate. 战争使这座城市变得荒凉。| synonyms: barren, bleak, deserted, empty, forlorn, godforsaken, lonely, solitary | antonyms: cheerful, comforting, crowded, happy, populous, warm | adjective

desolate:/ˈdesəleɪt/ (US) /ˈdɛsəleɪt/ (UK)| vt. | 使荒芜，使孤寂，使凄凉 | To make a place empty and without people, or to make someone feel very sad and alone. | The hurricane desolated the coastal region. 飓风使沿海地区变得荒芜。| synonyms: devastate, ravage, ruin, wreck | antonyms: build, construct, develop, improve, populate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dēsōlātus*, past participle of dēsōlāre (“to abandon, forsake, lay waste”), from dē- (“un-, de-”) + sōlāre (“to make alone”), from sōlus (“alone”).
