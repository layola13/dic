palpus:/ˈpælpəs/ (US), /ˈpalpəs/ (UK)| n. |触须; 颚须|A segmented appendage near the mouth of an invertebrate, such as an insect or arachnid, used for sensing and manipulating food.|The spider used its palpus to explore the captured fly.|Synonyms: feeler, antenna|Antonyms: N/A|noun

palpi:/ˈpælpʌɪ/ (US), /ˈpalpaɪ/ (UK)| n. |触须; 颚须 (palpus的复数形式)|The plural form of palpus, referring to multiple segmented appendages near the mouth of an invertebrate.|The butterfly's palpi twitched as it landed on the flower.|Synonyms: feelers, antennae|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *palpus*, past participle of *palpāre* "to stroke, touch gently, feel," related to *palma* "palm of the hand."
