dilettante: /ˌdɪləˈtænti/  /ˌdɪləˈtɑːnti/ | n. | 业余爱好者；浅尝辄止的人 | A person who cultivates an area of interest, such as the arts, without real commitment or knowledge. | He's a bit of a dilettante when it comes to photography; he enjoys taking pictures, but he doesn't have much technical knowledge. 他在摄影方面有点像个业余爱好者；他喜欢拍照，但没有多少技术知识。 |  Example 2: She dabbled in painting as a dilettante, never taking classes or seriously studying technique. 她把绘画当作一种业余爱好，从来没有上过课或认真学习过技巧。 |  Synonyms: amateur, dabbler, superficial, trifler | Antonyms: professional, expert, connoisseur | noun

dilettanti: /ˌdɪləˈtænti/  /ˌdɪləˈtɑːnti/ | n. | 业余爱好者；浅尝辄止的人（复数） | Plural form of dilettante. | The gallery was filled with dilettanti, more interested in being seen than in appreciating the art. 画廊里挤满了业余爱好者，他们更感兴趣的是被人看到，而不是欣赏艺术。 | Example 2:  A group of dilettanti gathered to discuss the latest opera, though few had any real understanding of music. 一群业余爱好者聚集在一起讨论最新的歌剧，尽管很少有人真正了解音乐。| Synonyms: amateurs, dabblers | Antonyms: professionals, experts | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *dilettante*, present participle of *dilettare* "to delight," from Latin *delectare* "to delight, charm, allure."
