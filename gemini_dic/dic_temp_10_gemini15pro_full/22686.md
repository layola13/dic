bordman:/ˈbɔːrdmən/ | n. |  船员；水手；膳宿生| A sailor or someone who lives and eats at a boarding house. | The old bordman told tales of his voyages across the seven seas.  这位老水手讲述了他穿越七大洋的航海故事。 | Synonyms:  seaman, mariner, boarder | Antonyms: landsman | noun


Etymology: From Middle English *bordman, from bord (board, table) + man.  Originally referred to someone who ate at a common table or board.  The nautical meaning developed later, likely referring to sailors who ate and slept on board ship.
