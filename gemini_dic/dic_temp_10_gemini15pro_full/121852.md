misrepresentation:/ˌmɪsrɛprɪzɛnˈteɪʃən/| n.|虚假陈述，歪曲，误传|an act of describing something in a false way, either deliberately or unintentionally, that creates a wrong impression |The advertisement was a blatant misrepresentation of the product's capabilities. 这个广告公然歪曲了产品的功能。|Synonyms: distortion, falsification, misstatement, perversion|Antonyms: accurate representation, truth, honesty|noun

misrepresent:/ˌmɪsrɛprɪˈzɛnt/| vt.|歪曲，误传，虚报|to describe or present something in a false way, often deliberately, so that people have a wrong impression of it|The media misrepresented the politician's views. 媒体歪曲了这位政治家的观点。|Synonyms: distort, falsify, misstate, pervert|Antonyms: represent accurately, portray truthfully|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (prefix meaning 'wrong' or 'bad') + represent (verb meaning 'to describe or portray something') + -ation (suffix forming nouns from verbs).  The word ultimately derives from Latin *repraesentare*, meaning "to make present again, to exhibit."
