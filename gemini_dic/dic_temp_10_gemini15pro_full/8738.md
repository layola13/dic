antidepressants:/ˌæntidɪˈprɛsənts/| n.|抗抑郁药|A class of drugs used to treat depression, typically by increasing the levels of certain neurotransmitters in the brain.|She's been taking antidepressants for several months now. 她已经服用抗抑郁药好几个月了.|Synonyms: mood elevators, thymoleptics|Antonyms: stimulants, depressants|noun


antidepressant:/ˌæntidɪˈprɛsənt/| adj.|抗抑郁的|Relating to or used in the treatment of depression.|The doctor prescribed an antidepressant medication. 医生开了抗抑郁药。|Synonyms: mood-lifting, mood-elevating|Antonyms: depressant|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- (against) + depressant (something that lowers or reduces).  The term "depressant" in this context refers to the lowered mood and activity associated with depression.
