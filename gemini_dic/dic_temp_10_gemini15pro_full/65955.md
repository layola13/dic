exactors:/ɪɡˈzæktərz/| n. |勒索者，强索者，催收员|  A person who demands something, often money, from someone, especially without the right to do so or by using force or threats.| The villagers were terrorized by the tax exactors. 村民们被税吏恐吓。|近义词： extortionists, collectors, demanders |反义词: donors, givers | noun

etymology->
From Late Middle English *exacteur*, from Anglo-Norman *exacteur*, from Old French *esacteur* (modern French *exacteur*), from Latin *exactor*, agent noun from *exigō* (“I demand, require, enforce”).
