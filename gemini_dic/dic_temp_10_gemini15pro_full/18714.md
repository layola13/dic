bereaver: /bɪˈriːvər/ | n. | 丧亲者，使人丧失者 | Someone who deprives another of something or someone valued, especially through death.  A person who has been bereaved. |  The bereaver, overcome with grief, could barely speak.  丧亲者悲痛欲绝，几乎说不出话来。 | Synonyms: mourner, griever | Antonyms: comforter, consoler | noun

bereave: /bɪˈriːv/ | vt. | 使丧失，使痛失（尤指亲人）|  (of death) deprive (someone) of a close relation or friend.  Deprive (something) of a desirable element. | The accident bereaved them of their only son.  这场事故使他们失去了唯一的儿子。 The war bereaved her of her home and family. 战争使她失去了家园和家人。| Synonyms: deprive, strip, rob, dispossess | Antonyms: provide, give, endow | verb
