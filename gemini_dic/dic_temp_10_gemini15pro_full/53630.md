discernible:/dɪˈsɜːrnəbl/ /dɪˈsɝːnəbl/| adj.|可辨别的，可识别的|Clearly perceptible or distinguishable; detectable.|The faint outline of the mountains was barely discernible through the mist. 山脉的微弱轮廓在薄雾中几乎难以辨认。|The difference in quality is easily discernible.  质量上的差异很容易辨别。|近义词：perceptible, noticeable, detectable, apparent, evident, visible, observable|反义词：imperceptible, indistinguishable, invisible, unnoticeable, obscure|词性:形容词
discern:/dɪˈsɜːrn/ /dɪˈsɝːn/| v.|辨别，识别；看出，察觉|To perceive or recognize something, typically after careful observation or thought.|I could discern a figure in the distance. 我能辨认出远处有一个身影。|He discerned a note of anger in her voice. 他察觉到她语气中带着一丝愤怒。|近义词：perceive, detect, recognize, distinguish, make out|反义词：miss, overlook, ignore|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *discernable*, from Old French *discernable*, from Late Latin *discernibilis*, from Latin *discernō* (“distinguish between, separate, divide, decide”) (from *dis-* (“apart, asunder”) + *cernō* (“distinguish, separate, perceive, decree”)).
