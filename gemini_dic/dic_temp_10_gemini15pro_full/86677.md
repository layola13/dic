hellholes:/ˈhɛlhoʊlz/| n. |地狱般的地方；极其糟糕的地方|A very unpleasant place, often dangerous or depressing. |The refugee camp was described as a living hellhole.  这个难民营被描述为人间地狱。

The prison was a dark and overcrowded hellhole. 这座监狱是一个黑暗而拥挤的地狱般的地方。|近义词：cesspools, dumps, nightmares, horrors |反义词: paradises, havens |词性: 名词

Etymology:  Compound of "hell" (from Old English "hel,"  the underworld) and "hole" (from Old English "hol," a cavity or hollow).  The combination emphasizes the unpleasant and confining nature of the place.
