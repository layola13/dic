legitimators:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmeɪtərz/
n.
合法化者；赋予合法性的人或事物
Those who give legitimacy to something or someone.  Those who make something or someone seem acceptable or valid.
The United Nations acted as legitimators for the new government. 联合国使新政府合法化。
International observers served as legitimators for the election process. 国际观察员使选举过程合法化。
近义词: validators, ratifiers, authorizers
反义词: delegitimizers, denouncers, condemners
词性:noun


legitimate:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmət/
adj.
合法的；正当的；合理的
Conforming to the law or to rules. Able to be defended with logic or justification; valid.
The government is a legitimate authority. 政府是合法当局。
He had a legitimate reason for being late. 他迟到有正当理由。
近义词: lawful, legal, valid, justifiable
反义词: illegal, illegitimate, invalid, unjustified
词性:adjective


legitimate:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmeɪt/
v.
使合法化；证明…为合法
To make something legal or lawful. To show something to be justified or valid.
The new law legitimized the sale of alcohol. 新法律使酒类销售合法化。
The government tried to legitimate its actions. 政府试图使其行为合法化。
近义词: validate, ratify, authorize, justify
反义词: delegitimize, denounce, condemn
词性:verb


legitimacy:/ləˈdʒɪtɪməsi/
n.
合法性；正统性
Conformity to the law or to rules. The quality of being justifiable or valid.
The legitimacy of the government was questioned. 政府的合法性受到质疑。
The study questioned the legitimacy of the claims. 这项研究质疑了这些说法的合法性。
近义词: lawfulness, legality, validity, justifiability
反义词: illegality, illegitimacy, invalidity
词性:noun


词源:
From Late Middle English: legitimat, from Latin lēgitimātus, past participle of lēgitimāre ‘to make lawful,’ from lēgitimus ‘lawful,’ from lex, lēg- ‘law.’
