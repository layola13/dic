faintness:/ˈfeɪntnəs/| n.|虚弱，无力，昏厥|The faintness overwhelmed her and she collapsed.  虚弱感让她难以承受，最终晕倒了。|例句：She experienced a sudden faintness and had to sit down. 她突然感到一阵虚弱，不得不坐下来。

例句：The heat and the long journey caused faintness among the travelers.  高温和长途旅行使旅客们感到虚弱。


近义词：weakness, dizziness, lightheadedness, giddiness|反义词:strength, vigor|词性:名词

faint:/feɪnt/| adj.|微弱的，模糊的，虚弱的|There was a faint smell of smoke in the air.  空气中有一丝淡淡的烟味。|例句：She felt faint with hunger. 她饿得头晕眼花。

例句：He heard a faint cry for help in the distance. 他听到远处传来微弱的呼救声。

近义词：weak, slight, dim, feeble|反义词:strong, clear, distinct|词性:形容词

faint:/feɪnt/| v.|昏厥，晕倒|He fainted from the heat. 他因高温而晕倒了。|例句：She nearly fainted when she heard the news. 听到这个消息，她差点晕倒了。

例句：He fainted at the sight of blood. 他一看到血就晕倒了。


近义词：swoon, collapse, pass out|反义词:revive, recover|词性:动词

词源: From Old French *feint, past participle of feindre “to feign, pretend,” from Latin fingere "to shape, form, invent, feign," from PIE root *dheigh- "to form, build." The sense of "lose consciousness" developed from the idea of "pretending to be dead."
