deflect:/dɪˈflekt/ | v. | 使偏斜；使转向；转移；|To cause (something) to change direction by hitting or touching a surface at an angle.|The bullet was deflected by the metal plate. 子弹被金属板弹开了。|The politician deflected the question by changing the subject. 政治家通过改变话题来转移问题。|synonyms: divert, deviate, avert, sidetrack|antonyms: attract, focus, direct|verb


deflection:/dɪˈflekʃən/ | n. | 偏斜；转向；转移|The act of deflecting something.|The deflection of the light beam created a rainbow. 光束的偏斜形成了彩虹。|The deflection of criticism is a common political tactic. 转移批评是常见的政治策略。|synonyms: deviation, divergence, digression|antonyms: attraction, convergence|noun
