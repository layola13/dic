gossips:/ˈɡɒsɪps/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːsɪps/ (US)| n. vi.|流言蜚语；闲聊；说闲话的人|Casual or unconstrained conversation or reports about other people, typically involving details that are not confirmed as being true.  (n.) The act of spreading such rumors. (v.) To engage in gossip.|She enjoys reading celebrity gossips. 她喜欢看名人八卦。|Synonyms: rumors, whispers, hearsay, tattle, chatter|Antonyms: facts, truth, reality|noun

gossips:/ˈɡɒsɪps/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːsɪps/ (US)| vi.|闲聊；说闲话|To talk informally, especially about other people.|They gossiped about their neighbors all afternoon. 他们整个下午都在谈论邻居的闲话。|Synonyms: chat, chatter, prattle, gab|Antonyms: be silent, keep quiet|verb
