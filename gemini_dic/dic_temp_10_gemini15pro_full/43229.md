crackers:/ˈkrækərz/ (US) /ˈkrækəz/ (UK)| n.|薄脆饼干；鞭炮；爆竹|Thin, crisp biscuits, often eaten with cheese or other savory toppings; also, firecrackers or fireworks that make a sharp cracking sound.|

例句：
I like to eat cheese and crackers. 我喜欢吃奶酪和饼干。

The children were excited to light the crackers on New Year's Eve. 孩子们很兴奋能在除夕夜点鞭炮。


近义词：biscuits, snacks (for food); firecrackers, fireworks (for explosives)|
反义词:(No direct antonym, depends on the context.  For food, perhaps "soft food". For explosives, "duds")|
词性:noun|

crackers:/ˈkrækərz/ (US) /ˈkrækəz/ (UK)| adj. (informal)|疯狂的；精神错乱的；极好的，一流的|Insane; mentally unstable; excellent, first-rate (especially British English).|

例句：
He went crackers after losing his job. 他失业后精神崩溃了。

That band played a crackers gig last night.  (UK) 那支乐队昨晚的演出棒极了。

近义词：crazy, insane, mad (for mentally unstable); excellent, brilliant, fantastic (for excellent)|
反义词：sane, rational (for mentally unstable); terrible, awful (for excellent)|
词性:adjective|
