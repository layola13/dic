overbalanced:/ˌoʊvərˈbælənst/ | adj. v. | 失去平衡的，过重的；使失去平衡，使过重 |  out of balance, having more weight on one side than the other (adjective); to cause something to lose its balance (verb) | The canoe overbalanced and tipped us into the water. 独木舟失去了平衡，我们翻进了水里。| 近义词：topheavy, unstable, outweigh,  | 反义词: balanced, stable | 词性:形容词, 动词

overbalance:/ˌoʊvərˈbæləns/ | v. | 使失去平衡，使过重 | To cause something to lose its balance. |  His sudden movement overbalanced the chair. 他的突然移动使椅子失去了平衡。 | 近义词：upset, capsize, tip over | 反义词: stabilize, balance | 词性:动词


词源：
From over- + balance.  "Over-"  is a prefix meaning "excessively," "too much," or "beyond." "Balance" comes from Old French "balance" (scales, balance), from Late Latin "bilanx" (having two scales), from Latin "bis" (twice) + "lanx" (plate, dish, scale of a balance).
