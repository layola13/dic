lipid:/ˈlɪpɪd/| n. |脂质；类脂|Any of a class of organic compounds that are fatty acids or their derivatives and are insoluble in water but soluble in organic solvents. They include many natural oils, waxes, and steroids.|Lipids are essential for cell function. 脂质对细胞功能至关重要。| High levels of lipids in the blood can be harmful.  血液中高水平的脂质可能是有害的。|Synonyms: fat, oil, wax|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider "water-soluble compounds")|Noun
