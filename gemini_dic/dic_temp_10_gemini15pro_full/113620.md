machiavellianism: /ˌmækiəˈveliənɪzəm/ | n. | 马基雅维利主义；权术；不择手段 |  Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian political philosopher whose book "The Prince" described how rulers should maintain power, even if it meant using deceit and manipulation. |  His machiavellianism helped him climb the corporate ladder quickly. 他的马基雅维利主义使他很快地爬上了公司的阶梯。 | synonyms: cunning, duplicity, ruthlessness, artifice, guile | antonyms:  honesty, integrity, forthrightness | noun
