buttoned-up:/ˈbʌtn̩d ˈʌp/ | adj. | 拘谨的，保守的，一本正经的 | Reserved, conventional, and somewhat repressed or inhibited in behavior or appearance. Often implies a rigid adherence to formality and a lack of spontaneity. |  He always seemed so buttoned-up at work, never cracking a smile. 他在工作中总是显得那么拘谨，从不笑。 |  She wore a buttoned-up blouse and a serious expression. 她穿着一件扣子扣得严严实实的衬衫，表情严肃。| 近义词: reserved, formal, conservative, prim, proper, straitlaced, uptight | 反义词: relaxed, informal, casual, easygoing, open, expressive | 词性: 形容词

詞源:  This term originates from the literal act of buttoning up one's clothing, especially a suit or coat.  The image of being fully buttoned suggests a sense of formality and restraint, which extended metaphorically to describe a person's demeanor.
