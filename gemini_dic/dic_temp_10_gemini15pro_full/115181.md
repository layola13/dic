manichaeisms:/ˌmænɪˈkiːɪzəmz/| n. |摩尼教主义；二元论；绝对善恶观|The doctrines or practices of Manichaeism, a religion founded in the 3rd century by Mani, characterized by dualism, especially regarding good and evil. Also, any system of thought resembling Manichaeism.|His worldview reflected certain Manichaeisms, seeing everything as a battle between good and evil.|Synonyms: dualism, absolutism|Antonyms: holism, relativism|noun
