eavesdrop:/ˈiːvzdrɒp/ (US) /ˈiːvzdrɒp/ (UK)| vi. n.|偷听，窃听|Secretly listen to a conversation.|例句：She was eavesdropping on their conversation from behind the curtain. 她躲在窗帘后偷听他们的谈话。

例句：He was punished for eavesdropping on private conversations. 他因偷听私人谈话而受到惩罚。|近义词：listen in, overhear, snoop|反义词：ignore, disregard|词性：动词 (vi.), 名词 (n.)

eavesdrop (n.): /ˈiːvzdrɒp/ (US) /ˈiːvzdrɒp/ (UK)| n. |偷听，窃听（名词形式较少用）|The act of secretly listening to a conversation.|例句：The eavesdrop revealed important information. 这次偷听揭露了重要信息。

例句：His eavesdrop gave him an unfair advantage in the negotiations. 他的偷听让他在谈判中获得了不公平的优势。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *evesdroppen*, from *eves* "eaves" + *droppen* "to drip, drop," referring to the practice of standing under the eaves of a house to listen to conversations within.  The original sense was related to water dripping from the eaves.  Later, it evolved to mean listening to conversations taking place within the house.
