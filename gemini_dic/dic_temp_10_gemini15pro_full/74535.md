fraternizer: /ˈfrætərnaɪzər/ | n. | 交往者， fraternizer | Someone who fraternizes, especially with members of a hostile group. |  The soldier was reprimanded for fraternizing with the enemy.  这个士兵因与敌人 fraternize 而受到谴责。| synonyms: socializer, mixer, mingler | antonyms: isolator, recluse | noun


fraternize: /ˈfrætərnaɪz/ | v. | 交往，亲善 | To associate or mingle with others in a friendly or amiable way, especially with those of a different group or nation. | The officers were warned not to fraternize with the enlisted men.  军官们被警告不要与士兵们 fraternize。| synonyms: socialize, mingle, consort | antonyms: shun, avoid, ostracize | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fraterniser*, from Latin *fraternus* "brotherly," from *frater* "brother".  The original sense was "to treat as a brother."
