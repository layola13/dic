词源:  From Late Latin *desponsationem, accusative of desponsatio, from desponsare "to betroth," from de- "from, away" + sponsare "to promise solemnly," from sponsus "bridegroom," past participle of spondere "to promise solemnly."  The original sense was of promising something away, specifically a daughter in marriage.
