galumphing:/ɡəˈlʌmfɪŋ/| v. | 笨拙地行走，得意洋洋地走 | Moving in a clumsy, heavy-footed way, often with a sense of boisterous delight. | He galumphed across the field, a wide grin on his face. 他得意洋洋地穿过田野，脸上挂着灿烂的笑容。|The children galumphed down the stairs, eager to open their presents. 孩子们笨手笨脚地走下楼梯，急于打开他们的礼物。|近义词：lumbering, stomping, clomping |反义词：tiptoeing, gliding, creeping |词性:动词

词源:

Coined by Lewis Carroll in "Through the Looking-Glass" (1871), likely a blend of "gallop" and "triumph."  由刘易斯·卡罗尔在《爱丽丝镜中奇遇记》（1871 年）中创造，可能是“gallop”（飞奔）和“triumph”（胜利）的混合词。
