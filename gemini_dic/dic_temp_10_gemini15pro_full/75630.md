fugitivenesses:/ˌfjuːdʒɪˈtɪvnəsɪz/
|n.|逃亡，短暂，易逝|The state or quality of being fugitive; transience.|The fugitivenesses of youth's pleasures are often lamented in old age.  青春的快乐转瞬即逝，这在老年时期常常令人感叹。|Synonyms: transience, ephemerality, brevity, impermanence|Antonyms: permanence, eternity, durability|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From fugitive + -ness.  Fugitive itself comes from Late Latin *fugitīvus, from Latin fugit-, past participle stem of fugere "to flee."
