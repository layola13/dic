inhabitiveness:/ɪnˌhæbɪtɪvnəs/
n.
居住性；定居性；恋家性
The desire to stay in a particular place or environment, or the quality of a place that makes it desirable to live in.
Pigeons have strong inhabitiveness and will often return to the same location.  鸽子有很强的恋家性，经常会回到同一个地方。
Her inhabitiveness kept her rooted to the small town where she grew up. 她的恋家情结使她扎根于她长大的小镇。
Synonyms:  domesticity, settledness, attachment to home
Antonyms:  nomadism, wanderlust, restlessness
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From inhabit + -ive + -ness.  Inhabit comes from the Latin inhabitare, meaning "to dwell in".
