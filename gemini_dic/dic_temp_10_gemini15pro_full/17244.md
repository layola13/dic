bayad:/baɪˈjɑːd/| adj. n.| paid, due, fee, payment |Bayad is a Tagalog word that generally translates to "paid," "due,"  "fee," or "payment" in English. It signifies something that has been or needs to be settled financially.  It can also function as a noun referring to the payment itself. |例句：Na-bayad ko na ang utang ko. = I have already paid my debt.|近义词：settled, cleared, paid off|反义词:unpaid, due, outstanding|词性:adjective, noun
Etymology:  Austronesian origin, likely related to other Tagalog words concerning exchange or value.  Further etymological details are scarce in readily available resources.
