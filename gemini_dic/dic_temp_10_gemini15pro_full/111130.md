literati:/ˌlɪtəˈrɑːti/ | n. | 文人; 文学界; | Well-educated people who are interested in literature. | The literati were quick to criticize the new author's work.  文人墨客很快批评了这位新作家作品。| synonyms: intellectuals, scholars, men/women of letters | antonyms: philistines, ignoramuses | noun

literati:/ˌlɪtəˈrɑːti/ | n. | 文人; 学者 |  A collective term for people who are knowledgeable about literature. | The event was attended by many of the city's literati.  该活动有许多城市文人参加。 | synonyms: intelligentsia, academics | antonyms: unlearned, uneducated | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *litteratus* "lettered, educated," past participle of *litterare* "to instruct in letters," from *littera* "letter, literature." Borrowed into English in the 17th century.
