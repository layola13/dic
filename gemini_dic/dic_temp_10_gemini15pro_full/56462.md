dolphin:/ˈdɒlfɪn/ (UK) /ˈdɑːlfɪn/ (US)| n.|海豚|A small toothed whale with a pointed snout and a curved fin on the back.|Dolphins are known for their intelligence. 海豚以其智力而闻名。|The dolphin jumped out of the water. 海豚跳出了水面。|近义词：porpoise, cetacean|反义词:None|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dolfin*, from Old French *daulphin*, from Medieval Latin *dolfinus*, from Latin *delphinus*, from Ancient Greek *δελφίς (delphís)*. Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European root *ǵwelh₁- (“womb, belly”)*, referring to the dolphin's shape.
