fleabane:/ˈfliːbeɪn/| n. |蚤草；飞蓬|Any of various plants, especially of the genera *Erigeron* and *Conyza*, that were formerly believed to repel fleas.|The field was covered in white fleabane.|近义词：daisy, horseweed|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *flebane*, from Old English *flēabāne*, from *flēa* (flea) + *bāne* (bane, killer).  So literally, "flea killer".
