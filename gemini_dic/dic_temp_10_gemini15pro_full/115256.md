manipulative:/məˈnɪpjʊlətɪv/| adj.|操纵的，控制的，善于摆布的|Inclined or having the skill to manipulate someone or something, often in a dishonest way for personal gain.|She's very manipulative - she always gets what she wants. 她很会操纵别人——她总是能得到她想要的。|The politician was accused of using manipulative tactics to gain votes.  这位政客被指控使用操纵策略来获得选票。|近义词：controlling, scheming, exploitative, crafty, wily|反义词：honest, straightforward, forthright, genuine, artless|词性:形容词

Etymology: From Late Latin *manipulativus*, from Latin *manipulus* ("handful, troop") + *-ativus*.  The original meaning referred to handling things skillfully, but the modern sense carries a negative connotation of dishonest or unfair influence.
