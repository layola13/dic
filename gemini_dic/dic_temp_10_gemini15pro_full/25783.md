bureaucracy: /bjʊˈrɒkrəsi/ (UK) /bjʊˈrɑːkrəsi/ (US)
n.
官僚主义；官僚机构；官僚作风
A system of government in which most of the important decisions are made by state officials rather than by elected representatives.  A complex administrative system characterized by excessive paperwork, procedures, and regulations.
The reforms are designed to reduce bureaucracy. (这些改革旨在减少官僚主义。)
Dealing with the bureaucracy was a nightmare. (与官僚机构打交道是一场噩梦。)
近义词: red tape, officialdom, administration
反义词: democracy, meritocracy
词性: noun

Etymology: From French *bureaucratie*, from *bureau* (“desk, office”) + *-cratie* (from Greek *-kratia*, from *kratos* "power, rule").  Ultimately from Late Latin *burra* (“coarse woolen cloth”), referring to the baize used to cover desks.
