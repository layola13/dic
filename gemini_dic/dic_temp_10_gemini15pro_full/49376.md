delirious:/dɪˈlɪəriəs/| adj.|神志不清的；精神错乱的；发狂的；极其兴奋的|Being in a state of mental confusion, often accompanied by hallucinations and restlessness; wildly excited.|例句：He became delirious from the fever. 他因为发烧而神志不清。

例句：She was delirious with joy after winning the lottery. 她中了彩票后欣喜若狂。|近义词：mad, insane, frenzied, ecstatic|反义词：lucid, sane, rational|词性:形容词

delirium:/dɪˈlɪriəm/| n.|谵妄；神志不清；精神错乱；极度兴奋|A state of mental confusion, often accompanied by hallucinations and restlessness; wild excitement.|例句：He suffered from delirium caused by high fever. 他因高烧而出现谵妄。

例句：The crowd was in a delirium of excitement.  人群欣喜若狂。|近义词：madness, insanity, frenzy, ecstasy|反义词：lucidity, sanity, rationality|词性:名词


词源:
From Latin dēlīrāre (“to go off the furrow, be crazy”), from dē (“off, away from”) + līra (“furrow, ridge between furrows”).
