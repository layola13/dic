convivialists:/kənˈvɪviəˌlɪsts/
|n.|  欢乐者；好交际的人；酒宴上活跃的人
|People who are fond of feasting, drinking, and good company; sociable people who enjoy lively gatherings.|
|The convivialists gathered around the table, laughing and sharing stories. 欢乐者们围坐在桌子旁，笑着分享故事。|
|The party was filled with convivialists, making it a truly enjoyable evening. 派对上挤满了爱热闹的人，使得当晚的气氛非常愉快。|
|近义词：socializers, partygoers, merrymakers, revelers, companions|
|反义词：loners, recluses, introverts|
|词性: noun (plural)|


convivial:/kənˈvɪviəl/
|adj.|欢乐的；好交际的；酒宴上活跃的
|Friendly, lively, and enjoyable, especially when involving eating, drinking, and good company.|
|The convivial atmosphere of the pub made it a popular gathering spot. 酒馆里欢乐的气氛使它成为一个受欢迎的聚会场所。|
|He was a convivial host, always making sure his guests were well entertained. 他是一个热情好客的主人，总是确保他的客人玩得开心。|
|近义词：jovial, sociable, festive, merry, cheerful|
|反义词：unsociable, unfriendly, dour, sullen|
|词性: adjective|

词源:
From Late Latin convivialis, from Latin convivium ‘a feast,’ from con- ‘together’ + vivere ‘to live.’
