coddling:/ˈkɒdlɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːdlɪŋ/ (US)| n. v. adj.|溺爱，娇惯；呵护；炖蛋|The act of treating someone with excessive care and indulgence, often to the point of hindering their development or resilience; gentle cooking in liquid, often used for eggs; overly protective or indulgent.|She resented her mother's coddling. 她讨厌她母亲的溺爱。|近义词：pampering, babying, spoiling, overprotecting|反义词:neglecting, toughening, disciplining|词性:noun, verb, adjective

coddle:/ˈkɒdl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːdl/ (US)|vt.|溺爱，娇惯；呵护；慢煮|To treat someone with excessive care and indulgence, often to the point of hindering their development or resilience; to cook gently in liquid.|She coddled her youngest child. 她溺爱她最小的孩子。|近义词：pamper, baby, spoil, overprotect|反义词:neglect, toughen, discipline|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *codlen, from codle "to cook gently," from Old Northern French *codler "to stew, boil," of uncertain origin.
