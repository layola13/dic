magnet:/ˈmæɡnət/| n.|磁铁，磁体；有吸引力的人或物|A magnet is an object that produces a magnetic field and attracts iron and some other metals.|The fridge magnets held up her children's drawings. 冰箱上的磁铁吸附着她孩子们的画。|Synonyms: lodestone, attraction|Antonyms: repellent|noun


magnets:/ˈmæɡnəts/| n.|磁铁，磁体（magnet的复数形式）|The plural form of magnet, referring to more than one magnet.|She bought some colorful magnets as souvenirs. 她买了一些彩色磁铁作为纪念品。|Synonyms: lodestones, attractions|Antonyms: repellents|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *magnete* (Modern French *aimant*), from Latin *magnetum* (nominative *magnes*), from Greek *Μάγνης (lithos)* "Magnesian (stone)", referring to Magnesia, a region in Thessaly, Greece, where magnetic ore was found.
