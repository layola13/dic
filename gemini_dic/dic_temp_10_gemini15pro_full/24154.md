bribes:/braɪbz/| n. |贿赂; 行贿; 贿赂物;|Something, typically money, offered or given to someone, especially someone in a position of authority, in order to persuade them to act dishonestly in one's favor.|He was accused of accepting bribes from arms dealers. 他被指控接受军火商的贿赂。|Synonyms: payoff, kickback, inducement|Antonyms: reward, gift (when given legitimately)|noun

bribe:/braɪb/| vt.|贿赂; 向…行贿;|To give someone money or something else of value, typically secretly, in order to persuade them to do something, especially something dishonest or illegal.|He bribed the officials to approve his plans. 他贿赂官员批准他的计划。|Synonyms: corrupt, buy off, pay off|Antonyms: dissuade, deter|verb
