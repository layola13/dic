boothale:/ˈbuːθeɪl/| n.|宴席啤酒，庆典麦酒|A kind of strong ale brewed for a festive occasion, especially a wedding.|The boothale flowed freely at the village wedding. 村里的婚礼上宴席啤酒源源不断地供应着。|近义词：ale, beer, brew|反义词: None|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *boutale*, from Old English *beodend ealu* (literally "ale of the inhabitants," with *beodend* being present participle of *beodan* "to offer, command").  Compare with "bidale," a similar word for a public feast where guests contributed provisions.
