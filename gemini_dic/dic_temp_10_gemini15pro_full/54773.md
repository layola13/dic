dispeller:/dɪˈspɛlər/
n.|驱散者，消除者|Someone or something that dispels (drives away) something unpleasant, such as fear, doubt, or a misconception.|The new evidence acted as a dispeller of the rumors.|近义词：remover, eliminator, erradicator|反义词：creator, instigator, promoter|词性:名词

Etymology:
From dispel + -er.  Dispel comes from the Old French *despeler, from des- (apart) + peler (to drive, push).  Ultimately from the Latin expellere (to drive out).
