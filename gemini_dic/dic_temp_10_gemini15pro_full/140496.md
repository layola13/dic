old-fashioned:/ˌoʊldˈfæʃənd/| adj.|老式的，过时的，守旧的|Characteristic of a past era, especially in a way considered quaint or charming; not modern or fashionable.|She wore an old-fashioned dress to the party. 她穿着一件老式连衣裙去参加聚会。|近义词：outdated, antiquated, traditional, conventional, retro|反义词：modern, contemporary, fashionable, trendy|词性:形容词
Etymology: From old + fashioned, past participle of fashion ("to make, shape, mold"), from Old French *faschon ("make, form"), from Latin factio ("a making, a doing"), from facere ("to make, do").
