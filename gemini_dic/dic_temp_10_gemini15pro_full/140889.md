on your guard:/ɒn jɔː(r) ɡɑːd/ /ɑːn jʊr ɡɑrd/
adjective
警惕的，提防的
Being watchful and prepared for danger or trouble.
例句：Be on your guard against pickpockets in crowded areas.  在拥挤的地方要小心扒手。

例句：She was always on her guard around strangers.  她在陌生人面前总是很警惕。

近义词：wary, cautious, vigilant, alert
反义词：careless, unguarded, trusting, naive
词性: adjective


on guard:/ɒn ɡɑːd/ /ɑːn ɡɑrd/
adjective, adverb
站岗，值班; 警惕着，提防着
Performing the duty of a guard; watchful and prepared for danger or trouble.
例句：The soldiers were on guard at the palace gates. 士兵们在宫殿门口站岗。

例句：He stayed on guard all night, fearing an attack. 他整夜保持警惕，担心遭到袭击。

近义词：watchful, vigilant, alert
反义词：unaware, unguarded, inattentive
词性: adjective, adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
"On guard" originates from the French phrase "en garde," a fencing term meaning "on your guard" or "prepare to defend yourself." It was adopted into English in the 17th century and retains its connection to vigilance and preparedness.
