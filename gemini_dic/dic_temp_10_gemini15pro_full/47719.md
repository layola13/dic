decadency:/ˈdɛkədənsi/ /dɪˈkeɪdənsi/| n. |颓废，衰落；堕落|A state of moral or cultural decline or decay.  A period of decline or decay in morals, art, literature, etc.|The historian documented the decadency of the Roman Empire.  历史学家记录了罗马帝国的衰落。|Near synonyms: decline, decay, degeneration, deterioration|Near antonyms:  growth, development, progress, improvement|noun

decadent:/ˈdɛkədənt/ /dɪˈkeɪdənt/| adj. |颓废的，衰落的；堕落的|Characterized by or reflecting a state of moral or cultural decline.|The decadent lifestyle of the wealthy elite was criticized by many.  许多人批评富裕精英的颓废生活方式。|Near synonyms: corrupt, degenerate, dissolute, immoral|Near antonyms: virtuous, moral, upright, wholesome|adjective
