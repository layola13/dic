empiricism:/ɪmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/
n.
经验主义
The theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience. Stimulated by the rise of experimental science, it developed in the 17th and 18th centuries, expounded in particular by John Locke, George Berkeley, and David Hume.
Empiricism emphasizes the role of experience and evidence, especially sensory perception, in the formation of ideas, over the notion of innate ideas or traditions.
例句：
Modern science is based on empiricism. 现代科学是建立在经验主义的基础之上的。

The doctor's diagnosis was based on empiricism rather than theory.  这位医生的诊断是基于经验而不是理论。

近义词：experientialism, pragmatism, positivism
反义词：rationalism, idealism, apriorism
词性：noun
