discreditably:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪtəbli/
adverb|不光彩地，丢脸地|In a manner that brings discredit or disgrace; shamefully.|He behaved discreditably at the party, embarrassing his family. 他在聚会上的行为不光彩，让他的家人感到尴尬。|近义词：shamefully, disgracefully, ignominiously|反义词：honorably, creditably, respectably
词性: 副词

discredit:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪt/
verb|使丢脸，败坏名声|To harm the reputation of; to cause to be doubted or disbelieved.|The scandal discredited the politician. 这桩丑闻败坏了这位政治家的名声。|近义词：disgrace, shame, dishonor|反义词：honor, praise, commend
词性: 动词

discredit:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪt/
noun|耻辱，丢脸|Loss or damage to reputation; something that causes disgrace.|The accusations brought discredit upon the entire organization. 这些指控给整个组织带来了耻辱。|近义词：disgrace, shame, dishonor|反义词：honor, credit, prestige
词性: 名词

词源:  From Late Latin *discreditāre, from Latin dis- "not" + creditāre "to believe, trust" (from creditum, past participle of credere "to believe").
