machiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | adj. | 马基雅维利的；权谋术的；不择手段的 |  Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian political philosopher whose book "The Prince" described obtaining and maintaining political power by any means necessary, often viewed as immoral. |  His machiavellian tactics ensured he won the election, even though he lost the respect of many. 他不择手段的策略确保他赢得了选举，尽管他失去了很多人的尊重。|  Synonyms: cunning, devious, scheming, unscrupulous, artful, wily | Antonyms: honest, ethical, principled, straightforward | adjective
