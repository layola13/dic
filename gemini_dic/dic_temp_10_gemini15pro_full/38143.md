compresses:/kəmˈprɛsɪz/| v.|压缩，压紧|To press or squeeze something together so that it becomes smaller or more compact. |The nurse compresses the wound to stop the bleeding. 护士按压伤口以止血。|synonyms: condense, compact, squeeze, crush|antonyms: expand, enlarge, stretch|verb (third-person singular simple present compresses, present participle compressing, simple past compressed)


compress:/ˈkɒmprɛs/| n.|敷布，压布|A pad of material pressed onto a part of the body to relieve pain or reduce inflammation.|The doctor applied a cold compress to his forehead. 医生在他的额头上敷了一块冷敷布。|synonyms: pad, dressing, poultice|antonyms: N/A|noun


compress:/kəmˈprɛs/| v.|压缩（文件）|To reduce the size of a computer file so that it takes up less storage space.|The program can compress files into a ZIP archive.  这个程序可以将文件压缩成 ZIP 存档。|synonyms: condense, compact, zip|antonyms: expand, extract, unzip|verb (third-person singular simple present compresses, present participle compressing, simple past compressed)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French compresser, from Latin compress-, past participle stem of comprimere ‘press together’, from com- ‘together’ + premere ‘to press’.
