inconceivability:/ˌɪnkənˌsiːvəˈbɪləti/| n. | 不可思议，不可想像|The inconceivability of the situation made it difficult to process.  这种情况的不可思议性使得它难以理解。|例句：The sheer inconceivability of winning the lottery kept her from buying a ticket.  赢得彩票的绝对不可思议性让她没有买票。|近义词：unthinkableness, implausibility, impossibility|反义词：conceivability, possibility, plausibility|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From in- (not) + conceivable + -ity (noun suffix).  Conceivable comes from conceive (to form an idea or plan), from Old French concevoir, from Latin concipere (to take hold of, conceive), from com- (together) + capere (to take).
