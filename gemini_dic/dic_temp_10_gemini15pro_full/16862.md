basihyal:/ˈbeɪsiˌhaɪəl/| n.|基舌骨|The basihyal is a single bone forming the base of the hyoid apparatus in many vertebrates. |The basihyal is a prominent feature in the frog's vocalization mechanism. 基舌骨是青蛙发声机制中的一个显著特征。|Synonyms: body of hyoid, hyoid body|Antonyms: N/A|noun
Etymology->
From basi- +‎ hyoid, from Ancient Greek ὑοειδής (huoeidḗs, “U-shaped”), from ὗς (hûs, “pig”) +‎ -ειδής (-eidḗs, “-shaped, -like”).  The "basi-" prefix denotes "base" or "foundation".
