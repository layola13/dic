bury: /ˈberi/ (US), /ˈbɛri/ (UK) | v. | 埋葬，掩埋；隐藏，遮盖 | to place (a dead body) in the earth, a tomb, etc; to cover something completely; to hide something |  He was buried in the local cemetery. 他被安葬在当地墓地。| synonyms: inter, entomb, conceal, hide | antonyms: unearth, exhume, uncover, reveal | verb

bury: /ˈberi/ (US), /ˈbɛri/ (UK) | n. |  (英国方言)大宅邸，庄园 | a large house or mansion, especially in the country (chiefly British) |  They lived in a grand bury in the countryside. 他们住在乡间的一座宏伟的庄园里。| synonyms: mansion, manor, estate | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *byrgan, from Proto-Germanic *burjaną, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰerǵʰ- (“to hide, protect, save”).
