cfc:/ˌsiːɛfˈsiː/ | n. |氟氯化碳|Chlorofluorocarbons, synthetic organic compounds first created in the 1930s. They were commonly used as refrigerants, propellants (in aerosol sprays), and solvents.  |CFCs were widely used in refrigerators until it was discovered they were damaging the ozone layer. 氟氯化碳曾被广泛用于冰箱，直到人们发现它们会破坏臭氧层。|Synonyms: freon, refrigerant|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but alternatives include HCFCs and HFCs)|noun

cfc:/ˌsiːɛfˈsiː/ |abbr.| 氯氟烃; 氟氯碳化物| Abbreviation for chlorofluorocarbon. |CFCs have been banned in many countries because of their harmful effects on the ozone layer.  许多国家已经禁止使用氟氯碳化物，因为它们对臭氧层有有害影响。|Synonyms:  freon|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but alternatives include HCFCs and HFCs)|abbreviation
