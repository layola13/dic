/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ /ɛnˈfræntʃaɪz/
verb
给予选举权；使享有权利
To grant the privileges of citizenship, especially the right to vote.
The Nineteenth Amendment enfranchised women in the United States.  美国第十九修正案赋予了妇女选举权。

/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ /ɛnˈfræntʃaɪz/
verb
解放；赋予权力
To free, as from slavery or bondage; liberate.
The organization worked to enfranchise the oppressed people.  该组织致力于解放被压迫的人民。

Etymology:
From Old French enfranchiser, from en- "in" + franchir "to free," from franc "free." Ultimately from Frankish *frank "free," referring to the status of the Franks as free people in the Roman Empire.

Synonyms:
empower, liberate, free, emancipate

Antonyms:
disenfranchise, enslave, oppress, restrict
