maybloom:/ˈmeɪbluːm/| n.|五月花；春天的花朵；青春|A flower that blooms in May; a symbol of spring and youth; can also refer to a person who embodies youthful beauty and vitality. |The maybloom filled the air with a sweet fragrance. 五月花使得空气中充满了甜美的芬芳。| The poet compared the young girl's beauty to a delicate maybloom. 诗人把这位年轻女孩的美貌比作娇嫩的五月花。|近义词：blossom, spring flower|反义词:winter flower|词性:名词

词源:
Compound word from "May" (referring to the month) and "bloom" (meaning to flower).  "May" comes from Old English "Maius," ultimately from Latin "Maius" (of Maia, goddess of growth). "Bloom" comes from Old Norse "blóma," meaning "flower, blossom."
