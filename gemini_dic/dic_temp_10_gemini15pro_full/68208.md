fair-weather:/ˈferˈwɛðər/ | adj. | 好天气的；晴天的；（喻）只能同甘不能共苦的；虚假的 |  (Figuratively) describes someone who is only friendly or supportive during good times, abandoning others during difficulty.  Derived from the literal meaning of weather suitable for outdoor activities. |  He's a fair-weather friend; don't expect him to be there when things get tough. 他只是一个酒肉朋友；别指望他在你困难的时候出现。| Synonyms: fickle, unreliable, inconstant | Antonyms: loyal, steadfast, true | adjective


fair-weather:/ˈferˈwɛðər/ | n. | 好天气 |  A period of good weather.  | We had fair weather for the entire trip. 我们整个旅程都是好天气。| Synonyms: good weather, sunshine | Antonyms: bad weather, storm | noun
