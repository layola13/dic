briefcases: /ˈbriːfˌkeɪsɪz/
noun
公文包（复数）
A flat, rectangular case, typically made of leather or other sturdy material, used for carrying documents, papers, and other business-related items.
The lawyer carried two briefcases into the courtroom. 律师带着两个公文包走进了法庭。
Synonyms: attaché cases, portfolios
Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps backpacks or satchels for a different carrying style)
