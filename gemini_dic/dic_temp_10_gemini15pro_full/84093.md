half-ingenuousnesses:/ˌhæfˌɪnˈdʒɛnjuːəsnɪsɪz/
| n. | 半天真; 半坦率 | A state of being partially or seemingly ingenuous, often suggesting a mixture of innocence and cunning. |  His half-ingenuousnesses made it difficult to determine if he was being truthful or manipulative. 他的半坦率让人很难判断他是在说实话还是在耍花招。 |  partial ingenuousness, affected naivete |  disingenuousness, guile | noun

Etymology:
half- (prefix) + ingenuousness (noun)

*   "half-" from Old English "healf," meaning "one of two equal parts."
*   "ingenuousness" from Late Latin "ingenuitas," derived from "ingenuus" meaning "native, freeborn," hence "noble, frank, sincere." "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns expressing a state or quality.
