fulminator:/ˈfʊlmɪneɪtər/| n. |雷管；起爆剂；严厉谴责者|Something that causes something else to explode or detonate suddenly and violently; someone who criticizes harshly.|The fulminator detonated the explosive charge. 雷管引爆了炸药。|近义词：detonator, exploder, critic, denouncer|反义词:supporter, advocate|词性:noun

fulminating:/ˈfʊlmɪneɪtɪŋ/| adj. |爆炸的；怒喝的；迅猛的|Exploding or detonating suddenly and violently; speaking or expressing oneself in a forceful and angry way; occurring suddenly and with great intensity.|The fulminating attack took the enemy by surprise. 猛烈的攻击打了敌人一个措手不及。 |近义词：explosive, detonating, thundering, raging|反义词:gentle, calm, mild|词性:adjective

fulminate:/ˈfʊlmɪneɪt/| v. |爆炸；怒喝；谴责|To explode or detonate suddenly and violently; to speak or express oneself in a forceful and angry way; to issue a condemnation or denunciation.|The general fulminated against the incompetence of his officers. 将军怒斥军官们的无能。|近义词：explode, detonate, thunder, rage, denounce|反义词:praise, commend, support|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin fulminatus, past participle of fulminare "to strike with lightning," from Latin fulmen "lightning flash, thunderbolt," from fulgere "to flash, gleam."
