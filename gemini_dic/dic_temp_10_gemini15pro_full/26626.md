cack-handedness:/ˌkækˈhændɪdnəs/ | n. | 笨拙，不灵巧 | Awkwardness or clumsiness, especially in the use of the hands. |  His cack-handedness in the kitchen resulted in a burnt dinner. 他在厨房的笨拙导致晚餐烧焦了。 | clumsiness, awkwardness, ineptness, gaucheness | dexterity, skillfulness, adroitness | noun

cack-handed:/ˌkækˈhændɪd/ | adj. | 笨手笨脚的 | Awkward or clumsy, especially in the use of the hands. | He made a cack-handed attempt to fix the leaky faucet. 他笨手笨脚地试图修理漏水的水龙头。 | clumsy, awkward, inept, gauche | skillful, dexterous, adroit | adjective

From 'cack' (dialectal, meaning left-handed, awkward) + 'handed' + '-ness' (suffix forming nouns of quality or state). 'Cack' is likely related to Old Norse 'keikr' (bent, crooked).
