actuary: /ˈæktʃʊəri/ (US) /ˈæktʃʊəri/ (UK)| n. |精算师|A person who compiles and analyzes statistics and uses them to calculate insurance risks and premiums.|The actuary calculated the probability of a major earthquake in the region.  这位精算师计算了该地区发生大地震的概率。|Synonyms: statistician, analyst|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *actuarius* "clerk, recorder," from Latin *actus* "a doing, a driving, an impulse," from *agere* "to drive, lead, act." Originally referred to a clerk or registrar, especially in Roman courts. The modern sense developed in the 18th century with the rise of insurance and the need for statistical analysis.
