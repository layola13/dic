destituteness:/ˈdɛstɪtjuːtnəs/| n.|赤贫；穷困；缺乏|The state of being destitute; extreme poverty and lack of resources.|The earthquake left thousands of people in utter destituteness.|近义词：poverty, indigence, penury, impoverishment|反义词：wealth, affluence, prosperity|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From destitute + -ness.  Destitute comes from Latin *dēstitūtus, past participle of dēsistere “to abandon, leave, forsake,” from de- “from, away” + sistere “to stand, place, set”.
