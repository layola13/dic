disorganization:/dɪsˌɔːrɡənəˈzeɪʃn/ |n.| 混乱，无序 |Lack of organization or orderly arrangement. | The disorganization of the office made it difficult to find anything. 办公室的混乱使得很难找到任何东西。| The disorganization in the house was starting to stress her out. 家里的杂乱无章让她开始感到压力很大。|近义词: disorder, chaos, mess, confusion |反义词: organization, order, tidiness |词性: 名词

disorganize:/dɪsˈɔːrɡənaɪz/ |v.| 使混乱，扰乱 |To destroy the organization or systematic arrangement of; throw into disorder. | The sudden storm disorganized our picnic plans. 突如其来的暴风雨打乱了我们的野餐计划。|  Moving house completely disorganized my life. 搬家彻底打乱了我的生活。 | 近义词: disrupt, disorder, unsettle, confuse | 反义词: organize, order, arrange | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation, reversal) + organization.  The prefix "dis-" comes from Latin, meaning "apart," "asunder," "away," "utterly," or having a privative, negative, or reversing force.  "Organization" comes from Old French organisation and ultimately from Latin organum, meaning "instrument" or "organ."
