miterer:/ˈmaɪtərər/| n.|主教冠，斜接冠|A tall, pointed headdress worn by bishops and other high-ranking clergy, especially in ceremonial contexts.  A miter is a symbol of episcopal authority.|The bishop donned his miter for the ordination ceremony. 主教在授任仪式上戴上了主教冠。|Synonyms:  headdress, crown, tiara|Antonyms: None (lack of a specific antonym for a ceremonial headpiece)|noun
