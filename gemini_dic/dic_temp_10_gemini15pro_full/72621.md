flyer:/ˈflaɪər/ (US) /ˈflaɪə(r)/ (UK)| n.|传单；飞行物；飞鸟；飞行员|A piece of paper, often printed, that advertises something or gives information about something; something that flies, such as a bird or insect; a person who flies an aircraft.|Example 1: They handed out flyers advertising the concert. 他们分发了宣传音乐会的传单。

Example 2: The bird was a graceful flyer. 这只鸟飞翔姿态优美。|Synonyms: leaflet, handbill, circular, aviator, pilot|Antonyms: walker, grounded (for pilot/aircraft)|noun


flyer:/ˈflaɪər/ (US) /ˈflaɪə(r)/ (UK)| n.|冒险者；投机者|Someone who takes a risk, especially in financial matters.|Example 1: He's a flyer in the stock market. 他是股票市场的投机者。

Example 2:  She's known as a bit of a flyer, always trying new and risky ventures. 她被认为有点冒险，总是尝试新的冒险事业。|Synonyms: risk-taker, gambler, speculator|Antonyms: cautious person, conservative investor|noun
