gem:/dʒɛm/ | n. | 宝石；珍宝；精华；人才 | a precious or semiprecious stone, especially when cut and polished or engraved.  Something that is highly prized, valued, or excellent. |  This ring has a beautiful gem.  这枚戒指上有一颗美丽的宝石。 | synonyms: jewel, treasure, prize, masterpiece | antonyms: junk, trash | noun

gem:/dʒɛm/ | v. |  点缀；用宝石装饰 | to decorate with gems |  The crown was gemmed with diamonds and rubies.  王冠上镶嵌着钻石和红宝石。 | synonyms: adorn, embellish, decorate | antonyms: strip, bare | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "geme" (Modern French "gemme"), from Latin "gemma" meaning "bud, precious stone," perhaps from Proto-Indo-European "*yeb-" meaning "to take hold of, grasp."
