illocution:/ˌɪləˈkjuːʃən/ | n. | 言外之意；施为句；言外行为 | An illocutionary act is the performance of an act in saying something (as opposed to the locutionary act of saying something meaningful and the perlocutionary act of producing consequential effects on the audience). It is what is done in saying something, often termed the illocutionary force.  |  "By saying 'I do,' at a wedding, the speaker is performing the illocutionary act of marrying."  我愿意（在婚礼上说）说话者正在执行结婚的言外行为。

| Synonym:  speech act, performative utterance, pragmatic force | Antonym:  locution (this contrasts with the *act* of illocution, not its meaning), perlocution (another aspect of the speech act theory, distinct from illocution) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *illocutio*,  meaning "speaking out," from *in-* "in, on" + *locutio* "speech, speaking," from *loqui* "to speak."  The term was introduced into speech act theory by J.L. Austin.
