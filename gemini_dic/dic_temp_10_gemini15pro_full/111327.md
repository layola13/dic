live-ins:/ˈlɪvɪnz/ | n. |同居者，住家佣人|People who live together, especially in a sexual relationship without being married, or domestic help who live in the employer's house. |例句：The live-ins helped with the children and housework. 住家佣人帮忙照顾孩子和做家务。

例句：Many young couples choose live-ins before marriage. 很多年轻夫妇在结婚前选择同居。|近义词：cohabitants, roommates (for those sharing accommodation), domestic staff (for live-in help) |反义词: (No direct opposite, perhaps) landlords (if the live-ins are renting), employers (if they are employed staff)|词性:noun
