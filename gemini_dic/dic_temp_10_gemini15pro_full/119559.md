microsurgery:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌsɜːrdʒəri/| n.|显微外科手术；显微手术|Surgery performed using a microscope and miniaturized instruments, typically on very small structures such as nerves and blood vessels.|例句：Microsurgery is often used to reattach severed limbs. 显微外科手术常用于断肢再植。

例句：The surgeon performed delicate microsurgery on the patient's eye.  外科医生对病人的眼睛进行了精细的显微外科手术。


|近义词：minimally invasive surgery|反义词:macrosurgery|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From micro- + surgery. "Micro-" comes from Greek "mikros" meaning "small".  "Surgery" ultimately derives from Greek "kheirourgia," from "kheir" (hand) + "ergon" (work).
