brinksmanship: /ˈbrɪŋkmənʃɪp/ | n. |边缘政策；冒险策略 | The practice of pushing a dangerous situation to the limit of safety in order to force a desired outcome. |  The two countries engaged in a dangerous game of brinksmanship, each threatening to use nuclear weapons. 这两个国家进行了一场危险的边缘政策游戏，每个国家都威胁要使用核武器。

|  His brinksmanship during the negotiations almost cost him the deal.  他在谈判中的边缘政策几乎让他失去了这笔交易。 | synonyms:  gambling, daring, high-stakes diplomacy, coercive diplomacy | antonyms: caution, prudence, compromise, negotiation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From brink (edge) + -manship (skill, art).  Coined in the mid-20th century, popularized by US Secretary of State John Foster Dulles during the Cold War.
