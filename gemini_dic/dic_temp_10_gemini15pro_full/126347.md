myriad:/ˈmɪriəd/| adj. n.|无数的，大量的；无数，大量|A countless or extremely great number.|例句：There are myriad stars in the night sky. 夜空中有无数的星星。|例句：They face myriad problems. 他们面临着无数的问题。|近义词：innumerable, countless, numerous, manifold|反义词:few, scarce|词性:形容词, 名词

myriad:/ˈmɪriəd/| n.|无数，大量|A countless or extremely great number.|例句：A myriad of possibilities lay before her. 她面前有无限的可能。|例句：The city offers a myriad of attractions for visitors. 这座城市为游客提供了大量的景点。|近义词：host, multitude, abundance|反义词:lack, scarcity|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *myriade*, from Late Latin *myrias*, from Ancient Greek *μυριάς (myriás)*, meaning "ten thousand". Originally referring to a specific number (10,000), it later came to mean an indefinitely large number.
