antiscians:/ænˈtɪʃənz/
n.
对蹠人；住在相对纬度上的人
people living on opposite sides of the equator, especially those whose shadows at noon are cast in opposite directions because their latitudes are equally distant from the equator
The Greek geographer Strabo discussed the concept of antiscians.  希腊地理学家斯特拉博讨论过对蹠人的概念。
The antiscians experience opposite seasons. 对蹠人经历相反的季节。
近义词: antipodes
反义词: None
词性: Noun


Etymology:
From Late Latin *antiscii, from Ancient Greek ἀντίσκιοι (antískioi, “those who cast shadows opposite one another”), from ἀντί (antí, “opposite, against”) + σκιά (skiá, “shadow”).
