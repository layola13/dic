brace-up:/breɪs ˈʌp/| phrasal verb | 振作起来；打起精神；做好准备 | To prepare oneself physically or mentally for something difficult or unpleasant.  It can also mean to strengthen or support something physically. |例句：He braced himself up for the bad news. 他做好了听到坏消息的心理准备。

例句：She braced herself up to tell him the truth. 她鼓起勇气告诉他真相。|近义词：prepare, steel oneself, gird oneself, strengthen |反义词: relax, unwind |词性:动词短语

brace-up:/breɪs ˈʌp/| n. | 振作；支撑 | The act of bracing oneself or something else. (Noun form is less common) | 例句：The brace-up of the old bridge prevented its collapse.  对旧桥的加固防止了它倒塌。

例句：He gave himself a mental brace-up before the interview. 他在面试前让自己精神振作了一下。|近义词：reinforcement, support |反义词: weakening |词性:名词
