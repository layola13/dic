blandisher:/ˈblændɪʃər/| n.|奉承者，甜言蜜语者|A person who blandishes; one who uses flattery. |He was a blandisher, always praising people to get what he wanted. 他是个奉承者，总是赞扬别人以得到他想要的东西。|近义词：flatterer, sycophant, adulator|反义词：critic, detractor|noun

blandish:/ˈblændɪʃ/| v.|奉承，哄骗，甜言蜜语|To coax or influence by gentle flattery; to cajole. |She blandished him with promises of wealth and power. 她用财富和权力的承诺来哄骗他。|近义词：coax, cajole, flatter, wheedle|反义词：criticize, condemn|verb

blandishment:/ˈblændɪʃmənt/| n.|奉承，甜言蜜语，哄骗|Something used to blandish; flattery or cajolery. |He resisted her blandishments and refused to change his mind. 他抵制了她的甜言蜜语，拒绝改变主意。|近义词：flattery, cajolery, allurement|反义词：criticism, condemnation|noun
