girondist: /ʒɪˈrɒndɪst/ (British) /ʒɪˈrɑːndɪst/ (American)| n. |吉伦特派（成员）|A member of a moderate republican political faction during the French Revolution, named after the department of Gironde.|The Girondists advocated for a constitutional monarchy and opposed the execution of Louis XVI. 吉伦特派主张君主立宪制，反对处死路易十六。|Synonyms: moderate, republican|Antonyms: Jacobin, radical|Noun
