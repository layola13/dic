casuarina:/ˌkæʒuəˈriːnə/ | n. | 木麻黄 | An evergreen tree of the genus Casuarina, native to Australia, Southeast Asia, and islands of the western Pacific Ocean, typically having needlelike leaves and woody cones.  They are known for their distinctive jointed branchlets that resemble pine needles. | The casuarina trees lined the beach, providing welcome shade. 木麻黄树排列在海滩上，提供了受欢迎的阴凉处。 | Casuarina trees are often planted as windbreaks. 木麻黄树经常被种植作为防风林。 | synonym: she-oak, Australian pine | antonym: (No direct antonym for a specific tree) | noun
