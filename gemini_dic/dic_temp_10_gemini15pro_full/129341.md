noisinesses:/ˈnɔɪzinəsiz/| n. |嘈杂声，喧闹声|The state or quality of being noisy; a collection of noisy sounds or instances of noise.  It refers to multiple instances or occurrences of noise.| The noisinesses of the city kept him awake all night. 城市的各种噪音让他整夜无法入睡。|近义词：din, clamor, racket, hubbub|反义词：quiet, silence, stillness|词性: Noun

词源: From "noisy" + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns of state or quality) + "-es" (plural suffix).  "Noisy" ultimately comes from the Middle English "noyse," from Anglo-French.
