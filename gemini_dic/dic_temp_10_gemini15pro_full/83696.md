haggler:/ˈhæɡlər/| n.|讨价还价者；讲价的人|A person who bargains persistently, especially over the price of something.|She's a tough haggler and always gets a good deal. 她是个很厉害的讨价还价者，总是能拿到好价钱。|Synonyms: bargainer, negotiator, barterer|Antonyms: acquiescer, pushover|noun


haggle:/ˈhæɡəl/| v.|讨价还价；争论|To bargain persistently, especially over the price of something.|We haggled with the street vendor over the price of the souvenirs. 我们和街边小贩就纪念品的价钱讨价还价。|Synonyms: bargain, negotiate, barter|Antonyms: acquiesce, concede|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Low German *hakkelen "to chop, hack," also "to dispute, quarrel," probably from a Low German variant of Old Norse höggva "to hew, strike." The sense development likely went from "to chop" to "to argue over small points" to "to bargain."
