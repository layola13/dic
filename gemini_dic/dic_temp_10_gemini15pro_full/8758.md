antidogmatism:/ˌæntiˈdɒɡmətɪzəm/
n.|反教条主义|Opposition to or rejection of dogmatism; the belief that no doctrine or dogma can claim absolute or final authority.|The rise of antidogmatism in the 20th century led to a questioning of established truths.|近义词:open-mindedness, skepticism, liberalism|反义词:dogmatism, orthodoxy, rigidity|词性:名词

Etymology:
From anti- + dogmatism.  "Anti-" is a prefix of Greek origin meaning "against," "opposite of," "instead of." "Dogmatism" comes from the Late Latin "dogmatismus," meaning "philosophical tenet," and ultimately from the Greek "dógma," meaning "opinion," "belief," or "decree."
