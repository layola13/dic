fail-safe:/ˈfeɪlˌseɪf/ | adj. n. |自动防故障的；故障保险装置|Designed to return to a safe condition in the event of a failure. |例句：The plane has a fail-safe system that will allow it to land even if the engines fail. 这架飞机有一个故障保险系统，即使发动机失效也能让它着陆。

例句：The nuclear reactor has several fail-safe mechanisms to prevent a meltdown. 核反应堆有几个故障保险装置，以防止熔毁。|近义词：reliable, dependable, foolproof |反义词:risky, dangerous |词性:形容词, 名词


fail-safe:/ˈfeɪlˌseɪf/ | n. |故障保险装置|A device or system that is designed to return to a safe condition in the event of a failure. |例句：The engineers installed a fail-safe to prevent the system from crashing. 工程师安装了一个故障保险装置，以防止系统崩溃。

例句：The bridge's fail-safe prevented a complete collapse. 桥梁的故障保险装置防止了完全倒塌。|近义词：safety device, backup system |反义词: N/A |词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
