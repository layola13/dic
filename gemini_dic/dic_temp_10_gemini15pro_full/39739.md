consociate:/kənˈsoʊʃieɪt/ /kənˈsəʊsiɪt/| adj. n. v. |联合的，结交的；伙伴，同事；使联合，结交|A consociate is a partner or colleague, especially in a profession or business. It can also describe something that is united in a league or association.  As a verb, it means to unite in or form a league or association.|

例句：
The lawyers consociated to form a new firm.  律师们联合起来成立了一家新公司。

The two companies are consociates in a joint venture. 这两家公司是合资企业的伙伴。

近义词：associate, partner, colleague, ally, confederate|
反义词：opponent, adversary, enemy|
词性：adjective, noun, verb|

Etymology:
From Latin consociatus, past participle of consociare ("to associate together, unite, join"), from con- ("together") + sociare ("to associate"), from socius ("companion, ally").
