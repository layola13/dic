commensurability:/kəˌmɛnʃərəˈbɪləti/| n. |可公度性，相称性|The state of being commensurable; having a common measure.  Two quantities are commensurable if their ratio can be expressed as a rational number. This concept is important in mathematics, particularly in geometry and number theory, and also has applications in philosophy and other fields.  It implies a shared standard or unit of measurement that allows for comparison and meaningful relationships.|The commensurability of the two lengths made it easy to construct the geometric figure. 这两个长度的可公度性使得构建几何图形变得容易。|Synonyms: proportionality, compatibility, comparability|Antonyms: incommensurability, disproportionality, incompatibility|noun


commensurate:/kəˈmɛnʃərət/| adj. |相称的，相当的|Corresponding in size or degree; proportionate.  It implies a suitable or appropriate relationship between two things.|Her salary is commensurate with her experience. 她的薪水与她的经验相称。|Synonyms: proportionate, corresponding, equivalent, appropriate|Antonyms: disproportionate, unequal, inadequate|adjective
