feodary:/ˈfjuːdəri/ /ˈfiːdəri/| n. adj.|封臣，封地；封建的|A person who held land under the conditions of feudal service; relating to or characteristic of feudalism.  A feudal tenant; feudal.|例句：The king rewarded his loyal feodary with a large estate. 国王用一大片地产奖励了他忠诚的封臣。

例句：The feodary system was prevalent in medieval Europe. 封建制度在中世纪的欧洲很盛行。|近义词：vassal, liegeman, feudal|反义词:lord, overlord|词性:名词，形容词

feodary:/ˈfjuːdəri/ /ˈfiːdəri/| adj.|封建的|Relating to or characteristic of feudalism.|例句：The feodary system was complex and hierarchical. 封建制度复杂且等级森严。

例句：Feodary obligations included military service and loyalty to the lord. 封建义务包括服兵役和效忠领主。|近义词：feudal|反义词:modern, contemporary|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *feudataire, from Medieval Latin feudatarius (“holding a fief”), from feudum (“fief”). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *fehu (“cattle, wealth”).
