palisade:/ˌpælɪˈseɪd/ /ˌpælɪˈsɑːd/| n. |栅栏，木栅；悬崖 |A fence of pointed stakes set firmly in the ground, especially as a defense against attack; a line of steep cliffs. |The village was protected by a palisade of sharpened logs. 这个村庄受到削尖的原木栅栏的保护。|The climbers scaled the sheer palisade of rock. 登山者攀登了陡峭的岩壁。|Synonyms: fence, barrier, cliff, precipice |Antonyms: opening, gap| noun


词源: From Old French *palissade, from palisser "to enclose with pales," from pal "pale, stake" (from Latin palus).
