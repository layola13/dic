estuary:/ˈestʃuˌeri/ (US), /ˈestʃʊəri/ (UK)| n. | 河口，港湾 |A partially enclosed coastal body of brackish water with one or more rivers or streams flowing into it, and with a free connection to the open sea.|The Thames estuary is home to a variety of wildlife. 泰晤士河口是各种野生动物的家园。| synonym: inlet, firth, bay | antonym: headwater, source | noun


estuary:/ˈestʃuˌeri/ (US), /ˈestʃʊəri/ (UK)| n. | 河口，港湾 |The wide part of a river where it joins the sea.| Many species of fish breed in estuaries. 许多种类的鱼在河口繁殖。| synonym: river mouth, delta | antonym: upstream | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin aestuarium "tidal part of a shore, marsh, creek," from aestus "tide, heat, boiling, surge," from PIE root *aidh- "to burn."
