coventry: /ˈkʌvəntri/ | n. |抵制；拒绝与…来往；排斥|A state of ostracism, where a person is deliberately ignored and excluded by a group or community.|例句：He was sent to Coventry by his colleagues for informing on them to the boss.  他因为向老板告发同事而被同事们排斥。

例句：After the argument, she was in Coventry with her friends. 争吵之后，她被朋友们排斥了。|近义词：ostracism, boycott, blackballing, exclusion, isolation|反义词：inclusion, acceptance, welcome|词性: 名词

词源:  This term originated from the English Civil War, where Royalist prisoners were sent to the Parliamentarian stronghold of Coventry. They were often ignored and ostracized by the local population.  这个词起源于英国内战时期，保皇党俘虏被送到议会派的要塞考文垂。他们经常被当地居民忽视和排斥。
