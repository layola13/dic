declarant:/dɪˈklɛərənt/| n.|申报人；报关人；声明人|A person who makes a formal statement or declaration, especially one made under oath or in legal proceedings. |The declarant testified in court. 申报人在法庭上作证。| synonym: testifier, witness, affiant| antonym:  (No direct antonym, consider perhaps "defendant" in some contexts)| noun

declarant:/dɪˈklɛərənt/| n.|申报者；报关者；宣誓人|Someone who officially declares something, often in writing or in a court of law.| The declarant signed the affidavit. 宣誓人在宣誓书上签了字。 | synonym: announcer, attester, deponent| antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "denier" in some contexts) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Old French word "declarant," present participle of "declarer," meaning "to declare," which comes from the Latin word "declarare," meaning "to make clear, to reveal, to explain."  Ultimately derived from the Latin "clarus," meaning "clear, bright."
