drudgers:/ˈdrʌdʒərz/| n.|苦工；做单调乏味工作的人|People who perform tedious or menial tasks. |The drudgers in the factory worked long hours for little pay. 工厂里的苦工长时间工作，却只能拿到微薄的薪水。|Synonyms: laborers, toilers, workers, grinders|Antonyms:  idlers, loafers|noun

Etymology: From the verb "drudge," which comes from Middle English *druggen*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *draga* "to draw, pull," suggesting the sense of "toil" or "heavy labor."
