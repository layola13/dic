courages:/ˈkʌrɪdʒɪz/
n.
勇气，胆量 (plural of courage)
The soldiers showed great courages in the face of danger.
士兵们在危险面前表现出极大的勇气。
Etymology: From Old French "corage" (heart, innermost feelings, mind, spirit, courage), derived from Vulgar Latin "*coraticum" (heart), ultimately from Latin "cor" (heart).
The word "courage" is typically uncountable, so its plural form "courages" is uncommon. It is usually used poetically or when referring to multiple acts of bravery by different individuals.
Synonym: bravery, valor, boldness
Antonym: fear, cowardice
