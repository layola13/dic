cunningness:/ˈkʌnɪŋnɪs/ | n. | 狡猾，诡诈，奸诈 | The quality of being cunning; skill in deception. | His cunningness allowed him to escape the trap. 他的狡猾让他逃脱了陷阱。| synonyms:  craftiness, guile, slyness, wiliness | antonyms:  honesty, frankness, openness, sincerity | noun


cunning:/ˈkʌnɪŋ/ | adj. | 狡猾的，精巧的 | Having or showing skill in achieving one's ends by deceit or evasion. |  A cunning plan. 一个狡猾的计划。 | synonyms:  sly, crafty, wily, artful | antonyms:  honest, frank, open, sincere | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cunninge*, from Old English *cunnung* (“knowledge, skill, experience”), from Proto-Germanic *kunnaną* (“to know, be able to”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵneh₃- (“to know”). Related to can.
