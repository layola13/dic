impasse:/ˈɪmpæs/ (US), /ˈɪmpɑːs/ (UK)| n.|僵局，死胡同|A situation in which no progress is possible, especially because of disagreement; a deadlock.|Negotiations have reached an impasse.  谈判陷入了僵局。|The project reached an impasse due to lack of funding.  由于缺乏资金，该项目陷入了僵局。|近义词：deadlock, stalemate, standstill|反义词：breakthrough, progress|词性: 名词

Etymology: From French *impasse*, from *im*- (not) + *passe* (passage). Ultimately from Latin *passus* (step).
