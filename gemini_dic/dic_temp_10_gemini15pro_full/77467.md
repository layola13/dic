gaylussite:/ˈɡeɪləsaɪt/ | n. | 盖吕萨克石 | A hydrated carbonate mineral containing calcium and sodium, Na₂Ca(CO₃)₂·5H₂O.  It is named after the French chemist Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac. | Gaylussite is often found in saline lakes. 盖吕萨克石常发现于盐湖中。|  Gaylussite crystals are typically colorless or white. 盖吕萨克石晶体通常是无色或白色的。 | Synonyms: none (as it's a specific mineral name) | Antonyms: N/A | Noun
