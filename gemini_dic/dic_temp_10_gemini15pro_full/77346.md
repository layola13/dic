gaudier:/ˈɡɔːdiər/ | adj. | 更花哨的，更俗丽的 | More gaudy; excessively showy or ornamented. |  The costumes in the new production were even gaudier than last year's.  新作品中的服装比去年的还要花哨。|  Synonyms: showier, flashier, more flamboyant, more ornate | Antonyms: simpler, plainer, more understated, more subdued | adjective

gaudy:/ˈɡɔːdi/ | adj. | 花哨的，俗丽的 | Extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless. | The carnival was a riot of gaudy colours and loud music.  嘉年华充满了花哨的色彩和喧闹的音乐。| Synonyms: flashy, garish, showy, flamboyant | Antonyms: simple, plain, understated, subdued | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gaudir "to rejoice," from Latin gaudere "to rejoice."  The sense evolution went from "joyful" to "brightly colored (associated with festive occasions)" to "showy, tasteless."
