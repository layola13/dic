leucoxene:/ˌluːkɒkˈsiːn/ | n. | 白钛石，白钛矿 | A white or yellowish mineral formed by the alteration of ilmenite or other titanium-bearing minerals, consisting mainly of titanium dioxide. | Leucoxene is a common accessory mineral in many igneous and metamorphic rocks. 白钛石是许多火成岩和变质岩中常见的副矿物。| Leucoxene is often mistaken for other titanium oxides, such as rutile and anatase. 白钛矿经常被误认为是其他钛氧化物，如金红石和锐钛矿。| Synonyms: ilmenite alteration product, titanium dioxide mineral | Antonyms: None | noun
