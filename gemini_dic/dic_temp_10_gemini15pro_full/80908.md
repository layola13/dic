gowd:/ɡuːld/ | n. | gold |  A precious yellow metal, highly valued for its beauty, resistance to corrosion, and malleability.  From Old English *gōld, from Proto-Germanic *gulþą, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰelh₃- (“yellow, green, bright”). |  She wore a gowd necklace. 她戴着一条金项链。|Synonyms:  gold, bullion, aureate | Antonyms: dross, worthless | noun
