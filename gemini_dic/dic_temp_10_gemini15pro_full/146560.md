overgraciously: /ˌoʊvərˈɡreɪʃəsli/ | adv. | 过分殷勤地，过分客气地 |  Excessively or overly gracious; polite or kind to an excessive degree, often to the point of seeming insincere.  | He thanked her overgraciously, making her feel uncomfortable. 他过分殷勤地感谢她，让她感到不舒服。 |  Example: The host overgraciously insisted we stay for another night, even though we had already made other plans. 主人过分热情地坚持让我们再住一晚，尽管我们已经有了其他安排。 | Synonyms: excessively politely, too kindly, overly courteously | Antonyms: rudely, impolitely, curtly | 词性: 副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "graciously" (adverb form of "gracious," from Old French "gracios," ultimately from Latin "gratia" meaning "grace, favor").
