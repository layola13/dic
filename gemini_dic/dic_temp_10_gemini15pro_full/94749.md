improvisation:/ˌɪmprəvaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 即兴创作；即兴表演；临时凑合 | The act of improvising; something that is improvised, especially a piece of music, drama, etc., created spontaneously or without preparation. |  The musician's improvisation on the piano was breathtaking.  这位音乐家在钢琴上的即兴演奏令人叹为观止。| synonyms: ad-libbing, extemporization, off-the-cuff performance | antonyms: preparation, rehearsal, premeditation | noun

improvise:/ˈɪmprəvaɪz/ | v. | 即兴创作；临时凑合；临时做 | To create and perform (music, drama, or verse) spontaneously or without preparation.  To make or do something using whatever is available. | The chef improvised a delicious meal from the leftovers.  厨师用剩菜即兴做了一顿美味的饭菜。 | synonyms: ad-lib, extemporize, wing it | antonyms: plan, prepare, rehearse | verb
