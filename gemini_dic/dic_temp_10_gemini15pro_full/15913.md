balls-up:/ˈbɔːlz ʌp/ (UK) /ˈbɑːlz ʌp/ (US)| n. | 搞砸；弄糟；一团糟 | A mess; a muddle; something that has gone wrong. |  The whole project was a complete balls-up.  整个项目彻底搞砸了。

balls-up:| n. | 混乱；糟糕的局面 | A chaotic or badly handled situation. | The organization of the event was a balls-up. 活动的组织工作一团糟。

balls-up:| v. | 搞砸；弄糟 | To make a mess of something; to spoil something. |  He really ballsed up the presentation. 他真的把演讲搞砸了。

balls-up:| n. | 失败；失误 | A failure; a mistake. |  Losing the contract was a major balls-up. 失去这份合同是一个重大失误。


近义词:mess, muddle, cock-up, screw-up, foul-up
反义词:success, triumph
词性: noun, verb (informal)


词源:
The origin is uncertain, but likely related to the idea of tangling something up like a ball of yarn.  Another theory suggests a link to the game of snooker, where "ballsed up" could refer to a poorly executed shot that leaves the balls in a difficult position. It's considered vulgar slang.
