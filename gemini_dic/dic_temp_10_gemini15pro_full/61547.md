embush:/ˈɛmˌbʊʃ/ (US), /ˈæmˌbʊʃ/ (UK)| n. v.|埋伏，伏击|An ambush is a surprise attack by people lying in wait in a concealed position.  As a verb, it means to attack someone from an ambush. |The soldiers were ambushed by enemy forces. 士兵们遭到了敌军的伏击。|近义词：ambuscade, trap, snare|反义词:open combat, fair fight|noun

embush:/ˈɛmˌbʊʃ/ (US), /ˈæmˌbʊʃ/ (UK)| v.|埋伏，伏击|To ambush someone means to attack them suddenly and unexpectedly from a hidden position. |The rebels planned to ambush the government convoy. 叛军计划伏击政府车队。|近义词：waylay, surprise, attack|反义词:confront, meet openly|verb
