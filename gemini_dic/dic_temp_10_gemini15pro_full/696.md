abseil: /ˈæbseɪl/ (British) /æpˈseɪl/ (American)| vi. & vt. & n.|绕绳下降；绳降|To descend a cliff or other steep slope by using a doubled rope coiled around the body and fixed at a higher point.|The climber abseiled down the rock face. = 登山者用绳索从岩壁上滑下来。

The team abseiled into the canyon. = 队员们顺着绳索下到峡谷里。|近义词：rappelling, roping down|反义词：climb, ascend|词性：动词(vi. & vt.)，名词(n.)

ETYMOLOGY->
From German *abseilen*, from *ab* ("down, off") + *seil* ("rope").  The word entered English in the 1950s.

USAGE->
