nonpalatalizations:/nɒnˌpælətəlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. | 非腭化 | The absence or lack of palatalization, a phonetic process where a consonant becomes more like a palatal consonant (such as /j/) due to the influence of a nearby vowel or another palatal sound.  It refers to instances where sounds *do not* undergo this shift, retaining their original articulation. |  The preservation of /k/ before front vowels in some dialects is an example of nonpalatalization.  这些方言中前元音前的/k/音的保留是非腭化现象的一个例子。| non-occurrence of palatalization| palatalization | noun
