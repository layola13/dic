encapsulation:/ɪnˌkæpsəˈleɪʃn/ | n. |封装，概括|The process of enclosing something in or as if in a capsule.  The act of expressing or representing something in a concise or comprehensive form. |The encapsulation of the drug ensures its slow release into the bloodstream.  药物的封装确保其缓慢释放到血液中。|  This documentary offers a powerful encapsulation of the civil rights movement. 这部纪录片对民权运动进行了强有力的概括。|synonyms: enclosure, containment, summary, abstract| antonyms: exposure, revelation, expansion, elaboration | noun

encapsulations:/ɪnˌkæpsəˈleɪʃnz/ | n. |封装，概括（复数）|Plural form of encapsulation, referring to multiple instances of enclosing something or summarizing something.|The various encapsulations of the vitamin protect it from degradation.  各种维生素的封装保护其免受降解。|  The speaker offered several concise encapsulations of complex philosophical ideas. 演讲者对复杂的哲学思想进行了几个简洁的概括。|synonyms: enclosures, containments, summaries, abstracts| antonyms: exposures, revelations, expansions, elaborations | noun
