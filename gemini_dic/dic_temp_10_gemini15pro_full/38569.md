concoct: /kənˈkɒkt/ (UK) /kənˈkɑːkt/ (US) | vt. | 调制，配制；编造，捏造 | To create or devise something, especially in a skillful or ingenious way, often by combining various ingredients or elements. |  She concocted a delicious soup from leftover vegetables. 她用剩下的蔬菜调制了一碗美味的汤。

The journalist concocted a story about the mayor. 记者编造了一篇关于市长的报道。 | 近义词：create, devise, invent, fabricate, formulate | 反义词：discover, find, reveal | 词性：动词

concoction: /kənˈkɒkʃən/ (UK) /kənˈkɑːkʃən/ (US) | n. | 调制品，混合物；捏造的故事，谎言 | Something that has been concocted, especially a mixture of various ingredients or a story that has been fabricated. | The drink was a strange concoction of fruit juices and spices.  这饮料是一种由果汁和香料混合而成的奇怪调制品。

His explanation was a pure concoction. 他的解释纯属捏造。| 近义词：mixture, blend, combination, fabrication, invention | 反义词：truth, reality, fact | 词性：名词
