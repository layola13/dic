mount:/maʊnt/| n. v.|山，峰;  登上，骑上;  安装，固定;  增加，上升|A large natural elevation of the earth's surface rising abruptly from the surrounding level; a landmass higher than a hill.  To go up or climb. To fix something to a support.  To increase in amount or intensity.|例句：The climbers tried to mount the treacherous peak. 登山者试图登上险峻的山峰。|近义词：ascend, climb, increase, rise, install|反义词:descend, decrease, dismount, remove|词性:noun, verb
mount:/maʊnt/| v.|安放，裱贴|To fix (a picture, photograph, etc.) in a frame or setting, or on a backing or support.|例句：She carefully mounted the dried flowers. 她小心翼翼地把干花裱了起来。|近义词:frame, set, fix|反义词:remove, dismantle|词性:verb
