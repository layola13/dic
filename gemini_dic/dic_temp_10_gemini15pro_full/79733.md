glugging:/ˈɡlʌɡɪŋ/| n. |汩汩声，咕嘟声|The sound of liquid being poured or drunk rapidly, with air bubbles.|例句：I heard the glugging sound of the water draining from the bathtub. 我听到了水从浴缸里排出的汩汩声。

例句：The glugging of the milk as he poured it into his glass was quite loud. 他把牛奶倒进杯子里的咕嘟声很大。|近义词：gurgling, sloshing, chugging|反义词:dripping, trickling|词性:noun

glugging:/ˈɡlʌɡɪŋ/| adj. (informal) |大量的，快速的（通常指喝饮料）|Describing the rapid consumption of a liquid, often in large quantities.|例句：He took a few glugging gulps of water after his run. 他跑完步后大口大口地喝了几口水。

例句：The thirsty hikers took glugging drinks from their water bottles.  口渴的徒步旅行者咕嘟咕嘟地喝着水瓶里的水。|近义词：guzzling, swilling|反义词:sipping, tasting|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "glug," which is imitative of the sound.  It likely originated in the late 19th century.
