deferred:/dɪˈfɜːrd/ /dəˈfɜːrd/| adj.| 延期的，推迟的|Postponed or delayed to a later time.|例句：The deferred payment plan allows customers to buy now and pay later.  延期付款计划允许顾客现在购买，以后付款。

例句：He has a lot of deferred maintenance on his house.  他的房子有很多延期维护要做。|近义词：postponed, delayed, shelved|反义词：immediate, prompt, advanced|词性:形容词

/dɪˈfɜːr/ /dəˈfɜːr/| v.| 推迟，延期|To postpone or delay (something) to a later time.|例句：They decided to defer the decision until next week. 他们决定将这个决定推迟到下周。

例句：She deferred her university entrance for a year to travel. 她为了旅行将大学入学推迟了一年。|近义词：postpone, delay, put off|反义词：expedite, hasten, advance|词性:动词

/dɪˈfɜːrəns/ /ˈdɛfərəns/| n.| 尊重，敬重|Respectful submission or yielding to the judgment, opinion, will, etc., of another.|例句：He showed deference to his elders. 他对长辈表示尊重。

例句：In deference to her wishes, they cancelled the party. 出于对她的尊重，他们取消了聚会。|近义词：respect, reverence, homage|反义词：disrespect, contempt, disregard|词性:名词


词源:  From Old French *deferrer, from Latin differre "to carry different ways, scatter, differ, disagree, put off," from dis- "apart" + ferre "to carry, bear."
