fixers:/ˈfɪksərz/ | n. | 修理工，固定器，中间人，摆平事情的人 | People who arrange things, often in business or politics, sometimes using unofficial or illicit methods. They also can refer to people who repair things. | The film crew hired local fixers to secure permits and locations.  电影摄制组雇佣了当地的中间人来获得许可证和拍摄地点。 | synonyms:  agent, negotiator, troubleshooter, repairman | antonyms: saboteur, disrupter | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "fix," from Middle English fixen, from Old French fixer, from Late Latin fixare "to fasten, make firm," from Latin fixus, past participle of figere "to fix, fasten." The -er suffix denotes an agent or one who performs the action of the verb.
