over-fertilizes: /ˌoʊvərˈfɜːrtəˌlaɪzɪz/ | v. | 过度施肥 | Applies too much fertilizer to (soil or plants).  The act of adding more fertilizer than necessary for plant growth, which can have negative environmental impacts. | Example: The farmer over-fertilizes his crops, leading to runoff that pollutes the nearby river.  这位农民过度施肥，导致径流污染了附近的河流。

Example: Over-fertilizing your lawn can harm the grass and the environment. 过度施肥会损害草坪和环境。 | 近义词: overfeed, saturate (with fertilizer) | 反义词: under-fertilize | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
"over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *ubari, meaning "above, over") + "fertilize" (from Late Latin fertilis "fertile," from Latin ferre "to bear, carry," related to "bring forth").  Thus, "over-fertilize" literally means to fertilize excessively.
