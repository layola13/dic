amphorae:/ˈæmfəriː/| n. |双耳陶罐，双耳瓶（amphora的复数）|A large, ancient Greek or Roman jar with two handles and a narrow neck, used for storing oil, wine, or other liquids. |The archaeologists unearthed several amphorae containing traces of olive oil. 考古学家挖掘出几个装有橄榄油痕迹的双耳陶罐。|
The museum displayed a collection of beautifully decorated amphorae.  博物馆展出了一系列装饰精美的双耳陶罐。|
Synonyms: jar, vase, urn, flagon|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a specific container type)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin amphora, from Ancient Greek ἀμφορεύς (amphoreús), from ἀμφι- (amphi-, “on both sides”) + φέρω (phérō, “I carry”).  So literally, "carried on both sides" referring to the two handles.
