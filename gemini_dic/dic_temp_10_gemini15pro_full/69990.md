fetch-and-carry:/ˈfɛtʃ ən ˈkæri/| adj. n.|跑腿的，杂役；跑腿，打杂|The act of fetching and carrying things, often implying menial or unskilled labor.  It can also be used adjectivally to describe work of this nature.|He had a fetch-and-carry job at the office. 他在办公室做跑腿的工作。|synonyms:  gofer, errand-boy, dogsbody|antonyms:  manager, executive|adjective, noun

fetch:/fɛtʃ/| v. n.|取来，接来；拿来；(货物)售价，卖得(多少钱)|To go to another place to get something or someone and bring them back.|Could you fetch me my glasses from the other room, please? 你能帮我从另一个房间把眼镜拿过来吗？|synonyms: retrieve, get, obtain|antonyms: leave, abandon|verb, noun

carry:/ˈkæri/| v. n.|提；抱；扛；输送；传送；传播；携带|To support and move something from one place to another.|The waiter carried the tray of drinks to our table. 服务员把托盘里的饮料端到我们桌子上。|synonyms: transport, convey, bear|antonyms: drop, leave|verb, noun
