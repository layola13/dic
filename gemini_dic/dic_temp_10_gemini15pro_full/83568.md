haemapodous:/ˌhiːməˈpɒdəs/| adj.|具足的，有柄足的|Having feet adapted for walking, especially over mud or soft ground.  Relating to or characteristic of Haemapoda, a group of crustaceans.  Sometimes used to describe insects or other arthropods with similar leg adaptations.|The haemapodous insect moved easily across the muddy terrain.|Near synonyms:  Gressorial, cursorial, ambulatory |Antonyms:  Sessile, fossorial|adjective
