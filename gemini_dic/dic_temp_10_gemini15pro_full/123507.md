monothelitism:/ˌmɒnəˈθiːlɪtɪzəm/ |n.|一志论|The doctrine that Christ had only one will, the divine, despite having two natures (human and divine).  This doctrine was condemned as heresy by the Third Council of Constantinople in 680-681.|Monothelitism was a major theological controversy in the 7th century.  一志论是七世纪的一个主要神学争论。|Near synonyms: Monergism|Antonyms: Dyothelitism|noun
