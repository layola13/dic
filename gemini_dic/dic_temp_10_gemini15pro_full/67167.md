expostulate: /ɪkˈspɒstjʊleɪt/ /ɪkˈspɑːstʃəleɪt/
verb
抗议；劝诫；告诫；责难
To reason earnestly with someone against something that person intends to do or has done; remonstrate.
例句：She expostulated with him about the risk involved. 她劝诫他不要冒险。
例句：He expostulated with the authorities about the injustice of the decision. 他向当局抗议这项决定的不公正。
近义词：remonstrate, protest, object, argue, plead
反义词：agree, consent, approve, support
词性：v.

expostulation: /ɪkˌspɒstʃʊˈleɪʃn/ /ɪkˌspɑːstʃəˈleɪʃn/
noun
抗议；劝诫；告诫；责难
The act of expostulating; remonstrance.
例句：His expostulations were ignored. 他的抗议被忽视了。
例句：Her expostulation with her son about his messy room fell on deaf ears. 她劝诫儿子打扫房间，但儿子置若罔闻。
近义词：remonstrance, protest, objection, argument, plea
反义词：agreement, consent, approval, support
词性：n.

词源:  Late Middle English: from Latin *expostulat- ‘demanded urgently’, from the verb expostulare, from ex- ‘out, fully’ + postulare ‘demand’.
