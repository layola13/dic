leafier: /ˈliːfiər/ | adj. | 多叶的；叶茂盛的；枝繁叶茂的 | Having more leaves or foliage than something else being compared to.  | The tree on the left is leafier than the one on the right.  左边的树比右边的树叶茂盛。 | The garden is much leafier this year than last. 今年的花园比去年枝繁叶茂。 |  近义词: lush, verdant, bushy | 反义词: bare, sparse, leafless | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From leaf + -y + -er (comparative suffix).  "Leaf" comes from Old English "lēaf,"  related to Dutch "loof" and German "Laub."  The "-y" suffix forms adjectives indicating "having the quality of" or "full of," while "-er" marks the comparative degree.
