dafter:/ˈdæftər/| adj.|愚蠢的，傻的|Silly or foolish; crazy.|He’s completely dafter, spending all his money on lottery tickets.|近义词：silly, foolish, stupid, crazy, insane|反义词：sensible, wise, intelligent|词性:形容词
词源: From Middle English *dafter, from Old Norse *daft, from Proto-Germanic *daf-, from Proto-Indo-European *(dh₂bʰ-) (to fit, be suitable). Related to deft.  The original sense was “mild, meek,” later “modest, reserved,” then “foolish.”
