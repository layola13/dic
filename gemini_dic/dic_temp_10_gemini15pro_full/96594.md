induction's:/ɪnˈdʌkʃənz/
n.|归纳，归纳法，感应，就职，诱导|The process of discovering a general principle from particular facts or instances.  The production of an electric or magnetic state by the proximity (without contact) of an electrified or magnetized body. The act or process of inducting (as into office or military service).|The scientist used induction's power to derive a new theory from experimental data. 科学家利用归纳法的力量从实验数据中推导出一个新理论。|Synonyms: inference, deduction, generalization, initiation, inauguration|Antonyms: deduction, analysis, discharge|noun
