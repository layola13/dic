bletonism: /ˈblɛtənɪzəm/
noun
色盲，色弱
A defect of vision characterized by a lowered capacity to distinguish certain colors.
John's bletonism prevented him from seeing the difference between red and green.  约翰的色盲让他无法分辨红色和绿色。
Synonyms: color blindness, daltonism
Antonyms: normal color vision
n.

Etymology:  Named after John Dalton (1766-1844), an English chemist and physicist who himself had red-green color blindness and was the first to scientifically investigate the phenomenon.
