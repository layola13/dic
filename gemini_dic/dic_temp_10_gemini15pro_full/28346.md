carapaced:/ˈkærəpeɪst/| adj.|有壳的，甲壳状的|Having a carapace; covered with a carapace.  A carapace is a hard, protective outer covering, typically on the back of an animal such as a turtle or crustacean.|The carapaced turtle slowly retreated into its shell.  这只带壳的乌龟慢慢地缩回壳里。|近义词：shelled, armored|反义词: soft, unprotected|词性:adjective
Etymology: From French *carapace*, from Spanish *carapacho* "shell, armor," probably from  *carapaça* "tortoise shell" of unknown origin.
