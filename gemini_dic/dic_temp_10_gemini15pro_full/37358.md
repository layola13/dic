commensalism:/kəˈmɛnsəlɪzəm/
|n.|共生，共栖|A symbiotic relationship between two organisms of different species in which one derives some benefit while the other is unaffected.|The relationship between the cattle egret and the cow is an example of commensalism. 牛背鹭和牛之间的关系是共生的一个例子。|
|近义词:symbiosis, mutualism (sometimes)|反义词:parasitism, amensalism|noun|

commensal:/kəˈmɛnsl/
|adj.|共生的，共栖的|Pertaining to commensalism; living in a relationship in which one organism derives food or other benefits from another organism without hurting or helping it.|The commensal bacteria in our gut help us digest food. 我们肠道中的共生细菌帮助我们消化食物。|
|近义词:symbiotic, mutualistic (sometimes)|反义词:parasitic|adjective|

commensal:/kəˈmɛnsl/
|n.|共生生物|An organism that lives in a relationship with another in which it derives benefit without harming or benefiting the other.|The remora fish is a commensal of the shark. 鮣鱼是鲨鱼的共生生物。|
|近义词:symbiont|反义词:parasite|noun|
