chromoplast:/ˈkroʊməˌplæst/ | n. | 色素体；杂色体 |A plastid containing pigments other than chlorophyll, typically imparting a yellow, orange, or red color to fruits, flowers, and roots. | Example 1: The bright red color of tomatoes is due to chromoplasts. 番茄鲜艳的红色是由于色素体造成的。

Example 2: Chromoplasts develop from chloroplasts during fruit ripening. 果实成熟过程中，色素体由叶绿体发育而来。 | Synonym: plastid | Antonym: chloroplast, leucoplast | noun
