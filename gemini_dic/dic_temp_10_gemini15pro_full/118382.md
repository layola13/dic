merling:/ˈmɜːrlɪŋ/ | n. | 美人鱼；人鱼宝宝 |A mythical creature with the upper body of a human and the tail of a fish, especially a young or infant mermaid. |  The fishermen claimed to have seen a merling playing in the shallows. 渔民声称看到一条小人鱼在浅滩玩耍。

| Example: The merling swam gracefully through the coral reef. 小美人鱼优雅地游过珊瑚礁。


| Synonyms: mermaid (especially for adults), merbaby, merchild | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a mythical creature) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *mer- (sea) + -ling (diminutive suffix). Cognate with German Meerling.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *mari- (sea, lake) and *līngaz (descendant, offspring).
