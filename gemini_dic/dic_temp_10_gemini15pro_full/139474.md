obstinately: /ˈɒbstɪnətli/ (UK), /ˈɑːbstɪnətli/ (US) | adv. | 固执地，顽固地 | In a stubborn and unyielding manner; resolutely adhering to one's purpose, opinion, or course of action, despite reason, arguments, or persuasion. |  He obstinately refused to admit he was wrong. 他固执地拒绝承认自己错了。

The mule obstinately refused to move.  这头骡子顽固地不肯移动。

近义词: stubbornly, persistently, doggedly, resolutely

反义词: compliantly, submissively, yieldingly

词性: adverb

词源: From Late Latin *obstinatus, past participle of obstinare (“to persist, stand firm”), from Latin obstināre (“to persist, stand firm, stand still”), from ob- (“against, towards”) + stō, stāre (“to stand”).
