cloaca:/kloʊˈeɪkə/| n.|泄殖腔|A common cavity at the end of the digestive tract for the release of both excretory and genital products in vertebrates (except most mammals) and certain invertebrates.|Birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish have a cloaca. 鸟类、爬行动物、两栖动物和鱼类都有泄殖腔。|Synonym: vent|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, as mammals have separate openings)|Noun

cloaca:/kləʊˈeɪkə/| n.|下水道；阴沟|A sewer or drain, especially one that is large or covered.|The ancient Roman cloaca maxima is still in use today. 古罗马的大下水道至今仍在使用。|Synonym: sewer, drain|Antonym:  (No direct antonym)|Noun
