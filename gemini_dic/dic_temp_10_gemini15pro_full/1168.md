acclamations: /ˌækləˈmeɪʃənz/ | n. | 欢呼，喝彩，称赞 |  Loud acclamations greeted the announcement.  响亮的欢呼声迎接了这一宣布。 | The president was greeted with acclamations. 总统受到了欢呼。| synonyms: cheers, applause, plaudits, ovation, praise | antonyms: boos, jeers, disapproval, condemnation | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "acclamatio,"  from "acclamare" (to cry out, applaud), from "ad-" (to) + "clamare" (to shout).

USAGE->
The team received acclamations from the crowd after their victory. = 队伍在获胜后受到了人群的欢呼。

The author's new book was met with acclamations from critics. =  这位作者的新书受到了评论家的赞扬。


PHRASE->
a chorus of acclamations =  一片欢呼声
receive acclamations =  受到欢呼
greet with acclamations =  以欢呼声迎接
shower someone with acclamations =  向某人发出阵阵欢呼
burst into acclamations =  爆发出欢呼声
