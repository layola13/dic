overcluttering:/ˌoʊvərˈklʌtərɪŋ/
|n.|过度堆放，杂乱|The act of filling a space with too many things, making it messy and disorganized.|Her overcluttering of the room made it difficult to move around. 她把房间堆得太满了，很难走动。|
近义词: cluttering, overcrowding|反义词: tidying, organizing|词性: 名词 (derived from verb "overclutter")

overclutter:/ˌoʊvərˈklʌtər/
|vt.|过度堆放，使杂乱|To fill a space with too many things, making it messy and disorganized.|She tends to overclutter her desk with papers. 她总是把桌子上堆满文件。|近义词: overcrowd, cram|反义词: tidy, organize|词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (prefix indicating excess) + "clutter" (verb meaning to fill or cover with scattered things). "Clutter" likely originates from a Middle English word meaning "to clot" or "coagulate."
