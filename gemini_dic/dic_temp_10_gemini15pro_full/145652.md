overcontracting:/ˌoʊvərˈkənˌtræktɪŋ/ | n. | 过度收缩，过度紧缩 | The act of contracting too much or too intensely, often leading to negative consequences, especially in muscles or the economy. |  Example: Overcontracting of the muscles can lead to cramps. 肌肉过度收缩会导致抽筋。

Example:  Overcontracting of credit markets can trigger a recession.  信贷市场过度紧缩可能引发经济衰退。 | 近义词: overtightening, constriction | 反义词: expansion, relaxation | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "contracting" (present participle of "contract," from Latin "contractus," past participle of "contrahere" -  "to draw together, to reduce in size").
