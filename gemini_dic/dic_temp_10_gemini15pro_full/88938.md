holdout:/ˈhəʊldaʊt/ (British), /ˈhoʊldaʊt/ (American)| n.|坚持者，拒不妥协者；抵抗的据点；保留物|A person or group who refuses to agree or comply with something, such as a demand, offer, or rule. A holdout can also refer to a place that resists attack or surrender, or something kept back or reserved.|例句：The farmer was the last holdout against selling his land to the developers. 这位农民是最后一个拒绝将自己的土地卖给开发商的人。

|近义词：resister, dissenter, objector, nonconformist, recusant, diehard, stronghold, bastion|反义词:conformist, cooperator, collaborator|词性:noun

hold out:/həʊld ˈaʊt/ (British), /hoʊld ˈaʊt/ (American)| v.|坚持；维持；伸出；提供；抵挡|To resist or endure, especially in difficult circumstances. To offer or present something. To extend or stretch out. To withstand attack or pressure.|例句：The rebels held out against the government forces for months. 叛军抵抗政府军长达数月。


|近义词：resist, endure, persevere, persist, offer, present, extend, proffer, withstand|反义词:give in, surrender, collapse, withdraw|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrasal verb "hold out," composed of the verb "hold" (Old English *healdan, from Proto-Germanic *haldaną) and the adverb "out" (Old English ūt, from Proto-Germanic *ūt).  The noun meaning developed from the verb, referring to someone who "holds out" against something.
