man-of-word:/ˌmæn əv ˈwɜːrd/ | n. | 言而有信的人；信守诺言的人 | A person who keeps their promises; someone who can be relied on to do what they say they will do. | He's a man of his word; if he said he'd help, he will. 他是个言而有信的人；如果他说他会帮忙，他就一定会帮。| 近义词: trustworthy person, reliable person | 反义词: liar, dishonest person | 词性: 名词

man-of-word: /ˌmæn əv ˈwɜːrd/ | adj. | 言而有信的 | Keeping one's promises; reliable. | His man-of-word reputation preceded him. 他言而有信的名声远扬。 | 近义词: trustworthy, reliable | 反义词: untrustworthy, unreliable | 词性: 形容词


词源:  This phrase is a compound noun formed from "man" and "word".  It emphasizes the value placed on keeping one's word, particularly in a more traditional or formal context.  The concept of a "man of his word" dates back centuries and reflects a cultural emphasis on honesty and integrity.
