gradable:/ˈɡreɪdəbl/| adj.|可分级的，可等级化的|An adjective that can be used in comparative and superlative forms, or with adverbs like 'very', 'quite', 'rather', etc.  It describes qualities that can exist in different degrees.|例句： "Tall" is a gradable adjective, unlike "pregnant." “高”是一个可分级的形容词，不像“怀孕”。|例句：Happiness is a gradable quality; one can be very happy, somewhat happy, or not at all happy. 幸福是一种可分级的品质；一个人可以非常幸福，有点幸福，或者根本不幸福。|近义词：scalable, rankable|反义词：non-gradable, absolute|词性:adjective
