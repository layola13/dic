carthaginian:/ˌkɑːrθəˈdʒɪniən/ | adj. n. | Carthaginian 的；迦太基人；迦太基城的 |  Relating to ancient Carthage, a city-state in North Africa, or its people.  |  Example: The Carthaginian army was famous for its elephants. 迦太基军队以其战象而闻名。


carthaginian:/ˌkɑːrθəˈdʒɪniən/ | adj. n. | Carthaginian 的；迦太基人；迦太基城的 |  A native or inhabitant of ancient Carthage.  | Example:  Hannibal was a famous Carthaginian general. 汉尼拔是一位著名的迦太基将军。


carthaginian:/ˌkɑːrθəˈdʒɪniən/ | n. | 迦太基人 | A person from Carthage. | Example: Many Carthaginians were skilled traders. 许多迦太基人都是熟练的商人。


Etymology->
From Latin *Carthaginiensis*, from *Carthago* (Carthage).  Ultimately from Phoenician *qrt-ḥdšt* (New City).

Synonyms->
(n.) Punic

Antonyms->
(n.) Roman
