blethering:/ˈblɛðərɪŋ/ | n. vi.| 胡说八道，闲聊 |Meaningless or foolish talk; the act of talking at length but saying little of value. |He was blethering on about football all night. 他整晚都在胡扯足球. |近义词：babbling, chattering, prattling, rambling|反义词：concise, succinct|词性: noun, verb


blethering:/ˈblɛðərɪŋ/ | adj.| 喋喋不休的，胡说八道的 |Given to or characterized by meaningless or foolish talk. |His blethering monologue bored everyone. 他喋喋不休的独白让每个人都感到无聊.|近义词：garrulous, loquacious, talkative|反义词：quiet, reserved, taciturn|词性: adjective


词源:  From Scots *blether*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *blaðra* (“to talk nonsense”).
