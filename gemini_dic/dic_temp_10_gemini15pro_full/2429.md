adjudging: /əˈdʒʌdʒɪŋ/
verb
判决；裁定；宣判
The act of formally deciding or declaring something, especially in a legal context.  Making a judgment or decision about something.
The court is adjudging the defendant guilty. = 法院判决被告有罪。

The panel is adjudging the painting as a forgery. = 专家组裁定这幅画是赝品。

近义词: judging, deciding, determining, ruling, decreeing
反义词: acquitting, exonerating, pardoning
词性: verb (present participle)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *ajugen*, from Old French *ajuger*, from Latin *adjudicare* (“to award, assign, adjudge”), from *ad-* (“to”) + *judicare* (“to judge”), from *judex* (“judge”).
