overserved:/ˌoʊvərˈsɜːrvd/ | adj. | 过度招待的；被劝酒过多的 | Having been served more alcohol than is good for one.  It implies being given too much to drink, often to the point of intoxication. |  The guests at the wedding were quite overserved. 婚礼上的客人都被劝酒过多了。| The restaurant overserved the customer and he became unruly.  这家餐馆给顾客劝酒过量，他变得难以控制。 |  intoxicated, drunk, tipsy | sober, moderate | adjective

overserved:/ˌoʊvərˈsɜːrvd/ |  verb (passive) | 被过度招待 |  (past participle of overserve) Having received excessive service, especially of alcohol. | He was clearly overserved at the bar. 他显然在酒吧被劝酒过多了。 | The diners felt overserved and uncomfortable. 用餐者觉得被过度招待，感到不舒服。 | plied with drink, given too much alcohol | under-served, moderately served | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "served" (past participle of "serve").  The word "serve" itself ultimately derives from Latin "servire" (to serve, comply with).
