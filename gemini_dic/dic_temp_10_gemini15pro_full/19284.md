biasing:/ˈbaɪəsɪŋ/| n.|偏置，偏压|The act of introducing a bias into something, often an electrical circuit or a statistical analysis.|例句：The biasing of the transistors is crucial for proper amplifier operation. 晶体管的偏置对于放大器的正常工作至关重要。

例句：Researchers need to be mindful of potential biasing in their data collection methods.  研究人员需要注意他们数据收集方法中潜在的偏差。|近义词：prejudice, influencing, skewing, weighting|反义词： impartiality, neutrality|noun

bias:/ˈbaɪəs/| n. vt.|偏见，偏置；使有偏见|A particular preference or point of view that is personal, rather than scientific.|例句：The judge was accused of bias in favor of the defendant.  法官被指控偏袒被告。

例句：He showed a bias towards his own team. 他对自己的团队表现出偏见。|近义词：prejudice, partiality, inclination|反义词：impartiality, fairness, objectivity|noun, verb

biased:/ˈbaɪəst/| adj.|有偏见的，偏向一方的|Having or showing a prejudice for or against something or someone unfairly.|例句：The news report was biased against the government. 这篇新闻报道对政府有偏见。

例句：She felt the jury was biased against her. 她觉得陪审团对她有偏见。|近义词：prejudiced, partial, one-sided|反义词：impartial, unbiased, fair, neutral|adjective
