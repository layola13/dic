chapping:/ˈtʃæpɪŋ/ | n. | 皲裂；冻疮 | the cracking or splitting of skin, especially on the hands and lips, caused by cold or wind |  My lips get chapped in the winter. 我的嘴唇在冬天会皲裂。 |近义词： cracking, splitting, roughening | 反义词： healing, moisturizing | 词性: 名词

chapped:/ˈtʃæpt/ | adj. | 皲裂的；冻伤的 | rough, cracked, and sore, especially from cold weather | My hands are chapped from the cold. 我的手因为寒冷而皲裂了。 | 近义词：cracked, split, raw |反义词：smooth, soft, supple | 词性: 形容词

chap:/tʃæp/ | v. | (使)皮肤皲裂；(使)冻伤 | to become or make rough, cracked, and sore, especially from cold weather | The cold wind chapped my face.  冷风使我的脸皲裂了。| 近义词：crack, split, roughen | 反义词：heal, soften, moisturize | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English chappen (“to cut, chop”), from Old North French chapper (“to chop, hack”), of Germanic origin. Related to chop.
