off-peak:/ˌɒfˈpiːk/ (UK), /ˌɔːfˈpiːk/ (US)| adjective, adverb|非高峰的，非高峰期地|A time when something is less busy or less expensive than usual.  Relating to or occurring during a period of less than maximum activity or demand.|例句：Off-peak train tickets are much cheaper. 非高峰时段的火车票要便宜得多。|近义词：quiet, slack|反义词:peak, rush|词性:adjective, adverb

off-peak:/ˌɒfˈpiːk/ (UK), /ˌɔːfˈpiːk/ (US)| adjective, adverb|非高峰的，非高峰期地|We try to travel off-peak to avoid the crowds.  我们尽量在非高峰期出行以避开人群。|近义词：low-season|反义词:peak-season, on-peak|词性:adjective, adverb


词源: "off-" (meaning not or away from) + "peak" (referring to the highest point of activity).  The term gained popularity with the rise of utilities like electricity and telephone services, where usage fluctuated throughout the day.
