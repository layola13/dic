词源: From Middle English *hawi, from Old Norse hávi ("handle, haft"), from Proto-Germanic *hafiz ("handle, haft").  The original meaning related to a tool or weapon with a rough handle. The sense of "clumsy" or "awkward" developed from this, referring to someone who handles things roughly.
