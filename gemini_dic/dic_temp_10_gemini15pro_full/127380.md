neanderthal:/niˈændərˌθɔːl/ /niˈændərˌtɑːl/| n. |尼安德特人；粗鲁的人|An extinct species of human that lived in Europe and parts of western and central Asia. Also used informally to describe someone who is considered to be unintelligent or uncivilized.|例句：Neanderthals were skilled hunters. 尼安德特人是熟练的猎人。

例句：He called me a Neanderthal just because I don't understand modern art. 他就因为我不懂现代艺术就叫我尼安德特人。|近义词：caveman, primitive man|反义词:modern human|词性:名词


neanderthals:/niˈændərˌθɔːlz/ /niˈændərˌtɑːlz/| n. |尼安德特人（复数）；粗鲁的人（复数）|Plural form of Neanderthal. Refers to multiple individuals of the extinct species or multiple people considered unintelligent or uncivilized.|例句：Neanderthals lived in Europe for thousands of years. 尼安德特人在欧洲生活了数千年。

例句：Some people think that all politicians are Neanderthals. 有些人认为所有的政客都是尼安德特人。|近义词：cavemen, primitive men|反义词:modern humans|词性:名词
