chancinesses:/ˈtʃænsɪnɪsɪz/| n. |偶然性；不确定性；风险性|The quality of being subject to chance; uncertain or risky. |The chancinesses of the weather made it difficult to plan the picnic. 天气的变幻莫测使得野餐计划难以制定。|The chancinesses of the stock market make it a risky investment. 股票市场的波动性使其成为一项高风险投资。|近义词：uncertainties, risks, hazards|反义词：certainties, stabilities, assurances|词性:名词

词源:
From "chancy" + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns expressing quality or state) + "-es" (plural suffix).  "Chancy" itself comes from "chance" + "-y" (suffix forming adjectives).  Ultimately derived from Old French "cheance" meaning "fall of the dice."
