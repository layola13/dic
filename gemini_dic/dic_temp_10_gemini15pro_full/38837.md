confabulation:/kənˌfæbjuˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 虚构，编造；闲谈 | the act of creating false memories without the intention to deceive; a casual conversation |  Example: He filled in the gaps in his memory with confabulation. 他用虚构的记忆填补了记忆中的空白。

Example:  Their confabulation lasted for hours, covering everything from politics to gardening. 他们的闲聊持续了几个小时，从政治到园艺无所不谈。 | 近义词: fabrication, invention, falsehood, chat, discussion | 反义词: truth, reality, fact | 词性: noun

词源: From Late Latin confabulatio, from Latin confabulari "to chat, talk together," from com- "together" + fabulari "to talk," from fabula "story."
