cleanup:/ˈkliːnʌp/ |n. | 清理；扫除；清除 | a thorough cleaning or tidying of a place or thing. |  The city organized a cleanup of the park after the festival. 城市在节日后组织了一次公园的清理。|近义词: cleaning, tidying, purging | 反义词: mess, clutter | 词性: 名词

cleanup:/ˈkliːnʌp/ |n. | 整顿；肃清 | the removal of unwanted people or things from an organization or place. |  The police launched a cleanup of drug dealers in the city. 警方在城市开展了打击毒贩的行动。|近义词: purge, crackdown | 反义词: corruption, disorder | 词性: 名词


词源: "cleanup" is a compound noun formed from the verb "clean" and the adverb/particle "up."  "Clean" comes from Old English "clæne," while "up" also originates from Old English "up" or "upp." The combination emphasizes the thoroughness of the cleaning or removal action.
