cochlea:/ˈkɒkliə/ (UK) /ˈkoʊkliə/ (US)| n. |耳蜗|The spiral cavity of the inner ear containing the organ of Corti, which produces nerve impulses in response to sound vibrations.|The cochlea is a vital part of the hearing system. 耳蜗是听觉系统的重要组成部分。|The cochlea transforms sound vibrations into nerve impulses that are sent to the brain. 耳蜗将声音振动转换成发送到大脑的神经冲动。|近义词:inner ear (not a direct synonym, but refers to the location)|反义词:None|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cochlea*, from Ancient Greek κοχλίας (kokhlías, “snail, spiral”), from κόχλος (kókhlos, “spiral shell”).
