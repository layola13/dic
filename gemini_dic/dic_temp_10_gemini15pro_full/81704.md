greenhouse: /ˈɡriːnhaʊs/ (US) /ˈɡriːnˌhaʊs/ (UK)| n.|温室；暖房|A building with glass or plastic walls and roof, used for growing plants that need warmth and protection. |The tomatoes are grown in a greenhouse.  这些西红柿是在温室里种植的。|Synonyms: glasshouse, hothouse, conservatory|Antonyms: open field, outdoors|noun

greenhouse effect: /ˈɡriːnhaʊs ɪˈfekt/ (US) /ˈɡriːnˌhaʊs ɪˈfekt/ (UK)| n.|温室效应|The trapping of the sun's warmth in a planet's lower atmosphere due to the greater transparency of the atmosphere to visible radiation from the sun than to infrared radiation emitted from the planet's surface.|The greenhouse effect is causing global warming. 温室效应正在导致全球变暖。|Synonyms: atmospheric warming, global warming|Antonyms: global cooling|noun

greenhouse gas: /ˈɡriːnhaʊs ɡæs/ (US) /ˈɡriːnˌhaʊs ɡæs/ (UK)| n.|温室气体|A gas that contributes to the greenhouse effect by absorbing infrared radiation. |Carbon dioxide is a major greenhouse gas. 二氧化碳是一种主要的温室气体。|Synonyms: heat-trapping gas|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but one might consider "non-greenhouse gas")|noun
