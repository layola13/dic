baculite: /ˈbækjʊˌlaɪt/ | n. | 杆菊石 | An extinct cephalopod mollusk of the Cretaceous period, having a straight, conical shell. |  Example 1: The museum displayed a well-preserved baculite fossil.  博物馆展出了一块保存完好的杆菊石化石。

Example 2: Baculites are easily recognizable due to their unique straight shells. 杆菊石很容易辨认，因为它们有独特的直壳。 | Synonym: straight-shelled ammonite,  | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps coiled ammonite as a contrasting example) | Noun


Etymology: From French *baculite*, from Latin *baculus* ("staff, stick") + -ite (suffix used in mineralogy).
