colluders:/kəˈluːdərz/| n. |共谋者，勾结者|People who secretly cooperate to achieve a fraudulent or illegal purpose. |The colluders were caught and charged with price-fixing. 这些勾结者被抓获并被指控操纵价格。|
The colluders planned to rig the election. 这些共谋者计划操纵选举。|
近义词：conspirators, collaborators, partners in crime|反义词: opponents, adversaries|词性:名词(复数)
colluder:/kəˈluːdər/| n. |共谋者，勾结者|A person who secretly cooperates to achieve a fraudulent or illegal purpose. |The colluder was granted immunity in exchange for testimony against his partners.  这个共谋者为了换取针对其同伙的证词而获得了豁免。|
The colluder denied any involvement in the scheme.  这名共谋者否认参与了该计划。|
近义词：conspirator, collaborator, partner in crime|反义词: opponent, adversary|词性:名词

词源: From the verb "collude," which comes from the Latin "colludere," meaning "to play together," from "com-" (together) + "ludere" (to play).  Over time, the meaning shifted to imply secret cooperation for deceitful purposes.
