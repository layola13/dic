multiscious: /ˌmʌlˈtɪʃəs/
adjective
多意识的，多重意识的
Having or relating to multiple states of consciousness within a single individual.  This term is not widely established in mainstream psychology or psychiatry, but is used in some subfields and in fiction.
The protagonist in the science fiction novel experienced a multiscious existence, shifting between distinct personalities with their own memories and motivations.  小说中的主人公经历了一种多意识的存在，在具有各自记忆和动机的不同人格之间转换。
Near Synonyms: polyconscious (also not widely used), fragmented consciousness (more clinical)
Antonyms: unified consciousness, singular consciousness
Word Origin: Likely a neologism formed from "multi-" (many) and "conscious".
词性：形容词
