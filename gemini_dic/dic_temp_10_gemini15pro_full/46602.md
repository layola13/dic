damasks: /ˈdæməsks/ /ˈdɑːmɑːsks/
n.|锦缎；大马士革织物；（尤指旧时）大马士革钢制成的刀剑|A rich, figured fabric of silk, linen, wool, or cotton, often woven with a pattern that appears and disappears depending on the direction of the light. Originally from Damascus.|The tablecloth was made of the finest damasks.  桌布是用最好的锦缎制成的。|近义词: brocade, satin, tapestry|反义词: burlap, muslin|词性: 名词

damask: /ˈdæməsk/ /ˈdɑːmɑːsk/
n.|锦缎；大马士革织物；（尤指旧时）大马士革钢；淡玫瑰色|A rich, figured fabric... (see above).  Also, a pale pink color.|She wore a damask gown to the ball. 她穿着一件锦缎礼服去参加舞会。|近义词: brocade, satin, tapestry (for fabric); rose, blush (for color)|反义词: burlap, muslin (for fabric)|词性: 名词

damask: /ˈdæməsk/ /ˈdɑːmɑːsk/

damask: /ˈdæməsk/ /ˈdɑːmɑːsk/
adj.|锦缎制的；大马士革钢制的；玫瑰色的|Made of or resembling damask; of a pale pink color.|She admired the damask roses in the garden. 她欣赏花园里淡玫瑰色的玫瑰花。|近义词: silken, satiny, rosy|反义词: plain, dull|词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *damaske*, from Old French *damas*, from Medieval Latin *damascēnus*, meaning "of Damascus," referring to the city in Syria where these fabrics were originally made.
