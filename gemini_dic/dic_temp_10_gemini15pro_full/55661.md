ditransitive:/ˌdaɪˈtrænzɪtɪv/ | adj. | 双及物 | (of a verb) taking both a direct and an indirect object. | Example:  She gave him the book. (book is the direct object, him is the indirect object)| Synonyms: bitransitive | Antonyms: intransitive, monotransitive | Part of Speech: Adjective
