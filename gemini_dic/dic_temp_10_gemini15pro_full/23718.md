brassiest:/ˈbræsɪɪst/ | adj. | 最厚颜无耻的；最俗气的；最炫耀的 |  superlative adjective of brassy: most resembling brass in color or sound;  impudent and vulgar.  It can refer to a loud, showy, or overly confident manner, often considered vulgar or tasteless. It can also describe a harsh, metallic sound. |  Her brassiest outfit was a sequined jumpsuit. 她最俗气的服装是一件亮片连体衣。

The singer's brassiest performance was met with mixed reviews.  这位歌手最厚颜无耻的表演评价不一。| 近义词:  loud, showy, flashy, vulgar, tasteless, gaudy, brazen, bold, impudent | 反义词:  refined, subtle, understated, modest, demure, quiet | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From brassy + -est.  "Brassy" itself comes from brass (the metal), referring to its bright, harsh sound and color. The figurative sense of "impudent" developed later, likely based on the association of loudness and boldness with vulgarity.
