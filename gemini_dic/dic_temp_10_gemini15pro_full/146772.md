overidealism: /ˌoʊvər aɪˈdiːəlɪzəm/ | n. | 过分理想化 | The state of having or showing too many ideals, especially ones that are impractical or unrealistic.  | His overidealism led him to believe that world peace could be achieved overnight. 他过分理想化，以至于相信世界和平可以一夜之间实现。 |  近义词: utopianism, romanticism | 反义词: realism, pragmatism | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "idealism" (from "ideal" + "-ism")

"ideal" comes from Late Latin "idealis" meaning "existing in idea," from Latin "idea" (from Greek "idea" meaning "form, appearance, nature").

"-ism" is a suffix forming nouns of action, condition, doctrine, etc.
