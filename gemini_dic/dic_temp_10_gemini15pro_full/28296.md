capuchin:/ˈkæpjuːtʃɪn/| n.|卡布奇诺（咖啡）； капуцин猴|A type of coffee made with espresso and steamed milk, often topped with foamed milk. Also, a type of monkey with a hooded appearance.|例句：I ordered a capuchin with extra foam. 我点了一杯加奶泡的卡布奇诺。

例句：The capuchin monkey skillfully peeled the banana. капуцин猴熟练地剥开了香蕉。|近义词：(for coffee) latte, macchiato (for monkey) cebid, primate|反义词: (no direct antonym)|词性:noun


capucins:/ˈkæpjuːtʃɪnz/| n.|卡布奇诺咖啡（复数）； капуцин猴（复数）|Plural form of "capuchin", referring to either the coffee or the monkey.|例句：We ordered two capucins and a croissant. 我们点了两杯卡布奇诺和一个羊角面包。

例句：The capucins swung effortlessly through the trees. капуцин猴轻松地在树间荡来荡去。|近义词：(for coffee) lattes, macchiatos (for monkey) cebids, primates|反义词: (no direct antonym)|词性:noun


词源: From French *capucin*, referring to the colour of the hooded robes worn by Capuchin friars, resembling the colour of the coffee or the monkey's fur. Ultimately from Italian *cappuccio* "hood".
