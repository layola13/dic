anthropopathy:/ˌænθrəˈpɒpəθi/ | n. | 拟人论，神人同形说 | The attribution of human emotions or characteristics to a god, animal, or object. |  Many ancient cultures believed in anthropopathy, portraying their gods with human-like qualities and flaws. 许多古代文化都相信拟人论，将他们的神描绘成具有类似人类的品质和缺陷。

| Example:  Greek mythology is full of examples of anthropopathy, with gods experiencing jealousy, anger, and love. 希腊神话充满了拟人论的例子，众神经历着嫉妒、愤怒和爱。


| Synonym: anthropomorphism, personification | Antonym:  deification (Note:  A true antonym is difficult to find as anthropopathy describes a specific type of attribution. Deification implies raising something to godhood, which might involve anthropopathy, but not necessarily.) | noun
