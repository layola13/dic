flectionless:/ˈflɛkʃənləs/| adj.|无屈折的，不变形的|Lacking inflection; not changing form.  This describes words or grammatical elements that do not change their form to express different grammatical functions (e.g., tense, case, number).|The language of the aliens was described as flectionless, each word carrying the same meaning regardless of context.|近义词：invariable, unchanging|反义词：inflected, variable|词性:adjective

Etymology:
From flection + -less.  Flection (also spelled inflection) comes from Late Latin *flexio, from Latin flectere "to bend."  The suffix -less is a native English suffix meaning "without."
