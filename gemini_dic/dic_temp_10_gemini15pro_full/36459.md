cold-turkeying:/ˌkəʊld ˈtɜːkiɪŋ/ | n. | 冷火鸡疗法；突然戒断 | The abrupt and complete cessation of something, typically a harmful habit like smoking or drug use, often causing unpleasant withdrawal symptoms.  This is like going “cold turkey.”  | He decided to quit smoking cold turkey. 他决定立即戒烟。 |  She stopped taking the medication cold turkey and experienced severe withdrawal symptoms. 她突然停止服药，经历了严重的戒断症状。 | 近义词: abrupt cessation, withdrawal, detoxification | 反义词: gradual reduction, weaning | 词性: 名词 (derived from the adjective "cold-turkey")

cold-turkey:/ˌkəʊld ˈtɜːki/ | adj. | 突然的；直截了当的；断然的| Abrupt and complete; blunt and straightforward. | He gave me a cold-turkey refusal. 他断然拒绝了我。 | The detective asked him cold-turkey questions. 侦探直接问他问题。 | 近义词: abrupt, sudden, blunt, straightforward | 反义词: gradual, indirect | 词性: 形容词

词源: The origin of "cold turkey" is uncertain, but several theories exist. One suggests it relates to the goosebumps and pallor of someone experiencing withdrawal, resembling the skin of a plucked, uncooked turkey.  Another connects it to the straightforward, unembellished nature of cold turkey meat.
