nondualism:/nɒnˈdjuːəlɪzəm/ | n. | 非二元论 | The philosophical, spiritual, and religious concept that the universe is not composed of separate, independent entities but is instead a unified whole.  It asserts that the perceived separation between subject and object, self and other, is an illusion. | Example:  Nondualism is a core concept in Advaita Vedanta. 非二元论是吠檀多不二论的核心概念。

Example:  He explored the concept of nondualism through meditation. 他通过冥想探索了非二元论的概念。 | Synonym: oneness, unity, monism | Antonym: dualism, pluralism | noun

Etymology: non- (Latin prefix meaning "not") + dualism (from Latin duo "two")
