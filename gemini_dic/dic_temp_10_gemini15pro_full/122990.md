monandrous: /məˈnændrəs/
| adj. |
单雄蕊的；一雄蕊的；只有一枚雄蕊的 |
Having only one stamen.  Relating to or characterized by the possession of a single stamen. |
Example 1:  The monandrous flower attracted a specific type of bee.  这种单雄蕊的花吸引了一种特殊的蜜蜂。
Example 2: Monandrous plants are less common than those with multiple stamens. 单雄蕊植物比多雄蕊植物少见。
|
Synonyms: monandric |
Antonyms: polyandrous |
Part of Speech: Adjective |

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek mónandros (μόνανδρος, “having one husband, one stamen”), from mónos (μόνος, “single, alone”) + anḗr (ἀνήρ, “man”).  In botany, the "man" refers to the stamen, the male reproductive organ of a flower.
