an-arm-and-a-leg:/ən ˌɑːrm ənd ə ˈleɡ/ | idiom | 一大笔钱；非常昂贵 |  This idiom means something is very expensive. It implies that you would have to sacrifice a limb to afford it, obviously an exaggeration. | That car cost me an arm and a leg. 那辆车花了我一大笔钱。| 近义词：a fortune, a king's ransom, a small fortune | 反义词：a bargain, a steal, cheap | idiom
cost an arm and a leg: /kɔːst ən ˌɑːrm ənd ə ˈleɡ/ | idiom | 花费一大笔钱 |  This phrase specifically refers to the act of spending a lot of money on something. | It cost an arm and a leg to fix the roof.  修理屋顶花了一大笔钱。| 近义词：cost a fortune, cost a bomb | 反义词：cost next to nothing, be a bargain | idiom
pay an arm and a leg: /peɪ ən ˌɑːrm ənd ə ˈleɡ/ | idiom | 付出一大笔钱 |  Similar to "cost an arm and a leg," this emphasizes the act of paying. | I paid an arm and a leg for this antique watch. 我为这块古董表付了一大笔钱。| 近义词：pay a fortune, shell out a fortune | 反义词：pay peanuts, get a good deal | idiom
worth an arm and a leg: /wɜːrθ ən ˌɑːrm ənd ə ˈleɡ/ | idiom | 非常值钱 |  This describes something that is extremely valuable. | This painting is worth an arm and a leg. 这幅画非常值钱。| 近义词：invaluable, priceless, extremely valuable | 反义词：worthless, cheap | idiom
charge an arm and a leg: /tʃɑːrdʒ ən ˌɑːrm ənd ə ˈleɡ/ | idiom | 收取高价 | This refers to someone selling something for an exorbitant price. | They charged an arm and a leg for the concert tickets.  他们对音乐会门票收取了高价。| 近义词：overcharge, rip off, price gouge | 反义词：undercharge, give a discount | idiom
