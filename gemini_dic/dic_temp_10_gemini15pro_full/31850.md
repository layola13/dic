cheer-up:/ˌtʃɪr ˈʌp/ | phrasal verb | 振作起来；高兴起来 | To become more cheerful or make someone feel more cheerful. |  例句：He tried to cheer her up with a joke. 他试图用一个笑话让她高兴起来。

例句：Cheer up! It's not the end of the world. 振作起来！这不是世界末日。 | 近义词：brighten up, perk up, encourage | 反义词：depress, sadden, discourage | 词性:短语动词

cheer-up:/ˌtʃɪr ˈʌp/ | n. |  鼓励; 令人振奋的事物 | Something that makes you feel happier. | 例句：The good news was a real cheer-up.  这个好消息真是令人振奋。

例句：A visit from her friend was a much-needed cheer-up. 朋友的来访让她倍感振奋。 | 近义词：encouragement, boost, pick-me-up | 反义词：downer, depressant | 词性:名词

词源：From the verb "cheer" (Old French "chiere" - face, countenance) + the adverb "up."
