cabalist:/ˈkæbəlɪst/ | n. | 卡巴拉学者，神秘主义者 | A person who studies or practices Cabala, a Jewish mystical tradition. |  He was known as a learned cabalist, delving into ancient texts. 他被认为是一位博学的卡巴拉学者，钻研古代文献。|  The cabalist interpreted the hidden meanings in the sacred scriptures.  这位卡巴拉学者解释了圣经中的隐藏含义。 | synonyms: mystic, occultist, esoteric | antonyms: pragmatist, realist | noun

cabalistic:/ˌkæbəˈlɪstɪk/ | adj. | 卡巴拉的，神秘的 | Relating to or characteristic of Cabala. |  The symbols on the amulet were cabalistic, imbued with hidden power. 护身符上的符号是卡巴拉的，充满了隐藏的力量。 | The book contained cabalistic diagrams and cryptic messages. 这本书包含了卡巴拉图表和神秘的信息。| synonyms: mystical, occult, esoteric, arcane | antonyms: mundane, ordinary, commonplace | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cabaliste*, from Medieval Latin *cabalista*, from Hebrew *qabbālā* "received (lore)," from *qibbel* "to receive."
