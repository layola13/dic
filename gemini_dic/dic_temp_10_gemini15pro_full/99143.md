intercessionate:/ˌɪntərˈsɛʃəneɪt/
verb
求情；代祷；斡旋
to plead on behalf of someone else; to mediate
The lawyer intercessionated for his client, asking the judge for leniency. 律师为他的客户求情，请求法官宽大处理。
The priest intercessionated with God on behalf of the sinners.  神父向上帝为罪人代祷。
近义词: plead, mediate, intervene, intercede
反义词: accuse, condemn, prosecute
词性: verb

intercession:/ˌɪntərˈsɛʃən/
noun
求情；代祷；斡旋
the act of pleading on behalf of someone else; mediation
Through the intercession of a friend, I was able to get a job interview.  通过朋友的斡旋，我得到了一次面试机会。
Her intercession saved him from prison.  她的求情使他免于牢狱之灾。
近义词: mediation, intervention, plea, appeal
反义词: accusation, condemnation, prosecution
词性: noun

intercessor:/ˈɪntərˌsɛsər/
noun
调解人；说情者；代祷者
a person who pleads on behalf of someone else; a mediator
She acted as an intercessor between the warring factions.  她充当了交战各派之间的调解人。
He prayed to God, asking him to be an intercessor for humanity. 他向上帝祈祷，请求他为人类代祷。
近义词: mediator, intermediary, advocate, pleader
反义词: accuser, prosecutor
词性: noun

词源: From Late Latin *intercessionatus, past participle of intercessare (“to intercede”), from Latin intercedere (“to go between, intervene, interpose”), from inter (“between”) + cedere (“to go, yield”).
