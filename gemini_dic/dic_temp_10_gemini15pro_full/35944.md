coddle:/ˈkɑːdl/ (US), /ˈkɒdl/ (UK)| v. |溺爱，娇惯；细心照料|to treat someone very carefully and protect them from anything unpleasant|例句：The child was coddled by his parents. 这孩子被父母溺爱了。

例句：She coddles her plants to protect them from the harsh winter. 她悉心照料她的植物以保护它们免受严冬的侵袭。


|近义词：pamper, indulge, spoil, baby|反义词:neglect, ignore, discipline|词性:verb

coddled:/ˈkɑːdl/ (US), /ˈkɒdl/ (UK)| adj. |被溺爱的，娇生惯养的|overprotected; treated with excessive care|例句：The coddled prince had never faced a day of real work. 这位娇生惯养的王子从未真正工作过一天。

例句：He was so coddled as a child that he never learned to be independent. 他小时候被溺爱，以至于从未学会独立。

|近义词：pampered, spoiled, overprotected|反义词:independent, self-reliant|词性:adjective


词源: From Middle English *codlen (“to stew gently”), of uncertain origin. Perhaps related to Old French caudeler (“to warm”).
