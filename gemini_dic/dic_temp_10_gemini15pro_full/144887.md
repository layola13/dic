over-run:/ˌoʊvərˈrʌn/ | v. n. | 超过；泛滥；蔓延；超支 | to spread over or occupy (a place, area, etc.) especially in large numbers; to go beyond (a limit, etc.): The weeds overran the garden. | The project overran its budget. 项目超出了预算。| 近义词: overrun, overwhelm, infest, exceed, surpass | 反义词: under-run | verb

over-run:/ˌoʊvərˈrʌn/ | n. | 超过；超限；泛滥；蔓延 | the act or an instance of overrunning: an overrun of locusts | The overrun of the project was significant. 项目的超支很严重。| 近义词: excess, surplus, overflow | 反义词: shortfall, deficit | noun

词源: From over + run.  The verb sense of "spread over and occupy" is from the mid-17th century, while the noun sense of "amount by which something overruns" dates from the late 18th century.
