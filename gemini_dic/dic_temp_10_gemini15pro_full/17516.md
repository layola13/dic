beat: /biːt/ (UK), /biːt/ (US) | n. v. adj. |  敲打; 打败; (心脏等)跳动; 节拍 |  A regular, rhythmic movement or sound, especially that made by the heart or a musical instrument. To defeat someone in a competition or election. | The drummer set a fast beat. 鼓手设定了一个快速的节拍。 He beat his opponent in the final. 他在决赛中击败了他的对手。 | 近义词: defeat, conquer, pulse, rhythm | 反义词: lose, surrender, failure | 词性: 名词, 动词, 形容词 | 词源: From Old English *bēatan "to strike repeatedly," of Germanic origin.

beat: /biːt/ (UK), /biːt/ (US) | n. v. adj. |  (警察等的)巡逻路线; 筋疲力尽的; | A regular route that someone, especially a police officer, walks or patrols.  Extremely tired; exhausted. | The officer walked his beat. 警察巡逻了他的路线。 I'm absolutely beat after that run.  跑完步后我累坏了。 | 近义词: exhausted, tired, worn out, route | 反义词: energetic, refreshed | 词性: 名词, 形容词 | 词源: From Old French *batre* "to beat, strike."
