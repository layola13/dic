micromanagement:/ˌmaɪkroʊˈmænɪdʒmənt/| n. |微观管理，事无巨细的管理|The act of managing or controlling with excessive attention to minor details.  It often involves excessively close monitoring and controlling of subordinates or employees by a manager or supervisor.|例句：The team was stifled by the manager's micromanagement.  团队被经理事无巨细的管理窒息了。

例句：Micromanagement can demotivate employees and stifle creativity. 微观管理会使员工失去动力并扼杀创造力。|近义词：overmanagement, excessive control|反义词: macromanagement, delegation, empowerment|词性:noun

micromanage:/ˌmaɪkroʊˈmænɪdʒ/| v. |微观管理，事无巨细地管理|To manage or control with excessive attention to minor details.|例句：He micromanaged every aspect of the project, which frustrated the team. 他对项目的每个方面都进行了微观管理，这使团队感到沮丧。

例句：She felt micromanaged by her new boss, who constantly checked her work. 她觉得她的新老板对她进行了微观管理，不断地检查她的工作。|近义词：oversupervise, overcontrol|反义词: delegate, empower|词性:verb


词源:  "Micro-" (small) + "management" (the process of dealing with or controlling things or people).  The term emerged in the 1980s.
