by-all-manner-of-means:/baɪ ɔːl ˈmænər əv miːnz/ | idiom | 无论如何；当然可以；一定 | Certainly; definitely; by all means. | Example: "If you need help, by all manner of means, ask." 如果你需要帮助，尽管问。| Synonym: absolutely, certainly, definitely, of course, by all means | Antonym: no way, absolutely not, certainly not | idiom

by-all-manner-of-means:/baɪ ɔːl ˈmænər əv miːnz/ | idiom | 务必；千方百计 | Using all possible methods; by hook or by crook. | Example: "He tried by all manner of means to get the job." 他想方设法地想得到那份工作。| Synonym: by any means necessary, by hook or by crook, using every possible method | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, implies lack of effort) giving up easily, half-heartedly | idiom

ETYMOLOGY->
This idiom is an emphatic extension of "by all means," which itself intensifies "by any means."  "Manner" adds another layer of emphasis, implying the use of every possible type of method.
