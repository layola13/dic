hooligans:/ˈhuːlɪɡənz/
noun
流氓， hooligans
A person who engages in rowdy or violent behavior, typically in a group or crowd.
The hooligans started a fight in the pub. 这些流氓在酒吧里挑起了一场斗殴。
synonyms:thugs, vandals, delinquents, ruffians, rowdies
antonyms:law-abiding citizens, peaceful protesters


Etymology:
The origin of "hooligan" is uncertain, but a popular theory links it to the surname "Houlihan," which supposedly belonged to a rowdy Irish family featured in a music hall song in the late 19th century.
