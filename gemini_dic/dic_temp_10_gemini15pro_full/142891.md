out-of-the-woods:/ˌaʊt əv ðə ˈwʊdz/ | adj. | 脱离危险；脱离困境 | No longer in danger or difficulty.  |  The patient is out of the woods now and expected to make a full recovery.  病人已经脱离危险，预计会完全康复。 | Example: After the surgery, he was still not out of the woods, but his condition was improving. 手术后，他还没有完全脱离危险，但他的情况正在好转。 | Synonyms: safe, secure, clear | Antonyms: in danger, at risk | adjective

out-of-the-woods:/ˌaʊt əv ðə ˈwʊdz/ | idiom | 脱离险境；走出困境 |  Free from difficulties or problems. | The company is finally out of the woods financially. 这家公司终于摆脱了财务困境。 | Example:  While the project faced setbacks, the team's hard work pulled them out of the woods. 虽然项目遭遇了挫折，但团队的努力使他们摆脱了困境。 | Synonyms: in the clear, safe and sound, over the hump | Antonyms: in trouble, in a precarious situation | idiom

词源： This idiom originates from the literal sense of being lost and disoriented in a forest.  Being "out of the woods" meant escaping the dangers and uncertainties of the wilderness and reaching safety.  This meaning was then figuratively applied to any situation of danger or difficulty.
