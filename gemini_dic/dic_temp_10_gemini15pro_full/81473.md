grave: /ɡreɪv/ | adj. n. | 严重的；严肃的；坟墓 |  A place of burial for a dead body, typically a hole dug in the ground and marked by a stone or mound.  Serious or solemn in manner or appearance; weighty or important. |  He visited his mother's grave. 他去拜访了他母亲的坟墓。 The situation is very grave. 局势非常严峻。 |  serious, solemn, important, severe, critical |  trivial, unimportant, light, frivolous | adjective, noun

grave: /ɡreɪv/ | v. | 雕刻；铭记 |  Engrave (an inscription or image) on a hard surface.  Fix (an idea) deeply in one's mind. |  The date is graved on my memory.  这个日期铭刻在我的记忆中。 They graved their initials on the tree. 他们在树上刻了他们的姓名首字母。|  carve, engrave, inscribe, etch | erase, forget | verb
