architraved: /ˈɑːrkɪtreɪvd/ | adj. | 有框缘的，有楣饰的 | Having an architrave; framed by an architrave.  An architrave is the lowest part of an entablature, resting directly on the capitals of the columns. It's essentially a molded frame around a door or window. | The architraved doorway gave the house a grand entrance. 这扇有楣饰的门廊赋予了房屋宏伟的入口。| The architraved windows added a touch of elegance to the room.  带框缘的窗户为房间增添了一丝优雅。 | Synonym: framed, bordered | Antonym: unframed, plain | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *architravato*, from *architrave* (architrave) + *-ato* (past participle suffix).  The word *architrave* itself comes from Greek *arkhitekton* (chief builder), from *arkhi-* (chief) + *tekton* (builder, carpenter).
