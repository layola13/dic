microprobe:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌproʊb/| n.|微探针，显微探针|A very small probe, especially one used to analyze the chemical composition of a substance or a surface.|Scientists used a microprobe to analyze the Martian soil.|Synonyms:  microanalyzer, electron probe, ion probe|Antonyms:  macroprobe (rarely used)|noun
