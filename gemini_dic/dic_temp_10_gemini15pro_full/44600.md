crowd:/kraʊd/| n. vt. vi.|人群，群众；拥挤，挤满|A large number of people gathered together in a disorganized or unruly way.  A large number of people gathered close together.|The crowd cheered as the band took the stage.  人群在乐队上台时欢呼起来。|The fans crowded around the stage. 歌迷挤在舞台周围。|近义词：throng, mob, horde, swarm|反义词:few, individuals|名词, 动词

crowd:/kraʊd/| v. |挤满，塞满；涌上心头；催促；逼迫|To fill (something) to excess; cram. To press or force (someone) into a small space. To come in large numbers; throng. To treat (someone) with unwelcome attentions.|Memories of her childhood crowded into her mind. 童年的记忆涌上她的心头。|He felt crowded by the city. 他感觉被城市逼得透不过气。|近义词：pack, cram, throng, press|反义词:thin, disperse|动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *crūdan, from Proto-Germanic *krōdōną (“to press, urge”). Cognate with Middle Dutch cruden (“to push, thrust”), Old High German krūten (“to push, press”), German kräuten (“to weed”).
