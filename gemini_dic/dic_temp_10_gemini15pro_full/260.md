abduct:/əbˈdʌkt/ /æbˈdʌkt/| vt.|绑架，劫持，诱拐|To take (someone) away illegally by force or deception; kidnap.|The rebels abducted three foreign aid workers. 叛军绑架了三名外国援助人员。

He was abducted from his home. 他被人从家中绑架。|近义词：kidnap, seize, snatch, capture|反义词:release, free|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin abducere "to lead away," from ab- "away" + ducere "to lead."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They abduct children for ransom. = 他们绑架儿童勒索赎金。
Some gangs abduct tourists for money. = 一些帮派绑架游客以获取钱财。

一般过去时:
The police believe she was abducted late last night. = 警方认为她是在昨晚深夜被绑架的。
He abducted the heiress and demanded a million dollars. = 他绑架了女继承人并索要一百万美元。

现在完成时:
Several businessmen have been abducted in recent months. = 最近几个月有几名商人被绑架。
The group has abducted several high-profile individuals. = 该组织绑架了几名知名人士。

过去完成时:
The police discovered that the child had been abducted several days earlier. = 警方发现这名儿童几天前就被绑架了。
By the time the police arrived, the victim had been abducted. = 当警察到达时，受害者已经被绑架了。

将来时:
If they don't pay the ransom, the kidnappers will abduct another person. = 如果他们不支付赎金，绑匪将绑架另一个人。
The police fear that the gang will abduct more victims. = 警方担心该团伙会绑架更多受害者。

被动语态:
The journalist was abducted by a terrorist group. = 这名记者被一个恐怖组织绑架了。
Two children were abducted from the park yesterday. = 昨天有两个孩子在公园被绑架了。


PHRASE->
abduct someone from somewhere = 从某地绑架某人
attempt to abduct = 企图绑架
abduct for ransom = 为勒索赎金而绑架
be abducted by someone = 被某人绑架
threaten to abduct = 威胁要绑架
