depreciators:/dɪˈpriːʃieɪtərz/| n. |贬低者；折旧者|People who lessen the value of something, either literally (like through accounting practices) or figuratively (like through criticism).|例句：The constant depreciators of the artist's work eventually drove him to quit painting. 那些不断贬低这位艺术家作品的人最终迫使他放弃了绘画。

例句：The company's depreciators calculated the loss in value of the equipment over time. 公司的折旧员计算了设备随时间推移的价值损失。|近义词：critics, detractors, belittlers|反义词:praisers, admirers, supporters|词性:noun
