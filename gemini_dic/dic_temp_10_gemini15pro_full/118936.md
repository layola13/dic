meteorite:/ˈmiːtiəraɪt/| n. |陨石|A stony or metallic object that has fallen to Earth from outer space.  A meteoroid becomes a meteor when it enters the Earth's atmosphere and burns up, leaving a bright streak. If any of it survives the journey and lands on Earth, it is called a meteorite.|例句：Scientists studied the meteorite to learn more about the early solar system. 科学家们研究了这块陨石，以了解更多关于早期太阳系的信息。

例句：The impact of the meteorite created a large crater. 陨石的撞击形成了一个大陨石坑。|近义词：meteoroid, bolide|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps terrestrial rock)|词性: noun
