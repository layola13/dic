gypsies:/ˈdʒɪpsiz/ | n. |吉普赛人；流浪者；（尤指）吉普赛女人；吉普赛语|A traditionally nomadic ethnic group thought to have originated in India and now living chiefly in Europe and North America, often as itinerant traders or entertainers.  This term is now often considered offensive.  It is preferred to use Roma/Romany. |  Many gypsies travel in caravans. 许多吉普赛人乘坐大篷车旅行。|Synonym: Roma, Romany, traveller (British), Romani people|Antonym: settled person|noun


gypsy:/ˈdʒɪpsi/ | n. |吉普赛人；流浪者；（尤指）吉普赛女人；吉普赛语|A member of a traditionally nomadic ethnic group thought to have originated in India and now living chiefly in Europe and North America, often as itinerant traders or entertainers. This term is now often considered offensive.  It is preferred to use Roma/Romany.|She has a gypsy soul. 她有一颗流浪的心。|Synonym: Roma, Romany, traveller (British), Romani people|Antonym: settled person|noun


gypsy:/ˈdʒɪpsi/ | adj. |吉普赛的；流浪的|Of or relating to gypsies or their way of life.|They lived a gypsy lifestyle, travelling from place to place. 他们过着吉普赛式的生活，四处流浪。|Synonym: nomadic, wandering, itinerant|Antonym: settled, sedentary|adjective
