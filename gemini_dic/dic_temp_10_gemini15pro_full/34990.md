cloister:/ˈklɔɪstər/ /ˈklɔɪstɚ/ | n. vt.| 修道院, 寺院, 迴廊; 隔离, 使与世隔绝 |A covered walkway, especially in a religious institution, with an open colonnade on one side, typically surrounding a courtyard.  To seclude or shut away, as in a monastery. |例句：The monks walked slowly around the cloister.  僧侣们绕着修道院的迴廊慢慢地走。

例句：She cloistered herself in her study to finish the novel.  她把自己关在书房里完成小说。|近义词：monastery, abbey, convent, seclusion, retreat |反义词:world, society|词性:noun, verb


cloistered:/ˈklɔɪstərd/ /ˈklɔɪstɚd/ | adj.|  与世隔绝的 |Secluded from the world; sheltered. | 例句：He lived a cloistered life dedicated to prayer. 他过着与世隔绝的生活，专注于祈祷。

例句：The cloistered atmosphere of the university library was conducive to study. 大学图书馆与世隔绝的氛围有利于学习。|近义词：secluded, sheltered, isolated, withdrawn |反义词: worldly, public |词性:adjective
