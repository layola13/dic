overconservativeness:/ˌoʊvər kənˈsɜːrvətɪvnəs/| n. |过度保守，过分保守|The state of being excessively cautious and resistant to change.  It implies a reluctance to adopt new ideas, methods, or approaches, often prioritizing traditional values and practices.|His overconservativeness prevented him from investing in the new technology. 他的过度保守使他没有投资这项新技术。|Synonyms:  caution, wariness, hesitancy, timidity, rigidity, inflexibility|Antonyms:  radicalism, progressiveness, boldness, adventurousness, innovation|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "conservative" (adjective meaning "averse to change or innovation") + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns expressing a state or quality)
