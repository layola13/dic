panspermist:/pænˈspɜːrmɪst/
n.|泛种论者|A believer in the theory of panspermia, which suggests that life exists throughout the universe and is distributed by space dust, meteoroids, asteroids, comets, planetoids, and also by spacecraft carrying unintended contamination by microorganisms. |The panspermist argued that life on Earth originated from microorganisms carried by comets.| 近义词:cosmologist (in a broader sense)| 反义词: None (lack of a direct antonym for a person believing in a specific scientific theory)|词性:名词

panspermia:/pænˈspɜːrmɪə/
n.|泛种论|The theory that life exists throughout the universe and is distributed by space dust, meteoroids, asteroids, comets, planetoids, and also by spacecraft carrying unintended contamination by microorganisms.|The discovery of organic molecules in meteorites lends credence to the theory of panspermia.|近义词:exogenesis|反义词:abiogenesis (in a limited sense, as it refers to life originating on Earth specifically)|词性:名词
