doctrinalist:/ˈdɑːktrɪnəlɪst/ | n. | 教条主义者；学说拥护者 | A person who adheres rigidly to a set of doctrines or beliefs, especially religious or political ones.  |  Example: The doctrinalist refused to compromise on even the smallest details of his beliefs.  这位教条主义者拒绝在他的信仰的任何细枝末节上妥协。

Example:  He was known as a strict doctrinalist and often clashed with those who held more liberal views. 他以严谨的教条主义者而闻名，经常与那些持有更自由主义观点的人发生冲突。 |  Synonyms: dogmatist, ideologue, partisan | Antonyms: freethinker, pragmatist, skeptic | Noun
