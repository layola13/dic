carried-on:/ˈkærid ɒn/ | adj. | 继续的，持续的 |  Continuing, usually after an interruption.  | The meeting carried on for another hour. 会议又继续进行了一个小时。| continued, resumed, ongoing | discontinued, halted | adjective

carried-on:/ˈkærid ɒn/ | adj. |  (非正式) 过分的，夸张的 | (Informal) Excessive, exaggerated; behaving in a dramatic or overly emotional way. |  She had a carried-on reaction to the news. 她对这个消息反应过度。 | exaggerated, over-the-top, dramatic, hysterical | understated, calm, restrained | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrasal verb "carry on," meaning to continue. The past participle form "carried on" can function as an adjective. The verb "carry" originates from Old North French "carier" (to carry, transport).
