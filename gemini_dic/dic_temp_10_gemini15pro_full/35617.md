coastlines:/ˈkoʊstlaɪnz/| n. |海岸线|The boundary between land and sea.  The outline of a coast, especially with regard to its shape and appearance.|例句：The rugged coastlines of Maine are breathtaking. 缅因州崎岖的海岸线令人叹为观止。

例句：Rising sea levels threaten many of the world's coastlines. 海平面上升威胁着世界上许多海岸线。|近义词：shores, beaches, coasts|反义词:inland|词性:名词

词源:coast + line.  "Coast" comes from Old French "coste" and ultimately from Latin "costa" (rib, side, flank). "Line" comes from Old French "ligne" and ultimately from Latin "linea" (linen thread, line).
