dismissively:/dɪsˈmɪsɪvli/
adverb
轻蔑地，不屑一顾地
in a way that shows you do not think something is worth considering; scornfully
She waved her hand dismissively. 她轻蔑地挥了挥手。
近义词: contemptuously, scornfully, disdainfully
反义词: respectfully, attentively, thoughtfully
词性: adverb

dismiss:/dɪsˈmɪs/
verb
解散；解雇；驳回
1. to allow or tell someone to leave
2. to officially tell someone to leave their job
3. to decide that something or someone is not important and not worth considering
The teacher dismissed the class. 老师解散了班级。
近义词: discharge, fire, reject
反义词: retain, hire, accept
词性: verb


词源: From Late Latin *dismissivus, from Latin dismissus, past participle of dimittere "to send away."
