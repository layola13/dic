beatification:/ˌbiːætɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 福列入圣品，宣福 | The official act of the Roman Catholic Church of declaring a deceased person to be among the blessed, the first step toward canonization (sainthood). | The beatification ceremony drew thousands of pilgrims.  宣福仪式吸引了成千上万的朝圣者。| 近义词：canonization, exaltation | 反义词：condemnation | 词性: noun

beatification:/ˌbiːætɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 美化，圣化 | The act of making something beautiful or holy, or of portraying something as beautiful or holy. | The artist's beatification of nature in his paintings was breathtaking. 艺术家在他的画作中对大自然的圣化令人叹为观止。| 近义词：glorification, idealization | 反义词：vilification | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *beatificacion*, from Late Latin *beatificātiō*, from Latin *beatificātus*, past participle of *beatificāre* (“to make happy, bless”), from *beātus* (“happy, blessed”) + *-ficāre* (from *facere* (“to make, do”)).
