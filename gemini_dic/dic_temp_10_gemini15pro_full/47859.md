deceit:/dɪˈsiːt/| n.|欺骗，诡计|the action or practice of deceiving someone by concealing or misrepresenting the truth.|例句：He was accused of obtaining money by deceit. 他被指控以欺骗手段获取金钱。

例句：She felt a deep sense of hurt and betrayal after discovering his deceit. 在发现他的欺骗行为后，她感到深深的伤害和背叛。|近义词：trickery, fraud, duplicity, guile, dishonesty|反义词:honesty, integrity, candor, truthfulness|词性:Noun

词源: From Old French *deceite* (Modern French *décevoir* "to disappoint"), which comes from Latin *dēcipere*, from *dē-* (expressing removal) + *capere* "to take, seize."  The notion is of "taking someone away" from the truth.
