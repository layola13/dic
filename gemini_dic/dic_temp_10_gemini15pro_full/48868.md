deformities:/dɪˈfɔːrmɪtiz/| n.|畸形，缺陷|a physical abnormality or defect; a malformation. |The child was born with severe deformities. 这个孩子生下来就有严重的畸形。|Synonyms: malformations, abnormalities, imperfections, disfigurements|Antonyms: perfections, normalities|noun

deformity:/dɪˈfɔːrmɪti/| n.|畸形，缺陷|a physical abnormality or defect; a malformation. |The accident resulted in a permanent deformity of his hand.  这场事故导致他的手永久畸形。|Synonyms: malformation, abnormality, imperfection, disfigurement|Antonyms: perfection, normality|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *deformite*, from Old French *deformité*, from Latin *dēformitās*, from *dēformis* (“misshapen, deformed”), from *dē-* (“from, away from”) + *fōrma* (“form, shape”).
