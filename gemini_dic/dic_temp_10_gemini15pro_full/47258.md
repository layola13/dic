/wɒt/ /hwɒt/ | pron. det. adv.| 什么，多么 |

What is your name? | 你叫什么名字？
What do you want for dinner? | 你晚餐想吃什么？

What a beautiful day! | 多么美好的一天！
What an amazing performance! | 多么精彩的表演！

Etymology: From Old English *hwæt, neuter singular nominative and accusative of hwā "who," which also functioned as an interrogative and relative pronoun.  Cognate with Old Frisian hwet, Old Saxon hwat, Old High German hwaz, Old Norse hvat, Gothic hva.

Synonyms: which, how

Antonyms:  (What doesn't have direct antonyms as it's a question word.  Antonyms would depend on the context.)
