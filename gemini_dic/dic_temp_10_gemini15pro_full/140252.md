offset:/ˈɒfset/ (UK) /ˈɔːfset/ (US)| n. v. adj.|抵消，补偿；偏移量；支管；弯管; 抵消，补偿；用平版印刷;|

**Noun (名词):**

* 英文解析:  A consideration or amount that diminishes or balances the effect of an opposite one.  Something that counterbalances, compensates, or makes up for something else. A side shoot or branch of a plant. A bend in something, especially a pipe.
* 例句：The company's losses were offset by gains in other areas. 公司在其他领域的收益抵消了亏损。
* 例句：The tax offset helped to reduce her overall tax burden.  税收抵免帮助减轻了她的整体税负。
* 近义词：counterbalance, compensate, balance, neutralize
* 反义词：increase, augment, exacerbate

**Verb (动词):**

* 英文解析: To counterbalance, compensate for, or make up for something. To print by offset lithography.
* 例句：The increase in prices was offset by a reduction in demand. 价格上涨被需求下降所抵消。
* 例句：The company plans to offset its carbon emissions by planting trees.  该公司计划通过植树来抵消其碳排放。
* 近义词：counteract, balance, neutralize
* 反义词：enhance, amplify

**Adjective (形容词):**

* 英文解析: Placed or positioned so as to counterbalance something else; relating to offset printing.
* 例句：The building has an offset entrance to maximize space.  该建筑有一个偏移的入口以最大化空间。
* 例句：Offset printing is a commonly used printing technique. 平版印刷是一种常用的印刷技术。
* 近义词：counterbalanced, staggered, displaced
* 反义词：centered, aligned


词源: From the verb phrase "set off," meaning to place against as a counterbalance.
