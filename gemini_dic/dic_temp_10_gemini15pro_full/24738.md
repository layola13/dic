brooded:/ˈbruːdɪd/| v. | 沉思；担忧；(鸟)孵蛋 |  past tense and past participle of "brood" which means to think deeply about something that makes one unhappy, or (of a bird) to sit on (eggs) in order to keep them warm and hatch them. |  He brooded over the problem for days. 他为这个问题苦思冥想了好几天。| synonyms: worried, fretted, dwelled, pondered, incubated | antonyms: ignored, disregarded, celebrated | verb

brooded:/ˈbruːdɪd/| adj. | 闷闷不乐的；阴郁的 |  (Used as an adjective, though less common) Having a thoughtful or worried appearance. | He had a brooded look on his face. 他脸上带着沉思忧虑的表情。| synonyms: gloomy, sullen, morose | antonyms: cheerful, bright, happy | adjective
