coax:/koʊks/| v.|哄骗；劝诱；慢慢将…弄好|To persuade someone gently and patiently to do something or go somewhere, or to get something to work after much effort.|例句：I coaxed the cat out from under the bed. 我把猫从床底下哄了出来。

例句：He coaxed the car into starting. 他费了半天劲才把汽车发动起来。|近义词：persuade, cajole, wheedle, entice|反义词：force, compel, coerce|词性:动词
词源:Probably from Middle English cokes, meaning "simpleton, fool," implying treating someone as if they were a simpleton in order to persuade them.
