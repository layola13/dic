arpeggio:/ɑːrˈpedʒioʊ/ (US), /ɑːˈpedʒiːəʊ/ (UK)| n.|琶音|A musical chord in which the notes are played one after another, rather than all together.|The pianist played a delicate arpeggio.|Synonyms: broken chord|Antonyms: chord|noun

arpeggiated:/ɑːrˈpedʒiːeɪtɪd/ (US), /ɑːˈpedʒiːeɪtɪd/ (UK)| adj.|琶音式的|Played as an arpeggio.|The guitarist played an arpeggiated version of the melody.|Synonyms: broken, rolled|Antonyms: blocked, solid|adjective

arpeggiate:/ɑːrˈpedʒiːeɪt/ (US), /ɑːˈpedʒiːeɪt/ (UK)| v.|演奏琶音|To play the notes of (a chord) one after another.|He arpeggiated the chord slowly and deliberately.|Synonyms: roll, break|Antonyms: block|verb
