mesymnicum: /mɛsɪmˈnaɪkəm/ | n. | 中肠系膜 |  The mesymnicum is a fold of peritoneum that connects the midgut to the dorsal abdominal wall. It plays a crucial role in the development and positioning of the intestines. | The mesymnicum provides support and allows blood vessels and nerves to reach the small intestine. | 近义词: mesentery (more general term) | 反义词: None | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Ancient Greek *μέσος (mésos, “middle”) + *ἔντερον (énteron, “intestine”). The term reflects the mesymnicum's role in supporting the middle part of the digestive system.
