gladiatorism:/ˈɡlædiəˌtərɪzəm/
n.
角斗士制度；角斗士比武；你死我活的斗争
The practice of gladiatorial combat.  A ruthless, desperate struggle.
The Roman Empire was known for its gladiatorism. 罗马帝国以其角斗士制度而闻名。

The political debate descended into gladiatorism, with each candidate trying to destroy the other. 这场政治辩论沦落成你死我活的斗争，每个候选人都试图摧毁对方。

近义词: combat, bloodshed, brutality, savagery
反义词: pacifism, peace, compassion
词性: noun

词源:  From Latin *gladiātōrius* (“gladiator”) + *-ismus* (suffix forming nouns of action or condition). Ultimately from Latin *gladius* ("sword").
