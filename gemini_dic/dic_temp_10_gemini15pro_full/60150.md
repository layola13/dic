effectuations:/ɪˌfɛktʃʊˈeɪʃənz/
|n.| 实现，实行，生效 |The effectuation of the new policy will take time. 新政策的实行需要时间。|
|n.|  完成，实现 |The effectuation of his dreams took years of hard work.  他梦想的实现花费了多年的努力。|
|近义词:|implementation, realization, accomplishment, achievement|
|反义词:|failure, non-achievement, inaction|
|词性:|noun|


Etymology:
From Late Latin *effectuationem,  accusative singular of *effectuatio (“a performing, accomplishing”), from Latin effectuatus, past participle of effectuare (“to effect, accomplish”), from effectus (“effect”) + -are (frequentative suffix).
