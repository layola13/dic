deprecatingly:/ˌdɛprɪkeɪtɪŋli/| adv.|贬低地，自谦地，不赞成地|Expressing disapproval or deprecation; apologetically minimizing one's own accomplishments or importance.|He smiled deprecatingly, waving away the compliments. 他谦虚地笑了笑，挥手示意不必恭维。|近义词：modestly, humbly, apologetically, self-effacingly|反义词：boastfully, proudly, arrogantly|词性:副词

deprecating:/ˈdɛprɪkeɪtɪŋ/| adj.|贬低的，反对的，不赞成的|Expressing disapproval; tending to belittle or disparage.|He made a deprecating remark about his own work. 他对自己的作品做了一个自谦的评论。|近义词：disapproving, critical, belittling|反义词：approving, praising, laudatory|词性:形容词


deprecate:/ˈdɛprɪkeɪt/| vt.|反对，不赞成，贬低|Express disapproval of; plead against; belittle.|She deprecated the use of violence. 她反对使用暴力。|近义词：disapprove of, criticize, condemn, belittle|反义词：approve of, praise, commend, extol|词性:动词

Etymology: From Late Latin *deprecatus*, past participle of Latin deprecari (“to avert by prayer, pray against, entreat”), from de- (“down, away”) + precari (“to pray”).
