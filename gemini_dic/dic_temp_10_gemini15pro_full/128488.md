newspapers:/ˈnjuːzˌpeɪpərz/ (US) /ˈnjuːzˌpeɪpəz/ (UK)| n. |报纸|Printed publications containing news, articles, advertisements, etc., usually issued daily or weekly.|I read the newspapers every morning to stay informed. 我每天早上读报纸以保持消息灵通。|synonyms: journals, periodicals, gazettes,  press |antonyms: none |noun

Etymology->
From "news" + "paper".  "News" comes from Middle English "newes," plural of "newe," meaning "new thing." "Paper" comes from Old French "papier" and ultimately from Latin "papyrus," referring to the material used for writing.
