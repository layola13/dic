corinium:/kɔːˈrɪniəm/| n.|科里尼乌姆 (古罗马城市，今英国科茨沃尔德的西伦塞斯特)|Corinium was a flourishing Roman town located in present-day Cirencester, Gloucestershire, England. It was the second largest town in Roman Britain.|The ruins of Corinium are a popular tourist attraction. 科里尼乌姆的废墟是一个热门的旅游景点。|Corinium Dobunnorum was an important centre for trade and administration. 科里尼乌姆多布诺鲁姆是重要的贸易和行政中心。|Synonyms: Cirencester (modern name), Durocornovium (alternative Roman name)|Antonyms: None (as it is a proper noun)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Latin *Corinīum*, derived from the name of the Dobuni tribe, whose territory the Romans conquered and established the town.
