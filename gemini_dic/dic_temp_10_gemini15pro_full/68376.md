fallings-out:/ˈfɔːlɪŋzaʊts/
|n.|争吵，不和|Disagreements or quarrels between people, often leading to an end of a relationship.|There were several fallings-out between the band members before they finally split up. 乐队成员在最终解散前发生过几次争吵。|近义词：disagreements, quarrels, arguments, disputes, clashes|反义词：reconciliations, agreements, harmonies|词性: 名词

fallings-out:|/ˈfɔːlɪŋzaʊts/|
|n.|争吵，不和|A breakdown in a friendly relationship.|The business partners had a serious falling-out over money.  商业伙伴因为金钱问题发生了严重的争执。|近义词：rifts, breaches, estrangements|反义词：reconciliations, rapprochements|词性: 名词

词源:  "Falling-out" is formed from the verb "fall out," which itself originates from the Old English *feallan ūt*, meaning "to fall out" or "to result."  Over time, the meaning evolved to encompass disagreements and quarrels.
