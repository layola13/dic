auscultation:/ˌɔːskəlˈteɪʃən/ | n. |听诊|The act of listening to sounds within the body, typically with a stethoscope, for diagnostic purposes. |The doctor performed an auscultation of the patient's chest. 医生对病人的胸部进行了听诊。| auscultate, examine, check | palpation, percussion | noun

auscultations:/ˌɔːskəlˈteɪʃənz/ | n. |听诊（复数）|Multiple instances of listening to sounds within the body. |The doctor performed several auscultations over the course of the treatment.  医生在治疗过程中进行了多次听诊。| examinations, checks |  palpations, percussions | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin auscultātiō, from Latin auscultāre (“to listen attentively”), from aus (“from, out”) + cultāre (frequentative of colere “to cultivate, inhabit, dwell, protect, till, worship”).
