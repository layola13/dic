bushwhackings:/ˈbʊʃˌwækɪŋz/
n.
伏击，丛林游击战，艰难跋涉
The act of ambushing someone from a concealed position in a wooded area, or making one's way through dense woods.
Early settlers faced the constant threat of bushwhackings by Native Americans. 早期定居者面临着美洲原住民不断伏击的威胁。

The soldiers endured days of arduous bushwhackings through the jungle. 士兵们在丛林中忍受了数天的艰苦跋涉。

近义词: ambushes, attacks, raids, hikes, treks
反义词: open warfare, planned assaults, strolls

词性: noun (plural)
词源: From bushwhack + -ing (suffix denoting an action or process).  Bushwhack itself is a blend of bush and whack, referring to the act of cutting through bushes.
