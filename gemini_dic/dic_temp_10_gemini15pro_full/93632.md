imbonity: /ɪmˈbɒnɪti/ | n. | 慷慨；仁慈；宽宏大量 |  A disposition to be kind and generous.  It implies a noble or elevated generosity, often exceeding ordinary kindness. |  His imbonity extended to forgiving even his worst enemies.  他宽宏大量，甚至原谅了他最坏的敌人。 | Synonyms: generosity, magnanimity, benevolence, charity | Antonyms: stinginess, meanness, selfishness, malevolence | noun

Etymology: From Latin *inbonitas*, from *in-* (in, on, upon) + *bonus* (good). Though not directly attested in Classical Latin, the word likely arose in later Vulgar Latin or Medieval Latin.
