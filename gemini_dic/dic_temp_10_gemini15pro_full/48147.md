decolonizing: /diːˈkɒlənaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 去殖民化 | The act of freeing a country or territory from colonial rule, and dismantling colonial systems of power and control.  It also refers to the process of challenging and changing colonial mindsets, practices, and institutions. |  Many countries in Africa were decolonizing in the mid-20th century.  许多非洲国家在20世纪中期都在进行去殖民化。

The museum is decolonizing its collection by returning artifacts to their countries of origin.  这家博物馆正在通过将文物归还原籍国的方式对其藏品进行去殖民化。 | 近义词: liberating, emancipating, freeing | 反义词: colonizing, subjugating | 词性: 动词 (present participle of decolonize)

Efforts are underway to decolonize the curriculum. 正在努力对课程进行去殖民化。 | 近义词: liberate, emancipate, free | 反义词: colonize, subjugate | 词性: 动词

词源:  From de- + colonize, from colony.  "De-" is a prefix meaning "remove" or "reverse".  "Colonize" comes from the Latin word "colonia" meaning "farm," "settlement," or "colony."
