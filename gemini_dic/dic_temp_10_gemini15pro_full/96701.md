ineffablenesses:/ɪnˈɛfəbəlˈnɛsɪz/| n. |不可名状；难以言喻|The quality of being too great or extreme to be expressed or described in words.  The plural form of ineffableness.|The ineffablenesses of religious experience are difficult to convey to others.  宗教体验的难以言喻之处很难向别人传达。|Synonyms: inexpressibility, indescribability, unutterability|Antonyms: expressibility, describability, communicability|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English ineffable + -ness. Ineffable from Latin ineffābilis, from in- + effābilis (“speakable”), from ex- + fāri (“to speak”).
