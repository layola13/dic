panish:/ˈpænɪʃ/| adj.| 惊恐的，恐慌的 | Feeling or showing sudden fear, especially in a way that makes you act without thinking. |  The panish horse reared up, throwing its rider.  惊恐的马直立起来，把骑手摔了下来。 | synonyms: frightened, terrified, alarmed, scared, afraid | antonyms: calm, composed, unafraid | word type: adjective

panish:/ˈpænɪʃ/| v.| 使恐慌，使惊恐 | To cause someone to feel sudden fear, especially in a way that makes them act without thinking. | The loud bang panished the birds, sending them scattering in all directions. 巨响吓坏了鸟儿，使它们四处飞散。| synonyms: frighten, terrify, alarm, scare, startle | antonyms: calm, soothe, reassure | word type: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *panir (“to make pale with fear”), from pan- + Old French issir (“to go out”), from Latin exire.  More at pan-, issue.  Influenced by vanish. The original sense was "to cause to disappear suddenly," later "to cause to faint from fear," finally "to terrify."
