gorgers:/ˈɡɔːrdʒərz/
| n. |峡谷；贪婪的人；暴食者|A deep, narrow ravine, typically one with steep rocky walls and a stream running through it; a person who eats greedily or to excess. |We hiked through the stunning gorgers of the national park. 我们徒步穿越了国家公园里令人惊叹的峡谷。|近义词：ravine, canyon, glutton |反义词:moderate eater|名词


gorgers:/ˈɡɔːrdʒərz/
| n. |华丽的服饰；（尤指旧时）精美衣饰|Fine clothes or ornaments, especially those worn in former times. |The king appeared in all his gorgers. 国王身着盛装出现。|近义词：finery, attire, raiment |反义词:rags|名词


词源:
From Old French *gorgeure* "throat," from gorge "throat." The sense of "ravine" likely arose from the image of a narrow opening like a throat. The sense of "glutton" comes from the idea of stuffing one's throat.
