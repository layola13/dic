empusa:/ɛmˈpjuːsə/ | n. | 冥府复仇女神; 妖怪; 恶鬼;  |A female demon or specter in Greek mythology, believed to seduce and destroy young men.  A kind of phantom or hobgoblin.  Also sometimes used to refer to a kind of insect, the empusa pennata (a type of mantis).| In Greek mythology, the empusa was a terrifying creature sent by Hecate. | 近义词: demon, specter, phantom, hobgoblin, Lamia | 反义词: angel, deity | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Ancient Greek *ἐмпуσα* (empousa), from *ἐν* (en, “in, on”) + *πούς* (poús, “foot”).  The name likely refers to its single bronze foot.
