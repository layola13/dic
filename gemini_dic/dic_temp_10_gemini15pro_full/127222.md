nauropometer:/nɔːrəˈpɒmɪtər/ | n. |步数计，步程计 | An instrument for measuring the distance one walks.  A device, often worn on the body, that counts steps taken and calculates distance traveled. | Example 1: The hiker used a nauropometer to track how far they walked on the trail.  徒步旅行者使用步数计来追踪他们在路上走了多远。

Example 2:  She clipped the nauropometer to her belt before starting her daily walk.  她在开始每日散步前将步数计夹在腰带上。 | Synonym: pedometer, odometer | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "map" or "GPS" as alternative methods of measuring distance) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *naus* (ship) + *pous* (foot) + *-meter* (measure).  Though the prefix refers to "ship," the instrument is used for measuring distances walked, not sailed.  The etymology likely reflects an early use of measuring distances by counting steps.
