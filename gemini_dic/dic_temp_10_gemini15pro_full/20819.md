blasted:/ˈblɑːstɪd/ /ˈblæstɪd/ | adj. |该死的，讨厌的；枯萎的；被炸毁的 | From "blast" (meaning to explode) + "-ed" (past participle suffix).  Originally referred to something literally damaged by an explosion, but now more commonly used as an intensifier to express annoyance or frustration. | The blasted train is late again!  这该死的火车又晚点了！| synonyms: damned, cursed, annoying, wretched, blighted | antonyms: blessed, fortunate, delightful | adjective


blasted:/ˈblɑːstɪd/ /ˈblæstɪd/ |  adj. |枯萎的；被摧毁的 |  Refers to something withered or destroyed, often by natural forces or disease.  Derived from the literal meaning of "blast" as a destructive force. | The blasted crops were a sign of the harsh winter. 枯萎的庄稼是严冬的迹象。| synonyms: withered, blighted, ruined, destroyed | antonyms: thriving, flourishing, healthy | adjective
