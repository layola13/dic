fig:/fɪɡ/
n.|无花果; 少量; 无价值的东西; 服装; 打扮|A small, soft, purple or green fruit with many small seeds, or the tree that produces this fruit.  Also used to refer to a small amount or something of little value. Can also mean clothing or dress, especially in the phrase "in full fig."|例句：I love fresh figs. 我喜欢新鲜的无花果。

例句：He doesn't care a fig about what people think. 他不在乎别人怎么想。

近义词：fruit, bit, trifle
反义词: lot (when referring to quantity)

词性: noun


fig:/fɪɡ/
v. (figs, figging, figged)|打扮; 盛装|To dress, adorn, or equip, often used in the phrase "fig out."  Sometimes also used to mean to make an appearance or to perform a particular action.|例句：They were all figged out in their finest clothes. 他们都穿着最好的衣服打扮起来。

例句：He figged himself out as a pirate for the party.  他把自己打扮成海盗去参加派对。


近义词：dress up, adorn, deck out
反义词: undress

词性: verb


词源: From Old French *figue, from Latin *fica, from earlier *feica, ultimately from Proto-Italic *fikā.
