despondent:/dɪˈspɑːndənt/ /dɪˈspɒndənt/| adj.|沮丧的，灰心的，失望的|In low spirits from loss of hope or courage. Feeling or showing profound hopelessness and discouragement.|She was despondent over the loss of her job. 她对失去工作感到沮丧。|近义词：dejected, discouraged, disheartened, downcast, gloomy, glum, hopeless, melancholy, miserable, sad, unhappy|反义词：cheerful, happy, hopeful, optimistic, positive|词性:形容词

despondent:/dɪˈspɑːndənt/ /dɪˈspɒndənt/| adj.|沮丧的，灰心的，失望的|From Latin despondent- ‘giving up, despairing’, from the verb despondere, from de- ‘down, away’ + spondere ‘to promise’.|He became despondent after failing the exam. 他考试不及格后变得很沮丧。|近义词：depressed, despairing|反义词：joyful, elated|词性:形容词
