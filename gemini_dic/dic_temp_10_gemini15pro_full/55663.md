ditransitivities:/ˌdaɪtræn.sɪˈtɪv.ɪ.tiz/ | n. | 双及物性; 双宾语性 | The property of a verb that takes both a direct and an indirect object. | Many English verbs exhibit ditransitivities, allowing constructions like "She gave him the book." 她给了他那本书。| ditransitive verbs, double-transitive verbs | intransitivities | noun
