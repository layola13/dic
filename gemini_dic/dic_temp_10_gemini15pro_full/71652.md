flattered:/ˈflætərd/ | adj. | 感到受宠若惊的，感到高兴的 |  Pleased and honored; feeling complimented.  Derived from the verb "flatter," which comes from the Old French word "flater" (to stroke, caress, blandish). |  I was flattered by his invitation to dinner. 我对他的晚餐邀请感到受宠若惊。 |  complimented, honored, gratified, pleased |  unimpressed, indifferent, displeased | adjective

flattered:/ˈflætərd/ | v. | 奉承，谄媚（flatter的过去分词）|  Past participle of the verb "flatter," meaning to praise excessively or insincerely, often to gain favor. |  He flattered her with compliments on her appearance. 他用恭维她外貌的话奉承她。 (This example uses the verb form, not the past participle adjective) |  complimented, praised, lauded |  criticized, condemned, disparaged | verb
