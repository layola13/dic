glare:/ɡlɛr/| v. n.| 怒视，瞪眼；发出刺眼的光| to look at someone or something with an angry expression; to shine with a very bright, unpleasant light | He glared at her across the table. 他隔着桌子怒视着她。|  The sun glared down from a clear blue sky. 太阳从晴朗的蓝天上照射下来。| synonyms: scowl, frown, glower, stare, blaze, dazzle | antonyms: smile, beam, soften, dim | verb, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *glaren, *glouren, from Old Norse *glora (“to stare”), from Proto-Germanic *glōrijaną (“to glow, to stare”). Cognate with Danish glore (“to stare”), Swedish glo (“to stare”), Icelandic glóra (“to stare”).
