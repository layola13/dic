billet:/ˈbɪlɪt/ (US), /ˈbɪleɪ/ (UK)| n. v.| 兵舍； billets (pl.)  住宿；(尤指官方提供的) 岗位，职位；金属坯；|A billet is a place, usually a private home, where soldiers are lodged.  A billet can also refer to a job or position, especially one given by the government.  In another context, it refers to a piece of metal that has been cast or rolled into a specific shape, ready for further processing.|例句：
The soldiers were billeted in local homes. = 士兵们被安排住在当地居民家中。

The mayor secured a billet for his nephew in the city council. = 市长为他的侄子在市议会谋得了一个职位。

The steel billet was ready for forging. = 钢坯准备锻造。|近义词：quarters, lodging, post, position, job, bar, ingot|反义词: none (meaning dependent)|n.


billet:/ˈbɪlɪt/ (US), /ˈbɪleɪ/ (UK)| v.| 安排…住宿；派驻；|To billet means to lodge or quarter soldiers in private homes, or to assign someone to a post or position.|例句：
The army billeted the troops in the village. = 军队把部队安排在村子里住宿。
