calumbin: /kəˈlʌmbɪn/
noun
鸽子；美貌女子；鸽属植物；白屈菜
a plant with divided leaves and yellow, orange, or red flowers, of the buttercup family.  A dove or pigeon, especially a young one. A beautiful young woman.
The calumbin in her garden were a vibrant shade of orange. 她花园里的白屈菜是鲜艳的橙色。
He saw a calumbin perched on the windowsill. 他看到一只鸽子栖息在窗台上。
Synonyms: columbine, dove, pigeon, maiden
Antonyms: (For dove/pigeon) hawk, eagle (For beautiful woman) hag, crone
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *columbīna "dove-like," from Latin columba "dove."  The sense of "beautiful young woman" arises from the perceived gentleness and beauty of doves.
