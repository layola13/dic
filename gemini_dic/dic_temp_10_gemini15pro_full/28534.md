cardinalist:/ˈkɑːrdɪnəlɪst/| n. adj.| 基数主义者；基数的|A cardinalist is someone who believes that utility can be measured and given a numerical value.  As an adjective, it relates to this principle. |Example: The economist was a staunch cardinalist, believing that all value could be quantified. 这位经济学家是一位坚定的基数主义者，认为所有价值都可以量化。

Example:  Cardinalist utility theory attempts to assign specific numbers to levels of satisfaction. 基数效用理论试图将具体的数字分配给满意度水平。 |Synonym: quantitative, numerical|Antonym: ordinalist| n. adj.

/ˈkɑːrdɪnəlɪst/| n. adj.| 基数主义者；基数的|Relating to or denoting a system of assigning numerical values to represent subjective magnitudes, such as pleasure and pain, as in cardinal utility.  A person who holds this view. |Example: The cardinalist approach to welfare economics has been largely superseded by ordinal utility theory.  基数主义的福利经济学方法在很大程度上已被序数效用理论所取代。

Example:  He argued against the cardinalist principles of the earlier economists. 他反对早期经济学家的基数主义原则。 |Synonym: quantifiable, measurable|Antonym: ordinal| n. adj.
