aggrandization:/əˌɡrænˈdaɪˈzeɪʃn/ | n. |  增大; 扩张; 夸大; 吹捧 | The act of increasing the power, status, or wealth of something or someone; the act of making something appear greater than it is. | His latest book details the aggrandizement of his family's wealth and power.  他的新书详细描述了他的家族财富和权力的扩张。|  The emperor's aggrandizement of his own power led to resentment among his people.  皇帝对自身权力的扩张导致了人民的不满。 | Synonyms: enlargement, expansion, elevation, exaggeration, magnification | Antonyms: reduction, diminution, minimization, understatement | noun


aggrandize:/əˈɡrænˌdaɪz/ | vt. | 增大; 扩张; 夸大; 吹捧 | To increase the power, status, or wealth of; to make something appear greater than it is. | He sought to aggrandize himself by claiming credit for the work of others. 他试图通过把别人的功劳归于自己来抬高自己。 |  The king aggrandized his territory by conquering neighboring lands. 国王通过征服邻近的土地扩张了他的领土。 | Synonyms: enlarge, expand, elevate, exaggerate, magnify | Antonyms: reduce, diminish, minimize, understate | verb
