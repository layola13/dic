mammies:/ˈmæmiz/
n.
乳房; (尤指) 女性的乳房; (婴儿语) 妈妈;
A woman's breasts.  Often used in a childish or informal way to refer to a mother.
例句：The baby reached for its mammies.  婴儿伸手去抓妈妈的乳房。

例句：He still calls his mother "mammies". 他仍然叫他的妈妈“mammies”。

反义词:  (No direct antonym for a body part)
词性: 名词 (plural)

mammie:/ˈmæmi/
n.
(尤指黑人保姆或女佣) 妈妈;
(formerly, in the US) a Black woman employed as a nursemaid or general domestic servant.  This term is now considered offensive.
例句：The old mammy cared for the children. (This usage is outdated and offensive.)  这位老妈子照顾孩子们。（这种用法已经过时且具有冒犯性。）

例句：In the past, "mammie" was a common stereotype in films and on television. (Referring to the historical usage.)  过去，“mammie”是电影和电视中常见的刻板印象。（指的是历史用法。）
近义词：nanny, maid (but these don't carry the same racial connotations)
反义词:  (No direct antonym for a profession)
词性: 名词 (singular)
