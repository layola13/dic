digestiveness:/daɪˈdʒɛstɪvnəs/ /daɪˈdʒɛstɪvnɪs/| n. |消化率，易消化性|The quality of being easy to digest.  The ease with which something is digested.|例句：The digestiveness of cooked food is generally higher than that of raw food.  熟食的消化率通常高于生食。|近义词：digestibility|反义词：indigestibility|词性:名词


Etymology:
From *digest* + *-ive* + *-ness*. *Digest* comes from Latin *digestus*, past participle of *digerere* "to separate, distribute, arrange; digest," from *dis-* "apart, asunder" + *gerere* "to carry, bear, manage, conduct." *-ive* is a suffix forming adjectives from verbs. *-ness* is a suffix forming nouns expressing a state or quality.
