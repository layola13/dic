hypercorrection:/ˌhaɪpərkrˈɛkʃən/| n.|过度纠正，矫枉过正|An instance of the use of a non-standard linguistic form or construction based on a mistaken belief about its correctness or prestige.  It often involves applying a rule of grammar or pronunciation in a context where it doesn't apply or overapplying a rule.|She avoided using "me" even when it was grammatically correct, a classic example of hypercorrection. 她即使在语法正确的情况下也避免使用“me”，这是一个典型的过度纠正的例子。|近义词：overcorrection, overcompensation|反义词: undercorrection|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- + correction.  Hyper- comes from Greek *hyper* meaning "over, above, beyond." Correction comes from Latin *corrigere* "to set right, make straight, correct," from *com-* "together" (see com-) + *regere* "to lead straight, guide, rule."
