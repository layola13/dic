outblundered:/ˌaʊtˈblʌndərd/
|v.|犯更大的错误，在失误方面胜过|To make more or bigger mistakes than someone else; to surpass in blundering.|He outblundered his opponent, making three crucial errors in the final minutes of the game. 他在比赛的最后几分钟犯了三个关键性的错误，在失误方面胜过了他的对手。|Synonyms: outmistake, outfox (in a negative sense), surpass in errors|Antonyms: outsmart, outmaneuver|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "out-" (prefix indicating exceeding or surpassing) + "blunder" (verb meaning to make a stupid or careless mistake).  "Blunder" itself comes from probably Scandinavian origin, compare with Old Norse *blundra "to shut one's eyes".
