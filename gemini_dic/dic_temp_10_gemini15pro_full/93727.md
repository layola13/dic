immantle:/ɪˈmæntəl/| vt.| 覆盖，遮盖；赋予（权力、职责等）| To cover or envelop something completely, as if with a mantle; to invest with something (as power, office, or authority) | The new king was immantled with the royal robes. 新国王披上了皇家礼袍。| 近义词：cloak, drape, invest, endow | 反义词：uncover, expose, divest | 词性：动词

immantle:/ɪˈmæntəl/| n.| 覆盖物，遮盖物 | A covering or cloak | The fog served as an immantle over the city. 雾笼罩着整个城市。| 近义词：covering, cloak, mantle | 反义词：uncovering, exposure | 词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->

From im- (variant of in- meaning "in, into, on, upon, against") + mantle (n.) "loose sleeveless cloak," from Old French mantel (12c.), from Latin mantellum "cloak, covering," perhaps from Late Latin mantum "woolen cloth."  The notion is of being "covered with a mantle."
