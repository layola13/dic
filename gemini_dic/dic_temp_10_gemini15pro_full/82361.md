grown:/ɡroʊn/ (US) /ɡrəʊn/ (UK)| adj.| 成长的，成熟的；长大的；种植的 | fully developed or adult; cultivated |  The trees have grown tall.  这些树已经长得很高了。| Synonyms: mature, adult, developed, cultivated | Antonyms: immature, undeveloped, young, wild | adjective

grown:/ɡroʊn/ (US) /ɡrəʊn/ (UK)| past participle of "grow"| 生长 | past participle of "grow" | The children have grown so much this year. 今年孩子们长大了很多。| Synonyms: matured, developed | Antonyms: shrunk, withered | verb (past participle)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *growen*, from Old English *grōwen*, past participle of *grōwan* (“to grow”), from Proto-Germanic *grōaną* (“to grow”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵreh₁- (“to grow, become green”). Cognate with Dutch *gegroeid* (“grown”), German *gewachsen* (“grown”), Danish *groet* (“grown”), Swedish *grodd* (“sprout”).
