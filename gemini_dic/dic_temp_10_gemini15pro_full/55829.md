dividednesses:/dɪˈvaɪdɪdnɪsɪz/
n.
分裂，分歧
The state of being divided or separated.
The dividednesses within the party threatened its future.  党内的分歧威胁着它的未来。
近义词:divisions, disagreements, schisms, factions, rifts
反义词:unity, agreement, harmony, concord, solidarity
词性:名词


Etymology:
From divide + -ed + -ness + -es.  "Divide" comes from the Old French "dividir," ultimately from the Latin "dividere" meaning "to force asunder, distribute."  The suffix "-ed" forms the past participle, "-ness" forms a noun indicating a state or quality, and "-es" marks the plural.
