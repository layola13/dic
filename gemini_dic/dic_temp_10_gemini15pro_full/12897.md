assume:/əˈsuːm/ | v. | 假定，假设；承担；呈现 | to suppose to be the case, without proof; to take or begin to have (power or responsibility); to begin to have (a particular quality, appearance, etc.) | She assumed that he was telling the truth. 她假定他说的是实话。| synonyms: presume, suppose, posit | antonyms: doubt, question | verb

assumption:/əˈsʌmpʃən/ | n. | 假定，假设；承担；担任 | a thing that is accepted as true or as certain to happen, without proof; the act of taking or beginning to have power or responsibility |  His assumption proved to be incorrect. 他的假设被证明是错误的。 | synonyms: presumption, supposition, hypothesis | antonyms: fact, certainty, proof | noun

assumed:/əˈsuːmd/ | adj. | 假定的，假设的；装出来的 | accepted as real or true without proof; pretended; adopted in order to deceive | He spoke with assumed confidence. 他说话装作很自信。 | synonyms: pretended, feigned, affected | antonyms: genuine, real, authentic | adjective
