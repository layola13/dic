abeyancy: /əˈbeɪənsi/
n.
中止，搁置，暂时停顿
A state of temporary inactivity or suspension.
The project is in abeyance until we get more funding.  该项目暂停，直到我们获得更多资金。

The development has been held in abeyance pending further investigation.  该开发项目在进一步调查之前一直处于搁置状态。

近义词: suspension, dormancy, quiescence, inactivity
反义词: activity, continuance, operation, progress
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *abeance "aspiration, gaping after," from abeer "to gape," from Latin ad- "to" + bavere "to gape." Originally, in law, a state of expectancy in respect of property, titles, or offices, when the right to them is not vested in any one person, but awaits the appearance or determination of the true owner.


USAGE->
The bill is currently in abeyance. = 这项法案目前处于搁置状态。

The project was held in abeyance due to lack of funding. = 由于缺乏资金，该项目被搁置。

PHRASE->
hold in abeyance = 搁置
fall into abeyance = 逐渐失效
be in abeyance = 处于搁置状态
keep in abeyance = 保持搁置状态
place in abeyance =  搁置
