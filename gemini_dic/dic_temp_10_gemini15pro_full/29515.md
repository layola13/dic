catastrophism: /kəˈtæstrəˌfɪzəm/ | n. |灾变说，灾变论|The theory that the Earth has been affected in the past by sudden, short-lived, violent events, possibly worldwide in scope.|例句：Catastrophism was a popular theory in the 19th century.  19世纪，灾变说是盛行的理论。

例句：Some scientists believe catastrophism played a role in the extinction of the dinosaurs. 一些科学家认为灾变论在恐龙灭绝中起了作用。|近义词：cataclysm, disaster, upheaval|反义词：gradualism, uniformitarianism|词性: noun

Etymology: From French *catastrophisme*, from Ancient Greek *καταστροφή* (katastrophḗ, “overturn, sudden turn, overthrow, ruin, catastrophe”) + *-isme*.
