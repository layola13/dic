blackfoot:/ˈblækˌfʊt/| n. adj.|黑脚族（人）；黑脚病|A member of a North American Indian people of the northwestern plains, now living chiefly in Montana, Alberta, and Saskatchewan. Also: the Algonquian language of this people.  (As an adjective) Relating to the Blackfoot people or their language. |例句：The Blackfoot tribe held a powwow. 黑脚族部落举行了一场帕瓦仪式。

|例句：She is studying the Blackfoot language. 她正在学习黑脚族语。|近义词：Siksika (often used interchangeably, especially for the people),  Piegan (a division of the Blackfoot)|反义词: None (as it refers to a specific group and language)|词性: Noun, Adjective


blackfoot:/ˈblækˌfʊt/| n.|黑脚病|A disease of grapevines, characterized by darkening and rotting of the roots.|例句：The vineyard was affected by blackfoot disease.  葡萄园受到了黑脚病的影响。

|例句：Blackfoot can devastate a grape crop. 黑脚病会摧毁葡萄作物。|近义词：None (as it refers to a specific plant disease)|反义词: None|词性: Noun
