chevage:/ˈtʃɛvɪdʒ/
n.
人头税；身丁税
A poll tax or head tax paid by serfs or villains to their feudal lord for permission to live outside the manor.
In medieval times, serfs often paid chevage to their lord for the right to live and work in a town. 中世纪时期，农奴常常向他们的领主缴纳人头税以获得在城镇生活和工作的权利。
