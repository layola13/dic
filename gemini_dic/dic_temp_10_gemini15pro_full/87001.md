henotheism:/ˈhɛnəʊθiːɪzəm/ | n. | 唯一神崇拜;单一神崇拜 |The belief in and worship of a single god while accepting the existence or possible existence of other deities.  Acknowledging other gods, but elevating one above all others in worship and devotion. |Ancient Egyptians practiced a form of henotheism, focusing their worship on a particular deity depending on the region and time period, while not denying the existence of others. 古埃及人实行一种唯一神崇拜的形式，根据地区和时间段将他们的崇拜集中于一个特定的神，同时不否认其他神的存在。

Many tribal societies exhibit henotheism, choosing a patron deity while acknowledging a broader pantheon. 许多部落社会都表现出唯一神崇拜，选择一个守护神，同时承认一个更广泛的神殿。 | 近义词:monolatry | 反义词:polytheism, atheism | 词性:noun

Etymology: From Greek *ἕνος (henos, “one”) + *θεός (theos, “god”) + *-ισμός (-ismos, suffix forming abstract nouns).
