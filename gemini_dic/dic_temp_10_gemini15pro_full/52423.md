didn't: /dɪdnt/ | aux. v. | 没有 | Contraction of "did not," used to form the negative of the simple past tense.  It indicates that an action did not occur in the past. | I didn't go to the party last night. 我昨晚没去参加派对。|  Near Synonyms: wasn't/weren't | Antonym: did | auxiliary verb

词源: "didn't" is a contraction of "did not." "Did" is the past tense form of the auxiliary verb "do," which originates from Old English "dōn."  "Not" comes from Old English "nawiht" meaning "nothing."
