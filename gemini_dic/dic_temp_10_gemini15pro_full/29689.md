cathetometer: /ˌkæθɪˈtɒmɪtər/ | n. | 测高仪，视距仪 | An optical instrument used for measuring vertical distances precisely. It typically consists of a telescope mounted on a vertical pillar, with a graduated scale and vernier for accurate readings. | Example 1: The scientist used a cathetometer to measure the height of the liquid in the capillary tube. 科学家用测高仪测量毛细管中液体的液位高度。

Example 2: A cathetometer was employed to determine the precise vertical displacement of the platform.  测高仪被用来确定平台的精确垂直位移。 | Synonym: height gauge, altimeter (for altitude) | Antonym: N/A (no direct antonym for a measuring instrument) | Noun
