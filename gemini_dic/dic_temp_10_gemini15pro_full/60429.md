eightscore:/ˌeɪtˈskɔːr/ | num. |一百六十|One hundred and sixty; eight times twenty.  It's an archaic term, not commonly used in modern English. |例句：Eightscore years ago, our forefathers brought forth on this continent, a new nation... 一百六十年前，我们的先辈们在这片大陆上创建了一个新的国家……|近义词：one hundred and sixty, one hundred sixty |反义词: N/A|词性:Number(基数词)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *eahta + scoru  (eight + score (twenty)).  "Score" as a unit of twenty is of Scandinavian origin.
