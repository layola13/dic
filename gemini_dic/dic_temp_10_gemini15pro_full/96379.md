indiscreet:/ˌɪndɪˈskriːt/| adj.|不慎重的，轻率的，鲁莽的|Not showing good judgment or care in behavior or speech, especially in revealing private information.|例句：It was indiscreet of him to mention the matter in public.  他不谨慎地在公共场合提到了这件事。

例句：She made some indiscreet remarks about her colleagues. 她对同事们说了一些不谨慎的话。|近义词：imprudent, unwise, careless, tactless|反义词：discreet, prudent, careful, tactful|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From in- (not) + discreet (from Latin discretus, past participle of discernere "to separate, distinguish").  The original meaning was "not separated or distinct," but the modern sense of "lacking caution" developed later.
