gangion:/ˈɡæŋɡjən/| n. |钓钩组，子线，脑线|A length of fishing line, typically made of gut or nylon, connecting a hook to the main line.  It often includes multiple hooks. |The angler lost a large trout when the gangion snapped. 钓者因为子线断了而失去了一条大鳟鱼。

The gangion was baited with a lively worm. 这条子线挂着一条活蹦乱跳的蠕虫。|近义词：leader, snood, trace|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gangon, *gangoun, from Anglo-Norman *gangun, *gangoun, from Old North French *gangon, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old French *gange, *jange "trap, snare," or to gang "a going, a course."
