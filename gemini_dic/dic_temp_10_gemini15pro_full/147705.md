overpreoccupied: /ˌoʊvərpriˈɑːkjupaɪd/ | adj. | 过度关注的，过分投入的 | Excessively preoccupied; too absorbed or engrossed in something, often to the detriment of other things.  It implies an unhealthy or excessive level of concern or attention. | He was overpreoccupied with his work and neglected his family. 他过度专注于工作而忽略了家人。|  She's been overpreoccupied with her health lately. 她最近过分关注自己的健康。 | 近义词: obsessed, fixated, engrossed, consumed | 反义词: detached, indifferent, unconcerned | 词性: 形容词

词源：The prefix "over-" means "too much" or "excessive."  "Preoccupied" comes from the Latin "praeoccupatus," past participle of "praeoccupare," meaning "to seize beforehand."  Therefore, "overpreoccupied" literally means "too much seized beforehand" or "excessively engrossed."
