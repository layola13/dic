deconsecrate:/ˌdiːˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/| vt.| 褫夺神圣性；世俗化；|To deprive (a church, graveyard, etc.) of its sacred character.  To declare (something formerly sacred) no longer sacred.|The church was deconsecrated and turned into a restaurant.  这座教堂被褫夺了神圣性，变成了一家餐馆。|近义词：desecrate, profane|反义词：consecrate, sanctify|词性:动词

Etymology:
From de- + consecrate.  "De-" is a prefix meaning "remove" or "reverse," and "consecrate" means "to make or declare sacred."
