admonishing: /ədˈmɒnɪʃɪŋ/ (UK) /ˌædˈmɑːnɪʃɪŋ/ (US)
adjective
劝诫的；警告的；责备的
Giving or conveying a warning or reprimand.
The teacher gave the students an admonishing look. 老师给了学生们一个警告的眼神。

Her tone was admonishing, but her eyes were kind. 她的语气是责备的，但她的眼神是友善的。

近义词: reproving, reprimanding, rebuking, chiding, scolding, cautioning, warning
反义词: praising, commending, encouraging
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin admonit-, past participle stem of admonēre "to advise, remind," from ad- "to" + monēre "to warn."

USAGE->
作为形容词修饰名词:
He delivered an admonishing speech about the dangers of drug use. = 他发表了一篇关于吸毒危害的劝诫性演讲。
She cast an admonishing glance at her noisy children. = 她向她吵闹的孩子们投去警告性的一瞥。

作为谓语的组成部分（通常用进行时态）:
He was admonishing his son for staying out too late. = 他在责备他的儿子在外面待得太晚。
The coach was admonishing the team for their lackluster performance. = 教练正在责备球队缺乏活力的表现。

PHRASE->
an admonishing glance = 警告性的一瞥
an admonishing tone = 警告性的语气
an admonishing letter = 警告信
an admonishing lecture = 劝诫性的演讲
an admonishing look = 警告的眼神
