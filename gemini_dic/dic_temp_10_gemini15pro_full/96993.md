infantilizing: /ˈɪnfəntɪˌlaɪzɪŋ/
infantilize: /ˈɪnfənˌtɪlaɪz/ | v. |  幼稚化；像对待小孩一样对待 | To treat (someone) as if they were a child, especially by not allowing them to do things or make decisions that they are old enough to do. |  He resented being infantilized by his parents. 他讨厌被父母当作小孩看待。 |  patronize, condescend, coddle | empower, respect | verb

infantilization: /ɪnˌfæn.tɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən/ | n. | 幼稚化；幼儿化 | The act of infantilizing someone or something. | The infantilization of women in advertising is a common complaint.  广告中对女性的幼稚化是一个普遍的抱怨。 | patronization, condescension | empowerment, respect | noun
