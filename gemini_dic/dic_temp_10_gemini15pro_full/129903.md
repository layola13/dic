non-issue:/ˌnɒnˈɪʃuː/ (US) /ˌnɒnˈɪsjuː/ (UK)
n.|无关紧要的事；不成问题的问题|Something that is not a real problem or cause for concern, often something that people are arguing or worrying about unnecessarily.|The parking situation turned out to be a non-issue, as there were plenty of spaces. 停车位问题结果根本不是问题，因为有很多空位。|Synonyms: trifle, triviality, nothing, bagatelle|Antonyms: problem, issue, dilemma, crisis|noun

non-issue:/ˌnɒnˈɪʃuː/ (US) /ˌnɒnˈɪsjuː/ (UK)
Etymology:|From non- + issue.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Issue" comes from Old French "issue," meaning "exit, outcome, result."| | | | | |
