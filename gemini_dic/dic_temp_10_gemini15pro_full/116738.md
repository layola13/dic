maunch:/mɔːntʃ/ /mɑːntʃ/ | v. | 大嚼；狼吞虎咽 | To eat something greedily and noisily. | He maunched his apple with gusto. 他津津有味地大嚼着苹果。| The hungry boy maunched down the entire pizza. 饥饿的男孩狼吞虎咽地吃掉了整个披萨。| 近义词: munch, chew, devour, gobble | 反义词: nibble |词性: 动词

maunch:/mɔːntʃ/ /mɑːntʃ/ | n. | (过时的) 袖子；袖套 | A sleeve, especially a wide or hanging sleeve. (Archaic) |  The king's maunch was adorned with jewels. 国王的袖子上装饰着珠宝。(古语用法，现在几乎不用) | Her medieval gown had long, flowing maunches. 她的中世纪长袍有着长长的、飘逸的袖子。(古语用法，现在几乎不用) | 近义词: sleeve | 反义词:  (无) |词性: 名词

词源：
From Middle English *maunchen, *manchen, from Anglo-Norman manchier, manger (“to eat”),  from Latin mandūcāre (“to chew”). Related to mandate.  与“mandate”（命令）有关联，最初的含义是“咀嚼”。
