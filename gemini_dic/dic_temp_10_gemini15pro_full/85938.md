headlongs:/ˈhedlɒŋz/ (UK), /ˈhedlɔŋz/ (US)| adv.|轻率地，鲁莽地，迅猛地|With great speed and without thinking carefully; recklessly.  Derived from "headlong," which itself comes from "head" + "long." Originally used adverbially (14th century) to mean "with the head foremost," later evolving to its current sense of reckless haste. |He rushed headlongs into the burning building. 他冲进燃烧的建筑物。|She fell headlongs down the stairs. 她一头栽下楼梯。|近义词：recklessly, hastily, rashly, precipitously|反义词：cautiously, carefully, deliberately|词性：副词
