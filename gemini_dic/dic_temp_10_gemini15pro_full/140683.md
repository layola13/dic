ominously:/ˈɒmɪnəsli/ (US) /ˈɒmɪnəsli/ (UK)| adv.|不祥地，有预兆地|In a way that suggests that something bad is going to happen.|The sky darkened ominously. 天空不祥地暗了下来。|The music grew ominously louder.  音乐声越来越大，预示着不祥。|近义词：threateningly, forebodingly, menacingly|反义词：auspiciously, favorably|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *ominousli*, from ominous + -ly.  Ominous itself comes from Latin *ominōsus* (“full of foreboding”), from *ōmen* (“omen, portent”) + *-ōsus* (suffix forming adjectives of abundance).
