credit unions:/ˈkrɛdɪt ˈjuːnjənz/ | n. |信用合作社|A non-profit financial institution that is owned and operated by its members.  It provides financial services, such as loans and savings accounts, to its members. |Example 1: Credit unions often offer better interest rates than traditional banks. 信用合作社通常提供比传统银行更好的利率。

Example 2:  My parents have been members of the same credit union for over 30 years. 我的父母是同一家信用合作社的会员已经超过30年了。|Synonym: cooperative bank, savings and loan association |Antonym: commercial bank |noun
