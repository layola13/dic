charnel: /ˈtʃɑːrnl/ | adj. n.| 停尸房的，阴森的；停尸房，纳骨堂 | A charnel house or charnel is a vault or building where human skeletal remains are stored.  A place or situation suggestive of death and decay. |  The battle left a charnel house of mangled bodies.  战争过后，战场上尸横如堆，如同停尸房。 The air was thick with the charnel stench of decay.  空气中弥漫着腐烂的尸臭味。 | 近义词： sepulchral, macabre, gruesome, ghoulish | 反义词: lively, vibrant | 词性: adjective, noun

charnel: /ˈtʃɑːrnl/ | n. | 停尸房，纳骨堂 | A building or vault, typically in a churchyard, where corpses or bones are deposited.  | The monks prayed in the charnel house amidst the bones of their predecessors.  僧侣们在停尸房里，在他们前辈的骸骨中祈祷。| 近义词： ossuary, crypt, tomb | 反义词:  None (lack of a direct antonym for a place of storing remains) | 词性: noun


词源:  From Old French charnel, from Late Latin carnāle “relating to the flesh,”  from Latin caro, carn- “flesh.”
