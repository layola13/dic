opals:/ˈoʊpəlz/ | n. | 猫眼石；蛋白石 | A gemstone consisting of hydrated silica, typically semitransparent and showing varying colors against a white or dark ground. | She wore a necklace of shimmering opals. 她戴着一条闪闪发光的猫眼石项链。 | The queen's opals were legendary. 女王的猫眼石具有传奇色彩。 | synonyms: gemstone, jewel, precious stone | antonyms: none | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *opalle, from Latin opalus, from Greek opallios.  Ultimately of uncertain origin, possibly from Sanskrit upala (“precious stone, gem”).
