gnarled: /nɑːrld/ | adj. | 疙瘩的，多节的，扭曲的 | Covered with gnarls; knotty and twisted, especially with age.  Typically used to describe wood, branches, or hands. | The old man's gnarled hands told a story of years of hard work. 老人的粗糙双手讲述了多年辛勤劳作的故事。| The tree's gnarled branches reached up towards the sky. 树木扭曲的树枝伸向天空。 |  近义词：knotty, twisted, knobby, misshapen | 反义词：smooth, straight | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English knorren (“to twist, knot”), from Old English *gnor (“knot”), of Germanic origin.  Related to Old Norse gnurr (“knot, protuberance”).
