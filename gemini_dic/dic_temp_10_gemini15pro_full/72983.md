foolhardy:/ˈfuːlˌhɑːrdi/ | adj. | 鲁莽的，蛮干的 | Recklessly bold or rash; marked by daring or boldness that disregards possible dangers or consequences. |  It was foolhardy to try to climb the mountain in such bad weather. 在如此恶劣的天气里尝试爬山是鲁莽的。|  reckless, rash, impetuous, imprudent, daredevil | cautious, prudent, circumspect, sensible | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *folhardi, from Old English *folheard "foolishly bold," from fol "foolish" + heard "hard, brave." The original sense was "foolishly brave," but the modern sense emphasizes the negative aspect of recklessness.
