hold-on:/ˈhoʊld ɑːn/ (US), /ˈhəʊld ɒn/ (UK)| int. n. |坚持住；等一下；别挂断|Used as an exclamation to tell someone to wait or stop.  As a noun, it refers to the act of holding on, especially in a difficult situation. |Example: "Hold on, I'll get the door."  “等一下，我去开门。” |Synonyms: wait, hang on, stay on the line|Antonyms: go ahead, proceed, continue|interjection, noun

hold-on:/ˈhoʊld ɑːn/ (US), /ˈhəʊld ɒn/ (UK)| n. |抓紧，控制|The act of gripping something tightly. |Example: "He lost his hold-on and fell." “他没抓稳，摔倒了.”|Synonyms: grip, grasp|Antonyms: release, let go|noun
