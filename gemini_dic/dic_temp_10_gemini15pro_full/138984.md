nymphaea:/nɪmˈfiːə/ | n. |睡莲；睡莲属植物|A genus of freshwater plants, commonly known as water lilies, characterized by large, floating leaves and showy flowers.|Example: The nymphaea in the pond bloomed beautifully. 池塘里的睡莲开得很漂亮。|Example:  She admired the vibrant colors of the nymphaea. 她欣赏睡莲鲜艳的颜色。|Synonym: water lily, lotus (loosely) |Antonym: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *nymphaea*, from Ancient Greek νυμφαία (numphaía, “water lily”), from νύμφη (númphē, “nymph, bride”).  The association is likely due to the beauty and aquatic habitat of the plant, reminiscent of the mythical nymphs associated with water.
