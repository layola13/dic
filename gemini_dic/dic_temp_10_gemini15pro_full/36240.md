cognizable:/ˈkɑːɡnɪzəbl/ /ˈkɒɡnɪzəbl/| adj.|可认识的；可感知的；可审理的|Something that is cognizable is perceptible, noticeable, or able to be known or understood, especially by the senses or the mind. In legal contexts, it refers to a case that can be tried or examined by a court.|The difference in sound quality was barely cognizable.  音质上的差别几乎难以察觉。|perceptible, noticeable, discernible, perceivable|imperceptible, unnoticeable, indiscernible|adjective

cognizance:/ˈkɑːɡnɪzəns/ /ˈkɒɡnɪzəns/| n.|认识；审理；(尤指法庭的)权限，管辖|Cognizance refers to knowledge, awareness, or notice taken of something. In legal contexts, it means the right of a court to hear and decide a case.|The court took cognizance of the defendant's previous convictions. 法庭注意到被告之前的定罪记录。|awareness, knowledge, notice, perception|ignorance, unawareness, obliviousness|noun
