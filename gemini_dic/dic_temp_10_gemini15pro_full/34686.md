clerkish:/ˈklɜːrkɪʃ/ (US) /ˈklɑːkɪʃ/ (UK)| adj.|职员似的；文书的；呆板的|Resembling or characteristic of a clerk; especially : given to or suggesting meticulous and often overly formal procedures.  Suggesting a clerk in behavior or appearance, especially in being too concerned with small details and rules.|He has a rather clerkish manner. 他举止相当文绉绉的。|近义词：bureaucratic, pedantic, officious|反义词：informal, casual, dynamic|词性:adjective
Etymology: From clerk + -ish.  "Clerk" comes from Old English "clerc," meaning "priest, scholar," ultimately from Greek "klerikos," meaning "of the clergy."  The suffix "-ish" forms adjectives meaning "somewhat like" or "having the quality of."
