horrifies:/ˈhɔːrɪfaɪz/ | v. | 使恐惧，使震惊 | To fill with horror; shock greatly. | The violence in the movie horrifies me.  这部电影里的暴力让我感到恐惧。| synonyms: terrifies, frightens, shocks, appalls | antonyms: delights, pleases, charms | verb

horrified:/ˈhɔːrɪfaɪd/ | adj. | 恐惧的，震惊的 | Filled with horror; greatly shocked. | I was horrified by the news of the accident. 我对事故的消息感到震惊。| synonyms: terrified, frightened, shocked, appalled | antonyms: delighted, pleased, charmed | adjective

horrifying:/ˈhɔːrɪfaɪɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人恐惧的，令人震惊的 | Causing horror; shocking. | The horrifying images of the war were hard to forget.  战争中可怕的画面难以忘记。| synonyms: terrifying, frightening, shocking, appalling | antonyms: delightful, pleasing, charming | adjective

Etymology:  From Late Middle English *horrifyen*, from Old French *horrifier*, from Latin *horrificāre* (“to make rough or bristly, make terrible”), from *horrificus* (“causing horror”), from *horror* (“horror, dread”) + *-ficus* (suffix forming adjectives of action or effect).
