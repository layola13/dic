chinone: /ˈkɪnoʊn/ | n. |醌|A cyclic organic compound containing two carbonyl groups, C=O, either adjacent or separated by a vinylene group, –CH=CH–, in a six-membered unsaturated ring.  They are often colored and serve as important components in many biological electron transport systems.|Quinones play a crucial role in photosynthesis.|近义词: cyclohexadienedione (specifically for benzoquinone)|反义词:  (No direct chemical antonym)|词性:noun
