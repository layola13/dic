make-a-show-of:/meɪk ə ʃoʊ əv/ | phrasal verb | 炫耀；装腔作势；假装；小题大做 | To ostentatiously display or exaggerate something, often to impress others or create a particular impression.  It implies a degree of insincerity or artificiality. | He made a show of checking his watch, as if he was in a hurry. 他假装看了看手表，好像很匆忙似的。|  Synonyms: show off, pretend, feign, exaggerate, put on an act | Antonyms: be genuine, be sincere, downplay, understate | verb phrase

make-a-show-of:/meɪk ə ʃoʊ əv/ | phrasal verb | 炫耀；装腔作势；假装；小题大做 | To behave in a way intended to attract attention and impress people. | She made a big show of donating to charity, but it was a relatively small amount. 她大张旗鼓地捐款给慈善机构，但实际上数额很小。| Synonyms:  boast, parade, flaunt | Antonyms: be modest, be humble, hide, conceal | verb phrase


ETYMOLOGY->
"make" comes from Old English "macian" meaning "to make, form, build, create, compose, perform, do, cause."

"show" comes from Old English "scēawian" meaning "to look at, see, inspect, view; appear, show oneself."

"of" comes from Old English "of" meaning "away, away from, off; out of, from, by."
