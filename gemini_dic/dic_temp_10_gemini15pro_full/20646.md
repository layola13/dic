blackout:/ˈblækaʊt/| n.|停电；灯火管制；消息封锁；昏厥；|A period of darkness caused by a failure of the electrical power supply.  A suppression of information, especially one imposed by government.|例句：The storm caused a city-wide blackout.  暴风雨导致全城停电。

The government imposed a news blackout after the coup. 政变后政府实行新闻封锁。|近义词：power outage, power failure, outage, amnesia, loss of consciousness|反义词: illumination, enlightenment|词性:名词

blackout:/ˈblækaʊt/| vt.|使昏厥；使漆黑一片；封锁消息；|To cause a loss of consciousness. To cause a period of darkness by extinguishing lights or interrupting the electricity supply. To suppress information about something.|例句：The boxer blacked out after the knockout punch. 拳击手被击倒后昏迷了。

The city was blacked out during the air raid.  空袭期间城市一片漆黑。|近义词：knock out, darken, suppress, censor|反义词: illuminate, publicize|词性:动词
