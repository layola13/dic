ETYMOLOGY->
From harvest + -able + -ity + -'s (possessive).  "Harvest" comes from Old English *hærfest (autumn), from Proto-Germanic *harbistaz. "-able" is a suffix meaning "capable of being," from Old French -able and Latin -abilis. "-ity" is a suffix forming nouns of quality or state, from Old French -ité and Latin -itas. "-'s" is the possessive suffix.
