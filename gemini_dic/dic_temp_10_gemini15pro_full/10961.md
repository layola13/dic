arbitraging:/ˈɑːrbɪtrɑːʒɪŋ/ | n. | 套利，套汇 | The simultaneous buying and selling of securities, currency, or commodities in different markets or in derivative forms in order to take advantage of differing prices for the same asset. | He made a fortune by arbitraging between the London and New York gold markets.  他通过在伦敦和纽约黄金市场之间套利发了财。 | 近义词: profiting, speculating | 反义词: N/A | 词性: Noun (Gerund)

arbitrage: /ˈɑːrbɪtrɑːʒ/ | n. v. | 套利，套汇 | The practice of taking advantage of a price difference between two or more markets: striking a combination of matching deals that capitalize upon the imbalance, the profit being the difference between the market prices. |  The arbitrage between the two markets offered a significant profit opportunity. 这两个市场之间的套利提供了巨大的获利机会。 | 近义词: speculation, trading | 反义词: N/A | 词性: Noun, Verb

词源: From French arbitrage, from arbitrer ‘to arbitrate’, from Latin arbitrari.
