far-away:/ˌfɑːr əˈweɪ/ (UK) /ˌfɑːr əˈweɪ/ (US)| adj.|遥远的；久远的|Situated at a great distance; remote in space or time. |例句：She dreamed of far-away lands. 她梦想着遥远的国度。|例句：He had a far-away look in his eyes. 他的眼神很茫然。|近义词：distant, remote, far-off|反义词：near, nearby, close|词性:形容词
词源: From "far" + "away".  "Far" comes from Old English "feorr," from Proto-Germanic "*ferraz," from Proto-Indo-European "*per-," meaning "forward, through, across."  "Away" comes from Old English "onweg," meaning "on the way, away."
