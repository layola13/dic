neostriatum:/ˌniːoʊstraɪˈeɪtəm/| n. |新纹状体|A subcortical structure of the brain, part of the basal ganglia, involved in motor control, motor learning, executive functions and decision-making; it receives input from the cerebral cortex and other brain areas and is composed of the caudate nucleus and the putamen.|The neostriatum plays a crucial role in the control of movement.|Synonym: striatum|Antonym: N/A|noun
