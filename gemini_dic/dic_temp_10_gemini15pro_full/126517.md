naafi:/ˈnɑːfi/| adj.|有益的，有利的|Beneficial; advantageous; helpful.  Derived from the Arabic word "nafi`" meaning "useful" or "profitable". It was commonly used in British military slang, particularly during World War II, to describe amenities and services provided for soldiers' welfare, such as canteens and entertainment facilities.|The NAAFI provided a welcome break from the rigors of army life.|Synonyms: beneficial, advantageous, helpful, useful, profitable|Antonyms: detrimental, harmful, disadvantageous|adjective
