commination: /ˌkɒmɪˈneɪʃn/ (US) /ˌkɑːmɪˈneɪʃn/| n. |威胁，恐吓; 咒逐; 严厉的谴责|An expression of anger or disapproval; a threat of punishment or vengeance.  A denunciation, especially involving divine vengeance or a church's curse.|The king issued a commination against the rebels. 国王对叛乱分子发出了威胁。|近义词：threat, denunciation, curse, anathema|反义词：benediction, blessing|词性: noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *comminationem (nominative comminatio) "a threatening," noun of action from past-participle stem of comminari "to threaten greatly," from com- "together" (see com-) + minari "to threaten" (see menace).
