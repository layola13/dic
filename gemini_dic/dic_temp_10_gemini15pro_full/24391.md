brinkless:/ˈbrɪŋklɪs/
adj.
无边缘的，无边际的
Having no brink or edge; boundless.
The poet described a brinkless expanse of ocean. 诗人描绘了一片无边无际的海洋。
近义词：boundless, limitless, infinite
反义词：limited, finite, bounded
词性：形容词

词源:
From brink + -less.  "Brink" comes from Middle English *brinke, from Old Norse *brekka ("slope, edge").  "-less" is a common Old English suffix indicating lack or absence of something.

PHRASE->
(Brinkless is not typically used in phrases.  Here are some phrases using "brink" instead.)

on the brink of:  濒临...边缘
The company is on the brink of bankruptcy.  这家公司濒临破产。

the brink of disaster: 灾难的边缘
The world stood on the brink of disaster during the Cuban Missile Crisis.  在古巴导弹危机期间，世界处于灾难的边缘。

pulled back from the brink: 从边缘拉回来
Negotiations pulled the two countries back from the brink of war. 谈判将两国从战争边缘拉了回来。

to the brink:  到...的边缘
His constant complaining drove her to the brink.  他不断的抱怨把她逼到了崩溃的边缘。

brinkmanship:  边缘政策
The senator accused the president of brinkmanship in the international crisis.  参议员指责总统在国际危机中采取边缘政策。
