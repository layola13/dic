break-out:/ˈbreɪkaʊt/| n. adj.|爆发，突围，越狱|A sudden escape from prison or confinement; a sudden increase or occurrence, typically of something unpleasant, such as a disease or war.|例句：There was a breakout at the local prison last night. 昨晚当地监狱发生了一起越狱事件。|近义词：escape, eruption, outbreak|反义词:containment, suppression|noun

break-out:/ˈbreɪkaʊt/| adj.|突破性的，杰出的|Characterized by or involving a sudden increase or occurrence, typically of something unpleasant, such as a disease or war; Exceptionally good or successful.|例句：The breakout performance of the season belonged to the rookie quarterback.  本赛季的突破性表现属于这位新秀四分卫。|近义词：outstanding, exceptional, remarkable|反义词:ordinary, mediocre|adjective
