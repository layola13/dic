fledgling:/ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ/
n.|雏鸟；新手；无经验的人|A young bird that has just developed wing feathers that are large enough for flight; a person or organization that is immature, inexperienced, or underdeveloped.|例句：The fledgling company is struggling to compete in the market. 这家新成立的公司正在努力在市场上竞争。|近义词：beginner, novice, trainee, apprentice, newcomer, rookie, tyro|反义词：expert, veteran, professional, master|词性:名词

fledgling:/ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ/
adj.|初出茅庐的；羽翼未丰的|New and inexperienced.|例句：The fledgling democracy is facing many challenges.  这个新生的民主国家面临着许多挑战。|近义词：emerging, nascent, budding, developing, inexperienced|反义词：mature, established, experienced, developed|词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *fleglinge, equivalent to fledge + -ling.
