hypermodesty:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɒdɪsti/ |n.| 过分谦虚，极度谦逊 | The quality of being excessively modest, often to a degree that seems insincere or affected. |  His hypermodesty made it difficult to assess his true abilities. 他的过分谦虚让人很难评估他的真实能力。| synonyms: excessive modesty, affected humility, self-deprecation | antonyms: arrogance, boastfulness, conceit | noun

modest:/ˈmɒdɪst/ |adj.| 谦虚的，谦逊的；适度的，不太大的；端庄的，朴素的 | Not boasting about or drawing attention to one's abilities or achievements; unassuming in the estimation of one's abilities or achievements. | She's very modest about her success. 她对自己的成功非常谦虚。| synonyms: humble, unassuming, self-effacing | antonyms: arrogant, boastful, conceited | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French modeste, from Latin modestus ‘keeping due measure’, from modus ‘measure’.
