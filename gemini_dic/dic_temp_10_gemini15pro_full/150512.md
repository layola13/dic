pantomimical:/ˌpæntəˈmɪmɪkl/ | adj. | 哑剧的；默剧的；像哑剧的；滑稽可笑的 | Expressed or performed by pantomime; resembling or characteristic of pantomime; exaggerated or ludicrous in expression or action. | Her pantomimical performance of the story captivated the children. 她用哑剧表演故事吸引了孩子们。| The clown's pantomimical expressions were hilarious. 小丑滑稽的表情令人捧腹大笑。 | synonyms: mimetic, silent, gestural, expressive, dramatic | antonyms: spoken, verbal, unexpressive | adjective

pantomime:/ˈpæntəmaɪm/ | n. | 哑剧；默剧 | A dramatic entertainment, originating in Roman mime, in which performers express meaning through gestures accompanied by music. | We took the children to see a pantomime at Christmas.  我们带孩子们去看圣诞哑剧。| The pantomime was a hilarious retelling of a classic fairy tale. 这部哑剧是对一个经典童话故事的滑稽演绎。 | synonyms: mime, dumb show, charade | antonyms: dialogue, speech | noun
