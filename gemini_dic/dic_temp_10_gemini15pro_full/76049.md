furlough:/ˈfɜːrloʊ/ (US), /ˈfɜːləʊ/ (UK)| n. v.|休假；暂时解雇|A period of unpaid leave from work, often as a result of economic difficulties; to grant a furlough.|例句：Many companies were forced to furlough employees during the pandemic. 许多公司在大流行期间被迫让员工休假。|近义词：layoff, leave of absence, sabbatical|反义词：hire, employ|词性:名词,动词

furlough:/ˈfɜːrloʊ/ (US), /ˈfɜːləʊ/ (UK)| v.|准许休假；暂时解雇|To grant a period of unpaid leave from work.|例句：The company furloughed 20% of its workforce. 这家公司暂时解雇了20%的员工。|近义词：lay off, dismiss temporarily|反义词：hire, employ|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Dutch "verlof," meaning "permission" or "leave." It entered English in the 17th century, initially referring to leave granted to a soldier to return home.  Over time, the meaning broadened to encompass any authorized leave of absence, particularly from work.
