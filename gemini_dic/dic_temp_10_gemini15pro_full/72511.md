flurried: /ˈflɜːrid/ (US), /ˈflʌrid/ (UK) | adj. | 慌乱的，慌张的 | agitated and confused; in a state of flurry |  She was flurried by the sudden news. 她被突如其来的消息弄得慌乱不堪。|  He gave a flurried explanation for his lateness. 他慌乱地解释了他迟到的原因。| 近义词: agitated, disconcerted, ruffled, flustered | 反义词: calm, composed, serene | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the noun "flurry," meaning a sudden gust of wind or a brief shower of snow, from earlier "flurt" (to flick or move quickly), of imitative origin.  The sense of being agitated or confused arose from the image of being blown about by a gust of wind.
