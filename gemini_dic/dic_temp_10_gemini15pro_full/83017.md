gunshots: /ˈɡʌnʃɒts/ (UK) /ˈɡʌnʃɑːts/ (US) | n. | 枪声，枪击声 | The sound of gunfire, usually multiple shots. |  We heard gunshots in the distance and immediately ducked for cover. 我们听到远处传来枪声，立即卧倒躲避。

The gunshots shattered the peaceful silence of the night. 枪声打破了夜晚宁静的寂静。 | 近义词： gunfire, shooting, reports | 反义词: silence, quiet | 词性: 名词

词源:  Compound of "gun" and "shot" (past participle of "shoot"). "Gun" comes from Middle English "gonne," probably of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse "gunnr," a woman's name, perhaps originally applied to a catapult.  "Shot" comes from Old English "gesceot," meaning "that which is shot," from "sceotan," meaning "to shoot."
