liddell: /ˈlɪdəl/ | n. | 利德尔 | A surname, derived from a place name in Northumberland, England, meaning "lime tree valley".  It is also associated with the Liddell family, particularly Henry George Liddell, a lexicographer known for his work on "A Greek-English Lexicon", and his daughter Alice Liddell, the inspiration for Lewis Carroll's "Alice's Adventures in Wonderland". |  Example: Henry Liddell was a renowned scholar.  亨利·利德尔是一位著名的学者。 | Example:  Alice Liddell inspired a classic children's story. 爱丽丝·利德尔启发了一个经典的儿童故事。 | Synonym: (For the surname itself, there are no true synonyms, but for meaning, consider) family name, last name | Antonym: (N/A for a proper noun) | Noun
