beautifying:/ˈbjuːtɪfaɪɪŋ/ | v. | 美化，装饰 |  Making something more beautiful; enhancing the appearance of something. |  She spent the afternoon beautifying her garden. 她一下午都在美化她的花园。| Synonyms: adorning, embellishing, enhancing, ornamenting | Antonyms: uglifying, disfiguring | verb

beautifying:/ˈbjuːtɪfaɪɪŋ/ | adj. | 美化的，装饰的 |  Used to describe something that makes something else more beautiful. | The beautifying effect of the new paint was immediately noticeable. 新油漆的美化效果立刻显现出来。| Synonyms:  decorative, ornamental, enhancing | Antonyms:  uglifying, disfiguring | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From beautify + -ing.  Beautify comes from  beauty + -fy. Beauty ultimately derives from the Old French "beauté," and "-fy" is a suffix meaning "to make" or "to cause to be."
