continental:/ˌkɑːntɪˈnɛntl/ | /ˌkɒntɪˈnɛntl/ | adj. n.|大陆的，大陆性的；大陆人|Forming or belonging to a continent; relating to or characteristic of a continent. |The continental breakfast was simple but satisfying.  大陆式早餐简单但令人满意。|continental drift 大陆漂移|Synonyms: mainland, landmass |Antonyms: insular, island |adjective, noun


continental:/ˌkɑːntɪˈnɛntl/ | /ˌkɒntɪˈnɛntl/ | n.|欧洲大陆人|A person from the continent of Europe. |He was a continental, not accustomed to the British way of life. 他是一位欧洲大陆人，不习惯英国的生活方式。|Synonyms: European |Antonyms: British, Islander |noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *continental*, from Late Latin *continentālis*, from Latin *continēns* ("holding together, continuous, adjacent") (present participle of *continēre*), from *con-* ("together") + *tenēre* ("to hold").
