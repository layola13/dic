gripeful: /ˈɡraɪpfʊl/ | adj. | 满腹牢骚的，抱怨的 | Full of gripes; complaining. |  He was gripeful about the long hours he had to work. 他抱怨他必须工作很长时间。|  She's always so gripeful, it's hard to be around her. 她总是满腹牢骚，很难和她相处。 | 近义词: complaining, querulous, whiny, grumpy | 反义词: content, satisfied, cheerful | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From *gripe* (noun and verb) + *-ful* (suffix forming adjectives meaning "full of").  *Gripe* comes from Old English *grīpan* "to grip, seize," related to Dutch *grijpen* and German *greifen*. The sense of "complain" developed from the notion of clutching or holding onto something tightly, as in pain or discomfort.
