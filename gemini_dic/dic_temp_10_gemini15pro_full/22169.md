bombastic:/bɒmˈbæstɪk/ | adj. |夸大的，言过其实的 | High-sounding but with little meaning; inflated. | His bombastic speech was full of long words, but it lacked substance. 他的夸夸其谈充满了长篇大论，但缺乏实质内容。|  The mayor's bombastic pronouncements impressed no one. 市长夸大的言论没有给任何人留下印象。|近义词：inflated, pompous, pretentious, overblown |反义词：understated, simple, modest, plain |词性:形容词

bombast:/ˈbɒmbæst/ | n. |夸大的言辞 | High-sounding language with little meaning; inflated or pretentious language. |  The critic dismissed the novel as mere bombast.  评论家认为这部小说只不过是夸夸其谈。| His bombast concealed a lack of real knowledge. 他的夸夸其谈掩盖了他缺乏真正的知识。|近义词：pomposity, grandiloquence, rhetoric |反义词：understatement, simplicity, modesty |词性:名词

词源：
From French *bombaste* "cotton padding, padding," from Italian *bombasino* "cotton," from Late Latin *bombax, bambax*, from Greek *bombyx* "silk, silkworm."  The sense evolution was from "padding" to "inflated language."  最初的意思是“棉絮，填充物”，后来引申为“浮夸的语言”。
