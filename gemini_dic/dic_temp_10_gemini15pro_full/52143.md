diaphane:/ˈdaɪəfeɪn/| adj.|透明的，半透明的|Allowing light to pass through diffusely; translucent.  Characterized by such fineness of texture as to permit seeing through; delicate and sheer.|The artist used diaphane to create a stained-glass effect.|Synonyms: translucent, sheer, transparent, pellucid|Antonyms: opaque, cloudy, obscure|Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *diaphane*, from Latin *diaphanus*, from Greek *diaphanēs* "transparent," from *dia- "through" + *phainein* "to show."
