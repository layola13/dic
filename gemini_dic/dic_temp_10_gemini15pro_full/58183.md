drowses:/ˈdraʊziz/
| v. | 打瞌睡，昏昏欲睡 | to sleep lightly; doze |  He often drowses in front of the television. 他经常在电视机前打瞌睡。 | synonyms: dozes, nods off, slumbers | antonyms: awakens, arouses, energizes | verb (third-person singular simple present drowses, present participle drowsing, simple past drowsed, past participle drowsed)

drowse: /draʊz/
| n. | 瞌睡，昏睡 | a state of sleeping lightly | He fell into a drowse after lunch.  午饭后他陷入了瞌睡。 | synonyms: doze, nap, slumber | antonyms: wakefulness, alertness, vigilance | noun

drowsy: /ˈdraʊzi/
| adj. | 困倦的，昏昏欲睡的 | sleepy and lethargic | The warm room made me feel drowsy. 温暖的房间让我感到昏昏欲睡。 | synonyms: sleepy, lethargic, somnolent | antonyms: alert, awake, energetic | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *drūsian, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Old Norse drȳgja (“to make heavy, dull”).
