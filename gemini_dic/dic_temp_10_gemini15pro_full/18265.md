belisarius:/ˌbɛlɪˈsɛəriəs/| n. |贝利撒留|A famous Byzantine general of the 6th century AD, known for his military skill and loyalty to Emperor Justinian I, despite facing accusations and disgrace.|Belisarius was a brilliant military strategist. 贝利撒留是一位杰出的军事战略家。|Synonym: general, commander, strategist|Antonym: civilian, pacifist|Noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin Belisarius, from Late Latin Belisarius, likely of ultimately Thracian or Illyrian origin.
