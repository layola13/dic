agitation:/ˌædʒɪˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 激动，不安，焦虑；鼓动，煽动 |A state of anxiety or nervous excitement. | She was in a state of great agitation. 她处于极度焦虑的状态。| The speaker's fiery words caused agitation in the crowd. 演讲者激昂的言辞引起了人群的骚动。 | anxiety, nervousness, distress, restlessness, unease | calmness, tranquility, peace, composure, serenity | noun


agitate:/ˈædʒɪteɪt/ | v. | 使激动，使不安；搅动，摇动；鼓动，煽动  | To make someone worried or upset, or to make something move violently. | The news agitated her. 这消息让她很不安。 |  He agitated the mixture with a spoon. 他用勺子搅拌混合物。|  The group was agitating for political reform.  该团体正在鼓动政治改革。| disturb, upset, worry, bother, fluster | soothe, calm, pacify, tranquilize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *agitationem, from Latin agitatus, past participle of agitare "to drive, move, shake, stir up," frequentative of agere "to drive, lead, act, do."
