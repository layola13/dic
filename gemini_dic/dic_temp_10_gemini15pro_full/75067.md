frightless:/ˈfraɪtləs/| adj.|无畏的，不怕的|Not feeling or showing fright; fearless.|The frightless child walked right up to the big dog.  这个无所畏惧的孩子径直走向了那条大狗。|近义词：fearless, undaunted, intrepid|反义词：fearful, timid, apprehensive|词性:形容词
Etymology: fright + -less.  From fright (noun, meaning "sudden fear") and the suffix -less (meaning "without").  Ultimately from Middle English *fryghten, from Old English *fyrhtan, causative of *forhtian "to frighten," from Proto-Germanic *furkhtijanan, from *furkhtuz "frightened."
