Etymology:
From Middle English *maken*, from Old English *macian* (to make, do, perform, prepare, build, compose, create, cause), from Proto-Germanic *makōną* (to make, fit, prepare), from Proto-Indo-European *meǵh₂-* (to be able, have power).  Cognates include German *machen*, Dutch *maken*, and Swedish *maka*.
