dumpling:/ˈdʌmplɪŋ/ | n. |饺子；汤圆；团子|A small ball of dough (= flour and water mixed together) cooked and eaten, often with meat and vegetables inside. |例句：She made delicious pork dumplings for dinner. 她晚饭做了美味的猪肉饺子。

例句：I love eating steamed dumplings with soy sauce. 我喜欢吃蘸酱油的蒸饺。|近义词：potsticker, ravioli, pierogi |反义词: None |词性: noun

dumpling:/ˈdʌmplɪŋ/ | n. |矮胖的人；矮胖的动物|A short, fat person or animal. |例句：He called his chubby little nephew a dumpling. 他叫他胖乎乎的小侄子小胖墩。

例句：The little hamster looked like a furry dumpling.  这只小仓鼠看起来像一个毛茸茸的汤圆。|近义词：chubby, plump |反义词: skinny, thin |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dumplyng, *dompling, possibly from German *Pfannkuchen (“pancake”), or imitative of the sound of a dumpling dropped into liquid.  The word suggests something small and thick.
