ensnarer:/ɪnˈsnɛərər/
n.|诱捕者；陷害者|Someone or something that ensnares.  A person or thing that traps or entangles someone in a difficult situation.|The spider is an ensnarer of insects. 蜘蛛是昆虫的诱捕者。|近义词：trapper, entangler|反义词: liberator, rescuer|词性:名词

Etymology:
From en- + snare + -er.  "En-" acts as a prefix meaning "to cause to be in" or "to put into." "Snare" comes from the Old English word "sneru" meaning "noose" or "trap."  "-er" is a suffix denoting an agent or one who performs an action.
