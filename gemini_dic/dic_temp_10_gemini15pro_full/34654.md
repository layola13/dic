clerestories:/ˈklɪəˌstɔːriz/ (US), /ˈklɪəˌstɔːrɪz/ (UK)| n.|天窗，高侧窗|A window, or series of windows, in the upper part of a wall, or in the roof of a building, especially a church, designed to admit light.|The clerestories in the cathedral allowed sunlight to stream in, illuminating the intricate stained glass windows.|Synonyms: high windows, upper windows, skylights|Antonyms: low windows, base windows|noun
