matricentricity:/ˌmeɪtrɪˈsɛntrɪsɪti/ | n. | 母系中心; 母权制 | The dominance or over-importance of mothers in a society or group. It refers to a social system where mothers are the head of the family and have primary responsibility for raising children and making decisions. |  The anthropologist's research focused on the matricentricity of the Minangkabau culture in Indonesia.  这位人类学家对印度尼西亚米南佳保文化的母系中心制进行了研究。

The family exhibited strong matricentricity, with the grandmother holding a position of significant authority.  这个家庭展现出强烈的母系中心特征，祖母拥有重要的权威地位。 | 近义词：matriarchy, matriarchal system | 反义词：patricentricity, patriarchal system | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "mater" (mother) + "centric" (centered) + "-ity" (forming abstract nouns).
