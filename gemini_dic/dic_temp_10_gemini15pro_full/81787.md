grenade:/ɡrəˈneɪd/ | n. | 手榴弹 | A small bomb thrown by hand or launched mechanically. | The soldier pulled the pin and threw the grenade. 士兵拔掉保险销，扔出了手榴弹。| Synonyms: bomb, explosive | Antonyms: peacekeeper, dove | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French "grenade" (pomegranate), because early grenades resembled pomegranates in shape and size, ultimately from Latin "granatum" (having many seeds).
