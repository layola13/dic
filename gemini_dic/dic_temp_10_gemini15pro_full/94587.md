impracticably:/ɪmˈpræktikəbli/
| adv. |不切实际地，无法实行地|In such a way that it is impossible to do or put into practice.|The plan was impracticably expensive.  这个计划贵得无法实行。|
|近义词：unfeasibly, impossibly, unrealistically|反义词：practically, feasibly, realistically|词性:副词


Etymology:
From Late Middle English *inpracticable, from Medieval Latin impracticābilis, from Latin in- “not” + practicābilis “practicable”.  Surface analysis:  im- (not) + practicable (-able, capable of being put into practice) + -ly (adverbial suffix).
