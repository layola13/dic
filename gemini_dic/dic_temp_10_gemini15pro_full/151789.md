pass the Rubicon: /ˌpæs ðə ˈruːbɪkɒn/ (British), /ˌpæs ðə ˈruːbɪkɑːn/ (American)
|idiomatic phrase| 渡过卢比孔河；破釜沉舟；义无反顾|To take a decisive, irrevocable step, often involving some danger.|Caesar's crossing of the Rubicon River in 42 BC marked the beginning of civil war in Rome.  Hence, "passing the Rubicon" signifies a point of no return. |The company president passed the Rubicon when he decided to invest heavily in the new technology, despite the risks involved.|Synonyms: cross the Rubicon, take the plunge, burn one's bridges, point of no return|Antonyms: hesitate, waver, vacillate|phrase
