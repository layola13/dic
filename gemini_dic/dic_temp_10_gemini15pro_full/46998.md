/dæʃt/ | adj. | 划破的；虚线的；匆忙的；突击的 | marked with dashes; done suddenly and hastily |
The dashed line on the map represents the border. 地图上的虚线代表边界。
He made a dashed attempt to escape. 他仓促地试图逃跑。
| synonyms: broken, hurried, rushed | antonyms: continuous, leisurely, unhurried | adjective


/dæʃt/ | n. | 破折号；少量（尤指液体）；冲撞；短跑 | a punctuation mark (—); a small amount of something, especially liquid; a sudden, rapid movement; a short, fast race |
Add a dash of lemon juice to the sauce.  在酱汁里加一点柠檬汁。
He made a dash for the door. 他冲向门口。
| synonyms: sprint, dash, drop, sprinkle | antonyms:  prolongation, crawl, stagnation | noun

/dæʃt/ | v. | 猛冲；撞击；泼溅；使…破灭 | to run or move quickly and suddenly; to throw or splash something; to destroy or frustrate |
The waves dashed against the rocks.  海浪猛烈地拍打着岩石。
Her hopes were dashed when she heard the news.  听到这个消息后，她的希望破灭了。
| synonyms: rush, bolt, fling, shatter | antonyms:  crawl, creep, build, construct | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dascen (unattested), probably a variant of dascian "to strike, beat, thrash," related to Danish daske "to slap." The sense development is likely from striking something and causing it to break or scatter.
