bondability:/ˌbɒndəˈbɪləti/ /ˌbɑːndəˈbɪləti/| n.|可担保性，可保释性|The quality or state of being bondable; suitability for being bonded, especially in a financial context.  This often refers to a person's or company's creditworthiness and trustworthiness in handling financial obligations.|The contractor's bondability was a key factor in awarding the project.  承包商的可担保性是授予该项目的一个关键因素。|近义词：creditworthiness, reliability, trustworthiness|反义词：unreliability, untrustworthiness|词性:名词

词源:
From "bondable" + "-ity".  "Bondable" comes from "bond" (meaning to secure a debt or obligation with a bond) + "-able" (suffix meaning "capable of being").  "Bond" ultimately derives from Old English "bindan," meaning "to tie, bind."
