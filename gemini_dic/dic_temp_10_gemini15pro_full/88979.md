holinesses:/ˈhoʊlinɪsɪz/
noun
神圣性；圣洁
The state or quality of being holy.  A plural form of "holiness."
例句：The holinesses of the saints inspire awe. 圣徒的神圣性令人敬畏。

例句：He spoke of the holinesses of different religious traditions. 他谈到了不同宗教传统的神圣性。

近义词：sanctities, sacredness, piety
反义词：profanity, impiety, wickedness
词性:n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *holynesses*, plural of *holynesse*, from Old English *hālignes* (“holiness, sanctity”), equivalent to *hālīg* (“holy”) + *-nes* (suffix forming abstract nouns denoting state or quality). Cognate with Old Frisian *hēlichnesse*, *hēlignes* (“holiness”), Old Saxon *hēligīnissa* (“holiness”), Middle Dutch *heilignesse* (“holiness”), Old High German *heiligīnissa* (“holiness”), German *Heiligkeit* (“holiness”). More at holy, -ness.
