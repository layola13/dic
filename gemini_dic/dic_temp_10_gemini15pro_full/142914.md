out-searching:/ˌaʊtˈsɜːrtʃɪŋ/
|adj.|胜过搜索的，搜索更彻底的|Surpassing others in searching; searching more thoroughly than others.|例句：Her out-searching efforts led to the discovery of the lost artifact. 她锲而不舍的搜索最终找到了丢失的文物。|例句：His out-searching gaze scanned the horizon. 他锐利的目光扫视着地平线。|近义词：thorough, exhaustive, meticulous|反义词：cursory, superficial|词性:形容词

Etymology:
"out-" (prefix): Old English *ūt, from Proto-Germanic *ūt, meaning "out, outside, outwards."  Indicates exceeding or surpassing.
"search": From Old French "cerchier" meaning "to search, explore, seek." Ultimately from Late Latin "circare"  meaning "to go around, wander."
"-ing": Present participle suffix, forming a verb or adjective.
