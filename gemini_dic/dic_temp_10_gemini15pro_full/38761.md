condoners:/kənˈdoʊnərz/| n.| 宽恕者；容忍者；原谅者 |  People who condone something, especially something bad or wrong.  They forgive or overlook an offense, usually a serious one. |  The condoners of bullying create an unsafe environment for everyone. 容忍欺凌行为的人为每个人都创造了一个不安全的环境。| The condoners of his criminal behavior are equally culpable.  宽恕他犯罪行为的人同样有罪。 |  approvers, excusers, tolerators | critics, objectors, dissenters | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "condone," which comes from the Latin "condonare," meaning "to give completely, to pardon."  "Con-" (completely) + "donare" (to give, to present).
