abrogating:/ˈæbrəɡeɪtɪŋ/
verb
废除；取消
The government abrogated the treaty. 政府废除了条约。
例句：
The company is abrogating its contract with the supplier. = 这家公司正在废除与供应商的合同。

The king abrogated the law. = 国王废除了这条法律。

近义词：repealing, revoking, annulling, rescinding
反义词：enacting, establishing, upholding, ratifying
词性:v.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *abrogare*, from *ab-* (“away from”) + *rogare* (“to ask, propose a law”).  The original sense was of repealing a law by popular vote.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The law abrogates certain rights. = 该法律废除某些权利。
He abrogates his responsibilities. = 他放弃自己的责任。

一般过去时:
The treaty was abrogated last year. = 该条约于去年被废除。
They abrogated the agreement. = 他们废除了协议。

现在完成时:
The government has abrogated the old law. = 政府已经废除了旧法律。
They have abrogated their contract with us. = 他们已经废除了与我们的合同。

过去完成时:
The king had abrogated the law before the revolution. = 国王在革命前就废除了法律。
They had abrogated the treaty before the war broke out. = 他们在战争爆发前就废除了条约。

将来时:
The council will abrogate the bylaw next month. = 理事会下个月将废除该细则。
They will abrogate the contract if the conditions are not met. = 如果条件不满足，他们将废除合同。

被动语态:
The law was abrogated by the parliament. = 该法律被议会废除。
The agreement has been abrogated. = 协议已被废除。
