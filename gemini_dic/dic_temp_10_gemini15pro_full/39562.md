conscionableness:/ˈkɒnʃənəblənəs/ /ˈkɑːnʃənəblənəs/| n. |良心；公正；合理|The conscionableness of the contract was questioned by the judge.  法官质疑合同的公正性。|Etymologically, "conscionableness" comes from the Late Middle English word "consciencious," meaning "governed by conscience." This, in turn, derives from the Old French "consciencieux" and ultimately from the Latin "conscientia," meaning "knowledge within oneself," from "com-" (with) + "scire" (to know).|例句：The conscionableness of his actions was debated.  他的行为是否合理受到了争议。

The lawyer argued the conscionableness of the terms of the loan. 律师辩称贷款条款是合理的。|近义词：fairness, justice, reasonableness, equitableness|反义词：unfairness, injustice, unreasonableness|词性:noun
