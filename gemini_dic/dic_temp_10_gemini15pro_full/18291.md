bail-out:/ˈbeɪlˌaʊt/| n.|紧急财政援助，救助；跳伞；保释|An act of giving financial assistance to a failing business or economy to save it from collapse.  The pilot had to bail out of the plane when the engine failed.|例句：The government provided a bailout to the struggling airline. 政府向陷入困境的航空公司提供了紧急援助。

例句：The company received a bailout from the government. 这家公司获得了政府的救助。|近义词：rescue, lifeline, bailout package|反义词: refusal, denial|noun

bail out:/beɪl ˈaʊt/| v.|保释（某人）; 帮助（某人）摆脱困境；跳伞；舀出|To rescue someone from a difficult situation, often financial. To jump out of an aircraft with a parachute.|例句：The government bailed out the failing bank. 政府救助了这家濒临倒闭的银行。

例句：He had to bail out of the boat when it started sinking.  他不得不在船开始下沉时跳船。|近义词：rescue, save, assist|反义词: abandon, desert|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
"bail" (桶) 源于古法语 "baille," 最终来自拉丁语 "bajula" （搬运）。 "bail out" 最初指从船中舀水，后来引申为帮助某人摆脱困境，尤其指金融困境，以及跳伞逃生。
