eugenic:/juːˈdʒɛnɪk/
adjective
优生的；优生学的
relating to or fitted for the production of good offspring through controlled breeding
例句：Eugenic policies were popular in the early 20th century.  优生政策在20世纪早期很流行。

例句：Some people believe that eugenic practices are unethical.  有些人认为优生措施是不道德的。
近义词：selective breeding, genetic engineering
反义词：dysgenic
词性：adjective


/juːˈdʒɛnɪks/
noun
优生学
the study of or belief in the possibility of improving the qualities of the human species or a human population, especially by such means as discouraging reproduction by persons having genetic defects or presumed to have inheritable undesirable traits (negative eugenics) or encouraging reproduction by persons presumed to have inheritable desirable traits (positive eugenics)
例句：The history of eugenics is a dark chapter in the 20th century. 优生学史是20世纪黑暗的一章。

例句：He studied the controversial science of eugenics. 他研究了备受争议的优生学。
近义词：genetics, heredity
反义词：N/A
词性：noun
