concretive:/ˈkɒŋkrɪtɪv/ /kɑːnˈkriːtɪv/ | adj. |  使具体的，使凝固的 |  Tending to unite or solidify; causing concretion.  |  The concretive properties of cement are essential for building. 水泥的凝固性对于建筑至关重要。 |  coagulative, solidifying, binding  |  dissolving, dispersive | adjective

concretion:/kɒŋˈkriːʃ(ə)n/ /kɑːnˈkriːʃn/ | n. | 凝结; 凝结物; 结石 | The act or process of becoming concrete, solid, or coalesced; a hard solid mass formed by the local accumulation of matter, especially within the body or within a mass of loose material such as soil or rock. | The kidney stones were a painful concretion. 肾结石是一种痛苦的凝结物。| coagulation, solidification, mass | dissolution, dispersion | noun
