emacerate:/ɪˈmeɪsɪəreɪt/ /ɪˈmæsəreɪt/| v. |使消瘦；使衰弱；使憔悴|to make someone thin and weak, especially because of illness or lack of food|The long illness had emaciated him.  长期疾病使他消瘦了。|He was emaciated by the fever. 他因发烧而变得消瘦。|synonyms: weaken, debilitate, enfeeble, attenuate|antonyms: strengthen, invigorate, fortify|verb

emacerate:/ɪˈmeɪsɪəreɪt/ /ɪˈmæsəreɪt/| adj. |消瘦的；衰弱的；憔悴的|(of a person or animal) abnormally thin or weak, especially because of illness or lack of food|He appeared pale and emaciated. 他显得面色苍白，形容枯槁。|The emaciated refugees were given food and medical care.  消瘦的难民得到了食物和医疗照顾。|synonyms: thin, gaunt, skeletal, haggard|antonyms: plump, robust, stout|adjective
