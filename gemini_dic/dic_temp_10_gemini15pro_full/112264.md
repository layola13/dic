loper:/ˈloʊpər/| n.| 闲荡者；游荡者；(尤指)狼；骗子|Someone who lopes; a person who runs at a slow, easy pace, especially over long distances. Also, can refer to a predatory animal, especially a wolf, or a swindler.| The loping gait of the wolf made it easy to track in the snow.  狼闲散的步态使它在雪地里很容易被追踪。|近义词：wanderer, roamer, stroller, wolf, swindler|反义词:worker, resident|词性:noun


lopers:/ˈloʊpərz/| n.| 闲荡者们；游荡者们；(尤指)狼群；骗子们|Plural form of "loper." Refers to a group of individuals who lope. Can also refer to a pack of wolves or a group of swindlers.|  The lopers were seen near the outskirts of town.  有人看到那些游荡者在城郊附近。|近义词：wanderers, roamers, strollers, wolves, swindlers|反义词:workers, residents|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "lope" + "-er."  "Lope" comes from Middle English *lopen*, of Scandinavian origin; related to Old Norse *hlaupa* "to run, leap," from Proto-Germanic *hlaupaną*. The "-er" suffix is used to form agent nouns, indicating someone or something that performs the action of the verb.
