compensated:/ˈkɒmpənseɪtɪd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmpənseɪtɪd/ (US)| adj.|已补偿的，已赔偿的|Having been paid or given something in return for a loss, service, or injury.|例句：He was fully compensated for his injuries. 他因受伤获得了全额赔偿。|近义词：reimbursed, indemnified, paid, recompensed|反义词：uncompensated, unpaid|词性:形容词

compensate:/ˈkɒmpənseɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmpənseɪt/ (US)| v.|补偿，赔偿|To give something to someone in recognition of loss, suffering, or injury; to make up for something unwelcome or unpleasant by exerting an opposite force or effect.|例句：Nothing can compensate for the loss of a child. 没有什么能弥补失去孩子的痛苦。|近义词：make up for, atone for, make amends for, offset|反义词：fail to compensate, forfeit|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin compensatus, past participle of compensare "to weigh together, counterbalance," from com- "together" + pensare "to weigh."
