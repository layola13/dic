bridle:/ˈbraɪdl/ (US), /ˈbraɪd(ə)l/ (UK)| n. v. |马勒；约束；控制|A piece of equipment used to control a horse, consisting of straps fitted around the horse's head. Also used figuratively to mean restraining or controlling something.|
The rider adjusted the bridle before mounting the horse. 骑手上马前调整了马勒。

He bridled his anger and spoke calmly. 他抑制住怒气，平静地说话。

|近义词:harness, restrain, curb|反义词:release, unleash|词性:noun, verb

bridle:/ˈbraɪdl/ (US), /ˈbraɪd(ə)l/ (UK)| v. |昂首表示不满；动怒；生气|To show resentment or anger, often by tossing the head.|She bridled at the suggestion that she wasn't qualified. 她对认为她不够格的建议表示愤慨。

He bridled at the implication that he was lying. 他对暗示他在撒谎感到愤怒。

|近义词:resent, take offense, bristle|反义词:submit, acquiesce|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *brīdels, from Proto-Germanic *bridlaz, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *bʰrīd-. Cognate with Old High German brittil "bridle," Old Norse bitill.
