follow-on:/ˈfɒləʊɒn/ (UK) /ˈfɑːloʊɑːn/ (US)| adj. n.|后续的，接下来的；后续行动，后续事件|Something that is a follow-on happens or is done after something else and is connected with it. |The band had a hugely successful follow-on album.  这支乐队后续的专辑获得了巨大的成功。|
follow-on:/ˈfɒləʊɒn/ (UK) /ˈfɑːloʊɑːn/ (US)| adj. n.|后续的，接下来的；后续行动，后续事件|The conference is a follow-on from the meeting last week.  这次会议是上周会议的后续。|近义词：subsequent, succeeding, ensuing|反义词：initial, preceding, prior|词性: adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrasal verb "follow on," meaning to continue after something else.  "Follow" comes from Old English "folgian," and "on" is also from Old English "on," meaning "on," "in," or "upon."
