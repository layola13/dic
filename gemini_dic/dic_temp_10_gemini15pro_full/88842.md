hogringer:/ˈhɒɡˌrɪŋər/ (US) /ˈhɒɡˌrɪŋə(r)/ (UK)| n.|猪圈; 猪栏; 养猪户; (尤指)贪吃的人|A place where hogs are kept; a person who raises hogs; a gluttonous person, especially one who eats a lot of pork.|例句：The farmer led the hogs to the hogringer. 农民把猪赶到猪圈。|例句：He ate like a hogringer at the buffet. 他在自助餐上吃得像个饿死鬼。|近义词：pigsty, pigpen, glutton, gourmand|反义词: (对于养猪户) none; (对于贪吃的人) ascetic|词性:名词

词源: hog + ringer.  "Hog" refers to a pig, and "ringer" in this context likely refers to an enclosure or a person who rings or rounds up hogs.  The word's meaning as "glutton" is a figurative extension, comparing a person's voracious appetite to that of a pig.
