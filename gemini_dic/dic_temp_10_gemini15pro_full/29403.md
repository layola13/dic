cataclysm: /ˈkætəklɪzəm/ | n. | 大灾难；剧变；灾变 | A cataclysm is a sudden, violent, and destructive event, often a natural disaster. | The earthquake was a cataclysm that destroyed entire cities. 地震是一场毁灭了整个城市的大灾难。 | The political upheaval led to a cataclysm in the country's economy. 政治动荡导致了国家经济的灾难。| 近义词: disaster, catastrophe, upheaval, calamity | 反义词: boon, blessing | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cataclysme*, from Late Latin *cataclysmus*, from Ancient Greek *κατακλυσμός (kataklusmós)* meaning "deluge, flood", from *κατα- (kata-)*  "down" + *κλύζω (kluzō)* "to wash, dash over".
