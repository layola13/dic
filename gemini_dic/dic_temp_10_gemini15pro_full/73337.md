forefathers:/ˈfɔːrˌfɑːðərz/ (US) /ˈfɔːˌfɑːðəz/ (UK)| n. |祖先，先辈|People from whom one is descended; ancestors, especially remote ancestors.|Our forefathers fought for the freedoms we enjoy today. 我们的祖先为我们今天享有的自由而战。|
forefathers:/ˈfɔːrˌfɑːðərz/ (US) /ˈfɔːˌfɑːðəz/ (UK)| n. |祖先，先辈|Those who came before and established something, like a tradition or institution.|The forefathers of modern medicine made remarkable discoveries. 现代医学的先驱们取得了非凡的发现。|
forefathers:/ˈfɔːrˌfɑːðərz/ (US) /ˈfɔːˌfɑːðəz/ (UK)| n. |祖先，先辈|  The founders of a movement or a country.|The forefathers of this nation envisioned a land of opportunity. 这个国家的先辈们设想了一个充满机会的土地。|
forefathers:/ˈfɔːrˌfɑːðərz/ (US) /ˈfɔːˌfɑːðəz/ (UK)| n. |祖先，先辈|  (Figurative)  Pioneers or originators in a particular field.|The forefathers of rock and roll influenced generations of musicians. 摇滚乐的先驱影响了几代音乐家。|

forefathers:|近义词: ancestors, forebears, progenitors|反义词: descendants, offspring, posterity|词性: 名词|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *forefæder (attested as *fore-fǣderas), from fore- "before" + fæder "father".  Cognate with Dutch voorvader, German Vorvater.
