nimrod:/ˈnɪmrɒd/ (US) /ˈnɪmrɔːd/ (UK)| n.|猎手；暴君；白痴|A biblical figure described as a mighty hunter. Now used ironically or humorously to refer to a foolish or inept person.|He called the politician a nimrod for his disastrous policies. 他称这位政治家为笨蛋，因为他推行了灾难性的政策。|Synonyms: hunter, huntsman (archaic), fool, idiot, dunce|Antonyms: genius, intellectual|noun

Etymology: From the biblical figure Nimrod (Genesis 10:8-9), described as "a mighty hunter before the Lord." The meaning shifted to "fool" or "idiot" in the 19th century, possibly due to a misinterpretation of the biblical text.
