crare: /kreɪr/
n.|小艇，驳船|A small, flat-bottomed boat used for transport, especially along rivers or coasts.|The fisherman used a crare to transport his catch to the market. 渔夫用小艇把他的渔获运到市场。|Example: The crare was loaded with goods and ready to sail. 这艘驳船装满了货物，准备起航。|Synonyms: barge, lighter, scow|Antonyms: ship, vessel|noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *crāre*, from Old North French *crarie, crere* (compare Modern French *crarier*), from Middle Dutch *craiere*, from *kraie* (“crow”), from the bird's resemblance to the prow of the boat. Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *krajō* (“crow”).
