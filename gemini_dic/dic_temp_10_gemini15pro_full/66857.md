expectationist: /ɛkspɛkˈteɪʃənɪst/
n.
期望主义者;抱有极高期望的人
Someone who has very high expectations, often unrealistically so.
He's such an expectationist; he's always disappointed when things don't go perfectly. 他就是一个期望主义者；当事情没有完美进行时，他总是很失望。
近义词: optimist, idealist
反义词: pessimist, cynic
词性: noun


Etymology:
From "expectation" + "-ist" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person who practices or believes in a particular thing).  "Expectation" comes from Late Middle English: from late Latin expectatio(n-), from the verb expectare ‘await’.
