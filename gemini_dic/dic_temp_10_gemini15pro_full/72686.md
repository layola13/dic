fobbing:/ˈfɒbɪŋ/ (British) /ˈfɑːbɪŋ/ (American)| v. |欺骗；搪塞；用劣质货冒充；(把某人/某物)打发走|deceiving or tricking someone, often by giving them something inferior to what they expect; dismissing someone or something casually or lightly|He tried fobbing me off with some excuse about the train being late. 他试图用火车晚点的借口搪塞我。|Synonyms: deceiving, cheating, tricking, palming off, defrauding |Antonyms:  being honest, being truthful|verb
fob:/fɒb/ (British) /fɑːb/ (American)| n. |表袋；短表链；钥匙扣；骗子|1. A small pocket for a watch, especially in the waistband of trousers. 2. A chain attached to a watch for carrying in a fob pocket. 3. A key chain or ring. 4. (informal) A swindler or cheat.|He kept his watch in his fob. 他把表放在表袋里。|Synonyms: (for sense 4) con artist, swindler, cheat, fraudster |Antonyms: (for sense 4) honest person|noun
