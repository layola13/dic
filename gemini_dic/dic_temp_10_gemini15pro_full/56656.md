dons:/dɒnz/ (British), /dɑːnz/ (American)| v. (third-person singular simple present *dons*, present participle *donning*, simple past and past participle *donned*)| 穿上，戴上 | To put on (an item of clothing).| He donned his coat and hat and went out. 他穿上外套，戴上帽子，出去了。|  She donned a beautiful gown for the ball. 她为舞会穿上一件漂亮的礼服。 | synonyms: put on, wear, assume | antonyms: doff, take off, remove | verb

dons:/dɒnz/ (British), /dɑːnz/ (American)| n. (plural)| 大学老师，导师，尤其指牛津大学和剑桥大学的 | A university teacher, especially a senior member of a college at Oxford or Cambridge.| Several dons attended the graduation ceremony. 几位大学老师出席了毕业典礼。| The dons at Cambridge are renowned for their scholarship. 剑桥大学的导师以其学识而闻名。| synonyms: professor, lecturer, tutor, fellow | antonyms: student, pupil | noun
