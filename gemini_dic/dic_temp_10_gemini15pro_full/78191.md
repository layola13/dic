geosynclinal:/ˌdʒiːoʊˈsɪŋklɪnəl/
adjective|地槽的|Relating to a geosyncline, a large-scale, linear trough in the Earth's crust that subsides over a long period and accumulates a thick sequence of sedimentary and volcanic rocks.|The geosynclinal theory was once popular in geology. 地槽理论曾经在地质学中很流行。
noun|地槽|A large-scale, linear trough in the Earth's crust that subsides over a long period and accumulates a thick sequence of sedimentary and volcanic rocks. |The formation of mountains is often associated with geosynclinals. 山脉的形成通常与地槽有关。
近义词:basin, trough
反义词:(No direct antonym, could consider 'craton' for a stable part of the crust)
词性: adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From geo- (earth) + syn- (together) + -cline (slope, lean).  The term was coined by the American geologists James Dwight Dana and James Hall in the mid-19th century.
