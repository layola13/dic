词源:
From misleading + -ness.  "Misleading" comes from the Old English prefix "mis-" (meaning "wrongly" or "badly") + "leading" (from "lead," meaning "to guide").  The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
