intermeasurable:/ˌɪntərˈmɛʒərəbl/
adjective
可互相测量的，可以比较的
Able to be measured against each other; commensurable.  Two quantities are intermeasurable if each can be expressed as a multiple of the other.
The two lengths are intermeasurable, meaning one can be expressed as a multiple of the other. 这两段长度是可以互相测量的，意味着一个可以表示为另一个的倍数。
The territories of the two tribes were intermeasurable.  这两个部落的领土是可以互相测量的。

近义词:commensurable, comparable
反义词:incommensurable, incomparable
词性:adjective

词源:
From inter- + measurable.  "Inter-" is a prefix meaning "between" or "mutually." "Measurable" comes from the Late Middle English "mesurable," from Old French "mesurable,"  ultimately from Latin "mensurabilis," from "mensurare" ("to measure").
