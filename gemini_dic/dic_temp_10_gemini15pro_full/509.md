abolishes:/əˈbɒlɪʃɪz/
verb
废除，取消
puts an end to a system, practice, or institution
The government abolished the death penalty. 政府废除了死刑。

The company abolished its traditional performance review system. 公司废除了传统的绩效考核制度。

近义词
eliminate, eradicate, extinguish, terminate, remove, end, annihilate, destroy, nullify, revoke, repeal, cancel, invalidate, discontinue
反义词
establish, institute, introduce, create, reinstate, restore
词性
verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new law abolishes several outdated regulations. = 新法律废除了几项过时的规章制度。
This company abolishes mandatory overtime on weekends. = 这家公司取消了周末强制加班。

一般过去时:
The government abolished slavery in 1865. = 政府在1865年废除了奴隶制。
They abolished the old system last year. = 他们去年废除了旧制度。

现在完成时:
The country has abolished all forms of corporal punishment. = 该国已经废除了所有形式的体罚。
They have abolished the unpopular tax. = 他们已经取消了不受欢迎的税收。

过去完成时:
By the time I arrived, they had already abolished the rule. = 在我到达之前，他们已经废除了这条规则。
The company had abolished the policy before the complaints arose. = 在投诉出现之前，公司已经取消了这项政策。

将来时:
The committee will abolish the outdated practice next month. = 委员会下个月将废除过时的做法。
They will abolish this restriction soon. = 他们很快就会取消这项限制。

被动语态:
Capital punishment was abolished in this country many years ago. = 这个国家多年前就废除了死刑。
The outdated policy will be abolished next year. = 这项过时的政策将于明年被废除。


PHRASE->

词源
From Middle English *abolisshen*, from Old French *aboliss-,* present participle stem of *abolir*, from Latin *abolēre* (“to destroy, abolish”), from *ab-* (“from, away from”) + *olēre* (“to grow”), which is of uncertain origin.
