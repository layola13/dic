emancipate: /ɪˈmænsɪpeɪt/ | v. | 解放，使摆脱 | to free someone from slavery or other legal restrictions |  Abraham Lincoln emancipated the slaves. 亚伯拉罕·林肯解放了奴隶。| synonyms: liberate, free, release | antonyms: enslave, imprison, confine | verb

emancipates: /ɪˈmænsɪpeɪts/ | v. | 解放，使摆脱(第三人称单数形式) |  to free someone from slavery or other legal restrictions | The new law emancipates young people from outdated social norms.  这项新法律将年轻人从过时的社会规范中解放出来。 | synonyms: liberates, frees, releases | antonyms: enslaves, imprisons, confines | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin emancipatus, past participle of emancipare "to transfer ownership of," from ex- "out" + mancipare "to transfer formally," from manus "hand" + capere "to take."
