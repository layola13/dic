equivoke: /ɪˈkwɪvəˌvoʊk/ (US), /ɪˈkwɪvəʊk/ (UK) | v. n. | 含糊其辞；模棱两可的话 | to use ambiguous language so as to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself; a statement or expression capable of two interpretations, one usually risqué |  He equivocated when asked about his involvement in the scandal. 他在被问及卷入丑闻时含糊其辞。| synonyms: prevaricate, hedge, evade | antonyms: articulate, clarify, be forthright | verb

equivoke: /ˈɛkwɪˌvəʊk/ (US), /ˈɛkwɪˌvəʊk/ (UK)| n. | 模棱两可的话 | an ambiguous expression or statement | Her answer was a clever equivoke that allowed her to avoid the question. 她的回答是一个巧妙的模棱两可的话，让她得以回避这个问题。 | synonyms: ambiguity, double entendre, pun | antonyms: clarity, precision, directness | noun
