gaping:/ˈɡeɪpɪŋ/| adj.|张开的，裂开的；目瞪口呆的|Wide open, as a hole or wound; staring in amazement.|The earthquake left a gaping hole in the ground. 地震在地面上留下了一个大洞。|近义词：wide, open, yawning, vast, enormous|反义词：closed, shut, narrow, small, tiny|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Middle English gapen, from Old Norse gapa (“to gape, yawn”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *gapōną (“to gape, yawn”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰeh₂p- (“to yawn, gape”).
