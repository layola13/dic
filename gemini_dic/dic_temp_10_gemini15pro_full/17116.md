batten-down-the-hatches:/ˈbætən daʊn ðə ˈhætʃɪz/| idiom (v.)|准备迎接困难；严阵以待|To prepare for a difficult situation, especially a storm or other potential trouble. Originally a nautical term referring to securing hatches on a ship before a storm.|Example:  "Knowing a recession was coming, the company battened down the hatches and cut all unnecessary spending."  知道经济衰退即将来临，公司严阵以待，削减了所有不必要的开支。|Example: "With the hurricane approaching, residents began to batten down the hatches, boarding up windows and securing loose objects." 随着飓风的临近，居民们开始严阵以待，用木板封住窗户，固定松散的物品。|近义词: prepare, brace oneself, take precautions, hunker down|反义词: take no precautions, be unprepared|词性 idiom (functions as a verb)


batten:/ˈbætən/| n. vt.|板条；用板条固定|A long, flat strip of wood; to fasten or secure something with battens.|Example: "The roof was supported by sturdy wooden battens." 屋顶由坚固的木板条支撑。|Example: "They battened the windows shut before the storm."  他们在暴风雨来临前用板条封住了窗户。|近义词: plank, strip, lath|反义词: (no direct antonym)|词性 noun, verb

down:/daʊn/| adv. prep. adj. n. vt.|向下；沿着；下降；绒毛；写下|Downward; along; a descent; soft feathers; to write down.|Example: "The ball rolled down the hill." 球滚下了山。|Example: "He walked down the street." 他沿着街道走。|近义词: below, under, beneath|反义词: up, above|词性 adverb, preposition, adjective, noun, verb

hatches:/ˈhætʃɪz/| n.|舱口；开口|An opening or door, especially in a ship's deck, aircraft, or roof.|Example: "The sailors secured the hatches before the storm." 水手们在暴风雨来临前固定好了舱口。|Example: "Escape hatches are essential for safety in an emergency."  逃生舱口在紧急情况下对安全至关重要。|近义词: openings, doors, portals|反义词: (no direct antonym)|词性 noun
