lockstep: /ˈlɒkˌstɛp/ (UK), /ˈlɑːkˌstɛp/ (US)
n.| 锁定步骤; 步调一致|a way of operating in which things happen or change together at the same time and speed|The two countries have been in lockstep on this issue for years. 这两个国家在这个问题上多年来一直步调一致。|synonym: unison, harmony, synchrony|antonym: discord, disagreement|noun

lockstep: /ˈlɒkˌstɛp/ (UK), /ˈlɑːkˌstɛp/ (US)
adv.| 步调一致地;协调一致地|in a way that is closely coordinated or synchronized|The dancers moved in lockstep. 舞者们步调一致地移动。|synonym: in sync, in unison, together|antonym: out of sync, independently|adverb

词源:  "Lockstep" comes from the military marching practice of keeping in step so closely that the heel of one soldier's foot touches the toe of the soldier in front. The "lock" refers to this close, fixed position of the feet.
