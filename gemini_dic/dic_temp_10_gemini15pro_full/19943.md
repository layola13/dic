biodegrade:/ˌbaɪoʊdɪˈɡreɪd/| v. |生物降解|To decompose or break down naturally, especially by the action of microorganisms, resulting in environmentally safe byproducts.|Example: Many plastics don't biodegrade, contributing to pollution. 许多塑料不会生物降解，导致了污染。

Example:  The new packaging is designed to biodegrade quickly in landfills.  新的包装设计成在垃圾填埋场中快速生物降解。|近义词：decompose, rot, decay|反义词: synthesize, build up|词性:动词

biodegradable:/ˌbaɪoʊdɪˈɡreɪdəbl/| adj. |可生物降解的|Capable of being decomposed by bacteria or other living organisms.|Example: Biodegradable packaging helps reduce environmental impact. 可生物降解的包装有助于减少对环境的影响。

Example: Consumers are increasingly demanding biodegradable products. 消费者越来越需要可生物降解的产品。|近义词：compostable, degradable|反义词: non-biodegradable, persistent|词性:形容词

biodegradation:/ˌbaɪoʊˌdɛɡrəˈdeɪʃən/| n. |生物降解（名词）|The process by which organic substances are broken down by living organisms, such as bacteria, into simpler substances.|Example: The biodegradation of oil spills can take many years. 石油泄漏的生物降解可能需要很多年。

Example: Scientists are studying the biodegradation of plastics in the ocean. 科学家正在研究海洋中塑料的生物降解。|近义词：decomposition, decay, rotting|反义词: synthesis, formation|词性:名词
