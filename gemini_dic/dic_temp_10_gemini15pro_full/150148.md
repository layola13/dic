pan-diatonicism:/pænˌdaɪəˈtɒnɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 全音阶主义 |  A musical system or style that utilizes all seven notes of a diatonic scale without emphasizing the tonic or dominant, resulting in a sense of tonal ambiguity. It is often associated with the music of composers like Stravinsky and Bartók. |  The composer's use of pan-diatonicism creates a sense of floating tonality. 作曲家对全音阶主义的使用创造了一种漂浮的调性。 | near synonyms: extended tonality, modal mixture | antonym: strict diatonicism | noun
