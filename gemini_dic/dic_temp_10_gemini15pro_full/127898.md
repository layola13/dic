neoimpressionism:/ˌniːoʊɪmˈprɛʃənɪzəm/
|n.|新印象派|A late 19th-century movement in French painting characterized by the systematic use of small dots of color applied to the canvas, also known as Pointillism.  It aimed to create a greater luminosity and vibrancy of color than was possible with traditional painting techniques.|Seurat and Signac were the leading figures of Neoimpressionism. 修拉和西涅克是新印象派的主要人物。|近义词:Pointillism, Divisionism|反义词:Impressionism|词性:noun
