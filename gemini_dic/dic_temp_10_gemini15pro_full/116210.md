masochism:/ˈmæsəkɪzəm/ |n.| 受虐狂，受虐倾向 |The derivation of "masochism" comes from the name of Leopold von Sacher-Masoch, an Austrian writer whose novels explored themes of sexual gratification derived from pain or humiliation.  Masochism refers to the enjoyment of experiences that most people would find unpleasant or painful. |He showed signs of masochism by repeatedly engaging in self-destructive behaviors. 他通过反复进行自我毁灭的行为表现出受虐倾向。|Synonyms: self-abuse, flagellation |Antonyms: sadism |noun
