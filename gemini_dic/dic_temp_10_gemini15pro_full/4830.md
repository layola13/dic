alibi: /ˈælɪbaɪ/ | n. |不在场证明；托辞，借口 | a claim or piece of evidence that one was elsewhere when an act, typically a criminal one, is alleged to have taken place. | He had a perfect alibi for the night of the murder. 他在谋杀案发生当晚有完美的不在场证明。|  synonyms: excuse, plea, defense, justification | antonyms: accusation, charge, incrimination | noun

alibi: /ˈælɪbaɪ/ | vt. | 为…提供不在场证明；辩解 | to offer an excuse or defense for (someone or something), especially by providing an account of one's whereabouts at the time of an alleged act. |  He tried to alibi himself by saying he was at work. 他试图通过说自己当时在工作来为自己辩解。 | synonyms: excuse, justify, defend | antonyms: accuse, blame, incriminate | verb
