awkwardness:/ˈɔːkwərdnəs/ | n. | 尴尬，笨拙，不方便 |The awkwardness of the situation made everyone uncomfortable. 这种情况的尴尬让每个人都不舒服。|例句：There was an awkwardness in their conversation after the argument. 争吵过后，他们的谈话中充满了尴尬。

例句：She felt a sense of awkwardness when she realized she had mispronounced his name. 当她意识到自己把他的名字念错了时，感到一阵尴尬。

近义词：clumsiness, uneasiness, embarrassment |反义词: grace, ease, comfort |词性: 名词

awkward:/ˈɔːkwərd/ | adj. | 尴尬的，笨拙的，不方便的 | He felt awkward asking for help. 他觉得请求帮助很尴尬。 |例句：She made an awkward attempt to start a conversation. 她笨拙地试图开始一段对话。

例句：It was an awkward moment when they bumped into their ex. 当他们撞见前任时，场面一度非常尴尬。


近义词：clumsy, uncomfortable, embarrassing |反义词: graceful, easy, comfortable |词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *awkwart, from Old Norse afugr (“turned the wrong way, perverse”), equivalent to a- + -kward. Cognate with Icelandic öfugur (“contrary, reverse”). More at a-,kward.
