overbriefed:/ˌoʊvərˈbriːft/
adjective
过度指示的，被给予过多指示的
Having been given too much or too detailed instruction or information.
The lawyer felt overbriefed and overwhelmed by the sheer volume of documents. 律师觉得指示过多，被大量的文件压得喘不过气来。
The witness was so overbriefed that their testimony sounded rehearsed.  证人被过度指导，以至于他们的证词听起来像是排练过的。
近义词:over-instructed, over-prepared
反义词:underbriefed, unprepared
词性:adjective

词源:
"over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "briefed" (past participle of "brief," meaning "to instruct or inform concisely").  The word "brief" ultimately comes from the Latin "brevis," meaning "short."
