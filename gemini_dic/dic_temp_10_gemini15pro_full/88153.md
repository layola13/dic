hillbillies:/ˈhɪlbɪliz/| n.|乡巴佬，山里人，山区居民|A term, often derogatory, for people who live in rural, mountainous areas, especially in the Appalachian region of the United States.  It often implies a lack of sophistication, education, or modern amenities.|The movie portrayed the hillbillies as uneducated and isolated.|近义词：country folk, mountain people, rustics, yokels|反义词:city dwellers, urbanites|词性:noun

Etymology:
From "hill" + "billy," a familiar form of the given name "William."  The exact origin is uncertain, but it likely arose in the late 19th or early 20th century. One theory suggests it originated as a derogatory term for Scots-Irish settlers in the Appalachian Mountains.
