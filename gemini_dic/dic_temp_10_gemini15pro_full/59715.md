eclecticists:/ɪˈklɛktɪsɪsts/
|n.|折衷主义者；博采众长者
|A person who derives ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.|
|Many great philosophers are eclecticists, drawing inspiration from various schools of thought.|许多伟大的哲学家都是折衷主义者，他们从不同的思想学派中汲取灵感。
|The museum's collection is an eclectic mix of ancient artifacts and modern art.|博物馆的藏品是古代文物和现代艺术的折衷组合。
|Synonym: generalists, syncretists, polymaths|
|Antonym: purists, specialists, dogmatists|
|noun|

eclectic:/ɪˈklɛktɪk/
|adj.|折衷的；兼收并蓄的
|Deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.|
|Her eclectic taste in music ranges from classical to hip-hop.|她对音乐的品味兼收并蓄，从古典到嘻哈都有。
|The interior design of the house was an eclectic mix of styles.|房子的室内设计是多种风格的折衷组合。
|Synonym: diverse, varied, wide-ranging|
|Antonym: uniform, homogeneous, narrow|
|adjective|

eclecticism:/ɪˈklɛktɪsɪzəm/
|n.|折衷主义
|The practice of deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.|
|Eclecticism in architecture can lead to interesting and unique buildings.|建筑中的折衷主义可以创造出有趣而独特的建筑。
|His philosophical eclecticism allowed him to consider different perspectives.|他的哲学折衷主义使他能够考虑不同的观点。
|Synonym: syncretism, pluralism|
|Antonym: purism, dogmatism|
|noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Greek *eklektikos*  ("selective"), from *eklegein* ("to pick out, select"), from *ek* ("out") + *legein* ("to gather, choose").
