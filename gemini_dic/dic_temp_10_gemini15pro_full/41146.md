copula:/ˈkɒpjʊlə/ (UK), /ˈkɑːpjələ/ (US)| n.|系动词，连系动词|A copula is a word used to link the subject of a verb to a subject complement, such as the word *is* in the sentence "The sky is blue."|The verb "to be" is the most common copula in English.  天空是蓝色的。“to be”动词是英语中最常见的系动词。|Synonym: linking verb|Antonym: (No direct antonym, as it's a grammatical function)|noun

copula:/ˈkɒpjʊlə/ (UK), /ˈkɑːpjələ/ (US)| adj.|连接的|Relating to or serving as a copula.|Copular verbs link the subject to a predicate adjective or noun.  系动词将主语连接到谓语形容词或名词。|Synonym: connecting, linking|Antonym: disconnecting, separating|adjective
