demoralizations:/diːˌmɒrəlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/ /diːˌmɔːrələˈzeɪʃənz/
n.
士气低落，泄气，道德败坏
The act of demoralizing; the state of being demoralized.  A reduction in morale. A decline in morality.
The team's repeated losses led to widespread demoralizations among the players.  该队屡次失利导致球员普遍士气低落。
Constant criticism can lead to demoralizations in the workplace.  不断的批评会导致工作场所的士气低落。
近义词: discouragement, dejection, depression, corruption, depravity
反义词: encouragement, inspiration, upliftment, morality, virtue
词性: Noun

demoralize:/diːˈmɒrəlaɪz/ /diːˈmɔːrəlaɪz/
vt.
使士气低落，使泄气，使道德败坏
To deprive a person or group of spirit, courage, or morale; to corrupt the morals of.
The coach's harsh words demoralized the team. 教练的严厉话语使球队士气低落。
Failing the exam demoralized him. 考试不及格让他灰心丧气。
近义词: discourage, dishearten, dispirit, corrupt, deprave
反义词: encourage, hearten, inspire, uplift
词性: Verb


词源:  From French *démoraliser*, from dé- (expressing reversal) + moraliser (“to moralize”), from moral (“moral”).
