increpation:/ɪnˌkrɛˈpeɪʃən/ | n. | 斥责，责骂 |  A strong rebuke or reprimand, often delivered in an angry or passionate manner. |  The teacher's increpation silenced the disruptive student. 老师的斥责让捣乱的学生安静了下来。 |  rebuke, reprimand, scolding, chiding | praise, commendation | noun

Etymology->
From Late Latin *increpatio*, from Latin increpare ("to chide, rebuke, scold, blame"), from in- ("on, against, upon") + crepare ("to rattle, crack, creak, clang, ring, resound").
