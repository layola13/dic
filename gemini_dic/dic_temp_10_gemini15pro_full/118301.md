mercurials:/mərˈkjʊəriəlz/
|n.|汞制剂；水银制剂|Medicines or other preparations containing mercury.|The doctor prescribed mercurials for the patient's syphilis. 医生给病人开了汞制剂治疗梅毒。|Synonyms: mercury compounds, mercury preparations|Antonyms: None|Noun


mercurial:/mərˈkjʊəriəl/
|adj.|善变的；活泼的；水银的|Characterized by rapid and unpredictable changeableness of mood; relating to or containing mercury.|He has a mercurial temperament, shifting quickly from joy to anger. 他性情善变，喜怒无常。|Synonyms: volatile, erratic, fickle, temperamental|Antonyms: stable, steady, constant|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mercuriālis*, from Latin Mercurius (the Roman god Mercury, associated with quicksilver).  The association with mood swings likely comes from the perceived quick and unpredictable movements of liquid mercury.
