moor:/mʊr/ (UK), /mɔːr/ (US)| n. | 荒野，沼泽；停泊 | An open area of land, often high but poorly drained, with acidic soil and typically covered with heather, coarse grasses, and bracken.  A place to anchor a ship. | The boat was moored to the dock. 船停泊在码头。 | synonyms: heath, wasteland, moorland, anchor, berth | antonyms: meadow, grassland | noun

moor:/mʊr/ (UK), /mɔːr/ (US)| v. |  停泊，系泊(船只) | To secure (a boat, ship, etc.) in a particular place, as by cables or anchors. | We moored the boat overnight in the harbor. 我们把船停泊在港口过夜。 | synonyms: anchor, berth, dock | antonyms: unmoor, cast off | verb
