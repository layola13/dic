gorgeable:/ˈɡɔːrdʒəbl/ | adj. | 令人垂涎的；极好的；令人愉悦的 | Extremely enjoyable; delightful, especially to the senses, often used to describe food or experiences. | The cake was so gorgeable that I ate two slices. 这蛋糕太好吃了，我吃了两块。 | Synonyms: delicious, delectable, scrumptious, luscious, delightful | Antonyms: unappetizing, unpleasant, distasteful | adjective

gorge:/ɡɔːrdʒ/ | n. | 峡谷；暴食；大量 | a narrow valley between hills or mountains, typically with steep rocky sides and a stream or river flowing through it;  the act of eating a large amount greedily; a large amount |  We hiked through the beautiful gorge. 我们徒步穿越了美丽的峡谷。 | Synonyms: canyon, ravine, chasm, abyss | Antonyms: peak, summit | noun

gorge:/ɡɔːrdʒ/ | v. | 狼吞虎咽；塞满 | to eat a large amount greedily; to fill something completely | He gorged himself on the buffet. 他在自助餐上狼吞虎咽。 | Synonyms: stuff, cram, devour, gobble | Antonyms: nibble, peck | verb
