lindals:/ˈlɪndɑːlz/ | n. | 林达尔效应; 林达尔均衡  | Refers to Lindahl equilibrium, a theoretical state where the optimal quantity of a public good is produced, and each individual contributes an amount equal to their marginal benefit.  This concept is named after Erik Lindahl, a Swedish economist. | The government tried to achieve a Lindals equilibrium in funding the new park. 政府试图在新公园的资金筹措中实现林达尔均衡。|  Lindahl pricing rarely works perfectly in practice due to the free-rider problem.  由于搭便车问题的存在，林达尔定价在实践中很少能完美运作。| 近义词: public goods provision, optimal allocation | 反义词: market failure, underprovision | 词性: noun
