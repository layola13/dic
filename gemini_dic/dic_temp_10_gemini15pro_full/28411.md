carbone:/kɑːrˈboʊn/ (US), /ˈkɑːbɒn/ (UK)| n.|碳|A nonmetallic chemical element with atomic number 6, occurring in a pure crystalline form (as diamond and graphite) or in an amorphous form (as charcoal, coke, and soot), and forming organic compounds in combination with hydrogen, oxygen, etc.|Diamonds are a crystalline form of pure carbone. 钻石是纯碳的结晶形式。|Synonyms: C (chemical symbol), carbon element|Antonyms: None (as it's an element)|Noun
