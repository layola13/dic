anaemics: /əˈniːmɪks/ | n. | 贫血患者 | People with anaemia; those who have a deficiency of red blood cells or haemoglobin. |  The anaemics were given iron supplements. 贫血患者被给予铁补充剂。|  The doctor prescribed medication for the anaemics in his care. 医生给在他护理下的贫血患者开了药。 |  近义词: sufferers of anaemia | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, consider "healthy individuals" or "people with normal blood counts") | 词性: noun


anaemic: /əˈniːmɪk/ | adj. | 贫血的；缺乏活力的；苍白的 | Suffering from anaemia; lacking in colour, spirit, or vitality. | The patient looked anaemic and weak. 病人看起来面色苍白，很虚弱。| The play was an anaemic production, lacking energy and passion.  这出戏的制作很差，缺乏活力和激情。 | 近义词: pale, wan, listless, bloodless | 反义词:  ruddy, vibrant, energetic | 词性: adjective


**词源:**

From Greek *anaimia*, meaning "lack of blood," from *an-* (without) + *haima* (blood).
