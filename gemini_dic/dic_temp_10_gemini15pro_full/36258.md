cognovit:/kɒɡˈnəʊvɪt/ /kɑːɡˈnoʊvɪt/| n.| 认罪书，认罪状|A written confession of guilt, especially one made in court, acknowledging a debt and authorizing the creditor to take judgment against the debtor.|The defendant signed a cognovit, admitting his guilt and allowing the court to seize his assets.|近义词：confession, acknowledgment, admission|反义词：denial, plea of not guilty|词性: noun

Etymology:
From Latin *cognōvit*, third-person singular perfect active indicative of *cognōscō* (“I acknowledge, examine, learn”)
