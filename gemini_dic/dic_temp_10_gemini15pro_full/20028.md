biomechanicses: /ˌbaɪoʊməˈkænɪksɪz/
| n. |生物力学|The study of the mechanical laws relating to the movement or structure of living organisms.| He's studying biomechanicses to understand how the human body moves. 他正在学习生物力学以了解人体是如何运动的。 |Synonyms: biomechanics (often used interchangeably, though some argue "biomechanicses" is less common and might be considered non-standard), kinesiology | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps "static analysis" in certain contexts) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From bio- + mechanics. The plural form is often simply "biomechanics," but "biomechanicses" is also grammatically acceptable, though less commonly used.
