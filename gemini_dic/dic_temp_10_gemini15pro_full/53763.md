discomfort's:/dɪsˈkʌmfərts/ | n. | 不适；不便；不安 |  The possessive form of "discomfort," referring to something belonging to or caused by a state of being uncomfortable.  It signifies the unpleasantness or unease associated with a particular situation or condition. |  The discomfort's source was the lumpy mattress.  这不安的来源是凹凸不平的床垫。|  unease, distress, inconvenience | comfort, ease, relief | noun


Etymology:
From dis- (expressing negation) + comfort.  "Comfort" comes from Old French "confort" which traces back to Latin "confortare" meaning to strengthen much.
