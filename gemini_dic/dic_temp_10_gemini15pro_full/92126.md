hyperscrupulosities:/ˌhaɪpərˌskruːpjəˈlɒsɪtiz/ | n. | 过度谨慎；过分小心；洁癖 |  Excessive concern with fine points of conscience or with minute details of conduct; extreme conscientiousness. |  His hyperscrupulosities prevented him from enjoying life. 他的过分小心谨慎让他无法享受生活。 | 近义词: meticulousness, conscientiousness, fastidiousness | 反义词: carelessness, negligence, recklessness | 词性: 名词

词源: From Late Latin *hyperscrupulositas, from hyperscrupulosus (over-scrupulous) + -itas (suffix forming nouns of quality).  The root word "scrupulous" comes from Latin scrupulus (a small sharp stone, hence a source of anxiety or uneasiness). The prefix "hyper-" means "excessive" or "over."
