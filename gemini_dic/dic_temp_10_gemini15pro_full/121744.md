misproposes:/ˌmɪsprəˈpoʊzɪz/
verb
错误地求婚；提出不恰当的建议
to propose marriage to someone unsuitable or in an unsuitable way, or to propose something inappropriate.
He misproposed to her in a drunken stupor. 他在醉酒的恍惚中向她求婚，这很不得体。
The committee misproposed a plan that was ultimately rejected. 委员会提出了一个最终被否决的计划。
近义词: suggests inappropriately, proposes unwisely
反义词: proposes appropriately, suggests wisely
词性: verb

mispropose:/ˌmɪsprəˈpoʊz/
verb
错误地求婚；提出不恰当的建议
From mis- + propose.  Mis- is a prefix of Latin origin meaning "wrong," "bad," "wrongly," or "badly."  Propose comes from Old French proposer, from Latin prōpōnere  "to put forth, set before, offer,"  from prō- "forth" + pōnere "to put, place."
