overmodification:/ˌoʊvərˌmɑːdəfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |过度修饰|The excessive or unnecessary use of modifiers, often resulting in awkward or unclear writing or speech.  It can involve using too many adjectives, adverbs, or other modifying words, or using modifiers that are redundant or contradictory.| Her writing suffered from overmodification, with every noun preceded by two or three adjectives.  她的写作过度修饰，每个名词前都有两三个形容词。|近义词：overwriting, purple prose|反义词: conciseness, brevity|词性: noun

overmodification:/ˌoʊvərˌmɑːdəfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 过度修饰 | The act of modifying something excessively, often resulting in a less effective or natural result.  This is frequently seen in language, particularly in writing, where too many adjectives or adverbs can clutter the text and obscure the meaning. | The architect's overmodification of the original design made the building look cluttered and less elegant. 建筑师对原始设计的过度修改使建筑看起来杂乱无章，不那么优雅。| 近义词： embellishment, exaggeration | 反义词： simplification, minimalism | 词性: noun
