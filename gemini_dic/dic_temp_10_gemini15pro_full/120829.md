misauthorizing:/ˌmɪsˈɔːθəˌraɪzɪŋ/
verb
未经授权；错误授权
wrongfully authorizing something; authorizing something that should not have been authorized.
The bank teller misauthorized a large withdrawal from the customer's account.  银行出纳员错误地授权从客户账户中提取大笔款项。
He misauthorized the release of confidential information. 他错误地授权发布了机密信息。
Synonyms: misapprove,  disallow,  reject,  prohibit
Antonyms: authorize, permit, sanction, approve
词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
mis- (prefix):  From Old English *mis-, from Proto-Germanic *missa-, meaning "wrongly," "badly," "wrong," "amiss."  This prefix adds a sense of error or incorrectness to the base word.
authorize (verb):  From Late Latin auctorizare, from Latin auctor "author, originator, founder."  Meaning "to give official permission or legal power to."
