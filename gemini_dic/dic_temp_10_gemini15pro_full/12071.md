artificialness:/ˌɑːrtɪˈfɪʃəl.nəs/ | n. | 人工性；不自然；矫揉造作 | The artificialness of her smile made me uncomfortable. |  Her demeanor had a certain artificialness. 她举止有点矫揉造作。 | artificiality, unnaturalness, contrivedness | naturalness, genuineness, authenticity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From artificial + -ness.  Artificial comes from Late Latin *artificialis, from Latin artificiālis (“of or belonging to an artificer, artistic”), from artificium (“a trade, craft, profession, art; skill, ingenuity”), from artifex (“artificer, artist, craftsman; master of an art”), from ars (“art, skill, craft”) + -fex (agent suffix, akin to facere (“to make, do”)).
