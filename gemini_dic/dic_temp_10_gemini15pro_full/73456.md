forensic:/fəˈrɛnsɪk/ | adj. | 法医的，法庭的，适于法庭的 | Relating to or denoting the application of scientific methods and techniques to the investigation of crime. | Forensic evidence was used to convict the criminal. 法医证据被用来定罪罪犯。| synonyms: legal, judicial, medicolegal | antonyms: illegal, nonlegal | adjective


forensics:/fəˈrɛnzɪks/ | n. | 法医学 | The application of scientific knowledge or methods to legal questions, especially in a criminal investigation. |  He studied forensics in college. 他在大学学习法医学。 | synonyms: criminology | antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *forensis* "of the forum, public," from *forum* "marketplace, forum."  Originally referred to legal proceedings conducted in public. The modern sense of relating to scientific investigation of crime developed later.
