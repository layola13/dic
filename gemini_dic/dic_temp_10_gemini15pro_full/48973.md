degenerationism:/dɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃənɪzəm/| n. |退化论，堕落论|The theory that a culture or society is declining or has declined from a previous state of greatness or purity. It often involves a belief that the present is inferior to the past.  It can be applied to various aspects of society, including morality, art, politics, or technology.|Historians debated whether the decline of the Roman Empire was a case of degenerationism.|近义词：decline, decay, deterioration, devolution|反义词：progress, evolution, advancement|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "degeneration" + "-ism."  "Degeneration" comes from Late Latin *degenerātiōn-, from Latin degenerāre "to degenerate," from de- "down, away" + genus "race, kind."  "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns meaning a doctrine, belief, or practice.
