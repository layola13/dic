mankiest:/ˈmæŋkiɪst/| adj.|最破旧的；最肮脏的；最糟糕的|Superlative form of manky: most manky.  It means extremely unpleasant, dirty, or of poor quality.|That's the mankiest hotel I've ever stayed in. 那是我住过的最破旧的旅馆。|近义词：shabby, scruffy, dilapidated, grim, disgusting|反义词：pristine, immaculate, spotless|词性:形容词

词源:
manky (adj.) - late 19th century British slang, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to dialectal mank (n.) "defect, flaw," or to mangle (v.) "to mutilate, disfigure," or perhaps influenced by murky (adj.) "dark, gloomy." The superlative form "mankiest" simply follows standard English morphology.
