mending:/ˈmendɪŋ/| n. |修理，修补；改进，改善|the act of repairing something that is broken or damaged|例句：The mending of the torn shirt took longer than expected.  修补那件破衬衫比预期的要久。

例句：The roof is in need of some mending. 屋顶需要一些修补。|近义词：repairing, fixing, patching|反义词:breaking, damaging|词性:noun

mending:/ˈmendɪŋ/| adj. |修理的，修补的|used for mending something|例句：She kept a mending kit in her bag. 她在包里放了一个修理包。

例句：He used a mending tape to fix the tear in the paper. 他用修补胶带粘贴了纸上的裂缝。|近义词：repairing, restorative|反义词:damaging|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mendung, verbal noun of mendan "to amend, correct, improve, free from faults, heal, cure, repair," from Proto-Germanic *mandijo- "to improve" (source also of Old Frisian menda, Middle Dutch, Dutch mennen "to drive (cattle)," Old High German mendian "to announce, inform," German mendeln "to improve (a breed)," Old Norse menna "to improve"), from PIE root *mend- "to fail, fault, blemish."  The original sense of the verb was "to improve, correct."
