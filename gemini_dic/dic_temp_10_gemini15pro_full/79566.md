gloom-and-doom:/ˈɡluːm ən duːm/| adj. |悲观的，末日般的|Characterized by negativity and pessimism, predicting disaster and ruin.|The media's gloom-and-doom reporting about the economy is discouraging investors.|近义词：pessimistic, fatalistic, apocalyptic, negative, depressing|反义词:optimistic, hopeful, positive, cheerful, bright|词性:形容词
Etymology: This phrase is a compound of "gloom" (from Old English *glōm, meaning "twilight, dusk") and "doom" (from Old English dōm, meaning "judgment, law, decree, fate").  The combined phrase emphasizes a sense of impending disaster and hopelessness.
