archaeopteryx: /ˌɑːrkiːˈɒptərɪks/ | n. | 始祖鸟 | An extinct primitive toothed bird of the Jurassic period having a long feathered tail and hollow bones, and considered a link between reptiles and birds. |  Archaeopteryx is a crucial fossil in the study of evolution. 始祖鸟是进化研究中的重要化石。|  The discovery of Archaeopteryx revolutionized our understanding of the origin of birds. 始祖鸟的发现彻底改变了我们对鸟类起源的理解。 | synonym: urvogel (German) | antonym:  (No direct antonym, potentially modern bird) | noun
