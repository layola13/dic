cordon:/ˈkɔːrdən/ (US), /ˈkɔːdn/ (UK)| n. v.|警戒线，封锁线；饰带，绶带|A line or circle of police, soldiers, or guards preventing people from entering or leaving an area; a ribbon or cord worn as a badge of honor or membership in an organization.| Police set up a cordon around the crime scene. 警方在犯罪现场设置了警戒线。|Synonyms: barrier, barricade, line, perimeter, rope, chain|Antonyms: access, entrance, opening|noun

cordon:/ˈkɔːrdən/ (US), /ˈkɔːdn/ (UK)| v.|设置警戒线，封锁|To enclose or surround with a cordon.|The area was cordoned off after the explosion. 爆炸发生后，该地区被封锁了。|Synonyms: seal off, close off, block off, shut off|Antonyms: open, unlock, unseal|verb
