challenged: /ˈtʃælɪndʒd/ | adj. |受到挑战的；残疾的；能力不足的|Facing a difficult task or situation; having a disability that limits physical or mental abilities.|例句：He felt challenged by the difficult climb. 他觉得这次艰难的攀登对他来说是一项挑战。|例句：The school provides support for challenged students. 学校为残疾学生提供支持。|近义词：disabled, handicapped, impaired, struggling, tested|反义词：able, capable, competent, skilled, gifted|词性:形容词
challenged: /ˈtʃælɪndʒd/ | v. |挑战；质疑；要求；邀请比赛|To invite someone to compete or take part in a contest; to question something; to dispute something.|例句：She challenged him to a game of chess. 她向他挑战一盘棋。|例句：The lawyer challenged the witness's testimony. 律师质疑证人的证词。|近义词：question, dispute, contest, defy, oppose|反义词：accept, concede, surrender, yield|词性:动词


词源: From the Old French word *chalengier*, meaning "to accuse," derived from the Latin *calumniare*, meaning "to slander." The meaning shifted to "dispute" and then "invite to a contest."
