burrowed:/ˈbʌrəʊd/ (UK) /ˈbɜːroʊd/ (US)| v. |挖洞；躲藏；钻研| to dig a hole or tunnel into or under something; to hide oneself in a safe or comfortable place; to immerse oneself deeply in study or research | The rabbit burrowed into the ground. 兔子挖洞钻进了地里。|Synonyms: dig, tunnel, excavate, nestle, delve, immerse |Antonyms: emerge, surface, uncover|verb

burrowed:/ˈbʌrəʊd/ (UK) /ˈbɜːroʊd/ (US)| adj. |挖地洞的| having made a burrow | The burrowed animal was safe from predators.  挖地洞的动物躲避了捕食者。|Synonyms:  underground, subterranean |Antonyms: aboveground, exposed|adjective
