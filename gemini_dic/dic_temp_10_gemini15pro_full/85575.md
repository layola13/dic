hath:/hæθ/
verb
有
Archaic third-person singular present indicative form of *have*.
He hath given me a new car. 他给了我一辆新车。
Synonyms: has, possesses, owns
Antonyms: lacks, needs, wants
词性: 动词

USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
He hath a new car.  他有一辆新车。
She hath a beautiful garden. 她有一个美丽的花园。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
This tense is not used with "hath" as it is an archaic form.

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
This tense is not used with "hath" as it is an archaic form.

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
This tense is not used with "hath" as it is an archaic form.

一般过去时 (Simple Past):
This tense would use "had," not "hath."

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
This tense would use "had" with "been," not "hath."

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
This tense would use "had," not "hath."


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
This tense would use "had" with "been," not "hath."

一般将来时 (Simple Future):
This tense would use "will have," not "hath."

将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
This tense would use "will be having," not "hath."

将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
This tense would use "will have had," not "hath."

将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
This tense would use "will have been having," not "hath."


虚拟语气:
If he hath the money, he will buy it. 如果他有钱，他会买它。
I wish she hath more time. 我希望她有更多时间。(Although grammatically correct in archaic usage, it sounds very unnatural and would not be used in modern English. "I wish she had more time" is correct.)

被动语态:
This is unlikely to be used in the passive voice due to its archaic nature.

疑问句:
Hath he a car? 他有车吗？
Hath she finished her work? 她完成工作了吗？

否定句:
He hath not a car. 他没有车。
She hath not finished her work. 她没有完成工作。


PHRASE->

Phrases with "hath" are very archaic and not used in modern English.  It's more likely to be found in older texts like the King James Bible.  Therefore, common phrases are difficult to provide.  Focus on understanding its use as a third-person singular present tense form of "have."


词源：From Middle English *hath*, *hafth*, from Old English *hæfþ* (third-person singular present indicative of *habban*, “to have”), from Proto-Germanic *habaiþi*, from Proto-Indo-European *keh₂p-éyti* (third-person singular present indicative of *keh₂p-, “to seize, grasp”). Cognate with Scots haes (“has”), West Frisian hat (“has”), Dutch heeft (“has”), German hat (“has”), Swedish har (“has”), Icelandic hefur (“has”).
