non-speculative:/ˌnɑːnˈspɛkjʊlətɪv/
adjective
非投机的；非投机性的
Not based on speculation or conjecture; based on concrete evidence or facts.
This investment is considered non-speculative due to its strong fundamentals.  这笔投资因其强大的基本面而被认为是非投机的。
The company adopted a non-speculative approach, focusing on proven markets.  该公司采取了一种非投机性的方式，专注于成熟的市场。
近义词:safe, secure, conservative, risk-averse
反义词:speculative, risky, hazardous, adventurous
词性:adjective

non-speculative:/ˌnɒnˈspɛkjʊlətɪv/
adjective
非投机的；非投机性的
Not based on speculation or conjecture; based on concrete evidence or facts.
The report provided a non-speculative analysis of the current economic situation. 这份报告对当前的经济形势进行了非投机性的分析。
They made a non-speculative purchase of the property based on its appraised value. 他们根据房产的评估价值进行了非投机性购买。
近义词:safe, secure, conservative, risk-averse
反义词:speculative, risky, hazardous, adventurous
词性:adjective


词源:
From non- + speculative.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "speculative" comes from the Latin word "speculatus," past participle of "speculari," meaning "to look at, observe, contemplate."
