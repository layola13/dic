clouterly: /ˈklaʊtərli/ (US) /ˈklaʊtəli/ (UK)| adj.| 笨拙的，粗鲁的|Awkward and clumsy; lacking grace and finesse in movement or behavior.  Characterized by a lack of refinement and tact.|He made a clouterly attempt to catch the ball and tripped over his own feet. 他笨拙地试图接住球，结果被自己的脚绊倒了。|近义词: awkward, clumsy, ungainly, gawky, lumbering|反义词: graceful, elegant, deft, agile, nimble|词性: 形容词
词源: From clout + -erly.  Clout originally referred to a piece of cloth or leather used for patching or mending.  The sense of clumsiness likely arose from the image of someone patched together or moving in a stiff, constrained manner.
