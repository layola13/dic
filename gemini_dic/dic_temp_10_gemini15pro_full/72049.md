flirtatiousness:/ˌflɜːrˈteɪʃəsnəs/ | n. |轻浮；调情；卖弄风情|The quality of being flirtatious; a tendency to flirt. |Her flirtatiousness made some people uncomfortable. 她的轻浮让一些人感到不舒服。|He was annoyed by her constant flirtatiousness. 他对她不断的调情感到厌烦。|近义词：coquetry, teasing, dalliance|反义词：seriousness, reserve, modesty|词性:名词

flirtatious:/flɜːrˈteɪʃəs/ | adj. |轻浮的；调情的；卖弄风情的|Behaving in such a way as to suggest a playful sexual attraction to someone. |She gave him a flirtatious smile. 她给了他一个轻浮的微笑。|He has a flirtatious manner. 他有一种轻浮的举止。|近义词：coquettish, playful, teasing|反义词：serious, reserved, modest|词性:形容词

flirt:/flɜːrt/ | v. n. |调情；玩弄；轻率地对待|Behave as though sexually attracted to someone, but playfully rather than with serious intentions. A person who habitually flirts. |She was flirting with the bartender. 她正在和酒保调情。|He's a terrible flirt. 他是个很会调情的人。|近义词：coquet, tease, dally|反义词：ignore, shun|词性:动词/名词


词源: From flirt + -atious + -ness.  Flirt itself has uncertain origins, possibly from Old French fleureter "to touch lightly," or perhaps imitative.
