legislatorships:/ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪtərʃɪps/
n.
立法机构（通常指一个国家或地区的立法机关）,立法职位（复数）
The position or role of a legislator, often collectively referring to all such positions in a particular body.  The term can also refer to the duration of a legislator's term.
The United States Congress, composed of the Senate and the House of Representatives, is one of the most powerful legislatorships in the world. 美国国会，由参议院和众议院组成，是世界上最有权力的立法机构之一。
Due to term limits, many legislatorships see a significant turnover of members every few years. 由于任期限制，许多立法机构每隔几年就会有大量的成员更替。
近义词:legislatures, legislative bodies, lawmaking bodies
反义词:executive branch, judicial branch
