intensive:/ɪnˈtɛnsɪv/ | adj. n. | 密集的，加强的；加强器 | Concentrated on a single area or subject or into a short time; giving complete attention. |  例句：The course was very intensive, with classes meeting six hours a day.  这课程非常密集，每天上课六个小时。| 近义词：concentrated, thorough, rigorous, exhaustive | 反义词：superficial, casual, relaxed | 词性: adjective

intensive:/ɪnˈtɛnsɪv/ | n. | 加强器；加强物 | Something that intensifies. | 例句：Farmers are increasingly turning to chemical  intensives to boost crop yields. 农民越来越多地转向使用化学强化剂来提高作物产量。| 近义词：booster, enhancer | 反义词：reducer, inhibitor | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French intensif, from Late Latin intensus, past participle of intendere "to stretch out, strain," from in- "toward" + tendere "to stretch."  来自法语 intensif，源自晚期拉丁语 intensus，intendere 的过去分词，意思是“伸展，拉紧”，来自 in- “朝向” + tendere “伸展”。
