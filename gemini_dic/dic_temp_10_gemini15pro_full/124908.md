mudfish:/ˈmʌdfɪʃ/| n. |泥鱼，肺鱼|A fish that lives in muddy water and can breathe air.  A type of fish that can survive out of water for extended periods by burying itself in mud.| The mudfish burrowed deep into the mud to escape the drought. 泥鱼钻到泥土深处以躲避干旱。|  lungfish, bowfin | | noun
Etymology: mud + fish.  "Mud" refers to the muddy habitats these fish prefer, and "fish" denotes their classification.
