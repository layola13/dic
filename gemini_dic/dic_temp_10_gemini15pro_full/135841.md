nonproprieties:/ˌnɒnprəˈpraɪətiz/ | n. | 不得体，失礼，不适当的行为 | Actions or behaviors that are considered improper, indecent, or inappropriate according to social conventions or moral standards. |  Her nonproprieties at the dinner table shocked the guests. 她在餐桌上的不得体行为震惊了客人。| Synonyms: improprieties, indecorum, indecencies | Antonyms: proprieties, decorum, decency | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + propriety (proper behavior). Propriety comes from Old French proprieté, from Latin proprietas, meaning "property, ownership," later evolving to mean "proper character or quality."
