decastere: /deɪˈkæstər/ |v.| 废黜; 革职; 剥夺圣职| To remove from an office or position of authority, especially from the priesthood. | The corrupt bishop was decastered and excommunicated.  腐败的主教被革职并逐出教会。| synonyms: dismiss, remove, oust, dethrone, unfrock, defrock | antonyms: appoint, install, ordain, elect| verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *deschaster, from des- (expressing removal) + chaster (“to chase, pursue”), from Latin captare (“to seize, catch”).  The specific meaning related to the priesthood likely evolved from the idea of removing someone from their sacred “chase” or pursuit.
