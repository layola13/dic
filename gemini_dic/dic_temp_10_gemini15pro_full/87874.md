hideyoshis:/ˌhɪdeɪˈjoʊʃɪs/ | n. |  丰臣秀吉（人名） |  Hideyoshis is the plural form, less commonly used, referring to multiple individuals named Hideyoshi, or sometimes used humorously or ironically to refer to a single person named Hideyoshi as if they were multiple people.  Hideyoshi Toyotomi (豊臣 秀吉, March 17, 1537 – September 18, 1598) was a Japanese daimyō and politician of the late Sengoku period regarded as the second "Great Unifier" of Japan. |  The Hideyoshis of history left a complex legacy. 历史上的丰臣秀吉们留下了复杂的遗产。 |  Synonym: Toyotomi Hideyoshi (formal name) | Antonym: N/A (Proper noun) | Noun
