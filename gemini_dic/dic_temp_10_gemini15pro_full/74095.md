ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *fostren*, from Old English *fostrian* (“to nourish, support, bring up, cherish”), from Proto-Germanic *fōstrijaną* (“to supply with food, nourish”), from *fōstrą* (“food, nourishment”). Cognate with Old Frisian *fostria* (“to nourish”), Old Saxon *fostrian* (“to nourish”), Middle Dutch *voesteren* (“to bring up, nourish, educate”), Dutch *voesteren* (“to nurse”), Old High German *fostren* (“to nourish”), German *füttern* (“to feed”). Related to Old English *fōdor* (“food, fodder”).
