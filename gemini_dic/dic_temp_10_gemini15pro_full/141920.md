ordinand:/ˈɔːrdɪnənd/ | n. | 受命者，准备接受圣职的人 | A person who is preparing for ordination as a priest or deacon. |  The ordinand spent years studying theology before his ordination.  这位受命者在接受圣职前研读了多年的神学。| Synonyms: candidate for ordination, postulant | Antonyms: layperson, secular | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *ordinand*, from Latin *ordinandus* "one to be ordained," gerundive of *ordinare* "to ordain," from *ordo* "order, rank."
