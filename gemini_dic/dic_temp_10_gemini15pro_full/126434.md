mysticism:/ˈmɪstɪsɪzəm/| n. |神秘主义， mysticism |A belief in the possibility of attaining direct communion or union with God or the ultimate reality through spiritual intuition, contemplation, or insight.| She was drawn to the mysticism of Eastern religions. 她被东方宗教的神秘主义所吸引。| 近义词：spiritualism, occultism | 反义词：rationalism, materialism | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mysticus*, from Ancient Greek μυστικός (mustikós, “of mysteries”), from μύστης (mústēs, “initiate”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *mew- (“to close, shut”).
