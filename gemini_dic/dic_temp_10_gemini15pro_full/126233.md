mycetozoa:/ˌmaɪsiːtəˈzoʊə/ | n. | 粘菌 | A group of eukaryotic organisms formerly classified as animals or fungi but now placed in the kingdom Protista. They are characterized by a life cycle that includes both amoeboid and flagellated stages. | Mycetozoa are fascinating organisms that blur the lines between animal and fungal kingdoms. 粘菌是介于动物界和真菌界之间的迷人生物。 |  Synonyms: slime molds, slime moulds | Antonyms: N/A | Noun
