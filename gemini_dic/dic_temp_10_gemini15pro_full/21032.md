blighted: /ˈblaɪtɪd/ | adj. | 枯萎的，荒芜的，衰败的 | Damaged, destroyed, or spoiled.  Affected by blight; deteriorated; ruined. | The blighted crops were a sign of the coming famine. 枯萎的庄稼预示着饥荒即将到来。 |  The once vibrant neighborhood had become blighted and crime-ridden. 曾经充满活力的社区变得荒芜，犯罪猖獗。| Synonyms: ruined, decayed, withered, devastated, ravaged | Antonyms: flourishing, thriving, healthy, prosperous | adjective


blight: /blaɪt/ | n. | 枯萎病，祸害，不良影响 |  A disease that causes plants to wither and die. Something that spoils or damages something else. |  The potato blight caused widespread famine in Ireland. 马铃薯枯萎病在爱尔兰造成了 widespread 的饥荒。 | The closing of the factory cast a blight on the town. 工厂关闭给小镇蒙上了一层阴影。| Synonyms: curse, affliction, scourge, plague, bane | Antonyms: blessing, boon, benefit | noun

blight: /blaɪt/ | vt. |  使枯萎，破坏，摧残 | To cause something to wither and die; to spoil or damage something. | The frost blighted the tomato plants. 霜冻使番茄植株枯萎。 | His reputation was blighted by the scandal.  他的名誉被丑闻玷污了。 | Synonyms: ruin, spoil, destroy, mar, impair | Antonyms: enhance, improve, benefit, nourish | verb
