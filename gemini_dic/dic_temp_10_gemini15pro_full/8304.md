Etymology:
"anti-" (prefix): from Greek "anti" meaning "against," "opposite of"
"normal": from Late Middle English, from Late Latin "normalis" meaning "according to a carpenter's square," from Latin "norma" meaning "carpenter's square, rule, pattern."
"-ness" (suffix): Old English "-nes," forming nouns of state, condition, or quality.
