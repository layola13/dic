garrotting:/ɡəˈrɒtɪŋ/
n.|绞杀，勒死|The act of strangling someone with a cord or wire.  This method of execution was once common in Spain. |The garrotting was swift and silent. 绞杀迅速而无声。|
近义词：strangulation, choking, asphyxiation|
反义词: resuscitation, revival|
词性:noun


garrotte:/ɡəˈrɒt/
n.|绞索，勒杀工具|A device, typically a wire or cord, used for strangulation.|The assassin used a garrotte to silence his victim. 刺客用绞索使他的受害者噤声。|
近义词：noose, ligature|
反义词: None|
词性:noun

garrotte:/ɡəˈrɒt/
vt.|勒死，绞杀|To strangle someone with a garrotte.|The victim was garrotted in his sleep. 受害者在睡梦中被勒死了。|
近义词：strangle, choke, asphyxiate|
反义词:resuscitate, revive|
词性:verb



ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *garrote* "cudgel, club," related to Catalan *garrot* and Old Provençal *garrot*, from Vulgar Latin *garra* "claw."  The sense shifted from "club" to "method of execution by strangulation" in the 19th century.
