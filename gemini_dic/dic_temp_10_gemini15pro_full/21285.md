bloodlettings: /ˈblʌdˌlɛtɪŋz/
noun
放血；瀉血
The practice of drawing blood from a patient to cure or prevent illness.
In the past, bloodlettings were a common medical practice. 过去，放血是一种常见的医疗方法。
Bloodletting was believed to cure a variety of ailments. 人们相信放血可以治疗各种疾病。
synonym: phlebotomy, venesection
antonym: blood transfusion, infusion
n.

Etymology:
From Middle English *blodletting*, equivalent to *blood* + *letting*.  The *letting* part comes from Old English *lētan* "to allow, let".  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *lētaną*, and from Proto-Indo-European *leH₁-t-*.
