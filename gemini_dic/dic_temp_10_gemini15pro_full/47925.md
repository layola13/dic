deceptivity:/dɪˌsɛpˈtɪvɪti/
n.
欺骗性；迷惑性
The deceptivity of the advertisement led many customers to believe they were getting a better deal than they actually were.  这则广告的欺骗性让许多顾客相信他们得到了比实际情况更好的交易。
His deceptivity was evident in his feigned innocence. 他假装无辜，欺骗性显而易见。
Synonyms: deceitfulness, trickery, fraudulence, guile
Antonyms: honesty, sincerity, genuineness
noun


Etymology:  From deceptive + -ity.  "Deceptive" comes from the Late Latin "deceptīvus," from the past participle stem of "dēcipere" (to deceive), from "de-" (down, away) + "capere" (to take).
