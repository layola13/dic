obfuscation:/ˌɒbfʌsˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 混淆，模糊，困惑 | The lawyer’s obfuscation of the facts confused the jury. 律师对事实的混淆让陪审团感到困惑。 |  The government's report was filled with deliberate obfuscation. 政府的报告充满了故意的混淆。 | 近义词：confusion, obscurity, mystification | 反义词：clarification, elucidation | 词性: 名词

obfuscate:/ˈɒbfʌskeɪt/ | v. | 使模糊，使困惑 | The writer obfuscated the issue with jargon. 作者用术语混淆了问题。 |  She deliberately obfuscated the details of the agreement. 她故意混淆了协议的细节。 | 近义词：confuse, obscure, bewilder | 反义词：clarify, explain, illuminate | 词性: 动词

词源: From Latin *obfuscatus*, past participle of *obfuscare* "to darken," from *ob* "over, against" + *fuscare* "to darken," from *fuscus* "dark."
