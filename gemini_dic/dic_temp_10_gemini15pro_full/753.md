absolutes:/ˈæbsəluːts/| n.|绝对值；绝对事物；绝对真理|Things that are absolute are total and complete. |The artist painted in simple black and white, dealing in absolutes.|近义词:certainties, fundamentals|反义词:relatives|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *absolutus*, past participle of absolvere (“to absolve, set free, finish”), from ab (“from”) + solvere (“to loosen”).

USAGE->
Plural form of absolute.  Generally refers to principles, values, or rules considered universally valid or true, regardless of context.

Example 1: Moral absolutes are difficult to define in a complex world.
在复杂的世界中，道德绝对主义很难定义。

Example 2:  He believed in the absolutes of right and wrong.
他相信绝对的对与错。
