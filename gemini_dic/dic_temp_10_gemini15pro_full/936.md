abysses: /əˈbɪsɪz/
n.
深渊，深淵
plural of abyss:  A deep or seemingly bottomless chasm. A profound or immeasurable space, gulf, or cavity; vast chasm. Anything profound, unfathomable, or infinite.
The submarine plunged into the dark abysses of the ocean. 潜水艇 plunged into the dark abysses of the ocean.
近义词: depths, chasms, gorges, voids
反义词: heights, peaks, summits
词性: noun (plural)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French abisme, from Latin abyssus, from Ancient Greek ἄβυσσος (ábyssos, “bottomless”), from ἀ- (a-, “without”) + βυσσός (byssós, “bottom”).

USAGE->
The ship sank into the murky abysses. = 船沉入了黑暗的深渊。

The explorers discovered strange creatures in the ocean's abysses. = 探险家在海洋深处发现了奇怪的生物。

Their despair plunged them into emotional abysses. = 他们的绝望使他们陷入了情感的深渊。

The novel explores the psychological abysses of the human mind. = 这部小说探讨了人类心灵深处的心理深渊。

He stared into the abysses of her dark eyes. = 他凝视着她的黑眼睛的深渊。


PHRASE->
