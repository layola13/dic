glibly:/ˈɡlɪbli/| adv.|流利地，流畅地，油嘴滑舌地|Speaking or spoken in a smooth, fluent, and easy manner, often in a way that is not sincere or convincing.  Characterized by a ready flow of speech, often superficial or insincere.|He glibly explained away his mistakes. 他油嘴滑舌地解释了自己的错误。|Synonyms: smoothly, fluently, volubly, readily, effortlessly|Antonyms: haltingly, awkwardly, hesitantly|adverb

Etymology: From glib + -ly.  "Glib" comes from Middle Low German *glibberich "slippery," related to Dutch glibberig.  Originally meant "smooth" or "slippery," then came to mean "fluent" in speech, and eventually acquired a negative connotation of insincerity.
