The word "hailse" doesn't exist in standard English.  It appears to be a Dutch word.  Therefore, the following information pertains to the Dutch word "hailse".


hailse: /ˈɦɛɪ̯lsə/ | n. | greeting, salutation | A formal greeting, often used in writing or official contexts.  It can be translated as "greetings" or "salutations." | Example: Met vriendelijke hailse, Jan. (With kind regards, Jan.) | Example:  Aan het begin van zijn brief stond een korte hailse. (At the beginning of his letter was a short greeting.) | Near synonyms: groet, begroeting | Antonym: afscheid (farewell) | noun

hailse: /ˈɦɛɪ̯lsə/ | vt. | to greet, to salute | To greet someone formally, often in writing.  | Example: Ik hailse u namens de directie. (I greet you on behalf of the management.) | Example: Hij hailse de koningin met een diepe buiging. (He greeted the queen with a deep bow.) | Near synonyms: groeten, begroeten | Antonym: afscheid nemen van (to say farewell to) | verb
