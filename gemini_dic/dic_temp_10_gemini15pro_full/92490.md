hypothecator:/haɪˈpɑːθəˌkeɪtər/ | n. | 抵押人，质押人 | A person who hypothecates property; one who pledges something as security for a debt. | The hypothecator retained possession of the ship, but the bank held the title as security for the loan. 抵押人保留了船的所有权，但银行持有所有权作为贷款担保。

The hypothecator defaulted on the loan, and the bank seized the collateral. 抵押人拖欠贷款，银行扣押了抵押品。 | Near synonyms: pledger, mortgagor | Near antonyms: pledgee, mortgagee | Noun
