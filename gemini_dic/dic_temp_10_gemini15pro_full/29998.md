cavity:/ˈkævəti/| n.|洞，腔，空穴|A hollow space within a solid body.|The dentist discovered a cavity in my tooth. 牙医在我的牙齿里发现了一个蛀牙。|synonyms: hole, gap, void, opening|antonyms: fullness, solidity|noun

cavity:/ˈkævəti/| n.|（感情上的）空虚，空白|A feeling of emptiness or lack.|The loss of his job left a huge cavity in his life. 失去工作在他的生活中留下了一个巨大的空虚。|synonyms: emptiness, void, vacuum|antonyms: fullness, completeness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English cavite, from Old French cavité, from Latin cavitās (“hollowness”), from cavus (“hollow”).
