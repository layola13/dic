branchia: /ˈbræŋkiə/ (US), /ˈbræŋkɪə/ (UK)| n. |鳃|A respiratory organ of aquatic animals, typically a series of thin filaments or plates through which water passes, enabling the animal to absorb oxygen and excrete carbon dioxide.|The fish's branchia were clearly visible through its thin skin. 这条鱼的鳃透过它薄薄的皮肤清晰可见。|The lobster uses its branchia to breathe underwater. 龙虾用鳃在水下呼吸。|Synonym: gill|Antonym: lung|noun
