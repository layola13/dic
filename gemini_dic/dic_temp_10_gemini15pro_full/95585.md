incommensurable:/ˌɪnkəmɛnʃərəbl/ | adj. | 不可通约的；不能相比的 | Not commensurable; having no common measure or standard of comparison.  Often used to describe values, beliefs, or systems of thought. |  The two theories are incommensurable, making it impossible to determine which is superior. 这两种理论不可通约，因此无法确定哪一种更好。 | incompatible, disparate, different, incongruous | commensurable, similar, compatible | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *incommensurabilis, from in- "not" + com- "together" + mensurabilis "measurable," from mensurare "to measure."
