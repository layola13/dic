improvised: /ˈɪmprəvaɪzd/ | adj. | 即兴的，临时准备的 | Done or made using whatever is available, without planning or preparation. |  The musicians improvised a bluesy tune.  音乐家们即兴演奏了一曲布鲁斯调。| extemporized, impromptu, ad-lib | planned, rehearsed | adjective

improvise: /ˈɪmprəvaɪz/ | v. | 即兴创作，临时凑合 | Create and perform (music, drama, or verse) spontaneously or without preparation. |  He improvised a song about the journey. 他即兴创作了一首关于旅行的歌。| extemporize, ad-lib, wing it | plan, rehearse | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French improviser, from Italian improvvisare, from improvviso 'unexpected', from Latin improvisus, from in- 'not' + provisus, past participle of providēre 'foresee'.
