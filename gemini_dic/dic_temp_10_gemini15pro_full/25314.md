buggy:/ˈbʌɡi/ (US), /ˈbʌɡi/ (UK)| n. adj.|轻便马车；婴儿车；越野车；多故障的|A small, light vehicle, often open and horse-drawn, or a baby carriage, or a small motor vehicle designed for off-road use. Also used as an adjective to describe something full of bugs (errors) or problems. |例句：We took a buggy ride through the park. 我们乘轻便马车游览了公园。

The software is still buggy and needs more testing.  这个软件仍然有很多错误，需要更多测试。


|近义词：(n.) carriage, stroller, pram, quad bike, ATV; (adj.) faulty, defective, glitchy |反义词:(adj.) working, functional, flawless|词性: noun, adjective

buggy:/ˈbʌɡi/ (US), /ˈbʌɡi/ (UK)| adj.|多故障的；充满虫子的|Full of bugs (errors) or problems, or infested with insects. |例句：This program is so buggy, it crashes all the time. 这个程序问题很多，总是崩溃。

The room was buggy and uncomfortable. 房间里有很多虫子，很不舒服。


|近义词：faulty, defective, glitchy, insect-ridden |反义词:working, functional, flawless, insect-free|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->

From bug (insect) + -y (suffix forming adjectives).  The computing sense originated in the early days of computing when actual insects sometimes caused malfunctions in hardware.
