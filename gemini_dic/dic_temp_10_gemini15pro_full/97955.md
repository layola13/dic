Inuit: /ˈɪnjuɪt/ (US), /ˈɪnjuːɪt/ (UK)| n. |因纽特人；因纽特语|A member of an indigenous people inhabiting northern Canada, Greenland, Alaska, and northeastern Siberia, traditionally living by hunting, fishing, and trapping.  The Inuit language, or languages, spoken by the Inuit people.| Example: The Inuit have a deep understanding of the Arctic environment. 因纽特人对北极环境有深刻的理解。

Example:  She is learning to speak Inuit. 她正在学习说因纽特语。|Synonym: Eskimo (outdated and sometimes considered offensive), Inuk (singular)|Antonym: N/A|Noun
