demagogic:/ˌdeməˈɡɑːɡɪk/ | adj. | 煽动性的，蛊惑人心的 | Appealing to emotions and prejudices rather than reason, typically to gain popular support. |  The politician's demagogic speeches riled up the crowd.  这位政客煽动性的演讲激怒了人群。| synonyms: inflammatory, rabble-rousing, incendiary | antonyms:  rational, reasoned, logical | adjective

demagogue:/ˈdeməɡɑːɡ/ | n. | 煽动者，蛊惑民心的政客 | A political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument. | The demagogue easily swayed public opinion with his fiery rhetoric.  这个煽动者用他激烈的言辞轻易地左右了公众舆论。| synonyms: agitator, firebrand, rabble-rouser | antonyms: statesman, diplomat, conciliator | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek dēmagōgos (leader of the people), from dēmos (people) + agōgos (leading, leading the way), from ágein (to lead).
