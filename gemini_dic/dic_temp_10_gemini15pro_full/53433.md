disappoint:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt/ | v. | 使失望，辜负 | To fail to fulfill the hopes or expectations of. |  I hate to disappoint you, but I can't come to the party.  我很抱歉让你失望，但我不能参加派对了。

The team's performance disappointed their fans. 球队的表现让球迷们失望。| 近义词：frustrate, let down, sadden | 反义词：please, delight, satisfy | 词性: 动词


disappointed:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntɪd/ | adj. | 失望的，沮丧的 | Feeling sadness or displeasure because someone or something has failed to fulfill one's hopes or expectations. | I was disappointed that I didn't get the job. 我很失望我没有得到那份工作。


She was disappointed with her exam results. 她对自己的考试成绩感到失望。 | 近义词：disheartened, discouraged, let down | 反义词：pleased, delighted, satisfied | 词性: 形容词


disappointment:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt/ | n. | 失望，沮丧 | The feeling of sadness or displeasure caused by the non-fulfillment of one's hopes or expectations. |  Missing the concert was a huge disappointment. 错过演唱会是一个巨大的遗憾。

His failure to get promoted was a bitter disappointment. 他没能升职是一个痛苦的失望。 | 近义词：frustration, letdown, setback | 反义词：satisfaction, pleasure, delight | 词性: 名词


词源: dis- (Latin prefix meaning "apart," "away," "reversal") + appoint (from Old French apointer, meaning "to arrange, settle, fix"). Originally meant to undo an appointment or agreement. The modern sense of frustrating expectations developed later.
