on-the-fly:/ˌɑːn ðə ˈflaɪ/ /ˌɒn ðə ˈflaɪ/| adv. adj.|动态地，即时地，匆忙地|Done or made while in motion or progress; without prior preparation.  Created or assembled as needed, dynamically.|例句：We had to make some on-the-fly changes to the presentation. 我们不得不临时对演示文稿进行一些修改。|近义词：improvised, impromptu, ad hoc, in real time|反义词：pre-planned, prepared, prearranged|词性: 副词, 形容词
词源:  This term originates from the early 20th century, likely from the idea of catching a fly in mid-air, implying quick action and improvisation.
