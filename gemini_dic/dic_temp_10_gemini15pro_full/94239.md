imperilments:/ɪmˈpɛrɪlmənts/ | n. | 危险，危难，危害 | The act of imperiling, or the state of being imperiled; danger; jeopardy; risk. |  The company's financial imperilments led to its eventual bankruptcy. 该公司的财务危机导致其最终破产。| Synonyms: dangers, hazards, jeopardies, perils, risks | Antonyms: safety, security | noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *imperilment, from Anglo-Norman emperillement, from Old French emperiller (“to put in peril”), from en- (“in, into”) + péril (“peril”).
