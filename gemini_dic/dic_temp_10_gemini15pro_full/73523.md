foreshortening:/fɔːrˈʃɔːrtnɪŋ/ /fɔːrˈʃɔːrtənɪŋ/| n. |透视缩短（法）|The technique of depicting an object or human figure in a picture so that it appears to recede or project sharply into space, especially by contracting the lines of perspective. |The artist used foreshortening to make the outstretched hand appear closer to the viewer. 艺术家用透视缩短法使伸出的手看起来更靠近观看者。|近义词：perspective, contraction|反义词: elongation|词性: noun

foreshorten:/fɔːrˈʃɔːrtn/ /fɔːrˈʃɔːrtən/| v. |（使）透视缩短|To represent (a figure or object) in perspective, especially so that it appears shorter than its actual length.|The painting foreshortens the subject's legs, making them appear smaller than they would in real life. 这幅画使主体的腿透视缩短，使它们看起来比实际生活中更小。|近义词：abbreviate, condense|反义词: elongate, lengthen|词性: verb
