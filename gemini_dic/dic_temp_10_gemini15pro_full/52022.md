diaheliotropism:/ˌdaɪəˌhiːliˈɒtrəpɪzəm/| n. |向日性|The diurnal motion of plant parts such as leaves and flowers as they follow the sun across the sky. This maximizes light interception for photosynthesis.|The sunflower exhibits strong diaheliotropism, its head tracking the sun throughout the day.|近义词：heliotropism|反义词:apheliotropism|词性:noun
Etymology: From dia- (through, across) + helio- (sun) + tropism (turning).
