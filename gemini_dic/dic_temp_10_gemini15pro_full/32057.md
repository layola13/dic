cherry-picking:/ˈtʃɛriˌpɪkɪŋ/ | n. | 拣选；选择性接受（对自己有利的）；断章取义 |  The act of selecting only the most favorable or desirable items, data, or evidence, while ignoring those that are less favorable or contradictory.  | He was accused of cherry-picking data to support his theory. 他被指责断章取义地选择数据来支持他的理论。 |  selective choosing,  suppressing evidence, bias selection | comprehensive analysis, objective assessment | noun
