leashed:/liːʃt/
| adj.|被拴住的，受约束的|restrained or controlled, especially by a leash|The dog was leashed and waiting patiently for a walk. 狗被拴着，耐心地等待着散步。|近义词：tied, restrained, bound|反义词：unleashed, free, unbound|词性:形容词
leash:/liːʃ/
| n. vt.| leash (n.) a strap or cord used to hold an animal in check; (v.) to restrain with a leash|狗绳；用皮带系住|例句：She put a leash on her dog. 她给她的狗系上了皮带。|近义词：lead, rope, tether|反义词：unleash|词性:名词,动词

PHRASE->
on a leash: 被拴住，受控制  The child was kept on a tight leash. 孩子被严加管束。
keep someone on a short leash:  严密控制某人  His boss kept him on a short leash. 他的老板对他严加控制。
strain at the leash:  渴望开始，跃跃欲试 The athletes were straining at the leash to begin the race.  运动员们都跃跃欲试地想要开始比赛。
slip the leash:  挣脱束缚，摆脱控制  The company slipped the leash of government regulation.  这家公司摆脱了政府的监管。
leash laws:  拴狗法规  Most cities have leash laws requiring dogs to be leashed in public areas.  大多数城市都有拴狗法规，要求狗在公共场所必须拴上皮带。


词源：From Old French *laissier "to let go, allow," from Latin laxare "to loosen, relax," from laxus "loose."  The meaning shifted from "letting go" to "holding by a leash."
