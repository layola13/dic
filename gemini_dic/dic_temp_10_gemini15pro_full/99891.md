internalize:/ɪnˈtɜːrnəlaɪz/ (US), /ɪnˈtɜːnəlaɪz/ (UK)| v. |内化；使…内在化|To make (attitudes or behavior) part of one's nature by learning or unconscious assimilation.|She had internalized her parents' values.|近义词：absorb, assimilate, incorporate, integrate|反义词：externalize|词性：动词

internalizing:/ɪnˈtɜːrnəlaɪzɪŋ/ (US), /ɪnˈtɜːnəlaɪzɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |内化的；内向的|Relating to or characterized by the tendency to internalize emotions or experiences, often leading to psychological distress.|Internalizing problems such as anxiety and depression are common in adolescents.|近义词：introspective, inward-looking|反义词：externalizing|词性：形容词
