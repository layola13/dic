off-the-reel:/ˌɒf ðə ˈriːl/ (British), /ˌɔːf ðə ˈriːl/ (American)| adj.|现成的，即席的；未经剪辑的|Recorded, performed, or broadcast as it happens, without editing or processing.  Originates from the era of reel-to-reel tape recorders. When something was "off the reel," it meant it was played directly from the reel, unedited.  Now more broadly used to describe anything spontaneous or readily available.|The band played an off-the-reel performance for the local radio station.|Synonyms: impromptu, extemporaneous, spontaneous, unrehearsed, live, improvised|Antonyms: rehearsed, planned, prepared, edited|adjective

off-the-reel:/ˌɒf ðə ˈriːl/ (British), /ˌɔːf ðə ˈriːl/ (American)| adv.|现成的，即席地；未经剪辑地|In a manner that is recorded, performed, or broadcast as it happens, without editing or processing.|The comedian performed off-the-reel, making up jokes as he went along.|Synonyms: impromptu, extemporaneously, spontaneously, unrehearsedly, live, improvisedly|Antonyms: rehearsedly, plannedly, preparedly, editedly|adverb
