overobjects:/ˈoʊvərˌɒbdʒekts/| n. |过度客体|Objects that hold excessive emotional significance, often stemming from early childhood experiences, particularly related to the primary caregiver.  They represent a fusion of the self with the object, where the object becomes an extension of the self and a source of comfort and security, often blurring the boundaries between self and other.|The child's blanket became an overobject, inseparable from them for years.  孩子们的毯子变成了一个过度客体，多年来与他们形影不离。|近义词: transitional object, comfort object|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps) indifferent object|词性:noun
