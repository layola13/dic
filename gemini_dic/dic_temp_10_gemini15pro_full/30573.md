ceratobranchial: /ˌsɛrətəˈbræŋkiəl/
noun
角鳃骨; 角鳃节
One of a series of paired ventral cartilages or bones extending dorsally from the ceratohyal bone of the hyoid arch and supporting the gills in fishes.
The ceratobranchials are important for gill support in fish. 角鳃骨对鱼类的鳃支撑很重要。
The dissection revealed five ceratobranchials on each side of the fish's head. 解剖显示鱼头两侧各有五个角鳃骨。
Synonyms: branchial bone, gill support
Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps structures not related to gill support)
n.

Etymology:
From Greek *keras, keratos* "horn" + *branchia* "gills".
