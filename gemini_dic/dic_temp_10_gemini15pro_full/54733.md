disparagers:/dɪˈspærɪdʒərz/ | n. | 贬低者，诋毁者 | People who belittle or criticize the value or importance of something or someone. |  The disparagers of modern art often fail to understand its nuances. 现代艺术的贬低者往往无法理解它的细微之处。|  His constant disparagers made it difficult for him to feel confident in his work.  他不断的诋毁者让他很难对自己的工作感到自信。 | synonyms: critics, detractors, denigrators, slanderers | antonyms:  praisers, admirers, supporters, advocates | noun

disparage:/dɪˈspærɪdʒ/ | v. | 贬低，诋毁 | To speak of or treat slightingly; depreciate; belittle. | Don't disparage her efforts; she's trying her best. 不要贬低她的努力；她正在尽力而为。|  He disparaged his opponent's record in an attempt to win votes. 他诋毁对手的记录，试图赢得选票。| synonyms: belittle, denigrate, deprecate, minimize, underestimate, undervalue | antonyms: praise, commend, extol, laud, acclaim | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desparagier* (Modern French *déparager*), from *des-* (from Latin *dis-* meaning "apart, away") + *parage* "rank, lineage" (from Late Latin *paraticum* "peerage").  The original meaning was "to degrade in rank," later evolving to the more general sense of "to belittle."
