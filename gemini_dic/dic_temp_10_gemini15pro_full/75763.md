fulmine:/ˈfʊlmɪneɪ/ /ˈfʊlˌmaɪn/| n.|霹雳，闪电，雷电；爆炸；谴责，抨击|A sudden loud noise or flash of light caused by the discharge of electricity between clouds or between a cloud and the earth. Also, a sudden outburst of anger or denunciation.|例句：The fulmine struck the tree. 闪电击中了那棵树。|例句：His speech was a fulmine against the government's policies. 他的演讲是对政府政策的猛烈抨击。|近义词：thunderbolt, lightning, blast, denunciation|反义词: compliment, praise|词性:名词

fulminate:/ˈfʊlmɪneɪt/| v.|爆炸；怒斥，抨击；发出轰隆声|To explode with a loud noise; to criticize severely and angrily.|例句：The bomb fulminated, destroying the building. 炸弹爆炸了，摧毁了那座建筑。|例句：He fulminated against the injustice of the system. 他抨击了这个制度的不公正。|近义词：explode, detonate, denounce, condemn|反义词: praise, commend|词性:动词

词源: From Latin *fulmināre* "to flash with lightning, hurl lightning," from *fulmen* (genitive *fulminis*) "lightning flash, thunderbolt," perhaps from *fulgēre* "to shine, flash, glitter."
