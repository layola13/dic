haber:/aˈβeɾ/ (Spanish)| v. |有 (auxiliary verb), 必须, 应该|To have (as an auxiliary verb), must, should. It is primarily used in forming compound tenses.|例句：He ha comido. = He has eaten. 他已经吃了。

例句：Ha de venir mañana. = He must come tomorrow. 他明天必须来。|近义词：tener (to have - as a main verb), deber (must, should)|反义词: N/A (auxiliary verbs don't typically have antonyms)|词性: verb

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
No se usa haber como verbo principal en presente simple. = Haber is not used as a main verb in the simple present.  一般现在时不用haber作为主动词。
Only used as auxiliary:  Han llegado tarde. = They have arrived late. 他们来晚了。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
No se usa haber como verbo principal en presente continuo. = Haber is not used as a main verb in the present continuous.  现在进行时不用haber作为主动词。
Only used as auxiliary:  Hemos estado trabajando todo el día. = We have been working all day. 我们整天都在工作。

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
He hablado con él. = I have spoken with him. 我已经和他谈过了。
Han viajado mucho este año. = They have travelled a lot this year. 他们今年旅行了很多。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
Hemos estado estudiando español durante dos años. = We have been studying Spanish for two years. 我们学习西班牙语已经两年了。
Ha estado lloviendo toda la noche. = It has been raining all night.  整晚都在下雨。


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
No se usa haber como verbo principal en pasado simple. = Haber is not used as a main verb in the simple past. 一般过去时不用haber作为主动词。
Only used as auxiliary: Hubo una fiesta anoche. = There was a party last night. 昨晚有一个派对。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
No se usa haber como verbo principal en pasado continuo. = Haber is not used as a main verb in the past continuous. 过去进行时不用haber作为主动词。
Only used as auxiliary: Habíamos estado esperando durante horas. = We had been waiting for hours. 我们已经等了好几个小时。


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
Había terminado el trabajo antes de que llegaras. = I had finished the work before you arrived. 在你到达之前我已经完成了工作。
Habían comido cuando los llamé. = They had eaten when I called them. 我打电话给他们时，他们已经吃过了。


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
Habíamos estado viviendo en Madrid durante cinco años. = We had been living in Madrid for five years. 我们在马德里住了五年。
Había estado trabajando en ese proyecto durante meses. = He had been working on that project for months. 他在这个项目上工作了好几个月。


一般将来时 (Simple Future):
No se usa haber como verbo principal en futuro simple. = Haber is not used as a main verb in the simple future. 将来时不用haber作为主动词。
Only used as auxiliary: Habré terminado para mañana. = I will have finished by tomorrow. 我明天之前会完成。


将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
No se usa haber como verbo principal en futuro continuo. = Haber is not used as a main verb in the future continuous.  将来进行时不用haber作为主动词。
Only used as auxiliary: Habremos estado trabajando en ello durante una semana. = We will have been working on it for a week.  我们将已经工作一周了.


将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
Habré leído el libro para el viernes. = I will have read the book by Friday. 我会在星期五前读完这本书。
Habrán terminado el proyecto para el final del mes. = They will have finished the project by the end of the month. 他们将在月底前完成这个项目。


将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
Habré estado estudiando español durante tres años. = I will have been studying Spanish for three years. 我学习西班牙语将有三年了。
Habrán estado viviendo aquí durante diez años. = They will have been living here for ten years. 他们将在这里住了十年。



PHRASE->
ha de + infinitive = must/has to  必须/不得不
habrá que + infinitive = it will be necessary to... 有必要...
no hay de qué = you're welcome 不客气
hay que + infinitive = one must/it is necessary to... 必须/有必要...
¿qué hay? = what's up? / what's new?  怎么了？/有什么新鲜事？


注意事项:
Haber as a main verb is archaic in modern Spanish and only survives in certain set phrases and as an auxiliary verb.  Haber作为主要动词在现代西班牙语中是古老的，只在某些固定短语中和作为助动词存在。
