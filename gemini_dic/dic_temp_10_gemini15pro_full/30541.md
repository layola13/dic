cephalopod:/ˈsefələpɒd/ (US) /ˈkɛfələpɒd/ (UK)| n. |头足纲动物|A marine mollusk of the class Cephalopoda, such as an octopus, squid, or cuttlefish, characterized by well-developed eyes, a ring of sucker-bearing tentacles around the mouth, and the ability to squirt ink.|例句：Octopuses are fascinating cephalopods known for their intelligence. 章鱼是迷人的头足纲动物，以其智慧而闻名。

例句：Many cephalopods are masters of camouflage, able to change their skin color and texture to blend in with their surroundings. 许多头足类动物是伪装大师，能够改变皮肤颜色和纹理以融入周围环境。|近义词：mollusk, invertebrate|反义词: vertebrate |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *kephalópoda*  (literally “head-feet”), from *kephalḗ* (“head”) + *poús* (“foot”).
