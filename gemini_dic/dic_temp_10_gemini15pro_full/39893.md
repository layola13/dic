constitutionalism:/ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəlɪzəm/ |n.|宪政主义；立宪主义|A system of government in which power is limited by law or a formal constitution. It emphasizes the rule of law, the separation of powers, and the protection of fundamental rights.|The country's commitment to constitutionalism ensured a peaceful transfer of power.|Synonyms: constitutional government, limited government|Antonyms: absolutism, dictatorship|Noun

constitutionality:/ˌkɒnstɪˌtjuːʃəˈnælɪti/ |n.|合宪性；符合宪法|The quality of being in accordance with a constitution.|The Supreme Court will rule on the constitutionality of the new law.|Synonyms: lawfulness, legality|Antonyms: unconstitutionality, illegality|Noun

constitutional:/ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl/ |adj.|宪法的；立宪的；本质的；体质上的|Relating to or authorized by the constitution of a country or organization; inherent in a person's nature or physical makeup.|The constitutional right to free speech is essential for a democratic society.|Synonyms: legal, legitimate, inherent, innate|Antonyms: unconstitutional, illegal, acquired|Adjective
