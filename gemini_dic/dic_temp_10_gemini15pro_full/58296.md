dry:/draɪ/ | adj. v. n. | 干的，干燥的；干涸的；口渴的；枯燥无味的；禁酒的 | Free from moisture or liquid; not wet or moist.  | The desert is a dry and barren landscape. 沙漠是一片干燥荒芜的景象。 | near: arid, parched, thirsty | ant: wet, damp, moist | adjective

dry:/draɪ/ | v. |  (使)变干，(使)干燥；(使)脱水；擦干 | To make or become free from moisture or liquid. |  Hang the clothes outside to dry. 把衣服挂在外面晾干。 | near: dehydrate, desiccate, parch | ant: wet, soak, drench | verb

dry:/draɪ/ | n. |  干旱；干燥地区 | A period of time without rain or with very little rain. |  The long dry has caused a lot of damage to crops. 长时间的干旱对庄稼造成了很大的损害。 | near: drought, aridity | ant: rainfall, wetness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *drȳge*, from Proto-Germanic *draugiz*, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰrewgʰ-*.  Cognate with Dutch *droog*, German *trocken*, Old Norse *draugr*.  The original sense was probably "lasting, enduring," with the notion of dryness developing later.
