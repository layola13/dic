n.|加入黑名单的人; 将…列入黑名单的人|People who add others to a blacklist, or who blacklist something.
The blacklisters refused to comment on their reasons for banning the journalist.|那些将记者列入黑名单的人拒绝评论他们这样做的原因。
近义词:banners, boycotters
反义词:supporters, endorsers

blacklister:/ˈblækˌlɪstər/
n.|加入黑名单的人; 将…列入黑名单的人|A person who puts someone or something on a blacklist.
The blacklister claimed the company was engaging in unethical practices.|将该公司列入黑名单的人声称该公司从事不道德的行为。
近义词:banner, boycotter
反义词:supporter, endorser
词性:名词


blacklist:/ˈblækˌlɪst/
vt. |将…列入黑名单|To put someone or something on a blacklist.
Several countries have blacklisted the organization for its terrorist activities.|几个国家已将该组织列入黑名单，因为它从事恐怖活动。
n.|黑名单|A list of people or things that are disapproved of or are to be avoided.
His name was on the blacklist, so he couldn't get a job.|他的名字在黑名单上，所以他找不到工作。
近义词:(v.) ban, boycott, bar; (n.) register of undesirables
反义词:(v.) support, endorse; (n.) whitelist
词性:动词, 名词

词源:
From "black" (meaning negative or undesirable) + "list" (a written or printed series of items).  The term originated in the 17th century, referring to lists of those considered disloyal or undesirable.
