carousal: /kəˈraʊzəl/ (US), /kəˈraʊzl/ (UK)| n.|旋转木马；狂欢；喧闹的宴会|A merry-go-round, a noisy revel, or a drunken feast.|例句：The children loved riding the carousal at the fair. 孩子们喜欢在游乐场里坐旋转木马。

例句：The office Christmas party was a wild carousal. 办公室的圣诞派对是一场疯狂的狂欢。|近义词：merry-go-round, revelry, spree, binge|反义词:quiet, calm, peace|词性:noun

carouse: /kəˈraʊz/ (US), /kəˈraʊz/ (UK)| v. |畅饮；狂欢|To engage in a noisy or drunken revel.|例句：They caroused until the early hours of the morning. 他们狂欢作乐到凌晨。

例句：The sailors caroused in the taverns after months at sea.  在海上航行了几个月后，水手们在酒馆里狂欢。|近义词：revel, celebrate, party|反义词:abstain, refrain|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *carrousel*, from Italian *carosello*, literally "little war," from *carro* ("chariot, cart") and a diminutive suffix. The original meaning referred to a military tournament or exercise, evolving later to describe a festive procession and finally the amusement ride.
