mispassion:/ˌmɪsˈpæʃən/
n.
误导的热情；错误的激情
A strong feeling or enthusiasm directed towards something inappropriate or unworthy.
His mispassion for gambling led him to financial ruin. 他对赌博的误导热情导致他财务破产。
近义词: infatuation, obsession, delusion
反义词: apathy, indifference, detachment
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (wrong, bad) + passion (strong feeling or enthusiasm).  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old English "mis-", meaning "wrong, bad, astray," from Proto-Germanic "*missa-", from PIE "*mei-s-" (wrong, bad).  "Passion" comes from Old French "passion" (suffering, Christ's Passion), from Latin "passionem" (suffering, enduring), from "pati" (to suffer, endure).
