bromogelatin:/ˌbroʊmoʊˈdʒɛlətɪn/
n.
溴化明胶,溴化银明胶
A photographic emulsion of silver bromide suspended in gelatin.  It was widely used in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
The photographer used bromogelatin paper for his portraits.  这位摄影师用溴化银明胶纸来拍摄肖像。
近义词:silver gelatin
反义词: (No direct antonym exists as it refers to a specific photographic process.)
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *bromo-* (combining form meaning "bromine") + *gelatin*.  Bromine, a chemical element, is named from Greek *brōmos* "stench" because of its strong and unpleasant smell. Gelatin is derived from Latin *gelāre* "to freeze".
