antiassimilation:/ˌæntiəˌsɪməˈleɪʃn/
n.|反同化，反同化作用|The process or policy of resisting assimilation, especially cultural or racial assimilation.|Many immigrant groups practiced antiassimilation to preserve their cultural identity. 许多移民群体实行反同化以保留其文化认同。|近义词：anti-integration, segregation|反义词：assimilation, integration|词性：名词

Etymology:
anti- (prefix): from Greek anti- "against, opposite of, instead of, in return"
assimilation (noun): from Late Latin assimilationem (nominative assimilatio) "a making similar," noun of action from past-participle stem of assimilare "to make similar," from ad- "to" (see ad-) + similis "like" (see similar).
