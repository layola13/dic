obeisance:/oʊˈbeɪsəns/ /əˈbiːsəns/ | n. | 敬礼，鞠躬，尊崇 | a gesture expressing deference or respect, such as a bow or curtsy |  The knight made a deep obeisance to the queen. 骑士向女王深深鞠了一躬。| synonyms: bow, curtsy, salute, homage, reverence | antonyms: disrespect, irreverence, disdain | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *obeissance "obedience, submission," from obeir "to obey" (see obey).  The sense shifted from "obedience" to "a gesture of obedience or submission" (mid-15c.).
