brandisher:/ˈbrændɪʃər/| n.|挥舞者；炫耀者|Someone who brandishes something, typically a weapon.|The knight was a skilled brandisher of the sword.  这位骑士是一位熟练的剑术家。|Synonyms: wielder, waver, flourisher|Antonyms: concealer, hider|noun

Etymology->
From Middle English *brandisshen*, from Old French *brandir*, *brandissant* (“to brandish, shake, flourish”), of Germanic origin.
