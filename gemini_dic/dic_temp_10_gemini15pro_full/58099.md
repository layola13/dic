drop-the-ball:/ˌdrɒp ðə ˈbɔːl/ (British), /ˌdrɑːp ðə ˈbɔːl/ (American)| idiom (verb phrase)|失职，犯错，搞砸|To fail to do something that you are responsible for; to make a mistake that causes something to fail.|例句：He really dropped the ball when he forgot to book the flights. 他忘记预订航班，真是搞砸了。

例句：The team dropped the ball and lost the championship game.  团队失误，输掉了冠军赛。|近义词：mess up, screw up, blunder, fumble, fail, flub|反义词：succeed, excel, come through, perform well|词性:idiom (verb phrase)

drop-the-ball:/ˌdrɒp ðə ˈbɔːl/ (British), /ˌdrɑːp ðə ˈbɔːl/ (American)| n.|失误，错误|An instance of failing to do something that you are responsible for.|例句：That was a major drop of the ball on their part. 那是他们方面的重大失误。


例句：Losing that client was a real drop the ball for the company. 失去那个客户对公司来说是一个真正的失误。|近义词：mistake, error, blunder, failure|反义词：success, achievement, triumph|词性:noun (derived from the idiom)
