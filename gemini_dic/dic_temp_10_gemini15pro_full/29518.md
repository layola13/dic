catastrophe:/kəˈtæstrəfi/ (US), /kəˈtɑːstrəfi/ (UK)| n.|灾难，灾祸|A sudden event that causes very great trouble or destruction.|The earthquake was a catastrophe for the region.  这场地震对该地区来说是一场灾难。|The hurricane was a major catastrophe. 这场飓风是一场重大灾难。|近义词：disaster, calamity, cataclysm|反义词:triumph, success|词性:名词

catastrophic:/ˌkætəˈstrɒfɪk/ (US), /ˌkætəˈstrɒfɪk/ (UK)| adj.|灾难性的，毁灭性的|Involving or causing a sudden great disaster.|The oil spill had catastrophic consequences for the environment. 石油泄漏对环境造成了灾难性的后果。|The economic crisis was catastrophic for many businesses. 经济危机对许多企业来说是灾难性的。|近义词：disastrous, calamitous, devastating|反义词:fortunate, beneficial|词性:形容词

词源:
From French *catastrophe*, from Late Latin catastropha, from Ancient Greek καταστροφή (katastrophḗ, “overturning, sudden turn, a down-turn, end, conclusion”), from καταστρέφω (katastréphō, “I overturn”), from κατά (katá, “down”) + στρέφω (stréphō, “I turn”).
