cowardice: /ˈkaʊərdɪs/ | n. | 懦弱，胆怯 | Lack of courage to face danger, difficulty, opposition, pain, etc. | He showed cowardice in the face of the enemy.  他在敌人面前表现出懦弱。| Synonyms: timidity, fearfulness, pusillanimity | Antonyms: bravery, courage, valor | noun

Etymology: From Middle English *cowardyse, from Old French couardise, from couard (“coward”), from coe (“tail”), from Latin cauda (“tail”), referring to an animal with its tail between its legs.
