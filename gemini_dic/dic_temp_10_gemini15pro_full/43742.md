creep:/kriːp/| v. n.|爬行；蔓延；卑鄙小人|To move slowly and quietly, especially in order to avoid being noticed; to gradually spread or increase; an unpleasant person who tries to make someone like them by being insincerely nice.|例句：The ivy crept up the wall. 常春藤爬上了墙。|近义词：crawl, slither, sneak, toady|反义词:bolt, dash, stride|词性:verb, noun

creep:/kriːp/| n.|令人毛骨悚然的感觉；卑鄙小人|A feeling of fear and disgust; an unpleasant person who tries to make someone like them by being insincerely nice.|例句：He gives me the creeps. 他让我毛骨悚然。|近义词：shivers, goosebumps, weirdo, jerk|反义词:hero, friend|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *crēopan, from Proto-Germanic *kreupaną (“to creep”), from Proto-Indo-European *gʰrebʰ- (“to seize, grasp, grab”). Cognate with Dutch kruipen (“to creep”), German kriechen (“to crawl, creep”), Swedish krypa (“to crawl”), Lithuanian grėbti (“to rake, grab”).
