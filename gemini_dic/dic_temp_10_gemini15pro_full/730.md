absinth: /ˈæbsɪnθ/ | n. | 苦艾酒 | A green, anise-flavored liqueur containing wormwood. It is traditionally high in alcohol content and was popular in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, particularly in France. |  He ordered a glass of absinthe. 他点了一杯苦艾酒。 |  Near synonyms:  vermouth, pastis | Antonyms: None (specific drinks don't usually have antonyms) | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *absinthe*, from Latin *absinthium*, from Ancient Greek *ἀψίνθιον* (apsínthion), meaning "wormwood".  The word ultimately derives from a reconstructed Proto-Indo-European root *h₂epsin-*, meaning "unpleasant" or "bitter," referring to the bitter taste of the wormwood plant.
