bequeath:/bɪˈkwiːð/ /bɪˈkwiːθ/| vt.|遗赠；传留|To leave (property) to someone by means of a will. To pass something on (e.g., a tradition, quality, or problem) to a person or other beneficiary who will survive after one's own death. |He bequeathed his entire estate to his daughter. 他把全部财产遗赠给了他的女儿。|近义词：leave, will, bestow, impart|反义词:inherit, receive|词性:动词

bequeathed:/bɪˈkwiːðd/ /bɪˈkwiːθt/| vt. (past participle)|被遗赠的；被传留的|Past participle of bequeath.  Indicates something has been left to someone through a will or passed down. |The antique clock was bequeathed to her by her grandmother.  这个古董钟是她祖母遗赠给她的。|近义词：left, willed, bestowed, imparted|反义词:inherited, received|词性:动词
