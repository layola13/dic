oracus: /ˈɔːrəkəs/ | n. | 神谕，神示，圣言 |  A divine communication or revelation, often ambiguous or obscure.  Derived from Latin *oraculum*, meaning "oracle," ultimately from *orare*, "to speak, pray." | The priestess delivered the oracus from the god. 女祭司传达了神的神谕。 | synonyms: prophecy, prediction, revelation | antonyms:  silence, suppression, concealment | noun
