hurricane:/ˈhɜːrɪkeɪn/ (US) /ˈhʌrɪkən/ (UK)| n. |飓风，暴风|A violent, tropical, cyclonic storm of the western North Atlantic, having wind speeds of or in excess of 74 miles per hour (119 kilometers per hour).|The hurricane devastated the coastal region. 飓风摧毁了沿海地区。|近义词：cyclone, typhoon, storm|反义词:calm, breeze|词性:名词

hurricane:/ˈhɜːrɪkeɪn/ (US) /ˈhʌrɪkən/ (UK)| vt. |以飓风的速度或力量猛烈袭击|The wind hurricaned the ship against the rocks. 狂风使船撞上岩石。|近义词：batter, lash, ravage|反义词:protect, shelter|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *huracán*, from Taino (Arawakan language family) *hurakán* ("god of the storm").
