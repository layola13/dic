emanatist:/ˈɛmənətɪst/
noun
流溢说信奉者；流出论者
A believer in emanationism, the doctrine that the universe is derived from a divine source by a process of emanation.
The Neoplatonists were emanatists, believing that the world flowed out from the One. 新柏拉图主义者是流溢论者，他们相信世界是从“太一”流出来的。
近义词:monist, idealist
反义词:materialist
词性:n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin emanatista, from emanare "to flow out" (from ex- "out" + manare "to flow").
