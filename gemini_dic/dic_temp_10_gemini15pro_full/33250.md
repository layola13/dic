chronophotograph:/ˈkrɒnəʊˈfəʊtəɡrɑːf/ (UK) /ˌkroʊnoʊˈfoʊtəɡræf/ (US)| n. |时间序列照片，连续摄影照片|A photograph that records several phases of movement in one print, especially one made before cinematography was invented.|Example:  The museum exhibited a series of chronophotographs showing a horse galloping. 博物馆展出了一系列显示马匹奔跑的连续摄影照片。

Example:  Muybridge's chronophotographs proved that all four hooves of a horse leave the ground at some point during a gallop. 迈布里奇的连续摄影照片证明，马在奔跑过程中，所有四个蹄子都会在某个时刻离开地面。|近义词:sequence photograph, series photograph|反义词:None|词性:noun

chronophotographic:/ˌkrɒnəʊˌfəʊtəˈɡræfɪk/ (UK) /ˌkroʊnoʊˌfoʊtəˈɡræfɪk/ (US)| adj. |时间序列摄影的，连续摄影的|Relating to or produced by chronophotography.|Example:  He used a chronophotographic gun to capture the bird's flight. 他使用了一把连续摄影枪来捕捉鸟的飞行。

Example:  The chronophotographic images revealed the subtle movements of the dancer. 连续摄影图像揭示了舞者微妙的动作。|近义词:sequence photographic, serial photographic|反义词:None|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From chrono- + photograph. Chrono- comes from Greek khronos "time". Photograph comes from Greek phōs "light" + graphē "drawing, writing, record".
