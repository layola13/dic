disjointness:/dɪsˈdʒɔɪntnəs/| n. |不相交性，互斥性|The state of being disjoint; having no elements in common.|The disjointness of the two sets is evident from the lack of overlap in their Venn diagram. 这两个集合的不相交性从它们的维恩图中没有重叠部分可以明显看出。|近义词：separation, disunion, disconnection|反义词：intersection, overlap, union|词性:名词

Etymology: From disjoint + -ness.  Disjoint comes from the Old French desjoindre, meaning "to disjoin," from des- (expressing negation) + joindre (to join), ultimately from Latin jungere (to join).
