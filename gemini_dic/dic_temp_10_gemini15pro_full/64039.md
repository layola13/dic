epithet:/ˈɛpɪθɛt/| n. |绰号，称号，修饰语|An epithet is a descriptive word or phrase used to characterize a person or thing, often suggesting a quality or attribute considered typical or striking.  It can be positive, negative, or neutral.|Richard the Lionheart is an epithet for King Richard I.  狮心王理查是理查一世国王的称号。|synonyms: appellation, sobriquet, nickname, moniker, label, tag, designation|antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, as the presence or absence of an epithet is the key difference.  One might consider "anonymity" or "namelessness" as conceptually opposite.)|noun
