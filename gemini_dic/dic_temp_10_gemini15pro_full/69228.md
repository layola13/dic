fearsome:/ˈfɪərsəm/ | adj. | 可怕的，令人畏惧的 | Causing fear; frightening; terrifying. |  The dragon was a fearsome beast, its scales shimmering like a thousand emeralds. 这条龙是一个可怕的野兽，它的鳞片像一千颗祖母绿一样闪闪发光。

The warrior faced the fearsome enemy with unwavering courage.  这位战士以毫不动摇的勇气面对可怕的敌人。 | Synonyms: terrifying, frightful, dreadful, intimidating, menacing | Antonyms: comforting, reassuring, harmless, innocuous | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fersum*, from Old English *fēarsum* (“causing fear, terrible”), from *fēar* (“fear, danger”) + *-sum* (suffix forming adjectives meaning “characterized by, full of”). Cognate with West Frisian *fearsum* (“terrible, awful”), Dutch *vreeslijk* (“terrible, awful”), German *furchtbar* (“terrible, awful”).
