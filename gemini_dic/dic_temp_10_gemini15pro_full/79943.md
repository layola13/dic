gnostic: /ˈnɒstɪk/ (UK), /ˈnɑːstɪk/ (US) | adj. n. |  诺斯替的；诺斯替教徒 | Relating to knowledge, especially esoteric or mystical knowledge.  A person who believes that personal spiritual knowledge is more important than established doctrine and religious practice. |  例句：He held a gnostic view of the universe. 他对宇宙持有诺斯替的观点。

例句：She considered herself a gnostic, seeking enlightenment through inner knowledge. 她认为自己是一个诺斯替教徒，通过内在知识寻求启迪。 | 近义词：esoteric, mystical, occult | 反义词：exoteric, conventional, orthodox | 词性: adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *gnosticus*, from Greek gnōstikos "knowing, good at knowing," from gignōskein "to know."
