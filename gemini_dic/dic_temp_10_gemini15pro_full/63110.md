ensign:/ˈɛnsaɪn (US), ˈɛnsən (UK)| n.|旗帜; 徽章; 海军少尉|A flag or banner, especially a naval or military one. Also, a junior officer in the US Navy or Coast Guard.|The ship flew the ensign of the United States. 这艘船悬挂着美国国旗。|近义词：flag, banner, standard, pennant, colors (US), colours (UK), lieutenant (for rank)|反义词:N/A|词性:noun

ensign:/ˈɛnsaɪn (US), ˈɛnsən (UK)| n.|海军少尉|A junior officer in the US Navy or Coast Guard, ranking next below a lieutenant junior grade.|He was commissioned as an ensign after graduating from the Naval Academy. 他从海军学院毕业后被委任为海军少尉。|近义词：lieutenant (junior grade)|反义词:N/A|词性:noun

词源：From Old French *enseigne (Modern French enseigne), from Latin insignia, plural of insigne "distinguishing mark, emblem," neuter of insignis "remarkable, distinguished," from in- "in, on" + signum "mark, sign."


注意事项:  As noted above, the pronunciation and meaning differ slightly between US and UK usage.  In the US, the word primarily refers to a flag and secondarily to a naval officer rank.  In the UK, it primarily refers to a naval officer and secondarily to a flag.
