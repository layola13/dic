depravity:/dɪˈprævəti/| n. |堕落，腐败，邪恶|Moral depravity is the corruption of morality or virtue.  It often involves a deviation from what is considered right or proper behavior.|The city was known for its depravity and vice.  这座城市以其堕落和邪恶而闻名。|Synonyms: corruption, wickedness, perversion, immorality, debauchery|Antonyms: virtue, goodness, morality, righteousness|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dēprāvitās, from Latin dēprāvātus (past participle of dēprāvāre “to corrupt, pervert”), from dē- (thoroughly) + prāvus (“crooked, perverse”).
