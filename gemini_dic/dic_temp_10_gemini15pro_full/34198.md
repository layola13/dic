clams:/klæmz/ | n. | 蛤蜊; 沉默; 守口如瓶; 钳子（俚语） |  A type of bivalve mollusk, often eaten as seafood. Also can refer to money (slang) or keeping quiet (slang).| We dug for clams on the beach. 我们在海滩上挖蛤蜊。|Synonyms: shellfish, bivalves, mollusks (for the sea creature); money, cash, dough (slang); silence, reticence (for keeping quiet) | Antonyms:  (no direct opposite for the sea creature);  spendthrift, extravagance (for money, slang);  loquaciousness,  garrulity (for keeping quiet)| noun

clams:/klæmz/ | v. | 闭嘴；保持沉默 （俚语） | To become silent; to refuse to speak. | "Clam up!" he hissed. “闭嘴！”他嘶嘶地说。| Synonyms: shut up, be quiet, hush | Antonyms: speak up, talk, chatter | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *clamm, from Proto-Germanic *klammaz, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *(s)klei- (“to stick, adhere”). Related to clay. The slang meanings for "money" and "silence" are of uncertain origin.
