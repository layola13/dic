insinuator:/ɪnˈsɪnjuˌeɪtər/| n. |暗讽者；造谣者；中谗者|Someone who insinuates; someone who makes indirect or subtle suggestions, especially of a harmful or unpleasant kind.|He was known as an insinuator, always planting seeds of doubt in people's minds. 他被称为一个造谣者，总是在人们心中播下怀疑的种子。|Synonyms:  backbiter, slanderer, gossipmonger, detracter |Antonyms:  supporter, defender, advocate|noun

insinuate:/ɪnˈsɪnjuˌeɪt/| v. |暗示；含沙射影地说；迂回地潜入|To suggest or hint (something bad or reprehensible) in an indirect and unpleasant way.|She insinuated that he was dishonest. 她暗示他不诚实。|Synonyms:  imply, suggest, intimate, hint|Antonyms:  declare, state, assert|verb
