dressed: /drɛst/ | adj. | 穿着衣服的，打扮好的 |  wearing clothes, especially a particular kind of clothes | She was dressed in a blue silk dress. 她穿着一件蓝色丝绸连衣裙。 |  clothed, attired, garbed | naked, undressed | adjective

dressed: /drɛst/ | v. (past tense and past participle of dress) |  给…穿衣；打扮 | put clothes on oneself or someone else |  He dressed quickly and went out. 他迅速穿好衣服出去了。 | clothe, attire, garb | undress, disrobe | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *dresser (Modern French dresser), "to arrange, straighten, prepare," from Vulgar Latin *directiare, frequentative of Latin dirigere, "to direct, guide, straighten," from dis-, "apart, asunder" + regere, "to direct, guide, rule." The sense of "to put clothes on" developed from the idea of arranging or preparing oneself.
