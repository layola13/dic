loft:/lɒft/ (UK) /lɔːft/ (US)| n.  v.|阁楼；顶楼；高尔夫球杆的杆面斜度；|A room or space directly under the roof of a house or other building, often used for storage or as living space.  To hit or propel (a ball) high in the air.|例句：We converted the loft into a bedroom. 我们把阁楼改成了一间卧室。|近义词：attic, garret|反义词:basement, cellar|词性:名词, 动词

loft:/lɒft/ (UK) /lɔːft/ (US)| v.|击高球；使高飞|To hit or propel (a ball) high in the air. |例句：He lofted the ball over the infield. 他把球高高地打过了内场。|近义词：chip, lob|反义词:ground|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *lopt, meaning "air, sky," related to Old English lyft "air, sky."  Originally referred to the upper story of a building, open to the roof.
