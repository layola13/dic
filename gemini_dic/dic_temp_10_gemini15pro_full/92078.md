hyperrationalities: /ˌhaɪpərˌræʃəˈnælɪtiz/ | n. | 超理性，过度理性 |  The state or quality of being excessively or impractically rational, often to the detriment of other considerations like emotions, intuition, or social context.  It suggests a focus on logic and optimization taken to an extreme. |  His hyperrationalities led him to make decisions that, while logically sound, lacked empathy and ultimately alienated his colleagues. 他的过度理性导致他做出的决定虽然逻辑上合理，但却缺乏同理心，最终疏远了他的同事。 |  The company's hyperrationalities prioritized profit maximization above all else, even employee well-being.  这家公司的超理性将利润最大化置于一切之上，甚至高于员工福祉。 | 近义词: over-rationality, excessive rationality | 反义词: irrationality, emotionalism | 词性: noun (plural)

Etymology:
From hyper- (prefix meaning "excessive," "above," from Greek *hupér) + rationality (from rational + -ity).
