outmanoeuvring:/ˌaʊtməˈnuːvərɪŋ/
|v.|用计胜过，智取|The company successfully outmanoeuvred its competitors by launching a new product first. 这家公司通过率先推出新产品成功地胜过了竞争对手。|例句：The chess player skillfully outmanoeuvred his opponent.  这位棋手巧妙地用计胜过了他的对手。

|例句：The general outmanoeuvred the enemy army and forced them to retreat.  将军智取敌军，迫使他们撤退。
|近义词：outwit, outsmart, surpass, circumvent|反义词：be outmanoeuvred, be outsmarted|词性：动词

outmanoeuvre:/ˌaʊtməˈnuːvər/
|v.|用计胜过，智取|To cleverly position oneself to gain an advantage over someone else.  巧妙地定位自己以获得优于其他人的优势。|例句：The politician outmanoeuvred his rivals and won the election.  这位政治家用计胜过对手，赢得了选举。

|例句：In the negotiations, she skillfully outmanoeuvred the other party and secured a better deal. 在谈判中，她巧妙地胜过了对方，并达成了更好的协议。
|近义词：outwit, outsmart, surpass, circumvent|反义词：be outmanoeuvred, be outsmarted|词性：动词


词源:
From out- + manoeuvre.  源自 out- + manoeuvre（策略，调动）。"Out-" 表示超过或胜过。
