co-estate:/koʊɪˈsteɪt/
n.
共同财产；共同产业；共同不动产
A co-estate is property owned jointly by two or more people.
The siblings inherited the co-estate from their parents. 这对兄弟姐妹从父母那里继承了共同财产。
The co-estate was divided equally among the heirs. 共同财产被继承人平分了。
近义词: joint property, joint ownership, shared estate
反义词: separate property, individual ownership
词性: 名词

Etymology:
From "co-" (together, joint) + "estate" (property, land).  "Co-" comes from the Latin prefix "com-" (with, together), and "estate" comes from Old French "estat" (state, condition), ultimately from Latin "status" (standing, condition).
