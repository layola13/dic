blitheful: /ˈblaɪθfəl/ | adj. | 愉快的；快乐的；无忧无虑的 | Showing a casual and cheerful indifference considered to be callous or improper. |  He spoke with blitheful ignorance of the true situation. 他愉快地说着，完全不知道真实的情况。 |  近义词: cheerful, merry, joyous, lighthearted, carefree | 反义词: sorrowful, unhappy, miserable, depressed, anxious | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From blithe + -ful.  Blithe itself comes from Old English blíðe "joyous, kind, friendly, gracious, merciful," from Proto-Germanic *blithiz (source also of Old Saxon blithi, Old Norse blíðr, Old Frisian blithe, Middle Dutch blide, Dutch blijde "joyful," Gothic bleiths "merciful").
