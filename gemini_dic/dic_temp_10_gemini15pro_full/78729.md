giggles: /ˈɡɪɡəlz/ | n. |咯咯笑，傻笑|A series of short, high-pitched laughs, often associated with amusement or nervousness.| The child's giggles filled the room.  孩子的咯咯笑声充满了整个房间。|Synonyms: chuckles, titters, laughs|Antonyms: sobs, cries|noun

giggle: /ˈɡɪɡəl/ | v. |咯咯地笑，傻笑|To laugh lightly in a series of short, high-pitched sounds.|  She giggled at his joke. 她听了他的笑话咯咯地笑了。|Synonyms: chuckle, titter, laugh softly|Antonyms: sob, cry, wail|verb

giggly: /ˈɡɪɡli/ | adj. |爱傻笑的|Inclined to giggle; characterized by giggling.| The giggly girls couldn't stop laughing.  这些爱傻笑的女孩们笑个不停。|Synonyms: lighthearted, merry, mirthful|Antonyms: serious, somber, grave|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gigelen*, frequentative of  *gigen*, of imitative origin.  Related to Old Norse *geggja* (to cackle).
