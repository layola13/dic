catalyze:/ˈkætəlaɪz/ (US), /ˈkætəlaɪz/ (UK)| v. |催化，促进|to cause or accelerate (a reaction) by acting as a catalyst.|The presence of platinum catalyzes the reaction. 铂的存在催化了反应。|近义词：accelerate, expedite, facilitate, promote, stimulate|反义词：inhibit, hinder, obstruct|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *catalyser*, from Greek *katalýein* "to dissolve," from *kata-* "down" + *lýein* "to loosen."  The modern scientific meaning was introduced by Jöns Jakob Berzelius in 1835.
