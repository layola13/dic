clankety-clank:/klæŋkɪti klæŋk/
| adv.  adj. |叮当作响，哐啷哐啷|A reduplicative word imitating the sound of metallic objects hitting each other.|The old train moved along the tracks, clankety-clank, clankety-clank. 老火车沿着铁轨行驶，发出哐啷哐啷的声音。|clanging, rattling, jangling|silent, quiet|adverb/adjective
clankety-clank:/klæŋkɪti klæŋk/
| n. |叮当声，哐啷声|The sound of metallic objects hitting each other, often repeated.|The clankety-clank of the machinery was deafening.  机器的哐啷声震耳欲聋。|clanging, rattling, jangling|silence, quiet|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the imitative word "clank,"  which likely originated in the late 16th century. The reduplication "clankety-clank" emphasizes the repeated nature of the sound.
