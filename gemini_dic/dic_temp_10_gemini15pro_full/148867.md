overween:/ˌoʊvərˈwiːn/| vi.|自负，傲慢，过于自信|To be presumptuously or excessively confident; arrogant.|He overweened himself by boasting about his skills before the competition. 他在比赛前吹嘘自己的技能，显得过于自负。|近义词：arrogant, haughty, presumptuous, conceited|反义词：humble, modest, unassuming|verb

词源:
From Middle English *overwenen*, from Old English *oferwēnan* (“to be arrogant, presume, be overconfident”), from *ofer* (“over, above”) + *wēnan* (“to suppose, expect, hope”).
