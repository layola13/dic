derogate:/ˈdɛrəˌɡeɪt/ /ˈdɪrəɡeɪt/| v. | 贬低，损害，减损 | to belittle or detract from something; to take away a part, as from authority or privilege |  His actions derogated from his authority. 他的行为损害了他的权威。

derogate:/ˈdɛrəˌɡeɪt/ /ˈdɪrəɡeɪt/| v. | 违背，背离（法律、习俗等）| to deviate from a rule or law |  They derogated from accepted practice. 他们违背了公认的做法。


derogatory:/dɪˈrɒɡət(ə)ri/ /dɪˈrɑːɡətɔːri/| adj. | 贬低的，诽谤的 | tending to lessen the merit or reputation of a person or thing; disparaging |  She made some derogatory remarks about her colleagues. 她对她的同事发表了一些贬低的言论。

词源: Late Middle English: from Latin *derogatus, past participle of derogare ‘repeal (part of a law), detract from’, from de- ‘away’ + rogare ‘ask, propose (a law)’.

近义词: detract, diminish, belittle, disparage, denigrate
反义词: enhance, elevate, praise, commend, extol
词性: verb (derogate), adjective (derogatory)
