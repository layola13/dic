acquitting: /əˈkwɪtɪŋ/
verb
宣告无罪；表现；履行职责
The act of freeing someone from a criminal charge by a verdict of not guilty.  Also, conducting oneself or performing in a specified way.
The jury is acquitting the defendant of all charges. 陪审团宣判被告所有罪名不成立。

He acquitted himself well in his first job interview. 他在第一次求职面试中表现出色。

近义词: exonerating, absolving, clearing, vindicating
反义词: convicting, condemning, incriminating
词性: Verb (present participle of acquit)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French aquiter, from a- (from Latin ad 'to') + quite 'free, clear' (from Latin quietus 'at rest, undisturbed').
