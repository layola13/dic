amphigory: /ˈæmfɪɡɔːri/ (US), /ˈæmfɪɡəri/ (UK) | n. |  滑稽古怪的故事；荒诞不经的诗文；胡言乱语 | A nonsensical piece of writing or speech, typically one that is meant to be humorous or satirical.  It often involves a mixture of fantastical elements, wordplay, and illogical connections. |  The author's latest novel is a delightful amphigory, full of whimsical characters and absurd situations.  作者最新的小说是一部令人愉快的滑稽古怪的故事，充满了奇异的角色和荒谬的情境。

The comedian's routine was a hilarious amphigory of puns and non-sequiturs.  这位喜剧演员的表演是一连串滑稽的双关语和不合逻辑的妙语。

近义词: nonsense, gibberish, balderdash, doggerel | 反义词: sense, logic, rationality | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *amphigouri*, from Italian *anfìguri*, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Greek *amphi* ("around, on both sides") and *gyros* ("circle, ring").  This suggests the idea of something going around in circles without making sense.
