fared:/fɛərd/ | v. |  进展；过活；遭遇 |  To experience a particular situation or outcome.  |  He fared well in the competition. 他在比赛中表现出色。

The company fared badly during the recession.  这家公司在经济衰退期间经营状况很差。 | 近义词: progressed, managed, performed | 反义词: failed, struggled | 词性: 动词

词源:  From Middle English *faren, from Old English faran (“to go, travel, journey, fare”), from Proto-Germanic *faraną (“to travel, go, fare”), from Proto-Indo-European *per- (“to lead, pass over”). Cognate with Scots fare (“to go, travel, get along, do”), West Frisian farre (“to go, travel”), Dutch varen (“to sail, travel”), German fahren (“to drive, travel, go”), Danish fare (“to travel, go, happen, fare”), Swedish fara (“to travel, go, fare, rush”), Icelandic fara (“to go, travel, fare”).
