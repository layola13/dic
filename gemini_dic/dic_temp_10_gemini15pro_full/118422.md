merrymakings:/ˈmɛriˌmeɪkɪŋz/| n. |欢乐的活动，庆祝活动|Festive activities, celebrations; often involving eating, drinking, and entertainment.|The town's annual merrymakings include a parade, fireworks, and a street fair.  这个小镇的年度庆祝活动包括游行、烟花和街头集市。|Synonyms: festivities, celebrations, revelries, carousing|Antonyms: mourning, grieving|noun

merrymakings:/ˈmɛriˌmeɪkɪŋz/| n. |欢乐的气氛，热闹的场面|A scene of lively and cheerful activity.|The merrymakings continued late into the night.  欢乐的气氛一直持续到深夜。|Synonyms: gaiety, mirth, revelry|Antonyms: gloom, sadness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *merymaking*, from *mery* (merry) + *making* (making).  The word "merry" itself comes from Old English *myrge*, meaning "pleasant, agreeable," ultimately from Proto-Germanic *murgijaz*.
