antifeudalist:/ˌæntiˈfjuːdəlɪst/

词源:  anti- (against) + feudalist. "Anti-" comes from Greek and means "against" or "opposite". "Feudalist" refers to a person who supports or is part of a feudal system.  Feudal systems were hierarchical social structures prevalent in medieval Europe, characterized by land ownership and reciprocal obligations between lords and vassals.
