contraindicated:/ˌkɒntrəˈɪndɪkeɪtɪd/ (US) /ˌkɑːntrəˈɪndɪkeɪtɪd/| adj.|禁忌的，不适宜的|Something that is contraindicated is not advisable or is forbidden because it is likely to be harmful.  This term is typically used in a medical context.|例句：The drug is contraindicated in patients with liver disease.  这种药物对肝病患者是禁忌的。|近义词：inadvisable, unsuitable, inappropriate, proscribed|反义词：indicated, advisable, recommended|词性:adjective

contraindication:/ˌkɒntrəˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃn/ (US) /ˌkɑːntrəˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃn/| n.|禁忌症|A contraindication is a specific situation in which a drug, procedure, or surgery should not be used because it may be harmful to the person.|例句：A history of blood clots is a contraindication to this medication.  有血栓病史是服用这种药物的禁忌症。|近义词：objection, reason against|反义词：indication|词性:noun
