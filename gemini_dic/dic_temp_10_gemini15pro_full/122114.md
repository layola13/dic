mistradition:/ˌmɪstrəˈdɪʃən/
n.
错误的传统；谬传
An inherited or customary practice, belief, story, etc., that is not based on fact or sound evidence.
Many mistraditions surround the origins of this ancient festival.  许多谬传围绕着这个古老节日的起源。
近义词: fallacy, myth, misconception, error
反义词: truth, fact, accuracy
词性: noun

Etymology:
From mis- + tradition.  "Mis-" is a prefix meaning "wrong" or "badly". "Tradition" comes from the Latin "traditio", meaning "handing over" or "delivery".
