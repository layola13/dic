hil:/hɪl/ | n. | 小山 | A small hill; a mound. |  We climbed the hil to get a better view. 我们爬上小山，以便更好地观看。 | synonyms: mound, knoll, hillock | antonyms: valley, plain | noun

Etymology: From Middle English *hil*, from Old English *hyll*, from Proto-Germanic *hulniz* (“hill, elevation”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱel- (“to rise, be elevated, hill”). Cognate with Scots *hill*, West Frisian *hil*, Dutch *heuvel*, German *Hügel*, Danish *høj*, Swedish *hög*, Icelandic *hæð* (“height”).
