glad-hander:/ˈɡlædˌhændər/ | n. | 热情握手者；虚情假意的人 |  A person who shakes hands frequently and enthusiastically, especially in an insincere or overly effusive way, often to gain favor or popularity. | He's such a glad-hander, always trying to impress everyone. 他真是个虚情假意的人，总是试图给每个人留下好印象。| The politician worked the room, a practiced glad-hander, greeting each person by name.  这位政客在房间里来回走动，像个老练的热情握手者，和每个人都打招呼并叫出他们的名字。|  Synonyms: schmoozer, backslapper | Antonyms: introvert, recluse | Noun

glad-handing:/ˈɡlædˌhændɪŋ/ | n. |  热情握手；虚情假意的行为 | The act of shaking hands frequently and enthusiastically, especially in an insincere or overly effusive way. |  There was a lot of glad-handing at the political rally.  在政治集会上，有很多虚情假意的握手。| I'm tired of all the glad-handing in this industry. 我厌倦了这个行业里所有的虚情假意。| Synonyms: schmoozing, networking (sometimes) | Antonyms: aloofness, reserve | Noun

glad-hand:/ˈɡlædˌhænd/ | v. | 热情握手；虚情假意地对待 | To shake hands with someone frequently and enthusiastically, especially in an insincere or overly effusive way. | He glad-handed his way through the crowd. 他热情地和人群中的每个人握手。|  She felt glad-handed by the salesman, who seemed more interested in making a sale than in her actual needs.  她觉得那个销售员对她很虚情假意，他似乎更感兴趣的是销售，而不是她的实际需求。| Synonyms: schmooze, butter up | Antonyms: ignore, snub | Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "glad" (happy) + "hand" (referring to the act of shaking hands), implying a superficially joyful greeting.  First recorded in the early 20th century, particularly in American English, in the context of politics and business.
