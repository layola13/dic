ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *inkel, from Old French *ingle, from Frankish *ingil, from Proto-Germanic *angilō (“hook, angle”).  Cognate with Old High German angul, angil (“hook, angle”), Old English angel (“hook, fishhook”), Old Norse angull.  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂enk- (“to bend, curve”). The sense development from "hook" to "linen thread" is unclear.
