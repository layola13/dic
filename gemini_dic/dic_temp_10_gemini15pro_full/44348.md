cross-beak:/ˈkrɒsˌbiːk/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌbiːk/ (UK)| n. | 交喙鸟 | A bird, especially a crossbill, with mandibles that cross at the tips. |  The cross-beak used its specialized bill to pry open pine cones. 交喙鸟用它特殊的喙撬开松果。| 近义词: crossbill | 反义词:  (No common antonym) | 词性: noun


Etymology:
From 'cross' + 'beak'. 'Cross' refers to the crossed mandibles, and 'beak' is the bird's bill.  来源于 'cross' + 'beak'。 'cross' 指的是交叉的喙，'beak' 指的是鸟嘴。
