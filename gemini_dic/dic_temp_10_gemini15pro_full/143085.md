outbrazened: /ˌaʊtˈbreɪzənd/
verb
厚颜无耻地胜过；在厚颜无耻方面超过
To surpass in boldness or impudence.
He outbrazened his opponent by loudly proclaiming his innocence despite overwhelming evidence to the contrary. 他厚颜无耻地胜过他的对手，尽管有压倒性的证据证明他有罪，但他还是大声宣称自己是无辜的。
Synonyms: outface, outdare, surpass in boldness
Antonyms: cower, recoil, be intimidated
词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + brazen (meaning shameless or impudent).  "Out-" acts as a prefix indicating exceeding or surpassing. Brazen ultimately derives from the Old English "bræs" meaning brass.  This connection likely arose from the perceived hardness and impudence associated with brass.
