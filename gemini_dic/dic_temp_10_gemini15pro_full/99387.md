interdiction:/ˌɪntərˈdɪkʃən/| n.|禁止，禁令，阻断|An act of forbidding or prohibiting something.|The government imposed an interdiction on the sale of firearms. 政府禁止销售枪支。|
近义词：prohibition, ban, embargo, restriction|
反义词：allowance, permission, authorization|
词性: noun


interdiction:/ˌɪntərˈdɪkʃən/| n.|【宗教】停止神职人员职务|The act of forbidding a priest from performing his duties.|The priest received an interdiction from the bishop. 主教停止了该神父的职务。|
近义词：suspension, debarment|
反义词：reinstatement|
词性: noun


interdiction:/ˌɪntərˈdɪkʃən/| n.|【军事】封锁，阻断|The act of cutting off enemy supply lines.|The air force carried out an interdiction campaign to disrupt enemy logistics. 空军开展了阻断敌方后勤的封锁行动。|
近义词：blockade, disruption, obstruction|
反义词：access, supply|
词性: noun

词源:
From Late Middle English *interdiccioun, from Old French interdicion, from Latin interdictiō (“a prohibiting, interdict”), from interdictus, past participle of interdīcere (“to forbid, prohibit, interdict”), from inter (“between, among”) + dīcere (“to say, speak”).
