diallel:/daɪˈæliːl/| n. adj.|双列杂交; 双列杂交的|A breeding experiment in which each member of a group of genotypes is crossed with each other member.  A diallel cross is often used to determine the combining ability of a set of inbred lines.|A complete diallel cross was conducted using six parental maize lines.|近义词:crossbreeding, hybridization|反义词:None|词性:noun, adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From di- (two) + -allel (form of allele, ultimately from Greek allélos "each other").
