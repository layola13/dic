absolve:/əbˈzɑːlv/ /əbˈsɒlv/| vt.|赦免，免除|to free someone from guilt, blame, or responsibility|The court absolved him of all responsibility for the accident. 法庭免除了他對事故的所有責任。

The priest absolved him of his sins. 神父赦免了他的罪孽。|近义词：exonerate, acquit, pardon, forgive|反义词：blame, condemn, convict|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *absolven*, from Old French *absoldre*, *assoldre*, from Latin *absolvō* (“I acquit, release, set free, complete, finish”), from *ab* (“from, away from”) + *solvō* (“I loosen, untie, release, pay”).

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The court absolves him of all charges. = 法庭宣布他无罪。
The priest absolves the sinners. = 神父赦免了罪人。

一般过去时:
The judge absolved the defendant of the crime. = 法官宣布被告无罪。
The church absolved her from her vows. = 教会免除了她的誓言。

现在完成时:
The jury has absolved the defendant of all charges. = 陪审团已经宣布被告无罪。
He has been absolved of any wrongdoing. = 他已经被免除所有不当行为的责任。

过去完成时:
After the new evidence emerged, the court had absolved him of the crime. = 新证据出现后，法院宣布他无罪。
She had been absolved of her oath before she left the order. = 在她离开修会之前，她已经被免除了誓言。

将来时:
If they find him innocent, the jury will absolve him. = 如果他们发现他无罪，陪审团将宣布他无罪。
The Pope will absolve the pilgrims of their sins. = 教皇将赦免朝圣者的罪孽。

虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, the court would absolve him. = 如果证据更充分，法院就会宣布他无罪。
I wish the committee would absolve me of responsibility. = 我希望委员会能免除我的责任。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

The church will not absolve him unless he repents. = 除非他忏悔，教会不会赦免他。
