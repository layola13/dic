factitive:/ˈfæktɪtɪv/| adj.| 使役的，致使的 |A factitive verb is a verb that indicates that the subject causes someone or something else to perform an action or be in a certain state, often without the subject directly performing the action themselves.  It expresses an action that causes something else to happen. |She had her hair cut. (She caused someone else to cut her hair.) |近义词：causative |反义词: N/A |词性: adjective
