manoeuvred:/məˈnuːvərd/ (UK) /məˈnuːvərd/ (US)| v.|操纵，调动；巧妙地移动|to move skillfully or carefully; to skillfully guide or manipulate a situation to achieve a desired outcome|The captain skillfully manoeuvred the ship through the narrow channel. 船长熟练地驾驶着船穿过狭窄的海峡。|The politician manoeuvred himself into a position of power. 这位政治家巧妙地将自己置于一个有权势的地位。|synonyms: navigate, manipulate, steer, guide, maneuver (US)|antonyms: neglect, abandon|verb

manoeuvre:/məˈnuːvər/ (UK) /məˈnuːvər/ (US)| n. v.|策略，调动；演习；用策略操纵|a movement or series of moves requiring skill and care; a planned action designed to achieve a particular goal; to perform a series of moves skillfully|The army carried out a series of manoeuvres in the desert. 军队在沙漠中进行了一系列演习。|It was a clever manoeuvre to get the bill passed.  这是一个巧妙的策略，使法案得以通过。|synonyms: tactic, strategy, operation, ploy, maneuver (US)|antonyms: inaction, standstill|noun, verb
