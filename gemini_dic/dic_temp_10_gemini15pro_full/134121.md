nonimpeachabilities:/ˌnɒnɪmˈpiːtʃəˈbɪlɪtiz/
|n.| 不可弹劾性；无懈可击 |The state of being nonimpeachable; freedom from liability to impeachment.  This word refers to the quality of being beyond reproach or question, particularly in a legal or moral sense. | The judge's nonimpeachabilities were questioned after the scandal.  法官的无懈可击性在丑闻之后受到了质疑。 |  integrity, irreproachability, unimpeachability | censurability, impeachability | noun


Etymology:
From non- (not) + impeachable + -ity (suffix forming nouns of quality or state).  Impeachable comes from impeach (to accuse), ultimately from Old French empechier (to hinder, prevent).
