bonfire: /ˈbɒnfaɪər/ (UK) /ˈbɑːnfaɪər/ (US)| n.|篝火，营火|A large outdoor fire, typically one built for celebration or as part of a festival.|例句：We built a bonfire on the beach and roasted marshmallows. 我们在沙滩上点燃篝火，烤棉花糖。|近义词：fire, blaze, pyre|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "darkness" or "cold")|词性: noun

词源：From bone + fire. Originally referred to fires in which bones were burned.  Later generalized to any large outdoor fire.
