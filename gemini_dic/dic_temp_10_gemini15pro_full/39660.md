conservatism:/kənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/ | n. |保守主义，守旧性| A political or theological orientation advocating the preservation of the best in society and opposing radical changes. |  Example 1: Her conservatism prevented her from embracing new technologies. 她保守的思想使她无法接受新技术。

Example 2:  The party's platform reflected a growing conservatism among its members.  该党的纲领反映了其成员日益增长的保守主义。| 近义词: traditionalism, orthodoxy | 反义词: liberalism, radicalism | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *conservatisme*, from *conserver* "to preserve," from Latin *conservare*  "to keep, preserve, maintain," from *com-* "together" (see com-) + *servare* "to keep, guard" (see observe).
