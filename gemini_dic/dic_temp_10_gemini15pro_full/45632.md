cursitor:/ˈkɜːrsɪtər/ | n. | 抄写员；书记员 | A clerk of a court, especially one who copies documents. |  He worked as a cursitor in the local court, diligently transcribing legal documents. 他在当地法院担任书记员， diligently transcribing legal documents. | 近义词: clerk, scribe, copyist | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Anglo-French *cursour, from Medieval Latin *cursor, from Latin currere "to run."  Originally, a cursitor was a messenger or runner for a court.  Over time, the term evolved to refer to a clerk who prepared and copied court documents.
