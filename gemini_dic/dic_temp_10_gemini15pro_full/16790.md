bascinet: /ˈbæsɪnɪt/ | n. | 头盔，盆盔 | A medieval helmet, typically with a visor and a rounded or pointed top. It evolved from the earlier great helm and offered better vision and ventilation. | The knight lowered his bascinet before entering the jousting field. 骑士在进入马上长矛比武场前放下了他的盆盔。 | The warrior's bascinet gleamed in the sunlight.  武士的盆盔在阳光下闪闪发光。 | synonyms: helmet, helm, casque | antonyms: None (lack of a specific antonym for a type of helmet) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *bacinet*, diminutive of *bacin* "basin," referring to the helmet's resemblance to a basin. Ultimately from Late Latin *baccinus*, from *bacca* "water vessel."
