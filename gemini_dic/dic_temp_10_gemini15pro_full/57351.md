dowse's:/ˈdaʊzɪz/| n.|占卜者的（东西）; 探寻水源者的（东西）|dowse's is the possessive form of "dowser," meaning something belonging to or associated with a dowser. A dowser is someone who searches for underground water or minerals using a forked stick or rod, or by intuition.| The dowse's rod twitched, indicating the presence of water. 探寻水源者的竿子抽动了一下，表明那里有水。|近义词: diviner's, water witcher's |反义词:  (No direct antonym, could consider scientist's if contrasting methods) |词性: noun (possessive)
