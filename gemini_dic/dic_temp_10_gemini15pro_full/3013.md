aerenchyma:/ˌeərˈɛŋkɪmə/ /ˌɛərˈɛŋkɪmə/ | n. | 通气组织 | A spongy tissue with large air spaces found between the cells of the stems and leaves of some aquatic plants.  It provides buoyancy and allows the circulation of gases to submerged parts of the plant. | The aerenchyma in the stems of water lilies helps them float.  睡莲茎中的通气组织帮助它们漂浮。

The extensive aerenchyma in mangrove roots allows them to survive in oxygen-poor mud. 红树林根部发达的通气组织使它们能够在缺氧的泥浆中生存。 | synonyms: aeriferous tissue | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek "aer" (air) + "enchyma" (infusion, tissue).  源自希腊语“aer”（空气）+“enchyma”（灌输，组织）。


USAGE->
The presence of aerenchyma is a key adaptation for aquatic plants.  通气组织的存在是水生植物的关键适应性。

Scientists are studying the development of aerenchyma in rice to improve its tolerance to flooding.  科学家们正在研究水稻通气组织的发育，以提高其对洪水的耐受性。

Aerenchyma allows for the efficient transport of oxygen to the roots of submerged plants. 通气组织允许氧气有效地输送到淹没植物的根部。

The formation of aerenchyma is induced by low oxygen levels in the surrounding environment.  周围环境中低氧水平会诱导通气组织的形成。
