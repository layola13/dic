an-arm-and-a-leg:/ən ˈɑːrm ənd ə ˈleɡ/ | idiom |代价高昂,非常昂贵 |  This idiom means that something is very expensive. It implies that you would have to give up a significant part of yourself (figuratively, an arm and a leg) to afford it. | That car cost him an arm and a leg. 那辆车花了他一大笔钱。| synonyms: a fortune, a king's ransom, a bomb | antonyms: cheap, inexpensive, a bargain | noun phrase
