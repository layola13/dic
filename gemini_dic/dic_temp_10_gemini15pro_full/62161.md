encoach:/ɪnˈkoʊtʃ/ |v. |指导，训练；培养|To train or instruct someone; to encourage and support someone in developing a skill or reaching a goal. |例句：The teacher encoached the students in public speaking. 老师指导学生进行公开演讲。

例句：She was encoached by her mentor to pursue a career in medicine. 她的导师鼓励她从事医学事业。|近义词：coach, tutor, mentor, train, instruct|反义词：neglect, discourage|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->

From en- + coach. "En-" is a prefix meaning "to put into" or "to cause to be." "Coach" originally referred to a horse-drawn carriage, then later to a tutor or trainer, likely because a coach helps to "carry" someone to their destination or goal.
