dissonant:/ˈdɪsənənt/ | adj. | 不和谐的，刺耳的 | Lacking harmony; clashing.  Characterized by a  lack of agreement or compatibility. | The dissonant chords created a jarring effect. 不和谐的和弦产生了刺耳的效果。| synonyms: discordant, inharmonious, jarring, clashing, cacophonous | antonyms: harmonious, consonant, melodious, euphonious | adjective

dissonance:/ˈdɪsənəns/ | n. | 不和谐，刺耳的声音 |  A tension or clash resulting from the combination of two disharmonious or unsuitable elements. Lack of agreement or harmony between people or things. |  There is a dissonance between what he says and what he does. 他的言行不一致。| synonyms: discord, disagreement, incongruity, clash, friction | antonyms: harmony, agreement, concord, consonance | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dissonantia, from Latin dissonantem (nominative dissonans), present participle of dissonare "to sound harshly," from dis- "apart, away" + sonare "to sound."
