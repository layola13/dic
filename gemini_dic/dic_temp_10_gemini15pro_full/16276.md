baptismal: /bæpˈtɪzməl/ | adj. |洗礼的；浸礼的| Relating to baptism, the Christian rite of admission and adoption, almost invariably symbolized by the use of water, and sometimes by the additional symbol of anointing with oil.| Her baptismal gown was a family heirloom. 她的洗礼袍是一件传家宝。| The baptismal ceremony was held in the church. 洗礼仪式在教堂举行。 | 近义词: christening | 反义词: None | 词性: adjective

词源: Late Middle English: from Old French baptesmal, or late Latin baptismalis, from Latin baptisma ‘baptism’.
