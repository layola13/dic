demotivate:/diːˈməʊtɪveɪt/ /diːˈmoʊtɪveɪt/
|v.| 使失去动力；使消极 |To make someone less enthusiastic or willing to do something.|The constant criticism demotivated the team. 不断的批评使团队失去了动力。|discourage, dishearten|motivate, encourage|verb

demotivated:/diːˈməʊtɪveɪtɪd/ /diːˈmoʊtɪveɪtɪd/
|adj.|  desmotivated的过去分词和过去式; 失去动力的；消极的|Lacking motivation; discouraged.|The employees felt demotivated by the lack of recognition for their hard work.  员工因工作得不到认可而感到沮丧。|discouraged, disheartened|motivated, enthusiastic|adjective

demotivating:/diːˈməʊtɪveɪtɪŋ/ /diːˈmoʊtɪveɪtɪŋ/
|adj.| 令人泄气的；使人失去动力的|Causing someone to lose motivation.|The demotivating news of the budget cuts made everyone feel hopeless. 预算削减的令人沮丧的消息让每个人都感到绝望。|discouraging, disheartening|motivating, inspiring|adjective

demotivation:/ˌdiːˌməʊtɪˈveɪʃn/ /ˌdiːˌmoʊtɪˈveɪʃn/
|n.| 失去动力；消极|The process of making someone less enthusiastic.|The team's demotivation was apparent in their poor performance. 该团队的士气低落在他们糟糕的表现中显而易见。|discouragement, disillusionment|motivation, inspiration|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- (prefix meaning "reduce" or "remove") + motivate (from Latin motivus, "moving," from motus, past participle of movēre, "to move").
