basidiomycetes:/bəˌsɪdi.oʊˈmaɪˌsiːts/ | n. | 担子菌 |A large and diverse group of fungi characterized by the production of sexual spores (basidiospores) on a specialized club-shaped structure called a basidium.  | Example: Mushrooms, puffballs, and rusts are all examples of basidiomycetes.  蘑菇、马勃菌和锈菌都是担子菌的例子。| Synonym: Basidiomycota | Antonym: N/A | Noun

Etymology: From Modern Latin *Basidiomycetes*, from *basidium* + *-mycetes* ("fungi").  *Basidium* comes from Greek *basis* ("base, pedestal").
