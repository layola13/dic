formalism:/ˈfɔːrməlɪzəm/ | n. | 形式主义;拘泥形式;形式体系 | Excessive adherence to prescribed forms or conventions, often at the expense of substance or meaning. A theory that emphasizes form and structure, as in art, literature, or mathematics. |  The artist rejected formalism and embraced a more expressive style.  这位艺术家摒弃了形式主义，转而采用更具表现力的风格。 | synonym: rigidity, conventionalism, dogmatism | antonym: informality, flexibility, pragmatism | noun

formalism:/ˈfɔːrməlɪzəm/ | n. | 形式主义;拘泥形式;形式体系 | A system of formal rules or procedures used in a particular field of study or activity. | Mathematical formalism provides a rigorous framework for proving theorems. 数学形式主义为证明定理提供了一个严谨的框架。 | synonym: method, system, procedure | antonym: improvisation, spontaneity, informality | noun
