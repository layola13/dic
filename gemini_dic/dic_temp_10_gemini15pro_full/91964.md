hypermoralities:/ˌhaɪpər məˈrælətiz/ | n. | 超道德；过度道德化 |  A system of morals that goes beyond the conventionally accepted norms, often involving excessive or extreme moral judgments. It can refer to a set of extremely strict moral principles or the tendency to apply moral judgments to areas where they may not be relevant or appropriate. | Some critics argue that certain political ideologies promote hypermoralities that stifle individual freedom. 一些评论家认为，某些政治意识形态助长了扼杀个人自由的过度道德化。|Synonyms: excessive moralizing, extreme moralism, rigid morality, moral absolutism | Antonyms: moral relativism, amorality, immorality | noun
