diverge:/daɪˈvɜːrdʒ/ (US) /daɪˈvɜːdʒ/ (UK)| v. | 分歧；偏离；相异 | to separate and go in different directions; to become different | The two roads diverged in the wood. 这两条路在树林里分开了。| synonyms: separate, deviate, branch off | antonyms: converge, meet, unite | verb

diverges:/daɪˈvɜːrdʒɪz/ (US) /daɪˈvɜːdʒɪz/ (UK)| v. | 分歧；偏离；相异（第三人称单数）|  Third-person singular simple present form of diverge |  His opinion diverges from the norm. 他的观点与常理相悖。| synonyms: separates, deviates, branches off | antonyms: converges, meets, unites | verb

divergence:/daɪˈvɜːrdʒəns/ (US) /daɪˈvɜːdʒəns/ (UK)| n. | 分歧；差异；偏离 | the process or state of diverging | There is a significant divergence of opinion on this issue. 在这个问题上，意见分歧很大。| synonyms: difference, deviation, disparity | antonyms: convergence, agreement, similarity | noun

divergent:/daɪˈvɜːrdʒənt/ (US) /daɪˈvɜːrdʒənt/ (UK)| adj. | 分歧的；相异的；发散的 | tending to be different or develop in different directions | The two species followed divergent evolutionary paths. 这两个物种沿着不同的进化路径发展。| synonyms: differing, varying, dissimilar | antonyms: convergent, similar, uniform | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *divergere*, present active infinitive of divergō (“I bend away, turn aside, digress, differ”), from di- (“apart, away”) + vergō (“I incline, turn, tend”).
