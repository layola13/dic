oversilence:/ˈoʊvərˈsaɪləns/| n.|过度沉默，过分安静|An excessive or prolonged period of silence, often implying a deliberate withholding of speech or communication.  A state of being too quiet, especially when it seems unnatural or meaningful.| The oversilence in the room after his announcement was deafening.  他宣布之后房间里死一般的寂静震耳欲聋。|近义词：hush, stillness, quiet, muteness|反义词: noise, clamor, talkativeness|词性:noun
词源:  "Over-" (prefix indicating excess) + "silence" (noun).  The prefix "over-" intensifies the meaning of "silence," suggesting an excessive or unnatural degree of quiet.
