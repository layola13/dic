diacid:/ˌdaɪˈæsɪd/| n.|二元酸|A diacid is an acid that has two acidic hydrogen atoms in its molecule.  It can donate two protons (H+) per molecule in an acid-base reaction.|Tartaric acid is a diacid found in grapes.|近义词：diprotic acid|反义词:monobasic acid|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From di- + acid.  Di- is a prefix meaning "two," derived from Greek.  Acid comes from the Latin word "acidus," meaning "sour" or "sharp."
