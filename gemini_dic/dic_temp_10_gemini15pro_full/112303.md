ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "lord" (meaning "to act as a lord"), which comes from the noun "lord" (meaning "master, ruler").  The noun "lord" has its origins in Old English "hlāford," meaning "loaf-keeper" (literally "bread guardian"), reflecting the lord's role in providing for his dependents.
