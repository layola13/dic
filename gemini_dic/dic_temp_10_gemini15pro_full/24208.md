bridewell:/ˈbraɪdwɛl/| n.|拘留所，监狱；教养院|A house of correction; a prison. Originally a palace in London used as a prison. Now, often used to refer to any prison, especially a small or local one. |例句：The vagrant was sent to the bridewell for a month.  流浪汉被送到拘留所关了一个月。

例句：The old bridewell has been converted into a museum.  这座古老的监狱已被改建成博物馆。|近义词：jail, prison, lockup, detention center|反义词:freedom, liberty|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From St. Bride's Well, a well in London near St. Bride's Church, where a palace was built and later used as a prison. The name of the well comes from St. Bride (or St. Bridget), an Irish saint.
