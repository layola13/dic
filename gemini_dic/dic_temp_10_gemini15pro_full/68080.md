fade:/feɪd/| v. n.|褪色，逐渐消失，衰弱|To lose freshness, brightness, or strength gradually; to disappear slowly.|The flowers faded in the hot sun.  花朵在烈日下枯萎了。|近义词：wane, diminish, disappear, vanish|反义词:brighten, intensify, strengthen|词性:动词, 名词

fade:/feɪd/| n.|褪色，逐渐消失|The process of losing freshness, brightness, or strength gradually.|The fade of the curtains was noticeable. 窗帘的褪色很明显。|近义词：decline, decay|反义词:growth, rise|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *faden* “to lose color, wither,” borrowed from Old French *fader*, from Late Latin *fatīgāre*  "to grow tired, weary," related to Latin *fatīgus* "tired, weary."  The sense development is from “grow weary” to “lose vigor” to “lose color.”
