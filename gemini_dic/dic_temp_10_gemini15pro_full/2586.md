admonitioner:/ˌædməˈnɪʃənər/
noun
劝诫者，告诫者
Someone who admonishes; one who gives a warning or reprimand.
The admonitioner spoke sternly to the children about their behavior. 劝诫者严厉地告诫孩子们要注意他们的行为。
He served as an admonitioner to his younger siblings, always reminding them of their responsibilities. 他充当弟弟妹妹的劝诫者，总是提醒他们自己的责任。
Synonyms: reprover, rebuker, critic, censor, monitor
Antonyms: praiser, flatterer, encourager, supporter
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From admonish + -er.  Admonish comes from the Latin "admonēre," meaning "to advise, warn, remind."  The suffix "-er" denotes a person who performs the action of the verb.
