clavicles: /ˈklævɪkəlz/
n.
锁骨
The clavicles are the bones that connect the sternum (breastbone) to the shoulder blades.
The cyclist fractured both his clavicles in the accident.  骑自行车的人在事故中摔断了双侧锁骨。

Etymology: From Late Latin *clavicula, diminutive of Latin clavis "key".  So called because its shape resembles a small key.

Synonyms: collarbones
Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a body part)
Part of Speech: Noun
