burning-out:/ˈbɜːrnɪŋ aʊt/ | adj. | 烧坏的；精疲力竭的 | Experiencing a state of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion caused by prolonged or excessive stress. | Many teachers are burning out from the demands of their job. 许多老师因为工作压力过大而精疲力竭。 |  The constant pressure led to him burning out.  持续的压力导致他精疲力竭。 | 近义词: exhausted, worn out, stressed out, drained | 反义词: energized, refreshed, invigorated |词性:形容词


burning out:/ˈbɜːrnɪŋ aʊt/ | n. |  精疲力竭 | The process of becoming burned out. |  Teacher burnout is a serious problem. 教师倦怠是一个严重的问题。 | He's suffering from burnout. 他正遭受着精疲力竭的痛苦。 | 近义词: exhaustion, fatigue | 反义词: rejuvenation, revitalization |词性:名词


词源：From the verb "burn out," literally meaning to stop burning due to lack of fuel, figuratively extended to describe a state of exhaustion.
