grimacing:/ˈɡrɪməsɪŋ/ | adj. | 做鬼脸的，痛苦的 | Showing a facial expression of pain, disgust, or disapproval. | She grimaced at the bitter taste of the medicine. 她对药的苦味做了个鬼脸。 | synonyms: wincing, flinching | antonyms: smiling, beaming | adjective

grimacing:/ˈɡrɪməsɪŋ/ | n. | 鬼脸，痛苦的表情 | A facial expression of pain, disgust, or disapproval. |  His grimace showed how much pain he was in. 他的鬼脸显示出他有多痛苦。| synonyms: wince, frown | antonyms: smile, grin | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Old French word "grimace," meaning "distorted face," ultimately of uncertain origin. Possibly related to a Frankish word meaning "mask."
