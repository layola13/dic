flit:/flɪt/
verb
轻快地飞;掠过;一闪而过
to fly or move quickly and lightly
The butterfly flitted from flower to flower. 蝴蝶在花丛中飞来飞去。
近义词:dart, flutter, skim
反义词:plod, trudge
词性:动词


flit:/flɪt/
noun
搬家;迁居;飞;快速移动
an act of moving quickly and lightly from one place to another
The family made a moonlight flit to avoid the landlord. 这家人趁着月色搬家，以避开房东。
近义词:move, relocation
反义词:stay
词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *flyttan, frequentative of fleotan "to float, swim, drift," from Proto-Germanic *fleutaną (source also of Old Norse flytja "to convey, transport," Old Frisian flitta "to remove, transport," Dutch vlieden "to flee," German fluten "to flow, flood"). The original sense was "to remove, transport," later "to move quickly and lightly."
