laughing-off: /ˈlɑːfɪŋ ɒf/ (UK) /ˈlæfɪŋ ɑːf/ (US)
|phrasal verb|一笑置之；付之一笑|To dismiss something, usually a problem or criticism, by laughing at it, suggesting it is unimportant or not serious.|He laughed off the accusations, saying they were ridiculous. 他对这些指控一笑置之，说它们很荒谬。|dismiss, shrug off, make light of|take seriously, dwell on|verb phrase

laughing-off: /ˈlɑːfɪŋ ɒf/ (UK) /ˈlæfɪŋ ɑːf/ (US)
|noun|一笑置之|The act of dismissing something by laughing.|His laughing-off of the situation angered his colleagues. 他对局势一笑置之的态度激怒了他的同事。|dismissal, disregard|acknowledgment, acceptance|noun
