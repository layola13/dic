actualize:/ˈæktʃuəlaɪz/| v. |实现，实行|To make something real or happen; to put something into action.  To make actual; to realize in action or fact.|She finally actualized her dream of traveling the world. 她终于实现了环游世界的梦想。

He struggled to actualize his vision for the company. 他努力将自己对公司的愿景付诸实践。|近义词：realize, achieve, fulfill, accomplish|反义词：negate, prevent, hinder|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin actualis "active, working," from actus "act" (from past participle stem of agere "to do, drive, act," from PIE root *ag- "to drive, draw out or forth, move"). The meaning "existing in fact" is from 1640s.  -ize is a productive suffix forming verbs with the general sense "to make or cause to be," and in this case, "to make actual."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He actualizes his potential through hard work. = 他通过努力工作实现了自身的潜能。
She actualizes her plans with precision. = 她精确地执行了她的计划。

一般过去时:
They actualized the project within the given timeframe. = 他们在规定的时间内完成了这个项目。
He actualized his dream of becoming a pilot. = 他实现了成为飞行员的梦想。

现在完成时:

She has actualized her vision for a more sustainable community. = 她实现了建立一个更可持续发展的社区的愿景。

过去完成时:

They had actualized the initial phase of the plan before encountering any problems. = 他们在遇到任何问题之前已经完成了计划的初始阶段。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
