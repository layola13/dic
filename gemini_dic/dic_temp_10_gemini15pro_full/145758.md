overcunning:/ˌoʊvərˈkʌnɪŋ/ | adj. | 过于狡猾的，诡计多端的 | excessively cunning; too clever or sly. |  He was overcunning for his own good, and his intricate schemes ultimately backfired. 他过于狡猾，结果他的复杂阴谋最终适得其反。| Synonyms: crafty, wily, devious, scheming, artful | Antonyms: naive, ingenuous, guileless, artless | adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *overcunnyng, from Old English *ofercunnung, from ofer- + cunnung (“knowledge, skill, experience”).  Equivalent to over- +‎ cunning.  The sense "excessively cunning" appears to be a relatively recent development, from the 19th century.
