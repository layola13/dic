bellows:/ˈbɛloʊz/ (US), /ˈbɛləʊz/ (UK)| n. |风箱；吼叫声；肺部|a device that produces a strong current of air when its sides are pressed together, used especially for blowing air into a fire|The blacksmith worked the bellows to heat the metal.  铁匠拉动风箱给金属加热。|The injured man's bellows of pain echoed through the hospital.  伤者的痛苦的吼叫声在医院里回荡。|synonyms: blower, air pump, roar, bellow (singular)|antonyms: inhale, whisper|noun

bellows:/ˈbɛloʊz/ (US), /ˈbɛləʊz/ (UK)| v. |吼叫；怒吼；发出轰鸣声|(of a person or animal) emit a deep loud roar, typically in pain or anger|(of a fire) burn fiercely, especially with a roaring sound|The bull bellowed in rage. 公牛愤怒地吼叫。|The fire bellowed and crackled. 火焰咆哮着噼啪作响。|synonyms: roar, bellow (singular), yell, shout|antonyms: whisper, murmur|verb
