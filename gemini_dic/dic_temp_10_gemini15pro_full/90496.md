hucksterdom:/ˈhʌkstərdəm/| n. |叫卖；兜售；江湖骗子行径；骗术|The behavior or practices characteristic of a huckster; aggressive, often deceptive salesmanship.|The politician's speech was full of empty promises and hucksterdom. 这位政客的演讲充满了空洞的承诺和江湖骗子的伎俩。|近义词：trickery, chicanery, deception, sharp practice|反义词：honesty, integrity|词性:名词

Etymology: From huckster + -dom.  "Huckster" comes from Middle Dutch "hokester" (hawker, retailer), frequentative of "hoeken" (to peddle). "-dom" is an Old English suffix forming nouns meaning "state, condition, domain."
