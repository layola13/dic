bilirubins:/ˌbɪlɪˈruːbɪnz/ | n. |胆红素|An orange-yellow pigment formed in the liver by the breakdown of hemoglobin and excreted in bile.  It is responsible for the yellow color of bruises and the brown color of feces. |High levels of bilirubins in the blood can indicate liver problems.|Synonym: bile pigment|Antonym: None|Noun
