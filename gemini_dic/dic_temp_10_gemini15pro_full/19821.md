bimetallism:/baɪˈmɛtəlɪzəm/| n.|双本位制|A monetary system in which two metals (usually gold and silver) are used as legal tender, with a fixed ratio between their values.|The collapse of bimetallism in the late 19th century led to the gold standard.|Synonyms: double standard|Antonyms: monometallism|noun
