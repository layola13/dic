earmark:/ˈɪərmɑːrk (UK), ˈɪrmɑːrk (US)/| n. vt.| 专款；指定用途；预留；标记 |A distinguishing mark or feature; to designate (something, typically funds or resources) for a particular purpose. | Congress earmarked $5 million for cancer research. 国会指定了500万美元用于癌症研究。| synonyms: allocate, assign, reserve, designate, set aside | antonyms: withhold, retain, keep back | noun, verb

earmark:/ˈɪərmɑːrk (UK), ˈɪrmɑːrk (US)/| n. vt.| 耳标；标记 |A mark on the ear of an animal for identification; to mark an animal in this way. |  The farmer earmarked his sheep to distinguish them from his neighbor's.  农民给他的羊做了耳标，以便与邻居的羊区分开来。| synonyms: tag, label, brand | antonyms: N/A | noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "ear" + "mark." Originally referred to marking the ears of livestock for identification.  The figurative sense of setting something aside for a specific purpose developed later.
