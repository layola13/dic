chumminesses:/ˈtʃʌmɪnɪsɪz/ |n.|闺蜜，女性密友|Close female friends; a group of women who are close friends.| We've been chumminesses since kindergarten. 我们从幼儿园起就是闺蜜。|  She went on holiday with her chumminesses. 她和她的闺蜜们去度假了。|近义词：friends, pals, besties|反义词：enemies, foes|词性：名词

chumminess:/ˈtʃʌmɪnɪs/ |n.|亲密，友好|The state of being chummy; friendliness.|The chumminess between the two families was heartwarming. 这两个家庭之间的亲密关系令人感到温暖。|There's a real chumminess among the team members. 团队成员之间有一种真正的亲密关系。|近义词：friendliness, camaraderie, amity|反义词：hostility, animosity|词性：名词

chummy:/ˈtʃʌmi/ |adj.|亲密的，友好的|Friendly and sociable.| They have a very chummy relationship. 他们之间关系非常亲密。 |The atmosphere in the pub was chummy and welcoming. 酒吧里的气氛亲切而友好。|近义词：friendly, amicable, convivial|反义词：unfriendly, hostile|词性：形容词

chum:/tʃʌm/ |n.|密友，伙伴|A close friend.|They've been chums since school. 他们从上学起就是好朋友。|He went fishing with his old chum. 他和他的老朋友去钓鱼了。|近义词：friend, pal, buddy|反义词：enemy, foe|词性：名词


词源: chum (n.)  1816, originally nautical slang, "shipmate, cabin mate," perhaps a shortened form of chamber fellow, or from dialectal chum "to chew food" (1590s), thus perhaps originally referring to messmates. The meaning "close friend" is from 1826.
