leaf:/liːf/ | n. | 叶子 | A flattened structure of a higher plant, typically green and blade-like, that is attached to a stem directly or via a stalk. Leaves are the main organs of photosynthesis and transpiration. | The autumn leaves fell from the trees. 秋天的叶子从树上落下。|  foliage, petal | root, branch | noun

leaf:/liːf/ | v. | 翻页；匆匆翻阅 | Turn the pages of (a book or magazine) quickly or casually. |  He leafed through the magazine, looking at the pictures. 他翻阅着杂志，看着图片。| browse, skim | pore over, scrutinize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lēaf, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch loof and German Laub.
