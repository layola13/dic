incog:/ɪnˈkɒɡ/ /ɪnˈkɑːɡ/ | adj. adv.| 隐姓埋名的，匿名的 |Short for incognito.  Used to describe someone who is concealing their true identity. | He travelled incog to avoid being recognized. 他隐姓埋名地旅行以避免被认出。| 近义词: disguised, anonymous, undercover | 反义词: known, identified, public | 词性: adjective, adverb

incognito:/ˌɪnkɒɡˈniːtəʊ/ /ˌɪnkɑːɡˈniːtoʊ/ | n. adj. adv.| 隐姓埋名，匿名，微服出行 |From Italian *incognito*, derived from Latin *incognitus* "unknown," past participle of *incognoscere* "not to know, fail to recognize," from *in-* "not" + *cognoscere* "to learn, get to know." Refers to a person who is concealing their true identity. | The prince travelled incognito. 王子微服私访。 | 近义词: disguised, anonymous, undercover | 反义词: known, identified, public | 词性: noun, adjective, adverb
