gauntletted:/ˈɡɔːntlətɪd/
adjective
戴着金属手套的；准备战斗的
Wearing gauntlets; figuratively, prepared for a challenge or ordeal.
The knight, gauntletted and ready for battle, rode into the arena.  这位骑士戴着金属手套，准备战斗，骑马进入竞技场。
The CEO, gauntletted for a tough round of questioning from the press, entered the conference room. 首席执行官戴着“金属手套”，准备迎接来自媒体的严厉质询，进入了会议室。
近义词: armored, prepared, ready
反义词: unarmed, unprepared, vulnerable
词性: adjective

词源: From gauntlet + -ed. Gauntlet comes from the Old French word "gantelet," diminutive of "gant," meaning "glove."
