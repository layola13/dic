electroglow:/ɪˈlɛktroʊɡloʊ/
noun
电辉光
A faint optical emission from the upper atmospheres of planets (especially Jupiter and Saturn) produced by the interaction of atmospheric gases with high-energy electrons from the planet’s magnetosphere.
Electrostatic forces cause dust grains to levitate above the surface and emit light. This faint glow is known as electroglow.  静电力导致尘埃颗粒悬浮在地表上方并发出光。这种微弱的光芒被称为电辉光。
