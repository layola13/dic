flinching:/ˈflɪntʃɪŋ/ | n. | 畏缩，退缩 |  The act of suddenly withdrawing oneself (usually followed by *from*) because of pain, fear, or surprise. | He couldn't help flinching from the sudden loud noise. 他忍不住因突如其来的巨响而畏缩。 |  wincing, recoiling, shrinking |  braving, confronting | noun

flinch:/flɪntʃ/ | v. | 畏缩，退缩 | To make a sudden, involuntary movement of the body as from pain or fear; wince. |  The horse flinched when the whip cracked. 当鞭子抽响时，马匹畏缩了一下。| wince, recoil, shrink | brave, confront | verb
