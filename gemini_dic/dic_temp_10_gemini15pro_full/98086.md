inquisition:/ˌɪnkwɪˈzɪʃən/ | n. |调查，审查，审讯|A period of prolonged and intensive questioning or investigation.|The police launched an inquisition into the missing person's whereabouts. 警方对失踪人员的下落展开了调查。|inquiries, investigations, examinations|tolerance, acceptance|noun
inquisition:/ˌɪnkwɪˈzɪʃən/ | n. |(尤指宗教法庭的) 审讯，审判|A judicial or official inquiry, especially one conducted with little regard for individual rights, often used in historical contexts referring to the Inquisition.|The Spanish Inquisition was a notorious period of religious persecution. 西班牙宗教裁判所是一个臭名昭著的宗教迫害时期。|trial, hearing, tribunal|pardon, amnesty|noun
inquisitions:/ˌɪnkwɪˈzɪʃənz/ | n. |(inquisition 的复数形式) 多次调查，审讯|Multiple instances of inquisitions.|The government has launched several inquisitions into corporate corruption. 政府对企业腐败发起了多次调查。|investigations, inquiries, probes|absolutions, pardons|noun


Etymology:
From Late Middle English: inquisicion, from Old French inquisicion, from Latin inquisitio(n-), from inquirere ‘seek into’.
