fidelio:/fɪˈdeɪlioʊ/ | n. | 忠诚，忠实 |  Fidelio is often used to represent the ideal of unwavering loyalty and faithfulness, especially in the face of adversity. It comes from the title of Beethoven's only opera, about a wife who disguises herself as a man to rescue her imprisoned husband. |  His fidelio to his principles cost him his job.  他对他原则的忠诚让他失去了工作。 | synonyms: loyalty, faithfulness, devotion, constancy | antonyms: treachery, betrayal, disloyalty | noun

fidelio:/fɪˈdeɪlioʊ/ | adj. | 忠诚的，忠实的 (less common usage) | While less commonly used as an adjective, fidelio can describe someone or something that embodies loyalty and faithfulness. |  A fidelio friend will always be there for you. 一位忠诚的朋友会永远在你身边。 | synonyms: loyal, faithful, devoted, constant | antonyms: treacherous, betraying, disloyal | adjective
