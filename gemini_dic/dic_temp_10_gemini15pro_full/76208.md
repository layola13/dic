fustily:/ˈfʌstɪli/| adv.|古板地；陈腐地；霉臭地|In a fusty, old-fashioned manner; smelling stale or moldy.|The old library smelled fustily of aged paper and leather.  这间古老的图书馆散发着陈旧纸张和皮革的霉味。|例句：He fustily complained about the loud music. 他古板地抱怨音乐太吵了。|近义词：stuffily, musty, stale, old-fashioned|反义词：fresh, modern, airy|词性:副词
Etymology: From *fust* (noun) meaning a strong musty smell, from Old French *fust* (wine cask), from Latin *fustis* (club, staff).  The association is with the stale smell of old wine casks.
