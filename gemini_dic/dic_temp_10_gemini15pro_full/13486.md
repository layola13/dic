attabal:/ˈætəbɑːl/| n. |阿塔巴尔鼓，一种小型的凯特尔鼓|A small kettledrum, especially one used in pairs in the Middle Ages and Renaissance.|The musicians played a rhythmic tune on the attabals.|Near synonyms: kettledrum, nakers, timpani|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps silence)|Noun


Etymology->
From Arabic الطبل (aṭ-ṭabl, “the drum”), from Aramaic טַבְלָא (ṭavlā). Ultimately from Akkadian ṭablu.
