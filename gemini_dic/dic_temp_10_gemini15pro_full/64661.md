escalate:/ˈɛskəleɪt/ (US), /ˈɛskəleɪt/ (UK)| v. |升级；扩大；加剧；恶化|to increase or intensify, especially rapidly or to a serious degree; to become or cause to become more intense or serious|The conflict escalated quickly into a full-blown war. 冲突迅速升级为全面战争。|The price of oil has escalated sharply in recent months. 近几个月油价急剧上涨。|intensify, increase, worsen, aggravate, heighten|de-escalate, decrease, lessen, reduce|verb

escalated:/ˈɛskəleɪtɪd/ (US), /ˈɛskəleɪtɪd/ (UK)| adj. |升级的；扩大的；加剧的；恶化的|increased, intensified, made worse or more serious|The escalated tensions led to a military build-up.  紧张局势升级导致了军事集结。|They took escalated measures to contain the virus. 他们采取了升级措施来控制病毒。|intensified, increased, worsened, aggravated, heightened|de-escalated, decreased, lessened, reduced|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *scalare "to climb," from scala "ladder."  The prefix "e-" (ex-) adds the meaning of "out" or "up."
