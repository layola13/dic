nonlugubriously:/ˌnɒnluːˈɡuːbriəsli/
| adv. | 不悲伤地；不忧郁地 | In a manner that is not lugubrious; cheerfully.  Not sadly or mournfully. |  She danced nonlugubriously, despite the bad news.  她尽管听到了坏消息，还是兴高采烈地跳着舞。| cheerfully, brightly, merrily, joyfully | sadly, mournfully, gloomily, lugubriously | adverb

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  Meaning "not," from Latin non "not."
lugubrious (adjective): From Latin lugubris "mournful, doleful," perhaps from *lugeo-bri-s, from PIE root *leug- "to cause grief, be sad."
-ly (suffix): Forms adverbs from adjectives.
