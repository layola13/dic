molon:/ˈmoʊlɒn/| interj.| 来拿吧；你来拿啊|An ancient Greek phrase meaning "come and get them," famously attributed to King Leonidas of Sparta during the Battle of Thermopylae. It is a defiant challenge, typically used in the context of defending one's possessions or rights, especially firearms.|Example:  When the government threatened to confiscate their weapons, the citizens responded with a resounding "Molon Labe!" 当政府威胁要没收他们的武器时，公民们以响亮的“Molon Labe！”回应。
Example:  The protesters held signs saying "Molon Labe" as a symbol of their resistance to the new gun control laws. 抗议者举着写有“Molon Labe”的标语，以此作为他们抵制新枪支管制法的象征。|近义词: Come and get them, defy, challenge|反义词: surrender, yield, submit|词性: interjection
