nonchalant:/ˌnɒnʃəˈlɑːnt/ (US) /ˌnɑːnʃəˈlɑːnt/| adj.|漠不关心的，若无其事的，冷淡的| Appearing casually calm and relaxed; not displaying anxiety, interest, or enthusiasm.|He strolled in with a nonchalant air, as if nothing had happened. 他若无其事地走了进来，好像什么都没发生过。|Synonyms: indifferent, unconcerned, apathetic, casual, cool, calm|Antonyms: anxious, concerned, eager, enthusiastic|adjective

nonchalance:/ˈnɒnʃələns/ (US) /ˈnɑːnʃələns/ | n. |漠不关心，冷淡，冷静|The state of being casually calm and relaxed; lack of anxiety, interest, or enthusiasm.|She faced the crisis with remarkable nonchalance.  她在危机面前表现得异常冷静。|Synonyms: indifference, unconcern, apathy, coolness, composure|Antonyms: anxiety, concern, eagerness, enthusiasm|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *nonchalant*, from *non* (not) + *chaloir* (to matter, to concern), from Latin *calēre* (to be warm).  The literal meaning is therefore "not being warm" or "not caring".
