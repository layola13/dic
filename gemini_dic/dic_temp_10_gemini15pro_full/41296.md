cordon:/ˈkɔːrdən/ (US) /ˈkɔːdn/ (UK)| n. v. |警戒线，封锁线；饰带，绶带|A line or circle of police, soldiers, or vehicles preventing people from entering or leaving a place; a decorative ribbon or cord.|
The police set up a cordon around the crime scene. 警察在犯罪现场周围设置了警戒线。
The area was cordoned off by the police.  该区域被警方封锁了。
|近义词：barrier, barricade, blockade, line, perimeter|反义词：access, entrance, opening|词性:名词，动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French "cordon" (cord, string), from Old French "cordon," ultimately from Late Latin "chorda" (cord, rope), from Greek "khordē" (gut, string).
