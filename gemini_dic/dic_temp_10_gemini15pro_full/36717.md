collocated: /ˈkɒləkeɪtɪd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləkeɪtɪd/ (US)
| adj. |共同定位的，放在一起的|Placed or located together in a particular place or position.  Existing or happening together in the same place or at the same time.|The two departments were collocated in the same building to improve communication. 这两个部门被安排在同一栋楼里以加强沟通。|例句：The offices are collocated with the warehouse for greater efficiency. 办公室与仓库位于同一地点，以提高效率。|近义词：co-located, situated together, juxtaposed, adjacent|反义词：separated, dispersed, scattered|词性:形容词

collocate: /ˈkɒləkeɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləkeɪt/ (US)
| v. |并置，排列|To place or arrange things together in a particular place or position.|例句：The museum collocates ancient artifacts with modern art.  博物馆将古代文物与现代艺术品并置陈列。|近义词：juxtapose, position together|反义词：separate, disperse|词性:动词
