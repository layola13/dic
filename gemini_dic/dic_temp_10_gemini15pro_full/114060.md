magnetizability:/ˌmæɡnɪˌtaɪzəˈbɪləti/| n.|磁化率|The measure of a material's ability to become magnetized in response to an applied magnetic field.  It represents the ease with which a magnetic dipole moment can be induced in the material.|The magnetizability of iron is much higher than that of aluminum. 铁的磁化率比铝高得多。|susceptibility, permeability|reluctance|noun
