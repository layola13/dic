benzine:/ˈbɛnzˌinː/ /ˈbɛnˌzinː/ | n. | 汽油, 苯, 挥发油 |A colorless, flammable liquid obtained from petroleum and used as a solvent and as fuel for internal combustion engines. Specifically, a mixture of light hydrocarbons distilling between 30 and 200 degrees Celsius.|  The old car ran on benzine.  这辆旧车用汽油。 | synonym: gasoline, petrol | antonym: none | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Benzin*, from *Benz* (the surname of Karl Benz, a German engine designer and automobile engineer) + *-in* (a suffix forming nouns denoting chemical substances).
