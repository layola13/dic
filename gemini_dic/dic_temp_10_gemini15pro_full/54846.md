displacency:/dɪsˈpleɪsənsi/| n. |自满，沾沾自喜|A feeling of smug satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements.|The team's displacency after their early victory cost them the championship.|近义词：complacency, smugness, self-satisfaction|反义词:anxiety, insecurity, humility|词性:名词

Etymology: From dis- (expressing negation) + complacent + -cy (suffix forming nouns of quality or state). Ultimately from Latin complacere (to please greatly).
