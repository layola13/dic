bog:/bɒɡ/ (British), /bɑːɡ/ (American)| n. |沼泽，泥塘|A wet muddy ground too soft to support a heavy body.| We got bogged down in the marsh. 我们陷入了沼泽。|近义词：marsh, swamp, mire|反义词:dry land|noun


bog:/bɒɡ/ (British), /bɑːɡ/ (American)| v. |使…陷入泥沼，陷入困境|To become stuck in wet, soft ground, or to cause something to become stuck in this way.|The car bogged down in the mud. 汽车陷在了泥里。|近义词：mire, stall, founder|反义词:free, extricate|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English bogge, probably from Irish or Scottish Gaelic bogach (“soft, wet ground”), from bog (“soft”), from Proto-Celtic *bukos (“soft, yielding”).
