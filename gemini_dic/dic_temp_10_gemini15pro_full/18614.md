bent:/bɛnt/ | adj. n. | 弯曲的，决意的；嗜好，倾向 | determined to do something; curved or angled | The tree branch was bent low with the weight of the snow.  树枝被雪压弯了。| synonyms: curved, angled, crooked, determined, resolute | antonyms: straight, unbent, undecided, hesitant | adjective

bent:/bɛnt/ | n. | 嗜好，倾向，才能 | a natural skill or interest | He has a bent for music. 他有音乐天赋。| synonyms: talent, inclination, aptitude, propensity | antonyms: aversion, disinclination | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Proto-Germanic *bandijaną (“to bind, bend”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰendʰ- (“to bind, tie, string”). Cognate with Old Saxon bindian (“to bind”), Old Norse binda (“to bind”), Old Frisian binda (“to bind”), Dutch binden (“to bind”), German binden (“to bind”), Gothic 𐌱𐌹𐌽𐌳𐌰𐌽 (bindan, “to bind”).  The noun sense developed from the past participle of the verb "bend".
