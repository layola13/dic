intangible:/ɪnˈtændʒəbl/| adj. n.|无形的，难以捉摸的；非物质资产|Something that is intangible is not able to be physically touched, but can be felt or experienced in some other way.|例句：The benefits of a good education are intangible. 良好教育的益处是无形的。

例句：She has that intangible quality that makes her a star. 她拥有一种难以言喻的特质，使她成为明星。|近义词：imperceptible, impalpable, abstract, ethereal|反义词：tangible, concrete, material|词性：adjective, noun


intangible:/ɪnˈtændʒəbl/| n.|无形资产|assets that are not physical in nature.|例句：Goodwill is an intangible asset. 商誉是一种无形资产。

例句：The company's most valuable intangibles are its brand and reputation. 公司最有价值的无形资产是它的品牌和声誉。|近义词：non-physical assets|反义词：tangible assets|词性：noun
