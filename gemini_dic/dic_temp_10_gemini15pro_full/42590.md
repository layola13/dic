counterpole:/ˈkaʊntərˌpoʊl/ (US), /ˈkaʊntəˌpəʊl/ (UK)| n.|对立面，对极，相反的极点|Something diametrically opposed; a contrary; an antithesis.|The Arctic and the Antarctic are counterpoles.|Synonyms: opposite, antithesis, contrary, inverse, reverse|Antonyms: complement, counterpart, similar|noun

counterpole:/ˈkaʊntərˌpoʊl/ (US), /ˈkaʊntəˌpəʊl/ (UK)| n.|对应物，对应极|A thing that corresponds to or complements another.|While they are often at odds, they are also counterpoles in a strange way, each needing the other to define themselves.|Synonyms: counterpart, complement, correlate, parallel|Antonyms: opposite, antithesis, contrary, inverse, reverse|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + pole, from Old French *contrepol (unattested) equivalent to contre (“against”) + pol (“pole”), ultimately from Latin contra (“against”) and polus (“pole”).
