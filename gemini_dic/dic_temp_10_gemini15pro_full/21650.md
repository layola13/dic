blushed: /blʌʃt/ | v. | 脸红；感到羞愧 | to become pink in the face, usually from embarrassment |  She blushed when he complimented her. 她被他夸奖时脸红了。| synonyms: reddened, flushed | antonyms: paled, blanched | verb

blushed: /blʌʃt/ | adj. |  脸红的 | Having become red in the face, typically from embarrassment. |  He gave a blushed smile. 他羞涩地笑了笑。 | synonyms: flushed, reddened | antonyms: pale, blanched | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bluschen, *blusshen, from Old English *blyscan, from Proto-Germanic *blusjaną (“to blaze, glow”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰleg- (“to shine, flash, burn”). Cognate with Dutch blozen (“to blush”), German blühen (“to bloom, blossom”), Swedish blossa (“to blaze”).  The sense development is from "glowing red" to "blushing."
