delectation:/ˌdiːlɛkˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 喜悦，乐趣，享受 | Great pleasure or enjoyment. |  She ate the cake with evident delectation. 她吃蛋糕时显露出明显的喜悦。

The book was a source of delectation for many years. 这本书多年来一直是乐趣的源泉。

| 近义词: enjoyment, pleasure, delight, relish, gusto | 反义词: displeasure, dissatisfaction, aversion | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin dēlectātiō (“delight, pleasure”), from Latin dēlectō (“I delight, charm, allure”), from dē (“from, down from”) + lactō (“I entice, allure, deceive”).
