modulative:/ˈmɒdjʊlətɪv/| adj.|调制的，调节的|Having the power or quality of modulating; specifically relating to modulation, the process of varying one or more properties of a periodic waveform, called the carrier signal, with a separate modulating signal that typically contains information to be transmitted.|The singer's modulative voice captivated the audience. 歌手的抑扬顿挫的嗓音迷住了观众。|近义词: adjustable, variable, regulating, controlling|反义词: fixed, static, unchanging|词性: adjective

Etymology:
From Late Latin *modulativus, from Latin modulatus (past participle of modulari “to regulate, measure”), from modulus “measure, standard”
