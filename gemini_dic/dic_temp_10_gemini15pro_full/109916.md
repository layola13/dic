libel:/ˈlaɪbl/ (US), /ˈlaɪbəl/ (UK)| n. vt.|诽谤，中伤；诽谤性文字|A published false statement that is damaging to a person's reputation. |例句：The newspaper was sued for libel after publishing the false article.  这家报纸因刊登不实文章而被起诉诽谤。

例句：He claimed the report was a libel against him and his company. 他声称这份报告对他和他公司构成诽谤。

近义词：slander, defamation, calumny|反义词:praise, commendation|词性:noun, verb


libel:/ˈlaɪbl/ (US), /ˈlaɪbəl/ (UK)| vt.|以文字诽谤，中伤|To publish a libel against. |例句：The article libelled the politician, falsely accusing him of corruption. 这篇文章诽谤了这位政治家，诬告他腐败。

例句：She threatened to libel the newspaper if they didn't print a retraction. 她威胁说如果报纸不刊登撤回声明，她就将他们告上法庭。


近义词：slander, defame, malign|反义词:extol, laud|词性:verb
