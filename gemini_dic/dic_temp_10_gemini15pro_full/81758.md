greeve:/ɡriːv/
n.|悲伤，悲痛|A feeling of deep sorrow, especially caused by someone's death.|She was filled with greeve at the news of her father's death. 她听到父亲去世的消息后充满了悲痛。|近义词:sorrow, grief, anguish|反义词:joy, happiness
v.|使悲伤，使悲痛|To cause someone to feel deep sorrow.|The loss of his dog greeved him deeply. 失去他的狗使他深感悲痛。|近义词:sadden, distress, grieve|反义词:gladden, cheer

词性: 名词，动词

词源: From Middle English *greven, *grewen, from Old French grever “to burden, oppress, harm, injure, aggravate,” from Late Latin gravare “to burden,” from Latin gravis “heavy.” Related to grave.
