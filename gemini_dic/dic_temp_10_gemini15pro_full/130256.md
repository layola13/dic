non-starter:/ˈnɒnˈstɑːtə(r)/, /ˈnɑːnˈstɑːrtər/| n.|无望之人/事，注定要失败的计划/想法|Something that has no chance of being successful; a hopeless case or futile attempt.|His proposal for a new stadium was a non-starter from the beginning due to lack of funding.|dud, failure, flop, washout|success, winner|noun
Etymology->
From the literal meaning of something that won't start, particularly a vehicle.  Figuratively extended to mean any plan, project, or idea that is doomed to fail.  First recorded use in the early 20th century.
