indisposednesses:/ˌɪndɪspəʊzɪdnɪsɪz/ | n. | 不适，微恙（复数） |  A state of being slightly unwell or unable to do something.  It refers to a mild illness or a temporary inability to function normally, often used in a formal or polite context.  The singular form, "indisposedness," is rarely used. |  His frequent indisposednesses prevented him from attending regular meetings.  他频繁的微恙使他无法参加例会。

Due to a series of indisposednesses, she was unable to complete the project on time. 由于一系列的小病，她没能按时完成项目。 | 近义词：ailments, illnesses, infirmities | 反义词：wellness, health, fitness | 词性: noun

词源:
From "indisposed" + "-ness". "Indisposed" comes from the French "indisposé,"  the past participle of "indisposer," meaning "to make unfit," from "in-" (not) + "disposer" (to dispose, arrange).  "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition.
