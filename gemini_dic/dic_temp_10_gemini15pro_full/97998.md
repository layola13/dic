inofficious:/ˌɪnəˈfɪʃəs/ | adj. | 不合法的，不适宜的，违反自然义务的 | Contrary to natural duty or affection, especially in neglecting family obligations; unkind; undutiful.  Failing to observe expected social or conventional proprieties. | Example: His inofficious behavior towards his elderly parents shocked everyone. 他对年迈父母的冷漠行为震惊了所有人。

Example:  Leaving his family destitute was considered an inofficious act.  让他家人一贫如洗被认为是不合人道的行为。 | 近义词: undutiful, unfilial, unkind, improper, inappropriate | 反义词: dutiful, filial, kind, proper, appropriate | 词性: adjective

词源: Late Middle English: from late Latin *inofficiosus, from in- ‘not’ + officium ‘duty’.
