babuism:/ˈbɑːbuːɪzəm/
n.
官僚主义；文牍主义；繁文缛节
Excessive adherence to bureaucratic rules and formalities, often resulting in inefficiency and delays.  A style of speaking or writing perceived as overly formal, pedantic, or characteristic of a babu (a clerk or official in India).
The project was stalled by endless babuism and red tape.  这个项目因无休止的官僚主义和繁文缛节而停滞不前。
His babuism made it difficult for him to connect with ordinary people. 他官僚作风让他难以与普通人沟通。
近义词:bureaucracy, red tape, officialdom, formalism
反义词:efficiency, streamlining, informality
词性:noun

词源:
From Hindi bābū (“clerk, gentleman, Mr.”), from Bengali bābū (“father, gentleman, Mr.”), from Prakrit bāppā (“father”), ultimately from Sanskrit bāpa (“father, respectable man”).  The suffix -ism denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice.
