glamourously: /ˌɡlæmərəsli/ | adv. | 迷人地，富有魅力地 | In a glamorous manner; attractively, excitingly, and often in a way that suggests wealth and success. | She was glamourously dressed in a sparkling evening gown. 她穿着闪闪发光的晚礼服，显得魅力十足。|  The actress waved glamourously to her fans.  这位女演员魅力十足地向她的粉丝挥手。 | 近义词: attractively, elegantly, stylishly, beautifully, charmingly | 反义词: plainly, simply, dully, unattractively | 词性: 副词

glamour: /ˈɡlæmər/ | n. | 魅力，魔力，吸引力 |  An attractive or exciting quality that makes certain people or things seem appealing or special. | The city has lost its glamour. 这座城市已经失去了它的魅力。 |  She was captivated by the glamour of Hollywood. 她被好莱坞的魅力所吸引。 | 近义词: allure, charm, magic, fascination | 反义词: dullness, ordinariness | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Scottish *glamour, a variant of grammar, influenced by gramarye "magic, enchantment".  Originally, *glamour referred to a magic spell, often specifically a spell that made something appear different from what it really was. This sense broadened to encompass any kind of alluring or fascinating quality.
