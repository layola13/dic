flip-flopped:/ˌflɪpˈflɑːpt/ | v. |改变主意，出尔反尔 | To change one's opinion or position, especially abruptly. | The politician flip-flopped on the issue of taxes. 这位政治家在税收问题上改变了立场。|
flip-flopped:|/ˌflɪpˈflɑːpt/ | v. |来回摇摆，摆动 | To move back and forth or up and down. | The fish flip-flopped on the deck of the boat. 鱼在船甲板上跳来跳去。|
flip-flopped:|/ˌflɪpˈflɑːpt/ | adj. (informal) |摇摆不定的，反复无常的 | Characterized by changing one's mind or position frequently. | He has a flip-flopped opinion on the matter. 他在这个问题上的观点摇摆不定。|

近义词: wavered, vacillated, changed, reversed, U-turned (especially in British English)
反义词: persisted, remained, stayed, held firm
词性: verb, adjective (informal)
