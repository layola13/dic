aeromagnetometer:/ˌɛroʊmæɡnɪˈtɒmɪtər/ (US) /ˌɛərəʊmæɡnɪˈtɒmɪtər/ (UK)| n.|航空磁力计|An instrument used to measure the Earth's magnetic field from an aircraft, typically used in geophysical surveys to detect variations in the magnetic field caused by underlying geological structures.|The aeromagnetometer survey revealed a large iron ore deposit. 航空磁力计测量揭示了一个大型铁矿床。

The geologists used an aeromagnetometer to map the magnetic field of the region. 地质学家使用航空磁力计绘制该地区的磁场图。|近义词:magnetometer|反义词:N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From aero- (relating to air or aircraft) + magnetometer (an instrument for measuring magnetic force).  Aero- comes from Greek ἀήρ (aēr, “air”). Magnetometer comes from magnet + -meter (meaning "measuring instrument"). Ultimately from Greek μαγνῆτις λίθος (magnētis lithos, “Magnesian stone”), referring to magnetite, a naturally magnetic iron ore found in Magnesia, a region of ancient Greece.
