middlebrow: /ˈmɪdlˌbraʊ/ | adj. n. | 中庸的；中等文化水平的；中庸作品；适合中等文化水平的人 | Appealing to or typical of a person with conventional, unchallenging tastes in literature, the arts, etc.; of average or slightly below average intellectual interests. |  Example: He reads mostly middlebrow novels. 他主要读一些中庸的小说。| Example:  The museum's latest exhibition was criticized for being too middlebrow.  博物馆最新的展览因过于中庸而受到批评。| 近义词: conventional, un anspruchsvoll, mainstream, pedestrian | 反义词: highbrow, lowbrow, sophisticated, intellectual | 词性: adjective, noun


Etymology:
From mid- + brow (in reference to the supposed height of the forehead indicating intelligence).  Coined in the 1920s.  Initially used somewhat disparagingly, but now often simply descriptive.
