ECMO: /ˈɛkmoʊ/ (American) /ˈɛkməʊ/ (British)| n. |体外膜氧合|Extracorporeal membrane oxygenation, a technique that provides prolonged cardiac and respiratory support to patients whose heart and lungs are unable to provide an adequate amount of gas exchange or perfusion to sustain life.|The patient was put on ECMO after suffering a severe heart attack.|Synonym: extracorporeal life support (ECLS)|Antonym: None|Noun
