mainstreamer:/ˈmeɪnstriːmər/
n.|主流人士；追随主流的人； 墨守成规者|A person who belongs to or supports the mainstream.|Mainstreamers tend to follow popular trends.|近义词:conformist, traditionalist|反义词:nonconformist, maverick, radical|词性:名词

mainstreaming:/ˈmeɪnˌstriːmɪŋ/
n.|主流化；融合教育|The practice of placing students with special needs in regular classrooms.|Mainstreaming helps children with disabilities integrate into society.|近义词:integration, inclusion|反义词:segregation, exclusion|词性:名词

mainstream:/ˈmeɪnstriːm/
n.|主流；主要倾向|The ideas, attitudes, or activities that are shared by most people and regarded as normal or conventional.|The mainstream media often ignores minority viewpoints.|近义词:norm, convention|反义词:fringe, periphery|词性:名词
adj.|主流的|Belonging to or characteristic of the mainstream.|Mainstream culture can sometimes be homogenizing.|近义词:conventional, orthodox|反义词:alternative, unconventional|词性:形容词
v.|使…主流化；使…融入主流|Bring something into the mainstream.|They are trying to mainstream alternative medicine.|近义词:integrate, incorporate|反义词:marginalize, exclude|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From main + stream.  "Main" meaning primary or principal, and "stream" referring to the prevailing current or direction of activity or influence.
