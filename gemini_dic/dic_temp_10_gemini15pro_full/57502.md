dragging:/ˈdræɡɪŋ/ | adj. | 拖曳的，缓慢的，无聊的 | Something that is dragging is slow and seems to take a long time.  It can also describe something being physically pulled. | The meeting was dragging on for hours. 会议拖了好几个小时。| Synonyms: slow, tedious, laborious | Antonyms: fast, quick, exciting | adjective

dragging:/ˈdræɡɪŋ/ | n. | 拖，拉，拖曳 | The act of pulling something along a surface. | The dragging of the heavy box across the floor made a loud noise.  沉重的箱子在地板上拖发出很大的噪音。| Synonyms: pulling, hauling, towing | Antonyms: pushing, lifting | noun

drag something out:  拖延某事 (He dragged the meeting out longer than necessary. 他不必要地延长了会议时间。)
drag someone down: 拖累某人 (His negativity was dragging everyone down.  他的消极情绪拖累了大家。)
drag someone into something:  把某人卷入某事 (Don't drag me into your argument! 不要把我卷入你们的争论！)
drag on:  拖延 (The performance dragged on and on. 表演拖拖拉拉。)
