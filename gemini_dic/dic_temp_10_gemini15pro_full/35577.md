coaltit: /ˈkoʊlˌtɪt/ | n. | 山雀（尤指北美产煤山雀）| A small North American songbird of the titmouse family (Paridae), typically with a dark gray back, lighter underparts, and a small crest. | Example: The coaltit flitted from branch to branch, searching for insects.  煤山雀在树枝间飞来飞去，寻找昆虫。| Example: We saw a coaltit in our backyard feeder this morning.  今天早上我们在后院的喂食器里看到一只煤山雀。 | Synonym: chickadee, titmouse | Antonym: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "coal" + "tit" (titmouse).  The "coal" refers to the dark gray color of the bird's back, and "tit" is an old word for a small bird.
