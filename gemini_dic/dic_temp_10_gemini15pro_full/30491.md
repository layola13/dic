centrosome: /ˈsɛntrəˌsoʊm/ (US), /ˈsɛntrəˌsəʊm/ (UK)| n. |中心体|An organelle near the nucleus of a cell which contains the centrioles (in animal cells) and from which the spindle fibers develop in cell division.|The centrosome plays a crucial role in cell division. 中心体在细胞分裂中起着至关重要的作用。|The centrosome duplicates during interphase. 在间期，中心体复制。|Synonym: microtubule organizing center (MTOC)|Antonym: N/A|Noun
