despondency:/dɪˈspɒndənsi/ (US) /dɪˈspɑːndənsi/ (UK)| n.|沮丧，失望，灰心|A state of low spirits caused by loss of hope or courage.|例句：A deep despondency settled over him.  深深的沮丧笼罩着他。|例句：Her despondency was evident in her slumped posture. 她的沮丧从她颓丧的姿势中显露出来。|近义词：dejection, despair, hopelessness|反义词：hope, optimism, cheerfulness|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *despondent- (stem of despondēns), present participle of despondēre "to lose heart, give up," from de- "down, away" + spondere "to promise, engage oneself."  The original sense was "to renounce a solemn pledge," hence "to lose courage, despair."
