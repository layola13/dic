altruism: /ˈæltruɪzəm/ | n. |利他主义；无私 |  The principle or practice of unselfish concern for or devotion to the welfare of others. |  Mother Teresa was known for her altruism.  特蕾莎修女以其利他主义而闻名。 | synonyms: selflessness, benevolence, charity | antonyms: selfishness, egoism, greed | noun


altruisms: /ˈæltruɪzəmz/ | n. | 利他行为；无私行为 (altruism的复数形式，较少使用) | Specific acts or instances of unselfish concern for others. |  Her altruisms extended beyond her immediate family to the entire community. 她的利他行为超越了她的直系亲属，扩展到了整个社区。 | synonyms: selfless acts, benevolent deeds, charitable actions | antonyms: selfish acts, egoistic deeds, greedy actions | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *altruisme*, coined by Auguste Comte from Italian *altrui* "of others, to others," from Latin *alteri*, dative singular of *alter* "other."
