foll:/fɒl/ | n. | fool 的古体拼写；傻瓜 | An archaic spelling of "fool"; a silly person. | He was a foll to trust her. 他真是个傻瓜，竟然相信她。 | 近义词: idiot, moron, imbecile | 反义词: genius, sage | 词性:名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *fol*, from Old French *fol*, *folle*, from Latin *follis* (“bellows, windbag”), hence, figuratively, “a foolish person, one puffed up with vanity”.  Related to *inflate*.
