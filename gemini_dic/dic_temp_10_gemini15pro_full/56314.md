dogie:/ˈdoʊɡi/ (US) /ˈdəʊɡi/ (UK)| n.|小牛，孤儿，母牛刚断奶的小牛|A motherless calf, or a very young calf.|The rancher rounded up the dogies and brought them in for the night. 牧场主把小牛们赶到一起，带它们过夜。|Synonyms: calf, yearling, maverick|Antonyms: bull, cow|noun

dogie:/ˈdoʊɡi/ (US) /ˈdəʊɡi/ (UK)| n.|流浪汉，孤儿（非正式）|A lonely and neglected person, especially a child.|He felt like a dogie, wandering from town to town. 他感觉自己像个流浪汉，从一个镇流浪到另一个镇。|Synonyms: orphan, waif, stray|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider:  cared-for, protected)|noun
