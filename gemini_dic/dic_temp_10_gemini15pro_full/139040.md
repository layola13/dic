oar:/ɔːr/ (US) /ɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. v.|桨；划（船）|A long pole with a flat blade at one end, used to row a boat. / To propel a boat with oars.|He dipped his oars into the water and began to row. 他把桨放进水里，开始划船。|近义词：paddle, scull|反义词: N/A|词性:名词, 动词|词源: Old English *ār, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch roer ‘rudder’, also to Latin rēmus.
