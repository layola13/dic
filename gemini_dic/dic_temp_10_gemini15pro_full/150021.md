palp:/pælp/| n.|触须，触肢|A segmented appendage near the mouth of an invertebrate, such as an insect or arachnid, used for sensing or manipulating food.|The spider used its palps to explore the web.|Synonyms: antenna, feeler|Antonyms: N/A|noun

palp:/pælp/| v.|触摸，抚摸|To touch or feel something gently, typically with the fingers or palps.|He palped the patient's abdomen to check for swelling.|Synonyms: touch, feel, stroke|Antonyms: N/A|verb
