gadfly:/ˈɡædˌflaɪ/| n.|牛虻；讨厌的人；令人讨厌的人|A gadfly is a fly that bites livestock, especially cattle. Figuratively, a gadfly is a person who persistently annoys or challenges others, especially those in authority.|The journalist was a gadfly, constantly questioning the government's policies.|近义词：nuisance, pest, irritant, provocateur|反义词:conformist, yes-man|noun

gadflies:/ˈɡædˌflaɪz/| n.|牛虻（复数）；讨厌的人（复数）；令人讨厌的人（复数）|Plural form of gadfly, referring to multiple flies that bite livestock or multiple people who persistently annoy or challenge others.|The gadflies of the press wouldn't leave the politician alone.|近义词：nuisances, pests, irritants, provocateurs|反义词:conformists, yes-men|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *gād-flieġe* "gadfly," composed of *gād* "goad, spike" (related to Old Norse *gaddr*, Old High German *gart*) + *flieġe* "fly." The sense of "a person who persistently annoys or challenges others" is figurative, based on the fly's behavior.
