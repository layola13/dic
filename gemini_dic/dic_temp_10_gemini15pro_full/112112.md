longshore:/ˈlɔːŋʃɔːr/ (US) /ˈlɒŋʃɔː(r)/ (UK)| adj. n.|近岸的，沿岸的；码头工人，码头装卸工人|Relating to or employed on the shore, especially in loading and unloading ships.|例句：The longshore drift carried the sand down the beach. 近岸漂移把沙子带到海滩下游。|近义词：coastal, waterfront, dockside, stevedore (n.)|反义词：offshore, inland|词性:形容词,名词

longshoreman:/ˈlɔːŋˈʃɔːrmən/ (US) /ˈlɒŋˈʃɔːmən/ (UK)| n.|码头工人，码头装卸工人|A dockworker who loads and unloads cargo from ships.|例句：The longshoremen were busy unloading the cargo ship. 码头工人正忙着卸货船上的货物。|近义词：docker, stevedore, dockworker|反义词：None|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "long" + "shore."  "Long" in this context refers to the length of the shoreline. The term began to be used in the late 16th century.
