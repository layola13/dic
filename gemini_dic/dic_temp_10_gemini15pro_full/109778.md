levitators:/ˈlevɪteɪtərz/
n.
悬浮者；使…悬浮的人或物
People or things that levitate (cause something to rise and float in the air, especially by means of supernatural power).
The levitators in the magic show amazed the audience.  魔术表演中的悬浮者使观众惊叹。
The new technology uses magnetic fields to act as levitators for high-speed trains.  这项新技术利用磁场使高速列车悬浮。
近义词:floaters
反义词:sinkers
词性:noun


levitate:/ˈlevɪteɪt/
v.
(使) 悬浮；(使) 飘浮
To rise or float in the air, especially by means of supernatural power; to cause something to rise and float in the air.
The magician seemed to levitate the table without touching it. 魔术师似乎不用碰桌子就能让它悬浮起来。
He claims to be able to levitate objects with his mind. 他声称能够用意念使物体悬浮。
近义词:float, hover
反义词:fall, drop, sink
词性:verb


levitation:/ˌlevɪˈteɪʃən/
n.
悬浮；飘浮
The act or process of levitating.
The levitation of the magician was a remarkable illusion.  魔术师的悬浮是一个非凡的幻觉。
Scientists are exploring magnetic levitation for transportation. 科学家们正在探索磁悬浮技术用于交通运输。
近义词:floating, hovering
反义词:falling, dropping, sinking
词性:noun

词源:
From Latin *levitatus, past participle of levitare "to lighten, lift up," from levis "light (in weight)."
