grata:/ˈɡrɑːtə/ | adj. | grateful, pleasing, welcome |  pleasing; welcome; agreeable.  Often used in the feminine form, especially in the phrase "persona grata" (welcome person). Derived from the Latin word "gratus," meaning "pleasing" or "thankful." | Her assistance was most grata. 她的帮助非常受欢迎。|Synonyms: welcome, appreciated, agreeable, pleasing, acceptable | Antonyms: unwelcome, undesirable, unpleasant, disagreeable, unacceptable | adjective

grata:/ˈɡrɑːtə/ | n. | thanks, gratitude (archaic or poetic) | An expression of thanks or gratitude, now mostly archaic or used in poetic contexts. From Latin "gratus," meaning "pleasing" or "thankful." |  He offered his grata for the kindness shown. 他对所表现出的 kindness 表示感谢。(archaic usage) |Synonyms: thanks, gratitude, appreciation | Antonyms: ingratitude, resentment | noun
