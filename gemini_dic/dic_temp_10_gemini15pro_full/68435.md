falterer:/ˈfɔːltərər (UK), ˈfɑːltərər (US)| n.|踌躇者，犹豫不决的人；支吾者|A person who hesitates or stammers. |He was a falterer in the face of danger.  他面对危险时犹豫不决。|近义词：hesitator, waverer, stammerer|反义词: decisive person, confident person|noun

falter:/ˈfɔːltər (UK), ˈfɑːltərər (US)| vi.| 1. 蹒跚；踉跄；摇晃: To move unsteadily; stumble. |His steps faltered as he climbed the steep hill. 他爬上陡峭的山坡时脚步踉跄。|近义词：stumble, stagger, wobble|反义词: stride, walk steadily|verb
falter:/ˈfɔːltər (UK), ˈfɑːltərər (US)| vi.| 2. 犹豫；迟疑；支吾: To speak hesitantly; stammer. |Her voice faltered as she delivered the bad news.  她 menyampaikan berita buruk 时声音颤抖。|近义词：hesitate, waver, stammer|反义词: speak fluently, be decisive|verb
falter:/ˈfɔːltər (UK), ˈfɑːltərər (US)| vi.| 3.  衰退；减弱；动摇: To lose strength or momentum; weaken. |The economy began to falter after the stock market crash. 股市崩盘后，经济开始衰退。|近义词：weaken, decline, fade|反义词: strengthen, improve, grow|verb
