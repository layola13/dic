constant:/ˈkɑːnstənt/ (US), /ˈkɒnstənt/ (UK)| adj. n.| 持续的，不变的；常数，常量 |Something that is constant stays the same over a period of time.| The temperature remained constant throughout the experiment.  实验过程中温度保持恒定。| synonyms: continuous, unchanging, invariable, uniform, consistent, regular, persistent, perpetual, incessant, ceaseless | antonyms: variable, changing, fluctuating, intermittent, irregular, sporadic | adjective

constant:/ˈkɑːnstənt/ (US), /ˈkɒnstənt/ (UK)| n.| 常数，常量 |A constant is a numerical value that does not change.| The speed of light is a fundamental constant in physics. 光速是物理学中的一个基本常数。| synonyms: fixed value, parameter | antonyms: variable | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French constant, or from Latin constans, constant- ‘standing firm, steadfast’, present participle of constare ‘stand firm’.
