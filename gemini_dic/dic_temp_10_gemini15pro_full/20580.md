blackcoat: /ˈblækˌkoʊt/ (US) /ˈblækˌkəʊt/ (UK)
|n.|黑衣人; 牧师(尤指苏格兰长老会的); |A person, especially a clergyman (particularly a Presbyterian minister), who wears a black coat.  Originally used to refer to members of the clergy who wore black coats as part of their clerical attire.  The term can also be used more generally to describe anyone who habitually wears a black coat.| The blackcoat preached a fiery sermon. 那位黑衣牧师讲了一篇热情洋溢的布道。|Synonyms: clergyman, minister, preacher, parson, reverend |Antonyms: layman, parishioner| noun
