croaker:/ˈkroʊkər/ (US) /ˈkrəʊkə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 呱呱叫的人/动物；抱怨者；石首鱼|A person or animal that croaks; a complainer; a type of fish.|The old man was a croaker, always predicting doom and gloom.  那个老头是个悲观主义者，总是预言厄运。| The fisherman caught a large croaker. 渔夫钓到了一条大石首鱼。|synonyms: complainer, pessimist, grumbler, fish (specifically Sciaenidae family)|antonyms: optimist, enthusiast|noun
croak:/kroʊk/ (US) /krəʊk/ (UK)| v.  n.|发出低沉沙哑的声音(如蛙鸣)；抱怨；死亡 |To make a deep hoarse sound, like a frog; to complain; to die. |The frogs croaked loudly in the swamp. 青蛙在沼泽里大声呱呱叫。| "I'm doomed!" he croaked. “我完蛋了！”他沙哑地说。 |synonyms: complain, grumble, groan, croak (noun)|antonyms: whisper, be quiet|verb & noun
