lautverschiebung:/ˈlaʊtvɛɐ̯ˌʃiːbʊŋ/
n.
sound shift (specifically Grimm's Law and Verner's Law)
A systematic sound change affecting all instances of a particular phoneme in a language or family of languages.  Grimm's Law describes the sound changes from Proto-Indo-European to Proto-Germanic.
Grimm's Law is a famous example of Lautverschiebung. 格里姆定律是语音转换的一个著名例子。
The Lautverschiebung helped shape the Germanic languages. 语音转换有助于塑造日耳曼语系。
近义词: sound change, phonetic shift
反义词: sound retention
