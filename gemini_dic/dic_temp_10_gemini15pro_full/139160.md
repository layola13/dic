obfuscatory:/ˌɒbfʌˈskeɪtəri/ /ˌɑːbfʌˈskeɪtɔːri/| adj.| 模糊的，混乱的，故弄玄虚的 | Making something obscure or unclear, especially intentionally. obfuscate:/ˈɒbfʌskeɪt/ /ˈɑːbfʌˈskeɪt/| v.| 使模糊，使混乱，使困惑 | To make something obscure or unclear, especially intentionally. To render indistinct or dim; darken. |  The speaker obfuscated the issue with jargon and technical terms.  演讲者用术语和专业词汇混淆了这个问题。| synonyms: confuse, bewilder, mislead, obscure, cloud, complicate, muddle, blur, veil, shroud, mask | antonyms: clarify, elucidate, explain, illuminate, simplify | 词性: 动词

obfuscation:/ˌɒbfʌˈskeɪʃn/ /ˌɑːbfʌˈskeɪʃn/| n.| 模糊，混乱，困惑 | The act of making something obscure or unclear, especially intentionally. The state of being obscured or unclear. | The lawyer's obfuscation of the facts made it difficult for the jury to reach a verdict. 律师对事实的混淆使得陪审团难以作出裁决。 | synonyms: confusion, bewilderment, misleading, unclearness, vagueness, ambiguity, opacity, enigma, mystification, obscurity | antonyms: clarity, lucidity, transparency, straightforwardness, plainness, simplicity, unambiguousness | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *obfuscat-, past participle stem of obfuscare "to darken," from Latin ob "over, against" + fuscare "to darken," from fuscus "dark, dusky."
