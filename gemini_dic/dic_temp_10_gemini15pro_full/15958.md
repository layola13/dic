balsam: /ˈbɔːlsəm/ (US) /ˈbɔːlsəm/ (UK)| n. adj.|香脂，香膏；凤仙花；慰藉|A fragrant, resinous substance, typically one with medicinal properties, obtained from certain trees and other plants. Also refers to a tree that yields such a substance, or a garden plant with brightly coloured flowers (Impatiens).  Can also be used figuratively to mean something soothing or comforting.|The air was filled with the balsam of the pine trees. 空气中充满了松树的香味。|Synonyms: balm, ointment, salve, unguent, lotion, liniment, emollient, anodyne, Impatiens|Antonyms: irritant, stimulant|noun, adjective


balsamic: /bɔlˈsæmɪk/| adj.|香脂的，香膏的；有镇痛作用的|Having the qualities of or containing balsam; soothing.|She used a balsamic vinegar in the salad dressing. 她在沙拉酱里用了香醋。|Synonyms: fragrant, aromatic, soothing, healing|Antonyms: acrid, pungent, irritating|adjective
