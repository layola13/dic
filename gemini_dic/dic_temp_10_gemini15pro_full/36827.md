colonies: /ˈkɒləniz/ (British) /ˈkɑːləniz/ (American)| n. |殖民地|A country or area under the full or partial political control of another country, typically a distant one, and occupied by settlers from that country. |例句：Many European countries had colonies in Africa. 许多欧洲国家在非洲都有殖民地。

例句：The thirteen colonies declared independence from Britain. 十三个殖民地宣布脱离英国独立。

近义词：settlements, territories, dependencies, possessions|反义词: homeland, motherland |词性: noun

colonies: /ˈkɒləniz/ (British) /ˈkɑːləniz/ (American)| n. |群体|A group of animals or plants of the same kind living close together. |例句：A colony of ants was busy collecting food. 一群蚂蚁正忙着收集食物。

例句：There's a large colony of seabirds nesting on the cliffs. 悬崖上有一个大型海鸟群的巢穴。

近义词：community, group, cluster, population|反义词: individual |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French colonie, from Latin colonia (“farm, landed estate, settlement”), from colonus (“farmer, husbandman, settler, planter”), related to colere (“to till, cultivate, inhabit”).
