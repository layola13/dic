accustomed-to: /əˈkʌstəmd tuː/
adjective
习惯于
Used to something; familiar with something.  This phrase indicates a state of being used to or familiar with a particular situation, activity, or thing.
She is accustomed to the city noise. 她习惯了城市的喧嚣。

He was accustomed to getting up early. 他习惯早起。

近义词: used to, familiar with, habituated to
反义词: unaccustomed to, unfamiliar with, unused to
词性: adjective (短语形容词)

ETYMOLOGY->
From the past participle of the verb "accustom," which comes from the Old French "acoustumer," meaning "to make familiar."  This, in turn, derives from the Vulgar Latin "*accostumare," a combination of "ad-" (to) and "consuetudo" (custom, habit).

USAGE->
Accustomed to is typically followed by a noun or a gerund (verb ending in -ing).

I'm accustomed to the cold weather. 我习惯了寒冷的天气。
They are accustomed to working long hours. 他们习惯了长时间工作。

He's become accustomed to living alone. 他已经习惯了独自生活。
She's not accustomed to driving on the left side of the road.  她不习惯在道路的左侧驾驶。


PHRASE->

become accustomed to + noun/gerund 变得习惯于…
get accustomed to + noun/gerund  逐渐习惯于…
not accustomed to + noun/gerund 不习惯于…
