haemophilia:/ˌhiːməˈfɪliə/ (US) /ˌhiːməˈfɪliə/ (UK)| n.|血友病|A medical condition, typically inherited by males from their mother, in which the blood clots slowly or not at all.|He suffers from haemophilia and has to be careful not to cut himself.|Synonym: bleeder's disease|Antonym: normal blood clotting|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *haîma* (blood) + *-philia* (loving).  The sense is that the disease "loves blood" because it causes bleeding.
