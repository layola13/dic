abecedarian:/ˌeɪˌbiːˌsiːˈdɛəriən/| adj. n.|按字母顺序的；初学者；|arranged alphabetically; a beginner in any field of learning|He is an abecedarian in the world of finance. 他是金融界的新手。|近义词：alphabetical, elementary, rudimentary|反义词: advanced, complex|名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *abecedarius* "alphabetical," from the first four letters of the Latin alphabet (a, be, ce, de), equivalent to Latin *abecedarium*.  Ultimately from Greek *alphabētos*.

USAGE->
Example as adjective:
The abecedarian arrangement of the books made them easy to find. = 书籍按字母顺序排列，很容易找到。
She wrote an abecedarian poem for her child. = 她为她的孩子写了一首字母诗。

Example as noun:
He was still an abecedarian in the art of cooking. = 他在烹饪艺术方面仍然是初学者。
The abecedarians were given a simple task to complete. = 初学者被分配了一项简单的任务。
