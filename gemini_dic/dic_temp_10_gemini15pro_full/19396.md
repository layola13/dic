bicarburetted:/baɪˈkɑːrbjʊˌrɛtɪd/
adjective
双碳化合物的；加倍碳化的
Combined with twice the normal amount of carbon.  Specifically, combined with two carbon atoms or radicals.
This term is largely archaic and rarely used in modern chemistry.  It was more common in the 19th century.
Bicarbureted hydrogen was an old name for ethylene (C2H4).
