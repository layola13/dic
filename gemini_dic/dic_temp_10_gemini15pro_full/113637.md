machination:/ˌmækɪˈneɪʃən/
n.
阴谋，诡计，策划
a crafty plot; a scheme.
例句：The politician's downfall was caused by the machinations of his rivals. 这位政治家的垮台是由他对手的阴谋诡计造成的。

例句：His success was due to hard work, not through any machinations. 他的成功是由于努力工作，而不是任何阴谋诡计。

近义词：plot, scheme, intrigue, conspiracy
反义词：fairness, honesty


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin machinat-, machinat- "contrived," past participle stem of machinari "to contrive, devise, plot," from machina "machine, engine, device, contrivance," from Greek mēkhanē "contrivance, means, expedient, machine."
