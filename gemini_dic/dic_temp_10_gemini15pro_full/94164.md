impenitence:/ɪmˈpɛnɪtəns/
|n.|不悔悟，顽固，执迷不悟
|Lack of remorse or regret for wrongdoing; stubborn refusal to repent.|
|His impenitence made it difficult for anyone to forgive him. 他的顽固不悔使得任何人都难以原谅他。|
|The judge was shocked by the defendant's impenitence. 法官对被告的顽固不悔感到震惊。|
|近义词: remorselessness, obduracy, hardness, |
|反义词: penitence, remorse, contrition, repentance|
|词性: 名词|


impenitent:/ɪmˈpɛnɪtənt/
|adj.|不悔悟的，顽固的，执迷不悟的
|Not feeling or showing regret for wrongdoing.|
|He remained impenitent despite the evidence against him. 尽管证据确凿，他仍然拒不悔改。|
|Her impenitent attitude angered her parents. 她不悔改的态度激怒了她的父母。|
|近义词: remorseless, unrepentant, obdurate, |
|反义词: penitent, remorseful, contrite, repentant|
|词性: 形容词|


词源: Late Middle English: from Old French impenitent, from Latin impenitens, from in- ‘not’ + paenitens ‘repenting’.
