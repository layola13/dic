beanstalk: /ˈbiːnˌstɔːk/ | n. | 豆茎，豆杆；魔豆的茎（常指童话《杰克与魔豆》中的魔豆茎） | A fast-growing stem of a bean plant, especially the giant one in the fairy tale "Jack and the Beanstalk". | Jack climbed the magic beanstalk. 杰克爬上了魔豆茎。 | 近义词: stem, stalk | 反义词: (无直接反义词) | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
From bean + stalk.  "Bean" comes from Old English *bēan, from Proto-Germanic *baunō, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *bʰābh- ("bean").  "Stalk" comes from Old English stelca, stela, related to Old High German stilli, meaning "prop, support."
