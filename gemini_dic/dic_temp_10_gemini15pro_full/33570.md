cilium:/ˈsɪliəm/ (singular), cilia:/ˈsɪliə/ (plural)| n.|纤毛|A short, microscopic, hairlike vibrating structure found in large numbers on the surface of certain cells, either causing currents in the surrounding fluid, or, in some protozoans and other small organisms, providing propulsion.|The cilia of the respiratory tract sweep out mucus and debris.|近义词:flagellum (for locomotion), hair (general term)|反义词:None|Noun

cilium:/ˈsɪliəm/ (singular), cilia:/ˈsɪliə/ (plural)| n.|睫毛 (less common usage)|An eyelash.|Tears welled up in her cilia.|近义词:eyelash|反义词:None|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cilium* "eyelid, eyelash," of uncertain origin.
