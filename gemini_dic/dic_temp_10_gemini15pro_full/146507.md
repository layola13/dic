overgeneralizations:/ˌoʊvərˌdʒɛnərələˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. | 过度概括；以偏概全 | The process of extending a rule or observation to cases where it does not apply.  It often occurs in language learning, where children might apply grammatical rules too broadly. | Young children often make overgeneralizations, like saying "goed" instead of "went."  小孩子经常会过度概括，比如用“goed”代替“went”。|  Overgeneralizations can lead to stereotypes and prejudice. 过度概括会导致刻板印象和偏见。 | synonyms: generalizations, sweeping statements, stereotypes | antonyms:  specific examples, nuanced understanding | noun

overgeneralize: /ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪz/ | v. | 过度概括；以偏概全 | To draw a conclusion or make a statement about a larger group based on limited or insufficient evidence. |  It's easy to overgeneralize about a culture based on a single experience.  很容易根据单一经验对一种文化进行过度概括。|  He tends to overgeneralize about women.  他倾向于对女性进行过度概括。| synonyms:  generalize excessively, stereotype, make sweeping statements | antonyms: specify, be precise, differentiate | verb


overgeneralized: /ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪzd/ | adj. | 过度概括的 | Characterized by or resulting from overgeneralization. |  His overgeneralized conclusions about the group were offensive.  他对这个群体过度概括的结论具有冒犯性。|  The study's findings were criticized for being based on overgeneralized data.  这项研究的发现因其基于过度概括的数据而受到批评。| synonyms:  sweeping, stereotypical, broad-brush | antonyms: specific, nuanced, detailed | adjective
