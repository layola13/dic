megalocyte: /ˈmɛɡəlɵˌsaɪt/ | n. | 巨红细胞 | An abnormally large red blood cell, characteristic of pernicious anemia and certain other disorders. | Example 1: The blood test revealed a significant number of megalocytes, indicating a possible vitamin B12 deficiency. 血液检查显示大量的巨红细胞，表明可能缺乏维生素B12。

Example 2:  The presence of megalocytes in the bone marrow confirmed the diagnosis of megaloblastic anemia. 骨髓中巨红细胞的存在证实了巨幼红细胞性贫血的诊断。 | Synonym: macrocyte | Antonym: microcyte | noun

Etymology: mega- (large) + -cyte (cell)  From Greek *μέγας (megas, “great, large”) + κύτος (kytos, “a hollow, receptacle, the body of a vessel, a jar”).
