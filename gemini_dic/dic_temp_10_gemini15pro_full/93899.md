immortalized: /ɪˈmɔːrtəlaɪzd/ | v. | 使不朽，使永垂不朽 | to make someone or something famous or remembered for a long time |  His heroic deeds were immortalized in song and story. 他的英雄事迹在歌谣和故事中永垂不朽。| immortalise (British English) |  forgotten, obscure | verb

immortalized: /ɪˈmɔːrtəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 不朽的，永生的| made famous or remembered for a long time | The actress was immortalized by her role in the classic film.  这位女演员因在经典电影中的角色而名垂青史。|  immortal, legendary | forgotten, obscure | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *immortalisen*, from Late Latin *immortalizāre*, from Latin *immortālis* ("immortal") + *-izāre* (suffix forming verbs).  The English word is a back-formation from *immortalization*.
