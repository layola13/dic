doctrinarianism:/ˌdɒktrɪˈnɛːrɪənɪzəm/ | n. | 教条主义，学究主义 |  The tendency to adhere rigidly to a set of doctrines or principles, especially in politics or religion, regardless of practical considerations or changing circumstances.  It implies a narrow-mindedness and inflexibility in applying these doctrines. | He was accused of doctrinarianism for refusing to compromise on his political beliefs. 他因拒绝在政治信仰上妥协而被指责为教条主义。|  dogmatism, rigidity, orthodoxy | pragmatism, flexibility, open-mindedness | noun
