mettled:/ˈmetld/ | adj. | 勇敢的，有精神的 | Having courage and fortitude; spirited. |  He showed his mettle in the face of danger.  他面对危险展现出了他的勇气。| 近义词: courageous, spirited, plucky, valiant | 反义词: cowardly, timid, spineless | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Middle English *imeteled, past participle of imetelien (“to measure, test, appraise”), from Old English metan (“to measure”) + -eled, suffix forming past participles.  The original sense was "tested and proven, of good quality," which evolved to the current meaning of "showing courage."
