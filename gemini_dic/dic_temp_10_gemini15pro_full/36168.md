coffer:/ˈkɒfə(r) (UK), /ˈkɔːfər (US)| n.|保险箱，金库；(尤指教堂、寺庙等公共建筑的) 奉献箱；天花板的凹格|A strongbox or chest for holding valuables, especially money. A recessed panel in a ceiling.|The church coffers were empty after the costly renovations. 教堂在昂贵的翻修后，奉献箱空空如也。|chest, strongbox, safe, treasury|debt, expenditure|noun


coffer:/ˈkɒfə(r) (UK), /ˈkɔːfər (US)| n.|围堰|A watertight enclosure pumped dry to permit construction work below the waterline.|The engineers built a cofferdam to repair the bridge foundations. 工程师们建造了一个围堰来修复桥梁基础。|caisson, dam|open water|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French cofre, from Latin cophinus "basket, chest," from Greek kophinos "basket."
