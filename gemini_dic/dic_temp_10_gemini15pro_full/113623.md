machiavellian: /ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | adj. n.| 马基雅维利的；权谋的；不择手段的 |  Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian political philosopher known for his book "The Prince," which advocated pragmatism and amorality in political leadership. |  His machiavellian tactics ensured his rise to power. 他的权谋手段确保了他掌权。 | Synonyms: cunning, devious, scheming, unscrupulous, artful, wily | Antonyms: honest, principled, straightforward, ethical, scrupulous | adjective, noun

machiavelli: /ˌmækiəˈveli/ | n. | 马基雅维利；权谋家 | Niccolò di Bernardo dei Machiavelli (3 May 1469 – 21 June 1527) was an Italian Renaissance diplomat, philosopher, historian, politician, playwright, and poet. He is best known for his book *The Prince* (Il Principe), written c. 1513.  He is often regarded as the "Father of Modern Political Philosophy and Political Science". | He has been called the "father of modern political philosophy and political science." 他被称为“现代政治哲学和政治科学之父”。 | Synonyms:  schemer, strategist, tactician | Antonyms: moralist, idealist | noun
