galled:/ɡɔːld/ | adj. | 恼怒的，激怒的；擦伤的 | irritated; chafed; feeling bitter resentment or anger.  |  He felt galled by her constant criticism. 他被她不断的批评激怒了。 | Synonyms: irritated, annoyed, vexed, angered, irked, chafed, rankled,  | Antonyms: pleased, soothed, calmed, gratified | adjective


galled:/ɡɔːld/ | v. | (使)擦伤；(使)恼怒 |  (of something restrictive or irritating) rub the skin to make it sore. | The tight collar galled his neck. 紧的衣领擦伤了他的脖子。| Synonyms: chafe, rub sore, abrade, graze, skin, excoriate | Antonyms: soothe, heal | verb


Etymology:
From Middle English *gallen, from Old Norse galli (“fault, flaw, defect”).  Related to Old English gealla (“gall”).  The sense of "irritate" developed from the physical irritation caused by chafing.
