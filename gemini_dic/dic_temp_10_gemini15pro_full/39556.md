conscientious:/ˌkɒnʃiˈenʃəs/| adj.|认真的，尽责的，一丝不苟的| Guided by or in accordance with conscience or sense of right and wrong; principled.  Characterized by extreme care and great effort.| She is a conscientious student who always does her homework. 她是一位认真的学生，总是认真完成作业。|He's a conscientious worker, always striving to do his best. 他是一位尽责的员工，总是力求做到最好。|近义词：meticulous, diligent, careful, thorough, scrupulous|反义词：careless, negligent, irresponsible|词性: adjective

conscience:/ˈkɒnʃ(ə)ns/| n.|良心，道德心|An inner feeling or voice viewed as acting as a guide to the rightness or wrongness of one's behavior.|I have a guilty conscience about not telling her the truth.  我没有告诉她真相，感到良心不安。|He had a clear conscience. 他问心无愧。|近义词：morals, scruples|反义词：N/A|词性: noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin conscientia “knowledge within oneself, consciousness,” from Latin consciens, present participle of conscire “to be conscious,” from com- “with” + scire “to know.”
