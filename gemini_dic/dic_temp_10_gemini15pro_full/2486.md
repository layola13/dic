ad-lib:/ædˈlɪb/| adv. adj. n. v. |即兴的，即兴表演，即兴创作，即兴表演|To improvise or perform something spontaneously, without preparation.  It can be used as an adverb, adjective, noun, or verb.|The comedian ad-libbed a joke about the current events.  这位喜剧演员即兴讲了一个关于时事的笑话。

The actor forgot his lines and had to ad-lib the rest of the scene.  这个演员忘了台词，不得不即兴表演完剩下的场景。|近义词：improvise, extemporize, off-the-cuff|反义词：rehearsed, planned, scripted|词性: adverb, adjective, noun, verb

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often ad-libs during his stand-up routine. = 他在单口喜剧表演中经常即兴发挥。
She ad-libs when she forgets her lines. = 她忘记台词时会即兴发挥。

一般过去时:
The musician ad-libbed a solo during the concert. = 这位音乐家在音乐会上即兴演奏了一段独奏。
He ad-libbed a speech at the wedding. = 他在婚礼上即兴发表了演讲。

现在完成时:
The actors have ad-libbed several scenes in the play. = 演员们在这部戏中即兴表演了几个场景。
She has ad-libbed her way through many difficult situations. = 她凭借即兴发挥克服了许多困境。

过去完成时:
The comedian had ad-libbed a joke before realizing the microphone was off. = 喜剧演员即兴讲了个笑话，才意识到麦克风关了。
He had ad-libbed the entire presentation because he forgot his notes. = 他即兴完成了整个演讲，因为他忘了笔记。

将来时:
I will ad-lib if I forget my lines during the presentation. = 如果我在演讲中忘记台词，我会即兴发挥。
They will ad-lib a scene in the play tomorrow. = 他们明天将在这部戏中即兴表演一个场景。

虚拟语气:
If I were to ad-lib, I would probably mess it up. = 如果我要即兴发挥，我可能会搞砸。
I wish I could ad-lib as well as he can. = 我希望我能像他一样即兴发挥。


被动语态:

疑问句:
Did he ad-lib that part of the speech? = 他那部分演讲是即兴的吗？
Can you ad-lib a song right now? = 你现在能即兴唱首歌吗？

否定句:
She didn't ad-lib during the performance. = 表演期间她没有即兴发挥。
I can't ad-lib in front of a large audience. = 我不能在很多人面前即兴表演。
