orotundities: /ˌɔːrəˈtʌndɪtiz/ | n. | 夸夸其谈，华而不实的言辞 |  Pompous or inflated language; excessive use of grand or high-sounding words. | The politician's speech was filled with orotundities, but lacked any real substance. 这位政客的演讲充满了夸夸其谈，但却缺乏任何实质内容。| synonyms: bombast, grandiloquence, pomposity | antonyms: simplicity, conciseness, understatement | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *orotundus* "round-mouthed, full-voiced," from *os* "mouth" + *rotundus* "round."  The original meaning referred to a full, resonant voice, but the meaning shifted to describe the kind of pompous language often associated with such a voice.
