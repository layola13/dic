commercialised:/kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪzd/ | adj. |商业化的|Made to appeal to a wide audience and sold for profit; exploited for profit.|例句：The festival has become increasingly commercialised in recent years.  近年来，这个节日越来越商业化了。|例句：Critics argue that the movie industry has become too commercialised, focusing on profits over artistic merit.  评论家认为，电影业变得过于商业化，只注重利润而忽略了艺术价值。|近义词：marketed, merchandised, monetized|反义词：non-commercial, uncommercialized|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From commercialize + -ed.  Commercialize comes from commercial + -ize. Commercial ultimately derives from Latin "commerciālis" meaning "pertaining to trade".
