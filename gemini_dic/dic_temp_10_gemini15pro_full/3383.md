affy:/əˈfaɪ/ | v. | 订婚，保证，宣称 | To promise or pledge solemnly; to betroth. | He affied himself to her. 他和她订婚了。|  She affied that she would tell the truth. 她保证会说实话。| 近义词：pledge, vow, promise, betroth | 反义词：renounce, disavow | 词性: 动词

PHRASE->
affy oneself to someone  与某人订婚
affy one's faith/loyalty  宣誓自己的信念/忠诚
affy that...  保证...
(These are the most common usages; distinct "phrases" with affy are rare.)


词源:  Middle English *affien*, from Old French *affier* "to trust, confide," from Latin *affidare*, from *ad- "to" + *fidare* "to trust," from *fides* "faith."


注意事项:
Affy is an archaic word and rarely used in modern English.  It is more commonly found in historical or literary contexts.  While grammatically correct in all tenses, its archaic nature makes some constructions sound unnatural in contemporary usage.
