epicure:/ˈɛpɪkjʊr/ | n. | 美食家；享乐主义者 | A person who takes particular pleasure in fine food and drink. | He was known as an epicure, always seeking out the finest restaurants. 他以美食家著称，总是寻找最好的餐厅。| 近义词:gourmet, gourmand, connoisseur | 反义词:ascetic | 词性: 名词

epicurean:/ˌɛpɪˈkjʊəriən/ | adj. |  享乐主义的；追求感官享受的 | Devoted to the pursuit of pleasure; relating to Epicurus or his philosophy. |  She led an epicurean lifestyle, indulging in fine wines and luxurious travel. 她过着享乐主义的生活方式，沉迷于美酒和奢华旅行。| 近义词:hedonistic, luxurious, sybaritic | 反义词:ascetic, spartan | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *épicurien*, from Latin *Epicūrus*,  the name of a Greek philosopher (341-270 BC) who taught that pleasure is the highest good.  His name ultimately derives from Greek *epikouros* "helper, assistant".
