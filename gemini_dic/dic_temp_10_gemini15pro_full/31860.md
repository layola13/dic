cheerier:/ˈtʃɪriər/ | adj. | 更快乐的，更兴高采烈的 | Comparative form of cheery: more cheery.  Meaning happier and more cheerful. |  The flowers made the room cheerier.  这些花让房间更欢快了。| Synonyms: happier, more cheerful, brighter, merrier, jollier | Antonyms: gloomier, sadder, more depressing, drearier | adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *cherier, comparative of *cheri (“cheery”), from Old French cher (“dear, expensive, beloved”), from Late Latin cārus (“dear, costly”).
