only-too:/ˈəʊnli tuː/ | adv. | 非常；实在太；极其 | Used to emphasize how much something is true, especially something negative or undesirable.  It indicates an excessive degree of a quality, often with negative connotations.  The feeling expressed is usually one of regret, disappointment, or exasperation. |  I'm only too aware of my shortcomings. 我非常清楚自己的缺点。| Synonyms: very, extremely, excessively, all too | Antonyms: slightly, hardly, barely | adverb
