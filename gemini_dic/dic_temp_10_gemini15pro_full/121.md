abanaki:/əˈbɑːnəkiː/
noun
阿贝纳基人（北美印第安人的一支）
A member of an Algonquian people formerly inhabiting northern New England and adjacent parts of Canada, now living primarily in Vermont, New Hampshire, and Quebec.
The Abanaki tribe held annual powwows to celebrate their heritage.  阿贝纳基部落每年都会举行盛会来庆祝他们的传统。

The Abanaki traditionally lived along the Connecticut River valley. 阿贝纳基人传统上居住在康涅狄格河谷。
近义词:Algonquian, Native American
反义词: (No direct antonym for a specific tribe)
词性:n


abanaki:/əˈbɑːnəkiː/
adjective
阿贝纳基人的；阿贝纳基语的
Relating to the Abanaki people or their language.
The Abanaki language is part of the Algonquian family. 阿贝纳基语属于阿尔冈昆语系。

Abanaki basketry is known for its intricate designs. 阿贝纳基人的编篮工艺以其复杂的图案而闻名。
近义词:Algonquian
反义词:(No direct antonym for a tribal adjective)
词性:adj
