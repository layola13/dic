blackleg:/ˈblækˌleg/ | n. | 破坏罢工者；骗子；（尤指牛的）黑腿病 | A person who continues to work during a strike, especially one who accepts employment at less than the standard wage. Also, a swindler or cheat, and a disease of cattle and sheep characterized by gas gangrene. |  The strikers were furious at the blacklegs who crossed the picket line. 罢工者对那些越过纠察线的破坏罢工者感到愤怒。| Synonyms: scab, strikebreaker, cheat, fraud | Antonyms: striker, unionist | Noun

blackleg:/ˈblækˌleg/ | v. |  破坏罢工；诈骗 | To act as a blackleg; to swindle or cheat. | He was accused of blacklegging during the miners' strike. 他被指控在矿工罢工期间破坏罢工。| Synonyms: scab, double-cross, defraud | Antonyms: support, uphold | Verb
