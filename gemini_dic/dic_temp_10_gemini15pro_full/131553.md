noncommodiously:/ˌnɒnkəˈməʊdiəsli/
|adv.|不方便地，不舒适地|Not in a commodious or spacious manner; inconveniently; uncomfortably.|The small apartment was noncommodiously furnished, with barely enough room to move around.|Synonyms: inconveniently, uncomfortably, crampedly, tightly, restrictively|Antonyms: commodiously, spaciously, comfortably, conveniently|词性: 副词

noncommodious:/ˌnɒnkəˈməʊdiəs/
|adj.|不宽敞的，不方便的|Not commodious; not spacious or convenient.|The noncommodious cabin made for a cramped journey.|Synonyms: cramped, inconvenient, uncomfortable, restricted, tight|Antonyms: commodious, spacious, comfortable, convenient|词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + commodious.  "Commodious" comes from the Late Latin "commodiosus," meaning "advantageous, suitable," from Latin "commodus" ("convenient, proper").  The prefix "non-" simply negates the meaning.
