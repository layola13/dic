break-with:/breɪk wɪð/ | phrasal verb | 断绝关系，放弃，与…决裂 | To end a relationship or association with someone or something; to abandon a tradition or practice. | He broke with his family after a bitter argument. 他在一次激烈的争吵后与家人断绝了关系。| 近义词：separate from, part from, renounce, abandon | 反义词：unite with, join, embrace | 词性：动词短语

词源:  "Break" comes from Old English "brecan," meaning "to shatter, destroy." "With" comes from Old English "wiþ," meaning "against, opposite, towards, near." The combination "break with" signifies a decisive separation or rupture.
