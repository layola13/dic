opinicus:/oʊˈpɪnɪkəs/| n. |狮鹫兽；臆想之物|A fabulous beast, often described as a griffin with the head and wings of an eagle, the body of a lion, and sometimes the tail of a horse or wolf. It is often used to symbolize vigilance or guardianship, but can also represent the dangers of imagination and delusion.|The opinicus, a creature of myth, was said to guard vast treasures.|近义词：griffin, chimera|反义词: (No direct antonym, as it's a mythical creature.  Perhaps "reality" in a conceptual sense)|词性:noun
