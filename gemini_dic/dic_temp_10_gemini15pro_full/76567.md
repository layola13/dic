gallfly:/ˈɡɔːlflaɪ/ | n. | 五倍子蜂，瘿蜂 | A small wasp-like insect that lays its eggs in plants, causing galls to form. | The gallfly lays its eggs on oak trees, which then produce galls.  五倍子蜂在橡树上产卵，然后橡树会产生虫瘿。 | synonyms: gall wasp, cynipid | antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for a specific insect) | noun
