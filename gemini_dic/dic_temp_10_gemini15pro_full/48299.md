decrees:/dɪˈkriːz/| n. pl.  v. (third-person singular present tense)|法令，命令；判决（decree 的复数）|An official order that has the force of law; a judgment of a court. |The king issued several decrees regarding taxes. 国王发布了几项关于税收的法令。|
decrees:/dɪˈkriːz/| v. (third-person singular present tense)|颁布法令，规定；判决|To issue a decree; to order something by decree; to decide or order judicially. |The court decreed that the company must pay a fine. 法院判决该公司必须支付罚款。|
decree:/dɪˈkriː/| n.|法令，命令；判决|An official order that has the force of law; a judgment of a court. |The decree was met with widespread opposition. 这项法令遭到广泛反对。|近义词：edict, order, ruling, judgment, ordinance|反义词：appeal, petition|
decree:/dɪˈkriː/| v.|颁布法令，规定；判决|To issue a decree; to order something by decree; to decide or order judicially. |Fate decreed that they would meet again. 命运注定他们会再次相遇。|近义词：ordain, command, pronounce, rule, adjudge|反义词：appeal, petition|词性: 名词(n.), 动词(v.)|词源：From Old French *decreer (Modern French décerner), from Latin decernere "to decide, determine, decree," from de- "down, completely" + cernere "to separate, distinguish, perceive."
