abstractionists: /æbˈstrækʃənɪsts/
n.
抽象主义者
Artists who create abstract art, focusing on form, color, and composition rather than realistic representation.
The exhibition showcased works by several prominent abstractionists.  这场展览展示了几位著名抽象主义者的作品。

The museum's collection includes paintings by early abstractionists like Kandinsky and Mondrian.  博物馆的收藏包括康定斯基和蒙德里安等早期抽象主义者的绘画。

近义词:  non-representational artists, abstract artists
反义词:  realists, representational artists
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From abstraction + -ist.  Abstraction comes from Late Latin abstractionem (nominative abstractio) "a drawing away, withdrawal," noun of action from past-participle stem of abstrahere "to draw away," from ab- "away" + trahere "to draw."  The -ist suffix is used to form agent nouns, denoting a person who practices or is skilled in something.
