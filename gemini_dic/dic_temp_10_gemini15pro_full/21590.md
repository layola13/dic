bluffy:/ˈblʌfi/| adj.|虚张声势的；吓唬人的；爱吹牛的|Given to or characterized by bluffing.  Pretending to be stronger or more confident than one really is.|He put on a bluffy exterior, but inside he was terrified. 他装出一副虚张声势的样子，但内心却很害怕。|近义词：boastful, swaggering, pretentious|反义词：modest, humble, unassuming|词性:形容词

词源: From bluff (n.) + -y (suffix forming adjectives).  The noun "bluff" originally referred to a steep cliff or bank, and later came to mean a pretense of strength or confidence, perhaps because such a cliff might appear daunting.
