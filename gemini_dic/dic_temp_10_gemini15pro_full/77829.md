generativism: /ˈdʒɛnərətɪvɪzəm/ | n. | 生成主义 | A linguistic theory that postulates the existence of an innate language faculty in the human mind, which allows humans to acquire and use language.  It emphasizes the creative aspect of language use and the ability to generate an infinite number of sentences from a finite set of rules. |  Generativism has profoundly influenced the field of linguistics. 生成主义深刻地影响了语言学领域。 |  Chomsky's work is central to generativism. 乔姆斯基的著作是生成主义的核心。 | synonyms: generative grammar, transformational grammar | antonyms: behaviorism, structuralism | noun
