aerobicses:/ɛˈroʊbɪksɪz/ | n. | 有氧运动 |  A form of physical exercise that combines rhythmic aerobic exercise with stretching and strength training routines with the goal of improving all elements of fitness (flexibility, muscular strength, and cardiorespiratory fitness).  It is usually performed to music and may be practiced in a group setting led by an instructor (aerobics instructor), although it can be done solo and without musical accompaniment. |  She does aerobicses three times a week. 她每周做三次有氧运动。| synonyms: aerobic exercises, cardio, workout | antonyms: inactivity, sedentary lifestyle | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From aerobic + -ics (plural suffix).  Aerobic itself comes from aero- (relating to air) + -bic (relating to life).  Ultimately from Greek *āēr* (air) and *bios* (life).
