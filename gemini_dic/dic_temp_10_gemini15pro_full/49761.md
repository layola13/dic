demolishment:/dɪˈmɒlɪʃmənt/ /dɪˈmɑːlɪʃmənt/| n. |拆除，毁坏|The act of demolishing or destroying something, especially a building.|The demolition of the old factory made way for a new shopping mall.  旧工厂的拆除为新的购物中心腾出了空间。|Synonyms: destruction,  razing, wrecking|Antonyms: construction, building,  establishment|noun

demolish:/dɪˈmɒlɪʃ/ /dɪˈmɑːlɪʃ/| vt. |拆除，毁坏；驳倒|To destroy (a building or other structure), especially by explosives; to thoroughly refute an argument or theory.|They demolished the old building to make way for a new park. 他们拆除了旧建筑，以便为新公园腾出空间。  The lawyer demolished the witness's testimony. 律师驳倒了证人的证词。|Synonyms: destroy, raze, wreck, dismantle|Antonyms: construct, build, establish|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *demoliss-, stem of demolir  "to demolish," from Latin demoliri "to pull down, tear down," from de- "down" + moliri "to build, erect, set in motion," from moles "mass, burden, pile."
