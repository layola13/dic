fabricators:/ˈfæbrɪkeɪtərz/
n.
制造者，装配者，捏造者
People who make or build something, especially using tools and machines; also refers to those who invent false information.
The fabricators worked tirelessly to complete the bridge. 制造者们不知疲倦地工作以完成桥梁的建造。
The journalist exposed the fabricators of the fake news story. 记者揭露了这条假新闻的捏造者。
Synonyms: manufacturers, producers, constructors, inventors, liars, falsifiers
Antonyms: destroyers, demolishers, consumers
noun

fabricate:/ˈfæbrɪkeɪt/
v.
制造，装配，捏造
To make or build something, especially using tools and machines; also, to invent false information.
The company fabricates high-quality steel components. 这家公司制造高质量的钢组件。
He fabricated a story about being robbed. 他编造了一个关于被抢劫的故事。
Synonyms: manufacture, produce, construct, invent, lie, falsify
Antonyms: destroy, demolish, consume
verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *fabricātor, from Latin fabricāre (“to make, construct, build”), from fabrica (“workshop, forge”), from faber (“craftsman, worker in hard materials”).
