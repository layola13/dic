overmystifications: /ˌoʊvərˌmɪstɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. | 故弄玄虚，过度神秘化 | The act of making something appear more mysterious or complicated than it actually is, often intentionally. |  His explanation of the simple process was filled with overmystifications, leaving the audience more confused than enlightened. 他对这个简单过程的解释充满了故弄玄虚，让听众更加困惑而不是明白。 |近义词: obfuscations, mystifications, complications | 反义词: clarifications, simplifications | 词性: 名词

词源:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much") + "mystification" (noun from "mystify," from "mystic" ultimately from Greek "mystes" meaning "one initiated into mysteries").
