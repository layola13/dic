obbligati:/ˌɒblɪˈɡɑːti/ | n. | 義務；責任；債務 (复数) |  A bond, especially one that is financial.  It is the plural form of "obbligato," which is less common. The singular form is usually used musically.  In Italian, "obbligato" means "obligated." |  The company's obbligati were substantial, leading to its eventual bankruptcy. 这家公司的债务十分庞大，最终导致破产。 | 近义词：debts, liabilities, obligations | 反义词：assets | 词性: 名词

obbligato:/ˌɒblɪˈɡɑːtoʊ/ | n. adj. | (音樂) obbligato；必須的；不可缺少的 |  (n.) A musical part that is essential to a piece and cannot be omitted. (adj.) Essential; indispensable. From Italian, meaning "obligated." |  (n.) The violin obbligato added a layer of complexity to the sonata. 小提琴 obbligato 为奏鸣曲增添了一层复杂性。
(adj.)  The conductor considered the cello part obbligato to the symphony's success. 指挥认为大提琴部分对于交响乐的成功至关重要。 | 近义词：(n.) accompaniment, solo, (adj.) essential, necessary, required | 反义词：(adj.) optional, unnecessary | 词性: 名词, 形容词
