hypervigilance:/ˌhaɪpərˈvɪdʒɪləns/| n.|过度警觉，高度警惕|The state of being highly sensitive to your surroundings, often as a result of trauma or stress.  This heightened awareness can lead to constantly scanning for potential threats, even when there is no real danger.|After the robbery, she experienced hypervigilance, constantly looking over her shoulder.|近义词:watchfulness, alertness, paranoia (in extreme cases)|反义词:calmness, relaxation|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- + vigilance.  "Hyper-" is a prefix of Greek origin meaning "over, above, beyond." "Vigilance" comes from the Latin "vigilantia," meaning "watchfulness," from "vigilans," present participle of "vigilare" "to keep watch."
