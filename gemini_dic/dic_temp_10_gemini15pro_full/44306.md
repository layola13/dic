crook:/krʊk/| n. |骗子；弯曲部分；钩；（牧羊人的）曲柄杖|A person who is dishonest or a criminal.  A curved or bent part of something. A hook or a shepherd's staff with a hooked end.| The police caught the crook red-handed. 警方当场抓获了那个骗子。

The crook of his arm was sore. 他的胳膊肘很痛。|Synonyms: criminal, thief, robber, swindler, cheat, fraudster, con artist, scoundrel, villain, rascal |Antonyms: honest person, law-abiding citizen|noun


crook:/krʊk/| v. |弯曲；使成钩状|To bend or curve something.|He crooked his finger to beckon her. 他弯曲手指示意她过来。 She crooked her arm around his. 她弯着胳膊搂住他。|Synonyms: bend, curve, hook |Antonyms: straighten|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *krókr meaning "hook, bend," related to Old English cróc "hook, crook."
