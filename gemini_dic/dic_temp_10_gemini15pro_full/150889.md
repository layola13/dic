paraleipsis:/ˌpærəˈlaɪpsɪs/ | n. |  佯谬, 假省,  欲盖弥彰 |  A rhetorical device in which one pretends to omit or pass over something, but in doing so, actually emphasizes it.  | Example: "I won't even mention his previous failures, his lack of experience, or his questionable ethics."  (This clearly emphasizes the very things the speaker claims to be omitting.) | Synonym: apophasis, preterition | Antonym: explicit mention, direct statement | noun

paraleipsis:/ˌpærəˈlaɪpsɪs/ | n. |佯谬, 假省, 欲盖弥彰|The speaker or writer brings up a subject by denying that it should be brought up.| Example:  "Not to mention your poor performance on the project, let's move on to the next topic." (This emphasizes the poor performance while pretending to move on.) | Synonym: preterition, occupatio | Antonym: directness, candidness | noun
