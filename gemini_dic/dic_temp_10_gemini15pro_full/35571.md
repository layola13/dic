coalmine:/ˈkoʊlˌmaɪn/| n.|煤矿|A place where coal is dug from the ground.|The coalmine employed hundreds of workers. 这座煤矿雇佣了数百名工人。|Synonyms: colliery, pit|Antonyms: None|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From *col* (coal) + *mine* (a place where minerals are extracted).  *Col* comes from Old English *col*, from Proto-Germanic *kula*, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *gʷelH-* (to burn). *Mine* comes from Old French *mine*, from Medieval Latin *mina* (ore, mine).
