fusionists:/ˈfjuːʒənɪsts/| n.|融合主义者；融合论者|People who advocate or believe in fusion, especially the merging of different styles, ideas, or groups.|The fusionists believe that the best approach combines elements of both traditional and modern practices.|近义词：integrators, synthesizers, combiners|反义词：separatists, purists|词性:名词

词源: From "fusion" + "-ist".  "Fusion" comes from Late Latin "fusionem", meaning "a pouring out", derived from "fundere", meaning "to pour, melt".  The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine, system, or belief.
