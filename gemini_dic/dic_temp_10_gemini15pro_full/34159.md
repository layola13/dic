clambake:/ˈklæmˌbeɪk/| n.|蛤蜊烘烤; 海鲜野餐; (非正式)欢乐喧闹的聚会|A traditional New England clambake involves steaming clams, lobsters, corn, and potatoes over seaweed and hot rocks.|例句：We're having a clambake on the beach this weekend.  这个周末我们要去海滩吃蛤蜊烧烤。

例句：The annual company picnic was a real clambake. 公司的年度野餐真是热闹非凡。|近义词：beach party, cookout, picnic, feast|反义词:|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From clam + bake.  First recorded in 1844.  Originally referred specifically to a method of cooking clams by steaming them over hot rocks and seaweed, but later expanded to include other foods and to refer to the social gathering itself.
