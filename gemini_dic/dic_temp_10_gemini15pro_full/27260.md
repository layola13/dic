calorimotor:/ˌkæləriˈmoʊtər/ | n. | 热量计 | An instrument for measuring the heat generated by a chemical reaction, change of state, or formation of a solution. (This definition is less common than the adjective form.) | Example: The calorimotor was used to measure the heat released during combustion. 热量计用于测量燃烧过程中释放的热量。

Etymology:

From Latin *calor* ("heat") + *motor* ("mover"), from *movēre* ("to move").
