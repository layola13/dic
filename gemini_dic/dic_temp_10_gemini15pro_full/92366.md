hypochondriacism:/ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪæsɪzəm/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪəsɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |疑病症，臆想病|A psychological disorder characterized by excessive worry about having a serious illness, even when there is no medical evidence to support the concern.|She suffers from hypochondriacism and is always convinced she has a deadly disease.|近义词:health anxiety, illness anxiety disorder|反义词:robust health|词性:名词

hypochondriac:/ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪæk/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪæk/ (UK)| n. adj. |疑病症患者，臆想症患者; 疑病的|A person who is excessively anxious about their health; exhibiting or characterized by excessive concern about one's health.|He's a hypochondriac and always rushing to the doctor for minor ailments. |My hypochondriac aunt is constantly self-diagnosing.|近义词:valetudinarian|反义词: None|词性:名词，形容词


词源: From Late Latin *hypochondriacus, from Greek *hypochondriakós, from hypochóndria (“the abdomen, the soft parts of the body between the ribs and pelvis”), from hypó (“under”) + chondrós (“cartilage”).  The ancients believed that melancholy and other emotional disorders originated in the hypochondria.
