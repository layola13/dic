neoplatonic:/ˌniːoʊˈplɑːtɑːnɪk/ | adj. |新柏拉图主义的| Relating to or characteristic of Neoplatonism, a philosophical system that originated in the 3rd century AD and sought to synthesize the teachings of Plato with other philosophical and religious traditions. |  The author's neoplatonic worldview is evident in his emphasis on spiritual transcendence. 作者的新柏拉图主义世界观体现在他对精神超越的强调上。| synonyms: idealist, transcendental, metaphysical | antonyms: materialist, empirical, realist | adjective


neoplatonism:/ˌniːoʊˈplɑːtənɪzəm/ | n. |新柏拉图主义| A philosophical system that originated in the 3rd century AD and sought to synthesize the teachings of Plato with other philosophical and religious traditions. | Neoplatonism exerted a significant influence on Christian thought in the Middle Ages. 新柏拉图主义对中世纪的基督教思想产生了重大影响。| synonyms: idealism, transcendentalism | antonyms: materialism, empiricism | noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From neo- + Platonic.  "Neo-" is a combining form meaning "new," "recent," "revived," or "modified."  "Platonic" refers to Plato, the ancient Greek philosopher.  Therefore, "Neoplatonic" refers to a new or revived form of Platonism.
