acanthodians: /ˌækænˈθəʊdiənz/
n.
棘鱼类
An extinct group of primitive jawed fishes, characterized by spines at the front of their fins and scales made of dentine and bone.
Acanthodians were among the earliest jawed vertebrates. 棘鱼是已知的最早的有颌脊椎动物之一。

Acanthodians are often referred to as "spiny sharks," though they are not closely related to sharks. 棘鱼通常被称为“多刺的鲨鱼”，尽管它们与鲨鱼并没有密切的亲缘关系。

近义词: gnathostomes (有颌类)
反义词: agnathans (无颌类)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek akantha (thorn, spine) + -oid (resembling).  源自希腊语 akantha（刺）+ -oid（类似）。
