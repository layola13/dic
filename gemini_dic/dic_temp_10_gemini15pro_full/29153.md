cases:/ˈkeɪsɪz/ (US) /ˈkeɪsiːz/ (UK)| n.|案例，情况，病例|Instances, situations, or examples; containers designed for a specific purpose.|The lawyer presented several cases to support his argument. 律师提出了几个案例来支持他的论点。|synonyms: instances, examples, situations, circumstances, occurrences, containers, boxes, lawsuits|antonyms: generalities, wholes, non-occurrences|noun

cases:/ˈkeɪsɪz/ (US) /ˈkeɪsiːz/ (UK)| v.|装入箱子|To enclose or protect something in a case.|They carefully cased the fragile vase before shipping it. 他们在运送前小心地将易碎的花瓶装入箱子。|synonyms: enclose, pack, box, cover, protect|antonyms: unpack, unbox, uncover, expose|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *cas*, from Latin *casus* ("a fall, chance, accident, event, occurrence"), from *cadere* ("to fall").  The meaning "container" developed later, based on the idea of a container enclosing or protecting something.
