creaky:/ˈkriːki/| adj.|吱吱作响的；破旧的；摇摇欲坠的|Making a harsh, grating sound, typically as a result of old age or poor condition.  Also used figuratively to describe something unstable or unreliable.|The stairs were old and creaky.  楼梯又旧又嘎吱作响。|The creaky economic system is in need of reform.  这个摇摇欲坠的经济体系需要改革。|synonyms: squeaky, rickety, wobbly|antonyms: sturdy, stable, solid|adjective

creak:/kriːk/| n. v.|吱吱声；发出吱吱声|A harsh, grating sound; to make such a sound.|The creak of the floorboards woke me up. 地板的吱吱声把我吵醒了。|The door creaked open. 门吱吱作响地打开了。|synonyms: squeak, groan|antonyms: silence, hush|noun, verb
