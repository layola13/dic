overpersuade:/ˌoʊvərˌpərˈsweɪd/| vt.|过度说服，劝服过头|To persuade someone excessively, to the point where they agree to something they might later regret. |He overpersuaded her to invest in the risky venture, and she lost all her savings. 他过度劝说她投资于风险项目，结果她赔光了所有积蓄。|Synonyms: coerce, pressure, badger|Antonyms: dissuade, deter|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + persuade. "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much". "Persuade" comes from Latin "persuadere," meaning "to prevail upon," from "per-" (thoroughly) + "suadere" (to advise).
