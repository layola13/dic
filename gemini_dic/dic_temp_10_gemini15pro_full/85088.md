hardballed:/ˈhɑːrdbɔːld/ | v. | 采取强硬手段；以强硬方式对待 | To act aggressively or ruthlessly, especially in business or politics. |  He hardballed the competition into submission. 他用强硬手段迫使竞争对手屈服。

The union was hardballed by management during the negotiations.  在谈判期间，工会被管理层强硬对待。 | 近义词: pressured, intimidated, bullied, coerced | 反义词: compromised, negotiated, cooperated | 词性: 动词

词源:  "Hardball" originally referred to baseball played with a hard ball as opposed to softball.  The metaphorical use, meaning "tough and uncompromising," emerged in the late 20th century, derived from the perceived toughness of the sport.
