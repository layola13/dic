freights:/freɪts/
n.
货物，货运
Goods carried by a vehicle, ship, aircraft, or train.
The ship carries freights of various goods across the ocean. 这艘船载着各种货物穿越海洋。

freights:/freɪts/
v.
运送（货物）
Transport (goods) by air, land, or sea.
The company freights goods all over the world.  这家公司向世界各地运送货物。


Etymology:
From Middle English *freghten, *fraughten, from Middle Dutch *vrechten, from Old French freter (“to load, freight”), from Frankish *frahton (“to load”), from Proto-Germanic *fraχtaną (“to load”), from Proto-Indo-European *proǵʰ- (“to transport, carry”). Cognate with Old English fraht (“freight”), Old High German frehtan (“to load”), Old Norse frakt (“freight”).

Synonyms:
cargo, shipment, load, goods, merchandise

Antonyms:
passenger (if referring to the type of transport)
