disavowers:/ˌdɪsəˈvaʊərz/
|n.|否认者；拒绝承认者；不承认者
|People who disavow something; those who deny responsibility or knowledge of something.|
|The disavowers of the conspiracy theory were quickly silenced.|阴谋论的否认者很快就被噤声了。
|The disavowers of climate change are becoming increasingly marginalized.|否认气候变化的人正日益被边缘化。
|近义词：deniers, repudiators, rejectors
|反义词：acceptors, supporters, endorsers
|词性：名词(plural)

disavowal:/ˌdɪsəˈvaʊəl/
|n.|否认；拒绝承认；不承认
|The act of denying responsibility or knowledge of something.|
|His disavowal of the accusations was met with skepticism.|他对这些指控的否认遭到了怀疑。
|The government issued a disavowal of any involvement in the scandal.|政府发表声明，否认与这起丑闻有任何牵连。
|近义词：denial, repudiation, rejection
|反义词：acceptance, support, endorsement
|词性：名词


词源:
From disavow + -er.  Disavow comes from the Old French desavouer, from des- (expressing negation) + avouer "to avow, confess."
