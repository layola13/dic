mystically:/ˈmɪstɪkli/| adv.|神秘地，不可思议地|In a manner suggesting mystery or magic; in a way that is difficult or impossible to explain.|The candle flickered mystically in the darkened room.  蜡烛在昏暗的房间里神秘地闪烁着。|The ancient ruins were mystically shrouded in fog. 古老的废墟神秘地笼罩在雾中。|近义词：magically, supernaturally, enigmatically|反义词：ordinarily, plainly, clearly|词性:副词

mystical:/ˈmɪstɪkəl/| adj.|神秘的，玄妙的|Relating to mystics or mysticism; inspiring a sense of mystery and wonder.|The mystical experience left her feeling deeply connected to the universe.  这次神秘的经历让她感觉与宇宙深深地联系在一起。|He was drawn to the mystical teachings of the ancient philosophers. 他被古代哲学家的神秘教义所吸引。|近义词：spiritual, esoteric, occult|反义词：mundane, ordinary, practical|词性:形容词

词源: From Late Latin *mysticus, from Greek mystikos "of mysteries, mystic," from mystēs "one initiated into mysteries."
