bootlick:/ˈbuːtlɪk/| n. v.|拍马屁，谄媚；奉承|To behave obsequiously towards someone in power or authority, typically in order to gain favor or advantage.  The act of flattering someone in a servile way.|He's such a bootlick, always praising the boss. 他真是个马屁精，总是拍老板马屁。|近义词:fawn, flatter, kowtow, toady|反义词:criticize, defy, oppose|词性:noun, verb

词源: The term "bootlick" originated in the 1800s, likely referring to the literal act of licking someone's boots as a gesture of extreme subservience.  Over time, the term evolved to describe any obsequious behavior aimed at gaining favor.
