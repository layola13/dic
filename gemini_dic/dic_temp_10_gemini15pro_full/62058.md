encasement:/ɪnˈkeɪsmənt/| n.|套子，包装，外壳|The encasement of the artifact protected it from damage.  文物的套子保护它免受损坏。|例句：The diamond was displayed in a glass encasement.  钻石被展示在一个玻璃套子里。

The encasement of the wires prevented short circuits. 电线的套管防止了短路。|近义词：casing, covering, sheath, enclosure|反义词:exposure, uncovering|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From en- (meaning "in" or "into") + case + -ment (suffix forming nouns).  Ultimately from Latin "in" and "capsa" (box).
