disinclination:/ˌdɪsɪnklɪˈneɪʃn/
n.
不愿; 不情愿
A lack of willingness or desire to do something; reluctance.
例句：He showed a marked disinclination to get involved. 他明显表示不愿参与。
例句：She had a strong disinclination to go to the party. 她非常不愿意去参加聚会。
近义词：reluctance, unwillingness, aversion, dislike, distaste
反义词：inclination, willingness, eagerness, desire

词性: 名词 (Noun)

词源:  Late Middle English: from Old French desclination or late Latin disinclinatio(n-), from dis- ‘not’ + inclinare ‘to bend’.
