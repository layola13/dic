creole:/ˈkriːoʊl/ (US), /ˈkriːɒl/ (UK)| n. adj.|克里奥尔人，克里奥尔语，克里奥尔式的|A person of mixed European and black descent, especially in the Caribbean.  A language that has developed from a mixture of different languages.|例句：The creole cuisine of Louisiana is world-renowned. 路易斯安那州的克里奥尔菜肴世界闻名。|例句：She spoke fluent Creole. 她讲一口流利的克里奥尔语。|近义词：hybrid, mixed|反义词:pure, unmixed|词性:名词, 形容词


Etymology: From French *créole*, from Spanish *criollo*, ultimately from Portuguese *crioulo*, meaning "native to the place, locally raised". Originally used to refer to people born in the colonies, as opposed to those born in Europe.
