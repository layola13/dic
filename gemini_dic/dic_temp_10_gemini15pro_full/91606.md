hyperaltruism:/ˌhaɪpərˈæltruɪzəm/| n. |极度利他主义|Extreme altruism, often at a cost to oneself, potentially explained by evolutionary mechanisms like inclusive fitness.|Example: The soldier's act of jumping on the grenade to save his comrades was an act of hyperaltruism.  这个士兵为了救他的战友而跳到手榴弹上的行为是极度利他主义的行为。

Example:  Some scientists argue that hyperaltruism in humans is a result of our social evolution. 一些科学家认为，人类的极度利他主义是我们社会进化的结果。|近义词：selflessness, abnegation|反义词：selfishness, egoism|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- (meaning "excessive, above") + altruism (from French altruisme, coined from autrui "other people," ultimately from Latin alter "other").
