confidant:/ˈkɒnfɪdænt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːnfɪdɑːnt/ (US)| n.| 知己, 密友, 心腹|A confidant is a person with whom one shares a secret or private matter, trusting them not to repeat it to others. |例句：She was my closest confidant, and I told her everything. 她是我最亲密的知己，我把一切都告诉了她。

例句：He needed a confidant to share his worries with. 他需要一个可以倾诉烦恼的知己。|近义词：close friend, intimate, confidante, intimate friend|反义词: enemy, foe, stranger|词性:noun


confidante:/ˈkɒnfɪdænt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːnfɪdɑːnt/ (US)| n.| (女性)知己, 密友, 心腹|A confidante is a female person with whom one shares a secret or private matter, trusting them not to repeat it to others.  |例句：The queen had several trusted confidantes within the court. 女王在宫廷里有好几个值得信赖的知己。

例句：She became his confidante and helped him through a difficult time. 她成了他的知己，帮助他度过了一段艰难的时期。|近义词：close friend, intimate, confidant, intimate friend|反义词: enemy, foe, stranger|词性:noun


词源:
From French *confident*, from Italian *confidente*, from Latin *confīdens*, present participle of *confīdere* (“to confide”).
