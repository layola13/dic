furrow:/ˈfɜːroʊ/ (US) /ˈfʌrəʊ/ (UK)| n. v.|犁沟，皱纹|A long, narrow, shallow trench made in the ground by a plow, especially for planting seeds; a wrinkle in the skin, especially on the forehead.|The farmer plowed deep furrows in the field. 农民在地里犁出了深深的犁沟。|synonyms: groove, trench, wrinkle, crease|antonyms: ridge, smoothness|noun

furrow:/ˈfɜːroʊ/ (US) /ˈfʌrəʊ/ (UK)| v.|犁地，使起皱纹|To make furrows in (something); to wrinkle (the brow).|He furrowed his brow in concentration. 他皱着眉头，全神贯注。|synonyms: plow, wrinkle, crease, corrugate|antonyms: smooth, unwrinkle|verb
