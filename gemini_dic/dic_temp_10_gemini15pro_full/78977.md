girn:/ɡɜːrn/ /ɡɝːn/
girn: |v.|露齿而笑，咧嘴笑|To smile broadly, often in a way that suggests sarcasm, smugness, or amusement.|He girned at his rival's misfortune. 他对对手的不幸咧嘴一笑。|Synonyms: grin, smirk, sneer|Antonyms: frown, grimace|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English grennen, from Old Norse grenja (“to grin, grimace”), from Proto-Germanic *granjaną (“to weep, groan, grin”), from Proto-Indo-European *ghrenh- (“to grin, bare the teeth”). Cognate with Old English grennian (“to grin, snarl”), Old High German grennan (“to grin, snarl”), German greinen (“to weep”), Swedish grina (“to weep, whine”).
