evolvability:/ɪˌvɒlvəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可进化性，进化能力 | The capacity of a biological system to evolve.  The ability of a system to undergo adaptive evolution. | The evolvability of a species determines its long-term survival.  一个物种的可进化性决定了它的长期生存。 | adaptability, plasticity | rigidity, inflexibility | noun

evolve:/ɪˈvɒlv/ | v. |进化，发展 | Develop gradually, especially from a simple to a more complex form. | Birds are thought to have evolved from dinosaurs. 鸟类被认为是从恐龙进化而来的。  The company has evolved from a small startup to a multinational corporation.  这家公司已经从一家小型初创企业发展成为一家跨国公司。 | develop, progress, advance | regress, stagnate, deteriorate | verb

evolution:/ˌiːvəˈluːʃn/ | n. | 进化，演变 | The gradual development of something, especially from a simple to a more complex form. | Darwin's theory of evolution revolutionized biology. 达尔文的进化论彻底改变了生物学。 The evolution of the internet has transformed communication. 互联网的演变改变了通讯方式。 | development, progression, advancement | regression, stagnation | noun

evolutionary:/ˌiːvəˈluːʃənəri/ | adj. | 进化的，演化的 | Relating to or denoting evolution. | Evolutionary biology studies the processes of change in all forms of life. 进化生物学研究所有生命形式的变化过程。  The company underwent an evolutionary change in its management structure.  该公司在其管理结构方面经历了演变。 | developmental, progressive | static, unchanging | adjective
