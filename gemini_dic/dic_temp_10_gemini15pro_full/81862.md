gridlocked:/ˈɡrɪdˌlɒkt/ (US) /ˈɡrɪdˌlɒkt/ (UK)| adj.| 交通堵塞的；僵持不下的 |A situation where streets are so full of traffic that nothing can move. Also used figuratively to describe a situation where progress is impossible due to disagreement.| The city centre was completely gridlocked during rush hour. 市中心在高峰时间完全堵塞了。|  The negotiations are gridlocked, with neither side willing to compromise. 谈判陷入了僵局，双方都不愿妥协。 | 近义词: congested, stalled, deadlocked | 反义词: flowing, free-flowing | 词性:形容词

词源:  "grid" (网格) + "locked" (锁住的).  The word originated in the 1970s in the US, referring to traffic jams so severe that intersections are blocked, creating a grid of locked vehicles.
