dingy:/ˈdɪndʒi/ | adj. | 昏暗的，肮脏的 | Gloomy and drab; lacking brightness or freshness. Often implies dirtiness and shabbiness. | The room was small and dingy, with peeling wallpaper. 房间又小又脏，墙纸都剥落了。| synonyms: gloomy, drab, dark, grimy, shabby, murky | antonyms: bright, clean, cheerful, light, sparkling | adjective

Etymology: From the late 18th century.  Origin uncertain, possibly from 'dung' referring to its dirty appearance, or perhaps related to 'dingy' (small boat), suggesting a cramped and unpleasant space.
