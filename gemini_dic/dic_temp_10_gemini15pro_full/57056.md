doubting-thomases:/ˈdaʊtɪŋ ˈtɒməsɪz/ (US) /ˈdaʊtɪŋ ˈtɒməsiz/ (UK)| n.|怀疑者，多疑的人|A doubting Thomas is someone who habitually or instinctively doubts or questions. This term originates from the biblical account of the apostle Thomas, who initially refused to believe that Jesus had resurrected until he saw and touched his wounds.|Many people were initially doubting Thomases about the efficacy of the new vaccine. 许多人最初对新疫苗的功效表示怀疑。|synonyms: skeptic, cynic, doubter|antonyms: believer, optimist, trusting|noun
