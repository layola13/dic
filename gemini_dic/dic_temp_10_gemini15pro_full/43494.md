craters:/ˈkreɪtərz/ (US), /ˈkreɪtəz/ (UK)| n. |陨石坑；火山口；弹坑|A large, bowl-shaped cavity in the ground or on the surface of a planet or moon, typically caused by the impact of a meteorite or other celestial body, or by a volcanic eruption or explosion.|The moon is covered in craters. 月球上布满了陨石坑。|
The meteor left a massive crater in the desert.  这颗流星在沙漠中留下了一个巨大的陨石坑。|
Synonyms: cavity, depression, hole, pit, indentation|
Antonyms: mound, hill, elevation|Noun
