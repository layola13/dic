grounding:/ˈɡraʊndɪŋ/ | n. | 接地, 基础, 根基; (青少年)禁足|The act of connecting something to the earth, either electrically or for stability; a basic understanding of a subject;  the punishment of a child or teenager by not allowing them to go out for a period of time. |The electrician checked the grounding of the appliance. 电工检查了电器的接地。| synonyms: earthing, foundation, basis, grounding, confinement (for punishment) | antonyms:  disconnection, instability,  freedom | noun

grounding: /ˈɡraʊndɪŋ/ | adj. | 有根据的，有基础的|Providing a basis for something;  bringing someone back to reality. |Her arguments were well-grounding in facts. 她的论点很有根据，基于事实。 | synonyms:  foundational, basic, fundamental | antonyms: unfounded, baseless, ungrounded | adjective
