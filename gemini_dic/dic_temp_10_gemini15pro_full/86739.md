helotism:/ˈhɛləˌtɪzəm/
n.|奴役制，奴隶状态|A form of symbiosis in which one species is forced to perform labor for another species, often with some benefit in return, but under duress or threat.  This term is derived from the Helots, a subjugated population in ancient Sparta.|The ants' relationship with the aphids could be described as a form of helotism, as the ants protect the aphids but also harvest their honeydew.|近义词：servitude, slavery, bondage|反义词:freedom, liberty|词性:noun

Etymology: From Greek "Heilōtēs" (Εἵλωτες), referring to the serfs of ancient Sparta.
