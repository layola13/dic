conceders:/kənˈsiːdərz/
n.|让步者，承认者|People who concede something; those who admit or acknowledge something (often a point in an argument or a defeat).|The conceders in the debate acknowledged their opponents' strong arguments.  辩论中的让步者承认了对手强有力的论点。|synonyms: admitters, acknowledgers|antonyms: deniers, disputants
word origin: From the verb "concede," which comes from the Latin "concedere," meaning "to yield, grant, or allow."  源自动词“concede”，来自拉丁语“concedere”，意思是“屈服、授予或允许”。
