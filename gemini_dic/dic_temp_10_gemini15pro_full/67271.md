exquisite:/ɪkˈskwɪzɪt/, /ɛkˈskwɪzɪt/ | adj. | 精美的；精致的；剧烈的；异常的 | extremely beautiful and delicate; of extraordinary quality; intensely felt |  The museum houses an exquisite collection of antique jewelry.  博物馆收藏了精美的古董珠宝。| synonyms: delicate, elegant, refined, superb, perfect | antonyms: crude, rough, coarse, clumsy | adjective

exquisites:/ɪkˈskwɪzɪts/, /ɛkˈskwɪzɪts/ | n. | 过分讲究的人；穿着讲究的人 | a person who is excessively fastidious and refined, especially in dress or manner | He was known as an exquisite among his peers, always impeccably dressed. 他在同龄人中被称为穿着讲究的人，总是衣着无可挑剔。| synonyms: dandy, fop | antonyms: slob | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: exquisit, from Old French exquisit, exquis, from Latin exquisitus, past participle of exquirere ‘search out, ascertain’, from ex- ‘out’ + quaerere ‘seek’.
