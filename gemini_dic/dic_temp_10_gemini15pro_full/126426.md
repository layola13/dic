mystic: /ˈmɪstɪk/ | adj. n. | 神秘的；神秘主义者 | Relating to mystics or mysticism; inspiring a sense of mystery and wonder. | The mystic rituals of the ancient tribe were fascinating. 古老部落的神秘仪式令人着迷。 | 近义词：enigmatic, occult, spiritual, esoteric | 反义词：mundane, ordinary, clear, obvious | 词性：形容词，名词

mystic: /ˈmɪstɪk/ | n. | 神秘主义者 | A person who seeks by contemplation and self-surrender to obtain unity with or absorption into the Deity or the absolute, or spiritual truths. |  The mystic spent years in meditation seeking enlightenment.  这位神秘主义者花了数年时间冥想以寻求启迪。| 近义词：spiritualist, seer, visionary | 反义词：materialist, realist | 词性：名词

词源：From Late Latin *mysticus, from Greek mystikos "of mysteries, secret, mystic," from mystēs "one initiated," from myein "to close the eyes or mouth, initiate into mysteries," from PIE root *meu- "to bind, to shut."
