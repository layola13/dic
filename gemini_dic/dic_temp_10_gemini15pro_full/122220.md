misween:/mɪsˈwiːn/
verb
感到不适；行为不端
to behave badly or feel unwell, especially because one is pregnant or has just given birth.
She was misweening during her first trimester. 她怀孕的第一个三个月里感到不适。
She misweened terribly after the birth of her twins. 她生完双胞胎后身体非常不舒服。
近义词: ail, suffer
反义词: thrive, flourish
词性: verb

misween:/mɪsˈwiːn/
adjective
感觉不适的;行为不端的
feeling unwell or behaving badly, especially due to pregnancy or recent childbirth.
She felt misween and couldn't keep any food down. 她感到不舒服，吃什么吐什么。
The child was misween and wouldn't stop crying. 孩子很不舒服，一直哭个不停。
近义词: unwell, sickly
反义词: healthy, well
词性: adjective



词源:
From Middle English *miswenen, from mis- + wenen (“to think, suppose; expect; hope; ween”).  The original meaning was "to think wrongly, misjudge," which later evolved into the current senses related to discomfort and misbehavior, particularly in the context of pregnancy and childbirth.
