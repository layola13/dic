accumulatively: /əˈkjuːmjələtɪvli/
adverb
累积地；渐增地
in a way that gradually increases in amount or intensity over a period of time
The changes have been small but accumulatively significant. 这些变化虽小，但累积起来却意义重大。
The drug has an accumulatively detrimental effect on the liver. 这种药物对肝脏有累积性的不利影响。
近义词: increasingly, progressively, gradually
反义词: suddenly, abruptly, immediately
词性: adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *accumulatīvus, from Latin accumulātus (past participle of accumulāre ‘to heap up’), from ad- ‘to’ + cumulāre ‘to heap’, from cumulus ‘heap’.

USAGE->
This word is primarily used as an adverb to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.

Here are examples demonstrating its usage in different contexts:

Describing a gradual increase:

The snow accumulatively piled up, eventually blocking the road. = 雪逐渐堆积起来，最终阻塞了道路。
The stress of the job accumulatively wore her down. = 工作的压力日积月累地把她压垮了。

Modifying an adjective:

The evidence is accumulatively persuasive. = 证据越来越有说服力。
The benefits of regular exercise are accumulatively significant. = 经常锻炼的好处累积起来意义重大。
