bark-at-the-moon:/bɑːrk ət ðə muːn/ | idiom |  对着月亮吠叫;徒劳无功;做无用功 | To express futile rage or frustration, like a dog barking at something it can't reach. This idiom implies a sense of helplessness and pointless anger. |  He was barking at the moon, complaining about the government but not voting. 他对着月亮吠叫，抱怨政府却不投票。

The protestors were barking at the moon; the company wasn't going to change its policy. 抗议者们是在做无用功；公司不会改变其政策。 | 近义词: rage in vain, tilt at windmills, flog a dead horse | 反义词: take effective action, address the issue, make a difference | 词性: idiom
