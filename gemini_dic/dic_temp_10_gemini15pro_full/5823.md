amateurishly:/ˌæməˈtʃɜːrɪʃli/ | adv. | 业余地；不熟练地 | In a manner characteristic of an amateur; lacking professional skill or expertise. | He played the piano amateurishly, hitting wrong notes frequently. 他弹钢琴弹得很业余，经常弹错音符。|  She amateurishly attempted to repair the broken vase, making it worse. 她笨手笨脚地试图修理破损的花瓶，结果反而弄得更糟了。 | clumsily, ineptly, unskillfully | expertly, professionally, skillfully | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From amateur + -ish + -ly.  "Amateur" comes from French "amateur," from Latin "amator," meaning "lover," from "amare," meaning "to love."  The suffix "-ish" forms adjectives meaning "somewhat like" or "having the quality of." The suffix "-ly" forms adverbs.
