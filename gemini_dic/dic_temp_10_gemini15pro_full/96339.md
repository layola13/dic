indigogen:/ɪnˈdɪɡəˌdʒɛn/ |n.|靛蓝原|A colorless, water-soluble glucoside found in various plants, notably woad and Indigofera species, which yields indigo blue upon enzymatic hydrolysis and oxidation. It's a precursor to the natural dye indigo.|The indigogen in the leaves is converted to indigo through a process of fermentation and oxidation.|Synonym: indican|Antonym: N/A|Noun
