interlotted:/ˌɪntərˈlɒtɪd/ | adj. | 相互交错的，互相分配的 |  Arranged or assigned in alternate lots or portions, often with different uses or owners. | The farmers interlotted their fields for different crops. 农民们将他们的田地交错分配，种植不同的作物。| The residential and commercial properties were interlotted throughout the development. 住宅和商业地产在整个开发过程中交错分布。|  intermingled, interspersed, intermixed | segregated, separated | adjective

interlot:/ˌɪntərˈlɒt/ | v. |  相互交错分配 | To arrange or assign in alternate lots or portions.  | They interlotted the land between farming and residential use. 他们将土地交错分配用于农业和住宅。 | The city council decided to interlot the park with playgrounds and picnic areas. 市议会决定将公园与游乐场和野餐区交错布置。| intermingle, intersperse, intermix | segregate, separate | verb


Etymology:
"inter-" (prefix meaning "between, among") + "lot" (noun meaning a portion of land or a measured parcel).
