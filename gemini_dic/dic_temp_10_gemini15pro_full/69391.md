feedbag:/ˈfiːdbæɡ/
n.|饲料袋；(尤指马的)料袋；(非正式)食物|A bag containing food for animals, especially for a horse.  Informally, it can also refer to a meal.|The horse reached for his feedbag eagerly. 马儿急切地伸向它的饲料袋。|synonyms: nosebag, fodder bag|antonyms: N/A|noun

feedbag:/ˈfiːdbæɡ/
n.|(非正式)一餐，一顿饭|A meal (informal).|He tucked into his feedbag with gusto. 他津津有味地吃着他的饭。|synonyms: meal, repast, grub|antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "feed" + "bag". "Feed" comes from Old English *fēdan, from Proto-Germanic *fōdijaną, meaning "to feed, nourish." "Bag" comes from Old Norse baggi, meaning "bag, sack."  The combined term "feedbag" likely emerged in the late 19th century.
