ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *los, from Old English los (“loss, destruction, perdition”), from Proto-Germanic *lausaz (“loss”). Cognate with Old Norse los (“loss, destruction”), Old Frisian los (“loss, damage”), Middle Dutch loos (“bad, loose, false, lost”), Dutch loos (“loose, dissolute”), Old High German lōs (“loose, free, dissolute”), German los (“loose, free, rid of”). Related also to Old English lēosan (“to lose”).
