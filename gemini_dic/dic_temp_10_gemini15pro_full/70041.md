fetter:/ˈfetər/| n. v.|脚镣；束缚；限制|n. A chain or shackle placed on the feet. v. To restrain with chains or shackles; to restrict or hamper.|n. The fetters prevented the prisoner from escaping. 脚镣阻止了囚犯逃跑。 v.  His creativity was fettered by the strict rules. 他的创造力受到了严格规则的束缚。|近义词：(n.) shackle, chain, manacle; (v.) restrain, restrict, confine|反义词：(v.) liberate, free, unleash|词性:名词,动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English "feter" (plural "fetras"), from Proto-Germanic "*fetrō," meaning "foot band, fetter," related to Old Norse "fetill" "band, strap" and Gothic "fōtus" "foot."  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*ped-" "foot."
