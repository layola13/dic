blurt:/blɜːrt/| v. |脱口而出，突然说出|to say something suddenly and without thinking, usually because you are excited or nervous|He blurted out the secret before he could stop himself. 他还没来得及停下来就把秘密脱口而出了。|近义词：exclaim, utter, divulge, spill|反义词: conceal, suppress|词性:动词
词源:  From Middle English *blorten, from Old Norse *blyrta (“to gush, spurt”), from Proto-Germanic *blustjaną (“to blow, blast”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰleyH- (“to blow, swell”). Cognate with Old Norse blása (“to blow”).
