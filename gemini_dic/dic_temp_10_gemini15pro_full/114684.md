malignity:/məˈlɪɡnəti/ | n. | 恶意，恶毒，怨恨 | Intense ill will or hatred; the quality of being malevolent. |  He spoke of her with surprising malignity. 他以惊人的恶意谈论她。

The malignity of the rumors spread quickly. 这些谣言的恶毒传播得很快。 | 近义词: malevolence, spite, venom, viciousness, animosity, hostility | 反义词: benevolence, kindness, compassion, goodwill | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *malignite, from Old French malignité, from Latin malignitās (“badness, ill-will, spite”), from malignus (“bad, wicked”), from male (“badly”) + genus (“kind, sort”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵenos- (“birth, race, kind”).
