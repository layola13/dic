electre:/ɪˈlɛktri/ | n. |Electra, 伊莱克特拉 | In Greek mythology, Electra is the daughter of Agamemnon and Clytemnestra, and sister of Orestes. She helped Orestes avenge their father's murder by killing Clytemnestra and her lover Aegisthus.  |  Electra complex is a psychoanalytic term used to describe a girl's unconscious sexual desire for her father and rivalry with her mother. | synonym:  daughter, princess | antonym: mother, son | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek Ἠλέκτρα (Ēléktra), meaning "amber" or "bright," also "shining," from ἤλεκτρον (ḗlektron, "amber").  The name was given to the character in Greek mythology.
