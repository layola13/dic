beef:/biːf/| n. vi. |牛肉；抱怨；牢骚|The flesh of a cow, bull, or ox, used as food. Also, a complaint or grievance.|例句：We had roast beef for dinner. 我们晚餐吃了烤牛肉。|例句：He had a beef with the manager about his salary. 他对经理就他的薪水问题颇有微词。|近义词：complaint, grievance, gripe, meat (as in food)|反义词:praise, compliment|词性:noun, verb

beef:/biːf/| vi. |抱怨；发牢骚|To complain.|例句：He's always beefing about something. 他总是抱怨个不停。|例句：She beefed to the waiter about the cold food. 她向服务员抱怨食物冷了。|近义词：complain, grumble, whine|反义词:praise, commend|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *beof*, from Old French *buef*, from Latin *bovēs* (plural), nominative singular *bos*, meaning "ox," "cow," or "bull."  The semantic shift from "cow" to "complaint" originated in American slang in the late 19th century.
