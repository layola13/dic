gondwanaland:/ɡɒndˈwɑːnəlænd/ |n.|冈瓦纳大陆|A vast southern supercontinent that existed from the late Paleozoic to the early Mesozoic, comprising present-day South America, Africa, Australia, Antarctica, and the Indian subcontinent.|The breakup of Gondwanaland led to the formation of the Atlantic and Indian Oceans.|Synonym:supercontinent|Antonym:N/A|noun

gondwanalands:/ɡɒndˈwɑːnəlændz/ |n.|冈瓦纳大陆（复数，较少用）|Plural form of Gondwanaland, referring to the hypothetical fragments or sub-regions within Gondwanaland.|While less common, "gondwanalands" might be used in a geological context discussing fragmented portions of the original landmass.|Synonym:supercontinents (in a specific context)|Antonym:N/A|noun
