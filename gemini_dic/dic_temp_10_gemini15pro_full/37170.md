come-uppings:/ˌkʌmˈʌpɪŋz/ | n. |报应；应得的惩罚；结果|  A come-uppance, often used in the plural, refers to a deserved punishment or fate, especially one resulting from arrogance or misdeeds. It often carries a sense of satisfaction or justice being served. | He finally got his come-uppings for all his lies and deceit. 他所有的谎言和欺骗终于得到了报应。 |Synonyms: retribution, deserts, just deserts, payback, consequences | Antonyms: reward, benefit | Noun

词源:  "Come-uppance" originated in the late 19th century, likely from "come up" in the sense of "to happen" or "to arise," combined with "ance" forming a noun of action or process.  The plural form "come-uppings" is less common but still used, especially in informal contexts.
