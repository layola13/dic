hippie:/ˈhɪpi/| n.|嬉皮士；嬉皮|A person, especially of the 1960s, associated with a subculture involving a rejection of conventional values and the taking of hallucinogenic drugs.|He lived like a hippie in his youth. 他年轻时过着嬉皮士般的生活。|近义词：bohemian, counter-culturist, nonconformist|反义词:conformist, traditionalist|词性:noun

hippie:| adj.|嬉皮的；嬉皮风格的|Characteristic of a hippie.|She wore hippie clothes and had long, flowing hair. 她穿着嬉皮士风格的衣服，留着飘逸的长发。|近义词：bohemian, unconventional|反义词:conventional, traditional|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From hipster (1940s slang for a jazz aficionado), perhaps influenced by hep.  The term "hippie" became widely used in the mid-1960s.
