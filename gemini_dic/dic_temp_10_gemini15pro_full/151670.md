partisan: /ˈpɑːrtɪzæn/ (US), /ˈpɑːtɪzan/ (UK)| n. adj.|党羽，支持者；偏袒的，盲目拥护的|A strong supporter of a party, cause, or person, especially one who shows a biased, emotional allegiance.|The politician's partisan supporters cheered loudly. 这位政治家的党羽支持者大声欢呼。|近义词：supporter, adherent, devotee, follower, advocate, backer|反义词：opponent, adversary, critic, detractor|词性: 名词, 形容词

partisan: /ˈpɑːrtɪzæn/ (US), /ˈpɑːtɪzan/ (UK)| adj.|党羽的，支持者的；偏袒的，盲目拥护的|Showing strong support for a party, cause, or person, often without considering the facts or other opinions.|His partisan views on the issue made it difficult to have a reasonable discussion. 他对这个问题的盲目拥护的观点使得很难进行理性的讨论。|近义词：biased, prejudiced, one-sided|反义词：impartial, unbiased, objective, neutral|词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *partisan*, from Italian *partigiano*, from *parte* "part, party," from Latin *pars* "part."
