overdestructivenesses: /ˌoʊvər dɪˈstrʌktɪvnəsɪz/

Etymology:
"over" (Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond") + "destructive" (from Latin "destructus," past participle of "destruere" - "to pull down, demolish") + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns from adjectives, denoting state or quality) + "-es" (plural suffix)
