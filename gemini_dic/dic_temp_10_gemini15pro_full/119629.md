middle-american:/ˌmɪdəl əˈmerɪkən/| adj. n.|中美洲的；美国中产阶级的；思想观念传统保守的美国人|Relating to the central part of America, or its people, especially those considered to be conventional or conservative in their views.|He has very middle-American values. 他拥有非常传统保守的美国价值观。|Middle-American families often prioritize home ownership. 美国的中产阶级家庭通常优先考虑拥有住房。|近义词：conventional, conservative, mainstream|反义词：unconventional, radical, liberal|词性：形容词, 名词


middle-class:/ˌmɪdəl ˈklæs/| adj. n.|中产阶级的；中产阶级|Relating to the middle class; a member of the middle class.|They live in a middle-class neighborhood. 他们住在中产阶级社区。|She comes from a middle-class family. 她来自一个中产阶级家庭。|近义词：bourgeois|反义词：working-class, upper-class|词性：形容词, 名词

词源: "Middle" + "American." "Middle" indicates the central part or the middle social class. "American" refers to the United States of America.  The term gained prominence in the mid-20th century to describe a perceived social and cultural group.
