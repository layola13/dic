homoeomery:/ˌhɒmiˈɒməri/ /ˌhoʊmiˈɑːməri/ | n. | 均勻性; 同質性; 類同; | The state or quality of being homoeomerous; similarity of structure throughout.  The doctrine that the elements composing the body are similar to the parts they compose. |  Ancient philosophers believed in the homoeomery of the universe, thinking all parts were made of the same fundamental substance.  古希臘哲學家相信宇宙的同質性，認為所有部分都是由同一基本物質構成的。 |  homogeneity, uniformity, similarity | heterogeneity, diversity, difference | noun

homoeomerous:/ˌhɒmiˈɒmərəs/ /ˌhoʊmiˈɑːmərəs/ | adj. | 同質的; 由相同部分組成的;  | Having similar parts throughout; composed of similar elements. |  Anaxagoras believed that bone was homoeomerous, meaning it was made of tiny, imperceptible bones.  阿那克薩哥拉認為骨頭是同質的，意味著它是由微小、難以察覺的骨頭組成的。 | homogeneous, uniform, similar | heterogeneous, diverse, different | adjective
