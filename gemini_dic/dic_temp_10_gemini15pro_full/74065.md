fossiled:/ˈfɒsɪld/ (UK) /ˈfɑːsəld/ (US)| adj.|变成化石的；僵化的；老化的|Preserved in fossil form; also, figuratively, rigid and unchanging, antiquated.|The museum displayed a fossiled dinosaur egg.  博物馆展出了一颗恐龙蛋化石。| The company's fossiled management structure was hindering innovation.  公司僵化的管理结构阻碍了创新。|Synonyms: petrified, fossilized, ossified, antiquated, outdated|Antonyms: modern, contemporary, flexible, adaptable|adjective


fossilize:/ˈfɒsɪlaɪz/ (UK) /ˈfɑːsəlaɪz/ (US)| v.|使成化石；使僵化|To convert into a fossil; also, figuratively, to make or become rigid and unchanging.|The dinosaur bones were fossilized over millions of years.  恐龙骨骼经过数百万年变成了化石。|Their thinking has become fossilized. 他们的思维僵化了。|Synonyms: petrify, ossify|Antonyms: modernize, adapt|verb
