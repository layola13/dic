absurdism: /əbˈsɜːrdɪzəm/ (US) /əbˈzɜːdɪzəm/ (UK)| n. | 荒诞主义 | The philosophical and literary doctrine that human beings live in an irrational and meaningless universe and that their search for order brings them into conflict with the universe.  |  The play captured the sense of absurdism prevalent in post-war society.  这出戏捕捉了战后社会普遍存在的荒诞感。

Camus's "The Stranger" is a classic example of absurdist literature.  加缪的《局外人》是荒诞主义文学的经典范例。 | 近义词: nihilism, existentialism | 反义词: rationalism, meaningfulness | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *absurde* "absurd" (from Latin *absurdus* "out of tune, discordant, irrational," from *ab-* "off, away from" + *surdus* "deaf, dull, mute") + *-ism* (suffix forming nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, principles, or practices).
