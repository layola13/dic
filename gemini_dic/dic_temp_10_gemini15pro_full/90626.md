humanisms:/ˈhjuːmənɪzəmz/
n.
人文主义（复数）
A Renaissance intellectual movement that studied human culture, based on classical texts.  A variety of ethical theory and practice that emphasizes reason, scientific inquiry, and human fulfillment in the natural world and often rejects the importance of belief in God.
例句：Many forms of humanisms flourished during the Renaissance. 文艺复兴时期，各种形式的人文主义蓬勃发展。

例句：Secular humanisms emphasize ethical values without reference to a deity. 世俗人文主义强调不依赖于神灵的伦理价值观。

近义词：humanity, culture, learning, scholarship
反义词：barbarity, ignorance, inhumanity
词性: Noun


Etymology:
From Late Latin *humanismus, from Latin humanus (“human”).  First attested in 1808.
