escutcheoned:/ɪˈskʌtʃənd/ | adj. | 纹章的，有纹章装饰的 | Displayed or marked with an escutcheon (a shield bearing a coat of arms).  Often used figuratively to describe something adorned or embellished as if with a coat of arms. |  The knight's shield was escutcheoned with a golden lion.  骑士的盾牌上饰有金狮。| Synonyms: emblazoned, adorned, decorated | Antonyms: bare, plain, unadorned | adjective

escutcheon:/ɪˈskʌtʃən/ | n. | 紋章，盾形纹章 | A shield or shield-shaped surface on which a coat of arms is displayed. Can also refer to a flat piece of metal used to protect a keyhole. | The family's escutcheon featured three eagles.  这个家族的纹章上有三只鹰。 | Synonyms: shield, crest, coat of arms | Antonyms: N/A | noun
