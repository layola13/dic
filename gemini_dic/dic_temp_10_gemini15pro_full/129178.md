nobbily:/ˈnɒbɪli/| adv.|傲慢地，势利地|In a snobbish or aristocratic manner, often implying a sense of superiority based on wealth, social status, or taste.|He spoke nobbily about his family's connections. 他傲慢地谈论着他家族的关系。|近义词：haughtily, snobbishly, aristocratically, pretentiously|反义词：humbly, modestly|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From *nob* (meaning a person of wealth or high social standing) + *-ly* (adverbial suffix).  The word "nob" itself is likely a shortened form of "nobleman".
