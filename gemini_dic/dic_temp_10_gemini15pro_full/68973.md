fathom:/ˈfæðəm/ | n. v. | 英寻（水深单位，等于6英尺或1.829米）；理解，领会 | A fathom is a unit of length equal to six feet (approximately 1.8 m), chiefly used in reference to the depth of water.  To fathom something is to understand it thoroughly. | The ship sank in 30 fathoms of water. 这艘船沉没在30英寻深的水中。

I can't fathom his motives. 我无法理解他的动机。 | synonyms: understand, comprehend, grasp, penetrate | antonyms: misunderstand, misinterpret | noun, verb


fathomless:/ˈfæðəmləs/ | adj. | 深不可测的；无法理解的 | Fathomless describes something too deep to be measured or something that cannot be understood. |  Her eyes were fathomless pools of blue. 她的眼睛像深不可测的蓝色池塘。

The mysteries of the universe are fathomless. 宇宙的奥秘深不可测。| synonyms: unfathomable, immeasurable, inscrutable, bottomless | antonyms: shallow, superficial, comprehensible | adjective
