dissipating:/ˈdɪsɪpeɪtɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
消散，驱散，浪费
gradually disappearing or lessening; wasting or frittering away (something, especially energy or money)
The fog was slowly dissipating, revealing the city skyline. 雾气慢慢消散，露出了城市的天际线。
He dissipated his inheritance on gambling and fast cars. 他把遗产都浪费在了赌博和跑车上。
近义词: dispersing, scattering, vanishing, fading, squandering, wasting
反义词: accumulating, gathering, collecting, intensifying, strengthening
词性: v.

dissipate:/ˈdɪsɪpeɪt/
verb
消散，驱散，浪费
to (cause to) gradually disappear or waste
The smoke dissipated into the air. 烟雾消散在空气中。
The crowd dissipated after the concert. 音乐会结束后，人群散开了。
近义词: disperse, scatter, vanish, fade, squander, waste
反义词: accumulate, gather, collect, intensify, strengthen
词性: v.

词源: From Latin dissipare "to scatter," from dis- "apart" + sipare "to throw, scatter."
