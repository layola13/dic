analepsis:/əˈnæləpsɪs/| n.|闪回，倒叙|A literary device that interrupts the chronological order of a narrative to present an earlier event.  It is used to provide background information, develop character, or create suspense.|The novel uses analepsis to reveal the protagonist's troubled childhood. 这部小说运用倒叙来揭示主人公童年时期的坎坷经历。

The film employed analepsis to show the events leading up to the murder. 这部电影用倒叙来展现谋杀案发生前的种种事件。|近义词：flashback, retrospection|反义词：prolepsis, flashforward|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *analēpsis*  "a taking up," from *analambanein* "to take up," from *ana-* "up" + *lambanein* "to take."
