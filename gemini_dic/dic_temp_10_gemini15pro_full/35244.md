clumsinesses:/ˈklʌmzɪnəsɪz/
n.
笨拙; 笨手笨脚; 粗心大意;
The state of being clumsy; a clumsy act or acts.  Awkwardness in movement or handling things.
Her clumsinesses led to several broken dishes. 她的笨手笨脚导致了几个盘子被打碎。
The series of clumsinesses during the performance was embarrassing. 表演中的一系列失误令人尴尬。
近义词:awkwardness, gaucheness, ineptness, ungainliness
反义词:grace, dexterity, skill, adroitness
词性:名词(plural noun)

clumsy:/ˈklʌmzi/
adj.
笨拙的; 笨手笨脚的; 粗心大意的;
Awkward in movement or handling things.
He's so clumsy he's always tripping over. 他太笨拙了，总是绊倒。
She made a clumsy attempt to apologize. 她笨拙地试图道歉。
近义词:awkward, ungainly, inept, gauche
反义词:graceful, dexterous, skillful, adroit
词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *clomsen, from Old Norse *klumsa (“to stammer, mumble”).  Related to Dutch klompen (“wooden shoes”). The original meaning may have been "benumbed with cold," hence "awkward, clumsy."
