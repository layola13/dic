ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *byrgan "to bury," related to beorgan "to protect, save, shelter," from Proto-Germanic *burjaną, from Proto-Indo-European *bhergh- "to hide, protect." The sense of "covered or obscured" developed from the notion of being "hidden" or "buried."
