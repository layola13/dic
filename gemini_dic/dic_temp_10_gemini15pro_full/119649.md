middlebrow: /ˈmɪdəlˌbraʊ/ | n. adj. | 中雅人士；中等趣味的 | A person with conventional, middle-class tastes in literature, music, or art, considered to be lacking depth or originality.  Also used to describe cultural products appealing to such tastes. |  Example: The gallery catered to a middlebrow audience, showcasing accessible but not particularly challenging art.  这家画廊迎合了中雅人士的口味，展出了一些通俗易懂但并非特别具有挑战性的艺术作品。

Example:  He dismissed the novel as middlebrow sentimentalism. 他认为这部小说是庸俗的情感小说。 | 近义词: conventional, unadventurous, bourgeois, Philistine | 反义词: highbrow, lowbrow, sophisticated, avant-garde | 词性: 名词，形容词
