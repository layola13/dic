countersunk:/ˈkaʊntərˌsʌŋk/ /ˈkaʊntərsʌŋk/| adj. v. n.|埋头的，锥形的；|A countersunk hole or screw has a sloping edge so that the screw or bolt does not stick out above the surface.|The screws are countersunk and covered with wooden plugs.|近义词:recessed, embedded|反义词:protruding, raised|词性:形容词, 动词, 名词

countersinking:/ˈkaʊntərˌsɪŋkɪŋ/ /ˈkaʊntərsɪŋkɪŋ/| n.|埋头孔；埋头处理|The process of making a countersunk hole.|The carpenter used a special bit for countersinking the screws.|近义词:recessing|反义词:N/A|词性:名词


Etymology:  "countersink" comes from "counter-" (meaning "opposite" or "reverse") and "sink" (referring to making a depression).  Originally, it referred to a second sinking or drilling operation to enlarge the upper part of a hole.
