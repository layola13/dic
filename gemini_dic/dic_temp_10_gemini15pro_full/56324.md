dogmatically:/dɒɡˈmætɪkli/ /dɑːɡˈmætɪkli/| adv.|教条地；武断地；独断地|In a manner asserting opinions as if they were facts, without consideration of evidence or other opinions.|He dogmatically asserted that the earth was flat. 他武断地断言地球是平的。|Near Synonyms: assertively, peremptorily, authoritatively, dictatorialy|Near Antonyms: open-mindedly, flexibly, receptively|Adverb

dogma:/ˈdɒɡmə/ /ˈdɑːɡmə/| n.|教条；信条；武断的意见|A principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true.|The dogma of the church. 教会的教条。|Near Synonyms: doctrine, tenet, creed|Near Antonyms: heresy, skepticism|Noun


dogmatic:/dɒɡˈmætɪk/ /dɑːɡˈmætɪk/| adj.|教条的；武断的；独断的|Inclined to lay down principles as undeniably true.|He has a dogmatic approach to education. 他对教育持教条主义的态度。|Near Synonyms: opinionated, peremptory, assertive, doctrinaire|Near Antonyms: open-minded, flexible, receptive|Adjective
