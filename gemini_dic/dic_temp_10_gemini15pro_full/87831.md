hidabilities: /ˌhaɪdəˈbɪlətiz/ | n. | 隐藏能力; 隐藏的才能; 潜能 |  The inherent capacity to conceal one's abilities or skills, often due to lack of opportunity, self-doubt, or social pressure.  This word is not standard English and is likely a neologism formed by combining "hide" and "abilities." It suggests talents or capabilities that are not readily apparent or actively demonstrated. |  She had untapped hidabilities that only emerged when she was given a chance to shine. 她拥有未开发的潜能，只有当她有机会展现自己的时候才会显现出来。|  Hidden talents, latent potential, undeveloped skills |  Exposed abilities, obvious skills, manifest talents | Noun
