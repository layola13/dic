bloodshot:/ˈblʌdʃɒt/ (US) /ˈblʌdʃɒt/ (UK)| adj.|充血的，布满血丝的|Having blood vessels that are enlarged and visible, especially in the whites of the eyes, typically as a result of tiredness or irritation.|His eyes were bloodshot from lack of sleep. 他的眼睛因缺乏睡眠而布满血丝。|
例句：
He had bloodshot eyes after staying up all night.  他熬了一整夜后眼睛布满血丝。

Her eyes were bloodshot and puffy from crying. 她哭得眼睛又红又肿。


近义词：red, inflamed, injected|反义词:clear, white|词性:adjective

bloodshot:/ˈblʌdʃɒt/ |adj.|充血的|  (of eyes) inflamed and red because the small blood vessels have become wider. |  He staggered in, his face bruised and his eyes bloodshot. 他跌跌撞撞地走了进来，脸上青一块紫一块，眼睛布满血丝。|
例句：
The doctor noticed the patient's bloodshot eyes and suspected an infection. 医生注意到病人眼睛充血，怀疑是感染。

Allergies can cause itchy, bloodshot eyes. 过敏会导致眼睛发痒和充血。

近义词：red-rimmed, red-eyed|反义词: clear-eyed|词性: adjective

词源：blood + shot.  "Shot" here means "filled" or "permeated," similar to its use in "shot silk" (silk interwoven with another color).  Therefore, "bloodshot" literally means "filled with blood" (in the small vessels).
