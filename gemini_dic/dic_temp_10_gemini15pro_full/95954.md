inculcating: /ɪnˈkʌlkeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 灌输，谆谆教诲（inculcate的现在分词形式） |  The process of impressing something upon the mind of another by frequent instruction or repetition. | They are inculcating good manners in their children. 他们正在谆谆教诲孩子们良好的礼仪。 | synonyms: instilling, implanting, ingraining, impressing, infusing | antonyms: eradicating, effacing, expunging | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin inculcare "to tread or stamp in," from in- "in" + calcare "to tread," from calx "heel."  The figurative sense of "impressing an idea on the mind" arose from the notion of repeatedly stamping something in.
