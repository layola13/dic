borsholder:/ˈbɔːrzˌhoʊldər/ | n. |  治安官；户长；十户长 | A local official responsible for maintaining order and collecting taxes, especially in medieval England.  |  The borsholder summoned the villagers to a meeting. 十户长召集村民开会。 |  The borsholder was responsible for collecting taxes. 十户长负责收税。|Synonym: headman, constable, tithingman | Antonym: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *burh-spearu-healdere, literally "borough spear-holder," from burh "borough" + spearu "spear, lance" + healdere "holder, keeper."  The term originally referred to someone responsible for guarding a borough or town. Over time, the role evolved to include more administrative and judicial duties.
