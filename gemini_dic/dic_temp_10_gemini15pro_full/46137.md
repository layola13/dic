cymidine: /ˈsaɪmɪdiːn/ | n. | 西咪替丁 | An antihistamine drug (trade name Tagamet) used to treat peptic ulcers and other gastrointestinal conditions by reducing the production of stomach acid. |  Cimetidine, sold under the brand name Tagamet among others, is a histamine H2 receptor antagonist that inhibits stomach acid production. It is used to treat heartburn and peptic ulcers. | 近义词: ranitidine, famotidine, nizatidine | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for a specific drug, but potentially antacids which neutralize acid rather than prevent its production) | noun
