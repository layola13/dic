circumstantiality: /ˌsɜːrkəmˌstænʃiˈælɪti/
| n. | 详尽性；枝节性；迂回曲折 | The tendency to provide excessive and irrelevant detail in conversation or writing.  A speech pattern characterized by rambling, unnecessary details, and indirectness in reaching a point. |  The patient's circumstantiality made it difficult to get a clear history of his illness. 病人的枝节性叙述使得很难获得清晰的病史。|  Her circumstantiality in describing the event made it last much longer than it needed to. 她对事件的冗长描述使得它比实际需要的时间长得多。 | prolixity, verbosity, loquacity | conciseness, brevity | noun
