disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/ | v. n. | 蔑视，鄙视 | To consider someone or something to be unworthy of one's respect or attention; scorn. | She disdained to answer his rude remarks. 她不屑于回答他粗鲁的言论。 |  Synonyms: scorn, despise, contemn | Antonyms: admire, respect, appreciate | verb
disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/ | n. | 蔑视，鄙视 | A feeling of contempt or scorn. |  He looked at her with disdain. 他轻蔑地看着她。 | Synonyms: scorn, contempt, derision | Antonyms: admiration, respect, appreciation | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desdeigner*, from des- (expressing negation) + deigner (“to deign”), from Latin dignari (“to deem worthy”).
