compel:/kəmˈpɛl/| v. |强迫，迫使|To force someone to do something; to necessitate or require.|例句：His conscience compelled him to confess. 他的良心迫使他坦白。|例句：The law compels employers to provide health insurance. 法律强制要求雇主提供健康保险。|近义词：force, oblige, coerce|反义词：allow, permit|词性:动词
Etymology: From Latin compellere "to drive together, force, constrain," from com- "together" (see com-) + pellere "to drive" (see pulse (n.)).
