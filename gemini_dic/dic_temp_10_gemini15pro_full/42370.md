counterattack:/ˈkaʊntərəˈtæk/
|n.|反击，反攻|An attack made in response to an initial attack.|The general ordered a swift counterattack. 将军下令迅速反击。|Synonyms: retaliation, riposte, comeback|Antonyms: defense, retreat|noun
|v.|反击，反攻|To attack in response to an initial attack.|The team counterattacked effectively, scoring the winning goal.  该队有效地反击，打进了致胜一球。|Synonyms: retaliate, strike back, respond|Antonyms: defend, retreat|verb

Etymology:
From counter- + attack.  "Counter-" comes from the Old French "contre-" meaning "against". "Attack" comes from the Old French "atachier"  meaning "to fasten, attach," later evolving to mean "to attack."
