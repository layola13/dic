cut: /kʌt/ | v. n. adj. | 切，割，剪，伤口 | to divide something with a sharp-edged tool such as a knife or scissors; a wound made by cutting something |  She cut the cake into six pieces. 她把蛋糕切成了六块。 | slice, carve, chop, gash, wound | join, unite | verb, noun, adjective

cut: /kʌt/ | n. | 减少，削减 | a reduction in something, such as the amount of money spent or the number of jobs | The government announced cuts in public spending. 政府宣布削减公共开支。 | reduction, decrease, trim | increase, rise | noun

cut: /kʌt/ | adj. | 切好的，割伤的 | having been cut | cut flowers 切花 | sliced, chopped, wounded | whole, uncut | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *kutten*, from Old Norse *kutta* (“to cut”), from Proto-Germanic *kuttaną* (“to cut”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kewd- (“to cut, split”). Cognate with Scots cut (“to cut”), West Frisian kutte (“to cut”), Dutch kutte (“to cut”), German kutte (“monk's cowl”), Icelandic kuta (“rag”).
