cozen:/ˈkʌzən/| v. |欺骗，哄骗|To deceive or trick someone, typically by being superficially charming or friendly.|He cozened them out of their life savings. 他骗走了他们的毕生积蓄。|例句：The con man cozened the elderly woman into giving him her jewelry. 骗子哄骗老太太把她的珠宝给了他。|近义词：deceive, cheat, defraud, trick, dupe, swindle|反义词：be honest, be truthful|词性：动词

cozen:/ˈkʌzən/| v. |骗取|To obtain something by deception.|She cozened her way into the exclusive club. 她骗取了进入专属俱乐部的资格。|例句：He managed to cozen a free meal from the restaurant. 他设法从餐馆骗取了一顿免费的餐食。|近义词：obtain by deception, wangle, finagle|反义词：earn, work for|词性：动词

词源：From Middle English cosinen, from Old French cousiner (“to act as a cousin”), from cousin (“cousin”).  The original sense was to treat someone like a cousin, then to familiarize oneself with someone in order to deceive them, and finally simply to deceive.
