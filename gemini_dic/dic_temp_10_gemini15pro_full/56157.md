dodder:/ˈdɒdə(r)/ (British), /ˈdɑːdər/ (American)| n. vi.|菟丝子，寄生植物；蹒跚，摇晃|A leafless parasitic plant with yellowish thread-like stems that twine around other plants.  It lacks chlorophyll and obtains nutrients by attaching to a host plant.  As a verb, it means to tremble or totter, typically due to old age. |The dodder had completely engulfed the bush in the garden.  花园里的菟丝子完全吞没了灌木丛。|Synonym: twine, entwine,  parasitize (v.), stagger, totter|Antonym: support, nourish, steady|noun
dodder:/ˈdɒdə(r)/ (British), /ˈdɑːdər/ (American)| vi.|蹒跚，摇晃|To walk unsteadily, typically due to old age. |The old man doddered down the street. 老人蹒跚地走在街上。|Synonym: totter,  stagger,  falter|Antonym: stride,  run, sprint|verb
