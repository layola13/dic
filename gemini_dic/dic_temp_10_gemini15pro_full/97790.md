injunctions:/ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃənz/
|n.|命令；禁令；指令|An authoritative warning or order.|The court issued an injunction to stop the construction. 法院发布了禁令，阻止施工。|Synonyms: order, command, decree, mandate|Antonyms: allowance, permission|noun


injunction:/ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən/
|n.|（尤指法律的）强制令，禁制令|A judicial process or order requiring the person or persons to whom it is directed to do a particular act or to refrain from doing a particular act.|The judge granted an injunction against the protesters. 法官批准了针对抗议者的禁令。|Synonyms:  restraining order, ban, prohibition|Antonyms:  authorization, permit|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English enjonccion, from Old French enjoindre (“to enjoin”), from Latin iniungere (“to join in, to impose, to inflict”), from in- (“in, on”) + iungere (“to join”).
