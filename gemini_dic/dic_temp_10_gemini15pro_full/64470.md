erlking:/ˈɜːrlkɪŋ/ | n. | 妖精，魔王 | A mythical creature, often depicted as a malevolent spirit who preys on children.  Derived from the German word "Erlkönig". | The erlking in Goethe's poem lures a child to his death.  歌德诗中的魔王引诱一个孩子走向死亡。 | synonyms: goblin, demon, bogeyman | antonyms: angel, guardian angel | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Erlkönig*, a mistranslation of Danish *ellerkonge* (“king of the elves”).  The German word *Erle* (“alder tree”) was substituted for *elle* (“elf”) due to a mishearing or misreading.
