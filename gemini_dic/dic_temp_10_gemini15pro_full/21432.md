blowers:/ˈbloʊərz/ (US) /ˈbləʊəz/ (UK)| n. |吹风机；鼓风机；吹制工|A machine or device that produces a strong current of air or gas.|The blowers in the data center keep the servers cool. 数据中心的鼓风机使服务器保持冷却。|
The glass blowers skillfully shaped the molten glass. 玻璃吹制工巧妙地塑造了熔化的玻璃。|
近义词：fans, ventilators, pumps|反义词: extractors, vacuum cleaners|词性:noun


blowers:/ˈbloʊərz/ (US) /ˈbləʊəz/ (UK) | n. |吹牛者；自夸者 (Informal, less common) |Someone who boasts or brags.|He's one of those blowers who always exaggerates his achievements. 他是那种总是夸大自己成就的吹牛者。|近义词：boasters, braggarts|反义词:  modest people, humble people |词性:noun


词源: From the verb "to blow" (Old English *blāwan), meaning to expel air from the mouth or lungs.  The "-er" suffix denotes an agent or instrument that performs the action of the verb.
