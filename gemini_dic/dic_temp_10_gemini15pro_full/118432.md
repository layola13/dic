mesa:/ˈmeɪsə/| n. |台地，平顶山|A broad, flat-topped elevation with one or more clifflike sides, common in arid and semiarid regions.  A large, flat, elevated area of land with steep sides.|例句：The mesa offered a stunning view of the surrounding desert. 这座台地提供了周围沙漠的壮丽景色。

例句：We hiked to the top of the mesa to watch the sunset. 我们徒步到台地的顶部去看日落。|近义词：plateau, butte, tableland|反义词:valley, canyon|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *mesa* "table," from Latin *mensa* "table."  The term was adopted into English in the early 19th century to describe the landform common in the American Southwest.
