merrys:/ˈmɛriz/| n.|欢乐，乐趣 (古语，现已很少使用)|A now archaic word meaning mirth or merriment.  It survives primarily in the fixed phrase "Merrie England".|In days of yore, the people of Merrie England celebrated with much feasting and merrys. (古时，快乐英格兰的人民以盛宴和欢乐来庆祝。)|近义词：mirth, merriment, gaiety, jollity|反义词:sadness, gloom, sorrow|词性:名词
词源: From Middle English *meryes*, plural of *merye* (“pleasant, amusing”), from Old English *myrġe* (“pleasant, sweet, delightful, merry”), from Proto-Germanic *murgijaz* (“short, brief, pleasant”), from Proto-Indo-European *mréǵʰus* (“short, small”). Cognate with Old Frisian *merig*, Old Saxon *murgio*, Old High German *murgi*, Old Norse *myrgr* (“short”). More at merry.
