accord:/əˈkɔːrd/ (US) /əˈkɔːd/ (UK)| n. v.|一致，符合；协议；授予|An official agreement or treaty.|例句：The two sides signed a peace accord. 双方签署了和平协议。|近义词：agreement, treaty, pact, harmony|反义词：disagreement, conflict, discord|noun

accord:/əˈkɔːrd/ (US) /əˈkɔːd/ (UK)| v.|给予，授予；使一致，使符合|Grant or give especially as appropriate, due, or earned.|例句：The mayor accorded the visiting dignitaries a warm welcome. 市长给予来访的政要热烈的欢迎。|近义词：grant, give, bestow, confer|反义词：withhold, deny, refuse|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *acorder* "to agree, be in harmony," from Vulgar Latin *accordare*, from Latin *ad* "to" + *cor, cordis* "heart".  The literal sense is "to bring to heart," hence "to agree."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The agreement accords with our policy. = 这项协议符合我们的政策。
His views accord with mine. = 他的观点与我的一致。

一般过去时:
The judge accorded him a fair trial. = 法官给予了他公平的审判。
They accorded him the respect he deserved. = 他们给予了他应得的尊重。


现在完成时:
The two countries have accorded each other most-favored-nation status. = 这两个国家已经相互给予了最惠国待遇。
The university has accorded her an honorary degree. = 大学授予了她荣誉学位。


过去完成时:
The king had accorded him many privileges. = 国王已经给予了他很多特权。
They had accorded him a hero's welcome. = 他们已经给予了他英雄般的欢迎。



将来时:
The committee will accord him full membership. = 委员会将授予他正式成员资格。
We will accord him all the necessary assistance. = 我们将给予他一切必要的帮助。


虚拟语气:
If I were to accord him this privilege, it would set a precedent. = 如果我给予他这项特权，那将开创一个先例。
I wish they would accord us the same respect. = 我希望他们给予我们同样的尊重。



被动语态:
He was accorded a warm welcome. = 他受到了热烈的欢迎。
Special privileges were accorded to senior staff. = 高级职员被赋予了特殊待遇。


疑问句:
Does this accord with your understanding? = 这与你的理解一致吗？
What privileges were accorded to him? = 他被赋予了哪些特权？



否定句:
His actions do not accord with his words. = 他的行为与言辞不符。
The judge did not accord him bail. = 法官没有给予他保释。



PHRASE->
in accord with = 与...一致
of one's own accord = 自愿地
with one accord = 一致地
accord a warm welcome = 给予热烈的欢迎
accord due respect = 给予应有的尊重
