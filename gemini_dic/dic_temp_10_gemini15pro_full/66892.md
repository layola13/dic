expedientness:/ɪkˈspiːdiəntnəs/| n. |权宜之计；方便；利己；合算|The quality of being expedient; appropriateness for a particular purpose, especially one involving advantage or self-interest, even at the expense of ethics. |His expedientness in handling the situation was questionable. 他处理这种情况的权宜之计值得怀疑。|Synonyms: convenience, practicality, utility, advantage, opportunism |Antonyms:  disadvantage, impracticality, inexpediency, principle |noun

expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/| adj. |权宜的；方便的；有利的；合算的|(of an action) convenient and practical although possibly improper or immoral. |It is expedient to use a cheaper material. 使用更便宜的材料是权宜之计。|Synonyms: advantageous, beneficial, helpful, useful, practical |Antonyms:  disadvantageous, unhelpful, impractical, inexpedient |adjective
