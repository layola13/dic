Amish: /ˈɑːmɪʃ/ | n. adj.| 阿米什人；阿米什人的 | A member of a strict Mennonite sect, known for its simple lifestyle, plain dress, and rejection of modern technology. |  The Amish live a life separate from modern society. 阿米什人过着与现代社会隔绝的生活。|  We visited an Amish community in Pennsylvania. 我们参观了宾夕法尼亚州的一个阿米什社区。| 近义词: Mennonite, plain people | 反义词: modern, worldly | 词性: 名词, 形容词 | 词源: From Jakob Ammann, a 17th-century Swiss Anabaptist leader.
