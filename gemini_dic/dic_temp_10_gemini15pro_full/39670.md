conservatory:/kənˈsɜːrvətɔːri/ (US) /kənˈsɜːvətri/ (UK)| n.|温室；音乐学院|A room with a glass roof and walls, attached to a house, used as a greenhouse or a sunroom.  A school where people are trained in music or acting. |例句：My grandmother loves to spend time in her conservatory, tending to her plants. 我的祖母喜欢在她的温室里打理她的植物。

例句：He studied violin at the conservatory for four years. 他在音乐学院学习了四年小提琴。|近义词：greenhouse, glasshouse (for building); music school, academy (for school)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


conservatory:/kənˈsɜːrvətɔːri/ (US) /kənˈsɜːvətri/ (UK)| adj. (rare)| 保存的；保护的|Serving to preserve; protective.|例句：The conservatory measures were put in place to protect the endangered species.  采取了保护措施来保护濒危物种。

例句：The government implemented conservatory policies to safeguard the historical buildings. 政府实施了保护政策来保护历史建筑。|近义词：preservative, protective|反义词:destructive|词性:adjective (rarely used)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *conservatorium, from Latin conservare (“to preserve”), from con- (“together”) + servare (“to keep, guard”).
