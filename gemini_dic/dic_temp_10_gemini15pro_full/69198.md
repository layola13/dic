FDIC:/ˌɛf.diː.aɪ̯ˈsiː/
n.
Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (联邦存款保险公司)
A U.S. government corporation created by the Glass-Steagall Act of 1933. It insures deposits in banks and savings institutions for up to $250,000 per depositor, per insured bank, for each account ownership category.
The FDIC protects your money in the bank in case of bank failure.  联邦存款保险公司在银行倒闭的情况下保护你的存款。
My savings account is FDIC-insured. 我的储蓄账户是由联邦存款保险公司承保的。
近义词:deposit insurance, banking regulation
反义词: (No direct antonym, but conceptually: deregulation)
词性: noun
