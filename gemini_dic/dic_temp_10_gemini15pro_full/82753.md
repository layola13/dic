guilefully:/ˈɡaɪlfəli/ | adv. | 狡猾地，诡诈地 | In a cunning or deceitful manner; with artful deception. | He guilefully convinced his friend to invest in the failing business.  他狡猾地说服他的朋友投资于那家快要倒闭的公司。| synonym: craftily, deceitfully, slyly, cunningly | antonym: honestly, frankly, openly | 词性: 副词

guilefully:/ˈɡaɪlfəli/ | adv. | 狡猾地，诡诈地 |  Using or showing skill in deception; subtly deceitful. | The fox guilefully lured the chicken away from the coop. 狐狸狡猾地把鸡从鸡舍里引诱出来。 | synonym: artfully, insidiously, trickily | antonym: ingenuously, artlessly, candidly | 词性: 副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gilefulli*, from *gileful* + *-li*. *Gileful* is from Old French *guileful*, from *guile* ("deceit, trickery") + *-ful*. *Guile* comes from Old North French *wile* (compare Norman *guile*), from Frankish *wigila*, from Proto-Germanic *wigalō* (“trick, cunning, magic”). Cognate with Old English *wigle* (“magic, divination, sorcery, soothsaying”). More at wile.
