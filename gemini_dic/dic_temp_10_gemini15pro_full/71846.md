flench:/flɛntʃ/| v.| 畏缩，退缩，躲避| To shrink back involuntarily, as from pain or danger. | He flenched when the doctor injected him with the needle. 他在医生给他打针时畏缩了一下。|近义词：wince, flinch, recoil, shrink|反义词：brave, confront, face|词性：动词

flench:/flɛntʃ/| n.| 畏缩，退缩| An act of flinching or shrinking back.|  His flench betrayed his fear. 他的畏缩暴露了他的恐惧。|近义词：wince, flinch, recoil, shrinking|反义词：bravery, boldness, courage|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *flenchen, frequentative of  *flinchen (“to bend, turn aside”), from Old English *flincan, from Proto-Germanic *flinganą (“to bend, give way”). Cognate with Old Norse flinga (“to throw, fling”).
