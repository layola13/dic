eukaryote:/juːˈkæriəʊt/ (UK) /juːˈkærioʊt/ (US)| n. |真核生物|An organism whose cells contain a nucleus surrounded by a membrane and whose DNA is bound together by proteins (histones) into chromosomes. The cells of eukaryotes also contain an endoplasmic reticulum and numerous specialized organelles not present in prokaryotes, especially mitochondria and, in plants, chloroplasts.|Eukaryotes include animals, plants, and fungi. 真核生物包括动物、植物和真菌。| synonym: eucaryote | antonym: prokaryote | noun

eukaryotic:/juːˌkæriˈɒtɪk/ (UK) /juːˌkæriˈɑːtɪk/ (US) | adj. |真核的|Relating to or characteristic of a eukaryote, or organisms whose cells contain a membrane-bound nucleus.|Eukaryotic cells are more complex than prokaryotic cells. 真核细胞比原核细胞更复杂。| synonym: eucaryotic | antonym: prokaryotic | adjective
