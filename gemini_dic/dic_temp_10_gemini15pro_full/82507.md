gryphaea:/ɡrɪˈfiːə/| n. |鬼扇贝，牡蛎|An extinct genus of fossil oysters, characteristic of the Mesozoic, especially abundant in the Lias. They are often called Devil's toenails because of their thick, curved shells.|Example 1: The paleontologist unearthed a large gryphaea fossil, its curved shell resembling a devil's toenail.  古生物学家出土了一块巨大的鬼扇贝化石，其弯曲的外壳像个魔鬼的脚趾甲。

Example 2: Gryphaea were abundant in the Jurassic seas. 鬼扇贝在侏罗纪的海洋中大量存在。|近义词：oyster（牡蛎）|反义词: None (No direct opposite for an extinct genus)|词性: Noun
