cognitivism:/ˈkɒɡnɪtɪvɪzəm/ (US) /ˈkɑːɡnɪtɪvɪzəm/| n.|认知主义|A theoretical framework in psychology emphasizing the role of mental processes, such as memory, attention, and problem-solving, in understanding human behavior.|Cognitivism replaced behaviorism as the dominant paradigm in psychology.|Synonyms: mentalism, cognitive psychology|Antonyms: behaviorism|noun
