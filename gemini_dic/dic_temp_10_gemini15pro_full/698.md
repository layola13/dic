abseiler: /ˈæbseɪlər/ (UK), /əbˈseɪlər/ (US) | n. | 绳降者，绕绳下降者 | A person who descends a rock face or other steep surface by using a doubled rope coiled around the body and fixed at a higher point. | The abseiler skillfully navigated the cliff face.  绳降者熟练地绕过悬崖面。

The experienced abseiler checked his equipment before descending.  经验丰富的绳降者在下落前检查了他的装备。


近义词: rappeller | 反义词: climber (somewhat, as climbers ascend while abseilers descend) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From German  *abseilen*,  "to rope down," from *ab* "down, off" + *Seil* "rope".


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The abseiler uses a harness and rope. = 绳降者使用安全带和绳索。
Abseilers often work in teams. = 绳降者通常团队合作。


一般过去时:
The abseiler descended the cliff quickly. = 绳降者快速下降了悬崖。
They abseiled down the building to escape the fire. = 他们顺着大楼绳降以逃离火灾。


现在完成时:
The abseiler has reached the bottom safely. = 绳降者已安全到达底部。
She has abseiled many times before. = 她以前进行过多次绳降。

They had abseiled down the mountain before the storm hit. = 他们在暴风雨来临之前从山上绳降下来。
