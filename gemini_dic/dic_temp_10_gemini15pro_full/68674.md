farce:/fɑːrs/| n.|闹剧；滑稽剧；荒唐可笑的事情|A light, humorous play in which the plot depends upon a skillfully exploited situation rather than upon the development of character.|The meeting turned into a complete farce when the chairman fell asleep.|近义词：mockery, travesty, sham, parody|反义词：tragedy, drama|词性:noun
词源:From Old French *farsir* "to stuff," from Latin *farcire* "to stuff." Originally referred to stuffing added to liturgical texts, later to comic interludes stuffed into religious dramas, and eventually to any broadly humorous play.
