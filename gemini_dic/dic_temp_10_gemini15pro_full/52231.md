diatribe: /ˈdaɪətraɪb/ | n. | 抨击；檄文；长篇痛骂 | A forceful and bitter verbal attack against someone or something. |  He launched into a diatribe against the government's economic policies. 他对政府的经济政策展开了猛烈的抨击。

The article was a diatribe against the evils of modern society. 这篇文章是对现代社会弊病的猛烈抨击。

synonym: tirade, rant, harangue, denunciation, fulmination
antonym: praise, commendation, eulogy, panegyric
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *diatriba, from Greek diatribē (“a wearing away, spending of time”), from dia- (“through”) + tribein (“to rub, wear”).
