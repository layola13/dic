expedite:/ˈekspədaɪt/| vt.|加快；促进；发出|To speed up the progress of; hasten.  To issue officially, as a document.|The company hired a consultant to expedite the project. 公司聘请了一位顾问来加快项目进度。 The government expedited the visa process for the refugees. 政府加快了难民的签证办理流程。|synonyms: accelerate, hasten, quicken, rush, advance, forward|antonyms: delay, hinder, obstruct, impede|verb

expediter:/ˈekspədaɪtər/| n.|催促者；促进者|A person who expedites something.|The shipping expediter ensured the goods arrived on time. 货运催促员确保货物准时到达。|synonyms: facilitator, agent, coordinator|antonyms: obstructor, hinderer|noun

expedition:/ˌekspəˈdɪʃn/| n.|远征；探险；考察；迅速|A journey or voyage undertaken by a group of people with a particular purpose, especially that of exploration, scientific research, or war. Speed in performance or action.|The scientists embarked on an expedition to the Amazon rainforest. 科学家们开始了一次亚马逊雨林的探险。 The expedition was completed with remarkable expedition. 这次远征以惊人的速度完成。|synonyms: journey, voyage, trek, quest, speed, haste, swiftness|antonyms: delay, slowness|noun

expeditious:/ˌekspəˈdɪʃəs/| adj.|迅速的；敏捷的|Done with speed and efficiency.|The company provided expeditious service. 公司提供了快捷的服务。 The expeditious handling of the crisis prevented further damage. 对危机迅速的处理防止了进一步的损害。|synonyms: quick, swift, speedy, prompt, efficient|antonyms: slow, inefficient, dilatory|adjective
