collotype:/ˈkɒləˌtaɪp/| n.|珂罗版，胶版印刷|A printing process using a photographic negative image transferred to a gelatin-coated plate.  It's known for its ability to reproduce fine detail and tonal range.|Example: The museum displayed a rare collotype print of a famous painting. 博物馆展出了一幅名画的珍稀珂罗版印刷品。

Example: Collotype was a popular method for reproducing artwork in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.  珂罗版是19世纪末20世纪初复制艺术品的流行方法。|近义词：photogelatin, heliotype|反义词:|noun
