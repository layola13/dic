overfall:/ˈoʊvərˌfɔːl/ (US), /ˈəʊvəfɔːl/ (UK)| n.| 瀑布；跌水；突然的灾祸；倾覆|A steep descent of water in a stream or river; a sudden downfall or catastrophe; the act of capsizing.|The kayaker skillfully navigated the treacherous overfall.  皮划艇运动员巧妙地绕过了危险的瀑布。|The overfall of the government came as a shock to everyone. 政府的垮台令所有人震惊。|近义词：waterfall, cascade, cataract, collapse, downfall|反义词: ascent, rise|词性:名词

overfall:/ˈoʊvərˌfɔːl/ (US), /ˈəʊvəfɔːl/ (UK)| v.|  倾覆；翻倒|To capsize or overturn.|The small boat overfell in the strong winds.  小船在强风中翻了。|近义词：capsize, overturn, upset|反义词:right, stabilize|词性:动词
