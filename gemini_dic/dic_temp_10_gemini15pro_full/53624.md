discern:/dɪˈsɜːrn/ /dɪˈsɝːn/| v. |察觉，识别，辨别|To perceive or recognize something, especially something that is not obvious.|She was able to discern a faint light in the distance. 她能够辨认出远处微弱的光芒。|I could discern no difference between the two paintings. 我看不出这两幅画有什么区别。|近义词:perceive, detect, distinguish, recognize|反义词:misinterpret, overlook, confuse|词性:动词

discernible:/dɪˈsɜːrnəbl/ /dɪˈsɝːnəbəl/| adj. |可辨别的，可识别的|Able to be perceived or recognized.|There was a discernible improvement in her health. 她的健康状况有了明显的改善。|The difference between the two colors is barely discernible. 两种颜色之间的差异几乎难以察觉。|近义词: perceptible, noticeable, detectable|反义词: imperceptible, unnoticeable, invisible|词性:形容词

discernment:/dɪˈsɜːnmənt/ /dɪˈsɝːnmənt/| n. |洞察力，识别力|The ability to judge well.|He showed great discernment in choosing his friends. 他在选择朋友方面表现出极佳的洞察力。|Her literary discernment is impeccable. 她的文学鉴赏力无懈可击。|近义词: judgment, insight, perception, acumen|反义词: naiveté, ignorance, blindness|词性:名词

词源: From Latin *discernere*, from *dis-* (apart) + *cernere* (to separate, distinguish).
