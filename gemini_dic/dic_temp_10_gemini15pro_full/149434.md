pacos:/ˈpɑːkoʊz/ (US), /ˈpækoʊz/ (UK)| n.|警察 (贬义，主要用于拉丁美洲)|A derogatory term for police officers, primarily used in Latin America.  It originates from the Spanish abbreviation "PACO" (Policía Armada y de Orden Público - Armed and Public Order Police), used during the Franco dictatorship in Spain. This term carries negative connotations and is often used in protests or by individuals critical of law enforcement.|例句：The pacos showed up at the protest and started arresting people. 警察出现在抗议现场并开始逮捕人。

例句：People were chanting anti-pacos slogans. 人们高喊反警察的口号。|近义词：cops, pigs (both derogatory), police (neutral), law enforcement (neutral)|反义词:None (lack of a direct opposite, but could consider "civilians" or "protesters" in certain contexts)|词性:Noun
