dharma:/ˈdɑːrmə/ (US), /ˈdɑːmə/ (UK)| n.| 法, 道, 正法, 义务, 责任, 教义, 佛法 |In Hinduism and Buddhism, dharma signifies the principle of cosmic order, righteousness, and duty. It refers to the behaviours and actions that are considered to be in accordance with rta, the order that makes life and universe possible.  |He sought to live his life according to his dharma. 他力求按照自己的正法生活。|Synonyms: righteousness, duty, law, truth, justice, virtue, piety, religion, teaching, doctrine|Antonyms: adharma (unrighteousness), injustice, impiety|noun
