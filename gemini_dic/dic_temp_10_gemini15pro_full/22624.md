boozy:/ˈbuːzi/ | adj. | 醉醺醺的，嗜酒的 |  Given to or marked by excessive drinking of alcohol; intoxicated. | He had a boozy lunch and couldn't focus in the afternoon. 他喝得醉醺醺地吃了午饭，下午无法集中注意力。| The bar was filled with boozy laughter. 酒吧里充满了醉醺醺的笑声。| 近义词: drunken, intoxicated, tipsy | 反义词: sober, temperate | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From booze + -y.  Booze itself comes from Middle Dutch *būsen "to drink," related to Old English būan "to dwell, inhabit," suggesting a connection to staying in a place to drink.
