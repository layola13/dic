mass-observationist: /ˌmæs ˌɒbzəˈveɪʃənɪst/ | n. | 大众观察员 | A person who participates in or conducts Mass Observation, a social research organization founded in Britain in 1937 that aimed to create an "anthropology of ourselves" by documenting everyday life and opinions. |  Example: The mass-observationist meticulously recorded the reactions of ordinary people to the declaration of war.  大众观察员一丝不苟地记录了普通人对宣战的反应。 | Example: Mass-observationists gathered data on everything from shopping habits to political views. 大众观察员收集了从购物习惯到政治观点的各种数据。 | Near synonyms: social researcher, observer, surveyor, anthropologist | Antonym: N/A | Noun
