anaglyph: /ˈænəɡlɪf/ | n. | 浮雕;立体照片;立体图 | A picture printed in two colors, usually red and green or red and blue, which appears three-dimensional when viewed through glasses with lenses of corresponding colors. | Example 1:  I watched the 3D movie using an anaglyph. 我用立体眼镜观看了这部3D电影。

Example 2:  The museum displayed several anaglyphs of ancient sculptures.  博物馆展出了几幅古代雕塑的立体照片。 | Near Synonyms: stereogram, 3D image | Antonyms: 2D image, flat image | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *anaglyphe*, from Ancient Greek *ἀνάγλυφος (anágluphos)*, from *ἀνά (aná)* meaning "up" + *γλύφω (glúphō)* meaning "carve".  Ultimately relates to the raised appearance of the image.
