hawked:/hɔːkt/ | v. |叫卖；兜售；散播（消息、想法等）| to sell goods by calling out in public places; to offer (something) for sale by going from place to place; to make widely known especially by repetition | The street vendor hawked his wares. 街头小贩叫卖他的商品。|  synonyms: peddle, vend, tout | antonyms: buy, purchase | verb

hawked:/hɔːkt/ | adj. |(指鼻子)钩状的| shaped like a hawk's beak; hooked | He had a hawked nose. 他有一个钩状的鼻子。 | synonyms: hooked, aquiline | antonyms: straight | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hawken, from Old English *hafoc (“hawk”), of Germanic origin.  The verb sense "to hunt with hawks" later developed into "to carry about and offer for sale," presumably from the image of a hawk swooping down on prey.

PHRASE->
hawk around:  兜售；四处推销 He was hawking around his latest invention. 他四处推销他的最新发明。
hawk one's wares:  兜售货物；推销服务  Many vendors were hawking their wares at the festival. 许多小贩在节日里兜售他们的商品。
hawk something to someone:  向某人推销某物  He tried to hawk his old car to me. 他试图把他的旧车卖给我。
hawk after: 渴望得到; 追求  He's always hawking after the latest gadgets. 他总是渴望得到最新的电子产品。
