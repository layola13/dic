hypostome:/ˈhaɪpəˌstoʊm/| n. |口下板; 垂唇|A plate or fold on the ventral side of the mouth in various invertebrates, such as hydroids and arachnids.  In ticks and mites, it bears recurved teeth and is used for anchoring to the host.|The tick's hypostome was firmly embedded in the dog's skin.|Synonyms: rostrum, proboscis|Antonyms: N/A|noun
