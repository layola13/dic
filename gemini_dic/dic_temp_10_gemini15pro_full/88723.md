hobnail:/ˈhɒbneɪl/ (UK) /ˈhɑːbneɪl/ (US)| n. adj.|鞋钉；以大头钉装饰的|A short nail with a thick head, used to protect the soles of boots and shoes.  Also used as an adjective to describe something studded with, or resembling, such nails.|例句：The farmer wore hobnail boots.  农民穿着钉了鞋钉的靴子。|近义词：stud, cleat, spike|反义词:(no direct antonym, perhaps "smooth-soled")|词性: noun, adjective

hobnail:/ˈhɒbneɪl/ (UK) /ˈhɑːbneɪl/ (US)| adj.|以大头钉装饰的|Studded with hobnails; resembling hobnails.|例句：He had a hobnail liver. 他的肝硬化得很厉害 (比喻用法, 指表面粗糙).|近义词：studded, rough, textured|反义词:smooth|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From *hob* (a projection, peg) + *nail*.  The "hob" part likely refers to the hob of a fireplace, the raised sides or shelf, likened to the raised head of the nail.
