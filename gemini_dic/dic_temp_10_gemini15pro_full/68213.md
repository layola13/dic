fairest:/ˈfeə.rɪst/ /ˈfer.ɪst/| adj.|最美丽的；最公平的|superlative of fair: most fair|She was the fairest of them all. 她是所有人中最美丽的。|近义词：most beautiful, most attractive, prettiest, loveliest, most just, most impartial|反义词：ugliest, least attractive, most unjust, most biased|词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *fairest*, *fayrest*, from Old English *fægerest* (superlative of *fæger* (“beautiful, fair, lovely, pleasant; clean, pure; calm, peaceful”)), from Proto-Germanic *fagraz* (“beautiful, fitting”), from Proto-Indo-European *peh₂ǵ-rós* (from *peh₂ǵ-* (“to decorate, adorn”)). Cognate with West Frisian *faagerst*, Dutch *vaarst* (“best, most skilled”), German *feist* (“fat, corpulent”).  The semantic development is from "fitting, attractive" to "beautiful" to "fair, just".
