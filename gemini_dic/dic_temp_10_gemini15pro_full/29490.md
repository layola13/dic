catapult:/ˈkætəpʌlt/ (US) /ˈkætəpʌlt/ (UK)| n. v. |弹弓，投石器；突然猛增；|A catapult is a device used to hurl or launch objects, typically large stones or other projectiles, over a distance. As a verb, it means to hurl or launch something with or as if with a catapult, or to undergo a sudden and significant increase or rise.|
The Romans used catapults to besiege cities.  罗马人使用弹弓攻城。

His new book catapulted him to fame. 他的新书使他一举成名。


近义词：sling, launch, propel, hurl, boost|
反义词：-|
词性：noun, verb
