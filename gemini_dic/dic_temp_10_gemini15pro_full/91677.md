hyperclassicality:/ˌhaɪpərˈklæsɪkælɪti/ |n.| 超古典主义，极端古典主义 |A heightened or exaggerated form of classicism, often characterized by strict adherence to traditional forms and a rejection of modern influences. It emphasizes order, balance, and restraint, sometimes to an excessive degree.|  His compositions display a marked hyperclassicality, reminiscent of the Baroque era. 他的作品展现出明显的超古典主义，让人想起巴洛克时代。|  The architecture of the building embodied hyperclassicality, with its rigid symmetry and ornate detailing. 这座建筑的结构体现了超古典主义，具有严格的对称性和华丽的细节。 |  近义词:  formalism, traditionalism | 反义词: modernism, avant-gardism | 词性: noun

Etymology:
hyper- (Greek): prefix meaning "over, beyond, excessive"
classicality (from classical + -ity):  the state or quality of being classical
