paralipsis:/ˌpærəˈlɪpsɪs/ | n. |  佯谬,欲盖弥彰 |  A rhetorical figure in which a speaker emphasizes something by pretending to pass over it. | I won't mention his criminal record, or the fact that he's been fired from every job he's ever had. 我不会提及他的犯罪记录，或者说他被每一份工作都解雇了的事实。 |  preterition, apophasis |  emphasis | noun

paralipsis:/ˌpærəˈlɪpsɪs/
Etymology: From Late Latin *paralipsis*, from Ancient Greek παράλειψις (paráleipsis, “omission, passing over”), from παραλείπω (paraleípō, “I omit, pass over”), from παρά (pará, “beside, alongside”) + λείπω (leípō, “I leave”).
