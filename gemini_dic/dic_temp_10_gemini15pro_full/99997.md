interpeal:/ˌɪntərˈpiːl/| n. |申诉，抗议，呼吁|An act of pleading or protesting against a decision, especially a legal one.  A request for interference or help, especially to a higher authority.|例句：The lawyer filed an interpeal to the higher court.  律师向高等法院提出上诉。

例句：Their interpeal for mercy went unanswered. 他们的求饶没有得到回应。|近义词：appeal, plea, petition, protest|反义词:acceptance, acquiescence|词性:noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *interpellare, from Latin inter- (between) + pellere (to push, drive).  Originally meaning "to interrupt," the sense of "to make an urgent request" developed later.
