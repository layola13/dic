histophyly:/ˌhɪstoʊˈfaɪli/ | n. | 组织亲和性；组织嗜性 | The tendency of some pathogens or substances to have an affinity for specific tissues.  It refers to the localization of a parasite, bacteria, or virus in certain tissues of the host. | Example 1: The histophyly of the virus for nervous tissue explains the neurological symptoms.  这种病毒对神经组织的组织嗜性解释了神经系统症状。

Example 2:  Understanding the histophyly of certain cancer cells is crucial for developing targeted therapies. 了解某些癌细胞的组织嗜性对于开发靶向疗法至关重要。| Synonym: tissue tropism, tissue affinity | Antonym: N/A | noun
