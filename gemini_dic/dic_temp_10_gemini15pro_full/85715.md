haw:/hɔː/ | n. | 山楂，山楂树；支吾，吞吐 | a thorny shrub or small tree that produces small, bright red fruits |  The birds were feasting on the haws in the hedgerow. 鸟儿们在树篱上的山楂上尽情享用。 | synonym: hawthorn, hesitation (when referring to speech) | antonym: fluency (when referring to speech) | noun

haw:/hɔː/ | v. |  (犹豫或不情愿地)说话吞吐，支吾 | to speak hesitantly or with frequent pauses | He hawed and hemmed before finally answering the question.  他吞吞吐吐，最后才回答了这个问题。| synonym: stammer, stutter, hesitate | antonym: articulate, speak fluently | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *haga, meaning "hedge," "enclosure," or "hawthorn."  The fruit and the tree likely got their name from the tree's common use in hedgerows. The verb sense of "to haw" developed from the sound one might make when hesitating in speech.
