expurgatory:/ˈekspərɡətɔːri/
adjective
净化（的），删节（的）
Tending to expurgate.
例句：The editor made several expurgatory changes to the manuscript before publication. 编辑在出版前对手稿进行了几处删改。

例句：The expurgatory version of the novel removed all the offensive language. 小说的删节版删除了所有冒犯性的语言。

近义词：cleansing, purifying, bowdlerizing, censoring
反义词：unabridged, unexpurgated, uncensored
词性：adjective

expurgate:/ˈekspərɡeɪt/
verb
净化；删去（不当之处）
To remove matter thought to be objectionable or unsuitable from (a book or account).
例句：The publisher expurgated the novel before releasing it to a younger audience. 出版商在将小说发行给年轻读者之前对其进行了删改。

例句：They expurgated all references to sex and violence from the film. 他们从电影中删去了所有关于性和暴力的内容。

近义词：cleanse, purify, bowdlerize, censor
反义词：include, retain, insert
词性：verb

expurgation:/ˌekspərˈɡeɪʃn/
noun
净化；删节
The act of expurgating.
例句：The expurgation of the text resulted in a much shorter version.  文本的删节导致了更短的版本。

例句：The film underwent significant expurgation before being shown on television. 这部电影在电视上播出之前经历了大量的删节。

近义词：cleansing, purification, bowdlerization, censorship
反义词：inclusion, retention, insertion
词性：noun
