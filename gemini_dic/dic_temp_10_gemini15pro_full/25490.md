bullfinches:/ˈbʊlfɪntʃɪz/
n.
红腹灰雀（复数）
A small finch, *Pyrrhula pyrrhula*, with a stout bill and mainly grey plumage, the male having a rosy red breast and cheeks.
Several bullfinches were feeding on the sunflower seeds. 几只红腹灰雀正在吃葵花籽。

ETYMOLOGY->
From "bull" + "finch".  "Bull" likely refers to the bird's thick, bull-like neck and head, or perhaps its reddish breast resembling a bull's. "Finch" comes from Old English "finc", related to German "fink" and Dutch "vink".
