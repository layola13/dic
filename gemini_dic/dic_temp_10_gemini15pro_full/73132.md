forager:/ˈfɔːrɪdʒər/ (US) /ˈfɒrɪdʒə(r)/ (UK)| n.|觅食者，搜寻者|A person or animal that searches widely for food or provisions.|例句：The forager returned to the camp with a basket of berries. 觅食者带着一篮子浆果回到了营地。

例句：Squirrels are natural foragers, constantly searching for nuts and seeds. 松鼠是天生的觅食者，不断地寻找坚果和种子。

|近义词：hunter, gatherer, searcher, scavenger|反义词:farmer, cultivator|词性:名词

forage:/ˈfɔːrɪdʒ/ (US) /ˈfɒrɪdʒ/ (UK)| v.  n.|觅食，搜寻；饲料| (v.) To search widely for food or provisions; (n.) Food for animals, especially farm animals. |例句：The pigs foraged in the woods for acorns. 猪在树林里觅食橡子。

例句：The farmer provided forage for the cows during the winter. 农民在冬天为奶牛提供饲料。

|近义词：(v.) hunt, gather, search, rummage; (n.) feed, fodder|反义词:(v.) plant, cultivate; (n.) None|词性:动词，名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *fourrager "to rummage, search," from fourrage "fodder," from Vulgar Latin *foderagium, from Latin foderum "fodder," from fodere "to dig."
