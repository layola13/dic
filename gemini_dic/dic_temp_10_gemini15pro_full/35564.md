coalitionism:/ˌkoʊəˈlɪʃənɪzəm/| n. |联合主义，联盟主义|The act or principle of forming or participating in a coalition government.|The country's history is marked by periods of coalitionism, where multiple parties share power.  这个国家的历史以联合主义时期为标志，多个政党分享权力。|Synonyms: coalition politics, alliance building, power-sharing|Antonyms: one-party rule, single-party system|noun


coalitionism:/ˌkəʊəˈlɪʃənɪzəm/| n. |联合主义，联盟主义|The practice or principle of forming coalitions, especially in politics.|Coalitionism can lead to more representative governments, but it can also be unstable. 联合主义可以导致更具代表性的政府，但也可能不稳定。|Synonyms: coalition politics, collaborative governance, multi-party system|Antonyms: one-party rule, authoritarianism|noun
