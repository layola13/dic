carnivora: /kɑːrˈnɪvərə/ | n. | 食肉目 | An order of placental mammals characterized by their specialized teeth and claws for tearing flesh.  This includes animals like cats, dogs, bears, and seals. | Lions and tigers belong to the order Carnivora. 狮子和老虎属于食肉目。 Wolves are also members of the Carnivora order. 狼也是食肉目的一员。 | 近义词: carnivores | 反义词: herbivora | 词性: noun

carnivore: /ˈkɑːrnɪvɔːr/ | n. | 食肉动物 | An animal that feeds primarily on meat. |  A lion is a carnivore. 狮子是食肉动物。 Sharks are carnivores of the ocean.  鲨鱼是海洋中的食肉动物。| 近义词: predator, meat-eater | 反义词: herbivore | 词性: noun


Etymology:
From Latin *carnivorus (“flesh-eating”), from carn- (stem of carō (“flesh”)) + vorāre (“to devour”).  The -vora ending is related to the English word "voracious."
