bewitchingness:/bɪˈwɪtʃɪŋnɪs/| n.| 迷人; 魅力; 妖娆 |The quality of being enchanting and captivating; the power to fascinate and charm. |Her bewitchingness captivated everyone in the room. 她的魅力迷住了房间里的每一个人。|Synonyms: allure, charm, enchantment, magnetism, magic|Antonyms: repulsiveness, unpleasantness, ugliness|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From bewitching + -ness.  Bewitching comes from the Old English verb *bewiccian*, meaning "to practice witchcraft upon," from *be-* (making a verb causative) + *wiccian* "to bewitch," related to *wicca* "witch."  The suffix *-ness* forms nouns expressing a state or quality.
