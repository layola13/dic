goody-goody:/ˈɡʊdi ˈɡʊdi/ | n. |好好先生；伪善者|A person who behaves in a way intended to appear morally superior to others, often excessively so.  A self-righteous person.|例句：He's such a goody-goody, always telling everyone what to do. 他真是个好好先生，总是告诉大家该怎么做。|例句：She acted like a goody-goody in front of the teacher, but behind her back, she was just as naughty as the rest of us. 她在老师面前装作好好先生，但在背后，她和我们其他人一样淘气。|近义词：prig, self-righteous person, do-gooder (often used ironically)|反义词：rebel, nonconformist, scoundrel|词性: noun

goody-goody:/ˈɡʊdi ˈɡʊdi/ | adj. |伪善的；假正经的|Excessively or affectedly virtuous; smugly righteous. |例句：He had a goody-goody attitude that annoyed everyone. 他假正经的态度惹恼了所有人。|例句：Don't be so goody-goody, it's okay to have some fun sometimes. 别那么假正经，偶尔玩玩也没关系。|近义词：sanctimonious, self-righteous, pietistic|反义词：rebellious, nonconformist, wicked|词性: adjective
