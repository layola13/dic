exergue: /ˈɛksɜːɡ/ | n. |题辞；铭文；短文；（书或文件的）引言，开场白|A motto or short quotation, especially one set at the beginning of a literary work or at the head of a section or chapter, or a short inscription on a coin or medal. |The novel begins with an exergue from a poem by Walt Whitman. 这部小说以惠特曼一首诗的题辞开头。|近义词: inscription, motto, epigraph |反义词:  (No direct antonym exists, potentially "postscript" or "afterword") |词性: noun

Etymology:
From French *exergue*, from Ancient Greek *ἐξέργον (exérgon)*, from *ἐξ- (ex-)* (“out of, from”) + *ἔργον (érgon)* (“work”).  Literally "out of the work".  Originally referred to the small space on a coin or medal outside the main design.
