paraphrasian: /ˌpærəˈfreɪʒən/ | adj. |释义的，解释的；意译的| Relating to or expressed in paraphrase; characterized by paraphrase.  A paraphrasian is someone who paraphrases or uses paraphrase frequently.  It can also describe something that is itself a paraphrase. | The professor offered a paraphrasian explanation of the complex theorem. 教授对这个复杂的定理进行了解释说明。

The student's essay was highly paraphrasian, relying heavily on rewording other sources.  这个学生的论文大量释义，严重依赖于改写其他来源。| 近义词: explanatory, interpretive, expository | 反义词: literal, verbatim | 词性: adjective

Etymology:  From the Greek "paraphrasis" (παράφρασις), meaning "a restatement of a text or passage giving the meaning in another form," from "para-" (παρά) "beside" + "phrazein" (φράζειν) "to tell, declare."
