overromanticizing: /ˌoʊvərroʊˈmæntɪsaɪzɪŋ/
|v.|过度浪漫化|The act of portraying something in an excessively romantic or idealized way, often ignoring or downplaying negative aspects.|She was overromanticizing the idea of living in a cabin in the woods, forgetting about the isolation and harsh winters. 她过度浪漫化了住在森林小屋的想法，忘记了孤立和严酷的冬天。|
|v.|过度理想化|The act of viewing something in an unrealistically positive light, often overlooking potential difficulties or flaws.|He was overromanticizing his past relationship, remembering only the good times and ignoring the reasons why it ended. 他过度理想化了他过去的关系，只记得美好的时光，而忽略了它结束的原因。|
|近义词:|idealizing, glorifying, sentimentalizing|
|反义词:|being realistic, being pragmatic, being cynical|
|词性:|动名词 (gerund), 现在分词 (present participle)|
|词源:|From "over-" (excessively) + "romanticize" (to make romantic) + "-ing" (present participle/gerund suffix).  "Romanticize" comes from "romantic" ultimately deriving from the Old French word "romanz" meaning a narrative in the Romance language.|
