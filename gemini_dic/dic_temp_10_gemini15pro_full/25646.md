bundists:/ˈbʌndɪsts/
noun
崩得主义者
Members of the General Jewish Labour Bund, a socialist political party founded in the Russian Empire in 1897.  It advocated for Jewish workers' rights and cultural autonomy within a socialist framework.
The Bundists, unlike the Zionists, believed in a future for Jews within Eastern Europe.
近义词:socialists, labor activists
反义词:Zionists, capitalists
词性:n.


bundist:/ˈbʌndɪst/
noun
崩得主义者
A member of the General Jewish Labour Bund.
Many Bundists were persecuted by the Tsarist regime.
近义词:socialist, labor activist
反义词:Zionist, capitalist
词性:n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Yiddish בונד (bund, "alliance," "union," "federation"), from German Bund (same meaning).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *bundą (“band, tie”).
