chaconne:/ʃəˈkɒn/ (UK), /ʃəˈkɑːn/ (US)| n.|恰空舞曲；恰空|A slow, stately dance in triple time, popular in the 17th and 18th centuries, typically in a major key and with variations over a repeated bass line.  It is also a musical form that uses the same structure.|Bach's Chaconne for solo violin is a masterpiece. 巴赫的独小提琴恰空舞曲是一部杰作。|Synonyms: passacaglia, ciaccona|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
