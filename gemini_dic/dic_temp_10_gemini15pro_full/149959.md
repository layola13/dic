palm:/pɑːm/ (UK) /pɑːm/ (US)| n. v.|手掌，棕榈树，胜利|The inner surface of the hand between the wrist and the fingers.  A tree of tropical and warm regions, typically having a tall, unbranched trunk crowned with large, fan-shaped leaves.|She held the butterfly gently in the palm of her hand. 她轻轻地把蝴蝶放在她的手掌里。|He climbed the palm tree to get a coconut. 他爬上棕榈树去摘椰子。|Synonyms: hand,  frond (for palm leaves) |Antonyms: back of hand |noun

palm:/pɑːm/ (UK) /pɑːm/ (US)| v.|用手掌托，欺骗|To conceal something in the hand, often for purposes of deception.|The magician palmed the coin and made it disappear. 魔术师把硬币藏在手里，然后让它消失了。|He palmed off the fake watch as a genuine antique. 他把假表当作真古董骗人。|Synonyms: conceal, manipulate |Antonyms: reveal, expose |verb
