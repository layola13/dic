euclidean:/juːˈklɪdiən/ | adj. | 欧几里得的；欧氏几何的 | Relating to the system of geometry described by Euclid, especially dealing with two and three dimensions.  |  Example: Euclidean geometry is based on a small set of axioms. 欧几里得几何建立在一小组公理之上。 | Example:  Non-Euclidean geometries are used in modern physics. 非欧几里得几何用于现代物理学。 | Synonym: geometric | Antonym: non-Euclidean | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Euclid, the name of a Greek mathematician (c. 300 BC),  + -ean.  Euclid was the author of the Elements, a highly influential treatise on geometry.
