casbah's:/ˈkæzbɑːz/| n.| 城堡，聚居区| A casbah is the citadel of a North African city, typically one that is walled. It often refers to the older part of a city, with maze-like narrow streets and traditional markets.  Casbah's is the possessive form, indicating ownership or association with a casbah.|  We wandered through the casbah's twisting alleys. 我们在城堡蜿蜒的小巷中漫步。 |Synonym: citadel, fortress, old town|Antonym: new town, suburb|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Arabic *qasba*  meaning "citadel". Borrowed into French as *casbah*, then into English.
