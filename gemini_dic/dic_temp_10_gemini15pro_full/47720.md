decadent:/ˈdekədənt/ /dɪˈkeɪdənt/| adj. n.|堕落的，颓废的；放纵的人|Characterized by or reflecting a state of moral or cultural decline; luxuriously self-indulgent.|

例句：
The decadent Roman emperor indulged in lavish feasts and orgies.
那个堕落的罗马皇帝沉溺于奢华的宴会和狂欢。

The decadent dessert was rich with chocolate and cream.
这道颓废的甜点充满了巧克力和奶油。


近义词：corrupt, degenerate, dissolute, profligate, self-indulgent|
反义词：austere, moral, virtuous, upright|
词性：adjective, noun|

decadent:/ˈdekədənt/ /dɪˈkeɪdənt/| n. |堕落的人|A person who is morally or culturally decadent.|

例句：
He lived like a decadent, squandering his fortune on frivolous pleasures.
他像个堕落的人一样生活，把自己的财产浪费在轻浮的享乐上。

The city was full of decadents, pursuing only pleasure and sensation.
这座城市充满了堕落的人，他们只追求享乐和刺激。


近义词：degenerate, reprobate|
反义词：puritan, ascetic|
词性：noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *décadent*, present participle of *décadrer*, "to decay," from Latin *cadere*, "to fall."  The word gained popularity in the late 19th century, associated with the Aesthetic and Decadent movements in art and literature.
