blackguard:/ˈblækɡɑːrd/ | n. vt.| 恶棍，诽谤 | A scoundrel; a villain.  |  He was a notorious blackguard who swindled many innocent people. 他是一个臭名昭著的恶棍，骗了好多无辜的人。 | synonyms: villain, scoundrel, rogue, rascal | antonyms: hero, gentleman, saint | noun
blackguard:/blækɡɑːrd/ | vt.| 辱骂，诽谤 | To abuse or vilify someone with coarse language. |  The newspaper blackguarded the politician with false accusations.  这家报纸用不实的指控诽谤了这位政治家。| synonyms: vilify, slander, defame, libel | antonyms: praise, commend, laud | verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From "black guard," originally referring to the lowest menials in a large household, such as kitchen help, who often wore sooty clothes.  Over time, the term became associated with disreputable behavior and evolved into its current meaning.
