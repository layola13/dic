interleukins:/ˌɪntərˈluːkɪnz/| n. |白细胞介素|A group of proteins (cytokines) produced primarily by leukocytes (white blood cells) that regulate various aspects of the immune system, including inflammation, immune cell differentiation, and cell growth.  They play a crucial role in cell-to-cell communication and are involved in both innate and adaptive immunity.|Interleukins play a critical role in regulating the immune response. 白细胞介素在调节免疫反应中起关键作用。|
Interleukin-6 is often elevated in inflammatory conditions. 白细胞介素-6在炎症性疾病中经常升高。|Synonyms: cytokines (more general), lymphokines (older term)|Antonyms: immunosuppressants (functional opposites)|noun
