batrachia: /bəˈtreɪkiə/ | n. | 两栖动物 | An order of amphibians, traditionally including frogs, toads, salamanders, newts, and caecilians.  Modern classifications tend to restrict Batrachia to frogs and toads, recognizing salamanders and caecilians as separate orders. |  The study of batrachia is a fascinating branch of zoology. 对两栖动物的研究是动物学中一个引人入胜的分支。|  Amphibia, amphibia| Reptiles, birds, mammals | Noun
