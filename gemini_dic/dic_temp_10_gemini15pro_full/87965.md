high-flyer: /ˈhaɪˌflaɪər/ (US) /ˈhaɪˌflaɪə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 成功人士；雄心勃勃的人；高飞者 | A person who is or has the potential to be very successful, especially in their career. |  She's a real high-flyer in the world of finance.  她在金融界是一位真正的成功人士。| synonyms: achiever, go-getter, success story, rising star | antonyms: underachiever, failure, also-ran | noun


high-flying: /ˈhaɪˈflaɪɪŋ/ (US) /ˈhaɪˈflaɪɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|  成功的；雄心勃勃的；高飞的 | Very successful, ambitious, or at a high level. | He landed a high-flying job in a prestigious law firm. 他在一家著名的律师事务所找到了一份高薪的工作。| synonyms: successful, ambitious, prestigious, high-powered | antonyms: unsuccessful, unambitious, low-level, mediocre | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the combination of "high" (meaning at a great height) and "flyer" (meaning one who flies or something that flies). The figurative meaning of someone who achieves great success emerged in the mid-20th century.
