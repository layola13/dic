interinhibition:/ˌɪntərɪnhɪˈbɪʃən/
noun
相互抑制
The mutual suppression of activity between different parts of a system, such as neurons or muscles.
Stimulation of one muscle group can cause interinhibition of another group.  刺激一组肌肉会导致另一组肌肉的相互抑制。
The brain uses interinhibition to filter out irrelevant sensory information.  大脑利用相互抑制来过滤掉不相关的感官信息。
近义词: mutual suppression, reciprocal inhibition
反义词: facilitation, excitation
词性: n.

Etymology:  From inter- + inhibition, from Latin inhibitus, past participle of inhibere "to restrain, check, prohibit," from in- "in, on" + habere "to hold."
