electrocardiogram:/ɪˌlɛktroʊˈkɑːrdioʊɡræm/ | n. |心电图|A record of the electrical activity of the heart, used in diagnosing heart disorders.|The doctor ordered an electrocardiogram to check the patient's heart rhythm.| electrocardiographs, ECGs, EKGs | None | Noun

electrocardiograms:/ɪˌlɛktroʊˈkɑːrdioʊɡræmz/ | n. |心电图（复数）|Plural form of electrocardiogram, referring to multiple records of the electrical activity of the heart.|The doctor compared the patient's electrocardiograms from different days.| electrocardiographs, ECGs, EKGs | None | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
electro- (from Greek ēlektron, meaning "amber," referring to static electricity) + cardio- (from Greek kardia, meaning "heart") + -gram (from Greek gramma, meaning "something written")
