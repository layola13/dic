hullabaloo:/ˌhʌləbəˈluː/| n. |喧闹，骚动，吵闹；|A loud confused noise, especially caused by a large mass of people. |例句：There was a great hullabaloo in the marketplace. 市场上一片喧闹。

例句：The announcement caused a hullabaloo among the students. 这项宣布在学生中引起了轩然大波。|近义词：commotion, uproar, din, racket, clamor, hubbub|反义词：quiet, silence, peace, tranquility|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain, perhaps imitative. First recorded in the mid 16th century.
