disembellish:/ˌdɪsɪmˈbɛlɪʃ/| v. | 去除...的装饰; 讲实话; 朴实地叙述| To remove embellishments or decorations from something; to tell a story plainly without exaggerating or adding fictitious details. | The historian disembellished the heroic account of the battle, revealing the mistakes and losses.  这位历史学家还原了那场战役的英雄事迹，揭示了其中的错误和损失。| Synonyms: strip, denude, simplify, deflate, understate | Antonyms: embellish, adorn, decorate, exaggerate, embroider | verb

disembellishment:/ˌdɪsɪmˈbɛlɪʃmənt/| n. |  去除装饰; 讲实话; 朴实地叙述| The act of removing embellishments or decorations; a plain or unadorned account of something. | The author's disembellishment of the myth revealed its more human aspects. 作者对神话的还原揭示了它更人性化的一面。| Synonyms: stripping, denudation, simplification | Antonyms: embellishment, adornment, decoration | noun
