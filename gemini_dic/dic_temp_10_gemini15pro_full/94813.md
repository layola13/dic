impulsivity:/ɪmˌpʌlˈsɪvɪti/
n.|冲动, 易冲动|The tendency to act on a whim, displaying behavior characterized by little or no forethought, reflection, or consideration of the consequences.|His impulsivity often led him into trouble. 他的冲动经常给他带来麻烦。|
近义词:rashness, recklessness, impetuousness|
反义词:prudence, caution, self-control|
词性:noun


impulsive:/ɪmˈpʌlsɪv/
adj.|冲动的, 易冲动的|Acting or done without forethought.|She made an impulsive decision to quit her job. 她一时冲动决定辞职。|
近义词:spontaneous, hasty, rash|
反义词:cautious, deliberate, thoughtful|
词性:adjective


impulsively:/ɪmˈpʌlsɪvli/
adv.|冲动地|In a way that is characterized by sudden action without thinking.|He impulsively bought a sports car he couldn't afford. 他一时冲动买了一辆他负担不起的跑车。|
近义词:rashly, recklessly, spontaneously|
反义词:cautiously, deliberately, thoughtfully|
词性:adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *impulsivus, from Latin impulsus (“impulse”), past participle of impellō (“I push, drive on, urge”).
