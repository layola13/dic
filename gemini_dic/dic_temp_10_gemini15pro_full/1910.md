acting: /ˈæktɪŋ/
n.|代理; 表演; 演技|The process or skill of performing a role, especially in a play, film, or television program.  It also refers to the occupation of an actor.|He's been doing some acting on the side. 他一直在兼职做一些表演工作。|Synonyms: performance, portrayal, impersonation|Antonyms: inaction, reality|noun

acting: /ˈæktɪŋ/
adj.|代理的; 临时的|Temporarily holding a position or performing a role, usually while the permanent holder is absent or unavailable.|She's the acting principal while Mr. Jones is away.  琼斯先生不在的时候，她是代理校长。|Synonyms: temporary, interim, substitute|Antonyms: permanent, regular|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the present participle of the Middle English verb *acten*, meaning "to do" or "to perform," derived from the Old French *acter* and ultimately from the Latin *agere*, meaning "to drive," "to lead," or "to do."

USAGE->
As a Noun:

一般现在时:
His acting is superb. = 他的演技很棒。
Her acting has improved greatly. = 她的演技进步很大。

一般过去时:
The acting in that play was unforgettable. = 那部戏的表演令人难忘。
His acting was criticized by some reviewers. = 他的一些表演受到了评论家的批评。

现在完成时:
His acting has earned him several awards. = 他的表演为他赢得了几个奖项。
She has been focusing on her acting career. = 她一直专注于她的演艺事业。

过去完成时:
He had been studying acting for years before he got his big break. = 在他获得重大突破之前，他已经学习表演多年了。
She had taken acting classes in college. = 她在大学里上过表演课。

将来时:
He will be showcasing his acting skills in the upcoming film. = 他将在即将上映的电影中展示他的演技。
She will continue her acting training next year. = 她明年将继续她的表演训练。


As an Adjective:

一般现在时:
He is the acting manager. = 他是代理经理。
She is the acting president of the company. = 她是公司的代理总裁。

一般过去时:
He was the acting chairman of the board last year. = 他去年是董事会的代理主席。
She was acting as his lawyer during the trial. = 在审判期间，她担任他的律师。

现在完成时:
She has been acting as the interim CEO for three months. = 她担任临时首席执行官已有三个月了。
He has been acting strangely lately. = 他最近表现得很奇怪。 (Note: this usage is less common and relates more to the verb "act")

过去完成时:
He had been acting as her advisor until his retirement. = 在他退休之前，他一直担任她的顾问。
She had been acting suspiciously before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，她一直表现得可疑。 (Note: this usage is less common and relates more to the verb "act")

将来时:
He will be acting as the liaison between the two departments. = 他将担任两个部门之间的联络人。
She will be acting as the project manager until a replacement is found. = 她将担任项目经理，直到找到替补为止。
