objurgate:/ˈɒbdʒəɡeɪt/ (US) /əbˈdʒɜːrɡeɪt/ (UK)| v. |斥责，谴责，痛骂|to rebuke severely; scold.| The teacher objurgated the student for disrupting class. 老师严厉斥责了扰乱课堂秩序的学生。| synonyms: reprimand, rebuke, chide, scold, castigate, upbraid | antonyms: praise, commend, laud, extol | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin objurgatus, past participle of objurgare (“to scold, chide, rebuke”), from ob- (“against, towards”) + jurgare (“to quarrel, scold”).
