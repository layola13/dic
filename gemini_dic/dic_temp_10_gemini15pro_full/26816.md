cages:/keɪdʒɪz/ | n. |笼子，牢笼|An enclosure made of bars or wire mesh, used to confine birds or animals.  A structure or situation that restricts someone's freedom. |The bird sang in its cage.  这只鸟在它的笼子里唱歌。|
He felt trapped in a cage of his own making. 他感觉被困在自己制造的牢笼里。|synonyms: enclosures, pens, confines, prisons | antonyms: freedom, liberty| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "cage" (13th century), from Late Latin "cavea" meaning "cavity, enclosure for animals," from Latin "cavus" meaning "hollow."
