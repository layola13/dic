ill-advisedly:/ˌɪl ədˈvaɪzɪdli/
adverb
不明智地，欠考虑地
in a way that is not sensible or wise; unwisely
He ill-advisedly invested all his money in one company. 他不明智地把所有的钱都投资在一家公司。
She ill-advisedly spoke her mind at the meeting. 她在会议上不明智地说出了自己的想法。
近义词: unwisely, foolishly, imprudently, rashly
反义词: wisely, prudently, judiciously
词性: adverb

ill-advised:/ˌɪl ədˈvaɪzd/
adjective
不明智的，欠考虑的
not sensible or wise; unwise
It was an ill-advised decision to quit your job. 辞掉你的工作是不明智的决定。
Making such a comment was ill-advised.  发表这样的评论是不明智的。
近义词: unwise, foolish, imprudent, rash
反义词: wise, prudent, judicious
词性: adjective

词源：
"ill-" (Old English "yfele," meaning "badly, wickedly") + "advised" (from the verb "advise," ultimately from Latin "ad-" (to) + "visum" (seen)).  Therefore, "ill-advised" literally means "badly seen" or "badly considered."
