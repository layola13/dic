eavesdrop: /ˈiːvzdrɒp/ (UK) /ˈiːvzdrɑːp/ (US)| v. | 偷听; 窃听 |To secretly listen to a conversation.|  She eavesdropped on their conversation from behind the curtain. 她躲在窗帘后偷听他们的谈话。

He was eavesdropping on his parents' argument. 他在偷听父母的争吵。

| 近义词：listen in, overhear, snoop | 反义词: ignore, disregard |词性：动词

词源：
From Middle English *evesdroppen, from eve + drop.  The original meaning referred to someone who stood under the eaves of a house to listen to conversations inside.  “Eaves”指的是屋檐，“drop”指的是从屋檐滴落的水滴。最初的含义是指站在屋檐下偷听屋内谈话的人。
