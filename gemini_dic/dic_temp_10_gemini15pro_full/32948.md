chopping:/ˈtʃɒpɪŋ/ (US) /ˈtʃɑːpɪŋ/ (UK)| n. v. adj.|切碎;砍;剁碎|The act of cutting something into small pieces, typically with a sharp tool like a knife or axe.  It can also refer to the result of this action (the chopped pieces). As an adjective, it describes something related to or involving chopping.|
例句：She was chopping vegetables for dinner. 她正在切蔬菜准备晚餐。

例句：The chopping board was covered in onions. 砧板上满是洋葱。

近义词：dicing, mincing, cutting, hacking, hewing|
反义词:joining, connecting, uniting|
词性: noun, verb, adjective (present participle)

chopping:/ˈtʃɒpɪŋ/ (US) /ˈtʃɑːpɪŋ/ (UK)| n. v. adj.|汹涌的;波涛起伏的|Describes a rough sea with short, breaking waves.|
例句：The boat struggled in the chopping waves.  小船在汹涌的波浪中挣扎。

例句：The chopping sea made me feel seasick. 波涛汹涌的大海让我感到晕船。

近义词：rough, turbulent, choppy|
反义词:calm, smooth, tranquil|
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *choppen*, from Old English *ceapian* ("to buy, bargain, exchange, trade").  The meaning shifted to "cut, strike with a blow" around the 14th century.
