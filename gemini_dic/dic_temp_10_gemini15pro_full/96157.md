indentureship:/ɪnˈdɛntʃəʃɪp/ | n. | 包身工制度；契约劳工身份 | A system of bonded labor, where a person agrees to work for a particular employer for a fixed period of time in exchange for passage to a new country or other benefits.  This system was often exploitative. |  Example 1:  Indentureship was a common practice in the 19th century, especially for transporting laborers from India and China to other parts of the British Empire.  19世纪，包身工制度十分普遍，尤其是将劳工从印度和中国运送到大英帝国其他地区。

Example 2: Her grandfather arrived in Trinidad under an indentureship system.  她的祖父是以契约劳工的身份来到特立尼达的。| 近义词: bonded labor, servitude, contract labor | 反义词: freedom, liberty | 词性: 名词
