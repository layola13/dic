integrability:/ˌɪntɪˈɡreɪtəbɪləti/ | n. |可积性，可整合性 | The quality of being integrable; capable of being integrated.  This often refers to mathematical functions that can be integrated, or systems that can be combined or unified. | The integrability of this function is crucial for solving the equation. 这个函数的可积性对于解方程至关重要。 | synonyms: coherence, cohesiveness, compatibility, unification | antonyms: incompatibility, disunity, separateness | noun

integrable:/ˈɪntɪɡrəbl/ | adj. |可积的，可整合的 | Capable of being integrated, often used in a mathematical context to describe functions that can be integrated.  Can also mean capable of being incorporated into a larger whole. | This function is integrable over the given interval.  这个函数在给定的区间内是可积的。| synonyms: combinable, mergeable, unifiable | antonyms: non-integrable, indivisible | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *integrabilis, from Latin integratus (past participle of integrare "to make whole"), from integer "whole, complete".  The suffix -ability denotes the capability or quality of being.
