infusoria:/ˌɪnfjʊˈsɔːriə/ | n. | 微生物，草履虫 | Microscopic animals, especially ciliates and flagellates, found in infusions of decaying organic matter.  | Example 1:  He examined the infusoria under the microscope. 他在显微镜下检查微生物。

Example 2:  The pond water teemed with infusoria. 池水里充满了微生物。 | Near synonyms: protozoa, microorganisms, microbes | Antonym: N/A (Difficult to establish a direct opposite for a broad category of organisms) | Noun

infusion:/ɪnˈfjuːʒən/ | n. | 注入，灌输，浸泡 | The act of introducing a liquid into something else, or the liquid itself. Can also refer to introducing a quality or element into something else. | Example 1:  The infusion of new capital saved the company. 新资本的注入挽救了公司。

Example 2:  She prepared an infusion of herbs. 她准备了一壶草药茶。| Near synonyms: injection, introduction, instillation, tea, brew | Antonym: extraction, removal | Noun
