bloviate:/ˈbloʊvieɪt/| v. |夸夸其谈；高谈阔论|To speak or write verbosely and pompously.|He bloviated for hours about his supposed accomplishments. 他滔滔不绝地吹嘘着他所谓的成就。|例句：The politician bloviated about the economy. 这位政客夸夸其谈地谈论经济。|例句：She tends to bloviate on subjects she knows little about. 她倾向于在她不了解的科目上高谈阔论。|近义词：ramble, rant, orate, declaim|反义词: be concise, be succinct|词性:动词

bloviation:/ˌbloʊviˈeɪʃn/| n. |夸夸其谈；空洞的言辞|The act of speaking or writing verbosely and pompously.|His speech was full of bloviation and lacked substance. 他的演讲充满了夸夸其谈，缺乏实质内容。|例句：The audience grew tired of the speaker's endless bloviation. 听众对演讲者无休止的夸夸其谈感到厌倦。|例句：Her bloviation about her accomplishments was tiresome. 她对她成就的夸夸其谈令人厌烦。|近义词：verbosity, bombast, rhetoric|反义词: conciseness, brevity|词性:名词
