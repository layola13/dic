half-truth:/ˌhɑːfˈtruːθ/
n.
半真半假;部分真相
A statement that conveys only part of the truth, especially one used deliberately in order to deceive.
He told a half-truth about his past. 他讲述了他过去的部分真相，有所隐瞒。

The politician's speech was full of half-truths and misleading statements. 这位政客的演讲充满了半真半假和误导性的陈述。

近义词:lie, deception, prevarication, equivocation
反义词:truth, honesty, candor, whole truth
词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From half + truth.  First recorded in the early 19th century.
