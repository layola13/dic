pamphleteers:/ˌpæmfləˈtɪərz/
noun
小册子作者；宣传册作者
Writers or distributors of pamphlets, often for political or religious purposes.
Seventeenth-century pamphleteers were a powerful force in shaping public opinion. 十七世纪的小册子作者是塑造公众舆论的一股强大力量。
synonyms:propagandists, publicists, agitators
antonyms:censors, suppressors
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From pamphlet + -eer.  Pamphlet comes from the name of a 12th-century Latin love poem, Pamphilus, seu de Amore, which became widely copied and circulated.  The -eer suffix denotes a person who engages in or deals with something.
