overhearing:/ˌoʊvərˈhɪərɪŋ/| n. |偷听，无意中听到|The act of hearing something unintentionally, especially a conversation.|例句：I was in the cafe overhearing a conversation between two women. 我在咖啡馆无意中听到两个女人之间的谈话。|例句：My overhearing of their conversation gave me a valuable clue. 我无意中听到他们的谈话，获得了一个宝贵的线索。|近义词：eavesdropping, listening in|反义词: ignoring, not paying attention|词性: noun

overhear:/ˌoʊvərˈhɪr/| v. |偷听，无意中听到|To hear (something) without the speaker's intention or knowledge.|例句：I overheard them talking about the surprise party. 我无意中听到他们在谈论惊喜派对。|例句：She overheard her name mentioned in the next room. 她在隔壁房间听到有人提到她的名字。|近义词：eavesdrop, catch|反义词: miss, ignore|词性: verb

词源: From Middle English *overheren, from Old English oferhīeran (“to overhear”), from ofer- (“over”) + hīeran (“to hear”).
