cast-off:/ˈkɑːst ɒf/ (US) /ˈkɑːst ɔːf/ (UK)| adj. n.|丢弃的，抛弃的；被遗弃的人/物|Something that has been discarded or rejected.|She gave her cast-off clothes to charity. 她把不要的衣服捐给了慈善机构。|近义词：discarded, rejected, abandoned|反义词：cherished, valued|adjective

cast-off:/ˈkɑːst ɒf/ (US) /ˈkɑːst ɔːf/ (UK)| n.|丢弃物，被遗弃的人/物|A person or thing that has been discarded or rejected.|The streets were filled with cast-offs from society. 街上到处都是被社会遗弃的人。|近义词：outcast, reject|反义词：keeper|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb phrase "cast off," meaning to throw off or discard.  "Cast" comes from Old Norse *kasta, meaning "to throw."
