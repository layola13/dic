bredenwoods /ˈbrɛdənˌwʊdz/ |  n. (proper noun) | 布雷顿森林 |  Refers to the Bretton Woods system, established in 1944 at the United Nations Monetary and Financial Conference held in Bretton Woods, New Hampshire, USA. This system established the rules for commercial and financial relations among the world's major industrial states in the mid-20th century. It created the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD), now part of the World Bank. |  The Bretton Woods agreement pegged currencies to the US dollar, which was in turn pegged to gold. | 近义词: Bretton Woods system, Bretton Woods Agreement | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but contrasting concepts could include "floating exchange rates," "gold standard") | 词性: 名词
