compulsion:/kəmˈpʌlʃən/| n.|强迫，冲动|An irresistible urge to behave in a certain way, especially against one's conscious wishes.|He felt a compulsion to tell her everything. 他感到一种不得不告诉她一切的冲动。|synonyms: urge, impulse, drive, obsession, need|antonyms: aversion, reluctance|noun

compulsive:/kəmˈpʌlsɪv/| adj.|强迫性的，难以抑制的|Resulting from or relating to an irresistible urge, especially one that is against one's conscious wishes.|Compulsive gambling can ruin lives. 赌博成瘾会毁掉生活。|synonyms: obsessive, uncontrollable, addictive, involuntary|antonyms: controlled, voluntary|adjective

compulsively:/kəmˈpʌlsɪvli/| adv.|强迫地，不由自主地|In a compulsive manner.|He compulsively checks his phone every few minutes. 他每隔几分钟就会不由自主地查看手机。|synonyms: obsessively, uncontrollably, involuntarily|antonyms: controlledly, voluntarily|adverb

compel:/kəmˈpɛl/| vt.|强迫，迫使|Force or oblige (someone) to do something.|The law compels employers to provide health insurance. 法律强制雇主提供健康保险。|synonyms: force, oblige, require, coerce, pressure|antonyms: dissuade, discourage|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin compulsiō, from Latin compellere "to drive together, force," from com- "together" + pellere "to drive."
