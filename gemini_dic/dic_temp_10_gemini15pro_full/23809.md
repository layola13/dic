brazen:/ˈbreɪzən/| adj.| 厚颜无耻的，肆无忌惮的；黄铜色的，黄铜制的 | made of brass; bold and without shame. | He has a brazen disregard for the rules.  他厚颜无耻地无视规则。| Synonyms: bold, audacious, impudent, shameless, brassy | Antonyms: shy, timid, modest, humble | adjective


brazens:/ˈbreɪzənz/| v. (third-person singular simple present brazens, present participle brazening, simple past and past participle brazened)| 厚颜无耻地做（某事），公然做（某事）| to face or undergo something with bold self-assurance | He brazened it out, pretending he hadn't heard the insults. 他装作没听见那些侮辱，厚着脸皮挺了过去。 | Synonyms: defy, face out, confront, withstand | Antonyms: cower, recoil, shrink from | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English brasen, from Old English bræsen “made of brass,” from bræs “brass,” from Proto-Germanic *brasa-, perhaps of Celtic origin. The figurative sense of "impudent" arose from the association of brass with loudness and ostentation.
