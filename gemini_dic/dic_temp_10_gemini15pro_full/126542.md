nacker:/ˈnækə(r)/| n.| 废马；劣马；老马；骗子；不老实的人|A horse, especially one that is old or in poor condition, that is used for ordinary riding or work. Also, a dishonest person or a cheat.|He bought a nacker for a few pounds. 他花了几英镑买了一匹老马。|近义词：nag, hack, rogue, swindler|反义词: thoroughbred, honest person|词性:名词

nacker:/ˈnækə(r)/| n.|屠马业者|Someone whose business is slaughtering horses, or an establishment where horses are slaughtered.|The farmer sent the old mare to the knackers.  农民把老母马送到了屠宰场。|近义词:  horse slaughterer|反义词:  None (This is a specific occupation)|词性:名词

词源：
From the Middle English *knakare*, meaning "old horse dealer," likely related to the verb *knacken*, "to kill or crack." This eventually evolved to include the slaughtering of horses as well.
