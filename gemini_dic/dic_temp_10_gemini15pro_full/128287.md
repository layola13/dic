neuropteran:/ˌnjʊərˈɒptərən/ | adj. n. |神经翅目的，脉翅目昆虫|Relating to or denoting insects of the order Neuroptera, which includes lacewings, antlions, and mantisflies.  Characterized by membranous wings with a network of veins. Also used as a noun to refer to an insect of this order. |例句：The delicate neuropteran wings shimmered in the sunlight. 纤细的脉翅目昆虫翅膀在阳光下闪闪发光。

例句：The entomologist studied the life cycle of a specific neuropteran. 昆虫学家研究了一种特定脉翅目昆虫的生命周期。|近义词：lacewing, antlion, mantisfly (when referring to specific types), net-winged insect |反义词: N/A |词性: Adjective, Noun

neuropteral: /ˌnjʊərˈɒptərəl/ | adj. |神经翅目的|Of or relating to the order Neuroptera; having net-veined wings. |例句：The neuropteral wings were intricately patterned.  脉翅目昆虫的翅膀图案复杂精巧。

例句：He identified the insect as neuropteral due to its distinctive wing venation. 他根据其独特的翅脉，将这种昆虫鉴定为脉翅目。|近义词：neuropteran, net-winged |反义词: N/A |词性: Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From neuro- (nerve) + -ptera (wing), referring to the network of veins resembling nerves in their wings. Ultimately from Greek neuron (nerve) + pteron (wing).
