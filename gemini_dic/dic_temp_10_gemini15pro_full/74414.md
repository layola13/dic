franchise:/ˈfræntʃaɪz/ | n. v. |特许经营权；特许经营；公民选举权；赋予特权 |  A right or privilege granted by a government or other authority to a company or individual.  | McDonald's is a large franchise. 麦当劳是一个大型特许经营企业。 |  He franchised his business to several investors. 他将自己的生意特许经营给了几个投资者。 | synonyms: license, permit, charter, authorization, concession | antonyms: prohibition, restriction, ban | noun, verb

franchise:/ˈfræntʃaɪz/ | n. |公民选举权 | The right to vote in public, political elections. |  Women fought for franchise in the early 20th century.  女性在20世纪初为争取选举权而斗争。 | synonyms: suffrage, right to vote | antonyms: disenfranchisement | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "franchir" (to free), derived from Frankish "frank" (free).  The original sense was of freedom or exemption from servitude.
