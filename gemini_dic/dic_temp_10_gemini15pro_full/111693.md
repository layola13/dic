lockin's:/ˈlɒkɪnz/ | n. |锁定；锁定效应；锁定效应的(复数)| The process of becoming locked in to a particular product or technology, often because of its widespread use or compatibility with other products or technologies.  It can also refer to events or situations where people are "locked in," unable to easily change or leave. |  We suffered from the lockin's of the old software system. 我们受到了旧软件系统锁定效应的影响。| 近义词：limitations, restrictions, constraints | 反义词: freedom, flexibility | 词性: 名词


lock-in:/ˈlɒkɪn/ | n. |锁定；锁定效应| The process of becoming locked in to a particular product or technology, often because of its widespread use or compatibility with other products or technologies.  |  The company's lock-in strategy prevented customers from switching to competitors. 该公司的锁定策略阻止了客户转向竞争对手。| 近义词：monopoly, tie-in, vendor lock-in | 反义词: open market, competition | 词性: 名词

词源：From the verb "lock in," meaning to secure something or someone in place, often figuratively.  It gained prominence in the context of technology and economics to describe situations where switching costs create inertia.
