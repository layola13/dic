hobnob:/ˈhɒbnɒb/ (British) /ˈhɑːbnɑːb/ (American)| vi.| 交往，亲密交谈，(与….)过从甚密；(与要人)厮混|To socialize with someone, especially someone important or famous. |例句：She hobnobs with royalty. 她与皇室成员交往。|例句：He likes to hobnob with celebrities at parties. 他喜欢在派对上与名人交往。|近义词：socialize, mingle, fraternize, associate |反义词: shun, avoid, isolate |动词

hobnobs:/ˈhɒbnɒbz/ (British) /ˈhɑːbnɑːbz/ (American)| n.| 一种甜饼干 (通常指燕麦消化饼)|A type of sweet biscuit, usually an oat-based digestive biscuit. |例句：We had tea and hobnobs. 我们喝了茶，吃了燕麦饼干。 |例句：She offered me a plate of hobnobs. 她给了我一盘燕麦饼干。|近义词：biscuits, cookies, crackers|反义词: N/A |名词
