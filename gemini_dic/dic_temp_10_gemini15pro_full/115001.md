mandarins:/ˈmændərɪnz/ | n. | 官员；柑橘；普通话 |  A high-ranking official in the Chinese Empire.  A small, sweet type of orange. The standard official language of China. |  The mandarins lived in luxury. (官员们过着奢侈的生活。) These mandarins are very juicy. (这些柑橘很多汁。) She speaks fluent Mandarin. (她说一口流利的普通话。) | 近义词: officials (官员), tangerines (柑橘), Putonghua (普通话) | 反义词:  (对于官员) commoners (平民), (对于语言) dialects (方言) | 词性: 名词

mandarin:/ˈmændərɪn/ | adj. | 官僚的；中国的 | Relating to or characteristic of a mandarin. | The mandarin dialect is spoken in Beijing. (北京人说的是官话。) He had a mandarin attitude towards the common people. (他对老百姓的态度很官僚。) | 近义词: bureaucratic (官僚的), Chinese (中国的) | 反义词:  democratic (民主的), foreign (外国的) | 词性: 形容词
