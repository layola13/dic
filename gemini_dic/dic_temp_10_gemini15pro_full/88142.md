hilarity:/hɪˈlærəti/ | n. |欢闹，高兴，热闹|Great merriment or amusement. |The party was filled with hilarity and laughter.  派对上充满了欢声笑语。| Synonyms: mirth, glee, joviality, amusement, laughter | Antonyms: gloom, sadness, seriousness, solemnity | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hilaritas, from Latin hilaris (“cheerful”), from Proto-Italic *hilaṛos, possibly from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰel- (“to shine, gleam; green, yellow”).
