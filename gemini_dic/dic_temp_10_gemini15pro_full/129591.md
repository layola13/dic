non-committalism:/nɒn.kəˈmɪt.əl.ɪ.zəm/ (US) /nɑːn.kəˈmɪt.əl.ɪ.zəm/| n.|不表态，不承诺|The state of being non-committal; unwillingness to commit oneself to a particular opinion or course of action.|His non-committalism on the issue frustrated his colleagues. 他在这个问题上的不表态让他的同事们很沮丧。|近义词：indecision, reluctance, ambiguity, evasiveness|反义词：commitment, decisiveness, directness|词性:名词

non-committal:/nɒn.kəˈmɪt.əl/ (US) /nɑːn.kəˈmɪt.əl/| adj.|不表态的，不承诺的|Not expressing or implying a definite opinion or commitment.|She gave a non-committal answer to the question. 她对这个问题给出了一个不置可否的回答。|近义词：evasive, ambiguous, indecisive, guarded|反义词：committed, decisive, definite, straightforward|词性:形容词

词源:  non- + committal + -ism.  "Committal" comes from the verb "commit," ultimately from Latin "committere" (to join together, entrust).  The prefix "non-" negates the meaning. The suffix "-ism" forms a noun indicating a doctrine, belief, or practice.
