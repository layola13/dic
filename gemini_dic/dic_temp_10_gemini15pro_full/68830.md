fasciole:/ˈfæʃiˌoʊl/| n.|小带，（吸虫类的）吸盘|A narrow band or stripe, especially one of the bands of color on a shell or the stripe on a military decoration;  In zoology, specifically refers to a small band, bundle, or group of nerve fibers, or a small sucker-like organ in certain invertebrates, especially flukes.|The snail is an intermediate host for the liver fluke, which attaches by its fasciole to the snail's tissue.|Synonym: band, stripe, stria|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps 'whole' in the context of it being a part)|noun


fasciole:/ˈfæʃiˌoʊl/| n.|（吸虫）吸附器官|A small sucker-like organ in certain invertebrates, especially flukes, used for attachment.|The fasciole helps the fluke anchor itself within its host.|Synonym: sucker,  adhesive organ|Antonym: (No direct antonym exists, perhaps 'release mechanism')|noun
