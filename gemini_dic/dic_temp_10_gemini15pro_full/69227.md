fears:/fɪərz/| n.|恐惧，害怕，担忧；担心的事；|She has a fear of spiders. 她很怕蜘蛛。|近义词：worries, anxieties, apprehensions, concerns|反义词：confidence, courage, bravery|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *feres*, plural of *fere* (“fear, terror, danger, sudden attack, peril”), from Old English *fǣr* (“sudden danger, peril, sudden attack, fear, fright, terror”), from Proto-Germanic *fērą* (“danger, sudden attack”), from Proto-Indo-European *pēr-*, *per-*. Cognate with West Frisian *fear* (“danger”), Dutch *vaar* (“danger, peril”), German *Gefahr* (“danger”), Danish *fare* (“danger”), Swedish *fara* (“danger, peril, to travel”), Icelandic *fari* (“danger, difficulty; epidemic, disease”). Related also to Old English *faran* (“to travel, go, fare”).
