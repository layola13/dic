externalism: /ɪkˈstɜːnəlɪzəm/ | n. | 外在主义 |  A philosophical theory (especially in the philosophy of mind) that holds that factors external to the thinking subject (e.g., the environment, the body, or other minds) play a significant role in the determination of the content of mental states and the meaning of utterances. |  Externalism suggests that the meaning of 'water' is partly determined by the actual stuff in the world, H2O, not just by what's in the speaker's head. | 近义词: environmentalism (in some contexts) | 反义词: internalism | 词性: noun
