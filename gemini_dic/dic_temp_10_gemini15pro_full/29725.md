cation:/ˈkætˌaɪən/| n.|阳离子|A positively charged ion, i.e., one that would be attracted to the cathode in electrolysis.|The sodium cation (Na+) plays a crucial role in nerve function.|近义词:positive ion|反义词:anion|词性:noun

Etymology: From French cation, from Greek kata- ‘down’ + ienai ‘to go’.
