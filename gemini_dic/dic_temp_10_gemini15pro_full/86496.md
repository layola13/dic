heinousnesses:/ˈheɪnəsnəsɪz/
n.
极恶劣的行为；滔天罪行；罪大恶极
The extreme wickedness or egregiousness of something.  The plural form of heinousness.
The heinousnesses of the war crimes shocked the world. 战争罪行的滔天罪行震惊了世界。
近义词: atrocities, abominations, enormities, wickednesses, evils
反义词: virtues, goodnesses, righteousnesses
词性: 名词

heinousness:/ˈheɪnəsnəs/
n.
极恶；凶恶；滔天大罪
The quality of being heinous; extreme wickedness or egregiousness.
The heinousness of the crime warranted a severe punishment.  这起罪行的滔天罪行应该受到严厉的惩罚。
近义词: atrocity, abomination, enormity, wickedness, evil
反义词: virtue, goodness, righteousness
词性: 名词

heinous:/ˈheɪnəs/
adj.
极恶的；凶恶的；可憎的
(Of a person or wrongful act, especially a crime) utterly odious or wicked.
The judge described the murder as a heinous crime. 法官形容这起谋杀案是滔天罪行。
近义词: atrocious, abominable, egregious, wicked, evil, monstrous, odious, shocking, appalling
反义词: virtuous, good, righteous, admirable, commendable
词性: 形容词

词源:
From Middle English *heinous, *haynous, from Anglo-Norman and Old French haïnos (“hateful, odious”), from hair (“to hate”), from Frankish *hatjan, from Proto-Germanic *hatjaną (“to hate”), from Proto-Indo-European *ked- (“sorrow, anxiety, hate”).  Cognate with Old English hettan (“to pursue, persecute; hate”), Dutch haten (“to hate”), German hassen (“to hate”), Old Norse hata (“to hate”), Gothic  ненавижу (nenavižu, “I hate”).
