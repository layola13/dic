anesthesia: /ˌænəsˈθiːʒə/ (US) /ˌænəsˈθiːziə/ (UK)| n.|麻醉|A loss of feeling or awareness, especially so that painful medical procedures can be carried out.|The patient was given general anesthesia before the surgery. 病人在手术前进行了全身麻醉。|Synonyms: numbness, insensibility|Antonyms: sensation, consciousness|noun

anesthetic: /ˌænəsˈθetɪk/ (US) /ˌænəsˈθetɪk/ (UK)| n. adj.|麻醉剂; 麻醉的|A substance that causes anesthesia.|The dentist used a local anesthetic to numb the patient's gums. 牙医使用了局部麻醉剂来麻痹病人的牙龈.  |The anesthetic wore off quickly. 麻醉剂很快就失效了。|Synonyms: narcotic, opiate (n.)|Antonyms: stimulant (n.), stimulating (adj.)|noun, adjective
