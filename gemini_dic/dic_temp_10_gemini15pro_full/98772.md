intangible:/ɪnˈtændʒəbl/ | adj. n. |无形的，难以捉摸的；无形资产|Something that is intangible cannot be touched or grasped physically, but it is still real and can have an effect.  |The company's intangible assets, such as its brand recognition and customer loyalty, are invaluable.  这家公司的无形资产，例如品牌认知度和客户忠诚度，是非常宝贵的。|Synonyms: impalpable, abstract, ethereal |Antonyms: tangible, concrete, material|adjective
intangible:/ɪnˈtændʒəbl/ | n. |无形资产|An intangible asset is something valuable that a company or organization has that cannot be touched or grasped physically, such as a brand reputation or a patent.  |Goodwill is an important intangible for many businesses. 商誉对许多企业来说是一项重要的无形资产。 |Synonyms: non-physical asset |Antonyms: tangible asset|noun
