bowdlerism:/ˈboʊdlərɪzəm/| n. |删节；净化；(为避免引起反感而对书籍、剧本等的)删改；|An expurgated version of a text, often intended to make it more suitable for children or to remove content considered offensive. The removal of offensive passages from a work of literature.|The editor's bowdlerisms obscured the author's original intent.|近义词：censorship, expurgation, purification|反义词:unabridged, unexpurgated|词性:noun

bowdlerize:/ˈboʊdləˌraɪz/| vt. |删节；净化；(为避免引起反感而对书籍、剧本等的)删改；|To remove or change parts considered vulgar or immoral from (a play, novel, etc.). To expurgate a text, often by removing passages considered offensive.|The publisher bowdlerized the novel before releasing it to a younger audience.|近义词：censor, expurgate, sanitize|反义词:publish unabridged|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Thomas Bowdler (1754–1825), a physician who published an expurgated edition of Shakespeare.
