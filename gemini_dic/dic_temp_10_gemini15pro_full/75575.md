fuddled:/ˈfʌdld/| adj.|困惑的，糊涂的|Confused and unable to think clearly, usually because of alcohol.|After three glasses of wine, I felt completely fuddled.|近义词：confused, bewildered, disoriented, muddled, tipsy, intoxicated|反义词:sober, clear-headed|词性:形容词
Etymology: From the verb "fuddle," which originated in the 16th century.  It's likely a frequentative form of an earlier verb "fud," meaning "to cheat or deceive," possibly related to "fond," meaning "foolish."  The sense of being mentally confused likely developed from the idea of being tricked or deceived by alcohol.
