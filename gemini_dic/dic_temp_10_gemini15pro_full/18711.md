bereavement:/bɪˈriːvmənt/| n.|丧失亲友，丧亲之痛|The death of her husband left her in a state of deep bereavement. 她丈夫的去世使她陷入深深的悲痛之中。|例句：

Her recent bereavement has been very difficult for her.
她最近的丧亲之痛让她很难熬。

He offered his condolences on my bereavement.
他对我的丧亲之痛表示慰问。

|近义词：grief, mourning, loss, sorrow|反义词:joy, happiness|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *berēafian, from be- + rēafian “to rob, deprive,” from Proto-Germanic *raubōną (“to rob”).  Cognate with Dutch beroven (“to rob”), German berauben (“to rob, deprive”). The sense shifted from the act of robbing to the state of being deprived, specifically of a loved one through death.
