hepatization:/hɛpətɪˈzeɪʃən/ |n.|肝化|The process by which tissue, especially lung tissue, becomes liver-like in consistency. This typically occurs in pneumonia, where the air sacs fill with inflammatory exudate.|The doctor noted hepatization of the patient's lung, indicating pneumonia.|近义词: consolidation|反义词: resolution|词性:名词

hepatize:/ˈhɛpəˌtaɪz/ |v.|肝化|To cause or undergo hepatization, making tissue resemble liver in consistency.|The pneumonia hepatized the affected lung tissue. |近义词: solidify, congest|反义词: resolve, clear|词性:动词
