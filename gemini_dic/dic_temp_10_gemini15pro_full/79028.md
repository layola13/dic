give-a-darn: /ɡɪv ə dɑːrn/ | v. | 关心，在意 | To care about something; to be concerned or bothered. | I don't give a darn what he thinks. 我不在乎他怎么想。|  I don't give a darn about the weather. 我一点也不在乎天气。 | 近义词: care, mind, be bothered, be concerned | 反义词: not care, be indifferent | 词性: verb

词源:  "Give a darn" is an American euphemism for "give a damn," which uses "darn" as a milder substitute for "damn." "Damn" ultimately comes from the Latin word "damnare," meaning "to condemn or curse."  The use of "darn" dates back to the early 19th century.
