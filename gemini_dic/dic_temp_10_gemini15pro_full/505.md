abolishable:/əˈbɒlɪʃəbl/
adjective
可废除的
Capable of being abolished; that may be done away with.
Slavery is an abolishable practice.  奴隶制是一种可以废除的做法。

Capital punishment is an abolishable penalty. 死刑是一种可以废除的刑罚。

近义词:
removable, eradicable, terminable

反义词:
irrevocable, unalterable, permanent

词性:
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From abolish + -able.  Abolish comes from Old French aboliss-, stem of abolir "to abolish," from Latin abolere "to destroy, do away with," from ab- "away" + olere "to grow" (related to adolere "to grow up").  The suffix -able is from Old French -able, from Latin -abilis.


USAGE->
This word is typically used in formal contexts.

Capital punishment is abolishable in many countries. = 在许多国家，死刑是可以废除的。
Many outdated laws are abolishable. = 许多过时的法律是可以废除的。

The practice of child labor should be abolishable everywhere. = 童工的做法应该在任何地方都被废除。
The committee decided that the old rule was abolishable. = 委员会决定旧规则可以废除。
