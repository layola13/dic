cryptozoon:/ˈkrɪptoʊˌzoʊɒn/ | n. | 隐生动物; 藻类化石 | An organism of uncertain taxonomic affinity, especially a Precambrian fossil thought to be a calcareous alga. |  Precambrian cryptozoons were initially interpreted as primitive multicellular animals.  前寒武纪隐生动物最初被解释为原始的多细胞动物。

| Example: The researchers discovered a new type of cryptozoon in the ancient rock formations. 研究人员在古老的岩层中发现了一种新的隐生动物。| Synonym: fossil algae, stromatolite | Antonym: N/A | noun
