bloated: /ˈbloʊtɪd/ (US) /ˈbləʊtɪd/ (UK) | adj. |肿胀的，膨胀的；傲慢的，自负的 |  Swollen with fluid or gas; excessively large or elaborate.  Figuratively, it can also mean inflated with pride or self-importance. |  My stomach felt bloated after the large meal.  我的肚子在大餐后感觉胀胀的。|  The company's bloated bureaucracy made decision-making slow and inefficient.  公司臃肿的官僚机构使得决策缓慢而低效。| synonyms: swollen, distended, inflated, puffed up | antonyms: deflated, shrunken, slim, thin | adjective


bloat: /bloʊt/ (US) /bləʊt/ (UK) | v. |（使）膨胀，肿胀 | To swell or puff up. | The dead fish bloated in the sun. 死鱼在阳光下膨胀了。 |  Eating too much salt can bloat you. 吃太多盐会让你肿胀。 | synonyms: swell, distend, inflate | antonyms: deflate, shrink | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bloten*, from Old Norse *blása* "to blow, inflate," related to Old English *blǣsan*.  The figurative sense of "inflated with pride" developed later.
