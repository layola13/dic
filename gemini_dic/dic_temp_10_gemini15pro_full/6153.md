amidin:/ˈæmɪdɪn/ | n. |脒| An organic compound containing the group -C(=NH)NH2.  A derivative of a carboxylic acid in which the hydroxyl group has been replaced by an amino group, and the oxo group has been replaced by an imino group.|Amidines are often used in organic synthesis as building blocks for other compounds. |近义词: imidamide |反义词:  (No direct antonym for a specific chemical compound) |词性:noun

Etymology: From French *amidine, from amide + -ine.  Ultimately from Latin *ammoniacus “of ammonia” (see ammonia) + -ide + -ine.
