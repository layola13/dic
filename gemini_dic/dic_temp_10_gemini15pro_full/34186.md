clamourer:/ˈklæmərər/ (US) /ˈklæmərə(r)/ (UK)| n. |喧闹者；大声疾呼者；强烈要求者|A person who clamours; someone who shouts loudly and persistently, especially in demand or protest.|The clamourers outside the government building demanded immediate action.  政府大楼外的抗议者要求立即采取行动。|Synonyms: protester, agitator, shouter, advocate, campaigner|Antonyms: quietist, conformist|noun

clamour:/ˈklæmər/ (US) /ˈklæmə(r)/ (UK) |n. vi. vt.|喧闹；吵闹；大声要求|A loud and persistent outcry from many people.|The clamour of the crowd grew louder as the team scored. 球队得分时，人群的欢呼声越来越大。|Synonyms: uproar, commotion, din, hubbub, outcry|Antonyms: silence, quiet, stillness|noun, verb intransitive, verb transitive
