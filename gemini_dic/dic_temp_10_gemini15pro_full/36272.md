cohabited:/koʊˈhæbɪtɪd/
verb
同居，姘居
lived together as or as if a married couple.
例句：They cohabited for five years before getting married. 他们在结婚前同居了五年。

例句：The study found that couples who cohabited before marriage had a higher divorce rate. 研究发现，婚前同居的夫妇离婚率更高。

近义词：lived together, shacked up
反义词：lived apart, separated
词性：v.


Etymology:
From Late Latin *cohabitatus, past participle of *cohabitare (“to dwell together”), from Latin co- (“together”) + habitare (“to dwell, inhabit”).
