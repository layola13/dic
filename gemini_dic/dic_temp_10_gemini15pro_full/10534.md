appositive:/əˈpɒzɪtɪv/
|n. adj.|同位语|A word or phrase that renames or further explains a noun or noun phrase it follows.|My best friend, Sarah, is a doctor. 我最好的朋友，莎拉，是个医生。|Synonyms: equivalent, restating, clarifying|Antonyms: N/A|noun
appositive:/əˈpɒzɪtɪv/
|adj.|同位|Placed in apposition; explanatory.|The appositive phrase "a doctor" further explains "Sarah". 同位语短语“一位医生”进一步解释了“莎拉”。|Synonyms: juxtaposed, accompanying, supplementary|Antonyms: N/A|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *appositivus*, from Latin appōsitus (“placed near, applied”), past participle of appōnō (“I place near, apply”), from ad (“to, near”) + pōnō (“I put, place”).
