climacteric: /klaɪˈmæk.tər.ɪk/ /klaɪˈmæk.tər.ɪk/
n.|更年期；转折点；关键时期|A period of significant change or transition, especially relating to the physiological changes associated with menopause in women or a similar period in men; a critical period or stage.|例句：She experienced mood swings during her climacteric.  她更年期时经历了情绪波动。

例句：The company is at a climacteric in its history. 这家公司正处于其历史上的一个转折点。|近义词：turning point, critical point, milestone, menopause (specifically for women)|反义词: None (lacks a direct opposite, depends on context)|词性: noun


climacteric: /klaɪˈmæk.tər.ɪk/ /klaɪˈmæk.tər.ɪk/
adj.|更年期的；转折点的；关键时期的|Relating to or characteristic of a climacteric; critically important.|例句：The climacteric changes in her body were noticeable. 她身体上的更年期变化很明显。

例句：This is a climacteric moment in the negotiations. 这是一个谈判的关键时刻。|近义词：critical, crucial, decisive, pivotal|反义词: unimportant, trivial|词性: adjective
