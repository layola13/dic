brownian:/ˈbraʊniən/| adj.|布朗的，布朗运动的|Relating to or exhibiting Brownian motion, the random movement of particles suspended in a fluid resulting from their collision with the fast-moving atoms or molecules in the fluid.|The Brownian motion of the dust particles in the sunlight was clearly visible.|近义词：random, erratic|反义词:stable, stationary|词性:形容词
Etymology: Named after Robert Brown, a Scottish botanist who first observed the phenomenon in 1827.  From Brown + -ian.
