lieber:/ˈliːbər/| adj.|亲爱的，喜爱的|Dear, beloved, preferred.  It is used as a term of endearment or to express preference.  It's the comparative form of "lieb" (dear, beloved) and thus means "dearer," "more beloved," or "more preferable."|例句：Lieber Freund,  亲爱的朋友 (Dear friend)|近义词：beloved, dear, preferred|反义词：hated, disliked|词性:形容词

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Ich trinke lieber Tee. = I prefer to drink tea. 我更喜欢喝茶。
Er liest lieber Bücher als Zeitungen. = He prefers reading books to newspapers. 他更喜欢看书而不是报纸。


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Ich esse gerade lieber Obst als Kuchen. = I am currently preferring fruit to cake. 我现在更喜欢吃水果而不是蛋糕。 (Note:  While grammatically correct, using "lieber" with present continuous is uncommon and may sound unnatural.)
Er spielt lieber draußen als drinnen. = He is preferring playing outdoors to indoors. 他更喜欢在户外玩耍而不是在室内。(Note:  While grammatically correct, using "lieber" with present continuous is uncommon and may sound unnatural.)

否定句:
Ich mag das nicht lieber. = I don't prefer that. 我不喜欢那个。 (This is less common.  Usually you would phrase it positively with the other option.)
Er will das nicht lieber tun. = He doesn't prefer to do that. 他不喜欢那样做。 (Again, less common; you would usually phrase it positively with the preferred option.)



PHRASE->
lieber Gott = dear God 天哪
mein lieber Scholli (or similar variations) = my dear friend 我亲爱的朋友 (informal, often used ironically)
lieber nicht = rather not 还是不要了
was ist dir lieber? = what do you prefer? 你更喜欢什么?
lieber so als gar nicht = better than nothing 总比没有好


注意事项:
"Lieber" is primarily used in German, not English. The examples provided above are German sentences with English translations to illustrate the usage of "lieber" in different tenses and contexts.

词源：From Old High German *liobiro, comparative form of *liob. Cognate with Dutch liever, English liefer.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *leubiz (“dear, beloved”).
