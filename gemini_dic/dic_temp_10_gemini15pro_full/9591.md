antisophist:/ˈæntiˈsɒfɪst (UK), ˈæntiˈsɑːfɪst (US)| n. |反智者; 反诡辩家|A person who opposes the views or methods of sophists.  Sophists were ancient Greek teachers of rhetoric and philosophy, often associated with using clever but misleading arguments. An antisophist would therefore be someone who valued truth and straightforward reasoning over rhetorical skill and manipulation.| Plato is often seen as an antisophist, criticizing the sophists' focus on rhetoric over truth. 柏拉图通常被视为反智者，批评智者注重修辞而不是真理。

The antisophist argued for the importance of genuine knowledge over persuasive speech.  这位反智者主张真正的知识比有说服力的演讲更重要。|近义词：critic of sophistry, opponent of sophists |反义词: sophist|词性: noun
