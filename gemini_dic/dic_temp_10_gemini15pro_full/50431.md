depravingly:/dɪˈpreɪvɪŋli/
| adv. |堕落地，腐败地|In a way that corrupts or perverts morally.|He spoke depravingly about the exploitation of women. 他堕落地谈论剥削女性。|Synonyms: corruptly, perversely, wickedly, sinfully|Antonyms: virtuously, righteously, morally|Adverb

Etymology:
From *deprave* + *-ing* + *-ly*.  *Deprave* comes from the Latin *dēpravāre* "to make crooked, distort," from *dē-* "from, away" + *pravus* "crooked, perverse."  *-ing* forms the present participle, and *-ly* is a suffix that forms adverbs.
