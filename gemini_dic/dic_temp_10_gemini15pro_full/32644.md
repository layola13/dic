chisel:/ˈtʃɪzəl/ (US) /ˈtʃɪzl/ (UK)| n. v.|凿子；雕刻；欺骗|A tool with a sharp, beveled edge used for cutting or shaping wood, stone, or metal.  As a verb, it means to cut or shape with a chisel, or to obtain something by trickery or deception.|例句：The sculptor used a chisel to shape the marble. 雕刻家用凿子塑造大理石。|近义词：gouge, carve, swindle, cheat|反义词: blunt,  honest|词性: noun, verb

chisels:/ˈtʃɪzəlz/ (US) /ˈtʃɪzlz/ (UK)| n.|凿子(复数)；|The plural form of chisel.|例句：He keeps a set of chisels in his workshop. 他在车间里放着一套凿子。|近义词：gouges|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French cisel, from Vulgar Latin *cisellum, diminutive of Latin caesum, past participle of caedere "to cut."  Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European root *kae-id- "to strike, chop."
