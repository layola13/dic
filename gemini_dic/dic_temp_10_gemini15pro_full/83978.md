half-critical:/ˈhæf krɪtɪkəl/
adjective
半临界的
Relating to a point or condition halfway to a critical point, especially in physics or engineering, often referring to a state where a system is halfway to becoming unstable or undergoing a significant change.
The reactor reached a half-critical state before the emergency shutdown was initiated.  反应堆在启动紧急关闭之前达到了半临界状态。
The system became half-critical, indicating a potential instability. 系统达到半临界状态，表明存在潜在的不稳定性。
近义词: semi-critical, approaching criticality
反义词: stable, subcritical
词性: adjective

critical:/ˈkrɪtɪkəl/
adjective
临界的；关键的；批判的；危急的
From Late Latin criticus (“critical”), from Ancient Greek κριτικός (kritikós, “of or for judging”), from κριτής (kritḗs, “judge”), from κρίνω (krínō, “I separate, distinguish, judge”).  源自晚期拉丁语 criticus（“批判的”），来自古希腊语 κριτικός (kritikós，“用于判断的”)，来自 κριτής (kritḗs，“法官”)，来自 κρίνω (krínō，“我分开，区分，判断”)。
The patient is in critical condition. 病人情况危急。
This is a critical moment in the negotiations. 这是谈判的关键时刻。
He wrote a critical essay on the novel. 他写了一篇关于这部小说的评论文章。
近义词: crucial, vital, essential, dangerous, serious, analytical, judgmental
反义词: unimportant, trivial, minor, stable, healthy, complimentary, uncritical
词性: adjective
