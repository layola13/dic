echo-location:/ˌekəʊloʊˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 回声定位 | The process of locating objects by emitting sounds and interpreting the reflected sounds.  | Bats use echo-location to navigate in the dark.  蝙蝠利用回声定位在黑暗中导航。 |  近义词: biosonar | 反义词:  (No direct antonym) | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From echo + location.  Echo from Latin ēchō, from Greek ἠχώ (ēkhṓ), "echo." Location from Old French locacion, from Latin locātiō, locātiōnem,  "a placing, position," noun of action from past-participle stem of locāre "to place," from locus "a place."
