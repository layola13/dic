parabolists:/ˈpærəbəlɪsts/| n.|寓言家；比喻者|A person who tells or writes parables.  Those who speak in parables often aim to teach moral or spiritual lessons through symbolic stories.|The parabolists of ancient Greece used stories to convey complex philosophical ideas.|近义词：storyteller, fabulist, moralist|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "liar" if the parables are intentionally deceptive) |noun
