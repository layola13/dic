leadon:/ˈliːdɒn/ (UK) /ˈliːdɑːn/ (US)| n.| 诱饵; 引诱物; 序曲; 开场白 |Something used to entice or lure someone into a particular course of action, often with harmful or deceptive intent; a prelude or introduction.| The con man used a leadon about a lucrative business opportunity to swindle investors. 骗子用一个关于有利可图的商业机会的诱饵来诈骗投资者. |近义词：bait, lure, enticement, decoy, come-on, inducement, preface, introduction, preamble |反义词: deterrent, discouragement |词性:名词

lead on:/ˌliːd ˈɒn/ (UK) /ˌliːd ˈɑːn/ (US)| phrasal verb |  欺骗; 误导;  (尤指在感情方面)给人以虚假的希望;  带领; 引领| To deceive or mislead someone, especially romantically, by giving them false hope; to guide or lead someone along a path or route.| She led him on for months, making him believe she loved him. 她欺骗了他好几个月，让他相信她爱他。| 近义词：deceive, mislead, string along, guide, escort, conduct|反义词: be honest, be truthful |词性:动词短语

词源:  "lead on" 作为动词短语源于动词 "lead" 的基本含义“引导，带领”。  "on"  则增加了持续和方向的含义。 作为名词的 "leadon" 则是从动词短语演变而来，指用于引诱或引导的事物。
