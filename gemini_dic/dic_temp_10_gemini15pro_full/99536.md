intergossiped:/ˌɪntərˈɡɒsɪpt/ | v. |互相八卦，交头接耳|To gossip amongst each other; to share gossip reciprocally.|They intergossiped about their neighbors all afternoon. 他们整个下午都在互相八卦邻居的事情。| The children intergossiped about the new teacher. 孩子们互相八卦着新老师的事。 |近义词：chatted, gossiped, whispered|反义词: remained silent, kept secrets|词性: 动词

词源:
"inter-" (prefix): between, among, mutually.  From Latin "inter" (between, among).
"gossip": idle talk or rumor, especially about the personal or private affairs of others. From Old English "godsibb" (sponsor at baptism, close friend), later meaning a person, especially a woman, who enjoys idle talk.
