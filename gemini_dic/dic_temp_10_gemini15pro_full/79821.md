glycosaminoglycan:/ˌɡlaɪkəʊsəˌmiːnəˈɡlaɪkæn/ /ˌɡlaɪkoʊsəˌmiːnoʊˈɡlaɪkæn/| n.|糖胺聚糖|A long unbranched polysaccharide consisting of a repeating disaccharide unit.  They are negatively charged and attract water molecules, forming a gel-like substance that provides hydration, lubrication, and structural support to tissues.|Hyaluronic acid is a glycosaminoglycan found in the synovial fluid of joints.|Synonym: mucopolysaccharide|Antonym: N/A|Noun
