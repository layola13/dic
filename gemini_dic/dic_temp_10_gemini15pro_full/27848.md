canonising: /ˈkænənaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 正式宣布（死者）为圣徒；推崇；高度赞扬 | The process of officially declaring someone a saint, especially by the Catholic Church; by extension, to treat someone or something as being very important or special. | The church is canonising the late Mother Teresa. 教会正在追封已故的特蕾莎修女为圣徒。 | 近义词：glorifying, exalting, deifying | 反义词：condemning, vilifying | 词性: 动词

Etymology: From Late Latin *canonizare, from Latin canon "rule, standard," from Greek kanōn "straight rod, rule."
