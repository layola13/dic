iconograph: /aɪˈkɒnəɡrɑːf/ (US) /aɪˈkɑːnəɡræf/
n.|圣像，肖像，图像；肖像研究；肖像学|A representation of a person, especially one in which the subject is idealized.  The study of the identification, description, and interpretation of the content of images. |The museum houses a fascinating collection of Byzantine iconographs.  这个博物馆收藏了引人入胜的拜占庭圣像。|Synonyms: icon, portrait, image, likeness |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "abstraction")|Noun

iconography: /ˌaɪkəˈnɒɡrəfi/ (US) /ˌaɪkəˈnɑːɡrəfi/
n.|肖像学；图像志；肖像画法|The study or interpretation of images and symbols, especially in art. The visual images and symbols used in a work of art or the study or interpretation of these. |The iconography of the painting suggests a religious narrative. 这幅画的图像志暗示了一个宗教故事。|Synonyms: symbolism, imagery |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *iconographia*, from Greek eikonographia "painting of portraits," from eikon "image" + -graphia "writing, description."
