paraphrases:/ˈpærəfreɪzɪz/| n. |释义；改述；意译|An expression of the meaning of something written or spoken using different words, especially to achieve greater clarity.|The teacher asked the students to paraphrase the poem in their own words. 老师要求学生用自己的话解释这首诗。|synonyms: rewording, rephrasing, restating, translation, rendition, version, interpretation, explanation, gloss, commentary|antonyms: quote, repeat, verbatim|noun

paraphrase:/ˈpærəfreɪz/| v. |释义；改述；意译|Express the meaning of (something written or spoken) using different words, especially to achieve greater clarity.|He paraphrased the poem in modern English. 他用现代英语改写了这首诗。|synonyms: reword, rephrase, restate, translate, render, interpret, explain, gloss, comment on|antonyms: quote, repeat|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *paraphrase*, from Late Latin *paraphrasis*, from Greek *paraphrasis* "a telling in other words," from *paraphrazein* "to tell in other words," from *para-* "beside" + *phrazein* "to tell, declare."
