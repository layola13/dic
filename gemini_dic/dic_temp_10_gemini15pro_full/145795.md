overdecadences /ˌoʊvərˈdɛkədənsɪz/
n.
过度颓废，极度衰败
Excessive decadence; a state of extreme decline or decay, often marked by moral or artistic corruption.
The historian lamented the overdecadences of the late Roman Empire.  这位历史学家对罗马帝国后期的极度颓废感到惋惜。
The overdecadences of the ruling class ultimately led to revolution. 统治阶级的过度颓废最终导致了革命。
近义词: degeneration, decline, decay, corruption
反义词:  revival, regeneration, flourishing, prosperity
词性: 名词 (Noun)


词源:
"Over-" (prefix) + "decadence" (noun)

"Over-"  means "excessive" or "too much."
"Decadence" comes from the French word "décadence," which in turn comes from the Latin word "decadentia," meaning "a falling away" or "decline."  It's ultimately derived from the Latin verb "cadere," meaning "to fall."
