depth-of-focus:/ˌdepθ əv ˈfoʊkəs/ | n. | 景深；焦深 | The extent to which the position of the image plane can be varied without loss of sharpness of image.  The distance between the nearest and farthest objects in a scene that appear acceptably sharp in an image. | The camera's depth of focus was shallow, blurring the background. 相机的景深很浅，使背景变得模糊。 | The photographer adjusted the aperture to control the depth-of-focus. 摄影师调整光圈来控制景深。 | Synonym: focal range, focus range | Antonym: - | noun
