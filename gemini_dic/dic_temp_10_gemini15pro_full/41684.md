corresponder:/ˌkɔːrɪˈspɑːndər/ /ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːndər/| n.|通讯员，记者，通信者|A person who communicates regularly with another, especially by letter or email; a journalist employed to report from a particular place or on a particular subject.|例句：The war correspondent sent regular dispatches from the front line. 战地记者定期从前线发回报道。

例句：She has been a foreign correspondent for many years. 她多年来一直担任驻外记者。|近义词：reporter, journalist, writer, communicator|反义词:recipient, receiver|词性:noun

corresponded:/ˌkɔːrɪˈspɑːndɪd/ /ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːndɪd/| v.|相符，一致，通信|Be similar or analogous; communicate by exchanging letters or emails.|例句：Her account of events corresponds with theirs. 她对事件的描述与他们的描述相符。

例句：I've been corresponding with a pen pal in France for years. 我和一位在法国的笔友通信多年了。|近义词：match, agree, concur, communicate, write|反义词:differ, disagree, clash|词性:verb

corresponding:/ˌkɔːrɪˈspɑːndɪŋ/ /ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːndɪŋ/| adj.|相应的，对应的|Analogous; similar in character, form, or function.|例句：The increase in sales corresponds to an increase in demand. 销售额的增长与需求的增长相对应。

例句：There was a corresponding increase in unemployment. 失业率也相应增加。|近义词：equivalent, matching, similar, analogous|反义词:different, dissimilar, contrasting|词性:adjective
