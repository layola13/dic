dissipativity:/ˌdɪsɪˈpeɪtɪvɪti/
n.
消散性，耗散性
The property of a system that tends to dissipate energy, typically as heat.  A system's inherent ability to disperse or scatter energy, often in the form of heat, and approach a state of equilibrium.
The dissipativity of the material makes it suitable for use in brakes.  这种材料的耗散性使其适用于制动器。
Synonyms: damping, attenuation, dissipation, diffusion
Antonyms:  accumulation, concentration,  preservation
noun
