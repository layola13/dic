half-deafened:/ˌhɑːf ˈdefənd/ /ˌhæf ˈdefənd/| adj.|半聋的，听觉部分受损的|Partially deafened; having one's hearing impaired or reduced.  Describes someone whose hearing has been significantly diminished, but not completely lost.  Often implies a temporary state caused by a loud noise.|After the rock concert, I was half-deafened for a few hours.  演唱会结束后，我几个小时都半聋了。|The explosion left him half-deafened and disoriented. 爆炸让他半聋了，而且迷失了方向。|Near-deaf, partially deaf|Hearing clearly, unimpaired hearing|adjective
