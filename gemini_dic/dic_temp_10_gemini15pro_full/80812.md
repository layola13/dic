gothism: /ˈɡɒθɪzəm/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːθɪzəm/ (US) | n. | 哥特主义，哥特式风格 | A style of architecture prevalent in western Europe in the 12th–16th centuries, characterized by pointed arches, rib vaults, and stained glass, or a style of literature characterized by a gloomy setting, grotesque or violent events, and an atmosphere of mystery and dread.  A modern subculture characterized by dark clothing, dark makeup, and an interest in Gothic literature, music, and art. |  例句：The cathedral is a stunning example of Gothicism. 这座大教堂是哥特式建筑的惊人典范。

例句：Her novels are steeped in Gothicism, with crumbling castles and mysterious figures. 她的小说充满了哥特主义，带有破败的城堡和神秘的人物。 | 近义词：Gothic architecture, Gothic style, Gothic literature | 反义词: Classicism, Romanticism | 词性: noun
