infeodation:/ɪnˌfiːˈdeɪʃn/ | n. | 封地授予；臣服；依附 | The act of granting land in exchange for feudal service; the state of being bound to a lord or superior; subservience. |  The infeodation of the peasants to the lord of the manor meant they were tied to the land and owed him service.  农民被封建授予给庄园主意味着他们被束缚在这片土地上并需要为他服役。| 近义词：enfeoffment, vassalage, subjection | 反义词：independence, autonomy | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Old French *infeodacion, from infeoder ("to enfeoff"), from Medieval Latin *infeudare, from in- ("in") + feudum ("fief").  最终源自日耳曼语。
