mischievously:/ˈmɪstʃɪvəsli/ | adv. | 顽皮地，淘气地 | In a way that shows a fondness for causing trouble in a playful or harmless way. |  He grinned mischievously and hid the cookie jar. 他顽皮地咧嘴一笑，藏起了饼干罐。| synonyms: playfully, naughtily, impishly, roguishly | antonyms: seriously, obediently, respectfully | word type: adverb

mischievous:/ˈmɪstʃɪvəs/ | adj. | 顽皮的，淘气的 | Showing a fondness for causing trouble in a playful or harmless way. | The mischievous child painted on the walls.  这个淘气的孩子在墙上画画。| synonyms: playful, naughty, impish, roguish | antonyms: serious, obedient, respectful | word type: adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *mischevous, borrowed from Old French meschevous (“unlucky, unfortunate, wicked”), from meschef (“misfortune, mishap”), from mescheoir (“to turn out badly, miscarry”), from mes- (“badly”) + cheoir (“to fall”), ultimately from Latin cadere (“to fall”).
