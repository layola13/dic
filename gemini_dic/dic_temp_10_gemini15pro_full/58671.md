dummier:/ˈdʌmiər/| adj.|更愚蠢的，更假的|Comparative adjective of "dummy".  Someone or something that is even more unintelligent or fake than something already considered a dummy.|This crash test dummy is dummier than the last one. 这个碰撞测试假人比上一个更蠢。|Near synonyms: stupider, faker, more artificial, more unintelligent|Opposite: smarter, more genuine, more intelligent|adjective

dummy:/ˈdʌmi/| n. adj.|假人，仿制品；愚蠢的人；假的；沉默的|Noun: A model of a human figure, often used for displaying clothes or in crash tests.  Also, an informal term for a stupid person. Adjective:  Fake or not real; unable or unwilling to speak.|Noun: The tailor pinned the dress to the dummy. 裁缝把衣服别在假人身上。|Noun: He called me a dummy after I failed the test. 我考试不及格后，他叫我笨蛋。|Adjective:  We used dummy data for the software test.  我们使用虚拟数据进行软件测试。|Adjective:  She remained dummy during the interrogation.  在审讯过程中，她保持沉默。|Near synonyms: (n.) mannequin, figure, imitation, idiot, fool; (adj.) fake, sham, imitation, silent, mute|Opposite: (n.) genius, expert; (adj.) real, genuine, talkative|noun, adjective
