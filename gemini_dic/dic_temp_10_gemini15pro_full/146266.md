overfactitious: /ˌoʊvərˌfækˈtɪʃəs/ | adj. | 过于矫揉造作的，过于人为的 |  Excessively artificial or contrived; not natural or spontaneous.  Characterized by an excessive display of artificiality, often to impress. | Her overfactitious laughter grated on everyone's nerves. 她矫揉造作的笑声让每个人都很不舒服。 |  The politician's overfactitious concern for the poor was clearly a ploy to win votes.  这位政客对穷人过于做作的关心显然是为了赢得选票。| 近义词：affected, artificial, contrived, feigned, phony, pretentious | 反义词：genuine, natural, sincere, spontaneous | 词性: 形容词

词源:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "factitious" (from Latin "facticius," meaning "made by art," from "facere," meaning "to make, do").
