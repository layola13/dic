adiantum: /ˌeɪdiˈæntəm/ | n. | 铁线蕨; 孔雀羊齿 | A genus of ferns, the maidenhair ferns, having delicate, often fan-shaped fronds and slender, wiry black or purplish stalks. | The adiantum in my garden thrives in the shade.  我的花园里的铁线蕨在阴凉处生长茂盛。

The delicate fronds of the adiantum unfurled in the spring rain. 春雨中，铁线蕨娇嫩的叶子舒展开来。 | 近义词: maidenhair fern | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *adiantum, from Greek *adianton, neuter of adiantos "unwetted," from a- "not" + diainein "to wet."  So called because the leaves repel water.
