manganite:/ˈmæŋɡəˌnaɪt/ | n. |锰矿；水锰矿|A black mineral consisting of hydrated manganese oxide, occurring typically in massive or crystalline form.  It is an ore of manganese. |Manganite is often found alongside other manganese oxides. 水锰矿经常与其他氧化锰一起被发现。|The miners discovered a rich vein of manganite. 矿工们发现了一条富含水锰矿的矿脉。|Synonyms:  pyrolusite, psilomelane, hausmannite|Antonyms: N/A (Minerals typically don't have direct antonyms.)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *manganese* + *-ite* (a suffix used in mineralogy to denote minerals, especially salts of acids).  Ultimately from Latin *magnes* (magnet) and Greek *lithos* (stone).
