nonterm:/ˈnɒntɜːrm/ | n. | 非终结符 |A symbol or expression in a formal grammar that can be replaced by other symbols or expressions according to the rules of the grammar.  It represents a syntactic category, such as a noun phrase or a verb phrase, rather than a specific word. | Example: In the grammar rule "S -> NP VP", S, NP, and VP are nonterminals. | Synonyms: syntactic variable, metavariable | Antonyms: terminal symbol | noun
