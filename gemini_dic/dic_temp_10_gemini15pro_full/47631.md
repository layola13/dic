debilitatingly:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪŋli/
adverb
使人衰弱地，使人虚弱地
in a way that greatly reduces someone's strength or ability
The illness was debilitatingly painful. 这种疾病带来的疼痛让人虚弱不堪。
Synonyms: weakening, enervating, crippling, incapacitating
Antonyms: strengthening, invigorating, empowering
词性: 副词


debilitate:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪt/
verb
使衰弱，使虚弱
to make someone or something physically weak
A stroke can debilitate a person for life. 中风会使一个人终身衰弱。
Synonyms: weaken, enfeeble, sap, disable
Antonyms: strengthen, invigorate, empower
词性: 动词


debility:/dɪˈbɪlɪti/
noun
衰弱，虚弱
physical weakness, especially as a result of illness
She suffered from a general debility after the operation. 手术后，她身体十分虚弱。
Synonyms: weakness, frailty, infirmity, feebleness
Antonyms: strength, vigor, robustness
词性: 名词


词源: From Latin *dēbilis* "weak" (from *dē* "from, away from" + *habilis* "able, apt, fit").
