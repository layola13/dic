gig:/ɡɪɡ/| n. |演出；临时工作；轻便双轮马车|A performance, especially of jazz or popular music; a job, especially one that is temporary or that pays by the performance, or a light two-wheeled carriage pulled by one horse.|例句：He landed a gig playing guitar in a local band. 他在一个当地乐队找到了一份弹吉他的演出工作。

|例句：She had a few gigs lined up for the summer. 她夏天安排了一些临时工作。|近义词：job, performance, concert, show|反义词: unemployment|词性:noun


gig:/ɡɪɡ/| v. |乘坐轻便双轮马车；用鱼叉刺|To travel in a gig; to spear fish with a gig.|例句：They gigged across the countryside in their horse-drawn carriage. 他们乘坐轻便马车穿越了乡村。

|例句：He used to gig frogs in the swamp. 他过去常在沼泽地里用鱼叉刺青蛙。 |近义词：spear, ride|反义词: - |词性:verb
