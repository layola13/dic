mistral:/ˈmɪstrəl, miˈstrɑːl/| n.|密史脱拉风；西北风|A strong, cold, dry, northerly wind that blows through the Rhône valley and southern France to the Mediterranean coast, especially in winter.|The mistral was whipping the waves into a frenzy. 密史脱拉风使海浪狂暴起来。|近义词：north wind, northerly|反义词: southerly|词性:noun
Etymology: From Provençal *mistral, from Latin magistralis (“of a master, principal”), from magister (“master, chief”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *meg₂- (“great”).
