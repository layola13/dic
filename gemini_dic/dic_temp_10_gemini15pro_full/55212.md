disserved:/ˌdɪsˈsɜːvd/
verb
中文翻译：损害，伤害，帮倒忙
英文解析：To do a disservice to someone or something means to harm or damage them, often unintentionally. It implies doing something that has a negative effect, despite perhaps having good intentions.
例句：
The media disserved the public by misrepresenting the facts.
媒体歪曲事实，损害了公众的利益。

By focusing only on the negative aspects, the report disserved the community it was meant to help.
这份报告只关注消极方面，帮倒忙了它本应帮助的社区。

近义词：harmed, damaged, undermined, impaired, hindered, hurt
反义词：helped, aided, assisted, supported, benefited, improved
词性：v.
