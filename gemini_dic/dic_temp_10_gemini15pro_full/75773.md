fulsomenesses:/ˈfʊlsəmˌnɛsɪz/ |n. | 丰满，过分，肉麻，油腻| The fulsomeness of his praise was embarrassing.  他过分的赞扬令人尴尬。|例句：The fulsomeness of the language in the love letter made her cringe.  情书中肉麻的语言让她感到畏缩。

The fulsomenesses of the politician's speeches turned many voters away. 这位政客演讲的油腻劲让许多选民反感。|近义词：excess, exuberance, lavishness, opulence, profusion, richness, superabundance|反义词：meagerness, scantiness, sparseness|词性: 名词(复数)

Etymology: From Middle English *fulsomeness, from fulsom + -ness.  Fulsom itself comes from Old English *fūlsum "cloying, disgusting, loathsome," from *fūl "foul" + -sum "-some."  The meaning shifted over time from "disgusting" to "excessive" and then to "overly flattering."
