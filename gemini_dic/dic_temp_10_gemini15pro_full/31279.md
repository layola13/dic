chapelled:/ˈtʃæpəld/
adjective
内设小教堂的；附有小教堂的
having a chapel or chapels
The grand house was chapelled, offering a place for private worship. 这座大宅邸内设有小教堂，提供私人礼拜的场所。
The hospital is chapelled for the convenience of patients and staff.  这家医院附设有小教堂，方便病人和工作人员。
近义词: consecrated, sacred
反义词: secular, unconsecrated
词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From chapel + -ed.  "Chapel" comes from Old North French "chapele," diminutive of "capele," meaning "short cloak, hood," referring to the cloak of St. Martin, a relic of which was kept in a sanctuary that became the model for chapels.  The "-ed" suffix forms adjectives from nouns, indicating "having, characterized by, or provided with."
