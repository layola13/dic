botching:/ˈbɒtʃɪŋ/ (US) /ˈbɑːtʃɪŋ/| v. | 笨拙地做；搞砸 | Spoiling something by doing it badly. | Example: He botched the repair job and now the computer is worse than before. 他把修理工作搞砸了，现在电脑比以前更糟了。 | Example:  She completely botched her audition and didn't get the part. 她完全搞砸了她的试镜，没有得到那个角色。 | Synonyms: bungling, messing up, ruining, spoiling | Antonyms: perfecting, accomplishing, succeeding | Verb

botch:/bɒtʃ/ (US) /bɑːtʃ/| n. | 拙劣的工作；失败 | A badly done piece of work; a mess. | Example: The whole project was a botch from beginning to end. 整个项目从头到尾都是一团糟。 | Example: The surgery was a botch and left him with permanent scars. 手术很失败，给他留下了永久的疤痕。 | Synonyms: mess, bungle, failure, disaster | Antonyms: success, triumph, masterpiece | Noun
