exhort:/ɪɡˈzɔːrt/ /ɛɡˈzɔːrt/ | v. | 劝告；勉励；规劝 | To strongly encourage or urge (someone) to do something. | The coach exhorted his team to play harder. 教练鼓励他的队员们更加努力地比赛。|  The general exhorted his troops to fight bravely. 将军激励他的部队英勇作战。 | synonyms: urge, encourage, advise, press, entreat | antonyms: discourage, dissuade, deter | verb

exhortation:/ˌɛɡzɔːrˈteɪʃən/ /ˌɛɡzərˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 劝告；忠告；勉励 | An act or instance of exhorting; urgent recommendation or advice. |  He delivered an exhortation to the graduates to work hard. 他向毕业生们发表了努力工作的劝勉。| The teacher's exhortation to study harder resonated with the students. 老师劝诫学生更加努力学习，学生们深有感触。 | synonyms: urging, encouragement, advice, counsel | antonyms: discouragement, dissuasion | noun
