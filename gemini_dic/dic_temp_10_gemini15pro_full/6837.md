anathema: /əˈnæθəmə/ | n. | 诅咒，令人厌恶的人或事 | something or someone that one vehemently dislikes. |  The very idea of a dress code is anathema to him.  他极其厌恶着装规范的想法。| 近义词: abomination, curse, bane, horror | 反义词: blessing, beloved | 词性: 名词

anathema: /əˈnæθəmə/ | n. |  革出教门，诅咒 | a formal curse by a pope or a council of the Church, excommunicating a person or denouncing a doctrine. |  In the Middle Ages, heretics were declared anathema. 在中世纪，异教徒会被革出教门。 | 近义词: excommunication, denunciation | 反义词: benediction | 词性: 名词


词源: Late Middle English: from ecclesiastical Latin, from Greek anathema ‘thing devoted’, later ‘thing accursed’, from anatithenai ‘set up, dedicate’.
