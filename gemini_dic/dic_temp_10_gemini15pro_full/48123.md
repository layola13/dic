decoction:/dɪˈkɒkʃən/ | n. | 汤剂；煎剂；熬制 |  A liquid medicine or extract obtained by boiling medicinal herbs, roots, or other materials in water. |  She made a decoction of chamomile flowers to soothe her nerves. 她用洋甘菊花熬制了一剂汤药来舒缓神经。|  The traditional decoction was believed to have healing properties.  人们相信这种传统的汤药具有疗效。| Synonyms: infusion, extract, brew | Antonyms:  N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English decoccion, from Old French decoction, from Latin decoctiō (“a boiling down”), from decoquere (“to boil down”), from de- (“down, away”) + coquere (“to cook, to boil”).
