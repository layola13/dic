cox:/kɒks/ (UK), /kɑːks/ (US)| n. |舵手；驾驶员|A person who steers a boat, especially a racing boat, or a coxswain.|The cox steered the boat skillfully through the rough waters. 舵手熟练地驾驶着船穿过汹涌的水域。|Synonyms: steersman, helmsman, coxswain|Antonyms: passenger|noun


cox:/kɒks/ (UK), /kɑːks/ (US)| v. |操舵；指挥（赛艇）|To steer a boat, especially a racing boat.|She coxed the university team to victory. 她指挥大学队取得了胜利。|Synonyms: steer, helm, pilot|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, consider "drift")|verb
