emulsification's:/ɪˌmʌlsɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/| n.|乳化（作用）|The process of making an emulsion, where two immiscible liquids (like oil and water) are mixed so that one is dispersed as small droplets in the other.|The emulsification's of the oil and water mixture was achieved by adding a surfactant.|近义词：mixing, blending, homogenization|反义词:separation, demulsification|noun

词源:  From Late Latin *emulsionem, accusative of *emulsio (“a milking out”), from emulgēre (“to milk out”), from ex- (“out”) + mulgēre (“to milk”).  The "-ification" suffix denotes the process of making or becoming something.
