cavesson:/ˈkævəsən/ | n. |  鼻革, 马笼头 | A type of noseband used on a horse's bridle, typically a flat strap fastened around the nose above the nostrils. | The cavesson helps to keep the horse's mouth closed and provides a point of attachment for training aids. |  The rider adjusted the cavesson before mounting the horse.  骑手上马前调整了鼻革。 | Synonyms: noseband,  lunge cavesson | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but a bit can be contrasted as the primary means of control in the horse's mouth) | noun

cavesson:/ˈkævəsən/ | n. | 马笼头 | A part of a bridle that fits over the horse's nose. | The cavesson was too tight and the horse was uncomfortable. | 马笼头太紧了，马很不舒服。 | Synonyms: headstall, bridle | Antonyms: (No direct antonym as it's a piece of equipment) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *caveçon*, diminutive of *caveça*, from Old Occitan *cabesalha*, from Latin *capistrāle* "halter," from *capistrum* "halter," from *caput* "head."
