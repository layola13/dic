bigoted:/ˈbɪɡətɪd/| adj.|顽固不化的；心胸狭窄的；偏执的|Showing strong, unreasonable beliefs or opinions, and unwilling to change them; intolerant or prejudiced.|He made several bigoted remarks about immigrants. 他对移民发表了一些偏执的言论。|近义词：prejudiced, biased, intolerant, narrow-minded, dogmatic|反义词：tolerant, open-minded, unbiased, unprejudiced|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From bigot + -ed. Bigot originally referred to a religious hypocrite, especially a sanctimonious person.  Over time, the meaning broadened to encompass intolerance and prejudice in general.
