daguerreotype:/dəˈɡɛrəˌtaɪp/ |n.| 银版照相；银版相片 |A photograph taken by an early photographic process employing an iodine-sensitized silvered plate and mercury vapor.| Example: The museum displayed a collection of daguerreotypes from the 19th century.  博物馆展出了一系列19世纪的银版照片。| Synonym: photograph, picture, image | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, could consider "modern photograph" for contrast) | Noun

Etymology: Named after Louis-Jacques-Mandé Daguerre, the inventor of the process. From French *daguerréotype*.
