bezzle:/ˈbɛzəl/| n.| 侵吞，盗用（尤指小额公款）|The embezzlement of funds, especially in small amounts, often undetected at first, owing to the perpetrator's apparent trustworthiness.  It represents the amount by which one's assets appear greater than they actually are due to this undetected theft.|The cashier's bezzle went unnoticed for months.  那个收银员的盗用行为好几个月都没被发现。|近义词：embezzlement, pilfering, defalcation|反义词:reimbursement, restitution|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Coined by John Kenneth Galbraith in his 1952 book *American Capitalism: The Concept of Countervailing Power*.  He formed it by analogy with "embezzlement," suggesting the undetected portion of an embezzlement as the "bezzle."
