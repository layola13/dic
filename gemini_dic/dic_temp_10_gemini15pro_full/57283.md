downscale:/ˈdaʊnskeɪl/| v. adj.|缩小规模，降低档次|to reduce the size or extent of something, especially a business or operation; relating to or appealing to people at the lower end of the social or economic scale.|Example: The company downscaled its operations during the recession. 公司在经济衰退期间缩减了业务规模。|近义词：reduce, decrease, shrink, contract|反义词: upscale, expand, increase|词性：动词, 形容词


downscale:/ˈdaʊnskeɪl/| adj.|低档的，面向低收入人群的|relatively inexpensive or simple; designed for or suitable for people with less money.|Example: They live in a downscale neighborhood. 他们住在比较低档的社区。|近义词：budget, inexpensive, modest|反义词: upscale, luxury, premium|词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
"down-" (prefix indicating reduction or lowering) + "scale" (n. from Old Norse "skāl" - bowl, scale)
