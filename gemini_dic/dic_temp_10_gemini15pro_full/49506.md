demagog:/ˈdɛməɡɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈdɛməɡɑːɡ/ (US)| n.|煽动者，蛊惑民心的政客|A political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument.|The demagog stirred up the crowd with his fiery rhetoric.  煽动者用他激烈的言辞煽动了人群。|近义词：rabble-rouser, agitator, firebrand|反义词：statesman, conciliator|词性:noun

demagogue:/ˈdɛməɡɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈdɛməɡɑːɡ/ (US)| n.|煽动者，蛊惑民心的政客|(Same as demagog -  This is the more common spelling in American English)|History has shown us how dangerous demagogues can be. 历史告诉我们，煽动者是多么危险。|近义词：rabble-rouser, agitator, firebrand|反义词：statesman, conciliator|词性:noun

demagogs: /ˈdɛməɡɒɡz/ (UK) /ˈdɛməɡɑːɡz/ (US)| n. |煽动者（复数）|Plural of demagog/demagogue|The demagogs used fear and hatred to gain power. 这些煽动者利用恐惧和仇恨来获得权力。|近义词：rabble-rousers, agitators, firebrands|反义词：statesmen, conciliators|词性:noun


词源: From Ancient Greek dēmagōgós (δημαγωγός, “popular leader”), from dēmos (δῆμος, “people”) + agōgos (ἀγωγός, “leading, leader”).
