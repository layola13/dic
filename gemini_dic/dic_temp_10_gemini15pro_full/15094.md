bacchanalian: /ˌbækəˈneɪliən/ | adj. n. | 狂饮作乐的；酒神节的；狂欢者 | Characterized by or given to drunken revelry; relating to the festival of Bacchus. | The bacchanalian party lasted until dawn.  狂欢派对一直持续到黎明。|  The festival had a bacchanalian atmosphere.  节日气氛狂热喧闹。 | 近义词：orgiastic, drunken, riotous, debauched | 反义词：sober, restrained, temperate | 词性: adjective, noun

bacchanalian: /ˌbækəˈneɪliən/ | n. | 狂欢者 | A person who engages in drunken revelry. |  The bacchanalians danced wildly through the streets. 狂欢者在街上疯狂地跳舞。 | 近义词：reveler, carouser | 反义词：teetotaler, abstainer | 词性: noun


Etymology: From Latin *Bacchānalia*, neuter plural of *Bacchānālis* (“of Bacchus”), from *Bacchus*, the Roman god of wine. Ultimately from Greek Βάκχος (Bákkhos).
