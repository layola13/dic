hyperpersonal:/ˌhaɪpərˈpɜːrsənəl/ | adj. | 超个性化的，超个人化的 | Relating to online interactions that are more intense and intimate than those experienced in face-to-face settings, often due to factors like selective self-presentation and asynchronous communication. |  Online dating can sometimes lead to hyperpersonal communication where individuals reveal more about themselves than they would in person. 网上约会有时会导致超个性化的交流，个人会透露比他们本人更多关于自己的信息。

Online anonymity can foster hyperpersonal communication, allowing individuals to express themselves more freely. 网络匿名性可以促进超个性化的交流，允许个人更自由地表达自己。| 近义词: intimate, intensified | 反义词: impersonal, detached | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- (meaning "excessive" or "beyond") + personal.  The term was coined by psychologist Joseph Walther in the 1990s.
