cross-plough:/ˈkrɒs plaʊ/ (UK) /ˈkrɑːs plau/ (US)| n.|交叉耕作，交叉犁|The act of cross-ploughing a field.|The cross-plough left the field ready for sowing. 交叉耕作使田地准备好播种。|Synonyms: cross-tilling, double-ploughing|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From 'cross' + 'plough'.  'Cross' indicates the intersecting directions of ploughing, and 'plough' refers to the agricultural implement used for tilling the soil.
