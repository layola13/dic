disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/ | n. vt. |轻蔑，鄙视；不屑| To consider or treat as beneath oneself; scorn. |She disdained to answer his rude remarks. 她不屑于回答他粗鲁的言论。|  He looked at the cheap plastic toys with disdain. 他轻蔑地看着那些廉价的塑料玩具。 | 近义词: scorn, contempt, condescension | 反义词: admiration, respect, esteem | 词性: noun, verb | 词源: From Old French *desdeigner*, from Latin *disdignari* "to deem unworthy," from *dis-* "not" + *dignari* "to deem worthy."
