histrionic:/ˌhɪstriˈɑːnɪk/ | /ˌhɪstriˈɒnɪk/ | adj. | 戏剧性的；做作的；装腔作势的 |  Excessively theatrical or dramatic in character or style. Often used to describe behavior that is overly emotional and attention-seeking. |  She put on a histrionic display of grief at the funeral. 她在葬礼上装出一副悲伤的样子。|  His histrionic personality made him the center of attention. 他装腔作势的性格使他成为关注的焦点。 | 近义词: dramatic, theatrical, melodramatic, exaggerated, affected | 反义词: understated, reserved, subdued, genuine, authentic | 词性: 形容词

histrionics:/ˌhɪstriˈɑːnɪks/ | /ˌhɪstriˈɒnɪks/ | n. |  装腔作势；夸张的言行 |  Exaggerated dramatic behavior designed for effect.  |  Her histrionics were embarrassing to watch. 她的装腔作势令人尴尬。 |  He resorted to histrionics to get his way. 他 resorted to histrionics to get his way. 他为了达到目的而装腔作势。| 近义词: dramatics, theatrics, melodrama | 反义词: sincerity, genuineness | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Latin *histrionicus*, from *histrio* "actor."  源自拉丁语 *histrionicus*，来自 *histrio* “演员”。
