heel-in:/ˈhiːl ɪn/| n. |假植；暂时栽植| The act of temporarily planting a young tree or shrub in a shallow trench, covering the roots with soil, to protect it until it can be permanently planted. |  We heeled in the saplings until we were ready to plant them in the orchard. 我们把树苗假植起来，直到准备好把它们种在果园里。|  The gardener heeled in the rose bushes for the winter. 园丁把玫瑰丛假植起来过冬。| 近义词: temporary planting | 反义词: permanent planting | 词性: noun
