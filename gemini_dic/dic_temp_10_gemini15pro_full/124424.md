motorcars:/ˈməʊtəkɑːz/ (UK) /ˈmoʊtərˌkɑːrz/ (US)| n. |汽车（尤指旧式）|A motorcar, chiefly a British term, refers to an automobile, especially an older one.  It emphasizes the vehicle's motorized nature.  In modern usage, "car" is much more common. |例句：The vintage motorcars were on display at the museum. 老式汽车在博物馆展出。

例句：His grandfather used to own a collection of classic motorcars.  他的祖父曾经拥有一批经典汽车。|近义词：cars, automobiles, vehicles|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *motor* + *car*.  *Motor* comes from the Latin *movēre* "to move". *Car* is shortened from *carriage*, ultimately from Latin *carrus* "wheeled vehicle".
