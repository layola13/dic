absoluter:/ˈæbsəluːtər/| n.|专制主义者；绝对主义者|A person who holds absolute principles in political, philosophical, or theological matters.  A supporter of absolutism.| The absoluter believed in the divine right of kings.  这个专制主义者相信君权神授。|近义词：autocrat, dictator, tyrant|反义词：democrat, liberal|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From absolute + -er.  "Absolute" comes from Late Latin absolutus, past participle of absolvere "to set free, acquit," from ab- "from" + solvere "to loosen."
