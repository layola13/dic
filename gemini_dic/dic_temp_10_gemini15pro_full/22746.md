boroughholder:/ˈbʌrəˌhoʊldər/
n.
自由地主; 市镇居民
A person who owns property in a borough, especially one who formerly had the right to vote in parliamentary elections.
In medieval England, a boroughholder was a leading citizen, often responsible for collecting taxes. 在中世纪的英格兰，boroughholder是一位主要的公民，通常负责收税。
The boroughholder was an important figure in local government. 自由地主是地方政府中的重要人物。
freeman, citizen, burgess
(No clear antonym)
noun
