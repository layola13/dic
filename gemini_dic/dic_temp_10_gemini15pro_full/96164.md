independent:/ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt/ | adj. | 独立的，自主的，不相关的 | Not influenced or controlled by others in matters of opinion, conduct, etc.; not subject to another's authority or jurisdiction; not dependent. |  She is an independent woman who runs her own business. 她是一位经营自己事业的独立女性。

| Example: He made an independent decision. 他做了一个独立的决定。| Synonyms: autonomous, free, self-governing, self-reliant | Antonyms: dependent, reliant, subordinate | adjective


independent:/ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt/ | n. | 无党派人士 | A person who is not affiliated with any political party. |  He ran for office as an independent. 他以无党派人士身份竞选公职。

| Example: Many independents voted in the last election. 许多无党派人士在上次选举中投票。| Synonyms: nonpartisan, unaffiliated | Antonyms: partisan | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: via Old French from Latin *independens, from in- ‘not’ + dependens (see dependent).
