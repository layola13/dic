controverted: /ˈkɒntrəvɜːtɪd/ (US) /ˈkɑːntrəvɜːrtɪd/
| adj. | 有争议的；被反驳的；争论的 | Disputed, challenged, or contested. | The judge's decision was immediately controverted by the defense lawyer.  法官的判决立即遭到辩护律师的反驳。| Synonyms: disputed, challenged, contested, debated, argued | Antonyms: undisputed, unchallenged, accepted, agreed upon | Adjective

controvert: /ˈkɒntrəvɜːt/ (US) /ˈkɑːntrəvɜːrt/
| v. | 反驳；争论；否认 | To deny the truth of (something); to oppose or dispute. |  The lawyer controverted the witness's testimony. 律师反驳了证人的证词。 | Synonyms: dispute, challenge, contest, debate, argue, deny, refute, rebut | Antonyms: confirm, corroborate, support, uphold | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin controverto, -are (“to turn against, oppose in argument”), from Latin contro- (“against”) + verto, -ere (“to turn”).
