neoconservatism:/ˌniːoʊkənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/
|n.|新保守主义|A political ideology that emerged in the United States in the 1970s, characterized by an emphasis on free-market capitalism, a strong national defense, and a more assertive foreign policy, often including military intervention.  It also often includes socially conservative views.|Neoconservatism influenced the foreign policy of the George W. Bush administration.|近义词：right-wing conservatism|反义词：liberalism, progressivism
|词性:名词
