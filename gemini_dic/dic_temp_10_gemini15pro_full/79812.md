glycol:/ˈɡlaɪˌkɑːl/ /ˈɡlaɪˌkɒl/ | n. |乙二醇，甘醇|A viscous, sweet-tasting liquid, the simplest member of a class of organic compounds called diols, each molecule of which consists of two hydroxyl groups attached to different carbon atoms of an aliphatic chain. It is used as antifreeze and in the manufacture of polyester fibers.|Ethylene glycol is commonly used as antifreeze in car radiators.|近义词：diol|反义词:  (Glycol doesn't have a direct antonym as it's a specific chemical compound.)| noun
