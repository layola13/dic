gad:/ɡæd/ | vi. | 漫游，闲逛 | To move about restlessly or aimlessly, especially in search of pleasure or amusement. | He spent the summer gadding about Europe. 他整个夏天都在欧洲漫游。| 近义词：roam, wander, gallivant | 反义词: stay, remain | verb

gad:/ɡæd/ | n. | 尖头棒，刺激物 | A pointed stick; something that provokes or stimulates. |  The fly was a constant gad to the horse. 苍蝇一直刺激着马。| 近义词：goad, spur, prod | 反义词: deterrent | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gadden*, from Old Norse *gaddr* ("spike, nail"), from Proto-Germanic *gazdaz* ("spike, sting"), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰes- (“to prick, sting”). Related to Old English *gād* ("goad, spear, spike").
