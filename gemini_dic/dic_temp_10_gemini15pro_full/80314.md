goldbricker:/ˈɡoʊldˌbrɪkər/| n. |金砖; 偷懒的人| 1. A brick made of gold. (Obsolete) 2. A person who shirks their duties or avoids work, often by pretending to be busy.| He called them a bunch of goldbrickers for taking so many breaks. | 近义词：loafer, idler |反义词：workaholic |词性:noun


词源: From "gold brick," a worthless brick painted gold and used to swindle someone.  The verb "to goldbrick" meaning "to swindle" emerged in the late 19th century, followed by the noun "goldbricker" referring to someone who swindles or shirks work.
