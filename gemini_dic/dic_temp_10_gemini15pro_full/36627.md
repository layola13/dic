collectivising: /kəˈlɛktɪvaɪzɪŋ/
verb
集体化
The process of organizing something, such as agriculture or industry, on the basis of collective ownership and control.
The government was collectivising farms in the region. 政府正在该地区进行农业集体化。
The collectivising of small farms into large state-owned enterprises was a key feature of the communist regime. 将小农场集体化成大型国有企业是共产主义政权的一个主要特征。
近义词: nationalizing, communalizing, socializing
反义词: privatizing, individualizing
词性: verb (动名词)

collectivise: /kəˈlɛktɪvaɪz/
verb
集体化
To organize something, such as agriculture or industry, on the basis of collective ownership and control.
The Soviet Union collectivised agriculture in the 1930s.  苏联在20世纪30年代对农业进行了集体化。
Many countries collectivised their key industries after the war.  战后许多国家将他们的主要工业集体化。
近义词: nationalize, communalize, socialize
反义词: privatize, individualize
词性: verb

collective: /kəˈlɛktɪv/
adjective, noun
集体的，集体
Formed by a group of people or things; done or shared by a group of people.  (adjective)
A group of people or things. (noun)
The collective responsibility of the team was emphasized by the coach. 教练强调了团队的集体责任。(adjective)
The art collective showcased their latest work at the gallery.  艺术团体在画廊展示了他们的最新作品。(noun)
近义词: joint, combined, shared (adjective); group, assembly, body (noun)
反义词: individual, separate (adjective)
词性: adjective, noun

词源: From Latin *collectivus*, from collectus (past participle of colligere “to gather together”), from com- "together" + legere "to gather".
