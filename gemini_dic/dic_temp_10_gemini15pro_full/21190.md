blockading:/blɒˈkeɪdɪŋ/ | adj. | 封锁的，实行封锁的 |  The act of blockading; a state of being blockaded; surrounding a place to prevent entry or exit. | The blockading forces prevented any ships from entering the port. 封锁部队阻止任何船只进入港口。 | synonyms: besieging, encircling, surrounding | antonyms: opening, freeing, releasing | adjective

blockade: /blɒˈkeɪd/ | n. vt. | 封锁，阻塞 |  The isolating, closing off, or surrounding of a place, such as a port, harbor, or city, by enemy troops or ships to prevent entrance or exit or to cut off supplies.  The act or means of blockading. Any obstruction that hinders passage or progress. | The navy imposed a blockade on the enemy's port. 海军对敌人的港口实施了封锁。 |  synonyms: siege, obstruction, barrier | antonyms: access, opening, passage | noun, verb


Etymology:
From French *blocus* (later *blocus*), from Dutch *blokhuis* "blockhouse," literally "log house," from *blok* "log" + *huis* "house." The sense shifted from "fortified place" to "siege" to "blockade."
