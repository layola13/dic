compartmentalize: /ˌkɒmpɑːtˈmentəlaɪz/ (UK) /ˌkəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəlaɪz/ (US)| v. |划分；分隔；区划；（尤指感情或思想）隔离| To separate something into isolated compartments or categories, especially to avoid unpleasant thoughts or feelings from affecting other areas of one's life.| He compartmentalizes his life by keeping his work and personal life completely separate. 他将工作和个人生活完全分开，以此区隔自己的生活。|  She compartmentalized her grief after her mother's death, focusing on work to avoid the pain.  在她母亲去世后，她将悲伤隔离起来，专注于工作以避免痛苦。| synonyms: categorize, classify, separate, isolate, pigeonhole | antonyms: integrate, combine, unify | verb

compartmentalization: /ˌkɒmpɑːtˈmentəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌkəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ (US) | n. | 区分；划分；隔离|The act of compartmentalizing something, especially emotions or thoughts.| The compartmentalization of her emotions allowed her to function despite the stress. 她对情绪的隔离使她能够在压力下正常工作。| Excessive compartmentalization can hinder personal growth. 过度隔离情绪会阻碍个人成长。| synonyms: categorization, classification, separation, isolation | antonyms: integration, combination, unification | noun

compartmentalized: /ˌkɒmpɑːtˈmentəlaɪzd/ (UK) /ˌkəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəlaɪzd/ (US) | adj. | 分隔的；区分的；隔离的| Divided into separate compartments or categories.|  His life was highly compartmentalized, with little overlap between his work and personal spheres. 他的生活高度区隔，工作和个人领域几乎没有重叠。| The company's operations are compartmentalized to minimize risk. 该公司的运营被划分开来，以最大程度地降低风险。| synonyms: separated, categorized, classified, isolated | antonyms: integrated, combined, unified | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From compartment + -alize. Compartment comes from French compartiment, from compartir "to divide," from Latin compartiri, from com- "together" + partire "to part, divide."
