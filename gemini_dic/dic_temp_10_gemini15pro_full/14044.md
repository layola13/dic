auster:/ˈɔːstər/| adj.| 严肃的，朴素的，苦行的|Characterized by severity or strictness, especially in moral or religious matters; plain and simple, without luxury or self-indulgence; practicing self-denial.|The monk lived an auster life of prayer and fasting.  这个僧侣过着祈祷和禁食的苦行生活。|Synonyms: severe, strict, ascetic, simple, plain, spartan|Antonyms: luxurious, extravagant, indulgent, lavish|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin austerus ("sour, harsh, severe, strict"), from Proto-Italic *aus-tero-, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ews- ("dry").  Related to austere (adjective).
