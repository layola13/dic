ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *contritnes*, from Old French *contritesse*, from Late Latin *contrītiō*, from Latin *contrītus*, past participle of *conterere* (“to grind, bruise, rub together, wear away”), from *com-* (intensive prefix) + *terere* (“to rub, grind”).
