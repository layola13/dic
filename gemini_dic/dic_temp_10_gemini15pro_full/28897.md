carrack: /ˈkærək/ | n. | 大型帆船，卡拉克帆船 | A large sailing ship of the 15th to 17th centuries, used for trade and war, typically square-rigged on the foremast and mainmast and lateen-rigged on the mizzenmast. | The carrack was a vital vessel for European exploration and trade during the Age of Discovery. 卡拉克帆船是大航海时代欧洲探险和贸易的重要船只。 |  The carrack Mary Rose was a flagship of Henry VIII's navy. 玛丽玫瑰号卡拉克帆船是亨利八世海军的一艘旗舰。 | synonyms: galleon, nao | antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps small boat, dinghy) | noun
