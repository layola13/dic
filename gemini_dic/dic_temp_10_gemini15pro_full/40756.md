conviction:/kənˈvɪkʃən/ | n. | 定罪；坚信；确信；信念 | A fixed or strong belief; the act or process of finding a person guilty of a crime especially in a court of law. |  The lawyer appealed the conviction. 律师对判决提出上诉。 | synonyms:  belief, confidence, certainty, persuasion, assurance | antonyms: doubt, uncertainty, skepticism | noun


conviction:/kənˈvɪkʃən/ | n. | 说服力；令人信服 | The quality of showing that you believe strongly in what you are saying. | He spoke with great conviction. 他讲话很有说服力。| synonyms:  persuasiveness, fervor, earnestness | antonyms: apathy, indifference | noun
