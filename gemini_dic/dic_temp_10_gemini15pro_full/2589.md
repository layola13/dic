admonitor:/ˌædməˈnɪtər/ | v. | 劝告；警告；训诫 | To warn or advise someone earnestly. | The teacher admonished the students to be quiet. 老师告诫学生们要安静。

The coach admonished his players for their lack of effort. 教练因队员们缺乏努力而训诫他们。

| 近义词：warn, caution, advise, rebuke, reprimand | 反义词：praise, commend, encourage | 词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *admonēre*, from ad- + monēre "to warn, advise".

USAGE->
一般现在时:
I admonish you to be careful. = 我劝你要小心。
He admonishes his children regularly about their behavior. = 他经常告诫他的孩子们要注意他们的行为。


一般过去时:
The judge admonished the defendant for his outburst. = 法官因被告的爆发而训诫他。
My mother admonished me for coming home late. = 我妈妈因为我回家晚了而责备我。


现在完成时:
The principal has admonished the students several times about bullying. = 校长已经多次告诫学生们不要恃强凌弱。
I have admonished him not to repeat his mistake. = 我已经警告过他不要再犯同样的错误。


过去完成时:
The teacher had admonished the class about cheating before the exam. = 老师在考试前已经警告过全班不要作弊。
She had admonished her brother about his reckless driving before the accident. = 在事故发生之前，她已经警告过她哥哥不要鲁莽驾驶。


将来时:
I will admonish him if he continues to disrupt the class. = 如果他继续扰乱课堂，我会警告他。
The manager will admonish the employees about the new dress code. = 经理将告诫员工们关于新的着装规范。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would admonish him privately. = 如果我是你，我会私下劝他。
I wish he had admonished me sooner. = 我希望他早点提醒我。


被动语态:
The students were admonished by the teacher for their misbehavior. = 学生们因为他们的不良行为而受到老师的训诫。
He was admonished by his boss for being late. = 他因为迟到而被老板训诫。


疑问句:
Did the teacher admonish the student? = 老师训诫了学生吗？
Why did you admonish her? = 你为什么训诫她？


否定句:
I did not admonish him. = 我没有训诫他。
She did not admonish her children for their messy room. = 她没有因为孩子们房间乱而责备他们。


PHRASE->
admonish someone against something = 劝告某人不要做某事
admonish someone for something = 因某事而训诫某人
admonish someone to do something = 劝告某人做某事
sternly admonish = 严厉地警告
gently admonish = 温和地劝告
