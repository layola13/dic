militarist:/ˈmɪlɪtərɪst/| n. |军国主义者|A person who believes in the importance of military strength and advocates an aggressive military policy.|The militarist urged the country to increase its defense spending.  这个军国主义者敦促国家增加国防开支。|Example: The rise of militarists in the 1930s contributed to the outbreak of World War II.  20世纪30年代军国主义者的崛起促成了第二次世界大战的爆发。|Synonyms: warmonger, hawk, jingoist|Antonyms: pacifist, dove|noun

militaristic:/ˌmɪlɪtəˈrɪstɪk/| adj. |军国主义的|Advocating or characteristic of militarism; aggressively military.|The country's militaristic policies led to international tensions. 这个国家的军国主义政策导致了国际紧张局势。|Example: The government's militaristic rhetoric alarmed neighboring countries. 政府的军国主义言论令邻国感到震惊。|Synonyms: hawkish, bellicose, warmongering|Antonyms: pacifistic, dovish|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *militariste*, from Latin *militaris* ("military") + *-ist*. Ultimately from Latin *miles* ("soldier").
