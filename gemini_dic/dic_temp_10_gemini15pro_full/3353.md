affricate:/ˈæfrɪkət/ (US), /ˈæfrɪkeɪt/ (UK)| n. & adj.|塞擦音；塞擦音的|A sound that begins as a stop and releases as a fricative, such as the /tʃ/ sound in "church" or the /dʒ/ sound in "judge".  As an adjective, it describes a sound of this type.|
The "ch" sound in "chair" is an affricate.|"chair"中的"ch"音是一个塞擦音。

The /dʒ/ sound at the beginning of "judge" is an affricate.|"judge"开头的 /dʒ/ 音是一个塞擦音。

|近义词: (as noun) none (specific phonetic term), (as adjective)  plosive-fricative|反义词: none (specific phonetic term)|词性: noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *affricāta, feminine past participle of affricāre (“to rub against”), from ad- (“to, towards”) + fricāre (“to rub”).
