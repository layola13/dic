metafictional:/ˌmɛtəˈfɪkʃənəl/| adj.|元小说的，后设小说的|A metafictional work of literature is one that self-consciously addresses the devices of fiction, exposing the artificiality of the work.  It often involves characters who are aware they are in a story, authors inserting themselves into their narratives, or stories that comment on the process of storytelling itself.|The novel was highly metafictional, with the narrator frequently breaking the fourth wall to address the reader directly. 这部小说非常具有元小说性质，叙述者经常打破第四堵墙直接与读者对话。

The film's metafictional elements, such as the characters discussing their own roles in the story, added a layer of complexity.  这部电影的元小说元素，比如角色讨论自己在故事中的作用，增加了一层复杂性。|近义词：self-reflexive, postmodern|反义词：conventional, traditional|词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
"meta-" (beyond, after, self-referential) + "fiction" (a literary work whose content is produced by the imagination and is not necessarily based on fact). "Meta" comes from Greek, while "fiction" comes from Latin. The term gained prominence in the late 20th century.
