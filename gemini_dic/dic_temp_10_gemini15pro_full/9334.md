antipragmatical:/ˌæntɪpræɡˈmætɪkl/
| adj. | 不切实际的；反对实用的；不务实的 | Opposed to or averse to practicality or pragmatism; impractical.  Not concerned with practical results or immediate usefulness. |  His antipragmatical approach to business ultimately led to the company's downfall. 他不切实际的商业方法最终导致了公司的倒闭。 | synonyms: impractical, unrealistic, unworkable, idealistic, visionary, utopian, quixotic | antonyms: practical, pragmatic, realistic, down-to-earth, sensible | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + pragmatical.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposed to." "Pragmatical" comes from the Greek "pragma" meaning "deed" or "act," and later evolved to mean "practical" or "businesslike."
