eudaemonism:/ˌjuːdɪˈmoʊnɪzəm/ | n. | 幸福主义；快乐主义 |  A system of ethics that bases moral obligation on the pursuit of what is intrinsically good and especially of personal well-being.  It holds that the ultimate goal of human action is happiness and flourishing. |  Eudaemonism emphasizes achieving a life well-lived through the cultivation of virtues and the pursuit of excellence. |  Synonyms:  happiness, well-being, flourishing, felicity | Antonyms: misery, unhappiness, suffering | noun

eudaemonia:/ˌjuːdɪˈmoʊniə/ | n. | 幸福；福祉 |  A state of human flourishing or prosperity; a contented state of being happy and healthy and prosperous. | Aristotle believed that eudaemonia was the ultimate goal of human life. |  Synonyms: happiness, well-being, flourishing, felicity | Antonyms: misery, unhappiness, suffering | noun
