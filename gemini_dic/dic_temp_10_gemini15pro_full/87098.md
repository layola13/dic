herakline: /ˌhɛrəˈklaɪn/ | n. | 海拉克莱恩; 海格力斯线 | A line named after Heracles (Hercules), especially the Pillars of Hercules, marking a limit.  A perceived boundary between the known and the unknown, especially in relation to geographical exploration. Also, the apparent boundary of the heavens. |  Sailors in ancient times believed that the Herakline marked the edge of the world.  |  Synonyms: boundary, limit, edge, perimeter, horizon | Antonyms: center, middle, interior | noun
