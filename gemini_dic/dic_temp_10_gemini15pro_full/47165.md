dawdle:/ˈdɔːdl/ (US), /ˈdɔːdəl/ (UK)| v. | 闲逛，磨蹭，拖延 | to waste time; to move slowly and idly |  He dawdled on his way to school and was late. 他在上学的路上闲逛，结果迟到了。

| Example: Stop dawdling and finish your homework!  别磨蹭了，快完成你的作业！| Synonyms: linger, loiter, tarry, delay, procrastinate | Antonyms: hurry, hasten, rush, expedite | Verb


dawdle:/ˈdɔːdl/ (US), /ˈdɔːdəl/ (UK)| n. | 闲逛，磨蹭 | the act of dawdling | The dawdle in the morning made him miss his train. 早晨的磨蹭让他错过了火车。

| Example:  There's no time for a dawdle; we have to leave immediately.  没有时间闲逛了，我们必须马上离开。 | Synonyms: lingering, loitering, tarrying, delay, procrastination | Antonyms: haste, speed, rush, expedition | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From frequentative of *daddle*, from Middle English *dadlen* “to walk unsteadily,” of imitative origin.
