digressively:/daɪˈɡrɛsɪvli/ /dɪˈɡrɛsɪvli/
adv. | 离题地，枝节横生地 | in a digressive manner; straying from the main point |  He spoke digressively, often wandering off into anecdotes and personal stories. 他说话很离题，经常扯到一些轶事和个人故事。| synonym: ramblingly, discursively, circuitously | antonym: directly, succinctly, concisely |词性: 副词

digression:/daɪˈɡrɛʃən/ /dɪˈɡrɛʃən/
n. |  离题; 枝节; 插话 | a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing |  The professor's lecture was filled with amusing digressions. 教授的讲座充满了有趣的插曲。| synonym: deviation, detour, aside | antonym: adherence, focus, concentration |词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *digressio, from Latin digressus (“a going aside”), past participle of digredi (“to go aside”), from dis- (“apart, away”) + gradi (“to walk, go”).
