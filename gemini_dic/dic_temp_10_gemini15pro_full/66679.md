exoduses: /ˈɛksədəsiːz/ | n. | 出埃及记; 大批离去 |A mass departure or emigration.  The Israelites' exodus from Egypt is a foundational story in Judaism and Christianity. Also used figuratively to describe any large-scale departure or emigration. | The exodus of refugees from the war-torn country was heartbreaking. 难民从饱受战争蹂躏的国家大批离去，令人心碎。The company witnessed a mass exodus of employees after the announcement of layoffs. 公司宣布裁员后，员工大批离去。| 近义词: emigration, migration, departure, flight | 反义词: arrival, influx, immigration | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin exodus, from Greek exodos "a going out, departure," from ex- "out" + hodos "way, road."
