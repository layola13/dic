organophyly:/ɔːrˌɡænəˈfɪli/ | n. | 亲有机性; 有机亲和性 |  The tendency of a substance to dissolve in or absorb organic liquids, especially oils and fats. This property is often related to the hydrophobic nature of the substance.  |  The organophyly of the pesticide allows it to be readily absorbed by the plant's waxy cuticle. 这种杀虫剂的亲有机性使其易于被植物的蜡质表皮吸收。 The researcher measured the organophyly of the new polymer. 研究人员测量了这种新聚合物的亲有机性。 |  lipophilicity, hydrophobicity |  hydrophilicity | noun
