blubbered:/ˈblʌbərd/| v.| 哭诉，抽泣，大声哭|to cry noisily and uncontrollably| The little boy blubbered when he lost his toy. 小男孩丢了玩具后哭了起来。|Synonyms: sobbed, wept, cried, bawled|Antonyms: laughed, chuckled, giggled|verb

blubber:/ˈblʌbər/| n.| (鲸鱼、海豹等的)鲸脂，脂肪|the fat of sea mammals, such as whales and seals| Whales use blubber to stay warm in cold water. 鲸鱼用鲸脂在冷水中保暖。|Synonyms: fat, lard|Antonyms: leanness, thinness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *blobberen*, probably of imitative origin. Related to Dutch *lobberen,* German *lobern.*
