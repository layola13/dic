aced: /eɪst/
verb
取得好成绩；出色地完成
to succeed very easily in something, especially a test or exam
例句：I aced my driving test. 我驾驶考试得了满分。
例句：She aced all her exams. 她所有考试都得了A。
近义词：excel, triumph, master
反义词：fail, flunk
词性：verb

aced: /eɪst/
adjective
(非正式)一流的；极好的
(informal) very good or excellent
例句：He's an aced tennis player. 他是一流的网球运动员。
例句：That new restaurant is aced. 那家新餐馆棒极了。
近义词：excellent, superb, outstanding
反义词：terrible, awful
词性：adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the highest playing card in some card games, an ace, which is from Old French as, meaning "one," from Latin as, assis, meaning "unit, Roman copper coin".  The usage relating to achieving an "A" grade originated in the US in the early 20th century.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He aces every test. = 他每次考试都得满分。
She aces her presentations. = 她的演讲总是非常出色。

一般过去时:
I aced the exam yesterday. = 我昨天的考试得了满分。
They aced their performance last night. = 他们昨晚的演出非常成功。

现在完成时:
He has aced all his exams so far. = 到目前为止，他所有考试都得了满分。
She has aced every presentation she's given. = 她每次演讲都表现出色。

过去完成时:
I had aced the test before I told my parents. = 在我告诉父母之前，我已经考了满分。
They had aced the rehearsal before the actual performance. = 在正式演出之前，他们的彩排就非常成功。

将来时:
I will ace the next exam. = 我下次考试会得满分。
She will ace her job interview. = 她工作面试会表现出色。

虚拟语气:
If I aced this test, I would be so happy. = 如果我这次考试得了满分，我会很高兴。
I wish I had aced that interview. = 我真希望那次面试我表现得更好。

被动语态 (aced 很少用于被动语态，但理论上可以构成):
The test was aced by him. = 他考了满分。(不自然)
The presentation was aced by the team. = 团队的演讲非常成功。(不自然)

疑问句:
Did you ace the exam? = 你考试得满分了吗？
Is he going to ace his presentation? = 他演讲会表现出色吗？

否定句:
I didn't ace the exam. = 我考试没有得满分。
She doesn't always ace her presentations. = 她的演讲并非总是很出色。
