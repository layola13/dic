generalize:/ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz/ (US) /ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəlaɪz/ (UK)| v. |概括，归纳；推广|To infer or form a conclusion from available information, often creating a general principle from specific examples. Also, to make something more widespread or common. |Scientists generalize from specific experimental results to form broader theories. 科学家从具体的实验结果中概括出更广泛的理论。|synonyms: summarize, abstract, extrapolate, infer, deduce, broaden, expand, extend |antonyms: specify, particularize, restrict, limit| verb

generalization:/ˌdʒɛnərəlaɪzˈeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəlaɪzˈeɪʃ(ə)n/ (UK)| n. |概括，归纳；普遍化；推广|A general statement or concept obtained by inference from specific cases. The act or process of generalizing. |Making generalizations about entire cultures can be dangerous. 对整个文化进行概括可能是危险的。|synonyms: summary, abstraction, inference, conclusion, extension |antonyms: specification, particularization, restriction | noun
