a-degree:/ə dɪˈɡriː/| adv.|稍微，有点，在某种程度上|To a degree, I agree with you. 我在某种程度上同意你的观点。|例句：The film was a-degree disappointing. 这部电影有点令人失望。

She felt a-degree uneasy about the situation. 她对这种情况感到有点不安。|近义词：somewhat, slightly, partly, to some extent|反义词：completely, totally, entirely|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Middle English phrase "to a degree," meaning "to a certain extent" or "in a certain measure."  The word "degree" itself comes from Old French "degré" and ultimately from Latin "gradus," meaning "step" or "rank."
