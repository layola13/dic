ordovician:/ɔːr.dəˈvɪʃ.ən/ | adj. n.| 奥陶纪的；奥陶纪 |Relating to or denoting the second period of the Palaeozoic era, between the Cambrian and Silurian periods, or the system of rocks deposited during it.  It is named after the Ordovices, an ancient Celtic tribe of North Wales. | The Ordovician period saw a diversification of life in the oceans. 奥陶纪见证了海洋生物的多样化。| The Ordovician rocks are rich in fossils. 奥陶纪的岩石富含化石。| Synonyms: none (as it's a specific geological period) | Antonyms: none (as it's a specific geological period) | adjective, noun
