imponderably:/ɪmˈpɒndərəbli/ /ɪmˈpɑːndərəbli/| adv.|不可估量地，难以察觉地|In a way that is so slight or subtle as to be almost impossible to notice or measure.  It describes something that's too small or insignificant to be weighed or measured, often used figuratively for things that are difficult to grasp mentally.|The air shifted imponderably, a hint of rain on the breeze. 空气难以察觉地变化，微风中夹杂着一丝雨意。|The butterfly's wings beat imponderably, creating tiny air currents. 蝴蝶的翅膀难以察觉地拍打着，产生微小的气流。|近义词：incalculably, imperceptibly, subtly|反义词：measurably, noticeably, significantly|词性: 副词

Etymology:  From "imponderable" + "-ly."  "Imponderable" comes from Late Latin "imponderabilis," from "in-" (not) + "ponderabilis" (weighable), from "ponderare" (to weigh), ultimately from "pondus" (weight).
