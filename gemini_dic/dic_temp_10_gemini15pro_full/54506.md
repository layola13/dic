disjoin:/dɪsˈdʒɔɪn/ | v. | 分离，拆散 | To separate or become separated; disconnect. | The two halves of the sphere disjoined with a loud crack.  两个半球伴随着一声巨响分开了。|近义词：separate, detach, disconnect, disunite |反义词：join, unite, connect |词性：动词

disjoin:/dɪsˈdʒɔɪn/ | adj. | 分离的，不相交的 |  Logically unconnected; distinct. (Mainly used in logic and mathematics) | The two events were entirely disjoin. 这两个事件完全不相干。|近义词：separate, distinct, unconnected |反义词：joined, connected, intersecting |词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *disjungere, from Latin dis- "apart" + jungere "to join".
