CFCs:/ˌsiːˌɛfˈsiːz/| n.|氯氟烃; 氟氯化碳|Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) are nontoxic, nonflammable chemicals containing atoms of carbon, chlorine, and fluorine. They are used in the manufacture of aerosol sprays, blowing agents for foams and packing materials, as solvents, and as refrigerants.|CFCs were widely used in refrigerators before their harmful effects on the ozone layer were discovered. 氯氟烃在人们发现其对臭氧层的危害之前被广泛用于冰箱中。|

CFCs are responsible for depleting the ozone layer. 氯氟烃是导致臭氧层损耗的原因。|synonyms:  Freon, halocarbon|antonyms: ozone-friendly refrigerants|noun
