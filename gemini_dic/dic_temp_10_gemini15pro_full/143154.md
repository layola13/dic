outchattering: /ˌaʊtˈtʃætərɪŋ/
verb
胜过…的饶舌；比…更健谈
talking more, longer, or louder than someone else
He outchattered everyone at the party. 他在聚会上比任何人都健谈。

outchattering: /ˌaʊtˈtʃætərɪŋ/
adjective
胜过…的饶舌；比…更健谈
talking more, longer, or louder than someone else
She has an outchattering personality. 她性格健谈。


Etymology:
"out-" (prefix indicating exceeding or surpassing) + "chatter" (verb meaning to talk rapidly or incessantly about unimportant matters)

Synonyms:
outtalking, outbabbling

Antonyms:
silent, quiet, reserved
