alderman:/ˈɔːldərmən/ | n. | 市議員；市府參議員 | A member of a municipal council, especially in the US.  An elected member of a municipal council. |  The alderman proposed a new park for the city.  市議員提議在市內建一個新公園。

The alderman voted against the proposed tax increase.  市議員投票反對擬議的增稅。 |  councilor, councilman, selectman |  N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ealdormann (attested as ealdormon, aldormon) "chief, prince, magistrate, head of a shire," literally "elder man," from ealdor "elder, chief, senior" (see elder (adj.)) + mann "man" (see man (n.)). Cognate with Old Frisian alderman, Old Saxon aldarman, Old High German altman. In Anglo-Saxon times, an officer of high rank, next to the king and ruling ealdormen of provinces or shires. Later, the term came to be applied to the chief magistrate of a borough or city corporation, and from there to a member of a municipal council.
