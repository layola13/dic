accroach:/əˈkroʊtʃ/| vt.|侵占，蚕食；非法获取|To gradually and wrongfully take something, especially power or land, that belongs to someone else.|The government is accused of accroaching on the rights of individuals. 政府被指控侵犯个人权利。

The company was accused of accroaching on its competitor's market share.  该公司被指控蚕食其竞争对手的市场份额。|近义词：encroach, infringe, usurp, impinge|反义词: relinquish, surrender|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *acrochier "to seize, grasp, hook," from a- "to" + croche "hook," from Frankish *krok, from Proto-Germanic *krakon.  Meaning shifted to "to usurp" in the 16th century.
