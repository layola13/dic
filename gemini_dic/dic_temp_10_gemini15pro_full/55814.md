divestitures:/daɪˈvɛstɪtʃərz/
|n.|剥离，撤资|The act of selling off subsidiary business interests or investments.|The company announced a series of divestitures to focus on its core business. 公司宣布了一系列剥离以专注于其核心业务。|Synonyms: sale, disposal, spin-off, liquidation|Antonyms: acquisition, investment, purchase|noun

divestiture:/daɪˈvɛstɪtʃər/
|n.|剥离，撤资|The act of selling off subsidiary business interests or investments.|Due to the economic downturn, the company had to make some difficult divestitures. 由于经济低迷，公司不得不进行一些艰难的剥离。|Synonyms: shedding, unloading|Antonyms: acquiring, accumulating|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From divest + -ure.  Divest comes from Latin *divestire, from dis- "off, away" + vestire "to clothe".  The original meaning was "to undress," later evolving to "deprive of power, rights, etc." and then to its current financial sense.
