illinition:/ɪlɪˈnɪʃən/
n.|欺骗，谬误，幻觉|The act of deceiving; misleading; the state of being deceived. |He was under the illinition that he was invincible. 他误以为自己是无敌的。|近义词：delusion, fallacy, misconception|反义词：reality, truth|词性:名词
Etymology: From Late Latin *illinitio*, from Latin illino (“smear, bedaub”) + -tio.  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ley- (“to smear, anoint”).
