orthopraxy:/ˈɔːrθəˌpræksi/
n.
正统实践，正确行为
Right conduct, as opposed to right belief or doctrine.  It focuses on practice rather than theory.
He valued orthopraxy over orthodoxy, believing actions spoke louder than words. 他重视正统实践胜过正统信仰，认为行动胜于言语。
近义词：correct practice, proper conduct, right action
反义词：heteropraxy, heresy, misconduct
词性: 名词


Etymology:
From Ancient Greek ὀρθοπραξία (orthopraxia), from ὀρθός (orthós, “straight, right”) + πρᾶξις (prâxis, “practice, action”).
