capillariness:/ˌkæpɪˈlærɪnəs/
n.
毛细作用，毛细现象
The tendency of a liquid to rise or fall in a narrow tube due to surface tension.
The capillariness of the soil helps the plants absorb water. 土壤的毛细作用帮助植物吸收水分。

The capillariness allows the water to climb up the thin tube. 毛细作用使水能够爬上细管。
近义词：capillary action, capillary attraction
反义词：(No direct antonym, but concepts related to impeding capillary action could be considered, e.g., hydrophobicity)
词性：noun


capillary:/ˈkæpɪˌleri/
adj.
毛细血管的，毛细管的
Relating to or resembling a capillary; fine and slender.
Capillary tubes are used in various scientific experiments. 毛细管用于各种科学实验。

The capillary network in our bodies is vast and complex. 我们体内的毛细血管网络庞大而复杂。

反义词：thick, wide
词性：adjective


capillary:/ˈkæpɪˌleri/
n.
毛细血管，毛细管
Any of the smallest blood vessels that connect the smallest arteries with the smallest veins.
A tiny tube, especially one of the minute blood vessels connecting arteries and veins.
Blood is transported throughout the body via capillaries. 血液通过毛细血管输送到全身。

The scientist examined the liquid in the capillary. 科学家检查了毛细管中的液体。
近义词：blood vessel,  (for tubes)  tubule
反义词：(No direct antonym for the anatomical sense.  For tubes, perhaps 'main pipe/tube')
词性：noun
