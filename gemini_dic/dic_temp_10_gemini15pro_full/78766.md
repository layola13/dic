gilds:/ɡɪldz/| n.| 行会，协会，同业公会；镀金，涂上金色|A medieval association of craftsmen or merchants, often having considerable power.| The Goldsmiths' Guild was very powerful in the 14th century. 金匠行会在14世纪非常强大。| synonyms: association, society, union, league, fraternity, fellowship | antonyms: individual, loner | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gilde*, from Old Norse *gildi* ("payment, value, feast, guild"), from Proto-Germanic *geldō* ("payment, tribute, value"). Cognate with German *Gilde* ("guild"), Dutch *gilde* ("guild"). Related to *yield*. The sense of "to overlay with gold" is a later development.
