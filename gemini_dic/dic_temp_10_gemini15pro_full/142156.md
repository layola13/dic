orisont: /ˈɒrɪˌzɒnt/ /ɔːˈraɪzən/ | n. | 地平线，眼界，视野 |  The apparent line that separates earth from sky.  A limit of a person's mental perception, experience, or interest. | The sun dipped below the orisont. 太阳落到了地平线下。| synonyms: skyline, horizon, vista | antonyms: nadir | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French orisonte, from Latin horizontem, accusative of horizon, from Ancient Greek ὁρίζων (horízōn, “limiting, defining”), present participle of ὁρίζω (horízō, “I limit, define, separate”), from ὅρος (hóros, “boundary, limit, landmark”).
