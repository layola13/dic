malebranchism:/ˈmeɪlbrɑːntʃɪzəm/ | n. | 马勒伯朗士主义; 偶然论 | The doctrine of occasionalism, developed by Nicolas Malebranche, which holds that God is the only true cause, and that what appear to be causes in the natural world are merely occasions for God to act.  It asserts that created substances cannot be efficient causes of events.  Instead, all events are caused directly by God. |  Malebranchism played a significant role in the philosophical debates of the 17th century.  马勒伯朗士主义在17世纪的哲学辩论中扮演了重要的角色。 | occasionalism | determinism, pantheism | noun
