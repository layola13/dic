emporia:/ɛmˈpɔːriə/ | n. | 商业中心，商场，贸易中心 | A place of trade, especially one with a large variety of goods.  A center of commerce; a market. |  We visited the emporia in the city center, looking for unique gifts. 我们去了市中心的商场，寻找独特的礼物。 | The ancient city was known for its bustling emporia. 这座古城以其繁华的商业中心而闻名。 | synonyms: marketplace, bazaar, mart, trading post | antonyms:  - (No direct antonym, consider "wasteland" or "ghost town" in certain contexts) | noun

emporium:/ɛmˈpɔːriəm/ | n. | 商场，店铺，(尤指)专卖店 | A large retail store, especially one selling a wide variety of goods; a store selling a particular type of merchandise. |  They found a rare book at a dusty emporium on a side street. 他们在一条小街上一家满是灰尘的旧书店里找到了一本珍本书。 | The local emporium sells everything from groceries to hardware. 当地的这家商店出售从食品杂货到五金用品的各种商品。 | synonyms: store, shop, market, outlet | antonyms: - (No direct antonym) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin empōrium, from Ancient Greek ἐμπορεῖον (emporeîon, “trading place”), from ἐμπόριος (empórios, “trading”), from ἐν (en, “in”) + πόρος (póros, “passage, journey”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *per- (“to lead, pass over”).
