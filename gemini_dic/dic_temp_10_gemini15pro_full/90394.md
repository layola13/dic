howl:/haʊl/| n. vi. vt.|嗥叫，怒吼，嚎啕大哭；(风)呼啸，(机器)轰鸣；大声叫喊，抱怨|A long, loud, mournful cry or wailing sound, typically made by a dog or wolf.  To emit such a sound; to cry out in pain, anger, or amusement. To utter (words) in a loud, unrestrained way.|The wolves howled at the moon.  狼对着月亮嚎叫。|近义词：wail, cry, yell, scream, roar, shriek, bay, ululate|反义词:whisper, murmur|词性:noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hūwlian, from Proto-Germanic *hūlōną, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱlew- (“to shout, howl”). Cognate with Dutch huilen (“to cry, weep”), German heulen (“to howl, wail”), Swedish yla (“to howl”), Latin ululāre (“to howl, shriek”).
