escarpment:/ɪˈskɑːrpmənt/| n.|悬崖，陡坡|A long, steep slope, especially one at the edge of a plateau or separating areas of land at different heights.|The hikers reached the edge of the escarpment and gazed at the valley below.  徒步旅行者到达了悬崖边缘，凝视着下面的山谷。|近义词：cliff, precipice, bluff|反义词:slope, incline|noun


escarp:/ɪˈskɑːrp/| vt. n.|使成陡坡；陡坡|To make or form a steep slope; a steep slope.|The engineers had to escarp the hillside to prevent landslides. 工程师们不得不把山坡削成陡坡以防止山体滑坡。  The escarp offered a natural defense for the ancient city. 陡坡为这座古城提供了天然的防御。|近义词：cliff, precipice, bluff|反义词:slope, incline|verb, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *escarper "to cut down steeply," from Italian scarpa "slope, scarp," from a Germanic source related to Old High German scharf "sharp."  The noun form "escarpment" arose later.
