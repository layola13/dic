menace:/ˈmenɪs/ (US), /ˈmenəs/ (UK)| n. vt.|威胁，危险；恐吓，威胁|Something that is likely to cause harm; a threat or danger.  A person who causes trouble or annoyance.|例句：He is a menace to society. 他是对社会的威胁。|近义词：threat, danger, peril, hazard, risk|反义词：safety, security, protection|词性:名词,动词

menace:/ˈmenɪs/ (US), /ˈmenəs/ (UK)| vt.|威胁，恐吓|To threaten, especially in a way that makes someone afraid.|例句：He menaced the shopkeeper with a knife. 他用刀威胁店主。|近义词：threaten, intimidate, bully|反义词：protect, defend|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *manace (menace, threat), from Late Latin minacia (“threat”), from minax (“threatening”), from minari (“to threaten”), of unknown origin.
