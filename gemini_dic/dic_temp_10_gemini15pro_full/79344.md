gleek: /ɡliːk/ | n. vi. | 幸灾乐祸；嘲弄；傻笑；（古）音乐家，歌手 |  To express malicious glee; to gloat. Also, an archaic term for a musician or singer. | He gleeked at his rival's misfortune. 他对竞争对手的不幸幸灾乐祸。| 近义词: gloat, sneer, smirk, jeer | 反义词: commiserate, sympathize | 词性: noun, verb

gleek: /ɡliːk/ | vi. | 傻笑；（古）唱歌；演奏音乐 |  To make a sound expressive of mirth, scorn, or triumph. In archaic usage, to sing or make music. |  She gleeked when she heard the news. 她听到这个消息后傻笑了。 | 近义词: giggle, chuckle, chortle | 反义词: frown, scowl | 词性: verb

词源: From Middle English *gleken, gleken, from Old English *glēcan, from Proto-Germanic *glikōną (“to shine, glitter”).  The modern sense of "gleek" likely developed from the idea of a bright or lively expression accompanying mockery or glee.
