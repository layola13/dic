enterocoele: /ˈentərəˌsiːl/ | n. |肠腔；真体腔|A body cavity formed by the outpouching of the primitive gut (archenteron).  It is characteristic of deuterostomes, such as echinoderms, chordates, and hemichordates.|The starfish has a well-developed enterocoele.|Synonyms: coelom, true coelom |Antonyms: pseudocoelom, acoelomate|noun


enterocoelous: /ˌentərəˈsiːləs/ | adj. |肠腔的；真体腔的|Relating to or possessing an enterocoele, a body cavity formed by the outpouching of the primitive gut.|The development of an enterocoelous body cavity is a key characteristic of deuterostomes.|Synonyms: coelomate, eucoelomate|Antonyms: pseudocoelomate, acoelomate|adjective
