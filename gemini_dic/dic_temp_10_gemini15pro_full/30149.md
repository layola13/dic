celandine: /ˈselənˌdaɪn/ (US), /ˈselənˌdiːn/ (UK) | n. | 白屈菜；金凤花 | A plant with small yellow flowers, of the poppy family. It has yellow sap which is poisonous but has been used in traditional medicine to treat warts and other skin problems. |  The celandine grew wild along the riverbank. 白屈菜沿着河岸疯长。|  The herbalist used celandine to treat the wart. 草药医生用白屈菜来治疗疣。 | 近义词: greater celandine, swallowwort (for the greater celandine species) | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *celidoine, from Late Latin chelidonia, from Latin chelidōnia (herba) "(herb) of the swallow," from Greek khelidónion "swallow," so called because it was believed to bloom when the swallows arrived and wither when they left.
