handcuffing:/ˈhændkʌfɪŋ/
n.
给…戴上手铐；束缚
The act of putting handcuffs on someone; restraining someone.
The police officer began the handcuffing procedure. 警察开始戴手铐的程序。
The handcuffing of the suspect was captured on video.  嫌疑人被戴上手铐的过程被录像拍了下来。
arrest, restraint, shackling
liberation, freeing, release
noun

handcuffs:/ˈhændkʌfs/
n.
手铐
A pair of metal rings connected by a chain, used for restraining someone's wrists.
The police officer placed the handcuffs on the suspect. 警察给嫌疑人戴上了手铐。
The suspect was in handcuffs when he was brought into the courtroom. 嫌疑人被带进法庭时戴着手铐。
manacles, shackles, restraints
freedom, release, liberty
noun

handcuff:/ˈhændkʌf/
vt.
给…戴上手铐；束缚
To restrain someone by putting handcuffs on them; to restrict someone's freedom of action.
The police handcuffed the suspect and took him to the station.  警察给嫌疑人戴上手铐，并把他带到了警察局。
The company's strict rules handcuffed the employees' creativity. 公司严格的规定束缚了员工的创造力。
restrain, manacle, shackle
free, release, liberate
verb
