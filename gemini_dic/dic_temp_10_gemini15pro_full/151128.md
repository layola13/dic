parasymbiotic:/ˌpærəˌsɪmbaɪˈɒtɪk/ | adj. | 准共生的 | Existing in a relationship that resembles symbiosis but in which one organism benefits while the other is unaffected; exhibiting parasymbiosis. |  The relationship between the epiphytic orchid and the tree it grows on is often described as parasymbiotic, as the orchid benefits from the tree's height and access to sunlight, while the tree is neither harmed nor helped. | 例句：The parasymbiotic relationship between the two species allowed one to flourish without impacting the other. 这两个物种之间的准共生关系使得其中一个物种能够繁荣发展而不影响另一个物种。

| 近义词：commensal, commensalistic | 反义词：parasitic, pathogenic | 词性：adjective


Etymology: para- (beside, alongside) + symbiotic (living together)
