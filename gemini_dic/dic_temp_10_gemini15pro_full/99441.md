interest-groups:/ˈɪntrɪst ɡruːps/| n.|利益集团，压力集团|A group of people that seeks to influence public policy on the basis of a particular common interest or concern.|Lobbyists for various interest groups try to influence legislation. 各个利益集团的说客试图影响立法。|Synonyms: pressure groups, lobby groups, advocacy groups|Antonyms: general public, individuals|noun

词源：
"interest" comes from the Old French "interest" (profit, damage, loss), from Latin "interesse" (to be of importance, to concern, to make a difference).
"group" comes from the Italian "gruppo" (knot, mass, group), perhaps from a Germanic source.
