harmful:/ˈhɑːrmfəl/ (US) /ˈhɑːmfʊl/ (UK)| adj.|有害的；危险的|causing or likely to cause harm|Smoking is harmful to your health. 吸烟有害健康。|
The chemical is harmful to the environment. 这种化学物质对环境有害。|
detrimental, damaging, injurious, pernicious|
beneficial, harmless, advantageous, safe|
adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *harmful*, equivalent to harm + -ful.  Harm ultimately derives from Old English *hearm*, meaning "grief, sorrow, hurt, injury," with cognates in other Germanic languages. The suffix *-ful* derives from Old English *-full*, *-ful*, meaning "full of, characterized by."
