nautiloids:/ˈnɔːtɪlɔɪdz/ | n. | 鹦鹉螺类 | Cephalopods of the subclass Nautiloidea, characterized by a coiled or straight shell divided into chambers. |  Nautiloids are ancient marine animals related to octopuses and squid. 鹦鹉螺类是与章鱼和鱿鱼相关的古老海洋动物。|  Fossil nautiloids are common in Paleozoic rocks. 古生代岩石中鹦鹉螺类化石很常见。 | synonyms:  cephalopods, mollusks | antonyms: None | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Modern Latin *Nautiloidea*, from Ancient Greek ναυτίλος (nautílos, “paper nautilus, sailor”) + -oidea (“resembling”).  The Greek word ναυτίλος refers to the Argonauts, sailors on the ship Argo in Greek mythology, and also to the nautilus's supposed ability to sail using its shell as a boat.
