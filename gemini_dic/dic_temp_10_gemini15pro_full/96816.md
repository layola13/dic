inerudite:/ˌɪnɪˈruːdaɪt/ | adj. | 无学问的，不博学的 | Lacking knowledge or learning; unscholarly. | He made an inerudite remark about the complexities of quantum physics, revealing his lack of understanding. 他对量子物理学的复杂性发表了无知的评论，暴露了他缺乏理解。|  Synonyms: ignorant, unlearned, uneducated, illiterate, unlettered | Antonyms: erudite, learned, scholarly, knowledgeable, educated | adjective

Etymology: From Latin *ineruditus*, from *in-* (not) + *eruditus* (learned, educated), past participle of *erudire* (to educate, instruct), from *e-* (out) + *rudis* (rough, untrained).
