overmerciful: /ˌōvərˈmɜːrsɪfəl/ | adj. | 过度仁慈的，过于宽容的 | Excessively merciful; too lenient or forgiving.  | He was overmerciful to his enemies, which ultimately led to his downfall. 他对敌人过于仁慈，最终导致了他的垮台。|  The judge was criticized for being overmerciful to the convicted criminal. 法官因对被定罪的罪犯过于仁慈而受到批评。 | Synonyms: lenient, forgiving, indulgent, compassionate | Antonyms: merciless, ruthless, unforgiving, cruel | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->

From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "merciful" (from Middle English "merciable," from Old French "merciable," ultimately from Latin "merces" meaning "reward, price").
