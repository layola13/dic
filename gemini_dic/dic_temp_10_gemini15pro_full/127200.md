naturalize:/ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪz/ /ˈnætʃrəlaɪz/| v. |使归化；使入籍；移植；引进；使适应|To grant full citizenship to a foreigner.  To introduce (a plant or animal) to a new region and cause it to flourish as if native. To make something seem natural or acceptable.|例句：He became a naturalized citizen of the United States. 他成为了美国公民。

例句：The plant has become naturalized in the area. 这种植物已在该地区生长繁殖。|近义词：acclimatize, adapt, assimilate|反义词: expatriate|词性：动词

naturalization:/ˌnætʃrələˈzeɪʃən/ /ˌnætʃrəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/| n. |归化；入籍；移植；引进|The process of becoming a citizen of a country. The introduction of a plant or animal to a new region.|例句：He applied for naturalization after living in the country for five years. 在这个国家生活了五年后，他申请了入籍。

例句：The naturalization of foreign plants can have unforeseen consequences. 外来植物的引进可能会产生无法预料的后果。|近义词：citizenship, acclimatization|反义词: expatriation|词性：名词
