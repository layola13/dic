convoluted:/ˈkɒnvəluːtɪd/ /ˈkɑːnvəˈluːtɪd/
| adj. | 迂回的，复杂的 | Extremely complex and difficult to follow. |  The lawyer's argument was so convoluted that the jury couldn't understand it. 律师的论证如此复杂，陪审团无法理解。 | intricate, complex, tortuous | simple, straightforward | adjective


Etymology:
From Latin *convolutus*, past participle of *convolvere* "to roll together, roll up," from *com-* "together" (see com-) + *volvere* "to roll" (see voluble).
