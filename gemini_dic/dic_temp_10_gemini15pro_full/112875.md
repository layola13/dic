lucubration:/ˌluːkjuːˈbreɪʃn/| n.|苦心研究；苦思冥想之作|laborious study, especially at night; the product of such study|例句：His latest lucubration is a 500-page treatise on the history of cheese. 他的最新著作是一部关于奶酪历史的500页的论文。

例句：After years of lucubration, she finally finished her masterpiece. 经过多年的潜心研究，她终于完成了她的杰作。|近义词：study, meditation, contemplation, deliberation|反义词: idleness, inactivity|词性:noun

词源: From Latin *lucubrationem*,  from *lucubrare* "to work by lamplight," from *lucubrare*  "to work by lamplight,"  from *lux* "light" + *-brare* (frequentative suffix).
