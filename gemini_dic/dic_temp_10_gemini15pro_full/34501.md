clean-cut:/ˈkliːnˈkʌt/| adj.|整洁的；整齐的；轮廓分明的；(尤指男人)相貌端正的|Having a neat, tidy, and conventionally attractive appearance, especially of men.|He's a clean-cut young man with a bright future. 他是一个相貌端正、前途光明的年轻人。|近义词：tidy, neat, trim, well-groomed|反义词:scruffy, unkempt, messy|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From clean + cut.  Originally referred to meat cleanly cut from the bone. The figurative sense of a person being "clean-cut" arose in the early 20th century.
