half-expectantly: /ˌhɑːf ɪkˈspektəntli/ /ˌhæf ɪkˈspektəntli/
| adv. | 半期待地；略带期盼地 |  In a way that suggests some expectation, but also some doubt or uncertainty.  It combines "half" meaning partially and "expectantly" meaning with anticipation. | She looked at him half-expectantly, hoping he would understand. 她半期待地看着他，希望他能理解。 |  hopefully, anticipatively, with a degree of expectation |  unexpectedly, without expectation | adverb

half: /hɑːf/ /hæf/
| n. adj. adv. | 一半的；半个的；一半地 | One of two equal parts of something. |  She ate half the cake. 她吃了一半的蛋糕。 | half, part, portion | whole, entire | noun, adjective, adverb

expectantly: /ɪkˈspektəntli/
| adv. | 期待地；期望地 | In a way that shows you are hoping for something, especially something good.  Derived from "expectant" which comes from the Latin "expectare" meaning to await. | The children waited expectantly for the magician to begin. 孩子们期待地等待着魔术师开始表演。| hopefully, anticipatively |  apathetically, indifferently | adverb
