manneristically:/ˌmænəˈrɪstɪkli/ | adv. | 方式主义地，矫揉造作地 | In a manner that is exaggerated or affected, often drawing attention to a particular style or manner of behavior.  Characterized by an excessive adherence to a particular style or manner, often to the point of artificiality or affectation. | He delivered his lines manneristically, emphasizing every gesture and inflection. 他矫揉造作地念台词，每一个姿势和语调都刻意强调。 | The artist painted in a manneristically elongated style.  这位艺术家以夸张拉长的方式作画。 | 近义词: affectedly, artificially, pretentiously | 反义词: naturally, genuinely, sincerely | 词性: 副词

词源: manner (n.) + -istic (adj. suffix) + -ally (adv. suffix)  From "manner," meaning a way of doing something, with "-istic" forming an adjective meaning "relating to a particular manner," and "-ally" turning it into an adverb.
