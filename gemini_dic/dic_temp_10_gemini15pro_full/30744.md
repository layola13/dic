cesarean:/sɪˈzɛəriən/ /sɪˈzær.i.ən/ | n. |剖腹产|A surgical operation for delivering a child by cutting through the wall of the abdomen and the uterus.  |例句：She had to have a cesarean because the baby was breech. 她不得不进行剖腹产，因为胎位不正。

|例句：The doctor performed an emergency cesarean. 医生进行了紧急剖腹产。|近义词：C-section |反义词:vaginal birth/delivery|noun


cesareans:/sɪˈzɛəriənz/ /sɪˈzær.i.ənz/ | n. |剖腹产（复数）|Plural form of "cesarean," referring to multiple surgical operations for delivering children by cutting through the wall of the abdomen and the uterus. |例句：The hospital has seen a rise in the number of cesareans performed. 这家医院的剖腹产手术数量有所上升。

|例句：There are risks associated with multiple cesareans. 多次剖腹产存在风险。|近义词：C-sections |反义词:vaginal births/deliveries|noun
