non-native:/ˌnɒnˈneɪtɪv/ (UK) /ˌnɑːnˈneɪtɪv/ (US)| adj. n.|非母语的，非本地产的；非母语人士|Someone who did not grow up speaking a particular language, or a plant or animal that does not naturally live in a particular place. |例句：He speaks English fluently, but he's a non-native speaker. 他英语说得很流利，但他不是母语人士。

例句：The non-native species of plant is crowding out the native flora.  这种非本地植物正在排挤本地植物群。|近义词：foreign, alien, exotic, introduced|反义词：native, indigenous|词性：形容词/名词


Etymology:
From non- + native.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "native" refers to something originating or belonging naturally to a particular place.
