immediatism:/ɪˈmiːdiətɪzəm/| n.|直接主义；立即行动主义|A doctrine that emphasizes the importance of immediate action or results, especially in art or politics.  It often rejects traditional methods or processes in favor of direct, spontaneous expression or action.| The artist's embrace of immediatism led to a series of vibrant, gestural paintings.  这位艺术家对直接主义的拥护导致了一系列充满活力、富有表现力的绘画作品。|近义词：direct action, spontaneity, impulsiveness|反义词：gradualism, deliberation, planning|词性:noun

Etymology:  From immediate + -ism.  "Immediate" comes from Late Latin *immediatus, from in- "not" + mediatus "mediated."
