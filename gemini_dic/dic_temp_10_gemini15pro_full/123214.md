monoclinal:/ˌmɒnəˈklaɪnəl/ | adj. n.|单斜的，单斜层|A geological structure in which rock strata are inclined in a single direction, like a tilted stack of papers.  It represents a simple flexure in otherwise horizontal or gently dipping beds.|The geologist identified the formation as a monoclinal fold.| 近义词:homocline | 反义词: anticline, syncline |词性:形容词, 名词


monoclinal:/ˌmɒnəˈklaɪnəl/ | n.|单斜层|A geological formation characterized by a dip in one direction only.|The Colorado Plateau is known for its monoclinals.| 近义词:homocline | 反义词: anticline, syncline |词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From mono- (one) + -clinal (inclined), ultimately from Greek monos (single, alone) and klinein (to lean, slope).
