gouger:/ˈɡaʊdʒər/| n.| 敲诈者，骗子|Someone who charges excessively high prices for goods or services, especially taking advantage of a shortage or emergency. |例句：The gougers were selling bottled water for ten times the normal price after the hurricane. 飓风过后，这些奸商以十倍于正常价格出售瓶装水。

例句：During the gas shortage, some gas stations turned into gougers, exploiting desperate drivers.  汽油短缺期间，一些加油站变成了敲诈者，剥削绝望的司机。


|近义词：profiteer, extortionist, racketeer|反义词: philanthropist|词性: noun
