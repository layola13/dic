disjunctions:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋkʃənz/| n. |分离，分裂，析取|A disjunction is a lack of connection or a separation between two things. In logic, it refers to a compound statement formed by joining two or more statements with the word "or".|The disjunction between theory and practice can lead to problems. 理论和实践的分离会导致问题。|synonyms: separation, division, split, disunion, gap, chasm, rift, breach, divorce, disconnection, OR (logic)|antonyms: conjunction, union, connection, junction|noun

disjunction:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋkʃən/| n. |逻辑“或”|In logic, a disjunction is a compound statement formed by joining two or more statements with the word "or".  It is true if at least one of the statements is true.|The statement "A or B" is a disjunction.  陈述“A或B”是一个析取。|synonyms: OR (logic), alternation|antonyms: AND (logic), conjunction|noun
