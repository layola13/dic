experimentist:/ɪkˈspɛrɪmɛntɪst/
n.|实验主义者，实验家|A person who performs experiments, especially in science. | The experimentist carefully recorded the results of each trial.  这位实验家仔细记录了每次试验的结果。|Synonym: experimenter, researcher, scientist|Antonym: theorist, theoretician|noun

experimentist:/ɪkˈspɛrɪmɛntɪst/
n.|实验主义者，实验家|Someone who favors experimentation or practical experience over theory. |The experimentist argued that real-world data was more valuable than abstract models.  这位实验主义者认为，真实世界的数据比抽象模型更有价值。|Synonym: pragmatist, empiricist|Antonym: theorist, dogmatist|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From experiment + -ist.  "experiment" comes from Latin experimentum, meaning "trial, proof, test," from experiri "to try, test." "-ist" is a suffix forming agent nouns.
