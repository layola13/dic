conga:/ˈkɒŋɡə/ (UK) /ˈkɑːŋɡə/ (US)| n. |康加舞；康加鼓| A Latin American dance, performed by a line of people following each other, typically performing a series of three steps and a kick. Also refers to the tall, narrow, single-headed drum used to play conga music.|  The band played a lively conga and everyone joined in.  乐队演奏了一曲欢快的康加舞曲，大家都加入了进来。|Synonyms:  conga line,  rumba |Antonyms:  waltz (different style of dance)| noun

conga:/ˈkɒŋɡə/ (UK) /ˈkɑːŋɡə/ (US)| v. |跳康加舞| To dance the conga.| They congaed around the room. 他们在房间里跳起了康加舞。|Synonyms:  dance,  party|Antonyms:  sit, stand still| verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Congolese word *nkunga*, meaning a drum.  The dance originated in Cuba and takes its name from the drum used to play the music.
