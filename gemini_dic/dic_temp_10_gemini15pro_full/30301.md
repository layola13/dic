cenobitism:/ˈsiːnəbaɪtɪzəm/| n. |修道院生活; 共居生活 |The practice of living in a community, especially in a monastery or convent.  A communal life, especially of monks or nuns.|  Early Christian cenobitism involved strict adherence to rules and rituals. 早期基督教的修道生活包括严格遵守规则和仪式。| The monastery is a place of cenobitism and prayer.  这座修道院是修道和祈祷的地方。 |  monasticism, cloistered life | secular life, hermitage | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *coenobitēs*, from Ancient Greek *κοινοβίτης* (koinobítēs, “one who lives in community”), from *κοινός* (koinós, “common”) + *βίος* (bíos, “life”).
