danger:/ˈdeɪndʒər/ (US) /ˈdeɪndʒə(r)/ (UK)| n.|危险，风险，威胁|Exposure to radiation is a danger to health. 暴露于辐射中对健康有害。|The crumbling cliff edge posed a real danger to walkers. 摇摇欲坠的悬崖边缘对步行者构成了真正的危险。|近义词：peril, hazard, risk, threat|反义词：safety, security|词性:名词

词源: From Old French *dangier "power to harm, domination, peril, difficulty" (12c.), from Late Latin *dominiarium "power of a lord," from Latin dominus "lord, master." The semantic evolution in Old French involved the senses of "power of a lord," then "arrogance," then "resistance, hostility," then "difficult situation," then "exposure to harm."
