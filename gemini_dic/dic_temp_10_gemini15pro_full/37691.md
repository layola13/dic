ETYMOLOGY->
From companionable + -ness.  "companionable" comes from "companion" (from Old French "compagnon," meaning "one who shares bread," from Latin "com-" (with) + "panis" (bread)) + "-able" (suffix meaning "capable of" or "worthy of").  "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition.
