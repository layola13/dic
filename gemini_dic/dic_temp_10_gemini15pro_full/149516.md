paeon:/ˈpiːən/ | n. | 诗歌中的一种音步； pæon |A metrical foot in poetry consisting of one long or stressed syllable followed by three short or unstressed syllables, or (less often) of three short syllables followed by one long.  A celebratory song or hymn, especially one dedicated to Apollo. | The poet used a paeon in the third line of the verse.  诗人在诗歌的第三行使用了 pæon。|  The ancient Greeks sang paeons to Apollo. 古希腊人向阿波罗唱赞歌。 | synonym: foot, meter, hymn, ode | antonym:  (No direct antonym for a metrical foot) | noun
