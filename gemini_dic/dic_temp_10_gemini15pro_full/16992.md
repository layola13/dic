bastion:/ˈbæstʃən/ | n. |堡垒，防御工事，(喻)捍卫者，主要支持者 | a projecting part of a fortification built at an angle to the line of a wall, so as to allow defensive fire in several directions. (A fortified place.)  A person, group, or principle strongly defending or upholding something. | The old castle stands as a bastion against the modern world.  这座古老的城堡屹立着，成为了对抗现代世界的堡垒。| The library was a bastion of free speech in the community.  图书馆是社区言论自由的堡垒。 | synonyms: stronghold, bulwark, citadel, fortress, rampart, defender, advocate, champion | antonyms: vulnerability, weakness | noun
