dellacruscan: /ˌdɛləˈkrʌskən/ | adj. n.|德拉克鲁西派的；德拉克鲁西派诗人|Of or relating to a school of sentimental poets of the late 18th century, characterized by excessive sentimentality and ornate diction.  A member of this school.  Derived from the Accademia della Crusca in Florence, Italy, whose name means "academy of the bran" (sifting out impurities).|Her poetry was criticized for being too dellacruscan and lacking substance. 她的诗歌被批评为过于德拉克鲁西派，缺乏实质内容。| The young poet, enamored with flowery language, embraced the dellacruscan style wholeheartedly.  这位年轻的诗人，醉心于华丽的辞藻，全心全意地接受了德拉克鲁西派的风格。 | 近义词:sentimental, mawkish, affected, florid| 反义词:austere, simple, unaffected|词性: adjective, noun
