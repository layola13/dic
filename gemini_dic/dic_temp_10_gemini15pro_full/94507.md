importunate:/ɪmˈpɔːrtʃənət/ /ɪmˈpɔːrtənət/ | adj. | 缠扰不休的；再三恳求的；持续不断的 | Persistent, especially to the point of annoyance or intrusion.  Making repeated or persistent requests, especially in a troublesome way. | The importunate beggar followed them for blocks.  那个纠缠不休的乞丐跟着他们走了好几个街区。 | Synonyms: persistent, insistent, tenacious, relentless, demanding | Antonyms: reserved, diffident, hesitant, unobtrusive, unassuming | adjective

importunately:/ɪmˈpɔːrtʃənətli/ /ɪmˈpɔːrtənətli/ | adv. | 缠扰不休地；再三恳求地 | In an importunate manner; persistently. |  He knocked importunately on the door until someone answered. 他不停地敲门，直到有人来应门。 | Synonyms: persistently, insistently, relentlessly | Antonyms: reservedly, diffidently, hesitantly | adverb

importunateness:/ɪmˈpɔːrtʃənətnəs/ /ɪmˈpɔːrtənətnəs/ | n. | 缠扰不休；再三恳求 | The quality or state of being importunate; persistence to the point of annoyance. |  Her importunateness finally wore him down, and he agreed to help. 她没完没了的请求最终使他屈服了，他同意帮忙。 | Synonyms: persistence, insistence, tenacity | Antonyms: reserve, diffidence, hesitation | noun

词源:  Late Middle English: from Old French importun, from Latin importunus ‘inconvenient, unsuitable’, from in- ‘not’ + portus ‘harbour, haven’.
