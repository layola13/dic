词源:  From Middle English *noyous, from Anglo-Norman noious, noiouse, from Old French noiouse, feminine of noios (“troublesome, annoying”), from Late Latin inodiōsus (“troublesome”), from Latin inodiōsus (“full of hatred, offensive”), from inodī (“to cause hatred”), from in- + ōdī (“to hate”).
