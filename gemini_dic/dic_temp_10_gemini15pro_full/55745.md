diverge:/daɪˈvɜːrdʒ/ (US), /daɪˈvɜːdʒ/ (UK)| v. | 分歧，偏离，岔开 | to separate and go in different directions; to become different | The two roads diverge at the crossroads. 这两条路在十字路口岔开。| synonyms: deviate, separate, branch off | antonyms: converge, meet, unite | verb

divergence:/daɪˈvɜːrdʒəns/ (US), /daɪˈvɜːdʒəns/ (UK)| n. | 分歧，差异，背离 | the act or process of diverging | There's a divergence of opinion on this issue. 在这个问题上意见存在分歧。| synonyms: difference, deviation, discrepancy | antonyms: convergence, agreement, unity | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *divergere*, present active infinitive of *divergo* ("I diverge, I go apart"), from *di-* ("apart, asunder") + *vergo* ("I incline, I tend").
