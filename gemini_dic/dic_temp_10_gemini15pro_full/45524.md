curfew: /ˈkɜːrfjuː/ (US) /ˈkɜːfjuː/ (UK)| n. |宵禁; 晚钟; 规定儿童必须回家时间的法令|A regulation requiring people to remain indoors between specified hours, typically at night.|例句：The city imposed a curfew after the riots.  这座城市在骚乱后实施了宵禁。

例句：The curfew requires everyone to be indoors by 10 pm. 宵禁要求每个人晚上10点前待在室内。


|近义词：restriction, prohibition, ban|反义词:freedom, liberty|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *cuevrefeu, literally "cover fire," from cuevre (couvrir, "to cover") + feu ("fire").  Originally referred to the practice of covering fires at night to prevent fires from spreading.
