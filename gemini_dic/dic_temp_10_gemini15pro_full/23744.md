bravado:/brəˈvɑːdoʊ/ (US) /brəˈvɑːdəʊ/ (UK)| n.|虚张声势，逞能|A show of boldness intended to impress or intimidate.|He displayed a false bravado, masking his insecurity. 他故作勇敢，掩饰内心的不安。|例句：His bravado impressed the other boys. 他的虚张声势给其他男孩留下了深刻印象。|例句：Despite his bravado, he was actually quite nervous. 尽管他装作勇敢，实际上他非常紧张。|近义词：swagger, bluster, boastfulness|反义词：timidity, humility, modesty|词性:名词

bravado:/brəˈvɑːdoʊ/ (US) /brəˈvɑːdəʊ/ (UK)| n.|逞能|An ostentatious display of courage.|The matador's bravado in the face of the bull was thrilling. 斗牛士面对公牛的勇敢令人激动。|例句：Her bravado hid a deep fear of failure.  她逞强掩饰了内心深处对失败的恐惧。|例句：He entered the room with a bravado he didn't feel. 他故作镇定地走进房间，其实内心忐忑不安。|近义词：swagger, bluster, boastfulness|反义词：timidity, humility, modesty|词性:名词
