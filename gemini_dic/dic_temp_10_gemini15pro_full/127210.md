naughtier:/ˈnɔːtiər/ (US) /ˈnɔːtɪə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更淘气的，更顽皮的|Comparative form of "naughty," meaning more disobedient, mischievous, or badly behaved.|例句：My younger brother is naughtier than me. 我的弟弟比我更淘气。|例句：The puppy was naughtier today than yesterday. 这只小狗今天比昨天更淘气。|近义词：more mischievous, more disobedient, worse behaved|反义词：better behaved, more obedient, less mischievous|词性:形容词

naughty:/ˈnɔːti/ (US) /ˈnɔːti/ (UK)| adj.|淘气的，顽皮的|Disobedient, mischievous, or badly behaved.|词源: From Middle English *naughti*, from *naught* ("nothing") + *-i* (adjective suffix). Originally meaning "worthless," the sense shifted to "wicked" and then to "mischievous."|
