colchicine: /ˈkɒltʃɪsiːn/ (UK) /ˈkoʊltʃɪsiːn/ (US) | n. | 秋水仙碱 | An alkaloid drug, originally extracted from the autumn crocus (Colchicum autumnale), used to treat gout and familial Mediterranean fever.  It inhibits cell division by preventing the formation of microtubules. | Colchicine is commonly prescribed for gout attacks. 秋水仙碱通常用于治疗痛风发作。 | Colchicine is effective in relieving the pain of gout. 秋水仙碱能有效缓解痛风疼痛。 | 近义词: (for gout treatment) allopurinol, febuxostat | 反义词: None (no direct pharmacological opposite) | 词性: noun

Etymology: From French *colchique* "autumn crocus, colchicum," from Latin *colchicum*, from Greek *Kolkhikon (pharmakon)*, literally "(poison) of Colchis," referring to the region east of the Black Sea where the meadow saffron was said to grow.  Colchis was the mythical land where Medea, a sorceress skilled in the use of poisons, lived.
