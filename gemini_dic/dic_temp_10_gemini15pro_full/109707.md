leveche:/ləˈveʃ/ /ləˈvɛtʃ/| n.|热风，焚风，莱万特风|A hot, dry, dust-laden southeasterly wind blowing from the Sahara across southern Spain and the Mediterranean.|The leveche brought with it a suffocating blanket of dust. 莱万特风带来了令人窒息的尘土。|近义词：sirocco, harmattan, foehn|反义词:breeze, northerly wind|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Spanish *leveche*, from Arabic *لَبِيش* (*labīš*), from Latin *libycus* (“Libyan, southwest wind”). Ultimately from Ancient Greek *Λίβυς* (*Líbus*), *Λιβύη* (*Libúē*) ("Libya").
