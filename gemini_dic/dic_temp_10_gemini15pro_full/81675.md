greenbacker:/ˈɡriːnˌbækər/| n.|绿背党人；支持绿钞运动者|A supporter of the Greenback Party, a political party in the United States in the late 19th century that advocated for the issuance of paper money not backed by gold.|The greenbackers believed that increasing the money supply would help farmers and other working people. 绿背党人认为，增加货币供应量将有助于农民和其他劳动人民。|Synonyms: inflationist, agrarian|Antonyms: goldbug|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "greenback" (referring to the color of the paper money) + "-er" (suffix forming agent nouns).  The Greenback Party advocated for the continued use of paper money ("greenbacks") issued during the Civil War, as opposed to returning to a gold standard.
