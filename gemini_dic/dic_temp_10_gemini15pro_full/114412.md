make heads roll:/meɪk hɛdz roʊl/
|idiomatic phrase|
|使人丢官；使人付出代价；引起震动|
|To cause significant consequences, often involving dismissals or demotions of people in positions of authority.|
|例句：The new CEO came in and made heads roll, firing several top executives. 新任首席执行官上任后大刀阔斧，解雇了几名高管。|

|例句：The scandal is going to make heads roll in the government. 这起丑闻将使政府官员付出代价。|

|近义词：cause repercussions, bring down the house, shake things up|
|反义词：maintain the status quo, keep things as they are|
|phrase|

ETYMOLOGY->
This idiom likely originated from the French Revolution, where the guillotine was used to execute people, literally making their heads roll. While the exact origin is uncertain, the imagery of heads rolling evokes a sense of dramatic and swift consequences.
