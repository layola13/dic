aleck:/ˈælɪk/| n.|自作聪明的人，爱抬杠的人|A person, especially a man, who is regarded as annoyingly clever and self-assured.|He's such an aleck, always showing off how much he knows.|近义词:smart aleck, know-it-all, wise guy|反义词:fool, simpleton|词性:名词

词源:
From the given name Aleck, a diminutive of Alexander, which by the late 19th century began to be used ironically to refer to someone who is overly self-assured and clever in an irritating way.
