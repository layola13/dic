ETYMOLOGY->
From Malay *kadi "judge, official," which became *kati in the pidgin forms. Borrowed into English via Portuguese *catti. Originally referred to an Indian or Malaysian assistant or messenger, later specifically to a tea caddy and then to a golf caddy.
