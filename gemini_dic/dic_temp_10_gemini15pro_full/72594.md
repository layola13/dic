fly-by-night:/ˈflaɪ baɪ naɪt/| adj. n.|不可靠的，不诚实的；骗子，无赖|A fly-by-night is someone or something that is unreliable and likely to disappear, especially owing money or failing to fulfill an agreement. It describes a business or person that is not trustworthy and likely to cheat people.|例句：Be careful dealing with that fly-by-night car salesman; he might sell you a lemon. 小心和那个不靠谱的汽车销售员打交道；他可能会卖给你一辆次品车。

例句：They set up a fly-by-night operation and disappeared with the investors' money. 他们建立了一个骗人的公司，卷走了投资者的钱。|近义词：untrustworthy, unreliable, shady, dishonest, disreputable|反义词：reliable, trustworthy, reputable, honest|词性：形容词，名词

fly-by-nights:/ˈflaɪ baɪ naɪts/| n.|骗子们，无赖们|Plural form of fly-by-night, referring to multiple unreliable and dishonest people or businesses.|例句：The town was plagued by fly-by-nights who preyed on unsuspecting tourists.  这个小镇被那些专门欺骗毫无戒心游客的骗子们所困扰。

例句：Beware of fly-by-nights offering quick riches; they are often scams.  要小心那些提供快速致富机会的骗子；他们通常是骗局。|近义词：swindlers, fraudsters, cheats, con artists|反义词：philanthropists, benefactors|词性：名词
