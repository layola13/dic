ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *brigge*, of uncertain origin, probably from Old North French *brique*, *brigue* (“brig, brigantine”), perhaps ultimately from Proto-Germanic *brugją* (“bridge”).  The sense may have developed from a type of ship used to defend bridges.  Compare Dutch *brik*.
