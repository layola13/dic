monopolylogue:/məˈnɒpəliˌlɒɡ/| n. |独白;垄断性独白|A long speech made by one person, especially one which prevents other people from speaking or being heard.  A speech, article, etc. that represents only one viewpoint, especially one that dominates or excludes others.|The CEO's annual address often feels like a monopolylogue, with no opportunity for employee feedback.|近义词：monologue, soliloquy|反义词:dialogue, conversation|词性:noun

Etymology:
From *monopolo-* (combining form of *monopoly*) + *-logue* (from Greek *logos* "speech, word, discourse").  The term highlights not just a single speaker, but the act of that speaker dominating the conversation and preventing others from contributing.
