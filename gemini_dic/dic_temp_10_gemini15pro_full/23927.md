breakthrough: /ˈbreɪkθruː/
n.
突破；重大进展；突破性进展
A sudden, important discovery or development that helps to improve a situation or provide an answer to a problem.
Scientists have made a major breakthrough in the treatment of cancer. 科学家们在癌症治疗方面取得了重大突破。

breakthrough: /ˈbreɪkθruː/
n.
突破；突破点
An act of overcoming or penetrating an obstacle or restriction.
The army made a breakthrough in the enemy lines. 军队突破了敌人的防线。


English Explanation:  A significant development or achievement that removes a barrier and allows for further progress.  This can be in science, technology, medicine, or any other field.  It can also refer to a military advance through enemy lines.

Synonyms: advance, development, discovery, innovation, success, victory

Antonyms: setback, failure, obstacle, hindrance, impasse

Etymology:  From "break" + "through".  "Break" meaning to shatter, fracture, or overcome, and "through" meaning from one side to the other of (something).
