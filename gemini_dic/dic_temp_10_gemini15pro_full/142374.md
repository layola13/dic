orthoptera:/ɔrˈθɑːptərə/ | n. | 直翅目昆虫 | An order of insects that comprises grasshoppers, locusts, crickets, etc., characterized by biting mouthparts, two pairs of wings (the front pair often thickened), and strong hind legs for jumping. |  Example: Grasshoppers and crickets belong to the order Orthoptera. 蚱蜢和蟋蟀属于直翅目。| Synonym:  None (as it's a taxonomic classification) | Antonym: None (as it's a taxonomic classification) | Noun
