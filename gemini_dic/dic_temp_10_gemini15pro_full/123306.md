monoglyceride:/ˌmɒnəʊˈɡlɪsəraɪd/ /ˌmɑːnəˈɡlɪsəˌraɪd/| n. |单甘油酯|A glyceride formed by the esterification of one fatty acid molecule with glycerol.  It is an emulsifier, meaning it helps oil and water to mix.|Monoglycerides are often used in food processing. 单甘油酯常用于食品加工。|Synonym: monoacylglycerol|Antonym: triglyceride|noun
