hoarding:/ˈhɔːrdɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈhɔːrdɪŋ/ (US)| n. |囤积物; 围板; 招贴板|A large board in a public place, used to display advertisements.  A supply or accumulation that is hidden or carefully guarded for preservation, future use, etc. |例句：The company put up hoardings around the construction site. 公司在工地周围竖起了围板。

例句：He had a hoarding of old newspapers in his attic.  他的阁楼里囤积着旧报纸。|近义词：stockpile, cache, store, billboard, signboard|反义词:lack, shortage|词性:noun
