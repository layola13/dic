infamies:/ˈɪnfəmɪz/
| n. |恶行，丑行；声名狼藉|Extremely wicked or cruel acts; the state of being infamous.|The dictator was responsible for countless infamies.|近义词：atrocities, villainies, outrages, misdeeds|反义词：virtues, noble deeds, heroic acts|词性:noun


infamy:/ˈɪnfəmi/
| n. |恶行，丑行；声名狼藉|The state of being well known for some bad quality or deed.|He achieved infamy for his crimes.|近义词：notoriety, disgrace, dishonor|反义词：fame, renown, honor|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *infamie*, from Latin *infamia* ("ill repute, dishonor, disgrace"), from *in-* ("not") + *fama* ("fame, reputation"). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\fā-*, meaning "to speak".
