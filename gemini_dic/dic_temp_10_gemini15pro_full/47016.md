dastard:/ˈdæstərd/| n. |懦夫，卑鄙的人|A cowardly and treacherous person.|The dastard who betrayed his comrades was despised by all.  这个背叛了他同志的懦夫被所有人鄙视。|Synonyms: coward, craven, poltroon, villain|Antonyms: hero, brave|noun
dastardly:/ˈdæstərdli/| adj. |卑鄙的，懦弱的|Cowardly and treacherous.|The dastardly attack on the innocent villagers shocked the nation. 对无辜村民的卑鄙袭击震惊了全国。|Synonyms: cowardly, treacherous, base, vile|Antonyms: heroic, brave, noble|adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *dastard, from Old French *dastart, possibly from a combination of daze (to daze, stun) and start (to startle). The suffix -ard is pejorative.
