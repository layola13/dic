complacency:/kəmˈpleɪsənsi/
n.|自满，沾沾自喜，满足|A feeling of smug satisfaction with one's achievements, possessions, or situation. |Example: The team's complacency after their early victory led to their downfall in the finals.  该队在早期胜利后的自满导致了他们在决赛中的失败。

Example: His complacency about his health was shattered when he received the diagnosis. 他对自己健康的沾沾自喜在他得到诊断结果时破灭了。|近义词:satisfaction, smugness, self-satisfaction|反义词:anxiety, insecurity, humility|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Late Latin complacentia, from Latin complacēns, present participle of complacēre (“to please greatly”), from com- (“with, together”) + placēre (“to please”).
