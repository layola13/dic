climbdown:/ˈklaɪmdaʊn/| n.|让步，妥协，退却|An act of admitting that you were wrong or of changing your position on something, especially because you have been forced to.|The government was forced into a climbdown on its tax proposals. 政府被迫在其税收提案上做出让步。|近义词：concession, retreat, backdown, compromise|反义词：insistence, refusal, resistance|词性:名词

climbdown:/ˈklaɪmdaʊn/| v.|让步，妥协，退却|To admit that you were wrong or to change your position on something, especially because you have been forced to.|The president had to climb down on his earlier statements. 总统不得不放弃他早先的声明。|近义词：concede, retreat, back down, compromise|反义词：insist, refuse, resist|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the literal sense of "climbing down" from a high position, either literally (e.g., from a tree or wall) or figuratively (e.g., from a position of power or authority).  The figurative sense developed in the late 19th century.
