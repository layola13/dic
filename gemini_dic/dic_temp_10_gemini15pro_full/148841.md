overwar:/ˈoʊvərˌwɔːr/| adj.|过度交战的，过度战争化的|Excessively focused on or preoccupied with war, often to the detriment of other important considerations.  Characterized by an excessive emphasis on military solutions and a disregard for the consequences of prolonged or excessive conflict.|The country's overwar stance made it difficult to negotiate peace.  这个国家过度战争化的立场使其难以进行和平谈判。

The overwar rhetoric fueled public fear and discouraged diplomatic efforts. 过度战争化的言论加剧了公众的恐惧，并阻碍了外交努力。|近义词：militaristic, belligerent, hawkish|反义词:pacifistic, dovish, peaceful|词性:形容词
词源: "Over-" (prefix indicating excess) + "war" (noun).  This word is a relatively modern formation, likely arising in the late 20th or early 21st century to describe the increasingly prevalent focus on military solutions and the normalization of perpetual conflict.
