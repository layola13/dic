conservatism:/kənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/| n.|保守主义；保守性|A political or theological orientation advocating the preservation of the best in society and opposing radical changes.|Example 1: Her conservatism prevented her from accepting the new ideas. 她的保守主义使她无法接受新思想。

Example 2:  The party's conservatism appealed to older voters. 该党的保守主义吸引了年长的选民。|Synonyms: traditionalism, orthodoxy|Antonyms: radicalism, liberalism|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *conservatisme*, from *conserver* "to preserve," from Latin *conservare* "to keep, preserve," from *com-* "together" (see com-) + *servare* "to keep, guard" (from PIE root *ser- (1) "to protect").
