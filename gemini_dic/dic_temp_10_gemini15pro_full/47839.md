decayer:/dɪˈkeɪər/| n.|腐烂物，衰败物; 衰变体|Something that decays or causes decay.  A substance undergoing decay, especially radioactive decay.|The decayer of the wood was a fungus.  这种木材的腐烂物是一种真菌。|Synonyms: decomposer,  rotter,  putrefier|Antonyms: preserver,  restorer|noun
decay:/dɪˈkeɪ/| v. n.|腐烂，衰败; 衰变|To rot or decompose; to decline in health, strength, or vigor. The process of rotting or decomposition.|The fruit began to decay after a few days. 水果几天后开始腐烂。|Synonyms: decompose, rot, deteriorate, decline|Antonyms: grow, flourish, thrive, improve|verb/noun
decayed:/dɪˈkeɪd/| adj.|腐烂的，衰败的|Having rotted or decomposed; being in a state of decline.|The decayed tooth had to be extracted.  这颗蛀牙必须拔掉。|Synonyms: rotten, decomposed, deteriorated,  dilapidated|Antonyms: fresh, healthy, sound,  intact|adjective
