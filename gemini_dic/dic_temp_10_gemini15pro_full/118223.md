mercantilist: /ˈmɜːrkəntɪlɪst/ (US) /ˈmɜːkəntaɪlɪst/ (UK)| n. adj.| 重商主义者(n.)，重商主义的(adj.)| A believer in mercantilism, an economic system prevalent from the 16th to 18th centuries that promoted governmental regulation of a nation's economy for the purpose of augmenting state power at the expense of rival national powers. It was characterized by government intervention to secure a surplus in the balance of trade by restricting imports and encouraging exports.|  The mercantilist policies of the 17th century aimed to increase national wealth through trade. 17世纪的重商主义政策旨在通过贸易增加国家财富。|  As an adjective:  Mercantilist ideas shaped European economic policy for centuries. 重商主义思想塑造了几个世纪的欧洲经济政策。 | 近义词: protectionist, bullionist | 反义词: free trader | 词性: noun, adjective
