long-suffering:/ˌlɔːŋˈsʌfərɪŋ/
adjective
长期受苦的，忍耐的
Patient and tolerant, especially of hardship or annoyance over a long period.
She was long-suffering with her demanding children. 她对苛刻的孩子们很有耐心。
Synonyms: patient, forbearing, tolerant, enduring, stoic
Antonyms: impatient, intolerant, irritable, short-tempered
词性: adjective

long-suffering:/ˌlɔːŋˈsʌfərɪŋ/
noun
长期受苦，忍耐
The state or quality of being patient and tolerant, especially of hardship or annoyance over a long period.
Her long-suffering was finally rewarded. 她的长期忍耐最终得到了回报。
Synonyms: patience, forbearance, tolerance, endurance, stoicism
Antonyms: impatience, intolerance, irritability, short temper
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From long + suffering.  "Long" emphasizes the extended duration of the suffering endured.  "Suffering" comes from the Old French "sofrir" and ultimately from the Latin "sufferre" meaning "to bear, endure, undergo."
