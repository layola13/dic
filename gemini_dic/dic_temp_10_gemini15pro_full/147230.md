overmeddling: /ˌoʊvərˈmɛdlɪŋ/ | adj. | 过分干预的，多管闲事的 | Excessively interfering in the affairs of others; meddling too much.  | She has an overmeddling aunt who always tries to run her life. 她有一个爱管闲事的阿姨，总是试图操纵她的人生。|  He resented his neighbor's overmeddling in his family matters. 他很讨厌邻居对他家事的过度干预。 |  interfering, intrusive, meddlesome, officious | noninterfering, respectful, unobtrusive | adjective

Etymology:
From over- + meddle + -ing.  "Over-" intensifies the meaning of "meddle," which comes from Old French *medler (modern French mêler), meaning "to mix, mingle." The "-ing" suffix creates the present participle form, which is used here as an adjective.
