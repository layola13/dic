claus:/klɔːz/| n.|圣诞老人；(Santa Claus的简称)|Santa Claus, a legendary figure who is said to bring gifts to children on Christmas Eve.|例句：The children were excited to see Claus. 孩子们很兴奋能见到圣诞老人。

例句：Claus came down the chimney and left presents under the tree. 圣诞老人从烟囱下来，把礼物放在树下。

|近义词：Santa, Father Christmas, Saint Nicholas, Kris Kringle|反义词:None|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Dutch *Klaas*, a short form of *Nikolaas*, from Late Latin *Nicolaus*, from Greek *Νικόλαος (Nikólaos)*, meaning "victory of the people".  This ultimately refers to Saint Nicholas, a 4th-century Greek bishop of Myra in Asia Minor (modern-day Turkey), known for his generosity.
