over-logicalness:/ˌoʊvərˈlɒdʒɪkəlnəs/ | n. | 过分逻辑化；过分理性 | The state of being excessively logical or rational, sometimes to the detriment of practicality or emotional considerations. | His over-logicalness prevented him from seeing the bigger picture. 他的过分逻辑化使他看不到全局。| synonyms: over-rationality, excessive logic | antonyms: irrationality, emotionalism | noun

over-logical:/ˌoʊvərˈlɒdʒɪkəl/ | adj. | 过分逻辑的；过分理性的 | Excessively logical or rational, sometimes to the detriment of practicality or emotional considerations. | Her over-logical approach to problem-solving sometimes made her seem cold and unfeeling. 她过分逻辑的解决问题的方法有时让她显得冷漠无情。| synonyms: over-rational, excessively logical | antonyms: irrational, emotional | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"over-" (prefix): from Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond, excessively."
"logical": from Late Middle English "logicale,"  from Old French "logique," ultimately from Greek "logikos" meaning "of or pertaining to reason or speech."
"-ness" (suffix): from Old English "-nes(s)," forming nouns expressing state, quality, or condition.
