magistrate:/ˈmædʒɪstreɪt/ | n. | 地方法官；治安法官 | A civil officer who administers the law, especially one who conducts a court dealing with minor offenses and holds preliminary hearings of serious cases. |  The magistrate sentenced the thief to six months in jail. 地方法官判处小偷六个月监禁。| synonyms: judge, justice, official, administrator | antonyms: criminal, lawbreaker | noun

magistracy:/ˈmædʒɪstrəsi/ | n. | 地方法官的职位；地方司法官的职权 | The office or authority of a magistrate. | He held the magistracy for many years. 他担任地方法官多年。| synonyms: judgeship, justiceship, office, authority | antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *magistrat*, from Latin *magistrātus* ("magistracy, office of a magistrate, magistrate, public office, dignity, authority"), from *magister* ("master, chief, director, president, teacher, superintendent"), from *magis* ("more").
