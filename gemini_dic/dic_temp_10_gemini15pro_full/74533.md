fraternize: /ˈfrætərnaɪz/ (US) /ˈfrætənaɪz/ (UK) | vi. | 交往；亲善；结交(尤指与敌方或社会地位较低者) | To associate or form a friendship with someone, especially when it is disapproved of because of their lower social rank or enemy status. | The soldiers were warned not to fraternize with the locals. 士兵们被警告不要与当地人 fraternize.| Synonyms: associate, socialize, mingle | Antonyms: shun, avoid | Verb


fraternization: /ˌfrætərnɪˈzeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌfrætənɪˈzeɪʃən/ (UK) | n. | 交往；亲善；结交 | The act of associating or forming friendships, especially with people of a different social group or with enemies. | Fraternization between the officers and enlisted men was discouraged.  军官和士兵之间的 fraternization 是不被鼓励的。 | Synonyms: association, socialization, mingling | Antonyms: segregation, isolation | Noun
