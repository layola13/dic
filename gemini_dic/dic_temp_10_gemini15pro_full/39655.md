conservative:/kənˈsɜːrvətɪv/ | adj. n.|保守的，守旧的；保守主义者|Averse to change or innovation and holding traditional values.  Characterized by caution or moderation. |  He has a conservative approach to investment. 他对投资持保守态度。| synonyms: traditional, conventional, cautious, moderate | antonyms: radical, liberal, progressive | adjective
conservative:/kənˈsɜːrvətɪv/ | n. |保守主义者；保守党人|A person who is averse to change and holds traditional values. |  The conservatives are likely to oppose the new policy. 保守党人可能会反对这项新政策。 | synonyms: traditionalist, right-winger | antonyms: radical, liberal, progressive | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *conservatyf*, from Late Latin *conservātīvus* ("preservative"), from Latin *conservātus*, past participle of *conservāre* ("to preserve, keep, maintain"), from *con-* ("together") + *servāre* ("to keep, guard, preserve").
