cuttlefish:/ˈkʌt(ə)lfɪʃ/| n. |乌贼；墨鱼|A marine mollusk with eight arms and two tentacles, related to the octopus and squid, that can eject an inky fluid to confuse predators and escape.|例句：The cuttlefish changed color to blend in with the coral. 乌贼改变颜色以融入珊瑚中。

例句：Cuttlefish are known for their intelligence and camouflage abilities.  乌贼以其智力和伪装能力而闻名。|近义词：squid, cephalopod|反义词:N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cudele, from Proto-Germanic *kutilō, from Latin cucullus (“hood, cowl”) + fish.  The name refers to the cuttlebone, the animal's internal shell, which resembles a monk's hood.
