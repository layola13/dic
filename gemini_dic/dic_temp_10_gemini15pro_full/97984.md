inoculating:/ɪˈnɒkjʊleɪtɪŋ/
verb
接种，灌输

The doctor is inoculating the child against measles. 医生正在给孩子接种麻疹疫苗。
近义词: vaccinating, immunizing, injecting
反义词: infecting, contaminating
词性: Verb present participle


inoculate:/ɪˈnɒkjʊleɪt/
verb
接种，灌输
introduce (an infective agent) into an organism.
The patients were inoculated with a vaccine. 病人们接种了疫苗。
近义词: vaccinate, immunize, inject
反义词: infect, contaminate
词性: Verb


inoculation:/ɪˌnɒkjʊˈleɪʃən/
noun
接种，灌输
The introduction of a pathogen or antigen into a living organism to stimulate the production of antibodies.
The inoculation against smallpox was successful. 天花疫苗接种成功了。
近义词: vaccination, immunization, injection
反义词: infection, contamination
词性: Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin inoculatus, past participle of inoculare (“to engraft, implant, inoculate”), from in- (“in, into”) + oculus (“eye, bud”).  Originally referred to grafting a bud onto a tree. The medical sense developed later, based on the analogy of introducing a foreign substance.
