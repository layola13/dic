crithomancy:/ˈkrɪθəˌmænsi/| n.|占卜麦术；谷物占卜|Divination by observing the meal or flour offered to a god or spirit, or by interpreting the movement of grains scattered on a sacrificial victim.|The ancient Greeks practiced crithomancy to predict the outcome of battles.|近义词：divination, augury, prophecy|反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "scientific analysis")|词性:noun
Etymology: From Greek *krithe* (barley) + *manteia* (divination).
