demoralization:/diːˌmɒrəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ /diːˌmɔːrələˈzeɪʃn/
n.
士气低落，泄气
The state of having lost confidence or hope; discouragement.
The team's demoralization was evident after their third consecutive loss.  该队在连续三次失利后士气明显低落。
The constant criticism led to his demoralization.  不断的批评导致他灰心丧气。
近义词:discouragement, dejection, despondency
反义词:encouragement, inspiration, morale
词性:名词


demoralize:/diːˈmɒrəlaɪz/ /diːˈmɔːrəlaɪz/
vt.
使士气低落，使泄气
To cause (someone) to lose confidence or hope; discourage.
The loss demoralized the entire team.  这场失利使整个团队士气低落。
The news of the layoffs demoralized the staff.  裁员的消息使员工们感到沮丧。
近义词:discourage, dishearten, dispirit
反义词:encourage, hearten, inspire
词性:动词


词源:
From French *démoraliser*, from dé- (expressing removal) + moraliser (“to moralize”), from moral (“moral”).  来自法语 *démoraliser*，由 dé-（表示移除）+ moraliser（“使道德化”）+ moral（“道德的”）组成。
