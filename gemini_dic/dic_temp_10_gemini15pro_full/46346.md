dactylet:/ˈdæktɪlɪt/| n.|指节, 指形物, (韵律学)长短格|A dactylet is a metrical foot consisting of one long syllable followed by two short syllables, or (in quantitative verse) one stressed syllable followed by two unstressed syllables.  It's also a small finger-like or toe-like part.|例句：The poet used dactylets throughout the verse. 诗人整首诗都用了长短格。

例句：The fossilized dactylet of the dinosaur was remarkably preserved.  这只恐龙的指节化石保存得非常好。|近义词：finger, toe, digit|反义词: N/A|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek δάκτυλος (dáktulos, “finger”) +‎ -let. Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeigʰ- (“to show, point”).
