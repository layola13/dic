harpagon:/ˈhɑːrpəɡɒn/ (US) /ˈhɑːpəɡən/ (UK)| n. |吝啬鬼，守财奴|A miser; a greedy, grasping person.  Derived from the name of the main character in Molière's play, "L'Avare" (The Miser), Harpagon is a stereotypical miser obsessed with hoarding his wealth.|He's such a harpagon, he refuses to turn on the heat even in winter. 他真是个吝啬鬼，即使在冬天也拒绝开暖气。|Synonyms: miser, skinflint, tightwad, penny-pincher|Antonyms: philanthropist, spendthrift|Noun
