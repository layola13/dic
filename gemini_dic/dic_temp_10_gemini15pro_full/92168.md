It implies a level of sophistication that is often seen as pretentious or artificial. | She dressed hypersophisticatedly for the party, in a gown that seemed more suited for a red carpet event. 她为了派对打扮得过于世故，穿着一件似乎更适合红毯活动的礼服。|  The artist hypersophisticatedly combined elements of classical and modern music.  这位艺术家矫揉造作地将古典音乐和现代音乐的元素结合在一起。 | 近义词：pretentiously, affectedly, elaborately | 反义词：simply, naturally, unaffectedly | 词性: 副词

词源:
* hyper- (prefix):  from Greek, meaning "over," "beyond," "excessive."
* sophisticated (adjective): from Latin *sophisticatus*, past participle of *sophisticare* "to make wise or learned," from *sophistes* "wise man, teacher of wisdom," from Greek *sophistēs*.
