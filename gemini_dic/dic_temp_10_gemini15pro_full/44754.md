crumbling:/ˈkrʌmblɪŋ/| adj.|崩溃的，破碎的，衰落的|breaking or falling apart into small fragments; deteriorating|The crumbling walls of the old castle stood as a testament to time.  古老城堡摇摇欲坠的墙壁是时间的见证。|The empire is crumbling under the weight of its own corruption.  帝国正因自身腐败的重压而崩溃。|近义词：disintegrating, decaying, deteriorating, collapsing|反义词：stable, solid, strong|词性:形容词
crumbling:/ˈkrʌmblɪŋ/| n.|崩溃，破碎，衰败|the process of breaking or falling apart into small fragments; deterioration|The crumbling of the Soviet Union reshaped the global political landscape. 苏联的崩溃重塑了全球政治格局。|We witnessed the crumbling of the old building during the earthquake.  我们目睹了地震期间那栋老建筑的倒塌。|近义词：disintegration, decay, deterioration, collapse|反义词：stability, strengthening|词性:名词

词源: From the verb "crumble," which comes from Middle English *cromblen*, frequentative of *crumen* "to crumble," from Old English *crymelan*.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *krum* "crooked, bent."
