bdellomorpha:/dɛˌlɒˈmɔːrfə/ | n. |蛭形轮虫纲|  A class of rotifers characterized by their ability to reproduce asexually through parthenogenesis, their desiccation tolerance, and their apparent long-term evolutionary success without sexual reproduction.  These microscopic animals are found in freshwater habitats worldwide. | Bdellomorpha are fascinating creatures that have thrived for millions of years without sex. 蛭形轮虫纲是迷人的生物，数百万年来在没有有性繁殖的情况下繁衍生息。 | 近义词: rotifer (a broader term) | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From Ancient Greek βδέλλα (bdélla, “leech”) + μορφή (morphḗ, “form, shape”).
