mugger:/ˈmʌɡər/ (US), /ˈmʌɡə(r)/ (UK)| n.|抢劫犯；行凶抢劫者|A person who attacks and robs someone in a public place.|例句：The mugger grabbed her purse and ran. 抢劫犯抢了她的钱包就跑了。

例句：She was attacked by a mugger in the park. 她在公园里被一个抢劫犯袭击了。

近义词：robber, thief, bandit, assailant|反义词:victim|词性:noun


mug:/mʌɡ/ (US), /mʌɡ/ (UK)| n. |马克杯；脸；傻瓜|1. A large cup, typically cylindrical and with a handle, used for drinking hot beverages. 2. A person's face. 3. A foolish or gullible person. |例句：He poured himself a mug of coffee. 他给自己倒了一杯咖啡。

例句：She’s got a nice mug on her. 她长得挺好看的。(俚语)


近义词：(for 1) cup, tankard (for 2) face, countenance (for 3) idiot, fool|反义词:(for 3) genius|词性:noun


mug:/mʌɡ/ (US), /mʌɡ/ (UK)| v. |扮鬼脸；死记硬背；行凶抢劫|1. To make faces, especially exaggerated or comical ones.  2. To study hard, especially by rote. 3. To attack and rob someone in a public place.|例句：The children mugged for the camera. 孩子们对着镜头做鬼脸。

例句：He was mugged on his way home from work. 他在下班回家的路上被抢劫了。


近义词：(for 1) grimace, clown (for 2) cram, swot (for 3) rob, attack|反义词:(for 3) protect, defend|词性:verb
