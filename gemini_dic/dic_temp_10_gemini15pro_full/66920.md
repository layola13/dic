expendabilities:/ɪkˌspɛndəˈbɪlətiz/| n. |消耗品；可牺牲的人（或物）|Items or people considered not essential and therefore readily sacrificed to achieve a larger goal.|The general saw the troops as expendabilities in his grand strategy.|近义词：consumables, disposables, nonessentials|反义词：essentials, necessities|词性: 名词

词源：From expendable + -ities.  Expendable comes from expend (meaning to use up or spend) which has roots in Latin *expendere* (weigh out, pay out).
