notarised:/ˈnəʊtəraɪzd/ | adj. | 公证的；经公证的 |  Officially certified by a notary public. | Example: The contract was notarised before being submitted to the court.  合同在提交法院之前经过了公证。 | Example:  Make sure all the documents are notarised and signed. 确保所有文件都经过公证和签字。 | Synonyms: certified, authenticated, validated | Antonyms: uncertified, unauthenticated | word property: adjective


notarize:/ˈnəʊtəraɪz/ | vt. | 公证 | To certify (a document, signature, etc.) officially by a notary public. | Example: I need to have this document notarized. 我需要公证这份文件。 | Example:  She had her signature notarized on the affidavit. 她在宣誓书上公证了她的签名。 | Synonyms: certify, authenticate, validate | Antonyms:  invalidate | word property: verb

notary:/ˈnəʊtəri/ | n. | 公证人 | A person authorized to perform certain legal formalities, especially to certify signatures on documents. | Example: We went to a notary to get the document signed and sealed. 我们去找公证人签字盖章。| Example: The notary verified the signatures on the will. 公证人核实了遗嘱上的签名。| Synonyms: notary public | Antonyms: N/A | word property: noun


Etymology:
From Late Middle English *notarien, from Old French *notarier, from Medieval Latin *notāriāre, from Latin *notārius (“shorthand writer, secretary”), from *nota (“mark, shorthand character”).
