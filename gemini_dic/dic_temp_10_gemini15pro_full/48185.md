decompose:/ˌdiːkəmˈpoʊz/ (US), /ˌdiːkəmˈpəʊz/ (UK)| v. |分解，腐烂|To break down into smaller or simpler parts; to rot.|The fallen leaves will decompose over time. 落叶会随着时间的推移而分解。|The body had started to decompose.  尸体已经开始腐烂。|近义词：decay, rot, disintegrate, break down|反义词: synthesize, compose, combine|词性:动词

decomposable:/ˌdiːkəmˈpoʊzəbl/ (US), /ˌdiːkəmˈpəʊzəbl/ (UK)| adj. |可分解的，可腐烂的|Capable of being decomposed.|Organic waste is decomposable. 有机废物是可分解的。|Most plastics are not readily decomposable. 大多数塑料不易分解。|近义词：biodegradable, perishable, putrescible|反义词: non-biodegradable, indestructible|词性:形容词

decomposition:/ˌdiːˌkɑːmpəˈzɪʃn/ (US), /ˌdiːˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃn/ (UK)| n. |分解，腐烂|The process of decomposing.|The decomposition of organic matter creates compost. 有机物的分解产生了堆肥。|The smell was caused by the decomposition of the body.  气味是由尸体的腐烂引起的。|近义词：decay, rot, disintegration, breakdown|反义词: synthesis, composition|词性:名词
