leaped:/liːpt/ | v. | 跳跃，跳过 | jumped quickly or a long way; moved suddenly | The cat leaped onto the table. 猫跳上了桌子。|  jumped, sprang, bounded | crawled, walked | verb

leaped:/liːpt/ | v. | 猛增，激增 | increased suddenly and by a large amount | Sales leaped 20% last year. 去年销售额猛增了20%。|  soared, surged, rocketed | plunged, plummeted | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English lepen, from Old English hlēapan (“to leap, jump, run, spring”), from Proto-Germanic *hlaupaną (“to run, leap”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)lewbʰ- (“to slip, slide, jump”). Cognate with Scots loup (“to leap, jump”), West Frisian leppe (“to jump”), Dutch lopen (“to walk, run”), German laufen (“to run, walk”), Icelandic hlaupa (“to run”), Swedish löpa (“to run”).
