crags:/kræɡz/| n.|峭壁，悬崖|A steep, rugged rock face, typically one forming the edge of a mountain or cliff.|例句：The climber scaled the treacherous crags. 攀登者攀上了险峻的峭壁。|例句：Eagles nested high on the crags overlooking the valley. 老鹰在俯瞰山谷的峭壁上筑巢。|近义词：cliffs, precipices, bluffs|反义词:valleys, plains|名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cragge*, from Old Norse *kragi* (masculine), meaning "rock, crag," from Proto-Germanic *kraggō* (masculine), meaning "something bent, crooked," from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ker-*, meaning "to turn, bend."  Cognates include Dutch *krag* ("collar, ruff"), German *Kragen* ("collar"), and English *crook*.
