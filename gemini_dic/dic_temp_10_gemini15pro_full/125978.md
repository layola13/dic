musket:/ˈmʌskɪt/| n.|滑膛枪，火枪|A heavy, large-caliber smoothbore gun for infantry soldiers, fired from the shoulder, and superseded by the rifle.|The soldiers were armed with muskets and bayonets. 士兵们配备了滑膛枪和刺刀。|Synonyms: firearm, gun, rifle|Antonyms: bow and arrow, sword|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *mousquet*, from Italian *moschetto* ("a sparrowhawk"), diminutive of *mosca* ("fly").  The name originally referred to a kind of crossbow, then a type of firearm.  The association with the bird likely stems from the idea of the weapon being a small, fast projectile.
