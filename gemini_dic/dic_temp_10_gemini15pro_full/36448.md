cold-calling:/ˈkoʊld ˌkɔːlɪŋ/| n.|陌生电话推销，冷电|The act of contacting a potential customer or client by phone without any prior interaction or appointment.|Example: Cold-calling can be an effective way to generate new leads, but it also has a low success rate. 陌生电话推销可以是一种有效的产生新线索的方式，但成功率也很低。

Example: Many salespeople dread cold-calling, as it often involves facing rejection. 许多销售人员害怕陌生电话推销，因为它经常要面对被拒绝。|近义词：telesales, telemarketing, canvassing|反义词:warm calling, following up|词性:名词

cold-call:/ˈkoʊld ˌkɔːl/| v.|进行陌生电话推销，打冷电|To contact a potential customer or client by phone without any prior interaction or appointment.|Example: I spent the morning cold-calling potential clients. 我早上都在给潜在客户打陌生推销电话。

Example:  She was nervous about cold-calling businesses, but she knew it was a necessary part of the job. 她对给企业打陌生推销电话感到紧张，但她知道这是工作中必不可少的一部分。|近义词：solicit, canvass|反义词:follow up|词性:动词
