feeble:/ˈfiːbl/| adj.|虚弱的，微弱的|Lacking physical strength, especially as a result of age or illness; (of a sound) faint; lacking intensity or power; not effective or convincing.|例句：He was a feeble old man. 他是一位虚弱的老人。|例句：Her voice was so feeble I could hardly hear her. 她的声音很微弱，我几乎听不见。|近义词：weak, frail, infirm, delicate, faint, ineffective|反义词：strong, robust, powerful, effective|词性:形容词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *feble, *floible (Modern French faible), from Late Latin flebilis "lamentable," from Latin flere "to weep."  The semantic shift is from "causing tears (of pity)," to "pitiable, wretched," to "weak."
