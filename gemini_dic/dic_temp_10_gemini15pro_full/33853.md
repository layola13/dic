circumposition:/ˌsɜːrkəm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən/ |n.|环介词，包围词|A type of adposition that consists of two parts which appear on both sides of their complement (object).  It is equivalent to a preposition with a postposition, or a combination of a preposition and a particle.|The German word "vom" (contraction of "von dem") acts as a circumposition surrounding the noun phrase "Haus".  This literally translates to "from the house." |近义词:adposition|反义词: (No direct antonym exists) |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From circum- ("around") +‎ position.
