brawls: /brɔːlz/
| n. | 喧闹的争吵；打架；乱斗 | A noisy, rough, uncontrolled fight or quarrel involving a group of people. |  The two groups of football fans got into a brawl outside the stadium.  这两个足球球迷团体在体育场外打了起来。 | 近义词: fights, scuffles, melees, fracas, uproars | 反义词: peace, harmony, tranquility | 词性: 名词

brawl: /brɔːl/
| n. | 喧闹的争吵；打架；乱斗 | A noisy, rough, uncontrolled fight or quarrel involving a group of people. | The bar erupted into a brawl when two patrons started arguing. 酒吧里两个顾客开始争吵，随后爆发了一场斗殴。 | 近义词: fight, scuffle, melee, fracas, uproar | 反义词: peace, harmony, tranquility | 词性: 名词

brawl: /brɔːl/
| vi. | 争吵；打架 | To take part in a noisy, rough, uncontrolled fight or quarrel. | The drunken men brawled in the street. 醉汉在街上打架。 | 近义词: fight, scuffle, wrangle | 反义词:  negotiate, compromise | 词性: 动词


词源:  From Old French *brauller (attested only in derivative brailleur "brawler"), possibly from a Germanic source akin to Old High German brallen "to boast, make noise" and brollen "to bubble, boil."
