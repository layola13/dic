guarantees:/ˌɡærənˈtiːz/
| n. |保证，担保；保证书，保单|A formal promise or assurance (typically in writing) that certain conditions will be fulfilled, especially that a product will be repaired or replaced if not of a specified quality. |The store guarantees satisfaction or your money back.| Synonyms: assurances, pledges, warranties | Antonyms: disclaimers, caveats
| noun
guarantees:/ˌɡærənˈtiːz/
| v. |保证，担保；承诺|Provide a formal assurance or promise, especially that a product will be repaired or replaced if not of a specified quality; undertake to do or ensure.|This company guarantees its products for one year. | Synonyms: assure, promise, pledge, warrant | Antonyms: disavow, renounce
| verb


Etymology:
From Old French *guarantir*, from *guarant*, itself from Frankish *warand*, from Proto-Germanic *wērand* (meaning "warrant").
