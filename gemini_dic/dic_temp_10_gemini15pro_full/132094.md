noncredulously:/ˌnɑːnˈkrɛdʒələsli/ | adv. | 不轻信地，怀疑地 | In a manner showing disbelief or skepticism. | He looked at her noncredulously, raising an eyebrow at her outlandish claim. 他怀疑地看着她，对她古怪的说法扬起了眉毛。|  She listened noncredulously to his promises, having heard similar empty vows before.  她不相信地听着他的承诺，以前听过类似的空洞誓言。| 近义词: skeptically, doubtfully, distrustfully | 反义词: credulously, believingly, trustingly | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From non- (not) + credulous (believing easily) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  Credulous ultimately derives from Latin "credulus" (believing, trusting), from "credere" (to believe).
