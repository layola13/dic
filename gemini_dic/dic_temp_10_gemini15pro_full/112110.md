longship: /ˈlɒŋʃɪp/ (UK) /ˈlɔːŋʃɪp/ (US) | n. | 长船，北欧海盗船 | A long, narrow warship with oars and a sail, used by the Vikings. | The Vikings sailed their longships up rivers to raid inland settlements. 维京人驾着他们的长船沿河而上，袭击内陆的定居点。 | 近义词: warship, galley | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "merchant ship" as a contrasting vessel type) | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Old Norse *langskip, from langr (“long”) + skip (“ship”).
