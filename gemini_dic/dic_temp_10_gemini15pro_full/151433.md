parody: /ˈpærədi/ (US), /ˈpærədi/ (UK)| n. vt.|滑稽模仿作品；滑稽地模仿|An imitation of the style of a particular writer, artist, or genre with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect. |例句：The show was a parody of a popular soap opera.  这个节目是对一部流行肥皂剧的滑稽模仿。

例句：His performance was a hilarious parody of the president's mannerisms. 他的表演是对总统举止的滑稽模仿，令人捧腹。|近义词：satire, lampoon, spoof, burlesque|反义词：homage, tribute|词性: noun, verb

parody: /ˈpærədi/ (US), /ˈpærədi/ (UK)| vt.|滑稽地模仿|To produce a humorous imitation of something. |例句：He parodied the teacher's accent perfectly. 他完美地模仿了老师的口音。

例句：The comedian parodied several famous singers in his act.  这位喜剧演员在他的表演中滑稽地模仿了几位著名歌手。|近义词：mimic, mock, satirize|反义词：emulate, imitate (seriously)|词性: verb
