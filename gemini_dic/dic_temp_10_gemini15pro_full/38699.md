condescend:/ˌkɑːndɪˈsend/ /ˌkɒndɪˈsend/| v. |屈尊；俯就；摆架子|To behave as if one is conscious of descending from a superior position, rank, or dignity.|例句：The CEO condescended to eat lunch with the factory workers. 首席执行官屈尊与工厂工人一起吃午饭。

例句：He never condescends to speak to his staff. 他从不屈尊与他的员工说话。

近义词：deign, stoop, patronize|反义词: respect, admire |词性:动词

condescending:/ˌkɑːndɪˈsendɪŋ/ /ˌkɒndɪˈsendɪŋ/| adj. |居高临下的；恩赐的；屈尊的|Having or showing a feeling of patronizing superiority.| 例句：She spoke to me in a condescending tone. 她用居高临下的语气跟我说话。

例句：I hate his condescending attitude. 我讨厌他那副屈尊俯就的态度。

近义词：patronizing, supercilious, haughty|反义词: humble, respectful |词性:形容词
