aorta:/eɪˈɔːrtə/ /əˈɔːrtə/| n. |主动脉|The main artery that carries blood from the left ventricle of the heart to the rest of the body.|The doctor said the aorta was enlarged.|artery, main blood vessel|vein|noun
Etymology->From Late Latin aorta, from Greek aortē,  “what is hung up or suspended,” from aeirein, "to lift, raise, heave."  Originally referred to the great artery, so called because it seemed to be suspended from the heart.
