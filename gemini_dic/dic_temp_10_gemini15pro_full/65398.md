euphemism: /ˈjufəmɪzəm/ | n. |委婉语，委婉说法 | A euphemism is a mild or indirect word or expression substituted for one considered to be too harsh or blunt when referring to something unpleasant or embarrassing. 委婉语是一种温和或间接的词语或表达方式，用来代替那些在指称不愉快或尴尬的事情时被认为过于刺耳或直白的词语。 | "Passed away" is a euphemism for "died". “去世”是“死亡”的委婉说法。| synonyms:  circumlocution, understatement | antonyms:  dysphemism | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *euphēmismos* "use of words of good omen," from *euphēmizein* "speak with good omens, use auspicious language," from *eu* "good, well" + *phēmē* "speech, voice, utterance, prophecy, rumor, report."
