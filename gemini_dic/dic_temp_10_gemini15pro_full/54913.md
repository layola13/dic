dispose:/dɪˈspəʊz/ (UK) /dɪˈspoʊz/ (US)| v. |处理，处置；安排；使倾向于|To get rid of something, especially by throwing it away, or to deal with something so that it is no longer a problem; to arrange things or people in a particular way or position; to make someone feel a particular way towards someone or something|例句：The company disposed of its waste products responsibly.  公司负责任地处理了其废弃物。

例句：She disposed her guests around the table. 她安排客人们围坐在桌子旁。|近义词：arrange, discard, get rid of, incline|反义词：retain, keep, hoard|词性: verb


dispose of:/dɪˈspəʊz əv/ (UK) /dɪˈspoʊz əv/ (US)| phrasal v. |清除，处理|To get rid of something, especially by throwing it away|例句：How did they dispose of the body? 他们是如何处理尸体的？

例句：I need to dispose of this old furniture. 我需要处理掉这件旧家具。|近义词：get rid of, throw away, discard|反义词：keep, retain|词性: phrasal verb


be disposed to:/bi dɪˈspəʊzd tuː/ (UK) /bi dɪˈspoʊzd tuː/ (US)| phrasal v. |倾向于，有意于|To be willing or likely to do something|例句：I'm not disposed to argue. 我不想争论。

例句：She was disposed to trust him. 她倾向于信任他。|近义词：inclined, willing, prone|反义词：disinclined, unwilling|词性: phrasal verb


at one's disposal:/æt wʌnz dɪˈspəʊzəl/ (UK) /æt wʌnz dɪˈspoʊzəl/ (US)| idiom |由某人支配，供某人使用|Available for someone to use|例句：I have a car at my disposal. 我有一辆车可以支配。

例句：He had a large sum of money at his disposal. 他可以支配一大笔钱。|近义词：available, accessible|反义词：unavailable, inaccessible|词性: idiom


dispose towards:/dɪˈspəʊz təˈwɔːdz/ (UK) /dɪˈspoʊz təˈwɔːrdz/ (US)| phrasal v. |使倾向于，使有意于|To make someone feel a particular way towards someone or something|例句：His friendly manner disposed me towards him. 他友好的态度使我对他有好感。

例句：Her kindness disposed me towards helping her. 她的善良使我倾向于帮助她。|近义词：incline, influence|反义词：deter|词性: phrasal verb
