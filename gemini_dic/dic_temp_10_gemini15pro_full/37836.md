词源：
From Latin competens, present participle of competere "to coincide, agree, be suitable," from com- "together" (see com-) + petere "to strive for, seek, fall upon" (from PIE root *pet- "to rush, fly"). The notion is of things "coming together" properly. Meaning "fit, suitable" is from 1590s; sense of "duly qualified" is from 1640s.
