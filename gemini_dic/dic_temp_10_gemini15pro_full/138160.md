northstar:/ˈnɔːrθstɑːr/ (US), /ˈnɔːθstɑː(r)/ (UK)| n.|北极星; 指导原则; 核心价值|A star, specifically Polaris, that is situated close to the north celestial pole and serves as a navigational guide.  Figuratively, something that serves as a guiding principle or central value. |例句：Sailors used the North Star to navigate across the oceans.  水手们利用北极星在海洋中航行。

例句：Our company's north star is innovation and customer satisfaction. 我们公司的指导原则是创新和客户满意。|近义词：Polaris, guiding principle, lodestar, guiding light|反义词: None (for literal meaning), aimlessness (for figurative meaning)|词性:noun
