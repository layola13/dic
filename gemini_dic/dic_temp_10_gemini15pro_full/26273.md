buttonholing: /ˈbʌtnˌhəʊlɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈbʌtnˌhoʊlɪŋ/ (US)| n. |强留（某人）讲话；拉住（某人）不放|The act of detaining someone in conversation, often against their will, typically by holding onto their buttonhole (originally) or otherwise preventing them from leaving.|He was buttonholing everyone at the party, trying to sell his new product. 他在聚会上缠着每个人，试图推销他的新产品。|近义词：accosting, cornering, waylaying|反义词: releasing, avoiding|词性:noun (gerund or present participle)
