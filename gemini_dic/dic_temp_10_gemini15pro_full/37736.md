compartmentalization:/kəmˌpɑːrtˌmɛntələˈzeɪʃən/ |n.| 分隔化，划分，区划化 |The act or process of dividing something into separate compartments or categories.  The state of being divided into separate compartments or categories.  |He copes by compartmentalizing his life, keeping his work separate from his personal problems. 他通过将生活划分开来，将工作与个人问题分开来应对。| Synonyms: categorization, classification, division, segregation, pigeonholing | Antonyms: integration, unification, synthesis, blending, merging | noun

compartmentalize:/kəmˌpɑːrtˈmɛntəˌlaɪz/ |v.| 分隔，划分，区划 |To divide something into separate compartments or categories. To separate something into isolated compartments or categories, especially to prevent one aspect from affecting another.  |She compartmentalizes her life, keeping work separate from her personal life. 她把自己的生活划分开来，把工作和个人生活分开。| Synonyms: categorize, classify, divide, segregate, pigeonhole | Antonyms: integrate, unify, synthesize, blend, merge | verb

compartmentalized:/kəmˌpɑːrtˈmɛntəˌlaɪzd/ |adj.| 分隔的，划分的 |Divided into separate compartments or categories. Existing or occurring in separate compartments or categories.  |His life is highly compartmentalized. 他的生活被高度划分。| Synonyms: categorized, classified, divided, segregated, pigeonholed | Antonyms: integrated, unified, synthesized, blended, merged | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From compartment + -alize + -ation.  Compartment ultimately comes from the Latin word "compartiri," meaning "to divide."
