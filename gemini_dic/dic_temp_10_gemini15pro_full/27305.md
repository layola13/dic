calycine:/ˈkælɪsaɪn/ |adj.| 花萼状的；杯状的；似花萼的| Relating to or resembling a calyx (the sepals of a flower); cup-shaped. |The calycine structure of the fruit protected the developing seeds.  这种水果的花萼状结构保护了正在发育的种子。| The artist captured the calycine form of the poppy in his painting.  艺术家在他的画中捕捉到了罂粟花的花萼形状。 |Near Synonyms: cup-shaped, cuplike, calyx-like| Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, consider antonyms for shape like 'irregular', 'amorphous') |adjective

calycine:/ˈkælɪsaɪn/ |v.| 煅烧；焙烧|To heat a substance to a high temperature but below its melting point, usually in order to remove impurities or volatile substances. |The chemist calcinated the limestone to produce quicklime.  化学家煅烧石灰石以生产生石灰。|  They calcinated the bones to create bone ash for fertilizer. 他们煅烧骨头来制造骨灰肥料。|Near Synonyms:  burn, roast, bake, heat, incinerate| Antonyms: cool, chill|verb
