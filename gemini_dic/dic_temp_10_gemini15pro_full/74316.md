fractures:/ˈfræktʃərz/ (US), /ˈfræktʃəz/ (UK)
n.|骨折；断裂；裂痕|The act or process of breaking or the state of being broken.  A break, crack, or rupture in a bone or other hard material.|The earthquake caused fractures in the building's foundation. 地震导致建筑物地基断裂。
fractures:/ˈfræktʃərz/ (US), /ˈfræktʃəz/ (UK)
v.| (使)断裂，(使)破裂|(cause something to) break or crack.|The bone fractured in the fall.  这块骨头在摔倒时断裂了。

Etymology:  From Late Middle English *fracture*, from Latin *fractūra* (“a breach, break”), from *frangere* (“to break”).

Synonyms:  breaks, cracks, ruptures, fissures, splits
Antonyms:  mends, repairs, heals, joins
Word type: Noun (plural), Verb (third-person singular present)
