blitzed:/blɪtst/| adj. v.| 酩酊大醉的，喝醉了的; 闪电战,快速突击|  Extremely intoxicated from alcohol or drugs; subjected to a sudden, overwhelming attack, especially a military one. | After the party, he was completely blitzed. 派对结束后，他彻底喝醉了。| The enemy blitzed the city at dawn. 敌人拂晓时突袭了这座城市。| 近义词：drunk, intoxicated, bombed (informal), plastered (informal), hammered (informal); attacked, bombarded| 反义词：sober; defended| 词性: 形容词/动词

blitzes:/ˈblɪtsɪz/ |n. v.| 闪电战，突袭;  |A sudden, overwhelming attack, especially a military one; to attack suddenly and overwhelmingly. |The German blitzes during World War II were devastating. 二战期间德国的闪电战是毁灭性的。| The team blitzes the quarterback on almost every play.  这个队几乎每次进攻都突击四分卫。| 近义词：attack, assault, onslaught, bombardment| 反义词：defense, retreat| 词性: 名词/动词
