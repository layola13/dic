buccaneerish:/ˌbʌkəˈnɪərɪʃ/
adjective
海盗似的；冒险的；狂放不羁的
Resembling or suggestive of a buccaneer, especially in being bold, adventurous, or recklessly daring.
He had a buccaneerish swagger as he strode into the room. 他大摇大摆地走进房间，一副海盗式的狂妄姿态。
The buccaneerish captain set sail for uncharted waters. 这位狂放不羁的船长起航前往未知水域。
synonyms: piratical, adventurous, daring, swaggering, swashbuckling, bold, reckless
antonyms: timid, cautious, reserved, restrained
ETYMOLOGY->
From buccaneer + -ish.  Buccaneer comes from French boucanier, from boucan "a wooden frame for smoking meat," from Tupi.
