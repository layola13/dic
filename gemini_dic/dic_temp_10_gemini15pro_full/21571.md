bluestockings: /ˈbluːˌstɒkɪŋz/ (UK) /ˈbluːˌstɑːkɪŋz/ (US) | n. | 蓝袜子；女学者；女才子 | A woman with strong scholarly or literary interests.  Originally applied derogatorily to women who prioritized intellectual pursuits over traditional feminine roles. |  例句：She was dismissed as a bluestocking by the more conventional members of society. 她被社会上更传统的人视为女学者而不屑一顾。

例句：The bluestockings of the 18th century challenged societal norms. 18世纪的女学者们挑战了社会规范。

近义词：intellectual, academic, learned woman | 反义词：socialite, debutante | 词性：名词

bluestocking: /ˈbluːˌstɒkɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈbluːˌstɑːkɪŋ/ (US) | adj. |  蓝袜子的；女学者的；女才子的 | Relating to or characteristic of a bluestocking. | 例句：She had a reputation for being rather bluestocking. 她以相当女学者的名声而闻名。

例句：The bluestocking circles of the era were known for their lively discussions. 那个时代的女学者圈子以其热烈的讨论而闻名。

近义词：intellectual, academic, highbrow | 反义词：frivolous, superficial | 词性：形容词

词源:  The term originated in mid-18th century England, referring to literary discussions held at the home of Elizabeth Montagu, where some attendees wore informal blue worsted stockings instead of the black silk stockings fashionable at the time. The term eventually became associated with learned women, often with negative connotations.
