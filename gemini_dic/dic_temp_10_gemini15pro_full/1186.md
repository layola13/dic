acclimatized: /əˈklaɪmətaɪzd/
adjective
适应气候的；适应环境的
Adapted to a new climate or environment.
The climbers acclimatized themselves to the high altitude before attempting the summit.  登山者在尝试登顶之前先适应了高海拔。

The plants had become acclimatized to the desert conditions. 这些植物已经适应了沙漠环境。

近义词: adapted, adjusted, habituated
反义词: unaccustomed, unadapted
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French acclimater, from à (to) + climat (climate). Ultimately from Greek klima (region, slope).

USAGE->
一般现在时:
She is acclimatized to the cold weather. = 她已经适应了寒冷的天气。
He is not yet acclimatized to the new culture. = 他还没有适应新的文化。


一般过去时:
They acclimatized quickly to the hot and humid climate. = 他们很快就适应了炎热潮湿的气候。
The explorers acclimatized themselves to the harsh conditions. = 探险家们使自己适应了恶劣的环境。


现在完成时:
The team has acclimatized to the altitude. = 团队已经适应了海拔高度。
She hasn't acclimatized to the time difference yet. = 她还没有适应时差。


过去完成时:
By the time they reached the summit, they had acclimatized to the thin air. = 当他们到达山顶时，他们已经适应了稀薄的空气。
He had not acclimatized to the local customs before the incident occurred. = 在事件发生之前，他还没有适应当地的风俗习惯。


将来时:
The climbers will acclimatize for a week before attempting the summit. = 登山者将在尝试登顶前适应一周。
They will be acclimatized to the new environment soon. = 他们很快就会适应新环境。



虚拟语气:
If he were acclimatized to the heat, he wouldn't be so tired. = 如果他适应了炎热的天气，他就不会这么累了。
I wish I were already acclimatized to the high altitude. = 我希望我已经适应了高海拔。


被动语态:
The animals are being acclimatized to their new surroundings. = 动物们正在适应新的环境。
The seedlings were acclimatized in a greenhouse before being planted outside. = 幼苗在被移植到室外之前先在温室中适应环境。


疑问句:
Are you acclimatized to the new time zone yet? = 你适应新的时区了吗？
Has he fully acclimatized to the local culture? = 他完全适应了当地文化吗？


否定句:
They are not acclimatized to the cold weather. = 他们不适应寒冷的天气。
She was not fully acclimatized to the altitude when she started the climb. = 当她开始攀登时，她还没有完全适应海拔高度。
