fines:/faɪnz/| n.|罚款，罚金|A sum of money exacted as a penalty for an offense or dereliction.|The court imposed heavy fines on the company. 法院对该公司处以巨额罚款。|Synonyms: penalties, forfeits, mulcts|Antonyms: rewards, bonuses|noun

fines:/faɪnz/| v.|处…以罚金|To punish by a fine.|The judge fined him $500 for speeding.  法官因他超速行驶对他处以500美元的罚款。|Synonyms: penalize,  sanction|Antonyms: reward, compensate|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *finer* "to end, settle, pay,"  from Latin *finis* "end, limit, boundary."  The noun sense developed from the verb, referring to the money paid to "end" or settle a legal matter.
