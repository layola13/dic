absorptions:/əbˈzɔːrpʃənz/ /əbˈsɔːrpʃənz/| n. |吸收; 吸收作用; 专注; 全神贯注|The process by which one substance takes in another substance.  The state of being engrossed in something. |The absorptions of water by soil can prevent flooding. 土壤对水的吸收可以防止洪水泛滥。

His absorption in the video game made him oblivious to his surroundings. 他对电子游戏的专注使他对周围的环境浑然不觉。|近义词：intake, uptake, assimilation, immersion, preoccupation|反义词：emission, release, distraction|词性:名词(plural)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *absorpcioun, from Late Latin absorptiōn- (stem of absorptiō ), from absorbēre (to absorb). Ultimately from Latin ab- (from: away from) + sorbēre (to suck up).
