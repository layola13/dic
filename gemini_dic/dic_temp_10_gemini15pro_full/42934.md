covenanter:/ˈkʌvənəntər/| n.|圣约者，盟约者|A person who makes a covenant, especially one who signed the Scottish National Covenant of 1638 or the Solemn League and Covenant of 1643.|The covenanters fought for religious freedom.|近义词：signer, signatory, subscriber|反义词:opponent, dissenter|词性:noun

Etymology:
From covenant + -er.  Ultimately from Latin *convenīre "to come together, agree."
