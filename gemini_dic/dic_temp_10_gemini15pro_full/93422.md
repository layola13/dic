ill-treat:/ɪlˈtriːt/| vt.|虐待，苛待|To treat someone badly, cruelly, or unfairly.|He was accused of ill-treating his animals. 他被指控虐待动物。|Synonyms: mistreat, abuse, maltreat|Antonyms: treat well, cherish, care for|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From ill- + treat.  "Ill" acts as an intensifier, meaning "badly" or "poorly". "Treat" comes from the Old French "traitier," meaning "to handle, deal with." Thus, "ill-treat" literally means "to handle or deal with badly."
