desolation:/ˌdesəˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 荒凉，凄凉，孤寂 | A state of complete emptiness or destruction, often causing great sadness. | The earthquake left a scene of utter desolation. 地震过后，现场一片荒凉。| synonyms: barrenness, bleakness, devastation, ruin, wasteland | antonyms: comfort, hope, joy, life, prosperity | noun

desolate:/ˈdesələt/ | adj. | 荒凉的，凄凉的，孤寂的 | Empty and without people, making you feel sad or lonely. | The desolate landscape stretched for miles. 荒凉的景象绵延数英里。 | synonyms: barren, bleak, deserted, empty, lonely | antonyms: cheerful, comforting, crowded, happy, populated | adjective

desolate:/ˈdesəleɪt/ | v. | 使荒芜，使凄凉 |  To make a place empty and without people, or to make someone feel very sad and lonely. | The war desolated the countryside. 战争使乡村变得荒芜。 | synonyms: devastate, destroy, ravage, ruin, wreck | antonyms: build, comfort, construct, create, improve | verb
