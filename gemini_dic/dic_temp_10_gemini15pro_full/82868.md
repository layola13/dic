gullibly:/ˈɡʌləbli/| adv.|轻信地，易受骗地|In a manner that shows one is easily tricked or persuaded to believe something.|He gullibly believed the stranger's promise of riches.|近义词：naively, credulously, unsuspectingly|反义词：skeptically, cynically, suspiciously|词性:副词
Etymology: From "gullible" + "-ly." "Gullible" comes from "gull" (to trick or deceive) + "-ible" (capable of being).  "Gull" likely originates from a now obsolete word meaning "young bird," referring to their vulnerability.
