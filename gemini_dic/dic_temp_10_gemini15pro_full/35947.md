coddle:/ˈkɒd(ə)l/ (UK) /ˈkɑːdl/ (US)| v.| 娇惯；溺爱；细心照料 |To treat someone with excessive care and attention, often preventing them from experiencing hardship or becoming independent. |She coddles her youngest son. 她溺爱她的小儿子。|  He coddled the egg in boiling water for three minutes. 他将鸡蛋在沸水中煮了三分钟。 | pamper, indulge, spoil, baby | neglect, ignore | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *codlen*, from Old Northern French *codler* "to stew gently," perhaps from Latin *caldāre* "to heat, warm," from *caldus* "warm, hot."
