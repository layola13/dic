lodge:/lɒdʒ/ (British), /lɑːdʒ/ (American)| n. v.|小屋，旅馆；存放；提出；寄宿|A small house at the gates of a park or in the grounds of a large house, occupied by a gatekeeper, gardener, or other employee.  To present something formally, such as a complaint or claim. To stay temporarily; to rent a room in another person's house.|例句：They lodged a complaint with the city council. 他们向市议会提出了投诉。|近义词：cabin, cottage (n.), file, submit (v.), stay, board (v.)|反义词: evict (v.)|词性: noun, verb

lodge:/lɒdʒ/ (British), /lɑːdʒ/ (American)| v.|存放；卡住|To become stuck or wedged.|例句：The bone lodged in his throat. 骨头卡在他的喉咙里。|近义词：stick, wedge|反义词: dislodge|词性: verb

lodges (plural of lodge):/lɒdʒɪz/ (British), /lɑːdʒɪz/ (American)| n.|小屋，旅馆（复数）|Plural form of lodge, referring to multiple small houses or accommodations.|例句：The ski lodges were fully booked for the season.  滑雪小屋整个季节都被订满了。|近义词：cabins, cottages|反义词: None|词性: noun
