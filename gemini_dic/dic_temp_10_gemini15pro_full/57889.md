drift-off:/ˈdrɪft ɒf/ (US) /ˈdrɪft ɒf/ (UK)| vi.|逐渐入睡；不知不觉睡着；渐渐消失；话题跑题|To gradually fall asleep, or to become less focused or intense.|例句：I drifted off during the movie. 我在电影播到一半时睡着了。|例句：The conversation drifted off into silence.  谈话逐渐消失在沉默中。|近义词：doze off, nod off, fade away|反义词：wake up, perk up|词性:动词

drift-off:/ˈdrɪft ɒf/ (US) /ˈdrɪft ɒf/ (UK)| n.|睡着；渐行渐远|The act of gradually falling asleep.|例句：Her drift-off was so subtle, I didn't notice at first. 她睡得很轻，一开始我没注意到。|例句：The drift-off of the conversation left me feeling unsatisfied.  谈话的渐行渐远让我感到不满意。|近义词：dozing off, fading|反义词：awakening|词性:名词

词源：drift (v.) + off (adv.). "Drift" originally referred to the movement of something carried by a current (water, wind, etc.).  "Off" indicates a departure or separation.  Therefore, "drift off" literally means to be carried away from a state of wakefulness or focus.
