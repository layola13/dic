The word "highe" isn't a standard English word. It's likely a misspelling of "higher," which is the comparative form of "high."  Therefore, the following analysis is for "higher":

/ˈhaɪər/ (US) /ˈhaɪə(r)/ (UK)| adj. adv.|更高的，较高的；高级的；高等的|  situated or extending above in place, position, or rank.  Used to compare something to another thing that is less high. Also used to refer to more advanced levels of education or a more important status. |The bird flew higher and higher.  鸟越飞越高。| higher than, above, superior to| lower, inferior to| adjective, adverb
