optography:/ɒpˈtɒɡrəfi/ | n. |视写术，光学书写|The study of retinal images after death, especially for use in identifying the deceased person or determining the cause of death.  It is based on the theory that the last image seen by a person is retained on the retina.| Optography, though theoretically possible, has never been proven to be practically useful in forensic science. 视写术虽然理论上可行，但在法医学中从未被证明具有实际用途。|Near synonyms: retinal imaging|Antonyms: None|noun
