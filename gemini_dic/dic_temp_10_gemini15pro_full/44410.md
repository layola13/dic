cross-license:/ˌkrɒs ˈlaɪsns/ /ˌkrɔːs ˈlaɪsns/ | n. |交叉许可，交互许可|An agreement between two or more parties where each grants the others the right to use their intellectual property.|Example 1: The two companies signed a cross-license agreement to avoid patent litigation.  两家公司签署了一份交叉许可协议，以避免专利诉讼。

Example 2: The cross-license allowed both companies to benefit from each other's technology. 交叉许可允许两家公司都能从彼此的技术中受益。|近义词：reciprocal license, mutual license|反义词: exclusive license|词性: noun

cross-license:/ˌkrɒs ˈlaɪsns/ /ˌkrɔːs ˈlaɪsns/ | v. |进行交叉许可|To grant a license to another party in exchange for a license to their intellectual property.|Example 1: They decided to cross-license their patents to gain access to each other's markets. 他们决定交叉许可他们的专利，以进入彼此的市场。

Example 2: The companies cross-licensed their technologies to avoid costly litigation.  这些公司交叉许可了它们的技术，以避免代价高昂的诉讼。|近义词：reciprocally license, mutually license|反义词: exclusively license|词性: verb
