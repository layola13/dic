donnishnesses:/ˈdɒnɪʃnɪsɪz/ | n. | 学究气，迂腐 |  The quality of being excessively learned, pedantic, or bookish; an instance of this. | He displayed a certain donnishness in his manner of speaking. 他说话的方式显示出某种学究气。 | 近义词: pedantry, bookishness, academicism | 反义词: informality, worldliness | 词性: noun (plural)

词源:  From donnish + -ness.  "Donnish" comes from "don," a fellow or tutor of a college or university, especially at Oxford or Cambridge. Ultimately from Latin dominus "lord, master."
