cageful:/ˈkeɪdʒfʊl/| n. |一笼；满笼|A cage filled with something, typically animals or birds.  A quantity that fills a cage.|The farmer brought a cageful of chickens to the market. 农民带了一笼鸡到市场。|近义词： load, batch |反义词: none |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From cage + -ful.  "Cage" comes from Old North French "cage," ultimately from Latin "cavea" (cage, enclosure).  "-ful" is a suffix meaning "full of" or "having the qualities of."
