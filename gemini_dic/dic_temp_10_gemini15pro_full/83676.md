hagberry:/ˈhæɡˌbɛri/ | n. | 黑果越橘;欧洲越橘 | A dark blue berry, similar to a bilberry, that grows on a low shrub. The berries are used for making preserves and are sometimes fermented to make an alcoholic drink. |  The path was lined with hagberry bushes laden with ripe fruit. 小路两旁长满了果实累累的黑果越橘灌木丛。| synonym: bilberry, whortleberry, huckleberry | antonym:  (No direct antonym for a specific berry) | noun

Etymology:
From Old English *hægebere, from hæg "hedge" + bere "berry".  The "hag" part refers to the fact that the plant often grows in hedgerows.
