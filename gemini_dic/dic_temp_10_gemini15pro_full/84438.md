hallucinogen:/həˈluːsɪnədʒən/ /həˈluːsɪnəˌdʒen/ | n. | 迷幻剂；致幻剂 |A drug that causes hallucinations, i.e., perceptions of sights, sounds, etc. that are not real. |  He experienced vivid hallucinations after taking the drug. 他服药后出现了生动的幻觉。| synonyms: psychedelic, psychotomimetic | antonyms:  antipsychotic | noun

hallucinogens:/həˈluːsɪnədʒənz/ /həˈluːsɪnəˌdʒenz/ | n. | 迷幻剂（复数） | Plural form of hallucinogen.  |  Many hallucinogens are derived from plants. 许多迷幻剂来源于植物。| synonyms: psychedelics, psychotomimetics | antonyms: antipsychotics | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *hallucinogène*, from halluciner (“to hallucinate”) + -gène (“-gen”).  Halluciner comes from Latin alucinārī (“to wander in mind, talk idly, dream”),  from alucinor, which is of uncertain origin.
