degradingness:/dɪˈɡreɪdɪŋnəs/| n. | 贬低；降低身份；丢脸 | The state or quality of being degrading; humiliation. |  The degradingness of the situation made her cry.  这种有辱人格的情况让她哭了。| 近义词：humiliation, indignity, abasement, debasement | 反义词: honor, dignity, respect | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From degrading + -ness.  "Degrading" comes from the verb "degrade," which ultimately derives from the Latin "degradare," meaning "to step down from," from "de-" (down) + "gradus" (step).  "-ness" is a suffix used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
