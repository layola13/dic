fulmination:/ˌfʊlmɪˈneɪʃn/ | n. | 猛烈抨击；爆炸；闪电 | a sudden loud noise or flash of light; a violent denunciation or condemnation | The preacher delivered a fulmination against the evils of gambling.  牧师猛烈抨击赌博的罪恶。| synonyms: denunciation, condemnation, explosion, outburst, diatribe | antonyms: praise, commendation, compliment | noun

fulminate:/ˈfʊlmɪneɪt/ | v. | 爆炸；猛烈抨击；发出轰隆声 | to explode suddenly and violently; to issue denunciations or condemnations vehemently | The bomb fulminated, destroying the building. 炸弹爆炸了，摧毁了这栋建筑。  The senator fulminated against the proposed tax increase. 参议员猛烈抨击拟议中的增税方案。 | synonyms: explode, detonate, denounce, condemn, rail | antonyms: praise, commend, support | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin fulminare "to strike with lightning," from fulmen "lightning flash, thunderbolt," related to flamma "flame" and flagrare "to burn."
