depos:/dɪˈpɒz/, /ˈdiːpoʊz/ | n. | 存款，押金，沉积物 | A sum of money placed in a bank or other financial institution as security or to earn interest; a layer of accumulated matter. | Example 1: He made a large depos in his savings account. 他在他的储蓄账户里存了一大笔钱。

Example 2: The geological depos revealed fossils from millions of years ago.  地质沉积物揭示了数百万年前的化石。 | 近义词: deposit, payment, installment | 反义词: withdrawal, debt | 词性: noun (primarily), can also function as a verb prefix (meaning "down" or "away")


词源: From Latin *dēpositum*, the past participle of *dēpōnere* ("to put down, lay aside"), from *dē* ("down, away") + *pōnere* ("to put, place").
