brustle: /ˈbrʌsəl/ | vi. | 噼啪作响；沙沙作响；（人或动物）怒气冲冲地快速移动 | To make a crackling or rustling sound; to move quickly and angrily (of people or animals). | The leaves brustled in the wind. 树叶在风中沙沙作响。 |  The angry bear brustled towards us. 那只愤怒的熊怒气冲冲地朝我们走来。 | 近义词: rustle, crackle, bristle, swagger | 反义词: soothe, calm | 词性: 动词


Etymology:  From Middle English *brustlen, frequentative of *brusten (“to burst, break”), from Old English berstan (“to burst”), from Proto-Germanic *brestaną (“to burst, break, crack”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰregs- (“to break”). Related to burst. The meaning shifted from "to burst, break" to "to make a crackling sound," likely influenced by the similar-sounding "rustle."
