accordions: /əˈkɔːrdiənz/
n.
手风琴
A portable musical instrument with reeds and bellows, played by pressing keys or buttons and expanding and contracting the bellows.
He plays the accordions beautifully. 他手风琴弹得非常好。

The street musician was playing a lively tune on his accordions.  街头音乐家正用他的手风琴演奏一首欢快的曲子。

近义词: concertina, squeezebox
反义词: (No direct antonym for a musical instrument)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From German Akkordion, from Akkord (“chord”), because the left-hand buttons play chords. Ultimately from Italian accordo.
