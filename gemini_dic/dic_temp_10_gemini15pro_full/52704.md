digresser:/daɪˈɡrɛsər/| n.|离题者，跑题的人|Someone who digresses; a person who deviates from the main subject in speech or writing.|The digresser lost the attention of his audience.  这个跑题的人失去了听众的注意。|近义词：wanderer, rambler|反义词:focuser|noun

digress:/daɪˈɡrɛs/| v.|跑题，离题|To deviate or wander away from the main subject or purpose in speaking or writing.|He digressed from the main topic to tell a personal anecdote. 他从主题跑题，讲了一个个人轶事。|近义词：deviate, stray, ramble|反义词:focus, stay on topic|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin digressus, past participle of digredi (“to go aside, depart, digress”), from dis- (“apart, away”) + gradi (“to walk, step”).
