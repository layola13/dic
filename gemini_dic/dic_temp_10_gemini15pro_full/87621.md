heuristic's:/hjʊˈrɪstɪks/ | n. | 启发式方法，经验法则 |  A heuristic is a practical method not guaranteed to be optimal or perfect, but sufficient for the immediate goals. Heuristics can be mental shortcuts that ease the cognitive load of making a decision. | Example: "Using a heuristic, he quickly estimated the distance to the next town."  他用启发式方法快速估算了到下一个城镇的距离。 | Example: "The chess player relied on heuristics to evaluate the board position." 这位棋手依靠启发式方法来评估棋盘位置。 | Synonym: rule of thumb, shortcut, guideline | Antonym: algorithm, systematic approach | noun

heuristic:/hjʊˈrɪstɪk/ | adj. | 启发式的 | Enabling a person to discover or learn something for themselves. | Example: "Socratic questioning is a heuristic method of teaching." 苏格拉底式提问是一种启发式教学方法。 | Example: "Heuristic learning involves exploring and experimenting." 启发式学习包括探索和实验。 | Synonym: exploratory, investigative, discovery-based | Antonym: didactic, rote, algorithmic | adjective
