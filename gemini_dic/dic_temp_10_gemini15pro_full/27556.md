canaille:/kəˈnaɪ/ | n. | riffraff; rabble; the masses; the common people | A contemptuous term for the common people or the lower classes.  It suggests a disdainful view of those considered vulgar, uneducated, or of low social standing. | The aristocrat sneered at the canaille gathered in the streets.  贵族对聚集在街道上的乌合之众嗤之以鼻。|  The writer satirized the political posturing of the canaille. 作家讽刺了民众的政治姿态。| synonyms: rabble, riffraff, mob, populace, masses, dregs of society | antonyms: aristocracy, elite, gentry, nobility | noun
