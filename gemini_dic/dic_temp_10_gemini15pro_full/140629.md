olusatrum:/ˌɒljuːˈseɪtrəm/ /ˌɑːljuːˈseɪtrəm/| n. |黑芥,  亚历山大草 |A plant, *Smyrnium olusatrum*, also known as Alexanders, a biennial herb from the Mediterranean region, formerly cultivated as a vegetable. Its leaves, stems, and roots were eaten, and it was used medicinally. | Alexanders (*olusatrum*) was a common vegetable in ancient Rome.  黑芥(亚历山大草)在古罗马是一种常见的蔬菜。 |Synonyms: Alexanders, black lovage, horse parsley| Antonyms: None (specific antonyms for plants are uncommon) | noun
