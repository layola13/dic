intent's:/ɪnˈtɛnts/ | n. | 意图，目的，打算 |  It is formed by adding the possessive suffix -'s to the noun "intent".  "Intent" itself comes from the Old French word "entente," meaning "understanding" or "purpose," ultimately derived from the Latin "intendere," meaning "to stretch out" or "to direct one's attention towards."  The possessive form "intent's" indicates that something belongs to or is associated with a specific intent. | The lawyer questioned the intent's true meaning. 律师质疑了该意图的真正含义。 |  The intent's clarity was crucial to the success of the project.  这个意图的清晰度对项目的成功至关重要。 | synonyms: aim's, purpose's, goal's, objective's | antonyms:  lack of purpose's, aimlessness's | noun
