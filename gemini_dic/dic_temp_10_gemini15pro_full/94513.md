importuner:/ˌɪmpɔːrˈtjuːnər/ | n. | 恳求者；不断请求者；强求者 |  A person who makes persistent and unwelcome requests or demands, especially for attention or favors. | He was known as an importuner, always begging for money or favors.  他被称为一个不断请求者，总是乞讨金钱或恩惠。 |  beggar, supplicant, solicitor | objector, refuser | noun


importuner:/ˌɪmpɔːrˈtjuːnər/ | n. | 纠缠不休者；死缠烂打者 |  A person who persistently pesters or harasses someone, especially with unwanted sexual advances. |  The woman filed a complaint against her importuner.  这位女士对纠缠她的人提出了控诉。| harasser, pest, stalker |  non-botherer | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *importuner*, from Old French *importuner*, from Late Latin *importunāre*, from Latin *in-*, "in, on" + *portus*, "harbor," figuratively "refuge, peace."  The original sense was "to disturb someone's peace."  So, literally, "to be in/on someone's harbor/peace."
