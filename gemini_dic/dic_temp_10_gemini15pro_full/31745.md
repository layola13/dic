The word "chear" is likely a misspelling of "cheer".  Therefore, the following analysis is for "cheer".

cheer: /tʃɪr/ | n. v. | 欢呼；喝彩；使高兴；振奋 | A shout of encouragement, praise, or joy. To give a shout of encouragement, praise, or joy. To make someone feel happier or more hopeful. | The crowd cheered as the team scored.  人群为球队的得分欢呼。 He tried to cheer her up with a joke. 他试图用笑话让她高兴起来。 | 近义词: applaud, encourage, comfort, gladden | 反义词: discourage, depress, sadden | 词性: noun, verb

Etymology: From Middle English *cheren*, from Anglo-Norman *cherer*, from Late Latin *carāre* "to hold dear, to care for".
