fractionalist:/ˈfrækʃənəlɪst/
n.|宗派主义者，分裂主义者|A person who promotes internal division or factionalism within a group or organization.  A person who advocates for fractional reserves in banking.|The fractionalist movement threatened to split the party.|近义词:separatist, schismatic, dissident, sectarian|反义词:unitarian, integrationist|词性:noun

fractionalist:/ˈfrækʃənəlɪst/
adj.|宗派的，分裂的|Relating to or characterized by factionalism or internal division.|The fractionalist tendencies within the group were becoming increasingly problematic.|近义词:divisive, schismatic, sectarian|反义词:unifying, integrating|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From fraction + -alist.  "Fraction" comes from Late Latin *fractionem, from Latin fractus, past participle of frangere "to break."  "-alist" is a suffix forming nouns denoting persons who adhere to a particular doctrine or system.
