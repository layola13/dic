contraversion:/ˌkɒntrəˈvɜːʃən/ /ˌkɑːntrəˈvɜːrʒən/| n. |争议，辩论，反驳|A contraversion is a dispute or disagreement, often involving a strong opposing argument or point of view. |The lawyer's skillful contraversion of the witness's testimony cast doubt on the prosecution's case. 律师巧妙地反驳了证人的证词，使控方的案件产生了疑问。|近义词：controversy, dispute, argument, debate, contention|反义词：agreement, concord, harmony|词性:名词

contraversion:/ˌkɒntrəˈvɜːʃən/ /ˌkɑːntrəˈvɜːrʒən/| n. |矛盾，对立|Contraversion can also refer to a direct opposition or antithesis between two things. |There's a clear contraversion between his public statements and his private actions. 他的公开声明和私下行动之间存在明显的矛盾。|近义词：opposition, antithesis, contradiction, conflict|反义词：similarity, resemblance, agreement|词性:名词
