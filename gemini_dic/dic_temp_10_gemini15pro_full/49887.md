demureness:/dɪˈmjʊrnəs/| n. |端庄，娴静，矜持|The quality of being demure; reserved, modest, and shy behavior, often in a way that is considered attractive.|Her demureness and quiet grace made her instantly likeable. 她的端庄娴静和优雅气质让人一见倾心。|Synonyms: modesty, shyness, reserve, reticence|Antonyms: boldness, brashness, forwardness|noun

Etymology:
From demure + -ness.  Demure comes from Old French *demourer "to linger, stay," from Latin demorari "to delay, linger," from de- "down" + morari "to delay." The sense shifted to "grave, serious," then to "modest, reserved."
