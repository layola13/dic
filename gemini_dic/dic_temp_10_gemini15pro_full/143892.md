outpour:/ˈaʊtpɔːr/ (US), /ˈaʊtpɔː(r)/ (UK)| n.|倾泻，流露，迸发|A sudden and abundant expression of strong feelings, often in the form of words or tears.  A large amount of something that is produced or released.|例句：There was an outpour of grief after the tragic accident.  惨剧发生后，人们悲痛欲绝。

例句：The volcano's eruption led to an outpour of lava and ash. 火山爆发导致熔岩和火山灰倾泻而出。|近义词：effusion, outburst, discharge, flood, torrent|反义词：suppression, restraint, withholding|词性:noun

outpour:/ˈaʊtpɔːr/ (US), /ˈaʊtpɔː(r)/ (UK)| v.|倾泻，流露，迸发|To flow or pour out rapidly and copiously.|例句：The crowd outpoured into the streets after the concert.  音乐会结束后，人群涌上街头。

例句：The poet outpoured his emotions in his writing.  诗人将自己的情感倾注于笔端。|近义词：pour forth, gush, spill, overflow|反义词：contain, restrain, hold back|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + pour.  The prefix "out-" intensifies the action of pouring, implying a copious and forceful flow.
