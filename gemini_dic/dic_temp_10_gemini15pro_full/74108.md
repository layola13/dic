fouchyauchi: /fuːʃiˈaʊtʃi/ | adj. | 挑剔的，难伺候的，过于讲究的 |  A Scottish and Northern English word meaning fussy or overly particular, especially about food or comfort.  Derived from the Scots word "fouchy" (fussy, irritable) and possibly influenced by "faugh" (an expression of disgust). | Example: He's a bit fouchyauchi about his food and won't eat anything with onions. 他对食物有点挑剔，不吃任何带洋葱的东西。

Example:  She's very fouchyauchi about the temperature of her bathwater. 她对洗澡水的温度非常讲究。

| 近义词: fussy, finicky, picky, particular, fastidious | 反义词: easygoing, undemanding, tolerant | 词性: 形容词
