adulterers: /əˈdʌltərərz/
n.
通奸者，奸夫淫妇
A person who commits adultery, engaging in sexual relations with someone other than their spouse.
The tabloids were filled with stories about adulterers and their illicit affairs.  小报上充斥着关于通奸者及其不正当关系的故事。

The priest condemned the adulterers for their sins. 牧师谴责通奸者的罪行。

近义词: cheaters, infidels, unfaithfuls
反义词: faithful spouses, loyal partners
词性: noun (plural)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French avoutre, from Latin adulter, adulterium ("adultery"), from ad- ("to") + alter ("other"). The -er suffix denotes a person who performs the action.  来自古法语 avoutre，源自拉丁语 adulter、adulterium（“通奸”），来自 ad-（“到”）+ alter（“其他”）。 -er 后缀表示执行该动作的人。
