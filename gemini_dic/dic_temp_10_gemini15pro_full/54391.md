disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/ | n. vt. | 失望，幻灭 | The state of having lost one's illusions, especially about someone or something previously admired or respected. |  The election result was a disillusionment for many young voters. 选举结果让很多年轻选民感到失望。 |  He quickly became disillusioned with his new job. 他很快就对自己的新工作感到失望。| synonyms: disenchantment, disappointment,  discouragement | antonyms:  encouragement, hope, optimism | noun
disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/ | vt. | 使失望，使醒悟 | To free from illusion or naive belief. | The harsh realities of life disillusioned her. 现实生活的残酷使她不再抱有幻想。 | The experience disillusioned him about politics. 这次经历让他对政治不再抱有幻想。| synonyms: disenchant, undeceive | antonyms:  deceive, delude | verb
