misappraises:/ˌmɪsəˈpreɪz/ | v. | 误判，错误估价 | To assess or estimate something incorrectly, especially by undervaluing it. |  He misappraised the value of the antique and sold it for far less than it was worth. 他错误地估计了古董的价值，把它卖得很便宜。 | The realtor misappraised the house, leading to a significant loss for the seller. 房地产经纪人对房子的估价有误，导致卖家损失惨重。 |  underestimate, undervalue, misjudge, miscalculate | overestimate, overvalue | verb
