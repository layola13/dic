awkward:/ˈɔːkwərd/ (US) /ˈɔːkwəd/ (UK)| adj.|笨拙的，尴尬的，棘手的|Lacking grace or skill in movement or behavior; causing embarrassment or difficulty.|例句：He felt awkward dancing with her because he was much taller. 他和她跳舞时感到很尴尬，因为他比她高得多。|例句：It was an awkward moment when we realized we had both dated the same person. 当我们意识到我们都和同一个人约会过时，气氛变得很尴尬。|近义词：clumsy, ungainly, embarrassing, difficult|反义词:graceful, elegant, easy, comfortable|词性:形容词
Etymology: From the Old Norse *afugr, meaning "turned the wrong way," from *af- "off, away" + *ugga "to fear."
