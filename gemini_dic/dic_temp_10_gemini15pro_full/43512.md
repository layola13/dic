ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cravant, from Old French cravanté (“defeated, crushed”), past participle of cravanter (“to crush, defeat”), from cravante (“throat, nape of the neck”), from Vulgar Latin *crepantem, accusative singular of *crepans, present participle of *crepare (“to break, crack”), from Latin crepō (“to rattle, creak, crack”).
