canting:/ˈkæntɪŋ/ /ˈkæntɪŋ/| n. adj.|隐语，黑话；伪善的，虚伪的|The use of specialized language, especially by a particular group, often to exclude or mislead outsiders; insincere and hypocritical, especially in showing moral or religious concern.|例句：The thieves communicated in a canting jargon that the police couldn't understand. 小偷们用一种警察听不懂的黑话交流。

例句：His canting pronouncements about morality fooled no one. 他虚伪的道德宣言骗不了任何人。|近义词：jargon, slang, argot, hypocrisy, sanctimoniousness|反义词：sincerity, genuineness|词性:名词, 形容词

词源：From the Latin "cantāre" (to sing), via Old French "canter" (to chant, beg). Originally referred to the whining tone used by beggars, then extended to the specialized jargon they used, and finally to insincere or hypocritical language in general.
