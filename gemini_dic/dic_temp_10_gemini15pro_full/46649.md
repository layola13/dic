damning:/ˈdæmɪŋ/ | adj. |  定罪的； condemnatory; incriminating; 非常不利的； harmful; damaging | showing clearly that someone is guilty of a crime or serious mistake | The evidence against him was damning.  对他来说，证据非常不利。| synonyms: incriminating, condemning, convicting, accusatory | antonyms: exculpatory, vindicating, absolving | Adjective

damning:/ˈdæmɪŋ/ | adj. |  (Informal) 极糟糕的；terrible; awful | extremely bad | The review of his latest film was damning. 他最新电影的评论糟透了。| synonyms: terrible, awful, dreadful, atrocious | antonyms: excellent, superb, wonderful | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *damnynge, present participle of damnen (“to damn”), from Old French damner, from Latin damnāre (“to inflict loss upon, condemn, doom”), from damnum (“loss, damage”).
