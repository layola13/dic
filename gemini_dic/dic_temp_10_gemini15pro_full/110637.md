limpnesses:/ˈlɪmpnəsɪz/
n.| 柔软；无力；松弛 |The state of being limp; lack of firmness or rigidity. |  The old man's increasing limpnesses made it difficult for him to walk.  这位老人的身体越来越无力，使他行走困难。|flaccidity, laxity, softness|firmness, rigidity, stiffness|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From limpness + -es (plural suffix).  Limpness itself comes from limp (adjective) + -ness (noun-forming suffix). The adjective "limp" originates from Old English *limpian, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old Norse limpr "weak, crippled."
