breakthroughs:/ˈbreɪkθruːz/
|n.|突破；重大进展|A sudden, important discovery or development that helps to improve a situation or solve a problem.|Scientists have made a major breakthrough in the treatment of cancer. 科学家们在癌症治疗方面取得了重大突破。|
|近义词：advancement, progress, discovery, innovation|
|反义词：setback, failure, obstacle, impediment|
|词性: 名词(plural)|


breakthrough:/ˈbreɪkθruː/
|n.|突破；重大进展|A sudden, important discovery or development that helps to improve a situation or solve a problem.|The development of the vaccine was a major breakthrough.  疫苗的研制是一项重大突破。|
|近义词：advancement, progress, discovery, innovation|
|反义词：setback, failure, obstacle, impediment|
|词性: 名词(singular)|


词源:
From "break through,"  meaning to force a way through a barrier.  "Break" (Old English *brecan) and "through" (Old English þurh).
