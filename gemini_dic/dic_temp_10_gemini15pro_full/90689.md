humbugging:/hʌmˈbʌɡɪŋ/
n.|欺骗，哄骗|The act of deceiving or tricking someone.
|He accused the company of humbugging consumers with false advertising.|他指责该公司用虚假广告欺骗消费者。
|近义词：deceiving, tricking, defrauding, misleading|
|反义词：honesty, truthfulness|
|词性：名词|


humbug:/ˈhʌmbʌɡ/
n.|骗局，花招；骗子|Something designed to deceive or trick; a person who deceives others.
|The weight-loss pills were a complete humbug.|这些减肥药完全是个骗局。
|近义词：fraud, hoax, sham, trickery, charlatan|
|反义词：truth, reality|
|词性：名词|


humbug:/ˈhʌmbʌɡ/
v.|欺骗，哄骗|To deceive or trick someone.
|Don't let them humbug you into buying something you don't need.|别让他们骗你买你不需要的东西。
|近义词：deceive, trick, defraud, mislead|
|反义词：be honest, tell the truth|
|词性：动词|

humbug:/ˈhʌmbʌɡ/
adj.|假的，骗人的|False or deceptive.  (Less common usage)
|His claims of wealth proved to be humbug.|他声称自己很富有，结果证明是假的。
|近义词：fake, bogus, spurious, fraudulent|
|反义词：genuine, real, authentic|
|词性：形容词|


词源：
Origin uncertain. Possibly from a slang term for a hoax or trick.
