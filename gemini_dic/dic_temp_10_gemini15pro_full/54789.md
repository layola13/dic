dispensationalism:/ˌdɪspənˈseɪʃənəlɪzəm/| n. |时代论|A theological system that interprets biblical history as a series of distinct periods (dispensations) in which God deals with humanity in different ways, each characterized by a specific covenant or arrangement. It often emphasizes a literal interpretation of prophecy and a distinction between God's plans for Israel and the Church.|Many evangelical Christians adhere to dispensationalism.|Synonym: covenant theology (partially)|Antonym: n/a|noun
