federalist:/ˈfɛdərəlɪst/| n. adj.|联邦主义者；联邦制的|A person who advocates or supports a system of government in which several states unite under a central authority.|例句：He was a staunch federalist, believing in a strong national government. 他是一位坚定的联邦主义者，相信强大的中央政府。

例句：The Federalist Papers argued for the ratification of the Constitution. 《联邦党人文集》主张批准宪法。|近义词：unionist, nationalist|反义词：anti-federalist, separatist|词性:n. adj.

Etymology:
From Late Latin *foederālis* (“of a treaty, league”), from Latin *foedus* (“treaty, league, covenant”).
