pandoor:/pænˈdʊər/ | n. |  潘多尔兵；印度兵 | A soldier or policeman in India, especially during the period of British rule. Originally referring to an armed watchman or foot soldier in India. |  The pandoors were known for their fierce loyalty. 潘多尔兵以其强烈的忠诚而闻名。|  The British employed pandoors to maintain order in the colonies. 英国人雇佣潘多尔兵在殖民地维持秩序。 | synonyms: sepoy, soldier, guard | antonyms: civilian | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Portuguese *pandur*, from Hungarian *pandúr*, from Serbo-Croatian *pandur*, ultimately from Medieval Latin *banderius* (“standard-bearer”).
