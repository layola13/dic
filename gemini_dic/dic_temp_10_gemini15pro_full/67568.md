extrapolating:/ɪkˈstræpəleɪtɪŋ/
|v.|推断；外推；|Estimating or concluding something by assuming that existing trends will continue or a current method will remain applicable.|Scientists are extrapolating from current trends to predict future climate change. 科学家们根据目前的趋势推断未来的气候变化。|infer, deduce, project, conjecture|N/A|Verb

extrapolate:/ɪkˈstræpəleɪt/
|v.|推断；外推；|To infer or estimate by extending or projecting known information.|We can extrapolate the results of the study to the general population. 我们可以将研究结果推断到一般人群。|infer, deduce, project, conjecture|N/A|Verb

extrapolation:/ɪkˌstræpəˈleɪʃn/
|n.|推断；外推；|The act or process of extrapolating.|The economist's extrapolation of current economic trends suggests a recession is likely. 经济学家对当前经济趋势的推断表明经济衰退的可能性很大。|inference, deduction, projection, conjecture|N/A|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin extra ("outside, beyond") + pōlāre ("to polish, smooth") + -ate (verb-forming suffix)
