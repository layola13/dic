concha:/ˈkɒŋkə/ (UK), /ˈkɑːntʃə/ (US)| n.| 外耳; 耳廓 |The hollow part of the external ear. |The sound waves are collected by the concha. 声波被耳廓收集。 |He cupped his hand to his concha to hear better. 他用手捂住耳廓以便听得更清楚。| Synonyms: auricle, pinna |Antonyms: None| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *concha*, meaning "shell," from Latin *concha*, also meaning "shell," ultimately from Greek *κόγχη* (*konkhē*), also meaning "shell," especially a mussel or conch.
