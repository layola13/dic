grovel:/ˈɡrɒvəl/ (British), /ˈɡrɑːvəl/ (American)| vi.|卑躬屈膝，奴颜婢膝，匍匐前进|To behave in a way that shows you are very sorry for something or that you want something very much, often in a way that other people find unpleasant.|He groveled at the feet of the king, begging for mercy. 他跪伏在国王脚下，乞求怜悯。|近义词：crawl, fawn, cower|反义词：stand up to, defy|词性：动词

Etymology:
From Middle English *grovelen, frequentative of *groven, from Old Norse *grufa (“to stoop down,” “lie prone”).
