overexposes:/ˌoʊvərɪkˈspoʊzɪz/| v.|过度曝光|To expose (film, a photographic print, etc.) to light for too long a time or to too much light, resulting in a pale, washed-out image. Also, to expose (a person, idea, etc.) to something too much or for too long.|The photographer overexposed the film, ruining the pictures. 摄影师过度曝光了胶片，毁了照片。|近义词：overdo, saturate|反义词: underexpose|词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + expose.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively," "too much." "Expose" comes from the Latin "exponere," meaning "to put out," "to exhibit."
