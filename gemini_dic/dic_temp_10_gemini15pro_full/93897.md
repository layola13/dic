immortalizations:/ɪˌmɔːrtələˈzeɪʃənz/
n.
不朽化，永垂不朽，使不朽的事物
The act or process of making something immortal; the state of being immortalized.
The artist achieved immortalization through his iconic paintings.  这位艺术家通过他标志性的绘画作品获得了不朽的声誉。
The museum houses immortalizations of the nation's heroes.  博物馆收藏着国家英雄的不朽事迹。
近义词: commemorations, remembrances, perpetuation
反义词: oblivion, forgetting, destruction
词性: noun (plural)

immortalization:/ɪˌmɔːrtələˈzeɪʃən/
n.
不朽化，永垂不朽，使不朽的事物
The act or process of making something immortal; the state of being immortalized.
Shakespeare's works are an immortalization of the English language.  莎士比亚的作品使英语语言永垂不朽。
His bravery earned him immortalization in the annals of history. 他的英勇为他赢得了历史上的不朽地位。
近义词: commemoration, remembrance, perpetuation
反义词: oblivion, forgetting, destruction
词性: noun

词源:
From immortalize + -ation.  Immortalize comes from immortal + -ize. Immortal from Latin immortalis, from in- "not" + mortalis "mortal."
