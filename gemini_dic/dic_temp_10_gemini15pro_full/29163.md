cash-in-your-chips:/kæʃ ɪn jɔːr tʃɪps/| idiom | 兑现筹码；放弃；死；认输 | To quit or give up on something, often due to difficulty or failure; also, to die. Originally referring to exchanging gambling chips for money, it has broadened to mean ceasing any activity. | After failing the exam for the third time, he decided to cash in his chips and pursue a different career.  他考试三次不及格后，决定放弃，追求另一份职业。|Synonyms: give up, quit, surrender, die, pass away | Antonyms: persevere, continue, persist | idiom
