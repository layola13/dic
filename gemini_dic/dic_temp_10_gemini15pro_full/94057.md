Etymology:
From Late Latin *impassabilis, from in- "not" + passabilis "passable," from passus, past participle of pandere "to spread, stretch," from Proto-Indo-European *peth₂- "to spread, stretch."  The "-ities" suffix indicates the plural form of the abstract noun.
