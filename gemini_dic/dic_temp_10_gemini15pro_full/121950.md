missionary: /ˈmɪʃənəri/ (US) /ˈmɪʃənri/ (UK)| n. adj.|传教士，传教的|A person sent on a religious mission, especially one sent to promote Christianity in a foreign country.  Also, relating to or characteristic of missionaries or their work.|例句：The missionary traveled to Africa to spread the Gospel.  这位传教士前往非洲传播福音。|近义词：apostle, evangelist, preacher|反义词: nonbeliever, heathen|noun

missionary: /ˈmɪʃənəri/ (US) /ˈmɪʃənri/ (UK)| adj.|传教的|Relating to or characteristic of missionaries or their work.|例句：She embarked on a missionary journey to India. 她开始了去印度的传教之旅。|近义词：evangelical, apostolic|反义词: secular|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *missionarius, from Latin missio "a sending," from mittere "to send."  The sense of "person sent to propagate religious doctrines" is from 1559.
