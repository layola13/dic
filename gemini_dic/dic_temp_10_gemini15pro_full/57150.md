dowagerism:/ˈdaʊədʒərɪzəm/
n. |守寡，寡妇身份；老年妇女的专横跋扈；老年气派| The state or condition of being a dowager; the behavior, attitudes, or appearance considered typical of a dowager, often characterized by a dignified or imperious manner.  |She exhibited a certain dowagerism, even though she had never been married. 她表现出某种寡妇的气派，尽管她从未结过婚。| 近义词：authoritarianism, imperiousness, haughtiness|反义词:humility, modesty|名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "dowager" + "-ism."  "Dowager" comes from Old French "douairiere,"  meaning "endowed woman,"  from "douaire" (dower), ultimately from Latin "dotare" (to endow). "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine, etc.
