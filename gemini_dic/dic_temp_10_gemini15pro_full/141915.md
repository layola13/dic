ordinalism:/ˈɔːrdɪnəlɪzəm/
n.|教条主义; 等级主义|The belief that salvation is earned through participation in religious rituals and good works, as opposed to grace or faith alone.  It emphasizes the importance of the ordained clergy and the hierarchical structure of the church.|The theologian argued against ordinalism, emphasizing the importance of personal faith.|近义词:ritualism, legalism, formalism|反义词:pietism, evangelicalism|词性:名词

Etymology:
From ordinal (relating to order or rank) + -ism (forming nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, or practices).  Ultimately from Latin *ordo* (order, rank).
