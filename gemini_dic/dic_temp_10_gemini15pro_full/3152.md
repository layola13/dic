aethiops:/ˈiːθiɒps/| n.|埃塞俄比亚人；（古）黑人；一种黑色蝴蝶|A term historically used to refer to people from Ethiopia or, more broadly and often offensively, dark-skinned people. Also refers to a type of black butterfly.|He read about the aethiops in an old travelogue. 他在一本古老的游记中读到了关于埃塞俄比亚人的记载。|近义词：Ethiopian, African (though these are not perfect synonyms and can be problematic depending on context)|反义词: N/A (avoiding antonyms due to potential for racial implications)|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Latin aethiops, from Ancient Greek Αἰθίοψ (Aithíops, “Ethiopian; burnt-faced person”), from αἴθω (aíthō, “I burn”) + ὤψ (ṓps, “face”).
