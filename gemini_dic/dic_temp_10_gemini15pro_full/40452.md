contravallation:/ˌkɒntrəvəˈleɪʃn/ |ˌkɑːntrəvəˈleɪʃn/
n. |防御工事，围城工事|  A line of fortifications, especially one consisting of walls and trenches, built around a besieged place to protect the besiegers from sorties. | The Roman army built a massive contravallation around the city to prevent any breakout attempts.| Synonyms: rampart, fortification, bulwark, entrenchment | Antonyms:  breach, opening, gap | noun
