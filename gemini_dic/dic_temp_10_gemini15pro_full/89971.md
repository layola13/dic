horseweed:/ˈhɔːrswiːd/| n. |马草，加拿大飞蓬|A common North American weed (Conyza canadensis) with small white or yellowish flowers, also known as Canadian fleabane. It is tall and erect, often found in disturbed areas. Its leaves resemble those of some horsetails, hence the name. |The field was overgrown with horseweed.| 近义词：Canadian fleabane, butterweed, hogweed |反义词: N/A |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From horse + weed.  "Weed" in this context refers to any wild plant, not necessarily an undesirable one. The name likely arose from the plant's resemblance to some horsetails or because it was considered a weed commonly found where horses grazed.
