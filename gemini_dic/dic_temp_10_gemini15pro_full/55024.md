disqualified:/dɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪd/| adj.|不合格的，被取消资格的|Declared ineligible or unsuitable; prevented from participating or competing.|He was disqualified from the race for a false start. 他因起跑犯规被取消了比赛资格。|Synonyms: ineligible, unsuitable, debarred, ruled out|Antonyms: qualified, eligible, suitable|adjective

disqualify:/dɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪ/| vt.|取消…的资格，使…不合格|To declare ineligible or unsuitable; to prevent from participating or competing.|His poor eyesight disqualified him from military service. 他的视力差使他无法服兵役。|Synonyms: bar, preclude, prohibit, debar|Antonyms: qualify, enable, permit|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + qualify (to make suitable).  The "dis-" prefix comes from Latin, meaning "apart," "asunder," "away," "utterly," or having a privative, negative, or reversing force. "Qualify" comes from Late Latin qualificare "to give qualities to, characterize," from Latin qualis "of what sort" + facere "to make."
