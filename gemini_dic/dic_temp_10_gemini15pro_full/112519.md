louvre:/ˈluːvər/ (US), /ˈluːvrə/ (UK)| n.|卢浮宫；百叶窗；通风窗|A famous museum in Paris, France, or a window or part of a roof with angled slats that can be adjusted to control light and ventilation.|例句：We visited the Louvre Museum in Paris. 我们参观了巴黎的卢浮宫博物馆。

例句：The louvres on the roof were open to let in the cool breeze. 屋顶上的通风窗打开了，让凉风吹进来。|近义词：museum, gallery (for museum); vent, ventilator (for window)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


louvre:/ˈluːvər/ (US), /ˈluːvrə/ (UK)| n.|百叶窗；散热孔；天窗|A framework of angled slats that can be adjusted to admit more or less light and air, used in windows, roofs, and ventilation systems.|例句：The louvres of the air conditioning unit were clogged with dust. 空调机的散热孔被灰尘堵塞了。


例句：They opened the louvres in the roof to ventilate the attic. 他们打开了屋顶的天窗来给阁楼通风。|近义词：vent, shutter, blind, grille|反义词: N/A|词性:noun
