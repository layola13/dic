micro-scale:/ˈmaɪkroʊ skeɪl/ | adj. |微尺度的，微型的|Relating to or involving minute quantities or a minute scale; extremely small.  Characterized by small dimensions or a small scale, especially smaller than the macroscopic scale but larger than the atomic or molecular scale. |Scientists are studying micro-scale phenomena in the lab. 科学家们正在实验室里研究微尺度现象。|Micro-scale robots are being developed for medical applications.  微型机器人正在被开发用于医疗应用。| 近义词: microscopic, miniature, minuscule | 反义词: macro-scale, large-scale | 词性: adjective

micro-scale:/ˈmaɪkroʊ skeɪl/ | n. |微观尺度|The scale of things that are very small, typically smaller than the macroscopic scale but larger than the atomic or molecular scale. |Research at the micro-scale is crucial for understanding cellular processes. 在微观尺度的研究对于理解细胞过程至关重要。|The micro-scale of the experiment allows for precise manipulation of individual molecules. 实验的微观尺度允许精确操纵单个分子。| 近义词: microscopic level, miniature scale | 反义词: macro-scale, large-scale | 词性: noun
