interstitial:/ˌɪntərˈstɪʃəl/ |adj. n.|间质的，间隙的；间隙物|Existing in the interstices or gaps of a tissue or structure.  Relating to or situated in the interstices of a tissue or organ.  Also, something situated within the interstices.|

例句：
Interstitial fluid plays a vital role in transporting nutrients to cells.
间质液在将营养物质输送到细胞中起着至关重要的作用。

The doctor diagnosed an interstitial lung disease.
医生诊断出一种间质性肺病。


近义词：intervening, intermediate, in-between|
反义词：continuous, solid|
词性：adjective, noun|

interstitial:/ˌɪntərˈstɪʃəl/ |n.|间隙物|Something situated within the interstices, such as an advertisement appearing between sections of a website or program.|

例句：
The website displayed annoying pop-up interstitials.
该网站显示恼人的弹出式插页广告。

Many mobile games use interstitials to generate revenue.
许多手机游戏使用插页式广告来创收。


近义词：advertisement, insert, interruption|
反义词：N/A|
词性：noun|


词源: From Latin *interstitialis*, from interstitium "interval, space between," from inter- "between" + sistere "to stand, set, place".
