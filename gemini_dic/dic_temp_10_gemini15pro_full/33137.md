christom: /ˈkrɪsəm/ | n. | 圣油；洗礼油；受洗者 | Consecrated oil used in Christian ceremonies, especially baptism, confirmation, and ordination.  It symbolizes the Holy Spirit and spiritual rebirth.  Derived from the Greek word "chrisma" meaning "anointing." | The priest applied the christom to the baby's forehead during the baptism.  神父在婴儿洗礼时将圣油涂抹在其额头上。| synonyms: anointing oil, holy oil | antonyms: N/A | noun
