nonimputably:/ˌnɒnɪmˈpjuːtəbli/
| adv. | 不可归咎地，无罪地 | Not in a way that can be blamed or considered culpable. |  He acted nonimputably in the situation, following all the rules and regulations. 他在这种情况下行事无可非议，遵守了所有的规章制度。|  blamelessly, irreproachably, unimpeachably | culpably, blameworthy | adverb

Etymology:
From non- + imputable + -ly.  "Imputable" comes from Late Latin *imputabilis, from imputare "to ascribe, attribute," from in- "in" + putare "to think, reckon."
