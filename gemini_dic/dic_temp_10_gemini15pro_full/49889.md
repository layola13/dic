demurer:/dɪˈmjʊərər/| n.|抗辩；异议 (尤指法律上的) |A demurer is a legal pleading that objects to an opponent's pleading, stating that even if the facts alleged are true, they are insufficient to establish a cause of action or defense. |The defendant filed a demurer, arguing that the plaintiff's complaint failed to state a claim upon which relief could be granted.| 近义词:objection, challenge, protest| 反义词:acceptance, concession| 词性:名词

demure:/dɪˈmjʊər/| adj.|娴静的；端庄的；矜持的|Someone who is demure is quiet and modest in a way that is considered proper for women, especially in the past. |She gave him a demure smile.| 近义词:modest, reserved, shy| 反义词:bold, immodest, outgoing| 词性:形容词

词源:
From Old French *demorer "to linger, delay," from Latin demorari "to delay, linger, tarry," from de- "down" + morari "to delay." The legal sense of "to raise an objection" developed from the sense of "to delay proceedings." The adjective "demure" developed from the sense of "reserved, making a show of reluctance."
