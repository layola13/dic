muskox:/ˈmʌskˌɑks/ | n. | 麝牛 | A large, shaggy-coated wild ox (Ovibos moschatus) with a strong musky odor, native to the Arctic regions of North America and Greenland. | Muskoxen are well adapted to the harsh Arctic climate. 麝牛非常适应严酷的北极气候。|  Muskoxen form defensive circles to protect their young from predators. 麝牛会形成防御圈来保护幼崽免受捕食者的攻击。 | synonyms: ovibos | antonyms: N/A | noun

Etymology: From musk + ox.  "Musk" comes from Late Middle English muske, from Old French musc, ultimately from Sanskrit muṣká meaning "testicle," referring to the scent gland.  "Ox" comes from Old English oxa.
