microlith:/ˈmaɪkrəlɪθ/ | n. | 微石器 | A small, chipped stone tool, typically a blade or flake, characteristic of the Mesolithic period. |  Archaeologists unearthed numerous microliths at the site. 考古学家在遗址中挖掘出大量的微石器。

microlith:/ˈmaɪkrəlɪθ/ | n. | 微晶 | A minute crystal, often occurring in masses. | Microliths are often found in volcanic rocks. 微晶经常在火山岩中发现。


microlith: /ˈmaɪkrəlɪθ/ | n. | 微结石 | A very small calculus. | The patient's urine contained microliths. 病人的尿液中含有微结石。


Etymology:  From Greek *mikros* (small) + *lithos* (stone)

Synonyms:  chip, flake, bladelet (for stone tools); crystallite (for crystals)

Antonyms:  megalith (for stone tools - opposite in size)
