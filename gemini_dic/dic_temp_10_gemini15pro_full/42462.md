counterfactually:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəli/ | adv. | 反事实地，与事实相反地 |  In a way that is not consistent with what actually happened; contrary to the facts. |  Counterfactually, if I had studied harder, I would have gotten a better grade.  反事实地，如果我学习更努力，我就能取得更好的成绩。| Synonyms: hypothetically, theoretically | Antonyms: factually, actually | Adverb

Etymology:
From "counter-" (against, opposite) + "factual" (relating to facts) + "-ly" (adverb suffix).  The word "fact" ultimately derives from Latin "factum" (deed, act).
