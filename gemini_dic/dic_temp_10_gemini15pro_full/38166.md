compromiser:/ˈkɑːmprəmaɪzər/ | n. |妥协者，折衷者|A person who is willing to compromise or make concessions.|Example: He is a compromiser, always trying to find the middle ground. 他是一个妥协者，总是试图找到中间立场。|Example:  She's known as a compromiser, skilled at finding solutions that satisfy everyone. 她以善于妥协而闻名，能够找到让每个人都满意的解决方案。|近义词:mediator, conciliator, negotiator|反义词:hardliner, zealot|词性:noun

compromise:/ˈkɑːmprəmaɪz/ | n.  v. |妥协，折衷；妥协方案，折衷方案；危害，损害；（使）妥协，（使）让步|An agreement or settlement of a dispute that is reached by each side making concessions.|
Example: They reached a compromise after hours of negotiation. 经过数小时的谈判，他们达成了妥协。|
Example:  The compromise involved both sides giving up something they wanted.  这个妥协方案涉及双方都放弃了他们想要的东西。|
近义词:agreement, settlement, concession|反义词:disagreement, conflict, stand-off|词性:noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *compromis*, past participle of compromettre "to mutually promise," from Latin compromittere "to make a mutual promise," from com- "together" + promittere "to promise."
