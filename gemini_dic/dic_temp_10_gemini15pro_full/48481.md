deep-sixes: /ˈdiːpˈsɪksɪz/ | v. | 彻底放弃；销毁；抛弃 | To discard or get rid of something completely and permanently, often in a secretive or clandestine manner. Originally referring to discarding something by throwing it overboard in the deep sea. |  The company deep-sixed the project after it proved too costly. 公司因为成本太高而彻底放弃了这个项目。

| The government deep-sixed the controversial report. 政府销毁了这份有争议的报告。 | Synonyms: discard, ditch, scrap, jettison, abandon, bury | Antonyms:  keep, retain, salvage, resurrect | verb


deep-six: /ˈdiːpˈsɪks/ | n. |  (非正式) 彻底放弃；销毁；抛弃 (名词形式较少用) | The act of discarding something completely and permanently. | The project got the deep-six after the CEO resigned.  CEO辞职后，这个项目就被彻底放弃了。 | Synonyms: disposal, discarding, elimination | Antonyms: retention, preservation | noun
