longtail:/ˈlɔːŋteɪl/ | adj. n. |长尾的，长尾效应；长尾市场|Relating to or part of the long tail (of a statistical distribution).  Also a noun referring to the long tail itself, particularly in a business context.| Many online bookstores profit from the longtail, selling small quantities of a vast number of different books.  许多在线书店都从长尾效应中获利，销售大量不同种类书籍的小数量。 |近义词: niche, specialized |反义词: mainstream, popular |词性: adjective, noun

long tail:/ˈlɔːŋ teɪl/ | n. |长尾| The part of a statistical distribution having a long, low-probability tail. Also refers to a business strategy of selling a large number of niche products. |The long tail of the sales curve represented less popular items but still contributed significantly to overall revenue. 销售曲线的长尾代表了不太受欢迎的商品，但仍然对总收入做出了重大贡献。 |近义词: niche market |反义词: mass market |词性: noun

词源：The term "long tail" was coined by Chris Anderson in 2003 in an article in Wired magazine, describing the business strategy of selling a large number of unique items with relatively small quantities sold of each.  The concept is derived from statistical distributions with a long, low-probability tail.
