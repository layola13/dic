词源:  From "causable" + "-ity" + "-'s" (possessive). "Causable" derives from "cause" (from Old French "cause," ultimately from Latin "causa") + "-able" (suffix indicating capability).  "-ity" forms abstract nouns from adjectives, and "-'s" marks possession.
