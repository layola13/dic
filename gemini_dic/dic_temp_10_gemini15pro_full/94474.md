impone:/ɪmˈpoʊz/ (US) /ɪmˈpəʊz/ (UK)| v. |强加；征税；欺骗|to force something on someone; to establish something as a rule or law; to take unfair advantage of someone|The government imposed a new tax on cigarettes. 政府对香烟征收了新的税。|Synonyms: inflict, levy, enforce|Antonyms: remove, lift, repeal|verb

impone:/ɪmˈpoʊz/ (US) /ɪmˈpəʊz/ (UK)| v. |利用；欺骗|to take unfair advantage of someone's kindness or generosity|Don't let him impose on your good nature.  别让他利用你的好心。|Synonyms: exploit, take advantage of|Antonyms: assist, help|verb
