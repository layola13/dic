loughs:/lɒks/ | n. | 湖泊；海湾（尤指爱尔兰和苏格兰的） | A lake or an inlet of the sea, especially in Ireland or Scotland. | We visited the beautiful loughs of Killarney. 我们参观了美丽的基拉尼湖泊。|Synonyms: lakes, inlets | Antonyms: mountains, plains | noun

Etymology: From Irish *loch and Scottish Gaelic loch, from Proto-Celtic *lokos, meaning "pool, lake, pond". Cognate with Welsh llwch ("lake"), Breton louch ("pond, marsh"), Cornish logh ("pool, lake"), and ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *lewk- ("to bend, curve").
