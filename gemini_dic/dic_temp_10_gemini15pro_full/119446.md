microliths: /ˈmaɪkrəlɪθs/ | n. | 微石器; 小结石 | Small stone tools, typically a centimeter or so in length, that were used in prehistoric times;  minute concretions, usually of crystalline matter, formed in various parts of the body. |  Archaeologists discovered numerous microliths at the site, indicating a prehistoric settlement. 考古学家在该遗址发现了大量的微石器，表明这里曾有一个史前定居点。 Kidney stones are sometimes composed of microliths. 肾结石有时由小结石组成。 | synonyms: flints, chips (for stone tools); calculi (for body stones) | antonyms: macroliths (for stone tools) | noun
