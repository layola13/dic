humor's:/ˈhjuːmərz/ | n. | 幽默，诙谐 | humor's is the possessive form of "humor," meaning "belonging to humor" or "of humor." It refers to a quality, characteristic, or aspect of humor. | The humor's strength lies in its subtle irony. 这种幽默的力量在于其微妙的讽刺。| synonyms: wit's, comedy's | antonyms: seriousness's, gravity's |  possessive noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English humour, from Old French humour, from Latin humor (“moisture, liquid”), from Proto-Indo-European *sug- (“juice; sap”). Cognate with Spanish humor, Italian umore.  More at humid. The sense development in English started with the ancient physiological theory of the four humours, thought to determine a person's health and temperament.
