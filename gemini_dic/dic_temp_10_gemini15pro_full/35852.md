cockpit: /ˈkɒkpɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkpɪt/ (US)| n.| 驾驶舱，驾驶座，战场|A compartment for the pilot and sometimes other crew members in an aircraft or spacecraft.  Also historically, a pit for cockfighting. |The pilot checked the instruments in the cockpit before takeoff.  飞行员在起飞前检查了驾驶舱的仪表。|Synonyms: flight deck, control cabin|Antonyms: cabin (in a broader sense)|noun

cockpit: /ˈkɒkpɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkpɪt/ (US)| n.| 竞技场，斗争场所|A place of combat or intense competition.  Derived from its historical use as a cockfighting arena. |The boardroom became a cockpit of competing egos.  董事会议室成了竞争激烈的自我角逐的场所。|Synonyms: battleground, arena|Antonyms: haven, sanctuary|noun
