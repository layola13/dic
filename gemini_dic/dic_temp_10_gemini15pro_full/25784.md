bureaucrat: /ˈbjʊərəkræt/ (US), /ˈbjʊərəkræt/ (UK) | n. | 官僚 | An official in a government department, especially one perceived as being concerned with procedural correctness at the expense of people's needs. |  The bureaucrats insisted on following every rule to the letter, even though it delayed the aid getting to the victims. 官僚们坚持严格遵守每一条规则，即使这会延延误援助到达灾民手中。| Example 2:  The project was stalled by bureaucratic red tape.  这个项目因官僚主义的繁文缛节而停滞不前。 | Synonyms: official, administrator, civil servant | Antonyms: nonconformist, maverick | noun

bureaucracy: /bjʊˈrɒkrəsi/ (US), /bjʊˈrɒkrəsi/ (UK) | n. | 官僚主义 | A system of government in which most of the important decisions are made by state officials rather than by elected representatives.  | The endless bureaucracy made it nearly impossible to start a new business. 无休止的官僚主义使得几乎不可能创办新企业。| Example 2: She complained about the bureaucracy and inefficiency of the local government. 她抱怨当地政府的官僚作风和低效。 | Synonyms: red tape, officialdom, administration | Antonyms:  democracy,  anarchy | noun
