contend:/kənˈtɛnd/ | v. | 竞争；主张；声称；争夺 | To strive in competition or rivalry; to assert or maintain as a position in an argument. |  He contended for the championship. 他竞争冠军。 | 近义词: compete, argue, assert, maintain | 反义词: yield, surrender | verb

contested:/kənˈtɛstɪd/ | adj. | 有争议的；竞争激烈的 | Disputed or argued against; subject to competition or rivalry. | The election was highly contested. 选举竞争非常激烈。 | 近义词: disputed, challenged, controversial | 反义词: undisputed, unchallenged, agreed upon | adjective

content:/ˈkɒntɛnt/ | n. | 内容；目录；容量 | The things that are held or included in something; the subjects or topics covered in a book, speech, or program. | The content of the book is very interesting. 这本书的内容很有趣。| 近义词: subject matter, substance, text | 反义词: form, style | noun

content:/kənˈtɛnt/ | adj. | 满足的；满意的 | In a state of peaceful happiness. | She is content with her life. 她对自己的生活很满足。| 近义词: satisfied, happy, pleased | 反义词: dissatisfied, unhappy, displeased | adjective

contented:/kənˈtɛntɪd/ | adj. | 满足的，满意的 | Showing or feeling happiness and satisfaction, especially in a quiet way. | He led a contented life in the countryside. 他在乡下过着满足的生活。| 近义词: satisfied, happy, pleased, cheerful | 反义词: dissatisfied, unhappy, discontented, restless | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "contendere" (to stretch out, strive, contend), from "com-" (together) + "tendere" (to stretch).
