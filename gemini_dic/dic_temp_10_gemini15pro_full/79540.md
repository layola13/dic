glochidium: /ɡləˈkɪdiəm/ | n. | 钩介幼虫；瓣鳃幼虫 | A larval stage of certain freshwater mussels (Unionidae), characterized by a bivalved shell with hooks or teeth that enable it to attach to the gills or fins of a fish, where it undergoes further development. | Example 1: The glochidium larva of the mussel attaches to the gills of a fish.  瓣鳃的钩介幼虫附着在鱼的鳃上。

Example 2:  The fish serves as a host for the mussel's glochidium stage.  这种鱼是贻贝钩介幼虫阶段的宿主。 | Synonym: larva | Antonym: adult | noun
