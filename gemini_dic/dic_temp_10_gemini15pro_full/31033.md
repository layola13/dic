chamber:/ˈtʃeɪmbər/ (US), /ˈtʃeɪmbə/ (UK)| n.|房间，室；会议厅；腔，心室|A room, especially a bedroom or private room; a hall used by a legislative or judicial body; an enclosed space or cavity.|
The queen retired to her chamber. 女王退到她的房间。
The members of parliament debated the issue in the chamber. 国会议员在会议厅辩论这个问题。
The heart has four chambers. 心脏有四个腔室。
|近义词：room, hall, compartment, cavity|反义词:|n.

chamber:/ˈtʃeɪmbər/ (US), /ˈtʃeɪmbə/ (UK)| v.|把…关进房间；装填（弹药）|To enclose in or as if in a chamber; to load (a firearm).|
He chambered a round in the rifle. 他把子弹装进步枪。
The prisoner was chambered in a small cell. 囚犯被关在一个小牢房里。
|近义词：confine, enclose, load|反义词:release|v.
