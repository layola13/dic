abiding:/əˈbaɪdɪŋ/| adj.|持久的，永恒的，不变的|Lasting, enduring, permanent.  It implies a firm and unshakeable attachment or belief.|She has an abiding love for her family. 她对家人有着永恒的爱。

He has an abiding faith in his abilities. 他对自己的能力有着坚定的信念。|近义词：lasting, enduring, permanent, steadfast, unwavering|反义词：temporary, fleeting, transient, ephemeral|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English abiden, from Old English ābīdan (“to await, remain, continue, endure, last”), from a- (“at, on, in”) + bīdan (“to wait, remain, abide”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *bidaną (“to wait, tarry”).  Cognate with Dutch bijden (“to wait”), German beiden (“to offer, present, announce”).  The present participle abiding became an adjective.

USAGE->
An abiding characteristic or quality is one that continues for a long time and is not likely to change. It is often used to describe emotions, beliefs, and interests.

Example 1:  Despite the challenges, her abiding optimism never faltered. 尽管面临挑战，她始终保持乐观。

Example 2:  He has an abiding interest in history. 他对历史有着持久的兴趣。
