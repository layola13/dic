discoloured:/dɪsˈkʌləd/ /dɪsˈkʌlɚd/| adj.| 变色的，褪色的，脏的| Having lost its original colour; stained or marked with foreign matter; faded. | The old photographs were discoloured with age.  这些老照片因年代久远而褪色了。| 近义词：faded, stained, bleached, tarnished | 反义词：bright, vibrant, colourful |词性: 形容词

discolour:/dɪsˈkʌlə(r)/ /dɪsˈkʌlɚ/| v.| 使变色，使褪色| To cause (something) to change colour, typically to a duller or less attractive shade.  |Exposure to sunlight can discolour fabrics. 暴露在阳光下会使织物褪色。|近义词：stain, tarnish, fade, bleach|反义词：brighten, colour, dye|词性: 动词


词源:
dis- (prefix) + colour + -ed (suffix)

"dis-"  是一个表示否定或相反意义的前缀，源自拉丁语。
"colour" 源自古法语 "colur," 最终源自拉丁语 "color."
"-ed" 是一个形容词后缀，表示“具有…的特征”。
