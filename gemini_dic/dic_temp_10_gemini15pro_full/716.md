absenteeism: /ˌæbsənˈtiːɪzəm/
n.
旷工，缺勤
The high rate of absenteeism is costing the company a lot of money.  高缺勤率使公司损失了很多钱。
The new policy is designed to reduce absenteeism. 新政策旨在减少旷工。
English Explanation: habitual failure to be present at work or at school.
例句：
The company is struggling with high levels of absenteeism. = 公司正在努力应对高缺勤率。

Absenteeism is a serious problem in some schools. = 旷课在一些学校是一个严重的问题。

近义词：truancy, non-attendance, absence
反义词：attendance, presence
词性：noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From absent + -ee + -ism.  Absent derives from Latin absens, present participle of abesse "to be away," from ab- "away" + esse "to be."  The suffix -ee denotes one who is the object of a specified action (as in employee, trustee). The suffix -ism denotes a practice, system, doctrine, etc.

USAGE->
Because absenteeism is a noun, it doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It is used as the subject or object of a sentence. It can be modified by adjectives and prepositions.
