hypermutabilities:/ˌhaɪpərˌmjuːtəˈbɪlɪtiz/ | n. | 超突变性；高度突变性 | The tendency of certain organisms or cells to have a significantly increased rate of mutation.  This can be caused by defects in DNA repair mechanisms or other factors. |  The researchers studied the hypermutabilities of certain cancer cells. 研究人员研究了某些癌细胞的超突变性。 |  High mutation rates, increased mutability |  Low mutation rates, stability | noun
