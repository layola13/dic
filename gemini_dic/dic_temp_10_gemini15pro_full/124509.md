moundbuilder:/ˈmaʊndˌbɪldər/
n.
土墩建造者，指美洲原住民
A member of one of the various American Indian peoples, especially of the eastern United States, who built large mounds of earth.
The moundbuilders left behind intricate earthworks. 土墩建造者留下了复杂的土方工程。
The museum displayed artifacts from the moundbuilder cultures. 博物馆展出了土墩建造者文化的文物。
synonyms: ancient American Indian, pre-Columbian American
antonyms: N/A
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From mound + builder.  "Mound" comes from Old English *mund, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *mundaz.  "Builder" comes from Old English byldan, from Proto-Germanic *bildaną.
