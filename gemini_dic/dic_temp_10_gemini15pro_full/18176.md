belabour:/bɪˈleɪbər/| vt.|痛打；反复强调；冗长地讨论|To explain, discuss, or argue something at excessive length or in excessive detail.|He belaboured the point about the importance of punctuality. 他反复强调准时的重要性。|Synonyms: overemphasize, dwell on, harp on|Antonyms: understate, downplay|verb

belabour:/bɪˈleɪbər/| vt.|痛打；|To beat or attack someone violently.|The thugs belaboured him with sticks. 暴徒用棍棒痛打他。|Synonyms: batter, thrash, pummel|Antonyms: caress, soothe|verb
