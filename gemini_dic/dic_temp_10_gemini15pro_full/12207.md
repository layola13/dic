asafoetida: /æsəˈfɛtɪdə/ | n. | 阿魏，阿魏胶 | A pungent-smelling resinous gum obtained from the roots of several species of Ferula, used as a flavoring agent in Indian cooking and formerly in medicine. |  Example 1: A pinch of asafoetida is added to the lentil soup for flavor. 一小撮阿魏被添加到扁豆汤中调味。

Example 2:  Asafoetida is believed to have medicinal properties in some cultures.  在某些文化中，阿魏被认为具有药用价值。 | Synonym: hing, devil's dung, stinking gum | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a specific spice) | Noun
