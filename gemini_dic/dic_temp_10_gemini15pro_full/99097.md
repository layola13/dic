intercalate:/ɪnˈtɜːrkəleɪt/ | v. | 插入，闰插 | To insert (something) between layers or into a series; to insert (a day) into the calendar. |  The extra day is intercalated into February every four years.  闰日每四年插入二月一次。| synonyms: insert, interpose, interpolate | antonyms: remove, extract, delete | verb

intercalate:/ɪnˈtɜːrkəleɪt/ | n. | 闰; 闰日 | An intercalated thing, especially an extra day added to the calendar. | The Gregorian calendar reform involved changing the rules for intercalation. 格里高利历改革涉及改变闰日规则。 | synonyms: insertion, interpolation | antonyms: removal, extraction | noun

intercalation:/ɪnˌtɜːrkəˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 插入，插层；闰；（化）嵌入 | The act of intercalating something. | The intercalation of a leap day keeps the calendar synchronized with the solar year. 闰日的插入使日历与太阳年保持同步。 | synonyms: insertion, interpolation, interposition | antonyms: removal, extraction, deletion | noun
