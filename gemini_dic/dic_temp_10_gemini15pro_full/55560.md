distrusting: /dɪsˈtrʌstɪŋ/ | adj. | 不信任的，怀疑的 | Inclined not to trust; suspicious. |  She cast a distrusting glance at the stranger. 她用怀疑的目光看着那个陌生人。|  He had a distrusting nature, always questioning everyone's motives. 他生性多疑，总是质疑每个人的动机。| synonyms: suspicious, wary, skeptical, cynical, dubious | antonyms: trusting, gullible, naive, credulous | adjective

distrust: /dɪsˈtrʌst/ | n. vt. | 不信任，怀疑 | Lack of trust or confidence.  To regard with doubt or suspicion. |  There is a deep distrust between the two communities. 这两个社群之间存在着深深的不信任。| She distrusted his motives for helping her. 她不信任他帮助她的动机。| synonyms: suspicion, mistrust, doubt, skepticism, wariness | antonyms: trust, confidence, faith, belief, credence | noun, verb

Etymology:
From dis- "not, opposite of" + trust.  The prefix "dis-" negates the meaning of "trust."
