disloyalist:/dɪsˈlɔɪəlɪst/
n.|不忠者，变节者|A person who is disloyal to a cause, country, or ruler. |Example: The disloyalists were punished for their treachery.  变节者因其背叛行为受到了惩罚。|Example:  During the war, disloyalists were often imprisoned. 战争期间，不忠者经常被监禁。|Synonym: traitor, rebel, defector, renegade |Antonym: loyalist, patriot|noun


disloyal:/dɪsˈlɔɪəl/
adj.|不忠的，不忠诚的|Not loyal; faithless. |Example: He was accused of being disloyal to the company. 他被指控对公司不忠。|Example: Her disloyal actions shocked her friends. 她不忠的行为震惊了她的朋友们。 |Synonym: faithless, treacherous, unfaithful, perfidious |Antonym: loyal, faithful, true, steadfast|adjective



ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + loyal + -ist (forming agent nouns).  "Loyal" ultimately comes from the Old French "loial," meaning "legal," from the Latin "legalis."
