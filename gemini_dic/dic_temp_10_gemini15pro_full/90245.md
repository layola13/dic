houseboating:/ˈhaʊsˌboʊtɪŋ/
n.|乘船屋旅行，居住在船屋上|The act of living on and traveling in a houseboat, especially for recreational purposes.  It combines the comforts of a home with the freedom of exploring waterways.|We spent our summer vacation houseboating on Lake Powell. 我们在鲍威尔湖上乘船屋度过了暑假。|近义词：canal boating, boating, cruising, sailing|反义词: land-based vacation


houseboat:/ˈhaʊsboʊt/
n.|船屋|A boat that is designed and equipped for use as a dwelling.|They lived in a houseboat on the river. 他们在河上的船屋里生活。|近义词：barge, floating home|反义词:apartment, house

ETYMOLOGY->
house (n.) Old English hus "dwelling, shelter, house, building," from Proto-Germanic *husan (source also of Old Saxon, Old Frisian hus, Old Norse hus, Dutch huis, German Haus, Gothic hus).
boat (n.) Old English bat "boat," from Proto-Germanic *baitaz (source also of Old Norse batr, Middle Dutch boot, Dutch boot, Old High German boito, German Boot).
-ing (suffix) forming present participles and gerunds.
