notionalities:/ˌnəʊʃəˈnælɪtiz/ | n. | 国籍（复数）| The legal relationship between a person and a state, by which the person owes allegiance to the state and the state in turn is obliged to protect the person. |  Many people of dual nationalities find it convenient for business and travel. 许多双重国籍的人发现这对商务和旅行很方便。| nationalities: citizenships, allegiances | antonym: statelessness | noun

notionality:/ˌnəʊʃəˈnælɪti/ | n. | 概念；名义上的东西| A concept or theoretical construct; something that exists in theory but not necessarily in reality. | The notional value of the contract was $1 million, but the actual profit was much lower. 合同的名义价值是100万美元，但实际利润要低得多。| notionality: concept, abstraction, idea | antonym: reality, actuality | noun
