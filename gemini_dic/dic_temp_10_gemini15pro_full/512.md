abolishments: /əˈbɒlɪʃmənts/
n.
废除，取消
The act of formally putting an end to a system, practice, or institution.
The abolition of slavery marked a turning point in history. 奴隶制的废除标志着历史的转折点。

The French Revolution saw the abolition of the monarchy. 法国大革命见证了君主制的废除。

近义词: eliminations, eradications, terminations
反义词: establishments, institutions, restorations
词性: 名词(plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French abolissement, from abolir "to abolish," from Latin abolere "to destroy, do away with," from ab- "from, away from" + olere "to grow" (related to adolere "to grow up").  The suffix "-ment" forms nouns of action or result.


USAGE->
