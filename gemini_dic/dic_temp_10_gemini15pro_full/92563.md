hysteroepilepsy: /ˌhɪstərəʊˈɛpɪlɛpsi/ (UK) /ˌhɪstəroʊˈɛpɪlɛpsi/ (US) | n. | 歇斯底里性癫痫，癔病性癫痫 | A historical term for a form of epilepsy, now considered a conversion disorder, characterized by seizures resembling those of epilepsy but without the characteristic EEG changes and often associated with psychological stress or trauma. It often involves dramatic, convulsive movements and apparent loss of consciousness but is considered a psychological rather than a neurological condition. | She was diagnosed with hysteroepilepsy after experiencing seizure-like episodes during times of extreme stress.  她经历了极端压力下的癫痫样发作之后被诊断为歇斯底里性癫痫。|  conversion disorder, pseudoseizures | epilepsy, true seizures | noun
