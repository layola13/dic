crock:/krɒk/ (UK) /krɑːk/ (US)| n. |罐子；瓦罐；废话；胡说八道|A large earthenware pot with a wide mouth. Also, something that is old and broken, or an idea or statement that is foolish or untrue.|例句：She cooked the stew in a large crock. 她用一个大瓦罐炖了肉。

例句：That's a crock! I don't believe a word of it. 那是一派胡言！我一个字也不信。

近义词：pot, jar, nonsense, rubbish, baloney|反义词:truth, fact|词性:noun


crock:/krɒk/ (UK) /krɑːk/ (US)| v. |(使)破裂；(使)衰弱|To break or become weak, especially under strain.|例句：The pressure of the job was beginning to crock him. 工作的压力开始让他崩溃。

例句：The handle of the mug crock ed under the weight of the hot tea.  杯子的把手在热茶的重量下裂开了。

近义词：break, weaken, crumble|反义词:strengthen, reinforce|词性:verb
