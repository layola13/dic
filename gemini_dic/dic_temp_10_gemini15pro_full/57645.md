draw-in:/ˈdrɔː ɪn/ |n. |收缩；卷入；吸引|A gradual reduction or decrease in something, or the act of attracting someone or something.  It can also refer to the closing stages of a contest or activity.|The draw-in of capital during the financial crisis worsened the situation. 金融危机期间资本的收缩使情况恶化。|Synonyms: decrease, reduction, contraction, attraction |Antonyms: expansion, increase, repulsion|noun

draw-in:/ˈdrɔː ɪn/ |v. |（天）渐短；（使）参与，卷入；吸入|To become shorter (days); to involve or cause someone to become involved; to breathe in.|As winter approaches, the days draw in.  随着冬天的临近，白天变短了。|Synonyms: shorten, involve, engage, inhale |Antonyms: lengthen, exclude, exhale|verb
