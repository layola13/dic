connivance:/kəˈnaɪvəns/
n.|共谋，纵容，默许|Secret or tacit consent to wrongdoing.  A willingness to allow something to happen, especially something wrong or immoral, by pretending not to know about it.|The police were accused of connivance in the escape. 警方被指控纵容犯人逃跑。|collusion, complicity, acquiescence|opposition, resistance|Noun

connive:/kəˈnaɪv/
v.|共谋，纵容，默许|To secretly allow something to happen, especially something wrong or illegal.|The guards connived at the prisoner's escape. 警卫纵容犯人逃跑。|conspire, collude, plot|oppose, prevent|Verb
