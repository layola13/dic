monetarist:/ˈmʌnɪtərɪst/ | n. adj. |货币主义者；货币主义的|A person who believes in monetarism (the theory that changes in the money supply directly affect the economy).  An adherent of monetarism; also: of or relating to monetarism.|Example: The monetarist argued that controlling the money supply was the key to fighting inflation.  这位货币主义者认为，控制货币供应是抗击通货膨胀的关键。|Synonym: monetarian|Antonym: Keynesian|noun, adjective


monetarism:/ˈmʌnɪtərɪzəm/ | n. |货币主义|The theory or practice of controlling the supply of money as the chief method of stabilizing the economy.|Example: Monetarism gained popularity in the 1970s as a response to high inflation.  货币主义在20世纪70年代作为应对高通货膨胀的措施而流行起来。|Synonym: monetary policy|Antonym: fiscal policy|noun
