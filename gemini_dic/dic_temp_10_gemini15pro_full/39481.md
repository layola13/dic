connoisseurship:/kəˌnɑːsəˈsɜːrʃɪp/ | n. | 鉴赏力；鉴赏家的身份 | The painting is a testament to the artist's skill and the connoisseurship of the collector.  这幅画证明了艺术家的技巧和收藏家的鉴赏力。|  Expertise, discernment, appreciation, judgment | Ignorance, inexperience, lack of taste | noun
Etymology->
From French *connoisseurship*, from *connoisseur* + *-ship*.  *Connoisseur* from French *connaisseur* "a knower, judge, expert," from *connaître* "to know," from Latin *cognoscere* "to get to know, learn."
