paroxysm:/ˈpærəkˌsɪzəm/ | n. | 阵发；突发；痉挛 | A sudden attack or outburst of a particular emotion or activity. |  She was seized by a paroxysm of coughing. 她一阵咳嗽发作。 | synonyms: spasm, fit, convulsion, seizure, outburst, eruption | antonyms: calm, peace, tranquility | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French paroxysme, from Late Latin paroxysmus, from Greek paroxusmos "irritation, exacerbation," from paroxunein "to irritate, provoke," from para- "beside" + oxunein "to sharpen."
