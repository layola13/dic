grice:/ɡraɪs/ | n. | 格赖斯;  | Herbert Paul Grice (1913-1988), a British philosopher of language known for his theory of implicature and the cooperative principle in conversation.  He argued that meaning in conversation goes beyond the literal meaning of words and involves understanding the speaker's intentions. |  Grice's work on implicature is highly influential in pragmatics. 格赖斯关于含意的研究在语用学中很有影响力。 | synonyms:  philosopher, linguist | antonyms: N/A | noun
