ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *knibblen, frequentative of *knibben (“to bite, gnaw, nibble”), from Old Norse *knibba (“to gnaw, pinch, nip”), from Proto-Germanic *knibbōną (“to bite, pinch”). Cognate with Danish knibe (“to pinch, squeeze”), Swedish knipa (“to pinch”), Middle Low German knippen (“to pinch, snip”). Related to nip.  First attested around 1475.
