clot:/klɑːt/ (US), /klɒt/ (UK)| n. v. |凝块，血块；阻塞；使凝结|A thick mass of coagulated liquid, especially blood, or of material stuck together.  To form or cause to form clots.|The blood around the wound began to clot. 伤口周围的血液开始凝结。|近义词：coagulate, thicken, congeal|反义词：dissolve, liquefy|词性:名词,动词
Etymology: From Old English *clott, akin to Middle Dutch klotte, and probably ultimately from a base meaning "lump, clod."
