anadiplosis:/ˌænədəˈploʊsɪs/| n.|首语重复法；顶真|Repetition of the last word of a preceding clause. The word is used at the end of one clause and then used again at the beginning of the next.|Fear leads to anger. Anger leads to hate. Hate leads to suffering.  恐惧导致愤怒。愤怒导致仇恨。仇恨导致痛苦。|
|synonym:reduplication, repetition, epanadiplosis|antonym:N/A|noun|
