fall: /fɔːl/ (British) /fɑːl/ (American) | n. vi. vt. adj.| 秋天; 落下; 瀑布; 跌倒 |  The act of falling, or dropping from a higher to a lower position. Also refers to autumn, a decrease in amount, or a surrender (like the fall of a city). |  The leaves fall from the trees in autumn. 秋天树叶从树上落下。| 近义词: autumn (n.), drop (v.), descend (v.), decline (n.), collapse (n.) | 反义词: rise (v.), ascend (v.), spring (n.), increase (n.) | 词性: 名词, 动词, 形容词

fall: /fɔːl/ (British) /fɑːl/ (American) | v. | 落下; 跌倒; 下降; 沦陷  | To descend freely under the influence of gravity. Can also mean to decrease, to succumb to temptation, or to be overthrown. |  The rain began to fall heavily. 雨开始下大了。| 近义词: drop, descend, decline, plummet, tumble | 反义词: rise, ascend, climb, soar | 词性: 动词


fall: /fɔːl/ (British) /fɑːl/ (American) | n. | 秋天 | The season after summer and before winter. | Fall is my favorite season. 秋天是我最喜欢的季节。| 近义词: autumn | 反义词: spring | 词性: 名词

fall: /fɔːl/ (British) /fɑːl/ (American) | adj. | 秋天的 | Relating to the season of autumn. | The fall colors are beautiful. 秋天的颜色很美。| 近义词: autumnal | 反义词: spring | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Old English *feallan, from Proto-Germanic *fallaną, from Proto-Indo-European *phol- (to fall).
