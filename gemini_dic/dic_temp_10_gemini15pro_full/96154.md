indentured:/ɪnˈdɛntʃərd/| adj.| 契约的，受契约束缚的|Bound by an indenture; obligated to work for another for a specified period of time, especially in return for passage to a new country or release from prison.|Example: The indentured servants worked long hours in the fields. 这些契约佣工在地里长时间工作。|Example:  Many early settlers in America were indentured laborers. 许多早期的美洲定居者都是契约劳工。|Synonym: bonded, contracted, apprenticed|Antonym: free, liberated|word property: adjective
