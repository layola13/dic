ammonias: /əˈmoʊniəz/
noun
氨（复数，不常用）
A colorless, pungent gas composed of nitrogen and hydrogen (NH3), which is highly soluble in water and used in cleaning products, fertilizers, and industrial processes.  The plural form "ammonias" is rarely used; "ammonia" is typically used even when referring to multiple instances or types.
Ammonia is often used in household cleaning products. 氨经常用于家用清洁产品。
Near Synonyms:  (Generally, just "ammonia" is used)  If referring to different types, one might say "ammonia solutions" or "ammonia compounds."
Antonyms: (No direct antonym.  One might consider substances that neutralize ammonia, like acids.)
