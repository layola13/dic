drooper:/ˈdruːpər/| n.|下垂物；萎靡不振的人；衰败的东西|Something that droops; a person who is lacking in energy or enthusiasm; something that is declining or failing.|The flowers were droopers after being left in the hot sun. 这些花在烈日下暴晒后都蔫了。|近义词：sag, wilt, flag, decline|反义词: thrive, flourish|词性:noun

droop:/druːp/| v.|下垂，低垂；萎靡，失去活力|To bend or hang downward; to lose energy or enthusiasm.|The branches drooped under the weight of the snow. 树枝在雪的重压下低垂下来。|近义词：sag, wilt, flag, decline|反义词: rise, perk up|词性:verb
