dandy:/ˈdændi/| n. adj.| 花花公子，纨绔子弟；最佳的，极好的 | A man unduly devoted to style, neatness, and fashion in dress and appearance.  A first-rate or excellent example of something.| He was quite the dandy in his youth, always impeccably dressed. 他年轻时是个十足的花花公子，总是穿着无可挑剔。|  That's a dandy new car you've got there! 你那辆新车真棒！| 近义词：fop, coxcomb, swell, excellent, superb | 反义词：slob, slob, terrible, awful | 词性: 名词，形容词

dandy:/ˈdændi/|Etymology:  Origin uncertain, perhaps from Andrew, a familiar name for a Scottish soldier, or from a dialectal word meaning "small and neat."|
