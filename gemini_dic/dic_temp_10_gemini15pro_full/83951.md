half-conquered:/ˌhɑːf ˈkɒŋkəd/
half-conquered| adj.|半征服的，未完全征服的|Partially conquered; not completely subdued or brought under control.  It implies that the conquest is incomplete and that resistance or challenges remain.|The half-conquered territory was still a source of conflict.|Near synonyms: partially subdued, incompletely conquered, partly vanquished|Near antonyms: completely conquered, fully subdued, totally vanquished|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From "half" + "conquered." "Half" is from Old English "healf," meaning "side," "part," or "half." "Conquered" is the past participle of the verb "conquer," from Old French "conquerre," ultimately from Latin "conquirere," meaning "to seek together," "to collect," or "to gain."
