forebow:/ˈfɔːrboʊ/
n.
前舱, 船头
The part of a ship forward of the foremast.
The cargo was stored in the forebow of the ship. 货物存放在船的前舱。

Etymology: From Middle English *foreboue, from fore- + bow. Equivalent to fore- + bow (“curved part of a ship's side”).
The term "forebow" combines "fore" (meaning "front" or "forward") and "bow" (referring to the front part of a ship).  It signifies the forward-most part of a vessel's bow.

Synonyms:  bow, prow, stem
Antonyms: stern, aft
