contingent:/kənˈtɪndʒənt/| adj. n.|偶然的，依情况而定的；小分队，代表团|A contingent event may or may not happen.  偶然事件可能会发生，也可能不会发生。|The size of the grant is contingent upon/on the number of applicants.  拨款的金额取决于申请者的人数。|例句：The trip is contingent on the weather.  这次旅行取决于天气。|例句：The school sent a small contingent to the competition. 学校派了一个小分队去参加比赛。|近义词：dependent, conditional; group, delegation|反义词:independent, unconditional; individual|词性:形容词, 名词

contingent:/kənˈtɪndʒənt/| n.|小分队，代表团|A contingent is a group of people representing a larger group. 代表团是由代表一个更大群体的成员组成。|例句：The French contingent at the conference was small. 出席会议的法国代表团规模很小。|例句：A large contingent of police officers were present at the protest.  抗议现场有大批警察。|近义词：group, delegation, detachment|反义词:individual|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contingent-, *contingens, present participle of contingere "to touch, befall," from Latin con- "with, together" + tangere "to touch."
