diatom: /ˈdaɪətɒm/ (US) /ˈdaɪətəm/ (UK)| n. |硅藻|A single-celled alga that has a cell wall of silica. Diatoms exist in huge numbers in plankton.|Diatoms are a major source of food for marine life. 硅藻是海洋生物的主要食物来源。|The intricate patterns on diatom shells are beautiful under a microscope. 在显微镜下，硅藻壳上的复杂图案很漂亮。|近义词: phytoplankton|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Modern Latin *diatoma*, from Ancient Greek διάτομος (diátomos, “cut in half, divided”), from διά (diá, “through, across”) + τέμνω (témnō, “I cut”).  This refers to the two halves, or valves, of the diatom frustule (silica shell).
