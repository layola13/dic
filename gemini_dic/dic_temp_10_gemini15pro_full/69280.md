featherweights:/ˈfeð.ə(rˌ)weɪts/| n.| 羽量级选手; 无足轻重的人或物 |People or things that are insignificant or unimportant; boxers weighing no more than 126 pounds.|The featherweights were easily defeated by the heavier boxers.| 近义词: lightweights, insignificants | 反义词: heavyweights, giants | 词性: 名词

Etymology->
From feather + weight.  The original meaning referred to boxers in the lowest weight class.  The figurative meaning developed from this, referring to someone or something lacking in influence or importance.
