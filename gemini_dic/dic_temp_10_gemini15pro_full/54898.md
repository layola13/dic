disport:/dɪˈspɔːrt/ /dɪˈspɔrt/
verb
嬉戏，玩耍
to enjoy oneself unrestrainedly; frolic; play
The children disported themselves in the pool. 孩子们在游泳池里嬉戏。
近义词: frolic, play, gambol, cavort, revel
反义词: toil, labor, work

disport:/dɪˈspɔːrt/ /dɪˈspɔrt/
noun
娱乐，消遣
pastime, amusement, entertainment
The king's favorite disport was hunting. 国王最喜欢的娱乐活动是狩猎。
近义词: pastime, amusement, entertainment, recreation
反义词: work, labor

词源:
From Middle English disporten, from Old French desporter, deporter (“to divert, amuse, recreate, relax”), from des- (intensive prefix) + porter (“to carry”).  The original sense was "to carry away (from one's cares)".
