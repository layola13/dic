capellane:/ˈkæpəleɪn/ | n. | 军中牧师；私人牧师 | A chaplain is a member of the clergy attached to a private chapel, institution, ship, armed-force unit, etc. | The queen's chaplain led the prayers. 女王的牧师带领祈祷。| synonym: clergyman, minister, pastor, priest | antonym: atheist, nonbeliever | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *capelain*, from Medieval Latin *capellanus*, from *capella* ("chapel"), diminutive of *cappa* ("cape, cope").  Ultimately from Late Latin *cappa* (“hooded cloak”).
