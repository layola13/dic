disquietly: /dɪsˈkwaɪətli/ | adv. |不安地，焦躁地 | In a manner that shows or causes unease or worry. | She paced disquietly around the room, waiting for the news. 她焦躁不安地在房间里踱来踱去，等待着消息。|  He coughed disquietly and shifted in his seat. 他不安地咳嗽了一声，并在座位上挪了挪身子。| 近义词: anxiously, nervously, restlessly, uneasily | 反义词: calmly, peacefully, serenely | 词性: 副词

disquiet: /dɪsˈkwaɪət/ | n. vt. |不安，焦虑；使不安 | A feeling of worry or unease; to make someone worried or uneasy. | The news caused widespread disquiet among the population.  这条消息引起了民众的普遍不安。| The rumors disquieted her. 谣言让她感到不安。 | 近义词: unease, anxiety, worry, concern | 反义词: calm, peace, tranquility | 词性: 名词，动词

词源:  From Old French *desquiet (unattested), from des- (expressing reversal) + quiet (“quiet”).  来源于古法语 *desquiet（未经证实），由 des-（表示反转）+ quiet（“安静”）组成。
