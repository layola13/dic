disavowedly:/ˌdɪsəˈvaʊɪdli/
| adv. |否认地，不承认地|In a manner that indicates disavowal; so as to disavow something.  Explicitly denying responsibility or support for something.|He disavowedly shrugged his shoulders, pretending he had nothing to do with the broken vase.| 近义词:  explicitly, openly, plainly, unequivocally | 反义词: tacitly, implicitly, covertly | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From disavow + -ed + -ly.  Disavow comes from the Old French desavouer, from des- (expressing negation) + avouer "to avow," from Latin advocare "to summon."
