floated:/ˈfloʊtɪd/ | v. |漂浮，浮动，提出，发行|Past tense of "float".  It describes something that was held up by water or air, drifted gently, or was put forward as an idea or plan. |The boat floated gently on the lake. 小船在湖面上轻轻漂浮。|drift, glide, hover, launch, propose |sink, submerge|verb

float:/floʊt/ | v. n. |漂浮，浮动，提出，发行，浮标，彩车|To rest or move on or near the surface of a liquid without sinking, or to cause something to do this; to move slowly in air or water; to suggest an idea or plan for other people to consider; a hollow object that floats on the surface of a liquid to mark a position or a safe passage for boats; a decorated platform, either on a vehicle or carried by people, that is used in processions. |A balloon floated across the sky. 一个气球飘过天空。|drift, glide, hover, launch, propose |sink, submerge|verb/noun
