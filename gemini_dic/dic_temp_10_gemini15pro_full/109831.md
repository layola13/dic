lexicographies:/ˌlɛksɪˈkɒɡrəfiz/| n.|词典编纂学；词典学|The study of the principles and practices of dictionary making.  It involves analyzing the lexicon of a language and organizing it into a dictionary format, considering factors such as word meaning, etymology, pronunciation, and usage. |His work in lexicographies has contributed significantly to our understanding of the evolution of the English language. 他的词典编纂工作极大地促进了我们对英语演变的理解。|近义词：dictionary making, lexicology|反义词:|n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From French lexicographie, from Greek lexikographikos (“of or pertaining to lexicography”), from lexikon (“dictionary”) + -graphia (“writing”).  Lexikon itself is from lexis ("word, speech").
