insubstantially:/ˌɪnsəbˈstænʃəli/
adjective
无实质地；脆弱地；虚弱地
Lacking firmness or substance; flimsy or weak.
The argument was insubstantially based on hearsay.  这个论点缺乏根据，建立在传闻之上。
The old bridge seemed insubstantially constructed. 这座老桥的结构看起来很不牢固。
Synonyms: flimsily, weakly, slightly, tenuously, vaguely
Antonyms: substantially, strongly, solidly, firmly, concretely
词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From in- (not) + substantial (having substance) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  Ultimately from Latin *substantialis* (having substance).
