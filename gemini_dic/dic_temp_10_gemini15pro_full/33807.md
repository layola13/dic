circumflex:/ˈsɜːrkəmfleks/ (US) /ˈsɜːkəmfːleks/ (UK)| n. adj.|抑扬符；抑扬的|A mark (^) placed over a vowel in some languages to indicate pronunciation, often a long vowel or a falling and then rising pitch.  It can also be used as an adjective to describe something that has or uses a circumflex.|The word "crêpe" uses a circumflex.  "crêpe"这个词使用了抑扬符。|近义词:caret, diacritic|反义词:None|Noun, Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *circumflexus, past participle of circumflectere (“to bend around”), from circum (“around”) + flectere (“to bend”).
