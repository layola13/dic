motorcaded:/ˈmoʊtərˌkeɪdɪd/| adj.|车队护送的，列队行进的|A procession of vehicles, typically carrying and escorting an important person.|The president was motorcaded through the city. 总统的车队在市内列队行进。|Synonyms: escorted, accompanied, convoyed|Antonyms: unescorted, unattended|adjective

motorcade:/ˈmoʊtərˌkeɪd/| n.|车队，汽车行列|A procession of motor vehicles, typically one accompanying a person of importance.|The royal motorcade made its way through the cheering crowds.  皇家车队在欢呼的人群中缓缓前进。|Synonyms: procession, convoy, caravan|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "motor" + "cavalcade."  "Cavalcade" originally referred to a procession on horseback, from the Italian "cavalcata."  The prefix "motor-" indicates the use of motorized vehicles.
