merchant-ship:/ˈmɜːrtʃənt ʃɪp/ | n. |商船|A ship used for commercial purposes, typically for carrying cargo or passengers. |例句：The merchant-ship was loaded with containers bound for Shanghai. 这艘商船装满了运往上海的集装箱。

例句：Several merchant-ships were anchored in the harbor. 几艘商船停泊在港口。

近义词：cargo ship, freighter, trading vessel |反义词:warship|词性:名词

merchant:/ˈmɜːrtʃənt/ | n. | 商人 | A person or company involved in wholesale trade, especially one dealing with foreign countries or supplying merchandise to a particular trade. |词源：From Old French *marchant* (Modern French marchand), from Late Latin *mercatantem*, present participle of *mercatare* "to trade," from *mercatus* "market" ultimately from Latin *merx* "merchandise". |

ship:/ʃɪp/ | n. vt. | 船，舰；运送 | n. A large boat for transporting people or goods by sea. vt. To transport goods or people by ship. |词源：Old English *scip* "ship, vessel," from Proto-Germanic *skipą* (source also of Old Norse *skip*, Old Frisian *skip*, Dutch *schip*, Old High German *skif*, German *Schiff*), from PIE root *(s)keu-* "to cover, conceal."  The verb senses are from the noun. |
