crow:/krəʊ/ (UK) /kroʊ/ (US)| n. vi.|乌鸦；啼叫|A large, black bird with a harsh cry. |例句：A crow perched on the top of the tree. 一只乌鸦栖息在树顶。|例句：The crows were cawing loudly in the morning. 早晨，乌鸦大声地啼叫。|近义词：raven, rook|反义词: N/A|词性:名词,动词

crow:/krəʊ/ (UK) /kroʊ/ (US)| vi.|啼叫；自鸣得意|To make the loud, harsh cry of a crow; to boast or brag loudly.|例句：The rooster crowed at dawn. 公鸡在黎明时啼叫。|例句：He crowed about his victory in the competition. 他吹嘘着自己在比赛中的胜利。|近义词：boast, brag|反义词:be modest|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *crāwe, from Proto-Germanic *krāwō, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱrewh₂-. Cognate with Old High German krāwa ("crow"), Old Norse kráka ("crow"), Latin crāx ("raven, crow").
