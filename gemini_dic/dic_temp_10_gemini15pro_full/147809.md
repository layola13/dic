overprovidentnesses:/ˌoʊvərˈprɒvɪdəntnəsɪz/
|n.|过度节俭；过分谨慎|The state of being excessively provident or careful, often to the point of being stingy or miserly.|His overprovidentnesses bordered on miserliness, refusing to spend money even on necessities.|近义词: frugality, thrift, parsimony|反义词: extravagance, prodigality|词性: Noun


Etymology:
From "over-" (excessive) + "provident" (making provision for the future) + "-ness" (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).  "Provident" comes from Latin "providens," present participle of "providēre" (to foresee, provide), from "pro-" (before) + "vidēre" (to see).
