dorian:/ˈdɔːriən/ (US), /ˈdɒriən/ (UK)| adj. n.|多利安式的, 多里安调式; 多里安人|Relating to or denoting a style of classical Greek architecture, one of the three orders, characterized by a plain, unadorned column capital and a fluted shaft. Also, a member of one of the four main branches of the Hellenic people, inhabiting the district of Doris in ancient Greece. In music, relating to or denoting a minor scale in which the third and seventh degrees are minor. |例句1：The temple was built in the Dorian style. 这座寺庙是以多利安式建造的。

例句2：The Dorians were known for their austere and disciplined culture. 多里安人以其简朴和自律的文化而闻名。|近义词：Spartan, austere (for the cultural aspect) |反义词：Ionian, Corinthian (for architectural styles)|词性: adjective, noun
