off-record:/ˌɒf ˈrɛkɔːd/ (British), /ˌɔːf ˈrɛkərd/ (American)| adj. adv.|非正式的，私下的，不公开的|Not intended for official publication or quotation; unofficial; confidential.| "The mayor spoke off-record about the city's budget problems." 市长私下谈了该市的预算问题。|近义词：unofficial, confidential, private, background|反义词：on-record, official, public|词性:形容词, 副词

off-record:/ˌɒf ˈrɛkɔːd/ (British), /ˌɔːf ˈrɛkərd/ (American)| n.|私下谈话|A conversation or statement that is not intended for official publication or quotation.| "The journalist's off-record conversation with the senator provided valuable insights." 记者与参议员的私下谈话提供了宝贵的见解。|近义词：private conversation, confidential discussion|反义词：official statement, public announcement|词性:名词

词源:  "Off-record" originated in the early 20th century in journalism, combining "off" (indicating removal or separation) and "record" (referring to an official or public account). It initially referred to information not intended for inclusion in an official record.
