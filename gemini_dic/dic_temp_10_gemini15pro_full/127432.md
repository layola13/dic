neatress: /ˈniːtnɪs/ | n. | 整洁，整齐 | The state or quality of being neat; tidiness. | Her neatness was evident in her perfectly organized desk. 她的整洁从她井然有序的桌子上就能看出来。 |  Example: The neatness of her handwriting impressed her teacher.  她的字迹工整给老师留下了深刻的印象。| synonyms: tidiness, orderliness, cleanliness, smartness | antonyms: messiness, disorder, untidiness, sloppiness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *nete, from Old English *nēat, from Proto-Germanic *naitaz (“shining, bright”), from Proto-Indo-European *kʷóy- (“white, shining”).  Related to neat (adjective). The suffix -ness is a common way to form nouns from adjectives in English, indicating a state or quality.
