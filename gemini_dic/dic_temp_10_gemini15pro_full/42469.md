counterfeiting:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪtɪŋ/
n.|伪造；仿造|The act of making something fake, especially money or goods, with the intent to deceive.|The police are investigating the counterfeiting of banknotes. 警方正在调查伪钞案。|synonyms: forgery, faking, imitation, copying|antonyms: authenticity, genuineness|noun

counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/
n.|伪造品；仿制品|Something that is fake, especially money or goods, made to deceive.|The handbag was a counterfeit.  这个手提包是假货。|synonyms: forgery, fake, imitation, copy|antonyms: original, genuine|noun
counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/
vt.|伪造；仿造|To make something fake, especially money or goods, with the intent to deceive.|He was arrested for counterfeiting passports. 他因伪造护照被捕。|synonyms: forge, fake, imitate, copy|antonyms: authenticate, verify|verb
counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/
adj.|伪造的；仿造的|Made to look like something real in order to deceive.|The painting was discovered to be a counterfeit. 这幅画被发现是赝品。|synonyms: forged, fake, imitated, copied|antonyms: authentic, genuine, real|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French contrefait, past participle of contrefaire "to imitate, copy, feign," from contre- "against" + faire "to make, do."
