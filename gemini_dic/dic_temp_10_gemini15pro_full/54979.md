disproportioned:/ˌdɪsprəˈpɔːrʃənd/ | /ˌdɪsprəˈpɔːrʃənd/ | adj. | 不成比例的，不相称的 |  Not having the correct or appropriate proportions; out of proportion, unbalanced. |  The house had a disproportioned roof; it seemed far too large for the rest of the building. 这房子屋顶不成比例；它看起来比其余的建筑大得多。 | The dog's head seemed disproportioned to its small body. 这只狗的头与其小身体相比似乎不成比例。 |  uneven, unbalanced, asymmetric, lopsided, mismatched, incongruous | proportionate, balanced, symmetrical, harmonious | adjective


disproportion:/ˌdɪsprəˈpɔːrʃən/ | /ˌdɪsprəˈpɔːrʃən/ | n. | 不成比例，不相称 |  A lack of proportion or balance. | There is a huge disproportion between the number of men and women in the tech industry. 科技行业男女比例严重失衡。| The disproportion in their salaries was shocking. 他们的薪资差距令人震惊。 | imbalance, disparity, inequality, asymmetry |  balance, equality, symmetry, proportion | noun

词源:  From dis- "not" + proportion + -ed. Ultimately from Latin pro portione "according to a share."
