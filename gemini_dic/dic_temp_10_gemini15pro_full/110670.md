linder:/ˈlɪndər/ | n. |椴树; 椴木|A deciduous tree with heart-shaped leaves and fragrant yellowish-white flowers, native to north temperate regions. Its wood is soft and easily worked.|The carpenter used linder to make the dollhouse.|Synonyms: lime tree, basswood|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lind, from Proto-Germanic *lindō, from Proto-Indo-European *lent-. Cognate with Dutch linde, German Linde.
