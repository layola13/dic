entrenching:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
巩固；加强；使处于牢固地位
Establishing something firmly so that it is difficult to change.
The government's policies are entrenching poverty. 政府的政策正在加剧贫困。
The organization was deeply entrenched in the community.  这个组织在社区中根深蒂固。
近义词: reinforcing, strengthening, solidifying, establishing, securing
反义词: weakening, undermining, destabilizing
词性: verb (present participle)

entrenched:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃt/
adjective
根深蒂固的；牢固的
Firmly established and difficult to change.
The company's entrenched position in the market made it difficult for competitors.  该公司在市场上根深蒂固的地位使其竞争对手难以与其竞争。
These attitudes are deeply entrenched in our society. 这些态度在我们的社会中根深蒂固。
近义词: established, rooted, fixed, ingrained, deep-seated
反义词:  weak, unstable, precarious
词性: adjective

entrenchment:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt/
noun
巩固；加强；壕沟
The state of being established firmly or the act of establishing firmly. Also, a ditch or trench.
The entrenchment of corruption made reform difficult. 腐败的根深蒂固使得改革困难重重。
The soldiers dug entrenchments for protection. 士兵们挖了壕沟以求保护。
近义词: fortification, consolidation, reinforcement, establishment
反义词: weakening, undermining
词性: noun

词源: From French "retrencher," meaning "to cut again," from "re-" (again) + "trenchier" (to cut).  The meaning shifted from literal digging of trenches to a figurative sense of establishing firmly.
