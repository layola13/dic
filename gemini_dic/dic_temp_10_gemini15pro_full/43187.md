crabbedly:/ˈkræbɪdli/
adverb
暴躁地，乖戾地
in a bad-tempered, irritable, or peevish way.
He crabbedly refused to answer any questions. 他暴躁地拒绝回答任何问题。
近义词：grumpily, peevishly, irritably, sullenly
反义词：good-naturedly, pleasantly, amiably
词性: adverb

crabbedly:/ˈkræbɪdli/
Etymology: From crabbed + -ly.  Crabbed itself comes from crab (the crustacean) and originally referred to something crooked or bent like a crab's legs.  Over time, the meaning shifted to include the ill-tempered nature sometimes associated with crabs.
中文翻译：暴躁地，乖戾地
英文解析：In a bad-tempered, irritable, or peevish manner.  Often implies a sour or unpleasant disposition.
例句：The old man crabbedly complained about the noise.  老人暴躁地抱怨噪音。
例句：She crabbedly snapped at the waiter for the slow service. 她不耐烦地斥责服务员上菜太慢。
近义词：grumpily, sullenly, morosely, peevishly
反义词：cheerfully, pleasantly, amiably, genially
词性: adverb
