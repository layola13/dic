malignantly:/məˈlɪɡnəntli/
| adv. | 恶性地； malignantly; | showing great malevolence or hostility;  harmfully or destructively. | The tumor was diagnosed as malignantly cancerous.  肿瘤被诊断为恶性癌。| Synonym: malevolently, viciously, spitefully, venomously | Antonym: benignly, kindly, benevolently | adverb

malignant:/məˈlɪɡnənt/
| adj. | 恶性的；有害的；怀有恶意的 | (of a disease) very virulent or infectious. |  The doctor discovered a malignant tumor. 医生发现了一个恶性肿瘤。| Synonym: cancerous, virulent, harmful, malevolent | Antonym: benign, harmless, benevolent | adjective

malign:/məˈlaɪn/
| vt. | 诽谤；中伤；污蔑 | speak about (someone) in a spitefully critical manner. |  He was maligned by his political opponents. 他被他的政敌恶意中诋毁。| Synonym: slander, defame, libel, vilify | Antonym: praise, commend, extol | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin malignus (“ill-disposed, wicked”), from male (“badly”) + -gnus (akin to genus (“birth, race, kind”)).  Doublet of malengine.
