coexecutrix:/ˌkoʊɪɡˈzɛkjətrɪks/
noun
女共同执行人
A woman appointed jointly with one or more other persons to administer the estate of a deceased person.
My aunt was named coexecutrix of her sister's will. 我阿姨被任命为她姐姐遗嘱的共同执行人。
She served as coexecutrix with her brother. 她和她兄弟一起担任共同执行人。
Synonym: joint executrix
Antonym: (No direct antonym, but perhaps "beneficiary" represents an opposing role)
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From co- + executrix.  "Executrix" comes from Late Middle English, from Anglo-French executrice, feminine of executeur "executor," and ultimately from Latin executor "performer, executor," agent noun from past participle stem of exsequi "to follow out, carry out," from ex- "out" + sequi "to follow."
