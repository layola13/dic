flosculous:/ˈflɒskjʊləs/ | adj. | 花哨的；辞藻华丽的；浮夸的 | Characterized by flowery, overly elaborate, or pretentious language; full of rhetorical flourishes. |  His writing style is often criticized for being too flosculous. 他的写作风格常因过于华丽而受到批评。

The politician delivered a flosculous speech that lacked substance. 这位政客发表了一篇辞藻华丽但缺乏实质内容的演讲。 | 近义词: flowery, ornate, bombastic, pretentious | 反义词: plain, simple, unadorned, straightforward | 词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *flosculus (small flower), diminutive of flos (flower).
