incommensurability:/ɪnkəmɛnʃərəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 不可通约性，不可比较性 |The state of being incommensurable; having no common measure or standard of comparison.  Lack of a common standard of measurement. | Example: The incommensurability of their worldviews made communication difficult.  他们的世界观不可通约，使得沟通变得困难。

Example:  There is an incommensurability between qualitative and quantitative research methods. 定性和定量研究方法之间存在着不可通约性。| 近义词: incomparability, disparity, difference, divergence | 反义词: commensurability, comparability, similarity | 词性: noun

incommensurable: /ɪnkəmɛnʃərəbl/ | adj. | 不可通约的，不能比较的 | Not commensurable; having no common measure or standard of comparison. Not able to be judged by the same standard as something else. | Example: The two systems of belief are incommensurable. 这两种信仰体系是不可通约的。

Example: The value of art is often seen as incommensurable with its monetary price. 艺术的价值通常被认为与其货币价格不可通约。| 近义词: incomparable, disparate, different, divergent | 反义词: commensurable, comparable, similar | 词性: adjective
