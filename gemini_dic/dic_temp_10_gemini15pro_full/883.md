absurdities: /əbˈsɜːrdɪtiz/ /æbˈsɜːrdɪtiz/
| n. | 荒谬；谬论；荒唐事 |  Something that is unreasonable, illogical, or ridiculous. Plural form of absurdity. | The play was full of absurdities that made the audience laugh.  这出戏充满了荒谬之处，让观众大笑。 |
| | | The politician's claims were filled with absurdities and contradictions.  这位政客的说法充满了荒谬和矛盾。 |
| | | synonyms: follies, silliness, nonsense, ridiculousness | antonyms: sense, logic, rationality | noun |
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: absurdite, via Old French from Latin absurditas, from absurdus ‘out of tune, discordant, irrational’, from ab- ‘from’ + surdus ‘deaf’.
