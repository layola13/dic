bushwhacker:/ˈbʊʃˌwækər/| n.|游击队员，丛林伏击兵|A person who uses guerrilla warfare from a concealed position, especially in wooded country.  A bushwhacker is someone who ambushes and harasses their enemy from hiding, typically in a wooded area.  The term originated during the American Civil War.|例句：The bushwhackers ambushed the Union soldiers from the dense forest. 游击队员从茂密的森林中伏击了联邦士兵。

例句：Fear of bushwhackers kept travelers off the roads. 对游击队员的恐惧使旅行者远离道路。|近义词：guerrilla, partisan, irregular|反义词:regular soldier, conventional soldier|词性:noun
