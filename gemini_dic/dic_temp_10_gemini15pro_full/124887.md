muddle:/ˈmʌd(ə)l/| n. v. |混乱，糊涂；弄乱，弄糟|A state of confusion or disorder. To bring into a state of confusion or disorder.|例句：The papers on his desk were in a complete muddle. 他桌子上的文件一片混乱。|近义词：mess, confusion, jumble, disorganize, confuse|反义词：order, organize, clarify|词性:名词,动词

muddle:/ˈmʌd(ə)l/| n. v. |困境，窘境；使陷入困境|A difficult or embarrassing situation. To cause to be in a difficult or embarrassing situation.|例句：He got himself into a terrible muddle with his finances. 他把自己弄得经济上一团糟。|近义词：predicament, dilemma, trouble, complicate|反义词：solution, ease|词性:名词,动词
