decubitus: /dɪˈkjuːbɪtəs/ | n. | 褥疮; 卧位; |A posture or position of lying down; specifically :  the act or posture of lying down, esp. on the back, or the position assumed in lying down.  Also refers to an ulceration of the skin resulting from lying in one position for a long time. |The patient developed a decubitus on his sacrum from prolonged bed rest. 病人因长时间卧床休息，骶骨处出现了褥疮。 |Synonyms: bedsore, pressure sore, pressure ulcer |Antonyms: standing, upright |noun
