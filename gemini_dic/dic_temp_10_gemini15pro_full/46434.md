daggy:/ˈdæɡi/| adj.|邋遢的，过时的，土气的|Unfashionable, outdated, or shabby in style or appearance.  Often used in Australian and New Zealand English.|Her daggy old sweater had holes in the elbows. 她那件土气的旧毛衣手肘处有洞。|近义词：unfashionable, outdated, frumpy, dowdy, shabby|反义词：stylish, fashionable, trendy, chic|词性:形容词
Etymology:  Likely a shortened form of "daglock," referring to clumps of matted wool or dirt hanging from a sheep's rear end.  The meaning then transferred to describe something unkempt or unstylish.
