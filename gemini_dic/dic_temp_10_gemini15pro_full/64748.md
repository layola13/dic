escout:/ɪˈskaʊt/ | n. |侦察员，侦察兵；搜索，侦察| A person sent out to obtain information, especially a soldier sent to explore enemy territory.  A soldier or other person sent out ahead of the main force so as to gather information about the enemy's position, strength, or movements.| The general sent out scouts to assess the enemy's position. 将军派出了侦察兵去评估敌人的位置。| synonyms:  reconnoiter, scout out, spy, explore | antonyms: conceal, hide | noun


escout:/ɪˈskaʊt/ | v. |侦察，搜索；寻找，物色| To explore an area or territory, especially in order to gather information about an enemy or resources.  To search for someone or something, especially for a specific purpose such as talent or a business opportunity.| They scouted the area for a suitable campsite. 他们在该地区侦察了一个合适的露营地。| synonyms: explore, reconnoiter, search, seek, prospect | antonyms: ignore, neglect | verb
