cryptogamous:/krɪpˈtɒɡəməs/ | adj. | 隐花植物的；隐孢子的 | Relating to plants that reproduce by spores, without flowers or seeds.  Plants that do not have visible flowers or seeds, such as ferns, mosses, algae, and fungi. | Example: Ferns and mosses are cryptogamous plants.  蕨类植物和苔藓是隐花植物。| Example: The study of cryptogamous plants is an important part of botany. 对隐花植物的研究是植物学的重要组成部分。 | Synonym: spore-bearing, flowerless | Antonym:  phanerogamous, flowering | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *kryptos* (hidden) + *gamos* (marriage), referring to the hidden reproductive organs.
