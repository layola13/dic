distraught:/dɪˈstrɔːt/
|adj.|
|心烦意乱的，极度不安的|
|Extremely worried, upset, and confused.|
|She was distraught after hearing the news of her husband's accident.|她听到丈夫出事的消息后心烦意乱。|
|worried, upset, agitated, frantic, distressed|
|calm, composed, serene, tranquil|
|adjective|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *distraught*, *distracte*, past participle of *distracten*, from Latin *dīstractus*, past participle of *dīstrahō* (“I pull asunder, divide, separate, distract”), from *dis-* (“apart, asunder”) + *trahō* (“I draw, pull, drag”).
