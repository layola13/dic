muttering:/ˈmʌtərɪŋ/ | n. | 嘟囔，咕哝 | a low, indistinct, and often continuous sound of speaking |  He was muttering to himself as he walked down the street. 他沿着街道走时，一直在自言自语地嘟囔。 | mumbling, murmuring | shouting, yelling | noun

muttering:/ˈmʌtərɪŋ/ | adj. | 嘟囔的，咕哝的 | speaking in a low, indistinct voice |  A muttering voice came from the back of the room.  房间后面传来一阵低沉的咕哝声。 | mumbling, murmuring | loud, clear | adjective

mutter:/ˈmʌtər/ | v. | 嘟囔，咕哝 | to speak in a low, indistinct voice |  "I don't know," she muttered. “我不知道，”她咕哝道。 | mumble, murmur | shout, yell | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mutteren, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Dutch *motteren "to mumble," German *mutrern "to grumble," or Norwegian *mutra "to sulk."
