gael:/ɡeɪl/ /ɡeɪəl/ | n. | 盖尔人；盖尔语 |A member of a Celtic people formerly inhabiting Ireland, Scotland, and the Isle of Man, or their descendants. Also: the Goidelic languages (especially Irish or Scottish Gaelic) spoken by them.|例句：The gael gathered for the annual festival. 盖尔人聚集起来参加年度节日。

|例句：She spoke fluent gael. 她能说一口流利的盖尔语。|近义词：Celt, Highlander, Irishman, Scotsman |反义词: N/A |词性:noun


Etymology->
From Old Irish *Goídel, from Proto-Celtic *Woidelos. Ultimately from the same Proto-Indo-European root as the name of the Germanic tribe, the *Walhaz, from which the words "Welsh" and "Walloon" derive.
