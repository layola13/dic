absent-mindedness: /ˌæbsəntˈmaɪndɪdnəs/
n.|心不在焉，健忘|The state of being forgetful or preoccupied.|His absent-mindedness led him to leave his keys in the car. 他的心不在焉导致他把钥匙忘在了车里。

Her absent-mindedness often resulted in missed appointments. 她的健忘经常导致她错过约会。
|近义词：forgetfulness, preoccupation, distraction, inattention|反义词：attentiveness, mindfulness, alertness|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From absent + minded + -ness.  "Absent" traces back to Latin "absens," meaning "being away." "Minded" comes from Old English "gemyndig," meaning "mindful."  "-ness" is a suffix that forms nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition.

USAGE->
Because absent-mindedness is a noun, it doesn't conjugate in the same way verbs do.  However, it can be used in different contexts and sentence structures:

As the subject of a sentence:
Absent-mindedness can be a real problem in some professions. = 在某些职业中，心不在焉可能是一个真正的问题。
Absent-mindedness caused him to miss the crucial deadline. = 心不在焉导致他错过了关键的截止日期。

As the object of a sentence:
He blamed his mistake on absent-mindedness. = 他把自己的错误归咎于心不在焉。
She struggled with absent-mindedness throughout the day. = 她一整天都在与心不在焉作斗争。

As the object of a preposition:
Because of his absent-mindedness, he often forgot important details. = 由于心不在焉，他经常忘记重要的细节。
His errors were attributed to absent-mindedness. = 他的错误被归因于心不在焉。

In possessive form:
My grandfather's absent-mindedness is getting worse. = 我祖父的健忘越来越严重了。
The professor's absent-mindedness amused the students. = 教授的心不在焉逗乐了学生们。
