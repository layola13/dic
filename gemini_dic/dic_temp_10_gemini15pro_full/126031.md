mussulman: /ˈmʌsəlmən/ | n. | 穆斯林，伊斯兰教徒 | An archaic and often offensive term for a Muslim.  This term is considered outdated and potentially offensive due to its historical usage in colonial contexts. It is recommended to use "Muslim" instead. |  Example: In older texts, you might encounter the word "mussulman" used to refer to a follower of Islam. 在一些古旧的文本中，你可能会遇到用“mussulman”来指代伊斯兰教的信徒。 | Example:  The term "mussulman" carries colonial undertones and should be avoided. “mussulman”一词带有殖民色彩，应避免使用。 | 近义词: Muslim, Moslem | 反义词: None (It's inappropriate to list a religious opposite) | 词性: noun


Etymology:
From Middle French *musulman, from Old Italian musulmano, musolino, from Medieval Latin musulmanus, from Arabic muslim 'submitting (to God), a Muslim'.
