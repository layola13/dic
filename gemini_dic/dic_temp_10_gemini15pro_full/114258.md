Etymology:
From maim + -ed + -ness + -es.  "Maim" comes from Old French "mahaignier" meaning "to injure, wound, mutilate."  The suffix "-ed" forms the past participle and adjective.  "-ness" creates a noun denoting a state or quality. "-es" marks the plural form.
