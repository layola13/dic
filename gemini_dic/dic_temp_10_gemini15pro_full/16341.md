barbarize:/ˈbɑːrbəˌraɪz/| v. |使野蛮化，使变得粗俗|to make or become barbarous or uncivilized; to corrupt (language, taste, etc.)|The constant warfare has barbarized the region.  不断的战争使该地区变得野蛮。|Synonyms: brutalize, savage, corrupt|Antonyms: civilize, refine, cultivate|verb

barbarize:/ˈbɑːrbəˌraɪz/| n. |野蛮化，粗俗化|The act or process of making or becoming barbarous or uncivilized. |The barbarization of language is a concern for many linguists.  语言的粗俗化是许多语言学家关注的问题。|Synonyms: brutalization, savagery|Antonyms: civilization, refinement|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *barbariser*, from Late Latin *barbarizare*, from Latin *barbarus* (barbarian) + *-izare* (suffix forming verbs). Ultimately from Greek *bárbaros* (foreign, non-Greek).
