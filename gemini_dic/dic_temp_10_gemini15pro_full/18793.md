berretta:/bəˈrɛtə/| n.|贝雷帽，方形帽|A square cap, usually of cloth or leather, with three or four stiff projecting corners, sometimes topped with a tuft or pompom.  Traditionally worn by Roman Catholic clergy, academics, and some other groups.|The cardinal wore a scarlet berretta.|Synonyms: cap, biretta, bonnet|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a specific type of hat)|noun
