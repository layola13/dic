clotter:/ˈklɑːtər/ (US) /ˈklɒtər/ (UK)| v. n.|凝结，凝块，使…凝结|To cause (a liquid, such as blood) to change to a semisolid mass; to coagulate.  As a noun, it refers to something that clots, or an agent that causes clotting.|The blood clotted quickly after the injury. 受伤后血液很快凝结了。|近义词：coagulate, thicken, congeal|反义词:thin, liquefy|词性:verb, noun
clotter:/ˈklɑːtər/ (US) /ˈklɒtər/ (UK)| n.|凝结物，凝块剂|A substance or agent that causes clotting.|The doctor prescribed a clotter to help stop the bleeding. 医生开了凝血剂来帮助止血。|近义词：coagulant, clotting agent|反义词:anticoagulant|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clotteren*, frequentative of *clotten* "to clot," from Old English *clott* "clod, lump, mass."  Related to Dutch *klot* "lump, block."
