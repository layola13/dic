futurism:/ˈfjuːtʃərɪzəm/| n. |未来主义|An early-20th-century artistic and social movement that emphasized and glorified themes associated with contemporary concepts of the future, including speed, technology, youth, violence, and objects such as the car, the airplane, and the industrial city.|Italian Futurism celebrated the dynamism of modern technology.|近义词: modernism, avant-gardism|反义词: traditionalism, conservatism|词性: noun

Etymology: From French *futurisme*, from Italian *futurismo*, from *futuro* (“future”), from Latin *futūrus*.
