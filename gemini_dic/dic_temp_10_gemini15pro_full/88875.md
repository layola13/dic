hoity-toity:/ˌhɔɪtiˈtɔɪti/
adjective
傲慢的，轻浮的
supercilious, haughty, snobbish, arrogant behavior or manner.
She gave me a hoity-toity look. 她傲慢地看了我一眼。
近义词: haughty, arrogant, supercilious, snobbish, condescending
反义词: humble, modest, unassuming
词性: adjective

hoity:/ˈhɔɪti/
adjective, adverb, noun (now rare)
傲慢的，轻浮的；喧闹地
Origin:  Probably from obsolete *hoit 'to romp or behave boisterously,' of unknown origin.
No longer used alone, it primarily serves as the first element of the compound adjective hoity-toity.  When used alone, it carries connotations of boisterous or frivolous behavior.
He walked into the room with a hoity air. (adjective - now rare) 他带着傲慢的神气走进房间。
"Don't be so hoity!" she scolded. (adverb - now rare) “别这么轻浮！”她责骂道。
The hoity of the crowd was disturbing. (noun - archaic) 人群的喧闹令人不安。
近义词: (For hoity-toity) haughty, arrogant, supercilious, snobbish, condescending
反义词: (For hoity-toity) humble, modest, unassuming
词性: Primarily used as part of the compound adjective "hoity-toity".  When used alone, it can function as an adjective, adverb, or noun (though the latter two are now archaic).
