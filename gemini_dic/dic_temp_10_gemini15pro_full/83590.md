haemoglobin:/ˌhiːməˈɡloʊbɪn/ (US), /ˌhiːməˈɡləʊbɪn/ (UK)| n.|血红蛋白|A red protein responsible for transporting oxygen in the blood of vertebrates.  It contains iron, and gives blood its characteristic red color.|Example 1:  A low haemoglobin count can indicate anaemia. 血红蛋白计数低可能表明患有贫血。

Example 2:  The doctor checked her haemoglobin levels. 医生检查了她的血红蛋白水平。|Synonym:  Hb (abbreviation)|Antonym:  None (no direct antonym)|noun
