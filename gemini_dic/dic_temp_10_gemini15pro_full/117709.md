melancholian: /ˌmelənˈkəʊliən/ (US) /ˌmelənˈkɒliən/ (UK)
adjective
忧郁的；悲伤的；令人悲伤的
Characterized by or causing a feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause.
例句：
The melancholian melody brought tears to her eyes.
忧郁的旋律让她热泪盈眶。

There was a melancholian atmosphere in the abandoned house.
废弃的房子里弥漫着忧伤的气氛。

近义词：sad, gloomy, mournful, wistful, pensive
反义词：happy, cheerful, joyous, upbeat

Etymology:
From Late Latin *melancholicus, from Ancient Greek μελαγχολικός (melankholikós, “suffering from black bile”), from μέλας (mélas, “black”) + χολή (kholḗ, “bile”). The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholy.

词性: adjective
