alexandrine:/ˌælɪɡˈzændraɪn/ | n. adj. | 十二音步诗; 十二音步的 | A line of verse having six iambic feet.  A verse form of twelve syllables. |  Example:  "The poem was written in alexandrines, giving it a stately and formal feel." 这首诗是用十二音步诗写的，给人一种庄严正式的感觉。

Example: "Spencer's 'The Faerie Queene' is written in alexandrine stanzas." 斯宾塞的《仙后》是用十二音步诗节写的。 | 近义词: iambic hexameter | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *alexandrin*, from *Alexandre*, Alexander, because it was used in a 12th-century French romance about Alexander the Great.
