marching:/ˈmɑːrtʃɪŋ/ (US) /ˈmɑːtʃɪŋ/ (UK)| n. adj. v.| 行进，行军；游行示威；(音乐)进行曲|The act of walking with regular steps, especially in a group, often with music; a piece of music with a strong rhythm suitable for marching.|例句：The soldiers were marching in formation. 士兵们正在列队行进。|近义词：walking, parading, proceeding, progressing|反义词：retreating, halting, standing|词性:名词/形容词/动词

marching:/ˈmɑːrtʃɪŋ/ (US) /ˈmɑːtʃɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.| 行进的，前进的|Relating to or used for marching.|例句：The marching band played a lively tune.  游行乐队演奏了一首活泼的曲子。|近义词：advancing, progressing|反义词：retreating, static|词性:形容词

marching:/ˈmɑːrtʃɪŋ/ (US) /ˈmɑːtʃɪŋ/ (UK)| v.| 行进，前进；游行示威|(present participle of march) To walk with regular steps, especially in a group, often with music.|例句：They were marching to protest against the war. 他们游行抗议战争。|近义词：walking, parading, proceeding|反义词：retreating, halting|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "marcher" (to walk, tread, trample), from Frankish "*markōn" (to mark, border on), from Proto-Germanic "*markōną" (to mark, border on), from Proto-Indo-European "*merk-" (boundary).  The musical sense developed from the military use.
