homoeomeria:/ˌhɒmiəˈmɪəriə/ | n. | 均一性; 同类粒子说 |The doctrine that the body is composed of particles similar to the organs they constitute.  This theory, proposed by Anaxagoras,  posited that everything is made up of small, indivisible particles called "seeds" or "homoeomeries." |  Ancient Greek philosophers believed in homoeomeria, a theory about the composition of matter. 古希腊哲学家相信同类粒子说，这是一种关于物质构成的理论。| synonym: homogeneity, uniformity | antonym: heterogeneity, diversity | noun

homoeomery:/ˌhɒmiˈɒməri/ | n. | 均一性; 同类粒子说 |A variant spelling of homoeomeria, referring to the ancient Greek theory of matter. |  His studies focused on the concept of homoeomery in pre-Socratic philosophy. 他的研究集中于前苏格拉底哲学中同类粒子说的概念。| synonym: homogeneity, uniformity, homoeomeria | antonym: heterogeneity, diversity | noun
