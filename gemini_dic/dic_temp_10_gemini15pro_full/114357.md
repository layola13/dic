majorat:/ˌmeɪdʒəˈrɑːt/ | n. | 长子继承权；限定继承 |The right of inheritance by the eldest son, especially of landed property.  A system of inheritance in which land or property passes by law to the eldest son.| The majorat ensured that the family estate remained intact. 长子继承权确保了家族财产的完整。|  primogeniture, entail | ultimogeniture (幼子继承权) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *majorat*, from Late Latin *majoratus* "eldership," from Latin *major* "greater, elder."
