diprotodon:/daɪˈproʊtədɒn/ /daɪˈproʊtədɑːn/| n. |双门齿兽|An extinct genus of giant marsupial herbivores, the largest known marsupial to have ever existed, native to Australia during the Pleistocene epoch.|The diprotodon was a massive creature, about the size of a rhinoceros.|近义词:giant wombat (loosely),  |反义词: N/A |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *di-*, "two" + *protos*, "first" + *odous* (genitive *odontos*), "tooth".  Referring to the two large incisors in the lower jaw.
