lubberly: /ˈlʌbərli/ | adj. |笨拙的，笨重的|Clumsy, awkward, and unskilled, especially in seamanship.  Referring to someone lacking grace and coordination, often in a nautical context. | The lubberly sailor tripped over a rope and fell into the sea.  那个笨手笨脚的水手被绳子绊倒掉进了海里。|  The captain sighed at the lubberly attempts of the new recruits to climb the rigging. 船长看着新兵笨拙地尝试爬索具，叹了口气。| 近义词: clumsy, awkward, ungainly, inept | 反义词: graceful, agile, dexterous, skillful | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From "lubber" (a clumsy person, especially a seaman) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix forming adjectives). "Lubber" is likely of Scandinavian origin, possibly related to Norwegian "lubb" (a clumsy fellow).
