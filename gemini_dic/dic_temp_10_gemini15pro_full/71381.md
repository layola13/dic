flagitate:/ˈflædʒɪteɪt/ | v. | 恳求；强烈要求 | To demand urgently or persistently; to implore. | The citizens flagitated the mayor for better public transportation. 市民强烈要求市长改善公共交通。 |  They flagitated for the release of the political prisoners. 他们强烈要求释放政治犯。 | 近义词：implore, beseech, entreat, importune, supplicate | 反义词：demand, order, command | 词性: 动词

flagitate:/ˈflædʒɪteɪt/ | n. | 恳求；强烈要求 (较少用作名词) | An urgent or persistent demand; an imploration. |  His constant flagitate for attention became tiresome. 他不断地要求关注变得令人厌烦。 |  The flagitate of the refugees for asylum fell on deaf ears.  难民寻求庇护的恳求置若罔闻。 | 近义词：plea, appeal, entreaty, supplication | 反义词：demand, order, command | 词性: 名词

词源: From Latin "flagitatus," past participle of "flagitare" (to demand urgently), from "flagrare" (to burn).  The sense evolution suggests the image of burning with desire or urgency for something.
