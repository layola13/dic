afeard:/əˈfɪərd/| adj.|害怕的，恐惧的|An archaic or dialectal form of afraid, meaning frightened or scared.|例句：She was afeard of the dark. 她害怕黑暗。|近义词：afraid, frightened, scared, terrified|反义词:brave, courageous|词性:形容词

Etymology: From Middle English *aferd, from Old English *afæred, past participle of *afæran “to frighten,” from a- (intensive prefix) + *færan “to frighten.”  Cognate with Dutch vervaard (“afraid”), German verfehrt (“perverse, distorted”). The modern form "afraid" likely developed through influence from the verb "affray."
