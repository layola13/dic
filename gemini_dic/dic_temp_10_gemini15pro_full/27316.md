calypsoes:/kəˈlɪpsoʊz/
noun
卡利普索歌曲（复数）
A type of West Indian (originally Trinidadian) music in syncopated African rhythm, typically with improvised lyrics on topical or satirical subjects.
Calypsoes were often used to tell stories or share news. 卡利普索歌曲经常被用来讲述故事或分享新闻。
The calypsoes of the islands told tales of love, loss, and everyday life.  岛上的卡利普索歌曲讲述了爱情、失落和日常生活的故事。
Synonyms: songs, tunes, melodies, chants
Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but consider) silence, quiet
词性: 名词


calypso:/kəˈlɪpsoʊ/
noun
卡利普索歌曲（单数）
A type of West Indian (originally Trinidadian) music in syncopated African rhythm, typically with improvised lyrics on topical or satirical subjects.
The calypso told a story of a fisherman's struggle with a giant squid. 这首卡利普索歌曲讲述了一位渔夫与巨型乌贼搏斗的故事。
She sang a lively calypso about the local carnival. 她唱了一首关于当地狂欢节的欢快卡利普索歌曲。
Synonyms: song, tune, melody, chant
Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but consider) silence, quiet
词性: 名词
