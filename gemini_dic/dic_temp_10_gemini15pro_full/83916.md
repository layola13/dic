half-blindnesses:/ˌhɑːf ˈblaɪndnɪsɪz/ | n. | 半盲; 視力部分喪失 | Partial loss of sight; a state of being half-blind.  This term refers to conditions where vision is significantly impaired but not completely lost. It can manifest in various ways, affecting one or both eyes, or specific areas of the visual field. |  Due to his half-blindness, he struggled to read the small print. 由于半盲，他难以阅读小字体。

His half-blindnesses made driving dangerous. 他的视力部分丧失使得驾驶很危险。 | 近义词: partial blindness, impaired vision | 反义词: perfect vision, 20/20 vision | 词性: Noun
