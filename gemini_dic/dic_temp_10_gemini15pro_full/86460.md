hegelianism:/ˌheɪɡəˈliːənɪzəm/ | n. | 黑格尔主义；黑格尔哲学 |  The philosophy of G.W.F. Hegel, marked by the belief that the world (and all reality) is in a continual process of change toward the Absolute, manifested in the thesis-antithesis-synthesis triad. |  The professor's lectures focused on Hegelianism and its impact on modern thought. 教授的讲座集中讨论了黑格尔主义及其对现代思想的影响。| synonym:  Hegel's philosophy, Absolute Idealism | antonym: materialism, empiricism | noun

hegelian:/ˌheɪˈɡiːliən/ | adj. | 黑格尔的；黑格尔哲学的 | Of or relating to Hegel or his philosophy. | His Hegelian interpretation of history emphasized the dialectical process. 他对历史的黑格尔式解释强调了辩证过程。| synonym: Hegelianistic | antonym: non-Hegelian | adjective
