outrager:/ˈaʊtreɪdʒər/
n.|施暴者，暴徒，令人愤慨的人或事|a person or thing that commits or causes outrage; someone who enrages or shocks people. |The outrageous price increases made him an outrager in the eyes of his customers.  价格的惊人上涨使他在顾客眼中成了一个令人愤慨的人。|近义词: offender, villain, wrongdoer|反义词:  pacifist, peacemaker|词性: 名词


outrage:/ˈaʊtreɪdʒ/
n.|暴行，愤怒，侮辱|an extremely strong reaction of anger, shock, or indignation.|The terrorist attack was an outrage against humanity. 恐怖袭击是对人类的暴行。|近义词: offense, affront, insult|反义词:  respect, honor|词性: 名词


outrage:/aʊtˈreɪdʒ/
vt.|激怒，使愤慨，强奸|to arouse intense anger, shock, or indignation in (someone).|The judge's decision outraged many people. 法官的判决激怒了很多人。|近义词:  enrage, infuriate, incense|反义词:  please, delight|词性: 动词
