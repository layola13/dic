advowee:/ˌædvaʊˈiː/
noun
受推荐人；被提名者
The advowee is the person who has been nominated or recommended for a position, especially a church living.
The Duke of Devonshire, as advowee, appointed the vicar. 德文郡公爵作为提名人，任命了牧师。
He was named as the advowee for the vacant bishopric. 他被提名为空缺的主教职位的人选。
Synonyms: nominee, candidate, appointee
Antonyms: nominator, patron
n.

From Anglo-French *avoué, past participle of avouer (“to avow”), from Latin advocare (“to call to one's aid, summon”), from ad- + vocare (“to call”).
