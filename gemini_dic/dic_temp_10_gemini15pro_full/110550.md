limerick: /ˈlɪmərɪk/ | n. | 打油诗; 利默里克市 | A humorous five-line poem with a specific rhyming pattern (AABBA) and syllable structure. It often tells a short, funny, and sometimes nonsensical story, often with a punchline in the final line.  |  Example: There once was a fellow named Kyle,/Who swallowed a fish with a smile./He said with a burp,/“It gave quite a slurp!”/Then went off to swim for a while.  凯尔吞了一条鱼，还带着微笑。他说，打了个嗝，“它发出很大的咕噜声！”然后就去游泳了。 | Synonyms:  nonsense verse, doggerel, jingle | Antonyms:  epic, sonnet, serious poem | noun

limerick: /ˈlɪmərɪk/ | adj. | 利默里克市的 | Relating to the city of Limerick, Ireland. | Example: Limerick ham is a local specialty. 利默里克火腿是当地特产。| Synonyms:  Limerikian | Antonyms: N/A | adjective
