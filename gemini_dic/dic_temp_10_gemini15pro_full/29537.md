catcalls:/ˈkætˌkɔːlz/ (US) /ˈkætˌkɔːlz/ (UK)| n.|嘘声，口哨声；调戏的喊叫声|Loud, sexually suggestive calls or whistles made at someone, especially a woman, in a public place. |Example: She ignored the catcalls from the construction workers. 她无视了建筑工人的口哨声。

Example:  The actress was subjected to a barrage of catcalls as she walked down the street.  这位女演员走在街上时遭到了一连串的口哨声和调戏。|近义词：whistles, jeers, taunts, leers|反义词: compliments, praise|词性:名词


catcall:/ˈkætˌkɔːl/ (US) /ˈkætˌkɔːl/ (UK)| v. |发出嘘声，吹口哨；调戏地喊叫|To make a loud, sexually suggestive call or whistle at someone, especially a woman, in a public place. |Example:  The men catcalled at the women passing by.  那些男人对着路过的女人吹口哨。

Example: He was catcalled by a group of teenagers as he walked through the park.  他穿过公园时，一群青少年对他发出了嘘声和调戏的喊叫。|近义词：jeer, taunt, leer|反义词: compliment, praise|词性:动词
