noncausative:/ˌnɒnˈkɔːzətɪv/ | adj. | 非使役的，非致使的 | Not causative; not causing or producing an effect; intransitive.  A noncausative verb describes a situation or state, not an action that directly causes something else to happen.  | The window broke. (Noncausative - implies the window broke on its own or due to an unspecified cause) | 近义词: intransitive, inchoative | 反义词: causative, transitive | 词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + causative.  "Causative" ultimately comes from the Latin "causa" meaning "cause".

PHRASE->

(Noncausative verbs don't typically form strong collocations or phrases in the same way transitive verbs do.  They describe states of being or changes that happen without a direct agent.  However, here are some examples of how they might appear in phrases:)

The situation deteriorated rapidly. = 情况迅速恶化。
The market collapsed suddenly. = 市场突然崩溃。
The problem arose unexpectedly. = 问题意外出现。
His health declined gradually. = 他的健康状况逐渐下降。
The tension increased noticeably. =  紧张局势明显加剧。
