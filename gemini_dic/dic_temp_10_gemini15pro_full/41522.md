coronal:/ˈkɒrənl/ (UK), /kəˈroʊnl/ (US)| adj. n.|冠状的，冠状物|Relating to a corona; crown-like.  A consonant sound, such as /t/ or /d/, made by placing the tip of the tongue against the alveolar ridge (the part of the roof of the mouth just behind the upper teeth).|The coronal suture is a dense, fibrous connective tissue joint that separates the two parietal bones of the skull from the frontal bone. 冠状缝是将颅骨的两个顶骨与额骨分开的致密纤维结缔组织关节。

The sounds /t/, /d/, /n/, and /l/ are coronal consonants.  /t/、/d/、/n/ 和 /l/ 是冠状辅音。|近义词：crown-like, apical|反义词: N/A|词性: adjective, noun

Etymology: From Latin *corōnālis*, from corōna (“crown, garland”).
