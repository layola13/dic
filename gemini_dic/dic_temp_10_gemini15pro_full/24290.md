brigantine:/ˈbrɪɡənˌtiːn/ |n.|双桅横帆船；轻型帆船|A two-masted sailing ship with a square-rigged foremast and a fore-and-aft rigged mainmast.  A swift, two-masted vessel, square-rigged on the foremast and schooner-rigged on the mainmast.|The pirates sailed their brigantine across the Caribbean. 海盗们驾驶着他们的双桅横帆船穿越加勒比海。|The brigantine was faster than the galleon. 这艘轻型帆船比大帆船快。|Synonyms: schooner, barque, sloop|Antonyms: galleon (somewhat), steamship|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *brigantino*, diminutive of *brigantina*, from *brigante* ("brigand, pirate"), ultimately of Germanic origin.
