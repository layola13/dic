owlishly:/ˈaʊlɪʃli/
adverb
像猫头鹰般地；学究气地；假装聪明地
In a way that resembles an owl, often suggesting wisdom, solemnity, or sometimes a pretense of wisdom.
He looked at me owlishly over his spectacles. 他戴着眼镜像猫头鹰般看着我。
近义词:wisely, sagely, solemnly, studiously
反义词:foolishly, unintelligently, carelessly
词性:adverb

Etymology:
From owl + -ish + -ly.  "Owl" comes from Old English "ūle,"  related to other Germanic words for owl. The suffix "-ish" forms adjectives meaning "like" or "having the qualities of," and "-ly" forms adverbs from adjectives.
