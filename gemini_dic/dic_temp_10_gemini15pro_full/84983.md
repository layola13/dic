happy:/ˈhæpi/ | adj. | 快乐的，幸福的 | Feeling or showing pleasure or contentment. |  She had a happy childhood. 她有一个快乐的童年。 | Synonyms: joyful, cheerful, merry, glad, delighted | Antonyms: sad, unhappy, miserable, sorrowful | adjective

Etymology: From Middle English *happi, from Old Norse *happ (meaning "luck, chance, fortune"), from Proto-Germanic *happuz.  The original meaning was more related to good fortune than to feeling good.  The modern sense developed later.
