albertype: /ˈælbərˌtaɪp/ | n. | 阿尔伯版画，蛋白印相 | A photomechanical process for making high-quality prints from a gelatin relief, invented by Joseph Albert. | Example: The albertype was a popular method for reproducing photographs in the 19th century.  阿尔伯版画是19世纪复制照片的流行方法。| Example:  Many art books of the era used albertypes to showcase detailed illustrations. 那个时代的许多艺术书籍都使用阿尔伯版画来展示精美的插图。 | Synonym: collotype, photogravure | Antonym: (No direct antonym, but could be contrasted with less precise printing methods like woodcut) | noun
