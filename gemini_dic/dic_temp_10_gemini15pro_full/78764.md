gilding: /ˈɡɪldɪŋ/ | n. | 镀金，金箔，虚饰 | The act of applying gold leaf or gold paint to a surface; something that is gilded; a superficial layer that makes something appear better than it actually is. | The gilding on the frame was flaking off.  画框上的镀金正在剥落。| Synonyms: gold leaf, gold plating, ornamentation, embellishment | Antonyms: plainness, simplicity | noun

gilding: /ˈɡɪldɪŋ/ | v. | 镀金，虚饰 |  To cover something with a thin layer of gold or gold paint; to give a deceptively attractive appearance to something. |  They gilded the statue to make it look more impressive. 他们把雕像镀了金，使它看起来更令人印象深刻。 | Synonyms: adorn, decorate, embellish, enhance | Antonyms: strip, expose, tarnish | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gilden*, from Old French *gilder*, from Frankish *gildan*, from Proto-Germanic *gulþijaną* (“to gild”), from *gulþą* (“gold”). Cognate with Old English *gyldan* (“to gild”), Dutch *gilden* (“to gild”), German *vergolden* (“to gild”), Gothic *𐌲𐌹𐌻𐌸𐌰𐌽* (gilþan, “to make of gold, to gild”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰelh₃-tó-m* (“gold”).
