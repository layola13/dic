distemperate:/dɪsˈtɛmpərɪt/| adj.|放纵的，无节制的， intemperate;  (of weather) unseasonably harsh or stormy|Lacking moderation or self-control; unrestrained by reason or prudence. Also, (of weather) unseasonably harsh or stormy.|His distemperate outbursts of anger made him difficult to work with. 他无节制的愤怒爆发让他很难共事。|Synonyms: immoderate, intemperate, unrestrained, excessive, inordinate, uncontrolled, unbridled, wild, unruly, rampant, riotous, vehement, violent, passionate, impetuous, rash, reckless, headstrong, unruly, ungovernable|Antonyms: temperate, moderate, restrained, controlled, calm, sober, reasonable, mild|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin distemperatus "immoderate," past participle of distemperare "to disorder," from dis- "away, apart" + temperare "to mix, blend, moderate."
