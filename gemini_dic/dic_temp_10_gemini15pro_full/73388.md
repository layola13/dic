forehearth:/ˈfɔːrˌhɑːrθ/| n. |熔炉前炉|A small reverberatory furnace or chamber forming an extension to a larger furnace, used especially for finishing (e.g., adjusting the temperature or composition) molten glass. |The glass is refined in the forehearth before being sent to the forming machines.|Synonyms: working end, front hearth|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, potentially the main melting chamber of the furnace)|Noun
