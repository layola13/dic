acclimation: /ˌækləˈmeɪʃən/ | n. | 适应，驯化 | The process of adapting or adjusting to a new climate, environment, or situation.  | The acclimation of the plant to its new environment took several weeks.  这种植物适应新环境花了几个星期。 | Synonyms: adaptation, adjustment, acclimatization | Antonyms: maladaptation | noun

一般现在时:
Acclimation is important for survival in new environments. = 在新环境中，适应能力对生存至关重要。
The acclimation period for new employees is three months. = 新员工的适应期是三个月。

一般过去时:
The acclimation of the refugees took longer than expected. = 难民的适应时间比预期的要长。
Their acclimation to the city life was surprisingly fast. = 他们对城市生活的适应速度出奇地快。

现在完成时:
The team has completed its acclimation to the high altitude. = 团队已经完成了对高海拔的适应。
She has undergone acclimation training for the desert environment. = 她已经接受了沙漠环境的适应训练。

过去完成时:
The climbers had finished their acclimation before attempting the summit. = 登山者在尝试登顶之前已经完成了适应。
By the time the scientists arrived, the animals had completed their acclimation to the laboratory. = 当科学家到达时，动物们已经完成了对实验室的适应。

将来时:
The astronauts will undergo acclimation training before their mission to Mars. = 宇航员将在前往火星的任务之前接受适应训练。
The new species will require a period of acclimation before it can be released into the wild. = 新物种需要一段时间的适应才能被放归野外。

虚拟语气:
