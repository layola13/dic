marechal:/ˈmɑːrʃəl/ | n. | 元帅；最高指挥官 | A high-ranking military officer, especially the chief commander of an army. |例句：He was promoted to the rank of marechal. 他被晋升为元帅。

例句：The marechal led the troops into battle. 元帅率领部队投入战斗。

近义词：general, field marshal | 反义词: private, soldier | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *marescal, from Frankish *marhskalk "horse servant," from *marha- "horse" + *skalk- "servant."  The original meaning referred to a person in charge of the horses of a king or nobleman. The word evolved over time to denote a high military rank.
