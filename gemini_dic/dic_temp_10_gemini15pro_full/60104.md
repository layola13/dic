eerinesses:/ˈɪərinɪsɪz/
|n.|怪异，阴森，令人毛骨悚然|The eerinesses of the abandoned house made me shiver. 这栋废弃房屋的阴森森的气氛让我不寒而栗。|The eerinesses of the forest at night can be quite unsettling. 夜晚森林的诡异气氛可能令人十分不安。|近义词：creepiness, spookiness, uneasiness, strangeness|反义词：comfort, normalcy, familiarity|词性:名词

词源:
From eerie + -ness + -es.  "Eerie" comes from Middle English eri, "timid, frightened," from Old English earg, "cowardly."  "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, denoting a state or quality. "-es" is a plural suffix.
