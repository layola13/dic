imperceptibly:/ˌɪmpərˈsɛptɪbli/| adv.|不知不觉地，难以察觉地|In a way that is so slight, gradual, or subtle as not to be perceived.|The days grew longer almost imperceptibly. 白昼几乎不知不觉地变长了。|The tumor had grown imperceptibly over the years. 多年来，肿瘤在不知不觉中生长。|近义词：gradually, subtly, slightly, unnoticeably|反义词：noticeably, obviously, perceptibly|词性:副词
Etymology: From Late Latin *imperceptibilis, from in- "not" + percept-, past participle stem of percipere "to perceive" + -ibilis "-ible".
