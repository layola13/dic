bombardman:/bɒmˈbɑːdmən/ (UK), /bɑːmˈbɑːrdmən/ (US)| n.|轰炸机飞行员，炮手；轰炸机|A person who operates a bomber aircraft or artillery.|The bombardier carefully aimed the weapon. 轰炸机瞄准手仔细瞄准武器。|Synonyms: bomber pilot, gunner|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, possibly civilian, pacifist)|Noun

bombard:/bɒmˈbɑːd/ (UK), /bɑːmˈbɑːrd/ (US)| vt.|轰炸，炮击；大量提问；涌入|To attack with bombs or artillery; to assail persistently with questions or requests; to inundate.|The enemy bombarded the city. 敌人轰炸了城市。|Synonyms: shell, attack, barrage, pepper|Antonyms: defend, protect|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From bomb + -ard (suffix forming nouns and adjectives, often of depreciative or pejorative force). Ultimately from the Latin "bombus" (a booming or humming sound).
