glad-hander: /ˈɡlædˌhændər/ | n. |  热衷于握手的人；虚情假意的人 | A person who shakes hands frequently and enthusiastically, especially in an insincere or overly friendly way, typically to gain favor or popularity. | He's such a glad-hander, always trying to impress everyone. 他真是个爱握手的人，总是试图给每个人留下好印象。|  The politician worked the room, a glad-hander extraordinaire.  这位政治家在房间里四处活动，是个握手寒暄的高手。 |Synonyms: backslapper, schmoozer | Antonyms: introvert, recluse | Noun


glad-hand: /ˈɡlædˌhænd/ | v. | 热情握手；虚情假意地打招呼 | To shake hands with someone enthusiastically, often insincerely, to gain favor. |  He glad-handed his way through the reception. 他在招待会上不停地与人握手寒暄。 | The salesman glad-handed all the potential customers.  这个销售员热情地和所有潜在客户握手。 | Synonyms: schmooze | Antonyms: ignore, snub | Verb


Etymology:  The term "glad hand" originated in American English in the late 19th century. "Glad" refers to the apparent happiness or enthusiasm conveyed, while "hand" refers to the handshake itself.  The term quickly acquired negative connotations, suggesting insincerity and superficiality.
