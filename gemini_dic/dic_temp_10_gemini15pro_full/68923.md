fatalism: /ˈfeɪtəlɪzəm/ | n. | 宿命论，听天由命 | The belief that all events are predetermined and therefore inevitable. | He accepted his fate with a sense of fatalism. 他以宿命论的态度接受了自己的命运。 |  synonyms: determinism, predestination, resignation | antonyms: free will, self-determination | noun

Etymology->
From French *fatalisme*, from *fatal* (“fatal”) + *-isme*.  *Fatal* is from Latin *fātālis*, from *fātum* (“fate”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European **bʰeh₂- (“to speak, tell”).
