megalomania: /ˌmɛɡələˈmeɪniə/ | n. | 夸大妄想症；自大狂 | A mental disorder marked by delusions of grandeur, power, wealth, or omnipotence. | He suffered from megalomania and believed he was a god. 他患有夸大妄想症，认为自己是个神。 |  She showed signs of megalomania when she announced her plans to single-handedly solve world hunger. 当她宣布要独自解决世界饥饿问题时，她表现出了夸大妄想的迹象。 | synonyms: narcissism, egomania, grandiosity | antonyms: humility, modesty | noun

Etymology:  From Greek *megalomania*, from *megalo-* (large, great) + *mania* (madness, frenzy).
