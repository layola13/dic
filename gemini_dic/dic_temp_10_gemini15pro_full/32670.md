chivalrous:/ˈʃɪvəlrəs/| adj.| 骑士精神的；侠义的；殷勤的|Marked by or exhibiting chivalry; gallant or courteous.  Relating to or characteristic of knights or knighthood. |He opened the door for her with a chivalrous gesture. 他以骑士风度为她开了门。|近义词：gallant, courteous, honorable, noble, brave|反义词:rude, cowardly, ungentlemanly|词性:adjective
chivalry:/ˈʃɪvəlri/| n.| 骑士精神；骑士制度|The medieval knightly system with its religious, moral, and social code. The combination of qualities expected of an ideal knight, especially courage, honor, courtesy, justice, and a readiness to help the weak.|The knight displayed chivalry in battle.  这位骑士在战斗中展现了骑士精神。|近义词：gallantry, courtesy, valor|反义词:cowardice, rudeness, dishonor|词性:noun

词源：
From Old French chevalerie (“knighthood”), from chevalier (“knight”), from Late Latin caballarius, from Latin caballus (“horse”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱabalos (“pale; dappled”).
来自古法语 chevalerie (“骑士身份”)，来自 chevalier (“骑士”)，来自晚期拉丁语 caballarius，来自拉丁语 caballus (“马”)。 最终来自原始印欧语 *ḱabalos (“苍白的；有斑点的”)。
