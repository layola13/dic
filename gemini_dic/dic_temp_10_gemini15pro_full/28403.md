carbonate:/ˈkɑːrbənət/ (US), /ˈkɑːbənɪt/ (UK)| n.  adj.| 碳酸盐; 碳酸化的 |A salt of carbonic acid.  Containing carbonate.|例句：Calcium carbonate is a common mineral. 碳酸钙是一种常见的矿物。|近义词：bicarbonate (specifically for hydrogen carbonates)|反义词: N/A| noun
carbonate:/ˈkɑːrbənət/ (US), /ˈkɑːbənɪt/ (UK)| vt.| 使碳酸化 |To charge with carbon dioxide. To convert into a carbonate.|例句：They carbonate the water to make it fizzy.  他们将水碳酸化以使其冒泡。|近义词：aerate|反义词: N/A| verb
carbonate:/ˈkɑːrbənət/ (US), /ˈkɑːbənɪt/ (UK)| adj.| 碳酸化的 |Charged with carbon dioxide.  Converted into a carbonate.|例句：Carbonated drinks are popular in summer. 碳酸饮料在夏天很受欢迎。|近义词：fizzy, sparkling|反义词: still, flat| adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *carbonate*, from carbone (“carbon”) + -ate (“suffix forming nouns and adjectives from verbs, and especially verbs of Latin origin”). From Latin carbo (“charcoal, coal”).
