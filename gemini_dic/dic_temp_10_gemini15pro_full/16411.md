barded:/ˈbɑːrdɪd/| adj.|披甲的，戴盔甲的；（马）披着马衣的|Covered with armor or protective covering; (especially of a horse) wearing barding (protective armor).|The knight rode a barded warhorse into battle. 骑士骑着一匹披甲的战马进入战斗。|Synonyms: armored, caparisoned, harnessed|Antonyms: unarmored, unprotected|adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *barded*, from Old French barde "horse armour," from Frankish *barda*, of Germanic origin; related to Old High German *bart* "beard," from Proto-Germanic *bardō ("axe, blade").  The original sense may have referred to a protective metal plate worn like a beard.
