coffering:/ˈkɔf(ə)rɪŋ/ | n. | 格子天花板，镶板，方格装饰 | A series of recessed panels or coffers in a ceiling, vault, or dome. | The ceiling of the cathedral was adorned with intricate coffering.  大教堂的天花板装饰着复杂的格子图案。| synonyms: paneling, wainscoting, casing | antonyms: plainness, smoothness | noun

coffering:/ˈkɔf(ə)rɪŋ/ | n. | 围堰，沉箱 | A watertight enclosure pumped dry to permit construction work below water level. |  They used coffering to build the foundations of the bridge. 他们使用沉箱建造桥梁的地基。| synonyms: caisson | antonyms:  (No direct antonym) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *coffrer, from coffre "chest, box," from Latin cophinus "basket," from Greek kophinos "basket."  The architectural sense developed from the resemblance of the recessed panels to the lids of chests or boxes.
