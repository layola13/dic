brutalist: /ˈbruːtəlɪst/ | adj. | 野兽派的，粗野主义的 |  Relating to or denoting a style of architecture characterized by massive, imposing structures of exposed concrete and austere geometric designs.  It originated from the French word "brut" meaning raw (concrete). |  The Barbican Centre in London is a classic example of brutalist architecture.  伦敦的巴比肯中心是野兽派建筑的典型例子。 | The new library is a brutalist concrete structure.  这座新图书馆是一座野兽派的混凝土建筑。| synonyms: harsh, stark, austere, severe | antonyms: ornate, decorated, embellished | adjective
