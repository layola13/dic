calvary:/ˈkælvəri/| n.|髑髅地，磨难，苦难|A place of suffering; ordeal.  It derives its meaning from the biblical place of Jesus' crucifixion. |He faced his own personal calvary when his business failed.|synonyms: ordeal, trial, tribulation, affliction, agony, suffering, anguish, torment, torture|antonyms: joy, bliss, comfort, ease, peace, relief|noun

calvary:/ˈkælvəri/| n.|骑兵|A group of soldiers who fight on horseback. (Archaic usage) |The calvary charged across the field. (Archaic)|synonyms: cavalry, horsemen|antonyms: infantry|noun
