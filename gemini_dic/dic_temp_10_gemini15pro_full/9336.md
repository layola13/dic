antipragmatism:/ˌæntaɪˈpræɡmətɪzəm/| n. |反实用主义|A philosophical doctrine that opposes or rejects pragmatism.  It often emphasizes abstract principles, absolute truths, or the importance of tradition over practical consequences or experimental verification.|He accused his opponent of antipragmatism, claiming the proposed policy ignored real-world implications.|近义词：idealism, absolutism|反义词:pragmatism, utilitarianism|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + pragmatism.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposed to," derived from the Greek "anti." "Pragmatism" comes from the Greek "pragma," meaning "deed" or "action."
