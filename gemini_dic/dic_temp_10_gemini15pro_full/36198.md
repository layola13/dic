cogent:/ˈkoʊdʒənt/| adj.|令人信服的；有说服力的|Appealing forcibly to the mind or reason; convincing.|Her argument was cogent and persuasive, leaving little room for doubt. 她的论证令人信服且有说服力，几乎没有留下任何疑问的空间。|The lawyer presented a cogent case to the jury. 律师向陪审团提出了一个令人信服的案例。|Synonyms: convincing, persuasive, compelling, forceful, strong, valid, sound, logical, reasonable, credible|Antonyms: unconvincing, weak, ineffective, illogical, unreasonable, flawed|adjective

Etymology->
From Latin *cogentem*, present participle of *cogere* "to drive or bring together, compel, force," from *co-* "together" (see co-) + *agere* "to set in motion, drive, lead, act, do"
