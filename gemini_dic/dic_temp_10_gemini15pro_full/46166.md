cynoidea:/saɪˈnɔɪdiə/ | n. | 犬型亚目 |  A suborder of carnivorous mammals that includes dogs, wolves, foxes, jackals, and related animals.  It is characterized by a relatively long snout, digitigrade locomotion, and non-retractile claws. |  Example 1: Cynoidea are a diverse group of mammals found throughout the world. 犬型亚目是遍布世界各地的多种哺乳动物。

Example 2: The evolution of Cynoidea has been extensively studied. 犬型亚目的进化已被广泛研究。 | 近义词: Caniformia (sometimes used interchangeably), dog-like carnivores | 反义词: Feliformia (cat-like carnivores) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *kynós (genitive kynós) "dog" + -oidea "resembling".
