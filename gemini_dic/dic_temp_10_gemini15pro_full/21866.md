bodock: /ˈbɒdək/ (British) /ˈbɑːdək/ (American)| n. |粗布; 粗呢|A coarse woolen cloth.|  The beggar's cloak was made of rough bodock. 乞丐的斗篷是用粗糙的牛蒡布做的。 (This meaning is archaic and rarely used.) |Synonyms: homespun, burlap| Antonyms: silk, satin| Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bātdocce, from bāt (“boat”) + docce (“dock,” a kind of plant). The reason for the "boat" element is uncertain.
