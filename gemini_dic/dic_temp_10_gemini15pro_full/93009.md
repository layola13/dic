idiosyncrasy:/ˌɪdiəˈsɪŋkrəsi/ | n. | 特质，癖好，古怪之处 | A mode of behavior or reaction peculiar to an individual or group. |  He has a few harmless idiosyncrasies, like always wearing purple socks. 他有一些无伤大雅的怪癖，比如总是穿紫色的袜子。

| Example: Her biggest idiosyncrasy is her insistence on wearing mismatched shoes. 她最大的怪癖就是坚持穿不配套的鞋子。 | Synonym: peculiarity, quirk, eccentricity | Antonym: normality, conformity | noun


idiosyncratic: /ˌɪdiəˌsɪŋˈkrætɪk/ | adj. | 特殊的，独特的，怪异的 | Relating to idiosyncrasy; peculiar or individual. |  The building's idiosyncratic design made it stand out from the rest. 这栋建筑独特的设计使其从其他建筑中脱颖而出。

| Example: His idiosyncratic writing style made his novels instantly recognizable. 他独特的写作风格使他的小说立即被认出来。 | Synonym: distinctive, peculiar, characteristic | Antonym: typical, common, ordinary | adjective
