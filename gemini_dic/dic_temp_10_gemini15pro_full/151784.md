pass-off:/ˈpɑːs ɒf/ (US) /ˈpɑːs ɔːf/ (UK)| phrasal verb | 假冒; 蒙混;  | to pretend that something is something else, in order to deceive people | He tried to pass the fake watch off as a genuine Rolex. 他试图把这块假表冒充成真的劳力士。|近义词：palm off, fob off, disguise, masquerade |反义词: expose, reveal | verb phrase

pass-off:/ˈpɑːs ɒf/ (US) /ˈpɑːs ɔːf/ (UK)| noun |  (事件的)结束, 完成 | the successful completion of something | The pass-off of the project was celebrated with a party. 项目的顺利完成以一个派对来庆祝。|近义词：completion, conclusion, finish |反义词: beginning, start | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrasal verb "pass off," combining "pass" (meaning to move or transfer) and "off" (indicating removal or separation). The noun sense likely developed from the verb, referring to the point at which something is successfully passed off or completed.
