fast-forward:/ˌfæst ˈfɔːrwərd/ /ˌfæst ˈfɔːrwərd/| v. n. adj.| 快进，快速前进 |To move quickly to a later point in time, especially when recounting a story or using a recording device. Also, the function on such a device that allows you to do this.| I fast-forwarded through the boring parts of the movie. 我快进了电影中无聊的部分。 |近义词：skip, advance, accelerate|反义词: rewind, reverse|词性: verb, noun, adjective

fast-forward:/ˌfæst ˈfɔːrwərd/ /ˌfæst ˈfɔːrwərd/| n.| 快进，快速前进 |The act of moving quickly to a later point in time or the button/function that allows you to do this.|  The fast-forward button on the remote is broken. 遥控器上的快进键坏了。|近义词：skip, advance|反义词: rewind|词性: noun

fast-forward:/ˌfæst ˈfɔːrwərd/ /ˌfæst ˈfɔːrwərd/| adj.| 快进的，快速前进的|Relating to or characterized by rapid advancement or progress.|  The fast-forward changes in technology are astonishing.  科技的快速发展令人震惊。|近义词：rapid, accelerated|反义词: slow, gradual|词性: adjective

USAGE->

词源:  Compound formed from "fast" (meaning quick) and "forward" (meaning in a direction ahead).  The term originated in the context of tape recorders and video players.
