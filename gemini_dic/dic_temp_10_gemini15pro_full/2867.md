adverseness:/ədˈvɜːrsnəs/| n.|逆境，不幸，不利|The adverseness of the economic climate made starting a business difficult. 恶劣的经济环境使创业变得困难。|例句：They showed courage in the face of adverseness.  他们在逆境中表现出了勇气。

They overcame the adverseness of their situation through hard work and determination. 他们通过努力工作和决心克服了困境。|近义词：misfortune, hardship, adversity, difficulty, trouble|反义词：prosperity, fortune, advantage, benefit|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From adverse + -ness.  Adverse comes from Latin adversus "turned towards, facing, opposite, hostile," from ad- "to" + versus "turned," past participle of vertere "to turn."

USAGE->
The word "adverseness" is primarily a noun and doesn't have tense forms like verbs. It describes a state of being, so its usage revolves around how to describe or relate to that state.

Describing Adverseness:

The adverseness of the situation was apparent to everyone.  情况的逆境对每个人来说都是显而易见的。
She spoke of the adverseness she faced growing up in poverty. 她讲述了她在贫困中长大所面临的逆境。


Responding to Adverseness:

He maintained a positive attitude despite the adverseness of his circumstances. 尽管情况不利，他仍然保持着积极的态度。
Their resilience in the face of adverseness was inspiring. 他们在逆境中的韧性令人鼓舞。
