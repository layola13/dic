coelom:/ˈsiːləm/ (US), /ˈsiːləʊm/ (UK)| n. |体腔|The main body cavity in most multicellular animals.  It is lined with a membrane derived from mesoderm.|The earthworm has a well-developed coelom.|Synonyms: body cavity|Antonyms: N/A|noun


coelomic:/siːˈlɒmɪk/ (UK), /siːˈloʊmɪk/ (US)| adj. |体腔的|Relating to the coelom, the main body cavity in most animals.|The coelomic fluid helps to protect the internal organs.|Synonyms: peritoneal (sometimes)|Antonyms: N/A|adjective


coelenterate: /sɪˈlɛntəreɪt/| n. / adj. |腔肠动物（的）|An aquatic invertebrate animal of the phylum Cnidaria, which comprises the corals, sea anemones, jellyfish, hydroids, and Portuguese men-of-war.  Characterized by a single internal cavity and radial symmetry.|Jellyfish are coelenterates.|Synonyms: cnidarian|Antonyms: N/A|noun/adjective
