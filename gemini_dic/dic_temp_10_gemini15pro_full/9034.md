antimaterialisms:/ˌænti məˈtɪəriəlɪzəmz/
|n.|反物质主义|The rejection of materialism, prioritizing spiritual or intellectual values over material possessions.  It emphasizes the importance of non-material things like experiences, relationships, and personal growth over accumulating wealth or objects.|He embraced antimaterialisms, finding fulfillment in simple living and community engagement. 他信奉反物质主义，在简朴的生活和社区参与中找到了满足感。|近义词:spiritualism, idealism, asceticism|反义词:materialism, consumerism|词性:名词

Etymology:
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to (from Greek anti-)
materialism (noun): a tendency to consider material possessions and physical comfort as more important than spiritual values. (from Late Latin materialis 'relating to matter')
-ism (suffix): forming nouns denoting action, practice, or a system of beliefs.
