disgusted:/dɪsˈɡʌstɪd/ | adj. | 厌恶的，憎恶的 | feeling strong dislike or disapproval for something |  I was disgusted by his behavior. 我对他的行为感到厌恶。|sickened, repulsed, nauseated | pleased, delighted | adjective


disgust:/dɪsˈɡʌst/ | n. | 厌恶，憎恶 | a strong feeling of dislike or disapproval |  She felt a wave of disgust wash over her. 她感到一阵厌恶感涌上心头。| revulsion, aversion, loathing | liking, admiration | noun

disgust:/dɪsˈɡʌst/ | vt. | 使厌恶，使作呕 | to cause someone to feel strong dislike or disapproval | The smell of the rotten food disgusted me. 腐烂食物的气味让我作呕。| sicken, repel, nauseate | please, delight | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desgouster (Modern French dégoûter), from des- "dis-" + gouster "to taste," from Latin gustare "to taste."  The sense evolution was from "to dis-taste" to "to cause dislike" to "to cause nausea."
