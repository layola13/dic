cardiograph: /ˈkɑːrdioʊɡræf/ /ˈkɑːdiəɡrɑːf/
| n. |心动描记器；心电图仪|An instrument that graphically records movements of the heart.  A device used to record the electrical activity of the heart.|The doctor used a cardiograph to monitor the patient's heart rate.| electrocardiograph, ECG machine | - |noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From cardio- (relating to the heart) + -graph (an instrument for recording). Cardio- comes from the Greek kardia (heart). -graph comes from the Greek graphein (to write).
