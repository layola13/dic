anthropomorphizing:/ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːrfaɪzɪŋ/
|v.|赋予人性，人格化|The process of giving human qualities to non-human things, such as animals or objects.|She was anthropomorphizing the cat, talking to it as if it understood her every word. 她正在把这只猫人格化，跟它说话就好像它能听懂她说的每一个字一样。|
|例句：Children often anthropomorphize their toys, giving them names and personalities. 孩子们经常将他们的玩具人格化，给它们起名字和赋予个性。

|例句：He was criticized for anthropomorphizing the behavior of the wolves in his documentary. 他因为在纪录片中将狼的行为人格化而受到批评。
|近义词：personifying, humanizing|反义词: objectifying, dehumanizing|词性: 动词 (present participle)


anthropomorphism:/ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːrfɪzəm/
|n.|拟人化，人格化|The attribution of human characteristics or behavior to a god, animal, or object.|Her novel relied heavily on anthropomorphism to create relatable animal characters. 她的小说大量运用拟人化来塑造引人共鸣的动物角色。
|例句：The cartoon used anthropomorphism to make the animals funny and engaging. 这部动画片用拟人化的手法使动物们显得滑稽有趣，引人入胜。

|例句：Anthropomorphism can be a useful literary device, but it can also be misleading if taken too literally. 拟人化可以是一种有效的文学手法，但如果过于字面理解，也会造成误导。
|近义词：personification, humanization|反义词: objectification|词性: 名词


anthropomorphic:/ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːrfɪk/
|adj.|拟人化的，人格化的|Having or resembling human form or attributes.|The teddy bear had an anthropomorphic face, with big eyes and a smiling mouth. 泰迪熊有一张拟人化的脸，大大的眼睛和微笑的嘴巴。
|例句：Ancient myths are full of anthropomorphic gods and goddesses. 古代神话中充满了拟人化的神和女神。

|例句：The artist's anthropomorphic sculptures of animals were both whimsical and thought-provoking. 艺术家创作的拟人化的动物雕塑既异想天开又发人深省。
|近义词：humanlike, personified|反义词:  N/A|词性: 形容词


词源:
From the Greek ánthrōpos (ἄνθρωπος) meaning "human being" and morphē (μορφή) meaning "form" or "shape."
