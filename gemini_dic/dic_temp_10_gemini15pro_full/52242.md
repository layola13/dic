dibasic:/ˌdaɪˈbeɪsɪk/
adjective
二元的；二碱基的
Having two acidic hydrogen atoms in its molecule.  A dibasic acid is an acid that has two acidic hydrogen atoms in its molecule.  These hydrogens can react with bases to form salts.
Dibasic acids can donate two protons (H+) per molecule in an acid-base reaction.
Citric acid is a tribasic acid, while sulfuric acid is a dibasic acid.  柠檬酸是一种三元酸，而硫酸是一种二元酸。
synonyms: diprotic, bivalent
antonyms: monobasic, tribasic
词性: adjective
