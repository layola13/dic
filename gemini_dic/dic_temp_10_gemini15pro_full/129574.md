non-chronic:/ˌnɒnˈkrɒnɪk/ | adj. | 非慢性的 | Not chronic; not lasting a long time.  |  The doctor assured me that the pain was non-chronic and would subside soon. 医生向我保证，这种疼痛不是慢性的，很快就会消退。 | Example: Thankfully, his cough was non-chronic and cleared up quickly.  幸好，他的咳嗽不是慢性的，很快就痊愈了。| Synonyms: acute, temporary, transient | Antonyms: chronic, persistent, long-term | adjective

Etymology:
"non-" + "chronic"

"non-" is a prefix of Latin origin meaning "not".

"chronic" comes from the Late Latin "chronicus", from Greek "khronikos" meaning "of time" or "lasting a long time", ultimately from "khronos" meaning "time".
