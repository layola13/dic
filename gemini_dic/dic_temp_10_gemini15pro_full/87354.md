heroicomic:/ˌhɪəˈroʊˈkɒmɪk/| adj.|滑稽史诗的，英雄喜剧的|Relating to or resembling both heroic and comic literature, often by treating a trivial subject in a grand or elevated style, or by juxtaposing heroic characters or situations with humorous ones.|The poem had a heroicomic tone, describing a squirrel's battle for a nut as if it were the Trojan War.|近义词：mock-heroic, burlesque, satirical|反义词：serious, tragic|词性:adjective

heroicomics:/ˌhɪəˈroʊˈkɒmɪks/| n.|滑稽史诗体，英雄喜剧体|A genre of literature that blends heroic and comic elements, often by treating a trivial subject in a grand or elevated style, or by juxtaposing heroic characters or situations with humorous ones.|Alexander Pope's "The Rape of the Lock" is a classic example of heroicomics.|近义词：mock-heroic, burlesque, satire|反义词：tragedy, drama|词性:noun
