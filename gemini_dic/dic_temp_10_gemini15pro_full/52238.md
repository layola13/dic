diazeutic:/ˌdaɪəˈzutɪk/
adjective
分开的，不相连的
Separating; disjunctive.  Referring to a grammatical pause or break in a line of verse, typically marked by punctuation.  Also used more generally to describe anything that separates or disjoins.
The diazeutic pause after the first clause emphasizes the speaker's uncertainty.  说话者在第一个从句后的停顿强调了其不确定性。
Synonyms: disjunctive, separative, dividing
Antonyms: conjunctive, connecting, uniting
词性: adjective
