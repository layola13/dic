bugging:/ˈbʌɡɪŋ/ | v. | 烦扰，打扰；窃听 |  annoying or irritating someone; secretly listening to someone's conversations |  The constant noise was really bugging me.  不断的噪音真的让我很烦。 | synonyms: annoying, irritating, bothering, pestering, harassing, eavesdropping (窃听) | antonyms: pleasing, soothing, comforting | verb

bugging:/ˈbʌɡɪŋ/ | n. | 窃听 | the act of secretly listening to someone's conversations | The police were bugging the suspect's phone. 警方正在窃听嫌疑人的电话。 | synonyms: eavesdropping, wiretapping | antonyms:  None | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "bug" (insect), which came to mean a defect or fault in a machine, especially in early computers.  Later, "bug" was extended to mean a concealed microphone used for eavesdropping, leading to the verb "to bug" meaning to install such a device or, more generally, to annoy or bother.
