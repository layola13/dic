logical-empiricism: /ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ɛmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ (British) /ˈlɑːdʒɪkəl ɛmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ (American) | n. | 逻辑经验主义 | A philosophical movement that emphasizes the importance of empirical evidence and logical analysis in the development of scientific knowledge, holding that all meaningful statements are either analytic or empirically verifiable.  |  Logical empiricism was a dominant force in 20th-century philosophy. 逻辑经验主义是20世纪哲学的主导力量。| Synonym: logical positivism, neopositivism | Antonym: idealism, metaphysics | noun

logical: /ˈlɒdʒɪkəl/ (British) /ˈlɑːdʒɪkəl/ (American)| adj. | 逻辑的 | characterized by or capable of clear, sound reasoning. | It was a logical conclusion based on the evidence.  这是基于证据得出的合乎逻辑的结论。| Synonym: rational, reasonable, coherent | Antonym: illogical, irrational, absurd | adjective

empiricism: /ɛmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 经验主义 | the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience. Stimulated by the rise of experimental science, it developed in the 17th and 18th centuries, expounded in particular by John Locke, George Berkeley, and David Hume. | Empiricism emphasizes the role of observation and experimentation in acquiring knowledge. 经验主义强调观察和实验在获取知识中的作用。 | Synonym:  experientialism | Antonym: rationalism, apriorism | noun
