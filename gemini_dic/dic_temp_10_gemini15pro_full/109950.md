liberalized:/ˈlɪbərəlaɪzd/ |v. |自由化的，放宽限制的|Made less strict or restrictive. |The government has liberalized trade policies to stimulate economic growth. 政府已经放宽贸易政策以刺激经济增长。|近义词：deregulated, eased, relaxed|反义词：restricted, tightened, controlled|词性: 动词(过去分词)
liberalize:/ˈlɪbərəlaɪz/ |v. |使自由化，放宽…的限制|To make something less strict or restrictive. |The government is planning to liberalize the banking sector. 政府正计划放宽银行业的限制。|近义词：deregulate, ease, relax|反义词：restrict, tighten, control|词性: 动词
liberalization:/ˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ |n. |自由化，放宽限制|The act or process of making something less strict or restrictive. |The liberalization of trade has led to increased global competition. 贸易自由化导致全球竞争加剧。|近义词：deregulation, easing, relaxation|反义词：restriction, tightening, control|词性: 名词

词源:  From "liberal" + "-ize" (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become"). "Liberal" comes from Latin "liberalis" meaning "of a freeman, noble, generous."
