cabinetmaking:/ˈkæbɪnɪtˌmeɪkɪŋ/
n.|橱柜制造；细木工|The art or trade of designing and constructing cabinets and other fine furniture.| He learned cabinetmaking from his father. 他从他父亲那里学习了细木工。|近义词：joinery, woodwork, carpentry|反义词：(No direct antonym, perhaps destruction or demolition in a broader sense)|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From cabinet + making.  "Cabinet" originates from the Old French word "cabinet," meaning a small room or private chamber, which in turn comes from the Old North French word "cabin," a diminutive of "cabane," meaning hut or cabin. "Making" comes from the Old English word "macian," meaning to make or create.
