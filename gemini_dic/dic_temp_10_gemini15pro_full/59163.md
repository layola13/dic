dyscrasia: /dɪsˈkreɪʒə/ /dɪsˈkreɪziə/ | n. | 体液不调，恶液质 |  An abnormal or disordered state of the body, especially of the blood or other body fluids.  It refers to an imbalance of the four humors (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile) that were once believed to determine a person's health and temperament.  In modern medicine, it is used more generally to denote any abnormal condition of the blood. | Example 1:  The patient's blood dyscrasia was causing him severe fatigue. 病人的血液恶液质使他感到非常疲倦。

Example 2:  Certain types of dyscrasia can be treated with medication. 某些类型的体液不调可以通过药物治疗。 | 近义词: disorder, imbalance, disease | 反义词: health, balance, equilibrium | 词性: noun
