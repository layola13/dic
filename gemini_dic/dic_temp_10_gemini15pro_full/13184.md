at his mercy:/æt hɪz ˈmɜːrsi/
phrase
受他摆布；任凭他处置
being in a situation where someone has complete power over you
The hostages were at the mercy of the terrorists. 人质任凭恐怖分子摆布。
synonyms: powerless, vulnerable, defenseless, helpless
antonyms: in control, powerful, dominant
phrase

at:/æt/
preposition
在，于；向，朝；处于…状态；以，达；因为
expressing location or arrival in a particular place or position.
The meeting is at 3 p.m. 会议在下午3点举行。
synonyms: by, near, close to
antonyms: away from, distant from
preposition

his:/hɪz/
pronoun
他的
belonging to or associated with a male person or animal previously mentioned or easily identified.
That's his car. 那是他的车。
synonyms: of him
antonyms: her, hers
pronoun

mercy:/ˈmɜːrsi/
noun
仁慈；怜悯；幸运；恩惠
compassion or forgiveness shown toward someone whom it is within one's power to punish or harm.
The judge showed no mercy. 法官没有表现出任何怜悯。
synonyms: compassion, pity, kindness, clemency
antonyms: cruelty, ruthlessness, mercilessness
noun
