comprador:/kəmˈprɑːdɔːr/ (US), /kɒmˈprədɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. | 买办 | A native agent of a foreign business house in China, Japan, or other Asian countries, typically in the past. | The comprador managed the firm's local affairs and often acted as an intermediary between foreign merchants and local producers. | The British trading company relied heavily on its comprador to navigate the complexities of the Chinese market. 这家英国贸易公司非常依赖其买办来应对中国市场的复杂性。

The comprador system was a key feature of foreign trade in 19th-century China. 买办制度是19世纪中国对外贸易的一个主要特征。 | agent, intermediary, middleman, factor, representative |  | noun
