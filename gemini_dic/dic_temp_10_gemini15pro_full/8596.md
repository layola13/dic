anticlimax:/ˌæntiˈklaɪmæks/| n.|虎头蛇尾；突降法；反高潮|A disappointing or ineffective ending to a series of events, especially after a build-up of excitement or tension.  A sudden descent from the impressive to the trivial, especially at the end of a series of statements, events, etc.|The movie had a thrilling opening scene, but the rest of the plot was a complete anticlimax. 这部电影的开场戏很刺激，但剩下的剧情完全是虎头蛇尾。

The ending of the book was a disappointing anticlimax after such a strong beginning.  这本书的结尾在如此精彩的开头之后令人失望，虎头蛇尾。|近义词：disappointment, letdown, bathos|反义词: climax, culmination|词性:名词

anticlimactic:/ˌæntiklaɪˈmæktɪk/| adj.|虎头蛇尾的；反高潮的|Disappointing or ineffective after a build-up of excitement or tension.|After all the hype, the concert was rather anticlimactic.  在所有宣传之后，这场音乐会相当令人失望。

The final scene was anticlimactic, leaving the audience feeling unsatisfied.  最后一幕虎头蛇尾，让观众感到不满意。|近义词：disappointing, underwhelming|反义词: climactic, exciting|词性:形容词
