expanse:/ɪkˈspæns/ | n. |广阔; 广阔的区域; 宽阔的区域|A wide and open area or surface, especially of land, sea, or sky. |例句：The vast expanse of the ocean stretched before them.  浩瀚的海洋在他们面前伸展开来。

例句：The Sahara Desert is a vast expanse of sand. 撒哈拉沙漠是一片广阔的沙地。|近义词：area, stretch, extent, vastness|反义词：limit, confinement|词性：名词

expand:/ɪkˈspænd/ | v. |扩大; 扩张; 展开; 发展; 膨胀|To increase in size, scope, or importance.  |例句：The company plans to expand its operations overseas.  该公司计划将其业务扩展到海外。

例句：Metals expand when heated. 金属受热膨胀。|近义词：enlarge, increase, grow, develop, swell|反义词：contract, shrink, reduce, diminish|词性：动词

expansion:/ɪkˈspænʃən/ | n. |扩张; 发展; 膨胀; 扩大; 扩展|The act or process of expanding or the state of being expanded. |例句：The rapid expansion of the city led to housing shortages.  城市的快速扩张导致住房短缺。

例句：The company is undergoing a period of rapid expansion.  公司正处于快速扩张时期。|近义词：growth, development, enlargement, increase, extension|反义词：contraction, reduction, shrinkage, decline|词性：名词

expansive:/ɪkˈspænsɪv/ | adj. |广阔的; 浩瀚的; 辽阔的;  开朗的; 健谈的; 豪爽的|Covering a wide area in terms of space; (of a person or their manner) open, demonstrative, and communicative. |例句：The hotel had expansive grounds and gardens.  这家酒店拥有广阔的庭院和花园。

例句：He was in an expansive mood, talking about his future plans. 他心情舒畅，谈论着他的未来计划。|近义词：wide, extensive, broad, vast, comprehensive (for space); outgoing, communicative, sociable, gregarious (for personality)|反义词：limited, restricted, narrow (for space); reserved, introverted, withdrawn (for personality)|词性：形容词
