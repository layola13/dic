glacier:/ˈɡleɪʃər/ (US), /ˈɡlæsiə(r)/ (UK)| n. |冰川；冰河|A large mass of ice formed from snow on high ground or in polar regions, and moving very slowly down a slope or valley or spreading outwards over a land surface. |例句：The glaciers are melting at an alarming rate. 冰川正在以惊人的速度融化。

例句：The hikers were awestruck by the sheer size of the glacier.  徒步旅行者对冰川的巨大规模感到敬畏。|近义词：ice sheet, ice cap, ice field|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider "desert" or "arid land" for contrasting environments)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *glacier*, from Franco-Provençal *glaciér*, from Vulgar Latin *glaciārium*, from Latin *glacies* ("ice").
