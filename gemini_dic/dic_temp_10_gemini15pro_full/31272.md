chapel:/ˈtʃæpəl/ | n. |小教堂，礼拜堂|A small church or a room in a hospital, school, prison, etc. used for worship.|例句：The wedding was held in a small chapel. 婚礼在一个小教堂举行。|例句：The hospital has a chapel for patients and their families. 医院里有一个供病人及其家属使用的礼拜堂。|近义词：church, oratory|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "secular building")|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old North French *chapele, diminutive of capele "cape, cope, short cloak," from Late Latin cappella "short cloak; chapel, sanctuary where relics of St. Martin's cape were kept," diminutive of cappa "cape, hooded cloak." The sense evolution is from the relic to the sanctuary, then to any small place of worship.
