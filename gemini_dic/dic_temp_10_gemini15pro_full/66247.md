excludability:/ˌɛksˌkluːdəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 排他性，可排除性 | The property of a good or service that allows it to be kept from those who do not pay for it.  This means that it is possible to prevent individuals from consuming the good or service unless they pay the required price. |  Many digital goods lack excludability, making it difficult to charge for them. 许多数字商品缺乏排他性，因此难以收费。|  The fence around the private park ensured excludability.  围栏确保了私人公园的排他性。| synonyms: exclusivity, restrictiveness | antonyms: inclusivity, non-excludability | noun
