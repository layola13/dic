discriminated: /dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪd/ | v. | 歧视；区别；辨别 | treated differently and unfairly, especially on the basis of race, sex, or religion; recognized a difference; distinguished |  例句：She felt discriminated against because of her age. 她觉得因为年龄而受到歧视。 | 近义词：differentiated, distinguished, prejudiced, biased | 反义词：equalized, included | 词性：动词(过去式/过去分词)

discriminated: /dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪd/ | adj. | 被区别对待的；有识别力的 | having been treated differently and unfairly, especially on the basis of race, sex, or religion;  able to recognize small differences or draw fine distinctions | 例句：The discriminated groups protested against the unfair treatment. 受歧视的群体抗议不公平待遇。 | 近义词：differentiated, distinguished, prejudiced, biased | 反义词：equalized, included | 词性：形容词

词源: From Latin discriminat- ‘separated, distinguished’, from the verb discriminare, from discrimen ‘separation, distinction’.
