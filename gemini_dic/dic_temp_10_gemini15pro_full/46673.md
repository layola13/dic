dampen:/ˈdæmpən/ (US), /ˈdæmpən/ (UK)| v. |使潮湿；抑制；减弱；使沮丧|To make something slightly wet or to reduce the intensity of something (like enthusiasm, sound, or vibrations).|The rain dampened the ground. 雨水使地面潮湿了。|moisten, stifle, curb, restrain|dry, encourage, intensify|verb
dampening:/ˈdæmpənɪŋ/ (US), /ˈdæmpənɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |抑制；阻尼；减幅|The act of dampening something, or the reduction in intensity of something.|The dampening effect of the news on the market was significant.  这消息对市场的抑制效应很明显。|reduction, suppression, mitigation|increase, amplification, encouragement|noun
dampening:/ˈdæmpənɪŋ/ (US), /ˈdæmpənɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |抑制的；减震的；阻尼的|Having the effect of dampening; tending to reduce intensity.|The dampening effect of the thick curtains made the room very quiet. 厚厚的窗帘的抑制效果使房间非常安静。|suppressing, restraining, mitigating|amplifying, intensifying, encouraging|adjective
