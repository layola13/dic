concordat: /kənˈkɔːrdæt/ (UK), /kənˈkɔːrdæt/ (US) | n. | 协定，合约，一致协议 | An official agreement between two or more parties, especially one between the Vatican and a secular government relating to matters of mutual interest. | The concordat between the Church and the State allowed for the appointment of bishops. 教会与国家之间的协议允许任命主教。|  The government signed a concordat with the Vatican regarding religious education in schools. 政府与梵蒂冈签署了一项关于学校宗教教育的协议。| agreement, treaty, pact, covenant | disagreement, conflict, discord | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: via Old French from medieval Latin concordatum ‘thing agreed upon’, neuter past participle of concordare, from con- ‘together’ + cord- ‘heart’.
