imprudencies: /ɪmˈpruːdənsiz/ | n. | 轻率行为；鲁莽举动 | Lack of caution or good judgment; rashness. |  His youthful imprudencies cost him dearly later in life.  他年轻时的轻率行为在他日后生活中付出了沉重的代价。 | Synonyms: follies, indiscretions, recklessness | Antonyms: caution, prudence, wisdom | noun

Etymology:  From Late Middle English *imprudence, from Middle French imprudence, from Latin imprudentia (“lack of foresight, want of caution”), from imprudent- (“not foreseeing, incautious”), from in- (“not”) + prudent- (“foreseeing, provident”).
