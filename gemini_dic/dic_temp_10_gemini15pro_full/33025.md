chortling: /ˈtʃɔːrtlɪŋ/ (US), /ˈtʃɔːtlɪŋ/ (UK)
| n. |  咯咯笑；欢笑 |  An act of chortling; a gleeful chuckle. | He let out a chortle of delight when he heard the good news. 他听到好消息时高兴地咯咯笑了起来。 | synonyms: chuckling, giggling, snickering | antonyms: sobbing, crying, wailing | Verb (present participle)

chortle: /ˈtʃɔːrtl/ (US), /ˈtɔːtl/ (UK)
| v. | 咯咯笑；欢笑 |  To laugh in a gleeful, chuckling way, often suggesting self-satisfaction or triumph. |  She chortled with glee at her success. 她因自己的成功而高兴地咯咯笑了起来。 | synonyms: chuckle, giggle, snicker | antonyms: sob, cry, wail | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
Coined by Lewis Carroll in his book "Through the Looking-Glass, and What Alice Found There" (1871). It's a portmanteau of "chuckle" and "snort."
