intercolonizing: /ˌɪntərˈkɒlənaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |相互殖民，互相殖民|The process by which different groups establish colonies within each other's territories or spheres of influence.|例句：The two ant species were observed intercolonizing, each establishing nests within the other's foraging range. 两种蚂蚁被观察到相互殖民，各自在对方的觅食范围内建立巢穴。|例句：The study explored the complex dynamics of intercolonizing between competing European powers in the 18th century. 这项研究探讨了18世纪欧洲列强之间相互殖民的复杂动态。|近义词：intermingling, co-colonizing|反义词: segregating, separating|词性:动词

词源:
inter- (prefix): between, among (来自拉丁语)
colonize (verb): to establish a colony (来自拉丁语 colonus, meaning "farmer, settler")
-ing (suffix): present participle ending
