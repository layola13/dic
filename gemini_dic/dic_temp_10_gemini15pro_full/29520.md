catatonia: /ˌkætəˈtoʊniə/ (US), /ˌkætəˈtəʊniə/ (UK)
|n.|紧张症，僵住症|A state of immobility and stupor, often associated with schizophrenia or other mental illnesses, characterized by a lack of responsiveness to external stimuli.|The patient exhibited catatonia, remaining motionless and unresponsive for hours.|近义词: stupor, rigidity, immobility|反义词: responsiveness, mobility, activity|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek κατά (katá, “down”) + τόνος (tónos, “tension, tone”).
