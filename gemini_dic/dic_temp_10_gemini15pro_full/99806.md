intermication: /ˌɪntər.mɪˈkeɪ.ʃən/
noun
间歇；中断；插入；穿插
The act of intermicating or intermixing; interruption. The act of flashing or gleaming intermittently.
The speaker's intermication of jokes into the serious lecture kept the audience engaged. 演讲者在严肃的讲座中穿插笑话，使观众保持了兴趣。
近义词: intermission, interruption, pause, break, interval, hiatus
反义词: continuation, continuance, persistence
词性: n.


Etymology: From Late Latin *intermicatio,  from Latin intermicare (“to flash, gleam, glitter; to shine between or among”), from inter (“between”) + micare (“to flash, glitter”).
