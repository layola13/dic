nonbody:/ˈnəʊbɒdi/ (UK) /ˈnoʊbɑːdi/ (US)|pron. n.|无名小卒，小人物；没有人|A person of no importance or influence; no one.| He was a nobody before he won the lottery. 他在赢得彩票之前是个无名小卒.|Synonyms: insignificant person, unimportant person, nonentity, cipher|Antonyms: somebody, VIP, celebrity, dignitary|词性: 代词/名词

nobody:/ˈnəʊbɒdi/ (UK) /ˈnoʊbɑːdi/ (US)|pron.|没有人|No one; not a single person.|Nobody answered the phone. 没有人接电话.|Synonyms: no one, not a soul, not anyone|Antonyms: everybody, everyone, somebody, someone|词性: 代词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *nān body (“no body”), from nān (“no, not any”) + body (“person”).  The word "body" used to mean "person," a usage that still survives in phrases like "somebody" and "anybody."
