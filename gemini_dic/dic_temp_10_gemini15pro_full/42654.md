counterstated:/ˌkaʊntərˈsteɪtɪd/
|adj.|反驳的，相反的|stated the opposite of something; denied the truth of something|His claims were counterstated by evidence presented in court. 他的说法被法庭上提出的证据反驳了。|Synonyms: contradicted, refuted, denied, opposed|Antonyms: confirmed, supported, corroborated|adjective

counterstated:/ˌkaʊntərˈsteɪtɪd/
|v.|反驳，提出相反意见|(past participle of counterstate) to state the opposite of something; to deny the truth of something|The witness counterstated the defendant's alibi.  证人反驳了被告的不在场证明。|Synonyms: contradicted, refuted, denied, opposed|Antonyms: confirmed, supported, corroborated|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- (prefix meaning "against, opposite") + stated (past participle of state, meaning "to express in words").  The prefix "counter-" comes from the Old French "contre-" and ultimately from the Latin "contra" meaning "against."
