blundering:/ˈblʌndərɪŋ/| adj.|笨手笨脚的；错误百出的；粗枝大叶的|Clumsy and careless in one's actions or words. Making repeated mistakes. |例句：His blundering attempt to fix the sink only made things worse. 他笨手笨脚地试图修理水槽，结果反而弄得更糟了。

例句：The blundering waiter spilled soup on three customers.  笨手笨脚的服务员把汤洒在了三位顾客身上。

近义词：clumsy, awkward, inept, maladroit, stumbling|反义词：skillful, adept, dexterous, graceful|词性:形容词

blunder:/ˈblʌndər/| n. vi.|错误；失误；犯大错；踉踉跄跄地走|A stupid or careless mistake. To move clumsily and unsteadily.|例句：I made a terrible blunder by forgetting her birthday. 我犯了一个严重的错误，忘记了她的生日。

例句：He blundered into the room, knocking over a lamp. 他踉踉跄跄地走进房间，撞翻了一盏灯。

近义词：mistake, error, gaffe, slip-up|反义词：success, triumph|词性:名词/动词

词源：From Middle English *blundren, probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian blundra (“to close one’s eyes; blunder”), Icelandic blunda (“to doze”).
