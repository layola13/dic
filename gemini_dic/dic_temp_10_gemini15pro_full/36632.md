/kəˈlɛktɪvɪsts/ | n. | 集体主义者 | People who believe in collectivism, prioritizing the needs and goals of the group over individual desires. |  Collectivists often emphasize cooperation and shared responsibility. | 近义词:communalists, socialists | 反义词:individualists | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From "collective" + "-ist."  "Collective" comes from the Latin "collectus," the past participle of "colligere," meaning "to gather together."  The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system.
