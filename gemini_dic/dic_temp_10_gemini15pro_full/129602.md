non-compounder:/ˌnɒnˈkɒmpaʊndər/ | n. | 非复合者；非累积者；非复利计算者 | Someone or something that does not compound, specifically referring to interest, growth, or other cumulative processes.  It can also refer to someone who doesn't make things worse by adding to a problem. |  The non-compounder lost out on significant gains over the long term.  这个非复利计算者从长远来看损失了巨大的收益。

Warren Buffett prefers investments that compound over time, while a non-compounder might miss these opportunities.  沃伦·巴菲特更喜欢随着时间的推移而复利的投资，而非复利计算者可能会错过这些机会。 | 近义词: simple investor, non-accumulator | 反义词: compounder, accumulator | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
non- (prefix): from Latin *non "not"
compound (verb): from Old French compondre, from Latin componere "to put together," from com- "together" + ponere "to put, place"
-er (suffix): forms agent nouns, meaning "one who performs the action of the verb"
