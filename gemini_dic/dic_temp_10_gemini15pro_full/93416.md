ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: illogical + -ity + -ies (plural).  Illogical comes from in- ‘not’ + logical, and logical comes from  late Latin logicalis, from Greek logikos ‘of or pertaining to reason’, from logos ‘word, reason’.
