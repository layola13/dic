menacingly:/ˈmenɪsɪŋli/ | adv. | 威胁地，险恶地 | In a way that suggests the presence of danger or threat. |  The dog growled menacingly at the stranger. 狗对着陌生人发出威胁的低吼。| Synonyms: threateningly, ominously, forebodingly | Antonyms: reassuringly, comfortingly | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From menace (n.) + -ing + -ly.  "Menace" comes from Old French "menace" (noun) and "menacer" (verb), from Late Latin "minaciare,"  "to threaten," from Latin "minaci-" (stem of "minax") "threatening," which is related to "minari" "to threaten."
