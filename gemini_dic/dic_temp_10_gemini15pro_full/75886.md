fundamentalisms: /ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪzəmz/ | n. | 原教旨主义 | A usually religious movement or point of view characterized by a return to fundamental principles, strict adherence to those principles, and often intolerance of other views and practices. |  The rise of fundamentalisms around the world is a complex phenomenon. 全球原教旨主义的兴起是一个复杂的现象。|  The conference explored the various forms of religious fundamentalisms.  这次会议探讨了各种形式的宗教原教旨主义。 |近义词：dogmatism, orthodoxy, traditionalism | 反义词：modernism, liberalism, secularism | 词性: 名词


Etymology:

From fundamental + -ism.  "Fundamental" traces back to the Latin "fundamentum," meaning "foundation."  The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, belief system, or practice.
