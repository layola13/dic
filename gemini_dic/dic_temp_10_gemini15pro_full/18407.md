below-stairs:/bɪˈləʊ ˈsteərz/
| adj.  n.|楼下的，仆人用的；底层的|Relating to the servants of a house, or the part of a house where they live and work.|The below-stairs staff were not allowed to use the front door.  楼下的工作人员不允许使用前门。|
| |The below-stairs area was cramped and poorly lit. 楼下的区域狭小且光线昏暗。|近义词:downstairs, servants' quarters|反义词:upstairs, masters' quarters|词性:形容词, 名词


Etymology:
From "below" + "stairs," literally meaning "below the stairs," referencing the traditional location of servants' quarters in large houses.  "Below" comes from Old English "be-" (by, around) + "low" (near the ground). "Stairs" comes from Old English "stæger" (a set of steps).
