murmuring:/ˈmɜːrmərɪŋ/ (US) /ˈmɜːmərɪŋ/ (UK)| n. v. adj.|低语声，喃喃声; 抱怨声;  低声说; 发出低沉连续的声音|A low, continuous, indistinct sound, like that of running water.  The act of speaking softly and indistinctly. A complaint uttered in a low tone.|The murmuring of the stream was soothing. (小溪的潺潺流水声令人舒缓。)  He was murmuring something about the unfairness of it all. (他正在低声抱怨这一切的不公平。) The murmuring crowd grew restless. (低声抱怨的人群变得焦躁不安。) |近义词：whispering, muttering, grumbling, complaining|反义词: shouting, yelling, roaring|词性: Noun, Verb, Adjective (present participle)
Etymology: From Old French murmurer, from Latin murmurare ("to murmur, to roar softly").
