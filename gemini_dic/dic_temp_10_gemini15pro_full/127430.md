词源：From Middle English *netenes, from Old English *nēatnes (“neatness”), from nēat (“cattle, animal”) + -nes, equivalent to neat + -ness.  The original meaning referred to the quality of being sleek and well-fed like livestock, later evolving to mean tidiness and orderliness.
