hydrophora:/haɪˈdrɒfərə/ | n. |水囊，水螅体| A sac containing fluid, especially in a plant or invertebrate.  In zoology, it refers specifically to the polyp stage in the life cycle of a hydrozoan. | The hydrophora of the jellyfish released a cloud of stinging cells. 水母的水螅体释放出一团刺细胞。 |  Synonyms:  polyp (in zoology), vesicle, sac | Antonyms: medusa (in zoology - contrasting life stage) | noun

Etymology:  From Ancient Greek  ὕδωρ (húdōr, “water”) +‎  φέρω (phérō, “I carry, I bear”).
