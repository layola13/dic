paralyse:/ˈpærəlaɪz/ (UK) /ˈpærəlɪz/ (US)| vt.|使瘫痪，使麻痹；使丧失作用；使惊呆|To cause (a person or part of the body) to lose the ability to move or feel.|The accident paralysed him from the waist down.  事故使他腰部以下瘫痪。|Synonyms: disable, incapacitate, immobilize, cripple|Antonyms: enable, empower, activate|Verb

paralysis:/pəˈrælɪsɪs/ (UK) /pəˈræləsɪs/ (US)| n.|瘫痪，麻痹；停顿；无力|The loss of the ability to move some or all of your body, usually caused by disease or injury.|He suffered paralysis of the legs after the accident. 事故后他的双腿瘫痪了。|Synonyms: immobility, incapacity, powerlessness|Antonyms: mobility, ability, power|Noun
