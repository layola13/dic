barrage:/bəˈrɑːʒ/ /bəˈrɑːdʒ/
noun
阻塞，拦阻；弹幕；堰
a rapid, large-scale outpouring of something
The star athlete faced a barrage of questions from reporters.  这位明星运动员面临记者连珠炮似的提问。

barrage:/bəˈrɑːʒ/ /bəˈrɑːdʒ/
verb
以密集火力攻击；(以大量问题、批评等)攻击
to bombard someone with something
The troops were barraged with enemy fire.  部队遭到敌人的炮火猛烈攻击。

Etymology: French *barrage*, from *barre* "bar" (ultimately from Late Latin *barra*)

Synonym: bombardment, volley, onslaught, deluge, flood
Antonym: trickle, dribble, dearth, paucity
