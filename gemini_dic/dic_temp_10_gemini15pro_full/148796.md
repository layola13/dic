overvaluations: /ˌōvərˌvæljuˈeɪʃənz/
n.
高估，估价过高
An act of overestimating the worth or value of something.
The overvaluations in the housing market led to the financial crisis. 房地产市场的高估导致了金融危机。

Overvaluations of assets can lead to significant losses. 资产的高估会导致重大损失。

近义词: overestimates, inflations
反义词: undervaluations, underestimates
词性: noun (plural)


overvaluation: /ˌōvərˌvæljuˈeɪʃən/
n.
高估，估价过高
The act of assigning a value that is too high; an excessively high valuation.
The stock market is prone to periods of overvaluation. 股票市场容易出现估值过高的时期。

There was a significant overvaluation of the company's assets. 该公司资产存在严重高估。


近义词: overestimate, inflation
反义词: undervaluation, underestimate
词性: noun


词源:
From over- + valuation.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much". "Valuation" comes from the Late Latin "valuatio," from "valuare" meaning "to value."
