intake:/ˈɪnteɪk/ (US); /ˈɪnteɪk/ (UK)| n. |摄入量；吸入量；入口；（尤指一年内）招生人数|An act of taking something in, especially breath or food. The amount of something that is taken in.  A place where liquid or air is taken into something (such as a pipe or channel). The group of people who are accepted into a school or organization at a particular time.|例句：The doctor advised her to reduce her sugar intake. 医生建议她减少糖的摄入量。

例句：The college has increased its intake of overseas students. 这所大学增加了海外学生的招生人数。|近义词：consumption, ingestion, admission|反义词：output, exhaust, discharge|词性: noun


intake:/ˈɪnteɪk/ (US); /ˈɪnteɪk/ (UK)| adj. |入口的；输入的|Relating to or used for taking something in.|例句：The intake valve of the engine is faulty.  发动机的进气阀坏了。

例句：The intake pipe was blocked. 进水管堵塞了。|近义词：inlet, input|反义词：outlet, output|词性: adjective
