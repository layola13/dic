marle:/mɑːrl/| n. |泥灰岩；泥灰土；（尤指淡蓝绿、灰绿或红褐色等）杂色织物|A calcareous clay or loam, or a mixture of clay and sand, often used as a fertilizer. Also, a fabric, especially yarn or cloth, woven from different-colored fibers, often in muted shades of blue-green, gray-green, or reddish-brown.|The farmer spread marle on his fields to improve the soil.  农民在田里撒了泥灰来改良土壤。|Synonyms: loam, clay, marlstone|Antonyms: (For the soil meaning) sand, gravel|noun


marle:/mɑːrl/| v. |用泥灰施肥；使杂色|To fertilize with marle; to variegate in color.|He marled the field before planting the wheat. 他在播种小麦之前给田地施了泥灰肥。|Synonyms: fertilize, enrich, variegate|Antonyms: deplete, impoverish|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *marle, from Medieval Latin margila, of uncertain origin.
