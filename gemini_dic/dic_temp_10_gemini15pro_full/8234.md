anti-idealism:/ˌænti aɪˈdiːəlɪzəm/| n. |反理想主义|A doctrine or practice that rejects idealism. It emphasizes the practical, the materialistic, or the real, often denying the relevance or importance of abstract or ideal principles.|His anti-idealism led him to believe that all motivations were ultimately selfish.|近义词:realism, pragmatism, cynicism|反义词:idealism, romanticism|词性:名词
Etymology: anti- (prefix meaning "against, opposite of") + idealism (from ideal + -ism, denoting a doctrine or system)
