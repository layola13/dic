词源:
From Middle English cruel, from Old French cruel (French cruel), from Latin crūdēlis (“raw, unripe, cruel, harsh”), from crūdus (“raw, bloody, uncooked, immature, rude”).


注意事项:
The word "crueller" is less common than "more cruel," especially in formal writing.  However, both are grammatically correct.  The noun form "cruller" has variations in spelling (e.g., "kruller") and pronunciation, particularly in American English, where the "r" sound is often pronounced.
