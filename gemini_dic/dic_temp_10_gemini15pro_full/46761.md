danegeld:/ˈdeɪnɡɛld/| n.|丹麦金，贡金|A tax levied in Anglo-Saxon England to buy off Danish invaders or to pay mercenaries to fight them.  It has come to mean any payment made under duress to avoid further trouble.|The government's bailout of the failing bank felt like a danegeld, paid only to stave off further economic disaster. 政府对这家倒闭银行的救助感觉就像丹麦金，只是为了避免进一步的经济灾难。|Near synonyms: tribute, protection money, ransom, payoff|Near antonyms: reward, earnings|noun
Etymology: From Old English *Denegeld "Dane-money," from Dene "Danes" + geld "payment, tribute, tax."
