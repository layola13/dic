flitting:/ˈflɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 轻快地移动的；一闪而过的；短暂的 | Moving swiftly and lightly; passing quickly; lasting only a short time | Butterflies were flitting from flower to flower. 蝴蝶在花丛中飞来飞去。 | synonyms: darting, skimming, flashing, fleeting | antonyms: lingering, remaining, staying | adjective

flitting:/ˈflɪtɪŋ/ | n. |  轻快的移动；一闪而过 | A swift, light movement; a brief stay | The flitting of the hummingbird's wings was too fast to see. 蜂鸟翅膀的快速扇动难以看清。| synonyms: flutter, dart, flash | antonyms: permanence, stay | noun

Etymology: From Middle English flitten, from Old English *flyttan (“to remove, change one's dwelling”), from Proto-Germanic *flutjaną (“to float, swim, fly”). Related to float.
