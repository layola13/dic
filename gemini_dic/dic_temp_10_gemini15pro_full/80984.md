gradate:/ˈɡrædʒueɪt/ (US), /ˈɡrædʒʊeɪt/ (UK)| v.  n. | 毕业；授予学位；分等级；渐变 | to receive an academic degree or diploma; to arrange or sort in a series or scale; to change gradually |例句：He graduated from Harvard University last year.  他去年从哈佛大学毕业。|近义词：graduate, complete, finish, qualify |反义词: commence, begin, start |词性: verb

gradate:/ˈɡrædʒueɪt/ (US), /ˈɡrædʒʊeɪt/ (UK) | n. | 毕业生 | a person who has earned a degree or diploma from a school, college, or university| 例句：She is a graduate of Yale University. 她是一位耶鲁大学的毕业生。 | 近义词：alumnus, alumna, graduate |反义词: undergraduate, freshman, sophomore | 词性: noun

gradate:/ˈɡrædjʊeɪt/ (US), /ˈɡrædjʊət/ (UK)| adj. | 毕业的；分等级的；渐变的 | relating to or designed for graduates; arranged in a series or scale; changing gradually | 例句：He is pursuing a graduate degree in physics. 他正在攻读物理学研究生学位。 | 近义词：graduated, graded, progressive | 反义词: abrupt, sudden | 词性: adjective
