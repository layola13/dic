deprivational:/ˌdɛprɪˈveɪʃənəl/
adjective
剥夺的; 缺乏的; 丧失的
Relating to or characterized by deprivation.  Resulting from a lack of something considered to be a necessity.
The deprivational effects of poverty can have lasting consequences. 贫困带来的剥夺效应会产生持久的影响。
Children raised in deprivational circumstances often struggle in school. 在匮乏环境中长大的孩子在学校里往往很吃力。
近义词: deficient, lacking, impoverished, disadvantaged
反义词: affluent, privileged, enriched
词性: adjective
词源:  From deprivation + -al. Deprivation comes from the Late Latin dēprīvātiōn-, dēprīvātiō, from dēprīvāre (to dispossess, deprive), from dē- (de-) + prīvāre (to deprive), from prīvus (private, one's own).
