disincentivize:/ˌdɪsɪnˈsɛntɪvaɪz/
verb
使失去动力；打消积极性
To discourage someone from doing something by removing an incentive or introducing a negative consequence.
The new tax laws disincentivize investment in small businesses. 新的税法打消了人们对小企业的投资积极性。

The threat of fines disincentivizes speeding. 罚款的威胁降低了超速行为的发生。

近义词:deter, discourage, dissuade
反义词:incentivize, encourage, motivate
词性:verb


Etymology:
From dis- (expressing negation) + incentivize, which is from incentive + -ize.  The word "incentive" comes from Late Latin incentivum "something that incites," from neuter of incentivus "inciting," from past participle stem of incentivare "to set the tune," from in- "into" + cantare "to sing."
