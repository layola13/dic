fragging:/ˈfræɡɪŋ/| n. | 友军互残，故意杀害（战友）| The act of deliberately killing or attempting to kill a fellow soldier, typically by means of a fragmentation grenade. |  The reports of fragging during the Vietnam War were deeply disturbing.  越战期间关于友军互残的报道令人深感不安。

|Synonyms:  murder, assassination, killing, friendly fire (sometimes)|Antonyms:  cooperation, camaraderie, teamwork|Noun|

fragging:/ˈfræɡɪŋ/| v. |  友军互残，故意杀害（战友）| To deliberately kill or attempt to kill a fellow soldier, typically by means of a fragmentation grenade. |  The sergeant was suspected of fragging his lieutenant.  这名中士被怀疑杀害了他的中尉。


|Synonyms:  murder, assassinate, kill |Antonyms:  protect, defend, support|Verb|

ETYMOLOGY->
From the noun "frag" (short for fragmentation grenade), which became a verb meaning "to attack with a fragmentation grenade," and then evolved to mean specifically attacking a fellow soldier.
