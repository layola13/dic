hedonists:/ˈhiːdənɪsts/ | n. |享乐主义者|  A person who believes that pleasure or happiness is the most important goal in life.  A hedonist is someone who pursues pleasure and self-indulgence above all else. | The ancient Greeks had philosophers who were hedonists. 古希腊有一些享乐主义哲学家。|  Some people criticize consumerism for creating a society of hedonists.  有些人批评消费主义创造了一个享乐主义的社会。|Synonyms: pleasure-seeker, sensualist, epicurean, sybarite | Antonyms: ascetic, puritan, stoic | Noun


Etymology:
From Greek  ἡδονή (hēdonē, “pleasure”) + -ist.
