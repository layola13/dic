butterbird:/ˈbʌtərˌbɜːrd/| n.|黄鹂|A North American oriole, especially the Baltimore oriole (Icterus galbula), noted for its bright orange and black plumage.  It is sometimes called this due to its fondness for eating caterpillars that feed on plants in the genus *Asclepias*, commonly known as milkweed or butterfly weed, which contains cardiac glycosides that can make the bird unpalatable to predators.|The butterbird flashed its bright orange wings as it flew through the garden.|Synonyms: Baltimore oriole, oriole|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
