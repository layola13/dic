amanuenses:/əˌmænjuˈɛnsiːz/
noun
抄写员，书记员
A person employed to write from dictation or to copy manuscript.
The author thanked his amanuenses for their tireless work in preparing the manuscript.  这位作者感谢他的抄写员们为准备手稿所做的不懈努力。
Medieval monks often served as amanuenses, copying religious texts. 中世纪的僧侣经常担任抄写员，抄写宗教文本。
scribes, copyists, clerks
typists
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *amanuensis*, from *a manu* "by hand" + *-ensis* (suffix forming nouns denoting place, origin, or material).
