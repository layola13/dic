bedfellowship:/ˈbedˌfeləʊʃɪp/ (US) /ˈbedˌfeləʊʃɪp/ (UK)| n.|伙伴关系；盟友关系；（尤指暂时的）合作关系|The state of being bedfellows; the sharing of a bed. Figuratively, it refers to an alliance or association, often temporary and sometimes uncomfortable, formed for a specific purpose.|The two rival companies found themselves in an unlikely bedfellowship to lobby for the new tax law. 这两家竞争公司为了游说新的税法，出人意料地结成了联盟。|近义词：alliance, partnership, association, coalition|反义词：rivalry, antagonism, opposition|词性:名词

Etymology:
From bed + fellow + ship.  "Bedfellow" dates back to the late 14th century, referring literally to someone sharing a bed. The figurative sense of "ally" or "associate" developed by the late 16th century.  "-ship" is a suffix forming abstract nouns denoting state, condition, or character.
