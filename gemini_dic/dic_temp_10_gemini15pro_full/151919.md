pasteurization:/ˌpæstərɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 巴氏灭菌法；巴氏消毒法 | The partial sterilization of a product, such as milk or wine, to make it safe for consumption and improve its keeping quality, without changing its fundamental composition.  |  Pasteurization is a crucial process for ensuring milk safety. 巴氏灭菌法是确保牛奶安全的关键工序。 | sterilization, disinfection | contamination | noun

pasteurize:/ˈpæstərˌaɪz/ | vt. | 巴氏消毒；巴氏灭菌 | To subject (milk, wine, or other products) to pasteurization. |  The milk is pasteurized to kill harmful bacteria.  牛奶经过巴氏消毒以杀死有害细菌。| sterilize, disinfect | contaminate | verb


Etymology:
From French *pasteurisation*, from Louis Pasteur, French chemist and microbiologist (1822–1895) + -isation.
