nonpracticably:/ˌnɒnˈpræktɪkəbli/ | adv. | 不切实际地 | In a manner that is not practical or feasible; impossibly. |  The plan was nonpracticably expensive.  这个计划贵得离谱，不切实际。| Synonyms:  impracticably, infeasibly, impossibly | Antonyms: practicably, feasibly, possibly | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From non- + practicably.  "Practicably" comes from "practicable" + "-ly." "Practicable" derives from Late Middle English: from late Latin *practicabilis, from practicare ‘perform,’ from Latin practice ‘done, performed,’ from the verb practicare, frequentative of praestare ‘perform.’
