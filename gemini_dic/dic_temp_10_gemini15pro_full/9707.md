antitraditionalists:/ˌæntitrəˈdɪʃənəlɪsts/
|n.|反传统主义者|People who oppose or reject traditional beliefs, customs, or practices.  Those who challenge established norms and advocate for change.|The antitraditionalists questioned the relevance of age-old customs in modern society. 反传统主义者质疑古老习俗在现代社会中的相关性。|
|
|synonyms: iconoclasts, reformers, radicals, revolutionaries, modernists|
|
|antonyms: traditionalists, conservatives, conformists|
|noun|


antitraditionalist:/ˌæntitrəˈdɪʃənəlɪst/
|adj.|反传统的|Opposing or rejecting traditional beliefs, customs, or practices. Characterized by a departure from established norms.|Her antitraditionalist views on marriage surprised her conservative family. 她反传统的婚姻观令她保守的家人感到惊讶。|
|
|synonyms: iconoclastic, reformist, radical, revolutionary, modernist|
|
|antonyms: traditional, conservative, conformist|
|adjective|


antitraditional:/ˌæntitrəˈdɪʃənəl/
|adj.|反传统的|Opposing or rejecting traditional beliefs, customs, or practices. Characterized by a departure from established norms.|They took an antitraditional approach to education, emphasizing creativity over rote learning. 他们在教育上采取了反传统的方法，强调创造力而不是死记硬背。|
|
|synonyms: iconoclastic, reformist, radical, revolutionary, modernist|
|
|antonyms: traditional, conservative, conformist|
|adjective|


Etymology:  "anti-" (Greek, meaning "against") + "tradition" (from Latin "traditio," meaning "handing down") + "-al" (suffix forming adjectives) + "-ist" (suffix forming nouns and adjectives denoting a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system).
