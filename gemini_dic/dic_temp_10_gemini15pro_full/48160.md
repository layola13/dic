decommission:/ˌdiːkəˈmɪʃən/
verb
使退役；使停止使用
To officially take something out of service, especially a ship, aircraft, or nuclear power station.
The navy decommissioned several of its older ships. 海军让几艘旧船退役了。

decommission:/ˌdiːkəˈmɪʃən/
noun
退役；停止使用
The act of officially taking something out of service.
The decommissioning of the nuclear power plant will take many years.  核电站的退役将需要很多年。


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- (prefix meaning 'reverse' or 'remove') + commission (meaning 'to put into service').  Ultimately from Latin committere 'to entrust'.

SYNONYMS->
retire, withdraw, mothball, dismantle, scrap

ANTONYMS->
commission, launch, activate
