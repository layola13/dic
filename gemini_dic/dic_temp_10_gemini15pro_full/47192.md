n. vi.|白日梦，幻想|A series of pleasant thoughts that distract one's attention from the present.|例句：He was lost in a daydream about winning the lottery. 他沉浸在赢得彩票的白日梦里。|近义词：fantasy, reverie, hallucination|反义词：reality, actuality|词性：名词，动词

daydream: /ˈdeɪdriːm/ (US) /ˈdeɪdriːm/ (UK)
vi.|做白日梦，幻想|To have pleasant thoughts that distract one's attention from the present.|例句：She often daydreams during class. 她上课时经常做白日梦。|近义词：fantasize, muse, woolgather|反义词：focus, concentrate|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "day" + "dream".  The word signifies a dream dreamt during the day, hence a pleasant visionary fancy.  First recorded use: mid-17th century.
