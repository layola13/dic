hotelling:/hoʊˈtɛlɪŋ/| n.|霍特林模型，霍特林竞争|A model in economics that describes the location choices of firms in a spatial market, where firms compete by choosing locations that minimize transportation costs for consumers.  It is named after economist Harold Hotelling.|Hotelling's model explains why businesses often cluster together geographically, even when they are competitors. 霍特林模型解释了为什么企业即使是竞争对手，也经常在地理位置上聚集在一起。|Hotelling's law predicts that in many markets, product offerings will tend to converge over time. 霍特林法则预测，在许多市场中，产品供应会随着时间的推移趋于一致。|synonyms: spatial competition, location model|antonyms: perfect competition, monopoly|noun
