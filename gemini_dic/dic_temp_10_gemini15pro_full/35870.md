cocktail:/ˈkɒkteɪl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkteɪl/ (US)| n.|鸡尾酒;混合物;（尤指药物的）混合剂;（西式婚礼等的）午后冷餐招待会|An alcoholic drink consisting of a spirit or several spirits mixed with other ingredients, such as fruit juice, lemonade, or cream.|例句：He ordered a cocktail for her. 他为她点了一杯鸡尾酒。|例句：This situation is a cocktail of different problems.  这种情况是各种问题的混合体。|近义词：mixed drink, beverage|反义词: None|词性:noun


cocktail:/ˈkɒkteɪl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkteɪl/ (US)| adj.|鸡尾酒的;（食物）用于鸡尾酒会的|Relating to or served at a cocktail party.|例句：She wore a cocktail dress to the party. 她穿着一件鸡尾酒礼服去参加聚会。|例句：They served cocktail snacks. 他们供应了鸡尾酒会小吃。|近义词：mixed, blended (for drinks)|反义词: None|词性:adjective

词源：
From the cock's tail, referring to a mixed drink. The exact origin is disputed, but one theory suggests it's named after the practice of garnishing drinks with a rooster's tail feathers. Another suggests it comes from the mixed colors of a drink, resembling a rooster's tail.
