babbler:/ˈbæblər/| n. |喋喋不休的人，饶舌的人；（鸟）鸣禽|A person who talks constantly and rapidly, especially about unimportant things; a bird that makes a continuous chattering sound.|The child was a babbler, constantly talking about anything and everything.  这个孩子是个饶舌的人，总是喋喋不休地谈论各种事情。|The babblers in the aviary created a constant background noise.  鸟舍里的鸣禽发出持续的背景噪音。|近义词：chatterbox, gossip, prattler|反义词：reticent person, quiet person|词性:noun
babble:/ˈbæbl/| v.  n. |喋喋不休地说，胡言乱语；潺潺声|To talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way; the sound of water flowing over stones.|The toddler babbled excitedly about his day at the park.  这个蹒跚学步的孩子兴奋地讲述了他在公园里的一天。|The babble of the brook was soothing.  小溪的潺潺流水声令人舒缓。|近义词：chatter, prattle, murmur (v.); murmur, gurgling (n.)|反义词：articulate (v.)|词性:verb, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bablen, frequentative of baben, probably of imitative origin.  Related to babble (v.).
