comanager:/kəʊˈmænɪdʒər/ | n. | 共同经理；联合经理 | A person who shares the responsibility of managing a business, organization, or team with one or more other people. |  Example: She and her brother are comanagers of the family business. 她和她哥哥是家族企业的共同经理。

Example: The project has two comanagers, each with different areas of expertise. 这个项目有两个共同经理，每个经理都有不同的专业领域。| 近义词: co-director, joint manager | 反义词: subordinate, employee | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From co- + manager. Co- comes from the Latin prefix com-, meaning "together, jointly." Manager comes from the Old French word manage, meaning "to handle, to administer."
