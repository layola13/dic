alliterations:/əˌlɪtəˈreɪʃənz/ | n. |  头韵，押头韵 | The repetition of the same sounds—usually initial consonants of words or of stressed syllables—in any sequence of neighboring words. | "Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers" is a classic example of alliteration. 彼得·派珀摘了一小撮腌辣椒是押头韵的经典例子。 | synonyms:  assonance, consonance | antonyms: N/A | noun
