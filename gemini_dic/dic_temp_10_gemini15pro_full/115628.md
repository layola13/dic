marchet: /ˈmɑːrʃeɪ/ /ˈmɑːrket/ | n. | 市场，集市 | A market, especially one held outdoors.  A marketplace. |  In medieval times, the town's main products were sold at the marchet. 在中世纪，城镇的主要产品在集市上出售。|  The marchet was bustling with activity. 集市熙熙攘攘，热闹非凡。 | synonyms: market, marketplace, bazaar, fair | antonyms: supermarket, store | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "marchet", from Late Latin "mercatum", from Latin "mercatus", past participle of "mercari" (to trade, traffic). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*merḱ-" (to exchange, trade).
