ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *nonetides*, from Old English *nōntīd*, from *nōn* (“noon”) + *tīd* (“time, tide”).  The Old English word originally referred to the ninth hour of the liturgical day (3 p.m.), but shifted to mean midday.
