chain-smoke:/ˈtʃeɪn sməʊk/ (US) /ˈtʃeɪn sməʊk/ (UK)| v. |连续抽烟，一支接一支地抽烟|To smoke cigarettes continuously, one after another.|He chain-smoked nervously throughout the meeting. 他在整个会议过程中紧张地一支接一支地抽烟。|She chain-smoked all the way through the movie. 她看电影的全程都在不停地抽烟。|近义词: smoke heavily, smoke incessantly|反义词: abstain from smoking|词性:动词

词源:  "Chain" + "smoke."  The "chain" refers to the act of lighting one cigarette from the butt of the previous one, creating a chain of cigarettes.  While this literal meaning is less common now, the term persists to describe continuous smoking.
