infantilize: /ɪnˈfæn.tɪ.laɪz/ (US) /ɪnˈfæn.taɪ.laɪz/ (UK)| vt.|幼稚化；把…当作婴儿对待|To treat (someone) as a child or in a way that denies their maturity.|He accused her of infantilizing him by constantly reminding him to take his medicine.|近义词：patronize, condescend, coddle|反义词: empower, respect, encourage|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From infant (n.) + -ize (suffix).  "Infant" comes from the Latin "infans," meaning "unable to speak," from "in-" (not) + "fari" (to speak).  The suffix "-ize" forms verbs meaning "to make or become."
