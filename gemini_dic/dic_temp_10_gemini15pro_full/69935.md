fescennine:/fɛˈsiːnaɪn/ | adj. |  粗俗的；猥亵的；放肆的 | Characterized by coarse or indecent humor or language, often involving personal or sexual innuendo; licentious; unrestrained. | The comedian's fescennine jokes offended many in the audience. | 近义词: bawdy, ribald, obscene, lewd, licentious, vulgar, indelicate, coarse, improper, indecent | 反义词: decent, proper, polite, refined, tasteful | 词性: 形容词

Etymology: From Latin *fescenninus*, relating to Fescennia (an ancient Etruscan town in Italy), where licentious verses were traditionally recited, perhaps originally to avert evil influences.  Some scholars also connect the word to *fascinum*, meaning "spell," "charm," or "phallus," suggesting a link to fertility rites.
