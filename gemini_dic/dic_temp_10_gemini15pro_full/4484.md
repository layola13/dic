albigenses:/ˌælbiˈdʒɛnsiz/ | n. | 阿尔比派教徒 | A member of a heretical Christian sect that flourished in southern France in the 12th and 13th centuries. They believed in two gods, one of good and one of evil, and rejected many Catholic doctrines.  | The Albigensian Crusade was a bloody conflict aimed at suppressing the Albigensian heresy. | 近义词: Cathar, heretic | 反义词: Catholic, orthodox | noun
