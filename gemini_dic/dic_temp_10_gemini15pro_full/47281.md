de-democratized:/diː-dɪˈmɒkrətaɪzd/
|v.|去民主化|To reverse or undo the process of democratization, often by restricting rights or freedoms previously enjoyed.|The military coup effectively de-democratized the country, stripping citizens of their voting rights.|近义词: undermine democracy, dismantle democracy,  authoritarianize|反义词: democratize, liberalize|词性:动词

de-democratized:/diː-dɪˈmɒkrətaɪzd/
|adj.|被去民主化的|Having undergone a process of de-democratization, resulting in a less democratic system.|The de-democratized nation saw a resurgence of censorship and political repression.|近义词: undemocratic, illiberal, authoritarian|反义词: democratic, liberal|词性:形容词
