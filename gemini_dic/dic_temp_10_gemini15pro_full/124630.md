mouth: /maʊθ/ (both US and UK)| n. v.|嘴，口; 开口，说（话）|The part of the face, including the lips, teeth, and tongue, through which food is taken in and sounds are made.  As a verb, it means to form words with the lips without producing sound, or to speak or say something in a particular way.|
She opened her mouth to speak. 她张开嘴说话。
He carefully mouthed the words "I love you". 他小心翼翼地用口型说出“我爱你”。|
近义词：(n.) opening, orifice, aperture; (v.) articulate, utter, pronounce|
反义词: N/A |
词性: noun, verb


Etymology:
From Old English *mūþ, from Proto-Germanic *munþaz, from Proto-Indo-European *méndʰos (“mouth, chin, jaw”). Cognate with Dutch mond (“mouth”), German Mund (“mouth”), Gothic 𐌼𐌿𐌽𐌸𐍃 (munþs, “mouth”).
