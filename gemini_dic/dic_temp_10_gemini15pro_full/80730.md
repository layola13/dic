gorse:/ɡɔːrs/| n. |金雀花，荆豆|A spiny, evergreen shrub with bright yellow flowers, common in Europe and North America.  It often grows in dense thickets and is difficult to penetrate. |The hillsides were covered in gorse, its yellow flowers vibrant against the blue sky. 山坡上覆盖着金雀花，黄色的花朵在蓝天下显得格外鲜艳。

The gorse bushes provided shelter for the nesting birds. 金雀花丛为筑巢的鸟类提供了庇护。|近义词：furze, whin|反义词: None|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *gorst, from Proto-Germanic *gursta-, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰers- (“to bristle”).  Related to Dutch gorst, German Gerste (“barley”), and possibly Latin hordeum (“barley”). The connection likely comes from the bristly or spiky nature of both the gorse plant and barley ears.
