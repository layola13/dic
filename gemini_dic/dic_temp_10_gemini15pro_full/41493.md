cornucopian:/ˌkɔːrnjəˈkoʊpiən/ | adj. n. |丰饶的，富饶的；信奉丰饶理论的人| relating to or resembling a cornucopia; denoting or relating to an optimistic view that human ingenuity will always overcome any natural limits to population and economic growth | The Thanksgiving table was a cornucopian display of food. 感恩节的餐桌上摆满了丰盛的食物。 | synonyms: abundant, plentiful, copious, lavish, profuse | antonyms: scarce, meager, sparse, deficient | adjective

cornucopian:/ˌkɔːrnjəˈkoʊpiən/ | n. |信奉丰饶理论的人| a person who believes that there is enough matter and energy on Earth to provide for the population regardless of its size | Some cornucopians argue that technological advancements will always provide solutions to resource scarcity. 一些信奉丰饶理论的人认为，技术进步将永远为资源短缺提供解决方案。 | synonyms: optimist, utopian | antonyms: pessimist, doomsayer, Malthusian | noun
