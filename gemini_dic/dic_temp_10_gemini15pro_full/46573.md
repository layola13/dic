dam:/dæm/| n. v.|水坝，障碍物，筑坝|A barrier constructed to hold back water and raise its level, forming a reservoir used to generate electricity or as a water supply.|The Hoover Dam is a massive concrete structure. 胡佛水坝是一个巨大的混凝土结构。|
|They dammed the river to create a reservoir. 他们在河上筑坝形成了一个水库。|
|synonyms: barricade, barrier, embankment, obstacle|
|antonyms: opening, passage|
|noun|

dam:/dæm/| v.|筑坝，抑制，控制|To build a dam across a river or stream.|They dammed the river to prevent flooding. 他们在河上筑坝以防止洪水泛滥。|
|He tried to dam his emotions, but tears welled up in his eyes. 他试图抑制自己的情绪，但眼泪还是涌上了眼眶。|
|synonyms: block, obstruct, restrain, contain|
|antonyms: release, unleash|
|verb|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dam*, from Middle Dutch *dam*, from Old Dutch *dam*. Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *dammaz*, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₂-*, meaning "to put, place."
