antimoralists:/ˌæntiˈmɒrəlɪsts/ /ˌæntiˈmɔːrəlɪsts/
| n. |反道德主义者；不道德者|People who oppose or reject traditional morality or moral principles.  They might believe that morality is subjective, culturally relative, or simply a tool used to control people.| Some philosophers argue that Nietzsche was an antimoralist, challenging conventional views of good and evil. 尼采被一些哲学家认为是反道德主义者，他挑战了善与恶的传统观念。|
|近义词：immoralists, libertines, nonconformists|
|反义词：moralists, ethicists|
|词性：名词|


antimoralist:/ˌæntiˈmɒrəlɪst/ /ˌæntiˈmɔːrəlɪst/
| adj. |反道德的|Opposing or rejecting traditional morality or moral principles.| The antimoralist stance of the character shocked many viewers.  这个角色的反道德立场震惊了许多观众。|
|近义词：immoral, unethical|
|反义词：moral, ethical|
