babblery: /ˈbæbləri/ | n. | 喋喋不休，胡言乱语 |  Meaningless or excessive talk; chatter.  It often implies foolish or childish talk. |  The constant babblery of the children made it difficult to concentrate. 孩子们不停的叽叽喳喳让人很难集中注意力。| synonyms: chatter, prattle, gibberish, jabber, nonsense | antonyms: silence, quiet, conciseness, brevity | noun

babble: /ˈbæbl/ | v. | 喋喋不休，胡言乱语 | To talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way. | The baby babbled happily in her crib. 婴儿在婴儿床里高兴地咿呀学语。 | synonyms: chatter, prattle, gibber, jabber | antonyms: be quiet, be silent | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bablen, from Old French babillier, of imitative origin.  Relates to the sounds of babies trying to speak.
