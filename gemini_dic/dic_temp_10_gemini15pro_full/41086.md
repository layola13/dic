coppice:/ˈkɒpɪs/ (UK), /ˈkɑːpɪs/ (US)| v.|修剪，砍伐（矮林）|To cut back (trees or shrubs) to ground level to stimulate growth.|They coppiced the hazel trees every few years. 他们每隔几年就砍伐一次榛树。|synonyms: trim, prune, pollard|antonyms: plant, cultivate|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *copeiz, from Vulgar Latin *colpare, "to cut, chop," related to Latin colaphus, "a blow with the fist."
