argean:/ɑːrˈdʒiːən/| adj.|阿耳戈斯的，希腊的|Relating to Argos, an ancient city in Greece, or to Greece in general.  Often used poetically or in a literary context to evoke a sense of ancient Greek culture or mythology. |The argean fleet sailed for Troy.  阿耳戈斯舰队驶向特洛伊。|Synonyms: Greek, Hellenic, Grecian|Antonyms: Roman, barbarian|adjective

argean:/ɑːrˈdʒiːən/| n.|阿耳戈斯人，希腊人|A person from Argos or Greece. This usage is less common than the adjectival form. |The argean welcomed the travelers.  这个阿耳戈斯人欢迎旅行者。|Synonyms: Greek, Hellene, Grecian|Antonyms: Roman, barbarian|noun
