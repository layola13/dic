centrostaltic: /ˌsɛntrəˈstɔːltɪk/ | adj. | 向心的；趋中的 |  Relating to centrostaltic movements or forces, which are directed toward the center.  Derived from "centro-" (center) + "staltic" (contracting or compressing).  Often used in the context of physiological processes like peristalsis in the digestive system, where muscles contract to move contents towards a central point. | The centrostaltic contractions of the esophagus move the food bolus towards the stomach. 食道的向心收缩将食团移向胃部。 |  The drug enhanced the centrostaltic waves in the intestines, improving digestion. 这种药物增强了肠道的向心蠕动波，改善了消化。 |  centripetal, convergent | centrifugal, divergent | adjective
