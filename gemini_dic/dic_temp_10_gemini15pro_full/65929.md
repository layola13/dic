exacerbating: /ɪɡˈzæsərbeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 加剧，恶化 | Making a problem, bad situation, or negative feeling worse. | The humid weather is exacerbating my asthma. 潮湿的天气加剧了我的哮喘。|  The stress of the situation was exacerbated by the lack of sleep.  睡眠不足加剧了情况的压力。| 近义词: aggravating, worsening, intensifying | 反义词: alleviating, mitigating, improving | 词性: Verb (present participle)

exacerbate: /ɪɡˈzæsərbeɪt/ | v. | 加剧，恶化 | To make a problem, bad situation, or negative feeling worse. |  Stress can exacerbate health problems. 压力会加剧健康问题。 | Lack of communication only exacerbated the conflict. 缺乏沟通只会加剧冲突。| 近义词: aggravate, worsen, intensify | 反义词: alleviate, mitigate, improve | 词性: Verb


词源: From Late Latin exacerbatus, past participle of exacerbare “to irritate, provoke,” from ex- “out” + acerbare “to make bitter,” from acerbus “bitter, harsh.”
