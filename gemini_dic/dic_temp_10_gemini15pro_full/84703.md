handicap:/ˈhændikæp/ (US) /ˈhændikæp/ (UK)| n. vt.|障碍，不利条件；残疾；(体育比赛等)让分，差点|A circumstance that makes progress or success difficult.  A physical or mental condition that limits a person's movements, senses, or activities.|例句：Her lack of experience is a handicap. 她缺乏经验是一个障碍。|近义词：disadvantage, impediment, obstacle, disability|反义词：advantage, benefit|词性:名词, 动词

handicap:/ˈhændikæp/ (US) /ˈhændikæp/ (UK)| vt.|妨碍，使不利|To place at a disadvantage.|例句：The injury handicapped him throughout his career.  伤病在他的整个职业生涯中都对他造成了不利影响。|近义词：hinder, impede, obstruct|反义词：aid, assist|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From hand in cap, an old game of chance where forfeits were held in a cap.  Later, it evolved to refer to a system of equalizing chances in horse races, and eventually broadened to its current meaning.
