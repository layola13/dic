chronologically: /ˌkrɑːnəˈlɑːdʒɪkli/ | adv. | 按时间顺序地 | In a way that follows the order in which a series of events happened. | The events are described chronologically in the book. 这本书按时间顺序描述了这些事件。| The museum displays artifacts chronologically, from ancient times to the present day.  博物馆按时间顺序陈列文物，从古代到现代。|  Synonyms: sequentially, consecutively | Antonyms: randomly, haphazardly | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *chronologicālis, from Ancient Greek χρόνος (khrónos, “time”) + -λογικ- (-logik-, from λόγος (lógos, “word, reason”)).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱer- (“time, year”).
