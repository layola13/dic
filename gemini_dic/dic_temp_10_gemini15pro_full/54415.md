disinclined:/ˌdɪsɪnˈklaɪnd/ | adj. | 不情愿的；不愿的 | Not wanting to do something; averse. |  I'm disinclined to go out tonight. 我今晚不想出去。 |  He seemed disinclined to offer any help. 他似乎不愿意提供任何帮助。 | reluctant, unwilling, averse, loath | willing, eager, keen | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + inclined (disposed to do something).  The word "inclined" itself comes from the Latin "inclinare," meaning "to bend or lean towards."
