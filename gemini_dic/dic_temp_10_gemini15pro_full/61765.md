empathize:/ˈɛmpəθaɪz/| v. |同情，共情，感同身受 | To understand and share the feelings of another. | I can empathize with your frustration because I've been in a similar situation. 我能理解你的沮丧，因为我也有过类似的经历。

| Example: She empathized with the refugees and offered them shelter. 她同情难民，并为他们提供住所。
| Synonym:  understand, sympathize, relate, share, commiserate
| Antonym:  disregard, ignore, detach, apathetic
| Word type: verb

empathetic:/ˌɛmpəˈθɛtɪk/| adj. |有同情心的，表示同情的 | Showing an ability to understand and share the feelings of another. | She offered an empathetic ear to her friend's troubles.  她对朋友的烦恼表示同情并认真倾听。

| Example: He gave her an empathetic look, understanding her pain. 他同情地看着她，理解她的痛苦。
| Synonym: compassionate, understanding, sympathetic, sensitive
| Antonym: unsympathetic, callous, indifferent, apathetic
| Word type: adjective

empathy:/ˈɛmpəθi/| n. |同情，共情，同感 | The ability to understand and share the feelings of another. |  She showed great empathy for the victims of the earthquake. 她对地震灾民表现出极大的同情。

| Example:  It's important to have empathy for others, even if you don't agree with them.  即使你不同意别人的观点，也要对他们有同理心。
| Synonym: compassion, understanding, sympathy, rapport
| Antonym: apathy, indifference, coldness, detachment
| Word type: noun

Etymology: From Greek empatheia, from em- 'in' + pathos 'feeling.'
