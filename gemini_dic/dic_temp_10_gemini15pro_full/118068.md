mendiant:/mɑ̃.djɑ̃/ | n. | 乞丐；（尤指）托钵僧；（法式）巧克力小点心 | A beggar, especially a mendicant friar; a type of French confection consisting of a chocolate disk studded with nuts and dried fruits. | The mendiant held out his bowl for alms. 托钵僧伸出碗来接受施舍。 |She offered him a mendiant, a dark chocolate disc studded with hazelnuts and dried apricots. 她给了他一块缀有榛子和杏干的黑巧克力小点心。| Synonym: beggar, supplicant, pauper (for person); confection, sweet (for food) | Antonym: philanthropist, benefactor (for person) | noun


mendiant:/ˈmɛndɪənt/ | adj. | 乞讨的；托钵的 | Begging; practicing mendicancy. | The mendiant friar traveled from town to town, relying on the charity of others. 托钵修士辗转于各个城镇，依靠别人的施舍生活。 | The mendiant orders faced increasing scrutiny from the authorities. 乞讨的修会面临着当局越来越严格的审查。 | Synonym: begging, mendicant | Antonym: wealthy, affluent, prosperous | adjective
