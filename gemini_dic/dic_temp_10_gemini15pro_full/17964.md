before-the-fact:/bɪˈfɔːr ðə fækt/ | adj. | 事前的，预先的 | Existing or occurring before an event, especially a crime. |  The before-the-fact information was crucial to preventing the robbery. 事前的情报对于阻止抢劫至关重要。|  Example: The police were able to stop the crime because they had before-the-fact knowledge of the plan.  警方能够阻止犯罪，因为他们事先知道了这个计划。| Synonym:  prior, anticipatory, preliminary | Antonym: after-the-fact, subsequent, ex post facto | adjective
