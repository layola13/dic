alcornoque: /alkɔrˈnɔkeɪ/ (Spanish pronunciation)
| n. | 软木橡树；（引申）傻瓜，笨蛋 |  Cork oak (Quercus suber), the tree from which cork is obtained. Figuratively, it refers to a blockhead, a stupid or foolish person. |  El alcornoque es un árbol muy importante en España. (The cork oak is a very important tree in Spain.) /  ¡No seas alcornoque! (Don't be a fool!) |  Synonym: (for tree) cork oak; (for person) idiot, fool, dunce | Antonym: (for person) genius, wise person | Noun
