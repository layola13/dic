braveheart: /ˈbreɪvˌhɑːrt/ | n. | 勇敢的心，勇士 | A person with great courage and determination, often used in a historical or legendary context. |  William Wallace was a true braveheart, leading his people against English oppression. 威廉·华莱士是一位真正的勇士，带领他的人民反抗英国的压迫。

He showed the braveheart of a lion in the face of danger. 面对危险，他表现出了狮子的勇敢。 | 近义词: hero, warrior, champion | 反义词: coward, craven | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->

From the combination of the English words "brave" (meaning courageous) and "heart" (referring to the seat of emotions and courage).
