nonpropitiative: /ˌnɒnprəˈpɪʃɪəˌtɪv/ | adj. | 不讨好的，不抚慰的，不能平息的 | Not intended to propitiate or appease; not conciliatory. |  His nonpropitiative stance only angered the crowd further. 他的不讨好的姿态进一步激怒了人群。 | The dictator's nonpropitiative response to the protestors' demands led to further unrest.  独裁者对抗议者要求的不妥协回应导致了进一步的动荡。| 近义词:unappeasing,  intransigent, unyielding | 反义词:propitiatory, conciliatory, appeasing | 词性:形容词

Etymology:
From non- + propitiate + -ive.  "Propitiate" comes from Latin propitiare "make favorable, appease," from propitius "favorable, gracious, kind," from pro- "before, forth" + *pet- "to fall, fly" (source also of Sanskrit patati "flies, falls," Greek piptein "to fall").
