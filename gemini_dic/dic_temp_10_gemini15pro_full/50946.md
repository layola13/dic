designated:/ˈdɛzɪɡˌneɪtɪd/ | adj. |指定的，指派的|Chosen or appointed for a particular purpose or role. | The designated driver is responsible for getting everyone home safely. 指定的司机负责安全地把每个人送回家。|  近义词：appointed, assigned, selected, chosen, nominated | 反义词：undesignated, unappointed, random | 词性: 形容词

designated:/ˈdɛzɪɡˌneɪtɪd/ | v. |指定，指派 (过去式 designated, 过去分词 designated, 现在分词 designating, 第三人称单数 designates) | To officially choose or appoint someone or something for a particular purpose or role. | The city council designated the building a historical landmark. 市议会指定该建筑物为历史地标。 | 近义词：appoint, assign, select, choose, nominate | 反义词：unappoint | 词性: 动词


词源:  From Latin *designatus*, past participle of designare (“to mark out, indicate”), from de- (“down, from”) + signare (“to mark”), from signum (“mark, sign”).
