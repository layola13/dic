charioteer:/ˌtʃɛriəˈtɪr/ | n. |战车驾驶员；御者|A person who drives a chariot, a two-wheeled horse-drawn vehicle used in ancient warfare and racing.|The charioteer skillfully maneuvered his horses around the tight turns of the track.|driver, coachman|passenger|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *charretier, from charrete ("cart") + -ier (agent suffix). Ultimately from Latin carrus ("cart").
