paraconid:/pærəˈkoʊnɪd/| n. |下内尖|A cusp situated on the lingual side of a lower molar tooth of a mammal, mesial to the protoconid.  It is a characteristic feature of tribosphenic molars, found in many mammals, including early primates. |The paraconid is a key feature used in identifying fossil teeth.|近义词:cusp (in a broader sense)|反义词:N/A| noun
