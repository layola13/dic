lionised:/ˈlaɪənaɪzd/| v.|被捧为名人的，被奉为偶像的|To treat (someone) as a celebrity.|例句：The young author was lionised after the success of her first novel.  这位年轻作家在她的第一部小说获得成功后被捧为名人。|例句：He was lionised by the media and the public. 他被媒体和公众奉为偶像。|近义词：celebrated, acclaimed, lauded, exalted, glorified|反义词：vilified, condemned, criticized, ignored|词性:动词

词源: From lion + -ize.  The verb originates from the practice of exhibiting lions as objects of curiosity and wonder.
