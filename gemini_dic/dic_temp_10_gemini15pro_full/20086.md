bioscientist: /ˈbaɪoʊˌsaɪəntɪst/ (US) /ˈbaɪəʊˌsaɪəntɪst/ (UK)| n. |生物科学家|A scientist who studies living organisms.  A specialist in bioscience.| Example: The bioscientist dedicated her life to researching cures for diseases.  这位生物科学家毕生致力于研究疾病的治疗方法。|Example:  Bioscientists are working to develop new biofuels. 生物科学家正在努力开发新的生物燃料。|近义词: biologist, life scientist|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


Etymology:
bio- + scientist

bio-: from Greek bios "life"
scientist: from science + -ist

science: from Old French science "knowledge, learning, application," from Latin scientia "knowledge," from sciens, present participle of scire "to know," probably originally "to separate one thing from another, to distinguish," related to scindere "to cut, divide"
-ist: a suffix forming agent nouns or nouns denoting a person who practices or is concerned with something, or holds certain principles, doctrines, etc.
