ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "cleanse," which comes from Middle English "clensen," from Old English "clǣnsian," meaning "to make clean." Ultimately derived from the Proto-Germanic "*klainison," related to Old High German "klenzen" and Old Norse "kleinsa." The "-er" suffix denotes an agent or instrument that performs the action of the verb.
