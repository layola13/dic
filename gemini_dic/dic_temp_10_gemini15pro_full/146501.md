overgaze: /ˈoʊvərɡeɪz/
n.
凝视，长时间注视
The act of looking at someone for an extended period, often considered intrusive or dominant.
She felt uncomfortable under his intense overgaze. 她在他强烈的凝视下感到不舒服。

例句：
The child's overgaze made the speaker nervous.  孩子的凝视让演讲者感到紧张。

The overgaze of the security cameras was unsettling.  安全摄像头的凝视令人不安。


近义词：stare, gaze, fixed look
反义词：glance, quick look


词性：名词 (n.)

词源:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much") + "gaze" (noun meaning "a steady intent look").  From Old English *gēan (to yawn, gape), related to Dutch geeuwen (to yawn) and German gähnen (to yawn).  The sense of looking intently developed later.
