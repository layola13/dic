counterchange:/ˈkaʊntərˌtʃeɪndʒ/ | n. v. | 对换，交替，棋盘状 | An interchange of elements, especially the alternation of colours in a pattern, as in heraldry. | The counterchange of light and shadow created a dramatic effect. 光影交错营造出戏剧性的效果。|  alternation, interchange, exchange |  monotony, uniformity | noun, verb

counterchange:/ˈkaʊntərˌtʃeɪndʒ/ |  v. | 对换，交替 | To vary by interchanging, as colours. | The artist counterchanged the colours in the painting to create a sense of balance.  艺术家交换了画作中的颜色，以营造一种平衡感。| alternate, interchange, exchange | maintain, preserve | verb

From Old French *contrechanger, from contre- "against" + changer "to change".
