extrapolable:/ɪkˈstræpələbl/| adj.|可外推的，可推断的|Able to be extrapolated.  This means that information or data from a known situation can be used to make predictions or estimates about an unknown situation, often one that is outside the range of the original data.|The results of the small study are extrapolable to larger populations.  这项小型研究的结果可以推断到更大的人群。|近义词：predictable, projectable, inferable|反义词: non-extrapolatable|词性:形容词
词源: From extrapolate + -able. Extrapolate comes from Latin extra- 'outside' + pōlāre 'to polish, smooth'.
