lithotomy: /lɪˈθɒtəmi/ (US) /lɪˈθɒtəmi/ (UK)| n.|切石术；膀胱切开术；截石位|The surgical incision of the bladder for removal of stones; the position of a patient lying on their back with the hips and knees flexed and the thighs abducted and externally rotated, used for gynaecological and urological examinations and procedures. |The patient was placed in the lithotomy position for the surgery.|Synonyms: cystotomy (for bladder incision), dorsal lithotomy position|Antonyms: None (for the surgical procedure);  None specific for the position, perhaps simply "standing" or "prone" could be considered broadly opposite. |noun
