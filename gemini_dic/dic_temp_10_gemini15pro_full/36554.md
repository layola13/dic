collapse:/kəˈlæps/| v. n.|倒塌，崩溃，瓦解|To fall down suddenly because of pressure or having no strength or support.  The roof collapsed under the weight of the snow. 屋顶在雪的重压下倒塌了。|例句：The building collapsed after the earthquake.  大楼在地震后倒塌了。

例句：His business collapsed after the economic downturn. 他的生意在经济衰退后倒闭了。|近义词：crumble, fall, fail, give way|反义词:build, construct, rise, strengthen|词性：动词/名词

collapse:/kəˈlæps/| n.|倒塌，崩溃|The sudden failure of something, such as an institution or a plan. The collapse of the Soviet Union. 苏联的解体。|例句：The collapse of the bridge caused major traffic delays. 桥梁的倒塌造成了严重的交通延误。

例句：She suffered a nervous collapse after the accident. 事故发生后，她精神崩溃了。|近义词：breakdown, failure, downfall|反义词:success, recovery, strengthening|词性：名词
