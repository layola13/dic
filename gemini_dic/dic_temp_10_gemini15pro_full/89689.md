ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *houten*, from Old English *hōtan* (“to threaten, shout, hoot”), from Proto-Germanic *hōtaną (“to threaten, call, shout”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱewə- (“to cry, shout”). Cognate with Scots *hoot* (“to shout”), West Frisian *húte* (“to hoot”), Dutch *hoeten* (“to hoot”), German *hüten* (“to guard, watch over, protect, tend”), Old Norse *heyja* (“to hoot”). Related to *hew*.
