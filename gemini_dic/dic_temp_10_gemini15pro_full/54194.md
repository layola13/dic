disesteem:/ˌdɪsɪˈstiːm/
n.|轻视，蔑视|lack of esteem; low regard.|He felt disesteem for those who betrayed their principles. 他蔑视那些背叛自己原则的人。|
近义词:contempt, scorn, disdain|
反义词:esteem, respect, admiration|
词性:noun

disesteem:/ˌdɪsɪˈstiːm/
vt.|轻视，不尊重|to regard with disrespect or contempt.|She disesteems anyone who puts money before honesty. 她轻视任何把金钱置于诚信之上的人。|
近义词:despise, scorn, undervalue|
反义词:esteem, respect, value|
词性:verb

词源：From dis- + esteem.  "Dis-" is a prefix indicating negation or reversal. "Esteem" comes from Old French "estimer" (to estimate, appraise), ultimately from Latin "aestimare" (to value).  Therefore, "disesteem" literally means "to not value."
