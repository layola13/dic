oral-histories:/ˈɔːrəl ˈhɪstəriz/| n.|口述历史|Accounts of the past based on spoken interviews with individuals who experienced or witnessed historical events. These narratives are typically recorded, transcribed, and then analyzed or preserved.|The library has a rich collection of oral-histories documenting the experiences of World War II veterans.|Synonyms: spoken history, recorded memories, testimonials|Antonyms: written history, official documents|noun
