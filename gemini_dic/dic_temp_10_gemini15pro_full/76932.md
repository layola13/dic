gaped:/ɡeɪpt/| v.|张口结舌地看；目瞪口呆；裂开；豁开|To stare with an open mouth, typically in amazement or wonder; to become wide open.|He gaped at the astonishing sight. 他对这惊人的景象目瞪口呆。|Synonyms: stared, gawked, gazed, ogled|Antonyms: ignored, overlooked|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English gapen, from Old Norse gapa (“to gape, yawn”), from Proto-Germanic *gapaną (“to gape, open wide”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰébʰ- (“to gape, yawn”). Cognate with Scots gape (“to gape”), West Frisian geape (“to gape”), Dutch gapen (“to yawn, gape”), German gaffen (“to gape, stare”), Danish gabe (“to gape, yawn”), Swedish gapa (“to gape, yell”).
