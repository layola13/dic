co-optation:/ˌkoʊˌɑːpˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 收编；拉拢；纳入 | The process of adding members to a group, especially to a governing body, by the invitation of existing members rather than by election or appointment by an outside authority.  This can sometimes be seen as a way to neutralize opposition or to maintain control. | The co-optation of several prominent environmentalists onto the board of the oil company was seen as a cynical move.  将几位著名的环保人士纳入石油公司董事会，被认为是一种玩世不恭的举动。 | 近义词: absorption, assimilation, incorporation, inclusion | 反义词: exclusion, rejection | 词性: noun

co-opt:/ˌkoʊˈɑːpt/ | vt. | 收编；拉拢；指定 | To appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members. To adopt (an idea or policy) for one's own use, especially to appeal to a wider group. | The rebels co-opted several members of the former government into their new administration. 叛军将前政府的几名成员拉拢到他们的新政府中。 The advertising campaign co-opted the language of social justice to sell its products.  广告活动借用了社会正义的语言来销售其产品。 | 近义词: appropriate, borrow, enlist, recruit | 反义词: reject, exclude | 词性: verb
