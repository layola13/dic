dismantle:/dɪsˈmæntl/ /dɪsˈmɑːntl/| vt.|拆除，拆卸|to take apart a machine or structure so that it is in separate pieces|The workers dismantled the old bridge. 工人们拆除了旧桥。|synonyms: disassemble, take apart, break down|antonyms: assemble, construct, build|verb

dismantle:/dɪsˈmæntl/ /dɪsˈmɑːntl/| vt.|解散，废除|to end an organization or system gradually in an organized way|The government is dismantling the state-owned industries. 政府正在逐步废除国有企业。|synonyms: abolish, dissolve, disband|antonyms: establish, found, create|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desmanteler, from des- "apart" + manteler "to cloak, cover."  The original sense was "to strip of a mantle or cloak."  This evolved metaphorically to mean "to strip of equipment or defenses," and then to the general sense of "to take apart."
