convulsibilities:/kənˌvʌlsəˈbɪlɪtiz/| n. |抽搐，痉挛，易激动|The state or quality of being convulsible; a tendency to convulsions; proneness to violent disturbance or agitation.|His convulsibilities were a symptom of the underlying neurological disorder.|近义词：spasms, fits, seizures, paroxysms, contractions|反义词:stability, calmness, composure|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *convulsibilitās, from Latin convulsibilis (“subject to convulsions”), from convulsus, perfect passive participle of convellō (“I convulse, shake violently”), from con- (“together, completely”) + vellō (“I pluck, pull, tear”).
