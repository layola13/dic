ludditism:/ˈlʌdaɪtɪzəm/
n.|卢德主义; 机器破坏运动|Opposition to new technology, especially automation, often manifested in the destruction of machinery.  It's derived from the Luddites, textile workers who protested against newly developed labour-economizing machinery from 1811 to 1817.|The factory owner accused the disgruntled workers of ludditism after they sabotaged the new automated assembly line.|Near synonyms:  technophobia, anti-technologism,  resistance to innovation|Near antonyms:  technophilia,  innovation,  modernization
n.|反对技术进步; 反对自动化|
