cabining:/ˈkæbɪnɪŋ/
词性:n.
中文翻译:居住在小屋里；限制；禁闭
英文解析:The act of living in a cabin, or the state of being confined to a small space.  Often implies a sense of rusticity or isolation.
例句:
He enjoyed the quiet solitude of cabining in the woods. 他享受在树林里小屋居住的宁静独处。

Their cabining experience allowed them to connect with nature. 他们的木屋生活让他们与大自然联系在一起。

近义词:rusticating, secluding, confining
反义词:urbanizing, socializing

词性:v.
中文翻译:住在小屋里；限制；禁闭
英文解析:(Present participle of 'cabin')  Confining or restricting someone or something to a small space.
例句:
The long winter was cabining them indoors. 漫长的冬天把他们限制在室内。

The illness was cabining her to her bed. 疾病把她困在床上。

近义词:confining, restricting, imprisoning
反义词:liberating, freeing, releasing


词源:From the noun "cabin" + "-ing" suffix, indicating the act or process of.  "Cabin" ultimately derives from the Old French "cabine" (small room, hut).
