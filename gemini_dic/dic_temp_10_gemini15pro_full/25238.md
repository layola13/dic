buenos:/ˈbwe.nos/| adj.|好的，美好的|Good; a Spanish word often used as a greeting or part of a phrase.  It's the plural masculine form of "bueno".  Used to describe masculine plural nouns.|例句：¡Buenos días! Good morning!|近义词：good, excellent, fine (English), bueno (singular masculine Spanish)|反义词:malo (Spanish), bad (English)|adjective

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Los días son buenos. = The days are good.
Mis amigos son buenos. = My friends are good.

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Not applicable as "buenos" is an adjective and doesn't conjugate in a continuous tense.


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):

Los resultados han sido buenos. = The results have been good.


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
Not applicable.

虚拟语气:
Si los resultados fueran buenos, estaríamos contentos. = If the results were good, we would be happy.
Ojalá los días fueran buenos. = I wish the days were good.


被动语态:
Not applicable.  "Buenos" is an adjective and cannot be used in the passive voice.


疑问句:
¿Son buenos los resultados? = Are the results good?
¿Fueron buenos los días? = Were the days good?


否定句:
Los resultados no son buenos. = The results are not good.
Los días no fueron buenos. = The days were not good.



PHRASE->
Buenos días = Good morning/Good day
Buenos tardes = Good afternoon/Good evening
Buenas noches = Good night/Good evening
¡Buenos! (informal) = Hey there! / Hi!
Buenos Aires =  (Proper noun) Buenos Aires (city)


注意事项:

"Buenos" is the masculine plural form.  If referring to a singular masculine noun, use "bueno". If referring to a feminine noun, use "buena" (singular) or "buenas" (plural).

While "buenos" itself doesn't conjugate, the verbs used with it will change depending on the tense.
