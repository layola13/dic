newfangled:/ˈnjuːˈfæŋɡəld/ (US) /ˌnjuːˈfæŋɡld/ (UK)| adj.|新奇的；新式的；最新款的；新式的（常含贬义）|Of a new kind or fashion, especially one perceived as being unnecessarily modern. Often used disapprovingly to suggest something is overly complicated or impractical.|例句：My grandmother doesn't trust these newfangled gadgets. 我的祖母不相信这些新奇的小玩意儿。

例句：He prefers his old camera to the newfangled digital ones. 他喜欢他的旧相机，不喜欢那些新式的数码相机。

|近义词：modern, contemporary, innovative, latest, state-of-the-art|反义词：old-fashioned, traditional, outdated, antiquated, obsolete|词性: adjective
