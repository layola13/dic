|v. |钻孔的，训练有素的|pierced or made by drilling; having been trained thoroughly, especially by repetitive practice.|The workers drilled a hole in the wall. 工人们在墙上钻了一个孔。|bore, pierce, penetrate|fill|verb
drilled:/drɪld/
|adj. |训练有素的|trained thoroughly, especially by repetitive practice.|The soldiers were well-drilled and disciplined. 士兵们训练有素，纪律严明。|trained, disciplined, practiced|untrained, undisciplined|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Low German *drillen, frequentative of *drīven  "to drive," from Proto-Germanic *dreibaną ("to drive, push"). Related to Old English drīfan (“to drive, expel, chase”).  The sense of "to bore a hole" emerged in the 15th century.
