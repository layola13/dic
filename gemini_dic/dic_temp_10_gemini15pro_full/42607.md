counterquestioned:/ˌkaʊntərˈkwɛstʃənd/ | v. | 反问，提出相反的问题 |  To respond to a question with another question, often to challenge or evade the original inquiry. |  The lawyer counterquestioned the witness, trying to expose inconsistencies in his testimony. 律师反问证人，试图揭露他证词中的矛盾之处。 |  challenge, query, interrogate | answer, respond | verb
counterquestion:/ˌkaʊntərˈkwɛstʃən/ | n. | 反问 | A question posed in response to another question, often to challenge or evade the original inquiry. | The witness's counterquestion caught the lawyer off guard.  证人的反问让律师措手不及。| challenge, query, interrogation | answer, response | noun

Etymology:
From counter- + question.  "Counter-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "in opposition to," derived from the Old French "contre" and ultimately from the Latin "contra."
