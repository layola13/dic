egregiously: /ɪˈɡriːdʒəsli/ /ɪˈɡriːdʒiəsli/ | adv. | 惊人地；过分地；极其恶劣地 |  In a conspicuously bad or offensive way.  Markedly bad; shocking. |  He was egregiously wrong in his assessment. 他对情况的评估错得离谱。|  flagrantly, outrageously, shockingly, appallingly | slightly, mildly | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *egregius (“distinguished, excellent”), from ex (“out of, from”) + grex, gregis (“flock, herd”).  Originally meant "standing out from the herd," but over time the meaning shifted to "standing out in a bad way."
