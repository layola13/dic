caustically:/ˈkɔːstɪkli/| adv.| 尖刻地，刻薄地 |Sarcastically or bitingly, with the intent to harm or injure.  It refers to a manner of speaking or writing that is sharp, bitter, and critical, often with the intention of mocking or wounding someone.| He spoke caustically about his former employer. 他尖刻地谈论他的前任雇主。 | 近义词: acrimoniously, bitingly, sarcastically, mordantly, vitriolically | 反义词: kindly, gently, politely, respectfully | 词性: 副词

caustic:/ˈkɔːstɪk/ | adj. | 腐蚀性的；尖刻的 | 1. Able to burn or corrode organic tissue by chemical action. 2. Sarcastic in a scathing and bitter way. |1.  The chemical was so caustic that it burned through the protective gloves.  这种化学物质腐蚀性很强，甚至烧穿了防护手套。 2.  Her caustic remarks about his performance stung him deeply. 她对他表现的尖刻评论深深地刺痛了他。|近义词: (for 1) corrosive, acidic, burning; (for 2) acrimonious, biting, sarcastic, mordant, vitriolic | 反义词: (for 1) mild, harmless; (for 2) kind, gentle, polite, respectful |词性: 形容词

词源: From the Late Latin causticus, from Greek kaustikos ‘burning,’ from kaiein ‘to burn.’
