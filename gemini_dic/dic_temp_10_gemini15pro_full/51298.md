detachments:/dɪˈtætʃmənts/
n.
分离, 分遣队, 超然
the state of being objective or aloof.  A group of people, especially soldiers, who are sent away from the main group for a special task. The action or process of detaching something.
例句：The detachment of the soldiers was ordered to secure the bridge.  这支分遣队奉命保护桥梁。

例句：She observed the events with a sense of detachment. 她以一种超然的态度观察着这些事件。

近义词：separation, disconnection, objectivity, impartiality, indifference, aloofness, unit, contingent, squad
反义词：attachment, connection, involvement, bias, partiality

词性: 名词 (plural)


词源: Late Middle English: from Old French detachement, from detacher, based on Latin de- ‘away’ + French attacher ‘attach’.
