bolshie:/ˈbɒlʃi/ (UK) /ˈboʊlʃi/ (US)| adj. |不服从的，反抗的，难对付的 |Rebellious, uncooperative, difficult to manage. Derived from "Bolshevik," a member of the majority faction of the Russian Social Democratic Party, which seized power in the October Revolution of 1917.  Over time, the term broadened to describe anyone perceived as rebellious or difficult. |例句：The bolshie teenager refused to do his chores.  不服从的少年拒绝做家务。

例句：He's got a bit bolshie lately, questioning everything I say. 他最近有点不服管教，质疑我说的每件事。|近义词：rebellious, defiant, recalcitrant, insubordinate, awkward |反义词:obedient, compliant, docile, cooperative|词性:形容词
