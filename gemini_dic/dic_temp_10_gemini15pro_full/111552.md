lobby:/ˈlɑːbi/ (US) /ˈlɒbi/ (UK)| n. v.|大厅，休息室；游说，疏通|A large hall or waiting area inside the entrance of a building, such as a hotel or theater.  To try to persuade officials to support or reject a cause.|例句：The hotel lobby was grand and impressive.  酒店大堂富丽堂皇，令人印象深刻。|近义词：foyer, entrance hall, antechamber,  to persuade, influence, pressure|反义词：corridor, backroom|词性: 名词, 动词
