abolitionist: /ˌæbəˈlɪʃənɪst/
n.
废奴主义者
A person who advocates for the abolition of a practice or institution, especially slavery.
Frederick Douglass was a prominent abolitionist.  弗雷德里克·道格拉斯是一位著名的废奴主义者。

John Brown, a radical abolitionist, believed in using violence to end slavery.  约翰·布朗，一位激进的废奴主义者，相信使用暴力来结束奴隶制。

近义词: reformer, activist, advocate
反义词:pro-slavery advocate, slaveholder
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Late Latin abolitiōn- (stem of abolitiō ), from abolēre  "to abolish,"  from ab- "away, from" + -olēre, related to adolēscere "to grow up."  The -ist suffix denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system.
