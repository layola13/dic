cerealia: /sɪriˈeɪliə/ /sɪrɪˈælɪə/ | n. | 谷物，谷类食品 |  From Latin *cereālia, neuter plural of cereālis (“of Ceres”), from Ceres, the Roman goddess of agriculture. Refers to edible grains considered gifts from this goddess. | The Roman Empire relied heavily on cerealia for its staple diet. 罗马帝国的主要食物是谷物。| Cerealia formed the basis of many ancient civilizations' diets. 谷物是许多古代文明饮食的基础。| synonyms: grains, cereals | antonyms: meat, vegetables | noun
