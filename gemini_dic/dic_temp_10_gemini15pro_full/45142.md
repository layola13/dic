cuddliest:/ˈkʌdliɪst/| adj.|最令人想拥抱的，最可爱的|Superlative form of cuddly: most cuddly.  Meaning extremely appealing for cuddling, soft, and comforting.|That kitten is the cuddliest thing I've ever seen. 那只小猫是我见过的最可爱的。|The cuddliest teddy bear in the shop was quickly bought by a little girl.  商店里最惹人想拥抱的泰迪熊很快就被一个小女孩买走了。|近义词：adorable, huggable, sweet, charming|反义词：unappealing, repulsive, unpleasant|词性:形容词

cuddle:/ˈkʌdl/| v. n.|拥抱，依偎|To hold close in one's arms, especially to show affection.|She cuddled the baby close. 她紧紧地抱着婴儿。|They cuddled up together on the sofa. 他们依偎在沙发上。|近义词：hug, snuggle, embrace|反义词：reject, push away|词性:动词/名词

-ly:/-li/| suffix.|（构成形容词或副词）…地，…般地|Forms adjectives and adverbs, often indicating manner or characteristic.  Added to "cuddle" to form "cuddly," meaning suitable for or inviting cuddling.|It was a friendly dog with soft, cuddly fur.  这是一只友善的狗，有着柔软可爱的毛。|The blanket was warm and cuddly.  毯子温暖舒适。|近义词：N/A (suffix)|反义词：N/A (suffix)|词性:后缀

词源:

"Cuddle" comes from the 16th-century word "cuddle," meaning "to lie close," which may be related to the Middle Dutch word "kudden," meaning "to press."  The "-ly" suffix is a common way to form adjectives in English.
