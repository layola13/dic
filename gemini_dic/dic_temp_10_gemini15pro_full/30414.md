central:/ˈsɛntrəl/ (US), /ˈsentrəl/ (UK)| adj.|中心的，中央的，重要的，主要的|Located in the middle or at the heart of something; of primary importance.|例句：The central idea of the book is forgiveness. 这本书的中心思想是宽恕。|近义词：middle, chief, main, principal, key, core|反义词:peripheral, outer, external|词性:adjective

central:/ˈsɛntrəl/ (US), /ˈsentrəl/ (UK)| n.|中心，中央，电话总机|The middle point or part; a telephone exchange.|例句：The town square is the central hub of activity. 城镇广场是活动的中心枢纽。|近义词：center, middle, core|反义词:periphery, edge|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *central*, from Late Latin *centralis*, from Latin *centrum* ("center"), from Ancient Greek *κέντρον* (*kéntron*, "center point, stationary point of a pair of compasses, sting").
