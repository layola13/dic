moralised:/ˈmɒrəlaɪzd/ /ˈmɔːrəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 说教的，道德化的 | Having or showing a tendency to moralize; excessively concerned with morals.  |  He gave a rather moralized lecture on the dangers of drinking. 他做了一个关于饮酒危害的说教式演讲。 | 近义词: preachy, sanctimonious, didactic | 反义词: amoral, non-moral | 词性: 形容词

moralised:/ˈmɒrəlaɪzd/ /ˈmɔːrəlaɪzd/ | v. | 说教，进行道德说教 (过去式/过去分词) | To make moral judgments, especially in a self-righteous or tedious way. | He moralized about the decline of family values. 他对家庭价值观的衰落进行了道德说教。 | 近义词: preach, sermonize, lecture | 反义词: -- | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From moralize + -ed.  "Moralize" comes from the French "moraliser," and ultimately from the Latin "moralis," meaning "concerning character or customs."
