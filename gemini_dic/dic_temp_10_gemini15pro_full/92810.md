iconoclast: /aɪˈkɒnəˌklæst/ (US) /aɪˈkɑːnəklæst/ (UK)| n.| 破坏偶像者；攻击传统观念的人|A person who attacks cherished beliefs or institutions.|Martin Luther was considered an iconoclast for challenging the authority of the Catholic Church. 马丁·路德因挑战天主教会的权威而被认为是反偶像崇拜者。|synonyms: rebel, radical, nonconformist, dissident, heretic|antonyms: conformist, traditionalist|noun

iconoclastic: /aɪˌkɒnəˈklæstɪk/ (US) /aɪˌkɑːnəˈklæstɪk/ (UK)| adj.| 破坏偶像的；攻击传统观念的|Characterized by attack on cherished beliefs or institutions.|Her iconoclastic views on education often put her at odds with the school board. 她对教育的反传统观点经常使她与学校董事会发生冲突。|synonyms: rebellious, radical, nonconformist, dissident, heretical|antonyms: conformist, traditional, orthodox|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *eikonoklastes*  meaning “image-breaker,” from *eikon* (“image”) + *klan* (“to break”).  The original iconoclasts were those who opposed the veneration of religious images in the Byzantine Empire.
