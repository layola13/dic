insinuative:/ɪnˈsɪnjuːeɪtɪv/| adj.|暗示的，含沙射影的，迂回的|Suggesting or implying something bad or unpleasant in an indirect way.|She made some insinuative remarks about his past behavior. 她对他过去的行为做了一些含沙射影的评论。|近义词：suggestive, allusive, implying, hinting, intimate|反义词：direct, forthright, explicit|词性:adjective
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *insinuativus*, from Latin insinuare (“to introduce by windings and turnings, to wind or worm one's way into”), from in- (“in”) + sinuare (“to curve, bend”).
