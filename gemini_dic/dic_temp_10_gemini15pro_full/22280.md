boneshaw:/ˈboʊnˌʃɔː/
n.
骨锯; 骨刀
A saw used for cutting bone, especially in surgery or amputation.
The surgeon used a boneshaw to cleanly sever the limb.  外科医生用骨锯干净地切断了肢体。
近义词: bone saw, osteotome
反义词: N/A
词性: noun


Etymology:
bone (n.) Old English ban "bone," from Proto-Germanic *bainam (source also of Old Norse bein, Old Frisian ben, Old Saxon, Dutch been, Old High German bein, German Bein, Gothic bain), from PIE root *ost- "bone."
shaw (n.) from Old English sceaga "small wood, thicket, copse," of uncertain origin. Later, "something projecting," hence "part of a tool that projects from the handle."
