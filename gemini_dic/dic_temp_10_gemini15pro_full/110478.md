lilts:/lɪlts/| n. v.|轻快的曲调，轻快的节奏，轻快的语调；轻快地唱，轻快地说|A lilting song is one that has a pleasant, gentle rhythm that rises and falls.  A lilting voice is one that is light and pleasant to listen to.|She sang a lilting Irish melody. 她唱了一首轻快的爱尔兰民歌。|Synonyms:  trill, warble, carol, croon|Antonyms: drone, monotone|n. v.

lilts:/lɪlts/| v.|轻快地唱，轻快地说|To lilt means to speak or sing with a pleasant, rising and falling rhythm.|Her voice lilted with happiness as she told him the good news.  她告诉他这个好消息时，她的声音因快乐而轻快.|Synonyms:  trill, warble, carol, croon|Antonyms: drone, monotone|v.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lilten, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Old Norse ljoða (“to sing, chant”).  First recorded usage c. 1500.
