词源：Old English *gemædd "out of one's mind," from Proto-Germanic *ga-maid-jan "to injure, disable" (source also of Old Norse meidhr "injured, maimed," Gothic gamaidjans "crippled").  The meaning shifted from "mentally disabled" to "insane" to "enraged."
