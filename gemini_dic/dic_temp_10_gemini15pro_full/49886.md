demurely:/dɪˈmjʊərli/ | adv. | 害羞地，娴静地 | In a reserved or shy manner, often in a way that is intended to appear attractive. | She smiled demurely and lowered her eyes. 她害羞地笑了笑，垂下眼睛。| 近义词: modestly, shyly, reservedly | 反义词: boldly, immodestly, brazenly | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From Middle French *demourer* "to linger, stay, remain," from Latin *demorari* "to delay, linger," from *de-* "down" + *morari* "to delay." The original sense of demure was "grave, serious," later developing the connotations of "reserved" and "modest," especially in women.
