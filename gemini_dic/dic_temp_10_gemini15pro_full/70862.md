finlet:/ˈfɪnlɪt/| n. |小鳍；副鳍|A small fin, typically found between the dorsal and caudal fins of fish, such as tuna or mackerel.  These small fins help to streamline the fish and reduce drag.| The tuna's finlets help it swim at high speeds. 金枪鱼的小鳍帮助它高速游动。|Synonym:  fin, finling |Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "body" in the context of fish anatomy) |noun


finlet:/ˈfɪnlɪt/| n. |小鳍；副鳍|A small, detached fin located on the body of some fish, typically between the dorsal and caudal fins, that helps to stabilize the fish while swimming.| Mackerel have several finlets along their backs. 鲭鱼的背部有几个小鳍。|Synonym:  fin, finling |Antonym: (No direct antonym) |noun
