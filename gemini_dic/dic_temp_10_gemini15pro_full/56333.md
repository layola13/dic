dogmatizer:/ˈdɒɡmətaɪzər/ (US) /ˈdɔːɡmətaɪzər/ (UK)| n. |教条主义者，独断论者|A person who speaks or teaches dogmatically; someone who asserts opinions as though they were incontrovertible truths.|He was known as a dogmatizer, unwilling to consider any perspective other than his own. 他被称为教条主义者，不愿考虑除自己以外的任何观点。|近义词：doctrinaire, authoritarian, rigid, inflexible|反义词：freethinker, liberal, skeptic, open-minded|词性: noun

dogmatize:/ˈdɒɡmətaɪz/ (US) /ˈdɔːɡmətaɪz/ (UK)| v. |武断地提出；教条化|To speak or write dogmatically; to assert opinions as undeniable truths.|He tends to dogmatize about politics, alienating those who disagree. 他倾向于对政治问题进行武断的评论，疏远那些不同意他观点的人。|近义词：pontificate, preach, lay down the law|反义词：question, discuss, debate|词性: verb

dogmatic:/dɒɡˈmætɪk/ (US) /dɒɡˈmætɪk/ (UK)| adj. |教条的；武断的|Expressing opinions as if they were facts; characterized by or given to the expression of opinions very strongly or positively as if they were facts.|His dogmatic approach to teaching made it difficult for students to challenge his ideas. 他教条式的教学方法使学生很难挑战他的观点。|近义词：doctrinaire, authoritarian, peremptory, assertive|反义词：open-minded, flexible, questioning, skeptical|词性: adjective
