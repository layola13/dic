foreclosed:/fɔːrˈkloʊzd/ | adj. |  取消赎取权的；被取消赎取权的；禁止的，除名的 | (of a property) having been taken back by a lender because the borrower failed to repay the loan; (of a right, option, or possibility) ruled out; prevented. | Example 1: The bank foreclosed on their house after they missed several mortgage payments.  银行在他们拖欠了几次抵押贷款后取消了他们的房屋赎回权。

Example 2:  The possibility of a peaceful resolution has been foreclosed. 和平解决的可能性已被排除。|  Synonyms: repossessed, seized, confiscated, barred, precluded | Antonyms: redeemed, restored, allowed, permitted | word property: adjective

foreclose:/fɔːrˈkloʊz/ | v. | 取消赎取权；排除；禁止 | (of a lender) take possession of a mortgaged property as a result of the mortgagor's failure to keep up their mortgage payments; rule out or prevent (a course of action). | Example 1:  The bank foreclosed on his home after he defaulted on his loan.  他拖欠贷款后，银行取消了他房屋的赎回权。

Example 2:  The decision effectively foreclosed any further negotiations.  这个决定有效地阻止了进一步的谈判。| Synonyms: repossess, seize, confiscate, bar, preclude | Antonyms: redeem, restore, allow, permit | word property: verb
