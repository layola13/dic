duded:/ˈduːdɪd/ /ˈdjuːdɪd/| v. | 把…称为“dude”（主要用于美国英语）; 像dude一样对待; 以dude的方式行动| To call someone "dude". To treat someone like a dude. To act in the manner of a dude. | "He totally duded me out when I asked for help." 他在我寻求帮助时完全不理我。| 近义词: addressed, called, treated | 反义词: ignored, disregarded | 词性: 动词

词源:  Derived from the noun "dude," which originated in the late 19th century as a term for a dandy or fop, especially one from the East Coast of the United States visiting the West. The verb form "duded" likely emerged in the late 20th or early 21st century with the broader, more casual use of "dude."
