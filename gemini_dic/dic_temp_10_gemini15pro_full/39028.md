conflate:/kənˈfleɪt/ | v. | 合并，混合，混淆 | To combine two or more things into one, often in a way that blurs or confuses them. |  He conflated the two issues, making it difficult to discuss them separately.  他把两个问题混为一谈，使得很难单独讨论它们。| Example: The report conflated data from several different sources. 报告混合了来自几个不同来源的数据。 | Synonyms: merge, combine, blend, fuse, mix | Antonyms: separate, distinguish, differentiate | verb

conflated:/kənˈfleɪtɪd/ | adj. | 合并的，混合的 | Combined or blended, often in a way that blurs or confuses. | The conflated accounts of the incident made it difficult to determine what really happened.  合并后的事件描述使得很难确定真正发生了什么。| Example: The final product was a conflated version of several earlier drafts. 最终产品是几个早期草稿的合并版本。| Synonyms: merged, combined, blended, fused, mixed | Antonyms: separate, distinct, differentiated | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin conflāre (“to blow together, melt together”), from con- (“together”) + flāre (“to blow”).
