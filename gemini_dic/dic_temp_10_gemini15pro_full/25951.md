burr:/bɜːr/ (US) /bɜː(r)/ (UK)| n. v.| 毛刺，刺果，粗喉咙声，嗡嗡声|A rough or prickly edge or projection, especially one left on an object after it has been cut or shaped.  A whirring sound.  To speak with a guttural roll.|The metal had a burr on it after being filed.  金属锉过后有毛刺。  The machine made a loud burr. 机器发出很大的嗡嗡声. He burred his 'r's when he spoke. 他说话时r音发得粗重。|近义词：barb, prickle, whir,  rasp|反义词:smoothness, silence|词性:名词, 动词

Etymology:
From Middle English *burre*, from Old Norse *burr*, meaning "prickle, bristle". Cognate with Old English *burh*.
