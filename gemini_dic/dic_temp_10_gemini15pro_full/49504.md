demagoging: /ˈdɛməˌɡɑːɡɪŋ/
|n. |煽动；蛊惑人心|The act of appealing to people's emotions and prejudices in order to gain power, rather than using rational argument.|His skillful demagoging roused the crowd to a frenzy. 他巧妙的煽动使人群陷入疯狂。|demagoguery, rabble-rousing|reasoned discourse, logical argument|noun

demagogue: /ˈdɛməˌɡɑːɡ/
|n. |煽动者；蛊惑人心者|A political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument.|The demagogue stirred up the crowd with his fiery rhetoric.  这个煽动者用他激烈的言辞煽动了人群。|agitator, firebrand|statesman, diplomat|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek dēmagōgos (meaning "leader of the people"), from dēmos ("people") + agōgos ("leading").
