lithographs:/ˈlɪθəˌɡræfs/| n.|石版画|A picture printed by lithography. A method of printing from a flat stone or metal plate on which the image areas are worked using a greasy substance so that the ink will adhere to them by, while the non-image areas are made ink-repellent.|The museum has a collection of rare lithographs by Toulouse-Lautrec.|Synonyms: prints, engravings, etchings|Antonyms: N/A|noun
