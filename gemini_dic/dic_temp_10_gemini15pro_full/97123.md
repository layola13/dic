infiltrate: /ˈɪnfɪltreɪt/ | v. | 渗透，潜入 | To enter or gain access to (an organization, place, etc.) surreptitiously and gradually, especially in order to acquire secret information or cause damage. |  The reporter infiltrated the cult to expose their activities.  记者渗透进邪教组织以揭露他们的活动。

The spy infiltrated the enemy lines.  间谍潜入了敌人的防线。

近义词: penetrate, permeate, invade | 反义词: exfiltrate, withdraw | 词性: 动词

infiltration: /ˌɪnfɪlˈtreɪʃən/ | n. | 渗透，潜入 | The act of infiltrating. | The infiltration of enemy agents was a major security breach. 敌方特工的渗透是一次重大的安全漏洞。

The infiltration of rainwater into the soil is essential for plant growth. 雨水渗入土壤对于植物生长至关重要。

近义词: penetration, permeation, invasion | 反义词: exfiltration, withdrawal | 词性: 名词
