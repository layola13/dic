bed-chamber:/ˈbedˌtʃeɪmbər/ (US), /ˈbedˌtʃeɪmbə/ (UK)| n. |卧室，寝室|A room used primarily for sleeping.|The king retired to his bed-chamber for the night. 国王回到他的寝宫过夜。|Synonyms: bedroom, chamber, sleeping room|Antonyms: living room, kitchen|Noun

bed-chamber:/ˈbedˌtʃeɪmbər/ (US), /ˈbedˌtʃeɪmbə/ (UK)| n. |卧室，寝室|From Middle English *bedchambre, borrowed from Old French chambre, from Latin camera (“vault, arched roof”),  with the addition of "bed".  Originally referred to a room with a vaulted or arched ceiling, later specifically a sleeping room.|The queen's bed-chamber was lavishly decorated.  女王的卧室装饰奢华。|Synonyms: bedroom, chamber, sleeping room|Antonyms: living room, kitchen|Noun
