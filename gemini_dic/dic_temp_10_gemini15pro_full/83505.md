hackneyman:/ˈhæknimən/| n. |出租马车夫；雇工；文人；妓女|A driver of a hackney carriage (a carriage for hire); a person who works for hire, especially in a menial or unskilled capacity; a writer or journalist, especially one who works quickly and carelessly; (archaic) a prostitute.| In Victorian London, the hackneyman was a familiar sight, plying his trade on the busy streets.  在维多利亚时代的伦敦，出租马车夫是街头常见的景象，忙于他们的生意。|近义词：cabman, driver, hireling, drudge, hack writer|反义词:employer, client|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hakeneiman*, from Old French *haquenée* ("an ambling horse or mare, especially one for hire"), + *-man*.  Ultimately from Frankish *hakk* (“horse”).
