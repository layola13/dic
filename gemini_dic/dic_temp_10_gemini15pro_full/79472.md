gloatingly:/ˈɡləʊtɪŋli/ (US) /ˈɡloʊtɪŋli/| adv. |幸灾乐祸地，沾沾自喜地|In a manner expressing triumphant and often malicious satisfaction or delight in another's misfortune.|He gloatingly watched his rival's downfall. 他幸灾乐祸地看着对手的垮台。|Synonyms: smugly, triumphantly, gleefully|Antonyms: humbly, modestly, sympathetically|词性:副词

gloat:/ɡləʊt/ (US) /ɡloʊt/| vi. |幸灾乐祸，沾沾自喜|To feel or express great, often malicious, pleasure or satisfaction because of your own success or another's misfortune.|She gloated over her rival's failure. 她对竞争对手的失败感到幸灾乐祸。|Synonyms: revel, delight, crow|Antonyms: commiserate, sympathize, condole|词性:动词

Etymology: From Middle English *gloten, perhaps from Old Norse *gláta (“to grin, smile”).
