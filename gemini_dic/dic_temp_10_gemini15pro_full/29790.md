caucasians:/kɔːˈkeɪʒənz/| n.|高加索人；白种人|People of European and West Asian origin, traditionally classified as belonging to the Caucasian race (a now outdated and scientifically inaccurate term). This term is increasingly considered outdated and potentially offensive due to its association with racist ideologies.|Many scientists no longer consider Caucasians a distinct biological group. 许多科学家不再认为白种人是一个独特的生物群体。|The term "Caucasians" was historically used to describe people from Europe, North Africa, and West Asia.  “高加索人”一词在历史上用来描述来自欧洲、北非和西亚的人。|近义词：whites, Europeans (though not all Caucasians are European)|反义词: N/A (Avoid using antonyms based on race)|词性:noun
