electro-hydraulics:/ɪˌlektroʊ haɪˈdrɔːlɪks/
noun|电动液压学|The branch of engineering dealing with systems that use both electrical and hydraulic power. The study and application of combined electric and hydraulic power.|Electro-hydraulics plays a crucial role in modern automation. 电动液压学在现代自动化中起着至关重要的作用。|electrohydraulic systems|N/A|noun



ETYMOLOGY->
electro- (prefix): from Greek "ēlektron" meaning "amber," referring to static electricity.
hydraulics: from Greek "hydraulikos" meaning "water organ," relating to the use of water for power.
