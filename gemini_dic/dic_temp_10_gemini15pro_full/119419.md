microfossil:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌfɑːsəl/| n. |微体化石|A fossil that can only be studied using a microscope, such as a single-celled organism, pollen grain, or spore.|Paleontologists study microfossils to learn about past environments and climates.|Synonyms: microfauna, microflora|Antonyms: macrofossil|noun
