falter:/ˈfɔːltər/ (US) /ˈfɔːl.tər/ (UK)| n.| 犹豫，迟疑；蹒跚；支吾|An act of faltering; a hesitation, waver, or stumble.|例句：There was a slight falter in her step as she climbed the stairs. 她爬楼梯时脚步略有蹒跚。|近义词：hesitation, waver, stumble, stammer|反义词:  confidence, stability|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *falteren*, frequentative of *fallen* (“to fall”), from Old English *feallan* (“to fall”), from Proto-Germanic *fallaną* (“to fall”), from Proto-Indo-European *phol-/*bʰol- (“to fall, fail”).
