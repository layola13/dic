curate:/ˈkjʊreɪt/ | v. n. | 策展；精选；组织；牧师 |  To select, organize, and present (online content, merchandise, information, etc.), typically using professional or expert knowledge.  As a noun, it refers to a person who does this, especially a clergyman. | The museum curator carefully selected the artifacts for the exhibition.  博物馆馆长仔细挑选了展览的文物。| synonyms: organize, compile, assemble, gather, select | antonyms:  disorganize, scatter, disperse | verb

curate:/ˈkjʊrət/ | n. | 牧师；助理牧师 | A member of the clergy, especially one who assists a vicar or rector. | The curate visited the sick members of his parish.  助理牧师探望了他教区的病人。| synonyms: clergyman, minister, pastor, chaplain | antonyms:  laity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English curat, from Old French curat, from Medieval Latin curatus, past participle of curare (“to take care of”), from Latin cura (“care, concern”).
