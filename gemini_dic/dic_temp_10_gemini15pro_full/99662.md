interlabeled:/ˌɪntərˈleɪbəld/
adjective
相互标记的，互相贴标签的
Marked or labeled with each other's names or identifiers.  The samples were interlabeled to prevent bias during the experiment.
例句：The boxes were interlabeled, leading to confusion about their contents. 箱子相互贴错了标签，导致里面的东西混乱不清。

例句：The vials were interlabeled with patient IDs to track their progress.  小瓶上互相标注了病人的ID，以便追踪他们的进展。

近义词：cross-labeled, mislabeled
反义词：(No direct antonym, could consider "correctly labeled" or "uniquely labeled")
词性：adjective
