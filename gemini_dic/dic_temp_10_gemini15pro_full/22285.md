bonfires:/ˈbɒnfaɪəz/ (UK) /ˈbɑːnfaɪərz/ (US)| n. |篝火|A large outdoor fire, typically one built for celebration or warmth.|例句：We built a bonfire on the beach. 我们在沙滩上点燃了篝火。

例句：The bonfire blazed brightly in the night. 篝火在夜里熊熊燃烧。

近义词：fire, blaze, pyre|反义词:  (No direct antonym, consider "ice", "cold" if referring to temperature) |词性:noun

词源: From bone + fire. Originally referred to large fires in which bones were burned, especially after battles.  源自bone（骨头）+ fire（火）。最初指的是燃烧骨头的大火，尤其是在战斗之后。
