interdictors:/ˌɪntərˈdɪktərz/| n. |禁令者，阻碍者|People or things that prohibit or forbid something.  Those who issue or enforce an interdiction.|例句：The interdictors prevented the shipment of arms to the warring factions.  禁令者阻止了武器运往交战派别。

例句：The UN acted as interdictors, preventing further aggression. 联合国充当了阻碍者，防止了进一步的侵略。|近义词：prohibitors, obstructors, blockers|反义词:facilitators, enablers|Noun

interdictor:/ˌɪntərˈdɪktər/| n. |禁令者，阻碍者|A person or thing that interdicts.  Someone who prohibits or forbids something.  Something that obstructs or impedes.|例句：The judge acted as an interdictor, forbidding contact between the parties.  法官充当了禁令者，禁止双方接触。
