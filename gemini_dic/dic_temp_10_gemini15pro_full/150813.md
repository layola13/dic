paraffin:/ˈpærəfɪn/ (US), /ˈpærəfɪn/ (UK)| n. |石蜡，烷烃|A white or colorless, waxy solid mixture of hydrocarbons, obtained from petroleum and used in candles, cosmetics, and sealing materials; also, any of the saturated hydrocarbons that make up this mixture.|Paraffin wax is used to make candles. 石蜡用于制作蜡烛。|Synonyms: alkane, wax|Antonyms: None|Noun

paraffins:/ˈpærəfɪnz/ (US), /ˈpærəfɪnz/ (UK)| n. |烷烃|Plural form of paraffin, referring to a class of saturated hydrocarbons.|Alkanes, also known as paraffins, are important components of natural gas and petroleum. 烷烃，也称为石蜡，是天然气和石油的重要组成部分。|Synonyms: alkanes|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *paraffina, from Latin parum (“too little”) + affinis (“related, neighboring”). So called because of its unreactive nature.
