gastroscope:/ˈɡæstrəskoʊp/ (US) /ˈɡæstrəskəʊp/ (UK)| n. |胃镜|An instrument used to examine the inside of the stomach.  A thin, flexible tube with a camera and light on the end, inserted through the mouth and down the esophagus.|The doctor used a gastroscope to examine the patient's stomach. 医生用胃镜检查了病人的胃。|Synonyms: endoscope (more general), fiberscope|Antonyms: N/A|noun


Etymology:
From gastro- (Greek "stomach") + -scope (Greek "viewing instrument")
