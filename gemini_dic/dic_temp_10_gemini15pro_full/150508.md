pantomime: /ˈpæntəmaɪm/ (US), /ˈpæntəmaɪm/ (UK)| n. v.|哑剧，默剧；用哑剧表演|A dramatic entertainment, especially for children, that involves music, topical jokes, and slapstick comedy, but no dialogue.  The art or technique of conveying emotions, actions, or stories by gestures and facial expressions without speech.|The children loved the Christmas pantomime. 孩子们喜欢圣诞哑剧。|He pantomimed climbing a ladder. 他模仿爬梯子的动作。|synonyms: mime, dumb show, charade|antonyms: dialogue, speech|noun, verb
