deflection:/dɪˈflɛkʃən/ | n. | 偏斜，偏转，挠度 |The deflection of the compass needle indicates the presence of a magnetic field.  指南针指针的偏转表明存在磁场。|例句：The missile's deflection by the anti-missile system saved the city. 反导系统使导弹偏离了方向，拯救了这座城市。|近义词：deviation, divergence, digression |反义词: adherence, directness |词性:名词

deflection:/dɪˈflɛkʃən/ | n. | 偏差，偏离 |The poll showed a significant deflection from the expected results. 民意调查显示，结果与预期存在显著偏差。|例句：His deflection from the truth was evident in his hesitant speech. 他说话吞吞吐吐，明显是在回避事实。|近义词：deviation, aberration |反义词: accuracy, precision |词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin dēflectiōn-, dēflectiō, from Latin dēflectere (to bend down, turn aside), from de- (down, away) + flectere (to bend).
