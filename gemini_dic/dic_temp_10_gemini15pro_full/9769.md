antonomastic:/ˌæntənəˈmæstɪk/| adj.|代称的，用专有名词代替普通名词的|Referring to a person or thing by a descriptive phrase or epithet instead of the proper name, or using a proper name as a synonym for a general idea or quality.|例句：Shakespeare is often antonomastically referred to as "The Bard." 莎士比亚常被代称为“诗圣”。

例句：Calling a wise man a "Solomon" is an antonomastic usage.  将智者称为“所罗门”是一种代称用法。|近义词：eponymous, metonymic|反义词: N/A|词性: adjective

Etymology->
From Late Latin *antonomasticus*, from Ancient Greek ἀντονομαστικός (antonomāstikós, “of or pertaining to substituting one name for another”), from ἀντονομάζειν (antonomazein, “to call by a different name”), from ἀντί (antí, “instead of”) + ὀνομάζειν (onomazein, “to name”).
