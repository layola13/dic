edulcorate:/ɪˈdʌlkəreɪt/ | v. | 使甜，使温和，使悦耳 |To make something more pleasant or agreeable, often by removing unpleasant or harsh elements.  It can refer to sweetening something literally (like adding sugar) or figuratively (like making language more palatable). | The company tried to edulcorate the bad news by emphasizing the positive aspects. 公司试图通过强调积极方面来淡化坏消息。|  sweeten, soften, mitigate, palliate | worsen, exacerbate, aggravate | verb

edulcorate:/ɪˈdʌlkəreɪt/ | adj. | (rarely used) 甜的，温和的 | Sweetened, made mild or pleasant.  This adjectival form is much less common than the verb. |  The edulcorate version of the story left out all the gritty details.  这个温和版本的故事省略了所有残酷的细节。| sweet, mild, pleasant | harsh, bitter, unpleasant | adjective

词源：From Latin *ēdulcorāre, from ē- + dulcor (“sweetness”).  The prefix *ē- means "out of" or "from," while dulcor relates to sweetness.  Thus, the word literally means "to take the sweetness out of" something, which in extended usage means to make something more palatable or less harsh.
