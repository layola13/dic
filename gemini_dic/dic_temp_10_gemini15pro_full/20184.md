birched:/bɜːrtʃt/ | v. | 用桦条鞭打；鞭笞 | To punish someone by hitting them with a birch rod. |  He was birched for stealing apples. 他因为偷苹果而被桦条鞭打。


The naughty boy was birched by his schoolmaster.  这个淘气的男孩被他的校长用桦条打了。


birched:/bɜːrtʃt/ | adj. | 用桦木制成的 | Made from birch wood. |  We sat on a birched bench in the park. 我们坐在公园里用桦木做的长椅上。


They admired the beautiful birched furniture in the cabin. 他们欣赏小屋里美丽的桦木家具。


Etymology: From birch (n.) + -ed.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *birkjo.


Synonym: flog, whip, lash, cane


Antonym:  (For verb) pardon, forgive, excuse  (For adjective - no direct antonym, would depend on the specific meaning, e.g., oak, pine, etc. if referring to the type of wood)
