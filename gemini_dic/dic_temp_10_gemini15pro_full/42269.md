counter-charm:/ˈkaʊntərˌtʃɑːrm/| n.|反咒语，对抗符|Something that is believed to have the power to counteract a charm or spell; an antidote to a charm.|例句：She wore a counter-charm to protect herself from evil spirits. 她佩戴了一个反咒语来保护自己免受邪灵的侵害。

例句：He believed the amulet was a powerful counter-charm against bad luck. 他相信这个护身符是一个强大的对抗厄运的反咒语。|近义词：antidote, remedy, protection|反义词：charm, spell|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + charm.  "Counter-" acts as a prefix meaning "against" or "opposite." "Charm" comes from the Old French "charme," from the Latin "carmen," meaning "song," "verse," or "incantation." Thus, a counter-charm is literally something that works against a charm or spell.
