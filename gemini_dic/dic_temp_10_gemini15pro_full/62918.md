enlivenment:/ɪnˈlaɪvənmənt/| n. |活跃，生动，振奋|The act of making something more lively or interesting.|The music provided a welcome enlivenment to the party.  音乐为派对增添了受欢迎的活力。|Synonyms: animation, invigoration, stimulation|Antonyms: dullness, boredom, monotony|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From en- (make, cause to be) + liven (make lively) + -ment (noun suffix forming abstract nouns).  Ultimately from the Old English word *lifian* (to live).
