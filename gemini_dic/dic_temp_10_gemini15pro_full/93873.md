immolate: /ˈɪməleɪt/ | vt. | 献祭；牺牲；毁灭 | to kill or destroy something, especially by burning | The ancient Mayans would immolate prisoners of war to appease their gods. 古代玛雅人会献祭战俘来取悦他们的神。

The monks immolated themselves in protest against the war.  僧侣们自焚以抗议战争。| 近义词: sacrifice, kill, destroy | 反义词: save, rescue, protect | 词性: verb

Etymology:  From Latin *immolatus*, past participle of *immolare* (“to sprinkle with sacrificial meal; to sacrifice”), from *in-* (“in, on”) + *mola* (“sacrificial meal, salt cake”).
