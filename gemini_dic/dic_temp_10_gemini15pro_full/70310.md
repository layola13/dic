fiduciary:/fɪˈduːʃiəri/ |adj. n.| 受托的，信托的；受托人，托管人 |  Relating to or involving trust, especially with regard to the relationship between a trustee and a beneficiary.  A person who holds a legal or ethical relationship of trust with one or more other parties (person or group of persons).  |  Example (adj.):  The lawyer has a fiduciary duty to his client. 律师对他的客户负有信托责任。|  Example (n.): The fiduciaries of the estate invested the money wisely.  遗产受托人明智地投资了这笔钱。 |Synonym (adj.): trustee, confidential,  | Antonym (adj.): untrustworthy,  |Synonym (n.): trustee, guardian, custodian | Antonym (n.): beneficiary | Word Type: adjective, noun


fiduciaries:/fɪˈduːʃiɛriz/ |n.| 受托人，托管人（复数）|  Plural form of "fiduciary," referring to multiple people or entities holding positions of trust. | Example: The fiduciaries of the pension fund are responsible for managing the assets. 养老基金的受托人负责管理资产。 | Synonym: trustees, guardians, custodians | Antonym: beneficiaries | Word Type: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *fiduciarius*, from fiducia (“trust, confidence”), from fidus (“faithful, trusty”), related to fides (“faith”).
