cosying:/ˈkoʊziɪŋ/ (UK), /ˈkoʊziɪŋ/ (US)| v. |使舒适，使温暖；依偎，蜷伏；|Making something or someone feel warm, comfortable, and safe, often involving physical closeness.| She was cosying up to the fire on a cold winter's night. 她在一个寒冷的冬夜依偎在火炉旁。|近义词：snuggling, cuddling, nestling|反义词: distancing, separating|词性:动词(现在分词)

ETYMOLOGY->
From cosy + -ing.  "Cosy" originates from Scots "cozie," likely influenced by Low German "kose" (basket, nook).  The "-ing" suffix creates the present participle form, indicating an ongoing action.
