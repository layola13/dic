loop-stitching:/ˈluːp stɪtʃɪŋ/ | n. | 环形缝合；锁边缝 | A method of sewing in which loops are formed, often used for decorative edges or to prevent fabric from fraying.  | The loop-stitching on the edge of the blanket prevented it from unraveling.  毯子边缘的环形缝合防止它散开。 | Synonyms: blanket stitch, buttonhole stitch | Antonyms: N/A | Noun

loop:/luːp/ | n. v. | 环;圈;回路;循环  |  n. a shape like a circle or a curve, or a piece of wire, string etc bent into this shape. v. to make something into the shape of a loop, or to form a loop. |  The plane did a loop in the air. 飞机在空中做了一个环形飞行。/  Loop the rope around the post. 把绳子绕在柱子上。 | Synonyms: circle, ring, circuit, cycle | Antonyms: straight, linear | Noun, Verb

stitch:/stɪtʃ/ | n. v. |  缝;针脚;一针  | n. one of the small lines of thread that you can see when something has been sewn, or the action of making one of these. v. to use a needle and thread to repair, join, or decorate pieces of cloth, etc. |  I need three stitches in my arm. 我的胳膊需要缝三针。 /  She stitched a new button onto his coat. 她在他的外套上缝了一颗新纽扣。 | Synonyms: sew, seam | Antonyms: rip, tear  | Noun, Verb
