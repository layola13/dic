callus:/ˈkæləs/ (US) /ˈkæləs/ (UK)| n.|老茧；胼胝；(植物伤口的)愈伤组织|A thickened and hardened part of the skin or soft tissue, especially in an area that has been subjected to friction.|The guitarist had calluses on his fingertips.|近义词：thickening, induration|反义词: softness|noun


callus:/ˈkæləs/ (US) /ˈkæləs/ (UK)| v.|结茧；使生老茧|To form or cause to form a callus.|The constant digging callused his hands.|近义词：toughen, harden|反义词: soften|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *callus* "hard skin," of uncertain origin.
