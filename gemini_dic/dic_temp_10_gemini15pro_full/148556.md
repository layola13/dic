oversystematic:/ˌoʊvərˌsɪstəˈmætɪk/| adj.|过度系统化的；过分规则化的|Applying rules of grammar or language too rigidly, often resulting in incorrect forms.  This is especially common in children learning a language.|The child oversystematized the past tense, saying "goed" instead of "went".  这个孩子过度系统化了过去式，说“goed”而不是“went”。|例句：

His oversystematic application of the spelling rules led to many errors. 他对拼写规则的过度系统化应用导致了很多错误。

Young children often oversystematize grammatical rules, leading to forms like "goed" or "mouses."  幼儿经常过度系统化语法规则，导致出现“goed”或“mouses”之类的形式。


|近义词：rigid, inflexible, dogmatic|反义词：flexible, adaptable, irregular|词性:形容词
