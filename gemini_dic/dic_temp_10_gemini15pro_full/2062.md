acute: /əˈkjuːt/ (US) /əˈkjuːt/ (UK)| adj.| 敏锐的，尖锐的，剧烈的，严重的，急性的 | Characterized by sharpness or severity; having a sharp point or tip.  |  He has an acute sense of smell. 他有敏锐的嗅觉。

The patient complained of acute abdominal pain. 病人抱怨腹部剧烈疼痛。


|近义词：sharp, severe, intense, critical, piercing |反义词: mild, slight, obtuse, chronic, dull |词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *acūtus*, past participle of *acuō* (“I sharpen”), from *acus* (“needle”).

USAGE->
Attributive Use (修饰名词):

The acute angle of the triangle was less than 90 degrees. = 三角形的锐角小于90度。
The hospital specializes in acute care for patients with serious injuries. = 这家医院专门为严重受伤的病人提供紧急护理。


Predicative Use (作表语):

The pain in his chest was acute. = 他胸部的疼痛很剧烈。
The shortage of qualified nurses is becoming acute. = 合格护士的短缺正变得日益严重。


With Sense Verbs (与感官动词连用):

Dogs have an acute sense of hearing. = 狗有敏锐的听觉。
He possesses an acute awareness of the political situation. = 他对政治局势有着敏锐的意识。


Describing Illnesses (描述疾病):

She was admitted to the hospital with acute appendicitis. = 她因急性阑尾炎住进了医院。
Acute bronchitis can often be treated with rest and fluids. = 急性支气管炎通常可以通过休息和补充水分来治疗。


Describing Problems (描述问题):

The company is facing an acute financial crisis. = 这家公司正面临严重的财务危机。
There is an acute shortage of housing in the city. = 这个城市住房严重短缺。

PHRASE->
acute angle = 锐角
acute pain = 剧痛
acute illness = 急性病
acute shortage = 严重短缺
acute awareness = 敏锐的意识
