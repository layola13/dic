anti-pragmatic:/ˌæntipræɡˈmætɪk/| adj.|反语用的，非语用的|Contrary to the principles of pragmatics; disregarding the practical effects or consequences of language use.  It refers to something that doesn't consider the context, implied meanings, or the listener's understanding when communicating. |His anti-pragmatic approach to the negotiation, focusing solely on literal meanings, ultimately led to its failure.  他反语用的谈判方式，只关注字面意思，最终导致谈判失败。

His anti-pragmatic language, while technically correct, often confused his audience. 虽然技术上正确，但他反语用的语言经常让听众感到困惑。|近义词:impractical, unrealistic, literal-minded|反义词:pragmatic, practical, context-aware|词性:adjective
