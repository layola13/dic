eugenol:/ˈjuːdʒɪnɒl/ (US) /ˈjuːdʒɪnɔːl/ (UK)| n. |丁香酚；丁香油酚|A colorless or pale yellow aromatic liquid extracted from clove oil, having antiseptic and analgesic properties.|Eugenol is used in perfumes and flavorings, and also as a local antiseptic and anesthetic. 丁香酚用于香水和调味剂，也用作局部防腐剂和麻醉剂。|Synonyms: caryophyllene, allylguaiacol|Antonyms: None|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *eugénol*, from *eugenine* + *-ol*, ultimately from Latin *eugenia* (the clove tree), from Modern Latin *Caryophyllus aromaticus*.
