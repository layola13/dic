equivocation:/ɪˌkwɪvəˈkeɪʃən/| n.|含糊其辞，模棱两可，诡辩|The politician's equivocation on the issue angered many voters.  这位政客在这个问题上的含糊其辞激怒了许多选民。|例句：His answer was filled with equivocations. 他的回答充满了模棱两可。|近义词：ambiguity, prevarication, evasion, double-talk|反义词:clarity, directness, honesty, straightforwardness|词性:noun

equivocate:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪt/| v.|含糊其辞，支吾其词，说模棱两可的话|When asked directly about his plans, he equivocated and changed the subject. 当被直接问及他的计划时，他含糊其辞，并转移了话题。|例句：She equivocated when asked about her involvement in the scandal.  当被问及她是否卷入丑闻时，她支吾其词。|近义词：prevaricate, hedge, waffle|反义词:clarify, be direct, be honest|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: equivocacioun, from Old French: equivocation, from Latin: aequivocatio, from aequivocat- ‘called by the same name’, from the verb aequivocare, from aequi- ‘equally’ + vocare ‘to call’.
