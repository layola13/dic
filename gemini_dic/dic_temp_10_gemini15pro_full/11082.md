archaicisms: /ˈɑːrˌkeɪˌɪzəmz/ | n. | 古语；古体词 |  Words or phrases that are no longer in common use but are sometimes used to give a sense of old-fashioned style. | The author used several archaicisms, such as "thee" and "thou," to create a historical atmosphere.  作者使用了一些古语，例如“thee”和“thou”，以营造历史氛围。 | synonyms: old words, obsolete words, antiquated language | antonyms: modern words, contemporary language | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *archaismus, from Ancient Greek ἀρχαϊσμός (arkhaïsmós, “antiquated word”), from ἀρχαῖος (arkhaîos, “ancient”).
