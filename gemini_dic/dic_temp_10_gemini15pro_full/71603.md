flat-packing:/ˈflætˌpækɪŋ/
noun
扁平包装
The act of packing furniture or other items in a disassembled state, typically in flat boxes, for ease of transportation and assembly by the end user.
We bought some flat-packing furniture for our new apartment.  我们为新公寓买了一些扁平包装的家具。
This table comes in flat-packing, so it's easy to transport.  这张桌子是扁平包装的，所以很容易运输。
pre-packed, knockdown, ready-to-assemble
built-in, assembled

flat-pack:/ˈflætˌpæk/
noun
扁平包装的家具或物品
A piece of furniture or other item that is sold disassembled and flat-packed.
The flat-pack was easy to assemble.  这个扁平包装的家具很容易组装。
We struggled to put together the flat-pack wardrobe. 我们费劲地组装扁平包装的衣柜。
kit furniture, ready-to-assemble furniture
built-in furniture, assembled furniture

flat-pack:/ˌflætˈpæk/
verb
扁平包装
To pack (furniture or other items) in a disassembled state in flat boxes.
The company flat-packs all its furniture to reduce shipping costs.  这家公司将其所有家具扁平包装以降低运输成本。
They flat-packed the bookshelf for easy transport.  他们将书架扁平包装以便于运输。
pre-pack, knockdown
assemble, build


ETYMOLOGY->
"flat-pack" and "flat-packing" are relatively recent additions to the English language, emerging in the mid-20th century with the rise of self-assembly furniture.  They are derived from the literal description of the items being packed "flat" in "packs" or boxes.
