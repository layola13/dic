moored:/mʊərd/ | adj. | 停泊的；系泊的 | secured in place with ropes or anchors; figuratively, emotionally attached or fixed | The boat was moored to the dock. 这艘船停泊在码头。| Synonyms: anchored, docked, secured, fastened | Antonyms: adrift, loose, untethered | word type: adjective

moored:/mʊərd/ | v. | 停泊；系泊（moor的过去式和过去分词）| past tense and past participle of "moor" which means to secure (a vessel) by cables or ropes or anchors |  We moored the boat overnight in the harbor. 我们把船停泊在港口过夜。 | Synonyms: anchored, docked, secured, fastened | Antonyms: unmoored, released, cast off | word type: verb

Etymology: From Middle English *moren, from Old Norse *móra "to moor, delay, hinder," from Proto-Germanic *mōrijaną "to hinder, delay."
