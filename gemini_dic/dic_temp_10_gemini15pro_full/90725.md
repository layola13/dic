humiliate: /hjuːˈmɪlieɪt/ (US) /hjuːˈmɪlɪeɪt/ (UK) | vt. | 羞辱，使丢脸 | To make someone feel ashamed or foolish by injuring their dignity and pride. |  He was humiliated by his failure in the exam. 他因考试不及格而感到羞辱。 | Synonyms: disgrace, shame, mortify, abase | Antonyms: honor, exalt, praise | Verb

humiliation: /hjuːˌmɪliˈeɪʃn/ (US) /hjuːˌmɪlɪˈeɪʃn/ (UK) | n. | 羞辱，丢脸 | The feeling of being ashamed or foolish by injuring one's dignity and pride. | The public humiliation was too much for him to bear.  公开的羞辱让他难以忍受。 | Synonyms: disgrace, shame, mortification | Antonyms: honor, exaltation, praise | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin humiliatus, past participle of humiliare "to humble,"  from Latin humilis "lowly, humble,"  from humus "ground, earth."
