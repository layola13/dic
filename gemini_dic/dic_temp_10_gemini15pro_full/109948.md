liberalizations:/ˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/
|n.|自由化，放宽管制|The process of making something less strict or controlled, often referring to laws, regulations, or social attitudes.  The act of making something more liberal.|
|例句：The liberalizations of trade policies have led to increased global commerce. 贸易政策的自由化导致了全球贸易的增长。|

|例句：The government's liberalizations of immigration laws made it easier for people to enter the country. 政府对移民法的放宽使得人们更容易进入该国。|
|近义词：reforms, deregulation, easing, loosening|
|反义词：restrictions, tightening, control|
|词性: 名词 (plural)|


liberalization:/ˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
|n.|自由化，放宽管制|The process of making something less strict or controlled, often referring to laws, regulations, or social attitudes.  The act of making something more liberal.|
|例句：The liberalization of the financial markets has brought both benefits and risks. 金融市场的自由化带来了好处和风险。|

|例句：Many countries are undergoing a liberalization of social attitudes towards same-sex marriage. 许多国家对同性婚姻的社会态度正在趋于自由化。|
|近义词：reforms, deregulation, easing, loosening|
|反义词：restrictions, tightening, control|
|词性: 名词 (singular)|


词源：From the verb "liberalize," which comes from "liberal" + "-ize." "Liberal" traces back to the Latin "liberalis," meaning "of freedom" or "befitting a free person."
