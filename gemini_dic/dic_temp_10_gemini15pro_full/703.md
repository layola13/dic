absent: /ˈæbsənt/ (US) /ˈæbsənt/ (UK)| adj.| 缺席的；不在的；缺乏的；心不在焉的 | Not present at a place, event, or time; lacking or missing. | He was absent from school due to illness. 他因病缺课。

Three members of the team were absent.  该队有三名成员缺席。

近义词: missing, away, lacking, unavailable
反义词: present, attending, here, there
词性: adjective

absent: /ˈæbsənt/ (US) /ˈæbsənt/ (UK)| vt.|  缺席；不参加 | To deliberately not go to a place or event. | He absented himself from the meeting. 他没有出席会议。

She deliberately absented herself from work. 她故意旷工。


近义词: avoid, skip, ditch
反义词: attend, present oneself
词性: verb

absent: /ˈæbsənt/ (US) /ˈæbsənt/ (UK)| n.| 缺席者 | A person who is not present at a place or event. | The teacher took a roll call of the absents. 老师点名缺席的学生。

All absents must provide a valid reason. 所有缺席者必须提供正当理由。


近义词: non-attendee
反义词: attendee, participant
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French _absent_, from Latin _absens_, present participle of _abesse_ "to be away," from _ab_ "away, from" + _esse_ "to be."

USAGE->
As an adjective:

一般现在时:
He is absent from school today. = 他今天没来上学。
The required documents are absent from the file. = 文件中缺少所需的文件。


一般过去时:
She was absent from the meeting yesterday. = 她昨天没有参加会议。
Oxygen was absent in the Martian atmosphere. = 火星大气中缺乏氧气。


现在完成时:
He has been absent for three days. = 他已经缺席三天了。
Key evidence has been absent from the trial. = 审判中缺少关键证据。


过去完成时:
She had been absent for a week before they noticed. = 他们注意到之前她已经缺席一周了。
The necessary funding had been absent for years. = 多年来一直缺乏必要的资金。


将来时:
I will be absent tomorrow. = 我明天会缺席。
If you are absent, please notify your teacher. = 如果你要缺席，请通知你的老师。


As a verb:

一般现在时:
He absents himself from meetings frequently. = 他经常缺席会议。
She absents herself from work without permission. = 她未经许可擅自缺勤。


一般过去时:
He absented himself from the party last night. = 他昨晚没去参加聚会。
She absented herself from the vote. = 她没有参加投票。



PHRASE->
absent from =  缺席...

conspicuously absent =  引人注目地缺席
an absent look/expression = 茫然的表情/心不在焉的神情
