candlewicks:/ˈkændəlˌwɪks/| n. |蜡烛芯|The braided strands of cotton or other fibrous material in a candle that draw up the melted wax by capillary action to be burned.|The candlewicks were too short and the candle wouldn't light.|近义词：wicks|反义词: None|词性:noun

Etymology:
From "candle" + "wick". "Candle" comes from Old English *candel (from Latin candela "candle") and "wick" from Old English *weoce (from Proto-Germanic *wiką).
