abstract:/ˈæbstrækt/ /æbˈstrækt/| adj. n. vt.|抽象的，摘要，提取|Existing in thought or as an idea but not having a physical or concrete existence.|He made an abstract painting. 他画了一幅抽象画。|近义词：theoretical, conceptual, ideal, intangible|反义词：concrete, specific, tangible|adjective

abstract:/ˈæbstrækt/ /æbˈstrækt/| adj. n. vt.|抽象的，摘要，提取|A summary of the contents of a book, article, or formal speech.|The abstract of the article summarized the main points. 这篇文章的摘要总结了要点。|近义词：summary, synopsis, précis|反义词：full text, complete version|noun

abstract:/ˈæbstrækt/ /æbˈstrækt/| adj. n. vt.|抽象的，摘要，提取|Consider (something) theoretically or separately from something else.|To abstract science and art from reality is to destroy their value. 将科学和艺术从现实中抽象出来会破坏它们的价值。|近义词：extract, isolate, separate|反义词：combine, unite, integrate|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin abstractus, past participle of abstrahere "to draw away," from Latin abs-, ab- "away, from" + trahere "to draw."

USAGE->
Adjective (形容词):

His abstract ideas were difficult to grasp. = 他的抽象想法很难理解。

The artist's abstract paintings were open to interpretation. = 这位艺术家的抽象画可以有多种解读。


Noun (名词):

Please submit an abstract of your research paper. = 请提交你的研究论文的摘要。

The abstract of the presentation provided a brief overview of the topic. = 演示文稿的摘要简要概述了主题。


Verb (动词):

We can abstract the main principles from this complex theory. = 我们可以从这个复杂的理论中提取主要原则。

The chemist abstracted the essential oil from the plant. = 化学家从植物中提取了精油。


PHRASE->
in the abstract = 从理论上来说
