obsolescence:/ˌɒbsəˈlɛsəns/ | n. | 过时，陈旧，淘汰 |The obsolescence of old technology is inevitable. 旧技术的淘汰是不可避免的。|例句：The rapid obsolescence of computer hardware is a problem for many businesses. 计算机硬件的快速过时对许多企业来说是一个问题。|近义词：outdatedness, antiquity, superannuation |反义词: currency, modernity, newness | noun
obsolescences:/ˌɒbsəˈlɛsənsɪz/ | n. | 过时，陈旧，淘汰（复数）| The obsolescences of certain manufacturing processes have led to job losses.  某些制造工艺的过时导致了失业。|例句：Planned obsolescences are a controversial tactic employed by some manufacturers. 计划性报废是一些制造商采用的有争议的策略。 |近义词：outdatedness(plural), antiquities, superannuations |反义词: currencies, modernities, newnesses | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *obsolescentia, from obsolescent- (stem of obsolescens), present participle of obsolescere “to become obsolete, fall into disuse,” from ob- “away” + solescere “to become accustomed, grow old,” from solere “be accustomed.”
