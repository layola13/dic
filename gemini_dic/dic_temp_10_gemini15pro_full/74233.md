fowler:/ˈfaʊlər/| n.|捕鸟人；诱捕者|A person who hunts or traps wild birds, especially as a job. |The fowler set his traps before dawn.  捕鸟人在黎明前设置了陷阱。|Synonym: birdcatcher, huntsman, trapper|Antonym: protector, conservationist|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *foulere*, from Old English *fugelere*, equivalent to *fugol* (fowl) + *-ere* (one who). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *fuglaz* (bird) and *-ari* (one who).
