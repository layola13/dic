orthopteran: /ɔːˈθɒptərən/ (UK), /ɔːrˈθɑːptərən/ (US)| n. |直翅目昆虫|An insect of the order Orthoptera, such as grasshoppers, crickets, and locusts.  These insects typically have long hind legs adapted for jumping.|Example: The field was alive with the chirping of orthopterans.  田野里充满了直翅目昆虫的鸣叫声。

Example:  Locusts, a type of orthopteran, can cause significant damage to crops. 蝗虫，一种直翅目昆虫，会对农作物造成重大损害。|近义词: grasshopper, cricket, locust |反义词:  (No direct antonym for a taxonomic group) |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ὀρθόπτερος (orthópteros, “straight-winged”), from ὀρθός (orthós, “straight”) + πτερόν (pterón, “wing”).
