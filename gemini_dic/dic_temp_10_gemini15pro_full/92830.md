iconomachy: /ˌaɪkɒˈnɒməki/ (US) /ˌaɪkəˈnɒməki/ (UK)| n. |圣像破坏；反对崇拜圣像|The act of attacking or opposing the religious veneration of icons and other sacred images.  It can also refer more broadly to the opposition to images in general.|The Byzantine Iconoclasm was a period of iconomachy in the Byzantine Empire.|Synonyms: iconoclasm, image-breaking|Antonyms: iconophilia, iconolatry|noun
iconoclast: /aɪˈkɒnəklæst/ (US) /aɪˈkɒnəklɑːst/ (UK)| n. |圣像破坏者；打破传统者|A person who attacks cherished beliefs or institutions.|The young entrepreneur was an iconoclast, challenging established business practices.|Synonyms: radical, revolutionary, rebel, nonconformist|Antonyms: conformist, traditionalist|noun
iconoclastic: /aɪˌkɒnəˈklæstɪk/ (US) /aɪˌkɒnəˈklɑːstɪk/ (UK)| adj. |圣像破坏的；打破传统的|Characterized by attack on cherished beliefs or institutions.|His iconoclastic views on education challenged the traditional curriculum.|Synonyms: revolutionary, rebellious, unconventional, unorthodox|Antonyms: conventional, orthodox, traditional|adjective
