missis's:/ˈmɪsɪzɪz/| n.|太太的（所有格）|Possessive form of "missis," a colloquial and often considered disrespectful term for "Mrs." (mistress).  It indicates ownership or possession by a married woman.  Due to its informal and potentially offensive nature, its use is generally discouraged.|Example:  The missis's car is in the driveway. 太太的车停在车道上。|Example:  Don't touch the missis's belongings.  别碰太太的东西。|近义词：Mrs.'s, wife's|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider Mr.'s or husband's for contrasting possession)|词性:noun (possessive)
