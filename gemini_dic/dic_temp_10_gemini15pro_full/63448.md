entrenched:/ɪnˈtrentʃt/ /ɛnˈtrentʃt/| adj.|根深蒂固的；牢固确立的；不容易改的|Firmly established and difficult or unlikely to change; deeply ingrained.|例句：The enemy troops were entrenched in the hills. 敌军在山上筑起了壕沟。|例句：Sexism is deeply entrenched in our society. 性别歧视在我们社会根深蒂固。|近义词：established, fixed, ingrained, rooted, firm, secure|反义词：weak, unstable, precarious|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From French *entrencher*, from en- (“in”) + trenchier (“to cut”).  Ultimately from Vulgar Latin *trincare* (“to cut”), from Late Latin *truncare* (“to maim, mutilate”).
