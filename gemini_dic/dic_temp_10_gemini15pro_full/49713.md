democrat:/ˈdeməkræt/ (US) /ˈdɛməkræt/ (UK)| n. | 民主主义者，民主党人 | A person who believes in and supports democracy; a member of the Democratic Party (especially in the US). |  例句：He is a staunch democrat. 他是一位坚定的民主主义者。

例句：She is a registered Democrat. 她是一位注册的民主党人。

近义词：republican (in some contexts), egalitarian, liberal | 反义词：autocrat, dictator, totalitarian, Republican (US politics) | 词性：noun


democratical:/ˌdeməˈkrætɪkl/ (US) /ˌdɛməˈkrætɪkl/ (UK)| adj. |民主的，民主主义的，民主精神的| Relating to or supporting democracy. | 例句：They believe in democratical principles. 他们相信民主原则。

例句：The country has a democratically elected government. 这个国家有一个民选的政府。

近义词：republican (in some contexts), egalitarian, popular, representative | 反义词：autocratic, dictatorial, undemocratic | 词性：adjective


democratically:/ˌdeməˈkrætɪkli/ (US) /ˌdɛməˈkrætɪkli/ (UK) | adv. |民主地，民主主义地| In a democratic manner. | 例句：The decision was made democratically. 这个决定是通过民主的方式做出的。

例句：They voted democratically for their leader. 他们以民主的方式投票选出了他们的领导人。

近义词：representatively, popularly | 反义词：autocratically, dictatorially, undemocratically | 词性：adverb


词源: From French *démocrate*, from Ancient Greek *dēmokratēs*  (member of the people, democrat), from *dēmos* (people) + *kratos* (power, rule).
