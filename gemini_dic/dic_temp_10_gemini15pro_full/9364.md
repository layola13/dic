antipuritans:/ˌæntiˈpjʊrɪtənz/
|n.|反清教徒|People opposed to the strict moral and religious principles of the Puritans, often characterized by a more relaxed or liberal attitude towards pleasure and entertainment.|The antipuritans frequented the taverns and theaters, much to the dismay of the more pious citizens.|Near synonyms: libertines, freethinkers|Antonyms: puritans, moralists|noun


antipuritan:/ˌæntiˈpjʊrɪtən/
|adj.|反清教徒的|Opposed to or rejecting the strict moral and religious principles of the Puritans.|The antipuritan sentiment was growing among the younger generation.|Near synonyms: libertine, freethinking|Antonyms: puritanical, moralistic|adjective
