long-winded:/ˌlɔːŋˈwɪndɪd/ | adj. |冗长的，啰嗦的|tediously long in speaking or writing; using too many words|The professor's lectures were notoriously long-winded, often leaving students struggling to stay awake.| 近义词: verbose, prolix, rambling | 反义词: concise, succinct, brief | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From "long" + "wind" (breath).  Originally referred to needing a lot of breath to say something, implying length.
