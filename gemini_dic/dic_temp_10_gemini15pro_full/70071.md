feudalization:/ˌfjuːdəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 封建化 | The process of establishing or transforming a society into a feudal system, characterized by hierarchical relationships, land ownership as the basis of power, and reciprocal obligations between lords and vassals. | The feudalization of Europe took centuries. 欧洲的封建化历经了几个世纪。| 近义词：feudalism | 反义词: modernization | 词性: noun


feudalization:/ˌfjuːdəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 封建化 |  The act of transforming a system, society, or region into one based on feudal principles.  This involves the granting of land in exchange for service and loyalty, creating a hierarchy of lords and vassals. | The historian argued that the feudalization of Japan occurred later than in Europe.  这位历史学家认为，日本的封建化比欧洲晚。| 近义词： enfeoffment | 反义词: democratization | 词性: noun
