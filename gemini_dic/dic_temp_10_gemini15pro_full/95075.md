inadvisably:/ˌɪnədˈvaɪzəbli/| adv. |不明智地，不谨慎地|Not wisely or prudently; in a way that is not recommended.|He inadvisably invested all his money in one stock. 他不明智地把所有钱都投资在一支股票上。|例句：She inadvisably left her front door unlocked. 她很不谨慎地没锁前门。|近义词：imprudently, unwisely, foolishly|反义词：wisely, prudently, judiciously|词性:副词

Etymology:
From in- (not) + advisable + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Advisable" comes from the Old French "avisable," meaning "worthy of being advised."  Ultimately, the roots trace back to Latin "ad-" (to) and "visum" (seen, considered).
