globalisation:/ˌɡləʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ /ˌɡloʊbələˈzeɪʃn/ | n. |全球化|The process by which businesses or other organizations develop international influence or start operating in many different countries.|例句：Globalization has led to increased interconnectedness between countries. 全球化导致国家间的相互联系日益增强。

例句：The globalization of markets has created new opportunities for businesses. 市场全球化为企业创造了新的机遇。


|近义词：internationalization, mundialization |反义词: localization, regionalization |词性:noun


globalize:/ˈɡləʊbəlaɪz/ /ˈɡloʊbəˌlaɪz/ | v. |全球化|Make (something) global in scope or application.|例句：Many companies are seeking to globalize their operations. 许多公司正在寻求将其业务全球化。

例句：The internet has globalized communication. 互联网使通信全球化。

|近义词：internationalize, broaden, expand |反义词: localize, restrict |词性:verb


global:/ˈɡləʊbl/ /ˈɡloʊbl/ | adj. |全球的|Relating to the whole world; worldwide.|例句：Global warming is a serious threat to the environment. 全球变暖是对环境的严重威胁。

例句：The company has a global presence in over 50 countries. 该公司在 50 多个国家/地区开展全球业务。


|近义词：worldwide, international, universal |反义词: local, regional, national |词性:adjective



ETYMOLOGY->
From globe + -al + -ization/-isation.  The word "globe" ultimately comes from the Latin "globus", meaning "sphere" or "ball".
