desperado:/ˌdɛspəˈrɑːdoʊ/ (US), /ˌdɛspəˈreɪdoʊ/ (UK)| n. |亡命之徒；不顾一切的人|A desperate or reckless person, especially a criminal.|He lived life as a desperado, constantly on the run from the law. 他过着亡命之徒的生活，不断逃亡以躲避法律的制裁。|Example: The desperado held up the stagecoach. 那个亡命之徒抢劫了驿站马车。|Synonyms: outlaw, bandit, gangster, gunfighter, rogue|Antonyms: lawman, police officer|noun

desperate:/ˈdɛspərɪt/| adj. |绝望的；不顾一切的；极度渴望的|Feeling, showing, or involving a hopeless sense that a situation is so bad as to be impossible to deal with.|He was desperate for a job to support his family. 他非常渴望得到一份工作来养家糊口。|Example: In a desperate attempt to escape, the prisoner climbed the wall. 囚犯为了越狱，不顾一切地爬上了墙。|Synonyms: frantic, hopeless, dire, extreme|Antonyms: hopeful, calm, optimistic|adjective
