elementaries: /ˌɛlɪˈmɛntəriz/ | n. | 基础知识；初步；小学（通常用复数形式） |  The basics or first principles of a subject. Often used in the plural to refer to the first years of school (primary school). | She's going back to school to brush up on her elementaries. 她要回学校去温习一下基础知识。| synonyms: basics, fundamentals, rudiments, primary school | antonyms: advanced studies, complexities | noun

elementaries: /ˌɛlɪˈmɛntəriz/ | adj. | 基本的；初步的；小学的 | Relating to the basic principles of a subject or the first stages of learning. |  Elementary education is crucial for a child's development.  小学教育对儿童的发展至关重要。 | synonyms: basic, fundamental, rudimentary, introductory, primary | antonyms: advanced, complex, sophisticated | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *elementairie*, from Old French *elementaire*, from Latin *elementarius* (“pertaining to the elements or letters of the alphabet”), from *elementum* (“element, first principle, letter of the alphabet”).
