cabinetmaker:/ˈkæbɪnɪtˌmeɪkər/| n. |橱柜制造者，细木工|A person who makes cabinets and other fine furniture.| The cabinetmaker crafted a beautiful mahogany desk.  这位细木工制作了一张漂亮的红木桌子。|Synonyms: joiner, woodworker, carpenter |Antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From cabinet + maker.  "Cabinet" comes from Old French "cabinet," diminutive of "cabine" (cabin, hut). "Maker" comes from Old English "maca" (maker, poet) and Proto-Germanic "*makōną" (to make).
