geotextile: /ˌdʒiːoʊˈtɛkstʌɪl/ | n. |土工织物；土工布|A permeable fabric made of synthetic fibers, used in civil engineering projects such as roads, dams, and landfills, to stabilize soil, filter water, or reinforce structures.|Geotextiles are often used to prevent erosion on steep slopes.|Synonyms: geofabric, filter fabric|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps natural materials like clay or rock could be considered functionally opposite in some applications)|Noun
