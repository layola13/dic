chromidrosis:/ˌkroʊmɪˈdroʊsɪs/ | n. | 色汗症 | A rare condition characterized by the secretion of colored sweat, usually black, blue, green, yellow, red, or brown. It's often caused by the presence of lipofuscin or other pigments in the sweat glands. | Example 1: The doctor diagnosed her with chromidrosis after observing the blue-tinged sweat on her skin. 医生观察到她皮肤上的蓝色汗液后，诊断她患有色汗症。

Example 2:  Chromidrosis can be a psychologically distressing condition due to its visible nature. 由于色汗症的可见性，它可能是一种心理上令人痛苦的疾病。 | Synonym: coloured sweat, pigmented perspiration | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but normal sweat/perspiration could be considered an opposite state) | Noun
