marsipobranchia:/ˌmɑːrsɪpoʊˈbræŋkiə/ | n. |圆口纲|A superclass of eel-like aquatic vertebrates that have a round, jawless mouth with horny teeth and a rasping tongue. They include lampreys and hagfishes.|Example 1: Marsipobranchia are considered to be one of the most primitive groups of vertebrates. 圆口纲被认为是最原始的脊椎动物类群之一。

Example 2: Scientists study Marsipobranchia to understand the early evolution of vertebrates. 科学家研究圆口纲以了解脊椎动物的早期进化。|近义词:Cyclostomata|反义词: Gnathostomata|词性: noun


Etymology:
From Greek *μάρσιπος (mársipos, “pouch, purse”) + *βράγχια (bránkhia, “gills”).  Referring to the pouch-like gill openings.
