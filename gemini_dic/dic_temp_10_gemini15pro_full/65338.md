euk:/juːk/ | n. | 真核生物 | A eukaryote; an organism whose cells contain a nucleus and other organelles enclosed within membranes. | Example: The euk, like the amoeba, is a single-celled organism.  像变形虫一样，真核生物是单细胞生物。| Example:  Scientists study euks to understand complex cellular processes. 科学家研究真核生物以了解复杂的细胞过程。 | Synonym: eukaryote | Antonym: prokaryote | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
Shortened form of "eukaryote".  "Eukaryote" comes from the Greek "eu" (true, good) + "karyon" (nut, kernel, referring to the nucleus).  真核生物是 eukaryote 的缩写形式。Eukaryote 来自希腊语 eu（真的，好的）+ karyon（坚果，核，指细胞核）。
