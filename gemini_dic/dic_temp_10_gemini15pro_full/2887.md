advertisement: /ˌædvərˈtaɪzmənt/ (US) /ədˈvɜːtɪsmənt/ (UK)| n.|广告|A public promotion of some product or service.|The company launched a new advertisement for their latest phone. 这家公司为其最新款手机发布了一则新广告。|Synonyms: commercial, promo, publicity, notice, announcement|Antonyms:  concealment, suppression|noun

advertisement: /ˌædvərˈtaɪzmənt/ (US) /ədˈvɜːtɪsmənt/ (UK)| v.|做广告，宣传|To make something known generally or in public, especially in order to sell it.|They advertised their new product on television. 他们在电视上宣传他们的新产品。|Synonyms: promote, publicize, market, announce|Antonyms:  hide, suppress|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English advertisemente, from Anglo-Norman advertissement, from Old French advertir "to warn, inform," from Latin advertere "to turn toward," from ad- "to" + vertere "to turn." The meaning shifted from "notification" to "public announcement" in the 18th century.


USAGE->
Countable Noun:
I saw an interesting advertisement in the newspaper. = 我在报纸上看到一则有趣的广告。
There are many advertisements on this website. = 这个网站上有很多广告。

Uncountable Noun:
The company spent a lot of money on advertisement. = 这家公司在广告上花费了很多钱。
Good advertisement is essential for a successful business. = 好的广告对于一个成功的企业至关重要。

Verb:
They are advertising their new product on TV. = 他们在电视上宣传他们的新产品。
The company advertised for a new marketing manager. = 这家公司登广告招聘一位新的市场营销经理。


PHRASE->
place an advertisement = 刊登广告

advertisement for = …的广告
take out an advertisement = 登广告
