eutrophication:/ˌjuːtrəfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 富营养化 | The excessive richness of nutrients in a lake or other body of water, frequently due to runoff from the land, which causes a dense growth of plant life and death of animal life from lack of oxygen. | The algal bloom was a clear sign of eutrophication in the lake.  湖泊中藻类的大量繁殖是富营养化的明显标志。| 近义词：hypertrophication | 反义词: oligotrophication | 词性: noun


eutrophic:/juːˈtrɒfɪk/ | adj. | 富营养的 | Characterized by excessive plant growth due to an abundance of nutrients. |  The eutrophic lake was covered in algae. 富营养化的湖泊被藻类覆盖。| 近义词：hypertrophic | 反义词: oligotrophic | 词性: adjective


eutrophications:  (plural of eutrophication) /ˌjuːtrəfɪˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. | 富营养化（复数）| Multiple instances of the process of eutrophication. | The study examined the eutrophications of several lakes in the region. 这项研究调查了该区域几个湖泊的富营养化情况。| 近义词：hypertrophications | 反义词: oligotrophications | 词性: noun (plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From eu- (good, well) + -trophic (nourishment, growth) + -ation (forming nouns of process).  Ultimately from Greek *eu* (good) + *trophē* (nourishment).
