internalise:/ɪnˈtɜːnəlaɪz/ (UK) /ɪnˈtɜːrnəlaɪz/ (US)| v. |使内化；将…藏于心中|To make (attitudes or behaviour) part of one's nature by learning or unconscious assimilation.|She had internalized her parents' values. 她已经内化了父母的价值观。|internalize (US), incorporate, absorb, assimilate|externalize|verb


internalized:/ɪnˈtɜːnəlaɪzd/ (UK) /ɪnˈtɜːrnəlaɪzd/ (US)| adj. |内化的|Made a part of one's nature by learning or unconscious assimilation.|His anger was deeply internalized. 他的愤怒被深深地内化了。|inward, ingrained, suppressed|externalized|adjective


Etymology:  From internal + -ize.  The word "internal" comes from the Latin "internus" meaning "inward".
