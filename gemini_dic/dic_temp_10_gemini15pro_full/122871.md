mollifyingly: /ˈmɒlɪfaɪɪŋli/ (UK) /ˈmɑːlɪfaɪɪŋli/ (US)| adv.| 平息地，抚慰地，安抚地 |In a way that appeases or soothes someone who is angry or upset.| She spoke mollifyingly to the upset customer, trying to calm him down. 她安抚着那位恼火的顾客，试图让他平静下来。| Example: He smiled mollifyingly at her, hoping to defuse the situation. 他对她安抚地笑了笑，希望能缓和一下局势。| Synonyms: soothingly, appeasingly, placatingly | Antonyms: aggravatingly, provokingly, antagonistically | Word Type: Adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "mollify," which comes from the Latin "mollificare," meaning "to make soft" (from "mollis" meaning "soft" and "facere" meaning "to make"). The "-ing" suffix transforms it into a present participle, and the "-ly" suffix makes it an adverb.
