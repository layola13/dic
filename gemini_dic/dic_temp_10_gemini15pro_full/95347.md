ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *incentivum, neuter of *incentivus ("inciting, setting on fire"), from Latin incentivus ("a tune played on a shepherd's pipe"), from incinere ("to sing"), frequentative of canere ("to sing").  The sense evolution was from "pipe tune" to "anything which incites or spurs on".
