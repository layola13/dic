overcivility: /ˌoʊvərˌsɪˈvɪləti/
n.|过度礼貌；过分客气|Excessive politeness or courtesy, sometimes to the point of being insincere or annoying.|She greeted me with such overcivility that I suspected she was hiding something. 她以如此过分的礼貌迎接我，以至于我怀疑她隐瞒了什么。|近义词:excessive politeness, overpoliteness|反义词:rudeness, impoliteness|词性:名词

Etymology:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "civility" (from Middle French "civilité," from Latin "civilitas" meaning "citizenship, courtesy").  The prefix "over-" intensifies the meaning of "civility" to suggest an excess of politeness.
