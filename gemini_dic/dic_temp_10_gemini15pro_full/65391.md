euphemiously:/juːfəˈmɪsli/ | adv. |委婉地|In a way that uses mild or indirect words or expressions to avoid mentioning something unpleasant or offensive. |She euphemiously referred to his passing away as "going to a better place." 她委婉地称他去世为“去了一个更好的地方”。| 近义词:indirectly, delicately, subtly | 反义词: bluntly, directly, frankly |词性: 副词

euphemistic:/juːfəˈmɪstɪk/ | adj. |委婉的|Using mild or indirect words or expressions to avoid mentioning something unpleasant or offensive. |"Passed away" is a euphemistic expression for "died." “去世”是“死亡”的委婉说法。| 近义词:indirect, delicate, subtle | 反义词: blunt, direct, frank |词性: 形容词

euphemism:/ˈjuːfəmɪzəm/ | n. |委婉语|A mild or indirect word or expression substituted for one considered to be too harsh or blunt when referring to something unpleasant or embarrassing.|Using "restroom" instead of "toilet" is a common euphemism. 用“洗手间”代替“厕所”是一个常见的委婉语。| 近义词:understatement, circumlocution | 反义词: dysphemism |词性: 名词
