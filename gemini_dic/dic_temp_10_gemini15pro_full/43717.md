credulence: /ˈkrɛdʒələns/ | n. | 轻信，相信 | a willingness to believe or accept something as true, especially without proof. | Example: The credulence given to astrology by some people is surprising.  一些人对占星术的轻信令人惊讶。| Example: He lent credulence to her story by nodding seriously. 他认真地点头，使她的故事显得可信。 | Synonyms: belief, faith, trust, acceptance | Antonyms: skepticism, disbelief, doubt, distrust | Noun

credulous: /ˈkrɛdʒʊləs/ | adj. | 轻信的，易受骗的 | having or showing too great a readiness to believe things. | Example:  A credulous person is easily fooled by scams. 一个轻信的人很容易被骗局愚弄。 | Example:  She was so credulous that she believed everything he said. 她如此轻信，以至于相信了他所说的一切。 | Synonyms: gullible, naive, trusting, unwary | Antonyms: skeptical, suspicious, cynical, wary | Adjective
