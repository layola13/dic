boulevard: /ˈbuːləvɑːrd/ (US) /ˈbuːləvɑːd/ (UK)| n. |林荫大道，大马路|A broad, often landscaped avenue, typically one with a median strip.|We strolled along the boulevard, enjoying the shade of the trees. 我们沿着林荫大道漫步，享受着树荫。|synonyms: avenue, promenade, thoroughfare|antonyms: alley, lane|noun

Etymology: From French boulevard, originally meaning "bulwark," from Middle Dutch bolwerc "rampart." The meaning shifted from "rampart" to "broad street on the site of a former rampart" as city walls were demolished and replaced with wide avenues.
