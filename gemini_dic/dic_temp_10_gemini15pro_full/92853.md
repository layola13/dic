ictus: /ˈɪktəs/ | n. | 击打；中风；节拍；强音 | A sudden blow or stroke. Also refers to a stroke or seizure. In music, it refers to a beat or stressed note. | He suffered an ictus and lost the use of his left side. 他中风了，左半身失去了知觉。

The ictus of the conductor's baton brought the orchestra to a crescendo. 指挥棒的落下使乐队达到了高潮。 | synonyms: stroke, beat, pulse, accent | antonyms: pause, rest | noun

Etymology: From Latin ictus ("a blow, stroke"), past participle of īcere ("to strike").
