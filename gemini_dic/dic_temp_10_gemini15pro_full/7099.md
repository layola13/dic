anesthetize: /əˈnɛsθəˌtaɪz/ (US) /əˈniːsθətaɪz/ (UK)| vt.|麻醉，使麻木|To induce a loss of sensation, especially of pain, by administering an anesthetic.|The surgeon anesthetized the patient before the operation. 外科医生在手术前给病人麻醉了。|Synonyms: numb, desensitize, sedate|Antonyms: stimulate, sensitize|Verb

anesthesia: /ˌænəsˈθiːʒə/ (US) /ˌænəsˈθiːziə/ (UK)| n.|麻醉，麻木|Loss of sensation, especially of pain, induced by an anesthetic.|The patient was under general anesthesia during the surgery. 病人在手术期间处于全身麻醉状态。|Synonyms: numbness, insensibility|Antonyms: sensation, feeling|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek ἀναισθησία (anaisthesia)  meaning “lack of sensation,” from ἀν- (an-) "not" +  αἴσθησις (aisthēsis) "sensation, feeling."
