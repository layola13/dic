affect:/əˈfekt/ | v. | 影响；感动；(疾病)侵袭 | To produce a change in; to influence | The weather affects my mood. 天气影响我的心情。 | influence, impact, touch | leave unaffected, be immune to | verb

affect:/ˈæfekt/ | n. | 情感；感情 | Feeling or emotion, especially as shown by facial expression or body language | He displayed a flat affect.  他面无表情。| feeling, emotion, sentiment | apathy, indifference | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *affectus, past participle of afficere (“to do something to; to act on; to exert influence on”), from ad + facere ("to do, to make").
