crookish: /ˈkrʊkɪʃ/ | adj. | 弯曲的，不诚实的 | dishonest or likely to be dishonest; not straight |  The salesman had a crookish smile that made me distrust him.  那个销售员的笑容奸诈，让我不信任他。|  The path was crookish and difficult to follow. 这条小路弯弯曲曲的，很难走。 | synonyms: dishonest, deceitful, fraudulent, shady, devious, bent, twisted, winding | antonyms: honest, straightforward, upright, straight | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From crook (meaning "bent or curved") + -ish (suffix forming adjectives).  The original meaning referred to something physically bent or twisted. The dishonest sense developed later, perhaps from the image of a thief with a bent back trying to hide.
