pantheon: /ˈpænθiːən/ (US) /ˈpænθɪən/ (UK)| n. |万神殿；伟人祠；（某领域的）一批杰出人物|A temple dedicated to all the gods, or a building in which the illustrious dead of a nation are buried or commemorated; all the gods of a people or religion collectively; a group of illustrious or notable persons or things.|例句：The Pantheon in Rome is a remarkable building. 罗马的万神殿是一座非凡的建筑。

例句：The novel features a pantheon of colorful characters. 这部小说以众多丰富多彩的人物为特色。|近义词：temple, shrine, hall of fame|反义词: None|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French panthéon, from Ancient Greek πάνθειον (pántheion, “temple of all the gods”), from πᾶν (pân, “all”) + θεῖος (theîos, “of or belonging to a god; divine”).
