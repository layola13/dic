equivocatingly:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪtɪŋli/
| adv. |含糊其辞地，模棱两可地|Speaking in a way that is intentionally unclear or ambiguous, often to avoid committing to a particular position or to deceive.|He answered equivocatingly, hoping to avoid a direct confrontation. 他含糊其辞地回答，希望避免直接冲突。|例句：She equivocatingly agreed to help, but I wasn't sure she actually would. 她含糊其辞地同意帮忙，但我不知道她是否真的会。|近义词：ambiguously, evasively, vaguely|反义词：clearly, directly, frankly|词性:副词

equivocate:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪt/
| v. |含糊其辞，支吾|To use ambiguous language so as to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself.|The politician equivocated when asked about his stance on the issue. 当被问及他对这个问题的立场时，这位政客含糊其辞。|例句：He equivocated about his plans for the future, leaving everyone confused. 他对未来的计划含糊其辞，让每个人都很困惑。|近义词：prevaricate, hedge, waffle|反义词：be honest, be straightforward, be direct|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin aequivocatus, past participle of aequivocare ("to call by the same name, use words ambiguously"), from Latin aequus ("equal") + vocare ("to call").
