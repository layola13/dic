oversocialized:/ˌoʊvərˈsoʊʃəlaɪzd/
adjective
过度社会化的
Excessively or inappropriately socialized; having internalized social norms and expectations to an excessive degree, often leading to a lack of individuality or authenticity.  Being overly concerned with social acceptance and conforming to social norms.
He seemed oversocialized, always saying the "right" thing and never expressing a truly personal opinion. 他似乎过度社会化了，总是说“正确”的话，从不表达真正的个人意见。

The child was so oversocialized that she never misbehaved, even when it would have been appropriate.  这个孩子过度社会化了，以至于她从不行为不端，即使在适当的时候也是如此。


近义词: conventional, conformist, compliant
反义词: individualistic, nonconformist, rebellious
词性: adjective

词源:  The word is formed by combining the prefix "over-" (meaning "excessively" or "too much") with the adjective "socialized" (meaning "trained to behave in a way that is acceptable to society").
