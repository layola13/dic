nonjuring:/nɒnˈdʒʊərɪŋ/ | adj. | 拒绝宣誓的；不宣誓效忠的 | Refusing to take an oath, especially the oath of allegiance.  Specifically referring to those members of the Church of England who refused to take the oath of allegiance to William and Mary after the Glorious Revolution of 1688. |  The nonjuring bishops were deprived of their sees.  拒绝宣誓效忠的主教被剥夺了教区。| Synonyms: recusant, dissident | Antonyms: conformist, loyalist | adjective
