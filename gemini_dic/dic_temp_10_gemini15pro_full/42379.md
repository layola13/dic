counterbidding:/ˌkaʊntərˈbɪdɪŋ/
n.
加价；反竞价；竞争性出价
The act of making a higher bid than someone else, especially at an auction.
There was a fierce round of counterbidding before the painting was finally sold.  在画作最终售出之前，经历了一轮激烈的竞价。
counterbidding:/ˌkaʊntərˈbɪdɪŋ/
n.
竞争性报价
The act of offering a better price than a competitor for a product or service.
The company engaged in aggressive counterbidding to win the contract.  该公司进行了激进的竞争性报价以赢得合同。

counterbidding:/ˌkaʊntərˈbɪdɪŋ/
n.
还价；对抗性出价
The act of making a bid in response to another bid, often in a competitive situation.
The counterbidding between the two companies drove up the price significantly. 两家公司之间的还价大大提高了价格。

counterbidding:/ˌkaʊntərˈbɪdɪŋ/
n.
(比喻)竞争；对抗
(Figurative) The act of competing or opposing someone.
There's been a lot of counterbidding for power within the organization.  该组织内部一直在争夺权力。


近义词:bidding, outbidding, competition
反义词:concession, acquiescence
词性:noun
