niggle:/ˈnɪɡəl/| v.|使烦恼；使焦虑；不断地烦扰；挑剔；吹毛求疵|To worry or irritate someone slightly, especially for a long time; to find fault with minor details. |The fact that she hadn't replied to his email niggled him all day.  她没有回复他的电子邮件这件事让他整天都很烦。|Synonyms: bother, irritate, annoy, vex, irk, pester, pick at|Antonyms: soothe, comfort, please, delight|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *nigelen, frequentative of *nigen (“to gnaw, nibble”), from Old English *gnīgan (“to gnaw, rub, chafe”), from Proto-Germanic *gnīganą (“to gnaw, rub”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰneH₁- (“to rub, grind”). Cognate with Dutch nijgen (“to rub”), German kneifen (“to pinch”).
