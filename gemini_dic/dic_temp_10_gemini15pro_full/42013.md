costermongers:/ˈkɒstəməŋɡəz/ (UK), /ˈkɑːstərmɑːŋɡərz/ (US)| n. |水果小贩；菜贩|A person who sells fruit, vegetables, or other goods from a stall or barrow in the street.|The costermongers called out their wares, attracting customers with their loud voices. 水果小贩大声叫卖他们的商品，用响亮的声音吸引顾客。|Synonyms: street vendor, hawker, peddler|Antonyms: shopkeeper, retailer|Noun

Etymology->
From "costard" (a type of large apple) + "monger" (a dealer or trader).  The word originally referred specifically to apple sellers, but broadened over time to include sellers of other fruits and vegetables.
