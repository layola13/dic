idolatrize: /aɪˈdɑːlətraɪz/ (US) /aɪˈdɒlətraɪz/ (UK)
| vt. | 崇拜；奉为偶像；盲目崇拜 | To admire, revere, or love someone or something to an excessive or extreme degree, bordering on worship. |  She idolizes her older brother. 她把她哥哥奉为偶像。|  He idolized the famous actress, collecting all her movies and interviews. 他崇拜那位著名女演员，收集了她所有的电影和采访。 | venerate, worship, adore, exalt | criticize, condemn, disparage | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *idolatrare, from Latin idololatria (“idolatry”), from Greek εἰδωλολατρεία (eidōlolatreia, “worship of idols”), from εἴδωλον (eídōlon, “image, idol”) + λατρεία (latreia, “worship, service”).
