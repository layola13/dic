misleading:/mɪsˈliːdɪŋ/| adj.|误导性的，使人产生误解的|Giving the wrong idea or impression.|The advertisement was misleading, as the product did not perform as advertised. 这则广告具有误导性，因为该产品并不像广告宣传的那样有效。|Synonyms: deceptive, fallacious, spurious, dishonest|Antonyms: truthful, accurate, honest, forthright|adjective

mislead:/mɪsˈliːd/| vt.|误导，使人产生误解|To cause someone to believe something that is not true.|The map misled us, and we ended up lost in the woods. 这张地图误导了我们，结果我们在树林里迷路了。|Synonyms: deceive, delude, trick, fool|Antonyms: guide, enlighten, inform, direct|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *misleden*, from Old English *mislēdan* (“to misguide”), from *mis-* (“wrongly”) + *lēdan* (“to lead”).
