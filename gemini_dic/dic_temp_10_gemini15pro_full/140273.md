offuscate:/ˈɒfəskeɪt/ (UK), /ˈɑːfəskeɪt/ (US)| v. |使模糊，使混乱，使困惑|To make something unclear, obscure, or unintelligible.|The writer's style is so dense and convoluted that it obfuscates rather than clarifies the main points.  这位作者的风格如此密集和复杂，以至于它混淆而不是阐明了要点。|The government tried to obfuscate the truth about the scandal. 政府试图掩盖丑闻的真相。|近义词：obscure, confuse, bewilder, muddle, cloud|反义词：clarify, elucidate, illuminate|词性:动词

offuscation:/ˌɒfəsˈkeɪʃn/ (UK), /ˌɑːfəsˈkeɪʃn/ (US)| n. |模糊，混乱，困惑|The act of making something unclear, obscure, or unintelligible.|The report was filled with legal obfuscation. 报告充满了法律上的含糊其辞。|The company's financial statements were deliberately designed for obfuscation. 该公司的财务报表是故意设计成难以理解的。|近义词：obscurity, confusion, bewilderment|反义词：clarity, lucidity|词性:名词

词源:
From Late Latin *obfuscatus, past participle of obfuscare “to darken,” from Latin ob- “over, against” + fuscare “to darken,” from fuscus “dark, dusky.”
