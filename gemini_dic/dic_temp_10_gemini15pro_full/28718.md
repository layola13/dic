carmine:/ˈkɑːrmɪn, ˈkɑːrmaɪn/| n. adj.|胭脂红，深红色|A vivid crimson pigment made from cochineal insects, used as a dye and in paints and cosmetics.  Also used as an adjective to describe something of this color.|例句：The artist used carmine to paint the sunset. 艺术家用胭脂红来画日落。|近义词：crimson, scarlet, ruby, vermilion|反义词:pale, light|词性:名词，形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *carmin*, from Medieval Latin *carminium*, from Arabic *qirmiz* (“kermes”), from Sanskrit *kṛmi* (“worm, insect”) (referring to the cochineal insect from which the dye is derived). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*kʷŕ̥mis*.  Related to crimson.
