dynast: /ˈdaɪnəst/ | n. | 君主，王朝成员 | A ruler or member of a ruling family, especially one who holds power by inheritance. |  The Ming dynast ruled China for centuries. 明朝统治者统治了中国几个世纪。|  The young dynast was eager to prove himself worthy of the throne.  这位年轻的王朝成员渴望证明自己配得上王位。| synonym: ruler, monarch, sovereign, emperor, king, queen | antonym: subject, commoner | noun

dynasty: /ˈdaɪnəsti/ | n. | 王朝，世家 | A line of hereditary rulers of a particular country.  A succession of people from the same family who play a prominent role in business, politics, or another field. | The Tang dynasty is considered a golden age in Chinese history. 唐朝被认为是中国历史上的黄金时代。 | The Kennedy dynasty has had a profound impact on American politics. 肯尼迪家族对美国政治产生了深远的影响。 | synonym: reign, empire, house, lineage | antonym: anarchy, disorder | noun
