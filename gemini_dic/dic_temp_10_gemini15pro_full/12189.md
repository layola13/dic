as-it-were:/ˌæz ɪt ˈwɜːr/
adverb
仿佛；可以说是；好像
Used to indicate that a word or phrase is not exactly accurate but is close enough to be used.  It implies a slight reservation or hesitancy about the appropriateness of the term used.
He became, as it were, a father to her. 他可以说是她的父亲。
Synonyms: so to speak, in a way, sort of, kind of
Antonyms: precisely, exactly, literally
词性: 副词
