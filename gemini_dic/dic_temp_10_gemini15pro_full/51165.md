despotize: /ˈdɛspətaɪz/ | v. | 暴虐统治，专横对待，实行专制 | To rule with absolute power, often in a cruel and oppressive way.  To deprive of freedom or individuality. | The king despotized over his subjects, demanding absolute obedience. 国王对他的臣民实行专制统治，要求绝对服从。

The dictator despotized the country, suppressing all dissent. 独裁者对国家实行专制统治，压制所有异议。| 近义词: tyrannize, oppress, dictate | 反义词: liberate, empower, democratize | 词性: 动词

despot:/ˈdɛspɒt/ | n. | 专制君主，暴君 | A ruler or other person who holds absolute power, typically one who exercises it in a cruel or oppressive way. | The despot ruled with an iron fist, silencing any opposition.  暴君用铁腕统治，压制任何反对意见。

He was accused of being a despot in his management style. 他被指责在其管理风格上是个暴君。| 近义词: tyrant, dictator, autocrat | 反义词: democrat | 词性: 名词

despotic: /dɪˈspɒtɪk/ | adj. | 专制的，暴虐的 | Of or typical of a despot; tyrannical. | The despotic regime crushed any attempts at rebellion. 专制政权粉碎了任何叛乱的企图。

His despotic behavior alienated his employees. 他的专制行为疏远了他的员工。| 近义词: tyrannical, authoritarian, dictatorial | 反义词: democratic, liberal | 词性: 形容词

despotism: /ˈdɛspətɪzəm/ | n. | 专制统治，暴政 | The exercise of absolute power, especially in a cruel and oppressive way. | The country suffered under years of despotism.  这个国家在多年的专制统治下饱受苦难。

The people revolted against the despotism of the ruling class. 人民反抗统治阶级的专制统治。| 近义词: tyranny, dictatorship, autocracy | 反义词: democracy, freedom | 词性: 名词


词源: From French *despotisme*, from Late Latin *despoticus*, from Greek *despotikos* "of a master," from *despotes* "master, lord," from *des- "house" + *potis* "powerful, able."
