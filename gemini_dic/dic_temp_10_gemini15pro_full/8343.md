anti-platonism:/ˌæntiˈpleɪtənɪzəm/| n. |反柏拉图主义|The philosophical doctrine that denies the existence of abstract objects (like numbers, forms, or universals) outside of the mind. It opposes Plato's Theory of Forms.|Example: Anti-platonism argues that mathematical objects are not discovered, but invented. 反柏拉图主义认为，数学对象不是被发现的，而是被发明的。

Example:  His anti-platonism led him to reject the idea of a universal "good." 他的反柏拉图主义使他拒绝接受普遍“善”的概念。|近义词：nominalism, materialism |反义词: Platonism |词性:noun
