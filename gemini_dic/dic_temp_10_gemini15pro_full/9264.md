antipatriots:/ˌæntiˈpeɪtriəts/
n.|反爱国者，不爱国的人|People who oppose their own country's policies or ideals, sometimes to the point of actively working against them.  They are not necessarily traitors, but they express strong dissent. |Some people labeled him an antipatriot for his criticism of the war.  有些人因为他批评战争而给他贴上不爱国者的标签。|
antipatriots:|n.|反爱国者，不爱国的人|Those who actively oppose the interests or policies of their own country, often considered disloyal or subversive.|His anti-government speeches led many to brand him an antipatriot. 他的反政府言论导致许多人给他打上不爱国者的标签。|
近义词:dissident, traitor, rebel, nonconformist
反义词:patriot, nationalist, loyalist
词性:noun (plural)
词源:  From anti- "against" + patriot.  The prefix "anti-" is of Greek origin. "Patriot" comes from Old French patriote, ultimately from Late Latin patriota "fellow countryman," from Greek patriotes "fellow countryman," from patrios "of one's fathers," from pater "father."
