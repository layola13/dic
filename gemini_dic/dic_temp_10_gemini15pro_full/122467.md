mobilizing: /ˈmoʊbəˌlaɪzɪŋ/ (US) /ˈməʊbɪlaɪzɪŋ/ (UK)
|v. |动员; 调动; |Making something movable or capable of movement.  Putting resources into motion for a particular purpose. Assembling and preparing something, like troops or resources, for action. |The government is mobilizing troops to the border. 政府正在向边境调动军队。
|activate, assemble, deploy, marshal, rally|demobilize, disband, stand down|verb
mobilizing: /ˈmoʊbəˌlaɪzɪŋ/ (US) /ˈməʊbɪlaɪzɪŋ/ (UK)
|adj. |动员的; 调动的; |Characterized by or relating to the act of mobilizing. |The mobilizing force of the protest was impressive. 抗议活动的动员力量令人印象深刻。
|activating, assembling, deploying|demobilizing, disbanding|adjective
