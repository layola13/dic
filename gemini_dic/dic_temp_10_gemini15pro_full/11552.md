aristocrat: /ˈærɪstəkræt/ (US) /ˈærɪstəkræt/ (UK)| n.|贵族；贵族成员|A member of the aristocracy; a nobleman or noblewoman.  A person who behaves like a member of the aristocracy.|He lived like an aristocrat, with servants and a large estate. 他过着贵族般的生活，有仆人，还有一大片地产。|Synonyms: nobleman, noblewoman, lord, lady, peer, patrician, blue blood|Antonyms: commoner, peasant, plebeian|noun


aristocratic: /ˌærɪstəˈkrætɪk/ (US) /ˌærɪstəˈkrætɪk/ (UK)| adj.|贵族的；贵族式的|Belonging to, typical of, or resembling the aristocracy.|She has an aristocratic bearing. 她举止优雅，很有贵族气质。|Synonyms: noble, highborn, patrician, blue-blooded|Antonyms: common, plebeian, lowborn|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *aristocrate*, from Greek *aristokratēs* (meaning "ruled by the best"), from *aristos* (meaning "best") + *kratēs* (meaning "ruler, power").
