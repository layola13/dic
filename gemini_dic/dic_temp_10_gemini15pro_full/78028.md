gentrify: /ˈdʒɛntrɪfaɪ/ (US) /ˈdʒɛntrɪfaɪ/ (UK)
|v. |使贵族化；使绅士化；中产阶级化|to renovate and improve a house or district so that it conforms to middle-class taste. This often displaces the poorer residents.|The old neighborhood is being gentrified with new condos and upscale shops.  老社区正在被新的公寓和高档商店中产阶级化。|synonyms:  renovate, upgrade, improve, revitalize, upscale|antonyms:  degrade, decline, deteriorate|verb


gentrification: /ˌdʒɛntrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌdʒɛntrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ (UK)
|n. |贵族化；绅士化；中产阶级化|The process of renovating and improving a house or district so that it conforms to middle-class taste.|The gentrification of the area has led to a sharp increase in property values.  该地区的贵族化导致房产价值急剧上升。|synonyms:  urban renewal, redevelopment, revitalization|antonyms:  urban decay, blight|noun
