distraught:/dɪˈstrɔːt/| adj.|心烦意乱的，极度不安的|So upset and worried that you cannot think clearly.|She was distraught with grief after her son's death. 她儿子去世后，她悲痛欲绝。|近义词：distressed, frantic, agitated, upset|反义词：calm, composed, serene|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Middle English *distraught, *distracted, past participle of distracten (“to distract”), from Old French destrait, past participle of destraire (“to distract”), from Latin distractus, past participle of distrahere (“to pull apart, divide, distract”), from dis- (“apart”) + trahere (“to draw, pull”).
