heliotype:/ˈhiːliəˌtaɪp/ | n. | 日光照片，日光版 | A photomechanical print made by exposing a gelatin film to light and hardening it to create a printing surface. | Example: The museum displayed a collection of 19th-century heliotypes.  博物馆展出了一系列19世纪的日光照片。| Example: Heliotypes were an important step in the development of photography. 日光照片是摄影发展的重要一步。|Synonym: photogravure, collotype |Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "original artwork") |noun
