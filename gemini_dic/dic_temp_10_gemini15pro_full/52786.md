dilettanteish:/ˌdɪləˈtɑːntiʃ/ | adj. | 浅薄的；业余爱好的；半吊子的 |  Superficial or amateurish, characteristic of a dilettante; someone who dabbles in an art or pursuit without serious commitment or knowledge. | He has a dilettanteish interest in painting, never going beyond copying famous works. 他对绘画只有浅薄的兴趣，从未超越过临摹名作。 | 近义词：amateurish, superficial, dabbling, shallow, frivolous | 反义词：professional, expert, skilled, accomplished, proficient | 词性:形容词

词源: From Italian *dilettante* "lover of the arts," from present participle of *dilettare* "to delight," from Latin *delectare*. The "-ish" suffix transforms the noun into an adjective indicating the quality of being like a dilettante.
