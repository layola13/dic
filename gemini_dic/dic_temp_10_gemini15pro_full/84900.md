hankerings: /ˈhæŋkərɪŋz/
| n. |渴望，热望|A strong desire or craving for something.|She had persistent hankerings for a life less ordinary. 她一直渴望过一种不平凡的生活。|
|例句：He had hankerings for the days of his youth. 他渴望回到青春岁月。|
|例句：My hankerings for chocolate are getting worse. 我对巧克力的渴望越来越强烈了。|
|近义词：cravings, yearnings, longings, desires|
|反义词：aversions, dislikes|
|词性：名词（复数）|


hanker: /ˈhæŋkər/
| v. |渴望，热望|To have a strong desire or craving for something.|He hankered after a simpler life in the country. 他渴望在乡下过上更简单的生活。|
|例句：She hankered for a return to her homeland. 她渴望回到故土。|
|例句：I've been hankering for a good pizza all week. 我整个星期都渴望吃一顿美味的披萨。|
|近义词：crave, yearn, long, desire|
|反义词：dislike, detest|
|词性：动词|


词源:
From Middle English *hankeren, from Old Norse haŋk "to hang, dangle," of uncertain origin. The original sense may have been "to hang about, linger," hence "to linger in expectation," and thus "to yearn."
