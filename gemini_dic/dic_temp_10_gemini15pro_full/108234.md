laughing-at:/ˈlɑːfɪŋ æt/ | adj./prep. phrase | 嘲笑，因…而发笑 | The act of laughing at someone or something, often implying mockery or derision. |  They were laughing at his clumsy attempt to dance. 他们嘲笑他笨拙的舞姿。

|  It's not kind to laugh at people's misfortunes.  嘲笑别人的不幸是不友善的。 | 近义词: mocking, ridiculing, deriding | 反义词: admiring, respecting, praising | 词性:  prepositional phrase (functions as an adjective)

laughing:/ˈlɑːfɪŋ/ | adj. |  笑的，引人发笑的 | Showing or feeling amusement or pleasure. | The laughing children enjoyed the clown's antics.  孩子们笑着欣赏小丑的滑稽动作。 | The movie had a laughing audience.  这部电影让观众捧腹大笑。| 近义词:  amused, mirthful, cheerful | 反义词:  sad, gloomy, serious | 词性: adjective


at:/æt/ | prep. | 在，向，朝 | Expressing location or direction.  |  The dog barked at the stranger. 狗对着陌生人吠叫。 |  He threw the ball at the target. 他把球扔向目标。 | 近义词: to, toward, in | 反义词: from, away from | 词性: preposition
