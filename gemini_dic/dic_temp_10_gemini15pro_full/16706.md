barring out:/ˈbɑːrɪŋ aʊt/
n.
关在外面，阻止…进入；把…关在外面（尤指学生将老师关在教室外以示抗议）
The act of students locking their teachers out of the classroom as a form of protest.
The pupils staged a barring out on the last day of term. 学生们在学期最后一天把老师关在了教室外面。
近义词：lockout, exclusion
反义词：admittance, inclusion
词性：名词

barring:/ˈbɑːrɪŋ/
prep.
除…之外；除非
Except for; unless.
Barring any unforeseen circumstances, we should arrive on time. 除非发生不可预见的情况，我们应该会准时到达。
近义词：except for, excluding, save
反义词：including
词性：介词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "bar" (meaning to obstruct or prevent entry) + "out" (indicating exclusion or expulsion). The verb "bar" comes from Old French "barre" (a bar or barrier), ultimately from Late Latin "barra."  "Out" comes from Old English "ūt."
