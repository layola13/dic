moodishly:/ˈmuːdɪʃli/| adv.|喜怒无常地，闷闷不乐地|In a way that shows changing moods, often unhappy moods.  Reflecting or expressing a sullen or gloomy mood.|He stared moodishly out the window. 他闷闷不乐地盯着窗外。|近义词：sullenly, gloomily, morosely, petulantly|反义词：cheerfully, happily, brightly|词性:副词
Etymology: From moody + -ly.  Moody comes from Middle English *modi, from Old English *mōdig "proud, arrogant, brave," from mōd "mind, mood, heart, spirit." The meaning shifted in Middle English to "changeable in mood, fickle."
