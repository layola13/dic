anticly: /ˈæntɪkli/ | adv. | 古怪地；滑稽地；可笑地 | In an antic manner; playfully mischievous or grotesque.  |He danced anticly around the room, making everyone laugh. 他滑稽地在房间里跳舞，逗得大家都笑了。 |  The jester anticly juggled five balls at once.  小丑滑稽地同时耍五个球。 | Synonyms: playfully, whimsically, comically, grotesquely, bizarrely | Antonyms: seriously, solemnly, gravely | Part of Speech: adverb

Etymology:
From Middle English *antiklich, from Old French antiquement (“strangely, oddly”), from antique (“ancient”) + -ment (adverbial suffix). Ultimately from Latin antiquus (“old, ancient”). The sense shifted from "in an old-fashioned way" to "in a strange, grotesque way."
