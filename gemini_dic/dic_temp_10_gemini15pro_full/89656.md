hooky's:/ˈhʊkiz/ | n. | 逃学，旷课（通常指学生的逃学行为） |  Hooky's is the possessive form of "hooky," indicating ownership or association.  It's not standard English, and its usage is informal and primarily found in colloquial speech, possibly referring to a place or time associated with playing hooky. "Hooky" itself derives from the Dutch word "hoekje," meaning "corner" or "hiding place." |  "Let's meet at hooky's after school." (Meaning a designated place for skipping school). | 近义词: truancy, absenteeism | 反义词: attendance, presence | 词性: 名词
