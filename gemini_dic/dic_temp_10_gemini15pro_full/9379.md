antiquary:/ˈæntɪkwəri/| n.| 古董收藏家；古物研究者；研究古代风俗习惯的人|A person who studies or collects antiquities or is an expert in the history and culture of ancient times.|He was a renowned antiquary with a vast collection of Roman artifacts. 他是一位著名的古董收藏家，收藏了大量的罗马文物。|Synonyms: collector, connoisseur, historian, archaeologist|Antonyms: modernist, innovator|noun

antiquary:/ˈæntɪkwəri/| n.|古董; 古物|Something belonging to ancient times.|The museum housed many valuable antiquaries.  博物馆收藏了许多珍贵的古董。|Synonyms: antique, relic, artifact|Antonyms: modern artifact, contemporary object|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English:  *antiquarie*, from Old French: *antiquaire*, from Latin: *antiquarius* ‘belonging to antiquity’, from *antiquus* ‘old’.
