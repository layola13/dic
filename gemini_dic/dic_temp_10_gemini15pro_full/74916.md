fretfully:/ˈfrɛtfʊli/ | adv. | 烦躁地；焦急地 | In a way that shows irritation or worry. | She paced the room fretfully, waiting for the news. 她焦急地在房间里踱来踱去，等待着消息。|例句：The child cried fretfully because he was hungry. 孩子饿得烦躁地哭了起来。|近义词：irritably, anxiously, nervously |反义词：calmly, patiently |词性:副词

fretfully:/ˈfrɛtfʊli/ | adv. | 烦躁地；焦急地 | Characterized by or expressing irritation and worry. | He fretfully adjusted his tie, aware of the time ticking away.  他烦躁地调整着领带，意识到时间一分一秒地流逝。|近义词：restlessly, peevishly |反义词：peacefully, serenely |词性:副词

词源:
From *fret* (verb) + *-fully* (adverbial suffix).  The verb *fret* comes from Old English *fretan* "to devour, consume," related to Old Norse *freta* "to gnaw, eat," ultimately from Proto-Germanic *fretaną*.  The meaning shifted over time to "wear away, corrode," then to "agitate, worry."
