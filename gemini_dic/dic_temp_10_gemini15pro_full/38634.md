conculcate:/kɑnˈkʌlkeɪt/ | v. |反复灌输；谆谆教诲 | To impress something upon the mind of another by frequent repetition or insistent urging. |  Parents often conculcate moral values in their children. 父母经常向孩子灌输道德价值观。 |  inculcate, indoctrinate | disregard, overlook | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin conculcare "to tread under foot, trample down," from com- "together" + calcare "to tread," from calc- "heel."  The meaning shifted from physical trampling to a metaphorical trampling of ideas or principles into someone's mind.
