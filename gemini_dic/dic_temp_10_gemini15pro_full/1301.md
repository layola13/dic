accountings: /əˈkaʊntɪŋz/
noun
会计；账目；决算
The practice or profession of maintaining financial records, especially of businesses.  The system of recording and summarizing business and financial transactions and analyzing, verifying, and reporting the results.
The company's accountings are audited annually.  该公司的账目每年都会被审计。

The accountings for the project showed a significant loss.  这个项目的决算显示出巨大的亏损。

synonyms: bookkeeping, reckoning, tallying
antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, but consider concepts like "mismanagement," "fraud," or "disorganization" in specific contexts)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "acontance" (counting, reckoning), derived from "aconter" (to count), which in turn comes from Latin "computare" (to compute).  The "-ing" suffix transforms the verb into a noun representing the action or process.
