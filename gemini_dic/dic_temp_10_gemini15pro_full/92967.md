idian:/ˈɪdiən/ | n. | 成语，习语 | A group of words established by usage as having a meaning not deducible from those of the individual words (e.g., rain cats and dogs, see the light). |  Example: "Rain cats and dogs" is a common English idiom.  “倾盆大雨”是一个常见的英语习语。

Example:  Learning idioms can be challenging for language learners. 学习习语对语言学习者来说可能很有挑战性。 | Synonyms: expression, phrase, saying | Antonyms: literalism | noun
