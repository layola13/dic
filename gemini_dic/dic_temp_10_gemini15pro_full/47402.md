deadhouse:/ˈdɛdˌhaʊs/ | n. |停尸房，太平间 | A building or room, typically in a hospital, where dead bodies are kept before burial or cremation. | The body was taken to the deadhouse after the autopsy. 尸体在尸检后被送往停尸房。|  The detective examined the evidence in the deadhouse.  侦探在停尸房检查了证据。 | synonyms: morgue, mortuary | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could consider "living quarters") | noun

deadhouse:/ˈdɛdˌhaʊs/ | adj. | (informal)  沉闷的，死气沉沉的 | (informal)  Lacking life, activity, or excitement. | The party was a deadhouse; everyone just stood around. 派对死气沉沉，每个人都只是站在那里。| The office was a deadhouse on Sundays. 周日办公室里空无一人，死气沉沉。 | synonyms: dull, lifeless, boring | antonyms: lively, exciting, vibrant | adjective
