hypersalinity:/ˌhaɪpər səˈlɪnɪti/ | n. |高盐度，超盐度 | Excessive salinity; a salinity significantly greater than that of normal seawater, typically exceeding 40 parts per thousand. | The hypersalinity of the Dead Sea makes it easy to float. 死海的高盐度使其很容易漂浮。| The hypersalinity of the lake prevents most aquatic life from thriving.  这个湖的高盐度阻止了大多数水生生物的繁衍。 | 近义词: excessive salinity | 反义词: hyposalinity, low salinity | 词性: noun

hypersaline:/ˌhaɪpərˈseɪlaɪn/ | adj. |高盐度的，超盐度的 | Excessively saline; containing an excessive amount of salt. | Hypersaline lakes are often found in arid regions. 高盐湖泊经常出现在干旱地区。|  Hypersaline environments can support specialized organisms called extremophiles. 高盐环境可以支持被称为极端微生物的特殊生物。 | 近义词: excessively salty | 反义词: hyposaline, low salinity | 词性: adjective
