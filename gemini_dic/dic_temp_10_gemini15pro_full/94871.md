in-balance:/ɪnˈbæləns/ | adj. |不平衡的，失衡的 | Lacking balance or equilibrium; not in a stable or steady state. | The ecosystem is in-balance due to pollution. 生态系统由于污染而失衡。| Near Antonyms: balanced, harmonious, stable, steady | Antonyms: unbalanced, unstable, unsteady | adjective


in-balance:/ɪnˈbæləns/ | n. |不平衡，失衡 | The state of lacking balance or equilibrium. | The in-balance of power led to conflict. 权力失衡导致了冲突。| Near Antonyms: balance, harmony, stability, steadiness | Antonyms: unbalance, instability, unsteadiness | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From in- (not) + balance (equilibrium).  The prefix "in-" negates the meaning of "balance". The word "balance" comes from Old French "balance" and ultimately from Late Latin "bilanx" (having two scales), from Latin "bis" (twice) + "lanx" (plate, dish).
