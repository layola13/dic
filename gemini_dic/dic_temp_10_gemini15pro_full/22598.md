bootstrapper:/ˈbuːtˌstræpər/| n.| 引导程序；自举程序；创业者|A person, group, or organization that starts up a business or other activity with limited resources, relying heavily on their own efforts and ingenuity rather than external funding or support; a program that starts up a computer system. |The bootstrapper built his business from the ground up with just a small loan. 这位创业者仅凭一小笔贷款就白手起家。 The bootstrapper program initializes the operating system. 引导程序初始化操作系统。|Synonyms: entrepreneur, founder, initiator, pioneer, self-starter|Antonyms: follower, dependent, freeloader|noun

bootstrapping:/ˈbuːtˌstræpɪŋ/| n.| 自举；引导；自助|The process of starting up a business or other activity with limited resources, relying heavily on one's own efforts and ingenuity; a technique in statistics and machine learning. |Bootstrapping a new business is challenging but rewarding.  创办新企业充满挑战，但也回报丰厚。  Bootstrapping is a common resampling technique. 自举是一种常见的重采样技术。|Synonyms: self-starting, self-financing, self-sufficiency|Antonyms: dependence, reliance, external funding|noun
