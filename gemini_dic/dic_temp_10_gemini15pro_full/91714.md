hyperconstitutionalisms:/ˌhaɪpərˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃənəlɪzəmz/
n.
超宪政主义，极端宪政主义
A set of beliefs or practices that place extreme emphasis on the importance of a constitution, often to the point of hindering effective governance or ignoring practical realities.  It emphasizes strict adherence to the constitution above all other considerations, potentially leading to gridlock or misapplication of constitutional principles.
The political scientist argued that the country's descent into hyperconstitutionalisms had paralyzed its ability to address pressing social issues.  这位政治学家认为，该国陷入极端宪政主义已经使其无力解决紧迫的社会问题。
The senator's unwavering adherence to a narrow interpretation of the constitution, even in the face of a national emergency, was seen as an example of hyperconstitutionalisms.  即使面对国家紧急情况，参议员仍坚持对宪法的狭隘解释，这被视为极端宪政主义的一个例子。
近义词: constitutional fundamentalism, strict constructionism
反义词: constitutional flexibility, pragmatism
词性: noun
