circumlocutional: /ˌsɜːrkəmloʊˈkjuːʃənəl/ | adj. | 迂回的，绕圈子的 | Expressing an idea indirectly, using more words than necessary; wordy. |  He gave a circumlocutional explanation, avoiding the main point. 他给了一个迂回的解释，避开了要点。| The politician gave a circumlocutional answer to the journalist's question.  这位政治家对记者的提问给出了一个绕圈子的回答。| 近义词: periphrastic, roundabout, indirect, evasive | 反义词: direct, concise, succinct | 词性: adjective

circumlocution: /ˌsɜːrkəmloʊˈkjuːʃən/ | n. | 迂回的说法，累赘的表达 | The use of many words where fewer would do, especially in a deliberate attempt to be vague or evasive. | His explanation was filled with circumlocution. 他的解释充满了迂回的表达。|  She used circumlocution to avoid answering the question directly. 她用迂回的措辞来避免直接回答问题。| 近义词: periphrasis, verbosity, prolixity | 反义词: directness, conciseness, brevity | 词性: noun

词源: From Late Latin *circumlocutiōn-, from circumlocūtiō, from circumloquī (“to speak around”)  from circum (“around”) + loquī (“to speak”).
