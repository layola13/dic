bushwhacking: /ˈbʊʃwækɪŋ/ | n. | 丛林穿越；开路；游击战 |  The act of traveling through dense forest or undergrowth, where paths are unclear or non-existent; also, a form of irregular warfare. |  Example: Bushwhacking through the dense jungle was exhausting.  在茂密的丛林中开路令人筋疲力尽。| Example:  The soldiers engaged in bushwhacking tactics against the enemy. 士兵们对敌人采取了游击战术。 | Synonyms:  trailblazing, pioneering, ambushing, guerrilla warfare | Antonyms: following a path, conventional warfare | noun

bushwhack: /ˈbʊʃwæk/ | v. |  在丛林中开路；进行游击战 | To travel through dense forest or undergrowth, clearing a path as one goes; also, to engage in irregular warfare. | Example:  They had to bushwhack their way through the overgrown forest. 他们不得不一路披荆斩棘穿过杂草丛生的森林。| Example: The rebels bushwhacked the government troops. 叛军对政府军进行了游击战。 | Synonyms:  blaze a trail, pioneer, ambush | Antonyms: follow a path, fight conventionally | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "bush" + "whack" (to strike or cut with a sharp blow).  The term originally referred to clearing bushes with a machete or other tool, and later extended to the act of traveling through such terrain and also to irregular warfare tactics.
