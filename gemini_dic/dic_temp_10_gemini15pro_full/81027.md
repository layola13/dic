graduates:/ˈɡrædʒuɪts/ (US), /ˈɡrædʒʊəts/ (UK)| n.| 毕业生|A person who has successfully completed a course of study, especially one leading to a degree or diploma.|The university produces thousands of graduates every year. 这所大学每年培养数千名毕业生。

graduates:/ˈɡrædʒuɪts/ (US), /ˈɡrædʒʊəts/ (UK)| v. (third-person singular simple present graduates, present participle graduating, simple past and past participle graduated)| 毕业|To successfully complete a course of study and receive a degree or diploma; to confer an academic degree or diploma on.|She graduated from Harvard with honors. 她以优异的成绩毕业于哈佛大学。


Etymology: From Latin *graduatus*, past participle of *graduari* "to take steps, walk, go", from *gradus* "step, degree".

Synonyms: alumni (plural), alumnus (masculine singular), alumna (feminine singular),  leavers, completers

Antonyms:  undergraduates, freshmen, sophomores, juniors, seniors (referring to current students)


USAGE->

Simple Present:
The college graduates hundreds of students annually.  这所学院每年有数百名学生毕业。
He graduates next spring. 他明年春天毕业。


Present Continuous:
She is graduating next month. 她下个月毕业。
They are graduating with degrees in engineering. 他们将获得工程学位毕业。


Present Perfect:
He has graduated from university. 他已经大学毕业了。
She has graduated with a degree in law. 她已经获得了法律学位毕业。


Present Perfect Continuous:
He has been graduating summa cum laude. 他一直以最优等成绩毕业。 (This usage is less common for the verb "graduate" but grammatically correct.)
They have been graduating from this program for over 50 years.  50多年来，他们一直从这个项目毕业。 (Referring to the program producing graduates)



Simple Past:
She graduated last year. 她去年毕业了。
They graduated from the same high school. 他们毕业于同一所高中。


Past Continuous:
He was graduating when the pandemic hit.  疫情爆发时他正要毕业。
She was graduating with a degree in medicine. 她当时正要获得医学学位毕业。


Past Perfect:
He had graduated before he started his own business. 他在创业之前就已经毕业了。
She had graduated years before they met. 他们在相遇前几年她就毕业了。


Past Perfect Continuous:
She had been graduating with honors until she failed her final exam.  她一直以优异的成绩毕业，直到期末考试不及格。（This usage is less common for "graduate" but grammatically correct in certain contexts.)
They had been graduating students from this program for decades.  几十年来，他们一直在这个项目中培养毕业生。



Simple Future:
He will graduate next year. 他明年毕业。
She will graduate with a degree in business. 她将获得商科学位毕业。


Future Continuous:
This time next year, she will be graduating. 明年这个时候，她将要毕业。
They will be graduating from the same university. 他们将从同一所大学毕业。


Future Perfect:
By next June, he will have graduated. 到明年六月，他就毕业了。
She will have graduated by the time she turns 22. 到她22岁时，她就已经毕业了。


Future Perfect Continuous:
By next year, she will have been graduating from medical school. 到明年，她将从医学院毕业。(This sounds unnatural.  "She will have graduated from medical school by next year" is preferred)
By 2025, they will have been graduating students from this program for a century. 到2025年，他们将在这个项目中培养学生一个世纪了。（Referring to the program producing graduates)


Passive Voice:
The degrees were conferred upon the graduates. 学位授予了毕业生。
The graduates were congratulated by the faculty.  毕业生们受到了老师们的祝贺。


Negative Sentences:
He hasn't graduated yet. 他还没有毕业。
She didn't graduate from that university. 她没有从那所大学毕业。


Interrogative Sentences:
When did you graduate? 你什么时候毕业的？
Have you graduated yet? 你毕业了吗？


Subjunctive Mood:
If I were to graduate tomorrow, I would celebrate with my family. 如果我明天毕业，我会和家人一起庆祝。
I wish I had graduated sooner. 我希望我早点毕业。
