clanny: /ˈklæni/ | n. | 克兰尼安全灯 | A type of early safety lamp for use in coal mines, invented by William Reid Clanny in 1813. It consisted of a metal lamp surrounded by a cylinder of glass, with the air supply drawn through water below and the exhaust gases passing through water above. | The miners preferred the Davy lamp to the clanny lamp because it gave more light. 矿工们更喜欢戴维灯而不是克兰尼灯，因为它更亮。 | 近义词: safety lamp | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "naked flame" in the context of mining) | 词性: noun
