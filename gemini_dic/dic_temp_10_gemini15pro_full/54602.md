dismast:/dɪsˈmɑːst/ | vt. | 卸下桅杆; 使失去桅杆; | To remove the mast(s) from a ship; to deprive a ship of its mast(s). | The storm dismasted the ship, leaving it adrift.  暴风雨使船失去了桅杆，使其漂流。 | The ship was dismasted in the hurricane.  这艘船在飓风中失去了桅杆。| Synonyms: unmast, derig | Antonyms: rig, mast | verb

dismast:/dɪsˈmɑːst/ | n. | 卸下桅杆的行为; 失去桅杆的状态 | The act of removing a mast or the state of being without a mast. | The dismasting of the ship left the crew stranded.  船只失去桅杆使船员滞留。 | The dismast occurred during a violent storm. 失去桅杆发生在猛烈的暴风雨中。 | Synonyms: unmasting | Antonyms: rigging, masting | noun
