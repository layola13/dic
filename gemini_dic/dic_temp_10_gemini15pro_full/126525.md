nabbed:/næbd/ | v. | 抓住，逮捕，抢夺 | to catch or arrest someone suddenly, or to take something quickly and often dishonestly | The police nabbed the thief as he tried to escape. 警察在小偷试图逃跑时抓住了他。| Synonyms: caught, apprehended, seized, snatched, grabbed | Antonyms: released, freed, missed | verb

Etymology:  From the late 18th century, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Low German *nabben "to grab."
