dory: /ˈdɔːri/ (British), /ˈdɔːri/ (American) | n. | 刺尾鱼；健忘的人 | A small, brightly colored fish found in tropical and subtropical oceans, also known as surgeonfish.  Figuratively, it refers to a forgetful person. | The dory swam among the coral.  刺尾鱼在珊瑚中游来游去。 |  She's such a dory, she forgets everything! 她真是个健忘鬼，什么都忘！ | synonyms: surgeonfish, goldfish (loosely, for appearance), scatterbrain (figurative) | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for the fish. For the figurative meaning:) mindful person, sharp, astute | noun


dory: /ˈdɔːri/ (British), /ˈdɔːri/ (American) | n. | 平底小渔船 | A small, flat-bottomed fishing boat. | The fisherman rowed his dory out to sea. 渔夫划着他的平底小渔船出海。| He used a dory for fishing in the shallow waters. 他用平底小渔船在浅水区捕鱼。| synonyms: skiff, dinghy, rowboat | antonyms: yacht, ship, large vessel | noun
