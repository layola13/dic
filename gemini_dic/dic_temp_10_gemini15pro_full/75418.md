frugalness:/ˈfruːɡəlnəs/| n. |节俭，俭省 |The quality or state of being frugal; thriftiness.  It implies a careful and economical use of resources, avoiding waste and extravagance. |Her frugalness allowed her to save a significant portion of her income. 她的节俭使她能够节省收入的很大一部分。|Synonyms: thrift, economy, parsimony, prudence |Antonyms: extravagance, lavishness, prodigality |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *frugalnesse, from Old French frugalité, from Latin frugalitas (“frugality, temperance, thrift”), from frugālis (“economical, thrifty”), from frux (“fruit, produce, profit, value”).
