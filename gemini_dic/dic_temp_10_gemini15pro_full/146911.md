overinstructs:/ˌoʊvərɪnˈstrʌkts/| v. |过度指导，过分指示|To give excessive or unnecessarily detailed instructions, often to the point of hindering understanding or performance. |The coach overinstructed the team, confusing them with too many details.|近义词：micromanage, overcoach|反义词:underinstruct, neglect|词性:动词

Etymology:
"over-" (prefix) + "instruct"
"over-" from Old English "ofer" meaning "above, beyond, excessively"
"instruct" from Latin "instruere" meaning "to build, arrange, teach, inform"
