booger:/ˈbʊɡər/ (US) /ˈbuːɡə(r)/ (UK)| n.|鼻屎；讨厌鬼；惊吓|A piece of dried nasal mucus.  Informally, it can also refer to an annoying person, or something surprising or scary. |例句：
The little boy had a booger hanging from his nose. 小男孩的鼻子上挂着鼻屎。

He's such a booger, always complaining about everything. 他真是个讨厌鬼，总是抱怨个不停。

|近义词：(for nasal mucus) snot,  (for annoying person) pest, nuisance|反义词: (no direct antonym)|词性:noun

booger:/ˈbʊɡər/ (US) /ˈbuːɡə(r)/ (UK)| vt.|吓唬，使惊恐|To scare or frighten someone. |例句：
The loud noise boogered me. 巨大的噪音吓了我一跳。

Don't try to booger me with that silly mask.  别想用那个傻面具吓唬我。


|近义词：frighten, scare, startle|反义词：comfort, soothe|词性:verb
