bird-in-the-hand:/bɜːrd ɪn ðə hænd/ | n. | 一鸟在手，胜过双鸟在林 | Something that is certain is worth more than something that is possible but not certain.  It refers to the idea that it is better to hold onto something one has than to risk losing it by trying to get something better. | A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush.  把握到手的才实际，不要贪图更多未到手的。|  proverb, certainty, sure thing | risk, gamble, uncertainty | noun
