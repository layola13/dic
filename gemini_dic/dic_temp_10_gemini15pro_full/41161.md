copy-cat: /ˈkɒpiˌkæt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpiˌkæt/ (US)
n.|模仿者，抄袭者|A person who copies another person's actions, words, or ideas, especially to annoy them or to gain an advantage.|He's such a copy-cat; he always copies my homework. 他真是个爱模仿的人；他总是抄我的作业。|imitator, mimic, ape|innovator, original|noun

copy-cat: /ˈkɒpiˌkæt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpiˌkæt/ (US)
adj.|模仿的，抄袭的|Made or done in imitation of something else.|She wore a copy-cat version of the designer dress. 她穿了一件模仿名牌的裙子。|imitative, unoriginal|original, innovative|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
Early 20th century: probably from copy + cat, perhaps influenced by the playful way in which cats will imitate each other's movements.
