discreditable: /dɪsˈkrɛdɪtəbəl/
adjective
丢脸的，不光彩的
bringing discredit; shameful.
例句：His discreditable conduct led to his dismissal. 他的不光彩行为导致他被解雇。

例句：It was a discreditable affair for all concerned. 这对所有相关人员来说都是一件不光彩的事情。

近义词：shameful, disgraceful, dishonorable, ignominious, reprehensible
反义词：creditable, honorable, respectable, praiseworthy
词性: adjective


discredit: /dɪsˈkrɛdɪt/
noun
丧失名誉，耻辱
loss or damage to a person's reputation; disgrace.
例句：The scandal brought discredit on the whole industry.  这起丑闻给整个行业带来了耻辱。

例句：His actions brought discredit upon his family. 他的行为给他的家庭带来了耻辱。

近义词：disgrace, dishonor, shame, ignominy, opprobrium
反义词：credit, honor, reputation, esteem
词性: noun


discredit: /dɪsˈkrɛdɪt/
verb
使丧失名誉，败坏名声，使不可信
harm the good reputation of.
例句：The politician was discredited by the scandal.  这位政治家因丑闻而名誉扫地。

例句：Attempts to discredit the witness failed.  试图败坏证人名声的企图失败了。

近义词：disgrace, dishonor, shame, tarnish, defame
反义词：credit, honor, praise, commend
词性: verb



ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *discreditable*, from *dis-* (not, opposite of) + *creditable* (worthy of belief, bringing honor).  *Creditable* ultimately derives from Latin *crēdere* (to believe).
