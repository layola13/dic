guardant:/ˈɡɑːrdənt/| adj.| 警惕的，守护的；(纹章学)面向观察者的|Facing the observer; vigilant, watchful.  A term used in heraldry to describe an animal shown full-faced. |The lion on the shield is guardant. 盾牌上的狮子是面向观察者的。|近义词：vigilant, watchful, alert|反义词：unaware, inattentive|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Old French *guardant (Modern French gardant), present participle of garder “to guard,” from Frankish *wardōn “to watch,” from Proto-Germanic *wardōną “to watch,” from Proto-Indo-European *wer- “to perceive, watch out for.”
