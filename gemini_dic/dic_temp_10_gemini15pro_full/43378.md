cranioscopy:/kreɪniˈɒskəpi/ | n. |颅相学|The study of the shape and size of the skull as a supposed indication of character and mental abilities.|Cranioscopy was a popular pseudoscience in the 19th century.| 近义词: phrenology | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but consider: scientific neurology) | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From cranio- (Greek kranion, meaning "skull") + -scopy (Greek skopein, meaning "to view, examine").
