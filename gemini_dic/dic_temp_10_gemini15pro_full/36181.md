coffle:/ˈkɒfəl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːfəl/ (US)| n.| 枷锁，镣铐；一队被镣铐锁在一起的人（尤指奴隶）|A group of people, especially slaves, chained together; a shackle or fetter.|The coffle of slaves was marched to the auction block.  这队奴隶被押送到拍卖场。|Synonyms: chain gang, slave train|Antonyms: freedom, liberation|Noun

coffle:/ˈkɒfəl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːfəl/ (US)| vt.| 用镣铐锁住|To fasten or restrain with shackles or fetters.|The slavers coffled the captives together.  奴隶贩子把俘虏们用镣铐锁在一起。|Synonyms: shackle, fetter|Antonyms: free, release|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *coffle,* from Arabic *qafilah* ("caravan"), ultimately from *qafala* ("to follow").  The semantic shift occurred because caravans often included chained slaves.
