doughface:/ˈdoʊfeɪs/| n.|伪善者，迎合奴隶主的人 (尤指美国南北战争前北方的)|A person, especially a Northerner in the period before the American Civil War, who sympathized with or courted the favor of Southern slaveholders.|He was considered a doughface by his abolitionist neighbors for his refusal to condemn slavery. 他因为拒绝谴责奴隶制而被他的废奴主义邻居视为迎合奴隶主的人。|Synonyms: appeaser, compromiser, timeserver|Antonyms: abolitionist, firebrand, radical|noun
