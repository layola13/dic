dominate:/ˈdɒmɪneɪt/ (UK) /ˈdɑːmɪneɪt/ (US)| v. |支配，控制，统治；在…中占主要地位|to have control or power over someone or something, or to be the most important or noticeable person or thing in a particular situation|例句：The company dominates the market for computer software.  这家公司在计算机软件市场占据主导地位。

例句：He tended to dominate the conversation. 他往往左右着谈话方向。|近义词：control, govern, rule, lead, command|反义词：submit, obey, follow|词性：动词

dominating:/ˈdɒmɪneɪtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈdɑːmɪneɪtɪŋ/ (US)| adj. |占支配地位的；主要的；专横的|having a commanding position or controlling influence; most important, powerful, or influential|例句：She has a dominating personality. 她个性很强势。

例句：The castle stands on a dominating position over the city. 城堡雄踞城市之上，俯瞰全城。|近义词：controlling, commanding, powerful, authoritative|反义词：submissive, passive|词性：形容词

dominance:/ˈdɒmɪnəns/ (UK) /ˈdɑːmɪnəns/ (US)| n. |优势；支配地位；统治地位|power and influence over others|例句：The company's dominance in the market is unquestioned. 该公司在市场上的主导地位毋庸置疑。

例句：The team's dominance was evident throughout the game. 该队的优势在整场比赛中都很明显。|近义词：supremacy, ascendancy, control, power|反义词：submission, subordination|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin dominatus, past participle of dominari "to rule, govern," from Latin dominus "lord, master."
