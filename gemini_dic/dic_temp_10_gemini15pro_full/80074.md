词源:

"goalwards" is a combination of "goal" and the suffix "-wards," which indicates direction.  "Goal" ultimately comes from Old Norse "gafl," meaning "gable," likely referring to the forked posts used in early forms of the game.  "-wards" comes from Old English "-weardes."

用法说明:

"Goalwards" is an adverb that describes movement or direction towards a goal, typically in sports like soccer, hockey, or other games involving goals. It is often used interchangeably with "goalward."
