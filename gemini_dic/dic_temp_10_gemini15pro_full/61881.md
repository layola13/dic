emprison:/ɪmˈprɪzən/| vt.|监禁，关押；束缚，限制|To confine someone, typically in a prison; to restrict someone's freedom.|The government imprisoned the dissidents. 政府监禁了持不同政见者。|
The high walls seemed to imprison her spirit.  高墙似乎禁锢了她的精神。|
近义词：confine, jail, incarcerate, imprison, restrain|
反义词：release, liberate, free|
词性：动词

emprisonment:/ɪmˈprɪznmənt/| n.|监禁，关押|The act of imprisoning someone or the state of being imprisoned.|His imprisonment lasted for ten years. 他的监禁持续了十年。|
She felt a sense of imprisonment in her unhappy marriage.  她在不幸的婚姻中感到了一种被禁锢的感觉。|
近义词：confinement, incarceration, captivity|
反义词：release, liberation, freedom|
词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French emprisoner, from en- "in" + prison "prison".  Ultimately from Latin prehensio "a seizing".
