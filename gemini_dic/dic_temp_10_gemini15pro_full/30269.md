celus: /ˈsiːləs/ | n. | 天空，天堂;  Celestial being; a god |Celus is a poetic or literary term for the sky or heavens, often used to refer to a deity or celestial being.  It derives from the Latin word "caelum" meaning "sky, heaven, atmosphere." |The poet invoked Celus in his verse. 诗人在他的诗歌中祈求上天。|Synonyms: heaven, sky, firmament, vault of heaven, empyrean| Antonyms: earth, hell, underworld | noun
