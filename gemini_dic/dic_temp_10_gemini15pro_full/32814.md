choke-off:/ˈtʃoʊk ˈɔːf/| phrasal verb |抑制；扼杀；阻止|To suppress or stop something from developing or continuing.|The government's new regulations are designed to choke off inflation. 政府的新规旨在抑制通货膨胀。|The company's expansion plans were choked off by a lack of funding. 公司的扩张计划因缺乏资金而受阻。|近义词：suppress, stifle, curb, restrict, constrain|反义词：encourage, promote, stimulate, facilitate|词性:动词短语

choke:/tʃoʊk/| v. |窒息；阻塞；使哽咽|1. To have difficulty breathing because your throat is blocked. 2. To block a passage or opening so that something cannot pass through it.|He choked on a piece of bread. 他被一块面包噎住了。|The weeds were choking the flowers in the garden. 杂草把花园里的花都扼杀了。|近义词：suffocate, gag, obstruct|反义词：breathe, clear|词性:动词

off:/ɔːf/| adv. prep. adj.|离开；关闭；脱落|1. Away from a place or position. 2. Not operating or connected. 3. No longer fresh or good.|He turned the lights off. 他关掉了灯。|The milk has gone off. 牛奶变质了。|近义词：away, out, disconnected|反义词：on, connected|词性:副词，介词，形容词
