improvidentness:/ɪmˈprɒvɪdəntnəs/
|n.|缺乏远见；挥霍；浪费|Lack of foresight; a failure to provide for the future; wasteful extravagance.|His improvidentness led him into serious debt. 他的缺乏远见使他负债累累。|近义词：shortsightedness, recklessness, extravagance, prodigality|反义词：foresight, prudence, thrift, economy|词性：名词

improvident:/ɪmˈprɒvɪdənt/
|adj.|缺乏远见的；挥霍的；浪费的|Not providing for the future; not thrifty; wasteful.|It was an improvident decision to spend all his savings on a luxury car. 把所有的积蓄都花在一辆豪车上是一个缺乏远见的决定。|近义词：shortsighted, reckless, extravagant, prodigal|反义词：foresightful, prudent, thrifty, economical|词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Latin *improvidens, improvident- ‘not foreseeing, not providing’, from in- ‘not’ + providens, present participle of providēre (see provide).
