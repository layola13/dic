barbiturates: /bɑːrˈbɪtʃʊrɪts/ | n. | 巴比妥类药物 | A group of drugs that act as central nervous system depressants, and can therefore produce a wide spectrum of effects, from mild sedation to total anesthesia. They are also effective as anxiolytics, hypnotics, and anticonvulsants.  | Barbiturates were once commonly used to treat insomnia and anxiety, but they have largely been replaced by safer drugs like benzodiazepines. 巴比妥类药物曾被广泛用于治疗失眠和焦虑，但现在它们很大程度上已被苯二氮卓类等更安全的药物所取代。 | Synonyms: downers, sleeping pills | Antonyms: stimulants | Noun

barbiturate: /bɑːrˈbɪtʃʊrɪt/ | n. | 巴比妥酸盐 | Any of a group of barbituric acid derivatives, used as sedatives, hypnotics, and antispasmodics. |  The doctor prescribed a barbiturate to help him sleep. 医生给他开了巴比妥酸盐帮助他睡眠。 | Synonyms:  sedative, hypnotic | Antonyms: stimulant | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From barbituric acid + -ate. Barbituric acid was discovered by Adolf von Baeyer in 1864.  The name is believed to be derived from Barbara, either the name of a woman or of Saint Barbara, and urea, reflecting the components used in the original synthesis.
