crinkle:/ˈkrɪŋkəl/
n.|皱纹;沙沙声;波纹|A small line or ridge on a surface, especially one caused by folding or crushing.|The wrapping paper crinkles when you squeeze it. |包装纸在你挤压时会发出沙沙声。|wrinkle, crease, ripple|smoothness, flatness
n.|词性:名词


crinkle:/ˈkrɪŋkəl/
v.|（使）起皱纹;（使）发出沙沙声|To become covered in or cause something to become covered in small lines or ridges.|The leaves crinkled in the sun. |叶子在阳光下晒皱了。|wrinkle, crease, rumple|smooth, flatten, iron
v.|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *crinkelen, frequentative of crinken (“to bend, twist, curl”), from Old English crincan (“to bend, yield, cringe”), from Proto-Germanic *krankjaną (“to bend, turn, twist”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ker- (“to turn, bend”). Cognate with Dutch krinkelen (“to twist, curl, crinkle”), German krank (“sick, ill”).
