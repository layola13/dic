fable:/ˈfeɪbl/| n.|寓言，传说；无稽之谈|A short story, typically with animals as characters, conveying a moral.|The fable of the tortoise and the hare teaches us that slow and steady wins the race. 龟兔赛跑的寓言告诉我们，慢而稳才能赢得比赛。|synonym: allegory, parable, myth, legend|antonym: truth, reality, fact|noun


fable:/ˈfeɪbl/| v.|编造；虚构|To invent or tell a fable.|He fabled about his adventures in the jungle. 他虚构了他在丛林中的冒险故事。|synonym: fabricate, invent, concoct|antonym: verify, confirm, prove|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fable*, from Old French *fable*, from Latin *fabula* (“a talking, a telling, a story, tale, play”), from *fari* (“to speak”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *bhā-* (“to speak”).
