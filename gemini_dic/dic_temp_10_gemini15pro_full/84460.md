halon:/ˈheɪlɒn/ (US) /ˈheɪlɒn/ (UK)| n.|哈龙|A halon is any of a number of unreactive gaseous compounds of carbon, bromine, chlorine, and fluorine, used in fire extinguishers, but now known to damage the ozone layer.|Halon 1301 was commonly used in aircraft fire suppression systems.|Synonyms: fire suppressant, extinguishing agent|Antonyms: accelerant, fuel|noun
