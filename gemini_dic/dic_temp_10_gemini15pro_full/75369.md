frow:/fraʊ/
noun
皱眉；蹙额
An expression of displeasure or concentration characterized by a furrowing of one's brows.
She gave him a frow when he interrupted her. 她在他打断她时朝他皱了皱眉。
近义词: scowl, grimace
反义词: smile, grin
词性:n.

frow:/fraʊ/
verb
皱眉；蹙额
To wrinkle one's brow, typically in displeasure or concentration.
He frowed at the complicated instructions. 他看着复杂的说明皱起了眉头。
近义词: scowl, grimace
反义词: smile, grin
词性:v.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *frouwen, from Old English frēowan (“to frown”), from Proto-Germanic *fraugianą (“to frown, be afraid”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰrewh₁- (“eyebrow”). Cognate with Dutch fronsen (“to frown”), German fronsen (“to frown, make a wry face”), Icelandic frá (“from”).
