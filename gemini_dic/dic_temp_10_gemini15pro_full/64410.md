ergativity:/ɜːɡəˈtɪvɪti/ | n. | 作格性，作格性 |  A grammatical system in which the subject of an intransitive verb and the object of a transitive verb are marked in the same way, while the subject of a transitive verb is marked differently. This contrasts with nominative-accusative systems like English. |  Ergativity is a complex grammatical feature found in languages like Basque and Georgian. 作格性是一种复杂的语法特征，存在于巴斯克语和格鲁吉亚语等语言中。 |  近义词: (No direct synonym, but related terms include) absolutive case, transitive subject | 反义词: (No direct synonym, but conceptually opposite is) nominativity | 词性: noun
