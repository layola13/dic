balmy: /ˈbɑːmi/ (US), /ˈbɑːmi/ (UK) | adj. | 温和的，宜人的，芳香的 | Pleasantly warm and mild; soothing.  Characterized by a fragrant aroma. | The evening air was balmy and fragrant.  夜晚的空气温暖宜人，散发着芳香。 | Synonyms: mild, gentle, pleasant, temperate, soothing, fragrant | Antonyms: harsh, cold, chilly, unpleasant, acrid | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bawmy, from Old English *bealmsam, from Late Latin balsamum (“balsam”), from Greek βάλσαμον (balsamon, “balsam”).  The semantic shift from "having the qualities of balsam" (soothing, fragrant) to "pleasantly warm" occurred in the 18th century.
