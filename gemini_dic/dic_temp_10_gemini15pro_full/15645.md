biased /ˈbaɪəst/ | adj. | 有偏见的；偏向…的；偏心的 | Showing an unreasonable preference or dislike for something or someone; prejudiced. |  The referee was biased towards the home team. 裁判偏袒主队。|  prejudiced, partial, unfair, one-sided | impartial, unbiased, neutral, objective | adjective


Etymology:
From Old French *biés "sloping, sideways, oblique," from a Vulgar Latin *biaxus, of uncertain origin. The sense of "prejudiced" arose in the 16th century, perhaps via the notion of a "slanting" or "oblique" view.
