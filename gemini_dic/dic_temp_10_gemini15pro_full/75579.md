fuddy-duddy:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi/| n.|老顽固，守旧的人|A fuddy-duddy is a person who is old-fashioned and disapproving of anything new or different.|Example 1: My grandfather is a bit of a fuddy-duddy; he refuses to use a computer. 我的祖父有点老顽固；他拒绝使用电脑。

Example 2:  She called him a fuddy-duddy for not wanting to try the new restaurant. 她称他为老顽固，因为他不想尝试新餐厅。|近义词：conservative, traditionalist, diehard, stick-in-the-mud|反义词:modernist, innovator, progressive|词性:noun

fuddy-duddy:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi/| adj.|老顽固的，守旧的|Used to describe someone or something that is old-fashioned and disapproving of anything new or different.|Example 1: He has very fuddy-duddy ideas about how children should be raised. 他对如何养育孩子有非常守旧的观念。

Example 2:  The company's fuddy-duddy image was hurting its sales. 该公司守旧的形象正在损害其销售。|近义词：conservative, traditionalist, outdated, stuffy|反义词:modern, innovative, progressive|词性:adjective
