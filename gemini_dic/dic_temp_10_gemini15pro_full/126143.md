mutiny:/ˈmjuːtɪni/ | v. n. | 叛变，暴动 |  Open rebellion against constituted authority, especially by seamen or soldiers against their officers. | The crew mutinied against the captain. 船员们反抗船长。| revolt, rebel, rise up | obey, submit | verb, noun


mutinied:/ˈmjuːtɪnid/ | v. | 叛变了，暴动了(mutiny的过去式和过去分词) | Past tense and past participle of mutiny, meaning engaged in open rebellion against constituted authority. | The soldiers had mutinied against their commanding officer. 士兵们已经反抗了他们的指挥官。| rebelled, revolted | obeyed, submitted | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mutiner (French mutiner), from the Italian mutinare "to mutiny," perhaps from moto "movement, revolt," from Latin motus, past participle of movere "to move."
