monumentalisms: /ˌmɒnjʊˈmentəlɪzəmz/ | n. | 纪念主义，宏伟主义 |  A tendency or style characterized by imposing scale, grandeur, and permanence, often associated with large structures or projects intended to commemorate or impress.  It can also refer to an excessive focus on grand gestures or displays, sometimes at the expense of practicality or human scale. | The monumentalisms of Soviet architecture are striking. 苏联建筑的宏伟主义令人印象深刻。|  The regime's monumentalisms were evident in the massive parades and rallies. 该政权的宏伟主义体现在大规模的游行和集会上。 | synonyms: grandeur, immensity, vastness, enormity, gigantism | antonyms:  modesty, simplicity, smallness, insignificance | noun
