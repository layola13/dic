guaiacol:/ˈɡwaɪəˌkɔːl, ˈɡwaɪəˌkɒl/| n.|愈创木酚；邻甲氧基苯酚|A colorless, oily liquid with a smoky, aromatic odor, obtained from guaiacum resin or wood creosote, or prepared synthetically. It is used as an expectorant and antiseptic, and in the manufacture of vanillin.|Guaiacol is used in many cough syrups.|Synonyms:  2-methoxyphenol |Antonyms: N/A|noun
