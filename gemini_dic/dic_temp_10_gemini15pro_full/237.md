abdicating: /ˈæbdɪkeɪtɪŋ/ | v. |  退位；放弃；逊位 | The act of formally giving up a high office or responsibility. | The king is abdicating the throne. 国王正在退位。

The CEO is abdicating his responsibilities. 首席执行官正在放弃他的责任。| 近义词：resigning, relinquishing, renouncing | 反义词：assuming, taking on, usurping | 词性: 动词 (present participle)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin abdicatus, past participle of abdicare (“to disown, renounce, reject, disclaim, abdicate”), from ab- (“away from”) + dicare (“to proclaim, dedicate, devote”).

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is abdicating his role as chairman. = 他正在放弃他作为主席的职务。
She is abdicating all responsibility for the project. = 她正在放弃该项目的所有责任。

一般过去时:
The king abdicated his throne last year. = 国王去年退位了。
He abdicated his position as CEO due to ill health. = 由于健康状况不佳，他辞去了首席执行官的职务。

现在完成时:
He has abdicated his claim to the throne. = 他已经放弃了对王位的要求。
She has abdicated her leadership role in the company. = 她已经放弃了在公司的领导角色。

过去完成时:
The king had abdicated before the war started. = 国王在战争开始前就退位了。
He had abdicated his responsibilities long before the crisis hit. = 早在危机爆发之前，他就已经放弃了他的责任。

将来时:
The queen will be abdicating next month. = 女王将于下个月退位。
He will abdicate his position if the board doesn't agree to his terms. = 如果董事会不同意他的条件，他将放弃他的职位。

虚拟语气:
If he were to abdicate, his son would become king. = 如果他退位，他的儿子将成为国王。
I wish he would abdicate his leadership role. = 我希望他放弃他的领导角色。

被动语态:
The throne was abdicated by the king. = 王位被国王放弃了。
His responsibilities were abdicated last year. = 他的责任在去年被放弃了。

疑问句:

Why is she abdicating her responsibilities? = 她为什么要放弃她的责任？

否定句:
He is not abdicating his responsibilities. = 他没有放弃他的责任。
She did not abdicate her role as CEO. = 她没有辞去首席执行官的职务。
