mislead:/mɪsˈliːd/ /mɪsˈlid/| v. |误导，欺骗|To cause someone to believe something that is not true. |The advertisement was designed to mislead consumers about the product's effectiveness.  这则广告旨在误导消费者对产品功效的认知。

The politician misled the public with false promises.  这位政客用虚假的承诺误导了公众。

|近义词：deceive, delude, trick, fool|反义词：guide, enlighten, inform|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *misleden*, from Old English *mislēdan* (“to mislead, misguide, lead astray”), from *mis-* (“wrongly, badly”) + *lēdan* (“to lead”).
