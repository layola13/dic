despisedness:/dɪˈspaɪzɪdnəs/| n. |被鄙视；受憎恨|The state of being despised; the condition of being hated or scorned.|His despisedness made him a social outcast. 他的被鄙视使他成了社会弃儿。|近义词：contempt, disdain, scorn, odium|反义词：admiration, respect, esteem|词性:noun
Etymology: From *despised* + *-ness*. *Despised* comes from the Middle English *despisen*, from Old French *despiser*, from Latin *despicere* "look down on," from *de-* "down" + *specere* "to look."  *-ness* is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being."
