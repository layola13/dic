monogenist:/məˈnɒdʒɪnɪst/ | n. |一元论者，单一起源论者 |A person who believes that all human beings belong to a single species that originated in one place and time.  |例句：The monogenist theory contrasts sharply with polygenism.  一元论与多元论截然相反。

|例句：19th-century anthropologists debated whether monogenism or polygenism was the correct explanation for human variation.  19世纪的人类学家争论一元论还是多元论才是对人类变异的正确解释。|近义词：unitarian |反义词: polygenist |词性:noun


monogenism:/məˈnɒdʒɪnɪzəm/ | n. |一元论，单一起源论 |The theory that all human beings belong to a single species that originated in one place and time. |例句：Monogenism was a popular theory in the 19th century. 一元论在19世纪是一个流行的理论。

|例句：Many religious scholars supported monogenism based on their interpretation of scripture. 许多宗教学者根据他们对经文的解释支持一元论。 |近义词：unitarianism |反义词: polygenism |词性:noun


monogenic:/ˌmɒnəˈdʒɛnɪk/ | adj. |单基因的，单一起源的|Produced or caused by a single gene or originating from a single source. |例句：Cystic fibrosis is a monogenic disorder. 囊性纤维化是一种单基因疾病。

|例句：The monogenic nature of the trait made it easy to track through generations.  这种性状的单基因特性使其易于追踪世代。 |近义词：single-gene |反义词: polygenic |词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *mónos* ("single, alone") + *génos* ("race, kind, offspring") + *-ist*.
