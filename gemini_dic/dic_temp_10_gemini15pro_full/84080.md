half-inclined:/ˌhɑːf ɪnˈklaɪnd/ | adj. | 半倾向的，有点想做的，不太情愿的 | Partly willing or wanting to do something, but also hesitant or reluctant. |  I was half-inclined to go to the party, but I was also tired. 我有点想去参加派对，但也觉得很累。|  She was half-inclined to believe him, but his story seemed too good to be true. 她有点想相信他，但他的故事听起来好得令人难以置信。 | 近义词:  disposed, tempted, partially willing | 反义词:  disinclined, unwilling, averse | 词性: adjective

half:/hɑːf/ | n. adj. adv. | 一半，半个 | One of two equal or approximately equal parts of a divisible whole. | I ate half the pizza. 我吃了一半披萨。 | Cut the apple in half. 把苹果切成两半。 | 近义词: moiety, one-half | 反义词: whole, double | 词性: noun, adjective, adverb

inclined:/ɪnˈklaɪnd/ | adj. | 倾向于…的；有…意向的 | Having a tendency or disposition to do or be something; feeling willing or favorably disposed toward something. | I'm inclined to agree with you. 我倾向于同意你的看法。 | He's inclined to be lazy. 他有点懒。 | 近义词:  disposed, prone, apt | 反义词: disinclined, unwilling | 词性: adjective
