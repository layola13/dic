frolicker:/ˈfrɒlɪkə(r)/ (US) /ˈfrɑːlɪkər/ (UK)| n. |嬉戏者，玩闹的人|A person who frolics; someone who engages in playful activity, often in a lively or boisterous way. |The children were frolickers in the park, chasing pigeons and laughing. 孩子们在公园里嬉戏，追逐鸽子，欢笑着。|近义词：reveller, merrymaker, partygoer|反义词：killjoy, spoilsport|词性: noun

词源: From the verb "frolic" + "-er" (agent suffix). "Frolic" comes from Middle Dutch "vrolijck" (joyful, merry), from "vro" (merry) + "-lijck" (like).
