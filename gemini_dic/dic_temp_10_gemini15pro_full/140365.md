oilbird:/ˈɔɪlbɜːrd/ | n. | 油鸱 | A nocturnal fruit-eating bird (Steatornis caripensis) of northern South America, the only nightjar to navigate by echolocation.  It is also known as the guácharo. | Oilbirds roost in caves and emerge at night to feed on the fruits of oil palms and other trees. | 近义词: guácharo | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for a specific bird species) | noun
