paralyzed: /ˈpærəˌlaɪzd/ (US) /ˈpærəlaɪzd/ (UK)| adj.|瘫痪的，麻痹的；惊呆的，吓坏的|Unable to move or act; incapacitated.|例句：He was paralyzed from the waist down after the accident. 事故发生后，他腰部以下瘫痪了。

例句：She was paralyzed with fear when she saw the snake. 当她看到蛇时，她吓得动弹不得。

近义词：disabled, immobile, crippled, incapacitated, stunned, shocked|反义词：able-bodied, mobile, active, functional|词性：形容词

paralyze: /ˈpærəˌlaɪz/ (US) /ˈpærəlaɪz/ (UK)| vt.|使瘫痪，使麻痹；使惊呆，使吓坏|To cause someone or something to become unable to move or act; to incapacitate.|例句：The venom of the spider can paralyze its prey. 蜘蛛的毒液可以麻痹它的猎物。

例句：The news of the accident paralyzed her with grief. 事故的消息让她悲痛欲绝。

近义词：disable, immobilize, cripple, incapacitate, stun, shock|反义词：enable, mobilize, activate|词性：动词

paralysis: /pəˈræləsɪs/ (US) /pəˈræləsɪs/ (UK)| n.|瘫痪，麻痹；停顿，停滞|The loss of the ability to move or act; a state of inactivity or stagnation.|例句：The stroke resulted in paralysis of his left arm. 中风导致他左臂瘫痪。

例句：The country is suffering from economic paralysis.  这个国家正遭受经济停滞之苦。

近义词：immobility, incapacitation, stagnation|反义词：mobility, activity, function|词性：名词


词源：From Late Latin *paralysus, from Greek παράλυσις (paralusis, “loosening, disabling”), from παραλύειν (paraluein, “to disable”), from παρά (para, “beside, alongside”) + λύειν (luein, “to loosen”).
