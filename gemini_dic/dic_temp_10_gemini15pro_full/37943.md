complex:/ˈkɒmpleks/ (UK) /kəmˈpleks/ (US)| adj. n.|复杂的；复合体；情结|A complex system or issue is one that is difficult to understand because it has many different parts.  A complex is a group of buildings, or a large building with many parts. In psychology, a complex is a strong, often hidden, feeling of fear, guilt, etc., about something.|例句：The situation is very complex.  情况很复杂。|近义词：complicated, intricate, involved, elaborate|反义词：simple, easy, straightforward|词性:形容词, 名词

complex:/ˈkɒmpleks/ (UK) /kəmˈpleks/ (US)| n.|复合体；综合设施；情结|A complex is a group of similar things or people positioned close together or a large building with many parts. In psychology, a complex is a strong, often hidden, feeling of fear, guilt, etc., about something.|例句：They live in a modern housing complex.  他们住在一个现代化的住宅区。|近义词：development, compound, center|反义词：N/A|词性:名词
