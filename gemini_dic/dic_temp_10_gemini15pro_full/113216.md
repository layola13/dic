lush:/lʌʃ/| adj.| 茂盛的，葱郁的；丰富的，大量的；（非正式）性感的，诱人的| Characterized by abundant growth, luxurious, or appealing to the senses. | The garden was lush with colorful flowers. 花园里开满了五颜六色的花，郁郁葱葱。| synonyms: abundant, luxuriant, verdant, opulent, plush | antonyms: barren, sparse, arid, meager | adjective

lush:/lʌʃ/| n.| 酒鬼，醉汉 | (informal) A heavy drinker; a drunkard. | He's been a lush for years. 他酗酒多年了。 | synonyms: drunkard, alcoholic, inebriate | antonyms: teetotaler | noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *lusshe*, probably from obsolete *lushen* "to splash, to drench," of uncertain origin.  The sense of "luxuriant" likely developed from the idea of plants being well-watered.  The slang meaning of "drunkard" arose in the 19th century.
