outgroup:/ˈaʊtˌɡruːp/ | n. | 外群体；外围群体 | A group of people with which a person does not identify.  A social group toward which a person feels competition or opposition; contrasted with ingroup. |  Example 1:  They viewed members of the outgroup with suspicion. 他们对外部群体成员抱有怀疑态度。

Example 2:  Prejudice against outgroups is a common social problem. 对外群体的偏见是一个普遍的社会问题。 | 近义词: outsider group, foreign group | 反义词: ingroup | 词性: 名词
