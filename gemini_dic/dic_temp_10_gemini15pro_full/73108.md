for-all-one-is-worth:/fɔːr ɔːl wʌn ɪz wɜːθ/| idiom | 拼尽全力；竭尽所能；全力以赴 |  To the utmost extent of one's power or ability. This idiom emphasizes putting in maximum effort, often with a sense of urgency or desperation. | He fought for all he was worth to keep his job. 他为了保住工作，竭尽全力。| synonyms: with all one's might, tooth and nail, hammer and tongs, with every ounce of one's strength | antonyms: half-heartedly, perfunctorily | idiom
