metafiction:/ˌmetəˈfɪkʃən/ | n. | 元小说 |  A type of fiction that self-consciously addresses the devices of fiction, often blurring the lines between fiction and reality by commenting on the act of storytelling itself.  It often draws attention to its own artificiality. | Example:  "The French Lieutenant's Woman" by John Fowles is a famous example of metafiction, as the author frequently interrupts the narrative to address the reader directly. | Example: Many postmodern novels employ metafiction to explore the nature of reality and the limitations of language. | 近义词: self-reflexive fiction, postmodern fiction | 反义词:  realistic fiction, traditional fiction | 词性: noun
