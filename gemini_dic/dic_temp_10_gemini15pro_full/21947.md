boggle:/ˈbɒɡəl/ (UK) /ˈbɑːɡəl/ (US)| v.  n.| 使…吃惊；犹豫；笨拙地工作；(眼睛)睁大；惊愕；难办的事 | The sheer amount of data boggles the mind.  海量的数据令人难以置信。|Synonyms: amaze, astound, bewilder, confuse, confound, nonplus, overwhelm, startle, stun|Antonyms: clarify, enlighten, explain|verb

boggle:/ˈbɒɡəl/ (UK) /ˈbɑːɡəl/ (US)| n.| 令人困惑的事情|Trying to understand quantum physics is a real boggle for me. 试图理解量子物理对我来说真是件难事。|Synonyms:  conundrum, enigma, mystery, puzzle, riddle|Antonyms: solution, answer|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From 16th century; origin uncertain, possibly related to bogle (goblin, scarecrow) and thus to bugbear.  Meaning shifted from "cause to shy away" (as a horse might) to "be startled or amazed," then to "hesitate or raise objections."
