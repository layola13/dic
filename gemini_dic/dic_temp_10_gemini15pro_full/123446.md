monopoly:/məˈnɒpəli/ (US) /məˈnɒpəli/ (UK)| n.|垄断，独占；专卖权；垄断商品；大富翁游戏|The government has a monopoly on tobacco sales. 政府拥有烟草销售的专卖权。|The company has a virtual monopoly of the market.  这家公司实际上垄断了市场。|近义词：control, dominance, exclusive possession|反义词: competition, open market|noun


monopolistic:/məˌnɒpəˈlɪstɪk/ (US) /məˌnɒpəˈlɪstɪk/ (UK)| adj.|垄断的；独占的|Monopolistic practices are harmful to consumers. 垄断行为对消费者有害。|The company adopted a monopolistic pricing strategy. 这家公司采取了垄断定价策略。|近义词：exclusive, controlling|反义词: competitive|adjective


monopolize:/məˈnɒpəlaɪz/ (US) /məˈnɒpəlaɪz/ (UK)| vt.|垄断；独占；拥有…的专卖权|Large companies shouldn't monopolize the market. 大公司不应该垄断市场。|He tends to monopolize the conversation. 他往往垄断了谈话。|近义词：dominate, control, corner|反义词: share, distribute|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *monopolium, from Greek monopōlion, from monos "single, alone" + pōlein "to sell"
