dither:/ˈdɪðər/| v. n.|犹豫，踌躇；抖动，颤动|To be indecisive; to hesitate.  Also, to vibrate rapidly; to quiver.|She dithered over which dress to wear to the party. 她为参加派对穿哪件衣服而犹豫不决。|Synonyms: hesitate, vacillate, waver, falter, tremble, quiver|Antonyms: decide, resolve, settle, steady, stabilize|verb

dither:/ˈdɪðər/| n.|犹豫，踌躇；抖动，颤动|A state of indecision or agitation.|She was in a dither about the upcoming exam. 她对即将到来的考试感到焦虑不安。|Synonyms: hesitation, indecision, vacillation, trembling, quivering|Antonyms: decisiveness, resolution, stability, calmness|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *didderen*, frequentative of an unrecorded verb *diden*, probably from Old Norse *ditra* (“to tremble”).
