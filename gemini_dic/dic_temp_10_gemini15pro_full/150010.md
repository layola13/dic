palm:/pɑːm/ (US) /pɑːm/ (UK)| n.| 手掌；棕榈树；胜利|The monkey held a banana in its palm. 猴子手里拿着一个香蕉。|Synonyms: hand, frond |Antonyms: back of hand|noun

palm:/pɑːm/ (US) /pɑːm/ (UK)| vt.| 把…藏于手中；用棕榈叶覆盖|He palmed the coin and made it disappear. 他把硬币藏在手里，让它消失了。|Synonyms: conceal, hide |Antonyms: reveal, expose|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English palma, from Latin palma "palm of the hand, palm tree," related to palmus "span, handbreadth."  The connection between the tree and the hand is due to the shape of the tree's leaves, resembling a spread hand.
