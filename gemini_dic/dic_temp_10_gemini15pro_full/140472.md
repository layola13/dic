old-maidy:/ˌoʊldˈmeɪdi/ | adj. | 老处女的，古板的，拘谨的 | Resembling or characteristic of an old maid; prim, fussy, and overly fastidious. |  Example 1: She gave me an old-maidy look when I put my elbows on the table. 她当我把胳膊肘放在桌子上时，给了我一个老处女似的眼神。

Example 2:  He considered her taste in furniture rather old-maidy. 他认为她对家具的品味相当古板。 |  Near Synonyms: prim, prissy, fussy, fastidious, prudish | Antonyms:  easygoing, relaxed, casual, unconventional | adjective


old-maid:/ˌoʊldˈmeɪd/ | n. | 老处女 | An older woman who has never married. | Example 1:  In the past, the term "old maid" carried negative connotations. 过去，“老处女”一词带有负面含义。

Example 2:  She worried she would become an old maid if she didn't find a husband soon. 她担心如果很快找不到丈夫，她会变成老处女。 | Near Synonyms: spinster | Antonyms:  wife, married woman | noun
