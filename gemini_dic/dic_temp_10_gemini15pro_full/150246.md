panderly:/ˈpændərli/ | adv. | 媚俗地，迎合低级趣味地 | in a way that panders to the lower tastes or passions of others.  |  He panderly wrote a song about easy money and fast cars to appeal to teenagers. 他媚俗地写了一首关于容易赚钱和跑车的歌来迎合青少年。 | 近义词: obsequiously, fawningly, sycophantically | 反义词:  disdainfully, contemptuously | 词性: 副词

pander:/ˈpændər/ | v. | 迎合，怂恿 | gratify or indulge (an immoral or distasteful desire, need, or habit or a person with such a desire, etc.). | The tabloid newspapers pander to the public's curiosity about the lives of celebrities.  小报迎合了公众对名人生活的好奇心。| 近义词: indulge, gratify, cater to | 反义词:  resist, defy, oppose | 词性: 动词

词源: From the character Pandarus in Chaucer's *Troilus and Criseyde* and Shakespeare's *Troilus and Cressida*, who acts as a go-between for the lovers.  源于乔叟的《特洛伊罗斯与克丽西达》和莎士比亚的《特洛伊罗斯与克丽西达》中的潘达鲁斯，他充当这对恋人的中间人。
