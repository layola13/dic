fiduciarily:/ˌfɪduːˈʃɪərəli/ | adv. | 受托地，信托地 | In a fiduciary capacity; as a trustee.  Acting in the best interests of another party, especially in financial matters. | The lawyer invested the money fiduciarily for his client.  这位律师以信托方式为他的客户投资了这笔钱。| Synonyms: trustworthily, responsibly, ethically | Antonyms: dishonestly, irresponsibly, unethically |词性: 副词

fiduciary:/fɪˈduːʃiəri/ | adj. n. | 信托的，受信托的；受托人 |  (adj.) Holding something in trust for another; (n.) a person who holds assets in trust for a beneficiary. | (adj.) The company has a fiduciary duty to its shareholders. 这家公司对其股东负有信托责任。 | (n.) He acted as fiduciary for the estate. 他担任遗产的受托人。 |  Synonyms: (adj.) trustee, entrusted, custodial; (n.) trustee, guardian, custodian | Antonyms: (adj.) untrustworthy, irresponsible; (n.) beneficiary | 词性: 形容词，名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *fiduciarius*, from fiducia (“trust, confidence”), from fidus (“faithful, trustworthy”), ultimately from Proto-Italic *fīdō (“I trust”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰeydʰ- (“to trust, confide”). Related to Latin foedus ("treaty, pact").
