gloominesses:/ˈɡluːminɪsɪz/| n. |忧郁，阴郁，沉闷|The state of being gloomy; a pervading atmosphere of darkness or depression. | The gloominesses of the winter months can be difficult to bear. 冬月里的沉闷令人难以忍受。| synonyms: melancholy, dejection, sadness, depression, despair, despondency, gloom, bleakness, dreariness, somberness | antonyms: happiness, cheerfulness, brightness, joy, lightheartedness | noun

Etymology: From gloominess + -es (plural suffix). Gloominess is derived from gloomy + -ness (suffix forming abstract nouns). Gloomy comes from Middle English *gloumi, from Old English *glom, meaning "twilight, dusk."
