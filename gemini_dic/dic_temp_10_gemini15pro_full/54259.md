disgracer:/dɪsˈɡreɪsər/| n. |玷辱者，丢脸的人|Someone who brings shame or dishonor upon themselves or others. |例句：He is a disgracer to his family. 他是他家族的耻辱。

例句：Her actions made her a disgracer to her profession. 她的行为使她成为她行业的耻辱。|近义词：shame, embarrassment, dishonor, scandal |反义词: honor, credit, pride |词性:noun
词源: From disgrace + -er. Disgrace from dis- "expressing reversal" + grace "favor, pleasing quality".
