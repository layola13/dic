dudish:/ˈduːdɪʃ/| adj.| 花花公子的；纨绔子弟的；衣着讲究的；时髦的 |Resembling or characteristic of a dude; stylish, fashionable, or showy, especially in a masculine way.  Originally referred to a man excessively concerned with his clothes and appearance. Now often used more generally to describe something stylish or cool.| He looked very dudish in his new suit. 他穿着新西装看起来很帅气。| 近义词：stylish, fashionable, dapper, trendy, chic | 反义词：sloppy, unfashionable, frumpy | 词性: adjective

词源: From dude + -ish.  "Dude" originally (late 19th century) referred to an Easterner, especially one visiting the American West, who affected Western dress and mannerisms.  Later it came to mean a dandy or fop.
