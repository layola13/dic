Etymology:
From Old English *hwæt (Northumbrian hwæt, West Saxon hwæt, Anglian hwæt), from Proto-Germanic *hwat, from Proto-Indo-European *kʷod. Cognate with Scots quhat (“what”), West Frisian wat (“what”), Dutch wat (“what”), German was (“what”), Danish hvad (“what”), Swedish vad (“what”), Icelandic hvað (“what”), Gothic 𐍈𐍀 (“what”).
