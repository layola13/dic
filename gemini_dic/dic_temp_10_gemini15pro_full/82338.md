groves:/ɡroʊvz/| n. |小树林，树丛|A small group of trees.|We walked through the shady groves. 我们穿过阴凉的小树林。|近义词：woods, thicket, copse, forest|反义词:plain, field|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *groves, plural of grove, from Old English grāf (“grove, copse, thicket”), from Proto-Germanic *grōbō (“grove, copse, thicket”), from Proto-Indo-European *gerbʰ- (“to carve, scratch, engrave”). Cognate with Old Saxon grōvi (“grove”), Old Frisian grōve (“grove, ditch”), Dutch groeve (“groove, furrow”), German Grube (“pit, mine”), Old Norse grǫf (“ditch, grave, cavity”).
