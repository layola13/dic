magnifying:/ˈmæɡnɪfaɪɪŋ/| adj.|放大的，夸大的|making something appear larger than it is, either by using a lens or by describing it in a way that makes it seem more important than it really is.|例句：She examined the stamp under a magnifying glass. 她用放大镜检查邮票。|例句：He has a magnifying way of describing even the smallest problems. 他有一种夸大其词的方式来描述即使是最小的问题。|近义词：enlarging, amplifying, exaggerating|反义词：reducing, minimizing, diminishing|词性:adjective

magnify:/ˈmæɡnɪfaɪ/| v.|放大，扩大，夸大|to make something appear larger than it is, especially with a lens or microscope; to make something seem more important or serious than it really is|例句：The microscope magnifies the object 100 times.  显微镜将物体放大了100倍。|例句：The media tends to magnify the dangers of travelling abroad. 媒体往往夸大出国旅行的危险。|近义词：enlarge, amplify, exaggerate, overstate|反义词：reduce, minimize, diminish, understate|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: *magnifiyng, *magnifien, from Old French magnifier, from Latin magnificare ‘make magnificent’, from magnus ‘great’ + -ficare (related to facere ‘do, make’).
