newfanglednesses:/ˌnjuːˈfæŋɡəldnəsəz/| n. | 新奇的事物；新玩意儿（复数）|  The state or quality of being newfangled; novelty.  It refers to a fondness or obsession with new gadgets, inventions, or ideas.  | He dismissed their suggestions as mere newfanglednesses. 他认为他们的建议只是一些新奇玩意儿，不予理会。| 近义词：novelties, innovations, modernisms | 反义词：traditions, old ways, antiquities | 词性: noun


Etymology:  From newfangled + -ness.  Newfangled itself comes from 'new' + 'fangle' (invented from 'fang' in the sense of 'take hold of').  Originally used in the 16th century, it carried a somewhat negative connotation, suggesting something overly elaborate or unnecessarily new.
