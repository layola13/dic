collocate:/ˈkɒləkeɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləkeɪt/ (US)| v. |搭配；组合；并置|To occur in close association with another word or phrase in a language.|Certain adjectives collocate with particular nouns, for example, 'rancid' collocates with 'butter' but not with 'wine'.|近义词：combine, associate, link, pair|反义词：separate, detach|词性：动词

collocation:/ˌkɒləˈkeɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌkɑːləˈkeɪʃn/ (US)| n. |搭配；组合；排列|The habitual juxtaposition of a particular word with another word or words with a frequency greater than chance.| 'Heavy rain' is a common collocation in English.|近义词：combination, association, pairing|反义词：separation, detachment|词性：名词
