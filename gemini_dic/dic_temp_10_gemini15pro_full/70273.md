fiddles:/ˈfɪdlz/| n.|小提琴(复数); 无聊的举动; 欺骗; 琐碎的事|Small violins (plural); trifling or restless movements; acts of deception or cheating; trivial things|He plays the fiddles beautifully. 他小提琴拉得很好。|近义词:violins, distractions, tricks, trifles|反义词:necessities, essentials|noun

fiddles:/ˈfɪdlz/| v.|拉小提琴; 拨弄; 浪费时间; 篡改|Plays the violin; fidgets or plays restlessly; wastes time; tampers with or alters deceptively|He fiddled with the radio knob. 他拨弄着收音机旋钮。|近义词:plays, fidgets, wastes, tampers|反义词:works, focuses, repairs, preserves|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fiðele, from Proto-Germanic *fiþulō, from Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰedʰ- (“to rub, scrape”).  Cognate with Old Frisian fiðele (“fiddle”), Old Saxon fiðla (“fiddle”), Old High German fidula (“fiddle”), Old Norse fiðla (“fiddle”), Gothic 𐍆𐌹𐌸𐌻𐌰 (fiþla, “fiddle”).  Doublet of viol.
