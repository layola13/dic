marry-off:/ˈmæri ɒf/ | vt. | 把…嫁出去，为…安排婚姻 | To arrange for someone to get married, often implying a sense of controlling the situation or marrying someone off for social or economic reasons. |  Her parents married her off to a wealthy businessman. 她父母把她嫁给了一个富商。

They married their daughter off young. 他们早早地把女儿嫁出去了。| 近义词： wed, give away in marriage | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "remain single") | 词性: Verb (Transitive)


ETYMOLOGY->
From "marry" + "off".  "Marry" comes from Middle English marien, from Old French marier, from Latin marītāre "to wed," from marītus "husband." "Off" in this context indicates completion or separation, like "send off" or "pay off."
