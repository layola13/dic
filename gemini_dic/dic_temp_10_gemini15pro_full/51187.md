destabilize: /diːˈsteɪbəlaɪz/ | v. | 使不稳定，动摇 | To make something unstable or less firm. | The sudden change in leadership destabilized the country. 突然的领导层变动使国家动荡不安。| The rumors destabilized the stock market. 谣言扰乱了股票市场。| synonyms: undermine, weaken, unsettle, disrupt | antonyms: stabilize, strengthen, reinforce | verb

destabilized: /diːˈsteɪbəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 不稳定的，动摇的 |  Having been made unstable or less firm. | The destabilized government was unable to function effectively.  不稳定的政府无法有效运作。| The destabilized region became a breeding ground for extremism.  动荡不安的地区成了极端主义的滋生地。| synonyms: unstable, shaky, precarious, insecure | antonyms: stable, secure, firm, steady | adjective
