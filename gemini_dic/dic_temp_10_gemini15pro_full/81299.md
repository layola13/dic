granulation:/ˌɡrænjʊˈleɪʃən/| n.| 颗粒化，造粒，肉芽组织 |The process of forming grains or granules from a powder or other fine material; also, the granular material itself, especially the small, fleshy, grain-like projections that form on the surface of a wound during healing. |The granulation tissue on the wound indicates it is healing properly. 伤口上的肉芽组织表明它正在正常愈合。| Synonyms: granulating, granularity, graininess, particulate | Antonyms: smoothness, homogeneity | noun

granulation:/ˌɡrænjʊˈleɪʃən/| n.| 颗粒化，造粒，肉芽组织 |The formation of granules, small grains or particles. In medicine, it refers to the formation of granular tissue in a wound as part of the healing process.| The doctor observed healthy granulation in the patient's wound. 医生观察到病人伤口上健康的肉芽组织。 | Synonyms: granulating, granularization, grain formation | Antonyms: dissolution, disintegration | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *granulationem, derived from granulum (small grain), diminutive of granum (grain).
