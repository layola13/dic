caducary: /kəˈdjuːkəri/ | adj. n.|  绝嗣的；无主的；无人继承的；绝嗣财产；无主财产 | Relating to or applied to an escheat or forfeiture of property to the state in cases where there are no legal heirs.  Also, the property so forfeited. |  The caducary laws of the kingdom dictated that the king would inherit any estate lacking an heir.  这个王国的绝嗣法规定，国王将继承任何没有继承人的遗产。 | Synonyms: escheated, forfeit, intestate | Antonyms: inherited, bequeathed, willed | adjective

caducary: /kəˈdjuːkəri/ | n.| 绝嗣财产；无主财产 | Property that has been forfeited to the state because there are no legal heirs. | The government claimed the caducary, a vast estate with no known living relatives of the deceased owner. 政府声称这笔绝嗣财产，一个庞大的庄园，已故业主没有已知的在世亲属。| Synonyms: escheat, forfeiture | Antonyms: inheritance, legacy | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *caducaire*, from Latin *cadūcus* ("falling, liable to fall, perishable, frail"), from *cadō* ("I fall").
