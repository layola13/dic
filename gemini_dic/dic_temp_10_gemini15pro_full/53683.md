disciplinarily:/ˌdɪsɪˈplɪnɛrɪli/| adv.|纪律上地，惩戒性地|In a way that relates to discipline or punishment.  Relating to the imposition or administration of discipline.|He was disciplinarily dismissed for misconduct. 他因行为不检而被纪律处分开除。|Synonyms: punitively, correctively|Antonyms: leniently, forgivingly|词性: 副词

Etymology: From "disciplinary" + "-ly". "Disciplinary" comes from the Late Latin "disciplinārius",  from "disciplīna" (instruction, knowledge, discipline)  derived from "discipulus" (pupil, learner), from "discō" (I learn).  The suffix "-ly" transforms adjectives into adverbs.
