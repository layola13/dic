compartmentalism:/kəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəlɪzəm/
|n.|划分；分隔；条块分割；【心理】心理区隔化|The act or condition of being divided into compartments or categories.  The mental process or strategy of separating different aspects of one's life or thoughts into isolated compartments.|He deals with stress through compartmentalism, separating his work life from his personal life. 他通过划分来应对压力，将工作和生活分开。|近义词：categorization, classification, pigeonholing, segregation|反义词：integration, unification, holism|词性:名词

compartmentalize:/kəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəlaɪz/
|v.|划分；分隔；条块分割；【心理】心理区隔化|To separate something into isolated compartments or categories.|She compartmentalizes her life, keeping work and personal matters strictly separate. 她把自己的生活划分开来，将工作和个人事务严格分开。|近义词：categorize, classify, pigeonhole, segregate|反义词：integrate, unify|词性:动词

compartmentalized:/kəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəlaɪzd/
|adj.|划分好的；分隔的；条块分割的|Divided into compartments or categories.|The company has a highly compartmentalized structure, with each department operating independently.  这家公司结构划分非常细致，每个部门都独立运作。|近义词：categorized, classified, segregated|反义词：integrated, unified|词性:形容词


词源: From compartment + -al + -ism.  Ultimately from the Latin word compartiri, meaning "to share."
