impertinency:/ɪmˈpɜːrtnənsi/| n. |无礼，鲁莽；不恰当，不相干|Lack of respect; rudeness; irrelevance.|例句：His impertinency to the judge resulted in a contempt of court charge. 他对法官的无礼导致他被控藐视法庭。

例句：The impertinency of her remarks shocked everyone present. 她言辞的无礼震惊了在场的所有人。

|近义词：rudeness, insolence, impudence, audacity, presumption|反义词: politeness, courtesy, respect, relevance, propriety|词性:名词


impertinent:/ɪmˈpɜːrtnənt/| adj. |无礼的，鲁莽的；不恰当的，不相干的|Not showing proper respect; rude; irrelevant.|例句：She asked some impertinent questions about his private life. 她问了一些关于他私生活的无礼问题。

例句：His impertinent interruption angered the speaker. 他无礼的打断激怒了发言者。

|近义词：rude, insolent, impudent, audacious, presumptuous|反义词: polite, courteous, respectful, relevant, proper|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *impertinentia, from Latin impertinēns (“not pertinent”), from in- (“not”) + pertinēns (“pertaining”), present participle of pertinēre (“to pertain”), from per- (“thoroughly”) + tenēre (“to hold”).
