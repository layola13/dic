indoctrinate:/ɪnˈdɒktrɪneɪt/ /ɪnˈdɑːktrɪneɪt/| vt.|灌输，教导|To teach someone to fully accept a set of beliefs without questioning them.|The government has been accused of indoctrinating children with nationalist ideology. 政府被指控向儿童灌输民族主义思想。|They were indoctrinated from an early age to believe in the party.  他们从小就被灌输要信仰这个党。|Synonym: brainwash, propagandize, instill, inculcate|Antonym: deprogram, disenchant, enlighten|Verb

indoctrination:/ɪnˌdɒktrɪˈneɪʃən/ /ɪnˌdɑːktrɪˈneɪʃən/| n.|灌输，教导|The process of teaching someone to fully accept a set of beliefs without questioning them.|Religious indoctrination begins in childhood. 宗教灌输从童年开始。|The constant indoctrination made him a firm believer. 不断的灌输使他成为一个坚定的信徒。|Synonym: brainwashing, propaganda, inculcation|Antonym: deprogramming, education|Noun
