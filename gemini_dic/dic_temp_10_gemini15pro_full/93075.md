idolographical:/ˌaɪdiəˈɡræfɪkəl/
adjective
表意文字的;独特的
Relating to or in the style of an idiograph; unique, peculiar, or individual.
His idlographical handwriting made it difficult to forge.  他独特的笔迹使伪造变得困难。
近义词:unique, distinctive, peculiar, individual, characteristic
反义词:common, general, universal, typical
词性:adjective

idolograph:/ˈɪdiəɡræf/
noun
表意文字;独特记号
A written symbol that represents an idea or object directly rather than a particular word or speech sound, as a Chinese character; a mark or sign peculiar to an individual.
Chinese characters are considered idiographs. 汉字被认为是表意文字。
近义词:symbol, character, sign, mark
反义词:phoneme, sound
词性:noun

词源：
From  idio- + -graphical.  Idio- comes from Greek idios, meaning “own, private, peculiar.” -Graphical comes from Greek graphikos, meaning “of or for writing.”  Thus, idlographical refers to writing that is unique or peculiar to an individual.
