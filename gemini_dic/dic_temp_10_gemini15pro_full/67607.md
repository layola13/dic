extravasation:/ɪkˌstrævəˈseɪʃən/| n.|渗出；外渗|The leakage of blood, lymph, or other fluid, such as an anticancer drug, from a blood vessel or tube into the surrounding tissue.|The patient experienced extravasation of the chemotherapy drug, causing tissue damage.|近义词：leakage, seepage, infiltration|反义词:containment, retention|词性:noun
Etymology: From Late Latin extravasatus, past participle of extravasare "to force out of the vessels," from Latin extra- "outside" + vas "vessel"
