notarize: /ˈnəʊtəˌraɪz/ (British) /ˈnoʊtəˌraɪz/ (American) | vt. | 公证，认证 | To certify or attest something, usually a document, officially, especially by a notary public. | Example: I had to get my passport application notarized. 我必须公证我的护照申请。

Example:  She notarized the signature on the will. 她公证了遗嘱上的签名。

| 近义词: certify, attest, authenticate, validate | 反义词:  invalidate,  deny, reject | 词性: verb


notarized: /ˈnəʊtəˌraɪzd/ (British) /ˈnoʊtəˌraɪzd/ (American) | adj. | 已公证的，经认证的 |  Certified or attested officially, especially by a notary public. | Example:  A notarized copy of the contract was required. 需要一份经公证的合同副本。

Example: The document was notarized to make it legally binding.  这份文件经过公证，使其具有法律约束力。


| 近义词: certified, attested, authenticated, validated | 反义词: uncertified, unattested, unauthenticated, invalid | 词性: adjective
