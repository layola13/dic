mosh:/mɒʃ/ (British) /mɑːʃ/ (American)| v. n.|激烈碰撞，（在摇滚音乐会上）冲撞，猛撞，mosh pit|To dance in a mosh pit; to aggressively push and shove other people while dancing to loud music.  As a noun, it refers to this style of dancing or the area where it happens. |例句：The crowd started moshing as soon as the band started playing. 乐队一开始演奏，人群就开始冲撞。

|例句：He got injured in the mosh at the concert. 他在音乐会的冲撞中受伤了。|近义词：slam dance, pogo, body surf|反义词:waltz, slow dance|词性:verb, noun
词源:Probably a shortening of mash, influenced by posh. First recorded in the early 1980s.
