elegy: /ˈɛlɪdʒi/ | n. | 挽歌；哀歌；悲歌 | A poem of serious reflection, typically a lament for the dead. |  Example 1: He wrote an elegy for his deceased friend. 他为去世的朋友写了一首挽歌。

Example 2:  The music was a mournful elegy for a lost love.  这首音乐是一曲哀悼逝去爱情的悲歌。 | 近义词: lament, dirge, requiem | 反义词: celebration, paean | 词性: noun

词源: From Middle French *elegie*, from Latin *elegia*, from Ancient Greek *ἐλεγεία (elegeía)*, from *ἔλεγος (elegos, “song of mourning”)*.
