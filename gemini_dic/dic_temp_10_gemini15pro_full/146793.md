overillustration:/ˌoʊvərɪləˈstreɪʃən/
n.
过度阐释; 过度说明
The act of illustrating something excessively, often to the point of redundancy or distraction.  It can involve using too many images, or images that are too detailed or complex, for the concept being explained.
The overillustration in the children's book made it difficult for them to focus on the story.  这本童书中过多的插图让孩子们难以集中注意力在故事本身上。

The presentation suffered from overillustration, with too many charts and graphs obscuring the main points.  这个演示文稿过度阐释，过多的图表和图形掩盖了要点。

近义词:overexplanation, excessive illustration
反义词:underillustration, minimalism


ETYMOLOGY->
"over-" (prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much") + "illustration" (from Latin "illustratio," meaning "a making bright, light, or clear")
