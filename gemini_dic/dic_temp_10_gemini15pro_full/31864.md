cheerinesses: /ˈtʃɪriːnəsɪz/
| n. |快乐，高兴，兴高采烈|The state of being cheerful; cheerfulness.  A plural form of cheeriness.|Her cheerinesses were infectious, brightening everyone's day. 她的快乐很有感染力，照亮了每个人的一天。|近义词：gaiety, merriment, joyousness, happiness, mirth|反义词：gloom, sadness, dejection, melancholy|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From cheeriness + -es (plural suffix).  Cheeriness itself comes from cheerful + -ness.  Cheerful traces back to Middle English *cherful, from chere (face, countenance) + -ful (full of).  Ultimately, chere derives from Old French chiere, from Late Latin cara, from Greek kara (head).
