blustery: /ˈblʌstəri/ (US), /ˈblʌstəri/ (UK)| adj.|狂风的，寒冷的|Characterized by strong winds and typically cold weather.  It describes weather conditions that are windy and often cold or unpleasant.|例句：It was a blustery day, with the wind howling and the trees swaying.  狂风大作，树木摇曳，真是个寒冷的日子。

例句：The blustery weather made it difficult to walk.  狂风天气使得行走困难。|近义词：windy, gusty, stormy, tempestuous|反义词：calm, still, tranquil|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "blast" (a sudden strong gust of wind) + "-ery" (a suffix forming adjectives meaning "characterized by"). The word "blast" itself comes from Old Norse "blastr" meaning "blowing, gust of wind."
