gilded:/ˈɡɪldɪd/| adj.|镀金的，虚饰的，富丽堂皇的|Covered with a thin layer of gold or something that appears valuable but is actually worthless; having a deceptively attractive appearance.|The gilded cage of Hollywood fame can be both alluring and suffocating. 好莱坞名利的镀金笼既诱人又令人窒息。|近义词：golden, ornate, embellished, ostentatious|反义词：plain, simple, unadorned, austere|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gilden*, from Old English *gyldan* (“to gild”), from Proto-Germanic *guldijaną* (“to gild”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰelh₃-/*ǵʰolh₃- (“yellow, green, gold”).  Cognate with Dutch *gilden* (“to gild”), German *vergolden* (“to gild”), Swedish *gylla* (“to gild”).
