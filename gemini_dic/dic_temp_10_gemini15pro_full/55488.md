distractible:/dɪˈstræktəbəl/| adj.|易分心的，易受干扰的|Easily diverted from one's attention; easily distracted.|Example: He's very distractible and has trouble focusing on his studies. 他很容易分心，很难集中精力学习.|Synonyms: inattentive, unfocused, scatterbrained|Antonyms: focused, attentive, concentrated|adjective

distracted:/dɪˈstræktɪd/| adj.|分心的，心烦意乱的|Having one's attention diverted; unable to concentrate.|Example: She seemed distracted and didn't hear what I said. 她看起来心不在焉，没听到我说的话.|Synonyms: preoccupied, absent-minded, inattentive|Antonyms: focused, attentive, concentrated|adjective

distract:/dɪˈstrækt/| v.|使分心，分散注意力|To draw someone's attention away from something; to divert.|Example: The noise from the street distracted me from my work. 街上的噪音让我无法专心工作.|Synonyms: divert, sidetrack, disturb|Antonyms: focus, concentrate|verb

distraction:/dɪˈstrækʃən/| n.|分心，分心的事物|Something that prevents someone from giving their full attention to something else.|Example: The constant distractions made it difficult to complete the task.  持续不断的干扰使得很难完成任务。|Synonyms: diversion, interruption, disturbance|Antonyms: focus, concentration|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *distractus*, past participle of *distrahere* "to pull apart, divide, separate, distract," from *dis-* "apart" + *trahere* "to draw, pull."
