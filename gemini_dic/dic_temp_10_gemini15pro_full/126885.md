narc: /nɑːrk/ (US); /nɑːk/ (UK)| n. | 告密者，毒品管制局探员 | A police officer who specializes in narcotics. Also, a person who informs on someone to the police or other authorities.  | Example 1: The narc busted the dealer with a kilo of cocaine. 那个缉毒警察抓住了带着一公斤可卡因的毒贩。

Example 2:  He was labeled a narc after he told the teacher who cheated on the test. 他告诉老师谁在考试中作弊后，被贴上了告密者的标签。 | 近义词: informer, snitch, rat | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "accomplice" in some contexts) | 词性: noun


narc: /nɑːrk/ (US); /nɑːk/ (UK)| v. |  告发，密报 | To inform on someone to the police or other authorities. | Example 1:  She narced on her roommate for smoking weed in the dorm. 她向宿舍管理员举报了她的室友在宿舍里吸大麻。

Example 2: Don't narc me out to the boss! 不要向老板告发我! | 近义词: inform, snitch, rat | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "cover up" in some contexts) | 词性: verb
