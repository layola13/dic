PHRASE->

(It's difficult to provide "phrases" in the conventional sense with "cui" as it's primarily a formal/legal term. The examples below are more like common usages within legal or formal contexts.)

1. Cui bono? =  Who benefits? (a fixed expression)
2. Cui prodest scelus, is fecit. = He who benefits from the crime, committed it. (a legal maxim)
3. Cuius regio, eius religio. = Whose realm, his religion. (a historical principle)
4. Cuius est solum, eius est usque ad coelum et ad inferos. = For whoever owns the soil, it is theirs all the way to Heaven and all the way to Hell. (legal principle regarding property ownership)
5.  In re Cui vs. Regina = In the case of Cui versus the Queen (legal case title example)


注意事项:

"cui" is a dative/genitive case pronoun, primarily found in legal or formal Latin and less frequent in everyday language.  Its direct equivalent in modern English is "whose" but is often replaced by other constructions like "to whom" or "of whom."
