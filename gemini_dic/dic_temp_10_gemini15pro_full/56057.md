dochmiac: /ˈdɒkmiæk/ | adj. | Dochmiac的；抑扬格和短长格混合的 |  A dochmiac rhythm is a complex metrical foot in poetry, consisting of a long syllable followed by two short syllables, then a long syllable, and finally another short syllable. It creates a sense of urgency and emotional intensity.  | The poet used dochmiac rhythms to convey the speaker's anguish. 诗人用抑扬格和短长格混合的节奏来表达说话人的痛苦。 | Near synonyms:  erratic, uneven, agitated (referring to the rhythm) | Antonyms: regular, smooth, even (referring to the rhythm) | adjective
