engender:/ɪnˈdʒɛndər/
verb
产生，引起
to cause or give rise to (a feeling, situation, or condition).
The issue engendered controversy. 这个问题引起了争议。
近义词:cause, create, generate, produce, provoke, give rise to
反义词:prevent, suppress, stop, halt
词性:v.

engender:/ɪnˈdʒɛndər/
词源:From Old French engendrer, from Latin ingenerare (“to implant, engender”), from in- (“in, into”) + generare (“to beget, produce”).
用法说明:Engender is a transitive verb, meaning it takes a direct object. It is typically used to describe the creation of abstract things like feelings, situations, or conditions.

例句:
The announcement engendered a feeling of excitement among the employees.
这个公告在员工中引起了一种兴奋的感觉。

His careless words engendered a heated argument.
他不经意的话引起了一场激烈的争论。

近义词:
cause:  The storm caused widespread damage.  风暴造成了广泛的破坏。
create:  The artist created a masterpiece.  艺术家创作了一幅杰作。
generate:  The project generated a lot of interest.  这个项目引起了很多人的兴趣。
produce:  The factory produces high-quality goods.  这家工厂生产高质量的商品。
provoke: His insults provoked a fight. 他的侮辱引发了一场斗殴。
give rise to: The incident gave rise to several rumors.  这起事件引发了几种传言。

反义词:
prevent:  The vaccine can prevent the spread of the disease.  疫苗可以防止疾病传播。
suppress:  The government suppressed the uprising.  政府镇压了起义。
stop:  The police stopped the car.  警察拦住了那辆车。
halt: The project was halted due to lack of funding.  由于缺乏资金，该项目被暂停。
