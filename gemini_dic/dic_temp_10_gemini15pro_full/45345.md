cumulonimbus:/ˌkjuːmjʊləˈnɪmbəs/| n. |积雨云|A dense, towering vertical cloud associated with thunderstorms and atmospheric instability, forming from water vapor carried by powerful upward air currents.  These clouds are capable of producing intense precipitation, hail, lightning, and even tornadoes.|例句：The darkening sky and the appearance of cumulonimbus clouds signaled an approaching storm. 天色变暗和积雨云的出现预示着暴风雨即将来临。

例句： Pilots are trained to avoid flying through cumulonimbus clouds due to the severe turbulence they can create. 飞行员接受过训练，要避开积雨云飞行，因为积雨云会造成严重的湍流。|近义词：thunderhead, storm cloud|反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps clear sky or cirrus cloud -  describes opposite weather conditions) |词性: Noun
