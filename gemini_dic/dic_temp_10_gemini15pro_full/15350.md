backstitching: /ˈbækˌstɪtʃɪŋ/
n.
回针缝，倒缝
The act of backstitching a seam for reinforcement.
She used backstitching to secure the seam. 她用回针缝加固了接缝。

backstitching: /ˈbækˌstɪtʃɪŋ/
v.
回针缝，倒缝
To sew backward and forward over a seam for reinforcement.
He backstitched the edges of the fabric to prevent fraying. 他回针缝合了织物边缘以防止磨损。


ETYMOLOGY->
From back + stitching, the present participle of stitch.  "Back" refers to the reverse direction of the stitching.

SYNONYMS->
backstitch (v.), reverse stitch (v.)

ANTONYMS->
No direct antonym, but conceptually the opposite would be not reinforcing a seam or using a different reinforcement method.
