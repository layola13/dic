circuiteer:/ˈsɜːrkɪtɪər/| n.|巡回者，巡回演出者|A person who travels a circuit, especially a preacher or performer who travels a regular route of churches or venues.|The circuiteer visited a different town each week, performing his magic show to enthusiastic crowds. | 近义词: traveler, itinerant, performer, preacher|反义词: resident, settler|词性:noun

Etymology:
From "circuit" + "-eer" (a suffix forming nouns denoting agents or persons associated with something).  "Circuit" comes from Old French "circuit" and ultimately from Latin "circuitus,"  past participle of "circumire" ("to go around"), from "circum" ("around") + "ire" ("to go").
