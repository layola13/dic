outlier:/ˈaʊtˌlaɪər/| n.| 异常值，局外人，离群值 |A data point that differs significantly from other observations.  A person or thing differing from all other members of a particular group or set.|The outlier in the data set skewed the results.  这个数据集中的异常值使结果发生了偏差。 He was an outlier in the group, preferring to read while the others played games. 他是这个团体中的一个异类，喜欢读书，而其他人则喜欢玩游戏。|近义词：anomaly, aberration, exception, maverick|反义词：norm, standard, insider|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From out +‎ lier ("one that lies"), from lie ("to recline").  Originally referred to something lying outside or detached from the main body.
