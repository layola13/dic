ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *faru (“journey, passage, expedition”), from Proto-Germanic *farō (“journey, passage”), from Proto-Indo-European *per- (“to lead, pass over”). Cognate with Old Frisian fere (“journey, expedition”), Old Saxon fari (“journey, passage”), Old High German fara (“journey, expedition, transport”), Old Norse för (“journey, expedition”), Gothic 𐍆𐌰𐍂𐌰 (fara, “journey, passage”). More at ferry. The meaning "price of passage" is first recorded in the 1590s.
