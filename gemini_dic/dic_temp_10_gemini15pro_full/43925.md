crewel:/ˈkruːəl/| n. | 绒线, 刺绣用细毛线 | A loosely twisted worsted yarn used for embroidery. | She bought some crewel to start a new embroidery project. 她买了一些绒线来开始一个新的刺绣项目。|  The museum displayed antique crewel work. 博物馆展出了古董绒线绣。 |  yarn, thread, floss | - | noun
Etymology: From Middle English *crewel, *crule, from Old French crouel (“ball of thread, skein”), diminutive of croc (“hook”), from Old Norse krókr (“hook”). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *kraukō (“hook”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ker- (“to turn, bend”).
