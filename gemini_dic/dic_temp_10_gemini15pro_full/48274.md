decouple:/diːˈkʌpəl/
verb
分离，去耦，使…脱钩
to separate two or more things or systems so that they are no longer connected or dependent on each other
The government is trying to decouple the link between pay and inflation. 政府正在努力使工资和通货膨胀脱钩。
近义词：separate, disconnect, disengage, unlink
反义词：couple, connect, link, combine
词性：v.

decouple:/diːˈkʌpəl/
noun
脱钩，分离
the act of separating two or more things or systems
The decoupling of the two economies has had a significant impact on trade.  这两个经济体的脱钩对贸易产生了重大影响。
近义词：separation, disconnection, disengagement
反义词：coupling, connection, linkage
词性：n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- + couple.  "De-" is a prefix meaning "off," "away from," "down," "reversal," "removal," or "reducing." "Couple" comes from Old French "couple," ultimately from Latin "copula" meaning "a link, tie, bond."
