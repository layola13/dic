heuristic:/hjʊˈrɪstɪk/ /hjuːˈrɪstɪk/| adj. n.|启发式的，探索性的；启发式方法|A heuristic technique, or a heuristic, is any approach to problem solving or self-discovery that employs a practical method that is not guaranteed to be optimal, perfect, or rational, but is nevertheless sufficient for reaching an immediate, short-term goal or approximation.  |例句：Using a heuristic approach, they were able to quickly find a solution, though not necessarily the best one. 使用启发式方法，他们能够快速找到解决方案，尽管不一定是最优解。

|例句：The chess player relied on heuristics to evaluate board positions quickly. 棋手依靠启发式方法快速评估棋盘位置。|近义词：exploratory, investigative, trial-and-error|反义词：algorithmic, systematic|词性: adjective, noun


Etymology: From Greek *εὑριστικός (heuriskos) "serving to discover or find out," from εὑρίσκω (heuriskō) "I find, discover."
