endeictic:/ɛnˈdaɪktɪk/ | adj. |指示的，指示性的 | Relating to or denoting a word or expression whose meaning is dependent on the context in which it is used (such as here, you, me, that, there, then, or next Tuesday). | Example: "Here" is an endeictic word, because its meaning depends on where the speaker is when they say it. “这里”是一个指示词，因为它的意思取决于说话者说这个词时所在的位置。 | Example: Pronouns like "I" and "you" are inherently endeictic, as their reference shifts with each speaker and addressee.  像“我”和“你”这样的代词本质上是指示性的，因为它们的指称会随着每个说话者和听话者而改变。 | Synonyms: deictic, demonstrative | Antonyms:  non-deictic | word class: adjective
