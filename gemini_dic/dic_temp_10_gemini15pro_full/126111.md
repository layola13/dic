mutchkin:/ˈmʌtʃkɪn/| n.|小酒杯；少量（尤指液体）；矮子；小孩|A small measure of liquid, especially spirits; a small person, especially a child.|例句：He poured himself a mutchkin of whisky. 他给自己倒了一小杯威士忌。

例句：She's a wee mutchkin, isn't she? 她是个小不点，不是吗？|近义词：nip, tot, dram, drop (for liquid measure); dwarf, midget, shorty (for person)|反义词:giant (for person)|词性:noun

PHRASE->
a wee mutchkin = a small child (especially in Scottish English) = 一个小孩（尤用于苏格兰英语）
a mutchkin of whisky = a small amount of whisky = 一小杯威士忌
just a mutchkin = only a small amount = 只有一点点
not a mutchkin = nothing at all = 什么都没有
a mutchkin of a lad/lass = a small boy/girl = 一个小男孩/女孩


词源:  From Scottish Gaelic *`muitkin`*, diminutive of `muta` "bottle, container".  Ultimately from Latin `muttire` "to mutter, murmur" (perhaps referring to the gurgling sound of liquid being poured).
