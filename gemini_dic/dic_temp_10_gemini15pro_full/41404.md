cornea:/ˈkɔːrniə/ (US), /ˈkɔːniə/ (UK)| n. |角膜|The transparent front part of the eye that covers the iris and pupil and admits light to the interior.|Example 1:  The doctor examined his cornea for scratches. 医生检查了他的角膜是否有划痕。

Example 2: A contact lens rests on the cornea. 隐形眼镜放在角膜上。|Synonym:  --  (No direct single-word synonym, but related terms include "ocular surface" and "anterior segment")|Antonym: -- (No antonym)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cornea (tela or tunica), meaning "horny (membrane or covering)," from Latin corneus "horny," from cornu "horn."  The term reflects the cornea's tough, somewhat horn-like texture.
