machiavellisms:/ˌmækiəˈvɛliənɪzəmz/ | n. | 马基雅维利主义，权术，不择手段的行为 |  The employment of cunning and duplicity in statecraft or in general conduct, especially the use of deceit, treachery, and expediency.  Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian political philosopher, whose book "The Prince" described such practices. |  His machiavellisms alienated his colleagues. 他的权谋诡计疏远了他的同事。 | synonyms: cunning, duplicity, guile, trickery, subterfuge, manipulation | antonyms: honesty, integrity, forthrightness | noun
