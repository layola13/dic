esquimau: /ˈeskɪmoʊ/ (US), /ˈɛskɪməʊ/ (UK) | n. | 爱斯基摩人 |  A term formerly used to refer to the Inuit and Yupik peoples of the Arctic regions of North America and Greenland.  This term is now considered outdated and often offensive, and Inuit or Yupik are the preferred terms. |  Example 1: The esquimau hunted seals for food and clothing. (爱斯基摩人猎海豹以获取食物和衣服。)
