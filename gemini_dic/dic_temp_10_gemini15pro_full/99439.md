interest:/ˈɪntrɪst/ (US), /ˈɪntrest/ (UK)| n. vt.|兴趣，利益，利息|Something that concerns, involves, draws the attention of, or excites the curiosity of a person; benefit, profit; money paid regularly at a particular rate for the use of money lent, or for delaying the repayment of a debt.|She has a keen interest in music. 她对音乐有浓厚的兴趣。|近义词：fascination, hobby, stake, concern, benefit, advantage|反义词：apathy, indifference, disadvantage|词性: noun, verb

interest:/ˈɪntrɪst/ (US), /ˈɪntrest/ (UK)| vt.|使…感兴趣|To arouse the curiosity or attention of; to involve.|The book didn't interest me at all. 这本书一点也引不起我的兴趣。|近义词：engage, fascinate, intrigue|反义词：bore, tire|词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French interesse ("to be of importance"), from Latin interesse ("to be between, to be of importance, to make a difference, to concern"), from inter ("between") + esse ("to be").
