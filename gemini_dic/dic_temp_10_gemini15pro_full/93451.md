illuminism:/ɪˌluːmɪnɪzəm/
n.
启蒙运动; 光照派
a philosophical movement of the 18th century, characterized by belief in the power of human reason and by innovations in political, religious, and educational doctrine.
The Enlightenment, also known as the Age of Reason, was a powerful intellectual and cultural movement of the 17th and 18th centuries that emphasized reason and individualism over tradition.  It produced numerous books, essays, inventions, scientific discoveries, laws, wars and revolutions. The American and French Revolutions were directly inspired by Enlightenment ideals.
近义词: enlightenment, rationalism
反义词: obscurantism, mysticism
词性: noun


Etymology:
From French *illuminisme*, from *illuminer* (“to illuminate”), from Latin *illuminare*.
