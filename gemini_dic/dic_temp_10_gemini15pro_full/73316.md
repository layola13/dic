foreclosure:/fɔːrˈkloʊʒər/| n.|丧失抵押品赎回权；取消赎回权；房屋止赎|The act of taking possession of a mortgaged property when the mortgagor fails to keep up their mortgage payments.|The bank initiated foreclosure proceedings on the property after the owner defaulted on their loan. 银行在业主拖欠贷款后启动了该房产的止赎程序。|Example:  Rising interest rates have led to a surge in foreclosures. 利率上升导致丧失抵押品赎回权的案例激增。

|Synonyms: repossession, seizure, eviction|Antonyms: redemption, reinstatement|noun


foreclosures:/fɔːrˈkloʊʒərz/| n. |丧失抵押品赎回权（复数）|The plural form of "foreclosure," referring to multiple instances of properties being taken possession of due to mortgage defaults.|Foreclosures across the country are impacting the housing market. 全国各地的止赎正在影响房地产市场。|Example: Many families are facing foreclosures due to the economic downturn. 许多家庭由于经济衰退而面临丧失抵押品赎回权。|Synonyms: repossessions, seizures, evictions|Antonyms: redemptions, reinstatements|noun
