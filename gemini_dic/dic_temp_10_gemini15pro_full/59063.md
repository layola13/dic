dwelt:/dwɛlt/ | v. | 居住，居住过 |  past tense and past participle of dwell. Lived in or at a specified place.  |  He dwelt in the forest for many years. 他在森林里住了很多年。 | 近义词：resided, lived, stayed, inhabited | 反义词：departed, left, moved | 词性: 动词

Etymology:
From Middle English *dwellen*, from Old English *dwellan* (“to lead astray, hinder, delay, deceive, err”), from Proto-Germanic *dwalōną* (“to wander, linger, go astray”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰwelh₁- (“to whirl, turn, wander”). Cognate with Old Saxon *dwalōn* (“to go astray, err”), Old Frisian *dwala* (“to err, be mistaken, delay”), Dutch *dwalen* (“to err, wander, stray, roam”), Old High German *twellan* (“to hinder”), German *täuschen* (“to deceive”), Old Norse *dvelja* (“to delay, tarry”).  Related to Old English *gedwolan* (“error, heresy”).
