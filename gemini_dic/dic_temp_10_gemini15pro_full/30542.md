cephalopoda:/ˌsefələˈpɒdə/ (US) /ˌsefələˈpɒd/ (UK)| n. |头足纲|A class of marine mollusks characterized by well-developed heads, large eyes, and a ring of sucker-bearing tentacles around the mouth.  This class includes octopuses, squid, cuttlefish, and nautiluses.|Octopuses and squid are both cephalopods.|近义词: cephalopod mollusks|反义词: None (as it is a taxonomic classification)|Noun


Etymology->
From Greek *kephalópoda*  (κεφαλόποδα), from *kephalḗ* (κεφαλή)  "head" + *poús* (πούς) "foot,"  plural *pódes* (πόδες).
