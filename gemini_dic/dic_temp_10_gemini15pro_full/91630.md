hyperbaton:/haɪˈpɜːrbətɒn/ (UK) /haɪˈpɜːrbætɑːn/ (US)| n. |倒装法，换语法|A figure of speech in which the expected word order is inverted, for emphasis or poetic effect.  It involves placing words or phrases in an unexpected order, often separating elements that would normally appear together.|From ancient Greek ὑπέρβατον (hupérbaton, “transposition”), from ὑπερβαίνειν (huperbainein, “to step over, transgress”), from ὑπέρ (hupér, “over”) + βαίνειν (bainein, “to go, walk, step”).|

Example 1:  "Object there was none. Passion there was none. I loved the old man." (Edgar Allan Poe, "The Tell-Tale Heart")  例句1： “没有对象。没有激情。我爱那个老人。”（埃德加·爱伦·坡，《泄密的心》）

Example 2:  "Some rise by sin, and some by virtue fall." (Shakespeare, Measure for Measure) 例句2：“有些人因罪恶而崛起，有些人因美德而堕落。”（莎士比亚，《一报还一报》）


|近义词: inversion, anastrophe, transposition|反义词: natural word order|词性: noun|

(Hyperbaton doesn't typically appear in set phrases. It's a stylistic device employed within sentences. The following are examples of its use, rather than phrases.)


1. "Patience you must have, my young Padawan." (Yoda-like inversion) 你必须要有耐心，我的年轻的学徒。
2. "Powerful you have become, the dark side I sense in you." (Yoda-like inversion) 你已经变得很强大，我能感觉到你身上的黑暗面。
3.  "Echoes of ages past, heard I them."  我听到了来自过去的时代的回声。
4. "From the depths of despair, rose he triumphant." 他从绝望的深渊中 triumphantly 站了起来。
5. "In the face of adversity, courage find we must." 面对逆境，我们必须找到勇气。
