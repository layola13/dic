groundling:/ˈɡraʊndlɪŋ/| n.|底层观众，平民百姓|A person who frequented the cheapest standing-room area in Elizabethan theaters, hence, a spectator of unsophisticated tastes; a commoner.|Shakespeare's groundlings would have loved this bawdy humor. 莎士比亚时代的底层观众一定会喜欢这种粗俗的幽默。|近义词：commoner, plebeian, peasant|反义词：aristocrat, nobleman|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From ground + -ling.  Originally referred to a fish that lived near the bottom of the water. Later, it was used to describe the spectators who stood in the cheapest, unroofed area near the ground in Elizabethan theatres.
