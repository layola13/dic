intercalibration:/ˌɪntərˌkælɪˈbreɪʃn/
n.|相互校准，比对校准|The process of comparing and adjusting the measurements or standards of different instruments or systems to ensure consistency and accuracy.|例句：Intercalibration of the instruments is essential for reliable results. 仪器的相互校准对于获得可靠的结果至关重要。|例句：The laboratories participated in an intercalibration exercise to validate their methods. 这些实验室参与了一项比对校准工作，以验证他们的方法。|近义词：cross-calibration, standardization, harmonization|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "discrepancy" or "incompatibility")|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
inter- + calibration
inter-: prefix meaning "between," "among," "mutually," "reciprocally" (from Latin inter "between, among")
calibration: from calibrate (v.), from French calibrer, from Italian calibrare "to measure the caliber of," from calibro "caliber," from Arabic qālib "mold, last"
