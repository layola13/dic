haustellata:/hɔːˈstɛlətə/ | n. | 吸口类动物 |A large subclass of insects within the Endopterygota that includes those that feed by sucking fluids, such as butterflies, moths, flies, and fleas. They are characterized by having mouthparts adapted for sucking and a complete metamorphosis.| Butterflies and moths belong to the Haustellata. 蝴蝶和蛾子属于吸口类动物。| synonyms: Endopterygota (partially), Antliophora | antonyms: None | noun

haustellate:/hɔːˈstɛlɪt/ | adj. | 吸管状的，虹吸式的 | Adapted for sucking; having a proboscis or sucking mouthparts. |  The mosquito's haustellate mouthparts allow it to pierce skin and draw blood. 蚊子吸管状的口器使其能够刺穿皮肤并吸血。 The butterfly has a long, haustellate proboscis for sipping nectar. 蝴蝶有一条长长的、吸管状的喙，用来吸食花蜜。 | synonyms: sucking, siphoning | antonyms: chewing, biting | adjective

Etymology: From Latin *haustus*, past participle of *haurire* "to draw, drink," + *-ellus* diminutive suffix + *-atus* adjectival suffix.  The plural form *Haustellata* is derived by adding the neuter plural ending *-a*.
