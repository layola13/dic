armet:/ˈɑːrmɪt/| n. |头盔，盔甲|A type of helmet that enclosed the entire head, popular in Europe from the 15th to the 17th centuries. It typically had a visor, a bevor (chin defense), and sometimes a gorget (neck defense).|The knight's armet gleamed in the sunlight. 骑士的头盔在阳光下闪闪发光。|近义词：helmet, helm|反义词: None|词性:noun

Etymology: From Old French *armete, diminutive of arme ("weapon"), from Latin arma ("arms, weapons"). The armet was considered a crucial piece of defensive weaponry.
