denlike:/ˈdɛnˌlaɪk/| adj.|像兽穴的，兽穴似的|Resembling a den; suggestive of a den, especially in being small, dark, and secluded.|The room was denlike, with low ceilings and heavy curtains.  这个房间像兽穴一样，天花板低矮，窗帘厚重。|The basement was dark and denlike.  地下室阴暗得像兽穴。|近义词：cavelike, cavernous, secluded|反义词：open, spacious, airy|词性:形容词

Etymology:
den + like

"den" comes from Old English "denn," meaning "lair of a wild animal."  "like" comes from Old English "lic," meaning "having the resemblance of."  Therefore, "denlike" literally means "having the resemblance of a den."
