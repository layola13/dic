countertenor:/ˈkaʊntərˌtɛnər/| n.|男高音|A male singer with a vocal range equivalent to that of a female contralto or mezzo-soprano, typically achieved by the use of falsetto.|The countertenor's voice soared above the other singers. 男高音的声音在其他歌手之上飙升。|Synonyms: alto, contralto|Antonyms: bass, baritone|noun


countertenors:/ˈkaʊntərˌtɛnərz/| n.|男高音们|Plural form of countertenor, referring to multiple male singers with high vocal ranges.|The countertenors blended their voices beautifully in the chorus. 男高音们在合唱中优美地融合了他们的声音。|Synonyms: altos, contraltos|Antonyms: basses, baritones|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *contre tenor* (attested in the Latinized form *contratenor*), meaning "against the tenor," referring to a voice part that sang a countermelody against the tenor line.  The "counter-" prefix indicates "against" or "opposite."
