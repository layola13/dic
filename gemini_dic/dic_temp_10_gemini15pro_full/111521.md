loathe:/loʊð/| v. |憎恶，厌恶|To feel intense dislike or disgust for something or someone.|I loathe spiders. 我讨厌蜘蛛。|
She loathes hypocrisy. 她厌恶虚伪。|
Synonyms: detest, abhor, despise, hate|
Antonyms: love, adore, cherish, like|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lothen*, from Old English *lāþian* (“to be hateful, loathsome, or hostile; to be displeasing; to offend; to injure”), from Proto-Germanic *laþōną* (“to invite, summon; to blame, accuse”), from Proto-Indo-European *lēdʰ- (“to be hateful; to incite”). Cognate with Old Saxon *lāthian* (“to loathe”), Old High German *ladōn* (“to invite, summon”), Old Norse *laða* (“to invite”), Gothic *𐌻𐌰𐌸𐍉𐌽* (*laþōn*, “to invite”).
