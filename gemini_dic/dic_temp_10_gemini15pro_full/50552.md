deputes:/ˈdɛpjʊˌteɪz/| n. |代表，议员，委员|(usually plural) A person appointed to represent others.  Derived from Old French *depute* and ultimately from Latin *deputatus*, past participle of *deputare* "to assign, appoint." |The deputes met to discuss the new legislation. 代表们开会讨论新的立法。|Synonyms: representatives, delegates, commissioners, envoys|Antonyms: rulers, leaders (in some contexts)|noun
