mitochondrion: /ˌmaɪtəˈkɒndriən/ (UK) /ˌmaɪtəˈkɑːndriən/ (US)| n. |线粒体|An organelle found in large numbers in most cells, in which the biochemical processes of respiration and energy production occur. It has a double membrane, the inner layer being folded inward to form layers (cristae).|Mitochondria are the powerhouses of the cell. 线粒体是细胞的能量工厂。|The mitochondrion plays a crucial role in cellular respiration. 线粒体在细胞呼吸中起着至关重要的作用。|近义词:None (Specific organelle)|反义词:None|词性:Noun

mitochondria: /ˌmaɪtəˈkɒndriə/ (UK) /ˌmaɪtəˈkɑːndriə/ (US) | n. |线粒体（复数）|Plural form of mitochondrion.|The mitochondria generate most of the chemical energy needed to power the cell's biochemical reactions. 线粒体产生大部分细胞生化反应所需的化学能。| Damaged mitochondria can lead to various diseases.  受损的线粒体会导致各种疾病。|近义词:None (Specific organelle)|反义词:None|词性:Noun
