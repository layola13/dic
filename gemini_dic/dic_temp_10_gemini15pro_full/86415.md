heeders:/ˈhiːdərz/ | n. | 注意者；听从者；服从者 | People who pay attention to something, especially advice or a warning. |  例句：The heeders of the warning escaped the fire. 注意到警告的人逃脱了火灾。| 近义词：listeners, followers, observers | 反义词：ignorers, disbelievers | 词性: noun

Etymology: From the verb "heed," which comes from Old English "hēdan," meaning "to pay attention to, observe, care for."
