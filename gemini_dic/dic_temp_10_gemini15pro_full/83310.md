gynobase:/ˈdʒaɪnəˌbeɪs/ | n. |雌蕊基; 子房柄|A fleshy, columnar extension of the receptacle in certain flowers (e.g., of the mint family Lamiaceae) that supports the gynoecium (the female reproductive structures) and often persists as a small projection below the fruit.|The gynobase of the mint flower is quite prominent.  薄荷花雌蕊基很突出。|Synonym: gynophore|Antonym: N/A|Noun
