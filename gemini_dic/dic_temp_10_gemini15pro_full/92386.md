hypocrite:/ˈhɪpəkrɪt/ (US), /ˈhɪpəkrɪt/ (UK)| n. |伪君子，伪善者|A person who pretends to have virtues, moral or religious beliefs, principles, etc., that he or she does not actually possess, especially a person whose actions belie stated beliefs.|He's a hypocrite – he talks about the importance of family values, but he never spends any time with his own children. 他是个伪君子 – 他谈论家庭价值观的重要性，但他从不陪自己的孩子。|Synonyms: charlatan, fraud, imposter, phony, pretender|Antonyms: saint, righteous person|noun

hypocrites:/ˈhɪpəkrɪts/ (US), /ˈhɪpəkrɪts/ (UK)| n. |伪君子们（hypocrite的复数形式）|Plural form of hypocrite, referring to a group of people who pretend to have virtues, moral or religious beliefs, etc., that they do not actually possess. |The hypocrites were quick to condemn others for their flaws, while ignoring their own. 这些伪君子们迅速谴责别人的缺点，却无视自己的缺点。|Synonyms: charlatans, frauds, imposters, phonies, pretenders|Antonyms: saints, righteous people|noun
