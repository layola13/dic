gul:/ɡʌl/| n.|受骗的人；容易上当的人；海鸥|A person who is easily tricked or cheated; a seagull.| He was such a gul that he believed everything the salesman said. 他太容易上当了，竟然相信了销售员说的所有话。| The gulls circled overhead, looking for scraps of food. 海鸥在头顶盘旋，寻找食物残渣。| synonyms: dupe, sucker, fool; seagull | antonyms: skeptic, cynic | noun


gul:/ɡʌl/| v.|吞食；狼吞虎咽|To swallow or eat greedily.| The dog gulled down the entire bowl of food in seconds. 那条狗几秒钟就把整碗食物吞了下去。| He gulled the last of his coffee. 他大口喝完了剩下的咖啡。| synonyms: devour, gobble, gulp | antonyms: nibble, sip | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *gulr, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to the verb *gula "to roar, bellow".  The sense of "dupe, credulous person" developed later, perhaps influenced by the idea of a bird easily caught.
