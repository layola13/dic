obsequious: /əbˈsiːkwiəs/ /ɑːbˈsiːkwiəs/| adj. | 谄媚的，奉承的 | Excessively eager to please or obey; fawning. Characterized by or showing servile complaisance or deference; fawning.|  He was so obsequious to the boss that it was sickening to watch. 他对老板阿谀奉承的样子令人作呕。|  The obsequious waiter bowed deeply as he presented the menu.  那个谄媚的侍者深深鞠了一躬，递上了菜单。| 近义词: servile, fawning, sycophantic, subservient | 反义词: assertive, independent, rebellious | 词性: adjective

obsequious: /əbˈsiːkwiəs/ /ɑːbˈsiːkwiəs/| noun.(rare) | 谄媚者 | A person who behaves in an obsequious way. | He's known to be an obsequious to anyone in power. 他是出了名的对有权势的人阿谀奉承。 |  She surrounded herself with obsequious followers who constantly praised her. 她身边围绕着一群谄媚的追随者，他们不断地赞美她。 | 近义词: sycophant, flatterer, toady | 反义词: rebel, nonconformist | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin obsequiosus (“compliant, obedient”), from obsequium (“compliance; dutiful service”), from obsequi (“to comply with, obey, yield to”), from ob- (“towards”) + sequi (“to follow”).
