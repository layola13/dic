dyspraxia:/dɪsˈpræksiə/
|n.|运动障碍，运用障碍|A disorder affecting the ability to plan and execute coordinated movements. It is often characterized by clumsiness and difficulty with fine motor skills.|He has dyspraxia, which makes it hard for him to tie his shoelaces. 他患有运动障碍，这使得他难以系鞋带。|近义词:developmental coordination disorder (DCD), motor learning difficulty|反义词:coordination, dexterity|词性:noun


Etymology:  From Greek *dyspraksia*, from *dys-* (bad, difficult) + *praxis* (doing, act).
