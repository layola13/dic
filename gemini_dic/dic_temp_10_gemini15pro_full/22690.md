bore:/bɔːr/ (UK) /bɔːr/ (US)| v. n.|钻孔；使厌烦；令人讨厌的人或事|To make a hole in something using a tool; to make someone feel tired and uninterested by being dull and tedious; a person or thing that is tedious and uninteresting.|He bored a hole in the wood. 他在木头上钻了个孔。|synonyms: drill, pierce, tire, weary|antonyms: interest, excite, amuse|verb

bore:/bɔːr/ (UK) /bɔːr/ (US)| n.|令人讨厌的人或事；钻孔|A person or thing that is tedious and uninteresting; the hollow inside of a tube or gun barrel.|That meeting was such a bore. 那次会议真是无聊透顶。|synonyms: nuisance, pest, drag|antonyms: delight, pleasure, thrill|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *borian, from Proto-Germanic *burōną (“to bore, pierce”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰwer- (“to cut, pierce, prick”). Cognate with Old Frisian bora (“to bore”), Old Saxon borōn (“to bore”), Old High German borōn (“to bore”), Old Norse bora (“to bore”), Gothic 𐌱𐌰𐌿𐍂𐌰𐌽 (bauran, “to bore”).
