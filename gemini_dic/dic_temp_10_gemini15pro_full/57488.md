drag-on:/ˈdræɡɒn/ (UK) /ˈdræɡɑːn/ (US)| n.|龙; 拖延很久的事; 令人讨厌的人|A mythical creature typically depicted as a large, winged reptile with magical powers. Something that is tedious and lasts for a long time. A difficult or unpleasant person.|例句：The dragon breathed fire. 龙喷出了火。

例句：The meeting was a real drag-on.  这个会议真是拖拖拉拉。


近义词：monster, beast (for mythical creature); nuisance, bore (for tedious thing); ogre, harpy (for unpleasant person)|反义词：(No direct opposite for mythical creature)  |词性:noun


drag-on:/ˈdræɡɒn/ (UK) /ˈdræɡɑːn/ (US)| vi.|拖延; 缓慢费力地行进|To continue for too long; to proceed slowly and with difficulty.|例句：The negotiations dragged on for months. 谈判拖了几个月。

例句：He dragged on for miles, exhausted and injured. 他精疲力竭，伤痕累累，步履艰难地走了好几英里。
