acetylcholine:/əˌsiːtɪlˈkoʊliːn/ /əˌsiːtəlˈkəʊliːn/| n.|乙酰胆碱|A neurotransmitter, a chemical messenger that transmits nerve impulses across the synapse between nerve cells.|Acetylcholine plays a crucial role in muscle contractions. 乙酰胆碱在肌肉收缩中起着至关重要的作用。

Acetylcholine levels are often reduced in Alzheimer's patients. 阿尔茨海默病患者的乙酰胆碱水平通常会降低。|近义词:neurotransmitter (更广义)|反义词:cholinesterase inhibitor|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From acetyl + choline.  Acetyl comes from Latin *acetum "vinegar". Choline comes from Greek *cholē "bile".  The name reflects its initial isolation from bile.
