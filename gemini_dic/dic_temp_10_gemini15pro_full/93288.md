ilk:/ɪlk/
|n.|种类；类型|kind, sort, class; family, breed|People of that ilk tend to be very conservative. 那种类型的人往往非常保守。|kind, sort, type|opposite, different|noun
etymology->From Old English *ilca (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *aljaz (“same”). Cognate with Scots ilk (“same”), Icelandic allr (“all”).  More at else. Originally meant “same”. The current meaning developed from phrases like “of that ilk”, originally meaning “of the same place, name, or territorial designation”, which came to mean "of that kind."
