assizes:/əˈsaɪzɪz/
n.
巡回审判;  (旧时)定期巡回法庭
A form of periodic criminal court sessions held in English counties, typically for serious crimes.  The judges "rode circuit" to hold these sessions.
The assizes were held twice a year in each county.  该郡每年举行两次巡回审判。
The judge sentenced the prisoner at the assizes. 法官在巡回法庭上判处了囚犯。
Synonyms: court, tribunal, court of law
Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a specific court session.  Perhaps "informal hearing" or "arbitration" could be considered loosely antonymous in terms of formality and legal weight.)
Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French assises, meaning "sessions," ultimately from Latin assidere, meaning "to sit beside."
