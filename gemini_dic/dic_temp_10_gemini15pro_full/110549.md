limerick: /ˈlɪmərɪk/ | n. | 打油诗; Limerick | A five-line poem with a specific rhyme scheme (AABBA) and rhythm, often humorous and sometimes obscene.  | Example: There once was a fellow named Kyle,  Whose shoes were tied up with a smile. He tripped on the laces, and fell on his face, is now laying still for a while.| Synonym:  nonsense verse, doggerel | Antonym:  epic, sonnet | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Limerick, a city in Ireland, though the exact connection is uncertain. Possibly related to a parlor game involving composing and singing such verses.
