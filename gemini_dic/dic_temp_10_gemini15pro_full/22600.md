bootstrapping:/ˈbuːtˌstræpɪŋ/
n.|自举，引导，自力更生|The process of starting something with limited resources, often by using existing resources to generate more resources.  A technique in statistics for estimating properties of an estimator (such as its variance) by measuring those properties when sampling from an approximating distribution.|He built his business from the ground up through bootstrapping, reinvesting early profits to fuel growth. 他通过自举的方式白手起家，将早期利润再投资以推动增长。|synonyms: self-starting, self-financing, cold-starting|antonyms:  dependent, subsidized|noun

bootstrapped:/ˈbuːtˌstræpt/
v. |用引导程序启动，自力更生|Past tense of "bootstrap," referring to having started something with limited resources.|She bootstrapped her company with just a small loan and a lot of hard work. 她仅凭一小笔贷款和大量的辛勤工作就自力更生地创办了公司。|synonyms: self-started, self-financed|antonyms: relied, depended|verb
