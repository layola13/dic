drawcansir: /drɔːˈkænsər/ | n. | 吹牛大王；虚张声势的人 | A bragging, blustering bully, especially one who is cowardly.  Derived from a character in George Villiers, Duke of Buckingham's play "The Rehearsal" (1671), who boasted of killing all the characters in the play single-handedly. |  He acts like a real drawcansir, but I bet he'd run at the first sign of trouble. 他表现得像个十足的吹牛大王，但我打赌他一遇到麻烦就会逃之夭夭。 |  braggart, bully, boaster, swaggerer | coward, milksop | noun
