offside:/ˌɒfˈsaɪd/ /ˌɔːfˈsaɪd/| adj. n.|越位的，越位位置|In a position in a game, especially football or hockey, that is not allowed by the rules, because a player is closer to their opponents' goal line than the ball when the ball is passed to them.|The referee blew the whistle for offside. 裁判吹哨判罚越位。|近义词：illegal, disallowed|反义词：onside|词性:形容词, 名词

offside:/ˌɒfˈsaɪd/ /ˌɔːfˈsaɪd/| n.|越位|The state of being offside.|He was caught in an offside position. 他被抓到越位了。|近义词：illegal position|反义词：onside position|词性:名词

词源:  From off + side.  Originally (18th century) used more generally, as in hunting, meaning "to one side, out of the way". The specialized sporting sense emerged in the 19th century, particularly in association with football.
