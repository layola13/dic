coagulation:/koʊˌæɡjʊˈleɪʃən/| n. |凝固，凝结|The process by which a liquid, especially blood, changes to a solid or semi-solid state.|Blood coagulation is essential to stop bleeding. 血液凝固对止血至关重要。|The coagulation of the milk is the first step in cheese making. 牛奶的凝固是奶酪制作的第一步。|近义词：clotting, thickening|反义词: thinning, liquefaction|词性:noun

coagulate:/koʊˈæɡjʊleɪt/| v. |凝结，凝固|To change from a liquid to a thick mass or solid.|The blood began to coagulate around the wound. 血液开始在伤口周围凝结。|Heat can coagulate egg whites.  热可以使蛋清凝固。|近义词：clot, thicken|反义词: thin, liquefy|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *coagulātiōn- (stem of coagulātiō), from Latin coagulātus (past participle of coagulāre ‘to curdle’), from co- ‘together’ + agere ‘to drive, do’.
