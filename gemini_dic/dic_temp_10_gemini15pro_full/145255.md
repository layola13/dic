overblown:/ˌoʊvərˈbloʊn/| adj.|夸大的；过分渲染的；（花）凋谢的|inflated; exaggerated; excessively elaborate or ornate; (of a flower) past its prime|The media coverage of the event was overblown. 媒体对该事件的报道过于夸大。|The roses were overblown and beginning to drop their petals. 玫瑰花开过了，开始掉落花瓣。|近义词：exaggerated, inflated, overstated, pretentious|反义词：understated, modest, simple|词性:形容词
词源: From over + blown (past participle of blow).  Originally referring to flowers that have blossomed too much and are past their prime.  The figurative sense of "exaggerated" developed later.
