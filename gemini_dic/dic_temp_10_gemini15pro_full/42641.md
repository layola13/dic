countersign:/ˈkaʊntərˌsaɪn/ (US), /ˈkaʊntəsaɪn/ (UK)| n. v.|副署，会签；口令|A signature added to another signature on a document to attest to its authenticity.  To add such a signature. |例句：The check requires a countersign. 这张支票需要副签。|近义词：endorse, co-sign, ratify|反义词：reject, refuse| n.

countersign:/ˈkaʊntərˌsaɪn/ (US), /ˈkaʊntəsaɪn/ (UK)| v.|副署，会签|To add a validating signature. To confirm something.|例句：The general countersigned the order. 将军在命令上副署。|近义词：endorse, co-sign, ratify|反义词：reject, refuse|v.



ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *contresigner*, from contre- “against, opposite” + signer “to sign”.  Ultimately from Latin contra- “against” and signare "to mark".
