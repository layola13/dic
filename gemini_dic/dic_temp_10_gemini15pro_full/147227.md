overmeddle:/ˌoʊvərˈmɛdəl/| v.| 过度干预，多管闲事 | To interfere in something that is not one's concern; to meddle excessively. | She tends to overmeddle in her children's lives, causing them resentment. 她总是过度干预孩子的生活，导致他们不满。| He overmeddled in the dispute, making the situation worse. 他过度干预了这场争端，使情况变得更糟。| 近义词: interfere, meddle, intrude | 反义词: ignore, neglect | 词性: 动词

词源:
From over- + meddle.  "Over-" acts as a prefix indicating "excessively" or "too much".  "Meddle" comes from Old French "medler" meaning "to mix, mingle," which in turn derives from the Latin "miscere" "to mix."  Thus, the word literally means "to mix in too much."
