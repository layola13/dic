lunet:/luːˈnɛt/ | n. | 月牙形,半月形,小新月形|A crescent-shaped figure or object, especially an architectural feature.| The architect incorporated lunettes above the doorways. 建筑师在门口上方加入了月牙形的装饰。|Synonyms: crescent, demilune, half-moon| Antonyms: full moon | noun

lunette:/luːˈnɛt/ | n. | (尤指护墙上的)半月形通气窗,半月窗 | A semicircular window, especially one over a doorway or in a vaulted ceiling.| The lunette provided ventilation and natural light to the attic. 半月窗为阁楼提供了通风和自然光。| Synonyms: semicircular window, fanlight| Antonyms: square window | noun

lunette:/luːˈnɛt/ | n. | (护目镜的)扁平透镜|A flattened, crescent-shaped lens, as in a pair of goggles.| The lunettes of the goggles protected the pilot's eyes from the wind. 护目镜的扁平透镜保护飞行员的眼睛免受风吹。| Synonyms: lens, eye protector| Antonyms: N/A | noun

lunette:/luːˈnɛt/ | n. | (马蹄铁)侧夹| A crescent-shaped clip attached to a horseshoe. | The lunette helped secure the horseshoe to the horse's hoof. 侧夹帮助将马蹄铁固定在马蹄上。 | Synonyms: clip, fastener| Antonyms: N/A | noun

Etymology: From French "lunette," diminutive of "lune" (moon), from Latin "luna" (moon).
