coagulative:/koʊˈæɡjʊlətɪv/| adj.|凝固的，凝结的|Causing coagulation; tending to coagulate.  Relating to or involving coagulation.|The doctor prescribed coagulative medication to stop the bleeding. 医生开了凝血药物来止血。|Synonyms: clotting, thickening, congealing|Antonyms: thinning, liquefying|adjective


coagulate:/koʊˈæɡjʊleɪt/| v.|凝结，凝固| (of a fluid, especially blood) change to a solid or semisolid state.|The blood began to coagulate around the wound. 血液开始在伤口周围凝结。|Synonyms: clot, thicken, congeal|Antonyms: thin, liquefy|verb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *coagulativus, from Latin coagulatus (past participle of coagulare “to curdle”), from co- "together" + agere "to drive, do."
