cezar:/ˈseɪzər/| n.|凯撒（罗马帝国的统治者称号，尤指尤利乌斯·凯撒）; 沙皇（或 tsar）|A cezar is a title of imperial character associated primarily with Julius Caesar, later adopted by Roman and Byzantine emperors, and subsequently used by Tsars/Czars.|Julius Caesar was a powerful Roman cezar. 尤利乌斯·凯撒是一位强大的罗马皇帝。|The Russian czars were also known as cezars.  俄国沙皇也被称为凯撒。|Synonym: emperor, tsar, czar|Antonym: commoner, subject|noun
