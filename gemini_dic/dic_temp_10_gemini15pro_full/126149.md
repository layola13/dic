mutiny:/ˈmjuːtɪni/ | n. |叛乱，兵变 |An open rebellion against constituted authority, especially by sailors or soldiers against their officers. |The crew staged a mutiny and took control of the ship. 船员发动了叛变，控制了船只。| Synonyms: rebellion, revolt, uprising, insurrection | Antonyms: obedience, submission | noun

mutiny:/ˈmjuːtɪni/ | vi. |叛变，反抗 |To engage in mutiny; revolt. |The soldiers mutinied against their cruel commander. 士兵们反抗他们残暴的指挥官。| Synonyms: rebel, revolt, rise up, resist | Antonyms: obey, submit | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mutiner (Modern French mutiner), from meute "mutiny, riot, pack of hounds," of uncertain origin, possibly from Late Latin *movita, from Latin movere "to move."
