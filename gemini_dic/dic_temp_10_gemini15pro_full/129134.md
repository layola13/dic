no-ball:/ˈnəʊ bɔːl/ (UK) /ˈnoʊ bɔːl/ (US)| n.|无球，犯规投球|A delivery in cricket that is illegal because the bowler's front foot lands beyond the popping crease or because the ball is bowled above waist height without bouncing. |The umpire called a no-ball, giving the batting team an extra run. |裁判判罚无球，给击球队加一分。|Synonyms: illegal delivery, foul ball (in other sports)|Antonyms: legal delivery, fair ball (in other sports)|noun

no-ball:/ˈnəʊ bɔːl/ (UK) /ˈnoʊ bɔːl/ (US)| n.|无球，犯规投球|An advantage or opportunity that is lost or missed due to a mistake or error. (Figurative use, less common) |He dropped the ball on that project, it was a real no-ball. |他在那个项目上犯了错误，真是个失误。|Synonyms: mistake, error, blunder|Antonyms: success, achievement|noun
