enquirer:/ɪnˈkwaɪərər/ (UK), /ɪnˈkwaɪrər/ (US)| n. |询问者，打听者|A person who asks for information.|The enquirer wanted to know the price of the car.  这位询问者想知道这辆车的价格。|Synonym: inquirer, questioner, investigator|Antonym: respondent, answerer|noun

enquirer:/ɪnˈkwaɪərər/ (UK), /ɪnˈkwaɪrər/ (US)| n. |打听者，探问者|Someone who seeks knowledge or information, often through investigation.|The newspaper sent an enquirer to investigate the rumors. 报社派了一名探问者去调查传闻。|Synonym: researcher, seeker, inquisitor|Antonym: informant, source|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English enquerour, from Anglo-Norman enquerour, from Old French enquereor, from Late Latin inquīsitor, from Latin inquīsītor (“examiner, investigator”), agent noun from past participle stem of inquīrere (“to inquire”), from in- (“in, into, on, upon”) + quaerere (“to seek, ask”).
