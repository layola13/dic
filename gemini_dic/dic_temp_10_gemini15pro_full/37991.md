complicative:/ˈkɒmplɪkətɪv/ /ˈkɑːmplɪkeɪtɪv/| adj.|使复杂的，使更难懂的，使更麻烦的|Making something more difficult or confusing; introducing more complications.|This new regulation is complicative and makes the process harder.  这项新规定很复杂，使流程更加困难。|The surgery was complicative due to the patient's pre-existing conditions. 由于病人先前存在的疾病，手术变得复杂。|近义词：complex, involved, intricate, convoluted|反义词：simple, straightforward, easy|词性:形容词

complicate:/ˈkɒmplɪkeɪt/ /ˈkɑːmplɪkeɪt/| v.|使复杂化，使更难懂，使更麻烦|To make something more difficult or confusing by adding more details or problems.|The situation is already difficult; don't complicate it further.  情况已经很困难了，不要再复杂化了。|Several factors complicated the investigation.  几个因素使调查复杂化了。|近义词：confuse, muddle, entangle|反义词：simplify, clarify, streamline|词性:动词

complication:/ˌkɒmplɪˈkeɪʃn/ /ˌkɑːmplɪˈkeɪʃn/| n.|并发症，复杂化，难题|A new problem or illness that makes someone’s existing illness or situation more serious; the act of making something more complicated.|The patient developed serious complications after surgery.  病人术后出现严重并发症。|There were complications in the peace talks. 和谈中出现了难题。|近义词：difficulty, problem, snag, hitch|反义词：solution, simplification, ease|词性:名词
