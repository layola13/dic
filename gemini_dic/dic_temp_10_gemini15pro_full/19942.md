biodegradations: /ˌbaɪoʊˌdɛɡrəˈdeɪʃənz/ | n. | 生物降解（复数） | The process by which organic substances are broken down into simpler substances by the action of living organisms, such as bacteria, fungi, or other microorganisms.  | Example 1: The biodegradations of plastics are crucial for reducing environmental pollution.  塑料的生物降解对于减少环境污染至关重要。

Example 2:  Scientists are studying the biodegradations of various pollutants in the soil. 科学家们正在研究土壤中各种污染物的生物降解。 | Synonyms: decomposition, decay, breakdown | Antonyms: synthesis, buildup, accumulation | Noun
