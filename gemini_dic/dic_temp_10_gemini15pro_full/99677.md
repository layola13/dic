interlingualisms:/ˌɪntərˈlɪŋɡwəlɪzəmz/ | n. |语际词;国际通用词 |Words or phrases that are borrowed from one language into another with little or no change in form or meaning, often due to cultural or technological influence.| Example: The word "computer" is an interlingualism, used in many languages with minimal adaptation.  “计算机”这个词是一个语际词，在许多语言中使用，几乎没有改编。| Example:  "Okay" is a widely recognized interlingualism, understood across numerous cultures. “好的”是一个广为人知的语际词，在许多文化中都能理解。|Synonym: loanwords, borrowings| Antonym:  neologisms (newly coined words specific to a language) | noun
