enervatingly:/ɪˈnɜːrvˌeɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 令人衰弱地，使人失去活力地 | in a way that causes someone to feel drained of energy or vitality. | The heat was enervatingly intense, making any physical activity a struggle.  炎热的天气令人衰弱，使得任何体力活动都成为一种挣扎。 | Example: The humid weather hung enervatingly over the city. 湿热的天气笼罩着整个城市，令人感到疲倦。 | Synonyms: weakeningly, exhaustingly, debilitatingly | Antonyms: invigoratingly, energizingly | Part of Speech: Adverb

enervate:/ˈɛnərveɪt/ | v. | 使衰弱，使无力 | cause (someone) to feel drained of energy or vitality. | The long journey enervated the travelers. 漫长的旅程使旅行者感到疲惫不堪。 | Example: The illness had enervated him to the point of collapse. 疾病使他虚弱到快要崩溃的地步。 | Synonyms: weaken, exhaust, debilitate | Antonyms: invigorate, energize | Part of Speech: Verb

Etymology: From Latin *enervatus*, past participle of *enervare* "to weaken, deprive of nerves or sinews," from *e-* "out" + *nervus* "sinew, nerve."
