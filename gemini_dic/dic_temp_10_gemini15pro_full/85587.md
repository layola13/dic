hatred:/ˈheɪtrɪd/| n.|仇恨，憎恶|An intense feeling of dislike or aversion.|She felt a burning hatred for her abuser. 她对虐待她的人感到强烈的憎恨。|synonyms: animosity, loathing, detestation, abhorrence, aversion|antonyms: love, affection, fondness, liking|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hatrede*, from Old English *hetru*, *hatred*, from Proto-Germanic *hatrō*, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱod-/*ḱed- (“hate”). Cognate with Old Frisian *hetrede* (“hatred”), Old Saxon *hatar* (“hate”), Old High German *hazzar* (“hate”), Old Norse *hatr* (“hatred”), Gothic *𐌷𐌰𐍄𐌹𐍃* (*hatis*) (“hate”).
