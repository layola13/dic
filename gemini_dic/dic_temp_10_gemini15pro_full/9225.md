antipapists:/ˌæntiˈpeɪpɪsts/
|n.| 反教皇主义者；反对教皇统治的人|A person who opposes the authority of the Pope, especially in England during the Reformation.|During the reign of Elizabeth I, antipapists feared the return of Catholic rule.  在伊丽莎白一世统治时期，反教皇主义者担心天主教统治的回归。|Synonyms: Protestant, reformer|Antonyms: Papist, Catholic|noun


antipapist:/ˌæntiˈpeɪpɪst/
|adj.| 反对教皇的；反教皇主义的|Opposed to the authority of the Pope.|Antipapist sentiment was strong in England after Henry VIII's break with Rome.  亨利八世与罗马决裂后，英国的反教皇情绪高涨。|Synonyms: anti-papal, anti-Roman|Antonyms: pro-papal, pro-Roman|adjective
