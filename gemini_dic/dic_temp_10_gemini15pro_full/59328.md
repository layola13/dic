earldorman:/ˈɜːrlˌdɔːrmən/| n. |伯爵；郡长；古代英国贵族的头衔|A high-ranking nobleman in Anglo-Saxon England, originally a royal official governing a shire or province, later developing into a hereditary title of nobility, roughly equivalent to an earl.|The earldorman ruled over a large territory. 郡长统治着一片广阔的领土。|Synonyms: earl, governor, magistrate, lord|Antonyms: commoner, peasant|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ealdorman "elder man, chief, captain, leader," from ealdor "elder, chief, prince" (from Proto-Germanic *aldiz "growing old, adult," from PIE root *al- "to grow, nourish") + man "man."  The word evolved to "earl" in later Middle English.
