cue someone in:/kjuː ˈsʌmwʌn ɪn/ | phrasal verb | 通知某人；使某人了解情况 | To inform someone about something they need to know, especially something secret or private. |  "Could you cue me in on what's been happening while I was away?"  你能告诉我我不在的时候发生了什么吗？ | fill someone in, bring someone up to speed,  inform someone, brief someone |  leave someone out, keep someone in the dark | verb phrase


cue:/kjuː/ | n. | 提示，暗示 | A signal for something to happen. | The conductor gave the cue for the orchestra to begin. | signal, prompt, hint | - | noun

someone:/ˈsʌmwʌn/ | pronoun | 某人 | An unknown or unspecified person. |  Someone left their umbrella here. | somebody, anybody | nobody | pronoun

in:/ɪn/ | adv. prep. adj. | 在...里面，进入 | Expressing inclusion or location within. | He went in the house. | inside, within | out | adverb, preposition, adjective
