pants: /pænts/ (American English) /pænʔts/ (British English)
| n. | 裤子 |  A garment extending from the waist to the ankle or the knee, covering each leg separately. |  He wore a pair of blue pants. 他穿了一条蓝色裤子。 | trousers, slacks, jeans | skirt, dress | Noun


pant's: /pænts/ (American English) /pænʔts/ (British English)
| n. | 裤子(所有格) | Belonging to or associated with pants.  This signifies possession, similar to adding 's to a name. | The pant's leg was ripped. 裤腿撕破了。 | - | - | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *pantes*, alteration (influenced by French *pance*) of *hosen*, plural of *hose* (meaning "covering for the legs and lower part of the trunk"), from Proto-West Germanic *hosa*, from Proto-Germanic *hosǭ*, from Proto-Indo-European *kos(o)-* (meaning "covering").  The modern sense of "pants" emerged in the 19th century.
