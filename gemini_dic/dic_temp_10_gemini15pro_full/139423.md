obsessionalism: /əbˈseʃənəlɪzəm/ | n. | 痴迷; 着迷; 困扰 | The state of being obsessed or preoccupied with something.  A persistent and intrusive preoccupation with an idea or feeling. | He suffered from obsessionalism with cleanliness. 他对清洁有着近乎痴迷的困扰。|  Her obsessionalism with her weight was damaging her health. 她对体重的痴迷正在损害她的健康。 | 近义词: fixation, preoccupation, obsession | 反义词: indifference, apathy | 词性: noun

Etymology: From "obsession" + "-ism".  "Obsession" comes from Late Latin obsessionem (nominative obsessio) "a siege, blockade," figuratively "a harassing by an evil spirit," from Latin obsidere "to besiege, occupy," from ob "against" (see ob-) + sedere "to sit" (see sedentary).
