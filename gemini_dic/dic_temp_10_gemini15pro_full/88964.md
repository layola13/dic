holiday-maker:/ˈhɒlədeɪ ˌmeɪkə(r)/ (US: /ˈhɑːlədeɪ ˌmeɪkər/)| n.|度假者，旅游者|From "holiday" + "maker" (one who makes or takes something).  "Holiday" originates from "holy day," referring to a religious festival. Over time, its meaning broadened to encompass any day of exemption from work. "Maker" comes from Old English "macian," meaning "to make" or "to do." Thus, a "holiday-maker" is someone who "makes" or "takes" a holiday.|
