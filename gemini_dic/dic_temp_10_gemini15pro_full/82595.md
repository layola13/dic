guardians:/ˈɡɑːrdiənz/ (US) /ˈɡɑːdiənz/ (UK)| n.|监护人，守护者|A person who has the legal authority and responsibility to care for the personal and property interests of another person, particularly a minor or someone legally incapable of managing their own affairs.|The guardians of the children were very strict. 孩子们的监护人非常严格。|custodians, protectors, keepers|enemies, adversaries|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *garden, *guardien, from Frankish *warding, from Proto-Germanic *wardōną (“to watch, guard”), from *wardō (“watch, guard”). Cognate with Old High German wartōn (“to watch”), Old English weardian (“to guard, watch over”).
