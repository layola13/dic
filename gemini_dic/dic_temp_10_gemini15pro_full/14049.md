austere:/ɔːˈstɪr/ | adj. | 简朴的，苦行的，严厉的 |  Severe or strict in manner, attitude, or appearance.  Having an extremely plain and simple style or appearance; unadorned.  (of living conditions or a way of life) having no comforts or luxuries; harsh. | The austere courtroom intimidated the witnesses.  简朴的法庭让证人们感到害怕。 |  He maintained an austere lifestyle, devoid of luxury. 他保持着简朴的生活方式，没有任何奢侈品。 |  They lived in an austere cabin in the mountains. 他们在山里一个简陋的小屋里生活。 | simple, plain, strict, severe, harsh | luxurious, comfortable, ornate, lenient | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin austerus (“harsh, severe, sour, rough”), from Ancient Greek αὐστηρός (austērós, “rough to the taste, harsh, severe, stern, grave, solemn”).
