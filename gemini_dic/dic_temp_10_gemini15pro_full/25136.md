buckhound:/ˈbʌkhaʊnd/| n. |猎鹿犬|A dog, originally a large breed, trained to hunt deer; now, any of several breeds of hound.|The buckhound pursued the deer relentlessly through the forest.|Synonyms:  stag hound, deerhound, harrier|Antonyms: N/A (No direct antonym for a specific breed of dog)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *buck* (male deer) + *hound* (a type of hunting dog).  The term dates back to the 14th century.
