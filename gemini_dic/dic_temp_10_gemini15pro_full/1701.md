acorn:/ˈeɪkɔːrn/ (US) /ˈeɪkɔːn/ (UK)| n.|橡子；橡实|The acorn is the fruit of the oak tree, containing a single seed enclosed in a tough, leathery shell.|Squirrels bury acorns for the winter. 松鼠把橡子埋起来过冬。

The trail was littered with acorns. 小径上到处都是橡子。|近义词：oak nut|反义词:|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *æcern, from Proto-Germanic *akraną (“acorn”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eǵ- (“sharp; pungent”).  Originally referred to any forest fruit, especially of the oak.  The sense narrowed to specifically oak fruit later.

USAGE->
Countable Noun:
An acorn fell from the tree. = 一颗橡子从树上掉了下来。

Many acorns lay on the ground. = 许多橡子躺在地上。


PHRASE->
from little acorns grow mighty oaks = 千里之行，始于足下；合抱之木，生于毫末 (idiomatic, meaning great things may come from small beginnings)
a squirrel away acorns = 像松鼠一样储藏橡果 (referring to saving or hoarding something, often for future use)
