fossilization:/ˌfɒsɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌfɑːsələˈzeɪʃən/| n. |化石化，僵化|The process by which organic matter becomes a fossil.  The fossilization of the dinosaur bones took millions of years.|例句：The fossilization of the ancient tree created beautiful petrified wood. 古树的化石化形成了美丽的硅化木。

fossilization:/ˌfɒsɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌfɑːsələˈzeɪʃən/| n. |化石化，僵化|The process of something becoming unchanging or fixed. The fossilization of outdated practices hinders progress. |例句：The fossilization of their thinking prevented them from adapting to the new market.  他们思维的僵化阻碍了他们适应新的市场。|近义词：petrification, hardening, ossification|反义词: decay, decomposition|词性: noun
