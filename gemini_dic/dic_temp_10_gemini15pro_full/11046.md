arch:/ɑːrtʃ/ | n. | 拱, 拱门, 弓形 | a curved structure that supports a bridge, roof, etc., or a curved shape like this | The bridge has several arches. 这座桥有几个拱门。| synonyms: arc, curve | antonyms: straight, line | noun

arch:/ɑːrtʃ/ | adj. | 主要的, 首要的, 调皮的 | chief; principal; mischievous | He's the arch-villain of the piece. 他是这出戏中的主要反派。| synonyms: chief, principal, main, primary; mischievous, playful, roguish | antonyms: minor, secondary; serious, solemn | adjective

arch:/ɑːrtʃ/ | v. | 拱起, 形成拱形 | to form or bend into a curve or arch | The cat arched its back. 猫弓起了背。| synonyms: curve, bend, arc | antonyms: straighten, flatten | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *arche*, from Latin *arcus* ("bow, arch"), from Proto-Italic *arku-, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂erkʷ-  ("to bend, turn"). Cognates include Greek *ἁρkýs (harkýs)* ("net, noose").
