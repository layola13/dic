filoplume:/ˈfɪləˌpluːm/ | n. | 绒羽 | A hairlike feather with a short, soft shaft and few or no barbs, usually concealed beneath contour feathers. It is believed to have a sensory function, helping the bird keep its plumage arranged properly. | The filoplumes of the bird were barely visible beneath its brightly colored contour feathers. 这只鸟的绒羽几乎看不见，隐藏在其鲜艳的廓羽之下。| 近义词: pinfeather (although not exactly the same, refers to a developing feather) | 反义词:  contour feather (provides shape and color), down feather (provides insulation) | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From *filo- "thread" + plume "feather."
