epone:/ˈɛpəʊn/ | n. | 颂诗; 挽歌; 哀歌 | A lyric poem or song lamenting a deceased person. |  The bard composed a moving epone for the fallen hero.  吟游诗人为这位倒下的英雄创作了一首感人的挽歌。 | synonym: elegy, lament, dirge | antonym: paean, ode | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *éppone*, from Greek *epos* (word, song, poem) + *onos* (ass). Originally referred to songs accompanied by the lyre, which was sometimes made with asses' skin.  The meaning shifted over time to focus on songs of lamentation.
