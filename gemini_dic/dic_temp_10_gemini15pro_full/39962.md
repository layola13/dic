construed:/kənˈstruːd/ | v. | 解释，理解，领会 | to interpret or understand the meaning of something, especially in a particular way |  His silence was construed as a sign of agreement. 他的沉默被理解为同意。 | The letter could be construed in two different ways. 这封信可以有两种不同的解释。 | interpret, understand, perceive, take, regard | misinterpret, misunderstand, misperceive | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *construere, from Latin construere (“to construct, build, compile, arrange”), from com- (“together”) + struere (“to pile, build”).
