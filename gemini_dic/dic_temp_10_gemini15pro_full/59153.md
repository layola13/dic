dynastidan:/daɪˈnæstɪdən/ | n. | 王朝成员；王朝动物 | A member of a dynasty; An animal belonging to the Dynastidae family (a type of rhinoceros beetle). |  The dynastidan displayed its impressive horn.  这只王朝动物展示了它令人印象深刻的角。|  The museum showcased artifacts from various dynastidans of the Ming Dynasty.  博物馆展示了明朝各王朝成员的文物。 | 近义词:dynast, member, descendant | 反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly commoner, non-royal) | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Dynasty + -idan (suffix indicating membership or belonging to a group). Dynasty itself comes from Late Latin dynastia, from Greek dynasteia "lordship, power, dominion," from dynastes "lord, ruler, master," from dynasthai "to be able, to be powerful, to prevail."
