nominalist:/ˈnɒmɪnəlɪst/ | n. |唯名论者；名义论者|A person who believes that universals or abstract concepts have no objective reality but exist only as names or labels.|The nominalist argued that "redness" is merely a label we apply to objects, not a real entity itself.|近义词:conceptualist |反义词:realist |词性:noun

nominalistic:/ˌnɒmɪˈnəlˈɪstɪk/ | adj. |唯名论的；名义论的|Relating to or characteristic of nominalism.|His nominalistic approach to philosophy rejected the existence of abstract entities.|近义词:conceptualistic |反义词:realistic |词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English nominal, from Late Latin nominalis (“of or belonging to a name”), from Latin nomen (“name”) + -alis.
