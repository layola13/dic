noblemanly:/ˈnoʊblmənli/| adj.|贵族的；高贵的；慷慨的|Having or showing qualities that are considered typical of a nobleman, such as dignity, generosity, and courage.|He behaved in a noblemanly fashion, offering his coat to the shivering woman.|近义词：aristocratic, noble, dignified, generous, gracious, chivalrous|反义词：ignoble, base, vulgar, mean, selfish, cowardly|词性: adjective

词源: From nobleman + -ly.  "Nobleman" comes from "noble" (from Old French, ultimately from Latin *nobilis "known, famous, high-born") + "man."  The suffix "-ly" forms adverbs and adjectives, here creating an adjective meaning "like or characteristic of a nobleman."
