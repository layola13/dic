middle-of-the-roadism: /ˌmɪdl əv ðə ˈroʊdɪzəm/
n.
中间路线主义；中庸之道
The avoidance of extreme views or policies, especially in politics.  A tendency to hold moderate opinions.
The party's platform reflected a bland middle-of-the-roadism. 该党的纲领体现了平淡无奇的中庸之道。
近义词: centrism, moderation, neutrality
反义词: extremism, radicalism, fanaticism
词性: noun


middle-of-the-road: /ˌmɪdl əv ðə ˈroʊd/
adj.
中间路线的；中庸的；不偏不倚的
Occupying a middle position between extremes, especially in politics.  Moderate; avoiding extremes.
He has a middle-of-the-road approach to politics. 他对政治采取中庸之道。
近义词: moderate, centrist, neutral
反义词: extreme, radical, fanatical
词性: adjective


Etymology:
The term "middle-of-the-road" literally refers to the center of a road, the safest place to be to avoid colliding with traffic from either direction. This physical concept was metaphorically applied to political and social viewpoints, implying a position that avoids extremes. The suffix "-ism" transforms the adjective or phrase into a noun denoting a doctrine, belief system, or practice.
