missing:/ˈmɪsɪŋ/ | adj. | 缺失的，失踪的 | absent; not found; not present | The missing piece of the puzzle was finally found. 丢失的拼图块终于找到了。 | absent, lost, lacking | present, found, existing | adjective


missing:/ˈmɪsɪŋ/ | n. | 缺少的东西；失踪的人 | something or someone that is absent and cannot be found | The police are still searching for the missings. 警察仍在搜寻失踪人员。 | absence, loss | presence, finding | noun


missing:/ˈmɪsɪŋ/ | v. | 错过，想念 | to fail to hit, catch, reach, etc.; to feel sad because someone is not present | I missed the bus this morning. 我今天早上错过了公共汽车。 | fail to hit, miss out on | hit, catch, attend | verb

Etymology: From Middle English *missen, from Old English missan (“to fail to hit, miss, err, go astray”), from Proto-Germanic *missjaną (“to go astray, err”), from Proto-Indo-European *meyth- (“to exchange, change”). Cognate with Middle Low German missen (“to miss, fail, lack”), Middle Dutch missen (“to miss, escape, avoid”), Old High German missen (“to miss, fail, lack”), German missen (“to miss, lack”).
