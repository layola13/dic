cropt:/krɒpt/| adjective (archaic)|收割的，剪短的|A past participle of *crop*, meaning harvested or cut short.  It's no longer commonly used in modern English.|He saw fields of cropt barley. 他看到了收割后的大麦田。| 近义词：harvested, reaped, trimmed, cut|反义词: growing, unharvested, long|adjective (archaic)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cropt*, past participle of *croppen*, from Old English *croppian* (“to cut off, reap”), from Proto-Germanic *krupōną* (“to cut off”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kerp-*  (“to cut, harvest”).  Related to "crop" (n.).
