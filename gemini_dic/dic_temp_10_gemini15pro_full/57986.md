drivel:/ˈdrɪvəl/| n. vi.|胡言乱语，口水；废话|Silly, nonsensical talk or writing; saliva flowing from the mouth.|He was talking such drivel that I stopped listening.|近义词：nonsense, rubbish, gibberish, twaddle|反义词：sense, wisdom|词性:noun, verb

drivel:/ˈdrɪvəl/| vi.|胡说，流口水|To talk nonsense; to let saliva flow from the mouth.|He drivelled on about the good old days.|近义词：babble, ramble, drool|反义词：articulate, enunciate|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *drevelen*, frequentative of *dreven* "to drive, push," from Old Norse *drífa* "to drive." The sense development is from "to dribble, slobber" to "talk nonsense."
