cochlear:/ˈkɒkliə(r)/ (UK), /ˈkɑːkliər/ (US)| adj. n.|耳蜗的；耳蜗|Relating to the cochlea (the spiral cavity of the inner ear containing the organ of Corti, which produces nerve impulses in response to sound vibrations).  As a noun, it refers to something related to the cochlea, particularly a cochlear implant.|例句：Cochlear implants can restore hearing for some deaf people. 耳蜗植入物可以恢复一些聋人的听力。

The cochlear nerve transmits auditory signals to the brain. 耳蜗神经将听觉信号传递到大脑。|近义词：aural, auditory|反义词: N/A|词性: adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cochleāris, from cochlea "snail, spiral, screw," from Greek kokhlias (κοχλίας)  "snail, screw," related to kokhlos (κόχλος) "spiral shell."
