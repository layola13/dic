nonflagrancy: /ˌnɑːnfləˈɡrænsi/
| n. |无罪；不显著；不恶劣的行为 | The absence of a flagrant offense or crime; the state of not being flagrant. | The nonflagrancy of the act led to its dismissal.  这一行为的不恶劣性导致它被驳回。 | 近义词: innocence, blamelessness, unimpeachability | 反义词: flagrancy, guilt, culpability | 词性: 名词


词源:  From non- + flagrancy.  Flagrancy comes from the Latin "flagrans" meaning "burning," referring to something obvious or glaring.  Therefore, nonflagrancy indicates the absence of such obviousness or glaringness.
