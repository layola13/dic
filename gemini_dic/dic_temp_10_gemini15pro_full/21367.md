blot:/blɑːt/ (US), /blɒt/ (UK)| n. v. |污点，墨渍；缺点，污点；吸墨纸|a mark or stain, typically one made by ink, paint, or dirt; a fault or imperfection in character; a piece of blotting paper|He spilled coffee, making a large blot on the tablecloth. 他洒了咖啡，在桌布上留下了一大块污渍。|近义词：stain, spot, blemish, flaw, mark|反义词：cleanliness, perfection|词性：名词，动词

blot:/blɑːt/ (US), /blɒt/ (UK)| v. |吸干；涂污，弄脏；遮蔽，掩盖|dry (ink or a wet surface) by pressing an absorbent material onto it; make a blot or blots on; obscure or cover|She blotted the letter with blotting paper. 她用吸墨纸吸干了信上的墨水。|近义词：dry, soak up, absorb, smudge, stain|反义词：wet, soak|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *blotten*, from Old Norse *blot*, meaning "sacrifice" and also "stain, blemish." The sense of "stain" likely developed from the idea of sacrificial blood staining an altar.
