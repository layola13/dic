incisiveness:/ɪnˈsaɪsɪvnəs/| n.|深刻；敏锐；尖锐|The incisiveness of her analysis impressed everyone. 她的分析的深刻性给每个人都留下了深刻的印象。|Etymology: From incisive + -ness.  Incisive comes from Late Latin incisivus "cutting into," from Latin incis-, past participle stem of incidere "to cut into," from in- "into" + caedere "to cut."|Synonyms: acuity, sharpness, penetration, perspicacity|Antonyms: dullness, obtuseness, vagueness|noun

incisive:/ɪnˈsaɪsɪv/| adj.|深刻的；敏锐的；尖锐的|He made an incisive comment on the current political situation. 他对当前的政治局势作出了深刻的评论。|Etymology: From Late Latin incisivus "cutting into," from Latin incis-, past participle stem of incidere "to cut into," from in- "into" + caedere "to cut."|Synonyms: penetrating, acute, sharp, keen, insightful|Antonyms: dull, vague, obtuse, superficial|adjective
