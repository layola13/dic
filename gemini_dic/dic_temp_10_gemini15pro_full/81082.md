grallatores:/ɡræləˈtɔːriːz/
|n.|涉禽类|An order of birds characterized by long legs, adapted for wading.|例句：Grallatores are commonly found in wetland habitats. 涉禽类常见于湿地栖息地。|例句：The heron is a classic example of a grallator. 苍鹭是涉禽类的典型例子。|近义词：wading birds, shorebirds|反义词:|名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *grallator*, from *gralla* ("stilts") + *-tor* (agent suffix).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ghrendh-* ("to grind, crush").  The connection likely refers to the birds' long, thin legs, resembling stilts.
