overwildnesses:/ˌoʊvərˈwaɪldnɪsɪz/
n.
过度荒凉，极度野蛮的状态
The state of being excessively or extremely wild; an abundance of wilderness.
The explorer was struck by the sheer overwildnesses of the uncharted territory. 探险家被这片未知领域的极度荒凉所震撼。
近义词：wildness, savagery, untamedness
反义词：tameness, cultivation, domestication
词性：名词

Etymology:
"over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *ubar) + "wildness" (from "wild" (Old English wilde) + "-ness" (Old English -nes(s), a suffix forming nouns from adjectives)).  The prefix "over-" intensifies the meaning of "wildness," indicating an excessive or extreme degree.
