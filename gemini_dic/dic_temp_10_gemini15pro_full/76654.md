galumph:/ɡəˈlʌmf/ | vi. | 欢快地笨拙行走；得意洋洋地走 | To move in a clumsy, heavy-footed way, often with a suggestion of boisterousness or triumph. | He galumphed across the field, delighted with his victory. 他得意洋洋地穿过田野，为自己的胜利感到高兴。 | The children galumphed through the puddles, splashing water everywhere. 孩子们笨拙地穿过水坑，把水溅得到处都是。 | 近义词：lumber, stomp, clump, plod | 反义词：tiptoe, glide, creep | 词性：动词

词源:
Coined by Lewis Carroll in "Through the Looking-Glass" (1871), possibly a blend of "gallop" and "triumph."  由路易斯·卡罗尔在《爱丽丝镜中奇遇记》（1871 年）中创造，可能是“gallop（疾驰）”和“triumph（胜利）”的混合词。
