arquebusier:/ˌɑːrkəbəˈsɪər/| n.|火绳枪兵，钩铳兵|A soldier armed with an arquebus (an early type of firearm).|The arquebusiers formed a line in front of the pikemen. 火绳枪兵在长矛兵前面排成一列。|The arquebusier took aim and fired. 那位火绳枪兵瞄准开火。|Synonyms: musketeer, rifleman, gunner|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, consider perhaps: civilian, non-combatant)|noun

Etymology:
From French *arquebusier*, from *arquebuse* (arquebus) + *-ier* (suffix denoting a person who uses or makes something).  The word *arquebus* itself comes from Dutch *haakbus*, literally "hook gun," referring to a hook used to steady the weapon.
