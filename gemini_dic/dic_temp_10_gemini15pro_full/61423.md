emboldens:/ɪmˈboʊldənz/| vt.|使大胆，使有勇气|to make someone feel braver or more confident|Her success emboldened her to try even greater things. 她的成功使她更有勇气尝试更大的事情。|The coach's pep talk emboldened the team to play harder. 教练的激励讲话鼓舞了球队更加努力地比赛。|近义词：encourage, hearten, inspire|反义词:discourage, dishearten, intimidate|词性:动词
ETYMOLOGY->
From en- + bold + -en.  "En-" is a prefix meaning "to cause to be," "to put into," or "to intensify." "Bold" comes from the Old English "beald," meaning "brave" or "confident." The suffix "-en" turns an adjective into a verb, meaning "to make or become."
