deadlock:/ˈdedlɒk/ (UK) /ˈdɛdlɑːk/ (US)| n. v.|僵局，死锁|A situation, typically one involving opposing parties, in which no progress can be made.|Negotiations have reached a deadlock.  谈判陷入了僵局。|synonyms: standstill, impasse, stalemate|antonyms: agreement, breakthrough, solution|noun
deadlock:/ˈdedlɒk/ (UK) /ˈdɛdlɑːk/ (US)| v.|使陷入僵局，停顿|Cause (a situation or process) to become deadlocked.|The talks were deadlocked by disagreements over finance.  会谈因财务方面的分歧而陷入僵局。|synonyms: stall, block, obstruct|antonyms: expedite, facilitate, resolve|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From dead + lock.  "Dead" in this context means "immovable" or "fixed," and "lock" refers to a state of being fastened or blocked.
