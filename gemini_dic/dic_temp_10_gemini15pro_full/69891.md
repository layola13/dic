fertilised: /ˈfɜːtɪlaɪzd/ (British) /ˈfɜːrtəlaɪzd/ (American)| adj.|已受精的，已施肥的|Made fertile; having had fertilizer applied or having been pollinated.|例句：The fertilised egg will develop into a fetus. 受精卵将发育成胎儿。|例句：The farmer spread fertilised manure on his fields. 农民在他的田里撒了肥料。|近义词：impregnated, enriched, manured|反义词：unfertilised, barren|词性:形容词

fertilise: /ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz/ (British) /ˈfɜːrtəlaɪz/ (American)| vt.|使受精，施肥|To make fertile; to cause (an egg, female animal, or plant) to develop a new individual by introducing male reproductive material; to add fertilizer to.|例句：Bees fertilise flowers by carrying pollen from one to another. 蜜蜂通过携带花粉从一朵花到另一朵花来授粉。|例句：He fertilised the lawn with chemicals. 他用化肥给草坪施肥。|近义词：impregnate, enrich, manure|反义词：sterilize|词性:动词

词源:  From Late Middle English *fertilisen, from Anglo-Norman fertilizer, fertiliser, and Old French fertiliser, from Medieval Latin fertilisāre, from Latin fertilis (“fertile”) + -āre (suffix forming verbs).
