inkiest:/ˈɪŋkiɪst/| adj.|最墨黑的，最黑暗的|superlative adjective of inky; covered with or as if with ink; very dark|The inkiest black I've ever seen. 我见过最墨的黑。|The night was inkiest black, without a single star. 夜晚漆黑一片，没有一颗星星。|近义词：darkest, blackest, pitch-black|反义词:lightest, brightest|词性:形容词

词源:
From ink + -y + -est.  "Ink" comes from Old French "enque," from Late Latin "encaustum," meaning "burnt in," referring to a purple-red ink.  "-y" is a suffix forming adjectives from nouns, meaning "characterized by" or "full of."  "-est" is a suffix forming the superlative degree of adjectives and adverbs.
