intempestively:/ˌɪntɛmˈpɛstɪvli/
adverb
不合时宜地；鲁莽地
Occurring at an inappropriate or inopportune time; untimely. Done in a rash or impulsive way.
He chose an intempestively moment to announce his resignation, right before the big presentation. 他选择在一个极不合时宜的时刻宣布辞职，就在重要的演讲之前。
The driver intempestively honked his horn in the middle of the night, waking up the entire neighborhood. 司机在半夜里不合时宜地按喇叭，吵醒了整个社区。
近义词:untimely, inappropriately, rashly, impulsively
反义词:appropriately, timely, cautiously, prudently
词性:adverb

词源:
From Late Latin *intempestivus, from Latin in- "not" + tempestivus "timely, seasonable" (from tempestas "time, season, weather").
