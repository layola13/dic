grooving:/ˈɡruːvɪŋ/
n.|开槽，切槽;享受|The act of creating grooves or the state of enjoying something intensely.|例句：The grooving on the record was impressive. 唱片上的凹槽令人印象深刻。|近义词：enjoying, digging, vibing|反义词：disliking, hating
v.|开槽，切槽;享受;沉浸在…的节奏中|Present participle of "groove".  To enjoy something, often music, intensely; to be immersed in the rhythm.|例句：I was really grooving to the music at the concert. 我在音乐会上真的很享受音乐。|近义词：enjoying, digging, vibing|反义词：disliking, hating
adj.|绝妙的；令人愉快的|Excellent, enjoyable, especially in relation to music or a rhythmic activity.|例句：The band played a grooving set. 乐队演奏了一套绝妙的曲目。|近义词：fantastic, excellent, awesome|反义词：terrible, awful
