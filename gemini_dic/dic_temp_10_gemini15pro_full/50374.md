deport:/dɪˈpɔːrt/ /diːˈpɔːrt/
verb
驱逐出境
to force (a person who is not a citizen) to leave a country
The government deported the illegal immigrants. 政府驱逐了非法移民。

deport:/dɪˈpɔːrt/ /diːˈpɔːrt/
verb
放逐；流放（尤指古代）
(especially in ancient times) to send (someone) away from a place as a punishment
He was deported to a remote island. 他被流放到一个偏远的岛屿。


SYNONYM->
expel, banish, exile

ANTONYM->
admit, allow, receive

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French deporter "to carry away, transport," from Latin deportare "to carry away," from de- "away" + portare "to carry."
