bullheadedly:/ˌbʊlˈhɛdɪdli/
| adv. |固执地，顽固地|In a stubborn and determined manner, refusing to change one's mind or consider other opinions; obstinately.  Resembling or characteristic of a bull's head in being unyielding or determined.|He bullheadedly refused to listen to any advice. 他固执地拒绝听取任何建议。|Synonyms: stubbornly, obstinately, persistently, doggedly, inflexibly|Antonyms: flexibly, open-mindedly, reasonably, cooperatively|adverb

bullheaded:/ˈbʊlˌhɛdɪd/
| adj. |固执的，顽固的|Stubborn and determined, unwilling to change one's mind or consider other opinions.|He's so bullheaded, he won't listen to reason. 他太固执了，不肯听道理。|Synonyms: stubborn, obstinate, headstrong, pigheaded, willful|Antonyms: flexible, open-minded, reasonable, amenable|adjective

bullhead:/ˈbʊlˌhɛd/
| n. |牛头；傻瓜；鲶鱼|The head of a bull; a stupid person; a type of catfish.|He acted like a bullhead and ignored all the warnings. 他像个傻瓜一样无视所有警告。|Synonyms: (for stupid person) fool, idiot, dolt|Antonyms: (for stupid person) genius, sage, intellectual|noun
