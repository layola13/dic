hobbes:/hɒbz/ (UK) /hɑːbz/ (US)| n.|霍布斯; 哲学家托马斯·霍布斯|Thomas Hobbes (1588-1679), English philosopher, known for his political philosophy, especially as articulated in his masterpiece Leviathan (1651). His theory of social contract influenced Western political thought. |例句：Hobbes' Leviathan is a cornerstone of Western political philosophy. 霍布斯的《利维坦》是西方政治哲学的基石。

例句：Hobbes argued for a strong sovereign power. 霍布斯主张强有力的君主权力。|近义词：philosopher, theorist|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


hobbesian:/hɒbziən/ (UK) /hɑːbziən/ (US)| adj.|霍布斯主义的;  托马斯·霍布斯哲学思想的|Relating to or characteristic of the philosophy of Thomas Hobbes.|例句：Hobbesian philosophy emphasizes the importance of a social contract. 霍布斯哲学强调社会契约的重要性。

例句：A Hobbesian worldview often sees human nature as inherently selfish. 霍布斯主义的世界观通常将人性视为天生自私的。|近义词：authoritarian, realistic|反义词:anarchic, utopian|词性:adjective

词源:Named after Thomas Hobbes (1588-1679). The surname "Hobbes" is a patronymic form of the given name "Robert."
