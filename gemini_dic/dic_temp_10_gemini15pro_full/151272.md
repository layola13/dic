paresis: /pəˈriːsɪs/ /pəˈrisɪs/ | n. | 部分麻痹;轻瘫 |  A condition of muscular weakness caused by nerve damage or disease; partial paralysis. |  The stroke resulted in paresis of his left arm. 中风导致他左臂部分麻痹。|  weakness, palsy | paralysis, full strength | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek πάρεσις (páresis, “slackening, letting go, paralysis”), from παρίημι (paríēmi, “I let go, relax, remit”), from παρά (pará, “beside, alongside”) + ἵημι (híēmi, “I send, throw”).
