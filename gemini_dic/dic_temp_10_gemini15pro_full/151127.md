parasymbiosis:/ˌpærəˌsɪmbaɪˈoʊsɪs/ /ˌpærəˌsɪmbaɪˈəʊsɪs/
n.
偏利共生
A relationship between two organisms where one benefits and the other is neither harmed nor benefited.
One species benefits from the association, the other is apparently unaffected.  Parasymbiosis is exemplified by the relationship between a whale and barnacles.  The barnacles benefit by gaining access to food as the whale swims through nutrient-rich waters.  The whale is neither helped nor harmed by the presence of the barnacles.
鲸鱼和藤壶之间的关系是偏利共生的一个例子。藤壶通过鲸鱼在营养丰富的水域中游动而获得食物，从而受益。鲸鱼既没有因为藤壶的存在而受益，也没有因为藤壶的存在而受害。

parasitism, mutualism
