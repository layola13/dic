nonmystically:/ˌnɑːnˈmɪstɪkli/ | adv. | 非神秘地，不神秘地 | In a way that is not mysterious or supernatural; plainly, simply. | He explained the phenomenon nonmystically, focusing on scientific evidence. 他用非神秘的方式解释了这种现象，重点放在科学证据上。 | He presented the facts nonmystically, without any hint of esoteric knowledge. 他平实地陈述了事实，没有任何深奥知识的暗示。 | 近义词: plainly, simply, clearly, rationally | 反义词: mystically, esoterically, supernaturally | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
non- (prefix) + mystical + -ly (suffix)

*   "non-" is a prefix of Latin origin meaning "not," "opposite of," or "lack of."
*   "mystical" derives from the Greek word "mystikos," relating to mysteries or secrets, often religious or spiritual.
*   "-ly" is a suffix that forms adverbs.
