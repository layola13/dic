drivel:/ˈdrɪvəl/| n. vi.|胡说，废话；口水，流口水|Silly, nonsensical talk or writing; saliva running from the mouth.|He was talking such drivel that I just tuned him out. 他一直在胡说八道，所以我干脆不理他了。|Synonyms: nonsense, rubbish, twaddle, babble|Antonyms: sense, wisdom|noun
drivel:/ˈdrɪvəl/| vi.|胡说八道；流口水|To talk nonsense; to let saliva run from the mouth.|He driveled on and on about nothing important. 他不停地胡扯些无关紧要的事情。|Synonyms: babble, ramble|Antonyms: articulate, enunciate|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *drevelen*, frequentative of *dreven*, from Old Norse *drafa* (“to dribble, to slaver”). Related to Old English *dreflian* (“to dribble”).
