capriccioso:/kəˌprɪtʃiˈoʊsoʊ/ /kəˌprɪtʃiˈəʊsəʊ/
| adj. |反复无常的，任性的；(音乐)自由奔放的| Characterized by or subject to whim; impulsive and unpredictable; (Music) performed in a free and impulsive style. |The capriccioso child refused to eat his vegetables.  这个任性的孩子拒绝吃蔬菜。|Synonyms: whimsical, fickle, erratic, impulsive, temperamental, fanciful, mercurial|Antonyms: steady, consistent, stable, predictable, reliable|adjective

capriccioso:/kəˌprɪtʃiˈoʊsoʊ/ /kəˌprɪtʃiˈəʊsəʊ/
| adv. |反复无常地，任性地；(音乐)自由奔放地| In a capricious or whimsical manner; (Music) in a free and impulsive style. |The piece was played capriccioso, full of unexpected flourishes.  这首曲子演奏得自由奔放，充满了意想不到的华丽装饰。|Synonyms: whimsically, erratically, impulsively|Antonyms: steadily, consistently, predictably|adverb

capriccioso:/kəˌprɪtʃiˈoʊsoʊ/ /kəˌprɪtʃiˈəʊsəʊ/
| n. |(音乐)随想曲，狂想曲| A capricious or whimsical piece of music, often in a free and impulsive style. |The composer's capriccioso was full of unexpected changes in tempo and mood.  这位作曲家的狂想曲充满了节奏和情绪的意外变化。|Synonyms: caprice, fantasy|Antonyms: - |noun
