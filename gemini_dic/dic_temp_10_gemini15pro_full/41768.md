corrupting:/kəˈrʌptɪŋ/
verb
腐败; 堕落; 贿赂; 使腐败变质;
causing someone or something to become dishonest or immoral.
The corrupting influence of power. 权力的腐蚀作用。
Synonyms:debauching, perverting, depraving, demoralizing
Antonyms: purifying, cleansing, improving, edifying
词性: 动词(Present Participle)

corrupt:/kəˈrʌpt/
adjective
腐败的; 堕落的; 贪污的; 被破坏的;
dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power, typically involving bribery.
Corrupt officials. 腐败的官员。
Synonyms: dishonest, crooked, fraudulent, unscrupulous
Antonyms: honest, upright, moral, ethical
词性: 形容词

corrupt:/kəˈrʌpt/
verb
腐蚀; 使堕落; 贿赂;
cause to act dishonestly in return for money or personal gain.
He was corrupted by the lure of easy money. 他被轻松赚钱的诱惑腐蚀了。
Synonyms: bribe, buy off, suborn, grease someone's palm
Antonyms: purify, cleanse, reform, rehabilitate
词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *corrumpere*  "to break up completely," from *com-* "completely" + *rumpere* "to break".
