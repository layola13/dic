acclimatation: /əˌklaɪməˈteɪʃn/ | n. | 适应，驯化，服水土 | The process of becoming accustomed to a new climate or environment. |  It took him a few weeks for acclimatation to the high altitude. 他花了几周时间才适应了高海拔。

The climbers spent a few days at base camp for acclimatation before attempting the summit. 登山者在尝试登顶之前在基地营呆了几天以适应环境。 | 近义词：adaptation, adjustment, habituation | 反义词：maladaptation | 词性：名词

acclimatization: /əˌklaɪməˌtɪˈzeɪʃn/ | n. | 适应，驯化，服水土 |  The process of becoming accustomed to a new climate or environment. |  The acclimatization period for new employees is usually one month. 新员工的适应期通常是一个月。

She experienced some difficulties with acclimatization when she moved to the tropics.  她移居热带地区时，在适应环境方面遇到了一些困难。 | 近义词：adaptation, adjustment, habituation | 反义词：maladaptation | 词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French acclimatation, from acclimater (“to acclimatize”), from à (“to”) + climat (“climate”), from Latin clīma (“region, slope”), from Ancient Greek κλίμα (“inclination, region, zone”), from κλίνειν (“to incline, slope”).
