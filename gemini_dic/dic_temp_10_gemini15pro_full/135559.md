nonpolemically:/ˌnɒnpəˈlɛmɪkli/ | adv. | 非辩论性地，不带争议地 |  In a way that avoids argument or controversy.  It describes communication that is presented in a calm, objective, and factual manner, without the intention of starting a debate or argument. | He discussed the sensitive topic nonpolemically, focusing on facts and figures. 他心平气和地讨论了这个敏感话题，重点关注事实和数据。|  The author presented her findings nonpolemically, letting the data speak for itself. 作者心平气和地陈述了她的研究结果，让数据说话。 | objectively, neutrally, dispassionately | polemically, contentiously, argumentatively | adverb

Etymology:
"non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "polemic" (from Greek "polemikos" meaning "warlike," relating to controversy or argument) + "-ally" (adverbial suffix)
