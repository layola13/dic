call-in:/ˈkɔːlɪn/ (US), /ˈkɔːlɪn/ (UK)| adj. n.| 打电话进来的，来电直播节目|A call-in show is a radio or television programme in which members of the public telephone the studio to express their opinions or ask questions.|The radio station hosted a call-in show about the election.|近义词：phone-in|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps pre-recorded)|词性: adjective, noun


call-in:/ˈkɔːlɪn/ (US), /ˈkɔːlɪn/ (UK)| n.| 打电话进来，来电直播节目|An instance of calling in to a radio or television programme.|There were hundreds of call-ins during the show.|近义词：phone-in|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性: noun


Etymology:  "call" (from Old Norse *kalla) + "in" (from Old English in).  The compound "call-in" emerged in the 20th century with the rise of broadcast media.
