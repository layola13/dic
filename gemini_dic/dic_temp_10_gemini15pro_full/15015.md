babiism: /ˈbɑːbiɪzəm/ | n. | 巴比教 | A religious movement founded in Persia in 1844 by Mirza Ali Muhammad, who claimed to be the Bab ("Gate"), a divinely inspired intermediary between humans and the hidden Imam.  It emphasized spiritual and social reform and anticipated the coming of a greater prophet. | The Babi uprising was brutally suppressed. 巴比教起义被残酷镇压。 |  Baha'i Faith,  Shaykhism |  Islam, Orthodox Shia Islam | noun
