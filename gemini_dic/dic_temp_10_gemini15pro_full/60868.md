elegiacs: /ɪˈliːdʒɪæks/ | n. | 挽歌; 哀歌; 挽诗; 哀诗 |  A poem or song of mourning, especially one written in elegiac couplets.  |  The poet composed elegiacs for his deceased friend.  这位诗人为他去世的朋友创作了挽歌。 |  Synonyms: lament, dirge, requiem | Antonyms: celebration, paean | noun


elegiac: /ɪˈliːdʒɪæk/ | adj. | 哀伤的; 挽歌体的; 哀悼的 |  Relating to or characteristic of an elegy; mournful.  | Her elegiac tone conveyed the depth of her sorrow. 她悲伤的语气表达了她深深的悲痛。 | Synonyms: mournful, plaintive, melancholic | Antonyms: joyful, celebratory | adjective

Etymology: From Late Latin *elegiacus*, from Greek *elegeiakós*, from *élegos* "elegy".
