calcine:/ˈkælsaɪn/ /kælˈsaɪn/ |v.  n.|煅烧，烧制;  煅烧后留下的物质|To heat a substance to a high temperature but below the melting or fusing point, causing loss of moisture, or volatile matter, or oxidation, or reduction.  |例句：The bones were calcined to produce bone ash.  这些骨头被煅烧以产生骨灰。

|近义词：burn, roast, bake, fire|反义词: dissolve, melt|词性: 动词, 名词


calcine:/ˈkælsaɪn/ /kælˈsaɪn/ |n.|煅烧后留下的物质|A product of calcination. |例句：The calcine from the process is used in cement production. 这个过程产生的煅烧物用于水泥生产。|近义词：residue, ash, clinker|反义词: raw material|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *calcinare, from Latin calx (“lime, limestone”) + -inō.
