behaviourism:/bɪˈheɪvjərɪzəm/ /biˈheɪvjəˌrɪzəm/
n.|行为主义|A school of psychology that takes the objective evidence of behavior (such as bodily movements and speech) as the only concern of its study and thus ignores the mental processes involved in behavior.| Pavlov's experiments with dogs led to the development of behaviorism. 巴甫洛夫对狗的实验导致了行为主义的发展。|behavior modification, conditioning|cognitivism, constructivism|noun
