amongst /əˈmʌŋst/ | prep. |在...之中，在...之间|Being situated in the middle of a group of things or people.|She felt comfortable amongst her friends. 她和朋友们在一起感到很自在。|近义词: among, amid, between, amidst |反义词: outside, beyond |词性:介词

Etymology:
From Middle English amongest, amonge, from Old English amang, onmang ("among, during, at the same time"), from a- + gemang ("mixture, crowd, company"). Cognate with Old Frisian amang, among ("among"), Old Saxon imang ("among"), Middle Dutch ommeganc ("circle, circuit"), Old High German inmanc ("among"), Gothic ᐃ𐌽 + 𐌼𐌰𐌽𐌲𐍃 (in + mangs, "among the crowd").
