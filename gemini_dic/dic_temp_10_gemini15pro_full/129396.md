nominal:/ˈnɒmɪnl/ (UK) /ˈnɑːmɪnl/ (US)| adj. n.|名义上的，象征性的；名词性的；名词|Of a value or amount, very small; far below the real value or cost; in name or thought but not in fact or not as things really are; relating to nouns|

例句：He paid a nominal fee for the service.  他为这项服务支付了象征性的费用。

例句：She's the nominal head of the company, but her son makes all the decisions. 她名义上是公司的主管，但她儿子才是做所有决定的人。


近义词：symbolic, token, titular, trifling, insignificant|
反义词：real, actual, substantial|
词性：adjective, noun|


nominal:/ˈnɒmɪnl/ (UK) /ˈnɑːmɪnl/ (US)| n.|名词词组|A word or group of words that functions as a noun|

例句：In the sentence "Swimming is fun," "swimming" is the nominal.  在句子“游泳很有趣”中，“游泳”是名词词组。

例句：The nominal "the big red ball" functions as the subject of the sentence. 名词词组“那个又大又红的球”在句子中作主语。


近义词：noun phrase|
反义词：-|
词性：noun|



ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English nominal, from Old French nominal, from Latin nōminālis (“of or belonging to a name”), from nōmin-, stem of nōmen (“name”).
