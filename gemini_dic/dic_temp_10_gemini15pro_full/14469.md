avalanche:/ˈævəlæntʃ/| n.|雪崩；大量；涌流|A large mass of snow, ice, and rocks falling rapidly down a mountainside; a sudden arrival or occurrence of something in overwhelming quantities.|The skiers were caught in an avalanche. 滑雪者遭遇了雪崩。|Synonyms: landslide, snowslide, flood, deluge, torrent|Antonyms: trickle, dribble|noun


avalanches:/ˈævəlæntʃɪz/| n.|雪崩（avalanche的复数形式）|Plural form of avalanche, referring to multiple instances of large masses of snow, ice, and rocks falling rapidly down a mountainside, or multiple sudden arrivals or occurrences of something in overwhelming quantities.|Several avalanches blocked the mountain pass. 几次雪崩阻塞了山口。|Synonyms: landslides, snowslides, floods, deluges, torrents|Antonyms: trickles, dribbles|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *avalanche*, from Franco-Provençal *avalance*, from *avaler* "to descend," from *a val* "downhill," from Latin *ad vallem* "to the valley."
