fluvialist: /ˈfluːviəlɪst/ | n. | 河流學家；河流作用論者 | A specialist in the study of rivers and their action in shaping the land.  A person who believes that the present appearance of the Earth's surface resulted primarily from the action of rivers. | The fluvialist argued that the canyon was carved by the river over millions of years.  河流學家認為，這個峽谷是河流經過數百萬年沖刷形成的。 |  geomorphologist, hydrologist |  | noun
Etymology->
From Latin *fluviālis* (“of or belonging to a river”), from *fluvius* (“river”) + *-ālis* (suffix forming adjectives).
