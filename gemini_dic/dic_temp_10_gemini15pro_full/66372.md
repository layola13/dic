exclusive:/ɪkˈskluːsɪv/| adj. n.|独有的，排外的；独家新闻|Something that is exclusive is only for a limited number of people, often because it is expensive or of high quality.  An exclusive is a piece of news that is published by only one newspaper, magazine, television station, etc.|This club is very exclusive and only accepts members who are very rich.  这个俱乐部非常高级，只接受非常富有的会员。|The newspaper ran an exclusive on the corruption scandal.  这家报纸刊登了关于腐败丑闻的独家新闻。|近义词：sole, private, restricted, select|反义词:inclusive, general, public|词性:形容词, 名词

exclusive:/ɪkˈskluːsɪv/| n.|独家新闻；独家经营的项目；排外者|A piece of news that is published by only one newspaper, magazine, television station, etc.  A product or service that is only available from a particular company.|The reporter got an exclusive on the mayor's resignation. 记者拿到了市长辞职的独家新闻。|They have the exclusive on selling those products in this region. 他们在该地区拥有销售这些产品的独家经营权。|近义词：scoop|反义词: N/A|词性:名词
