onboard:/ˈɒnbɔːd/ (UK) /ˈɑːnbɔːrd/ (US)| adj. adv.|在船上，在飞机上，在火车上；加入，融入|Being or taking place on board a vehicle, such as a ship, aircraft, or train; integrated or included as part of something.|The passengers were welcomed onboard the plane. 乘客们受到飞机上的欢迎。|近义词：aboard, on, in|反义词：offboard, ashore, disembarked|词性:形容词/副词

onboard:/ˈɒnbɔːd/ (UK) /ˈɑːnbɔːrd/ (US)| vt.|使上船（或飞机、火车等）；让…加入|To take or bring onto a vehicle, such as a ship, aircraft, or train; to integrate or include someone as part of something.|The airline onboarded the passengers quickly. 航空公司迅速让乘客登机。|近义词：embark, enroll, integrate|反义词：disembark, offload|词性:动词

onboard:/ˈɒnbɔːrd/ (UK) /ˈɑːnbɔːrd/ (US)| n.| 飞机、船舶等内部的设备|The equipment or features inside a vehicle, such as a ship or aircraft.|The onboard entertainment system was excellent.  机载娱乐系统非常棒。|近义词：facilities, equipment|反义词：N/A|词性:名词


USAGE->

词源： "on" + "board," from Middle English "bord," meaning "plank, side of a ship."  “on” + “board”，源自中古英语“bord”，意思是“木板，船侧”。
