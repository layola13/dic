bulwark:/ˈbʊlwərk/ | n. | 壁垒，防御工事，保障 | A defensive wall or embankment.  A person, institution, or principle that acts as a defense. | The castle walls formed a sturdy bulwark against invaders. 城堡的城墙构成了抵御入侵者的坚固壁垒。  | Synonyms: rampart, fortification, bastion, safeguard, defense, protection | Antonyms: weakness, vulnerability | noun


bulwark:/ˈbʊlwərk/ | v. | 保护，防御 | To fortify or defend something. |  The new law bulwarks consumers against fraud. 新法律保护消费者免受欺诈。 | Synonyms: protect, defend, shield, safeguard | Antonyms: expose, endanger | verb

PHRASE->
bulwark against: 防御...的壁垒  (e.g., a bulwark against inflation 通货膨胀的壁垒)
bulwark of: ...的保障 (e.g., a bulwark of democracy 民主的保障)
as a bulwark: 作为保障 (e.g., serving as a bulwark against tyranny 作为抵御暴政的保障)

bulwarked by: 被...保护 (e.g., bulwarked by strong allies 被强大的盟友保护)


词源：From Middle Dutch *bolwerc, from bol "plank, tree trunk" + werc "work."  Originally referred to the curved timbers of a ship's hull above the deck.  Later extended to land fortifications and then to any strong defense.
