colliquation:/ˌkɒlɪˈkweɪʃən/ | n. | 熔化，液化；（身体组织的）溶解,崩溃 |The process of becoming liquid; abnormal discharge of fluid from the body. | The extreme heat caused the colliquation of the metal.  极度高温导致金属熔化。|The doctor was concerned about the colliquation of the patient's tissues. 医生担心病人组织的溶解。|  synonyms: melting, liquefaction, dissolution, disintegration | antonyms: solidification, freezing, coagulation | noun

colliquative:/kəˈlɪkwətɪv/ | adj. | 熔化的，液化的 | Relating to or involving colliquation. | The colliquative properties of the substance were studied. 对该物质的熔化特性进行了研究。| The patient's colliquative state was worsening. 病人的液化状态正在恶化。| synonyms: melting, liquefying | antonyms: solidifying, freezing | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin colliquātiōn- (stem of colliquātiō ), from Latin colliquāre ‘melt, dissolve’, from com- (expressing intensification) + liquāre ‘make liquid’, from liquor, liquōr- ‘liquid’.
