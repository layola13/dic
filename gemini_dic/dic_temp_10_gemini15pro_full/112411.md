loathed:/loʊðd/ | adj. | 讨厌的，令人憎恶的 (past participle of loathe used as an adjective) | Feeling intense dislike or disgust for something or someone.  | The loathed dictator was finally overthrown. 那位令人憎恶的独裁者最终被推翻了。| 近义词: detested, abhorred, despised | 反义词: loved, adored | 词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *lothen*, from Old English *lāthian* (“to be hateful, loathsome, to loathe”), from Proto-Germanic *laþōną* (“to invite, summon; to be hostile”), from Proto-Indo-European *lēdʰ- (“to be hostile, hateful”). Cognate with Old Saxon *lāthian*, Old High German *ladōn*, Old Norse *laða* (“to invite, summon”), Gothic *𐌻𐌰𐌸𐍉𐌽* (*laþōn*, “to invite”).
