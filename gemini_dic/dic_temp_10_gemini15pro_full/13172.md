at-bar:/æt ˈbɑːr/| adj.|在法庭上；出庭受审；受审的；在吧台|Present in court; on trial.|例句：The defendant was at-bar, awaiting the judge's decision. 被告在法庭上，等待法官的判决。|例句：The lawyer addressed the at-bar witness. 律师向出庭作证的证人提问。|近义词：on trial, in court|反义词：released, acquitted|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Anglo-French *barre, from Late Latin *barra "bar".  Originally referred to the railing that separates the area where the judge sits from the rest of the courtroom.  Someone "at bar" was literally standing at that railing to plead their case.
