burh: /bʊrˠx/ (Early West Saxon), /bʌrə/ (Modern pronunciation)| n.| 城堡，设防城镇 |A fortified town, especially in Anglo-Saxon England.  It originated from the Old English word "burh," meaning "fortress" or "stronghold."|The burh offered protection from Viking raids.|Synonyms: fortress, stronghold, citadel, fortified town|Antonyms: village, hamlet|Noun
