hoazin:/ˈhoʊɑːtsɪn/ (US), /ˈhɔːɑːtsɪn/ (UK)| n.|麝雉|A South American bird (Opisthocomus hoazin) with a large crest, known for its noisy calls and the musky odor of its flesh, caused by the bacteria in its crop that help it digest its leafy diet.  It is also notable for the claws on the wings of its chicks, a primitive feature.|The hoatzin is a unique bird found in the Amazon basin.  麝雉是一种在亚马逊河流域发现的独特鸟类。

The hoatzin chicks use their claws to climb trees. 麝雉雏鸟用它们的爪子爬树。|近义词：stinkbird (informal), Canje pheasant |反义词: N/A|词性:noun
