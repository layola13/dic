antennas:/ænˈtenəz/
n.
天线；触须
A metallic device for radiating or receiving radio waves. Also, either of a pair of slender, movable sensory organs on the head of an insect, crustacean, or other invertebrate.
The car radio had a broken antenna. 汽车收音机的天线坏了。
The butterfly fluttered its antennas. 蝴蝶拍打着它的触须。
synonyms: aerial, receiver, sensor, feeler
antonym: (No direct antonym for a physical object.  Could consider "ground" in the context of radio transmission, or perhaps "insensitive" in the metaphorical sense)
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian antenna “sail yard,” from Latin antenna "sail yard," related to antemna, probably from ante "before" + *mena "projection."  The sense of "feeler" (in insects) is first recorded in English in 1835.  The radio sense emerged in the late 19th century.
