monographing:/ˈmɒnəˌɡrɑːfɪŋ/
|n.| 专题论文写作; 专题论述 |
|Eng.| The act of writing or producing a monograph, a detailed written study of a single specialized subject or an aspect of it. |
|例句：He is currently monographing the history of the early Roman Empire. 他目前正在撰写早期罗马帝国的历史专著。|
|例句：Monographing such a complex topic requires extensive research.  撰写如此复杂的专题需要大量的研究。|
|近义词：writing, composing, authoring, researching, studying|
|反义词：skimming, summarizing, abstracting|
|词性：noun (gerund)|

monographs:/ˈmɒnəˌɡrɑːfs/
|n.| 专题论文; 专著|
|Eng.| A detailed written study of a single specialized subject or an aspect of it. |
|例句：She has published several monographs on medieval literature. 她已经出版了几本关于中世纪文学的专著。|
|例句：The library holds a vast collection of monographs on various subjects.  图书馆收藏了大量关于各种主题的专著。|
|近义词：treatise, dissertation, study, paper, essay|
|反义词：pamphlet, leaflet, overview|
|词性：noun|


monographer:/məˈnɒɡrəfər/
|n.| 专题论文作者; 专著作家|
|Eng.| A person who writes a monograph. |
|例句：He is a renowned monographer in the field of astrophysics. 他是天体物理学领域著名的专著作家。|
|例句：The conference attracted monographers from all over the world.  这次会议吸引了来自世界各地的专著作者。|
|近义词：writer, author, researcher, scholar, specialist|
|反义词：amateur, generalist|
|词性：noun|


词源:
From mono- (one) + -graph (writing)
