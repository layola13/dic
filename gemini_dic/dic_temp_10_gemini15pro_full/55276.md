dissipativities:/dɪˌsɪpəˈtɪvɪtiz/| n. |耗散性（复数）|The tendency of a system to lose energy or information over time, often due to friction or other irreversible processes.  The plural form of "dissipativity".|The study of dissipativities in complex systems is crucial for understanding their long-term behavior.  对复杂系统中耗散性的研究对于理解它们的长期行为至关重要。|Synonyms: damping, attenuation, decay, loss|Antonyms: amplification, growth, intensification|noun
