outsource:/ˈaʊtsɔːrs/| v.|外包，外判|To obtain (goods or a service) from an outside or foreign supplier, especially in place of an internal source.|Many companies outsource their customer service operations to other countries. 许多公司将客户服务业务外包到其他国家。|例句：The company outsources its IT support.  这家公司将其IT支持外包。

例句：They decided to outsource the manufacturing of the components. 他们决定将组件的制造外包。|近义词：subcontract, contract out, delegate, farm out|反义词：insource|词性：动词

outsourcing:/ˈaʊtsɔːsɪŋ/| n.|外包，外判|The practice of obtaining goods or a service from an outside or foreign supplier, especially in place of an internal source.|Outsourcing has become a common practice in many industries. 外包已成为许多行业的普遍做法。|例句：The company is considering outsourcing its payroll functions. 这家公司正在考虑将其工资核算功能外包。

例句：Outsourcing can help companies reduce costs. 外包可以帮助公司降低成本。|近义词：subcontracting, contracting out|反义词：insourcing|词性：名词
