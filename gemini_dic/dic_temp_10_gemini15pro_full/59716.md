eclectic:/ɪˈklɛktɪk/
adjective
折衷的，兼收并蓄的
selecting or choosing from various sources.
例句：She has an eclectic taste in music, enjoying everything from classical to hip-hop. 她对音乐的品味很折衷，从古典到嘻哈都喜欢。

例句：The museum's eclectic collection includes artifacts from all over the world.  这家博物馆的藏品兼收并蓄，包含了来自世界各地的文物。

近义词：diverse, varied, broad, comprehensive
反义词：narrow, limited, restricted, specific
词性: adjective

eclectics:/ɪˈklɛktɪks/
noun
折衷主义者；博采众长者
people who select or choose from various sources, especially in matters of opinion, taste, or style.
例句：The conference attracted eclectics from various fields of study.  这次会议吸引了来自各个研究领域的博采众长者。

例句：Eclectics often find inspiration in unconventional combinations of ideas. 折衷主义者经常在非传统的思想组合中找到灵感。

近义词：generalists, syncretists
反义词：purists, specialists
词性: noun

词源：From Greek *eklektikos* "selecting", from *eklegein* "to pick out, select", from *ek* "out" + *legein* "to gather, choose".
