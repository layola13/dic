daunt:/dɔːnt/ | v. | 使气馁，使畏惧 | To lessen the courage of; intimidate; dishearten. | The sheer size of the mountain daunts many climbers.  这座山的巨大规模使许多登山者望而生畏。| Synonyms: intimidate, discourage, dismay | Antonyms: encourage, embolden, hearten | verb

daunting:/ˈdɔːntɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人生畏的，令人气馁的 | Seeming difficult to deal with in a way that discourages you. |  The task ahead seemed daunting, but they were determined to succeed. 前方的任务似乎令人畏惧，但他们决心要成功。 | Synonyms: intimidating, formidable, discouraging | Antonyms: encouraging, easy, simple | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *danter, from Vulgar Latin *domitare "to tame," frequentative of Latin domare "to tame, subdue."
