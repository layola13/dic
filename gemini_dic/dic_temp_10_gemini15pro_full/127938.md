neonomianism:/ˌniːoʊˈnoʊmiənɪzəm/
n.
新律法主义
The doctrine that the gospel is a new law, with its promises and threats, as a motive to obedience.
Neonomianism was a controversial theological doctrine in 17th-century England.  新律法主义是17世纪英格兰一个备受争议的神学教义。
Neonomianism often led to debates about the role of good works in salvation. 新律法主义经常引发关于善行在救赎中作用的辩论。
近义词: legalism
反义词: antinomianism
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *neos* (new) + *nomos* (law) + *-ian* (adherent of) + *-ism* (doctrine).
