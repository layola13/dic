limpers:/ˈlɪmpərz/ | n. | 跛行者；一瘸一拐行走的人 | People who limp.  Those who walk with difficulty because of an injury to a leg or foot. |  The group of limpers slowly made their way across the field.  这群跛行者慢慢地穿过田野。| synonyms:  hoblers, cripples (somewhat offensive), those who walk with a limp | antonyms:  sprinters, runners, walkers | noun

limp:/lɪmp/ | vi. | 跛行；一瘸一拐地走 | To walk with difficulty because of an injury to a leg or foot. | He limped across the room. 他一瘸一拐地走过房间。| synonyms: hobble | antonyms: stride, sprint | verb

limp:/lɪmp/ | adj. | 柔软的；无力的；松软的 | Lacking firmness; flaccid. | The lettuce was old and limp.  生菜又老又软。 | synonyms: flaccid, soft, droopy | antonyms: firm, stiff, crisp | adjective
