exculpations: /ˌɛkskʌlˈpeɪʃənz/
|n.|
|开脱；免罪；辩解|
|Acts or statements that clear someone of blame or guilt.|
|The lawyer presented several exculpations for his client's actions. 律师为其当事人的行为提出了几项辩解。|

|The defendant offered several exculpations for his presence at the scene of the crime. 被告就其出现在犯罪现场提出了几种辩解。|

|Synonyms: apologies, defenses, justifications, explanations, excuses|
|Antonyms: accusations, condemnations, incriminations|
|noun|
