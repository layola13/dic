flossification:/flɒsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 棉絮化，成絮状 | The process of forming floss or becoming floss-like; the state of being flossified.  This refers to the formation of a soft, woolly, or thread-like substance, often used in a derogatory sense to describe weak or unsubstantial writing or thought. | The flossification of his arguments made them easy to refute. 他的论点空洞无力，很容易被反驳。| 近义词：fluff, padding, verbosity | 反义词：conciseness, succinctness | 词性: noun

Etymology: From "floss" (meaning a soft, thread-like substance) + "-ification" (a suffix denoting the process of making or becoming).  "Floss" itself comes from Old French "flosche" meaning "silk thread."
