lightening:/ˈlaɪtnɪŋ/ | n. | 闪电；减轻，减少 | the occurrence of a flash of natural electrical discharge of very short duration and high voltage between a cloud and the ground or within a cloud, accompanied by a bright flash and typically also thunder; the act of making something lighter in weight or less burdensome. |  The lightening lit up the whole sky. 闪电照亮了整个天空。|  The lightening of her workload made her feel less stressed.  减轻她的工作量让她感觉轻松了一些。| synonyms: flash, bolt, discharge; reduction, alleviation, easing | antonyms: darkening; increase, aggravation, intensification | noun

lightening:/ˈlaɪtnɪŋ/ | v. | (lighten的现在分词) 变亮，照亮; 减轻，减少 | present participle of lighten; to make or become lighter in color or brighter; to make or become less heavy or burdensome. | The sky was lightening as the sun began to rise. 随着太阳开始升起，天空渐渐变亮。 |  They are lightening the ship's load to improve its speed. 他们正在减轻船的载重以提高其速度。| synonyms: brightening, illuminating; reducing, alleviating, easing | antonyms: darkening; increasing, aggravating, intensifying | verb
