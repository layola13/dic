parroted:/ˈpærətɪd/ | v. | 模仿，鹦鹉学舌 | to repeat something someone else has said or written without understanding or thinking about it | He just parroted what he heard on the news. 他只是鹦鹉学舌般地重复了他在新闻里听到的内容。 | repeat, mimic, echo | originate, create | verb
parroted:/ˈpærətɪd/ | adj. |  被模仿的，鹦鹉学舌的 |  repeated without understanding, like a parrot mimicking sounds | His parroted phrases revealed his lack of original thought.  他鹦鹉学舌般的短语暴露了他缺乏原创思想。| imitative, unoriginal | original, innovative | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From parrot (n.) + -ed.  Ultimately from Old French *perrot, of uncertain origin.
