dulls:/dʌlz/| v. | 使变钝; 使迟钝; 减轻; 使阴暗 | Makes less sharp, sensitive, or vibrant.  | The rain dulled the colors of the sunset. 雨水使日落色彩暗淡。 |近义词：blunts, weakens, dims, subdues |反义词: sharpens, brightens, intensifies |词性:动词

dull:/dʌl/| adj. | 钝的; 迟钝的; 呆滞的; 阴暗的 | Not sharp, sensitive, or vibrant. | The knife was too dull to cut the bread. 这把刀太钝了，切不了面包。|近义词：blunt, obtuse, dim, drab |反义词: sharp, bright, keen, vibrant |词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dol "foolish," of Germanic origin; related to Dutch dol "mad, wild," German toll "mad, furious." The semantic shift from "foolish" to "uninteresting" and then to "not sharp" occurred over time.
