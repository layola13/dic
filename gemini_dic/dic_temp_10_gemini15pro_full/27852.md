canonizations:/ˌkænənɪˈzeɪʃənz/
n.
册封圣徒；认可；推崇；圣徒列入圣品名册
The act of officially declaring someone a saint, especially by the Catholic Church.  The process also refers to recognizing someone as important and worthy of respect.
The canonizations of the new saints took place in a grand ceremony at the Vatican.  新圣徒的册封仪式在梵蒂冈隆重举行。
The museum's exhibition amounts to a canonization of the artist's entire body of work.  博物馆的展览相当于对这位艺术家全部作品的推崇。
近义词:deification, glorification, exaltation
反义词:condemnation, denunciation
