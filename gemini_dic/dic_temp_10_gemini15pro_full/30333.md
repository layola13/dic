censorship: /ˈsensərʃɪp/
n. |审查制度，审查|The act of suppressing speech, public communication, or other information, on the basis that such material is considered objectionable, harmful, sensitive, or "inconvenient."|The government imposed strict censorship on the media. 政府对媒体实施了严格的审查。|近义词：suppression, restriction, control|反义词：freedom of speech, free expression|词性: 名词

censorship: /ˈsensərʃɪp/
n. |审查|The practice of officially examining books, movies, etc., and suppressing unacceptable parts.|The censorship of the press was lifted.  新闻审查制度被取消了。|近义词：examination, review|反义词：permission, allowance|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin  "censor" (a Roman magistrate who conducted the census and supervised public morals) + "-ship" (suffix forming abstract nouns).
