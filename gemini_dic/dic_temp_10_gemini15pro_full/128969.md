niobe:/ˈnaɪoʊbi/| n.|尼俄柏, 泪如雨下的人|A woman in Greek mythology who boasted of her fourteen children, causing Apollo and Artemis to kill them all.  She wept inconsolably and was turned into a rock from which water perpetually flowed.  Now used to refer to someone who grieves excessively or is tearful.|Niobe wept for her children, slain by Apollo and Artemis. 尼俄柏为她被阿波罗和阿尔忒弥斯杀死的孩子们哭泣。|Synonyms: mourner, weeper|Antonyms: rejoicer, celebrant|noun
