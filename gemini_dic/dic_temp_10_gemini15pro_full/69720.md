feod:/fjuːd/| n.|封地，采邑|A fief; an estate in land, especially one held on condition of feudal service.|例句：The king granted him a feod for his loyal service. 国王因其忠诚的服务而授予他一块封地。

例句：The knight held his feod from the duke. 骑士从公爵那里获得封地。|近义词：fief, benefice, estate|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *feohd, from Proto-Germanic *fehudiz, from *fehu "cattle, property".  Cognate with Old Frisian fiād, Old Saxon fehu, Old High German fihu, Old Norse fé, Gothic faihu.  Originally referring to movable property given as wages, it later came to mean land held in return for service.
