cuckoo:/ˈkʊkuː/| n. adj. vi.|杜鹃，布谷鸟，傻瓜|A bird known for its distinctive call and the habit of laying its eggs in other birds' nests. Can also be used informally to describe someone as crazy or foolish. |例句：The cuckoo's call echoed through the woods. 杜鹃的叫声在树林中回荡。|近义词：fool, crazy person|反义词:genius, sane person|noun
cuckoo:/ˈkʊkuː/| adj. |愚蠢的，疯狂的|Informal term meaning crazy or foolish.|例句：He had some cuckoo ideas about running the company. 他对经营公司有一些疯狂的想法。|近义词：crazy, foolish|反义词:sensible, wise|adjective
cuckoo:/ˈkʊkuː/| vi. |发出杜鹃的叫声|To make the sound of a cuckoo.|例句：The bird cuckooed from a nearby tree.  鸟儿在附近的树上发出杜鹃的叫声。|近义词：call, chirp|反义词:be silent|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cucu*, from Old French *cucu*, from Latin *cuculus*.  Ultimately imitative of the bird's call.
