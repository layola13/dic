malinger:/məˈlɪŋɡər/ | v. | 装病；逃避责任 | To pretend to be ill or incapacitated in order to avoid work or duty. | He malingered for weeks, hoping to avoid the upcoming exam. 他装病数周，希望逃避即将到来的考试。

He was suspected of malingering to get out of military service. 他被怀疑装病以逃避兵役。 | 近义词：shirk, feign, dodge, pretend  | 反义词：persevere, confront | verb

Etymology:
From French *malingre* "sickly, ailing," from Old French *malingre* "ill-disposed," of uncertain origin.
