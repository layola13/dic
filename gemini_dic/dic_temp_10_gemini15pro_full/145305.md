overbrief:/ˌoʊvərˈbriːf/
verb
过度指示；指示过简
to instruct or inform (someone) excessively or too concisely, often to the point of confusion or lack of preparedness.
The lawyer overbriefed his client, leading to confusion during the trial. 律师对他的客户指示过细，导致庭审期间的混乱。
The general overbriefed the troops, giving them so much information that they became overwhelmed. 将军向部队过度指示，给了他们太多的信息，使他们不知所措。
overinstruct, overexplain
underbrief
v.
