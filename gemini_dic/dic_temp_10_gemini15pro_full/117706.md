melancholia:/ˌmɛlənˈkəʊliə/ | n. | 忧郁，忧伤，悲哀 | A feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause. |  She suffered from deep melancholia after the death of her husband. 她丈夫去世后，她陷入了深深的忧郁之中。

She was overcome by a sudden wave of melancholia. 她突然被一阵忧伤淹没。

近义词: sadness, depression, gloom, dejection, wistfulness | 反义词: happiness, joy, elation, cheerfulness | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin melancholia, from Ancient Greek μελαγχολία (melankholía), from μέλας (mélas, “black”) + χολή (kholḗ, “bile”).  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholia.
