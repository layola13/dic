lifesaver:/ˈlaɪfˌseɪvər/| n.| 救命者；救生员；救生圈；救命的东西|A person or thing that saves someone's life, or rescues them from a difficult situation.|The doctor was a lifesaver when I broke my leg.  医生在我摔断腿的时候真是我的救命恩人。|  That quick loan was a real lifesaver. 那笔快速贷款真是帮了我大忙。|Synonym: rescuer, savior, godsend, help, aid|Antonym: menace, danger, threat|noun


lifesaver:/ˈlaɪfˌseɪvər/| n.|（通常指环形的）硬糖|A ring-shaped hard candy.|The child was given a lifesaver as a treat.  这孩子得到了一块救生圈形状的糖果作为奖励。|She always keeps a pack of lifesavers in her purse. 她包里总是放着一包救生圈糖。|Synonym: candy, sweet, hard candy|Antonym: (No direct antonym, but could consider something unhealthy or bitter)|noun
