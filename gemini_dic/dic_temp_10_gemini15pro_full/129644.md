non-count:/ˌnɒnˈkaʊnt/ (UK) /ˌnɑːnˈkaʊnt/ (US)| adj.|不可数的|Not able to be counted; referring to something that cannot be divided into separate units.  It's used to describe nouns that represent substances, concepts, or qualities that are not typically counted.|例句：Water is a non-count noun.  水是不可数名词。|近义词：mass, uncountable|反义词:count, countable|词性:adjective
