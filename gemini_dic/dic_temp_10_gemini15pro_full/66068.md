excalfaction: /ˌɛkskælˈfækʃən/
n.
加热；变热；发烧
the act or process of making something hot or the state of being made hot.
The excalfaction of the metal caused it to glow red. 金属的加热使它发出红光。
近义词: heating, warming
反义词: cooling, chilling
词性: 名词

词源:  From Latin *excalfactus*, past participle of *excalfacere* (“to heat up”), from *ex-* (“out, thoroughly”) + *calefacere* (“to make warm”).  *Calefacere* is from *cale-* (“warm”), related to *calidus* (“hot”), + *facere* (“to make, do”).
