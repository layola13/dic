equivocation:/ɪˌkwɪvəˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 含糊其辞，模棱两可，遁辞 | The use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or to avoid committing oneself. |  He was known for his equivocations and never gave a straight answer. 他以含糊其辞而闻名，从不直截了当地回答问题。 | synonyms: ambiguity, prevarication, evasion, double-talk, vagueness | antonyms: clarity, directness, honesty, forthrightness | noun

equivocate:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪt/ | v. | 含糊其辞，支吾其词 | To use ambiguous language so as to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself. |  When asked directly about his plans, the politician equivocated. 当被直接问及他的计划时，这位政客含糊其辞。 | synonyms: prevaricate, evade, hedge, fudge | antonyms: be forthright, be direct, be honest | verb
