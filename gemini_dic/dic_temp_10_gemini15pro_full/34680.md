clerk:/klɜːrk/ (UK), /klɜːrk/ (US)| n. v.|职员，办事员；店员；书记；牧师助理|A person employed in an office or bank to keep records, accounts, and undertake other routine administrative duties. Also, a salesperson in a store.  A lay officer of a church or parish who leads the responses of the congregation and performs other duties.|The clerk at the hotel was very helpful.  酒店的职员非常乐于助人。|Synonyms: assistant, employee, office worker, secretary, sales assistant, cashier|Antonyms: manager, executive, boss|noun
clerk:/klɜːrk/ (UK), /klɜːrk/ (US)| v.|担任职员；做店员|To work as a clerk.|He clerked in a grocery store during the summer. 他夏天在一家杂货店当店员。|Synonyms: work, staff|Antonyms: manage, supervise|verb
