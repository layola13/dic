backfire:/ˌbækˈfaɪər/ (US), /ˌbækˈfaɪə(r)/ (UK)| vi. n.|适得其反，逆火| (of a plan or scheme) rebound adversely on the originator; have the opposite effect to what was intended. (of an internal combustion engine) undergo a premature explosion in the inlet or exhaust manifold. A premature explosion in the inlet or exhaust manifold of an internal combustion engine. |

例句：
His plan to get a promotion backfired when his boss found out about his unethical tactics.
他升职的计划适得其反，因为老板发现了他不道德的策略。

Her attempt to control the situation backfired, making things even worse.
她试图控制局面的做法适得其反，使事情变得更糟。

The engine backfired loudly, startling everyone nearby.
发动机发出巨大的逆火声，吓到了附近的所有人。

The old car backfired as it went down the hill.
那辆旧车在下山时发出逆火声。


近义词：rebound, boomerang, misfire, flop, fail|
反义词：succeed, work, flourish|
词性：verb, noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From back + fire.  Originally referred to the explosion of a firearm's charge in the wrong direction. This sense extended metaphorically to schemes or plans.
