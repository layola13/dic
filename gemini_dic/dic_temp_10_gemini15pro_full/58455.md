ductible:/dʌk.tɪl/ /ˈdʌktəl/
n.|扣除额；免赔额|The amount of loss or damage that must be paid by the insured before an insurance company will pay a claim.|例句：The deductible on my car insurance is $500. 我的汽车保险的免赔额是500美元。|例句：A higher deductible usually means a lower premium.  更高的免赔额通常意味着更低的保费。|近义词：excess, out-of-pocket|反义词: N/A|词性:名词

ductile:/ˈdʌk.taɪl/ /ˈdʌktəl/
adj.|可延展的；易塑的；韧性的|Able to be drawn out into a thin wire or hammered into thin sheets without breaking.|例句：Copper is a very ductile metal. 铜是一种延展性很好的金属。|例句：Gold is the most ductile metal, capable of being drawn into extremely thin wires. 金是最具延展性的金属，可以拉成极细的金属丝。|近义词：malleable, pliable, flexible|反义词:brittle, rigid, inflexible|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *ductilis, from ductus (“a leading, drawing out”), past participle of ducere (“to lead, draw”).
