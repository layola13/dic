flailing:/ˈfleɪlɪŋ/| v. |挥舞；挣扎；乱动；（生意）失败 |Moving or swinging your arms or legs in a wild, uncontrolled way, often because you are trying to avoid falling or hitting something; failing badly. |The swimmer was flailing in the water. 那位游泳者在水中挣扎。|Synonyms: thrashing, waving, swinging, struggling, failing |Antonyms: controlled, steady, succeeding |verb

flailing:/ˈfleɪlɪŋ/| adj. |挥舞的；挣扎的；失败的 |Characterized by wild, uncontrolled movements, especially of the arms and legs; failing badly. |The company's flailing attempts to stay afloat ultimately failed. 公司为维持运营而做的徒劳挣扎最终失败了。|Synonyms: thrashing, waving, failing, struggling |Antonyms: controlled, steady, succeeding |adjective
