disruptiveness:/dɪsˈrʌptɪvnəs/
n.|破坏性，扰乱性|The quality of being disruptive; causing disorder or interruption.|The disruptiveness of the new technology changed the entire industry. 新技术的破坏性改变了整个行业。|
近义词:disorder, chaos, interruption, turbulence, instability|
反义词:order, stability, calmness, peacefulness|
词性:noun

disrupt:/dɪsˈrʌpt/
v.|扰乱，中断，破坏|To interrupt the normal progress or activity of something.|The loud noise disrupted my concentration.  巨大的噪音扰乱了我的注意力。|
近义词:interrupt, disturb, interfere with, upset|
反义词:organize, order, arrange, facilitate|
词性:verb

disruptive:/dɪsˈrʌptɪv/
adj.|破坏性的，扰乱性的|Causing or tending to cause disruption.|The disruptive student was asked to leave the classroom.  扰乱课堂秩序的学生被要求离开教室。|
近义词:disturbing, troublesome, unruly, chaotic|
反义词:orderly, peaceful, calm, constructive|
词性:adjective
