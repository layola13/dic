exalters:/ɪɡˈzɔːltərz/
|n.|颂扬者，赞美者|People who exalt or praise someone or something highly.  Those who raise others in rank, power, or character.|The king's exalters filled the court, singing his praises. 国王的赞美者挤满了宫廷，歌颂着他的功绩。|Synonyms: praisers, eulogists, lauders|Antonyms: critics, detractors|Noun


exalts:/ɪɡˈzɔːlts/
|vt.|提升；赞扬；使兴奋|1. To raise in rank, power, or character. 2. To praise highly; glorify. 3. To fill with joy or pride.|The queen exalts her loyal knights. 女王提升了她忠诚的骑士。|Synonyms: elevates, promotes, praises, lauds|Antonyms: degrades, demotes, criticizes|Verb



ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *exalten*, from Old French *exalter*, from Latin *exaltare*, from *ex-* (out, upward) + *altus* (high).
