matronlike:/ˈmeɪtrənlaɪk/| adj.|像主妇的，像保姆的，成熟稳重的|Resembling or characteristic of a matron, especially in being dignified, serious, and conventionally respectable.  Suggests an older woman, often plump, who is imposing and authoritative, sometimes in a kindly way, but often with a sense of social superiority.|例句：She had a rather matronlike air about her, despite her young age. 尽管她很年轻，但她有一种相当成熟稳重的气质。

例句：Her matronlike figure commanded respect. 她那成熟稳重的姿态令人肃然起敬。|近义词：motherly, stately, dignified, imposing|反义词：childish, immature, frivolous, girlish|词性:形容词

词源: From "matron" (meaning a married woman, especially one who is mature and dignified) + "-like" (a suffix meaning resembling or characteristic of).  "Matron" comes from Old French "matrone," ultimately from Latin "māter" (mother).
