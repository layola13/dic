deaned:/diːnd/
|v.|革除…的院长职务；免去…的系主任职务|Removed from the position of dean or department head. |The university deaned him for misconduct. 大学因他的不当行为免去了他的院长职务。| dismissed, removed, ousted | appointed, reinstated | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From *dean* (n.) + *-ed* (verb suffix forming the past participle).  The noun *dean* comes from Old French *deien*, from Late Latin *decanus*, meaning "leader of ten," ultimately from Greek *deka* "ten."  Originally referred to a monastic superior in charge of ten monks.
