dementate:/dɪˈmɛnteɪt/ | vt. | 使精神错乱；使痴呆 | To deprive of reason or understanding; make insane or demented. |  The illness dementated him, leaving him unable to care for himself.  这种疾病使他精神错乱，无法自理。|  The trauma of the war dementated many soldiers. 战争的创伤使许多士兵精神错乱。 | 近义词: madden, derange, unhinge | 反义词:  stabilize, soothe, calm | 词性: 动词

dementate:/dɪˈmɛnteɪt/ | adj. | 精神错乱的；痴呆的| Deprived of reason or understanding; insane or demented. | The dementate patient wandered the halls of the hospital.  痴呆的病人徘徊在医院的走廊里。|  He became increasingly dementate in his old age. 他在老年时变得越来越痴呆。 | 近义词: insane, mad, demented | 反义词: sane, lucid, rational | 词性: 形容词


词源:  Late Middle English: from Latin dementat- ‘made mad’, from the verb dementare, from de- ‘away, out of’ + mens, ment- ‘mind’.
