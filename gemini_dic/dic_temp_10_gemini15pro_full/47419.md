deadlocking:/ˈdɛdlɒkɪŋ/ | n. | 死锁，僵局 |A situation in which two or more processes are unable to proceed because each is waiting for the other to release a resource.|The deadlocking of the peace talks frustrated both sides. 和平谈判陷入僵局，双方都感到沮丧。|近义词：stalemate, impasse, standstill|反义词：agreement, compromise, resolution|词性:名词 (gerund or present participle of deadlock)


deadlock:/ˈdɛdlɒk/ | n. v. | 死锁，僵局；使陷入僵局 |(n.) A situation, typically one involving opposing parties, in which no progress can be made. (v.) Cause (a situation or process) to become deadlocked.| (n.) The negotiations reached a deadlock. 谈判陷入了僵局。| (v.) The jury deadlocked on the final verdict. 陪审团在最终裁决上陷入僵局。| 近义词：(n.) stalemate, impasse, standstill; (v.) stall, block, obstruct | 反义词：(n.) agreement, compromise, breakthrough; (v.) expedite, facilitate, resolve |词性:名词、动词
