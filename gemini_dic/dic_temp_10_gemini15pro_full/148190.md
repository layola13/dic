overshadowing:/ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊɪŋ/
verb|使黯然失色，使相形见绌，使蒙上阴影|to make someone or something seem less important or impressive|Her success was overshadowed by her brother's. 她的成功被他哥哥的光芒所掩盖。|
近义词: eclipse, obscure, dwarf
反义词: highlight, emphasize
词性: verb

overshadowing:/ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊɪŋ/
noun|遮蔽，蒙阴|the act of overshadowing something or someone|The overshadowing of her achievements by her husband's fame made her bitter.  丈夫的名声使她的成就黯然失色，这让她很痛苦。|
近义词: eclipsing, obscuring
反义词: highlighting, emphasizing
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From over + shadow + -ing.  "Over" indicates something above or beyond, and "shadow" refers to a darkened area.  Thus, overshadowing literally means casting a shadow over something, making it less noticeable or important.
