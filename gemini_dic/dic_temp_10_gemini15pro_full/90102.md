hot-airs:/ˈhɒt ɛəz/ (UK), /ˈhɑːt ɛrz/ (US)| n.|夸夸其谈，空话|Empty or pretentious talk, often boastful or pompous.|He's full of hot air, promising things he can't deliver. 他满嘴空话，承诺的事情他做不到。|She dismissed his speech as nothing but hot air. 她认为他的演讲只不过是夸夸其谈。|近义词：bluster, bombast, bragging, boasting, empty talk, rhetoric|反义词：facts, truth, sincerity, understatement|词性:名词

hot-air:/ˈhɒt ɛə(r)/ (UK), /ˈhɑːt ɛr/ (US)| adj.|夸夸其谈的|Characterized by or full of empty, boastful talk.|His hot-air pronouncements did not impress anyone. 他夸夸其谈的声明并没有给任何人留下印象。|It was just another hot-air political speech. 这只是又一次夸夸其谈的政治演讲。|近义词：boastful, pompous, pretentious|反义词：humble, modest, down-to-earth|词性:形容词

词源: hot + air.  "Hot air" literally refers to the heated air that makes a hot air balloon rise.  Figuratively, it refers to empty, boastful talk, as if the speaker were inflated with nothing but hot air.
