metastoma: /ˌmɛtəˈstoʊmə/ | n. | 后口板 | A plate-like or shield-like structure on the ventral side of the prosoma (cephalothorax) in certain extinct arthropods, particularly eurypterids (sea scorpions), thought to have functioned in feeding. |  The metastoma of this eurypterid fossil is remarkably well-preserved.  这只广翅鲎化石的后口板保存得非常完好。| synonyms:  mouthplate (less precise) | antonyms:  N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *μετα-* (meta-) "after, behind, changed" + *στόμα* (stoma) "mouth."
