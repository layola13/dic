equitable:/ˈɛkwɪtəbəl/ | adj. |公平的，公正的；合理的| Fair and just; based on what is morally right. | The judge made an equitable decision in the case. 法官对此案作出了公正的判决。| Synonyms: fair, just, impartial, unbiased | Antonyms: unfair, unjust, biased, prejudiced | adjective

equitable:/ˈɛkwɪtəbəl/ | adj. |公平合理的，合法的(指行为、权利等)| Valid in equity as distinct from law. |  He sought an equitable remedy in court. 他在法庭上寻求公平的补救。 | Synonyms: rightful, legitimate, valid | Antonyms: unlawful, illegitimate, invalid | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *equitable, from Old French equitable, from Latin aequitābilis (“equitable, just”), from aequitās (“equity, justice, fairness”), from aequus (“equal, level, fair, just”).
