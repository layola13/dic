pantography:/pænˈtɒɡrəfi/ | n. | 缩放仪，缩放术；缩放画|The art or process of copying a drawing or diagram on a different scale using a pantograph.  A device used for copying a drawing or diagram on a different scale, consisting of four bars linked in a parallelogram with a tracing point at one corner and a pencil at another.|He used a pantograph to enlarge the map for the presentation. 他用缩放仪放大地图用于演示。|Synonyms: scaling, enlargement, reduction, copying |Antonyms: N/A | Noun


pantograph:/ˈpæntəɡrɑːf/ | n. | 缩放仪，缩放画；（电车的）集电弓，受电弓| A device, typically consisting of four jointed rods forming a parallelogram, used for copying drawings to a different scale or transferring designs to a surface.  A device on an electric train or tram that collects power from an overhead wire.|The pantograph failed, causing the train to lose power. 集电弓故障导致火车断电。|Synonyms: scaling device, copying device, current collector |Antonyms: N/A | Noun
