ingenuously:/ɪnˈdʒɛnjuəsli/
|adv.|
|天真地；坦率地；正直地|
|In a manner that is innocent, frank, and straightforward; without deception or guile.|
|She ingenuously confessed her ignorance of the subject.|她坦率地承认自己对这个话题一无所知。|
|candidly, frankly, honestly, artlessly, naively|
|deceitfully, craftily, guilefully, disingenuously|
|adverb|

ingenuous:/ɪnˈdʒɛnjuəs/
|adj.|
|天真的；坦率的；正直的|
|Innocent and unsuspecting; showing innocent or childlike simplicity and candidness.|
|He had an ingenuous smile that made everyone trust him.|他那天真的笑容让每个人都信任他。|
|candid, frank, honest, artless, naive|
|deceitful, crafty, guileful, disingenuous|
|adjective|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *ingenuus* "native, freeborn, noble, frank, open," from *in* "in" + *gignere* "to beget."  The sense shifted from "noble" to "honorably straightforward" and then to "naive."
