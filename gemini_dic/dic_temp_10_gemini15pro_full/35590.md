coarctation:/ˌkəʊɑːkˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 狭窄，收缩，缩窄 | A narrowing or constriction of a passage, such as a blood vessel or the aorta. |  The doctor diagnosed a coarctation of the aorta in the infant.  医生诊断婴儿患有主动脉缩窄。| synonyms: constriction, narrowing, stenosis | antonyms: dilation, widening | noun


coarctate:/koʊˈɑːrkteɪt/ | adj. | 缩窄的，收缩的 | Compressed; constricted.  | The patient's coarctate aorta required surgery. 病人的缩窄性主动脉需要手术。 | synonyms: constricted, narrowed, compressed | antonyms: dilated, widened | adjective


Etymology:

From Late Latin *coarctātiōn-*, from Latin coarctāre ‘to press together, restrict’, from co- ‘together’ + arctāre ‘to press close, confine’ (from arctus ‘narrow, close’).
