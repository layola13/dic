blolly:/ˈblɒli/ (UK) /ˈblɑːli/ (US)| n.|棒棒糖；（英俚）钱；傻瓜|A lollipop, especially a large one. (UK) Also slang for money (UK) and a foolish person (UK).|例句：The child was happily sucking on a bright red blolly.  这孩子开心地吮吸着一根鲜红色的棒棒糖。

例句：He's made a blolly out of selling those old cars. 他靠卖那些旧车赚了一大笔钱。(UK slang)


近义词：lollipop, sucker, sweet (for candy); cash, dough, money (for money); fool, idiot, dolt (for person)|反义词: (No direct antonym, depends on the meaning used) |词性:Noun

blolly:/ˈblɒli/ (UK) /ˈblɑːli/ (US)| n.|钱|Slang for money. Primarily used in British English.|例句：He came into some blolly when his uncle died. 他叔叔去世后，他得到了一笔钱。(UK slang)

例句：I need some blolly to get this project off the ground. 我需要一些资金来启动这个项目。(UK slang)


近义词：cash, dough, readies, loot, dosh (UK slang)|反义词:debt |词性:Noun

blolly:/ˈblɒli/ (UK) /ˈblɑːli/ (US)| n.|傻瓜|Slang for a foolish person. Primarily used in British English.|例句：Don't be such a blolly!  别这么傻！ (UK slang)

例句：He's a bit of a blolly, but he's got a good heart. 他有点傻，但心地善良。(UK slang)


近义词：fool, idiot, dolt, twit, nitwit (UK slang)|反义词:genius, sage |词性:Noun
