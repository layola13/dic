downsize:/ˈdaʊnsaɪz/| v. | 缩减规模，裁员 | To reduce the size and scope of a company or organization, often by laying off employees. |  Many companies were forced to downsize during the recession. 许多公司在经济衰退期间被迫裁员。

The company downsized its workforce by 10%.  这家公司裁员了10%。| 近义词：reduce, shrink, cut back | 反义词：expand, grow, increase | 词性: 动词

downsized:/ˈdaʊnsaɪzd/| adj. |  缩减规模的，被裁员的 | Made smaller, reduced in size, typically referring to a company or its workforce. | He took a job with a downsized company. 他在一家规模缩小的公司找到了一份工作。

She felt lost and uncertain after being downsized from her long-term position.  在她长期任职的职位被裁员后，她感到迷茫和不确定。| 近义词：reduced, smaller | 反义词：expanded, larger | 词性: 形容词
