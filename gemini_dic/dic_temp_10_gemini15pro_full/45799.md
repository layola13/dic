custodialism:/kʌˈstoʊdiəlɪzəm/
n.|监护主义；监护式管理；监禁主义|A system where the state or an institution plays a dominant role in providing for and controlling the lives of individuals, often offering basic needs but limiting freedom and autonomy.  It can refer to the care of vulnerable populations like children, the elderly, or the disabled, as well as to the management of prisoners.|The prison system is often criticized for its focus on custodialism rather than rehabilitation. 监狱系统经常因其注重监护而不是改造而受到批评。|近义词:paternalism, institutionalization, confinement|反义词:independence, autonomy, self-reliance|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "custody" (Latin: custodia "a guarding, keeping, watching, care, charge, protection") + "-ial" (forming adjectives) + "-ism" (forming nouns signifying a doctrine, practice, or characteristic).
