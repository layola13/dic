anachronism:/əˈnækrənɪzəm/
|n.|时代错误；年代误植；不合时代的人或物|Something that is mistakenly placed in a time where it does not belong in a story, movie, etc., or something that belongs to the past and does not fit in the present.|The movie is full of anachronisms, like characters using cell phones in ancient Rome.|近义词:misplacement, error, inconsistency|反义词:synchronism, contemporaneity|Noun

anachronisms:/əˈnækrənɪzəmz/
|n.|时代错误（anachronism的复数）|Plural form of anachronism, referring to multiple instances of things out of their proper time.|The history book was riddled with anachronisms, making it unreliable.|近义词:misplacements, errors, inconsistencies|反义词:synchronisms, contemporarieties|Noun
