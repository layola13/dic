halloween: /ˌhæləˈwiːn/ (US) /ˌhæləʊˈiːn/ (UK)| n.|万圣节前夕|The eve of All Saints' Day, October 31, observed especially by children who dress in costumes and solicit candy or other treats.  It is thought to have originated with the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain, when people would light bonfires and wear costumes to ward off ghosts.| Children go trick-or-treating on Halloween. 孩子们在万圣节前夕去讨要糖果。|近义词: All Hallows' Eve, Samhain|反义词: None|词性: noun

词源: From Scottish Gaelic *Samhuinn*, literally "summer's end," from *samhradh* "summer" + *fhuin* "end."  Later influenced by All Hallows' Eve, the evening before All Saints' Day (November 1st).
