imaginist:/ɪˈmæʤɪnɪst/| n. adj.|意象主义者，意象派的|A member of a school of early 20th-century poets who advocated the use of clear, sharp, precise images and the rejection of traditional poetic forms and diction.  Also used as an adjective to describe the style of poetry advocated by these poets.| Ezra Pound was a leading figure in the Imagist movement.  Ezra Pound 是意象派运动的领军人物。

The poem is written in a characteristically imaginist style. 这首诗是以典型的意象派风格写成的。|近义词：modernist, symbolist|反义词：traditionalist|词性: noun, adjective

Etymology: From *image* + *-ist*. *Image* comes from the Latin *imago*, meaning "likeness, copy, representation." The suffix *-ist* denotes a person who practices or adheres to a particular doctrine, system, or belief.
