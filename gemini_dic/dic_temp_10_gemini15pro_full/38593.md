concordist:/kənˈkɔːrdɪst/
n.
调和主义者; 协调者; 妥协者
A person who advocates or practices concordism, especially in biblical interpretation.
The concordist attempted to reconcile the scientific evidence with the biblical account of creation.  这位调和主义者试图使科学证据与圣经的创世记相协调。
近义词:harmonizer, reconciler, mediator
反义词:dissenter, nonconformist
词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From concord + -ist. Concord from Late Latin concordia "agreement, harmony," from Latin concors (genitive concordis) "harmonious, agreeing," literally "hearts together," from com- "together" (see com-) + cor "heart" (see heart (n.)). -ist from French -iste, from Latin -ista (agent noun suffix), from Greek -istes.
