The word "burge" doesn't exist in standard English. It's likely a misspelling of "bulge" or "burgeon."  I'll provide analyses for both.

**Bulge:**

/bʌldʒ/ | n. vi. | 膨胀，凸起；骤增 | A rounded swelling or protuberance that distorts a flat or straight surface.  To swell outwards.|  His pockets bulged with candy. 他的口袋里装满了糖果，鼓起来了。 | The dam began to bulge under the pressure of the water.  大坝在水压下开始膨胀。 | synonyms: swell, protrude, expand | antonyms: contract, shrink, deflate | noun, verb


**Burgeon:**

/ˈbɜːrdʒən/ | vi. |  迅速生长；发展；繁荣 | To begin to grow or increase rapidly; flourish. |  The city’s population has burgeoned in recent years.  近年来，该市的人口迅速增长。 |  New industries are burgeoning in the developing world. 新兴产业在发展中国家蓬勃发展。 | synonyms: flourish, thrive, prosper | antonyms: wither, decline, diminish | verb
