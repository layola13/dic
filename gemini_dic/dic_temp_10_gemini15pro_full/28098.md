capitalisable:/ˈkæpɪtəlaɪzəbl/| adj.|可大写的，可资本化的|Able to be capitalized (i.e., written with an initial capital letter).|Example: Proper nouns are capitalisable. 专有名词需要大写。|Synonym: uppercasable|Antonym: non-capitalisable|adjective

capitalisable:/ˌkæpɪtəˈlaɪzəb(ə)l/| adj.|可大写的，可资本化的|Able to be capitalized (i.e., written with an initial capital letter).|Example: The first word of a sentence is always capitalisable. 句子的第一个单词总是要大写的。|Synonym: uppercasable|Antonym: non-capitalisable|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From capitalize + -able. Capitalize comes from capital, ultimately from Latin *capitālis* "of the head," from *caput* "head".
