bursting:/ˈbɜːstɪŋ/ (US), /ˈbɜːstɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.| 爆裂的，充满的，快要…的 |  full to the point of overflowing or splitting open; extremely full of a particular quality or feeling. | The dam was bursting with the pressure of the floodwaters.  大坝快要被洪水冲垮了。| synonyms: overflowing, crammed, packed, teeming, exploding | antonyms: empty, depleted, lacking| adjective


bursting:/ˈbɜːrstɪŋ/ (US), /ˈbɜːrstɪŋ/ (UK)| v.| 爆裂，突然… | present participle of burst. |  He was bursting with pride after his daughter's graduation. 他为女儿的毕业感到无比自豪。 | synonyms: exploding, rupturing, splitting | antonyms: imploding, collapsing| verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bersten, from Old English berstan (“to burst”), from Proto-Germanic *brestaną (“to break, burst, crack”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰreg- (“to break”). Cognate with Old Frisian bersta (“to burst”), Middle Dutch bersten, barsten (“to burst”), German bersten (“to burst”), Old Norse bresta (“to break, burst, crack”).
