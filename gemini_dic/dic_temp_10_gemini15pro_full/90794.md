humour:/ˈhjuːmə(r)/ (UK) /ˈhjuːmər/ (US)| n. vt.|幽默，诙谐;  体液; 情绪; 奇想; 迁就|A person's humour is their mood or state of mind.  In ancient medicine, humour was thought to be the four fluids in the body that determined a person's health and character.  To humour someone is to indulge or go along with their wishes.|He has a great sense of humour.  She humoured him by agreeing to his request.|Synonyms: moods, temperaments, dispositions, whims, fancies; indulge, pamper|Antonyms: seriousness, solemnity; frustrate, deny|Noun, Verb
