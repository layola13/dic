deliriously:/dɪˈlɪriəsli/
| adv. |
| 狂喜地，疯狂地，神志不清地 |
| In a manner characterized by delirium; ecstatically, wildly, or incoherently.  It describes a state of extreme happiness, excitement, or mental confusion. |
| She laughed deliriously when she heard the good news. 她听到好消息后狂喜地大笑起来。 |
| He was deliriously happy after winning the lottery.  他中了彩票后欣喜若狂。|
|  The patient was talking deliriously due to the high fever.  病人因为高烧而胡言乱语。 |
| 近义词: ecstatically, rapturously, wildly, frantically, madly |
| 反义词: calmly, rationally, lucidly |
| 词性: 副词 |


delirium:/dɪˈlɪriəm/
| n. |
| 谵妄，精神错乱，极度兴奋 |
| A state of mental confusion and disorientation, often accompanied by hallucinations, restlessness, and incoherence.  It can also refer to a state of wild excitement or ecstasy. |
| The high fever induced delirium in the patient.  高烧使病人神志不清。 |
| He was in a delirium of joy after his team won. 他在球队获胜后欣喜若狂。|
| 近义词: madness, frenzy, hysteria |
| 反义词: lucidity, sanity, coherence |
| 词性: 名词 |
