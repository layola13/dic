expositor:/ɪkˈspɑːzɪtər/ /ɛkˈspɑzɪtər/ | n. |讲解员，阐释者|A person who explains or interprets something, especially a difficult text or subject. |The expositor clarified the complex philosophical concepts for the audience. 讲解员为观众阐明了复杂的哲学概念。| The museum hired an expositor to guide visitors through the new exhibition.  博物馆聘请了一位讲解员引导游客参观新展览。|Synonym: interpreter, explainer, commentator |Antonym: obfuscator, misinterpreter | noun

expositor:/ɪkˈspɑːzɪtər/ /ɛkˈspɑzɪtər/ | adj. |说明的，解释的 (古用法，现在很少使用)|Serving to explain or expound. |His expositor notes on the text were very helpful. 他对文本的解释性注释非常有帮助。(古用法，现代英语中很少这样使用)|  (现代英语中更倾向于用explanatory)  |Synonym: explanatory, interpretive |Antonym: unclear, confusing | adjective
