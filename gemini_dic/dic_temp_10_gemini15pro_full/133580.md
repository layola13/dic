nonflaky:/ˌnɑːnˈfleɪki/
adjective
稳定的，可靠的，不薄片的
Not flaky; reliable, consistent, and not prone to breaking down or failing.
The software is known for its nonflaky performance.  这个软件以其稳定的性能而闻名。
We need a nonflaky solution to this problem. 我们需要一个可靠的解决方案来解决这个问题。
近义词:reliable, stable, dependable, robust, solid
反义词:flaky, unreliable, unstable, erratic, unpredictable
词性:adjective

词源:
The word "nonflaky" is formed by adding the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") to the adjective "flaky."  "Flaky" originally referred to something that breaks or separates easily into flakes or thin pieces.  This meaning was then extended metaphorically to describe someone or something unreliable or inconsistent.  Therefore, "nonflaky" means the opposite: not flaky, or reliable.
