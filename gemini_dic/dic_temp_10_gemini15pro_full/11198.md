architectonic: /ˌɑːrkɪˈtɛktɒnɪk/ (US) /ˌɑːkɪtɛkˈtɒnɪk/ (UK)| adj. n.| 建筑学的，构造的；体系的|Relating to the science of architecture or building, or having a structured and unified form like a well-designed building.  Also, the science or theory of architecture itself (as a noun).| The architectonic quality of the cathedral was breathtaking.  这座大教堂的建筑风格令人叹为观止。|synonyms: structural, organizational, architectural, systematic| antonyms: chaotic, disorganized, formless| adjective, noun
Etymology->
From Greek *arkhitektonikos*, from *arkhitektōn* "master builder, architect," from *arkhi-* "chief" + *tektōn* "builder, carpenter."
