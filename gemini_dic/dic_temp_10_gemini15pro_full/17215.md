bawble:/ˈbɔːbl/ (US), /ˈbɔːbəl/ (UK)| n. |小玩意儿；廉价珠宝；华而不实的东西|A small, inexpensive trinket or piece of jewelry, often gaudy or showy. It can also refer to something unimportant or frivolous.|She was fascinated by the glittering bawbles at the market stall. 她被市场摊位上闪闪发光的小玩意儿迷住了。

The politician dismissed the proposal as a mere bawble.  这位政治家认为这项提议只是一个华而不实的东西。|近义词：trinket, knick-knack, trifle, gewgaw, bauble|反义词：necessity, essential, valuable|词性：名词

词源:
From Middle English *babel, probably from Old French *babel, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to bauble (verb), meaning "to babble" or "to cheat," suggesting a connection to worthless talk or deception.
