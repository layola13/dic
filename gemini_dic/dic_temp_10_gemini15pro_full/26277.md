buttons:/ˈbʌtnz/ (US), /ˈbʌtənz/ (UK)| n.|按钮，纽扣|Small, usually round, discs or objects used for fastening clothes or activating something.|She sewed buttons onto the coat. 她把纽扣缝在外套上。|synonyms: fasteners, toggles, switches|antonyms: zippers, hooks|noun

buttons:/ˈbʌtnz/ (US), /ˈbʌtənz/ (UK)| v.|扣上纽扣|To fasten something with buttons.|He buttoned his shirt. 他扣上了他的衬衫。|synonyms: fasten, secure|antonyms: unbutton, unfasten|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *boton*, from Old French *boton* (“bud, button”),  from Late Latin *bottō*, from a Germanic source, related to *butt*  "thick end".  The semantic development is from "bud" to "small rounded object."
