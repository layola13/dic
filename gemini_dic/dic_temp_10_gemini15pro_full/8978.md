antilegomena:/ˌæntɪlɪˈɡɒmənə/ |n.|次正经书，次经|Books or passages of the Bible whose canonical status has been disputed.|The pastor mentioned that some scholars consider the Book of Jude to be among the antilegomena.|近义词:apocrypha, deuterocanonical books|反义词:protocanonical books|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek ἀντιλεγόμενα (antilegómena, “things spoken against”), neuter plural present passive participle of ἀντιλέγω (antilégō, “I speak against”), from ἀντί (antí, “against”) + λέγω (légō, “I speak”).
