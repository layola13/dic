leave-well-enough-alone:/ˌliːv wɛl ɪˈnʌf əˈloʊn/ | idiom | 适可而止；不要画蛇添足；不要越俎代庖 |  To refrain from trying to improve something that is already satisfactory, often because any change might make things worse. |  The cake was a little lopsided, but I decided to leave well enough alone and not try to fix it.  蛋糕有点歪，但我决定适可而止，不去试图修复它。| synonyms: let sleeping dogs lie, don't rock the boat, if it ain't broke, don't fix it | antonyms: tamper with, meddle with, interfere with | phrase

leave-well-enough-alone:/ˌliːv wɛl ɪˈnʌf əˈloʊn/ | idiom | 不要多此一举；不去管它也无妨 | To avoid interfering in a situation that is already satisfactory and potentially making it worse. | He was tempted to offer advice, but decided to leave well enough alone. 他很想提建议，但决定不去管它也无妨。 | synonyms:  let it be, keep one's hands off, mind one's own business | antonyms: interfere, intervene, meddle | phrase
