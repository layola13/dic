/nɒn-hɪˈbreɪɪkli/ | adv. | 非希伯来地；不用希伯来语 | In a manner not characteristic of or associated with Hebrew language or culture. |  She spoke of the prophets non-hebraically, focusing on their universal message. 她以非希伯来方式谈论先知，重点关注他们的普世信息。 |  non-biblically, secularly | Hebraically, Biblically | adverb

Etymology:
* non-: Prefix meaning "not." From Latin *non*.
* Hebraically:  Derived from "Hebrew" + "-ically." "Hebrew" from Middle English *Ebreu*, from Old French *ebreu*, ultimately from Latin *Hebraeus*, from Greek *Hebraios*,  from Hebrew *`Ivri* (meaning "one from across the river").  "-ically" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives, indicating "in a ... manner."
