debauch:/dɪˈbɔːtʃ/ /dɪˈbɑːtʃ/ | n. v. | 放荡，堕落；使…堕落 |  An act or occasion of excessive indulgence in pleasure, especially alcohol or sex; to corrupt or lead astray morally. |  He went on a weekend debauch of drinking and gambling. 他周末纵情酒色，赌博。| synonyms: dissipation, carousing, revelry, orgy, corruption | antonyms: abstinence, sobriety, morality, virtue | noun

debauch:/dɪˈbɔːtʃ/ /dɪˈbɑːtʃ/ | v. | 使…堕落；腐蚀 | To corrupt or lead astray morally. | The emperor was debauched by his advisors. 皇帝被他的顾问们腐蚀了。 | synonyms: corrupt, pervert, deprave, demoralize | antonyms: purify, elevate, uplift, edify | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *desbaucher*, "to lead astray, seduce," from *des-* (reversal) + *bauche* "workshop, joist."  The original sense was to entice a worker away from his job.
