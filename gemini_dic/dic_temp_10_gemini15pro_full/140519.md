oleaster: /ˌoʊliˈæstər/ | n. |沙枣树，俄罗斯橄榄 |A shrub or small tree (Elaeagnus angustifolia) of the oleaster family, native to Eurasia, having silvery leaves and fragrant flowers followed by small, yellowish, edible fruits. |The oleaster is a hardy plant that can tolerate drought conditions. 沙枣树是一种耐旱的植物。| The oleaster's fruit is used to make jams and jellies. 沙枣果可以用来制作果酱和果冻。|Synonyms: Russian olive, wild olive, silverberry | Antonyms: N/A | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *oleaster, from olea (“olive”) + -aster (“-like”).  The name reflects the superficial resemblance of the fruit to that of the olive tree.
