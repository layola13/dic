filches:/fɪltʃɪz/
| v. (third-person singular simple present filches, present participle filching, simple past and past participle filched)|偷窃，窃取|To steal something, typically of small value, in a furtive or sly manner.|He filched a candy bar from the store when no one was looking. 他趁没人注意的时候从商店里偷了一块糖果。|
|近义词：pilfer, purloin, swipe, snatch|
|反义词：return, give, offer|
|词性: verb|

filches:/ˈfɪltʃɪz/
| n. (plural of filch)|偷窃行为；赃物|The act of stealing something small or insignificant; stolen goods.|The filches he'd accumulated over the years amounted to a small fortune. 多年来他积累的赃物相当于一小笔财富。|
|近义词：theft, pilferage, larceny|
|反义词：donation, gift|
|词性: noun|
