filibustering:/ˈfɪlɪbʌstərɪŋ/
n.
冗长演说；阻挠议事
The act of obstructing progress in a legislative assembly by using prolonged speeches or other tactics.
The senator used filibustering to prevent the bill from coming to a vote.  这位参议员用冗长演说来阻止该法案进行投票。
近义词：stonewalling, delaying, obstructing
反义词：expediting, facilitating
词性：名词

filibuster:/ˈfɪlɪbʌstər/
n.
阻挠议事的人
A person who engages in filibustering.
The filibuster held the floor for over 12 hours.  这个阻挠议事的人占据发言席超过12个小时。
近义词：obstructionist
反义词：supporter
词性：名词


filibuster:/ˈfɪlɪbʌstər/
v.
进行冗长演说以阻挠议事
To obstruct progress in a legislative assembly by using prolonged speeches or other tactics.
The opposition party filibustered the bill for weeks.  反对党用冗长演说阻挠了该法案数周。
近义词：stonewall, delay, obstruct
反义词：expedite, facilitate
词性：动词

词源: From Spanish *filibustero* "freebooter," from Dutch *vrijbuiter* "freebooter, pirate." Originally referred to pirates in the Caribbean, then to adventurers who interfered in Central American politics in the 19th century. The meaning later shifted to its current parliamentary sense.
