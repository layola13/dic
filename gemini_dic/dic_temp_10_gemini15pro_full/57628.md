draughts:/drɑːfts/ | n. | 西洋跳棋; 气流; 通风; (尤指房间、烟囱、炉子等供暖设备的) 抽力 | A board game played on a checkered board with 12 pieces per side, where the pieces move diagonally and capture by jumping over an opponent's piece.  Also refers to a current of air. |  例句：We played a game of draughts. 我们玩了一局西洋跳棋。

例句：There's a terrible draught in this room. 这个房间里有股可怕的穿堂风。 | 近义词：checkers (US), current (of air), draft (US) | 反义词: stillness (for current of air) | 词性: 名词

draught:/drɑːft/ | n. | (尤指啤酒的) 一杯，一口; (尤指药水的) 一剂，一服; (船的) 吃水;  | A single act of drinking or swallowing; a dose of liquid medicine; the depth of water needed for a ship to float. | 例句：He took a long draught of beer. 他喝了一大口啤酒。

例句：The doctor prescribed a draught of cough medicine. 医生开了一剂止咳药水。| 近义词：sip, gulp, dose, swig | 反义词: N/A | 词性: 名词

draft:/dræft/ | n. | 草稿; 汇票; (尤指房间、烟囱、炉子等供暖设备的) 抽力;  | A preliminary version of a piece of writing; a written order to pay a sum of money; a current of air. | 例句：I've finished the first draft of my novel. 我已经完成了我的小说的初稿。

例句：I felt a cold draft coming from under the door. 我感觉门底下有一股冷风。| 近义词：draught (UK), sketch, outline, current (of air) | 反义词: final version (for draft), stillness (for current of air) | 词性: 名词

draft:/dræft/ | vt. | 起草;  | To prepare a preliminary version of a document. | 例句：I need to draft a letter to the council. 我需要给议会起草一封信。

例句：She was drafted in to help with the project. 她被选派来协助这个项目。 | 近义词：compose, write, formulate | 反义词: finalize | 词性: 动词
