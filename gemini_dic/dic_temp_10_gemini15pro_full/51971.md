diacritics:/ˌdaɪəˈkrɪtɪks/| n.|变音符号|A diacritic is a glyph added to a letter, or basic glyph. The term derives from the Greek διακριτικός (diakritikós, "distinguishing"), from διακρίνω (diakrī́nō, "to distinguish"). Diacritics may appear above or below a letter, or in some other position such as within the letter or between two letters.|The word "résumé" uses two acute accents as diacritics.|近义词：accent marks, accentuation|反义词: (No direct antonym)|noun
