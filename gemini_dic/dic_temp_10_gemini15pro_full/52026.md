dialect:/ˈdaɪəlekt/ | n. | 方言，土语 | A particular form of a language which is peculiar to a specific region or social group. |  She spoke with a thick Southern dialect. 她说话带有浓重的南方口音。

dialect:/ˈdaɪəlekt/ | n. | 方言，土语 | A regional or social variety of a language distinguished by pronunciation, grammar, or vocabulary. | The dialect spoken in this remote village is almost unintelligible to outsiders. 这个偏远村庄的方言对外人来说几乎难以理解。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *dialecte (Modern French dialecte), from Latin dialectus "manner of speaking, conversation, dialogue, language of a particular region," from Greek dialektos "talk, conversation, manner of speaking, language, dialect," from dialegesthai "converse with each other," from dia- "across" + legein "to speak."
