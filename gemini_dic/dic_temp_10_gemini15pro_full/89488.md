honeymoon:/ˈhʌnimuːn/ /ˈhʌniˌmuːn/| n.|蜜月；蜜月期；短暂的和谐时期|A period of harmony immediately after marriage or a significant event.|They went to Hawaii for their honeymoon. 他们去夏威夷度蜜月。|synonyms: holiday, vacation, trip|antonyms:anniversary, work, routine|noun


honeymoon:/ˈhʌnimuːn/ /ˈhʌniˌmuːn/| v.|度蜜月|To spend one's honeymoon.|They honeymooned in Venice. 他们在威尼斯度蜜月。|synonyms:  vacation, celebrate|antonyms: work, separate|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hony moone*, literally "honey month," referring to the sweetness of the first month of marriage, or ironically, to its transience.  "Hony" refers to the sweetness of honey, and "moone" to the idea that happiness, like the moon, waxes and wanes.
