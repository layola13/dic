cormorant:/ˈkɔːrmərənt (UK), ˈkɔːrmərɑːnt (US)/| n.|鸬鹚|A large, dark-colored waterbird with a long neck, hooked bill, and webbed feet, known for its diving and fishing skills. It is often found near coasts and inland waters.|The cormorant plunged into the water and emerged with a fish in its beak. 鸬鹚一头扎进水里，嘴里叼着一条鱼浮出水面。

The cormorants were perched on the rocks, drying their wings. 鸬鹚栖息在岩石上，晾干翅膀。|近义词：shag, seabird|反义词: (No direct antonym for a specific animal)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *corp marin* (sea raven), from Latin *corvus marinus*.  The spelling was influenced by *orant*, a suffix used in bird names.
