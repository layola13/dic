fulsomely:/ˈfʊlsəmlɪ/| adv.|过分地，谄媚地，令人生厌地|Characterized by abundance, copiousness, or excess, often to the point of being offensive or insincere.  Originally meant "abundant, plentiful," but over time acquired negative connotations of insincerity and excessiveness, especially in praise or flattery.|He fulsomely praised the dictator, which made everyone uncomfortable. 他过分地赞扬了独裁者，这使得每个人都很不舒服。|近义词：excessively, lavishly, effusively, unctuously|反义词：moderately, sparingly, reservedly|词性:副词
词源: From Middle English *fulsom, from Old English *fūlsum (“foul, loathsome, disgusting; excessive, overmuch”), equivalent to foul +‎ -some.  The original sense of "offensive, disgusting" is now obsolete.  The current sense developed from the notion of excessive or insincere praise.
