anti-Malthusianism:/ˌænti mælˈθuːziənɪzəm/ | n. | 反马尔萨斯主义 |  The belief that Malthus's theory about population growth outstripping food supply is incorrect, often emphasizing the role of technological innovation in increasing food production or advocating for birth control as a means of population control. |  Anti-Malthusianism gained traction in the late 19th and early 20th centuries as agricultural advancements increased food production. 反马尔萨斯主义在19世纪末和20世纪初得到了发展，因为农业的进步增加了粮食产量。|  近义词: Cornucopianism | 反义词: Malthusianism | 词性: 名词
