huckstress:/ˈhʌkstres/| n.|女商贩；叫卖的女人|A woman who sells small, inexpensive items, often in a loud or aggressive manner.  A woman who is perceived as pushy or aggressive in her sales tactics.| She was a real huckstress, always trying to push her wares on unsuspecting tourists.| 近义词：vendor, hawker, peddler, saleswoman|反义词: buyer, customer|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "huckster" + "stress" (female suffix). "Huckster" comes from Middle Dutch "hokester" meaning "hawker."
