break ranks:/breɪk ræŋks/
verb
中文翻译：打破常规；公开反对；背叛
英文解析：To deviate from the norm or established order, especially within a group or organization; to disagree publicly with a group or leader, especially one that one is expected to support; to betray the trust or confidence of a group or leader.
例句：Several members of the party broke ranks and voted against the proposed legislation.  几个党员打破常规，投票反对拟议中的立法。

例句：He risked his career by breaking ranks and criticizing the CEO. 他冒着丢掉工作的风险打破常规，批评了首席执行官。
近义词：rebel, dissent, defect, disagree, oppose
反义词：conform, comply, obey, support, follow
词性：v.

break:/breɪk/
noun
中文翻译：破裂；休息；中断
英文解析：An instance of breaking something; a pause or interruption in an activity; a gap or opening.
例句：The break in the dam caused widespread flooding. 大坝的破裂造成了大范围的洪水。

例句：Let's take a break from work and have some coffee. 让我们从工作中休息一下，喝杯咖啡。
近义词：pause, interruption, gap, opening, fracture
反义词：continuation, connection, closure
词性：n.

ranks:/ræŋks/
noun
中文翻译：队伍；行列；等级
英文解析：A row or line of people or things; the members of a group or organization, especially the military; a position in a hierarchy.
例句：The soldiers stood in ranks awaiting inspection. 士兵们排成队等待检阅。

例句：He rose through the ranks to become a general. 他晋升了等级，成为了一名将军。
近义词：rows, lines, tiers, levels, hierarchy
反义词：disorder, chaos
词性：n.
