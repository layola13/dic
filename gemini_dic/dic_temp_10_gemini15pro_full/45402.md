cupidities:/kjuːˈpɪdɪtiz/| n. |贪婪，贪欲|An intense desire, especially for wealth or possessions.  Excessive or inordinate desire, particularly for material gain. It signifies a strong craving or lust for something, often with a negative connotation, implying greed or avarice.| His cupidities led him to embezzle millions from the company. 他的贪婪让他从公司盗用了数百万美元。|Synonyms: avarice, greed, rapacity, covetousness|Antonyms: generosity, contentment, altruism|noun
