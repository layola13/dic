domesticated:/dəˈmɛstɪkeɪtɪd/| adj.|驯化的，家养的|Adapted to living with humans and serving their purposes, especially of animals or plants.|例句：Domesticated animals like dogs and cats are common pets. 像狗和猫这样的家养动物是常见的宠物。|近义词：tame, trained, cultivated|反义词：wild, feral, untamed|词性:形容词
domesticate:/dəˈmɛstɪkeɪt/| vt.|驯化，家养|To tame (an animal) and keep it as a pet or for farm produce; to adapt (a plant) to cultivation.|例句：Humans domesticated wolves thousands of years ago, which led to the evolution of dogs.  人类在几千年前驯化了狼，这导致了狗的进化。|近义词：tame, train, cultivate|反义词：release, free|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin domesticatus, past participle of domesticare "to tame, domesticate," from Latin domesticus "belonging to the house," from domus "house."
