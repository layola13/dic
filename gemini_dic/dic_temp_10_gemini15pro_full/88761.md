hocus-pocused:/ˈhoʊkəs poʊkəst/| adj.|被欺骗的；被施魔法的；被迷惑的|Having been tricked, deceived, or subjected to meaningless rituals or words intended to create a false impression of magic or power.|The hocus-pocused investors lost their life savings. 被骗的投资者失去了他们的毕生积蓄。|The audience was completely hocus-pocused by the magician's performance. 观众完全被魔术师的表演迷惑了。|Synonyms: tricked, deceived, bamboozled, hoodwinked|Antonyms: enlightened, informed, aware|adjective

hocus-pocus:/ˈhoʊkəs poʊkəs/| n.|戏法；花招；胡言乱语|Meaningless words or rituals used to create a false impression of magic or power.|The salesman's promises were just a bunch of hocus-pocus.  推销员的承诺只是一堆花言巧语。|With a bit of hocus-pocus, he managed to convince them to invest. 他用一些花招设法说服他们投资。|Synonyms: trickery, deception, sleight of hand, mumbo jumbo|Antonyms: honesty, truth, reality|noun
hocus-pocus:/ˈhoʊkəs poʊkəs/| v.|欺骗；迷惑|To trick or deceive someone using meaningless rituals or words.|The con artist hocus-pocused the elderly woman out of her money. 骗子用花言巧语骗走了老妇人的钱。|Don't let yourself be hocus-pocused by their promises. 不要被他们的承诺所迷惑。|Synonyms: trick, deceive, bamboozle, hoodwink|Antonyms: enlighten, inform, educate|verb
