decadist:/ˈdɛkədɪst/| n.|十年主义者；十日论者|A person who advocates or believes in a system of decades (periods of ten years) as a basis for social or political organization, especially the French Revolutionary calendar.  Decadists often rejected traditional seven-day weeks and religious observances associated with them.|The decadist argued that the traditional calendar was outdated and needed to be reformed.|近义词:revolutionary, reformer|反义词:traditionalist, conservative|词性:noun
