enceladus:/ɛnˈsɛlədəs/| n. |土卫二|A moon of Saturn, the sixth-largest, known for its icy surface and the geysers that erupt from its south polar region, suggesting the presence of a subsurface ocean.|例句：Enceladus is considered a prime location for the search for extraterrestrial life. 土卫二被认为是寻找外星生命的主要地点。

例句：The Cassini spacecraft captured stunning images of Enceladus' plumes. 卡西尼号宇宙飞船拍摄到了土卫二羽流的惊人图像。|近义词：satellite, moon|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Enceladus, from Greek Ἐγκέλαδος (Enkélados), one of the Gigantes in Greek mythology, who was buried under Mount Etna.
