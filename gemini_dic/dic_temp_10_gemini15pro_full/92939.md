identitarian:/aɪˌdɛntɪˈtɛəriən/
| adj. n.|认同主义的；认同主义者|Relating to or supporting identitarianism, a political ideology that emphasizes the importance of preserving the cultural and ethnic identity of a particular group, often based on the belief that this identity is threatened by globalization, immigration, or multiculturalism.  (As a noun) A person who adheres to identitarianism.|例句：Identitarian movements are gaining traction in some parts of Europe. 认同主义运动在欧洲某些地区获得了关注。|近义词：nationalist, nativist, ethnocentrist|反义词:cosmopolitan, globalist, internationalist|词性:adjective, noun

identitarian:/aɪˌdɛntɪˈtɛəriən/
| n.|认同主义者|A person who adheres to identitarianism, a political ideology that emphasizes the importance of preserving the cultural and ethnic identity of a particular group. |例句：Some identitarians believe that immigration poses a threat to their cultural identity. 一些认同主义者认为移民对他们的文化认同构成威胁。|近义词：nationalist, nativist, ethnocentrist|反义词:cosmopolitan, globalist, internationalist|词性:noun


Etymology:  Derived from "identity" + "-arian," a suffix used to form nouns and adjectives referring to a person or ideology advocating a particular principle or system.  "Identity" itself comes from Late Latin "identitas," meaning "sameness."
