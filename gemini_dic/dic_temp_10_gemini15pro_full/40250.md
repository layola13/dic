continental-crust:/ˌkɒntɪˈnɛnt(ə)l ˈkrʌst/ | n. |大陆地壳|The outermost layer of the Earth's lithosphere that forms the continents.|The continental crust is thicker than the oceanic crust.|近义词: Earth's crust, sial|反义词: oceanic crust|词性: noun

Etymology:
continental: From Late Middle English *continental, from Old French continental, from Medieval Latin continentālis, from Latin continēns (present participle of continēre (“to hold together; to contain”)).
crust: From Middle English crust, cruste, from Old French cruste, from Latin crusta (“rind, shell, bark”).
