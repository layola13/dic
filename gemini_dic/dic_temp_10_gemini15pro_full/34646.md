clenung: /ˈkliːnɪŋ/ | n. | 清洁，清理，净化 |  The act of making something clean or the state of being clean. |  The spring cleaning took all weekend. 春季大扫除花了整个周末。|synonyms: cleansing, purifying, scrubbing, washing | antonyms: dirtying, soiling, polluting | noun


clenung: /ˈkliːnɪŋ/ | adj. |  清洁的，用于清洁的 |  Related to or used for cleaning. |  She bought some new cleaning supplies. 她买了一些新的清洁用品。| synonyms: cleansing, purifying | antonyms: dirtying, soiling | adjective


Etymology:  From Middle English *clēning, *clennyng,  verbal noun from *clēnen, clennen (“to cleanse, purify, make clean”), from Old English clǣnsian (“to cleanse, purify”), from Proto-Germanic *klainijaną (“to make clean, clear”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ḱley- (“to smear, stick”). Cognate with German klein (“clean”).
