impendence: /ɪmˈpɛndəns/ | n. | 迫近，即将发生 | The impendence of war cast a shadow over the land. |  The feeling of impendence filled him with dread. | 近义词：imminence, nearness, approach | 反义词：remoteness, distance | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From Latin *impendentia, from impendēns, present participle of impendēre (“to overhang, be near, impend”), from in- (“in, on, upon, at, towards”) + pendēre (“to hang, be suspended”).
