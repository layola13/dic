blench:/blɛntʃ/ /blɛnʧ/| v. | 畏缩，退缩；变白 | To shrink back or flinch in fear or disgust.  To turn pale.| The sight of the blood made him blench.  看到血他吓得畏缩了。| synonyms: flinch, recoil, cringe, blanch | antonyms: advance, approach, face, embrace | verb

blenches:/blɛntʃɪz/ /blɛnʧɪz/| v. | 畏缩，退缩；变白（第三人称单数） | Third person singular present tense form of "blench". | He blenches at the thought of public speaking. 一想到要公开演讲，他就畏缩了。| synonyms: flinches, recoils, cringes, blanches | antonyms: advances, approaches, faces, embraces | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *blencan "to deceive, cheat," from Proto-Germanic *blinkan "to blink, gleam," related to blink. The sense shifted to "start, flinch" in Middle English. The meaning "to turn pale" is due to confusion with blanch.
