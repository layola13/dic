flat-footedness:/ˌflætˈfʊtɪdnəs/ | n. | 平足；笨拙；措手不及 |The state of having flat feet; awkwardness or unpreparedness. | His flat-footedness made him trip easily. 他的平足使他很容易绊倒。|  The government was caught flat-footed by the sudden crisis. 政府对突如其来的危机措手不及。 | synonyms: clumsiness, awkwardness, unpreparedness | antonyms: agility, grace, preparedness | noun

flat-footed:/ˌflætˈfʊtɪd/ | adj. | 平足的；笨拙的；措手不及的 | Having flat feet; clumsy or unprepared. |  The flat-footed boy struggled to keep up with his friends. 平足的男孩很难跟上他的朋友。 |  The company was flat-footed in its response to the changing market.  这家公司对不断变化的市场反应迟钝。| synonyms: clumsy, awkward, unprepared | antonyms: agile, graceful, prepared | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From flat-footed + -ness.  "Flat-footed" itself comes from "flat foot," describing the condition where the arch of the foot collapses, causing the entire sole to touch the ground.  This physical characteristic was then metaphorically extended to describe clumsiness and lack of preparedness.
