enamellers:/ɪˈnæmələrz/
n.
珐琅匠；搪瓷工人；上釉工人
Workers who apply enamel, a usually glossy protective or decorative coating, to metal or other materials.
The enamellers carefully applied the intricate design to the copper plate. 珐琅匠小心地将复杂的图案涂在铜板上。
The factory employed skilled enamellers to finish the bathtubs. 这家工厂雇佣了熟练的珐琅匠来完成浴缸的上釉工作。
近义词:  enamel workers,  decorators (in some contexts)
反义词:  (No direct antonym, possibly consider roles involved in removing enamel)


词性: Noun (Plural)

词源: From the verb "enamel" + "-er" (agent suffix). "Enamel" comes from Old French "esmaillier," from "esmail" (enamel), which in turn is derived from Frankish "smalt" (enamel). Ultimately, it's related to the Proto-Germanic "*smeltaną" (to melt).
