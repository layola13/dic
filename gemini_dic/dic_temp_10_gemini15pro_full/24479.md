brittle:/ˈbrɪt(ə)l/
adjective
易碎的；脆弱的
Easily broken or cracked.
The old bone was brittle and snapped easily.  这块老骨头很脆，很容易折断。
brittle:/ˈbrɪt(ə)l/
adjective
(of a person or behaviour) appearing tough but easily hurt or offended.
(指人或行为)表面上很坚强，但内心脆弱易受伤害的
Beneath his brittle exterior, he was a sensitive man. 在他脆弱的外表下，他是一个敏感的人。
brittle:/ˈbrɪt(ə)l/
adjective
(of a sound) sharp and hard.
(指声音)尖锐而刺耳的
The brittle sound of ice cracking filled the air.  冰裂的脆响充满了空气。
近义词:fragile, frail, delicate, crisp, sharp
反义词:flexible, resilient, tough, strong
词性:adjective


brittle's:/ˈbrɪt(ə)lz/
noun
(usually used with a possessive pronoun) a brittle state or quality.
(通常与物主代词连用)易碎的状态或性质
The glass reached its brittle's at a high temperature. 玻璃在高温下达到了最易碎的状态。
近义词:fragility, brittleness
反义词:flexibility, resilience, toughness
词性:noun (used with possessive)


词源: From Old English *bryttian, from Proto-Germanic *breutaną (“to break”). Cognate with Dutch breken (“to break”), German brechen (“to break”). The suffix -le forms adjectives from verbs.
