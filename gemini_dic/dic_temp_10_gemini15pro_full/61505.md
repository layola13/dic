embroilments:/ɪmˈbrɔɪlmənts/
noun
纠纷，牵连，卷入
The state of being embroiled in a conflict or difficult situation.  A complicated or confusing situation that someone is involved in.
The country was drawn into the embroilments of a foreign war.  这个国家被卷入了一场外国战争的纠纷中。
The president's embroilments in the scandal eventually led to his resignation.  总统卷入丑闻最终导致他辞职。
近义词:conflicts, disputes, entanglements, involvements
反义词:agreements, harmonies, resolutions
词性:noun (plural)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *embroillier, from en- (“in”) + brooillier (“to mix, stir, confuse”), from broil (“mud, mire”), of uncertain origin.
