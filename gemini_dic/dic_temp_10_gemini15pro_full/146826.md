overimpressing:/ˌoʊvərɪmˈprɛsɪŋ/
verb
过度留下印象；过分打动
Trying too hard to impress someone, often with exaggerated or insincere behavior.
He overimpressed the interviewers with his elaborate answers, and they suspected he was being disingenuous. 他用精心准备的答案给面试官留下了过深的印象，他们怀疑他不够真诚。
She overimpressed her date by pretending to know about opera. 她假装了解歌剧，给约会对象留下了过分的印象。
近义词:overdoing it, exaggerating, being excessive
反义词:understating, downplaying
词性:verb (present participle)

overimpressed:/ˌoʊvərɪmˈprɛst/
adjective
被过度打动的；留下过深印象的
Having been excessively impressed; feeling overwhelmed by someone's attempts to impress.
I was slightly overimpressed by his flashy car and designer clothes. 我被他炫耀的汽车和名牌服装弄得有点不知所措。
The audience seemed overimpressed by the politician's promises. 观众似乎被政客的承诺过度打动了。
近义词:awed, overwhelmed, taken aback
反义词:unimpressed, skeptical, dubious
词性:adjective


词源:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "impressing" (present participle of "impress," from Latin "impressus," past participle of "imprimere" - to press into, stamp).  The prefix "over-" indicates an excess or going beyond a normal or desirable limit.
