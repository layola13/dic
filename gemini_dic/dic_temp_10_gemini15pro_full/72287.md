flotsam:/ˈflɒtsəm/ (UK), /ˈflɑːtsəm/ (US)| n. |漂浮物，废料；（社会）渣滓|Floating wreckage of a ship or its cargo; refuse.  Figuratively, people or things regarded as worthless. |The beach was littered with flotsam and jetsam after the storm.  风暴过后，海滩上到处都是漂浮的残骸和垃圾。|The city's flotsam and jetsam gathered in the park. 这座城市的流浪汉和无业游民聚集在公园里。|Synonyms: debris, wreckage, refuse, jetsam |Antonyms: treasure, valuables|noun

flotsams:/ˈflɒtsəmz/ (UK), /ˈflɑːtsəmz/ (US)| n. |漂浮物（flotsam 的复数形式，较少使用）|Plural form of flotsam, referring to multiple instances of floating wreckage or refuse.  Less commonly used than the singular form.|The currents carried the flotsams of several shipwrecks to the shore.  水流把几次沉船事故的漂浮物带到了岸边。| Rarely used, prefer "flotsam" for both singular and plural contexts in most cases.|Synonyms: debris, wreckage, refuse, jetsams (also uncommon)|Antonyms: treasures, valuables|noun
