goose-stepped:/ˈɡuːsˌstɛpt/ | v. | 正步走，以夸张的抬腿正步走 |  To march in a stiff-legged, straight-kneed fashion, reminiscent of the goose-stepping of Prussian soldiers.  This style of marching is often associated with authoritarian regimes. |  The soldiers goose-stepped across the parade ground.  士兵们在阅兵场上正步走。 | The dictator's troops goose-stepped through the capital, a display of military might. 独裁者的军队正步走过首都，展示了军事力量。 |  march, parade |  stroll, saunter | verb

goose-stepped:/ˈɡuːsˌstɛpt/ | adj. | 以正步走的，正步走的 |  Characterized by goose-stepping. | The goose-stepped march was a chilling reminder of the regime's power.  正步走令人不寒而栗地想起了该政权的权力。 |  The goose-stepped soldiers were a symbol of oppression.  正步走的士兵是压迫的象征。|  rigid, formal, militaristic |  relaxed, informal, casual | adjective
