half-sightedly: /ˌhɑːf ˈsaɪtɪdli/ /ˌhæf ˈsaɪtɪdli/ | adv. | 目光短浅地；缺乏远见地 | In a way that lacks foresight or long-term perspective; without full consideration of the potential consequences. | He half-sightedly pursued a course of action that ultimately proved disastrous. 他目光短浅地采取了一项最终被证明是灾难性的行动。|  shortsightedly, myopically, imprudently, unwisely | farsightedly, prudently, wisely | adverb

half-sighted: /ˌhɑːf ˈsaɪtɪd/ /ˌhæf ˈsaɪtɪd/ | adj. | 目光短浅的；缺乏远见的 | Lacking foresight or long-term perspective; without full consideration of the potential consequences. | The half-sighted policy failed to address the underlying issues.  这项目光短浅的政策未能解决根本问题。| shortsighted, myopic, imprudent, unwise | farsighted, prudent, wise | adjective

Etymology: "half" + "sighted."  "Half" indicates a partial or incomplete state, while "sighted" refers to the ability to see.  The combination suggests a limited ability to see, both literally (as in having impaired vision) and metaphorically (as in lacking foresight).
