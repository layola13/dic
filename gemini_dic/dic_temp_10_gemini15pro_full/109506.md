lernaeacea:/lɜːrniˈeɪsiə/ | n. | 锚头鳋科 |A family of copepod crustaceans that are parasitic on fish. They are characterized by their modified, anchor-like appendages used to attach to their host. |例句：Lernaeacea can cause significant damage to fish populations. 锚头鳋科会对鱼类种群造成重大损害。

|例句：The study focused on the life cycle of lernaeacea. 这项研究的重点是锚头鳋科的生命周期。|近义词：anchor worm (common name for some species), copepod (broader classification) |反义词: N/A |词性: noun


Etymology: From Modern Latin, derived from the genus *Lernaea*, named after the Lernaean Hydra of Greek mythology, a multi-headed serpent.
