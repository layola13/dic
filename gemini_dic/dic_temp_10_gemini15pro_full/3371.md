affrontedness:/əˈfrʌntɪdnəs/
|n.|受辱，被冒犯|The state of being affronted; a feeling of indignation or offense.  Being treated with disrespect or contempt.|Her affrontedness at his remark was clear. 她对他的话感到很生气。|近义词: indignation, offense, resentment, pique|反义词: pleasure, delight, satisfaction
|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From affronted + -ness.  Affronted comes from the Old French afronter, meaning "to strike on the forehead," from a- "to" + front "forehead."  The meaning shifted to "to insult" over time.  The suffix -ness forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
