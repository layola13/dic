out-guard:/ˈaʊtɡɑːrd/
n.|外卫; 前哨|A soldier or group of soldiers stationed outside a fortified place to defend it against attack.  A forward position or outpost.|例句：The out-guard was the first line of defense against the enemy. 外卫是抵御敌人的第一道防线。

例句：The general positioned an out-guard on the hilltop to watch for approaching troops. 将军在山顶设置了前哨以监视逼近的部队。|近义词：sentinel, picket, outpost, vanguard|反义词:rearguard|词性:名词

out-guard:/ˈaʊtɡɑːrd/
v.|担任外卫; 设置前哨|To act as an out-guard; to station as an out-guard.|例句：They out-guarded the camp during the night. 他们在夜间担任营地的外卫。

例句：The commander out-guarded the bridge to prevent the enemy from crossing. 指挥官在桥上设置了前哨以阻止敌人过桥。|近义词：guard, protect, defend|反义词:abandon|词性:动词
