diseducate:/dɪsˈɛdʒʊkeɪt/ |v. |误导；使失教；灌输有害思想|To teach someone wrong information or to prevent someone from receiving a proper education. |Some argue that certain television programs diseducate children, filling their minds with violence and consumerism.  一些人认为某些电视节目会误导儿童，使他们的思想充满暴力和消费主义。| The constant barrage of negative news can diseducate the public about the true complexities of global issues.  不断涌入的负面新闻会误导公众对全球问题真正复杂性的理解。| 近义词:mislead, misinform, indoctrinate|反义词:educate, enlighten, teach|词性:动词

Etymology:
dis- (Latin prefix) meaning "not," "opposite of," "away from" + educate (from Latin educare, meaning "to bring up," "to rear," "to educate")
