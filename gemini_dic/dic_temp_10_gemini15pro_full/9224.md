antipapist:/ˌæntiˈpeɪpɪst/ | n. adj. | 反教皇主义者；反教皇主义的 |  A person or idea opposed to the authority of the Pope or the doctrines of the Roman Catholic Church. Historically, the term carried strong negative connotations and was often used pejoratively. | The antipapist sentiment in England during the 16th century led to the persecution of Catholics. 16世纪英格兰的反教皇情绪导致了对天主教徒的迫害。 | Synonyms: anti-Catholic, anti-Romanist | Antonyms: papist, pro-Catholic | noun, adjective
