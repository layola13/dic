iconographs: /ˌaɪkɒˈnɒɡrɑːfs/ /ˌaɪkəˈnɑːɡræfs/ | n. |图像志，图像学；肖像，肖像画|A study or analysis of the subject matter and symbolism of works of art, especially with reference to their historical or cultural significance.  The iconography of the painting suggests a religious narrative. |例句：The iconographs of the Renaissance are rich in symbolism. 文艺复兴时期的图像学充满了象征意义。

例句：Researchers studied the iconographs of ancient Egyptian tombs to understand their beliefs about the afterlife.  研究人员研究了古埃及陵墓的图像，以了解他们对来世的信仰。|近义词：symbolism, imagery, iconology|反义词: N/A |词性: noun

iconograph: /ˌaɪˈkɒnəɡrɑːf/ /ˌaɪˈkɑːnəɡræf/ | n. |图像学家；肖像画家|A person who studies or creates iconography. The iconograph specializes in Byzantine art. |例句：The iconograph carefully documented the symbols found in medieval manuscripts.  这位图像学家仔细记录了中世纪手稿中发现的符号。
