misqualified:/ˌmɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪd/
adjective
不合格的，没有资格的
lacking the qualifications required for a particular job or activity.
He was misqualified for the position due to his lack of experience. 他由于缺乏经验而不符合这个职位的要求。
近义词: ineligible, unqualified, unsuitable
反义词: qualified, eligible, suitable
词性: adjective

词源:
mis- (prefix):  wrongly; badly.  From Old English *mis-, from Proto-Germanic *missa- "wrongly," cognate with Old High German missi-, Old Norse mis-, Gothic missō "mutually, reciprocally, wrongly."
qualified (adjective): officially recognized as being trained to perform a particular job; having passed the necessary exams, etc.  From Late Latin qualificatus, past participle of qualificare "to give a quality to, characterize," from Latin qualis "of what sort" + facere "to make."
