infelicitous:/ˌɪnfəˈlɪsɪtəs/| adj.|不幸的；不适当的；不恰当的|Not appropriate or suitable for the circumstances; unfortunate.  Derived from "felicitous," meaning "well-suited or appropriate," with the prefix "in-" indicating negation.|Her infelicitous remark about his weight loss surgery caused him great embarrassment. 她关于他减肥手术的不恰当言论让他非常尴尬。|Synonyms: inappropriate, unsuitable, unfortunate, awkward, clumsy, inept|Antonyms: appropriate, suitable, fortunate, apt, felicitous|adjective

infelicitous:/ˌɪnfəˈlɪsɪtəs/| adj.|不幸的；不适当的；不恰当的|Not appropriate or suitable for the circumstances; unfortunate.  Derived from Latin "felix" (lucky, happy)  -> "felicitas" (happiness, luck) -> Old French "felicite" -> English "felicity" -> "felicitous" (suitable, appropriate). The prefix "in-" negates the meaning.|His choice of words was infelicitous, considering the sensitive nature of the topic. 考虑到话题的敏感性，他的措辞很不恰当。|Synonyms: inappropriate, unsuitable, unfortunate, awkward, clumsy, inept, maladroit|Antonyms: appropriate, suitable, fortunate, apt, felicitous, fitting|adjective
