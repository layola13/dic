braggart: /ˈbræɡərt/ (US), /ˈbræɡɑːt/ (UK)| n.| 吹牛者，自夸者 |A person who boasts about achievements or possessions.| He's such a braggart, always going on about how much money he makes. 他真是个吹牛大王，总是炫耀自己赚了多少钱。|  例句：The braggart annoyed everyone with his constant boasting.  这个吹牛的人不停地吹嘘，惹恼了所有人。| 近义词：boaster, show-off, bragger| 反义词：humble person, modest person| 词性：名词

braggarts: /ˈbræɡərts/ (US), /ˈbræɡɑːts/ (UK)| n.| 吹牛者，自夸者（复数）|Plural form of braggart, referring to multiple people who boast.|  The braggarts were eventually ignored by everyone. 这些吹牛的人最终被所有人无视了。| 例句：Those braggarts are always competing to see who can tell the tallest tale.  那些吹牛大王总是比赛谁能讲出最夸张的故事。 | 近义词：boasters, show-offs, braggers| 反义词：humble people, modest people| 词性：名词

词源：From Middle English *braggen* "to boast," of uncertain origin, perhaps imitative.  The "-art" suffix is a pejorative ending, forming nouns denoting a person characterized by the root word (like "drunkard," "coward").
