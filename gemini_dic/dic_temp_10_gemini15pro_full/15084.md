baccalaureate: /ˌbækəˈlɔːriət/ /ˌbækəˈlɔːrɪət/ | n. | 学士学位；学士学位授予仪式 | A bachelor's degree; a ceremony at which bachelor's degrees are conferred. |She received her baccalaureate from Harvard University. 她从哈佛大学获得了学士学位。| synonym: bachelor's degree, undergraduate degree | antonym: master's degree, doctoral degree | noun

baccalaureates: /ˌbækəˈlɔːriəts/ /ˌbækəˈlɔːrɪəts/ | n. | 学士学位（复数）；学士学位授予仪式（复数） | Bachelor's degrees; ceremonies at which bachelor's degrees are conferred. | The university awarded over 500 baccalaureates this year. 今年这所大学授予了500多个学士学位。| synonym: bachelor's degrees, undergraduate degrees | antonym: master's degrees, doctoral degrees | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *baccalaureate, from Medieval Latin baccalaureatus "bachelor's degree," from baccalaureus "bachelor."  Ultimately from Late Latin baccalaria "baccalaureate, bachelor's degree"  (originally a laurel berry, given to poets, hence bacca lauri "laurel berry"), and -atus, suffix forming adjectives and nouns from verbs.
