dilapidation:/dɪˌlæpɪˈdeɪʃn/
n.|破败, 损坏, 荒废|The state of being dilapidated; disrepair or ruin.|The old house was in a state of dilapidation.  这栋老房子处于破败的状态。|例句：The dilapidation of the building was evident in the crumbling brickwork. 这栋建筑的破败在破碎的砖墙中显而易见。|近义词：disrepair, decay, deterioration, ruin|反义词：repair, restoration, renovation|词性：名词

dilapidated:/dɪˈlæpɪdeɪtɪd/
adj.|破旧的，失修的|Reduced to or fallen into partial ruin or decay, as from age, wear, or neglect.|The dilapidated barn was barely standing.  破旧的谷仓几乎站不住了。|例句：The dilapidated house was an eyesore in the neighborhood. 这栋破旧的房子是附近的一大碍眼之物。|近义词：ramshackle, run-down, broken-down, decrepit|反义词：well-maintained, renovated, restored|词性：形容词
