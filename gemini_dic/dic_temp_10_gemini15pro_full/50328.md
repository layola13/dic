deplorement:/dɪˈplɔːrmənt/
|n.|悲叹，痛惜，哀悼|The deplorement of the loss of life was evident in the community. 社区中对生命损失的悲痛显而易见。|
|Etymology: From Old French deplorer "to bewail, lament," from Latin deplorare "to weep bitterly, bewail, lament," from de- "fully" + plorare "to weep, cry, wail."|
|Synonyms: lamentation, mourning, grieving, sorrow, regret|
|Antonyms: celebration, rejoicing, joy, happiness|
|Word type: Noun|
