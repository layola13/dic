belabor:/bɪˈleɪbər/ /biˈleɪbər/| vt.|过分冗长地讨论；反复强调；抨击|To explain, worry about, or work at (something) more than is necessary.|The lawyer belabored the point about his client's innocence. 律师反复强调他的客户是无辜的。|Synonyms: overwork, overdo, dwell on, harp on|Antonyms:  ignore, neglect, overlook|verb

belabor:/bɪˈleɪbər/ /biˈleɪbər/| vt.|痛打；责骂|To attack verbally or physically.|The critic belabored the author for his poor writing style.  评论家严厉批评了作者糟糕的写作风格。|Synonyms: lambaste, criticize, censure|Antonyms: praise, commend, laud|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From be- + labor.  Be- is an intensifying prefix. Labor means "work."  Therefore, belabor means to overwork or work at something excessively.
