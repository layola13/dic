crannoge:/ˈkrænɒɡ/ (UK) /kræˈnɑːɡ/ (US)| n. |人工岛；湖上住所|A prehistoric dwelling of wood or stone built on piles in a lake, river, or marsh.|例句：Archaeologists discovered a well-preserved crannoge in the peat bog. 考古学家在泥炭沼泽中发现了一座保存完好的湖上住所。

例句：The crannoge provided a safe refuge for the ancient villagers. 这座人工岛为古代村民提供了一个安全的避难所。|近义词：lake dwelling, pile dwelling|反义词: None|词性: Noun

Etymology: From Irish *crannóg* or Scottish Gaelic *crannag*, from *crann* "tree" + *óg* "young".  Essentially meaning a "young tree".  Refers to the wooden piles used in construction.
