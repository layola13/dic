obdurate:/ˈɒbdjʊrət/ (UK) /ˈɑːbdərət/ (US)| adj.|顽固的，执拗的，冷酷无情的|stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action, despite attempts to persuade one to do so.|He remained obdurate, refusing to admit his mistake. 他仍然顽固不化，拒绝承认自己的错误。|synonyms: stubborn, obstinate, intransigent, inflexible, unyielding|antonyms: compliant, amenable, yielding, flexible|adjective
obdurately:/ˈɒbdjʊrətli/ (UK) /ˈɑːbdərətli/ (US)| adv.|顽固地，执拗地，冷酷无情地|in a stubborn and unyielding manner.|He obdurately refused to listen to any advice.  他顽固地拒绝听取任何建议。|synonyms: stubbornly, obstinately, intransigently|antonyms: compliantly, amenably, yieldingly|adverb
obduracy:/ˈɒbdjʊrəsi/ (UK) /ˈɑːbdərəsi/ (US)| n.|顽固，执拗，冷酷无情|stubborn or unyielding adherence to a course of action or a purpose.|Her obduracy in the face of criticism was admirable, if somewhat misguided.  面对批评，她的顽固令人钦佩，尽管有些误入歧途。|synonyms: stubbornness, obstinacy, intransigence, inflexibility|antonyms: compliance, amenability, yielding, flexibility|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *obdūrātus*, past participle of *obdūrāre* (“to harden, to make hard”), from *ob-* (“against, toward, in the way”) + *dūrāre* (“to harden”), from *dūrus* (“hard”).
