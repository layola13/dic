contraindicant:/ˌkɑːntrəˈɪndɪkənt/ | adj. n. |禁忌的，禁忌症|Something (such as a symptom or a condition) that makes a particular treatment or procedure inadvisable.|The patient's high blood pressure is a contraindicant for this medication.  这个病人的高血压是服用这种药物的禁忌症。|近义词:proscription,  inadvisable, unadvisable|反义词:indication, advisable|词性:adjective, noun

contraindication:/ˌkɑːntrəˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃn/ | n. |禁忌症|A factor that makes it inadvisable to prescribe a certain drug or carry out a certain procedure.|The doctor considered the patient's allergies before prescribing medication, checking for any contraindications. 医生在开药前考虑了病人的过敏情况，检查是否有任何禁忌症。|近义词:prohibition,  objection, caveat|反义词:indication|词性:noun


词源:
From Latin contra (“against”) + indicare (“to point out, indicate”).
