cataline: /ˈkætəlaɪn/ | n. | 阴谋家，叛徒 | A conspirator, especially one involved in a treacherous plot.  Derived from Lucius Sergius Catilina, a Roman senator who led a conspiracy against the Republic. |  He was denounced as a cataline and banished from the city. 他被谴责为阴谋家并被驱逐出城。 |  conspirator, plotter, traitor, rebel | loyalist, patriot | noun
