cornage:/ˈkɔːrnɪdʒ/| n.| 号角税；号角权；(旧时)对牛征收的税 |A type of feudal tax or due paid for the right to blow a horn, especially as a signal or announcement, or sometimes levied on horned beasts.|The lord of the manor collected cornage from tenants who owned cattle.| 近义词: tax, levy, duty | 反义词: exemption | 词性: noun
Etymology: From Old French *cornage*, from corne (“horn”) + -age. Ultimately from Latin cornu (“horn”).
