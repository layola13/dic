counterbalanced:/ˌkaʊntərˈbælənst/ | adj. v. | 平衡的，抵消的，相称的 | made equal or balanced by an opposing force or factor; having a weight that balances another or keeps something balanced | The study design counterbalanced the order of treatments to avoid bias.  这项研究设计平衡了治疗顺序，以避免偏差。 | The weights were carefully counterbalanced to ensure stability. 重量经过仔细平衡以确保稳定性。 |  synonyms: offset, compensate, neutralize, equalize | antonyms:  unbalanced, unequal, asymmetric, lopsided | adjective

counterbalance:/ˌkaʊntərˈbælənst/ | v. | 平衡，抵消 | to have an opposing force or influence on something and make it equal or balanced |  The advantages and disadvantages of the new system appear to counterbalance each other. 新系统的优缺点似乎相互抵消。 | The government's spending cuts were counterbalanced by tax increases. 政府的支出削减被增税所抵消。 | synonyms: offset, compensate, neutralize, equalize | antonyms:  exacerbate, aggravate, worsen, intensify | verb

counterbalance: /ˈkaʊntərˌbæləns/ | n. | 平衡，平衡力，平衡物 | a weight or force that balances another | The large weight acts as a counterbalance to the smaller one. 大重量用作小重量的平衡物。 | The political power of the king was a counterbalance to the power of the church. 国王的政治权力是对教会权力的制衡。 | synonyms:  offset, counterpoise, equalizer | antonyms: imbalance, instability | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + balance.  "Counter-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposite," and "balance" refers to a state of equilibrium.
