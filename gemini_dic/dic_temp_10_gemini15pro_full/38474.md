conchies:/ˈkɒnʃiz/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnʃiz/ (US)| n.|拒服兵役者（尤指出于宗教或道德原因）|A person who refuses to perform military service, especially on religious or moral grounds.  Conscientious objectors, often called "conchies" during World War I, faced social ostracism and even imprisonment.|During World War I, many conchies were imprisoned for their beliefs.|近义词：conscientious objectors, pacifists|反义词:soldiers, warmongers|词性:名词
