chirped:/tʃɜːrpt/ | v. | 吱喳叫，唧唧喳喳地说 |  To make a short, high-pitched sound like a bird.  | The bird chirped merrily in the tree. 小鸟在树上欢快地啁啾。| synonyms: twitter, peep, cheep | antonyms: croak, growl | verb

chirped:/tʃɜːrpt/ | adj. | 吱吱喳喳的，轻松愉快的（指说话的语气） |  Said in a cheerful, high-pitched voice. | She chirped a greeting to her friends. 她愉快地和朋友们打招呼。 | synonyms: cheerful, bright, merry | antonyms: gloomy, morose | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English chirpen, from Old English *ceorpian, of imitative origin.
