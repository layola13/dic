pretzel: /ˈprɛtsəl/ (US), /ˈprɛtsl/ (UK)| n. |椒盐脆饼干；一种脆饼干|A crisp, often salty biscuit, typically in the shape of a knot or loop, of German origin. |例句：I love to eat pretzels with mustard. 我喜欢吃蘸芥末的椒盐脆饼干。

例句：Freshly baked pretzels are a popular snack at the market.  新鲜出炉的椒盐卷饼是市场上很受欢迎的小吃。|近义词：cracker, biscuit, snack|反义词: None|词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle High German *brezel, from Old High German *brezzila, *brezitella, diminutive of *brezza "a type of baked good".  Ultimately from Late Latin *brachitellum, diminutive of Latin brachium "arm," referring to the folded arms shape.
