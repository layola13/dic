gargantua:/ɡɑːrˈɡæntʃuə/ | n. | 巨人; 庞然大物; 巨大的东西 | A giant; a person of immense size or appetite; something gigantic. |  He had a gargantuan appetite, capable of devouring an entire roast chicken in one sitting. 他胃口巨大，能一口气吞下一整只烤鸡。| synonyms: giant, behemoth, colossus, mammoth | antonyms: dwarf, midget, pygmy | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *Gargantua*, the name of the giant king in François Rabelais's satirical novels *Gargantua and Pantagruel* (1532-1564).  The name itself may be derived from Spanish *garganta* "throat," or from the Old French *gargantuiller* "to gargle."
