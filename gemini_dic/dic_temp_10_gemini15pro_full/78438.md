ETYMOLOGY->
"Get-up-and-go" originated in the late 19th century as an American colloquialism. It's a reduplication of "get up," emphasizing the action of rising and starting something with energy.  "Go" adds to the sense of forward momentum and drive.  The phrase became more widely used in the 20th century.
