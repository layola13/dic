gag:/ɡæɡ/| n. v.|塞口物，箝制言论；作呕，窒息|A piece of cloth put in or over someone's mouth to prevent them from speaking; something that suppresses free speech; to prevent someone from speaking freely; to feel like vomiting.|
The kidnappers put a gag in her mouth. 绑匪用塞口物堵住了她的嘴。

He was gagged and bound. 他被塞住嘴巴捆了起来。

The government tried to gag the press. 政府试图压制新闻界。

The smell gagged me. 这气味让我作呕。|近义词：muzzle, silence, suppress, choke|反义词:permit, allow, encourage|词性:名词,动词

PHRASE->
running gag:  a joke or funny situation that is repeated throughout a story, film, or performance.  一个贯穿故事、电影或表演的笑话或滑稽场景。
gag order: a judge's order that a case cannot be discussed in public.  法官下令禁止公开讨论某个案件。
gag reflex:  the involuntary contraction of the throat muscles caused by something touching the back of the mouth. 咽反射。
gag gift: a humorous or impractical gift, especially one given as a joke.  搞笑或不实用的礼物，尤指开玩笑送的礼物。
gag rule:  a rule that limits or prevents debate or discussion on a particular topic.  限制或阻止对特定主题进行辩论或讨论的规则。
