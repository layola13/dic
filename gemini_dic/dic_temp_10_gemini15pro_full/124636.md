mouthes:/ˈmaʊðz/ | n. | 嘴；河口 | Third-person singular present tense form of "mouth" (to speak, especially in an affected or pompous way). It refers to the action of forming words with the mouth, often implying an artificial or insincere manner of speaking. |  She mouthes the words, but I can't hear her over the music.  她用唇语说，但我听不见她在音乐声中的话。 | synonyms: utters, articulates, pronounces (more neutral), declaims, intones (more formal) | antonyms: whispers, mumbles, mutters (quieter), remains silent | verb

mouth:/maʊθ/ | n. | 嘴；河口 | The opening in the lower part of the human face, surrounded by the lips, through which food is taken in and from which speech and other sounds are emitted. Also, the opening or outlet of a river, cave, etc. | The baby put the toy in its mouth. 婴儿把玩具放进嘴里。The mouth of the river is wide. 河口很宽。 | synonyms:  opening, orifice, aperture (more formal), lips, jaws | antonyms: none (for the anatomical sense) | noun
