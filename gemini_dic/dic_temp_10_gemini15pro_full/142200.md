ornerinesses: /ˈɔːrnərinəsəz/ | n. | 固执，乖戾，暴躁 | The state of being ornery; stubbornness, cantankerousness. |  His ornerinesses made it difficult to work with him. 他的固执使得很难和他共事。| Synonyms: stubbornness, cantankerousness,  contrariness,  cussedness,  perversity | Antonyms: agreeableness,  amenability,  compliance,  docility,  tractability | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From ornery + -ness (suffix forming nouns of quality or state).  Ornery itself is of uncertain origin, possibly from ordinary in a derogatory sense, or from the dialectal ornery meaning "mean, contemptible," perhaps from the obsolete ornary meaning "ignoble," from Anglo-French orne "ignoble."
