cockled:/ˈkɒkəld/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkəld/ (US)| adj.|起皱的，扭曲的；(指贝壳)有凹凸的；感到暖和的，满足的|wrinkled or warped; having a surface with rounded projections or indentations (especially of shells); feeling warm and contented|The old map was cockled and torn. 这张旧地图又皱又破。|近义词：wrinkled, warped, ruffled, crinkled, uneven, contorted|反义词:smooth, flat, even|词性:形容词

cockled:/ˈkɒkəld/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkəld/ (US)| v.|使起皱，使翘曲；使温暖舒适|(transitive) to cause something to become wrinkled or warped; to make someone feel warm and comfortable|The heat cockled the pages of the book.  高温使书页卷曲了。|近义词：wrinkle, warp, crinkle, contort|反义词:smooth, flatten|词性:动词


词源: From Middle English *cokel, from Old English *coccel, diminutive of cocc "cockle (seashell)." The sense development is from the ridges on a cockleshell.
