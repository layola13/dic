blazonings: /ˈbleɪzənɪŋz/ | n. | 纹章描述; 装饰; 公开宣扬 | A formal description of a coat of arms, shield, or flag, often in heraldic language. Also, the act of displaying or proclaiming something widely. | The blazonings of the knights' shields were intricate and colorful. 骑士盾牌上的纹章图案复杂而多彩。|  The company's success was blazoned across the newspapers.  该公司的成功被各大报纸广泛报道。 | synonyms: descriptions, depictions, proclamations, declarations | antonyms: concealments, suppressions | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "blason" (shield, coat of arms, description of a coat of arms), from Frankish "*blaso" (a white spot, blaze), from Proto-Germanic "*blasaną" (to blow, shine). The original sense referred to the prominent marks on a shield used for identification.
