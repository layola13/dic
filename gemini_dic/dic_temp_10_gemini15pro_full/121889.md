miss-tarts:/ˈmɪsˌtɑːrts/ | n. | 小甜心（一种流行的美国糕点品牌）| A brand of pre-baked, convenient toaster pastries. They typically consist of a thin, rectangular crust filled with fruit preserves or other sweet fillings and topped with frosting. | Example: I had a box of miss-tarts for breakfast. 我早餐吃了一盒小甜心。| Example: My kids love miss-tarts as a snack. 我的孩子们喜欢吃小甜心作为零食。 | 近义词: pastries, Pop-Tarts (a similar brand) | 反义词: None (brand name, no direct opposite) | 词性: Noun


miss-tart:/ˈmɪsˌtɑːrt/ | n. |  (俚语，贬义) 傲慢无礼的年轻女子；轻佻女子| A slang term, often derogatory, referring to a young woman perceived as conceited, frivolous, or promiscuous.  | Example: He called her a miss-tart, which was completely inappropriate. 他叫她轻佻女子，这完全是不合适的。 | Example: The movie portrays the character as a manipulative miss-tart. 电影将这个角色描绘成一个工于心计的轻佻女子。 | 近义词: hussy, floozy, tart | 反义词: None (slang, no direct opposite) | 词性: Noun
