decentralising:/ˌdiːˈsɛntrəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 分散化，去中心化 | The process of distributing power or control away from a central authority. | The company is decentralising its operations to empower regional offices. 公司正在将其运营分散化，以赋予区域办事处更多权力。| synonyms: distributing, dispersing, devolving | antonyms: centralising, consolidating | verb

decentralise:/ˌdiːˈsɛntrəlaɪz/ | v. | 分散化，去中心化 | To distribute power or control away from a central authority. | The government decided to decentralise control of education. 政府决定将教育控制权下放。| synonyms: distribute, disperse, devolve | antonyms: centralise, consolidate | verb

decentralised:/ˌdiːˈsɛntrəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 分散的，去中心化的 | Having power or control distributed away from a central authority. |  The company has a decentralised management structure. 该公司拥有分散的管理结构。| synonyms: distributed, dispersed, diffused | antonyms: centralised, consolidated | adjective

decentralization:/ˌdiːˌsɛntrələˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 分散化，去中心化 | The act or process of decentralising. |  The decentralization of power led to greater regional autonomy. 权力下放导致更大的区域自治。| synonyms: distribution, dispersal, diffusion | antonyms: centralization, consolidation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- (meaning "away from, down, reverse, remove") + centralize + -ing/-ed/-ise/-ation (verb/adjective/verb/noun suffixes).  "Centralize" itself comes from "central" (from Latin "centralis," meaning "relating to the center")  + "-ize" (a verb-forming suffix).
