chasubled:/ˈtʃæsəbəld/ | adj. | 穿着祭披的；穿着法衣的 | Dressed in a chasuble.  A chasuble is the outermost liturgical vestment worn by clergy celebrating the Eucharist. | The priest, chasubled in white, began the Mass.  神父穿着白色的祭披，开始了弥撒。 | Synonyms: robed, vested | Antonyms: unrobed, disrobed | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *chasuble*, from Late Latin *casubula*, from Latin *casula* "hood, cloak," diminutive of *casa* "house, hut."  The word originally referred to a hooded outer garment.
