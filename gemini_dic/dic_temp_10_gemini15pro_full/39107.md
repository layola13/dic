confronte:/kənˈfrʌnt/| vt.|面对；对抗；遭遇|To meet face-to-face, especially in hostility or defiance; to present for acknowledgement, contradiction, or solution.|例句：I confronted him with the evidence of his guilt. 我用他犯罪的证据与他对质。|例句：The hikers were confronted by a bear on the trail.  徒步旅行者在路上遇到了一只熊。|近义词：face, encounter, challenge, oppose|反义词：avoid, evade, dodge|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French confronter, from Latin confrontāre ("to compare, bring face to face"), from com- ("together") + frōns ("forehead, front").  The original sense was to bring people face to face to compare their statements.
