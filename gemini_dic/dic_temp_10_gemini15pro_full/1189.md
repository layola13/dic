acclimatizing: /əˈklaɪməˌtaɪzɪŋ/
verb
适应气候; 适应环境
The process of becoming accustomed to a new climate or environment.
It took him a few weeks to acclimatize to the high altitude. = 他花了几个星期才适应了高海拔。
The plants are acclimatizing well to the greenhouse. = 这些植物很好地适应了温室环境。
近义词: adapting, adjusting, habituating
反义词: unaccustomed


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *acclimater*, from *a-* (to) + *climat* (climate), from Latin *climatis* (of climate), from Greek *klima* (region, slope, zone).  The suffix *-ize* forms verbs meaning "to make or become."

USAGE->

He acclimatizes to the cold weather every winter. = 他每年冬天都会适应寒冷的天气。

一般过去时:
The climbers acclimatized to the altitude before attempting the summit. = 登山者在尝试登顶之前先适应了海拔高度。
She acclimatized to the new time zone within a few days. = 她在几天内就适应了新的时区。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:
The explorers had acclimatized to the desert before their expedition began. = 探险家们在探险开始之前就已经适应了沙漠环境。
