/dɪtʃ/ | pron. | which | used to ask for a choice between two or more people or things | Example: Dich of these colors do you prefer? 你更喜欢这些颜色中的哪一种？ | Synonym: which | Antonym: none | Pronoun

/dɪtʃ/ |  determiner | which | used to ask for a choice between two or more people or things | Example: Dich book did you buy? 你买了哪本书？ | Synonym: which | Antonym: none | Determiner


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *which, from Old English hwilc (“which”), from Proto-Germanic *hwelīkaz (“which of two”), from Proto-Indo-European *kʷo- (“who, what”). Cognate with Scots whilk (“which”), West Frisian hokker (“which”), Dutch welke (“which”), German welcher (“which”), Danish hvilken (“which”), Swedish vilken (“which”), Icelandic hver (“who, which”).
