chloroform:/ˈklɔːrəfɔːrm/ (US) /ˈklɒrəfɔːm/ (UK)| n.|氯仿|A colorless, volatile, sweet-smelling liquid used as a solvent and formerly as a general anesthetic.|The doctor used chloroform to anesthetize the patient before the surgery. 医生在手术前用氯仿麻醉病人。|Synonyms: trichloromethane|Antonyms: None|noun

chloroforms:/ˈklɔːrəfɔːrmz/ (US) /ˈklɒrəfɔːmz/ (UK)| n.|氯仿（复数）|Plural form of chloroform, referring to multiple instances or types of chloroform.|The lab stocked various chloroforms for different experiments.  实验室储存了各种氯仿用于不同的实验。|Synonyms: trichloromethanes|Antonyms: None|noun

Etymology:  From chloro- +‎ formic, referring to its derivation from formic acid.
