namby-pambyish:/ˌnæmbi ˈpæmbiɪʃ/ | adj. | 幼稚的，优柔寡断的，婆婆妈妈的 | Lacking in character or substance; weak and sentimental.  Characterized by excessive sentimentality or weakness. |  His nambly-pambyish attitude made him unsuitable for a leadership role. 他婆婆妈妈的态度让他不适合担任领导角色。| sentimental, mawkish, wishy-washy, insipid | decisive, assertive, strong, robust | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From the name "Ambrose Philips" (1674-1749), an English poet whose pastoral poems were ridiculed by Henry Carey in a satirical ballad called "Namby Pamby," mocking Philips's childlike and sentimental style.  The name "Namby Pamby" was a childish alteration of "Ambrose Philips."
