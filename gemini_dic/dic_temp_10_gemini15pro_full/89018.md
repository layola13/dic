hollows:/ˈhɒləʊz/ (UK) /ˈhɑːloʊz/ (US)| n.|低洼地，山谷；洞穴；凹陷处|A low-lying area of land, typically surrounded by higher ground; a small valley.  Also, a hole or depression in a surface.|The deer sheltered in the hollows of the hills. 鹿在山谷中躲避。|synonyms: valleys, depressions, basins, craters, pits, cavities|antonyms: peaks, summits, ridges, mounds|noun

hollow:/ˈhɒləʊ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːloʊ/ (US)| adj.|空的；中空的；凹陷的；虚假的|Having an empty space inside; sunken; insincere.|The tree trunk was hollow.  树干是空的。|synonyms: empty, vacant, concave, sunken, false, insincere|antonyms: solid, full, convex, protruding, genuine, sincere|adjective

hollow:/ˈhɒləʊ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːloʊ/ (US)| vt.|挖空；使凹陷|To make a hole or depression in something.|The birds hollowed out a nest in the tree. 鸟儿在树上挖了一个窝。|synonyms: excavate, gouge, scoop|antonyms: fill, pack, stuff|verb
