ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ǣmtig, from Proto-Germanic *amtijaz (“leisure, free time”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂emh₂- (“to take one's rest”). Cognate with Old Norse autt (“empty”), Old Frisian emtich (“empty”), Dutch ledig (“empty, vacant, unoccupied”), Old High German āmati (“free time, leisure”), German Amt (“office, charge”), Gothic 𐌰𐌼𐍄𐍃 (amts, “office, service”).
