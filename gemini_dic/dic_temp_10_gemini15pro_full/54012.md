discruciate:/dɪˈskruːʃɪeɪt/ /dɪˈskruːsiˌeɪt/ | adj. | 交叉形的，十字形的 | Formed like a cross; intersecting in the form of a cross.  |  The discruciate ligaments in the knee provide stability.  膝盖中的十字韧带提供稳定性。 |  cruciform, cross-shaped, intersecting  | N/A | adjective

Etymology:
From Late Latin *discruciatus, past participle of discruciare (“to torment, torture, crucify”), from dis- (“apart, asunder”) + cruciare (“to crucify, torture”), from crux (“cross”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kreuk- (“to bend, curve”).
