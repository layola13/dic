heterocarpism:/ˌhɛtərəˈkɑːrpɪzəm/| n. |异果性；异形果实|The production of two or more distinctly different types of fruit by a single plant.|Example 1: Heterocarpism is an adaptive strategy observed in many plant species. 异果性是许多植物物种中观察到的一种适应性策略。

Example 2:  The dandelion exhibits heterocarpism, producing both heavier seeds for local dispersal and lighter seeds with parachutes for wind dispersal. 蒲公英表现出异果性，既产生较重的种子用于局部传播，也产生带有降落伞的较轻种子用于风传播。|近义词:anisocarpism, dimorphism (in a broader sense)|反义词:homocarpism (although not a perfect antonym)|词性:noun


Etymology:
From hetero- (Greek, "different") + -carp (Greek, "fruit") + -ism (suffix forming nouns of condition or state).
