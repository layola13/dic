besom:/ˈbiːzəm/| n.|扫帚；女巫的扫帚|A besom is a broom, especially one made of twigs tied to a handle.  It is also sometimes used figuratively to refer to a witch's broom.|She swept the floor with a besom. 她用扫帚扫地。|The witch flew on her besom. 女巫骑着她的扫帚飞走了。|synonyms: broom, brush|antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps vacuum cleaner in a modern context)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bēsom, from Proto-Germanic *bēsamą (“broom”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰeh₂- (“to strike, beat”). Cognate with Dutch bezem (“broom”), German Besen (“broom”), dialectal Swedish basa (“brushwood”).
