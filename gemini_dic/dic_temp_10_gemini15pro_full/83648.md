haemophiliacs:/ˌhiːməˈfɪliæks/ | n. | 血友病患者 | People with haemophilia, a medical condition in which the ability of the blood to clot is severely reduced, causing the sufferer to bleed severely from even a slight injury. | Haemophiliacs require careful monitoring and treatment to prevent excessive bleeding. 血友病患者需要仔细监测和治疗以防止过度出血。| synonyms: sufferers of haemophilia | antonyms: (No direct antonym, possibly individuals with normal blood clotting) | noun

haemophiliac:/ˌhiːməˈfɪliæk/ | n. | 血友病患者 | A person with haemophilia. | He is a haemophiliac and has to be careful to avoid injuries. 他是一名血友病患者，必须小心避免受伤。 | synonyms: sufferer of haemophilia | antonyms: (No direct antonym) | noun

haemophiliac:/ˌhiːməˈfɪliæk/ | adj. | 血友病的 | Relating to or affected with haemophilia. |  Haemophiliac patients are at risk of spontaneous bleeding. 血友病患者有自发出血的风险。 | synonyms:  related to haemophilia | antonyms: non-haemophiliac | adjective
