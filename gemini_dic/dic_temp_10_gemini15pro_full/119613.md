mid:/mɪd/| adj.|中间的，中部的；中央的|situated in the middle of something; central.|例句：The mid-1990s witnessed a surge in internet usage. 20世纪90年代中期见证了互联网使用量的激增。

例句：She sat mid-way between the two men. 她坐在两个男人中间。|近义词：middle, central, medial|反义词：outer, external, peripheral|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *midde, from Old English midde (“middle”), from Proto-Germanic *midjaz (“middle”), from Proto-Indo-European *médʰyos (“middle”). Cognate with Old Frisian midde, Middle Dutch midde, Old High German mitti, German mitte, Old Norse miðr. Related also to Old English mid (“with”).
