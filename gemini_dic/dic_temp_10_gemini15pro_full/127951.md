neoplasm: /ˈniːəˌplæzəm/ | n. |肿瘤；赘生物|An abnormal mass of tissue that forms when cells grow and divide more than they should or do not die when they should.  A neoplasm can be benign (not cancerous) or malignant (cancerous).|The doctor discovered a neoplasm on her lung. 医生在她的肺部发现了一个肿瘤。|The biopsy revealed the neoplasm was malignant. 活检显示肿瘤是恶性的。|Synonyms: tumor, growth, lesion, mass|Antonyms: healthy tissue|noun
