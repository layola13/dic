epidermic:/ˌɛpɪˈdɜːrmɪk/ | adj. | 表皮的；流行性的 |Of, relating to, or affecting the epidermis. Also, widespread, prevalent, affecting a large number of people at the same time, like an epidemic. |  The epidermic layer of skin protects the body from external elements. 表皮层保护身体免受外界因素的侵害。

The epidermic spread of the flu caused widespread panic. 流感的流行性传播引起了广泛的恐慌。 | 近义词：cutaneous, superficial, widespread, prevalent | 反义词：internal, deep-seated, localized | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *épidermique*, from Ancient Greek *ἐπιδερμικός (epidermikós)*, from *ἐπιδερμίς (epidermís)*  "outer skin," from *ἐπί (epí)* "upon" + *δέρμα (dérma)*  "skin."  The sense of "widespread" arose from the notion of something spreading over a surface like skin.
