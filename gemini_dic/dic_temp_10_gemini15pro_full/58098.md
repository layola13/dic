drop-outs:/ˈdrɑːpaʊts/ (US), /ˈdrɒpaʊts/ (UK)| n. |辍学者，退学者|A person who leaves school, college, or university before completing their studies.|Many drop-outs find it difficult to get well-paying jobs. 许多辍学者发现很难找到高薪工作。|Example: The program aims to reduce the number of high school drop-outs.  这个项目旨在减少高中辍学人数。

|Synonyms: non-graduates, leavers|Antonyms: graduates, completers|Noun


drop-out:/ˈdrɑːpaʊt/ (US), /ˈdrɒpaʊt/ (UK)| n. |辍学;退出;脱落|The act of leaving school, college, or university before completing their studies. Also, a failure of equipment or a loss of reception.|The drop-out rate has increased in recent years. 近年来辍学率有所上升。|Example:  The satellite experienced a signal drop-out. 卫星信号中断了。|Synonyms: withdrawal, attrition|Antonyms: continuation, persistence|Noun
