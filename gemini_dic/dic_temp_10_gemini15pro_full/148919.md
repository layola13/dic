overwinder:/ˌoʊvərˈwaɪndər/| n.|过卷，过绕，超绕（尤指磁带）|A device or mechanism that winds something, such as tape or film, beyond its normal capacity, resulting in damage or malfunction. It can also refer to the act of overwinding itself.  An overwinder on a tape recorder causes the tape to become tangled.|The tape recorder's overwinder broke, causing the tape to spill out.  录音机的过卷器坏了，导致磁带散落出来。|Synonyms: none (specific to the mechanical function)|Antonyms: none (specific to the mechanical function)|noun


overwind:/ˌoʊvərˈwaɪnd/| vt.|过卷，过绕，超绕（尤指发条、磁带等）|To wind something, such as a clock spring or tape, beyond its normal limit, often causing damage. |I overwound the clock and now it doesn't work. 我把钟表上得太紧了，现在它不工作了。 He overwound the film in his camera. 他把相机里的胶卷绕过头了。|Synonyms: overwind (itself is a synonym),  perhaps 'overload' in a general sense|Antonyms: underwind|verb
