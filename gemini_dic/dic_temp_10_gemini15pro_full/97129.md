infiltrate:/ˈɪnfɪltreɪt/| v. |渗透，潜入；（液体或气体）渗入|To enter or gain access to (an organization, place, etc.) surreptitiously and gradually, especially in order to acquire secret information or cause damage.|Enemy agents infiltrated the government. 敌方特工渗透进了政府。|The rain infiltrated the soil. 雨水渗入土壤。|synonyms: penetrate, permeate, infiltrate, pervade |antonyms: exfiltrate, withdraw, retreat|verb
infiltration:/ˌɪnfɪlˈtreɪʃn/| n. |渗透，潜入；渗透物|The act of infiltrating.|The infiltration of enemy agents into the government. 敌方特工对政府的渗透。|The infiltration of water into the basement. 水渗入地下室。|synonyms: penetration, permeation |antonyms: exfiltration, withdrawal|noun
infiltrator:/ˈɪnfɪltreɪtər/| n. |渗透者，潜入者|A person who infiltrates.|The infiltrator was caught and arrested. 渗透者被抓获并逮捕。|The infiltrators managed to steal important documents. 渗透者设法窃取了重要文件。|synonyms: spy, mole, agent, saboteur |antonyms: defender, protector|noun
