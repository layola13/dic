midst's:/mɪdstz/
|n.|中心，当中|The possessive form of "midst," indicating belonging or association with the middle of something.|He found himself in the midst's of a chaotic situation. 他发现自己身处混乱的局势之中。|Synonym: center's, middle's|Antonym: edge's, periphery's|Noun

词源:
From Middle English *middes, from Old English middes (“midst”), genitive of middel. Cognate with Old Frisian middels, Old Saxon middiles, Middle Dutch middels (Dutch middels), Old High German mittiles (German mittels), Old Norse miðils (Icelandic miðils). More at middle.  The 's is the possessive ending.
