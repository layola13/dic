overtruthfully: /ˌoʊvərˈtruːθfəli/
adv. |过于真实地，过于诚实地|  Excessively truthfully; more truthfully than is necessary or desirable.  It emphasizes an excessive degree of truthfulness, often implying a negative connotation such as being blunt, tactless, or unnecessarily detailed. | He described the situation overtruthfully, not sparing any of the gruesome details. 他过于真实地描述了情况，没有放过任何可怕的细节。|  例句：She overtruthfully recounted the embarrassing incident, making everyone uncomfortable. 她过于真实地叙述了那件尴尬的事情，让每个人都不舒服。 | 近义词：too honestly, excessively candidly, bluntly, frankly| 反义词：dishonestly, deceptively, evasively| 词性: 副词


词源:
"Over-" (prefix) + "truthfully" (adverb). "Over-" signifies "excessively" or "too much". "Truthfully" derives from "truth" (from Old English "trēowth," meaning "faithfulness, constancy") + "-ful" (suffix indicating "full of") + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).
