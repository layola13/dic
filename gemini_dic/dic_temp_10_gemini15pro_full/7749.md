anorexia: /ˌænəˈrɛksiə/ (US) /ˌænəˈrɛksiə/ (UK)| n. |厌食症；食欲缺乏|An eating disorder characterized by an abnormal fear of becoming obese, leading to excessive dieting and often dangerous weight loss.  It is a serious mental health condition.|She was diagnosed with anorexia after losing a significant amount of weight.|Synonyms: anorexia nervosa, inappetence |Antonyms: bulimia, hyperphagia|noun


anorexic: /ˌænəˈrɛksɪk/ (US) /ˌænəˈrɛksɪk/ (UK)| adj. n. |厌食的；厌食症患者|1.(adj.)  Suffering from or relating to anorexia. 2.(n.) A person who suffers from anorexia. |1. She appeared anorexic due to her extreme thinness.  2.  The clinic specialized in treating anorexics.|Synonyms: (adj.) anorectic, (n.)  anorexia nervosa sufferer |Antonyms: (adj.) healthy, (n.) glutton |adjective, noun
