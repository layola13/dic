lead-on:/ˌliːdˈɒn/ (British) /ˌliːdˈɑːn/ (American)| n.| 欺骗；引诱；假象|An act of deception or enticement, often suggesting false hope or promise. |He gave her a lead-on, making her think he was interested when he wasn't. 他给了她一个假象，让她以为他对她有意思，其实并没有。| 近义词：deception, enticement, allurement, tease| 反义词：honesty, sincerity| 词性：名词

lead-on:/ˌliːdˈɒn/ (British) /ˌliːdˈɑːn/ (American)| v. | 欺骗；引诱 |To deceive or entice someone, often with false hopes. |He led her on for months, pretending to be in love. 他欺骗了她好几个月，假装爱她。|近义词：deceive, mislead, entice, string along| 反义词：be honest, be upfront| 词性：动词
