conservatist:/kənˈsɜːrvətɪst/ | n. adj.| 保守主义者；保守的 | A person who is averse to change and holds traditional values.  Someone who is conservative in their beliefs, attitudes, or behavior. |  She's a social conservatist who believes in traditional family values. 她是一位相信传统家庭价值观的社会保守主义者。|  He's quite conservatist in his dress sense, always wearing a suit and tie. 他的穿着打扮很保守，总是西装革履。| 近义词: traditionalist, right-winger, diehard | 反义词: liberal, progressive, radical | 词性: 名词, 形容词

conservatism:/kənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/ | n. | 保守主义 |  A political or theological orientation advocating the preservation of existing institutions and traditional values. | Conservatism is a dominant force in many countries. 保守主义在许多国家都是一股主导力量。|  The party's platform reflected a growing conservatism. 该党的纲领反映了日益增长的保守主义。 | 近义词: traditionalism, orthodoxy | 反义词: liberalism, radicalism | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *conservativus, from Latin conservare (“to preserve”), from con- (“together”) + servare (“to keep”).
