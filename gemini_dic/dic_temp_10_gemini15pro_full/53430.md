disappendency: /ˌdɪsˌæpənˈdɛnsi/
n.
沮丧，失望
The state of being disappendent; dejection; loss of hope.
He felt a deep disappendency after failing the exam.  他考试不及格后感到非常沮丧。
近义词: despondency, dejection, discouragement, hopelessness
反义词: hope, optimism, cheerfulness
词性: 名词

disappend: /ˌdɪsəˈpɛnd/
v.
使失望
To disappoint; to fail to meet the expectations of.
His performance disappended his fans. 他的表现让他的粉丝们失望了。
近义词: disappoint, frustrate, let down
反义词: please, satisfy, encourage
词性: 动词

词源:  From dis- (expressing negation) + append (to attach, belong). The original meaning was "to detach or separate from," which evolved into the current sense of "to disappoint."
