anticlassicalness:/ˌæntiˈklæsɪkəlnɪs/| n.|反古典性，非古典性|The quality or state of being anticlassical; opposition to or deviation from classical principles or styles.  It describes something that breaks from the established norms of classicism.  |His paintings embody a spirit of anticlassicalness, rejecting traditional forms and embracing abstraction.|近义词：modernism, unconventionality, avant-gardism|反义词:classicism, traditionalism|词性:noun

Etymology:
anti- (prefix): against, opposite of (from Greek "anti")
classical (adjective): relating to ancient Greek or Roman literature, art, or culture, or to the qualities or style characteristic of them.  Also, of the first or highest quality, order, or rank. (from Latin "classicus")
-ness (suffix): forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state, quality, or condition.
