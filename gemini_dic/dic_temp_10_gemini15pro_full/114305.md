mainstreams:/ˈmeɪnstriːmz/ | n. | 主流，主流思想 | The ideas expressed in her novels are not in the mainstream of American thought. 她的小说中表达的思想并非美国思想的主流。 | Example:  Social media has become one of the mainstreams of communication. 社交媒体已经成为交流的主流之一。

Example:  Her music is finally entering the mainstream. 她的音乐终于进入了主流。 | Synonyms:  trend, norm, convention, orthodoxy | Antonyms:  alternative, unconventional, fringe, underground | Noun

Etymology: From main + stream.  "Main" meaning primary or principal and "stream" referring to the main current of a river.  It began being used figuratively in the mid-19th century to describe the prevailing current of opinion or influence.
