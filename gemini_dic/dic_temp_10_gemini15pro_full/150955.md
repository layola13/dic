paralyzing:/ˈpærəlaɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人麻痹的；使瘫痪的；使丧失作用的 | Causing a person or part of the body to become partly or wholly incapable of movement; bringing activity or progress to a standstill. | The paralyzing fear gripped him as he stood on the edge of the cliff.  令人麻痹的恐惧攫住了他，他站在悬崖边上。 |  crippling, debilitating, incapacitating, immobilizing |  invigorating, empowering, energizing | adjective

paralyze:/ˈpærəlaɪz/ | v. | 使麻痹；使瘫痪；使丧失作用 |  Cause (a person or part of the body) to become partly or wholly incapable of movement; bring activity or progress to a standstill. | The accident paralyzed him from the waist down. 事故导致他腰部以下瘫痪。 | disable, cripple, immobilize | enable, empower, mobilize | verb

词源: From Late Latin *paralysare, from Greek paralyein “disable, loosen, dissolve,” from para- “beside” + lyein “to loosen.”
