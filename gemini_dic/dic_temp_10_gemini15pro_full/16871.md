basilic:/bəˈzɪlɪk/ /ˈbæzəlɪk/| adj. n.|皇家药草,罗勒,教堂|A fragrant herb used in cooking, especially in Mediterranean cuisine. Also, a large and important church.|Basil is a key ingredient in pesto. 罗勒是香蒜酱的主要成分。|The basilic of Saint Peter is a magnificent structure. 圣彼得大教堂是一座宏伟的建筑。|近义词：(herb) mint, oregano, thyme (church) cathedral, temple|反义词:N/A|词性:adjective, noun

Etymology:
From Old French *basilique*, from Latin *basilica*, from Ancient Greek *βασιλική (basilikḗ, “royal [stoa]”)*, from *βασιλεύς (basileús, “king”)*.  The herb was so named because it was thought to be fit for a king. The church was so named because basilicas were originally royal audience chambers.
