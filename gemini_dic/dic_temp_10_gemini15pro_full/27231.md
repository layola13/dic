calmest:/ˈkɑːməst/ | adj. |最平静的，最沉着的|The superlative form of "calm," meaning experiencing the least amount of excitement, anxiety, or disturbance.|例句：She was the calmest person in the room during the emergency.  在紧急情况下，她是房间里最镇静的人。|近义词：most peaceful, most tranquil, most serene, most composed|反义词：most agitated, most frantic, most turbulent, most chaotic|词性：形容词

词源: From Middle English *calme, from Old French calme, from Late Latin cauma (“heat of the sun”), alteration of Latin caluma (“burning heat”), from Ancient Greek καῦμα (kaûma, “burning heat, fever”). The semantic development was from “midday heat, time of quiet” to “quiet, stillness,” then figuratively to “peace of mind.”
