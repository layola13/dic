acanthocephala:/əˌkænθəˈsɛfələ/
noun
棘头动物门
A phylum of parasitic worms characterized by a retractable proboscis armed with hooks, used to attach to the host's intestine.
Acanthocephala are commonly known as thorny-headed worms or spiny-headed worms due to the spiny proboscis. 棘头动物通常被称为棘头虫或刺头虫，因为它们具有带刺的吻。

Acanthocephala infect a wide range of vertebrates, including fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals. 棘头动物感染各种脊椎动物，包括鱼类、两栖动物、爬行动物、鸟类和哺乳动物。
