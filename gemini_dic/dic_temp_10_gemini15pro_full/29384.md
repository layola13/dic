casuistic:/ˌkæzjuˈɪstɪk/ | adj. | 诡辩的，决疑论的 | Relating to casuistry; using or based on subtle, often specious reasoning, especially concerning questions of morality. |  The lawyer's casuistic arguments failed to convince the jury. 律师诡辩的论点未能说服陪审团。| synonyms: sophistical, specious, equivocal | antonyms: straightforward, honest, candid | adjective

casuistry:/ˈkæzjuɪstri/ | n. | 决疑论，诡辩术 |  The art or practice of applying moral principles to particular cases; specious or excessively subtle reasoning intended to rationalize or excuse questionable behavior. | The professor's lecture on casuistry explored the ethics of lying. 教授关于决疑论的讲座探讨了说谎的伦理问题。| synonyms: sophistry, quibbling, hairsplitting | antonyms:  straightforwardness, honesty | noun
