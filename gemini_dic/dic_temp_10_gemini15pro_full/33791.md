circumclusion:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈkluːʒən/ | n. | 包围，环绕; 限制; 逻辑上的推论 |The act of enclosing on all sides; encirclement. The act of restricting or limiting. A conclusion reached by logical inference. | The circumclusion of the city by the enemy forces prevented any supplies from entering. 敌军对城市的包围阻止了任何物资进入。 | 近义词：encirclement, enclosure, confinement, restriction | 反义词： liberation, release, freedom | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From Late Latin circumclusio, from circumcludere "to enclose around," from circum "around" + claudere "to close."
