hardliner:/ˈhɑːrdˌlaɪnər/| n.|强硬派，强硬路线者|A person who advocates a firm or uncompromising stance, especially in politics.|The party's hardliners opposed any compromise with the opposition. 该党的强硬派反对与反对派妥协。|Synonyms: extremist, diehard, zealot|Antonyms: moderate, centrist, pragmatist|noun


hard-line:/ˈhɑːrdˈlaɪn/| adj.|强硬的，不妥协的|Advocating a firm or uncompromising stance, especially in politics.|The senator took a hard-line stance on immigration. 参议员对移民问题采取了强硬立场。|Synonyms: uncompromising, inflexible, rigid|Antonyms: moderate, flexible, lenient|adjective


Etymology:  "hard line" (adj.) is from 1950, American English political slang; "hard-liner" (n.) is attested by 1955. From "hard" + "line".
