cave-ins:/ˈkeɪvɪnz/| n.|坍塌，塌方|A sudden collapse or sinking of land, a roof, or other structure, typically creating a hole or cavity.|The heavy rains caused several cave-ins in the old mining tunnels.  暴雨导致老矿井隧道发生了几次塌方。|Synonyms: collapses, subsidence, sinkholes|Antonyms: constructions, buildups|noun

cave-in:/ˈkeɪvɪn/| n.|屈服，让步|The act of yielding or giving in to pressure or demands.|After weeks of protest, the government finally caved in and agreed to the workers' demands.  经过数周的抗议，政府最终屈服，同意了工人的要求。|Synonyms: surrender, concede, yield|Antonyms: resist, oppose, defy|noun
