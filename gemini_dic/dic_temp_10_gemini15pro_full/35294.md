clypeus:/ˈklɪpiəs/| n. |楯板;唇基|A shield-like plate or structure, especially the broad, anterior portion of the head of an insect or arachnid, situated below the front of the head and above the labrum (upper lip).  In other invertebrates, it refers to a similar plate-like structure. Also used in anatomy to describe certain bony structures in the human skull.|The clypeus of the beetle was unusually large. 甲虫的唇基异常大。|Synonym: shield, plate|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps consider structures that are *not* shield-like, e.g., cavity, depression)|noun


clypeus:/ˈklɪpiəs/| n. |（人体的）面颅|In human anatomy, refers to a part of the facial skeleton.|The clypeus forms part of the facial structure. 面颅形成了面部结构的一部分。|Synonym: facial bone|Antonym: (No direct antonym in this context)|noun
