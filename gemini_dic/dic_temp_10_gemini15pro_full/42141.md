cotyledon:/ˌkɒtɪˈliːdən/ (UK) /ˌkɑːtɪˈliːdən/ (US)| n. |子叶；胚叶|The first leaf or one of the first pair or whorl of leaves developed by the embryo of a seed plant, seed leaf, or seminal leaf.  It is a significant part of the embryo within the seed of a plant, and it is defined as "the embryonic leaf in seed-bearing plants, one or more of which are the first leaves to appear from a germinating seed."|The bean seed has two cotyledons.  豆类种子有两片子叶。

The cotyledons provide nutrients to the seedling until it can develop true leaves and begin photosynthesis. 子叶为幼苗提供营养，直到它长出真叶并开始光合作用。|Synonym: seed leaf, plumule|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific plant part)|Noun
