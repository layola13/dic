estimate:/ˈɛstɪmɪt/ (US), /ˈɛstɪmeɪt/ (UK)| n. vt.|估计; 估价; 评估|An approximate calculation or judgment of the value, number, quantity, or extent of something. |例句：The builder's estimate for the repairs was $2,000.  建筑商对维修费的估价是2000美元。

例句：My estimate of the crowd was around 5,000 people. 我估计人群大约有5000人。|近义词：guess, approximation, calculation, appraisal, valuation|反义词:precise calculation, actual figure|词性:noun, verb


estimate:/ˈɛstɪmɪt/ (US), /ˈɛstɪmeɪt/ (UK)| vt.|估计; 估价; 评估|To form an approximate judgment or opinion regarding the worth, amount, size, weight, etc., of; calculate approximately. |例句：We estimated the cost of the project to be $1 million. 我们估计该项目的成本为100万美元。

例句：It's difficult to estimate the time it will take.  很难估计需要多长时间。|近义词：assess, evaluate, gauge, judge, reckon|反义词:misjudge, underestimate, overestimate|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *estimacion*, from Old French *estimacion*  and directly from Latin *aestimatio*, from *aestimat-* (past participle stem of *aestimare* “to value, appraise”), from *aes* “bronze, copper, money” + *-timare* (frequentative suffix).
