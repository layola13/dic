forgerer:/ˈfɔːrdʒərər/ (US) /ˈfɔːdʒərə(r)/ (UK)| n.|伪造者；造假者|A person who makes a copy of something in order to deceive.|The forgerer was eventually caught and sentenced to prison.  伪造者最终被抓获并判处监禁。|Synonyms: counterfeiter, faker, imposter, imitator|Antonyms: authenticator, verifier|Noun

forgery:/ˈfɔːrdʒəri/ (US) /ˈfɔːdʒəri/ (UK)| n.|伪造；伪造品|The action of forging or producing a copy of a document, signature, banknote, or work of art with the intention to deceive.|The painting was discovered to be a forgery. 这幅画被发现是赝品。|Synonyms: counterfeit, fake, imitation, fraud|Antonyms: original, genuine|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *forgier, from forger "to forge," from Latin fabrica "workshop," from faber "workman, craftsman."
