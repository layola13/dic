calibration:/ˌkælɪˈbreɪʃn/ | n. | 校准，标定，刻度 |  The act of checking or adjusting (by comparison with a standard) the accuracy of a measuring instrument. |  The laboratory carries out regular calibrations of its equipment.  实验室定期对其设备进行校准。 | standardization, adjustment |  inaccuracy | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *calibratus, past participle of *calibrare “to measure with a calibra,”  from Latin calibra “a carpenter’s rule.”
