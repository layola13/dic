commandingness:/kəˈmɑːndɪŋnɪs/| n.|威严， commandingness |The general's commandingness intimidated the new recruits. 将军的威严吓到了新兵。|近义词：authority, dominance, imperiousness, ascendancy|反义词：submissiveness, meekness, humility|noun

Etymology:
From commanding + -ness.  "Commanding" comes from the Old French "commander" and ultimately from the Latin "commandare" (to entrust, enjoin, order).  The suffix "-ness" is used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
