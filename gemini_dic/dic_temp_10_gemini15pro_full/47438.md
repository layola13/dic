deafen:/ˈdɛfən/ (US), /ˈdɛfn/ (UK)| v. |使聋，使听不见，震耳欲聋|To make someone temporarily or permanently unable to hear, or to make a loud noise that is unpleasant or overwhelming.|The explosion deafened me for a few minutes. 爆炸声让我几分钟都听不见。|Synonyms: stun, drown out, blast|Antonyms:  soften, quiet, muffle|Verb

deafening:/ˈdɛfənɪŋ/ (US), /ˈdɛfnɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |震耳欲聋的|Loud enough to cause temporary or permanent hearing loss; extremely loud.|The deafening roar of the jet engine made it impossible to talk.  喷气发动机的巨大轰鸣声使人无法交谈。|Synonyms: earsplitting, thunderous, piercing|Antonyms: quiet, soft, muted|Adjective
