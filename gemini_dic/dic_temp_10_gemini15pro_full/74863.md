fres:/frɛʃ/ | adj. | 新鲜的；清新的；淡水的；无经验的 |  New, not stale or decayed; recently made or obtained; not previously known or experienced; (of water) not salty. | 例句：The air was fresh and cool. 空气清新凉爽。| 近义词：new, recent, clean, pure, inexperienced, raw | 反义词：stale, old, polluted, experienced | 词性:形容词

fres:/frɛʃ/ | n. |  新生；涌入；泛滥 | An influx of fresh water into a body of salt water. (Often used in plural form "freshes") | 例句：The spring freshes flooded the riverbanks. 春天的洪水淹没了河岸。 | 近义词：flood, inundation, surge | 反义词：drought, ebb | 词性:名词


词源:
From Middle English *fressh, *fresh, from Old French fresche, freis, fraische, from Frankish *frisc (compare Middle Dutch versch, Old High German frisc), from Proto-Germanic *friskaz. Cognate with Old English fersc (“fresh”). Related to frisky.
