nonspiritedly:/ˌnɒnˈspɪrɪtɪdli/ /ˌnɑːnˈspɪrɪtɪdli/
| adv. |无精打采地，缺乏热情地 |Without spirit or enthusiasm; listlessly. |She played the piano nonspiritedly, her mind clearly elsewhere. 她无精打采地弹着钢琴，心思显然在别处。|Synonyms: listlessly, apathetically, halfheartedly, lackadaisically |Antonyms: enthusiastically, energetically, spiritedly |adverb

Etymology:
From non- + spirited + -ly.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Spirited" comes from the noun "spirit," ultimately from Latin "spiritus" (breath, courage, vigor).  The suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective "spirited" into an adverb.
