confronted:/kənˈfrʌntɪd/| v.| 面对，遭遇，对抗| to face, meet, or deal with a difficult situation or person|  例句：She was confronted with a difficult decision. 她面临着一个艰难的决定。| 近义词：faced, encountered, challenged, opposed| 反义词：avoided, evaded, ignored| 词性: 动词

confronted:/kənˈfrʌntɪd/| adj.(rare, usually passive)|  被…面对的，被…质问的|  brought face to face with| 例句：He was confronted by evidence of his guilt.  他被出示了证明他有罪的证据。| 近义词：challenged, faced| 反义词：unaware, oblivious| 词性: 形容词

PHRASE->
be confronted with = 面临
confront someone with something =  用某事与某人对质

confront the truth =  面对事实


词源：From Old French confronter "to compare, bring together," from Latin  con- "together" +  frons "forehead, front."  The original meaning was to bring people face to face, literally forehead to forehead.
