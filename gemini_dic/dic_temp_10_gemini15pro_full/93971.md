imp:/ɪmp/ | vt. |嫁接，移植；（尤指）在（树的切口处）接入嫩枝 | To graft or insert (especially a shoot into a slit on a tree).  | The gardener carefully imped the new branch onto the apple tree. 园丁小心翼翼地把新枝嫁接到苹果树上。 | synonyms: graft, insert | antonyms: detach, remove  | verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old English *impa, from Proto-Germanic *umbon-, "sprout, shoot," related to Old Norse imbi, "sprout."  The sense of "mischievous child" developed later, likely influenced by the idea of a small, active being like a demon or sprite.
