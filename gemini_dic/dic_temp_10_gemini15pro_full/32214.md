chicano:/tʃɪˈkɑːnoʊ/ (US), /tʃɪˈkɑːnəʊ/ (UK)| n. adj.| 墨西哥裔美国人；奇卡诺文化|A person of Mexican descent born or living in the United States, especially in the Southwest.  Often used to assert a distinct cultural identity.  Can also be used as an adjective to describe this culture. |例句：Many Chicanos are proud of their heritage. 很多奇卡诺人以他们的传统为豪。

|例句：Chicano art often reflects the struggles and triumphs of the community. 奇卡诺艺术通常反映了社区的斗争和胜利。
|近义词：Mexican American, Latino/a/x, Hispanic|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly Anglo-American depending on context)|词性: noun, adjective

chicana:/tʃɪˈkɑːnə/ (US), /tʃɪˈkɑːnə/ (UK)| n. adj.| 墨西哥裔美国女性；奇卡诺女性文化|A female person of Mexican descent born or living in the United States, especially in the Southwest. Often used to assert a distinct cultural identity. Can also be used as an adjective to describe aspects related to Chicana women or culture. |例句：The Chicana writer Sandra Cisneros is known for her powerful stories. 奇卡诺作家桑德拉·西斯内罗斯以其充满力量的故事而闻名。

|例句：Chicana feminism explores the unique experiences of Mexican American women. 奇卡诺女性主义探讨了墨西哥裔美国女性的独特经历。
|近义词：Mexican American woman, Latina, Hispanic woman|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性: noun, adjective
