ingroove:/ɪnˈɡruːv/
verb
开槽；形成槽；使固定；刻痕
to establish a habit or routine
例句：The athletes are ingrooving their routines for the upcoming competition.  运动员们正在为即将到来的比赛固定他们的常规。

例句：Repetitive practice helps ingroove new skills. 反复练习有助于巩固新技能。
近义词：establish, settle, fix, root, embed, implant, instill
反义词：disrupt, unsettle, uproot
词性：verb


ingroove:/ɪnˈɡruːv/
noun
槽；纹；常规；习惯
a habit or routine
例句：He's in a real ingroove with his writing at the moment. 他目前写作状态非常好，很有规律。

例句：Getting into an ingroove can boost productivity. 养成习惯可以提高效率。
近义词：routine, habit, groove, rut
反义词：disruption, change
词性：noun
