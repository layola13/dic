intemperancy:/ɪnˈtɛmpərənsi/ | n. | 放纵；无节制；过度 | Lack of moderation or restraint, especially in the consumption of alcohol; excessive indulgence. | His intemperancy ultimately led to his downfall. 他的放纵最终导致了他的垮台。|  Synonyms: excess, immoderation, overindulgence, dissipation, intemperateness | Antonyms: moderation, temperance, abstinence, self-control | noun

intemperate:/ɪnˈtɛmpərɪt/ | adj. | 无节制的；放纵的；过度的 | Having or showing a lack of self-control; immoderate. | He was known for his intemperate outbursts of anger.  他以无节制的愤怒爆发而闻名。| Synonyms: excessive, immoderate, unrestrained, uncontrolled, inordinate | Antonyms: moderate, temperate, restrained, controlled, reasonable | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin intemperantia, from Latin in- "not" + temperantia "moderation".
