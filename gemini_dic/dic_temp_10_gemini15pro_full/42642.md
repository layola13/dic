countersignatures:/ˈkaʊntərˌsɪɡnətʃərz/
| n. |副署，连署，会签|A signature added to a document already signed by another person, especially to confirm its authenticity.  A second signature on a document that has already been signed by another person, often required for official or legal purposes.|
The check required two countersignatures to be valid. 这张支票需要两份副署才有效。
The treaty needed countersignatures from both presidents to become law. 该条约需要两位总统的会签才能成为法律。

|近义词：co-signatures, endorsements|
|反义词：N/A|
|词性：noun|

词源: From counter- + signature.  "Counter-"  means "opposite" or "in response to," and "signature" is the act of signing.  So, a countersignature is a signature added in addition to or in response to another signature.
