anagrammatism:/ˌænəˈɡræməˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 字母易位症; 拼写错乱症 | A psychological condition characterized by the transposition of letters or syllables in writing or speaking. |  He suffered from anagrammatism after the stroke, often mixing up letters in words. 他在中风后患上了字母易位症，经常混淆单词中的字母。 |  An example of anagrammatism is writing "lvoe" instead of "love". 字母易位症的一个例子是把“love”写成“lvoe”。 | metathesis, spoonerism | - | noun
