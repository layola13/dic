mystique:/mɪˈstiːk/ | n. | 神秘感，神秘气氛；奥秘 | An aura of mystery or fascination surrounding something or someone. |  The artist cultivated a mystique of rebelliousness.  这位艺术家刻意营造一种叛逆的神秘感。 | synonyms: aura, charisma, allure, magic, enigma | antonyms: clarity, transparency | noun


mystiques:/mɪˈstiːks/ | n. | 神秘事物；奥秘（mystique 的复数）| Plural form of mystique, referring to multiple auras of mystery or fascination. | The mystiques surrounding ancient civilizations draw many to archaeology. 古代文明的神秘事物吸引了许多人从事考古学。 | synonyms: auras, charismas, allures, enigmas | antonyms: clarities, transparencies | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mystique*, from Late Latin *mysticus* "mystic", from Greek *mystikos* "secret, connected with the mysteries", from *mystes* "initiate".
