imposers:/ɪmˈpəʊzəz/ (British) /ɪmˈpoʊzərz/ (American)| n. |强加者，施加者|People who impose something (a belief, rule, tax, etc.) on others.  Those who exert their will or authority over others, often in a domineering or unwelcome way. |The new tax laws were seen as the work of imposers who didn't understand the plight of the working class. 新的税法被认为是那些不了解工人阶级困境的强加者的杰作。

The imposers of the curfew were met with resistance from the community. 实施宵禁的强加者遭到了社区的抵制。


近义词：dictators, tyrants, enforcers, authoritarians |反义词:  liberators, emancipators |词性: noun (plural)


词源:
From the verb "impose," from Old French "imposer" (to impose, put on), from Late Latin "imponere" (to place upon, impose), from Latin "in-" (in, on) + "ponere" (to place, put).
