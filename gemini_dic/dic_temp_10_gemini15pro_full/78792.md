gillyflower:/ˈdʒɪliˌflaʊər/| n.|康乃馨，麝香石竹|A clove pink, having grayish-green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.|She wore a corsage of pink gillyflowers. 她戴着粉色康乃馨胸花。|Synonyms: carnation, clove pink|Antonyms: N/A|noun



ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *gilofre, from Old French girofle, from Medieval Latin caryophyllon, from Latin caryophyllum, from Ancient Greek καρυόφυλλον (karuóphullon, “clove tree; clove”), from κάρυον (káruon, “nut”) + φύλλον (phúllon, “leaf”).  The original meaning was "clove," later extended to other fragrant flowers.
