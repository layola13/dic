abrenounce: /əˈbreˈnaʊns/
verb
放弃，宣布放弃，断绝关系
to formally reject or disavow a belief, cause, or claim.
He abrenounced his claim to the throne. 他放弃了对王位的要求。

She abrenounced her former lifestyle and joined a convent. 她放弃了以前的生活方式，加入了一个修道院。

近义词: renounce, relinquish, forswear, abandon, repudiate
反义词: affirm, embrace, uphold, maintain
词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *abrenuntiare, from Latin ab- + renunciare "to renounce," from re- "back" + nuntiare "to announce."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He abrenounces all worldly possessions. = 他放弃所有世俗财产。
She abrenounces her right to an inheritance. = 她放弃了继承权。

一般过去时:
He abrenounced his allegiance to the king. = 他宣布放弃效忠国王。
She abrenounced her faith in the political system. = 她放弃了对政治制度的信仰。

现在完成时:
He has abrenounced his former life of crime. = 他已经放弃了以前的犯罪生活。
She has abrenounced her membership in the organization. = 她已经放弃了在该组织的成员资格。

过去完成时:
He had abrenounced his claim to the property before the trial. = 在审判前，他已经放弃了对该财产的所有权。
She had abrenounced her friendship with him after the betrayal. = 在背叛之后，她断绝了与他的友谊。

将来时:
He will abrenounce his right to appeal the decision. = 他将放弃对该决定的上诉权。
She will abrenounce her involvement in the project. = 她将宣布退出该项目。


虚拟语气:
If he were to abrenounce his claim, the case would be closed. = 如果他放弃索赔，案件就会结案。
I wish he would abrenounce his bad habits. = 我希望他能改掉他的坏习惯。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

She will not abrenounce her commitment to the cause. = 她不会放弃对这项事业的承诺。
