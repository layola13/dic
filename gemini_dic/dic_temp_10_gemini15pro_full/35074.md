closings:/ˈkloʊzɪŋz/
n.
关闭，结束，终结
the act of closing something, or the way something closes
The closings of several factories led to job losses in the area.  几家工厂的关闭导致该地区的工作岗位流失。

n.
（尤指房屋买卖的）最后手续，成交
the final stages of a business deal, especially one involving the sale of a house
The lawyer is handling the closings on two properties this week.  律师这周正在处理两处房产的成交手续。

n.
（广播或电视节目的）结束语，结尾
the final words or music of a broadcast
The show's closings were always very emotional.  这个节目的结尾总是很感人。

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "close," which comes from Old French "clos" (closed), ultimately from Latin "claudere" (to shut, close).  来自动词“close”，它来源于古法语“clos”（关闭的），最终来自拉丁语“claudere”（关闭）。


近义词:
conclusion, ending, termination, finish, completion
反义词:
beginnings, openings, commencements
词性:
Noun
