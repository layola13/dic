discursivity:/dɪsˈkɜːsɪvɪti/
noun
散漫性；离题性；论述性
The quality of being discursive; rambling from one topic to another.
Her essay suffered from excessive discursivity, making it difficult to follow her main argument.  她的文章过于散漫，难以理解其主要论点。
The discursivity of the novel reflects the stream of consciousness style. 这部小说的离题性反映了意识流的风格。
近义词: rambling, digressiveness, prolixity
反义词: conciseness, succinctness, brevity
词性: noun

discursive:/dɪsˈkɜːsɪv/
adjective
散漫的；离题的；论述的
Passing aimlessly from one subject to another; digressive. Proceeding by reasoning or argument rather than intuition.
The professor's lecture was discursive, covering a wide range of topics. 教授的讲座很散漫，涵盖了广泛的主题。
His discursive style made it hard to pinpoint his main thesis.  他散漫的风格使得难以确定他的主要论点。
近义词: rambling, digressive, prolix
反义词: concise, succinct, brief
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *discursīvus, from Latin discursus (“a running about”), past participle of discurrere (“to run about, run to and fro, digress”), from dis- (“apart, in different directions”) + currere (“to run”).
