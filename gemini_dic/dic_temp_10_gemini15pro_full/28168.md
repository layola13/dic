caponeus: /kəˈpōnɪəs/ /kæˈpoʊniəs/ | n. | 卡蓬纽斯; 傲慢自大的人 |  In Greek mythology, Capaneus was one of the Seven against Thebes, known for his hubris and defiance of the gods. He was struck down by Zeus for boasting that even Zeus could not stop him from scaling the walls of Thebes.  The word "caponeus" can also be used more generally to refer to someone who is arrogant and excessively proud, similar to a braggart. | Example 1: Capaneus, blinded by his pride, challenged the gods themselves.  卡蓬纽斯被骄傲蒙蔽了双眼，向众神发起了挑战。

Example 2:  He acted like a true caponeus, boasting about his achievements to everyone he met. 他表现得像个十足的卡蓬纽斯，逢人就吹嘘自己的成就。| 近义词: braggart, boaster, swaggerer | 反义词: humble person, modest person | 词性: noun
