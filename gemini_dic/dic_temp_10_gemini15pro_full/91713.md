hyperconstitutionalism:/ˌhaɪpərˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃənəlɪzəm/ | n. | 超宪政主义；极端宪政主义 |  A political philosophy that emphasizes strict adherence to the constitution, sometimes to the point of hindering effective governance or disregarding practical considerations.  It often involves a belief that the constitution is a perfect or near-perfect document and should be interpreted literally. | The judge's ruling was seen as an example of hyperconstitutionalism, prioritizing a strict reading of the Second Amendment over public safety concerns.  |Synonyms: strict constitutionalism, originalism (sometimes)| Antonyms:  judicial activism, living constitutionalism | noun
