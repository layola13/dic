fuzz-up:/fʌz ˈʌp/ | n. |混乱，模糊不清的状态；失误，错误 |A state of confusion or blurring; a mistake or error | The witness's testimony was a complete fuzz-up, full of contradictions.  证人的证词完全是一团糟，充满了矛盾。

fuzz-up:/fʌz ˈʌp/ | v. |使模糊；弄乱；搞砸 | To make blurred or indistinct; to mess up; to bungle | He fuzzed up the presentation with too many technical details. 他用太多的技术细节把演示搞砸了。


ETYMOLOGY->
From "fuzz" (n.) meaning a mass of short, fine fibers or hairs, suggesting a blurred or indistinct image, + "up" (adv.) indicating completion or intensification of the action.  "Fuzz" likely originated from Middle English "fusen" meaning "to drive out," perhaps referring to the scattering of fine particles.


SYNONYMS->
mess-up, bungle, muddle, confuse, blur, obscure, screw up, foul up

ANTONYMS->
clarify, clear up, explain, organize, straighten out, fix
