highroad:/ˈhaɪˌroʊd/| n.| 公路；正道；光明磊落的行为 |A course of action which is seen as being proper or moral.| He took the high road and refused to get involved in the smear campaign. 他坚持光明磊落，拒绝参与诽谤活动。|Synonyms: moral path, righteous path, honest course, principled approach|Antonyms: low road, shady dealings, unethical behavior|noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *hih rode*, *hegh rode*, from Old English *hēah rād* (“high road”), equivalent to *high* + *road*.  Cognate with Dutch *hogerode* (“main road”).
