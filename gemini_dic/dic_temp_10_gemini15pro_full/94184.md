imperative:/ɪmˈpɛrətɪv/| adj. n.|必要的，紧急的；祈使语气，命令|Of vital importance; crucial.  A verb form that expresses a command or request.|例句：It is imperative that we act now. 我们现在必须采取行动。|近义词：essential, crucial, vital, urgent, command, order|反义词:optional, unnecessary|词性:adjective, noun

imperative:/ɪmˈpɛrətɪv/| n.|祈使语气，命令|A verb form that expresses a command or request.|例句："Go!" is an imperative. “走！”是一个祈使句。|近义词：command, order, directive|反义词:request, suggestion|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *imperatyf, from Old French imperatif, from Latin imperātīvus (“of or pertaining to command”), from imperō (“I command”).

USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Close the door. = 关门。
Be quiet, please. = 请安静。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Be listening carefully. =  仔细听着。（强调正在进行）
Be working on this until I get back. =  在我回来之前一直做这个。（强调持续进行）


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):  (Imperatives are not typically used in the present perfect.)

一般将来时 (Simple Future):
Don't forget to call me tomorrow. = 明天别忘了给我打电话。（Technically, a negative imperative, but expresses a future action.）
Be there by 8 o'clock. = 八点钟之前到那里。（Implied future）

虚拟语气:

被动语态: (Imperatives are not typically used in the passive voice, though constructions like "Be seated" or "Be assured" are sometimes considered passive imperatives.)


疑问句: (Imperatives are not questions.)


否定句:
Don't go. = 不要走。
Do not open the window. = 不要打开窗户。
