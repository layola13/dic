blisteringly:/ˈblɪstərɪŋli/| adv.|极度地，快速地，猛烈地|Extremely, rapidly, intensely.  It describes something happening with great speed, force, or intensity.|The sun was blisteringly hot. 太阳炙热无比。|The car sped blisteringly fast along the highway. 汽车沿着高速公路飞驰。|近义词：intensely, fiercely, extremely, rapidly|反义词：mildly, gently, slowly|词性:副词
blistering:/ˈblɪstərɪŋ/| adj.|极热的，快速的，猛烈的|Extremely hot, rapid, or intense.|It was a blistering summer day. 那是一个酷暑难耐的夏日。|He delivered a blistering attack on the government. 他对政府进行了猛烈的抨击。|近义词：intense, fierce, extreme, rapid|反义词：mild, gentle, slow|词性:形容词
blister:/ˈblɪstər/| n. v.|水疱，起泡；使起泡|A bubble on the skin filled with fluid, caused by burning or friction; to cause blisters to form.|I got a blister on my heel from the new shoes. 新鞋子把我脚后跟磨出了水疱。|The hot sun blistered the paint. 烈日晒得油漆起了泡。|近义词：(n.) bleb, vesicle; (v.) scald, burn|反义词: (没有直接的反义词)|词性:名词，动词


词源:  From "blister" + "-ing" (present participle) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).  "Blister" itself comes from Old Norse "blystr," meaning "to swell."
