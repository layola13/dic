harks:/hɑːrks/ | v. (third-person singular simple present harks, present participle harking, simple past and past participle harked) | 注意听；倾听； |  hark is an archaic word, primarily used in literature or formal settings. It means to listen attentively. It derives from the Old English *hearcian, meaning "to listen." | Hark! The herald angels sing. 注意听！天使的歌声。| listen, pay attention | ignore, disregard | verb

harks:/hɑːrks/ | n. | 注意听；倾听； (This usage is less common and usually found in older texts.) |  hark as a noun is less common than the verb form. It refers to the act of listening. |  Give hark to my words. 注意听我的话。| attention, listening | inattention, disregard | noun
