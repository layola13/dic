filch:/fɪltʃ/| v. |偷窃，窃取 (尤指小东西)|To steal (something, especially something small or of little value) in a casual or sneaky way.|He filched a cookie from the jar. 他从罐子里偷了一块饼干。|近义词：pilfer, swipe, snatch, steal|反义词：give, return, offer|词性:动词

词源:
From Middle English *filchen*, from Old English *fylcian* "to roll up, wrap up," also "to seize, rob," probably ultimately from Proto-Germanic *fulkōną* "to wrap, fold." The sense development is perhaps from "wrapping up and carrying off" to "stealing."
