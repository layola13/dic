mozarabic: /ˌmoʊzərˈæbɪk/ | adj. n. | 摩尔人统治下的基督教的；摩萨拉布人（指公元8至11世纪居住在伊比利亚半岛的讲西班牙方言的基督教徒）| Relating to the Mozarabs or their culture, especially their language and liturgy.  A Mozarab. | The Mozarabic rite is still celebrated in some Spanish cathedrals. 摩萨拉布仪式至今仍在一些西班牙大教堂举行。|  Mozarabic chant is known for its distinctive melodic character.  摩萨拉布圣歌以其独特的旋律特征而闻名。| 近义词: Visigothic | 反义词: Islamic | 词性: adjective, noun


Etymology:
From Spanish *mozárabe*, from Arabic مستعرب (*mustaʿrib*), meaning "one who adopts Arab customs",  from the verb استعرب (*istaʿraba*) "to arabize oneself".  Ultimately from عرب (*ʿarab*).
