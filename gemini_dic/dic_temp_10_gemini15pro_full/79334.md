gleby:/ˈɡliːbi/| adj.|肥沃的；黏土的|Rich in clay; clayey.  Referring to soil that is good for growing crops because it contains a lot of clay.|The farmer praised the gleby soil of his fields.|近义词：clayey, fertile, loamy|反义词:infertile, barren, sandy|词性:形容词

词源: From Polish *gleba* "soil," from Proto-Slavic *gleba.  Ultimately from the same Proto-Indo-European root as "globe."
