ambiguously:/æmˈbɪɡjuəsli/ | adv. | 模棱两可地，含糊不清地 |  in a way that can be understood in more than one way; unclearly. |  She answered the question ambiguously, leaving everyone confused. 她模棱两可地回答了这个问题，让每个人都很困惑。| The contract was worded ambiguously, leading to disagreements later on. 合同措辞含糊不清，导致后来出现分歧。 | vaguely, unclearly, equivocally, obscurely | clearly, distinctly, unequivocally, precisely | adverb

Etymology->
From Late Latin *ambiguosus, from Latin ambiguus (“doubtful, uncertain”), from ambigō (“wander about, waver, hesitate”).
