naughtiest:/ˈnɔːtiɪst/ | adj. | 最淘气的，最顽皮的 |  Superlative form of naughty: most naughty.  It describes someone who behaves badly, disobediently, or mischievously to the greatest degree. |  The naughtiest child in the class was sent to the principal's office. 班上最淘气的孩子被送到了校长办公室。|  mischievous, disobedient, unruly, troublesome |  well-behaved, obedient, good | adjective

Etymology->
From Old English *nōwiht (nothing), from *ne + *ōwiht (aught).  The original meaning was "worthless," which later evolved into "wicked" and then "mischievous."  The "-y" suffix turns it into an adjective (naughty), and "-est" creates the superlative form (naughtiest).
