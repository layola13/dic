galiot:/ˈɡælɪət/ | n. | 荷兰平底帆船；一种小帆船 | A small Dutch sailing vessel; a type of small galleon. | The galiot sailed smoothly across the calm waters. 荷兰平底帆船平稳地航行在平静的水面上。| The fishermen used a galiot to navigate the shallow coastal waters. 渔民们使用一种小帆船在浅海沿岸航行。 | synonym: sloop, ketch | antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "large vessel" or "warship") | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Dutch *galjoot*, from Middle Dutch *galei*, *galie*, from Old French *galie*, from Medieval Latin *galea*, from Late Latin *galea* ("galley").  Ultimately from Greek *γαλέα* (*galea*), meaning "weasel".  The connection likely refers to the swiftness of the vessel.
