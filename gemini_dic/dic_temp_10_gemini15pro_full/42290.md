counter-indicate:/ˌkaʊntərˈɪndɪkeɪt/| v. |禁忌，指示禁忌症|To make (a treatment or procedure) inadvisable.|The patient's history of blood clots counter-indicates the use of this medication.  该病人的血栓病史禁忌使用这种药物。|Synonyms: contraindicate, proscribe|Antonyms: indicate, advise, recommend|verb

counter-indication:/ˌkaʊntərˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃn/| n. |禁忌症|A factor that makes a particular treatment or procedure inadvisable.|A history of allergy to penicillin is a counter-indication to its use. 对青霉素过敏史是使用青霉素的禁忌症。|Synonyms: contraindication|Antonyms: indication|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + indicate.  "Counter-"  acts as a prefix meaning "against" or "opposite." "Indicate" comes from the Latin "indicare," meaning "to point out" or "to show."
