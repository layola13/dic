paronomastic:/ˌpærənəˈmæstɪk/ | adj. |双关的，谐音的 |Characterized by the use of paronomasia, a rhetorical device that involves using words that are similar in sound but different in meaning, often for humorous or poetic effect.| The comedian's paronomastic jokes had the audience in stitches.  这位喜剧演员的谐音笑话让观众捧腹大笑。|Synonyms: punning, wordplay| Antonyms: literal, straightforward| adjective

paronomasia:/ˌpærənəˈmeɪʒə/ | n. |双关语，谐音 |A rhetorical or literary device involving a play on words with similar sounds but different meanings.| Shakespeare's works are full of clever paronomasia.  莎士比亚的作品充满了巧妙的双关语。|Synonyms: pun, wordplay| Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider:  plain speech, literal language) | noun
