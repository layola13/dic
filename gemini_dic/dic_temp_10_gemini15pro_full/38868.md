confederationists:/kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪsts/ | n. |邦联主义者；支持邦联制者 | Supporters or advocates of a confederation, a union of states or political units in which each member retains considerable independence. |  The confederationists argued for a weaker central government. 邦联主义者主张建立一个较弱的中央政府。 | The confederationists believed states' rights were paramount. 邦联主义者认为州的权利至高无上。 | synonyms: federalists (in some contexts), states' rights advocates, secessionists (in some contexts) | antonyms: unionists, centralists | noun
