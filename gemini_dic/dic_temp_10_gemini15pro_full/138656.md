nudge:/nʌdʒ/
|n.|轻推，推动；肘击；提醒|A gentle push or poke, especially with the elbow, to attract attention or give a signal.  A reminder or prompt.|
|vt.|轻推，推动；用肘轻推；劝诱；鼓动|To push gently, typically with the elbow, in order to get someone's attention or to encourage them to do something. To persuade or gently encourage (someone) to do something.|
|例句：He nudged me and pointed to the stage. 他用胳膊肘碰了碰我，并指向舞台。|
|例句：She nudged him to remind him to pay attention. 她推了他一下，提醒他要注意。|
|近义词：poke, prod, push, encourage, prompt|
|反义词：discourage, dissuade|
|词性：名词，动词|


nudges:/nʌdʒɪz/
|n.|轻推，推动（nudge 的复数形式）|Plural form of "nudge," referring to multiple instances of gentle pushes or prods.|
|例句：He gave me a few nudges to wake me up. 他推了我几下把我叫醒。|
|例句：The report offers some helpful nudges to improve performance. 这份报告提供了一些有益的建议来提高绩效。|
|近义词：pokes, prods, pushes, encouragements, prompts|
|反义词：N/A (plural form doesn't have a direct antonym)|
|词性：名词|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *noggen* "to push, strike," probably of imitative origin.
