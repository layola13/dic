fatalistically:/ˌfeɪtəlˈɪstɪkli/
adverb|宿命地，听天由命地|Accepting events as inevitable; in a way that shows a belief in fate.|He fatalistically accepted the news of his diagnosis.|近义词：resignedly, passively, apathetically|反义词：optimistically, hopefully, proactively|词性: 副词

Etymology:
From fatalist + -ically. Fatalist from fatal + -ist. Fatal from Late Latin fatalis "ordained by fate," from Latin fatum "fate, destiny, what is ordained, prediction, oracle," literally "what has been spoken," past participle of fari "to speak" (from PIE root *bha- (2) "to speak, tell, say").
