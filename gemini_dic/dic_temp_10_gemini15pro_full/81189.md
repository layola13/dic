grandiloquous: /ˌɡrændɪˈləʊkwəs/ | adj. | 夸夸其谈的，辞藻华丽的 |  Using pompous or extravagant language; bombastic.  |  The politician's grandiloquent speech failed to impress the voters, who were more interested in concrete solutions.  这位政客夸夸其谈的演讲未能打动选民，他们更感兴趣的是具体的解决方案。

|  He delivered a grandiloquent eulogy, full of elaborate metaphors and high-sounding phrases. 他发表了一篇辞藻华丽的悼词，充满了精巧的比喻和冠冕堂皇的词句。 | 近义词：pompous, bombastic, verbose, florid | 反义词：simple, plain, concise, unassuming | 词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *grandiloquus*, from *grandis* (great, large) + *loqui* (to speak).
