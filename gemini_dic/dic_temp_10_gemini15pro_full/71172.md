fɪˈsɪpiːdiə/| n. | 鳍脚类动物|A suborder of carnivorous mammals characterized by having separate toes, as opposed to webbed feet like pinnipeds (seals, sea lions, walruses).  It includes most terrestrial carnivores like dogs, cats, bears, and weasels.|Dogs, cats, and bears are all members of the Fissipedia suborder.  狗、猫和熊都是鳍脚亚目成员。|Synonyms:  Caniformia (sometimes used interchangeably, but technically a different clade), land carnivores |Antonyms: Pinnipedia|noun

fɪˈsɪpiːd/| adj. | 鳍脚类的|Having separate toes. |The fissiped paw of the cat allows for agile climbing. 猫的鳍脚爪子使它能够灵活地攀爬。 |Synonyms:  toe-footed|Antonyms: pinniped, web-footed|adjective
