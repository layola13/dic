imprudentnesses:/ɪmˈpruːdntnəsɪz/
|n.|轻率，鲁莽，不明智的行为|The plural form of imprudentness, referring to multiple instances of lacking caution or good judgment.|His youthful imprudentnesses cost him dearly later in life. 他年轻时的轻率行为在他日后生活中付出了沉重的代价。|Synonyms: follies, indiscretions, recklessnesses|Antonyms: cautiousness, prudence, wisdom|Noun

Etymology:
From Late Middle English: imprudent + -ness.  Imprudent itself comes from French, or from late Latin imprudent- ‘not foreseeing,’ from in- ‘not’ + prudent- ‘foreseeing.’ Ultimately from Latin provident-, from providere ‘foresee.’
