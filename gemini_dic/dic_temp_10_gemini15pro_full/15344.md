backstairs:/ˈbækˈstɛəz/ | adj. | 后楼梯的；秘密的；私下的 | Relating to or conducted in secrecy, especially with regard to intrigue or illicit relationships; done in a clandestine or underhanded way. |  The backstairs negotiations resulted in a surprising compromise. 后楼梯的谈判达成了一个令人惊讶的妥协。| synonyms: clandestine, secret, covert, surreptitious, underhand, furtive | antonyms: open, public, aboveboard | adjective

backstairs:/ˈbækˈstɛəz/ | n. | 后楼梯；秘密手段 |  A back staircase; a private or secluded staircase. (Often used figuratively to refer to secret or underhanded dealings.) |  He gained access to the confidential documents through backstairs influence. 他通过秘密手段获得了机密文件。| synonyms: intrigue, machination, scheme | antonyms: openness, transparency | noun

Etymology:
From 'back' + 'stairs'.  The literal meaning refers to a secondary staircase, often used by servants, and thus associated with activities hidden from the main household. This evolved into the figurative meaning of secrecy and intrigue.
