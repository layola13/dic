burlesque: /bərˈlɛsk/ (US), /bɜːˈlɛsk/ (UK) | n. adj. | 滑稽模仿；讽刺作品；滑稽歌舞表演 | A form of entertainment consisting of satirical or comic imitations; a parody. As an adjective, it describes something that is relating to or characteristic of burlesque. |  The play was a burlesque of Shakespearean tragedies.  这出戏是对莎士比亚悲剧的滑稽模仿。 | synonyms: parody, satire, travesty, caricature, mockery | antonyms: seriousness, solemnity, reverence, tribute | noun, adjective

burlesque: /bərˈlɛsk/ (US), /bɜːˈlɛsk/ (UK) | vt. | 滑稽地模仿；讽刺 | To represent in a ludicrous or mocking way. | The comedian burlesqued the president's speech.  这位喜剧演员滑稽地模仿了总统的演讲。 | synonyms: parody, satirize, mock, caricature | antonyms: honor, praise, extol | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *burlesco*, from *burla* "joke, mockery," of uncertain origin.
