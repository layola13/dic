imprescriptibly:/ˌɪmprɪˈskrɪptəbli/
adverb
不可剥夺地，无法废除地
Not subject to prescription; incapable of being lost or alienated through lapse of time or discontinued use.
The right to free speech is imprescriptibly guaranteed by the constitution. 言论自由的权利是由宪法不可剥夺地保障的。
近义词: inalienably, indefeasibly
反义词: conditionally, revocably
词性: adverb

Etymology:
From in- (not) + prescript (rule, order) + -ible (capable of) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  The root "prescript" comes from Latin praescriptus, past participle of praescribere (to prescribe).
