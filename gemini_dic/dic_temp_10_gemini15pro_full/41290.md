cordites:/ˈkɔːrdaɪts/| n.|柯代火药|A family of smokeless propellants used in firearms and artillery, based on nitrocellulose gelatinized with nitroglycerin.|The British army adopted cordite as its standard propellant in 1891.|近义词：gunpowder, propellant|反义词:None|词性:noun

Etymology:  From *cordite*, a blend of *cord* (referring to the cord-like shape of the propellant) and *-ite* (a suffix used in the names of minerals and explosives).
