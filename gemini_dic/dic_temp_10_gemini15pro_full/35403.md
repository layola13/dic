co-optable:/koʊˈɑːptəbl/
adjective
可被吸收的; 可被拉拢的
able to be co-opted.  Capable of being absorbed into a larger group or system, often by assimilation or manipulation.
例句：The small political party proved remarkably co-optable, quickly losing its identity after merging with the larger group.  这个小政党被证明非常容易被吸收，在与更大的团体合并后很快就失去了自己的身份。

例句：The independent research institute was co-optable by the corporation, leading to concerns about its objectivity. 这家独立研究机构被该公司吸收，导致人们对其客观性感到担忧。
近义词：absorbable, assimilable, incorporable
反义词：resistant, unyielding, inflexible
词性: adjective

co-opt:/koʊˈɑːpt/
verb
吸收；拉拢
(1) appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members. (2) adopt (an idea or policy) for one's own use.
例句：The committee co-opted several new members to help with the increased workload. 委员会吸收了几名新成员以帮助处理增加的工作量。

例句：The marketing campaign co-opted the language of social justice, leading to accusations of exploitation. 营销活动盗用了社会正义的语言，导致人们指责其剥削。
近义词：appropriate, assimilate, embrace, incorporate
反义词：reject, exclude, repudiate
词性: verb
