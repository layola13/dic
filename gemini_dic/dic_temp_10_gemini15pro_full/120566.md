mire:/maɪər/ | n. | 泥潭; 困境; 沼泽 |  A stretch of swampy or boggy ground. A difficult or unpleasant situation from which it is hard to extricate oneself. | The car got stuck in the mire. 汽车陷入了泥潭。 He was sinking deeper into a mire of debt. 他越陷越深地陷入债务泥潭。 | synonyms: bog, marsh, swamp, quagmire, morass, predicament, plight | antonyms:  solid ground, advantage, ease | noun

mire:/maɪər/ | v. |  使陷入泥潭; 陷入困境 | To cause to become stuck in mire. To involve in a difficult situation. |  Heavy rains mired the roads. 大雨使道路泥泞不堪。 The project became mired in bureaucracy. 该项目陷入官僚主义的泥潭。 | synonyms: entangle, embroil, bog down | antonyms: extricate, free, liberate | verb
