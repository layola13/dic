paranoid: /ˈpærənɔɪd/ (US), /ˈpærənɔɪd/ (UK) | adj. n. | 偏执的，患妄想症的；妄想狂患者 | Showing or suffering from paranoia, characterized by  unreasonable suspicion and mistrust of others. |  He was becoming increasingly paranoid, convinced that everyone was plotting against him. 他越来越偏执，确信每个人都在密谋反对他。| synonyms: suspicious, distrustful, mistrustful, wary, apprehensive, anxious, fearful, insecure | antonyms: trusting, confident, secure | adjective

paranoid: /ˈpærənɔɪd/ (US), /ˈpærənɔɪd/ (UK)| n. | 妄想狂患者 | A person who is paranoid. |  He's a paranoid who thinks everyone is out to get him. 他是一个妄想狂，认为每个人都想害他。 | synonyms:  lunatic, madman, psychotic | antonyms:  None | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek "para-" (beside, beyond, amiss) + "nous" (mind, intellect), via medical Latin "paranoia."  Originally referred to a specific mental disorder, but now often used more loosely.
