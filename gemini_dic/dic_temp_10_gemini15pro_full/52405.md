diddle:/ˈdɪdəl/| v. | 欺骗; 骗取(尤指小钱); 拨弄; 玩弄 | to cheat someone, usually out of a small amount of money; to move something repeatedly up and down or from side to side | He diddled investors out of millions of dollars. 他骗取了投资者数百万美元。| synonyms: cheat, swindle, defraud, fiddle, trifle with | antonyms: be honest, compensate, repay | verb

diddle:/ˈdɪdəl/| n. | 欺骗; 骗局 | an act of cheating someone, usually out of a small amount of money | The whole investment scheme was a diddle. 整个投资计划就是一个骗局。| synonyms: cheat, swindle, fraud, deception | antonyms: honesty, integrity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From perhaps a frequentative of *diddle, of uncertain origin, but perhaps related to dawdle.
