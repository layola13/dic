grafted:/ˈɡræftɪd/ /ˈɡrɑːftɪd/| adj.|嫁接的，移植的|Joined or attached (as if) by grafting; implanted or incorporated.|The surgeon grafted skin from her leg onto her arm.  外科医生把她腿上的皮肤移植到她的手臂上。|例句：A new variety of apple was created by grafting two existing types. 通过嫁接两种现有的苹果品种培育出一个新品种。|近义词：attached, joined, implanted, spliced|反义词:detached, separated|词性:形容词
graft:/ɡræft/ /ɡrɑːft/| n. v.|嫁接，移植；贪污，受贿|
(n.) A shoot or bud of one plant inserted into another plant in which it continues to grow; something grafted or transplanted.  (v.) To insert (a shoot or bud) as a graft. To join or unite closely. To obtain by dishonest or questionable means.|
(n.) The doctor performed a skin graft. 医生进行了皮肤移植。  (v.) He grafted the branch onto the tree. 他把树枝嫁接到树上。|近义词：(n.) transplant, implant, (v.) attach, join, implant|反义词:(v.) detach, separate|词性:名词,动词
