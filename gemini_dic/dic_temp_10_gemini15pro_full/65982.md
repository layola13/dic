exaltation:/ˌɛɡzɔːlˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 兴奋，得意，狂喜;  晋升，提升 |A feeling of intense happiness and elation. The act of raising someone to a higher rank or position. |例句：She felt a sense of exaltation as she accepted the award. 她接受奖项时感到一阵欣喜若狂。

He was appointed to the position with great exaltation. 他被晋升到这个职位，非常兴奋。| 近义词：elation, joy, rapture, elevation, promotion | 反义词：depression, sadness, despair, demotion, degradation | 词性: noun


exalt:/ɪɡˈzɔːlt/ | vt. | 提升，提拔;  赞扬，歌颂 | To raise to a higher rank or position; to praise highly. |例句：The king exalted his loyal knight to a higher position. 国王将他的忠诚骑士提升到更高的职位。

The poem exalts the beauty of nature. 这首诗歌颂了大自然的美丽。| 近义词：praise, glorify, elevate, promote | 反义词：degrade, humiliate, demote | 词性: verb
