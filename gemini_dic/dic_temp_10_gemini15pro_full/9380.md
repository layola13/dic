antiquate:/ˈæntɪkweɪt/| v.|使过时，使古旧；使具有古色古香|To make something old-fashioned or obsolete.  To give something an appearance of age.|例句：New technology is antiquating many traditional skills. 新技术正在使许多传统技能过时。

例句：The furniture was deliberately antiquated to give the room a more historic feel.  家具被故意做旧，以赋予房间更浓厚的历史感。|近义词：outdate, obsolete, archaic|反义词:modernize, update|词性:动词
antiquated:/ˈæntɪkweɪtɪd/| adj.|过时的，陈旧的；古色古香的|Old-fashioned or obsolete.  Having an appearance of age.|例句：The company's antiquated machinery was hindering production.  公司陈旧的机器阻碍了生产。

例句：She has some rather antiquated ideas about marriage. 她对婚姻有一些相当过时的想法。|近义词：outdated, obsolete, archaic, old-fashioned|反义词:modern, contemporary, up-to-date|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *antiquatus, past participle of antiquare (“to make old”), from Latin antiquus (“old, ancient”).
