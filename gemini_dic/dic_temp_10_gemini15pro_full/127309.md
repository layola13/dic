navyist:/ˈneɪviɪst/
noun
海军主义者，海权论者
A person who advocates maintaining a strong navy as a vital component of national defense and power projection.
He was a staunch navyist, believing that a powerful fleet was essential for national security. 他是一位坚定的海军主义者，认为强大的舰队对国家安全至关重要。
近义词:navalist, militarist
反义词:pacifist
词性:n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From navy + -ist.  "Navy" comes from Old French "navie" meaning "fleet of ships," ultimately from Latin "navis" meaning "ship."  The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system, or is engaged in a particular activity.
