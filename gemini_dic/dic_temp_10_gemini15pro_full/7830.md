antagonism:/ænˈtæɡənɪzəm/| n.|敌意，对抗，敌对|Active opposition or hostility.|There was deep antagonism between the two groups. 这两组之间存在着严重的敌对情绪。|Synonyms: hostility, animosity, friction, antipathy, opposition, conflict|Antonyms: friendship, amity, goodwill, harmony|noun

antagonist:/ænˈtæɡənɪst/| n.|对手，敌手，拮抗物|A person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone or something; an adversary.|The protagonist and antagonist clashed in the final scene. 主人公和反派在最后一幕发生了冲突。|Synonyms: opponent, adversary, rival, foe, enemy|Antonyms: protagonist, supporter, ally, friend|noun

antagonistic:/ænˌtæɡəˈnɪstɪk/| adj.|对抗性的，敌对的，反对的|Showing or feeling active opposition or hostility towards someone or something.|He took an antagonistic stance towards the new policy. 他对新政策采取了对抗的立场。|Synonyms: hostile, opposed, inimical, adversarial, unsympathetic|Antonyms: friendly, sympathetic, supportive, cooperative|adjective

antagonize:/ænˈtæɡənaɪz/| vt.|引起…的敌意（或反感），与…对抗|Cause (someone) to become hostile.|His aggressive behavior antagonized his colleagues. 他的侵略性行为引起了同事们的反感。|Synonyms: provoke, irritate, anger, alienate, offend|Antonyms: placate, appease, conciliate, soothe|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *antagonisme*, from Ancient Greek *ἀνταγωνιστής (antagonistes)*  "competitor, opponent, rival,"  from *ἀντί (anti)*  "against" + *ἀγωνίζομαι (agonizomai)*  "I contend for a prize,"  from *ἀγών (agon)* "contest."
