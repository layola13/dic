boycott:/ˈbɔɪkɒt/ (UK) /ˈbɔɪkɑːt/ (US)| n. vt.|抵制，联合抵制|An act of refusing to buy, use, or participate in something as a way of protesting.  The origin of the word comes from Captain Charles Boycott, a land agent in Ireland who was ostracized by his tenants in 1880.|例句：The consumers announced a boycott of the company's products. 消费者宣布抵制这家公司的产品。|例句：They are boycotting the elections. 他们正在抵制选举。|近义词：ban, embargo, shun, ostracize|反义词：patronize, support|词性：noun, verb
