abolishing:/əˈbɒlɪʃɪŋ/
v.
废除,取消
The act of formally putting an end to a system, practice, or institution.
The government is considering abolishing the death penalty. 政府正在考虑废除死刑。
synonyms: eliminating, eradicating, extinguishing, terminating
antonyms: establishing, instituting, restoring, creating
verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin abolere "to destroy, do away with, annul, abolish," from ab- "away from" + olere "to grow."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company is abolishing its outdated policies. = 公司正在废除其过时的政策。
They are abolishing the use of plastic bags in the store. = 他们在商店里禁止使用塑料袋。

一般过去时:
The government abolished the tax last year. = 政府去年取消了这项税收。
They abolished slavery in the 19th century. = 他们在19世纪废除了奴隶制。

现在完成时:
Congress has abolished several unnecessary regulations. = 国会已经废除了几项不必要的规章制度。
They have abolished the practice of child labor in many countries. = 他们在许多国家已经废除了童工的做法。

过去完成时:
By the time I arrived, they had abolished the old rule. = 在我到达之前，他们已经废除了旧规则。
The country had abolished the monarchy before the war began. = 在战争开始之前，这个国家已经废除了君主制。

将来时:
They will be abolishing this law next year. = 他们明年将要废除这项法律。
The company will abolish the commission system soon. = 公司很快将取消佣金制度。

虚拟语气:
If they abolished the law, it would benefit everyone. = 如果他们废除这项法律，将会使每个人受益。
I wish they would abolish this outdated custom. = 我希望他们能废除这种过时的习俗。

被动语态:
The law was abolished in 1964. = 该法律于1964年被废除。
The death penalty has been abolished in many countries. = 死刑在许多国家已被废除。
