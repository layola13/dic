misconduct: /ˌmɪskɒndʌkt/ (British) /ˌmɪskɑːndʌkt/ (American)
misconduct: v. n.|行为不端；处理不当|To behave improperly or illegally, especially in a professional capacity. As a noun, it refers to such behavior.| The lawyer was accused of misconduct for tampering with evidence.  该律师因篡改证据而被指控行为不端。| Synonyms: wrongdoing, misbehavior, impropriety, malpractice | Antonyms: good conduct, propriety, rectitude| verb/noun


misconducted: /ˌmɪskənˈdʌktɪd/ (British) /ˌmɪskənˈdʌktɪd/ (American)
misconducted: adj. |行为不端的；管理不善的|Badly managed or handled; carried out improperly.| The investigation revealed a series of misconducted experiments. 调查揭露了一系列管理不善的实验。| Synonyms: mishandled, mismanaged, botched | Antonyms: well-managed, properly conducted| adjective
