minstrel: /ˈmɪnstrəl/ | n. |吟游诗人；歌手；乐师| A medieval singer or musician, especially one who travelled from place to place and entertained people with songs and stories. |  In medieval times, minstrels would travel from castle to castle, entertaining the nobility with their music and tales.  在中世纪时期，吟游诗人会从一个城堡旅行到另一个城堡，用他们的音乐和故事娱乐贵族。

The minstrel sang a ballad about a brave knight. 那位吟游诗人唱了一首关于勇敢骑士的民谣。


|近义词：bard, musician, singer, poet, troubadour |反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "audience" as the receiver rather than the giver of entertainment) |词性: noun
