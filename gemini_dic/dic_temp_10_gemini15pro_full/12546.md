aspidobranchia: /ˌæspɪdoʊˈbræŋkiə/
| n. | 盾鳃类 |A subclass of marine mollusks (gastropods) characterized by having a single gill in the mantle cavity, also known as Archaeogastropoda.  They are considered a more primitive group of gastropods. | Example: Aspidobranchia includes limpets and abalones.  盾鳃类包括帽贝和鲍鱼。|Synonym: Archaeogastropoda|Antonym: (No direct antonym, potentially other gastropod subclasses like Vetigastropoda or Caenogastropoda could be considered contrasting groups.)|noun


Etymology:
From Greek *ἀσπίς* (aspis, "shield") + *βράγχια* (branchia, "gills") referring to the single, shield-like gill.
