mouth-watering:/ˈmaʊθˌwɔːtərɪŋ/ /ˈmaʊθˌwɑːtərɪŋ/| adj.|令人垂涎的，好吃的|Making someone want to eat; appetizing.  It describes food that looks or smells so delicious that it makes your mouth produce saliva.|The restaurant is known for its mouth-watering steaks. 这家餐厅以其令人垂涎的牛排而闻名。|Synonyms: appetizing, delicious, tempting, tantalizing|Antonyms: unappetizing, bland, tasteless|adjective

Etymology->
From "mouth" + "watering" (present participle of "water" used in the sense of "to salivate").  The image is of saliva accumulating in the mouth in anticipation of delicious food.
