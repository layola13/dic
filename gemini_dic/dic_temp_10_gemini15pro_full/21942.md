bogging:/ˈbɒɡɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈbɑːɡɪŋ/ (US)| v. |陷入泥沼；停滞不前；使陷入困境|Becoming stuck in mud or a bog; slowing down or becoming stuck in a difficult situation. |例句：The car got bogged down in the mud. 汽车陷入了泥里。

例句：The project is bogging down due to lack of funding.  由于缺乏资金，该项目陷入了停滞。|近义词：miring, stalling, hindering|反义词：advancing, progressing|词性:动词(Verb)

bogging:/ˈbɒɡɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈbɑːɡɪŋ/ (US)| adj. |令人沮丧的；使人陷入困境的 |Causing something to slow down or become stuck; depressing or discouraging.|例句：The bogging workload made it difficult to meet the deadline. 繁重的工作使得很难按时完成任务。

例句：It was a bogging experience trying to get a refund.  尝试退款是一次令人沮丧的经历。|近义词：frustrating, difficult, challenging|反义词：encouraging, motivating|词性:形容词(Adjective)
