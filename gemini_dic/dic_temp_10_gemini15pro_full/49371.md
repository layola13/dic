deliquium:/dɪˈlɪkwiəm/
n.
昏厥，晕厥；神志不清
A sudden and temporary loss of consciousness, typically caused by a lack of blood flow to the brain.
She suffered a deliquium from the heat. 她因高温而昏厥。

近义词: faint, swoon, syncope
反义词: consciousness, alertness
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin dēliquium ("a melting away, dissolving, sinking, swooning, failure"), from dēliquēscō ("to melt away"), from dē- ("down, away") + liquēscō ("to become liquid, melt"), from liqueo ("to be fluid").
