lose: /luːz/ (British) /luːz/ (American)
| v. | 失去，丢失，输掉，失败，减少，浪费，错过，使迷路，使沉溺于，专心致志于，摆脱，解脱，迷失方向，走失，亏损 | To be deprived of or cease to have or retain (something). | I lost my keys. 我把钥匙丢了。| Near synonyms: misplace, miss, forfeit, drop, shed, fail, be defeated by | Antonyms: find, win, gain, obtain, keep, retain
词性: 动词

lose: /lɒst/ (British) /lɔːst/ (American)
| adj. | 迷路的，输掉的，失败的，注定的，毁灭的，迷惑的，不知所措的 | Unable to find one's way; not knowing one's whereabouts. | The lost hikers wandered in the woods for hours.  迷路的徒步旅行者在树林里徘徊了几个小时。| Near synonyms: missing, misplaced, astray, disoriented, bewildered | Antonyms: found, located, oriented, certain, confident
词性: 形容词

词源：From Old English *lēosan (attested only in forlēosan “lose utterly”), from Proto-Germanic *leusaną (source also of Old Frisian liāsa, Old Saxon farliosan, Old High German virliosan, German verlieren, Old Norse liosa), from Proto-Indo-European *leu- (“to loosen, divide, cut apart”).
