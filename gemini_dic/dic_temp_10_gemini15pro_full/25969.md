burrows:/ˈbʌroʊz/ (US), /ˈbʌrəʊz/ (UK)| n. v.|洞穴，地道；挖洞，挖地道|A burrow is a hole or tunnel dug in the ground by an animal, such as a rabbit, fox, or mole, to live in.|The rabbit disappeared down its burrow. 兔子消失在它的洞穴里。|近义词：hole, tunnel, den, lair|反义词: mound, hill|noun
burrows:/ˈbʌroʊz/ (US), /ˈbʌrəʊz/ (UK)| v.|挖洞，挖地道|To burrow means to dig a hole or tunnel in the ground.|The moles burrowed deep into the earth. 鼹鼠在地下挖了很深的洞。|近义词：dig, excavate, tunnel|反义词: fill, cover|verb

词源: From Old English *burhrow "fortified place," from burh "fortress" + row "row, line."  Meaning shifted to "hole, lair."
