bequeath: /bɪˈkwiːð/ /bɪˈkwiːθ/ | vt. | 留下；遗赠；传下 | To leave (property) to someone by means of a will. To pass (something) on or leave (something) to someone else. |  She bequeathed her entire estate to her niece. 她把全部财产遗赠给了侄女。

He bequeathed his knowledge and skills to his apprentices. 他把自己的知识和技能传授给了他的学徒。 | 近义词: leave, will, impart, transmit | 反义词: inherit, receive | 词性: 动词

词源: From Old English *bi-cwēthan (attested only in the past participle *bi-cwēðen), from bi- "by" + cwēthan "to say, speak."  The original sense was "to speak to, address," later "to give by speaking," hence "to give or leave by will."
