laveer:/ləˈvɪr/| v. |转向，改变航向；(喻)改变立场，改变策略|To change the direction of a sailing vessel or, more generally, to change one's course or direction. |The captain laveered the ship to avoid the rocks. 船长转向以避开礁石。

The politician laveered his position on the issue after public outcry.  这位政治家在公众的强烈抗议后改变了他在这个问题上的立场。|近义词：swerve, veer, shift, tack, zigzag|反义词：maintain course, stay the course|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Dutch *laveren*, "to tack," from Middle Dutch *laveren*, related to *loef* "luff."  The sense of "to steer a ship" is first attested in the late 16th century. The figurative sense of changing direction or opinion emerged in the late 18th century.
