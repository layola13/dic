medol: /ˈmɛdəl/ | n. |奖牌；勋章；纪念章|A flat piece of metal with an inscription or design, given as an award or commemoration.|She won a gold medal at the Olympics. 她赢得了奥运会金牌。| He received a medal for bravery. 他因勇敢而获得了一枚奖牌。 | synonym: award, decoration, prize | antonym: forfeit | noun

etymology: From Old French *medaille*, from Italian medaglia, from Late Latin medalla "coin worth half a denarius," from Latin medialis "of the middle, medial."  The sense shift from "coin" to "medal" arose because coins were sometimes used as medals.
