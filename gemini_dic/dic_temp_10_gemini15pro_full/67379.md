extenuatingly:/ɪkˈstɛnjuːeɪtɪŋli/ | adv. |  减轻地，情有可原地 |  In a way that lessens the seriousness of guilt or an offense. |  He behaved badly, but extenuatingly, he'd been under a lot of stress. 他行为不端，但情有可原，因为他承受了很大的压力。 | 近义词:  mitigatingly, palliatively | 反义词:  inexcusably, unforgivably | 词性: 副词

extenuate:/ɪkˈstɛnjuːeɪt/ | v. | 减轻，使显得轻微 |  Make (guilt or an offense) seem less serious or more forgivable. |  His youth extenuates his crime to some degree. 他的年轻在某种程度上减轻了他的罪行。| 近义词:  mitigate, lessen, diminish | 反义词:  aggravate, worsen | 词性: 动词

词源:  From Late Latin extenuatus, past participle of extenuare "to make thin, lessen, weaken," from ex- "out" + tenuare "make thin," from tenuis "thin."
