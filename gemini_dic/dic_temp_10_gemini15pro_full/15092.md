bacchanal: /ˌbækəˈnæl/ (US), /ˌbækəˈneɪl/ (UK)| n. adj.|狂欢，狂欢节；喧闹的酒宴；狂欢作乐的人|A wild, drunken revelry; an orgy.  Also, a person who participates in such revelries.|例句：The annual music festival became a bacchanal of dancing and drinking.  年度音乐节变成了一场狂欢的歌舞和饮酒。

例句：The fraternity house was known for its bacchanals.  兄弟会会所以其狂欢而闻名。|近义词：orgy, revelry, carousal, debauchery|反义词：solemnity, restraint, temperance|词性: noun, adjective

bacchanalian: /ˌbækəˈneɪliən/ (US), /ˌbækəˈneɪlɪən/ (UK)| adj.|狂欢的，放荡的|Characterized by or relating to a bacchanal; riotously drunken and merry.|例句：The bacchanalian atmosphere of the party was infectious. 派对上狂欢的气氛很有感染力。

例句：Their bacchanalian celebrations lasted for days. 他们的狂欢庆祝活动持续了好几天。|近义词：orgiastic, riotous, drunken|反义词：sober, restrained, quiet|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "Bacchanalia," plural of "Bacchanale," from Greek "Bakkheia," referring to festivals of Bacchus (Dionysus), the god of wine.
