loosed:/luːst/
|v.|释放，解开，发动|Past participle of loose.  The past participle of the verb "loose," meaning to release something or someone from a constraint.  It can also mean to unleash something powerful or destructive.|The hounds were loosed on the fox.猎犬被放出去追狐狸。|近义词：released, freed, unleashed, unbound|反义词：tied, bound, restrained, fastened|词性:动词

词源:
From Middle English *losen, from Old Norse *leysa (“to loosen, release”), from Proto-Germanic *lausjaną (“to loosen, release”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)leu- (“to loosen, dissolve, divide”). Cognate with Icelandic leysa (“to solve, redeem”), Swedish lösa (“to loosen, solve”), Danish løse (“to loosen, solve”), Dutch lossen (“to unload, fire (a gun)”), German lösen (“to loosen, solve”). Related to lose.
