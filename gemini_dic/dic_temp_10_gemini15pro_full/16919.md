basmachi:/bɑːsˈmɑːtʃi/| n. |巴斯玛奇| Members of a Central Asian Muslim guerrilla movement that fought against Russian/Soviet rule in the early 20th century.  They were primarily active in Turkestan (present-day Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, Kyrgyzstan, and parts of Kazakhstan and Turkmenistan) between 1917 and the late 1920s. | The Basmachi revolt was a complex and brutal conflict. 巴斯玛奇起义是一场复杂而残酷的冲突。| The Red Army struggled for years to suppress the Basmachi. 红军多年来一直在努力镇压巴斯玛奇。| synonyms: rebels, guerrillas, resistance fighters | antonyms: loyalists, collaborators | noun
