overfluency: /ˌoʊvərˈfluːənsi/ | n. | 过度流利；口若悬河 | Excessive fluency of speech, sometimes to the point of being incoherent or inappropriate.  It can manifest as rapid, pressured speech, often with tangential or irrelevant details, and can be a symptom of certain neurological or psychiatric conditions. |  His overfluency made it difficult to follow his train of thought.  他说话太流利了，让人很难跟上他的思路。 | synonyms: volubility, loquacity, garrulity | antonyms: hesitancy, reticence, taciturnity | noun


Etymology:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "fluency" (from Latin "fluens," present participle of "fluere" meaning "to flow").
