melodramatists:/ˈmɛlədrɑːmətɪsts/
|n.|夸张的剧作家；煽情剧演员|A melodramatist is a playwright or actor who specializes in melodrama, a dramatic genre characterized by exaggerated emotions, sensational plots, and stereotypical characters.|The melodramatists of the 19th century often portrayed villains as purely evil and heroes as impossibly virtuous.  19世纪的煽情剧作家通常将反派描绘成纯粹的邪恶，将英雄描绘成不可能的正直。|synonyms:  dramatists, playwrights, actors, performers|antonyms: realists, minimalists|noun
