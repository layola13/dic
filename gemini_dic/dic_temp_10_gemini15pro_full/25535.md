bullshitters:/ˈbʊlʃɪtərz/
n.
胡说八道的人；满嘴跑火车的人
People who habitually talk nonsense or make false claims, often to impress or deceive others.
Those bullshitters tried to convince us their product could cure cancer. 那些满嘴跑火车的人试图让我们相信他们的产品可以治愈癌症。

bullshitters:/ˈbʊlʃɪtərz/
近义词: liars, fabricators, deceivers, charlatans, frauds
反义词: truth-tellers, honest people


bullshitters:/ˈbʊlʃɪtərz/
词性: 名词 (plural)
词源:  Derived from "bullshit" (nonsense) + "-er" (suffix forming agent nouns).  "Bullshit" itself likely originated from the literal meaning of cow dung, evolving to represent worthless or deceptive talk.
