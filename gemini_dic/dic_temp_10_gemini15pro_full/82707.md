guaiacol:/ˈɡwaɪəkɒl/ (US) /ˈɡwaɪəˌkɒl/ (UK)| n.|愈创木酚;  邻甲氧基苯酚|A colorless, oily liquid with a smoky, aromatic odor, extracted from guaiacum resin or wood creosote, or prepared synthetically. It is used as an expectorant and antiseptic, and in the manufacture of vanillin.|Guaiacol is used in some cough syrups.|Synonyms:  o-methoxyphenol, 2-methoxyphenol|Antonyms: N/A|noun
