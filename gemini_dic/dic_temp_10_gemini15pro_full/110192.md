lieutenant:/luːˈtenənt/ (US), /lefˈtenənt/ (UK)| n.|中尉；副官；助理|A commissioned officer in the armed forces, ranking next below a captain or corresponding rank.  A deputy or substitute acting for a superior.|The lieutenant led his platoon into battle. 中尉带领他的排投入战斗。|Synonyms: deputy, assistant, second-in-command|Antonyms: commander, chief, leader|noun

lieutenant:/luːˈtenənt/ (US), /lefˈtenənt/ (UK)| n.|代理人；代表|Someone who acts in place of or represents another.|She served as the president's lieutenant during his absence.  她在他缺席期间担任总统的代理人。|Synonyms: representative, agent, proxy|Antonyms: principal, head, leader|noun
