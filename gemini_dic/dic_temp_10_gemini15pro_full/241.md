abdicator:/ˈæbdɪkeɪtər/| n.|退位者，放弃者|A person who abdicates a position of power or responsibility.  Someone who gives up a throne, right, claim, power, or the like. |The king, an abdicator, lived a quiet life after leaving the throne. 国王退位后过着平静的生活。

The abdicator of the company's CEO position surprised everyone. 公司CEO的退位者让所有人感到惊讶。|近义词：renouncer, forsaker, relinquisher|反义词：successor, inheritor|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *abdicātor*, from abdicō (“I renounce, disown, reject, abdicate”) +‎ -tor (“-er, one who”).  The verb abdicate comes from Latin abdicare, from ab- (“away, from”) + dicare (“to proclaim, dedicate”).
