croupade:/kruːˈpɑːd/ | n. |  腾跃; 后退跃 | A ballet step in which the dancer jumps straight into the air from a demi-plié and lands with both feet on the ground simultaneously in any position. | The dancer executed a perfect croupade.  这位舞者完美地完成了一个腾跃。 | synonyms:  cabriole,  sissonne | antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *croupade*, from *croup*, the rump, from Old Norse *kryppa*
