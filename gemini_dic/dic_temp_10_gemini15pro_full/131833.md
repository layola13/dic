nonconsoling:/ˌnɒnkənˈsəʊlɪŋ/
adjective
不令人安慰的，无法安慰的
Not providing comfort or solace; unable to be consoled.
The parents received the nonconsoling news that their missing child had been found deceased.  父母收到了令人悲痛的消息，他们失踪的孩子被发现已经死亡。
The doctor's prognosis was nonconsoling, leaving the patient with little hope.  医生的预后并不乐观，让病人几乎失去了希望。
Near Synonyms:  discouraging, disheartening, bleak, grim, distressing
Antonyms: comforting, consoling, reassuring, encouraging, hopeful
word origin:  "non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "consoling" (from "console," from Latin "consolari" - to comfort, solace)
