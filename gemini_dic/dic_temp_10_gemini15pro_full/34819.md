clinches:/ˈklɪntʃɪz/ /ˈklɪntʃəz/| v. (third-person singular simple present clinches, present participle clinching, simple past and past participle clinched)| 确定，最终获得；抱紧；敲弯钉尖| to confirm or settle (something) finally. To hold tightly. To bend over the point of (a nail, etc.)|  He clinched the victory with a final goal. 他以最后一粒进球锁定了胜局。| synonyms: secure, confirm, seal, settle, grasp, grip | antonyms: lose, forfeit, release, loosen | verb

clinch:/klɪntʃ/ /klɪntʃ/| n.| 敲弯钉尖；决定性因素；抱紧；牢牢抓住| the act of clinching something; something that clinches a matter.| The handshake was the clinch of the deal. 握手确定了这笔交易。| synonyms: embrace, grapple, hold, grip | antonyms: release | noun
