driftwood:/ˈdrɪftˌwʊd/| n. |浮木，漂流木|Wood that has been carried along by water or washed ashore.|例句：The beach was littered with driftwood. 海滩上到处都是浮木。|例句：We gathered some driftwood to build a fire. 我们收集了一些浮木来生火。|近义词：flotsam, jetsam|反义词: (none -  driftwood describes a specific type of wood, it doesn't have a direct opposite)|noun


driftpiece:/ˈdrɪftˌpiːs/| n. |漂流物，尤指漂流的木块；（比喻）无目的、无方向的人|A piece of wood, or other material, floating or washed ashore, especially by the sea; (figuratively) a person who drifts aimlessly.|例句：She felt like a driftpiece, tossed about by the currents of life. 她觉得自己像一块漂流木，被生活的潮流冲来冲去。|例句：The artist used driftpieces he found on the beach to create his sculptures.  这位艺术家用他在海滩上找到的漂流木创作雕塑。|近义词：driftwood, flotsam|反义词: (none - similar to driftwood, it lacks a direct opposite)|noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From drift (n.) + piece (n.).  "Drift" comes from Old Norse *drifa "to drive, push," related to Old English drīfan "to drive, expel." "Piece" comes from Old French piece "piece, portion," ultimately from Vulgar Latin *pettia, of unknown origin.
