disjunction:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋkʃən/| n.|分离，分裂，析取|a separation or division into two or more parts; (in logic) a compound proposition that is true if either or both of its component propositions are true|The disjunction between theory and practice is a common problem.|近义词：separation, division, split, disunion, (in logic) or|反义词：union, conjunction, connection|noun

disjunction:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋkʃən/| n.|逻辑析取|A logical operation that returns true if at least one of the operands is true.|The disjunction of "A" and "B" is true if either "A" is true, "B" is true, or both are true.|近义词：or, inclusive or|反义词：conjunction, and|noun
