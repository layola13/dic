conscripted:/kənˈskrɪptɪd/
adjective
被征召的，被强制入伍的
Enrolled compulsorily, especially into military service.
例句：The conscripted soldiers were young and inexperienced. 被征召的士兵都很年轻，缺乏经验。

例句：He felt resentful about being conscripted into the army. 他对被强制征召入伍感到愤慨。

近义词：drafted, enlisted, levied
反义词：volunteer, voluntary
词性: adjective

conscript:/ˈkɒnskrɪpt/
verb
征召，强制入伍
Enroll compulsorily, typically into military service.
例句：The government conscripted thousands of young men into the army. 政府征召了数千名年轻人入伍。

例句：They were conscripted to fight in the war. 他们被征召去打仗。

近义词：draft, enlist, levy
反义词：discharge, release
词性: verb

conscript:/ˈkɒnskrɪpt/
noun
被征召的士兵，新兵
A person compulsorily enrolled for military service.
例句：The conscripts were given basic training before being sent to the front. 新兵在被派往前线之前接受了基础训练。

例句：Many conscripts were barely old enough to shave. 许多新兵几乎还不到刮胡子的年龄。

近义词：draftee, recruit
反义词：veteran
词性: noun


词源:
From Latin conscriptus, past participle of conscribere ‘enroll’, from con- ‘together’ + scribere ‘write’.
