correlated: /ˈkɒrəleɪtɪd/ (UK), /ˈkɔːrəleɪtɪd/ (US) | adj. |相关的，关联的 | Statistically related; having a mutual relationship or connection. |  Example 1: The study found that income and education levels are strongly correlated. 这项研究发现，收入和教育水平密切相关。

Example 2:  The rise in crime was correlated with a decrease in police presence. 犯罪率的上升与警察人数的减少有关。 | 近义词: related, connected, associated, linked | 反义词: unrelated, independent, uncorrelated | 词性: adjective


correlate: /ˈkɒrəleɪt/ (UK), /ˈkɔːrəleɪt/ (US) | v. | 使相互关联；显示…相关的 | To establish a mutual relationship or connection between. | Example 1:  Researchers attempted to correlate drug use with criminal behavior. 研究人员试图将吸毒与犯罪行为联系起来。

Example 2: This data correlates climate change with rising sea levels.  这些数据将气候变化与海平面上升联系起来。 | 近义词: relate, connect, associate, link | 反义词: separate, disconnect | 词性: verb


correlation: /ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃn/ (UK), /ˌkɔːrəˈleɪʃn/ (US) | n. | 相互关系，关联 | A mutual relationship or connection between two or more things. | Example 1: There's a strong correlation between smoking and lung cancer. 吸烟和肺癌之间有很强的相关性。

Example 2:  The study explored the correlation between poverty and crime rates. 这项研究探讨了贫困与犯罪率之间的关系。 | 近义词: relationship, connection, association, link | 反义词: independence, separation | 词性: noun


词源: From the Late Latin word *correlātus*, past participle of *correlāre* ("to bring into reciprocal relation"), from *com-* ("together") + *relātus*, past participle of *referre* ("to carry back, relate").
