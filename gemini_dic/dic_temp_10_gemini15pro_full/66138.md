exceptionally:/ɪkˈsɛpʃənəli/ | adv. | 格外地，非常地，异常地 |  To a greater degree than usual; in an unusual way.  |  She is an exceptionally talented musician. 她是一位才华横溢的音乐家。 | extraordinarily, remarkably, unusually | ordinarily, typically, commonly | adverb

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *excepcioun*, from Old French *excepcion*, from Latin *exceptiō*, from *excipere* ("to take out, except"). The adverbial form derives from the adjective *exceptional* + *-ly*.
