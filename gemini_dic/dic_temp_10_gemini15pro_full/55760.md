diversifiabilities:/dɪˌvɜːrsɪˌfaɪəˈbɪlɪtiz/| n. | 多样化能力，可分散性 | The quality or state of being diversifiable; the extent to which something can be diversified.  This refers to the potential or capacity for diversification, particularly in investment or business contexts. It describes how easily or effectively assets, products, or operations can be spread across different areas to reduce risk or increase opportunities. |  The company's diversifiabilities allowed it to weather the economic downturn. 这家公司的多元化能力使其能够安然度过经济衰退。| Synonyms: flexibility, adaptability, versatility | Antonyms: inflexibility, rigidity, concentration | noun
