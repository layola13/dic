freemason:/ˈfriːmeɪsən/| n. |共济会会员，石匠|A member of a secret society, often associated with elaborate rituals and charitable work, with origins in stonemasonry.|例句：He was a Freemason and attended lodge meetings regularly. 他是一位共济会会员，定期参加地方分会集会。

例句：The Freemasons have a long and complex history. 共济会历史悠久而复杂。|近义词：Mason|反义词: (No direct antonym exists, potentially "non-Mason")|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fre mason*, meaning "free mason".  "Free" originally referred to the medieval stonemasons who were free to travel and work as opposed to serfs.  Later, the term evolved to describe members of the fraternal organization.
