lionlier:/ˈlaɪənliər/ (US) /ˈlaɪənliə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更孤独的，更寂寞的|Comparative form of lonely: more lonely.  Experiencing loneliness to a greater degree than someone or something else, or than before.|例句：She felt lionlier than ever after her children moved out. 她自从孩子们搬出去后感到比以往任何时候都更加孤独。

例句：The city seemed lionlier at night.  到了晚上，这座城市显得更加寂寞。|近义词：lonelier, more desolate, more isolated|反义词：less lonely, more sociable, more connected|词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *lonli, from Old English ānlic (“alone, solitary, single, unique”), equivalent to alone +‎ -ly. Cognate with Old Frisian ēnlik (“single, alone”), Old Saxon ēnlik (“single, similar”), Old High German einlīh (“single, unique, distinguished”), and Gothic 𐌰𐌹𐌽𐌻𐌴𐌹𐌺𐍃 (ainleiks, “single, unique”).  The comparative and superlative forms are analogical; the original forms were *onlier, *onliest.
