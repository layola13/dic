blackjacked:/ˈblækˌdʒækt/| v. |敲诈；胁迫；以武力强迫；|To force someone to do something by threatening them with violence or other harm.  The term originates from the card game blackjack, where a player can be "forced" to take another card (hit) or stand depending on the dealer's hand.  While it can literally refer to being robbed at gunpoint, it is often used figuratively to describe situations where someone feels pressured or coerced.|He felt blackjacked into signing the contract. 他感觉被迫签了合同。|Synonyms: coerce, extort, force, pressure, intimidate|Antonyms: persuade, encourage|verb

blackjacked:/ˈblækˌdʒækt/| adj. |被敲诈的；被胁迫的；|Describing someone who has been forced to do something against their will through threats or violence.|He felt completely blackjacked after they made him pay for the damages.  在他们强迫他赔偿损失后，他感到完全被胁迫了。|Synonyms: coerced, extorted, forced, pressured|Antonyms: willing, voluntary|adjective
