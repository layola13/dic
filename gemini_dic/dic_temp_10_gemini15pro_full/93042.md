idlest:/ˈaɪdlɪst/ | adj. | 最懒惰的; 最闲散的 |  Superlative form of idle: most idle.  Characterized by a lack of activity or employment; not busy or occupied. | He was the idlest boy in the class, always daydreaming. 他是班里最懒的孩子，总是做白日梦。 |  Synonyms: laziest, most inactive, most slothful | Antonyms: busiest, most active, most industrious | adjective

Etymology->
From Middle English *idellest, *idyllest, from Old English īdelest (“vainest, emptiest, most useless”), superlative form of īdel (“idle, empty, vain, useless”). More at idle.
