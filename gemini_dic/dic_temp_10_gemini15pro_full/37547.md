commonplace:/ˈkɒmənpleɪs/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmənpleɪs/ (US)| adj. n.|平凡的，普通的；陈词滥调|Something that is commonplace is ordinary and not interesting.  A commonplace remark or idea has been expressed so often that it is no longer interesting or effective. |The idea that we should all eat five portions of fruit and vegetables a day has become commonplace.  每天我们都应该吃五份水果和蔬菜的想法已经变得司空见惯了。|Synonyms: ordinary, mundane, banal, trite, clichéd|Antonyms: unusual, remarkable, extraordinary, original|adjective

commonplace:/ˈkɒmənpleɪs/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmənpleɪs/ (US)| n.|老生常谈；司空见惯的事|A commonplace is a remark or idea that has been expressed so often that it is no longer interesting or effective. |He regards the idea of human rights as a commonplace. 他认为人权理念是老生常谈。|Synonyms: cliché, banality, platitude, truism|Antonyms: novelty, originality|noun
