middle-class:/ˈmɪdl ˌklæs/ (US), /ˈmɪd(ə)l ˌklɑːs/ (UK)| adj. n.|中产阶级的；中产阶级|Relating to or typical of the social class between the upper and working classes, having a moderate income and standard of living.  As a noun, it refers to this social class itself.|例句：They live in a middle-class neighborhood. 他们住在中产阶级社区。|近义词：bourgeois, middle-income|反义词:working-class, upper-class|词性:adjective, noun
middle class:/ˈmɪdl ˌklæs/ (US), /ˈmɪd(ə)l ˌklɑːs/ (UK)| n.|中产阶级|The social class between the upper and working classes, typically with moderate incomes and standards of living.|例句：The middle class is shrinking. 中产阶级正在萎缩。|近义词：bourgeoisie|反义词:working class, upper class|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *middel classe*, equivalent to middle + class.  The term began to gain prominence in the 18th century, reflecting the growing social and economic complexity of industrializing societies.
