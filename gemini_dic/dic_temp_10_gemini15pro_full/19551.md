bifurcately: /baɪˈfɜːrkətli/ /baɪˈfɜːrkeɪtli/
| adv. |分叉地，成两叉地|In a bifurcated manner; dividing into two branches or forks. |The river bifurcates just north of the city, flowing bifurcately around the island.| 近义词：forkedly, branchingly |反义词:straight, linearly|词性:副词

bifurcate: /ˈbaɪfərkeɪt/ /ˈbaɪfɜːrkeɪt/
| v. |分叉，分成两支|To divide into two branches or forks. |The road bifurcates at the top of the hill.| 近义词：fork, branch, divide |反义词:merge, join, unite|词性:动词

bifurcate: /ˈbaɪfərkeɪt/ /ˈbaɪfɜːrkeɪt/
| adj. |分叉的，有分叉的|Divided into two branches or forks. |A bifurcate tree stood tall in the garden.| 近义词：forked, branched, divided |反义词:undivided, single, unified|词性:形容词

bifurcation: /ˌbaɪfərˈkeɪʃən/ /ˌbaɪfɜːrˈkeɪʃən/
| n. |分叉，分岔处|The point or action of dividing into two branches or forks. |The bifurcation of the river created two distinct channels.| 近义词：fork, branch, division |反义词:merging, junction, union|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin bifurcatus, past participle of bifurcare (“to divide into two forks”), from bi- (“two”) + furca (“fork”).
