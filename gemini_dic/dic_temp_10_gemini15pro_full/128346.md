neutralization:/ˌnjuːtrələˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 中和; 平息; 抵消 |The act or process of neutralizing something.  The process of making something neutral, such as making an acid or base into a neutral solution. |The neutralization of the acid with a base produced salt and water. 用碱中和酸生成了盐和水。| synonyms: counteracting, balancing, offsetting | antonyms: intensification, escalation, aggravation | noun

neutralize:/ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz/ | v. | 中和；使无效；使中立 | To make something neutral, especially a chemical substance; to counteract or cancel the effect of something. | The antibodies neutralize the toxins released by the bacteria. 抗体中和细菌释放的毒素。| synonyms: counteract, negate, annul | antonyms: activate, enhance, intensify | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *neutralisatio, from Latin neutralis (neutral) + -atio (forming nouns of action).
