palisade: /ˌpælɪˈseɪd/ | n. | 栅栏，篱笆；(解剖学)栅状组织 | A fence made of pointed wooden stakes fixed upright in the ground, typically used for defense. |  The castle was surrounded by a high palisade. 城堡周围环绕着高高的栅栏。|Synonyms: fence, barrier, barricade, stockade | Antonyms: opening, gap, breach | noun

palisade: /ˌpælɪˈseɪd/ | v. | 用栅栏围住 | To enclose or fortify with a palisade. |  They palisaded their camp against attack. 他们用栅栏围住营地以防袭击。| Synonyms: enclose, surround, fortify, fence in | Antonyms: open, expose | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *palissade, from palisser "to enclose with pales," from palis "stake, pale," from Latin pālus "stake."
