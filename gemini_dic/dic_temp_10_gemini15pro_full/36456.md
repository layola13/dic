cold-sweats:/koʊld swɛts/ | n. |冷汗|A state of profuse sweating, typically accompanied by a feeling of coldness, often caused by fear, anxiety, or illness.|  He woke up in cold sweats after having a nightmare. 他做噩梦后醒来浑身冷汗。|  The thought of public speaking gives me the cold sweats. 一想到要公开演讲我就浑身冒冷汗。| synonyms: chills, clamminess | antonyms: dryness, warmth | noun

cold:/koʊld/ | adj. |冷的；寒冷的|Having a low temperature.| It's a cold day today. 今天很冷。|  She gave him a cold stare. 她冷冷地瞪了他一眼。| synonyms: chilly, freezing, icy | antonyms: hot, warm | adjective

sweats:/swɛts/ | n. |汗水；出汗|The moisture exuded through the pores of the skin, typically as a result of heat, exertion, or stress.| He was drenched in sweats after his workout. 他锻炼后浑身是汗。|  The fever brought on night sweats. 发烧引起了盗汗。| synonyms: perspiration, moisture | antonyms: dryness | noun

Etymology:
"Cold sweat" combines "cold," from Old English "ceald," and "sweat," from Old English "swæt." The combination describes the physical sensation of sweating while feeling cold, often associated with fear or illness.
