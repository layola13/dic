brutalism: /ˈbruːtəlɪzəm/| n.|野兽派,粗野主义|A style of architecture characterized by massive, blocky forms, exposed concrete surfaces, and a lack of ornamentation.|The Barbican Centre in London is a classic example of brutalism.  伦敦的巴比肯中心是野兽派的典型例子。|The National Theatre is a striking example of brutalism. 国家剧院是野兽主义的一个引人注目的例子。|近义词:  functionalism, modernism|反义词: ornamentation, decoration|词性:noun

brutal: /ˈbruːtl/ | adj.|野蛮的，残忍的，粗暴的|Savagely violent, cruel, or harsh.|He was brutally murdered. 他被残忍地杀害了。|The weather was brutal. 天气非常恶劣。|近义词: savage, cruel, vicious, harsh|反义词: gentle, kind, humane|词性:adjective

brutally: /ˈbruːtəli/ | adv.|野蛮地，残忍地，粗暴地|In a savage, cruel, or harsh manner.|He was brutally honest. 他非常直白。|The truth was brutally obvious. 事实残酷地显而易见。|近义词: savagely, cruelly, viciously, harshly|反义词: gently, kindly, humanely|词性:adverb
