fretted:/ˈfrɛtɪd/| adj.|焦躁的，苦恼的；有弦品(指乐器)的|Worried and unhappy.  Having frets (metal bars on the neck of a stringed instrument). |例句：He was fretted by the constant noise. 他一直被持续不断的噪音烦扰。|近义词：worried, anxious, distressed, agitated, vexed, irritated,  (for instruments) strung|反义词：calm, relaxed, tranquil, serene, content, (for instruments) fretless|词性:形容词

fretted:/ˈfrɛtɪd/| v.| (fret的过去式和过去分词) 焦躁，烦躁，苦恼；(尤指反复地) 咬，啃|To be constantly or visibly worried or anxious.  To gnaw or bite persistently.|例句：She fretted about the children. 她为孩子们担忧。|近义词：worry, agonize, brood, stew, chafe, (for biting/gnawing) nibble, gnaw|反义词：relax, unwind, (for biting/gnawing) swallow|词性:动词

词源：From Old English *fretan "to devour, consume," from Proto-Germanic *fratan "gnaw, rub" (source also of Old Saxon fretan "devour," Old Frisian freta "gnaw," Dutch vreten "to devour," Old High German frezzan "to eat greedily," German fressen "to devour," Gothic fra-itan "to devour"). The meaning "to be worried" is from late 14c.  The noun meaning "a ridge on a fingerboard" is from 1580s.
