bad-mouther: /ˈbædˌmaʊðər/ | n. | 诽谤者，说坏话的人 | Someone who says bad things about other people. | He's such a bad-mouther, always gossiping about everyone. 他真是个爱说闲话的人，总是说每个人的坏话。| Synonyms: slanderer, gossipmonger, detractor | Antonyms: admirer, praiser, supporter | Noun

bad-mouth: /ˌbædˈmaʊθ/ | vt. | 说坏话，诋毁 | To criticize someone or something unfairly. |  She bad-mouthed her former employer to anyone who would listen. 她对任何愿意听的人诋毁她的前任雇主。 | Synonyms: criticize, denigrate, disparage, slander, malign | Antonyms: praise, commend, compliment | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "bad" + "mouth," literally meaning "bad mouth."  The verb form "bad-mouth" appeared first in the late 19th century, with the noun "bad-mouther" following in the early 20th century.  The term refers to someone who uses their mouth to speak negatively or harmfully about others.
