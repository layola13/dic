goodwill:/ˈɡʊdˈwɪl/| n. | 商誉；友好；善意 |  the established reputation of a business regarded as a quantifiable asset and calculated as the difference between the price paid for the business and the fair market value of its tangible assets; friendly, helpful, or cooperative feelings or attitude. |  Example 1: The company acquired a significant amount of goodwill through its excellent customer service. (该公司通过其优质的客户服务获得了大量的商誉。)

Example 2:  He bears no ill will towards his former rivals. (他对以前的对手没有恶意。) | 近义词: benevolence, kindness, amity | 反义词: hostility, animosity, ill will | 词性: 名词
