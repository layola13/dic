co-optative:/koʊˈɑːptətɪv/
adjective
选择性的;  可选择的; (语法) 愿望的
Expressing or indicating a wish or desire. In grammar, the co-optative mood expresses a wish.
例句：He used the co-optative mood to express his longing for peace. 他用愿望语气表达了他对和平的渴望。

例句：The co-optative form of the verb expresses a desire or hope.  动词的愿望式表达了一种愿望或希望。
近义词：desiderative, optative
反义词: imperative, mandatory
词性: adjective

co-opt:/koʊˈɑːpt/
verb
增选；指派；吸收；拉拢
To appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members.  To adopt (an idea, plan, etc.) for one's own use.
例句：The committee co-opted several new members. 委员会增选了几名新成员。

例句：They co-opted the proposal and presented it as their own. 他们盗用了这个提议，并把它当作自己的提议提出。
近义词：appropriate, assimilate, incorporate
反义词: exclude, reject
词性: verb

co-option:/koʊˈɑːpʃən/
noun
增选；指派；吸收；拉拢
The action or process of appointing a person to a position of authority or membership, especially by invitation. The adoption of an idea or policy for one's own use.
例句：The co-option of several new members strengthened the committee. 增选几名新成员加强了委员会的力量。

例句：The co-option of their ideas by the rival company was a serious blow.  竞争对手公司盗用他们的想法是一个严重的打击。
近义词：appropriation, assimilation, incorporation
反义词: exclusion, rejection
词性: noun
