lollardism:/ˈlɒlərdɪzəm/ |n.|罗拉德派；罗拉德主义|A religious movement in 14th- and 15th-century England, led by John Wycliffe, that advocated church reform and challenged the authority of the Pope.|Lollardism was a significant precursor to the Protestant Reformation.| 近义词: Wycliffism, heresy (from the perspective of the Catholic Church) | 反义词: Catholicism, orthodoxy (from the perspective of the Catholic Church) |词性: noun
