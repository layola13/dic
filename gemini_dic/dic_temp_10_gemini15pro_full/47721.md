decadentism:/dɪˈkædənˌtɪzəm/| n.|颓废主义，颓废派|A movement in late 19th-century literature and art characterized by a rejection of conventional morality and a fascination with the morbid and artificial.|The novel explores themes of decadentism and the decline of aristocratic society. 这部小说探讨了颓废主义和贵族社会衰落的主题。|近义词：degeneration, decline, decay|反义词:  vitality, growth, flourishing|词性: noun

decadence: /ˈdekədəns/ | n. | 颓废，衰落 | Moral or cultural decline as characterized by excessive indulgence in pleasure or luxury. | The Roman Empire fell into decadence before its eventual collapse.  罗马帝国在最终崩溃前陷入了颓废。 | 近义词： corruption, depravity, dissipation | 反义词:  virtue, morality, austerity | 词性: noun

decadent: /ˈdekədənt/ | adj. | 颓废的，衰落的 | Characterized by or reflecting a state of moral or cultural decline. |  They lived a decadent lifestyle, filled with parties and extravagance. 他们过着颓废的生活，充满了派对和奢侈。 | 近义词： degenerate, corrupt, dissipated | 反义词:  wholesome, virtuous, austere | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *décadentisme*, from *décadent* ("decadent"), from Latin *dēcadēns*, present participle of *dēcadere* ("to decay, fall down"), from *de-* ("down") + *cadere* ("to fall").
