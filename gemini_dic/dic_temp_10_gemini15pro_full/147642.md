overpolished: /ˌoʊvərˈpɑːlɪʃt/ | adj. | 过度抛光的；过分修饰的；矫揉造作的 | Polished to an excessive degree; often implying a loss of naturalness or character. |  Her overpolished prose felt artificial and lacking in genuine emotion. 她过度修饰的散文感觉很做作，缺乏真情实感。 | The salesman's overpolished shoes and slicked-back hair made him seem untrustworthy.  推销员过度擦亮的鞋子和梳得油光锃亮的后背头发让他看起来不值得信任。 | 近义词：overrefined, excessively polished, slick, contrived | 反义词：rough, natural, unpolished, artless | 词性：形容词

词源: over- + polished.  "Over-" acts as a prefix indicating excess, while "polished" comes from the verb "polish," ultimately from Old French "polir," meaning "to smooth, polish."
