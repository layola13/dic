flashier:/ˈflæʃiər/ | adj. | 更浮华的，更闪亮的 | More ostentatious or showy; more brightly colored or decorated. |  She wore a flashier dress than usual to the party. 她穿了一件比平时更华丽的连衣裙去参加聚会。| synonyms: gaudier, showier, more flamboyant, more ostentatious | antonyms: simpler, plainer, more understated, less showy | adjective

flashy:/ˈflæʃi/ | adj. | 浮华的，闪亮的 | Ostentatious or showy; brightly colored or decorated. | He drives a flashy sports car. 他开着一辆炫目的跑车。| synonyms: gaudy, showy, flamboyant, ostentatious | antonyms: simple, plain, understated, subtle | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From flash (v.) + -y (suffix forming adjectives meaning 'having the quality of').  The comparative form "flashier" is formed by adding "-er" to "flashy".  "Flash" itself originates from Old Norse *flasa "to burst forth, dash."
