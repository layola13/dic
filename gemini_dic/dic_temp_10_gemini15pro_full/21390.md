blotting:/ˈblɒtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈblɑːtɪŋ/ (US)| n. |吸墨，印迹，污点|The act of absorbing excess liquid, especially ink, using an absorbent material. Also, a mark or stain made by such an action. |She used blotting paper to dry the ink. 她用吸墨纸吸干了墨水。

The wine left a dark blot on the tablecloth.  葡萄酒在桌布上留下了一个深色的污点。|近义词：absorbing, soaking, staining, marking|反义词：spreading, wetting|词性：noun


blotting:/ˈblɒtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈblɑːtɪŋ/ (US)| adj. |吸墨的|Used to describe paper or other material that absorbs ink.  |Blotting paper is essential for calligraphy. 吸墨纸对于书法来说是必不可少的。|近义词：absorbent|反义词：non-absorbent|词性：adjective (less common)


词源: From the verb "blot," which comes from Middle English *blotten*, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Old Norse *blotna* "to become soft."
