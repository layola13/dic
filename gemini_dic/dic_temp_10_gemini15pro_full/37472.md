commissions:/kəˈmɪʃənz/| n. |佣金，委托，委任，犯罪|a sum of money that is paid to someone for selling goods and which is directly related to the amount of goods sold;  the act of asking someone to do a piece of work for you; the official authority to perform a task or certain duties; the act of committing a crime |She gets a 15 percent commission on every machine she sells. 她每卖出一台机器就能得到15%的佣金。|Synonyms: fee, payment, reward, charge, delegation, assignment, perpetration|Antonyms:  omission, prohibition, prevention|noun

commission:/kəˈmɪʃən/| v. |委托，委任|to formally ask someone to write or create something or to do a task for you|The newspaper commissioned a series of articles on the worst excesses of the fashion industry. 报社委托撰写了一系列关于时装业最过度行为的文章。|Synonyms: authorize, engage, appoint, hire, employ|Antonyms: dismiss, fire, discharge|verb
