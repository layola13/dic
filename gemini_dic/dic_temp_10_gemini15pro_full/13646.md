attorn:/əˈtɜːrn/ | v. | 承认，同意，转让 | To agree to transfer one's tenancy or vassalage to a new landlord or overlord.  To transfer (one's loyalty, allegiance, etc.) to another. | The tenant attorned to the new landlord after the property was sold.  租户在房产出售后向新房东表示承认。 He attorned his allegiance to the new king. 他宣布效忠新国王。 | 近义词: transfer, acknowledge, assent | 反义词: deny, refuse, reject | verb

attornment:/əˈtɜːrnmənt/ | n. | 承认，同意，转让 | The act of attorning.  The acknowledgment by a tenant of a new landlord. | The attornment of the tenants was necessary to complete the sale of the property.  租户的承认对于完成房产出售是必要的。 The king demanded the attornment of all his vassals. 国王要求所有封臣的效忠。 | 近义词: transfer, acknowledgment, assent | 反义词: denial, refusal, rejection | noun
