calflike:/ˈkæflaɪk/| adj.|小牛般的，像小牛的|Resembling a calf, especially in being awkward or clumsy.|Her calflike legs struggled to keep up with the others. 她的小牛般的腿努力跟上其他人。|Synonyms: awkward, clumsy, ungainly, gawky|Antonyms: graceful, elegant, agile, coordinated|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From calf + like.  Calf (meaning the young of a cow) comes from Old English *cealf, from Proto-Germanic *kalbaz. Like comes from Old English *gelīc, from Proto-Germanic *galikaz.
