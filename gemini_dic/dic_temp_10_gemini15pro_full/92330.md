hypnotized:/ˈhɪpnətaɪzd/| adj.|被催眠的；着迷的|Being in a state of hypnosis; captivated or fascinated to the point of obsession.|例句：The audience was hypnotized by the magician's performance. 观众被魔术师的表演迷住了。

例句：She was hypnotized by the flickering flames of the fire. 她被闪烁的火焰迷住了。

|近义词：mesmerized, entranced, captivated, spellbound|反义词：alert, aware, conscious|词性:形容词


hypnotize:/ˈhɪpnətaɪz/| vt.|催眠；使着迷|To induce a state of hypnosis; to fascinate or captivate.|例句：The therapist hypnotized the patient to help her overcome her fear. 治疗师催眠病人以帮助她克服恐惧。

例句：The beauty of the landscape hypnotized me.  美丽的风景使我着迷。

|近义词：mesmerize, entrance, captivate, spellbind|反义词：awaken, arouse|词性:动词


词源: From French *hypnotiser*, from Greek *hypnōtizein*  "put to sleep," from *hypnos* "sleep."
