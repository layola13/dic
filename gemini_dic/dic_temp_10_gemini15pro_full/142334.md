orthogeosyncline:/ˌɔːrθoʊˈdʒiːoʊˌsɪŋklaɪn/ | n. | 正地槽 | A large linear downwarp in the Earth's crust, believed by some geologists to be a site of mountain building.  A major geosyncline that is supposed to be associated with relatively thin continental crust and unrelated to continental platforms. |  The Appalachian Mountains were formed from an orthorgeosyncline. 阿巴拉契亚山脉是由一个正地槽形成的。| synonym: geosyncline | antonym: geanticline | noun
