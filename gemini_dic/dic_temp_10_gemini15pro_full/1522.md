acetylene:/əˈsɛtɪˌlin/ | n. |乙炔|A colorless, highly flammable gas, C2H2, used as a fuel in welding and cutting, and as a starting material in chemical synthesis.  It is the simplest alkyne.|Welders use acetylene for cutting steel.  焊工使用乙炔切割钢材。|Near synonyms:  ethyne (its IUPAC name), alkyne|Antonyms: N/A (gases don't typically have direct antonyms)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *acétylène*, coined by French chemist Marcellin Berthelot in 1864, from *acétyl-* (acetyl group) + *-ène* (suffix forming names of hydrocarbons).  The acetyl group itself comes from Latin *acetum* "vinegar" (acetic acid being a component of vinegar) + *-yl* (chemical suffix).
