aaka:/ˈɑːkɑː/
noun
elder brother (in Hawaiian)
A term of respect and endearment used to address or refer to an older male, particularly a brother, but also sometimes a close male friend.  It is derived from the Hawaiian language.
Example: My aaka always looked out for me. 我的哥哥总是照顾我。
Example: He greeted his friend, "Aloha, aaka!"  他向他的朋友打招呼：“你好，兄弟！”
Synonym: brother, bra, cuz (informal)
Antonym: younger brother
