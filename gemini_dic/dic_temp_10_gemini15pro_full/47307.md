de-intensifies:/ˌdiːɪnˈtɛnsɪfaɪz/
|v.|降低强度，缓和|Reduces the intensity of something; makes something less intense or severe.|The new policy de-intensifies the focus on standardized testing.|近义词:mitigates, moderates, softens, alleviates, lessens|反义词:intensifies, escalates, aggravates, exacerbates|词性:verb

词源:
"De-" (prefix): From Latin, meaning "down, away from, off, reversal, removal, separation."
"Intensify": From "intense" (Latin "intensus," meaning "stretched out, strained, violent") + "-ify" (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or cause to be").
