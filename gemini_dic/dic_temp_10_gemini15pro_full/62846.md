enjoin:/ɪnˈdʒɔɪn/| vt.|命令；禁止；嘱咐|To instruct or urge (someone) to do something. To prohibit or restrain by a judicial order.|The judge enjoined the defendant from contacting the victim. 法官命令被告不得联系受害人。|Synonyms: command, order, direct, forbid, prohibit, proscribe|Antonyms: allow, permit|verb

enjoin:/ɪnˈdʒɔɪn/| vt.|命令；禁止；嘱咐|To prescribe (an action or attitude) with authority; impose.|The doctor enjoined a strict diet on the patient. 医生嘱咐病人要严格控制饮食。|Synonyms: prescribe, impose, dictate|Antonyms: allow, permit|verb
