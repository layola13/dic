exuberant: /ɪɡˈzjuːbərənt/ /ɪɡˈzuːbərənt/ | adj. | 充满活力的；热情洋溢的；兴高采烈的 | Filled with or characterized by a lively energy and excitement. |  The children were exuberant when they opened their presents. 孩子们打开礼物时兴高采烈。| synonyms: lively, enthusiastic, energetic, high-spirited, elated, exhilarated | antonyms: subdued, restrained, quiet, listless, apathetic | adjective

exuberance: /ɪɡˈzjuːbərəns/ /ɪɡˈzuːbərəns/ | n. |  活力；热情洋溢；兴高采烈 | The quality of being full of energy, excitement, and cheerfulness. | Her exuberance is infectious. 她的热情很有感染力。 | synonyms: liveliness, enthusiasm, energy, high spirits, elation, exhilaration | antonyms: subduedness, restraint, quietness, listlessness, apathy | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *exuberant- (stem of exuberans), present participle of exuberare "to be abundant, overflow," from ex- "out" + uber "fertile, fruitful, copious."
