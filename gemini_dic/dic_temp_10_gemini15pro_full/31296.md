chaplaincies: /ˈtʃæplɪnsiz/
|n.| 牧师职位；牧师的职务；牧师工作|The position or office of a chaplain, or the provision of chaplaincy services.  The work or ministry of a chaplain.  | Many hospitals and universities offer chaplaincies. 许多医院和大学都提供牧师职位。|Synonyms: chaplain posts, chaplain services, spiritual guidance programs|Antonyms: secular positions, non-religious services|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From chaplaincy (singular), derived from chaplain + -cy (suffix denoting office, rank, or position). Chaplain is from Old French chapelain, ultimately from Late Latin capellanus "guardian of the cloak of St. Martin," from cappella "chapel," diminutive of cappa "cloak."
