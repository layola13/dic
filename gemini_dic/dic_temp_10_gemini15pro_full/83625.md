haemocyanin:/ˌhiːməˈsaɪənɪn/ | n. |血蓝蛋白|A type of respiratory pigment found in the blood of some invertebrates, notably mollusks and arthropods. It contains copper and appears blue when oxygenated.|The horseshoe crab's blood contains haemocyanin, which makes it appear blue.|Synonyms:  respiratory pigment|Antonyms: None|noun
