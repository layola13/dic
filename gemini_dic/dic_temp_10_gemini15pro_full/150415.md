panselectionist:/ˌpænɪˈlɛkʃənɪst/| n. |泛选择论者|A proponent of panselectionism, the view that natural selection is the primary driving force of all evolutionary change.  It emphasizes the role of natural selection in shaping all aspects of biological organisms, often minimizing the role of other evolutionary mechanisms like genetic drift or neutral mutations. |Richard Dawkins is often considered a panselectionist, arguing that natural selection is the dominant force in evolution. 理查德·道金斯通常被认为是泛选择论者，他认为自然选择是进化中的主导力量。

The panselectionist view has been criticized for oversimplifying the complexities of evolution. 泛选择论的观点因其过度简化了进化的复杂性而受到批评。 |近义词：adaptationist, selectionist|反义词：neutralist|词性: noun
