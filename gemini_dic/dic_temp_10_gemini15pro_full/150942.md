paralyser:/ˈpærəlaɪzər/ (UK) /ˈpærəˌlaɪzər/ (US)| n.|瘫痪者，麻痹者；使瘫痪或麻痹的东西|Something that paralyses or causes paralysis. |The venom of the snake acted as a paralyser, quickly immobilising its prey.  蛇的毒液起到麻痹作用，迅速使其猎物动弹不得。|Synonyms: disabling agent, immobilizer|Antonyms: stimulant, energizer|noun


paralyse:/ˈpærəlaɪz/ (UK) /ˈpærəˌlaɪz/ (US)| vt.|使麻痹，使瘫痪；使丧失作用；使惊呆|To cause (a person or part of the body) to lose the ability to move; to make something unable to function normally; to make someone unable to act or react properly, usually because of fear or shock.|The accident paralysed him from the waist down. 事故导致他腰部以下瘫痪。|Synonyms: disable, cripple, immobilize, incapacitate, stun, numb|Antonyms: enable, empower, invigorate, stimulate|verb
