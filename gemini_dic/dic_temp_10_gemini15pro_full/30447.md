centreboard: /ˈsɛntərbɔːrd/ (US) /ˈsɛntəbɔːd/ (UK)| n. |中心板，活动龙骨|A retractable keel that pivots up and down, used in sailboats to provide stability and reduce leeway.  It's housed in a slot or well in the hull.|The centreboard was lowered as the boat headed out into open water. 中心板在船驶入开阔水域时被降下。|Synonyms: daggerboard, drop keel, lifting keel|Antonyms: fixed keel|noun
