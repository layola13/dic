paramitome: /ˌpærəˈmɪtəʊm/ | n. |基因组旁系同源物;旁系同源基因组|The paramitome refers to the set of genes that are found in close proximity to a particular gene of interest, often within the same chromosomal region.  These genes may be functionally related or co-regulated with the gene of interest.| The researchers studied the paramitome of the BRCA1 gene to identify potential modifiers of breast cancer risk. 研究人员研究了BRCA1基因的旁系同源基因组，以确定乳腺癌风险的潜在修饰因子。|  Synonyms: genomic neighborhood, gene cluster | Antonyms: N/A | Noun
