ligger:/ˈlɪɡə(r)/| n.|懒汉，躺着不做事的人；长期住院病人；长期领取失业救济金的人|Someone who stays in bed longer than usual, especially in the morning; A person who spends their time doing nothing useful or productive; A person who has been in a hospital or other institution for a long time; Someone who is habitually unemployed and receives government benefits.|He's such a ligger on weekends, never getting out of bed before noon. 他周末真是个懒虫，不到中午不起床。|Synonyms: loafer, idler, slacker, layabout|Antonyms: worker, hustler|noun

ligger:/ˈlɪɡə(r)/| n.|长期系泊的船；长期积压的货物|A ship that has been moored or anchored in the same place for a long time;  Goods that have remained unsold or unused for a long time.|The old barge was a ligger in the harbor, slowly rusting away. 那艘旧驳船是港口里的长期系泊船，正在慢慢生锈。|Synonyms: (for ship) hulk, (for goods) dead stock|Antonyms: (for ship) active vessel, (for goods) fast-moving consumer goods|noun
