corporalship:/ˈkɔːrpərəlʃɪp/| n.|下士职务；下士职位|The position or rank of a corporal.  It refers to the state of being a corporal.  It can also encompass the responsibilities and duties associated with that rank.|He was promoted to corporalship after his bravery in battle. 他在战斗中的英勇表现使他被提升为下士。|Synonyms: rank, position, grade|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, consider demotion or lower ranks like private)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "corporal" + "-ship".  "Corporal" comes from Old French "corporal" (bodily), from Latin "corporalis", from "corpus" (body).  The suffix "-ship" denotes a state, condition, or office.
