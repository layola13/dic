fluked:/fluːkt/ | adj. | 侥幸的；意外的；偶然的 | Achieved or acquired by chance rather than skill or effort. | He made a fluked shot and won the game. 他侥幸射门赢得了比赛。 | synonyms: lucky, fortunate, accidental, chance | antonyms: intentional, deliberate, planned | adjective

fluked:/fluːkt/ | v. | (fluke 的过去式和过去分词) 侥幸成功；意外获得 | Achieved or acquired something by chance rather than skill or effort (past tense). | He fluked the exam despite not studying. 尽管没有学习，他侥幸地通过了考试。| synonyms: chanced upon, stumbled upon, lucked out, | antonyms: earned, deserved, achieved through hard work | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From fluke (n.) meaning "a stroke of luck," of uncertain origin, possibly related to Dutch "vlok" meaning "flake, tuft."  The verb sense developed from the noun.
