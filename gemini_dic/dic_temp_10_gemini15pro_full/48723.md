defilade:/ˌdefɪˈleɪd/ | n. vt.|散兵坑，掩体；保护，使免受炮火|A position sheltered from enemy fire.|The troops were defiladed behind a ridge. 部队在山脊后面得到了掩护。|  The engineers defiladed the position by building earthworks. 工程师们通过修筑土方工事来保护阵地。|近义词：protect, shield, cover, shelter|反义词：expose|词性: 名词, 动词


Etymology:
From French *défiler*, "to march in file," from Italian *sfilare*, "to unthread, unravel," from *s-* (out) + *filare*, "to spin."  The military sense developed from the idea of troops marching in a narrow, protected formation.
