mardus: /ˈmɑːrdəs/ | n. | 恶棍; 流氓; 坏蛋 | A rogue or villain. | He was a mardus, always getting into trouble. 他是个恶棍，总是惹麻烦。|  The local mardus was known for his petty thefts. 当地的流氓以小偷小摸而闻名。 | 近义词: rogue, villain, scoundrel, rascal | 反义词: hero, saint | 词性: 名词


词源:  Likely a variant of "mardy," a British dialect word meaning "spoiled, sulky, or bad-tempered," possibly influenced by words like "marauder."  "Mardy" itself may have connections to the French word "marri," meaning "sad" or "sorrowful."
