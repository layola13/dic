cavatina:/ˌkævəˈtiːnə/ | n. | 小夜曲，抒情短曲 | A short, simple melody, often of a sentimental character.  A type of short song, usually for one voice, that is part of a larger work such as an opera or oratorio or is a stand-alone piece. | The opera features a beautiful cavatina in the second act.  这部歌剧的第二幕有一段优美的小夜曲。|  aria, arietta, canzonetta | symphony, sonata | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *cavatina*, diminutive of *cavata* ("melody, air, song"), from *cavare* ("to dig out, extract").  Ultimately from Latin *cavare* ("to hollow").
