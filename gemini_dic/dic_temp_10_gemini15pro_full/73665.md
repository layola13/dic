forger's:/ˈfɔːrdʒərz/
noun
伪造者(的),  
belonging to or associated with a forger.  A forger is someone who creates fake versions of something valuable, such as documents, money, or art, with the intent to deceive.  The word "forger's" is the possessive form, indicating ownership or association.
The forger's tools were confiscated by the police.  伪造者的工具被警方没收了。
The forger's skill was undeniable, even though his intentions were criminal.  即使伪造者的意图是犯罪，他的技艺也不可否认。
Synonyms: counterfeiter's, fabricator's
Antonyms: authenticator's, verifier's
POS: noun (possessive)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French forgier "to forge, fabricate," from Late Latin fabricare "to construct, manufacture," from Latin fabrica "workshop, something skillfully made," from faber "workman, craftsman."
