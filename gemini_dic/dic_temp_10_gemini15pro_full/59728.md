eclogue: /ˈɛklɒɡ/ (UK), /ˈɛklɔːɡ/ (US) | n. | 牧歌; 田园诗 | A short poem, especially one written in a classical style, idealized and descriptive of rural life. |  Virgil wrote a number of eclogues celebrating the simple life of shepherds. 维吉尔写了很多歌颂牧羊人简朴生活的牧歌。


Example:  Her latest collection of poetry includes several beautiful eclogues. 她最新的诗集中包含几首优美的田园诗。

Example:  The eclogue traditionally depicts shepherds engaged in a singing contest.  传统上，田园诗描绘了牧羊人参加歌唱比赛的情景。

Synonym:  pastoral, idyll, bucolic

Antonym:  epic, tragedy

Part of Speech: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *ecloga, from Greek eklogē "selection, choice, extract", from eklegein "to pick out, select", from ek "out" + legein "to gather, speak". Originally referred to a select or choice piece of writing.
