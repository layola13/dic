imbankment: /ɪmˈbæŋkmənt/ | n. |堤防，路堤| A wall or bank of earth or stone built to prevent a river flooding an area.  A raised mass of earth or other material, typically used to support a roadway or railway track. |  The railway runs along a high embankment overlooking the valley.  铁路沿着俯瞰山谷的高高的路堤延伸。| synonyms: levee, dike, bank, rampart | antonyms: ditch, channel | noun

imbankment: /ɪmˈbæŋkmənt/ | n. |筑堤，堤岸| The action of embanking something, especially a river.  The side of a river, canal, etc., especially one that has been artificially raised or strengthened.| The embankment of the river prevented further flooding.  该河的堤岸防止了进一步的洪水泛滥。| synonyms:  embanking, reinforcement | antonyms: erosion, excavation | noun
