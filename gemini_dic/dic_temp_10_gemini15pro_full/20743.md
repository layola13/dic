blanched:/blɑːntʃt/ /blæntʃt/| adj.|变白的，(脸色)苍白的，漂白的，(杏仁等)去皮的| having lost color, especially because of shock or fear; (of almonds, etc.) having had the skin removed |例句：Her face blanched when she heard the news. 听到这个消息，她的脸变得苍白。|例句：Blanched almonds are often used in baking.  去皮杏仁经常用于烘焙。|近义词：pale, white, ashen, bleached|反义词：flushed, ruddy, tanned|词性:形容词

blanch:/blɑːntʃ/ /blæntʃ/| v.| (使)变白，(使脸色)苍白，漂白，(用沸水)烫(蔬菜) | to become pale, typically through shock or fear; whiten (something) by applying bleach or boiling; prepare (vegetables) for freezing or further cooking by immersing briefly in boiling water|例句：He blanched at the sight of so much blood. 看到这么多血，他吓得脸色发白。|例句：The chef blanched the green beans before sautéing them. 厨师在炒青豆之前先将它们焯水。|近义词：pale, whiten, bleach, scald|反义词：flush, redden, darken|词性:动词
