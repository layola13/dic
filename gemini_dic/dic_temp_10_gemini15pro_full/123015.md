monastic:/məˈnæstɪk/ | adj. | 修道的；僧侣的；禁欲的 | Relating to monasteries, monks, nuns, or their way of life; characterized by self-denial and seclusion. |  He led a monastic life devoted to prayer and study. 他过着一种致力于祈祷和学习的修道生活。

| The monastic order followed strict rules of silence and fasting. 这个修道会遵循严格的沉默和禁食规则。

| 近义词: ascetic, cloistered, contemplative, religious | 反义词: secular, worldly, lay | 词性: adjective

monastic:/məˈnæstɪk/ | n. | 修士；修女 | A person who lives a monastic life, such as a monk or nun. (Less common usage) |  The monastery housed several monastics. 这座修道院里有好几位修士/修女。(较少使用)

|  She became a monastic after years of searching for spiritual meaning. 在多年寻求精神意义之后，她成了一名修女。(较少使用)

| 近义词: monk, nun, friar | 反义词: layman, laywoman | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *monasticus, from Greek monastikos "solitary," from monastes "monk, hermit," from monazein "to live alone," from monos "alone, single."
