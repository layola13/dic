alcoholist: /ˈælkəhɑːlɪst/ | n. | 酒鬼；酗酒者 | A person who is addicted to alcohol and suffers from alcoholism.  A person who habitually drinks alcoholic beverages to excess. | He admitted to being an alcoholic and sought help. 他承认自己是酒鬼并寻求帮助。| My uncle is a recovering alcoholic. 我的叔叔是一个正在康复的酒鬼。| 近义词: drunkard, inebriate, dipsomaniac | 反义词: teetotaler, abstainer | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From alcohol + -ist.  "Alcohol" comes from Medieval Latin *alcohol, from Arabic *al-kuḥl "kohl, fine metallic powder used as eye makeup," which acquired the meaning "essence, pure spirit" and was then applied to the distilled spirit of wine.  "-ist" is a suffix forming agent nouns and nouns of personal occupation.
