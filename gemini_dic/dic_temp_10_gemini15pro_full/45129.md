cud:/kʌd/ | n. | 反刍的食物 | Partly digested food returned from the first stomach of ruminants to the mouth for further chewing. | The cow chewed its cud contentedly.  奶牛心满意足地嚼着它的反刍食物。 |Synonyms: bolus, chew | Antonyms: N/A | Noun

cud:/kʌd/ | n. | (非正式) 嚼，思考 | (informal) The act of chewing. Often used figuratively to mean thinking something over. | He sat chewing his cud, pondering the problem. 他坐着思考，琢磨着这个问题。| Synonyms: chew, ponder, ruminate | Antonyms: N/A | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cudu (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *kuzdō (compare West Frisian kodde “cud,” Dutch kodde “fiber, clot,” German Koder “bait”), from Proto-Indo-European *geu- “to chew.”
