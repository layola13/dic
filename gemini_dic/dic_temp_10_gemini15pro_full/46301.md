d-structure:/ˈdiː strʌktʃər/ | n. | 深层结构 |  In linguistics, particularly in generative grammar, D-structure (deep structure or underlying structure) is an abstract syntactic representation of a sentence. It represents the basic semantic relations between the constituents of a sentence, before transformations such as movement and deletion have applied. | The d-structure of "John was seen by Mary" would show John as the object of "see". |  underlying structure, base structure | surface structure | noun
