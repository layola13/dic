amygdala: /əˈmɪɡdələ/ (US), /əˈmɪɡdələ/ (UK)| n. |杏仁核；扁桃体|A roughly almond-shaped mass of gray matter inside each cerebral hemisphere, involved with the experiencing of emotions.|The amygdala plays a key role in processing fear and other emotions. 杏仁核在处理恐惧和其他情绪方面起着关键作用。| synonyms:  tonsillar nucleus| antonyms: None (There is no single anatomical structure that serves as a direct opposite to the amygdala.)| noun

amygdala: /əˈmɪɡdələ/ (US), /əˈmɪɡdələ/ (UK)| n. |杏仁核；扁桃体|An almond-shaped neural structure in the medial temporal lobe of the brain linked to the processing and memory of emotional reactions.|Damage to the amygdala can impair the ability to recognize fear in others.  杏仁核受损会损害识别他人恐惧的能力。| synonyms:  tonsillar body| antonyms: None| noun
