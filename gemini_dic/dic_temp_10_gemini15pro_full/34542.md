cleanwood:/ˈkliːnwʊd/ | n. |清洁木材；干净的木材|Wood that is free from dirt, debris, and pollutants.  It can refer to wood that has been specifically cleaned or wood from a sustainably managed forest.| The carpenter used cleanwood to build the furniture. 木匠用干净的木材制作家具。| The company only uses cleanwood sourced from sustainable forests. 这家公司只使用来自可持续管理森林的清洁木材。 |近义词：untreated wood, pure wood|反义词: dirty wood, contaminated wood |词性: 名词

Etymology:
"clean" (adj.) Old English *clæne "clean, pure, unblemished, innocent," from Proto-Germanic *klainja- (source also of Old Frisian klene "small, slender, fine, pure," Dutch klein "small," Old High German chlini "fine, elegant, pure, small," German klein "small, little"), from PIE root *glei-no-, from *gel- "bright, shining."

"wood" (n.) Old English wudu "wood, forest, grove, timber; wood, firewood," from Proto-Germanic *widu- (source also of Old Saxon wudu, Old Norse viðr, Old Frisian wud, Middle Dutch wout, Dutch hout "wood," Old High German witu, German Holz), from PIE *widhu- "wood, tree" (source also of Sanskrit vidhuh "tree, log").
