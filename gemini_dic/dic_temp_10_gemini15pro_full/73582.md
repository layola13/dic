foretack: /ˈfɔːrtæk/ | n. | 前下支索，迎风帆桁的支索 | A rope used to hold down the lower forward corner of a sail, especially a jib or staysail. | The foretack snapped under the strain of the strong wind.  强风拉断了前下支索。 | The sailor adjusted the foretack to optimize the sail's shape. 水手调整了前下支索以优化帆的形状。 | synonyms: downhaul | antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "halyard" as it pulls upward) | noun
