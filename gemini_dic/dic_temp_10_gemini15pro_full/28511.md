carders:/ˈkɑːrdərz/
n.
盗刷信用卡的人；梳毛工人
People who steal credit card information and use it fraudulently; Workers who card wool.
Carders often operate in organized crime rings. 盗刷信用卡的人经常在有组织的犯罪团伙中活动。
The carders worked diligently to prepare the wool for spinning.  梳毛工人努力地准备用于纺纱的羊毛。
近义词:fraudsters, scammers (for credit card thieves); wool combers (for wool workers)
反义词:(No direct antonym.  Could consider "law enforcement" for credit card thieves, perhaps "consumers" as the opposite of producers)
词性:名词

carder:/ˈkɑːrdər/
n.
盗刷信用卡的人；梳毛工人；梳棉机
A person who steals credit card information and uses it fraudulently; A worker who cards wool; A machine for carding fibers.
The police arrested the carder for stealing credit card numbers. 警察逮捕了那个盗刷信用卡的人，因为他窃取信用卡号码。
The carder separated the fibers, making them ready for spinning. 梳棉机分离纤维，使它们准备好纺纱。
近义词:fraudster, scammer (for credit card thieves); wool comber (for wool workers)
反义词:(No direct antonym.  Could consider "law enforcement" for credit card thieves)
词性:名词

词源: From the verb "to card," meaning to comb or disentangle fibers like wool or cotton.  This verb's meaning was then extended to the fraudulent use of credit cards, likely because the process of stealing and using stolen card information was seen as a kind of "combing" or sorting through data.
