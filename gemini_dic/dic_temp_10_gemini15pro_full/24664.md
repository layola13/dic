bromide:/ˈbrəʊmaɪd/ (US) /ˈbroʊmaɪd/| n.|溴化物；陈词滥调；镇静剂|A binary compound of bromine with another element or radical. A commonplace or hackneyed statement; a platitude. A sedative.|

例句：
The doctor prescribed a bromide to calm her nerves.
医生给她开了溴化物来镇定她的神经。

He bored everyone with his bromides about the importance of hard work.
他用关于努力工作重要性的陈词滥调让每个人都感到厌烦。

近义词：platitude, cliché, commonplace, banality, sedative, tranquilizer|
反义词：originality, novelty, stimulant|
词性：noun


bromides:/ˈbrəʊmaɪdz/ (US) /ˈbroʊmaɪdz/| n.|溴化物；陈词滥调；镇静剂（复数）|Plural form of bromide.  A binary compound of bromine with another element or radical. A commonplace or hackneyed statement; a platitude. A sedative.|

例句：
She offered a few bromides about the importance of staying positive.
她说了几句关于保持积极重要性的陈词滥调。

These bromides are no longer effective in calming her anxiety.
这些镇静剂对她缓解焦虑已经不再有效了。

近义词：platitudes, clichés, commonplaces, banalities, sedatives, tranquilizers|
反义词：originalities, novelties, stimulants|
词性：noun


词源: From *brom- (stem of bromine) + -ide (chemical suffix).  The figurative sense of "platitude" arose from the use of potassium bromide as a sedative in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
