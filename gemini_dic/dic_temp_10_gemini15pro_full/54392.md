disillusive:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːsɪv/ | adj. | 令人失望的；醒悟的；使人幻想破灭的 | Causing disappointment or disillusionment; tending to shatter illusions. | The disillusive experience of war made him a pacifist.  战争令人失望的经历使他成为了一名和平主义者。|  disappointing, disillusioning, disenchanting |  encouraging, inspiring, heartening | adjective

disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/ | n. vt. | 失望；幻灭；醒悟 |  A feeling of disappointment resulting from the discovery that something is not as good as one believed it to be; to free from illusion or self-deception. | The reality of the job was a stark disillusion after the exciting promises of the interview. 这份工作的现实与面试时令人兴奋的承诺相比，令人大失所望。 | disappointment, disenchantment |  hope, belief, illusion | noun, verb
