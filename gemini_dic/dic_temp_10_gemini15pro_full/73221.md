forces:/ˈfɔːrsɪz/ (US) /ˈfɔːsɪz/ (UK)| n.|力量；武力；军队；势力|A force is any interaction that, when unopposed, will change the motion of an object. In other words, a force can cause an object with mass to change its velocity (which includes to begin moving from a state of rest), i.e., to accelerate. Force can also be described intuitively as a push or a pull.|The forces of nature can be devastating. 自然的力量可以是毁灭性的。|synonyms: strength, power, might, energy|antonyms: weakness, impotence, powerlessness|noun

force:/fɔːrs/ (US) /fɔːs/ (UK)| n. vt.|力量；武力；军队；势力；强迫|As a noun, force refers to strength or power exerted on an object. As a verb, it means to compel someone to do something against their will.|He used force to open the door. 他用力打开了门。|synonyms: compel, coerce, oblige, press|antonyms: allow, permit, release, persuade|noun, verb
