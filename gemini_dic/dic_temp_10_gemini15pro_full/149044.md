own-to:/oʊn ˈtuː/| phrasal verb |承认，坦白；归功于| To admit something is true or that one has done something wrong; to attribute something to someone or something. | She owned up to breaking the vase. 她承认打碎了花瓶。|近义词：admit, confess, concede, attribute |反义词：deny, refuse, disavow |词性：动词短语

词源: "Own" comes from Old English "āgan," meaning "to possess." "To" is a preposition indicating direction or connection.  The combination "own to" developed the sense of acknowledging possession of a fault or responsibility.
