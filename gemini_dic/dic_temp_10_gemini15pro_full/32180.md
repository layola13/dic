chiaroscurist:/ˌkɪɑːrəˈskjʊərɪst/ | n. | 明暗对照画家；明暗对照艺术爱好者 | An artist who uses chiaroscuro.  | Caravaggio was a master chiaroscurist, renowned for his dramatic use of light and shadow. 卡拉瓦乔是一位明暗对照大师，以其对光影的戏剧性运用而闻名。 | Rembrandt is another famous example of a chiaroscurist.  伦勃朗是明暗对照画法的另一个著名例子。 |  synonym: painter, artist | antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "colorist" for someone who emphasizes color over light and shadow) | Noun

chiaroscuro:/ˌkɪɑːrəˈskjʊəroʊ/ | n. | 明暗对照法；明暗对比 | The treatment of light and shade in drawing and painting. | The chiaroscuro in this painting creates a sense of depth and drama. 这幅画中的明暗对比营造出一种深度和戏剧性的感觉。 |  The artist's skillful use of chiaroscuro brought the figures to life.  艺术家熟练地运用明暗对比法，使人物栩栩如生。| synonym: light and shade, contrast | antonym: flat lighting, even illumination | Noun
