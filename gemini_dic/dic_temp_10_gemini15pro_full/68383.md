fallouts:/ˈfɔːlˌaʊts/| n.| 附带后果，余波；放射性尘埃 |Unforeseen consequences or side effects of an event or action, often negative; radioactive particles dispersed by a nuclear explosion.|例句：The political fallouts of the scandal were significant. 这起丑闻的政治后果很严重。

例句：The fallout from the Chernobyl disaster spread across Europe. 切尔诺贝利灾难的放射性尘埃散布到整个欧洲。|近义词：repercussions, consequences, after-effects, aftermath|反义词:benefits, advantages|词性:名词

词源: From "fallout," originally referring to the radioactive particles falling to earth after a nuclear explosion.  Extended figuratively to any kind of negative consequence.
