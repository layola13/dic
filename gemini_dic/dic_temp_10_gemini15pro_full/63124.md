enslavements:/ɪnˈsleɪvmənts/
|n.|奴役，束缚|The enslavements of African people is a dark chapter in human history. 非洲人民的奴役是人类历史上黑暗的一章。|The system led to the enslavements of generations. 这个制度导致了几代人的奴役。|近义词：bondage, servitude, captivity, oppression|反义词：freedom, liberation, emancipation|词性: 名词

词源:  From enslave + -ment.  "Enslave" comes from the Old French word "esclaver," meaning "to reduce to slavery."  The root is ultimately from the Medieval Latin "sclavus," meaning "Slav," as many Slavic peoples were enslaved in the early Middle Ages.
