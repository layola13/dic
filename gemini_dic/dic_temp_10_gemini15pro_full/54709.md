disoriented:/dɪsˈɔːriənˌteɪtɪd/
adjective
迷失方向的，不知所措的
Confused and not knowing where to go or what to do.
The jet lag left her feeling completely disoriented.  时差让她完全迷失了方向。
He woke up in the hospital, disoriented and confused. 他在医院醒来，感到迷失方向和困惑。
近义词: confused, bewildered, lost, perplexed
反义词: oriented, clearheaded, aware

词性: adjective

词源: dis- (not) + orient (to align or position oneself) + -ed (past participle suffix)  来自拉丁语 "oriens" (rising, east).  最初的意思是失去方向感，特别是指失去东方方向。
