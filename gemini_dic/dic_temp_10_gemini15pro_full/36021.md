codillion:/kəˈtɪljən/ |n.|科蒂利昂舞；方形舞；四对方舞|A brisk, lively dance for four couples, popular in 18th- and 19th-century Europe and America.|The debutantes practiced their codillion for weeks before the ball. |初次参加社交活动的年轻女子在舞会前几周练习科蒂利昂舞。|近义词:quadrille, square dance|反义词:None|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cotillon*, alteration of *cotillion*, from Old French *cote*, "skirt, petticoat."  The dance may have been named after the women's skirts swirling during the dance.
