chaps:/tʃæps/| n.| 皮套裤；家伙（尤指男人或男孩）|Leather leggings worn over trousers by cowboys to protect their legs. Also used informally to refer to men or boys.|例句：The cowboys wore chaps to protect their legs from the brush. 牛仔们穿着皮套裤来保护他们的腿免受灌木丛的伤害。

例句：Those chaps look like they're having a good time. 那些家伙看起来玩得很开心。|近义词：fellows, guys, blokes (informal)|反义词: (No direct antonym, depends on the meaning used.  Could be women/girls if referring to men/boys.)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *chaparreras*, from *chaparro* meaning "low-growing, dense evergreen oak," hence the dense brush such leg coverings would protect against.
