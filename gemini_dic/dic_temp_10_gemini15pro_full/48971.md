degeneration:/dɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən/ | n. | 退化；恶化；堕落 |  A decline or deterioration from a former or original state, either physical, mental, or moral. | The degeneration of the city's infrastructure was evident in the crumbling roads and bridges. 城市基础设施的退化在摇摇欲坠的道路和桥梁上显而易见。|  decline, deterioration, decay, worsening | improvement, development, progress | noun

degenerated:/dɪˈdʒɛnəreɪtɪd/ | adj. | 退化的；堕落的 | Having declined or deteriorated from a former or original state. | The degenerated condition of the building made it unsafe to live in.  这栋建筑退化的状况使其不适合居住。| decayed, deteriorated, corrupt | improved, developed, enhanced | adjective

degenerate:/dɪˈdʒɛnəreɪt/ | v. | 退化；堕落 | To decline or deteriorate from a former or original state. | His eyesight began to degenerate as he got older.  随着年龄的增长，他的视力开始退化。 | worsen, decline, deteriorate | improve, develop, enhance | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin degenerātiō, from Latin degenerāre (“to depart from one's kind, degenerate”), from dē- + genus (“race, stock, kind”).
