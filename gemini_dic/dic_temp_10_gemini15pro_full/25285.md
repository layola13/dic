bufonite:/ˈbjuːfəˌnaɪt/ | n. | 水牛石 | A variety of calcite pseudomorph after aragonite, typically occurring as brownish or greyish concretions.  It resembles buffalo horns in shape, hence the name. | The museum displayed a collection of bufonites, some of which were remarkably large.  博物馆展出了一系列水牛石，其中一些非常大。 | Example: Some people believed bufonites were magical stones.  有些人认为水牛石是魔法石。 | Synonym: concretion, nodule | Antonym: N/A | noun
