frantically:/ˈfræntɪkli/ | adv. | 疯狂地，狂乱地 | In a distraught way owing to fear, anxiety, or other emotion. | She searched frantically for her missing keys. 她疯狂地寻找她丢失的钥匙。| Example: He paced frantically while waiting for the news. 他焦急地踱步等待消息。| Synonyms: desperately, wildly, hysterically, frenetically | Antonyms: calmly, peacefully, quietly | 词性: 副词

frantic:/ˈfræntɪk/ | adj. | 疯狂的，狂乱的 | Wild or distraught with fear, anxiety, or other emotion. |  She became frantic when she realized her child was missing. 她意识到孩子失踪时变得疯狂起来。| Example: He made a frantic attempt to escape the fire. 他疯狂地试图逃离火灾。 | Synonyms: frenzied, distraught, desperate, hysterical | Antonyms: calm, composed, relaxed, peaceful | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *frénétique* (frentic), from Late Latin *phreneticus*, from Greek *phrenetikos* ("suffering from delirium"), from *phrenitis* ("inflammation of the brain").  Ultimately from Greek *phrēn* ("mind").
