exalt:/ɪɡˈzɔːlt/ | v. | 高举，提升；赞扬，歌颂 | To raise in rank, power, or character; to praise highly. | The king was exalted to a position of supreme power. 国王被提升至最高权力地位。

The poem exalts the beauty of nature. 这首诗歌颂了大自然的美丽。 | 近义词：praise, elevate, glorify, laud | 反义词：debase, degrade, humble | 词性:动词

exalted:/ɪɡˈzɔːltɪd/ | adj. | 高贵的，崇高的；兴奋的，得意洋洋的 | Raised in rank, character, or status; filled with a sense of grandeur. |  She felt an exalted sense of joy after winning the competition.  赢得比赛后，她感到无比喜悦。

He holds an exalted position in the company. 他在公司担任要职。 | 近义词：noble, lofty, elevated, majestic | 反义词：lowly, humble, base | 词性:形容词

词源:  From Latin exaltare "to raise up, elevate," from ex- "up" + altus "high."
