bedjacket:/ˈbedˌdʒækɪt/| n.|床罩衫，短睡衣|A short, lightweight jacket worn in bed, typically over nightclothes.|She wore a pretty floral bedjacket over her nightgown.|Near synonyms: dressing gown, housecoat, robe, peignoir|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps outdoor clothing)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "bed" + "jacket". "Bed" comes from Old English "bedd",  from Proto-Germanic *badjaz, meaning a place to sleep. "Jacket" comes from Middle French "jaquet", a diminutive of "jaque", a type of tunic, ultimately of uncertain origin.
