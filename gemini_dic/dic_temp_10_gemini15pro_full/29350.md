castom: /ˈkæstəm/ (This appears to be a misspelling.  Assuming the intended word is "custom") | n. adj.| 习俗，惯例；定制的|A traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something that is specific to a particular society, place, or time. Also refers to something made or done to order for a particular customer.|  例句：It is a local custom to celebrate the harvest.  庆祝丰收是当地的习俗。

例句：She had a custom-made dress for the wedding. 她为婚礼定制了一件礼服。|近义词：tradition, habit, practice, bespoke, tailor-made|反义词: unconventional, non-traditional|词性: noun, adjective

Etymology:  From Old French *custume*,  from Latin *consuetudo* "habit, usage, custom," from *consuescere* "accustom," from *con-* "together" (see con-) + *suescere* "become accustomed," related to *suus* "one's own."
