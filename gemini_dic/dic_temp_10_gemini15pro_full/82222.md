grottoes:/ˈɡrɑːtəʊz/ (US), /ˈɡrɒtəʊz/ (UK)| n. |洞穴，石窟|A natural or artificial cave, typically one that is large and impressive or decorated with carvings or paintings. |例句：The grottoes of Lascaux contain some of the most famous prehistoric cave paintings. 拉斯科的石窟包含一些最著名的史前洞穴壁画。

例句：The monks built grottoes in the hillside for meditation. 僧侣们在山坡上建造了石窟用于冥想。|近义词：caves, caverns|反义词: N/A|词性:noun
