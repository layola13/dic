chantership:/ˈtʃæntəʃɪp/
n.
圣歌队领唱的职位；领唱的职务
The position or office of a chanter.  The leadership of a choir or group of singers, especially in a religious context.
He was appointed to the chantership of the cathedral. 他被任命为大教堂的领唱。
近义词:  choirmaster, precentorship, leadership (of a choir)
反义词:  (No direct antonym, consider perhaps "followership" in a choir context)
词性: noun

Etymology:
From "chanter" + "-ship."  "Chanter" comes from Old French "chanteor," from Late Latin "cantator," from Latin "cantare" (to sing).  "-ship" is an Old English suffix denoting state, condition, or office.
