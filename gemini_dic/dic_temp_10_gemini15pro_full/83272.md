gymnosophy:/ˌdʒɪmnɒˈsɒfi/ | n. | 裸体修行；禁欲主义 | the practice or doctrine of the gymnosophists; ascetic principles or practices. Gymnosophy refers to the philosophies and practices of ancient Indian ascetics who lived naked or with minimal clothing, emphasizing meditation, self-denial, and spiritual purification. |  Example 1:  The gymnosophy of the ancient sages emphasized a life of extreme austerity. 古代圣贤的裸体修行强调极端禁欲的生活。

Example 2:  His commitment to gymnosophy led him to renounce all worldly possessions and live a life of solitude in the forest. 他对禁欲主义的奉献使他放弃了所有的世俗财产，在森林里过着孤独的生活。 | Synonym: asceticism, austerity, self-denial | Antonym: hedonism, indulgence, luxury | noun
