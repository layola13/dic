hedgers:/ˈhedʒərz/| n.| 套期保值者；避险者 |  People or institutions who use financial strategies to reduce the risk of loss from adverse price movements. | Example: Hedgers often use futures contracts to lock in prices. 套期保值者经常使用期货合约来锁定价格。 Example:  The company acted as a hedger in the currency market. 该公司在外汇市场上充当套期保值者。 | Synonyms: speculators (in some contexts), investors (in some contexts), arbitrageurs (in some contexts) | Antonyms:  gamblers (in some contexts) | Noun

hedger:/ˈhedʒər/| n.| 套期保值者；避险者 | A person or institution that hedges. | Example: The hedger sought to minimize their exposure to market volatility. 套期保值者试图将他们对市场波动的风险敞口降到最低。 Example:  Experienced hedgers understand the complexities of the derivatives market.  经验丰富的套期保值者了解衍生品市场的复杂性。| Synonyms: speculator (in some contexts), investor (in some contexts), arbitrageur (in some contexts) | Antonyms: gambler (in some contexts) | Noun
