nerve-racking: /ˈnɜːrvˌrækɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人紧张的，令人焦虑的 |  Causing a lot of worry or anxiety. It's formed from "nerve" and "racking," where "racking" refers to a feeling of being stretched or tortured. |  Waiting for the results was a nerve-racking experience. 等待结果是一段令人焦虑的经历。 | stressful, anxious, tense | calming, relaxing | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From nerve + racking.  "Racking" comes from the verb "rack," meaning to torture or torment.  The sense of extreme mental stress is an extension of this physical meaning.
