nonsinkable:/ˌnɒnsɪŋkəbl/ /ˌnɑːnsɪŋkəbl/| adj.|不会沉没的；不可摧毁的|Not able to sink; incapable of being sunk. Figuratively, indestructible or resilient.|The Titanic was famously deemed “nonsinkable,” a claim that proved tragically false.  泰坦尼克号曾被誉为“永不沉没”，这一说法后来被证明是悲剧性的错误。|近义词：unsinkable, indestructible, buoyant, resilient|反义词：sinkable, vulnerable, fragile|词性: 形容词

nonsinkable:/ˌnɒnsɪŋkəbl/ /ˌnɑːnsɪŋkəbl/| n.|不沉之物|Something that cannot sink.|While technically not a standard noun form, it can be used in context like: "The ship was considered a nonsinkable." 这艘船被认为是不会沉没的。|近义词：unsinkable (as a noun), lifeboat (in some contexts)|反义词：N/A (rarely used as a noun)|词性: 名词 (rarely used)

Etymology:  "non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "sinkable" (from "sink" + "-able" suffix meaning "capable of being"). The word gained prominence after the sinking of the Titanic.
