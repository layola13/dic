excoriations:/ˌekskɔːriˈeɪʃənz/
n.
擦伤；剥落；苛责；痛骂
scratches or abrasions on the skin's surface; harsh criticism or denunciation.
The cat's constant scratching resulted in excoriations on its neck.  这只猫不停地抓挠导致它的脖子上出现了擦伤。
His review contained harsh excoriations of the director's work. 他的评论对导演的作品进行了严厉的斥责。
近义词:abrasions, scratches, lacerations, criticisms, condemnations, denunciations
反义词:praises, commendations, compliments
词性:noun


excoriation:/ˌekskɔːriˈeɪʃən/
n.
擦伤；剥落；苛责；痛骂
an act or instance of excoriating; the state of being excoriated.
The hiker suffered an excoriation on his leg from a fall.  徒步旅行者因摔倒腿部擦伤。
The politician's excoriation of his opponent was met with applause.  这位政治家对对手的痛斥赢得了掌声。
近义词:abrasion, scratch, laceration, criticism, condemnation, denunciation
反义词:praise, commendation, compliment
词性:noun

词源: From Latin excoriatus, past participle of excoriare (“to flay, skin”), from ex- (“out of, from”) + corium (“hide, skin”).
