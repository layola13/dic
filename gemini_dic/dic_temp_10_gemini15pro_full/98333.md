insinuant:/ɪnˈsɪnjuːənt/ | /ɪnˈsɪnjuːɪt/ | adj. | 谄媚的，暗示的，曲意逢迎的 | Suggesting or implying something bad or unpleasant in an indirect way |  He made an insinuant remark about her past. 他含沙射影地说了些关于她过去的事。| 近义词: suggestive, allusive, hinting,  innuendo, sly, subtle | 反义词: direct, forthright, frank | 词性: 形容词

insinuate:/ɪnˈsɪnjuːeɪt/ | v. |  暗示，巧妙地插入，使潜入  | To suggest or hint (something bad or reprehensible) in an indirect and unpleasant way; to introduce (something) gradually or in a subtle, indirect, or covert way | He insinuated that she had cheated on the exam. 他暗示她在考试中作弊了。| 近义词: imply, suggest, hint, intimate | 反义词: declare, state, assert | 词性: 动词

词源: Late Middle English: from Latin insinuare, from in- ‘into’ + sinuare ‘to wind, curve’ (from sinus ‘curve, fold’).
