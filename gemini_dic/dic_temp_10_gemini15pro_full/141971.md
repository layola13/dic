orewood:/ˈɔːrˌwʊd/ | n. | 矿木;矿石木 |  Wood that is petrified or mineralized, often containing valuable ores. | The miners discovered a vein of orewood rich in silver. 矿工们发现了一条富含银的矿石木矿脉。 | 近义词: petrified wood, mineralized wood | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "common wood") | 词性: noun

Etymology:  "ore" + "wood". "Ore" comes from Old English *ār, meaning "brass, copper, metal". "Wood" comes from Old English wudu, meaning "wood, forest".
