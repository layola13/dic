cato:/ˈkeɪtoʊ/ /ˈkɑːtoʊ/ | n. | 加图（罗马政治家、作家） |  Marcus Porcius Cato (234-149 BC) was a Roman statesman and writer known for his conservatism and opposition to Carthage.  | Cato the Elder was a prominent figure in Roman history. 老加图是罗马历史上杰出的人物。| Cato the Younger followed in his great-grandfather's footsteps. 小加图继承了他曾祖父的衣钵。| synonym: statesman, moralist | antonym: radical, hedonist | noun
