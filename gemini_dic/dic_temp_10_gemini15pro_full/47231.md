dazzled: /ˈdæzəld/ (US), /ˈdæzld/ (UK) | adj. | 眼花缭乱的，耀眼的 |  (of a person) made unable to see properly for a short time by a very bright light; extremely impressed by something |  The sunlight dazzled me as I stepped outside.  我走到外面时，阳光让我眼花缭乱。 | synonyms: blinded, amazed, awestruck, impressed, overwhelmed | antonyms: unimpressed, bored, indifferent | adjective

dazzle: /ˈdæzl/ (US), /ˈdæzl/ (UK) | v. | 使眼花缭乱，使目眩 | to impress somebody a lot with your skill, qualities, etc. |  He was dazzled by her beauty. 他被她的美貌所倾倒。 | synonyms: amaze, impress, overpower, fascinate | antonyms: bore, disappoint, disillusion | verb
