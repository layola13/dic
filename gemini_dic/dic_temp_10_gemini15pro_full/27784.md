cannicula:/kəˈnɪkjʊlə/ | n. | 天狼星；伏天；酷暑 |The period of greatest heat, usually occurring in the Northern Hemisphere in late July and early August.  It originally referred to the period when Sirius, the Dog Star, rises with the sun. | The canicula is the hottest part of the summer.  伏天是一年中气温最高的时期。| synonyms: dog days, heatwave | antonyms: winter, cold snap | noun


canicular:/kəˈnɪkjʊlər/ | adj. |  天狼星的；盛暑的；酷热的 | Relating to the dog days or the hottest part of the summer. |  The canicular weather made it difficult to work outdoors. 酷热的天气使得户外工作变得困难。 | synonyms: sultry, torrid, scorching | antonyms: frigid, arctic, wintry | adjective


Etymology:
From Latin canicula ("little dog," diminutive of canis, "dog"), referring to Sirius, the Dog Star, in the constellation Canis Major.  The Romans believed that the combined heat of the sun and Sirius caused the hottest weather.
