germinal:/ˈdʒɜːrmɪnəl/
adjective
萌芽的;  初期发展的;  创始的
Relating to or occurring in the early stages of development.
例句：The germinal phase of pregnancy is the first two weeks after conception.  怀孕的萌芽期是指受孕后的前两周。

例句：The artist's germinal ideas for the masterpiece came to him in a dream.  这位艺术家创作杰作的萌芽想法来自他的一个梦。
近义词：embryonic, nascent, incipient, budding
反义词：developed, mature, ripe
词性: adjective


germinal:/ˈdʒɜːrmɪnəl/
noun
胚芽;  起源
The origin or earliest form of something. (Often used in a biological context, but can also be used figuratively.)
例句：The germinal center of a lymph node is where B cells proliferate and differentiate.  淋巴结的生发中心是B细胞增殖和分化的地方。

例句：The book contained the germinal ideas that would later become a major social movement. 这本书包含了后来发展成为一场重大社会运动的萌芽思想。
近义词：seed, origin, root, beginning
反义词：end, conclusion, result
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *germinalis, from Latin germen ("bud, sprout") + -alis (adjective suffix).
