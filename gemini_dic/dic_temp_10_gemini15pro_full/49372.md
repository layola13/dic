delirium:/dɪˈlɪriəm/ /dəˈlɪriəm/| n. |谵妄，神智错乱|A state of mental confusion and excitement characterized by disorientation, hallucinations, and incoherent speech. |The high fever brought on a state of delirium. 高烧引起谵妄状态。| 近义词: insanity, madness, psychosis | 反义词: lucidity, sanity | 词性: 名词

deliracy:/dɪˈlɪrəsi/ /dəˈlɪrəsi/| n. |谵妄，精神错乱|The state of being delirious; wild excitement or emotion.|He was in a state of deliracy after the accident. 事故发生后，他处于谵妄状态。|近义词: frenzy, mania, hysteria |反义词: calmness, lucidity, sanity | 词性: 名词

词源: Late Middle English: from late Latin deliria, plural of delirium, from Latin delirare ‘be crazy’, from de- ‘away from’ + lira ‘furrow, track’ (in the sense ‘go off the straight track’).
