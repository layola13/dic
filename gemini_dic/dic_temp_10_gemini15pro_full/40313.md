contortionistic:/kənˌtɔrʃəˈnɪstɪk/ | adj. | 柔术的；扭曲的 |  Relating to contortion or contortions; involving or characterized by the twisting and bending of the body into unusual positions. |  The contortionistic act was both impressive and slightly disturbing.  柔术表演令人印象深刻，但也略微令人不安。|  flexible, supple, acrobatic, agile, limber | stiff, rigid, inflexible | adjective


contortionist:/kənˈtɔrʃənɪst/ | n. | 柔术演员；扭曲的人 | A person who practices contortion, the art of twisting and bending the body into unusual positions. | The contortionist amazed the audience with her incredible flexibility.  柔术演员以其惊人的柔韧性令观众惊叹。 | acrobat, gymnast, performer | rigid person | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From contortion + -istic. Contortion from Late Latin contortio, contortion-, from contortus, past participle of contorquēre "to twist, wrench," from com- "together" + torquēre "to twist."
