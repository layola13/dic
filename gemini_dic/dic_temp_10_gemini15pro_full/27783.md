cannibal:/ˈkænɪbəl/ | n. |食人者；吃同类的动物|A person who eats human flesh.  A person or animal that eats its own kind. |The tribe was rumored to be cannibals.  据传闻，这个部落是食人族。|The praying mantis is a cannibalistic insect. 螳螂是一种吃同类的昆虫。|  synonyms:  man-eater, anthropophagite | antonyms: vegetarian, herbivore | noun

词源： From Spanish *caníbal*, from Carib *caniba* (“Carib, Cannibal”), via French *cannibale*.  Ultimately from the Taíno word *cariba* meaning "bold" or "fierce".  The Spanish misunderstood the term to refer to the Carib people, who were believed to practice cannibalism.
