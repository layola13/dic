exarate:/ˈɛksəreɪt/| adj.|裸露的，无壳的，羽化后的|Emerging from the pupal case; having the pupal skin shed.  Used primarily in entomology.|The exarate pupa of the beetle wriggled free of its pupal casing.|近义词：eclosed, emerged|反义词: pupated, encased|词性: 形容词

Etymology: From Latin *exaratus*, past participle of *exarare* "to plow up, scratch out," from *ex-* "out" + *arare* "to plow."  The entomological sense refers to the insect emerging from its pupal casing as if it were plowed or scratched out.
