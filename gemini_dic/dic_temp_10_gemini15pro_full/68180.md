faintly:/ˈfeɪntɪŋli/ | adv. | 微弱地，模糊地，隐约地 | To a small degree; not strongly. |  He could hear music faintly coming from the next room. 他能隐隐约约地听到隔壁房间传来的音乐声。 | faintly, slightly, dimly, weakly | strongly, clearly, distinctly | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From faint + -ly.  "Faint" comes from Old French *feint, past participle of feindre "to feign, pretend," from Latin fingere "to shape, form, invent, devise, feign."
