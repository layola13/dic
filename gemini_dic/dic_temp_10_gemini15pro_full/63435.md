entreatful: /ɪnˈtriːtfəl/ | adj. | 恳求的，哀求的 | Expressing entreaty; pleading; beseeching. |  She cast an entreatful glance at her father, hoping he would change his mind. 她恳求地看了她父亲一眼，希望他能改变主意。|  He made an entreatful plea for mercy. 他恳求怜悯。 | synonyms: pleading, beseeching, imploring, supplicating | antonyms: demanding, commanding, assertive | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From entreat + -ful.  "Entreat" comes from Old French "entraitier"  meaning "to treat, handle, deal with," later "to beg, beseech," from "en-" (in) + "traitier" (to treat). "-ful" is a suffix meaning "full of" or "characterized by."
