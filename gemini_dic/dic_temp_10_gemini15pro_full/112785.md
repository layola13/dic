lubricator:/ˈluːbrɪkeɪtər/| n.|润滑器，润滑剂，加油器|A device or substance used to apply a lubricant to reduce friction between moving parts.|The mechanic used a lubricator to oil the engine. 技工用润滑器给发动机加油。|Synonyms: oiler, greaser|Antonyms: desiccant, abrasive|noun

lubricate:/ˈluːbrɪkeɪt/| vt.|润滑，加润滑油|To apply a lubricant to something in order to reduce friction.|He lubricated the bicycle chain. 他给自行车链条上了油。|Synonyms: oil, grease|Antonyms: dry, roughen|verb

lubricant:/ˈluːbrɪkənt/| n.|润滑剂，润滑油|A substance, such as oil or grease, used to reduce friction between moving parts.|The lubricant helped the machine run smoothly. 润滑剂帮助机器平稳运转。|Synonyms: oil, grease|Antonyms: desiccant, abrasive|noun

lubrication:/ˌluːbrɪˈkeɪʃən/| n.|润滑，上油|The action or process of applying a lubricant.|Regular lubrication is essential for the proper functioning of machinery. 定期润滑对机器的正常运转至关重要。|Synonyms: oiling, greasing|Antonyms: drying|noun

lubricative:/ˈluːbrɪkətɪv/| adj.|润滑的，有润滑作用的|Having the quality of reducing friction.|The lubricant had a lubricative effect on the engine. 润滑剂对发动机有润滑作用。|Synonyms: slippery, oily|Antonyms: dry, rough|adjective
