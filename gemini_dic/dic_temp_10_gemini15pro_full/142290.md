orthid:/ˈɔːrθɪd/| n. |正形贝类，小嘴贝类|An extinct articulate brachiopod of the order Orthida, common in Palaeozoic rocks, having a shell with a straight hinge line and prominent teeth.  This group is characterized by their biconvex shells, a wide hinge line, and well-developed teeth and sockets.|The museum displayed several well-preserved orthid fossils from the Devonian period.  博物馆展出了几块保存完好的泥盆纪正形贝化石。|近义词:brachiopod|反义词:N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ortho- (straight, correct) + -id (suffix denoting a member of a group or family), referring to the straight hinge line characteristic of these brachiopods.  Ultimately from Greek "orthos" (straight).
