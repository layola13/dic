caballed:/kəˈbæld/
| v. |密谋，秘密策划；(尤指政治上)结党|They caballed against the prime minister. 他们密谋反对首相。|
|例句：The disgruntled employees caballed together to undermine their boss.  心怀不满的员工们密谋破坏他们的老板。|
|例句：It was rumored that several generals had caballed to overthrow the government.  有传言说几位将军密谋推翻政府。|
|近义词：conspired, plotted, intrigued, schemed|
|反义词：cooperated, collaborated|
|词性：动词|

caballing:/kəˈbælɪŋ/
| n. |密谋，秘密策划；(尤指政治上)结党|The caballing within the party led to its downfall. 党内的密谋导致了它的垮台。|
|例句：The caballing of the rival factions threatened the unity of the kingdom. 对立派系的密谋威胁着王国的统一。|
|例句：Their constant caballing and backstabbing created a toxic work environment. 他们不断的密谋和背后捅刀子造成了一个有毒的工作环境。|
|近义词：conspiracy, plotting, intrigue, scheming|
|反义词：cooperation, collaboration|
|词性：名词|
