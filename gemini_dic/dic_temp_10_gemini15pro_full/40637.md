conventionalist:/kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪst/
n.|因循守旧者，墨守成规者|Someone who adheres strictly to conventions or established practices, especially in art or literature.|The conventionalist refused to consider any new ideas, preferring to stick with traditional methods.|近义词:traditionalist, conformist, formalist|反义词:rebel, innovator, iconoclast|词性:noun
Etymology: From convention + -ist.  "convention" comes from Late Latin *conventionem (nominative conventio) "assembly, meeting, agreement," from past participle stem of convenire "to come together, assemble," from com- "together" (see com-) + venire "to come" (from PIE root *gwa- "to go, come").
