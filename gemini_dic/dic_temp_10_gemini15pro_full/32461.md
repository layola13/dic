chin-up:/ˈtʃɪn ʌp/ (US), /ˈtʃɪn ʌp/ (UK)| n.|引体向上；振作精神|An exercise in which you pull yourself up to a bar by your hands, with your palms facing towards you.  Also used figuratively to mean "be brave and cheerful in a difficult situation".|He can do twenty chin-ups. 他可以做二十个引体向上。|Synonyms: pull-up (mainly US), bracer (figurative)|Antonyms: None (for exercise); despair (figurative)|noun

chin-up:/ˈtʃɪn ʌp/ (US), /ˈtʃɪn ʌp/ (UK)| n.|鼓励；安慰|Words of encouragement or comfort.  Usually used in the phrase "give someone a chin-up".|He gave me a chin-up when I was feeling down. 当我情绪低落时，他鼓励了我。|Synonyms: encouragement, pep talk|Antonyms: discouragement, criticism|noun
