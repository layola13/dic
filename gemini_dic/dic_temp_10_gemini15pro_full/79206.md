glandered:/ˈɡlændərd/| adj.| 患鼻疽的；腐烂的| Affected with glanders (a contagious disease of horses); decayed, rotten. |The glandered horse had to be put down. 那匹患鼻疽的马不得不被宰杀。|Synonyms: diseased, infected, rotten, decayed |Antonyms: healthy, sound, wholesome| adjective

glandered:/ˈɡlændərd/| adj.| 粗鄙的，下流的| (figurative) Coarse, vulgar. | He used such glandered language that I was offended. 他使用了如此粗鄙的语言，以至于我感到被冒犯了。|Synonyms: vulgar, coarse, obscene, foul |Antonyms: refined, polite, decent| adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From glanders + -ed.  Glanders itself comes from Old French *glandres, plural of glandre "gland, swelling," from Latin glandula "small acorn, gland."  The disease was so named because of the swelling of the lymph nodes.
