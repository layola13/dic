onomatopoeia: /ˌɒnəˌmætəˈpiːə/ (UK) /ˌɑːnəˌmɑːtəˈpiːə/ (US) | n. |拟声词，象声词 |The formation of a word from a sound associated with what is named (e.g. *cuckoo*, *sizzle*). | The word "buzz" is an example of onomatopoeia.  “buzz”这个词就是一个拟声词的例子。 | synonyms: imitation, mimicry, echoing | antonyms: N/A | noun

onomatopoeic: /ˌɒnəˌmætəˈpiːɪk/ (UK) /ˌɑːnəˌmɑːtəˈpiːɪk/ (US)| adj. |拟声的，象声的 |Of or relating to the formation of words by imitation of sounds. |  The poem used onomatopoeic words like "splash" and "gurgle" to create a vivid image of the stream. 这首诗使用了像“splash”和“gurgle”这样的拟声词来创造生动的溪流形象。 | synonyms: imitative, mimetic | antonyms: N/A | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Latin *onomatopoeia*, from Greek *onomatopoiia*, from *onoma*, *onomat-*  'name' + *poiein* 'to make'.
