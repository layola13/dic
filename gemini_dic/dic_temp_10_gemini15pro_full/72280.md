flote:/floʊt/| v. |漂浮，浮动|To rest or move on or near the surface of a liquid without sinking. |The boat floated gently on the lake. 小船在湖面上轻轻漂浮。|近义词：drift, float, hover|反义词:sink, submerge|词性:动词
Etymology: From Middle English *floten, from Old English *flotian, from Proto-Germanic *flotōjaną (“to float”), from Proto-Indo-European *plewd- (“to flow, float, swim”). Cognate with Old Frisian flotian (“to float”), Dutch vlotten (“to float, raft”), German flößen (“to float, raft”), Old Norse flota (“to float”), Gothic 𐍆𐌻𐍉𐍄𐌰𐌽 (flōtan, “to float”). Related to Old English flōwan (“to flow”).
