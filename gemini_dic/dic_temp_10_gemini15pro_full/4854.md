alienation: /ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃən/ | n. | 疏远，离间，异化 |  The state of being isolated or estranged.  The process whereby the worker is made to feel foreign to the products of his/her own labor. |  His alienation from his family has been growing for years. 他与家人的疏远已经持续多年了。| synonyms: estrangement, isolation, detachment | antonyms: connection, integration, belonging | noun

alienation: /ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃən/ | n. | 精神错乱 | A state of depersonalization in which everything feels unreal and one's own actions appear to be controlled by an outside force. | He experienced feelings of alienation after his traumatic experience.  经历了创伤后，他感到精神错乱。| synonyms: disorientation, derangement, psychosis | antonyms: sanity, lucidity, normalcy | noun
