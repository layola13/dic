blueprint:/ˈbluːprɪnt/ | n. | 蓝图；方案；计划 | a detailed plan or design that explains how something might be achieved | The architects presented the blueprint for the new stadium. 建筑师们展示了新体育场的蓝图。| synonyms: plan, design, scheme, draft | antonyms: improvisation,  off-the-cuff | noun

blueprint:/ˈbluːprɪnt/ | n. |  (尤指照相复制的)蓝图，晒图 | a photographic print of a plan or technical drawing, with white lines on a blue background |  The engineers studied the blueprint carefully before starting construction. 工程师们在开始施工前仔细研究了蓝图。| synonyms:  photocopy, blueprint | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From blue + print.  Originally referred to a type of photographic print used for copying technical drawings, characterized by white lines on a blue background. This process, called cyanotype, was invented in 1842.  The metaphorical meaning of "plan or design" developed from this literal sense.
