ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *coken*, from Old English *cōc* (noun, "cook"), from West Germanic *kōk*, from Proto-Germanic *kōkaz* ("cook"), from Proto-Indo-European *peh₂ḱ-* ("to cook, ripen"). Cognate with West Frisian *koek*, Dutch *kok*, German *Koch*, Old Norse *kokkr*. The verb is a back-formation from the noun.
