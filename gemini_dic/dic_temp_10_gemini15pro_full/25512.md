bullirag:/ˈbʊlɪræɡ/| v. |恐吓，欺凌|To intimidate or bully someone.  It implies persistent teasing, tormenting, or harassing.|He was bulliragged by the older boys at school. 他在学校被年纪大的男孩欺负。|Synonyms: bully, harass, intimidate, persecute, torment|Antonyms: protect, support, defend, encourage|verb

Etymology:  Likely a blend of "bully" and "rag" (to tease or scold). "Bully" comes from Middle Dutch "boele" meaning "lover," which later evolved to mean "pimp" and then "blusterer." "Rag" comes from Old Norse "rögg" meaning "shaggy tuft."
