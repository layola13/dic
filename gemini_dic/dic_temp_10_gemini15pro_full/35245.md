clumsy:/ˈklʌmzi/ | adj. | 笨拙的，不灵巧的 | Awkward in movement or handling things. |  He's so clumsy, he's always tripping over things. 他太笨拙了，总是被东西绊倒。

The clumsy waiter spilled coffee on the customer.  笨手笨脚的服务员把咖啡洒在了顾客身上。

| 近义词: awkward, ungainly, inept, maladroit | 反义词: graceful, deft, agile, skillful | 词性: 形容词

clumsily:/ˈklʌmzɪli/ | adv. | 笨拙地，不灵巧地 | In a clumsy manner. | He clumsily knocked over the vase. 他笨手笨脚地打翻了花瓶。

She clumsily tried to catch the ball but missed. 她笨手笨脚地想接住球，但没接住。

| 近义词: awkwardly, ungainly | 反义词: gracefully, deftly | 词性: 副词


clumsiness:/ˈklʌmzɪnɪs/ | n. | 笨拙，不灵巧 | The quality of being clumsy. |  Her clumsiness resulted in a broken glass.  她的笨拙导致了一个玻璃杯被打碎。

The clumsiness of his movements made everyone laugh. 他动作的笨拙让每个人都笑了。


| 近义词: awkwardness, ungainliness | 反义词: grace, deftness | 词性: 名词

词源: From Old Norse *klumsa (“to make a lump, to be numb”), related to klump (“lump”).
