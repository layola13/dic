fougade:/fʊˈɡɑːd/ | n. | 地雷；坑道爆破；坑道地雷 | A small mine placed in a cavity dug in the ground, especially one designed to collapse a tunnel or crater a road. |  The engineers planted a fougasse to destroy the enemy's supply route. 工兵们埋设了一个地雷，以摧毁敌人的补给线。

The fougade created a massive crater in the road, effectively halting the enemy advance.  坑道爆破在道路上炸出了一个巨大的弹坑，有效地阻止了敌人的前进。| 近义词: mine, landmine, explosive device | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, consider "defense" or "fortification" as conceptually opposite) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fougasse*, from Old Provençal *fogassa*, from *focacia* "hearth cake," from Latin *focus* "hearth."  The word originally referred to a type of bread baked in a hearth, and later applied to mines due to the similarity in shape and placement.
