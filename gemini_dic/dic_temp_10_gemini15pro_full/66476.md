exercises:/ˈek.sɚ.saɪz.ɪz/ /ˈek.sə.saɪ.zɪz/| n.  |练习，运动|A series of actions or activities performed to improve or develop a skill, ability, or physical condition.  Also, the use or application of a faculty, right, or process.|She does exercises every morning to stay fit. 她每天早上做运动以保持健康。|synonyms: activities, drills, workouts, practice, application|antonyms: inactivity, rest, idleness|noun

exercises:/ˈek.sɚ.saɪz.ɪz/ /ˈek.sə.saɪ.zɪz/| v. |锻炼，运用|To engage in physical activity to improve strength and fitness; to make use of something, such as a skill, right, or quality.|He exercises his right to vote. 他行使了他的投票权。|synonyms: train, work out, practice, utilize, employ|antonyms: neglect, ignore, rest|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *exerciser*, from Latin exercitāre “to train, practice,” frequentative of exercēre “to keep busy, train, practice, cultivate,” from ex- “out” + arcēre “to keep in, enclose, confine, ward off, prevent.”
