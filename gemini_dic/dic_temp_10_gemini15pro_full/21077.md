blindsiding:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪdɪŋ/| v. |突袭；使措手不及；|To attack or hit someone from the side they cannot see, often unexpectedly.  Also used figuratively to describe surprising someone with unpleasant news or information. |The team was blindsided by their opponent's unexpected strategy. 该队被对手出其不意的策略打了个措手不及。|近义词：ambushing, surprising, stunning|反义词:forewarning, informing|词性:动词

blindsides:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪdz/| v. |突袭；使措手不及；|Third-person singular present tense form of "blindside."|He blindsides his colleagues with his resignation. 他以辞职的方式让他的同事们措手不及。|近义词：ambushes, surprises, stuns|反义词:forewarns, informs|词性:动词

blindsided:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪdɪd/| adj. |被突袭的；措手不及的；|Past participle and adjective form of "blindside," describing someone who has been taken by surprise and is unprepared.|I was completely blindsided by the news of her departure. 我对她的离去的消息完全措手不及。|近义词：ambushed, surprised, stunned|反义词:forewarned, informed|词性:形容词


词源:  From "blind side," referring to the side a person cannot see.
