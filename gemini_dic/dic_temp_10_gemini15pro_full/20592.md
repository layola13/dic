blackfaces:/ˈblækˌfeɪsɪz/ | n. | 黑脸，扮黑脸 |  Theatrical makeup used predominantly by non-black performers to represent a caricature of a black person. This practice is now widely considered offensive due to its historical association with minstrel shows and racist stereotypes. |  Minstrel shows, featuring performers in blackface, were popular in the 19th century.  黑脸艺人在19世纪的吟游诗人表演中很受欢迎。| synonym: minstrel makeup | antonym:  N/A (The antonym would imply a specific type of non-offensive makeup, which doesn't exist as a single term.) | noun
