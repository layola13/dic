demilitarising: /ˌdiːˈmɪlɪtəraɪzɪŋ/
demilitarize: /ˌdiːˈmɪlɪtəraɪz/ | v. | 使非军事化，解除武装 | To remove military forces or weapons from a place or area; to make something no longer military. | The treaty requires both countries to demilitarize the border zone. 该条约要求两国对边境地区进行非军事化。 | disarm, neutralize | militarize, arm | verb


demilitarised: /ˌdiːˈmɪlɪtəraɪzd/ | adj. | 非军事化的，解除武装的 | Having had its military forces or weapons removed. | The demilitarized zone between the two countries was heavily patrolled.  两国之间的非军事区受到严密巡逻。 | unarmed, neutral | militarized, armed | adjective

demilitarisation: /ˌdiːˌmɪlɪtəraɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 非军事化，解除武装 | The action or process of removing military forces or weapons from a place or area. | The demilitarisation of the region was a key part of the peace agreement. 该地区的非军事化是和平协议的关键部分。 | disarmament, neutralization | militarization, armament | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- + militarize.  "De-" is a prefix meaning "remove" or "reverse." "Militarize" comes from "military," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "miles" meaning "soldier."
