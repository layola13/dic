coyish:/ˈkɔɪɪʃ/ (US), /ˈkɔɪɪʃ/ (UK)| adj.|害羞的，腼腆的，忸怩作态的|Pretending to be shy or modest, often in a playful or flirtatious way.  Suggesting a reluctance to reveal something, often for reasons of modesty or coquetry.|She gave a coyish smile and looked down at her feet. 她害羞地笑了笑，低头看着自己的脚。|近义词：bashful, shy, demure, reserved, retiring, self-effacing, timid, diffident, modest, unassuming|反义词：bold, brazen, forward, immodest, shameless, unreserved|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From coy + -ish.  "Coy" itself comes from Middle English coi, from Old French quei, ultimately from Latin quietus "quiet."
