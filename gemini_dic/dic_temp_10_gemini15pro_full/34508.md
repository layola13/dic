clean-up:/ˈkliːnʌp/ (US), /ˈkliːnʌp/ (UK)| n.| 清理，清除；整顿；扫除| An act of cleaning a place or thing, especially thoroughly. |例句：The city organized a major clean-up of the riverbanks. 该市组织了一次对河岸的大规模清理。

clean-up:/ˈkliːnʌp/ (US), /ˈkliːnʌp/ (UK)| n.| 清理工作；清扫工作；清除工作| The process of cleaning something, especially thoroughly. |例句：The after-party clean-up took hours.  派对后的清理工作花了几个小时。


Etymology: Compound of 'clean' and 'up'. 'Clean' comes from Old English 'clæne', meaning 'pure, free from dirt'. 'Up' is from Old English 'up', meaning 'upward, higher'.

Synonyms: cleaning, tidying, purging, purification

Antonyms: mess, clutter, disorganization
