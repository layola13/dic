gerrymandered: /ˈdʒɛriˌmændərd/ | adj. | 不公正划分的，(指选区)为一方利益划分的 | Manipulated for an electoral advantage.  Divided into election districts in a way that gives one political party an unfair advantage. |  The gerrymandered districts made it nearly impossible for the opposition party to win.  这些不公正划分的选区使得反对党几乎不可能获胜。 | Synonyms: rigged, manipulated, fixed | Antonyms: fair, impartial, unbiased | adjective

gerrymander: /ˈdʒɛriˌmændər/ | v. | 不公正地划分(选区) | To divide (a geographic area) into voting districts so as to give unfair advantage to one party in elections. | The state legislature gerrymandered the districts to favor the incumbent party. 州立法机关不公正地划分选区，以利于现任政党。 | Synonyms: manipulate, rig, fix | Antonyms:  -- (verb forms rarely have direct antonyms) | verb

gerrymander: /ˈdʒɛriˌmændər/ | n. | 不公正的选区划分 | The dividing of a state, county, etc., into election districts so as to give one political party a majority in many districts while concentrating the voting strength of the other party into as few districts as possible. | The gerrymander gave the ruling party an unfair advantage.  这种不公正的选区划分给了执政党不公平的优势。 | Synonyms: manipulation, rigging | Antonyms: fairness, impartiality | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Elbridge Gerry + salamander.  Elbridge Gerry (1744–1814) was governor of Massachusetts when in 1812 the state senate redistricted the state to favor his party.  A newspaper editor noted that one of the districts resembled a salamander, and the term "Gerry-mander" was coined.
