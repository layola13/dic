monad:/ˈmɒnæd/ (US) /ˈmoʊnæd/| n.|单子， монада (monada) |A single unit; an ultimate atom or simple substance; something that is complete in itself. In computer science, a monad is a structure that represents computations defined as sequences of steps.  In philosophy, a monad is a simple substance without parts, seen as the fundamental unit of reality.| Leibniz believed that monads were the fundamental building blocks of reality. | synonyms: unit, entity, individual, atom | antonyms: aggregate, composite, whole | noun
