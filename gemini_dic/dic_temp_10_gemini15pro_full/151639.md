particularism:/pərˈtɪkjələrɪzəm/| n.|特殊主义，特殊论|The principle of basing actions or judgments on individual circumstances rather than on abstract rules or general principles.  The belief that what is right or good varies according to the particular circumstances.|His philosophical particularism led him to reject universal moral principles.|近义词：individualism, relativism|反义词: universalism, absolutism|词性:noun

Etymology: From "particular" + "-ism."  "Particular" comes from Late Latin *particularis* "of a part," from Latin *particula* "small part," diminutive of *pars* "part."
