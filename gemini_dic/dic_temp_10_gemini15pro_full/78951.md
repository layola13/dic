girdling: /ˈɡɜːrdlɪŋ/ | n. | 环剥，环切 | The act of cutting a ring through the bark all the way around a tree trunk. |  Girdling the tree eventually killed it.  环剥这棵树最终导致它死亡。 | synonyms: ringing, cincturing | antonyms: grafting, inarching | noun

girdling: /ˈɡɜːrdlɪŋ/ | v. | 环剥，环切 | To cut a ring through the bark all the way around a tree trunk. |  The gardener girdled the diseased tree to prevent the infection from spreading. 园丁环剥了这棵病树，以防止感染蔓延。 | synonyms: ringing, cincturing | antonyms: grafting, inarching | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From *gird* (v.) + *-ling* (suffix forming nouns of action or process).  *Gird* comes from Old English *gyrdan*, meaning "to gird, encircle, enclose," from Proto-Germanic *gurdjan*.
