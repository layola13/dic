atrians:/ˈeɪtriənz/
| n.|心房|Chambers of the heart that receive blood from the veins. |The atrians contract to push blood into the ventricles.|近义词:auricle|反义词:ventricle|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Latin *ātrium* meaning "forecourt, hall, main room".  Anatomically, the term likely refers to the resemblance of the heart's atria to the entrance halls of Roman houses.
