mournfuller: /ˈmɔːrnfələr/ | adj. | 更悲伤的 | More mournful; exhibiting or expressing greater sorrow or grief. |  The music grew mournfuller as the story reached its tragic climax.  音乐随着故事达到悲剧的高潮而变得更加悲伤。 |  The poet's later works were noticeably mournfuller than his earlier, more joyful verses.  这位诗人后期的作品明显比他早期更快乐的诗句更悲伤。 | 近义词: sadder, more sorrowful, more doleful | 反义词: happier, more joyful, more cheerful | 词性: 形容词

词源: From mournful + -er (comparative suffix).  Mournful itself comes from mourn (from Old French morner, ultimately from Latin lugere "to mourn") + -ful (suffix meaning "full of").
