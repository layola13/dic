ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fenn, from Proto-Germanic *fanją (“mud, dirt, mire”), from Proto-Indo-European *pŋHw- (“swamp, bog”). Cognate with Dutch ven (“fen, bog”), German Fenn (“fen, bog, marsh”).
