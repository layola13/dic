burble:/ˈbɜːrbl/ | v.  n. |  发出潺潺声；汩汩地流；兴奋地说；胡言乱语 | To make a continuous murmuring sound like water flowing over stones; to talk rapidly and excitedly, usually about unimportant things. | The brook burbled along. 小溪潺潺流淌。 | synonyms: babble, gurgle, murmur, ripple | antonyms: roar, shout, yell | verb

burble:/ˈbɜːrbl/ | n. | 潺潺声；汩汩声；兴奋的谈话 | A continuous murmuring sound; excited, rapid talk. |  The burble of the stream was soothing.  溪流的潺潺声令人舒缓。 | synonyms: babble, chatter, murmur | antonyms: silence, quiet | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *burblen, frequentative of burben (“to bubble”), from Old French bourber.
