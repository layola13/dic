ciceronianism: /ˌsɪsəˈroʊniənɪzəm/ | n. | 西塞罗主义，雄辩的风格 | A style of writing or speaking characterized by elaborate, flowing periods, balanced constructions, and copious use of classical allusions, reminiscent of the Roman orator Cicero. |  His speech was a perfect example of Ciceronianism, filled with eloquent pronouncements and learned references. 他的演讲是西塞罗主义的完美典范，充满了雄辩的言辞和博学的引用。| synonyms: eloquence, oratory, rhetoric | antonyms: terseness, conciseness, brevity | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Cicero, the Roman orator (Marcus Tullius Cicero, 106-43 BC), + -ian + -ism.
