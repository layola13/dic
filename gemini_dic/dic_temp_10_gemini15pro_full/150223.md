pandect:/ˈpændɪkt/| n.|法典；大全；文集|A comprehensive treatise or digest of law; a complete body of laws; a comprehensive collection or survey of any subject.|The lawyer consulted the pandects to research the historical precedent.|近义词：compendium, digest, collection, compilation|反义词: abridgment, summary | noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *pandēcta*, from Ancient Greek πανδέκτης (pandéktēs, “all-receiving, all-containing”), from πᾶς (pâs, “all”) + δέχομαι (dékhomai, “to receive”).
