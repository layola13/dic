high-grades:/ˈhaɪ ɡreɪdz/ | n. | 高分; 优异成绩 |  Refers to superior marks or academic achievements, typically indicating a high level of performance in coursework or examinations.  Derived from "high" meaning elevated or superior, and "grades" referring to marks or evaluations of academic work. |  She consistently earned high-grades throughout her school years. 她整个学生时代都一直获得高分。| synonyms: high marks, top marks, excellent grades, good grades | antonyms: low grades, poor grades, failing grades | noun

high-grade:/ˈhaɪ ɡreɪd/ | adj. | 高级的; 优质的; 高品位的 |  Of superior quality or grade.  Derived from "high" meaning elevated or superior, and "grade" referring to a level of quality or value. |  They only use high-grade materials in their products. 他们只在产品中使用优质材料。  The company specializes in high-grade leather goods. 这家公司专门生产高级皮具。 | synonyms: superior, premium, top-quality, first-class | antonyms: low-grade, inferior, poor-quality, second-rate | adjective
