dehydration:/ˌdiːhaɪˈdreɪʃn/
n.|脱水|The state of having lost a significant amount of water from the body.|The marathon runner suffered from dehydration.|dehydration, lack of fluids|hydration, fluid balance|noun
dehydrate:/ˌdiːhaɪˈdreɪt/
v.|脱水，使…脱水|To remove water from something; to lose a significant amount of water from the body.|The hot sun dehydrated the hikers quickly.  炎热的太阳很快使徒步旅行者脱水。|dry, desiccate|hydrate, moisten|verb
dehydrated:/ˌdiːhaɪˈdreɪtɪd/
adj.|脱水的|Lacking water; having lost a significant amount of water.|The dehydrated fruit was chewy. 脱水的水果很耐嚼。|dry, parched|hydrated, moist|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *déshydratation*, from *déshydrater* "to dehydrate," from *dés-* "de-" (expressing removal, separation) + *hydrater* "to hydrate," from Greek *hydōr* "water."
