improbation: /ˌɪmprəˈbeɪʃən/ | n. | 不赞成，非难，责备 | Disapproval; condemnation. |  The government's new policy met with widespread improbation. 政府的新政策遭到了广泛的非难。|  The teacher expressed her improbation of the student's disruptive behavior. 老师对学生扰乱课堂秩序的行为表示了非难。| 近义词: disapproval, condemnation, censure, reproach | 反义词: approval, praise, commendation | 词性: 名词

词源:  Late Middle English: from late Latin improbatio(n-), from the verb improbare ‘disapprove’, from in- ‘not’ + probare ‘approve’.
