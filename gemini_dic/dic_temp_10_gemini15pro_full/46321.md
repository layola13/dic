dabbler:/ˈdæblər/| n. |业余爱好者，浅尝辄止的人|A person who engages in an activity in a superficial or non-serious way, often trying various different things without mastering any. |He's a dabbler in many arts, but a master of none. 他涉猎多种艺术，但没有一项精通。

|Synonyms: amateur, dilettante, tinkerer, trifler |Antonyms: expert, professional, specialist, master |noun


dabbler:/ˈdæblər/| n. |涉猎者，浅尝辄止者|Someone who tries many different activities or subjects but does not become skilled in any of them. |She's a dabbler in various hobbies, never sticking with one for long.  她涉猎各种各样的爱好，但从不长期坚持一项。


|Synonyms:  dilettante, amateur, trifler, tinkerer |Antonyms:  expert, professional, specialist, master |noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "dabble," meaning to dip lightly or superficially, from Middle English dabben, of imitative origin.
