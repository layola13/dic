blighting: /ˈblaɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 枯萎的;使…枯萎的;损害的;使…蒙受损害的 | causing something to wither or decay; having a seriously detrimental effect on something. | The late frost blighted the crops. 晚霜使农作物枯萎了。| synonyms: withering, decaying, ruining, damaging, spoiling, impairing, marring, crippling, destroying. | antonyms: flourishing, thriving, prospering, improving, enhancing. | verb

blight: /blaɪt/ | n. | 枯萎病;祸害;阴影 | a disease that causes plants to wither and die; something that spoils or has a very bad effect on something, often for a long time.  | Urban blight has scarred this neighborhood.  城市衰败给这个社区留下了伤疤。 | synonyms: affliction, scourge, bane, curse, plague, menace, threat. | antonyms: blessing, boon, benefit. | noun

blight: /blaɪt/ | v. | 摧残;使…枯萎 | to spoil or damage something, especially by causing a lot of problems. | His career was blighted by injuries. 他的职业生涯因伤病而中断。| synonyms: ruin, spoil, wreck, damage, impair, mar, cripple, destroy. | antonyms: improve, enhance, benefit. | verb
