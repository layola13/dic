entombments:/ɪnˈtuːmmənts/| n. |埋葬，安葬；坟墓|The act of placing a body in a tomb, or the state of being entombed.  The entombments in the ancient pyramids are fascinating to archaeologists.  古代金字塔中的埋葬对于考古学家来说非常着迷。|近义词：burials, interments|反义词:exhumations|词性:noun

词源:
From Middle English *entombment,  from Old French entomber (“to place in a tomb”), from en- (“in”) + tombe (“tomb”). Ultimately from Late Latin tumba (“tomb, mound”), from Greek τύμβος (túmbos, “mound, barrow”).
