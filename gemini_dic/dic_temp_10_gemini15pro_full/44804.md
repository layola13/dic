crura: /ˈkruːrə/
n.
legs; shanks; branches
Anatomical term for leg-like or branch-like structures.  Specifically, refers to the legs of invertebrates, or certain anatomical structures resembling legs.
The crura of the diaphragm are tendinous structures that attach it to the lumbar vertebrae.  横膈的脚是将其附着到腰椎的腱状结构。
The crura cerebri are two stalks that connect the cerebrum with the pons. 大脑脚是连接大脑和小脑桥的两个柄。
Synonyms:  legs, branches, stalks, extensions, appendages
Antonyms:  body, trunk
