microphonics:/ˌmaɪkrəˈfɒnɪks/ (US) /ˌmaɪkrəˈfɒnɪks/ (UK)| n. |话筒效应；啸叫声|The unwanted noise produced in an electronic system, especially an amplifier, by mechanical vibrations of its components, especially when these vibrations are picked up by a microphone or other transducer.|The band's performance was marred by persistent microphonics. 乐队的演出因持续的啸叫声而大打折扣。|Near synonyms: feedback, squeal, howl|Antonyms: silence, quiet|Noun
