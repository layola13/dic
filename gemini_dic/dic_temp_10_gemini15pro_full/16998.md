basubutai: /bɑːˌsuːˈbuːtaɪ/ | n. | 舞台; (baseball) base | A raised platform on which actors, entertainers, or speakers perform;  In baseball, a base is one of four points a player must touch in succession to score a run. |  例句1：The actors bowed to the audience after the play ended on the basubutai.  演员们在舞台上的戏剧结束后向观众鞠躬。

例句2：He slid into third basubutai. 他滑垒到三垒。| 近义词：(for stage) stage, platform, dais; (for baseball) base | 反义词: (for stage) audience, seating area; (for baseball) outfield | 词性: noun


Etymology:  "basubutai" is a Japanese loanword. It combines "basu" (ベース), derived from the English word "base," and "butai" (舞台), the Japanese word for "stage."  This word is primarily used in Japanese contexts.
