bewailed:/bɪˈweɪld/
verb
恸哭；哀悼；悲叹
express great sorrow or distress over.
She bewailed the death of her cat. 她为她的猫的死而悲痛。
近义词: lamented, mourned, grieved
反义词: rejoiced, celebrated
词性: verb

词源: From Middle English *biwailen*, from Old English *biwǣlian* (“to lament, weep over”), from *bi-* (prefix meaning “thoroughly, intensely”) + *wǣlian* (“to wail”).

PHRASE->
This phrase isn't commonly used with "bewailed".  The word usually stands alone and doesn't take a preposition or object other than the cause of the lament.  Therefore, I'll offer alternatives that express similar sentiments:

what a pity = 真可惜
what a shame = 真遗憾
what a loss = 多么大的损失
alas = 唉
woe is me = 我真可怜
