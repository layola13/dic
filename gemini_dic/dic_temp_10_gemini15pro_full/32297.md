chieftainships: /ˈtʃiːftənʃɪps/
| n. | 首领地位；酋长职位；酋长统治 | The position or rank of a chieftain; the territory or people over which a chieftain rules. |  The struggle for chieftainships within the clan was fierce.  氏族内部对酋长地位的争夺非常激烈。|Synonyms: leadership, headship, dominion, authority | Antonyms: subordination, subjection | noun

| n. | 首领的职位（复数）|  The plural form of chieftainship, referring to multiple positions of leadership. |  The museum's exhibit displayed artifacts from various Native American chieftainships.  博物馆的展览展示了来自不同美洲原住民部落首领的文物。 | Synonyms: leaderships, headships, commands | Antonyms:  followings,  subordinates | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *chefteyneschipe, from Old English *cȳeftenscipe (“chieftainship”), from cȳeften (“chieftain”) +‎ -scipe (“-ship”).  Chieftain is from Old French chevetaine (“captain, leader”), ultimately from Latin caput (“head”).
