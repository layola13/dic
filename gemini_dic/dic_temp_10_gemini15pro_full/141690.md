opun:/ˈoʊpən/ | adj. | 打开的，开放的 |Open refers to something not closed or shut; accessible, or available for entry or passage. | The door is open. 门是开着的。| The store is open until 9 pm.  这家店营业到晚上九点。 | synonyms: unlocked, unclosed, ajar, accessible, public, available | antonyms: closed, shut, locked, sealed, private, restricted | adjective


opun:/ˈoʊpən/ | v. | 打开，开放 |To open means to move (something) so as to leave a passage or gap; to make or become accessible or available. | Please open the window. 请打开窗户。| The store opens at 8 am. 这家店早上八点开门。| synonyms: unlock, unseal, unveil, launch, inaugurate | antonyms: close, shut, lock, seal | verb

opun:/ˈoʊpən/ | n. | 露天，野外，公开 | An open space, especially in the countryside. This meaning is less common. |  They enjoyed a picnic in the open. 他们在户外享受野餐。 | The golf tournament was held in the open. 高尔夫锦标赛在露天举行。 | synonyms: outdoors, countryside, open air | antonyms: indoors, enclosed space | noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *open, from Old English open, from Proto-Germanic *upanaz, from Proto-Indo-European *(h₁)up-. Cognates include Old Frisian open, Old Saxon opan, Old High German offan, German offen, Old Norse opinn, Gothic 𐌿𐍀𐌰𐌽 (upan).
