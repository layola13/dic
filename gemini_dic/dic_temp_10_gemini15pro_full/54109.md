disembowered:/ˌdɪsɪmˈbaʊərd/| adj.|失去遮蔽的，失去bower的，暴露的|Deprived of its natural covering or shelter, especially of foliage; exposed.  The word "bower" refers to a shady, leafy shelter or recess.  Therefore, "disembowered" means to be removed from such a place, often implying a sense of vulnerability or exposure.|The storm left the birds disembowered and vulnerable.|Synonyms: exposed, unsheltered, unprotected, bare, denuded|Antonyms: sheltered, protected, covered, concealed|adjective

disembowel:/ˌdɪsɪmˈbaʊəl/| vt.|取出内脏，剖腹|To remove the bowels or entrails of; eviscerate.  Can also be used figuratively to mean to deprive of essential content or meaning.|The hunter disemboweled the deer.|Synonyms: eviscerate, gut|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "fill")|verb
