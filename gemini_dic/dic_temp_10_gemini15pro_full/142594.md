osteoid: /ˈɒstiːɔɪd/ | adj. n. | 类骨质的，骨样组织 | Resembling bone; the organic matrix of bone tissue which forms prior to the deposition of calcium salts. |  The osteoid later mineralizes to become bone.  类骨质后来矿化变成骨骼。|  The tumor was composed primarily of osteoid.  肿瘤主要由骨样组织组成。 |Synonyms: bony, osseous | Antonyms: non-osseous | noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From osteo- + -oid.  Osteo- comes from Greek "osteon" meaning "bone."  -oid is a suffix meaning "resembling" or "like."
