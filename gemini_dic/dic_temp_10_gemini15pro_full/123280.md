monoethanolamine:/ˌmɒnəʊˌɛθənɒləˈmiːn/ /ˌmɑːnoʊˌɛθənɒləˈmiːn/| n. |单乙醇胺|A colorless, viscous liquid used as a solvent and in the manufacture of detergents, emulsifiers, and pharmaceuticals.  It is also used for scrubbing certain acidic gases (such as hydrogen sulfide and carbon dioxide) from natural gas and refinery streams.| Monoethanolamine is used in the production of detergents. 单乙醇胺用于生产洗涤剂。|Synonyms: 2-aminoethanol, MEA|Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for a chemical compound, but acids could be considered opposites in terms of their reactions with MEA.)|noun
