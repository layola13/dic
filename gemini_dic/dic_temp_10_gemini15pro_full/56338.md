dogsbody:/ˈdɒɡzˌbɒdi/ (UK) /ˈdɔːɡzˌbɑːdi/ (US)| n. |杂役，勤杂工|A person who does menial tasks or all the unpleasant work.|He was treated like the office dogsbody. 他被当作办公室的勤杂工对待。|Synonyms:  gofer, lackey, minion|Antonyms: boss, manager, executive|noun


Etymology:  From "dogsbody," originally a sailor's term for a kind of pea soup or stew, later referring to a ship's cook, and eventually to anyone who performs menial tasks.
