cumulonimbus:/ˌkjuːmjʊləʊˈnɪmbəs/| n. |积雨云|A dense, towering vertical cloud, forming from water vapor carried by powerful upward air currents. These clouds are capable of producing thunderstorms and other severe weather, including tornadoes, hail, and heavy rain.|
The dark cumulonimbus clouds signaled an approaching storm.  乌黑的积雨云预示着暴风雨即将来临。

The pilot tried to avoid the cumulonimbus. 飞行员试图避开积雨云。

|近义词：thunderhead, storm cloud|反义词: None|词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cumulus* ("heap, pile") + *nimbus* ("rain cloud, rain storm").
