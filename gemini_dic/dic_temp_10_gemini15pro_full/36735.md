collop:/ˈkɒləp/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləp/ (US)| n.|肉片，一小块（尤指肉）；|A slice of meat, especially one cut for frying or grilling.  Figuratively, it can also refer to a small portion of something.|例句：He fried the collops of bacon until they were crispy. 他把培根片煎至酥脆。

例句：She savored each collop of the steak. 她细细品尝着牛排的每一小块。|近义词：slice, piece, portion, chunk|反义词: whole|词性:noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *coloppe, from Old Norse *kollops, from Proto-Germanic *kullaz (“lump, ball”), from Proto-Indo-European *gel- (“to roll, turn”). Cognate with Dutch klop (“lump, blow”), German Klops (“meatball”), and English clump.
