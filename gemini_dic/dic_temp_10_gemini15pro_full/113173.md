lurch:/lɜːrtʃ/ (US); /lɜːtʃ/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.| 摇晃，蹒跚，突然倾斜；踉跄|A sudden unsteady movement; an abrupt roll or tip to one side.  A staggering or tottering gait.|The bus lurched forward, throwing the passengers off balance. 公共汽车突然向前倾斜，使乘客失去平衡。|Synonyms: stagger, stumble, sway, wobble, reel, falter|Antonyms: steady, stabilize, balance|verb, noun

lurch:/lɜːrtʃ/ (US); /lɜːtʃ/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.| 困境，尴尬的处境|An awkward or difficult situation.|His career has been in a lurch ever since he lost his job. 自从他失业以来，他的事业一直处于困境。|Synonyms: predicament, dilemma, crisis, plight|Antonyms: advantage, benefit, boon|noun
