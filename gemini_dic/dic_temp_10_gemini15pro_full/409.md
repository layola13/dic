abjudication: /əˌbʒuːdɪˈkeɪʃn/
n.
正式剥夺；正式宣告丧失；判决
The act of formally depriving someone of something or declaring the loss of a right or property by judicial decision.
The abjudication of his parental rights was a devastating blow. 他被正式剥夺了父母的权利，这是一个毁灭性的打击。

The court ordered the abjudication of the property. 法院下令剥夺该财产。

近义词: deprivation, forfeiture, divestiture, confiscation, relinquishment
反义词: acquisition, granting, bestowal
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin abjudicatio, from Latin abjudicare (“to deprive of by judgment”), from ab (“from, away from”) + judicare (“to judge”), from judex (“judge”).

USAGE->
The abjudication of his claim was swift. = 他的索赔很快就被驳回了。

The abjudication process took several months. = 判决过程花了几个月的时间。


PHRASE->
abjudication of rights = 权利的剥夺
abjudication of property = 财产的剥夺
abjudication by a court = 法院判决
abjudication process = 判决程序
abjudication hearing = 判决听证会
