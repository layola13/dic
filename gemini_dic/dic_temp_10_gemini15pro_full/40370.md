contradict:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkt/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkt/ (US)| v. |反驳；与…矛盾；与…抵触|To assert the opposite of a statement; to deny the truth of (something).|His account of the accident contradicts hers.  他对于事故的描述与她的说法相矛盾。|近义词：deny, dispute, oppose, challenge, gainsay|反义词：confirm, support, agree, corroborate|词性:动词

contradictory:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪktəri/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪktɔːri/ (US)| adj. |相互矛盾的；对立的；不一致的|Containing elements which are inconsistent or in mutual opposition.|The witness gave contradictory statements to the police. 目击者向警方提供了相互矛盾的陈述。|近义词：conflicting, opposing, incompatible, inconsistent|反义词：consistent, compatible, harmonious|词性:形容词

contradiction:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkʃn/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkʃn/ (US)| n. |矛盾；反驳；否认|A combination of statements, ideas, or features of a situation that are opposed to one another.|There is a contradiction between what he says and what he does. 他的言行不一。|近义词：inconsistency, discrepancy, conflict, opposition|反义词：agreement, consistency, harmony|词性:名词
