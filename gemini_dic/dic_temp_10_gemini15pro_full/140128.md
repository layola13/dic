off-the-charts:/ˌɔːf ðə ˈtʃɑːrts/ | adj. | 破纪录的，极高的，非常棒的 | exceeding the normal or expected limits; exceptionally high or great |  Her popularity is off the charts. 她的人气爆棚。| synonyms: exceptional, extraordinary, phenomenal, outstanding, remarkable, amazing, incredible, unbelievable, unprecedented | antonyms: average, ordinary, mediocre, unimpressive, low, poor | adjective

off-the-charts:/ˌɔːf ðə ˈtʃɑːrts/ | adv. | 非常地，极度地 | to an extreme degree | The band's performance was off the charts good. 乐队的表演好极了。| synonyms: extremely, excessively, incredibly, remarkably, exceptionally | antonyms: moderately, slightly, marginally | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
"Off the charts" originates from the literal meaning of exceeding the highest value measurable on a chart or graph. This visual representation of exceeding the limits was then figuratively applied to other areas, signifying something exceptional or extreme.
