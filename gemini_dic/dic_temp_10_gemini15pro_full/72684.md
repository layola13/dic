fob:/fɑːb/ /fɒb/| n. v.|钥匙扣；表链；怀表；船上交货(free on board)；欺骗；骗子|A fob is a small pocket watch or a small decorative object attached to a chain or ribbon.  As a verb, it can mean to deceive someone.|例句：He checked his watch fob. 他看了看他的表链。

|例句：The salesman tried to fob her off with a faulty product.  那个推销员试图用一件有缺陷的产品把她打发走。|近义词：cheat, deceive, trick (v.), keychain, trinket (n.)|反义词: be honest (v.)|词性: noun, verb

fob:/fɑːb/ /fɒb/| v. |船上交货(free on board)；|FOB means the seller delivers the goods on board the vessel nominated by the buyer at the named port of shipment.  The risk of loss of or damage to the goods passes when the goods are on board the vessel, and the buyer bears all costs from that moment onwards.|例句：The price is $100 per ton FOB Shanghai. 价格是每吨100美元，上海船上交货。

|例句：The contract stipulated FOB delivery terms. 合同规定了船上交货条款。|近义词: None (specific to shipping terms)|反义词: None (specific to shipping terms)|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fobben*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to German *foppen* (to tease, mock). The shipping term "FOB" is an abbreviation of "free on board."
