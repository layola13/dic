maligned:/məˈlaɪnd/ | adj. | 诽谤的，恶意中伤的 |  Having been spoken ill of; defamed.  Damaged in reputation. |  The politician was maligned by the press.  这个政客被媒体恶意中伤。 | synonyms: defamed, slandered, vilified, disparaged, calumniated | antonyms: praised, lauded, extolled | adjective

Etymology:
From Middle French *malignier*, from Latin *malignare* "to treat maliciously," from *malignus* "ill-disposed, malicious," from *male* "badly" + *-gnus* akin to *genus* "birth, race, kind."
