delusion:/dɪˈluːʒən/| n.|错觉，妄想，谬见|An idiosyncratic belief or impression maintained despite being contradicted by reality or rational argument, typically as a symptom of mental disorder.|He had delusions of grandeur, believing himself to be a king.|近义词：hallucination, illusion, misconception, fallacy|反义词：reality, truth|词性:noun

delusional:/dɪˈluːʒənl/| adj.|妄想的，错觉的|Characterized by or holding idiosyncratic beliefs or impressions that are contradicted by reality or rational argument, typically as a symptom of mental disorder.|He had delusional thoughts about being persecuted.|近义词：hallucinatory, illusionary, mistaken, fallacious|反义词：realistic, rational, sane|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English delusioun, from Old French delus(i)on, from Latin dēlūsio (“a deceiving”), from dēlūdere (“to deceive, mock, delude”), from de- (“down, away”) + lūdere (“to play”).
