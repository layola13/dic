gallivants:/ˈɡælɪvænt/ /ˈɡæləˌvænt/| v.| 漫游，游荡，闲逛 | to go around from one place to another in a lively way, usually for pleasure and without any definite purpose |  She gallivanted around Europe for a month after graduation. 她毕业后在欧洲漫游了一个月。| 近义词：roam, wander, traipse, gad about | 反义词: stay, remain | 词性: 动词

词源: From the 16th century.  Possibly from obsolete French *gallivanter, perhaps of uncertain origin, but possibly related to gallant.
