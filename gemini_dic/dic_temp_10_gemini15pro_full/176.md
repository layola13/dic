abattoir:/ˈæbəˌtwɑːr/ (US) /ˈæbətɔːr/ (UK)| n.|屠宰场|A place where animals are slaughtered for food.|The meat comes from a local abattoir. 这肉来自当地一家屠宰场。|近义词：slaughterhouse, butchery|反义词: None|词性: Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *abatre*, "to beat down," from *a-* (to) + *batre* (to beat).  Originally referred to a place where animals were stunned before slaughter. The modern sense developed in the 19th century.

USAGE->
The abattoir processes thousands of animals each week.  这家屠宰场每周处理成千上万的动物。

The smell from the nearby abattoir was overpowering. 附近屠宰场的气味 overpowering。


He worked at the abattoir during the summer holidays. 他暑假期间在屠宰场工作。

New regulations were introduced to improve hygiene standards in abattoirs.  引入了新的法规以提高屠宰场的卫生标准。

The documentary showed the harsh realities of working in an abattoir.  这部纪录片展示了在屠宰场工作的残酷现实。
