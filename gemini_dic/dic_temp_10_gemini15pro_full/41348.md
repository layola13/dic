corinthian: /kəˈrɪnθiən/ | adj. n. | 科林斯的；哥林多人的；放荡的；花花公子 |  Relating to or characteristic of ancient Corinth, especially its luxurious and licentious lifestyle; designating the most ornate of the classical orders of architecture. | The Corinthian columns were a testament to the city's wealth. 这些科林斯式的圆柱证明了这座城市的财富。| Synonyms: ornate, elaborate, luxurious, decadent | Antonyms: simple, austere, plain | adjective

corinthian: /kəˈrɪnθiən/ | n. | 科林斯人；花花公子 | A native or inhabitant of Corinth; a wealthy, fashionable young man; a playboy. | He was known as a Corinthian, spending his days in leisure and pursuit of pleasure.  他被称为科林斯人，终日游手好闲，追求享乐。| Synonyms: playboy, socialite, dandy | Antonyms: ascetic, puritan | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Corinthius*, from Greek *Kórinthos* (Corinth).  The architectural sense derives from the elaborate style of the Corinthian order of columns, developed in ancient Corinth.  The pejorative sense of "playboy" or "debauchee" arose from the city's reputation for luxury and licentiousness.
