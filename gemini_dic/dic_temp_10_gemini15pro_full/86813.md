hematites:/ˈhɛməˌtaɪts/ /ˈhiːməˌtaɪts/| n. |赤铁矿|A common iron oxide (Fe2O3) that is the principal ore of iron and occurs in reddish-brown to black masses that have a metallic luster.|The hematite deposits were a source of wealth for the region.|近义词：iron ore, ferric oxide|反义词: None|词性: Noun


Etymology: From Late Latin *hematites, from Greek haimatitēs (lithos) ‘blood-like (stone)’, from haima, haimat- ‘blood’.
