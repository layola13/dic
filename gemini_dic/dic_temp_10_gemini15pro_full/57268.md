downplay:/ˌdaʊnˈpleɪ/ | v. | 淡化，轻描淡写 | To make something seem less important or less bad than it really is. | Examples: The coach downplayed the team's loss, focusing instead on the players' improvement. 教练淡化了球队的失利，而是专注于球员的进步。  He tried to downplay his involvement in the scandal. 他试图淡化自己与丑闻的关系。| Synonyms: minimize, understate, de-emphasize | Antonyms: exaggerate, overstate, emphasize | verb
