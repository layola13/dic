adelantadillo:/aðeˌlan.taˈði.ljo/| adj.|precocious, forward, early, premature (especially for fruit)|A term used primarily in Spanish to describe something, often fruit, that has ripened or developed earlier than expected. It can also be used to describe a person, particularly a child, who is precocious or exhibits behaviors considered advanced for their age.|例句：Las fresas están adelantadillas este año. = The strawberries are early this year.


El niño es muy adelantadillo para su edad. = The child is very precocious for his age.|近义词：premature, precocious, advanced, early|反义词:late, delayed, backward|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Spanish verb "adelantar" (to advance, to bring forward) + the diminutive suffix "-illo".  "Adelantar" itself comes from "delante" (in front, ahead), ultimately derived from Latin "de" (from) + "in" (in) + "ante" (before).


USAGE->


Ser/Estar + adelantadillo:
La cosecha está adelantadilla este año. = The harvest is early this year.
Mi hija es adelantadila en matemáticas. = My daughter is advanced in math.


Describing Fruit:
Los melocotones están adelantadillos y ya están listos para comer. = The peaches are early and ready to eat now.
Estas manzanas están un poco adelantadillas, pero saben bien. = These apples are a bit early, but they taste good.


Describing Children:
Es un niño muy adelantadillo; ya lee a los cuatro años. = He's a very precocious child; he's already reading at four.
María es adelantada para su edad, habla como una adulta. = Maria is advanced for her age, she speaks like an adult.



PHRASE->
fruta adelantadilla = early fruit
un niño adelantadillo = a precocious child
cosecha adelantadilla = early harvest
estar adelantadillo = to be early/precocious
ser adelantadillo = to be precocious/advanced
