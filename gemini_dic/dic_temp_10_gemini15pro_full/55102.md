disrobement:/dɪsˈroʊbmənt/
|n.|脱衣，卸妆，剥夺|The disrobement of the king symbolized the loss of his power. 国王的脱衣象征着他权力的丧失。|
|英文解析: The act of taking off one's clothes, or of removing a covering or adornment.|
|例句：The ceremonial disrobement of the priest signified the end of the ritual.  牧师的仪式性脱衣标志着仪式的结束。|

|例句：The disrobement of the statue was seen as an act of vandalism.  雕像被剥夺外衣被视为一种破坏行为。|

|近义词：undressing, stripping, uncovering|
|反义词：dressing, clothing, covering|
|词性: noun|

disrobe:/dɪsˈroʊb/
|v.|脱衣，使卸妆|The queen asked her handmaiden to disrobe her. 女王让她的侍女帮她脱衣服。|
|英文解析: To remove one's clothes or the clothes of another person.|
|例句：He disrobed quickly and jumped into the shower. 他迅速脱掉衣服，跳进淋浴间。|

|例句：The artist disrobed the model for the painting.  艺术家为了这幅画让模特脱了衣服。|


|近义词：undress, strip, uncover|
|反义词：dress, clothe, cover|
|词性: verb|
