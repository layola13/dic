hotfoot:/ˈhɒtfʊt/ (UK) /ˈhɑːtfʊt/ (US)| v. |匆匆离去；使某人迅速离开；催促 |To hurry or hasten; to cause someone to leave quickly; to urge onward. |例句：He hotfooted it out of there as soon as he realized he was late. 他意识到自己迟到了，就赶紧离开了那里。

例句：The news hotfooted him back to the office. 这消息促使他迅速返回办公室。|近义词：rush, hurry, hasten, speed, expedite|反义词：dawdle, linger, delay|词性：动词

hotfoot:/ˈhɒtfʊt/ (UK) /ˈhɑːtfʊt/ (US)| n. |匆忙；仓促 |A hurried departure; a state of urgency. |例句：He made a hotfoot for the exit. 他匆忙向出口走去。

例句：The announcement sent everyone into a hotfoot.  这个通知让每个人都匆匆忙忙的。|近义词：rush, hurry, haste|反义词：leisure, slowness|词性：名词
