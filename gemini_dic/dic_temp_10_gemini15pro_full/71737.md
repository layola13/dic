flax:/flæks/
n.|亚麻；亚麻布；亚麻纤维|A plant with blue flowers, cultivated for its seeds (linseed) and for the fibers of its stem, used to make linen.|The farmer sowed his field with flax. 农民在地里播种亚麻。
flax:/flæks/
n.|亚麻色；淡黄色|A pale yellowish gray color like that of dressed flax.|Her hair was a beautiful flax color. 她的头发是美丽的亚麻色。

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fleax, from Proto-Germanic *flahsaz, from Proto-Indo-European *pleḱ- (“to plait”). Cognate with Dutch vlas, German Flachs, Old Norse flax.

SYNONYM->
linen (for the fabric)
linseed (for the seeds)
tow (for the coarser fibers)
straw (for the dried stems)

ANTONYM-> (No direct antonym for flax as a plant or material.  Antonyms would depend on the specific context. For example, if referring to color, an antonym for flax (pale yellowish gray) might be ebony (dark black).)
