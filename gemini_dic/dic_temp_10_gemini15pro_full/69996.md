fetching:/ˈfɛtʃɪŋ/| adj.|迷人的，吸引人的，漂亮的|Pleasing and attractive; charming. |例句：She looked absolutely fetching in that red dress. 她穿那条红裙子看起来真是迷人。

例句：He has a fetching smile that wins everyone over. 他有一个迷人的微笑，征服了所有人。


近义词：attractive, charming, appealing, alluring, captivating|反义词：unattractive, unappealing, repulsive|词性：形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fetchen*, from Old English *feccan* (“to fetch, bring, obtain”), from Proto-West Germanic *fakjan*, from Proto-Germanic *fahaną* (“to catch, seize, take”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰép-*. Cognate with Scots fetch (“to fetch”), West Frisian fetje (“to fetch”), Dutch vatten (“to catch, seize, grasp”), German fassen (“to seize, grasp, hold”), Danish fatte (“to grasp, comprehend”). Related to fang.  The adjective meaning “attractive” arose from the notion of "something that fetches attention".
