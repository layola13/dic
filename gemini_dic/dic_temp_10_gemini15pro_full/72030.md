flippant:/ˈflɪpənt/| adj.|轻率的，轻浮的，油嘴滑舌的|Not showing a serious or respectful attitude.  Showing inappropriate levity.|例句：His flippant remarks about the tragedy were deeply offensive. 他对这场悲剧轻率的言论令人深感不快。

例句：She gave a flippant answer to a serious question. 她对一个严肃的问题给出了轻率的回答。|近义词：frivolous, impertinent, cheeky, disrespectful, glib|反义词：serious, respectful, earnest, solemn|词性:形容词

flippancy:/ˈflɪpənsi/| n.|轻率，轻浮，油嘴滑舌|The quality of being flippant; inappropriate levity.|例句：The teacher did not tolerate flippancy in the classroom. 老师不容忍课堂上的轻浮行为。

例句：Her flippancy during the interview cost her the job. 她在面试中的轻浮态度让她失去了这份工作。|近义词：frivolity, impertinence, cheekiness, disrespect|反义词：seriousness, respect, earnestness, solemnity|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From flip (v.) + -ant.  Originally meaning "nimble, quick," the sense evolved to "talkative, fluent," then to "shallow, pert," and finally to the current meaning of "disrespectfully casual."
