feoffment:/ˈfiːfmənt/ | n. | 封地；封地授予；封土 |The act of granting land or property, especially in feudal times, by which the grantor invested the grantee with possession. |  The king granted the duke a feoffment of vast lands. 国王将大片土地封给了公爵。 | synonyms: enfeoffment, investiture | antonyms: dispossession, forfeiture | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *feoffement*, from feoffer (“to enfeoff”), from fief (“fief”), from Frankish *fehu* (“cattle, property”). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *fehu* (“cattle, wealth”).
