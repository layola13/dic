anchoring:/ˈæŋkərɪŋ/| n.|抛锚，固定，锚定；(比喻)扎根，基础；(认知心理学)锚定效应|The anchoring of the ship prevented it from drifting. 船的抛锚防止了它漂流。|近义词：mooring, fixing, securing, grounding, basing|反义词：drifting, releasing|noun

anchoring:/ˈæŋkərɪŋ/| adj. |起固定作用的; 作为锚的|The anchoring ropes held the tent firm in the strong wind.  固定绳索使帐篷在强风中保持稳固。|近义词：fixing, securing|反义词：loosening, detaching|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *ankeryng, from Old English *ancorung, from ancor (“anchor”) + -ing (suffix forming nouns from verbs).  The verb "to anchor" comes from Old Norse *ankra (“to anchor”). Ultimately from Late Latin ancora, from Greek ἄγκυρα (ánkyra, “anchor”).
