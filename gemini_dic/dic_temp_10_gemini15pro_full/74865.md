fresco:/ˈfrɛskoʊ/ (US), /ˈfrɛskəʊ/ (UK)| n. adj.|壁画；湿壁画|A painting done rapidly in watercolor on wet plaster on a wall or ceiling, so that the colors penetrate the plaster and become fixed as it dries. Also, the method of painting in this way.|The Sistine Chapel ceiling is Michelangelo's most famous fresco. 西斯廷教堂的天花板是米开朗基罗最著名的壁画。|The artist specialized in fresco painting. 这位艺术家专门从事壁画创作。|synonyms: mural, wall painting|antonyms: (As a noun, fresco doesn't have a direct antonym. As an adjective, one might consider "canvas painting" or "panel painting" as contrasting techniques.)|noun, adjective
