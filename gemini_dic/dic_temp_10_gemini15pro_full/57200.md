down-the-tube:/ˌdaʊn ðə ˈtuːb/ | adj. | 失败的，完蛋的，每况愈下的 |  Going down the tube means failing or becoming ruined, often quickly and dramatically. It suggests a rapid decline or deterioration. | The company's profits went down the tube after the scandal. 这家公司的利润在丑闻之后一落千丈。|近义词：failing, ruined, deteriorating, collapsing, going downhill, going to pot, shot to hell |反义词: thriving, flourishing, improving, succeeding | 词性:形容词

down-the-tube:/ˌdaʊn ðə ˈtuːb/ | adv. | 失败地，完蛋地，每况愈下地 |  Describes the manner in which something fails or deteriorates. |  His career went down the tube after his arrest.  他被捕后，他的事业一落千丈。 |近义词： disastrously, ruinously |反义词: successfully, thrivingly | 词性:副词

词源：This idiom likely originated in the early 20th century, possibly referencing the disposal of waste down a drainpipe or the image of something disappearing quickly and irrevocably.  Another theory connects it to the pneumatic tubes used in some buildings to send messages or small items, with the idea that something sent down the tube is gone for good.
