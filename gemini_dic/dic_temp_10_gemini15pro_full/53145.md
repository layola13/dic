diplomatics: /ˌdɪpləˈmætɪks/ | n. |外交; 外交手腕; 交涉技巧; 古文書學| the art and practice of conducting negotiations between nations. It also refers to the study of ancient documents, especially to establish their authenticity. |例句：The success of the treaty depended on skillful diplomatics.  该条约的成功取决于娴熟的外交手腕。

例句：He studied diplomatics in graduate school. 他在研究生院学习古文書學。| 近义词：diplomacy, negotiation, statesmanship | 反义词：war, hostility, aggression | 词性: noun

diplomatic: /ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk/ | adj. |外交的; 外交人员的; 有手腕的; 策略的| of or relating to diplomacy; tactful and skillful in dealing with people. | 例句：Diplomatic relations between the two countries have been restored. 两国之间的外交关系已经恢复。

例句：He gave a diplomatic answer to avoid offending anyone. 他给出了一个不得罪任何人的外交辞令。|近义词：tactful, polite, discreet |反义词：blunt, rude, undiplomatic | 词性: adjective
