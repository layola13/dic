dram:/dræm/ | n. |少量, 一点点; (液量单位)打兰, 英制液体盎司; 少量烈酒| A small quantity, especially of alcoholic drink; a unit of liquid capacity equal to one eighth of a fluid ounce in the US system of units, and approximately one sixteenth of a fluid ounce in the UK system of units. |例句：He poured himself a dram of whisky. 他给自己倒了一小杯威士忌。|近义词：drop, sip, nip, tot|反义词:gallon, liter| noun

dram:/dræm/ | n. |戏剧; 戏剧性事件|A play for the theater, television, or radio; a dramatic event or situation.|例句：The courtroom dram was intense. 法庭上的戏剧性事件非常紧张。|近义词：play, drama, theatrical production|反义词:comedy (specifically as a genre contrasting with drama)| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *drame*, from Old French *dragme*, from Late Latin *dragma*, from Ancient Greek *δραχμή* (drakhmḗ), meaning "handful," originally a unit of weight, later a coin.  The word ultimately derives from the verb *δράσσομαι* (drássomai), meaning "to grasp."
