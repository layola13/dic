overfixing: /ˌoʊvərˈfɪksɪŋ/ | v. | 过度修复；过度关注 |  The act of excessively focusing on or trying to fix something, often to the point of making it worse. |  He was overfixing the problem, constantly tweaking settings that didn't need to be changed. 他过度修复了这个问题，不断调整不需要更改的设置。 | Over-correcting, overcompensating | Underfixing, neglecting | verb

overfixing: /ˌoʊvərˈfɪksɪŋ/ | n. | 过度修复；过度关注 | The state of being overfixed. This noun form is less common than the verb form. | The overfixing of the car's engine ultimately led to its breakdown. 对汽车发动机的过度修复最终导致其故障。 | Overcorrection, overcompensation |  Underfixing, neglect | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"over-" (prefix): From Old English "ofer," meaning "above, over, beyond."
"fix" (verb): From Latin "fixus," past participle of "figere," meaning "to fasten, fix, pierce."
"ing" (suffix):  Forms the present participle and gerund of verbs.
