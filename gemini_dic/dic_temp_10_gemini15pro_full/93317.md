ill-mannered:/ˌɪlˈmænərd/ | adj. | 粗鲁的，无礼的 | Having bad manners; rude. |  He was criticized for his ill-mannered behavior at the dinner party. 他在晚宴上粗鲁的行为受到了批评。

His ill-mannered outburst shocked everyone in the room. 他粗鲁的爆发让房间里的每个人都感到震惊。


| 近义词：rude, impolite, discourteous, uncivil | 反义词：polite, courteous, well-mannered, respectful | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From ill (bad, badly) + mannered (having manners).  The prefix "ill-" negates the positive connotation of "mannered."
