/ˌkaɪroʊˈθɪəriəm/ | n. | 手兽；手兽足迹 | An extinct genus of reptiles from the Triassic period, known primarily from its distinctive hand-shaped footprints. | Example 1:  Cheirotherium footprints have been found in sandstone formations across Europe. 手兽的足迹在欧洲各地的砂岩地层中都有发现。

Example 2: The discovery of Cheirotherium tracks provided early evidence of terrestrial reptiles. 手兽足迹的发现为陆生爬行动物提供了早期证据。 | Synonym:  Chirotherium (variant spelling), hand-beast (informal) | Antonym: N/A | noun
