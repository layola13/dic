boycott: /ˈbɔɪkɒt/ (UK) /ˈbɔɪkɑːt/ (US)
|n. v.|抵制，联合抵制|an act of refusing to buy or use goods and services or take part in something as a way of protesting:  The union called for a boycott of the company's products.|例句：The boycott of the bus company was successful in bringing about change. 对公交公司的抵制成功地带来了改变。

例句：They organized a boycott of the store because of its discriminatory hiring practices. 他们组织了对这家商店的抵制，因为它在招聘中存在歧视行为。|近义词：embargo, ban, rejection|反义词：support, patronage, endorsement|词性: 名词，动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Charles Cunningham Boycott (1832–97), an English land agent in Ireland who was ostracized for refusing to reduce rents.
