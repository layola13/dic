aesopian:/ˌiːsəˈpiːən/ |adj.|寓言式的；隐晦的；带有讽刺意味的|Conveying meaning through allusion or allegory, especially to avoid censorship or express a controversial opinion indirectly.  Used to describe language that appears harmless on the surface but carries a hidden, often critical, message, particularly in situations where direct expression is dangerous or prohibited.|例句：The author used aesopian language to criticize the government without being imprisoned.  作者使用寓言式的语言批评政府而没有被监禁。

例句：Her seemingly innocent stories were actually aesopian critiques of societal injustices. 她看似天真的故事实际上是对社会不公正的讽刺性批判。|近义词：allegorical, metaphorical, symbolic, allusive, veiled, indirect, oblique|反义词：direct, explicit, literal|词性：adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Aesop, the Greek fabulist, whose fables often contained moral or political messages disguised as animal stories.  源自希腊寓言作家伊索，他的寓言通常将道德或政治信息伪装成动物故事。
