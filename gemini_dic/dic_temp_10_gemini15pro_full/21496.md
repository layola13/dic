bludge:/blʌdʒ/| vi. n.|逃避工作；偷懒；骗取；敲诈|To avoid work or responsibility; to shirk. Also, to obtain something by deception or coercion.|He bludged his way through college, relying on others to do his work. 他大学期间一直偷懒，靠别人帮他完成学业。|近义词：shirk, loaf, dodge, malinger|反义词：work, toil, labor|词性:动词,名词

bludger:/ˈblʌdʒər/| n.|懒汉；寄生虫|A person who avoids work or responsibility; a shirker.|He's a real bludger, always expecting others to pick up his slack. 他真是个懒汉，总是指望别人帮他收拾烂摊子。|近义词：loafer, idler, parasite|反义词：worker, grafter|词性:名词

词源:  Origin uncertain. Possibly from British dialect *bludgel, a heavy stick or club, perhaps with the sense of "to strike heavily" or "to live off others by force."
