bidarka:/bɪˈdɑːrkə/
noun
双头皮艇，一种爱斯基摩人的小船
A small, skin-covered kayak-like boat originally used by the Aleut and Inuit people of Alaska.
The bidarka was essential for hunting and transportation in the Aleutian Islands. 双头皮艇在阿留申群岛对于狩猎和交通运输至关重要。

Bidarkas were traditionally made from seal or sea lion skins stretched over a wooden frame. 双头皮艇传统上是用海豹皮或海狮皮绷在木制框架上制成的。

synonym: kayak, umiak, canoe
antonym: ship, boat (in a broader sense, as bidarka is a type of boat)
