oligarch:/ˈɒlɪɡɑːk/ (US) /ˈoʊlɪɡɑːrk/| n. |寡头，寡头政治执政者|A member of a small group of people who control a country or organization.  A person who belongs to an oligarchy.  A very rich business leader with a lot of political influence. |例句：The Russian oligarchs amassed vast fortunes during the privatization of state assets.  俄罗斯寡头在国有资产私有化期间积累了巨额财富。

例句：The oligarch used his wealth to influence political decisions.  这位寡头利用他的财富来影响政治决策。|近义词：magnate, tycoon, plutocrat|反义词:commoner, citizen|词性:noun


Etymology:
From Late Latin *oligarcha, from Ancient Greek ὀλιγάρχης (oligárkhēs), from ὀλίγος (olígos, “few”) + ἄρχω (árkhō, “to rule, command”).
