fauvism:/ˈfɔːvɪzəm/| n.|野兽派|A style of painting with vivid expressionistic and non-naturalistic use of color that flourished in Paris from 1905 to 1908.  It emphasized painterly qualities and strong color over the representational or realistic values retained by Impressionism.|The museum's collection includes several important works of Fauvism.|近义词：Expressionism, Post-Impressionism|反义词:Realism, Impressionism|词性:noun
