Etymology: From Old English _hwæt_, neuter singular nominative and accusative of _hwā_  ("who", "what"), from Proto-Germanic *_hwat_, from Proto-Indo-European *_kʷod_.


USAGE->

Simple Present:
What do you eat for breakfast? 你早餐吃什么？
What does she think about the new policy? 她对新政策有什么看法？

Present Continuous:
What are you watching on TV? 你在电视上看什么？
What is he doing in the garden? 他在花园里做什么？

Present Perfect:
What have you learned from this experience? 你从这次经历中学到了什么？
What has she done to help? 她做了什么来帮忙？

Present Perfect Continuous:
What have you been working on lately? 你最近一直在忙什么？
What has he been reading this week? 他这周一直在读什么？

Simple Past:
What did you do last night? 你昨晚做了什么？
What happened at the party? 派对上发生了什么？

Past Continuous:
What were you doing when the phone rang? 电话响的时候你在做什么？
What was she thinking about during the meeting? 会议期间她在想什么？

Past Perfect:
What had you done before you came here? 你来这里之前做了什么？
What had they discussed before the decision was made? 决定做出之前他们讨论了什么？

Past Perfect Continuous:
What had you been doing before I interrupted you?  在我打断你之前你一直在做什么？
What had they been arguing about for so long? 他们一直在争论什么这么久？

Simple Future:
What will you do after graduation? 毕业后你会做什么？
What will the weather be like tomorrow? 明天天气怎么样？

Future Continuous:
What will you be doing this time next week? 下周这个时候你会在做什么？
What will they be discussing at the conference? 他们在会议上会讨论什么？

Future Perfect:
What will you have achieved by the end of the year? 到年底你会完成什么？
What will they have done by the time we arrive? 我们到达的时候他们会做什么？

Future Perfect Continuous:
What will you have been doing for ten years by 2033? 到2033年你将已经做什么十年了？
What will she have been studying by the time she graduates? 到她毕业时，她将学习什么专业多久了？
