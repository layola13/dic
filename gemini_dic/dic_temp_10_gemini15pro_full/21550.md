bluely:/ˈbluːli/| adv.|忧郁地，悲伤地|Bluely describes something done in a sad or melancholy way.  It modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb, indicating the manner in which an action is performed or a state exists.|She looked at him bluely, tears welling in her eyes. 她忧郁地看着他，眼里噙满了泪水。|Synonyms: sadly, mournfully, dejectedly, gloomily|Antonyms: happily, cheerfully, joyfully|Part of Speech: Adverb

Etymology:
From "blue" (meaning sad) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).  The word "blue" has been associated with sadness since at least the 14th century.
