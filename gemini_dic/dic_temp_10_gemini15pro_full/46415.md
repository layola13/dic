daftnesses:/ˈdɑːftnɪsɪz/
noun
愚蠢；愚昧（行为）；傻事
The state or quality of being daft; foolish behavior or actions.
His youthful daftnesses are now just embarrassing memories.  他年轻时的愚蠢行为现在只是令人尴尬的回忆。
The sheer daftness of the plan is breathtaking. 这个计划简直愚蠢得令人咋舌。
synonyms:  foolishness, stupidity, silliness, idiocy, absurdity
antonyms:  wisdom, intelligence, sense, rationality
词性: 名词 (plural noun)

Etymology:
From daftness + -es (plural suffix).  Daft itself comes from Middle English *dafte, likely of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse *daftr meaning "numb" or "lethargic." The meaning shifted from physical to mental slowness.
