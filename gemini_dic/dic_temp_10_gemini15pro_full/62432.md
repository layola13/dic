endoplasma:/ˈɛndoʊˌplæzəm/ | n. | 内质 | The portion of the cytoplasm of a cell outside the nucleus.  The substance inside a cell that holds all the cell's internal substructures (organelles) except the nucleus. | The endoplasma contains various organelles vital for cell function. 内质包含对细胞功能至关重要的各种细胞器。

The scientist observed the movement of organelles within the endoplasma. 科学家观察了细胞器在内质内的运动。 | cytoplasm, protoplasm |  (No specific antonym, perhaps extracellular fluid) | noun
