brahminism:/ˈbrɑːmɪnɪzəm/| n. |婆罗门教，婆罗门主义|A complex social and religious system that originated in ancient India, characterized by the dominance of the Brahmin priestly caste and the belief in reincarnation, karma, and dharma.  It laid the foundation for Hinduism.|The caste system within brahminism was rigid and hierarchical. 婆罗门教的种姓制度是僵化和等级森严的。

The influence of brahminism can still be seen in modern Indian society. 婆罗门教的影响在现代印度社会仍然可见。|近义词:Hinduism, Brahmanism|反义词:N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Brahmin + -ism.  Brahmin comes from Sanskrit *brāhmaṇa, meaning "priest, one who performs sacred rites."  The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, belief system, or practice.
