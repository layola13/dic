gateman:/ˈɡeɪtmən/ | n. | 门卫，守门人 | A person who attends a gate, especially at a railroad crossing or the entrance to a park, factory, etc. | The gateman opened the gate for the car to enter. 门卫打开大门让车进去。| The gateman refused to let anyone in without a pass.  门卫拒绝让任何没有通行证的人进入。 | synonyms: doorkeeper, porter, guard | antonyms: visitor, guest | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From gate + man.  "Gate" comes from Old English "geat" meaning "opening, passage." "Man" comes from Old English "mann" meaning "human being, person."
