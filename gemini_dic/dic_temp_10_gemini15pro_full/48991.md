deglazing:/diːˈɡleɪzɪŋ/
|n.|煺冰; 去冰;  (烹饪)去釉|The act of removing ice (as from a refrigerator, aircraft, or roadway). Also, in cooking, the act of adding liquid to a hot pan to loosen and dissolve browned food residue.|After roasting the chicken, she deglazed the pan with white wine to make a delicious sauce. 烤完鸡肉后，她用白葡萄酒给锅去釉，做成美味的酱汁。|近义词：Thawing (for ice removal), deglaciation (for glaciers)|反义词：Freezing, icing|词性: 名词

deglaze:/diːˈɡleɪz/
|vt.|煺冰; 去冰; (烹饪)去釉|To remove ice from something. Also, in cooking, to add liquid to a hot pan to loosen and dissolve browned food residue.|He deglazed the windshield before driving. 他在开车前给挡风玻璃除冰。|近义词：Thaw (for ice removal)|反义词：Freeze, ice|词性: 动词
