fabliau: /ˈfæbliˌoʊ/ (US), /ˈfæbliəʊ/ (UK)
n.
滑稽故事, 寓言, 法布里奥
A short metrical tale, usually ribald and comic, popular in medieval France.  These tales often featured characters from the lower and middle classes and revolved around themes of sex, trickery, and revenge.
The Canterbury Tales includes several fabliaux, such as "The Miller's Tale."  《坎特伯雷故事集》包含几个滑稽故事，例如“磨坊主的故事”。
The fabliau tradition influenced later comic writers.  滑稽故事的传统影响了后来的喜剧作家。
近义词: tale, story, anecdote, narrative
反义词: tragedy, drama, epic
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *fabliau, diminutive of fable (“story, tale”), from Latin fabula (“story, tale, play”), from fari (“to speak”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *bʰeh₂- (“to speak”).
