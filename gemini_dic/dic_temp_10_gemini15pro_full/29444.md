catalogs: /ˈkætəlɒɡz/ (British) /ˈkætəlɔːɡz/ (American)| n.|目录; 目录册; 产品样本|A list or record of things, often in alphabetical or other systematic order, especially of items for sale or available for use.| The library has several catalogs to help you find books. 图书馆有几本目录可以帮助你查找书籍.|synonyms: list, register, directory, index, inventory, record, archive|antonyms: disorder, chaos|noun


catalogs: /ˈkætəlɒɡz/ (British) /ˈkætəlɔːɡz/ (American)| v.|为…编目录; 将…编入目录|To make a list or record of things, often in alphabetical or other systematic order.|  The museum is cataloging its collection of ancient artifacts. 博物馆正在为其古代文物收藏编目。|synonyms: list, register, index, document, record|antonyms: disorganize, scramble|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *catalogue*, from Latin *catalogus*, from Greek *katálogos* "list, register," from *katalegein* "to choose, enumerate," from *kata* "down" + *legein* "to gather, speak."
