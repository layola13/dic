foreknowingly:/fɔːrˈnoʊɪŋli/
adverb
预先知道地，故意地
having previous knowledge of something, especially something wrong or illegal
He foreknowingly lied to the police. 他明知故犯地向警察撒谎。
Synonyms: deliberately, intentionally, wittingly, knowingly
Antonyms: unknowingly, unintentionally, unwittingly, innocently
词性: 副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From fore- + knowing + -ly.  Fore- is an Old English prefix meaning "before," "beforehand," or "in front." Knowing comes from the Old English word "cnawan" meaning "to know."  -ly is a suffix forming adverbs.
