expurgating:/ˈekspərˌɡeɪtɪŋ/
verb
净化；删除（不当内容）
Removing matter thought to be objectionable or unsuitable from (a book or account).
The editor expurgated the novel before its publication, removing several passages deemed too explicit for young readers. 编辑在小说出版前将其净化，删除了一些被认为对年轻读者过于露骨的段落。
近义词: censoring, purging, cleansing, bowdlerizing
反义词: including, adding, inserting, retaining
词性: verb (present participle)

expurgate:/ˈekspərˌɡeɪt/
verb
净化；删除（不当内容）
To remove matter thought to be objectionable or unsuitable from (a book or account).
They expurgated the book of all passages they considered obscene.  他们将书中所有他们认为淫秽的段落都删除了。
近义词: censor, purge, cleanse, bowdlerize
反义词: include, add, insert, retain
词性: verb

expurgation:/ˌekspərˈɡeɪʃn/
noun
净化；删除（不当内容）
The removal of matter thought to be objectionable or unsuitable from a book or account.
The expurgation of the text resulted in a much shorter, but less informative, version.  文本的净化导致了一个更短，但信息量更少的版本。
近义词: censorship, purging, cleansing
反义词: inclusion, addition, insertion
词性: noun


词源: From Latin expurgare "to cleanse, purify," from ex- "out" + purgare "to purge."
