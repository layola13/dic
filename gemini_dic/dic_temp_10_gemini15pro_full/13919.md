awkward:/ˈɔːkwərd/ (US), /ˈɔːkwəd/ (UK)| adj.|笨拙的，尴尬的，棘手的|Lacking grace or skill in movement or performance; causing or feeling embarrassment or inconvenience; difficult to handle or deal with.|例句：He felt awkward dancing with her. 他和她跳舞感觉很尴尬。|近义词：clumsy, ungainly, embarrassing, inconvenient|反义词:graceful, skillful, convenient, comfortable|词性:形容词

Etymology: From Old Norse *afugr, meaning "turned the wrong way."  The original sense was physical awkwardness, later extending to social and situational discomfort.
