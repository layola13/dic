glowing:/ˈɡloʊɪŋ/ | adj. | 发光的，容光焕发的，灼热的 | emitting light and heat; shining brightly; full of praise | The embers glowed in the fireplace.  余烬在壁炉里发出红光。| synonyms: radiant, luminous, incandescent | antonyms: dull, dark, dim | adjective

glowing:/ˈɡloʊɪŋ/ | adj. | 容光焕发的，健康的 | having a healthy warm colour, especially in the face | She gave a glowing account of her trip. 她绘声绘色地讲述了她的旅行。| synonyms: radiant, beaming, healthy | antonyms: pale, wan, sickly | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English glowen, from Old English glōwan (“to glow, shine”), from Proto-Germanic *glōwaną (“to glow”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰlew- (“to shine, gleam”).  Cognate with Scots glow (“to glow”), West Frisian gloeie (“to glow”), Dutch gloeien (“to glow, smoulder”), German glühen (“to glow”), Danish gløde (“to glow”), Swedish glöda (“to glow”).
