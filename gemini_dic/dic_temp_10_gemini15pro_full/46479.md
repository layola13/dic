daimyo:/ˈdaɪmjoʊ/| n. |大名|A feudal lord in Japan, especially during the Edo period (1603-1867).  They were powerful regional rulers subordinate to the shogun. |The daimyo controlled vast estates and commanded armies of samurai.|Synonym: feudal lord, magnate|Antonym: peasant, commoner|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Japanese 大名 (daimyō), from 大 (dai, “large”) + 名 (myō, from 名田 (myōden), “private rice land; private estate”).  Originally, myō referred to the private land holdings of powerful individuals, which formed the basis of their power and influence. The term evolved to refer to the lords themselves.
