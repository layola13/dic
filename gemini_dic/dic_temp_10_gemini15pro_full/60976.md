elicitate:/ɪˈlɪsɪteɪt/ | v. | 引出，探出，诱出 | To evoke or draw out a response, information, or fact from someone in reaction to something. | The lawyer skillfully elicited the truth from the witness. 律师巧妙地从证人那里 eliciting the truth. | 近义词：extract, evoke, obtain, draw out | 反义词：suppress, conceal, hide | 词性: 动词

词源:  From Latin ēlicitāre, frequentative of ēlicere “to draw out, entice,” from ex- “out” + lacere “to entice, allure.”
