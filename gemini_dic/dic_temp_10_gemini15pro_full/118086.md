menhaden:/mɛnˈheɪdən/ | n. | 鲱鱼 | A small, oily fish (Brevoortia tyrannus) found in schools along the Atlantic coast of North America, used for bait, fertilizer, and fish oil. |  Menhaden are an important food source for many larger fish. 鲱鱼是许多大型鱼类的重要食物来源。

Menhaden oil is rich in omega-3 fatty acids. 鲱鱼油富含 omega-3 脂肪酸。 | 近义词：pogy, mossbunker | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

Etymology: From the Narragansett word *munnawhatteaûg*, meaning "that which manures".  The word literally translates to "fertilizer" and reflects the Native American practice of using these fish to fertilize crops.
