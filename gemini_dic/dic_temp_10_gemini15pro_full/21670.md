blusterously:/ˈblʌstərəsli/ | adv. | 喧闹地；狂暴地；咆哮地 | In a loud, noisy, and aggressive way, often with empty threats or boasts. | The wind blusterously swept through the valley, carrying leaves and debris with it.  风喧嚣地掠过山谷，卷走了树叶和残骸。| The coach blusterously yelled at the referees, but they ignored his complaints. 教练对着裁判咆哮，但裁判们无视了他的抱怨。 | 近义词: boisterously, clamorously, vociferously, loudly | 反义词: quietly, calmly, softly, gently | 词性: 副词
ETYMOLOGY->
From bluster + -ously.  Bluster itself comes from Middle English blustren, probably of imitative origin.
