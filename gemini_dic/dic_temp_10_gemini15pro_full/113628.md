machicolated:/məˈʃɪkəleɪtɪd/
adjective
设有射击孔的；有雉堞的
Having machicolations; provided with projecting galleries or parapets with openings through which missiles can be dropped on attackers below.
The machicolated walls of the castle provided a strong defense against invaders. 城堡设有射击孔的城墙为抵御入侵者提供了坚固的防御。
Synonyms: battlemented, crenellated, fortified
Antonyms: undefended, unprotected

词源:
From Old French *machecoler "to have machicolations," from machecoulis "machicolations," probably from Old Provençal masecol "portcullis."  The word ultimately derives from Late Latin *mansioculus, diminutive of mansio "dwelling, stay, stopping place."
