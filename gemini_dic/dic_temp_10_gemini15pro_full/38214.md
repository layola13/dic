compurgator:/kəmˈpɜːrɡeɪtər/ | n. |宣誓证人；共誓者|A person who swore an oath supporting the innocence of an accused person in medieval law.  A person who gives evidence under oath to support the statement of another. |例句：In medieval times, a compurgator would swear to the good character of the accused.  在中世纪，宣誓证人会宣誓被告的良好品格。

例句：He called upon several compurgators to attest to his alibi. 他请了几位宣誓证人来证明他的不在场证明。|近义词：witness, testifier, oath-taker |反义词: accuser, prosecutor |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *compurgator, from Latin com- (together) + purgare (to cleanse, purify)  Related to purge.
