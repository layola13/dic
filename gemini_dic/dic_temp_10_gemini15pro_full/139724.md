ochlesis:/ɒkˈliːsɪs/
noun
群众，暴民，民众
A large gathering of people, a crowd, especially a disorderly or unruly one.  The term often carries a negative connotation, suggesting a mob mentality.
The ochlesis surged forward, demanding to see the king.  群众涌上前，要求见国王。
The politician skillfully manipulated the ochlesis to his advantage.  这位政客巧妙地操纵民众为自己谋利。
近义词: mob, crowd, throng, multitude
反义词: elite, few, minority
词性: noun

Etymology: From Ancient Greek ὀχλοποιέω (okhlopoieō, “to make a crowd, form a mob”) from ὄχλος (okhlos, “crowd, mob”) + ποιέω (poieō, “to make, do”).
