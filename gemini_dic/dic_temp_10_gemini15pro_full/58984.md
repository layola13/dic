dutifulness:/ˈdjuːtɪflnəs/
n.|尽职尽责；忠于职守|The employee's dutifulness was praised by the manager.  这位员工的尽职尽责受到了经理的赞扬。|English Explanation: The quality of being dutiful; conscientiousness in performing one's duties.|例句：Her dutifulness to her parents was admirable. 她对父母的孝顺令人钦佩。|近义词：responsibility, diligence, conscientiousness|反义词：negligence, irresponsibility|词性:名词
ETYMOLOGY->
From *dutiful* + *-ness*. *Dutiful* comes from *duty* + *-ful*. *Duty* comes from Old French *deu, dueté* "duty, debt," ultimately from Latin *debitum* "that which is owed." *-ful* is a suffix meaning "full of" or "characterized by." *-ness* is a suffix that forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
