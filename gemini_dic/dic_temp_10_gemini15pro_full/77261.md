gastropodan:/ˈɡæstrəˌpɑːdən/| n. | 腹足动物 |  A mollusk of the large class Gastropoda, such as a snail, slug, or whelk, having a single shell (often coiled) or no shell at all, and a flattened muscular foot for locomotion. | Snails are common gastropods.  蜗牛是常见的腹足动物。|Synonyms: univalve, snail, slug | Antonyms: bivalve (not a true antonym, but represents a contrasting group of mollusks)| noun

Etymology: From Modern Latin *Gastropoda*, from Ancient Greek *γαστήρ* (gastḗr, “belly”) + *πούς* (poús, “foot”).
