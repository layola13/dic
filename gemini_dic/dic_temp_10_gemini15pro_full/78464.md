gewgaw:/ˈɡuːɡɔː/ | n. | 小玩意儿；廉价而俗丽的饰品 | A showy trifle; a cheap ornament. |  She spent her allowance on cheap gewgaws. 她把零用钱都花在了廉价的小玩意儿上。| Synonyms: trinket, bauble, knick-knack, trifle | Antonyms: valuable, treasure | noun

gewgaw:/ˈɡuːɡɔː/ | n. | 毫无价值的东西；无用的东西 | Something useless or worthless. | The politician dismissed the proposal as a mere gewgaw. 这位政客认为这项提议毫无价值，不予理会。| Synonyms: frippery, gimcrack | Antonyms: necessity, essential | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain, perhaps related to Middle Low German *gegawe, denoting a gift or present, or to Old French joujou, a plaything.  First recorded in English in the 14th century.
