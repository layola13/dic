dieresis:/daɪˈɛrɪsɪs/ /daɪˈɪərɪsɪs/
noun
分音符
A diacritical mark ( ¨ ) placed over a vowel to indicate that it is pronounced in a separate syllable, as in naïve or Brontë.
The word "naïve" uses a dieresis to show that the "i" is pronounced separately from the "a".  “naïve”这个词使用分音符来表示“i”与“a”分开发音。
Near synonyms:  trema, diaeresis
No direct antonym.
