blasphemy: /ˈblæsfəmi/ | n. |亵渎，渎神|The act of insulting or showing contempt or lack of reverence for God or sacred things. |His words were considered blasphemy by the church. 他的话被教会认为是亵渎神明的。|  The comedian's jokes about religion were seen as blasphemy by some.  那位喜剧演员关于宗教的笑话被一些人视为亵渎。|  Near synonyms: sacrilege, profanation, irreverence, impiety | Antonyms: reverence, piety, respect | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French blasfemie, from Late Latin blasphemia, from Greek blasphēmia "slander, impious speech," from blasphemein "to speak evil of, revile, slander," from blasphemos "evil-speaking," from blaptein "to harm, injure" + phēmē "speech, utterance, rumor, report."
