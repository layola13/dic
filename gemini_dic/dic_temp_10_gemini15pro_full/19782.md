billows:/ˈbɪloʊz/ (US) /ˈbɪləʊz/ (UK)| n. v.| 巨浪；滚滚的烟雾；波涛汹涌；像波浪般起伏 |A large wave or a series of large waves. Also, a rising mass of something such as smoke or sound.| The ship rose and fell on the billows. 船随着巨浪起伏。| synonyms: waves, surges, swells, breakers, rollers | antonyms: ripples, calms, stillness | noun

billows:/ˈbɪloʊz/ (US) /ˈbɪləʊz/ (UK)| v.|  (像波浪般)翻滚，汹涌，波动 |To rise and fall or move like waves.| Smoke billowed from the burning building.  浓烟从燃烧的建筑物中滚滚而出。| synonyms: surge, swell, roll, undulate, wave | antonyms: flatten, recede, settle | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *bylja (“billow, wave”), from Proto-Germanic *bulgijaną (“to swell”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰelǵʰ- (“to swell”). Cognate with Old English bylgja (“billow, wave, sea”).
