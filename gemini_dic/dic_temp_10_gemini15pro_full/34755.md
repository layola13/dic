cliffed:/klɪft/ | adj. | 悬崖峭壁的；险峻的；陡峭的 | Characterized by cliffs; steep and precipitous. |  The coastline was cliffed and dramatic, with waves crashing against the rocks. 海岸线悬崖峭壁，景色壮观，海浪拍打着岩石。| synonyms: steep, precipitous, sheer, craggy | antonyms: gentle, sloping, flat | adjective

cliffed:/klɪft/ | v. | (非正式)偷窃，扒窃 | (informal) To steal or shoplift. | He cliffed a candy bar from the store. 他从商店里偷了一块糖果。| synonyms: steal, pilfer, shoplift, nick | antonyms: return, give, donate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From cliff (n.) + -ed (suffix forming adjectives).  The verb sense is a back-formation from "cliffhanger," referring to a suspenseful situation, like hanging off a cliff.  This then broadened to mean "to steal," perhaps with a connotation of taking something risky or precarious.
