imprecate:/ˈɪmprɪkeɪt/ | v. | 诅咒，咒骂 | To invoke evil or curses upon; to curse. | He imprecated the gods for his misfortune. 他咒骂众神给他带来的厄运。 |  The witch imprecated a plague on the village. 女巫诅咒村庄将遭受瘟疫。 |  curse, damn, execrate | bless, praise | verb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *imprecāre, present active infinitive of imprecō (“I invoke [evil] upon, curse”), from in- (“upon, towards”) + precor (“I pray, entreat”).  Equivalent to im- +‎ precate.
