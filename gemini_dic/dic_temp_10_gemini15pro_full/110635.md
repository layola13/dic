limply:/ˈlɪmpli/| adv.|无力地，柔软地|in a limp or drooping manner; without firmness or stiffness|The flag hung limply in the still air. 旗帜无力地垂在静止的空气中。|weakly, feebly, loosely|firmly, stiffly|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From limp (adjective) + -ly (adverb suffix).  The adjective "limp" comes from Middle English "limpen,"  likely of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse "limpra" (to weaken, disable). The "-ly" suffix is a common way to form adverbs from adjectives in English.
