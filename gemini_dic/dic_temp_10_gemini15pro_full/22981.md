botulism:/ˈbɒtʃʊlɪzəm/ (US) /ˈbɒtʃʊlɪzəm/ (UK)| n. | 肉毒中毒 | A serious type of food poisoning caused by toxins produced by the bacterium Clostridium botulinum. | Example: The improperly canned food caused a case of botulism.  这罐头食品处理不当导致了肉毒中毒。| Synonym: food poisoning, botulinus poisoning | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a disease) | noun


botulisms:/ˈbɒtʃʊlɪzəmz/ (US) /ˈbɒtʃʊlɪzəmz/ (UK)| n. |  肉毒中毒（复数，较少使用） |  Plural form of botulism, referring to multiple instances or types of the illness.  | Example:  Researchers are studying different botulisms to develop better treatments. 研究人员正在研究不同类型的肉毒中毒，以开发更好的治疗方法。 | Synonym: (Essentially the same as botulism - food poisoning, botulinus poisoning) | Antonym: (No direct antonym) | noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *botulus* ("sausage") + *-ismos* (Greek suffix forming nouns of condition).  The name derives from early cases linked to contaminated sausages.
