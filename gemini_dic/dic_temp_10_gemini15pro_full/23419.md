bracket:/ˈbrækɪt/ (US) /ˈbrækɪt/ (UK)| n. v.|支架，括号，等级，托架; 用括号括起，把…归入同一类|A bracket is a piece of metal, wood, or plastic that is fastened to a wall to support something, such as a shelf.  |The shelves were held up by metal brackets.  这些架子由金属支架支撑。|synonyms: support, brace, prop, |antonyms: detachment, separation|noun


bracket:/ˈbrækɪt/ (US) /ˈbrækɪt/ (UK)| v.|用括号括起，把…归入同一类|To bracket something means to put it in brackets.  |He bracketed the clause in the sentence. 他用括号把句子中的从句括起来。|synonyms: enclose, encompass, surround|antonyms: exclude, release, free|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "braguette" (codpiece), diminutive of "brague" (trousers), from Frankish "brākō" (trousers). The sense of "support" developed from the shape of codpiece supports, which resembled brackets. Later, the meaning extended to punctuation marks.
