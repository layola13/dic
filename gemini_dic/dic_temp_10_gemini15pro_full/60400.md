eidograph: /ˈaɪdəɡræf/ | n. | 缩放仪，放大绘图仪 | An instrument for mechanically copying a drawing or other design on a reduced, enlarged, or the same scale. | Example: The architect used an eidograph to create a larger version of his blueprint. 建筑师使用缩放仪来创建更大版本的蓝图。

Example: She used an eidograph to enlarge the small illustration for her children's book. 她用缩放仪放大儿童读物中的小插图。


|Synonym:| pantograph, proportional compass |
|Antonym:|  (No direct antonym, could consider "freehand drawing tools") |
|Word type:| Noun |


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *eidographe*, from Ancient Greek *εἶδος* (eîdos, “form, shape”) + *γράφω* (gráphō, “I write, draw”).
