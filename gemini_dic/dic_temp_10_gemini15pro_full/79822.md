glycosaminoglycans:/ˌɡlaɪkoʊsəˌmiːnoʊˈɡlaɪkænz/ | n. | 糖胺聚糖 | Long unbranched polysaccharides consisting of a repeating disaccharide unit. One of the two sugars in the repeating unit is always an amino sugar (N-acetylglucosamine or N-acetylgalactosamine), and the other sugar is usually a uronic acid (glucuronic acid or iduronic acid).  Many glycosaminoglycans are sulfated. | Glycosaminoglycans are essential components of connective tissues.  糖胺聚糖是结缔组织的重要组成部分。

Glycosaminoglycans play a crucial role in maintaining the structural integrity of cartilage. 糖胺聚糖在维持软骨的结构完整性方面起着至关重要的作用。 | 近义词: GAGs (abbreviation) | 反义词:  None | 词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"glyco-" from Greek *glukus* meaning "sweet"
"amino-" referring to the amino group present
"glycan"  from Greek *glukus* meaning "sweet" + "-an" denoting a polysaccharide
