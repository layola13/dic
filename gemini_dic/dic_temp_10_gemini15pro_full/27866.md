canoodlers:/kəˈnuːdlərz/
|n.|拥抱爱抚的人；卿卿我我的人|People who are canoodling; those who engage in affectionate cuddling and kissing.|The park bench was occupied by a pair of canoodlers. 公园的长椅上坐着两个卿卿我我的人。|Synonyms: lovers, sweethearts, couples|Antonyms: enemies, rivals|Noun

canoodle:/kəˈnuːdl/
|v.|拥抱爱抚；卿卿我我|To cuddle and kiss affectionately; to engage in amorous embraces.|They were canoodling on the sofa. 他们在沙发上卿卿我我。|Synonyms: cuddle, caress, neck, smooch|Antonyms: fight, argue|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
The origin of "canoodle" is uncertain, but it likely emerged in American English in the mid-19th century.  Some speculate a connection to "noodle," perhaps suggesting playful or amorous head-to-head contact.  Others suggest a link to German "knudeln," meaning "to cuddle."
