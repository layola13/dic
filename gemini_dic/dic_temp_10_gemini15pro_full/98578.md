institutionalized:/ˌɪnstɪˈtuːʃənəlaɪzd/ | adj. |制度化的；机构化的；被收容在机构内的 |Placed or kept in an institution, especially a hospital or prison; made a normal part of a formal system or organization. | He had become so institutionalized that he could no longer cope with life on the outside.  他已经被制度化了，以至于无法再应对外面的生活。|  The government has institutionalized racism in its policies. 政府在其政策中使种族主义制度化了。|  Synonyms: established, entrenched, ingrained, rooted, inbuilt | Antonyms: independent, autonomous, free | adjective

institutionalized:/ˌɪnstɪˈtuːʃənəlaɪzd/ | v. |使制度化；使机构化；把…收容在机构内 | To make something a normal part of a formal system or organization; to place or keep someone in an institution. |  Many elderly people are institutionalized against their will. 许多老年人违背自己的意愿被收容在机构中。|  The company has institutionalized a policy of flexible working hours.  公司已经将弹性工作时间制度化。 | Synonyms: established, entrenched, rooted | Antonyms: abolish, dismantle | verb
