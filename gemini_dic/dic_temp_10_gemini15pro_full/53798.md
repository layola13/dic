disconcertedly:/ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrtɪdli/ /ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrtidli/
| adv. | 慌张地，狼狈地，不知所措地 | In a manner that shows one is upset, confused, or embarrassed. | He looked at her disconcertedly. 他慌张地看着她。 | 近义词：nervously, anxiously, uneasily, awkwardly | 反义词：calmly, confidently, composedly | 词性: 副词

disconcert:/ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrt/ /ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrt/
| vt. | 使不安；使惊慌 | To disturb the composure of; unsettle. | The sudden question disconcerted him. 突如其来的问题使他不知所措。| 近义词：unsettle, disturb, ruffle, perturb | 反义词：calm, reassure, comfort | 词性: 动词

词源:
From dis- (expressing negation, reversal) + concert (to arrange, agree).  The original meaning was "to throw into disorder or confusion," later evolving to its current sense of causing emotional unease.
