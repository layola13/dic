forcipation: /fɔːrˈsɪpeɪʃən/
n.
预料，预期; 先发制人
The act of anticipating or forestalling something.
His forcipation of the market crash allowed him to save his investments. 他对市场崩盘的预见使他得以保住自己的投资。

n.
抢先; 占先
The act of seizing something before others can.
The forcipation of the best seats by the early arrivals annoyed those who came later.  早到的人抢占了最好的座位，这让晚到的人很恼火。

近义词: anticipation, foresight, preemption, forestalling
反义词: hindsight, reaction

词性: 名词


词源:  Derived from the Latin word "forceps" (meaning tongs or pincers) + "-ation" (forming a noun of action), implying a seizing or grasping beforehand.
