gargoyles: /ˈɡɑːrˌɡɔɪlz/ | n. |滴水嘴兽，怪兽状雕塑|A carved grotesque figure, typically resembling a deformed human or animal, projecting from the gutter of a building, originally designed as a spout to carry water clear of a wall.| The cathedral is adorned with fascinating gargoyles.  这座大教堂装饰着引人入胜的滴水嘴兽。|Synonyms: grotesque, chimera, monster|Antonyms: beauty, angel| Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gargouille "throat, gullet," from gargouiller "to gurgle," of imitative origin. Related to gargle. The word originally referred to the gurgling sound of water flowing through the spout.
