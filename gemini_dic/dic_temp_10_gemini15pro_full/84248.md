half-smiling:/ˌhɑːfˈsmaɪlɪŋ/ | adj. | 半笑着的，微微笑着的 |  A facial expression showing a slight smile, often suggesting amusement, reserve, or irony. | She gave a half-smiling, half-apologetic look. 她露出了半笑半抱歉的表情。| Near synonyms:  smirking, faintly smiling | Antonyms:  frowning, scowling | adjective

Etymology:
From "half" + "smiling," the present participle of "smile."  "Half" indicates a partial or incomplete action.  "Smile" comes from Middle English *smilen*, of Scandinavian origin.
