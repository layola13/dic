conjuration:/ˌkɒndʒʊˈreɪʃn/ (US) /ˌkɑːndʒəˈreɪʃn/| n.|魔术，魔法；恳求，祈祷|The magician amazed the audience with his feats of conjuration. 魔术师以其精湛的魔术技艺令观众惊叹。|Synonyms: invocation, summoning, spell, incantation, entreaty, plea|Antonyms: dispelling, banishment|noun

conjure:/ˈkʌndʒə(r)/ (UK) /ˈkɑːndʒər/ (US)| v.|变戏法；使…呈现于脑际；恳求，祈求|He seemed to conjure rabbits out of thin air. 他似乎能凭空变出兔子。  She conjured up images of her childhood. 她想起了童年的景象。  He conjured them to tell the truth. 他恳求他们说出真相。|Synonyms: evoke, summon, implore, beseech|Antonyms: banish, dispel|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French conjuration, from Latin coniuratio (“a swearing together, conspiracy, plot”), from coniurare (“to swear together, conspire”), from con- (“together”) + iurare (“to swear”).
