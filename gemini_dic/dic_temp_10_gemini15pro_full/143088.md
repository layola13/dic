outbreak:/ˈaʊtbreɪk/| n. |爆发，突然发生|A sudden occurrence of something unwelcome, such as war or disease.|例句：The outbreak of war caused widespread panic. 战争的爆发引起了 widespread 恐慌。|例句：There was an outbreak of measles in the school. 学校爆发了麻疹。|近义词：eruption, outburst, epidemic, pandemic|反义词：suppression, containment|词性:名词

词源: From out- + break (n.).  Originally referring to a bursting forth of something contained, now especially of something undesirable.
