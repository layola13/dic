comportment:/kəmˈpɔːtmənt/ |n.| 行为；举止；仪态 |  The way a person behaves, especially in public. | Her elegant comportment impressed everyone at the party. 她的优雅举止给派对上的每个人都留下了深刻的印象。| synonym: behavior, conduct, demeanor, bearing | antonym: misbehavior, misconduct | noun

comport:/kəmˈpɔːrt/ |v.|  举止；表现；相称 | To behave in a particular manner; to be in agreement or harmony with. |  He comported himself with dignity throughout the trial. 在整个审判过程中，他举止庄重。  She always comports herself gracefully. 她总是举止优雅。 | synonym: behave, conduct, act, bear | antonym: misbehave | verb


**Etymology (词源):**

From Old French *comporter* "to bear, endure, suffer, contain," from *com-* "together" (from Latin *com-*) + *porter* "to carry, bear" (from Latin *portare*).  The noun "comportment" is derived from the verb.
