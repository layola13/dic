diplostemony:/dɪˌplɒˈstɛməni/ | n. |双轮雄蕊，二强雄蕊|The condition in flowers where the stamens are arranged in two whorls, the outer whorl alternating with the petals and the inner whorl alternating with the sepals.  This arrangement is twice the number of petals (or sepals).|The flower exhibits diplostemony, with two sets of stamens clearly visible.|Synonym: none (highly specific term) | Antonym: none (highly specific term)|noun
