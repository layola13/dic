nonpalatalization:/ˌnɒnpælətəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 非颚化 | The absence of palatalization; the articulation of a sound without raising the tongue towards the hard palate.  It refers to the pronunciation of a consonant without the palatal secondary articulation that occurs in some languages, especially before front vowels. | The nonpalatalization of /k/ before /i/ in some dialects results in a distinct sound, unlike the palatalized version.  非颚化的/k/在某些方言中位于/i/之前会产生一个独特的声音，不同于颚化版本。|  The speaker's distinct nonpalatalization of consonants was a characteristic feature of his regional accent.  说话者明显的辅音非颚化是他区域口音的一个特征。 |  non-palatalisation (British spelling) | palatalization | noun
