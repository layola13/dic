ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hōp, from Proto-Germanic *hōpaz, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *keh₂p- (“to bend, curve”). Cognate with Dutch hoep, German Hopf (“hoop, hop”), dialectal German Hüppe (“hip”), Old Norse hóp (“troop, crowd”).  The verb senses are derived from the noun.
