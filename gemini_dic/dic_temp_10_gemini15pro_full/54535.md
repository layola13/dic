disleave:/dɪsˈliːv/| vt.| 分开，拆散（交错排列的东西）| To separate or un-interweave things that have been interwoven or interleaved. | The wind disleaved the pages of the open book. 风吹散了打开的书页。|  The child disleaved the carefully stacked papers. 孩子把仔细叠好的纸弄散了。| 近义词：separate, disunite, detach, unmix| 反义词：interleave, combine, unite, merge| 词性: verb

Etymology:
From dis- + leave (meaning "to put," from Old English *lǣfan, from Proto-Germanic *laubjaną (“to leave”)), influenced by cleave ("to adhere," from Old English clēofan).
