denigrate:/ˈdɛnɪɡreɪt/| v. |诋毁，贬低|To criticize unfairly; to blacken the reputation of; to disparage.|例句：The politician denigrated his opponent's character during the debate.  这位政治家在辩论中诋毁了对手的品格。

例句：They were quick to denigrate anyone who didn't share their views.  他们很快就会诋毁任何与他们意见不合的人。|近义词：belittle, disparage, defame, slander, vilify|反义词：praise, extol, laud, commend|词性:动词

denigratory:/dɪˈnɪɡrətɔːri/| adj. |诋毁的，贬低的|Showing a critical or disrespectful attitude.|例句：He made denigratory remarks about her appearance. 他对她的外表发表了贬损的言论。

例句：The article was full of denigratory comments about the local community. 这篇文章充满了对当地社区的贬低性评论。|近义词：derogatory, disparaging, belittling|反义词：complimentary, flattering, laudatory|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *denigrāre, from Latin de- (“down, completely”) + nigrāre (“to blacken”), from niger (“black”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *nekʷ- (“to be dark, night”).
