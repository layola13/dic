confrontationalists:/ˌkɑːnfrənˈteɪʃənəlɪsts/
noun
对抗者，喜欢对抗的人
People who tend to deal with situations in an aggressive, challenging, or argumentative way.  They often escalate conflict rather than seeking compromise.
The confrontationalists in the group were determined to challenge the speaker's every point.  小组中那些喜欢对抗的人决心挑战演讲者的每一个观点。
synonyms:  aggressors, challengers, disputants,  provocateurs
antonyms:  pacifists, mediators, conciliators, diplomats
n.

confrontational:/ˌkɑːnfrənˈteɪʃənəl/
adjective
对抗性的，好争论的
Tending to deal with situations in an aggressive way;  involving or likely to cause confrontation.
His confrontational style of management created a hostile work environment. 他对抗性的管理风格营造了一种敌对的工作环境。
synonyms: aggressive, antagonistic, belligerent, hostile
antonyms:  cooperative, conciliatory, diplomatic, pacific
adj.

confrontation:/ˌkɑːnfrənˈteɪʃən/
noun
对抗，冲突
A hostile or argumentative meeting or situation between opposing parties.
The two countries are on the brink of a military confrontation.  这两个国家正处于军事对抗的边缘。
synonyms: clash, conflict, encounter, showdown
antonyms: agreement, compromise, harmony, peace
n.

confront:/kənˈfrʌnt/
verb
面对，对抗
Meet (someone) face to face with hostile or argumentative intent.  Face up to and deal with (a problem or difficult situation).
She decided to confront her fears. 她决定面对自己的恐惧。
He was confronted by an angry mob.  他被一群愤怒的暴徒包围。
synonyms:  challenge, oppose, face, tackle
antonyms: avoid, evade, ignore, retreat
v.
