microtubules: /ˌmaɪkroʊˈtjuːbjuːlz/ | n. | 微管 | Microtubules are hollow, fibrous protein structures found in the cytoplasm of eukaryotic cells. They are part of the cytoskeleton and play crucial roles in cell division, intracellular transport, and maintaining cell shape. | Microtubules form the spindle fibers that separate chromosomes during cell division.  微管形成纺锤体纤维，在细胞分裂过程中分离染色体。

Microtubules act as tracks for motor proteins to transport organelles and vesicles within the cell. 微管充当运动蛋白的轨道，在细胞内运输细胞器和囊泡。 | 近义词: None (as a specific structure) | 反义词: None | 词性: Noun

Etymology: micro- + tubule
"micro-" comes from Greek "mikrós" meaning "small".
"tubule" comes from Latin "tubulus" meaning "small pipe".
