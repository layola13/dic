non-unitarians:/ˌnɒn juːnɪˈtɛəriənz/ | n. | 非一神论者 | People who do not believe in the doctrine of the Trinity, the Christian doctrine that God exists as a unity of three distinct persons: the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. |  In the 19th century, many non-unitarians criticized the Unitarian movement for its perceived coldness and lack of emotional fervor. 19世纪，许多非一神论者批评一神论运动缺乏热情和情感。

Many non-unitarians found the concept of the Trinity confusing and illogical. 许多非一神论者认为三位一体的概念令人困惑且不合逻辑。 | 近义词: non-trinitarians | 反义词: Trinitarians, Unitarians | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  Latin, "not"
unitarian (noun):  From Late Latin *unitarius, from Latin unitas "unity," from unus "one."
