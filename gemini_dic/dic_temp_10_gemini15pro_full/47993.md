decimation:/ˌdesɪˈmeɪʃn/
n.|大批杀害，严重破坏|The decimation of the population by the plague was devastating. 瘟疫对人口的大批杀害是毁灭性的。|
decimation:|n.|抽取，十分之一税|The Roman army practiced decimation as a form of punishment. 罗马军队实行抽杀十分之一作为一种惩罚形式。|
decimation:|n.|大幅减少，锐减|The decimation of the rainforests is a serious environmental problem.  雨林的大幅减少是一个严重的环境问题。|
近义词:destruction, devastation, slaughter, reduction|
反义词:growth, increase, construction|
词性:noun


词源: From Latin *decimatio*, from decimus (“tenth”).  Originally referred to the killing of every tenth soldier in a Roman legion as a punishment for mutiny or other serious offenses.  Later broadened to mean the destruction or killing of a large part of a group, and further generalized to a sharp reduction in number.
