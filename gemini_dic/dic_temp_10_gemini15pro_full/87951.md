high-command:/ˌhaɪ kəˈmænd/ | n. | 最高指挥部；最高统帅部 | The high command made the final decision about the deployment of troops. 最高指挥部对部队的部署作出了最终决定。 | The high-command issued orders for a full-scale retreat. 最高统帅部下令全面撤退。 | 近义词: headquarters, leadership, top brass | 反义词: rank and file, subordinates | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  From 'high' (meaning elevated in rank or importance) + 'command' (from Old French 'comander' and ultimately Latin 'commandare' meaning 'to entrust, commit, order').  The combined meaning refers to those in the highest positions of authority, especially in military contexts.
