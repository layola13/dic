factionalists:/ˈfækʃənəlɪsts/
n.
派系主义者；宗派主义者
People who are involved in or promote internal conflict within a group or organization.  They often prioritize the interests of their own faction over the well-being of the larger group.
The factionalists within the party were constantly fighting for control.  党内的派系主义者不断争夺控制权。
synonyms: partisans, sectarians,  dissidents,  cliquists
antonyms:  uniters, conciliators,  mediators
noun

factionalist:/ˈfækʃənəlɪst/
adj.
派系的；宗派的
Relating to or characterized by factions or internal conflict within a group.
The factionalist disputes threatened to tear the club apart.  派系纠纷有可能使俱乐部四分五裂。
synonyms: partisan, sectarian, divisive
antonyms: unifying, conciliatory,  harmonious
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "faction" (meaning a dissenting group within a larger one) + "-alist" (a suffix denoting a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system). "Faction" ultimately derives from the Latin "factio," meaning "a making, a doing, a party, a faction."
