guelfic: /ˈɡwɛlfɪk/ | adj. | 圭尔夫派的，支持教皇的 | Of or relating to the Guelphs, a faction in medieval Italy and Germany that supported the papacy against the Holy Roman Empire. |  The Guelfic family held considerable power in Florence. 圭尔夫家族在佛罗伦萨拥有相当大的权力。|  The Guelfic cause ultimately triumphed in the conflict. 圭尔夫派的事业最终在冲突中取得了胜利。 | synonyms: papal, pro-papal | antonyms: Ghibelline, imperial | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *Guelfo*, from German *Welf*, the name of a German ducal family. Ultimately from Old High German *hwelp* "cub, young dog."
