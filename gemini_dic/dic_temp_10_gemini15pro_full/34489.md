clays:/kleɪz/
noun
黏土; 泥土
a stiff, sticky fine-grained earth, typically yellow, red, or bluish-gray in color and often forming an impermeable layer in the soil. It can be molded when wet, and is dried and baked to make bricks, pottery, and ceramics.
The potter shaped the clay on his wheel. 陶工在轮子上塑造黏土。
近义词: earth, soil, mud
反义词: None
词性: n.

Etymology: From Old English *clæg (attested in compounds), from Proto-Germanic *klajjaz, from Proto-Indo-European *gley- (“to stick, glue”). Cognate with Middle Low German klei, Dutch klei, German Klei, Old Norse leir.
