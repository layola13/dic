dogmatisms: /ˈdɒɡməˌtɪzəmz/ | n. | 教条主义，武断 |  The tendency to lay down principles as undeniably true, without consideration of evidence or the opinions of others. |  His dogmatisms on the subject alienated many of his followers. 他在这个问题上的教条主义疏远了他的许多追随者。 | Synonyms: doctrinaireism, rigidity, orthodoxy | Antonyms: open-mindedness, flexibility, skepticism | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dogmatismus, from Ancient Greek δόγμα (dógma, “opinion, tenet, ordinance”), from δοκέω (dokéō, “I think, seem, appear”).
