pastes:/peɪsts/| n. v. |糊状物，浆糊；粘贴|A thick, soft, moist substance, typically produced by mixing a dry ingredient with a liquid; the action of applying such a substance to something.|She mixed the flour and water to make a paste. 她把面粉和水混合做成糊状物。|He pastes posters on the wall. 他把海报贴在墙上。|近义词：glues, adheres, sticks|反义词:separates, detaches|词性:名词, 动词

pastes:/peɪsts/| n. |(计算机) 粘贴内容，复制文本 | The text or other data that has been copied and is stored temporarily, ready to be inserted elsewhere. | He copied the paragraph and stored the pastes in the clipboard. 他复制了段落并将粘贴内容存储在剪贴板中。|近义词：copied text, clipboard content |反义词: original text |词性:名词


Etymology: From Middle English *pasten*, from Old French *paster* "to stick on, attach," from Late Latin *pastare* "to feed," from Latin *pastus*, past participle of *pascere* "to feed, graze." The semantic shift likely occurred through the notion of applying a moist, food-like substance.
