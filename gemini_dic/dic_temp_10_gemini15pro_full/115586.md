marats:/ˈmæræts/| n. |马拉特人|Marats is a plural form referring to the members of a historical Hindu warrior caste from western India, particularly associated with the Maratha Empire (1674-1818).  They were known for their cavalry tactics and played a significant role in Indian history.|The Marats, under Shivaji's leadership, challenged the Mughal Empire.|近义词：Marathas|反义词: (No direct antonym; potentially enemies of the Maratha Empire like the Mughals could be considered contextually opposite)|词性:noun
