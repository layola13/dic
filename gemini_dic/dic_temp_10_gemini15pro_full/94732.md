improvablenesses: /ɪmˈpruːvəblnəsɪz/
n.
可改进性；可改善性
the quality of being improvable; the capacity for improvement.
The improvablenesses of the education system are being debated.  教育系统的可改进性正在被辩论。
The report highlighted the improvablenesses of the company's current practices. 报告强调了公司现有实践的可改进性。
近义词:  malleability, plasticity, adaptability
反义词:  incorrigibility, fixity, immutability
词性:  noun (plural)


improvable: /ɪmˈpruːvəbl/
adj.
可改进的；可改善的
capable of being improved.
Her writing skills are improvable with practice. 她的写作技巧可以通过练习来改进。
The current situation is definitely improvable. 目前的状况肯定是可以改善的。
近义词:  amendable,  betterable,  enhancable
反义词:  perfect, unimprovable, immutable
词性: adjective


improve: /ɪmˈpruːv/
v.
改进；改善
make or become better.
He is working to improve his French. 他正在努力提高他的法语水平。
The weather improved in the afternoon. 下午天气好转了。
近义词:  enhance, better, ameliorate
反义词:  worsen, deteriorate, decline
词性: verb


-able: /-əbl/
suffix
可…的；能…的
capable of; susceptible of; tending to; given to.
This is a washable fabric. 这是一种可洗涤的织物。
He's a reliable person. 他是一个可靠的人。
近义词: -ible
反义词:  None (suffixes generally don't have antonyms)
词性: suffix


-ness: /nəs/
suffix
…的状态；…的性质
forms nouns chiefly from adjectives or participles, often denoting a state or quality.
Happiness is a state of mind. 幸福是一种心态。
Kindness is a virtue. 善良是一种美德。
近义词: None (suffixes generally don't have antonyms)
反义词: None (suffixes generally don't have antonyms)
词性: suffix

词源:
From "improve" + "-able" + "-ness".  "Improve" comes from the Old French "emprover" (to make a profit from, to put to good use), which ultimately derives from the Latin "probare" (to test, to approve).
