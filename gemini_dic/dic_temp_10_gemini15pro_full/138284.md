notarizes:/ˈnəʊtəˌraɪzɪz/
|v.|公证；证明|Officially certifies or validates a document. |Example: The lawyer notarizes the signature on the will. 律师公证了遗嘱上的签名。|Synonyms: certifies, validates, authenticates, legalizes|Antonyms: invalidates, rejects, denies|Verb (Third person singular present tense)


notarize:/ˈnəʊtəˌraɪz/
|v.|公证；证明|To certify or attest to (something, such as a document) officially. |Example: I need to have this document notarized. 我需要这份文件被公证。|Synonyms: certify, validate, authenticate, legalize|Antonyms: invalidate, reject, deny|Verb


notary:/ˈnəʊtəri/
|n.|公证人|A person authorized to perform certain legal formalities, especially to certify signatures or documents.|Example:  We went to a notary to have our documents signed and sealed. 我们去找公证人签字盖章。|Synonyms:  notary public|Antonyms: N/A|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *notarien*, from Old French *notarier*, from Latin *notārius* (“shorthand writer, secretary”), from *nota* (“mark, note, shorthand character”).
