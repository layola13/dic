indoctrinators:/ɪnˈdɑːktrɪneɪtərz/ | n. |灌输者；教导者|People who indoctrinate others, especially with a particular ideology or set of beliefs.|The cult leaders were skilled indoctrinators, manipulating their followers into unquestioning obedience.|近义词:teachers, instructors, propagandists, brainwashers|反义词:learners, students, skeptics|词性:名词

Etymology: From the verb "indoctrinate," which comes from the Latin "in-" (meaning "in" or "into") + "doctrina" (meaning "teaching" or "doctrine").
