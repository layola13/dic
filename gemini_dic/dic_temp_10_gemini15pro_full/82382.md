grub:/ɡrʌb/ | n. v. | 幼虫；食物；挖；翻找 | A soft, white larva of certain beetles, especially one that lives in soil and feeds on roots. Also, informally, food. As a verb, it means to dig superficially for food or other items. | The grubs are destroying my lawn. 这些幼虫正在毁坏我的草坪。| Synonyms: larva, maggot (n.); food, victuals (n.); forage, rummage (v.) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for grub in all its senses) | noun, verb

grub:/ɡrʌb/ | v. |  翻找；搜寻 | To search for something by digging or rummaging. | He grubbed around in the attic for old photographs. 他在阁楼里翻找旧照片。 | Synonyms: forage, rummage, search | Antonyms: (No direct antonyms) | verb
