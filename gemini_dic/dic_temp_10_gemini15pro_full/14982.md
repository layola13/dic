babbittry: /ˈbæbɪtri/
babbittry| n. |庸俗；市侩；陈腐的道德观|A narrow, self-satisfied, and complacent attitude, associated with conventional middle-class values. |His babbittry prevented him from appreciating the true beauty of the art. 他的市侩阻止了他欣赏艺术的真正美。|Synonyms: philistinism, materialism, conventionality|Antonyms: unconventionality, bohemianism, nonconformity|noun


词源:
From the character George F. Babbitt in Sinclair Lewis's 1922 satirical novel *Babbitt*. Babbitt is portrayed as a complacent, materialistic, and conformist businessman.
