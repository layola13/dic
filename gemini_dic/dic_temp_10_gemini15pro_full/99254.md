intercomparison:/ˌɪntərəmˈpærɪsn/ | n. |相互比较，比对|The intercomparison of different models helps to identify their strengths and weaknesses. |  Different climate models were subjected to intercomparison to assess their accuracy.| synonyms: comparison, correlation, cross-checking, benchmarking | antonyms: isolation, separation | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From inter- + comparison.  "Inter-" is a prefix meaning "between" or "among," derived from Latin. "Comparison" comes from the Late Middle English "comparisoun," ultimately from the Latin "comparare," meaning "to compare."
