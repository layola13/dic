caltrop:/ˈkælˌtræp/| n. |蒺藜，铁蒺藜；刺蒺藜；星头钉|A metal object with four sharp points arranged so that when it is thrown on the ground, one point always points upward, used to impede pursuit or the movement of vehicles.|The police scattered caltrops across the road to stop the escaping car. 警方在路上撒了蒺藜以阻止逃跑的车辆。|Synonyms: spike, jack, crow's-foot|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "smooth surface")|noun

caltrop:/ˈkælˌtræp/| n. |(植物)蒺藜|A plant with spiny fruit, common in warm regions.|Caltrops are a common weed in some areas. 蒺藜在某些地区是一种常见的杂草。|Synonyms: puncture vine, goathead|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for the plant itself)|noun
