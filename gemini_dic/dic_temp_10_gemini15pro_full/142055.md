organum:/ˈɔːrɡənəm/ | n. |奥尔加农，管风琴，早期复调音乐|A form of early polyphony in which a new voice or voices are added to an existing plainchant melody.|The Notre Dame school of music was famous for its organa.  巴黎圣母院乐派以其奥尔加农而闻名。|The choir sang a beautiful organum.  唱诗班演唱了一曲优美的奥尔加农。|Synonyms: polyphony, counterpoint|Antonyms: monophony|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin organum "musical instrument, organ," from Greek organon "instrument, tool, organ of the body," literally "that with which one works," from PIE root *werg- "to work, do." The musical sense developed from the association of the pipe organ with polyphonic music.
