concomitancy:/kənˈkɒmɪtənsi/ /ˌkɑːnkəˈmɪtənsi/ | n. |伴随，共存|The state of occurring or existing at the same time; the state of accompanying or coexisting.|The concomitancy of economic growth and environmental protection is a key challenge for the 21st century. 21世纪的关键挑战是经济增长与环境保护的并存。|近义词：coexistence, concurrence, simultaneity|反义词:non-concomitance,  dissociation|词性:名词

concomitant:/kənˈkɒmɪtənt/ /ˌkɑːnkəˈmɪtənt/ | adj. |伴随的，共存的|Naturally accompanying or associated.|The study found a concomitant increase in crime rates with rising unemployment. 研究发现，犯罪率随着失业率的上升而同步增长。|近义词：accompanying, attendant, concurrent, coincident|反义词:unrelated, separate|词性:形容词

concomitantly:/kənˈkɒmɪtəntli/ /ˌkɑːnkəˈmɪtəntli/ | adv. |伴随地，同时发生地|In a concomitant manner; concurrently.|The patient experienced fever and chills concomitantly. 病人同时经历了发烧和发冷。|近义词：concurrently, simultaneously, together|反义词:separately, independently|词性:副词



USAGE-> 词源:  From Late Latin *concomitantia, from concomitantem (nominative concomitans), present participle of concomitari "to accompany," from com- "together" + comitari "to accompany."

concomitant symptoms:  伴随症状  (e.g.,  The patient presented with fever and concomitant symptoms of headache and fatigue.)
concomitant factors: 伴随因素 (e.g., Several concomitant factors contributed to the economic downturn.)
concomitant changes: 伴随的变化 (e.g.,  The social changes were concomitant with technological advancements.)
