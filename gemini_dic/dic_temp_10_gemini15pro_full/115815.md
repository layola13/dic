markedness:/ˈmɑːrkɪdnəs/ | n. |显著性；标记性|  The property of a linguistic unit that stands out as unusual or difficult compared to a more basic or unmarked form.  It refers to the state of being marked, or distinguished, from a more neutral or common form.  |  The markedness of the plural form in English is demonstrated by the various irregular plural formations, unlike the regular "-s" ending.  英语中复数形式的显著性体现在各种不规则复数的构成，不像规则的“-s”结尾。

The use of the passive voice can be seen as a marked form in English, as the active voice is generally preferred. 使用被动语态可以被视为英语中的一种标记形式，因为通常优先使用主动语态。|  salience, distinctiveness | neutrality, unmarkedness | noun
