estrapade:/ˌɛstrəˈpeɪd/ | n. | 刑罚；严厉的惩罚；（尤指）吊刑 | A form of torture, especially by hoisting someone up by the hands tied behind their back and then letting them fall almost to the ground. |  The estrapade was a brutal form of punishment used in the Middle Ages. 中世纪时期，吊刑是一种残酷的惩罚方式。 | synonyms: torture, torment, ordeal | antonyms: comfort, solace | noun

etymology: From French *estrapade*, from Italian *strappare* "to pull, tear," from Vulgar Latin *extrappare*, from Latin *extirpare* "to root out, pluck up."
