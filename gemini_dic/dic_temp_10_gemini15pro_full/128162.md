nettle:/ˈnetl/ (US), /ˈnetəl/ (UK)| n. v.|荨麻; 激怒|A herbaceous plant that has jagged leaves covered with stinging hairs.  As a verb, it means to irritate or annoy someone.|

例句：
The nettles stung my legs as I walked through the tall grass.
我的腿在穿过高高的草丛时被荨麻刺痛了。

例句：
His constant criticism nettled her.
他不断的批评激怒了她。

近义词：irritate, annoy, vex, provoke, irk|
反义词：soothe, calm, please|
词性：noun, verb|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *netele, from Proto-Germanic *natilō, from Proto-Indo-European *nādlā, meaning "needle".  This refers to the stinging hairs of the plant.
