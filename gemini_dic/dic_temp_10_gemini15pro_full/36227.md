cognation:/kɒɡˈneɪʃn/ | /kɑːɡˈneɪʃn/ | n. | 同源；同族关系；认知 |  Relationship by descent from a common ancestor; kinship. cognate:/ˈkɒɡneɪt/ | /ˈkɑːɡneɪt/ | adj. | 同源的；同族的 | Related by birth; descended from a common ancestor. Related in origin, nature, or function. |  Spanish and Italian are cognate languages. 西班牙语和意大利语是同源语言。 | related, kindred, allied, similar | unrelated, dissimilar | adjective

cognate:/ˈkɒɡneɪt/ | /ˈkɑːɡneɪt/ | n. | 同源词；同族 | A word related to another in a different language, derived from the same ancestor word. |  "Night" in English and "Nacht" in German are cognates.  英语中的“Night”和德语中的“Nacht”是同源词。 | relative, counterpart, equivalent | antonym (for noun form is less common and context-dependent) | noun
