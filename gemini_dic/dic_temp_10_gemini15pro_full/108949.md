leered:/lɪərd/| v. | 睨视，斜视，不怀好意地看 | To look or gaze in a lascivious, sly, or malicious way. | He leered at her from across the bar. 他在吧台对面色眯眯地看着她。| synonyms: ogled, gawked, stared, glared | antonyms: glanced, ignored | verb

leering:/ˈlɪərɪŋ/| adj. | 睨视的，不怀好意地看的 | Characterized by a lascivious, sly, or malicious look. | He gave her a leering look. 他色眯眯地看了她一眼。| synonyms: lascivious, sly, malicious | antonyms: innocent, kind | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English leren, from Old English lēran (“to leer, look askance”), from Proto-Germanic *laizijaną (“to look askance”), from Proto-Indo-European *lois- (“track, furrow”).
