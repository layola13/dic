conject:/kənˈdʒɛkt/| v.|推测，猜想|To form an opinion or supposition about (something) on the basis of incomplete information.|例句：Scientists can only conject the cause of the extinction of dinosaurs. 科学家们只能推测恐龙灭绝的原因。|例句：He conjectured that the company would soon go bankrupt. 他推测这家公司很快就会破产。|近义词：guess, surmise, speculate, hypothesize|反义词：know, prove|词性:动词

conjecture:/kənˈdʒɛktʃər/| n.|推测，猜想|An opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information.|例句：The origin of the universe is still a matter of conjecture. 宇宙的起源仍然是一个推测的问题。|例句：His conjecture about the stock market proved to be correct. 他对股市的推测被证明是正确的。|近义词：guess, surmise, speculation, hypothesis|反义词：fact, certainty, knowledge|词性:名词

词源: Late Middle English: from Latin conjectura, from conicere ‘throw together’, from con- ‘together’ + jacere ‘throw’.
