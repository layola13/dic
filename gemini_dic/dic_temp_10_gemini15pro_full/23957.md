breastplates:/ˈbrɛstpleɪts/| n. |胸甲|A piece of armour covering the chest.|The knight polished his breastplate until it gleamed. 骑士把他的胸甲擦得闪闪发光。|Synonyms: armor, cuirass|Antonyms: None|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *brestplate*, from *brest* (breast) + *plate* (plate).  Ultimately from Old English *brēost* (breast) and Old French *plate* (plate), from Late Latin *platea* (broad street, open space), from Greek *πλατεῖα* (plateia, broad street).
