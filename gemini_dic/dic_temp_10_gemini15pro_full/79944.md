gnosticism:/ˈnɒstɪsɪzəm/ (US) /ˈnɒstɪsɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |诺斯替主义|A religious system, originating in the first century AD, that combined elements of Christianity, Judaism, and Greek philosophy. It emphasized personal spiritual knowledge (gnosis) above orthodox teachings and practices, often involving esoteric or mystical interpretations of scripture and the belief in a dualistic cosmology of good and evil.|Many scholars see traces of gnosticism in early Christian writings.|Near synonyms: esotericism, mysticism|Antonym: orthodoxy|Noun
