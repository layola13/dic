airling:/ˈeərlɪŋ/| n.|小鸟；轻浮的人；空中飞行物|A young bird, or a creature of the air; also used figuratively to describe a light or frivolous person.  A vehicle designed for air travel.|The airling took its first flight from the nest.|近义词：fledgling, chick, bird, waif, lightweight|反义词: expert, professional|Noun

词源: From Middle English *erlyng, derived from Old English *ēarling, a diminutive of ēar "early".  The sense shifted over time to refer to anything young or newly born, especially birds.  The connection to air likely reinforced this meaning, giving rise to the current sense.
