cut across:/kʌt əˈkrɒs/ (US) /kʌt əˈkrɔːs/ (UK)
|phrasal verb|
|横穿，抄近路；超越；影响到；与…相抵触|
|To cross an area rather than going around it; to transcend or affect different groups or areas; to be inconsistent with or contradict something.|
|We cut across the field to save time. 我们抄近路穿过田野以节省时间。|
|近义词:  traverse, cross, shortcut, transcend|
|反义词:  circumnavigate, go around|
|词性:  phrasal verb|


cut across:/kʌt əˈkrɒs/ (US) /kʌt əˈkrɔːs/ (UK)
|n.|
|捷径，横穿的道路|
|A shortcut across an area.|
|The path through the woods was a useful cut across to the village. 穿过树林的小路是到村庄的一条捷径。|
|近义词: shortcut, pathway|
|反义词: detour, long way around|
|词性: noun|
