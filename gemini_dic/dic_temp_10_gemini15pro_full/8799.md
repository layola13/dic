antiempiricism:/ˌæntiɛmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 反经验主义 | The doctrine that knowledge is not derived solely or primarily from sensory experience.  It emphasizes the role of reason, intuition, or innate ideas in acquiring knowledge. |  Philosophers like Plato and Descartes are often seen as proponents of antiempiricism. 柏拉图和笛卡尔等哲学家通常被视为反经验主义的支持者。 |  Kant's transcendental idealism is a form of antiempiricism. 康德的先验唯心主义是反经验主义的一种形式。 | rationalism, apriorism, idealism | empiricism | noun
