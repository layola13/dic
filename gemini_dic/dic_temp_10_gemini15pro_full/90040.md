hospice:/ˈhɑːspɪs/ (US), /ˈhɒspɪs/ (UK)| n.|收容所，临终关怀医院|A home providing care for the sick, especially the terminally ill.|例句：My grandmother spent her last days in a hospice. 我的祖母在临终关怀医院度过了她最后的日子。|例句：The hospice provides comfort and support to patients and their families.  临终关怀医院为病人及其家属提供安慰和支持。|近义词：hospital, sanatorium, asylum|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

hospital:/ˈhɑːspɪtl/ (US), /ˈhɒspɪtl/ (UK)| n.|医院|A place where people who are ill or injured are treated.|例句：He was rushed to the hospital after the accident. 事故发生后，他被紧急送往医院。|例句：She works as a nurse at the local hospital. 她在当地医院当护士。|近义词：clinic, infirmary|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *hospice "hospital," from Latin hospitium "guest house, lodging, hospitality," from hospes (genitive hospitis) "host, guest."  The specialized sense of "a place for the terminally ill" developed in the 19th century.
