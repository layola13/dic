flincher:/ˈflɪntʃər/| n. |退缩者；畏缩的人|A person who flinches, who shrinks back or avoids something unpleasant or difficult. |He's a flincher when it comes to hard work. 他在艰苦的工作面前是个退缩者。|Synonyms: coward, shirker, avoider|Antonyms: daredevil, risk-taker|noun


flinch:/flɪntʃ/ | v. |畏缩；退缩|To make a sudden, involuntary movement of the body, typically as a result of pain or fear.|She flinched at the sound of the gunshot. 她听到枪声吓得畏缩了一下。|Synonyms: recoil, wince, shrink|Antonyms: brave, face, confront|verb
