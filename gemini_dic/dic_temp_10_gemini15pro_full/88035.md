highbrow:/ˈhaɪbraʊ/| adj.|高雅的；知识分子的；曲高和寡的|Appealing to or considered suitable for highly educated and sophisticated people.|例句：The museum's new exhibit is quite highbrow.  博物馆的新展览相当高雅。|例句：She prefers highbrow literature to popular novels.  比起流行小说，她更喜欢高雅文学。|近义词：intellectual, sophisticated, cultured, refined|反义词：lowbrow, unsophisticated, popular|词性:形容词
Etymology:  From the phrenological theory that a high forehead indicated high intelligence.  "High" referring to the forehead and "brow" referring to the forehead itself.
