emasculating:/ɪˈmæskjʊleɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人丧失男子气概的；使人衰弱的 |  Depriving a man of his male role or identity; making someone feel weak or less effective. |  His constant criticism was emasculating. 他不断的批评使人丧失男子气概。 | synonyms: weakening, debilitating, unmanning, castrating | antonyms: empowering, strengthening, invigorating | adjective

emasculate:/ɪˈmæskjʊleɪt/ | vt. | 阉割；使丧失男子气概；使衰弱 |  1. To castrate. 2. To deprive a man of his male role or identity. 3. To make someone feel weak or less effective. |  The surgery emasculated him.  手术阉割了他。 The king emasculated the parliament by taking away its power. 国王通过剥夺议会的权力来削弱它。| synonyms: weaken, debilitate, unman, castrate | antonyms: empower, strengthen, invigorate | verb

词源： From Latin *emasculatus*, past participle of *emasculare* "to castrate," from *ex* "out" + *masculinus* "male, masculine," from *masculus* "male."
