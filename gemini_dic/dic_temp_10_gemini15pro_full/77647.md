gelatinously:/dʒɪˈlætɪnəsli/
adjective
胶状地；像胶状的
Resembling gelatin; having a jelly-like consistency.
The jellyfish drifted gelatinously through the water. 水母胶状地漂浮在水中。

The substance quivered gelatinously in the petri dish.  培养皿中的物质胶状地颤动着。

viscous, jelly-like, wobbly
firm, solid, rigid
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From gelatin + -ous + -ly.  Gelatin itself comes from the French gélatine, from the Italian gelatina, from Late Latin gelata, meaning "frozen," from the past participle of gelare, "to freeze."  This refers to the process of creating gelatin.
