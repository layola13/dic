cloyed:/klɔɪd/| adj.|厌腻的，腻烦的|Feeling disgust or aversion as a result of overindulgence or excess.|After eating too much candy, he felt cloyed.|近义词：satiated, surfeited, jaded|反义词：unsatisfied, eager, keen|词性:形容词
词源: From Middle English *cloyen*, from Old French *cloer* (“to nail, to stop up, to spike (a gun)”), from Vulgar Latin *claudere*, from Latin *claudere* (“to close, to shut”).  The sense development is from "to stop up, obstruct" to "to fill to excess" to "to cause disgust by excess".
