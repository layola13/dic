parrier's:/ˈpærɪərz/| n.|格斗者的；招架者的；防御者的|The word "parrier's" is the possessive form of "parrier," meaning "one who parries." It refers to something belonging to or associated with a person skilled in deflecting or warding off attacks, particularly in sword fighting or other forms of combat.|This is the parrier's sword. 这是那位格斗者的剑。|近义词：defender's, blocker's, protector's|反义词:attacker's, aggressor's|词性:名词

词源:
From French "parer" (to ward off, parry), derived from Latin "parare" (to prepare).  The "-er" suffix denotes the agent performing the action, and the "'s" indicates possession.
