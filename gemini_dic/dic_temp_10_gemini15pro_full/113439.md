ETYMOLOGY->
From Lynch Law, referring to Charles Lynch (1736–96), a Virginia planter and justice of the peace, or to his brother William, who supposedly used the term to describe his extrajudicial methods of punishing loyalists during the American Revolution.  来自林奇法，指的是弗吉尼亚种植园主兼治安法官查尔斯·林奇（1736-96 年），或者指他的兄弟威廉，据说他在美国独立战争期间用这个词来描述他对保皇党人的法外惩罚方法。
