moulted:/ˈmoʊltɪd (US), ˈməʊltɪd (UK)| v. |蜕皮; 换毛|shed or cast off (feathers, skin, or hair) periodically, especially as a prelude to new growth.|The snake moulted its skin. 这条蛇蜕了皮。|Synonyms: shed, cast off, slough, exuviate|Antonyms: grow, develop|verb

moulting:/ˈmoʊltɪŋ (US), ˈməʊltɪŋ (UK)| n. |蜕皮; 换毛|the process of shedding or casting off feathers, skin, or hair periodically.|The bird's moulting season is in the spring. 这只鸟的换羽季节在春天。|Synonyms: shedding, casting off, sloughing, exuviation|Antonyms: growth, development|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mouten, from Old English *mutian, of uncertain origin.  Cognate with German *mausen ("to moult").
