clocked:/klɒkt/ (UK) /klɑːkt/ (US)| v. |计时；达到（某速度）；注意到；打（某人）|To measure or record the speed or time of something or someone.  Also, to hit someone. |例句：The police clocked him driving at 90 miles per hour. 警察测出他的车速为每小时90英里。

例句：He clocked the runner at 10 seconds for the 100 meters. 他记录下这名跑步者100米跑了10秒。|近义词：timed, recorded, registered, observed, hit, punched |反义词: missed (for hitting)|词性:动词

clocked:/klɒkt/ (UK) /klɑːkt/ (US)| adj. |（非正式）被注意到的；被看到的| Seen or noticed.  Often used in the phrase "clocked it" or "clocked him/her/them." |例句：I totally clocked your new haircut. 我完全注意到了你的新发型。

例句：She clocked him staring at her from across the room. 她注意到他从房间的另一头盯着她。|近义词：noticed, observed, spotted |反义词: missed, overlooked|词性:形容词
