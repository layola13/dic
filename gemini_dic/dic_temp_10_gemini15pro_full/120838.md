misaward:/ˌmɪsəˈwɔːrd/
n.|错误判决，误判|An incorrect or unjust award, especially in a legal context.  A decision that gives something (like a prize or money) to the wrong person.|The judge's misaward of custody caused widespread outrage.|近义词：wrongful award, unjust decision, erroneous judgment|反义词：fair award, just decision, correct judgment|词性:名词


词源:  "mis-" (prefix meaning "wrong" or "bad") + "award" (verb meaning "to give or grant something as a prize or payment").  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old English "mis-", meaning "wrongly, badly, amiss." "Award" comes from Old North French "awarder," meaning "to adjudge, determine."
