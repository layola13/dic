imposer:/ɪmˈpəʊzər/ (British) /ɪmˈpoʊzər/ (American)| n.|冒名顶替者；强加者；征税人|Someone who pretends to be someone else in order to deceive people; someone who forces their opinions or beliefs on others; someone who imposes something, especially a tax or burden.|He was exposed as an imposter, pretending to be a doctor. 他被揭露是一个冒充医生的骗子。|Synonyms: pretender, fraud, charlatan, fake, cheat, deceiver, usurper,  dictator |Antonyms: genuine person, authentic individual|noun

imposer:/ɪmˈpəʊzər/ (British) /ɪmˈpoʊzər/ (American)| n.|强加于人者|Someone who imposes something, especially a tax or burden.|The government, seen as the imposter of new taxes, was unpopular.  被视为新税强加者的政府很不受欢迎。|Synonyms: enforcer, dictator, authoritarian|Antonyms: liberator, emancipator|noun
