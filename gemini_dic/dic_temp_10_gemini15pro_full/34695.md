cleronomy: /klɪˈrɒnəmi/
n.
世袭财产; 继承的份额; 份地
An inheritance or allotted portion of land.  The system of land inheritance.
Cleronomy refers to the system in ancient Israel where land was allotted to families and passed down through generations.  This ensured a stable social structure and prevented land accumulation by a few.
The cleronomy of the tribe of Judah was the largest.  犹大支派的份地是最大的。
The ancient Israelites practiced cleronomy to distribute land among families. 古代以色列人实行份地制，在家庭之间分配土地。

近义词: inheritance, patrimony, legacy
反义词: disinheritance

词性: noun

词源: From Greek *klēronomia*, from *klēronomos* "heir," from *klēros* "lot" + *nemein* "to distribute."
