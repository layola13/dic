fast-tracks:/ˈfæstˌtræks/ /fɑːst træks/| v. n.|快速通道，快速处理|To accelerate the progress of something, especially by expediting a usually lengthy process.|The new regulations fast-track the approval process for certain types of drugs. 新规定快速审批某些类型的药物。|The project was fast-tracked due to its importance. 由于项目的重要性，它被快速跟进了。|近义词：expedite, accelerate, hasten|反义词:delay, hinder, obstruct|词性:动词, 名词


fast-track:/ˈfæstˌtræk/ /fɑːst træk/| n.|快速通道|A route or method that provides for more rapid results than usual.|She was put on a fast track to partnership. 她被快速提升为合伙人。|The company offers a fast track to management for high-performing employees. 公司为表现优异的员工提供快速晋升管理层的途径。|近义词：express route, shortcut|反义词:standard route, slow lane|词性:名词

词源:  "Fast track" originated in American English in the mid-20th century, combining "fast" (meaning quick) and "track" (referring to a course or path).  It initially referred to expedited legal procedures, then broadened to encompass various accelerated processes.
