commiseration:/kəˌmɪzəˈreɪʃn/| n. |同情，怜悯|a feeling of sympathy and sorrow for the misfortunes of others; the expression of sympathy for someone|例句：She offered her commiserations on his loss. 她对他的损失表示同情。

例句：He received many expressions of commiseration after his accident. 事故发生后，他收到了许多慰问。

近义词：sympathy, compassion, condolence, pity|反义词：indifference, apathy|词性:noun

commiserate:/kəˈmɪzəreɪt/| v. |同情，怜悯|express or feel sympathy or pity; sympathize.|例句：She commiserated with him over his loss. 她对他的损失表示同情。

例句：I commiserated with her after she failed the exam. 她考试不及格后，我表示同情。


近义词：sympathize, empathize, console|反义词：ignore, disregard|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *commiseratio, from Latin commiserari "to feel pity," from com- "with" + miserari "to pity," from miser "wretched, unfortunate."
