mitre:/ˈmaɪtər/ (US), /ˈmʌɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n.|主教冠; 斜接; 斜接角|A tall, pointed hat worn by bishops and other high church officials. Also, a joint made by cutting two pieces of material at an angle and fitting them together.|The bishop wore a magnificent gold mitre. 主教戴着一顶华丽的金冠。|Synonyms: headdress, crown, tiara|Antonyms: N/A|noun

mitre:/ˈmaɪtər/ (US), /ˈmʌɪtə(r)/ (UK)| vt.|斜切，斜接|To join two pieces of material by cutting them at an angle and fitting them together.|He mitred the corners of the picture frame. 他斜切了画框的角。|Synonyms: bevel, join|Antonyms: separate|verb
