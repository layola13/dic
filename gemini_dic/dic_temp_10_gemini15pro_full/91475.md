hylozoic:/ˌhaɪləˈzoʊɪk/ | adj. | 泛灵论的; 物活论的 |  The doctrine that all matter, or all nature, is in some sense alive.  It attributes life to all matter, especially to the universe as a whole. |  Some ancient philosophers held hylozoic beliefs, seeing life force in everything.  一些古代哲学家持有物活论的信仰，认为万物皆有生命力。|  The author explored the hylozoic theory in his novel, depicting a world where even rocks possessed a form of consciousness. 作者在他的小说中探讨了物活论，描绘了一个即使是岩石也拥有一种意识形态的世界。 | animistic, pantheistic | materialistic, mechanistic | adjective
