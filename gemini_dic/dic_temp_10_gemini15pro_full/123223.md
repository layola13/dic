monoconsonantal:/ˌmɒnəkɒnˈsɒnəntl/ /ˌmɑːnəkɑːnˈsɑːnəntl/| adj.|单辅音的|Consisting of or characterized by a single consonant.  Relating to a root or stem containing a single consonant.|Semitic languages have many monoconsonantal roots.|近义词:uniconsonantal|反义词:polyconsonantal|词性:adjective
Etymology:  From mono- "one" + consonantal.  "Mono-" ultimately from Greek monos "alone, single." "Consonantal" from consonant + -al.  "Consonant" from Latin consonans, present participle of consonare "to sound together," from con- "together" + sonare "to sound."
