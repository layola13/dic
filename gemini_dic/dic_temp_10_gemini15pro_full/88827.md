hoggery:/ˈhɒɡəri/ (US) /ˈhɑːɡəri/ (UK)| n.|贪婪，自私，霸占|Excessive greed or selfishness; the practice of taking more than one's share.|He was accused of hoggery, grabbing all the best contracts for himself.|近义词：greed, avarice, selfishness, gluttony|反义词：generosity, altruism|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From hog + -ery.  "Hog" meaning a pig, often used figuratively to describe someone who is greedy or selfish.  The suffix "-ery" forms nouns denoting a place, condition, or practice.  Thus, "hoggery" refers to the practice of behaving like a hog, being greedy and selfish.
