bogus:/ˈboʊɡəs/| adj.|假的，伪造的，虚伪的|Something that is bogus is fake, fraudulent, or counterfeit. It can also refer to something that is spurious or sham.|例句：The company was selling bogus products. 这家公司在销售假冒产品。|例句：He presented bogus credentials to get the job. 他出示了伪造的证件以获得这份工作。|近义词：fake, counterfeit, spurious, sham, phony, fraudulent|反义词：genuine, authentic, real, legitimate|词性:形容词
boguses:/ˈboʊɡəsɪz/| n.|假货，伪造品|The plural form of "bogus," referring to multiple fake or counterfeit items.|例句：The police seized a shipment of boguses. 警方查获了一批假货。|例句：The market was flooded with boguses, making it difficult to find genuine products.  市场上充斥着假货，很难找到正品。|近义词：fakes, counterfeits, phonies|反义词：genuines, authentics|词性:名词

词源：Originating in the United States in the 1800s, "bogus" initially referred to a counterfeiting machine.  Its meaning later broadened to encompass anything fake or fraudulent.  The exact etymology is uncertain, but some theories link it to a slang term for counterfeit money.
