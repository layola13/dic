generality:/ˌdʒɛnəˈrælɪti/ | n. | 概论，通则；普遍性；大多数 | a statement or principle having general rather than specific validity or force |  The lecture dealt in generalities, avoiding any specific examples.  讲座泛泛而谈，没有举任何具体的例子。 | synonyms: abstract, abstraction, concept, generalization, principle | antonyms: specific, particular, concrete | noun

generality:/ˌdʒɛnəˈrælɪti/ | n. | 笼统，模糊 | the quality or state of being general | He spoke in generalities about the need for reform. 他笼统地谈了改革的必要性。 | synonyms: vagueness, ambiguity, imprecision | antonyms: precision, specificity, clarity | noun

generality:/ˌdʒɛnəˈrælɪti/ | n. | 大多数，大部分 | the greater part or number; the bulk |  In generality, people are law-abiding citizens.  总的来说，人们都是遵纪守法的公民。 | synonyms: majority, bulk, mass, preponderance | antonyms: minority, few | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *generalte*, from Old French generalité, from Late Latin generalitas, from Latin generalis (general).
