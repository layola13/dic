against-the-grain:/əˈɡenst ðə ɡreɪn/| adj. adv.|逆着潮流的，违反意愿的，格格不入的|Going against the grain means doing something that is difficult or unpleasant, or that is different from what is usual or expected.  It can also refer to the direction of the fibers in wood, and working against this direction makes tasks more difficult.|Sawing against the grain can splinter the wood. 逆着木纹锯会使木头裂开。|近义词:unnatural, difficult, awkward, rebellious, unconventional|反义词:natural, easy, conventional, compliant|词性:形容词, 副词

词源: This idiom originates from carpentry and woodworking. When working with wood, going "with the grain" is easier and produces a smoother result. Going "against the grain" is more difficult and can result in a rough, splintered finish.  The metaphorical meaning developed from this literal meaning.
