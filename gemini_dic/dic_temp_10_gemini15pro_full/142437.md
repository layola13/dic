oscillate:/ˈɒsɪleɪt/ (UK), /ˈɑːsɪleɪt/ (US)| v. |振荡，摆动，犹豫|To swing back and forth repeatedly between two points, positions, or states.  To vary or fluctuate between two opinions, beliefs, or courses of action. |The pendulum oscillated back and forth. 钟摆来回摆动。 The stock market oscillated wildly throughout the day. 股市全天剧烈波动。|近义词：swing, vibrate, fluctuate, waver|反义词: stabilize, steady|词性:动词

oscillation:/ˌɒsɪˈleɪʃn/ (UK), /ˌɑːsɪˈleɪʃn/ (US)| n. |振荡，摆动，犹豫|The act or state of oscillating; a fluctuation between two or more states or conditions.|The oscillation of the fan kept us cool. 风扇的摆动让我们保持凉爽。 The scientist observed the oscillations of the magnetic field. 科学家观察了磁场的振荡。|近义词：vibration, fluctuation, swing|反义词: stability, steadiness|词性:名词

oscillator:/ˈɒsɪleɪtə(r)/ (UK), /ˈɑːsɪleɪtər/ (US)| n. |振荡器，摆动物|A device that produces oscillations, especially of electrical or mechanical energy.|The oscillator in the radio generates the carrier wave. 收音机里的振荡器产生载波。 The heart acts as an oscillator, pumping blood rhythmically. 心脏就像一个振荡器，有节奏地泵血。|近义词：vibrator|反义词: N/A|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *oscillatus*, past participle of *oscillare* "to swing," from *oscillum* "swing."
