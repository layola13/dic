obligation: /ˌɑːblɪˈɡeɪʃn/ | n. | 义务，责任，债务 |Something a person is bound to do : DUTY,  a debt of gratitude|  He has an obligation to support his family. 他有义务抚养他的家庭。 | 近义词：duty, responsibility, commitment | 反义词：exemption, freedom, irresponsibility | 词性: 名词

obligate: /ˈɑːblɪɡeɪt/ | vt. |  使负有义务；强迫 | to bind morally or legally : CONSTRAIN |  The law obligates parents to send their children to school. 法律规定父母必须送孩子上学。 | 近义词：compel, require, bind | 反义词：exempt, release, excuse | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *obligacioun*, from Anglo-Norman *obligacioun*,  from Latin obligātiō (“a binding, tying up, engagement”), from obligātus, past participle of obligāre (“to bind, tie up, engage”), from ob- (“towards”) + ligāre (“to bind”).
