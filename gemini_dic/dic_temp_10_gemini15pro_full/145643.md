overcontentednesses:/ˌoʊvərˌkənˈtɛntɪdnɪsɪz/
|n.|过度满足，过分知足|The state of being excessively content or satisfied.  A feeling of complacency or self-satisfaction that goes beyond a healthy level of contentment.|His overcontentedness led him to ignore the warning signs.| 近义词：complacency, self-satisfaction|反义词：discontent, dissatisfaction|词性:名词

Etymology:
over- (prefix): excessively, beyond a normal degree. From Old English *ofer-, from Proto-Germanic *ubar, meaning "over, above."
content (adjective/noun): satisfied, happy. From Old French content, from Latin contentus, past participle of continēre "to hold together, contain, restrain," from com- "together" + tenēre "to hold."
-ed (suffix): forms the past participle of verbs.
-ness (suffix): forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
