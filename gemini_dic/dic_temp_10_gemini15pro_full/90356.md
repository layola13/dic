hover:/ˈhʌvər/ (US), /ˈhɒvə(r)/ (UK)| v. n.|盘旋，徘徊；犹豫，摇摆不定|To remain in one place in the air.|The helicopter hovered over the landing pad. 直升机在停机坪上方盘旋。|Synonyms: linger, float, drift|Antonyms: settle, land, descend|verb

hover:/ˈhʌvər/ (US), /ˈhɒvə(r)/ (UK)| n.|盘旋，徘徊|The state of remaining suspended.|The hummingbird's hover allowed it to feed from the flower. 蜂鸟的盘旋使它能够以花为食。|Synonyms: suspension, floating|Antonyms: landing, descent|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hoveren, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse hufra ("to heave up and down," "to waver").
