enclosing:/ɪnˈkloʊzɪŋ/ | adj. | 围住的，封闭的；附上的 | Surrounding or containing something; including something along with a letter or package. |  The enclosing wall provided privacy.  围墙提供了隐私。

The letter came with an enclosing brochure. 这封信附带了一份宣传册。 | synonyms: surrounding, encompassing, attaching, including | antonyms: opening, releasing, excluding, detaching | adjective

enclosing:/ɪnˈkloʊzɪŋ/ | n. |  围场，圈地；附件  | The act of enclosing something or something that encloses. | The enclosing of the common land caused unrest. 圈占公共土地引起了骚乱。

The enclosing with the application was my resume.  申请书的附件是我的简历。| synonyms: enclosure, fence, barrier, attachment, addition | antonyms: opening, release, detachment, removal | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English enclosen, from Old French enclore, from Latin inclūdō (“to shut in, enclose, include”), from in- (“in, into”) + claudō (“to shut, close”).
