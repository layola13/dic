monomya:/məˈnɒmiə/ | n. | 单壳贝; 单肌类 | An animal of the class Monomya, such as a clam or oyster, having a single shell or valve.  A now obsolete taxonomic class of mollusks, including those having only one adductor muscle in the shell. |  Monomya, such as oysters, were once a staple food for coastal communities. 单壳贝，例如牡蛎，曾经是沿海社区的主食。 | Example:  The museum's collection included several fossilized monomya.  博物馆的藏品中包括几个单壳贝化石。| Synonym: bivalve (in broader sense),  univalve (sometimes mistakenly used) | Antonym:  (No direct antonym as it's a taxonomic classification.  Could consider "multivalve" hypothetically) | Noun
