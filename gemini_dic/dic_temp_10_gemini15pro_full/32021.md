chequy:/ˈtʃɛki/| adj.|方格的，多变的，盛衰无常的|marked with alternating squares of different colors, often used to describe a varied or checkered past|例句：The family has a chequy past, marked by both great success and terrible misfortune. 这个家族有着盛衰无常的历史，既有巨大的成功，也有可怕的不幸。|例句：The chequy floor of the cathedral was breathtaking.  教堂里方格图案的地板令人叹为观止。|近义词：checkered, variegated, varied|反义词：uniform, consistent|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "eschequier" (chessboard), from "eschec" (check), ultimately from Persian "shah" (king). The evolution reflects the checkered pattern of a chessboard.
