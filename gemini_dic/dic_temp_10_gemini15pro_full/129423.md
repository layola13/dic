nominatives:/ˈnɒmɪnətɪvz/| n.|主格|The case of a noun or pronoun when it is the subject of a verb.|The nominative case in Latin is often marked with the ending -us.|近义词:subject|反义词:objective, accusative|词性:noun

Etymology: From Late Middle English nominatif, from Old French nominatif, from Latin nōminātīvus, from nōmināre (“to name”).
