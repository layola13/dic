flutter:/ˈflʌtər/ (US) /ˈflʌtə(r)/ (UK)| n.|飘动，颤动，挥动; 一阵兴奋（或激动、紧张）; |She felt a flutter of excitement. 她感到一阵兴奋。|近义词：tremor, thrill, palpitation|反义词：calm, stillness|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *flotteren, frequentative of *floten, from Old English *flotian (“to float, swim, flutter”), from Proto-Germanic *flōtijaną (“to float”), from Proto-Indo-European *plewd- (“to flow, float, swim”).  Cognate with Old High German flozzēn (“to flutter”), Dutch flodderen (“to flutter”), Old Norse flota (“to float”), Latin pluit (“it rains”).
