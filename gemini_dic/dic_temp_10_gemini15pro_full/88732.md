hoboed:/ˈhoʊboʊd/| v.|流浪；以流浪方式旅行；|To have traveled or lived like a hobo, typically by hopping freight trains.|He hoboed across the country during the Great Depression. 他在大萧条时期流浪全国。|近义词：tramped, vagabonded, hitchhiked|反义词: settled down, stayed put|词性:动词

词源: From hobo + -ed.  Hobo itself is of uncertain origin, possibly from a shortening of "hoe-boy," a term for a migratory farm worker.
