cakewalk:/ˈkeɪkwɔːk/| n.|轻而易举的事，容易的事|Something that is very easy to achieve.|The exam was a cakewalk for her. 考试对她来说轻而易举。|近义词：breeze, cinch, pushover, walkover|反义词：challenge, ordeal, struggle|名词

词源:  Originated in the late 19th century in the southern United States. Cakewalks were contests held at social gatherings where Black couples would perform elaborate, stylized walks, and the prize was often a cake.  Over time, the term evolved to mean something easily achieved.
