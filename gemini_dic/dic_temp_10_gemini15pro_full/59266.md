eadish: /ˈiːdɪʃ/ | adj. | 贪吃的，贪婪的；渴望的 | Having or showing a keen appetite for food or other things; greedy.  | He had an eadish look in his eyes when he saw the cake. 他看到蛋糕时，眼里露出贪婪的目光。 | The child has an eadish desire for sweets.  这个孩子对糖果有着强烈的渴望。| 近义词: greedy, gluttonous, ravenous, voracious | 反义词: abstemious, temperate | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From Old English *ēdig, from Proto-Germanic *ōdigaz.  Related to Old Norse auðigr ("rich, wealthy"). The original sense likely referred to prosperity and abundance, later shifting to an excessive desire for food.
