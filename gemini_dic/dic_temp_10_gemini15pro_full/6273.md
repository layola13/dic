amoralism:/eɪˈmɒrəlɪzəm/ | n. | 非道德主义；道德虚无主义 |  The belief that morals and morality are unimportant or irrelevant.  It suggests a rejection of moral principles, not simply an ignorance of them (which would be amorality).  |  Some philosophers argue that extreme amoralism can lead to a chaotic society. 一些哲学家认为，极端的非道德主义会导致社会混乱。|  The novel explores the dangers of amoralism in a post-apocalyptic world.  这部小说探讨了后世界末日世界中非道德主义的危险。 | Synonyms:  unprincipledness, nihilism,  | Antonyms: moralism,  principledness | noun

amorality:/ˌeɪməˈræləti/ | n. | 非道德性；无道德感 |  The state of being without morals or a sense of right and wrong.  Unlike amoralism, amorality doesn't necessarily imply a conscious rejection of morality, but rather a lack of it. |  He demonstrated a chilling amorality in his business dealings. 他在商业往来中表现出令人不寒而栗的无道德感。 |  The amorality of the dictator's regime was evident in their human rights abuses.  独裁政权对人权的践踏表明了他们的无道德性。 | Synonyms:  unscrupulousness, lack of conscience | Antonyms: morality, conscientiousness | noun

amoral:/eɪˈmɒrəl/ | adj. | 非道德的；无道德的 |  Lacking a moral sense; unconcerned with the rightness or wrongness of something.  |  The character's amoral actions shocked the audience.  这个角色的不道德行为震惊了观众。 |  His amoral approach to business ultimately led to his downfall. 他不道德的商业做法最终导致了他的垮台。 | Synonyms:  unprincipled, unethical, unscrupulous | Antonyms: moral, ethical, principled | adjective
