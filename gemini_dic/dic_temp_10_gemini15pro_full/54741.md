disparates:/ˈdɪspərəts/ /dɪˈspærɪts/ | n. | 不同的事物；相异的因素 | Things that are fundamentally different; unlike or distinct elements. | The conference brought together people from disparate backgrounds.  这次会议汇集了来自不同背景的人们。

The museum's collection includes disparate objects, from ancient pottery to modern paintings.  博物馆的藏品包括各种不同的物品，从古代陶器到现代绘画。| 近义词：different, dissimilar, diverse, various, heterogeneous | 反义词：similar, alike, homogeneous, uniform | 词性: 名词 (通常用复数形式)

disparate:/ˈdɪspərət/ /dɪˈspærɪt/ | adj. |  迥然不同的；不相干的 | Essentially different in kind; not allowing comparison. |  The two cultures were so disparate that communication was difficult. 这两种文化截然不同，以至于沟通很困难。

He had to juggle disparate pieces of information to form a coherent theory. 他不得不把各种不同的信息拼凑起来，形成一个连贯的理论。 | 近义词：different, dissimilar, diverse, various, heterogeneous | 反义词：similar, alike, homogeneous, uniform | 词性: 形容词


词源: Late Middle English: from Latin disparatus ‘separated, different’, past participle of disparare, from dis- ‘apart’ + parare ‘make ready’.
