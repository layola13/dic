burrowing:/ˈbʌroʊɪŋ/ (US), /ˈbʌrəʊɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. n.|挖洞的，穴居的；挖洞，穴居|The action of digging a hole or tunnel, especially by an animal; also used to describe something that resembles this action. |The rabbits were burrowing in the garden. 兔子正在花园里挖洞。|近义词：excavating, tunneling, digging|反义词:emerging, surfacing|词性: adjective, noun|词源: From burrow + -ing, from Old English *byrigan "to bury," related to beorgan "to protect, save, conceal."

burrow:/ˈbʌroʊ/ (US), /ˈbʌrəʊ/ (UK)| n. v.|洞穴，地道；挖洞，钻入|A hole or tunnel dug by an animal as a dwelling; also, the act of digging such a hole. |The badger retreated to its burrow. 獾退回到它的洞穴里。|近义词：den, hole, tunnel|反义词:mound, hill|词性: noun, verb|词源: From Old English *byrigan "to bury," related to beorgan "to protect, save, conceal."
