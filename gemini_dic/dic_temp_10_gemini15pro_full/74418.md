franchisement:/ˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
n.
特许经营权;选举权;公民权
The right to vote in political elections.
例句：Women's suffrage, or women's right to vote, was a hard-fought battle.  妇女的选举权，或者说妇女的投票权，是一场艰苦的斗争。

例句：He believes that franchisement should be extended to all citizens.  他认为所有公民都应该拥有选举权。

近义词：suffrage, right to vote, enfranchisement
反义词：disenfranchisement

franchisement:/ˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
n.
特许经营权
An authorization granted by a government or company to an individual or group enabling them to carry out specified commercial activities, e.g., providing a broadcasting service or acting as an agent for a company's products.
例句：He bought a fast-food franchisement and opened his own restaurant. 他买了一个快餐特许经营权，开了自己的餐厅。

例句：The franchisement agreement allowed her to sell the company's products in her store.  特许经营协议允许她在自己的商店里销售该公司的产品。

近义词：license, permit, authorization
反义词：prohibition
