gaitered:/ˈɡeɪtərd/ | adj. | 穿着绑腿的，戴着护腿的 | Wearing gaiters, a type of covering for the lower leg.  |  The gaitered hiker strode confidently through the muddy trail. 穿着绑腿的徒步旅行者自信地走过泥泞的小路。 |  The old gentleman, gaitered and bespectacled, looked the picture of a country squire.  戴着眼镜，穿着绑腿的老绅士，看起来就像一位乡村绅士。 | 近义词:booted, protected (in the context of lower legs) | 反义词:bare-legged, exposed (in the context of lower legs) |词性:形容词

Etymology: From "gaiter" + "-ed". "Gaiter" comes from the French "guêtre", which likely has Germanic origins.
