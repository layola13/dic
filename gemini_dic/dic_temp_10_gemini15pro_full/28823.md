carouses: /kəˈraʊzɪz/
| n. (plural) | 欢宴；狂饮作乐 |A noisy, drunken feast.  A series of festive gatherings involving drinking.  |The king and his knights caroused late into the night after the victory. 国王和他的骑士们在胜利后狂欢到深夜。|
| v. (third-person singular present) | 畅饮；狂欢 | To engage in a noisy, drunken feast. To drink and make merry. |He carouses with his friends every weekend. 他每个周末都和朋友们狂欢作乐。|
|近义词：celebrates, revels, feasts, parties|
|反义词：mourns, grieves, abstains|
|词性: n., v.|
|词源：From Middle French *carouser "to drink deeply," perhaps from Old French *carous "a full cup" (source also of Italian caroso), or from Vulgar Latin *carausia, a drinking bout.|
