humors:/ˈhjuːmərz/ (US) /ˈhjuːməz/ (UK)| n.| 幽默，诙谐;  体液; 情绪，心境|A person's mood or state of mind. Also, in ancient and medieval physiology, one of the four fluids (blood, phlegm, choler, and black bile) believed to determine temperament and health.|He was known for his dry sense of humor and witty humors. 他以其冷幽默和诙谐的性情而闻名。|Synonyms: moods, temperaments, dispositions, whims, fancies|Antonyms: seriousness, solemnity|noun

humors:/ˈhjuːmərz/ (US) /ˈhjuːməz/ (UK)| v.| 迎合，迁就|To indulge or gratify (a person's whims or desires).|She humored his desire for a new car, even though they couldn't afford it. 尽管他们买不起，她还是迁就了他想买新车的愿望。|Synonyms: indulge, gratify, pander to, cater to|Antonyms: thwart, oppose|verb
