nonculture:/ˈnɒnˌkʌltʃə(r)/ | n. | 非培养物; 非文化 | Absence of culture; something that is not a culture, especially a population of cells or microorganisms not deliberately grown in a laboratory setting. |  The sample taken from the wound revealed a nonculture of bacteria, indicating the infection wasn't caused by typical bacterial agents.  从伤口取出的样本显示细菌非培养物，表明感染不是由典型的细菌病原体引起的。| near synonyms: aculture, non-growth | antonyms: culture | noun

nonculture:/ˈnɒnˌkʌltʃə(r)/ | n. | 非文化; 未开化 | A lack of culture or refinement; a state of being uncultured or uncivilized.  | His nonculture was evident in his disinterest in art, literature, and music. 他的非文化表现在他对艺术、文学和音乐缺乏兴趣。 | near synonyms: barbarity, ignorance, philistinism | antonyms: culture, refinement, civilization | noun
