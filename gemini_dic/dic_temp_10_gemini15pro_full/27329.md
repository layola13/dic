camail:/kəˈmeɪl/| n.|锁子甲罩衫，锁子甲围脖|A piece of armor, typically made of chain mail, that protected the neck and shoulders.|The knight wore a camail beneath his helmet. 骑士的头盔下戴着锁子甲罩衫。|近义词：aventail, mail|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "unprotected")|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *camail*, from Late Latin *capmailla*, a diminutive of *cappa* ("cape, cloak"). Ultimately from Latin *cappa*.
