/daɪˌæsəˈtoʊdʒən/ | n. | 二碘化甘油酯;二碘化甘油 | A tonic and nutrient once believed to improve health and well-being, containing a compound of iodine and glycerin. | Diastogen was a popular tonic in the early 20th century, advertised as a cure for various ailments. | 近义词: tonic, supplement | 反义词: poison, toxin | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From Greek *dia- "through, across" + stear "fat" + -gen "producing".  The name refers to the presence of glycerin (glycerol, a type of fat) and iodine.
