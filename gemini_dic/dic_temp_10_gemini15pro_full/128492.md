newspeak:/ˈnjuːspiːk/| n.|新语，官话|A deliberately ambiguous and contradictory language used to mislead and manipulate the public, especially as described in George Orwell's novel "Nineteen Eighty-Four".|例句：The government used newspeak to control the population's thoughts. 政府使用新语来控制民众的思想。

例句：The party's use of newspeak made it difficult to express dissenting opinions.  党的新语使用使得表达不同意见变得困难。|近义词：doublespeak, propaganda, jargon|反义词: plain language, truth|名词
