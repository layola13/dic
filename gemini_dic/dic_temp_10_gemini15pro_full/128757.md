niggle:/ˈnɪɡəl/| v. n.|烦恼，困扰；吹毛求疵|To cause slight but persistent annoyance, discomfort, or anxiety.  Also, a minor complaint or worry.|The constant dripping of the faucet niggled at her. 水龙头不停地滴水让她很烦。|synonyms: bother, irritate, annoy, worry, fret, vex, plague, rankle|antonyms: soothe, comfort, please|verb

niggle:/ˈnɪɡəl/| n.|小问题，小毛病；吹毛求疵|A slight but persistent annoyance, discomfort, or anxiety. Also, a minor complaint or worry.|She had a niggle in her back. 她背部有点不舒服。|synonyms: worry, concern, qualm, scruple, reservation|antonyms: assurance, certainty|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *nigelen*, frequentative of *nigen* "to touch lightly," probably of imitative origin.
