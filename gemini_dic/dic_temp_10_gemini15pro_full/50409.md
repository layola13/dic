deposits:/dɪˈpɒzɪts/ (UK), /dɪˈpɑːzɪts/ (US)| n. |存款，沉积物，押金|A sum of money placed in a bank or other institution for safekeeping or earning interest, a layer of accumulated matter, a payment made to secure a purchase or as a guarantee.|
The bank offers a competitive interest rate on deposits. 银行为存款提供有竞争力的利率。
Geological deposits can reveal a lot about the Earth's history. 地质沉积物可以揭示很多关于地球的历史。
|近义词：payments, installments, sediments, accumulations|反义词：withdrawals, deductions|词性：名词

deposit:/dɪˈpɒzɪt/ (UK), /dɪˈpɑːzɪt/ (US)| v. |存放，沉积，支付押金|To put or set down something in a particular place, especially for safekeeping or as a payment.|
I deposited the check into my account. 我把支票存入了我的账户。
The river deposited silt on the floodplain. 河流在泛滥平原上沉积了淤泥。
|近义词：place, store, pay, lodge|反义词：withdraw, remove|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *depositer, from Latin depositus, past participle of deponere ("to lay down, put aside"), from de- ("down, away") + ponere ("to put, place").
