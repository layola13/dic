ark:/ɑːrk/ | n. | 方舟; 箱子; 避难所 | A large boat built by Noah to save his family and animals from the flood; A large chest or box; A place of safety or refuge | Noah built an ark to survive the flood. 诺亚建造了一艘方舟以在大洪水中幸存下来。| synonyms: vessel, chest, sanctuary | antonyms: danger, peril | noun

ark:/ɑːrk/ | n. | 电弧; 弧光 | A luminous electrical discharge between two electrodes | The welder created an ark to join the two pieces of metal. 焊工产生电弧来连接两块金属。| synonyms: arc, spark | antonyms:  | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *arc, *earc (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *arkō, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂erǵ- (“to shine; white; silver”). Cognate with Old Norse örk (“chest, ark”), Gothic arka (“ark, chest”).  The electrical sense is a shortening of electric arc.
