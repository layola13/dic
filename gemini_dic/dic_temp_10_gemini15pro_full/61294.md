embarrassed: /ɪmˈbærəst/ (US) /ɪmˈbærəst/ (UK)| adj.|尴尬的，窘迫的|Feeling or showing shame or awkwardness.|例句：He was embarrassed when he realized his fly was open. 他意识到自己的拉链没拉上时感到很尴尬。|例句：She felt embarrassed about her poor performance. 她对自己的糟糕表现感到很尴尬。|近义词：ashamed, mortified, humiliated|反义词：confident, comfortable, proud|词性:形容词

embarrass: /ɪmˈbærəs/ (US) /ɪmˈbærəs/ (UK)| vt.|使尴尬，使窘迫|To cause someone to feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed.|例句：The incident embarrassed her greatly. 这件事让她非常尴尬。|例句：Don't embarrass me in front of my friends. 不要在我的朋友面前让我难堪。|近义词：shame, humiliate, mortify|反义词：comfort, reassure, encourage|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *embarasser (unattested), from Spanish embarazar "to impede, embarrass," from Portuguese embaraçar, from Vulgar Latin *imbarricare "to bar, block," from in- "in" + Late Latin barrica "barrier."
