actual:/ˈæktʃuəl/ | adj. | 实际的；真实的；目前的 | Existing in fact; real. |  The actual cost was much higher than we expected. 实际成本比我们预期的要高得多。

The actual time of the meeting is still to be confirmed. 会议的实际时间还有待确认。 | synonyms: real, true, genuine, factual, existent, concrete | antonyms: theoretical, hypothetical, supposed, potential, nominal | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *actualis, from Latin actus (“act, action, performance, a driving, impulse”), from agō (“I do, act, drive”).

USAGE->
Attributive Usage (placed before a noun):

The actual cost of the project was surprising.  该项目的实际成本令人惊讶。
He gave the actual figures to the accountant. 他把实际数字给了会计。


Predicative Usage (follows a linking verb):

The story he told was actual. 他讲的故事是真实的。
Her fears proved actual when the storm hit. 当暴风雨来袭时，她的恐惧被证明是真实的。


With emphasis:

It was the actual President who made the announcement. 是现任总统宣布了这一消息。
The damage is worse than we initially thought; in actual fact, it's catastrophic.  损害比我们最初想象的要严重；事实上，这是灾难性的。


PHRASE->
in actual fact = 事实上
actual value = 实际价值
actual size = 实际大小
actual cost = 实际成本
actual situation = 实际情况
