enfranchise: /ɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ | vt. | 给予…公民权（或选举权）；赋予…权力；解放 | To grant the privileges of citizenship, especially the right to vote; to empower; to liberate. |  The Nineteenth Amendment enfranchised women in the United States.  美国第十九修正案赋予了妇女选举权。

The company decided to enfranchise its employees by allowing them to vote on major decisions. 公司决定赋予员工权力，允许他们对重大决策进行投票。 | 近义词: empower, liberate, free, emancipate | 反义词: disenfranchise, enslave, oppress | 词性: verb

enfranchiser: /ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzər/ | n. | 授予公民权（或选举权）的人；赋予权力的人 |  A person or entity that grants the privileges of citizenship, especially the right to vote, or empowers someone. | The government acted as the enfranchiser, extending voting rights to all citizens over the age of 18. 政府作为授权者，将投票权扩大到所有18岁以上的公民。

History remembers Abraham Lincoln as the great enfranchiser of enslaved people. 历史铭记亚伯拉罕·林肯是奴隶的伟大解放者。| 近义词: liberator, emancipator | 反义词: oppressor, enslaver | 词性: noun
