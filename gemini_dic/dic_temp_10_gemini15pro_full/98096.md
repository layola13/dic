inquisitor:/ɪnˈkwɪzɪtər/| n. |审判员，调查员|A person who makes a formal inquiry, especially one who conducts legal or official investigations.  A member of a tribunal, especially the Inquisition.|The inquisitor demanded a full confession. 调查员要求全面坦白。|Synonyms: investigator, examiner, interrogator, prosecutor|Antonyms: defendant, accused, respondent|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *inquisitour*, from Latin *inquisitor* (“inquirer, examiner”), from *inquirere* (“to seek into, inquire”), from *in* (“in, into”) + *quaerere* (“to seek, ask”).
