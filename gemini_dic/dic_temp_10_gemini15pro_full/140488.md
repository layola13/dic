old-worldliness:/ˌoʊld ˈwɜːrldlinəs/
|n.|旧世界的风俗/气质；守旧；古板|The old-worldliness of the village charmed the tourists. 这个村庄的旧世界风情吸引了游客。|Characteristic or evocative of the past; old-fashioned, especially in a charming or quaint way.  It refers to the customs, attitudes, or qualities associated with a former era, often suggesting a sense of formality, tradition, and elegance. |The antique shop had an air of old-worldliness about it. 这家古董店有一种旧世界的氛围。|近义词：traditionalism, quaintness, formality, conservatism|反义词：modernity, contemporaneity|词性:名词

old-worldly:/ˌoʊld ˈwɜːrldli/
|adj.|旧世界的；旧式的；古板的|She has an old-worldly charm about her. 她有一种旧世界的魅力。 |Belonging to or characteristic of a former era; old-fashioned. It can suggest either a positive connotation of charm and tradition or a negative connotation of being outdated and out of touch with modern times.|His old-worldly manners seemed out of place in the bustling city. 他那老派的举止在这座熙熙攘攘的城市里显得格格不入。|近义词：traditional, old-fashioned, quaint, conservative|反义词：modern, contemporary, progressive|词性:形容词
