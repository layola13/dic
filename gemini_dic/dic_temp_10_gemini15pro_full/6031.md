amen:/ˈɑːmɛn/ /ˈeɪˈmɛn/| n.|  阿门；诚心所愿；确实如此 |A word used at the end of a prayer or hymn, expressing assent. Also used to express agreement or fervent ratification.|例句：The congregation responded with a resounding "Amen".  会众以响亮的“阿门”回应。|例句：He said he would help, and I said "Amen" to that. 他说他会帮忙，我对此表示“阿门”（同意）。|近义词：so be it, truly, verily |反义词: (no direct antonym) |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *amen, from Late Latin amen, from Ecclesiastical Greek amēn, from Hebrew āmēn ‘certainty, truly’, used adverbially as a solemn affirmation.
