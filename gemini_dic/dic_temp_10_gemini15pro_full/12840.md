associationist:/əˌsoʊʃiˈeɪʃənɪst/
n.|联想主义者；联想心理学派的人|A person who believes in or advocates the principles of associationism, a theory that explains mental processes as resulting from the association of ideas. |John Stuart Mill, son of James Mill, was a prominent associationist. 约翰·斯图尔特·密尔，詹姆斯·密尔的儿子，是一位著名的联想主义者。|Synonyms: connectionist|Antonyms: nativist
adj.|联想主义的；联想心理学派的|Relating to or characteristic of associationism. |Associationist psychology dominated the field for a considerable period.  联想心理学在相当长的一段时间内主导了该领域。|Synonyms: connectionist|Antonyms: nativist


ETYMOLOGY->
From association + -ist.  The word "association" comes from Late Latin associationem, meaning "union, connection,"  from Latin associare "to join, unite." The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or theory.
