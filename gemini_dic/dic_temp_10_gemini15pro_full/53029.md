diocletian:/ˌdaɪəˈkliːʃən/ | n. |戴克里先| Roman emperor from 284 to 305 AD.  He is known for his persecution of Christians and for dividing the Roman Empire into two halves.| Diocletian's reign was a turning point in Roman history. 戴克里先的统治是罗马历史上的一个转折点。|Synonyms: emperor, ruler, Caesar | Antonyms: subject, citizen | Noun
