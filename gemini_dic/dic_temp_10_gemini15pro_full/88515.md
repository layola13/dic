histrionically:/ˌhɪstriˈɑːnɪkli/ | adv. | 戏剧性地；装腔作势地 | In a way that is overly theatrical, dramatic, or emotional, often to attract attention. | She reacted histrionically to the news, sobbing and wailing loudly. 她对这个消息反应夸张，大声哭泣和哀号。|  She threw herself on the floor histrionically. 她戏剧性地把自己摔倒在地板上。 | 近义词：dramatically, theatrically, melodramatically, exaggeratedly | 反义词：calmly, subtly, genuinely, sincerely | 词性: 副词

histrionic:/ˌhɪstriˈɑːnɪk/ | adj. | 戏剧性的；装腔作势的 | Overly theatrical, dramatic, or emotional, often to attract attention. | His histrionic displays of grief seemed insincere. 他夸张的悲伤表现显得不真诚。|  Her histrionic personality made her the center of attention. 她戏剧化的性格使她成为关注的焦点。 | 近义词：dramatic, theatrical, melodramatic | 反义词：calm, understated, genuine | 词性: 形容词


词源:  From Late Latin *histrionicus, from Latin histrio (“actor”), from Etruscan ister (“actor”).
