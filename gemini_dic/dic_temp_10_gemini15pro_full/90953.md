hurons:/ˈhjʊrɒnz/ (UK) /ˈhʊrɑːnz/ (US)| n. |休伦人|A member of a North American Indian people formerly living around Lake Huron, now mainly in Quebec, Canada and Oklahoma, US.  Originally part of the Wendat Confederacy, they were largely dispersed in the mid-17th century through warfare with the Iroquois.|例句：The Hurons were skilled agriculturalists. 休伦人是熟练的农业家。

例句：The Hurons traded furs with the French. 休伦人与法国人进行毛皮贸易。 |近义词：Wendat (more accurate, preferred term), Iroquois Confederacy (historical rivals)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun
