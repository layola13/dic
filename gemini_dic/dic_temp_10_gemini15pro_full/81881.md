griever:/ˈɡriːvər/| n.|悲痛者，哀悼者|A person who is grieving; someone who has experienced a loss and is feeling deep sorrow.|She was a griever, still raw from the loss of her husband.|mourner, bereaved|comforter|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From grieve (v.) + -er (suffix forming nouns denoting agents).  The verb "grieve" comes from Old French "grever" (to burden, injure, afflict), ultimately from Latin "gravare" (to burden).
