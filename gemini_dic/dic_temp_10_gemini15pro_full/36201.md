cogger:/ˈkɒɡə(r)/ (UK), /ˈkɑːɡər/ (US)| n.| 欺骗者，骗子；齿轮；（旧）中间人，掮客|A person who cheats or deceives others, especially in business dealings.  Originally referring to a wheel or cog in a machine, it later came to mean someone who is a part of a deceitful scheme, like a cog in a machine. Also, an archaic term for a middleman or broker.| He was known as a cogger in the local marketplace, always trying to inflate prices. 他在当地市场上被称为骗子，总是试图抬高价格。|Synonyms: cheat, fraudster, swindler, deceiver, trickster|Antonyms: honest person, trustworthy individual|noun


cogger:/ˈkɒɡə(r)/ (UK), /ˈkɑːɡər/ (US)| v.| 作弊，欺骗|To cheat or deceive someone, especially in a business transaction.| He cogged the dice to win the game. 他在掷骰子时作弊以赢得比赛。|Synonyms: cheat, defraud, swindle, deceive, trick|Antonyms: be honest, play fair|verb
