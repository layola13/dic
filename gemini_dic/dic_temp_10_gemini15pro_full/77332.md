gatvol:/ˈχɑtfɔl/| adj.| 厌倦的，受够了，忍无可忍的 | Fed up; irritated; had enough. Primarily used in South African English.  Derived from Afrikaans. Literally translates to "had full".  Implies a feeling of being completely overwhelmed and frustrated with a situation or person. | Example: I'm gatvol of his constant complaining. 我已经受够了他不断的抱怨。| Example: She's gatvol of the traffic. 她对交通堵塞忍无可忍了。| 近义词: fed up, sick of, tired of, exasperated | 反义词: patient, tolerant, content | 词性:形容词
