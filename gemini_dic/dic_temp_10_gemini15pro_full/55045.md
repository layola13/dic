disquiettude:/ˌdɪskwaɪˈɛtjuːd/| n.|不安，焦虑，忧虑|A state of uneasiness or anxiety.|例句：A sense of disquiettude settled over her as she read the letter.  她读信时感到一阵不安。

例句：The news of the impending storm filled the villagers with disquiettude.  即将到来的暴风雨的消息使村民们感到不安。|近义词：unease, anxiety, worry, apprehension, perturbation|反义词：calm, tranquility, peace, serenity|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Late Middle English: from Old French *desquietude, from desquieter ‘make uneasy’, from des- (expressing negation) + quiet ‘quiet’.
