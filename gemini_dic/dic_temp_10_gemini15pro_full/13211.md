at-the-worst:/æt ðə wɜːrst/ | adv. | 最坏的情况下；最糟糕的时候 |  At the worst, we'll have to postpone the trip.  |  At the worst, it will be a minor inconvenience. |  in the worst-case scenario, if worst comes to worst | at best | adverb

Etymology:
"At" from Old English "æt," meaning "at," "in," or "by." "The" from Old English "þē," meaning "the." "Worst" from Old English "wyrst," superlative of "yfel" (evil, bad).
