eclecticism: /ɪˈklɛktɪˌsɪzəm/
n.
折衷主义；博采众长
The practice of deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.
His house is a testament to his eclecticism, with furniture and art from all over the world. 他的房子证明了他的折衷主义，家具和艺术品来自世界各地。

eclectic: /ɪˈklɛktɪk/
adj.
兼收并蓄的；折衷的
Deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.
She has an eclectic taste in music, enjoying everything from classical to hip-hop. 她对音乐的品味兼收并蓄，从古典到嘻哈，什么都喜欢。


Etymology: From the Greek ἐκλεκτικός (eklektikos, “selective”), from ἐκλέγω (eklegō, “I pick out, choose”).

Synonyms:  selectiveness, catholicity, inclusiveness, broad-mindedness, all-embracing, assimilative
Antonyms: narrowness, exclusiveness, restriction, dogmatism
