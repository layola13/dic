carcassed:/ˈkɑːrkəst/ | adj. |  屠宰后的，已剥皮的；被杀死的；（建筑物、车辆等）损毁严重的，只剩下骨架的 | Having the carcass removed; slaughtered.  Also, resembling a carcass; stripped bare, ruined. | The battlefield was littered with carcassed soldiers. 战场上到处都是被杀死的士兵的尸体。| The carcassed building stood as a stark reminder of the war.  只剩下骨架的建筑物成为战争的残酷提醒。 | 近义词: butchered, slaughtered, gutted, ruined, destroyed | 反义词: living, intact, whole | 词性: 形容词

词源: From carcass + -ed.  Carcass comes from Old French *carquois, meaning "quiver," likely via Vulgar Latin *carcāsium. The meaning shifted from "quiver" to "skeleton" and then to "dead body of an animal."
