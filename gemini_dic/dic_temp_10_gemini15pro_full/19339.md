bibliolatry: /ˌbɪbliˈɒlətri/ (US) /ˌbɪbliˈɒlətri/ (UK)| n. |书籍崇拜；圣经崇拜| Excessive reverence for the Bible or other sacred texts, often to the point of treating them as literally true in every detail and neglecting reason or critical analysis.|  Example 1: His bibliolatry led him to believe that the Earth was only a few thousand years old. 他的圣经崇拜使他相信地球只有几千年的历史。

Example 2: Some critics accuse certain religious groups of bibliolatry, claiming they prioritize literal interpretations over context and historical understanding. 一些评论家指责某些宗教团体崇拜圣经，声称他们优先考虑字面解释而不是上下文和历史理解。 | 近义词:  bible-worship, fundamentalism | 反义词:  secularism, skepticism, freethinking | 词性: noun
