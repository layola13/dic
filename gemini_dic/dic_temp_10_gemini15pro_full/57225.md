downgrade:/ˈdaʊnˌɡreɪd/ /ˈdaʊnˌɡreɪd/| v. n.|降级，降低，低估|To reduce to a lower grade, rank, or level of importance.  To reduce the quality or value of something.|例句：The airline downgraded me from business class to economy. 航空公司把我从商务舱降到了经济舱。|近义词：degrade, demote, reduce, lower|反义词:upgrade, promote, elevate|词性:动词/名词

downgrade:/ˈdaʊnˌɡreɪd/ /ˈdaʊnˌɡreɪd/| n.|降级，降低|The act of downgrading or the state of being downgraded.|例句：The software downgrade caused some compatibility issues. 软件降级导致了一些兼容性问题。|近义词：reduction, demotion, lowering|反义词:upgrade, promotion, elevation|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "down-" + "grade".  "Down-" indicates a lowering or reduction, while "grade" refers to a level or rank.  The word first appeared in the late 19th century.
