connotatively: /kəˈnɑːtətɪvli/ /ˌkɒnəˈteɪtɪvli/ | adv. | 含蓄地；引申地 |  In a way that implies or suggests an idea or feeling in addition to the literal or primary meaning.  It refers to the emotions or associations that a word evokes beyond its denotation (literal definition). |  The word "home" connotatively suggests warmth, security, and family.  “家”这个词引申地暗示了温暖、安全和家庭。| Synonyms: suggestively, implicitly, indirectly | Antonyms: explicitly, literally, denotatively | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *connotativus, from connotat-, past participle stem of connotare "to mark in addition, signify," from com- "together" (see com-) + notare "to mark" (see note (n.)).
