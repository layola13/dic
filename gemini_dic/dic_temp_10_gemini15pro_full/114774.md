malthusians:/mælˈθuːziənz/
|n.|马尔萨斯主义者；人口过剩论者|Believers in the theories of Thomas Robert Malthus, especially that population tends to increase faster than the means of subsistence.|Many environmentalists are considered neo-Malthusians.|Synonyms:  populationists, neo-Malthusians|Antonyms: cornucopians|noun


malthusian:/mælˈθuːziən/
|adj.|马尔萨斯的；关于人口过剩的|Relating to the theories of Thomas Robert Malthus, especially that population tends to increase faster than the means of subsistence.|A Malthusian catastrophe is a prediction of a forced return to subsistence-level conditions once population growth has outpaced agricultural production.|Synonyms: overpopulationist, pessimistic|Antonyms: optimistic, cornucopian|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Thomas Robert Malthus (1766–1834), English economist and demographer.
