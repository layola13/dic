authenticly: /ɔːˈθɛntɪkli/ | adv. | 真正地，地道地 |  While "authenticly" is sometimes used, it's generally considered non-standard. The preferred adverbial form is "authentically," meaning "in a genuine or true way." It describes actions or qualities that are real, not fake or copied. |  He tried to authenticly portray the life of a cowboy. (While used, authentically is preferred). 他试图真实地描绘牛仔的生活。 | 近义词: genuinely, truly, really, actually | 反义词: falsely, artificially, fake | 词性: 副词


词源: From the adjective "authentic," derived from the Late Latin "authenticus," meaning "authoritative, genuine."  The "-ly" suffix transforms the adjective into an adverb.
