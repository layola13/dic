antihysterical:/ˌæntiˌhɪˈstɛrɪkəl/
| adj. | 抗歇斯底里的，抑制歇斯底里的 | Preventing or counteracting hysteria.  Used to describe medications or treatments aimed at calming or suppressing hysterical symptoms. Also used to describe a person or their behavior as calm and composed, especially in a situation where others might be prone to hysteria. | The doctor prescribed an antihysterical medication. 医生开了抗歇斯底里的药物。 |  She remained remarkably antihysterical throughout the crisis. 在整个危机中，她都保持着非凡的镇定。|  calm, composed, serene, imperturbable | hysterical, frantic, agitated | adjective

Etymology:
From anti- (prefix meaning "against, opposing") + hysterical (from Greek hysterikos "of the womb," later associated with emotional outbursts).
