burnishment:/ˈbɜːrnɪʃmənt/ | n. | 抛光，磨光; 润色; |the improvement or refinement of something, often to give it a more impressive or attractive appearance.  The act of burnishing something. | The burnishment of the silver made it gleam.  银器的抛光使它闪闪发光。 | 近义词: polish, gloss, shine, luster | 反义词: dullness, tarnish | 词性: 名词

burnishments:/ˈbɜːrnɪʃmənts/ | n. | 抛光，磨光（复数）; 润色（复数）; |plural form of burnishment | The burnishments on the antique furniture gave it a rich patina. 古董家具上的抛光使其具有丰富的包浆。 | 近义词: polishes, glosses, shines, lusters | 反义词: dullnesses, tarnishes | 词性: 名词


词源:
From Middle English *bornischen, from Old French burnir, from Frankish *brunnon (“to polish, make brown”), from Proto-Germanic *brunnijaną (“to make brown”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰerH- (“brown, bright”). Cognate with Old High German brunnen, brunnôn (“to burnish, polish”), Dutch bruinen (“to brown, tan”), German brünieren (“to brown, burnish”), Old English brūnian (“to make brown, become brown”).
