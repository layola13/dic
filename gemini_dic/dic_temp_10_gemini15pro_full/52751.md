dilapidated:/dɪˈlæpɪdeɪtɪd/| adj.|破旧的，失修的|Falling into ruin or decay, usually of a building or property, from neglect or disrepair.|The dilapidated house stood on a deserted street. 这栋破旧的房子矗立在一条荒凉的街道上.|The old car was dilapidated and rusty. 这辆旧车破旧不堪，锈迹斑斑.|Synonyms: ramshackle, derelict, run-down, tumbledown|Antonyms: well-maintained, pristine, renovated|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dilapidatus*, past participle of *dilapidare* "to squander, scatter like stones," from *dis-* "apart, asunder" + *lapidare* "to throw stones," from *lapis* "stone."  The original sense referred to scattering stones of a building, hence its association with ruin and decay.
