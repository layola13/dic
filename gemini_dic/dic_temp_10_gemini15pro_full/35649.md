coaxer:/ˈkoʊksər/| n.|哄骗者；劝诱者|Someone who coaxes or persuades someone else to do something, often by flattery or gentle urging. | She's a master coaxer, able to get anyone to do anything she wants. 她是个哄骗高手，能让人做任何她想做的事。| My grandmother was a coaxer; she could always convince me to eat my vegetables. 我的祖母是个劝诱高手；她总能说服我吃蔬菜。|  persuader, wheedler, cajoler| opponent, resister |noun

coax:/koʊks/| v.|哄骗；劝诱|To persuade someone gently and patiently to do something.|He coaxed the cat down from the tree. 他把猫从树上哄了下来。|I had to coax my son into taking his medicine. 我不得不哄着儿子吃药。| persuade, wheedle, cajole|dissuade, discourage|verb
