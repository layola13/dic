face-offs:/ˈfeɪsˌɔːfs/ /ˈfeɪsˌɒfs/| n.|对抗，对峙|A face-off is a confrontation or a situation where two people or groups are directly opposed to each other.|The hockey game began with a face-off in the center of the rink.  冰球比赛以球场中心的争球开始。

The presidential debate was a dramatic face-off between the two candidates. 总统辩论是两位候选人之间戏剧性的对峙。|近义词：confrontation, showdown, clash|反义词：agreement, cooperation|词性:名词

face-off:/ˈfeɪsˌɔːfs/ /ˈfeɪsˌɒfs/| v.| (尤指冰球)争球; 对峙|To face off means to engage in a confrontation or to start a hockey game with a face-off.|The two teams faced off in the championship game. 两支队伍在冠军赛中对决。

The center forward won the face-off, giving his team possession of the puck. 中锋赢得了争球，使他的球队获得了控球权。|近义词：confront, oppose, challenge|反义词：cooperate, collaborate|词性:动词
