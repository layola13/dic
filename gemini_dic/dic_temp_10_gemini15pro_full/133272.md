nonexistentialisms:/ˌnɒnɪɡzɪˈstɛnʃəlɪzəmz/
n.
非存在主义（通常用复数形式）
Theories or beliefs that reject or oppose the principles of existentialism, particularly the emphasis on individual existence, freedom, and responsibility.
He dismissed her concerns as mere nonexistentialisms, refusing to engage with the philosophical implications. 他认为她的担忧只不过是非存在主义的，拒绝探讨其哲学含义。
近义词: anti-existentialism
反义词: existentialism
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
non- + existentialism + -s
non-: prefix meaning "not"
existentialism:  a philosophical theory or approach that emphasizes the existence of the individual person as a free and responsible agent determining their own development through acts of the will.
-s: plural suffix
