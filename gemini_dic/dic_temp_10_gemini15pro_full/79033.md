give chase:/ɡɪv ˈtʃeɪs/| v.|追赶，追逐|To begin pursuing someone or something rapidly.|The police gave chase to the escaping robber. 警察追赶逃跑的抢匪。|The dog gave chase to the squirrel. 狗追逐松鼠。|近义词：pursue, follow, chase after|反义词：flee, escape, run away|词性:动词

词源:  "Give" comes from Old English "giefan," and "chase" comes from Old French "chacier," ultimately from Latin "captiare," meaning "to try to seize." The phrase "give chase" combines these two words to create the meaning of actively pursuing something.
