lofts:/lɒfts/ (UK), /lɔːfts/ (US)| n.|阁楼，顶楼房间，鸽舍|A loft is a space at the top of a building under the roof, often used for storage or converted into living space. It can also refer to a raised platform or upper floor in a large room, or a compartment for pigeons in a dovecote.|例句：
They converted the loft into a bedroom. 他们把阁楼改造成了一间卧室。

The pigeons were cooing in their lofts. 鸽子在它们的鸽舍里咕咕叫。

|近义词：attic, garret, upper floor, gallery|反义词:basement, cellar|词性:noun


loft:/lɒft/ (UK), /lɔːft/ (US)| v.|将球高击，使球升高|To hit or propel a ball high into the air, especially in golf or tennis.|例句：
He lofted the ball over the net. 他把球高高地打过了网。

She lofted a beautiful shot onto the green. 她打出了一个漂亮的球，落在了果岭上。

|近义词：chip, lob, elevate|反义词:ground, lower|词性:verb
