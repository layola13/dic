gouge:/ɡaʊdʒ/| v. n.|凿; 挖;  gouge out 凿出; 挖出; gouge from 从…挖出|To gouge means to make a hole or groove in something with a sharp tool or object, or to force something out of a surface in this way.  It can also refer to the tool itself.| The carpenter gouged a hole in the wood for the dowel. 木匠在木头上凿了一个洞，用来插销子。|The child gouged his initials into the tree bark. 孩子把他的名字首字母刻在树皮上。|synonyms: scoop, excavate, hollow, channel, groove, furrow, gouge, chisel|antonyms: fill, pack, stuff|verb

gouge:/ɡaʊdʒ/| n.|凿子; 挖凿; 敲竹杠; 诈骗|A gouge is a chisel with a curved blade used for scooping or carving out wood or other materials. It can also refer to an act of extortion or overcharging.|He used a gouge to carve the intricate details of the wooden sculpture. 他用凿子雕刻木雕的复杂细节。|The price of gasoline is a gouge these days. 最近汽油价格太高了。|synonyms: chisel, scoop, excavator, swindle, rip-off|antonyms: discount, bargain|noun
