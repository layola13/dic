diversion:/daɪˈvɜːrʒən/ (US), /daɪˈvɜːʃən/ (UK)| n.|转移, 娱乐, 消遣, 绕行|An act of turning something aside from its course; amusement or pastime; a detour.|例句：The magician's sleight of hand created a diversion, allowing him to conceal the card. 魔术师的手法制造了一个转移，让他得以藏起那张牌。

例句：Reading is her favorite diversion. 阅读是她最喜欢的消遣。|近义词： distraction, entertainment, recreation, detour, deviation|反义词: concentration, focus, work

divert:/daɪˈvɜːrt/ (US), /daɪˈvɜːt/ (UK)| vt.|转移, 使转向, 使消遣|To turn aside from a course or purpose; to amuse or entertain.|例句：The loud noise diverted my attention.  巨大的噪音转移了我的注意力。

例句：He diverted the funds to his own account. 他把资金转移到自己的账户。|近义词： distract, entertain, amuse, redirect, deflect|反义词: focus, concentrate
