nail-biter: /ˈneɪlˌbaɪtər/ | n. | 紧张刺激的比赛或事件; 指甲咬的人 | A situation, especially a contest, that is very exciting or suspenseful because the outcome is uncertain until the very end;  a person who bites their nails, often due to anxiety or nervousness. | The election was a real nail-biter, with the results too close to call until the final count.  这场选举真扣人心弦，直到最后计票结果才出来。| Synonyms: cliffhanger, thriller, close contest | Antonyms:  blowout, walkover | Noun
