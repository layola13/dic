bluffer:/ˈblʌfər/ | n. | 虚张声势的人；吓唬人的人 | Someone who pretends to be more confident or knowledgeable than they really are, especially in order to deceive someone. | He was a bluffer, pretending to know more about the subject than he actually did. 他是个虚张声势的人，假装对这个话题比实际了解得多。 |  近义词：pretender, imposter, deceiver, charlatan | 反义词：honest person, straight shooter | 词性：名词

bluff:/blʌf/ | v. | 虚张声势；吓唬 | To deceive someone by pretending to be more confident or knowledgeable than one really is. | He bluffed his way through the interview, despite knowing very little about the job. 尽管他对这份工作知之甚少，但他还是靠虚张声势蒙混过关了。 | 近义词：deceive, mislead, feign, pretend | 反义词：be honest, tell the truth | 词性：动词

bluff:/blʌf/ | adj. | 直率的；陡峭的 | (of a person's manner) direct and outspoken, sometimes in a way that seems rude. Also, a steep cliff or hill. |  He had a bluff, hearty manner that some people found intimidating. 他有一种直率而热情的举止，有些人觉得令人生畏。 The ship sailed close to the bluff coastline. 船靠近陡峭的海岸线航行。 | 近义词：(for person) frank, outspoken, blunt; (for cliff) steep, sheer, precipitous | 反义词：(for person) subtle, indirect, evasive; (for cliff) gentle, sloping | 词性：形容词


词源：Probably from Dutch bluffen "to boast, brag," related to Middle Low German bluffen "to puff, blow."
