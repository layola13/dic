ingrain:/ɪnˈɡreɪn/ | v. adj. |使根深蒂固；根深蒂固的|To fix firmly, as if by dyeing; to instill deeply. |The idea of service to others was ingrained in him from childhood.  从童年起，为他人服务的思想就深深地扎根于他的心中。|近义词：implant, embed, instill, fix, root |反义词：eradicate, uproot, remove |词性:动词，形容词

ingrain:/ɪnˈɡreɪn/ | n. |固有性质；原纱粗布|A deeply established habit, belief, or quality; a yarn dyed before being woven. |His frugality is an ingrain, developed over years of hardship. 他的节俭是多年艰苦生活中养成的固有品质。|近义词：nature, essence, character, fiber |反义词： - |词性:名词

词源: From in- + grain (n.). Originally referred to dyeing yarn before weaving, leading to a more permanent color.
