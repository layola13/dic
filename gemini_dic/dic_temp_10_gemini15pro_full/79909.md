gnaws:/nɔːz/| v. | 啃，咬，折磨 | to bite or chew on something persistently; to cause persistent worry or distress | The dog gnaws on its bone. 狗啃着它的骨头。| synonyms: nibble, chew, bite, erode, corrode | antonyms: soothe, comfort | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *gnāgan, from Proto-Germanic *gnaganą, from Proto-Indo-European *ghengh- (“to gnaw, bite”). Cognate with Old Norse gnaga (“to gnaw”), Dutch knagen (“to gnaw”), German nagen (“to gnaw”), Gothic 𐌲𐌽𐌰𐌲𐌰𐌽 (gnagjan, “to gnaw”).
