conserving:/kənˈsɜːrvɪŋ/
verb|保护，保存，节省|Protecting or preserving something from loss, damage, or waste; using resources carefully to prevent loss or waste.|例句：Conserving water is crucial during a drought.  干旱期间节约用水至关重要。|例句：They are conserving energy by installing solar panels. 他们通过安装太阳能电池板来节约能源。|近义词：preserving, protecting, safeguarding, saving|反义词：wasting, squandering, depleting|词性: present participle of "conserve"
conserve:/kənˈsɜːrv/
verb|保护，保存，节省|Protect (something, especially an environmentally or culturally important place or thing) from harm or destruction.|词源：From Latin conservare "to keep, preserve, maintain," from com-, intensive prefix (see com-), + servare "to keep, watch over" (see observe).|近义词：preserve, protect, save|反义词：waste, squander, deplete|词性: verb
