gitana:/xiˈtana/ | n. | 吉普赛女人；吉普赛女郎 | A Spanish word for a Gypsy woman, especially one who is a dancer or fortune teller. |  She danced with the fiery passion of a gitana. 她带着吉普赛女郎的热情奔放地跳舞。 |  Gypsy woman, Romani woman |  (No direct antonym, could use non-Romany woman for contrast) | Noun

gitano:/xiˈtano/ | n. | 吉普赛男人；吉普赛人 | A Spanish word for a Gypsy man. | The gitano played a haunting melody on his guitar.  那个吉普赛男人用他的吉他演奏了一曲萦绕心头的旋律。 | Gypsy man, Romani man | (No direct antonym, could use non-Romany man for contrast) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *gitano*, *gitana*, ultimately from Greek *Αἰγύπτιος* (Aigýptios) meaning "Egyptian".  This reflects the mistaken belief that the Romani people originated in Egypt.
