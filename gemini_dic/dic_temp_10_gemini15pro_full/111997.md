lonely: /ˈloʊnli/ (US) /ˈləʊnli/ (UK) | adj. | 孤独的，寂寞的 | Feeling sad because one is alone or without companions.  |  She felt lonely after her husband died. 她丈夫去世后她感到很孤独。 |  isolated, solitary, alone |  accompanied, sociable, gregarious | adjective

lonely: /ˈloʊnli/ (US) /ˈləʊnli/ (UK) | adv. | 孤独地，寂寞地 | In a lonely manner.  | The lighthouse stood lonely on the rocky coast.  灯塔孤零零地矗立在岩石海岸上。 | solitarily, isolatedly | together, jointly | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English `ānlic' (alone, solitary)  from `ān' (one) + `-lic' (like).  The meaning shifted over time to emphasize the feeling of sadness associated with being alone.
