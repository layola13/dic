conservers:/kənˈsɜːrvərz/| n.|保护者，保存者|People who actively protect and preserve something, such as the environment or resources.  They advocate for sustainable practices and work to prevent depletion or destruction.|例句：The conservers worked tirelessly to protect the endangered species.  保护者们不知疲倦地工作以保护濒危物种。

例句：Conservers of historical artifacts play a vital role in maintaining our cultural heritage.  历史文物的保护者在维护我们的文化遗产方面发挥着至关重要的作用。|近义词：preservationists, protectors, guardians, custodians|反义词：destroyers, wasters, squanderers|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "conserve," which comes from the Latin "conservare," meaning "to keep, preserve, maintain."  The suffix "-er" denotes a person who performs the action of the verb.
