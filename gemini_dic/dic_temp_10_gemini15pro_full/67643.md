extrinsic:/ɛkˈstrɪnsɪk/, /ɪkˈstrɪnsɪk/| adj.|外在的，外部的，非本质的|Not inherent or essential; originating from the outside; external.|The extrinsic motivation of money is what keeps many people working at jobs they dislike.  金钱的外在激励是许多人继续从事不喜欢的工作的原因。|例句：The book's cover is extrinsic to its value as literature. 这本书的封面与其作为文学作品的价值无关。|近义词：external, outward, outer, superficial, incidental|反义词：intrinsic, inherent, internal, essential|词性:形容词

extrinsic:/ɛkˈstrɪnsɪk/, /ɪkˈstrɪnsɪk/| n.|外在因素|Something external or not inherent.|Social status can be seen as an extrinsic reward. 社会地位可以被视为一种外在的奖励。|例句：Extrinsics like bonuses and promotions can motivate employees. 像奖金和晋升这样的外在因素可以激励员工。|近义词：external factor, outside influence|反义词：intrinsic factor, inner drive|词性:名词
