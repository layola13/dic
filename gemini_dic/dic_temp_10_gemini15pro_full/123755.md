moore:/mʊr/ | n. | 荒野，沼泽；高沼地 | A tract of open, rolling, infertile land, often having patches of heath and peat bogs. |  He lost his way in the bleak moore. 他在荒凉的沼泽中迷路了。| synonym: heath, bog, fen, marsh | antonym:  meadow, grassland | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *more, from Old English mōr (“moor, swamp, marsh, fen”), from Proto-Germanic *mōrą (“bog, swamp”), from Proto-Indo-European *mēy- (“wet”). Cognate with West Frisian moar (“moor”), Dutch moer (“fen, peat bog”), German Moor (“moor”), Danish mor (“moor”), Swedish myr (“bog, mire”), Icelandic mýri (“bog, marsh”).
