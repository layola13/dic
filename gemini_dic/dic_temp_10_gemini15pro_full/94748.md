improvisate: /ˈɪmprəvaɪˌzeɪt/ /ˈɪmprəvɪˌzeɪt/ | v. | 即兴创作，即兴表演 | To compose, perform, or deliver something extemporaneously, without prior preparation. | He had to improvise a speech at the last minute. 他不得不在最后一刻即兴发表演讲。

The musicians improvised a jazz melody. 音乐家们即兴演奏了一段爵士旋律。

| 近义词：extemporize, ad-lib, wing it | 反义词：plan, rehearse, prepare | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *improvisatus, past participle of improvisare "to do something extempore," from Latin in- "not" + provisus, past participle of providēre "to foresee, provide."
