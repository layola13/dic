belletristic:/ˌbɛləˈtrɪstɪk/| adj.|文艺的；文学的；精美的|Of or relating to belles lettres; literary or artistic. Characterized by elegant literary style.  Concerned with aesthetic rather than practical matters. |Her writing style is quite belletristic, full of elegant prose and poetic imagery. 她的写作风格非常文艺，充满了优美的散文和诗意的意象。| The museum showcased a collection of belletristic manuscripts from the 18th century.  博物馆展出了18世纪的一系列文学手稿。 |近义词：literary, artistic, refined, elegant, polished |反义词：functional, utilitarian, plain, unrefined |词性:adjective

belletristics: /ˌbɛləˈtrɪstɪks/ | n. |纯文学；文学作品|Literature that is an end in itself and not merely informative. Works of fiction, poetry, drama, etc., valued for their aesthetic qualities. |He devoted his life to the study of belletristics. 他一生致力于纯文学研究。 |She has a deep appreciation for the belletristics of the Romantic period. 她对浪漫主义时期的文学作品有着深刻的欣赏。 | 近义词：literature, belles lettres, literary works|反义词：nonfiction, informational writing|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *belles-lettres* ("fine letters"), plural of *belle lettre* ("beautiful letter"), from *belle* ("beautiful") + *lettre* ("letter"). Ultimately from Latin *bella* (feminine of *bellus*, "beautiful") and *littera* ("letter").
