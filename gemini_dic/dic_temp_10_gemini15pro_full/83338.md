gypsyhoods:/ˈdʒɪpsihʊdz/| n.|吉普赛人身份，吉普赛人的生活方式|The state or quality of being a gypsy; the collective characteristics and traditions of gypsies.  A life lived wandering, often associated with freedom and nonconformity.|He romanticized the gypsyhoods, dreaming of a life on the open road.|近义词：nomadism, wandering, roaming|反义词:settled life, stability|词性:名词

Etymology: From "gypsy" + "-hood".  "Gypsy" ultimately derives from the Middle English "Gypcian", a corruption of "Egyptian," reflecting a mistaken belief about the origins of the Roma people.  "-hood" is an Old English suffix denoting "state, condition, quality."
