nonsentences:/ˈnɒnsɛntənsɪz/ |n.| 谬论，胡言乱语 |Statements or utterances that are meaningless, illogical, or grammatically incorrect. |The politician's speech was filled with nonsentences, making it hard to understand his point. 这位政客的演讲充满了胡言乱语，让人很难理解他的观点。|His claims were pure nonsentences. 他的说法纯属胡扯。| synonyms: gibberish, balderdash, drivel, nonsense | antonyms: sense, truth, fact | noun

Etymology:  From non- + sentence.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Sentence" comes from Old French "sentence" meaning a judgment, meaning, or opinion.  Thus, "nonsentences" literally means "not sentences" or "not making sense."
