bookends: /ˈbʊkˌɛndz/
n.
书挡；结束；
A pair of supports placed at the ends of a row of books to hold them upright. Also used figuratively to refer to things that mark the beginning and end of something.
例句：The antique bookends were shaped like lions.  这对古董书挡形状像狮子。

例句：The two events served as bookends to a tumultuous year. 这两件事成了动荡不安的一年的开端和结尾。
近义词：supports, brackets
反义词: (No direct antonym)
词性: noun


bookend: /ˈbʊkˌɛnd/
vt.
作为...的开始和结束；
To mark the beginning and end of something.
例句：The concert was bookended by two of their most popular songs.  这场音乐会的开头和结尾都是他们最受欢迎的两首歌。

例句：Her career was bookended by two major awards. 她的职业生涯以两项大奖开始和结束。
近义词：enclose, bracket
反义词: (No direct antonym)
词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "book" + "end," literally the ends of a row of books.  由“book”（书）和“end”（末端）组成，字面意思是“一排书的两端”。
