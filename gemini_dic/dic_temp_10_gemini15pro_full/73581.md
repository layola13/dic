foret:/ˈfɒrɪt/ /ˈfɔːreɪ/ | n. |森林，尤指皇家狩猎林|A large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth, especially one reserved for hunting by royalty. |The king hunted deer in the royal foret.| 近义词：forest, woodland, wood | 反义词：plain, field | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *foret (Modern French forêt), from Late Latin forestis (silva) “(forest) outside (the park),” from Latin foris “outside, out of doors.”
