dextroglucoses: /ˌdɛkstroʊˈɡluːkoʊsiz/ | n. | 右旋葡萄糖 | Dextroglucoses is the plural form of dextroglucose, a simple sugar (monosaccharide) and an important carbohydrate in biology.  It is an isomer of glucose that rotates plane-polarized light to the right.  The term is often used interchangeably with D-glucose or glucose. | Dextroglucoses are the primary source of energy for the body.  右旋葡萄糖是身体的主要能量来源。 | The athlete consumed a drink containing dextroglucoses for quick energy.  这位运动员喝了一种含有右旋葡萄糖的饮料来快速补充能量。 | synonyms: D-glucose, glucose, grape sugar | antonyms: L-glucose | noun
