lock-down: /ˈlɒkdaʊn/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkdaʊn/ (US)
lock-down: n.|封锁，禁闭|A state of isolation or restricted access imposed on a population, typically in response to a threat such as a pandemic or security risk.  |The city was placed under lockdown after the bombing.  这座城市在爆炸事件后被封锁了。|近义词：quarantine, confinement, isolation, shutdown|反义词：freedom, liberation|词性:名词

lock-down: /ˈlɒkdaʊn/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkdaʊn/ (US)
lock-down: v.|封锁，禁闭|To confine or restrict access to a place or population. |The government decided to lock down the entire region. 政府决定封锁整个地区。|近义词：confine, restrict, isolate, shut down|反义词：open, free|词性:动词
