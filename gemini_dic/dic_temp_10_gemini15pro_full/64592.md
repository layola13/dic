erudite:/ˈerjʊˌdaɪt/ | adj. | 博学的，学问渊博的 | Having or showing great knowledge or learning. |例句：The professor was known for his erudite lectures.  这位教授以其博学的讲座而闻名。|例句：She impressed everyone with her erudite analysis of the poem. 她对这首诗的博学分析给每个人都留下了深刻的印象。|近义词：learned, scholarly, knowledgeable, well-read, educated |反义词：ignorant, uneducated, illiterate |词性:形容词
erudite:/ˈerʊdaɪt/ | n. | 博学的人，饱学之士 | A learned or scholarly person. |例句：He is considered an erudite in the field of ancient history. 他被认为是古代史领域的博学之士。 |例句：The conference attracted erudites from all over the world.  这次会议吸引了来自世界各地的饱学之士。|近义词：scholar, intellectual, savant |反义词：ignoramus, philistine |词性:名词
