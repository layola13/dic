baroqueness:/bəˈroʊknəs/
noun
华丽; 繁复; 巴洛克风格
The quality or state of being baroque; elaborate and complex ornamentation in art, architecture, and music, especially of the 17th and 18th centuries.
The baroqueness of the music was overwhelming.  这音乐的华丽繁复令人叹为观止。
近义词: elaborateness, ornateness, extravagance, flamboyance
反义词: simplicity, plainness, austerity
词性: n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *baroque* (irregularly shaped pearl), from Portuguese *barroco* (same meaning), probably from Latin *verruca* (wart).  The term was initially used derogatorily to describe the perceived excesses and irregularities of the Baroque style compared to the preceding Renaissance.
