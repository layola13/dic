anti-philosophy:/ˌænti fɪˈlɒsəfi/ | n. | 反哲学 |  A stance that rejects or opposes traditional philosophical methods or assumptions, often characterized by skepticism, irony, or a focus on non-traditional forms of inquiry.  | Example: Some consider Dadaism a form of anti-philosophy because of its rejection of reason and logic.  达达主义因其对理性和逻辑的排斥而被一些人认为是一种反哲学。

Example:  His anti-philosophy manifested in a distrust of grand narratives and a preference for lived experience. 他的反哲学体现在对宏大叙事的怀疑和对生活经验的偏爱。 | Synonyms:  iconoclasm, non-philosophy, skepticism | Antonyms:  philosophy, traditionalism | Noun
