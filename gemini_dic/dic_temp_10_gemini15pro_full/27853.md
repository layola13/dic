canonize:/ˈkænənaɪz/| vt.|册封为圣徒；使崇高；使成为经典|To declare (a deceased person) to be a saint, especially by the Catholic Church; to glorify or exalt; to accept (something) as being of high quality or great importance.|例句：Joan of Arc was canonized in 1920.  圣女贞德在1920年被封圣。

例句：His works have been canonized by literary critics. 他的作品已被文学评论家奉为经典。|近义词：deify, exalt, glorify, enshrine|反义词：condemn, denounce, vilify|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *canonizare, from Latin canon "church law, rule, standard," from Greek kanōn "straight rod, rule, standard."
