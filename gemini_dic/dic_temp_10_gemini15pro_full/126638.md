naive:/nɑɪˈiːv/ /naɪˈiv/| adj.|天真的，幼稚的，轻信的|Showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment.|She was very naive to believe that he would change.|近义词：innocent, gullible, unsophisticated, artless, ingenuous|反义词：sophisticated, worldly, experienced, cynical, shrewd|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *naïf*, earlier *naif*, from Latin *nativus* "native, natural, inborn."  The original sense was "natural, unaffected," later developing the sense of "unsophisticated, simple."
