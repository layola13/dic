luck-out:/ˌlʌk ˈaʊt/ | phrasal verb | 幸运，走运，交好运 | To have an unexpected piece of good fortune. | I really lucked out finding that parking spot right in front of the store. 我真的很幸运，在商店门口找到了一个停车位。|  I lucked out when I found a $20 bill on the sidewalk. 我在人行道上发现一张 20 美元的钞票，真是走运。 | 近义词: get lucky, be fortunate, strike it lucky | 反义词: be unlucky, have bad luck | 词性: 短语动词

Etymology:  "Luck out" likely originated in American English in the early 20th century. "Luck" itself comes from Middle Dutch "luc" meaning "fortune, happiness." The "out" particle suggests a positive outcome or escape from difficulty.
