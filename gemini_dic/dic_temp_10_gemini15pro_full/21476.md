blowzy: /ˈblaʊzi/ (US), /ˈblaʊzi/ (UK)| adj.|蓬乱的，邋遢的，(尤指女子)脸色红润的|Disheveled and untidy;  (especially of a woman) having a red or flushed complexion.|例句：The blowzy woman stumbled out of the bar.  那个蓬乱的女人跌跌撞撞地从酒吧里走了出来.|近义词：disheveled, unkempt, ruddy, flushed, frowzy|反义词：tidy, neat, kempt, pale|词性:形容词

blowzy: /ˈblaʊzi/ (US), /ˈblaʊzi/ (UK)| adj.|俗气的，艳俗的|Gaudy; excessively showy.|例句：She wore a blowzy pink dress to the party. 她穿了一件艳俗的粉红色连衣裙去参加聚会.|近义词：garish, gaudy, flashy, tasteless|反义词：tasteful, elegant, refined|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *blowsy*, perhaps from *blowen* (blown) + *-sy*.  The original sense likely referred to hair disarranged by the wind.
