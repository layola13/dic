developmentalism:/dɪˌvɛləpˈmɛntəlɪzəm/| n.|发展主义|The belief that economic and social progress should be achieved through planned government policies, often focused on industrialization and modernization.  It emphasizes the importance of economic growth as a primary goal and often prioritizes material progress over other considerations.|例句：Developmentalism was a dominant ideology in many Latin American countries during the mid-20th century.  发展主义是20世纪中期许多拉丁美洲国家的主导意识形态。

例句：Critics argue that developmentalism can lead to social inequality and environmental damage. 批评人士认为，发展主义可能导致社会不平等和环境破坏。|近义词：modernization, industrialization, progress, growth|反义词: stagnation, decline, traditionalism|词性:noun


Etymology: development + -ism

Development: from Old French *desveloper "to unwrap, unfold" (Modern French développer), from des- "un-, out" (from Latin dis-) + voloper "to wrap" (from Latin volvere "to roll").
-ism: a suffix forming abstract nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine, party, etc. (baptism, barbarism, criticism).
