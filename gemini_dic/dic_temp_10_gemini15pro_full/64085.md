epoch:/ˈiːpɒk/ (US) /ˈɛpɒk/ (UK)| n. |时代，纪元，时期|A period of time in history or a person's life, typically one marked by notable events or particular characteristics.|例句：The invention of the printing press marked a new epoch in the history of communication. 印刷机的发明标志着通讯史上一个新纪元的开始。

例句：The reign of Queen Victoria was an epoch of great social change. 维多利亚女王统治时期是一个社会巨变的时代。|近义词：era, age, period, time, date |反义词: instant, moment|词性:名词


epocha: This word is an older, less common variant of "epoch." It carries the same meaning and usage.  Consider using "epoch" instead, as it is the more widely accepted form in modern English.
