flywheel:/ˈflaɪˌwiːl/ /ˈflaɪˌhwiːl/| n.|飞轮|A heavy revolving wheel in a machine that is used to increase the machine's momentum and stores rotational energy, thereby smoothing out the operation or power output of the machine.|The flywheel helps to regulate the engine's speed. 飞轮有助于调节发动机的速度。|The large flywheel kept the pump running smoothly.  大型飞轮使泵运转平稳。|synonyms: inertia wheel, stabilizer|antonyms: brake|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From fly + wheel.  "Fly" in this context refers to the rotating motion.
