multi-negative:/ˌmʌltiˈnɛɡətɪv/| adj.|多重否定的|Containing more than one negative element, often resulting in a positive meaning, especially in non-standard dialects.|She ain't got no money. (Meaning she has no money). 她没有钱。|Near synonyms: double negative|Antonyms: affirmative|Adjective

multi-negative:/ˌmʌltiˈnɛɡətɪv/| n.|多重否定（现象）|The use of more than one negative word in a clause, often found in some dialects and considered non-standard in formal English.|The sentence "I ain't never seen nothing like that" is an example of a multi-negative. 句子“我从来没见过这样的事”是多重否定的一个例子。|Near synonyms: double negative|Antonyms: affirmative|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From multi- (meaning "many") + negative. "Multi-" comes from Latin "multus" (much, many). "Negative" comes from Latin "negativus" (denying).
