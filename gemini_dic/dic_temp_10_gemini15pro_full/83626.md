haemocytolysis:/ˌhiːməsaɪˈtɒlɪsɪs/| n. |溶血；血细胞溶解|The destruction or lysis of red blood cells.  This process releases hemoglobin into the surrounding fluid. |Example 1: The venom of certain snakes can cause rapid haemocytolysis. 某些蛇的毒液会导致迅速的溶血。

Example 2:  The lab technician observed haemocytolysis in the blood sample after adding the hypotonic solution.  实验室技术员在添加低渗溶液后观察到血液样本中的溶血现象。|近义词：hemolysis|反义词:  haematopoiesis (造血)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From haemo- (blood) + cyto- (cell) + -lysis (destruction).  The prefix "haemo-" comes from Greek "haima" meaning "blood". "Cyto-" comes from Greek "kytos" meaning "vessel" or "cell".  "-lysis" comes from Greek "lusis" meaning "loosening" or "dissolution".
