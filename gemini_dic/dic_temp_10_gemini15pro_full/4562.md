alchemy: /ˈælkɪmi/ | n. | 炼金术; 神秘的变化; | A medieval chemical science and speculative philosophy aiming to achieve the transmutation of base metals into gold, the discovery of a universal cure for disease, and the preparation of a potion that could prolong life indefinitely.  |  The idea of turning lead into gold is pure alchemy.  把铅变成金的想法纯粹是炼金术。 | synonyms: magic, sorcery, witchcraft | antonyms: science, chemistry | noun

etymology:  Late Middle English: from Old French alchimie, via Arabic from al-kīmiyā’ (with the Arabic definite article al-), probably from Greek khēmeia ‘art of transmuting metals’, possibly related to khumos ‘juice’.
