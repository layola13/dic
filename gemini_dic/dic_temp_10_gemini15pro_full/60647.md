eclecticism: /ɪˈklɛktɪˌsɪzəm/
n.
折衷主义；博采众长
the practice of deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.
Her home décor is a fascinating blend of Asian and European influences, a true testament to her eclecticism. 她的家居装饰融合了亚洲和欧洲的影响，真正体现了她的折衷主义。
The museum's eclecticism is evident in its collection, which ranges from ancient Egyptian artifacts to contemporary paintings.  博物馆的藏品种类繁多，从古埃及文物到当代绘画，无不体现其折衷主义。
近义词: pluralism, syncretism, catholicity
反义词:  purism, dogmatism, orthodoxy
词性: noun

Etymology:
From French éclectisme, from Ancient Greek ἐκλεκτικός (eklektikós) “selecting, choosing”, from ἐκλέγω (eklégō) "I pick out, choose," from ἐκ (ek, “out”) + λέγω (légō, “I gather, I say”).
