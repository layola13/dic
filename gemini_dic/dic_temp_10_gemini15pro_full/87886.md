hield: /hiːld/
n.|盾牌；保护；保护物|A broad piece of metal or another suitable material, held by straps or a handle attached to its back, used as a protection against blows or missiles.|The knight raised his shield to deflect the blow. 骑士举起盾牌挡开攻击。|近义词: buckler, aegis, protector, defense|反义词: vulnerability, exposure|词性: 名词

词源: From Old English *scield, from Proto-Germanic *skelduz, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kel- (“to cover, conceal”). Cognate with Old Saxon skild, Old Norse skildir, Old Frisian skeld, Middle Dutch scilt, Dutch schild, Old High German scilt, German Schild, Gothic 𐍃𐌺𐌹𐌻𐌳𐍃 (skildus).
