hypersensitivity:/ˌhaɪpərˌsensəˈtɪvəti/ | n. | 过敏性，超敏性 |  An abnormal or excessive sensitivity to a substance (such as a drug or antigen) or to physical stimuli (such as light, touch, or cold).  A state in which the body reacts with an exaggerated immune response to a foreign agent. |  He has a hypersensitivity to peanuts. 他对花生过敏。| 近义词: allergy, intolerance | 反义词: hyposensitivity, tolerance | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- (Greek 'over, above') + sensitivity (from sensitive, ultimately from Latin sentire 'to feel').
