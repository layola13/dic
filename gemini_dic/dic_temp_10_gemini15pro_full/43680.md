credentialism: /krɪˈdɛnʃəlɪzəm/
noun
文凭主义；资格主义；学历主义
An overemphasis on credentials, especially academic degrees, as a measure of a person's ability or worth.  The belief that academic qualifications are the most important factor in determining someone's suitability for a job or other role.
Many argue that credentialism has become rampant in the job market, making it difficult for those without advanced degrees to advance.  许多人认为，文凭主义在就业市场上已经变得猖獗，使得没有高等学位的人很难晋升。
The university's focus on research and publication reinforces credentialism in academia. 这所大学对研究和出版的重视强化了学术界的文凭主义。
synonyms:  elitism, academicism, meritocracy (sometimes), qualificationism
antonyms:  experientialism, pragmatism
