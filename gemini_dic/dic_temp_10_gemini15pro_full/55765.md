diversified: /dɪˈvɜːrsɪfaɪd/ | adj. | 多样化的，多元化的 | Made up of a variety of things, especially to reduce risk. |  Example:  They have a diversified portfolio of investments. 他们拥有多元化的投资组合。| Example: The company diversified its product line to appeal to a broader market. 公司将其产品线多样化，以吸引更广泛的市场。 | Synonyms: varied, assorted, heterogeneous, multifaceted | Antonyms: specialized, homogenous, uniform | adjective

diversify: /dɪˈvɜːrsɪfaɪ/ | v. | 使多样化，多元化 | To make or become more diverse or varied. | Example:  Farmers are being encouraged to diversify their crops. 鼓励农民使其作物多样化。| Example: The company has been trying to diversify its business interests. 该公司一直在努力使其业务利益多样化。| Synonyms: vary, expand, broaden, branch out | Antonyms: specialize, focus, narrow | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the late Middle English diversifien, from the Old French diversifier, from the Medieval Latin diversificare, from Latin diversus ("different") + facere ("to make").
