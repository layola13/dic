decussate:/ˌdiːˈkʌseɪt/ /dɪˈkʌseɪt/
|v.|交叉；X形交叉；|to cross in the form of an X.|The optic nerves decussate at the optic chiasm.|intersect, crisscross|parallel|verb


decussated:/dɪˈkʌseɪtɪd/
|adj.|交叉的；X形交叉的；|arranged in the shape of an X, often referring to anatomical structures.|The decussated fibers of the corpus callosum connect the two hemispheres of the brain.|crossed, intersected|parallel|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *decussatus*, past participle of *decusso*, from *decussis* "a Roman coin worth ten asses, an X", from *decem* "ten". The X shape of the numeral ten led to its use in describing crossed or intersecting structures.
