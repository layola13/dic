infallibly:/ɪnˈfælɪbli/| adv.| 绝对可靠地，万无一失地|in a way that is always right and never wrong; without making mistakes| The system infallibly detects any errors.  这个系统能万无一失地检测出任何错误。|  The predictions proved infallibly accurate. 这些预测被证明是绝对准确的。|certainly, reliably, unfailingly, invariably| fallibly, uncertainly|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin infallibilis (unfailing, not liable to err), from in- (not) + Late Latin fallibilis (liable to err), from Latin fallere (to deceive, to err) + -ibilis (capable of being).
