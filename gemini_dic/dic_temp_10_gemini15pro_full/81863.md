gridlock:/ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk/ (US) /ˈɡrɪdˌlɑːk/| n. v. |交通堵塞；僵局|
(n.) A situation where streets are so blocked by traffic that vehicles cannot move.  (v.) To cause a traffic jam; to bring to a standstill.
|The accident gridlocked traffic for miles. 事故造成了数英里的交通堵塞。
The negotiations were gridlocked by disagreements over funding. 谈判因资金方面的分歧而陷入僵局。|Synonyms: traffic jam, standstill, bottleneck, deadlock (n.); block, stall, impede (v.)|Antonyms: free flow, smooth sailing (n.); expedite, facilitate (v.)|noun, verb
