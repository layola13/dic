haggles:/ˈhæɡlz/ (US), /ˈhæɡəlz/ (UK)| v. (third-person singular simple present haggles, present participle haggling, simple past haggled, past participle haggled)|讨价还价；争论|To argue back and forth about a price or other terms of an agreement.|She haggled with the street vendor over the price of the scarf. 她和街头小贩就围巾的价格讨价还价。|近义词：bargain, negotiate, dicker|反义词：acquiesce, concede|词性：动词

haggle:/ˈhæɡl/ (US), /ˈhæɡəl/ (UK)| n.|讨价还价；争论|The act of arguing back and forth about a price or other terms of an agreement.|After much haggle, they finally agreed on a price. 经过一番讨价还价，他们终于就价格达成了协议。|近义词：bargaining, negotiation|反义词：agreement, concession|词性：名词

Etymology:  From Middle Dutch *haghelen, frequentative of hagen "to chop, hack," from Proto-Germanic *hakōną.  The semantic shift likely occurred from the idea of reducing something bit by bit, like chopping.
