burdensomely:/ˈbɜːrdənsəmli/ | adv. | 沉重地，繁重地 | In a burdensome manner; so as to be a burden.  Putting a strain on someone's resources or energy. | The regulations were applied burdensomly, stifling innovation.  这些规章制度执行起来繁琐沉重，扼杀了创新。| The old man walked burdensomly, weighed down by his worries. 老人忧心忡忡，步履沉重。 | 近义词: onerously, heavily, oppressively | 反义词: lightly, easily | 词性: 副词

词源: From burdensome + -ly.  Burdensome itself comes from burden (a load or weight) + -some (characterized by or tending to). Ultimately from Old English *byrðen.
