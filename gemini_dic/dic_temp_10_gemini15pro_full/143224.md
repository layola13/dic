outcast:/ˈaʊtkæst/| n. adj.|被驱逐者，被遗弃者；被社会排斥的|A person who has been rejected by society or a particular group.|例句：He lived as an outcast after being expelled from his community.  他被逐出社区后，过着被遗弃者的生活。

例句：She felt like an outcast when her friends started ignoring her. 当她的朋友开始无视她时，她感觉自己像个被遗弃的人。|近义词：pariah, exile, reject|反义词:insider, accepted, included|词性：名词，形容词

outcurst:/aʊtˈkɜːrst/| adj. (obsolete)|被诅咒的，遭天谴的|Under a curse; accursed.|例句：The outcurst villain met a tragic end.  这个被诅咒的恶棍迎来了悲惨的结局。

例句：The villagers believed the withered crops were a sign of an outcurst land. 村民们认为枯萎的庄稼是这片土地被诅咒的迹象。|近义词：cursed, damned, accursed|反义词:blessed, sacred|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From out + curst (cursed).  The prefix "out-" intensifies the meaning of "cursed."
