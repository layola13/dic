overplots: /ˌoʊvərˈplɑːts/ | v.  n. |  过度绘制；附加情节；覆盖图 |  (v.) To add too many subplots or complications to a story, making it convoluted.  (n.) The act of overplotting, or an instance of it; a subplot that is unnecessary or detracts from the main narrative. |  (v.) The author overplotted the novel, confusing the readers with too many characters and storylines. 作者在小说中设计了过多的情节，过多的角色和故事情节让读者感到困惑。 (n.) The overplots in the film made it difficult to follow the main narrative.  电影中过多的支线情节使得主线叙事难以理解。 | (v.) complicate, overcomplicate, embellish  (n.) subplot, complication, convolution | (v.) simplify, streamline (n.) simplicity, clarity | verb, noun
