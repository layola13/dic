hailshot:/ˈheɪlʃɒt/ (UK) /ˈheɪlʃɑːt/ (US)| n.|雹弹；霰弹；冰雹射击|A shot, or the pellets of a shot, consisting of small pieces of lead, or occasionally of other metal.  Originally, small projectiles like hail.  Now, usually refers to a shotgun shell loaded with very small shot. |The hunter used hailshot to bring down the small birds. 猎人用霰弹打下了小鸟。|Synonyms: birdshot, small shot|Antonyms: buckshot, slug|noun


hailshot:/ˈheɪlʃɒt/ (UK) /ˈheɪlʃɑːt/ (US)| n.|密集射击，猛烈攻击 (figurative, less common)|A barrage or volley of something (figurative).  Like a hail of shots. |The speaker faced a hailshot of questions from the audience. 演讲者面临观众一连串的提问。|Synonyms: barrage, volley, onslaught|Antonyms: lull, respite|noun
