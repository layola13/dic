grotty:/ˈɡrɒti/ (British) /ˈɡrɑːti/ (American)| adj.|肮脏的，邋遢的，令人不愉快的|Unpleasant, dirty, and run-down.  Often used to describe a place or object.|例句：The hotel room was grotty and smelled of stale cigarettes.  酒店房间很脏，一股陈旧的香烟味。

例句：He lives in a grotty little apartment downtown. 他住在市中心一间脏乱的小公寓里。|近义词：dirty, filthy, squalid, shabby, dingy, seedy|反义词：clean, pristine, immaculate|词性:形容词

grot:/ɡrɒt/ (British) /ɡrɑːt/ (American)| n.|洞穴，岩洞；肮脏的地方|A cave or cavern, especially one that is large and impressive; a dirty or unpleasant place.|例句：The kids explored the grot at the back of the beach. 孩子们探索了海滩后面的洞穴。

例句：His bedroom is a complete grot. 他的卧室简直就是一个猪窝。|近义词：cave, cavern, den, hovel|反义词：palace, mansion|词性:名词
