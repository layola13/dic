hareld: /ˈhærəld/ | n. | 传令官；使者 | A herald is an official messenger, especially one representing a monarch or a state. |  The king sent a herald to announce his arrival. 国王派了一名传令官宣布他的到来。

The herald read the royal proclamation. 传令官宣读了皇家公告。

近义词: messenger, envoy, emissary | 反义词: None | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *herold, from Old French herault, herald, from Frankish *hairovald, from Proto-Germanic *harja-waldaz (“army commander”).  The first element is from Proto-Germanic *harjaz (“army”). The second element is from Proto-Germanic *waldaz (“ruler, power”), from Proto-Indo-European *welh₁- (“to rule, to be strong”).  Cognate with Old High German herolt, Middle Dutch heraute, herald.
