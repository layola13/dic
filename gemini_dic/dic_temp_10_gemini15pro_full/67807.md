eyespot:/ˈaɪˌspɒt/ (US) /ˈaɪˌspɒt/ (UK)| n. |眼点，眼斑|A simple light-sensitive organ or area, as found in some invertebrates, consisting of a cluster of photoreceptor cells and sometimes a pigment shield; a pigmented spot resembling an eye.|The butterfly has eyespots on its wings to deter predators.|近义词：stigma, ocellus|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

Etymology:  From eye + spot.  The word "eye" comes from Old English *ēage*, from Proto-Germanic *augō*, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ōkʷ- ("to see"). The word "spot" comes from Middle English *spotte*, of uncertain origin.
