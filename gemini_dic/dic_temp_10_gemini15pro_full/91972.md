hypermutably:/ˌhaɪpərˈmjuːtəbli/ | adv. | 超突变地 | In a way that is characterized by hypermutation (a very high rate of mutation). | The virus evolved hypermutably, quickly developing resistance to the drugs.  病毒超突变地进化，迅速对药物产生了抗药性。| 近义词: highly mutably, extremely mutably | 反义词: stably, immutably | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
hyper- (prefix): from Greek, meaning "over, above, beyond"
mutable: from Latin "mutabilis," meaning "changeable," from "mutare" meaning "to change"
-ly (suffix): forms adverbs
