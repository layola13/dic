antischolastic:/ˌænti-skəˈlæstɪk/ | adj. | 反学院派的，反对学究式的 | Opposed to or hostile towards traditional academic principles, methods, or practices; rejecting or disdainful of scholarship or intellectual pursuits. |  The artist's antischolastic approach to painting prioritized emotional expression over technical skill. 这位艺术家的反学院派绘画方法优先考虑情感表达而不是技巧。

The movement was characterized by its antischolastic and anti-intellectual sentiments.这场运动的特点是其反学院派和反智主义情绪。 | Near synonyms:  anti-intellectual, iconoclastic, unconventional, heterodox  | Antonyms:  scholarly, academic, intellectual, traditional | adjective


antischolastics:/ˌænti-skəˈlæstɪks/ | n. | 反学院派人士 | People who oppose or are hostile towards traditional academic principles, methods, or practices. | The antischolastics believed that true knowledge came from experience, not books. 反学院派人士认为真正的知识来自经验，而不是书本。

The antischolastics often challenged the authority of established institutions.反学院派人士经常挑战既有机构的权威。 | Near synonyms:  iconoclasts, dissenters, freethinkers | Antonyms:  scholars, academics, intellectuals | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + scholastic. "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposed to." "Scholastic" comes from the Latin "scholasticus," meaning "of or pertaining to a school or scholar."
