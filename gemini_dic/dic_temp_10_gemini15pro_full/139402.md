observator:/əbˈzɜːrvətər/ | n. | 观察者；遵守者；评论员 |  A person who observes or notices something; someone who adheres to or keeps something (a law, custom, etc.); a commentator. |  The astronomer was a keen observator of the night sky.  这位天文学家是夜空的敏锐观察者。 | synonyms: onlooker, spectator, witness, watcher, monitor, commentator, analyst | antonyms: participant, actor, doer | noun

observator:/əbˈzɜːrvətər/ | n. | 观察员 | He served as a United Nations observator in the conflict zone. 他在冲突地区担任联合国观察员。 | synonyms:  inspector, supervisor, overseer | antonyms: participant, perpetrator | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *observator, from Latin observare ("to observe"), from ob- ("towards, against") + servare ("to watch, keep, guard").
