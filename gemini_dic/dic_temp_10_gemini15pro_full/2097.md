adamantly:/ˈædəm(ə)ntli/| adv.|坚决地，坚定不移地|In an adamant manner; resolutely; unyieldingly. Showing or characterized by a stubborn and inflexible determination not to change one's mind or course of action.|He adamantly refused to admit he was wrong. 他坚决不肯承认自己错了。

She adamantly maintained her innocence. 她坚决维护自己的清白。|近义词：resolutely, firmly, stubbornly, inflexibly|反义词：yielding, flexibly, waveringly|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *adamantinus, from Latin adamas, adamantis (“adamant, hard steel, diamond”), from Ancient Greek ἀδάμας (adámas, “untamed, invincible, diamond”).  The original meaning referred to the hardness of diamonds and other extremely hard substances. This evolved metaphorically to describe a firmness of will or opinion.

USAGE->
He adamantly refused to change his plans. = 他坚决拒绝改变他的计划。

He adamantly denied any involvement in the crime. = 他坚决否认参与了这起犯罪。
