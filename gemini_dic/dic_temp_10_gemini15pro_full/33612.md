cinchonine:/ˈsɪŋkənɪn/ /ˈsɪŋkənaɪn/| n. |金鸡纳碱|A bitter crystalline alkaloid extracted from cinchona bark. It is isomeric with cinchonidine and is used as an antimalarial drug.|Scientists are exploring new uses for cinchonine beyond malaria treatment. 科学家正在探索金鸡纳碱在疟疾治疗之外的新用途。|Synonyms: quinine, cinchona |Antonyms: None|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cinchonine*, from New Latin *Cinchona* (genus name of the cinchona tree), from *Countess of Chinchón*, wife of the viceroy of Peru, who was cured of a fever by the bark in 1638.
