belluard:/ˈbɛlwərd/| n.|堡垒，外围工事，斜堤|A belluard is an outwork of a fortification, a fortified wall or rampart.|The defenders retreated to the belluard as the enemy advanced.|Synonyms: rampart, bulwark, bastion, fortification|Antonyms: ditch, moat|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *bellouard*, from Middle French *belouard*, ultimately from Old French *bollewerk*, from Middle Dutch *bolwerc* (whence also English *bulwark*).
