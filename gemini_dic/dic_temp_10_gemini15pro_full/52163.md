diaphragm:/ˈdaɪəˌfræm/ (US), /ˈdaɪəfræm/ (UK)| n.|隔膜，横膈膜，光圈|A dome-shaped, muscular partition separating the thorax from the abdomen in mammals. It plays a major role in breathing, as its contraction increases the volume of the thorax and so inflates the lungs.  It can also refer to a thin sheet of material forming a partition, such as in a loudspeaker or contraceptive device.|The diaphragm contracts when you inhale. |Synonyms: membrane, septum|Antonyms: None|Noun

diaphragm:/ˈdaɪəˌfræm/ (US), /ˈdaɪəfræm/ (UK)| n.|光圈|A device in a camera or other optical instrument that controls the amount of light passing through the lens. |He adjusted the diaphragm of the camera to get the right exposure. |Synonyms: aperture, stop|Antonyms: None|Noun
