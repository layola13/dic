heliotypy:/ˌhiːliˈɒtɪpi/
noun
日光胶版术，日光照相术
A photomechanical process for producing printing plates by exposing a bichromated gelatin film under a photographic negative, hardening the gelatin where light penetrates, and then using the relief surface for printing.
Heliotypy was a popular method for reproducing photographs in the 19th century. 日光胶版术是19世纪一种流行的复制照片的方法。
heliography, photogravure
