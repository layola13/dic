gentrie: /ˈdʒɛntri/ | n. | 贵族；绅士阶层；上流社会人士 |  People of good social position, specifically below the nobility.  Refers to the landed gentry, a class of people who owned land and had a certain social standing, but were not titled nobility. | The local gentry held a lavish ball at the manor house. 当地绅士在庄园举办了奢华的舞会。 |  The gentry were known for their refined tastes and elegant manners. 绅士们以其高雅的品味和优雅的举止而闻名。 | 近义词: aristocracy, nobility, elite, upper class | 反义词: commoners, peasantry, lower class | 词性: noun

词源: From Old French *gentrie (Modern French: gentillesse) meaning "noble birth, nobility,"  from gentil "noble, high-born." Ultimately from Latin gentilis "of the same clan or family."
