bilious: /ˈbɪliəs/ | adj. | 脾气坏的；易怒的；令人作呕的 | Affected by or associated with nausea or vomiting; bad-tempered; spiteful.  Derived from the word "bile," which was believed to cause such moods. |  He had a bilious look on his face after the roller coaster ride.  他坐完过山车后脸色很难看。|  The critic's bilious review of the play was unnecessarily harsh.  评论家对这部剧的刻薄评论过于苛刻。| Synonyms: nauseous, queasy, irascible, choleric, peevish | Antonyms:  good-natured, pleasant, amiable | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *bilious*, borrowed from Late Latin *biliōsus*, from Latin *bilis* (“bile”) + *-ōsus* (suffix forming adjectives indicating fullness or abundance).
