/wɒt/, /hwɒt/ | pron. | 什么 | Used to ask for information specifying something. | What is your name? 你叫什么名字？ | Which, who |  N/A | pronoun


/wɒt/, /hwɒt/ | det. | 什么 | Used to ask for information specifying something. | What color is your car? 你的车是什么颜色的？ | Which, whose | N/A | determiner


/wɒt/, /hwɒt/ | int. | 什么 | Expressing surprise or disbelief. | What! You didn't go? 什么！你没去？ | Really? Seriously? | N/A | interjection


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hwæt, nominative and accusative neuter singular of hwā (“who”). Cognate with Scots what (“what”), West Frisian wat (“what”), Dutch wat (“what”), German was (“what”), Danish hvad (“what”), Swedish vad (“what”), Icelandic hvað (“what”), Gothic 𐍈𐍀 ("what").
