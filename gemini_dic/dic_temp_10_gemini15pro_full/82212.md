grotesque: /ɡroʊˈtɛsk/ | adj. n. | 怪诞的，荒诞的；怪诞的图案，怪诞的人物 | Odd or unnatural in shape, appearance, or character; fantastically ugly or absurd; a decorative motif characterized by distorted or fantastic human and animal forms often intertwined with foliage | The gargoyle on the church was a truly grotesque figure. 教堂上的滴水嘴兽真是一个怪诞的形象。 | synonyms: bizarre, absurd, deformed, monstrous | antonyms: beautiful, normal, ordinary | adjective

grotesque: /ɡroʊˈtɛsk/ | n. | 怪诞的图案，怪诞的人物 | A decorative motif characterized by distorted or fantastic human and animal forms often intertwined with foliage | The artist specialized in grotesques, creating strange and fantastical creatures. 这位艺术家擅长怪诞图案，创作奇异而荒诞的生物。| synonyms: caricature, gargoyle | antonyms: beauty, normality | noun

词源:
From French *grotesque*, from Italian *grottesco* “of a cave, like a grotto”, from *grotta* “cave”.  The term originally referred to paintings found in Roman grottoes or caves, which featured fanciful and distorted figures.
