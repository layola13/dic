mortified:/ˈmɔːrtɪfaɪd/ /ˈmɔːr.t̬ə.faɪd/| adj.|感到羞愧的，尴尬的，丢脸的|Feeling extremely embarrassed or ashamed.|例句：She was mortified when she realized she had forgotten her lines. 她意识到自己忘了台词时感到非常羞愧。

例句：He was mortified by his children's bad behavior.  他的孩子们的恶劣行为让他感到羞愧。|近义词：embarrassed, ashamed, humiliated|反义词：proud, pleased, delighted|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *mortifier, from Late Latin mortificāre (“to cause death, subdue the body, practice mortification”), from Latin mors (“death”) + facere (“to make, to do”). Related to mortal and mortify (verb). The sense development is from "subduing the body" to "humbling oneself" to "causing shame or humiliation to."
