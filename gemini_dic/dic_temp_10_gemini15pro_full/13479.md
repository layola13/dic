atropia:/əˈtroʊpiə/| n.|阿托品|A poisonous crystalline alkaloid extracted from deadly nightshade and other plants of the nightshade family. It is used in medicine as a muscle relaxant, e.g. to dilate the pupil of the eye.|Atropine is used to treat bradycardia (slow heart rate). 阿托品用于治疗心动过缓（心率缓慢）。|Atropine is administered before surgery to reduce salivation.  手术前使用阿托品以减少唾液分泌。|近义词: hyoscyamine (化学上的异构体),  scopolamine (药理作用相似)|反义词: physostigmine, pilocarpine (药理作用相反)|词性:noun

Etymology: From Modern Latin *Atropa* (the deadly nightshade genus, named after Atropos, one of the three Fates in Greek mythology who cuts the thread of life) + *-ia* (a suffix used to form the names of alkaloids).
