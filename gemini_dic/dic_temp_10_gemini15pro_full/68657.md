faradization:/ˌfærədaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 法拉第电疗，感应电疗 | The application of a faradic current to the body for therapeutic purposes.  It involves the use of alternating current to stimulate muscles or nerves. | He underwent faradization to treat his muscle atrophy. 他接受了法拉第电疗来治疗肌肉萎缩。|  Electrotherapy, electrostimulation |  N/A | noun

Etymology:
From Michael Faraday, the 19th-century English scientist who discovered electromagnetic induction + -ization.
