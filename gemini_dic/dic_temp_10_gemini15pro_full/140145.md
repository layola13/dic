offbeat:/ˈɔːfbiːt/ (US) /ˈɒfbiːt/ (UK)| adj. n.|不寻常的，另类的；弱拍|Unusual or unconventional; an unaccented beat in music.|

**英文解析:**

As an adjective, "offbeat" describes something that deviates from the norm, something unconventional, quirky, or eccentric.  It can refer to anything from fashion and music to humor and personalities.  As a noun, "offbeat" refers to an unaccented beat in music.

**例句:**

*She has a very offbeat sense of humor. 她有一种很不寻常的幽默感。

*They went on an offbeat vacation to a remote island. 他们去一个偏远的岛屿度过了一个另类的假期。


*The drummer emphasized the offbeats, creating a syncopated rhythm. 鼓手强调了弱拍，创造了一种切分音节奏。

*He preferred the offbeat cafes to the mainstream tourist traps. 他更喜欢另类咖啡馆，而不是主流的旅游陷阱。


**近义词:**unconventional, quirky, eccentric, unusual, irregular, unorthodox

**反义词:**conventional, ordinary, mainstream, normal, typical

**词性:**adjective, noun


offbeats:/ˈɔːfbiːts/ (US) /ˈɒfbiːts/ (UK)| n.|弱拍|Unaccented beats in music.|

**英文解析:**

As a noun, "offbeats" refers to the unaccented beats in a musical measure.  These are the beats that are not typically emphasized.

**例句:**

*The offbeats in this piece are crucial to the overall rhythmic feel.  这首曲子中的弱拍对整体的节奏感至关重要。

*He struggled to clap along on the offbeats. 他很难跟着弱拍拍手。


**近义词:**weak beats

**反义词:**downbeats, strong beats

**词性:**noun


**词源:**

The word "offbeat" originates from the musical term referring to a weak or unaccented beat.  This meaning was later extended metaphorically to describe anything unconventional or unusual.
