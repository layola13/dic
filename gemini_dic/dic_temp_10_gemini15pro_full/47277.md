de-cultural:/diːˈkʌltʃərəl/
adjective
去文化的，非文化的
The process of stripping away the cultural aspects of something.  Removing the cultural influence.
The museum's de-cultural approach makes the artifacts seem sterile and lifeless.  博物馆去文化的方法使这些文物显得毫无生气。
近义词:  deculturated, a-cultural
反义词:  cultural, enculturated
词性: adjective


de-culturize:/diːˈkʌltʃəraɪz/
verb
使失去文化特性，使非文化化
To deprive of culture; to cause to lose cultural characteristics.
Colonialism often serves to de-culturize indigenous populations. 殖民主义常常导致土著居民失去文化特征。
近义词:  assimilate, westernize
反义词:  enculturate, acculturate
词性: verb


词源:
The prefix "de-" signifies removal or reversal.  It is combined with "cultural," relating to culture, derived from the Latin "cultura" meaning "cultivation."
