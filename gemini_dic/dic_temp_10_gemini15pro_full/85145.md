hard-pressed:/ˌhɑːrdˈprɛst/ | adj. | 处于困境的；拮据的；难以应付的 |  Being under great difficulty or pressure, especially financial pressure or pressure to meet a deadline.  |  Example 1: The company is hard-pressed to meet its production targets this quarter.  这家公司难以完成本季度的生产目标。

Example 2:  I'm hard-pressed to think of a good reason for his behavior. 我很难想出一个合理的理由来解释他的行为。 |  Synonyms:  strapped (for cash), struggling, in difficulty, under pressure | Antonyms:  wealthy, prosperous, affluent, comfortable | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hard" (meaning difficult) + "pressed" (past participle of "press," meaning to exert pressure or force).  The original sense was of being pressed hard, as in being squeezed or forced into a difficult situation.
