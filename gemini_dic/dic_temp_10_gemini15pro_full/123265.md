monodist:/ˈmɒnədɪst/ | n. | 一元论者 | A person who believes in monism, the doctrine that reality is ultimately constituted by a single kind of substance or principle. |  John considered himself a monodist, believing that mind and matter were ultimately the same thing.  约翰自认为是一元论者，相信精神和物质最终是同一回事。 | synonyms: monist | antonyms: dualist, pluralist | noun
