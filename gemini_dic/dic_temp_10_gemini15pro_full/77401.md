gauzily:/ˈɡɔːzɪli/| adv.|薄纱地，朦胧地|In a manner resembling gauze; thinly or delicately veiled.  Like something seen through a gauze; vaguely or indistinctly.|The sun shone gauzily through the mist.  太阳朦胧地透过薄雾照耀。|近义词：thinly, delicately, vaguely, dimly|反义词:thickly, opaquely, clearly, distinctly|词性:副词
Etymology: From gauze + -ly. Gauze comes from Old French gaze, perhaps from Gaza, a city in Palestine known for its fine fabrics.
