nurturing: /ˈnɜːrtʃərɪŋ/ (US) /ˈnɜːtʃərɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.| 养育的，培育的；促进发展的| encouraging the growth or development of someone or something|例句：She provided a nurturing environment for her children. 她为她的孩子们提供了一个良好的成长环境。|例句：The teacher's nurturing approach helped the students to thrive. 老师的培育方法帮助学生们茁壮成长。|近义词：supportive, fostering, cherishing|反义词：neglectful, damaging, restrictive|词性:形容词

nurture: /ˈnɜːrtʃər/ (US) /ˈnɜːtʃər/ (UK)| vt.  n.| 养育，培育；滋养| to care for and protect somebody/something while they are growing and developing; to help or encourage something to grow and develop|例句：She nurtured her talent for music from a young age. 她从小就培养自己的音乐才能。|例句：The company nurtured the project from its initial stages. 公司从项目初期就对其进行了培育。|近义词：cultivate, foster, nourish|反义词：neglect, stifle, hinder|词性:动词/名词

词源: From Middle English *norturen, from Old French nurreture, norture (“nourishment, education”), from norrir (“to nourish”), from Latin nutrire (“to nourish, suckle”).
