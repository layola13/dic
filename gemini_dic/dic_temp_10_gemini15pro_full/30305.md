cenotaph:/ˈsenəˌtɑːf/ | n. |纪念碑，衣冠冢 | A monument erected in honor of a person or group of people whose remains are elsewhere. |  The city erected a cenotaph in memory of the fallen soldiers.  这座城市为阵亡将士建造了一座纪念碑。 | monument, memorial, shrine | tomb, grave | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cénotaphe*, from Ancient Greek *κενόταφον* (kenotáphon, “empty tomb”), from *κενός* (kenós, “empty”) + *τάφος* (táphos, “tomb”).
