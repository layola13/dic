dizzyingly:/ˈdɪzɪŋli/ | adv. | 令人晕眩地，快速旋转地 | In a manner that causes dizziness, often because of rapid movement or great height. | The city lights spun dizzyingly below. 城市的灯光在下面旋转，令人目眩。| The stock market has been rising dizzyingly for the past few months. 股市在过去几个月里一直在令人眼花缭乱地上涨。 | 近义词：giddily, rapidly, swiftly, breathtakingly | 反义词：slowly, gradually | 词性: 副词

dizzy:/ˈdɪzi/ | adj. | 头晕目眩的，使人眼花缭乱的 | Having or causing a sensation of whirling and a tendency to fall. | I felt dizzy after spinning around too many times.  转太多圈后，我感到头晕目眩。| The dizzying pace of modern life can be overwhelming.  现代生活的快节奏可能令人不知所措。| 近义词：giddy, lightheaded | 反义词：steady, stable | 词性: 形容词

词源: From dizzy + -ly.  Dizzy comes from Old English *dysig, from *dīsian "to become foolish," related to Old High German tusic "frantic."
