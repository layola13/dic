aperitif-wines:/əˌpɛrɪˈtiːf waɪnz/| n.|开胃酒|Alcoholic beverages, typically dry and light-bodied, served before a meal to stimulate the appetite.|We enjoyed a glass of aperitif-wines before dinner. 我们在晚餐前享用了一杯开胃酒。|近义词：aperitifs, pre-dinner drinks|反义词:digestifs, after-dinner drinks|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *apéritif*, from Latin *aperīre* "to open".  The "-wines" suffix simply clarifies that the aperitif in question is wine-based.
