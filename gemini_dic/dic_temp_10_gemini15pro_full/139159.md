obfuscation: /ˌɒbfʌsˈkeɪʃn/ (British) /ˌɑːbfʌsˈkeɪʃn/ (American)| n.| 混淆，模糊，困惑 |The act of making something unclear, obscure, or unintelligible.  It can refer to deliberately making something difficult to understand, or to the state of being unclear or confusing.|The lawyer's obfuscation of the facts made it difficult for the jury to reach a verdict. 律师对事实的混淆使得陪审团难以做出裁决。|Synonyms: confusion, obscurity, mystification, bafflement|Antonyms: clarification, elucidation, explanation|noun

obfuscations: /ˌɒbfʌsˈkeɪʃnz/ (British) /ˌɑːbfʌsˈkeɪʃnz/ (American)| n.| 混淆(复数)，模糊(复数)，困惑(复数) |Plural form of "obfuscation," referring to multiple instances of making something unclear or unintelligible.|The witness's testimony was filled with obfuscations, making it hard to determine the truth. 证人的证词充满了混淆，难以确定真相。|Synonyms: confusions, obscurities, mystifications, bafflements|Antonyms: clarifications, elucidations, explanations|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin obfuscatio, from obfuscare "to darken," from ob- "over" + fuscare "to darken," from fuscus "dark."
