muckerism:/ˈmʌkərɪzəm/
n.
粗俗行为；卑鄙行为；捣乱行为
The act of being a mucker, characterized by roughness, vulgarity, or disruptive behavior.
He was expelled for his persistent muckerism in class. 他因在课堂上持续捣乱而被开除。
近义词:rowdiness, hooliganism, mischief
反义词:politeness, civility, decorum
词性:noun

Etymology:
From "mucker" (one who deals with muck, especially a miner) + "-ism" (suffix forming nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, or practices).  The term gained its negative connotation in the early 20th century, particularly in the context of labor disputes and social unrest.
