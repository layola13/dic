elocutionary: /ˌɛləˈkjuːʃəneri/ | adj. |  雄辩的；演讲法的；表达清晰的 | Relating to the way in which something is said, or the style or manner of speaking, especially in public, and its effect on an audience. |  Her elocutionary skills impressed the judges. 她的演讲技巧给评委们留下了深刻的印象。| synonyms: articulate, eloquent, expressive, fluent, rhetorical | antonyms: inarticulate, mumbled, unclear | 词性: adjective

elocution: /ˌɛləˈkjuːʃən/ | n. | 演说术；演讲风格；口才 | The art of clear and effective public speaking, or the style or manner of speaking. | She took elocution lessons to improve her public speaking skills. 她上了演讲课来提高她的公开演讲技巧。| synonyms: oratory, rhetoric, delivery, articulation | antonyms: inarticulateness | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *elocutionem (nominative elocutio) "a speaking out," noun of action from past-participle stem of Latin eloqui "speak out, declaim, express," from ex- "out" (see ex-) + loqui "to speak" (see locution).
