gatekeeping:/ˈɡeɪtˌkiːpɪŋ/| n.|守门；把关；关卡；看门；(尤指)控制信息发布|The act of controlling, and usually limiting, general access to something.|The editor plays a crucial role in gatekeeping, deciding which stories are published and which are not. 编辑在把关方面扮演着至关重要的角色，决定哪些故事发表，哪些不发表。|近义词:control, censorship, screening, filtering|反义词:openness, transparency, accessibility|noun


gatekeeper:/ˈɡeɪtˌkiːpər/| n.|看门人；守门员；把关人|A person or thing that controls access to something.|Journalists often see themselves as gatekeepers of information. 记者通常将自己视为信息的守门人。|近义词:guardian, watchman, custodian|反义词:N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "gate" (meaning a physical barrier or entrance) + "keep" (meaning to guard or maintain).  The term originally referred to a person who literally guarded a gate. The metaphorical meaning of controlling access to information or resources developed later.
