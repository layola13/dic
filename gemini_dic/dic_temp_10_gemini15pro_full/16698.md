barricades: /ˈbærɪkeɪdz/ (US) /ˈbærɪkeɪdz/ (UK)| n. |路障，障碍物|A barrier, often hastily constructed, used to obstruct the passage of something, typically traffic or people.| The protestors erected barricades to block the street. 抗议者设置路障封锁了街道。|  synonyms: barrier, obstacle, blockade, obstruction | antonyms: opening, passage, access | noun

barricade: /ˈbærɪkeɪd/ (US) /ˈbærɪkeɪd/ (UK)| vt. |设置路障，阻挡|To obstruct or protect with a barricade.| They barricaded the doors and windows to keep the intruders out. 他们用路障挡住门窗，防止入侵者进入。| synonyms: block, obstruct, fortify, secure | antonyms: open, expose, clear | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French "barricade," from Old French "barrique" (barrel), from Latin "barrica" (barrel).  Originally, barricades were often made from barrels filled with earth or other materials.
