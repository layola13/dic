anglo-catholicism:/ˌæŋɡləʊˈkæθəlɪsɪzəm/| n. |盎格鲁天主教主义|A movement within the Church of England that emphasizes the Catholic aspects of its liturgy, doctrine, and practice, while remaining within the Anglican Communion.|Example: Anglo-Catholicism gained prominence in the 19th century. 盎格鲁天主教主义在19世纪变得突出。|Example: Some Anglo-Catholic churches have practices similar to Roman Catholic churches. 一些盎格鲁天主教教堂的实践与罗马天主教教堂相似。|Synonym: High Church, Anglo-Catholic|Antonym: Low Church, Evangelical|noun
