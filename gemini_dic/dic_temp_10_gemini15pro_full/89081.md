holour: /ˈhɒlə(r)/ (British) /ˈhɑːlər/ (American)| n.| 喧闹，吵闹；骚动；欢呼；呐喊 |A loud confused noise, especially that of people shouting vehemently.|The fans set up a tremendous holour when their team scored. |球迷们在他们的球队得分时发出巨大的欢呼声。|近义词：clamor, uproar, din, commotion, shout, yell|反义词：silence, quiet|词性：名词

词源:
From Middle English *holour, hollor, from Anglo-Norman holer, huller (“to shout, howl”),  from Old French huller, hurler (“to howl, shout, roar”), from Frankish *hūlōn (“to howl”), from Proto-Germanic *hūlōną (“to howl”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱel- (“to resound, make a noise”). Cognate with Dutch hollen (“to shout”), German hohlen, hullern (“to howl”), Icelandic hyla (“to howl”).
