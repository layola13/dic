mottle:/ˈmɑːtl/ (US), /ˈmɒtl/ (UK)| v. n. adj.|使…有斑点，斑点，杂色的|To mark with spots or blotches of different shades or colors.  A surface having colored spots or blotches.|例句：The disease mottled the leaves of the plant. 这种疾病使植物的叶子出现了斑点。|近义词：dapple, speck, fleck, blotch, spot|反义词：clear, plain, uniform|词性: verb, noun, adjective

mottle:/ˈmɑːtl/ (US), /ˈmɒtl/ (UK)| n.|斑点，杂色|A surface having colored spots or blotches.|例句：The old wall had a mottle of green and grey. 老墙上布满了绿色和灰色的斑点。|近义词：dapple, speckling, blotching|反义词：clearness, plainness, uniformity|词性: noun

mottle:/ˈmɑːtl/ (US), /ˈmɒtl/ (UK)| adj.|杂色的，有斑点的|Having colored spots or blotches.|例句：The mottled skin of the snake helped it blend in with the forest floor. 蛇身上杂色的皮肤帮助它与森林地面融为一体。|近义词：dappled, speckled, blotchy, spotted|反义词：clear, plain, uniform|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *motlen, frequentative of *motten (“to stain, spot”), of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse mattr (“matted, exhausted”).
