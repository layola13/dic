metonymy:/məˈtɒnɪmi/ (UK) /məˈtɑːnɪmi/ (US)| n. |转喻，借代|A figure of speech in which a thing or concept is referred to by the name of something closely associated with that thing or concept.|The White House announced new economic policies. (White House refers to the US government) 白宫宣布了新的经济政策。（白宫指代美国政府）|例句：The pen is mightier than the sword. (Pen refers to writing and sword to military force) 笔比剑更强大。（笔指代文字，剑指代武力）|近义词：synecdoche, metaphor|反义词: N/A|noun
