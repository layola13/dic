bitterwood:/ˈbɪtərwʊd/| n.|苦木|A tropical American tree (Quassia amara) or shrub of the family Simaroubaceae, the wood and bark of which are used as a tonic, febrifuge, and insecticide, and as a substitute for hops.  Also called quassia.|The bitterwood extract was used in traditional medicine.  苦木提取物用于传统医学。|Synonyms: quassia, amargo, simarouba|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, could consider sweetwood figuratively)|Noun

bitterwood:/ˈbɪtərwʊd/| adj.|苦的，由苦木制成的|Made from or having the qualities of bitterwood, especially its bitterness.|The bitterwood furniture had a distinctive aroma.  苦木家具有一种独特的气味。|Synonyms: bitter, acrid, pungent|Antonyms: sweet, sugary|Adjective
