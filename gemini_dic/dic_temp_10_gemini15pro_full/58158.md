droughty:/ˈdraʊti/| adj.|干旱的，口渴的|Affected by drought; thirsty.  Experiencing a prolonged period of dryness.|The droughty fields yielded a poor harvest. 干旱的田地收成不好。|The farmer was droughty after working in the sun all day.  在太阳底下工作了一整天，农民口渴了。| 近义词:arid, parched, dry, thirsty|反义词:wet, damp, moist, hydrated|词性:adjective
Etymology: From drought + -y.  "Drought" comes from the Middle English *drought*, *drougth*, from Old English *drugaþ* "dryness, drought," related to Old English *dryge* "dry."  The suffix *-y* forms adjectives meaning "characterized by" or "full of."
