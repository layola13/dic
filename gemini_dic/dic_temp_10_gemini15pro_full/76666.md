galvanist:/ˈɡælvənɪst/| n.| 电疗师；电流刺激研究者；鼓动者；激励者|A person who uses or studies galvanism (a direct current of electricity especially for medical use) or one who excites or inspires others to action.| Mary Shelley's husband, Percy Shelley, was known to dabble in galvanism, which inspired her novel "Frankenstein". 玛丽·雪莱的丈夫珀西·雪莱以涉猎电流刺激而闻名，这激发了她的小说《弗兰肯斯坦》的灵感。|近义词：motivator, inspirer, electrifier|反义词：demotivator, discourager|词性:noun

galvanism:/ˈɡælvənɪzəm/| n.| 电疗法；电流刺激；激励；鼓动|The therapeutic application of a direct current of electricity or the excitation of action or feeling.| The use of galvanism to treat muscle paralysis was popular in the 19th century.  在 19 世纪，使用电疗法治疗肌肉麻痹很流行。|近义词：electrostimulation, motivation, inspiration|反义词：discouragement, apathy|词性:noun

galvanize:/ˈɡælvənaɪz/| vt.| 电镀；激励；刺激；震动|To coat (iron or steel) with zinc to protect it from rust; to stimulate or excite as if by an electric shock.| The news galvanized the community into action. 这条消息激励了社区采取行动。|近义词：stimulate, energize, excite, jolt, shock|反义词：dampen, discourage, depress|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *galvanismo*, from the surname of Luigi Galvani (1737-1798), Italian physician and physicist, who discovered the effect of electricity on muscles.  Ultimately from a Germanic personal name, possibly related to Old High German *galan* "to sing, enchant."
