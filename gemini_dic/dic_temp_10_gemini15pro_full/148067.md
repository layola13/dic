oversatieties:/ˌoʊvərˈseɪʃieɪtiz/
|verb (third-person singular simple present oversatiates, present participle oversatiating, simple past and past participle oversatiated) |使过度饱和；使过分满足|to satisfy (a desire or an appetite) to excess.|He oversatiated his appetite with rich food. 他吃油腻的食物吃得太多了。|近义词：glut, gorge, surfeit, cloy|反义词：starve, deprive|词性:v.


oversatiety:/ˌoʊvərˈseɪʃiəti/
|noun|过度饱和；过分满足|the state of being oversatiated.|The oversatiety of the market led to a drop in prices.  市场的过度饱和导致价格下跌。|近义词：glut, surfeit, excess|反义词：lack, deficiency|词性:n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + satiety, from Latin satietas "sufficiency, fullness," from satis "enough."
