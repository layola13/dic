implicational:/ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃənəl/
adjective
蕴涵的；隐含的
Of, relating to, or characterized by implication.  Something that is implicational suggests something else without stating it directly.
His implicational criticism of the government's policy was clear to everyone. 他对政府政策的含蓄批评大家都很清楚。
The professor's lecture had several implicational meanings beyond the surface content.  教授的讲座除了表面内容外，还有几个隐含的意义。

近义词:implicit, inferred, suggested, tacit
反义词:explicit, direct, stated, overt
词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From implication + -al.  Implication comes from the Late Latin implicātiōn-, from the verb implicāre "to involve, entangle,"  from in- "in" + plicāre "to fold."
