decision-making:/dɪˈsɪʒənˌmeɪkɪŋ/| n.|决策，制定决策|The process of making choices by identifying a decision, gathering information, and assessing alternative resolutions.|The company's decision-making process is very efficient.  这家公司的决策过程非常高效。|synonyms:  choice, selection, determination|antonyms: indecision, hesitation|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From decision + making. Decision comes from the Late Middle English *decisioun*, from Old French *decision*, from Latin *dēcisiō*, *dēcisiōn*, from *dēcīdere* "to decide," from *de-* "off, down" + *caedere* "to cut."  Making comes from the Old English *macian* "to make, form, build, compose, create," of Germanic origin.
