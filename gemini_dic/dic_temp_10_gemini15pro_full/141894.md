ordeal:/ɔːrˈdiːl/
n.
严酷的考验；磨难
A difficult or painful experience, a severe test.
The hostages’ ordeal came to an end when soldiers stormed the building. 人质的苦难经历在士兵突袭大楼后结束了。

She went through a terrible ordeal. 她经历了一场可怕的磨难。

近义词:trial, tribulation, test, hardship
反义词:pleasure, comfort, ease
词性:noun

Etymology: From Old English *ordǣl "judgment, verdict," from Proto-Germanic *uzdailjam "that which is dealt out," from *uz- "out" + *dailjan "to deal, divide."  Originally referring to a primitive form of trial by subjecting the accused to physical danger, such as fire or water, to determine guilt or innocence. The meaning later broadened to encompass any severe test or experience.
