exp:/ɪkˈspɛns/ /ɛkˈsp-/| n. |费用，支出；经验；实验| An expense is the cost of something.  经验 refers to knowledge or skill gained through doing or seeing things. 实验 means a scientific procedure undertaken to make a discovery, test a hypothesis, or demonstrate a known fact.|例句：The travel expenses were high. 旅行费用很高。 He has a lot of experience in this field. 他在这个领域有很多经验。 The scientists conducted an experiment to test their theory. 科学家们进行了一项实验来验证他们的理论。|近义词：cost, outlay, expenditure (for expense); background, skill, knowledge (for experience); test, trial (for experiment)|反义词:income, revenue (for expense) |词性:noun

exp:/ɪkˈspɛns/ /ɛkˈsp-/| n. |经验| Experience refers to knowledge or skill gained through doing or seeing things. |例句：He has a lot of experience in this field. 他在这个领域有很多经验。  She gained valuable experience during her internship.  她在实习期间获得了宝贵的经验。|近义词：background, skill, knowledge|反义词:inexperience |词性:noun

exp:/ɪkˈspɛns/ /ɛkˈsp-/| n. |实验| Experiment means a scientific procedure undertaken to make a discovery, test a hypothesis, or demonstrate a known fact.|例句：The scientists conducted an experiment to test their theory. 科学家们进行了一项实验来验证他们的理论。 The results of the experiment were inconclusive. 实验结果尚无定论。 |近义词：test, trial|反义词:observation |词性:noun
