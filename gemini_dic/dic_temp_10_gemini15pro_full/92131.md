hyperscrupulousnesses:/ˌhaɪpərˈskruːpjələsnəsɪz/
|n.| 过分谨慎, 过分小心, 过分拘泥|The state of being excessively scrupulous; extreme conscientiousness, often to the point of being overly cautious or anxious about moral or ethical matters.|Her hyperscrupulousness about cleanliness made it difficult for anyone to share her living space. 她对清洁的过分谨慎让任何人难以和她共享生活空间。|His hyperscrupulousnesses in financial matters ensured he never made a risky investment. 他在财务问题上的过分谨慎确保他从不做冒险的投资。|Synonyms: meticulousness, fastidiousness, punctiliousness|Antonyms: carelessness, negligence, recklessness|noun


Etymology:
From hyper- (Greek: 'over, beyond') + scrupulous (from Latin scrupulus 'small sharp stone', figuratively 'anxiety, uneasiness') + -ness (suffix forming nouns from adjectives).
