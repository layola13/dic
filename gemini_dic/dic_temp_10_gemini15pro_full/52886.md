diminutive:/dɪˈmɪnjətɪv/| adj. n.|小的，微小的；爱称|A diminutive person or object is very small.  A diminutive word, name, or form of a word is made shorter and is often used to show affection.|例句：She has diminutive hands for an adult.  对于一个成年人来说，她的手很小。

例句：He’s known by the diminutive ‘Ed’. 他被人叫做昵称“艾德”。|近义词：small, tiny, miniature, petite|反义词：large, huge, enormous|词性:adjective, noun

diminutive:/dɪˈmɪnjətɪv/| n.|爱称，昵称|A diminutive is a shortened and often affectionate form of a name.|例句：'Tommy' is a diminutive of 'Thomas'.  “汤米”是“托马斯”的爱称。

例句：She prefers to be called by the diminutive "Liz". 她更喜欢别人叫她昵称“莉兹”。|近义词：nickname, sobriquet, pet name|反义词：formal name, full name|词性:noun
