moder:/ˈmoʊdər/ (US) /ˈməʊdə(r)/ (UK)| adj.| 温和的；适度的；有节制的 | moderate in temperature, behavior, or political views |  The hike was moderately difficult.  这次徒步旅行难度适中。| mild, temperate, reasonable | extreme, excessive, immoderate | adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *moderat*, borrowed from Old French *moderat*, from Latin *moderātus*, past participle of *moderārī* (“to restrain, control, regulate”), from *modus* (“measure, manner, mode”).
