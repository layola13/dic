heteroplasmy:/ˈhetərəˌplazmi/ | n. |异质性，异质体 | The presence of more than one type of organellar genome (mitochondrial DNA or plastid DNA) within a cell or individual. This can result in a mixture of wild-type and mutant organelles, and the severity of mitochondrial diseases often correlates with the proportion of mutant mtDNA. |  Mitochondrial diseases often exhibit heteroplasmy, meaning that cells contain a mixture of normal and mutated mitochondrial DNA.  线粒体疾病通常表现出异质性，这意味着细胞包含正常和突变的线粒体DNA的混合物。|  heterogeneity, variation | homoplasmy | noun

heteroplasmic:/ˌhetərəˈplazmɪk/ | adj. |异质性的，异质体的 |  Describing a cell or organism that contains more than one type of organellar genome. | The patient's cells were heteroplasmic for the mitochondrial DNA mutation.  病人的细胞对于线粒体DNA突变是异质的。| heterogeneous, varied | homoplasmic | adjective
