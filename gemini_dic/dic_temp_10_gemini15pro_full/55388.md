distemper:/dɪˈstɛmpər/ | n. | 坏脾气，疾病，颜料 | A state of being bad-tempered or irritable.  An infectious viral disease affecting various animals, especially dogs, causing fever, coughing, and vomiting. A type of paint using glue size or casein as a medium, formerly used for walls and ceilings. | The dog was suffering from distemper. 这只狗患有犬瘟热。 | Synonyms: illness, sickness, disease (for the disease sense); bad mood, irritability (for the bad temper sense); paint, color (for the paint sense) | Antonyms: health, wellness (for the disease sense); good mood, cheerfulness (for the bad temper sense) | noun

distemper:/dɪˈstɛmpər/ | v. | 使…烦躁，用…涂抹，用胶画 | To put someone in a bad mood or make them irritable. To paint with distemper. | The news distempered his mood. 这个消息让他心情烦躁。| Synonyms: upset, irritate, annoy (for the "make irritable" sense); paint, coat (for the "paint with" sense) | Antonyms: soothe, calm, placate (for the "make irritable" sense) | verb


Etymology: From Old French *destemprer, from des- (expressing reversal) + temprer (“to temper, moderate”).
