cratch:/krætʃ/
n.
rack; manger; crib; framework
a framework, rack, or crib.  Specifically, a manger.  Sometimes used humorously to refer to a bed.

Cratch was originally used to describe a hay rack or manger for animals, especially a Christmas manger like the one baby Jesus slept in. It can also refer more generally to any kind of frame or rack. Sometimes it's used humorously to mean a bed.

The carol mentions the "lowly cattle shed" with a "cratch" where Jesus lay.  (圣诞颂歌提到了耶稣躺着的带有“马槽”的“简陋牛棚”。)

The poor family had little more than a cratch for a bed. (这贫穷的家庭只有一张类似马槽的床。)

The carpenter built a sturdy cratch for the hay.  (木匠为干草搭建了一个结实的草架。)

近义词: manger, crib, rack, frame, bed (humorous)
反义词:  (No direct antonym)
词性: noun
