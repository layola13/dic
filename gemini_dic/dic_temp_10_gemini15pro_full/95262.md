incanton:/ɪnˈkæntən/ | vt. | 编入区，划分成行政区 | To divide (a region) into cantons or similar administrative divisions. | Example: The government incantoned the newly acquired territory into five separate administrative regions. 政府将新获得的领土划分为五个独立的行政区。

Example:  After the war, the conquered lands were incantoned and placed under military rule. 战后，被征服的土地被划分为行政区，并置于军事统治之下。 | 近义词: divide, partition, subdivide | 反义词: unite, merge, consolidate | 词性: 动词

词源: From French *incantonner*, from *canton* (“corner, district”).
