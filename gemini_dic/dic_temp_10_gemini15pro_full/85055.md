hard-boiled:/ˌhɑːrdˈbɔɪld/ | adj. |  冷酷无情的；强硬的；煮老的(鸡蛋) | (of a person) tough, unsentimental, and cynical, especially when dealing with crime or violence; (of an egg) boiled until both the white and yolk are solid |  The detective in the novel was a hard-boiled character who never showed any emotion. 小说中的侦探是个冷酷无情的角色，从不流露任何情感。 |  She likes her eggs hard-boiled. 她喜欢吃煮老的鸡蛋。 | 近义词: tough, callous, cynical, unemotional | 反义词: sentimental, soft, compassionate | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "hard" + "boiled."  The literal sense referring to eggs dates to the late 19th century.  The figurative sense, referring to a tough, unsentimental person, emerged in the early 20th century, popularized in hard-boiled detective fiction.
