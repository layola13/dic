epicoracoid: /ˌɛpɪˈkɔːrəkɔɪd/
n.
喙突上骨; 上喙骨
A small cartilage or bony element on the ventral side of the coracoid in the pectoral girdle of some vertebrates, including frogs, reptiles, and birds.  It often articulates with the clavicle.
The epicoracoid cartilages of the frog help to stabilize the shoulder girdle. 青蛙的喙突上软骨有助于稳定肩带。
The epicoracoids are fused in birds, forming part of the furcula. 在鸟类中，喙突上骨融合在一起，形成叉骨的一部分。
Synonyms: supracoracoid
Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a specific bone)
