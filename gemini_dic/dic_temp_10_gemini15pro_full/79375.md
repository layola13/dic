glibbery:/ˈɡlɪbəri/| adj.|油嘴滑舌的，流畅但不真诚的|Marked by ease and fluency in speaking or writing that often suggests or stems from insincerity or superficiality.  Characterized by a ready flow of speech; smooth-talking, often in a way that is not sincere. |He gave a glibbery answer to the interviewer's tough question, avoiding any real commitment.  他对面试官的尖锐问题给出了一个油嘴滑舌的回答，避免了任何真正的承诺。|Synonyms: smooth-talking, slick, voluble, facile, unctuous|Antonyms: sincere, earnest, genuine, forthright, inarticulate|adjective
