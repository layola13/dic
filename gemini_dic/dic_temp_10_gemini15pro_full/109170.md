legitimatize:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmaɪz/ | vt. |使合法化，使正当化|To make something legitimate; to give legal or official status to something that was not previously accepted.|The government passed a law to legitimize the sale of marijuana. 政府通过了一项法律，使大麻销售合法化。|The company tried to legitimize its questionable practices. 公司试图使其有问题的做法合法化。|Synonyms: validate, authorize, sanction, justify|Antonyms: outlaw, prohibit, ban, criminalize|verb


legitimate:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmət/ | adj. |合法的，正当的|Conforming to the law or to rules; justifiable.|The protest was a legitimate expression of public anger. 抗议是公众愤怒的合法表达。|He had a legitimate reason for being late. 他迟到是有正当理由的。|Synonyms: lawful, legal, valid, justifiable|Antonyms: illegal, unlawful, invalid, illegitimate|adjective


legitimacy:/ləˈdʒɪtɪməsi/ | n. |合法性，正当性|The state or quality of being legitimate.|The legitimacy of the election results was questioned. 选举结果的合法性受到质疑。|The new government struggled to establish its legitimacy. 新政府努力建立其合法性。|Synonyms: legality, validity, lawfulness|Antonyms: illegality, invalidity, unlawfulness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin legitimatus, past participle of legitimare "to make lawful," from Latin legitimus "lawful," from lex (genitive legis) "law."
