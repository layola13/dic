distrait:/dɪˈstreɪt/| adj.|心不在焉的，恍惚的|Inattentive or preoccupied because one's mind is elsewhere.|She gave a distraite wave and wandered off. 她心不在焉地挥了挥手，然后走开了。|近义词：absent-minded, preoccupied, inattentive|反义词：attentive, focused, alert|词性:形容词
Etymology: From French *distrait*, past participle of *distraire*, "to distract," from Latin *distractus*, past participle of *distrahere*, "to pull apart, divide, distract."  The Latin is formed from *dis-*, "apart, asunder," + *trahere*, "to draw, pull."
