admiration:/ˌædməˈreɪʃn/ | n. | 钦佩；赞赏；羡慕 | A feeling of respect and approval. | She looked at him with admiration. 她钦佩地看着他。

I have a deep admiration for her work. 我非常钦佩她的工作。 | 近义词: respect, esteem, appreciation, regard | 反义词: contempt, disdain, scorn | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *admiratio, from Latin admirari "to wonder at," from ad- "to" + mirari "to wonder."

USAGE->
With preposition "for":
I have a great admiration for his courage. = 我非常钦佩他的勇气。
He expressed his admiration for her talent. = 他表达了对她才华的钦佩。

With verb "express/feel/have":
She felt a surge of admiration for him. = 她对他感到一阵钦佩。
They expressed their admiration for the artist's skill. = 他们表达了对艺术家技艺的钦佩。

With adjective "deep/great/profound":
My admiration for her is profound. = 我对她非常钦佩。
He has a deep admiration for his mentor. = 他对他的导师非常钦佩。

In a sentence with "object of admiration":
He was the object of everyone's admiration. = 他是每个人钦佩的对象。
The painting was an object of admiration for art lovers. = 这幅画是艺术爱好者钦佩的对象。


PHRASE->
with admiration = 钦佩地
in admiration = 钦佩地
object of admiration = 钦佩的对象
admiration for = 对...的钦佩
filled with admiration = 充满钦佩
