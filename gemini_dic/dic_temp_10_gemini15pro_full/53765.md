discomforted: /dɪsˈkʌmfərtɪd/ | adj. | 不安的，不舒服的，尴尬的 | Feeling uneasy, embarrassed, or slightly ashamed. |  I was discomfited by his stare. 我被他盯得不舒服。| synonyms: uneasy, embarrassed, awkward, flustered, perturbed, disconcerted | antonyms: comfortable, at ease, relaxed, composed, confident | adjective

discomfort: /dɪsˈkʌmfərt/ | n. | 不适，不安 | A feeling of slight pain or physical unease.  | The tight shoes caused him considerable discomfort.  紧的鞋子让他感到相当不舒服。| synonyms: unease, pain, distress, soreness | antonyms: comfort, ease, relief | noun

discomfort: /dɪsˈkʌmfərt/ | v. | 使不安，使不舒服 | Make (someone) feel uneasy or embarrassed. |  The unexpected question discomfited her. 突如其来的问题让她感到不安。| synonyms:  unease, embarrass, disturb, perturb, disconcert  | antonyms: comfort, soothe, reassure | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French desconforter, from des- (expressing negation) + conforter “to comfort,” from Late Latin confortare, from Latin com- (together) + fortis (strong).  The meaning shifted from "to discourage" to "to make uneasy" over time.
