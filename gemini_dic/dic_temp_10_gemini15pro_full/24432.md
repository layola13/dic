bristle:/ˈbrɪsəl/ (US), /ˈbrɪsl/ (UK)| n. v.|刚毛，鬃毛；(刷子等的)毛；竖起；发怒|A short, stiff hair, typically one of those on an animal's skin or a man's face, or forming a brush.|The cat's fur bristled with fear. 猫的毛因恐惧而竖了起来。|近义词：hair, quill, spine, prickle|反义词:smoothness, softness|词性:名词

bristle:/ˈbrɪsəl/ (US), /ˈbrɪsl/ (UK)| v.|竖起；发怒；充满|To react defensively, typically by stiffening one's body and raising one's hair, or to be covered with bristles.|He bristled at the suggestion that he was wrong. 他对认为他错了的建议感到愤怒。|近义词：react angrily, bridle, take offense|反义词:calm, relax|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bristel, *bristle, from Old English *byrst, *brist (“bristle”), from Proto-Germanic *burstiz (“bristle”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰars- (“point, bristle”). Cognate with Dutch borstel (“brush”), German Borste (“bristle”), Old Norse burst (“bristle”).
