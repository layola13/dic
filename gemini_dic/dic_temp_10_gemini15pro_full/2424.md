adjourns: /əˈdʒɜːrnz/ (UK) /əˈdʒɜːrnz/ (US)
verb
休会，延期
To formally end a meeting, legal case, or gathering until a later time.
The judge adjourned the trial until the following Monday. 法官将审判延期至下周一。

The meeting adjourned at 5 p.m. 会议在下午5点休会。

近义词: postpone, suspend, recess, defer
反义词: convene, commence, begin
词性: verb

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The court adjourns at 4 pm every day. = 法院每天下午4点休庭。
The committee adjourns its meetings until further notice. = 委员会休会，直至另行通知。

一般过去时:
The judge adjourned the case due to lack of evidence. = 由于缺乏证据，法官休庭了。
The meeting adjourned early because everyone was tired. = 会议提前结束，因为大家都累了。

现在完成时:
The Senate has adjourned for the holidays. = 参议院因假期休会。
The conference has adjourned after three days of intense discussions. = 经过三天的激烈讨论，会议休会了。

过去完成时:
The meeting had adjourned before I arrived. = 我到达之前会议已经休会了。
The trial had adjourned until the witness could be located. = 审判已经休会，直到找到证人为止。

将来时:
The chairman will adjourn the meeting at noon. = 主席将在中午休会。
The committee will adjourn until next month. = 委员会将休会到下个月。

虚拟语气:
If the weather were better, the picnic would not adjourn. = 如果天气好一点，野餐就不会延期。
I wish they would adjourn the meeting; I'm tired. = 我希望他们能休会；我累了。


被动语态:
The meeting was adjourned due to bad weather. = 由于恶劣天气，会议休会了。
The case is being adjourned until next week. = 此案将延期至下周审理。

疑问句:
When will the meeting adjourn? = 会议什么时候休会？
Why was the trial adjourned? = 为什么休庭？

否定句:
The meeting did not adjourn until late in the evening. = 会议直到深夜才休会。
The judge will not adjourn the case without a valid reason. = 法官不会无故休庭。
