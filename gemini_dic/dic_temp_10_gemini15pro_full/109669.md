leucomaine:/ˌluːkəˈmeɪn/ | n. |白细胞胺，动物碱|A nitrogenous organic base or alkaloid formed as a normal product of tissue metabolism in animals and extracted from living tissues.  It is distinguished from ptomaines, which are formed by the action of putrefactive bacteria. |Leucomaine, such as creatine, is found in muscle tissue. 白细胞胺，例如肌酸，存在于肌肉组织中。|Leucomaines play a vital role in cellular function. 白细胞胺在细胞功能中起着至关重要的作用。|Synonym:  alkaloid (in a broader sense), nitrogenous base|Antonym:  ptomaine (in a historical context, as they were once contrasted)|noun
