fractiously:/ˈfrækʃəsli/ | adv. | 易怒地，暴躁地 | In a quarrelsome or irritable manner; readily given to ill temper. | The children were behaving fractiously due to lack of sleep. 孩子们因为缺乏睡眠而表现得烦躁不安。 | The team argued fractiously about which strategy to pursue. 团队就采取哪种策略进行了激烈的争论。 | 近义词:irritably, peevishly, crossly, petulantly, cantankerously | 反义词:agreeably, amiably, pleasantly, peaceably, harmoniously | 词性:副词

fractious:/ˈfrækʃəs/ | adj. | 易怒的，暴躁的 | (typically of children) irritable and quarrelsome. | The fractious child refused to eat his vegetables.  这个暴躁的孩子拒绝吃蔬菜。 |  Negotiations became fractious as both sides refused to compromise. 谈判变得剑拔弩张，因为双方都拒绝妥协。 | 近义词:irritable, quarrelsome, grumpy, bad-tempered, irascible | 反义词:agreeable, amiable, pleasant, good-natured, docile | 词性:形容词

词源: From Latin *fractiōsus, from fractus (“broken”), past participle of frangere (“to break”).  源自拉丁语 *fractiōsus，来自 fractus（“破碎的”），frangere（“打破”）的过去分词。
