paleoindian:/ˌpeɪlioʊˈɪndiən/ | n. | 古印第安人 |  A member of the earliest peoples inhabiting the Americas, typically associated with the Clovis and Folsom cultures and believed to have migrated from Asia during the late Pleistocene epoch. |  Paleoindians were skilled hunters of large game like mammoths. 古印第安人擅长猎杀猛犸象等大型猎物。|  Synonyms: early American, first American | Antonyms: modern human, contemporary human | Noun
