excern:/ɪkˈsɜːrn/ | v. | 筛选；选取；摘录 | To separate or extract (a specific substance) from a complex mixture or source. |  He excerned several key passages from the book for his essay. 他从书中摘录了几段关键段落用于他的论文。|  She excerned the most important information from the report. 她从报告中提取了最重要的信息。| 近义词: extract, cull, select, glean | 反义词: include, insert, add | 词性: 动词

词源: From Latin excernere "to sift out, separate," from ex- "out" + cernere "to separate, distinguish."
