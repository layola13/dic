nickeling-and-diming:/ˈnɪkəlɪŋ ənd ˈdaɪmɪŋ/| n./ v. |斤斤计较；为小钱斤斤计较；小额收费不断；积少成多|Charging small amounts of money repeatedly, often seen as unfair or annoying.  The practice of charging small amounts of money for things that used to be free, thus accumulating a significant amount of revenue.  Can also be used as a verb, meaning to charge someone in this way.|

例句：
The airline is nickeling-and-diming its customers with fees for checked bags and even seat selection.
这家航空公司对托运行李甚至选座都收取费用，斤斤计较。

The hotel nickels and dimes you for everything, from internet access to bottled water.
这家酒店对所有东西都斤斤计较，从互联网接入到瓶装水都要收费。


近义词：chiseling, gouging, profiteering, exploiting|
反义词: generous, magnanimous|
词性: noun, verb (gerund or present participle)|


词源:  This expression originated in the United States in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, when a nickel (5 cents) and a dime (10 cents) were small denominations of currency.  It refers to the practice of charging small, seemingly insignificant amounts for goods or services, which can add up over time to a substantial sum. |
