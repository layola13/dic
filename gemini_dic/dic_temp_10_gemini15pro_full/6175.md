The word "amise" is archaic and rarely used in modern English.  It's more likely to be encountered in older literature.

amise:/əˈmɪs/| adj.| 错误的，不正确的；不和谐的，不友好的 | amiss means wrong, faulty, or improper. It can also describe something that is unfriendly or hostile. |  The whole affair had gone terribly amiss. 整个事情都出了大错。| synonyms: awry, wrong, faulty, improper, erroneous, inaccurate, incorrect, flawed, defective, unsound, out of order, out of kilter, haywire, astray, off the mark, wide of the mark, off target; informal out, screwed up, cock-up, fouled up, messed up, mucked up; archaic a-miss. antonyms: right, correct, proper | adjective
