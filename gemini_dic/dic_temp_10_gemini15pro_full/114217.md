maieutic:/mɑɪˈjuːtɪk/ | adj. | 助产的；苏格拉底式的 | Relating to the method of Socrates, who used questions and discussion to elicit knowledge from his students, as a midwife helps to deliver a baby. |  Socrates' maieutic method of questioning is still used in education today. 苏格拉底的助产式提问法至今仍在教育中使用。|  Socratic, dialectical | didactic, dogmatic | adjective

maieutics:/mɑɪˈjuːtɪks/ | n. | 助产术；苏格拉底式问答法 | The art or method of eliciting knowledge from a person by interrogation and discussion, resembling the way a midwife helps to deliver a baby.  | The professor employed maieutics to guide the students to their own conclusions. 教授运用苏格拉底式问答法引导学生得出自己的结论。| Socratic method, dialectic | lecture, indoctrination | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek μαίευτικός (maieutikós, “of midwifery”), from μαία (maîa, “midwife”). Related to the verb μαιεύω (maieúō, “to act as midwife”).  The philosophical sense comes from Socrates' mother Phaenarete being a midwife, and Socrates comparing his method of questioning to her profession.
