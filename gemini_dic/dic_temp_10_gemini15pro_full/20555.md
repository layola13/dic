blackamoor:/ˌblækəˈmʊr/| n.|黑人（贬义，已废弃）|A dark-skinned person, especially a Black African or Moor.  This term is now considered offensive and outdated due to its historical association with slavery and racist caricatures.|

例句：
In older literature, you might encounter the term "blackamoor," but it's crucial to understand its offensive connotations.
在一些旧文学作品中，你可能会遇到“blackamoor”这个词，但理解它带有冒犯性的含义至关重要。

The use of "blackamoor" in modern context is highly inappropriate and perpetuates harmful stereotypes.
在现代语境中使用“blackamoor”非常不恰当，并且会延续有害的刻板印象。

反义词: N/A (There is no direct antonym for a racial slur.)


词性: Noun


词源: From Middle English blakemor, from Old English blæc "black" + mor "Moor."  The term "Moor" originally referred to the Muslim inhabitants of North Africa and the Iberian Peninsula.  Over time, the term "blackamoor" became associated with any dark-skinned person, particularly those of African descent, and was used in a derogatory manner.
