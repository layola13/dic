leucocyte: /ˈluːkəsaɪt/ (US) /ˈljuːkəsaɪt/ (UK)| n. |白细胞，白血球|A type of blood cell that is made in the bone marrow and found in the blood and lymph tissue. White blood cells are part of the body's immune system. They help the body fight infection and other diseases.|A high leucocyte count can indicate infection. 白细胞计数高可能表明感染。|Synonyms: white blood cell, white corpuscle|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
