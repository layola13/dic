mean:/miːn/
adjective
吝啬的，刻薄的;卑鄙的;出色的;平均的
not generous; unkind or unpleasant; (informal) very good or skillful; average
He's too mean to buy her a birthday present. 他太吝啬了，不肯给她买生日礼物。
That was a mean thing to say!  那样说太刻薄了！
He's a mean tennis player. 他是一名出色的网球运动员。
The mean temperature in July is 26°C. 七月份的平均气温是26摄氏度。
Synonyms: ungenerous, unkind, nasty, selfish, cruel, excellent, skillful, average
Antonyms: generous, kind, nice, unselfish, average (depending on context)
词性: 形容词


mean:/miːn/
verb
意思是;打算;导致;意味着
to express or represent something such as an idea, thought, or fact; to have a particular result; to have something as a purpose or intention; to have a particular meaning
What does this word mean?  这个词是什么意思？
I didn't mean to upset you. 我不是故意要让你难过的。
Heavy rain means flooded roads.  暴雨意味着道路会被淹没。
This could mean the end of our plans. 这可能意味着我们计划的结束。
Synonyms: signify, represent, intend, plan, result in, lead to
Antonyms: (often context-dependent, no single perfect antonym)
词性: 动词


mean:/miːn/
noun
平均值;中间;平均数
the average of a set of numbers; a method or course of action that is not extreme
Calculate the mean of these figures. 计算这些数字的平均值。
He's a man of mean stature. 他中等身材。
The golden mean is the best approach. 中庸之道是最好的方法。
There is no mean between these two extremes. 在这两个极端之间没有中间立场。
Synonyms: average, median, middle ground, moderation
Antonyms: extreme, excess
词性: 名词
