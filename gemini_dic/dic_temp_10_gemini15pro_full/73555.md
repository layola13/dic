forestalled:/fɔːrˈstɔːld/ | v. | 预先阻止，先发制人 | to prevent something from happening by acting first |  The company forestalled a hostile takeover by merging with a friendly partner.  该公司通过与友好伙伴合并来阻止恶意收购。 | anticipated, prevented, preempted | allowed, permitted | verb

forestalled:/fɔːrˈstɔːld/ | adj. | 被预先阻止的 | prevented from happening by advance action | Their attempt to seize power was forestalled by the government's quick action. 他们的夺权企图被政府的迅速行动阻止了。 | thwarted, blocked |  unimpeded | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *forstallen, from Old English forsteallan (“to intercept, anticipate, forestall”), from for- (“before, in front”) + steallan (“to steal, leap”).
