frontbenchers:/ˈfrʌntˌbɛntʃərz/ | n. | 前座议员（通常指执政党或反对党的主要成员） | Members of Parliament who sit on the front benches in the House of Commons or other legislatures, typically senior members of the governing party or opposition. |  The frontbenchers engaged in a heated debate about the new policy. 前座议员就新政策进行了激烈的辩论。 | The Prime Minister reshuffled the frontbenchers. 首相改组了前排议员。 |  ministers, shadow ministers, senior MPs | backbenchers | noun
