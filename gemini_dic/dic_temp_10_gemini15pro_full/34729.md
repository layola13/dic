click:/klɪk/| n. v.|点击，咔嗒声，一下子变得明白|A click is a short, sharp sound.  The mouse made a click when I pressed the button. /  I clicked on the link to open the webpage.  /  Suddenly, everything clicked and I understood the problem. |例句：The photographer waited for the perfect moment and then clicked the shutter. 摄影师等待最佳时机，然后按下快门。|近义词：snap, tick, press, choose, select|反义词:unclick|n. v.

click:/klɪk/|n.|点击，咔嗒声|The sudden, sharp sound made when you press something small and hard, like a button or a switch. |例句：I heard a click and the door opened. 我听到咔嗒一声，门开了。|近义词：snap, tick|反义词: - |n.


click:/klɪk/|n.|一下子变得明白|A moment of sudden understanding. |例句：Suddenly it clicked – she was the one who had stolen the money! 突然间一切都明白了——是她偷了钱！|近义词：realization, comprehension|反义词: confusion|n.


click:/klɪk/|v.|点击|To press a button on a computer mouse. |例句：Click on the icon to open the program. 点击图标打开程序。|近义词：press, select|反义词: unclick |v.


click:/klɪk/|v.|咔嗒作响|To make a short, sharp sound. |例句：The lock clicked shut. 锁咔嗒一声关上了。|近义词：snap, tick|反义词: - |v.


Etymology:  From Middle Low German *klicken, of imitative origin.
