off-shore: /ˌɒfˈʃɔːr/ (UK), /ˌɔːfˈʃɔːr/ (US)
adjective
离岸的，近海的
Situated or operating at some distance from the shore.
Example:  Off-shore oil rigs are a common sight in the North Sea. 北海的近海石油钻井平台很常见。

Example: The company registered its business in an off-shore tax haven. 这家公司在一个离岸避税天堂注册了业务。
近义词: coastal, maritime
反义词: onshore, inland
词性: adjective


off-shore: /ˌɒfˈʃɔːr/ (UK), /ˌɔːfˈʃɔːr/ (US)
adverb
离岸地，近海地
At some distance from the shore.
Example: They sailed off-shore to avoid the strong currents near the coast. 他们驶向近海以避开海岸附近的强流。

Example:  The wind was blowing off-shore, carrying the sound of the waves out to sea. 风吹向近海，把海浪的声音带到海上。
近义词: seaward
反义词: inshore, landward
词性: adverb


off-shore: /ˌɒfˈʃɔːr/ (UK), /ˌɔːfˈʃɔːr/ (US)
noun
近海区域
The region of the sea relatively close to the shore.
Example:  The off-shore is rich in marine life. 近海地区海洋生物丰富。

Example:  Fishing in the off-shore is regulated by the government. 政府对近海捕捞进行监管。
近义词: coastal waters
反义词: inland
词性: noun



词源:  'off' + 'shore'. 'Off' meaning away from and 'shore' meaning the land bordering a body of water.
