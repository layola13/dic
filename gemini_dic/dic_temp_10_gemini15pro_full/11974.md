arterioscleroses:/ɑːrˌtɪəriəʊskləˈroʊsiːz/ | n. | 动脉硬化 |A group of diseases characterized by thickening of the artery walls, resulting in a loss of elasticity and reduced blood flow.  It is a chronic disease and a major risk factor for heart attack, stroke, and other cardiovascular problems. | Arterioscleroses can lead to serious health complications, including heart attack and stroke. 动脉硬化会导致严重的健康并发症，包括心脏病和中风。| Synonyms: hardening of the arteries | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a disease process, but perhaps "vascular health") | Noun
