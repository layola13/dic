ETYMOLOGY ->
From Late Middle English *confeccionarie*, from Old French confectionarie (“confectionery, sweetmeat”), from Medieval Latin *confectionaria*, from Latin *confectio* (“preparation, making ready; composition, mixture; a sweetmeat, confection”) + *-aria* (suffix forming nouns denoting places or things related to the root).
