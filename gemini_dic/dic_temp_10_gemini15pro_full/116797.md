mawkish:/ˈmɔːkɪʃ/ | adj. | 多愁善感的；自作多情的；令人作呕的 | Excessively sentimental to the point of being sickening or unpleasant.  Characterized by sickly sentimentality. | The movie was so mawkish I almost threw up.  这部电影太伤感了，我差点吐了。| Near Synonyms: sentimental, saccharine, cloying, maudlin | Antonyms: unsentimental, stoic, dry | adjective

mawkish:/ˈmɔːkɪʃ/ | adj. | 令人厌恶的；令人作呕的 | Causing disgust or nausea, especially by being overly sweet or sentimental. |  He gave a mawkish speech about his dead dog. 他就他死去的狗发表了一通令人厌恶的感伤演讲。| Near Synonyms: nauseating, sickening, repulsive, distasteful | Antonyms: appealing, pleasant, agreeable | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mawke, alteration of *malke (“maggot”), from Old Norse mathkr (“maggot”). The original sense was "sickly sweet, nauseating", as from a maggot-infested wound.  The sense later shifted to "excessively sentimental."
