outslandered:/ˌaʊtˈslændərd/
|v. (past participle)|被诽谤的，被诋毁的|Having been falsely and maliciously spoken of; defamed.|He felt outslandered by the accusations in the newspaper. 他觉得报纸上的指控是对他的诽谤。|近义词：defamed, vilified, slandered, calumniated|反义词：praised, lauded, commended|词性：动词(过去分词)

outslander:/ˈaʊtˌlændər/
|n.|外地人，外国人|A person from a foreign land or a different region.|She was treated like an outlander in her new town. 在她的新城镇，她被当作外地人对待。|近义词：foreigner, stranger, alien|反义词：native, local|词性：名词

词源：
"outslander" comes from the Old Norse "útlandi", meaning "foreign land".  "outslander"最初指来自外国或不同地区的人。  "slander" comes from Old French "esclandre", meaning "scandal".  "slander"指口头诽谤。 "outslander"作为动词，是"slander"的强化形式，强调被诽谤的严重程度。
