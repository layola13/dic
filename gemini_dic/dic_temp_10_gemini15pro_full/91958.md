hypermoderns:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɑːdərnz/ | n. | 超现代主义者；极端现代主义者 | People who adhere to hypermodern principles, especially in art, architecture, or literature, characterized by a self-conscious rejection of traditional styles and values and an emphasis on extreme innovation and experimentation. | The hypermoderns of the 1920s embraced new technologies and materials in their architecture.  20世纪20年代的超现代主义者在他们的建筑中采用了新技术和新材料。| Synonyms: avant-garde, ultramodern | Antonyms: traditionalists, conservatives | noun

hypermodern:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɑːdərn/ | adj. | 超现代的；极端现代的 | Excessively or extremely modern; characterized by a radical departure from traditional styles and values. |  The hypermodern design of the building clashed with the surrounding historical architecture. 这座建筑的超现代设计与周围的历史建筑格格不入。| Synonyms: avant-garde, ultramodern | Antonyms: traditional, conventional | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- (meaning "over, beyond, excessive") + modern.  The prefix "hyper-" comes from the Greek "hyper," meaning "over, above." "Modern" comes from the Late Latin "modernus," meaning "of the present mode or fashion."
