heavy-handedness:/ˌhɛviˈhændɪdnəs/ | n. |笨拙，粗暴，高压|The government's heavy-handedness in dealing with the protests led to further unrest. 政府在处理抗议活动时的粗暴行径导致了进一步的动乱。|  Clumsiness, brutality, oppressiveness | Leniency, tact, subtlety | noun
heavy-handed:/ˌhɛviˈhændɪd/ | adj. |笨拙的，粗暴的，高压的|The police were accused of being heavy-handed in their treatment of the suspects. 警方被指控在对待嫌疑人时手段粗暴。|Clumsy, brutal, oppressive | Gentle, tactful, subtle | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From "heavy-handed" + "-ness".  "Heavy-handed" literally means having a heavy hand, implying clumsiness or lack of finesse.  Figuratively, it refers to using excessive force or authority.  "-ness" is a suffix that forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
