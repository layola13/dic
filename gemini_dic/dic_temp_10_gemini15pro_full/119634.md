middle-classnesses:/ˈmɪdl ˈklæsɪznɪsɪz/ |n.| 中产阶级性，中产阶级特质 |The increasing middle-classnesses of the suburbs is reflected in the rising property values. 郊区日益增长的中产阶级特质反映在不断上涨的房地产价值上。| This book explores the different middle-classnesses that have emerged in recent decades. 这本书探讨了近几十年来出现不同的中产阶级特质。 |近义词: bourgeois characteristics, middle-class values |反义词: working-class characteristics, lower-class values|词性: 名词


Etymology:
middle + class + -ness + -es
"middle" (adj.)  Old English middel "equidistant from the extremes," from Proto-Germanic *midlaz (source also of Old Saxon middil, Old Norse miðr, Old Frisian middel, Dutch middel, German mittel, Gothic midjis), from PIE root *medhyo- "middle."
"class" (n.) late 14c., "rank, order, grade, category of persons or things," from Old French classe "category, class, order" (12c.), from Latin classis "a division of the Roman people; a summoning; a fleet," of unknown origin.
"-ness" Old English -nes(s), -nis(s), -nys(s), forming abstract nouns from adjectives and participles, from Proto-Germanic *-inassu-, *-inussu-, *-anassu- (source also of Old Saxon and Old Frisian -nisse, Old High German -nissa, -nessi, German -nis, Dutch -nis, Gothic -inassus), from PIE *-nesso-.
"-es" plural suffix
