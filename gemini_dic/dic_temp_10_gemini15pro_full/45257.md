cultists:/ˈkʌltɪsts/| n.|信徒，狂热追随者|A person who is a member of a cult.  A cult is usually understood as a relatively small group of people having religious beliefs or practices regarded by others as strange or sinister.|The cultists gathered in the woods for a secret ceremony.|近义词：followers, devotees, fanatics|反义词:skeptics, doubters|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From cult + -ist.  "Cult" ultimately comes from the Latin word "cultus," meaning "care, cultivation, worship."
