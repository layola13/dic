holdup:/ˈhoʊldʌp/ (US) /ˈhəʊldʌp/ (UK)| n.|抢劫; 阻碍; 耽搁|A holdup is a robbery, especially one in which the robber uses a gun. A holdup can also refer to a delay or something that causes a delay.|例句：The bank holdup made headlines.  银行抢劫案成了头条新闻。

例句：There was a holdup on the freeway due to an accident.  由于发生事故，高速公路上出现交通阻塞。|近义词：robbery, delay, obstruction|反义词:advancement, progress|词性:名词


holdup:/ˈhoʊldʌp/ (US) /ˈhəʊldʌp/ (UK)| v.| 延迟，阻碍 | To holdup something means to delay or obstruct it.|例句：The construction work held up traffic for hours. 建筑施工延误了几个小时的交通。

例句：The negotiations were held up by disagreements over funding.  由于资金方面的分歧，谈判被搁置了。|近义词：delay, obstruct, impede|反义词:expedite, facilitate|词性:动词
