backstop:/ˈbækˌstɒp/ (UK), /ˈbækˌstɑːp/ (US)| n. v.|支撑; 后盾; 防止倒退的保障措施;  (棒球等)挡球网;  |A backstop is something that serves as a final safeguard or support if other measures fail.  |The government introduced a financial backstop to prevent the collapse of the banking system. 政府引入了金融支持措施，以防止银行体系崩溃。|Synonyms: safeguard, support, safety net, precaution, buffer|Antonyms:  risk, danger, vulnerability|noun

backstop:/ˈbækˌstɒp/ (UK), /ˈbækˌstɑːp/ (US)| v.| 为…设置安全保障;  |To backstop something is to provide it with a safety net or safeguard. |The central bank agreed to backstop the failing institution. 中央银行同意为这家倒闭的机构提供支持。|Synonyms: support, safeguard, protect, underwrite|Antonyms: endanger, jeopardize, undermine|verb
