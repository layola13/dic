mineshaft:/ˈmaɪnˌʃæft/ | n. |矿井，竖井|A vertical or inclined passageway in a mine. | The miners descended into the dark mineshaft. 矿工们下到黑暗的矿井里。| The old mineshaft was filled with water.  旧矿井里充满了水。 | synonyms: pit, shaft, well | antonyms: surface, ground | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "mine" (a place where minerals are extracted) + "shaft" (a vertical or inclined passageway).  "Mine" comes from Old French "mine," and ultimately from Medieval Latin "mina." "Shaft" comes from Old English "sceaft."
