fanatic:/fəˈnætɪk/ | n. | 狂热者，入迷者 | A person filled with excessive and single-minded zeal, especially for a religious or political cause. |  He's a football fanatic. 他是个足球迷。 | enthusiast, zealot, devotee | critic, skeptic | noun
fanatics:/fəˈnætɪks/ | n. | 狂热者们，入迷者们 | Plural form of "fanatic," referring to a group of people filled with excessive and single-minded zeal. | The fanatics were protesting outside the government building.  那些狂热分子在政府大楼外抗议。 | enthusiasts, zealots, devotees | critics, skeptics | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *fanaticus "inspired by a deity, frenzied," from Latin fanum "temple," related to fas "divine law." Originally referred to persons inspired by a deity, hence "frenzied, mad." The sense of "excessively enthusiastic" developed later.
