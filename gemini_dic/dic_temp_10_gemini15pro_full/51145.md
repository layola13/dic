despondence:/dɪˈspɒndəns/ /dɪˈspɑːndəns/| n. |沮丧，绝望|A state of low spirits caused by loss of hope or courage.|例句：He sank into deep despondence after failing the exam. 他考试不及格后陷入了深深的沮丧。

例句：Her despondence was evident in her slumped posture and tear-stained face. 她的沮丧从她颓丧的姿势和泪流满面的脸上显而易见。|近义词：despair, hopelessness, discouragement, gloom, melancholy|反义词：hope, optimism, cheerfulness, joy|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *despondentia, from Latin despondēns, present participle of despondēre (“to lose heart, give up, despair”), from de- (“down, away”) + spondere (“to promise, engage oneself”).
