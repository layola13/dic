manoeuvre:/məˈnuːvə(r)/ (British) /məˈnuːvər/ (American)| n. v.|策略，调遣；演习；用计谋；|To skillfully guide or manipulate something; a strategic action or movement.|The army performed complex manoeuvres in the field. 军队在战场上进行了复杂的调遣。|Synonyms: tactic, strategy, maneuver (American spelling), ploy, scheme|Antonyms: inaction, inactivity|noun, verb

manoeuvrer:/məˈnuːvə(r)/ (British) /məˈnuːvər/ (American)| v.|操纵，控制；调遣；演习；用计谋|To skillfully guide or manipulate something; to perform a strategic action or movement.|She skillfully manoeuvred the car into the tight parking space. 她熟练地把车开进了狭窄的停车位。|Synonyms: manipulate, navigate, steer, guide, strategize|Antonyms: neglect, abandon|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *manœuvrer*, from Old French *manovrer* "to work by hand," from *manouvre* "manual labor," from Late Latin *manuopera* "work done by hand," from Latin *manus* "hand" + *opera* "work."
