owlism:/ˈaʊlɪzəm/| n.|夜行性；夜猫子生活方式；|A nocturnal lifestyle, like that of an owl.  The practice of staying up late at night and sleeping during the day.|He embraced owlism, finding the quiet of the night conducive to his creative work. 他接受了夜猫子生活方式，发现夜晚的宁静有助于他的创作。|近义词：noctambulism|反义词：diurnalism|词性:名词

Etymology: From "owl" + "-ism"  "Owl" comes from Old English "ūle", of Germanic origin.  "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns denoting action, practice, or condition, from French "-isme", from Latin "-ismus", from Greek "-ismos".
