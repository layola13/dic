ostracizing:/ˈɑːstrəsaɪzɪŋ/
verb
排斥；孤立；故意避开
excluding someone from a society or group
例句：
He was ostracized by his colleagues for his controversial views.
他因为有争议的观点而被同事们排斥。

They ostracized her for revealing their secrets.
他们因为她泄露了他们的秘密而孤立她。

近义词：excluding, shunning, banishing, boycotting, blackballing, snubbing
反义词：welcoming, including, embracing, accepting
词性：verb (present participle of ostracize)


ostracize:/ˈɑːstrəsaɪz/
verb
排斥；孤立；故意避开
exclude (someone) from a society or group.
例句：
She was ostracized by her classmates after she reported the cheating incident to the teacher.
她向老师报告作弊事件后被同学们排斥了。

The community ostracized the family for their unconventional lifestyle.
社区因为这个家庭不传统的生活方式而排斥他们。

近义词：exclude, shun, banish, boycott, blackball, snub
反义词：welcome, include, embrace, accept
词性：verb

词源：From Ancient Greek ostrakizein, from ostrakon ‘oyster shell, potsherd’.  In ancient Athens, ostracism was a procedure by which any citizen could be expelled from the city-state for ten years.  Citizens would write the name of the person they wished to be ostracized on a piece of pottery (ostrakon).
