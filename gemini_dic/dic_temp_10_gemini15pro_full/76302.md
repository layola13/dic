gabblers:/ˈɡæblərz/| n.|喋喋不休的人；饶舌者|People who talk excessively, especially about unimportant things.  Those who chatter or prattle.|The gabblers in the cafe made it hard to concentrate. 咖啡馆里喋喋不休的人让我很难集中注意力。|近义词：chatterboxes, gossips, prattlers|反义词: reticent people, quiet people|noun

gabble:/ˈɡæbl/| v. |喋喋不休地说；急促而不清楚地说|To talk rapidly and unclearly; to chatter.|He gabbled excitedly about his new toy. 他兴奋地絮叨着他的新玩具。|近义词：chatter, prattle, babble|反义词: articulate, enunciate|verb

gabble:/ˈɡæbl/| n. |快而含糊不清的话|Rapid, unclear speech; chatter.|I couldn't understand a word of his gabble. 我一个字也听不懂他含糊不清的话。|近义词：chatter, prattle, babble|反义词: articulation, enunciation|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gabben*, of imitative origin.  Relates to sounds of rapid, indistinct speech.
