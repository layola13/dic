monkishness:/ˈmʌŋkɪʃnəs/
|n.|僧侣气质；禁欲主义；遁世生活|The quality or state of being monkish; resembling or characteristic of a monk, especially in devotion, asceticism, or seclusion.|He embraced a life of monkishness, devoting himself to prayer and study.|近义词：asceticism, austerity, piety|反义词：worldliness, hedonism|词性:名词

Etymology:
From monk + -ishness.  "Monk" comes from Old English munuc, from Late Latin monachus, from Greek monakhos, meaning "solitary," ultimately from monos "alone."  "-ishness" is a suffix forming abstract nouns from adjectives, denoting a state or quality.
