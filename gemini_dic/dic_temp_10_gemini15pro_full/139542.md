obtrude:/əbˈtruːd/ /ɑːbˈtruːd/| v. | 突出；强加；闯入 | to force oneself or one's ideas upon another who is unwilling or uninterested; to become noticeable in an unwelcome or intrusive way |  He obtruded his opinions upon everyone. 他把自己的意见强加于每个人。| intrude, impose, push | withdraw, recede | verb

obtruding:/əbˈtruːdɪŋ/ /ɑːbˈtruːdɪŋ/| adj. | 突出的；强加的；闯入的 |  Projecting or sticking out; imposing oneself or one's ideas on others in an unwelcome way. | The obtruding branches of the tree scratched the car. 突出的树枝刮伤了汽车。| intrusive, prominent, interfering | unobtrusive, discreet, retiring | adjective

Etymology: From Latin *obtrudere*, from *ob-* (towards, against) + *trudere* (to push, thrust).
