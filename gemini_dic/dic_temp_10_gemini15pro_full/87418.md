hertz:/hɜːrts/ /hɝːts/| n.|赫兹|A unit of frequency equal to one cycle per second.  Used to measure the frequency of waves, such as sound waves or electromagnetic waves.  Named after Heinrich Rudolf Hertz, a German physicist.|The radio station broadcasts at a frequency of 98.5 megahertz.|Synonyms: cycles per second |Antonyms: N/A|noun
