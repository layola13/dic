gaily:/ˈɡeɪli/| adv.|快乐地，高兴地|in a cheerful or lighthearted way; merrily.  Derived from "gay" (happy) + "-ly" (adverb suffix).  The word's meaning has shifted over time, with "gay" now primarily referring to homosexuality.|She danced gaily around the room. 她快乐地在房间里跳舞。|Near Synonyms: merrily, cheerfully, joyfully, happily, blithely|Near Antonyms: sadly, gloomily, morosely, despondently|adverb
