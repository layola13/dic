gipsire: /ˈdʒɪpsaɪər/ | n. | 钱袋，钱包 | A pouch or bag, especially one carried at the belt, used for money or small items. | He reached into his gipsire and pulled out a few coins. 他把手伸进钱袋，掏出几枚硬币。|  Example: The merchant kept his gold coins in a gipsire tied to his belt. 商人把他的金币放在系在腰带上的钱袋里。| Synonym: purse, pouch, wallet | Antonym: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gipcier*, from Old North French *gipcière*, from Frankish *gips*, meaning "gypsum" (perhaps referring to the material of the pouch) + *-ière* (suffix forming nouns denoting containers).
