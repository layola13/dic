glacis:/ˈɡleɪsɪs/ /ˈɡlɑːsiː/ | n. |斜堤，缓坡，冰川|A gently sloping bank, especially one that slopes down from a fortification. A smooth expanse of ice; a glacier. |The castle was defended by a wide glacis. 城堡由宽阔的斜堤保护着。 The climber traversed the treacherous glacis. 登山者穿越了危险的冰川。|近义词：slope, incline, rampart, glacier|反义词:cliff, precipice|词性:名词

Etymology->
From French *glacis*, from Old French *glacier* "to slip, slide, skate," from Vulgar Latin *glaciāre*, from Latin *glacies* "ice."  The sense evolved from the idea of a smooth, slippery surface.
