forever: /fərˈɛvər/ (US), /fəˈrɛvə/ (UK) | adv. | 永远，永久地 |  For an unlimited or unending period of time. |  I will love you forever. 我会永远爱你。

She'll be remembered forever for her contributions to science. 她对科学的贡献将永远被铭记。

近义词: eternally, always, perpetually, indefinitely

反义词: never, temporarily

词性: 副词

词源：From Middle English *forevre, from Old English for ēfre (“forever, always”), from for (“for”) + ā (“ever, always”).  The Middle English and early Modern English forms reflect a variety of spellings, including foreuer, for euere, for evere, etc., before settling on the modern spelling.
