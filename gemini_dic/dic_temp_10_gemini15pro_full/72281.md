flotery:/ˈfloʊtəri/ (US), /ˈfləʊtəri/ (UK)| adj.|轻飘飘的，飘浮的|Giving a sensation of floating or lightness; unsteady or buoyant.|The medication made her feel slightly flotery and disoriented. 这种药让她感觉有点轻飘飘的，失去了方向感。|The boat felt flotery and unstable in the choppy water.  这艘船在波涛汹涌的水中感觉飘忽不定，很不稳定。| 近义词：buoyant, lightheaded, dizzy, floaty|反义词：grounded, steady, stable|词性:形容词

Etymology: From "float" + "-ery," a suffix forming nouns and adjectives denoting a place or state associated with the root word. "Float" comes from Old English "flotian," related to Old Norse "flota" and Dutch "vlotten."
