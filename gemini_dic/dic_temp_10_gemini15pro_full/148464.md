overstraining:/ˌoʊvərˈstreɪnɪŋ/ | n. |过度劳累，过度紧张| The act of straining something too much, either physically or mentally. | He suffered a muscle injury from overstraining during the marathon. 他在马拉松比赛中因过度劳累而肌肉受伤。|  Overexertion, overworking, overdoing it | Relaxation, moderation | noun


overstrain: /ˌoʊvərˈstreɪn/ | vt. |使过度劳累，使过度紧张| To strain something too much, either physically or mentally. |  Don't overstrain yourself by lifting those heavy boxes.  不要抬那些沉重的箱子，以免过度劳累。| Overtax, overburden | Underuse, relax | verb


overstrained: /ˌoʊvərˈstreɪnd/ | adj. |过度劳累的，过度紧张的| Strained too much, either physically or mentally. |  Her voice was overstrained from shouting. 她的声音因为喊叫而过度紧张。|  Exhausted, stressed, overworked | Relaxed, calm | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + strain.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much". "Strain" comes from the Old French "estraindre," meaning "to tie tightly," "constrict," or "press."
