incarnadine:/ɪnˈkɑːrnədaɪn/ /ɪnˈkɑːrnədɪn/
| adj.  vt. | 肉红色的；血红色的；使成肉红色；使成血红色 |  Of a fleshy pink or red color, tinged with blood. |  "His hands were incarnadine with blood." 他的双手沾满了鲜血，变成了血红色。 | Synonyms: blood-red, crimson, scarlet, ruby, ruddy | Antonyms: pale, white, ashen | adjective
incarnadine:/ɪnˈkɑːrnədaɪn/ /ɪnˈkɑːrnədɪn/
| vt. | 使成肉红色；使成血红色 | To make flesh-colored or blood-red. | "The setting sun incarnadined the clouds." 落日把云彩染成了血红色。 | Synonyms: redden, color, tinge, dye | Antonyms: bleach, whiten | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *incarnadinus, from incarnatus (“made flesh, incarnate”) (from in- + caro, carnis (“flesh”)) + -inus (adjective suffix).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱerh₂- (“raw flesh”).  Related to carnal.
