laureation:/ˌlɔːriˈeɪʃən/ |n.| 授予学位；桂冠仪式；荣誉|The act or ceremony of conferring an academic degree or honor, especially a doctorate.  It often involves the crowning with a laurel wreath.|The laureation ceremony was held in the university's grand hall.  大学的桂冠仪式在大学的大厅举行。|Synonyms: graduation, commencement, investiture, conferring|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider: revocation, stripping,  dishonor)|noun

laureation:/ˌlɔːriˈeɪʃən/ |n.| 授予学位；桂冠仪式；荣誉|The act of crowning with laurel; the bestowing of an honor; an academic ceremony of conferring degrees.|She attended her son's laureation ceremony with pride. 她自豪地参加了她儿子的学位授予仪式。|Synonyms:  graduation, commencement, convocation|Antonyms: (No direct antonym,  but consider:  expulsion,  dismissal)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *laureacioun*, from Late Latin *laureatio*, from Latin *laureatus* ("crowned with laurel"), past participle of *laureare* ("to crown with laurel"), from *laurus* ("laurel").
