fightable:/ˈfaɪtəbl/| adj.|可战斗的，值得一战的|Capable of being fought against; worth fighting against or for.|The enemy's position was fightable, despite their superior numbers. 敌人的阵地尽管人数众多，但还是可以一战的。|近义词：contestable, defensible, winnable|反义词:unassailable, invincible|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From fight (v.) + -able.  "fight" comes from Old English *feohtan "to fight," from Proto-Germanic *fehtanan, from Proto-Indo-European *pekt- "to comb, pluck, shear."  "-able" is a suffix meaning "capable of being," from Old French -able and directly from Latin -abilis.
