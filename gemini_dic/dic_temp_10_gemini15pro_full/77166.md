gaslighting: /ˈɡæsˌlaɪtɪŋ/
| n. |煤气灯效应；心理操纵|The act of manipulating someone by psychological means into questioning their own sanity.|She was a victim of gaslighting by her abusive partner. 她是一个被她虐待的伴侣进行心理操纵的受害者。|Example: He used gaslighting tactics to make her doubt her own memory. 他使用心理操纵策略让她怀疑自己的记忆。|Synonyms: manipulation, brainwashing, mind control|Antonyms: support, encouragement, empowerment|noun

gaslights: /ˈɡæsˌlaɪts/
| v. (third-person singular simple present gaslights, present participle gaslighting, simple past and past participle gaslighted) |对…进行心理操纵；使…怀疑自己的理智|To manipulate someone by psychological means into questioning their own sanity.|He gaslights her by pretending he doesn't remember things that happened. 他假装不记得发生过的事情来对她进行心理操纵。|Example: She suspected he was gaslighting her, making her question her own perceptions. 她怀疑他正在对她进行心理操纵，让她质疑自己的感知。|Synonyms: manipulates, brainwashes, controls|Antonyms: supports, encourages, empowers|verb
