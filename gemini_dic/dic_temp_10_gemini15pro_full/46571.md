daltonism: /ˈdɔːltənɪzəm/ | n. | 色盲，红绿色盲 |  A hereditary visual defect in which one cannot distinguish certain colors, especially red and green. Named after John Dalton, a famous chemist who had the condition. |  Daltonism affects approximately 8% of men and 0.5% of women. 色盲影响大约8%的男性和0.5%的女性。|  He was diagnosed with daltonism at a young age. 他很小的时候就被诊断出患有色盲。|Synonyms: color blindness, colour blindness | Antonyms: normal color vision | Noun
