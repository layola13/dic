glad-hand:/ˈɡlædˌhænd/| v. n.|热情地握手（尤指为得到好处）; 握手；虚情假意的欢迎|To shake hands with someone in an overly friendly and enthusiastic way, often in order to gain an advantage.  As a noun, it refers to this action or the person who does it.|He glad-handed all the potential clients at the conference. 他在会议上热情地和所有潜在客户握手。|Synonyms: schmooze, butter up,  fawn over|Antonyms: snub, ignore, cold-shoulder|verb, noun

glad-hand:/ˈɡlædˌhænd/| n.|热情握手的人；虚情假意表示欢迎的人|A person who shakes hands with others in an overly friendly and enthusiastic way, often insincerely.|He's such a glad-hand, always trying to win people over with false charm. 他真是个虚情假意的人，总是试图用虚假的魅力赢得人心。|Synonyms:  schmoozer, flatterer|Antonyms:  snob, recluse|noun
