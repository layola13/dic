cardol: /ˈkɑːrdɒl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːrdɔːl/ (US) | n. |腰果酚| An oily phenolic compound found in the shell of the cashew nut (Anacardium occidentale), causing skin irritation and used in resins, varnishes, and insecticides. | Cashew nutshell liquid contains cardol, which is a skin irritant. 腰果壳液体含有腰果酚，它是一种皮肤刺激物。|  Cardol is used in the production of certain types of polymers. 腰果酚用于生产某些类型的聚合物。 |Synonyms:  anacardic acid (related compound), cashew nutshell liquid component| Antonyms:  None (no direct opposite for a chemical compound) | noun
