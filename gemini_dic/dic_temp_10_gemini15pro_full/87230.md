heresiarchy: /ˈherɪsiˌɑːrki/ | n. | 异端统治，异端政治| A system of government or rule by heretics or those considered heretical.  A situation where unorthodox or dissenting views hold sway. | The historian argued that the brief period of iconoclasm in the Byzantine Empire was a form of heresiarchy. 拜占庭帝国短暂的圣像破坏时期是一种异端统治，历史学家如是说。

The heresiarchy imposed strict limitations on religious practices. 异端统治对宗教活动施加了严格的限制。 | Near synonyms: heterodoxy, nonconformity, dissidence | Near antonyms: orthodoxy, conformity | noun
