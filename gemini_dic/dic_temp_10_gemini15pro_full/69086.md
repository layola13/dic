faunus:/ˈfɔːnəs/| n. | 福纳斯（罗马神话中的田野、森林和畜群之神）| In Roman mythology, Faunus was a nature god, often associated with woodlands and fields, and the protector of flocks. |  Faunus was worshipped by shepherds and farmers for the fertility of their flocks and fields. 福纳斯被牧羊人和农民崇拜，以保佑他们的羊群和土地肥沃。

Faunus is often depicted with horns and goat-like features. 福纳斯通常被描绘成有角和山羊般的特征。 | 近义词: Pan (Greek mythology), Silvanus | 反义词: None | 词性: Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Faunus*, related to *favere* "to favor," and possibly connected to the god *Favonius*, the west wind, who brought spring.
