condescendency:/ˌkɒndɪˈsendənsi/ | n. | 屈尊；俯就；恩赐态度 | An attitude or behavior of patronizing superiority; it implies treating someone as if they are inferior. |  He spoke to the workers with a tone of condescendency. 他以一种屈尊俯就的语气对工人们说话。

She showed condescendency towards her less fortunate relatives. 她对不太幸运的亲戚表现出居高临下的态度。| 近义词：patronizing, superciliousness, haughtiness | 反义词：humility, respect, deference | 词性: noun

词源: Late 16th century: from French condescendance, from condescendre ‘condescend’, based on Latin con- ‘together with’ + descendere ‘descend’.
