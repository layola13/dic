overinhibited:/ˌoʊvərɪnˈhɪbɪtɪd/
| adj. |过度抑制的，过分拘谨的| Excessively restrained or held back, often due to psychological factors. Characterized by an inability to act or express oneself freely. |She was so overinhibited she could barely speak in public. 她太拘谨了，以至于几乎无法在公共场合讲话。|
|The overinhibited child sat silently in the corner. 这个过分拘谨的孩子静静地坐在角落里。|
|近义词：repressed, constrained, reserved, shy, timid|
|反义词：uninhibited, unrestrained, outgoing, extroverted|
|词性：形容词|

词源：
"Over-" (Old English *ofer) meaning "above, beyond, excessively" + "inhibit" (from Latin inhibitus, past participle of inhibere "to hold in, restrain," from in- "in" + habere "to hold").  Therefore, the combined meaning is "excessively restrained."
