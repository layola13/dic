disserve:/dɪˈsɜːrv/
verb
损害；伤害；使不利
to do harm to; to fail to serve the interests of
The new policy disserves the interests of the working class. 这项新政策损害了工人阶级的利益。
近义词: harm, hurt, injure, damage, prejudice
反义词: serve, benefit, help, aid, assist
词性: 动词

disserve:/dɪˈsɜːrv/
词源:  From dis- + serve.  "Dis-" is a prefix meaning "apart," "asunder," "away," "utterly," or having a negative or reversing force. "Serve" comes from the Latin "servire" meaning "to serve" or "be a slave." Therefore, "disserve" literally means to serve poorly or in a way that is harmful.
用法说明:  "Disserve" is a transitive verb, meaning it takes a direct object. It is often used in formal contexts and discussions of policy or social issues.
