grubby's:/ˈɡrʌbiz/| n.| 格拉比的（东西）;脏孩子的（东西）| This refers to possessions of a grubby person (child).  It's a less common form, usually replaced by "the grubby child's things" or similar. | Example:  The grubby's toys were scattered all over the floor. 脏孩子的玩具散落一地。| 近义词: dirty one's, messy one's | 反义词: clean one's, tidy one's | 词性: noun (possessive form)

grubby:/ˈɡrʌbi/| adj.| 肮脏的，邋遢的;卑鄙的，不诚实的| Covered with dirt; grimy. Also, morally questionable or dishonest.  Derived from "grub," meaning to dig in the dirt.| Example 1: The child's grubby hands reached for the cookie.  孩子脏兮兮的手伸向饼干。 Example 2: He was involved in some grubby dealings. 他参与了一些卑鄙的交易。| 近义词: dirty, filthy, soiled, unscrupulous | 反义词: clean, immaculate, honest | 词性: adjective
