backbiter: /ˈbækˌbaɪtər/ | n. | 背后说坏话的人，诽谤者 | A person who says mean or unpleasant things about someone behind their back. | He's a backbiter, always spreading rumors about his colleagues. 他是个背后说坏话的人，总是散布关于同事的谣言。 |  slanderer, gossipmonger, defamer |  supporter, advocate | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bakbiter*, equivalent to *bak* (“back”) + *biter* (“biter”).  The word "bite" in this context refers to the metaphorical act of attacking someone's reputation with words.  The concept of "back" signifies the covert and underhanded nature of the attack.
