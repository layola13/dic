jackhammer: /ˈdʒækˌhæmər/ (US), /ˈdʒækˌhæmə(r)/ (UK)| n. |手提钻,风镐|A portable power tool with a chisel point, used for breaking up hard materials such as rock or concrete.|The construction worker used a jackhammer to break up the sidewalk. 建筑工人用风镐破碎人行道。|Synonyms: pneumatic drill, concrete breaker, demolition hammer|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps trowel for building up rather than breaking down)|noun

jackhammer: /ˈdʒækˌhæmər/ (US), /ˈdʒækˌhæmə(r)/ (UK)| v. |用风镐钻|To use a jackhammer.|They jackhammered the concrete for hours. 他们用风镐钻了好几个小时的混凝土。|Synonyms: drill, break up, demolish|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps build, construct)|verb

词源: "Jackhammer" is a compound word derived from "jack," referring to a mechanical lifting device, and "hammer," reflecting the tool's percussive action.  The term likely originated in the late 19th or early 20th century with the development of pneumatic tools.
