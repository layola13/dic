ditolyl:/daɪˈtɒlaɪl/ | n. |二甲苯基，甲苯基|A hydrocarbon radical, (CH3C6H4)2, formed by removing a hydrogen atom from each of two toluene molecules.  Specifically refers to any of six isomers depending on the positions of the methyl groups on the benzene rings.| Ditolylmethane is used as a heat transfer fluid. 二甲苯基甲烷用作传热流体。|Synonyms: bitolyl | Antonyms: N/A | noun
