figpecker:/ˈfɪɡˌpɛkər/| n. |无花果啄木鸟，比喻爱挑剔、吹毛求疵的人|A figpecker is a bird that pecks figs.  Figuratively, it refers to someone who is excessively critical or finds fault in everything. |The project manager was a real figpecker, constantly finding minor issues with our work.|近义词：nitpicker, faultfinder, critic |反义词: admirer, praiser | noun

Etymology:
fig +‎ pecker.  "Fig" refers to the fruit, while "pecker" refers to a bird that pecks. The figurative meaning developed from the idea of a bird meticulously picking at figs.
