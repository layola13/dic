cable:/ˈkeɪbl/ (US), /ˈkeɪbəl/ (UK)| n. v.|电缆，缆绳，钢索；发电报|A thick wire, or a group of wires inside a rubber or plastic covering, used for carrying electricity or electronic signals; a strong thick rope made of wire or metal, used for supporting a bridge or carrying people up and down a mountain.|The cable snapped, sending the elevator plummeting. 电缆断了，电梯直坠而下。|近义词：wire, cord, rope, line|反义词：wireless|n. v.

cable:/ˈkeɪbl/ (US), /ˈkeɪbəl/ (UK)|v.|发电报|To send a message by cable.|He cabled his family to tell them he had arrived safely. 他发电报给家人，告诉他们他已经安全抵达。|近义词：telegraph, wire|反义词：-|v.
