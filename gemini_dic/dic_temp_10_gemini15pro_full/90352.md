hovelled:/ˈhɒvəld/ (UK), /ˈhɑːvəld/ (US)| v. |  (使)蜷缩；(使)挤在小屋里 |  past tense and past participle of *hovel*, meaning "to dwell in or as if in a hovel." It implies being crammed into a small, uncomfortable space. | The refugees hovelled in makeshift shelters.  难民们蜷缩在临时搭建的避难所里。

The family hovelled around the small fire for warmth. 全家挤在小火堆旁取暖。 | 近义词：crouched, huddled, squeezed, crammed | 反义词：stretched out, spread out |词性: 动词

hovel:/ˈhɒvəl/ (UK), /ˈhɑːvəl/ (US)| n. |  小屋；陋室 | A small, mean dwelling; a wretched hut. | They lived in a hovel with a dirt floor. 他们住在一个泥土地板的小屋里。

The abandoned shack was little more than a hovel.  那个废弃的小棚屋简直就是一个陋室。 | 近义词：hut, shack, shanty | 反义词：mansion, palace |词性: 名词

词源：From Middle English *hovel*, *hovil*, perhaps from Middle Dutch *hovel* (“hut, shed”), from Old Dutch *huvil*, diminutive of *hūs* (“house”). Cognate with German *Hübel* (“hillock, mound”). The verb form is a back-formation from the noun.
