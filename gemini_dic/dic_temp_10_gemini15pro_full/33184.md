chromolithograph: /ˈkrəʊməʊˈlɪθəɡrɑːf/ (US) /ˈkroʊmoʊˈlɪθəɡræf/| n. |彩色石版画 |A picture printed in colors by lithography, using a separate stone for each color. |例句：The antique shop displayed a beautiful chromolithograph of a Victorian garden. 古董店里展示着一幅美丽的维多利亚花园的彩色石版画。

例句：Chromolithographs were popular in the 19th century for their vibrant colors and detailed imagery. 彩色石版画在19世纪因其鲜艳的色彩和精细的图像而广受欢迎。|近义词：color lithograph, print, picture |反义词: N/A |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From  chromo- "color" + lithograph. "Chromo-" ultimately from Greek *chrōma  "color, complexion." Lithograph from Greek lithos "stone" + -graph "writing."
