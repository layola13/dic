dooming:/ˈduːmɪŋ/| adj.| 注定失败的，劫数难逃的；不祥的 |Condemning to ruination or death; indicating or suggesting the approach of doom. |The dooming news of the factory closure shocked the town. 工厂关闭的噩耗震惊了全镇。|近义词：fateful, disastrous, ominous, catastrophic |反义词: promising, auspicious, fortunate |词性:形容词
词源: From the verb "doom" (Old English *dōm, "judgment, law, statute"), from Proto-Germanic *dōmaz. The meaning shifted from "passing judgment" to "condemning to punishment" and eventually to "destining to an unhappy fate."
