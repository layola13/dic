networked: /ˈnɛtˌwɜːrkt/ | adj. |联网的，网络化的| Connected within a network; having a network structure. |  The company has a highly networked system for sharing information.  这家公司拥有高度网络化的信息共享系统。 | Example: Our networked computers allow us to easily share files. 我们的联网电脑使我们能够轻松共享文件。| Synonyms: interconnected, linked, integrated | Antonyms: isolated, standalone | Adjective

networked: /ˈnɛtˌwɜːrkt/ | v. |联网，建立网络（network的过去式和过去分词）| To connect within a network; to establish a network. | He networked with potential clients at the conference. 他在会议上与潜在客户建立了联系。 | Example:  She has successfully networked with many influential people in her industry.  她在自己的行业中成功地与许多有影响力的人建立了联系。| Synonyms: connect, link, join | Antonyms: disconnect, isolate | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "network" + "-ed." "Network" originates from the late 16th century, combining "net" and "work."  "Net" comes from Old English "nett,"  and "work" comes from Old English "worc,"  "weorc." The suffix "-ed" forms the past participle and past tense of verbs and also adjectives.
