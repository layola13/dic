coercionists:/koʊˈɜːrʃənɪsts/
|n.|胁迫者，强迫者|People who practice coercion; those who force others to do something against their will.|The coercionists threatened violence if their demands weren't met. 胁迫者威胁说如果他们的要求得不到满足，他们将诉诸暴力。|Synonyms: bullies, oppressors, intimidators|Antonyms: negotiators, mediators, peacemakers|Noun

coercion:/koʊˈɜːrʃən/
|n.|胁迫，强迫|The practice of persuading someone to do something by using force or threats.|He claimed the confession was obtained under coercion. 他声称供词是在胁迫下获得的。|Synonyms: force, compulsion, duress|Antonyms: persuasion, encouragement, freedom|Noun

coerce:/koʊˈɜːrs/
|v.|胁迫，强迫|To persuade (an unwilling person) to do something by using force or threats.|He was coerced into signing the confession. 他被迫签署了供词。|Synonyms: force, compel, pressure|Antonyms: persuade, encourage, allow|Verb
