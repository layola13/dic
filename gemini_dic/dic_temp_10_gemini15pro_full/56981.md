double-cross:/ˌdʌbəlˈkrɔːs/| vt. n.|欺骗，背叛；双重欺骗|To deceive or betray (someone), especially after initially offering support or cooperation.|He double-crossed his partner and stole all the money. 他欺骗了他的伙伴，偷走了所有的钱。|Synonyms: betray, deceive, cheat, backstab|Antonyms: support, assist, help|verb

double-cross:/ˌdʌbəlˈkrɔːs/| n.|欺骗，背叛行为|An act of deceiving or betraying someone.|His double-cross cost them the entire deal. 他的背叛让他们损失了整笔交易。|Synonyms: betrayal, deception, treachery|Antonyms: loyalty, fidelity|noun
