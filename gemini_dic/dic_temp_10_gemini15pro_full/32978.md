choreographical:/ˌkɔːriəˈɡræfɪkl/ | adj. | 舞蹈设计的，编舞的 | Relating to choreography; the art of creating and arranging dances. | The choreographical style of the ballet was innovative and captivating.  芭蕾舞的编舞风格新颖迷人。 | The choreographical elements of the performance were stunning.  这场演出的编舞元素令人惊叹。| 近义词:  dance, movement, compositional | 反义词: improvised, unplanned | 词性: adjective

choreography: /ˌkɔːriˈɒɡrəfi/ | n. | 编舞，舞蹈设计 | The art or practice of designing sequences of movements of physical bodies (or their depictions) in which motion, form, or both are specified. | The choreography of the musical was breathtaking. 这部音乐剧的编舞令人叹为观止。 |  She studied choreography in New York. 她在纽约学习编舞。 | 近义词: dance composition, movement design | 反义词: improvisation | 词性: noun
