ounce: /aʊns/ | n. | 盎司；少量 | A unit of weight equal to one sixteenth of a pound (avoirdupois) or one twelfth of a pound (troy).  A small amount of something. | I need an ounce of sugar for this recipe.  我需要一盎司糖来做这个食谱。 |  She added an ounce of vanilla to the batter. 她往面糊里加了一点香草精。 | Synonyms: dram, drop, bit, smidgen | Antonyms: ton, pound | noun

ounce: /aʊns/ | n. | 液盎司；少量 | A unit of liquid capacity equal to one sixteenth of a pint.  A small amount of something, especially a liquid. |  I'd like an ounce of whiskey, please.  请给我一盎司威士忌。| He doesn't have an ounce of common sense. 他一点常识都没有。| Synonyms: dram, drop, bit, smidgen | Antonyms: gallon, liter | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *unce, from Latin uncia (“twelfth part, ounce”), from Proto-Italic *ounkia, from Proto-Indo-European *h₁óyḱos (“one, single”). Cognate with Ancient Greek οὖνκος (ounkos, “hook, barb”), Old Armenian ունչ (unčʿ, “weight”), Old Irish unce (“ounce”).
