cutoff:/ˈkʌtɒf/ (UK) /ˈkʌtɔːf/ (US)| n. adj.|截止;界限;中断;截止期限|A point or level beyond which something ceases to be possible or permitted.  A termination or interruption. A short high-banked turn on a sloping road.|The application cutoff date is June 1st. (申请截止日期是6月1日。) | Synonyms: deadline, limit, termination, end | Antonyms: beginning, start, commencement | noun

cutoff:/ˈkʌtɒf/ (UK) /ˈkʌtɔːf/ (US)| adj.|截断的;切断的|Shortened by cutting; abruptly terminated.|He was wearing cutoff jeans. (他穿着截短的牛仔裤。) | Synonyms: shortened, truncated, abbreviated | Antonyms: extended, lengthened, prolonged | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb phrase "cut off," which itself originates from the Old English "of," meaning "away" or "off."  The noun form emerged in the late 19th century.
