adjectival:/ˌædʒɪkˈtaɪvəl/
adjective
形容词的；用作形容词的
Relating to or having the function of an adjective; used as an adjective.
Her adjectival phrases were quite descriptive. 她的形容词短语非常具有描述性。
The writer's adjectival style made the story come alive. 作者的形容词风格使故事栩栩如生。
近义词: descriptive, attributive, modifying
反义词: nominal
词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *adjectyval, from Late Latin adjectivālis, from Latin adjectīvus (“added, supplemental; attributive”), from adjectus, past participle of adjicere (“to add to, attach, affix”), from ad (“to”) + jacere (“to throw, cast”). Surface analysis is ad- + -jectival.
来自晚期中古英语 *adjectyval，来自晚期拉丁语 adjectivālis，来自拉丁语 adjectīvus（“添加的，补充的；定语的”），来自 adjectus，adjicere（“添加到，附加，贴上”）的过去分词，来自 ad（“到”） + jacere（“投掷，抛”）。表面分析是 ad- + -jectival。


USAGE->
Attributive position:
The adjectival phrase "bright red" modifies the noun "car."  形容词短语“鲜红色”修饰名词“汽车”。
An adjectival clause can provide more detail about a noun.  形容词从句可以提供关于名词的更多细节。

Predicative position:
The sky was deep blue. 天空是深蓝色的。
The music sounds adjectivally pleasing.  这音乐听起来很悦耳。（用作形容词地）

With linking verbs:
The flower smells sweet.  这花闻起来很香。
The food tastes delicious.  这食物尝起来很美味。

In noun phrases:
The presidential election is coming soon. 总统选举即将到来。
He delivered a powerful adjectival speech. 他发表了一场强有力的、充满形容词的演讲。
