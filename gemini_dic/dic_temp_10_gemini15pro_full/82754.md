guilefulness:/ˈɡaɪlfʊlnəs/ | n. | 狡猾, 诡诈 | The quality of being guileful; deceitful cunning; trickiness. | His guilefulness was evident in the way he manipulated the situation. 他的狡猾体现在他操纵局势的方式上。| synonyms: cunning, craftiness, slyness, deviousness, trickery | antonyms: honesty, forthrightness, sincerity, ingenuousness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From guileful + -ness.  Guile from Old French guile, from Old North French wile, from Frankish *wigila, from Proto-Germanic *wigalō ("trickery, cunning"), from *wigą ("to fight, contend, struggle").  -ness from Old English -nes, from Proto-Germanic *-nassus, *-nissijaz.
