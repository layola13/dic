cheesily:/ˈtʃiːzɪli/| adv.|俗气地，庸俗地，做作地|In a cheesy way; in a way that is excessively sentimental or clichéd.  Characterized by exaggerated emotions, often in a way that is seen as cheap or insincere.|He grinned cheesily at the camera. 他对着镜头俗气地咧嘴一笑。|Synonyms: corny, tacky, sentimental, mawkish, schmaltzy|Antonyms: sophisticated, refined, tasteful, understated|Adverb

Etymology->
From cheesy + -ly.  "Cheesy" originally referred to something of poor quality, like cheap cheese, and later evolved to describe things that are overly sentimental or clichéd.
