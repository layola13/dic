carryon:/ˈkæriɒn/ (UK), /ˈkæriˌɑːn/ (US)| n. |随身行李；喧闹，吵闹；（感情的）放纵|A small suitcase or bag that you can take with you on a plane or bus.  Also can refer to disruptive behavior or an emotional outburst. |例句：I put my carryon in the overhead compartment. 我把我的随身行李放在了头顶的行李箱里。

例句：There was such a carryon when the fire alarm went off. 火警响起时，一片混乱。|近义词：luggage, baggage (for the object); commotion, fuss (for the behavior); outburst (for the emotional expression)|反义词: (No direct antonym for the object sense) quiet, calm (for the behavior sense)|词性:noun

carry on:/ˈkæri ɒn/ (UK), /ˈkæri ɑːn/ (US)| v. |继续；进行；举止失当|To continue doing something, or to behave in an excited or silly way. |例句：Please carry on with your work. 请继续你的工作。

例句：They carried on all night at the party. 他们在派对上闹了一整夜。|近义词：continue, proceed, persist, go on; behave badly, act up|反义词：stop, cease, halt; behave well|词性:verb
