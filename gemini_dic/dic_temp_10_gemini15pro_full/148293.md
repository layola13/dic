oversoften:/ˌoʊvərˈsɔːfn/ /ˌəʊvərˈsɒfn/ | adj. | 过度软化；过度弱化 |  Pronounced with a sound that is softer than usual, especially of consonants. This typically involves changing a voiceless consonant to a voiced one, or a stop consonant to a fricative or approximant.  |  The 'k' in 'school' is often oversoftened in some dialects.  一些方言中，“school”里的“k”音经常被过度弱化。 | 近义词: lenited, weakened | 反义词: hardened, strengthened | 词性: adjective

oversoften: /ˌoʊvərˈsɔːfn/ /ˌəʊvərˈsɒfn/ | v. | (使)过度软化；(使)过度弱化 | To pronounce (a sound) in an excessively soft manner. | Some speakers oversoften their consonants to the point of being unintelligible. 一些说话者过度弱化他们的辅音，以至于难以理解。 | 近义词: lenite, weaken | 反义词: harden, strengthen | 词性: verb
