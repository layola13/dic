institutionalizes: /ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənəlaɪzɪz/
verb
制度化；使…成为制度
To establish something as a norm, custom, or practice within an organization or society, especially formally or officially.
The new law institutionalizes the rights of disabled people.  新法律将残疾人的权利制度化。
The government institutionalized a new policy on education. 政府将新的教育政策制度化。
Synonyms: establish, formalize, regularize, systematize, codify
Antonyms: abolish, discontinue, informalize
词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From institution + -al + -ize.  "Institution" comes from Latin "institutio" meaning "establishment, instruction."  "-al" is a suffix forming adjectives from nouns. "-ize" is a suffix meaning "to make or become; to subject to."
