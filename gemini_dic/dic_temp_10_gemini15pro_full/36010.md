codifiability:/ˌkoʊdɪˌfaɪəˈbɪləti/| n. |可编纂性，可编码性|The quality of being able to be organized into a systematic code or set of rules.  This refers to the ease with which something can be expressed in a clear and concise set of instructions or principles.|The codifiability of legal principles varies across jurisdictions.  不同司法管辖区的法律原则的可编纂性各不相同。|codification, systematization|ambiguity, vagueness|noun
