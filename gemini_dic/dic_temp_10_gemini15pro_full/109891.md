liaison:/liˈeɪzən/ /liˈeɪzn/| n.|联络，联系；联络人；暧昧关系，私通|A close working relationship between people or organizations.|例句：She acts as a liaison between the school and parents. 她担任学校和家长之间的联络人。

例句：The police have appointed a liaison officer to work with the community. 警方任命了一名联络官与社区合作。

近义词：contact, connection, link, intermediary, affair|反义词:separation, division|词性:noun

liaison:/liˈeɪzən/ /liˈeɪzn/| n.|联络，联系；联络人；暧昧关系，私通|Communication and cooperation between units of an organization.|例句：He served as a liaison with the trade union. 他担任与工会的联络人。

例句：Effective liaison between departments is essential. 部门间的有效联系至关重要。


近义词：communication, cooperation, coordination|反义词:conflict, discord|词性:noun

liaison:/liˈeɪzən/ /liˈeɪzn/| n.|暧昧关系，私通|A secret sexual relationship.|例句：He was having a liaison with a married woman. 他与一个已婚妇女有暧昧关系。

例句：Their liaison was the talk of the town. 他们的私通成了全城的话题。

近义词：affair, romance, intrigue|反义词:fidelity|词性:noun
