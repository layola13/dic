gaier:/ˈɡeɪər/| adj.|更快乐的，更愉快的|Happier; more cheerful. Showing or expressing joy and merriment.  More lighthearted and carefree.  |例句：The music grew gaier as the evening progressed. 随着夜幕降临，音乐变得更加欢快。

例句：She felt gaier after a good night's sleep.  好好睡了一晚后，她感觉更快乐了。|近义词：merrier, cheerier, jollier, livelier|反义词：sadder, gloomier, more somber|词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *gayere, comparative of gai (“joyful, merry”), from Old French gai, from Frankish *gahi, from Proto-Germanic *gahiz (“sudden, quick”). Cognate with Old High German gāhi (“quick, sudden”), Dutch gauw (“quick”), German jäh (“sudden, steep”). Doublet of jäh.  More at gay.
