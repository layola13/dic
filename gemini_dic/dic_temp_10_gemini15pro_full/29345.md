castles: /ˈkɑːs(ə)lz/ | n. |城堡（复数）| Large fortified buildings, typically of the medieval period, often built by royalty or nobility for defense and as a symbol of power.  The plural form of "castle". |  The king owned several castles throughout the land. 国王在全国拥有几座城堡。|  fortresses, palaces, strongholds | houses, huts| Noun
Etymology: From Old French "castel", from Latin "castellum" (diminutive of "castrum" - fortified place).
