groggy: /ˈɡrɑːɡi/ (US) /ˈɡrɒɡi/ (UK) | adj. | 昏昏沉沉的，迷糊的 | Feeling dazed, weak, and unsteady, especially from lack of sleep or drinking alcohol. | I felt groggy after the long flight.  我长途飞行后感到昏昏沉沉的。 |  Example:  I woke up feeling groggy after only five hours of sleep. 我只睡了五个小时，醒来后感到昏昏沉沉的。
Example: The medication left her feeling groggy and disoriented.  药物让她感到头晕目眩。 | Synonyms: dazed, sleepy, drowsy, lethargic, sluggish | Antonyms: alert, awake, energetic, lively, refreshed | adjective

Etymology: From "grog", a mixture of rum and water, named after Admiral Vernon (nicknamed "Old Grog" because of his grogram cloak), + "-y" (suffix forming adjectives).
