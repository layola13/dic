expropriate: /ɪkˈsproʊprieɪt/ | v. | 剥夺，征用，没收 | To take property from its owner for public use, often with compensation but sometimes without. | The government expropriated the land for the construction of a new highway. 政府征用了这块土地来修建一条新的高速公路。|  The regime expropriated the factory. 该政权没收了这家工厂。 | synonyms: seize, confiscate, commandeer, appropriate | antonyms: return, restore, give back | verb

expropriated: /ɪkˈsproʊprieɪtɪd/ | adj. | 被剥夺的，被征用的，被没收的 |  Having been deprived of property, often by the government. | The expropriated landowners were given minimal compensation. 被征收土地的地主只得到了很少的补偿。| The family felt betrayed after their ancestral home was expropriated.  这个家族在他们的祖屋被征用后感到被背叛了。 | synonyms: seized, confiscated, commandeered, appropriated | antonyms: returned, restored, given back | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *expropriatus, past participle of expropriare (“to dispossess, take away”), from ex (“out of, from”) + proprius (“one's own”).
