meaner:/ˈmiːnər/| adj.|更卑鄙的，更吝啬的，更刻薄的|Comparative form of "mean," signifying more unkind, more ungenerous, or more unpleasant.|He's even meaner than his brother. 他甚至比他哥哥更刻薄。|The weather has been meaner this year. 今年的天气更恶劣了。|近义词：worse, nastier, crueler, stingier|反义词：kinder, nicer, more generous|词性:形容词
词源: From Middle English *menen*, from Old English *gemæne* (“common, general, public, universal, shared by all, belonging to the community; familiar, intimate; of low origin, vile, base”), from Proto-Germanic *gamainiz* (“common”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱoy-mon-* (“together”).  The sense development from "common" to "low, base" to "stingy" to "cruel" happened over centuries.
