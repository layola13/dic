dreary:/ˈdrɪəri/ (US) /ˈdrɪəri/ (UK)| adj.| 沉闷的，阴郁的，令人沮丧的|Causing a feeling of sadness or gloom; bleak and dismal.|The dreary weather made me feel depressed. 阴沉的天气让我感到沮丧。|Synonyms: gloomy, dismal, bleak, dull, depressing|Antonyms: cheerful, bright, sunny, lively, exciting|adjective

dreary:/ˈdrɪəri/ (US) /ˈdrɪəri/ (UK)| adj.| 乏味的，单调的|Tedious and monotonous.|The lecture was so dreary that I almost fell asleep.  讲座太乏味了，我差点睡着了。|Synonyms: tedious, monotonous, boring, uninteresting|Antonyms: interesting, exciting, stimulating, engaging|adjective

Etymology: From Old English *dreorig, from *dreor "gore, blood," related to Old High German trurar "sad." The original sense was "bloody, cruel," later developing into "sad, mournful," and finally "gloomy, dismal."
