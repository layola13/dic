floundering:/ˈflaʊndərɪŋ/ | adj. | 挣扎的，不知所措的 | struggling clumsily or helplessly; showing or feeling confusion |  The company is floundering in the current economic climate.  这家公司在当前的经济环境下苦苦挣扎。| synonyms: struggling, thrashing, faltering | antonyms: thriving, succeeding | adjective

flounder:/ˈflaʊndər/ | v. | 挣扎，不知所措，笨拙地移动 | struggle clumsily or helplessly; show or feel confusion; move clumsily or with difficulty | He floundered around in the deep snow. 他在深雪中艰难地行走。| synonyms: struggle, thrash, stumble | antonyms: glide, flow | verb

flounder:/ˈflaʊndər/ | n. | 比目鱼 | a flatfish | We caught several flounders while fishing. 我们钓鱼时钓到了几条比目鱼。| synonyms: flatfish, sole, plaice |  | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *flyðra ("to flutter"), from Proto-Germanic *fliþrōną ("to flutter").  Cognate with Old English floterian ("to flutter").  The noun meaning "flatfish" is likely related to the verb, referring to the fish's movements.
