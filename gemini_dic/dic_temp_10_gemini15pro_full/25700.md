bunking:/ˈbʌŋkɪŋ/
verb
逃课，躲避，(非正式)睡觉
the action of staying away from school or work without permission, or sleeping somewhere temporarily, especially in a makeshift bed
Example: He was caught bunking off school. 他被抓到逃学了。

Example:  We were bunking with friends for the weekend.  我们周末和朋友们一起凑合睡。
Synonyms:  skipping (school), dodging, avoiding, sleeping, dossing (down)
Antonyms:  attending, facing, confronting
词性:  动词(gerund or present participle)

bunks:/bʌŋks/
noun
床铺(通常指上下铺)
a narrow bed that is fixed to a wall, especially on a ship or train, or one that is part of a set of bunk beds
Example: The children sleep in bunks. 孩子们睡在双层床上。

Example:  He made the bunks in the cabin.  他在船舱里整理了床铺。
Synonyms:  bed, berth, cot
Antonyms:  N/A
词性:  名词

bunk:/bʌŋk/
noun
床铺，废话，胡说
nonsense
Example:  He’s talking a load of bunk. 他在胡说八道。
Example: The movie star slept in a bunk on set.  这位电影明星在片场睡在一个铺位上。
Synonyms: bed, berth, nonsense, rubbish, baloney
Antonyms: sense, truth
词性:  名词

bunk:/bʌŋk/
verb
(非正式)睡觉，逃课
to sleep somewhere temporarily, especially in a makeshift bed
Example: Can I bunk with you tonight? 今晚我可以和你挤一张床睡吗？
Example: He bunked off work to go to the football match. 他为了去看足球赛而翘班了。
Synonyms: sleep, kip, lodge, skip, dodge
Antonyms: attend, face, confront
词性:  动词
