crossbow:/ˈkrɒsbəʊ/ (UK), /ˈkrɑːsboʊ/ (US)| n. |弩，十字弓|A medieval weapon consisting of a bow fixed transversely on a stock, having a groove for the bolt and a trigger to release it.|The hunter used a crossbow to take down the deer.|近义词：arcuballista|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "sling" as a simpler ranged weapon)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *crosbow (Modern French arbalète), from Medieval Latin balista de cornu, literally "horn crossbow," referring to the bow made of horn.  The first element is from Latin crux, meaning "cross."
