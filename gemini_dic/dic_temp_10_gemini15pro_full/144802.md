over-garrisons: /ˌoʊvərˈɡærɪsən/ | v. | 过度驻军，驻军过多 | To station or deploy more troops than necessary or appropriate in a particular location.  | The general over-garrisoned the small town, causing resentment among the local population. 将军在这个小镇上驻扎了过多的军队，引起了当地居民的不满。

The government decided to over-garrison the border in response to the increased threat. 为了应对日益增长的威胁，政府决定在边境增派驻军。 | 近义词: over-deploy, over-man | 反义词: under-garrison, under-man | 词性: 动词

Etymology: "over-" (Old English *ofer) + "garrison" (from Old French garison, "protection, defense")
