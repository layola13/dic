interdisciplinarities:/ˌɪntərˌdɪsəplɪˈnɛrɪtiz/ | n. |跨学科性，跨学科研究|The quality or fact of involving or drawing on two or more branches of learning.  It refers to the combination of two or more academic disciplines into one activity. It draws knowledge from several other fields like sociology, anthropology, psychology, economics etc. |The growing interdisciplinarities of academic research is blurring the lines between traditional fields.| 近义词: interdisciplinarity, cross-disciplinarity | 反义词: specialization, disciplinarity | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From interdisciplinary + -ities (suffix forming plural nouns).  "Interdisciplinary" itself comes from inter- (prefix meaning "between") + disciplinary (relating to a particular field of study).
