dauber:/ˈdɔːbər/ (US), /ˈdɔːbə(r)/ (UK)| n.|涂抹者，涂抹工具，拙劣的画家|A person who daubs something, especially a surface with plaster, mud, or paint.  Also, a crude or inartistic painter. |The dauber slapped another layer of mud onto the wall.  这个涂抹者在墙上又抹了一层泥浆。

The critics dismissed him as a mere dauber.  评论家认为他只是一个拙劣的画家。|近义词: plasterer, painter, smearer, artist (sometimes)|反义词: N/A|词性: Noun


daub:/dɔːb/ (US), /dɔːb/ (UK)| n. v.|涂抹，涂抹物|1. (n.) A smear or patch of something soft or sticky. 2. (v.) To coat or smear something with a soft or sticky substance.|He wiped a daub of paint from his forehead. 他擦去了额头上的油漆污渍。

She daubed some ointment on the cut. 她往伤口上涂了些药膏。|近义词: smear, smudge, plaster, apply|反义词: clean, wipe|词性: Noun, Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dauben*, from Old French *dauber*, of uncertain Germanic origin.  Possibly related to Old High German *tūban* "to be noisy, rage."
