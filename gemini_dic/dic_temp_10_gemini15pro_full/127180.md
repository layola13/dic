naturalizes:/ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪzɪz/
v.
使归化；使入籍；移植；引进；吸纳
grants citizenship to (a foreigner).  Also, to introduce (something) to a new region or environment, making it suitable to the conditions there.
The government naturalized the refugees who had fled the war-torn country. 政府将逃离饱受战争蹂躏的国家的难民归化。
The botanist successfully naturalized the exotic flower in his garden.  植物学家成功地将这种外来的花卉移植到他的花园里。
近义词:acclimatize, adapt, assimilate, integrate
反义词:alienate, expatriate
词性:verb

naturalized:/ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪzd/
adj.
归化的；入籍的；移植的；引进的
having acquired citizenship in a foreign country. Also, (of a plant or animal) introduced and established in a new region or environment.
He is a naturalized citizen of the United States. 他是美国归化公民。
The naturalized plants thrived in the local climate. 这些移植的植物在当地气候下茁壮成长。
近义词:acclimated, adapted, assimilated, integrated
反义词:alien, foreign, exotic
词性:adjective

naturalization:/ˌnætʃrələˈzeɪʃən/
n.
归化；入籍；移植；引进
the process of becoming a citizen of a country that you were not born in. Also, the introduction of a plant or animal to a new region or environment.
The refugees applied for naturalization after living in the country for five years.  难民在该国居住五年后申请入籍。
The naturalization of non-native species can have unforeseen consequences for the ecosystem.  引进非本地物种可能会对生态系统造成不可预见的后果。
近义词:acclimation, adaptation, assimilation, integration
反义词:expatriation, deportation
词性:noun


词源: From Late Latin *naturalizāre, from Latin nātūrālis (“natural”).
