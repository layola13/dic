light-footed:/ˌlaɪtˈfʊtɪd/| adj.| 轻盈的，敏捷的|Moving lightly and gracefully; nimble.|The light-footed dancer captivated the audience. 轻盈的舞者吸引了观众。|近义词：nimble, agile, graceful, spry|反义词:clumsy, awkward|adjective

Etymology->
From "light" + "foot" + "-ed".  "Light" refers to a small weight or effort. "Foot" relates to the part of the leg below the ankle. "-ed" is a suffix used to form adjectives.  Therefore, "light-footed" literally describes having light feet, implying graceful and agile movement.
