bluffed:/blʌft/ | v. | 虚张声势，吓唬；蒙骗；| To pretend, deceive, or mislead someone, especially by showing more confidence than one actually feels. | He bluffed his way through the interview, even though he knew nothing about the job.  他虽然对这份工作一无所知，但还是虚张声势地完成了面试。|  deceived, feigned, pretended | admitted, conceded, confessed | verb

bluffed:/blʌft/ | adj. | 被吓唬的，被蒙骗的 | Having been deceived or misled by a bluff. |  She felt bluffed by his confident demeanor. 她被他自信的举止吓唬住了。| deceived, fooled | aware, knowing | adjective
