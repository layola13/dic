epizootic:/ˌɛpɪzoʊˈɒtɪk/| adj. n.|动物流行病的，动物流行病|An epizootic is a disease event in which a disease is spreading rapidly through an animal population.  It is analogous to an epidemic in humans.|The epizootic outbreak of avian flu devastated the poultry industry.|Synonym: epizootic outbreak, animal epidemic|Antonym: enzootic (constantly present in a population), sporadic|adjective, noun


epizootic:/ˌɛpɪzoʊˈɒtɪk/| n.|动物流行病|An epizootic refers to a widespread outbreak of disease in animals.|The epizootic affected a large number of cattle in the region.|Synonym: animal epidemic, epizootic outbreak|Antonym: sporadic case, enzootic|noun
