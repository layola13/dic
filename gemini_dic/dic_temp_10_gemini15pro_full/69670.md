fended:/fɛndɪd/ | v. | 防御，抵挡，挡开 |  Past tense and past participle of "fend". It means to have protected oneself from harm or attack, or to have managed to cope or survive. | He fended off the blows with his arms. 他用胳膊挡开了打击。| 近义词: defended, guarded, protected, parried, resisted | 反义词: yielded, surrendered, submitted | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fenden, from Old English *fendan (to defend, protect, provide for), from Proto-Germanic *fandijaną (to strive after, try to get), from Proto-Indo-European *pent- (to tread, go, strive). Cognate with German fänden (to find), Dutch vinden (to find).  The meaning shifted from "provide for" to "defend" over time.
