ichthyocol:/ˈɪkθiəˌkɒl/ /ˈɪkθiəˌkɑːl/| n. |鱼胶，鱼鳔胶|A substance obtained from the dried swim bladders of certain fish, especially sturgeons, used as a clarifying agent for wine and beer, and in the manufacture of glues and isinglass.|The winemaker used isinglass, a form of ichthyocol, to clarify the wine.|Synonym: isinglass, fish glue|Antonym: N/A|Noun
