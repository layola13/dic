antiliterates:/ˌæntiˈlɪtərəts/
|n.|文盲反对者；扫盲工作者|Those who oppose illiteracy or who work to eliminate it. |The antiliterates worked tirelessly to educate the community. 扫盲工作者不知疲倦地教育社区民众。|Synonyms: literacy advocates, educators|Antonyms:  illiteracy proponents (this is a less common and somewhat awkward antonym)|noun


antiliterate:/ˌæntiˈlɪtərət/
|adj.|反对文盲的；致力于扫盲的|Opposed to illiteracy; working to eliminate illiteracy. |The government launched an antiliterate campaign. 政府发起了一场扫盲运动。|Synonyms: pro-literacy, anti-illiteracy|Antonyms:  pro-illiteracy (this is a less common and somewhat awkward antonym)|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "anti-" (against) + "literate" (able to read and write).  "Literate" ultimately comes from the Latin "litteratus," meaning "lettered," past participle of "litterare," meaning "to furnish with letters."
