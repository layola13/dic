nonsubstantialities:/ˌnɑːnsəbˌstænʃiˈælɪtiz/
|n.|非实质性的事物；空洞无物的东西|Lack of real or solid basis; things that lack substance or significance.|  The philosopher dismissed the arguments as mere nonsubstantialities. 哲学家认为这些论点不过是空洞无物的东西。|Near Synonyms: trivialities, frivolities, insubstantialities|Antonyms: realities, essentials, substantives|noun

nonsubstantiality:/ˌnɑːnsəbˌstænʃiˈælɪti/
|n.|非实质性；空洞无物|Lack of real or solid basis; the state of lacking substance or significance.| The nonsubstantiality of the evidence led to the case being dismissed.  证据的非实质性导致案件被驳回。|Near Synonyms: triviality, frivolity, insubstantiality|Antonyms: reality, essence, substance|noun


Etymology:
From non- (not) + substantial (having substance) + -ity (forming nouns denoting a state or quality). Ultimately from Latin substantia ("substance").
