dilettanteism:/ˌdɪləˈtænˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 业余爱好；浅尝辄止 | The superficiality of her knowledge revealed her dilettanteism in the field. 她的肤浅的知识暴露了她在这个领域的浅尝辄止。|  Dilettanteism is the practice of engaging in an activity, especially the arts, in a superficial or amateurish way, without serious commitment or dedication.  浅尝辄止是指以肤浅或业余的方式从事一项活动，尤指艺术，缺乏认真投入或奉献精神。|  His dilettanteism in music was evident in his inability to read sheet music. 他在音乐上的浅尝辄止体现在他无法识谱。 |  superficiality, amateurism, dabbling | professionalism, expertise, mastery | noun

dilettante:/ˌdɪləˈtænti/ | n. | 业余爱好者；浅尝辄止的人 | He was a dilettante in many fields, but a master of none. 他在很多领域都是个浅尝辄止的人，但没有一个精通。| A dilettante is a person who cultivates an area of interest, such as the arts, without real commitment or knowledge.  一个浅尝辄止的人是指在某个兴趣领域，例如艺术，没有真正的投入或知识，只是涉猎一下的人。 |  She considered herself a serious artist, but others saw her as a mere dilettante. 她自认为是一位严肃的艺术家，但其他人认为她只是一个浅尝辄止的爱好者。| amateur, dabbler, trifler | professional, expert, master | noun
