hyperobtrusive:/ˌhaɪpərəbˈtruːsɪv/| adj.|极其突兀的，过分显眼的，非常扎眼的|Excessively obtrusive; unduly noticeable or prominent, often in a way that is irritating or unwelcome.|例句：The hyperobtrusive advertising billboards spoiled the scenic view. 极其突兀的广告牌破坏了风景。|近义词：garish, blatant, conspicuous, intrusive|反义词：subtle, understated, inconspicuous, discreet|词性:形容词

Etymology:
hyper- (prefix): from Greek, meaning "over, beyond, excessive"
obtrusive (adjective): from Latin obtrudere "to thrust against, push in front," from ob "against" + trudere "to thrust"
