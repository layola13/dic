manichean:/ˌmæniˈkiːən/ | adj. n. | 摩尼教的；二元论的；善恶二元论者 | relating to or characterized by Manicheism, a dualistic religious system that divides the world into good and evil principles |  The novel presents a Manichean worldview, where characters are either purely good or irredeemably evil. 这部小说展现了一种摩尼教的世界观，其中人物要么纯粹善良，要么无可救药地邪恶。 |  The Manichean struggle between good and evil is a recurring theme in literature. 善与恶之间的摩尼教斗争是文学中反复出现的主题。 | 近义词: dualistic, absolutist | 反义词: nuanced, relativistic | 词性: adjective, noun

manicheism:/ˈmænɪkiːɪzəm/ | n. | 摩尼教；二元论；善恶二元论 | a dualistic religious system originating in Persia in the 3rd century AD, that divides the world into good and evil principles |  Manicheism influenced various religious and philosophical movements. 摩尼教影响了各种宗教和哲学运动。 | His philosophy reflects a form of Manicheism, seeing the world as a battleground between light and darkness. 他的哲学反映了一种摩尼教的形式，将世界视为光明与黑暗之间的战场。 | 近义词: dualism | 反义词: monism | 词性: noun

词源: From Late Latin *Manichaeus, from Greek Μανιχαῖος (Manikhaîos), from Aramaic ࡌࡀࡍࡉࡄࡀ (Māniḵāyā), from ࡌࡀࡍࡉ (Mānī) "Mani" (founder of Manichaeism) +‎ -āyā (adjective suffix).
