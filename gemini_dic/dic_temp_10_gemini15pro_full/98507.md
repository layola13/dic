instantiate: /ɪnˈstænʃieɪt/ (US) /ɪnˈstænʃɪeɪt/ (UK)| vt.|实例化，实现|To represent (an abstract concept) by a concrete example; to create a particular realization of an abstract concept.|The company decided to instantiate its new policy with a series of workshops.|近义词：realize, embody, exemplify, manifest|反义词: abstract|词性: verb

instantiate: /ɪnˈstænʃieɪt/ (US) /ɪnˈstænʃɪeɪt/ (UK)| n.|实例|An instance of something.|The red car is an instantiate of the car model.|近义词：instance, example, specimen|反义词: abstraction|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin instantiare "to press upon, insist," from Latin instans (genitive instantis), present participle of instare "to stand upon, press upon, urge, be at hand, be present," from in- "upon" + stare "to stand."  The computing sense emerged in the late 20th century.
