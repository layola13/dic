cincinnatus:/ˌsɪnsɪˈneɪtəs/| n.|辛辛那图斯|A Roman statesman and military leader of the 5th century BC, famed for his selfless devotion to his country. He twice left his farm to serve as dictator during times of crisis, and both times he returned to his plow as soon as the danger had passed. He is considered a model of civic virtue.|Example: George Washington was often compared to Cincinnatus for his willingness to relinquish power.|Synonym: patriot, hero|Antonym: tyrant, traitor|Noun
