dislike: /dɪsˈlaɪk/ (UK) /dɪsˈlaɪk/ (US)| n. vt.| 不喜欢，厌恶|A feeling of aversion or disapproval.| I dislike his attitude. 我不喜欢他的态度。|近义词：hate, detest, loathe, abhor|反义词: like, love, admire, enjoy|词性: noun, verb

dislike: /dɪsˈlaɪk/ (UK) /dɪsˈlaɪk/ (US)| vt.| 不喜欢，厌恶|To regard with aversion or disapproval.|  She dislikes being told what to do. 她不喜欢别人告诉她该做什么。|近义词：hate, detest, loathe, abhor|反义词: like, love, admire, enjoy|词性: verb

Etymology:
From dis- + like.  "Dis-" is a prefix indicating negation or opposition.  "Like" comes from Old English *līcian "be pleasing," from Proto-Germanic *likōjaną "to be suitable, fitting," from PIE root *leig- "to bind, tie."  Thus, "dislike" literally means "not to be pleasing."
