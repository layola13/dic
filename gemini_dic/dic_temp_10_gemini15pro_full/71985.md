flinchers:/ˈflɪntʃərz/| n. |畏缩者，退缩者|People who flinch; those who recoil or hesitate from something difficult, unpleasant, or dangerous.|The flinchers in the group were unwilling to take the necessary risks.  小组中那些畏缩不前的人不愿意承担必要的风险。|近义词：cowards, shirkers, quitters|反义词: daredevils, heroes|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From flinch (v.) + -er (suffix forming nouns denoting persons performing the action of the verb). Flinch comes from Old French *flenchir, related to Old French flanchir "to give way, bend," and ultimately from Frankish *flankōn, related to Old High German flankōn "to waver."
