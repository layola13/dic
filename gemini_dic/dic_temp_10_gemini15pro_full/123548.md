monozygosity:/ˌmɒnəzaɪˈɡɒsɪti/ |n.|单卵性，同卵性|The state or condition of being monozygotic (derived from a single fertilized egg).  It refers to twins or multiple births that develop from one zygote, which splits to form two or more embryos.|Monozygotic twins share 100% of their DNA. 单卵双胞胎的DNA完全相同。|Identical twinning is a result of monozygosity. 同卵双胞胎是单卵性的结果。|Near Synonyms: identical twinning, uniovularity|Antonym: dizygosity|noun
