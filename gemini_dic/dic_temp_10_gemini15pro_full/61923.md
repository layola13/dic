emulate:/ˈɛmjʊleɪt/ | v. | 模仿；效仿；仿真 | to try to be like (someone or something you admire) | She tried to emulate her older sister in everything she did. 她试图在所有事情上都模仿她的姐姐。 |  He emulated the style of his favorite author. 他模仿了他最喜欢的作家的风格。 | imitate, copy, mimic, mirror | neglect, ignore | verb

emulation:/ˌɛmjʊˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 模仿；效仿；仿真 | the act of emulating someone or something | The new software is in emulation of the popular program.  这款新软件模仿了那个流行的程序。|  Her emulation of her role model led her to a successful career. 她对榜样的效仿使她获得了成功的职业生涯。| imitation, copying, mimicry | disregard, neglect | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin aemulatus, past participle of aemulari "to rival, strive to equal," from Latin aemulus "rivaling, emulous," of uncertain origin.
