overprolixities: /ˌoʊvərprəˈlɪksɪtiz/ | n. | 过于冗长，啰嗦 |  Excessive wordiness or lengthiness of speech or writing. The state of being overprolix. | His overprolixities made it difficult to understand his point. 他的冗长使得很难理解他的观点。 | synonyms: verbosity, wordiness, prolixity, long-windedness | antonyms: conciseness, brevity, succinctness | noun

Etymology: From "over" + "prolixity."  "Prolixity" comes from Late Latin *prolixitas, from Latin prolixus "extended, long," from pro- "forth" + laxus "loose, wide."
