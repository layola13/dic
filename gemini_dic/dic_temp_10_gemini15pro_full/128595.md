nicer:/ˈnaɪsər/| adj.|更好的，更友善的|Comparative form of "nice."  Used to describe something more pleasant, agreeable, or attractive than something else.|The weather is nicer today than it was yesterday. 今天的天气比昨天好。|近义词：better, finer, pleasanter, more agreeable|反义词：worse, meaner, nastier|词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *nicer, comparative of nice, from Old French nice (meaning "foolish, simple"), from Latin nescius ("ignorant"). The meaning shifted over time from "foolish" to "finicky" to "refined" to "pleasant."
