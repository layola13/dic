catkin:/ˈkæt.kɪn/| n. |柔荑花序；柳絮|A catkin is a slim, cylindrical flower cluster, with inconspicuous or no petals, usually wind-pollinated. It is typically found on trees such as willows, birches, and hazels.|The willow tree's branches were heavy with catkins.|近义词：ament|反义词: (No direct antonym exists, as it's a specific botanical term.)|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *katteken, diminutive of katte ("kitten"), likely from a resemblance to a kitten's tail.
