moated:/ˈməʊtɪd/| adj.|有护城河的；壕沟围绕的|Surrounded by a moat (a deep, wide ditch, often filled with water, surrounding a castle, fortification, or town as a defense).|例句：The moated castle stood on a hill overlooking the valley.  那座带护城河的城堡矗立在俯瞰山谷的小山上。

例句：They lived in a grand, moated manor house.  他们住在一座宏伟的有护城河的庄园里。|近义词：protected, fortified, defended|反义词:unprotected, undefended|词性:adjective

词源:
From Middle English *motyd, from Old French moté (“moated, furnished with a moat”), past participle of moter (“to provide with a moat”), from mot (“moat”), from Vulgar Latin *muttum, *motum, of uncertain origin, perhaps from Frankish *mot (“mound, hill”). Cognate with Middle Dutch mote (“moat”), German Motte (“motte (castle)”).
