overvariation:/ˌoʊvərˌveəriˈeɪʃən/| n.|过度变异，过度变化|The phenomenon of using more linguistic forms than necessary to express a meaning, often observed in language learners.  It involves applying grammatical rules too broadly. |Example: A learner might say "goed" instead of "went," overgeneralizing the past tense "-ed" ending. |近义词:hypercorrection, overgeneralization|反义词:simplification, undergeneralization|词性:noun

Etymology:
From "over-" (meaning "excessive") + "variation" (from Latin "variatio," meaning "a changing, difference").
