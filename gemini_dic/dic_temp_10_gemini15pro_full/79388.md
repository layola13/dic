gliden:/ˈɡlaɪdən/| v. |滑行，滑翔，悄悄地走|To move smoothly and effortlessly, as if floating.|The eagle glided effortlessly through the air.  老鹰毫不费力地在空中滑翔。|近义词：soar, drift, float, slide|反义词:lumber, trudge|verb

gliden (obsolete):/ˈɡlaɪdən/| n. |滑翔，滑行|The act of gliding or moving smoothly.|The hawk's gliden was a marvel to behold. 鹰的滑翔令人叹为观止。|近义词：glide, soaring, drifting|反义词:trudge, plod|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *glīdan, from Proto-Germanic *glīdaną, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰleydʰ- (“to slip, slide”). Cognate with Dutch glijden (“to glide, slide”), German gleiten (“to glide, slide”), Old Norse glita (“to shine, glitter”).
