epiphany: /ɪˈpɪfəni/ /əˈpɪfəni/ | n. |顿悟；突然的理解；显现（特指神的显现）| A sudden, intuitive perception of or insight into the reality or essential meaning of something, usually initiated by some simple, homely, or commonplace occurrence or experience.  A revealing scene or moment. |  She had an epiphany and realized that she was wasting her life. 她顿悟了，意识到自己正在浪费生命。 | synonyms: realization, insight, revelation, inspiration, awakening | antonyms: confusion, misunderstanding, ignorance | noun

epiphany: /ɪˈpɪfəni/ /əˈpɪfəni/ | n. |主显节（纪念耶稣显现的节日）| A Christian festival, held on January 6, celebrating the manifestation of Christ to the Gentiles as represented by the Magi. |  Many churches hold special services on Epiphany. 许多教堂在主显节举行特别仪式。 | synonyms: Twelfth Day, Three Kings' Day | antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin epiphania, from Ancient Greek ἐπιφάνεια (epipháneia, “appearance, manifestation”), from ἐπιφαίνω (epiphaínō, “I show, I reveal”), from ἐπί (epí, “upon, at”) + φαίνω (phaínō, “I show”).
