curdling:/ˈkɜːrdlɪŋ/| n. v. adj.|凝固，凝结|The process of milk separating into curds and whey.  The act of making something thicken or congeal. Can also be used figuratively to describe something becoming spoiled or unpleasant. |The curdling of the milk was a sign that it had gone bad. 牛奶的凝结表明它已经变质了。|近义词：coagulating, clotting, thickening, congealing|反义词: thinning, liquefying|词性: Primarily a verb (curdle), can be used as a noun (curdling) describing the process, or as an adjective (curdling) describing something that causes curdling (e.g., a curdling agent).

curdle:/ˈkɜːrdl/| v. |凝结，凝固|To cause a liquid, especially milk, to separate into curds and whey.  Can also be used figuratively to describe something becoming spoiled or unpleasant.|The lemon juice curdled the milk. 柠檬汁使牛奶凝结了。|近义词：coagulate, clot, thicken, congeal|反义词: thin, liquefy|词性: Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *curdelen*, frequentative of *crudden*, from Old English *crudian* (“to coagulate, curdle”), from Proto-Germanic *krudōną* (“to press, crowd, coagulate”), from Proto-Indo-European *kreut-* (“to press, crowd”).  Related to "crowd" and "crud."
