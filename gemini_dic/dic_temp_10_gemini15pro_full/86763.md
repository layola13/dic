helplessnesses:/ˈhɛlplɪsnəsɪz/
n.
无助，无力，无能为力（复数）
The feeling of being unable to cope or take action.  The plural form emphasizes multiple instances or degrees of helplessness.
The refugees faced many helplessnesses due to the war. 难民由于战争面临着许多无助的境况。
The natural disasters left many people in a state of utter helplessnesses. 自然灾害使许多人处于完全无助的状态。

helplessness:/ˈhɛlplɪsnəs/
n.
无助，无力，无能为力
The feeling of being unable to cope or take action.
The child's helplessness in the face of the bully was heartbreaking. 孩子面对恶霸的无助令人心碎。
He felt a sense of helplessness as the car plunged into the river. 当汽车冲入河里时，他感到一种无助感。

helpless:/ˈhɛlplɪs/
adj.
无助的，无力的，无能为力的
Unable to cope or take action; powerless.
The injured bird was completely helpless. 受伤的小鸟完全无助。
She felt helpless to stop the argument. 她感到无力阻止这场争吵。

help:/hɛlp/
v. n.
帮助，援助
To give assistance or support to someone.
Can you help me with this problem? 你能帮我解决这个问题吗？
I need some help carrying these boxes. 我需要有人帮我搬这些箱子。


Etymology:
From Old English *helpan (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *helpaną, from Proto-Indo-European *kelb- (“to help, support”). Cognate with Dutch helpen, German helfen, Gothic 𐌷𐌹𐌻𐍀𐌰𐌽 (hilpan).


Synonyms:
powerlessness, inability, incapacity, vulnerability, defenselessness

Antonyms:
capability, competence, power, strength, control
