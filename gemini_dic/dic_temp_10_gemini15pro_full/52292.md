dichromats:/ˈdaɪkrəʊmæts/| n.|二色视者|People or animals who have dichromacy, a type of color blindness in which only two types of color receptors (cones) work, typically allowing them to see colors along a blue-yellow axis and confusing reds and greens.|Dichromats have difficulty distinguishing between red and green. 二色视者难以区分红色和绿色。|Synonyms: colorblind individuals,  |Antonyms: trichromats, tetrachromats|noun
