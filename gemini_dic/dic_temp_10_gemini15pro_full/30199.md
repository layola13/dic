celibacy: /ˈsɛləbəsi/ | n. | 独身; 禁欲; | the state of abstaining from marriage and sexual relations, typically for religious reasons. |  例句：He chose a life of celibacy. 他选择独身生活。

例句：Celibacy is a requirement for Catholic priests.  独身是天主教神父的要求。

近义词：abstinence, chastity | 反义词：marriage, promiscuity | 词性：noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *caelibatus* "single, unmarried," from *caelebs* "unmarried, single."
