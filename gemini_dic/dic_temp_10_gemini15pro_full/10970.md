arbitrate: /ˈɑːrbɪtreɪt/ (US) /ˈɑːbɪtreɪt/ (UK)| v. n.|仲裁，公断|To judge a dispute between two opposing parties.|例句：They agreed to arbitrate their dispute. 他们同意仲裁他们的争议。|例句：The union and management arbitrated the contract dispute. 工会和管理层对合同纠纷进行了仲裁。|近义词：mediate, adjudicate, judge, umpire|反义词：litigate, contest|词性: verb, noun
arbitrator: /ˈɑːrbɪtreɪtər/ (US) /ˈɑːbɪtreɪtər/ (UK)| n.|仲裁员，公断人|A person chosen to judge a dispute.|例句：The arbitrator heard both sides of the story. 仲裁员听取了双方的陈述。|例句：An independent arbitrator was appointed to settle the dispute.  一位独立的仲裁员被任命来解决这场纠纷。|近义词：mediator, judge, umpire|反义词：litigant|词性: noun
arbitration: /ˌɑːrbɪˈtreɪʃn/ (US) /ˌɑːbɪˈtreɪʃn/ (UK)| n.|仲裁，公断|The process of judging a dispute.|例句：The dispute was settled by arbitration. 争议通过仲裁解决。|例句：They opted for arbitration instead of going to court. 他们选择仲裁而不是上法庭。|近义词：mediation, adjudication|反义词：litigation|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *arbitratus*, past participle of *arbitrari* "to witness, judge, consider," from *arbiter* "witness, judge."
