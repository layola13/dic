lieutenant: /luːˈtenənt/ (US) /lɛfˈtenənt/ (UK)| n.|中尉；副官；助理|A commissioned officer in the armed forces, ranking immediately below a captain (army, marines) or below a lieutenant commander (navy).  A person who acts as a deputy or substitute for a superior.|例句：The lieutenant led his platoon into battle. 中尉带领他的排投入战斗。

例句：She served as the lieutenant to the CEO. 她担任CEO的助理。|近义词：deputy, assistant, second-in-command|反义词: commander, captain (in some contexts)|词性: noun


lieutenant: /luːˈtenənt/ (US) /lɛfˈtenənt/ (UK)| adj.|代理的；副的|Acting as a deputy or substitute.|例句：He was appointed lieutenant governor. 他被任命为副州长。

例句：She is the lieutenant manager of the store. 她是这家商店的副经理。|近义词：acting, deputy, assistant|反义词: chief, principal|词性: adjective
