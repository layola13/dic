pagan:/ˈpeɪɡən/| n. adj.|异教徒，非基督教徒；异教的，非基督教的|A person holding religious beliefs other than those of the main world religions.  Relating to or characteristic of pagans or their beliefs.|例句：He was raised a pagan. 他从小就是个异教徒。|例句：They worshipped pagan gods. 他们崇拜异教神。|近义词：heathen, infidel, gentile|反义词：Christian, believer|词性:名词，形容词

pagan:/ˈpeɪɡən/|Etymology: Late Middle English: from late Latin paganus ‘villager, rustic’, from pagus ‘country district’. The word came to denote someone who was not a Christian or Muslim because Christianity spread first in towns and cities, leaving country people the last to convert.|
