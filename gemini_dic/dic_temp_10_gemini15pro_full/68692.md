farcy:/ˈfɑːrsi/| n.| 鼻疽（马的一种慢性传染病）|A contagious and often fatal disease of horses, resembling glanders, characterized by ulcerating nodules in the skin and mucous membranes.|The horse was diagnosed with farcy and had to be euthanized.|近义词:glanders|反义词:health|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *farcin*, from Late Latin *farcimen* ("sausage, stuffing"), from Latin *farcire* ("to stuff").  The connection to the disease might be due to the sausage-like appearance of the swollen lymph vessels.
