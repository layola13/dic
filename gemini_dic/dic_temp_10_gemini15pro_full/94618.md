impregnator:/ɪmˈprɛɡneɪtər/| n. |浸渍器，浸渍剂，使充满者|A person or thing that impregnates something, typically with a substance.  Someone or something that saturates or fills something else.|The wood was treated with an impregnator to protect it from rot. 木材经过浸渍剂处理以防止腐烂。|Synonyms: saturator, infuser, imbuer, permeator|Antonyms: drainer, emptier|noun

impregnate:/ɪmˈprɛɡneɪt/| v. |使怀孕，使充满，浸渍|To make pregnant; to fill something with a substance.|The air was impregnated with the smell of pine. 空气中充满了松树的气味。|Synonyms: saturate, infuse, imbue, permeate|Antonyms: drain, empty|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *impregnatus, past participle of impregnare ("to make pregnant, to fertilize, to impregnate"), from in- ("in, into") + praegnans ("pregnant").
