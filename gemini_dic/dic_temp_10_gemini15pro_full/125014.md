mugwumpisms:/ˈmʌɡwʌmpɪzəmz/
|n.|清高派作风；独立人士的言行|The behaviors and attitudes characteristic of a mugwump, particularly concerning their political independence and reformist ideals.|His mugwumpisms alienated him from both major political parties. 他的清高派作风使他疏远了两个主要政党。|Synonyms:  independence, self-righteousness, aloofness|Antonyms: partisanship, conformity|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Mugwump + -ism. Mugwump comes from the Algonquian word *mugwomp, meaning "great man," "war leader," or "king."  It was used satirically in the late 19th century to refer to Republicans who refused to support their party's nominee, James G. Blaine, in the 1884 presidential election.
