essayism:/ˈɛseɪɪzəm/| n.|散文主义，随笔写作|A style of writing characterized by personal reflection and informal exploration of ideas, often lacking a rigid structure.|Example: His work is a prime example of essayism, blending personal anecdotes with philosophical musings. 他的作品是散文主义的典型例子，将个人轶事与哲学思考融为一体。|Example: Modern essayism often blurs the lines between fiction and non-fiction. 现代散文主义经常模糊虚构和非虚构之间的界限。|Synonym: essay writing, personal essay, reflective writing|Antonym: formal writing, academic writing|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *essai* (meaning "attempt" or "trial") + *-ism* (suffix denoting a doctrine, belief, or practice). The French word *essai* ultimately derives from the Latin *exagium* (meaning "weighing" or "act of weighing").
