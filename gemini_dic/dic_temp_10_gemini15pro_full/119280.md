micracoustic:/ˌmaɪkrəˈkuːstɪk/| adj.|微声的，微音的|Relating to sounds of low intensity, often requiring amplification to be heard.  It refers to sounds that are very faint or quiet.|The scientist used a micracoustic device to study the subtle sounds made by insects.|近义词：faint, low, quiet, subtle, soft|反义词:loud, noisy|词性:形容词
Etymology:  "micr-" from Greek "mikros" (small) + "acoustic" from Greek "akoustikos" (relating to hearing).
