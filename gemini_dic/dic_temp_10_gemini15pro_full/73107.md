for-all-intents-and-purposes:| idiom |实际上；几乎；差不多|Originated in legal language in the 16th century, initially as "to all intents, constructions, and purposes." Over time, it evolved to its current form.| The house is, for all intents and purposes, ours. 这房子实际上已经是我们的了。| Synonym: effectively, practically, virtually, essentially | Antonym: theoretically, hypothetically | adverbial phrase
