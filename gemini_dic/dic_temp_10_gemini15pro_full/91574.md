hyperacoustics:/ˌhaɪpərəˈkuːstɪks/ | n. |超声学; 超高频声学|The study of sounds with frequencies above the audible range, typically above 20 kHz. It deals with the generation, propagation, and detection of such sounds and their applications.| Researchers are exploring the use of hyperacoustics in medical imaging. 研究人员正在探索超声学在医学成像中的应用。|近义词：ultrasonics |反义词: infrasonics |词性: noun

Etymology:  "hyper-" (Greek: ὑπέρ, hupér, 'over, beyond') + "acoustics" (from Greek ἀκουστικός (akoustikos), "of or for hearing, ready to hear")
