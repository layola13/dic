flagellates:/ˈflædʒəˌleɪts/ | n. |  鞭毛虫 |A group of single-celled organisms characterized by the presence of one or more whip-like appendages called flagella, used for locomotion. |  Many flagellates are free-living in aquatic environments, while others are parasitic. | 近义词:mastigophora, protozoa | 反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider non-motile organisms or specific groups like ciliates or amoebae based on context) | 词性:noun


flagellate:/ˈflædʒəˌleɪt/ | v. | 鞭打; 鞭笞 | To whip or flog; to punish by whipping. | The master flagellated the slave. 主人鞭打了奴隶. | 近义词: whip, flog, scourge, lash | 反义词: caress, soothe | 词性:verb


flagellate:/ˈflædʒəˌleɪt/ | adj. |  鞭毛状的；有鞭毛的 | Having or resembling a flagellum. |  The flagellate bacteria moved quickly through the water.  这种有鞭毛的细菌在水中快速移动。| 近义词: flagellar | 反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly aflagellar meaning lacking flagella) | 词性:adjective
