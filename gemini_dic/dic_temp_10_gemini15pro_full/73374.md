foregrounding:/ˈfɔːrɡraʊndɪŋ/ | n. |突出，前景化|The act of making something more prominent or noticeable.  It's a literary and stylistic device used to draw attention to certain elements of a text. |The poet uses repetition and alliteration as a form of foregrounding to emphasize the theme of loss. 诗人用重复和头韵作为前景化的一种形式来强调失去的主题。

|Synonyms: emphasis, highlighting, prominence, salience|Antonyms: backgrounding, obscuring, downplaying|noun

词源：From fore- + ground + -ing.  "Fore-" signifies "in front" or "prominent," while "ground" refers to the background or context. "-ing" forms the present participle, indicating the act or process of bringing something to the foreground.
