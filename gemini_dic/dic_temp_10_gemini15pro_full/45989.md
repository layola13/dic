cyanotype:/ˈsaɪənəˌtaɪp/ | n. | 蓝晒，青写真；蓝图 |A photographic print in Prussian blue, typically created by exposing sensitized paper to sunlight.|  Example: The artist displayed a series of cyanotypes of local wildflowers. 这位艺术家展出了一系列当地野花的蓝晒作品。

Example:  She learned how to create beautiful cyanotypes in her photography class. 她在摄影课上学了如何制作漂亮的蓝晒作品。| 近义词:blueprint, ferroprussiate | 反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider other printing methods like "silver gelatin print") | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *cyano-* (Greek *κυανός (kuanos)*, "dark blue") + *-type* (Greek *τύπος (tupos)*, "impression, mark").  Coined in 1844 by Sir John Herschel.
