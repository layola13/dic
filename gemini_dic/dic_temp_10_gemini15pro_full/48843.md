deforciant:/dɪˈfɔːrsiənt/ | n. |强夺者；非法剥夺者|  A person who wrongfully keeps someone else from possessing property, especially land, by force.  This term is primarily used in legal and historical contexts.|  The deforciant was ordered by the court to return the land to its rightful owner.| 近义词: usurper, intruder | 反义词: owner, possessor | 词性: noun

词源: From Anglo-French *deforçant*, present participle of *deforcer* "to dispossess by force," from *de- "out, away" + *force* "strength."
