benches:/ˈbɛntʃɪz/| n.|长凳，工作台|A long seat for several people, typically made of wood or stone; a worktable.|The park has many benches for people to rest on. 公园里有很多长椅供人们休息。|Synonyms: seats, pews, forms|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "standing room")|Noun

benches:/ˈbɛntʃɪz/| n.|法官席，法官|The seat or position of a judge or magistrate.|The full benches of the Supreme Court heard the case. 最高法院全体法官审理了此案。|Synonyms:  judiciary, court, tribunal|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "banc" meaning "bench, bank," from Frankish *banki, from Proto-Germanic *bankiz meaning "bench, slope, mound." Related to Old English "benc" and German "Bank." The plural form "benches" is formed by adding the regular English plural suffix "-es."
