overstating: /ˌoʊvərˈsteɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 夸大；夸张；言过其实 | The company was accused of overstating its profits.  该公司被指控夸大其利润。 | Example: He was overstating the case when he said everyone supported the proposal. 他说每个人都支持这个提议，这夸大了事实。 | Synonyms: exaggerating, embellishing, amplifying | Antonyms: understating, downplaying, minimizing | Verb (present participle)

overstate: /ˌoʊvərˈsteɪt/ | v. | 夸大；夸张；言过其实 | It's difficult to overstate the importance of this discovery.  很难夸大这一发现的重要性。 | Example:  She tends to overstate her qualifications in job interviews.  她在求职面试中往往会夸大自己的资历。 | Synonyms: exaggerate, embellish, amplify | Antonyms: understate, downplay, minimize | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + state.  "Over-"  is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much."  "State" comes from the Latin word "status" meaning "condition" or "position."
