franken-/ˈfræŋkən/| adj.|弗兰肯式的，拼凑的，粗制滥造的|Combining disparate elements; monstrously created, especially in a technological or scientific context.  Often used to describe things that are perceived as unnatural or ethically questionable due to their artificial or pieced-together nature.  Derived from "Frankenstein," the name of the scientist in Mary Shelley's novel who created a monster from assembled body parts.|例句：The franken-policy is a patchwork of contradictory rules. 这项弗兰肯式的政策是由相互矛盾的规则拼凑而成的。

例句：Scientists worry about the ethical implications of franken-foods. 科学家们担心弗兰肯食品的伦理含义。|近义词：hybrid, composite,  monstrous, unnatural|反义词: natural, organic, seamless|词性: adjective
