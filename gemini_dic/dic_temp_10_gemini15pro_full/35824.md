cockle:/ˈkɒkəl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkəl/ (US)| n. |  海扇蛤，鸟蛤；(心房的)心耳；起皱，褶皱；谷壳，杂草种子 |A small, edible saltwater clam with a ribbed, heart-shaped shell. Also refers to a wrinkle or pucker, and a weed seed that contaminates grain. | We gathered cockles at low tide. 我们在退潮时收集了海扇蛤。|Synonyms: clam, shellfish, wrinkle, crease, pucker |Antonyms: smooth, unwrinkled | noun

cockle:/ˈkɒkəl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkəl/ (US)| v. | (使)起皱，(使)翘起；(使)振作 | To become or cause to become wrinkled or puckered; to warm and cheer. | The wind cockled the sails. 风使帆起了皱褶。 |Synonyms: wrinkle, pucker, crumple, cheer, warm |Antonyms: smooth, flatten, depress | verb
