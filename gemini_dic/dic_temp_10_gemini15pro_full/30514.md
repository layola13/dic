ceorl: /ˈtʃɜːrl/ | n. | 自由民，农夫 | A freeman of the lowest rank in Anglo-Saxon England, holding land and owing service to a lord. |  The ceorl worked the land and paid taxes to his lord.  自由民耕种土地并向他的领主缴税。 | peasant, commoner | nobleman, thane | noun

Etymology:
From Old English *ceorl, from Proto-Germanic *karilaz, meaning "man."  Cognate with Old Frisian *kerl, Old Saxon *charal, Old High German *karal, Old Norse karl (meaning "man, husband").
