bear-on:/ber ˈɑːn/| phrasal verb | 与…有关；对…有影响 | To have relevance or a significant effect on something. |  The new evidence bears directly on the case. 新证据与案件直接相关。| 近义词: relate to, affect, influence, impact, pertain to | 反义词: be irrelevant to | 词性: 动词短语

词源:  "Bear" in this context comes from Old English "beran," meaning "to carry, bring forth, endure."  The preposition "on" indicates direction or connection.  The phrasal verb "bear on" developed from the idea of something "carrying weight" or having an impact on something else.
