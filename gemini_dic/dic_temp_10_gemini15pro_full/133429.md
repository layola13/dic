nonfarcicalness:/ˌnɒnfɑːrˈsɪkəlnəs/ | n. | 非闹剧性；严肃性 | The quality of not being farcical; seriousness. | The nonfarcicalness of the situation made it all the more terrifying.  这种局势的严肃性使它更加可怕。 | seriousness, gravity, solemnity | frivolity, levity | noun

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  Meaning "not," from Old French non-, from Latin non "not."
farcical (adjective): From farce + -ical.  Ultimately from Late Latin *farcire "to stuff."
-ness (suffix):  Forms nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being."
