adherents: /ədˈhɪərənts/ /ədˈhɪrənts/ | n. | 信徒，追随者，拥护者 | People who support a particular belief, cause, or leader.  Those who adhere to a particular doctrine, belief, or leader. | The politician had many adherents who supported his campaign.  这位政治家有很多支持他竞选的追随者。

The new religion quickly gained adherents among the younger generation.  这个新宗教很快在年轻一代中获得了信徒。 | 近义词: followers, supporters, believers, devotees, disciples | 反义词: opponents, detractors, dissenters | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *adherent-, from Latin adhaerēns, present participle of adhaerēre (“to adhere”), from ad- (“to”) + haerēre (“to stick”).

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The cult has thousands of adherents. =  这个邪教有数千名信徒。
His adherents believe in his vision for the future. = 他的追随者相信他对未来的愿景。

一般过去时:
The movement's adherents met in secret. = 该运动的追随者秘密会面。
The king's adherents defended him fiercely. = 国王的拥护者们拼死保卫他。

现在完成时:
The party has gained many adherents in recent years. =  近年来，该党赢得了许多支持者。
The artist has attracted a large number of adherents. =  这位艺术家吸引了大批追随者。

过去完成时:
The leader had lost many adherents before his resignation. = 这位领导人在辞职前已经失去了许多支持者。
The theory had gained few adherents before new evidence emerged. =  在新证据出现之前，这个理论几乎没有支持者。


将来时:
The candidate hopes to gain more adherents before the election. =  候选人希望在选举前获得更多支持者。
The new technology will likely find many adherents. =  这项新技术可能会找到很多拥护者。

虚拟语气:
If he had more adherents, he might win the election. = 如果他有更多支持者，他可能会赢得选举。
I wish I had more adherents to my cause. = 我希望我的事业有更多支持者。

被动语态:
New adherents are being recruited every day. = 每天都有新的追随者被招募进来。
Many adherents were persuaded to change their minds. = 许多拥护者被说服改变了主意。

疑问句:

否定句:

The idea didn't attract many adherents. = 这个想法没有吸引很多追随者。
