nitraniline: /ˌnaɪtrænˈɪlɪn/ | n. |硝基苯胺|An aromatic amine compound containing a nitro group (-NO2) attached to the benzene ring, in addition to an amino group (-NH2). It exists as isomers depending on the relative positions of these groups. |2-Nitraniline is used in the synthesis of certain dyes. 2-硝基苯胺用于合成某些染料。|Synonyms: nitrophenylamine |Antonyms: None| noun


nitraniline: /ˌnaɪtrænˈɪlɪn/ | n. |硝基苯胺|A derivative of aniline where one or more hydrogen atoms on the benzene ring are replaced by nitro groups.  The position of the nitro group relative to the amine group is indicated by a number (e.g., 2-nitraniline, 3-nitraniline, 4-nitraniline).  These isomers have different physical and chemical properties. |4-Nitraniline is a starting material for the production of certain pigments. 4-硝基苯胺是生产某些颜料的起始原料。|Synonyms: nitrophenylamine |Antonyms: None| noun
