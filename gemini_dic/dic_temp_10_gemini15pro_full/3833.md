agonistics:/ˌæɡəˈnɪstɪks/ | n. | 争斗学;  竞争行为研究; | The study of aggressive behavior, especially in animals, but also in humans, considering the role of conflict in social adaptation and natural selection. |  Example:  Researchers in agonistics study the ritualized displays of aggression in animals. 研究人员在争斗学中研究动物的仪式化攻击行为。

Example:  The book explored the agonistics of political debate. 这本书探讨了政治辩论中的竞争行为。 | 近义词: competition, rivalry, contest | 反义词: cooperation, collaboration, peace | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Greek *ἀγωνιστικός (agōnistikos) "combative, fond of contesting," from ἀγωνίζεσθαι (agōnizesthai) "to contend for a prize," from ἀγών (agōn) "contest, struggle."
