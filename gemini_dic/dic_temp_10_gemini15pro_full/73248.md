fords:/fɔːrdz/
n.
浅滩, 渡口
Shallow places in a river or stream that can be crossed by wading or driving.
We crossed the river at the fords. 我们在浅滩处渡过了河。

n.
(Ford) 福特（汽车品牌）
A brand of automobile.
He drives a Ford. 他开一辆福特汽车。


Etymology:
From Old English *ford, from Proto-Germanic *furduz ("ford"), from Proto-Indo-European *pr̥tús ("passage, ford"). Cognate with Old Frisian ford, Old Saxon ford, Old High German furt, Old Norse fjorðr, Gothic 𐍆𐌰𐌿𐍂𐌸𐌿𐍃 (faurþus).

Synonyms:
crossing, shallows (for the place)
Ford (for the car brand)


Antonyms:
deep (for the place - referring to the opposite of shallow)
