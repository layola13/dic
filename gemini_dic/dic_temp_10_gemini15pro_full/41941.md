cosmopolitanism:/ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtənɪzəm/ | n. | 世界主义；世界性；四海一家 | The ideology that all human beings belong to a single community, based on a shared morality. |  例句：She embraced cosmopolitanism and enjoyed exploring different cultures. 她信奉世界主义，喜欢探索不同的文化。

例句：The city's cosmopolitanism is reflected in its diverse cuisine. 这座城市的世界性体现在其多元的美食文化中。| 近义词：internationalism, globalism, universality | 反义词：nationalism, parochialism, insularity | 词性: noun


cosmopolitan:/ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtən/ | adj. | 世界性的；全球性的；见多识广的 |  Belonging to all the world; not limited to just one part of the world. | 例句：London is a cosmopolitan city, attracting people from all over the world. 伦敦是一座国际大都市，吸引着来自世界各地的人们。

例句：He has a very cosmopolitan outlook on life. 他的人生观非常国际化。 | 近义词：international, global, worldly, sophisticated | 反义词：provincial, parochial, insular | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cosmopolis "world city," from Greek kosmopolitēs "citizen of the world," from kosmos "world" + politēs "citizen."
