noisome:/ˈnɔɪsəm/ | adj. | 恶臭的；令人讨厌的；有害的 | Having an extremely offensive smell; disgusting; harmful. | The noisome fumes from the factory made the neighborhood uninhabitable. 工厂散发出的恶臭气体使附近无法居住。

The noisome stench of the rotting garbage filled the alleyway. 腐烂垃圾的恶臭弥漫着整个小巷。| 近义词: foul, offensive, noxious, fetid, putrid | 反义词: fragrant, pleasant, aromatic, sweet | 词性: 形容词

noisome:/ˈnɔɪsʌm/ | adj. | 恶臭的；令人讨厌的；有害的 | Having an extremely offensive smell; disgusting; harmful. |  His noisome behavior disgusted everyone in the room. 他令人讨厌的行为让房间里的每个人都感到恶心。

Her noisome comments about her colleagues created a hostile work environment. 她对同事的恶意评论制造了敌对的工作环境。| 近义词: disgusting, repugnant, repulsive, objectionable | 反义词: agreeable, pleasant, delightful, charming | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Middle English *noisome, from Old French *noiosome, from noiier ("to annoy, harm") +‎ -some, from Latin inodiōsus ("offensive, hateful").  The sense of "harmful" is now mostly obsolete, with the primary meaning being "offensive to the smell".
