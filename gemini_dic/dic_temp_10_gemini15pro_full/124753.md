mri:/ˈem ɑːr ˈaɪ/ (US) /ˌem ɑːr ˈaɪ/ (UK)| n.|磁共振成像|Magnetic Resonance Imaging, a medical imaging technique used in radiology to form pictures of the anatomy and the physiological processes of the body. It uses a magnetic field and radio waves to generate images of organs and tissues.|The doctor ordered an MRI to examine her knee.|Synonyms: magnetic resonance imaging scan, nuclear magnetic resonance imaging|Antonyms: N/A|noun
