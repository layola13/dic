词源:  From Middle English *fetest, *festest, from Old English *fætest (attested only as fæstost), superlative of fæst (“firm, fixed, steadfast, constant, strong, fortified; secure, safe; bound, tied; close, dense; tenacious, viscous; stingy, avaricious; confirmed, ratified; betrothed, engaged; observant of”), from Proto-Germanic *fastuz (“firm, fixed, fast”), from Proto-Indo-European *pastós (“fixed”). Cognate with Scots fest (“fast”), West Frisian fêst (“fast, firm, solid, fixed”), Dutch vast (“firm, fixed, fast”), German fest (“firm, solid, fixed; festive”), Danish fast (“firm, fixed, fast”), Swedish fast (“firm, fixed, fast”). More at fast.  The superlative suffix -est is from Middle English -est, from Old English -ost, -est, from Proto-Germanic *-istaz, *-ōstaz.
