enfranchisements:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənts/
|n.|解放;授予公民权;特许经营权|The act of enfranchising, or the state of being enfranchised; release from bondage or servitude; investiture with the rights and privileges of free citizenship, especially the right to vote; also, the admission of a borough or town to political privileges.|The 19th Amendment secured the enfranchisement of women in the United States.  美国宪法第十九修正案保障了美国妇女的选举权。|
|近义词：liberations, empowerments|
|反义词：restrictions, disenfranchisements|
|词性：名词|


enfranchisement:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
|n.|解放;授予公民权;特许经营权|The act of giving someone the right to vote, or the right itself.|The enfranchisement of black people was a major step forward in civil rights. 黑人的选举权是民权运动向前迈出的重要一步。|
|近义词：empowerment, liberation|
|反义词：disenfranchisement, suppression|
|词性：名词|
