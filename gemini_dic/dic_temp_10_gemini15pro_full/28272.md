captivatingly:/ˈkæptɪveɪtɪŋli/
adverb
迷人地；令人着迷地
in a way that holds the attention by being extremely interesting, exciting, pleasant, or attractive
Her story was captivatingly told. 她的故事讲得引人入胜。
近义词:charmingly, enchantingly, fascinatingly, alluringly
反义词:uninterestingly, dully, tediously
词性:adverb

词源: From captivating + -ly.  "Captivating" comes from the Latin "captivatus," the past participle of "captivare," meaning "to take captive" or "to ensnare," from "captivus" meaning "captive," ultimately from "capere" meaning "to take" or "to seize."
