bards:/bɑːrdz/
n.
吟游诗人，诗人
A poet, traditionally one reciting epics and associated with a particular oral tradition.
The bards of old sang tales of heroes and gods. 古代的吟游诗人吟唱英雄和神的故事。
近义词：poets, minstrels, singers, musicians
反义词:audience, listener
词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bard, from Old French barde, from Gaulish *bardos (“poet, singer, bard”), from Proto-Celtic *bardos. Cognate with Irish bard, Welsh bardd, Breton barzh.
