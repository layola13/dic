carney:/ˈkɑːrni/| n.|嘉年华工人；骗子；哄骗；谄媚|A person employed at a carnival, especially one who entices customers to games or sideshows; a person who deceives or swindles someone.|例句：The carney barked at passersby, trying to lure them into the tent.  嘉年华工人对着路人叫喊，试图引诱他们进帐篷。

例句：He's a real carney, always trying to get something for nothing. 他是个十足的骗子，总是想不劳而获。|近义词：con man, swindler, hustler, cheat|反义词:honest person|词性:noun

carney:/ˈkɑːrni/| v.|哄骗；谄媚|To entice or persuade by flattery or deception.|例句：He tried to carney me into buying a timeshare. 他试图哄骗我买分时度假房。

例句：She carneying the boss for a promotion. 她为了升职而谄媚老板。|近义词：cajole, wheedle, coax, flatter|反义词:be honest|词性:verb
