direful: /ˈdaɪərfʊl/ (US) /ˈdaɪəfʊl/ (UK)| adj. |可怕的，恐怖的| Causing or involving great fear or suffering; dreadful; terrible. | The survivors gave direful accounts of the earthquake. 幸存者讲述了地震的可怕经历。

The direful consequences of war are felt for generations. 战争的可怕后果会延续几代人。| 近义词: dreadful, terrible, awful, horrific, appalling | 反义词: wonderful, excellent, pleasant, delightful | 词性: adjective

词源: From Middle English *dirful, from Old English *dīerfull, from dīere (“dear, precious, valuable”) + -full. The original meaning was "grievous, causing great sorrow," reflecting the value of what was lost or at risk.  Over time, the meaning shifted to "terrible, dreadful."
