extrication:/ˈɛkstrɪˈkeɪʃən/
|n.|解救，摆脱，脱离|The extrication of the trapped miners took several days. 被困矿工的解救工作花了几天时间。|
|英文解析:The act of freeing or releasing someone or something from a difficult or dangerous situation.|
|例句:The firefighters worked tirelessly on the extrication of the victims from the wreckage. 消防队员不知疲倦地工作，将受害者从残骸中解救出来。|
|例句:The extrication of the company from its financial difficulties required a radical restructuring.  公司摆脱财务困境需要彻底的重组。|
|近义词:rescue, liberation, deliverance, freeing|
|反义词:entrapment, confinement, imprisonment|
|词性:noun|
