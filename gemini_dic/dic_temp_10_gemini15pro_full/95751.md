inconsolably:/ˌɪnkənˈsoʊləbli/ | adv. |  无法安慰地，悲伤得无法自抑地 |  In a way that cannot be consoled or comforted; beyond comfort.  | She wept inconsolably after hearing the news of her husband's death. 她听到丈夫去世的消息后，哭得死去活来。| 近义词: disconsolately, heartbrokenly, hopelessly | 反义词: consolably, cheerfully, happily | 词性: 副词

inconsolable:/ˌɪnkənˈsoʊləbəl/ | adj. | 无法安慰的，悲伤得无法自抑的 | Not able to be consoled or comforted. | The child was inconsolable after losing his favorite toy.  孩子丢了他最喜欢的玩具后，怎么也哄不好。| 近义词: disconsolate, heartbroken, devastated | 反义词: consolable, cheerful, happy | 词性: 形容词

console:/kənˈsoʊl/ | v. | 安慰，慰藉 | To comfort someone at a time of grief or disappointment. |  I tried to console her after her dog died.  在她狗狗死后，我试图安慰她。| 近义词: comfort, solace, soothe | 反义词: distress, upset, grieve | 词性: 动词

consolation:/ˌkɒnsəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 安慰，慰藉 | Comfort received by a person after a loss or disappointment. | Her only consolation was that her family was safe. 她唯一的安慰是她的家人平安无事。 | 近义词: comfort, solace, relief | 反义词: distress, grief, sorrow | 词性: 名词


词源:
From Late Latin *inconsolabilis, from in- "not" + consolabilis "capable of being consoled," from Latin consolari "to console."
