infeudation:/ɪnˌfjuːˈdeɪʃn/ | n. | 封地授予;  依附;  臣服 | The act of enfeoffing, or the state of being enfeoffed; the granting of a fief or fee, especially in return for homage and service; also, the fief or fee so granted; the condition of a tenant who holds lands by feudal tenure; subinfeudation. |  The infeudation of the peasantry to the local lord was absolute.  农民对当地领主的依附是绝对的。 | 近义词:enfeoffment, vassalage, subservience | 反义词:independence, autonomy | 词性:名词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *infeudatio, from infeudare “to enfeoff,” from in- + feudum “fief.”
