cicatricle: /ˈsɪkəˌtrɪk(ə)l/ /ˈsɪkəˌtrɪkl/
noun
胚珠；胚盘；叶痕；疤痕
A small, round, shallow scar; especially : the scar on a seed marking the point of its attachment.  Also: the light-colored area on the yolk of an egg where the embryo develops.
The cicatricle on the egg yolk is barely visible.  蛋黄上的胚盘几乎看不见。
synonyms: scar, mark, blemish, spot
antonyms:  perfection, flawlessness
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cicatriculus, diminutive of Latin cicatrix (“scar”).
