dissolved:/dɪˈzɒlvd/ (UK) /dɪˈzɑːlvd/ (US)| adj. v.|溶解的，解散的|Broken up or terminated, as an organization or assembly; (of a solid) having been mixed into a liquid to form a solution.|The sugar dissolved quickly in the hot tea. 糖在热茶中很快就溶解了。|Synonyms: melted, liquefied, broken up, terminated|Antonyms: solidified, congealed, established, united|Verb (past tense of dissolve), Adjective

dissolve:/dɪˈzɒlv/ (UK) /dɪˈzɑːlv/ (US)| v.|溶解，解散|To become incorporated into a liquid so as to form a solution; to formally end or break up (an assembly, meeting, or official body).|The parliament was dissolved before the election. 议会在选举前解散了。|Synonyms: melt, liquefy, disband, terminate|Antonyms: solidify, congeal, establish, unite|Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin dissolvere ("to loosen, untie, detach, disconnect, separate, disperse"), from dis- ("apart") + solvere ("to loosen, release").
