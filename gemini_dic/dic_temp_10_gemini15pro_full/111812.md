logarithmic:/ˌlɒɡərɪðmɪk/ (US) /ˌlɔːɡərɪðmɪk/ (UK)| adj.|对数的；对数尺度的|Relating to or expressed in terms of logarithms.  A logarithmic scale uses the logarithm of a value instead of the value itself.|The Richter scale is logarithmic, so an earthquake of magnitude 7 is ten times more powerful than one of magnitude 6.|近义词:exponential (in the opposite sense)|反义词:linear|词性:形容词

logarithm:/ˈlɒɡərɪðəm/ (US) /ˈlɔːɡərɪðəm/(UK)| n.|对数|A quantity representing the power to which a fixed number (the base) must be raised to produce a given number.|The logarithm of 100 to the base 10 is 2.|近义词:exponent (related concept)|反义词:antilogarithm|词性:名词
