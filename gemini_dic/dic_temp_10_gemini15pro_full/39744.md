consolate:/ˈkɒnsəleɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsəleɪt/ (US)| v. |安慰，慰藉|To comfort someone who is sad or disappointed.|She tried to console him after his dog died. 她试图在他失去爱犬后安慰他。|例句：
The grieving family was consoled by their friends.  悲痛的家人得到了朋友们的安慰。

He consoled her with a hug. 他用拥抱安慰她。

|近义词：comfort, soothe, solace|反义词:distress, upset|词性:动词(verb)

consolate:/ˈkɒnsələt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsələt/ (US)| adj. (obsolete)|得到安慰的，感到慰藉的|Consoled; comforted.|This meaning is now largely obsolete.|例句：
(Archaic) He felt somewhat consolate after hearing the news. (古语) 听到这个消息后，他感到有些欣慰。

(Archaic)  She offered a few consolate words. (古语) 她说了几句安慰的话.


|近义词：comforted, soothed, solaced|反义词:distressed, upset|词性:形容词(adjective - obsolete)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *consolatus, past participle of consolari (“to console, comfort, soothe”), from Latin con- (“together, with”) + solari (“to soothe, comfort”).
