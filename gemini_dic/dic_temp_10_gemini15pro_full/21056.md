blindest:/ˈblaɪndɪst/| adj.|最盲目的，最瞎的|Superlative form of blind: most blind.  Unable to see; lacking the sense of sight; also, lacking insight or understanding. |He was the blindest of them all, unable to see the obvious solution. 他是所有人中最盲目的一个，看不到显而易见的解决方案。|The blindest follower just obeys orders without thinking. 最盲目的追随者只是服从命令而不思考。|近义词:most unseeing, most sightless, most unaware|反义词:most perceptive, most insightful, most aware|词性:形容词

词源:
From Old English *blind, from Proto-Germanic *blindaz, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰlendʰ- (“shining, shimmering; white, blond”). Cognate with Dutch blind, German blind, Swedish blind. The figurative sense of "lacking understanding" arose in the 13th century.
