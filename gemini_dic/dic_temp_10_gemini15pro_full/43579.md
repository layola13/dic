creaking:/ˈkriːkɪŋ/ (US), /ˈkriːkɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.  n.|吱吱作响的，嘎吱嘎吱的声音|A harsh, grating sound made by something that rubs or moves stiffly, like old wood or rusty metal.|The creaking stairs betrayed his presence. 吱吱作响的楼梯暴露了他的行踪.|近义词：squeaking, groaning, grating|反义词:silent, quiet|词性:形容词, 名词
creak:/kriːk/ (US), /kriːk/ (UK)| v.  n.|发出吱吱声，嘎吱声；吱吱作响，嘎吱作响|To make a harsh, grating sound, as of old wood or rusty metal.|The door creaked open. 门吱吱呀呀地开了。|近义词：squeak, groan, grate|反义词:silence|词性:动词, 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *creken, *criken, of imitative origin.  Cognate with Dutch kraken (“to crack, creak”), German krachen (“to crash, crack”), Swedish kräka (“to vomit”).
