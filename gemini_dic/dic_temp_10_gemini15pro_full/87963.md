high-fiving:/ˈhaɪ ˈfaɪvɪŋ/| n./v.|击掌，相互庆祝|The act of slapping another person's raised hand as a gesture of celebration or greeting.|Example: They were high-fiving each other after winning the game. 他们赢了比赛后互相击掌。|近义词：celebrating, congratulating|反义词: commiserating, mourning|词性: 名词/动词


high-five:/ˈhaɪ ˈfaɪv/| n./v.|击掌|A gesture of celebration or greeting in which two people slap each other's raised hands.|Example:  He gave me a high-five after I finished the presentation. 我完成演讲后，他跟我击掌。 |近义词：hand slap, clap|反义词:  (No direct antonym, but consider gestures of sadness or disappointment)|词性: 名词/动词

Etymology:  A combination of "high" (referring to the raised hands) and "five" (referring to the five fingers of each hand).  Believed to have originated in the 1970s in American sports, specifically baseball and basketball.
