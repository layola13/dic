clinker:/ˈklɪŋkər/| n. |熟料；熔渣；矿渣；（尤指烧制砖、瓦后的）炉渣，渣块|A hard stony substance, typically brick, that is produced as a waste product when coal, clay, or other substances are burnt.  A piece of incombustible residue from burnt coal or other fuel, especially one that has fused into a hard mass.|The bricklayer swept up the clinkers.  砖匠扫除了炉渣。|Synonyms: slag, residue, cinder|Antonyms: raw material, ingredient|noun


clinker:/ˈklɪŋkər/ (British English also clinker-built) | adj. |（尤指船体）用叠板建造的| (especially of a boat hull) built with overlapping planks.|The clinker-built boat was sturdy and seaworthy. 这艘叠板建造的船坚固且适航。|Synonyms: lapstrake, overlapping|Antonyms: carvel-built|adjective
