extemporise: /ɪkˈstɛmpəraɪz/ | v. | 即兴创作，即兴表演 | To compose, perform, or produce something without preparation; improvise. |  He extemporised a speech at the wedding. 他在婚礼上即兴发表了演讲。| Synonyms: improvise, ad-lib, wing it | Antonyms: prepare, rehearse | verb


extemporised: /ɪkˈstɛmpəraɪzd/ | adj. | 即兴的，无准备的 | Done without preparation; improvised. | She delivered an extemporised speech that captivated the audience. 她发表了一场即兴演讲，吸引了观众。| Synonyms: improvised, impromptu, off-the-cuff | Antonyms: prepared, rehearsed, planned | adjective

extemporizing: /ɪkˈstɛmpəˌraɪzɪŋ/ | n. | 即兴创作，即兴表演 | The act of composing, performing, or producing something without preparation.  |  His extemporizing on the piano was impressive. 他在钢琴上的即兴演奏令人印象深刻。 | Synonyms: improvisation, ad-libbing, winging it | Antonyms: preparation, rehearsal | noun


Etymology: From Latin *ex tempore* "out of the time," from *ex* "out of" + *tempore* "time."
