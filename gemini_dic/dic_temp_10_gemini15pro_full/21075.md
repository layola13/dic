blindsided:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪdɪd/
verb
使措手不及，出其不意地打击
to surprise someone, usually with harmful or unpleasant information or an action that they were not expecting
The news of the merger completely blindsided the employees.  公司合并的消息让员工们完全措手不及。
The team was blindsided by their opponent's unexpected strategy.  该队被对手出人意料的策略打了个措手不及。
近义词: surprised, shocked, stunned, ambushed
反义词: prepared, forewarned


ETYMOLOGY->
From "blind side" + "-ed."  "Blind side" refers to the side of a person that cannot be seen, and therefore from which an attack is unexpected.
