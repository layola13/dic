coincide:/ˌkoʊɪnˈsaɪd/ (US), /ˌkəʊɪnˈsaɪd/ (UK)| v. | 一致，符合，同时发生 | To occur at the same time or occupy the same space; to agree or be similar. | The two events coincided, causing much confusion. 这两个事件同时发生，造成了很大的混乱。|  The band's American tour coincided with the release of their new album.  乐队的美国巡演与他们新专辑的发行同时进行。 | synonyms: concur, correspond, synchronize | antonyms: differ, diverge, clash | verb

coinincidence:/koʊˈɪnsɪdəns/ (US), /kəʊˈɪnsɪdəns/ (UK)| n. | 巧合，一致，同时发生 | The occurrence of events that happen at the same time by accident but seem to have some connection. | It was pure coincidence that we met in Paris. 我们在巴黎相遇纯属巧合。| By coincidence, I ran into my old friend at the airport.  很巧的是，我在机场碰到了我的老朋友。| synonyms: chance, accident, fluke | antonyms: intention, plan, design | noun
