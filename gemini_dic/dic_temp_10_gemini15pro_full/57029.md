doublespeaker:/ˈdʌbəlˌspiːkər/| n.|双重语言使用者；使用模棱两可语言的人；|Someone who uses language deliberately to disguise, distort, or reverse the meaning of words.|The politician was accused of being a doublespeaker, using ambiguous language to avoid taking a clear stance on the issue. 这位政治家被指责是一个双重语言使用者，使用模棱两可的语言来避免在问题上表明明确的立场。|近义词：propagandist, equivocator, prevaricator|反义词:straight talker, honest speaker|词性:noun

Etymology:
double (adj. & n.) + speaker (n.)

double (adj. & n.): from Old French doble, ultimately from Latin duplus "twofold," from duo "two"

speaker (n.): from speak (v.), from Old English sp(r)ecan "to speak," of Germanic origin
