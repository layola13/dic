moseyed:/ˈmoʊ.zid/ (US) /ˈməʊ.zid/ (UK)| v. |闲逛，漫步|To walk or go somewhere slowly and casually.|He moseyed on down to the general store. 他慢悠悠地走到杂货店。|We moseyed around the town square. 我们在城镇广场上闲逛。|近义词：stroll, amble, saunter, wander|反义词：rush, hurry, sprint|词性:动词


词源:
Originating in the American West in the mid-19th century, the etymology of "mosey" is uncertain. Some speculate it might be related to the Spanish word "vamos" (let's go), while others suggest a connection to obsolete English words like "mose" (to wander) or "mooze" (to dawdle).
