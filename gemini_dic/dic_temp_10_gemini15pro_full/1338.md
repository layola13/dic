accrue:/əˈkruː/
verb
积累，增加
(especially of sums of money or benefits) be received by someone in regular or increasing amounts over time.
Interest will accrue on the account until payment is made. 在付款之前，帐户将累积利息。
My savings have accrued a surprising amount of interest. 我的储蓄累积了数量惊人的利息。
synonyms: accumulate, collect, gather, build up, mount up, grow, increase, augment
antonyms: decrease, diminish, dwindle, lessen, reduce
词性: 动词


accrue:/əˈkruː/
adjective
应计的
(of sums of money or benefits) increasing over time.
accrued interest 应计利息
The accrued expenses for the last fiscal year were significant. 上一财政年度的累计费用相当可观。
synonyms: accumulated, aggregated, collected
antonyms: expended, spent, disbursed
词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French acreue (“increase, growth”), past participle of acreistre (“to grow, increase”), from Latin accrescere (“to grow to, be added to”), from ad- (“to”) + crescere (“to grow”).

USAGE->
一般现在时:
Interest accrues on the account daily. = 账户每天都会累积利息。
The benefits accrue to the employees over time. = 这些福利随着时间的推移而累积给员工。

一般过去时:
A significant amount of interest accrued on the investment last year. = 去年投资累积了大量利息。
Debts accrued rapidly during the economic downturn. = 在经济衰退期间，债务迅速累积。

现在完成时:
The company has accrued substantial profits this quarter. = 本季度公司已累计了可观的利润。
My vacation days have accrued steadily throughout the year. = 我的假期天数全年都在稳步累积。

过去完成时:
By the time the project was completed, significant costs had accrued. = 到项目完成时，已经累积了大量的成本。
He had accrued considerable wealth before he retired. = 他在退休前积累了相当多的财富。

将来时:
More benefits will accrue to members in the coming years. = 未来几年，会员将获得更多福利。
The debt will accrue interest at a rate of 5% per annum. = 债务将以每年 5% 的利率累积利息。

被动语态:
Interest is accrued on a daily basis. = 利息按日累积。
Significant losses were accrued by the company last year. = 去年公司累计了重大损失。
