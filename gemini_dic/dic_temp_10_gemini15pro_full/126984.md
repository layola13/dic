narwhal:/ˈnɑːrˌwɑːl/ (US) /ˈnɑːwəl/ (UK)| n.|独角鲸|A medium-sized toothed whale (Monodon monoceros) that lives year-round in Arctic waters.  The male has a long, straight, spirally grooved tusk that projects forward from the upper jaw.|例句：The narwhal is a fascinating creature of the Arctic. 独角鲸是一种迷人的北极生物。

|例句：Researchers are studying the narwhal's unique tusk. 研究人员正在研究独角鲸独特的长牙。|近义词：unicorn whale (非正式)|反义词: N/A (没有生物学上的反义词)|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Danish *narhval*, from Old Norse *nárhvalr*, from *nár* (“corpse”) + *hvalr* (“whale”).  The name refers to the mottled gray color of the narwhal's skin, which resembles that of a drowned sailor.
