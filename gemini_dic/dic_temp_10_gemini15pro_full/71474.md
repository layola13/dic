flamingly:/ˈfleɪmɪŋli/| adv.|炽烈地，火红地，显眼地|In a flaming manner; intensely; conspicuously.  Characterized by vibrant color or intense emotion, often suggesting anger or passion.|例句：He was flamingly angry. 他怒火中烧。|例句：The sunset was flamingly beautiful.  日落美得如火如荼。|近义词：fiery, intensely, passionately, vividly, brightly, conspicuously|反义词：drably, mildly, subtly, calmly|词性:副词

Etymology:
From flame + -ing + -ly.  "Flame" originates from the Old French "flambe," ultimately from the Latin "flamma."  "-ing" is a suffix forming present participles and also adjectives from verbs. "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives.
