equiponderancy:/ˌiːkwɪˈpɒndərənsi/ /ˌiːkwɪˈpɑːndərənsi/
noun
均势；同等重要；平衡
A state of balance or equal weight or importance.
The treaty aimed to achieve a state of equiponderancy between the two nations. 该条约旨在实现两国之间的均势。
The equiponderancy of power between the two parties ensured a stable political landscape.  双方力量的平衡确保了稳定的政治格局。
近义词:equilibrium, balance, equality, parity
反义词:imbalance, disparity, inequality
词性:n.


equiponderant:/ˌiːkwɪˈpɒndərənt/ /ˌiːkwɪˈpɑːndərənt/
adjective
均等的；同等重要的；平衡的
Being of equal weight or importance.
The two arguments were equiponderant, making it difficult to choose a side.  这两个论点同等重要，因此难以选择立场。
The negotiators sought an equiponderant solution that would satisfy both parties.  谈判代表寻求一个能满足双方的平衡解决方案。
近义词:equal, balanced, equivalent, commensurate
反义词:unequal, unbalanced, disproportionate
词性:adj.


Etymology:
From Latin "aequiponderans" (present participle of "aequiponderare"), from "aequi-" (equal) + "ponderans" (weighing), from "pondus" (weight).
