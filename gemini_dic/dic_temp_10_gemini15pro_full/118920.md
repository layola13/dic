meted:/ˈmiːtɪd/| v. |分配，给予，施加|to distribute or administer something, especially something unpleasant (usually followed by "out")|例句：The judge meted out a harsh sentence. 法官判处了严厉的刑罚。|例句：They meted out punishment to the wrongdoers.  他们对做错事的人给予了惩罚。|近义词：allocate, allot, dispense, administer, dole out, deal out|反义词: withhold, retain|词性:动词

词源:
From Old English *mētan "to measure, find the dimensions of," from Proto-Germanic *metan (source also of Old Saxon metan, Old Norse meta, Old Frisian meta, Dutch meten, Old High German mezzan, German messen "to measure"), from PIE root *me- (2) "to measure."  The sense shifted from "measure out" to "distribute, allot."
