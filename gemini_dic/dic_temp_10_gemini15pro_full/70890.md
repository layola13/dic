ETYMOLOGY->
From Norwegian *fjǫrðr, from Old Norse fjǫrðr, meaning "fjord, inlet," related to fara "to travel, pass," ultimately from Proto-Germanic *farją (“to travel, fare”). Cognate with Danish fjord, Swedish fjärd.
