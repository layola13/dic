词源:  The origin of "lickety-split" is uncertain, but it likely comes from the idea of something moving at a speed that "licks" up the ground and "splits" the air.  It's an example of  onomatopoeia,  a word that imitates a sound.  It first appeared in print in the mid-19th century in the United States.
