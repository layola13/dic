attitudinarianism: /ˌætɪtjuːdɪˈnɛəriənɪzəm/ | n. | 笃信教条主义；拘泥形式主义 |  The belief that outward observance of religious forms is more important than inward piety or moral conduct.  A rigid adherence to a system of beliefs or practices, especially in religion. |  His attitudinarianism alienated many members of the congregation. 他的拘泥形式主义疏远了许多会众。 |  dogmatism, formalism, rigidity |  flexibility, open-mindedness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From attitude + -inarian + -ism.  "Attitude" in this older sense refers to postures or gestures indicative of one's opinions or beliefs, especially religious ones.  "-inarian" forms nouns and adjectives referring to a person who holds a particular doctrine or follows a particular practice.  "-ism" forms nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, or practices.
