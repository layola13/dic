after-fact:/ˈæftərˌfækt/ | adj. | 事后的；马后炮的；亡羊补牢的 | Happening or done after something else, especially something bad, has happened; based on knowledge about an event only after it has occurred. |  The investigation was an after-fact, coming months after the disaster.  调查是事后诸葛亮，在灾难发生几个月后才进行。| synonyms: retrospective, subsequent, post hoc | antonyms: prior, anticipatory, beforehand | adjective

after-fact:/ˈæftərˌfækt/ | n. | 事后诸葛亮；马后炮 | Something that is done or said after an event, typically something bad, has happened, making it seem as though it had been planned or known about in advance. |  His apologies were just after-facts, offered only when he realized the seriousness of the situation. 他的道歉只是马后炮，只是在他意识到情况的严重性时才提出的。 | synonyms: hindsight, second thought, postmortem | antonyms: foresight, premonition, prediction | noun
