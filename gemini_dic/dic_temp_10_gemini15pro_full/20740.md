blanch:/blɑːntʃ/ /blænʧ/| v. |使变白；漂白；使(脸色)变苍白；烫漂|To make white or pale by removing color; to scald briefly, usually vegetables, in boiling water or steam.|She blanched the almonds before adding them to the cake. 她将杏仁烫漂后再加入蛋糕中。|The shock of the news caused her face to blanch.  这突如其来的消息使她脸色苍白。|synonyms: whiten, pale, bleach, scald |antonyms: darken, color, flush |verb

blanch:/blɑːntʃ/ /blænʧ/| n. |(金属或硬币的)坯材；未完成状态|An unprocessed or unfinished state, especially of metal or coins.|The coin was still in its blanch state. 这枚硬币仍处于坯材状态。|This is just the blanch of the project, much more work needs to be done. 这只是项目的雏形，还需要做很多工作。|synonyms: blank, rough draft|antonyms: finished product, completion |noun
