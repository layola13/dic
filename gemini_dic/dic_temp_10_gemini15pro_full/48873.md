defoul:/dɪˈfaʊl/ | vt. | 弄脏，污染；败坏，玷污 | To make something dirty or impure, especially by adding waste or sewage; to corrupt or spoil. | The factory defouled the river with its chemical waste. 工厂用化学废料污染了河流。|  The scandal defouled the reputation of the politician.  这桩丑闻玷污了这位政治家的名誉。| synonyms: pollute, contaminate, taint, corrupt, sully | antonyms: cleanse, purify, clean | verb

defoul:/dɪˈfaʊl/ | n. | 污染，污秽 | The act of defouling or the state of being defouled.  | The defoul of the lake made it unsafe for swimming.  湖泊的污染使它不适宜游泳。 | synonyms: pollution, contamination, taint | antonyms: cleanliness, purity | noun
