opisthobranchiata:/əˌpɪsθəˈbræŋkiˌɑːtə/ | n. |后鳃亚纲|A large and diverse subclass of marine gastropod molluscs, characterized by the partial or complete reduction of the shell and the posterior placement of the gills, if present.  They include sea slugs, bubble snails, and sea hares.|Sea hares, belonging to the Opisthobranchiata, are known for their ink-like secretions. 海兔，属于后鳃亚纲，以其类似墨水的分泌物而闻名。|Synonym:  Opisthobranchs|Antonym: (No direct antonym, as it's a taxonomic classification.  Perhaps "Prosobranchia" as a contrasting subclass)|noun
