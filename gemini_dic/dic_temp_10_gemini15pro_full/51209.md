destitute:/ˈdɛstɪtjuːt/| adj.|贫困的，赤贫的，缺乏的|Without the basic necessities of life; lacking something needed or desirable.|Many families were left destitute by the war. 战争使许多家庭一贫如洗。|Synonyms: impoverished, indigent, penniless, impecunious|Antonyms: wealthy, affluent, rich, prosperous|adjective

destitution:/ˌdɛstɪˈtjuːʃən/| n.|贫困，赤贫|Extreme poverty; lack of something needed or desirable.|The earthquake left thousands in destitution. 地震使数千人陷入贫困。|Synonyms: poverty, indigence, penury, impoverishment|Antonyms: wealth, affluence, prosperity, opulence|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *destitutus*, past participle of *destituere* "to abandon, forsake," from *de-* "down, away from" + *statuere* "to set, place."
