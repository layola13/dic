crinosity:/krɪˈnɒsɪti/ | n. | 好奇心，求知欲 | A strong desire to learn or know something; inquisitiveness. |  Example 1: Her crinosity about the world led her to travel extensively. 她对世界的好奇心驱使她四处旅行。

Example 2:  The child's crinosity was insatiable, always asking "why?".  孩子的好奇心无法满足，总是问“为什么？”。 | 近义词: curiosity, inquisitiveness | 反义词: apathy, indifference | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *crinositas, from crīnis ("hair") + -osus ("-ous").  The original meaning referred to having many hairs, later evolving metaphorically to describe intellectual inquisitiveness, perhaps likened to having many "feelers" reaching out for knowledge.
