huxter:/ˈhʌkstər/| n. |叫卖小贩；强迫推销者|A person who sells goods, especially food, in the street by shouting loudly. A person who persuades people to buy something in a forceful and annoying way.|The huxter shouted out the prices of his wares.  叫卖小贩大声喊出他的商品价格。

The aggressive huxter wouldn't leave us alone until we bought something.  这个强买强卖的小贩直到我们买了东西才肯罢休。|近义词：vendor, peddler, hawker, salesman|反义词: buyer, customer|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Low German *hukster, frequentative of huken  "to squat, crouch," hence "to peddle." Related to Dutch hokster and German Höker.
