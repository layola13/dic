cannibally:/ˌkænɪˈbælɪkli/ | adv. | 食人般地，残忍地 | In a manner resembling cannibalism; savagely and cruelly. |  He looked at the last piece of cake cannibally, as if ready to devour it whole. 他用食人般的目光看着最后一块蛋糕，仿佛要把它整个吞下去。 |  The corporation cannibally swallowed up smaller businesses.  这家公司残忍地吞并了较小的企业。 |  savagely, brutally, fiercely, ferociously |  kindly, gently, compassionately | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From cannibal + -ly.  Cannibal comes from Spanish *caníbal*, from the name of the Carib people, a West Indies tribe formerly believed to practice cannibalism.
