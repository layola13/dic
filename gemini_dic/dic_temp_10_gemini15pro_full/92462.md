hypostatic:/ˌhaɪpəˈstætɪk/ | adj. | 本体论的，位格的 | Relating to hypostasis; or the essential person of the Trinity, especially of Christ; or a distinct nature or essence.  |  The hypostatic union of Christ's divine and human natures is a central doctrine in Christian theology. 基督神性和人性的位格合一是基督教神学的核心教义。 |  consubstantial, essential |  accidental, inessential | adjective

hypostatic:/ˌhaɪpəˈstætɪk/ | n. | 位格，本体 | A distinct substance or essence; a person of the Trinity. | The three hypostatic persons of the Trinity are the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit.  三位一体的三个位格是圣父、圣子和圣灵。 | essence, substance | non-being, nothingness | noun
