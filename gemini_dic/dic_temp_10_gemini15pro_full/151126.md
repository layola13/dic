parasymbiont:/ˈpærəˈsɪmbaɪɒnt/| n. |共生生物；偏利共生生物|An organism in a parasymbiotic relationship, where one organism benefits and the other is neither harmed nor benefited.|The parasymbiont barnacle attaches to the whale, gaining transportation and access to food, while the whale is seemingly unaffected.|近义词：commensal|反义词:parasite, pathogen|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *para-* (beside, alongside) + *syn-* (together) + *bios* (life) + *-ont* (being, organism)
