melancholically: /ˌmɛlənˈkɒlɪkli/ (US) /ˌmɛlənˈkɒlɪkli/ (UK)| adv.|忧郁地，伤感地|In a melancholic manner; with melancholy; sadly and pensively.|She gazed melancholically out the window at the falling rain. 她忧郁地望着窗外落下的雨。|例句：He sighed melancholically and turned away. 他忧郁地叹了口气，转过身去。|近义词：sadly, gloomily, pensively, wistfully|反义词：happily, cheerfully, joyfully|词性:副词

词源:
From melancholy + -ly.  Melancholy comes from Middle French mélancolie, from Late Latin melancholia, from Greek melankholía "black bile," from melas, melan- "black" + kholé "bile."  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholy.
