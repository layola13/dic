carmagnole: /ˌkɑːrməˈnjɔːl/ | n. | 卡马尼奥尔舞；卡马尼奥尔歌曲 | A lively song and dance popular during the French Revolution. |  The revolutionaries danced the carmagnole in the streets. 革命者在街上跳卡马尼奥尔舞。 | synonyms: dance, song, revolutionary anthem | antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, perhaps "lament" or "dirge" in terms of mood/purpose) | noun


Etymology: From French *carmagnole*, from Carmagnola, a town in Piedmont, Italy, where the style of clothing associated with the dance supposedly originated.
