gargoyle:/ˈɡɑːrˌɡɔɪl/| n.|滴水嘴兽，怪兽状雕饰|A carved figure, usually of a grotesque monster, projecting from the gutter of a building, typically acting as a waterspout.|The cathedral is adorned with many intricate gargoyles.  这座大教堂装饰着许多复杂的滴水嘴兽。|The gargoyle leered down from the roof. 滴水嘴兽从屋顶上向下斜视。|近义词: grotesque, chimera|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Old French *gargouille* "throat, gullet," also "gargoyle," from *gargouiller* "to gurgle," from Latin *gurgulio* "gullet, windpipe."  The word ultimately imitates the sound of gurgling water.
