controllability's:/kənˌtrəʊləˈbɪlətiz/ | n. | 可控性，可控制性 | The quality or state of being controllable.  It refers to the extent to which something can be controlled or influenced. | The controllability's of the new aircraft is a major concern for pilots.  这种新飞机的可控性是飞行员主要关心的问题。 | synonyms: manageability, governability | antonyms: unmanageability, ungovernability | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From control + -able + -ity + -'s.  "Control" comes from Old French "controle" (a duplicate register used to verify accounts), ultimately from Medieval Latin "contrarotulus." "-able" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "capable of being," from Old French "-able," ultimately from Latin "-abilis." "-ity" is a suffix forming nouns of quality or condition, from Old French "-ité," ultimately from Latin "-itas." "-'s" is the possessive marker.
