lixivated: /lɪkˈsɪvieɪtɪd/ | adj. | 沥滤的，浸出的 |  Having had soluble constituents removed by percolation, as by the action of water through soil.  The process of extracting a soluble substance from a solid material by dissolving it in a liquid, often used in mining and soil science. | The lixiviated soil was barren and unable to support plant life. 沥滤后的土壤贫瘠，无法支撑植物生长。 | The archaeologist discovered lixiviated pottery shards, suggesting ancient brewing practices. 考古学家发现了沥滤的陶器碎片，表明古代有酿造的习俗。 |  leached, extracted, washed-out | concentrated, saturated | adjective
