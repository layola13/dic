partisanships:/ˈpɑːrtɪzənʃɪps/ | n. | 党派性，党派偏见 |  A strong adherence to a particular party, faction, cause, or person, often marked by a biased, uncritical, or emotional allegiance. | The current political climate is rife with partisanship, making it difficult to find common ground.  当前的政治气氛充满了党派偏见，很难找到共同点。|  The intense partisanship surrounding the issue made it impossible to have a rational discussion.围绕这个问题的强烈党派偏见使得理性讨论成为不可能。| synonyms: bias, prejudice, allegiance, loyalty, factionalism | antonyms: impartiality, neutrality, objectivity | noun
