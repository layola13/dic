bandwagon:/ˈbændˌwæɡən/| n. |潮流；时尚；浪潮；乐队花车|A current or fashionable trend, especially one that attracts increasing support.|例句：Many politicians are quick to jump on the bandwagon of a popular cause. 许多政客很快就会赶上一个受欢迎事业的潮流。

例句：She refused to jump on the environmental bandwagon just because it was fashionable. 她拒绝赶环保的时髦潮流。|近义词：trend, fad, fashion, craze|反义词：unpopular, outdated|词性:名词


bandwagons:/ˈbændˌwæɡənz/| n. |潮流；时尚；浪潮；乐队花车（复数）|Plural form of bandwagon.|例句：There are many political bandwagons rolling through the country right now.  现在全国各地都有许多政治潮流涌动。

例句：Beware of investment bandwagons that promise unrealistic returns.  要小心那些承诺不切实际回报的投资潮流。|近义词：trends, fads, fashions, crazes|反义词：unpopular things, outdated things|词性:名词


词源：
From band wagon, originally a wagon carrying a band in a parade or circus, which became associated with the idea of attracting followers or supporters.
