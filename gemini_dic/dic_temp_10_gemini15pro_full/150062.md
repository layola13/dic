palsical:/ˈpælsɪkəl/ | adj. |  变得苍白；患病的；虚弱的 |  Becoming or appearing pale; relating to or affected by illness or weakness.  Derived from "palsy" meaning paralysis or impairment of motor function. Implies a sickly or enfeebled state often associated with paleness. | He looked palsical after his long illness. 他久病之后看起来很虚弱。|  She had a palsical complexion, suggesting she hadn't been getting enough sun. 她的脸色苍白，表明她没有得到足够的阳光。| Near synonyms: pale, sickly, wan, feeble, weak, debilitated | Antonyms: robust, healthy, vigorous | Adjective
