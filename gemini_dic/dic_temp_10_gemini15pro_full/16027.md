bancus: /ˈbæŋkəs/| n. |bench, bank|A low seat, typically made of wood or stone; also, a raised area of earth, like a riverbank.  Derived from Proto-Germanic *bankiz, meaning "slope, ridge." Related to Old English benc and Old High German bank. Originally referred to a mound of earth, later extending to mean a seat and, metaphorically, a financial institution.|The weary traveler rested on a bancus by the roadside. 疲惫的旅行者在路边的长凳上休息。 The bancus of the river was overflowing with water. 河岸上的水溢出来了。|Synonyms: bench, seat, mound, bank|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps 'ditch' for riverbank context)|noun
