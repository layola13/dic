attendants:/əˈtɛndənts/| n.|服务员，侍者，出席者|People who are present at a place or event, especially those who perform a particular duty. |The attendants at the wedding were very helpful. 婚礼上的服务员非常乐于助人。|synonyms: servers, helpers, assistants, participants, attendees|antonyms: absentees, no-shows|noun


attendants:/əˈtɛndənts/| n.|随行人员|People who accompany someone, especially a person of high rank. |The queen's attendants followed her everywhere. 女王的随行人员到处跟随她。|synonyms: escorts, companions, retinue|antonyms:  leaders, bosses|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *attendant*, from Old French *atendant*, present participle of *atendre* "to wait for, attend to," from Latin *attendere* "to give heed to, attend, await," from *ad-* "to" + *tendere* "to stretch."
