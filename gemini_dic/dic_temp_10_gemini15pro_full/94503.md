importunate:/ɪmˈpɔːrtʃənət/ | adj. | 持续纠缠的；再三要求的；不达目的不罢休的 |  Persistent, especially to the point of annoyance or intrusion; making repeated or insistent requests, especially in a troublesome way. |  She was so importunate in her demands that he finally gave in. 她再三要求，他最终让步了。| Synonyms: insistent, persistent, tenacious, pertinacious, unrelenting, relentless | Antonyms: unassuming, modest, reserved, diffident | adjective


importunated:/ɪmˈpɔːrtʃəˌneɪtɪd/ | v. |  (通常被动)被纠缠；被再三要求 | To be persistently urged or requested, often to the point of annoyance.  Usually used in the passive voice. | He was importunated by beggars on the street. 他在街上被乞丐纠缠。 | Synonyms: harassed, pestered, badgered, hounded, besieged | Antonyms: ignored, neglected, disregarded | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *importunatus*, past participle of importunare (“to importune”), from Latin importunus (“unfit, inconvenient, troublesome”), from in- (“not”) + portus (“harbor, haven”).  The sense development is from "unseasonable, inconvenient" to "troublesome, persistent."
