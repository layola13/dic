chaperonage:/ˈʃæpərəˌnɪdʒ/ /ˈʃæpərəˌnɑːʒ/| n.|陪伴；监护；伴护|The act of chaperoning someone, or the state of being chaperoned.  The social system or practice of chaperoning young unmarried women.|Her mother insisted on chaperonage whenever she went out with a boy. 她和她母亲坚持每次她和男孩出去玩都要有人陪同。|Near synonyms: supervision, guidance, escort, attendance. |Antonyms:  neglect, freedom, independence.|noun

chaperon:/ˈʃæpəˌroʊn/ /ˈʃæpərən/| n.  v.|陪伴；监护；伴护；伴护人|A person who accompanies and looks after another person or group of people, especially a young unmarried woman, on social occasions.|She acted as a chaperon for her younger sister at the school dance. 她在学校舞会上担任她妹妹的伴护人。|Near synonyms:  escort, guardian, guide, attendant. |Antonyms: none (the absence of a chaperone implies freedom/independence).|noun, verb
