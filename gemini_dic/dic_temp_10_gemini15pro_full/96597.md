induction:/ɪnˈdʌkʃən/| n.| 就职；入门；归纳；感应 |The process of introducing someone to a new job, skill, organization, etc.; reasoning from specific facts to a general conclusion; the production of an electric or magnetic state by the proximity (without contact) of an electrified or magnetized body.|例句：The induction ceremony for new members will be held next week. 新成员的入会仪式将于下周举行。|例句：Scientists use induction to form hypotheses. 科学家用归纳法来形成假设。|近义词：initiation, onboarding, inference, deduction|反义词：deduction|词性:名词

inductions:/ɪnˈdʌkʃənz/| n.| 就职；入门；归纳；感应（复数）|Plural form of "induction," referring to multiple instances of the process of introducing someone to a new job, skill, organization, etc.; multiple instances of reasoning from specific facts to a general conclusion; multiple instances of the production of an electric or magnetic state by proximity.|例句：Several inductions into the hall of fame took place this year. 今年有几位名人入选名人堂。|例句：The scientist performed a series of inductions to test the hypothesis. 科学家进行了一系列的归纳推理来检验这个假设。|近义词：initiations, onboardings, inferences, deductions|反义词：deductions|词性:名词
