abbey:/ˈæbi/| n.|修道院，大教堂|A building or buildings occupied by a community of monks or nuns.|The monks lived a quiet life in the abbey.|monastery, convent, cloister|palace|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *abbeie*, from Late Latin *abbatia*, from Latin *abbas* (abbot). Ultimately from Aramaic *abba* (father).

USAGE->
The abbey is a popular tourist destination. = 这座修道院是一个受欢迎的旅游目的地。

The abbey was founded in the 12th century. = 这座修道院建于12世纪。

PHRASE->
Westminster Abbey = 威斯敏斯特教堂
Down(ton) Abbey =  唐顿庄园 (指剧名，其中Abbey是庄园名)

近义词:
monastery, convent, priory, cloister
反义词:
None (There isn't a direct opposite of a physical place like an abbey.)
词性:
noun
