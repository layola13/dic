atake:/əˈteɪk/| n.| 痉挛，发作; (感情的)一阵，突发|A sudden, intense, and often uncontrollable feeling or reaction, such as an attack of laughter, fear, or pain.  A sudden onset of illness, especially one with spasms or convulsions.| He suffered an atake of nerves before the exam. 他在考试前一阵紧张。|近义词：fit, seizure, spasm, outburst, spell, attack|反义词：calm, peace|词性: noun

词源: From Middle English *atake, from a- + take (“to seize”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *takaną (“to take”).
