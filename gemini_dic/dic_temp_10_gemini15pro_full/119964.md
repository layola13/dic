millennialist:/ˌmɪˈlɛniəlɪst/| n. adj.|千禧年主义者；千禧年主义的|A person who believes in the doctrine of millennialism, especially that a golden age or paradise will occur on earth.|例句：The millennialist preacher spoke of the coming apocalypse.  千禧年主义的传教士谈到了即将到来的末日。

例句：She's a millennialist who believes in a literal thousand-year reign of Christ on Earth. 她是一位千禧年主义者，相信基督将在人间统治一千年。|近义词：chiliast, premillennialist (sometimes)|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly secularist, atheist)|词性:noun, adjective


millenarian:/ˌmɪˈlɛnəriən/| n. adj.|千禧年主义者；千禧年主义的|Relating to a millennium or to the belief that a golden age will occur on earth.|例句：Millenarian movements often emerge during times of social upheaval.  千禧年主义运动经常出现在社会动荡时期。

例句：He held a millenarian view of the future. 他对未来持有千禧年主义的观点。|近义词：chiliastic, millennialist|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly secularist, atheist)|词性:noun, adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *millenarius, from Latin mille "thousand."  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *mil- "thousand."
