disqualification:/dɪsˌkwɒlɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/ | n. |取消资格，不合格|The act of disqualifying someone or something, or the state of being disqualified.  The athlete faced disqualification for using performance-enhancing drugs. |运动员因使用兴奋剂而面临取消资格。| Synonyms: ineligibility, exclusion, rejection | Antonyms: qualification, eligibility | noun

disqualify:/dɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪ/ | vt. |取消…的资格，使不合格|To make someone or something ineligible for something. His criminal record disqualified him from holding public office.|他的犯罪记录使他没有资格担任公职。| Synonyms: bar, preclude, prohibit | Antonyms: qualify, enable, permit | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation, reversal) + qualify (from Latin qualis "of what sort" + facere "to make").  Literally, "to make not of the right sort."
