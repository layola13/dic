button-down:/ˈbʌtn daʊn/ (US), /ˈbʌtn daʊn/ (UK)| adj. n.|扣纽扣的；保守的；衬衫|A button-down shirt is one that has buttons holding down the collar points. Figuratively, it refers to a person or thing that is conventional, traditional, or conservative.|He wore a crisp, white button-down shirt to the interview. 他面试时穿了一件挺括的白色扣领衬衫。|近义词：conservative, traditional, conventional|反义词:unconventional, radical, informal|词性:形容词, 名词

button-down:/ˈbʌtn daʊn/ (US), /ˈbʌtn daʊn/ (UK)| n.|扣领衬衫；保守的人|A button-down shirt.  Figuratively, a person who is conventional and somewhat conservative.|He's a real button-down - always follows the rules. 他是个真正的保守派——总是循规蹈矩。|近义词：traditionalist, conformist|反义词:rebel, nonconformist|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrase "button down" (verb phrase), referring to the action of buttoning down the collar points of a shirt.  First used as an adjective in the early 20th century, then later as a noun.
