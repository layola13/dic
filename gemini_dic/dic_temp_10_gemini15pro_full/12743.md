assibilation:/əˌsɪbɪˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 齿擦音化；[语] 咝音化 |  The process by which a non-sibilant consonant becomes a sibilant, often due to the influence of a nearby sound.  This often involves sounds like /s/, /z/, /ʃ/, /ʒ/, /tʃ/, and /dʒ/. | Example 1: The pronunciation of "educate" as "edjucate" is an example of assibilation.  教育发音成"edjucate"是齿擦音化的一个例子。

Example 2: Assibilation can occur across word boundaries, such as pronouncing "is she" as "iz she". 齿擦音化可以跨越单词边界发生，例如将“is she”发音为“iz she”。 | 近义词: palatalization (腭音化), spirantization (擦音化) | 反义词: None | noun
