off-putting: /ˌɒfˈpʊtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˌɔːfˈpʊtɪŋ/ (US)
| adj. | 令人不快的，倒胃口的，扫兴的 | Unpleasant, discouraging, or repellent. | The smell of the food was quite off-putting.  食物的气味很倒胃口。 | Synonyms:  discouraging, repellent, distasteful, unpleasant, forbidding | Antonyms: attractive, appealing, inviting, pleasant | 词性: 形容词

词源:  "off-putting" comes from the phrasal verb "to put off," meaning to discourage or repel. The "-ing" suffix transforms the verb into a present participle, which can function as an adjective. Adding "-ly" creates the adverb form "off-puttingly."
