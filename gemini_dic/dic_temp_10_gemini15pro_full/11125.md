archduchess:/ɑːrtʃˈdʌtʃɪs/ | n. | 女大公 | The title held by the wife or widow of an archduke, or a woman of similar rank in her own right. | Maria Theresa was an Austrian archduchess. 玛丽亚·特蕾莎是奥地利女大公。 | 近义词: princess, noblewoman | 反义词: commoner | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *archeduchesse, from archeduc ("archduke") + -esse (feminine suffix), ultimately from Late Latin archidux, from Ancient Greek ἀρχιδούξ (arkhidoux, "chief leader"), from ἄρχω (arkhō, "to rule") + ἡγεμών (hēgemōn, "leader").
