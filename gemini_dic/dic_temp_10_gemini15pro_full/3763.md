agitate:/ˈædʒɪteɪt/| v. |煽动，搅动，使不安|To make someone worried or upset, or to make something move violently.|The news agitated her greatly. 这消息让她非常不安。|近义词：disturb, upset, stir, shake|反义词：calm, soothe, settle|verb

agitate:/ˈædʒɪteɪt/| v. |鼓动，煽动|To argue strongly in public for something you want, especially a political or social change.|They agitated for better working conditions. 他们为争取更好的工作条件而鼓动。|近义词：campaign, advocate, lobby|反义词：oppose, resist|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *agitatus, past participle of agitāre (“to drive, urge, move”), frequentative of Latin agere (“to do, drive, lead, act”).
