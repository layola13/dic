fabian:/ˈfeɪbiən/| adj. n.|费边式的，拖延时间的；费边主义者|Relating to or characteristic of the Fabian Society, advocating gradual rather than revolutionary social reform.  A member of the Fabian Society.|例句：He took a Fabian approach to the problem, preferring a slow and steady solution. 他对这个问题采取了费边主义式的策略，倾向于缓慢而稳妥的解决办法。

例句：The Fabians believed in gradual social reform through existing political systems. 费边主义者相信通过现有的政治体制逐步进行社会改革。|近义词：gradualist, cautious, deliberate|反义词：radical, revolutionary, impetuous|词性:形容词,名词


Etymology:
From Quintus Fabius Maximus Verrucosus, a Roman general who avoided decisive battles against Hannibal during the Second Punic War, instead using delaying tactics to wear down the enemy.  The Fabian Society adopted his name to reflect their strategy of gradual social reform.
