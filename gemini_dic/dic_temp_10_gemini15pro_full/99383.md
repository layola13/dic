interdict:/ˈɪntərˌdɪkt/| v. n.|禁止，阻止，禁令|To prohibit or forbid (something); to restrain or hinder (someone) from doing something.  An authoritative prohibition.|The government interdicted the sale of the drug. 政府禁止出售这种药物。|The papal interdict forbade the king from entering the church. 教皇的禁令禁止国王进入教堂。|Synonyms: prohibit, forbid, ban, proscribe|Antonyms: permit, allow, authorize|verb
interdict:/ˈɪntərˌdɪkt/| n.|禁止，阻止，禁令|An authoritative prohibition; a decree that forbids something.|The interdict against gambling was strictly enforced.  禁止赌博的禁令得到了严格执行。|The pope placed an interdict on the entire kingdom. 教皇对整个王国施加了禁令。|Synonyms: prohibition, ban, proscription|Antonyms: permission, allowance, authorization|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *interdictum*, noun use of neuter of past participle of Latin interdicere "to forbid, prohibit, interpose," from inter- "between" + dicere "to say, speak."
