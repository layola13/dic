nodding:/ˈnɒdɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈnɑːdɪŋ/ (US)| n. / adj.|点头，点头表示同意/ 点头的|The act of moving one's head up and down, typically to indicate agreement, understanding, or acknowledgment, or as a greeting.  Also used as an adjective to describe something that involves nodding.|

例句：
He showed his agreement with a slight nodding of his head.
他微微点头表示同意。

Her nodding indicated that she understood the instructions.
她的点头表示她理解了指示。


近义词：bowing, acknowledging, concurring|
反义词: shaking (one's head)|
词性: noun, adjective|


nod:/nɒd/ (UK) /nɑːd/ (US)| v. / n.|点头，打盹 / 点头|To move one's head up and down, typically to indicate agreement, understanding, or acknowledgment, or as a greeting. Also, to briefly fall asleep unintentionally. As a noun, it refers to the act of nodding.|

例句：
I nodded in agreement.
我点头表示同意。

She nodded off during the lecture.
她在讲座期间打瞌睡了。


近义词：bow, acknowledge, concur, doze|
反义词: shake (one's head)|
词性: verb, noun|


词源: From Old English *hnoddian, frequentative of hnigan "to bow, bend down," from Proto-Germanic *hnuganą.
