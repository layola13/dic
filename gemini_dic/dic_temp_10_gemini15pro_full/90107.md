hot-bloodedness:/ˌhɒtˈblʌdɪdnəs/ | n. | 热血，性急，冲动 | The state of being easily roused to strong feeling; passionate impulsiveness. | His hot-bloodedness often led him into trouble. 他的冲动经常给他带来麻烦。|  synonyms: fervor, passion, vehemence, ardor, impetuosity | antonyms: calmness, composure, coolness, level-headedness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From hot-blooded + -ness.  "Hot-blooded" itself comes from "hot" (referring to temperature and by extension, temperament) + "blood" (seen as the seat of emotions).  The concept of blood influencing temperament is ancient, dating back to the four humors theory of ancient Greece.
