candlewaster:/ˈkændlwɛɪstər/ | n. | 挥霍无度的人；坐吃山空的人；虚度光阴的人 | Someone who stays up late, originally implying burning candles unnecessarily, now more generally referring to someone who wastes time or resources. | He was a notorious candlewaster, staying up all night reading obscure books. 他是个臭名昭著的挥霍无度的人，整夜不睡读一些晦涩难懂的书。| 近义词：spendthrift, prodigal, idler | 反义词：economizer, saver | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From "candle" + "waster," literally meaning someone who wastes candles.  The term originated in a time when candles were a significant expense, so wasting them was considered extravagant.
