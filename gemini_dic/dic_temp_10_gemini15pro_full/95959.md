inculcators:/ɪnˈkʌlkeɪtərz/
|n.|灌输者；谆谆教诲者|A person who instills particular principles or ideas into others, especially by frequent repetition.|The inculcators of this new ideology have a strong influence on the youth.|近义词：teachers, instructors, mentors, educators|反义词：corruptors, misleaders|词性: noun


Etymology:
From Late Latin *inculcātor, agent noun from inculcāre  "to tread or stamp in; impress; instill," from in- "in" + calcāre "to tread," from calx (genitive calcis) "heel."
