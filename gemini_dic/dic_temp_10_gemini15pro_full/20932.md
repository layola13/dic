bleaters:/ˈbliːtərz/| n.|咩咩叫的动物（通常指羊）；爱抱怨的人|People or animals, typically sheep, that bleat.  It can also refer to people who complain a lot. |The farmer could hear the bleaters in the field. 农民能听到田野里羊的咩咩叫声。| The constant bleaters in the office were starting to annoy everyone. 办公室里不断抱怨的人开始惹恼每个人了。 |synonyms: complainers, whiners, sheep |antonyms: optimists, enthusiasts| noun


bleat:/bliːt/| v.|（羊等）咩咩叫；抱怨| To make the characteristic cry of a sheep or goat.  Figuratively, to complain in a weak, querulous way. |The lamb bleated for its mother. 小羊咩咩地叫着它的妈妈。|He's always bleating about how unfair life is. 他总是抱怨生活多么不公平。|synonyms: whine, complain, moan |antonyms: praise, celebrate| verb
