hikaru:/hiːkɑːruː/ | n. adj.| 光る, 闪耀, 辉煌 |  "Hikaru" is a Japanese word that primarily functions as a verb meaning "to shine," "to glitter," or "to sparkle." It can also be used as an adjective meaning "shining," "bright," or "brilliant," and less commonly as a given name, usually for males. It stems from the Old Japanese verb "pikaru," meaning "to glitter" or "to flash." | Example:  The stars hikaru brightly in the night sky. 星光在夜空中闪耀。| Synonym:  kagayaku (輝く), kirameku (煌めく),  teru (照る) | Antonym: kurai (暗い) | word type: verb, adjective, name
