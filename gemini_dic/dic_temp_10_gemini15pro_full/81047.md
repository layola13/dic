grafter: /ˈɡrɑːftər/ (UK), /ˈɡræftər/ (US) | n. | 嫁接者；辛苦工作的人；贪污受贿者 | Someone who works hard; a hard worker. Also can refer to someone who takes advantage of their position for personal gain, especially dishonestly. | He's a real grafter, always the first to arrive and the last to leave. 他是一个真正努力工作的人，总是第一个来最后一个走。

The councilor was exposed as a grafter, accepting bribes for planning permission. 这位议员被揭露是一个贪污受贿者，接受贿赂以换取规划许可。 | 近义词: worker, toiler, hustler, swindler (negative connotation) | 反义词: loafer, idler, slacker | 词性: 名词


graft: /ɡrɑːft/ (UK), /ɡræft/ (US) | n. | 嫁接；移植；辛苦的工作；贪污 |  The process of transplanting living tissue.  Hard work, especially manual labor.  Dishonest or illicit activity, especially involving bribery. | The surgeon performed a skin graft on the burn victim.  外科医生给烧伤病人做了皮肤移植手术。

She put in years of graft to build her business. 她投入了多年的辛勤工作来建立她的事业。

He got the job through graft and corruption. 他通过贪污腐败得到了这份工作。| 近义词: work, labor, toil, bribery, corruption | 反义词: idleness, leisure | 词性: 名词


graft: /ɡrɑːft/ (UK), /ɡræft/ (US)| v. | 嫁接；移植；努力工作 | To transplant living tissue. To work hard. |  The gardener grafted the apple tree branch onto the rootstock. 园丁将苹果树枝嫁接到砧木上。

He's been grafting away at his dissertation for months. 他几个月来一直在努力写他的论文。 | 近义词: transplant, work, toil | 反义词: rest, idle | 词性: 动词
