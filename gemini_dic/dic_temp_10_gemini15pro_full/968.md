acalephae: /ˌækəˈliːfiː/
noun
刺胞动物
A group of marine invertebrates including jellyfish, sea anemones, and corals, characterized by stinging cells called nematocysts.
Jellyfish and sea anemones are acalephae. 水母和海葵是刺胞动物。
synonyms: Cnidaria, coelenterates
antonyms: (No direct antonym, but could consider non-stinging invertebrates)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *akalēphē* (nettle), from *akalē* (nettle) + *haphē* (touch).  The name refers to the stinging sensation caused by contact with many members of this group.
