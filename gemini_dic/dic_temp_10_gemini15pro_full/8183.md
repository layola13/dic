anti-depressant:/ˌænti.dɪˈprɛsənt/ | n. adj. |抗抑郁药，抗抑郁的|A medication used to treat depression by affecting the levels of neurotransmitters in the brain.|She was prescribed an anti-depressant to help manage her symptoms. 她被开了抗抑郁药来帮助控制她的症状。|Synonym: anti-depressive |Antonym: depressant |noun, adjective


anti-depressant:/ˌænti.dɪˈprɛsənt/ | adj. |抗抑郁的|Having the property of relieving depression.|The anti-depressant effects of exercise are well-documented.  运动的抗抑郁效果已被充分证实。|Synonym: mood-lifting, mood-elevating |Antonym: depressing |adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- (prefix meaning "against, opposing") + depressant (from the verb "depress," meaning "to lower or reduce").  The term "depressant" in this context refers to the effect of depression on mood.
