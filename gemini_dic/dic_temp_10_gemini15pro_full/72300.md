flounder:/ˈflaʊndər/ (US), /ˈflʊndə(r)/ (UK)| n. vi.|比目鱼；挣扎；[例句1] The flounder is a common flatfish. 比目鱼是一种常见的扁平鱼。 [例句2]  He caught a large flounder while fishing. 他钓鱼时钓到了一条大比目鱼。|英文解析: A flatfish that lives on the sea floor. flounder:/ˈflaʊndər/ (US), /ˈflʊndə(r)/ (UK)| vi.|挣扎；不知所措；[例句1]  He floundered in the deep snow. 他在深雪中挣扎。 [例句2] She floundered for a moment, unsure how to answer the question. 她愣了一会儿，不知道该如何回答这个问题。|英文解析: To struggle clumsily or helplessly; to move with difficulty. Also, to be in a state of confusion or uncertainty.|近义词：struggle, thrash, wallow|反义词:succeed, thrive|词性:verb


词源: From Middle English *floundren, frequentative of flounden, "to plunge, flounder," probably of Scandinavian origin; related to Old Norse flytja, "to cause to float."
