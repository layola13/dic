impress:/ɪmˈprɛs/| v. n.|给…留下深刻印象；使…铭记；印记；印象|To have a strong, positive effect on someone's feelings or thoughts.|His knowledge of history impressed me. 他的历史知识给我留下了深刻的印象. |The trip left a lasting impress on my memory.  这次旅行在我的记忆中留下了持久的印象。|Synonyms: affect, influence, move, touch (v); mark, stamp, imprint (n)|Antonyms: bore, disappoint, repel (v); erase, obliterate (n)|verb, noun

Etymology:
From Old French *impreissier, from Latin imprimere (to press into, imprint), from in- (into) + premere (to press).
