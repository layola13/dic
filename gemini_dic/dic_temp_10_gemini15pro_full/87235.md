heresy:/ˈherəsi/ | n. |异端邪说；谬论|An opinion or belief that is against the principles of a particular religion or an established belief.|例句：He was burned at the stake for heresy. 他因异端邪说被处以火刑。|例句：The idea that the earth is flat is now considered heresy.  地球是平的这种观点现在被认为是谬论。|近义词：heterodoxy, dissent, unorthodoxy, blasphemy|反义词：orthodoxy, dogma|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *heresie*, from Late Latin *haeresis*, from Greek *haíresis* "choice," from *haireîsthai* "to choose."  Originally, the term simply meant "choice" or "school of thought."  Over time, it came to denote a choice or belief that deviated from established doctrine, particularly in religious contexts.
