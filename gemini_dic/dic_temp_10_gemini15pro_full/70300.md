fidgeting:/ˈfɪdʒɪtɪŋ/ | n. | 坐立不安，烦躁不安 | The act of making small, restless movements, especially with the hands and feet, typically as a sign of nervousness or boredom. |  He couldn't stop fidgeting during the interview.  他面试时不停地坐立不安。 | synonyms: restlessness, nervousness, fidgetiness | antonyms: calmness, stillness, composure | noun

fidget:/ˈfɪdʒɪt/ | v. | 烦躁，坐立不安 | To make small, restless movements, especially with the hands and feet, typically as a sign of nervousness or boredom. |  She fidgeted with her pen throughout the meeting.  她在整个会议期间一直摆弄她的笔。 | synonyms: fuss, twitch, squirm | antonyms: relax, calm down, be still | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Middle English *figeten*, frequentative of unknown origin.  Possibly related to  *fike*, meaning to move quickly or jerkily.
