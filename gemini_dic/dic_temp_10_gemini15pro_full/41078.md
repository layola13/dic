copperplate:/ˈkɒpəpleɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpərpleɪt/ (US)| n. adj.|铜版；铜版印刷；铜版字体|A copperplate is a polished sheet of copper upon which an image is incised by an engraver, using a burin or other sharp implement.  Copperplates are used in intaglio printmaking, where ink is rubbed into the incised lines and then wiped from the surface, leaving ink only in the lines. When paper is pressed onto the plate, the ink transfers, creating the printed image. The term also refers to the print made from such a plate, or to a style of handwriting resembling the elegant lettering of such prints.|The museum has a collection of antique copperplates. (博物馆收藏了古董铜版画。) |近义词：engraving, etching|反义词: None|noun
