anastomosis:/əˌnæstəˈməʊsɪs/ (US) /əˌnæstəˈmoʊsɪs/| n.|吻合；吻合术|A surgical connection between two parts, such as blood vessels or loops of the intestine.|The surgeon performed an anastomosis to bypass the blocked artery. 外科医生进行了吻合术以绕过阻塞的动脉。|Synonyms: connection, junction, union|Antonyms: separation, division|Noun

anastomosis:/əˌnæstəˈməʊsiːz/ (US) /əˌnæstəˈmoʊsiːz/| v.|吻合；连接|To surgically connect two parts, such as blood vessels or loops of the intestine.|The surgeon anastomosed the two ends of the severed intestine. 外科医生将断裂的肠道的两端吻合。|Synonyms: connect, join, unite|Antonyms: separate, divide|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin anastomosis, from Greek anastomōsis “outlet, opening,” from ana- “up, again” + stomoun “to provide with a mouth or opening.”
