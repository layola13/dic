methicillin:/ˌmɛθəˈsɪlɪn/| n. |甲氧西林|A semisynthetic form of penicillin that is resistant to breakdown by bacterial enzymes.|Methicillin was once used to treat staphylococcal infections. 甲氧西林曾经用于治疗葡萄球菌感染。|Synonyms:  Staphcillin (brand name) |Antonyms: None|Noun
Etymology-> From methoxy + benzylpenicillin.  Methoxy refers to the methoxy group (CH3O-) present in the molecule, and benzylpenicillin is a type of penicillin.
