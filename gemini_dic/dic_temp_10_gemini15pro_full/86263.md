heavens:/ˈhɛvənz/| n.|天国，天堂；天空|A literary or archaic term used to express surprise, disbelief, anger, or other strong emotions, or as a general intensifier.  It's essentially a more emphatic way of saying "the sky" in these contexts, invoking a sense of the divine or the vastness of the universe.  In modern usage, it's often considered somewhat dramatic or old-fashioned.|Heavens, what a mess! 天哪，真是一团糟！|Synonyms: sky, firmament, paradise,  Good heavens!, Good God!, Oh my God!, My goodness!|Antonyms: hell, underworld|noun
