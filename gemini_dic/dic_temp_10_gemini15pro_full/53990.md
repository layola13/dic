discriminate:/dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪt/| v.|歧视，区别，辨别|To recognize a distinction; differentiate. To make a distinction in favor of or against a person or thing on a basis other than individual merit.|She could discriminate between the various instruments of the orchestra. 她能分辨出管弦乐队的各种乐器。|近义词：differentiate, distinguish, separate|反义词: confuse, confound|词性: verb

discriminate:/dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪt/| adj.|歧视性的，有识别力的|Making or showing a distinction. Having or showing refined taste or good judgment.|He has a discriminate palate. 他有敏锐的味觉。|近义词： discerning, selective, judicious|反义词: indiscriminate, unselective|词性: adjective

discriminating:/dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ/| adj.|有识别力的，有鉴别力的|Having or showing refined taste or good judgment.|A discriminating palate. 有鉴赏力的味觉。|近义词： discerning, selective, judicious|反义词: indiscriminate, unselective|词性: adjective

discrimination:/dɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪʃn/| n.|歧视，区别，辨别力|The unjust or prejudicial treatment of different categories of people or things, especially on the grounds of race, age, or sex. The ability to recognize or draw fine distinctions.|Discrimination based on race is illegal. 基于种族的歧视是非法的。|近义词： prejudice, bias, bigotry|反义词: fairness, impartiality, equality|词性: noun

USAGE->
词源：From Latin discriminare "to divide, separate, distinguish," from discrimen "separation, distinction," from discernere "to distinguish between," from dis- "apart" + cernere "to distinguish."
