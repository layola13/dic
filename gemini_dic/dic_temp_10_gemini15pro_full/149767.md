palatine:/ˈpælətaɪn/ | adj. n. |腭的；腭音的；宫廷的；伯爵；拥有特权的人|Relating to the palate or roof of the mouth; of or relating to a palace;  a high-ranking official, especially in the historical Holy Roman Empire, having local authority.| The palatine tonsils are located at the back of the throat. 腭扁桃体位于喉咙后部。|Synonyms: palatal, courtly, aristocratic|Antonyms: common, plebeian|adjective

palatine:/ˈpælətaɪn/ | n. |伯爵；拥有特权的人|A high-ranking official, especially in the historical Holy Roman Empire, having local authority.| The Count Palatine held significant power in his region. 伯爵在其地区拥有相当大的权力。|Synonyms: nobleman, lord|Antonyms: commoner, peasant|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *palatīnus*, from Latin palātium (“palace”), from Palatium (“Palatine Hill, one of the seven hills of Rome”), possibly from Pales (“an ancient Roman deity of shepherds”).
