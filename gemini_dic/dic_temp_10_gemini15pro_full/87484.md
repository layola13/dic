heterauxesis:/ˌhɛtərɔːˈzɪksɪs/| n.|异速生长，异速发育|Differential growth rates of different parts of an organism or different stages in the life cycle, resulting in a change in proportion over time.|The heterauxesis of the fiddler crab's claw is a remarkable example of sexual dimorphism.|近义词: allometry, differential growth|反义词: isometric growth|词性:noun

Etymology: From Greek *heteros* ("other", "different") + *auxesis* ("growth", "increase").
