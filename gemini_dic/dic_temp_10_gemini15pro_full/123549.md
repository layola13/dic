monroe:/mənˈroʊ/ | n. | 门罗 |  Referring to the Monroe Doctrine, a U.S. foreign policy regarding domination of the American continents in 1823. It stated that further efforts by European nations to colonize land or interfere with states in North or South America would be viewed as acts of aggression, requiring U.S. intervention. | The Monroe Doctrine shaped U.S. foreign policy for centuries. 门罗主义塑造了美国几个世纪的外交政策。 | Synonym: (for the doctrine) policy, dogma | Antonym: (for the doctrine) cooperation, collaboration | noun
