betrayals:/bɪˈtreɪəlz/| n. |背叛，辜负|The act of betraying someone or something, such as a trust, a confidence, or a principle.  It refers to instances of disloyalty, treachery, or deception.|His betrayals of our trust have left us deeply hurt. 他对我们信任的背叛使我们深受伤害。|
|近义词：treacheries, disloyalties, perfidies, deceits|反义词:loyalties, fidelities|词性:noun|

betrayal:/bɪˈtreɪəl/| n. |背叛，辜负|The action of betraying one's country, a group, or a person; treachery.|Her betrayal of her best friend was unforgivable. 她对她最好的朋友的背叛是不可原谅的。|
|近义词：treachery, disloyalty, perfidy, deceit|反义词:loyalty, fidelity|词性:noun|

Etymology:
From Middle English *betrayal*, from Anglo-Norman *betraial*, from Old French *betraial*, from *betrair* ("to betray"), from *be-* + *trair* ("to hand over, deliver"), from Late Latin *tradere* ("to hand over, deliver"), from Latin *trans-* ("across") + *dare* ("to give").
