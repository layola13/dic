calculated:/ˈkælkjuleɪtɪd/| adj.|计算的；预先计划的；蓄意的|carefully planned or arranged, especially in order to achieve a particular effect|例句：It was a calculated insult. 这是故意的侮辱。|近义词：deliberate, intentional, planned, premeditated|反义词:unintentional, unplanned, accidental|词性:形容词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *calculatus, past participle of calculare (“to calculate, reckon”), from Latin calculus (“pebble, small stone used in reckoning”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kel- (“to strike, cut”).  The original meaning related to the use of pebbles for counting.
