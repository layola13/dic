commatism: /ˈkɒmətɪzəm/ (US) /ˈkɒmətɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |共栖，共栖现象|A symbiotic relationship between two organisms where one organism benefits and the other is neither harmed nor benefited.|The clownfish and the sea anemone exhibit commatism; the clownfish gains protection while the anemone is unaffected.|近义词:symbiosis, commensalism|反义词:parasitism, mutualism|词性:名词
词源: From Latin *commensalis* "eating at the same table", from *com-* "together" + *mensa* "table".
