depressant:/dɪˈprɛsənt/| n. adj.|抑制剂，镇静剂；令人沮丧的|A depressant is a substance that slows down the activity of the central nervous system. It can reduce anxiety, tension, and pain, but it can also cause drowsiness, confusion, and slowed breathing. As an adjective, it describes something that causes a feeling of sadness or hopelessness.|
Example (noun): Alcohol is a common depressant. 酒精是一种常见的抑制剂。

Example (adjective): The news was depressant.  这个消息令人沮丧。

|近义词:(n.) sedative, tranquilizer, downer; (adj.) disheartening, discouraging|反义词:(n.) stimulant; (adj.) uplifting, encouraging|词性: noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin depressant-, present participle stem of depressare "to press down," from Latin de- "down" + premere "to press."  Meaning "agent that reduces nervous or functional activity" is from 1875.
