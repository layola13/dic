moonshots:/ˈmuːnʃɒts/ (US) /ˈmuːnʃɒts/ (UK)| n. |登月，远大目标，雄心勃勃的项目|An ambitious and groundbreaking project, often involving a high degree of risk and aiming for a significant advancement.  Originally referring to the Apollo moon landing program, it is now used metaphorically to describe any challenging and innovative endeavor.|例句：The company's new electric car is their latest moonshot.  该公司的新款电动汽车是他们最新的雄心勃勃的项目。|近义词：ambitious projects, long shots, game-changers, breakthroughs|反义词:safe bets, incremental improvements|词性:名词

moonshot:/ˈmuːnʃɒt/ (US) /ˈmuːnʃɒt/ (UK)| adj. |极具挑战性的，雄心勃勃的|Describing something that is extremely ambitious and groundbreaking.|例句：The CEO announced a series of moonshot goals for the next decade. 首席执行官宣布了未来十年的系列雄心勃勃的目标。|近义词：ambitious, groundbreaking, audacious|反义词:conservative, cautious|词性:形容词
