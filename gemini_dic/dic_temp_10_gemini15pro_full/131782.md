nonconnivance:/ˌnɑːnkəˈnaɪvəns/ | n. | 不纵容；不默许；不同谋；不串通 | Lack of collusion or secret cooperation, especially in wrongdoing. |  The auditor's report attested to the company's nonconnivance in the fraudulent scheme.  审计师的报告证明了该公司没有参与这起欺诈计划。

The prosecutor stressed the defendant's nonconnivance in the crime.  检察官强调被告没有参与犯罪。 | 近义词: noncomplicity, noncooperation, innocence | 反义词: connivance, complicity, collusion | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->

From non- + connivance.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Connivance" comes from the Old French word "conivance," meaning "to wink at" or "to pretend not to see."  Ultimately, it derives from the Latin "connivēre," formed from "com-" (with) and "nivēre" (to wink).
