garnished:/ˈɡɑːrnɪʃt/| adj.| 装饰的，加配料的 |Decorated or embellished, especially food, with something to add flavor or attractiveness.| The dish was garnished with fresh parsley. 这道菜用新鲜的欧芹装饰。| 近义词: decorated, adorned, trimmed | 反义词: plain, undecorated | 词性:形容词
garnish:/ˈɡɑːrnɪʃ/| n. vt.| 装饰物，配菜；装饰，加配料 |Something used to decorate or embellish something else, especially food; to decorate or embellish something, especially food.| The chef used a lemon twist as a garnish. 厨师用柠檬皮作为装饰。 / He garnished the fish with lemon slices. 他用柠檬片装饰了鱼。| 近义词: decoration, adornment, trimming | 反义词: none (for noun); strip, denude (for verb) | 词性:名词, 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *garniss-, stem of garnir "to furnish, provide, equip, defend, garnish," from Frankish *warnjan or some other Germanic source, from Proto-Germanic *warnjaną "to provide, take care of," related to Old English warnian "to warn, take heed," from PIE root *wer- (4) "to perceive, watch out for."
