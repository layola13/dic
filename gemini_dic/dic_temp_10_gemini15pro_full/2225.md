addressers:/əˈdrɛsɚz/
noun
演讲者，致辞者，写信人
People who give speeches or write letters are addressers.  Those who deliver a formal speech are often referred to as addressers.
The addressers at the conference captivated the audience.  大会上的演讲者吸引了观众。

The addressers of the petition demanded immediate action.  请愿书的提交者要求立即采取行动。


近义词:speakers, orators, writers, correspondents
反义词:audience, listeners, recipients
词性:n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "to address," which comes from the Old French "adresser," meaning "to direct, to turn towards." Ultimately from the Latin "ad" (to) + "directus" (straight).


USAGE->
The addressers delivered powerful speeches. 演讲者发表了强有力的演讲。
Many addressers at the conference were experts in their field.  许多与会发言者都是各自领域的专家。
