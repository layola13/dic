euchromatin:/ˌjuːˈkroʊməˌtɪn/ | n. | 常染色质 | A lightly packed form of chromatin (DNA, RNA, and protein) that is rich in gene concentration, and is often (but not always) under active transcription. | Example: Euchromatin is more loosely packed than heterochromatin, allowing for easier access by the transcriptional machinery.  常染色质比异染色质更松散地堆积，使转录机制更容易接近。 | Example:  The presence of euchromatin suggests that the cell is actively transcribing genes in that region. 常染色质的存在表明细胞正在积极转录该区域的基因。 | Synonyms: active chromatin | Antonyms: heterochromatin | Part of Speech: noun
