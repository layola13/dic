matriarchate: /ˈmeɪtriɑːrkeɪt/ | n. | 母系社会; 女族长制 | A social system in which women, especially mothers, have the central roles of political leadership, moral authority, and control of property. |  The island nation was a matriarchate, with all major decisions made by the queen mother.  这个岛国是一个母系社会，所有主要决定都由王太后做出。

The anthropologist studied the matriarchate and its impact on family structures.  这位人类学家研究了母系社会及其对家庭结构的影响。 | 近义词: matriarchy | 反义词: patriarchy | 词性: 名词


matriarch: /ˈmeɪtriɑːrk/ | n. | 女族长; 女家长 | A woman who is the head of a family or tribe. |  My grandmother, the matriarch of our family, always hosts Thanksgiving dinner. 我的祖母，我们家的女家长，总是主持感恩节晚餐。

The matriarch ruled her clan with wisdom and compassion.  这位女族长以智慧和同情心统治着她的家族。 | 近义词: matron | 反义词: patriarch | 词性: 名词
