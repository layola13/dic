direptitiously:/ˌdaɪrəpˈtɪʃəsli/
adverb
偷偷地；暗中地
in a way that is intended to avoid being noticed; secretly.
She direptitiously slipped the note into his pocket. 她偷偷地把纸条塞进了他的口袋。
secretly, stealthily, surreptitiously, clandestinely, covertly
openly, publicly, blatantly

Etymology:
From Latin *dīreptus, past participle of dīripere (“to plunder, snatch away”), from dis- (“apart, away”) + rapere (“to seize, snatch”).
