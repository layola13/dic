overspicing: /ˌoʊvərˈspaɪsɪŋ/
verb (present participle) | 过度调味；加太多香料 | The act of adding too much spice to food, resulting in a dish that is too pungent or flavorful. |  I think I ruined the curry by overspicing it. 我想我加太多香料，把咖喱毁了。| overseasoning | underspicing | v.

overspicing: /ˌoʊvərˈspaɪsɪŋ/
noun | 过度调味；加太多香料 | The condition of having too much spice added, resulting in an excessively strong flavor. | The overspicing of the dish made it inedible. 这道菜的过度调味使它无法入口。| overseasoning | underspicing | n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + spicing, the present participle of spice.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much". "Spice" comes from Old French "espice," ultimately from Latin "species."
