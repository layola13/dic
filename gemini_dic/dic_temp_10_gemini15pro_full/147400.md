overnoble:/ˌoʊvərˈnoʊbəl/
adjective
过分高贵的，矫揉造作的
Making (someone or something) seem nobler than is actually the case; excessively noble in sentiment or expression.
He gave an overnoble speech about the virtues of hard work, which felt a bit out of place given his inherited wealth. 他发表了一篇关于勤奋美德的过分高贵的演讲，鉴于他的财富是继承的，这感觉有点不合适。
His overnoble gesture of donating a small sum felt insincere. 他捐出一小笔钱的过分高贵的姿态让人感觉不真诚。
近义词:pretentious, affected, pompous
反义词:humble, modest, unassuming
词性:adjective

词源:
From over- + noble.  "Over-" acts as a prefix indicating "excessively" or "too much." "Noble" comes from Old French, ultimately from Latin nobilis "well-known, famous, high-born, excellent."
