adamants:/ˈædəmænts/ | n. | 坚固无比的东西；坚定不移的人 | Something extremely hard or unyielding; a person who is utterly unyielding in attitude or opinion. |  He was adamant in his refusal to apologize. 他坚决拒绝道歉。

The coach was adamant that the team needed more practice. 教练坚持认为球队需要更多的练习。 | 近义词:inflexible, unyielding, resolute, firm, determined | 反义词:flexible, yielding, irresolute, wavering | 词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *adamant* (Modern French *aimant*, "magnet") and Latin *adamantem* (nominative *adamans*), from Ancient Greek *ἀδάμας* (*adámas*, "untamed, invincible, hard steel, diamond").  Originally referred to the hardest substance known, often associated with diamonds or magnets.  The figurative meaning of "unyielding" developed later.
