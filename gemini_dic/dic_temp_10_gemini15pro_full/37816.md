compensatingly:/ˌkɒmpənˈseɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 补偿地，补充地 | In a way that compensates for something else; so as to offset or counterbalance something. |  He worked compensatingly harder after his illness. 他在病后加倍努力工作以弥补损失的时间。|  She spoke softly, compensatingly, after her outburst.  在她爆发之后，她轻声细语地说着，以示弥补。 | synonym:  correctively, counteractively, remedially | antonym: detrimentally, harmfully | Part of Speech: adverb

compensate:/ˈkɒmpənseɪt/ | v. | 补偿，弥补 | Give (someone) something, typically money, in recognition of loss, suffering, or injury incurred; reduce or counteract (something unwelcome or unpleasant) by exerting an opposite force or effect. | The company will compensate you for your losses. 公司将赔偿你的损失。|  Her enthusiasm compensates for her lack of experience. 她的热情弥补了她经验的不足。| synonym:  recompense, repay, offset | antonym:  fail to reward, penalize | Part of Speech: verb

compensation:/ˌkɒmpənˈseɪʃən/ | n. | 补偿，赔偿 | Something, typically money, awarded to someone as a recompense for loss, injury, or suffering. |  She received compensation for the damage to her car. 她得到了汽车损坏的赔偿。| He asked for no compensation for his services. 他没有要求任何服务报酬。 | synonym:  recompense, repayment, restitution | antonym: penalty, forfeiture | Part of Speech: noun
