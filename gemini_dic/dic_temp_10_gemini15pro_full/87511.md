heterodactylae:/ˌhɛtərəˈdæktɪliː/
n.
异趾鸟类
A group of birds characterized by having the outer toe reversed, enabling them to climb trees.
The heterodactylae, such as woodpeckers, use their specialized feet for climbing. 异趾鸟类，例如啄木鸟，用它们特化的脚来攀爬。
zygodactyl, anisodactyl

noun
