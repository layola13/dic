cacodyl: /ˈkækədɪl/ (US), /kəˈkɒdɪl/ (UK) | n. | 二甲胂基;  卡可基 | A poisonous, spontaneously flammable, oily liquid with an intensely unpleasant smell, (CH₃)₂As–As(CH₃)₂.  It is the simplest organoarsenic compound. |  The chemist noted the repulsive odor of cacodyl.  化学家注意到二甲胂基令人作呕的气味。| Synonyms: dicacodyl, tetramethyldiarsine | Antonyms: N/A | noun
