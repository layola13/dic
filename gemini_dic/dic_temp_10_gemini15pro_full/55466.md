distorted:/dɪˈstɔːrtɪd/| adj.|扭曲的，变形的，歪曲的|pulled or twisted out of shape; misrepresented; altered from the truth or original|例句：The loud music distorted the sound of her voice. 喧闹的音乐使她的声音失真了。|例句：His account of the events was completely distorted. 他对事件的描述完全是歪曲的。|近义词：twisted, warped, deformed, misrepresented, falsified|反义词：accurate, true, undistorted, clear|词性:形容词

distort:/dɪˈstɔːrt/| vt.|扭曲，变形，歪曲|to pull or twist out of shape; to give a false or misleading account of; to change the form of something, especially sound or image, so that it is no longer clear or true|例句：The heat distorted the image on the television screen.  高温使电视屏幕上的图像变形了。|例句：The media distorted the facts about the incident. 媒体歪曲了该事件的事实。|近义词：twist, warp, deform, misrepresent, falsify|反义词：clarify, represent accurately|词性:动词

词源: From Latin distortus, past participle of distorquere "to twist different ways," from dis- "apart" + torquere "to twist."
