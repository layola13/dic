nabobs:/ˈneɪbɒbz/| n. | 巨富，权贵 | A person of great wealth or importance, especially one who acquired it in India under the British Raj. |  The nabobs of Silicon Valley are known for their extravagant lifestyles. 硅谷的巨富们以他们奢华的生活方式而闻名。

The nabobs of the oil industry held a lavish party. 石油行业的巨头们举行了一个奢华的派对。 | 近义词：magnate, tycoon, plutocrat | 反义词：pauper, commoner | 词性: Noun

Etymology: From Hindi nawāb, from Urdu nawwāb, from Arabic nuwwāb (plural of nā’ib, "deputy"). Originally referred to a Mughal governor, later to any person of high status, and eventually to any wealthy or influential person.
