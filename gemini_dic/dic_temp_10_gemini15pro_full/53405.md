disambiguated:/dɪsæmˈbɪɡjueɪtɪd/ | adj. | 消除歧义的，明确的 |  Made clear or precise, having any ambiguity removed.  | The meaning of the sentence was disambiguated by the context.  这句话的意思通过上下文得到了明确。 | clarified, specified, distinguished | ambiguous, unclear | adjective

disambiguate:/dɪsæmˈbɪɡjueɪt/ | vt. | 消除歧义，使明确 | To remove uncertainty or ambiguity from the meaning of something. | The author disambiguated the passage by adding a footnote. 作者通过添加脚注消除了这段文字的歧义。| clarify, specify, distinguish | confuse, obscure | verb

disambiguation:/ˌdɪsæmˌbɪɡjuˈeɪʃn/ | n. | 消除歧义，歧义消除 | The act of removing ambiguity or uncertainty. |  The disambiguation of the term was crucial to understanding the contract.  对该术语的消除歧义对于理解合同至关重要。| clarification, specification | confusion, obscuring | noun


词源：
dis- (prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of") + ambiguous (having more than one possible meaning) + -ate (verb suffix) / -ed (past participle suffix) / -ion (noun suffix)
