legalist:/ˈliːɡəlɪst/| n.|法家;墨守成规者;拘泥法律或规则的人|A person who believes in or adheres strictly to the law or rules, especially to the exclusion of other considerations.|The legalist argued that the contract must be followed to the letter, regardless of the unforeseen circumstances.|Synonym: stickler, formalist, rule-follower|Antonym: anarchist, rebel, nonconformist|Noun

legalistic:/ˌliːɡəˈlɪstɪk/| adj.|法家的；法律的；拘泥法律条文的|Relating to or characteristic of legalism; excessively concerned with the letter of the law.|His legalistic approach to the problem alienated many of his colleagues.|Synonym: by-the-book, rigid, inflexible|Antonym: flexible, lenient, understanding|Adjective
