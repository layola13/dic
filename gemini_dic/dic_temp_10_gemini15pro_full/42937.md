covenant:/ˈkʌvənənt/| n.|契约，盟约，公约|A formal agreement or promise between two or more parties, especially one that involves religious or legal obligations.|例句：They signed a covenant to protect the environment. 他们签署了一项保护环境的契约。

例句：The ancient covenant between God and Abraham. 上帝和亚伯拉罕之间的古老盟约。|近义词：agreement, contract, pact, treaty|反义词：disagreement, breach|词性: noun

covenant:/ˈkʌvənənt/| v.|立约，订立契约|To agree by lease, deed, or other legal contract.|例句：The landlord covenanted to repair the roof. 房东约定修理屋顶。

例句：They covenanted with each other to keep the secret. 他们彼此约定保守秘密。|近义词：pledge, promise, contract|反义词：renege, break|词性: verb
