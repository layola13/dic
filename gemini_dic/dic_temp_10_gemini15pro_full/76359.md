gadfly:/ˈɡædˌflaɪ/| n. |牛虻；讨厌的人；令人讨厌的人|A gadfly is a fly that bites livestock, especially cattle. Figuratively, a gadfly is a person who annoys or criticizes others in order to provoke them into action or change.|The journalist was a gadfly, constantly challenging the government's policies.|近义词：nuisance, pest, irritant, provocateur|反义词:conformist, yes-man|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *gaðfluga (“gadfly”), from gaðr (“goad”) + fluga (“fly”).  The figurative sense developed from the insect's habit of irritating cattle.
