mitigatedly:/ˈmɪtɪɡeɪtɪdli/
| adv. | 减轻地，缓和地 | Less intensely; to a mitigated degree. | The news was mitigatedly positive, suggesting some improvement but not a complete recovery.  消息略微正面，表明有所改善，但尚未完全恢复。|例句：He smiled mitigatedly, acknowledging the compliment but not wanting to appear overly proud. 他淡淡一笑，表示接受了赞美，但不想显得过于骄傲。| 近义词：slightly, moderately, partially, somewhat | 反义词：severely, intensely, extremely | 词性: 副词

mitigatedly:/ˈmɪtɪɡeɪtɪdli/
| adv. | 减轻地，缓和地 | In a way that reduces the harmful effects of something. | The impact of the recession was mitigatedly felt in the tech sector due to increased demand for online services. 由于对在线服务的需求增加，科技行业受到的经济衰退的影响有所减轻。|例句：She spoke mitigatedly about her difficult experiences, sparing the listeners the full details. 她轻描淡写地讲述了自己的艰难经历，没有向听众透露全部细节。|近义词：mildly, gently, temperately | 反义词：harshly, severely, intensely | 词性: 副词
