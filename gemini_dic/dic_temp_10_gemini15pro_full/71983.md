flinch:/flɪntʃ/| vi.|畏缩，退缩， flinch 表示一种短暂的、不由自主的退缩或躲避动作，通常是由于害怕、疼痛或惊讶引起的。|She flinched at the sudden loud noise. 她被突如其来的巨响吓了一跳，畏缩了一下。|Synonyms: wince, recoil, shrink, cringe|Antonyms: brave, confront, face|verb

Etymology->
From Old French *flenchir, from Frankish *flinken "to bend, turn aside," from Proto-Germanic *flankiz "flank, side."  The original sense was "to turn aside, shy away," later developing the meaning of "to shrink back from pain or fear."
