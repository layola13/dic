ladino:/ləˈdinoʊ/ /læˈdɪːnoʊ/
n.|拉迪诺语；狡猾的人；拉丁美洲混血人|A Romance language derived from Old Spanish, spoken by Sephardic Jews expelled from Spain and Portugal in the late 15th century. Also, a cunning or crafty person.  Sometimes used to refer to a person of mixed Spanish and Native American descent in Latin America.|He spoke Ladino fluently, a testament to his Sephardic heritage. 他能说一口流利的拉迪诺语，这证明了他的西班牙系犹太人血统。|Synonyms: Sephardic, shrewd, astute, wily|Antonyms: naive, ingenuous, honest, straightforward|noun

ladino:/ləˈdinoʊ/ /læˈdɪːnoʊ/
adj.|狡猾的；奸诈的|Cunning, crafty, or shrewd.|He used ladino tactics to win the negotiation. 他用了狡猾的策略赢得了谈判。|Synonyms: cunning, sly, artful, deceitful|Antonyms: honest, straightforward, ingenuous, artless|adjective
