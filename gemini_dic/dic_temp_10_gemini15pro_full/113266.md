lusts:/lʌsts/ | n. | 强烈的欲望；贪欲；色欲 | Intense desires, especially for something forbidden or excessive; strong cravings; sexual desires. |  He was consumed by lusts for power and wealth. 他被对权力和财富的渴望所吞噬。| synonyms: desires, cravings, passions, yearnings, appetites, hungers | antonyms: aversion, distaste, indifference, restraint, moderation | noun

lusts:/lʌsts/ | v. | 渴望；贪求 | To have a strong desire for something, especially something considered wrong or excessive. | She lusted after the expensive jewelry in the shop window. 她渴望橱窗里昂贵的珠宝。 | synonyms: crave, covet, yearn for, hunger for, thirst for | antonyms: abhor, despise, loathe, dislike, shun | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lustes*, plural of *luste*, from Old English *lust* (“pleasure, delight, enjoyment, desire, lust”), from Proto-Germanic *lustiz* (“desire, pleasure”), from Proto-Indo-European *leudh-* (“to grow, sprout forth”).  Cognate with Old High German *lust* (“pleasure, desire”), Old Norse *lyst* (“desire, pleasure”).
