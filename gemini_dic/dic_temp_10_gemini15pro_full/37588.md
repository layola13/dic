commune:/ˈkɒmjuːn/ (UK) /kəˈmjuːn/ (US)| n. |公社；集体；（尤指法国的）市镇|A group of people living together and sharing possessions and responsibilities.|Example:  She lived in a commune in the 1970s. 她七十年代住在公社里。

commune:/ˈkɒmjuːn/ (UK) /kəˈmjuːn/ (US)| v. |亲密交谈；交流；感觉与…融为一体|To communicate with someone or something in a spiritual way.|Example:  He went to the mountains to commune with nature. 他去山上与大自然交流。

commune:/ˈkɒmjuːn/ (UK) /kəˈmjuːn/ (US)| n. |市镇; (尤指法国、比利时、意大利等国的) |The smallest administrative division in several European countries, particularly France, Belgium, and Italy.|Example:  The commune is responsible for local services. 这个市镇负责当地的服务。


近义词:(noun) community, collective, kibbutz; (verb) connect, interact, communicate
反义词: (noun) individual, separation


词性: noun, verb

词源: From Late Latin communia, neuter plural of communis ‘common’.
