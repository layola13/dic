hoarser:/ˈhɔːrsər (US), ˈhɔːsə(r) (UK)| adj.|更嘶哑的；更刺耳的|Comparative form of hoarse: having a rough or harsh voice, typically caused by a sore throat or shouting.|例句：Her voice was hoarser than usual after the concert.  演唱会后，她的声音比平时更嘶哑了。

例句：He woke up feeling hoarser than a frog. 他醒来时感觉嗓子比青蛙还嘶哑。|近义词：rougher, raspier, huskier, scratchier|反义词：clearer, smoother, softer|词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *hors, hars, from Old English hās, from Proto-Germanic *haisaz, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱeHs- (“to cough, clear the throat”). Cognate with Scots hoarse (“hoarse”), West Frisian hears (“hoarse”), Dutch hees (“hoarse”), German heiser (“hoarse”), Danish hæs (“hoarse”), Swedish hes (“hoarse”).
