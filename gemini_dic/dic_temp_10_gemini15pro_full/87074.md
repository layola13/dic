heptarchy:/ˈhɛptɑːrki/| n.|七国时代；七人统治|A period in English history, traditionally dated from the late 5th to the mid-9th century, when England was divided into seven kingdoms.|The heptarchy was a time of constant warfare and shifting alliances.|近义词:oligarchy|反义词:monarchy|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *heptarchia, from Ancient Greek ἑπτά (heptá, “seven”) + ἀρχία (arkhía, “rule”).
