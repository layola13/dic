deconsecrated: /ˌdiːkɒnˈsɪkreɪtɪd/ (British) /ˌdiːkɑːnˈsɪkreɪtɪd/ (American)
adjective
已废弃神圣性的；不再神圣的
Deprived of its sacred character; no longer consecrated.
The deconsecrated church was converted into apartments.  这座已废弃神圣性的教堂被改建成了公寓。
The deconsecrated graveyard was now a public park. 这块不再神圣的墓地现在成了一个公园。
synonyms: unconsecrated, desacralized, secularized, profane
antonyms: consecrated, sacred, hallowed
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- (prefix meaning "remove, reverse") + consecrate (from Latin consecratus, past participle of consecrare "to make sacred, dedicate, devote," from com- "together" + sacrare "to make sacred").
