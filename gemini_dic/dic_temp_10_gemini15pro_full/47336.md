de-shell:/diːˈʃɛl/
verb
去壳，脱壳
To remove the shell or outer covering from something, especially nuts, shellfish, or eggs.
The machine can de-shell peanuts quickly and efficiently.  这台机器可以快速高效地去壳花生。
Workers were employed to de-shell the prawns. 工人们被雇来剥虾壳。
近义词: shell, husk, peel, shuck
反义词: shell
词性: verb

de-shell:/diːˈʃɛl/
verb
(比喻) 剥夺保护；使脆弱
To remove the protective outer layer of something, leaving it vulnerable.
The new regulations will de-shell consumers and leave them exposed to fraud. 新的规定将剥夺消费者的保护，使他们容易受到欺诈。
The recession has de-shelled many small businesses, leaving them struggling to survive. 经济衰退使许多小企业失去了保护，使它们难以生存。
近义词: expose, weaken, undermine
反义词: protect, strengthen, fortify
词性: verb
