obstancy: /ˈɒbstɪnəsi/ (UK) /ˈɑːbstɪnənsi/ (US)| n. |固执，顽固，执拗|The quality of being unreasonably determined to act in a particular way and refuse to change one's mind.|His obstinacy in pursuing the case made him unpopular.|Synonyms: stubbornness, pertinacity, tenacity, inflexibility, doggedness, persistence|Antonyms: compliance, acquiescence, submissiveness|noun

obstinate: /ˈɒbstɪnət/ (UK) /ˈɑːbstɪnət/ (US)| adj. |固执的，顽固的，执拗的|Stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action, despite attempts to persuade one to do so.|He remained obstinate in his refusal to apologize.|Synonyms: stubborn, headstrong, willful, unyielding, inflexible, intractable|Antonyms: compliant, acquiescent, submissive|adjective
