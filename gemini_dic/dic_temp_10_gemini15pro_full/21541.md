bluecoat: /ˈbluːkoʊt/ (American) /ˈbluːkəʊt/ (British)| n.|蓝衣人；（旧时）慈善学校的学生；警察|A bluecoat traditionally refers to a person wearing a blue coat, particularly a policeman or a student of a charity school.  It can also be used metaphorically to represent authority or law enforcement. |例句：The bluecoats arrived quickly at the scene of the accident.  警察迅速到达了事故现场。

例句：In the 18th century, bluecoats were a common sight in London's streets. 在18世纪，蓝衣人是伦敦街头常见的景象。|近义词：policeman, officer, constable (for police); student, pupil (for charity school students)|反义词: civilian, criminal|词性:noun
