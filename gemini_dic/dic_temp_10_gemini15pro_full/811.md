abstaining:/əbˈsteɪnɪŋ/| v.| 节制；戒绝；弃权|The act of refraining from something, typically alcohol or other potentially harmful substances, or from voting.|He is abstaining from alcohol for his health.  他为了健康戒酒了。

She abstained from voting on the new policy. 她对新政策投了弃权票。|近义词：refraining, forbearing, declining|反义词：indulging, partaking|词性：动词(现在分词)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English abstenen, from Old French astenir, from Latin abstinēre (“to hold back, keep away from”), from abs- (“from”) + tenēre (“to hold”).

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He abstains from smoking. = 他戒烟。
She abstains from voting in most elections. = 她在大多数选举中都弃权。

一般过去时:
They abstained from eating meat during Lent. = 他们在四旬斋期间戒肉。
He abstained from voting on the controversial bill. = 他对这项有争议的法案投了弃权票。


现在完成时:
She has been abstaining from sugar for a month. = 她已经戒糖一个月了。
He has abstained from commenting on the case. = 他一直避免评论这个案件。


过去完成时:
He had been abstaining from alcohol for years before he relapsed. = 他在复发之前已经戒酒多年了。
She had abstained from voting in the past, but this time she felt compelled to participate. = 她过去弃权过，但这次她觉得有必要参与。


将来时:
I will be abstaining from caffeine for the next few weeks. = 我接下来的几周将戒掉咖啡因。
They will abstain from voting if their demands are not met. = 如果他们的要求得不到满足，他们将弃权。


虚拟语气:
If I were abstaining from sugar, I would lose weight. = 如果我戒糖，我就会减肥。
I wish I were abstaining from social media. = 我希望我能戒掉社交媒体。


被动语态 (不适用, abstaining是主动行为)


疑问句:
Are you abstaining from alcohol? = 你在戒酒吗？
Why are they abstaining from voting? = 他们为什么弃权？


否定句:
He is not abstaining from alcohol. = 他没有戒酒。
She did not abstain from voting. = 她没有弃权。



PHRASE->
abstaining from alcohol = 戒酒
abstaining from voting = 弃权
abstaining from red meat = 戒红肉
abstaining from smoking = 戒烟
abstaining from sweets = 戒甜食
