ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *likerous, from Old English liccian (“to lick”) + -erous, from Anglo-Norman -ous, -us, from Old French -eux, -eus, from Latin -ōsus.  The original sense was "fond of licking, eager to lap up," which evolved to mean "greedy" and later acquired sexual connotations.
