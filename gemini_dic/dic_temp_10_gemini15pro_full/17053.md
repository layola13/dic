bathos:/ˈbeɪθɒs/ (UK), /ˈbeɪθɑːs/ (US)| n. | 平庸；陈腐；突降法 | A sudden descent from the sublime or serious to the ridiculous or trivial.  A decline from a higher to a lower emotional point.  Sometimes used unintentionally, resulting in anticlimax; other times used intentionally for comedic or satiric effect.| The epic poem ended with the hero tripping over a cat, a moment of pure bathos. 这首史诗以英雄被一只猫绊倒而告终，真是太滑稽了。| Synonyms: anticlimax, triteness, platitude, banality | Antonyms: sublimity, grandeur, loftiness | noun


bathoses: /ˈbeɪθɒsiːz/ (UK), /ˈbeɪθɑːsiːz/ (US)| n. | 平庸；陈腐；突降法（复数） |  The plural form of "bathos." | The play was full of bathoses, shifting awkwardly between tragedy and farce. 这部戏充满了突降，在悲剧和闹剧之间尴尬地转换。 | Synonyms: anticlimaxes, tritenesses, platitudes, banalities | Antonyms: sublimities, grandeurs, loftinesses | noun
