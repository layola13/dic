cooling:/ˈkuːlɪŋ/ | n. adj.| 冷却，降温; 凉爽的，清凉的 |The process of becoming or causing something to become less hot.  Also used to describe something that has a cooling effect. | The cooling breeze felt refreshing on a hot day.  炎热的一天里，凉爽的微风让人感到神清气爽。 | synonyms: chilling, refrigerating | antonyms: heating, warming | noun/adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *coling*, present participle of *colen* (“to cool”), from Old English *cōlian* (“to cool, become cool”), from Proto-Germanic *kōlijaną* (“to cool”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵelh₂- (“cold, to freeze”).  Cognate with West Frisian *koelje* (“to cool”), Dutch *koelen* (“to cool”), German *kühlen* (“to cool”), Danish *køle* (“to cool”), Swedish *kyla* (“to cool”).  The adjective is a present participle used adjectivally.
