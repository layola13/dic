haskalah:/ˈhɑːskələ/; /hɑːsˈkɑːlə/ | n. | 启蒙运动;犹太启蒙运动 | The Haskalah, or Jewish Enlightenment, was an intellectual movement among the Jews of Central and Eastern Europe, with certain influence in Western Europe and Muslim lands, that took place in the 1770s to the 1880s. The Haskalah pursued two complementary aims: it sought to preserve the Jews as a separate, collective entity and to cultivate a sense of civic belonging to the societies in which they lived; | The Haskalah emphasized rational thought and secular learning. | 近义词: Enlightenment, Jewish Enlightenment | 反义词: Hasidism (in some contexts) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Hebrew הַשְׂכָּלָה‎ (maskalah, “enlightenment, education”), from the verb שִׂכֵּל‎ (sikkel, “to have insight, to understand, to be prudent”), from the root שׂ־כ־ל‎.
