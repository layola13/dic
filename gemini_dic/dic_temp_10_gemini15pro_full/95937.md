incubating: /ˈɪŋkjubeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. v. | 孵化的，培养的，潜伏的 | developing and growing; fostering or supporting the development of something; (of a disease) present but not yet showing symptoms |  The project is still incubating and not ready for launch.  这个项目仍在孵化阶段，尚未准备好发布。 | synonyms: developing, gestating, nurturing, fostering | antonyms: stagnating, declining, suppressing | 词性: adjective, present participle of incubate

incubate: /ˈɪŋkjubeɪt/ | v. | 孵化，培养，潜伏 | to sit on (eggs) in order to hatch them; to maintain (something, such as bacteria or a cell culture) under controlled conditions suitable for growth; to develop slowly and secretly | The birds incubate their eggs for about two weeks.  这些鸟孵蛋大约两周。 | synonyms: hatch, breed, cultivate, foster | antonyms: neglect, stifle, suppress | 词性: verb
