levity: /ˈlevəti/ | n. | 轻浮；轻率；不严肃；轻佻 |  A lack of seriousness, especially during a serious occasion; frivolity; lightness of manner or speech.  |  He injected a note of levity into the proceedings with a humorous anecdote. 他用一个幽默的轶事给会议增添了一丝轻松气氛。| Synonyms: frivolity, flippancy, lightheartedness, gaiety | Antonyms: gravity, seriousness, solemnity, sobriety | noun

levity: /ˈlevəti/ | n. | 轻浮；轻率；不严肃；轻佻 |  The quality of being light in weight. (Rarely used in this sense) | The levity of the material made it easy to carry.  这种材料很轻，便于携带。 | Synonyms: lightness, buoyancy | Antonyms: heaviness, weightiness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *levitee*, from Old French *levete*, from Latin *levitas* ("lightness"), from *levis* ("light").
