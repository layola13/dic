moloch:/ˈmoʊlɑːk/ | n. | 摩洛, 火神, 贪得无厌的魔鬼 | A Canaanite god to whom children were sacrificed by fire, now used figuratively to refer to something that demands great sacrifice, especially of human life or well-being. | The factory system became a moloch, consuming the lives of countless workers. 工厂制度变成了一个摩洛，吞噬了无数工人的生命。 |  Example: The pursuit of profit has become a moloch, demanding ever greater sacrifices from the environment. 追求利润已经变成了一个摩洛，要求对环境做出越来越大的牺牲。| Synonyms: juggernaut, monster, ogre | Antonyms: savior, protector | noun
