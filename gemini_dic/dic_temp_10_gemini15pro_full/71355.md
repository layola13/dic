flackeries:/ˈflækəriːz/
n.
吹捧；公关活动；虚伪的恭维；空洞的宣传
Excessive or insincere praise or publicity, especially of a promotional nature.
The celebrity's memoir was filled with flackeries about her supposed talent and kindness.  这位名人的回忆录里充满了对她所谓的才华和善良的吹捧。
The company hired a PR firm to generate positive flackeries.  这家公司聘请了一家公关公司来制造正面宣传。
近义词: hype, puffery, ballyhoo, propaganda
反义词: criticism, condemnation, censure
词性: noun (plural)

词源:
From "flack," originally meaning an anti-aircraft gun, then a barrage of criticism, and later, a publicist.  "Flackery" is the plural form and refers to the activities or products of flacks.  源自“flack”，最初指的是高射炮，然后是大量的批评，后来指宣传员。“Flackery”是复数形式，指的是宣传员的活动或产品。
