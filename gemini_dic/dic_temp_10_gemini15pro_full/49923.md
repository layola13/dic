denationalized:/ˌdiːˈnæʃnəlaɪzd/
| adj. | 国有化的 | Having been changed from being owned and controlled by the government to being privately owned. | Many formerly denationalized industries are now struggling. 许多先前私有化的产业现在举步维艰。|  The denationalized railway system has seen a significant increase in efficiency. 私有化的铁路系统效率显著提高。 | privatized, deregulated | nationalized | adjective


denationalize:/ˌdiːˈnæʃnəlaɪz/
| vt. | 使私有化 | To change (an industry or company) from being owned and controlled by the government to being privately owned. | The government plans to denationalize the postal service. 政府计划将邮政服务私有化。| They denationalized the airline to encourage competition. 他们将航空公司私有化以鼓励竞争。 | privatize, deregulate | nationalize | verb


denationalization:/ˌdiːˌnæʃnələˈzeɪʃn/
| n. | 私有化 | The act of changing an industry or company from government to private ownership and control. | The denationalization of the energy sector led to lower prices. 能源部门的私有化导致价格下降。| There was considerable debate surrounding the denationalization of the telecommunications industry. 电信行业的私有化引发了广泛的争论。 | privatization, deregulation | nationalization | noun
