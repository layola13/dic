homini:/ˈhɒmɪnɪ/| n.|人, 人类 (dative singular of "homo")|It is a dative singular form of the Latin word "homo," meaning "man" or "human being."  It is used in phrases like "cui bono" (who benefits?).|"Cui bono?" literally translates to "For what good?," but is idiomatically understood as "Who benefits?" or "Who profits?". It implies that the person who benefits most from a crime is likely the perpetrator. |Near synonyms: hominibus (dative plural), human|Antonym: none (as it is a grammatical form)|noun
