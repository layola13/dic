huttonian:/hʌˈtoʊniən/
adjective
赫顿的; 赫顿学说的; 均变论的
Relating to or characteristic of the theories of the Scottish geologist James Hutton (1726-1797), especially his theory of uniformitarianism, which holds that the Earth's geological features were formed by slow, gradual processes over long periods of time, rather than by sudden catastrophic events.
The Huttonian theory revolutionized the understanding of geological time. 赫顿理论彻底改变了人们对地质时间的理解。
Huttonian principles are fundamental to modern geology. 赫顿原理是现代地质学的基础。
近义词: uniformitarian, gradualistic
反义词: catastrophic, cataclysmic
词性: adjective
词源: From the name of James Hutton + -ian.
