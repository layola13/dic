dissembler:/dɪˈsɛmblər/
noun|伪君子，掩饰者，装腔作势的人|A person who conceals their true feelings or motives, typically by pretending to have different ones.|He was a dissembler, hiding his true intentions behind a mask of friendliness.|近义词:hypocrite, pretender, deceiver, liar|反义词:honest person, truthful person|词性:n.

Etymology:
From Middle English *dissemblen*, from Old French *dessembler* "to be unlike, differ, disguise oneself," from *des-* (from Latin *dis-*) "apart, away" + *sembler* "to seem, appear," from Latin *simulāre* "to copy, represent, feign."
