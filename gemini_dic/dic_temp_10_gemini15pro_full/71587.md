flashpoint:/ˈflæʃpɔɪnt/ | n. | 闪点；爆发点；燃点 | The point at which something is likely to cause a sudden, violent reaction or problem.  A place or time when trouble, such as violence or anger, is likely to start. |  Tensions in the region have reached flashpoint. 该地区的紧张局势已达到一触即发的程度。


The assassination of the Archduke was the flashpoint of the First World War.  大公遇刺是第一次世界大战的导火索。 |  Near synonyms: tipping point, breaking point, crisis point, trigger point | Near antonyms: peace, calm, stability | noun

flashpoint:/ˈflæʃpɔɪnt/ | adj. |  一触即发的；极其危险的 | Likely to cause a sudden, violent reaction or problem.  | The flashpoint topic of immigration sparked heated debate.  移民这个一触即发的话题引发了激烈的辩论。


The senator's comments created a flashpoint situation in the already tense negotiations. 参议员的评论在已经很紧张的谈判中制造了一个一触即发的局面。 | Near synonyms: volatile, explosive, inflammatory, dangerous | Near antonyms: safe, stable, peaceful, calm | adjective
