bass: /beɪs/ (US) /beɪs/ (UK)| n. adj.| 低音; 鲈鱼;|A low, deep sound, such as the lowest male voice or the lowest note of a musical instrument; a type of fish.| The bass in the music was so strong it made the floor vibrate. 音乐中的低音如此强烈，以至于地板都在震动。|近义词：low, deep (for sound); perch, grouper (for fish)|反义词：treble, soprano (for sound)|词性: 名词, 形容词

bass: /beɪs/ (US) /beɪs/ (UK)| n.| 低音吉他;|A stringed instrument played with the fingers or a plectrum, similar to a guitar but with a longer neck and thicker strings, producing a lower pitch.| He plays bass in a rock band. 他在一个摇滚乐队里弹贝斯。|近义词：bass guitar|反义词: N/A|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bas, from Old French bas ("low"), from Late Latin bassus ("thick, low"), from Vulgar Latin *bassus.
