overburden:/ˌoʊvərˈbɜːrdn/ /ˌəʊvəˈbɜːdən/| n. vt.| 过载，负担过重，覆盖物 |Something that is too much of a burden or load; excessively burdensome.  The rock or earth lying above a mineral deposit, especially coal.|The miners had to remove tons of overburden to get to the coal seam. 矿工们不得不清除大量的覆盖物才能到达煤层。|近义词：overload, overtax, overwhelm|反义词: underload, lighten|n.
overburden:/ˌoʊvərˈbɜːrdn/ /ˌəʊvəˈbɜːdən/| vt.|使负担过重，使过度负荷|To load with too great a burden; overtax. |The company was overburdened with debt.  这家公司债务缠身。|近义词：overload, overtax, overwhelm|反义词: underload, lighten|vt.

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + burden.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much". "Burden" comes from the Old English "byrthen" meaning "load" or "weight."
