fraken:/ˈfræŋkən/| adj.|弗兰肯式的，拼凑的，粗制滥造的|Refers to something that is cobbled together or crudely created, often in a monstrous or unnatural way, alluding to Frankenstein's monster.|例句：The fraken-furniture he built from scrap wood looked unstable. 他用废木料拼凑的弗兰肯式家具看起来很不稳当。

例句：The computer code was a fraken-mess, patched together from various sources. 这段电脑代码简直就是弗兰肯式的混乱，从各种来源拼凑而成。|近义词：patched-up, cobbled together, makeshift, monstrous|反义词：seamless, polished, refined|词性:形容词

词源:
Derived from "Frankenstein," the name of the scientist in Mary Shelley's novel who created a monster from assembled body parts.  The prefix "fraken-" is used to suggest a creation that is similarly pieced together, often with negative connotations.
