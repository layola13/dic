archipterygium: /ˌɑːrkɪpˈtɛrɪdʒiəm/ | n. | 原始鳍; 鳍基骨 |  The basic skeletal structure of the paired fins of fish, from which the limbs of terrestrial vertebrates are thought to have evolved.  A fin skeleton, such as that of the lungfish, from which the vertebrate limb may have evolved. | The archipterygium is considered to be the ancestral form of vertebrate limbs. 原始鳍被认为是脊椎动物四肢的祖先形式。

The discovery of the archipterygium provided key insights into vertebrate evolution.  原始鳍的发现为了解脊椎动物进化提供了关键信息。 |  Synonym: fin skeleton | Antonym:  (No direct antonym exists, as it's a specific anatomical term) | noun
