galliambic:/ˌɡæliˈæmbɪk/| adj. n.|高卢祭司的，奔放狂乱的（诗)|Of or relating to the galliambic meter, a rhythmic pattern used in ancient Greek and Roman poetry, characterized by a frenzied, ecstatic quality.  It can also refer to a poem written in this meter.  The word derives from the Galli, priests of Cybele, whose wild, orgiastic rituals influenced the meter.|The poem's galliambic rhythm evoked the frenzy of the ancient rites.|近义词：frenetic, frantic, wild, orgiastic|反义词：calm, peaceful, serene|词性: adjective, noun
