claustrophobics:/ˌklɔːstrəˈfəʊbɪks/ | n. | 幽闭恐惧症患者 | People who suffer from claustrophobia, an anxiety disorder characterized by a fear of enclosed spaces. |  Example: Claustrophobics often find it difficult to ride in elevators. 幽闭恐惧症患者通常觉得乘坐电梯很困难。

Example: The small, windowless room made the claustrophobics in the group feel uneasy.  那间没有窗户的小房间让小组里患有幽闭恐惧症的人感到不安。 | 近义词: sufferers of claustrophobia | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "agoraphobics" - those who fear open spaces) | 词性: noun


claustrophobic: /ˌklɔːstrəˈfəʊbɪk/ | adj. | 幽闭恐惧症的，患有幽闭恐惧症的；引起幽闭恐惧的 |  Relating to or suffering from claustrophobia; inducing claustrophobia. | Example: She felt claustrophobic in the crowded elevator.  她在拥挤的电梯里感到幽闭恐惧。

Example: The tiny cabin was quite claustrophobic. 这个小隔间相当令人感到幽闭。 | 近义词: confining, restrictive, stuffy | 反义词: open, airy, spacious | 词性: adjective
