arminianism: /ɑːrˈmɪniənɪzəm/ | n. | 阿米念主义 | A theological doctrine named after Jacobus Arminius (1560–1609), a Dutch theologian, that opposes the absolute predestination of Calvinism and emphasizes free will in salvation. It asserts that God's grace is offered to all, but individuals can choose to accept or reject it. |  Arminianism is often contrasted with Calvinism. 阿米念主义经常与加尔文主义对比。|  The debate between Arminianism and Calvinism continues to this day.  阿米念主义和加尔文主义之间的争论至今仍在继续。 |  Near synonyms: free grace, free will theology | Antonyms: Calvinism, predestination | Noun
