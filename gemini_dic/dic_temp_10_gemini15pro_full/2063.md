acutely:/əˈkjuːtli/
adverb
剧烈地；深刻地；敏锐地
In a way that is very strong, severe, or sharp.  It also means in a way that shows a very good understanding of a situation.
She felt the loss acutely. 她深深地感受到了失去的痛苦。
He is acutely aware of the problem. 他敏锐地意识到了这个问题。

近义词:severely, intensely, sharply, keenly
反义词:mildly, slightly, vaguely, dully
词性:副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin acutus  "sharpened,"  past participle of acuere "to sharpen" (from Latin acus "needle").  The "-ly" suffix transforms the adjective "acute" into an adverb.


USAGE->
1. Describing physical sensations:
The patient complained of acutely painful headaches. = 病人抱怨头痛剧烈。
I felt acutely embarrassed when I tripped and fell in front of everyone. = 当我在众人面前绊倒时，我感到非常尴尬。

2. Describing emotions:
She was acutely aware of his disapproval. = 她敏锐地意识到他不赞成。
They are acutely conscious of the dangers of the situation. = 他们敏锐地意识到情况的危险性。

3. Describing intellectual abilities:
He was an acutely intelligent man. = 他是一个非常聪明的人。
The problem is acutely perceived by many experts. = 许多专家都敏锐地察觉到了这个问题。

4. Modifying other adverbs or adjectives:
The situation was acutely embarrassing for everyone involved. = 这种情况对所有相关人员来说都非常尴尬。
He was acutely sensitive to criticism. = 他对批评非常敏感。


PHRASE->
