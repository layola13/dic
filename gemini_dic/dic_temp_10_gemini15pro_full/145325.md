Etymology:  "Over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi) + "browning" (from "brown" + "-ing," denoting the process of becoming brown).  Over- indicates excessive or beyond a desirable limit.  Browning refers to the process of food turning brown due to the Maillard reaction or caramelization.
词源：“over-”（古英语 *ofer，源自原始日耳曼语 *uberi）+“browning”（源自“brown”+“-ing”，表示变成棕色的过程）。over- 表示过量或超过理想限度。browning 指的是食物由于美拉德反应或焦糖化而变成棕色的过程。
