litters:/ˈlɪtərz/ /ˈlɪtərz/| n. v.|垃圾，杂物；一窝（幼崽）；（动物）产仔|A noun referring to scattered rubbish or a group of young animals born at one time.  As a verb, it means to give birth to a litter of young, or to scatter rubbish.|

例句：
The park was covered in litters. 公园里到处都是垃圾。

The cat had a litter of five kittens. 这只猫产下了一窝五只小猫。


近义词：rubbish, trash, debris (for noun); offspring, brood (for noun - young animals); give birth to, bear (for verb)|
反义词: tidiness, cleanliness (for noun - rubbish)|
词性:noun, verb


litter:/ˈlɪtər/ /ˈlɪtər/| v.|乱扔垃圾|To scatter rubbish carelessly.|

例句：
Please don't litter the streets. 请不要在街上乱扔垃圾。

People who litter should be fined. 乱扔垃圾的人应该被罚款。


近义词：scatter, strew, trash|
反义词: clean, tidy|
词性:verb


词源：From Old Norse *liðr, meaning "bed, couch".  The sense of "straw used as bedding" led to the meaning of "rubbish" and then to the meaning of "a birth of animals".
