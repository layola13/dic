alepole:/ˈeɪlpoʊl/| n.| 啤酒柱; 啤酒杆|A pole or post used as a sign for a tavern or alehouse.  A long pole or staff with a bush or other sign fixed at the top, formerly placed in front of a tavern or alehouse to indicate that ale was sold there. |例句：The alepole outside the tavern swayed in the wind. 酒馆外的啤酒柱在风中摇曳。

例句：The faded alepole was a reminder of the inn's long history.  褪色的啤酒柱提醒着人们这家客栈悠久的历史。|近义词：signpost, tavern sign|反义词: None|词性: Noun

词源: From Middle English *alepel, equivalent to ale + pole.  The word "ale" refers to a type of beer, and "pole" refers to a long, slender piece of wood.
