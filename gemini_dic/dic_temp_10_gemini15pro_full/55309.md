dissolvative:/dɪˈzɒlətɪv/ | adj. | 溶解的；分解的；破坏性的 | tending to dissolve; causing dissolution or disintegration; destructive | The dissolvative effects of acid rain on limestone buildings are a serious concern. 酸雨对石灰岩建筑的溶解作用是一个严重的问题。| The dissolvative power of certain enzymes breaks down complex molecules. 某些酶的分解能力可以分解复杂的分子。 |  近义词: destructive, disruptive, corrosive, disintegrative | 反义词: constructive, cohesive, unifying | 词性: 形容词

dissolve:/dɪˈzɒlv/ | v. | 溶解；解散；消失 | (of a solid) to become incorporated into a liquid so as to form a solution; to (cause something to) come to an end | Sugar dissolves in water. 糖溶于水。| The parliament was dissolved. 议会解散了。| 近义词: melt, liquefy, disintegrate, disperse | 反义词: solidify, congeal, coalesce | 词性: 动词

词源: From Latin dissolvere (to loosen, untie, dissolve), from dis- (apart) + solvere (to loosen).
