arraigners:/əˈreɪnərz/
n.
控告者，传讯者
People who formally accuse someone of a crime in a court of law.
The arraigners presented compelling evidence against the defendant.  控告者提出了针对被告的有力证据。
The judge listened carefully to the arguments of the arraigners. 法官仔细聆听了控告者的论点。
Synonyms: accusers, prosecutors, indicters
Antonyms: defenders, advocates
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *araignen (“to call (someone) before a court to answer an indictment”), from Anglo-Norman *araigner, areiner, from Old French araisnier, aresnier (“to address, speak to, reason with, call to account”), from Late Latin *adrationāre, from Latin ad (“to”) + ratiō (“reason, calculation, account”).
