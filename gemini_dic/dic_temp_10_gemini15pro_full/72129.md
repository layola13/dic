flooder:/ˈflʌdər/| n.| 泛滥者，水灾；涌出大量信息的人；|A person or thing that floods something, especially a person who sends a large number of emails simultaneously.  指泛滥某物的人或事物，尤指同时发送大量电子邮件的人。|The river flooded the town after heavy rain.  大雨过后，河水淹没了城镇。|近义词：inundator, overwhelmer|反义词: drainer|词性:noun


flooding:/ˈflʌdɪŋ/| n.| 泛滥，洪水；涌出大量信息|The overflowing of a large amount of water beyond its normal confines, especially over what is normally dry land. 指大量的水溢出其正常范围，尤指漫过通常是干燥的陆地。|The flooding caused significant damage to homes and businesses. 洪水对房屋和企业造成了重大损害。|近义词：inundation, deluge|反义词: drainage|词性:noun


flood:/flʌd/| n. v.| 洪水；泛滥；涌出；充满；大量涌入|A large amount of water covering an area that is usually dry. 大量的水覆盖了通常是干燥的区域。 / To fill or cover something with a large amount of water or other liquid. 用大量的水或其他液体填充或覆盖某物。 / To arrive or go somewhere in large numbers. 大量涌入或涌向某处。|The flood destroyed crops and homes. 洪水摧毁了庄稼和房屋. /  The stadium was flooded with fans. 体育场挤满了球迷。|近义词：deluge, inundation, overflow|反义词: drought, trickle|词性:noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *flōd, from Proto-Germanic *flōduz, from Proto-Indo-European *plewd-, meaning "to flow, float."  来自古英语 *flōd，源自原始日耳曼语 *flōduz，源自原始印欧语 *plewd-，意思是“流动，漂浮”。
