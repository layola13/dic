barbican: /ˈbɑːrbɪkən/ (US); /ˈbɑːbɪkən/ (UK)| n.| 城楼，堡垒，防御工事 | A fortified outpost, especially a projecting watchtower on a city wall or castle. |  The castle's barbican offered a strategic advantage during sieges. 城堡的城楼在围攻期间提供了战略优势。

The barbican protected the main gate from attack.  城楼保护了正门免受攻击。

| 近义词: fortification, bastion, rampart, bulwark | 反义词: breach, opening, vulnerability | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Old French *barbacane, from Arabic *barbakhana, perhaps from Persian *bala khana "upper house/story/floor".
