misrule:/ˌmɪsˈruːl/
n.|暴政，乱政|A period of disorder and bad government.|The country suffered years of misrule under the dictator.| tyranny, oppression|good governance, order
v.|对…施暴政，统治不善|To govern badly or unjustly.|The king misruled the kingdom, leading to widespread poverty.| misgovern, maladminister|govern well, administer justly
词性:名词,动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (badly) + rule (govern).  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old English "mis-", meaning "wrongly, badly, astray." "Rule" comes from Old French "reule" and ultimately from Latin "regula" meaning "straight stick, ruler, pattern."
