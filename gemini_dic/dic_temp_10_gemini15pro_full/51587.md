devaluation:/ˌdiːvæljuˈeɪʃn/ | n. | 贬值；降低价值 |  The act of reducing the official value of a currency in relation to other currencies.  | The devaluation of the currency led to higher import prices. 货币贬值导致进口价格上涨。 | synonyms: depreciation, reduction, decline | antonyms: appreciation, increase, revaluation | noun

devalue:/ˌdiːˈvæljuː/ | vt. | 贬值；降低…的价值；轻视 | To reduce the official value of (a currency) in relation to other currencies; to reduce the value or importance of something. |  The government decided to devalue the currency to boost exports. 政府决定将货币贬值以促进出口。 They devalued the importance of education. 他们低估了教育的重要性。 | synonyms: depreciate, lower, cheapen | antonyms: appreciate, enhance, raise | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *dévaluation*, from *dévaluer* "to devalue," from *dé-* "down" + *valuer* "to value," from Latin *valēre* "to be strong, be worth."
