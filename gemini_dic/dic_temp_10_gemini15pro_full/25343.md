build-up:/ˈbɪldʌp/ /ˈbɪldˌʌp/| n.|积累，增进，增强；宣传；准备工作；高潮|a gradual increase in something, or the process of increasing something; the advertising and discussion that makes people interested in something; preparations for something; the period of excitement before something important happens|例句：There was a gradual build-up of traffic on the roads. 道路上的交通逐渐拥堵。

例句：The build-up to the election was very exciting. 选举前的准备工作非常激动人心。|近义词：accumulation, increase, growth, development, promotion, preparation|反义词：decrease, decline, reduction|词性:名词


build-up:/ˈbɪldʌp/ /ˈbɪldˌʌp/| adj.|组合的，集成的|formed by the addition of several parts or stages|例句：The dam is a build-up structure of earth and rock. 这座大坝是由土和岩石堆积而成的结构。

例句：This is a complex build-up toy for children. 这是一个复杂的组合玩具，适合儿童玩耍。|近义词：composite, combined, integrated|反义词：simple, basic|词性:形容词
