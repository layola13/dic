dyscrasite:/ˈdɪskɹəˌsaɪt/| n. |辉锑矿|A naturally occurring mineral composed of silver and antimony, often found in silver ore deposits. It is an important source of silver.  Dyscrasite specimens can exhibit a variety of crystal habits and colors, ranging from silver-white to tin-white or lead-gray. |The geologist identified the mineral as dyscrasite. 地质学家鉴定这种矿物为辉锑矿。|Synonym: silver antimonide|Antonym: N/A|noun
