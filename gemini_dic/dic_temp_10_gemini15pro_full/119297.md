micro-metres:/ˌmaɪkrəʊˈmiːtəz/ (UK) /ˌmaɪkroʊˈmiːtərz/ (US)| n. |微米|A unit of length equal to one millionth of a metre.|The diameter of a human hair is typically between 50 and 100 micro-metres.|Synonyms:  microns, μm|Antonyms:  kilometres, miles|Noun

micro-meter:/ˌmaɪkrəʊˈmiːtər/ (UK) /ˌmaɪkroʊˈmiːtər/ (US)| n. |测微计|An instrument used for making precise measurements of small distances.|The engineer used a micro-meter to measure the thickness of the metal sheet.|Synonyms: micrometer gauge, caliper|Antonyms:  ruler (for less precise measurements)|Noun


Etymology:  "micro-" from Greek *mikrós* "small" + "-metre" from Greek *métron* "measure".
