monodramatic:/ˌmɒnədrəˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 独角戏似的；夸张的；感情用事过度的 | Characterized by unrestrained or excessive emotion, like a melodrama.  Relating to or resembling melodrama.  Often suggests a tendency to overreact or exaggerate emotions. | Her response to the spilled coffee was a bit monodramatic. 她对洒出的咖啡的反应有点小题大做。|  She delivered a monodramatic speech about the injustices she had suffered. 她发表了一篇关于她所遭受的不公正待遇的夸张的演讲。 | 近义词: histrionic, theatrical, melodramatic, overdramatic | 反义词: understated, restrained, subtle | 词性: adjective

monodrama:/ˈmɒnəˌdrɑːmə/ | n. | 独角戏 | A dramatic piece intended for a single performer.  A play or dramatic work for one actor. |  She performed a powerful monodrama about the life of a refugee. 她表演了一部关于难民生活的震撼人心的独角戏。 | The festival featured several innovative monodramas.  这个节日以几个创新的独角戏为特色。 | 近义词: solo performance, one-man show, one-woman show | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but consider: ensemble piece, multi-character play) | 词性: noun


词源:
mono- (prefix): from Greek monos "single, alone"
dramatic: from Late Latin dramaticus, from Greek dramatikos "pertaining to plays," from drama "dramatic work," from dran "to do, perform."
