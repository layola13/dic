bezonian:/bɪˈzoʊniən/
n.|乞丐，流氓，无赖|A beggar; a rogue; a rascal.|Shakespeare often used this word to describe a lowlife or scoundrel.  "Away, you three-inch fool!"  he cried to the bezonian.  |例句：The bezonian accosted me in the alleyway, demanding money.  那个无赖在小巷里拦住我，要钱。

例句：The play featured a comical bezonian who constantly got into trouble. 这部戏剧以一个滑稽的无赖为主角，他总是惹麻烦。|近义词：vagrant, rogue, rascal, scoundrel, knave, villain|反义词：gentleman, nobleman, lord|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *bisognoso* "needy," from *bisogno* "need."
