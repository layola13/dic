befell:/bɪˈfel/| v.|降临，发生(尤指不好的事情)|happen, especially something bad|A great misfortune befell the family.  这个家庭遭遇了巨大的不幸。|近义词：happen, occur, transpire|反义词: averted (避免), prevented (阻止)|动词

USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Misfortune rarely befalls those who are prepared.  做好准备的人很少会遇到不幸。
Trouble often befalls the unwary.  粗心大意的人经常会遇到麻烦。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
This usage is very rare and often sounds awkward.  这种用法很少见，而且通常听起来很别扭。
*Something is befalling them right now.  (不太自然)  现在正有什么事发生在他们身上。

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
I don't know what has befallen him.  我不知道他发生了什么事。
A terrible tragedy has befallen their family.  一场可怕的悲剧降临到他们家。

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
This tense is not used with 'befell'.  这个时态不与“befell”连用。

一般过去时 (Simple Past):

What befell you on your journey?  你在旅途中发生了什么事？

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):

一般将来时 (Simple Future):
We don't know what will befall us in the future.  我们不知道未来会发生什么。

将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):

虚拟语气:
If such a fate should befall me, I would accept it.  如果这样的命运降临到我身上，我会接受它。
If disaster were to befall us, we would be prepared.  如果灾难降临到我们身上，我们会做好准备。

被动语态:
This verb cannot be used in the passive voice.  这个动词不能用于被动语态。


否定句:
Nothing untoward befell them on their trip.  他们在旅途中没有发生任何不幸的事情。
No harm befell the children.  孩子们没有受到任何伤害。
