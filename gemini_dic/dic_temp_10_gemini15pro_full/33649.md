cinematograph: /ˌsɪnɪˈmætəɡræf/ /ˌsɪnɪˈmɑːtəɡrɑːf/ | n. |电影放映机；电影摄影机|A device for projecting motion pictures; a motion-picture camera. | The old cinematograph projected flickering images onto the sheet.  旧式电影放映机将闪烁的影像投射到幕布上。| synonym: projector, movie camera | antonym: None | noun

cinematograph: /ˌsɪnɪˈmætəɡræf/ /ˌsɪnɪˈmɑːtəɡrɑːf/ | v. |拍摄电影|To film or photograph a motion picture. |They cinematographed the entire wedding ceremony. 他们拍摄了整个婚礼仪式。| synonym: film, shoot, record | antonym: None | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cinématographe*, coined by the Lumière brothers, from *cinéma* ("movement") + Greek *graphein* ("to write, record"). *Cinéma* is from Ancient Greek *kínēma* (“movement”), from *kineîn* (“to move”).
