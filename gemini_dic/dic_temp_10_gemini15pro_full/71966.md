flimflam:/ˈflɪmˌflæm/| n. v.|欺骗；胡说；废话|nonsense, deceptive or evasive talk; a deception, trick, or lie.  |He tried to sell me some flimflam about how his product could cure all diseases. 他试图用他的产品能治愈所有疾病的胡言乱语来欺骗我。|近义词：trickery, deception, fraud, hoax, humbug|反义词：honesty, truth, sincerity|词性:名词, 动词

flimflam:/ˈflɪmˌflæm/| v.|欺骗；哄骗|to deceive or cheat (someone)|He flimflammed the old lady out of her life savings. 他骗走了老太太的毕生积蓄。|近义词：swindle, cheat, defraud, bamboozle|反义词：be honest with|词性:动词

词源：
The origin of "flimflam" is uncertain, but it likely emerged in the 16th century. Some speculate it might be imitative, echoing the sound of frivolous or deceptive talk. Others suggest a possible connection to the now-obsolete word "flam," meaning "a lie or deception."
