nongarrulousnesses:/ˌnɒnɡəˈruːləsnəsɪz/
|n.|不健谈，沉默寡言 |The state or quality of being not talkative or loquacious. |His nongarrulousness made him seem mysterious. 他的沉默寡言使他显得神秘。| reserve, reticence, taciturnity |garrulousness, loquacity, talkativeness |noun

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  Meaning "not," from Old French non-, from Latin non "not," cognate with Old English ne (see no).
garrulous (adjective): From Latin garrulus "talkative, chattering," from garrire "to chatter," of imitative origin.
-ness (suffix): Forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being."
