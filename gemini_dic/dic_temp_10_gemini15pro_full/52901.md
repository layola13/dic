dimmed-out:/dɪmdˈaʊt/| adj.|变暗的，灯光昏暗的|Having lights turned down low or partly off, often as a precaution during wartime or to create a specific atmosphere.|The streets were dimmed-out during the air raid. 空袭期间街道上的灯光都变暗了。|近义词：darkened, blacked-out|反义词:bright, illuminated|词性:形容词

dimmed-out:/dɪmdˈaʊt/| n.|灯火管制，灯光昏暗|The state of having lights turned down low, typically during wartime.|The dimmed-out city was eerily quiet. 灯火管制下的城市安静得可怕。|近义词：blackout, darkness|反义词:brightness, illumination|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "dim" (verb meaning to make less bright) + "out" (adverb indicating a complete or thorough action).  "Dim" traces back to Old English *dimmian.
