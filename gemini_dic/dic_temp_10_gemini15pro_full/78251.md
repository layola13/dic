germ:/dʒɜːrm/ | n. | 微生物，细菌，病菌；起源，萌芽 |A germ is a very small organism, especially one that causes disease.  A germ can also refer to the origin or earliest stage of something. | Wash your hands thoroughly to remove germs.  彻底洗手以去除细菌。 The germ of an idea began to form in her mind. 一个想法的萌芽开始在她脑海中形成。 | synonyms: microbe, bacterium, pathogen, seed, beginning, origin | antonyms: sterility, cleanliness | noun


germinate:/ˈdʒɜːrmɪneɪt/ | v. | 发芽，萌芽；发展 | To germinate means to begin to grow, as of a seed or spore; or to develop or come into existence. | The seeds will germinate in about two weeks. 种子大约两周后发芽。 The idea germinated in his mind for years before he acted on it. 这个想法在他心中酝酿多年，他才付诸行动。| synonyms: sprout, bud, develop, grow, originate | antonyms: wither, die, stagnate | verb
