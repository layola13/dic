lithography:/lɪˈθɒɡrəfi (British English), /lɪˈθɑːɡrəfi (American English)| n.|平版印刷术，光刻|A method of printing from a flat stone or metal plate on which the image areas are worked using a greasy substance so that the ink will adhere to them by chemical affinity, and the blank areas are made ink-repellent.|例句：Lithography is a key technology in microchip manufacturing. 平版印刷术是微芯片制造中的关键技术。

例句：The artist created a series of lithographs depicting scenes from nature.  这位艺术家创作了一系列描绘自然景色的平版印刷画。|近义词：printing, engraving|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *lithographie*, from Ancient Greek *λίθος* (*líthos*) meaning "stone" + *γράφειν* (*graphein*) meaning "to write".
