cajolement:/kəˈdʒoʊlmənt/
n.
劝诱；哄骗；甜言蜜语
the act of persuading someone to do something they don't want to do, by being nice to them or flattering them.
He finally agreed to her request after much cajolement.  他被哄了很久，最终同意了她的请求。

近义词:persuasion, coaxing, flattery, wheedling, blandishment
反义词:coercion, force, intimidation, threat
词性:noun

Etymology:
From French *cajoler*, "to chatter like a caged bird," from Old North French *cajol*, of imitative origin.
