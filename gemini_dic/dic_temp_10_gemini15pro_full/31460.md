charlatanry:/ˈʃɑːrlətənri/ | n. | 欺骗；江湖骗术；招摇撞骗 | The practice of making showy pretenses to knowledge or ability; fraudulent or exaggerated claims. |  His claims of being able to cure any disease were pure charlatanry. 他声称能治愈任何疾病，这纯属江湖骗术。

The salesman's charlatanry was eventually exposed, and he was fired.  这个推销员的招摇撞骗最终被揭穿，他被解雇了。 | 近义词:fraud, deception, trickery, quackery | 反义词:honesty, genuineness, authenticity | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *charlatanerie*, from *charlatan* (charlatan), from Italian *ciarlatano* (charlatan), from *ciarlare* (to chatter), of uncertain origin.
