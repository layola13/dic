blindsides:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪdz/| vt. n.|突袭，使措手不及，出其不意，攻其不备|To attack someone or something from an unexpected direction, typically leaving them disoriented and unprepared.  The news completely blindsided me. |例句：The CEO blindsided the board with his sudden resignation.  首席执行官突然辞职，使董事会措手不及。

例句：The boxer blindsided his opponent with a powerful left hook. 那位拳击手以一记强有力的左勾拳突袭了他的对手。|近义词：ambush, surprise, stun, shock|反义词:forewarn, prepare|词性:动词,名词


blindsides:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪdz/| n.|弱点，盲点|A person's weak point or vulnerability. His arrogance is his blindside. |例句：They attacked the enemy's blindside. 他们攻击了敌人的弱点。

例句：He's got a real blindside about his inability to communicate effectively. 他对他自己无法有效沟通的弱点一无所知。|近义词：weakness, vulnerability, flaw|反义词:strength, forte|词性:名词

词源: From "blind side," referring to the side of a person that cannot be seen, hence vulnerable to attack.
