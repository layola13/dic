hidy-holes:/ˈhɪdiˌhəʊlz/ (US) /ˈhaɪdiˌhəʊlz/ (UK)
n.
藏身之处，隐匿处
A hiding place; a place of concealment or refuge.
The squirrels had several hidy-holes in the oak tree where they stored their nuts. 松鼠在橡树上有几个藏坚果的隐匿处。
The children built a hidy-hole in the woods where they could play undisturbed. 孩子们在树林里建了一个藏身之处，在那里他们可以不受打扰地玩耍。
近义词: hiding places, hideouts, retreats, shelters
反义词: exposed areas, open spaces
词性: 名词

词源:
From "hiding holes," a compound of "hiding" (present participle of "hide") and "holes."  "Hide" comes from Old English *hȳdan, of Germanic origin. "Hole" comes from Old English hol, of Germanic origin.
