eudaemonism: /ˌjuːdɪˈmɒnɪzəm/ (UK) /ˌjuːdəˈmɑːnɪzəm/ (US)| n. | 幸福主义；快乐说 |  An ethical theory that defines right action as that which leads to the "well-being" of the individual, thus holding "well-being" to be the highest good.  | Aristotle's philosophy is considered a form of eudemonism.  亚里士多德的哲学被认为是一种幸福主义。 | synonym: welfarism | antonym: nihilism | noun
