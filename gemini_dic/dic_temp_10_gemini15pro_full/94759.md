improvisedly: /ɪmˈprɒvɪzaɪdli/ /ɪmˈprɑːvɪzaɪdli/
| adv. | 即兴地，临时地 | Done without preparation or planning; extemporaneously. | He spoke improvisedly, relying on wit and charm. 他即兴发言，依靠的是机智和魅力。|
|例句：She improvisedly created a shelter from branches and leaves. 她临时用树枝和树叶搭建了一个避难所。|
|近义词：extemporaneously, offhand, ad-lib, spontaneously |
|反义词：preparedly, deliberately, rehearsedly |
|词性: 副词 |

improvise: /ˈɪmprəvaɪz/ /ˈɪmprəvaɪz/
| v. | 即兴创作，临时凑合 | Create and perform (music, drama, or verse) spontaneously or without preparation. | The musicians improvised a bluesy melody. 音乐家们即兴创作了一段布鲁斯旋律。|
|例句：He had to improvise a solution when the equipment failed. 当设备出现故障时，他不得不临时想出一个解决方案。|
|近义词：extemporize, ad-lib, wing it, make do |
|反义词：plan, prepare, rehearse |
|词性: 动词 |

improvisation: /ɪmˌprɒvɪˈzeɪʃn/ /ɪmˌprɑːvɪˈzeɪʃn/
| n. | 即兴创作，临时凑合 | The act of improvising. | The performance was a brilliant improvisation. 这场演出是一次精彩的即兴创作。|
|例句：His improvisation on the piano amazed the audience. 他在钢琴上的即兴演奏让观众惊叹不已。|
|近义词：extemporization, ad-lib, spontaneity |
|反义词：preparation, rehearsal, planning |
|词性: 名词 |


词源：From the Late Latin improvisus, past participle of improvisare "to do something extempore," from Latin in- "not" + provisus, past participle of providēre "to foresee."
