alternate:/ˈɔːltərneɪt/ (US) /ˈɒltəneɪt/ (UK)| adj. v.| 交替的，轮流的；交替，轮流 | Happening or following one after the other regularly. | The shirt has alternate stripes of blue and white. 这件衬衫有蓝色和白色的条纹交替。|She alternates between cheerfulness and deep despair. 她一会儿兴高采烈，一会儿又深陷绝望。|近义词：alternative, rotating, intermittent|反义词：continuous, constant|词性:形容词/动词

alternate:/ˈɔːltərneɪt/ (US) /ˈɒltəneɪt/ (UK)| n.| 替补队员，候补人员 | A person acting in place of another; a substitute. | He served as an alternate on the committee. 他担任委员会的候补委员。| There are two alternates for this position. 这个职位有两个候补人选。 |近义词：substitute, replacement, reserve|反义词：primary, principal|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *alternatus, past participle of alternare (“to do by turns”), from Latin alternus (“one after the other, every other”), from alter (“other”).
