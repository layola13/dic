mouthful:/ˈmaʊθfʊl/ (US), /ˈmaʊθfʊl/ (UK)| n.|一口，满口，难以说出的长字|A quantity of food or drink that fills the mouth or that is taken into the mouth at one time.  Also, a long, awkward, or difficult word or phrase.|I took a large mouthful of the delicious cake. 我吃了一大口美味的蛋糕。|synonyms: bite, morsel, gulp|antonyms: N/A|noun


mouthful:/ˈmaʊθfʊl/ (US), /ˈmaʊθfʊl/ (UK)| adj.|（食物）塞满嘴巴的，（话语）冗长难懂的|Filling the mouth; also, (of a word or phrase) long, awkward, or difficult to say.|The name of the town was a real mouthful.  这个镇的名字真难念。|synonyms: awkward, unwieldy|antonyms: concise, succinct|adjective
