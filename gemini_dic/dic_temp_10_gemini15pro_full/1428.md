ace-up-my/one's-sleeve:/eɪs ʌp maɪ/wʌnz sliːv/| idiom | 王牌，秘密武器，锦囊妙计 |  A secret advantage or resource that can be used when needed.  It refers to a card player concealing an ace up their sleeve to cheat, but now generally refers to any hidden advantage. | I had an ace up my sleeve during the negotiation – I knew their biggest client was about to leave them.  我在谈判中藏有一张王牌——我知道他们最大的客户即将离开他们。

He always has an ace up his sleeve, so I'm never surprised when he wins. 他总是有锦囊妙计，所以他赢了我从不感到惊讶。 |  advantage, secret weapon, trump card | disadvantage, weakness, vulnerability | noun phrase


ETYMOLOGY->
From the literal practice of cheating at cards by hiding an ace up one's sleeve. This practice dates back to at least the 16th century. The figurative meaning developed later, signifying a hidden advantage in any situation.
