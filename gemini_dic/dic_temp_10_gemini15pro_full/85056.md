ETYMOLOGY->
From *hard-boiled*, meaning literally "boiled until hard," figuratively applied to a person who is tough and unemotional, likely influenced by the image of a hard-boiled egg.  The suffix *-ness* transforms the adjective into a noun denoting the quality or state of being hard-boiled.
