bolter:/ˈboʊltər/ (US), /ˈbəʊltə(r)/ (UK)| vi. |逃窜；脱党；筛选|To run away suddenly; to leave a political party, often because of disagreement; to sift or separate materials, especially flour.|The frightened deer bolted into the forest. 受惊的鹿逃窜进了森林。|近义词：flee, escape, desert, abscond, sift, strain|反义词：stay, remain, support|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bolten*, from Old Norse *bolta* (“to bolt”), from *boltr* (“bolt, arrow”).
