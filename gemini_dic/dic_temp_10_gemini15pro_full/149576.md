pahutes:/pəˈhjuːts/| n.|茅屋；简陋棚屋|A crude shelter, typically made of branches, brush, or other rough materials.  Often used in the context of Native American cultures of the Great Basin region.|The pahutes provided minimal protection from the harsh desert climate.|近义词：hut, shack, lean-to|反义词: mansion, palace|词性:noun

Etymology: From the Paiute language, referring to the indigenous people of the Great Basin area and by extension, their traditional dwellings.
