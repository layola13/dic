nicety:/ˈnaɪsəti/| n.|细微之处；细致；精妙；微妙之处；（尤指不必要的）细节；礼节|a detail or small difference that is only obvious to people who are very knowledgeable and perceptive; a fine point of etiquette|例句：They spent hours discussing the niceties of grammar. 他们花了几个小时讨论语法的细微之处。

例句：He was careful to observe the social niceties. 他小心翼翼地遵守社交礼仪。|近义词：subtlety, nuance, detail, refinement, precision, formality, courtesy, politeness|反义词: crudeness, rudeness, impoliteness, carelessness|词性:noun

词源: From Middle English *niceté, from Old French niceté, from nice (“foolish, simple”) + -eté.  Ultimately from Latin nescius (“ignorant”). The meaning shifted from "foolishness" to "delicacy" and then to "subtlety."
