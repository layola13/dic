maxilla:/mækˈsɪlə/ (US) /mækˈsɪlə/ (UK)| n. |上颌骨|The upper jawbone, one on each side of the face, that holds the upper teeth and forms part of the eye socket, nose, and palate.|The maxilla is the bone that forms the upper jaw. 上颌骨是构成上颚的骨头。|The surgeon repaired the patient's fractured maxilla.  外科医生修复了病人断裂的上颌骨。|Synonyms: jawbone, upper jaw |Antonyms: mandible (lower jaw)|noun

maxillae:/mækˈsɪliː/ (US) /mækˈsɪliː/ (UK)| n. |上颌骨（复数）|The plural form of maxilla, referring to both the left and right upper jawbones.|The maxillae are crucial for chewing and speaking. 上颌骨对于咀嚼和说话至关重要。|Damage to the maxillae can affect a person's facial structure. 对上颌骨的损伤会影响一个人的面部结构。|Synonyms: jawbones, upper jaws |Antonyms: mandibles (lower jaws)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *maxilla*, meaning "jawbone."  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *mak-/*mag-, related to "chewing" and "kneading."
