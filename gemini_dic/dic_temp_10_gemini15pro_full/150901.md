parallactically:/ˌpærəˈlæktɪkli/| adv.|视差地，因视差影响地|In astronomy, the apparent shift in the position of a celestial object due to the change in the observer's position is calculated parallactically.  在天文学中，由于观测者位置的变化而导致的天体位置的视差偏移是用视差法计算的。|The telescope was adjusted parallactically to account for the Earth's rotation.  望远镜经过视差调整以考虑地球的自转。| 近义词: parallax-based |反义词:  (No direct antonym exists. Consider phrases like "without considering parallax" or "ignoring parallax.") |词性: 副词

parallactic:/ˌpærəˈlæktɪk/| adj.|视差的|Parallactic measurements are crucial for determining the distances to nearby stars. 视差测量对于确定附近恒星的距离至关重要。|The parallactic angle changes as the Earth orbits the Sun. 视差角随着地球绕太阳运行而变化。| 近义词: parallax-related, parallax |反义词:  (No direct antonym) |词性: 形容词
