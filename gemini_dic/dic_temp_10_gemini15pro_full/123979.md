mormondom:/ˈmɔːrməndəm/| n.| 摩门教；摩门教徒；摩门教世界|The cultural or social system of Mormons.  A collective term for Mormons and their practices.|He was raised in Mormondom. 他在摩门教的环境中长大。|近义词: Mormonism, the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints|反义词: None (lacks a direct opposite; could consider other religions as antonyms in certain contexts)|词性: Noun


Etymology:
From Mormon + -dom.  "Mormon" comes from the Book of Mormon, a sacred text of the Latter Day Saint movement, named after a prophet-historian figure within the text. "-dom" is an Old English suffix denoting state, condition, or domain.
