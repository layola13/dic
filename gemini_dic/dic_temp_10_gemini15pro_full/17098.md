bats:/bæts/ | n. | 蝙蝠；球棒；球拍 |A nocturnal flying mammal, typically having large ears and navigating by echolocation; a specially shaped wooden club used for hitting the ball in various games. | Bats fly at night. 蝙蝠在夜间飞行。 |  He swung the baseball bat. 他挥舞着棒球棒。 | 近义词：(mammal) flying fox, (club) club, racket, stick | 反义词: (For the animal sense, there isn't a direct opposite) | 词性: noun


bats:/bæts/ | v. (third-person singular simple present bats, present participle batting, simple past and past participle batted) | (棒球)击球；眨(眼)；拍打 |To hit (a ball) with a bat; to flutter or flap (eyelashes or wings) rapidly. | He bats left-handed.  他用左手击球。 | She batted her eyelashes flirtatiously. 她调情地眨着眼睛。| 近义词：(hit) strike, hit, (flutter) flutter, blink | 反义词：(hit) miss | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English "batt," meaning club, stick.  Ultimately of uncertain origin, possibly related to a Gaulish word meaning to beat. The animal sense developed later, likely in the 16th century, due to the resemblance of the animal's wings to a club or stick.
