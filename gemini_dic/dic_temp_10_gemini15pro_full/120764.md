misappoint:/ˌmɪsəˈpɔɪnt/ | vt. | 使失望 | To fail to fulfill the hopes or expectations of (someone). | I was disappointed that the movie was not as good as I had hoped.  这部电影没有我想象中的好，我很失望。 | The team's performance disappointed their fans. 球队的表现让球迷们失望。 | 近义词：discourage, disillusion, let down | 反义词：encourage, please, delight | 词性：动词

Etymology:
From mis- + appoint, from Old French desapointer, from des- (expressing reversal) + apointer (to appoint).
