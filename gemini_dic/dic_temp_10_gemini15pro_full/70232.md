ficklenesses: /ˈfɪkəlnəsɪz/
n.
反复无常；善变；变化无常
The fickleness of the weather made it difficult to plan a picnic.
天气变化无常，很难计划野餐。

ficklenesses: /ˈfɪkəlnəsɪz/
n.
易变；不稳定
The fickleness of public opinion can be frustrating for politicians.
公众舆论的反复无常可能会让政治家感到沮丧。

ficklenesses: /ˈfɪkəlnəsɪz/
n.
薄情；无情
He was tired of her ficklenesses and decided to end the relationship.
他厌倦了她的薄情寡义，决定结束这段关系。



Etymology: From Middle English *fikelnesse*, from Old English *ficolnes*, from *ficol* (“deceitful, fickle”) + *-nes* (suffix forming abstract nouns).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *fīkulaz* (“changeable, unreliable”).


Synonyms: capriciousness, volatility, instability, changeability, inconstancy, mutability, variability, flightiness, whimsy, erraticism

Antonyms:  constancy, stability, steadfastness, loyalty, faithfulness, commitment
