ecclesiasticism: /ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪsɪzəm/
n.
教会主义; 教会制度;  注重教会仪式;
Emphasis on church practices and traditions; excessive formality and traditionalism in religion.
The archbishop's sermon was a prime example of ecclesiasticism, focusing more on ritual than spiritual experience.  大主教的布道是教会主义的一个主要例子，他更注重仪式而不是精神体验。
The new priest's strict adherence to tradition was seen by some as excessive ecclesiasticism. 新任神父对传统的严格遵守被一些人视为过分的教会主义。
synonym: clericalism, sectarianism, ritualism
antonym: secularism, nonconformity
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *ecclesiasticismus, from ecclesiastice (“ecclesiastical”) + -ismus (“-ism”).  Ultimately from Ancient Greek ἐκκλησιαστικός (ekklēsiastikós, “of the assembly, of the church”) +‎ -ισμός (-ismós).
