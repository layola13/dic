ludendorff:/ˈluːdəndɔːrf/
proper noun|
鲁登道夫|
Erich Ludendorff (1865-1937) was a German general, victor of Liège and Tannenberg, and, with Paul von Hindenburg, virtual dictator of Germany during the latter part of World War I. He later became a prominent nationalist politician and supporter of the Nazi Party.|
Ludendorff played a key role in the German war effort during World War I.|
Synonym: Hindenburg (in the context of their shared leadership)|
Antonym: None (proper nouns generally don't have antonyms)|
