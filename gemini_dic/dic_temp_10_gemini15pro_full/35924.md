cocultivating:/koʊˈkʌltɪveɪtɪŋ/
v.
共同培养，协同种植，共同发展
The act of growing two or more different types of organisms together, typically in close proximity, so that they can interact and potentially benefit from each other.
Researchers are cocultivating different bacterial strains to study their interactions. 研究人员正在共同培养不同的细菌菌株，以研究它们的相互作用。
Cocultivating cancer cells with immune cells can help researchers understand how the immune system responds to cancer. 将癌细胞与免疫细胞共同培养可以帮助研究人员了解免疫系统如何对癌症做出反应。

近义词:co-culturing, joint cultivation, collaborative cultivation
反义词: monoculturing, separate cultivation

词性: Verb (present participle of cocultivate)

词源:  "co-" (together) + "cultivate" (to nurture or grow). "Cultivate" comes from Latin "cultivare,"  from "cultus" (tilled, cultivated) + "-are" (frequentative suffix).
