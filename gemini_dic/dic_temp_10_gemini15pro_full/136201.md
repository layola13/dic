nonreliableness:/ˌnɒnrɪˈlaɪəbəlnəs/| n.|不可靠性, 不可靠|The nonreliableness of the data makes it difficult to draw any conclusions.  数据的不可靠性使得难以得出任何结论。|例句：The nonreliableness of the witness testimony led to the case being dismissed.  证人证词的不可靠性导致案件被驳回。|近义词：unreliability, undependability, precariousness|反义词: reliability, dependability, trustworthiness|词性:名词

Etymology: non- + reliable + -ness.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Reliable" comes from the Old French "relier" meaning "to tie back," implying something that can be counted on.  "-ness" is a suffix that forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
