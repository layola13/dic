dawsonite:/ˈdɔːsənˌaɪt/ | n. |片钠铝石|A carbonate mineral, NaAlCO3(OH)2, typically occurring as white bladed or fibrous crystals in sedimentary and metamorphic rocks, often associated with oil shale.  It is named after the Canadian geologist Sir John William Dawson.|Dawsonite was discovered in Montreal, Canada.  片钠铝石是在加拿大蒙特利尔发现的。|Synonyms:  No exact synonyms, but other related carbonate minerals include nahcolite and trona. | Antonyms: None. | noun
