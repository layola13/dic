dishonour:/dɪsˈɒnə(r)/ (UK) /dɪsˈɑːnər/ (US)| n. vt.|耻辱，丢脸；拒付|lack of respect or honesty; refusal to pay a cheque or accept a bill of exchange|例句：His actions brought dishonour to his family. 他的行为给他的家庭带来了耻辱。|近义词：shame, disgrace, ignominy|反义词：honour, respect|词性:名词,动词

dishonour:/dɪsˈɒnə(r)/ (UK) /dɪsˈɑːnər/ (US)| vt.|使丢脸，玷辱；拒付|to cause someone or something to lose honour or respect; to refuse to pay a cheque or accept a bill of exchange|例句：The bank dishonoured my cheque. 银行拒付了我的支票。|近义词：disgrace, shame, stigmatize|反义词：honour, respect|词性:动词

词源: From Old French *deshonor, from Latin dis- "not" + honor "honor".
