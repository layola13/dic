antiperistasis:/ˌæntɪpɛrɪˈsteɪsɪs/
noun
反环境, 逆环境作用, 增强作用
The doctrine that a quality, faculty, or feeling is increased by the presence of its opposite; also, the supposed intensification of a quality by the opposition of its contrary.  Specifically:  (a)  An increase in the intensity of a quality or function due to the constraint of its opposite. (b) The doctrine that cold is intensified by the surrounding heat.
The alchemists believed in antiperistasis, arguing that fire was intensified by being surrounded by water.
近义词: intensification, augmentation, strengthening
反义词: weakening, diminution, reduction
词性: n.
