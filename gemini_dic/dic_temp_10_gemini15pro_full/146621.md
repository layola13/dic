overhandicapping: /ˌoʊvərˈhændɪˌkæpɪŋ/
| n. | 过度让分；故意示弱 | The act of deliberately handicapping oneself or exaggerating one's weaknesses, often as a strategy to lower expectations or avoid pressure to perform. |  He was accused of overhandicapping himself by claiming to be ill before the race. 他被指责在比赛前声称自己生病是故意示弱。| 近义词: sandbagging, downplaying, feigning weakness | 反义词: boasting, exaggerating strengths | 词性: 名词

overhandicapping: /ˌoʊvərˈhændɪˌkæpɪŋ/
| v. | 过度让分；故意示弱 | To deliberately handicap oneself or exaggerate one's weaknesses. | He overhandicapped himself by staying up all night before the exam. 他在考试前熬夜，故意削弱了自己的实力。| 近义词: sandbag, downplay, feign weakness | 反义词: boast, exaggerate strengths | 词性: 动词
