coacervate: /koʊˈæsərveɪt/ /kəʊˈæsəveɪt/
n. |凝聚层;凝聚体|A tiny spherical droplet of assorted organic molecules (specifically, lipids, amino acids, and nucleic acids) held together by electrostatic forces; thought to have been a precursor to the first cells.|Laboratory simulations of early Earth conditions have produced coacervates. 早期地球环境的实验室模拟已经产生了凝聚层。|
Coacervate droplets can absorb substances from their surroundings. 凝聚层液滴可以从周围环境中吸收物质。|
synonyms: aggregate, cluster, clump|
antonyms: dispersion, solution|
noun


coacervate: /koʊˈæsərveɪt/ /kəʊˈæsəveɪt/
v. |凝聚; (使)形成凝聚层|To cause (a colloid) to separate into two liquid phases or to form coacervates.|The experiment demonstrated how certain molecules can coacervate under specific conditions.  该实验演示了某些分子如何在特定条件下凝聚。|
The researchers coacervated the proteins in the solution. 研究人员使溶液中的蛋白质凝聚。|
synonyms: aggregate, coalesce, condense|
antonyms: disperse, dissolve, separate|
verb
