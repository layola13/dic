anchoretism:/ˈæŋkəˌraɪtɪzəm/| n.|隐居；遁世；禁欲主义|The state or practice of living the life of an anchorite; seclusion or solitude, especially for religious reasons.  A hermit-like existence dedicated to contemplation and prayer.|He chose a life of anchoretism, retreating to a remote cabin in the woods.|近义词：asceticism, seclusion, solitude, hermitage|反义词：sociability, gregariousness|词性:noun
Etymology: From Late Latin *anchorētismus, from Ancient Greek ἀναχωρητισμός (anakhōrētismos, “withdrawal, retirement”), from ἀναχωρέω (anakhōreō, “I withdraw, retire”), from ἀνά (aná, “up, back”) + χωρέω (khōreō, “I give way, go, withdraw”).
