flagellators:/ˈflædʒəˌleɪtərz/| n. |鞭毛虫|A microscopic organism that moves using a whip-like appendage called a flagellum.|Many flagellators live in freshwater environments. 许多鞭毛虫生活在淡水环境中。|Flagellates, protozoa, mastigophora|cilia, pseudopods|noun
flagellum:/fləˈdʒɛləm/| n. |鞭毛|A whip-like appendage that enables many protozoa, bacteria, spermatozoa, etc. to swim.|The flagellum propels the bacterium through the liquid. 鞭毛推动细菌在液体中前进。|cilium, pseudopod| |noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *flagellator*, from *flagellare* "to whip," from *flagellum* "whip, scourge."
