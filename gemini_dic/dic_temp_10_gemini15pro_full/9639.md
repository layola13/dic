antistructuralists: /ˈæntiˈstrʌktʃərəlɪsts/
|n.|反结构主义者|Those who oppose or critique structuralism, a theoretical approach that emphasizes the importance of underlying structures in language, culture, and other systems.|The antistructuralists argued that meaning is not fixed but rather fluid and constantly negotiated.|近义词: post-structuralists, deconstructionists|反义词: structuralists|词性: 名词

antistructuralist: /ˈæntiˈstrʌktʃərəlɪst/
|adj.|反结构主义的|Relating to or characteristic of antistructuralism.|The antistructuralist movement challenged the established norms of literary criticism.|近义词: post-structuralist, deconstructionist|反义词: structuralist|词性: 形容词

词源：anti- (against) + structuralist.  "Anti-" comes from Greek *anti, meaning "against, opposite of, instead of, in return". "Structuralist" comes from "structure" + "-ist."  "Structure" ultimately derives from Latin *struere* meaning "to build." "-ist" is a suffix forming agent nouns.
