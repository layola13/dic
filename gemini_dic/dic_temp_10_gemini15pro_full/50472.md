depressed:/dɪˈprɛst/ /dɪˈprɛst/| adj.|沮丧的，抑郁的，萧条的|Feeling very sad and without hope. Characterized by a state of unhappiness and low spirits.|例句：She felt depressed after losing her job. 她失业后感到很沮丧。

例句：The economy is currently in a depressed state. 目前的经济状况很不景气。|近义词：sad, unhappy, down, dejected, gloomy, melancholy, blue|反义词：happy, cheerful, elated, joyful|词性:adjective

depressed:/dɪˈprɛst/ /dɪˈprɛst/| vt.|使沮丧，使萧条|To make someone feel very sad and without hope. To reduce the level of activity in something, especially a business or economy.|例句：The bad news depressed him. 这个坏消息使他沮丧。

例句：The recession has depressed the housing market. 经济衰退使房地产市场萧条。|近义词：sadden, discourage, dampen, weaken|反义词：encourage, uplift, boost, strengthen|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin depressus, past participle of deprimere (“to press down”), from Latin de- (“down”) + premere (“to press”).
