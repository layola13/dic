democratizations: /dɪˌmɒkrətɪˈzeɪʃənz/ /dɪˌmɑːkrətɪˈzeɪʃənz/
|n.|民主化|The process of making something more democratic, involving increasing public participation in political decision-making or broadening access to resources and opportunities.|The democratizations of Eastern Europe after the fall of the Berlin Wall were a significant historical event. 柏林墙倒塌后东欧的民主化进程是一个重大的历史事件。|
|Synonym: liberalizations, reforms|Antonym: authoritarianism, oppression|Noun|

democratizations: /dɪˌmɒkrətɪˈzeɪʃənz/ /dɪˌmɑːkrətɪˈzeɪʃənz/
|Etymology:  From democratization + -s (plural suffix). Democratization comes from democratize + -ation. Democratize comes from democracy + -ize. Democracy ultimately derives from the Greek words "demos" (people) and "kratos" (power).|词源：来自democratization + -s（复数后缀）。Democratization来自democratize + -ation。Democratize来自democracy + -ize。Democracy最终源自希腊语单词“demos”（人民）和“kratos”（权力）。
