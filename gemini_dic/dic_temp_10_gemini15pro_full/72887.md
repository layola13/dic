follow-up's:/ˈfɒləʊ ʌps/ /ˈfɑːloʊ ʌps/ | n. |后续行动，后续跟进，跟进（复数）| An action or actions that are done after and as a result of a previous event or situation.  It often refers to additional actions taken after an initial meeting, communication, or treatment. |  例句：The doctor scheduled several follow-up's to monitor the patient's progress. 医生安排了几次后续检查以监测病人的进展。

例句：These follow-up's are crucial for ensuring the project stays on track.  这些后续行动对于确保项目按计划进行至关重要。| 近义词：check-in, review, sequel, continuation | 反义词: prelude, beginning, initiation | 词性:名词

follow-up:/ˈfɒləʊ ʌp/ /ˈfɑːloʊ ʌp/ | vt. |跟进，追查 |To take additional actions after and as a result of a previous event or situation, typically to check on progress or ensure completion. |  例句：I need to follow up on that lead. 我需要跟进那个线索。

例句：The police are following up on several reports of suspicious activity. 警方正在跟进几起可疑活动的报告。| 近义词：pursue, investigate, check in on | 反义词: initiate, begin, start | 词性:动词


词源： "follow" (追随) + "up" (向上，完全地).  The original sense implied a complete and thorough following of something, which evolved into the modern sense of taking further action after an initial event.
