drench:/drɛntʃ/
verb
浸透，湿透
to make someone or something completely wet
例句：The sudden downpour drenched us to the skin. 突如其来的倾盆大雨把我们淋得浑身湿透。

例句：The athletes were drenched in sweat after the marathon. 马拉松比赛后，运动员们汗流浃背。
近义词：soak, saturate, deluge
反义词：dry
词性：v.


drench:/drɛntʃ/
adjective (古语，罕用)
湿透的
soaked
例句:  His drench cloak clung to him. 他湿透的斗篷紧紧地贴在他身上。(古语用法，现在很少使用)

例句: The drench fields steamed in the morning sun.  湿透的田野在早晨的阳光下冒着热气。(古语用法，现在很少使用)
近义词：soaked, saturated
反义词：dry
词性：adj.


词源：
From Middle English *drenchen, from Old English drencan (“to cause to drink, give drink to, drown, submerge”), from Proto-Germanic *drankjaną (“to cause to drink, give drink to”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰrengʰ- (“to draw, pull, drag”). Cognate with Dutch drenken (“to water, irrigate, soak”), German tränken (“to give drink to, soak, saturate”), Old Norse drekkja (“to drown, sink”).
