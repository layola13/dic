free-and-clear:/ˌfriː ænd ˈklɪr/
adjective
完全的，彻底的；无债务的，没有抵押的
Completely free of encumbrances or obligations, especially financial ones.  Possessing something outright, without any debts or other claims against it.
The house is finally ours, free and clear. 这房子终于完全属于我们了，没有任何债务。
He walked away from the accident free and clear. 他安然无恙地离开了事故现场。
近义词:unencumbered, debt-free, clear
反义词:mortgaged, indebted, encumbered
词性:adjective

free:/friː/
adjective
自由的，免费的
Not under the control or in the power of another; costing nothing.
Birds are free to fly where they want. 鸟儿可以自由地飞到任何想去的地方。
The drinks are free tonight. 今晚的饮料是免费的。

and:/ænd, ənd, ən/
conjunction
和，与，而且
Used to connect words of the same part of speech, clauses, or sentences, that are to be taken jointly.
He plays the guitar and the piano. 他会弹吉他和歌唱。
She is smart and beautiful. 她既聪明又漂亮。

clear:/klɪr/
adjective, adverb, verb
清晰的，清楚的；完全地；清除
Easy to perceive, understand, or interpret; completely; remove an obstruction.
The water is so clear you can see the bottom. 水很清澈，你可以看到底部。
He stated his intentions clear and concisely. 他清晰而简洁地陈述了他的意图。


词源:
The phrase "free and clear" originated in legal contexts, specifically property law. "Free" refers to being free from restrictions or encumbrances, while "clear" emphasizes the absence of any other claims or debts. The combination emphasizes the absolute and uncontested ownership or possession of something.
