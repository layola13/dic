gentrifiers:/ˈdʒɛntrɪfaɪərz/ | n. | 中产阶级化推动者；仕绅化推手 | People who move into a working-class neighborhood, causing property values to rise and often displacing the original residents.  |  The gentrifiers renovated the old buildings, but many long-time residents were forced to move out due to rising rents. 仕绅化推动者翻新了旧建筑，但许多长期居民由于租金上涨而被迫搬走。|  Synonyms: urban pioneers, renovators,  developers (in some contexts) | Antonyms: locals, original residents | noun


gentrifier:/ˈdʒɛntrɪfaɪər/ | n. | 中产阶级化推动者；仕绅化推手 | A person who moves into a working-class neighborhood, causing property values to rise and often displacing the original residents. | The influx of gentrifiers transformed the neighborhood, bringing with it trendy cafes and boutiques.  仕绅化推动者的涌入改变了这个社区，带来了时尚的咖啡馆和精品店。 | Synonyms: urban pioneer, renovator,  developer (in some contexts) | Antonyms: local, original resident | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "gentrify," which is derived from "gentry" (upper or ruling class) + "-fy" (to make or become). The term reflects the socioeconomic changes that occur when wealthier residents move into traditionally working-class or lower-income areas.
