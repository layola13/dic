drawled:/drɔːld/ | v. | 慢吞吞地说着 | spoke in a slow, lazy way, often prolonging vowels. |  He drawled his greetings in a Southern accent. 他用南方口音慢吞吞地打招呼。|  The cashier drawled, "Next in line, please." 收银员慢吞吞地说：“下一位。” | 近义词: droned, mumbled | 反义词:  articulated, enunciated, snapped |词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From *drawl* + *-ed*. *Drawl* likely originated as a frequentative of *draw*, suggesting the stretching out of words like pulling something.
