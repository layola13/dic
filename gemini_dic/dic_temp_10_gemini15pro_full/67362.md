extensionality's:/ɪkˌstɛnʃənˈælɪtiz/ | n. | 外延性 | The quality or state of being extensional; the principle that the meaning or identity of a concept or property is determined solely by its extension (the set of things to which it applies). |  Philosophers debate the implications of extensionality's role in defining concepts. 哲学家们争论外延性在定义概念中的作用的含义。| 近义词: extension, denotation | 反义词: intension, connotation | 词性: noun


词源: From "extensional" + "-ity" + possessive suffix "-'s". "Extensional" comes from Late Latin "extensionalis," from "extensio" (stretching out), from "extendere" (to stretch out).
