obscurant:/əbˈskjʊrənt/ | adj. n. | 模糊的，晦涩的；蒙蔽者，遮蔽物 |Something that obscures, makes unclear, or hinders understanding.  As a noun, it refers to a person who deliberately makes things unclear. | The writer's style is obscurant, full of jargon and long sentences.  这位作者的风格晦涩难懂，充满了术语和长句。| Synonyms: confusing, unclear, ambiguous, vague, equivocal, enigmatic | Antonyms: clear, lucid, transparent, explicit, unambiguous | adjective

obscurant:/əbˈskjʊrənt/ | n. | 模糊的，晦涩的；蒙蔽者，遮蔽物 | A person who deliberately obscures or hinders understanding. | The politician was accused of being an obscurant, deliberately hiding the truth from the public. 这位政治家被指责是一个蒙蔽者，故意向公众隐瞒真相。| Synonyms: obfuscator, prevaricator, equivocator | Antonyms: explainer, clarifier, elucidator | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin obscurans, present participle of obscurare "to darken," from Latin obscurus "dark, dim, indistinct."
