flip-flopper:/ˈflɪpˌflɑːpər/ | n. |反复无常的人；立场摇摆不定的人|Someone who changes their opinion or position frequently, especially in politics. | The senator was labeled a flip-flopper for changing his stance on the issue.  这位参议员因为改变了对这个问题的立场而被贴上了反复无常的标签。| Example:  He's such a flip-flopper - he keeps changing his mind about everything! 他真是个反复无常的人——他对每件事都改变主意！| Synonyms: waverer, turncoat, opportunist | Antonyms: stalwart, steadfast, resolute | Noun


flip-flop:/ˈflɪpˌflɑːp/ | n. |人字拖；触发器|A type of sandal, typically made of rubber or plastic; a device that stores a single bit of data. | I wore my flip-flops to the beach. 我穿着人字拖去了海滩。| Example: The computer's memory uses millions of flip-flops. 电脑的内存使用数百万个触发器。| Synonyms: (for sandal) thong, sandal; (for device) toggle | Antonyms: (for device) latch | Noun


flip-flop:/ˈflɪpˌflɑːp/ | v. |(观点或立场) 改变；(来回地) 翻转|To change an opinion or position suddenly; to turn over or cause something to turn over with a light quick movement. | The candidate flip-flopped on several key issues during the campaign. 这位候选人在竞选期间在几个关键问题上改变了立场。| Example:  The fish flip-flopped on the deck of the boat. 鱼在船的甲板上翻来覆去。| Synonyms:  (for change opinion) backpedal, reverse, U-turn; (for turn over) flop, flap | Antonyms: (for change opinion)  stand firm, hold fast | Verb
