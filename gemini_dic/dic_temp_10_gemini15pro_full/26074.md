bushrangers:/ˈbʊʃˌreɪndʒərz/
| n. |丛林强盗，绿林好汉|Australian outlaw living and operating in the bush; originally, an escaped convict.  A person who lived in the Australian bush and robbed people, especially in the 19th century. |例句：The bushrangers held up the stagecoach and stole the gold. 丛林强盗拦截了驿站马车，偷走了黄金。

例句：Ned Kelly is perhaps the most famous of Australia's bushrangers. 内德·凯利也许是澳大利亚最著名的丛林强盗。|近义词：outlaw, bandit, robber, highwayman|反义词:lawman, police officer|词性:noun
