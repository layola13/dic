gonya:/ˈɡɔnjə/ (Pronunciation may vary regionally) | n. | 骗子，狡猾的人;  诡计，骗局 | A cunning or deceitful person; a trick or swindle. |  He's a real gonya, always trying to get something for nothing. 他真是个骗子，总是想不劳而获。| Synonym:  trickster, con artist, swindler, cheat, fraud, rogue | Antonym: honest person, dupe | noun


Etymology->

Gonya is thought to be derived from Yiddish גנבֿ (ganev), meaning "thief."  It has entered colloquial English, especially in Australian and South African English.  The Yiddish word itself comes from Hebrew גנב (ganav), also meaning "thief."
