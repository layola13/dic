monolatry:/ˈmɒnəˌlætri/ (US) /ˈmɑːnəˌleɪtri/| n. |一神崇拜; 獨神崇拜|The worship of one god without denying the existence of other gods.  It differs from henotheism, which is the worship of one god at a time.|Ancient Egyptians practiced a form of monolatry, focusing on a particular deity in a specific region or time period. 古埃及人实行一种一神崇拜的形式，在特定地区或时期专注于一个特定的神灵。|Synonyms: henotheism (often used interchangeably, though with subtle differences), restricted polytheism|Antonyms: polytheism, atheism, pantheism|Noun
