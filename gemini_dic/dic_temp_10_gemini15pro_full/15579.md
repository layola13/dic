Etymology:  From Bahāʼī,  the adjective for followers of Bahāʼu'llāh (meaning "Glory of God"), the founder of the Baha'i Faith.  The term is derived from the Arabic word Bahāʼ (بهاء), meaning "splendor" or "glory."
