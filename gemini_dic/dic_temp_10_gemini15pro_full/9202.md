antiorganization:/ˌæntiˌɔːrɡənɪˈzeɪʃn/
n.|反组织；非组织；反对组织|A sentiment or philosophy that opposes formal organization and structure, often favoring individual autonomy and decentralized networks.|The antiorganization movement gained traction in the 1960s.  反组织运动在20世纪60年代获得了关注。|
antiorganization:/ˌæntiˌɔːrɡənɪˈzeɪʃn/
n.|反组织；非组织；反对组织|A group or entity that actively disrupts or opposes established organizations or systems.|Some hackers consider themselves part of an antiorganization, challenging corporate control of information. 一些黑客认为自己是反组织的一部分，挑战企业对信息的控制。|
antiorganization:/ˌæntiˌɔːrɡənɪˈzeɪʃn/
adj.|反组织的|Characterized by opposition to formal organization and structure.|His antiorganization stance made it difficult for him to work within a traditional corporate hierarchy.  他反组织的立场使他难以在传统的企业等级制度中工作。|synonyms:  nonconformist, rebellious, iconoclastic, dissident, anarchist, decentralized|antonyms:  organized, structured, hierarchical, conventional, conformist|noun, adjective
