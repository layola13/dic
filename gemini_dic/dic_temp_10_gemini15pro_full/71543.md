flare:/fler/ | n. vi. |闪光; 耀斑; 照明弹; (衣裙等)底部张开;  爆发; 突然加剧|A sudden brief burst of bright flame or light. A sudden outburst of anger or excitement.  To burn with a sudden unsteady flame. To (cause to) become wider towards the bottom.|
The match flared and went out. 火柴闪了一下就灭了。
Temperatures flared again between the two countries. 两国之间的局势再度紧张起来。
The skirt flares out from the waist. 裙子从腰部向下展开。
His nostrils flared with anger. 他气得鼻孔都张大了。
|近义词：blaze, flash, burst, erupt, widen, spread |反义词：diminish, dwindle, contract, narrow |词性: noun, verb


flare:/fler/ | v. |闪耀, 燃烧, (使)爆发, (使)突然发作, (衣裙等)张开|To burn with a sudden unsteady flame. To suddenly become angry, violent, or active. To (cause to) become wider towards the bottom.|
The fire flared up again. 火又突然烧起来了。
Violence flared after the football match. 足球赛后突然爆发了暴力事件。
The jeans flare at the ankle. 这条牛仔裤裤腿在脚踝处张开。|近义词：blaze, flash, burst, erupt, widen, spread |反义词：diminish, dwindle, contract, narrow |词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *flēarian (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *flairōną (“to blow, flutter”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰleh₂- (“to blow”). Cognate with Old Norse flæra (“to flutter, wave, flap”), Danish flære ("to grin, sneer"), Swedish flära (“to flutter, stream, fan”).
