eriach:/ˈɛriːæk/ | n. | 赔偿；赎金；人命价 |  A fine or payment made to the family of a murdered person, especially under Brehon law in ancient Ireland.  It represents the value of a person's life and serves as compensation for their death. |  The eriach for the slain chieftain was substantial, consisting of cattle and land.  被杀酋长的赔偿金数额巨大，包括牛和土地。| synonyms: compensation, restitution, recompense, indemnity, reparation | antonyms: penalty, fine, forfeiture | noun
