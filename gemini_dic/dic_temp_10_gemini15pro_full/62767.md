engrain:/ɪnˈɡreɪn/| vt.|使根深蒂固，牢牢铭记|to fix deeply and firmly, as in the mind or memory|The lessons of childhood are often deeply engrained in our minds. 童年的教训常常深深地铭刻在我们的脑海中。|近义词：implant, instill, embed, fix, root|反义词：erase, forget, uproot|词性:动词

engrained:/ɪnˈɡreɪnd/ | adj.|根深蒂固的|firmly fixed or established; deeply rooted|His ingrained prejudices made it difficult for him to accept new ideas. 他根深蒂固的偏见使他难以接受新思想。|近义词：deep-rooted, fixed, inherent, inveterate|反义词：superficial, shallow|词性:形容词

词源:
From Old French *engraignier*, from *en-* (in) + *grain* (grain, seed).  The original sense was to dye in the raw grain or kermes, hence to dye deeply and permanently.
