discolor:/dɪˈskʌlər/ /dɪsˈkʌlɚ/| v. |使变色；使褪色；玷污|To change the color of something, often in a way that makes it look less attractive.|The intense sunlight discolored the curtains. 强烈的阳光使窗帘褪色了。|近义词：stain, tarnish, fade, bleach|反义词: brighten, color, enhance|词性:动词

discolor:/dɪˈskʌlər/ /dɪsˈkʌlɚ/| adj. |变色的；褪色的|Having lost its original color.|The discolored photographs brought back memories of childhood. 褪色的照片唤起了童年的回忆。|近义词：faded, stained, bleached|反义词: bright, colorful|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *descolorer, from des- (expressing reversal) + colorer (to color).  Ultimately from Latin dis- (apart, away) and color (color).
