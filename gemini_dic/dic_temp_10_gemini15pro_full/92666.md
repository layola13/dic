icarian: /ɪˈkɛəriən/ /aɪˈkɛəriən/
adjective
伊卡洛斯的；轻率冒险的；注定要失败的
Relating to Icarus, the mythical figure who flew too close to the sun on wings of wax and feathers; characterized by rash, ambitious aspirations that are likely to fail.
His Icarian ambition led to his downfall. 他伊卡洛斯式的野心导致了他的垮台。

icarian: /ɪˈkɛəriən/ /aɪˈkɛəriən/
noun
轻率冒险的人；注定要失败的人
A person who is rashly ambitious and likely to fail.
Many tech entrepreneurs are seen as modern-day Icarians, aiming for the sun but often crashing back down to earth. 许多科技企业家被视为现代的伊卡洛斯，目标是太阳，但往往会坠落回地面。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Icarus, the son of Daedalus in Greek mythology. Icarus was given wings made of feathers and wax but ignored his father's warnings and flew too close to the sun, melting the wax and causing him to fall to his death.

SYNONYMS->
rash, reckless, audacious, foolhardy, impetuous, overambitious, doomed, hubristic

ANTONYMS->
cautious, prudent, careful, circumspect, realistic, grounded
