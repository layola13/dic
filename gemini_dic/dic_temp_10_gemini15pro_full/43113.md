coxing: /ˈkɒksɪŋ/ (UK), /ˈkɑːksɪŋ/ (US)| n.| 舵手；指导；指挥| The act of steering and directing a racing boat, especially a rowing boat.  The coxswain gives instructions to the rowers regarding pace, technique, and strategy.|  The coxing for the Cambridge crew was excellent.  剑桥队的舵手表现出色。| Synonyms: steering, piloting, navigating| Antonyms: rowing, paddling| noun

coxing: /ˈkɒksɪŋ/ (UK), /ˈkɑːksɪŋ/ (US)| v.| 指挥；指导；当舵手|  To act as a coxswain, steering and directing a racing boat. |  He was coxing the eight for the first time. 他第一次指挥八人赛艇。| Synonyms: steering, piloting, navigating | Antonyms: rowing, paddling| verb
