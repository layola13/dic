cowpoxes: /ˈkaʊpɒksɪz/ | n. |牛痘|A mild disease, especially of children, caused by the vaccinia virus and characterized by eruptions that resemble those of smallpox but are less severe.  It is also a term sometimes used to refer to localized infections with cowpox virus. | The child had cowpoxes and had to stay home from school.  这个孩子得了牛痘，不得不待在家里不去上学。|Synonym: vaccinia | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a disease, but one could consider "health" in a broad sense) | noun

cowpox: /ˈkaʊpɒks/ | n. |牛痘|A contagious disease of cows, caused by the cowpox virus. Can infect humans, causing localized lesions. Historically used to create the first smallpox vaccines.| The farmer contracted cowpox from milking his infected cow. 农民因挤感染奶牛的牛奶而感染了牛痘。|Synonym: vaccinia (when referring to the human infection)| Antonym: (No direct antonym for a disease) | noun
