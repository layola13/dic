marshiness:/ˈmɑːrʃɪnɪs/ /ˈmɑːrʃɪnəs/
noun
沼泽性；湿软；泥泞
The state or quality of being marshy; the softness and wetness of marsh land.
The marshiness of the ground made it difficult to walk.  这块地泥泞不堪，很难行走。
Near synonyms:  bogginess, swampiness, sogginess, quagginess, miriness
Antonyms:  firmness, solidity, dryness, aridity
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From marsh + -iness.  Marsh ultimately comes from the Old English *mersc, related to Dutch moeras and German Marsch. The suffix -iness forms nouns indicating a state or quality.
