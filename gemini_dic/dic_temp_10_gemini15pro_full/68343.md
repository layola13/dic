fall short:/fɔːl ˈʃɔːrt/
verb phrase
达不到；不符合；缺乏
fail to reach a desired standard, level, or amount.
The movie fell short of my expectations. 这部电影没有达到我的期望。
The company's profits fell short of analysts' estimates. 该公司的利润低于分析师的估计。
近义词: underperform, disappoint, fail to meet, be deficient, lack
反义词: exceed, surpass, excel, achieve, fulfill
词性: 动词短语

ETYMOLOGY->
"fall" (Old English *feallan*, from Proto-Germanic *fallaną) meaning "to descend freely by the force of gravity."
"short" (Old English *sceort*, from Proto-Germanic *skurta-) meaning "not long," also "insufficient," "lacking."
The phrase "fall short" combines these meanings to convey the idea of not reaching a desired length or standard.
