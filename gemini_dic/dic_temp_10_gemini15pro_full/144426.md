outsulks:/aʊtˈsʌlks/
verb
超过…的生气时间；比…更长时间地生闷气
to sulk for a longer time than someone else
He outsulked everyone else after the argument. 争吵之后，他比任何人都生闷气的时间长。
近义词: outlast, surpass in sulking
反义词: forgive, reconcile


ETYMOLOGY->
"outsulk" is a combination of the prefix "out-" (meaning "to surpass or exceed") and the verb "sulk" (meaning "to be silent, morose, and bad-tempered out of annoyance or disappointment").  The word likely emerged in the late 19th or early 20th century, building upon the existing verb "sulk" which has roots in Middle English and Old English words related to being sluggish or gloomy.
