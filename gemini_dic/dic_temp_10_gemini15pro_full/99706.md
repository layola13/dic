interlope:/ˈɪntərˌloʊp/| v. n.|干涉，闯入，介入；干涉者，闯入者|To intrude or interfere in a place or situation where one is not wanted or has no right to be; a person who does this.|例句：Several insurance companies have interloped on their traditional territory.  几家保险公司侵入了他们的传统领域。|例句：New companies are interloping on their market. 新公司正在侵入他们的市场。|近义词：intrude, interfere, meddle, encroach|反义词： abstain, refrain|词性：动词，名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From inter- (between) + lope (to run).  Originally referring to unauthorized persons trading within the limits of an exclusive company.  From Dutch "loopen" (to run).
