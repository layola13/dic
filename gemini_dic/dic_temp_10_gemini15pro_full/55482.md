distractednesses: /dɪˈstræktɪdnɪsɪz/
|n.|心烦意乱；注意力分散|The state of being easily distracted; inability to concentrate.  A multitude of distractions resulted in a general distractedness among the students.|The student's distractedness in class was evident.  这个学生在课堂上的心不在焉很明显。|distraction, inattentiveness, absent-mindedness|focus, concentration, attentiveness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From distracted + -ness.  Distracted comes from the Latin *distractus*, past participle of *distrahere* "to pull apart, divide, distract," from *dis-* "apart" + *trahere* "to draw."
