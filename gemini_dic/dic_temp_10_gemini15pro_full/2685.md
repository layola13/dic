adrenaline:/əˈdrɛnəlɪn/| n. | 肾上腺素 | A hormone secreted by the adrenal glands, especially in conditions of stress, increasing rates of blood circulation, breathing, and carbohydrate metabolism and preparing muscles for exertion. |  The sudden danger triggered an adrenaline rush. 突然的危险引发了肾上腺素激增。

The athlete felt a surge of adrenaline before the race.  这位运动员在比赛前感到肾上腺素激增。 | 近义词: epinephrine | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but consider terms like "tranquilizer" or "sedative" for opposing effects) | 词性: noun

adrenalines:/əˈdrɛnəlɪnz/| n. | 肾上腺素（复数，非标准用法）|  While grammatically possible, the plural "adrenalines" is rarely used. Adrenaline is typically treated as a mass noun.  It refers to the hormone itself, not discrete units of it. |  (Generally avoid using this plural form. Stick to "adrenaline.") | 近义词: epinephrines (also uncommon) | 反义词: (Same as singular form) | 词性: noun (plural, non-standard)


ETYMOLOGY->
From French adrénaline, from Latin ad (to) + renalis (of the kidneys) + -ine (chemical suffix).
