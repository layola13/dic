entrancements:/ɪnˈtrænsmənts/
|n.|着迷，狂喜，入迷|A state of being entranced; a feeling of delight and wonder; rapture.|She listened to the music, lost in entrancement. 她听着音乐，完全陶醉其中。|近义词：fascination, rapture, captivation, spell, enchantment|反义词：disinterest, boredom, apathy
|noun|

entrancements:/ɪnˈtrænsmənts/
|n.|魅力，吸引力|Something that entrances or captivates; an allurement.|The beauty of the sunset was a true entrancement. 日落的美丽是一种真正的魅力。|近义词：allure, attraction, charm, magnetism|反义词：repulsion, aversion
|noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *entrauncement*, from Old French *entrancement*, from *entrancier* "to put into a trance," from *en-* "in" + *trancier* "to pierce," from Vulgar Latin *transire* "to pass over, cross," from Latin *trans-* "across" + *ire* "to go."
