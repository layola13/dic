dogmata:/ˈdɒɡmətə/ (UK), /ˈdɔːɡmɑːtə/ (US)| n. |教条，信条，教义|A dogma is a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true.|The dogma of the divine right of kings was widely accepted in medieval Europe.  欧洲中世纪普遍接受君权神授的教条。|Synonyms: tenet, doctrine, creed, belief|Antonyms: heresy, heterodoxy|Noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *dogmata*, plural of *dogma*, from Ancient Greek δόγμα (dógma, “opinion, tenet, ordinance, decree”), from δοκέω (dokéō, “I think, I seem, I appear”).
