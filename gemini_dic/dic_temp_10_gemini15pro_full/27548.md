canaanite:/ˈkeɪnənˌaɪt/| n. adj.|迦南人，迦南人的|A member of a people who lived in ancient Canaan (a region corresponding roughly to modern-day Israel, Palestine, Lebanon, and western Syria) in the 2nd millennium BCE.|The Canaanites were known for their advanced civilization and their worship of multiple gods.|近义词：Phoenician (sometimes), ancient Palestinian|反义词: Israelite|词性:noun, adjective

canaanite:/ˈkeɪnənˌaɪt/| adj.|迦南人的|Relating to the Canaanites or their culture.|Canaanite religion involved the worship of deities like Baal and Astarte.|近义词：Phoenician (sometimes)|反义词: Israelite|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Canaanīta, from Ancient Greek Χαναναῖος (Khanaanaîos), from Hebrew כְּנַעֲנִי (kənāʿănī), from כְּנָעַן (kənāʿan, “Canaan”). Ultimately of Semitic origin.
