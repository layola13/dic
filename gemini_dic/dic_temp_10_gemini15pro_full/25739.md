buoyancy:/ˈbɔɪənsi/ | n. | 浮力，轻快，乐观 | The ability to float in water or other fluids. The power of a liquid to keep things afloat. A feeling of cheerfulness and optimism. | The ship's buoyancy kept it afloat despite the damage. 这艘船的浮力使它在受损的情况下仍然漂浮着。| synonyms: lightness, floatability, cheerfulness, optimism | antonyms: heaviness, negativity, pessimism | noun

buoyant:/ˈbɔɪənt/ | adj. | 有浮力的，轻快的，乐观的 | Able to float easily; cheerful and optimistic. | The buoyant economy boosted investor confidence.  蓬勃发展的经济提振了投资者信心。| synonyms:  light, floating, cheerful, optimistic, jaunty | antonyms: heavy, sinking, depressed, pessimistic, gloomy | adjective

buoyantly:/ˈbɔɪəntli/ | adv. | 轻快地，乐观地 | In a buoyant manner; cheerfully and optimistically. |  He buoyantly greeted the news of his promotion. 他愉快地迎接了升职的消息。| synonyms: cheerfully, optimistically, lightly | antonyms: heavily, depressingly, pessimistically | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French "boie," meaning "buoy," from Dutch "boei," of uncertain origin.
