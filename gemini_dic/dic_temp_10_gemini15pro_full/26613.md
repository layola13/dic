cachexia: /kəˈkɛksiə/ | n. | 恶病质，极度消瘦 |  A condition of physical wasting away due to the loss of weight and muscle mass that occurs in patients with diseases such as advanced cancer or AIDS.  It is characterized by weakness and loss of appetite. | The cancer patient was suffering from cachexia.  这位癌症病人患有恶病质。

| The advanced stages of AIDS can lead to cachexia.  艾滋病晚期会导致恶病质。 |  Near synonyms:  wasting, atrophy, emaciation | Near antonyms:  obesity, corpulence | noun
