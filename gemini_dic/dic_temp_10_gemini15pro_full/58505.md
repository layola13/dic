duelling:/ˈdjuːəlɪŋ/ /ˈduːlɪŋ/| n. |决斗|The act of fighting a duel.  A formal fight between two people, usually with swords or pistols, intended to settle a point of honor.|例句：Duelling was common among gentlemen in the 18th century.  18世纪，决斗在绅士中很常见。

例句：The duelling between the two rivals ended in tragedy.  两个对手之间的决斗以悲剧告终。|近义词：fighting, combat, single combat|反义词:peacemaking, reconciliation|noun

duelling:/ˈdjuːəlɪŋ/ /ˈduːlɪŋ/| adj. |决斗的|Engaged in or used for duelling.|例句：Duelling pistols were often elaborately decorated.  决斗用的手枪通常装饰精美。

例句：The duelling scars on his face told a story of a violent past.  他脸上的决斗疤痕讲述了他暴力的过去。|近义词：combative, warring|反义词:peaceful, harmonious|adjective
