demicadence: /ˌdɛmɪˈkeɪdəns/
n.|半衰期; 半衰落|A half-life, a half-decline, particularly in reference to a decline in moral or cultural values, but not necessarily a full collapse.|The building showed signs of demicadence, with peeling paint and crumbling facades, yet it retained a certain faded grandeur.| 近义词: decline, decay, deterioration|反义词: growth, improvement, renaissance|词性: 名词

Etymology: "Demi-" (French, from Latin "dimidiō") meaning "half" + "-cadence" (from Latin "cadere" meaning "to fall").  Thus, literally "a half-fall".
