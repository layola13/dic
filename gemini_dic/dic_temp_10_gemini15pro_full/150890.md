paralepsis:/ˌpærəˈlɛpsɪs/ | n. |  佯谬, 佯述, 假省 | A rhetorical device in which one pretends to omit or pass over something, while actually emphasizing it.  It involves mentioning something by saying you won't mention it. |  "I won't even mention his questionable financial dealings, his shady connections, or his outright lies."  我不打算提他可疑的金融交易、他阴暗的关系，或者他彻头彻尾的谎言。| synonyms: preterition, apophasis | antonyms: explicit mention, direct address | noun
