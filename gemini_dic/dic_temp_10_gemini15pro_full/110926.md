liposome:/ˈlaɪpəˌsoʊm/ /ˈlɪpəˌsəʊm/| n. |脂质体|A minute spherical sac of phospholipid molecules enclosing a water droplet, especially as formed artificially to carry drugs or other substances into the tissues.|Liposomes are used to deliver drugs to cancer cells. 脂质体被用来向癌细胞输送药物。|Synonym: vesicle|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "non-encapsulated drug")|noun

liposomes:/ˈlaɪpəˌsoʊmz/ /ˈlɪpəˌsəʊmz/| n. |脂质体（复数）|Plural form of liposome.|Liposomes are being investigated for their potential in targeted drug delivery.  脂质体正在被研究用于靶向药物输送的潜力。|Synonym: vesicles|Antonym: (No direct antonym)|noun
