hegira:/hɪˈdʒɪərə/ /hɪˈdʒaɪrə/| n. |逃亡；迁徙；出走|An exodus or migration, especially Muhammad's flight from Mecca to Medina in AD 622. |例句：The family made their hegira to a new town to escape the war.  这家人为了躲避战争而迁徙到一个新的城镇。

例句：Her sudden departure felt like a hegira, leaving her friends wondering what had happened. 她的突然离开就像是一次逃亡，让她的朋友们想知道发生了什么。|近义词：flight, exodus, migration, escape, departure|反义词：arrival, return, homecoming|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hegira, from Arabic hijra "flight, emigration," verbal noun from hajara "he emigrated." Specifically, the Hegira refers to the migration of Muhammad and his followers from Mecca to Medina in 622 AD, marking the beginning of the Islamic calendar.
