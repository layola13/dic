papillomas:/ˌpæpɪˈləʊməz/ (US) /ˌpæpɪˈləʊməs/ (UK)| n. |乳头状瘤；乳突瘤|A small benign tumor projecting from the skin or a mucous membrane and typically attached by a stalk. Papillomas are caused by a virus and are contagious. They can occur in various parts of the body, including the skin, genitals, and respiratory tract.|

Example 1:  The doctor diagnosed the skin lesions as papillomas.

Example 2:  Multiple papillomas were found in the patient's throat.
