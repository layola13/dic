marbles:/ˈmɑːrblz/ (US) /ˈmɑːbəlz/ (UK)| n.|弹珠；理智；镇静；大理石制品|Small, hard balls of glass, clay, or other materials, used in children's games or as collectibles. Can also refer to sanity or composure, or objects made of marble.|例句：The children were playing marbles in the park. 孩子们在公园里玩弹珠。

例句：He lost his marbles when he heard the bad news. 他听到坏消息后失去了理智。|近义词：glass balls (for the toy), sanity, composure, wits|反义词：insanity, hysteria|词性：名词

marbles:/ˈmɑːrblz/ (US) /ˈmɑːbəlz/ (UK)| n.|理智，神智清醒|Mental stability; sanity.|例句：He's completely lost his marbles. 他完全疯了。

例句：I thought I was losing my marbles when I saw the ghost. 当我看到鬼魂时，我以为我疯了。|近义词：sanity, wits, reason, mind|反义词：insanity, madness, craziness|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *marbrele*, diminutive of *marbre* (marble), from Latin *marmor*.  The use of "marbles" to mean "sanity" is likely a metaphorical extension, suggesting that someone who has lost their marbles has lost something precious and essential like the smooth, perfect spheres of the game.
