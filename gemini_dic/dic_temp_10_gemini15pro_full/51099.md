desperately: /ˈdespərətli/ | adv. | 绝望地，拼命地 | In a desperate manner; with great urgency or despair.  Showing a willingness to take risks. |  He desperately needed money.  他急需用钱。 | desperately tried to reach the shore. 他拼命地游向岸边。 | 近义词: frantically, urgently, wildly | 反义词: calmly, patiently, hopelessly (Note: "hopelessly" can be an antonym in the sense of "without hope of success," but "desperately" can also mean "without hope," so context is important.) | 词性: 副词

desperate: /ˈdespərət/ | adj. | 绝望的，拼命的，极度渴望的 | Feeling or showing despair about something.  Reckless or dangerous because of despair, especially of losing something. | He was desperate for a job. 他非常渴望得到一份工作。 | The situation was desperate.  情况十分危急。| 近义词: dire, critical, urgent | 反义词: calm, hopeful, composed | 词性: 形容词


词源:  Late Middle English: from Old French desperat, from Latin desperatus ‘given up’, past participle of desperare (see despair).
