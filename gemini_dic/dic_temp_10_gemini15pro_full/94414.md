implicature: /ˈɪmplɪkətʃər/ | n. | 含意；言外之意；暗指 |  What is meant by a speaker's utterance that is not part of what is explicitly said. It relies on context, shared knowledge, and the cooperative principle of conversation. | A: "Are you going to John's party?" B: "I have to work." (The implicature is that B is not going to the party.) | synonyms: implication, suggestion, innuendo, connotation | antonyms: explicit meaning, direct statement | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "implicate" (to involve or imply), ultimately from Latin "implicare" (to enfold, entangle). The "-ature" suffix forms nouns from verbs, similar to "-ure" (as in "closure").
