arm-in-arm:/ˌɑːrm ɪn ˈɑːrm/ | adv. | 臂挽着臂，手挽着手 |  With arms linked together, typically as a sign of affection or solidarity. | The couple strolled along the beach, arm-in-arm. 这对夫妇手挽着手沿着海滩漫步。 | Synonyms: hand-in-hand, linked, together | Antonyms: apart, separated | adverb

arm-in-arm:/ˌɑːrm ɪn ˈɑːrm/ | adj. | 臂挽着臂的，手挽着手的 |  Describing people linked together by their arms. |  They walked arm-in-arm down the aisle. 他们手挽着手沿着过道走。 | Synonyms: hand-in-hand, linked, together | Antonyms: apart, separated | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrase "arm in arm," literally meaning with one's arm placed within another person's arm.  The construction reflects the physical act of linking arms.
