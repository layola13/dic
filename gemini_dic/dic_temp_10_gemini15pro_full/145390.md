overcasualness:/ˌoʊvərˈkæʒuəlnəs/| n.| 过分随意；过于随便|A state of being excessively casual in appearance, behavior, or attitude.|His overcasualness in the interview cost him the job.  他的面试过于随意，让他失去了这份工作。|近义词：informality, nonchalance, carelessness|反义词：formality, meticulousness, conscientiousness|词性: noun
Etymology-> From over- + casualness. "Over-" is an English prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much". "Casualness" is derived from the adjective "casual" which ultimately comes from the Medieval Latin word "casualis", meaning "happening by chance".
