eft:/ɛft/| n.|幼体, 稚虫|A young amphibian, especially a newt or salamander, in its aquatic, gill-breathing stage.|The eft swam in the shallow pond. 幼体在浅水池塘里游动。|近义词：larva, tadpole|反义词:adult|词性:名词

词源: From Middle English *evete, from Old English *efete, from Proto-Germanic *afōtiz.  Ultimately related to Old English ātor ("poison"), referring to the toxic secretions of some species.
