flounciest: /ˈflaʊnsiɪst/
adjective
最炫耀的，最招摇的
Superlative form of "flouncy":  most characterized by showy or exaggerated movements or displays, especially of clothing.
She wore the flounciest dress at the party, twirling and swirling to show it off. 她穿着晚会上最招摇的裙子，旋转飞舞着炫耀它。
The actress gave the flounciest performance, clearly playing to the audience.  这位女演员的表演最为炫耀，显然是在迎合观众。
近义词: showiest, flashiest, most flamboyant
反义词: plainest, simplest, most understated
词性: adjective

词源:
From flounce (v.) "to move abruptly or impatiently," from French *floncer "to plunge, dash" (compare Old French flancer "to strike, fling, launch").  The meaning "to go with exaggerated movements" is from 1794.  The noun meaning "a wide ornamental strip of material gathered and sewn to a garment" is from 1771.  "Flouncy" (adj.) meaning "ornamented with flounces" is from 1786, extended to "ostentatious" by 1851.
