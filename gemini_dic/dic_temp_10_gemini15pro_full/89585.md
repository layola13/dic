hoodcap: /ˈhʊdkæp/ | n. | 头巾，风帽，兜帽 | A hood-shaped cap that covers the head and neck, often attached to a coat or jacket.  |  She pulled her hoodcap over her ears to protect them from the cold wind. 她把兜帽拉过来盖住耳朵，以防寒风吹袭。 |  Example: The hiker wore a hoodcap to keep warm in the mountains.  这名徒步旅行者戴着兜帽在山上保暖。 | synonyms: hood, cowl, bonnet | antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, perhaps "brimmed hat" for contrast) | noun
