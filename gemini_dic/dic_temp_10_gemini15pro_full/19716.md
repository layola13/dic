biliousnesses:/ˈbɪliəsnəsəz/ | n. | 坏脾气，易怒，暴躁 |  The state of being bilious; nausea, vomiting, indigestion, and peevishness.  It often implies a bad temper or irritability thought to be caused by liver problems. | His constant biliousnesses made him difficult to work with. 他持续的暴躁脾气让他很难相处。| Synonyms: irritability, irascibility, peevishness, grumpiness, churlishness, cantankerousness, crossness | Antonyms: good humor, cheerfulness, amiability, affability, geniality | Noun

Etymology:
From bilious + -ness.  "Bilious" comes from Late Latin *biliosus, from Latin bilis "bile." The connection to bad temper stems from ancient humoral theory, which linked personality traits to bodily fluids, with bile thought to cause anger and irritability.
