cocooning:/kəˈkuːnɪŋ/
n.
茧居；居家自娱
The practice of spending leisure time at home, as opposed to going out.
Example: She enjoyed cocooning on weekends, reading books and watching movies. 她喜欢周末宅在家里，读书看电影。

Example:  With the rise of streaming services, cocooning has become increasingly popular. 随着流媒体服务的兴起，居家自娱变得越来越流行。

近义词: nesting, hibernating, staying in
反义词: socializing, going out, partying
词性: noun

Etymology: From "cocoon" (n.), referring to the protective casing of an insect pupa, extended metaphorically to describe a similar protective and comforting environment.  The term was popularized in the 1980s by Faith Popcorn, a trend forecaster.
