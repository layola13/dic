emaciate:/ɪˈmeɪʃiˌeɪt/ /ɪˈmeɪsiˌeɪt/ |v. |使消瘦；使衰弱|to make abnormally thin or weak, especially by starvation or disease. |The illness emaciated him to the point where he was just skin and bones.| 近义词: weaken, debilitate, enfeeble |反义词: strengthen, invigorate, nourish |词性:动词

emaciated:/ɪˈmeɪʃiˌeɪtɪd/ /ɪˈmeɪsiˌeɪtɪd/ |adj. |消瘦的；衰弱的|abnormally thin or weak, especially from illness or lack of food.|The refugees arrived at the camp looking emaciated and exhausted.|近义词: thin, gaunt, skeletal |反义词: plump, robust, healthy |词性:形容词

词源: From Latin *emaciatus*, past participle of emaciare (“to make lean, waste away”), from ex- (“out”) + macies (“leanness”).
