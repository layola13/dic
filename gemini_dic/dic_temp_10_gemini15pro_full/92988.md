idiom:/ˈɪdiəm/| n.|习语，成语|A group of words established by usage as having a meaning not deducible from those of the individual words (e.g., rain cats and dogs, see the light ).|例句： "Rain cats and dogs" is a common English idiom. “倾盆大雨”是一个常见的英语习语。|近义词：expression, phrase, saying|反义词:literalism|noun
