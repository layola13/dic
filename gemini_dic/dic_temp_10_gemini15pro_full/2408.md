adjective:/ˈædʒɪktɪv/| adj.|形容词|A word that describes a noun or pronoun. It tells you more about the characteristics or qualities of the noun or pronoun it modifies.|The red car is fast.  红色的车很快。
The tall building overlooks the city. 高大的建筑俯瞰着城市。|近义词：descriptor, modifier|反义词: N/A|词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: adiectif, from Old French adjectif, from Latin adiectivum (nomen), from adiectus ‘added’, past participle of adicere, from ad- ‘to’ + iacere ‘throw’.

USAGE->
Attributive position (before a noun):
The small dog barked loudly. 小狗大声地叫。
He bought a new car. 他买了一辆新车。


Predicative position (after a linking verb):
The cake is delicious. 蛋糕很美味。
She seems tired. 她看起来很累。


Comparative form (comparing two things):
This book is more interesting than that one. 这本书比那本书更有趣。
She is taller than her brother. 她比她哥哥高。


Superlative form (comparing three or more things):
This is the most beautiful painting I've ever seen. 这是我见过的最美丽的画。
He is the tallest boy in the class. 他是班里最高的男孩。


With intensifiers:
The food was very spicy. 食物很辣。
She is incredibly talented. 她才华横溢。
