legitimacy:/lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi/ | n. |合法性，正统性| The state of being legitimate; lawfulness. Conformity to the law or rules. | The legitimacy of the election was questioned.  选举的合法性受到了质疑。| legality, validity, lawfulness | illegality, illegitimacy | noun

legitimate: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət/ | adj. |合法的，正当的| Conforming to the law or to rules. Able to be defended with logic or justification; valid. |  His claim to the throne is legitimate. 他对王位的要求是合法的。 | legal, lawful, valid | illegal, illegitimate, unlawful | adjective

legitimate: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmeɪt/ | v. |使合法化，使正当化| Make (something) lawful or allowable. | The government has legitimized the use of force. 政府已经将使用武力合法化。| legalize, validate, authorize | outlaw, prohibit, ban | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French legitime, from Latin legitimus ‘lawful’, from lex, leg- ‘law’.
