long-windedness:/ˌlɔːŋˈwɪndɪdnəs/ | n. |冗长，啰嗦|The long-windedness of his speech bored the audience.  他的演讲冗长乏味，使观众感到厌烦。|English Explanation:  The quality of being tediously long in speaking or writing; verbosity.|例句：His long-windedness made it difficult to follow his point. 他的冗长使得很难理解他的观点。|近义词：verbosity, prolixity, wordiness|反义词：conciseness, brevity|词性: noun

long-winded:/ˌlɔːŋˈwɪndɪd/ | adj. |冗长的，啰嗦的|The professor's lectures were notoriously long-winded.  这位教授的讲座出了名的冗长。|English Explanation: (of speech or writing) continuing at length and in a tedious way.|例句：She told a long-winded story about her childhood. 她讲了一个关于她童年的冗长故事。|近义词：verbose, prolix, wordy|反义词：concise, brief|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From long + wind + -ed + -ness.  "Wind" here refers to breath, implying speaking for a long time, hence using a lot of breath.
