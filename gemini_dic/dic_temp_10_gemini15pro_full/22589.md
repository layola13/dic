bootlegs:/ˈbuːtˌleɡz/ /ˈbuːtˌlɛɡz/| n. |私酒；走私酒；非法制造或销售的商品；（盗版）唱片，录音，录像|Illegally made, copied, or sold goods, especially alcohol, recordings, or videos.|例句：He was arrested for selling bootlegs of the latest Hollywood movies. 他因出售最新好莱坞电影的盗版而被捕。

例句：During Prohibition, bootlegs were a common commodity. 在禁酒令期间，私酒是一种常见的商品。

|近义词：contraband, illicit, unauthorized, pirated, knock-off|反义词:legitimate, authorized, genuine|词性:名词

bootleg:/ˈbuːtˌleɡ/ /ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ/| v. |非法制造或销售（尤指酒）；走私|To make, sell, or transport (especially liquor) illegally.|例句：They were caught bootlegging cigarettes across the border. 他们因走私香烟过境而被捕。

例句：He made a fortune bootlegging whiskey during Prohibition. 他在禁酒令期间靠走私威士忌发了财。

|近义词：smuggle, traffic|反义词: |词性:动词

bootleg:/ˈbuːtˌleɡ/ /ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ/| adj. |非法制造的；走私的；盗版的|Made, sold, or transported illegally.|例句：The police raided the warehouse and confiscated a large quantity of bootleg alcohol. 警方突袭了仓库，没收了大量私酒。


例句：He was selling bootleg CDs at the flea market. 他在跳蚤市场卖盗版CD。
