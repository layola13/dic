farcical:/ˈfɑːrsɪkəl/ | adj. | 滑稽的，荒唐的，闹剧的 | Relating to or resembling a farce, especially because of absurd or ridiculous aspects. | The whole situation was so farcical it was hard to believe it was actually happening. 整个情况太荒谬了，很难相信它真的发生了。

The play was a farcical comedy with ridiculous costumes and slapstick humor.  这出戏是一部滑稽的喜剧，服装滑稽，充满闹剧式的幽默。 | 近义词: ludicrous, absurd, preposterous, ridiculous | 反义词: serious, realistic, sensible, reasonable | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *farcesque*, from Old French *farce* ("stuffing, forcemeat; mockery, derision, jest"), from *farcir* ("to stuff"), from Latin *farcire* ("to stuff").  The original sense referred to plays "stuffed" with jokes and gags.
