discriminative:/dɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv/| adj.| 区别的；歧视的；有识别力的；有鉴别力的| Making or showing a distinction; discerning; perceptive.  Able to recognize or understand the difference between things. Biased; prejudiced.|例句：She has a discriminative palate and can distinguish between subtle flavors. 她味觉敏锐，能分辨出细微的味道。

discriminative:/dɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv/| adj.| 区别的；歧视的；有识别力的；有鉴别力的| Making or showing a distinction; discerning; perceptive. Able to recognize or understand the difference between things. Biased; prejudiced. |例句：The company was accused of using discriminative hiring practices.  这家公司被指控在招聘过程中存在歧视。|近义词：distinctive, discerning, perceptive, selective, biased, prejudiced|反义词：indiscriminate, unbiased, unprejudiced, impartial|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin discriminativus, from discriminat-, past participle stem of discriminare "to divide, separate, distinguish," from Latin discrimen "interval, difference," from discernere "to separate, distinguish" (see discern).
