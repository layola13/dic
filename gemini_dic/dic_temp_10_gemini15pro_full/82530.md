guacho:/ˈɡaʊtʃoʊ/ (US), /ˈɡaʊtʃəʊ/ (UK)| n. adj.| 高乔人，南美牛仔；流浪汉；孤儿；勇敢的，独立的|A skilled horseman, cattle herder, and folk hero of the South American pampas, similar to the North American cowboy. Can also refer to a person, especially a child, who is alone, orphaned, or abandoned. As an adjective, it can describe someone as brave, independent, or skillful.|

Example 1: The gaucho skillfully lassoed the runaway calf.  高乔人熟练地用套索套住了逃跑的小牛。

Example 2:  He lived a solitary life, like a gaucho roaming the plains. 他过着孤独的生活，像一个在平原上游荡的高乔人。

Example 3: The little guacho, orphaned at a young age, learned to fend for himself.  这个小流浪儿，很小就成了孤儿，学会了自力更生。

Example 4: She admired his guacho spirit, his courage in the face of adversity.  她钦佩他勇敢的精神，他在逆境中的勇气。


近义词:cowboy, herdsman, horseman, orphan, vagabond, independent, brave|
反义词:cowardly, dependent|
词性:noun, adjective|


词源: From Spanish *gaucho*, possibly from Quechua *wakcha* meaning "orphan" or "poor person".|
