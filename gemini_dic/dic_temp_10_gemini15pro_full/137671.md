nonunitarian:/ˌnɒnjuːnɪˈteəriən/
adjective|非一神论的|Not believing in the doctrine of the Trinity, especially denying the divinity of Christ.| Many early Christians were considered nonunitarian because they did not accept the concept of the Trinity as it was later defined.|Synonyms: antitrinitarian, heterodox|Antonyms:  trinitarian, orthodox|adj.

nonunitarian:/ˌnɒnjuːnɪˈteəriən/
noun|非一神论者|A person who does not believe in the doctrine of the Trinity, especially one who denies the divinity of Christ.|Arius, a 4th-century priest, was a prominent nonunitarian who believed that Jesus was subordinate to God the Father.|Synonyms: antitrinitarian|Antonyms: trinitarian|n.
