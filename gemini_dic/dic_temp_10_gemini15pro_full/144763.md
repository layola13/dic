over-designing:/ˌoʊvər dɪˈzaɪnɪŋ/
|n.|过度设计|The act of designing something to be more complex or elaborate than necessary.  This often involves adding features or functionalities that are not essential or that complicate the design unnecessarily.|The team wasted weeks over-designing the software, adding features no one would use.|over-engineering, overcomplicating|under-designing, simplifying|noun


over-design:/ˌoʊvər dɪˈzaɪn/
|v.|过度设计|To design something to be more complex or elaborate than necessary.|They over-designed the bridge, making it far stronger than it needed to be.|over-engineer, overcomplicate|under-design, simplify|verb
