martello:/mɑːrˈtɛloʊ/ /mɑːˈtɛləʊ/| n.| 铁锤，榔头；（音乐）重音，强音|A type of defensive tower built in the Mediterranean region during the 16th century, designed to protect against coastal raids, particularly by Barbary pirates.  It typically features a circular or square base, thick walls, and a platform on top for artillery.  The name comes from the Italian word "martello" meaning hammer, possibly referring to the shape or the sound of the cannon fire. |The Martello towers were built to defend against naval attacks.  马尔泰洛塔是用来防御海军攻击的。

The pianist played the final chord with a martello. 钢琴家用重音弹奏了最后一个和弦。|近义词：hammer, mallet, fortress, tower|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "weakness" in the musical sense)|noun
