impressionism:/ɪmˈprɛʃənɪzəm/
n.|印象主义，印象派|A style of painting developed in France in the late 19th century, characterized by short brushstrokes and an emphasis on capturing the fleeting impression of a scene rather than precise detail. It also refers to a similar style in other arts, such as music and literature.|The painting is a classic example of Impressionism. 这幅画是印象主义的典型代表。|近义词:Post-Impressionism|反义词:Realism|词性:noun

Etymology: From French *impressionnisme*, from *impression* (“impression”) + *-isme* (“-ism”).  The term was initially used derisively by art critics.
