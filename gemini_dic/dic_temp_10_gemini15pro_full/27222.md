callused: /ˈkæləst/ (US) /ˈkæləsɪd/ (UK)| adj.| 长满老茧的；变得冷酷无情的 |  Having calluses; hardened and thickened.  Also, emotionally hardened or insensitive.|  His hands were callused from years of manual labor.  他的双手因多年的体力劳动而长满了老茧。|  She had become callused to the suffering of others. 她对别人的痛苦变得麻木不仁了。 | Synonyms: hardened, toughened, insensitive, unfeeling | Antonyms: soft, tender, sensitive, compassionate | adjective

callus: /ˈkæləs/ (US) /ˈkæləs/ (UK)| n.| 老茧；硬皮 | A thickened and hardened part of the skin or soft tissue, especially in an area that has been subjected to friction.| The hiker developed a callus on his heel.  这名徒步旅行者脚后跟上长出了老茧。 | Synonyms:  hardening, thickening | Antonyms: softness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
