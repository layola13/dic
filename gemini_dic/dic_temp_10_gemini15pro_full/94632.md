impress:/ɪmˈprɛs/ (US), /ɪmˈprɛs/ (UK)| v. n.|留下印象；使铭记；盖印；强征|To have a strong, positive effect on someone's feelings or thoughts.  To fix something firmly in someone's mind.|
His knowledge of history impressed me. 他的历史知识给我留下了深刻的印象。

The scene was deeply impressed on my memory.  那一幕深深地印在我的记忆里。

The government impressed young men into the army. 政府强征青年男子入伍。

The company impressed its logo on all its products. 公司在其所有产品上都印上了商标。
|近义词：affect, influence, impact, move, touch, imprint, stamp|反义词：disappoint, bore, repel|词性:动词,名词

impress:/ˈɪmprɛs/ (US), /ˈɪmprɛs/ (UK)| n.|印象；印记；特征|A mark or effect made on something by pressure. A strong effect produced on the intellect, feelings, conscience, etc.|
The tire left an impress in the mud. 轮胎在泥里留下了印记。

His speech made a strong impress on the audience. 他的演讲给听众留下了深刻的印象。
|近义词：mark, imprint, impression, effect, influence|反义词： N/A (No direct antonym for the noun form)|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *impressen*, from Old French *empreindre* and directly from Latin *imprimere, impressum*, meaning "to press into, stamp, imprint."  The word's roots are *in-* (in, into, on, upon, against) and *premere* (to press).
