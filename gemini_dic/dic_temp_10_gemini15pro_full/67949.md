facias:/ˈfeɪʃəz/| n. |面板，饰带|(plural of fascia) The flat surface or band, often of wood or stone, covering the ends of rafters or other structural parts of a building, especially the lower edge of a roof.  It can also refer to a board or other flat piece covering something.  Derived from Latin "fascia" meaning "band, bandage, strip."|The builder installed new PVC facias on the house.|Synonyms: cladding, covering, veneer, lining, facing|Antonyms: interior, inside|Noun
