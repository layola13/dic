decorticate: /diːˈkɔːrtɪkeɪt/ | v. | 去皮，剥皮; (比喻)除去…的精华;  (医学)去除…的皮质 | To remove the cortex, husk, or outer layer from something; (figuratively) to deprive something of its essential part; (medicine) to remove the cortex of an organ or structure. | The chef decorticated the shrimp before adding them to the paella.  厨师在把虾仁放入西班牙海鲜饭之前先去掉了虾壳。| synonyms: peel, husk, shell, pare, flay | antonyms: cover, coat, enclose | verb

decorticate: /diːˈkɔːrtɪkeɪt/ | adj. | 去皮的，剥皮的 | Having had the cortex, husk, or outer layer removed. | The decorticated logs were ready for processing. 去皮的原木已准备好进行加工。| synonyms: peeled, husked, shelled, pared | antonyms: covered, coated, enclosed | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin decorticatus, past participle of decorticare (“to peel, husk”), from de- (“off, down from”) + cortex (“bark, rind, shell, husk”) + -icare (frequentative suffix).
