overindividualizations:/ˌoʊvərˌɪndɪˌvɪdʒuəlɪˈzeɪʃənz/
|n.|过度个体化|The act of emphasizing individual characteristics or needs to an excessive degree, often at the expense of collective or social considerations.|The current educational system promotes overindividualizations, neglecting the importance of collaborative learning.|近义词:atomization, fragmentation, isolation|反义词:collectivism, integration, unification|词性:名词

Etymology:
"over-" (prefix): from Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond, excessively"
"individualize": from "individual" + "-ize" (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or cause to be")
"individual": from Late Latin "individualis," meaning "indivisible," from "in-" (not) + "dividuus" (divisible)
"-ization": suffix forming nouns from verbs ending in "-ize"
