birdlime: /ˈbɜːrdlaɪm/ | n. | 鸟胶，粘鸟胶 | A sticky substance smeared on twigs or branches to trap small birds. |  The poacher used birdlime to catch songbirds.  偷猎者用鸟胶捕捉鸣禽。 |Synonyms: glue, adhesive, paste, gum | Antonyms: solvent | noun


birdlime: /ˈbɜːrdlaɪm/ | v. | 涂鸟胶，用鸟胶粘 | To trap with birdlime. | They birdlimed the branches in hopes of catching some finches. 他们在树枝上涂了鸟胶，希望能捉到一些雀类。| Synonyms: ensnare, trap, snare | Antonyms: release, free | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bridlym*, from Old English *brīdlim*  ("birdlime"), from *brīd* ("bird") + *līm* ("lime," sticky substance).  The word "lime" in this context refers to a sticky substance, not the citrus fruit.
