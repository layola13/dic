hermeneutics:/ˌhɜːrməˈnjuːtɪks/| n. |释义学，解释学|The study of the interpretation of texts, especially religious texts, but also including legal and literary texts.  It involves understanding the meaning and significance of the text in its historical and cultural context, as well as the role of the interpreter in shaping that meaning.|Hermeneutics plays a crucial role in understanding ancient scriptures. 释义学在理解古代经文方面起着至关重要的作用。

The professor's lecture on hermeneutics was fascinating. 教授关于解释学的讲座引人入胜。|近义词：interpretation, exegesis|反义词:misinterpretation|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἑρμηνευτική (hermēneutikḗ, “art of interpretation”) from ἑρμηνεύειν (hermēneuein, “to interpret”) ultimately from Hermes, the messenger god in Greek mythology, who was considered the interpreter of the gods' will to humans.
