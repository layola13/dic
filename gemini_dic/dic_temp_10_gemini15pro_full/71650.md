flatter:/ˈflætər/ (US) /ˈflætə(r)/ (UK)| v. |奉承；使高兴；使显得更漂亮|To praise someone excessively or insincerely, often to gain favor. Also, to make someone look more attractive than they really are. |He flattered her with compliments about her appearance. 他用恭维她外貌的话奉承她。

The soft lighting flattered her complexion. 柔和的灯光使她的肤色显得更漂亮。|近义词：compliment, praise, butter up|反义词：criticize, disparage, insult|词性:动词

flatter:/ˈflætər/ (US) /ˈflætə(r)/ (UK)| adj. (less common) |(尤指比实际)显得更漂亮|Making something appear more attractive than it really is.|That dress is very flattering on you. 那条裙子你穿着真漂亮。|近义词: becoming, enhancing |反义词: unflattering |词性:形容词
