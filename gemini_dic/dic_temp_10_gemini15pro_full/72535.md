flustering:/ˈflʌstərɪŋ/| adj.| 令人慌乱的，使人不安的| Making someone feel agitated and confused; disconcerting. |The unexpected question was flustering.  这个突如其来的问题令人慌乱。|近义词：unnerving, disconcerting, upsetting, agitating|反义词：calming, soothing, reassuring|词性:adjective
fluster:/ˈflʌstər/| vt.  n.| 使慌乱，使不安；慌乱，不安| To make (someone) agitated and confused; To be agitated and confused.|  His constant interruptions flustered her. 他不断的打断让她感到慌乱。  She was in a fluster after dropping the cake.  把蛋糕掉在地上后，她感到很慌乱。|近义词：unnerve, disconcert, agitate, confuse|反义词：calm, soothe, reassure|词性: verb, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the late 18th century.  Possibly of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse *flysja "to hurry."
