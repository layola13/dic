dork:/dɔːrk/ | n. | 笨蛋，呆子，书呆子 | A person who is socially awkward or not fashionable. | He was such a dork in high school, always wearing mismatched clothes. 他高中时是个呆子，总是穿着不搭调的衣服。 |  例句：He's a real dork, but he's a nice guy. 他真是个呆子，但他是个好人。

例句：She called him a dork for wearing those glasses. 她因为他戴着那副眼镜而叫他呆子。


近义词：nerd, geek, goofball | 反义词：cool, popular | 词性：名词

dorky:/ˈdɔːrki/ | adj. | 笨拙的，呆呆的 | Like a dork; socially awkward or unfashionable. |  Her dorky glasses and braces made her a target for bullies. 她笨拙的眼镜和牙套使她成为欺凌的目标。| 例句：He had a dorky laugh that everyone teased him about. 他有一种呆呆的笑声，每个人都取笑他。


例句：She thought his oversized sweater looked dorky. 她觉得他的超大号毛衣看起来很呆。


近义词：nerdy, geeky, awkward | 反义词：cool, stylish, fashionable | 词性：形容词


词源:  The origin of "dork" is uncertain, but it is thought to have originated in the 1960s as college slang.  One theory suggests it is a euphemism for a vulgar term.  Another connects it to "dork," meaning penis, in the Shelta language used by Irish Travellers.
