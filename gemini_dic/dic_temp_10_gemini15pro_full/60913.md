elephantiac:/ˌelɪfənˈtaɪək/| adj.|象皮病的；巨大的，庞大的|Relating to or affected with elephantiasis, a condition characterized by the gross enlargement of a limb or body part.  Also used figuratively to describe something enormous or unwieldy.|The man's elephantiac leg made it difficult for him to walk.  这个男人的象皮病腿使他行走困难。|The government's response to the crisis was elephantiac and ineffective. 政府对这场危机的反应笨拙而无效。|近义词：massive, huge, enormous, gigantic, swollen|反义词：small, tiny, minuscule|词性:形容词

elephantiasis:/ˌelɪfənˈtaɪəsɪs/| n.|象皮病|A condition characterized by the gross enlargement of a limb or body part, typically due to a parasitic infection.|Elephantiasis is a debilitating disease that can severely impact a person's quality of life. 象皮病是一种使人衰弱的疾病，会严重影响患者的生活质量。|The mosquito-borne parasite can cause elephantiasis.  这种通过蚊子传播的寄生虫会导致象皮病。|近义词：lymphedema (more general term)|反义词：N/A|词性:名词
