harpies:/ˈhɑːrpiːz/ (US) /ˈhɑːpiːz/ (UK)| n.|鹰身女妖，泼妇，悍妇|In Greek mythology, harpies were monstrous birds with the heads of women, known for their foul temper and greed.  They would steal food and defile anything they touched.  Figuratively, a harpy is a shrewish, grasping woman.|The old woman was a real harpy, constantly nagging and complaining.|近义词：shrew, virago, nag, scold|反义词:angel, sweetheart|名词
