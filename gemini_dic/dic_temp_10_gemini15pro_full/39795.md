consortable:/kənˈsɔːrtəbl/ /kənˈsɔːrtəbəl/
adjective
可交往的，合得来的
Suitable for being together with; compatible.
例句：She found him consortable and they became friends. 她觉得他很好相处，于是他们成了朋友。

例句：Their personalities were consortable, leading to a harmonious relationship. 他们的性格很合得来，导致了一种和谐的关系。

近义词：compatible, agreeable, congenial, harmonious
反义词：incompatible, disagreeable, uncongenial, discordant
词性：adjective

consort:/ˈkɒnsɔːrt/ /ˈkɑːnsɔːrt/
noun
配偶（尤指皇室成员的）
A husband or wife, especially of a reigning monarch.
例句：The Queen Consort accompanied the King on his tour. 王后陪同国王进行了访问。

例句：Prince Philip was the consort of Queen Elizabeth II. 菲利普亲王是伊丽莎白二世的配偶。

近义词：spouse, partner, companion
反义词：N/A
词性：noun

consort:/kənˈsɔːrt/ /kənˈsɔːrt/
verb
(与…)交往，(与…)结伴
To habitually associate with (someone), typically with the disapproval of others.  To keep company; associate.
例句：He consorted with known criminals. 他与已知的罪犯来往。

例句：She consorts with artists and musicians. 她与艺术家和音乐家交往。

近义词：associate, fraternize, mix, mingle
反义词：avoid, shun, eschew
词性：verb
