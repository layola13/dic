graticule: /ˈɡrætɪkjuːl/
noun
网格，刻度线，标线
A network of fine lines, usually crossing at right angles, used on a map, chart, or aerial photograph to aid in the accurate location of points.
The map's graticule made it easy to pinpoint the coordinates.  地图上的网格使得精确定位坐标变得容易。
synonyms: grid, network, mesh, reticle
antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but consider "blank" or "featureless" in certain contexts)
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *craticula, diminutive of cratis "wickerwork," from PIE root *kert- "to weave, plait."
