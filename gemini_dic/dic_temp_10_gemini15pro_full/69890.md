fertilise: /ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz/ (British) /ˈfɝːtɪlaɪz/ (American) | vt. | 使肥沃；使多产；施肥；使受精 | To make something fertile or more fertile; to enrich or enhance something. |  The farmer fertilised the fields with manure.  农民用粪便给田地施肥。


Farmers fertilise their crops to increase yield. 农民给庄稼施肥以提高产量。


近义词: enrich, enhance, nourish, nurture, augment
反义词: deplete, impoverish, sterilize
词性: verb

fertilize: /ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz/ (British) /ˈfɝːtɪlaɪz/ (American) | vt. | 使受精 | To cause an egg or ovum to develop a new individual by uniting with a sperm. |  Flowers are fertilised by bees carrying pollen.  蜜蜂携带的花粉使花朵受精。


The egg is fertilised by the sperm. 卵子由精子受精。



近义词: inseminate, impregnate
反义词: sterilize
词性: verb

词源:  From Late Middle English *fertilisen, from Old French fertiliser, from Late Latin fertilisāre, from Latin fertilis (“fertile”).
