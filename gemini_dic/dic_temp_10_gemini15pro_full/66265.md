exclusive: /ɪkˈskluːsɪv/ | adj. n. | 独家的，排外的；独家新闻，专有物 |  Something exclusive is restricted to a particular person or group.  As a noun, it refers to a news story reported by only one source. | This nightclub is very exclusive, and it's hard to get in.  这家夜总会非常高级，很难进去。 |  The reporter got an exclusive on the mayor's resignation. 记者获得了市长辞职的独家新闻。 |  private, select, restricted | inclusive, general | adjective, noun

exclusive: /ɪkˈskluːsɪv/ | n. | 独家新闻，专有物 | An exclusive is a news story reported by only one source, or something only available to a limited group. |  She landed an exclusive with the celebrity. 她拿到了对这位名人的独家专访。 | The company has exclusives on several popular products.  这家公司拥有几种热门产品的独家经销权。 | scoop, special | general news, common item | noun
