breeds:/briːdz/
| n. v. |品种；种类；生育；繁殖|A breed is a particular type of animal or plant, especially one that has been carefully developed by people.  To breed means to keep animals or plants for the purpose of producing young animals or plants in a controlled way.|
|例句：This breed of dog is known for its intelligence.  这种狗以聪明著称。|
|例句：The farmer breeds prize-winning cattle.  这位农场主繁殖获奖的牛。|
|近义词：types, kinds, varieties, procreates, reproduces, generates|
|反义词：extinguishes, eradicates|
|词性：noun (plural), verb (third person singular present)|


breeds:/briːdz/
| n. |(人或事物的)类型，种类|A breed of something is a particular type of it.|
|例句：A new breed of entrepreneur is emerging. 一种新型的企业家正在兴起。|
|例句：The internet has created a new breed of criminal. 互联网催生了一种新型的罪犯。|
|近义词：types, kinds, varieties, sorts|
|反义词：N/A|
|词性：noun (plural)|


词源:
From Middle English *breden, *breeden, from 古英语 brēdan (“to breed, cherish, keep warm, produce, generate, originate, cause”), from Proto-Germanic *brōdjaną (“to warm, hatch, brood”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰrew- (“to boil, bubble, seethe, brew”). Cognate with West Frisian briede (“to breed”), Dutch broeden (“to brood, hatch”), German brüten (“to brood, hatch, incubate”). Related also to brew.
