hydatoid:/ˈhaɪdəˌtɔɪd/| adj. n.|水样的，水囊状的；水囊状物|Resembling a hydatid; cystic. A cyst or vesicle containing a clear watery fluid, especially one of those found in the liver, lungs, etc., of human beings and other mammals, due to the larval stage of the tapeworm Echinococcus. |The doctor discovered a hydatoid cyst in the patient's liver.|近义词：cystic, vesicular|反义词:solid|词性:adjective, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *hudatōeidēs* "like water," from *hudōr* "water" + *-oeidēs* "like."
