cyclopedic:/ˌsaɪkləˈpiːdɪk/ | adj. | 百科全书式的；知识渊博的 | encompassing a wide range of knowledge, like an encyclopedia |  He has a cyclopedic knowledge of history. 他有着百科全书式的历史知识。 | comprehensive, exhaustive, encyclopedic | limited, narrow, specialized | adjective

cyclopaedia:/ˌsaɪkləˈpiːdiə/ | n. | 百科全书 | a book or set of books giving information on many subjects or on many aspects of one subject and typically arranged alphabetically. |  He consulted the encyclopaedia for information on the French Revolution. 他查阅了百科全书，寻找有关法国大革命的信息。 | encyclopedia, lexicon |  | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cyclopédique*, from Ancient Greek *κύκλος* (kúklos, “circle, cycle”) + *παιδεία* (paideía, “education, rearing of a child”).  The sense is of a work containing the whole circle of knowledge.
