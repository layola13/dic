diorama:/ˌdaɪəˈrɑːmə/ /ˌdaɪəˈræmə/
noun
透景画，立体模型
A three-dimensional scene, often in miniature, usually enclosed in a glass showcase, and depicting a landscape, historical event, or the like.
The museum had a diorama of a prehistoric forest.  博物馆里有一个史前森林的透景画。
model, miniature, exhibit, scene, display
antonym:  (No direct antonym, but consider) reality, actuality
词性: 名词


diorama:/ˌdaɪəˈrɑːmə/ /ˌdaɪəˈræmə/
noun
西洋景
A life-size exhibit, often of a historical event, meant to be viewed through a small opening. (Less common usage)
The old-time amusement park had a diorama of the Battle of Gettysburg.  这个老式游乐园里有一个葛底斯堡战役的西洋景。
panorama, spectacle, pageant
antonym: (No direct antonym)
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *diorama*, from Greek *dia* ("through") + *orama* ("that which is seen, a view"), from *horan* ("to see").  Coined by Louis Daguerre and Charles Marie Bouton in 1822.
