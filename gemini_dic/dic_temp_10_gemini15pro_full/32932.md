chop-up:/ˈtʃɒp ʌp/ (British) /ˈtʃɑːp ʌp/ (American)| phrasal verb | 切碎；剁碎；分割；打乱 | To cut something into small pieces. Also, to disrupt or interrupt something. | The chef chopped up the vegetables for the stew.  厨师把蔬菜切碎炖汤。

The frequent commercials chopped up the flow of the movie.  频繁的广告打断了电影的流畅性。 | 近义词：dice, mince, cut up, fragment, divide | 反义词: combine, unite, join | 词性: 短语动词


chop-up:/ˈtʃɒp ʌp/ (British) /ˈtʃɑːp ʌp/ (American)| n. | 切碎的东西；混乱的状态 | (Less common usage) The state of being chopped up. | The chop-up of the land into small plots made farming difficult.  土地被分割成小块，使得耕作变得困难。| 近义词：mess, jumble, confusion | 反义词: order, organization | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "chop" (meaning to cut) and "up" (indicating completion or thoroughness).  The term likely originated from the action of chopping something into smaller pieces until it was completely "up" or finished.
