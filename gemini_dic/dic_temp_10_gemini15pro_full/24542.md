broadpiece:/ˈbrɔːdpiːs/| n.|大键琴;大羽管键琴|A large harpsichord, especially one with a wing-shaped case, popular in the 17th and 18th centuries.|The museum boasts a beautifully preserved broadpiece from the 18th century.  博物馆拥有一台保存完好的18世纪的大键琴。|Synonym: harpsichord, clavichord|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps electronic keyboard as a modern contrast)|Noun
Etymology: From broad + piece, referring to the large, wing-shaped case.  来自 broad（宽的）+ piece（块），指的是其宽大的、翼状的外壳。
