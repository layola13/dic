overcivil:/ˌoʊvərˈsɪvəl/ | adj. | 过分客气的，过分礼貌的 | Excessively polite or civil, often to the point of seeming insincere or affected. | He gave an overcivil bow, which made her suspicious. 他鞠了个过分客气的躬，让她起了疑心。 |  She found his overcivil manner rather irritating. 她觉得他过分客气的举止相当令人恼火。 | 近义词: excessively polite, overly courteous, unctuously polite | 反义词: rude, impolite, discourteous | 词性: adjective

词源: "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "civil" (from Latin "civilem," meaning "relating to citizens or the state," later developing the sense of "courteous").
