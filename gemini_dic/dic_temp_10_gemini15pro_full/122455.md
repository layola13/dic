mobilise:/ˈməʊbəlaɪz/ (UK) /ˈmoʊbəlaɪz/ (US)| v. |动员，调动|To organize and prepare something, such as a group of people, for a particular purpose.|The government mobilized the army for war. 政府动员军队准备战争。|synonyms:  assemble, rally, marshal, deploy|antonyms: demobilize, disband|verb

mobilize:/ˈməʊbəlaɪz/ (UK) /ˈmoʊbəlaɪz/ (US)| v. |动员，调动；(使)能活动|To make something movable or capable of movement.|Doctors are working to mobilize the patient's stiff joints. 医生们正在努力使病人的僵硬关节活动起来。|synonyms: activate, energize|antonyms: immobilize, restrict|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French mobiliser, from mobile (“movable”)  Ultimately from Latin mobilis.
