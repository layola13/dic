clingier: /ˈklɪŋ.i.ər/ (US), /ˈklɪŋ.ɡi.ər/ (UK) | adj. | 更粘人的，更依赖的 | More clingy; displaying a greater need for attention and reassurance, often to an excessive degree.  It's the comparative form of "clingy." |  She's become much clingier since they started dating.  她自从开始和他约会后变得更加粘人了。|  He's so clingier now that he's retired. 他退休后变得更加粘人了。| 近义词: needier, more dependent, more attached | 反义词: independent, detached, aloof | 词性: 形容词

clingy: /ˈklɪŋ.i/ (US), /ˈklɪŋ.ɡi/ (UK) | adj. | 粘人的，依赖的 | Having a strong emotional attachment or dependence on someone, often to an excessive degree. | The child was very clingy with his mother.  这个孩子非常粘他的妈妈。|  She finds his clinginess a bit suffocating. 她觉得他的粘人有点令人窒息。| 近义词: needy, dependent, attached | 反义词: independent, detached, aloof | 词性: 形容词

词源: From the verb "cling" + "-y" (suffix forming adjectives).  "Cling" comes from Old English "clingan," meaning "to adhere closely."
