palatalizing:/ˈpælətəˌlaɪzɪŋ/| v. adj.|腭化（的）|The process of making a sound more palatal, by raising the tongue towards the hard palate.  It can also be used as an adjective to describe a sound that has been palatalized.|Example: The pronunciation of "t" before "i" in some languages involves palatalizing the "t" sound.  一些语言中，“t”在“i”之前的发音涉及“t”音的腭化。|Example: The 'y' sound in 'yes' is a palatalizing influence on the preceding consonant.  “yes”中的“y”音会对前面的辅音产生腭化影响。 |近义词: softening, affricating|反义词: velarizing, gutturalizing|词性: Verb (present participle), Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "palatalize" + "-ing." "Palatalize" comes from "palatal" + "-ize." "Palatal" derives from Late Latin "palatalis," meaning "of the palate," from Latin "palatum," meaning "palate."  "-ize" is a suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become." "-ing" is a suffix used to form present participles and gerunds.
