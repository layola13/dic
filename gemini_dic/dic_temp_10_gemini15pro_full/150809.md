paraffine:/ˈpærəfɪn/ /ˈpærəˌfɪn/ | n. adj.|石蜡，链烷烃|A white, waxy substance used in candles, cosmetics, and sealing.  A type of hydrocarbon with only single bonds between carbon atoms.|Candles are made of paraffin. 蜡烛是用石蜡制成的。|Synonyms: wax, alkane|Antonyms: -(Not commonly used as an adjective, thus no direct antonym)|noun
paraffine:/ˈpærəfɪn/ /ˈpærəˌfɪn/ | n. adj.|石蜡的，链烷的|Made of or containing paraffin. Relating to or denoting alkanes.|Paraffin wax is commonly used in candles. 石蜡通常用于蜡烛中。|Synonyms: waxy, aliphatic|Antonyms: -(Not commonly used as an adjective, thus no direct antonym)|adjective
