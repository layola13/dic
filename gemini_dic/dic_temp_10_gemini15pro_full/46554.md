dallier:/ˈdæliər/| n.|闲荡者，拖延者|Someone who dallies; a lingerer, one who spends time idly.|He was a dallier, never taking anything seriously. 他是个闲荡者，从不认真对待任何事。|近义词：lingerer, loiterer, idler, procrastinator|反义词:hustler, go-getter|词性:noun
dallies:/ˈdæliz/| v. (third-person singular simple present dallies, present participle dallying, simple past and past participle dallied)|闲荡，玩弄，拖延|To waste time; to dawdle; to trifle with something; to flirt.|She dallied in the garden, admiring the flowers. 她在花园里闲逛，欣赏着花朵。|近义词：dawdle, linger, loiter, tarry, trifle|反义词:hasten, hurry, rush|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dalien*, from Old French *dalier* "to chat, converse, amuse oneself," of uncertain origin. Possibly from a Germanic source.
