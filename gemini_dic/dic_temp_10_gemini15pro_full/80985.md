gradation:/ɡræˈdeɪʃən/ /ɡrəˈdeɪʃn/ | n. |渐变，等级，层次，阶段|a process of change by degrees; a series of gradual changes|The painting captures the subtle gradations of light and shadow. 这幅画捕捉到了光影的微妙渐变。| synonyms: progression, sequence, scale, spectrum | antonyms: abruptness, suddenness | noun

gradation:/ɡræˈdeɪʃən/ /ɡrəˈdeɪʃn/ | n. |等级，级别|a step or degree in a scale or series; a rank or level|There's a clear gradation of authority within the organization. 该组织内部存在明显的等级制度。|synonyms: hierarchy, ranking, order | antonyms: equality, uniformity | noun

gradation:/ɡræˈdeɪʃən/ /ɡrəˈdeɪʃn/ | n. |(颜色、光线等的)渐变|the gradual blending of one shade of colour into another|The artist used subtle gradations of colour to create a sense of depth. 艺术家运用微妙的色彩渐变来营造一种深度感。|synonyms: shading, blending, transition | antonyms: contrast, discontinuity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin gradationem, accusative of gradatio (“a stepping, a going step by step”), from Latin gradus (“a step, pace”).
