abomination:/əˌbɑːməˈneɪʃn/| n.|憎恶，可憎的事物，令人厌恶的东西|Something that causes disgust or hatred; a vile, shameful, or detestable action, condition, habit, etc.|The cruel treatment of prisoners of war is an abomination.  虐待战俘是令人憎恶的行为。

The pollution of the river is an abomination. 这条河的污染令人憎恶。|近义词：atrocity, disgrace, evil, horror, outrage, shame, sin, vice|反义词：delight, joy, love, pleasure|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *abominacioun*, from Old French *abomination*, from Latin *abōminātiō*, from *abōminārī* (“to deprecate as ominous, abhor, detest”), from *ab-* (“away from”) + *ōmen* (“omen, portent”).

USAGE->
Example Sentences:

They viewed the practice as an abomination. = 他们认为这种做法是一种令人憎恶的行为。

His cruelty towards animals was an abomination. = 他对动物的残忍令人憎恶。


PHRASE->
abomination of desolation = 令人憎恶的荒凉
an abomination to = 对...来说是可憎之物
an abomination in the sight of = 在...看来是可憎的
commit abomination = 犯下令人憎恶的行为
full of abominations = 充满令人憎恶的事物
