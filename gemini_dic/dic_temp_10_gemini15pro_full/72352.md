Etymology: floweriness + -es (plural suffix).  Ultimately from "flour," from Old French "flor," meaning "flower," referring to the best part of something, like the finest flour.  Over time, the meaning shifted to also encompass ornate or embellished language.


Synonyms:  ornateness, embellishment, flamboyance, floridity, elaborateness

Antonyms:  simplicity, plainness, conciseness, austerity, directness
