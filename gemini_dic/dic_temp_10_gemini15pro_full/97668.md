inhibitory:/ɪnˈhɪbɪtəri/ /ɪnˈhɪbətɔːri/| adj.|抑制的，禁止的|Hindering or preventing an action, process, or function.|例句：The drug has an inhibitory effect on the central nervous system. 这种药物对中枢神经系统有抑制作用。|近义词：restrictive, repressive, suppressive|反义词：stimulatory, excitatory|词性:形容词
inhibition:/ˌɪnhɪˈbɪʃən/ /ˌɪnhɪˈbɪʃn/| n.|抑制，禁止|The act of inhibiting or the state of being inhibited.|例句：Social inhibitions can prevent people from speaking their minds.  社交禁忌会阻止人们说出自己的想法。|近义词：restraint, suppression|反义词：encouragement, stimulation|词性:名词
inhibit:/ɪnˈhɪbɪt/| vt.|抑制，禁止|To hinder, restrain, or prevent (an action, process, or function).|例句：Fear can inhibit personal growth. 恐惧会抑制个人成长。|近义词：restrain, suppress, hinder|反义词：encourage, stimulate, promote|词性:动词
