cradled:/ˈkreɪdld/| v. |轻轻抱着，安放|The mother cradled her newborn baby in her arms. 母亲轻轻地把新生儿抱在怀里。|例句：The valley is cradled by the surrounding mountains. 山谷被周围的群山环抱着。|近义词：held, embraced, supported, nestled|反义词:dropped, exposed|verb

cradled:/ˈkreɪdld/| adj. |被轻轻抱着的，被支撑的|The fragile vase was cradled in a bed of straw. 易碎的花瓶被安放在稻草窝里。|例句：The town is cradled between two rivers. 小镇坐落在两条河流之间。|近义词：held, supported, protected|反义词:exposed, unprotected|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cradlen, frequentative of craden (“to load, pack”), from Old English cradian (“to load, crowd together, stow”). More at crowd.
