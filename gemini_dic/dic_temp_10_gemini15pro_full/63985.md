epistemology: /ɪˌpɪstəˈmɒlədʒi/ (UK) /ɪˌpɪstəˈmɑːlədʒi/ (US)| n. | 认识论 | The study or a theory of the nature and grounds of knowledge especially with reference to its limits and validity. | Epistemology asks questions like "What is knowledge?" and "How do we know what we know?". | 近义词: theory of knowledge, gnoseology | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps ignorance or nescience could be considered contrasting concepts) | 词性: noun

epistemological: /ɪˌpɪstəməˈlɒdʒɪkəl/ (UK) /ɪˌpɪstəməˈlɑːdʒɪkəl/ (US)| adj. | 认识论的 | Relating to the theory of knowledge. | The book takes an epistemological approach to the problem of consciousness.  | 近义词: gnoseological | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | 词性: adjective

Etymology: From Ancient Greek ἐπιστήμη (epistḗmē, “knowledge”) + -λογία (-logía, “study of, discourse”).
