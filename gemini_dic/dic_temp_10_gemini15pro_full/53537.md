disavowals:/ˌdɪsəˈvaʊəlz/
|n.|否认，拒绝承认，放弃|
|The government issued disavowals of any involvement in the scandal. 政府发表声明，否认与这起丑闻有任何牵连。|
|His disavowals of responsibility were met with skepticism. 他否认承担责任的说法受到了质疑。|
|近义词:denials, repudiations, rejections|
|反义词:acknowledgements, admissions, confessions|
|词性:名词|

disavowal:/ˌdɪsəˈvaʊəl/
|n.|否认，拒绝承认，放弃|
|The suspect's disavowal of the crime was unconvincing. 嫌疑人否认犯罪的说法 unconvincing.|
|Her disavowal of her previous statements caused confusion. 她否认自己先前声明的做法引起了混乱。|
|近义词:denial, repudiation, rejection|
|反义词:acknowledgement, admission, confession|
|词性:名词|

词源：From dis- "expressing reversal" + avow "acknowledge, confess."  Ultimately from Latin *advocare "to call to witness."
