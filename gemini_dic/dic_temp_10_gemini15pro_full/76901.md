gannet:/ˈɡænɪt/| n.|塘鹅；鰹鳥|A large seabird with a mainly white plumage, a long pointed bill, and a long pointed tail. It dives steeply into the sea to catch fish. |例句：The gannet plummeted into the ocean, snatching a fish from the waves.  塘鹅猛地扎进海里，从波浪中叼起一条鱼。

例句：Gannets are known for their spectacular diving skills. 塘鹅以其壮观的潜水技巧而闻名。|近义词：sea bird, booby|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ganot, of Germanic origin; related to Old Norse gandr ‘magic wand’, also ‘aggressive woman’. The current sense probably arose from the bird’s habit of plunging head first into the sea.
