crankest:/ˈkræŋkɪst/ | adj. | 最暴躁的，最古怪的 |  Superlative form of cranky: most cranky.  It describes someone in an extremely irritable and bad-tempered mood. | He was at his crankest first thing in the morning. 他早上起来脾气最暴躁。| Synonyms: grumpiest, crabbiest, sourest, testiest | Antonyms: happiest, cheeriest, most pleasant | adjective

Etymology:
From crank (n.) "a fanciful notion, whim" (1830), perhaps from crank (n.) "a bend, twist" (1570s), which may be related to Old Norse kringr "circle, ring." The sense of "irritable person" arose from the notion of a twisted or distorted temper.
