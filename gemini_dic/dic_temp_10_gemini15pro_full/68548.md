fanfare:/ˈfænfeə(r)/| n.|号角齐鸣；夸耀；吹嘘；炫耀；|A short loud flourish of trumpets or other brass instruments, typically used to announce the arrival of someone important. A showy display or celebration, often designed to draw attention to something.|The president's arrival was heralded by a fanfare of trumpets. 总统的到来以号角齐鸣来宣告。|synonyms: flourish, blare, blast, trumpet call, flourish of trumpets, peal, chorus, fanfare of trumpets, tucket|antonyms: silence, hush, stillness|noun

fanfare:/ˈfænfeə(r)/| v.|大肆宣扬；|To announce or celebrate with or as if with a fanfare.|The company fanned its new product with a lavish advertising campaign.  该公司大肆宣传其新产品，开展了铺天盖地的广告活动。|synonyms: publicize, promote, broadcast, trumpet, herald, proclaim, announce|antonyms: conceal, hide, suppress|verb
