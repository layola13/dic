coalfield:/ˈkoʊlˌfiːld/ | n. |煤田|A region containing substantial deposits of coal. |The Ruhr coalfield in Germany is historically significant. 德国的鲁尔煤田具有重要的历史意义。| The Appalachian coalfield provided much of the fuel for early American industrialization. 阿巴拉契亚煤田为美国早期的工业化提供了大量的燃料。|Synonyms: coal basin, coal region|Antonyms: None|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From coal + field.  "Coal" comes from Old English "col," meaning "glowing ember,"  and ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*kula-," related to Latin "calere" (to be warm). "Field" comes from Old English "feld," meaning "open country," and ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*pelh₂-," meaning "flat, open."
