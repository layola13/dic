dissimulour:/dɪˈsɪmjʊleɪtər/| n.|伪君子，掩饰者|A person who conceals their true motives, feelings, or beliefs.|He was a dissimulour, pretending to be their friend while secretly plotting against them. 他是个伪君子，假装是他们的朋友，却暗中密谋反对他们。|近义词：hypocrite, pretender, deceiver|反义词:honest person, truthful person|词性:noun

dissimulate:/dɪˈsɪmjʊleɪt/| v.|掩饰，假装|To conceal or disguise one's true thoughts, feelings, or character.|She dissimulated her anger with a forced smile. 她强颜欢笑以掩饰她的愤怒。|近义词：pretend, feign, mask, conceal|反义词:reveal, expose, disclose|词性:verb

dissimulation:/ˌdɪsɪmjʊˈleɪʃn/| n.|掩饰，假装|The act of concealing or disguising one's true thoughts, feelings, or character.|Her dissimulation was so perfect that no one suspected her true motives. 她的掩饰如此完美，以至于没有人怀疑她的真正动机。|近义词：pretense, deception, feigning|反义词:honesty, openness, candor|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dissimulātor, from dissimulāre (“to disguise, conceal”), from dis- (“not”) + simulāre (“to resemble, feign”).
