cataphonic: /ˌkætəˈfɒnɪk/ | adj. |紧张性精神分裂症的，紧张症的| Relating to or characterized by catatonia, a state of immobility and stupor.| The patient exhibited cataphonic behavior, remaining motionless for hours.  病人表现出紧张症的行为，一动不动地保持几个小时。| 近义词: catatonic, akinetic, rigid | 反义词: mobile, active, responsive | 词性: adjective

Etymology: From Greek *katá* (down, against) + *phonḗ* (voice, sound).  Originally referred to disorders thought to be caused by problems with the vocal cords, later broadened to encompass the wider range of symptoms associated with catatonia.
