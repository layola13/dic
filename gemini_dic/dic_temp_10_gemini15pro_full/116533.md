mathematicians:/ˌmæθəməˈtɪʃənz/
|n.|数学家们|A person who studies or is an expert in mathematics.|Many famous mathematicians have contributed to the development of calculus. 许多著名的数学家为微积分的发展做出了贡献。|近义词: experts in mathematics, number crunchers|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps) mathematically illiterate|词性:Noun, plural form of mathematician


mathematician:/ˌmæθəməˈtɪʃən/
|n.|数学家|An expert in or student of mathematics.|Alan Turing was a brilliant mathematician. 艾伦·图灵是一位杰出的数学家。|近义词:expert in mathematics, number cruncher|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps) mathematically illiterate|词性:Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mathematicianus, from Latin mathematicus (“mathematical”) + -ianus (suffix forming personal nouns).  The Latin word is derived from Ancient Greek  μαθηματικός (mathēmatikós, “fond of learning”), from  μάθημα (máthēma, “science, knowledge, mathematical knowledge; lesson”).
