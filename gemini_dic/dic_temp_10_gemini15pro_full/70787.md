fingerboard:/ˈfɪŋɡərbɔːrd/| n. |指板|The part of a stringed instrument, such as a guitar, violin, or cello, against which the strings are pressed in order to produce different notes.  A thin, narrow board, typically made of ebony or rosewood, that is glued to the neck of the instrument.|He carefully polished the fingerboard of his violin.|Synonyms: fretboard (primarily for guitars and similar instruments)|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym)|noun
