immurations:/ˌɪmjʊˈreɪʃənz/
|n.|监禁，禁闭，筑墙围住|The act of immuring or the state of being immured; imprisonment; confinement, especially within walls.|The ancient practice of immuration was a gruesome form of punishment. 古代的活埋是一种可怕的惩罚形式。|
|近义词：confinement, imprisonment, incarceration, internment|
|反义词：liberation, release, freedom|
|词性：名词|


immurations:/ˌɪmjʊˈreɪʃənz/
|n.|筑墙围住|The act of enclosing within walls, as for defense or punishment.|The immurations surrounding the castle made it virtually impenetrable. 城堡周围的墙壁使其几乎坚不可摧。|
|近义词：enclosure, walling in|
|反义词：opening, demolition|
|词性：名词|

词源:
From Late Latin *immurationem, accusative singular of *immuratio, from Latin immuratus, past participle of immurare “to wall in,” from in- “in” + murus “wall.”
