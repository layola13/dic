diffluency:/dɪˈfluːənsi/ | n. |不流利，言语不畅|Lack of fluency in speech, often characterized by hesitations, repetitions, or other disruptions.  It can be caused by a variety of factors, including nervousness, language learning difficulties, or speech disorders.|Her diffluency made it difficult to understand her presentation.  她的言语不流畅使得她的陈述难以理解。|The speaker's diffluency was noticeable due to frequent pauses and repetitions. 演讲者的不流畅很明显，因为他频繁停顿和重复。|近义词：hesitancy, stammering, stuttering |反义词: fluency, eloquence |词性:名词

Etymology: From the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of") + "fluent" (meaning "flowing smoothly") + "-cy" (suffix forming nouns of quality or state).  最终来源于拉丁语 "fluere"，意思是 "to flow."
