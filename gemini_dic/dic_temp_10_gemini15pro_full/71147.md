fishwives:/ˈfɪʃˌwaɪvz/
n.
卖鱼妇；泼妇；粗野的女人
A woman who sells fish, especially one who is loud and quarrelsome.
The fishwives at the market were known for their sharp tongues and quick wit.  市场上的卖鱼妇以她们的伶牙俐齿和机智闻名。
The argument between the two neighbors escalated quickly, turning them into veritable fishwives. 这两个邻居间的争吵迅速升级，使她们变成了十足的泼妇。
近义词:shrews, viragoes, harpies
反义词:lady, gentlewoman
词性:名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *fisshwyf*, from Old English *fiscwīf* (“fishwife”), from *fisc* (“fish”) + *wīf* (“woman, wife”). Cognate with Dutch *viswijf*, German *Fischweib*.
