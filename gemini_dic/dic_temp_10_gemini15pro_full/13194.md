at loose ends:/æt luːs ɛndz/| adj.|无所事事，闲散无聊，无着落|Feeling restless and unsettled, typically because of having nothing to do.|例句：He's been at loose ends ever since he lost his job.  他失业后一直无所事事。|例句：She felt at loose ends during the summer holidays. 暑假期间她感到很无聊。|近义词：idle, aimless, unoccupied, restless, bored|反义词：busy, occupied, engaged|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
The phrase "at loose ends" originated in the 16th century, referring to ropes or threads that are untied or unfastened.  Over time, the meaning expanded metaphorically to describe a person's state of being unsettled or without direction.
