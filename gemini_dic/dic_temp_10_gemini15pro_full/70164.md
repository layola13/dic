fiber:/ˈfaɪbər/ (US) /ˈfaɪbə(r)/ (UK)| n.|纤维；光纤；食物纤维|A thread or filament from which a vegetable tissue, mineral substance, or textile is formed.  Dietary material containing substances such as cellulose, lignin, and pectin, which are resistant to the action of digestive enzymes.|例句：
The sweater is made of natural fiber. 这件毛衣是由天然纤维制成的。

The doctor recommended a diet high in fiber. 医生建议多吃富含纤维的食物。

|近义词：filament, thread, strand,  roughage (dietary fiber)|反义词:|n.

fiber optic:/ˌfaɪbər ˈɑːptɪk/ (US) /ˌfaɪbər ˈɒptɪk/ (UK)| adj. n.|光纤的；光纤|Relating to or using thin flexible fibers of glass or other transparent solids to transmit light signals, especially for telecommunications or for internal examination of the body.|例句：
Fiber optic cables transmit data at high speeds. 光纤电缆高速传输数据。

The doctor used a fiber optic camera to examine the patient's stomach. 医生使用光纤摄像头检查病人的胃。

|近义词：optical fiber|反义词:|adj. n.
