chilled:/tʃɪld/ | adj. | 冷却的，冰镇的；感到冷的；轻松的，冷静的 |  Cooled, especially by refrigeration; feeling cold; relaxed and calm. |  The chilled wine was perfect for the summer evening. 冰镇的葡萄酒非常适合夏天的夜晚。 |  cool, cold, relaxed, calm | warm, heated, anxious, nervous | adjective

chilled:/tʃɪld/ | v. |  (chill的过去式和过去分词) 冷却，冷藏；使…感到冷；使…感到恐惧 | Past tense and past participle of "chill"; to make something cold; to cause someone to feel cold; to frighten or horrify someone. |  The sudden drop in temperature chilled me to the bone. 温度的骤降让我感到彻骨的寒冷。 | cool, refrigerate, scare, frighten | heat, warm, comfort, reassure | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "chill," which comes from Old English "ciele" meaning "cold," ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*kaliz."
