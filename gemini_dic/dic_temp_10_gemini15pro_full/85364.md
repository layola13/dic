harrowingly:/ˈhærəʊɪŋli/| adv.|令人痛苦地，悲惨地|In a way that causes extreme distress; terrifyingly.|The film harrowingly depicts the horrors of war. 这部电影悲惨地描绘了战争的恐怖。|近义词：disturbingly, frighteningly, terribly, agonizingly|反义词：comfortingly, pleasantly, soothingly|词性:副词
Etymology: From harrow (v.) "to break up clods of earth with a harrow," from Old Norse *harwa "harrow," from Proto-Germanic *harwon. The figurative sense of "to torment, distress" arose from the image of the harrow's teeth tearing and breaking. The adverb "harrowingly" is formed by adding the suffix "-ly" to the adjective "harrowing."
