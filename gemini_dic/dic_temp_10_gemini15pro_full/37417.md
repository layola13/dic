commercializing:/kəˈmɜːʃ(ə)laɪzɪŋ/
commercialize: /kəˈmɜːʃ(ə)laɪz/ | v. | 商业化；商品化 |  To develop (something, such as an invention, product, or idea) for commercial or profitable use.  | The company is commercializing a new drug.  这家公司正在将一种新药商业化。 |  market, merchandise, monetize | decommercialize | verb

commercialized: /kəˈmɜːʃ(ə)laɪzd/ | adj. | 商业化的；商品化的 |  Developed for commercial or profitable use.  | Christmas has become a highly commercialized holiday. 圣诞节已经变成了一个高度商业化的节日。 |  marketable, merchandised | noncommercial | adjective

commercialization: /kəˌmɜːʃ(ə)laɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n/ | n. | 商业化；商品化 | The process of developing something for commercial or profitable use. | The commercialization of space travel is becoming a reality.  太空旅行的商业化正在成为现实。 | marketing, merchandising, monetization | decommercialization | noun
