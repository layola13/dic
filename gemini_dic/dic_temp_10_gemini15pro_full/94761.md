improvisers:/ˈɪmprəvaɪzərz/ (US), /ˈɪmprəvaɪzəz/ (UK)| n. |即兴表演者，即兴创作人|People who create and perform something spontaneously, without preparation.|The jazz improvisers were incredibly talented, creating beautiful melodies on the spot.  爵士乐即兴演奏者才华横溢，现场创作出优美的旋律。|Synonyms: ad-libbers, extemporizers, improvisators|Antonyms: planners, preparers|Noun

improvise:/ˈɪmprəvaɪz/ (US), /ˈɪmprəvaɪz/ (UK)| v. |即兴创作，临时凑合|To create and perform something spontaneously, without preparation; to make something from whatever is available.|He had to improvise a speech when the scheduled speaker failed to show up.  当预定的演讲者没有出现时，他不得不即兴发表演讲。|Synonyms: ad-lib, extemporize, wing it|Antonyms: plan, prepare, rehearse|Verb
