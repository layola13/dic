hackles:/ˈhækəlz/| n. |颈毛；怒气；敌意|The hairs on the back of an animal's neck, especially of a dog or wolf, that rise when it is angry or frightened. Figuratively, it refers to feelings of anger or resentment.|The dog's hackles rose when the stranger approached. 那条狗在陌生人靠近时竖起了颈毛。|
|The politician's hackles rose at the journalist's pointed question.  这位政治家对记者尖锐的提问感到愤怒。|
|Synonyms: fur, bristles (literal); ire, indignation, annoyance (figurative)|Antonyms: calmness, composure, equanimity|noun

词源：From Middle English *hakeles, from Old English *hace “hook, barb”, related to Old High German hako “hook”.  Originally referring to the hooked barbs of a feather.  The figurative sense of "rising hackles" developed from observing the behavior of animals.
