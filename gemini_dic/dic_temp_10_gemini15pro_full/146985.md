overjudiciousnesses:/ˌoʊvərˌdʒuːˈdɪʃəsnəsɪz/
|n.| 过分审慎, 过于明智 |The state of being overjudicious; excessive cautiousness and prudence in judgment.|His overjudiciousnesses prevented him from taking the necessary risks to succeed. 他过分的谨慎阻碍了他去承担获得成功所需的风险。|近义词: overcautiousness, overprudence|反义词: recklessness, imprudence|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From over- + judicious + -ness.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much." "Judicious" comes from the Latin "iudicium" meaning "judgment." "-ness" is a suffix that forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
