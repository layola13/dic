abdicates:/ˈæbdɪkeɪts/| v.|退位；放弃；逊位|to give up a position, right, or power, especially one of high rank or responsibility|The king abdicated the throne. 国王退位了。

The CEO abdicated his responsibilities and left the company in chaos. 首席执行官放弃了他的责任，使公司陷入混乱。|近义词：resign, renounce, relinquish, quit|反义词：assume, usurp, seize, retain|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *abdicatus*, past participle of *abdicare* "to disown, reject, renounce," from *ab* "away, from" + *dicare* "to proclaim, dedicate, devote."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He abdicates his responsibility as a father. = 他放弃了作为父亲的责任。
She abdicates her right to vote in this election. = 她放弃了在这次选举中投票的权利。

一般过去时:
The king abdicated his throne last year. = 国王去年退位了。
He abdicated his position as CEO due to the scandal. = 由于丑闻，他辞去了首席执行官的职位。

现在完成时:
The queen has abdicated in favor of her son. = 女王已经退位，让位给她儿子。
He has abdicated all responsibility for the project. = 他已经放弃了对这个项目的所有责任。

过去完成时:
The emperor had abdicated before the revolution began. = 皇帝在革命开始之前就退位了。
She had abdicated her role as team leader before the project was completed. = 在项目完成之前，她已经放弃了团队领导者的角色。

将来时:
The king will abdicate next month. = 国王下个月将退位。
If the pressure continues, he will abdicate his position. = 如果压力持续下去，他将放弃他的职位。

虚拟语气:
If he were to abdicate, his son would become king. = 如果他退位，他的儿子将成为国王。
I wish he would abdicate his responsibility. = 我希望他放弃他的责任。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
