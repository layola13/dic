established: /ɪˈstæblɪʃt/ | adj. | 已建立的，已确立的；被认可的 |  Having been in existence for a long time and therefore recognized and generally accepted. | The company is an established leader in the industry. 这家公司是业内公认的领导者。|  例句：The restaurant is an established favorite among locals.  这家餐厅是当地人最喜欢的餐厅。| 近义词：recognized, accepted, proven, founded, instituted | 反义词：new, nascent, unproven, struggling | 词性: 形容词

established: /ɪˈstæblɪʃt/ | v. | 建立，确立；使被接受 | To set up or create (something) on a firm or permanent basis.  | The company was established in 1922. 这家公司成立于1922年。| 例句：They established a new branch in Beijing. 他们在北京设立了新的分公司。| 近义词：found, create, set up, institute, organize | 反义词：abolish, destroy, dismantle, end | 词性: 动词


词源: From Middle English *establissen, from Anglo-Norman establir, from Old French establir, from Latin stabiliō (“make stable, establish”), from stabilis (“stable, steadfast”), from stāre (“to stand”).
