Plural form of "Douglas," referring to either the Douglas fir tree or people with the surname Douglas.

Etymology:
From the Scottish Gaelic surname *Dubhghlas*, meaning "black stream".  This ultimately derives from *dubh* "black, dark" and *glais* "stream, water". The tree was named after David Douglas, a Scottish botanist who introduced it to Britain.
