externalising:/ɪkˈstɜːnəlaɪzɪŋ/
|v.|外化，使外在化|The process of making internal thoughts, feelings, or problems external or observable.|He externalized his anger by yelling at his brother. 他通过对弟弟大喊大叫来发泄他的愤怒。|近义词：expressing, projecting, venting|反义词：internalizing, suppressing|verb (present participle)

externalise:/ɪkˈstɜːnəlaɪz/
|v.|外化，使外在化|To express or make external (thoughts, feelings, etc.).|She externalized her anxieties through painting. 她通过绘画来表达她的焦虑。|近义词：express, project, vent|反义词：internalize, suppress|verb

externalization:/ɪkˌstɜːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
|n.|外化，外在化|The act or process of externalizing.|The externalization of costs onto society is a major concern. 将成本外部化到社会是一个主要问题。|近义词：expression, projection, venting|反义词：internalization, suppression|noun
