biodegrades:/ˌbaɪoʊdɪˈɡreɪdz/| v. |生物降解| (Of a substance) decompose by the action of bacteria, fungi, or other living organisms.| Example 1: The packaging biodegrades within six months.  包装在六个月内生物降解。 Example 2:  Many plastics do not biodegrade easily. 许多塑料不易生物降解。|近义词：decompose, decay, rot|反义词:persist, remain|词性:动词

biodegradable:/ˌbaɪoʊdɪˈɡreɪdəbl/| adj. |可生物降解的|Capable of being decomposed by bacteria or other living organisms.| Example 1: Biodegradable plastics are becoming increasingly popular. 可生物降解的塑料正变得越来越流行。 Example 2: The company uses biodegradable packaging for its products. 这家公司使用可生物降解的包装来包装其产品。|近义词：compostable, decomposable|反义词:non-biodegradable|词性:形容词

biodegradation:/ˌbaɪoʊˌdɛɡrəˈdeɪʃən/| n. |生物降解|The process by which organic substances are broken down by living organisms, such as bacteria, fungi, or other biological entities.| Example 1: Biodegradation is an important process for waste management. 生物降解是废物管理的一个重要过程。 Example 2: The rate of biodegradation depends on several factors, including temperature and moisture. 生物降解的速度取决于几个因素，包括温度和湿度。|近义词：decomposition, decay|反义词:preservation|词性:名词
