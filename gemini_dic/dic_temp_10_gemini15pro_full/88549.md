hitch:/hɪtʃ/| n. v.|障碍，故障；钩住，拴住|A temporary problem or difficulty that causes a short delay. To fasten or attach something with a knot, hook, etc.|The project hit a hitch when funding was withdrawn.  项目在资金撤回时遇到了障碍。  He hitched the horse to the wagon. 他把马套到马车上。|近义词：problem, snag, difficulty, fasten, attach|反义词：solution, ease, detach|词性:名词，动词
hitch:/hɪtʃ/| n.|急促向上拉；免费搭车|A quick pull upward; a free ride in someone's vehicle.|She gave her skirt a hitch. 她把裙子向上拉了一下。 He got a hitch to the nearest town. 他搭便车到了最近的城镇。|近义词：tug, jerk, lift, ride|反义词：drop, lower|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hicchen*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old English *hiccetan* "to hiccup," suggesting a sudden movement.
