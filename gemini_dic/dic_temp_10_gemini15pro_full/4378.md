aladdin:/əˈlædɪn/| n. |阿拉丁|A character from a Middle Eastern folk tale, typically portrayed as a young man who finds a magic lamp containing a genie that grants him wishes.  Derived from Arabic "al-Dīn," meaning "the faith" or "the religion."|Example: Aladdin rubbed the lamp, and a genie appeared. 阿拉丁擦了神灯，一个精灵出现了。|Example:  The story of Aladdin is a classic tale of rags to riches. 阿拉丁的故事是一个经典的从贫穷到富有的故事。|Synonym:  None (as it's a proper noun)|Antonym:  None (as it's a proper noun)|Noun
