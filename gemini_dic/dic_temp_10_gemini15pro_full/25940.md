burnt-out:/ˌbɜːrnt ˈaʊt/ | adj. | 精疲力尽的，耗尽的 |  (of a person)  exhausted from overwork or stress; (of a building or vehicle) gutted by fire. |  The teacher was feeling burnt-out after a long school year.  这位老师在漫长的学年后感到精疲力尽。| Synonyms: exhausted, drained, spent, worn out | Antonyms: energetic, refreshed, invigorated | adjective

burnt-out:/ˌbɜːrnt ˈaʊt/ | n. | 烧毁的状态 | The state of being burnt out, especially from overwork or stress. | The burnt-out of the building was a stark reminder of the fire's intensity.  这栋建筑的烧毁状态清楚地提醒着人们这场大火的猛烈程度。| Synonyms: exhaustion, fatigue | Antonyms: energy, vitality | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb phrase "burn out," combining "burn" (Old English *bærnan, from Proto-Germanic *brannjanan) and "out" (Old English ūt, meaning "outward, outside").
