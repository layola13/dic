housecarl:/ˈhaʊskɑːrl/| n.|家臣，扈从，卫士|A retainer or soldier in the household of a Scandinavian nobleman, especially in pre-Norman England.|King Harold had many loyal housecarls who fought bravely at the Battle of Hastings. 哈罗德国王有许多忠诚的家臣，他们在黑斯廷斯战役中英勇作战。|Example: The housecarl swore an oath of fealty to his lord.  这位家臣向他的领主宣誓效忠。|Synonyms: retainer, guard, soldier, warrior|Antonyms:  enemy, foe, traitor|noun
