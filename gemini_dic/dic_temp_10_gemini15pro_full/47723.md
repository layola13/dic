decadent:/ˈdekədənt/ /dɪˈkeɪdənt/| adj. n.|颓废的，衰落的；颓废者|Characterized by or reflecting a state of moral or cultural decline; a person who is luxuriously self-indulgent.|

例句：
The decadent Roman Empire eventually crumbled.  腐朽的罗马帝国最终崩溃了。

The decadent lifestyle of the wealthy elite was criticized. 富裕精英的颓废生活方式受到了批评。


近义词：dissolute, degenerate, corrupt, depraved, profligate|
反义词：moral, virtuous, upright, austere|
词性: adjective, noun|


decadents:/ˈdekədənts/ /dɪˈkeɪdənts/| n.|颓废者们|Plural form of decadent, referring to a group of people who are luxuriously self-indulgent and characterized by a state of moral or cultural decline.|

例句：
The decadents of the Jazz Age were known for their lavish parties.  爵士时代颓废的人以奢华派对闻名。

Critics labeled the artists as decadents, rejecting their unconventional styles.  批评家将这些艺术家贴上颓废者的标签，拒绝接受他们非传统的风格。

近义词：debauchees, libertines|
反义词：moralists, ascetics|
词性: noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *décadent*, present participle of *décadrer* "to decay," from Latin *cadere* "to fall."
