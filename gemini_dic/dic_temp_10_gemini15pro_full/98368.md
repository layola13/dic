insition: /ɪnˈsɪʒən/ | n. | 切口；开刀；开始；着手 | An act of cutting or making an incision, especially during surgery.  The beginning of something. | The surgeon made a small incision above the patient's knee. 外科医生在病人膝盖上方开了一个小口。 The novel's opening scene is a powerful insition into the protagonist's troubled mind. 小说的开场是对于主角混乱精神状态强有力的切入。 | 近义词: cut, gash, slit, start, beginning | 反义词: closure, suture, end | 词性: 名词

词源: From Latin *incīsiōn- (stem of incīsiō ), from incīsus, past participle of incīdere  "to cut into," from in- "into, in, on, upon" + caedere "to cut."
