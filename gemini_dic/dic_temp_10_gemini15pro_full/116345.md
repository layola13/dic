masterstroke:/ˈmæstərstroʊk/ /ˈmɑːstərstroʊk/| n. |大师之作；妙笔；神来之笔|A masterstroke is a brilliant action or plan that brings about a desired result, often unexpectedly or cleverly. |例句：His resignation was a political masterstroke. 他的辞职是一项政治上的妙举。

例句：The director's use of light and shadow was a masterstroke. 导演对光影的运用堪称神来之笔。|近义词：coup, triumph, tour de force, stroke of genius|反义词:blunder, mistake, failure|词性:noun

masterstrokes:/ˈmæstərstroʊks/ /ˈmɑːstərstroʊks/| n. |大师之作；妙笔；神来之笔（复数）|Plural form of masterstroke.  Refers to multiple brilliant actions or plans.|例句：The campaign was marked by a series of political masterstrokes. 这场运动的特点是一系列政治上的妙举。

例句：Her novel is full of stylistic masterstrokes. 她的小说充满了文笔上的精妙之处。|近义词：coups, triumphs, tours de force, strokes of genius|反义词:blunders, mistakes, failures|词性:noun
