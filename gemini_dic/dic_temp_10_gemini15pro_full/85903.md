headhunting:/ˈhedˌhʌntɪŋ/| n.|猎头; 物色人才|The practice of approaching employed professionals to persuade them to take another job with a different company, usually for a higher salary.|Many companies use headhunting firms to find qualified candidates for executive positions. 许多公司利用猎头公司寻找合格的管理人员候选人。|近义词：recruitment, executive search|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

Etymology:  The term "headhunting" originally referred to the practice of some tribal cultures of literally taking and preserving the heads of enemies.  This evolved metaphorically to refer to the aggressive pursuit of talented individuals for employment, as if "capturing" a valuable asset.
