internationalised:/ˌɪntənæʃənəlaɪzd/ | /ˌɪntərˈnæʃənəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 国际化的 | Adapted or altered to suit the needs or tastes of different countries or cultures. |  The company has internationalised its operations, with offices in over 20 countries.  这家公司已经将其业务国际化，在20多个国家设有办事处。

| Example: The university has internationalised its curriculum to attract students from all over the world. 这所大学已经将其课程国际化，以吸引来自世界各地的学生。 | Synonyms: globalized, worldwide, cosmopolitan | Antonyms: localized, domestic, national | Word type: Adjective


internationalise:/ˌɪntənæʃənəlaɪz/ | /ˌɪntərˈnæʃənəlaɪz/ | v. | 使国际化 | To make something suitable for use in different countries or cultures. | The company plans to internationalise its products by offering them in multiple languages.  该公司计划通过提供多种语言版本的产品来使其国际化。

| Example: They are working to internationalise the brand. 他们正在努力使品牌国际化。 | Synonyms: globalize, expand globally | Antonyms: localize, restrict locally | Word type: Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From international + -ise/-ize.  "International" ultimately derives from Latin "inter" (between) and "natio" (nation).
