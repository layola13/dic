ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dyppan, from Proto-Germanic *duppjaną, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰubʰ- (“deep”). Cognate with Dutch doppen (“to dip, to tilt”), German tüpfen (“to dab”), Low German döppen (“to dip”).
