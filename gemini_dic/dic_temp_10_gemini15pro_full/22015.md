boin:/ˈbɔɪn/| n.|浮标; 浮筒; (系住船只的)系泊浮筒|A float, typically a large one, moored in water to mark a channel, navigational hazard, or position of a mooring or an anchor.|The boat was tied to a boin in the harbor. 船被拴在港口的浮标上。|近义词：buoy, float, marker|反义词:anchor|词性:名词

词源:  Likely from Dutch *boei* meaning "buoy".  While the spelling "boin" is less common than "buoy", it reflects a historical variant and is still occasionally encountered, particularly in nautical contexts.
