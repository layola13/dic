hypersuspiciousnesses:/ˌhaɪpər səˈspɪʃəsnəsɪz/ | n. |过度怀疑，极度猜疑|  Excessive suspiciousness, a heightened state of distrust and suspicion. |  His hypersuspiciousnesses made it difficult to have a normal conversation. 他的过度怀疑使得正常的谈话变得困难。|  The detective's hypersuspiciousnesses led him to uncover the truth.  侦探的极度猜疑使他发现了真相。| synonyms: paranoia, distrust, mistrust | antonyms: trust, faith, confidence | noun

Etymology:
From "hyper-" (Greek, meaning "over, excessive") + "suspiciousness" (from "suspicious" + "-ness").  The root of "suspicious" is the Latin "suspicere," meaning "to look up to," later evolving to mean "to mistrust."
