leapfroggers:/ˈliːpˌfrɒɡəz/ (US) /ˈliːpˌfrɒɡəz/ (UK)| n.|跳蛙游戏者，超越者|People who play leapfrog or metaphorically overtake others by large increments.  Those who advance rapidly and surpass others.|The smaller companies are leapfroggers, adopting new technology faster than their larger, more established competitors. 这些小公司是超越者，采用新技术的比那些更大、更成熟的竞争对手更快。|近义词：overtakers, outstrippers, surpassers|反义词: laggards, stragglers|词性:noun

leapfrog:/ˈliːpfrɒɡ/ (US) /ˈliːpfrɒɡ/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.|跳蛙，超越，跨越式发展|A game in which one player vaults over another who is bending over; or to surpass or overtake someone or something, often by a significant margin.  Derived from the literal game, implying a large jump forward.|He leapfrogged over his friend in the game. 他在游戏中跳过了他的朋友。
The company leapfrogged its competitors by investing in new technology.  这家公司通过投资新技术超越了竞争对手。|近义词：bypass, jump, vault, overtake, outstrip|反义词: lag, trail|词性:noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "leap" (Old English *hlēapan, to jump) + "frog" (Old English frogga). The image is of jumping over someone bent over like a frog.
