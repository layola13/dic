mesel: /ˈmeɪzəl/ (may-zel) | n. | 寓言，比喻，箴言 | A traditional story, especially one with a moral message.  A proverb or saying. |  He told a mesel about the importance of hard work. 他讲了一个关于勤奋重要性的寓言。 | synonym: parable, fable, allegory, proverb, maxim, aphorism | antonym:  None (Antonyms are not readily applicable to this word.) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Yiddish מעשׂה‎ (mayse, “story, tale, anecdote”), from Hebrew מַעֲשֶׂה‎ (maʿăśe, “deed, act, story, tale”). Ultimately from the Semitic root *ʿśy (“to do, make”).
