marsters:/ˈmɑːstərz/ | n. | 主人们；师傅们 | A nonstandard, dialectal, or archaic plural form of "master." It represents the possessive form, meaning "belonging to master" or more commonly, the plural form, meaning "more than one master." |  例句：The marsters were unkind to their slaves.  主人们对他们的奴隶很刻薄。| 近义词：masters, lords, bosses, owners | 反义词：slaves, servants | 词性: 名词

词源: From Middle English *maistres, *maystres, from Old French maistres, plural of maistre (“master”), from Latin magister (“master, chief, director, teacher, superintendent”). Cognate with Italian maestro, Spanish maestro, Portuguese mestre. Double pluralisation in -s arose by analogy with words like *masters, which was reanalysed as the singular.
