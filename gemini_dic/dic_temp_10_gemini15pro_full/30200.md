celibate: /ˈsɛləbət/ (US), /ˈsɛlɪbət/ (UK)| adj. n.| 独身的，禁欲的；独身者，禁欲者 | abstaining from marriage and sexual relations, typically for religious reasons.  A person who abstains from marriage and sexual relations. |  例句：He took a vow of celibacy. 他发誓禁欲。|近义词：abstinent, chaste|反义词：married, promiscuous|词性: adjective, noun
Etymology: From Late Latin *caelebs, caelibis "unmarried, single," from Latin caelebs "unmarried, single, widowed," of uncertain origin.
