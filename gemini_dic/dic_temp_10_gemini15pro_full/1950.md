actionably:/ˈækʃənəbli/| adv.|可操作地，可执行地|In a way that can be acted on; in a way that can be put into practice.  It describes something that can be acted upon, typically based on available information or data.|The report provided actionably insights into consumer behavior. 这份报告提供了对消费者行为可行的见解。

The data was presented actionably, allowing the team to make immediate changes.  数据以可操作的方式呈现，使团队能够立即做出改变。|近义词：practically, feasibly, viably|反义词: impractically,  unfeasibly|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From action + -able + -ly.  "Action" comes from the Latin "actio," meaning "a doing" or "a deed." "-able" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "capable of being" or "worthy of being." "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs, often from adjectives.


USAGE->

This word is primarily used as an adverb.  It modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, describing how something is done in a way that allows for action to be taken.

Here are examples in different sentence structures:

Statements:

The information was presented actionably. = 信息以可操作的方式呈现。

The report provided actionably insights. = 报告提供了可操作的见解。


Questions:

Can this data be presented more actionably? =  这些数据能否以更可操作的方式呈现？

How can we make this plan more actionably? = 我们如何使这个计划更具可操作性？
