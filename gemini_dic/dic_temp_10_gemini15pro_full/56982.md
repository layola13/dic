double-crossed:/ˌdʌbəlˈkrɒst/ (US) /ˌdʌbəlˈkrɔːst/ (UK)| adj. v. |被出卖的，背叛的；欺骗；双重背叛|Betrayed or cheated by someone you trusted. To deceive someone by working with their opponent secretly, especially after you have agreed to help them.| He felt double-crossed when his partner revealed their secret plan to the competition. 他感到被出卖了，因为他的伙伴向竞争对手透露了他们的秘密计划。|近义词：betrayed, cheated, deceived, backstabbed|反义词：loyal, faithful|词性: 主要为动词(past participle 可作形容词)
double-cross:/ˌdʌbəlˈkrɒst/ (US) /ˌdʌbəlˈkrɔːst/ (UK)|v.|欺骗；双重背叛|To deceive someone by working with their opponent secretly, especially after you have agreed to help them.|They double-crossed him by selling the information to the other side. 他们把他给出卖了，把情报卖给了对方。|近义词：betray, cheat, deceive, backstab|反义词：support, assist|词性: 动词

词源:  From the image of crossing someone's name off a list twice to indicate extreme treachery.  来自于将某人的名字从名单上划掉两次以表示极端背叛的形象。
