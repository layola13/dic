incensing:/ɪnˈsensɪŋ/
adjective
激怒人的
causing anger or outrage
例句：The incensing behavior of the customer made the manager furious. 顾客激怒人的行为让经理大发雷霆。

例句：His incensing words sparked a heated argument. 他激怒人的话引发了一场激烈的争论。

近义词：infuriating, maddening, aggravating, provoking
反义词：soothing, calming, placating, appeasing
词性：adjective

incense:/ˈɪnsɛns/
verb
激怒
to make someone very angry
例句：The new policy incensed the employees. 新政策激怒了员工。

例句：Her constant complaining incensed him. 她不断的抱怨激怒了他。

近义词：enrage, anger, infuriate, provoke
反义词：calm, soothe, placate, appease
词性：verb

incense:/ˈɪnsɛns/
noun
香
a substance that is burned to produce a sweet smell, especially as part of a religious ceremony
例句：The temple was filled with the smell of incense. 寺庙里充满了香的味道。

例句：She lit some incense to create a relaxing atmosphere. 她点了一些香来营造轻松的氛围。

近义词：fragrance, aroma, perfume
反义词：(没有直接的反义词，可以考虑 stench, odor if 指的是难闻的气味)
词性：noun


词源：From Old French encenser, from Late Latin incensare "to set on fire," from Latin in- "in" + candere "to shine, glow."  The noun form comes from Old French encens.
