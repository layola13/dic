antinomian:/ˌæntɪˈnəʊmiən/ | adj. n. | 反律法的，违反律法的；反律法主义者 |  Relating to the belief that Christians are not bound by moral laws, especially those of the Old Testament, as salvation is attained through grace alone.  A person who holds this belief. |  Some antinomian sects emerged during the Reformation.  一些反律法主义教派在宗教改革时期出现。| He was accused of being an antinomian. 他被指控为反律法主义者. | Synonyms: lawless, heretical, dissident | Antonyms: legalistic, orthodox, conformist | adjective, noun

antinomism:/ˌæntɪˈnəʊmɪɪzəm/ | n. | 反律法主义 |  The doctrine that Christians are not bound by moral law, especially that of the Old Testament, as salvation is attained through grace alone. | Antinomism was a controversial theological position during the Reformation. 反律法主义在宗教改革时期是一个有争议的神学立场。 | The preacher's words were interpreted as promoting antinomism.  传教士的话被解释为宣扬反律法主义。| Synonyms: lawlessness, heresy | Antonyms: legalism, orthodoxy | noun


Etymology:  From Greek *anti-* "against" + *nomos* "law".
