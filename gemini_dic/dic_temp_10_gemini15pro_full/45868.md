cutaway:/ˈkʌtəˌweɪ/| n. adj.|剖面图，剖视图；插播镜头；燕尾服|A cutaway is a drawing or model of something that has been cut in half so that you can see the inside.  A cutaway in a film or television programme is a short scene that shows something that is happening in a different place. A cutaway is also a man's formal jacket with tails at the back. |The diagram shows a cutaway of the engine. 该图显示了发动机的剖面图。

The news program showed a cutaway of the president arriving at the airport.  新闻节目插播了总统抵达机场的镜头。

He wore a cutaway to the opera. 他穿着一件燕尾服去歌剧院。|近义词：cross-section, inset, insert (for drawings/models); interruption, interlude (for broadcasts); tailcoat (for clothing)|反义词: (No direct antonym, depends on the meaning being used)|词性:noun, adjective
