dismarble:/dɪsˈmɑːrbl̩/
v.
剥夺大理石般的光泽或坚硬; 使失去光彩; 使失去坚固
To deprive of the qualities of marble, such as smoothness, hardness, or coolness.
The harsh weather dismarbled the ancient statue, leaving it rough and pitted. 恶劣的天气使古老的雕像失去了大理石般的光泽，变得粗糙且斑驳。
近义词:  mar, deface, spoil, ruin
反义词:  polish, enhance, beautify
词性: 动词

词源:  dis- (prefix denoting removal or reversal) + marble (n.)
