desolatingly:/ˈdɛsəleɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 荒凉地，凄凉地 |  In a way that causes or reveals desolation; bleakly and depressingly. | The wind howled desolatingly across the empty moor. 风凄凉地呼啸着掠过空旷的荒野。| synonym: bleakly, drearily, forlornly, miserably | antonym: cheerfully, brightly, joyfully | adverb

desolate:/ˈdɛsələt/ | adj. | 荒凉的，凄凉的 | (of a place) deserted of people and in a state of bleak and dismal emptiness. | The house stood desolate and empty.  房子孤零零地立在那里，空无一人。| synonym: barren, bleak, deserted, empty | antonym: populated, cheerful, lively | adjective

desolate:/ˈdɛsəleɪt/ | verb | 使荒芜，使凄凉 | Make (someone) feel utterly wretched and unhappy. | The news of her death desolated her family. 她去世的消息使她的家人悲痛欲绝。| synonym: devastate, ruin, wreck, destroy | antonym: comfort, cheer, encourage | verb
