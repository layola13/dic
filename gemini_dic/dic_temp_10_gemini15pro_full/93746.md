immaturely:/ˌɪməˈtʃʊrli/ /ˌɪməˈtʊrli/| adv.|不成熟地，幼稚地|In a way that shows a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment.  Acting in a manner not befitting one's age or status.|He behaved immaturely, throwing a tantrum when he didn't get his way. 他表现得很不成熟，因为没有达到目的就发脾气。|Synonyms: childishly, foolishly, thoughtlessly, irresponsibly|Antonyms: maturely, responsibly, wisely, thoughtfully|词性:副词

immature:/ˌɪməˈtʃʊr/ /ˌɪməˈtʊr/| adj.|不成熟的，幼稚的|Not fully developed; lacking in experience, wisdom, or judgment.|The immature fruit was sour and hard.  未成熟的果实又酸又硬。|Synonyms: childish, juvenile, undeveloped, naive|Antonyms: mature, developed, sophisticated, experienced|词性:形容词

immaturity:/ˌɪməˈtʃʊrəti/ /ˌɪməˈtʊrəti/| n.|不成熟，幼稚|The state of being immature; lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment.|His immaturity was evident in his impulsive decisions.  他的不成熟体现在他冲动的决定上。|Synonyms: childishness, foolishness, naiveté, inexperience|Antonyms: maturity, wisdom, sophistication, experience|词性:名词
