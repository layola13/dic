hearsecloth:/ˈhɜːrsklɒθ (UK), ˈhɜːrsklɔːθ (US)| n. |柩衣；灵车罩布|A large piece of cloth used to cover a coffin or hearse.|The hearse was draped in a somber hearsecloth.|近义词：pall, winding-sheet|反义词: None|词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hersecloth, equivalent to hearse + cloth.  Hearse itself comes from Old French herse "harrow," later a triangular frame for candles, then a framework to hold a coffin, and finally the vehicle itself.
