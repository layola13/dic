casuist:/ˈkæʒuɪst/| n.|诡辩家，决疑家|A person who studies and resolves moral problems by applying general principles of ethics, especially one who uses clever but unsound reasoning.|The professor, a renowned casuist, skillfully dissected the ethical dilemma presented by the student. 教授是一位著名的决疑家，巧妙地剖析了学生提出的伦理困境。|Synonyms: moralist, ethicist, theologian|Antonyms: amoralist, nihilist|Noun

casuistry:/ˈkæʒuɪstri/| n.|决疑论，诡辩|The art or practice of resolving moral problems by applying general principles of ethics, especially the use of clever but unsound reasoning.|His argument was pure casuistry, designed to justify his actions rather than address the real issue. 他的论点纯粹是诡辩，旨在为自己的行为辩护，而不是解决真正的问题。|Synonyms: sophistry, quibbling, hairsplitting|Antonyms: straightforwardness, honesty, candor|Noun
