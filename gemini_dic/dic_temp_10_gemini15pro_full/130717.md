nonallegation: /ˌnɒnˌæləˈɡeɪʃən/ | n. | 非指控; 未被指控的事项 |  The absence of an allegation; something that has not been alleged.  It often arises in legal contexts where something is explicitly stated *not* to be an accusation. |  "His statement was a nonallegation; he explicitly said he wasn't accusing anyone." 他声明并非指控，他明确表示自己没有指控任何人。| 近义词: non-accusation, denial of allegation | 反义词: allegation, accusation | 词性: 名词

词源:

non- (prefix):  From Latin *non*, meaning "not."  This prefix negates the word it's attached to.
allegation (noun): From Late Latin *allegationem*,  meaning "a bringing forth, adducing," from *allegare* "to adduce, allege," from *ad* "to" + *legare* "to send, depute, bequeath, appoint, allege."
