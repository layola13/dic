decouple:/diːˈkʌpəl/ | v. | 解耦，分离，使独立 | To separate or disengage something from something else, often to isolate it from external influences or dependencies. | The software decouples the user interface from the underlying code.  该软件将用户界面与底层代码分离。 | The company decided to decouple its online store from its physical retail locations. 该公司决定将其在线商店与其实体零售店分离。 | synonyms: separate, disconnect, disengage, isolate, unlink | antonyms: couple, connect, link, join | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- (meaning "off, away from") + couple (meaning "to join together").
