paranoically: /ˌpærəˈnɔɪɪkli/ | adv. |偏执地，妄想地|In a manner characterized by paranoia; excessively suspicious or distrustful.| He checked the locks paranoically, convinced someone was trying to break in. 他偏执地检查了所有的锁，确信有人试图闯入。|例句：She paranoically checked her phone for messages, fearing bad news. 她偏执地查看手机上的信息，害怕坏消息。|近义词：suspiciously, distrustfully, apprehensively, anxiously, fearfully|反义词：trustingly, confidently, calmly|词性:副词

paranoia: /ˌpærəˈnɔɪə/ | n. |妄想症，偏执狂|A mental condition characterized by delusions of persecution, unwarranted jealousy, or exaggerated self-importance, typically elaborated into an organized system. It may be an aspect of chronic personality disorder, of drug abuse, or of a serious condition such as schizophrenia.| He suffers from paranoia and believes everyone is out to get him. 他患有妄想症，认为每个人都想害他。 |例句：Her paranoia led her to believe that her neighbors were spying on her. 她的偏执让她相信邻居在监视她。 | 近义词: suspicion, distrust, mistrust | 反义词: trust, confidence | 词性:名词

词源: From Greek "para" (beside, beyond) + "nous" (mind), implying a mind "beside itself."
