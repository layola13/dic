menstruation: /ˌmɛnstruˈeɪʃən/ (US), /ˌmɛnstruˈeɪʃn/ (UK)| n. |月经；行经|The process in women and female primates of discharging blood and other materials from the lining of the uterus at intervals of about one lunar month from puberty until menopause, except during pregnancy.|例句：She experienced her first menstruation at the age of 13. 她13岁来了初潮。

例句：Irregular menstruation can be a sign of hormonal imbalance. 月经不调可能是荷尔蒙失衡的迹象。|近义词：period, menses|反义词: None|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *menstruacioun*, from Late Latin *mēnstruātiō*, from Latin *mēnstruāre* (“to menstruate”), from *mēnsis* (“month”) + *-struāre*, related to *construere* ("to build"). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *mḗh₁n̥s- (“moon, month”).
