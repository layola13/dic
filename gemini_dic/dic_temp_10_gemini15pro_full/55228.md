dissident:/ˈdɪsɪdənt/ | n. adj. |持不同政见者，异议者；持不同政见的，异议的| A person who publicly disagrees with and criticizes their government. |例句：The government imprisoned several dissidents. 政府囚禁了几名持不同政见者。|例句：She became a political dissident after the new regime took power. 新政权上台后，她成了政治异议人士。|近义词：rebel, dissenter, objector, nonconformist, iconoclast |反义词：conformist, loyalist |词性: 名词, 形容词

dissident:/dɪˈsɪdənt/ | adj. |持不同政见的，异议的| Characterized by dissent or disagreement, especially with an established religious or political system, organization, or belief. |例句：Dissident voices were suppressed by the authorities. 当局压制了异议。|例句：The dissident group planned a peaceful protest.  异议团体计划进行和平抗议。|近义词：rebellious, dissenting, challenging, unorthodox |反义词：conformist, orthodox, compliant |词性: 形容词
