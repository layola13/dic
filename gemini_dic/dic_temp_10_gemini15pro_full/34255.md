clapper:/ˈklæpər/ (US), /ˈklæpə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 拍板; 响板; (门环的)撞门锤; 铃舌|A device used to make a clapping sound, typically two pieces of wood hinged together, or a metal ball inside a bell.|The clapper on the bell was broken. 那个铃的铃舌坏了。|Synonyms: beater, striker, knocker|Antonyms: silencer, mute|noun

clapper:/ˈklæpər/ (US), /ˈklæpə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 鼓掌者;喝彩者|Someone who claps their hands in appreciation or approval.|The clappers cheered enthusiastically after the performance.  表演结束后，鼓掌者热情地欢呼。|Synonyms: applauder, cheerleader|Antonyms: critic, detractor|noun
