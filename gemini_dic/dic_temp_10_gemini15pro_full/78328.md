gerrymandering: /ˈdʒɛrimændərɪŋ/ | n. | 不公正的选区划分；杰利蝾螈 | The manipulation of the boundaries of (an electoral constituency) so as to favor one party or class.  |  Gerrymandering has become a common practice in some countries.  一些国家，不公正地划分选区已成为一种普遍做法。 | synonyms:  manipulation, rigging, fixing | antonyms: fair apportionment, equitable distribution | noun

gerrymander: /ˈdʒɛrimændər/ | v. | 不公正地划分选区；进行杰利蝾螈式划分  | To divide (a geographic area) into voting districts so as to give unfair advantage to one party in elections. | The state legislature gerrymandered the districts to ensure their party's victory. 州议会不公正地划分选区以确保他们党获胜。 | synonyms:  manipulate, rig, fix | antonyms: apportion fairly, distribute equitably | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Elbridge Gerry + salamander.  Elbridge Gerry was the governor of Massachusetts when in 1812 a district was created that, due to its shape, was said to resemble a salamander.
