aberrance: /əˈbɛrəns/ (US), /əˈbɛrəns/ (UK)
n.
异常，偏差，偏离
a departure from what is normal, usual, or expected, typically one that is unwelcome
The high rate of aberrance in this particular batch of products is alarming.  这批产品中异常高的偏差率令人担忧。

His sudden outburst of anger was an aberrance from his usual calm demeanor. 他突然爆发的愤怒与他平常的冷静举止大相径庭。

近义词: anomaly, deviation, abnormality, irregularity
反义词: normality, regularity, conformity
词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin aberrantia "a wandering away," from Latin aberrans, present participle of aberrare "to wander away," from ab- "away" + errare "to wander, stray, err."

USAGE->
作为名词，aberrance 通常接介词 of 或 from。

例句：
The genetic aberrance caused a rare disease. = 这种基因异常导致了一种罕见的疾病。

There was an aberrance in the data that needed further investigation. = 数据中存在一个异常，需要进一步调查。
