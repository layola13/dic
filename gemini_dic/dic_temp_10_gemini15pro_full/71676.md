flatworm:/ˈflætˌwɜːrm/ (US), /ˈflætˌwɜːm/ (UK)| n. |扁形虫，扁虫|A type of simple worm with a flattened body, belonging to the phylum Platyhelminthes.  They are bilaterally symmetrical and lack a body cavity.|Example: Flatworms are commonly found in freshwater habitats. 扁形虫常见于淡水栖息地。|Synonym: platyhelminth|Antonym: (No direct antonym, could use a more complex animal like a vertebrate)|noun


Etymology:
From flat + worm.  "Flat" referring to the flattened body shape, and "worm" denoting its worm-like appearance.
