flanch:/flæntʃ/
n.
凸缘，轮缘，边缘
A projecting rim, edge, or flange, especially of a wheel.
The flanch of the railway wheel prevents it from derailing. 火车车轮的轮缘防止它脱轨。
rim, edge, flange
---
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *flanche (unattested) or a related dialectal form, from Frankish *flanca (“flank”), from Proto-Germanic *flankō (“flank”). Cognate with Old High German flanka (“flank”), Middle Dutch flanke (“flank, side”), Old English flanc (“flank”). More at flank.
