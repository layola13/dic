lay:/leɪ/
v.
放置；产卵；下蛋；铺设；使躺下；提出；制定；打赌；把...归咎于
To put or place something down gently or carefully.  To produce eggs from the body.
He laid the book on the table. 他把书放在桌子上。
The hen laid an egg this morning. 母鸡今天早上下了个蛋。
近义词: place, put, set, deposit, position (for placing); produce (for eggs)
反义词: raise, lift, pick up (for placing)
词性: 动词

lay:/leɪ/
adj.
非专业的；世俗的；外行的
Not belonging to the clergy; not having specialized or professional knowledge.
He is a lay member of the church council. 他是教会理事会的非专业成员。
近义词: nonprofessional, secular, amateur
反义词: professional, clerical, expert
词性:形容词

lay:/leɪ/
n.
位置；层面；层；铺设；叙事诗
A position or level; a ballad or narrative poem.
The archaeologist carefully excavated each lay of the ancient city. 考古学家仔细地挖掘了这座古城的每一层。
近义词: level, stratum, tier, layer
反义词:  (No direct opposite for this sense)
词性: 名词

词源: From Old English *lecgan, from Proto-Germanic *lagjaną, from Proto-Indo-European *legh- (“to lay, lie”). Cognate with Dutch leggen (“to lay”), German legen (“to lay”), Swedish lägga (“to lay”).

注意事项: lay 和 lie 的区别经常混淆。lie (躺) 的过去式是 lay，这增加了混淆的可能性。请注意区分。
