lurk:/lɜːrk/ (US) /lɜːk/ (UK)| v. |潜伏，埋伏；暗中行动；隐藏；(危险、疑虑等)潜藏，存在|To wait or move in a secret way so that you cannot be seen, especially because you are about to do something bad or illegal. |The crocodile lurked in the murky water, waiting for its prey. 鳄鱼潜伏在浑浊的水中，等待猎物。

He suspected that danger lurked around every corner. 他怀疑每个角落都潜伏着危险。|近义词：skulk, prowl, sneak, loiter|反义词: appear, emerge|词性:动词

词源:
From Middle English *lurken*, from Old Norse *lurka* (“to lie in wait, lurk”), from Proto-Germanic *lurkōną* (“to crouch, bend down”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)lerk-* (“to bend, turn”). Cognate with Icelandic *lúra* (“to doze, slumber”), Danish *lurke* (“to lurk”), Swedish *lurka* (“to lurk”), German *lurken* (“to lurk”).
