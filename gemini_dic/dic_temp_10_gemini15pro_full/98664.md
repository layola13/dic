insularity:/ˌɪnsəˈlærəti/
n.
狭隘；偏狭；孤立；岛国状态
The state of being isolated or detached.  A narrow-mindedness or lack of interest in outside world.
The island's insularity makes its inhabitants resistant to change.  这个岛的孤立性使得其居民抗拒改变。
insularity can lead to prejudice. 偏狭会导致偏见。
近义词：narrow-mindedness, provincialism, isolation, detachment
反义词：cosmopolitanism, open-mindedness, worldliness
词性: 名词

insulated:/ˈɪnsjʊleɪtɪd/
adj.
隔离的；绝缘的
Protected from outside influence or contact.
The insulated community had its own unique customs. 这个与世隔绝的社区有其独特的风俗习惯。
The wires were insulated with rubber. 电线用橡胶绝缘。
近义词：isolated, protected, shielded
反义词：exposed, connected, integrated
词性: 形容词

insulate:/ˈɪnsjʊleɪt/
v.
使隔离；使绝缘；使免除（尤指不愉快的事）
To protect from outside influence or contact. To cover with a material that prevents the passage of heat, electricity, or sound.
They tried to insulate themselves from the political turmoil. 他们试图将自己与政治动荡隔离开来。
You can insulate your house to reduce energy costs. 你可以对你的房子进行隔热以降低能源成本。
近义词：protect, shield, isolate
反义词：expose, connect
词性: 动词

词源：From Late Latin insulāritās ("the state of being an island"), from Latin īnsula ("island").
