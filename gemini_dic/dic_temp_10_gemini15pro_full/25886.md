burlesquing:/bɜːrˈleskɪŋ/
|v.|滑稽模仿；以滑稽剧模仿|Making fun of something by imitating it in a humorous and exaggerated way. |He was burlesquing the pompous pronouncements of the politicians. 他用滑稽的方式模仿政客们浮夸的声明。|The comedian burlesqued the president's speech.  这位喜剧演员滑稽地模仿了总统的演讲。|近义词：mocking, satirizing, parodying, lampooning|反义词：praising, honoring, respecting|词性：动词

burlesque:/bɜːrˈlesk/
|n.|滑稽剧；滑稽模仿|A humorous and exaggerated imitation or parody.|The play was a burlesque of a popular melodrama.  这出戏是对一部流行情节剧的滑稽模仿。|Her performance was a burlesque of opera. 她的表演是对歌剧的滑稽模仿。|近义词：parody, satire, travesty, caricature|反义词：serious drama, tragedy|词性：名词

词源: From French *burlesque*, from Italian *burlesco*, from *burla* "joke, mockery".
