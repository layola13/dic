idiosyncratic:/ˌɪdiəsɪŋˈkrætɪk/ | adj. | 特有的，独特的，古怪的 | Relating to idiosyncrasy; peculiar or individual. |  He has an idiosyncratic way of tying his shoelaces. 他系鞋带的方式很独特。 |  She has a very idiosyncratic sense of humor. 她有一种非常独特的幽默感。| synonyms: peculiar, individual, characteristic, distinctive, quirky, eccentric | antonyms: typical, common, usual, normal | adjective

idiosyncratically: /ˌɪdiəsɪŋˈkrætɪkli/ | adv. | 特有地，独特地，古怪地 | In an idiosyncratic manner. | He dressed idiosyncratically, always wearing mismatched socks.  他的穿着很古怪，总是穿着不配对的袜子。 |  The artist painted idiosyncratically, using unconventional techniques. 这位艺术家以其独特的风格作画，使用非传统的技巧。| synonyms: peculiarly, individually, characteristically, distinctively, quirkily, eccentrically | antonyms: typically, commonly, usually, normally | adverb

词源：
From French *idiosyncratique*, from Ancient Greek ἰδιοσυγκρασία (idiosynkrasía, “peculiar temperament”), from ἴδιος (ídios, “one's own”) + σύν (sýn, “with”) + κρᾶσις (krâsis, “mixture, temperament”).
