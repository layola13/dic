dock:/dɑːk (US) /dɒk (UK)| n. |码头，船坞；被告席；酸模|A platform extending from the shore used for loading and unloading ships.  A place in a courtroom where the accused stands.|例句：The ship pulled into the dock to unload its cargo.  这艘船驶入码头卸货。

例句：The defendant stood in the dock. 被告站在被告席上。|近义词：pier, wharf, jetty, berth|反义词:None|词性:n.

dock:/dɑːk (US) /dɒk (UK)| vt. |（使）靠码头；扣除（工资）；剪短（尾巴）|To bring a ship into a dock. To deduct from someone's wages. To shorten something, especially an animal's tail.|例句：They docked the ship at the pier. 他们把船停靠在码头。

例句：The company docked his pay for being late. 公司因为他迟到而扣了他的工资。|近义词：moor, berth, trim, clip|反义词:None|词性:vt.

docks:/dɑːks (US) /dɒks (UK)| n. |码头区，船坞区|The area of a port containing docks. Often used in the plural.|例句：The docks were bustling with activity. 码头区熙熙攘攘。

例句：He grew up in the docks of Liverpool. 他在利物浦码头区长大。|近义词：waterfront, harbour, port|反义词:None|词性:n.
