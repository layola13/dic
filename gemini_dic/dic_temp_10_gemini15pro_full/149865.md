palfreyed:/ˈpɔːlfrid/ | adj. |骑着游步马的；适合骑游步马的 | Riding a palfrey; suitable for riding a palfrey; often used figuratively to describe something elegant, leisurely, or gentle. |  The knight, palfreyed in shining armor, rode through the forest.  骑士骑着披着闪亮盔甲的游步马穿过森林。|  近义词:  mounted, equestrian | 反义词:  pedestrian, walking | 词性: adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *palfrayd*, from Old French *palefrei* (modern French *palefroi*), from Late Latin *paraveredus*, from Latin *para* (“spare, extra”) + *veredus* (“post-horse”), from *vehere* (“to carry”).
