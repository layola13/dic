nicked:/nɪkt/| v. adj.|刻痕的，轻微割伤的；偷窃|From the verb "nick," meaning to make a small cut or notch.  The meaning of "steal" likely arose from the idea of quickly and stealthily cutting something loose, like a purse from a belt.|The car was nicked in the parking lot. 汽车在停车场被刮花了。|Synonyms: chipped, scratched, stolen, swiped|Antonyms: pristine, untouched, returned|verb, adjective

nicked:/nɪkt/| adj.|被偷走的；被捕的|From the verb "nick," meaning to make a small cut or notch, then extended to mean "steal."  Being "nicked" by the police implies being caught or arrested, perhaps through a quick and efficient action.|The police nicked him for shoplifting. 警察以入店行窃的罪名逮捕了他。|Synonyms: arrested, apprehended, caught|Antonyms: released, freed|adjective
