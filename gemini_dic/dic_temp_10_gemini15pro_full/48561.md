defamous:/dɪˈfeɪməs/| adj.| 诽谤的，中伤的| (of a statement) falsely and injuriously or dishonestly representing the character or reputation of another; libelous or slanderous|  The article made defamous allegations against the politician. 这篇文章对这位政治家提出了诽谤性的指控。|  The defamous rumors spread quickly through the town. 这些诽谤性的谣言迅速传遍了整个城镇。| synonyms: libelous, slanderous, calumnious, disparaging, derogatory | antonyms: complimentary, flattering, laudatory | word class: adjective

Etymology:
From Late Middle English: from Old French diffamant, -ant, present participle of diffamer, from Latin diffamare ‘spread abroad’, from dis- (expressing intensive force) + fama ‘fame, rumour’.
