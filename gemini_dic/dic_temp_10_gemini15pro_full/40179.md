contentions:/kənˈtɛnʃənz/
contentious:/kənˈtɛnʃəs/
| adj.|有争议的；爱争论的|likely to cause disagreement or argument|The contentious issue of immigration reform has divided the country. 移民改革这个有争议的问题使国家产生了分裂。|近义词：controversial, disputed, debatable|反义词：uncontroversial, undisputed, agreeable|词性:形容词

contention:/kənˈtɛnʃən/
| n.|争论；论点；竞争|heated disagreement; a point advanced or maintained in an argument; competition|Her main contention is that the new policy will harm the environment. 她的主要论点是这项新政策会损害环境。|近义词：argument, dispute, debate, claim, assertion|反义词：agreement, harmony, accord|词性:名词

PHRASE->
bone of contention: 争论的焦点; The ownership of the land is a major bone of contention between the two families. 土地所有权是两个家庭之间争论的主要焦点。
point of contention: 争论点; The price of the product remains a point of contention during the negotiations. 产品价格仍然是谈判过程中的一个争论点。
subject of contention: 争论的话题;  The interpretation of the law is the subject of much contention among legal scholars. 对法律的解释是法律学者之间争论的话题。
in contention:  有竞争力; With this win, she is now in contention for the championship. 凭借这场胜利，她现在有资格争夺冠军。
out of contention: 没有竞争力; After losing three games in a row, the team is now out of contention for the playoffs. 连续输掉三场比赛后，该队现在已经没有资格参加季后赛了。


词源：From Latin contentus, past participle of contendere “to strive,” from com- “together” + tendere “to stretch.”
