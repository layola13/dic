actium: /ˈæktɪəm/
noun
阿克提姆(古希腊地名)
Actium was a promontory in ancient Greece, located in the region of Epirus.  It was the site of the Battle of Actium in 31 BC.
The Battle of Actium marked a turning point in Roman history.  阿克提姆海战标志着罗马历史的一个转折点。
Octavian's victory at Actium secured his position as the future emperor Augustus.  屋大维在阿克提姆的胜利确保了他作为未来皇帝奥古斯都的地位。

近义词: promontory, headland
反义词: bay, inlet
词性: n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Actium*, from Ancient Greek *Ἄκτιον (Aktios)*.  Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂eḱ- (“sharp, pointed”)*, referring to the shape of the promontory.
