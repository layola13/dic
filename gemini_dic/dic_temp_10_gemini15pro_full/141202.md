oon:/uːn/ | n. | 大月亮; (苏格兰用语)下午; | A large moon. (Scottish) Afternoon. | The harvest moon was a giant oon in the sky.  秋收时节的月亮是天空中一个巨大的圆盘。/  "Meet me at the kirk at oon," she said. “下午在教堂见我，”她说。 | 近义词: moon | 反义词:  (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑sun/太阳) | 词性: 名词


词源:

From Middle English *mone, from Old English mōna (“moon”), from Proto-Germanic *mēnōn, from Proto-Indo-European *mēnsis (“month, moon”). Cognate with Scots mune (“moon”), West Frisian moanne (“moon”), Dutch maan (“moon”), German Mond (“moon”), Danish måne (“moon”), Swedish måne (“moon”), Icelandic máni (“moon”), Latin mēnsis (“month”), Ancient Greek μήν (mēn, “month”), Sanskrit मास (māsa, “month, moon”).
