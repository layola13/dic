fossil:/ˈfɑːsəl/ (US), /ˈfɒsɪl/ (UK)| n. adj.|化石；老顽固|The remains or impression of a prehistoric plant or animal embedded in rock and preserved in petrified form.  A person or thing that is outdated or resistant to change.|例句：The museum has a large collection of dinosaur fossils.  博物馆收藏了大量的恐龙化石。|近义词：relic, remnant, remain|反义词:modern, contemporary|词性:名词

fossil:/ˈfɑːsəl/ (US), /ˈfɒsɪl/ (UK)| adj.|化石的；陈旧的|Formed from or containing fossils.  Outdated or old-fashioned.|例句：Fossil fuels are a major source of energy. 化石燃料是主要的能源。|近义词：ancient, prehistoric, archaic|反义词:modern, contemporary|词性:形容词
