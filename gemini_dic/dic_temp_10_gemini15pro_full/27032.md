caledonians:/ˌkælɪˈdoʊniənz/ | n. | 加勒多尼亚人；苏格兰人 | People who lived in ancient Caledonia (roughly equivalent to modern Scotland), or, more generally, Scots.  |  The Caledonians resisted the Roman invasion fiercely. 加勒多尼亚人激烈抵抗罗马人的入侵。 | The Caledonians built impressive hillforts to defend their territory. 加勒多尼亚人建造了令人印象深刻的山地堡垒来保卫他们的领土。 | synonyms: Scots, Picts | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Caledōnii*, derived from *Caledonia*, the Roman name for northern Britain, of uncertain origin. Possibly from a Brittonic word meaning "hard-footed" or "possessing hard ground".
