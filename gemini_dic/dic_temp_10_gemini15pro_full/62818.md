enharmonically:/ɛnhɑːrˈmɑnɪkli/ | adv. | 等音地，和声地 |  In music theory, enharmonically refers to notes that sound the same but are written differently and have different functions within the harmony. |  C sharp and D flat are enharmonically equivalent. 升C和降D等音. | synonym: equivalently (in sound) | antonym:  (no direct antonym, consider "distinctly" in terms of notation) | adverb
