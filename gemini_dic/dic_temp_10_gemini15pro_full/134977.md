nonnaturalisms:/ˌnɒnˈnætʃrəlɪzəmz/ | n. |非自然主义|  Philosophical views that reject the idea that everything can be explained in terms of natural phenomena, often encompassing ethical, aesthetic, or theological concepts that are considered to be irreducible to natural explanations. |  Nonnaturalisms in ethics argue that moral properties are not reducible to natural properties like pleasure or happiness. | 近义词: supernaturalism, transcendentalism | 反义词: naturalism, physicalism | 词性: noun
