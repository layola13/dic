monozoa:/ˌmɒnəˈzoʊə/
n.
单生动物（门）
A phylum of parasitic protozoa characterized by a single flagellum and a simple, non-spore-forming life cycle.  They are typically found in the digestive systems of amphibians and reptiles.
Monozoa are a group of parasitic flagellates found in the intestines of amphibians.
近义词:flagellates (a broader category)
反义词: N/A (there isn't a direct opposite to a taxonomic group)
词性:noun

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek mónos (“single, alone”) + zôion (“animal”).
