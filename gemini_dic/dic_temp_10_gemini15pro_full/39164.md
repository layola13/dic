conge:/ˈkɒndʒeɪ/ (UK), /kɑːnˈʒeɪ/ (US)| n.|告别，告假，休假；（尤指）冻状物|A formal or official permission to depart; leave of absence, especially from a monastery. Also (especially archaic): a state of being frozen or congealed.|He took his conge of the king. 他向国王告辞。|近义词：farewell, leave, departure, adieu|反义词：greeting, arrival, welcome|noun

conge:/ˈkɒndʒeɪ/ (UK), /kɑːnˈʒeɪ/ (US)| v.|（使）冻结，凝固|To change from a soft or fluid state to a rigid or solid state, as by cooling or freezing.|The blood had congealed around the wound.  血液在伤口周围凝结了。|近义词：solidify, coagulate, thicken, freeze|反义词：melt, liquefy, thaw|verb
