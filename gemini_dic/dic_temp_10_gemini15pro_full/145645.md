overcontentiously: /ˌoʊvərˌkənˈtɛnʃəsli/ | adv. | 过于争论地，过分争辩地 |  In a manner characterized by excessive or unnecessary contention; argumentatively and excessively. | The issue was debated overcontentiously, with both sides refusing to compromise.  这个问题争论得过于激烈，双方都不愿妥协。| The lawyer overcontentiously cross-examined the witness, objecting to nearly every question.  律师过分争辩地盘问证人，几乎对每个问题都提出异议。|  contentiously, argumentatively, quarrelsomely |  amicably, agreeably, peacefully | adverb

词源:

over- (prefix):  meaning "excessive," "too much"
contentious (adjective): from Latin *contentĭōsus, from contentĭō "strife, struggle," from contendĕre "to strive, struggle," from com- "together" + tendĕre "to stretch, extend."
