henchman:/ˈhɛntʃmən/| n.|爪牙，帮凶，走狗|A faithful follower, especially one prepared to engage in crime or violence on behalf of their leader. |The dictator surrounded himself with heavily armed henchmen.  独裁者身边围满了全副武装的爪牙。

The mob boss and his henchmen were arrested. 黑帮老大和他的爪牙被逮捕了。|Synonyms: minion, accomplice, goon, thug, lackey|Antonyms: leader, boss, opponent, adversary|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *henxman*, from *hengest-man* (“groom”), from Old English *hengest* (“horse”) + *mann* (“man”).  Originally referred to a groom or stableman, later evolving to mean a trusted follower, often with negative connotations of illegal or violent activity.
