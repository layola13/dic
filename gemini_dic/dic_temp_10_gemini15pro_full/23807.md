brazenness:/ˈbreɪzənnəs/| n. |厚颜无耻，肆无忌惮|The brazenness of the thief shocked everyone.  The quality of being brazen; impudence or effrontery.|He had the brazenness to ask for a raise after only a week on the job. 他竟然厚颜无耻地要求只工作一周就加薪。|近义词：audacity, impudence, boldness, nerve, cheek, temerity|反义词：timidity, shyness, modesty, humility|词性:名词

Etymology:  From Middle English *brasennesse*, from Old French *braisness*, from *braise* (“burning coals, embers”) (from Frankish *brasa*, from Proto-Germanic *brasō, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰrēgʷ- (“to shine, glow”)).  The original sense was "like brass," referring to hardness and brightness, later evolving to "shameless" and "impudent."
