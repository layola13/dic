north-westward:/ˌnɔːrθˈwɛstwərd/| adj. adv.|西北方向的，向西北的|Situated in or towards the northwest; moving or directed towards the northwest.|The north-westward wind brought heavy rain.|northwesterly, westward|south-eastward|adjective, adverb


ETYMOLOGY->

From north-west + -ward or -wards.  "North-west" itself comes from Old English "northwest," a combination of "north" and "west."  "-Ward" and "-wards" are suffixes indicating direction, derived from Old English "-weard."
