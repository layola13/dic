impression:/ɪmˈprɛʃən/ | n. | 印象；感想；印记；压痕 | An idea, feeling, or opinion about something or someone, especially one formed without conscious thought or on the basis of little evidence. |  The meeting left me with the impression that they weren't very interested. 这次会议给我的印象是他们不太感兴趣。 | synonym: feeling, sense, notion, idea | antonym: reality, fact | noun

impression:/ɪmˈprɛʃən/ | n. | 模仿；滑稽模仿 | An attempt to copy the manner and speech of a person or character, usually in order to make people laugh. | He does a brilliant impression of the president. 他对总统的模仿惟妙惟肖。 | synonym: imitation, mimicry, impersonation | antonym: original | noun

impression:/ɪmˈprɛʃən/ | n. | 效果；影响；深刻印象 | The effect or influence that something or someone has on a person's thoughts or feelings. |  His speech made a strong impression on the audience. 他的演讲给听众留下了深刻的印象。 | synonym: impact, effect, influence | antonym: insignificance | noun

impression:/ɪmˈprɛʃən/ | n. | 印记；压痕 | A mark made on a surface by pressing something onto it. |  The dog left the impression of its paws in the wet cement. 狗在湿水泥地上留下了爪印。 | synonym: mark, imprint, indentation | antonym: smoothness | noun
