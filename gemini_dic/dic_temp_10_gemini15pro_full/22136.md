bolts:/bəʊlts/ (British) /boʊlts/ (American)| n. v.|螺栓，门闩，闪电，快速移动；闩住，用螺栓固定，狼吞虎咽|A metal pin or rod, usually with a head at one end and a screw thread at the other, used to fasten things together.|The engineer tightened the bolts on the bridge. 工程师拧紧了桥上的螺栓。|近义词：fasteners, screws, pins, dashes|反义词: loosen, unfasten|词性:名词, 动词

Etymology: From Old English *bolt or *bolte, meaning "arrow, javelin," from Proto-Germanic *bultom, perhaps related to *bhel- "to swell."  The sense of "metal fastener" developed later, likely from the resemblance to an arrow shaft.
