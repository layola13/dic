high-ranking:/haɪˈræŋkɪŋ/| adj.|高级的，高阶的|Having a high rank or position; important|例句：She is a high-ranking official in the government. 她是政府高级官员。

例句：High-ranking officers in the military have a lot of responsibility.  军队中的高级军官责任重大。

近义词：senior, top, leading, prominent, important|反义词：low-ranking, junior, subordinate|词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From "high" + "ranking." "High" comes from Old English "hēah," meaning "tall, elevated." "Ranking" comes from the verb "rank," which ultimately derives from Old French "renc," meaning "row, line."  The combination "high-ranking" denotes someone positioned high within a hierarchy or order.
