against the grain:/əˈɡenst ðə ɡreɪn/ | idiom |违反常规；格格不入；与…格格不入|Going against the grain means doing something that is difficult or unpleasant because it contradicts one's natural inclinations or the prevailing norms.|例句：It goes against the grain to have to work on a Sunday.  星期天还要工作，真是违反常规。

例句：Living a lie goes against the grain with me.  过着虚伪的生活让我觉得格格不入。|近义词：against one's nature, counterintuitive, unconventional, unnatural|反义词：natural, intuitive, conventional, with the grain|词性：idiom (习语)

against-one-grain (This is not a standard English word or phrase. The correct idiom is "against the grain.")
