barbarism: /ˈbɑːrbərɪzəm/ (US) /ˈbɑːbərɪzəm/ (UK) | n. | 野蛮；野蛮行为；粗野的言行；（语言的）不合规范 | An act, custom, or belief seen as uncivilized or primitive.  A word or expression that is incorrect or unacceptable according to standard usage. |  The museum highlighted the barbarism of the ancient conquerors. (博物馆着重展示了古代征服者的野蛮行径。)

The use of "irregardless" is considered a barbarism.  ("irregardless" 的使用被认为是语言的错误。) | 近义词: savagery, brutality, crudity, solecism | 反义词: civilization, refinement, civility | 词性: 名词

barbarous: /ˈbɑːrbərəs/ (US) /ˈbɑːbərəs/ (UK) | adj. | 野蛮的；残暴的；粗鲁的；（语言）不规范的 | Extremely cruel or unpleasant; uncivilized and primitive.  (Of language) incorrect or unacceptable according to standard usage. |  The barbarous treatment of prisoners was condemned by human rights organizations. (囚犯所遭受的野蛮待遇受到了人权组织的谴责。)

His barbarous grammar made it difficult to understand him. (他糟糕的语法让人很难理解他。) | 近义词: savage, brutal, cruel, uncivilized | 反义词: civilized, refined, humane | 词性: 形容词



ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *barbarisme*, from Latin *barbarismus*, from Ancient Greek *βαρβαρισμός (barbarismós)*, from *βάρβαρος (bárbaros)* 'foreign, barbarous'. Ultimately of imitative origin.
