confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/ /ˈkɑːnfaʊnd/| vt.|使困惑，使混淆，挫败|To cause surprise or confusion in (someone), especially by acting against their expectations.|Her sudden recovery confounded the doctors. 她突然康复了，这使医生们感到困惑。|Synonyms: confuse, bewilder, perplex, baffle|Antonyms: clarify, enlighten|verb

confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/ /ˈkɑːnfaʊnd/| n. (obsolete)|困惑，混乱|A state of confusion.|The confound of their language made it difficult to understand them. 他们的语言混乱，难以理解。|Synonyms: confusion, bewilderment|Antonyms: clarity, order|noun
