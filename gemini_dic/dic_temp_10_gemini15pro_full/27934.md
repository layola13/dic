cantilever: /ˈkæntɪliːvər/ (US), /ˈkæntɪliːvə(r)/ (UK) | n. | 悬臂，悬臂梁 | A beam or structure that is fixed at one end and projects horizontally, supporting a load at the other end. |  The balcony is a cantilever extending out from the building.  阳台是从建筑物延伸出去的悬臂。 | synonyms: overhang, projection | antonyms: support, pier | noun

cantilever: /ˈkæntɪliːvər/ (US), /ˈkæntɪliːvə(r)/ (UK) | v. |  悬挑，伸出 | To build or extend (something) as a cantilever. | The roof cantilevers over the terrace. 屋顶悬挑在露台上方。 | synonyms: project, extend, jut | antonyms: retract, withdraw | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *cantileva, probably from *canta + leva "lift, lever".
