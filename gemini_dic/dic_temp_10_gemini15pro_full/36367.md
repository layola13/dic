coincider:/kəʊɪnˈsaɪdə(r)/ | n. |巧合者，符合者|A person or thing that coincides with another. |例句：The two events were coinciders, occurring on the same day. 这两个事件是巧合的，发生在同一天。

例句：It was a pure coincider that they both chose the same hotel. 他们选择同一家酒店纯属巧合。|近义词：correspondent, counterpart |反义词: N/A |词性: noun


coincidence:/kəʊˈɪnsɪdəns/ | n. |巧合，一致，符合|The occurrence of events that happen at the same time by accident but seem to have some connection.|例句：It was a strange coincidence that we met in Paris. 我们在巴黎相遇真是奇怪的巧合。

例句：By coincidence, I met my old friend on the street yesterday. 碰巧的是，我昨天在街上遇到了我的老朋友。|近义词：accident, chance, fluke |反义词:intention, plan |词性: noun

coincide:/ˌkəʊɪnˈsaɪd/ | v. |同时发生，一致，符合|Occur at or during the same time.|例句：Her birthday coincides with Christmas. 她的生日与圣诞节是同一天。

例句：Our views coincide on this matter. 我们在这个问题上的观点一致。|近义词：concur, correspond, agree |反义词:differ, diverge |词性: verb


词源:

From Late Latin *coincidere, from Latin co- "together" + incidere "to fall into," from in- "into, in, on, upon" + cadere "to fall."
