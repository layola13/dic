depressiveness:/dɪˈprɛsɪvnɪs/
n.|抑郁，沮丧|The state of being depressed; sadness, gloominess.|Her depressiveness made it difficult for her to enjoy anything.|近义词:depression, gloom, melancholy, dejection|反义词:cheerfulness, happiness, elation, joy|词性:名词
Etymology: From depressive + -ness.  "Depressive" comes from the Late Latin "depressus," past participle of "deprimere" meaning "to press down," from "de-" (down) + "premere" (to press). The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
