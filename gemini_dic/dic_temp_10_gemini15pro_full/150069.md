palsy: /ˈpɔːlzi/ (UK) /ˈpɑːlzi/ (US) | n. | 麻痹，瘫痪 |  Paralysis, especially partial paralysis, often accompanied by involuntary tremors or spasms. |  Cerebral palsy affects muscle control. 脑瘫会影响肌肉控制。|  paralysis, paresis | function, mobility | noun


palsy: /ˈpɔːlzi/ (UK) /ˈpɑːlzi/ (US) | adj. | 瘫痪的 | Affected with palsy; paralyzed. | He suffered a stroke and his left arm became palsy. 他中风了，左臂瘫痪了。| paralyzed, crippled | healthy, mobile | adjective (less common)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *palsye*, from Old French *palasie*, from Late Latin *paralysis*, from Greek *παράλυσις* (*paralusis*) "a loosening at the side, paralysis," from *παραλύειν* (*paraluein*) "to disable on one side, paralyze," from *παρά* (*para*) "beside, amiss" + *λύειν* (*luein*) "to loosen."
