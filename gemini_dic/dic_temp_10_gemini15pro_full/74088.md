foster:/ˈfɒstə(r) (UK), ˈfɑːstər (US)/
|v. adj.|培养，促进；收养，寄养|To encourage the development or growth of something; to bring up a child that is not one's own by birth.|They fostered a culture of innovation in the company. 他们在公司里培养了创新文化。|近义词：encourage, promote, nurture, cultivate, raise, rear|反义词：neglect, hinder, discourage|词性:动词
foster:/ˈfɒstə(r) (UK), ˈfɑːstər (US)/
|adj.|寄养的，收养的|Having the legal status of being fostered.|She lived with a foster family for several years.  她在一个寄养家庭生活了几年。|近义词：adoptive|反义词：biological|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fostrian "to nourish, support," from *fostor "food, nourishment."  Related to Old Norse fostra "to nurse," and German füttern "to feed."
