fizgig:/ˈfɪzgɪɡ/| n.|玩艺儿；轻浮的人；小玩意儿；爆竹|A fizgig is a small, frivolous thing, often used to describe a toy, a gadget, or a lively, flighty person.|She was a fizgig, always flitting from one activity to another. 她是个轻浮的人，总是忙个不停。|Synonyms: trinket, bauble, gewgaw, trifle, flighty person|Antonyms: serious person, important matter|Noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *fisgig*, probably of imitative origin.  Relates to the fizzing or sputtering sound of fireworks.
