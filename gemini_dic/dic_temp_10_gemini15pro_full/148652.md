overthrowing: /ˌoʊvərˈθroʊɪŋ/ /ˌəʊvəˈθrəʊɪŋ/
|v.|推翻；颠覆|The act of removing a government or leader from power, usually by force.|例句：The overthrowing of the dictator was celebrated by the people. 人民庆祝推翻了独裁者。|近义词：deposing, ousting, toppling, unseating|反义词：establishing, installing, instating|词性: 动名词(gerund)
overthrow: /ˌoʊvərˈθroʊ/ /ˌəʊvəˈθrəʊ/
|v.|推翻；颠覆|To remove a government or leader from power, usually by force.|例句：The rebels are attempting to overthrow the government. 反叛者试图推翻政府。|近义词：depose, oust, topple, unseat|反义词：establish, install, instate|词性: 动词

词源: From Middle English *overthrawen*, from Old English *oferþrawan* (“to throw over, overturn, cast down, overcome, conquer”), from *ofer* (“over”) + *þrawan* (“to throw, twist”). Cognate with Dutch *overwerpen* (“to overthrow”), German *überwerfen* (“to throw over, to fall out with”).
