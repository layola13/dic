hatchway:/ˈhætʃweɪ/| n.|舱口，舱门；天窗|An opening or passage in the deck, roof, or floor of a ship, aircraft, or building, used for access or ventilation.|The sailors opened the hatchway to access the cargo hold below. 水手们打开舱口，进入下面的货舱。|Synonyms: opening, hatch, doorway, access|Antonyms: closure, blockage|noun

hatchway:/ˈhætʃweɪ/| n.|（尤指屋顶上的）活板门，天窗|A hinged or sliding door covering a hatchway, especially in a roof.|He climbed through the hatchway onto the roof. 他爬过天窗上了屋顶。|Synonyms: trapdoor, skylight|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English "haccheway," from "hacche" (hatch) + "wey" (way).  "Hatch" ultimately comes from Old English "hæcc" meaning "gate, wicket."
