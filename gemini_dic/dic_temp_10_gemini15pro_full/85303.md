harness:/ˈhɑːrnɪs/ (US) /ˈhɑːnɪs/ (UK)| n. vt.|马具，挽具；安全带；利用，控制|A set of straps and fittings by which a horse or other draught animal is fastened to a cart, plough, etc.  To control and make use of (natural resources), especially to produce energy.|The farmer put the harness on the horse. 农民给马套上挽具。|Synonyms:tackle, gear, equipment, apparatus, rigging, trappings, yoke, control, exploit, utilize, employ, apply|Antonyms:release, free, liberate|noun, verb

harness:/ˈhɑːrnɪs/ (US) /ˈhɑːnɪs/ (UK)| vt.|利用，控制|To bring under control and direct the force of something.|They harnessed the power of the river to generate electricity. 他们利用河水的力量发电。|Synonyms: control, exploit, utilize, employ, apply|Antonyms:release, free, liberate|verb
