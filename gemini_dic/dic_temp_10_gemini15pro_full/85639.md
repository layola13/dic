haunted:/ˈhɔːntɪd/ (US), /ˈhɔːntɪd/ (UK)| adj.|闹鬼的；困扰的|Often visited by ghosts; showing signs of worry or distress.|The haunted house stood on a hill overlooking the town. 那栋闹鬼的房子坐落在俯瞰小镇的山丘上。|He had a haunted look in his eyes. 他的眼神里充满了困扰。|近义词：spooky, eerie, ghostly, troubled, tormented|反义词：peaceful, serene, untroubled|词性:形容词

haunt:/hɔːnt/ (US), /hɔːnt/ (UK)| v. n.|（鬼魂）出没；萦绕；常去|To visit (a place) regularly; to continue to cause problems or affect someone.|The ghost of his former partner haunts him. 他以前搭档的鬼魂一直缠着他。|He haunts the local bars. 他经常光顾当地的酒吧。|近义词：frequent, visit, plague, torment|反义词：avoid, shun|词性:动词 & 名词

词源: From Old French *hanter "to frequent, practice," probably from a Scandinavian source (compare Norwegian and Danish *hanta "to grasp, to handle"). The meaning shifted from "frequent" to "to appear as a ghost" in the 16th century.
