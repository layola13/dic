pandered:/ˈpændərd/ | v. |迎合；迁就；纵容|gratified or indulged an immoral or distasteful desire, need, or habit or someone with such a desire, etc.|He pandered to the crowd by telling them what they wanted to hear. 他迎合群众，告诉他们想听的话。|近义词: indulged, catered to, gratified |反义词: resisted, defied, challenged |词性:动词

词源: From Pandarus, a character in Chaucer's *Troilus and Criseyde* who acted as a go-between for the lovers.  Originally meant to act as a go-between in a love affair, later broadened to mean gratifying someone's desires, especially if those desires are considered base or immoral.
