demagoguing:/ˈdeməɡɑːɡɪŋ/
n.|煽动，蛊惑人心|The act of appealing to people's emotions and prejudices rather than their reason or logic, often in order to gain political power.|His campaign was filled with demagoguing and fear-mongering.|近义词：rabble-rousing, inflammatory rhetoric, fear-mongering|反义词：reasoned discourse, logical argumentation|词性:名词(gerund)

demagogue:/ˈdeməɡɑːɡ/
n.|煽动者，蛊惑民心的政客|A political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument.|The demagogue stirred up the crowd with his hateful rhetoric.|近义词：rabble-rouser, agitator, firebrand|反义词：statesman, diplomat|词性:名词

demagogic:/ˌdeməˈɡɑːɡɪk/
adj.|煽动性的，蛊惑人心的|Relating to or characteristic of a demagogue; appealing to people's emotions and prejudices rather than their reason.|His demagogic speeches incited violence and hatred.|近义词：inflammatory, rabble-rousing, provocative|反义词：rational, reasoned, logical|词性:形容词

词源: From Ancient Greek dēmagōgos (δημαγωγός, “popular leader”), from dēmos (δῆμος, “people”) + agōgos (ἀγωγός, “leader”).
