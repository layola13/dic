ousted:/ˈaʊstɪd/
verb|驱逐，罢免，革职|removed from a position of power or authority, typically by force or vote.|The CEO was ousted after the company's poor performance.  首席执行官因公司业绩不佳而被罢免。|Synonyms: expelled, removed, deposed, overthrown, ejected|Antonyms: reinstated, appointed, elected|v.

ousted:/ˈaʊstɪd/
adjective|被驱逐的，被罢免的|removed from a position of power or authority.|The ousted president fled the country. 被罢免的总统逃离了这个国家。|Synonyms: deposed, former, ex-|Antonyms: reigning, incumbent|adj.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French oster "to remove, take away," from Latin obstare "to stand before, oppose, hinder," from ob "against" + stare "to stand." The meaning shifted from "opposing" to "removing" someone from a position.
