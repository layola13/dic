louche:/luːʃ/ | adj. | 不体面的；可疑的；放荡的；声名狼藉的 | Disreputable or shady; of questionable taste or morality; suggesting or characterized by excessive indulgence in pleasure or sensual delights.  Often implies a decadent or morally dubious atmosphere or character. |  The bar had a louche atmosphere, with dim lighting and questionable characters.  酒吧里的气氛很暧昧，灯光昏暗，人物可疑。

The singer's louche lifestyle was well documented in the tabloids.  这位歌手放荡的生活方式在小报上得到了充分的记载。 | 近义词: shady, disreputable, dubious, decadent, seedy, sleazy | 反义词: respectable, reputable, moral, upright, virtuous | 词性: adjective

Etymology:
From French "louche" (squinting, cross-eyed, ambiguous), from Late Latin "luscus" (one-eyed, blind in one eye).  The semantic shift from physical squinting to moral shadiness likely occurred because squinting was sometimes associated with suspicion or shiftiness.
