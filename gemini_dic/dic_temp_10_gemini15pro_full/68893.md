fastidiosity:/ˌfæstɪdiˈɒsɪti/ | n. |一丝不苟；挑剔；苛求 |The state of being fastidious; excessive attention to detail and a tendency to be overly critical or demanding.| Her fastidiosity made it difficult for anyone to work with her. 她过于挑剔，很难与人共事。|Synonyms: meticulousness, punctiliousness, scrupulousness, exactingness| Antonyms: carelessness, negligence, sloppiness | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *fastidiositas, from Latin fastidiosus ("disgusting, disdainful, squeamish"), from fastidium ("loathing, aversion, disgust"), from fastus ("haughtiness, arrogance, pride") + taedium ("weariness, tediousness").
