anthology:/ænˈθɒlədʒi/
n.|选集，文选|A collection of selected literary pieces, such as poems, short stories, or plays.|Example 1:  I'm reading an anthology of modern American poetry. 我正在读一本现代美国诗歌选集。

Example 2: The publisher is releasing a new anthology of science fiction stories. 出版社正在发行一本新的科幻小说选集。|Synonyms: collection, compilation, compendium|Antonyms:  None (Anthology refers to a specific type of collection; there isn't a direct opposite.)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἀνθολογία (anthología, “gathering of flowers, garland”),  from ἄνθος (ánthos, “flower, bloom”) + -λογία (-logía, from λέγω (légō, “I gather, collect, pick out”)).  The original meaning referred to a collection of small poems or epigrams, likened to a bouquet of flowers.
