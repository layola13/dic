emancipate: /ɪˈmænsɪpeɪt/ | v. | 解放，使摆脱 | To free someone from slavery, control, or the power of another person. |  Abraham Lincoln emancipated the slaves. 亚伯拉罕·林肯解放了奴隶。

The company emancipated its workers from the oppressive rules. 公司将工人从压迫性的规定中解放出来。


Synonyms: liberate, free, release, unchain, unshackle

Antonyms: enslave, imprison, confine, restrict, subdue

词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->

From Latin *emancipare*  "to transfer ownership (of a son) by formal declaration," from *ex- "out" + mancipare "to transfer ownership," from manus "hand" + capere "to take."
