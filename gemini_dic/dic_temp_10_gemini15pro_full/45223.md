culminate:/ˈkʌlmɪneɪt/| vi. vt.|达到顶点，达到高潮；以…告终|To reach the highest point of development; to end, especially after a long time, in a particular way. |例句：The festival culminated in a spectacular fireworks display.  这个节日以一场盛大的烟火表演达到高潮。

例句：His career culminated with his election to the presidency. 他的职业生涯以当选总统而达到顶峰。|近义词：climax, peak, conclude, end|反义词:begin, start, commence|词性:动词

culmination:/ˌkʌlmɪˈneɪʃn/| n.|顶点，高潮；最终结果|The highest point of development; the final result of something.|例句：The signing of the treaty was the culmination of years of negotiation.  签署条约是多年谈判的最终结果。

例句：Winning the championship was the culmination of a lifetime's dedication. 赢得冠军是他毕生奉献的最高成就。|近义词：climax, peak, apex, conclusion|反义词:beginning, start, commencement|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "culminatus," past participle of "culminare" meaning "to top, crown," from "culmen" meaning "top, summit."
