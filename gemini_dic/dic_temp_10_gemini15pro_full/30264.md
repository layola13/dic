celticism: /ˈkɛltɪˌsɪzəm/ | n. |凯尔特主义;凯尔特文化;凯尔特风格|A characteristic feature of the Celts or their culture, especially in art, literature, or music.  The belief in and promotion of Celtic cultural identity.|例句：The intricate knotwork designs are a clear example of Celticism in early medieval art.  这些复杂的结饰图案是中世纪早期艺术中凯尔特主义的一个明显例子。

例句：A revival of interest in Celticism swept through Europe in the 19th century. 19世纪，一股对凯尔特主义的兴趣复兴浪潮席卷了欧洲。|近义词：Celticity, Celtic culture |反义词: N/A |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Celt + -ism.  "Celt" ultimately derives from the Greek "Keltoi," used to refer to a group of tribal peoples in central and western Europe.  "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, principles, or practices of a particular kind.
