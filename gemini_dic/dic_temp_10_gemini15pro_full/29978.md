caviling:/ˈkævəlɪŋ/| adj. n.|吹毛求疵的，无端指摘的；吹毛求疵，无端指摘|Making petty or unnecessary objections.|He's always caviling about the smallest things. 他总是对最微不足道的事情吹毛求疵。|近义词：carping, nitpicking, quibbling|反义词：approving, praising|词性: adjective, noun
Etymology: From Middle English *caveillen*, from Old French *caviller* "to mock, jest, cavil," from Latin *cavillārī* "to jeer, joke, banter, make jests, rail at," probably frequentative of *callāre* (from which also comes "calumny").
