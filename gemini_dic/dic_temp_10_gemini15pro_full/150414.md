panselectionism:/ˌpænɪˈlɛkʃənɪzəm/ | n. |泛选择论|The theory that all significant evolutionary change is the result of natural selection.  It minimizes the role of other evolutionary mechanisms, such as genetic drift. | Panselectionism is a controversial theory in evolutionary biology. 泛选择论是进化生物学中一个有争议的理论。| Example: Some biologists argue that panselectionism oversimplifies the complexity of evolution. 一些生物学家认为泛选择论过度简化了进化的复杂性。| synonyms: adaptationism, neo-Darwinism | antonyms: neutralism, mutationism | noun
