drown:/draʊn/| v.|淹死，溺死；浸泡；压倒，淹没|to die by being submerged in water, or to kill someone in this way; to cover or submerge something completely in liquid; to overwhelm or engulf someone or something.|The child almost drowned in the lake.  这个孩子差点在湖里淹死。

The cook drowned the chicken in gravy. 厨师把鸡肉浸泡在肉汁里。

Her sorrows were drowned in work.  她的悲伤被工作淹没了。|近义词：submerge, suffocate, overwhelm, inundate|反义词：float, rescue, save|词性:动词

drown:/draʊn/| adj. (rare, poetic)|溺水的，浸透的|soaked or saturated with water|His drown clothes clung to his skin. 他湿透的衣服紧紧贴在他的皮肤上。|近义词：soaked, saturated|反义词：dry|词性:形容词

词源：From Old English *druncnian, from Proto-Germanic *drunkjaną (“to be drunk, drown”), from Proto-Indo-European *dhreugh- (“to disturb, muddy”). Cognate with Dutch dronken (“drunk”), German trunken (“drunk”).  The original sense was "to be drunk", later "to be overwhelmed (as by water)".
