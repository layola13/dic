mezzotinters:/ˌmetsəˈtɪntərz/ | n. | 铜版雕刻家；黑版画家 | An artist who creates mezzotints, a type of intaglio printmaking. |  The mezzotinters of the 18th century were renowned for their skill in capturing subtle gradations of light and shadow. 18世纪的铜版雕刻家以其捕捉微妙光影渐变的技巧而闻名。


The museum's collection includes mezzotinters by some of the most celebrated artists of the period.  这家博物馆的收藏包括一些同时期最著名艺术家的铜版画作品。 | 近义词: engravers, printmakers | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, could be considered the opposite of other types of artists, like sculptors or painters) | 词性: 名词(plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *mezzotinto* "half-tint," from *mezzo* "middle, half" + *tinto* "tinged, dyed."  This refers to the process of creating a mezzotint, which starts with a roughened plate that would print solid black and is then smoothed in areas to create lighter tones.
