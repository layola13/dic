egregious: /ɪˈɡriːdʒəs/ | adj. | 极坏的；过分的；惊人的 |  Conspicuously bad; shocking. Origin: from Latin *exgregius* “standing out from the flock,” from *ex-* “out of” + *grex* “flock.” The meaning shifted from “exceptional” to “exceptionally bad.” | The judge called the decision an egregious error. 法官称该判决是一个严重的错误。 |  atrocious, heinous, flagrant | commendable, laudable | adjective
