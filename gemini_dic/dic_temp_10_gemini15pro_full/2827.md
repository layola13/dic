adventure-playgrounds: /ədˈvɛntʃə(r) ˈpleɪɡraʊndz/ | n. |冒险乐园|A playground designed to provide opportunities for children to engage in challenging and imaginative play, often incorporating natural elements and loose parts that can be manipulated and rearranged. |The children spent hours exploring the adventure-playground, building forts and creating their own games. 孩子们花了几个小时探索冒险乐园，建造堡垒并创造自己的游戏。|近义词:playground, play area, playscape|反义词:structured playground|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
"adventure" (n.) : from Old French "aventure" (12c.), from Latin "adventurus" "about to happen," from "advenire" "to arrive," from "ad-" "to" + "venire" "to come".
"playground" (n.):  from "play" (v.) + "ground" (n.). "Play" (v.) Old English "plegan" "to exercise oneself, play," of Germanic origin. "Ground" (n.) Old English "grund" "ground, soil, bottom; territory; foundation, origin, abyss," from Proto-Germanic "*grunduz" (source also of Old Saxon, Old Frisian "grund," Dutch "grond," Old High German "grunt," German "Grund," Old Norse "grund," Gothic "grundus").
