on-ramp:/ˈɒnˌræmp/ (UK) /ˈɑːnˌræmp/ (US)| n. |入口匝道；渐增过程|A short road used for entering a highway or freeway. Also metaphorically used for a gradual process of introduction or increase.|例句：The car accelerated as it merged onto the on-ramp. 汽车并入入口匝道时加速行驶。

例句：The company designed an on-ramp for new employees to gradually integrate into the team.  公司为新员工设计了一个渐增过程，以便他们逐渐融入团队。|近义词：entrance ramp, access ramp, approach|反义词:off-ramp, exit ramp|词性:noun

on-ramp:/ˈɒnˌræmp/ (UK) /ˈɑːnˌræmp/ (US)| n. |渐增过程|A gradual process of introduction or increase, often used in a business or technological context.|例句：They're implementing a new on-ramp for customers to adopt their cloud services. 他们正在实施一个新的渐增过程，以方便客户采用他们的云服务。

例句：The government is providing an on-ramp for small businesses to access funding. 政府正在为小型企业提供一个逐步获得资金支持的途径。|近义词：phase-in, gradual introduction, ramp-up|反义词:phase-out, gradual decrease|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"on-" + "ramp". "On" indicating direction or position, and "ramp" referring to a sloping surface. The term gained popularity with the development of highway systems in the mid-20th century.
