half-willfulness:/ˌhæfˈwɪlfəlnəs/
|n.|半任性; 半故意的; 勉强|A state of being partially or somewhat willful; a deliberate, though perhaps not fully conscious, choice to act in a particular way, often against better judgment or advice.  It implies a blend of intention and impulsiveness.|She displayed a half-willfulness in her refusal to cooperate, knowing it would only make things worse.|Near synonyms:  half-heartedness, reluctance, hesitancy|Near antonyms:  determination, resolve, wholeheartedness|noun
