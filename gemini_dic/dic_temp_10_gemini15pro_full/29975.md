caviled: /ˈkævəld/ | v. | 挑剔；吹毛求疵 | To raise trivial objections; find fault unnecessarily. | He caviled at the wording of the contract. 他对合同的措辞吹毛求疵。| The critic caviled at minor details in the painting.  评论家对画作中的细枝末节挑剔。 | 近义词：nitpick, quibble, carp, criticize | 反义词：praise, commend, approve | 词性: 动词

词源: From Old French *caviller, from Latin cavillārī (“to jest, scoff, taunt”), frequentative of cavilla (“raillery, jesting”).
