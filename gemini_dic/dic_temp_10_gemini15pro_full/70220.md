fibula:/ˈfɪbjələ/| n. |腓骨|The smaller of the two bones between the knee and the ankle. |She fractured her tibia and fibula in a skiing accident. 她滑雪时摔断了胫骨和腓骨。|Synonyms: calf bone, splint bone |Antonyms: tibia (although not a true opposite, it's the other bone in the lower leg) | noun

fibula:/ˈfɪbjʊlə/| n. |搭扣；扣针|A clasp, buckle, or brooch, especially one of ancient Roman or Greek origin, used to fasten garments. |The museum displayed an intricate gold fibula from the Roman era.  博物馆展出了一枚来自罗马时代的复杂的金扣针。|Synonyms: clasp, buckle, brooch, pin |Antonyms: None (no single direct antonym) |noun
