admonitive:/ədˈmɒnɪtɪv/
adjective
劝诫的，警告的
Serving to admonish; containing admonition.  Expressing warning or disapproval, especially in a gentle, earnest, or solicitous manner.
The teacher gave the students an admonitive lecture about the dangers of plagiarism. 老师给学生们做了一场关于剽窃危险的劝诫性讲座。

The parent's tone was admonitive, not angry.  父母的语气是劝诫的，而不是生气的。

synonym: warning, cautionary, reproving, admonishing, exhortatory
antonym: encouraging, praising, laudatory, approving
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin admonitīvus, from admonitus (past participle of admonēre ‘admonish’), from ad- ‘to’ + monēre ‘warn’.

USAGE->
The admonitive tone of the letter made her reconsider her actions. 这封信的劝诫语气使她重新考虑自己的行为。
He cast an admonitive glance at his unruly children. 他向他不守规矩的孩子们投去警告性的一瞥。
