dinothere:/ˈdaɪnəˌθɪər/ | n. | 恐兽 | An extinct mammal resembling a large elephant, but with downward-curving tusks on the lower jaw.  It lived during the Miocene and Pliocene epochs. | Dinotheres were herbivores that roamed across Eurasia and Africa. 恐兽是曾漫游于欧亚大陆和非洲的食草动物。

The dinothere's tusks might have been used for digging up roots and tubers. 恐兽的獠牙可能用于挖掘根茎和块茎。 | 近义词: Gomphothere (closely related, but different tusk arrangement) | 反义词: N/A (extinct animal, no direct opposite) | noun
