high-courts:/ˌhaɪ ˈkɔːrts/
| n. |高等法院|A court of law superior to other courts in a hierarchy.  A court with general jurisdiction in civil and criminal matters and appellate jurisdiction from lower courts.|The case will be heard in the high courts.|Synonym: superior court, appellate court|Antonym: lower court|noun
high-court:/ˌhaɪ ˈkɔːrt/
| adj. |高等法院的|Relating to or characteristic of a high court.|This is a high-court judge.|Synonym: superior, appellate|Antonym: inferior, lower|adjective
