huskier:/ˈhʌskiər/ (US), /ˈhʌskɪə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更沙哑的，更强壮的，更魁梧的|Comparative form of husky: more husky.  Means having a hoarse or rough voice, or being strongly built and powerful.|例句：His voice was huskier than usual after shouting at the game.  比赛结束后，他的声音比平时更沙哑了。

例句：The new dog was huskier than their previous pet. 新来的狗比他们以前的宠物更强壮。|近义词：hoarser, rougher, stronger, sturdier, brawnier|反义词: clearer, weaker, smaller, slighter|词性:形容词
词源:From Middle English *husk*, *hosk* (“dry outer covering of fruits and seeds”) + *-y*, from Old English *hūsc* (“husk, pod, shell”).  The original meaning referred to something dry and rough, like a husk. This evolved to describe a hoarse voice, and later a strong, sturdy build.
