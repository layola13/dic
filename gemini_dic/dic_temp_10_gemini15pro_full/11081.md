archaicism:/ˈɑːrkeɪɪzəm/| n.|古语，古体; archaism (US) /ˈɑːrkeɪˌɪzəm/|
|A word or phrase that is no longer in common use but is sometimes used to give a sense of old-fashioned style.|
|The author's use of "thou" and "thee" are archaisms.|
|Synonym: antiquated word, obsolete word, old-fashioned word|
|Antonym: modernism, neologism|
|noun|

archaic:/ɑːrˈkeɪɪk/| adj.|古老的; archaic (US) /ɑːrˈkeɪɪk/|
|Very old or old-fashioned.|
|The museum houses an archaic collection of pottery.|
|Synonym: ancient, antiquated, obsolete, outdated|
|Antonym: modern, contemporary, new|
|adjective|
