low-churchism:/ˌloʊˈtʃɜːrtʃɪzəm/
|n.|低教会主义|A form of Anglicanism characterized by a reduced emphasis on ritual, sacraments, and clerical authority, and a greater emphasis on evangelical preaching and personal piety.|The vicar's low-churchism was reflected in the simplicity of the services.|Synonyms: evangelicalism, Low Church|Antonyms: high-churchism, High Church|noun
