ferrocalcite:/ˌfɛroʊˈkælˌsaɪt/| n. |铁方解石|A variety of calcite containing ferrous iron, often resulting in a yellowish or brownish color.|The cave formations were primarily composed of ferrocalcite, giving them a warm, honey-like hue.|近义词:calcite, siderite |反义词: N/A |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "ferro-" (iron, referring to the presence of ferrous iron) + "calcite" (a common carbonate mineral).
