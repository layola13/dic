inclusivist:/ɪnˈkluːsɪvɪst/
n.|包容主义者；兼收并蓄者|Someone who believes in inclusivism, a philosophy or policy of inclusiveness.  This often refers to religious or social contexts, where an inclusivist aims to include diverse groups or perspectives.|The theologian is known for his inclusivist views, believing that salvation is available to people of all faiths.|近义词：pluralist, liberal, tolerant|反义词：exclusivist, sectarian, bigoted|词性: 名词

inclusivist:/ɪnˈkluːsɪvɪst/
adj.|包容的；兼收并蓄的|Characterized by or promoting inclusivism; aiming to include a wide range of people, viewpoints, or things.|The university adopted an inclusivist approach to admissions, considering students from diverse backgrounds.|近义词：comprehensive, all-embracing, broad-minded|反义词：exclusive, restrictive, narrow-minded|词性: 形容词

词源:  From "inclusive" + "-ist".  "Inclusive" comes from the Latin "includere" (to shut in, enclose), from "in-" (in) + "claudere" (to shut).  The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or belief.
