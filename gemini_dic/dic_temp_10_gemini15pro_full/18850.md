beseech:/bɪˈsiːtʃ/| vt.|恳求，哀求|To ask (someone) urgently and fervently to do something; implore; beg.|She beseeched him to stay. 她恳求他留下。|  The villagers beseeched the king for help. 村民恳求国王的帮助。|Synonyms: implore, beg, plead, entreat, supplicate|Antonyms: demand, command|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bisechen*, from Old English *besēcan* (“to seek, beseech, implore, beg, ask”), equivalent to *be-* + *sēcan* (“to seek”). Cognate with Dutch *bezoeken* (“to visit”), German *besuchen* (“to visit”).  The original sense of "seek after, visit" was lost in English.
