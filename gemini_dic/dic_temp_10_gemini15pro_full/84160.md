half-playfully:/ˌhɑːfˈpleɪfəli/
| adv. | 半开玩笑地 |  In a way that is partly serious and partly not serious, often to make something seem less important or to avoid causing offense. | He half-playfully suggested they elope.  他半开玩笑地建议他们私奔。| Synonyms:  partly jokingly,  not entirely seriously,  semi-seriously | Antonyms: seriously, earnestly
Etymology-> From "half" + "playfully". "Half" indicates a partial state, and "playfully" refers to a lighthearted and not entirely serious manner.
