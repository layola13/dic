contrivedly:/kənˈtraɪvɪdli/
| adv. |人为地，不自然地|  In a way that seems artificial and deliberately created rather than arising naturally or spontaneously. | Her smile seemed contrivedly cheerful, as if she was forcing it. 她笑起来很不自然，好像是在强颜欢笑。| synonyms: artificially, unnaturally, forcedly, affectedly, elaborately, studiedly | antonyms: naturally, spontaneously, genuinely, authentically | adverb

contrived:/kənˈtraɪvd/
| adj. |人为的，不自然的|  Seeming artificial and deliberately created rather than arising naturally or spontaneously. | The plot of the movie felt contrived and unrealistic. 这部电影的情节让人觉得做作且不现实。| synonyms: artificial, unnatural, forced, affected, elaborate, studied | antonyms: natural, spontaneous, genuine, authentic | adjective

contrive:/kənˈtraɪv/
| verb |设计；发明；图谋；设法做到| Create or bring about (something) by deliberate use of skill and artifice.  Manage to do something foolish or create an undesirable situation. |  They contrived a plan to escape. 他们精心策划了一个逃跑计划。 He somehow contrived to lose his passport. 他不知怎么地把护照弄丢了。 | synonyms: devise, invent, concoct, fabricate, engineer, scheme, plot, plan | antonyms:  happen, occur (for unintentional events) | verb
