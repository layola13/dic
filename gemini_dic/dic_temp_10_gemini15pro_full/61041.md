elixirate:/ɪˈlɪksəreɪt/ | v. | 使长生不老；使充满活力 | To renew or invigorate as if with an elixir. |  She felt elixirated after a week in the mountains.  她在山里待了一周后感到精神焕发。|  rejuvenate, revitalize, invigorate | debilitate, weaken | verb

elixirate:/ɪˈlɪksəreɪt/ | n. |  长生不老药；使人充满活力的东西 |  A substance or experience that renews or invigorates. | The mountain air was a true elixirate for her weary soul. 山里的空气对她疲惫的灵魂来说是真正的活力剂。 | tonic, restorative, cure-all | poison, toxin | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From elixir + -ate.  "Elixir" comes from Medieval Latin *elixir, from Arabic al-ʾiksīr "the philosopher's stone," from Greek xērion "desiccative powder for wounds," from xēros "dry."
