melanocytes: /ˈmɛlənəˌsaɪts/ | n. | 黑色素细胞 | A mature melanin-forming cell, typically located in the skin.  These cells are responsible for producing melanin, the pigment that gives skin its color and protects it from the harmful effects of ultraviolet radiation. |  Melanocytes produce melanin which protects the skin from sun damage. 黑色素细胞产生黑色素，保护皮肤免受阳光伤害。 |  Melanocytes are found in the basal layer of the epidermis. 黑色素细胞位于表皮的基底层。 | synonyms: pigment cell | antonyms: N/A | noun
