adulators:/ˈædjʊˌleɪtərz/| n.|奉承者，谄媚者|People who praise someone excessively and insincerely, often to gain an advantage.  They flatter and fawn over the object of their admiration.|The king was surrounded by adulators who constantly praised his every decision. 国王身边围绕着阿谀奉承的人，他们不断地赞扬他的每一个决定。

The successful entrepreneur had many adulators who hoped to gain favor.  这位成功的企业家有很多谄媚者，他们希望得到他的青睐。|近义词：flatterers, sycophants, yes-men|反义词:critics, detractors|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *adulator*, from Latin adulat-, adulari "to fawn upon," from ad- "to" + ululo "howl, wail" (perhaps originally in a ritual or worshipful sense). The sense evolution is from "fawning like a dog" to "flattering."
