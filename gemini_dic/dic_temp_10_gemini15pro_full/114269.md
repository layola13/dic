main-liners:/ˈmeɪnˌlaɪnərz/
n.| (复数)主要人物；主流人士；吸毒者(尤指静脉注射毒品者)|People who are considered to be part of the established, conventional, or dominant group, especially in a particular field or activity. Also, a person who injects drugs intravenously. |The main-liners in the art world dismissed his work as amateurish. 艺术界的主流人士认为他的作品不成熟。|Synonyms: traditionalists, conservatives, conformists, establishment figures (for the first meaning); junkies, addicts (for the second meaning) |Antonyms: radicals, rebels, nonconformists, outsiders (for the first meaning)|noun
