outguard:/ˈaʊtɡɑːrd/
noun
前哨；外卫；警戒哨
A soldier or party of soldiers stationed at some distance from the main body of troops, as a guard against surprise.
The outguard spotted the enemy approaching. 前哨发现了敌人正在靠近。
synonyms: picket, sentinel, sentry, watch
antonyms: main force, rearguard
n.

outguard:/ˈaʊtɡɑːrd/
verb
担任前哨；警戒
To station troops as an outguard.
They outguarded the camp against night attacks. 他们担任营地的前哨以防夜袭。
synonyms: guard, patrol, protect, secure
antonyms: expose, endanger
v.
