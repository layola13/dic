footbinding: /ˈfʊtˌbaɪndɪŋ/ | n. | 缠足 | The traditional practice of tightly binding the feet of young girls to prevent further growth, considered a mark of beauty and status in some parts of China. | Footbinding was a painful and debilitating practice. 缠足是一种痛苦且使人衰弱的做法。| Footbinding was prevalent in China for centuries. 缠足在中国盛行了几个世纪。 |  Synonyms: foot-binding | Antonyms: natural foot growth | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From foot + binding.  The practice originated in China during the Song Dynasty (960-1279).
