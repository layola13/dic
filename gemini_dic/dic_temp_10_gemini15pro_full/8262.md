anti-integration:/ˌæntiˌɪntɪˈɡreɪʃən/
| adj. | 反融合的，反一体化的 | Opposing or resisting integration, especially racial or social integration. |  The politician's anti-integration stance cost him the election.  这位政治家的反融合立场让他输掉了选举。 |
| Example 1: Anti-integration policies were common in the American South during the Jim Crow era.  在美国吉姆·克劳时代，反融合政策很普遍。|
| Example 2:  There has been a resurgence of anti-integration sentiment in some parts of the world.  在世界某些地区，反融合情绪再度抬头。|
| Synonyms: segregationist, discriminatory, exclusionary | Antonyms: integrationist, inclusive,  |
| Word Origin: anti- (prefix meaning "against") + integration (from Latin integratio, meaning "making whole") |
