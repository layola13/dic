coifed:/kwɑːft/ | adj. |  梳理好的，做好了发型的 | Having one's hair styled or arranged, especially elaborately. |  She arrived at the gala perfectly coifed. 她盛装打扮，发型完美地出现在晚会上。 |  styled, arranged, groomed | unkempt, messy | adjective

coif:/kwɔɪf/ | n. |  头巾，发网；(尤指女子的)发型 | A close-fitting cap, now only worn by nuns or sometimes by serjeants-at-law; a hairstyle. | The nun's coif framed her face serenely.  修女的头巾衬托出她平静的脸庞。 | headdress, hairstyle |  -- | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *coife, coife, coiffe ("cap"), from Late Latin cofia, from Frankish *kufia ("cap"). Related to Old High German chuph ("hood").  The verb "to coif" is a back-formation from the noun.
