hoarsely:/ˈhɔːrsli/ (US), /ˈhɔːsli/ (UK)| adv.|嘶哑地；粗声地|In a hoarse voice; roughly.  Describes the manner of speaking when someone's voice is rough or harsh, often due to a sore throat or overuse.|He spoke hoarsely, his voice raspy from shouting all day. 他说话声音嘶哑，因为整天都在喊叫。|近义词：roughly, huskily, gratingly|反义词：clearly, smoothly, melodiously|词性:副词
词源: From Middle English *hors, from Old English hārs (“hoarse”), from Proto-Germanic *harzaz (“hoarse”).
