backtracked:/ˌbækˈtrækt/| v.|回溯，折返，撤回，放弃（原先立场）|To go back along a path or route that you have just followed. To withdraw or reverse a previous decision or action. |例句：We had to backtrack about a mile to find the turnoff. 我们不得不折返大约一英里才能找到岔路口。|近义词：retreat, withdraw, reverse, retract|反义词：advance, proceed, continue|词性：动词

Etymology:
From back + track.  The verb "track" originally meant "to travel, follow a course." Adding "back" to it creates the sense of reversing or returning along that course.
