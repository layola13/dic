carpetbagger: /ˈkɑːrpɪtˌbæɡər/ (US) /ˈkɑːpɪtˌbæɡə(r)/ (UK)| n. | 投机政客；外来者；背包客 | A person from the northern United States who went to the South after the Civil War to profit from the Reconstruction.  Originally, it referred to Northerners who carried their belongings in cheap suitcases made of carpet fabric. |  Example 1: Many carpetbaggers came to the South seeking political office and economic opportunities after the war.  许多投机政客在战后到南方寻找政治职位和经济机会。

Example 2: The locals resented the carpetbaggers, seeing them as opportunists exploiting their misfortune. 当地人憎恨这些外来者，视他们为利用自己不幸的投机分子。 | Synonym: opportunist, exploiter, outsider | Antonym: native, local, resident | Noun
