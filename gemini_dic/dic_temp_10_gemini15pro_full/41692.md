corrie:/ˈkɔːri/ (UK), /ˈkɔːri/ (US)| n.|山间小谷，圈谷|A deep, rounded, bowl-shaped hollow on a mountainside, often containing a small lake or tarn, formed by glacial erosion.|例句：The hikers rested by the corrie, admiring the view.  徒步旅行者在山间小谷旁休息，欣赏着景色。

例句：The corrie was filled with wildflowers in the spring. 春天，山间小谷里开满了野花。|近义词：cirque, cwm|反义词:peak, ridge|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Scottish Gaelic *coire* "cauldron, kettle," from Old Irish *coire*, from Proto-Celtic *kʷaria-*, from Proto-Indo-European *kʷer- "to turn, bend." The word refers to the rounded shape of the hollow.
