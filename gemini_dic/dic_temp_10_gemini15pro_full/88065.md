highlanders:/ˈhaɪləndərz/| n.| 高地人（尤指苏格兰高地人）|People who live in the highlands, especially those of Scotland, known for their distinctive culture and often associated with clans. |The highlanders were renowned for their fierce independence.  高地人以其强烈的独立性而闻名。|The pipers played a stirring tune, and the highlanders began to dance.  风笛手演奏了一首激动人心的曲子，高地人开始跳舞。|近义词：mountain dwellers, hill people|反义词：lowlanders|词性：名词

词源:
From "highland" + "-er" (denoting a person associated with a place). "Highland" comes from Old English "heah" (high) + "land" (land).
