cormous:/ˈkɔːrməs/| adj.|球茎状的；具有球茎的|Having a corm; bulb-like.  A corm is a short, vertical, swollen underground plant stem that serves as a storage organ that some plants use to survive winter or other adverse conditions such as summer drought and heat. |Crocuses and gladioli are cormous plants.  番红花和唐菖蒲都是球茎植物。|bulbous, tuberous|N/A|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cormosus, from cormus (“corm”) + -osus (suffix forming adjectives meaning 'full of', 'abounding in', 'prone to', 'like', 'having the quality of', 'characterized by').
