humour:/ˈhjuːmə(r)/ (UK) /ˈhjuːmər/ (US)| n. |幽默，诙谐；心情，情绪|The ability to be amused or to amuse others; the quality of being funny.  A mood or state of mind.|
He has a good sense of humour. 他很有幽默感。

Her humour was unpredictable. 她的情绪捉摸不定。

近义词:wit, fun, mood, temper
反义词:seriousness, gloom

词性:noun

词源:From Middle French humour, from Old French humor, from Latin humor "moisture, fluid," also "mental disposition," from PIE *ugro- "wet, moist." Originally referring to the four bodily humors (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile), which were believed to determine a person's temperament. The sense of "amusing quality" developed later.
