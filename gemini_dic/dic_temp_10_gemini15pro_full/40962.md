cooperatives:/koʊˈɑːpərətɪvz/| n.|合作社，合作组织|A business or organization owned and operated by the people who use its services or by the people who work there.  A cooperative is based on the principles of self-help, democracy, and social responsibility.|例句：Agricultural cooperatives help farmers market their crops.  农业合作社帮助农民销售他们的农作物。

|例句：The credit union is a type of financial cooperative. 信用合作社是一种金融合作社。|近义词：collective, partnership, alliance|反义词: corporation, conglomerate|词性:noun


co-operative:/koʊˈɑːpərətɪv/| adj.|合作的，协作的|Involving mutual assistance in working toward a common goal.|例句：The project required a co-operative effort from several departments.  这个项目需要几个部门的合作。

|例句：She has a co-operative attitude and is always willing to help. 她有合作的态度，并且总是乐于助人。|近义词：collaborative, joint, shared|反义词: uncooperative, obstructive|词性:adjective


Etymology: From the Latin word "cooperari," meaning "to work together," from "co-" (together) and "operari" (to work).
