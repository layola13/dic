outbluffs:/ˌaʊtˈblʌfs/
verb
以虚张声势取胜; 比…更会虚张声势
to surpass someone in bluffing; to bluff more effectively than another.
He outbluffed his opponent in the poker game and won the pot. 他在扑克游戏中以虚张声势战胜了对手，赢得了彩池。
近义词: outsmart, outwit
反义词: concede, yield
词性: verb

词源:  A combination of the prefix "out-" (meaning "to surpass or exceed") and the verb "bluff."  "Bluff" itself likely originates from the Dutch word "bluffen," meaning "to boast" or "to brag."
