lividly:/ˈlɪvɪdli/| adv.|愤怒地，青灰地，铁青地|In a livid manner, exhibiting a discolored, bluish, or grayish appearance often associated with anger, bruise, or illness.  It can also describe something done with intense anger or force.|He stared lividly at the man who had insulted him. 他愤怒地瞪着那个侮辱他的人。|近义词：furiously, angrily, enragedly|反义词：calmly, peacefully|词性:副词

词源: From the Latin word "lividus," meaning "bluish," "black and blue," or "envious."
