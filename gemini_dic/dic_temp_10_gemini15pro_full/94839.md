imputativeness:/ɪmˈpjuːtətɪvnəs/| n. |可归罪性，可归因性|The quality or state of being imputable; the fact that something can be attributed to a particular cause or source.  It refers to the ability to ascribe responsibility or blame for an action or event to a particular individual or entity.|The imputativeness of the crime to the defendant was questioned by his lawyer. 律师质疑被告对该罪行的可归罪性。|Near Synonyms:  attributability, ascribability, accountability, responsibility|Antonyms:  non-attributability, innocence, exculpation|noun
