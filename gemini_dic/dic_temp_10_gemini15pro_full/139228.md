oblate:/ˈɒbleɪt/ (US) /ˈɒbleɪt/ (UK)| adj. n.| 扁圆的；扁球形的；献身教会的(人)；|
Flattened at the poles (as a planet).  Used to describe something that is flattened or compressed, especially at the poles, like the Earth.  Can also refer to a person dedicated to religious life, especially under a vow of obedience. |
The Earth is an oblate spheroid. 地球是一个扁球体。

The oblate monk devoted his life to prayer.  这位献身教会的僧侣一生致力于祈祷。


近义词：flattened, compressed, spherical, dedicated, devoted|反义词:prolate, elongated, secular|词性:adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *oblātus*, past participle of *oblāre*  "to flatten," from *ob-*  "against, toward" + *lātus* "broad, wide."
