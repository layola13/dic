hawkishly:/ˈhɔːkɪʃli/ | adv. | 鹰派地；强硬地 | In a manner resembling a hawk; aggressively, especially in advocating war or other strong measures. |  The senator hawkishly advocated for increased military spending.  参议员强硬地主张增加军费开支。 |例句：He hawkishly defended the decision to invade. 他强硬地为入侵的决定辩护。| 近义词：aggressively, belligerently, fiercely | 反义词：dovishly, peacefully, conciliatorily |词性:副词

hawkish:/ˈhɔːkɪʃ/ | adj. | 鹰派的；强硬的 | Advocating an aggressive or warlike policy, especially in foreign affairs. | The hawkish stance of the new administration worried many citizens. 新政府的强硬立场让很多公民感到担忧。 |例句：His hawkish views on foreign policy are well known. 他在外交政策上的强硬观点众所周知。| 近义词：aggressive, bellicose, militant | 反义词：dovish, peaceful, conciliatory |词性:形容词
