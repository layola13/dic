give free rein to:/ɡɪv friː reɪn tuː/
|v.|放任；纵容；给…自由发挥|to allow someone or something complete freedom of action or expression|The boss gave free rein to the design team to develop the new product. 老板让设计团队自由开发新产品。|Synonyms:  indulge, allow, permit, unleash, unbridle|Antonyms: restrict, restrain, curb, control, limit|verb phrase

give free rein to:/ɡɪv friː reɪn tuː/
|v.|放任自流|to allow someone or something to do whatever they want|She gave free rein to her emotions and started crying. 她放任自己的情绪，开始哭泣。|Synonyms:  let loose, give way to, abandon oneself to|Antonyms:  control, suppress, repress|verb phrase


ETYMOLOGY->
"give free rein to" originates from horseback riding.  "Rein" refers to the straps used to control a horse.  "Giving free rein" meant loosening the reins, allowing the horse to move freely. This equestrian meaning was then extended metaphorically to mean giving someone or something freedom to act or develop without restraint.
