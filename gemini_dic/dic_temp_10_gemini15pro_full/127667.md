nef:/nef/ | n. | 教堂中殿；（尤指中世纪大教堂的）交叉甬道；（教堂中殿式）船身 | The central part of a church building, extending from the narthex or main entrance to the transepts (or, in the absence of transepts, to the chancel), where the congregation is seated. Also, a medieval ship built to resemble the nave of a church. | The high vaulted nef of the cathedral was breathtaking.  大教堂高耸的拱形中殿令人叹为观止。 | synonyms: aisle, nave | antonyms:  N/A (architectural term, lacks a direct opposite) | noun

nef:/nef/ | n. | (古语) 船 |  An archaic term for a ship. |  The nef sailed into the harbor.  这艘船驶入港口。 | synonyms: ship, vessel | antonyms:  N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *nef*, from Latin *nāvis* "ship," perhaps because the shape of a church nave resembled an upturned ship's hull.
