overexaggerating:/ˌoʊvərɪɡˈzædʒəreɪtɪŋ/
| v. | 过分夸大；夸张 |  Exaggerating something to an excessive degree. | He was overexaggerating his injuries to get sympathy. 他为了博取同情而夸大了自己的伤势。| synonyms: overstating, embellishing, amplifying | antonyms: downplaying, understating | verb (present participle)

overexaggerate:/ˌoʊvərɪɡˈzædʒəreɪt/
| v. | 过分夸大；夸张 | To exaggerate something to an excessive degree. | She tends to overexaggerate when telling stories. 她讲故事时往往会夸大其词。| synonyms: overstate, embellish, amplify | antonyms: downplay, understate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + exaggerate.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much." "Exaggerate" comes from the Latin "exaggeratus," past participle of "exaggerare," meaning "to heap up, increase."
