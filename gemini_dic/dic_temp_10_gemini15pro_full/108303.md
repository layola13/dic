laurelling: /ˈlɒrəlɪŋ/ (UK), /ˈlɔːrəlɪŋ/ (US) | n. | 授予桂冠；荣誉；表彰 | The act of bestowing an award or honor, especially a laurel wreath, as a symbol of victory or distinction.  It can also refer to the honors themselves. |  The laurelling of the Olympic champion was a moving ceremony. 奥运冠军的授予桂冠仪式令人感动。| synonyms: crowning, honoring, celebrating, acclaiming | antonyms:  condemning, criticizing, denouncing | noun

laurelled: /ˈlɒrəld/ (UK), /ˈlɔːrəld/ (US) | adj. |  戴桂冠的；享有声誉的 | Crowned with laurel; honored or celebrated for achievement. | The laurelled poet accepted the award graciously.  这位享有盛誉的诗人优雅地接受了奖项。 | synonyms: honored, celebrated, acclaimed, distinguished | antonyms:  unknown, undistinguished, obscure | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "laurel" + "-ing" or "-ed". "Laurel" comes from Old French "laurel", from Latin "laurus".  The laurel wreath was a symbol of victory and distinction in ancient Greece and Rome.
