ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bān (attested in compounds), from Proto-Germanic *bainą (“bone”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ost- (“bone”). Cognate with Old Frisian bān, Old Saxon bēn, Old High German bein, Old Norse bein, Gothic 𐌱𐌰𐌹𐌽 (bain).
