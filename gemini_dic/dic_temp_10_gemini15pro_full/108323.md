laurus: /ˈlɔːrəs/ | n. | 月桂树；荣誉；胜利 |  An evergreen shrub or small tree (Laurus nobilis) of the laurel family, native to the Mediterranean region, having dark green, glossy leaves and small, yellowish flowers. Its aromatic leaves are used in cookery and were formerly used to make laurel wreaths for victors.  The word "laurus" itself directly comes from the Latin word "laurus," meaning "laurel tree."  From Proto-Italic *dāwros, from Proto-Indo-European *dóh₁wros (“tree”).| The chef added a laurus leaf to the stew.  厨师在炖菜里加了一片月桂叶。 |  bay laurel, bay tree, laurel | antonym: N/A (No direct opposite for a plant) | noun
