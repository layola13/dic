counterpowers:/ˈkaʊntərˌpaʊərz/| n. |制衡力量，反制力量|A force or influence that balances or offsets another power, especially in politics or international relations.|The smaller nations formed a coalition of counterpowers to the larger, more aggressive states.|近义词：counterbalance, offset, check, restraint|反义词:power, dominance, hegemony|词性:名词

Etymology:
From counter- + power.  "Counter-" acts as a prefix meaning "against" or "opposite." "Power" comes from Old French "poer"  (modern French "pouvoir") meaning "to be able," ultimately from Latin "potere" meaning "to be able."
