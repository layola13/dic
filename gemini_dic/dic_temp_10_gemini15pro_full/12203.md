as-were:/æz-wɜːr/
| adv. |所谓的；仿佛；好像| Used to describe something that appears to be the case but may not be true in reality. |例句：He treated her like a queen, as it were. 他待她如女王一般，或者说像是女王。|例句：They lived, as it were, in a world of their own.  他们仿佛生活在自己的世界里。|近义词：so to speak, in a way, seemingly, apparently|反义词: truly, actually|词性:副词

词源:
From the phrase "as it were," which itself originates from the Middle English phrase "als it were," meaning "as if it were."  The "it" refers to the previously mentioned situation or description. Over time, the shortened form "as-were" emerged, particularly in informal contexts.
