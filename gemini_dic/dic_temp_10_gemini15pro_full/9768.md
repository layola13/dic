antonomasia:/ˌæntənəˈmeɪʒə/ | n. |换称，代称|Substitution of a title, epithet, or descriptive phrase for a proper name, or of a proper name for a common noun.  The use of a proper name to designate a general idea or characteristic associated with the name's bearer. |例句：He’s a real Scrooge. 他真是个吝啬鬼。(Scrooge 代指吝啬的人)

例句："The Bard" for Shakespeare is an example of antonomasia.  用“诗圣”指代莎士比亚是换称的一个例子。|近义词：metonymy, appellation, sobriquet |反义词: N/A |词性:noun
