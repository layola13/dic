ossify:/ˈɒsɪfaɪ/ (British), /ˈɑːsəfaɪ/ (American)| v. |骨化；僵化；墨守成规|To become bone or bony; to harden or become rigid, inflexible, or fixed, like bone.|例句：The cartilage will ossify over time.  软骨会随着时间推移而骨化。

例句：His political views have ossified over the years.  他的政治观点多年来已经僵化。|近义词：harden, solidify, rigidify, fossilize|反义词:soften, flexiblize|词性:动词

ossifying:/ˈɒsɪfaɪɪŋ/ (British), /ˈɑːsəfaɪɪŋ/ (American)| adj. |骨化的；僵化的；使僵化的|Causing or tending to cause ossification; becoming or making something inflexible.|例句：The ossifying effects of tradition can stifle innovation.  传统的僵化效应会扼杀创新。

例句：He has an ossifying belief in his own infallibility. 他对自己一贯正确的僵化信念。|近义词：hardening, solidifying, rigidifying|反义词:softening, flexiblizing|词性:形容词
