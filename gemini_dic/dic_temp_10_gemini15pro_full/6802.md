anaptotic:/ˌænəˈpɒtɪk/
adjective
变格不完全的; 无变格的
Relating to or characterized by a lack of inflection or case endings, especially in grammar.  A language or grammatical element that does not use inflection to express grammatical relationships.
The anaptotic nature of the language made it difficult to determine the grammatical function of words.  这种语言的无变格特性使得很难确定单词的语法功能。
A few anaptotic languages exist, relying on word order instead of inflections. 一些无变格语言存在，它们依靠语序而不是屈折变化。
近义词: uninflected, indeclinable
反义词: inflected, declinable
词性: adjective

词源: From Greek *ἀνάπτωτος (anáptōtos) ‘uninflected’, from ἀν- (an-) ‘not’ + πτωτός (ptōtós) ‘fallen’, verbal adjective of πίπτω (píptō) ‘to fall’.
