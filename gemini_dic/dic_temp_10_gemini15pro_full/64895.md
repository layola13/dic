essene:/ˈɛsiːn/| n.|艾赛尼人，艾赛尼派信徒|A member of a Jewish sect or brotherhood that flourished in Palestine from the 2nd century BC to the 2nd century AD. They lived a communal life, practiced asceticism, and held apocalyptic views.|The Essenes lived a simple life, separated from the world.|近义词：sectarian, ascetic|反义词:|名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin Esseni, from Greek Essēnoi, perhaps related to Aramaic ḥasayya "pious".
