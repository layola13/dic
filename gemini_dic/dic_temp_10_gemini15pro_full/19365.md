bibliophile:/ˈbɪbliəfaɪl/ | n. | 藏书家；爱书的人 | A person who collects or has a great love of books. | He was a renowned bibliophile, with a library containing thousands of first editions. 他是一位著名的藏书家，他的图书馆藏有数千本初版书籍。 |  book collector, book lover, bibliomaniac | book hater | noun

bibliomania:/ˌbɪbliəˈmeɪniə/ | n. | 藏书癖； bibliomania | An excessive fondness for acquiring and possessing books. |  His bibliomania led him to spend his entire fortune on rare books.  他的藏书癖使他把全部财产都花在了珍本书上。| book collecting addiction, bibliophilism | indifference to books | noun

bibliotaphist:/ˌbɪbliəˈtæfɪst/ | n. | 书籍收藏家(尤指珍本及善本);藏书家(尤指将藏书视为珍宝者，甚于阅读者);书虫 | A person who collects and hoards books, often rare or valuable ones, more for the sake of possessing them than for reading them. | The bibliotaphist kept his prized first editions locked away, rarely allowing anyone to even see them.  这位藏书家将他珍贵的初版书籍锁起来，很少允许任何人看到它们。| book collector, book hoarder, bibliophile | book hater, non-reader | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek biblion "book" + taphos "tomb, burial."  The word suggests someone who "buries" or hides away books.
