displacements:/dɪsˈpleɪsmənts/
n.|位移，取代，排水量|the moving of something from its place or position.  The act of replacing someone or something.|例句：The earthquake caused large displacements of the ground. 地震造成了地面的大位移。|近义词：shifts, movements, substitutions, removal|反义词:placements, insertions|词性:名词

displace:/dɪsˈpleɪs/
v.|取代，移置，撤换|to force something or someone out of its usual or original position.|例句：The rising floodwaters displaced thousands of people. 上涨的洪水使数千人流离失所。|近义词：replace, remove, supplant|反义词:place, insert|词性:动词

词源: From Late Latin *displaciare, from Latin dis- + placere "to please".  The original sense was "to displease," later evolving to "move from a place."
