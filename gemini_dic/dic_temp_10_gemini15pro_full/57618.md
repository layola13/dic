draughted:/ˈdræftɪd/ /ˈdrɑːftɪd/
| v. |起草；征召；选拔|To select or conscript someone for a particular purpose, especially military service; to prepare a preliminary version of a document.|He was draughted into the army at the age of 18. 他18岁时被征召入伍。

The lawyer draughted a new contract. 律师起草了一份新合同。
|近义词：conscripted, drafted, recruited, selected, chosen, composed, formulated, prepared|反义词：rejected, discharged, dismissed|词性：动词

draught:/drɑːft/ /dræft/
| n. |草稿；通风； draught beer 生啤|A current of cool air in a room or other enclosed space; a preliminary version of a piece of writing; a single act of drinking or swallowing; the depth of water needed for a ship to float.|Please close the window, there's a terrible draught. 请关上窗户，有股可怕的穿堂风。

The first draught of the novel was completed in three months. 小说的初稿三个月就完成了。

She took a long draught of beer. 她喝了一大口啤酒。

The ship had a draught of 10 meters. 这艘船吃水10米。
|近义词：draft, breeze, current, sketch, outline, gulp, swig|反义词：stuffiness, stillness|词性：名词
