conquered:/ˈkɒŋkəd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːŋkərd/ (US)| v. |征服，战胜，克服|defeated and took control of (a place or people) by military force.  Overcome a feeling or condition.|例句：The Romans conquered much of Europe. 罗马人征服了欧洲的大部分地区。|例句：She finally conquered her fear of flying. 她终于克服了对飞行的恐惧。|近义词：vanquished, defeated, overcame, subdued|反义词：surrendered, yielded, lost, failed|词性: 动词

conquered:/ˈkɒŋkəd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːŋkərd/ (US)| adj. |被征服的，克服了的|Having been defeated and taken control of by military force.  Having overcome a feeling or condition.|例句：The conquered territories were ruled by a governor. 被征服的领土由总督统治。 |例句：With a conquered sigh, she admitted defeat. 她叹了口气，承认失败。|近义词：defeated, vanquished, overcome, subdued|反义词：victorious, unconquered, undefeated|词性: 形容词


词源: From Old French *conquerre*, from Latin *conquirere* (“to seek together, acquire, gain, win”), from *com-* (“together”) + *quaerere* (“to seek”).
