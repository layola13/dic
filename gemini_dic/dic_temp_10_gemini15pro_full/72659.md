fo'c'sle: /ˈfoʊksəl/ (also /ˈfɔːksəl/) | n. | 前甲板；水手舱 | The forecastle is the part of a ship located at the bow, traditionally used as the crew's living quarters. | The sailors gathered on the foc'sle to watch the sunset. 水手们聚集在前甲板上看日落。 | Synonym:foredeck | Antonym:quarterdeck | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *forcastel, from Old French forcastel, from fors "outside, beyond" + castel "castle, fortress". Originally referred to a raised structure at the bow of a ship, resembling a small castle, used for defense. Over time, the term came to refer to the entire forward part of a ship below the deck, where the crew's quarters were located.
