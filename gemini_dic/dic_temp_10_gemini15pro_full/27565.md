canalised:/ˈkænəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 管状的；河道化的；被引导的 |  Restricted to a narrow course or range of action, like water flowing through a canal.  The word often carries a connotation of being artificially or forcibly constrained. | The government's economic policy has been canalized into a few key areas. 政府的经济政策已被引导到几个关键领域。 | The river was canalized to prevent flooding. 这条河被改造成运河以防止洪水泛滥。 |  channeled, directed, restricted | unrestricted, open, broad | adjective

canalised:/ˈkænəlaɪzd/ | v. | 引导；使成管状；使成河道 | To make something flow through a canal or channel, either literally or figuratively. To restrict or direct something into a narrow course. |  The company's efforts were canalized into developing a new product. 公司的努力被引导到开发新产品上。 |  Engineers canalized the river to make it navigable. 工程师们将河流改造成运河，使其可以通航。 | channel, direct, funnel, restrict |  diversify, broaden, expand | verb
