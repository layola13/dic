elitists:/ɪˈliːtɪsts/| n.|精英主义者；优越感强的人|People who believe that a society or system should be led by an elite group, often holding the view that they are superior to others.|The elitists in the art world often dismiss popular artists. 艺术界的精英主义者常常瞧不起大众艺术家。|近义词：snobs, highbrows|反义词：populists, egalitarians|词性:noun

elitist:/ɪˈliːtɪst/| adj.|精英主义的；优越感强的|Relating to or supporting the belief that a society or system should be led by an elite group, often implying a sense of superiority.|The elitist attitude of some academics can alienate students. 一些学者高高在上的精英主义态度会疏远学生。|近义词：snobbish, exclusive|反义词：inclusive, egalitarian|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *élite* (elite), from Latin *eligere* (to choose, elect), past participle *electus*. The suffix *-ist* denotes a person who adheres to a particular belief or system.
