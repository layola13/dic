paper-shufflers:/ˈpeɪpər ˌʃʌflərz/
|n.|  文牍主义者； paperwork bureaucracy;  (disapproving) someone who works in an office and deals with documents or administration, often used to suggest that they are not doing useful or important work|
|Those paper-shufflers in government waste so much time.|那些政府里的文牍主义者浪费了那么多时间。|
|The office was full of paper-shufflers who seemed to do nothing but file documents all day.|办公室里满是文牍主义者，他们似乎整天除了整理文件什么也不做。|
|bureaucrats, administrators, clerks|
|doers, action-takers|
|noun|
