fluxionary:/ˈflʌkʃənˌɛri/
adjective
流动的；易变的；不稳定的
Subject to constant change or fluctuation. Characterized by flux.
例句：The fluxionary nature of the stock market makes it a risky investment. 股票市场的波动性使其成为一项高风险投资。

例句：Fluxionary political alliances make it difficult to predict the outcome of the election.  变化无常的政治联盟使得难以预测选举结果。

近义词：changeable, variable, volatile, unstable, fluid, shifting, dynamic
反义词：stable, constant, fixed, static, unchanging
词性:adjective

fluxion:/ˈflʌkʃən/
noun
流；流动；变迁；涨落
The act of flowing; continuous change or fluctuation.
例句：The fluxion of the tide affects the movement of ships. 潮汐的涨落影响船舶的航行。

例句：The constant fluxion of ideas in academia keeps the field vibrant. 学术界思想的不断变化使该领域保持活力。

近义词：flow, change, fluctuation, variation, shift
反义词：stability, constancy
词性:noun



词源: From Latin *fluxio*, from fluxus (“a flowing”), past participle of fluere (“to flow”).
