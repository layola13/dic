go-around:/ˈɡoʊ əˌraʊnd/ (US), /ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd/ (UK)| n.| 复飞；绕行；争论；迂回|A maneuver in which an aircraft about to land aborts the landing and climbs back up in order to circle around and try again. Also used metaphorically for any kind of second attempt.|The pilot had to execute a go-around because another plane was still on the runway. 飞行员不得不复飞，因为另一架飞机还在跑道上。|synonyms: second attempt, retry, do-over, another try|antonyms:  completion, finalization|noun

go-around:/ˈɡoʊ əˌraʊnd/ (US), /ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd/ (UK)| n.| 争吵，争论|A disagreement or argument.|We had a bit of a go-around about the budget. 我们就预算问题进行了一番争论。|synonyms: argument, quarrel, dispute, disagreement|antonyms: agreement, harmony, accord|noun

go-around:/ˈɡoʊ əˌraʊnd/ (US), /ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd/ (UK)| n.| 传播，流传|Something that is passed from person to person, such as a rumor or illness.|There's a nasty flu go-around.  一种讨厌的流感正在传播。|synonyms: circulation, distribution, spreading|antonyms: containment, suppression|noun
