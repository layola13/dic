bureaucratic: /ˌbjʊərəˈkrætɪk/ | adj. | 官僚主义的；官僚作风的 | Relating to a system of government or other organization in which most of the important decisions are made by state officials rather than by elected representatives. Characterized by excessive adherence to rules and formalities, resulting in inefficiency and inflexibility. |  The process was slow and bureaucratic. 这个过程缓慢而官僚。| The bureaucratic system made it almost impossible to get anything done. 官僚体系使得几乎不可能完成任何事情。 | 近义词: administrative, official, governmental, red-tape | 反义词: efficient, streamlined, flexible | 词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *bureaucratique*, from *bureau* (desk, office) + *-cratie* (rule, government).  Ultimately from Late Latin *burra* (coarse woolen cloth), referring to the cloth covering of desks.
