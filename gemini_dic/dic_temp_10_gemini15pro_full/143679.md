outlawed:/ˈaʊtlɔːd/ | adj. | 非法的，被禁止的 |  Declared illegal and prohibited by law.  | The outlawed organization continued to operate underground.  这个被禁止的组织继续在地下运作。| synonyms: illegal, prohibited, banned, forbidden | antonyms: legal, permitted, allowed | adjective

outlaw:/ˈaʊtlɔː/ | n. | 歹徒，逃犯 | A person who has broken the law and is hiding or running away to avoid punishment.  |  The outlaw was finally captured after years on the run.  这名逃犯在逃亡多年后终于被抓获。| synonyms: criminal, fugitive, bandit, robber | antonyms: law-abiding citizen | noun

outlaw:/ˈaʊtlɔː/ | vt. | 禁止，宣布…为不合法 | To make something illegal or unacceptable.  | The government outlawed the sale of certain drugs. 政府禁止销售某些药物。| synonyms: prohibit, ban, forbid, illegalize | antonyms: legalize, permit, allow | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ūtlaga, from Old Norse útlagi, meaning "outlaw, exile".  The word literally meant someone placed outside the protection of the law.
