conspiration:/kənˌspɪˈreɪʃən/ |n.|阴谋，共谋，密谋|a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful.|The conspiracy to overthrow the government was discovered.  推翻政府的阴谋被发现了。|Synonyms: plot, scheme, intrigue|Antonyms: honesty, openness|noun

conspire:/kənˈspaɪər/ |v.|密谋，共谋，阴谋策划|make secret plans jointly to commit an unlawful or harmful act.|They conspired to rob the bank. 他们密谋抢劫银行。|Synonyms: plot, scheme, intrigue|Antonyms:  cooperate, collaborate (in a positive sense)|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French conspiracion, from Latin conspiratio(n-), from conspirare ‘agree, plot together’ (see conspire).
