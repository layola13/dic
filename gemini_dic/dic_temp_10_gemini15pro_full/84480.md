halteres:/ˈhælˌtɪəriz/ (US), /ˈhɔːltəriːz/ (UK)| n. |平衡棒|A pair of small, club-shaped organs on the metathorax of Diptera (true flies) that vibrate during flight and act as gyroscopes, used for balance and orientation.|The halteres of the fruit fly are essential for its aerial acrobatics.|Synonyms: balancers|Antonyms: N/A|noun

haltere:/ˈhælˌtɪər/ (US), /ˈhɔːltəriː/ (UK)| n. |平衡棒|A single club-shaped organ on the metathorax of a dipteran insect, vibrating during flight and acting as a gyroscope.|Scientists study the haltere to understand insect flight.|Synonyms: balancer|Antonyms: N/A|noun
