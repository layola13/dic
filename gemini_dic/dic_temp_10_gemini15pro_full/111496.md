loafers:/ˈloʊfərz/ (US), /ˈləʊfəz/ (UK)| n.|乐福鞋，懒汉鞋|A type of slip-on shoe, often made of leather or suede, characterized by its low heel and lack of laces.  They often have a moccasin construction.|He wore a pair of brown loafers to the office. 他穿了一双棕色乐福鞋去上班。|Synonyms: slip-ons, moccasins|Antonyms: boots, lace-up shoes|noun

loafers:/ˈloʊfərz/ (US), /ˈləʊfəz/ (UK)| n.|游手好闲的人|Someone who avoids work or responsibility, idler.|He's such a loafer, always sleeping in and avoiding chores. 他真是个懒汉，总是睡懒觉，不做家务。|Synonyms: idler, slacker, layabout|Antonyms: worker, hard worker|noun
