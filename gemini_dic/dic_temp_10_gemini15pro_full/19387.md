bicameralism:/ˌbaɪˈkæmərəlɪzəm/ | n. |两院制；双院制| The principle or system of having two legislative chambers or houses, like a Senate and a House of Representatives. | The United States operates under bicameralism, with the Senate and the House of Representatives. 美国实行两院制，由参议院和众议院组成。|The country's constitution establishes bicameralism as the foundation of its legislative branch. 该国宪法确立了两院制作为其立法部门的基础。| 近义词: two-chamber system | 反义词: unicameralism | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From bi- (two) + camera (chamber) + -ism (system).  Ultimately from Latin.
