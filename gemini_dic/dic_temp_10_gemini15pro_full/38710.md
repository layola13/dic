condign:/kənˈdaɪn/| adj.|适当的，应得的，恰当的|Deserved; appropriate (usually referring to punishment).  Suitable in character, extent, or degree.|The judge imposed a condign punishment for the heinous crime. 法官对这起令人发指的罪行处以了应有的惩罚。|近义词：deserved, appropriate, fitting, just, merited|反义词：undeserved, inappropriate, unfitting, unjust, excessive|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Late Middle English *condeigne*, from Old French *condigne*, from Latin *condignus* (“entirely worthy”), from *con-* (“with, together”) + *dignus* (“worthy”).
