glaucoma:/ɡlɔːˈkəʊmə/ (UK) /ɡlɔːˈkoʊmə/ (US)| n. |青光眼|A condition of increased pressure within the eyeball, causing gradual loss of sight.|例句：Glaucoma can lead to blindness if left untreated.  青光眼如果不治疗会导致失明。

例句：Regular eye exams are crucial for early detection of glaucoma. 定期眼科检查对于早期发现青光眼至关重要。|近义词：eye disease|反义词: (No direct antonym for a specific disease)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *glaucoma, from Ancient Greek γλαύκωμα (glaúkōma, “cataract, glaucoma”), from γλαυκός (glaukós, “gleaming, bluish-gray or green”).  Originally referred to cataracts, which can cause a bluish-grey or green appearance to the pupil.
