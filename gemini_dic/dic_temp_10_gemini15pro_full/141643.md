optimism: /ˈɒptɪmɪzəm/ (UK) /ˈɑːptɪmɪzəm/ (US) | n. |乐观，乐观主义| Hopefulness and confidence about the future or the successful outcome of something. |例句：Her optimism is infectious. 她的乐观情绪很有感染力。|例句：He maintained a sense of optimism despite the difficult circumstances. 尽管情况艰难，他仍然保持乐观。|近义词：hopefulness, positivity, confidence |反义词: pessimism, negativity |词性: noun

optimistic: /ˌɒptɪˈmɪstɪk/ (UK) /ˌɑːptɪˈmɪstɪk/ (US) | adj. |乐观的|  Feeling or showing hope and confidence about the future. |例句：She's optimistic about the outcome of the surgery. 她对手术结果感到乐观。|例句：An optimistic outlook on life can improve your overall well-being.  乐观的人生观可以改善你的整体健康。|近义词：hopeful, positive, confident |反义词: pessimistic, negative |词性: adjective

optimistically: /ˌɒptɪˈmɪstɪkli/ (UK) /ˌɑːptɪˈmɪstɪkli/ (US) | adv. |乐观地| In a hopeful and confident way. |例句：She optimistically predicted a quick recovery. 她乐观地预测会很快康复。|例句：He optimistically applied for the job, even though he lacked experience. 即使缺乏经验，他还是乐观地申请了这份工作。|近义词：hopefully, positively, confidently |反义词: pessimistically, negatively |词性: adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *optimisme*, from Latin *optimus* ("best").  The philosophical doctrine that this world is the best of all possible worlds.
