catalogues /ˈkætəlɒɡz/ (UK) /ˈkætəlɔːɡz/ (US) | n. |目录；产品样本；一系列事物| A complete list of items, typically one in alphabetical or other systematic order, in particular. |  The library has several catalogues of its books. 图书馆有几本藏书目录。| synonyms: list, register, directory, index, inventory, record, archive,  | antonyms: disorder, chaos | noun

catalogues /ˈkætəlɒɡz/ (UK) /ˈkætəlɔːɡz/ (US) | v. |为…编目录；记载；登记| To make a systematic list of items. | The museum is cataloguing its collection of antique toys. 博物馆正在为其古董玩具收藏编目。| synonyms: list, register, document, record, index, inventory | antonyms: disorganize,  jumble | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *catalogus, from Ancient Greek κατάλογος (katálogos, “list, register”), from καταλέγω (katalégō, “I list, enumerate”), from κατά (katá, “down, completely”) + λέγω (légō, “I gather, count, tell”).
