disquiet:/dɪsˈkwaɪət/| n. vt.|不安，焦虑；使不安，使焦虑|A feeling of anxiety or worry.|The news of the layoffs caused widespread disquiet among the employees. 裁员的消息在员工中引起了广泛的不安。|近义词：unease, anxiety, worry, concern, apprehension|反义词：calm, peace, tranquility|词性:名词,动词

disquiet:/dɪsˈkwaɪət/| vt.|使不安，使焦虑|To make someone feel anxious or worried.|The strange noises in the night disquieted her. 夜晚奇怪的噪音让她感到不安。|近义词：disturb, upset, worry, trouble, bother|反义词：calm, soothe, reassure|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desquetier "to disquiet," probably based on Latin quietus "rest," with the prefix des- "opposite of."
