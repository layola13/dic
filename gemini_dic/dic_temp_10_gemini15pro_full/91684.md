hypercolumn:/ˈhaɪpərˌkɒləm/ (US) /ˈhaɪpəˌkɒləm/ (UK)| n. |超柱; 超列|A group of neurons in the visual cortex of the brain that are thought to process information about a small region of the visual field, responding to various features such as orientation, color, and movement.|The researchers studied the activity of hypercolumns in the visual cortex of cats. 研究人员研究了猫视觉皮层中超柱的活动。|Synonyms:  cortical column, cell assembly|Antonyms: N/A|noun

hypercolumn:/ˈhaɪpərˌkɒləm/ (US) /ˈhaɪpəˌkɒləm/ (UK)| n. |超柱; 超列|A collection of neurons in the visual cortex that respond to a variety of stimuli from a specific area of the visual field.  This collection includes neurons sensitive to different orientations, colors, and depths, enabling a comprehensive analysis of that particular visual input.| The hypercolumn acts as a fundamental processing unit in vision. 超柱是视觉中一个基本的处理单元。|Synonyms: cortical module, functional unit|Antonyms: N/A|noun
