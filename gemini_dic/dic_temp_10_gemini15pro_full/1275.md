accost: /əˈkɒst/ (UK) /əˈkɔːst/ (US)| vt.|搭讪；上前攀谈；（通常指以略带威胁性的方式）走近；质问|To approach and speak to someone, often in a bold or aggressive way.|A stranger accosted me on the street and asked for money. 一个陌生人在街上和我搭讪，要钱。

A journalist accosted the mayor with questions about the scandal. 一名记者就这起丑闻向市长提出质问。|近义词：approach, address, confront, hail, buttonhole|反义词：avoid, evade, shun|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *acoster* "come alongside," from Vulgar Latin *accostare*, from Latin *ad* "to" + *costa* "rib, side, coast."  Originally a nautical term meaning "to come alongside," it later broadened to mean "to approach and speak to."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
A man often accosts tourists near the station. = 一个男人经常在车站附近搭讪游客。
She accosts her neighbor every morning for a chat. = 她每天早上都和邻居攀谈。

一般过去时:
The beggar accosted him for some spare change. = 乞丐向他乞讨零钱。
I was accosted by a salesperson who wouldn't leave me alone. = 我被一个不愿离开我的售货员缠住了。

现在完成时:
Several people have accosted me asking for directions today. = 今天有几个人过来问我方向。
He has accosted several women in the park, making them feel uncomfortable. = 他在公园里搭讪了好几个女人，让她们感到不舒服。

过去完成时:
She had been accosted several times before she reached her destination. = 在她到达目的地之前，她已经被搭讪了好几次。
He realized he had accosted the wrong person after he started speaking. = 他开始说话后才意识到自己搭讪错了人。

将来时:
If you accost him like that, he'll probably be offended. = 如果你那样跟他搭讪，他可能会生气。
I will accost the manager if I don't get a refund. = 如果我没有得到退款，我会去找经理。

虚拟语气:
If I were to accost a celebrity, I would be very polite. = 如果我要和名人搭讪，我会非常有礼貌。
I wish I hadn't accosted that stranger; he turned out to be rather aggressive. = 我希望我没有搭讪那个陌生人；他原来很咄咄逼人。


被动语态:
He was accosted by security guards as he tried to enter the building. = 他试图进入大楼时被保安拦住了。
She was accosted in the street and robbed. = 她在街上被拦住抢劫了。


疑问句:
Why did that stranger accost you? = 为什么那个陌生人要跟你搭讪？
Have you ever been accosted by someone asking for money? = 你曾经被别人要过钱吗？


否定句:
I didn't accost him, he came up to me. = 我没有和他搭讪，是他来找我的。
She didn't want to accost the celebrity, so she just watched from afar. = 她不想打扰那位名人，所以只是远远地看着。


PHRASE->
be accosted by = 被...搭讪
accost someone for something = 为某事搭讪某人
accost someone with something = 用某物搭讪某人 (e.g., a question, a comment)
accost someone in the street = 在街上搭讪某人
accost someone rudely = 粗鲁地搭讪某人
