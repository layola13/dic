houseful: /ˈhaʊsfʊl/ (US), /ˈhaʊsfʊl/ (UK)
noun
客满；满座；满屋的人
A houseful means a house filled with people, often to capacity.  It implies a large gathering or a situation where the house is full of guests or family.
The party was a huge success; we had a houseful of guests.  派对非常成功；我们家宾客满座。
We're expecting a houseful this weekend.  我们预计这周末家里会来很多人。
synonym: full house, packed house, capacity crowd
antonym: empty house
