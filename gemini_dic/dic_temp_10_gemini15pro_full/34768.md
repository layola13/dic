clifted:/ˈklɪftɪd/ | adj. | 峭壁的，悬崖的 | Characterized by cliffs; having cliffs. | The clifted coastline offered breathtaking views. 峭壁林立的海岸线提供了令人叹为观止的景色。 | The ship sailed past the clifted shores. 船驶过悬崖峭壁的海岸。 | 近义词：cliff-lined, precipitous, steep | 反义词：flat, level | 词性: 形容词

Etymology: From cliff + -ed.  "Cliff" comes from Old English *clif, related to Middle Dutch clif "cliff, rock," and Old Norse klif "cliff, crag."  The "-ed" suffix forms adjectives from nouns, meaning "having, characterized by."
