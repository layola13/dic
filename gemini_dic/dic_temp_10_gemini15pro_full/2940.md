advocators:/ˈædvəkɪtərz/ (US) /ˈædvəkətəz/ (UK)| n.|提倡者，拥护者，辩护者|A person who publicly supports or recommends a particular cause or policy.|She is a strong advocate for animal rights. 她是一位坚定的动物权利倡导者。

Advocators of free trade believe it benefits all countries. 自由贸易的倡导者认为它对所有国家都有利。

|近义词：supporter, proponent, champion, backer|反义词：opponent, critic, detractor|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English advocat, avocat, from Old French avocat, from Latin advocatus (“called to (one's aid), summoned”), past participle of advocare (“to summon”), from ad- (“to, towards”) + vocare (“to call”).

USAGE->
There are no specific verb conjugations for "advocators" as it is a noun.  However, it is commonly used with verbs like "are," "were," "have been," "will be," etc., depending on the tense and context. Here are some usage examples with different verb tenses and sentence structures:
