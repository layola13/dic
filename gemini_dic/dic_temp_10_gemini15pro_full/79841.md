glyphosate:/ˈɡlaɪfəˌseɪt/| n.|草甘膦|A broad-spectrum systemic herbicide and crop desiccant. It is an organophosphorus compound, specifically a phosphonate, which acts by inhibiting the plant enzyme 5-enolpyruvylshikimate-3-phosphate synthase (EPSPS).|Farmers use glyphosate to control weeds in their fields. 农民使用草甘膦来控制田间的杂草。|Synonyms: Roundup (brand name), N-(phosphonomethyl)glycine|Antonyms: None (no direct chemical antonym, but organic herbicides could be considered functional antonyms)|noun
