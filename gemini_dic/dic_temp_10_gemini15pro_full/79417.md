glires:/ˈɡlaɪriːz/
noun
啮齿类动物（总目）
A taxonomic group that includes rodents (Rodentia) and lagomorphs (Lagomorpha), such as rabbits, hares, and pikas.
The glires are thought to have originated from a common ancestor. 啮齿类动物被认为起源于一个共同的祖先。
Glires are characterized by their continuously growing incisors. 啮齿类动物的特点是门牙不断生长。
近义词: rodents, lagomorphs
反义词: (No direct antonym for a taxonomic group)
词性: n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *glīrēs, plural of glīs (“dormouse”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵleyH- (“glue, resin; slime”).  The connection likely refers to the sticky secretions of some dormice.
