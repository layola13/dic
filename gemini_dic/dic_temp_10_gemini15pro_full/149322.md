He paced nervously back and forth in the waiting room. 他在候诊室里紧张地来回踱步。

ETYMOLOGY->

"pace" comes from Old French "pas," ultimately from Latin "passus," meaning "step."  "Setting" comes from Old English "settan," meaning "to put, place, or fix."  "Pace-setting" combines these to mean "setting the pace" or establishing the speed or standard for others.
