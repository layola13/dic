domesday: /ˈduːmzdeɪ/ (UK), /ˈduːmzˌdeɪ/ (US)| n. | 末日审判; 命运攸关的时刻; 清算日 |  A record of a survey of England made in 1086 by order of William the Conqueror, listing landholders, the extent and value of their estates, and other resources. Figuratively, it refers to a time of reckoning or a crucial day of judgment. | Example 1:  Historians still consult the Domesday Book for information about land ownership in medieval England.  (历史学家仍然参考末日审判书来了解中世纪英格兰的土地所有权。)

Example 2: The company's annual report is seen as a domesday for the CEO's performance. (公司的年度报告被视为对首席执行官业绩的清算日。) | Synonym: day of reckoning, judgment day, moment of truth | Antonym:  N/A (abstract noun, doesn't have a direct opposite) | noun
