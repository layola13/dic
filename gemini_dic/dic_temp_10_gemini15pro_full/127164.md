naturalised:/ˈnætʃrəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 入籍的，归化的；驯化的；引进的 |  Admitted to citizenship; made to feel or behave as though belonging naturally to a particular place or group; (of a plant or animal) introduced from another region and established as part of the local flora or fauna. |  He became a naturalised British citizen after living in the UK for five years. 他在英国居住五年后成为英国公民。 The plant has become naturalised in many parts of the world. 这种植物已在世界许多地方归化。 | 近义词:  citizen, adopted, acclimated, indigenous, domesticated | 反义词: alien, foreign, exotic, wild | 词性: 形容词

naturalize:/ˈnætʃrəlaɪz/ | vt.& vi. | 使入籍；使归化；移植；吸收 |  Admit (a foreigner) to the citizenship of a country; make (something) seem natural or acceptable; introduce and establish (a plant or animal) in a new habitat or environment. |  The government is trying to naturalize more immigrants. 政府正试图让更多的移民入籍.  They attempted to naturalize the foreign customs. 他们试图吸收外国的风俗习惯。| 近义词:  acclimate, adapt, assimilate, accommodate | 反义词: expatriate, alienate | 词性: 动词

词源:  From Late Middle English *naturalisen, *naturisen, from Old French naturaliser, from Late Latin naturalizāre, from Latin nātūrālis (“natural”).
