deadheading:/ˈdedˌhedɪŋ/| n. v.|去除凋谢的花朵；空驶；空载；|Removing faded flowers to encourage new growth; traveling or operating a vehicle without passengers or cargo. |例句：Deadheading roses encourages more blooms.  去除玫瑰的凋谢花朵会促进更多花朵盛开。|例句：The truck driver was deadheading back to the depot. 卡车司机空车返回车站。|近义词： pruning, trimming (for plants); empty, unloaded (for vehicles)|反义词:  blooming, flowering (for plants); loaded, full (for vehicles)|词性: noun, verb

deadhead:/ˈdedˌhed/| n.| 凋谢的花朵;免费乘客；搭便车的人；|A faded flower; a person who travels or attends an event without paying.|例句：She snipped off the deadheads from the petunias. 她从矮牵牛上剪掉了凋谢的花朵。|例句：He managed to sneak in as a deadhead. 他设法偷偷溜进去当一个免费乘客。|近义词：freeloader|反义词: paying customer|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "dead" + "head".  "Dead" in this context refers to something no longer productive or useful. "Head" refers to the flower head or the leading vehicle in a convoy.  The meaning related to transportation developed later, referring to the unproductive return trip of a vehicle.
