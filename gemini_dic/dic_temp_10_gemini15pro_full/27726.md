canfuls: /ˈkænˌfʊlz/
noun
罐满; 几罐
The amount contained in a specified number of cans.
She brought three canfuls of water from the river. 她从河里提了三罐水。
Near synonyms: cans, containers
Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for a unit of measurement.  Consider "empty cans" or "a little")
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
"Canful" is derived from "can" (a metal container) + "-ful" (a suffix indicating fullness).  The "can" part comes from Old English *canne, likely from Proto-Germanic *kannǭ, possibly borrowed from Latin canna "reed, cane."  "-ful" comes from Old English -full.
