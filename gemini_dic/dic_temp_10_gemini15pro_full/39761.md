consolidant:/kənˈsɒlɪdənt/ | n. adj. |加固剂，巩固的|A substance used to strengthen or solidify something, especially stone or masonry.  Can also be used as an adjective to describe something that consolidates.|
例句：The conservators used a consolidant to stabilize the crumbling statue.  文物保护者使用了一种加固剂来稳定破碎的雕像。

例句：The consolidant effect of the new policy was immediately apparent. 新政策的巩固作用立即显现。

近义词：stabilizer, strengthener, reinforcer, binding agent|
反义词：weakening agent, solvent|
词性：noun, adjective|

consolidate:/kənˈsɒlɪdeɪt/ | vt. |巩固，加强，合并|To make something stronger or more solid; to combine several things into a single more effective or coherent whole.|
例句：The company consolidated its position in the market by acquiring its main competitor.  该公司通过收购其主要竞争对手巩固了其市场地位。

例句：They decided to consolidate their debts into a single loan. 他们决定把他们的债务合并成一笔贷款。

近义词：strengthen, reinforce, solidify, merge, combine|
反义词：weaken, divide, separate|
词性：verb|

词源: From Latin consolidare "to make firm, make strong," from con- "together" (see con-) + solidare "to make solid," from solidus "solid."
