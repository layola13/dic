outspurns:/ˌaʊtˈspɜːrnz/ | v. | 超越，胜过 | To surpass or excel someone, especially in speed or performance. | He easily outspurned the other runners in the final stretch. 他在最后冲刺阶段轻松超越了其他选手。|  The young apprentice quickly outspurned his master in skill and innovation.  年轻的学徒很快在技能和创新方面超过了他的师傅。 | 近义词: outstrip, outrun, surpass, excel, exceed | 反义词: lag behind, trail, fall behind | 词性: 动词

词源:  "outspurn" comes from "out-" (meaning "beyond" or "exceeding") + "spurn" (meaning "to kick or strike with the foot").  The original sense was to kick further than someone else, which then developed into the more general meaning of surpassing.
