casebooks: /ˈkeɪsbʊks/ | n. | 案例集；判例汇编 | A collection of case studies, typically used in law or business schools, to illustrate real-world applications of theories and principles. | Law students often use casebooks to prepare for class. 法学院的学生经常使用案例集来准备上课。|  compendium, collection, anthology |  | noun
Etymology:  From "case" (meaning a particular instance or situation) + "book" (a written or printed work).  The term originated in legal contexts, referring to compilations of legal cases.
