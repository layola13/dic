delegating:/ˈdɛlɪˌɡeɪtɪŋ/| n./v.|委托；授权；委派|The act of assigning responsibility or authority to another person.|例句：Delegating tasks effectively is a key management skill. 有效地委派任务是一项关键的管理技能。|近义词：assigning, entrusting, authorizing|反义词：micromanaging, retaining, centralizing|词性: 动名词 (gerund)

delegated:/ˈdɛlɪˌɡeɪtɪd/| adj. |已委派的；授权的|Assigned responsibility or authority.|例句：The delegated authority allowed the team to make quick decisions. 授权允许团队快速做出决策。|近义词：assigned, entrusted, authorized|反义词：retained, centralized|词性: 形容词 (adjective)

delegate:/ˈdɛlɪɡeɪt/| n. / v.|代表；委托；委派|A person sent or authorized to represent others, or the act of assigning responsibility.|例句1:  The delegates from each country met to discuss the treaty.  每个国家的代表会面讨论条约。|例句2: He delegated the task to his assistant. 他把任务委托给了他的助手。|近义词：(n.) representative, envoy, commissioner; (v.) assign, entrust, authorize|反义词：(v.) retain, centralize|词性: 名词 (noun) / 动词 (verb)


词源:  From Latin delegatus, past participle of delegare (“to send as a representative, assign, entrust”), from de- (“down, away”) + legare (“to send as a legate, appoint by a last will, bequeath”).
