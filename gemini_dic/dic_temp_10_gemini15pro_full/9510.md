antischolastically:/ˌæntiˌskɒˈlæstɪkəli/ | adv. | 反学院式地，非学术地 | In a manner opposed to or rejecting traditional academic principles or methods; in a way that is not scholarly. | He antischolastically dismissed the importance of formal education, preferring self-directed learning. 他以反学院派的方式驳斥了正规教育的重要性，更喜欢自学。 | Synonyms: unacademically, informally, non-academically | Antonyms: scholastically, academically, learnedly
ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + scholastic + -ally.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposed to." "Scholastic" relates to schools and education, particularly formal and traditional learning. "-ally" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives.
