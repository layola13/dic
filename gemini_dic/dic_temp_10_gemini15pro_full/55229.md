dissidently:/dɪˈsɪdəntlɪ/ | adv. |持不同政见地，异议地| In a manner that expresses disagreement or opposition, especially to official policy. | He dissidently refused to conform to the party line. 他持不同政见地拒绝遵守党的路线。|  She dissidently voiced her concerns about the new policy. 她表达了她对新政策的担忧，表明了她的不同意见。 | 近义词: rebelliously, defiantly, unconventionally, nonconformistly | 反义词: obediently, conformistly, submissively | 词性:副词

dissident:/ˈdɪsɪdənt/ | n. adj. |持不同政见者，异议人士；持不同政见的| A person who publicly disagrees with and criticizes their government. | The government imprisoned several dissidents. 政府囚禁了几名持不同政见者。 | He was known as a dissident writer. 他以一个持不同政见的作家而闻名。 | 近义词: rebel, protester, objector, nonconformist | 反义词: conformist, loyalist | 词性:名词/形容词


词源: From Latin dissident- ‘sitting apart, disagreeing’, from the verb dissidēre, from dis- ‘apart’ + sidēre ‘to sit’.
