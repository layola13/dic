about-turn:/əˈbaʊt tɜːrn/
n.|向后转，转变|An act of turning around to face the opposite direction; a complete change of opinion or policy.|The sergeant ordered the soldiers to make an about-turn. 中士命令士兵们向后转。|Her sudden about-turn on the issue surprised everyone. 她在这个问题上的突然转变让每个人都感到惊讶。|
近义词:about-face, U-turn, reversal, volte-face
反义词:continuation, perseverance
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the military command "about turn," instructing soldiers to turn 180 degrees.  The phrase itself likely originates from the earlier "right about turn," shortened over time.  The figurative meaning of a change in opinion or policy developed later, drawing on the physical action of turning around.

USAGE->
The word "about-turn" can be used as both a noun and an adjective.

As a Noun:

The government made a sudden about-turn on its tax policy. = 政府突然改变了其税收政策。

The general's about-turn shocked his troops. = 将军的转变震惊了他的部队。


As an Adjective:

The about-turn maneuver was executed perfectly. = 向后转的动作执行得很完美。

His about-turn decision surprised everyone. = 他转变的决定让每个人都感到惊讶。
