hellebores: /ˈhɛlɪˌbɔːrz/ | n. | 铁筷子属植物；嚏根草 | A genus of flowering plants in the buttercup family, Ranunculaceae, native to Europe and Asia. They are known for their early blooming, often in winter or early spring, and their attractive, cup-shaped flowers. |  The hellebores in my garden are already blooming, even though it's still February. 我的花园里的铁筷子已经开花了，尽管现在还是二月。

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *helleborus, from Greek ἑλλέβορος (helléboros), of unknown origin. Possibly related to ἑλεῖν (heleîn, “to injure”) + βορά (borá, “food”), referring to the plant's toxicity.
