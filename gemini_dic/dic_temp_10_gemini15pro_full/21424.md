blow-up:/ˈbloʊ ʌp/| n.| 爆炸；放大照片；勃然大怒；|A sudden, often violent outburst or explosion; an enlargement of a photograph or other image. |The blow-up between the two friends ended their relationship.  两个朋友之间的勃然大怒结束了他们的友谊。|Synonyms: explosion, outburst, eruption, tantrum, enlargement|Antonyms: calm, peace, reconciliation, reduction|noun
blow-up:/ˈbloʊ ʌp/| adj.| 可充气的；|Designed to be inflated with air or gas. |We bought a blow-up mattress for the camping trip. 我们为露营旅行买了一个充气床垫。|Synonyms: inflatable, air-filled|Antonyms: deflated, solid|adjective
blow up:/bloʊ ˈʌp/| v.| 爆炸；放大；勃然大怒；充气；|To explode; to enlarge (a photograph or other image); to suddenly become very angry; to inflate something with air or gas. |The building blew up after a gas leak.  煤气泄漏后，大楼爆炸了。|Synonyms: explode, detonate, enlarge, magnify, inflate, rage|Antonyms: deflate, shrink, reduce, calm down|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrasal verb "blow up," meaning to explode, which originated in the late 18th century. The noun and adjective forms developed later.
