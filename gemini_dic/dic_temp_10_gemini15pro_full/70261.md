fictitiousness:/fɪkˈtɪʃəsnəs/| n. |虚构性，假造性|The fictitiousness of the story was obvious from the beginning. 故事从一开始就显而易见是虚构的。|例句：The author acknowledged the fictitiousness of the characters in his novel.  作者承认他小说中人物的虚构性。

例句：The fictitiousness of the evidence presented in court led to the dismissal of the case.  法庭上提出的证据的虚构性导致案件被驳回。|近义词：fabrication, invention, artificiality, unreality|反义词：reality, truth, authenticity|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From fictitious + -ness.  "fictitious" comes from Late Latin fictitius “artificial, feigned,” from Latin fictus, past participle of fingere “to shape, fashion, pretend, feign.”  "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being."
