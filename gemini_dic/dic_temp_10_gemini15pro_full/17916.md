bees:/biːz/
n.
蜜蜂
Plural form of bee.  A flying insect that collects nectar and pollen, produces honey and beeswax, and lives in large colonies.
The bees were buzzing around the hive. 蜜蜂在蜂巢周围嗡嗡作响。
近义词：honeybees, apids
反义词: (无直接反义词，可考虑 predators 捕食者)
词性: 名词(plural)


bees:/biːz/
v.
(非正式) 困惑，使糊涂 (通常用于 be bees in one's bonnet)
To have an obsession with something.  Usually used in the idiom "to have a bee in one's bonnet".
She has a bee in her bonnet about recycling. 她对回收利用很着迷。
近义词：obsess, fixate
反义词: (无直接反义词)
词性: 动词(third-person singular simple present)

PHRASE->
a bee in one's bonnet:  对某事着迷  She has a bee in her bonnet about organic gardening. 她对有机园艺非常着迷。
the bee's knees:  非常出色的人或事  This new phone is the bee's knees! 这款新手机真是太棒了！
busy as a bee:  非常忙碌  She's been as busy as a bee all day preparing for the party. 她一整天都在忙着准备派对。
make a beeline for:  径直前往  As soon as the bell rang, the children made a beeline for the playground.  铃声一响，孩子们就直奔操场。
put the bee on someone:  向某人要钱  He put the bee on his parents for a new car. 他向父母要钱买新车。

bees的词源：
来源于古英语 *bēo，来自原始日耳曼语 *bēōn，最终来自原始印欧语 *bhei- “蜜蜂”。
