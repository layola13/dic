coppiced:/ˈkɒpɪst/ | adj. | 修剪过的，矮林作业的 |  (of a tree or shrub) having been cut back to ground level periodically to promote growth of new shoots. | The coppiced hazel provided a sustainable source of wood for fencing.  修剪过的榛树为栅栏提供了可持续的木材来源。 | synonyms: pruned, trimmed, pollarded | antonyms: overgrown, untrimmed | adjective

coppice:/ˈkɒpɪs/ | n. | 矮林，灌木丛 | An area of woodland in which the trees or shrubs are periodically cut back to ground level to stimulate growth. |  They walked through the coppice, the sunlight dappling the path ahead. 他们穿过矮林，阳光洒落在前方的路上。| synonyms: thicket, undergrowth, brushwood | antonyms: forest, woodland (in a broader, less managed sense) | noun

coppice:/ˈkɒpɪs/ | v. | 矮林作业，修剪 | Cut back (a tree or shrub) to ground level periodically to promote growth.  |  The farmer coppiced the willow trees every few years.  农民每隔几年就修剪柳树。 | synonyms: prune, trim, pollard | antonyms: - (no direct antonym for the verb form) | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *colper (Modern French couper, "to cut"), from Vulgar Latin *colpāre, from Latin colăphus (“blow with the fist”), from Greek κόλαφος (kólaphos, “a buffet, cuff”).
