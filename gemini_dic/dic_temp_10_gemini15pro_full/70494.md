filariasis:/ˌfɪləˈraɪəsɪs/| n. | 丝虫病 | A parasitic disease caused by filarial worms, transmitted by mosquitoes or other blood-feeding insects. It can lead to elephantiasis and other severe health problems. | Filariasis is a major public health problem in many tropical countries. 丝虫病是许多热带国家的重大公共卫生问题。|  The World Health Organization is working to eliminate filariasis. 世界卫生组织正在努力消除丝虫病。 | synonyms: elephantiasis (in advanced stages), helminthiasis | antonyms:  None (diseases don't typically have antonyms) | noun


Etymology:  From Modern Latin *Filaria* (a genus of parasitic nematode worms) + -iasis (suffix denoting a diseased condition).  Ultimately from Latin *filum* (thread).
