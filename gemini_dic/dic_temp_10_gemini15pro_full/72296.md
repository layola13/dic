flounder:/ˈflaʊndər/| v. |挣扎；笨拙地移动；支吾；(鱼)跳跃，挣扎|To struggle clumsily or helplessly; to move with difficulty in mud or water; to speak or act in a confused or awkward way.|The company floundered in the recession. 这家公司在经济衰退中苦苦挣扎。|synonyms: struggle, thrash, stumble, falter|antonyms: succeed, thrive, prosper|verb

flounder:/ˈflaʊndər/| n. |比目鱼|A flatfish, especially a small one.|We caught several flounders while fishing. 我们钓鱼时抓到了几条比目鱼。|synonyms: flatfish, sole, plaice|antonyms: (No direct antonym for a type of fish)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *flyðra "to flutter, struggle," related to Old English floterian "to flutter." The noun sense, referring to a flatfish, likely arose from the fish's characteristic flapping movements.
