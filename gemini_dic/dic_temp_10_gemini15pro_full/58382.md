duarchy:/ˈdjuːɑːki/| n.|二头政治，双重统治|A system of government in which two people or groups share power.|The Roman Republic, in its early days, could be considered a duarchy, with two consuls sharing supreme power.|Synonyms: diarchy, dyarchy|Antonyms: monarchy, autocracy|noun
