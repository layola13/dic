gloating:/ˈɡləʊtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈɡloʊtɪŋ/ (US)| n. adj.|幸灾乐祸，洋洋得意|dwelling on one's own success or another's misfortune with smugness or malignant pleasure.|He couldn't resist gloating over his rival's failure. 他忍不住对竞争对手的失败幸灾乐祸。|近义词：rejoicing, reveling, crowing|反义词：commiserating, sympathizing|词性: noun (primarily), adjective (less common)

gloat:/ˈɡləʊt/ (UK) /ˈɡloʊt/ (US)| vi.|幸灾乐祸，洋洋得意|to contemplate or dwell on one's own success or another's misfortune with smugness or malignant pleasure.|She gloated over her new expensive handbag. 她对自己的新昂贵手提包沾沾自喜。|近义词：rejoice, revel, crow|反义词：commiserate, sympathize|词性: verb

词源: From Middle English *gloten, from Old Norse *gláta (“to grin, smile scornfully”).
