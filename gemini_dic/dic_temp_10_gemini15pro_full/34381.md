classificatory:/ˈklæsɪfɪkətɔːri/| adj.|分类的，类别的|Relating to or concerned with classification; arranged according to a system of classification.|例句：The library uses a classificatory system to organize its books. 图书馆使用分类系统来整理书籍。|例句：Classificatory criteria are essential for effective research.  分类标准对于有效的研究至关重要。|近义词：taxonomic, categorical|反义词: unclassified, unsorted|词性:形容词
classify:/ˈklæsɪfaɪ/| v.|分类，归类|Arrange (a group of people or things) in classes or categories according to shared qualities or characteristics.|例句：Scientists classify animals into different groups. 科学家将动物分成不同的组。|例句：The books are classified according to subject matter. 书籍按主题分类。 |近义词：categorize, sort, arrange|反义词: jumble, mix|词性:动词
class:/klæs/| n.|种类，等级|A set or category of things having shared characteristics.|例句：She belongs to the middle class. 她属于中产阶级。|例句：He drives a luxury class car. 他开的是豪华型轿车。|近义词：category, group, type|反义词: individual|词性:名词
