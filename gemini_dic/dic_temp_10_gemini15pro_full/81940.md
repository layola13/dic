grimalkin: /ɡrɪˈmæl.kɪn/ /ɡrɪˈmɔːl.kɪn/ | n. | 母猫；脾气坏的老太婆 | A female cat, especially an old one. Often used to suggest a sinister or mysterious quality.  | The old woman, a grimalkin with piercing eyes, glared at the children from her porch. 那位老妇人，一个目光锐利的老猫，从门廊上怒视着孩子们。 |例句：The black grimalkin slunk through the shadows.  那只黑色的母猫偷偷地穿过阴影。

例句：Legends say a grimalkin guards the hidden treasure. 传说有一只老猫守护着隐藏的宝藏。 | 近义词：cat, feline, she-cat | 反义词：tom-cat (仅在性别方面) | 词性：名词

词源：grimalkin comes from "grey" + "Malkin," a diminutive of "Mary."  Malkin was a common name for a cat, and by extension, also a derogatory term for a woman.  Over time, grimalkin became associated specifically with female cats, particularly those perceived as old and unpleasant.
