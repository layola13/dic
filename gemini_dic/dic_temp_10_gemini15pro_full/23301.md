boy-o:/ˈbɔɪ oʊ/| interj. | 小伙子，老兄（表示亲切或轻蔑）| An informal term of address to a male, expressing familiarity or sometimes condescension. |"Boy-o, you're in trouble now!" 小伙子，你现在麻烦大了！|近义词：chap, fellow, mate, buddy (depending on context) | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, depends on intended nuance.  Could be 'girl' if contrasting gender, or a formal term of address like 'sir' if contrasting formality) |感叹词

boy-o:/ˈbɔɪ oʊ/| n. | 小伙子，老兄（名词用法较少见）|  A boy or young man. |"He's a tough boy-o." 他是个硬汉子。|近义词：lad, youth, youngster |反义词: (No direct antonym, could be 'girl' if contrasting gender, or an older male term like 'old man')|名词
