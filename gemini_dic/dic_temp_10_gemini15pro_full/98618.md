instrument's:/ˈɪnstrəmənts/ |n.|乐器的，仪器的| possessive form of instrument, meaning "belonging to an instrument." It indicates ownership or association.| The instrument's strings were broken.  这乐器的弦断了。|Synonyms: of the instrument, belonging to the instrument |Antonyms: N/A| Possessive Pronoun


Etymology:
From Old French instrument (12c.), from Latin instrumentum "tool, implement; a document, writing; a legal document, contract, writ," from instruere "build, construct, arrange, prepare, furnish, provide; teach, instruct, educate," from in- "upon, toward" (from PIE root *en "in") + struere "pile, heap up, build" (from PIE root *stere- "to spread").
