bandolier:/ˌbændəˈlɪr/ |n.|子弹带，弹药带|A belt worn over the shoulder and across the chest, originally to hold cartridges.  A broad band of fabric worn over the shoulder.|The soldier wore a bandolier across his chest.|Synonyms: cartridge belt, ammunition belt|Antonyms: None|noun

bandolier:/ˌbændəˈlɪr/ |n.| (比喻) 一系列（事物）|A series of things, especially problems or responsibilities, that someone has to deal with.|He faced a bandolier of problems when he took over the company.|Synonyms: string, series, chain, succession|Antonyms: None|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *bandoulière*, from *bande* ("band"). Ultimately from Frankish *binda* (“band, ribbon”).
