caretaken:/ˈkɛərˌteɪkən/ | adj. | 受照顾的；被照料的 |  Taken care of; looked after.  This word is generally used in a passive sense, implying that someone or something has been provided with care or attention. | The caretaken garden looked vibrant and healthy.  被精心照料的花园看起来生机勃勃，健康茁壮。|  The children felt safe and caretaken in their grandmother's home. 孩子们在祖母家感到安全和受到照顾。 | Synonyms:  tended, nurtured, protected, looked after | Antonyms: neglected, abandoned, forsaken | adjective

caretaker:/ˈkɛərˌteɪkər/ | n. |  管理员；看守人；守护者| A person who takes care of a person, a place, or an organization. | The caretaker keeps the school clean and tidy. 管理员保持学校的干净和整洁。 | The museum caretaker ensured all the exhibits were secure. 博物馆管理员确保所有展品的安全。 | Synonyms: custodian, guardian, superintendent | Antonyms: vandal, destroyer | noun
