hospitalism: /ˈhɒspɪtəlɪzəm/ (US) /ˈhɒspɪtəlɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |住院癖，住院综合症；医院性精神病|A state of impairment, both physical and mental, that can affect infants and young children, especially those who have been institutionalized or hospitalized for long periods and deprived of normal parental care and affection.|The study found a high incidence of hospitalism among children in long-term care facilities. 这项研究发现，长期护理机构中的儿童住院癖发病率很高。|Synonyms: institutionalization, deprivation syndrome|Antonyms:  well-being, thriving|noun

hospitalism: /ˈhɒspɪtəlɪzəm/ (US) /ˈhɒspɪtəlɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |住院病，医院获得性感染|Any infection acquired during hospitalization, regardless of whether or not it is manifested clinically.|The patient developed hospitalism after undergoing surgery.  这个病人在手术后出现了医院获得性感染。|Synonyms: nosocomial infection, hospital-acquired infection|Antonyms: community-acquired infection|noun
