magnet:/ˈmæɡnɪt/| n.|磁铁，磁体；有吸引力的人或物|A magnet is an object that produces a magnetic field.  A magnetic field is invisible but is responsible for the most notable property of a magnet: a force that pulls on other ferromagnetic materials, such as iron, steel, nickel, cobalt, etc. and attracts or repels other magnets.|The metal filings clustered around the magnet. 金属屑聚集在磁铁周围。|synonyms: lodestone, attraction|antonyms: repellent, deterrent|noun
