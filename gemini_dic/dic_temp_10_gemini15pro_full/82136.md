grommet: /ˈɡrɒmɪt/ (UK) /ˈɡrɑːmɪt/ (US) | n. | 金属扣眼，索环，垫圈 | A ring or edge of metal, plastic, or rubber used to reinforce a hole in a sheet of material, such as cloth or leather, and prevent it from tearing or fraying. |  The sails were attached to the mast with grommets.  帆用金属扣眼固定在桅杆上。| Synonyms: eyelet, ring, loop | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "unreinforced hole") | noun

grommet: /ˈɡrɒmɪt/ (UK) /ˈɡrɑːmɪt/ (US) | n. | (尤指用于电线的)橡胶套圈，橡胶护圈 | A rubber ring used to protect or insulate something, especially a wire. | He threaded the wires through the grommets in the firewall. 他把电线穿过防火墙上的橡胶套圈。 | Synonyms: bushing, seal, gasket | Antonyms: (No direct antonym) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gromette, diminutive of gromet "curb bit," of Germanic origin.
