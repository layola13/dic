homestead:/ˈhoʊmsted/ (US), /ˈhəʊmsted/ (UK)| n. v.|家园，宅地；取得宅地|A house, especially a farmhouse, and the outbuildings and land around it.  To acquire or occupy a homestead. |例句：They built their homestead on the prairie. 他们在草原上建造了他们的家园。|近义词：homestead, farmstead, ranch|反义词: city, apartment|词性: 名词，动词

homesteaded:/ˈhoʊmstedɪd/ (US), /ˈhəʊmstedɪd/ (UK)| v. | 取得宅地（的）; 居住于宅基地（的） |Past tense and past participle of "homestead," meaning to have acquired or occupied a homestead.  Can also be used as an adjective to describe something related to homesteading.|例句：They homesteaded 160 acres in Kansas. 他们在堪萨斯州获得了160英亩的宅基地。|近义词：settled, occupied|反义词: abandoned, vacated |词性: 动词, 形容词 (rare)

词源: From "home" + "stead" (place).  "Stead" comes from Old English "stede," meaning "place."
