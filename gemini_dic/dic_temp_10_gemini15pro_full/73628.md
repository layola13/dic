forewrite:/ˈfɔːrˌraɪt/ /ˈfɔːrˌraɪt/
n.|前言，序言|An introductory piece of writing, often by a person other than the author, that appears at the beginning of a book.|The book has a foreword by the author's mentor.|Synonym: preface, introduction, prologue|Antonym: afterword, epilogue|noun
forewrite:/fɔːrˈraɪt/ /fɔːrˈraɪt/
v.|为…写序|To write a foreword for (a book).|The famous historian forewrote the young author's debut novel.|Synonym: preface, introduce|Antonym: conclude, end|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *forewriten*, from Old English *forewrītan* (“to write before, preface”), from *fore-* (“before”) + *wrītan* (“to write”). Equivalent to fore- +‎ write.
