discriminatingly:/dɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪtɪŋli/| adv.|有识别力地，有鉴别力地，歧视地|Showing good taste or judgment; discerning.  Can also mean unfairly treating a person or group differently.|She dressed discriminatingly, always choosing elegant and stylish outfits. 她穿着很有品味，总是选择优雅时尚的服装。|The company was accused of discriminatingly hiring practices.  该公司被指控招聘过程中存在歧视。|近义词：judiciously, discerningly, selectively, astutely (positive); prejudicially, unfairly (negative)|反义词：indiscriminately, randomly, blindly|词性:副词

discriminating:/dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ/| adj.|有识别力的，有鉴别力的，歧视的|Having or showing refined taste or good judgment.|She has a discriminating palate for wine. 她对葡萄酒有很高的品味。|近义词：discerning, selective, judicious, astute|反义词：indiscriminate, uncritical|词性:形容词

discriminate:/dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪt/| v.|区分，辨别，歧视|Recognize a distinction; differentiate.  Also, to make an unjust or prejudicial distinction in the treatment of different categories of people or things.|It's important to discriminate between fact and opinion. 区分事实和观点很重要。|It is illegal to discriminate against someone based on their race or religion.  基于种族或宗教歧视某人是非法的。|近义词：differentiate, distinguish, separate (for distinguishing);  prejudice, bias (for discriminating against)|反义词：confuse, mix up (for distinguishing); treat equally (for discriminating against)|词性:动词

词源: From Late Latin discriminat- ‘distinguished,’ from the verb discriminare, from Latin discrimen ‘interval, difference.’
