loss-leaders:/ˈlɒs ˌliːdəz/ (US) /ˈlɒs ˌliːdəz/ (UK)| n.|亏本促销商品；特价商品|Products sold at a loss to attract customers.|Supermarkets often use loss-leaders, like milk or bread, to draw shoppers in. 超市经常使用像牛奶或面包这样的亏本促销商品来吸引顾客。

The electronics store advertised loss-leaders on Black Friday to attract a large crowd. 这家电子产品商店在黑色星期五做亏本促销广告来吸引大量人群。|近义词：promotional items, specials, deals|反义词：premium products, high-end goods|词性:名词

词源:  "Loss leader" originated in the late 19th or early 20th century. It refers to the idea of "leading" customers into a store with the "loss" incurred on the low-priced item, hoping they will purchase other, more profitable goods.
