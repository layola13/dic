evasion:/ɪˈveɪʒn/| n.|逃避，规避，躲避|The act of evading or avoiding something.  The defendant was charged with tax evasion. 被告被控逃税。|例句：His evasion of his responsibilities is unacceptable. 他逃避责任的行为是不可接受的。

例句：She practiced evasion by refusing to answer direct questions. 她通过拒绝回答直接问题来练习逃避。

|近义词：avoidance, dodging, eluding, escaping, shirking|反义词:confrontation, facing, meeting|词性:名词

evasive:/ɪˈveɪsɪv/| adj.|逃避的，推诿的，含糊其辞的|Tending or intended to evade; avoiding commitment or self-revelation, especially by responding only indirectly. The politician gave an evasive answer to the journalist's question. 这位政治家对记者的提问含糊其辞。|例句：He was being evasive about his plans for the future. 他对未来的计划闪烁其词。

例句：Her evasive answers only increased their suspicions. 她含糊其辞的回答反而加深了他们的怀疑。

|近义词：ambiguous, elusive, equivocal, indirect|反义词:direct, forthright, honest, open|词性:形容词
