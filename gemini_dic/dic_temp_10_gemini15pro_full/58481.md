dudgeon:/ˈdʌdʒən/
n.|愤慨，愠怒|A feeling of offense or resentment; anger.|He stormed off in high dudgeon. 他怒气冲冲地离开了。|Near Synonyms: indignation, resentment, anger, wrath, ire, pique.|Near Antonyms: calmness, composure, equanimity, pleasure, happiness|noun

dudgeon:/ˈdʌdʒən/
n.|柄，把柄(古语，现在很少用)|A hilt or handle, especially of a dagger.|The knight grasped the dudgeon of his sword. 骑士握紧了剑柄。(古语用法，现代英语中很少使用)|Near Synonyms: hilt, handle, grip|Near Antonyms: N/A (This meaning is archaic and lacks a direct antonym)|noun
