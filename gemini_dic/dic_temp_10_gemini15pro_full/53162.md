dipoles:/ˈdaɪpoʊlz/ (US), /ˈdaɪpəʊlz/ (UK)| n. |偶极子|A dipole is a pair of equal and oppositely charged or magnetized poles separated by a distance.  It refers to a separation of charge or magnetic poles. In chemistry, it's often used to describe the polarity of a molecule.|The water molecule is a dipole because of the difference in electronegativity between oxygen and hydrogen.|Synonyms: polar molecules (in chemistry context)|Antonyms: nonpolar molecules (in chemistry context)|noun
