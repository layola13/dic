blackface: /ˈblækˌfeɪs/ (US) /ˈblækˌfeɪs/ (UK) | n. | 黑脸妆，涂黑脸扮黑人 | The act of a non-black person wearing makeup to imitate the appearance of a black person, often for comedic or theatrical purposes, historically associated with racist caricatures and stereotypes. |  Minstrel shows, featuring white performers in blackface, were a popular form of entertainment in the 19th century.  涂黑脸的吟游诗人表演是19世纪一种流行的娱乐形式。

The use of blackface is now widely considered offensive and inappropriate.  现在，使用黑脸妆被广泛认为是冒犯和不恰当的。 | 近义词：minstrelsy | 反义词：N/A | 词性：名词
