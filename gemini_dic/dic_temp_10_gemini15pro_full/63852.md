epigrammatisms: /ˌɛpɪˈɡræmətɪzəmz/ | n. | 警句集; 警句体; | The use of epigrammatisms can make one's writing more memorable. |  Epigrammatisms are short, witty sayings, often paradoxical or satirical.  They are a collection or an instance of using epigrams.  Think of them as concise, pointed remarks that stick in your mind. |  近义词:  aphorisms, maxims, witticisms, quips | 反义词: platitudes, clichés, banalities | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From the Greek "epigramma" (inscription), via Latin "epigramma" and French "épigramme," ultimately from the Greek "epi-" (upon) + "graphein" (to write).  The "-ism" suffix denotes a characteristic or practice.
