orchestration:/ɔːr.kɪˈstreɪ.ʃən/ |ɔːrˈkes.treɪ.ʃən/| n. |编曲，配器；精心安排；协调|The orchestration of the symphony was complex and beautiful. 这部交响曲的编曲复杂而优美。|The government's orchestration of the economic recovery was widely praised. 政府对经济复苏的精心安排受到广泛赞扬。| arrangement, organization, coordination | improvisation, disorganization | noun

Etymology:
From French *orchestration*, from *orchestrer* "to orchestrate," from *orchestre* "orchestra."  Ultimately from Greek *orkhēstra* "place for dancing," later "space in front of the stage," from *orkheisthai* "to dance."
