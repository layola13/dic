far-off:/ˌfɑːr ˈɒf/ /ˌfɑːr ˈɔːf/ | adj. |遥远的；久远的|Situated at a great distance; remote in time or space.|例句：She dreamed of traveling to far-off lands. 她梦想着去遥远的国度旅行。|近义词：distant, remote, faraway|反义词：near, nearby, close|词性:形容词

far-off:/ˌfɑːr ˈɒf/ /ˌfɑːr ˈɔːf/ | n. |远方|A distant place or time.|例句：The far-off seemed to call to her adventurous spirit. 远方似乎在召唤她冒险的精神。|近义词：distance|反义词：nearness, proximity|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From far + off.  "Far" traces back to Old English "feorr," meaning "at a distance." "Off" comes from Old English "of," meaning "away from, off."
