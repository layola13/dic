crossjack:/ˈkrɒsˌdʒæk/ (UK) /ˈkrɔːsˌdʒæk/ (US)| n.| 后下帆；后下桁|A sail set on the lowermost yard on the mizzenmast of a square-rigged ship.|The crossjack was torn in the storm. 后下帆在暴风雨中被撕裂了。|Synonyms: mizzen course, driver|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a specific sail)|noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *cros-jack, from cross + jack (a diminutive of John, used generically for "man, fellow," and applied to various objects).  The "cross" likely refers to the yard's position across the ship, or possibly its relationship to the mast.
