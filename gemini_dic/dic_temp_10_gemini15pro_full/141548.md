opposable:/əˈpoʊzəbl/
adjective
可相对的，对生的
Capable of being placed opposite something else, especially of a thumb that can be moved to touch the other fingers on the same hand.
Humans have opposable thumbs, allowing them to grasp objects. 人类有对生拇指，可以抓握物体。

opposable:/əˈpoʊzəbl/
adjective
对握的
(especially of the thumb) able to be moved so that it touches each of the other fingers on the same hand, making it possible to grasp things.
Monkeys use their opposable thumbs to swing through trees. 猴子用它们的对生拇指在树上荡来荡去。

opposable:/əˈpoʊzəbl/
adjective
可对置的
Can be placed opposite to something else.
The opposable nature of the human thumb is crucial for tool use. 人类拇指的可对置性对于使用工具至关重要。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *opponibilis, from Latin oppōnere (to oppose).  From ob- (against) + ponere (to place).

SYNONYMS->
prehensile, opposing

ANTONYMS->
non-opposable
