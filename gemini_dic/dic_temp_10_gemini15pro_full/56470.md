dolphins:/ˈdɒlfɪnz/ (UK) /ˈdɑːlfɪnz/ (US)| n. |海豚|A highly intelligent marine mammal related to whales and porpoises, known for its playful behavior and ability to communicate through a variety of clicks and whistles.|Dolphins are known for their intelligence. 海豚以它们的智慧而闻名。

Dolphins can be trained to perform tricks. 海豚可以被训练表演技巧。


近义词:porpoises (鼠海豚，近亲), cetaceans (鲸类动物，更广泛的类别)
反义词:None (没有直接的反义词)
词性:Noun


Etymology: From Middle English *dolfin, from Old French daufin, from Medieval Latin delphinus, from Latin delphin, from Ancient Greek δελφίς (delphís, “dolphin”).  The ultimate origin is uncertain, but possibly related to the Greek δελφύς (delphús, “womb”), perhaps referring to its mammalian nature.
词源：源自中古英语 *dolfin，来自古法语 daufin，来自中世纪拉丁语 delphinus，来自拉丁语 delphin，来自古希腊语 δελφίς (delphís，“海豚”)。最终起源不确定，但可能与希腊语 δελφύς (delphús，“子宫”)有关，可能指的是它的哺乳动物性质。
