ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clucchen, from Old English *clyccan (“to clench, grasp”), from Proto-Germanic *klukjaną (“to bend, hook”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)klewk- (“to bend, curve”). Cognate with Dutch klauwen (“to claw, scratch, clutch”), German Kluft (“cleft, chasm”), dialectal German klitzen (“to clutch”).
