garland:/ˈɡɑːrlənd/ | n. | 花环，花冠，奖赏 | A wreath of flowers and leaves, worn on the head or hung as a decoration; an award or honor. | The bride wore a garland of white roses. 新娘戴着白色玫瑰花环。 | synonyms: wreath, chaplet, crown, festoon, lei | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical object, but conceptually: blemish, disgrace) | noun

garland:/ˈɡɑːrlənd/ | vt. | 用花环装饰，授予荣誉 | To decorate with a garland; to bestow an honor or award. | They garlanded the winning horse. 他们给获胜的马戴上花环。 | synonyms: decorate, adorn, festoon, wreathe, crown | antonyms: strip, denude | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *garlande, likely from Frankish *wirinda, related to Old High German wiara "ornament."
