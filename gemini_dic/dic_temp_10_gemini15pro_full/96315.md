indigestiblenesses: /ˌɪndɪˈdʒɛstɪblənəsɪz/
n.
难以消化；消化不良；难理解
The state or quality of being indigestible.  This refers to something being difficult or impossible to digest, either literally (food) or figuratively (information).
His lectures were filled with indigestiblenesses that left the students confused. 他的讲座充满了难以理解的内容，让学生们感到困惑。
近义词: indigestibility, unpalatability
反义词: digestibility, palatability
词性: 名词

The state or quality of being indigestible.  This refers to something being difficult or impossible to digest, either literally (food) or figuratively (information).
The indigestibleness of the legal jargon made it hard for the jury to understand the case.  法律术语的晦涩难懂使得陪审团难以理解案情。
近义词: indigestibility, unpalatability

词性: 名词


词源: From indigestible + -ness.  Indigestible comes from in- (not) + digestible (capable of being digested), which in turn comes from the Latin digestus, the past participle of digerere (to separate, distribute, digest).
