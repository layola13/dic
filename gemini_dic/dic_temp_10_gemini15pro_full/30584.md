cercaria:/sərˈkɛəriə/| n. |尾蚴|The larval form of a parasitic fluke (trematode worm) that develops in a snail and typically has a tail.  It is a free-swimming stage that seeks out a second intermediate host (e.g., a fish) or the definitive host (e.g., a human).|The cercaria penetrates the skin of the host.|近义词: larva (in a broader sense)|反义词: adult (of the fluke)|词性:noun
