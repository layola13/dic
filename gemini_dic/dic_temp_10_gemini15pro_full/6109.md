amerinds:/əˈmɛrɪndz/
|n.|美洲印第安人|A term used to refer to the indigenous peoples of the Americas, now generally considered outdated and potentially offensive due to its association with colonialism. It combines "Ameri" from "America" and "inds" from "Indians".|Many scholars now avoid the term "amerinds" and prefer more specific designations like "Cherokee" or "Inuit."  许多学者现在避免使用“amerinds”一词，而更喜欢更具体的名称，例如“切罗基人”或“因纽特人”。|Synonyms: American Indians, Native Americans, Indigenous peoples of the Americas|Antonyms: None (as it refers to a specific group of people)|Noun
