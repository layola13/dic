一般现在时 (Simple Present):
I eat breakfast every day.  我每天都吃早餐。

I go to school by bus. 我坐公共汽车上学。


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
I am watching TV now. 我现在正在看电视。

She is playing the piano. 她正在弹钢琴。


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
I have finished my homework. 我已经完成了我的作业。

They have traveled to many countries. 他们已经去过很多国家。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
I have been studying English for three years. 我已经学习英语三年了。

He has been working on this project for months. 他已经在这个项目上工作好几个月了。


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
I went to the cinema yesterday. 我昨天去看电影了。

She ate pizza for lunch. 她午餐吃了披萨。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
I was reading a book when the phone rang. 电话响的时候我正在读书。

They were playing football in the park. 他们在公园里踢足球。


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
I had finished my homework before I went out. 我出去之前已经完成了我的作业。

She had already left when I arrived. 我到的时候她已经离开了。


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
I had been waiting for an hour before the bus arrived. 我等了一个小时，公共汽车才到。

They had been discussing the issue for weeks. 他们已经讨论这个问题好几个星期了。


一般将来时 (Simple Future):
I will go to the beach tomorrow. 我明天要去海滩。

They will visit their grandparents next week. 他们下周要去看望他们的祖父母。


将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
I will be watching the game this time tomorrow. 明天这个时候我将会在看比赛。

She will be attending the conference next week. 她下周将会参加会议。


将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
I will have finished the project by next month. 我将在下个月之前完成这个项目。

They will have moved to a new house by the end of the year. 他们将在年底前搬到新房子。


将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
I will have been studying English for five years by next year. 到明年，我将已经学习英语五年了。

She will have been working at this company for ten years by next July. 到明年七月，她将已经在这家公司工作十年了。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would accept the offer. 如果我是你，我会接受这个提议。

I wish I had more time to travel. 我希望我有更多的时间去旅行。

被动语态:
The cake was eaten by the children. 蛋糕被孩子们吃掉了。

The problem is being investigated by the police. 警察正在调查这个问题。

疑问句:
What time is it? 现在几点了？

Where are you going? 你要去哪里？

否定句:
I don't like coffee. 我不喜欢咖啡。

She didn't go to the party. 她没有去参加派对。

注意事项:  'im'ing is not standard English and therefore doesn't have a formal phonetic transcription in dictionaries.  It's best avoided in formal writing.
