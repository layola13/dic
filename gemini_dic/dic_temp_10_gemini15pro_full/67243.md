expulsionist: /ɪkˈspʌlʃənɪst/
n.
驱逐论者，主张驱逐者
Someone who advocates expulsion, especially of a particular group of people from a country.
The expulsionist rhetoric fueled tensions between the two communities. 驱逐论者的言论加剧了两个社群之间的紧张关系。
The government's expulsionist policies led to widespread protests. 政府的驱逐政策导致了广泛的抗议。
近义词: deportationist,  advocate of expulsion
反义词: inclusionist, integrationist
词性: noun

Etymology:
From expulsion + -ist.  Expulsion comes from Late Middle English: from Old French expulsion, from Latin expulsio(n-), from the verb expellere (see expel).  The suffix -ist denotes a person who practices or advocates something.
