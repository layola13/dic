facecrime:/ˈfeɪskraɪm/| n. | 面部表情罪 |  The act of displaying an inappropriate or illegal facial expression, especially in a dystopian society where emotions and their outward expression are strictly controlled.  This is a term coined by George Orwell in his novel *Nineteen Eighty-Four*. |  In Oceania, even a fleeting look of discontent could be interpreted as facecrime. 在大洋国，即使是一闪而过的不满表情也可能被解释为面部表情罪。|  thoughtcrime |  (No direct antonym) | Noun
