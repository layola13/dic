ankylostomiasis:/ˌæŋkɪloʊstoʊˈmaɪəsɪs/ | n. | 钩虫病 | An infection of the intestines with hookworms, characterized by anemia, abdominal pain, and diarrhea.  It's a type of helminthiasis, specifically caused by Ancylostoma duodenale or Necator americanus. |  Ancylostomiasis is a serious health problem in many developing countries. 钩虫病在许多发展中国家是一个严重的健康问题。|  The doctor diagnosed him with ancylostomiasis after examining his stool sample.  医生检查了他的粪便样本后，诊断他患有钩虫病。 | Synonyms: hookworm disease, uncinariasis | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a disease, but one could say "health" or "wellness" as an opposite state.) | Noun
