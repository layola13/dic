buffoonism:/bəˈfuːnɪzəm/
n.
滑稽可笑的行为；丑角行径
The behavior of acting like a buffoon; clowning or ludicrous behavior.
His buffoonism entertained the children, but annoyed the adults. 他的滑稽动作逗乐了孩子们，却惹恼了大人们。

近义词:clowning, tomfoolery, silliness, antics, absurdity
反义词:seriousness, solemnity, dignity, gravity
词性:noun

buffoon:/bəˈfuːn/
n.
小丑；丑角
A person who amuses others by clowning, jesting, and buffoonery.
He acted the buffoon to get a laugh. 他扮小丑来博人一笑。
近义词:clown, jester, fool, comedian
反义词:sage, intellectual
词性:noun

buffoonery:/bəˈfuːnəri/
n.
滑稽可笑的行为；丑角行径
Behavior that is ridiculous but amusing.
The crowd loved his buffoonery.  人群喜欢他的滑稽表演。
近义词:clowning, tomfoolery, silliness, antics
反义词:seriousness, solemnity, dignity
词性:noun



词源:
From French *bouffonerie*, from Italian *buffoneria*, from *buffone* "clown," of uncertain origin. Possibly from Late Latin *buffare* "to puff," referring to the puffed-out cheeks of clowns.
