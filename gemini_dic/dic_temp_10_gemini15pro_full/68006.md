factive:/ˈfæktɪv/| adj.|事实的；预设的；以事实为基础的|An adjective describing a verb or predicate that presupposes the truth of its complement clause.  This means that the speaker, by using a factive verb or predicate, asserts the truth of the embedded clause.|例句：The teacher regretted that the student had cheated. (The teacher asserts that the student cheated.) 老师很后悔学生作弊了。（老师断言学生作弊了。）

例句：It's a shame that he lost the race. (The speaker asserts that he lost the race.) 很遗憾他输掉了比赛。(说话者断言他输掉了比赛。)|近义词：presuppositional|反义词:non-factive|词性:adjective
