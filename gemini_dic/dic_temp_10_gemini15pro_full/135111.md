nonobstructively:/ˌnɑːnəbˈstrʌktɪvli/
| adv. |无阻碍地，不引人注目地|In a way that does not block or get in the way of something or someone; inconspicuously. |The nurse nonobstructively monitored the patient's vital signs.  护士不引人注目地监测着病人的生命体征。|Synonyms: discreetly, subtly, unobtrusively, inconspicuously |Antonyms: obstructively, conspicuously, noticeably|adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + obstructive (causing or tending to cause obstruction) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Obstructive" comes from Latin *obstructus*, past participle of obstruere (to build against, block up), from ob- (against) + struere (to build).
