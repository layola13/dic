inconscionable:/ɪnˈkɒnʃənəbl/ (US) /ɪnˈkɒnʃ(ə)nəbl/ (UK)| adj.|不合理的，过分的，昧着良心的|Not right or reasonable; unreasonably excessive; showing no regard for what is right or reasonable.|例句：The price they charged was utterly inconscionable. 他们收取的价格简直太离谱了。|例句：It is inconscionable that so much food is wasted when millions are starving. 数百万人挨饿的时候，这么多食物被浪费掉，真是太不应该了。|近义词：unreasonable, excessive, outrageous, immoral, unethical|反义词：reasonable, fair, just, ethical, moral|词性:形容词

词源:
Late Middle English: from Old French inconscionable, from in- ‘not’ + conscionable (see conscionable), or from medieval Latin inconscionabilis.
