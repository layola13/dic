expedience:/ɪkˈspiːdiəns/ /ɛkˈspiːdiəns/| n.|权宜之计；方便；有利|the quality of being convenient and practical despite possibly being improper or immoral; a means to an end; an expedient action or solution|例句：For the sake of expediency, we'll have to postpone the meeting. 为了权宜之计，我们将不得不推迟会议。

例句：He acted out of expediency, not principle. 他出于权宜之计，而非原则行事。|近义词：convenience, practicality, advantage, opportunism|反义词：principle, morality, ethics|词性:名词

expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/ /ɛkˈspiːdiənt/| adj. n.|权宜的；方便的；有利的；权宜之计| (of an action) convenient and practical although possibly improper or immoral; (n.) a means to an end; a convenient and practical way of achieving something|例句：It is expedient to do so. 这样做是权宜之计。

例句：The government chose the most politically expedient solution. 政府选择了政治上最权宜的解决方案。|近义词：convenient, practical, advantageous, opportune|反义词：inconvenient, impractical, disadvantageous, inappropriate|词性:形容词，名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French expedient, or from Latin expediens, expedient- ‘disentangling, profitable’, present participle of expedire (see expedite).
