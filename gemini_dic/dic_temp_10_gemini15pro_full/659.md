abrogation:/ˌæbrəˈɡeɪʃən/ | n. | 废除，废止 | The abrogation of the treaty led to renewed hostilities. 该条约的废除导致了敌对行动的重新开始。 | The government announced the abrogation of the old law. 政府宣布废除旧法律。 | synonyms: repeal, revocation, annulment, cancellation | antonyms: enactment, ratification, establishment | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin abrogatio, from Latin abrogare "to repeal," from ab- "away" + rogare "to ask, propose a law."

USAGE->
The abrogation of the agreement was a major setback.  这项协议的废除是一个重大的挫折。

The unilateral abrogation of the contract caused a lot of problems. 单方面废除合同造成了很多问题。

The king's abrogation of the feudal system was met with resistance. 国王废除封建制度的举动遭到了抵制。

Parliament voted for the abrogation of the outdated law. 议会投票废除了过时的法律。

The abrogation of their rights led to widespread protests.  他们权利的被废除导致了广泛的抗议。

The company is seeking the abrogation of the current regulations. 该公司正在寻求废除现行法规。



PHRASE->
the abrogation of a law = 法律的废除
the abrogation of a treaty = 条约的废除
the abrogation of a contract = 合同的废除
the abrogation of a right = 权利的废除
the abrogation of a custom = 习俗的废除
