contemptibleness:/kənˈtɛmptɪbəlnəs/
n.
卑鄙；可鄙；下贱
The state or quality of being contemptible; deserving contempt.
His contemptibleness was evident in his deceitful actions. 他的卑鄙行为显而易见。
The contemptibleness of the politician's lies shocked the nation.  这位政客谎言的卑鄙令全国震惊。
近义词：despicableness, vileness, baseness
反义词：nobleness, worthiness, respectability
词性: noun

contemptible:/kənˈtɛmptɪbl/
adj.
卑鄙的；可鄙的；下贱的
Deserving contempt; despicable.
He made a contemptible remark about her appearance. 他对她的外貌发表了卑鄙的评论。
His contemptible behavior cost him his job and his reputation. 他卑鄙的行为使他失去了工作和名誉。
近义词：despicable, vile, base, mean
反义词：noble, worthy, respectable, admirable
词性: adjective

contempt:/kənˈtɛmpt/
n.
轻蔑；蔑视；鄙视
The feeling that a person or a thing is beneath consideration, worthless, or deserving scorn.
She looked at him with contempt. 她轻蔑地看着他。
He felt nothing but contempt for the corrupt officials. 他对腐败官员只有鄙视。
近义词：scorn, disdain, derision
反义词：respect, admiration, esteem
词性: noun
