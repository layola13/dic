disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/ | n. vt. | 幻灭，醒悟 | Disappointment resulting from the discovery that something is not as good as one believed it to be. |  He experienced a sense of disillusion when he realized his childhood hero was a fraud. 他意识到他儿时的英雄是个骗子后，他感到幻灭。 | synonyms: disenchantment, disappointment, disillusionment | antonyms:  enchantment, hope, idealism | noun

disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/ | vt. | 使醒悟，使不再抱幻想 | To free from illusion or false belief. | The harsh realities of war disillusioned many young soldiers. 战争的残酷现实使许多年轻士兵不再抱有幻想。 | synonyms: disenchant, undeceive | antonyms:  deceive, delude | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desillusiounen*, from des- (expressing removal) + illusion.  Ultimately from Latin illusio (“mockery”).
