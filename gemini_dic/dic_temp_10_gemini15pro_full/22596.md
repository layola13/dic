bootstrap:/ˈbuːtstræp/ | n. v. | 引导程序；自举；靴带 | a looped strap sewn at the side or rear top of a boot, used for pulling it on.  Also, something that helps a person or organization to improve their situation, especially without help from others. |  He pulled on his bootstraps and stood up. 他拉起靴带站了起来。| synonyms:  kickstart, jump-start, self-starter,  | antonyms: hindrance, obstacle | noun

bootstrap:/ˈbuːtstræp/ | v. | 引导；自举；靠自身努力获得成功 | to start up (something) by oneself, with minimal resources or outside assistance | The company bootstrapped its way to success with just a small loan. 这家公司仅凭一小笔贷款就靠自身努力获得了成功。 | synonyms: self-start, self-fund | antonyms: outsource, crowdsource | verb
