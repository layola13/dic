abate:/əˈbeɪt/ (US) /əˈbeɪt/ (UK)| v. |减轻，减少，缓和|to become less strong; to make something less strong|The storm began to abate.  暴风雨开始减弱。

The pain in his leg abated after he took the medicine.  他服药后腿痛减轻了。|近义词：decrease, diminish, lessen, subside, wane|反义词：increase, intensify, aggravate|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *abaten*, from Old French *abatre* (“to beat down, cast down, demolish”), from *a-* (from Latin *ad-*) + *batre* (“to beat”).

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The noise abates when the traffic dies down. =  交通减少时噪音就会减弱。
His enthusiasm never abates. = 他的热情从未减退。

一般过去时:
The floodwaters abated after a few days. = 几天后洪水消退了。
The wind abated, and the sailing became smoother. = 风变小了，航行变得更加平稳。

现在完成时:
The fever has abated somewhat. = 高烧已经有所减轻。
The protests have abated since the government's announcement. = 自从政府宣布以来，抗议活动已经平息。

过去完成时:
The storm had abated before we reached the coast. = 我们到达海岸之前，暴风雨已经减弱了。
The pain had abated significantly by the time the doctor arrived. = 在医生到达之前，疼痛已经明显减轻。

将来时:
The doctor believes the swelling will abate soon. = 医生相信肿胀很快就会消退。
We hope the crisis will abate in the coming weeks. = 我们希望危机在未来几周内能够缓解。


被动语态:
The problem can be abated by proper medication. =  通过适当的药物治疗可以缓解这个问题。
The noise was abated by the soundproofing. = 隔音措施降低了噪音。
