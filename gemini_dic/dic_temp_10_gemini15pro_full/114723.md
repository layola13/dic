mallophaga:/məˈlɒfəɡə/
| n. |嚼虱目；羽虱；鸟虱|An order of insects, now generally considered part of Phthiraptera, that are parasitic on birds and mammals.  They have chewing mouthparts and feed on feathers, hair, and skin debris.|Mallophaga are a common parasite of poultry.  嚼虱是家禽的常见寄生虫。|Synonyms: biting lice, bird lice|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a taxonomic order)
|noun
