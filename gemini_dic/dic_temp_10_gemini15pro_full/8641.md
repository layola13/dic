anticonfederationist:/ˌæntiˌkənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪst/ | n. | 反联邦主义者 |  A person who opposed the adoption of the United States Constitution.  They feared a strong central government and advocated for states' rights. | Example: Anticonfederationists argued that the Constitution lacked a bill of rights. 反联邦主义者认为宪法缺乏权利法案。

Example:  Many Anticonfederationists later joined the Democratic-Republican Party. 许多反联邦主义者后来加入了民主共和党。| 近义词:States' rights advocate, Antifederalist | 反义词: Federalist, Confederationist, Nationalist | 词性: noun
