moderated:/ˈmɒdəreɪtɪd/ | adj. | 缓和的，适度的，主持的 | made or become less extreme, intense, rigorous, or violent;  presided over a discussion or meeting | The chairman moderated the debate. 主席主持了辩论。| 近义词: tempered, controlled, chaired, mediated | 反义词: extreme, intensified, aggravated | 词性:形容词
Etymology: From Latin moderatus, past participle of moderari "to regulate, restrain," from modus "measure, manner."
