hobbling:/ˈhɒblɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːblɪŋ/ (US)| v. n. adj.|蹒跚；跛行；阻碍；束缚|To walk with difficulty, typically because of an injury to the foot or leg; also, to restrict the activity or development of something.|例句：The old man was hobbling along the road with a cane. 老人拄着拐杖在路上蹒跚而行。|近义词：limping, shuffling, stumbling, hindering, restricting|反义词:striding, running, facilitating, aiding|词性:verb (primarily), noun (less common), adjective (less common)

hobbling:/ˈhɒblɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːblɪŋ/ (US)| n.|跛行；束缚|The act of walking with difficulty or the act of restricting something.|例句：The hobbling of the economy due to sanctions has been severe.  由于制裁，经济受到了严重束缚。|近义词：limp, restriction, hindrance|反义词:stride, run, facilitation, aid|词性:noun

hobbling:/ˈhɒblɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːblɪŋ/ (US)| adj.|阻碍的；束缚的|Descriptive of something that hobbles or restricts.|例句：The hobbling regulations prevented the company from expanding. 这些限制性规定阻止了公司的扩张。|近义词：restrictive, hindering, limiting|反义词:facilitating, aiding, helpful|词性:adjective
