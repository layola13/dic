inknot:/ˈɪŋknɒt/ | n. | 墨水污渍，墨水结块 | A dried blob or smear of ink. | The old manuscript was covered in inknots and difficult to read. 这份古老的手稿上满是墨渍，难以阅读。 | Example:  I accidentally made an inknot on my important document. 我不小心在我的重要文件上弄上了一个墨点。| Synonyms: blot, smear, smudge | Antonyms: cleanness, neatness | noun

inknot:/ˈɪŋknɒt/ | n. | 困难，复杂局面（尤指写作中的） | A complicated or difficult situation, especially in writing.  |  The plot of the novel was tied up in such an inknot that it was hard to follow. 这部小说的情节如此复杂，以至于很难理解。 | Example: The author wrote himself into an inknot and had to rewrite the entire chapter. 作者把自己写进了一个死胡同，不得不重写整章。| Synonyms: tangle, muddle, snarl | Antonyms: clarity, simplicity | noun
