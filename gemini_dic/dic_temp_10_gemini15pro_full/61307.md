embarkment:/ɪmˈbɑːrkment/ /ɛmˈbɑːrkment/| n. |堤防，码头|A structure built along a river or other body of water to prevent flooding or erosion, or to provide a landing place for ships.| The city constructed a new embarkment to protect against rising sea levels.  这座城市建造了一座新的堤防以抵御海平面上升。|近义词：levee, wharf, pier, quay|反义词: None|词性: Noun

embarkment:/ɪmˈbɑːrkment/ /ɛmˈbɑːrkment/| n. |上船，登机|The act of boarding a ship or aircraft.|The embarkment of the passengers was delayed due to bad weather. 由于天气恶劣，乘客登机被延误了。|近义词：boarding, mounting|反义词: disembarkment|词性: Noun
