overstraightness:/ˌoʊvərˈstreɪtnəs/| n. |过度笔直，矫枉过正|The overstraightness of the lines made the drawing look stiff and unnatural. 线条过于笔直，使绘图看起来僵硬而不自然。|例句：The overstraightness of her posture suggested discomfort. 她过于笔直的姿势表明她感到不舒服。

Her overstraightness in applying the rules alienated her colleagues.  她在应用规则方面的矫枉过正疏远了她的同事。|近义词：rigidity, stiffness, inflexibility|反义词: flexibility, looseness, curvature|词性:noun


Etymology:
"over" + "straight" + "-ness"

"over-"  Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi (source also of Old Saxon obar, Old Frisian over, Old Norse yfir, Dutch over, Old High German ubar, German über, Gothic ufar “over, above”).

"straight" Old English streht "stretched, tight, direct," from Proto-Germanic *strakhtaz (source also of Old Norse strekkt "stretched," Old Saxon straht, Dutch strak "tight," Old High German strac, German strack "stiff, rigid"), past participle of *strekjanan "to stretch" (see stretch (v.)).

"-ness" forming abstract nouns from adjectives and participles, from Old English -nes(s), -nis(s), -nys(s), common Germanic suffix (cognates: Old Saxon and Old Frisian -nissi, Old Norse -neskja, Dutch -nis, German -nis, Gothic -inassus).
