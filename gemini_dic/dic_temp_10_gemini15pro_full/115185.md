manichee:/ˈmænɪkiː/
noun|摩尼教徒；二元论者；绝对善恶论者|A believer in Manichaeism, a religion that posited a cosmic struggle between good and evil.|He was accused of being a Manichee, seeing the world in black and white.|synonyms: dualist, absolutist|antonyms: pluralist, relativist|n.
manichean:/ˌmænɪˈkiːən/
adjective|摩尼教的；二元论的；绝对善恶论的|Relating to Manichaeism, or characterized by a dualistic worldview that sees a cosmic struggle between good and evil.|His Manichean worldview prevented him from seeing the nuances of the situation.|synonyms: dualistic, absolutist|antonyms: pluralist, relativist|adj.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Manichaeus*, from Greek Μανιχαῖος (Manikhaîos), from Aramaic mānīhāyā ("Manichean"), from Mānī, the prophet who founded Manichaeism.
