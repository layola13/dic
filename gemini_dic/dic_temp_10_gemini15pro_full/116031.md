marsupion:/mɑːrˈsuːpiən/ | n. | 袋鼠；有袋动物 | A marsupial, especially an American opossum.  This word is archaic and rarely used. |  The naturalist studied the marsupion in its native habitat.  博物学家在其原生栖息地研究了这种有袋动物。| 近义词: marsupial, opossum | 反义词: placental mammal (Although not a direct opposite, it highlights the key difference in reproductive strategy) | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *marsupium, from Ancient Greek μαρσύπιον (marsúpion, “pouch, purse”).
