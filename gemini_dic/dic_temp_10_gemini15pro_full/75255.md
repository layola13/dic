fromwards:/frɒmwədz/ | adv. | 从...向前；从...往外 | Moving onward or outward from a specified place or time. |  He walked fromwards the house. 他从房子那里往前走。 |  She looked fromwards the horizon. 她向地平线望去。| 近义词: onward, forward, outward | 反义词: backwards, inwards | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
"fromwards" is a combination of "from" and the suffix "-wards," indicating direction.  "From" has its roots in Old English "fram," meaning "away from, off."  "-wards" derives from Old English "-weardes," denoting direction of motion.
