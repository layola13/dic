naturalising:/ˈnætʃrəlaɪzɪŋ/ /ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪzɪŋ/
verb
使归化；使入籍；移植；使适应
The process of becoming a citizen of a country that you were not born in.  Making something foreign become a natural part of something. Introducing a plant or animal to a new region and helping it to become established there.
Many immigrants are naturalising as citizens. 许多移民正在归化入籍。
The gardeners are naturalising the exotic plants in the local soil. 园丁们正在使这些外来植物适应当地土壤。
近义词:acclimatizing, adapting, assimilating, integrating
反义词:denaturalizing, expatriating
词性:v.


naturalised:/ˈnætʃrəlaɪzd/ /ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪzd/
adjective
归化的；入籍的；移植的；适应的
Having become a citizen of a country that you were not born in.  Having adapted to a new environment.
He is a naturalised British citizen. 他是英国的归化公民。
The naturalised plants thrived in the new climate. 移植的植物在新气候下茁壮成长。
近义词:acclimatized, adapted, assimilated, integrated
反义词:foreign, alien
词性:adj.


naturalisation:/ˌnætʃrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ /ˌnætʃrəˌlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
noun
归化；入籍；移植；适应
The process of becoming a citizen of a country that you were not born in. The introduction of a plant or animal to a new region. The process of adapting to a new environment.
She applied for naturalisation after living in the country for five years.  她在该国居住五年后申请了入籍。
The naturalisation of the new species was successful. 新物种的移植是成功的。
近义词:acclimatization, adaptation, assimilation, integration
反义词:denaturalization, expatriation
词性:n.
