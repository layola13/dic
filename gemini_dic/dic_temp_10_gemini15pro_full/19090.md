ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *betrouthe*, from Old French *betrother*, from *be-* (intensive prefix) + *trother* "to pledge, promise," from Frankish * *truwa* "truce, covenant," from Proto-Germanic * *treuwō* "faith, loyalty, covenant."  Cognate with Old English *trēowth* "faith, fidelity," Old High German *triuwa* "faith, loyalty," Gothic *triggws* "faithful."  The noun *betrothment* is derived from the verb *betroth*.
