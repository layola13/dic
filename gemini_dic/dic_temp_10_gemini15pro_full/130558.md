nonacquisitiveness:/ˌnɒnˌækwɪˈzɪtɪvnəs/
n.
不贪婪，无占有欲
Lack of desire to acquire possessions or wealth; contentment with what one has.
He lived a life of voluntary nonacquisitiveness, finding joy in simple things. 他过着自愿的简朴生活，在简单的事物中寻找快乐。

Buddhist monks embrace nonacquisitiveness as a core principle. 佛教僧侣将不贪婪作为核心原则。

Synonyms:  unselfishness, detachment, contentment, asceticism
Antonyms: acquisitiveness, greed, materialism, avarice


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + acquisitive (having a strong desire to acquire things) + -ness (forming nouns expressing a state or quality).  The root of "acquisitive" comes from the Latin "acquirere" meaning "to acquire."
