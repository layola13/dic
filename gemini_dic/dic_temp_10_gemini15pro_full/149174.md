oxyammonia:/ˌɒksiəˈmoʊniə/ | n. | 羟胺 | A compound of nitrogen, hydrogen, and oxygen, NH₂OH, usually prepared by reducing nitrates or nitrites.  It is a colorless crystalline solid that is unstable and explosive. | Hydroxylamine is used as a reducing agent in various chemical reactions. 羟胺在各种化学反应中用作还原剂。| Example: Oxyammonia can react violently with oxidizing agents. 羟胺可以与氧化剂发生剧烈反应。 | Synonym: hydroxylamine | Antonym: N/A | noun
