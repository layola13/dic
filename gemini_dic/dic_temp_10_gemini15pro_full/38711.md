condignity:/kənˈdɪɡnɪti/ | n. |适当性；相当性；恰当的惩罚|Appropriateness of something, especially punishment, to the crime or wrongdoing; deservedness.| He received condign punishment for his crimes. 他因罪行受到了应有的惩罚。|近义词：just deserts, appropriate punishment, fitting retribution, due|反义词：injustice, unfairness, undeserved punishment, leniency, clemency|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Late Middle English: from Old French *condignité, from Latin condignus ‘worthy’, from con- ‘together with’ + dignus ‘worthy’.
