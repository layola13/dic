blockish:/ˈblɑːkɪʃ/ | adj. | 笨拙的，呆板的 | Resembling a block;  usually in a negative way, suggesting someone is clumsy, unrefined, or lacking social graces. | He was a blockish young man, not very good at dancing. 他是一个笨拙的年轻人，不太擅长跳舞。|  He stood there, a blockish figure, unsure of what to do. 他站在那里，像个木头人，不知道该做什么。 |近义词：clumsy, awkward, ungainly, lumbering |反义词: graceful, elegant, agile |词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From "block" + "-ish" (suffix indicating resemblance or having the qualities of). "Block" originally referred to a solid piece of wood, later extending to other solid shapes. The suffix "-ish" transforms the noun "block" into an adjective describing something with block-like qualities.
