adjuratory:/ˌædʒʊˈreɪtəri/
adjective
恳求的；誓言的；起誓的
Relating to an oath or solemn affirmation; expressing or involving an earnest appeal or entreaty.
The defendant made an adjuratory statement before the judge.  被告在法官面前作了誓言式的陈述。

The adjuratory tone of his voice convinced me of his sincerity. 他恳求的语气使我相信了他的真诚。

近义词:beseeching, imploring, supplicatory, earnest, solemn
反义词:demanding, commanding, frivolous, lighthearted
词性:adjective
词源:From Late Latin adjūrātōrius, from Latin adjūrāre (“to swear to, entreat”), from ad- + jūrāre (“to swear”).

USAGE->
Attributive Use (修饰名词):

He delivered an adjuratory speech, begging the crowd to remain calm. 他发表了恳求性的讲话，恳求人群保持冷静。
Her adjuratory plea fell on deaf ears. 她恳求的请求没有被理睬。


Predicative Use (作表语 - 较少见):

His tone was adjuratory, urging them to reconsider their decision. 他的语气是恳求的，敦促他们重新考虑他们的决定。
The message seemed adjuratory, hinting at dire consequences if ignored.  这条信息似乎是恳求的，暗示如果不理会就会有可怕的后果。
