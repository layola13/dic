bouncer:/ˈbaʊnsər/| n.|保镖，门卫，(酒吧、夜总会等的)保安人员；(使弹跳的)跳跃者；(使球反弹的)弹跳球；(支票等的)拒付，退票|a person employed by a nightclub or similar establishment to prevent troublemakers from entering or to eject them from the premises.  Also: something that bounces, or causes something to bounce.|The bouncer wouldn't let us in because we weren't dressed smartly enough.  保安不让我们进去，因为我们穿得不够整齐。|Synonyms: doorman, guard, security guard,  chucker-out (informal),  gatekeeper|Antonyms: patron, guest|noun

bouncer:/ˈbaʊnsər/| n.|跳跃者；(使球反弹的)弹跳球|something that bounces, or causes something to bounce.|The child enjoyed playing with the bouncy ball, a real bouncer! 孩子喜欢玩弹力球，一个真正的弹跳球！|Synonyms: hopper, springer|Antonyms: sinker|noun

bouncer:/ˈbaʊnsər/| n.|(支票等的)拒付，退票|a check that is returned by a bank because there is not enough money in the account to cover it.|I got a bouncer from the bank – my paycheck didn't clear. 我收到了银行的退票——我的工资支票没有兑现。|Synonyms: bad check, dishonored check, returned check|Antonyms: cleared check|noun
