cauterize:/ˈkɔːtəraɪz/| v. | 烧灼；烙 | to burn (a part of the body) with a hot iron or caustic substance, typically to stop bleeding or prevent infection. | The doctor cauterized the wound to stop the bleeding. 医生烧灼伤口以止血。| synonyms: burn, sear, singe | antonyms: heal, soothe | verb

cauter:/ˈkɔːtər/| n. | 烧灼器；烙铁 | a heated instrument or other agent used to cauterize tissue. | The surgeon used a cauter to seal the blood vessels. 外科医生用烧灼器封闭血管。| synonyms: branding iron, cautery | antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cauterizare, from Latin cauter, cauterium "branding iron," from Greek kautērion, from kaiein "to burn."
