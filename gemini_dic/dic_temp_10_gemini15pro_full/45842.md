custrel:/ˈkʌstrəl/| n.|侍从；马僮；无赖；恶棍|A young lad or page, especially one of low birth employed as a servant by a knight; a rogue; a vagabond.|He was no more than a custrel, but he dreamed of becoming a knight. 他不过是个马僮，却梦想着成为一名骑士。|近义词：knave, rascal, rogue, scoundrel, varlet|反义词：nobleman, gentleman|词性: noun

Etymology:
From Old French *costerel, a diminutive of coste "rib, side," perhaps referring to someone who hangs around another's side.
