fauld:/fɔːld/ | n. | 羊圈；畜栏；褶皱；裙褶 | A pen or enclosure for sheep or other domestic animals; A fold or pleat in a garment, especially a skirt. | The shepherd led the sheep into the fauld. 牧羊人把羊领进了羊圈。|  The fauld of her skirt flowed gracefully as she walked.  她裙子的褶皱随着她的走动优雅地飘动。 | synonyms: pen, enclosure, fold, pleat | antonyms: open space, smooth | noun

fauld:/fɔːld/ | v. | 赶入羊圈；折叠；包裹 | To drive (sheep) into a fold; to fold or wrap something. |  He faulded the sheep for the night. 他把羊赶进羊圈过夜。 | She carefully faulded the blanket and put it away. 她仔细地把毯子叠好收了起来。 | synonyms: enclose, pen, fold, wrap | antonyms: release, unfold, unwrap | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fald, from Proto-Germanic *falþą (“fold, enclosure”), from Proto-Indo-European *pelt- (“to fold, bend”). Cognate with Old Frisian fald (“fold”), Old Saxon fald (“fold”), Old High German falt (“fold”), Old Norse faldr (“fold”), Dutch vaalt (“pale, faded”), German Falte (“fold, wrinkle”), Gothic 𐍆𐌰𐌻𐌸𐍃 (falþs, “fold”).
