lodicule:/ˈlɒdɪˌkjuːl/ (US) /ˈlɒdɪkjuːl/ (UK)| n. |颖花|A small scale at the base of the ovary in grasses, believed to be involved in the opening of the flower.|The lodicules swell up with water, forcing the lemma and palea apart and allowing the anthers and stigmas to emerge. 颖片因充满水而膨胀，迫使外稃和内稃分开，让花药和柱头露出来。|Synonyms: none specific to this botanical term|Antonyms: none specific to this botanical term|noun
