overgloomily: /ˌoʊvərˈɡluːmɪli/ | adv. | 过于忧郁地，过度悲伤地 | In a way that is excessively or unnecessarily gloomy. | He stared overgloomily out the window at the rain. 他忧郁地望着窗外的大雨。| Synonyms: despondently, dejectedly, morosely, glumly | Antonyms: cheerfully, brightly, optimistically | Word Type: Adverb

Etymology:
From over- + gloomily.  "Over-" acts as an intensifier, meaning "excessively" or "too much". "Gloomily" comes from "gloom" (Old English *glōm, related to Dutch gloom "twilight"), meaning darkness, obscurity, and by extension, a state of sadness or depression.
