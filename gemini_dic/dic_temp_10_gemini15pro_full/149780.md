palaverers:/pəˈlævərərz/
noun
闲聊者，空谈者
People who engage in lengthy or idle talk.
The palaverers spent hours discussing trivial matters. 闲聊者花了几个小时讨论琐碎的事情。
The market was filled with palaverers catching up on the latest gossip.  市场上挤满了闲聊者，他们在谈论最新的八卦。
synonyms: chatterboxes, gossips, conversationalists
antonyms: quiet people, introverts
n.

Etymology->
From the Portuguese palavra ("word," "speech"), ultimately from Late Latin parabola ("speech," "parable"). The verb form, "to palaver," meaning to talk idly or at length, came into English first, with the noun form "palaverer" following later.
