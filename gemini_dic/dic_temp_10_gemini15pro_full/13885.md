augurize:/ˈɔːɡjəˌraɪz/ | v. | 预言；预测；占卜 | to predict, especially from omens; to foretell; to prophesy |  The fortune teller augurized a long and prosperous life for her. 算命先生预言她会长寿而且富裕。

He augurized that the team would win the championship. 他预测这个队伍会赢得冠军。 | 近义词：predict, foretell, prophesy, forecast | 反义词：N/A | 词性: verb

Etymology: From Late Latin *augurizare, from Latin augurium (“divination by the flight of birds”), from avis (“bird”) + -urium (suffix forming abstract nouns).

PHRASE->
augur well for (预示...的成功): The signs augur well for the future of the company. 这些迹象预示着公司未来的成功。
augur ill for (预示...的不祥):  Dark clouds augur ill for the picnic. 乌云预示着野餐不吉利。
augur something (预示某事): The recent economic data augurs a recession. 最近的经济数据预示着经济衰退。
augur that (预示...): The expert augured that the market would recover soon. 专家预言市场很快就会复苏。
to augur from something (从...预示): He augured from the bird's flight that the journey would be dangerous. 他从鸟的飞行预示旅程将是危险的。
