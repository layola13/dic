churning:/ˈtʃɜːrnɪŋ/ (US) /ˈtʃɜːnɪŋ/ (UK)| n. v. adj.| 搅动，剧烈搅动；（情绪）翻腾；客户流失|The act of agitating something, especially liquids, vigorously; a state of agitation or turmoil; the rate at which customers stop using a service.|

The churning of the sea made him feel sick. 海水的剧烈翻腾让他感到恶心。

The churning in his stomach told him he was nervous. 他胃里的翻腾告诉他他很紧张。

High customer churning rates are a problem for the company.  高客户流失率对公司来说是一个问题。


近义词：agitating, stirring, turbulent, roiling, unsettling, fluctuating|
反义词：calm, still, peaceful, stable|
词性: noun, verb (present participle), adjective


词源：From Old English *ċyornan, from Proto-Germanic *kwernaną (“to grind, mill”). Cognate with Scots churn (“to churn”), West Frisian tsjerne (“to churn”), Dutch kernen (“to churn”), German kirnen (“to churn”), Danish kærne (“to churn”), Swedish kärna (“to churn”), Icelandic kyrna (“to churn”).
