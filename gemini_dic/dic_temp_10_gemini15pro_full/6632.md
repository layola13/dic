anaesthesias:/ˌænɪsˈθiːʒɪəz/ /ˌænɪsˈθiːziəz/| n. |麻醉（复数）|Loss of feeling or sensation, especially so that surgery can be performed. anaesthesia:/ˌænɪsˈθiːʒɪə/ /ˌænɪsˈθiːziə/| n. |麻醉|Loss of feeling or sensation, especially so that surgery can be performed.|The patient was given a general anaesthesia before the surgery. 病人在手术前被给予全身麻醉。|近义词：narcosis, numbness|反义词: sensation, consciousness|词性: noun


Etymology:  From Modern Latin anaesthesia, from Ancient Greek ἀναισθησία (anaisthesía, “lack of sensation”), from ἀν- (an-, “not, without”) + αἴσθησις (aísthēsis, “sensation, feeling”).
