attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD):/əˈtɛnʃ(ə)n ˈdɛfɪsɪt haɪpərækˈtɪvɪti dɪsˈɔːdə(r)/ | n. | 注意力缺陷多动障碍 (ADHD) | A chronic condition including attention difficulty, hyperactivity, and impulsiveness. |  He was diagnosed with attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) at a young age. 他在很小的时候就被诊断出患有注意力缺陷多动障碍 (ADHD)。 | synonyms: ADD, hyperkinetic disorder | antonyms:  neurotypicality (this isn't a perfect antonym, but represents a contrasting state) | noun

attention-deficit disorder (ADD):/əˈtɛnʃ(ə)n ˈdɛfɪsɪt dɪsˈɔːdə(r)/ | n. | 注意力缺陷障碍 (ADD) | An older term for predominantly inattentive type of ADHD, now less commonly used.  |  She used to be diagnosed with ADD, but now it's classified as ADHD - predominantly inattentive presentation. 她以前被诊断为ADD，但现在被归类为ADHD——主要是不专心类型。 | synonyms: ADHD (predominantly inattentive), attention deficit disorder with or without hyperactivity | antonyms:  neurotypicality (this isn't a perfect antonym, but represents a contrasting state) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"attention" (from Late Latin attentionem "a stretching out," from attendere "to stretch toward," from ad- "to" + tendere "to stretch") + "deficit" (from Latin deficit, "it lacks," third-person singular present active indicative of deficere "to fail, desert, be wanting," from de- "down, away" + facere "to do, make") + "disorder" (from Old French desordre "confusion, disorder," from des- "opposite of" (from Latin dis-) + ordre "order")
