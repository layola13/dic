holostomate:/ˈhɒləstəmeɪt/ | adj. | 全口式 | Having a mouth that is not sucker-like, as in most flukes.  Characterized by a mouth that is a simple opening, not modified into a sucker, as in certain trematodes. | Example: The holostomate structure of the parasite's mouth allows it to feed more efficiently.  这种寄生虫的全口式嘴部结构使其能够更有效地进食。
Example: Holostomate trematodes are less common than those with modified mouths. 全口式吸虫比那些嘴部特化的吸虫少见。 | Near Synonyms: simple-mouthed | Antonyms:  sucker-mouthed, digenetic | Word type: adjective
