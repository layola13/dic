mitochondria:/ˌmaɪtəˈkɒndriə (UK), ˌmaɪtəˈkɑːndriə (US)| n.|线粒体|A membrane-bound organelle found in most eukaryotic cells, often described as the "powerhouse of the cell" because they generate most of the cell's supply of adenosine triphosphate (ATP), used as a source of chemical energy.|The mitochondria are essential for cellular respiration.|近义词: chondriosome (less common)|反义词: N/A|词性: Noun

mitochondrion:/ˌmaɪtəˈkɒndriən (UK), ˌmaɪtəˈkɑːndriən (US)| n.|线粒体(单数)|Singular form of mitochondria.  A single membrane-bound organelle found in most eukaryotic cells.|Each mitochondrion contains its own DNA.|近义词: chondriosome (less common)|反义词: N/A|词性: Noun
