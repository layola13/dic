blandation:/blænˈdeɪʃən/
n.
奉承；甜言蜜语；哄骗
the act of coaxing by flattery or wheedling
例句：He used blandation to get what he wanted. 他用甜言蜜语来得到他想要的东西。

例句：Her attempts at blandation failed to impress the CEO. 她试图奉承的举动未能给CEO留下深刻印象。

近义词：flattery, adulation, cajolery
反义词：criticism, condemnation
词性：noun


blandation:/blænˈdeɪʃən/
n.
(食物或饮料)清淡无味；平淡乏味
lack of strong flavor; insipidity
例句：The blandation of the hospital food made him lose his appetite.  医院食物的清淡无味让他食欲不振。

例句：The chef was criticized for the blandation of the soup.  厨师因为汤的味道过于清淡而受到批评。


近义词：insipidity, dullness, vapidity
反义词：flavor, spiciness, zest
词性：noun
