nomad:/ˈnoʊmæd/ (US) /ˈnəʊmæd/ (UK)| n.| 游牧民；流浪者 |A member of a people having no permanent abode, and who travel from place to place to find fresh pasture for their livestock.|The nomads travelled across the desert seeking water.|近义词：wanderer, migrant, rover|反义词：settler, resident|词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *nomade*, from Latin *nomas* (genitive *nomadis*), from Ancient Greek *νομάς* (*nomás*, “roaming, wandering, pasturing”), from *νέμω* (*némō*, “I distribute, allot; I pasture, graze”).
