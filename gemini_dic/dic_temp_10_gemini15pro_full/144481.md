outthrobbed:/ˌaʊtˈθrɒbd/ |v.| (强烈地)跳动，悸动，搏动| to pulsate or throb more strongly than something else | Her heart outthrobbed the drums. 她的心跳声盖过了鼓声。 |Synonyms: pulsed, throbbed, palpitated | Antonyms: subsided, calmed, quieted | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + throbbed, past tense of throb. "Out-" acts as an intensifier, meaning "more intensely than." "Throb" comes from Old English *þrobban, of imitative origin.
