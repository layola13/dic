mittimus:/ˈmɪtɪməs/| n.|拘捕令，送监令|A warrant committing a person to prison.  A writ for transferring records from one court to another.|The judge signed the mittimus, and the defendant was taken into custody.|Synonyms: warrant, commitment order|Antonyms: release order|noun
etymology-> From Latin *mittimus*, first person plural present active indicative of *mittere* "to send, release, let go".
