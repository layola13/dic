hyperoptimizing:/ˌhaɪpərˈɒptɪmaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |过度优化|The act of optimizing something to an excessive degree, often resulting in diminishing returns or negative consequences.  It involves focusing on minute details and improvements that offer negligible overall benefit while potentially sacrificing other important factors like readability, maintainability, or user experience.|He was hyperoptimizing the code, trying to shave off milliseconds, but it made the code much harder to understand.|Synonyms: over-optimizing, over-tweaking|Antonyms: under-optimizing, neglecting|Verb

hyperoptimization:/ˌhaɪpərˌɒptɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |过度优化|The process or result of hyperoptimizing.  It refers to a state where further optimization efforts yield minimal improvements or even detrimental effects.|The team's hyperoptimization of the website's loading speed led to a decrease in user engagement.|Synonyms: over-optimization, over-engineering|Antonyms: under-optimization, simplification|Noun
