dogmatician:/dɒɡməˈtɪʃən/ /dɑːɡməˈtɪʃən/ | n. |教条主义者；独断家|A person who adheres rigidly to a set of beliefs or doctrines, often without considering evidence or alternative viewpoints.  A dogmatician is insistent on the truth of their own beliefs and unwilling to consider other perspectives.| He's such a dogmatician, he refuses to consider any other interpretation of the text. 他真是个教条主义者，他拒绝考虑对文本的任何其他解释。|Synonyms: doctrinaire, rigid, inflexible, orthodox, authoritarian|Antonyms: open-minded, liberal, flexible, tolerant, pragmatic|Noun

dogmatic:/dɒɡˈmætɪk/ /dɑːɡˈmætɪk/ | adj. |教条的；武断的|Asserting opinions in a doctrinaire or arrogant manner; opinionated.|His dogmatic pronouncements alienated many followers. 他武断的言论疏远了许多追随者。|Synonyms: assertive, opinionated, peremptory, dictatorial, emphatic|Antonyms: open-minded, receptive, flexible, inquiring, questioning|Adjective



ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dogmaticus, from Ancient Greek δογματικός (dogmatikós, “pertaining to doctrines”), from δόγμα (dógma, “opinion, tenet, doctrine”) + -ικός (-ikós).
