come-home-to-roost:/kʌm hoʊm tuː ruːst/ | v. | (不好的后果)自食其果，自作自受 |  This idiom refers to the consequences of one's actions, especially negative ones, eventually returning to affect them. It suggests that bad deeds or mistakes will eventually have negative repercussions for the person responsible.  | His dishonesty finally came home to roost when he was caught stealing. 他的不诚实最终在他被抓到偷窃时自食恶果。 |  backfire, rebound, catch up with | - | verb phrase
