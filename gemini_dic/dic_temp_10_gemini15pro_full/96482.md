individuators:/ɪnˌdɪvɪdʒuˈeɪtərz/
| n. |个体化者，赋予个体性的事物|Things or processes that serve to distinguish or individuate something or someone; factors contributing to the development of individuality.|The artist used bold colors and unique textures as individuators, setting his work apart from his contemporaries.|近义词：differentiators, distinguishers, markers|反义词：unifiers, homogenizers|词性：名词

Etymology:
From individuate + -or.  Individuate comes from Late Latin individuatus, past participle of individuare "to make individual," from Latin individuus "indivisible," from in- "not" + dividuus "divisible," from dividere "to divide."
