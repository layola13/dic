anaphor:/ˈænəfɔːr/ (US) /ˈænəfɔː(r)/ (UK)| n.|回指, 前指代词|A word or phrase that refers back to an earlier word or phrase in a text or discourse.|The president spoke of his accomplishments; the anaphor "his" refers to the president.|Synonyms: referent, antecedent|Antonyms: cataphor|noun
