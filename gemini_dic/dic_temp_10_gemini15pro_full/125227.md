multiculturalism: /ˌmʌltɪˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm/ | n. | 多元文化主义 | The presence of, or support for the presence of, several distinct cultural or ethnic groups within a society. |  Canada embraces multiculturalism and encourages people from all backgrounds to maintain their traditions. 加拿大拥护多元文化主义，并鼓励来自各种背景的人们保持他们的传统。

multiculturalism: /ˌmʌltɪˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm/ | n. | 多元文化主义 |  Multiculturalism is a key aspect of modern societies. 多元文化主义是现代社会的一个关键方面。


multiculturalism: /ˌmʌltɪˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm/ | n. | 多元文化主义 |  The policy or practice of giving equal attention or representation to different cultures, especially within a particular organization or society. | The university promotes multiculturalism through its diverse range of courses and student clubs. 这所大学通过其多样化的课程和学生俱乐部来促进多元文化主义。


multiculturalism: /ˌmʌltɪˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm/ | n. | 多元文化主义 | The doctrine that several different cultures (rather than one national culture) can coexist peacefully and equitably in a single country. |  The city’s multiculturalism is reflected in its vibrant festivals and diverse restaurants.  这座城市的多元文化主义体现在其充满活力的节日和多元化的餐厅中。



近义词: pluralism, diversity, cosmopolitanism

反义词: monoculturalism, homogeneity


词源: multi- (many) + culture + -ism (doctrine, belief)
