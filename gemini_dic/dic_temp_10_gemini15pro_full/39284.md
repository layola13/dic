congruism: /ˈkɒŋɡruɪzəm/ (UK) /ˈkɑːŋɡruɪzəm/ (US)| n. |一致主义，相容主义|The doctrine that ethical and religious systems are compatible and mutually supporting, especially the belief that there is no essential conflict between reason and revelation.|Congruism suggests that science and religion can coexist peacefully. 一致主义认为科学和宗教可以和平共处。|近义词：compatibilism, harmony|反义词：incongruity, conflict|词性: 名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *congruismus*, from Latin congruus ("agreeing, suitable") + -ismus.
