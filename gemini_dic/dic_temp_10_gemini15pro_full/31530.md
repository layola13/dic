charter:/ˈtʃɑːrtər (US) /ˈtʃɑːtə(r) (UK)| n. vt.| 宪章，特许状；租赁；包租 |A written grant by a country's legislative or sovereign power, by which an institution such as a company, college, or city is created and its rights and privileges defined.  |The company received a royal charter in 1685.  这家公司于1685年获得了皇家特许状。| synonym:  license, permit, authorization | antonym: prohibition, restriction | noun

charter:/ˈtʃɑːrtər (US) /ˈtʃɑːtə(r) (UK)| vt.| 包租；特许 |To hire or lease (a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle). |They chartered a plane to fly to the island.  他们包租了一架飞机飞往岛上。| synonym: hire, rent, lease | antonym:  return, sell | verb
