curtseyed: /ˈkɜːrtsiːd/ /ˈkɝːtsiːd/
| v. |行屈膝礼；|Performed a curtsy; bent the knees as a gesture of respect. |She curtseyed to the Queen. 她向女王行了屈膝礼。|
| | |The little girl curtseyed politely to her grandmother. 小女孩礼貌地向祖母行了屈膝礼。|
| | |bow, genuflect| |verb|
Etymology->
From curtsey + -ed.  Curtsey itself comes from Middle English curtesie, from Old French curteisie "courtesy," ultimately from Vulgar Latin *cortis "court." The shift in meaning from "courtesy" to the specific gesture likely arose from the elaborate rituals of court life.
