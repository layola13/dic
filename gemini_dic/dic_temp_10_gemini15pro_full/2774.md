adumbrations: /ˌædʌmˈbreɪʃənz/ | n. | 略述；预示；轮廓 | a faint indication of something to come; a vague or slight representation | The artist's early sketches contained adumbrations of his later masterpieces.  这位艺术家的早期草图包含着他后来杰作的轮廓。

The speech contained several adumbrations of the new policy. 演讲中对新政策进行了几处略述。 | 近义词：indications, hints, suggestions, outlines, foreshadowings, intimations | 反义词：details, specifications, elaborations | 词性: 名词(plural)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *adumbratio, from Latin adumbrare "to sketch, represent faintly," from ad- "to" + umbra "shadow."  来自晚期拉丁语 *adumbratio，源自拉丁语 adumbrare “勾画，略微表现”，来自 ad- “到” + umbra “影子”。
