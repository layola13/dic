gobbled:/ˈɡɑːbld/ (US), /ˈɡɒbld/ (UK)| v. |狼吞虎咽，贪婪地吃；很快地读（或说）；（火鸡）咯咯叫|to eat something quickly and noisily|He gobbled down his breakfast and rushed out the door. 他狼吞虎咽地吃完早餐，冲出了门。|Synonyms: devour, gulp, guzzle|Antonyms: nibble, savor|verb

gobbled:/ˈɡɑːbld/ (US), /ˈɡɒbld/ (UK)| adj. (rare) |被狼吞虎咽地吃掉的|eaten quickly and noisily (rarely used)|The gobbled cake was gone in minutes. 被狼吞虎咽吃掉的蛋糕几分钟就没了。 (This usage is less common.)|Synonyms: devoured, consumed|Antonyms: untouched, remaining|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gobelen*, frequentative of *gobben* (“to swallow”), of imitative origin.
