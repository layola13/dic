amioidei:/ˌeɪmiˈɔɪdiˌaɪ/ | n. |  鳉形目 | An order of ray-finned fishes, also called Cyprinodontiformes, that includes killifishes, livebearers, and others, many of which are popular aquarium fish.  They are characterized by a protrusible premaxilla and usually by the absence of a lateral line. | Example 1: Many species of amioidei are found in freshwater habitats around the world. 许多鳉形目物种分布在世界各地的淡水栖息地。

Example 2:  The amioidei exhibit a wide variety of reproductive strategies, including egg-laying and live-bearing. 鳉形目表现出各种各样的繁殖策略，包括卵生和胎生。 | Near synonyms: Cyprinodontiformes, killifishes, livebearers | Antonyms: N/A (as it is a taxonomic classification) | noun
