escheatable:/ɪˈʃiːtəbəl/
adjective
可充公的
Liable to be escheated.  Property is escheatable to the state if the owner dies without legal heirs.
The escheatable property was seized by the government.  这栋可充公的房产被政府没收了。
Synonyms: forfeitable, confiscable
Antonyms: inalienable, unseizable
词性: adjective

escheat:/ɪˈʃiːt/
noun
充公；归属权的转移
The reversion of property to the state.
The escheat of the property to the state occurred after the owner died without heirs.  业主死后无继承人，财产充公给国家。
Synonyms: forfeiture, confiscation
Antonyms: retention, preservation
词性: noun

escheat:/ɪˈʃiːt/
verb
充公；（财产）归属
To revert to the state.
The property escheated to the state because the owner died intestate and without heirs.  由于业主死时未立遗嘱且无继承人，财产归属国家。
Synonyms: forfeit, confiscate
Antonyms: retain, preserve
词性: verb

词源：
From Old French *escheoir "to fall to, devolve," from Latin excidere "to fall out, slip away."  The meaning shifted from "to fall to" to "to revert to a lord because of lack of heirs."
