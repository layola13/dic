curd:/kɜːrd/| n.|凝乳，豆腐|A soft, white substance formed when milk sours, used as the basis for making cheese.|例句：She added lemon juice to the milk to make curds. 她往牛奶里加柠檬汁做凝乳。|例句：The curds were separated from the whey. 凝乳和乳清被分开了。|近义词：coagulum, clabber|反义词:whey|词性:noun

词源:
From Middle English *crudde, *curd, from Old English crudu (“curd, coagulated milk”), from Proto-Germanic *kruþuz (“curd”), from Proto-Indo-European *kreut- (“raw flesh, blood; to coagulate”). Cognate with Dutch wrong (“wrong”), German Grütze (“groats, grit”), Old Norse krōða (“crop, maw”), Latin crūdus (“raw, uncooked”), Old Irish crú (“blood, gore”).
