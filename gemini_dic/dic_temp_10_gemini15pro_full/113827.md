maddens:/ˈmæd(ə)nz/ | v. | 使…发狂，激怒 | to make someone very angry or annoyed | The constant noise from the construction site maddens me.  建筑工地的持续噪音让我抓狂。| infuriates, angers, exasperates | calms, soothes | verb

maddens:/ˈmæd(ə)nz/ | v. |  (尤指比赛)使兴奋，使激动 | (especially of a contest) to make more exciting and dramatic | The last-minute goal maddened the crowd.  最后一分钟的进球让观众兴奋不已。 | excites, thrills, invigorates | bores, dulls | verb

Etymology:
From Middle English *madden, from Old English *gemædan (“to make mad, madden”), from Proto-Germanic *gamadjaną (“to make foolish, make mad”).  Related to Old Norse *meyja (“to injure, hurt, maim”).  The word shares a common root with "mad."
