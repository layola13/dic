bebopper:/ˈbiːˌbɑːpər/ | n. | 爵士乐迷；比波普演奏者 | A devotee of bebop, a style of jazz characterized by complex harmonies, rapid tempos, and virtuosic improvisation. | Example: Charlie Parker was a legendary bebopper. 查理·帕克是一位传奇的比波普演奏者。 | Example:  The club was packed with beboppers, eager to hear the latest sounds.  俱乐部里挤满了比波普爱好者，渴望听到最新的声音。 | Synonym: jazz enthusiast, bebop musician, jazz aficionado | Antonym: (No direct antonym, could consider someone who dislikes bebop) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "bebop" (a style of jazz) + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person associated with something).  "Bebop" itself is onomatopoeic, imitating the characteristic rhythmic phrases of the music.
