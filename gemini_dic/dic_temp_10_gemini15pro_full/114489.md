malacopterygian: /ˌmæləˈkɒptərɪdʒɪən/ | n. | 软鳍鱼类 | A fish of the superclass Malacopterygii, characterized by soft, segmented fin rays.  These fish typically have fins supported by flexible, bony rays, as opposed to the spiny rays found in acanthopterygians. | The carp is a common example of a malacopterygian. 鲤鱼是软鳍鱼类的一个常见例子。 | synonyms: soft-rayed fish | antonyms: acanthopterygian (spiny-rayed fish) | noun
