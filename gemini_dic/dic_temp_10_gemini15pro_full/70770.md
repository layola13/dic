finessing:/fɪˈnɛsɪŋ/ | n. | 精巧操作，巧妙处理，策略 | The act of handling a situation or problem with skill and subtlety, often involving deception or manipulation. | He managed to finesse his way into the exclusive club. 他设法巧妙地进入了那个高级俱乐部。|  She finessed the negotiation to get a better deal. 她巧妙地运用策略在谈判中获得了更好的交易。 | synonyms: maneuvering, manipulating, strategizing, scheming, contriving | antonyms: blundering, bungling | noun

finessing:/fɪˈnɛsɪŋ/ | v. | 精巧操作，巧妙处理 | To handle a situation or problem with skill and subtlety, often involving deception or manipulation. | He finessed the deal to his advantage.  他巧妙地处理了这笔交易，使自己受益。| She finessed her way out of a difficult situation. 她巧妙地摆脱了困境。| synonyms: maneuver, manipulate, strategize, scheme, contrive | antonyms: blunder, bungle | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From the French word "finesse," meaning "fineness, delicacy, subtlety."  Ultimately derived from the Latin "finis," meaning "end, limit, boundary."
