parishionership:/pəˈrɪʃənərˌʃɪp/
n.
教区居民的身份; 教区居民的关系
The state or condition of being a parishioner.  A sense of shared identity and responsibility among the members of a parish.
He valued his parishionership at St. Michael's. 他很珍惜自己在圣迈克尔教堂的教区居民身份。
The church encouraged a strong sense of parishionership. 教堂鼓励教区居民之间强烈的归属感。
近义词: membership, fellowship, community
反义词: alienation, isolation
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From parishioner + -ship.  Parishioner comes from parish + -er. Parish comes from Old French paroisse, from Late Latin parochia "diocese," from Greek paroikia "sojourning in a foreign land," from paroikos "dwelling beside, stranger," from para- "beside" + oikos "house."
