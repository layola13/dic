conceit:/kənˈsiːt/| n.|自负，骄傲；奇想，幻想|An excessive pride in oneself; an ingenious or witty idea or thought.|He was puffed up with conceit after winning the award.  他获奖后变得非常自负。 She had a conceit that she could fly. 她有个奇想，认为自己会飞。|近义词：arrogance, vanity, pride, egotism|反义词：humility, modesty|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *conceit* "concept, idea, opinion," from Latin *concipere* "to take in, conceive," from *com-* "together" + *capere* "to take." The meaning shifted from "concept" to "favorable opinion of oneself" in the 16th century.
