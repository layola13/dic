augur:/ˈɔːɡər/ (US), /ˈɔːɡə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.|预言家，占卜师；预兆，预示|A Roman religious official who interpreted omens, especially the flight of birds; to be a sign of; to predict.|例句：The appearance of a comet augured disaster.  彗星的出现预示着灾难。|近义词：prophet, soothsayer, foretell, portend, presage|反义词: -- |词性: noun, verb

词源: From Latin *augur*, meaning "diviner, soothsayer." Related to *avis* "bird" and *garrire* "to chatter".  The original practice involved observing the flight and calls of birds to predict the future.
