anathema: /əˈnæθəmə/ | n. | 咒逐，诅咒；令人厌恶的人或事 | Something or someone that one vehemently dislikes.  A formal curse by a pope or a council of the Church, excommunicating a person or denouncing a doctrine. |例句：His political views were anathema to me. 他的政治观点让我深恶痛绝。

例句：The very idea of a tax increase is anathema to most conservatives. 对大多数保守派来说，增税的想法是令人深恶痛绝的。| 近义词：curse, abomination, execration, malediction | 反义词：blessing, benediction | 词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin anathema, from Ecclesiastical Greek anáthema "a thing devoted to evil, a curse," literally "a thing set up, laid by," from anatithenai "to set up, dedicate, offer," from ana- "up" + tithenai "to put, place."
