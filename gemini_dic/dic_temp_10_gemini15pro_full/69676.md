fender:/ˈfɛndər/ (US), /ˈfɛndə(r)/ (UK)| n.|挡泥板；防护板；护舷|A part of a vehicle, such as a car or bicycle, that arches over the wheels to protect the vehicle and people from mud or other debris thrown up by the wheels.  Also refers to the padded edge around a boat or ship to protect it from damage when it bumps against something.|The car's front fender was dented in the accident. 汽车的前挡泥板在事故中凹陷了。|synonyms: mudguard, wing (UK), splashguard|antonyms: (No direct antonym, but consider "exposed" in the context of the protected part)|noun

fender:/ˈfɛndər/ (US), /ˈfɛndə(r)/ (UK)| v.|抵挡；防御；保护|To ward off or protect from something, often a blow or attack. Can also mean to fend (for oneself).|He used his arm to fender the blow. 他用胳膊挡住了那一击。|synonyms: protect, shield, defend, guard, ward off|antonyms: expose, endanger|verb
