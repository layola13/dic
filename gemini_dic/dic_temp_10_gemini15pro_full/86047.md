ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *helsam, from Old English hǣlsum (“healthy, wholesome, salutary”), from hǣl (“health, salvation, prosperity”) +‎ -sum (suffix forming adjectives meaning ‘characterized by, having the quality of, tending to’). Cognate with Old Frisian hēlsum (“wholesome, healthy”), Old Saxon hēlsam, Old High German heilsam (German heilsam (“healing, salutary”)), Old Norse heilsamr (“wholesome”), Gothic 𐌷𐌰𐌹𐌻𐍃𐌰𐌼𐍃 (hailsams, “healthy”).
