cytococcus:/ˌsaitəʊˈkɒkəs/ /ˌsaɪtoʊˈkɑːkəs/ | n. | 细胞球菌 | A genus of bacteria that are typically non-motile and spherical or ovoid in shape.  They are part of the normal flora of the skin and mucous membranes but can also cause opportunistic infections. |  Cytococcus spp. are commonly found in the oral cavity. 细胞球菌属常见于口腔。| Synonyms: Micrococcus (older classification) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for a genus of bacteria. Could consider motile bacteria as a contrasting group, e.g., Bacillus) | noun
