ace-up-his-sleeve:/eɪs ʌp hɪz sliːv/| idiom|暗藏的王牌，秘计，锦囊妙计|Having a secret advantage or resource that can be used if needed.  It refers to a card player concealing an ace in their sleeve to cheat, but is more commonly used figuratively.|He had an ace up his sleeve – he knew the chairman of the selection committee. 他留了一手——他认识遴选委员会的主席。|secret advantage, hidden resource, trump card|disadvantage, weakness|noun phrase

ETYMOLOGY->
Originates from card games, specifically cheating by hiding an ace in one's sleeve to be used at a crucial moment. This practice gave rise to the figurative meaning of having a hidden advantage.

USAGE->
