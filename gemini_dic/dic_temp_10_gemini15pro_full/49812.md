demonstrability:/ˌdɛmənstrəˈbɪləti/| n.|可论证性，可证明性|The demonstrability of a scientific theory is crucial for its acceptance.  一个科学理论的可证性对其被接受至关重要。|例句：The scientist questioned the demonstrability of the new theory without further evidence.  如果没有进一步的证据，这位科学家质疑新理论的可证性。

The demonstrability of his skills impressed the hiring manager. 他的技能的可证明性给招聘经理留下了深刻印象。|近义词：provability, verifiability, testability|反义词：unprovability, unverifiability|词性:noun
demonstrate:/ˈdɛmənˌstreɪt/| v.|证明，演示，示威|He demonstrated the new product to the customers. 他向顾客演示了新产品。|例句：She demonstrated her ability to solve complex problems. 她展示了她解决复杂问题的能力。

The protesters demonstrated against the new law. 抗议者示威反对新法律。|近义词：show, prove, exhibit, display|反义词：conceal, hide|词性:verb
demonstrative:/dəˈmɒnstrətɪv/| adj.|指示的，证明的，公开表露感情的|Demonstrative pronouns include "this," "that," "these," and "those."  指示代词包括“这个”、“那个”、“这些”和“那些”。|例句：He gave a demonstrative presentation of the new software. 他对新软件进行了演示。

She is not a very demonstrative person. 她不是一个很外露感情的人。|近义词：expressive, affectionate, illustrative|反义词：reserved, unemotional, undemonstrative|词性:adjective
