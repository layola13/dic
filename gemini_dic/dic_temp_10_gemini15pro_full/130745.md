nonamenability:/ˌnɒnəˈmiːnəbɪləti/ | n. | 不可管理性，不服从性，难驾驭 | The nonamenability of the tribe to government control posed a significant challenge.  这个部落不服从政府管理，构成了重大挑战。 | nonamenable to | amenability | noun
nonamenable:/ˌnɒnəˈmiːnəbl/ | adj. | 不可管理的，不服从的，难驾驭的 |  The wild horses were nonamenable to training. 这些野马难以驯服。 | intractable, unmanageable, unruly, defiant, resistant, recalcitrant | amenable, manageable, compliant | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + amenable + -ity.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," "amenable" comes from Old French "amenable," meaning "liable to be brought before a court," ultimately from Latin "minari," meaning "to threaten," and "-ity" is a suffix forming nouns of quality or state.
