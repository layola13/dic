grenade:/ɡrəˈneɪd/ | n. | 手榴弹；榴弹 | A small bomb thrown by hand or launched mechanically. |  The soldier pulled the pin and threw the grenade. 士兵拔掉保险销，扔出了手榴弹。

| Example:  Enemy troops attacked their position with rifles and grenades. 敌军用步枪和手榴弹攻击他们的阵地。 | Synonym: bomb, explosive | Antonym: peace, safety | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French grenade "pomegranate," from Old Provençal granado, from Late Latin granatum, neuter of Latin granatus "having many seeds," from granum "grain, seed."  The fruit was so named for its many seeds, and the explosive device was so called from its resemblance to the fruit.
