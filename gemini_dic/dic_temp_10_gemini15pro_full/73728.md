forkednesses:/ˈfɔːr.kɪd.nəs.ɪz/ | n. | 分叉，分歧 |  The state or quality of being forked; the number of forks or branches.  This refers to the plural of "forkedness," which itself refers to the state of being divided or branched. |  The forkednesses of the river system made navigation challenging. 这条河流系统的分叉使得航行充满挑战。 |  branchings, divisions, bifurcations |  junctions, convergences | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "forked" + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns of state or quality). "Forked" comes from the Old English "forcod," past participle of "forcian" (to fork).
