alcyonacea:/ˌælsɪəˈneɪsiə/ | n. |  软珊瑚目；软珊瑚类 | An order of corals, commonly known as soft corals, characterized by polyps with eight tentacles.  These corals often have a fleshy or leathery appearance and lack a rigid calcium carbonate skeleton. |  例句：Alcyonacea are common inhabitants of coral reefs. 软珊瑚是珊瑚礁的常见居民。

例句：Many species of Alcyonacea exhibit vibrant colors. 许多种类的软珊瑚呈现出鲜艳的色彩。 | 近义词：soft corals | 反义词: hard corals | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Modern Latin, derived from Alcyone, a figure in Greek mythology transformed into a kingfisher, and the Latin suffix -acea, denoting an order or class of animals.  The connection likely stems from the perceived resemblance of some soft corals to the nesting burrows of kingfishers.
