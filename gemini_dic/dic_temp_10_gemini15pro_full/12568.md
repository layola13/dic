aspirin:/ˈæsprɪn/ (US), /ˈæspərɪn/ (UK)| n.|阿司匹林|Derived from *Spiraea ulmaria* (meadowsweet), the original source of salicylic acid, from which aspirin is synthesized. The "a-" prefix denotes acetyl, and "-in" is a common suffix for medications.|Doctors recommend low-dose aspirin to prevent heart attacks. 医生建议服用低剂量阿司匹林来预防心脏病发作。|Synonyms: acetylsalicylic acid|Antonyms: N/A|noun
