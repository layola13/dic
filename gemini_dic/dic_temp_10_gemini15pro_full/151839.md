passing:/ˈpæsɪŋ/ (US), /ˈpɑːsɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. n.|经过的，短暂的；逝世；通过|A passing grade is enough to pass the exam. 通过的分数足以通过考试。|Synonyms: transient, fleeting, ephemeral, momentary, brief|Antonyms: permanent, enduring, lasting, abiding|adjective

passing:/ˈpæsɪŋ/ (US), /ˈpɑːsɪŋ/ (UK)| n.|经过，流逝；逝世；通过|The passing of time heals all wounds. 时间的流逝治愈一切创伤。|Synonyms: passage, elapse, expiry, death, decease|Antonyms: arrival, birth|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *passinge, verbal noun from passen (“to pass”), from Old French passer (“to pass, cross, surpass, exceed, go beyond, die, spend (time), omit”), from Vulgar Latin *passāre, from Latin passus (“step, pace”), from pandere (“to spread, stretch”).
