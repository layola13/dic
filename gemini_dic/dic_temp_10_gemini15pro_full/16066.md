bandito:/bænˈdiːtoʊ/ (US), /bænˈdiːtəʊ/ (UK)| n. |土匪，强盗|An outlaw, especially one in a gang and typically operating in an isolated or lawless area.  A bandit is often romanticized as a dashing rogue.|The old Westerns often portrayed the bandito as a charming but dangerous figure.|Synonyms: outlaw, robber, highwayman, brigand, desperado|Antonyms: lawman, sheriff, police officer|Noun


bandito:/bænˈdiːtoʊ/ (US), /bænˈdiːtəʊ/ (UK)| n. |歹徒，恶棍|(Sometimes humorous) A mischievous or lawless person.|The little boy, covered in mud, looked like a bandito after raiding the cookie jar.|Synonyms: rascal, rogue, scoundrel, miscreant|Antonyms: saint, angel, goody-two-shoes|Noun
