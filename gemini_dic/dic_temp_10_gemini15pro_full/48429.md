deductible:/dɪˈdʌktɪbl/
adjective|可扣除的|Relating to an amount of money that can be subtracted from a total.|Health insurance plans often come with a deductible, meaning you pay a certain amount before the insurance kicks in.|Synonyms: subtractable, allowable, claimable|Antonyms: non-deductible, inclusive|词性:形容词

deductible:/dɪˈdʌktɪbl/
noun|免赔额，自付额|An amount of money that must be paid by the insured before an insurance company will pay a claim.|My car insurance has a $500 deductible.|Synonyms: excess, co-pay|Antonyms: premium|词性:名词

词源: From the late 19th century, from the verb 'deduct' + '-ible'.  'Deduct' comes from the Latin 'dēductus', past participle of 'dēdūcere'  meaning 'to lead down' or 'to subtract'.
