lockdown: /ˈlɒkdaʊn/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkdaʊn/ (US)| n.| 封锁，禁闭|A state of isolation or restricted access instituted as a security measure.|The city was placed under lockdown after the attack.  这座城市在袭击后被封锁了。|近义词：shutdown, closure, confinement, quarantine|反义词: opening, reopening|词性: 名词

lockdown: /ˈlɒkdaʊn/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkdaʊn/ (US)| v.| 封锁，禁闭|To put (a place or people) under lockdown.|The government decided to lockdown the country to control the spread of the virus. 政府决定封锁国家来控制病毒的传播。|近义词：confine, restrict, isolate|反义词: open, free|词性: 动词

词源:  "lockdown" is a compound word formed from "lock" and "down". "Lock" originates from Old English *loc, meaning "fastening device," while "down" comes from Old English dūn, meaning "hill, downwards."  The combined meaning relates to securing a place and restricting movement.
