mullioning:/ˈmʌljənɪŋ/
n.
竖框，直棂，窗棂
The vertical bars that divide the panes of a window.
The mullioning in the old church windows was intricate and beautiful.  教堂旧窗户上的竖框复杂而美丽。

mullioned:/ˈmʌljənd/
adj.
有竖框的，多直棂的
Divided by mullions.
The mullioned windows gave the room a medieval feel. 有竖框的窗户赋予房间一种中世纪的感觉。

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mulyon*, from Old French *moignon* (stump, end), from Vulgar Latin *mutione*, from Latin *mutō* (I change, alter).  The sense development likely relates to the mullion's function as a dividing element.

SYNONYMS->
muntin, glazing bar

ANTONYMS->
(No direct antonym, but consider "undivided" or "single pane" for the effect.)

PART OF SPEECH->
Noun (mullioning), Adjective (mullioned)
