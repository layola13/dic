blotchiest:/ˈblɒtʃiɪst/ | adj. | 有斑点的，斑驳的（最高级）| Covered with blotches, especially unevenly or unattractively; most blotchy. | Her face was blotchiest from the sunburn.  她的脸被晒伤后变得斑驳不堪。| The blotchiest paint job I've ever seen. 我见过最斑驳的油漆工作。| 近义词：mottled, speckled, patchy | 反义词：clear, even, smooth | 词性：形容词

blotchy:/ˈblɒtʃi/ | adj. | 有斑点的，斑驳的| Covered with blotches, especially unevenly or unattractively. | The wall had a blotchy appearance after the damp set in.  墙体受潮后出现了斑驳的痕迹。| His skin was blotchy with red marks. 他的皮肤上布满了红色的斑点。| 近义词：mottled, speckled, patchy | 反义词：clear, even, smooth | 词性：形容词

blotch:/blɒtʃ/ | n. | 污点，斑点| A mark or stain, typically one caused by ink, paint, or dirt. | There was a blotch of ink on the paper. 纸上有一块墨水渍。| He had a blotch of paint on his shirt. 他的衬衫上有一块油漆污渍。| 近义词：stain, spot, mark | 反义词：cleanliness, purity | 词性：名词
blotch:/blɒtʃ/ | v. | 弄脏，涂污| To make a blotch or blotches on. |  He blotched the paper with ink. 他把墨水弄到了纸上。| The rain blotched the freshly painted wall. 雨水弄脏了新粉刷的墙壁。| 近义词：stain, smear, smudge | 反义词：clean, cleanse | 词性：动词

词源:  From Middle English *blocche, from Old Norse *blottr (“bare”), from Proto-Germanic *blutaz (“bare, naked, blunt”).  More at blunt.  The sense of "stain" arose from the idea of a discolored or bare patch.
