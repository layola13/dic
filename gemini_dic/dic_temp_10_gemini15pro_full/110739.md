lingerer:/ˈlɪŋɡərər/| n.|逗留者，徘徊者|Someone who stays in a place longer than necessary, often out of reluctance to leave.|The lingerers at the party finally left around 2 am. 派对上的逗留者们最终在凌晨2点左右离开了。|近义词：loiterer, dawdler, idler|反义词: goer, leaver|词性:noun

lingering:/ˈlɪŋɡərɪŋ/| adj.|逗留的，徘徊的；挥之不去的|Lasting for a long time or slow to disappear.|She gave him a lingering kiss. 她给了他一个缠绵的吻。|近义词：remaining, persistent, enduring|反义词: fleeting, brief, short|词性:adjective

linger:/ˈlɪŋɡər/| vi.|逗留，徘徊；磨蹭；持续，存留|To stay somewhere beyond the usual or expected time.|The smell of smoke lingered in the air. 烟味在空气中弥漫不去。|近义词：remain, stay, tarry|反义词: leave, depart, rush|词性:verb


USAGE->
词源: From Middle English *lingren, from Old Norse *lingra (“to delay, linger”).
