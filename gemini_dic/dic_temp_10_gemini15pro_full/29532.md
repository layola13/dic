catcall: /ˈkætˌkɔːl/ (US), /ˈkætˌkɔːl/ (UK)| v.|发出嘘声；（尤指男人对女人）发出猥亵的口哨声|To make a loud whistle or shout of disapproval or, especially, a sexually suggestive call or comment directed at someone publicly, usually a woman.|例句：The men catcalled at the women passing by.  男人们对路过的女人吹口哨。|近义词：whistle, jeer, taunt, harass|反义词:compliment, praise|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "cat" + "call". Originally referring to a shrill cry like that of a cat, later specifically applied to derisive shouts or whistles, especially those directed at performers or public figures, and eventually to sexually suggestive calls directed at women.
