pandowdy:/ˈpændˌaʊdi/ | n. |潘道迪（一种水果馅饼）| A deep-dish baked fruit dessert, typically with apples, and a thick, crusty top. The crust is often broken and pushed down into the fruit during baking, allowing the juices to bubble up and create a moist, flavorful topping. | My grandmother's apple pandowdy is the best dessert I've ever tasted. 我祖母做的苹果潘道迪是我吃过的最好吃的甜点。

| We had a warm pandowdy with vanilla ice cream for dessert.  我们饭后甜点吃了加香草冰淇淋的热潘道迪。| 近义词: cobbler, crisp, crumble, pie | 反义词: (No direct antonym, as it's a specific type of dessert) | 词性: noun


Etymology:
Origin uncertain, possibly from Scottish Gaelic *pantog or Welsh *panadau ("little cakes or pies").  First recorded in the US in the early 19th century.  词源不确定，可能来自苏格兰盖尔语 *pantog 或威尔士语 *panadau（“小蛋糕或馅饼”）。  首次记录于 19世纪初的美国。
