bleared:/blɪrd/| adj.| 模糊的，视线不清的|Dim, indistinct, as if seen through tears or sleep; also, made dim by or as if by tears or sleep.  Having a dull or dimmed appearance.|His eyes were bleared from lack of sleep.|近义词：blurred, blurry, hazy, misty, unfocused|反义词：clear, sharp, focused|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Middle English *bleren*, from Old English *blēarian* (“to make dim, to darken, to blear”), from Proto-Germanic *blairijaną* (“to make blue, to blear”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰleh₁- (“to shine, to gleam”).  The original sense was "to make blue," then "to make dim, darken," and finally "to blur."
