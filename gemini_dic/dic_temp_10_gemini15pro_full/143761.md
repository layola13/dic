ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + march.  "Out-" is a prefix meaning "exceeding," "surpassing," or "outside."  "March" comes from Old French "marcher," meaning "to walk," "to tread."  Therefore, "outmarch" literally means to march further or faster than someone else.
