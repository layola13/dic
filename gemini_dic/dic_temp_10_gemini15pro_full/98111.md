inrush:/ˈɪnrʌʃ/| n.|涌入，浪涌；突入，冲入|A sudden, large flow of something, typically electrical current or a crowd of people.|例句：The inrush of current when the appliance is switched on can trip the circuit breaker.  电器开启时的电流涌入可能会导致断路器跳闸。

例句：The inrush of fans onto the field after the game was chaotic.  比赛结束后，球迷涌入场内，场面一片混乱。|近义词：surge, influx, rush, flood|反义词: outflow, trickle|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From in- + rush.  "In-" signifies inward movement or direction, while "rush" refers to a rapid, powerful movement.  Therefore, "inrush" literally means a rushing inward.
