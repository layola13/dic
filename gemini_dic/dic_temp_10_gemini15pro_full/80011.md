go-over-the-hill:/ɡoʊ ˈoʊvər ðə hɪl/ | adj. | 过盛年的；走下坡路的；衰老的 |  To be past one's prime; to be old and no longer effective or successful.  It often implies a decline in physical or mental abilities. |  He's gone over the hill as a football player. 他作为一名足球运动员已经过了巅峰时期了。 | synonyms: past one's prime, declining, aging, waning, deteriorating | antonyms: young, youthful, in one's prime, flourishing, thriving | adjective

go-over-the-hill:/ɡoʊ ˈoʊvər ðə hɪl/ | n. |  衰退期；过盛年期 | The period of decline after one's prime. | Reaching the go-over-the-hill stage can be difficult for some people.  对一些人来说，步入衰退期是件很困难的事。 | synonyms: decline, downturn, downswing | antonyms: prime, peak, heyday | noun
