insolvent:/ɪnˈsɒlvənt/ /ɪnˈsɑːlvənt/| adj. n.|无力偿还的；破产者|Unable to pay debts owed.  A person who is unable to pay their debts.|The company was declared insolvent after accumulating millions in debt.  这家公司在积累了数百万债务后被宣布破产。|Near synonyms: bankrupt, broke, ruined|Near antonyms: solvent, affluent, wealthy|adjective

insolvent:/ɪnˈsɒlvənt/ /ɪnˈsɑːlvənt/| n.|破产者|A person who is unable to pay their debts.|The insolvents were forced to sell their assets.  破产者被迫出售他们的资产。|Near synonyms: bankrupt, debtor|Near antonyms: creditor, millionaire|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *insolvens, from Latin insolvēns (“not paying”), present participle of insolvere (“not to pay, not to loosen, not to release”), from in- (“not”) + solvere (“to loosen, to pay”).
