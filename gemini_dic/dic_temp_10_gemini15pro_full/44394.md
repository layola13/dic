cross-fertilizing:/ˌkrɒsˈfɜːtɪlaɪzɪŋ/
| adj. v.|交叉受精，互相影响，促进发展|The process of fertilizing one plant or animal with pollen or sperm from another plant or animal of a different variety, or the process of exchanging ideas or information with people from different backgrounds or areas of expertise to stimulate new thinking or creativity.|例句：Cross-fertilizing ideas between different departments can lead to innovative solutions.  不同部门之间互相交流想法可以带来创新的解决方案。|近义词：interbreeding, hybridizing, integrating, synergizing|反义词：isolating, separating|词性:形容词/动词


cross-fertilize:/ˌkrɒsˈfɜːtɪlaɪz/
| vt.|交叉受精，互相影响，促进发展|To fertilize one plant or animal with pollen or sperm from another plant or animal of a different variety, or to exchange ideas or information with people from different backgrounds or areas of expertise to stimulate new thinking or creativity.|例句：The researchers cross-fertilized different strains of wheat to develop a more disease-resistant variety. 研究人员将不同的小麦品种进行杂交，以培育出更抗病的品种。|近义词：interbreed, hybridize, integrate, synergize|反义词：isolate, separate|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From cross- + fertilize.  "Fertilize" comes from Late Middle English: from Old French fertiliser, from medieval Latin fertilizzare, from Latin fertilis ‘fertile.’  "Cross" comes from Old Norse kross, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch kruis and German Kreuz.
