gamesome:/ˈɡeɪmsəm/| adj.|嬉戏的，爱玩的，好开玩笑的|Playful and full of energy; eager to play or join in games or activities.  Characterized by a playful, lighthearted, and mischievous disposition.|The gamesome puppies tumbled over each other in the grass. 嬉戏的小狗在草地上互相翻滚。|The children were in a gamesome mood, giggling and chasing each other around the park. 孩子们玩性大发，咯咯笑着，在公园里互相追逐。|近义词：playful, frolicsome, sportive, mischievous|反义词：serious, solemn, grave|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Middle English *gamsom*, from Old English *gāmsum* "joyful, merry," from *gamen* "game, sport, amusement" + *-sum* "characterized by."  The Old English *gamen* is related to Old High German *gaman* "joy, merriment," and ultimately to Proto-Germanic *gamaną*.
