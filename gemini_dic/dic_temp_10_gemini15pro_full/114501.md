maladaptive:/ˌmæləˈdæptɪv/ | adj. | 适应不良的，不适应的 | Not providing adequate or appropriate adjustment to the environment or situation.  Failing to adjust adequately or appropriately to the demands of one's environment or situation. |  His maladaptive behavior led to his dismissal from the company. 他的适应不良行为导致他被公司解雇。 |  Her maladaptive coping mechanisms made it difficult for her to deal with stress. 她不适应的应对机制使她难以应对压力。 | 近义词: dysfunctional, unhealthy, counterproductive, inapt | 反义词: adaptive, healthy, constructive, suitable | 词性: adjective

maladaptation:/ˌmælˌædæpˈteɪʃn/ | n. | 适应不良，不适应 | The process of failing to adapt or adjust adequately or appropriately to the demands of one's environment or situation; the state of being maladapted.  A trait that is more harmful than helpful. | The maladaptation of the polar bear to warmer climates is a serious concern. 北极熊对气候变暖的适应不良是一个严重的问题。 | The camel's hump is not a maladaptation, but rather a crucial adaptation for survival in the desert. 骆驼的驼峰并不是一种适应不良，而是一种在沙漠中生存的关键适应性特征。| 近义词: dysfunction, unsuitability | 反义词: adaptation, suitability | 词性: noun


Etymology:
From mal- (bad, ill) + adaptive.  "Mal-" comes from the French, ultimately from the Latin "malus" meaning "bad." "Adaptive" comes from the Latin "adaptare," meaning "to fit."
