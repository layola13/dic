chances:/ˈtʃɑːnsɪz/ /ˈtʃænsɪz/| n.|机会，可能性，机遇|A possibility of something happening.  The likelihood of something occurring.|例句：What are the chances of winning the lottery?  赢得彩票的几率是多少？

例句：She took a chance on a new job in a different city.  她冒险在一个不同的城市找到了一份新工作。|近义词：possibilities, prospects, odds, likelihood|反义词：impossibilities, unlikelihood|词性:名词

chance:/ˈtʃɑːns/ /ˈtʃæns/| n.|机会，可能性，机遇|A possibility of something happening.  The likelihood of something occurring.|例句：He had a chance to study abroad. 他有一个出国留学的机会。

例句：There's a slight chance of rain later today. 今天晚些时候可能有小雨。|近义词：opportunity, possibility, prospect|反义词：impossibility|词性:名词

chance:/ˈtʃɑːns/ /ˈtʃæns/| v.|碰巧，冒险|To happen upon something unexpectedly. To take a risk.|例句：I chanced upon a rare book in the bookstore. 我在书店里偶然发现了一本珍贵的书。

例句：He decided to chance the storm and drive home. 他决定冒险在暴风雨中开车回家。|近义词：risk, gamble, venture|反义词：avoid, evade|词性:动词

chance:/ˈtʃɑːns/ /ˈtʃæns/| adj.|偶然的|Happening by accident or without planning.|例句：It was a chance encounter that changed her life. 这是一次偶然的相遇，改变了她的人生。

例句：A chance remark led to a heated argument.  一句无心之言引发了激烈的争论。|近义词：accidental, random, fortuitous|反义词：planned, deliberate, intentional|词性:形容词
