modulators:/ˈmɒdjʊleɪtəz/ (US) /ˈmɑːdʒəleɪtərz/| n.|调制器；调节器|A modulator is a device or substance that varies some characteristic of a carrier signal, such as amplitude, frequency, or phase.  It is used in electronics, telecommunications, and music.|The modulator in the radio transmitter controls the amplitude of the carrier wave.  无线电发射机中的调制器控制载波的幅度。|Synonyms: controller, regulator, adjuster|Antonyms: demodulator|Noun

modulate:/ˈmɒdjʊleɪt/ (US) /ˈmɑːdʒəleɪt/| v.|调制；调节；缓和|To modulate means to vary the strength, tone, or pitch of something, such as a voice, a musical instrument, or a signal.|She modulated her voice to soothe the crying baby. 她调整了声音来安抚哭闹的婴儿。|Synonyms: adjust, regulate, temper, vary|Antonyms:  deregulate, amplify (in some contexts)|Verb
