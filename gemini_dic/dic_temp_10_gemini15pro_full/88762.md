hocus-pocus:/ˈhoʊkəs ˈpoʊkəs/| n. |戏法；胡言乱语；花招；骗人的把戏|Nonsense words traditionally used by magicians.  A method or belief that is based on false ideas and is intended to trick people.|The magician uttered some hocus-pocus and pulled a rabbit out of the hat. 魔术师念了几句咒语，就从帽子里变出一只兔子。|Synonyms: trickery, sleight of hand, magic, conjuring, deception, illusion, chicanery|Antonyms: reality, truth, honesty|noun

hocus-pocuses:/ˈhoʊkəs ˈpoʊkəsəz/|n.|戏法；胡言乱语；花招；骗人的把戏（hocus-pocus的复数形式）|The plural form of hocus-pocus, referring to multiple instances of trickery or nonsense words used in magic.|All his promises turned out to be just hocus-pocuses. 他所有的承诺都不过是些骗人的把戏。|Synonyms: tricks, deceptions, illusions|Antonyms: realities, truths|noun
