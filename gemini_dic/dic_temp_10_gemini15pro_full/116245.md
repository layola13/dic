mass-observe:/mæs əbˈzɜːrv/ | v. | 大规模观察；集体观察 | To observe something on a large scale, often involving many people or automated systems. | Scientists are mass-observing the behavior of bees to understand their declining populations. 科学家们正在大规模观察蜜蜂的行为，以了解它们数量下降的原因。

The project uses satellite imagery to mass-observe changes in forest cover. 该项目使用卫星图像大规模观察森林覆盖的变化。 | 近义词: monitor, survey, track | 反义词: ignore, neglect | 词性: 动词

Etymology:  "mass-" + "observe". "Mass-" comes from the Greek "maza" meaning "barley cake," later referring to a lump or large quantity. "Observe" comes from the Latin "observare" meaning "to watch, heed, comply with."
