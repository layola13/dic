mirth:/mɜːrθ/| n.|欢乐，高兴，欢笑|Amusement, especially as expressed in laughter.|The room was filled with mirth and laughter. 房间里充满了欢声笑语。|Synonyms: gaiety, merriment, glee, jollity|Antonyms: sadness, sorrow, gloom, grief|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mirthe*, from Old English *myrgþ* (“mirth, joy, pleasure, happiness”), from Proto-Germanic *murgijō* (“joy”), from Proto-Indo-European *merǵʰ-* (“to shine, gleam; to be excited, short”). Cognate with Old Frisian *merthe* (“joy”), Old Saxon *murthi* (“joy”), Middle Dutch *merte* (“joy, pleasure”), Old High German *murti* (“joy”), German *Mirte*, Old Norse *myrð* (“joy, pleasure”), Gothic *𐌼𐌰𐍂𐌶𐌴𐌹𐌽𐍃* (marzeins, “joyful”). Related to merry.
