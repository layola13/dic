conventionalists:/kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪsts/
n.
墨守成规者; 惯例主义者
Those who adhere to conventionalism, a philosophical theory that basic principles of science or morality are grounded on agreements rather than on objective reality.
Einstein's theories were initially met with resistance from conventionalists who clung to Newtonian physics.  爱因斯坦的理论最初遭到了墨守成规者的抵制，他们坚持牛顿物理学。
The conventionalists argued that the new artistic movement was merely a fad. 这些墨守成规者认为新的艺术运动只是一时的风尚。
synonyms: traditionalists, conformists, conservatives
antonyms: radicals, revolutionaries, innovators, nonconformists
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From conventional + -ist.  "Conventional" comes from the Late Latin "conventionālis",  meaning "agreed upon". "-ist" is a suffix denoting a person who adheres to a particular doctrine, system, or belief.
