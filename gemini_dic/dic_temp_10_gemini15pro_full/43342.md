crampness:/ˈkræmpnɪs/| n. |痉挛，抽筋，紧绷|A state of being cramped; a painful, involuntary contraction of a muscle or muscles.|The crampness in her calf muscle made it difficult to walk. 她小腿肌肉的痉挛使她行走困难。|近义词: tightness, stiffness, spasm, contraction|反义词: looseness, flexibility, relaxation|词性:名词

Etymology:
From cramp + -ness.  "cramp" comes from Middle English crampe, from Old North French crampe, from Frankish *krampōn "to bend, hook," of Germanic origin. "-ness" is a native English suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being."
