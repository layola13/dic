mediatize:/ˈmiːdiətaɪz/ | v. |使媒体化；通过媒体传播；受媒体影响|To make something subject to or dependent on the media; to influence or control something through the use of media; to be influenced or controlled by media. |例句：The war has been highly mediatized, with constant updates and analysis on television and online. 这场战争已经被高度媒体化，电视和网络上不断有更新和分析。

例句：Social media has mediatized many aspects of our lives, from how we communicate to how we consume news. 社交媒体已经使我们生活的许多方面媒体化，从我们如何沟通到我们如何消费新闻。|近义词：publicize, broadcast, disseminate |反义词: censor, suppress, conceal |词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *médiatiser*, from *média* (“media”) + *-iser* (suffix forming verbs from nouns or adjectives).  Ultimately from Latin *medius* ("middle").
