back-door:/ˌbækˈdɔːr/
back-door:| adj. n.|后门的，秘密的；后门，秘密途径|Relating to or involving a method that is not the usual or official one, often secret or indirect.  A way of achieving something that is not the usual, official, or intended way, often secret and involving deception. |The hackers gained access through a back-door in the system. 黑客通过系统中的一个后门获得了访问权限。|Synonyms: clandestine, covert, secret, surreptitious, unauthorized|Antonyms: authorized, official, overt, public|adjective, noun

back-door:/ˌbækˈdɔːr/
back-door:| v.|  非法侵入，走后门 |To gain access to a computer system or network illegally, especially by exploiting a security vulnerability. To achieve something in an indirect or unofficial way.|They back-doored the system to steal sensitive data. 他们走后门进入系统窃取敏感数据。|Synonyms: bypass, circumvent, exploit|Antonyms: comply, follow, obey|verb
