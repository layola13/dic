boggard:/ˈbɒɡərd/ (UK) /ˈbɑːɡərd/ (US)| n.| 鬼怪，妖怪，吓人的东西|A mythical creature that inhabits bogs and marshes, often taking a shape that reflects the viewer's deepest fears. It is often mischievous and frightening, but not necessarily malicious. |例句：The old woman told stories of boggards lurking in the swamp, waiting to snatch unsuspecting travelers. 老妇人讲述了潜伏在沼泽中的鬼怪，等待着抓走毫无戒心的旅行者的故事。

例句：He was convinced a boggard lived in his closet, transforming into his worst nightmares. 他确信他的衣橱里住着一个妖怪，会变成他最可怕的噩梦。|近义词：goblin, hobgoblin, bogeyman, bugbear|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *boggarde, from bogge (“bog”) + -ard (suffix forming nouns denoting people who regularly perform an action or engage in a specific activity, or live in a particular place).
