frowning: /ˈfraʊnɪŋ/ | adj. | 皱眉的，不悦的 |  An expression on the face in which the eyebrows are drawn together and the forehead wrinkled, typically expressing disapproval, displeasure, or concentration.  |  She gave him a frowning look. 她给了他一个皱眉的表情。| synonyms: scowling, glowering, grimacing | antonyms: smiling, beaming, grinning | adjective

frown: /fraʊn/ | n. vi. | 皱眉，蹙额 | An act of frowning; a facial expression of dislike or displeasure. | He looked at her with a frown. 他皱着眉头看着她。 | synonyms: scowl, glower, grimace | antonyms: smile, grin, beam | noun/verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *frygna (“to wrinkle”), from Proto-Germanic *frīgnōną (“to wrinkle one's nose, snort”). Cognate with Middle High German frunen (“to wrinkle”), Old English frīnan (“to ask, inquire”).
