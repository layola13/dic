fretty:/ˈfrɛti/| adj.|烦躁的，易怒的|Characterized by excessive irritability or nervousness.  Easily annoyed or upset.|The baby was fretty because it was teething.  婴儿因为正在长牙而烦躁不安。|irritable, peevish, fractious, petulant|calm, patient, placid|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fretten*, frequentative of Old English *fretan* (“to devour, consume”), from Proto-Germanic *fretaną* (“to gnaw, eat”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰer- (“to rub, grind”).  Cognate with Old Frisian *fretta* (“to gnaw, rub”), Old Saxon *fretan* (“to devour”), Old High German *frezzan* (“to devour”), Old Norse *freta* (“to gnaw, corrode”), Gothic *𐍆𐍂𐌹𐍄𐌰𐌽* (*fritan*, “to devour”).  More at fret.  The sense development is from the notion of being "gnawed at" by worry or annoyance.
