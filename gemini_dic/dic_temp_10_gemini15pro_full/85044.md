harboured:/ˈhɑːbəd/ (UK) /ˈhɑːrbərd/ (US)| v. |窝藏；怀有；庇护；包含|To give shelter or refuge to; to hold (a thought or feeling) secretly.|The fugitives were harboured by sympathetic villagers.  逃亡者被同情他们的村民藏匿起来。|Synonyms: shelter, conceal, harbor, shield, protect|Antonyms: expose, reveal, betray|Verb

harbour:/ˈhɑːbə(r)/ (UK) /ˈhɑːrbər/ (US)| n. |港口；避难所|A place on the coast where ships may moor in shelter, especially one protected from rough water by piers, jetties, and other artificial structures.|The ships sailed into the harbour at dawn.  船只在黎明时分驶入港口。|Synonyms: port, haven, refuge, sanctuary|Antonyms: exposure, danger|Noun
