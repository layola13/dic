eche:/eɪtʃ/| n.|蚀刻；腐蚀；水腐蚀|A shallow inlet or channel in a shoreline, especially one formed by the action of water.|The rising tide filled the eche.|近义词:inlet, channel, creek|反义词:promontory, headland|词性:名词

词源:
From Middle English *eche, from Old North French *esche, from Frankish *skankja (“shank, leg”), from Proto-Germanic *skankō (“bent, crooked”). Cognate with Old English sceanca (“leg”), Middle Dutch schenke (“shin, leg”), Old High German scenka, scanka (“leg”), German Schenkel (“leg”). More at shank.
