counteradaptations:/ˌkaʊntərˌædæpˈteɪʃənz/ | n. |反适应，对抗适应|Evolutionary responses by one organism to adaptations in another organism with which it interacts, such as a predator adapting to prey defenses or prey adapting to predator strategies.|Example: The rough skin of the newt is a counteradaptation to the garter snake's resistance to its toxins. 蝾螈粗糙的皮肤是对袜带蛇对其毒素抵抗力的反适应。|Example:  The development of camouflage in insects is a counteradaptation to the sharp eyesight of birds. 昆虫伪装的发展是对鸟类敏锐视力的反适应。| 近义词: coevolutionary responses, reciprocal adaptations | 反义词: adaptations | 词性: 名词
