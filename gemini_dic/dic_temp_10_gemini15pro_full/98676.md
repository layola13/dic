insulator:/ˈɪnsjəleɪtər/ (US), /ˈɪnsjʊleɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n. |绝缘体，隔离体|A material or device that prevents the passage of heat, electricity, or sound.|例句：Rubber is a good insulator. 橡胶是良好的绝缘体。|近义词：nonconductor, dielectric|反义词: conductor|词性: noun

insulators:/ˈɪnsjəleɪtərz/ (US), /ˈɪnsjʊleɪtəz/ (UK)| n. |绝缘体，隔离体（复数）|Plural form of "insulator," referring to multiple materials or devices that prevent the passage of heat, electricity, or sound.|例句：The insulators on the power lines prevent electrocution. 电力线上的绝缘体防止触电。|近义词：nonconductors, dielectrics|反义词: conductors|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *insulator, from Latin insulare "to make into an island, isolate," from insula "island."  The electrical sense emerged in the mid-19th century.
