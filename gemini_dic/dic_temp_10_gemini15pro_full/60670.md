electorship: /ɪˈlɛktərʃɪp/
n.
选民资格；选民的职位；选民身份
The position or status of an elector.  The right to vote in an election. The term of office of an elector.
He lost his electorship after moving out of the district. 他搬出该区后失去了选民资格。
She valued her electorship and always voted in every election. 她很重视自己的选民身份，每次选举都会投票。

近义词: franchise, suffrage, right to vote
反义词: disenfranchisement

词性: noun
词源: From "elector" + "-ship". "Elector" comes from the Latin "ēlector" (nominator), from "ēligere" (to choose out).  "-ship" is an Old English suffix denoting state, condition, or office.
