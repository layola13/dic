curialism: /ˈkjʊəriəlɪzəm/ | n. |教廷主义; 教皇至上论 | The belief in the supreme authority of the Pope in matters of faith and morals, and in the importance of the Roman Curia (the administrative body of the Holy See). |  The rise of curialism led to conflicts with national churches. 教廷主义的兴起导致了与国家教会的冲突。 |  The king rejected curialism and asserted the independence of the English Church. 国王反对教廷主义，并维护英国教会的独立性。| 近义词: papalism, ultramontanism | 反义词: conciliarism, Gallicanism | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cūriālis* "belonging to the court," from Latin *cūria* "court" + *-ālis*, suffix forming adjectives.  The English word "curialism" is a later formation, likely influenced by similar abstract nouns ending in "-ism."
