chamois:/ˈʃæmi/ (US), /ˈʃæmwɑː/ (UK)| n. |羚羊；麂皮；擦拭用软皮|A type of goat-antelope native to mountains of Europe, or a soft, pliable leather traditionally made from its skin, now also from sheepskin or other skins.|He used a chamois to dry the car. 他用一块麂皮擦干了汽车。|Synonyms: antelope, goat, leather, shammy|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for the animal. For the leather, perhaps "rough cloth")|noun


chamois:/ˈʃæmi/ (US), /ˈʃæmwɑː/ (UK)| adj. |麂皮色的；浅黄色的|Of a pale yellowish-beige color.|She wore a chamois-colored dress. 她穿着一件浅黄色的连衣裙。|Synonyms: buff, beige, cream|Antonyms: dark, black|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *chamois*, from Late Latin *camoxiā*, from Gaulish *camos* ("chamois").
