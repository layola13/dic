grandfatherhood:/ˈɡrændˌfɑːðərˌhʊd/| n. |祖父的身份；祖父的职责|The state or condition of being a grandfather; the role and responsibilities associated with being a grandfather. |He embraced grandfatherhood with enthusiasm. 他热情地拥抱了祖父的角色。| My grandfather found great joy in grandfatherhood. 我的祖父在身为人祖父的过程中找到了巨大的快乐。 | 近义词: paternity (fatherhood), maternity (motherhood) | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but conceptually: childhood, youth)| noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From grandfather + -hood.  Grandfather comes from Middle English *grandfader*, from Old French *grantpere*.  *-hood* is an Old English suffix denoting "state, condition, quality, character".
