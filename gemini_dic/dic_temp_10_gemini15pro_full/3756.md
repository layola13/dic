agistator:/ˈædʒɪteɪtər/| n.|煽动者，鼓动者|A person who stirs up public opinion or feeling, often in a political context; one who incites others to rebellion or other disruptive action.|The agitator was arrested for inciting a riot.  这个煽动者因煽动暴乱而被捕。|Synonyms: rabble-rouser, demagogue, firebrand, provocateur|Antonyms: peacemaker, mediator, conciliator|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *agitator, from Latin agitare ("to drive, move, stir up"), frequentative of agere ("to do, act, drive").
