idiomorphous:/ˌɪdiəˈmɔːrfəs/ | adj. | 自形的，特形的 | Having a distinct and characteristic form; crystallizing in a form peculiar to itself.  A mineral is said to be idiomorphous when its crystals have their own characteristic forms, not determined by the surrounding crystals. |  Example: The idiomorphous crystals of pyrite stood out against the matrix.  黄铁矿的自形晶体在基质中很突出。

Example:  Idiomorphous quartz crystals are often hexagonal prisms. 自形的石英晶体通常是六棱柱。 | Synonym:  euhedral, automorphic | Antonym:  anhedral, xenomorphic | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *idios* ("own, peculiar, private") + *morphē* ("form, shape").
