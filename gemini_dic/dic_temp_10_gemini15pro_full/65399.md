euphemize:/ˈjuːfɪmaɪz/ | v. |委婉地说；用委婉语表达|To express an unpleasant or offensive idea in a more pleasant or socially acceptable way. |The government euphemized the war as a "police action." 政府委婉地把这场战争称为“警察行动”。| The company euphemized the layoffs as "restructuring." 公司委婉地把裁员称为“重组”。 | 近义词: palliate, gloss over, whitewash, sugarcoat | 反义词: blurt out, speak frankly |词性: 动词

euphemism: /ˈjuːfɪmɪzəm/ | n. |委婉语；委婉说法 | A mild or indirect word or expression substituted for one considered to be too harsh or blunt when referring to something unpleasant or embarrassing. |"Passed away" is a euphemism for "died." “去世”是“死”的委婉说法。 | "Collateral damage" is a euphemism for civilian casualties. “附带损害”是对平民伤亡的委婉说法。 | 近义词: circumlocution, understatement | 反义词: dysphemism |词性: 名词

词源: From the Greek *euphemismos*, from *eu* "well" + *pheme* "speech" + *-ismos* suffix forming nouns of action.
