antaeus:/ænˈtiːəs/ | n. | 安泰俄斯; 安泰 | Antaeus, in Greek mythology, a giant of Libya, the son of Gaia (Earth) and Poseidon (Sea). He was invincible in wrestling as long as he remained in contact with his mother, Earth. |  Heracles discovered the secret of Antaeus's strength and, lifting him into the air, crushed him to death. | 近义词: giant, titan | 反义词: dwarf, pygmy | 词性: noun
