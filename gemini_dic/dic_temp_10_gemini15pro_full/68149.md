failers:/ˈfeɪlərz/
n.
失败者；失败的人
People who do not succeed in a particular activity or undertaking.
Many exam failers simply didn't prepare properly. 许多考试不及格的人只是没有做好充分的准备。
近义词:losers, non-achievers
反义词:winners, achievers
词性:名词

Etymology:
From the verb "fail" + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting persons who perform an action or are in a certain condition). "Fail" comes from Old French "faillir" (to be lacking, miss, default, err), from Latin "fallere" (to deceive, trick, disappoint).
