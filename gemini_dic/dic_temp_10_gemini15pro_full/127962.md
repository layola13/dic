neorealist:/ˌniːoʊˈriːəlɪst/ | n. adj. | 新现实主义者；新现实主义的 | A proponent or practitioner of neorealism, especially in film or literature, characterized by a focus on social realities, ordinary people, and everyday life, often with a documentary-like style.  |  Example: The film Bicycle Thieves is a classic example of Italian neorealism.  电影《偷自行车的人》是意大利新现实主义的经典之作。 | Example:  He was a staunch neorealist in his filmmaking approach. 他在电影制作方法上是一个坚定的新现实主义者。 | Synonyms: naturalist, verist | Antonyms: romanticist, idealist | noun, adjective

neorealistic:/ˌniːoʊˌriːəˈlɪstɪk/ | adj. | 新现实主义的 | Relating to or characteristic of neorealism. | Example: The film had a gritty, neorealistic style. 这部电影有一种粗粝的新现实主义风格。 | Example: Her neorealistic approach to writing resonated with readers. 她新现实主义的写作手法引起了读者共鸣。 | Synonyms: naturalistic, verist | Antonyms: romantic, idealistic | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"neo-" (new) + "realism" (the attitude or practice of accepting a situation as it is and being prepared to deal with it accordingly). From the Italian "neorealismo".
