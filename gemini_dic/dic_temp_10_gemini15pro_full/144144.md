outsettler:/ˈaʊtˌsɛtlər/| n.|移民，殖民者|A person who settles in a new region, especially one far from their native land, often displacing indigenous inhabitants.|The outsettlers established a new community in the uncharted territory. 移民们在未知的领土上建立了一个新的社区。|近义词：settler, colonist, pioneer, immigrant|反义词：native, indigenous person|词性:noun

Etymology:
From out- + settler.  "Out-" in this context implies moving away from a central or established location. "Settler" refers to one who establishes a residence in a new or previously uninhabited area.
