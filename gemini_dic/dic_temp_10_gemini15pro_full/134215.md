nonindividuals:/ˌnɒnɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlz/
| n. |非个体，非个人|Entities that are not considered individuals, often in a philosophical or political context.  Referring to things or concepts that lack the characteristics typically associated with individual persons, such as autonomy, self-awareness, or distinct identity.|The philosopher argued that corporations are nonindividuals and should not have the same rights as people.|近义词：entities, objects, things|反义词: individuals, persons|词性: Noun
