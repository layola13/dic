castaway:/ˈkæstəweɪ/| n. adj.| 被抛弃的人，漂流者；被抛弃的，遭难的|A person who has been shipwrecked and stranded on a deserted island or coast.|例句：The castaway survived for months on coconuts and fish.  那个漂流者靠椰子鱼活了好几个月。

例句：The castaway built a makeshift shelter from driftwood.  漂流者用浮木搭建了一个临时住所。|近义词：marooned, stranded, shipwrecked, adrift|反义词:rescued, saved|词性:noun, adjective
castaway:/kæst əˈweɪ/| v.|抛弃，丢弃|To throw or drop something overboard or away, especially from a ship or aircraft.|例句：They were forced to castaway most of their supplies to lighten the boat.  他们被迫丢弃大部分补给以减轻船的重量。

例句：The captain ordered the damaged cargo to be castaway. 船长下令将损坏的货物丢弃。|近义词：jettison, discard, dump|反义词:keep, retain|词性:verb

词源: From cast + away.  "Cast" meaning to throw and "away" meaning far or to a distance.  Therefore, "castaway" literally means "thrown far away".  The nautical sense emerged in the 16th century.
