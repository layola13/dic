geniality:/ˌdʒiːniˈælɪti/ | n. | 亲切；和蔼；友好；欢乐 | The quality of being friendly and cheerful. |  She exuded warmth and geniality. 她浑身散发着热情和友善。|  Kindness, warmth, affability, cordiality, amiability | coldness, hostility, unfriendliness | noun

genial:/ˈdʒiːniəl/ | adj. | 和蔼的；亲切的；友好的；温暖的 | Friendly and cheerful. |  He was a genial host, making everyone feel welcome. 他是一位和蔼可亲的主人，让每个人都感到宾至如归。|  Friendly, amiable, affable, cordial, warm | unfriendly, cold, hostile, aloof | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin genialis "festive, jovial, pertaining to marriage," also "of or belonging to a genius, native," from genius "guardian deity or spirit which watches over each person from birth; innate ability or inclination" (see genius). The meaning "cheerful, kindly" is first recorded in English 1716.
