cotemporaneous:/kənˌtɛmpəˈreɪniəs/| adj.|同时代的，同时发生的|Existing or occurring in the same period of time.|The two artists were cotemporaneous, both flourishing in the early 20th century.  这两位艺术家是同时代的，都在20世纪早期繁荣发展。|例句：The rise of the internet was cotemporaneous with the decline of traditional media. 互联网的兴起与传统媒体的衰落同时发生。|近义词：concurrent, simultaneous, synchronous|反义词：non-concurrent, asynchronous|词性:形容词

cotemporary:/kənˈtɛmpəˌrɛri/| adj.  n.|当代的，同时代的；同时代的人|Living or occurring at the same time.|Shakespeare and Marlowe were contemporaries. 莎士比亚和马洛是同时代的人。  The building's design is contemporary. 这栋建筑的设计很现代。|例句：He was a contemporary of George Washington. 他与乔治·华盛顿是同时代的人。|近义词：coeval, synchronous, simultaneous|反义词：ancient, prehistoric|词性:形容词,名词
