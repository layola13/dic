grabbed /ɡræbd/ | v. | 抓住，攫取 | past tense of "grab" -  took hold of something suddenly and roughly. | He grabbed his coat and ran out the door. 他抓起外套，冲出门外。|  She grabbed the child's hand to stop her from running into the street. 她抓住孩子的手，阻止她跑到街上。| seized, snatched, grasped | released, dropped | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Proto-Germanic *grabōną (“to scratch, dig”), from Proto-Indo-European *gʰrebʰ- (“to dig, scratch, scrape”). Cognate with Dutch grabbelen (“to grabble”), German graben (“to dig”). First attested around 1770.
