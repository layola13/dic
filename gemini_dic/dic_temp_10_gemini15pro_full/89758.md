hopscotching:/ˈhɒpskɒtʃɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːpskɑːtʃɪŋ/ (US)| n. v.|跳房子游戏；快速地从一地到另一地；跳跃式发展|The act of playing hopscotch or moving rapidly from one place or thing to another, often in a seemingly random or opportunistic way.  It can also refer to a method of progress that involves skipping over intermediate stages.|例句：The children were hopscotching on the sidewalk. 孩子们在人行道上跳房子。

例句：The politician was accused of hopscotching around the issues.  这位政客被指责对问题避重就轻。|近义词：skipping, jumping, leaping, bypassing, hopping, zigzagging|反义词:plodding, proceeding systematically, staying put|词性:Noun (primarily), Verb (Gerund or present participle)


Etymology:
From "hopscotch," a children's game involving hopping over marked squares. "Hop" (Old English *hoppan) + "scotch" (Old French escocher, "to cut, score").
