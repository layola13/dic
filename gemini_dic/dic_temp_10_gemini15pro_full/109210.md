leibniz: /ˈlaɪbnɪts/ | n. | 莱布尼茨 |  German polymath and philosopher Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz (1646-1716), known for his contributions to mathematics, philosophy, and logic.  He developed calculus independently of Newton and is also known for his work on the concept of monads. |  Leibniz is considered one of the greatest thinkers of the 17th century. 莱布尼茨被认为是17世纪最伟大的思想家之一。 | synonym: philosopher, mathematician, logician | antonym: N/A | noun
