outplod:/aʊtˈplɒd/ (UK) /aʊtˈplɑːd/ (US)| v. | 艰难地行走；跋涉 | to walk slowly and heavily, especially because you are tired or the ground is difficult |  He outplodded his rivals and won the marathon. 他艰难地战胜了对手，赢得了马拉松比赛。|  She outplodded the last mile of the hike, exhausted but determined. 她精疲力竭却意志坚定地走完了徒步旅行的最后一英里。 | 近义词：trudge, plod, slog | 反义词：sprint, dash, race | 词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + plod.  "Out-" here intensifies the meaning of "plod" suggesting a more arduous or prolonged effort. "Plod" itself comes from a Middle English word of uncertain origin, possibly imitative of the sound of heavy footsteps.
