caperers:/ˈkeɪpərərz/| n. |嬉戏玩闹的人; 跳跃者|People who are playfully jumping or skipping about.  Those who engage in frivolous activities.|The children were caperers in the park, running and jumping with glee. 孩子们在公园里嬉戏玩闹，高兴地跑来跑去。|近义词：revellers, frolickers, gambolers|反义词：sluggards, laggards|Noun

词源: From the verb "caper" + "-er" (agent suffix) + "-s" (plural suffix). "Caper" comes from the Middle French "capre" (a leap, jump), ultimately from the Latin "capreolus" (a wild goat), referring to the goat's playful jumping.
