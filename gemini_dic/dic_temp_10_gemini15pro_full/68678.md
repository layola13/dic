farce:/ˈfɑːrs/| n. |闹剧，滑稽剧|A farce is a comic dramatic work using buffoonery and horseplay and typically including crude characterization and ludicrously improbable situations.|The whole trial was a farce.|近义词：burlesque, parody, travesty|反义词：tragedy|词性:Noun (singular)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *farcir* "to stuff," from Latin *farcire* "to stuff." Originally referred to stuffing added to liturgical texts, later to comic interludes stuffed into religious dramas, and eventually to any broadly humorous play.
