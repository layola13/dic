overmodestly:/ˌoʊvərˈmɑːdɪstli/| adv.| 过分谦虚地，过分谦逊地 | In an excessively modest manner; more modestly than is warranted or appropriate.  The word is formed by combining the prefix "over-" (meaning "excessively" or "too much") with the adjective "modest" and the adverbial suffix "-ly".  "Modest" itself comes from the Latin word "modestus," meaning "keeping due measure." | She overmodestly downplayed her role in the project. 她过分谦虚地淡化了她在项目中的作用。| 近义词: unassumingly, diffidently | 反义词: proudly, vaingloriously | 词性: 副词
