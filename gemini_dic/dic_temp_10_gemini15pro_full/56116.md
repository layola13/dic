doctrinalism: /ˈdɒktrɪnəlɪzəm/ | n. | 教条主义; 教义至上论 | Adherence to doctrines or the tendency to regard them as of primary importance.  A focus on the theoretical or theologically correct aspects of a belief system, sometimes to the exclusion of practical considerations. |  His doctrinalism made him inflexible in his views. 他的教条主义使他的观点僵化。|  The party's strict doctrinalism alienated many potential supporters.  该党严格的教条主义疏远了许多潜在的支持者。 | synonyms: dogmatism, rigidity, orthodoxy | antonyms: pragmatism, flexibility, open-mindedness | noun
