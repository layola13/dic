hand-hot:/ˈhændˌhɒt/ | adj. |  滚烫的，刚出炉的 |  Warm enough to be uncomfortable to hold; very recently made or produced. | The baker handed me a hand-hot loaf of bread. 面包师递给我一个刚出炉的热面包。 | The hand-hot pies were delicious. 刚出炉的馅饼很好吃。 |  近义词： piping hot, scorching, burning | 反义词： cold, freezing | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
Compound of "hand" and "hot."  "Hand" in this context refers to the feeling of heat in one's hand. "Hot" refers to high temperature.
