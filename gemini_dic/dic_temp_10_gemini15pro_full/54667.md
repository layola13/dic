disoblige:/ˌdɪsəˈblaɪdʒ/ | vt. | 怠慢；使不悦；拒绝帮助 | To offend or annoy someone by refusing a request or failing to do something expected. |

例句：
I didn't mean to disoblige her, but I couldn't agree to her request.
我没有想怠慢她，但我无法同意她的请求。

I hope I haven't disobliged you by refusing your invitation.
我希望我拒绝你的邀请没有使你不悦。


近义词：offend, annoy, slight, displease, refuse, decline
反义词：oblige, accommodate, please, gratify
词性：动词


词源：
From French *dés- + obliger (“to oblige”), from Latin obligō, from ob (“toward”) + ligō (“to bind”).  The prefix dis- indicates negation or reversal.
