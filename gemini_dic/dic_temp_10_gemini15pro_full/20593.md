Blackfeet: /ˈblækˌfiːt/ (American English) /ˈblækfiːt/ (British English) | n. (plural) | 黑脚族（北美印第安部落） |  Refers to a Native American tribe, the Siksikaitsitapi, who historically inhabited the northern plains of North America.  The name likely originated from the dark color of their moccasins, possibly stained from prairie fires or dyed. |  The Blackfeet Nation holds an annual powwow.  黑脚族每年都会举行帕瓦仪式。

The Blackfeet Reservation spans parts of Montana.  黑脚族保留地跨越蒙大拿州的部分地区。 | 近义词: Siksika (their own name),  | 反义词: N/A (No single antonym for a specific tribe) | noun
