mendicant:/ˈmɛndɪkənt/| n. adj.|乞丐；行乞的|A beggar.  Characterized by begging.|例句：The mendicant sat by the roadside, hoping for spare change. 乞丐坐在路边，希望能得到一些零钱。

mendicant:/ˈmɛndɪkənt/| n. adj.|乞丐；行乞的|Someone who lives by begging.  Depicting the practice of begging.|例句：He lived a mendicant life, traveling from town to town. 他过着乞讨的生活，从一个城镇到另一个城镇。|近义词：beggar, pauper, supplicant|反义词:rich person, philanthropist|词性:noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *mendicaunt*, from Old French *mendicant*, present participle of *mendicanter* "to beg", from Latin *mendīcāns*, present participle of *mendīcāre* "to beg", from *mendīcus* "beggar".
