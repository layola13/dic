convoy:/ˈkɒnvɔɪ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnvɔɪ/ (US)| n. vt.|护航，护送；车队，船队|A group of vehicles or ships traveling together, typically one accompanied by armed troops, warships, or other vehicles for protection.|The army convoy moved slowly through the mountains. 军队车队缓慢地穿过山区。|The ships formed a convoy for protection against pirates. 这些船只组成船队以防海盗。|近义词：escort, guard, fleet, procession|反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "disperse" for the act of separating a convoy) |noun, verb


convoy:/ˈkɒnvɔɪ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnvɔɪ/ (US)| vt.|护航，护送|To accompany and protect (a group of vehicles, ships, etc.) on a journey.|Warships convoyed the merchant ships across the Atlantic. 军舰护送商船横渡大西洋。|Police convoyed the President's car to the airport. 警察护送总统的汽车去机场。|近义词：escort, guard, protect|反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "abandon" in the context of protection) |verb
