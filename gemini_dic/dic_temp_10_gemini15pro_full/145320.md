overbroadens:/ˌoʊvərˈbrɔːdənz/
|v.|过度扩展，扩大化|To expand or generalize something to an excessive degree, making it less precise or accurate.|The law overbroadens the definition of hate speech and could stifle legitimate criticism.  该法律过度扩展了仇恨言论的定义，可能会扼杀合法的批评。|extends too widely, generalizes too much|narrows, restricts, specifies|verb
overbroadening:/ˌoʊvərˈbrɔːdənɪŋ/
|n.|过度扩展，扩大化|The act of expanding or generalizing something to an excessive degree.|The overbroadening of the law has led to unintended consequences.  法律的过度扩展导致了意想不到的后果。|excessive expansion, overgeneralization|narrowing, restriction, specification|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
"Over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi) meaning "above, beyond, excessively" + "broaden" (from "broad" + "-en," a suffix forming verbs from adjectives) meaning "to make broad or broader."  Therefore, "overbroaden" literally means "to broaden excessively."
