dowlas: /ˈdaʊləs/| n.|粗麻布; Dowlas布 (一种廉价的亚麻布或棉布)|A coarse linen or cotton fabric. Dowlas was historically used for making inexpensive clothing, bedding, and other household items.|She wore a simple dowlas gown. 她穿着一件简单的粗麻布长袍。|Synonyms: calico, canvas, linen, muslin|Antonyms: silk, satin|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dowlas, from Old French *doulas, from Daoulas, a town in Brittany, France, where the cloth was originally made.
