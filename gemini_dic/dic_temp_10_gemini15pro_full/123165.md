monkflower: /ˈmʌŋkˌflaʊər/ (US), /ˈmʌŋkˌflaʊə(r)/ (UK)| n. |修道士花，猴面花|A plant of the genus *Mimulus*, especially *Mimulus guttatus*, with brightly colored, often spotted flowers.  These flowers resemble the hooded faces of monks, hence the name.|The monkflower thrives in damp environments.|近义词：bush monkeyflower, common monkeyflower, seep monkeyflower (for *Mimulus guttatus*)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "monk" + "flower".  The name "monkflower" comes from the resemblance of the flower's shape to the hooded face of a monk.  The genus name *Mimulus* comes from the Latin *mimus*, meaning "mime" or "actor," referring to the flower's "face-like" appearance. *Guttatus* means "spotted," referring to the spots often found on the petals.
