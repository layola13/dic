heterogynous:/ˌhɛtərˈɒdʒɪnəs/ | adj. | 異性的，雌雄異株的，異型配子的 | Having different kinds of female and male gametes or reproductive organs.  Relating to or characterized by heterogony.| Example: "Some algae exhibit heterogynous reproduction." 一些藻类表现出异型配子繁殖。| Example: "Heterogynous plants have separate male and female flowers." 异性植物有单独的雄花和雌花。 | Synonyms: dioecious, unisexual | Antonyms: homogamous, monoecious | Part of Speech: adjective

heterogony:/ˌhɛtərˈɒɡəni/ | n. | 異型世代交替，異速生長 | Alternation of generations in which the sexual and parthenogenetic generations differ morphologically. Also, the condition of having different growth rates in different parts of the body, resulting in a change of proportions during development. | Example: "Aphids display a complex life cycle involving heterogony." 蚜蟲表現出涉及異型世代交替的複雜生命週期。 | Example: "The fiddler crab’s enlarged claw is a result of heterogony." 招潮蟹的巨螯是异速生长的结果。 | Synonyms: allometry (for unequal growth rate) | Antonyms: isometry (for equal growth rate) | Part of Speech: noun
