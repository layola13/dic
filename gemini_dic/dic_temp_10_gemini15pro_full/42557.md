counteroffensives: /ˌkaʊntərəˈfɛnsɪvz/ | n. |反攻，反击|A counteroffensive is an attack made in response to an enemy's attack. |The general planned a swift counteroffensive to regain lost territory. 将军计划迅速反攻以夺回失地。| Counterattack, offensive |Defense, retreat | Noun

counteroffensive: /ˌkaʊntərəˈfɛnsɪv/ | adj. |反攻的，反击的|Relating to or constituting a counteroffensive. |The army launched a series of counteroffensive operations. 军队发动了一系列反攻行动。|Offensive, attacking |Defensive, retreating | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + offensive.  "Counter-" comes from the Old French "contre-"  meaning "against," ultimately from the Latin "contra." "Offensive" comes from the Old French "offensif,"  from the Late Latin "offensivus," meaning "attacking," from "offendere"  "to strike against."
