doorstepping:/ˈdɔːrˌstɛpɪŋ/ | n. |上门采访；上门推销；登门拜访 | The act of going to someone's home, often unexpectedly, to try to talk to them, especially a journalist seeking an interview or a salesperson trying to sell something. | Reporters were doorstepping the celebrity's house all day. 记者整天都在名人家的门口守候采访。| Synonyms: canvassing, soliciting, cold-calling | Antonyms: avoiding, ignoring | noun

doorstepping:/ˈdɔːrˌstɛpɪŋ/ | v. |上门采访；上门推销；登门拜访 | To go to someone's home, often unexpectedly, to try to talk to them, especially a journalist seeking an interview or a salesperson trying to sell something. | The charity workers were doorstepping residents to ask for donations. 慈善工作者挨家挨户上门募捐。| Synonyms: canvass, solicit, cold-call | Antonyms: avoid, ignore | verb
