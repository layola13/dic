mildewed:/ˈmɪlduːd/ | adj. | 发霉的；沾染霉菌的 | Affected with mildew; musty. | The old books in the attic were mildewed and smelled damp.  阁楼里的旧书发霉了，闻起来很潮湿。| Synonyms: moldy, musty, fusty | Antonyms: clean, fresh, dry | adjective


mildew:/ˈmɪlduː/ | n. | 霉，霉菌 | A superficial growth of minute fungi forming on various surfaces, especially in humid conditions. | The dampness in the basement caused mildew to grow on the walls.  地下室的潮湿导致墙壁上长了霉菌。| Synonyms: mold, fungus | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider "cleanliness") | noun


mildew:/ˈmɪlduː/ | v. |  (使)发霉 | To become or cause to become covered with mildew. | The damp clothes mildewed in the closet.  潮湿的衣服在壁橱里发霉了。 | Synonyms: mold, rot | Antonyms: dry, preserve | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mildēaw, from mil "honey" + dēaw "dew".  The original sense was "honeydew," referring to a sweet, sticky substance deposited by aphids. Over time, the term came to refer to the growth of fungi resembling this substance.
