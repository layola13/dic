discourse:/ˈdɪskɔːrs/ (US), /ˈdɪskɔːs/ (UK)| n. vi.| 谈话，演讲；论述，讨论；论文|Verbal or written communication; a formal discussion of a subject.|例句：The professor gave a discourse on the history of philosophy. 教授做了一个关于哲学史的演讲。|近义词：discussion, conversation, speech, lecture, treatise, essay|反义词:silence, quiet|词性:noun, verb

discourse:/ˈdɪskɔːrs/ (US), /ˈdɪskɔːs/ (UK)| vi.| 演讲，谈论|To communicate thoughts orally; to give a formal speech.|例句：He discoursed eloquently on the need for social justice. 他雄辩地论述了社会正义的必要性。|近义词：speak, talk, lecture, expound|反义词:be silent|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French discours (“reasoning, speech”), from Latin discursus (“a running about, a going to and fro; conversation”), from the past participle stem of discurrere (“to run about, run to and fro; speak discursively”), from dis- (“apart, in different directions”) + currere (“to run”).
