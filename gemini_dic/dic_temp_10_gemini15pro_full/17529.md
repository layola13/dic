beat-up:/ˌbiːt ˈʌp/ | adj. | 破旧的，破烂的；(非正式) 疲惫不堪的 | Damaged or worn from use or abuse; (informal) very tired |  The car was old and beat-up. 这辆车又旧又破。 | synonyms: battered, worn, shabby, dilapidated, exhausted (informal) | antonyms: new, pristine, mint, refreshed | adjective

beat-up:/ˌbiːt ˈʌp/ | n. | (非正式) 殴打 | (informal) A physical attack or beating | He gave the bully a good beat-up. 他把那个恶霸狠狠地揍了一顿。| synonyms: beating, thrashing | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb phrase "beat up," meaning to strike repeatedly, causing damage or injury.  The adjective form developed to describe the resulting state of something that has been beaten up. The noun form is a shortened version of the phrase.
