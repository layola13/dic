d-glucose:/ˌdiːˈɡluːkoʊs/ /ˌdiːˈɡluːkəʊs/
noun
D-葡萄糖
A simple sugar (monosaccharide) and an important carbohydrate in energy metabolism.  It is the most common form of glucose and is also known as dextrose.
D-glucose is the primary source of energy for the body's cells.
dextrose, grape sugar
