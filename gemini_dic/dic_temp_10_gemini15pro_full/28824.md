carousing: /kəˈraʊzɪŋ/
noun.|狂欢；寻欢作乐；畅饮|The act of drinking and enjoying oneself with others in a noisy, lively way.|They spent the evening carousing in the pub. 他们晚上在酒吧里狂欢作乐。|近义词：reveling, partying, merrymaking|反义词：abstaining, temperance|n.

carouse: /kəˈraʊz/
verb.|狂欢；畅饮；寻欢作乐|To drink and enjoy oneself with others in a noisy, lively way.|They caroused until dawn. 他们狂欢到天亮。|近义词：revel, party, celebrate|反义词：abstain, refrain|v.


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Gar aus* (entirely out), a drinking toast meaning "drink up!," which became *karaus* in early modern German.  Borrowed into French as *caraus*, and then into English as *carouse*. The meaning shifted from the toast to the act of drinking and merrymaking.
