blindfish:/ˈblaɪndˌfɪʃ/| n.|盲鱼|A fish that is blind, typically living in caves or underground waters.  It has adapted to its dark environment and often lacks eyes or has rudimentary, non-functional eyes.|The blindfish navigated the cave system with ease.  这条盲鱼轻松地在洞穴系统中穿行。|近义词：cavefish|反义词:sighted fish|Noun

Etymology->
From blind + fish.  "Blind" ultimately from Proto-Germanic *blindaz, meaning "without sight." "Fish" from Old English fisc, from Proto-Germanic *fiskaz.
