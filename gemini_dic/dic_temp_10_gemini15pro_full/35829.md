cockleshell:/ˈkɒkəlʃɛl/ (UK), /ˈkɑːkəlʃɛl/ (US)| n. |贝壳；小船；脆弱的东西|A small, fragile shell, often brightly colored, typically of a bivalve mollusk.  Figuratively, it can also refer to a small, frail boat.|例句：The child collected cockleshells on the beach.  孩子在沙滩上收集贝壳。

例句：They sailed to France in a cockleshell. 他们乘坐一艘小船航行到法国。|近义词：seashell, shell, skiff (for boat sense)|反义词: N/A |词性:noun


cockleshell:/ˈkɒkəlʃɛl/ (UK), /ˈkɑːkəlʃɛl/ (US)| adj. |(literary)  tiny; frail; insignificant|Made of or resembling a cockleshell, often implying smallness or fragility.  |例句：Their cockleshell boat was tossed about by the waves.  他们的小船被海浪抛来抛去。


例句：He built a cockleshell of a house. 他建造了一座像贝壳一样脆弱的小房子。|近义词：fragile, flimsy, delicate|反义词: sturdy, robust |词性:adjective
