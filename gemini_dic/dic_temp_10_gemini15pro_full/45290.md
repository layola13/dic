cultureshocked: /ˈkʌltʃərˌʃɑːkt/ | adj. | 文化冲击的 | Experiencing confusion and anxiety when encountering a new culture that is very different from one's own.  A feeling of disorientation and anxiety that people experience when they visit or live in a different culture. |  Example 1: After moving to Japan, she felt completely cultureshocked by the different customs and language.  搬到日本后，她对不同的风俗和语言感到非常不适应。

Example 2: He was so cultureshocked by the food that he only ate at McDonald's for the first week. 他对食物很不适应，以至于第一个星期只在麦当劳吃饭。 | 近义词: disoriented, bewildered, alienated | 反义词: acclimated, adapted, assimilated | 词性: 形容词

Etymology: culture (n.) + shocked (adj.) - The word combines "culture" referring to a set of customs, beliefs, and practices of a particular group, and "shocked" meaning experiencing a sudden, upsetting, or surprising event or experience.
