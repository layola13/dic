anti-pragmatism:/ˌæntiˈpræɡmətɪzəm/| n.|反实用主义|A philosophical doctrine that opposes pragmatism, often emphasizing abstract principles, absolute truths, or a priori reasoning over practical consequences and experience.|He accused the university of promoting anti-pragmatism in its curriculum, prioritizing theory over real-world application.|近义词：idealism, absolutism|反义词:pragmatism, practicality|词性:noun


Etymology:
anti- (prefix): from Greek anti- "against, opposite of"
pragmatism: from Greek pragma "deed, act, affair"
