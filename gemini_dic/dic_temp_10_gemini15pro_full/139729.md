ochre: /ˈoʊkər/ (US) /ˈəʊkə(r)/ (UK)| n. adj.|赭色，赭石|A moderate orange-yellow color;  An earthy pigment containing hydrated iron oxide, varying in color from light yellow to deep orange or brown.|The walls were painted a warm ochre.|Synonyms: yellow ocher, sienna, umber,  gold, tan|Antonyms: blue, green, white|noun
ochre: /ˈoʊkər/ (US) /ˈəʊkə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|赭色的|Having a moderate orange-yellow color.|The ochre cliffs glowed in the setting sun.|Synonyms: yellowish-brown, brownish-yellow, golden-brown|Antonyms: bluish, greenish, whitish|adjective
