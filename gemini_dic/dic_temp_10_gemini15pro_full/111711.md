loco:/ˈloʊkoʊ/ (US) /ˈləʊkəʊ/ (UK)| adj.|疯狂的，精神失常的|Crazy; insane; mentally unstable.  Origin: Spanish, meaning "crazy."  It entered English in the late 19th century, initially referring to a disease of horses and cattle caused by eating certain plants.|He went loco after losing his job. 他失业后疯了。|Synonyms: crazy, insane, mad, nuts, bonkers|Antonyms: sane, rational, sensible|adjective


loco:/ˈloʊkoʊ/ (US) /ˈləʊkəʊ/ (UK)| n.|疯子|A crazy person. |He's a complete loco! 他是个十足的疯子！|Synonyms: lunatic, madman, maniac|Antonyms: sane person|noun
