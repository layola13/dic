corbetts:/ˈkɔːrbɪts/| n. |科比特山峰（指海拔在2500-3000英尺之间的苏格兰山峰）|Scottish mountains between 2,500 and 3,000 feet (762 and 914.4 m) high. These are defined as separate mountains by relative height, and are named after the Scottish mountaineer and writer Jim Corbett, who compiled the first complete list of them.|He bagged three Corbetts in one day.|近义词：peaks, summits, mountains|反义词:valleys|词性:名词
