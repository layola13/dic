mailclad:/ˈmeɪlˌklæd/| adj.|身穿铠甲的，披甲的|Covered with mail; wearing armor.|The mailclad knight charged into battle. 身披铠甲的骑士冲入战场。|近义词：armored, clad in mail|反义词：unarmed, unprotected|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Middle English *maileclad, equivalent to mail + clad.  Mail (chainmail armor) comes from Old French maille, from Latin macula "mesh of a net." Clad comes from Old English geclæded, past participle of geclǣþan "to clothe."
