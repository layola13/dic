consumeristic:/kənˌsuːməˈrɪstɪk/ | adj. | 消费主义的；崇尚消费的 | Characterized by excessive or conspicuous consumption; relating to consumerism.  | Example:  Our society is becoming increasingly consumeristic. 我们的社会越来越趋向消费主义。
Example: The consumeristic culture encourages people to buy more than they need. 消费主义文化鼓励人们购买超过他们需要的东西。 | 近义词: materialistic, acquisitive | 反义词: frugal, thrifty | 词性: adjective

Etymology:  From consumer + -istic.  "Consumer" comes from Late Middle English, from the Old French *consumir, ultimately from the Latin consumere "to consume".  "-istic" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "relating to" or "characteristic of".
