disassimilation:/dɪsˌəˌsɪməˈleɪʃən/ | n. |异化，异化作用，异化作用|The process by which one sound becomes less like a neighboring sound.  This often happens to avoid difficult or repetitive sounds.|The word "February" is often pronounced "Feb-yoo-ary" due to dissimilation; the second 'r' sound is lost.| 近义词: differentiation, divergence | 反义词: assimilation, homogenization | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Latin dissimilāre (“to make unlike”), from dis- (“not, un-”) + similāre (“to make like”).
