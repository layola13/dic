antiliberalism:/ˌæntiˈlɪbərəlɪzəm/
n.|反自由主义|Opposition to liberalism, encompassing a range of ideologies and viewpoints that reject liberal principles, such as individual rights, limited government, and free markets.|The rise of antiliberalism in the 20th century led to various authoritarian regimes.|近义词:authoritarianism, conservatism, reactionism|反义词:liberalism|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to (from Greek)
liberalism (noun): a political and social philosophy that emphasizes individual rights, freedoms, and limited government. (from Latin liber "free")
