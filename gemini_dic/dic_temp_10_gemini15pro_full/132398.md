nondemonstratively:/ˌnɒndɪˈmɒnstrətɪvli/
nondemonstratively:| adv. |非明示地，不明显地|In a way that is not openly expressive or emotional.  Not clearly proven or demonstrated.|He accepted the award nondemonstratively, barely cracking a smile.| 近义词:unemotionally, impassively, subtly, quietly, undemonstratively|反义词:demonstratively, openly, expressively|词性:副词

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  Meaning "not," from Latin non "not."
demonstrative (adjective): From Late Latin demonstrativus, from Latin demonstrare "to point out, show," from de- "completely" + monstrare "to show."
-ly (suffix): Forms adverbs from adjectives.
