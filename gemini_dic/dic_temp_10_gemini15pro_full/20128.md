bipartisanship: /ˌbaɪˈpɑːrtɪzənʃɪp/
noun
两党合作；跨党派合作
Agreement or cooperation between two political parties that usually oppose each other's policies.
Example:  Bipartisanship is essential to address complex issues like climate change.  两党合作对于解决气候变化等复杂问题至关重要。

Example:  The bill passed with strong bipartisan support.  该法案在两党的大力支持下获得通过。

近义词: cooperation, collaboration, unity, concord
反义词: partisanship, division, discord, antagonism
词性: noun

词源:  bi- (two) + partisan + -ship (suffix forming abstract nouns)
From "bipartisan" meaning involving two parties, and the suffix "-ship" indicating a state or quality.
