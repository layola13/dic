inadaptation:/ˌɪnˌædæpˈteɪʃən/
n.
不适应，失调
The inability to adjust to new conditions or a new environment.
His inadaptation to city life caused him much stress. 他对城市生活的不适应给他带来了很多压力。

inadaptation:/ˌɪnˌædæpˈteɪʃən/
n.
不适宜，不妥当
The state of being unsuitable or inappropriate.
The inadaptation of the policy to the current situation is clear. 这项政策对当前形势的不适宜显而易见。


Etymology->
From French *inadaptation*, from *in-* (not) + *adaptation* (adaptation). Ultimately from Latin *adaptare* (to fit).


Synonyms->
maladaptation, unsuitability, inappropriateness, inaptness, incompetence, inability, failure to adapt


Antonyms->
adaptation, suitability, appropriateness, aptness, competence, ability
