liberalizer:/ˈlɪbərəlaɪzər/| n.|自由化者，放松管制者|A person who promotes or implements policies that reduce government control and promote free markets or individual liberties.|例句：The liberalizer argued for deregulation of the banking sector.  这位自由化倡导者主张放松对银行业的管制。

例句：She is known as a strong liberalizer of trade policies. 她以强力推进贸易政策自由化而闻名。|近义词：reformer, innovator, progressive|反义词：conservative, traditionalist, regulator|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From liberalize + -er.  "liberalize" comes from "liberal" (favoring individual freedom), ultimately from Latin "liber" (free).  The "-ize" suffix turns the adjective into a verb meaning "to make liberal," and the "-er" suffix denotes the person performing the action.
