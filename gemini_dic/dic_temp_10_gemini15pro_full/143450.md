outfox:/aʊtˈfɒks/ (UK) /aʊtˈfɑːks/ (US)| v. | 以智取胜，比…更狡猾 | To defeat or surpass someone in cunning or cleverness. | The young chess player outfoxed his grandmaster opponent.  年轻的棋手以智取胜了他的大师级对手。|  outwit, outsmart, outmaneuver | be outfoxed by | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + fox (verb), meaning "to act like a fox; be crafty." The verb "fox" dates back to the 16th century.
