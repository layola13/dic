词源:
From Middle English *cob, cobbe, from Old English cobb (“cob; testicle; head, top, summit; seed-pod”), from Proto-Germanic *kubbaną (“to swell, be round”). Cognate with Dutch kob (“male swan”), German Kopf (“head”).  The sense "corncob" is American English, first recorded in 1767.  The other senses are mainly British.
