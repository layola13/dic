defecting:/dɪˈfɛktɪŋ/
| adj. | 叛逃的，变节的 | abandoning one's country or cause and going over to another | The defecting scientist revealed top secret information.  叛逃的科学家泄露了最高机密。 | 近义词：deserting, absconding,renegade | 反义词：loyal, faithful | 词性: 形容词 (present participle of "defect")


defect:/ˈdiːfɛkt/
| n. | 缺陷，缺点 | an imperfection, flaw, or blemish | The product was recalled due to a manufacturing defect. 该产品因制造缺陷而被召回。 | 近义词：flaw, imperfection, fault | 反义词：perfection, strength, virtue | 词性: 名词

defect:/dɪˈfɛkt/
| vi. | 叛逃，变节 | to abandon one's country or cause and go over to another |  Several soldiers defected to the enemy side. 几名士兵叛逃到敌方。 | 近义词：desert, abscond | 反义词：remain loyal | 词性: 动词

词源: From the Latin word "defectus," meaning "failure," "lack," or "abandonment."  It's the past participle of "deficere," which means "to fail," "to be wanting," or "to desert."
