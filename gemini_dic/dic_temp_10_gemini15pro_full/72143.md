floodplain's:/ˈflʌdpleɪnz/
noun
泛滥平原的；河漫滩的
The area of low-lying ground adjacent to a river, formed mainly of river sediments and subject to flooding.  This refers to something belonging to or associated with a floodplain.
The floodplain's rich soil is ideal for farming.  泛滥平原肥沃的土壤非常适合耕作。
flood plain, river plain, bottomland, alluvial plain, riparian zone, valley floor
n/a
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "flood" + "plain". "Flood" comes from Old English "flod" meaning "a flowing, stream, flood, river". "Plain" comes from Old French "plain" meaning "flat, level, even," ultimately from Latin "planus" meaning "flat, level."  The apostrophe + s indicates possessive form.
