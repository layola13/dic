Marked by clarity, firmness, and briskness.|She answered crisply and confidently. 她回答得干脆而自信。|近义词：sharply, briskly, succinctly, firmly|反义词：vaguely, hesitantly, softly, indistinctly|词性: 副词
crisp:/krɪsp/| adj. n. v.|脆的，干脆的，凉爽的；薯片；使变脆|From Old English "crisp" and Proto-Germanic "kriuspą," meaning "curled."  As an adjective, it describes something firm and brittle, easily broken with a snap. It can also describe something concise and to the point, or weather that is cool and invigorating.  As a noun, it can refer to a thin slice of potato fried until crisp.  As a verb, it means to make something crisp.|The air was crisp and cool. 空气凉爽宜人。  He gave a crisp, five-dollar bill. 他递过来一张崭新的五美元钞票。|近义词：(adj.) brittle, firm, fresh, concise; (n.) chip, cracker; (v.) sear, toast|反义词：(adj.) soggy, limp, stale, verbose; (v.) soften|词性: 形容词, 名词, 动词
