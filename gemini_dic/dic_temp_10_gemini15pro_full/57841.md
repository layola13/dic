dressinesses:/ˈdrɛsɪnəsɪz/
noun
正式程度；讲究程度
The degree to which something or someone is dressed formally or elaborately.
The dressinesses of the guests varied widely, from jeans and t-shirts to elegant evening gowns.  宾客们的穿着正式程度差异很大，从牛仔裤和T恤到优雅的晚礼服都有。
The various dressinesses of the events required a diverse wardrobe. 这些活动的着装要求各不相同，需要准备各种各样的服装。
formality, elegance, fanciness
casualness, informality
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From dressy (adjective) + -ness (suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality) + -es (plural suffix). Dressy ultimately comes from dress (noun), from Old French dresser "to arrange, straighten, prepare," from Vulgar Latin *directiare, frequentative of Latin dirigere "to direct, guide," from dis- "apart" + regere "to rule, guide."
