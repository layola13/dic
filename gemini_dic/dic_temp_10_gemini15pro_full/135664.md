nonpreferentialisms: /ˌnɒnprɛfərˈɛnʃəlɪzəmz/ (British) /ˌnɑːnprɛfərˈɛnʃəlɪzəmz/ (American)
|n.| 非优惠主义；无差别待遇|The absence of or opposition to preferential treatment, especially in trade.  It advocates for equal treatment of all parties involved, regardless of their origin or status.|
例句：Nonpreferentialisms in international trade can lead to increased competition. 国际贸易中的非优惠主义会导致竞争加剧。

例句：Some argue that nonpreferentialisms hinder the development of less developed countries.  一些人认为非优惠主义阻碍了欠发达国家的发展。
|近义词：impartiality, neutrality, egalitarianism|
|反义词：preferentialism, favoritism, discrimination|
|词性: noun|


词源:
"non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "preferential" (adjective meaning "giving or showing preference") + "-ism" (suffix forming nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, or practices).  The word is derived from "preference" which ultimately comes from the Latin "praeferre" meaning "to set before."
