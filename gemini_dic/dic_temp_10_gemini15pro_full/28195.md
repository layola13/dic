caprice:/kəˈpriːs/| n.|反复无常，怪想，奇想|A sudden and unaccountable change of mood or behavior.|Her sudden decision to quit her job seemed like a caprice. 她突然决定辞掉工作，这看起来像是一时的奇想。|Synonyms: whim, fancy, vagary, impulse, notion|Antonyms: constancy, stability|noun

caprices:/kəˈpriːsiz/| n.|反复无常，怪想，奇想（复数）|Sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior.|His musical compositions were full of unexpected caprices. 他的音乐作品充满了出人意料的奇想。|Synonyms: whims, fancies, vagaries, impulses, notions|Antonyms: constancies, stabilities|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *caprice*, from Italian *capriccio* "shudder," from *capra* "goat" (from Latin *capra*) + *-iccio* (suffix indicating a sudden involuntary movement, like a goat's jump or frisk).
