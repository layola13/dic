exequy: /ˈɛksɪkwɪ/ | n. | 葬礼; 丧葬仪式 | A funeral rite or ceremony. |例句：The exequies were held in the cathedral. 葬礼在大教堂举行。

例句：The king's exequies were elaborate and somber. 国王的葬礼既隆盛又肃穆。

近义词：funeral, burial, obsequies | 反义词: celebration, birth | 词性: noun

exequies: /ˈɛksɪkwɪz/ | n. | 葬礼; 丧葬仪式 | Funeral rites or ceremonies. | 例句：The exequies for the fallen soldiers were held at Arlington National Cemetery.  阵亡士兵的葬礼在阿灵顿国家公墓举行。

例句：The family held private exequies for their beloved grandmother. 这家人为他们敬爱的祖母举行了私人葬礼。

近义词：funeral services, burial rites, obsequies | 反义词: celebrations, festivities | 词性: noun


词源: From Old French *essequie (plural), from Latin exsequiae “funeral procession, obsequies,” from exsequi “to follow to the grave, follow out, perform, enforce,” from ex- “out” + sequi “to follow.”
