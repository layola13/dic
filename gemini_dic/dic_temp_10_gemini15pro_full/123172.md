monkism:/ˈmʌŋkɪzəm/| n. |修道主义，僧侣主义|The practice or system of monastic life.  A devotion to or adherence to the principles of monastic life.|He dedicated his life to monkism, seeking solitude and spiritual enlightenment. 他毕生致力于修道主义，寻求孤独和精神启蒙。|近义词：monasticism, asceticism|反义词：worldliness, secularism|词性:noun

词源: From monk + -ism.  "Monk" comes from Old English "munuc," from Late Latin "monachus," from Greek "monakhos," meaning "solitary," ultimately from "monos," meaning "alone."  "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns meaning a practice, system, doctrine, etc.
