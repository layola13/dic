abrogators:/ˈæbrəɡeɪtərz/
noun
废除者；取消者
People or entities that abrogate something, meaning they officially end a law, agreement, or custom.
The abrogators of the treaty faced international condemnation. 废除条约的人面临国际谴责。

The new regime were the abrogators of the old constitution. 新政权废除了旧宪法。

近义词:repealers, abolishers, nullifiers, revokers
反义词:supporters, upholders, ratifiers, maintainers
词性:n


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *abrogator, from Latin abrogare (“to repeal”), from ab- (“away from”) + rogare (“to ask, propose a law”).


USAGE->
The abrogators of the agreement were held responsible for the ensuing chaos.  协议的废除者要对随之而来的混乱负责。

The company's decision to act as abrogators of the long-standing partnership caused considerable friction. 公司决定终止长期合作关系，这造成了相当大的摩擦。


PHRASE->
the abrogators of the law  法律的废除者
the abrogators of the treaty 条约的废除者
the abrogators of the custom  习俗的废除者
the abrogators of the contract 合同的废除者
the abrogators of the agreement 协议的废除者
