debunkers:/diːˈbʌŋkərz/
n.
揭穿真相者；揭秘者
people who expose the falseness of a belief or claim
The debunkers quickly proved the supposed miracle was a hoax. 揭秘者很快证明了所谓的奇迹是个骗局。
synonyms:exposers, skeptics, critics, investigators
antonyms:propagandists, promoters, supporters, believers
noun

debunk:/diːˈbʌŋk/
vt.
揭穿；揭露；拆穿
to expose the falseness or hollowness of (a myth, idea, or belief)
The documentary debunked the popular myth about the Loch Ness monster.  这部纪录片揭穿了关于尼斯湖水怪的流行神话。
synonyms:expose, disprove, discredit, refute
antonyms:confirm, validate, substantiate, verify
verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- + bunk (nonsense), influenced by debunk (to remove from a bunk).  "Bunk" meaning nonsense comes from a speech given by Buncombe County representative Felix Walker in the US House of Representatives in 1820, which he insisted on delivering in full despite its irrelevance, claiming he was speaking for Buncombe.
