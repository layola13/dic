flamboyant:/flæmˈbɔɪənt/ | adj. |艳丽的，炫耀的， flamboyant |(of a person or their behavior) tending to attract attention because of their exuberance, confidence, and stylishness. |  例句：He was a flamboyant dresser, favoring brightly colored shirts and unusual hats. 他衣着华丽，喜欢色彩鲜艳的衬衫和奇特的帽子。

例句：She has a flamboyant personality, always the center of attention. 她性格张扬，总是众人瞩目的焦点。

近义词：ostentatious, showy, flashy, extravagant, colorful | 反义词：understated, modest, reserved, subdued | 词性：adjective

flamboyance:/flæmˈbɔɪəns/ | n. |艳丽，炫耀， flamboyant |the quality of being flamboyant. | 例句：His flamboyance and extravagance made him a memorable figure. 他的华丽和奢侈使他成为一个令人难忘的人物。

例句：She loved the flamboyance of the carnival costumes. 她喜欢狂欢节服装的华丽。


近义词：showiness, ostentation, flashiness | 反义词：modesty, restraint, understatement | 词性：noun

词源:
From French flamboyant, from Old French flamboiant ‘flaming,’ present participle of flamboier ‘to flame,’ from flambe ‘flame.’ Ultimately from Latin flammāre.
