gentlemen:/ˈdʒɛntlmən/ | n. | 先生们；绅士们 | A polite term of address for men, often used in formal settings or when addressing a group.  A man of good social position, especially one of wealth and leisure. A civilized, courteous, or honorable man. | Example 1: Gentlemen, please be seated. 先生们，请坐。

Example 2: He was a true gentleman, always opening doors for others. 他是一位真正的绅士，总是为别人开门。| 近义词: sirs, men, lords (in some contexts) | 反义词: boors, ruffians, villains | 词性: 名词

词源: From Old French gentilhomme, from gentil "noble, gentle" + homme "man." Ultimately from Latin gentilis "of the same clan" and homo "man."
