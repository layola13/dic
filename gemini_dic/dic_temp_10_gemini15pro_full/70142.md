Simple Present:
What do you eat for breakfast? 你早餐吃什么？
What does she like to do in her free time? 她空闲时间喜欢做什么？

Present Continuous:
What are you watching on TV? 你在电视上看什么？
What is he doing in the garden? 他在花园里做什么？

Present Perfect:
What have you learned recently? 你最近学到了什么？
What has she done to help? 她做了什么来帮忙？

Present Perfect Continuous:
What have you been working on all week? 你整个星期都在忙什么？
What has he been reading lately? 他最近一直在读什么？

Simple Past:
What did you do yesterday? 你昨天做了什么？
What did she say about the movie? 她对这部电影说了些什么？

Past Continuous:
What were you doing when the phone rang? 电话响的时候你在做什么？
What was she thinking about during the meeting? 开会的时候她在想什么？

Past Perfect:
What had you done before you went to bed? 你睡觉前做了什么？
What had she eaten before she got sick? 她生病前吃了什么？

Past Perfect Continuous:
What had you been doing before I arrived? 我来之前你一直在做什么？
What had she been studying before she changed her major?  她换专业之前一直在学什么？

Simple Future:
What will you do after graduation? 你毕业后打算做什么？
What will the weather be like tomorrow? 明天天气怎么样？

Future Continuous:
What will you be doing this time next week? 下周这个时候你会在做什么？
What will they be discussing at the conference? 他们在会议上会讨论什么？

Future Perfect:
What will you have achieved by the end of the year? 到年底你将会取得什么成就？
What will she have finished by tomorrow morning? 到明天早上她将完成什么？

Future Perfect Continuous:
What will you have been doing for five years by then? 到那时你将已经做了五年什么？
What will she have been studying by the time she graduates? 到她毕业时，她将已经学习了多久什么专业？


PHRASE->
What's up?  怎么了？/ 你好吗？
What about...? ...怎么样？
What for? 为了什么？
What if...? 如果...会怎么样？
So what? 那又怎样？


Etymology: From Old English _hwæt_, neuter singular nominative and accusative of _hwā_  ("who", "what"). Cognate with Dutch _wat_, German _was_, Latin _quod_.
