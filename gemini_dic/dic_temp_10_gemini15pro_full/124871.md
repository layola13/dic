mud-slinging:/ˈmʌdˌslɪŋɪŋ/
n.
诽谤，中伤
The act of making malicious or scandalous allegations about someone, especially to damage their reputation.
The election campaign descended into mud-slinging. 选举活动沦为互相攻击。
近义词：smear campaign, character assassination, vilification, defamation, backbiting, slander, libel
反义词：praise, commendation, acclaim, applause
词性：名词

词源：
The term "mud-slinging" originated in the 19th century, likely referring to the literal act of throwing mud.  This evolved metaphorically to describe the throwing of accusations and insults, particularly in political contexts.  The image evokes dirtiness and a lack of integrity.
