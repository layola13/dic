entertake:/ˌʌndərˈteɪk/ | vt.| 承担，着手，从事，保证 | to begin and carry through; to commit oneself to and begin (an enterprise or responsibility); to agree to do something | The company undertook a major restructuring.  这家公司进行了一次重大的重组。| synonyms: attempt, begin, commence, embark on, engage in, essay, initiate, launch, set about, tackle, try, undertake | antonyms: abandon, desert, forsake, quit, relinquish | verb

entertake:/ˌʌndərˈteɪk/ | vt.| 承诺，答应，保证 | to promise or agree to do something | He undertook to finish the project by the end of the month. 他承诺在月底前完成这个项目。| synonyms:  guarantee, pledge, promise, vow | antonyms:  renege | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *undertaken, from Old English *undertacan, from under- (under) + tacan, which is related to Old Norse taka (to take). The modern form arose by analogy with other verbs ending in -take, such as overtake, mistake.
