degeneracy:/dɪˈdʒɛnərəsi/ | n. | 退化；堕落；简并性 |A decline or deterioration from a higher or more refined state, either physically, mentally, or morally. In physics and chemistry, it refers to a situation where two or more different states of a system have the same energy. |The degeneracy of the Roman Empire was marked by corruption and decadence. 罗马帝国的衰落以腐败和颓废为特征。|Synonyms: decay, decline, deterioration, degradation, depravity, corruption, debasement |Antonyms: improvement, progress, elevation, refinement| noun


degeneracy:/dɪˈdʒɛnərəsi/ | n. | 简并；简并度 | In physics, degeneracy describes the situation where two or more distinct states of a quantum mechanical system have the same energy. | The degeneracy of the energy levels in a hydrogen atom is removed by an external magnetic field. 氢原子中能级的简并性会被外部磁场消除。 | Synonyms: multiplicity | Antonyms: non-degeneracy | noun
