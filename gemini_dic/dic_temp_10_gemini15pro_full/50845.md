desecrator:/ˈdɛsɪˌkreɪtər/| n. |亵渎者，污损者|A person who desecrates; someone who treats a sacred place or thing with violent disrespect.|The vandals were desecrators of the ancient temple. 这些故意破坏者亵渎了古老的寺庙。|近义词：profaner, violator, defiler|反义词：protector, guardian|词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *desecrator, from Latin desecrat-, past participle stem of desecrare "to desecrate," from de- "un-, down" + sacrare "to consecrate, make sacred," from sacer "sacred."
