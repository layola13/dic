excentric:/ɪkˈsɛntrɪk/ /ɛkˈsɛntrɪk/ | adj. n. |古怪的，反常的；古怪的人|Deviating from the recognized or customary character, practice, etc.; irregular; odd; peculiar;  a person who has unusual or odd habits or opinions.|例句：He was a brilliant but eccentric scientist. 他是一位才华横溢但性格古怪的科学家。|例句：She's known for her eccentric fashion sense. 她以其古怪的时尚感而闻名。|近义词：peculiar, odd, strange, unusual, quirky, unconventional |反义词：normal, conventional, ordinary, typical |词性:形容词, 名词
excentrics:/ɪkˈsɛntrɪks/ /ɛkˈsɛntrɪks/ | n. |古怪的人（复数）|Plural form of eccentric, referring to people who have unusual or odd habits or opinions.|例句：The town was known for its artists and excentrics. 这个小镇以其艺术家和古怪的人而闻名。|例句：He enjoys spending time with fellow excentrics. 他喜欢和其他古怪的人在一起。|近义词：oddballs, characters, nonconformists |反义词：conformists |词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin eccentricus, from Greek ekkentros “out of the center,” from ek “out of” + kentron “center.”
