dodded:/ˈdɒdɪd/ (UK) /ˈdɑːdɪd/ (US)| v.| 打瞌睡，点头|(past tense and past participle of dodder) Moved feebly; trembled.  | The old man dodded off during the sermon. 老人布道时打瞌睡了。| 近义词：nodded off, dozed, napped | 反义词:  stayed awake, remained alert | 词性: verb

Etymology:
From Middle English *doderen, frequentative of dodden, of uncertain origin. Perhaps related to Dutch dodderen (“to shake, tremble”), or alternatively from a Scandinavian source akin to Icelandic dadra (“to toddle”).
