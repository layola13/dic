off-licensed:/ˌɒf ˈlaɪsənst/ | adj. | 非许可的，未经许可的 |  Used to describe a medicine or treatment that is being used for a condition it is not officially licensed for. This means it hasn't been specifically tested and approved for that particular use by regulatory authorities. | The doctor prescribed an off-licensed drug for my migraines.  医生给我开了种未经许可的药物来治疗偏头痛。| Synonyms: unlicensed, unapproved, unauthorized | Antonyms: licensed, approved, authorized | adjective

off-licensed:/ˌɒf ˈlaɪsənst/ | adj. | (指场所)无售酒许可的 | Used to describe a shop or business that is not licensed to sell alcohol.  |  I bought some soft drinks from the off-licensed shop. 我从那家无售酒许可的商店买了一些软饮料。 | Synonyms: unlicensed (for alcohol sales) | Antonyms: licensed (for alcohol sales), on-licensed | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"off-" (prefix indicating negation or separation) + "licensed" (past participle of "license," from Old French "licence," ultimately from Latin "licentia" meaning "freedom, permission")
