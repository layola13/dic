concertanti:/ˌkɒntʃɛrˈtænti/ | adj. | 协奏的，独奏的 |  Of, relating to, or resembling a concerto, especially in featuring a small group of solo instruments against an orchestral background. | The concertanti parts for violin and cello were beautifully interwoven. 小提琴和大提琴的协奏部分编织得非常精美。|  The orchestra performed a concertanti symphony. 乐队演奏了一首协奏交响曲。|  symphonic, concerted |  solo | adjective

concertante:/ˌkɒntʃɛrˈtænteɪ/ | n. | 协奏曲 | A concerto, especially one for two or more solo instruments and orchestra. |  The concertante featured a brilliant young pianist. 这场协奏曲的主角是一位才华横溢的年轻钢琴家。| The orchestra played a Mozart concertante. 乐队演奏了一首莫扎特的协奏曲。| concerto, symphony | sonata | noun


Etymology: Italian, from present participle of concertare 'to arrange, agree,' from Latin concertare 'to contend, dispute,' from com- 'together' + certare 'to contend.'
