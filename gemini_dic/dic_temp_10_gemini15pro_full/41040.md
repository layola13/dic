copernican:/ˌkoʊˈpɜːrnɪkən/ /ˌkoʊˈpɝːnɪkən/
adjective|哥白尼式的; 革命性的|Relating to Nicolaus Copernicus or his theories, especially the theory that the earth revolves around the sun.  Figuratively, it refers to something revolutionary or paradigm-shifting. |例句：His Copernican view of the business world challenged traditional hierarchies. 他对商业世界的哥白尼式观点挑战了传统的等级制度。|近义词：revolutionary, groundbreaking, transformative|反义词：traditional, conventional, orthodox|词性:adjective
copernican:/ˌkoʊˈpɜːrnɪkən/ /ˌkoʊˈpɝːnɪkən/
noun|哥白尼主义者；拥护日心说的人|A supporter or advocate of the Copernican theory.|例句：He was a staunch Copernican, even in the face of opposition. 即使面对反对，他也是一个坚定的哥白尼主义者。|近义词：heliocentrist|反义词：geocentrist|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Nicolaus Copernicus (1473-1543), a Polish astronomer who proposed a heliocentric model of the solar system.  The word "Copernican" is derived from his Latinized name, *Copernicus*. The suffix "-an" is used to form adjectives and nouns relating to persons or places.
