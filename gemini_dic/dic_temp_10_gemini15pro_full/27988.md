canvassed:/ˈkænvəst/ | v. | 拉选票；征求意见；兜售；游说；详细讨论；彻底检查 |  To solicit votes, opinions, or support; to examine thoroughly. |  The politician canvassed the neighborhood for votes.  这位政治家在附近拉选票。 | Synonyms:  solicited, polled, surveyed, scrutinized, examined | Antonyms: ignored, neglected | verb

canvassed:/ˈkænvəst/ | adj. |  (rarely used as an adjective)  |  Having been canvassed or thoroughly examined.  |  The canvassed area showed strong support for the proposal. 经过调查的地区显示出对该提案的强烈支持。 | Synonyms: surveyed, examined | Antonyms: unexamined, unexplored | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *canevas "canvas," also "sieve," from Late Latin *cannapaceus "made of hemp," from Latin cannabis "hemp." The sense shifted from "canvas" to "something that is spread out or covers a large area," hence the meanings of "soliciting" and "examining thoroughly."
