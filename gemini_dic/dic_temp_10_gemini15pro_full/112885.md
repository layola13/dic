lud:/lʌd/ | n. | Lord | A title of respect or a nobleman.  Derived from Old English *hlāford, meaning "loaf-keeper" or "bread guardian," indicating someone who provides for others. |  He addressed the man as "my lud," a common term in older English. 他称呼那人为“老爷”，这是古英语中的一个常见称呼。 | synonyms: lord, master, sir | antonyms: servant, commoner | noun
