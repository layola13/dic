dishonesty's:/dɪsˈɑːnɪstiz/
noun
不诚实，欺骗
the quality or an instance of being dishonest; lack of honesty; deception.
例句：Dishonesty's consequences can be severe. 不诚实的後果可能很严重。

例句：The dishonesty's impact on their relationship was devastating.  不诚实对他们关系的影响是毁灭性的。

近义词：deceit, fraud, duplicity, treachery
反义词：honesty, integrity, probity
词性：noun (名词)



ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *disoneste*, from Anglo-Norman *desoneste*, from Old French *desonesté*, from *deshoneste* (“dishonest”), from Latin *dishonestus* (“dishonorable, shameful”), from *dis-* (“not”) + *honestus* (“honorable, respectable”).  The suffix *-’s* is the possessive marker.
