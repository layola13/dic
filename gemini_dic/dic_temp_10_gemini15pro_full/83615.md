haematoxylon:/ˌhiːməˈtɒksɪlən/ /ˌheməˈtɑːksɪlən/| n.|苏木精|A crystalline compound, C16H14O6, extracted from the heartwood of the logwood tree (Haematoxylum campechianum), used as a dye, especially in microscopy to stain cell nuclei.|The haematoxylon stain highlighted the nuclei of the cells, making them easily visible under the microscope. 苏木精染色突出了细胞核，使它们在显微镜下很容易看到。|Synonyms: logwood extract, logwood dye|Antonyms: None|Noun

haematoxylin:/ˌhiːməˈtɒksɪlɪn/ /ˌheməˈtɑːksɪlɪn/| n.|苏木素|A dye derived from haematoxylon, used in microscopy to stain cell nuclei blue-violet.|The haematoxylin and eosin stain (H&E stain) is commonly used in histology. 苏木素和伊红染色 (H&E 染色) 常用于组织学。|Synonyms:  hematoxylin, natural black 1|Antonyms: None|Noun
