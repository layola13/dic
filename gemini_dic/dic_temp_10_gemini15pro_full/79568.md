gloom-and-doomers:/ˈɡluːm ənd ˈduːmərz/
|n.|悲观主义者；末日论者|People who predict disaster and decline; pessimists.|The gloom-and-doomers predict the economy will collapse.|近义词:pessimists, doomsayers, alarmists|反义词:optimists, Pollyannas|词性:名词

gloom:/ɡluːm/
|n.|昏暗；忧郁；沮丧|Darkness; a feeling of sadness and lack of hope.|A gloom settled over the town after the factory closed.|近义词:darkness, sadness, depression|反义词:brightness, happiness, cheer|词性:名词

doom:/duːm/
|n.|厄运；毁灭；劫数|Death, destruction, or some other terrible fate.|The newspapers predicted doom and gloom for the economy.|近义词:fate, destiny, ruin|反义词:fortune, luck, salvation|词性:名词
|vt.|注定；判决|To condemn to a certain fate, especially death or destruction.|The project was doomed from the start.|近义词:condemn, sentence, destine|反义词:save, rescue, redeem|词性:动词

-er:/ər/
|suffix.|...的人；...的物|Used to form nouns that refer to a person or thing that performs a particular action.|A writer is someone who writes. A cooker is something used for cooking.|近义词:N/A|反义词:N/A|词性:后缀

词源:  "Gloom" comes from Old English *glōm, meaning "twilight, dusk."  "Doom" comes from Old English dōm, meaning "judgment, law, statute; decree, sentence." "-er" is a common agentive suffix. "Gloom and doom" as a phrase predates the use of "gloom-and-doomer" as a noun, with the earliest attested use of the phrase dating back to the 1890s.
