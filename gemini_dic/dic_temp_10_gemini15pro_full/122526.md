一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Modal verbs are used to express general truths or habitual actions.
Modal verbs are commonly used in present tense questions.

例句：
He can speak French fluently. = 他能流利地说法语。
She must leave for work now. = 她现在必须去上班了。


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Modal verbs are used with be + -ing form to express actions in progress.
Modal verbs can indicate temporary situations or ongoing processes.

例句：
They may be arriving later today. = 他们今天晚些时候可能会到。
She should be studying for her exam right now. = 她现在应该正在准备考试。


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
Modal verbs + have + past participle express completed actions with present relevance.
Modal verbs can express past actions that continue to have an impact on the present.

例句：
He might have forgotten our appointment. = 他可能忘记了我们的约会。
She must have left already. = 她一定已经离开了。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
Modal verbs + have been + -ing form show duration of an action up to the present.
This tense emphasizes the continuous nature of a past action leading up to the present.

例句：
They could have been working on this project for months. = 他们可能已经在这个项目上工作好几个月了。
He must have been practicing the piano all day. = 他一定练习了一整天的钢琴。


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
Modal verbs + base form express past abilities, possibilities, or obligations.
Modal verbs can be used to express past habits or routines.

例句：
She could swim when she was five. = 她五岁时就会游泳。
He would often walk to school. = 他以前经常步行去上学。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
Modal verbs + be + -ing form describe actions in progress at a specific time in the past.
This tense often sets the scene for another past action.

例句：
They might have been sleeping when I called. = 我打电话时他们可能正在睡觉。
She should have been studying instead of watching TV. = 她本应该学习而不是看电视。


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
Modal verbs + have + past participle express actions completed before another past action.
This tense clarifies the sequence of past events.

例句：
He could have finished the project if he had had more time. = 如果他有更多时间，他本可以完成这个项目的。
She should have told me the truth earlier. = 她本应该早点告诉我真相。


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
Modal verbs + have been + -ing form show duration of an action before another past action.
This tense emphasizes the continuous nature of a past action leading up to another past event.

例句：
They might have been working on the problem for hours before they found a solution. = 他们可能在找到解决方案之前已经研究这个问题好几个小时了。
She must have been practicing for weeks before the concert. = 她一定在音乐会前练习了好几个星期。


一般将来时 (Simple Future):
Modal verbs + base form express future possibilities, predictions, or obligations.
Modal verbs can indicate future intentions or plans.

例句：
He will be able to drive next year. = 明年他就能开车了。
She should arrive by tomorrow evening. = 她应该会在明晚之前到达。



将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
Modal verbs + be + -ing form describe actions in progress at a specific time in the future.
This tense emphasizes the ongoing nature of a future action.

例句：
They will probably be working on the project next week. = 他们下周可能正在做这个项目。
She should be arriving at the airport around noon. = 她应该会在中午左右到达机场。


将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
Modal verbs + have + past participle express actions completed before a specific time in the future.
This tense clarifies the sequence of future events.


例句：
He will have finished the project by next month. = 他将在下个月之前完成这个项目。
She should have arrived by the time you get there. = 你到那里的时候她应该已经到了。



将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
Modal verbs + have been + -ing form show duration of an action up to a specific time in the future.

例句：
They will have been working on the project for six months by then. = 到那时，他们将已经在这个项目上工作了六个月。
She should have been living in London for two years by next summer. = 到明年夏天，她将在伦敦住了两年。



虚拟语气(Subjunctive Mood):
Modal verbs can be used to express hypothetical situations or unreal conditions.

例句：
If I were you, I would go. = 如果我是你，我就去。
He might have won the race if he hadn't fallen. = 如果他没有摔倒，他可能已经赢得了比赛。


被动语态(Passive Voice):
Modal verbs can be used in passive constructions.

例句：
The problem should be solved soon. = 这个问题应该很快就能解决。
The meeting might have been cancelled. = 会议可能已经取消了。
