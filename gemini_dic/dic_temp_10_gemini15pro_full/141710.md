oracle:/ˈɔːrəkl/ | n. | 神谕，预言，先知，权威 | A priest or priestess acting as a medium through whom advice or prophecy was sought from the gods in classical antiquity. A person or thing regarded as an infallible authority on something. | The oracle predicted a great victory.  神谕预言了一场伟大的胜利。|  The CEO is seen as the oracle of the company. 首席执行官被视为公司的权威。 | synonym: prophet, seer, soothsayer, advisor, expert | antonym:  skeptic, doubter | noun

oracles:/ˈɔːrəklz/ | n. | 神谕（复数），预言（复数），先知（复数），权威（复数）| Plural form of "oracle." | The ancient Greeks consulted oracles before making important decisions. 古希腊人在做出重要决定之前会咨询神谕。 |  Many tech companies are now seen as oracles on the future of artificial intelligence. 许多科技公司现在被视为人工智能未来的权威。| synonym: prophets, seers, soothsayers, advisors, experts | antonym: skeptics, doubters | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French oracle, from Latin oraculum "divine announcement, oracle," from orare "to speak, pray," related to os, oris "mouth."
