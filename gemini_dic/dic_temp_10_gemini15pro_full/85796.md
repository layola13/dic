hayya:/ˈhæjə/ | int. | 来吧；赶紧；加油 | An Arabic word used as an exclamation of encouragement, invitation, or urging someone to hurry.  It can also be used to express excitement or anticipation. | Hayya! Let's go! 来吧！我们走吧！| Hayya! We're going to be late!  快点！我们要迟到了！ |  Come on, let's go, hurry up |  (No direct antonym for an interjection like this) | Interjection

Etymology->
From Arabic هيا (hayya), imperative form of the verb هيّا (hayyā), meaning "let's go," "come on."
