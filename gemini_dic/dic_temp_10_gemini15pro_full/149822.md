paleologist:/ˌpeɪliˈɒlədʒɪst/ /ˌpeɪliˈɑːlədʒɪst/| n. | 古生物学家 | A scientist who studies fossils to learn about the history of life on Earth. | Example 1: The paleologist carefully examined the dinosaur bone.  这位古生物学家仔细地检查了恐龙骨骼。

Example 2:  Paleologists have discovered a new species of ancient mammal. 古生物学家发现了一种新的古代哺乳动物。| 近义词:  fossil expert, paleontologist | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun


paleologist:/ˌpeɪliˈɒlədʒɪst/ /ˌpeɪliˈɑːlədʒɪst/| n. | 古代历史学家,古代文献学家 | A scholar specializing in the study of antiquity, especially through its literature. | Example 1: The paleologist deciphered the ancient inscription. 这位古代文献学家破译了古代铭文。

Example 2: Paleologists contribute to our understanding of ancient civilizations. 古代历史学家有助于我们了解古代文明。| 近义词:  historian of antiquity, classicist | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun
