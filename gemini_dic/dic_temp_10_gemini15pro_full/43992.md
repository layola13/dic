criminalised:/ˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪzd/ |v. |使…成为犯罪；宣布…为非法|To make something illegal or treat something as a crime.|例句：Many countries have criminalized the possession of certain drugs. 许多国家已经将持有某些毒品定为犯罪。|例句：They criminalized dissent and imprisoned their opponents. 他们将异议定为犯罪，并将反对者投入监狱。|近义词：outlaw, prohibit, ban, illegalize|反义词:legalize, decriminalize|词性:动词

词源:
From criminal + -ize.  "Criminal" ultimately derives from the Latin word "crimen" meaning "accusation" or "crime."  The suffix "-ize" is used to form verbs meaning "to make or become."
