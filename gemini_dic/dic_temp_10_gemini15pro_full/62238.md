encrustations: /ɪnˌkrʌˈsteɪʃənz/ | n. | 结壳，硬壳; 积垢 |A hard coating or crust formed on a surface. | The ancient vase was covered in encrustations of dirt and minerals. 古老的花瓶上覆盖着一层泥土和矿物质的硬壳。| The kettle was full of encrustations from hard water. 水壶里充满了硬水形成的水垢。 | synonyms: deposits, coatings, crusts, scales, concretions | antonyms: smoothness, clarity | noun

encrustation: /ɪnˌkrʌˈsteɪʃən/ | n. | 包壳;镶嵌装饰 | The act of covering something with a hard crust or the crust itself. | The encrustation of jewels on the crown made it sparkle. 王冠上镶嵌的珠宝使它闪闪发光。 |  The artist created an encrustation of shells on the sculpture.  艺术家在雕塑上镶嵌了一层贝壳。 | synonyms: covering, overlay, inlay, ornamentation | antonyms: stripping, removal | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin "incrustatio," from Latin "incrustare" "to encrust," from "in-" (in, on) + "crusta" (crust, rind, shell).
