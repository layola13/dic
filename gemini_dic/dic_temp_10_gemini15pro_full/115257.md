manipulatively:/məˈnɪpjʊlətɪvli/
adverb|操纵地；控制地；玩弄地|in a way that controls or influences someone or something unfairly and dishonestly|He manipulatively used her emotions to get what he wanted. 他操纵她的感情来达到自己的目的。|
近义词:cunningly, craftily, deviously, schemingly
反义词:honestly, fairly, openly, frankly
词性:adverb

manipulative:/məˈnɪpjʊlətɪv/
adjective|操纵的；控制的；玩弄的|exercising unscrupulous control or influence over a person or situation.|She has a manipulative personality. 她性格很会操纵人。|
近义词:controlling, scheming, exploitative, cunning
反义词:honest, straightforward, forthright
词性:adjective

manipulate:/məˈnɪpjʊleɪt/
verb|操纵；控制；玩弄|control or influence (a person or situation) cleverly, unfairly, or unscrupulously.|He manipulated the stock market for his own profit. 他为了自己的利益操纵股票市场。|
近义词:control, influence, exploit, maneuver
反义词:be honest, be fair
词性:verb

manipulation:/məˌnɪpjʊˈleɪʃn/
noun|操纵；控制；处理|the action of manipulating something in a skillful manner.|The manipulation of public opinion is a common political tactic.  操纵公众舆论是常见的政治策略。|
近义词:control, influence, exploitation
反义词:honesty, fairness
词性:noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *manipulativus, from Latin manipulus (“handful, maniple”) + -ativus.  Ultimately from manus (“hand”).
