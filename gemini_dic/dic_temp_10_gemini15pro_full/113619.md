machiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | adj. | 马基雅维利的；权谋的；不择手段的 | Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian political philosopher whose book "The Prince" described how rulers can gain and maintain power, often through deceit and manipulation. |  His machiavellian tactics helped him climb the corporate ladder quickly. 他不择手段的策略使他很快地升到了公司的最高层。 | synonyms: cunning, devious, crafty, scheming, unscrupulous, manipulative, artful, wily, insidious | antonyms: honest, principled, ethical, scrupulous, straightforward | adjective
