hollow:/ˈhɑːloʊ/ (US), /ˈhɒləʊ/ (UK)| adj. n. v.|空的，中空的；虚伪的；凹陷的；山谷，洞|Having an empty space inside;  insincere; a sunken area; a valley|The tree trunk was hollow. 树干是空的。|Synonyms: empty, vacant, void, concave, insincere, false|Antonyms: solid, full, substantial, sincere, genuine|adjective

hollow:/ˈhɑːloʊ/ (US), /ˈhɒləʊ/ (UK)| n.|山谷，洞，洼地|A small valley or depression in the ground|The deer grazed in the hollow. 鹿在山谷里吃草。|Synonyms: valley, depression, dip, basin|Antonyms: hill, peak, summit|noun

hollow:/ˈhɑːloʊ/ (US), /ˈhɒləʊ/ (UK)| v.|挖空，使凹陷|To make something hollow by removing the inside part|The birds hollowed out the tree to make a nest. 鸟儿把树挖空做窝。|Synonyms: excavate, scoop out, gouge|Antonyms: fill, pack, stuff|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *holh, from Proto-Germanic *hulgaz, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱel- (“to cover, conceal”). Cognate with Dutch hol (“hollow, hole”), German hohl (“hollow”), Swedish hålig (“hollow”).
