dug-in:/ˌdʌɡˈɪn/
adjective
顽固的， entrenched, firmly established
The enemy troops were dug-in on the hilltop. 敌军在山顶上挖好了战壕，严阵以待。
Synonyms: entrenched, embedded, rooted, fixed
Antonyms: yielding, flexible, adaptable
词性: 形容词

dug-in:/ˌdʌɡˈɪn/
adjective
准备好的, prepared, ready
The team was dug-in and ready for the tough negotiations. 该团队已做好准备，迎接艰难的谈判。
Synonyms: prepared, ready, braced
Antonyms: unprepared, unready


词源：From the past participle of "dig in," meaning to dig trenches for protection or to establish a strong position. 源于“dig in”的过去分词，意思是挖掘战壕以进行保护或建立稳固阵地。
