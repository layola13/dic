dobber:/ˈdɒbə(r)/ (UK) /ˈdɑːbər/ (US)| n.|浮标；钓鱼浮子；告密者；（非正式）笨蛋|A small float used in fishing to indicate when a fish has taken the bait. Also can refer to an informant or (informally) a stupid person.|He attached the dobber to his fishing line. 他把浮标系在他的钓线上。|近义词：float, informant, snitch (for informant), idiot, fool (for stupid person)|反义词: None (for float), None (for informant/stupid person)|Noun

Etymology:
From the verb "to dob," meaning to throw something lightly or gently, which originated in the 16th century. The use of "dobber" as a fishing float arose from the action of gently dropping the float onto the water.  The meaning of "informant" is thought to be related to "dobbing someone in," meaning to report someone to the authorities.
