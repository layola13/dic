downstage:/ˌdaʊnˈsteɪdʒ/ | adj. adv. n. | 舞台下方的，靠近观众席的；在舞台下方；舞台前部 |  Relating to or situated in the part of a stage closest to the audience. | The actor moved downstage to deliver his monologue.  演员走到舞台前方进行独白。| 近义词: frontstage | 反义词: upstage | 词性: adjective, adverb, noun

downstages:/ˌdaʊnˈsteɪdʒɪz/ | vt. | 抢…的镜头，抢风头；使…相形见绌 | To draw attention away from (another performer), especially by moving downstage or upstaging. | The lead actor constantly downstages his supporting cast. 主演不断抢配角的风头。 | 近义词: upstages, overshadows | 反义词: supports, enhances | 词性: verb
