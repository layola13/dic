corneule:/kɔːrˈnjuːl/ | n. | 角质层细胞; 角膜小体 | A small, hard, transparent particle of keratin found in the stratum corneum, the outermost layer of the epidermis.  It is essentially a dead, flattened keratinocyte (skin cell). |  The corneules are constantly shed and replaced. 角质层细胞不断脱落并更新。 | Corneocytes are vital for maintaining the skin’s barrier function. 角质层细胞对维持皮肤的屏障功能至关重要。 | Synonyms:  corneocyte, squamous cell (in this context) | Antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *corneule*, diminutive of *cornée* ("cornea"), from Latin *cornea* ("horny, resembling horn"), feminine of *corneus*, from *cornu* ("horn").
