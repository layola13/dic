inhabitants:/ɪnˈhæbɪtənts/| n. |居民，居住者|People or animals that live in a particular place. |The city has around two million inhabitants.  这座城市大约有两百万居民。|
inhabitants:/ɪnˈhæbɪtənts/| n. |居住者|Those who reside in a specific area. |The island's inhabitants rely on fishing for their livelihood.  岛上的居民依靠捕鱼为生。|
inhabitants:/ɪnˈhæbɪtənts/| n. | обитатели (Russian) |Residents of a place, whether human or animal. |The forest is home to various inhabitants, including deer and squirrels. 这片森林是各种居民的家园，包括鹿和松鼠。|
inhabitants:/ɪnˈhæbɪtənts/| n. | habitants (French) |People who live in a specific location. |The inhabitants of the village welcomed the newcomers with open arms. 村民们张开双臂欢迎新来的人。|


Etymology: From Late Middle English *inhabitaunt*, from Old French *habitant*, present participle of *habiter* "to dwell," from Latin *habitare* "to dwell, inhabit," frequentative of *habēre* "to have, hold."

Synonyms: residents, occupants, dwellers, population, denizens

Antonyms:  non-residents,  outsiders,  evacuees
