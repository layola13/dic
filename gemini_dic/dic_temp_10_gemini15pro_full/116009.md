marshes:/ˈmɑːrʃɪz/| n. |沼泽，湿地|An area of low-lying land that is flooded in wet seasons or at high tide, and typically remains waterlogged at all times.  A marsh is characterized by its vegetation of grasses, rushes, and other aquatic plants.|The birdwatchers observed several rare species in the marshes.|Synonyms: swamps, bogs, fens, wetlands|Antonyms: deserts, uplands|Noun

marshes:/ˈmɑːʃɪz/| n. |沼泽地，湿地|  A tract of soft wet land usually characterized by reeds, grasses, cattails and the like.| They canoed through the marshes.|Synonyms: swamps, bogs, fens, wetlands|Antonyms: deserts, uplands|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *maresche*, from Frankish *marisk*, from Proto-Germanic *mariskaz ("marsh, swamp"). Cognate with Old English mersc, Dutch moeras, German Marsch.
