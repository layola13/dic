mournful:/ˈmɔːrnfəl/ (US) /ˈmɔːnfl/ (UK)| adj.|悲哀的，忧伤的|Feeling or expressing sorrow or grief.|例句：The mournful sound of the bagpipes filled the air.  风笛悲哀的声音弥漫在空气中。|例句：She gave a mournful sigh. 她悲伤地叹了口气。|近义词：sorrowful, sad, grieving, doleful, plaintive|反义词：joyful, happy, cheerful|词性:形容词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mornful*, from Old English *mornfull* (“sad, mournful”), equivalent to *morn* (“mourning”) + *-full*. Cognate with Scots *murnfu* (“mournful”).  *Morn* is from Proto-Germanic *murną* (“to mourn, grieve, care, be anxious”), from Proto-Indo-European *mer- (“to die”).
