hydrometeor:/ˈhaɪdrəˌmiːtiər/| n. |水凝物；大气中的水分|Any product of the condensation or sublimation of atmospheric water vapor, whether formed in the free atmosphere or at the earth's surface.  This includes things like rain, snow, hail, fog, and clouds.|Heavy rain and other hydrometeors caused significant flooding.|Synonyms: precipitation, atmospheric water, condensation product|Antonyms: dry air, aridity|noun
