careening:/kəˈriːnɪŋ/
verb
倾斜， 猛地转向；横冲直撞
moving swiftly and in an uncontrolled way, especially changing direction suddenly
The car careened down the hill. 汽车沿着山坡横冲直撞。
近义词：swerving, lurching, tilting, swaying, rocking
反义词：steady, stable
词性: verb

careen:/kəˈriːn/
verb
（使）倾斜，（使）猛地转向
(cause a boat or ship) to lean over on one side
The ship careened violently in the storm. 船在暴风雨中剧烈地倾斜。
近义词：tilt, list, lean, tip
反义词：right, straighten
词性: verb


词源：From French carène "keel, bottom of a ship," from Italian carena, from Late Latin carina "keel." The original sense was nautical, referring to the practice of turning a ship on its side for cleaning or repair. The sense of "to move rapidly and erratically" developed from the image of a ship tilting wildly.
