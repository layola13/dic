inned:/ɪnd/ | v. | 使得分; 使（跑垒员）跑回本垒得分 | To put a runner out by tagging a base while the runner is forced to run to it.  | The shortstop inned the runner at second. | 近义词: retired, put out | 反义词: safe | 词性: 动词

词源:  Derived from the noun "innings" (a turn at bat), which itself comes from the Old English "innan" meaning "within," "into."  The verb "to inn" arose from the idea of a batter completing their "innings" by reaching home and scoring a run, thus bringing the side's turn "in" to an end.
