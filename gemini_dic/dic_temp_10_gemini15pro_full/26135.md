busting:/ˈbʌstɪŋ/
busting| adj.|爆裂的，繁忙的，渴望的|Energetically breaking or splitting something open; extremely busy; eagerly wanting to do something|例句：They were busting to tell everyone the good news. 他们渴望告诉所有人这个好消息。|近义词：bursting, breaking, busy, eager|反义词：slow, lazy, unenthusiastic|词性：形容词

busting:/ˈbʌstɪŋ/
busting| n.|突袭，逮捕|A raid or arrest, typically by police|例句：The police made a busting on the drug dealer's house. 警察突袭了毒贩的房子。|近义词：raid, arrest|反义词：release|词性：名词

busting:/ˈbʌstɪŋ/
busting| v.|打破，突袭，逮捕，降级，揭穿|Breaking something; raiding; arresting; demoting; exposing|例句：He busted the door down. 他把门砸开了。|近义词：break, raid, arrest, demote, expose|反义词：repair, release, promote, conceal|词性：动词


词源：From the verb "bust," which originated from the Middle English word "bursten" meaning "to burst."
