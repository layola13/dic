groveled:/ˈɡrɒv(ə)ld/ (UK), /ˈɡrɑːv(ə)ld/ (US)| v. |卑躬屈膝，奴颜婢膝，匍匐前进|To behave in a subservient manner, as if by crawling on the ground; to abase oneself.|He groveled before the king, begging for mercy. 他跪在国王面前乞求怜悯。|Synonyms: crawl, cringe, fawn, kowtow|Antonyms: defy, resist, stand up to|verb

grovel:/ˈɡrɒv(ə)l/ (UK), /ˈɡrɑːv(ə)l/ (US)| n. |卑躬屈膝的行为|The act of groveling.|His constant grovel was embarrassing to watch.  他不断的卑躬屈膝让人看不下去。|Synonyms: fawning, subservience|Antonyms: defiance, resistance|noun
