brogueries:/ˈbrɑːɡəriːz/ /ˈbrɒɡəriːz/

粗革皮鞋（尤指乡村户外活动穿的）; 粗革皮鞋（尤指乡村户外活动穿的）
Strong, heavy shoes, typically worn for country walking or other outdoor activities.
He wore sturdy brogueries and thick socks for his hike. 他穿着结实的粗革皮鞋和厚袜子去远足。
近义词:hiking boots, walking shoes, outdoor shoes

brogue:/broʊɡ/ /brəʊɡ/
noun
（尤指爱尔兰或苏格兰）口音; （尤指爱尔兰或苏格兰）口音
A strong regional accent, especially an Irish or Scottish one.
His thick brogue made him difficult to understand at first. 他浓重的口音一开始让人很难理解。
近义词:accent, dialect
反义词:standard pronunciation
词性:n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Irish *bróg, from Old Irish bróc (“shoe”), from Proto-Celtic *brōkōs (“shoe”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)bʰrewg- (“to break”). Cognate with Welsh brog (“shoe”), Breton brok (“clog”).  The decorative perforations are thought to have originated in Ireland and Scotland, designed to allow water to drain from shoes worn in boggy terrain.
