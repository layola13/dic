countess:/ˈkaʊntɪs/| n.|女伯爵；伯爵夫人|A woman holding the rank of a count or earl, or the wife of a count or earl.|The countess presided over the grand ball.|近义词:noblewoman|反义词:commoner|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *contesse*, from Late Latin *comitissa*, feminine form of *comes* ("count"). Ultimately from Latin *comitem* (accusative of *comes*), meaning "companion".
