Etymology: From Old English *gāst (attested only in compounds), from Proto-Germanic *gaistaz, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰóys-tos (“fright, terror”). Cognate with Old Norse geisli (“ray of light”), Old High German geist (“spirit, ghost”), Gothic 𐌲𐌰𐌹𐍃𐍄𐍃 (gaists, “spirit”). Related to aghast.
