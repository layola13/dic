commuter:/kəˈmjuːtər/ (US) /ˈkɒmjuːtə(r)/ (UK)| n.|通勤者，上下班往返的人|A person who travels some distance to work on a regular basis.|She's a commuter who travels into London every day. 她是一位每天通勤到伦敦的上班族。|synonyms: traveler, suburbanite, passenger|antonyms: resident, local, native|noun

commuter:/kəˈmjuːtər/ (US) /ˈkɒmjuːtə(r)/ (UK)| adj.(较少使用)|通勤的|Relating to commuting or commuters.|Commuter traffic is heavy during rush hour.  高峰时段通勤交通拥堵。|synonyms: traveling, suburban|antonyms: residential, local|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "commute," which comes from the Latin "commutare," meaning "to change thoroughly, exchange, substitute."  The sense of traveling regularly between home and work developed in the late 19th century.
