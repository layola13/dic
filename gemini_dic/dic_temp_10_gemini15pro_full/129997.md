non-newtonian:/ˌnɒn njuːˈtəʊniən/ (US) /ˌnɑːn nuːˈtoʊniən/| adj.|非牛顿的|A non-Newtonian fluid is a fluid whose viscosity changes with the applied strain rate or shear stress.|Cornstarch mixed with water is a classic example of a non-Newtonian fluid. 玉米淀粉与水混合是非牛顿流体的典型例子。

Oobleck, made from cornstarch and water, is a non-Newtonian fluid. 由玉米淀粉和水制成的欧不裂，是一种非牛顿流体。|Near Antonym: Newtonian| Antonym: (not commonly used, concept typically expressed through Newtonian)|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
non- (prefix): from Latin *nōn* meaning "not"
Newtonian (adjective):  named after Sir Isaac Newton, referring to his laws of motion and viscosity.
