boorishly:/ˈbʊərɪʃli/ | adv. | 粗鲁地，粗野地 | In a manner that is rude, insensitive, and lacking in refinement or manners. | He boorishly interrupted the conversation, shouting his opinions without regard for others. 他粗鲁地打断谈话，不顾他人感受地大声嚷嚷自己的意见。 | rudely, crudely, uncivilly, coarsely | politely, courteously, graciously | adverb

boorish:/ˈbʊərɪʃ/ | adj. | 粗鲁的，粗野的 | Rude, insensitive, and lacking in refinement or manners. | His boorish behavior embarrassed his family at the dinner party. 他粗鲁的行为让他的家人在晚宴上感到尴尬。 | rude, crude, uncivil, coarse | polite, courteous, gracious | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *borel, *boor, from Old French *boire, *boure (“rustic, peasant”), of uncertain origin, probably from Late Latin *būrus (“dull, stupid”), from Ancient Greek βούς (boûs, “ox, cow”).  The sense shifted from "rustic" to "rude" over time.
