exclusionist:/ɪkˈskluːʒənɪst/
n.|排外主义者；排他主义者|A person who favors the exclusion of a particular group or groups from society, rights, or privileges.|The exclusionist policies of the club made it difficult for new members to join.|近义词： bigot, discriminatory, prejudiced, racist, sectarian, segregationist|反义词：inclusionist, integrationist|词性: noun

exclusionist:/ɪkˈskluːʒənɪst/
adj.|排外的；排斥的|Relating to or characterized by the exclusion of a particular group or groups from society, rights, or privileges.|The country's exclusionist immigration policies have been widely criticized.|近义词：discriminatory, restrictive, exclusive|反义词：inclusive, open, welcoming|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From late 19th century: from exclusion + -ist.  Exclusion comes from the Latin "exclusio",  meaning "a shutting out," from the past participle stem of "excludere"  ("to shut out"), from "ex-" ("out") + "claudere" ("to shut").
