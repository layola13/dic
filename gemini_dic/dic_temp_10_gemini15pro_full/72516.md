flurt:/flɜːrt/ /flɝːt/| v. n.|调情，打情骂俏；轻浮地对待；挥动；突然弹出|To behave amorously without serious intent, or to talk playfully and teasingly.|She was flirting with the bartender. 她正在和酒保调情。|近义词：coquet, toy, trifle, dally|反义词：ignore, shun|词性:动词,名词

flurt:/flɜːrt/ /flɝːt/| n.|调情，打情骂俏；轻浮的举动；挥动；突然弹出|An act of flirting; a playful or teasing remark or action.|Her flirt with the actor caused a scandal. 她与那位演员的调情引起了一场丑闻。|近义词：dalliance, coquetry|反义词：seriousness|词性:名词

词源:  From the Middle English *flurten* "to flick or move suddenly," probably imitative. The sense of "to behave amorously without serious intent" developed later.
