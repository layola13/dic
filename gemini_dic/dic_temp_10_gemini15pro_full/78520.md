ghetto:/ˈɡɛtəʊ/ (UK), /ˈɡɛtoʊ/ (US)| n. |贫民窟；少数民族聚居区；被隔离区|A part of a city, especially a slum area, occupied by a minority group or groups. |例句：The family lived in a crowded ghetto.  这家人住在拥挤的贫民区。

|例句：The Warsaw Ghetto was a tragic example of Nazi persecution. 华沙犹太人区是纳粹迫害的一个悲惨例子。|近义词：slum, barrio, enclave |反义词:affluent area, wealthy neighborhood|词性:noun

ghettos:/ˈɡɛtəʊz/ (UK), /ˈɡɛtoʊz/ (US)| n. |贫民窟；少数民族聚居区；被隔离区（ghetto的复数形式）|Plural form of ghetto.|例句：Many cities have ghettos where poverty and crime are rampant. 许多城市都有贫困和犯罪猖獗的贫民区。


|例句：Historically, ghettos have often been places of both oppression and cultural resilience. 从历史上看，贫民区往往既是压迫之地，也是文化韧性之地。|近义词：slums, barrios, enclaves |反义词:affluent areas, wealthy neighborhoods|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Venetian Italian *ghèto* "foundry", later "Jewish quarter in Venice", perhaps from Venetian *géto* "slag", or from Yiddish גהיט *gehit* "deed of separation". The first ghetto was instituted in Venice in 1516.
