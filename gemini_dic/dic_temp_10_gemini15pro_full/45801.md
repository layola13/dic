custodial: /kʌˈstəʊdiəl/ (British) /kəˈstoʊdiəl/ (American)| adj.|保管的；监护的；拘留的|Relating to or involving the legal right to keep and care for someone, especially a child, or to have control over something.|The father was awarded custodial rights of the children. 父亲获得了孩子的监护权。|Synonyms: guardian, protective, caring|Antonyms: neglectful, irresponsible|adjective

custodials: /kʌˈstəʊdiəlz/ (British) /kəˈstoʊdiəlz/ (American)| n.|保管人员；监护人；清洁工|People who work in custodial roles, often referring to cleaning and maintenance staff. (This is a less common usage, usually implying a group of custodial workers).|The custodials keep the building spotless.  清洁工们保持大楼一尘不染。|Synonyms: janitors, cleaners, maintenance staff|Antonyms: N/A (no direct antonym for a group of people in this profession)|noun
