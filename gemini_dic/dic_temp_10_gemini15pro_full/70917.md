firebare:/ˈfaɪərˌbɛər/| adj.|赤裸的，无保护的，易受攻击的|Exposed to fire, or metaphorically, vulnerable and unprotected.  Describes something stripped bare and defenseless, often in the face of danger or criticism.|The firebare hills were scarred by the recent wildfire.  被最近的野火烧得伤痕累累的光秃秃的山丘。|The company's finances were firebare after the scandal.  丑闻之后，这家公司的财务状况岌岌可危。|近义词：exposed, vulnerable, defenseless, unprotected, bare, open, undefended|反义词：protected, safe, secure, shielded, defended|词性:形容词

词源:  A compound word formed from "fire" and "bare."  "Fire" refers to the destructive element, and "bare" highlights the lack of protection.
