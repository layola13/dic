coelenterata:/sɪˈlɛntəˌreɪtə/ /sɪˈlɛntərətə/ | n. |腔肠动物（门）|An aquatic invertebrate animal of the phylum Coelenterata, which includes jellyfishes, corals, and sea anemones. Characterized by a radially symmetrical body with a single opening that serves as both mouth and anus, and tentacles armed with stinging cells. This term is now considered outdated and the phyla Cnidaria and Ctenophora are now used instead.|The coelenterata exhibit a remarkable diversity of forms. 腔肠动物展现出惊人的形态多样性。|Synonym: cnidarian, ctenophore |Antonym: (No direct antonym, as it's a taxonomic group) |Noun
