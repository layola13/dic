nonperverseness:/ˌnɒnpərˈvɜːsnəs/
n.
lack of perverseness; straightforwardness
The nonperverseness of her response was refreshing. 她的回答直截了当，令人耳目一新。
straightforwardness, honesty, sincerity, artlessness
perverseness, crookedness, dishonesty, deviousness
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + perverseness (the state of being perverse)

Perverse: from Old French pervers, from Latin perversus, past participle of pervertere (to turn the wrong way), from per- (thoroughly) + vertere (to turn)
