deadwood:/ˈdɛdwʊd/| n.|枯木；无用的人或物|A tree or branch that is dead and has become dry.  Also, a person or thing that is no longer useful or productive. |The forest was full of deadwood after the fire.  森林大火过后，到处都是枯木。| The company needs to get rid of some of its deadwood. 公司需要裁掉一些冗员。|synonyms:  rubbish, debris, waste, lumber, useless person, dead timber|antonyms:  living wood, valuable asset, essential personnel|noun


deadwood:/ˈdɛdwʊd/| adj. (informal)|过时的；无用的|Outdated or no longer useful.|The company is trying to eliminate deadwood regulations. 公司正试图取消过时的规定。|  The deadwood furniture was finally taken to the dump.  过时的家具最终被送到了垃圾场。|synonyms:  obsolete, outdated, redundant, superfluous|antonyms:  current, relevant, useful, essential|adjective

From Old English *dēadwōd*, from *dēad* (dead) + *wōd* (wood).
