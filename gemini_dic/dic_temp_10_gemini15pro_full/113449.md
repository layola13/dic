lynchs:/lɪntʃɪz/| n.|私刑处死（lynch的第三人称单数形式）|The act of killing someone without a legal trial, usually by hanging, often by a mob.|例句：The history books record several lynchs that occurred during that turbulent period. 历史书籍记载了在那动荡时期发生的几次私刑。

例句：False accusations led to several lynchs in the Wild West.  不实的指控导致了在蛮荒西部发生了几起私刑。|近义词：executions, murders|反义词:pardons, acquittals|词性:名词

lynch:/lɪntʃ/| vt. |私刑处死|To kill someone without a legal trial, usually by hanging, often by a mob.|例句：They threatened to lynch the suspected cattle thief. 他们威胁要将 suspected 牛贼私刑处死。

例句：The angry mob tried to lynch the prisoner.  愤怒的暴徒试图对囚犯处以私刑。|近义词：execute, murder|反义词:pardon, acquit|词性:动词


词源:From Lynch's Law, named after Charles Lynch (1736-1796), a Virginia planter and justice of the peace who headed an extralegal court during the American Revolution.  源自“林奇法”，以查尔斯·林奇（1736-1796）的名字命名，他是一位弗吉尼亚种植园主和治安法官，在美国独立战争期间领导了一个非法法庭。
