decrepit:/dɪˈkrɛpɪt/ | adj. | 破旧的，衰老的，老朽的 | Worn out or weakened by old age or long use.  Showing the marked effects of old age or long use. |  The decrepit old house was about to collapse.  这栋破旧的老房子快要倒塌了。 |  The decrepit bridge was deemed unsafe. 这座老朽的桥被认为不安全。 | 近义词: dilapidated, ramshackle, feeble, infirm, frail | 反义词: sturdy, robust, vigorous, sound | 词性: 形容词

Etymology: From Latin *decrepitus*,  past participle of *decrepare* "to crackle, rattle," from *de-* "down" + *crepare* "to crack, rattle, creak."
