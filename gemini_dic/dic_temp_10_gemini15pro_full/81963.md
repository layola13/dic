grimy:/ˈɡraɪmi/ | adj. | 肮脏的，污秽的 | Covered with or characterized by grime; dirty. | The grimy walls of the alley looked forbidding. 小巷肮脏的墙壁看起来令人望而生畏。 |  The child's grimy hands showed he had been playing in the mud. 孩子脏兮兮的手表明他一直在泥里玩。 | 近义词：dirty, filthy, soiled, grubby | 反义词：clean, spotless, pristine | 词性: 形容词

Etymology: From grime + -y.  Grime itself comes from Middle English *grim, from Old Norse grima (“mask, hood”), from Proto-Germanic *grimmaz, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ghrey- (“to rub, smear”).
