lug:/lʌɡ/| n. |把手，耳状物；笨重的人；（非正式）努力拖拉，费力搬运|A projecting part, typically of metal, that is used as a handle or a point of attachment.  A person who is awkward, clumsy, or slow-witting. (informal) To carry or drag something heavy with effort.|The suitcase had a broken lug.  He was a big lug of a man.  I had to lug the groceries up three flights of stairs.|近义词：handle, grip, haul, drag, carry,  clumsy person, oaf|反义词:leave, abandon|词性:noun, verb

lug:/lʌɡ/| v. |努力拖拉，费力搬运|To carry or drag something heavy with effort.|I lugged the suitcase up the stairs. 我费力地把行李箱搬上楼。|近义词：haul, drag, carry|反义词:leave, abandon|词性:verb

PHRASE->
lug around = 随身携带(重物):  I've been lugging around this heavy bag all day. 我一整天都带着这个沉重的包。
lug it =  (非正式) 辛苦地搬运:  I had to lug it all the way up the hill. 我不得不一路把它搬到山上。
lug something up/down/along etc. =  吃力地搬上/搬下/搬走等:  We lugged the piano up the stairs. 我们费力地把钢琴搬上了楼梯。
lug nuts =  (汽车) 轮胎螺母:  Make sure the lug nuts are tightened properly. 确保轮胎螺母已正确拧紧。
lug someone/something into =  (非正式)  勉强把某人/某物拉进:  They lugged him into the car. 他们把他硬塞进车里。
