corrupt:/kəˈrʌpt/ | adj. vt. | 腐败的，堕落的；使腐败，使堕落 |  (adj.) Having or showing a willingness to act dishonestly in return for money or personal gain; (v.) Cause to act dishonestly in return for money or personal gain. | (adj.) The corrupt official was eventually caught and prosecuted.  腐败的官员最终被抓获并被起诉。 | (v.)  Power tends to corrupt, and absolute power corrupts absolutely. 权力容易导致腐败，绝对的权力导致绝对的腐败。| 近义词: dishonest, crooked, contaminated, | 反义词: honest, upright, pure | 词性: 形容词，动词

词源:  From Latin *corruptus*, past participle of *corrumpere* "to break up, destroy, spoil, corrupt," from *com-* "together" (see com-) + *rumpere* "to break" (see rupture (n.)).  The notion is of "breaking up completely."
