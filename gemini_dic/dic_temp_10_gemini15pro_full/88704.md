hobble:/ˈhɑːbl/ (US), /ˈhɒbl/ (UK)| v. n.|蹒跚; 跛行; 束缚; 难题|To walk with difficulty, typically because of pain from an injury to the leg or foot; a rope or strap used to tie together the legs of a horse or other animal to prevent it from straying; a problem or difficulty that slows down progress or makes something difficult to achieve.|例句：The old man hobbled across the street. 老人蹒跚地穿过街道。|近义词：limp, shuffle, stagger, restrict, hamper, impede|反义词:run, sprint, facilitate, aid|词性:动词,名词
