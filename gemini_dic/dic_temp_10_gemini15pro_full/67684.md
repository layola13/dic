exuberant: /ɪɡˈz(j)uːbərənt/ | adj. | 充满活力的；茂盛的；热情洋溢的 | Filled with or characterized by a lively energy and excitement; growing luxuriantly;  effusively enthusiastic. |  The children were exuberant after their trip to the zoo. 孩子们去完动物园后兴高采烈。| synonyms: lively, energetic, enthusiastic, vibrant, lavish, profuse | antonyms: subdued, restrained, listless, apathetic, sparse | adjective


exuberance: /ɪɡˈz(j)uːbərəns/ | n. |  活力；茂盛；热情洋溢 | The quality of being exuberant. |  Her exuberance was infectious. 她的热情很有感染力。| synonyms: liveliness, energy, enthusiasm, vibrancy, lavishness, profusion | antonyms: subduedness, restraint, listlessness, apathy, sparseness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *exuberant- (stem of exuberans), present participle of exuberare "to be abundant, overflow," from ex- "out" + uber "fertile, fruitful."
