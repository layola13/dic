esotery:/ˈɛsəʊtɛri/ /ˈiːsəʊtɛri/
n.|深奥，秘传，难懂的事物|something intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people with a specialized knowledge or interest.|The professor’s lectures were filled with esotery that only a few students could grasp. 教授的讲座充满了深奥的知识，只有少数学生能够理解。

|The group's rituals were shrouded in esotery.  该团体的仪式笼罩在神秘之中。
|近义词：arcana, mysticism, occultism|反义词：exotericism|词性：noun

esoteric:/ˌɛsəˈtɛrɪk/ /ˌiːsəˈtɛrɪk/
adj.|深奥的，秘传的，难懂的|intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people with a specialized knowledge or interest.|The book delved into esoteric philosophical concepts. 这本书深入探讨了深奥的哲学概念。

|His esoteric knowledge of wine impressed everyone at the dinner party. 他对葡萄酒的深奥知识给宴会上的每个人都留下了深刻的印象。
|近义词：abstruse, arcane, cryptic|反义词：exoteric, common, simple|词性：adjective
