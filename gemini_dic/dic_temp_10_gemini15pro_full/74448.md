frangipani:/ˌfrændʒɪˈpɑːni/ | n. |鸡蛋花，素馨花 |A tropical American tree or shrub with clusters of fragrant white, yellow, or pink flowers. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental. |The frangipani blossoms filled the air with a sweet scent. 鸡蛋花的花朵使空气中充满了甜美的香味。|Synonyms: plumeria, temple tree |Antonyms: N/A | Noun


frangipanis:/ˌfrændʒɪˈpɑːniz/ | n. |鸡蛋花（复数）|Plural form of frangipani. |The frangipanis in the garden were in full bloom. 花园里的鸡蛋花盛开了。|Synonyms: plumerias, temple trees |Antonyms: N/A | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *frangipanier*, from Italian *frangipane* "coagulated milk", because of the milky sap of the plant. Ultimately from Frankish *franka-pan* "Frankish bread," a kind of almond cake.
