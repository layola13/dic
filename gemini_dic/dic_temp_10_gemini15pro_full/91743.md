hyperdicrotism:/ˌhaɪpərˈdaɪkrətɪzəm/ | n. |过度双脉波|A condition characterized by an exaggerated secondary wave in the pulse tracing, often indicating low blood pressure or reduced arterial elasticity.|The patient's weak pulse and hyperdicrotism suggested a significant drop in blood pressure.|Near synonyms: dicrotic pulse, double pulse|Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, consider normal pulse,  monocrotic pulse)|noun
