hole-in-corner:/ˌhəʊl ɪn ˈkɔːnə(r)/ (UK) /ˌhoʊl ɪn ˈkɔːrnər/ (US)| adj.|秘密的；鬼鬼祟祟的；偷偷摸摸的|Characterized by secrecy; clandestine; furtive. Done in a private place, especially because it is illegal or disapproved of.|They held hole-in-corner meetings to plan the rebellion. 他们秘密集会策划叛乱。|Synonyms: clandestine, secretive, furtive, surreptitious, covert, underhand, shady|Antonyms: open, public, overt|adjective

hole-in-corner:/ˌhəʊl ɪn ˈkɔːnə(r)/ (UK) /ˌhoʊl ɪn ˈkɔːrnər/ (US)| n.|隐蔽的角落；秘密的地方|A secluded or secret place.|Their hole-in-corner office was difficult to find. 他们隐蔽的办公室很难找到。|Synonyms: hideaway, hideout, retreat|Antonyms: public place, open area|noun
