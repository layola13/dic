legitimizing: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪˌmaɪzɪŋ/
verb, present participle
使合法化，使正当化
The government passed a law legitimizing the sale of marijuana. 政府通过了一项法律，使大麻销售合法化。
Making a large donation doesn't legitimize his criminal activities.  大额捐款并不能使他的犯罪活动合法化。
近义词: validating, justifying, authorizing, sanctioning
反义词: invalidating, condemning, denouncing, prohibiting
词性: verb (present participle)

legitimate: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət/
adjective
合法的，正当的；合理的
The president is the legitimate ruler of the country. 总统是这个国家的合法统治者。
He had a legitimate reason for being late. 他迟到是有正当理由的。
近义词: legal, valid, lawful, justifiable
反义词: illegal, invalid, unlawful, illegitimate
词性: adjective

legitimacy: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi/
noun
合法性，正当性
The legitimacy of the election results is being questioned.  选举结果的合法性受到质疑。
The government is struggling to maintain its legitimacy. 政府正在努力维持其合法性。
近义词: validity, lawfulness, legality
反义词: illegitimacy, invalidity, unlawfulness
词性: noun

legitimately: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmətli/
adverb
合法地，正当地
The business was legitimately acquired. 这家公司是合法收购的。
He can legitimately claim to be the best in his field. 他可以理直气壮地称自己是该领域的佼佼者。
近义词: legally, validly, lawfully, justifiably
反义词: illegally, invalidly, unlawfully, illegitimately
词性: adverb


词源: From late Middle English: from Old French legitimer, from Latin legitimare, from legitimus ‘lawful’.
