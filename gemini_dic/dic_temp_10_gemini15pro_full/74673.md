freebooters:/ˈfriːˌbuːtərz/
noun
掠夺者；海盗；强盗
A pirate or buccaneer, especially one operating in the West Indies during the 17th century.
The freebooters plundered the Spanish galleons.  这些海盗掠夺了西班牙大帆船。
Synonyms: pirate, buccaneer, corsair, privateer, raider, marauder
Antonyms: lawman, police


Etymology:
From Dutch *vrijbuiter*, from vrij "free" + buit "booty, plunder".  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *butin* "to profit, improve".
