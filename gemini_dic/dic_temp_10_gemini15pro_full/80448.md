gonfanon:/ˈɡɒnfənɒn/ (US) /ˈɡɑːnfənɑːn/ (UK)| n.| 旗帜，旌旗；（尤指）燕尾旗|A gonfanon is a type of heraldic flag, often pointed or swallow-tailed, historically used by important people or groups.|The knight carried his family's gonfanon into battle. 骑士带着他家族的旌旗冲入战场。|Synonyms: banner, standard, pennant, ensign|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "flaglessness")|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gonfanon*, from Italian *gonfalone*, from Frankish *gundfano* ("war-flag"), from *gund* ("war, battle") + *fano* ("cloth, flag"). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *gunþiz* ("battle") and *fanō* ("cloth").
