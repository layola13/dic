not-so-bad:/ˌnɒt səʊ ˈbæd/ /ˌnɑːt soʊ ˈbæd/ | adj. | 还不错的，过得去的 |  A colloquial expression used to describe something that is better than expected, but not necessarily good. It often implies a sense of relief or mild satisfaction. | The food at the new restaurant was not-so-bad.  这家新餐馆的菜还不错。| Synonyms: tolerable, decent, alright, okay, passable | Antonyms: terrible, awful, horrible, dreadful | adjective

not-so-bad:/ˌnɒt səʊ ˈbæd/ /ˌnɑːt soʊ ˈbæd/ | adv. | 相当好地，还可以地 |  A colloquial expression used to describe how something was done or performed, indicating that it was better than anticipated but not outstanding. | He played the piano not-so-badly for a beginner. 对于一个初学者来说，他的钢琴弹得还不错。 | Synonyms: tolerably, decently, alright, okay | Antonyms: terribly, awfully, horribly, dreadfully | adverb
