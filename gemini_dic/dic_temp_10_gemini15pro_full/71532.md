flapperism:/ˈflæpərɪzəm/ | n. |轻佻女子的行为；轻佻女子的作风|The behavior or lifestyle of a flapper, a young woman in the 1920s known for her unconventional and liberated style.|The flapperism of the 1920s was characterized by short skirts, bobbed hair, and a carefree attitude. 20世纪20年代的轻佻女子作风以短裙、波波头和无忧无虑的态度为特征。|近义词: recklessness, wildness, abandon |反义词: conservatism, restraint, modesty| noun


Etymology->

From *flapper* + *-ism*.  *Flapper* originally referred to a young prostitute or a young bird learning to fly, but in the 1920s it came to mean a young woman who flouted conventional social norms.  *-ism* is a suffix forming nouns denoting practices, doctrines, etc.
