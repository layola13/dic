bridgey:/ˈbrɪdʒi/
adjective
(Informal, primarily British) Upper-middle class or aristocratic, typically associated with certain pursuits like horse riding or sailing.
(非正式，主要英国) 上层中产阶级或贵族，通常与某些活动有关，如骑马或航海。
"He's got a very bridgey voice, doesn't he?" "He went to Eton, you know."  “他的声音很像上层阶级，不是吗？” “你知道他上过伊顿公学。”
近义词: posh, aristocratic, upper-class, hoity-toity
反义词: working-class, common, ordinary
词性: adjective

词源:  Derived from "bridge," likely in the context of card games popular among the upper-middle class, and extended metaphorically to describe the social group itself.  源自“桥牌”，可能指的是在上层中产阶级中流行的纸牌游戏，并隐喻地扩展到描述这个社会群体本身。
