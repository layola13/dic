fancywork: /ˈfænsiˌwɜːrk/ (US) /ˈfænsiˌwɜːk/ (UK)| n. |手工制品；精巧细致的针线活；刺绣品| Decorative needlework or other handiwork, especially that done for ornamentation or amusement. | She spent her evenings doing fancywork, creating intricate doilies and embroidered cushions. 她晚上做些精巧的针线活，制作复杂的桌布和刺绣靠垫。 | 近义词：needlework, handiwork, embroidery, craftwork | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, possibly "rough work" or "shoddy work" in terms of quality) | 词性: 名词

fancywork: /ˈfænsiˌwɜːrk/ (US) /ˈfænsiˌwɜːk/ (UK)| n. |空想；幻想; 花哨无用的东西|  Elaborate but often impractical ideas or plans. (less common usage) | He filled his head with fancywork about becoming a famous inventor, but he never actually built anything. 他满脑子都是想成为著名发明家的幻想，但他从未真正制造过任何东西。|近义词：fantasy, daydream, pipe dream | 反义词: reality, practicality | 词性: 名词
