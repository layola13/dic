geode:/ˈdʒiːoʊd/| n.|晶洞，晶球|A hollow rock inside which crystals have grown.|I found a beautiful geode while hiking in the mountains. 我在山上徒步旅行时发现了一个美丽的晶洞。|Synonyms: druse, vug|Antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French géode, from Latin geōdēs (“earthlike”), from Ancient Greek γεώδης (geṓdēs, “earthlike”), from γῆ (gê, “earth”) + -ώδης (-ṓdēs, resembling).
