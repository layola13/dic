affinity-groups:/əˈfɪnɪti ɡruːps/| n.|亲和团体，兴趣小组|A group of people linked by a common interest, goal, or experience, especially in a workplace or educational setting.  These groups provide a sense of belonging and support for individuals who share similar backgrounds or identities.|Our company has several affinity groups, including one for LGBTQ+ employees and one for working parents.|Synonyms: support groups, peer groups, networks, communities|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider)  isolated individuals, disparate groups|noun

affinity-group:/əˈfɪnɪti ɡruːp/| n.|亲和团体，兴趣小组|Singular form of affinity groups. Refers to a single group of people linked by a common interest, goal, or experience.|The women's affinity-group meets monthly to discuss career development.|Synonyms: support group, peer group, network, community|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider)  isolated individual|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "affinity" (late Middle English: from Old French afinite, from Latin affinitas, from affinis ‘bordering on, related to’) + "group" (Middle English: from Old French groupe, ultimately of Germanic origin and related to crop).
