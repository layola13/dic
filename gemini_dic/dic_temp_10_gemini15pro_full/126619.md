nail-biting:/ˈneɪlˌbaɪtɪŋ/| adj.|令人紧张的，扣人心弦的|Causing great anxiety or suspense; very exciting and dramatic. |The final game was a nail-biting finish.  决赛是一场扣人心弦的比赛。|Synonyms: suspenseful, thrilling, gripping, tense|Antonyms: relaxing, calming, boring|adjective

nail-biting:/ˈneɪlˌbaɪtɪŋ/| n.|咬指甲(尤指因紧张或焦虑)|The act of biting one's nails, especially due to nervousness or anxiety. |Her nail-biting got worse during the exam. 考试期间她咬指甲的习惯更严重了。|Synonyms: onychophagia|Antonyms: N/A|noun

词源:  "nail-biting" is a compound word formed from "nail" and "biting."  It literally refers to the act of biting one's nails, which is often associated with nervousness or anxiety.  This physical manifestation of tension led to the figurative use of the word to describe situations that evoke similar feelings of suspense and anxiety.
