mastodon: /ˈmæstəˌdɑːn/ (US) /ˈmæstədɒn/ (UK)| n. |乳齿象；庞然大物；庞大而笨拙的人或物|A large extinct elephant-like mammal of the Miocene to Pleistocene epochs, having teeth with cone-shaped cusps.  Figuratively, something huge or unwieldy.|
Example: The museum displayed a complete mastodon skeleton.  博物馆展出了一具完整的乳齿象骨架。

Example: The old factory was a mastodon of a building, taking up a whole city block. 这家老工厂是一座庞然大物般的建筑，占据了整个街区。


近义词: mammoth, behemoth, giant
反义词: dwarf, midget
词性: noun


词源: From French *mastodonte*, from Ancient Greek *μαστός (mastós, “breast”) + *ὀδούς (odoús, “tooth”)*.  The name refers to the nipple-like projections on the molar teeth.
