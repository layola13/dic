gypsum:/ˈdʒɪpsəm/| n. |石膏|A soft white or gray mineral consisting of hydrated calcium sulfate. It is used to make plaster of Paris and fertilizers, and occurs in many forms, including alabaster.|The walls were made of gypsum plaster.|Synonyms: plaster, plasterboard, drywall, alabaster|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a material, could consider materials with opposite properties like hard stones - granite, marble)|Noun
