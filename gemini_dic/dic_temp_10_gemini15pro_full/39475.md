connivers:/kəˈnaɪvərz/| n.|共谋者，串通者，纵容者|People who secretly plan to do something dishonest or illegal are connivers.  Those involved in the price-fixing scheme were connivers who sought to defraud consumers. |例句：The connivers were eventually caught and prosecuted. 这些串通者最终被抓获并被起诉。

例句：The police suspected the two men were connivers in the robbery. 警方怀疑这两个人是抢劫案的同谋。|近义词：plotters, schemers, collaborators|反义词: opponent, adversary|Noun

connive:/kəˈnaɪv/| v.|共谋，串通，纵容|To connive is to secretly plan with someone to do something dishonest or illegal. The CEO connived with the CFO to embezzle company funds. |例句：He connived with his colleagues to cheat on the exam. 他与同事串通作弊考试。

例句：She connived at her son's truancy by writing false notes for him. 她通过给他写假条来纵容儿子逃学。|近义词：conspire, collude, scheme|反义词: expose, obstruct |Verb
