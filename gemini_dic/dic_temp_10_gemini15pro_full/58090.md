drop-a-brick:/ˌdrɑːp ə ˈbrɪk/| idiom | 说错话；失言；犯尴尬的错误 | To say or do something that causes embarrassment or offense, usually unintentionally. |  Example 1: I really dropped a brick when I asked her how her husband was – he'd died last year! 我真是说错话了，我问她丈夫怎么样了——他去年就去世了！

Example 2: He dropped a brick by mentioning her ex-boyfriend in front of her new partner. 他在她现任男友面前提到了她的前男友，真是失言了。 | Synonyms: make a gaffe, put one's foot in one's mouth, blunder, commit a faux pas | Antonyms: behave tactfully, be diplomatic | idiom
