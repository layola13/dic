compulsive:/kəmˈpʌlsɪv/ | adj. |强迫性的；难以抑制的| Resulting from or relating to an irresistible urge, especially one that is against one's conscious wishes. | He's a compulsive gambler. 他是个嗜赌成瘾的人。| She's a compulsive shopper. 她是个购物狂。|  Synonyms: obsessive, driven, uncontrollable, involuntary | Antonyms: controlled, restrained, voluntary | adjective

compulsively:/kəmˈpʌlsɪvli/ | adv. |强迫地；不由自主地| In a compulsive manner. | He compulsively checks his phone every few minutes. 他每隔几分钟就忍不住要查看手机。 | She washes her hands compulsively. 她强迫性地洗手。| Synonyms: obsessively, involuntarily | Antonyms: restrainedly, voluntarily | adverb

compulsion:/kəmˈpʌlʃən/ | n. |强迫；冲动 | An irresistible urge to behave in a certain way, especially against one's conscious wishes. | He felt a compulsion to tell her the truth. 他感到一种不得不告诉她真相的冲动。| She has a compulsion to clean everything. 她有一种不得不把所有东西都打扫干净的冲动。| Synonyms: urge, impulse, obsession | Antonyms: aversion, reluctance | noun


Etymology:
From Late Latin compuls- “driven together,” from the verb compellere, from com- “together” + pellere “to drive.”
