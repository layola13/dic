unconscious: /ʌnˈkɒnʃəs/ (UK) /ʌnˈkɑːnʃəs/ (US)
adjective
无意识的；失去知觉的；未察觉的
Not conscious; without awareness, sensation, or cognition.
He was knocked unconscious by the blow. 他被那一击打昏了。

unconscious: /ʌnˈkɒnʃəs/ (UK) /ʌnˈkɑːnʃəs/ (US)
adjective
（行为、感觉）无意识的，下意识的
Done or existing without one realizing.
She had an unconscious habit of biting her nails. 她有无意识咬指甲的习惯。

unconscious: /ʌnˈkɒnʃəs/ (UK) /ʌnˈkɑːnʃəs/ (US)
noun
潜意识，无意识
The part of the mind that is inaccessible to the conscious mind but that affects behaviour and emotions.
Jung explored the world of the unconscious. 荣格探索了潜意识的世界。


Etymology:  From Late Latin *inconscius, from in- ‘not’ + conscius ‘conscious.’

Synonyms: insensible, senseless, inert, comatose, unaware, oblivious, unwitting
Antonyms: conscious, aware, sensible, alert
