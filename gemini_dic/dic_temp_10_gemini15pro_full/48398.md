dedicate: /ˈdedɪkeɪt/ (US) /ˈdedɪkeɪt/ (UK)
|vt.| 奉献，献身；致力于；题献 | To devote wholly and earnestly, as to some person or purpose. To address a book, poem, play, etc., to someone as a mark of respect or affection. | She dedicated her life to helping others. 她毕生致力于帮助他人。 He dedicated his new book to his wife. 他将他的新书献给他的妻子。|近义词: devote, commit, consecrate |反义词: profane, desecrate | verb

dedication: /ˌdedɪˈkeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌdedɪˈkeɪʃən/ (UK)
|n.| 奉献，献身；致力于；题词 | The act of dedicating. A ceremony in which something is dedicated. | Her dedication to her work is admirable. 她对工作的奉献精神令人钦佩。 The dedication of the new hospital was a grand event. 新医院的落成典礼是一场盛事。 | 近义词: commitment, devotion, consecration |反义词: indifference, apathy | noun
