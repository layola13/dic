disinhibit:/ˌdɪsɪnˈhɪbɪt/
verb
解除抑制，使不再拘束
To free from inhibition; to cause to lose restraint or reserve.
Alcohol can disinhibit some people, making them more talkative. 酒精会使一些人放得开，变得更健谈。
近义词：liberate, release, unleash
反义词：inhibit, restrain, suppress
词性：v.

disinhibited:/ˌdɪsɪnˈhɪbɪtɪd/
adjective
无拘无束的，放得开的
Free from inhibition; unrestrained.
His disinhibited behavior at the party surprised everyone. 他在聚会上的放肆行为让每个人都感到惊讶。
近义词：uninhibited, unrestrained, free
反义词：inhibited, restrained, reserved
词性：adj.

disinhibition:/ˌdɪsɪn.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən/
noun
解除抑制，去抑制
The removal of inhibition; a state of being disinhibited.
The disinhibition caused by the drug led to reckless behavior.  药物引起的去抑制导致了鲁莽的行为。
近义词：liberation, release
反义词：inhibition, restraint
词性：n.
