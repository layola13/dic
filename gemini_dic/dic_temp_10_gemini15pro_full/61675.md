emmetropia: /ˌɛmɪˈtrəʊpiə/ | n. | 正视，正视眼 | The condition of the eye when parallel rays are focused exactly on the retina, allowing clear vision of distant objects without the need for corrective lenses.  A state in which the eye is relaxed and focused for distant vision, and light rays entering the eye are refracted to form a sharp image on the retina. |  Her emmetropia allowed her to see the distant mountains clearly. 她的正视眼让她可以清晰地看到远处的山脉。| syn: normal vision | ant: ametropia, myopia, hyperopia | noun
