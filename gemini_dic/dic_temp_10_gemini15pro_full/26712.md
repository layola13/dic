cader:/ˈkeɪdər/| n.| 骨干；核心小组；基层骨干|A small group of trained people who are ready to lead a larger group, especially in politics or the military.|The party's cadres were mobilized to distribute leaflets.|近义词：core, nucleus, framework|反义词:masses, rank and file|词性:名词

Etymology: From French *cadre* "frame, framework," from Italian *quadro* "square," from Latin *quadrum*, neuter of *quattuor* "four."  The sense evolved from "framework of officers" to "group of trained personnel."
