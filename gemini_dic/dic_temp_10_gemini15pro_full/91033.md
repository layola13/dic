huskies:/ˈhʌskiz/ | n. |哈士奇犬（复数）; 坚韧强壮的人（复数）| A breed of sled dog known for its thick coat, blue eyes, and wolf-like appearance; Also refers to a strong and resilient person. | Example: The huskies howled at the moon. 这些哈士奇对着月亮嚎叫。| Example:  The team of huskies pulled the sled across the snowy terrain.  这群哈士奇拉着雪橇穿过雪地。| 近义词: sled dogs, (for people) tough individuals, stalwarts | 反义词: (for dogs) weaklings, (for people) weaklings, cowards | 词性: noun

husky:/ˈhʌski/ | adj. | 声音沙哑的;强壮的;粗糙的 |  (of a voice) hoarse or rough-sounding.  Also strong and powerfully built. | Example: He had a husky voice from shouting all day. 他喊了一整天，声音变得沙哑。 | Example: The husky lumberjack effortlessly lifted the heavy log.  强壮的伐木工人毫不费力地举起了沉重的原木。 | 近义词: (for voice) hoarse, raspy, gravelly; (for build) strong, sturdy, burly | 反义词: (for voice) clear, melodious; (for build) weak, frail, delicate | 词性: adjective

词源: From the obsolete word “Huskie,” a variant of “Eskie,” short for “Eskimo,” referring to the Eskimo people who bred these dogs.
