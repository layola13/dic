indispose:/ˌɪndɪˈspəʊz/ (UK), /ˌɪndɪˈspoʊz/ (US)| v. |使不想; 使不适; 使无能力|To make someone slightly unwell or unwilling to do something.|例句：The spicy food indisposed him.  辛辣的食物让他感到不适。

例句：A bad cold indisposed her for work.  重感冒使她无法工作。

近义词：ail, disable, incapacitate, disqualify|反义词:incline, dispose, enable|词性:动词


indisposed:/ˌɪndɪˈspəʊzd/ (UK), /ˌɪndɪˈspoʊzd/ (US)| adj. |不愿意的; 不想做的; 不舒服的|Slightly unwell or unwilling to do something.|例句：I'm feeling a little indisposed today. 我今天感觉有点不舒服。

例句：He was indisposed to accept the invitation. 他不愿接受邀请。


近义词：unwell, reluctant, unwilling, averse|反义词:well, healthy, willing, eager|词性:形容词
