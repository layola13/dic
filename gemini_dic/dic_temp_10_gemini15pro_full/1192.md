acclivitous: /əˈklɪvɪtəs/
adjective
陡峭的，向上倾斜的
rising or inclined upward; steep
The acclivitous path made the hike challenging. 这条陡峭的小路使徒步旅行变得很有挑战性。

The cyclists struggled up the acclivitous hill.  骑自行车的人艰难地爬上陡峭的山坡。

近义词: steep, uphill, ascending, inclined
反义词: declivitous, descending, downhill
词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *acclivitous, from acclivis (“sloping upward”), from ad- (“to, toward”) + clivus (“slope”).  Related to the word "clivity."

USAGE->
Attributive usage (modifying a noun):
The acclivitous terrain was difficult to navigate. =  陡峭的地形很难通行。
They avoided the acclivitous trail. = 他们避开了陡峭的小路。

Predicative usage (following a linking verb):
The road became increasingly acclivitous. = 道路变得越来越陡峭。
The path to the summit was acclivitous. = 通往山顶的小路很陡峭。


PHRASE->
an acclivitous slope = 一个陡峭的斜坡
acclivitous terrain = 陡峭的地形

an acclivitous road = 一条陡峭的道路
