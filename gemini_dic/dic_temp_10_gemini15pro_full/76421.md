gaia:/ˈɡaɪə/ /ˈɡeɪə/| n.|盖亚，盖娅|In Greek mythology, Gaia is the personification of the Earth and one of the Greek primordial deities.  In modern usage, "Gaia" often refers to the Gaia hypothesis, which proposes that living organisms interact with their inorganic surroundings on Earth to form a synergistic and self-regulating, complex system that helps to maintain and perpetuate the conditions for life on the planet.|例句：Gaia is honored as the mother of all life. 盖亚被尊为所有生命的母亲。

例句：The Gaia hypothesis suggests that the Earth is a self-regulating system. 盖亚假说认为地球是一个自我调节的系统。|近义词：Earth, Terra (Latin), Mother Earth|反义词:None (Gaia represents a unique concept)|词性:noun
