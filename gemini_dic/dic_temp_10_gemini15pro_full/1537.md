ache:/eɪk/ | n. vi. | 疼痛，酸痛；渴望 | A continuous or prolonged dull pain.  To suffer from a continuous dull pain; to yearn. | My head aches. 我的头疼。

I ache for home. 我渴望回家。 | synonyms: pain, hurt, soreness, throb, yearn, long | antonyms: comfort, relief, ease | noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *acan "to ache," of uncertain origin. Related to Old High German ahha "pain," Greek akhos "pain, grief," Latin aestus "heat, surge." The noun is from the verb.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
My back aches after a long day at work. = 我工作了一整天后背疼。
He aches for a chance to prove himself. = 他渴望有机会证明自己。

一般过去时:
My legs ached after the marathon. = 马拉松后我的腿疼。
She ached for her lost love. = 她渴望她失去的爱。

现在完成时:
I have ached for this opportunity for years. = 多年来我一直渴望这个机会。
My head has been aching all day. = 我的头疼了一整天。


过去完成时:
His muscles had ached for days after the climb. = 攀登之后他的肌肉疼痛了好几天。
She had ached to see her family for months. = 她渴望见到家人已经好几个月了。

将来时:
I know my feet will ache after this hike. = 我知道这次徒步旅行后我的脚会疼。
If I don't stretch, my body will ache tomorrow. = 如果我不做伸展运动，明天我的身体会疼。

虚拟语气:
If my head didn't ache, I would go to the party. = 如果我的头不疼，我就去参加派对。
I wish my heart didn't ache so much. = 我希望我的心不要这么痛。

被动语态: (Ache is rarely used in the passive voice.)

疑问句:
Does your stomach ache? = 你肚子疼吗？
What part of your body aches? = 你身体哪个部位疼？

否定句:
My head doesn't ache anymore. = 我的头不再疼了。
He doesn't ache for fame and fortune. = 他不渴望名利。
