floccillation:/ˌflɒkɪˈleɪʃən/ | n. |絮凝；结絮；毛状云形成|The process of forming small clumps or masses, especially in a liquid or suspension. This can refer to the gathering of wool fibers, the formation of cloud-like patterns, or the clumping of particles in a solution. |The floccillation of the particles in the water made it appear cloudy. 水中颗粒的絮凝使水看起来浑浊。|Synonyms: aggregation, clumping, coagulation, flocculence |Antonyms: dispersion, dissolution | noun

floccillate:/ˈflɒkɪleɪt/ | v. |絮凝；结絮|To form small clumps or masses, especially in a liquid or suspension. |The chemicals caused the impurities in the water to floccillate.  这些化学物质使水中的杂质絮凝。|Synonyms: aggregate, clump, coagulate |Antonyms: disperse, dissolve | verb
