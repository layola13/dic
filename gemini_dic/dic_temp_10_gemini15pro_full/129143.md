no-end:/noʊˈɛnd/| adv. adj.| 没完没了地，大量地；极大的，很多的 |A large amount or extent; endlessly.|  The children have been giving their parents no-end of trouble. | 近义词: endlessly, incessantly, continually, a great deal of, a lot of | 反义词: limited, slightly, a little | 词性: 副词/形容词

词源: "No end" originated as a colloquial expression in the late 19th century, combining the negative "no" with "end" to emphasize a large quantity or an unending process.  It gained popularity through usage and eventually became a standard idiom.
