barging:/ˈbɑːrdʒɪŋ/| n. |闯入， barges的动名词形式|The act of entering a place abruptly or rudely, often without invitation or permission.  It can also refer to the movement of a barge.|例句：His barging into the meeting disrupted the proceedings. 他闯进会议扰乱了议程。

例句：The barging of the coal down the river was efficient. 通过驳船沿河运输煤炭效率很高。|近义词：interrupting, intruding, bursting in, encroaching |反义词:leaving, exiting, withdrawing|词性: noun (gerund)
barge:/bɑːrdʒ/| n. vi. vt.|驳船；猛撞；闯入|A flat-bottomed boat for carrying freight, typically on canals and rivers. As a verb, it means to move forcefully or roughly, or to interrupt rudely.|例句：The barge transported goods along the canal. 这艘驳船沿着运河运送货物。

例句：He barged into the room without knocking. 他没敲门就闯进了房间。|近义词：(n.) boat, lighter, (v.) bump, shove, push, interrupt |反义词:(v.) tiptoe, sneak|词性: noun, verb
barges: /ˈbɑːrdʒɪz/ |n. |驳船（barge的复数形式）|Plural form of barge, referring to multiple flat-bottomed boats for carrying freight.|例句：Several barges were tied up along the riverbank. 几艘驳船系在河岸边。

例句：The barges carried heavy loads of timber. 这些驳船载着沉重的木材。|近义词：boats, lighters|反义词: N/A |词性: noun
