chimneying:/ˈtʃɪmniɪŋ/| n. vi.|烟囱效应；攀岩技术；（身体）紧贴狭缝向上移动|The act of climbing a narrow chimney-like passage, especially in rock climbing, using pressure with the back and feet against opposing walls.  It also refers to the upward movement of air or other gases through a narrow space due to temperature differences.|He used a chimneying technique to ascend the narrow crevice. 他运用烟囱攀爬技术登上了狭窄的裂缝。

The smoke was chimneying up the flue. 烟正沿着烟道向上升腾。|近义词：stemming, bridging|反义词:descending,rappelling|词性: noun, intransitive verb

Etymology: From "chimney" + "-ing" suffix, denoting an action or process.  The climbing sense originated in the late 19th century, referring to the resemblance of the climbing technique to ascending a chimney.
