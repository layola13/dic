inert:/ɪˈnɜːrt/ (US) /ɪˈnɜːt/ (UK)| adj.|惰性的；迟钝的；无活动的|Lacking the ability or strength to move or act; chemically inactive.|例句：The inert gas argon is used in welding. 惰性气体氩气用于焊接。|例句：He lay inert on the floor. 他一动不动地躺在地板上。|近义词：inactive, passive, sluggish, dormant|反义词：active, lively, dynamic|词性：形容词

inertness:/ɪˈnɜːrtnɪs/ (US) /ɪˈnɜːtnəs/ (UK)| n.|惰性；迟钝；不活动|The state of being inert.|例句：The inertness of the gas makes it safe to use. 这种气体的惰性使其使用安全。|例句：His inertness worried his friends. 他的迟钝让他的朋友们担心。|近义词：inactivity, passivity, sluggishness, dormancy|反义词：activity, liveliness, dynamism|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *iners*, meaning "unskilled, inactive," from *in-* (not) + *ars* (skill, art).
