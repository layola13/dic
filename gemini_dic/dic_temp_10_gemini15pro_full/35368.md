co-labourer:/kəʊˈleɪbərər/
n.
合作者，共同工作者
A person who works together with another person or group.
例句：She found a co-labourer in her husband for her charitable work. 她找到了丈夫作为她慈善事业的合作者。

例句：The project was a success thanks to the dedication of all the co-labourers.  由于所有合作者的奉献，该项目取得了成功。

近义词：collaborator, partner, associate, colleague
反义词：opponent, adversary, competitor
词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From co- (together) + labourer (worker).  The prefix "co-" comes from the Latin "com-" meaning "together" or "with." "Labourer" comes from the Old French "labourer," ultimately from the Latin "laborare" meaning "to work."
