antiutilitarianism:/ˌæntijuːˌtɪlɪˈtɛəriənɪzəm/
|n.|反功利主义|A philosophy that opposes utilitarianism, the belief that actions are right if they are useful or for the benefit of a majority.|Antiutilitarianism emphasizes individual rights and duties, even if they do not maximize overall happiness.|Many philosophers argue for antiutilitarianism because they believe utilitarianism can justify morally reprehensible actions.|近义词:deontology, moral absolutism|反义词:utilitarianism|词性:名词

antiutilitarian:/ˌæntijuːˌtɪlɪˈtɛəriən/
|adj.|反功利主义的|Opposing or rejecting the principles of utilitarianism.|His antiutilitarian stance led him to believe that some actions are inherently wrong, regardless of their consequences.|The antiutilitarian argument focuses on the importance of individual rights.|近义词:deontological|反义词:utilitarian|词性:形容词
