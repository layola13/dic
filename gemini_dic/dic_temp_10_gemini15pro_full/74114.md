foul-balls:/ˈfaʊl bɔːlz/
n.
界外球
A baseball hit outside the lines of fair territory.
The batter swung and hit a foul ball.  击球手挥棒击中了一个界外球。

foul-balls:/ˈfaʊl bɔːlz/
n.
犯规球（其他球类运动）
A ball hit outside the designated playing area, resulting in a penalty or point for the opposing team. (Can be used in other sports like tennis or volleyball)
He served a foul ball, giving the point to the opponent. 他发了一个犯规球，把分给了对手。


foul-ball:/ˈfaʊl bɔːl/
n.
糟糕的情况；不好的事情 (informal)
A bad or unfortunate situation. (Informal usage)
Losing all my money was a real foul ball.  把我所有的钱都输光了真是件糟糕的事。



ETYMOLOGY->
"foul" (adj.) from Old English *ful, from Proto-Germanic *fulaz "rotten, putrid, foul," from PIE root *pu- (2) "to rot, to stink."
"ball" (n.) from Old Norse bollr "ball," from Proto-Germanic *balluz, from PIE root *bhel- (2) "to swell, blow."

SYNONYMS->
(For baseball) out of play, illegal hit
(For informal usage) mishap, misfortune, blunder

ANTONYMS->
