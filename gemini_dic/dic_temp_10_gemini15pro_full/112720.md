loyalist:/ˈlɔɪəlɪst/| n.|忠诚者，保皇党|A person who remains loyal to a political cause, party, or government, especially during a time of revolt or change. |例句：The loyalists refused to join the rebellion.  这些忠诚者拒绝加入叛乱。

例句：During the American Revolution, loyalists supported the British crown. 在美国独立战争期间，效忠者支持英国王室。|近义词：patriot (when referring to one's own cause), adherent, supporter, devotee |反义词:rebel, traitor, dissident|词性:名词

词源: From "loyal" + "-ist."  "Loyal" comes from Old French "loial," ultimately from Latin "legalis" (lawful).
