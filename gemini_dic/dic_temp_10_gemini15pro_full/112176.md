looking-on: /ˈlʊkɪŋ ˈɒn/ (British) /ˈlʊkɪŋ ˈɑːn/ (American) | adj. |旁观的，观看的 |  Present participle of "look on," meaning to watch something happening without taking part. |  Example: There were many looking-on as the firefighters battled the blaze.  许多人旁观消防员与大火搏斗。 |  Example: He was merely a looking-on observer, not a participant in the event. 他只是一个旁观的观察者，而不是事件的参与者。| Synonyms: observing, watching, spectating | Antonyms: participating, involved, engaged |  Verb (present participle)

looking-on: /ˈlʊkɪŋ ˈɒn/ (British) /ˈlʊkɪŋ ˈɑːn/ (American) | n. |旁观者 |  Those who are looking on; spectators. This usage is less common than the adjectival form. | Example: The looking-on gasped as the acrobat fell. 旁观者看到杂技演员摔倒时倒吸了一口气。 | Example:  A crowd of looking-on gathered at the scene of the accident. 一群旁观者聚集在事故现场。 | Synonyms: onlookers, spectators, observers | Antonyms: participants, players | Noun
