condyle:/ˈkɒndɪl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːndɪl/ (US)| n.|髁; 骨节|A rounded protuberance at the end of a bone, usually forming an articulation with another bone.|The distal condyle of the femur articulates with the tibia. 股骨远端髁与胫骨相连。|Example 1:  Damage to the condyle can cause significant pain and limit mobility.  髁的损伤会导致剧烈的疼痛并限制活动能力。

Example 2: The surgeon carefully repaired the fractured condyle.  外科医生仔细地修复了骨折的髁。|Synonyms:  protuberance, knob, process|Antonyms:  depression, fossa|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *condylus, from Greek κόνδυλος (kondylos) "knuckle, knob,"  probably related to the base of  κόνδαξ (kondax), "a type of grain."
