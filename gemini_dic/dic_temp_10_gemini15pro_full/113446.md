lynching: /ˈlɪntʃɪŋ/
| n. |私刑处死（尤指绞死）|The act of killing someone without legal trial, especially by hanging, often by a mob. |The lynching of innocent people is a horrific crime. 对无辜人民的私刑是一种可怕的罪行。|近义词：execution, murder|反义词: pardon, acquittal|词性: 名词 (singular)


词源：
From Lynch's Law, named after Charles Lynch (1736–1796), a Virginia planter and justice of the peace, who headed an extralegal court during the American Revolution.  However, the term predates him and may be derived from a similar term used in the 17th century.
