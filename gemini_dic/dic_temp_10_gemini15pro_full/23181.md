bowdlerising:/ˈbaʊdləˌraɪzɪŋ/| n.|弓勒化；删节；净化|The bowdlerising of the play removed all the potentially offensive language.  这出戏的净化删除了所有可能具有冒犯性的语言。|例句：The bowdlerising of the classic novel made it suitable for children.  将这部经典小说净化后，它适合儿童阅读。

例句：Modern audiences might find the bowdlerising of older works to be unnecessary.  现代观众可能会觉得对旧作品的删节没有必要。|近义词：censoring, expurgating, sanitising|反义词：unabridged, unexpurgated|词性:noun (gerund or present participle)

bowdlerise:/ˈbaʊdləˌraɪz/| vt.|弓勒化；删节；净化|They bowdlerised the text to remove any references to sex or violence. 他们删改了文本，删除了所有关于性和暴力的内容。|例句：The publisher bowdlerised the author's manuscript, much to his dismay.  出版商删改了作者的手稿，这让他非常沮丧。

例句：Some parents choose to bowdlerise fairy tales before reading them to their children.  一些家长选择在给孩子读童话故事之前先进行删节。|近义词：censor, expurgate, sanitise|反义词：preserve, retain|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Thomas Bowdler (1754–1825), a British physician who published an expurgated edition of Shakespeare's works in 1818.
