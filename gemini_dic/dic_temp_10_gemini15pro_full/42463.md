counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/ | adj. n. | 反事实的，反事实的陈述 | relating to or expressing what has not happened or is not the case | Historians sometimes create counterfactual scenarios to explore what might have been. | 历史学家有时会创造反事实的场景来探索可能发生的事情。 |  hypothetical, speculative, conjectural | factual, real, actual | adjective, noun
counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/ |  n. | 反事实的陈述，反事实假设 | a statement that expresses what has not happened or is not the case |  He proposed a counterfactual: "What if the South had won the Civil War?" | 他提出了一个反事实的假设：“如果南方赢得了内战会怎样？” | hypothesis, speculation, conjecture | fact, reality, truth | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- (against, opposite) + factual (based on facts).  The word began to be used more frequently in the mid-20th century with the development of counterfactual history and philosophy.
