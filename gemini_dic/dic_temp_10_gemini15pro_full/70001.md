fetes:/feɪts/ (UK) /feɪts/ (US)| n. |节日，庆祝活动；盛宴|A large organized celebration, often held outdoors.  A public function, typically held outdoors and offering entertainment and refreshments.| The town held fetes to celebrate the harvest.  小镇举行庆祝丰收的节日。|synonyms: festivals, celebrations, carnivals, galas|antonyms: wake, funeral|noun


fetes:/feɪts/ (UK) /feɪts/ (US)| v. |宴请；款待|To entertain someone lavishly; to celebrate or honor something.|He feted his guests with a magnificent banquet. 他以盛大的宴会款待他的客人。|synonyms: entertain, regale, wine and dine|antonyms: neglect, ignore|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "feste" meaning "festival, feast," deriving from Latin "festa" (plural of "festum"), meaning "holiday, feast day." Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*dhes-" meaning "to do, perform (a religious rite)."
