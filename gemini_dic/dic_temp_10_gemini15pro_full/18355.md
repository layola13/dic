bellows:/ˈbeləʊz/ (UK) /ˈbɛloʊz/ (US)| n. |风箱；波纹管；(相机等的)褶腔；肺，呼吸|a device that produces a stream of air when its sides are pressed together, used for blowing air into a fire, etc.  A flexible expandable tube, such as the part of some cameras that allows the lens to move in and out.  (Informal) Lungs.|The blacksmith worked the bellows to make the fire hotter. 铁匠拉动风箱使火更旺。

The bellows of the old camera were damaged. 老式相机的褶腔损坏了.

He took a deep breath, filling his bellows with fresh air. 他深深地吸了一口气，让新鲜空气充满肺部。|近义词：air pump, blower, ventilator|反义词: -(not commonly used)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bylġes (plural), from Proto-Germanic *bulgijō (“bag, bellows”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰelǵʰ- (“to swell”). Cognate with Dutch balg (“bellows, bag”), German Balg (“bellows, bag, brat”), Icelandic belgur (“bellows, skin”).
