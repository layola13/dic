panopticon:/ˈpænɒptɪkɒn/ (UK) /pəˈnɑːptɪkɑːn/ (US)| n.|圆形监狱；全景敞视监狱|A circular prison with cells arranged around a central well, from which prisoners could at all times be observed.|The architect designed a panopticon prison where every inmate could be observed from a central watchtower.|Synonyms:  all-seeing, surveillance state|Antonyms:  unobserved, private, hidden|noun

panopticon:|/ˈpænɒptɪkɒn/ (UK) /pəˈnɑːptɪkɑːn/ (US)| adj.|全景敞视的|Descriptive of a system of control where individuals are aware of the possibility of being observed but do not know when they are actually being observed, leading to self-regulation of behavior.|The panopticon design has been applied metaphorically to various social structures, including schools and workplaces.|Synonyms:  observational, surveillant|Antonyms:  unobserved, private, hidden|adjective
