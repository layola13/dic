conservatism: /kənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/ | n. |保守主义|  A political philosophy based on tradition and social stability, stressing established institutions, and preferring gradual rather than abrupt change. |  Example:  Her conservatism prevented her from supporting the radical new policy. 她的保守主义使她不支持激进的新政策。

Example:  The party's platform reflected a strong vein of fiscal conservatism.  该党的纲领反映了强烈的财政保守主义倾向。 | Synonym: traditionalism, orthodoxy | Antonym: radicalism, progressivism | noun


conservatisms: /kənˈsɜːrvətɪzəmz/ | n. |保守主义（复数）| Different forms or instances of conservatism.  This plural form is less common but can be used to refer to various expressions or types of conservatism. | Example: The conservatisms of the various European countries differ significantly. 各个欧洲国家的保守主义差异很大。


Example:  The book explored the conservatisms of different religious groups. 这本书探讨了不同宗教团体的保守主义。 | Synonym: traditionalisms, orthodoxies | Antonym: radicalisms, progressivisms | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *conservatisme*, from *conserver* "to preserve," from Latin *conservare* "to keep, preserve, maintain," from *com-* "together" (see com-) + *servare* "to keep, guard" (see observe).
