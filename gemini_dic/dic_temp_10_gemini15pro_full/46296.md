D-Day: /ˈdiː deɪ/
noun
诺曼底登陆日；关键时刻，决战日
The day (June 6, 1944) in World War II on which Allied forces invaded northern France by means of beach landings in Normandy.  Also used to refer to any day scheduled for the start of a military operation.
The general told the troops that D-Day was approaching. 将军告诉部队，决战日即将来临。

D-Day: /ˈdiː deɪ/
noun
关键时刻，决战日
A day scheduled for the start of a military operation or other important undertaking.
The product launch is D-Day for our company. 产品发布是我们公司的关键时刻。


近义词: deadline, zero hour, the day of reckoning
反义词: None (D-Day refers to a specific point in time, not a contrasting concept)
词性: noun
