machiavellians:/ˌmækiəˈveliənz/
| n.| 马基雅维利主义者; 权谋家; 诡计多端的人 |People who are cunning, amoral, and manipulative, often in pursuit of power.  Those who follow the principles of Niccolò Machiavelli, especially advocating expediency and deception in political affairs. | Many politicians are accused of being machiavellians, prioritizing power above all else. 许多政客被指责是马基雅维利主义者，将权力置于一切之上。| synonyms: schemers, manipulators, intriguers | antonyms: idealists, moralists, altruists | noun

machiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/
| adj.| 马基雅维利主义的; 权谋的; 诡计多端的 |Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  Suggesting the principles of Niccolò Machiavelli. | His machiavellian tactics ensured he won the election, even though he lost the respect of many. 他马基雅维利式的策略确保他赢得了选举，尽管他失去了很多人的尊重。| synonyms: cunning, devious, unscrupulous | antonyms: honest, ethical, principled | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli (1469-1527), an Italian Renaissance political philosopher and author of *The Prince*. His writings, which advocated pragmatism and even deception in political leadership, gave rise to the term "Machiavellianism."
