hyperinflation:/ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪʃən/ | n. |恶性通货膨胀|A period of rapid and excessive price increases in an economy, typically at a rate exceeding 50% per month.|例句：The country experienced a period of hyperinflation after the war. 战后该国经历了一段恶性通货膨胀时期。

例句：Hyperinflation can wipe out savings and destabilize an economy. 恶性通货膨胀会使储蓄化为乌有，并破坏经济的稳定。|近义词：runaway inflation, galloping inflation|反义词:deflation, disinflation|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- +‎ inflation, from Latin inflātiō (“a blowing up, inflation”)
