embellishers: /ɪmˈbɛlɪʃərz/ | n. | 装饰物；润色者；修饰语 | People or things that embellish something. Those who add details, often fictitious or exaggerated, to make something more interesting or attractive. | The storytellers were natural embellishers, never letting the facts get in the way of a good tale. 讲故事的人天生就是润色者，从来不让事实妨碍一个好故事。| Near synonyms: decorators, adorners, ornamenters | Antonyms: simplifiers, minimalists | Noun

embellisher: /ɪmˈbɛlɪʃər/ | n. | 装饰物；润色者；修饰语 | A person or thing that embellishes something.  |  A skillful embellisher can transform a simple dress into a stunning creation. 一个熟练的装饰者可以把一件简单的连衣裙变成一件令人惊艳的作品。 | Near synonyms: decorator, adorner, ornamenter | Antonyms: simplifier, minimalist | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *embellisshen*, from Old French *embellir*, from *en-* (in, on) + *bel-* (beautiful) related to Latin *bellus*.
