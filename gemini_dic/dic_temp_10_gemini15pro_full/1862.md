acrook:/əˈkrʊk/| n.|骗子，坏蛋|A dishonest person, especially a criminal.|The police caught the acrook who had been robbing local businesses. 警察抓住了那个一直在抢劫当地商店的骗子。|Synonyms: rogue, scoundrel, villain, criminal, lawbreaker|Antonyms: honest person, law-abiding citizen|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *acroken*, meaning "bent," "crooked," from Old English *ācrōcian*, "to bend."  The semantic shift from "bent" to "dishonest" likely occurred through association with the idea of something morally twisted or not straight.
