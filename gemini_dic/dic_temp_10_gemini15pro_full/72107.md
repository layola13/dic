ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *flokken, from Old English *floccan, frequentative of *flōcan (“to fly”), from Proto-Germanic *flaugjaną (“to fly”), from Proto-Indo-European *plewk- (“to flow, float, fly”). Cognate with Scots flok (“flock”), West Frisian flokje (“to flock together”), Dutch vlokken (“to flock, flake”), German flocken (“to flake, flock”).
