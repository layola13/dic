impostor:/ɪmˈpɑːstər/ (US), /ɪmˈpɒstə(r)/ (UK)| n. |冒名顶替者，骗子|A person who pretends to be someone else in order to deceive others. |例句：He was exposed as an impostor, pretending to be a qualified doctor.  他被揭穿是一个冒充合格医生的骗子。

例句：The impostor tricked the elderly woman out of her life savings.  这个骗子骗走了老妇人的毕生积蓄。

近义词：fraud, charlatan, fake, pretender|反义词：genuine person, the real McCoy|词性：名词

impostors:/ɪmˈpɑːstərz/ (US), /ɪmˈpɒstəz/ (UK)| n. |冒名顶替者（复数）|Plural form of "impostor." |例句：The impostors were eventually caught and brought to justice.  这些冒名顶替者最终被抓获并绳之以法。

例句：Beware of impostors posing as charity workers.  小心冒充慈善工作者的骗子。

近义词：frauds, charlatans, fakes, pretenders|反义词：genuine people|词性：名词

词源：From Old French *impostor*, from Latin *impostor* "deceiver," from *impositus*, past participle of *imponere* "to impose, place upon."
