inkhorn:/ˈɪŋkhɔːrn/| n. |墨水瓶；(旧)学究气的语言；矫揉造作的文风|A small container for ink, typically made of horn or metal.  Figuratively, it refers to an overly elaborate or pretentious style of writing. |He dipped his quill into the inkhorn. 他把羽毛笔浸入墨水瓶。|Synonyms: inkpot, inkwell (literal); pedantic, pompous, affected (figurative)|Antonyms: natural, simple, unaffected (figurative)|noun


inkhorn:/ˈɪŋkhɔːrn/| adj. |学究气的；矫揉造作的|Characterized by an overly elaborate or pretentious style of writing. |Her inkhorn prose was difficult to understand. 她那学究气的散文很难理解。|Synonyms: pedantic, pompous, affected, pretentious|Antonyms: natural, simple, unaffected, unpretentious|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *inkhorne*, from Old English *inc-horn*  ("ink container made of horn"), composed of *inc* ("ink") + *horn* ("horn").
