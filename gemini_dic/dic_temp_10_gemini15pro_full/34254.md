clapped:/klæpt/| v.|拍手，鼓掌|The audience clapped enthusiastically after the performance.  观众在演出结束后热情地鼓掌。|近义词：applauded, cheered|反义词:booed|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clappen*, from Old English *clappan* (“to clap, beat, strike, palpitate”), from Proto-Germanic *klapōną* (“to clap”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱlep- (“to make a sharp noise; to clap, flap”)*. Cognate with West Frisian *klappe* (“to clap”), Dutch *klappen* (“to clap, chat, bang”), German *klappen* (“to clap, flap, work (out), succeed”), Danish *klappe* (“to pat, stroke, clap”), Swedish *klappa* (“to pat, caress, clap”).
