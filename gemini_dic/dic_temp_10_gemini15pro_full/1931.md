actinometry: /ˌæktɪˈnɒmɪtri/
noun
光化學測量法; 光能測量; 光度測定法
The measurement of the intensity of electromagnetic radiation, especially of the sun's rays.
Actinometry is used to measure the heating power of radiation. 光化學測量法用於測量輻射的加熱功率。

Scientists use actinometry to study the sun's energy output. 科學家用光化學測量法來研究太陽的能量輸出。

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *actinométrie*, from Ancient Greek *aktís* (ἀκτίς) "ray" + *-metria* (-μετρία) "-metry" (measurement).
