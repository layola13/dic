hyperconservative:/ˌhaɪpərˈkənˈsɜːrvətɪv/ | adj. |极端保守的|Extremely conservative; rigidly adhering to traditional values and practices, often resistant to change.|例句：His hyperconservative views on social issues alienated many voters. 他在社会问题上的极端保守观点疏远了许多选民。

例句：The hyperconservative faction within the party blocked any attempts at modernization. 党内极端保守派阻挠了任何现代化尝试。|近义词：ultraconservative, reactionary, right-wing|反义词：liberal, progressive, radical|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From hyper- + conservative.  "Hyper-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "beyond," derived from the Greek "hyper" (ὑπέρ), meaning "over," "above," or "beyond."  "Conservative" comes from the Latin "conservare," meaning "to preserve" or "to keep."
