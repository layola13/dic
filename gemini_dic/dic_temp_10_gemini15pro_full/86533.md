ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English holden, from Old English healdan (to hold, keep, guard, observe, maintain, continue, rule, govern, possess, occupy, celebrate, solemnize), from Proto-Germanic *haldaną (to hold, keep, tend, guard, pasture, contain, observe, perform, cultivate, celebrate), from Proto-Indo-European *kelh₁- (to hold, contain, protect, save). Cognate with Old Saxon haldan (to hold), Old Frisian halda (to hold, keep, feed, maintain), Dutch houden (to hold, keep, maintain, consider, think, estimate, deem, judge, stop, detain), German halten (to hold, stop, keep, consider, think), Old Norse halda (to hold, keep, observe, celebrate, rule), Gothic haldan (to tend, pasture, contain).
