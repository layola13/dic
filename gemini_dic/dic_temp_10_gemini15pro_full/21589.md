bluffs:/blʌfs/| n.| 悬崖，绝壁；虚张声势；欺骗|A steep, high bank beside a body of water or a plain.  Something said or done to deceive someone into thinking that one is going to do something that one does not really intend to do.|We hiked along the bluffs overlooking the ocean. 我们沿着可以俯瞰大海的悬崖徒步旅行。|近义词：cliffs, slopes, embankments, pretense, deception|反义词:truth, honesty|词性:名词

bluffs:/blʌfs/| v.|虚张声势，吓唬|To deceive someone by pretending to be stronger or more determined than one really is.|He bluffed his way through the interview, even though he knew very little about the subject. 即使他对这个话题知之甚少，他还是通过虚张声势蒙混过了面试。|近义词：feign, pretend, deceive|反义词:be honest, tell the truth|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From probably Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian *bluff* (“steep”), Swedish *bluff* (“bare, exposed”).  The verb sense likely developed from the idea of a ship sailing close to a bluff, appearing to be in danger but actually safe.
