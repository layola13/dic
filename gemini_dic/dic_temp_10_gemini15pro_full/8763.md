antidotary:/ˈæn.tɪˌdoʊ.tər.i/ /ˈæn.tɪˌdɒt.ər.i/
noun
解毒剂；解毒药；解毒手册
a book describing antidotes or remedies for poisons.  A collection of antidotes.
The physician consulted his antidotary to find the correct remedy for the poison.  医生查阅了他的解毒手册，以找到正确的解毒剂。
Synonyms: antidote, remedy, cure, counterpoison
Antonyms: poison, toxin, venom
n.

antidotary:/ˈæn.tɪˌdoʊ.tər.i/ /ˈæn.tɪˌdɒt.ər.i/
noun
解毒剂；解毒药；解毒手册
Having properties of an antidote. Serving to counteract.
The herbalist prepared an antidotary potion to combat the effects of the poisonous plant. 草药师调制了一种解毒药水来对抗有毒植物的影响。
Synonyms: antidotal, remedial, curative
Antonyms: poisonous, toxic, venomous
adj.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *antidotarius, from Latin antidotum (“antidote”) + -arius (suffix forming nouns denoting things or persons connected with what is denoted by the base).  The Latin antidotum is from Ancient Greek ἀντίδοτον (antídoto, “remedy given against poison”), from ἀντί (antí, “against”) + δίδωμι (dídōmi, “I give”).
