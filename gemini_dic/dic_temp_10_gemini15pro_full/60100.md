eerier:/ˈɪəriər (US), ˈɪərɪər (UK)| adj.|更怪诞的，更可怕的|Comparative form of eerie: more strange and frightening.|The forest grew eerier as the sun began to set.  随着太阳开始落山，森林变得更加怪诞。| The abandoned house had an eerier feel at night.  到了晚上，那栋废弃的房子感觉更阴森了。|近义词：creepier, spookier, more unsettling|反义词：less eerie, more normal, more comforting|词性：形容词
词源：From Middle English *ēri, from Old English *earg, from Proto-Germanic *argaz (“cowardly, timid”). Cognate with Scots eerie (“producing superstitious fear”), Dutch erg (“bad, awful”). More at arg.
