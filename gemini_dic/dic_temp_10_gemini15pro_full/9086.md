antimodernists:/ˌæntiˈmɒdənɪsts/ | n. | 反现代主义者 |  People who oppose or resist modernism, often favoring traditional values and practices. |  The antimodernists criticized the new architecture, preferring the classical styles. 反现代主义者批评了新的建筑风格，更喜欢古典风格。| The antimodernists longed for a simpler time. 反现代主义者渴望更简单的时代。 |  traditionalists, conservatives | modernists, progressives | noun

antimodernist:/ˌæntiˈmɒdənɪst/ | adj. | 反现代主义的 | Opposing or resisting modernism. | The antimodernist movement gained traction in the early 20th century. 反现代主义运动在20世纪初获得了吸引力。 |  He held an antimodernist view of art. 他对艺术持反现代主义的观点。 | traditional, conservative | modern, progressive | adjective

antimodernism:/ˌæntiˈmɒdərnɪzəm/ | n. | 反现代主义 |  A philosophical movement that opposes or resists modernism, often characterized by a preference for traditional values and practices. | Antimodernism often emphasizes the importance of community and religion. 反现代主义通常强调社区和宗教的重要性。 | The rise of antimodernism was a reaction to the rapid changes of the modern world. 反现代主义的兴起是对现代世界快速变化的一种反应。 | traditionalism, conservatism | modernism, progressivism | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix): from Greek anti "against, opposite"
modern (adjective): from Old French moderne, from Late Latin modernus "of the present mode or fashion," from Latin modo "just now," ablative of modus "measure, manner"
-ist (suffix): from Greek -istes, denoting an adherent of a doctrine or system.
