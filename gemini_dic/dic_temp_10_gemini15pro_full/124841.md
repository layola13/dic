muckraking: /ˈmʌkˌreɪkɪŋ/ | n. | 揭发丑闻；揭黑；扒粪 | the action of searching out and publicizing scandal about famous people |  The journalist was known for his muckraking, exposing corruption in the government. 这位记者以揭露政府腐败而闻名。 | synonyms: investigative journalism, exposing wrongdoing,  | antonyms:  cover-up, whitewashing | noun

muckrake: /ˈmʌkˌreɪk/ | v. | 揭发丑闻；揭黑；扒粪 | to search out and publicize scandal about famous people | The reporters muckraked the politician's past, revealing a history of bribery. 记者们揭发了这位政客过去的丑闻，揭露了他受贿的历史。 | synonyms: expose, unearth, unveil | antonyms: conceal, bury, whitewash | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "muck rake," a rake for scraping up manure.  President Theodore Roosevelt used the term in a 1906 speech to criticize journalists who focused on the negative aspects of American life.  While he acknowledged the need for investigative journalism, he felt some journalists went too far, dwelling excessively on "muck."
