grumblier:/ˈɡrʌmbliər/ (US), /ˈɡrʌmbliə/ (UK)| adj.|更爱抱怨的，脾气更坏的|Comparative form of "grumpy," meaning more inclined to complain or express dissatisfaction; more bad-tempered.|He seemed grumblier than usual this morning. 他今天早上似乎比平时更爱抱怨。|The grumblier the customer, the more patient the staff needed to be.  顾客越抱怨，工作人员就越需要耐心。|Synonyms: more irritable, more cantankerous, more churlish, more peevish|Antonyms: more cheerful, more pleasant, more agreeable|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "grumble" + "-ier" (comparative suffix). "Grumble" originated in the late 16th century, likely imitative of a low, rumbling sound of complaint.
