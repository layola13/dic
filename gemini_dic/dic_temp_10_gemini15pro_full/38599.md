concreate:/ˈkɒŋkriːt/ /kɑːnˈkriːt/ | v. | 使凝固；使具体化 | To solidify or make concrete; to form or create something from disparate parts. |  The artist concreted his vision in a stunning sculpture. 艺术家将他的愿景具体化成了一座令人惊叹的雕塑。

She concreted her ideas in a well-organized proposal. 她将她的想法具体化成了一份组织良好的提案。 | 近义词：solidify, consolidate, materialize, reify | 反义词：abstract, dissolve, dissipate | 词性: 动词


concreate:/ˈkɒŋkriːt/ /kɑːnˈkriːt/ | adj. | 具体的；实在的；凝固的| Existing in a material or physical form; not abstract; solid. |  The architect presented concrete plans for the new building. 建筑师提出了新建筑的具体计划。

The company needed concrete evidence of fraud before taking action. 公司在采取行动之前需要具体的欺诈证据。 | 近义词：tangible, real, solid, substantial, definite | 反义词：abstract, theoretical, intangible, hypothetical | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin concretus, past participle of concrescere (“to grow together”), from con- (“together”) + crescere (“to grow”).
