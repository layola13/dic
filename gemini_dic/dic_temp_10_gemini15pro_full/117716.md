melancholiness:/ˈmɛləŋkɒlɪnɪs/ (US) /ˈmɛləŋkɒlɪnəs/ (UK)| n.|忧郁，伤感，悲哀|A feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause.|例句：A sense of melancholiess pervaded the room.  房间里弥漫着一种忧郁的气氛。

例句：She couldn't shake off the melancholiess that had settled over her. 她无法摆脱笼罩着她的忧郁。|近义词：sadness, dejection, gloom, depression|反义词：happiness, joy, cheerfulness|词性：名词

Etymology:
From Middle French *mélancolie* (modern French *mélancolie*), from Late Latin *melancholia*, from Ancient Greek *μελαγχολία (melankholía)*, from *μέλας (mélas, “black, dark”) + χολή (kholḗ, “bile”)*.  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholia.
