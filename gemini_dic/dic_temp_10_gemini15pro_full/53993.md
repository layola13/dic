discriminant:/dɪˈskrɪmɪnənt/ | n. |判别式，鉴别式 |A function of the coefficients of a polynomial equation whose value gives information about the roots of the polynomial. |The discriminant of the quadratic equation ax² + bx + c = 0 is b² - 4ac. 二次方程 ax² + bx + c = 0 的判别式是 b² - 4ac。| synonym:determinant | antonym: N/A | noun

discriminating:/dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ/ | adj. |有识别力的，有鉴赏力的，敏锐的 |Having or showing refined taste or good judgment. |She has a discriminating palate for wine. 她对葡萄酒有敏锐的鉴赏力。| synonym: discerning, selective, astute | antonym: indiscriminate, undiscriminating | adjective

discriminate:/dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪt/ | v. |歧视，区别，辨别 |1. To make an unjust or prejudicial distinction in the treatment of different categories of people or things, especially on the grounds of race, sex, or age. 2. Recognize a distinction; differentiate. |1. It is illegal to discriminate against someone based on their race.  根据种族歧视某人是非法的。 2.  It's difficult to discriminate between the two shades of blue.  很难区分这两种蓝色。| synonym: differentiate, distinguish | antonym: confuse, confound | verb
