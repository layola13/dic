copartnerships: /ˌkoʊˈpɑːrtnərˌʃɪps/
n.
合伙企业，共同合伙关系
A copartnership is a business organization in which two or more individuals pool their resources and expertise to carry out a business venture, sharing profits and losses.
Example:  The two friends formed a copartnership to start their own bakery.  这两个朋友合伙开了一家面包店。

Example:  The copartnership dissolved after a year due to disagreements between the partners.  由于合伙人之间的分歧，合伙企业一年后解散了。
近义词: partnership, joint venture, alliance
反义词: sole proprietorship, corporation
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From co- + partnership.  "Co-" is a prefix meaning "together" or "jointly." "Partnership" comes from the Old French "partenarie" meaning "the sharing of plunder."
