beg-the-question:/ˌbeɡ ðə ˈkwɛstʃən/ | v. | 以未经证实的假设作为论据;  (错误地)认为某事不言自明 | To assume the conclusion of an argument—a type of circular reasoning. This is an informal fallacy where the conclusion that one is attempting to prove is included in the initial premises of an argument, often in an indirect way that conceals this fact. |  The politician begged the question when he said that because the educational system is broken, we need school choice. |  circular argument, circular reasoning, assume the conclusion, petitio principii |  n/a | verb

beg-the-question (informal use): /ˌbeɡ ðə ˈkwɛstʃən/ | v. | 引出一个问题;  使人不禁要问 | To raise a question or point; to invite an obvious question. |  Her sudden departure begs the question of where she's going. |  raise the question, prompt the question, suggest the question, invite the question |  n/a | verb
