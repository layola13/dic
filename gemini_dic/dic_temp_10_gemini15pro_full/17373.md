beadle:/ˈbiːdl/| n.|教区执事；小吏；门房|A beadle is a minor parish official who performs various duties, such as ushering or keeping order.|The beadle led the procession into the church. 教区执事带领队伍进入教堂。|The school beadle kept a watchful eye on the students. 学校的门房密切注视着学生们。|Synonyms: usher, attendant, custodian, janitor, doorkeeper|Antonyms: principal, headmaster, director|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English bydel "herald, messenger, crier, proclaimer," from Proto-Germanic *budilaz "messenger," from PIE root *bhedh- "to dig, pierce, prick," also forming words for "bid, ask, command." The sense shifted from "messenger" to "minor official."
