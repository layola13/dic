blockbuster: /ˈblɒkˌbʌstər/ (UK) /ˈblɑːkˌbʌstər/ (US)| n.| 大片，轰动一时的事物 |A film, book, or other product that is a great commercial success.| The new superhero movie is expected to be a summer blockbuster.  这部新的超级英雄电影预计会成为夏季大片。| synonyms: hit, smash, triumph | antonyms: flop, failure | noun

blockbuster: /ˈblɒkˌbʌstər/ (UK) /ˈblɑːkˌbʌstər/ (US)| adj.| 轰动性的，非常成功的 |Extremely successful or popular.|  The company launched a blockbuster advertising campaign. 该公司发起了一场轰动性的广告宣传活动。| synonyms: sensational, phenomenal, outstanding | antonyms: unsuccessful, insignificant | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
The word "blockbuster" originally referred to a large bomb capable of destroying an entire city block during World War II.  This destructive power was then metaphorically applied to films, books, and other products that achieved massive commercial success, "blowing away" the competition.
