dauntingness:/ˈdɔːntɪŋnəs/ | n. | 令人生畏; 艰巨性; 令人气馁 | The dauntingness of the task ahead made him hesitate.  |  The sheer scale of the project filled him with a sense of dauntingness. | 近义词: formidability, arduousness, difficulty | 反义词: easiness, simplicity, facility | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From "daunting" + "-ness". "Daunting" comes from the Old French word "danter" meaning "to tame or subdue," which in turn comes from Latin "domitare."  The suffix "-ness" is used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
