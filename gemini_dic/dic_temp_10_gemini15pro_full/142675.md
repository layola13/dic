ostrogoths:/ˈɒstrəɡɒθs/ /ˈɑːstrəɡɑːθs/| n. |东哥特人|An East Germanic people who established a kingdom in Italy in the 5th century AD.  They were originally part of the Goths who migrated from Scandinavia to the Black Sea region.  After the fall of the Western Roman Empire, they conquered Italy under Theodoric the Great.|The Ostrogoths, under Theodoric, ruled Italy for several decades.  东哥特人在狄奥多里克的统治下统治了意大利几十年。

The Ostrogoths' reign in Italy ended with the Gothic War. 东哥特人在意大利的统治以哥特战争而告终。|近义词：Goths, Visigoths (related, but distinct groups)|反义词:Romans (often in conflict with)|词性:noun
