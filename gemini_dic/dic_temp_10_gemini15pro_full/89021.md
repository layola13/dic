hollyhock:/ˈhɑːliˌhɑːk/ | n. |蜀葵；一丈红|A tall plant native to China, with large, brightly coloured flowers. |例句：The hollyhocks grew tall against the wall. 蜀葵沿着墙长得老高。

例句：She planted hollyhocks along the back fence. 她沿着后篱笆种了蜀葵。


近义词：alcea (genus name) |反义词:  (No direct antonym for a plant) |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English holihoc, from Old English holihoc, a compound of hālig "holy" and hoc "mallow."  The "holy" part is believed to refer to the plant's reputed use in treating illnesses, and the plant may have been brought back from the Holy Land during the Crusades.
