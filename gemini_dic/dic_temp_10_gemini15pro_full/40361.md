contractive:/kənˈtræktɪv/| adj.|收缩的，紧缩的，|Tending to contract or make smaller; causing contraction.  Relating to or involving a contraction. |The contractive force of the muscles pulled the bones together. 肌肉的收缩力把骨头拉到了一起。|The economy experienced a contractive phase. 经济经历了一个紧缩阶段。|Synonyms: constricting, shrinking, tightening, compressing|Antonyms: expansive, dilating, expanding, stretching|adjective


contractile:/kənˈtræktaɪl/| adj.|可收缩的，能收缩的，|Having the ability to contract or become smaller. Usually used to describe muscles or other tissues that can shorten. |Muscle tissue is contractile. 肌肉组织是可收缩的。|The contractile vacuole helps regulate the water content of the cell. 收缩泡有助于调节细胞的含水量。|Synonyms: contractive, retractile|Antonyms: inextensible, noncontractile|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contractivus, from Latin contractus (past participle of contrahere “to draw together, contract”), from com- (“together”) + trahere (“to draw”).
