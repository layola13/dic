archly:/ˈɑːrtʃli/ | adv. | 顽皮地，淘气地，狡黠地 | In a playful or mischievous manner, often suggesting sly humor or knowingness. | She smiled archly and said, "I know something you don't." 她顽皮地笑着说：“我知道一些你不知道的事情。” |  例句：He raised an eyebrow archly. 他淘气地挑起眉毛。| 近义词：mischievously, playfully, teasingly, slyly | 反义词：seriously, sincerely, innocently | 词性：副词

archly:/ˈɑːrtʃli/ | adv. | 狡猾地，诡诈地 |  In a cunning or sly manner. | "I suppose you've been up to no good," she said archly. “我想你一直在做坏事，”她狡猾地说。| 例句：He looked at her archly. 他狡猾地看着她。| 近义词：cunningly, slyly, craftily | 反义词：honestly, openly, frankly | 词性：副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From arch (adjective) + -ly (adverb suffix).  The adjective "arch" originally meant "chief" or "principal," but developed a sense of "roguish" or "mischievous" in Middle English.
