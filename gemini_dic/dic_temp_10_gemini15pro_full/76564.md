galley: /ˈɡæli/ (US) /ˈɡæli/ (UK)| n.| 船上的厨房; （印刷厂的）排字房; 校样| a kitchen on a ship or aircraft; a long, low ship with sails and oars, used in ancient times; a room in a printing house where type is set; a proof sheet| The galley on the ship was small and cramped. 船上的厨房又小又挤。 The galley proof needed to be corrected before printing. 排字房的校样需要在印刷前更正。| 近义词: kitchen (on a ship/aircraft), camboose, cookhouse | 反义词: (No direct antonym.  Could consider "dining room" in a loose sense, but not a true opposite) | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *galie*, from Medieval Latin *galea*, possibly of uncertain origin, perhaps from Late Latin *galea* "helmet," later applied to a kind of ship, perhaps from its resemblance to a helmet. The meaning "ship's kitchen" arose later.
