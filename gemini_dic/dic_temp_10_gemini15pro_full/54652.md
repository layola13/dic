disobedience:/ˌdɪsəˈbiːdiəns/ /ˌdɪsoʊˈbiːdiəns/ | n. |不服从，违抗|The act of not obeying rules or instructions. |His disobedience caused him a lot of trouble. 他的不服从给他带来了很多麻烦。|The child's disobedience led to a time-out.  孩子的抗命导致他被罚暂停活动。|近义词：defiance, insubordination, noncompliance |反义词: obedience, compliance, submission |词性: 名词

disobedient:/ˌdɪsəˈbiːdiənt/ /ˌdɪsoʊˈbiːdiənt/| adj. |不服从的，违抗的|Refusing or failing to obey rules or someone in authority. |The disobedient child refused to go to bed.  不听话的孩子拒绝上床睡觉。|A disobedient soldier can face serious consequences. 一个不服从命令的士兵可能会面临严重的后果。|近义词：defiant, insubordinate, unruly |反义词: obedient, compliant, submissive |词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desobeissant, present participle of desobeir ("to disobey"), from des- ("dis-") + obeir ("to obey"), from Latin oboedīre ("to obey, be subject to").
