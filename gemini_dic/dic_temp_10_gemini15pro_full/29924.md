cavalierism:/ˌkævəˈlɪərɪzəm/ | n. | 骑士精神；傲慢态度；轻率的举动 | Disdainful or offhand disregard; casual treatment.  A haughty or indifferent manner. | He treated the matter with cavalierism, dismissing concerns with a wave of his hand. 他对这件事表现出轻蔑的态度，挥手打消了人们的担忧。 |  Example 2: Her cavalierism about the project's deadline angered her colleagues. 她对项目截止日期的轻率态度激怒了她的同事。 | Synonyms: arrogance, disdain, haughtiness, superciliousness, indifference, offhandedness | Antonyms: humility, respect, deference, carefulness, diligence | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cavalierisme*, from *cavalier* (“horseman, knight”), from Italian *cavaliere*, from Late Latin *caballarius* (“horseman”), from Latin *caballus* ("horse").  Originally referred to the qualities, both positive and negative, associated with horsemen or knights, particularly those of the Royalist party during the English Civil War.
