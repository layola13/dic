credited-with:/ˈkrɛdɪtɪd wɪð/
verb phrase
被认为是…的原因；归功于…
To be publicly acknowledged as having done something, discovered something, etc.
Marie Curie is credited with the discovery of radium.  玛丽·居里被认为是镭的发现者。
The architect was credited with designing the innovative building.  这位建筑师被认为是这座创新建筑的设计者。
synonyms: attributed to, recognized for, acclaimed for
antonyms: blamed for, faulted for
verb phrase

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "credit," derived from the Latin "creditum" (something entrusted, a loan), past participle of "credere" (to believe, trust, entrust). The "with" adds the sense of association or connection to the action.
