fausen:/ˈfaʊzən/| v. |哄骗，欺骗| To deceive or trick someone, often by flattery or false promises. | He tried to fausen her into giving him money. 他试图哄骗她给他钱。| synonyms: deceive, trick, dupe, cheat, swindle, defraud, con, hoodwink | antonyms: be honest, be truthful | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle High German *vüsen, from Old High German *fūson "to ready, prepare, adorn," related to Old English *fysan "to make ready, hasten." The semantic shift to "deceive" likely occurred through the idea of "dressing up" or "putting on a false front."
