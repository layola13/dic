bodhisattva: /ˌboʊdiːˈsʌtvə/ | n. | 菩萨 |  A being in Mahayana Buddhism who has attained enlightenment but postpones Nirvana in order to help others achieve enlightenment. | Example: The bodhisattva Avalokiteshvara is known for compassion. 观音菩萨以慈悲为怀。| Example:  Many Buddhists strive to cultivate the qualities of a bodhisattva. 许多佛教徒努力培养菩萨的品质。 | Synonym: enlightened being, compassionate one | Antonym: Arhat (in some interpretations) | noun
