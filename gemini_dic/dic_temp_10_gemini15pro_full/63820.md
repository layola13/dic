epidictical: /ˌɛpɪˈdɪktɪk(ə)l/
adjective
演说的，展示的，炫耀的
intended to display eloquence or to win applause; demonstrative.
Example: The politician's speech was highly epidictical, focusing more on stirring emotions than presenting concrete policy proposals.  这位政治家的演讲极具炫耀性，更多地侧重于激发情绪，而不是提出具体的政策建议。

Example:  Her epidictical presentation style, full of grand gestures and soaring rhetoric, captivated the audience. 她那充满夸张手势和华丽辞藻的炫耀性演讲风格吸引了观众。

近义词: demonstrative, rhetorical, oratorical, declamatory
反义词: plain, simple, unadorned, factual
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἐπιδεικτικός (epideiktikós, “fit for display, suited for exhibition”), from ἐπιδείκνυμι (epideiknumi, “I show, I exhibit, I display”), from ἐπί (epí, “upon, at”) + δείκνυμι (deíknumi, “I show”).
