express:/ɪkˈsprɛs/ (US), /ɪkˈspres/ (UK)| v. n. adj.|表达，表示；快递；特快的|To convey (a thought or feeling) in words or by gestures and conduct.|She expressed her gratitude to him. 她向他表达了她的感激之情。|近义词：convey, communicate, articulate, voice, utter, state, declare, pronounce, show, manifest, display, exhibit, demonstrate, indicate, signify, denote, reveal, evince, betray|反义词：repress, suppress, conceal, hide|词性: verb

express:/ɪkˈsprɛs/ (US), /ɪkˈspres/ (UK)| n.|快递；特快列车|A service for the fast delivery of mail or goods; a fast train which makes few stops.|I sent the package by express. 我用快递寄了包裹。|近义词：courier, dispatch, special delivery|反义词：slow mail, regular mail|词性: noun

express:/ɪkˈsprɛs/ (US), /ɪkˈspres/ (UK)| adj.|明确的；特快的|Clearly and unmistakably stated; operating at high speed.|It was her express wish that he attend the funeral. 她明确表示希望他参加葬礼。|近义词：explicit, specific, definite, direct, clear, unmistakable, fast, rapid, swift, quick|反义词：implicit, vague, ambiguous, slow|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *expressus*, past participle of *exprimere* ("to press out," "represent," "portray," "pronounce," "express"), from *ex* ("out") + *premere* ("to press").
