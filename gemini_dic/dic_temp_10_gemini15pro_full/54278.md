disguisedness:/dɪsˈɡaɪzdnɪs/| n.|伪装的状态；乔装打扮|The disguisedness of the spy was so perfect that no one suspected him. 这个间谍的伪装如此完美，以至于没有人怀疑他。|Her disguisedness was remarkable; she looked like a completely different person. 她的伪装很出色；她看起来像一个完全不同的人。|近义词：concealment, camouflage, disguise|反义词：exposure, revelation|词性:名词

disguise:/dɪsˈɡaɪz/| v. n.|伪装，掩饰；伪装，假扮|He disguised himself as a woman to escape the police. 他把自己伪装成女人以逃避警察。|The spy's disguise was impeccable.  这个间谍的伪装无懈可击。|近义词：camouflage, mask, cloak|反义词：reveal, expose|词性:动词、名词

词源: From Middle English *disgisen*, from Old French *desguisier*, from *des-* (expressing reversal) + *guisier* (“to fashion, prepare, arrange”), from Frankish *wisōn* (“to guide, direct, show”), from Proto-Germanic *wisōną* (“to show, point out, teach”), from Proto-Indo-European *weyd- (“to see, know”).  The noun is from the verb.
