bronzite:/ˈbrɒnzaɪt/ | n. |古铜辉石|A common rock-forming mineral belonging to the pyroxene group.  It is a magnesium iron silicate, (Mg,Fe)₂Si₂O₆, and is typically brown, greenish-brown, or bronze in color.|The geologist identified the rock as containing bronzite. 地质学家鉴定这块岩石含有古铜辉石。|Synonyms: enstatite, hypersthene|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

Etymology: From German *Bronzit*, from *bronze* (“bronze”) + *-it* (mineral suffix), due to its bronze-like color.  Ultimately from Italian *bronzo* (“bronze”).
