ormolu:/ˈɔːrməluː/
n.
镀金青铜;镀金装饰品;金铜合金
A gold-colored alloy of copper, zinc, and tin, used for decorative objects.
The ormolu clock on the mantelpiece glittered in the firelight. 壁炉架上的镀金青铜钟在火光中闪闪发光。

ormolu:/ˈɔːrməluː/
n.
镀金青铜制品
Objects made of ormolu.
She collected antique ormolu and porcelain. 她收藏古董镀金青铜制品和瓷器。


Etymology->
From French *or moulu*, literally "ground gold". Originally referred to powdered gold used in gilding, but later came to denote the gold-colored alloy itself. 源自法语 *or moulu*，字面意思是“磨碎的金子”。最初指的是用于镀金的金粉，但后来指这种金色的合金本身。

Synonyms->
gilt bronze, gilded bronze, bronze doré

Antonyms->
(No direct antonym, as it refers to a specific material.  One could consider "plain bronze" or "unadorned bronze" as contrasting terms.)
