dispersive:/dɪˈspɜːsɪv/| adj.|分散的，弥散的，散布的|Tending to disperse or causing dispersion.  Relating to or causing dispersion of light or other electromagnetic radiation.|The dispersive properties of the prism separated the white light into a rainbow. 棱镜的色散特性将白光分离成彩虹。|Synonyms: scattering, diffusing, spreading, disseminating|Antonyms: concentrating, focusing, converging|adjective

dispersion:/dɪˈspɜːʃən/| n.|分散，散布，弥散|The action or process of distributing things or people over a wide area.|The dispersion of seeds by the wind is essential for the plant's survival.  风传播种子对植物的生存至关重要。|Synonyms: scattering, dissemination, diffusion, spreading|Antonyms: concentration, gathering, accumulation|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dispersivus, from Latin dispersus (past participle of dispergere "to scatter," from dis- "apart" + spargere "to strew").
