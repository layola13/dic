anthropics: /ænˈθrɒpɪks/ | n. | 人择原理 |  The philosophical consideration that observations of the universe must be compatible with the conscious and sapient life that observes it.  It argues that any data we collect about the universe is filtered by the fact that, for us to be able to observe it, the universe must have properties that allow life to develop and exist. | The strong anthropic principle suggests the universe *must* support life. | synonyms: anthropic principle, observation selection effects | antonyms: N/A | noun

anthropic: /ænˈθrɒpɪk/ | adj. | 人择的 | Relating to the anthropic principle; considering the universe in terms of its suitability for human life. | The anthropic argument is often debated in cosmology. | synonyms:  human-centered, anthropocentric | antonyms: N/A | adjective
