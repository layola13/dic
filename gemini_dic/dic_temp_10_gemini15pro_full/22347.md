bonsai:/ˈbɒnˌsaɪ/ (US) /ˈbɒnzaɪ/ (UK)| n. |盆景|A miniature tree or shrub grown in a pot or container, typically trained and pruned to resemble a full-sized tree in nature.|She has a collection of beautiful bonsai trees. 她收藏了很多美丽的盆景树。|The bonsai was carefully pruned to maintain its shape. 这盆盆景被精心修剪以保持其形状。|近义词:miniature tree, potted tree|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "full-sized tree")|词性:noun


bonsais:/ˈbɒnˌsaɪz/ (US) /ˈbɒnzaɪz/ (UK)| n. |盆景（复数）|Plural form of bonsai, referring to multiple miniature trees grown in containers.|He learned the art of cultivating bonsais from his grandfather. 他从他祖父那里学习了种植盆景的艺术。|The bonsais on display were all meticulously shaped. 展出的盆景都经过精心造型。|近义词:miniature trees, potted trees|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性:noun
