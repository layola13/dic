fungibles:/ˈfʌndʒəbəlz/ | n. | 代替物；可互换物 |Objects that are fungible are of such a nature or kind that one unit is just as good as another unit, for example, one barrel of oil or one bushel of wheat.  | Example:  Grains are fungible commodities, meaning one bushel of corn is equivalent to any other bushel of corn.  谷物是可替代的商品，这意味着一蒲式耳玉米等于任何其他一蒲式耳玉米。

Example: Cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin are designed to be fungible, so one Bitcoin is theoretically equal to any other Bitcoin. 像比特币这样的加密货币被设计成可替代的，因此理论上一个比特币等于任何其他比特币。 | 近义词: interchangeable, replaceable, equivalent | 反义词: non-fungible, unique, irreplaceable | 词性: 名词 (plural)

fungible:/ˈfʌndʒəbəl/ | adj. | 可替代的；可互换的 | (of goods or commodities) being of such nature or kind that one unit or part may be used in place of another in the satisfaction of an obligation. | Example:  Fungible goods are easily traded on commodity markets. 可替代商品在商品市场上很容易交易。

Example:  The contract specified the delivery of fungible goods, so the supplier could choose which specific items to send.  合同规定交付可替代的商品，因此供应商可以选择发送哪些特定项目。 | 近义词: interchangeable, replaceable, equivalent | 反义词: non-fungible, unique, irreplaceable | 词性: 形容词


词源:  From Late Middle English *fungibilis, from Latin fungibilis (“that may be used in place of another; exchangeable”), from fungi (“to perform, execute, discharge”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰunǵ- (“to enjoy”).
