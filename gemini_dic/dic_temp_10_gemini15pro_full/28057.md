An illegal or questionable act.|例句：The children were full of capers. 孩子们嬉闹个不停。The robbery was a daring caper. 这起抢劫案是一起大胆的恶作剧。|近义词：frolic, prank, escapade, antic, stunt|反义词:seriousness,boredom|词性:noun, verb

caper:/ˈkeɪpər/| vi.|跳跃，雀跃；嬉戏|To leap or skip playfully. To engage in pranks or mischievous behavior.|例句：The lambs capered in the field. 羊羔在田野里跳跃嬉戏。  He capered about, pretending to be a monkey. 他跳来跳去，假装自己是只猴子。|近义词：frolic, gambol, prance|反义词:mope, trudge|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *capre*, from Italian *capriola* "leap, caper," from Late Latin *capriolare* "to leap like a goat," from Latin *caper* "goat" (related to Greek *kapros* "boar"). The sense of "illegal act" arose from the notion of a "wild escapade."
