nonrelativities:/nɒnˌrɛləˈtɪvɪtiz/ | n. | 非相对性；非相对论效应 |  Things or phenomena that are not governed by the principles of relativity, especially Einstein's theory of relativity. This generally refers to situations where speeds are much slower than the speed of light and gravitational fields are relatively weak. | Example:  Newtonian mechanics accurately describes nonrelativities in everyday life.  牛顿力学准确地描述了日常生活中的非相对性。 | Synonym: classical mechanics, Newtonian physics | Antonym: relativities, relativistic effects | noun

nonrelativity:/nɒnˌrɛləˈtɪvɪti/ | n. | 非相对性 | The state or quality of not being relative; the condition where classical physics (Newtonian mechanics) provides an adequate description of phenomena. | Example: The nonrelativity of the situation allowed us to use simpler calculations.  这种情况的非相对性使我们可以使用更简单的计算。 | Synonym: classicality | Antonym: relativity | noun
