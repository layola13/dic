death-rattle:/ˈdɛθ ˌræt(ə)l/| n.| 临终呼吸; 垂死挣扎; 死亡前的征兆 |A sound produced by a person or animal in the throes of death, caused by air passing through mucus in the lungs and trachea.  A sound or sign that something is failing or coming to an end. | The old man's death-rattle could be heard throughout the hospital ward.  老人临终的呼吸声在整个病房都能听到。

The struggling company gave one last death-rattle before finally declaring bankruptcy. 这家苦苦挣扎的公司在最终宣布破产之前发出了最后的垂死挣扎。| 近义词:  gasp, gurgle, rattle,  last gasp, dying breath | 反义词:  breath of life,  vitality | 词性: 名词

death-rattle:/ˈdɛθ ˌræt(ə)l/| v.| 发出临终的呼吸声; 垂死挣扎| To make a death rattle. To show signs of imminent failure or collapse. | The engine death-rattled a few times before finally dying.  发动机发出几次临终的呼吸声，最后熄火了。

The old regime death-rattled for several months before it was finally overthrown. 旧政权垂死挣扎了几个月，最终被推翻了。| 近义词:  gasp, sputter, fail, decline | 反义词:  thrive, flourish | 词性: 动词
