ministerialism:/ˌmɪnɪˈstɪəriəlɪzəm/
n.
部长制；内阁制；牧师职务
The tendency to concentrate power in the hands of ministers or a cabinet.  A system of government in which the executive power is exercised by a cabinet of ministers who are responsible to the legislature.  The office or duties of a minister of religion.
Ministerialism is a growing concern in many democracies.  部长制在许多民主国家日益受到关注。
The country transitioned from a monarchy to ministerialism.  这个国家从君主制过渡到部长制。
His ministerialism was marked by compassion and understanding.  他的牧师职务的特点是富有同情心和理解力。
Synonyms: cabinet system, parliamentarism, pastorate
Antonyms: presidential system, dictatorship, laity
