hurdy-gurdy:/ˈhɜːdi ˈɡɜːdi/ /ˈhɝːdi ˈɡɝːdi/ | n. | 手摇风琴；街头手摇风琴艺人 |A musical instrument with a droning sound played by turning a crank.  Also, a person who plays this instrument.| The hurdy-gurdy man entertained the crowds in the market square.  街头手摇风琴艺人在市场广场上娱乐人群。 |Synonym: barrel organ, street organ | Antonym: (No direct antonym for the instrument.  Perhaps "silent instrument") | noun

Etymology:
From Old French *vielle à roue ("wheel fiddle"), ultimately from Medieval Latin *organolum rota ("small organ with a wheel").
