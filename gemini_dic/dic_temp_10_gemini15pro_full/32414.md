chilly:/ˈtʃɪli/ | adj. | 寒冷的，冷飕飕的 | Feeling uncomfortably cold. |  It's a bit chilly outside today, you might need a jacket. 今天外面有点冷，你可能需要一件夹克。|  The chilly wind made me shiver. 冷风让我瑟瑟发抖。 | cold, cool, crisp | warm, hot, balmy | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ciele (“chill,” literally “cold”), from Proto-Germanic *kaliz (“cold”), from Proto-Indo-European *gel- (“cold”). Cognate with Scots cheel (“cold”), West Frisian kjeld (“cold”), Dutch kil (“chill”), German kälte (“cold”), Swedish kyla (“cold”), Icelandic kala (“frost”).  The adjective developed from the noun "chill" which referred to the sensation of coldness.
