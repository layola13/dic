go cold turkey:/ɡoʊ koʊld ˈtɜːrki/ | idiom | 突然戒掉（坏习惯）；一下子完全停止 | To abruptly stop addictive or unhealthy behavior, such as smoking or drinking alcohol, without any gradual reduction or outside support. |  He decided to go cold turkey on caffeine and quit drinking coffee altogether. 他决定彻底戒掉咖啡因，完全不喝咖啡了。| quit abruptly, stop suddenly, give up immediately | gradually reduce, wean off | idiom
cold turkey:/koʊld ˈtɜːrki/ | n. | 冷火鸡（俚语，指突然戒除某种瘾）| The unpleasant physical and mental effects experienced when abruptly stopping an addictive substance or behavior. | He experienced severe cold turkey after quitting smoking. 他戒烟后经历了严重的戒断反应。| withdrawal symptoms, abstinence syndrome | comfort, relief | noun
turkey:/ˈtɜːrki/ | n. | 火鸡；笨蛋；失败之作 | A large bird native to North America, often eaten at Thanksgiving and Christmas; (informal) a foolish or inept person; something that is a complete failure. | We had turkey for Thanksgiving dinner. 我们感恩节晚餐吃了火鸡。| bird, fowl, gobbler | success, winner | noun
