melancholias: /ˌmɛlənˈkəʊliəz/ | n. | 忧郁，悲哀，忧伤 | A state of pensive sadness, typically without an obvious cause.  It is the plural form of melancholia, which is now rarely used in modern psychology or medicine, having been replaced by terms like depression. |  In the Renaissance, melancholias were sometimes considered a mark of artistic genius.  文艺复兴时期，忧郁有时被认为是艺术天才的标志。|Synonyms: sadness, depression, gloom, dejection | Antonyms: joy, happiness, elation, cheerfulness | Noun

Etymology: From Late Latin melancholia, from Greek melankholia "black bile," from melas "black" + khole "bile."  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholia.
