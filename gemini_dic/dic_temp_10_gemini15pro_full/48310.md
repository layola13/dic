decrepitude:/dɪˈkrɛpɪtjuːd/ /dɪˈkrɛpɪtuːd/
n.|衰老，老朽，破旧|The decrepitude of the building was evident in its crumbling facade.  这栋建筑的破败在其摇摇欲坠的正面显而易见。|例句：The once grand mansion had fallen into a state of decrepitude.  曾经宏伟的宅邸已经破败不堪。|近义词：feebleness, infirmity, decay, dilapidation|反义词：youth, vigor, robustness, soundness|词性:名词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *decrepitudo, from Latin decrepitus (“very old, infirm”) (from de- + crepitus (“rattled, creaking”)) + -udo (abstract noun suffix).
