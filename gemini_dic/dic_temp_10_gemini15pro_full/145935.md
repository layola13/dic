overdiscounting: /ˌoʊvərˈdɪskaʊntɪŋ/ | n. | 过度贴现，过度折扣 | Applying a discount rate that is too high, thereby undervaluing future cash flows or benefits.  This can lead to short-sighted decision-making, where immediate gains are prioritized over larger, long-term rewards. | Example: Overdiscounting future profits led the company to reject a promising long-term investment. 过度贴现未来利润导致该公司拒绝了一项有前景的长期投资。

Example:  Behavioral economists study how overdiscounting affects people's saving habits. 行为经济学家研究过度贴现如何影响人们的储蓄习惯。 | 近义词: excessive discounting, myopic discounting | 反义词: underdiscounting | 词性: noun

Etymology:  "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "discounting" (from "discount," ultimately from Old French "desconter," meaning "to count off, deduct").
