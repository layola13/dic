enviously:/ˈɛnviəsli/| adv.|羡慕地，嫉妒地|In a manner characterized by envy; with feelings of envy.  She looked enviously at her friend's new car. 她羡慕地看着她朋友的新车。|Synonyms: jealously, covetously, resentfully|Antonyms: admiringly, approvingly, generously|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From envy + -ously.  Envy comes from Old French envie, ultimately from Latin invidia "envy, jealousy," from invidere "to look askance at, cast an evil eye upon," from in- "upon" + videre "to see."
