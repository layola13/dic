blackout:/ˈblækaʊt/| n.|停电；断电；灯火管制；消息封锁；意识丧失|A blackout is a period of darkness caused by a failure of the electricity supply, or a period when the government prevents information from being broadcast or published. It can also refer to a temporary loss of consciousness.|The city experienced a blackout during the storm. 城市在暴风雨期间经历了停电。|synonyms: power outage, power cut, outage, failure, loss of power, power failure, electricity failure, loss of electricity, temporary darkness; news embargo, suppression, censorship|antonyms: illumination, enlightenment|noun

blackout:/ˈblækaʊt/| v.|使停电；封锁消息；使失去知觉|To blackout means to cause a loss of electricity supply, to suppress information, or to cause someone to lose consciousness.|The storm blacked out the entire city. 暴风雨使整个城市停电。|synonyms: power down, cut off, shut down, disable, suppress, censor, knock out, render unconscious|antonyms: power up, turn on, enable, illuminate, enlighten|verb
