connivency:/kəˈnaɪvənsi/
n.
串通，共谋，纵容
Secret or tacit encouragement or assent to wrongdoing by another.
The police were accused of connivency in the escape. 警方被指控纵容了越狱。
近义词:collusion, complicity, collaboration
反义词:opposition, resistance, obstruction
词性:名词

connive:/kəˈnaɪv/
v.
串通，共谋，纵容
Secretly allow (something considered immoral, illegal, wrong, or harmful) to occur.
Officials were accused of conniving with the company in the supply of arms to the rebels. 官员们被指控与该公司串通向叛乱分子提供武器。
近义词:conspire, collude, intrigue
反义词:oppose, resist, obstruct
词性:动词

词源:
From Late Latin *conniventia, from Latin connivēre ‘close the eyes (at), wink at, overlook,’ from con- ‘together’ + nivēre ‘wink.’
