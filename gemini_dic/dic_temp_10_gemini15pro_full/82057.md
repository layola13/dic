grith:/ɡrɪθ/| n.|围长；腰围；周长|The grith of the tree was over 20 feet.  这棵树的周长超过20英尺。|English Explanation: A measure of the circumference of something, especially the waist or the chest of a person or animal.|例句：The knight tightened the grith on his warhorse.  骑士勒紧了战马的肚带。|近义词：circumference, girth, perimeter|反义词:N/A|词性:noun

词源:
From Old Norse *grið, from Proto-Germanic *gridiz, meaning "peace, truce, sanctuary."  The sense of "girth" likely developed from the idea of a protected or enclosed space.
