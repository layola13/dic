anapaestic:/ˌænəˈpɛstɪk/ | adj. |抑抑扬格的，抑扬抑格的| Of or relating to a metrical foot consisting of two short or unstressed syllables followed by one long or stressed syllable.  | Example:  The poem was written in anapaestic meter. 这首诗是用抑抑扬格写的。

Example:  Anapaestic rhythms can create a sense of urgency or excitement. 抑抑扬格的节奏可以营造一种紧迫感或兴奋感。 | 近义词:  tribrach (less common) | 反义词:  dactylic, iambic | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *anapaistos* "struck back," from *anapaiein* "to strike back," from *ana-* "back" + *paiein* "to strike."  The term originally referred to a reversal of the dactylic meter (long-short-short), hence "struck back."
