charlemagne:/ˈʃɑːrləˌmeɪn/| n. |查理曼大帝|Charlemagne, also known as Charles the Great or Charles I, was King of the Franks from 768, King of the Lombards from 774, and Holy Roman Emperor from 800. He united much of Europe during the early Middle Ages.|Charlemagne is considered one of the most important figures in European history. |Synonym: Charles the Great,  Emperor of the Romans |Antonym: N/A|noun
