hypotheca:/hɪpɒˈθiːkə/ (US) /haɪpɒˈθiːkə/ (UK)| n.|抵押，担保物|A thing pledged or mortgaged as security for a debt; a mortgage.  It refers to the right in a thing belonging to another, which right constitutes a security for the payment of a debt or the discharge of some other obligation.  It differs from a pledge in that the possession of the property remains with the debtor.|The hypotheca was a form of security interest in Roman law. |Synonym: mortgage, security, pledge, lien|Antonym: unsecured loan|noun
