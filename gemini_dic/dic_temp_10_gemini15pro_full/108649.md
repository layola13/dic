leafed:/liːft/ | adj. | 有…叶的；…叶的 | Having leaves of a specified kind or number. |  The tree is broad-leafed. 这棵树叶子很宽阔。|  The plant is four-leafed. 这种植物有四片叶子。| Near synonyms: leafy, foliate | Near antonyms: leafless | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From leaf + -ed.  "Leaf" comes from Old English *lēaf, from Proto-Germanic *laubą, from Proto-Indo-European *leubʰ- (“to peel, to strip off”).  The suffix "-ed" forms adjectives from nouns, indicating possession or characteristic of the noun.
