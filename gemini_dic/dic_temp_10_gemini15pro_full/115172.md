manic:/ˈmænɪk/| adj.|躁狂的；狂热的|Showing wild, apparently unreasonable enthusiasm or excitement.|He had a manic grin on his face. 他脸上带着狂热的笑容。|Synonyms: frenzied, frantic, wild, hysterical|Antonyms: calm, peaceful, relaxed, subdued|adjective

manic:/ˈmænɪk/| n.|躁狂症患者|A person suffering from mania.|He was diagnosed as a manic. 他被诊断为躁狂症患者。|Synonyms:  lunatic, madman|Antonyms: - |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *manicus* "mad, raving, inspired by a god," from *manus* "hand" (perhaps originally in the sense of "laying hands on, taking hold of, seizing" by divine power).
