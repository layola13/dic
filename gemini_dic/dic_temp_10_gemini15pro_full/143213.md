outcrowd:/ˈaʊtkraʊd/
n.|局外人，边缘人群|A group of people who are not accepted as part of a larger social group.|They felt like the outcrowd at the exclusive party. 他们在那个专属派对上感觉像是局外人。|outsiders, misfits|in-crowd, mainstream|noun

Etymology->
"outcrowd" is a compound word formed from the adverb "out" and the noun "crowd". "Out" in this context means "outside" or "excluded from". "Crowd" refers to a large number of people gathered together. The combination creates the meaning of a group excluded from the main crowd.
