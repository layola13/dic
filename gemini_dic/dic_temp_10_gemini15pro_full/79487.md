globalization:/ˌɡloʊbələˈzeɪʃən/ /ˌɡləʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n.|全球化|The increasing interconnectedness and interdependence of countries through the exchange of goods, services, information, and ideas.|例句：Globalization has led to increased trade between nations. 全球化导致国家间贸易增加。

例句：The globalization of the economy has had a profound impact on local businesses. 经济全球化对当地企业产生了深远的影响。

近义词：internationalization, global integration|反义词:localization, regionalization|词性:noun


globalize:/ˈɡloʊbəˌlaɪz/ /ˈɡləʊbəlaɪz/| v.|全球化|To make something worldwide in scope or application.|例句：Many companies are seeking to globalize their operations. 许多公司正在寻求将其业务全球化。

例句：The internet has helped to globalize communication. 互联网帮助实现了通讯的全球化。

近义词：internationalize, broaden, expand|反义词:localize, restrict|词性:verb


global:/ˈɡloʊbl/ /ˈɡləʊbl/ | adj.|全球的|Relating to the whole world; worldwide.|例句：Climate change is a global issue. 气候变化是一个全球性问题。

例句：The company has a global reach. 该公司业务遍及全球。

近义词：worldwide, international, universal|反义词:local, regional, national|词性:adjective
