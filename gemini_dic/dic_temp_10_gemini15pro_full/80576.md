goofball:/ˈɡuːfbɔːl/| n.|傻瓜，笨蛋，搞笑的人|A silly or goofy person; someone who acts in a playful, clumsy, or eccentric way.|He's such a goofball, always making us laugh. 他真是个傻瓜，总是逗我们笑。|
|近义词：clown, buffoon, jester, klutz, goof, silly person|反义词：serious person, genius, intellectual|名词|

goofy:/ˈɡuːfi/| adj.|傻的，愚蠢的，笨拙的|Silly, foolish, or clumsy.|She made a goofy face at the camera. 她对着镜头做了个鬼脸。|
|近义词：silly, foolish, clumsy, awkward, ridiculous|反义词：serious, sensible, intelligent, graceful|形容词|


ETYMOLOGY->
goofball (n.):  From *goof* (meaning a silly or foolish person) + *ball* (a playful or whimsical suffix).  "Goof" itself is of uncertain origin, possibly related to "goff" or "gaff," which referred to a clumsy or awkward person. It emerged in American slang in the early 20th century.
