fallow:/ˈfæloʊ/ (US) /ˈfæləʊ/ (UK)| adj. n. v. |(of farmland) plowed and harrowed but left unsown for a period in order to restore its fertility or to avoid surplus production.| The field lay fallow for a year. 这块地休耕了一年。|Synonyms: uncultivated, resting, idle |Antonyms: cultivated, active|noun

fallow:/ˈfæloʊ/ (US) /ˈfæləʊ/ (UK)| adj. n. v. |plow (land) and leave it unseeded for a season or more; leave (land) fallow.|  He fallowed the north field this year.  他今年休耕了北边的田地。|Synonyms: rest, unplant, till|Antonyms: cultivate, plant|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fealgian, from Proto-Germanic *falgōną (“to plow, harrow”), related to Old High German falga (“harrow”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *plōg- (“to plow”).
