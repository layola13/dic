chivarros:/tʃɪˈvɑːroʊz/ | n. | 牛仔裤，牛仔装 |  Typically refers to tight-fitting jeans or denim clothing, often used in the context of Mexican or Western fashion. Derived from the Spanish word "chiva," meaning goat, possibly referring to goatskin leather traditionally used for clothing. | Example: He wore his chivarros to the rodeo. 他穿着牛仔裤去参加牛仔竞技表演。 | Example:  She looked stunning in her new chivarros. 她穿着新的牛仔裤看起来美极了。 | Synonym: jeans, denim | Antonym: formal wear | noun

chivarro:/tʃɪˈvɑːroʊ/ | n. |  年轻的男子，小伙子; 小山羊，小羊 |  Can refer to a young man, particularly in a rural or working-class context, or a young goat.  The meaning depends on the context.  Derived from the Spanish word "chiva," meaning goat. | Example: The chivarro helped his father with the farm work.  这个小伙子帮他的父亲做农活。| Example: The chivarro bleated for its mother. 小山羊咩咩地叫着它的妈妈。| Synonym: (for young man) lad, youngster, youth; (for goat) kid, billy | Antonym: (for young man) old man, elder; (for goat) adult goat | noun
