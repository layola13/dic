impuration:/ˌɪmpjuˈteɪʃn/ | n. | 归罪；归咎；污名化 | The act of attributing something undesirable, such as a fault or crime, to someone; accusation. |  Example 1: The impuration of guilt upon the innocent man was a travesty of justice.  (将罪名归咎于无辜的人是对正义的歪曲。)


Example 2: He resented the impuration that he was somehow responsible for the failure. (他怨恨别人暗示他应该为这次失败负责。) | Synonyms: accusation, attribution, charge, incrimination, imputation | Antonyms: exoneration, acquittal, vindication | noun


impute:/ɪmˈpjuːt/ | vt. | 归罪于；归咎于；归因于 | To attribute or ascribe (something, usually undesirable) to someone or something. | Example 1: The police impute the accident to the driver's negligence. (警方将事故归咎于司机的疏忽。)


Example 2:  They imputed his silence to guilt. (他们把他的沉默归咎于内疚。) | Synonyms: ascribe, attribute, assign, credit | Antonyms: exculpate, absolve | verb
