catalepsies: /ˌkætəˈlɛpsiːz/
n.
僵厥症（复数）
A medical condition characterized by a trancelike state of consciousness and postural rigidity.  A state of suspended animation or immobility, often associated with schizophrenia or other mental disorders.
The patient experienced a series of catalepsies during his psychotic episode.  这位病人在精神病发作期间经历了一系列的僵厥症。
近义词：trances, seizures, rigidity
反义词：flexibility, mobility
词性: noun (plural)

catalepsy: /ˈkætəˌlɛpsi/
n.
僵厥症
A medical condition characterized by a trancelike state of consciousness and postural rigidity. A state of suspended animation or immobility, often associated with schizophrenia or other mental disorders.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with catalepsy after observing his prolonged immobility. 医生观察到病人长时间不动后，诊断他患有僵厥症。
近义词：trance, seizure, rigidity
反义词：flexibility, mobility
词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *catalepsis, from Ancient Greek κατάληψις (katálēpsis, “seizure”), from καταλαμβάνω (katalambánō, “I seize, take possession of”), from κατά (katá, “down, against”) + λαμβάνω (lambánō, “I take”).
