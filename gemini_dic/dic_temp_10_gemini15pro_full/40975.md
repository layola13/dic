coopt:/koʊˈɒpt/ /kuːˈɒpt/| v. |吸收；指派；拉拢；征用|To add (someone) to a group, especially by the votes of the existing members; to appropriate something for a different or particular use.|例句：The rebels were eventually coopted into the mainstream political system. 叛军最终被吸纳到主流政治体系中。

例句：The committee was coopted by the government. 委员会被政府征用。|近义词：absorb, appropriate, enlist, incorporate|反义词:reject, exclude|词性:动词

coopted:/koʊˈɒptɪd/ /kuːˈɒptɪd/| adj. |被吸收的；被指派的|Having been added to a group, especially by the votes of the existing members; appropriated for a different or particular use.|例句：The coopted members of the board brought new perspectives. 被吸纳的董事会成员带来了新的视角。

例句：The coopted building served as a temporary hospital. 被征用的建筑物用作临时医院。|近义词：absorbed, incorporated, enlisted|反义词:rejected, excluded|词性:形容词
