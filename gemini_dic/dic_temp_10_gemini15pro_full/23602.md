brambling:/ˈbræmblɪŋ/ | n. |燕雀;花鸡|A small finch (Fringilla montifringilla) that breeds in northern Europe and Asia and winters farther south, often in large flocks.  It has a brightly colored plumage, especially in the male during breeding season.|例句：The bird feeder was covered in bramblings.  喂鸟器上全是燕雀。

例句：A flock of bramblings descended on the garden. 一群燕雀飞落到花园里。|近义词：finch, chaffinch|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *bramli, related to brambling's call.
