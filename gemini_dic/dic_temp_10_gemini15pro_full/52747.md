dilapidate:/dɪˈlæpɪdeɪt/| v. |使荒废，使破败|to cause (something) to fall into partial ruin or decay, as by neglect or misuse  (often passive)|The old house was dilapidated from years of neglect. 这栋老房子由于多年的疏于照顾而破败不堪。|synonyms:decay, deteriorate, ruin|antonyms:renovate, restore, repair|verb
dilapidated:/dɪˈlæpɪdeɪɪtɪd/| adj. |荒废的，破败的|fallen into partial ruin or decay, as from age, wear, or neglect|The dilapidated barn was a haven for stray cats.  破败的谷仓成了流浪猫的天堂。|synonyms:ramshackle, derelict, tumbledown|antonyms:pristine, immaculate, well-maintained|adjective
