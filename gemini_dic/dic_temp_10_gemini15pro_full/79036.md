give-full-rein-to:/ɡɪv fʊl reɪn tuː/| v.|放任；尽情发挥；|To allow something to happen freely and without control.  To give someone complete freedom to do something.|She gave full rein to her imagination and wrote a wonderful fantasy novel. 她充分发挥了自己的想象力，写出了一部精彩的奇幻小说。|近义词：indulge, unleash, vent, give free rein to|反义词: restrain, restrict, control|词性:动词短语

ETYMOLOGY->
From the equestrian practice of loosening the reins, allowing a horse to run freely.  "Rein" itself comes from Old French "resne," ultimately from Latin "retinere" (to hold back).  Giving "full rein" therefore means releasing the restraint.
