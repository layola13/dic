draconianism: /dreɪˈkoʊniənɪzəm/ | n. |  严酷，苛刻；严刑峻法 |  Excessive harshness or severity in laws or their application.  Derived from Draco, an Athenian law-maker of the 7th century BC, whose code of laws prescribed the death penalty for even minor offenses. | The draconianism of the new regime was evident in the strict curfews and harsh punishments. 新政权的严酷统治体现在严格的宵禁和严厉的惩罚上。 |  The school's draconianism regarding cell phone use resulted in many confiscations. 学校对手机使用的严苛规定导致许多手机被没收。 | severity, rigor, harshness | leniency, clemency | noun
