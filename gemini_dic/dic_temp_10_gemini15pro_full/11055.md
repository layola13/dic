arch-poet:/ˈɑːrtʃˌpoʊɪt/ | n. | 宫廷诗人；流浪诗人 | A medieval poet, typically one attached to a royal or noble court, often composing verses in praise of their patron or on topical events.  Sometimes used more broadly to refer to any itinerant poet who performed for their living, often relying on the generosity of patrons. |  The arch-poet entertained the court with his witty verses.  宫廷诗人用他诙谐的诗句娱乐了宫廷。 |  Synonyms: court poet, itinerant poet, bard, minstrel | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "prose writer" in a loose sense) | noun
