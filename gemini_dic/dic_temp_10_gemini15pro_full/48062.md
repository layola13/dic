declamatory:/dɪˈklæmətɔːri/
adjective
演讲的；朗诵的；雄辩的
Relating to or characterized by declamation; rhetorical; theatrical in style.
She delivered a declamatory speech about the importance of education. 她发表了一篇关于教育重要性的慷慨激昂的演讲。
The actor's declamatory style was well-suited to the Shakespearean play.  这位演员的朗诵风格非常适合莎士比亚的戏剧。
近义词:rhetorical, oratorical, bombastic, grandiloquent
反义词:understated, quiet, subdued, simple
词性:adjective

declamatory:/dɪˈklæmətɔːri/
noun (rare)
雄辩家, 朗诵者
A person who speaks in a declamatory manner.
He was known as a declamatory in his youth, often reciting poetry with great passion. 他年轻时以雄辩著称，经常充满激情地朗诵诗歌。
Though a skilled declamatory, she lacked the genuine emotion needed to truly move her audience. 虽然她是一个熟练的朗诵者，但她缺乏真正打动观众的真挚情感。
近义词:orator, rhetorician, speaker
反义词:listener, audience
词性:noun
