bootstraps:/ˈbuːtˌstræps/| n.| 靴带; 自力更生; 自举| a pair of loops of leather or other strong material at the top of a boot, used for pulling it on.  Figuratively, it refers to achieving something without external help. |He pulled himself up by his bootstraps and started his own business. 他靠自己的力量白手起家，创办了自己的企业。|近义词：self-reliance, initiative, resourcefulness|反义词: dependence, reliance|名词
