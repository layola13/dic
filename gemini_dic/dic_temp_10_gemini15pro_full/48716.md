deficits: /ˈdefɪsɪts/ (US), /ˈdɛfɪsɪts/ (UK)| n. |赤字，亏损，不足|A deficit is the amount by which something, especially a sum of money, is too small.|The government is facing a budget deficit. 政府面临预算赤字。|synonyms: shortfall, deficiency, shortage|antonyms: surplus, excess|noun

deficit: /ˈdefɪsɪt/ (US), /ˈdɛfɪsɪt/ (UK)| adj. |不足的，亏损的|(Less common usage as an adjective) Characterized by a deficit.|The company's deficit spending led to bankruptcy. 该公司的赤字支出导致破产。|synonyms: lacking, insufficient, inadequate|antonyms: surplus, excessive|adjective
