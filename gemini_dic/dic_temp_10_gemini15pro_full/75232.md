frolic:/ˈfrɒlɪk/ (UK) /ˈfrɑːlɪk/ (US)| vi. n.|嬉戏，玩耍；嬉戏玩耍；|To play and move about happily and energetically; a playful and lively action.|The lambs frolicked in the spring sunshine. 羊羔在春天的阳光下嬉戏。|近义词：play, romp, gambol, caper, frisk|反义词:mope, sulk|词性:动词,名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *froliken*, from Middle Dutch *vrolijken* (“to make merry, to cheer”), from *vro*, *vroe* (“happy, joyful”) + *-lijc* (an adjectival suffix) + *-en* (a verbal suffix).  The root *vro* is related to Old English *frēo* (“free, noble, joyful”).
