immateriality:/ˌɪməˌtɪəriˈælɪti/
n.
无关紧要；非物质性；精神性
The state or quality of being immaterial; inconsequentiality; irrelevance.  Lack of physical substance.
Given the vastness of the universe, human life seems to possess a certain immateriality. 考虑到宇宙的浩瀚，人类的生命似乎具有一定的无关紧要性。
The immateriality of the soul is a central tenet of many religions. 灵魂的非物质性是许多宗教的核心信条。
近义词:insignificance, irrelevance, unimportance, ethereality
反义词:materiality, relevance, importance, substantiality
词性:noun

immaterial:/ˌɪməˈtɪəriəl/
adj.
无关紧要的；非物质的；精神上的
Not relevant; unimportant. Not consisting of matter; spiritual.
The judge ruled that the evidence was immaterial to the case. 法官裁定该证据与案件无关。
Whether he likes it or not is immaterial; he has to do it. 他喜不喜欢无关紧要；他必须去做。
近义词:insignificant, irrelevant, unimportant, inconsequential
反义词:material, relevant, important, consequential
词性:adjective


词源:
From Late Latin immaterialitas, from immaterialis "not material," from in- "not" + materialis "material."
