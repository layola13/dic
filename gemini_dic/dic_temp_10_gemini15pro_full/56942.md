dotterel:/ˈdɒtərəl/ (UK) /ˈdɑːtərəl/ (US)| n.| 点斑原野鸻 |A small wading bird (Charadriidae family) known for its distinctive plumage and its remarkable tameness, making it easy to approach.|Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a dotterel during their hike.  观鸟者在徒步旅行中发现了一只点斑原野鸻，非常激动。|Synonyms: plover (more general), sandpiper (some species)|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a specific bird species)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *doterel, from Old French *doterel, of uncertain origin, possibly related to *dote, meaning "fool" or "simpleton," referring to the bird's perceived tameness and approachability.
