nickel-and-dimed:/ˈnɪkəl ænd daɪmd/ | adj. |  靠微薄收入过活的；锱铢必较的 |  Refers to a situation of being paid very low wages, or of having very little money, often forcing one to be extremely frugal. Derived from the small denominations of the US nickel (5 cents) and dime (10 cents).  |  She felt nickel-and-dimed by her employer, who constantly looked for ways to cut costs. 她觉得她的雇主对她锱铢必较，总是想方设法削减成本。|  low-paid, impoverished, struggling |  wealthy, affluent | adjective

nickel-and-dime:/ˈnɪkəl ænd daɪmd/ | v. |  克扣；斤斤计较 | To treat someone unfairly or exploit them financially, often by charging them for small or insignificant things. |  The landlord was nickel-and-diming the tenants with extra charges for everything. 房东对租户斤斤计较，对所有东西都额外收费。|  exploit, cheat, shortchange |  overpay, compensate fairly | verb
