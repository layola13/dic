imbecilitate: /ɪmˈbɛsɪlɪteɪt/
verb
使弱智；使愚蠢；使衰弱
to make someone or something weak or ineffective
The constant criticism from his boss had imbecilitated him, leaving him unable to make even simple decisions. 他老板不断的批评使他变得软弱无能，甚至连简单的决定都做不了。
The prolonged drought had imbecilitated the crops, making them vulnerable to disease. 长时间的干旱使农作物变得衰弱，容易感染疾病。
近义词: weaken, enfeeble, debilitate
反义词: strengthen, empower, invigorate
词性: v.

Etymology:
From Late Latin imbecilitatus, past participle of imbecilitare (“to weaken”), from imbecillis (“weak”).  The root "im-" is a negative prefix, "becillis" relates to "baculum" meaning "staff," suggesting a lack of support.
