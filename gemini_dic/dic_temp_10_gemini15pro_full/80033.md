go-to-town:/ˌɡoʊ tə ˈtaʊn/| idiom|尽情享受；全力以赴；大肆挥霍|To do something enthusiastically, energetically, or to excess.|例句：They really went to town on the decorations for the party. 他们真的在派对装饰上花了很多心思。|近义词：go all out, spare no expense, pull out all the stops|反义词：hold back, do things half-heartedly|phrase

go-to-town:/ˌɡoʊ tə ˈtaʊn/| idiom|倾诉衷肠；畅所欲言|To speak freely and at length, often with strong emotion.|例句：She went to town on him about how unfair he had been. 她对他倾诉了一番，说他有多么不公平。|近义词：let loose, speak one's mind, vent|反义词：keep quiet, bottle up|phrase
