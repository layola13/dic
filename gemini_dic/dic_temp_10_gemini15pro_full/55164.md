dissemblers:/dɪˈsɛmblərz/
|n.| 伪君子，掩饰者|People who conceal their true intentions or feelings, often by pretending to have different ones.  Those who disguise their true motives or beliefs.|The dissemblers at court whispered behind the king's back, plotting his downfall. 朝廷里的伪君子在国王背后窃窃私语，密谋推翻他。|Synonyms: hypocrites, pretenders, liars, deceivers, fakers|Antonyms: honest people, truthful individuals, straight shooters|noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *dissemblen, from Old French dissimuler, from Latin dissimulare (“to disguise, conceal, dissemble”), from dis- (“apart, away”) + simulare (“to make like, copy, imitate, pretend”), from similis (“like”).
