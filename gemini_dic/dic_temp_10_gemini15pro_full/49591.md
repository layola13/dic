demeritoriously:/ˌdiːˌmɛrɪˈtɔːriəsli/
adv.
无功地，不值得称赞地
in a way that does not deserve praise or reward; undeservedly.
He acted demeritoriously by accepting bribes. 他接受贿赂的行为不值得称赞。
近义词: undeservedly, unworthily, discreditably
反义词: meritoriously, deservedly, creditably
词性: 副词

demerit:/diːˈmɛrɪt/
n.
缺点，过失；短处
a fault or disadvantage.
One of the demerits of the plan is its high cost. 这个计划的缺点之一是成本高。
近义词: fault, flaw, defect, shortcoming
反义词: merit, advantage, strength
词性: 名词

merit:/ˈmɛrɪt/
n.
优点，价值；功绩
the quality of being particularly good or worthy, especially so as to deserve praise or reward.
The merits of the proposal were debated at length.  这个提议的优点经过了详细的辩论。
近义词: worth, value, virtue, excellence
反义词: demerit, fault, flaw, defect
词性: 名词

meritorious:/ˌmɛrɪˈtɔːriəs/
adj.
值得称赞的；有功的
deserving praise or reward.
Her meritorious service to the community was recognized with an award. 她对社区的功绩得到了表彰。
近义词: praiseworthy, commendable, laudable
反义词: demeritorious, undeserving
词性: 形容词
