madrigalism:/ˈmædrɪɡəlɪzəm/| n.|牧歌体; 牧歌风格; 牧歌式作品|A madrigalism is a device or technique characteristic of the madrigal, especially the use of word-painting.  It refers to the musical technique of depicting the text being sung in the music itself. |The composer's use of madrigalism brought the lyrics to life, with rising scales accompanying the word "ascend." 作曲家对牧歌体风格的运用使歌词栩栩如生，用上升的音阶伴奏“上升”一词。

The piece is full of madrigalisms, like the trill on "fluttering" and the low notes on "descending." 这首曲子充满了牧歌体的风格，例如“飘动”的颤音和“下降”的低音。|近义词：word-painting, text painting, musical illustration|反义词: None (No direct opposite exists)|词性:noun
