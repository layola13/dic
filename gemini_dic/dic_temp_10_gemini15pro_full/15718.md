balancing:/ˈbælənsɪŋ/| n. v. adj.|平衡，均衡，权衡|The act of keeping something in a steady position, or the act of comparing or assessing the importance of different things.|She lost her balancing and fell. 她失去了平衡，摔倒了。|近义词：stabilizing, equalizing, counterbalancing|反义词:destabilizing, upsetting|noun

balancing:/ˈbælənsɪŋ/| n. v. adj.|平衡的，均衡的|Being in a state of equilibrium.|The gymnast performed a balancing act on the beam. 体操运动员在平衡木上表演平衡动作。|近义词：stable, equal, even|反义词:unstable, unequal, uneven|adjective

balancing:/ˈbælənsɪŋ/| n. v. adj.|使平衡，使均衡|To make something steady or equal.|He was balancing a book on his head. 他头上顶着一本书保持平衡。|近义词：stabilize, equalize, counterbalance|反义词:destabilize, upset|verb

词源: From Old French "balancer" (to swing, sway), ultimately from Late Latin "bilanx" (having two scales), from "bis" (twice) + "lanx" (plate, dish, scale).
