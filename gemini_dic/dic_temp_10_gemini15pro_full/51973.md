diadelphia:/ˌdaɪəˈdɛlfiə/ | n. | 双体雄蕊；二强雄蕊 |  A class of plants in the Linnaean system, characterized by having the stamens united in two sets, or all but one united in one set. This arrangement is common in the pea family (Fabaceae). | Example:  Many members of the Fabaceae, such as clover, exhibit diadelphia. 许多豆科植物，例如三叶草，都表现出二强雄蕊。 | Example: Diadelphia is a characteristic feature used in plant classification. 二强雄蕊是植物分类中使用的特征。 | Synonym:  two-brotherhood (obsolete) | Antonym:  N/A (no direct antonym exists as it's a specific botanical term) | Noun
