gestalt:/ɡəˈʃtɑːlt, ɡəˈʃtɔːlt/ | n. |格式塔; 完形;  |An organized whole that is perceived as more than the sum of its parts.  A unified configuration having properties that cannot be derived from its parts. | Example 1: The painting lacked gestalt; it was just a collection of disparate images. 这幅画缺乏整体感；它只是一堆互不相关的图像的集合。

Example 2:  The therapist helped her client understand the gestalt of her childhood experiences. 治疗师帮助她的客户理解她童年经历的整体情况。 | 近义词: whole, totality, configuration, form, structure | 反义词: fragment, part, component | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Gestalt* "shape, form, figure," from Middle High German *gestalt*, from *gestalten* "to shape, form," from Old High German *gistaltan*, from *steltan* "to place, arrange, order."
