acquittals: /əˈkwɪt(ə)lz/
noun
无罪释放; 宣判无罪
a judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime with which the person has been charged.
The jury voted for acquittal. = 陪审团投票判决无罪。

The judge directed the jury to deliver a not guilty verdict, resulting in an acquittal. = 法官指示陪审团作出无罪判决，最终宣告无罪。

近义词: absolution, exoneration, discharge
反义词: conviction, condemnation
词性: noun (plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Anglo-French *aquitaunce, alteration of Old French aquitance (“payment, discharge of a debt”), from quite (“paid off, discharged”), from Latin quietus (“at rest, quiet”).
