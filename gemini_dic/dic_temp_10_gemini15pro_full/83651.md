haemorrhaged:/ˈhɛmərɪdʒd/| v. n.|出血；大量流失|To lose a large amount of something, especially money or skilled workers.  Also, to bleed profusely.|The company haemorrhaged money last year.  去年这家公司大量亏损。|The wound haemorrhaged profusely.  伤口大量出血。|Synonyms: bleed, lose, deplete|Antonyms: gain, accumulate|verb, noun

haemorrhage:/ˈhɛmərɪdʒ/| n. v.|出血；大量流失|The escape of blood from a ruptured blood vessel, especially when profuse. Also, a large loss of something, especially money or skilled workers.|The patient suffered a massive cerebral haemorrhage. 病人患有严重的脑出血。|The company is haemorrhaging skilled workers to its competitors.  这家公司的人才正在大量流向竞争对手。|Synonyms: bleeding, loss, outflow|Antonyms: inflow, gain|noun, verb
