anthropoidea:/ˌænθrəˈpɔɪdiə/ | n. |类人猿亚目|A suborder of primates traditionally comprising monkeys, apes, and humans, characterized by highly developed brains and complex social behavior.  Modern classifications tend to use Simiiformes instead, often including tarsiers within that group. |例句：Anthropoidea includes all primates more closely related to humans than to lemurs. 类人猿亚目包括所有比狐猴更接近人类的灵长类动物。

例句：The study of Anthropoidea is crucial to understanding human evolution.  对类人猿亚目的研究对于理解人类进化至关重要。|近义词：Simiiformes (more commonly used now), higher primates|反义词：Prosimii (an older, now less common grouping)|词性：noun
