intercedence:/ˌɪntərˈsɛdəns/| n. |说情；代祷；调解；斡旋|The act of pleading on behalf of another, especially to a person in authority or to a higher power; mediation. |例句：Through the intercedence of a mutual friend, the two warring parties finally agreed to talk.  通过一位共同朋友的斡旋，这两个交战方最终同意谈判。

例句：His intercedence with the governor secured a pardon for the convicted man. 他向州长求情，确保了被定罪者的赦免。|近义词：intervention, mediation, arbitration, intercession|反义词：noninterference, inaction|词性:noun


Etymology->

From Late Middle English *intercedence, from Late Latin *intercedentia, from Latin intercēdō (“intercede”), from inter (“between”) + cēdō (“go, yield, withdraw”).
