bounds:/baʊndz/| n.|界限，范围，限制；跳跃，弹回|A limit of an area or property. The act of leaping or jumping.|The ball went out of bounds.  球出界了。|synonyms: limits, confines, restrictions|antonyms: limitless, infinite|noun

bounds:/baʊndz/| v.|跳跃，弹回；给…划界，限制|To jump or spring; to set limits to something.|The dog bounded across the field.  狗蹦蹦跳跳地穿过田野。|synonyms: leap, jump, spring|antonyms: crawl, creep|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *boundes*, from Anglo-Norman *boundes*,  plural of *bounde* ("limit, boundary"),  from Old North French *bonde*, from Frankish *bunda*, from Proto-Germanic *bundō* (“sign, boundary”). Cognate with Old High German *bunta* (“sign, mark, standard, miracle, portent”), Old English *byndele* (“fetter, band”). Related to bind.
