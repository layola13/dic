harmonic:/hɑːrˈmɒnɪk/ |/hɑːrˈmɑːnɪk/| adj. n.| 和声的，谐波的；泛音；和声 | relating to harmony in music; a component frequency of an oscillation or wave.| The harmonic vibrations of the strings create a rich sound.  弦的谐波振动创造了丰富的声音。| synonyms: consonant, melodious, tuneful | antonyms: dissonant, inharmonic, discordant | adjective

harmonic:/hɑːrˈmɒnɪk/ |/hɑːrˈmɑːnɪk/| n. | 泛音；和声 | a component frequency of an oscillation or wave.| The guitarist played a series of harmonics. 吉他手演奏了一系列泛音。| synonyms: overtone, partial | antonyms: fundamental | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *harmonicus, from Ancient Greek ἁρμονικός (harmonikós, “harmonic, musical”), from ἁρμονία (harmonía, “harmony, concord of sounds”).
