mysticalness:/ˈmɪstɪkəlnəs/| n. |神秘性，神秘感|The quality of being mystical; mysteriousness.|The mysticalness of the ancient ruins captivated the explorers. 古老废墟的神秘感深深吸引了探险家们。|近义词：mystery, enigma, otherworldliness, spirituality |反义词：mundanity, ordinariness |词性: noun
Etymology: From mystical + -ness. Mystical derives from mystic, from Late Latin mysticus "of secret rites," from Greek mystikos "pertaining to secret rites, mystic," from mystes "one initiated into mysteries," related to myein "to close, shut," especially the eyes or mouth. The suffix -ness forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
