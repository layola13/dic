bond:/bɒnd/ (UK) /bɑːnd/ (US)| n. v. | 纽带，联系；债券；粘合；结合 |A force or feeling that unites people; a legal agreement or promise; a certificate issued by a government or company promising to pay back borrowed money with interest; to stick or join two surfaces together. |

例句：The bond between mother and child is very strong. 母子之间的联系非常牢固。

例句：He invested in government bonds. 他投资了政府债券。


近义词：tie, connection, link, attachment, adhesion, unite, join | 反义词：separation, division, detach | 词性：名词, 动词


bond:/bɒnd/ (UK) /bɑːnd/ (US)| v. | 粘合；结合 |To stick or join two surfaces together. |

例句：They bonded the two pieces of wood together with glue.  他们用胶水把两块木头粘合在一起。

例句：The experience bonded them together as friends. 这段经历使他们成为了朋友。


近义词：adhere, attach, connect, unite | 反义词：separate, divide, detach | 词性：动词
