catelectrotonic:/ˌkætɪˌlɛktrəˈtɒnɪk/
adjective
阴极电紧张的
Relating to the electrophysiological changes that occur in the region of a nerve or muscle fiber near the cathode during the passage of an electric current.  The changes involve a decrease in excitability and a hyperpolarization of the membrane.
The catelectrotonic potential change made the neuron less likely to fire. 阴极电紧张电位变化使神经元更不容易放电。
