licentiate: /laɪˈsɛnʃɪɪt/ /laɪˈsɛnʃiət/ | n. | 持有执照者；准许者；许可人 | A person who has been granted a license, especially in a profession or to practice a particular skill. |  The licentiate in medicine is allowed to practice under supervision.  这位持有医学执照的人可以在监督下行医。| synonym: licensee, graduate, diploma holder | antonym: amateur, unqualified | noun

licentiate: /laɪˈsɛnʃɪɪt/ /laɪˈsɛnʃiət/ | v. | 授予执照；许可 | To grant a license to someone. | The board will licentiate new doctors next month. 董事会将在下个月授权新的医生。 | synonym: authorize, certify, accredit | antonym: ban, prohibit, revoke | verb
