aggravatingly:/ˈæɡrəveɪtɪŋli/
adverb
令人恼火地，恼人地
making something worse, especially a problem or injury; annoying
The situation was made aggravatingly worse by the constant rain.  持续不断的雨使情况变得更加糟糕。
Her constant complaining was aggravatingly annoying. 她不停地抱怨，真是烦死人了。
近义词: annoyingly, irritatingly, exasperatingly
反义词: soothingly, calmingly, pleasantly
词性: adverb

aggravate: /ˈæɡrəveɪt/
verb
加重，恶化；使恼怒
(1) make (a problem, injury, or offense) worse or more serious.
(2) annoy or exasperate (someone), especially persistently.
From Latin aggravat- ‘made heavier’, from the verb aggravare, from ad- ‘to’ + gravare ‘to burden’, from gravis ‘heavy’.
The humid weather aggravated my eczema. 潮湿的天气加重了我的湿疹。
His constant interruptions aggravated her. 他不停地打断她，让她很恼火。
近义词: worsen, exacerbate, irritate, annoy
反义词: improve, alleviate, soothe, calm
词性: verb
