absolute:/ˈæbsəluːt/ (US) /ˈæbsəluːt/ (UK)| adj.|绝对的，完全的，无条件的，专制的|Complete; total; not limited in any way.  Free from any imperfection; perfect.  Not dependent on anything else.|例句：He ruled with absolute power. 他拥有绝对的权力。

The new car is an absolute dream to drive. 这辆新车开起来简直太棒了。|近义词：complete, total, utter, perfect, unconditional, unrestricted|反义词：relative, conditional, limited, restricted|词性:形容词

absolute:/ˈæbsəluːt/ (US) /ˈæbsəluːt/ (UK)| n.|绝对事物，绝对真理|Something that is absolute or considered to be absolute.|例句：Moral absolutes are difficult to define.  道德绝对主义很难定义。

There's no such thing as an absolute in this world.  这世上没有绝对的事情。|近义词：certainty|反义词：relativity|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *absolūtus, past participle of absolvere (“to absolve, acquit, finish”), from ab (“from, away from”) + solvere (“to loosen, release, solve”).

USAGE->
作为形容词:

限定性用法:
The judge made an absolute ruling. = 法官做出了绝对的裁决。
This is an absolute necessity for our survival. = 这对我们的生存来说是绝对必要的。


非限定性用法:
The story he told was absolute nonsense. = 他讲的故事完全是胡说八道。
Her performance was absolute perfection. = 她的表演绝对完美。


作为名词:

The philosopher discussed the concept of the absolute. = 这位哲学家讨论了绝对的概念。
He believed in the existence of moral absolutes. = 他相信道德绝对的存在。
