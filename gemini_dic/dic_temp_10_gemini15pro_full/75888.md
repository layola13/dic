fundamentalistic:/ˌfʌndəˌmɛnˈtəlˈɪstɪk/ | adj. | 基要主义的，原教旨主义的 | Relating to or adhering to fundamentalism, a religious movement characterized by a strict and literal interpretation of scripture. |  His fundamentalistic views often clashed with modern social norms. 他的基要主义观点经常与现代社会规范相冲突。 | The preacher's fundamentalistic interpretation of the text alienated many members of the congregation.  牧师对文本的原教旨主义解释疏远了许多会众。 | 近义词: orthodox, conservative, rigid | 反义词: liberal, progressive, modern | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From fundamental + -istic.  "Fundamental" traces back to the Latin "fundamentum," meaning "foundation."  The suffix "-istic" denotes adherence to a doctrine or system.
