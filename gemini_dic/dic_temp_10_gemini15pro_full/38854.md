confederate:/kənˈfɛdəreɪt/| adj. n. v.| 同盟的，联邦的；同盟者，同伙；结盟，联合|A person, group, or nation united with others for a common purpose, especially in a war or other struggle. |The Confederate states seceded from the Union.  邦联脱离了联邦。|Synonyms: ally, associate, partner, accomplice |Antonyms: enemy, opponent, adversary| adjective, noun, verb

confederate:/kənˈfɛdəreɪt/| v.| 结盟，联合|To unite for a common purpose, especially in a war or other struggle. |Several smaller tribes confederated to resist the invaders.  几个较小的部落联合起来抵抗入侵者。|Synonyms: ally, unite, join forces, combine |Antonyms: separate, divide, disunite| verb

confederacy:/kənˈfɛdərəsi/| n.| 联盟，邦联|A league or alliance, especially of states or nations. |The Southern Confederacy was formed in 1861.  南方邦联成立于1861年。|Synonyms: alliance, federation, league, union |Antonyms: division, separation, disunity| noun

confederation:/kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən/| n.| 联盟，邦联|The act of confederating; a league or alliance. |The articles of confederation established a weak central government.  邦联条例建立了一个软弱的中央政府。|Synonyms: alliance, federation, league, union |Antonyms: division, separation, disunity| noun

confeder:/kənˈfɜːr/ (British) /kənˈfər/ (American)| v.| 结盟，联合|To join in an alliance or confederacy. |The tribes confedered to fight against their common enemy. 各部落联合起来对抗共同的敌人。|Synonyms: ally, unite, join forces, combine |Antonyms: separate, divide, disunite| verb
