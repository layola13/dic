mantelletta:/ˌmæntəˈlɛtə/ | n. | 短披肩；披肩式长袍 | A short, sleeveless cape worn over a cassock by bishops, cardinals, and other prelates.  It is typically black silk, sometimes trimmed with red or purple. | During the ceremony, the cardinal's mantelletta flowed gracefully behind him. 在仪式中，红衣主教的短披肩优雅地飘在他身后。| Synonyms: cape, cloak, mozzetta | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *mantelletta*, diminutive of *mantella* (“mantle”), from Latin *mantellum* (“cloak”). Ultimately from Latin *manus* ("hand").
