enmeshing: /ɪnˈmɛʃɪŋ/ | adj. | 纠缠的，卷入的 | Involved in a complicated or confusing situation or relationship, often in a way that is difficult to escape. |  The two families were enmeshed in a bitter feud. 这两个家庭卷入了一场激烈的世仇。| 近义词：entangled, involved, caught up | 反义词: disentangled, separated, free | 词性: 形容词

enmesh: /ɪnˈmɛʃ/ | vt. | 使纠缠，卷入 | Cause to become entangled in something. | The fishing net enmeshed several large fish.  渔网缠住了几条大鱼。| 近义词：entangle, involve, trap | 反义词: disentangle, extricate, free | 词性: 动词


词源: From en- + mesh.  "En-" is a prefix meaning "in" or "into," and "mesh" refers to the openings in a net.
