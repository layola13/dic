nongrievousnesses:/ˌnɒnˈɡriːvəsnɪsɪz/ | n. | 无伤大雅，轻微 | The state or quality of not being grievous; lack of seriousness or severity. |  His nongrievousnesses were overlooked due to his overall good performance. 他的小错误由于他整体表现良好而被忽略了。 | Synonyms: trivialities, insignificances, minor details | Antonyms: severities, gravities, seriousnesses | noun


Etymology:
From non- (not) + grievousness + -es (plural suffix).  Grievous itself comes from Old French "grevos" meaning "heavy, painful, serious."
