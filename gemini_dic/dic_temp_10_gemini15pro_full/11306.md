ardipithecus:/ˌɑːrdɪˈpɪθɪkəs/| n. |始祖地猿;地猿属|An extinct primate genus, known from skeletal remains found in Ethiopia, believed to have lived 4.4 million years ago and considered a possible ancestor of humans.  It exhibits characteristics of both apes and humans.| Ardipithecus ramidus is a crucial fossil in understanding human evolution.  始祖地猿始祖种是理解人类进化的关键化石。|近义词：hominin, hominid |反义词: (No direct antonym for a genus name)|词性:noun
