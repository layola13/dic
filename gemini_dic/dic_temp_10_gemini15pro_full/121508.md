mislike:/mɪsˈlaɪk/| v. n.|不喜欢，厌恶|To dislike; to regard with disfavor.  |例句：She misliked the taste of the medicine. 她不喜欢这药的味道。

例句：He misliked being told what to do. 他不喜欢被指使做事。

近义词：dislike, detest, abhor, loathe|反义词:like, love, adore|词性:动词,名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *misliken*, from Old English *mislīcian* (“to displease, offend”), from *mis-* (prefix indicating something bad or wrong) + *līcian* (“to please, be pleasing”).  The noun form developed later.
