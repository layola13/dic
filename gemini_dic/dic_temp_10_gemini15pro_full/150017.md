palomedes: /ˌpæləˈmiːdiːz/ | n. |帕拉墨得斯|  A Greek hero in Greek mythology, famed for his cunning and inventiveness. He is credited with inventing the alphabet, numbers, weights and measures, dice, and the lighthouse, among other things. | Odysseus pretended to be insane to avoid going to the Trojan War, but Palamedes exposed his ruse. | 近义词: inventor, strategist | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "dupe" in the context of his outsmarting Odysseus) | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Latin *Palamedes*, from Ancient Greek *Παλαμήδης* (*Palamḗdēs*).
