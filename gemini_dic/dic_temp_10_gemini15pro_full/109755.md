levier: /ˈlɛviər/ (US), /ˈliːvə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 杠杆；控制杆；手段，途径 |A rigid bar resting on a pivot, used to help move a heavy or firmly fixed load with one end when pressure is applied to the other.|  I used a lever to pry open the crate. 我用杠杆撬开了板条箱。

levier: /ˈlɛviər/ (US), /ˈliːvə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 杠杆；控制杆；手段，途径 |A means of accomplishing; a tool: political leverage.|  He used his connections as leverage to get the job. 他利用自己的人脉关系作为筹码得到了这份工作。


Etymology: From Old French *levier, from lever "to raise," from Latin levāre "to lighten, raise," from levis "light."

Synonyms: crowbar, pry bar, handle, mechanism, tool, instrument, means, method, power, influence

Antonyms: disadvantage, weakness, hindrance

Word type: Noun
