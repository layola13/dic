drudger:/ˈdrʌdʒər/| n.|苦工；做单调乏味工作的人|a person who does hard, boring work|例句：He felt like a drudger, stuck in a dead-end job. 他感觉像个苦工，被困在一个没有前途的工作中。

例句：She's a real drudger and never complains about the workload. 她是一个真正的苦工，从不抱怨工作量。|近义词：laborer, worker, toiler, grind, plodder|反义词：loafer, idler|词性: Noun

Etymology:
From the verb "drudge," which comes from Middle English "druggen," of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old Norse "draga" (to draw, pull, drag).
