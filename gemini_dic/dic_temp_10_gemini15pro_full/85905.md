headhunts:/ˈhedˌhʌnts/| n.  v. |猎头|The act of seeking out and recruiting talented individuals for executive or other high-level positions, typically by a specialized recruiter or agency.|例句：The company is known for its aggressive headhunts of top talent. 这家公司以积极猎取顶尖人才而闻名。|近义词：recruit, poach, scout|反义词:dismiss, fire|词性:名词/动词

headhunt:/ˈhedˌhʌnt/| v. |猎头|To seek out and recruit (someone) for a position, typically a high-level or executive one.|例句：They were headhunted by a rival firm.  他们被一家竞争对手的公司挖走了。|近义词：recruit, poach, scout|反义词:dismiss, fire|词性:动词

词源:  "Headhunt" originated in the early 20th century, initially referring to the literal practice of taking and preserving the heads of enemies as trophies by some indigenous cultures.  The term was later adopted figuratively to describe the competitive recruitment of personnel.
