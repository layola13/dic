dragoonade:/ˌdræɡəˈneɪd/
n. |龙骑兵突袭；以武力压制|A sudden attack by dragoons.  The act of persecuting or oppressing by harsh or violent measures.|
例句：The town was subjected to a brutal dragoonade by the occupying forces.  这个城镇遭到了占领军的野蛮袭击。

例句：The government's dragoonade of the protesters led to international condemnation. 政府对示威者的武力镇压导致了国际社会的谴责。

近义词：attack, raid, assault, oppression, persecution
反义词：defense, protection, liberation
词性：名词

dragoonade:/ˌdræɡəˈneɪd/
vt. |以龙骑兵突袭；以武力压制|To attack with dragoons. To persecute or oppress by harsh or violent measures.|
例句：The army dragooned the rebellious villagers into submission. 军队以武力迫使反抗的村民屈服。

例句：The dictator dragooned the media into spreading his propaganda. 独裁者强迫媒体传播他的宣传。

近义词：attack, raid, assault, oppress, persecute
反义词：defend, protect, liberate
词性：动词

词源: From French *dragonner*, from *dragon*, "dragoon".  Originally referred to attacks by dragoons, mounted infantry who were often used for repressive purposes.  The meaning later broadened to encompass any harsh or violent repression.
