furor:/ˈfjʊərɔːr/ (US), /ˈfjʊərɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. |喧闹，狂热，轰动|An outbreak of public anger or excitement; a craze.|The furor over the politician's remarks lasted for weeks.  这位政治家言论引起的轰动持续了数周。|Synonyms: uproar, commotion, frenzy, rage, excitement, outburst, stir, brouhaha|Antonyms: calm, peace, tranquility|Noun

furor:/ˈfjʊərɔːr/ (US), /ˈfjʊərɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. |狂怒|A state of intense anger and agitation.|His face was red with furor.  他的脸因为狂怒而涨得通红。|Synonyms: rage, fury, wrath, ire, indignation|Antonyms: calmness, composure, serenity|Noun
