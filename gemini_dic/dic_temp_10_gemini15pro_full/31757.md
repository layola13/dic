cheat:/tʃiːt/
v.
欺骗，作弊
act dishonestly or unfairly in order to gain an advantage.
n.
欺骗，作弊，骗子
a person who acts dishonestly or unfairly in order to gain an advantage.
词源：From Middle English *cheten, from Old French escheat (“escheat, lapse, forfeiture”), from Late Latin excadere (“to fall due”), from Latin ex- + cadere (“to fall”).
