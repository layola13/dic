overwinding:/ˌōvərˈwaɪndɪŋ/ | n. | 过绕，超绕 | The act of winding something, such as a spring or a tape, too tightly or too far.  | The overwinding of the clock spring caused it to break. 时钟发条过绕导致它断裂。 | Overwinding of the winch cable can be dangerous.  绞盘钢缆过绕可能很危险。 | 近义词: over-winding | 反义词: underwinding | noun


overwind:/ˌōvərˈwaɪnd/ | vt. | 过绕，超绕 | To wind something, such as a spring or a tape, too tightly or too far. |  I overwound the film in my camera and it jammed. 我把相机里的胶卷绕得太紧，卡住了。| He overwound the fishing reel and the line snapped. 他把钓鱼竿的绕线轮绕得太紧，线断了。| 近义词: over-wind | 反义词: underwind | verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From over- + wind (v.).  The prefix "over-" indicates an excess or going beyond a limit.  The verb "wind" refers to the act of turning something repeatedly around a central point or axis.
