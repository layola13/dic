abominable:/əˈbɒmɪnəbl/ (US) /əˈbɑːmɪnəbl/| adj.|令人憎恶的；讨厌的；糟糕的|Inspiring moral revulsion; disgusting.  Exceptionally bad or unpleasant.|The weather was abominable, raining and freezing all day. 天气糟透了，整天又下雨又冰冻。

The food at the hotel was absolutely abominable.  这家酒店的食物糟透了。|近义词：detestable, loathsome, abhorrent, dreadful, awful, terrible|反义词：delightful, pleasant, wonderful, excellent|词性：形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin abominabilis "detestable," from Latin abominari "deprecate as an ill omen, abhor, detest," from ab- "away from" (see ab-) + ominari "forebode, predict," related to omen (see omen).

USAGE->
Attributive Use (修饰名词):
The abominable snowman is a legendary creature.  可怕的雪人是传说中的生物。
They lived in abominable conditions. 他们生活在恶劣的环境中。

Predicative Use (作表语):
The smell was abominable. 气味令人作呕。
His behavior was truly abominable. 他的行为实在令人憎恶。

With "absolutely," "utterly," "truly" (与程度副词连用):
The traffic was absolutely abominable. 交通拥堵不堪。
His manners were utterly abominable. 他的举止极其粗鲁。
