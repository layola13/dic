institutist: /ɪnstɪˈtjuːtɪst/ | n. | 建制派；因循守旧者 | A person who favors and adheres to established institutions, customs, or practices, often resisting change. | Example 1: The institutist blocked any attempts to reform the outdated system.  建制派阻挠了任何改革过时制度的尝试。

Example 2:  She was labeled an institutist for her unwavering support of traditional teaching methods. 她因坚定支持传统教学方法而被称为因循守旧者。 | Near Synonyms: conservative, traditionalist, conformist | Antonyms: reformer, radical, revolutionary | Noun
