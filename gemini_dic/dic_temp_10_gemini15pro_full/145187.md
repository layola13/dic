overawe:/ˌoʊvərˈɔː/ | v. | 使敬畏；使慑服 | to impress someone so much that they feel respectful and slightly afraid |  The children were overawed by the presence of the Queen. 孩子们对女王的驾临感到敬畏。 | synonyms: intimidate, daunt, awe | antonyms: embolden, encourage | verb

overawe:/ˌəʊvərˈɔː/ | v. | 使敬畏；使慑服 | to impress someone so much that they feel respectful and slightly afraid | His vast knowledge of the subject overawed me. 他对这门学科的渊博知识使我敬畏。 | synonyms: cow, subdue | antonyms: reassure, comfort | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *oferāgan "to frighten excessively," from ofer- "over" + āgan "to possess, own."  The sense shifted from "frighten" to "impress, inspire awe" over time.
