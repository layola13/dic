misers:/ˈmaɪzərz/| n. |吝啬鬼，守财奴|A person who hoards wealth and spends as little money as possible.|Scrooge McDuck is a famous fictional miser. 史克鲁奇·麦克鸭是著名的虚构吝啬鬼。|The old misers hid their gold in the basement. 这些老吝啬鬼把他们的黄金藏在地下室里。| synonyms: cheapskate, skinflint, tightwad, penny-pincher| antonyms: spendthrift, philanthropist| noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *misere* "misery, poverty, wretchedness," from Latin *miseria* "wretchedness, misery, misfortune, trouble," from *miser* "wretched, unfortunate." The sense shifted from "wretched person" to "greedy person" in Middle English.
