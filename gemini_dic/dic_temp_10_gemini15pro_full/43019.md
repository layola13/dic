cowboyed:/ˈkaʊbɔɪd/
verb
以牛仔的方式行事；粗暴地对待；强迫
acted like a cowboy; treated roughly or forced into doing something
The project manager cowboyed the team into working overtime. 项目经理强迫团队加班。
近义词: bullied, coerced, pressured
反义词: cooperated, assisted, facilitated
词性:v.

Etymology: From "cowboy" (n.) + "-ed" (suffix forming past tense and past participles of verbs). "Cowboy" originally referred to a cattle herder of the American West, but later acquired connotations of recklessness, independence, and sometimes roughness.
