deadbeat:/ˈdɛdˌbiːt/| n. adj.|赖账不还的人，游手好闲的人；拖欠债务的，不尽责任的|A person who deliberately avoids paying debts or fulfilling responsibilities.|He's a real deadbeat who never pays his rent on time. 他真是个赖账的，从来不按时付房租。|Synonyms: defaulter, slacker, freeloader|Antonyms: responsible person, contributor|noun

deadbeat:/ˈdɛdˌbiːt/| adj.|拖欠债务的，不尽责任的|Failing to meet financial or other obligations.|She's stuck with a deadbeat ex-husband who refuses to pay child support. 她被一个不负责任的前夫缠住了，他拒绝支付孩子的抚养费。|Synonyms: delinquent, irresponsible|Antonyms: responsible, dependable|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "dead" (exhausted, inactive) + "beat" (exhausted from walking). Originally referred to a worn-out shoe or a person exhausted from walking, later evolving to mean someone who avoids work or paying debts.
