hypocritic:/ˌhɪpəˈkrɪtɪk/ | adj. | 伪善的，虚伪的 | Behaving in a way that suggests one has higher moral standards than is actually the case. |  He condemned violence, but his own actions were hypocritical. 他谴责暴力，但他自己的行为却很虚伪。 |  sanctimonious, two-faced, insincere, deceitful | genuine, sincere, honest | adjective

hypocrite:/ˈhɪpəkrɪt/ | n. | 伪君子 | A person who indulges in hypocrisy. |  He's a hypocrite – he says one thing and does another. 他是个伪君子——言行不一。 |  phony, charlatan, fraud, imposter | saint | noun


Etymology:
From Late Middle English: ypocrite, via Old French ypocrite, from ecclesiastical Latin hypocrita, from Greek hypokritēs ‘actor’, from hypokrinesthai ‘act a part’, from hypo- ‘under’ + krinein ‘decide, judge’.
