hunker-down:/ˈhʌŋkər daʊn/ (US) /ˈhʌŋkə daʊn/ (UK)| phrasal verb | 蹲下；采取预防措施；准备应对困难 | To settle in for a period of confinement, inactivity, or siege. To prepare oneself for a difficult or unpleasant situation. | The family hunkered down in the basement during the tornado.  家人在龙卷风期间躲在地下室里。

The company hunkered down for a long recession.  公司为长期衰退做好了准备。 |近义词：settle in, dig in, batten down the hatches, brace oneself, prepare | 反义词: venture out, go out, face (without preparation) | 词性: 短语动词

hunker:/ˈhʌŋkər/ (US) /ˈhʌŋkə/ (UK) | verb | 蹲下；盘腿而坐 | To squat or crouch close to the ground. | He hunkered down beside the fire to warm himself. 他蹲在火堆旁取暖。| 近义词：squat, crouch, hunch | 反义词: stand, rise | 词性: 动词

down:/daʊn/ | adverb, preposition, adjective | 向下；沿着；下降；情绪低落 |  From a higher to a lower place or position; along; indicating a lower level or amount; depressed or sad. |  The ball rolled down the hill. 球滚下山坡。 / He walked down the street. 他沿着街道走。/ Turn the volume down. 把音量调低。/  She's feeling down today. 她今天心情不好。 | 近义词：(depending on context) below, lower, beneath, under, sad, depressed | 反义词: (depending on context) up, above, higher, happy, cheerful | 词性: 副词，介词，形容词
