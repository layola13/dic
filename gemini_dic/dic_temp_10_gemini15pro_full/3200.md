affectationist:/əˌfɛkˈteɪʃənɪst/
n.
做作的人；装腔作势的人
A person who behaves in an artificial way to impress people.
Her constant use of overly complex vocabulary made her seem like an affectationist. 她总是使用过于复杂的词汇，让她看起来像个装腔作势的人。
synonyms: poseur, pretender, phony, artificial person
antonyms: genuine person, natural person, sincere person
noun


affectationist:/əˌfɛkˈteɪʃənɪst/
adj.
做作的；装腔作势的
Behaving in an artificial way to impress people.
His affectationist mannerisms annoyed many of his colleagues. 他做作的举止惹恼了许多同事。
synonyms: affected, pretentious, artificial, phony
antonyms: genuine, natural, sincere, unaffected
adjective


Etymology:
From the late 16th century.  From French *affectationiste*, from *affectation* + *-iste*.  Ultimately from Latin *affectātiō* (“a striving after, aiming at”),  from *affectāre* (“to strive after, aim at”), frequentative of *afficere* (“to do something to, influence”), from *ad-* (“to”) + *facere* (“to do, make”).
