flobert:/floʊˈbɛər/ | n. |弗洛贝特步枪;弗洛贝特弹|A type of small-caliber rimfire cartridge, and the rifles chambered for it, typically used for target shooting and small game hunting.  It's known for its low report. | He used a Flobert rifle for target practice in his backyard. 他在后院用弗洛贝特步枪进行射击练习。 |Synonym:  salon rifle, garden gun |Antonym:  high-powered rifle |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Named after its inventor, Louis Flobert, a French gunsmith, in the mid-19th century.
