granted: /ˈɡrɑːntɪd/ (US) /ˈɡrɑːntɪd/ (UK)| adj. | 授予的；准许的；理所当然的 | given or allowed, especially officially; assumed to be true or expected |例句：He was granted permission to leave early. 他被允许提前离开。|近义词：allowed, permitted, assumed, acknowledged |反义词: denied, refused, withdrawn | 词性:形容词

granted: /ˈɡrɑːntɪd/ (US) /ˈɡrɑːntɪd/ (UK)| v. | 授予；准许；承认 | agree to give or allow (something requested) to someone; admit or agree that something is true |例句：The court granted him bail. 法院准许他保释。|近义词：allow, permit, concede, acknowledge |反义词: deny, refuse, reject | 词性:动词

词源: From Old French *granter (Modern French garantir "to guarantee"), from grantir, garantir "to warrant," of Germanic origin.
