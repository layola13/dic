majesties: /ˈmædʒɪstiz/ | n. |陛下（复数）; 雄伟，庄严（复数）|  The plural form of "majesty," referring to more than one person holding a royal title or to multiple instances of grandeur or magnificence. | Their Majesties, the King and Queen, will attend the ceremony. 国王和王后陛下将出席仪式。|  The majesties of the mountains awed the climbers.  群山的雄伟使登山者敬畏。| 近义词: highnesses, grandeurs, splendors | 反义词: insignificance, commonness | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "majeste" and directly from Latin "maiestas"  meaning "greatness, dignity, grandeur, honor, excellence," from "maius" meaning "greater."
