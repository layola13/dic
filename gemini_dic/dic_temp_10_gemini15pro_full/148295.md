oversoftening:/ˌoʊvərˈsɒfnɪŋ/ /ˌoʊvərˈsɑːfnɪŋ/| n.|过度软化|The oversoftening of consonants is a common feature of some dialects.|辅音的过度软化是某些方言的共同特征。|Example: The oversoftening of the 't' sound in 'butter' can make it sound like 'budder'.  'butter' 中 't' 音的过度软化会使其听起来像 'budder'。

Example:  Oversoftening of the 'g' sound can make it disappear altogether. 'g' 音的过度软化会使其完全消失。 |近义词: weakening, lenition|反义词: hardening, fortition|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + softening.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much". "Softening" refers to the process of making something softer. In phonetics, it refers to a lenition process where sounds become weaker.
