bunker mentality:/ˈbʌŋkər mɛnˈtælɪti/ | n. |掩体心态，闭关自守的心态|A defensive, isolated, and often paranoid attitude characterized by a refusal to consider outside perspectives or accept criticism.  It often arises in situations of perceived threat or crisis, where individuals or groups prioritize self-preservation and security above all else.|The company developed a bunker mentality after the scandal, refusing to engage with the media.|近义词:siege mentality, defensiveness, parochialism, insularity|反义词:openness, receptiveness, inclusiveness|词性:noun

bunker mentalities:/ˈbʌŋkər mɛnˈtælɪtiz/ | n. |掩体心态（复数）|Plural form of "bunker mentality," referring to multiple instances of this defensive and isolated attitude.|Several departments within the organization exhibited bunker mentalities, hindering collaboration and innovation.|近义词:siege mentalities, defensiveness, parochialism, insularity|反义词:openness, receptiveness, inclusiveness|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "bunker" (a fortified underground shelter) + "mentality" (a way of thinking). The term gained prominence during wartime, referring to the psychological state of those sheltering in bunkers.  It later broadened to describe a similar mindset in other contexts.
