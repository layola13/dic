borderline:/ˈbɔːrdərlaɪn/ /ˈbɔːrdərˌlaɪn/| adj. n.|边缘的，临界的；界限|A borderline condition is one that is between two different conditions, so that it is difficult to say which condition it is. |Her blood pressure was borderline.|近义词：marginal, ambiguous, indeterminate|反义词：definite, clear, unambiguous|词性:形容词

borderline:/ˈbɔːrdərlaɪn/ /ˈbɔːrdərˌlaɪn/| n.|边界线，界线|The dividing line or location between two areas.|The borderline between the two countries is heavily guarded.|近义词：boundary, border, frontier|反义词：center, interior|词性:名词

Etymology:
From border + line.  "Border" traces back to Old French "bordure" meaning "edge, border," ultimately from Germanic roots. "Line" comes from Old French "ligne," from Latin "linea" meaning "linen thread, string, line."
