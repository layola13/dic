arianism:/ˈɛəriənɪzəm/| n. |阿里乌斯教，阿里乌主义|A heresy denying the divinity of Christ, originating with Arius, a presbyter of Alexandria in the 4th century.  It maintains that Jesus was created by God and was therefore not divine in the same sense as God the Father.|Arius’s teachings, later condemned as heresy, formed the basis of Arianism.|近义词：heresy, heterodoxy|反义词：orthodoxy, Trinitarianism|词性:noun
