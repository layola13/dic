hamfatter:/ˈhæmfætər/| n.|过时的演员；拙劣的表演者；喜欢炫耀的人|A ham actor; a showy, often incompetent performer; someone who overacts or seeks attention.|He was such a hamfatter, always trying to steal the scene. 他真是个爱出风头的家伙，总是想抢戏。|近义词：show-off, exhibitionist, ham actor, grandstander|反义词：understated performer, wallflower|词性:noun

词源：The origin of "hamfatter" is uncertain, but it likely emerged in the late 19th or early 20th century in American theatrical slang.  "Ham" meaning a bad actor was already in use, and "fatter" might be a humorous intensifier or a reference to the exaggerated gestures often associated with such performers.
