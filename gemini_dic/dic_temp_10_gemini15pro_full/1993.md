acts out:/ækts aʊt/| phrasal verb | 付诸行动；将…表演出来；用行动表现出（尤指感情） | To express (an emotion, fantasy, etc) by acting it out, as in play, drama, or pantomime.  | The child acted out his anger by throwing his toys.  这个孩子通过扔玩具来发泄他的愤怒。

He acted out the story for the children. 他为孩子们表演了这个故事。 |  express, perform, demonstrate, portray | suppress, repress, internalize | verb phrase


ETYMOLOGY->
"act" comes from the Latin "actus," meaning "a doing, a driving, an impulse."
"out" comes from Old English "ūt," meaning "out, outside, outwards."  The combination creates the sense of bringing something from inside to the outside through action.


USAGE->

He acts out whenever he's frustrated. = 每当他感到沮丧时，他就会表现出来。

一般过去时:
