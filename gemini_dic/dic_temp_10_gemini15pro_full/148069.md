oversaturate: /ˌoʊvərˈsætʃəreɪt/ /ˌəʊvəˈsætʃəreɪt/ | vt. | 过饱和，使过度饱和 | To cause something to contain more of a substance than it can normally hold; to supply something with too much of something. | The market is oversaturated with cheap products.  市场上充斥着廉价产品。

The colors in the photograph were oversaturated, making it look unnatural. 照片中的颜色过度饱和，看起来不自然。| 近义词: saturate, flood, glut, overwhelm | 反义词: deplete, undersupply | 词性: 动词

oversaturated: /ˌoʊvərˈsætʃəreɪtɪd/ /ˌəʊvəˈsætʃəreɪtɪd/ | adj. | 过饱和的，过度饱和的 | Containing more of a substance than can normally be absorbed or held; supplied with too much of something. | The oversaturated job market makes it difficult for recent graduates to find work.  过度饱和的就业市场让应届毕业生很难找到工作。

The colors in the painting were oversaturated, giving it a garish look. 这幅画的颜色过于饱和，显得过于花哨。 | 近义词: saturated, flooded, glutted | 反义词: depleted, undersupplied | 词性: 形容词


词源: over- (prefix meaning "excessively") + saturate (from Latin saturatus, past participle of saturare "to fill full, soak," from satur "full")
