book-smart:/ˈbʊk smɑːrt/
adjective
书呆子式的聪明，学习好但缺乏实践经验
Having a lot of academic knowledge learned from books, but lacking practical skills or experience.
He's book-smart but lacks common sense. 他很书呆子，但缺乏常识。
近义词：academic, studious, erudite
反义词：street-smart, practical, savvy
词性：adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"book" + "smart".  "Book" refers to academic knowledge derived from books, while "smart" denotes intelligence or cleverness.  The combination signifies intelligence primarily gained from reading and studying, rather than practical experience.
