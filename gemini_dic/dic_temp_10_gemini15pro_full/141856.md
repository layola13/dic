orchestrated: /ˈɔːrkɪstreɪtɪd/ | adj. | 精心安排的，策划的 | Arranged or manipulated, especially in a skillful and elaborate way. | The entire event was carefully orchestrated to create a sense of excitement. 整个活动都经过精心策划，以营造一种兴奋感。 |  The marketing campaign was brilliantly orchestrated. 这场营销活动策划得非常出色。 |  近义词：arranged, planned, organized, coordinated, staged, managed, engineered | 反义词：improvised, unplanned, spontaneous | 词性: 形容词

orchestrate: /ˈɔːrkɪstreɪt/ | vt. | 精心安排，策划 | To arrange or direct the elements of something complex, like a large event or a campaign. | The CEO orchestrated the merger of the two companies. 首席执行官精心策划了两家公司的合并。 | The politician orchestrated a series of public appearances to boost his popularity.  这位政治家策划了一系列公开露面以提高他的知名度。 | 近义词：arrange, plan, organize, coordinate, stage, manage, engineer | 反义词：improvise | 词性: 动词

词源：From French *orchestrer*, from *orchestre* "orchestra," from Greek *orkhēstra* "place for dancing, orchestra."  Originally referred to the arrangement of music for an orchestra, and later extended to mean arranging any complex undertaking.
