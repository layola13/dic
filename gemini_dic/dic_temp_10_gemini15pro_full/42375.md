counterbalancer:/ˌkaʊntərˈbælənsər/ | n. | 平衡器，平衡物，抗衡力 | A counterbalancer is something that offsets or balances the effect of something else. | The heavy counterbalancer on the crane prevented it from tipping over.  起重机上的重型平衡器防止其倾覆。 | synonyms: equalizer, stabilizer, compensator | antonyms: destabilizer, agitator | noun

counterbalance:/ˌkaʊntərˈbæləns/ | n. | 平衡，抵消 | A counterbalance is something that offsets or balances the effect of something else. | The success of the new product was a counterbalance to the losses in the other divisions. 新产品的成功抵消了其他部门的损失。| synonyms: offset, compensation | antonyms:  aggravation, exacerbation | noun

counterbalance: /ˌkaʊntərˈbæləns/ | vt. | 平衡，抵消，抗衡 | To counterbalance means to offset or balance the effect of something. | The government's spending cuts were counterbalanced by tax increases. 政府的削减开支被增税所抵消。 | synonyms: offset, compensate, neutralize | antonyms:  aggravate, exacerbate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + balance.  "Counter-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposite." "Balance" comes from Old French "balance," ultimately from Late Latin "bilanx" (having two scales), from "bi-" (two) + "lanx" (plate, dish, scale).
