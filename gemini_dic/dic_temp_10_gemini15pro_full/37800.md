compellingly:/kəmˈpɛlɪŋli/ | adv. | 引人注目地，令人信服地 | In a manner that evokes interest, attention, or admiration, often in a way that is difficult to resist; persuasively. |  She argued her case compellingly, swaying the jury to her side. 她令人信服地陈述了她的案件，说服了陪审团站在她这边。|  convincingly, persuasively, forcefully | weakly, unconvincingly | adverb

Etymology:
From compel + -ing + -ly.  Compel comes from the Latin compellere "to drive together, force, constrain," from com- "together" + pellere "to drive."
