lithochromatics:/ˌlɪθəkrəˈmætɪks/ | n. |彩色石印术；彩色石印画|The art or process of printing in color from stone; a chromolithograph. | Lithochromatics became popular in the 19th century for producing vibrant illustrations. 彩色石印术在 19 世纪因其能制作鲜艳的插图而变得流行。 | synonyms: chromolithography, color lithography | antonyms: monochrome printing | noun


Etymology->

From  *litho-* (Greek *lithos* "stone") + *chromatics* (from Greek *chrōma* "color").  Essentially meaning "color from stone."
