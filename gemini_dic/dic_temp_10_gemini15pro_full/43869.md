cress:/krɛs/| n.|水芹，西洋菜|A small, pungent-tasting plant of the mustard family, usually eaten raw in salads.|She added some cress to her sandwich for extra flavor. 她往三明治里加了些水芹，以增加风味。|Synonyms: watercress, garden cress|Antonyms: None|Noun

Etymology:
From Old English *kærs, from Proto-Germanic *krasjon, from Proto-Indo-European *grō-, *gʰer- (“to grow”).  Cognate with Dutch kers, German Kresse.
