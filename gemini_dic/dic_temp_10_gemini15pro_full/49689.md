demiurgic:/ˌdemiˈɜːrdʒɪk/| adj.|造物主的，宇宙创造的|Relating to a demiurge, a divine craftsman or creator of the world.  Having the power to shape or create the world. |例句：The artist felt a demiurgic impulse to transform the raw materials into a masterpiece.  艺术家感到一种创造世界的冲动，要把原材料变成杰作。

例句：The novel explores the demiurgic power of language to shape our reality.  这部小说探讨了语言塑造我们现实的创造力。|近义词：creative, formative, generative, cosmogonic|反义词：destructive, ruinous|词性: adjective

demiurge:/ˈdemiˌɜːrdʒ/| n.|造物主|A being responsible for the creation of the physical world in Platonism and Gnosticism.  A powerful creative force or personality.|例句：Gnostics believed in a demiurge who was distinct from the ultimate, unknowable God.  诺斯替主义者相信一个与最终的、不可知的上帝不同的造物主。

例句：The architect was hailed as a demiurge for his innovative and transformative designs.  这位建筑师因其创新和变革性的设计而被誉为造物主。|近义词：creator, maker, author|反义词：destroyer|词性: noun
