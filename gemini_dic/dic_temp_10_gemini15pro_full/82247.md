groundbreaking:/ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ/| adj.|开创性的；突破性的|Introducing a significant change or innovation; pioneering.|例句：The scientist made a groundbreaking discovery in the field of medicine.  这位科学家在医学领域做出了突破性的发现。

例句：The groundbreaking ceremony for the new hospital was attended by many dignitaries. 新医院的奠基仪式有很多显要人物参加。|近义词：innovative, pioneering, revolutionary, seminal, original, novel|反义词：unoriginal, conventional, traditional|词性:形容词

groundbreaking:/ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ/| n.|破土动工；奠基仪式|The ceremony of breaking the ground to mark the beginning of construction.|例句：The groundbreaking for the new stadium is scheduled for next month. 新体育场的破土动工仪式定于下个月举行。

例句：The groundbreaking was attended by the mayor and other city officials.  市长和其他市政官员出席了奠基仪式。|近义词：commencement, inauguration, initiation|反义词：completion, conclusion, finish|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "ground" (land) + "breaking" (the act of breaking).  Originally referred to the literal act of breaking ground for construction, but later evolved to mean making a significant new start or innovation.
