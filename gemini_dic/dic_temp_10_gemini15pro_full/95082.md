inalienability:/ɪnˌeɪliənəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 不可剥夺性 | The state or quality of being inalienable; unable to be taken away or given away by the possessor. |  The inalienability of human rights is a cornerstone of modern democracy. 人权的不可剥夺性是现代民主的基石。

The declaration stressed the inalienability of the right to free speech.  宣言强调了言论自由权的不可剥夺性。 | 近义词：nontransferability,  inviolability | 反义词：alienability, transferability | 词性: noun


inalienable:/ɪnˈeɪliənəbəl/ | adj. | 不可剥夺的 | Unable to be taken away from or given away by the possessor. |  Everyone is endowed with certain inalienable rights. 每个人都被赋予某些不可剥夺的权利。

Freedom of speech is an inalienable right. 言论自由是一项不可剥夺的权利。 | 近义词：absolute, inherent, inviolable, nontransferable | 反义词：alienable, transferable | 词性: adjective


词源:
From in- (not) + alienable (capable of being alienated or transferred to another)  + -ity (suffix forming nouns of quality or state) and -able (suffix forming adjectives meaning "capable of being"). Ultimately from Latin *alienabilis, from alienare "to estrange, transfer," from alienus "belonging to another."
