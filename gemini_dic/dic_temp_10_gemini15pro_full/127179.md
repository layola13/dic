naturalizers:/ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪzərz/
n.|归化者；入籍者；移植者|People who become citizens of a country they were not born in.  Those who introduce a plant or animal species to a new environment.|Many naturalizers come to this country seeking better opportunities. 许多归化者来到这个国家寻求更好的机会。|Synonyms:  citizens, immigrants, newcomers|Antonyms: natives, aborigines|noun

naturalizer:/ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪzər/
n.|归化者；入籍者；移植者|A person who grants citizenship to a foreigner. A person or thing that naturalizes something, such as a plant or animal. |The judge acted as a naturalizer for the new citizens. 法官担任新公民的入籍法官。|Synonyms:  immigrant, settler, newcomer|Antonyms: native, aborigine|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "naturalize," which comes from the Late Latin "naturalizare," meaning "to make natural."  This in turn is derived from the Latin "naturalis," meaning "natural," from "natura," meaning "nature."
