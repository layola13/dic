outlaw:/ˈaʊtlɔː/| n. vt. |歹徒，亡命徒；宣布…为不合法|A person who has broken the law and is hiding to avoid punishment; to make something illegal or unacceptable.|The sheriff was determined to hunt down the outlaws. 警长决心追捕这些亡命之徒。|Synonyms: criminal, bandit, fugitive, exile, proscribe, ban|Antonyms: law-abiding citizen, legalize, permit|noun, verb


outlaw:/ˈaʊtlɔː/| vt. |宣布…为不合法|To make something illegal or unacceptable.|The government outlawed smoking in public places. 政府禁止在公共场所吸烟。|Synonyms: prohibit, forbid, ban, proscribe|Antonyms: legalize, permit, allow|verb

词源:
