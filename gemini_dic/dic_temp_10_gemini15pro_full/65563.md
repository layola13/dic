eutychianism:/juːˈtɪkiənɪzəm/| n.|优提克斯主义|A Christological heresy formulated by Eutyches, asserting that the human nature of Christ was absorbed by his divine nature, so that Christ had only one nature after the Incarnation.|Eutychianism was condemned as a heresy at the Council of Chalcedon.|Near Synonyms: Monophysitism|Antonyms: Dyophysitism|noun
