co-op:/ˈkoʊ ˌɑːp/ (US), /ˈkəʊ ɒp/ (UK)| n.|合作社，合作商店，合作公寓|A business or organization owned and run jointly by its members, who share the profits or benefits.  A type of apartment building owned and managed by a corporation in which shares are sold to individuals, who then have the right to occupy a unit in the building.|例句：I buy my groceries at the local co-op. 我在当地的合作社买食品杂货。

例句：She lives in a co-op on the Upper West Side. 她住在上西区的一套合作公寓里。|近义词：cooperative, collective|反义词:corporation, conglomerate|词性:noun


co-op:/ˈkoʊ ɑːp/ (US), /kəʊˈɒp/ (UK)| v.|合作|To work together on a joint project.|例句：We co-oped on a science project in school. 我们在学校合作完成了一个科学项目。

例句：They co-oped to write the screenplay. 他们合作编写了剧本。|近义词：collaborate, cooperate|反义词:compete|词性:verb


词源:Shortening of cooperative.
