collectivised:/kəˈlɛktɪvaɪzd/ | adj. | 集体化的，集体所有制的 | brought under collective ownership or control, especially by the state.  | Example: The collectivised farms struggled to meet production targets. 集体化农场难以达到生产目标。

Example:  The government collectivised the land, taking it from private owners. 政府将土地集体化，从私人业主手中夺取。| 近义词: communal, shared, centralized | 反义词: privatized, individual, independent | 词性: adjective

collectivise:/kəˈlɛktɪvaɪz/ | v. |  集体化，使集体化 |  to organize (something) on the basis of collective ownership or control, especially by the state. | Example: The government's plan to collectivise agriculture met with resistance. 政府的农业集体化计划遭到抵制。

Example: They collectivised the farms in the region. 他们将该地区的农场集体化了。 | 近义词: communalize, nationalize, socialize | 反义词: privatize, individualize | 词性: verb


词源: From the noun "collective" + "-ize" (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become").  "Collective" ultimately comes from the Latin "colligere" (to gather together).
