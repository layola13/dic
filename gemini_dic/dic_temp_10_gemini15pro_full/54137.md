disenfranchiser:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzər/ | n. |剥夺权利者；褫夺公民权者| someone who deprives others of the right to vote or other privileges |The disenfranchiers sought to manipulate the election by suppressing the vote in certain districts. 剥夺权利者试图通过压制某些地区的投票来操纵选举。| The company acted as a disenfranchiser by denying its workers the right to unionize.  这家公司剥夺了工人组织工会的权利，从而扮演了剥夺权利者的角色。 | synonyms: oppressor, tyrant, suppressor | antonyms: liberator, emancipator | noun

disenfranchise:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ | vt. |剥夺权利；剥夺公民权 | to deprive of a privilege, right, or power, especially the right to vote |  Many citizens were disenfranchised due to strict voter ID laws. 许多公民由于严格的选民身份证法而被剥夺了选举权。 |  Felons are often disenfranchised and cannot vote. 重罪犯通常被剥夺选举权，不能投票。 | synonyms: dispossess, deprive, marginalize | antonyms: empower, enfranchise | verb
