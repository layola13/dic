breastbone: /ˈbrɛstbəʊn/ (UK) /ˈbrɛstboʊn/ (US) | n. | 胸骨 | The breastbone, also called the sternum, is a long flat bone located in the center of the chest. It connects to the ribs via cartilage and forms the front of the rib cage, thus helping to protect the heart, lungs, and major blood vessels from injury. | The doctor checked his breastbone for any fractures. 医生检查了他的胸骨是否有骨折。 |  He hit the steering wheel hard, fracturing his breastbone. 他重重地撞上了方向盘，导致胸骨骨折。| Synonym: sternum | Antonym: N/A | Noun
