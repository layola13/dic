boondoggling:/ˈbuːndɑːɡlɪŋ/ | n. | 闲混；浪费时间；做不必要的工作 | Engaging in trivial or unproductive activity, often busywork, typically to appear busy or to justify one's continued employment. |  Example 1: The committee spent weeks boondoggling instead of addressing the real issues. 委员会花了几个星期的时间闲混，而不是解决真正的问题。

Example 2:  He accused the government of boondoggling with taxpayers' money. 他指责政府浪费纳税人的钱。 | 近义词：loafing, idling, dawdling, time-wasting | 反义词：working, producing, achieving | 词性: 名词

词源：
From a plaited leather cord or thong, often with decorative knots, originally made by Boy Scouts. Later applied to useless or wasteful work, especially government-funded projects, possibly influenced by the perceived frivolity of the original craft.
来源于童子军制作的一种编织皮绳或皮带，通常带有装饰性绳结。后来被用来指无用或浪费的工作，尤指政府资助的项目，可能受到最初工艺被认为轻浮的影响。
