omnivorousnesses:/ˌɒmˈnɪvərəsnəsɪz/ | n. | 杂食性 | The state or quality of being omnivorous; the habit or practice of eating both animal and plant foods. |  Pigs are known for their omnivorousnesses, consuming a wide variety of food sources. 猪以其杂食性而闻名，它们吃各种各样的食物。

The evolutionary advantages of omnivorousnesses are evident in many species. 杂食性的进化优势在许多物种中都很明显。 | 近义词: omnivory | 反义词: herbivory, carnivory | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *omnivorus* (all-devouring) + *-ness* (suffix forming nouns of state or quality).  *Omnivorus* is formed from *omni-* (all) + *vorare* (to devour).
