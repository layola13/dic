hellraiser:/ˈhelˌreɪzər/| n.|地狱制造者；捣乱者；麻烦制造者|Someone who causes trouble or disruption; a person who acts in a way that is considered shocking or extreme. |例句：He was a real hellraiser in his youth, always getting into fights. 他年轻时是个十足的捣乱鬼，总是惹事生非。

例句：The new kid in school is a bit of a hellraiser. 学校里的新来的孩子有点儿像个麻烦制造者。|近义词：troublemaker, rabble-rouser, mischief-maker|反义词：peacemaker, conciliator|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hell" + "raiser," literally meaning someone who "raises hell," causing commotion or disturbance.  The word gained popularity after the release of the horror film "Hellraiser" in 1987.
