anesthetizers:/əˈnɛsθəˌtaɪzərz/
|n.|麻醉师；麻醉剂|Agents or substances that cause anesthesia, a temporary loss of sensation or awareness.|The anesthetizers carefully monitored the patient's vital signs during the surgery. 麻醉师在手术期间仔细监测病人的生命体征。|Synonyms: anesthetics, narcotics|Antonyms: stimulants, analeptics|noun

anesthetizer:/əˈnɛsθəˌtaɪzər/
|n.|麻醉师|A person who administers anesthetics.|The anesthetizer explained the procedure to the patient before the operation. 麻醉师在手术前向病人解释了手术过程。|Synonyms: anesthesiologist, anesthetist|Antonyms: None|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From an- (not) + aesthesis (sensation) + -ize (to make) + -er (one who does).  Ultimately from Greek roots.
