oesophagus:/ɪˈsɒfəɡəs/ (US) /iːˈsɒfəɡəs/ (UK)| n. |食道|The tube that connects the throat to the stomach.|Food passes down the oesophagus to the stomach.|Synonyms: gullet |Antonyms: None|Noun
oesophaguses:/ɪˈsɒfəɡəsaɪz/ (US) /iːˈsɒfəɡəsaɪz/ (UK)| n. |食道（复数）|Plural form of oesophagus, referring to more than one oesophagus.|Rarely used, as most people only have one oesophagus.  Surgeons or anatomists might use it when discussing multiple specimens or in cases of anatomical abnormalities.|Synonyms: gullets|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *oesophagus, from Greek *oisophagos, from oisein (to carry, especially food) + phagein (to eat).
