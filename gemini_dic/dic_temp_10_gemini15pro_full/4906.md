alimonied: /ˈælɪˌmoʊnid/
adjective
已付赡养费的；接受赡养费的
Having been granted alimony (spousal support) by a court.  Usually used to describe the recipient of the alimony.
She was an alimonied wife, free to pursue her own interests. 她是一位已获赡养费的妻子，可以自由地追求自己的兴趣。
He was an alimonied husband, an unusual but increasingly common situation. 他是一位已获赡养费的丈夫，这种情况不常见，但越来越普遍。
近义词: supported (in a financial sense)
反义词: unsupported, impoverished (in a financial sense)
词性: adjective

词源: From "alimony" + "-ed". "Alimony" comes from Old French "alimoine," from Latin "alimonia," meaning "sustenance," from "alere" meaning "to nourish."
