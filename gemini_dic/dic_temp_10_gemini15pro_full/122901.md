molossine:/ˈmɒləsaɪn/ (US) /ˈmɒləsiːn/ (UK)| adj. n.|摩洛斯式的，摩洛斯诗行的；摩洛斯诗行|Of or relating to a molossus (a metrical foot of three long syllables).  A line of verse consisting of molossuses.  Sometimes used more loosely to refer to a line of verse with many long syllables or a heavily stressed rhythm.|

例句：
The poet experimented with molossine rhythms in his latest work.
这位诗人在他的最新作品中尝试了摩洛斯式的节奏。

The molossine line creates a sense of slow, deliberate movement.
摩洛斯诗行营造出一种缓慢而从容的运动感。

近义词：heavy, ponderous (referring to rhythm)|反义词：light, quick (referring to rhythm)|词性：adjective, noun
