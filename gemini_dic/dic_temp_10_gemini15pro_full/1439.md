ace-up-one's-sleeve:/eɪs ʌp wʌnz sliːv/| idiom | 藏有秘密武器；有锦囊妙计；有应急的妙计 |  Having a secret advantage or resource that can be used when needed.  It refers to a card player concealing an ace up their sleeve to cheat, giving them an advantage. | He had an ace up his sleeve for the negotiation. 他在谈判中藏有秘密武器。

He always seems to have an ace up his sleeve when he's in trouble.  当他遇到麻烦时，他似乎总是有锦囊妙计。 | 近义词: secret weapon, hidden advantage, trump card | 反义词: disadvantage, vulnerability | 词性: idiom


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She always has an ace up her sleeve. = 她总是有锦囊妙计。
He keeps an ace up his sleeve for emergencies. = 他留有锦囊妙计以备不时之需。

一般过去时:
He won the game because he had an ace up his sleeve. = 他赢得了比赛，因为他藏有秘密武器。
She knew she had an ace up her sleeve going into the meeting. = 她知道自己在参加会议时胸有成竹。

现在完成时:
He has always had an ace up his sleeve in business dealings. = 他在商业 dealings 中总是有锦囊妙计。
She has never revealed what her ace up her sleeve is. = 她从未透露过她的秘密武器是什么。

过去完成时:
He had had an ace up his sleeve all along, but hadn't needed to use it. = 他一直藏有秘密武器，但一直不需要使用它。
She hadn't realized he had an ace up his sleeve until the very end. = 她直到最后才意识到他藏有秘密武器。

将来时:
I'll have an ace up my sleeve if they try to challenge me. = 如果他们试图挑战我，我会有锦囊妙计。
She'll need an ace up her sleeve to win this competition. = 她需要锦囊妙计才能赢得这场比赛。


PHRASE->
have an ace up one's sleeve = 藏有秘密武器/有锦囊妙计
keep an ace up one's sleeve =  留有锦囊妙计以备不时之需
play one's ace =  使出绝招/王牌
an ace in the hole =  最后的王牌/杀手锏 (slightly different connotation, emphasizing a hidden advantage kept in reserve)
with an ace up one's sleeve =  胸有成竹地


ETYMOLOGY->
The phrase originates from card games, particularly cheating.  Gamblers would conceal an ace up their sleeve to introduce it into the game at a crucial moment, ensuring victory. This practice became a metaphor for having a hidden advantage.
