confirmed: /kənˈfɜːrmd/ (US) /kənˈfɜːmd/ (UK)
| adj. | 已确认的，已证实的 | Verified, proven, established as true or valid. | The confirmed cases of COVID-19 are rising. 已确认的新冠肺炎病例正在上升。| Synonyms: verified, validated, proven, established, corroborated | Antonyms: unconfirmed, unverified, disputed, denied | adjective

confirmed: /kənˈfɜːrmd/ (US) /kənˈfɜːmd/ (UK)
| v. | 确认，证实 | To establish the truth or validity of something. | The lab results confirmed his diagnosis. 实验室结果证实了他的诊断。| Synonyms: verify, validate, prove, establish, corroborate | Antonyms: deny, refute, disprove, invalidate | verb

词源: Late Middle English: past participle of *confirmen, from Old French confermer, from Latin confirmare, from con- ‘together’ + firmare ‘strengthen’.
