clock: /klɒk/ (UK) /klɑːk/ (US) | n. v. |时钟，计时器；记录，测速|A device for measuring and showing time, typically one showing the time by hands on a round dial or by digital display. To measure the time taken by someone or something to do something, especially to run a race or cover a distance. |例句：The clock on the wall chimed midnight. 墙上的钟敲响了午夜。|近义词：timepiece, chronometer, timer |反义词: None |词性: noun, verb

clock: /klɒk/ (UK) /klɑːk/ (US) | v. |记录，测速|To measure the time taken by someone or something to do something, especially to run a race or cover a distance. |例句：He clocked a time of 3 minutes in the 1500-meter race. 他在1500米比赛中跑了3分钟。|近义词：time, record, measure |反义词: None |词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English clok, clokke, from Middle Dutch clocke, klokke (“bell”), from Medieval Latin clocca (“bell”), of imitative origin.
