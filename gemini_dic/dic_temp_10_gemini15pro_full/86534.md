held-down:/held daʊn/ | adj. |被压制的，被控制的，地位低下的| suppressed, repressed, controlled, in a low position; often referring to a job with low pay and little opportunity for advancement |  He felt held-down by his demanding boss. 他觉得被苛刻的老板压制着。 |  oppressed, suppressed, restrained | liberated, free, empowered | adjective

held-down:/held daʊn/ | adj. |(按键)被按住的 | pressed and held in position (referring to a key or button)|  Hold down the shift key to capitalize the letter. 按住shift键将字母大写。 | pressed, depressed | released | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "hold" (Old English *healdan*) meaning "to grasp, keep, possess" and the adverb/preposition "down" (Old English *dūne*) meaning "from a higher to a lower place."  The combination "held down" literally describes something being kept in a lower position.
