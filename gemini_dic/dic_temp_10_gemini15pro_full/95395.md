inchoate:/ɪnˈkoʊeɪt/ | adj. | 未完成的，刚开始的，不成熟的 | just begun and so not fully formed or developed; rudimentary | The project is still in its inchoate stages.  这个项目仍处于初期阶段。| Example:  His inchoate ideas for the novel were jotted down in a notebook. 他把小说雏形的想法草草记在笔记本上。 | Synonyms: rudimentary, incipient, nascent, embryonic, undeveloped | Antonyms: developed, mature, complete, finished | adjective

inchoate:/ɪnˈkoʊeɪt/ | adj. | (法律)犯罪未遂的 | (of an offence, such as conspiracy) anticipating or preparatory to a further criminal act. |  The police charged him with inchoate offences related to the planned robbery.  警察指控他犯有与计划抢劫相关的未遂罪。| Example: Inchoate crimes are often difficult to prove.  未遂罪通常很难证明。 | Synonyms: attempted, incomplete, unfinished | Antonyms: completed, finished, executed | adjective
