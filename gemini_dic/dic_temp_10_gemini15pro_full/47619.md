debenture: /dɪˈbɛntʃər/ | n. | 债券；信用债券；无担保债券 | A type of bond or other debt instrument that is not secured by physical assets or collateral. Debentures are backed only by the general creditworthiness and reputation of the issuer. | Example 1: The company issued debentures to raise capital for its expansion project.  这家公司发行债券为其扩张项目筹集资金。

Example 2: Investors are hesitant to buy debentures from companies with poor credit ratings.  投资者不愿购买信用评级较差的公司的债券。 | Synonym: bond, loan, obligation | Antonym: asset, security | noun


debentured: /dɪˈbɛntʃərd/ | adj. | 负债的；以债券担保的 | Secured by or relating to a debenture.  | Example 1:  The debentured debt is a significant portion of the company's liabilities.  债券负债是公司负债的重要组成部分。

Example 2: The company's assets were debentured to secure the loan.  公司的资产被用作债券担保以获得贷款。 | Synonym: bonded, indebted, secured | Antonym: unencumbered, unsecured | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French debenture ("something owed"), from Latin dēbēre ("to owe").
