goose-step:/ˈɡuːsˌstep/| n. |正步，鹅步|A stiff-legged marching step used especially by troops in some European armies, in which the legs are swung high and straight forward with each stride.|The soldiers marched in goose-step.|Synonyms: lock-step, parade step|Antonyms: natural gait|noun

goose-step:/ˈɡuːsˌstep/| v. |正步走，鹅步走|To march in goose-step.|The troops were ordered to goose-step across the parade ground.|Synonyms: march stiffly, parade|Antonyms: walk naturally|verb
