diphthongation:/dɪfθɒŋɡeɪʃən/ |/daɪfθɔŋɡeɪʃən/| n. |双元音化|The process by which a single vowel sound changes into a diphthong. |The Great Vowel Shift in English involved a significant amount of diphthongation.|Synonym: vowel breaking|Antonym: monophthongization|noun
Etymology->
From Late Latin *diphthongatio*, from Ancient Greek διφθόγγησις (diphthóngēsis), from διφθόγγος (diphthóngos, “diphthong”).
