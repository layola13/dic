adamantine:/ˌæd.əˈmæn.taɪn/ /ˌæd.əˈmæn.ˌtin/
adjective
坚硬无比的；固执的
Made of or having the quality of adamant; unyielding.  Figuratively, it describes something extremely hard, inflexible, or unbreakable.
The knight's adamantine armor deflected the blow. 骑士坚硬无比的盔甲挡开了攻击。

His adamantine will could not be broken. 他坚定的意志无法动摇。

近义词:  unbreakable, impenetrable, inflexible, resolute, firm, unwavering
反义词:  breakable, fragile, yielding, flexible, pliable
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *adamantinus, from Latin adamas, adamantis (“adamant, hardest steel, diamond”), from Ancient Greek ἀδάμας (adámas, “untamed, invincible, diamond”), from ἀ- (a-, “not”) + δαμάζω (damázō, “I tame, I subdue”).

USAGE->
Attributive adjective:
He possessed an adamantine spirit. = 他拥有坚韧不拔的精神。
The fortress had adamantine walls. = 这座堡垒有着坚不可摧的城墙。

Predicative adjective (less common):
Their resolve was adamantine. = 他们的决心是坚定不移的。
Her faith remained adamantine throughout the ordeal. = 在整个考验过程中，她的信念始终坚定不移。
