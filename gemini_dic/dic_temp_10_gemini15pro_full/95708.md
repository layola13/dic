incongruent:/ɪnˈkɒŋɡruənt/ (UK) /ɪnˈkɑːŋɡruənt/ (US)| adj.|不一致的，不协调的，不相称的|Not in harmony or keeping with the surroundings or other aspects of something.|Her bright pink dress was incongruent with the somber atmosphere of the funeral. 她鲜艳的粉红色连衣裙与葬礼的肃穆气氛格格不入。|The modern sculpture looked incongruent in the ancient temple. 现代雕塑在古老的寺庙里显得格格不入。|近义词：incompatible, inconsistent, discordant, clashing, unsuitable|反义词：congruent, consistent, harmonious, compatible, suitable|词性:形容词

incongruence:/ɪnˈkɒŋɡruəns/ (UK) /ɪnˈkɑːŋɡruəns/ (US)| n.|不一致，不协调，不相称|The state of not being in harmony or keeping with the surroundings or other aspects of something.|There was a marked incongruence between his words and his actions. 他的言行之间存在着明显的矛盾。|The incongruence of the building's modern design with the historical neighborhood sparked controversy. 这栋建筑的现代设计与历史街区的格格不入引发了争议。|近义词：incompatibility, inconsistency, discordance, clash|反义词：congruence, consistency, harmony, compatibility|词性:名词
