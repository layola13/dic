fractionalism:/ˈfrækʃənəlɪzəm/| n. |派系主义，分裂主义|The state of being broken or divided into factions.  The tendency to form factions or splinter groups.|The fractionalism within the party threatened to derail their election campaign.  党内的派系主义有可能破坏他们的竞选活动。|factionalism, division, fragmentation, schism, disunity|unity, solidarity, cohesion, harmony|noun
Etymology->From fraction + -alism, ultimately from Latin *frangere "to break."
