break-in:/ˈbreɪkɪn/ (US), /ˈbreɪkɪn/ (UK)| n.|非法闯入，破门而入|An illegal entry into a building, especially by force, for the purpose of theft.|例句：There was a break-in at the jewelry store last night. 昨晚珠宝店发生了一起入室盗窃案。

例句：Police are investigating a series of break-ins in the neighborhood. 警方正在调查该社区发生的一系列入室盗窃案。

近义词：burglary, robbery, intrusion|反义词:|词性:noun

break in:/breɪk ˈɪn/ (US), /breɪk ˈɪn/ (UK)| v.|闯入，打断，训练，使适应|To enter a building illegally, usually by force.  Also, to interrupt someone or something.  To train someone or something, especially a horse, to do a particular job or activity.  To wear something, especially shoes, until they are comfortable.|例句：Thieves broke in and stole the computers. 小偷闯入并偷走了电脑。

例句：She broke in on their conversation to announce the news. 她打断他们的谈话宣布了这个消息。


近义词：interject, interrupt, intrude, barge in (for interrupting); burgle, burglarize (for illegal entry)|反义词:|词性:verb
