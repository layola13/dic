geofencing:/ˈdʒiːoʊˌfɛnsɪŋ/
n.|地理围栏，地理围栏技术|A virtual perimeter for a real-world geographic area. A geofence could be dynamically generated—as in a radius around a store or point location. Or a geofence can be a predefined set of boundaries, like school attendance zones or neighborhood boundaries used for local promotions.|Example: The store used geofencing to send coupons to customers' smartphones when they were nearby.|Synonyms:  geo-fencing, virtual perimeter, location-based service, proximity marketing|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
