caesium:/ˈsiːziəm/ (US), /ˈsiːziəm/ (UK)| n.|铯|A soft, silver-white metallic element of the alkali metal group, used in photoelectric cells and atomic clocks. It is the most electropositive and most alkaline element.|Caesium is used in atomic clocks because of its precise resonant frequency. 铯因其精确的共振频率而用于原子钟。

Caesium reacts violently with water. 铯与水剧烈反应。|近义词:cesium (US spelling)|反义词:None|词性:noun

From the Latin word "caesius," meaning "sky-blue," because of the blue lines in its spectrum.
