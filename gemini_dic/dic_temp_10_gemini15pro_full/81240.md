grandstand: /ˈɡrændˌstænd/ (US), /ˈɡrændstænd/ (UK) | n. | 露天看台；哗众取宠的言行 | a large structure at a race track, stadium, etc., with rows of seats for spectators | The fans cheered from the grandstands.  球迷们在露天看台上欢呼。| synonyms: bleachers, stands, gallery | antonyms: field, track, court | noun

grandstand: /ˈɡrændˌstænd/ (US), /ˈɡrændstænd/ (UK) | v. | 哗众取宠；作秀 | to behave in a way intended to impress people watching | He accused the opposition of grandstanding. 他指责反对派哗众取宠。| synonyms: show off, showboat | antonyms: be humble, be modest | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "grand" (large, impressive) + "stand" (a raised platform for viewing).  Originally referred to a large, impressive standing area for spectators.
