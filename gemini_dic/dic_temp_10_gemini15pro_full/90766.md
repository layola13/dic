humorism:/ˈhjuːmərɪzəm/ | n. | 体液学说，体液病理说 |A system of medicine detailing the makeup and workings of the human body, adopted by the ancient Greeks and Romans and further developed by Islamic and Western European physicians throughout the Middle Ages, that posits that an excess or deficiency of any of four distinct bodily fluids in a person—known as humors or humours—directly influences their temperament and health. | Hippocrates believed in the theory of humorism. 希波克拉底相信体液学说。 | The doctor explained the ancient theory of humorism. 医生解释了古代的体液学说。 | 近义词: humoralism, humoral pathology | 反义词: modern medicine, germ theory | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *humorismus, from Latin humor (“fluid, moisture”), from Proto-Indo-European *suH- (“to flow”).
