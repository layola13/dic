hold-the-fort:/ˌhoʊld ðə ˈfɔːrt/| idiom |坚守阵地，代管一段时间|To take responsibility for something while someone else is absent.  It originates from military terminology, referring to defending a fort. |"I'm going out for a bit, can you hold the fort?"  "我出去一会儿，你能帮我照看一下吗？"|近义词：take charge, look after, cover for, mind the shop|反义词:abandon, desert|phrase
