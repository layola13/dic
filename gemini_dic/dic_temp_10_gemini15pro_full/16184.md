bankrupt: /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (US) /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (UK)| adj. n. vt.|破产的；无力偿还债务的；完全缺乏的；使破产；使丧失|Lacking in a particular quality or resource. Declared in law as unable to pay outstanding debts.|例句：The company went bankrupt after the economic downturn.  这家公司在经济衰退后破产了。|近义词：insolvent, broke, ruined|反义词: solvent, affluent|词性:形容词

bankrupt: /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (US) /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (UK)| n.|破产者|A person who has been declared in law as unable to pay outstanding debts.|例句：He was declared a bankrupt after his business failed. 他在生意失败后被宣布破产。|近义词：insolvent debtor|反义词: creditor|词性:名词

bankrupt: /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (US) /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (UK)| vt.|使破产；使丧失|To make someone bankrupt or deprive them of something.|例句：The lawsuit bankrupted him. 这场官司让他破产了。|近义词：ruin, impoverish|反义词: enrich|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *banqueroute*, from Italian *bancarotta*, literally "broken bench," from *banco* "bench, moneychanger's table" + *rotto* "broken."  Moneychangers in medieval Italy conducted their business on benches or tables.  When a moneychanger could no longer meet his obligations, his bench was broken, signifying his bankruptcy.
