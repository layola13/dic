halle:/ˈhælə/ (US), /ˈhɔːlə/ (UK)| n.|大厅，市场|A large building or covered area for meetings, exhibitions, or other events, especially one used regularly for a particular purpose.  A market (especially a covered one).|例句：The conference was held in the main halle.  会议在主大厅举行。

例句：We bought fresh produce at the halle. 我们在市场买了新鲜农产品。|近义词：hall, market, auditorium, marketplace|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "small room" or "outdoor stall" in specific contexts)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle High German *halle, from Old High German halla, from Proto-Germanic *hallō. Cognate with Old English heall (“hall”), Old Saxon halla, Old Norse hall, Old Frisian hal (“hall”), Dutch hal (“hall, corridor”), German Halle (“hall, Halle (city)”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kol- (“cover, hide”). Related to cell.
