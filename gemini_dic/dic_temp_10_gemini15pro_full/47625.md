debilitating:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 使人衰弱的；削弱的 |  Making someone or something physically weak. |  The debilitating illness left him unable to work.  这种使人衰弱的疾病使他无法工作。 | synonyms: weakening, enervating, incapacitating, crippling | antonyms: strengthening, invigorating, empowering | adjective

debilitant:/dɪˈbɪlɪtənt/ | n. | 削弱物；使衰弱的东西 | Something that weakens or makes someone or something less strong or effective. | Stress can be a major debilitant. 压力可能是一个主要的削弱因素。 | synonyms: weakening agent, depressant | antonyms: tonic, restorative, stimulant | noun


debilitate:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪt/ | v. | 使衰弱；削弱 |  To make someone or something physically weak.  | The virus debilitated him for weeks. 这种病毒使他虚弱了好几个星期。| synonyms: weaken, enfeeble, enervate, sap | antonyms: strengthen, invigorate, empower | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dēbilitāre, from Latin dēbilis "weak" (from de- "from, away" + habilis "able") + -itāre (frequentative suffix).
