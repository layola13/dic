allotropy:/əˈlɒtrəpi/ |n.|同素异形体|The existence of an element in two or more different physical forms.  The property of some chemical elements to exist in two or more different forms, in the same physical state, known as allotropes of these elements.|Diamond and graphite are allotropes of carbon. 钻石和石墨是碳的同素异形体。|Synonyms: polymorphism, isomerism |Antonyms: N/A |noun
