enwind:/ɪnˈwaɪnd/ | v. |缠绕，盘绕；使卷入|To wrap or coil something around something else; to involve someone in something complicated or difficult. |The serpent enwinds its prey.  蛇缠绕着它的猎物。 |The young lovers were enwined in each other's arms.  这对年轻的恋人紧紧相拥。|近义词：twine, coil, entangle, involve|反义词：unwind, disentangle|词性:动词

词源:
From en- + wind (verb).  "En-" is a prefix meaning "in" or "into," intensifying the meaning of the verb.  "Wind" comes from Old English "windan," meaning "to turn, twist, or wind."
