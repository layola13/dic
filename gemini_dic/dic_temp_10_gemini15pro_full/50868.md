desensitised: /diːˈsɛnsɪtaɪzd/ | adj. | 脱敏的，失去敏感性的 |  Made less sensitive or responsive to something, especially something unpleasant or shocking.  The process of desensitization involves repeated exposure to a stimulus until it no longer elicits a strong emotional or physiological response. | After years of working in the emergency room, the doctor had become desensitised to the sight of blood. 多年在急诊室工作后，这位医生对看到血已经麻木了。| 近义词: numb, indifferent, hardened | 反义词: sensitive, responsive, susceptible | 词性: 形容词

desensitize: /diːˈsɛnsɪtaɪz/ | vt. | 使脱敏，使不再敏感 | To make someone less sensitive to something.  This is often done through repeated exposure to the stimulus. | Therapists use various techniques to desensitize patients to their phobias. 治疗师使用各种技术使患者对他们的恐惧症脱敏。| 近义词: numb, dull, harden | 反义词: sensitize, sharpen | 词性: 动词

词源: de- (Latin prefix meaning "down from, away from") + sense + -itize (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become")
