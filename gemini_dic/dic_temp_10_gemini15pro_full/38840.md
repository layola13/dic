confarreation:/ˌkɒnfəˈreɪʃən/ |n.|结婚仪式，共食婚|An ancient Roman form of marriage, the most sacred of the three forms, involving a ceremony in which the bride and groom shared a cake of spelt (far) and offered it to Jupiter.|The confarreation ceremony was rarely used even in ancient Rome, reserved primarily for patricians.  罗马的共食婚仪式即使在古代也很少使用，主要用于贵族。|Confarreation was a highly ritualized ceremony. 共食婚是一个高度仪式化的仪式。|Synonyms: wedding, matrimony, nuptials|Antonyms: divorce, annulment|noun
