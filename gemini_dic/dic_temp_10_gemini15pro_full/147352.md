overmystifying: /ˌoʊvərˈmɪstɪfaɪɪŋ/
overmystifying | adj. |  使过于神秘化；故弄玄虚 | Making something more mysterious or confusing than it needs to be, often deliberately. | The author's overmystifying prose obscured the simple message of the book. 作者故弄玄虚的散文掩盖了这本书简单的信息。|近义词: obfuscating, perplexing, bewildering |反义词: clarifying, simplifying, elucidating |词性: 形容词

词源:
"over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "mystify" (verb meaning "to make mysterious or obscure"). "Mystify" comes from the French "mystifier," from "mystique" ("mystic") and "-fier" (from Latin "-ficare," meaning "to make").
