derivationist: /ˌdɛrɪˈveɪʃənɪst/
n.
派生主义者，衍生学家
A linguist who emphasizes the importance of derivational morphology in the study of language.  A derivationist focuses on how words are formed from other words, through processes like affixation.
The derivationist argued that the meaning of the word "unhappiness" could be fully understood by analyzing its component parts: "un-", "happy", and "-ness".  派生主义者认为，“unhappiness”一词的含义可以通过分析其组成部分“un-”“happy”和“-ness”来完全理解。
The derivationist traced the etymology of the word back to its Proto-Indo-European roots.  这位派生学家追溯了这个词的词源到其原始印欧语词根。
近义词: morphologist, etymologist
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps a linguist with a different focus, such as a syntactician)
词性: noun
