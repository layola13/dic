consummate:/ˈkɑːnsəmət/ /ˈkɒnsəmeɪt/ | v. adj.|完成；使完美；圆房|To complete or fulfill; to make perfect, especially by the final act or ceremony; to make (a marriage or relationship) complete by having sexual intercourse.|例句：They consummated their business deal with a handshake. 他们以握手完成了交易。|近义词：complete, finish, fulfill, perfect, conclude, clinch|反义词: begin, start, initiate, abandon|词性:动词

consummate:/ˈkɑːnsəmət/ /ˈkɒnsəmeɪt/ | adj.|完美的；技艺高超的；圆满的|Extremely skilled and accomplished; complete and perfect in every respect.|例句：He is a consummate artist. 他是一位技艺高超的艺术家。|近义词：perfect, complete, supreme, ultimate, accomplished, masterful|反义词: imperfect, incomplete, flawed, amateurish|词性:形容词


词源:  Late Middle English: from Latin consummat- ‘summed up, completed’, from the verb consummare, from con- ‘together’ + summa ‘sum, total’.
