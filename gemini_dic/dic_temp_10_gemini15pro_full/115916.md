maroon:/məˈruːn/| n. adj. v.|栗色; 逃亡黑奴; (尤指船只失事后)被困者|To leave (someone) trapped and alone in an inaccessible place, especially without any means of escape.|例句：The sailors were marooned on a desert island.  水手们被困在一个荒岛上。|近义词：strand, abandon|反义词: rescue, save |词性:名词,形容词,动词


maroon (color): Derived from the French word "marron," meaning "chestnut," ultimately from the Italian "marrone."

maroon (verb/stranded):  Thought to be derived from the Spanish word "cimarrón," referring to escaped slaves or cattle in the West Indies.  These individuals often fled to remote or inaccessible areas, becoming effectively "marooned."
