book-binder: /ˈbʊkˌbaɪndər/ | n. | 装订工；书籍装订商 | A person who binds books, especially one who does so as a trade.  A bookbinder repairs and restores old books, and also creates new bindings for them. |  The bookbinder carefully stitched the pages together.  装订工仔细地把书页缝合在一起。

The bookbinder restored the antique Bible to its former glory.  这位装订工将这本古董圣经修复到昔日的辉煌。 | 近义词: binder | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "book destroyer" but that's hyperbolic) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bokbindere*, equivalent to book +‎ binder.  The word "bind" comes from Old English "bindan," meaning "to tie, fasten."
