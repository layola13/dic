marinism:/ˈmærɪnɪzəm/ | n. | 海事主义，马里尼派诗风 |  A style of poetry and literature characterized by elaborate and often strained conceits and metaphors, especially those involving the sea and ships, popular in 17th-century Italy and named after the Italian poet Giambattista Marino.  |  The poem was full of marinism, with metaphors comparing the lover's tears to pearls and his sighs to the wind. 这首诗充满了海事主义，用隐喻将情人的眼泪比作珍珠，将他的叹息比作风。 | 近义词:  conceitism, Gongorism | 反义词:  simplicity, naturalism | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *Marinismo*, derived from the name of the poet Giambattista Marino (1569-1625).
