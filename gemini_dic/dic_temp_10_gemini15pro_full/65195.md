ethylamine:/ˈɛθəlˌæmiːn/ |n.|乙胺|A colorless, volatile, flammable liquid with a strong ammoniacal odor, derived from ammonia by replacing one hydrogen atom with an ethyl group. It is used in the manufacture of various chemicals, including dyes and pharmaceuticals.|Ethylamine is used as a precursor in the synthesis of certain herbicides. 乙胺被用作合成某些除草剂的前体。|Ethylamine is corrosive and should be handled with care. 乙胺具有腐蚀性，应小心处理。|Synonyms: aminoethane|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
