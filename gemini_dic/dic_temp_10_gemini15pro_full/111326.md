live-in:/ˈlɪvɪn/| adj.|同居的，住家的|Living together in the same dwelling, especially as an unmarried couple.  Used to describe someone who lives in the place where they work. |例句：They are a live-in couple.  他们是一对同居的情侣。

例句：She hired a live-in nanny. 她雇了一个住家保姆。|近义词：resident, cohabiting|反义词：non-resident, commuting|词性:形容词

live-in:/ˈlɪvɪn/| n.|同居者，住家雇员|A person who lives in the place where they work. A person who cohabits. |例句：The live-in helps with the children. 住家佣人帮忙照顾孩子。

例句：He's her live-in. 他是她的同居伴侣。 |近义词：resident, roommate, partner|反义词：commuter, non-resident|词性:名词


词源:  From "live" (verb) + "in" (adverb).  The adjective sense developed from the verbal phrase "to live in".
