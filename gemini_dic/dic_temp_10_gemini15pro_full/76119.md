furtive:/ˈfɜːrtɪv/ (US) /ˈfɜːtɪv/ (UK)| adj. |偷偷摸摸的；鬼鬼祟祟的；躲躲闪闪的|Attempting to avoid notice or attention, typically because of guilt or a belief that discovery would lead to trouble; secretive.|He cast a furtive glance at the woman sitting across from him. 他偷偷地瞥了一眼坐在他对面的女人。|The thief made a furtive attempt to steal the jewels. 小偷偷偷地试图偷走珠宝。|近义词：stealthy, secretive, clandestine, surreptitious|反义词：open, honest, forthright|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Late Middle English: from Old French furtif, -ive, from Latin furtivus ‘stolen’, from furtum ‘theft’.
