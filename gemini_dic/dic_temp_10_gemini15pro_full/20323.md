bishoped:/ˈbɪʃəpt/
|v.|任命为主教；用象控制（对手的象）|To appoint someone as a bishop, especially in chess.  In chess, it refers to a situation where a bishop attacks an opponent's bishop which cannot move without being captured.|He was bishopeed in 1998. 他在1998年被任命为主教。|His bishop was bishopeed by my knight. 他的象被我的马控制住了。|Synonyms: consecrated, ordained (for religious sense), control, attack (for chess sense)|Antonyms: defrocked, unfrocked (for religious sense), defend, protect (for chess sense)
|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "bishop" (n.) + "-ed" (suffix forming past participles and adjectives). The word "bishop" comes from Old English "bisceop," from Late Latin "biscopus," and ultimately from Greek "episkopos" meaning "overseer."
