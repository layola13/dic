outjuggles:/ˌaʊtˈdʒʌɡəlz/
|v.|胜过，以杂耍技艺胜过|To surpass someone in juggling skills or, metaphorically, in clever manipulation or maneuvering.|He outjuggled his opponent in the competition, amazing the crowd with his complex tricks. 他在比赛中以杂耍技艺胜过了对手，用他复杂的技巧惊艳了全场。|Synonyms: outmaneuver, outsmart, outwit|Antonyms: be outdone, be outsmarted|verb

Etymology:
"out-" (prefix indicating exceeding or surpassing) + "juggle" (from Old French "jogler," meaning "to entertain by performing tricks")
