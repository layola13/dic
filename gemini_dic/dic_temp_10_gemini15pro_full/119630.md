middle-brow: /ˈmɪd(ə)lˌbraʊ/ | adj. n. | 中庸的；品味一般的；中产阶级趣味的；中庸作品；中产阶级趣味的人 | Appealing to or typical of a person with conventional, unimaginative tastes in literature, the arts, etc.; of the middle class; not highly intellectual or cultured, but not unsophisticated either. |  例句：He dismissed the novel as hopelessly middle-brow.  他认为这部小说无可救药地庸俗。| 近义词：conventional, unpretentious, mainstream | 反义词：highbrow, lowbrow | 词性: 形容词，名词

middle-brow: /ˈmɪd(ə)lˌbraʊ/ | n. | 中庸作品；中产阶级趣味的人 | A person with conventional, unimaginative tastes. | 例句：The gallery was full of middle-brows admiring the landscapes.  画廊里挤满了欣赏风景画的庸俗之辈。| 近义词：philistine | 反义词：highbrow, lowbrow | 词性: 名词


Etymology:  Compound of middle + brow.  "Brow" here refers to the forehead, and metaphorically to intellectual capacity.  Highbrow implies a high forehead, suggesting intelligence, while lowbrow suggests a low forehead, implying a lack of intellect.  Middlebrow falls between these two.
