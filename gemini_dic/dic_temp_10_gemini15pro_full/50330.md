词源：
From Late Latin deplorare "to weep for, bewail, lament," from de- "completely" (see de-) + plorare "to weep, cry, wail" (from PIE root *ple- (2) "to fill," with derivatives relating to the notion of swelling, especially of liquids or organs containing liquid).
