dusty:/ˈdʌsti/ (US), /ˈdʌsti/ (UK)| adj.|满是灰尘的；灰蒙蒙的；枯燥无味的|Covered with dust; dull and uninteresting.|The old books on the shelf were dusty. 架子上的旧书满是灰尘。|Synonyms: dusty, dirty, powdery|Antonyms: clean, spotless|adjective

dusty:/ˈdʌsti/ (US), /ˈdʌsti/ (UK)| n.|满是灰尘的东西；灰尘；<非正式>打架|Something covered in dust; dust itself; <informal> a fight|He wiped the dusty surface with a cloth. 他用一块布擦拭了满是灰尘的表面。(This usage is less common than the adjective form.)|Synonyms: dust, dirt, grime|Antonyms: cleanliness, purity|noun (rare)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dustiġ (unattested), from dust + -iġ (adjectival suffix).  Cognate with Middle Dutch dustich, German dustig.
