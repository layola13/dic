longshoremen: /ˈlɔːŋˈʃɔːrmən/ (US), /ˈlɒŋˈʃɔːmən/ (UK)| n. | 码头工人，港口装卸工人 | A worker who loads and unloads ships in a port. |  The longshoremen were busy unloading the cargo from the ship. 码头工人们正忙着从船上卸货。

The longshoremen's union negotiated a new contract. 码头工人工会协商了一份新的合同。

| 近义词: docker, stevedore, dockworker | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps shipper/receiver) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "long shore" + "man".  "Long shore" referred to the waterfront or shoreline.  The term emerged in the late 18th century.
