de-stressing:/diːˈstrɛsɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
减压；放松
The act of reducing or relieving stress.
例句：I find gardening very de-stressing. 我发现园艺很减压。

例句：She spent the weekend de-stressing at a spa. 她周末在水疗中心放松身心。

近义词：relaxing, unwinding, chilling out
反义词：stressing, tensing, worrying
词性：动词(现在分词)

stress:/strɛs/
noun
压力；强调；重音
A state of mental or emotional strain or tension resulting from adverse or very demanding circumstances.  Emphasis given to a syllable or word in speech by pronouncing it with greater force or duration.
例句：The stress of the job is getting to him.  工作压力让他难以承受。

例句：He put stress on the importance of education. 他强调教育的重要性。

近义词：pressure, strain, tension (for pressure sense); emphasis, accent (for emphasis sense)
反义词：relaxation, calm, peace (for pressure sense); de-emphasis (for emphasis sense)
