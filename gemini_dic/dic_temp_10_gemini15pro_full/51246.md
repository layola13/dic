destructiveness:/dɪˈstrʌktɪvnəs/| n. |破坏性，毁灭性|The quality of being destructive; causing destruction or damage.|The destructiveness of the hurricane was shocking. 飓风的破坏力令人震惊。|近义词：damage, devastation, ruin, havoc|反义词:constructiveness, creativity, productivity|词性:noun

词源: From destructive + -ness.  "destructive" comes from Late Latin *destructivus, from Latin destruct- "destroyed," past participle stem of destruere "tear down, demolish, destroy," from de- "down, completely" + struere "to pile, build."  The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
