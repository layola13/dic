canicular:/kəˈnɪkjʊlər/ | adj. | 犬日的；盛暑的；天狼星的 | Relating to the dog days; very hot.  Marked by the presence or influence of Sirius, the Dog Star. |  The canicular days of summer are upon us. 盛夏时节已经来临了。|Synonyms: sultry, sweltering, scorching, torrid | Antonyms: frigid, freezing, arctic, wintry | word property: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *caniculāris*, from *canicula* (“little dog, puppy; Dog Star (Sirius)”), diminutive of *canis* (“dog”).  The "dog days" traditionally refer to the hottest period of summer, roughly from early July to mid-August, coinciding with the heliacal rising of Sirius, the Dog Star.
