corbel:/ˈkɔːrbəl/ (US), /ˈkɔːbəl/ (UK)| n. v.| 构件，托座；(v.) 用托座支撑 |A piece of stone, wood, brick, etc., jutting out from a wall to support a structure above it. (n.) To support with or as if with a corbel. (v.)|The gargoyle perched on a stone corbel high above the street. 石像鬼栖息在街道上方高高的石制托座上。 He corbelled the arch to provide extra support. 他用托座支撑拱门以提供额外的支撑力。|近义词：bracket, support, console|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "base" in some contexts)|noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *corbel (modern French corbeau), diminutive of corb "raven," from Late Latin corvus.  The architectural feature is so named because of a fancied resemblance to a raven's beak.
