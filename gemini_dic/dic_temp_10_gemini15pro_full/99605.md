interjecting:/ˌɪntərˈdʒektɪŋ/| v. |插嘴，打断；突然插入|The teacher was constantly interjecting with helpful comments. 老师不断地插话进行有益的评论。| "Excuse me," she interjected, "but I think you've made a mistake." “对不起，”她插嘴道，“但我认为你犯了个错误。” | synonyms: interrupting, inserting, interposing | antonyms:  listening, allowing, permitting | verb

interjection:/ˌɪntərˈdʒekʃən/| n. |感叹词，插入语| "Ouch!" is an interjection. “哎哟！”是一个感叹词。 |  His speech was punctuated by frequent interjections from the audience. 他的演讲不时被观众的插话打断。| synonyms: exclamation, ejaculation, outburst | antonyms: statement, declaration, sentence | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *interjectus, past participle of interjicere ("to throw between"), from inter ("between") + jacere ("to throw").
