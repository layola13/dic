adlegation: /ˌædliˈɡeɪʃən/
n.
派遣；委托；委派；（尤指外交使团的）代表团
The act of sending someone as a representative or delegate, especially on a diplomatic mission.
The president sent an adlegation to the international summit. 总统派了一个代表团参加国际峰会。

The adlegation was tasked with negotiating a peace treaty.  这个代表团的任务是谈判一项和平条约。

近义词: delegation, mission, embassy, legation
反义词: recall, repatriation
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *adlegatio, from Latin adlegare (“to send as a legate, to depute”), from ad- + legare (“to send as a legate”), from legatus (“legate, envoy”).

USAGE->
There are limited common usages of "adlegation" in modern English.  It's a more formal and less frequently used synonym for "delegation."  Here are a few examples demonstrating its use in different tenses:

一般现在时:
The adlegation represents the interests of its country. = 这个代表团代表其国家的利益。
The adlegation meets with foreign officials. = 代表团与外国官员会面。


一般过去时:
The adlegation returned home after a month of negotiations. = 经过一个月的谈判，代表团回国了。
The adlegation signed the treaty on behalf of their government. = 代表团代表他们的政府签署了条约。


现在完成时:
The adlegation has completed its mission successfully. = 代表团已成功完成任务。
The adlegation has presented its credentials to the host country. = 代表团已向东道国递交了国书。


过去完成时:
The adlegation had already left before the news broke. = 在消息传出之前，代表团已经离开了。
The adlegation had achieved its objectives before the deadline. = 代表团在截止日期前实现了目标。


将来时:
The adlegation will travel to Brussels next week. = 代表团下周将前往布鲁塞尔。
The adlegation will hold a press conference tomorrow. = 代表团明天将举行新闻发布会。


被动语态:
An adlegation was sent to discuss the trade agreement. = 一个代表团被派去讨论贸易协定。
The adlegation is being briefed on the current situation. = 代表团正在听取有关当前情况的简报。
