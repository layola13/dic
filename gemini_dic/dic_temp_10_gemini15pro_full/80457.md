gongorism: /ˈɡɒŋɡərɪzəm/ (US) /ˈɡɒŋɡərɪzəm/ (UK)| n.| 龚戈拉主义 |A highly ornate and obscure style of writing, named after the 17th-century Spanish poet Luis de Góngora y Argote, characterized by elaborate metaphors, complex syntax, and allusions to classical mythology and other learned sources. |His prose is filled with gongorism, making it difficult for the average reader to understand. 他的散文充满了龚戈拉主义，使得普通读者难以理解。|Synonyms: bombast, fustian, euphuism|Antonyms: simplicity, clarity|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Luis de Góngora y Argote (1561–1627), Spanish poet + -ism.
