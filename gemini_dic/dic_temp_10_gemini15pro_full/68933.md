fatalness's:/ˈfeɪtəlnəsɪz/ | n. |致命性，宿命性| The quality or state of being fatal; deadliness.  This refers to the abstract concept of something being destined to cause death or destruction. It's the noun form of the adjective "fatal," with the possessive 's added, indicating the fatalness *of* something. While grammatically correct, it's extremely rare and often awkward in modern English. Using "the fatalness of..." is usually preferred. |The fatalness's of the disease shocked the medical community. (awkward and uncommon)  The fatalness of the disease shocked the medical community. (more natural)| Synonyms: lethality, mortality | Antonyms: harmlessness, innocuousness | noun
