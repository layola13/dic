bleating:/ˈbliːtɪŋ/ | n. | 羊叫声，哀鸣 | The sound a sheep or goat makes. |  The farmer recognized the bleating of his lost lamb. 农夫听出了他丢失的羊羔的叫声。 | synonyms: baa, bleat | antonyms: (No direct antonym for a sound.  Consider "silence" as a contextual antonym.) | noun


bleating:/ˈbliːtɪŋ/ | adj. |  哀怨的，哭诉的 |  Characterized by a plaintive, mournful sound. |  He made a bleating complaint about the unfairness of it all. 他哀怨地抱怨这一切的不公平。 | synonyms: whining, complaining, plaintive | antonyms: cheerful, optimistic | adjective


Etymology: From Middle English *bleten*, from Old English *blǣtan* (“to bleat”), from Proto-Germanic *blēt-aną* (“to bleat”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰléh₁- (“to blow, bark, bellow”).  Cognate with Dutch *bleten*, German *blöken*, Swedish *bläka*.
