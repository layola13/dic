coltishly: /ˈkoʊltɪʃli/ | adv. | 小马似地；欢蹦乱跳地；轻佻地 | In a way that resembles a young horse, especially in being playful, energetic, and sometimes awkward. |  She coltishly pranced around the room. 她像小马驹一样欢快地在房间里蹦蹦跳跳。| Synonyms: playfully, frisky, skittish, spirited | Antonyms: sedate, composed, serious | word type: adverb

coltish: /ˈkoʊltɪʃ/ | adj. | 小马似的；欢蹦乱跳的；轻佻的 | Resembling a young horse, especially in being playful, energetic, and sometimes awkward. | The coltish girl couldn't stop giggling.  这个像小马驹一样的女孩咯咯地笑个不停。| Synonyms: playful, frisky, skittish, spirited | Antonyms: sedate, composed, serious | word type: adjective

colt: /koʊlt/ | n. | 小雄马；新手 | A young male horse, typically less than four years old.  Also, can refer to an inexperienced person, especially a young sportsman. | The farmer proudly showed off his new colt. 农民自豪地炫耀他的新小马驹。| Synonyms: foal, filly (female), yearling | Antonyms: stallion, mare | word type: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *colt, from Proto-Germanic *kulþiz. Related to Old Norse kultr.  The figurative sense of "inexperienced youth" arose later.
