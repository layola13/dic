doppler:/ˈdɑːplər/ (US), /ˈdɒplə(r)/ (UK)| n. adj.|多普勒，多普勒效应的|Relating to or utilizing the Doppler effect, which is the change in frequency of a wave in relation to an observer who is moving relative to the source of the wave.|The Doppler radar detected the approaching storm. 多普勒雷达探测到正在逼近的风暴。|近义词：None (for the effect itself).  Related terms: frequency shift, redshift, blueshift|反义词：None|词性：noun, adjective

doppler shift:/ˈdɑːplər ʃɪft/ (US), /ˈdɒplə ʃɪft/ (UK)| n.|多普勒频移|The change in frequency of a wave (such as sound or light) due to the relative motion between the source and the observer.|The Doppler shift of the star's light indicated it was moving away from Earth. 星光的多普勒频移表明它正在远离地球。|近义词：frequency shift|反义词：None|词性：noun


Etymology: Named after Christian Doppler (1803-1853), an Austrian physicist who described the phenomenon in 1842.  From German *Doppler*, ultimately from a personal name.
