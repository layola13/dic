clowned:/klaʊnd/ | v. | 扮小丑；开玩笑；愚弄；(尤指) 装傻 | acted in a foolish or playful way; teased or mocked someone, often by mimicking them in a playful or exaggerated manner | He clowned around on stage, pretending to be a famous singer. 他在舞台上扮小丑，假装是一位著名的歌手。| Synonyms: joked, fooled, mocked, mimicked, kidded, jested | Antonyms:  serioused, behaved, respected | verb

clowned:/klaʊnd/ | adj. |  滑稽的，可笑的（非正式）|  funny, silly (informal usage, less common) |  He made a clowned-up version of the song. 他把这首歌改编成了滑稽的版本。 | Synonyms: comical, funny, silly, ridiculous | Antonyms: serious, solemn | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "clown" (n.), which originates from the late Middle English word "cloyne," meaning "rustic, boor," possibly of Scandinavian origin. The verb form "to clown" developed later, meaning to act like a clown.
