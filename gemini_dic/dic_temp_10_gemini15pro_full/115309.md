mannerism:/ˈmænərɪzəm/| n.|特殊习惯；怪癖；矫揉造作|A way of speaking or moving that is typical of a particular person, often without the person realizing that they do it.|He has a mannerism of scratching his head when he's nervous. 他紧张时有个挠头的习惯。|Synonyms: peculiarity, habit, affectation, idiosyncrasy|Antonyms: naturalness, simplicity, artlessness|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mannerismus, from Italian manierismo, from maniera "manner," from Latin manus "hand."  Originally referred to a distinctive characteristic of an artist or their work.
