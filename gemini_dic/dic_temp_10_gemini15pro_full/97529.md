ingraff: /ˈɪnˌɡræf/ |n.| 接穗；嫁接；移植 | A shoot or twig inserted into a slit on a living plant.  The act of grafting. The transplantation of living tissue. |  The ingraff took well and the tree began to bear fruit the following year.  接穗长势良好，树在第二年开始结果。| 近义词: graft, implant, transplant | 反义词:  excise, remove | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From Middle English *ingraf(f), borrowed from Anglo-Norman engraffer, engraf, Old French entrafer, entraffer (“to graft”), from en- + graffe (“graft”). Ultimately from Late Latin *graffium, from Greek γράφιον (grápʰion, “stylus, grafting knife”).
