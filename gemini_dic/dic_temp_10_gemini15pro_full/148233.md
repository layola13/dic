oversimplifications: /ˌoʊvərˌsɪmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. | 过度简化 | The act of making something seem simpler than it really is, often leaving out important details or nuances. |  Politicians are often accused of oversimplifications of complex issues. 政客们经常被指责过度简化复杂的问题。

The documentary's oversimplifications of the historical events led to criticism.  这部纪录片对历史事件的过度简化引来了批评。 | 近义词: distortions, misrepresentations, generalizations | 反义词: nuances, complexities, subtleties | 词性: noun

oversimplify: /ˌoʊvərˈsɪmplɪfaɪ/ | v. | 过度简化 | To describe or explain something in a way that makes it seem simpler than it really is, often leaving out important details. | The teacher oversimplified the scientific concept, confusing the students. 老师过度简化了科学概念，让学生们感到困惑。

It's easy to oversimplify the causes of the war. 简化战争的起因很容易。 | 近义词:  misrepresent, distort, generalize | 反义词:  elaborate, specify, nuance | 词性: verb

simplification: /ˌsɪmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 简化 | The act of making something simpler or easier to understand. | The simplification of the tax code was welcomed by businesses.  税法的简化受到了企业的欢迎。

The new software allows for the simplification of complex tasks. 新软件可以简化复杂的任务。 | 近义词: streamlining, abbreviation, reduction | 反义词: complication, elaboration, expansion | 词性: noun


词源:  From over- + simplification,  ultimately from Latin simplex "simple"
