diversionism:/daɪˈvɜːrʒənɪzəm/ | n. | 转移视线；牵制；声东击西 | The act of diverting or turning aside from a course or purpose; the state of being diverted.  Specifically, a political or military strategy of diverting an enemy's attention from the main target or theater of operations. | The government used diversionism to draw attention away from the economic crisis. 政府利用转移视线来分散人们对经济危机的注意力。| Synonyms: distraction, subterfuge, feint, ruse, misdirection | Antonyms: focus, concentration, directness | noun

diversionary:/daɪˈvɜːrʃəneri/ | adj. | 转移注意力的；牵制的 | Tending to divert or distract; serving to draw attention away from the main issue. | The government launched a diversionary attack to distract the enemy from their true objective. 政府发动了牵制性的攻击，以分散敌人对其真正目标的注意力。| Synonyms: distracting, diverting, deceptive | Antonyms: focusing, concentrating, direct | adjective
