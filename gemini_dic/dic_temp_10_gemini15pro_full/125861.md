muscarine:/ˈmʌskərɪn/ | n. | 毒蕈碱；蕈毒碱 | A poisonous alkaloid found in certain mushrooms, especially the fly agaric (Amanita muscaria).  It mimics the action of the neurotransmitter acetylcholine. |  Muscarine poisoning can cause symptoms such as sweating, salivation, and slowed heart rate.  毒蕈碱中毒会导致出汗、流涎和心率减慢等症状。 | Synonyms:  choline agonist, parasympathomimetic | Antonyms: atropine, scopolamine | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From New Latin *muscarina, from Latin Amanita muscaria (“fly agaric”), from musca (“fly”).  The mushroom was traditionally used to kill flies.
