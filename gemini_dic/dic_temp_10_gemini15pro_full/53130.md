diploidic:/dɪˈplɔɪdɪk/
|adj.|二倍体的|Having two sets of chromosomes, one from each parent.  A cell or organism containing two sets of chromosomes (2n), one inherited from each parent.|Most animal cells are diploid.|Synonyms: binal, dual, twofold|Antonym: haploid|adjective


diploid:/ˈdɪplɔɪd/
|n.|二倍体|A diploid cell or organism.|The diploid number of chromosomes in humans is 46.|Synonyms: binal, dual, twofold|Antonym: haploid|noun
