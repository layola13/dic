deontologist: /ˌdiːɒnˈtɒlədʒɪst/ (UK), /ˌdiːɑːnˈtɑːlədʒɪst/ (US)| n.|道义论者，义务论者|A person who believes in deontology, a system of ethics that judges the morality of an action based on the action's adherence to a rule or rules, rather than based on the consequences of the action.|例句：The deontologist argued that lying is always wrong, regardless of the potential benefits.|近义词：moralist, ethicist|反义词：consequentialist|词性：noun

deontological: /ˌdiːɒnˈtɒlədʒɪkəl/ (UK), /ˌdiːɑːnˈtɑːlədʒɪkəl/ (US)| adj.|道义论的，义务论的|Relating to deontology, the ethical theory that the morality of an action should be based on whether that action itself is right or wrong under a series of rules, rather than based on the consequences of the action.|例句：A deontological approach to ethics emphasizes duty and rules.|近义词：duty-based, principled|反义词：consequentialist, teleological|词性：adjective

词源:  From Greek *deon* (δέον), neuter participle of *dein* (δεῖν, “ought”), + *-logos* (-λόγος).
