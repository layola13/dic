anachronical: /əˌnækrəˈnɪkəl/ | adj. | 时代错误的，年代失调的 | Belonging or appropriate to an earlier period, especially so as to seem conspicuously old-fashioned.  Out of chronological order; placing something in the wrong historical period. | Example 1: The movie was full of anachronical details, like characters using cell phones in ancient Rome.  这部电影充满了时代错误的细节，例如古罗马的人物使用手机。

Example 2: His anachronical views on women's rights were out of place in a modern society. 他关于女性权利的过时观点在现代社会中格格不入。 | 近义词: outdated, obsolete, antiquated, anachronistic | 反义词: contemporary, modern, current | 词性: adjective

anachronism: /əˈnækrənɪzəm/ | n. | 时代错误，年代失调 | Something (such as a word, an object, or an event) that is mistakenly placed in a time where it does not belong in a story, movie, etc. A thing belonging or appropriate to a period other than that in which it exists, especially a thing that is conspicuously old-fashioned. | Example 1:  The inclusion of a computer in a film set in the 1800s is a glaring anachronism.  在一部以 19 世纪为背景的电影中加入电脑是一个明显的时代错误。

Example 2: He regards the monarchy as an anachronism in the modern world.  他认为君主制在现代世界是一个时代错误。| 近义词: misplacement, incongruity | 反义词: contemporaneity | 词性: noun

词源: From Late Latin  *anachronicus*, from Ancient Greek ἀνάχρονος (anáchronos, “against time”), from ἀνά (aná, “up, against”) + χρόνος (chrónos, “time”).
