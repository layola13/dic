inclination:/ˌɪnklɪˈneɪʃn/ | n. | 倾向，爱好，倾斜 | A feeling that makes you want to do something.  A slope or slant. | She has an inclination to overspend. 她有过度消费的倾向。The roof has a slight inclination. 屋顶略微倾斜。 |  tendency, propensity, leaning, slope, slant | aversion, dislike | noun

inclined:/ɪnˈklaɪnd/ | adj. | 倾向于...的，有...意愿的，倾斜的 | Wanting to do something. Sloping. | I'm inclined to agree with you. 我倾向于同意你的观点. The road is inclined at a steep angle. 这条路倾斜得很陡。| prone, disposed, apt | disinclined, averse | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English inclinacioun, from Latin inclinātiō (“bending, inclination”), from inclinō (“bend, incline”), from in (“in, into”) + clinō (“bend, lean”).
