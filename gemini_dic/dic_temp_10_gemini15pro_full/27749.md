canker:/ˈkæŋkər/ (US), /ˈkæŋkə(r)/ (UK)| n.|溃疡; 腐烂病; 祸根|A destructive fungal or bacterial disease of plants, characterized by the formation of localized lesions, often sunken and discolored. Also, a sore or ulcerous lesion in the mouth or on other parts of the body.|The apple tree was suffering from canker. 这棵苹果树得了腐烂病。|Synonyms: ulcer, sore, lesion, blight, آفت|Antonyms: health, well-being|Noun

cankers:/ˈkæŋkərz/ (US), /ˈkæŋkəz/ (UK)| n. (plural)|溃疡（复数）; 腐烂病（复数）; 祸根（复数）|The plural form of "canker," referring to multiple instances of the disease or sores.|The horse's mouth was full of cankers. 这匹马的嘴里满是溃疡。|Synonyms: ulcers, sores, lesions, blights|Antonyms: health, well-being|Noun
