cataphract:/ˈkætəˌfrækt/| n.|重装骑兵，铁甲骑兵|A heavily armed horseman, especially of ancient times, both horse and rider being protected by armour.|The cataphracts charged into the enemy lines, a wall of steel and muscle.|近义词:armored horseman, cuirassier, heavy cavalryman|反义词:light cavalry, infantry|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *cataphractus*, from Greek *kataphraktos* "armored," from *kataphrassein* "to arm completely," from *kata-* "down, completely" + *phrassein* "to fence, protect."
