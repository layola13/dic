coercionary:/koʊˈɜːrʃəneri/ | adj. |强制的，胁迫的 | Relating to or involving coercion; using force or threats to persuade someone to do something. | The government imposed coercionary measures to control the spread of the disease. 政府采取强制措施控制疾病传播。|  The king ruled by coercionary tactics, instilling fear in his subjects. 国王用高压手段统治，给臣民灌输恐惧。 | synonyms: compulsory, forced, obligatory, mandatory | antonyms: voluntary, optional, persuasive, elective | adjective

coercion:/koʊˈɜːrʒən/ | n. |强制，胁迫 | The practice of persuading someone to do something by using force or threats. |  He claimed the confession was obtained under coercion. 他声称供词是在胁迫下获得的。 | The company was accused of using coercion to silence whistleblowers.  该公司被指控使用胁迫手段让举报人闭嘴。 | synonyms: compulsion, force, duress, intimidation | antonyms: persuasion, encouragement, freedom, liberty | noun
