blitzkriegs:/ˈblɪtskriːɡz/| n.|闪电战（复数）|An intense military campaign intended to bring about a swift victory.|The blitzkriegs of World War II demonstrated the devastating power of mechanized warfare. 二战中的闪电战展示了机械化战争的破坏力。|例句：The blitzkriegs across Europe shocked the world. 闪电战席卷欧洲，震惊了世界。

例句：The general planned a series of blitzkriegs to quickly conquer the enemy territory. 将军计划了一系列闪电战，迅速征服敌方领土。

近义词：assaults, attacks, offensives|反义词:defense, retreat|词性:noun


blitzkrieg:/ˈblɪtskriːɡ/| n.|闪电战（单数）|An intense military campaign intended to bring about a swift victory.|The blitzkrieg was a key strategy for Germany in the early years of World War II. 闪电战是德国在二战初期的一个关键战略。|例句：The blitzkrieg was successful in the beginning but ultimately failed.  闪电战初期很成功，但最终失败了。

例句：The enemy was unprepared for the speed and ferocity of the blitzkrieg. 敌人对闪电战的速度和猛烈程度毫无准备。


近义词：assault, attack, offensive|反义词:defense, retreat|词性:noun


词源: From German Blitzkrieg, from Blitz ‘lightning’ + Krieg ‘war’.
