flurries:/ˈflɜːriz/ (US), /ˈflʌriz/ (UK)| n.|阵雪；一阵忙乱；激动；|A brief, light snowfall, often occurring in quick bursts. Also, a sudden burst of activity or excitement.|例句：The snow flurries came and went throughout the day. 一整天都断断续续地下着阵雪。

例句：She felt a flurry of excitement as she opened the letter. 她打开信时感到一阵兴奋。|近义词：bursts, gusts, showers, spate, flutter|反义词:calm, lull, peace|词性:noun


flurry:/ˈflɜːri/ (US), /ˈflʌri/ (UK)| n.|一阵忙乱；激动；|A sudden burst of activity or excitement.|例句：There was a flurry of activity in the kitchen as the chefs prepared for dinner. 厨师们准备晚餐时，厨房里一阵忙乱。

例句：A flurry of phone calls followed the announcement. 公告发布后，电话铃声此起彼伏。|近义词：burst, rush, spate, stir, commotion|反义词:calm, quiet, stillness|词性:noun


flurry:/ˈflɜːri/ (US), /ˈflʌri/ (UK)| v.|使慌乱；使激动；下阵雪|To cause a flurry of activity or excitement; to snow lightly in bursts.|例句：The news flurried the stock market. 这条消息搅乱了股票市场。

例句：It began to flurry just as we left the house. 我们刚离开家就开始下阵雪了。|近义词：agitate, excite, rouse, stir, bustle|反义词:calm, soothe, tranquilize|词性:verb
