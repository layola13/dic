covert:/ˈkʌvərt/ (US), /ˈkʌvət/ (UK)| adj. n.|隐蔽的，秘密的；隐蔽处，藏身处|Concealed; secret; disguised.  A hiding place; a thicket offering concealment.|The spy used covert operations to gather intelligence.  The fox disappeared into the covert. |近义词：clandestine, hidden, secret, furtive, stealthy; shelter, refuge, sanctuary|反义词：overt, open, public; exposed|词性: adjective, noun

covert:/ˈkʌvərts/ (plural noun)|覆羽; 隐蔽处|The small feathers covering the bases of the larger feathers of a bird's wings or tail. Also used to refer to hiding places, particularly for game birds.|The bird's coverts were a beautiful iridescent blue.  The pheasants were flushed from the coverts.|近义词：(for feathers) tectrices; (for hiding places) thickets, undergrowth|反义词：(for hiding places) open ground|词性: noun
