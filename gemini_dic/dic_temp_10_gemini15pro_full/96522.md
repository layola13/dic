indoctrinator:/ɪnˈdɑːktrɪneɪtər/ | n. | 教导者；灌输者 | A person who indoctrinates others, especially with a partisan or biased point of view.  | The cult leader was a skilled indoctrinator, convincing his followers to abandon their families and fortunes. 邪教头目是一个老练的灌输者，说服他的追随者放弃他们的家庭和财富。|  The teacher was accused of being an indoctrinator, pushing his political views onto the students.  老师被指控为灌输者，将自己的政治观点强加给学生。| Synonyms: propagandist, brainwasher | Antonyms: educator, enlightener | Noun


indoctrinate:/ɪnˈdɑːktrɪneɪt/ | v. | 教导；灌输 | To teach (someone) to fully accept the ideas, opinions, and beliefs of a particular group and to not consider other ideas, opinions, and beliefs. |  The regime indoctrinates children from an early age.  该政权从很小的时候就对儿童进行灌输。 | They were indoctrinated with the company's philosophy.  他们被灌输了公司的理念。| Synonyms: brainwash, proselytize | Antonyms: educate, enlighten | Verb
