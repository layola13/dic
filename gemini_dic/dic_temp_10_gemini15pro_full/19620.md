bigwigged:/ˈbɪɡwɪɡd/| adj.|戴着假发的；要人的；显赫的|Having a large or impressive wig, especially formerly as a symbol of importance; hence, important, influential.|The bigwigged judge entered the courtroom.  戴着假发的法官走进了法庭。|Synonyms: important, influential, powerful, prominent, leading, high-ranking, eminent, distinguished, notable, VIP,  |Antonyms: unimportant, insignificant, powerless, low-ranking, obscure|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From big wig + -ed.  "Big wig" referred to the large wigs worn by important people, particularly in the 17th and 18th centuries. The term "big wig" itself evolved to mean an important person, and the adjective "bigwigged" describes someone who is or appears to be important, often carrying a connotation of pomposity or self-importance.
