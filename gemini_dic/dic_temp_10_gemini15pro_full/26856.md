caisson:/ˈkeɪsɒn/ (US) /ˈkeɪsən/ (UK)| n.|沉箱；弹药箱；（尤指运送灵柩的）带轮盖板马车|A large watertight chamber, open at the bottom, from which water is kept out by air pressure so that work can be done underwater.  Also, a box or chest for ammunition, or a horse-drawn wagon with a wheeled cover, especially one used to carry a coffin.|The workers descended into the caisson to begin construction of the bridge pier.  工人们下降到沉箱里开始建造桥墩。

The soldiers loaded the caisson with artillery shells. 士兵们把炮弹装进弹药箱。

The funeral procession was led by a black caisson bearing the flag-draped coffin. 送葬队伍由一辆黑色的、载着覆盖着国旗的棺材的带轮盖板马车引领。|近义词：cofferdam (for underwater construction), ammunition box, hearse|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


caisson:/ˈkeɪsɒn/ (US) /ˈkeɪsən/ (UK)| n.|（医学）干式加压氧舱|A large chamber used for hyperbaric oxygen therapy.|The patient spent two hours in the caisson to treat a diving injury. 病人花了两小时在加压氧舱里治疗潜水损伤。|近义词：hyperbaric chamber|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *caisson*, diminutive of *caisse* "box, case", from Italian *cassa*, ultimately from Latin *capsa* "box, container".
