nonvolubility:/ˌnɒnvɒljuːˈbɪləti/
n.
沉默寡言，不健谈
lack of fluency or readiness of speech; reticence.
Her nonvolubility in meetings was often misinterpreted as disapproval.  她在会议上的沉默寡言常被误解为不赞成。
近义词: reticence, taciturnity, reserve
反义词: volubility, loquacity, talkativeness
词性: 名词

Etymology:
From non- + volubility.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "volubility" comes from the Latin "volubilis," meaning "easily rolling, fluent."  Therefore, "nonvolubility" literally means "not fluent."
