agitprop:/ˈædʒɪtprɒp/ (US) /ˈædʒɪtprɒp/ (UK)| n.| 政治宣传|Agitprop is political propaganda, especially disseminated through literature, drama, music, or art.|The play was a blatant piece of agitprop designed to stir up revolutionary sentiments.|Synonyms: propaganda, indoctrination, brainwashing|Antonyms: education, enlightenment, free speech|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Russian агитпроп (agitprop), a clipping of  агитация (agitaciya, “agitation”) and пропаганда (propaganda, “propaganda”). Ultimately from Latin agitare (“to set in motion, stir up”) and propagare (“to propagate”).
