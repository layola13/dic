make away with:/meɪk əˈweɪ wɪð/| phrasal verb | 偷走；卷走；拐走；杀死 | To steal something and escape with it; to carry something off clandestinely. Also, less commonly, to kill someone. | The thieves made away with the jewelry and cash.  小偷卷走了珠宝和现金。

The accountant made away with the company's funds. 会计卷走了公司的资金。

Less common usage:  They feared the dictator had made away with his rivals. 他们担心独裁者杀死了他的对手们。 | synonyms: steal, abscond with, pilfer, filch, purloin, embezzle (for money), kidnap, abduct (for people), murder, kill (less common) | antonyms: return, give back, surrender (for stolen items); protect, save (for people in the less common usage) | phrasal verb
