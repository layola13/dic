incredulous: /ɪnˈkrɛdʒʊləs/ | adj. | 怀疑的，不轻信的 | Unwilling or unable to believe something. |  She gave me an incredulous look when I told her I'd won the lottery. 她当我告诉她我中了彩票时，给了我一个怀疑的眼神。

He was incredulous that she could have done such a thing. 他不相信她会做出这样的事。

| 近义词: skeptical, doubtful, disbelieving | 反义词: credulous, believing, trusting | 词性: adjective

incredulity: /ˌɪnkrəˈdjuːləti/ | n. | 怀疑，不轻信 | The state of being unwilling or unable to believe something. | She stared at him in incredulity. 她难以置信地盯着他。

His voice was thick with incredulity. 他的声音充满了怀疑。

| 近义词: skepticism, doubt, disbelief | 反义词: credulity, belief, trust | 词性: noun

词源:  From Late Latin *incredulus, from in- "not" + credulus "believing," from credere "to believe."
