crystallinities:/ˌkrɪstəˈlɪnɪtiz/| n. |结晶度，结晶性|The degree to which a substance is crystalline, expressed as a percentage.  It represents the fraction of a material that exhibits a highly ordered molecular arrangement, like that of a crystal, as opposed to an amorphous or disordered state.|The polymer's crystallinity was enhanced by slow cooling.|Synonyms: crystallinity degree, degree of crystallinity|Antonyms: amorphousness|noun

crystallinity:/ˌkrɪstəˈlɪnɪti/| n. |结晶度，结晶性|The degree to which a substance is crystalline.  It's a measure of the ordered arrangement of molecules within a material.|The crystallinity of the sample was determined by X-ray diffraction.|Synonyms: degree of crystallinity|Antonyms: amorphousness|noun
