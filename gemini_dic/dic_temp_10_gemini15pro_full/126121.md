mutessarif:/ˌmʊtəˈsɑːrɪf/ |/ˌmuːtəˈsɑːrɪf/| n. |县长；地方行政长官|A governor of an administrative district in the Ottoman Empire.|The mutessarif of Jerusalem held considerable power.  耶路撒冷的县长拥有相当大的权力。|Synonyms: governor, prefect, administrator|Antonyms: subject, citizen|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ottoman Turkish *mutesarrıf*, from Arabic *mutaṣarrif* (“disposer, manager”), active participle of *taṣarrafa* (“to dispose, manage”). Ultimately from the root *ṣ-r-f* (“to spend, use”).
