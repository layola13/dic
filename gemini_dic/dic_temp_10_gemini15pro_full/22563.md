bootblack: /ˈbuːtˌblæk/ | n. |擦鞋童 |A person who polishes shoes, especially one who works in a public place. |  The bootblack worked diligently, bringing a shine to the scuffed leather. 擦鞋童工作很勤奋，把磨损的皮革擦得锃亮。| Example: He gave the bootblack a generous tip. 他给了擦鞋童丰厚的小费。| Synonyms: shoeshiner | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "customer" in this context) | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "boot" + "black" (as in to blacken).  The term originated because early shoe polish was primarily black.
