metafictionality:/ˌmɛtəfɪkʃəˈnælɪti/ | n. | 元小说性 |  The quality of a work of fiction that self-consciously draws attention to its status as fiction.  It involves techniques like breaking the fourth wall, addressing the reader directly, or commenting on the writing process itself within the narrative. |  The novel's metafictionality is evident in the narrator's frequent digressions about the challenges of writing. 这部小说的元小说性体现在叙述者频繁地离题讨论写作的挑战。|  self-reflexivity, self-consciousness | N/A | noun
