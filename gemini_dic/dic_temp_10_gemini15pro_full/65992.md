exaltment:/ɪɡˈzɔːltmənt/| n. |兴奋，得意，提升|A feeling of extreme happiness or elation. The state of being raised to a higher rank or position. |She felt a sense of exaltment as she accepted the award.  她接受奖项时感到无比兴奋。 The exaltment of the new king was a grand ceremony. 新国王的登基仪式非常隆重。|近义词：elation, joy, rapture, elevation, promotion|反义词：depression, sadness, despair, demotion, degradation|词性:noun

exalt:/ɪɡˈzɔːlt/| vt. |提升，赞扬，使得意|To raise to a higher rank or position. To praise highly; glorify. To intensify or heighten (a feeling or quality).|The king exalted his loyal knight to a higher position. 国王将忠诚的骑士提升到更高的职位。 The poem exalts the beauty of nature. 这首诗赞美了大自然的美丽。 The music exalted her mood. 音乐让她心情愉悦。 |近义词：praise, glorify, elevate, promote|反义词：degrade, demean, criticize, demote|词性:verb
