holography:/həˈlɒɡrəfi/ /hoʊˈlɑːɡrəfi/
n.
全息术，全息照相术
A technique that allows the light scattered from an object to be recorded and later reconstructed so that it appears as if the object were present in its original position.
Example 1: Holography is a fascinating technology with a wide range of applications. 全息术是一项引人入胜的技术，应用广泛。

Example 2:  The museum featured an exhibit on the history of holography.  博物馆举办了一个关于全息术历史的展览。
近义词: 3-D imaging, holographic imaging
反义词: photography (in a traditional, non-holographic sense)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *hólos* (ὅλος) "whole" + *-graphía* (-γραφία) "writing"
