mortgages:/ˈmɔːrɡɪdʒɪz/ (US) /ˈmɔːɡɪdʒɪz/ (UK)| n. |抵押贷款；按揭|A loan obtained through the conveyance of property as security, which becomes void upon payment or forfeited upon failure to comply with the agreed terms.|They took out a mortgage to buy their first home. 他们办理了抵押贷款来购买他们的第一套房子。|近义词：loan, lien|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

mortgages:/ˈmɔːrɡɪdʒɪz/ (US) /ˈmɔːɡɪdʒɪz/ (UK)| v. |抵押|To convey or pledge (property) as security for a loan.|They mortgaged their house to start a business.  他们抵押了房子来创业。|近义词：pawn, pledge|反义词:redeem|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mortgaige* “dead pledge,” from *mort* “dead” + *gage* “pledge.”  The idea is that the pledge becomes "dead" when the debt is paid or the property forfeited.
