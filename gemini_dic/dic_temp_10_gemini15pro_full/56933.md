dotish: /ˈdoʊtɪʃ/ | adj. | 愚蠢的，傻的 |  Foolish or silly; weak-minded.  Origin: From "dote," meaning to be excessively fond, suggesting a level of affection that clouds judgment. | She made a dotish remark about the weather, completely misreading the forecast. 她对天气做了一个愚蠢的评论，完全误读了预报。| He's not malicious, just a bit dotish sometimes. 他不是恶意的，只是有时有点傻。 | synonyms: foolish, silly, stupid, witless, inane, fatuous | antonyms: intelligent, clever, wise, sensible, astute | adjective
