amphibiology:/æmˈfɪbiˈɒlədʒi/
n.|两栖动物学|The study of amphibians, a class of vertebrate animals that live both in water and on land.|Amphibiology is a fascinating field, encompassing the study of frogs, toads, salamanders, and newts.|近义词:herpetology (although this includes reptiles)|反义词:N/A|词性:名词
amphibian:/æmˈfɪbiən/
n. |两栖动物|A cold-blooded vertebrate animal of a class that comprises the frogs, toads, newts, salamanders, and caecilians. They are distinguished by having an aquatic gill-breathing larval stage followed (typically) by a terrestrial lung-breathing adult stage.|The frog is a common amphibian.|近义词:None (specific to this class of animal)|反义词:N/A|词性:名词
amphibious:/æmˈfɪbiəs/
adj.|两栖的;水陆两用的|Relating to, living in, or suited for both land and water.|The Marines launched an amphibious assault.|近义词:aquatic, terrestrial (in specific contexts)|反义词:N/A|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek ἀμφίβιος (amphíbios, “living a double life”), from ἀμφί (amphí, “on both sides”) + βίος (bíos, “life”).
