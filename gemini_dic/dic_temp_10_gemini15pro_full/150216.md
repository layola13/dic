pandar:/ˈpændər/ (US), /ˈpændɑːr/ (UK)| n.|皮条客；怂恿者；迎合者|A person who caters to or exploits the weaknesses of others, especially to provide gratification for their own desires.  A go-between in love intrigues (archaic).|例句：He acted as a pandar to the king's desires for exotic women. 他充当国王对异国女子的欲望的皮条客。

例句：The tabloid press often acts as a pandar, sensationalizing stories to boost sales.  小报媒体经常充当迎合者，用耸人听闻的故事来提高销量。|近义词：panderer, procurer, pimp, facilitator, enabler|反义词：obstacle, hinderer|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *pandare, from Old French pandere, pandeor (“pander”), from Latin Pandārus, the name of a Trojan prince in Homer's *Iliad* who procured Cressida for Troilus.
