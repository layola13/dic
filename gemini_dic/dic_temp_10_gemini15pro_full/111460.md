Etymology:
From Middle English *what*, *hwat*, *quat*, from Old English *hwæt* (“what”), from Proto-Germanic *hwat* (“what”), from Proto-Indo-European *kʷod* (“what”). Cognate with Scots what (pron. “what”), West Frisian wat (“what”), Dutch wat (“what”), German was (“what”), Low German wat (“what”), Danish hvad (“what”), Swedish vad (“what”), Icelandic hvað (“what”). Related also to Latin quod (“what, which; because”).
