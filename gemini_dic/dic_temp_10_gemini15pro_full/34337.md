clasp: /klæsp/ (US) /klɑːsp/ (UK)| n. vt.|扣子，钩子；紧握，拥抱|A metal fastening for a belt, bag, or garment. To grip something firmly.|She fastened the clasp on her necklace. 她系上了项链上的扣子。|近义词：fastener, buckle, grip, embrace|反义词：release, unfasten|Noun, Verb

clasp: /klæsp/ (US) /klɑːsp/ (UK)| vt.|紧握，拥抱|To hold someone or something tightly.|He clasped her hand tightly. 他紧紧地握住她的手。|近义词：grasp, clutch, hug|反义词：release|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *claspen, *claspen, from Old Norse *klaspa (“to fasten, lump, clot”), from Proto-Germanic *klampōną (“to press together, to stick”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)klep- (“to stick, adhere”).  Cognate with Dutch klampen (“to clamp, to fasten”), German klampen (“to clamp, to fasten”), Swedish klampa (“to clamp, to fasten”).
