blow-in:/ˈbloʊ ɪn/| n. adj.|外来者，外来物；吹进来的东西|Something or someone that has arrived from somewhere else, often unexpectedly or recently.  A person who is not originally from a particular place but who has recently moved there.|The small town was wary of blow-ins who didn't share their values.  这个小镇对那些不认同他们价值观的外来者持谨慎态度。|newcomer, immigrant, outsider|native, resident|noun, adjective

blow-in:/ˈbloʊ ɪn/| n. adj.|（广告、传单等）吹进来的东西|A piece of paper, such as an advertisement or leaflet, that has been blown in by the wind.|There were a few blow-ins on the porch, mostly junk mail.  门廊上有几张吹进来的纸片，大部分是垃圾邮件。|flyer, leaflet, handbill|-|noun, adjective
