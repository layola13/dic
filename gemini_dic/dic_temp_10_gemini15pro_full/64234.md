equinoctial:/ˌiːkwɪˈnɒkʃəl/ /ˌiːkwəˈnɑːkʃəl/ | adj. n.|昼夜平分的，二分点的;春分或秋分|Relating to either of the two times of the year when the sun crosses the celestial equator and day and night are of approximately equal length (about September 22 and March 20).  Also, the circle on the celestial sphere formed by the intersection of the plane of Earth's equator with the celestial sphere.|The ancient Egyptians built temples aligned with the equinoctial sunrise.|近义词：equatorial|反义词:solstitial
equinoctials:/ˌiːkwɪˈnɒkʃəlz/ /ˌiːkwəˈnɑːkʃəlz/ | n. |二分点；分点线|The two points where the ecliptic intersects the celestial equator.|Sailors in the past relied on the equinoctials for navigation.|近义词：equinoxes|反义词:solstices
