creaturehood:/ˈkriːtʃərˌhʊd/
n.|生物性，动物性；受造物状态|The state of being a creature; the nature of a creature.  The inherent qualities of being a living creature, often emphasizing dependence, vulnerability, or animality.|He acknowledged his creaturehood, accepting his limitations and dependence on the natural world. 他承认了自己的生物性，接受了自己的局限性和对自然世界的依赖。|近义词：animality, creatureliness|反义词: divinity, godhood|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From creature + -hood.  "Creature" comes from Old French "creature"  (Modern French "créature"),  from Latin "creatura"  "thing created," from past participle stem of "creare" "to create." "-hood" is an Old English suffix denoting "state, condition, quality, character," related to German "-heit" and Dutch "-heid."
