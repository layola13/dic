condolence: /kənˈdoʊləns/ (US) /kənˈdəʊləns/ (UK) | n. | 哀悼，慰问 |  An expression of sympathy, especially on the occasion of a death. |  Please accept my condolences on the death of your father.  请接受我对您父亲去世的哀悼。| synonyms: sympathy, commiseration, solace | antonyms: indifference, apathy | noun

condolences: /kənˈdoʊlənsɪz/ (US) /kənˈdəʊlənsɪz/ (UK) | n. |  慰问，吊唁 | An expression of sympathy, especially on the occasion of a death, usually in the plural form. | We offer our sincere condolences to the bereaved family. 我们向死者家属表示诚挚的慰问。| synonyms: sympathies, commiserations | antonyms: indifference, apathy | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *condolence*, from Old French *condolence*, from Latin *condolentia*, from *condolēre* ("to feel pain with another, sympathize"), from *con-* ("with, together") + *dolēre* ("to grieve, suffer pain").
