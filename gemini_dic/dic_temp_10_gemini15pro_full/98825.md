intelligence: /ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns/ (US) /ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns/ (UK)| n.| 智力，情报，理解力 | The ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills.  |  She possesses a high degree of intelligence. 她拥有很高的智力。 | synonyms: intellect, mind, reason, understanding | antonyms: stupidity, ignorance | noun

intelligence: /ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns/ (US) /ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns/ (UK)| n.| 情报，信息 | Information concerning an enemy or competitor. | The intelligence gathered suggested an imminent attack.  收集到的情报表明即将发生袭击。| synonyms: information, data, news, facts | antonyms: misinformation, disinformation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French intelligence (12c.) and directly from Latin intelligentia "understanding, power of discerning; intelligence, notion, idea," from intelligentem (nominative intelligens), present participle of intelligere "to understand, comprehend, come to know," from inter- "between" + legere "choose, gather, read."
