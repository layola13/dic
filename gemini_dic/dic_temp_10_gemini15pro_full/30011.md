cawker:/ˈkɔːkər/ | n. | 补鞋匠; 修鞋匠; 皮匠 | A person who mends shoes; a cobbler. | The old cawker sat in his shop, hammering a sole onto a boot. 老补鞋匠坐在他的店里，把鞋底钉在一只靴子上。|  近义词: cobbler, shoemaker, shoe-mender | 反义词:  (无直接反义词) | 词性: 名词

词源:
From the Middle English *cawkere*, from *calk* "to stuff, fill," from Old North French *cauquer*.  Ultimately from Latin *calcare* "to tread," from *calx* "heel."  Relates to the practice of filling or stuffing shoes with material for repair.
