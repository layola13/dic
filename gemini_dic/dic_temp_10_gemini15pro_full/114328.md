maister:/ˈmeɪstər/ (Archaic)| n.| 大师，主人，先生 |A skilled craftsman or artist, especially in the Middle Ages; a teacher or tutor; a man in a position of authority.|The maister of the guild oversaw all the apprentices.  公会的大师监督所有的学徒。|Synonyms: master, teacher, expert, chief, lord|Antonyms: servant, apprentice, student|noun

maister:/ˈmeɪstər/ (Archaic)| n.| 先生 (用于尊称) |A term of respectful address to a man.|Good day, Maister Smith.  史密斯先生，您好。|Synonyms: sir, mister, gentleman|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *maister*, from Old French *maistre*, from Latin *magister* ("master, chief, director, teacher, superintendent"), from *magis* ("more"). Cognate with Spanish *maestro*, Italian *maestro*, Portuguese *mestre*, and German *Meister*.  Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European *\*megʰ₂-* ("great").
