manciple:/ˈmænsɪpl/| n.|采办员，伙食管理员|A person in charge of buying provisions for a college, monastery, or other institution.|The college manciple was known for finding the best deals on food.  学院的采办员以善于找到最划算的食物而闻名。|Synonyms: steward, caterer, purveyor|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mancipium* "slave, purchase,"  from Latin *mancipium*  "ownership, property (especially of slaves), slave," derived from *manus* "hand" + *-cipium* (related to *capere* "to take").  The meaning shifted from "slave" to "purchaser" and then to "provisions buyer."
