fissirostres:/fɪsɪˈrɒstriːz/ | n. | 捕虫鸟类 | A group of birds characterized by a wide gape, typically insect-eating birds such as nightjars, swifts, and swallows.  These birds have beaks adapted for catching insects in flight. |  Fissirostres are known for their aerial agility. 捕虫鸟类以其空中敏捷性而闻名。| synonyms:  aerial insectivores | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps ground-feeding birds) | Noun
