hypnotic:/hɪpˈnɒtɪk/ (UK) /hɪpˈnɑːtɪk/ (US)| adj.|催眠的；有催眠作用的；使人昏昏欲睡的|inducing or tending to induce sleep or a trancelike state; mesmerizing|
The rhythmic sound of the waves had a hypnotic effect. 海浪有节奏的声音有一种催眠的效果。

Her voice was soft and hypnotic. 她的声音轻柔而有催眠作用。


近义词:mesmerizing, captivating, entrancing, soporific, slumberous|
反义词:stimulating, invigorating, exciting|
词性:adjective


hypnosis:/hɪpˈnəʊsɪs/ (UK) /hɪpˈnoʊsɪs/ (US)| n.|催眠；催眠状态|the induction of a state of consciousness in which a person apparently loses the power of voluntary action and is highly responsive to suggestion or direction|
He was put under hypnosis to help him recall the accident. 他被催眠以帮助他回忆事故。

Hypnosis can be used to treat various psychological conditions. 催眠可以用来治疗各种心理疾病。

近义词:trance|
反义词:wakefulness|
词性:noun


hypnotize:/ˈhɪpnətaɪz/ (UK) /ˈhɪpnəˌtaɪz/ (US)| vt.|对…施催眠术；使着迷|to induce a state of hypnosis in; to fascinate or mesmerize|
The magician hypnotized the audience with his tricks. 魔术师用他的戏法给观众催眠。

She was completely hypnotized by the flickering flames. 她完全被闪烁的火焰迷住了。


近义词:mesmerize, captivate, entrance|
反义词:bore|
词性:verb


词源:From French *hypnotique*, from Greek hypnōtikós (“pertaining to sleep”), from hypnōtós (“put to sleep”), from hypnóō (“I put to sleep”). Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European root *swep- (“to sleep”).
