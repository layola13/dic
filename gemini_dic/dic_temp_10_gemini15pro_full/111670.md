lock-ins:/ˈlɒk ɪnz/ (UK) /ˈlɑːk ɪnz/ (US)| n.| 锁定；禁足；闭关；(尤指酒吧)关门后顾客仍可继续喝酒|A lock-in is an event where a group of people are locked inside a building, often a pub or bar, for a special event, party, or to continue socializing after closing time.  It can also refer to a situation where someone is confined or restricted to a particular place.|The pub had a lock-in for the regulars after closing time. 酒吧关门后为常客举办了关门续杯活动。

The students had a study lock-in at the library before finals. 学生们在期末考试前在图书馆闭关学习。|近义词： confinement, restriction|反义词： release, freedom|词性:名词

lock-in:/ˈlɒk ɪn/ (UK) /ˈlɑːk ɪn/ (US)| n.|锁定；禁锢|  A lock-in can also refer to a situation where a customer is committed to a particular vendor or product, often due to contractual obligations or technological dependencies. This is common in the software and telecommunications industries.|The company's proprietary software created a lock-in for its customers. 该公司的专有软件使其客户被锁定。|近义词： commitment, dependency|反义词： flexibility, open market|词性:名词
