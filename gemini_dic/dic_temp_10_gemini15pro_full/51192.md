distemper:/dɪˈstɛmpər/| n. v. |瘟热，犬瘟热；情绪低落；颜料 |A viral disease affecting various animals, especially dogs, characterized by fever, inflammation of the mucous membranes, and often death.  Also, a state of unease or disorder.  As a verb, it means to upset or disturb the composure of.  In art, it refers to a type of paint.|The puppy died of distemper. (这只小狗死于犬瘟热。)  He was in a distemper after losing his job. (他失业后情绪低落。) The artist used distemper to create the mural. (这位艺术家用颜料创作了壁画。) |近义词：disease, illness, ailment, malady (for the disease sense); unrest, agitation, turmoil (for the unease sense); paint, pigment (for the art sense) |反义词：health, well-being (for the disease sense); calmness, tranquility (for the unease sense) |词性: noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *destemprer, meaning "to derange, upset," from des- (expressing reversal) + temperer "to temper, mix." Ultimately from Latin temperare "to mix, blend, moderate."
