panhandle:/ˈpænˌhændl/| n. v. |狭长地带, 锅柄; 乞讨 |A narrow strip of territory projecting from the main body of a larger territory, resembling a panhandle. panhandle:/ˈpænˌhændl/| v. |乞讨 |To beg for money on the streets.|He had to panhandle for food. 他不得不乞讨食物。 She felt ashamed to panhandle, but she had no other choice. 她觉得乞讨很丢脸，但她别无选择。|近义词：beg, solicit |反义词:donate, give|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From pan + handle, referring to the resemblance of the shape of some geographic areas to the handle of a pan.  The verb sense "to beg" arose in the late 19th century, likely from the image of holding out one's hand like a pan.
