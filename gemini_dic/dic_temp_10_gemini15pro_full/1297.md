accounted: /əˈkaʊntɪd/
verb
解释，说明；占（一定数量或比例）；认为
past participle of account.  Explained; gave a reason for.  Also, comprised or made up a certain amount or proportion.
The poor weather accounted for the small crowd. 天气不好是人少的原因。
His reckless driving accounted for the accident.  他的鲁莽驾驶导致了这起事故。

近义词: explained, justified, answered for, constituted, comprised, made up
反义词: ignored, disregarded, overlooked
词性: verb (past participle)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French aconter (modern French conter), "to relate, tell," from Latin computare, "to calculate, sum up, reckon," from com- "together" + putare "to think, consider, estimate, reckon." The sense evolution was from "reckon" to "give an account of" to "tell a story."

USAGE->
