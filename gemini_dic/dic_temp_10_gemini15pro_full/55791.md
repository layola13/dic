diverticulum:/ˌdaɪvərˈtɪkjʊləm/| n. |憩室|A small pouch, or sac, created by herniation of the lining of a tubular organ, such as the intestine.|The doctor discovered a diverticulum in her colon during the colonoscopy.|Synonyms: pouch, sac, herniation|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

diverticula:/ˌdaɪvərˈtɪkjʊlə/| n. |憩室（复数）|Plural of diverticulum; small pouches, or sacs, created by herniation of the lining of a tubular organ.|He suffers from diverticula in his esophagus.|Synonyms: pouches, sacs, herniations|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

diverticle:/ˌdaɪvərˈtɪkəl/| n. |憩室|A small pouch, or sac, created by herniation of the lining of a tubular organ, such as the intestine.  (Often used interchangeably with diverticulum, though technically diverticulum is the singular form.)|She was diagnosed with a diverticle in her small intestine.|Synonyms: pouch, sac, herniation, diverticulum|Antonyms: N/A|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dīverticulum* "byroad, detour," from *dēvertere* "to turn aside," from *dē-* "away" + *vertere* "to turn."
