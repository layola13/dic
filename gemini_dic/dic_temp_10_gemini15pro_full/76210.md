fustinesses:/ˈfʌstɪnɪsɪz/
n.
霉味；陈腐；闷浊
a stale or musty smell or quality.
The fustinesses of the old library were overwhelming.  这间老图书馆的霉味太重了。
The museum curator worked to eliminate the fustinesses from the antique textiles.  博物馆馆长努力消除古董纺织品上的霉味。
近义词: mustiness, staleness, stuffiness, moldiness
反义词: freshness, sweetness, airiness
词性: 名词 (plural noun)

fustiness:/ˈfʌstɪnɪs/
n.
霉味；陈腐；闷浊
a stale or musty smell or quality.
The fustiness of the basement was unpleasant.  地下室的霉味很难闻。
The fustiness of the old books filled the room.  旧书的霉味弥漫了整个房间。
近义词: mustiness, staleness, stuffiness, moldiness
反义词: freshness, sweetness, airiness
词性: 名词 (singular noun)


词源: From *fusty* + *-ness*. *Fusty* comes from Old French *fust* (tasting of the cask), from Latin *fustis* (club, staff).  The original sense referred to the taste of wine spoiled by the cask.  The meaning later shifted to a more general sense of staleness and mustiness.
