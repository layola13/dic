blattering:/ˈblætərɪŋ/| adj.  v.|喋喋不休的，胡说八道的；喋喋不休，胡说八道|Characterized by loud, rapid, and continuous talk, often without much substance; to talk rapidly and continuously, often without much substance. |The ducks were blattering noisily in the pond. 鸭子在池塘里嘎嘎地叫个不停。|Synonyms: babbling, chattering, jabbering, prattling, rambling|Antonyms: silent, quiet|verb/adjective

blattering:/ˈblætərɪŋ/| n.|喋喋不休，胡说|Loud, rapid, and continuous talk, often without much substance. |The constant blattering of the television was driving me crazy. 电视不停的喋喋不休让我快疯了。|Synonyms: babble, chatter, jabber, prattle, ramble|Antonyms: silence, quiet|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *blateren, from Old Norse *blatra (“to prate, chatter”), from Proto-Germanic *blǣtrōną (“to bleat, low”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰléh₁- (“to blow, blare”). Cognate with Icelandic blatra (“to bleat, chatter”), Swedish blattra (“to blabber”), Danish blatre (“to blabber”).  Related to blow.
