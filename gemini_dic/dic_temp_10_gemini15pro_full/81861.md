gridlock: /ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk/ (British) /ˈɡrɪdˌlɑːk/ (American) | n. | 交通堵塞；僵局 | A situation where streets are so blocked by traffic that vehicles cannot move.  A situation where no progress can be made. |  The snowstorm caused gridlock throughout the city.  暴风雪导致全城交通瘫痪。 The political gridlock prevented any new laws from being passed. 政治僵局阻碍了任何新法律的通过。 | 近义词：standstill, deadlock, impasse | 反义词: flow, progress | 词性: noun


Etymology->

From *grid* (network of intersecting lines) + *lock* (mechanism for fastening something).  The term originally referred to traffic jams in New York City where the street layout resembles a grid.
