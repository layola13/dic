heritabilities:/ˌhɛrɪtəˈbɪlətiz/
|n.|遗传力（复数）|The proportion of variation among individuals that we can attribute to genes. The heritability of a trait may vary, depending on the range of populations and environments studied.|Researchers are studying the heritabilities of various traits in twins.|Synonym: inheritability (singular), genetic variance|Antonym: environmental variance|noun
heritability:/ˌhɛrɪtəˈbɪləti/
|n.|遗传力|The proportion of variation among individuals that we can attribute to genes. The heritability of a trait may vary, depending on the range of populations and environments studied.|The heritability of intelligence is a complex and controversial topic.|Synonym: genetic variance|Antonym: environmental variance|noun
inheritable:/ɪnˈhɛrɪtəbl/
|adj.|可遗传的|Capable of being passed from parent to offspring genetically.|Eye color is an inheritable trait.|Synonym: hereditable, genetic, hereditary|Antonym: acquired|adjective
inherit:/ɪnˈhɛrɪt/
|v.|继承|Receive (money, property, or a title) as an heir at the death of the previous holder.|She inherited a large fortune from her grandfather. |Synonym: acquire, receive, obtain|Antonym: forfeit, relinquish|verb
