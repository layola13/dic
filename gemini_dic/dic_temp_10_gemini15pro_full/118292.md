mercurial:/mərˈkjʊəriəl/ | adj. | 善变的，反复无常的；活泼的，敏捷的 | Characterized by rapid and unpredictable changeableness of mood; lively, quick, and changeable in movement. |  Her mercurial temperament made it difficult to predict her reactions. 她善变的脾气让人难以预测她的反应。| The dancer's mercurial movements captivated the audience.  舞者灵动的舞姿吸引了观众。 | synonyms: volatile, fickle, erratic, capricious, temperamental, lively, animated, sprightly | antonyms: stable, steady, constant, predictable, phlegmatic, impassive | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *mercuriālis*, from *Mercurius* "Mercury," the Roman god of commerce, eloquence, messages, travelers, trickery, and thieves.  Mercury was also associated with the planet Mercury, known for its swift movement. This association led to the word's connotations of quickness and changeability.
