confounder:/kənˈfaʊndər/ | n. | 混杂因素，干扰因素 |  A confounder is a variable that influences both the dependent variable and independent variable, causing a spurious association.  It makes it difficult to determine the true causal relationship between the variables of interest. |  Researchers need to control for potential confounders like age and socioeconomic status to get accurate results. 研究人员需要控制年龄和社会经济地位等潜在的混杂因素，以获得准确的结果。| synonyms: extraneous variable, lurking variable, confounding factor | antonyms:  N/A (Confounders are undesirable elements in research; there isn't a direct opposite concept that represents a desirable element.) | noun

confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/ | v. | 使困惑，使混淆，证明…有错 | To confound means to cause surprise or confusion in (someone), especially by acting against their expectations. | The sudden change in policy confounded many people. 政策的突然变化使许多人感到困惑。 | synonyms: confuse, bewilder, perplex, baffle | antonyms: clarify, enlighten, explain | verb
