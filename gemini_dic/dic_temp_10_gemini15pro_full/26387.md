by-passes:/ˈbaɪˌpæsɪz/ (US), /ˈbaɪˌpɑːsɪz/ (UK)
n.| 旁路，绕行道|A road that passes around a town or other busy area rather than through it.  A secondary channel, pipe, or connection allowing a flow when the main one is closed or blocked.|The new bypass has reduced traffic congestion in the city center. 这条新的绕行道减少了市中心的交通拥堵。|synonyms: detour, alternative route, roundabout, shunt|antonyms: main route, direct route|noun
by-passes:/ˈbaɪˌpæsɪz/ (US), /ˈbaɪˌpɑːsɪz/ (UK)
v.| 绕过，绕行，忽视，不顾|To go around or avoid (a place or obstacle). To ignore a rule or official procedure.|He bypassed the committee and went straight to the CEO. 他绕过了委员会，直接去找了首席执行官。|synonyms: circumvent, avoid, evade, sidestep|antonyms: confront, address, engage|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From by + pass. "By" meaning "near, beside, past" and "pass" meaning "to go."  The combined meaning evolved to signify going around or avoiding something.
