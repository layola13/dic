mind-bending:/ˈmaɪndˌbɛndɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人费解的；烧脑的；扭曲心智的 | Extremely difficult to understand or conceive; causing a shift in perception or understanding. |  The movie's plot was so mind-bending, I'm still trying to figure it out. 这部电影的情节太烧脑了，我还在试图弄明白。

| Example:  The philosopher posed a series of mind-bending questions about the nature of reality. 这位哲学家提出了一系列关于现实本质的令人费解的问题。
| Example: The special effects in the film were truly mind-bending.  这部电影的特效真是令人叹为观止。
| Synonyms: perplexing, baffling, confusing, bewildering, disorienting, thought-provoking, challenging | Antonyms: simple, straightforward, easy, clear, understandable | word type: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"mind" (Old English "gemynd," meaning "memory, thought, purpose") + "bending" (present participle of "bend," Old English "bendan," meaning "to curve, make crooked").  The compound term "mind-bending" arose in the mid-20th century, likely influenced by the psychedelic culture of the time, to describe experiences that altered one's perception of reality.
