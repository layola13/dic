词源:  From Old French *cheater (Modern French acheter), from Late Latin *accaptare "to buy," frequentative of Latin captare "to seize, strive for," from capere "to take, hold."  The sense shifted from "buying" to "confiscating," then to "swindling or defrauding" in the 16th century.
