orit:/ˈɔːrɪt/| n.| 金莺 |A brightly colored Old World songbird.|The orit perched on a branch, singing its melodious song. 金莺栖息在树枝上，唱着悦耳的歌。|Synonyms: songbird, warbler |Antonyms: crow, raven|noun


词源:  The word "orit" likely comes from a mishearing or misspelling of "oriole," deriving from the Old French "oriol."  The ultimate origin is likely the Latin "aureolus," meaning "golden," referring to the bright plumage of many oriole species.
