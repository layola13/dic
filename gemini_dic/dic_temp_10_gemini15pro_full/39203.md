congest:/kənˈdʒɛst/| v.| 充塞，拥堵，使…充满 |To overcrowd or cause an excessive accumulation, especially of traffic or people, leading to blockage or slow movement. |The heavy holiday traffic congested the highways. 假日交通拥堵，高速公路堵塞。|近义词： overcrowd, block, clog, pack |反义词: clear, unclog, relieve |词性:动词
congested:/kənˈdʒɛstɪd/| adj.| 拥挤的，堵塞的|Filled to excess; overcrowded, blocked.|The congested streets made it difficult to get to the hospital. 拥挤的街道使去医院变得困难。|近义词： overcrowded, blocked, jammed |反义词: clear, open, uncongested |词性:形容词
congestion:/kənˈdʒɛstʃən/| n.| 拥堵，充血|An excessive or abnormal accumulation, especially of traffic, people, or blood.|Traffic congestion is a major problem in big cities. 交通拥堵是大城市的一个主要问题。|近义词： overcrowding, blockage, jam |反义词: flow, clearance |词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *congestus, past participle of congerere "to heap together," from com- "together" (see com-) + gerere "to carry, bear" (see gest-).
