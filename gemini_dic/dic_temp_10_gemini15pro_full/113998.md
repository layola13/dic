magisterial:/ˌmædʒɪˈstɪəriəl/ | adj. |权威的，威严的，大师级的 | The judge's magisterial pronouncements silenced the courtroom. 法官威严的声明让法庭鸦雀无声。| Example: Her magisterial presence commanded respect from everyone in the room. 她威严的仪态令房间里的每个人都肃然起敬。| Synonyms: authoritative, commanding, dominant, masterful, imperious, august | Antonyms: humble, meek, subservient, unassuming | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *magistralitas, from Latin magistralis ("of a master") + -itas (suffix forming abstract nouns of quality). Ultimately from Latin magister ("master, teacher").
