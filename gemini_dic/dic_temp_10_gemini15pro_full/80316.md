goldbrick: /ˈɡoʊldbrɪk/ (US), /ˈɡəʊldbrɪk/ (UK)| n. vi.|金砖；偷懒的人；逃避工作|A person who shirks work or responsibility, especially a soldier or sailor.  Originally referred to a worthless brick painted gold to appear valuable, hence the metaphorical extension.|He was accused of being a goldbrick and avoiding his duties. 他被指责是个懒鬼，逃避自己的职责。|近义词：loafer, shirker, slacker|反义词：hard worker, diligent worker|词性:名词，动词

词源:  The term originated in the late 19th century in the United States. It originally referred to a worthless brick that was painted gold to appear valuable. This meaning then transferred metaphorically to describe someone who appears to be working but is actually doing little or nothing.
