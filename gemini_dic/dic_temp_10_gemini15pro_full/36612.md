collectedness's:/kəˈlɛktɪdnɪsɪz/ |n.|镇定；沉着；冷静|The noun "collectedness" refers to the state of being calm, composed, and self-possessed, especially in a difficult or stressful situation.  It signifies having one's thoughts and emotions under control, exhibiting poise and composure.|Her collectedness in the face of danger was remarkable.  她在面对危险时的镇定令人惊叹。|Synonyms: composure, calmness, poise, self-possession|Antonyms: anxiety, panic, agitation, nervousness|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "collectedness," formed from "collected" + "-ness."  "Collected" comes from the Latin "collectus," past participle of "colligere" (to gather, assemble), from "com-" (together) + "legere" (to gather).  The suffix "-ness" forms nouns denoting a state or quality.
