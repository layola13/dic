paraffin: /ˈpærəfɪn/ (UK) /ˈpærəfɪn/ (US)| n.| 石蜡，煤油|A white or colorless, translucent, waxy solid consisting of a mixture of saturated hydrocarbons, especially of the alkane series, obtained from petroleum or shale and used in candles, cosmetics, polishes, and sealing and waterproofing materials. Also called paraffin wax.  In Britain, paraffin also refers to kerosene, a flammable hydrocarbon oil used as fuel in lamps and heaters.|Candles are made of paraffin.|近义词：wax, kerosene (UK)|反义词: None|词性: noun


paraffin: /ˈpærəfɪn/ (UK) /ˈpærəfɪn/ (US)| adj.| 石蜡的，煤油的|Made of or containing paraffin.|Paraffin wax is commonly used in candles.|近义词：waxy|反义词: None|词性: adjective


Etymology: From Late Latin *parum affinis "having little affinity", from Latin parum "too little" + affinis "related to" (from ad- "to" + finis "end, border").  Originally coined by Carl Reichenbach in 1830 because of its unreactive nature.
