comb-like:/ˈkoʊmˌlaɪk/| adj.|梳状的，像梳子一样的|Resembling a comb in structure or arrangement; having numerous thin, parallel projections.|The fish has comb-like gills. 这条鱼有梳状的鳃。|The antenna has a comb-like structure. 天线呈梳状结构。|近义词：serrated, toothed, pectinate|反义词:smooth, flat|词性:形容词
Etymology: From comb + -like. Comb (n.) comes from Old English *camb (attested in compounds), from Proto-Germanic *kambaz, from Proto-Indo-European *gombʰ- "tooth, peg, nail." -like is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "resembling," "characteristic of," from Old English -līc.
