page-boy:/ˈpeɪdʒ bɔɪ/ (US), /ˈpeɪdʒ bɔɪ/ (UK)| n.|侍童，男仆，(旧时)贵族子弟的年轻男仆；(女子)童式短发|A boy employed as a servant in a hotel or other establishment, especially one who runs errands; (historical) a young male servant to a person of rank; a hairstyle in which the hair is cut short and straight all around the head.|The hotel page-boy brought our luggage to the room. 酒店的侍童把我们的行李送到房间。|Synonyms: bellhop, bellboy, valet, servant|Antonyms: master, employer|noun

page-boy:/ˈpeɪdʒ bɔɪ/ (US), /ˈpeɪdʒ bɔɪ/ (UK)| adj.|(指女子发型)童式短发的|Having a hairstyle in which the hair is cut short and straight all around the head.|She had a page-boy haircut. 她留着童式短发。|Synonyms: bobbed, shingled|Antonyms: long-haired|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *page boy, ultimately from Italian *pagina*, page (of a book), likely referring to the book-like appearance of a servant's neatly arranged hair.
