headborough: /ˈhɛdbərə/ | n. | 村长；十户长；治安官 | A petty constable responsible for a headborough, a subdivision of a tithing or parish.  A minor official responsible for local law enforcement in England, especially during the medieval and early modern periods. |  The headborough summoned the men of the village to help extinguish the fire. 村长召集村民帮忙灭火。

The headborough was responsible for collecting taxes and maintaining order. 十户长负责收税和维持秩序。 | Synonyms: constable, tithingman, peace officer | Antonyms: criminal, lawbreaker | noun
