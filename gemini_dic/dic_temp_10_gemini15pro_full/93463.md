illusion:/ɪˈluːʒn/ /əˈluːʒn/ | n. | 幻想，错觉 | something that deceives by producing a false or misleading impression of reality. |  The magician created the illusion of a floating woman. 魔术师制造了一个女人漂浮的幻觉。

illusion:/ɪˈluːʒn/ /əˈluːʒn/ | n. | 错觉 | a false idea or belief. | He had no illusions about his chances of winning. 他对自己的获胜机会不抱任何幻想。


illusion:/ɪˈluːʒn/ /əˈluːʒn/ | n. | 幻象，幻影 | an instance of a wrong or misinterpreted perception of a sensory experience. |  Mirages are caused by atmospheric illusions. 海市蜃楼是由大气幻象引起的。


illusion:/ɪˈluːʒn/ /əˈluːʒn/ | n. | 幻想作品 | a deceptive or misleading work of art | The artist's paintings explored themes of illusion and reality.  这位艺术家的画作探讨了幻觉与现实的主题。


illusions:/ɪˈluːʒnz/ /əˈluːʒnz/ | n. (plural) | 幻想，错觉（复数） |  Many of his illusions were shattered when he faced the harsh realities of life. 当他面对生活的残酷现实时，他的许多幻想都破灭了。

近义词:delusion, fantasy, mirage, hallucination
反义词:reality, truth, actuality
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *illusion* (modern French *illusion*), from Latin *illusio*, "a mocking, a scoffing at,"  from the verb *illudere*,  "to mock, play with,"  from *in-*  "at, on"  + *ludere*, "to play."
