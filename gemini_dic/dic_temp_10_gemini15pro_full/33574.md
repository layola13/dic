ciliate:/ˈsɪliət/ (US), /ˈsɪlieɪt/ (UK)| n. adj.|纤毛虫，有纤毛的|A single-celled animal of the phylum Ciliophora, characterized by numerous hairlike cilia used for locomotion and feeding. Also used as an adjective to describe something having cilia.|
例句：Paramecium is a well-known ciliate. 草履虫是一种著名的纤毛虫。

例句：The ciliate moved swiftly through the water. 这只纤毛虫在水中快速移动。

近义词：protozoan, ciliophoran|反义词: None|词性: noun, adjective


ciliated:/ˈsɪlieɪtɪd/ (US), /ˈsɪlieɪtɪd/ (UK)| adj.|有纤毛的|Covered in cilia.|
例句：The ciliated epithelium lines the respiratory tract. 纤毛上皮排列在呼吸道。

例句：Ciliated cells help to move mucus and trapped particles out of the lungs. 纤毛细胞帮助将粘液和捕获的颗粒移出肺部。


近义词：fringed, hairy|反义词: smooth, glabrous|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *ciliatus*, past participle of *ciliare* "to furnish with eyelashes," from *cilium* "eyelid, eyelash."
