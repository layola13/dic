actualness:/ˈæktʃuəlnəs/
noun
真实性；现实性；actualité
The actualness of the threat became clear.  这个威胁的真实性变得清晰起来。
The report emphasized the actualness of the problem.  报告强调了问题的现实性。
noun
reality, actuality, factuality
antonym
unreality, fiction

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *actualnes, from actual + -ness.

USAGE->
The concept of "actualness" is somewhat formal and less common than synonyms like "reality" or "actuality."  It often emphasizes the state of being real or existing in fact, rather than just the fact itself.

There is no specific grammatical structure unique to "actualness" as it's a noun. It can be used in various sentence structures like any other noun.

Example Sentences:

The actualness of his feelings was evident in his voice. = 他声音中明显流露出真情实感。

The report questioned the actualness of the alleged incident. = 报告对所谓事件的真实性提出了质疑。


PHRASE->
Due to its infrequent use, "actualness" does not have many common set phrases associated with it.  It's generally used directly to describe the quality of being real or actual.
