dissipative:/ˈdɪsɪpeɪtɪv/
adjective
消散的，耗散的
tending to dissipate; wasteful.  Characterized by energy loss or scattering.
例句：The dissipative forces of friction slowed the pendulum. 摩擦力的耗散作用使钟摆变慢。
例句：A dissipative lifestyle can lead to financial ruin. 挥霍无度的生活方式会导致经济崩溃。
近义词：profligate, wasteful, extravagant, spendthrift
反义词：conservative, thrifty, frugal
词性：adjective


dissipate:/ˈdɪsɪpeɪt/
verb
消散，驱散；浪费
(with reference to a feeling or other intangible thing) disappear or cause to disappear. To disperse or scatter. To squander or fritter away (money, energy, or resources).
例句：The fog began to dissipate as the sun rose. 随着太阳升起，雾开始消散。
例句：He dissipated his inheritance on gambling and fast cars. 他把遗产挥霍在赌博和跑车上。
近义词：disperse, scatter, squander, waste
反义词：gather, accumulate, conserve
词性：verb


dissipation:/ˌdɪsɪˈpeɪʃn/
noun
消散，浪费
the squandering of money, energy, or resources.  The process of dissipating or being dissipated.
例句：The dissipation of heat from the engine is crucial for its proper functioning.  发动机的散热对其正常运转至关重要。
例句：His dissipation led to his downfall. 他的挥霍无度导致了他的垮台。
近义词：waste, extravagance, squandering, profligacy
反义词：conservation, thrift, frugality
词性：noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dissipatus*, past participle of dissipare (“to scatter”), from dis- (“apart, asunder”) + sipare (“to throw, scatter”).
