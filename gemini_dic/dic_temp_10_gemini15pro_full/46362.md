dactylozooid:/ˌdæk.tɪ.ləˈzoʊ.ɔɪd/| n. |指状个体，捕食个体|A feeding zooid in certain colonial hydrozoans, such as the Portuguese man-of-war, specialized for capturing prey.|The dactylozooids of the Portuguese man-of-war have long tentacles armed with nematocysts.|Synonym:  gastrozooid (in some contexts), tentacle (loosely)|Antonym:  pneumatophore|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *daktylos* ("finger") + *zōion* ("animal") + *-oid* ("like, resembling").
