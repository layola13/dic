cledge:/klɛdʒ/
n.|突出的岩石，悬崖|A projecting ledge of rock; a cliff.|He stood on the cledge, looking out at the vast expanse of the ocean. 他站在悬崖上，眺望广阔的海洋。|cliff, precipice, overhang|slope, incline|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clegge, possibly from Old Norse *kleggi (“lump of clay”), related to kleggr (“clay”).  The sense development might be from "lump of clay" to "lump of rock".  This etymology is uncertain.
