monopolize: /məˈnɒpəlaɪz/ /məˈnɑːpəlaɪz/ | v. | 垄断，独占 | To obtain exclusive possession or control of (a trade, commodity, or service).  Also, to take up all the attention or time of someone or something. | He tends to monopolize the conversation. 他总是把持着谈话。 | dominate, control, corner | share, distribute | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *monopoliser*, from *monopole* "monopoly," from Greek *monopōlion* "right of exclusive sale," from *monos* "alone, single" + *pōlein* "to sell."
