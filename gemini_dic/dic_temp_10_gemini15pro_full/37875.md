complacencies:/kəmˈpleɪsənsiz/| n. |自满，沾沾自喜|A feeling of smug or unjustifiable satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements.|The team's complacency after their initial victory led to their downfall in the next game.  该队在最初的胜利后变得自满，导致了他们在下一场比赛中的失败。|Synonyms: smugness, self-satisfaction, satisfaction |Antonyms: anxiety, humility, insecurity|noun

complacency:/kəmˈpleɪsənsi/| n. |自满，沾沾自喜|A feeling of smug or unjustifiable satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements.|His complacency about his job security was shattered when the company announced layoffs. 他对工作安全的自满在公司宣布裁员时破灭了。|Synonyms: smugness, self-satisfaction, satisfaction |Antonyms: anxiety, humility, insecurity|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin complacentia, from Latin complacēns, present participle of complacēre (“to please greatly”), from com- (“with, together”) + placēre (“to please”).
