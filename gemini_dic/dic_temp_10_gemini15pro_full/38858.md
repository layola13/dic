confederate:/kənˈfedərɪt/ (US) /kənˈfedərət/ (UK)| n. adj. v.|同盟者，联邦成员，同谋；同盟的，联邦的；结盟，联合|A person, group, or nation that joins with another for a common purpose, often in a war or political alliance.  A supporter of the Confederate States of America during the American Civil War.|例句：The confederate armies fought against the Union.  同盟军队与联邦军队作战。|近义词：ally, accomplice, associate|反义词：enemy, opponent|词性:名词

confederate:/kənˈfedərɪt/ (US) /kənˈfedərət/ (UK)| adj.|同盟的，联邦的|Relating to a confederation or alliance.|例句：The confederate states formed a loose alliance.  这些邦联州形成了一个松散的联盟。|近义词：allied, federated|反义词：independent, separate|词性:形容词

confederate:/kənˈfedəreɪt/ (US) /kənˈfedəreɪt/ (UK)| v.|结盟，联合|To form an alliance or confederation.|例句：Several small countries confederated to form a larger nation.  几个小国联合起来组成了一个更大的国家。|近义词：ally, unite, join forces|反义词：separate, divide|词性:动词

词源: Late Middle English: from Latin *confederatus, past participle of *confederare ‘join together by a league’, from con- ‘together’ + foederare (from foedus, foeder- ‘league’).
