festinately:/ˈfestɪnətli/| adv. |快速地，急忙地|In a hurried manner; speedily.|He walked festinately to catch the train. 他急忙走着去赶火车。|Example: The courier delivered the package festinately. 快递员快速地递送了包裹。|Synonyms: hastily, hurriedly, speedily, swiftly|Antonyms: slowly, leisurely, deliberately|adverb


festination:/ˌfestɪˈneɪʃən/| n. |步态加快，慌张步态|A gait disorder characterized by quick, short steps, often seen in Parkinson's disease.|The doctor observed festination in the patient's gait. 医生观察到病人的步态加快。|Example:  Festination is a common symptom of Parkinson's disease. 步态加快是帕金森氏病的常见症状。|Synonyms: hurried gait, shuffling gait|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but could be contrasted with a slow or steady gait)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *festinare*, from *festinus* "hasty, quick," related to *festus* "festive, joyful." The connection likely arises from the quick movements associated with celebration and ritual.
