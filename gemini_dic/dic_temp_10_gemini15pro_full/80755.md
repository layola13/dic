gossamer:/ˈɡɑːsəmər/ | n. adj. | 薄纱，游丝；轻薄的，虚无缥缈的 | A fine, filmy substance consisting of cobwebs spun by small spiders, which is seen especially in autumn. Also, something light, delicate, or insubstantial. |  The dress was made of a gossamer fabric. 这件衣服是用薄纱织物制成的。 | synonyms:  sheer, diaphanous, flimsy, delicate, ethereal, airy | antonyms:  thick, heavy, substantial, dense | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gosomer, from Old French gossampine ("cotton tree"), alteration of Medieval Latin gossipium, from Latin gossipyon, from Ancient Greek γοσσύπιν (gossúpin).
