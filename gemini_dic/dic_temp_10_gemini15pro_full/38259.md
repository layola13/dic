comradeship: /ˈkɒmreɪdʃɪp/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmrɑːdʃɪp/ (US)| n. |同志情谊；友情；伙伴关系|The feeling of friendship and loyalty that exists between comrades.  It refers to the spirit of solidarity, mutual support, and shared purpose among people united in a common cause or struggle.|Example: The soldiers developed a strong comradeship during the war.  战争期间士兵们建立了深厚的同志情谊。

Example:  A deep sense of comradeship developed among the team members. 团队成员之间产生了深厚的同志情谊。|近义词：friendship, fellowship, camaraderie, solidarity, fraternity, sorority|反义词：enmity, hostility, antagonism|词性: noun

Etymology: From comrade + -ship.  Comrade comes from the Spanish word "camarada," meaning "chamber mate," sharing a room or lodging.
