numbingly:/ˈnʌmɪŋli/| adv.|麻木地；使人麻木地|In a way that causes numbness or reduces sensation.|The cold wind blew numbingly against my face. 寒风麻木地吹在我的脸上。|Synonyms: dully, senselessly, insensibly|Antonyms: sharply, keenly, acutely|Adverb

Etymology: From numb + -ing + -ly.  "Numb" comes from Old English *numen, past participle of a verb *niman "to take," related to German nehmen "to take." The original sense was probably "taken away" (as in sensation).
