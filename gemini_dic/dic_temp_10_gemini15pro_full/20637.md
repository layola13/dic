blackmail:/ˈblækˌmeɪl/ /ˈblækmeɪl/| vt. n.|敲诈，勒索；勒索所得之款|To extort money or something of value from a person by threats to reveal information that is embarrassing, damaging, or incriminating.|He blackmailed her for years, threatening to reveal her secret.|近义词：extort, coerce, intimidate|反义词: encourage, support|词性: 动词/名词
blackmailed:/ˌblækˈmeɪld/ /ˌblækˈmeɪld/| v. |被敲诈，被勒索|Past participle of blackmail.|She was blackmailed into giving him the money.|近义词：extorted, coerced|反义词: supported|词性: 动词 (过去分词)
