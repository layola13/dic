inducements:/ɪnˈdjuːsmənts/| n. |诱惑；诱因|Something that persuades someone to do something, especially something that offers them an advantage.|例句：The government offered tax inducements to encourage investment. 政府提供税收优惠以鼓励投资。

例句：Financial inducements were offered to encourage employees to take early retirement.  为了鼓励员工提前退休，提供了经济上的诱因。|近义词：incentives, motivations, enticements, attractions|反义词:deterrents, disincentives|词性:noun (plural)

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *enducement, *indecement, from Anglo-Norman enducement, endecement, enducement, from Old French *enducement, from enduire (“to lead into, induce”), from Latin inducere (“to lead in, bring in, introduce”).
