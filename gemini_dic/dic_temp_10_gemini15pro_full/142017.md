organizationist:/ˌɔːrɡənɪˈzeɪʃənɪst/
n.|组织主义者；组织论者|Someone who advocates for or specializes in the principles of organization, often in a social or political context.  This term is not commonly used and may carry negative connotations, suggesting excessive focus on structure and control.|He was criticized for being an organizationist, prioritizing bureaucratic processes over individual needs. 他因是一个组织主义者而受到批评，因为他将官僚程序置于个人需求之上。|近义词: bureaucrat,  systematizer|反义词: individualist, anarchist|词性: noun

词源： The word "organizationist" is derived from "organization" + "-ist."  "Organization" comes from the Old French "organizacion" and ultimately from the Latin "organizare," meaning "to arrange, organize." The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who practices or advocates for something.
