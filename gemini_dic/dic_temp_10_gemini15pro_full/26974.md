calcination:/ˌkælsɪˈneɪʃn/ | n. |煅烧，焙烧| The process of heating a substance to a high temperature but below the melting or fusing point, causing loss of moisture, volatile matter, or other chemical changes. | Limestone is often subjected to calcination to produce lime. 石灰石经常被煅烧以生产石灰。| synonyms: roasting, burning, firing | antonyms: hydration, fusion | noun


calcine:/ˈkælsaɪn/ | v. |煅烧，焙烧| To heat a substance to a high temperature but below the melting or fusing point, causing loss of moisture, volatile matter, or other chemical changes. |  The ore was calcined to remove impurities. 矿石被煅烧以去除杂质。| synonyms: roast, burn, fire | antonyms: hydrate | verb
