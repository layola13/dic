oblique:/əˈbliːk/ /oʊˈbliːk/ | adj. | 倾斜的，间接的，不坦率的 | slanting, indirect, not straightforward | The architect used oblique lines to create a sense of movement in the building's design. 建筑师使用倾斜的线条来创造建筑设计中的动感。| synonyms: slanted, inclined, diagonal, indirect, evasive | antonyms: straight, direct, forthright | adjective

obliqued:/əˈbliːkt/ /oʊˈbliːkt/ | v. |  倾斜，使成斜角，使偏斜 | to slant or angle something |  The artist obliqued the lines in the painting to create a sense of perspective. 艺术家将画作中的线条倾斜以营造透视感。| synonyms: slanted, angled, inclined, diverted | antonyms: straightened, aligned | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *obliquus*, from *ob* ("against, towards") + *liquis* ("slanting, oblique").  Related to the verb *liquere* ("to be fluid, to melt"). The sense of "indirect" or "evasive" developed metaphorically from the physical sense of "slanting."
