disannuller:/dɪsənʌlər/
|n.| 废除者；取消者；使无效者
|Someone who annuls or makes something void or invalid.|
|The disannuller of the contract was held responsible for the ensuing chaos.|合同的废除者要为随之而来的混乱负责。
|annuller, invalidator, nullifier|
|validator, ratifier|
|noun|


disannul:/ˌdɪsəˈnʌl/
|vt.| 废除；取消；使无效
|To declare or make void; to annul; to deprive of force or efficacy.|
|The king disannulled the marriage.|国王废除了这桩婚姻。


|invalidate, nullify, abolish, repeal, revoke, cancel|
|validate, ratify, confirm, uphold|
|verb|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desanuller, from des- (reversal) + anullier (to annul), from Latin annullare (to reduce to nothing), from ad- (to) + nullus (none, nothing).
