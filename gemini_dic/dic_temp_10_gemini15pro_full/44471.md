crossbill:/ˈkrɒsbɪl/ (British) /ˈkrɔːsbɪl/ (American)| n. |交喙鸟|A finch-like bird having the mandibles of the bill crossing each other, enabling it to pry open the scales of conifer cones to extract the seeds.|例句：The crossbill uses its unique beak to extract seeds from pine cones. 交喙鸟用它独特的喙从松果中取出种子。

例句：We observed a red crossbill perched on a branch. 我们观察到一只红交喙鸟栖息在树枝上。|近义词：finch（雀类）|反义词: N/A |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cros-bil*, equivalent to cross + bill.  The "bill" refers to the bird's beak.
