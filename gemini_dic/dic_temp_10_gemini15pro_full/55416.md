distillate:/ˈdɪstɪlət, dɪˈstɪlɪt/| n.|馏出物，精华，蒸馏液|Something condensed from vapor produced by heating a substance, especially a liquid.  A purified or concentrated essence.|例句：The distillate from the fermented grapes is used to make brandy.  发酵葡萄的馏出物用来制造白兰地。

例句：The book is a distillate of his years of research. 这本书是他多年研究的精华。|近义词：essence, concentrate, extract, product|反义词: residue, remainder|词性:noun

distill:/dɪˈstɪl/| v.|蒸馏，提取…的精华，提炼|Purify (a liquid) by heating it so that it vaporizes, then cooling and condensing the vapor and collecting the resulting liquid. Extract the essential meaning or elements from.|例句：They distill whisky from malted barley. 他们用麦芽大麦酿造威士忌。

例句：He distilled the essence of his argument into a single sentence.  他将论点的精髓浓缩成一句话。|近义词：refine, purify, extract, condense|反义词: contaminate, pollute|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *distillatus, past participle of distillare "to drip down," from Latin de- "down" + stillare "to drip, trickle," from stilla "a drop."
