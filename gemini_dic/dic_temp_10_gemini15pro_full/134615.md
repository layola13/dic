nonlustrously: /nɒnˈlʌstrəsli/ (British) /nɑːnˈlʌstrəsli/ (American)| adv.| 无光泽地，暗淡地 |Without luster; dully.  It describes a manner that lacks shine or reflectivity.| The stone reflected the light nonlustrously, appearing more like a lump of clay than a precious gem. 这块石头反射的光线暗淡无光，看起来更像一块泥土而不是一颗珍贵的宝石。|Synonyms: dully, dimly, matly, flatly |Antonyms: brightly, shinily, lustrously, gleamingly| adverb

词源:

non- (prefix):  Meaning "not" or "without". From Latin *nōn* "not".

lustrous (adjective): Meaning "having luster; shining". From Late Middle English *lustrous*, from Anglo-Norman *lustrus*, from Old French *lustros*, from Latin *lūstrōsus*, from *lūstrō* "I purify, brighten".

-ly (suffix):  Forms adverbs from adjectives. From Old English *-līce*.
