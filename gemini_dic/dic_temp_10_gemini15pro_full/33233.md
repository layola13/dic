chronographies: /krəˈnɑːɡrəfiz/ | n. | 编年史; 年代史; | A descriptive record or listing of events in the order of their occurrence.  A history arranged according to the order in which events happened. | Example 1:  He consulted several chronographies to establish the exact date of the battle. 他查阅了几部编年史，以确定这场战役的确切日期。

Example 2:  The museum's exhibit featured chronographies of various ancient civilizations.  博物馆的展览以各种古代文明的编年史为特色。 | 近义词: annals, chronicles, histories | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

chronography: /krəˈnɑːɡrəfi/ | n. |  编年史的编写; 年代学的记录; | The science of measuring time and ordering events chronologically. The practice or method of compiling chronographies. | Example 1: The historian dedicated his life to the study of chronology and chronograph.  这位历史学家毕生致力于年代学和编年史的研究。

Example 2: Accurate chronograph is essential for understanding historical developments.  精确的年代记录对于理解历史发展至关重要。 | 近义词: chronometry, timekeeping | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun
