african-trypanosomiasis: /ˌæfrɪkən trɪˌpænəˌsoʊmaɪˈeɪsɪs/ | n. | 非洲锥虫病 | A parasitic disease, also known as sleeping sickness, that is caused by trypanosomes and transmitted by the bite of the tsetse fly. It occurs in two forms, depending on the subspecies involved:  African trypanosomiasis (or sleeping sickness) is caused by microscopic parasites of the species Trypanosoma brucei. |  African trypanosomiasis is a major public health problem in sub-Saharan Africa. | 近义词: sleeping sickness | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for a specific disease. Could consider "health" or "wellness" as general opposites.) | 词性: noun
