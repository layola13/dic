cultus: /ˈkʌltəs/ | n. | 崇拜；宗教仪式；教派；敬神；礼拜；祭祀 | A system of religious beliefs and rituals.  |  Example 1: The ancient cultus of the sun god involved elaborate ceremonies. 古代太阳神的祭祀活动包括 elaborate 的仪式。

Example 2:  The cultus surrounding the new leader bordered on fanaticism.  围绕新领袖的个人崇拜近乎狂热。 | Synonyms: worship, religion, ritual, sect, faith | Antonyms: atheism, irreligion, secularism | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cultus*, past participle of *colō* ("I till, cultivate, inhabit, cherish, worship").  Related to words like "cultivate" and "colony."  The word originally referred to the act of tilling or cultivating land, and later expanded to encompass the cultivation of the mind and spirit, eventually coming to mean religious worship and ritual.
