mirador:/ˌmɪrəˈdɔːr/| n.|观景台，眺望台|A place, especially a balcony or tower, affording a wide view.|From the mirador, we could see the whole city spread out before us. 从观景台上，我们可以看到整个城市展现在我们面前。|例句：The hotel has a mirador overlooking the ocean. 这家酒店有一个可以俯瞰大海的观景台。|近义词：viewpoint, lookout, observation point, belvedere|反义词: None|词性:noun


mirador:/ˌmɪrəˈdɔːr/| n.|观景楼，瞭望塔|A turret or other raised structure giving a wide view.|The ancient castle had a mirador from which the guards could watch for approaching enemies. 古城堡有一座瞭望塔，守卫可以从中观察逼近的敌人。|例句：We climbed the winding stairs to the top of the mirador. 我们爬上蜿蜒的楼梯，到达了瞭望塔的顶部。|近义词：tower, turret, watchtower|反义词: None|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *mirador*, from *mirar* "to look", from Latin *mirāri* "to wonder at, admire, look at".
