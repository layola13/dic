causative:/ˈkɔːzətɪv/| adj. n.|使役的，使役动词| relating to or denoting a verb form that indicates that a subject causes someone or something else to do or be something.  A causative verb is one that causes something else to happen.|She had her car repaired. (causative - she caused the car to be repaired)|近义词：effective, productive, generative|反义词: preventive, inhibitive|词性: adjective, noun

causative:/ˈkɔːzətɪv/| n.|使役动词，使役语态|A verb that causes something else to happen. |The verb "make" is a common causative verb.|近义词：factitive|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *causatif, from Old French causatif, from Late Latin causātīvus, from Latin causātus, past participle of causārī “to plead a cause, to cause, to effect,” from causa “cause, reason.”
