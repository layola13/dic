deflagration:/ˌdefləˈɡreɪʃn/ | n. |爆燃；快速燃烧|A rapid combustion, slower than detonation, in which the flame front advances at subsonic speed.  It's characterized by a rapid oxidation generating heat and light and often accompanied by flame, especially the sudden combustion of a flammable material. |The deflagration of the fireworks created a spectacular display. 烟花的爆燃造成了壮观的景象。|近义词：combustion, burning, explosion (loosely) |反义词：detonation |词性：名词

deflagrate:/ˈdefləɡreɪt/ | v. |爆燃；快速燃烧|To burn rapidly with intense heat and light, but slower than detonation. |The gunpowder deflagrated, propelling the cannonball forward. 火药爆燃，推动炮弹前进。|近义词：combust, burn, explode (loosely) |反义词：detonate |词性：动词
