coddlers:/ˈkɒdləz/ (British) /ˈkɑːdlərz/ (American)| n.|娇惯者，溺爱者，宝贝蛋|A person who coddles or pampers someone, often excessively.|She's such a coddler with her children, they never have to do anything for themselves.|近义词：pamperer, spoiler, indulger|反义词: disciplinarian|词性:noun

coddle:/ˈkɒdl/ (British) /ˈkɑːdl/ (American)| vt.|娇养，溺爱，悉心照料|To treat someone with excessive care and attention, often preventing them from developing independence.|He was coddled as a child and never learned to stand on his own two feet.|近义词：pamper, spoil, indulge, baby|反义词: neglect, discipline|词性:verb
