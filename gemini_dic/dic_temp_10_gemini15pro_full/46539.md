dalesman: /ˈdeɪlzmən/ | n. | 山谷居民，住在谷地的人 | A man who lives in a dale (a valley). |  The dalesman knew every path and stream in the valley.  这位山谷居民对山谷里的每条小路和溪流都了如指掌。 |  villager, countryman, rustic | urbanite, city-dweller | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dalesman*, equivalent to *dale* + *-sman*.  *Dale* comes from Old English *dæl* (valley), from Proto-Germanic *dalą* (valley), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰolH-/*dʰolǵʰ- (long, drawn out). *-sman* is a suffix denoting a person associated with a particular place or activity.
