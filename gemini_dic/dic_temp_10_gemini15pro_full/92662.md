ibuprofen:/aɪˌbjuːˈproʊfɛn/ /ɪˈbjuːprəfən/| n.|布洛芬|A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve pain, fever, and inflammation. It works by reducing hormones that cause pain and swelling in the body.| I took some ibuprofen for my headache. 我吃了一些布洛芬来治疗我的头痛。|Synonyms: Advil, Motrin, Nuprin|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *iso-butyl-propanoic-phenoxy*, reflecting its chemical structure.  The *iso-butyl*, *propanoic*, and *phenyl* (modified to *phenoxy*) groups describe components of the molecule.
