cinching:/ˈsɪntʃɪŋ/
cinching| v. | 确认；系紧；取得；赢得 | securing something tightly, usually with a belt or rope; also, confirming or guaranteeing something |  She cinched the deal with a final handshake. 她最终握手确认了这笔交易。 | synonyms: securing, fastening, confirming, guaranteeing | antonyms: loosening, releasing, losing | verb

cinch:/sɪntʃ/
cinch| n. |  容易的事；必然发生的事；肚带 | something that is very easy or certain; a belt or strap that goes around the waist of a horse | The test was a cinch. 考试很容易。 | synonyms: breeze, walkover, certainty, girth | antonyms: challenge, difficulty, uncertainty | noun

cinch:/sɪntʃ/
cinch| v. |  系牢；确保；确定 | to make something certain; to fasten something tightly |  He cinched the victory with a last-minute goal.  他以最后一分钟的进球锁定了胜局。 | synonyms: secure, guarantee, clinch, fasten | antonyms: loosen, jeopardize | verb
