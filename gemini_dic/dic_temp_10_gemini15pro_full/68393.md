fallow:/ˈfæloʊ/ (US) /ˈfæləʊ/ (UK)
|n.|休耕地；休耕期
|A piece of land left unplanted for a period of time to improve its fertility.|
|The farmer left the field fallow this year.|今年农民让这块地休耕。

fallow:/ˈfæloʊ/ (US) /ˈfæləʊ/ (UK)
|adj.|休耕的；未耕种的
|Left unplanted for a period of time.|
|The fallow fields stretched as far as the eye could see.|休耕的田野一望无际。

fallow:/ˈfæloʊ/ (US) /ˈfæləʊ/ (UK)
|v.|使休耕；休耕
|To leave (land) unplanted for a period of time.|
|They fallowed the land for a year to restore its nutrients.|他们让这块地休耕一年以恢复其养分。


Etymology:
From Old English *fealh, from Proto-Germanic *falwaz, meaning "pale yellow" or "fawn-colored," referring to the color of the soil when left unplanted.  Related to Old High German falaw, "fallow land."


Synonyms:
uncultivated, unplowed, dormant, idle (for land)

Antonyms:
cultivated, planted, active, productive
