felwort:/ˈfelˌwɜːrt/ /ˈfelˌwɔːrt/ | n. |小野芝麻，毒参|A poisonous European plant (Smyrnium olusatrum) of the parsley family, formerly cultivated as a potherb. It has yellow flowers and fleshy, aromatic roots.  It is also known as Alexander or Alexanders.|The Romans cultivated felwort as a vegetable and introduced it to Britain. It can still be found growing wild in some areas. Felwort is considered poisonous now, and its consumption is discouraged. |近义词：Alexanders, smyrnium, black lovage|反义词: (No direct antonym for a specific plant.  Could use "safe herb" in a culinary context)|词性:noun
