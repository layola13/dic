palpitate: /ˈpæl.pɪ.teɪt/ (US) /ˈpæl.pɪ.teɪt/ (UK)| v. |  颤动，跳动；（心）怦怦跳| To beat rapidly, strongly, or irregularly; flutter.  | My heart palpitated with fear. 我的心因恐惧而怦怦直跳。 | synonyms: throb, pulsate, flutter, pound | antonyms: calm, steady | verb

palpitation: /ˌpæl.pɪˈteɪ.ʃən/ (US) /ˌpæl.pɪˈteɪ.ʃən/ (UK) | n. | 颤动，跳动；（心）怦怦跳  | A rapid, strong, or irregular heartbeat. |  He suffered from heart palpitations. 他患有心悸。 | synonyms: throbbing, pulsation, flutter, pounding | antonyms: calmness, steadiness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin palpitatus, past participle of palpitare (“to throb, tremble, flutter”), frequentative of palpare (“to stroke, touch gently”).
