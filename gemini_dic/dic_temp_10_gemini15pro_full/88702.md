hobbler:/ˈhɒblə(r)/ (US) /ˈhɑːblər/| n. |业余爱好者；修补匠；<古>笨拙的马；<古>蹩脚的诗人|A person who has a hobby, especially one that involves making or repairing things.  Someone who engages in an activity for pleasure or amusement, rather than professionally. Also, archaically, a clumsy horse or a poor poet.|例句：He's a keen hobbler, spending hours in his workshop. 他是一个热衷的业余爱好者，在他的工作室里一待就是几个小时。

例句：My grandfather was a hobbler, always tinkering with clocks and radios.  我祖父是个业余修补匠，总是摆弄钟表和收音机。|近义词：amateur, hobbyist, dabbler, tinkerer|反义词：professional, expert|词性: noun
