deploration:/ˌdɛpləˈreɪʃən/ | n. | 悲叹，哀悼，痛惜 |  The act of deploring; the expression of grief or sorrow; lamentation. | The president expressed his deploration of the violence. 总统对暴力事件表示痛惜。| 近义词: lamentation, mourning, grieving, regret, sorrow | 反义词: celebration, rejoicing, jubilation | 词性: 名词

deplorable:/dɪˈplɔrəbl/ | adj. |  deplorable |  Deserving strong condemnation; wretched. |  The living conditions in the refugee camp were deplorable. 难民营的生活条件极其恶劣。| 近义词: lamentable, regrettable, disgraceful, shameful, appalling | 反义词: commendable, admirable, excellent | 词性: 形容词

deplore:/dɪˈplɔr/ | v. | 悲叹，哀悼，强烈反对 | Feel or express strong disapproval of (something). |  We deplore the use of violence. 我们强烈反对使用暴力。 | 近义词: lament, bewail, condemn, criticize, denounce | 反义词: approve, commend, praise, support | 词性: 动词


词源:
From Late Middle English: from Old French deplorer, from Latin deplorare ‘lament, bewail’, from de- ‘down, fully’ + plorare ‘weep’.
