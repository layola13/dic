pantograph:/ˈpæntəɡræf/ |n.|缩放仪，比例放大器|An instrument for copying a drawing or plan on a different scale, consisting of four rigid bars jointed in a parallelogram, with a tracing point at one corner and a pencil at another.|The architect used a pantograph to enlarge the blueprint.|近义词:  proportional dividers, eidograph|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly "reducer" in a loose sense)|名词

pantographer:/pænˈtɒɡrɑːfər/ |n.|缩放仪操作员|A person who operates a pantograph.|The pantographer skillfully copied the intricate design.|近义词: draftsperson, copyist, tracer|反义词: (No direct antonym)|名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French pantographe, from Greek παντ- (pant-, “all”) + -γράφος (-graphos, “writing”).
