compartment:/kəmˈpɑːrtmənt/ | n. | 隔间；车厢；（容器或空间的）分隔间 | A separate section or part of a larger area or container. | The train was crowded, and we had to stand in the compartment. 火车很拥挤，我们不得不站在隔间里。| The first-class compartments are more spacious.  头等舱隔间更宽敞。| synonym: section, chamber, bay, cubicle | antonym: whole, entirety | noun


compartmentalize: /ˌkəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəˌlaɪz/ | vt. | 分隔；划分；（尤指在心理上）把……分成各自独立的部分 | To divide something into separate sections or categories, often to isolate or deal with them individually, especially in one's mind.  | He compartmentalizes his life by keeping work and personal issues separate. 他将工作和个人问题分开，从而把自己的生活划分开来。| She tends to compartmentalize her emotions, never letting them fully surface. 她倾向于把自己的情绪划分开来，从不让它们完全表露出来。| synonym: categorize, divide, separate, isolate | antonym: integrate, combine, unify | verb


compartmentalization: /kəmˌpɑːrtˌmentələˈzeɪʃn/ | n. | 分隔；划分；区分 | The act of dividing something into separate sections or categories. | The compartmentalization of the company allows for greater efficiency.  公司的划分提高了效率。|  The compartmentalization of her grief prevented her from truly healing.  她悲伤的划分阻碍了她真正的痊愈。| synonym: division, separation, categorization | antonym: integration, unification | noun
