kowtow:/ˌkaʊˈtaʊ/| vi. |叩头，磕头；卑躬屈膝，唯命是从 |To kneel and touch the ground with the forehead in worship or submission, especially in former China; to act in an excessively subservient manner. | The emperor’s subjects kowtowed before him. 皇帝的臣民在他面前叩头。|  He kowtowed to the boss in hopes of a promotion. 他为了升职巴结老板。| synonyms: grovel, fawn, prostrate, genuflect | antonyms: defy, resist, stand up to | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Chinese 磕頭 (kētóu, literally “knock head”).
