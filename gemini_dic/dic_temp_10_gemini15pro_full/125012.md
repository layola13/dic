mugwumpish:/ˌmʌɡˈwʌmpɪʃ/ | adj. | 自以为清高冷漠的；不偏不倚的；优柔寡断的 |  Showing a sanctimonious air of superiority and aloofness; neutral; undecided.  Derived from "mugwump," a term originally used in the late 19th century to describe Republicans who refused to support their party's nominee, James G. Blaine, in the 1884 presidential election.  |  He gave me a mugwumpish look, as if my question was beneath him. 他给了我一个自以为是的眼神，好像我的问题在他之下。 | Synonyms: supercilious, aloof, holier-than-thou, neutral, undecided | Antonyms: humble, approachable, partisan, decisive, committed | 词性: 形容词
