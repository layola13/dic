disrespective:/ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛktɪv/ | adj. | 不敬的，失礼的 | Showing a lack of respect; disrespectful.  This word is considered archaic or nonstandard and is rarely used in modern English. It is generally better to use "disrespectful." |  He made disrespective comments about his elders. (Archaic) 他对长辈发表了不敬的言论。| 近义词: disrespectful, irreverent, rude, impolite | 反义词: respectful, polite, courteous | 词性: adjective

词源: From dis- + respective.  The prefix "dis-" adds a negative meaning to "respective," which originally meant "regarding each individually."  Therefore, "disrespective" literally means "not regarding each individually with respect."
