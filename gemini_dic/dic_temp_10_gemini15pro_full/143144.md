outcavilling: /ˌaʊtˈkævəlɪŋ/
|v.|胜过诡辩; 以诡辩胜过|Outcavilling someone means surpassing them in cavilling, or raising petty objections.|He outcavilled his opponent in the debate, focusing on minor details and ignoring the main points.|近义词: outarguing, outwitting, confuting|反义词: conceding, yielding|词性: 动词

outcavilling: /ˌaʊtˈkævəlɪŋ/
|n.|诡辩的胜过|The act of surpassing someone in cavilling, or raising petty objections.|His outcavilling of his opponent was evident in his focus on trivial matters.|近义词: sophistry, quibbling, nitpicking|反义词: concession, agreement|词性: 名词
