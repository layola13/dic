defrocked:/diːˈfrɒkt/| adj.|被剥夺圣职的，被革除教籍的|Removed from the priesthood as a punishment.  Deprived of priestly authority, privileges, and functions.  Sometimes also used figuratively to describe someone who has lost a position of power or respect.|The defrocked priest now worked as a librarian.  这位被革除教籍的神父现在在图书馆工作。|The disgraced executive was defrocked of his title and responsibilities.  这位名誉扫地的执行官被剥夺了头衔和职责。|近义词：unfrocked, dismissed, expelled|反义词：ordained, consecrated|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From de- (prefix indicating removal or reversal) + frock (a monk's or priest's habit).  Originally meant to remove the frock or habit from a priest, thus symbolizing his removal from the priesthood.
