emboldening:/ɪmˈboʊldənɪŋ/
verb
使大胆；使有胆量；鼓励
making someone feel braver and more confident
Her success emboldened her to try even greater things. 她的成功鼓舞了她去尝试更伟大的事情。
synonyms: encouraging, inspiring, heartening
antonyms: discouraging, disheartening, intimidating
词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From embolden + -ing.  Embolden comes from the late Middle English word *embolden*, from en- + bolden, meaning "to make bold." Bolden itself derives from the adjective "bold."  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *balþaz* (bold, brave, strong), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰel- (to swell, blow).
