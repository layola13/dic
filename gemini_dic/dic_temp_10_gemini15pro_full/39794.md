consort:/ˈkɒnsɔːt (UK), kənˈsɔːrt (US)/
n.|配偶；伙伴；同事|A husband or wife, especially of a ruler.  A companion or partner. A ship sailing in company with another. |例句：The queen consort will attend the ceremony. 王后将出席仪式。|近义词：spouse, partner, companion, colleague|反义词：enemy, opponent|词性:名词

consort:/kənˈsɔːrt (US), ˈkɒnsɔːt (UK)/
v.|交往；结交；陪伴|To habitually associate with someone, typically with the disapproval of others. To be in accord or harmony.|例句：She consorts with known criminals. 她与已知的罪犯来往。|近义词：associate, fraternize, mingle|反义词：avoid, shun|词性:动词

词源：From Old French consorter "to share, associate, agree," from Latin consors "sharing, partner," from com- "together" + sors "lot, fate, share."

注意事项：consort作为名词时通常指统治者的配偶，而作为动词时则指与他人交往，通常带有负面含义，暗示与不好的人交往。
