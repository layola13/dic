outsiderishness:/ˌaʊtˈsaɪdəˌrɪʃnəs/
|n.|
|局外人的状态；格格不入；外来感|
|The state or quality of being an outsider; a feeling of not belonging or being different from others.|
|Her outsiderishness made her feel uncomfortable at the exclusive club.| 她在专属俱乐部里感觉格格不入，让她感到很不舒服。

|Her outsiderishness was palpable as she entered the room full of established artists.| 当她走进挤满知名艺术家的房间时，她外来者的感觉非常明显。

|alienation, estrangement, detachment, otherness, foreignness|
|belonging, inclusion, acceptance, integration, insider status|
|noun|


outsider:/ˌaʊtˈsaɪdər/
|n.|
|局外人；外来者；门外汉|
|A person who is not a member of a particular group or organization; someone who does not belong.|
|He felt like an outsider looking in on their close-knit community.| 他觉得自己像个局外人，看着他们紧密联系的社区。

|Being an outsider can be both isolating and liberating.| 成为一个局外人既可能让人感到孤立，也可能让人感到自由。

|stranger, foreigner, alien, non-member, newcomer|
|insider, native, member, local, belonging|
|noun|
