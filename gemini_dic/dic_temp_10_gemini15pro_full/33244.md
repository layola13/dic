chronometer:/krəˈnɒmɪtər/ (UK) /krəˈnɑːmɪtər/ (US)| n.|精密计时器，航海天文钟|An instrument for measuring time very accurately, especially one used for scientific purposes or by navigators at sea.|例句：The captain checked the chronometer to determine their longitude. 船长查看了精密计时器以确定他们的经度。

例句：The scientist used a chronometer to time the experiment precisely.  科学家使用精密计时器精确地计时实验。|近义词：clock, timer, stopwatch|反义词: None|词性:noun

chronometric:/ˌkrɒnəˈmetrɪk/ (UK) /ˌkrɑːnəˈmetrɪk/ (US)| adj.|精密计时的，计时器的|Relating to or using a chronometer; of or relating to chronometry.|例句：Chronometric testing is essential for accurate results. 精确计时测试对于获得准确结果至关重要。

例句：The chronometric data revealed the subtle variations in the athlete's performance. 计时数据揭示了运动员表现的细微变化。|近义词：timing, measured, precise|反义词:imprecise|词性:adjective

chronometry:/krəˈnɒmɪtri/ (UK) /krəˈnɑːmɪtri/ (US)| n.|精密计时法，计时学|The science of accurate time measurement.|例句：Chronometry plays a vital role in navigation and astronomy. 计时学在航海和天文学中起着至关重要的作用。

例句：He specialized in the study of chronometry and its applications. 他专门研究计时学及其应用。|近义词：timekeeping, horology|反义词: None|词性:noun
