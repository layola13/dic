divan:/daɪˈvæn/| n.|长沙发，卧榻；议事厅|A low couch, usually without arms or back, often found in Middle Eastern countries. Also, a council chamber or assembly hall.|The sultan received visitors in his opulent divan.|Synonyms: couch, sofa, settee|Antonyms: chair, stool|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *divan*, from Turkish *divan*, from Persian *dīwān*, originally meaning a list or register, then an office, then a council chamber, and finally a couch used in such a chamber.
