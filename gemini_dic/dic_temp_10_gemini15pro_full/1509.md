acetometer: /ˌæsɪˈtɒmɪtər/ (UK) /ˌæsɪˈtɑːmɪtər/ (US)| n.|加速度计|An instrument for measuring acceleration or vibrations, especially one used to measure the changes in speed of a vehicle.|The acetometer recorded the rapid deceleration of the car during the crash. 加速度计记录了汽车在碰撞过程中的快速减速。

The pilot relied on the acetometer to measure G-forces during acrobatic maneuvers. 飞行员依靠加速度计来测量特技飞行中的G力。|近义词: accelerometer|反义词: None|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *acétomètre*, from *acéto-* (from Latin *acētum* "vinegar") + *-mètre* (from Greek *métron* "measure").  Originally used to measure the strength of vinegar, later applied to measuring acceleration.
