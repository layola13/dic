isotope:/ˈaɪsəˌtoʊp/ (US), /ˈaɪsətəʊp/ (UK)| n. |同位素|One of two or more forms of the same element that contain equal numbers of protons but different numbers of neutrons in their nuclei, and hence differ in relative atomic mass but not in chemical properties; in particular, a radioactive form of an element.|

例句：
Carbon-14 is a radioactive isotope of carbon.
碳-14是碳的放射性同位素。

Uranium has several isotopes, some of which are fissile.
铀有几种同位素，其中一些是可裂变的。

近义词：nuclide (all isotopes are nuclides, but not all nuclides are isotopes)|
反义词: N/A (Isotopes don't have direct opposites.)|
词性: noun|


词源: From Greek *isos* "equal" + *topos* "place," because isotopes occupy the same place in the periodic table.
