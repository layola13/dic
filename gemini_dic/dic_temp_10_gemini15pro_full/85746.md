hawkishnesses:/ˈhɔːkɪʃnɪsɪz/
noun
鹰派作风；强硬态度
The quality of being hawkish, i.e., advocating aggressive or warlike policies, especially in foreign affairs.
His hawkishnesses on defense spending worried some voters. 他在国防开支上的强硬态度令一些选民担忧。
近义词: aggressiveness, belligerence, militancy
反义词: dovishness, pacifism
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From hawkish + -ness.  Hawkish itself comes from hawk (n.), referring to the bird of prey, known for its aggressive hunting behavior. The metaphorical use of "hawk" to describe someone who favors war or aggressive policies dates back to the mid-20th century.
