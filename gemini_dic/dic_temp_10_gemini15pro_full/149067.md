oxacid:/ˈɒksæsɪd/ | n. | 含氧酸 | An acid containing oxygen. An oxacid is an acid that contains oxygen. Specifically, it is a compound that contains hydrogen, oxygen, and at least one other element, with at least one hydrogen atom bound to oxygen that can dissociate to produce the H+ cation and an anion. |  Nitric acid (HNO3) is a common example of an oxacid. 硝酸 (HNO3) 是含氧酸的一个常见例子。

Sulfuric acid (H2SO4) is another important oxacid used in various industrial processes. 硫酸 (H2SO4) 是另一种重要的含氧酸，用于各种工业过程。 | 近义词: oxyacid | 反义词: hydracids (not always a direct opposite, but represents a different class of acids) | 词性: noun
