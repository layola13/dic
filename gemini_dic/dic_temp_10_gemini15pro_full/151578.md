Partenope: /pɑːrˈtɛnəpi/ /pɑːrˈtiːnəpi/ | n. |帕特诺佩, 那不勒斯的古名|  Partenope is the ancient Greek name for Naples, Italy. It refers to one of the Sirens in Greek mythology who was said to have thrown herself into the sea and washed ashore at the site of the city after failing to lure Odysseus with her song. | Naples was originally called Partenope. 那不勒斯最初被称为帕特诺佩。| The mythical figure of Partenope is still a symbol of Naples. 神话人物帕特诺佩仍然是那不勒斯的象征。| Synonyms: Naples, Neapolis | Antonyms: N/A | Noun
