Chikasaw:/ˈtʃɪkəˌsɔː/| n. adj.|奇卡索人；奇卡索语|A member of a Native American people originally inhabiting Mississippi and Alabama, now living chiefly in Oklahoma. Also, the Muskogean language of this people.|例句：The Chickasaw Nation has a rich cultural heritage. 奇卡索民族有着丰富的文化遗产。

例句：He speaks fluent Chickasaw. 他说一口流利的奇卡索语。|近义词：Choctaw (related language and people)|反义词: N/A|词性:名词，形容词

Etymology: From the Choctaw word *Chikashsha*, possibly meaning "rebel" or "those who separated."
