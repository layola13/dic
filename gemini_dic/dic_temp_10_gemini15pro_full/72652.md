flywheels:/ˈflaɪˌwiːlz/| n. |飞轮|A heavy revolving wheel in a machine that helps to increase the machine's momentum and thereby provide greater stability or a reserve of power.|The flywheel stores rotational energy and smooths out the power output of the engine.|近义词：rotor, impeller|反义词: brake|noun


flywheel:/ˈflaɪˌwiːl/| n. |飞轮|Something that adds momentum or impetus to an ongoing activity, process, or project.|Government spending is sometimes seen as a flywheel for the economy.|近义词：impetus, momentum, catalyst|反义词: obstacle, impediment|noun
