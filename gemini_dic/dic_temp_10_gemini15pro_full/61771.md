empathizing:/ˈɛmpəθaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 共情，感同身受 | The act of understanding and sharing the feelings of another. |  I've been empathizing with her situation ever since she lost her job.  自从她失业后，我一直对她感同身受。 | synonyms:  understanding, sharing, relating, commiserating, sympathizing | antonyms:  indifferent, apathetic, uncaring | verb (present participle)

empathize: /ˈɛmpəθaɪz/ | v. | 共情，感同身受 | To understand and share the feelings of another. | I can empathize with your frustration. 我能理解你的沮丧。 | synonyms:  understand, share, relate, commiserate, sympathize | antonyms:  ignore, disregard, neglect | verb

empathy: /ˈɛmpəθi/ | n. | 同理心 | The ability to understand and share the feelings of another. | She showed great empathy for the victims of the disaster. 她对灾难的受害者表现出了极大的同情心。| synonyms:  understanding, compassion, sympathy, rapport | antonyms:  indifference, apathy, coldness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the German *Einfühlung*, coined by Theodor Lipps (1851–1914) from *ein* ("in") and *Fühlung* ("feeling").  Borrowed into English in the early 20th century.
