deyntee:/ˈdeɪnti/| n. adj.|美味佳肴，精致的; 挑剔的|Something delicious, choice, or pleasing to the taste; also, delicately beautiful or charming.  Something considered choice or pleasing. Can also describe someone with refined or fastidious tastes.|The banquet offered a variety of deyntees. 宴会提供了各种各样的美味佳肴。|近义词：delicacy, treat, exquisite, dainty|反义词：coarse, crude, rough|词性:名词, 形容词
Etymology: From Old French *daintie*, from *dainté*, past participle of *daintier* "to treat with respect, esteem," from Latin *dignitare* "to treat as worthy," from *dignus* "worthy."
