intercepting:/ˌɪntərˈseptɪŋ/
adjective |拦截的，截取的| stopping or catching something or someone before that thing or person is able to reach a particular place | The police were intercepting phone calls. 警方正在窃听电话。|  Synonyms: catching, seizing, stopping | Antonyms: releasing, freeing, missing |adjective


intercepting:/ˌɪntərˈseptɪŋ/
verb (present participle) |拦截，截取| to stop or catch something or someone before that thing or person is able to reach a particular place | The missile was intercepted by our defense system. 我们的防御系统拦截了导弹。| Synonyms: catching, seizing, stopping | Antonyms: releasing, freeing, missing |verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin intercipere ("to intercept, seize, catch"), from inter- ("between") + capere ("to take").
