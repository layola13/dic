hypersuggestibleness:/ˌhaɪpər səˈdʒɛstəbəlnəs/ | n. |过度暗示性，高度受暗示性|The state of being excessively receptive to suggestion, often to the point of uncritically accepting and acting upon ideas or instructions.|The hypnotist relied on the subject's hypersuggestibleness to implant false memories.|近义词：suggestibility, persuadability|反义词：resistance, skepticism|词性:noun

Etymology:
hyper- (Greek: over, above, excessive) + suggest (Latin: suggerere, to carry or bring under) + -ible (suffix forming adjectives meaning "capable of being") + -ness (suffix forming nouns from adjectives)
