hocus-pocus:/ˌhoʊkəs ˈpoʊkəs/| n.  |戏法；花招；胡言乱语|An expression used when performing a trick or illusion, often implying deception or sleight of hand. It can also refer to meaningless or misleading talk.|The magician waved his wand and muttered "hocus-pocus" before pulling a rabbit out of the hat. 魔术师挥舞着魔杖，嘴里念叨着“胡言乱语”，然后从帽子里掏出一只兔子。|近义词：trickery, deception, sleight of hand, mumbo jumbo|反义词:honesty, truth, clarity|词性:noun


hocus-pocus:/ˌhoʊkəs ˈpoʊkəs/| v.  |变戏法；施魔法；欺骗|To deceive or manipulate someone using trickery or misleading words.|He hocus-pocused the investors into believing his scheme was legitimate. 他用花言巧语骗投资者相信他的计划是合法的。|近义词：trick, deceive, manipulate, hoodwink|反义词:be honest, be truthful|词性:verb
