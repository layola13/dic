acropolis: /əˈkrɒpəlɪs/ /əˈkrɑːpəlɪs/
noun
雅典卫城；城堡；（古希腊城市）卫城
A citadel or fortified part of an ancient Greek city, typically built on a hill.
The Acropolis of Athens is a UNESCO World Heritage site. 雅典卫城是联合国教科文组织世界遗产。

The Parthenon is the most famous building on the Acropolis.  帕特农神庙是雅典卫城最著名的建筑。

synonym: citadel, fortress, stronghold
antonym:  lowland


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἀκρόπολις (akrópolis, “upper city, citadel”), from ἄκρος (ákros, “highest, topmost”) + πόλις (pólis, “city”).


USAGE->
The Acropolis is a popular tourist destination.  雅典卫城是一个热门的旅游目的地。

Many ancient cities had an acropolis for defense. 许多古代城市都有卫城用于防御。
