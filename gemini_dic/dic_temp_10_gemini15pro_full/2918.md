advisers:/ədˈvaɪzərz/| n.|顾问，指导者|A person who gives advice, especially in a professional capacity.|The president sought the advice of his advisers before making a decision.  总统在做决定之前征求了顾问们的意见。

The company's financial advisers helped them navigate the complex investment landscape. 这家公司的财务顾问帮助他们在复杂的投资环境中导航。|近义词：consultants, mentors, counselors, guides|反义词: advisee|词性:名词(复数)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *avisour*, from Old French *aviseur*, from *aviser* (“to inform, advise”), from Vulgar Latin *advisāre*, present active infinitive of *advisō*, from Latin *ad* (“to”) + *vīsō*, frequentative of *videō* (“to see”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*weyd-*.
