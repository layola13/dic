haemosiderosis:/ˌhiːməˌsɪdəˈroʊsɪs/| n. |血铁质沉着症，血铁沉积症|A condition characterized by the excessive deposition of hemosiderin in tissues, often due to repeated blood transfusions or a disorder of iron metabolism.|Example 1: The patient's repeated blood transfusions led to the development of haemosiderosis. 病人的反复输血导致了血铁质沉着症的发生。

Example 2:  Haemosiderosis can cause damage to organs such as the liver and pancreas. 血铁质沉着症会损害肝脏和胰腺等器官。|Synonym: iron overload|Antonym: iron deficiency|noun
