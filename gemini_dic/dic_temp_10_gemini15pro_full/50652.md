derivably:/dɪˈraɪvəbli/| adv.|可推导地，可导出地|able to be derived; deducible.  Something is derivably true if it can be logically deduced or inferred from other established facts or principles.|The theorem is derivably true from the axioms. 这个定理可以从公理中推导出来.|近义词：deducibly, inferably|反义词:  non-deducible, unprovable|词性:副词
词源：From derive + -ably.  Derive ultimately comes from Latin derivare "to draw off (water), divert, derive," from de- "off" + rivus "stream."
