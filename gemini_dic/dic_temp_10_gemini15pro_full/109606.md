letout:/ˈlɛtaʊt/| n.| 放松，解脱；出租，租出；（衣服的）放宽,修改; (秘密的)泄露|A means of escape or release, especially from confinement or an unpleasant situation; A release of confined air or energy; The act of renting out property or accommodations; An alteration to a garment to make it larger. |After the stressful week, a weekend at the spa was a welcome letout.  压力大的一周后，周末去水疗中心是一种受欢迎的放松。|Synonyms: release, escape, vent, outlet, relief, rental, lease |Antonyms: confinement, restraint, imprisonment, retention, withholding|noun
let out:/lɛt aʊt/| v.| 放出，释放；出租，租出；发出（声音）；泄露（秘密）; 放大(衣服)|To allow someone or something to leave a place; To rent out property or accommodations; To make a sound; To reveal a secret; To make a garment larger. |She let out a scream when she saw the spider. 当她看到蜘蛛时，她发出一声尖叫。|Synonyms: release, free, discharge, liberate, vent, utter, divulge, expand, enlarge |Antonyms: confine, restrain, imprison, retain, withhold, conceal, shrink, reduce|verb
