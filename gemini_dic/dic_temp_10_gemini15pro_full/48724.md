defilading:/ˌdiːfɪˈleɪdɪŋ/ | n. | 防禦工事;散兵坑;掩體; 掩护;遮蔽| The act of protecting troops or equipment from enemy fire by using natural or artificial obstacles. |  The engineers were busy defilading the artillery positions. 工程师们正忙着构筑炮兵阵地的防御工事。|  The troops dug trenches for defilading against enemy fire. 部队挖掘散兵坑以掩护自己免受敌方炮火。 | synonyms: shielding, protecting, covering, screening | antonyms: exposing | noun


defilade:/ˌdiːfɪˈleɪd/ | v. |  构筑防御工事;掩护;遮蔽 | To protect (troops, positions, etc.) from enemy fire by screening with a natural or artificial parapet. |  The hill defiladed the troops from enemy observation. 这座小山掩护了部队，使他们不被敌人发现。 |  They defiladed the road from view. 他们把道路遮蔽起来，不让看到. | synonyms: shield, protect, cover, screen | antonyms: expose | verb
