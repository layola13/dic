abomination:/əˌbɒmɪˈneɪʃn/ | n. | 憎恶, 令人厌恶的事物, 讨厌的东西 | something that causes disgust or hatred | The slaughter of innocent civilians is an abomination.  屠杀无辜平民是令人发指的行为。

Animal cruelty is an abomination. 虐待动物是令人憎恶的行为。| 近义词: atrocity, disgrace, horror, outrage, evil, sin | 反义词: delight, pleasure, love, blessing | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *abominacioun*, from Old French abominacion, from Latin abominātiō (“abomination, omen of evil”), from abominārī (“to deprecate as ominous, abominate, abhor”), from ab- (“away from, from”) + omen (“omen”).


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His treatment of animals is an abomination. = 他对待动物的方式令人憎恶。
This policy is an abomination and must be repealed. = 这项政策令人深恶痛绝，必须废除。

一般过去时:
The judge described the crime as an abomination. = 法官称这起犯罪是令人发指的行为。
Slavery was an abomination that scarred the nation. = 奴隶制是给国家留下伤疤的丑恶行径。

现在完成时:
The pollution of our rivers has become an abomination. = 我们河流的污染已经到了令人发指的地步。
The war has created an abomination of destruction. = 这场战争造成了可怕的破坏。

过去完成时:
He had always considered such behavior an abomination. = 他一直认为这种行为是令人憎恶的。
They had witnessed abominations that they could never forget. = 他们目睹了永远无法忘记的可怕景象。

将来时:
Such an act will be considered an abomination by future generations. = 这样的行为将被后代视为令人发指的行为。
If we continue down this path, our society will become an abomination. = 如果我们继续沿着这条路走下去，我们的社会将会变得面目全非。

虚拟语气:
If it were an abomination, I would condemn it. = 如果这是令人憎恶的行为，我会谴责它。
I wish it weren't such an abomination. = 我希望它不是如此令人憎恶。

被动语态:
Child labor is considered an abomination by most people. = 童工被大多数人认为是令人憎恶的。
The act was condemned as an abomination by the international community. = 该行为被国际社会谴责为令人发指的行为。


疑问句:
Isn't that an abomination? = 那难道不是令人憎恶的吗？
Why do you consider this an abomination? = 你为什么认为这是令人憎恶的？


否定句:
It's not an abomination in their culture. = 在他们的文化中，这并不是令人憎恶的。
His actions were not considered an abomination by everyone. = 并非所有人都认为他的行为令人憎恶。
