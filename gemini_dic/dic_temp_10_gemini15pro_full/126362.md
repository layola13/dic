myriad:/ˈmɪriəd/ | adj. n. |无数的，大量的；无数，大量|A countless or extremely great number.  Derived from the Greek "myrioi" meaning "ten thousand". Originally referred to the number 10,000, but came to mean a vast, indefinite number.|例句：They offered a myriad of excuses for their lateness.  他们为迟到提供了各种各样的借口。|近义词：innumerable, countless, numerous, multitude|反义词：few, scarce|词性:形容词, 名词
