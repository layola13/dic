one-upmanship: /wʌnˈʌpmənʃɪp/ | n. | 胜人一筹；好胜心；争强好胜 | The act or practice of successively outdoing a competitor. | He's always trying to one-up me, it's so annoying. 他总是想胜过我，真烦人。|  She posted pictures of her exotic vacation, a clear act of one-upmanship. 她发布了异国度假的照片，这显然是在炫耀。 | 近义词: rivalry, competition, one-upsmanship | 反义词: cooperation, collaboration | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  A blend of "one-up" (verb meaning to surpass or outdo) and "-manship" (a suffix denoting skill or art), popularized by Stephen Potter's humorous books in the 1950s.
