hados: /ˈheɪdɒs/ | n. | 行为，举止，习惯；（尤指）礼仪，礼节 |  Manners, bearing, or demeanor, especially with respect to what is proper; etiquette. |  He displayed impeccable hados at the formal dinner. 他在正式晚宴上展现了无可挑剔的礼仪。 | synonyms: etiquette, decorum, manners, propriety, protocol | antonyms: rudeness, impropriety, disrespect | noun

Etymology: From Spanish "hados," meaning "fates" or "destinies."  Ultimately from Latin "fatum," meaning "fate." While the original Spanish meaning related to fate, in English, it evolved to refer to the behavior and conduct considered appropriate for a given situation.
