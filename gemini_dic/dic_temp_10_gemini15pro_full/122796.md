moldering:/ˈmoʊldərɪŋ/| adj.|腐朽的，朽烂的；衰败的|slowly decaying or disintegrating, especially because of dampness; declining or decaying from neglect or inactivity.|The moldering books in the attic were covered in dust. 阁楼里腐朽的书籍上满是灰尘。|Synonyms: decaying, rotting, decomposing, crumbling, disintegrating, deteriorating, declining, fading, withering|Antonyms: growing, flourishing, thriving, developing, improving|adjective

mouldering:/ˈmoʊldərɪŋ/| adj.|腐朽的，朽烂的；衰败的|slowly decaying or disintegrating, especially because of dampness; declining or decaying from neglect or inactivity.|The mouldering papers in the basement told a story of forgotten memories. 地下室里腐烂的纸张讲述了一个被遗忘的记忆故事。|Synonyms: decaying, rotting, decomposing, crumbling, disintegrating, deteriorating, declining, fading, withering|Antonyms: growing, flourishing, thriving, developing, improving|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *moldren, from Old English *molde (“dust, earth”), from Proto-Germanic *muldą (“dust, soft earth”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *melh₂- (“to grind, crush”). Related to mold (n.). The -er suffix denotes an agent or process.  The -ing suffix forms the present participle.
