civilizes: /ˈsɪvɪlaɪzɪz/
verb
使文明；教化；开化
To bring out of a savage, uneducated, or unrefined state, or to a less primitive condition.
European explorers often attempted to "civilize" the indigenous populations they encountered. 欧洲探险家经常试图“教化”他们遇到的土著居民。

The missionaries worked to civilize the tribes of the Amazon. 传教士努力使亚马逊部落开化。

近义词: educate, refine, cultivate, enlighten
反义词: barbarize, brutalize
词性: verb


civilize: /ˈsɪvɪlaɪz/
verb
使文明；教化；开化
To bring out of a savage, uneducated, or unrefined state, or to a less primitive condition.
Education can civilize people and promote understanding. 教育可以使人文明，促进理解。

Reading good literature can civilize the mind. 阅读优秀的文学作品可以陶冶心灵。


近义词: educate, refine, cultivate, enlighten
反义词: barbarize, brutalize
词性: verb


词源: From Old French *civiliser*, from Latin civilis (“civil”).
