assuetude:/əˈswiːtjuːd/ /əˈsuːɪtuːd/
n.
习惯；熟练；习以为常
Customary practice or habit; established practice.
The assuetude of power can corrupt even the most virtuous.  权力带来的习以为常会腐蚀即使是最有道德的人。
She performs her duties with the assuetude of long experience.  她以长期经验养成的习惯履行职责。
近义词: habit, custom, practice, routine, wont
反义词: unfamiliarity, inexperience, disuse
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *assuetudo (“habit”), equivalent to Latin assuetus (“accustomed”) + -tūdō. From Latin assuescere, frequentative of assuēscō, from ad + suēscō (“become accustomed”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *swékʷeti (“becomes accustomed”).
