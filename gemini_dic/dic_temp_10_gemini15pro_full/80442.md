gone-on:/ɡɒnˈɒn (UK) / ɡɔːnˈɑːn (US)| adj.|过时的，老式的；持续时间过长的；过分热情的，过分投入的|Something that is gone-on is old-fashioned or has lasted too long. It can also describe someone who is excessively enthusiastic or involved in something.|例句：That dress is a bit gone-on, isn't it? 那条裙子有点过时了，不是吗？|近义词：outdated, old-fashioned, over-the-top, excessive |反义词: modern, contemporary, current, restrained|词性:形容词

gone-on:/ɡɒnˈɒn (UK) / ɡɔːnˈɑːn (US)| n.|迷恋，痴迷；持续时间过长的事情|A strong infatuation or a situation/activity that has continued for too long.|例句：She has a real gone-on for him. 她对他很着迷。|近义词：infatuation, crush, obsession, fixation |反义词: indifference, apathy|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
"gone-on" originates from the phrasal verb "go on," meaning to continue or proceed.  Over time, it evolved into an adjective and noun with the specific connotations of being outdated, excessive, or a prolonged infatuation.  The exact date of its emergence in these forms is difficult to pinpoint, but it likely developed gradually through colloquial usage.
