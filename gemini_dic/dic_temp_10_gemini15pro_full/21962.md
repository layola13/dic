bogtrotter:/ˈbɒɡˌtrɒtər/ | n. | 沼泽居民，乡巴佬 | A person who lives in a boggy or marshy area; often used derogatorily to mean a rustic, ignorant person. |  He called them ignorant bogtrotters, unaware of their rich cultural heritage. 他称他们为无知的沼泽居民，没有意识到他们丰富的文化遗产。

| Example:  The locals were often dismissed as bogtrotters by those from the city.  当地人经常被城里人轻蔑地称为乡巴佬。 | Synonym: rustic, yokel, peasant, country bumpkin | Antonym: sophisticate, cosmopolitan | noun
