epithelioid:/ˌɛpɪˈθiːliɔɪd/| adj.|类上皮的，上皮样的|Resembling or having the characteristics of epithelium; specifically : composed of cells that resemble epithelial cells especially in being closely apposed with little intercellular substance.|The epithelioid cells in granulomas are modified macrophages.|近义词：epithelial, epithelial-like|反义词: mesenchymal, non-epithelial|词性: adjective

Etymology: epi- + -thelio- + -oid

epi-: Greek, meaning "upon," "on," "over," "at," "near," "before," "after," "besides," "in addition to"

-thelio-:  from Greek thēlē "nipple," referring to the appearance of epithelial cells

-oid: a suffix meaning “resembling,” “like,” used in the formation of adjectives and nouns (humanoid; spheroid).
