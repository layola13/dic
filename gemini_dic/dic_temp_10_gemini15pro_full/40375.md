contradicting: /ˌkɒntrəˈdɪktɪŋ/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪktɪŋ/ (US) | v. | 反驳；与…矛盾；与…抵触 | Present participle of contradict.  Meaning "to assert the opposite of" or "to be inconsistent with". |  His actions are contradicting his words. 他的行为与他的言辞相矛盾。 | synonyms: opposing, denying, gainsaying, challenging | antonyms: confirming, supporting, corroborating | verb

contradicting: /ˌkɒntrəˈdɪktɪŋ/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪktɪŋ/ (US) | adj. | 矛盾的；反驳的 |  Used as an adjective, especially in a prenominal position. Meaning "inconsistent with" or "opposing". |  He gave a contradicting statement to the police. 他向警方作出了相互矛盾的陈述。 | synonyms: conflicting, opposing, inconsistent | antonyms: consistent, supporting, corroborating | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin contradicere, from contra- (“against”) + dicere (“to say”).
