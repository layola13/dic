come up to scratch:/kʌm ʌp tə skrætʃ/
|verb phrase|
达到标准，符合要求|
To meet the required standard; to be satisfactory.|
例句：His work didn't come up to scratch, so he had to do it again. 他的工作没有达到标准，所以他不得不重做。

例句：The hotel room didn't come up to scratch; it wasn't very clean. 酒店房间不符合标准；不太干净。

近义词：meet expectations, measure up, be satisfactory
反义词：fall short, be inadequate, be unsatisfactory
词性：verb phrase


come up roses:/kʌm ʌp ˈroʊzɪz/
|verb phrase|
结果圆满，一切顺利|To turn out well; to have a successful outcome, often unexpectedly.|
例句：Despite the initial setbacks, the project eventually came up roses. 尽管初期遇到了一些挫折，但该项目最终还是取得了圆满成功。

例句：Everything came up roses for her on her birthday -  she got a promotion and a surprise party. 她生日那天一切都很顺利——她升职了，还有一个惊喜派对。

近义词：turn out well, succeed, be successful, go smoothly
反义词：fail, go wrong, fall apart
词性：verb phrase

词源:  This idiom likely originates from the practice of using rose petals for celebrations and positive outcomes. Roses symbolize beauty and success, so "coming up roses" implies a flourishing and positive result.
