calade:/kəˈleɪd/ | n. | 行列; 队伍; 游行 | A procession of people or vehicles, especially one celebrating a special day or event. |  例句：A calade of decorated floats moved slowly through the streets. 一列装饰华丽的花车缓缓穿过街道。

例句：The festival culminated in a vibrant calade of musicians and dancers.  这个节日以一场充满活力的音乐家和舞蹈家游行达到高潮。

近义词：procession, parade, motorcade | 反义词:  N/A | 词性: 名词

词源:  From French *calade*, from Provençal *calado* "slope paved with pebbles," from *calar* "to wedge, to pave with pebbles," from Latin *chalare* "to pave."


注意事项:  "Calade" is not a commonly used word in modern English.  It is more frequently encountered in historical contexts or descriptions of traditional festivals.
