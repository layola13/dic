clownfish:/ˈklaʊnfɪʃ/| n.|小丑鱼，海葵鱼|A small, brightly colored fish that lives among the tentacles of sea anemones, protected from their stings by a special mucus coating.  These fish have a symbiotic relationship with the anemones, providing them with food scraps and cleaning, while the anemones offer them protection from predators.|例句：The clownfish darted among the anemone's tentacles. 小丑鱼在海葵的触须间穿梭。|近义词：anemonefish|反义词:predator (predator refers to an animal that hunts and eats other animals. It's not a direct opposite, but represents the danger clownfish avoid by living with anemones.)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From clown + fish, referring to their bright, often orange and white, coloration reminiscent of a clown's makeup.
