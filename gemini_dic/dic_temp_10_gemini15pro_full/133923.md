nonhallucinated:/ˌnɒnhəˈluːsɪneɪtɪd/| adj.|非幻觉的，未产生幻觉的|Not having experienced hallucinations; based on reality.|The witness gave a nonhallucinated account of the events. 目击者对事件进行了真实的描述。|近义词：real, factual, genuine, authentic, verifiable, objective|反义词：hallucinated, delusional, illusory, unreal|词性:形容词

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  Latin, meaning "not"
hallucinate (verb): From Late Latin hallucinatus, past participle of hallucinari "to wander in mind, talk idly," from Latin alucinari "to wander in mind, dream," perhaps from Greek alyein "to be distraught, wander in mind."
-ed (suffix): Forms the past participle of verbs.
