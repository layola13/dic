casuistries: /ˈkæʒuɪstriːz/
|n.|诡辩，决疑论，案例推理|The application of general ethical principles to particular cases (especially of conscience).  The use of clever but unsound reasoning, especially in relation to moral questions; sophistry.|The lawyer's arguments were pure casuistry.|近义词: sophistry, quibbling, equivocation|反义词:  directness, honesty, forthrightness|noun
