lifespans:/ˈlaɪfspænz/| n. |寿命；使用期限|The average lifespans of humans have increased significantly over the past century.  过去一个世纪人类的平均寿命显著增加。|Lifespans of different species vary greatly. 不同物种的寿命差异很大。|近义词：lifetimes, durations|反义词: N/A|词性:名词

Etymology:
From 'lifespan',  'life' + 'span'. 'Life' comes from Old English 'līf' (life, body, spirit), from Proto-Germanic '*lībam' (life, body), from Proto-Indo-European '*leip-' (to stick, adhere, remain). 'Span' comes from Old English 'spann' (a measure of length), from Proto-Germanic '*spannō', from Proto-Indo-European '*spen-' (to draw, stretch, spin).
