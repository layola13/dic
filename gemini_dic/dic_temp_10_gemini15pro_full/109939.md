liberalising: /ˈlɪbərəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使自由化，放宽限制 |  The government is liberalising the telecommunications market. 政府正在放宽电信市场的限制。 |  Example: The country is liberalising its trade policies to attract foreign investment.  这个国家正在放宽贸易政策以吸引外国投资。 | Synonyms: deregulating, freeing, loosening, relaxing | Antonyms: restricting, regulating, controlling | Verb (present participle)


liberalise: /ˈlɪbərəlaɪz/ | v. | 使自由化，放宽限制 | To make something less strict or controlled. | Example:  They hope to liberalise the laws on divorce. 他们希望放宽离婚法。 | Synonyms: deregulate, free, loosen, relax | Antonyms: restrict, regulate, control | Verb

Etymology: From "liberal" + "-ise" (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become"). "Liberal" comes from Latin "liberalis" meaning "of freedom," from "liber" meaning "free."
