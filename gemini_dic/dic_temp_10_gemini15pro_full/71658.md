flatters:/ˈflætərz/ (US), /ˈflætəz/ (UK)| v.|奉承；恭维；使显得更漂亮|To praise someone excessively and often insincerely, especially in order to gain an advantage.  Also, to make someone look more attractive than usual.|She flatters him with her attention. 她用她的关注来奉承他。|Synonyms: compliments, praises, cajoles, charms|Antonyms: criticizes, insults, disparages|verb (third-person singular simple present flatters, present participle flattering, simple past flattered, past participle flattered)

flattering remarks:  恭维话;  He made several flattering remarks about her appearance. 他说了几句恭维她外貌的话。
