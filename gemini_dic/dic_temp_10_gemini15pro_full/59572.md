ebonics: /ɪˈbɑːnɪks/, /iːˈbɒnɪks/ | n. | 黑人英语；非裔美国人白话英语 | A variety of American English, associated primarily with African Americans, often considered a distinct dialect or sociolect. |  Example: Some linguists argue that Ebonics is a legitimate language system, while others consider it a dialect of English. 一些语言学家认为黑人英语是一种合法的语言系统，而另一些人则认为它是英语的一种方言。 | Example: The debate over the use of Ebonics in schools continues to be controversial.  关于在学校使用黑人英语的辩论仍然存在争议。 | Synonym: African American Vernacular English (AAVE), Black English, Black Vernacular | Antonym: Standard American English | noun
