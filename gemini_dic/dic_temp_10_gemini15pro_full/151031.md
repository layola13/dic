parapatrically: /ˌpærəpəˈtrɪkəli/ | adv. | 异域性地，邻域性地 |  In biology, it describes the relationship between organisms whose ranges do not significantly overlap but are immediately adjacent to each other.  They do not occur together except in a narrow contact zone. | Two species of butterflies exist parapatrically, meeting only at the edge of their respective territories.  两种蝴蝶异域分布，只在各自领地的边缘相遇。| 近义词: adjacent, neighboring | 反义词: allopatrically, sympatrically | 词性: 副词

Etymology:  From Greek *para-* ("beside") + *patris* ("fatherland," hence "range, distribution").
