paromology:/ˌpærəˈmɒlədʒi/ | n. |类比推理，类比论证 | The study of using analogies or parables for reasoning or explaining something. It often involves drawing comparisons between seemingly dissimilar things to highlight underlying similarities or principles. |  The preacher used paromology to explain complex theological concepts to his congregation, comparing God's love to a shepherd's care for his flock.  He employed paromology in his legal arguments, drawing parallels between historical precedents and the current case. | 近义词: analogy, comparison, similitude | 反义词: contrast, difference | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Greek "para" (beside, alongside) + "homo" (same) + "-logy" (study of).
