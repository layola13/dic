dishing:/ˈdɪʃɪŋ/| adj.|精彩的，有趣的；刻薄的；爱说闲话的| Engagingly or revealingly gossipy.  Characterized by the telling of tales and gossip, often in a malicious or scandalous way. |例句：She has a dishing personality, always sharing the latest gossip. 她性格爱说闲话，总是分享最新的八卦。

例句：The magazine article was a dishing exposé on the celebrity's secret life. 这篇杂志文章是对这位名人秘密生活的精彩爆料。|近义词：gossipy, scandalous, revealing|反义词：discreet, respectful, kind|词性：形容词

dishing:/ˈdɪʃɪŋ/| n.|闲话，八卦|Gossip, often scandalous or revealing.|例句：They spent the afternoon exchanging dishing about their colleagues. 他们一下午都在交换关于同事的八卦。

例句：The latest dishing is that the CEO is about to resign. 最新的传闻是首席执行官即将辞职。|近义词：gossip, rumors, scandal|反义词：facts, truth|词性：名词


词源:  From the verb "to dish," meaning to serve or distribute, in this case, information or gossip.  Ultimately from Old English *disc "plate, dish," from Latin discus "disk."
