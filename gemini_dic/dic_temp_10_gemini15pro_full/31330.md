ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *character-, from Latin character "a distinctive mark, brand; a character, letter," from Greek kharaktēr "instrument for marking, engraved or stamped mark, distinctive quality, character, personal stamp," from kharassein "to scratch, engrave," from kharax "pointed stake."  The meaning "person in a play, story, etc." is from 1660s.
