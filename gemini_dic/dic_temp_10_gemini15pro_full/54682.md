disorderedness:/dɪsˈɔːrdərdnəs/| n. |混乱，无序|The state of being disordered; lack of order or arrangement.|The room was in a state of complete disorderedness after the party. 房间在派对后一片混乱。|近义词：disorder, chaos, mess, confusion, disarray|反义词：order, tidiness, organization, neatness|词性:名词

词源:  From "disordered" + "-ness". "Disordered" comes from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of") added to "ordered," which comes from the Latin "ordinare" (to arrange). The suffix "-ness" turns the adjective "disordered" into a noun meaning "the state of being disordered."
