get-up:/ˈɡɛtʌp/ (US), /ˈɡetʌp/ (UK)| n.|穿着；装束；奇装异服|A particular outfit or costume, especially one that is unusual or striking. |例句：He wore a strange get-up to the party. 他穿着一身奇怪的装束去参加了派对。

例句：Her stage get-up included a feathered boa and sparkly shoes. 她的舞台装扮包括一条羽毛围巾和闪亮的鞋子。


近义词：outfit, costume, attire|反义词:|词性:noun


get-up:/ɡɛt ˈʌp/ (US), /ɡɛt ˈʌp/ (UK)| v. |起床；起立|To rise from bed after sleeping; to stand up.|例句：I usually get up at 7 am. 我通常早上7点起床。

例句：He got up from his chair to greet her. 他从椅子上站起来迎接她。

近义词：arise, awaken, rise|反义词: lie down, sit down|词性:verb


词源: From the phrasal verb "get up," meaning to rise from bed, which originated in the late 16th century.  The noun form, referring to an outfit, emerged later, likely in the 19th century, possibly influenced by theatrical costumes.
