pallidity:/pəˈlɪdɪti/ | n. | 苍白；灰白；暗淡；缺乏活力 | An unhealthy pale appearance.  The state or condition of being pale. Lack of vigor or intensity. |  Her face had a certain pallidity, a lack of color that suggested illness. 她的脸色有些苍白，缺乏血色，表明她生病了。

The pallidity of the winter sky made the landscape seem bleak. 冬日天空的暗淡让景色显得荒凉。 | 近义词：paleness, wanness, whiteness, ashenness, lackluster, dullness | 反义词： ruddiness, rosiness, vibrancy, brightness, colorfulness | 词性: 名词


pallid:/ˈpælɪd/ | adj. | 苍白的；灰白的；暗淡的；缺乏活力的 |  Pale, typically because of poor health. Lacking color or intensity. | He looked pallid and unwell. 他看起来脸色苍白，身体不适。

The pallid light of the moon cast long shadows.  苍白的月光投下长长的影子。| 近义词：pale, wan, ashen, colorless, dull, lackluster | 反义词：ruddy, rosy, vibrant, bright, colorful | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *palliditas, from Latin pallidus "pale," perhaps from pallere "to be pale".
