outequivocating:/ˌaʊtɪˈkwɪvəkeɪtɪŋ/
| adj. | 胜过模棱两可的，更加明确的 |  Surpassing or exceeding someone in equivocating; being more evasive or ambiguous than someone else.  This word is not standard English and is likely a humorous or ironic formation based on the word "equivocating." | "He was outequivocating even the most seasoned politicians with his vague responses." 他含糊其辞的回应甚至超过了那些老练的政客。| 近义词: outmaneuvering, outslicking | 反义词: straightforward, candid | 词性: 形容词

词源:  Derived from "out-" (meaning exceeding or surpassing) + "equivocating" (present participle of "equivocate," from Late Latin aequivocatus, from aequi- "equally" + vocare "to call").
