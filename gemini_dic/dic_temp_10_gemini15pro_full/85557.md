hatchment: /ˈhætʃmənt/ | n. | 菱形纹章，铭旌 | A diamond-shaped panel bearing a coat of arms, formerly erected as a memorial to a deceased person, usually on the front of their house. | Example: A hatchment was affixed to the manor house after the lord's death.  庄园主去世后，庄园的房子上贴上了一个菱形纹章。

Example: The faded hatchment served as a reminder of the family's history.  褪色的菱形纹章提醒着人们这个家族的历史。| Synonym: escutcheon, coat of arms | Antonym: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French hachement ("hatching, partitioning"), from hacher ("to hatch").  The original meaning referred to the partitioning of a shield with different colors or patterns.
