hamiltonian:/ˌhæmɪlˈtoʊniən/ | n. adj. |哈密顿量，哈密顿的|A function that represents the total energy of a physical system, used in classical mechanics and quantum mechanics.  Also used as an adjective relating to William Rowan Hamilton or his work. |例句：The Hamiltonian of this system is rather complex.  这个系统的哈密顿量相当复杂。|例句：We used a Hamiltonian approach to solve the problem. 我们用哈密顿方法解决了这个问题。|近义词：energy function (in some contexts) |反义词: N/A |词性:noun, adjective

Etymology: Named after William Rowan Hamilton (1805-1865), an Irish physicist, astronomer, and mathematician.
