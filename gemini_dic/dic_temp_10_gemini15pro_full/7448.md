anisodactyla:/ˌænɪsoʊˈdæktɪlə/ | n. |  异趾类 |  A suborder of artiodactyl mammals characterized by having the weight of the foot borne by the third and fourth toes, which are equal in size.  Includes ruminants (cattle, sheep, deer, etc.) and camels. | Example: Cattle and deer are members of the anisodactyla suborder. 牛和鹿是异趾亚目动物。 |  Example: The anisodactyla have evolved specialized digestive systems. 异趾类动物进化出了专门的消化系统。 | Synonym: Artiodactyla (in part),  Ruminantia (in part), Tylopoda (in part) | Antonym:  Perissodactyla | noun
