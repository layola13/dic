lynchpins:/ˈlɪntʃpɪnz/
noun
关键人物;核心人物;关键;核心;中枢
A lynchpin is someone or something that is very important to an organization or plan.
例句：He is the lynchpin of the team’s offense. 他是球队进攻的核心人物。

例句：The new factory will be the lynchpin of the regional economy. 新工厂将成为区域经济的核心。

近义词：keystone, cornerstone, linchpin, kingpin, pivotal
反义词：unimportant, insignificant, trivial
词性：n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From lynch + pin. Originally referred to a pin that fastens an axle to a carriage, preventing the wheel from falling off.  The crucial importance of this pin led to its metaphorical use for anything similarly essential.
