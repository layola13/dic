flounce:/flaʊns/| n.| 荷叶边；裙褶；猛然离去；轻快地走；|A wide ornamental strip of material gathered and sewn to a piece of clothing, typically around a skirt or at the lower edge of a sleeve.  The act of flouncing.|The dress had a pretty flounce at the hem. 这件连衣裙的裙摆有一圈漂亮的荷叶边。|近义词：ruffle, frill, furbelow|反义词：plainness|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *floncer*, from Old French *floncer* "to sag, hang limp," perhaps of imitative origin.  The sense shifted to "to move with exaggerated motion" in the 18th century.
