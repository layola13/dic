newton:/ˈnjuːtn/| n.|牛顿（力的单位）; 牛顿(姓氏)|A newton is the SI unit of force. It is equal to the force that gives a mass of one kilogram an acceleration of one metre per second per second. |One newton is roughly the force required to hold an apple against gravity. 一个牛顿大约是将一个苹果对抗重力所需的力。|近义词:N (symbol), unit of force|反义词:None (It's a unit of measurement)|noun

newton:/ˈnjuːtn/| n.|牛顿(人名)|Sir Isaac Newton was a renowned physicist and mathematician. |Isaac Newton is considered one of the most influential scientists of all time.  艾萨克·牛顿被认为是史上最有影响力的科学家之一。|近义词:None (It's a proper noun)|反义词:None (It's a proper noun)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the surname of Sir Isaac Newton (1643-1727), the English physicist and mathematician who formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation.
