cordoned:/ˈkɔːrdənd/ | adj. | 用警戒线围住的，隔离的 | Enclosed or separated off with a cordon. | Example: The crime scene was cordoned off by police.  犯罪现场被警察用警戒线封锁了。| Example: The infected area was cordoned off to prevent the spread of the disease. 受感染的区域被隔离，以防止疾病传播。 | Synonyms:  blocked off, sealed off, roped off, closed off | Antonyms: open, accessible | word property: adjective

cordon:/ˈkɔːrdən/ | n. | 警戒线，封锁线 | A line or circle of police, soldiers, or guards preventing access to or from an area or building. | Example:  A police cordon had been thrown around the building.  警察在建筑物周围设置了警戒线。 | Example: The protesters broke through the cordon. 抗议者冲破了警戒线。| Synonyms: barrier, barricade, line, perimeter | Antonyms: opening, access | word property: noun

cordon:/ˈkɔːrdən/ | v. |  用警戒线围住，隔离 | To enclose or separate off with a cordon. | Example: The police cordoned off the area.  警察用警戒线封锁了该地区。| Example: They cordoned off the street after the explosion. 爆炸发生后，他们用警戒线封锁了街道。| Synonyms: block off, seal off, rope off, close off | Antonyms: open,  unblock | word property: verb
