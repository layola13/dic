manis:/ˈmeɪnɪs/ | n. | 鳞甲，穿山甲 | A scaly anteater.  A mammal of the order Pholidota, found in tropical Africa and Asia, that has a body covered with overlapping horny scales and a long, sticky tongue used to catch ants and termites. | The manis curled up into a ball when it sensed danger. 穿山甲在感觉到危险时蜷缩成一团。| Synonym: pangolin | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for an animal) | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Malay *maŋgis*, ultimately from Proto-Malayo-Polynesian *ma-ŋiSek*, meaning "to roll up".  This refers to the animal's defensive behavior.
