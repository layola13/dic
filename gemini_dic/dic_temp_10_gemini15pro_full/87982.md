high-mindedly:/ˌhaɪˈmaɪndɪdli/
adverb
高尚地；高洁地
in a way that shows high moral principles
He high-mindedly refused the bribe. 他高尚地拒绝了贿赂。

high-minded:/ˌhaɪˈmaɪndɪd/
adjective
高尚的；高洁的
having strong moral principles
She is a high-minded woman who always puts others first. 她是一个高尚的女人，总是把别人放在第一位。


Etymology:
From high + minded + -ly.  "High" refers to elevated or noble, and "minded" refers to one's thoughts or intentions.  The suffix "-ly" turns the adjective "high-minded" into an adverb.


Synonyms:
nobly, virtuously, ethically, morally, righteously, principledly

Antonyms:
basely, ignobly, dishonorably, selfishly, meanly, despicably
