charter:/ˈtʃɑːrtər/ (US) /ˈtʃɑːtə(r)/ (UK)| n. vt.| 宪章，特许状；租赁；包租|A written grant by a country's legislative or sovereign power, by which an institution such as a company, college, or city is created and its rights and privileges defined.|The company received a royal charter in 1685.  这家公司于1685年获得了皇家特许状。|Synonym:  constitution,  franchise, license |Antonym:  prohibition, restriction|noun

charter:/ˈtʃɑːrtər/ (US) /ˈtʃɑːtə(r)/ (UK)| vt.|包租；特许设立|To hire or lease (a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle).|They chartered a bus to take them to the concert. 他们包了一辆巴士去听音乐会。|Synonym: hire, rent, lease |Antonym: sublet|verb
