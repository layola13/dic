defeasance:/dɪˈfiːzəns/
n.|解除；废除；失效；作废|The act of rendering something null and void; annulment.  The discharge of an obligation or debt, often through a payment or transfer of property.|The defeasance clause in the mortgage allowed the borrower to regain full ownership upon final payment.|annulment, cancellation, invalidation, revocation|establishment, enactment|noun
