middle-of-the-roadisms:/ˌmɪdl əv ðə ˈroʊdɪzəmz/
|n.| 中庸之道；不偏不倚的主张；温和的观点|The candidate's middle-of-the-roadisms appealed to a broad range of voters. 这位候选人的中庸之道吸引了广泛的选民。|Avoiding extremes or radical positions.  Characterized by moderation or neutrality.  It refers to opinions, policies, or attitudes that seek a compromise between opposing viewpoints.  Often used in political contexts.|The party's platform was full of middle-of-the-roadisms, designed not to offend anyone.  该党的纲领充满了中庸之道，旨在不得罪任何人。|近义词: centrism, moderatism, neutrality|反义词: extremism, radicalism|词性:名词

词源:  Compound formed from "middle of the road" + "-ism".  "Middle of the road" literally refers to the safest part of a road, away from the ditches on either side. Figuratively, it signifies avoiding extreme positions.  "-ism" is a suffix used to form nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, or practices.
