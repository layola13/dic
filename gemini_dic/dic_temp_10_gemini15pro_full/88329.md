hippocampi: /ˌhɪpəˈkæmpaɪ/ /ˌhɪpəˈkæmpaɪ/
n. |海马体|The hippocampi are a pair of structures in the brain that play a crucial role in memory, particularly spatial memory and the consolidation of short-term memory into long-term memory. |The hippocampi are essential for forming new memories.|近义词: hippocampus (singular)|反义词: N/A|词性: Noun (plural)

hippocampus: /ˌhɪpəˈkæmpəs/ /ˌhɪpəˈkæmpəs/
n. |海马体|A complex neural structure (shaped like a seahorse) consisting of gray matter and located on the floor of each lateral ventricle of the brain; intimately involved in motivation and emotion as part of the limbic system and important in the consolidation of new memories. |Damage to the hippocampus can result in severe memory impairment.|近义词: N/A|反义词: N/A|词性: Noun (singular)
