neanderthal: /niˈændərˌθɔːl/ (US), /niˈændəˌtɑːl/ (UK)| n. adj.|尼安德特人；粗鲁的人|An extinct species of human that was widely distributed in ice-age Europe between *c*. 120,000–35,000 years ago, with a receding forehead and prominent brow ridges. Also (informal, often offensive): a person who is regarded as unintelligent or uncivilized.|例句：Neanderthals were skilled hunters. 尼安德特人是熟练的猎人。|例句：He called him a Neanderthal for not understanding the joke. 他因为他不理解这个笑话而叫他尼安德特人。|近义词：caveman, primitive|反义词:modern human|词性:名词，形容词

Etymology: From *Neander Tal* (Neander Valley), where the first recognized fossil specimen was found in 1856.
