organised: /ˈɔːɡənaɪzd/ (UK) /ˈɔːrɡənaɪzd/ (US)
adjective
有条理的，有组织的
arranged or planned in a systematic way, especially in a way that is efficient and effective
例句：She's a very organised person. 她是一个非常有条理的人。
例句：The event was well organised and ran smoothly.  这次活动组织得很好，进行得很顺利。
近义词：structured, ordered, methodical, systematic, efficient
反义词：disorganised, chaotic, messy
词性: adjective

organised: /ˈɔːɡənaɪzd/ (UK) /ˈɔːrɡənaɪzd/ (US)
verb (past tense and past participle)
组织，安排
to arrange or plan something
例句：They organised a protest against the new law. 他们组织了一场抗议新法律的示威活动。
例句：The trip was organised by the school.  这次旅行是由学校组织的。
近义词：arrange, plan, coordinate, manage, structure
反义词：disorganise
词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the French "organiser," from "organe" (organ), ultimately from the Greek "organon" (instrument, tool). The suffix "-ised" denotes the act or process of making something organized.
