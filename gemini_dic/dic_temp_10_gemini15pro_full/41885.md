coshing:/ˈkʌʃɪŋ/ | n. |库欣综合征| Cushing's syndrome, sometimes called hypercortisolism, is a disorder that occurs when your body makes too much of the hormone cortisol over a long period of time. | Cushing's disease is a specific type of Cushing's syndrome. It occurs when a pituitary tumor causes the body to make too much cortisol. | 近义词: hypercortisolism | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, consider hypocortisolism - Addison's disease for contrasting condition) | 词性: noun


Etymology: Named after Harvey Cushing (1869-1939), an American neurosurgeon who first described the condition.
