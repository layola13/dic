ballaster:/ˈbæləstər/ | n. | 镇流器；压舱物；道碴 | A device that regulates the electric current in a fluorescent lamp or other gas-discharge tube. Also, heavy material, such as gravel, sand, iron, or water, placed low in a vessel to improve its stability.  Lastly, broken stone used to form the bed of a railroad track. | This old fluorescent light needs a new ballaster.  这个旧的荧光灯需要一个新的镇流器。

The ship took on ballaster in the harbor. 这艘船在港口装载了压舱物。

The railroad workers were laying new ballaster.  铁路工人正在铺设新的道碴。 | Synonyms: ballast, stabilizer, counterweight (for stability sense); regulator (for electrical sense) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, depends on context.  For stability, perhaps "destabilizer") | Noun
