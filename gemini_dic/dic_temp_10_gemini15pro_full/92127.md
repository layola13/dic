hyperscrupulosity:/ˌhaɪpərˌskruːpjəˈlɒsɪti/ | n. | 过度谨慎；过度小心；良心过度敏感 |  A psychological condition characterized by excessive worry and guilt about moral or religious issues, often involving a distorted sense of sin or wrongdoing.  | Example 1: Her hyperscrupulosity led her to confess minor infractions repeatedly. 她过度谨慎，导致她反复忏悔一些小过错。

Example 2: He suffered from hyperscrupulosity, constantly questioning the morality of his actions. 他患有良心过度敏感，不断质疑自己行为的道德性。 | Near Synonyms: scrupulosity, conscientiousness, overconscientiousness, moral anxiety | Antonyms: carelessness, recklessness, unscrupulousness | noun
