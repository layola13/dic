faintling:/ˈfeɪntlɪŋ/
n.|懦弱的人；胆小鬼|A timid or cowardly person. |He was considered a faintling by his peers because he was afraid of the dark.|近义词: coward, weakling, milksop|反义词: hero, brave, strong|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *feintling, from Old French *feint, present participle of feindre (“to feign, pretend”), from Latin fingō (“I shape, fashion, devise, invent, feign”).  The suffix "-ling" denotes a diminutive or disparaging sense.
