indentureship:/ɪnˈdentʃəʃɪp/ | n. | 包身工制度，契约劳工身份 | A system of labor in which a person is bound by contract to work for another for a specified period, often in return for travel expenses or training.  A state of being bound by indentures. |  Example 1:  Indentureship was a common practice in the 19th century, particularly for transporting laborers to colonies.  19世纪，包身工制度是一种常见的做法，尤其是在将劳工运送到殖民地的时候。

Example 2: Her grandfather's indentureship in the Caribbean lasted for seven years. 她祖父在加勒比地区的契约劳工身份持续了七年。 | Synonym: servitude, bondage, apprenticeship (in some contexts) | Antonym: freedom, liberty | noun
