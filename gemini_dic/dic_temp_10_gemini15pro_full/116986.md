mccarthyism:/məˈkɑːrθiɪzəm/
|n.|麦卡锡主义|The practice of making accusations of subversion or treason without proper regard for evidence.  It refers to a period in the United States, during the 1950s, characterized by heightened political repression against supposed communists and left-wingers.|Senator Joseph McCarthy's campaign was a dark period of McCarthyism in American history. 约瑟夫·麦卡锡参议员的运动是美国历史上麦卡锡主义的黑暗时期。|Synonyms: witch-hunt, red-baiting, demagoguery|Antonyms: tolerance, due process, fairness|Noun
