discolour:/dɪsˈkʌlər/ /dɪsˈkʌlɚ/ |v. |使变色，褪色|to change the colour of something, especially by making it paler or less bright|The sunlight discoloured the curtains. 阳光使窗帘褪色了。|近义词：fade, bleach, stain, tarnish|反义词: brighten, colour, dye|词性:动词

discolour:/dɪsˈkʌlər/ /dɪsˈkʌlɚ/ |adj. |变色的，褪色的|(especially of white or light-coloured things) having lost some of its original colour; stained|The discoloured walls showed signs of damp. 变色的墙壁显示出潮湿的迹象。|近义词：faded, bleached, stained, tarnished|反义词: bright, colourful, vibrant|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing reversal) + colour.  The prefix "dis-" signifies negation or reversal, indicating a change from the original colour.
