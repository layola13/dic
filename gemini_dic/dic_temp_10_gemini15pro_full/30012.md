cawky: /ˈkɔːki/ | adj. | 笨拙的，粗鲁的 |  Resembling or characteristic of a jackdaw, especially in being raucous or harsh.  Awkward and clumsy in manner or appearance.  | 例句：His cawky laugh grated on my nerves. 他那笨拙的笑声让我神经紧张。| 例句：She made a cawky attempt at a curtsy. 她笨拙地行了个屈膝礼。 | 近义词：awkward, clumsy, gawky, ungainly | 反义词：graceful, elegant, refined | 词性：形容词

词源:  From "caw" (the cry of a crow or jackdaw) + "-y" (adjective-forming suffix).  Mimicking the perceived awkwardness or harshness of the bird's cry and applying it to human behavior.
