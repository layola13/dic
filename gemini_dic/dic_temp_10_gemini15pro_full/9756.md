antlers:/ˈæntlərz/ (US), /ˈæntləz/ (UK)| n. |鹿角；茸角|The branched bony structures that grow on the heads of male deer (and some female deer species), typically shed and regrown annually. They are used for display, dominance, and fighting.|The stag's antlers were magnificent. 那只雄鹿的鹿角非常壮观。|Synonyms: horns, racks|Antonyms: N/A (Antlers are a specific biological feature, not a concept with a direct opposite.)|noun
