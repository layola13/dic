articulatorily:/ɑːrˌtɪkjʊˈleɪtərɪli/ | adv. |清晰地，表达清楚地 |  In a manner that is clear and distinct in speech; with clear articulation. |  She explained the complex theory articulatorily, making it easy to understand. 她清晰地解释了这个复杂的理论，使它易于理解。 | clearly, distinctly, eloquently | inarticulately, unclearly | adverb

articulate:/ɑːrˈtɪkjʊleɪt/ | adj. | 清晰的，表达清楚的 |  Able to express oneself clearly and effectively. | He is an articulate speaker who can captivate an audience. 他是一位口才流利的演说家，能够吸引观众。 | clear, distinct, eloquent | inarticulate, unclearly | adjective

articulate:/ɑːrˈtɪkjʊleɪt/ | v. | 清晰地表达，清晰地发音 | To express (an idea or feeling) fluently and coherently.  To pronounce (something) clearly and distinctly. |  He struggled to articulate his feelings about the loss. 他难以表达他对失去的感受。 She articulated each syllable with precision. 她清晰地发出每一个音节。| express, pronounce, voice | mumble, slur | verb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *articulātus, past participle of articulāre (“to utter distinctly, joint”), from Latin articulus (“a small joint”), diminutive of artus (“joint”).
