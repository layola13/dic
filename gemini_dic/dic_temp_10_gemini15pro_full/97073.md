inferno:/ɪnˈfɜːrnoʊ/ (US) /ɪnˈfɜːnəʊ/ (UK)| n.|地狱；火海；极炎热难耐的地方|a large fire that is dangerously out of control;  (figuratively) any place or situation of extreme heat or torment|The whole city was an inferno after the bombing.  整个城市在轰炸后变成了火海。|Synonyms: hell, blaze, conflagration, firestorm|Antonyms: paradise, heaven|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *inferno* "hell,"  from Latin *īnfernus*  "below, underground, of the lower world," literally "low," from *īnferus* "low," comparative adjective of *īnfer* "below."  Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European root *ndher-* "under."
