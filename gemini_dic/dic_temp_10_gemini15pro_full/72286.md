flotsam and jetsam: /ˈflɒtsəm ənd ˈdʒetsəm/ | n. |  漂浮物；废料；零碎物品；社会渣滓|  Refers to the wreckage of a ship or its cargo found floating on the sea (flotsam) or washed ashore (jetsam), often used figuratively to describe unimportant things or people.  The term is often used to describe homeless or vagrant people, which can be considered derogatory. | The beach was littered with flotsam and jetsam after the storm.  风暴过后，海滩上到处都是漂浮物和垃圾。| The city's flotsam and jetsam gathered in the park. 城市的流浪汉聚集在公园里。 | synonyms: debris, wreckage, refuse, rubbish, riffraff | antonyms: valuables, treasures | noun
