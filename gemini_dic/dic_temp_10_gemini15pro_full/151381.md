parley:/ˈpɑːrli/ /ˈpɑːrleɪ/| n. vi.|谈判，会谈；|A discussion or conference, especially between enemies or opposing sides, to discuss terms or settle a dispute.|例句：The two sides agreed to a parley to discuss a ceasefire. 双方同意进行会谈，讨论停火事宜。

例句：The rebels requested a parley with the government. 叛乱分子要求与政府进行谈判。|近义词：negotiation, discussion, conference, meeting|反义词：fight, battle, conflict|词性:noun, verb

parleys:/ˈpɑːrliz/ /ˈpɑːrleɪz/| n. vi.|谈判，会谈（parley的复数形式）；|Discussions or conferences, especially between enemies or opposing sides, to discuss terms or settle a dispute.|例句：The parleys between the warring factions failed to produce a peace agreement. 交战各方之间的会谈未能达成和平协议。

例句：Several parleys were held before a truce was declared. 在宣布停战之前举行了多次会谈。|近义词：negotiations, discussions, conferences, meetings|反义词：fights, battles, conflicts|词性:noun, verb


词源: From Old French *parler (“to speak”), from Latin parabolāre (“to speak in parables”), alteration of parabola (“parable”), from Ancient Greek παραβολή (parabolé, “comparison, parable”).  The sense of "to confer with an enemy" arose in the 16th century.
