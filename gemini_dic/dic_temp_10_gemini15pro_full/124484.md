mouldering:/ˈmoʊldərɪŋ/ (US), /ˈməʊldərɪŋ/ (UK)| adjective|腐烂的，朽烂的；衰败的|Slowly decaying or disintegrating, especially because of dampness or neglect.  Relating to something that is outdated or decaying.|例句：The mouldering books in the attic were covered in dust.  阁楼里腐烂的书籍上满是灰尘。|例句：The once grand mansion now stood mouldering and deserted.  曾经宏伟的宅邸如今破败不堪，无人居住。|近义词：decaying, rotting, disintegrating, crumbling, deteriorating, decomposing|反义词：flourishing, thriving, growing, developing|词性：adjective

moulder:/ˈmoʊldər/ (US), /ˈməʊldə(r)/ (UK)| verb|腐烂，朽烂；衰败|To decay or disintegrate slowly, especially because of dampness or neglect.  To become outdated or less important.|例句：The fruit was left to moulder in the bowl. 水果被留在碗里腐烂了。|例句：His ambitions mouldered as he grew older. 随着年龄的增长，他的雄心壮志逐渐消退。|近义词：decay, rot, disintegrate, crumble, deteriorate, decompose|反义词：flourish, thrive, grow, develop|词性：verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *muldren*, from Old Norse *mygla* ("mold, mildew"). Cognate with Dutch *molderen* ("to moulder"), German *modern* ("to rot, decay").  The sense of "to crumble into dust" developed later.
