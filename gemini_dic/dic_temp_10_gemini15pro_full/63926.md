epiploce:/ɪˈpɪpləsi/ | n. |反复法;同义反复 |  A rhetorical figure of speech in which successive clauses or sentences repeat the same word or phrase at the end for emphasis.  | Example: "He's learning fast; are *you* learning fast? I say, are *you* learning fast enough?" | Synonym:  epanalepsis, anadiplosis | Antonym:  N/A (stylistic device, no direct opposite) | noun
