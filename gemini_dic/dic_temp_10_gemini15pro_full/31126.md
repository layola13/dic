chancellors: /ˈtʃɑːnsələrz/ | n. | 校长，大臣 | A head of a university or college.  A high government official, such as in Germany or the UK.  |  The chancellor announced a new scholarship program. 校长宣布了一项新的奖学金计划。

The Chancellor of the Exchequer presented the budget.  财政大臣提交了预算。 | synonyms: head, director, president, minister, secretary  | antonyms: subordinate, assistant | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *canceler, from Late Latin cancellarius "chief scribe, secretary," from cancelli "lattice work," referring to the screen separating a judge or high official from petitioners. The sense shifted from a minor official guarding a lattice to a high official.
