hustle:/ˈhʌsl/ (US), /ˈhʌsl/ (UK)| v. n.| 催促，赶紧，奔波，忙碌，欺骗 |To work hard and energetically, often in a variety of ways, to achieve success; to force someone to move quickly; to sell something aggressively or illegally.|  She hustled to catch the train. 她急忙赶火车。|近义词：rush, hurry, hasten, scam |反义词： dawdle, linger, procrastinate|词性: Verb, Noun

词源: From the Dutch word *husselen*, meaning "to shake, toss," likely influenced by similar-sounding words in other Germanic languages related to shaking or moving quickly.
