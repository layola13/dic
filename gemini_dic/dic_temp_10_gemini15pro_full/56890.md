dossel:/ˈdɒsəl/ (US: /ˈdɑːsəl/)| n.|帐篷，华盖，天篷|A canopy or hanging cloth, especially one placed over an altar, throne, or bed.|The king sat beneath a rich dossel.|Synonyms: canopy, baldachin, drapery|Antonyms: None|Noun

Etymology->
From Old French *dossal, from Late Latin *dorsale, from Latin dorsum "back". Originally referring to a cloth hung at the back of an altar or throne.
