compromising: /ˈkɒmprəmaɪzɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmprəmaɪzɪŋ/ (US) | adj. | 妥协的，让步的；有损名誉的，不名誉的 |  damaging to someone's reputation or lowering their standards. |  The photos were compromising and could ruin his career.  这些照片有损他的名誉，可能会毁了他的事业。 | synonyms: incriminating, damaging, discrediting, embarrassing, shameful | antonyms: flattering, enhancing, commendable, honorable | adjective


compromising: /ˈkɒmprəmaɪzɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmprəmaɪzɪŋ/ (US) | v. | 妥协，折衷；危及，损害 |  to settle a dispute by mutual concession; to expose to danger, suspicion, or disrepute.  (present participle of *compromise*) |  They were unwilling to compromise their principles.  他们不愿在原则问题上妥协。 | synonyms: concede, negotiate, endanger, jeopardize | antonyms: resist, maintain, protect | verb (present participle)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin compromittere "to promise mutually," from com- "together" + promittere "to promise."
