drench:/drɛntʃ/| vt. |浸透，湿透| to make someone or something extremely wet | The sudden downpour drenched us to the skin. 突如其来的倾盆大雨把我们淋得浑身湿透。|The cyclists were drenched by the heavy rain. 骑自行车的人被大雨淋透了。|近义词：soak, saturate |反义词:dry|词性:动词

drench:/drɛntʃ/| n. |湿透|the act of making something completely wet; a large amount of something | The runners were caught in a drench. 跑步者被淋得浑身湿透。|Give the plants a good drench with the watering can. 用喷壶给植物浇透水。|近义词：soaking |反义词:drying|词性:名词

PHRASE->
drench with (something):  用…浸透/淋透  Drench the fabric with water before dyeing it.  染色前用水浸透织物。
drench in/with (sweat/blood):  浑身是（汗/血） He was drenched in sweat after the marathon. 马拉松比赛后，他浑身是汗。
drenched to the bone/skin:  浑身湿透 We were drenched to the bone after walking in the rain. 在雨中行走后，我们浑身湿透。
give something a good drench:  好好浇灌一下…  Give the garden a good drench with the hose. 用软管好好浇灌一下花园。
caught in a drench:  遭遇倾盆大雨 We were caught in a drench on our way home. 我们在回家的路上遇到了倾盆大雨。


词源: From Middle English *drenchen, from Old English *drencan, from Proto-Germanic *drinkaną (“to give to drink, drown”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰrénǵʰeti (“to draw, pull”). Cognate with Dutch drenken (“to water, soak”), German tränken (“to give to drink, water, soak”).  Related to drink.
