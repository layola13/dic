moralize: /ˈmɔːrəlaɪz/ (US), /ˈmɒrəlaɪz/ (UK)
verb
说教；教训；评论道德
To make moral judgments, especially in a self-righteous or tedious way.
He started moralizing about the dangers of drinking. 他开始对饮酒的危害进行说教。
preach, sermonize, lecture
- (This word doesn't typically have a direct antonym, but one could consider actions like "accepting" or "tolerating" as contrasting concepts.)
词性: verb


moralize: /ˈmɔːrəlaɪz/ (US), /ˈmɒrəlaɪz/ (UK)
noun
道德说教；道德评论
The act or practice of moralizing.
His constant moralizing was tiresome. 他不断的道德说教令人厌烦。
sermon, lecture, preaching
- (Similar to the verb form, a direct antonym is not readily available.)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *moralisen*, from Old French *moraliser* “to explain morally,” from *moral* (from Latin *moralis*, see moral) + *-iser* (from Latin *-izare*).
