cotyledonous:/ˌkɒtɪˈliːdənəs/ | adj. | 子叶的；有子叶的 | Having cotyledons; relating to or resembling a cotyledon. | Example 1:  Dicotyledonous plants have two cotyledons. 双子叶植物有两个子叶。

Example 2: The cotyledonous stage is crucial for seedling development. 子叶阶段对幼苗发育至关重要。|Synonym:  dicotyledonous (often used interchangeably, but strictly refers to having two cotyledons)| Antonym: acotyledonous (lacking cotyledons) | word property: adjective


cotyledon:/ˌkɒtɪˈliːdən/ | n. | 子叶 | The first leaf or one of the first pair or whorl of leaves developed by the embryo of a seed plant.  | Example 1: The bean's cotyledons store food for the developing plant.  豆类的子叶储存食物供正在发育的植物使用。

Example 2:  The cotyledons of a monocot are usually single and thin. 单子叶植物的子叶通常是单一的且薄的。| Synonym: seed leaf | Antonym: N/A (no direct antonym, but one could consider the mature leaves as a contrasting stage) | word property: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cotylēdon*, from Greek kotylēdōn ‘cup-shaped cavity’, from kotýlā ‘cup, bowl’.
