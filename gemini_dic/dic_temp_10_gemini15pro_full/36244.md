cognizant: /ˈkɒɡnɪzənt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːɡnɪzənt/ (US) | adj. | 知晓的，意识到的，认识到的 | Having knowledge or being aware of something. | The government is cognizant of the problem. 政府了解这个问题。|  The company was cognizant of the risks involved.  该公司了解所涉及的风险。 | Near synonyms: aware, conscious, mindful, informed | Antonyms: unaware, oblivious, ignorant, unconscious | adjective

cognizance: /ˈkɒɡnɪzəns/ (UK) /ˈkɑːɡnɪzəns/ (US) | n. | 认识，察知，审理 | Knowledge or awareness; the range of what has been perceived, noted, or understood. |  The court took cognizance of the new evidence. 法院注意到新的证据。 | The judge took cognizance of the defendant's youth. 法官考虑了被告的年轻。 | Near synonyms: awareness, knowledge, notice, perception | Antonyms: ignorance, unawareness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cogniscens*, present participle of *cognoscere* "to get to know," from *co-* "together" + *gnoscere* "to know."
