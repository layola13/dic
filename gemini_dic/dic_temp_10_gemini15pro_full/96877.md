inexorability:/ɪnˌɛksərəˈbɪləti/
n.
不可抗拒性；必然性；无情
The quality of being impossible to stop or prevent.
The inexorability of fate/death.  命运/死亡的不可抗拒性。
The inexorability of technological progress. 科技进步的必然性。

inexorably:/ɪnˈɛksərəbli/
adv.
不可抗拒地；必然地；无情地
In a way that is impossible to stop or prevent.
The disease progressed inexorably. 疾病无情地发展。
Time marches inexorably on. 时间无情地流逝。

inexorableness:/ɪnˈɛksərəbəlnəs/
n.
不可抗拒性；必然性；无情
Another term for inexorability.
The inexorableness of the aging process.  衰老过程的不可抗拒性。
He spoke of the inexorableness of change. 他谈到了变革的必然性。

inexorablere:/ɪnˈɛksərəbəl/
adj.
不可阻挡的；必然的；无情的
Impossible to stop or prevent.
The inexorable advance of technology.  科技的不可阻挡的进步。
The inexorable march of time.  时间无情的流逝。


词源:
From Late Latin inexorabilis, from in- ‘not’ + exorabilis ‘able to be moved by entreaty’ (from ex- ‘out, from’ + orare ‘pray’).

近义词:
relentlessness, implacability, inevitability, unstoppability

反义词:
avoidable, preventable, stoppable, flexible, yielding
