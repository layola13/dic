bustle:/ˈbʌsəl/ (US), /ˈbʌsl/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.|喧闹，熙熙攘攘；裙撑；忙碌，奔忙|A bustling place is full of activity.  A bustling person moves around energetically, doing many things.|The market bustled with shoppers.  市场里熙熙攘攘，挤满了购物者。|Synonyms: hustle, flurry, commotion|Antonyms: calm, quiet, stillness|noun

bustle:/ˈbʌsəl/ (US), /ˈbʌsl/ (UK)| vi. vt.|忙碌，奔忙；催促|To bustle is to move around energetically, doing many things.  To bustle someone is to make them hurry or cause them to be busy.|She bustled around the kitchen, preparing dinner. 她忙着在厨房里准备晚餐。|Synonyms: rush, hurry, dash|Antonyms: dawdle, linger, procrastinate|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bustelen*, frequentative of *busken* (“to prepare, make ready”), of uncertain origin.
