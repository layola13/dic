churl:/tʃɜːrl/ | n. | 乡下人，粗人，吝啬鬼 | A rude, bad-tempered, or stingy person. |  The churl refused to give the beggar even a penny.  那个粗人连一分钱都不肯施舍给乞丐。 | Synonyms: boor, lout, miser, skinflint | Antonyms: gentleman, philanthropist, generous person | noun

churl:/tʃɜːrl/ | adj. (obsolete) | 粗野的，吝啬的 | Rude, bad-tempered, or stingy. |  He gave a churl response to her kind offer.  他对她的好意作出了粗鲁的回应。 | Synonyms:  surly, churlish | Antonyms:  polite, courteous, generous | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ceorl "man, freeman, husband," from Proto-Germanic *karlaz "man, free man," of unknown origin.  The meaning deteriorated over time from "freeman" to "peasant" to "rude person."
