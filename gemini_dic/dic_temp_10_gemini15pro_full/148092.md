overscrupulous:/ˌoʊvərˈskruːpjələs/
adjective
过分谨慎的；过于拘泥的
Having or showing a tendency to be too concerned with correctness or propriety; excessively conscientious.
He was so overscrupulous in his work that he sometimes missed deadlines. 他工作过于谨慎，有时会错过截止日期。
overscrupulous:/ˌoʊvərˈskruːpjələs/
近义词: meticulous, conscientious, punctilious, fastidious
反义词: careless, negligent, remiss, unscrupulous

词源:
From over- + scrupulous.  "Over-" signifies "excessive" or "too much."  "Scrupulous" comes from the Latin "scrupulus," meaning "a small sharp stone," which figuratively referred to a cause of uneasiness or anxiety, hence a matter of conscience.
