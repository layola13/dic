blacklisting:/ˈblækˌlɪstɪŋ/| n.|列入黑名单，禁止|The act of putting someone or something on a blacklist.  This usually means refusing to do business with, or forbidding contact with, the listed person or entity.|Example 1: The company faced blacklisting after the scandal broke. 这家公司在丑闻曝光后被列入黑名单。

Example 2:  Blacklisting certain websites can help protect your computer from malware. 将某些网站列入黑名单可以帮助保护你的电脑免受恶意软件的侵害。|Synonym: banning, barring, excluding, ostracizing|Antonym:  approving, accepting, welcoming|noun

black-list:/ˈblækˌlɪst/| n. vt.|黑名单；将…列入黑名单|1.(n.) A list of people or entities who are disapproved of and are to be avoided or denied privileges. 2. (vt.) To put someone or something on a blacklist.|Example 1: His name was on the black-list, so he couldn't get a job. 他的名字在黑名单上，所以他找不到工作。

Example 2: The government black-listed the company for its illegal activities. 政府将这家公司列入黑名单，因为它从事非法活动。|Synonym:  blacklist (as one word), boycott list, proscription list|Antonym: whitelist, approved list|noun, verb
