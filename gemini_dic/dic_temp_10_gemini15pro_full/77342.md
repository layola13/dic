gaucho:/ˈɡaʊtʃoʊ/ (US), /ˈɡaʊtʃəʊ/ (UK)| n.| 南美牛仔；高乔人|A skilled horseman, typically one from the pampas of Argentina, Uruguay, or southern Brazil, known for their traditional way of life, often involving cattle herding and horsemanship.|The gaucho skillfully lassoed the runaway calf.  南美牛仔熟练地用套索套住了逃跑的小牛。|Synonyms: cowboy, herdsman, horseman|Antonyms: city-dweller|noun

gauchos:/ˈɡaʊtʃoʊz/ (US), /ˈɡaʊtʃəʊz/ (UK)| n.| 南美牛仔们；高乔人们|Plural form of gaucho, referring to multiple skilled horsemen from the pampas of South America.|The gauchos gathered around the campfire, sharing stories and songs.  南美牛仔们聚集在篝火旁，分享故事和歌曲。|Synonyms: cowboys, herdsmen, horsemen|Antonyms: city-dwellers|noun
