brake:/breɪk/| n. v.|刹车，制动器；阻碍，阻止|A device for slowing or stopping a vehicle or other moving mechanism. To slow or stop a vehicle or other moving mechanism.|I slammed on the brakes to avoid hitting the deer. 我猛踩刹车以避免撞到鹿。|The government's new policy is acting as a brake on economic growth. 政府的新政策阻碍了经济增长。|近义词：stop, halt, curb|反义词：accelerate, speed up|n. v.

brake:/breɪk/| n. v.|休息，暂停|A pause in work or activity.|Let's take a brake for lunch. 让我们休息一下吃午饭吧。|The negotiations reached a brake. 谈判陷入了停顿。|近义词：pause, rest, intermission|反义词：continuation, work, activity|n. v.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *brecan "to break, shatter," from Proto-Germanic *brekaną (source also of Old Saxon brekan, Old Frisian breka, Middle Dutch and Dutch breken, Old High German brehhan, German brechen, Old Norse breka "to break," Gothic brikan "to tear down").
