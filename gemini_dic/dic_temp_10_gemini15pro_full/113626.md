machiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | adj. n. | 马基雅维利主义的；权谋主义的；不择手段的 | Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  |  He displayed machiavellian tactics in his rise to power. 他在掌权的过程中展现了马基雅维利式的策略。 | Synonyms: crafty, devious, cunning, shrewd, wily | Antonyms:  honest, ingenuous, straightforward, principled | adjective

machiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | n. | 马基雅维利主义者；权谋家 | A person who schemes in a Machiavellian way. | He's a machiavellian who will stop at nothing to get what he wants. 他是一个不择手段的马基雅维利主义者，为了得到他想要的什么都做得出来。 | Synonyms:  schemer, manipulator, intriguer | Antonyms:  moralist, idealist | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Niccolò Machiavelli (1469-1527), Italian Renaissance writer and diplomat, author of *The Prince*, a treatise on acquiring and maintaining political power.  The adjective "machiavellian" is often used to describe his political philosophy, which advocated pragmatism and amorality in the pursuit of power.
