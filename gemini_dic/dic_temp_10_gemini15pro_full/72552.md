flutists: /ˈfluːtɪsts/ | n. | 长笛演奏家，笛手 | A person who plays the flute. | The orchestra had three flutists.  这支管弦乐队有三名长笛演奏家。 |  Synonyms: flautist, flute player | Antonyms: None | Noun

Etymology:
From flute + -ist.  "Flute" comes from Old French "flute" (14c.), from Old Provençal "flaut," from Vulgar Latin *flauta, of imitative origin.  "-ist" is a suffix forming agent nouns, from French "-iste" and Latin "-ista," from Greek "-istes."
