monadisms:/ˈmɒnədɪzəmz/ | n. | 单子论；单子主义 |  The philosophical doctrine of monads, a theory that the universe is composed of simple, indivisible, immaterial entities called monads, each reflecting the universe from its own unique perspective.  |  His writings delve into complex monadisms, exploring the nature of perception and reality.  他的著作深入探讨了复杂的单子论，探索了感知和现实的本质。 |  Near Synonyms: monadology, monism | Antonyms: pluralism, dualism | noun
