ball-breaking:/ˈbɔːlˌbreɪkɪŋ/
adjective
令人精疲力竭的，非常困难的，严苛的
Extremely difficult, demanding, or arduous.  This term often implies a sense of frustration and exhaustion.
That was a ball-breaking project, I barely slept for a week. 那是一个累死人的项目，我一星期几乎没睡。
The hike up the mountain was ball-breaking. 爬山真是累人。
近义词:grueling, arduous, exhausting, back-breaking, demanding
反义词:easy, simple, effortless
词性:adjective

ball-breaking:/ˈbɔːlˌbreɪkɪŋ/
adjective
(vulgar, slang)  折磨人的，刁难的，欺负人的
Used to describe someone or something that is deliberately difficult or unpleasant.  Often implies an intention to cause emotional or psychological distress, particularly to men.  Considered highly offensive.
He's got a ball-breaking boss who makes everyone's life miserable. 他有一个折磨人的老板，让每个人的日子都很难过。
She gave him a ball-breaking time over the smallest mistakes. 她为了一点小错误就刁难他。
近义词:harsh, cruel, tyrannical, oppressive, sadistic
反义词:kind, gentle, supportive, encouraging
词性:adjective


词源: This term originates from the literal act of crushing someone's testicles, which would be extremely painful and debilitating.  Figuratively, it refers to any situation that is similarly agonizing or incapacitating.
