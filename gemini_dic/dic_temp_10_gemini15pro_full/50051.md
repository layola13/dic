denouncements:/dɪˈnaʊnsmənts/
|n.|谴责，公开谴责| Public declarations of condemnation or disapproval. |The government issued strong denouncements of the terrorist attacks. 政府强烈谴责了恐怖袭击。|
| | |The newspaper published denouncements of the mayor's corruption. 报纸刊登了对市长腐败行为的谴责。|
| | |synonyms: condemnations, censures, criticisms, reproaches|
| | |antonyms: praises, commendations, endorsements|
| | |noun|


denouncement:/dɪˈnaʊnsmənt/
|n.|谴责，告发|A public statement declaring something evil or wrong.|His denouncement of the policy was met with applause. 他对这项政策的谴责赢得了掌声。|
| | |The whistleblower's denouncement of the company's illegal activities led to a full investigation.  告密者对公司非法活动的谴责导致了全面调查。|
| | |synonyms: accusation, indictment, incrimination, impeachment|
| | |antonyms: defense, exoneration, vindication|
| | |noun|
