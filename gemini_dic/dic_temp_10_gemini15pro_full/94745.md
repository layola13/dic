improvidently:/ɪmˈprɒvɪd(ə)ntli/| adv.|轻率地，不谨慎地| lacking foresight or thrift; carelessly.| He improvidently spent his inheritance and ended up penniless. 他挥霍了遗产，最后身无分文。|近义词：rashly, recklessly, thoughtlessly, carelessly|反义词：prudently, wisely, carefully, thoughtfully|词性：副词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *improvidentem (nominative improvidens), from Latin in- "not" + providentem (nominative providens) "foreseeing, providing," present participle of providēre "to foresee, take precautions, provide," from pro- "before, forward" + vidēre "to see."
