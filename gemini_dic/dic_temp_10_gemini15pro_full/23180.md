bowdlerises:/ˈboʊdləˌraɪzɪz/
| v. |删节；净化；修订|To remove or change parts of a book, play, etc. that are considered offensive or unsuitable, especially to make it acceptable for children.|The publisher bowdlerised the novel before releasing it to a younger audience. 出版商在将小说发行给年轻读者之前对其进行了删节。|expurgates, censors, cleanses|includes, keeps, preserves|verb

Etymology:
From Thomas Bowdler (1754–1825), a Scottish physician who published an expurgated edition of Shakespeare's works.
