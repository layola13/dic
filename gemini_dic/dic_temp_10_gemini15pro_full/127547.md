necrophore: /ˈnɛkrəˌfɔːr/ (US), /ˈnɛkrəfɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. |食腐昆虫；埋葬虫|An insect, especially a beetle, that consumes dead or decaying animal matter.|Necrophores play a vital role in the ecosystem by helping decompose carcasses. 食腐昆虫在生态系统中扮演着重要的角色，它们帮助分解尸体。|Synonyms: burying beetle, carrion beetle|Antonyms: N/A|noun

necrophore: /ˈnɛkrəˌfɔːr/ (US), /ˈnɛkrəfɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. |（尤指古埃及的）抬棺材的人|A person, especially in ancient Egypt, who carried a coffin or bier.|Ancient Egyptian necrophores were responsible for transporting the deceased to their final resting place. 古埃及的抬棺材的人负责将死者运送到他们的最终安息之地。|Synonyms: pallbearer|Antonyms: N/A|noun
