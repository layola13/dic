adjured:/əˈdʒʊərd/| vt.|恳求；命令；告诫|To earnestly urge or command someone to do something, especially under oath or with great solemnity.|The judge adjured the witness to tell the truth. 法官告诫证人要说实话。
The king adjured his people to remain loyal. 国王恳求他的人民保持忠诚。|近义词：implored, beseeched, urged, commanded, exhorted|反义词：discouraged, dissuaded|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French adjurer, from Latin adjurare, from ad- ‘to’ + jurare ‘swear’.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The lawyer adjures the witness to speak the truth. = 律师恳求证人说出真相。
He adjures his followers to remain calm. = 他告诫他的追随者保持冷静。

一般过去时:
She adjured him to reconsider his decision. = 她恳求他重新考虑他的决定。
The priest adjured the congregation to repent. = 牧师告诫会众忏悔。

现在完成时:
He has adjured them to keep the secret. = 他已经命令他们保守秘密。
I have adjured him several times to be careful. = 我已经多次告诫他要小心。

过去完成时:
They had adjured him to stay silent before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，他们已经命令他保持沉默。
She had adjured her children to behave well before the guests arrived. = 在客人到达之前，她已经告诫她的孩子们要表现得体。

将来时:
I will adjure him to stop spreading rumors. = 我会命令他停止散布谣言。
The court will adjure the defendant to cooperate. = 法庭将命令被告合作。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would adjure him to apologize. = 如果我是你，我会恳求他道歉。
I wish he would adjure his supporters to remain peaceful. = 我希望他会告诫他的支持者保持和平。


被动语态:
He was adjured by his friends to seek help. = 他的朋友恳求他寻求帮助。
The witnesses were adjured to tell the whole truth. = 证人被告诫要说出全部真相。


疑问句:
Why did you adjure him to leave? = 你为什么恳求他离开？
Did the judge adjure the jury to consider all the evidence? = 法官是否告诫陪审团考虑所有证据？

否定句:
I didn't adjure him to do anything illegal. = 我没有命令他做任何违法的事情。
She did not adjure them to lie. = 她没有告诫他们撒谎。

PHRASE->
adjure someone to do something = 恳求/命令某人做某事
adjure someone by something = 以...的名义恳求/命令某人
adjure someone to silence = 命令某人保持沉默
solemnly adjure = 郑重恳求/命令
be adjured to = 被恳求/命令去做
