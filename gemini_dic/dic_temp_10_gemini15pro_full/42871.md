courtiers:/ˈkɔːrtiərz/ (US), /ˈkɔːtɪəz/ (UK)| n. |廷臣；朝臣|A person who attends a royal court as a companion or adviser to the king or queen.|The courtiers whispered amongst themselves as the king entered the throne room. 国王进入王座室时，朝臣们互相窃窃私语。|Synonyms: attendants, retainers, vassals|Antonyms: commoners, subjects|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *corteier*, from *cort* (“court”), from Latin *cohors* (“enclosed yard, cohort”).
