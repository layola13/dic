out-of-pocket:/ˌaʊt əv ˈpɒkɪt/ (UK), /ˌaʊt əv ˈpɑːkɪt/ (US)| adj.|自掏腰包的；实际支出的|Paid for with one's own money, rather than by an insurance company or other organization.  Relating to expenses that someone has to pay themselves.|例句：The out-of-pocket expenses for the trip were quite high.  这次旅行的实际支出相当高。|近义词：direct expense, personal expense|反义词:covered, reimbursed|词性:形容词

out-of-pocket:/ˌaʊt əv ˈpɒkɪt/ (UK), /ˌaʊt əv ˈpɑːkɪt/ (US)| n.|自付费用|The expenses that someone has to pay themselves.|例句：Her out-of-pocket for the surgery was over $5,000. 她手术的自付费用超过了5000美元。|近义词：expense, cost|反义词:reimbursement|词性:名词

词源： "out of pocket" 最初指的是钱花光了，口袋空空如也。 后来演变为指个人必须支付的费用，而不是由其他人或组织（如保险公司）支付的费用。
