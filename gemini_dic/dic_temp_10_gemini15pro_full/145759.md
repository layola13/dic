overcunningly:/ˌoʊvərˈkʌnɪŋli/ | adv. | 过于狡猾地，过于精明地 |  In an excessively cunning or sly manner; with too much cleverness or craftiness. | The fox overcunningly planned his escape from the hunter.  狐狸过于狡猾地计划了从猎人手中逃脱。 | overcleverly, too cleverly, excessively slyly | naively, artlessly | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "over" (Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond") + "cunning" (Middle English "cunninge," from "cunnen," meaning "to know, be able to") + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).
