natural-philosopher:/ˈnætʃrəl fɪˈlɒsəfər/ (US) /ˈnætʃrəl fɪˈlɑːsəfər/ (UK)| n. |自然哲学家|A scholar who studied natural science in the period before the development of modern science, especially physics.  They sought to understand the workings of the world through observation and reason, often combining philosophical speculation with empirical investigation.  Before the 19th century, "natural philosophy" encompassed what we now call physics, chemistry, biology, astronomy, and other related fields.|Isaac Newton was a renowned natural-philosopher.  艾萨克·牛顿是一位著名的自然哲学家。
