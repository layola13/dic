nonmoderately:/nɒnˈmɒdərətli/ | adv. | 非适度地，不温和地 | Not in a moderate manner; excessively or extremely.  It describes actions or qualities that are not within reasonable or moderate limits. | The prices were nonmoderately high, making it difficult for many people to afford basic necessities. 价格过高，许多人难以负担基本生活必需品。| The team celebrated nonmoderately after their unexpected victory.  团队在意外获胜后进行了疯狂的庆祝。| 近义词: excessively, extremely, immoderately, inordinately | 反义词: moderately, reasonably, mildly | 词性: 副词

词源:

"non-" (prefix):  from Latin, meaning "not"
"moderate" (adjective/verb):  from Latin "moderatus," past participle of "moderari" (to regulate, restrain), from "modus" (measure, limit).
"-ly" (suffix): forms adverbs from adjectives.
