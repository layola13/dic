declarative:/dɪˈklærətɪv/| adj.|声明的，陈述的，说明的|Making a statement; relating to the use of or containing a declaration.|例句：A declarative sentence states a fact.  一个陈述句说明了一个事实。|近义词：assertive, affirmative, definitive|反义词：interrogative, imperative, exclamatory|词性:形容词
词源:Late Middle English: from late Latin declarativus, from declarare ‘make clear’ (see declare).

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Declarative sentences are commonly used in the simple present tense. 陈述句通常用一般现在时。
He eats an apple every day. 他每天吃一个苹果。

The sun rises in the east. 太阳从东方升起。


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
She is reading a book. 她正在读一本书。
They are playing football in the park. 他们在公园里踢足球。


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
I have finished my homework. 我已经完成了我的作业。
We have been to Paris twice. 我们去过巴黎两次。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
He has been studying English for five years. 他学习英语已经五年了。
She has been working on this project for months.  她已经在这个项目上工作了好几个月了。


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
He went to the store yesterday. 他昨天去了商店。
She ate a sandwich for lunch. 她午饭吃了个三明治。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
I had already eaten dinner when he arrived. 他到达时我已经吃过晚饭了。
She had finished her work before the deadline.  她在截止日期前完成了工作。


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
