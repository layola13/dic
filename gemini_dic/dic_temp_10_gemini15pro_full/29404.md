cataclysm: /ˈkætəˌklɪzəm/ (US) /ˈkætəklɪzəm/ (UK)| n.|大灾难，灾变；剧变|A cataclysm is a sudden, violent change or disaster that causes widespread destruction.|The earthquake caused a cataclysm in the region. 地震给该地区带来了巨大的灾难。|The sudden death of the CEO was a cataclysm for the company. 首席执行官的突然去世对公司来说是一个巨大的灾变。|近义词：disaster, catastrophe, upheaval, calamity|反义词: boon, blessing|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin cataclysmus, from Ancient Greek κατακλυσμός (kataklusmós, “deluge, flood”), from κατακλύζω (kataklúzō, “I deluge, flood”), from κατά (katá, “down”) + κλύζω (klúzō, “I wash, dash over”).
