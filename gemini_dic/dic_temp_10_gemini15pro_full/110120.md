lictor:/ˈlɪktər/| n.|侍从官，执法官|An officer in ancient Rome who attended a magistrate, bearing the fasces as a symbol of power.|The lictors cleared the way for the consul. 侍从官为执政官开道。|Synonyms: attendant, guard, official|Antonyms: commoner, civilian|noun
