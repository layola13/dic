handed-over:/ˈhændɪd ˈoʊvər/ (US) /ˈhændɪd ˈəʊvə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|移交的，交付的|Something that has been handed over is something that has been officially given to someone else, often with a ceremony.|The company was handed over to the new management.  这家公司移交给了新的管理层。|Synonyms: transferred, delivered, relinquished, ceded|Antonyms: retained, kept, withheld|adjective

handed-over:/ˈhændɪd ˈoʊvər/ (US) /ˈhændɪd ˈəʊvə(r)/ (UK)| n.|移交，交付|The act of handing something over.|The official handed-over of the property took place yesterday.  房产的正式移交发生在昨天。|Synonyms: transfer, delivery, relinquishment, cession|Antonyms: retention, withholding|noun

Etymology:  "Handed" comes from Old English "hand" + "-ed" (past participle suffix). "Over" comes from Old English "ofer."  The combination signifies the action of passing something from one hand to another, across or over a space.
