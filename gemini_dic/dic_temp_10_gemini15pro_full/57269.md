downplay: /ˌdaʊnˈpleɪ/ | v. | 淡化，轻视，低调处理 | to make something seem less important or less bad than it really is | He downplayed his role in the project. 他淡化了他在项目中的作用。| minimize, understate, de-emphasize | exaggerate, overstate, emphasize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "down-" (reducing or lessening) + "play" (to perform or act).  The original sense was of an actor underplaying a role, making it seem less significant. This sense broadened to encompass minimizing the importance of anything.
