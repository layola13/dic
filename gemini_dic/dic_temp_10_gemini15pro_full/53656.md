disheveled: /dɪˈʃevəld/ (US) /dɪˈʃevld/ (UK)| adj.|蓬乱的，凌乱的|Being in a state of disorder or disarray; untidy; rumpled.  Characterized by disorder or disarray, especially in personal appearance; unkempt.|Her hair was disheveled and her clothes were torn. 她的头发蓬乱，衣服也破了。|Synonyms: unkempt, messy, rumpled, tousled, untidy, disordered|Antonyms: neat, tidy, orderly, groomed, kempt|adjective

dishevel: /dɪˈʃevəl/ (US) /dɪˈʃevl/ (UK)| vt.|使蓬乱，使凌乱|To make untidy; to disarrange or rumple.|The wind disheveled her hair. 风吹乱了她的头发。|Synonyms: disorder, disarrange, rumple, tousle, mess up|Antonyms: order, arrange, tidy, groom, neaten|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *descheveler, from des- (expressing reversal) + chevel (hair), from Latin capillus (hair).
