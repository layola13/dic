calmer:/ˈkɑːmər/ (US) /ˈkɑːmə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更平静的，更镇定的|Comparative form of calm: more calm.  Indicates a greater degree of calmness than something else being compared.|The lake was calmer in the morning. 早晨湖面更平静。|Near synonyms: more peaceful, more tranquil, more serene, quieter. |Antonyms: more agitated, more turbulent, more frantic, wilder.|adjective

calm:/kɑːm/ (US) /kɑːm/ (UK)| adj. n. v.|平静的；镇静的；平静；使平静；使镇静|From Old French *calme (unattested), from Late Latin cauma "heat of the sun, burning heat," from Greek kauma, from kaiein "to burn." Originally referred to the stillness of the air in the heat of the day. Sense of "freedom from disturbance or agitation" developed later. |The sea was calm after the storm.  暴风雨过后，大海平静了。|Near synonyms: peaceful, tranquil, serene, quiet. |Antonyms: agitated, turbulent, frantic, wild.|adjective, noun, verb
