competencies:/ˈkɒmpɪtənsiz/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmpɪtənsiz/ (US)| n. |能力，技能，胜任力|The job requires competencies in communication, problem-solving, and decision-making. 这份工作需要沟通、解决问题和决策方面的能力。|例句：The company is looking for employees with strong digital competencies.  该公司正在寻找具有强大数字能力的员工。

例句：Assessing the core competencies of the team is crucial for project success.  评估团队的核心能力对于项目成功至关重要。

近义词：skills, abilities, capabilities, aptitudes, expertise|反义词:inabilities, weaknesses, inadequacies|词性:名词

competency:/ˈkɒmpɪtənsi/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmpɪtənsi/ (US)| n. |能力，技能，胜任力|He demonstrated competency in all areas of the job. 他在工作的各个领域都表现出了能力。|例句：Her competency in several languages is a valuable asset. 她精通多种语言是一项宝贵的资产。

例句：The court questioned the defendant's competency to stand trial. 法院质疑被告是否有能力出庭受审。


近义词：skill, ability, capability, aptitude, expertise|反义词:inability, weakness, inadequacy|词性:名词


词源: Late Middle English: from late Latin competentia ‘meeting, agreement’, from competent- ‘coming together, agreeing’, from the verb competere .
