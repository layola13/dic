overdrive:/ˈoʊvərˌdraɪv/ (US), /ˈəʊvəˌdraɪv/ (UK)| n. v.|超速运转，加倍努力；过载；超速档| An overdrive is a state of increased activity or effort beyond the normal level. As a verb, it means to work or operate too hard or beyond normal capacity.  In cars, it refers to a gear that allows the engine to run at a lower speed while maintaining a high road speed.|The company is in overdrive trying to meet the deadline.  公司正在加班加点地努力赶在截止日期前完成任务。|Synonyms: accelerate, boost, intensify, hasten, expedite|Antonyms: decelerate, slow, reduce, diminish|noun/verb
