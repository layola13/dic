over-speculate: /ˌoʊvərˈspɛkjʊˌleɪt/ | v. | 过度推测，过分臆测 | To engage in speculation excessively or to an unreasonable degree.  To speculate beyond what is warranted by the available evidence. |  He over-speculated in the stock market and lost a fortune. 他在股市过度投机，损失了一大笔钱。

The media tends to over-speculate about the private lives of celebrities. 媒体倾向于过度揣测名人的私生活。 | 近义词: overestimate, conjecture, surmise | 反义词: underestimate, understate | 词性: 动词

词源: From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "speculate" (from Latin "speculatus," past participle of "speculari" meaning "to observe, watch, spy, examine").
