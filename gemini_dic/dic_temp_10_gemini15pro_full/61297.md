embargo:/ɪmˈbɑːrɡoʊ/ (US) /ɪmˈbɑːɡəʊ/ (UK)| n. vt.|禁运，禁止|An official ban on trade or other commercial activity with a particular country.|The UN imposed an embargo on arms sales to the warring nation. 联合国对交战国实施了武器禁运。|Synonyms: ban, prohibition, restriction|Antonyms: allowance, permission|noun

embargo:/ɪmˈbɑːrɡoʊ/ (US) /ɪmˈbɑːɡəʊ/ (UK)| vt.|禁止，禁运|To officially ban trade or other commercial activity with a particular country.|The government embargoed all imports from that country. 政府禁止从该国进口任何商品。|Synonyms: ban, prohibit, restrict|Antonyms: allow, permit|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *embargar* "to impede, restrain," from *em-*, "in, on" + *barra* "bar." Ultimately from Late Latin *barra* "bar."
