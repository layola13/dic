cheeping:/ˈtʃiːpɪŋ/| v. |吱吱叫|to make a short, high-pitched sound, typically of a young bird|The baby birds were cheeping loudly in the nest. 雏鸟在巢里大声唧唧叫。|近义词：chirping, peeping, tweeting|反义词: (没有明确的反义词，可以考虑 be silent, be quiet)|词性: 动词

词源:  From Middle English *chepen, from Old English *cēpan (attested only in cīpan "to sell, bargain, trade"), of uncertain origin, possibly from Proto-Germanic *kōpijaną ("to buy, barter").  The sense of "to chirp" is a later development, probably imitative.
