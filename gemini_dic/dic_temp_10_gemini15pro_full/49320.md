delightfulnesses:/dɪˈlaɪtfʊlnəsɪz/
|n.|令人愉快的事物；快乐；令人高兴的特质|The delightfulnesses of the countryside in spring are numerous. 春天乡村的乐趣数不胜数。|
|英文解析:Plural form of delightfulness, meaning the state or quality of being delightful; the ability to give great pleasure.|例句：She was struck by the sheer delightfulnesses of the little village. 她被小村庄纯粹的魅力所打动。|近义词：pleasures, joys, charms|反义词：unpleasantness, misery, boredom|词性:名词

词源:
From delightful + -ness + -es. Delightful comes from the Old French "deleitable," ultimately from the Latin "delectabilis."  "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality. "-es" is a plural marker.
