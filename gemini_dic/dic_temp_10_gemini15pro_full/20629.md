blacklisted:/ˈblækˌlɪstɪd/ | adj. | 被列入黑名单的；被抵制的 | Put on a blacklist; barred or excluded from a privilege, service, or group. |  例句：The actor was blacklisted after making controversial remarks.  这位演员因发表有争议的言论而被列入黑名单。

例句：Several companies were blacklisted for their unethical practices.  几家公司因不道德行为被列入黑名单。| 近义词：banned, barred, boycotted, ostracized | 反义词：approved, accepted, welcome | 词性: 形容词

blacklist:/ˈblækˌlɪst/ | n. vt. | 黑名单；将…列入黑名单 | A list of people or things that are disapproved of or are to be avoided or penalized. To put someone or something on a blacklist. | 例句：The government maintains a blacklist of suspected terrorists. 政府保留一份可疑恐怖分子的黑名单。

例句：The company blacklisted the supplier after a series of late deliveries.  该公司在发生一系列延迟交货后将供应商列入黑名单。| 近义词：proscribe, condemn | 反义词：approve, accept | 词性: 名词, 动词
