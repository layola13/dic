growler:/ˈɡraʊlər/ (US), /ˈɡraʊlə/ (UK)| n.|咆哮者；低吼的人/动物；装啤酒的大罐|A person or animal that growls. A large jug, typically made of glass or ceramic, used to transport draft beer.|The dog, a fierce growler, protected its territory. 这条狗，一个凶猛的咆哮者，守护着它的领地。|He filled a growler with his favorite IPA. 他用他最喜欢的印度淡色艾尔啤酒装满了一个大罐。|Synonyms: grumbler, complainer (for person), jug, container (for object)|Antonyms: purrer (for animal), none (for object)|noun


growler:/ˈɡraʊlər/ (US), /ˈɡraʊlə/ (UK)| n.|冰山碎片|A small iceberg or piece of ice that has broken away from a larger one, often making a growling sound.|The ship navigated carefully through the growlers. 船小心翼翼地在冰山碎片中航行。|Several growlers were spotted near the coast. 在海岸附近发现了几块冰山碎片。|Synonyms: berg bit, ice fragment|Antonyms: none|noun
