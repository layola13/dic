conductorship:/kənˈdʌktərʃɪp/
n.
指挥(职位); 列车长(职位);  售票员(职位)
The position or role of a conductor, especially of an orchestra or choir, or of a train or bus.
He was appointed to the conductorship of the Berlin Philharmonic. 他被任命为柏林爱乐乐团的指挥。
The conductorship of the orchestra is a highly coveted position.  这个乐团的指挥职位是很多人梦寐以求的。
近义词：leadership, directorship, management
反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑subordination, followership根据语境)
词性: 名词

conductorship:/kənˈdʌktərʃɪp/
n.
领导; 管理; 行为; 实施方式;
The way in which something is managed or carried out. (较少用于此义)
The conductorship of the project was criticized for its lack of organization.  该项目的管理方式因缺乏组织性而受到批评。(此用法较少见)
His conductorship of the business led to its eventual downfall. 他对公司的管理导致了最终的倒闭。(此用法较少见)
近义词：management, leadership, administration
反义词: (没有直接的反义词)
词性: 名词


词源:
From conductor + -ship.  Conductor comes from the Latin word "conducere," meaning "to lead together." The suffix "-ship" denotes a state, quality, or office.
