dupable:/ˈdjuːpəbl/ /ˈduːpəbl/| adj.|易受骗的，容易上当的|Easily deceived or tricked.| He was so dupable that he fell for the oldest trick in the book. 他很容易上当，竟然上了最老套的当。|Synonyms: gullible, credulous, naive, unsuspecting|Antonyms: astute, discerning, shrewd, skeptical|adjective

Etymology:
From dupe (v.) + -able.  "Dupe" comes from the Old French word "de", meaning "of" or "from", and "huppe," a bird's tuft of feathers used as a decoy in fowling.  The original meaning was related to catching birds with a deceptive lure, extending later to tricking people.
