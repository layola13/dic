panto's:/ˈpæntəʊz/| n.| 童话剧，哑剧（尤指圣诞节期间上演的）|A pantomime, especially one performed around Christmas. |We took the kids to see the panto's at the local theatre. 我们带孩子们去当地剧院看了哑剧。|Synonyms: pantomime, mimodrama |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "serious drama")|noun


panto's:/ˈpæntəʊz/| n.| 童鞋，室内拖鞋|Slippers or soft shoes worn indoors. (Less common usage, primarily British informal)|He shuffled around the house in his panto's. 他穿着拖鞋在家裡拖著腳走來走去。|Synonyms: slippers, house shoes |Antonyms:  outdoor shoes|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From a shortening of "pantomime," ultimately from Greek "panto-," meaning "all," and "mimos," meaning "imitator," hence "imitating all."  The "panto's" form likely arose through colloquial usage, similar to how "photo's" is used for "photographs."
