lily-white:/ˈlɪli waɪt/ | adj. | 纯洁的，洁白的；苍白的；(尤指)白人至上主义的 |  Of a pure white color, like that of a lily; also, figuratively, of unimpeachable purity or innocence; sometimes used ironically, or with implications of excessive or affected purity. In a racial context, it can refer to a person of exclusively white European ancestry, often with connotations of racial exclusivity or supremacy. |  Her skin was lily-white and flawless. 她的皮肤洁白无瑕。 | Synonyms: pure, spotless, immaculate, unblemished, pristine, snow-white, pallid, pale | Antonyms: tainted, sullied, impure, dark, tanned | Adjective

lily-white:/ˈlɪli waɪt/ | n. | 纯白; 洁白; 白种人 | A person of exclusively white European ancestry, often associated with racial exclusivity or supremacy in specific contexts. |  The organization was criticized for its lily-white membership. 这个组织因其纯白人成员身份而受到批评。 | Synonyms: Caucasian (can be problematic), white | Antonyms: person of color | Noun
