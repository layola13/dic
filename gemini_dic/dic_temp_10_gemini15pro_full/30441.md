centre:/ˈsentə(r) (UK) / /ˈsɛntər (US)/| n. v.|中心，中央；中心点；中心人物；中锋|The town centre is always busy on Saturdays.  这个镇的中心在星期六总是很热闹。|近义词：middle, core, heart, hub|反义词:edge, periphery, boundary|noun
centre:/ˈsentə(r) (UK) / /ˈsɛntər (US)/| v.|集中；以…为中心；使…居中|The discussion centred on the issue of funding. 讨论集中在资金问题上。|近义词：focus, concentrate|反义词:disperse, scatter|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *centre*, from Latin centrum “center,” from Greek kentron “sharp point, stationary point of a pair of compasses, center of a circle,” originally “sting, goad,” related to kentein “to prick.”
