clean-living:/ˌkliːnˈlɪvɪŋ/
adjective
干净生活的；生活检点的
Living a clean life, avoiding excesses like drugs, alcohol, and unhealthy food.  Characterized by wholesome habits.
She is known for her clean-living lifestyle. 她以其干净的生活方式而闻名。
He attributed his longevity to clean living and a positive attitude. 他将自己的长寿归功于干净的生活和积极的态度。
近义词:wholesome, healthy, temperate, abstemious
反义词:dissolute, debauched, intemperate
词性:adjective

clean:/kliːn/
adjective/verb/adverb
干净的/打扫/干净地
Free from dirt, marks, or stains./Make something clean./Completely, entirely.
The room is clean. 房间很干净。
I need to clean the kitchen. 我需要打扫厨房。
I clean forgot about the meeting. 我完全忘记了会议。
近义词:(adj.)pure, spotless, immaculate; (v.)tidy, wash, scrub
反义词:(adj.)dirty, filthy, soiled
词性:adjective, verb, adverb

living:/ˈlɪvɪŋ/
adjective/noun
活着的/生活
Alive now./The pursuit of a lifestyle.
He is one of the greatest living artists. 他是仍然在世的伟大的艺术家之一。
They make a living by selling handmade crafts. 他们靠卖手工艺品为生。
近义词:(adj.)alive, existing; (n.)lifestyle, livelihood
反义词:(adj.)dead, deceased
词性:adjective, noun


词源:  "clean" comes from Old English "clæne," meaning "pure, free from dirt." "Living" comes from Old English "libban," meaning "to live."  The hyphenated form "clean-living" emerged later to describe a lifestyle characterized by moral purity and healthy habits.
