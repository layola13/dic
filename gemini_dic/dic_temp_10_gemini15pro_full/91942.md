hypermetropia:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɛtroʊpiə/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈmɛtrəʊpiə/ (UK)| n. |远视|A condition of the eye in which parallel rays of light are brought to a focus behind the retina, resulting in a blurred image for nearby objects.  It occurs when the eyeball is too short or the refractive power of the eye is too weak.|She was diagnosed with hypermetropia and now wears glasses.|Nearsightedness (myopia)|farsightedness|noun

hypermetropic:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɛtrɒpɪk/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈmɛtrɒpɪk/ (UK)| adj. |远视的|Affected with or showing hypermetropia; farsighted.|He has hypermetropic vision and needs reading glasses.|Nearsighted (myopic)|farsighted|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ὑπέρ (hupér, “over, above”) + μέτρον (métron, “measure”) + ὤψ (ōps, “eye, face”) + -ia.
