mobbed:/mɑːbd/ /mɒbd/| v. adj.|围攻，蜂拥而至，被包围|To surround (someone) in a crowd, especially to express enthusiasm, hostility, or curiosity. Also used as an adjective to describe being overwhelmed with work or requests.|例句：The singer was mobbed by fans as he left the concert hall. 歌手离开音乐厅时被歌迷围住了。

例句：After the game, the winning team was mobbed by their excited fans.  比赛结束后，获胜的队伍被兴奋的球迷围住了。


|近义词：swarmed, besieged, thronged, overwhelmed|反义词:ignored, avoided|词性:verb, adjective

mobbed:/mɑːbd/ /mɒbd/| adj.|挤满人的，非常忙碌的|Overwhelmed with work or requests; extremely busy.|例句：I'm absolutely mobbed at work today. 我今天工作忙得不可开交。

例句：The restaurant was mobbed on Friday night.  这家餐馆星期五晚上挤满了人。


|近义词：swamped, inundated, overwhelmed|反义词:idle, quiet|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the noun "mob" + "-ed."  "Mob" originates from the Latin "mobile vulgus," meaning "fickle crowd."  The verb form emerged in the late 18th century.
