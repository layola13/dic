mortling:/ˈmɔːtlɪŋ/| n.| 雏鸟，幼鸟 (尤指鸭、鹅等水禽的)|A young bird, especially of a waterfowl such as a duck or goose.|The mother duck led her brood of mortlings to the water.| 近义词: chick, duckling, gosling | 反义词: adult bird | 词性: noun

词源: From Middle English *mortlyng, from mort + -ling.  "Mort" refers to a young salmon or a young animal of various species.  The suffix "-ling" denotes a diminutive or youthful state.
