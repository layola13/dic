interclasped:/ˌɪntərˈklɑːspt/| adj.|互相紧握的，扣在一起的|Two figures stood in the shadows, their hands interclasped.  Their fingers were so tightly interclasped that it was difficult to separate them.|例句：The lovers sat on the bench, their hands interclasped.  恋人坐在长椅上，双手紧握。|近义词：clasped, intertwined, interlocked|反义词:un clasped, separated|词性:形容词
Etymology: From inter- + clasped, past participle of clasp.  "Inter-" is a prefix meaning "between," "among," or "together." "Clasp" comes from Old English "clasp" meaning "fastening," "buckle," or "brooch."
