contrast:/ˈkɑːntræst/ (US), /ˈkɒntrɑːst/ (UK)| n. v. adj.|对比，对照；差异，差别|a difference between two or more people or things that is clearly seen when they are compared or put close together|The contrast between the two brothers is striking. 这两兄弟之间的反差很明显。|近义词：difference, distinction, disparity|反义词：similarity, resemblance|noun

contrast:/ˈkɑːntræst/ (US), /ˈkɒntrɑːst/ (UK)| v. |对比，对照；使形成对比|to compare two people or things in order to show the differences between them|The report contrasts the benefits of organic and non-organic farming. 这份报告比较了有机农业和非有机农业的益处。|近义词：compare, juxtapose|反义词：harmonize, blend|verb

contrast:/ˈkɑːntræst/ (US), /ˈkɒntrɑːst/ (UK)| adj.|对比的，对照的|contrasting colors or patterns are very different from each other|She wore a dress with contrasting stripes. 她穿了一件带有对比色条纹的连衣裙。|近义词：different, dissimilar|反义词：similar, alike|adjective
