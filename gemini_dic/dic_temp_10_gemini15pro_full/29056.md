carucate:/ˈkærʊkeɪt/| n.|卡鲁卡特（旧时英国土地面积单位，约120英亩）|A carucate was a unit of land area used in medieval England, equivalent to the amount of land a team of eight oxen could plough in a year, roughly 120 acres.|The Domesday Book frequently mentions carucates as a measure of land holdings.  末日审判书经常提到卡鲁卡特作为土地持有量的衡量标准。

The lord of the manor held several carucates of land.  庄园主拥有几卡鲁卡特的土地。|近义词：hide (another old English land measure), ploughland|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps a smallholding or a plot)|词性:noun
