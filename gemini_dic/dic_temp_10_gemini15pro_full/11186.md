archimedes: /ˌɑːrkɪˈmiːdiːz/ | n. | 阿基米德 | A Greek mathematician, physicist, engineer, inventor, and astronomer.  Considered one of the greatest mathematicians of all time, he is known for his work on levers, buoyancy, and the approximation of pi. | Archimedes famously exclaimed "Eureka!" upon discovering the principle of buoyancy. 阿基米德在发现浮力原理时，兴奋地喊出了“尤里卡！” | synonyms:  mathematician, scientist, inventor | antonyms: N/A | noun
