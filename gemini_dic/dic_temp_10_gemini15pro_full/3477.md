aftercast:/ˈæftərˌkæst/
n.|后续预测，事后评估|A forecast made after an event has occurred, often based on analysis of its outcome.  It's a retrospective analysis and prediction. |The aftercast showed that the hurricane was more powerful than initially predicted.|近义词: post-mortem, review, analysis|反义词: forecast, prediction|词性:名词

Etymology:
after (adv., prep.) Old English æfter "following in place or time," from Proto-Germanic *after "after, behind," from PIE root *apo- "off, away."
cast (v.) Old Norse kasta "to throw," from Proto-Germanic *kastjanan "to throw."
