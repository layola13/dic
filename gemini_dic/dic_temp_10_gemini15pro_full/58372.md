dualist:/ˈduːəlɪst/| n. |二元论者|A person who believes in dualism, the theory that two distinct principles or forces govern the universe (e.g., good and evil, mind and matter).|Descartes was a dualist, believing in the separate existence of mind and body. 笛卡尔是一个二元论者，相信心灵和身体的独立存在。|

She explored the concept of free will from a dualist perspective. 她从二元论的角度探讨了自由意志的概念。


|近义词：dichotomist|反义词:monist|noun|
