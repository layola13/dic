blameworthiness:/ˈbleɪmwɜːrðɪnɪs/ |n.|应受责备；该受谴责；罪过|The blameworthiness of the defendant's actions was debated in court. 被告行为的应受谴责程度在法庭上进行了辩论。|English Explanation: The quality of deserving blame; culpability.|Example: The judge considered the defendant's blameworthiness before passing sentence. 法官在判刑前考虑了被告的罪责。|Synonyms: culpability, guilt, responsibility, liability|Antonyms: innocence, blamelessness, merit|Etymology:  From blameworthy + -ness. Blameworthy is from blame + -worthy. Blame comes from Old French "blasmer" (to blame), ultimately from Late Latin "blasphemare" (to blaspheme).|

blameworthiness:/ˈbleɪmwɜːrðɪnɪs/ |n.|可责性；罪责|The jury had to decide on the degree of blameworthiness. 陪审团必须决定罪责的程度。|English Explanation: The degree to which someone deserves blame; the extent of their culpability.|Example:  The blameworthiness of the driver was clear after the police investigation. 警方调查后，司机的罪责显而易见。|Synonyms:  guiltiness, censurability|Antonyms:  exculpability, merit|
