conceit:/kənˈsiːt/| n.|自负，骄傲；巧妙的比喻|An excessive pride in oneself or one's abilities.  A fanciful expression in writing or speech; an elaborate metaphor.|He was puffed up with conceit. 他趾高气扬。|Synonyms: vanity, arrogance, pride, egotism|Antonyms: humility, modesty|noun

conceit:/kənˈsiːt/| v.|幻想，认为|To form a flattering opinion of; to believe something about oneself (usually followed by *oneself* or *oneself to be*).|He conceited himself to be a genius. 他自以为是天才。|Synonyms: fancy, imagine, believe|Antonyms: doubt, disbelieve|verb
