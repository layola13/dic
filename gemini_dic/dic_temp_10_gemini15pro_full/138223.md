nosinesses:/ˈnoʊzinəsɪz/| n. |爱管闲事，多嘴，八卦|The state of being nosy; excessive curiosity or inquisitiveness in other people's affairs.|Her nosiness made her unpopular with her neighbors. 她的爱管闲事让她在邻居中不受欢迎。|Nosinesses about the new couple next door spread quickly. 关于隔壁新婚夫妇的八卦迅速传播开来。|Synonyms: inquisitiveness, curiosity, prying, meddling|Antonyms: indifference, apathy, unconcern|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From nosy + -ness.  "Nosy" originates from "nose," suggesting the image of sticking one's nose into other people's business.
