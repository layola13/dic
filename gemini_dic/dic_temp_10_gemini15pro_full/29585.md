catechise:/ˈkætɪkaɪz/ (UK) /ˈkætəˌkaɪz/ (US)| v. |教导；盘问；以问答方式教授|To instruct by question and answer; to question closely or examine searchingly.|The teacher catechised the children about the story they had just read. 老师就孩子们刚读过的故事对他们进行了提问。|Synonyms: interrogate, question, quiz, examine, grill, pump|Antonyms: answer, respond, reply|Verb

catechism:/ˈkætɪkɪzəm/ (UK) /ˈkætəˌkɪzəm/ (US)| n. |教义问答；问答式教学；一连串的提问|A summary of the principles of Christian religion in the form of questions and answers, used for instruction; a series of questions.|The children were learning the catechism in preparation for their confirmation. 孩子们正在学习教义问答，为他们的坚信礼做准备。|Synonyms: instruction, doctrine, dogma, creed, teaching|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "heresy" in a religious context)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin catechizare, from Greek katēkhein, from kata- 'down' + ēkhein 'to sound'.
