cancel:/ˈkænsəl/ (US) /ˈkænsl/ (UK)| v. n.|取消，撤销; 作废，注销|To stop something that has already been arranged from happening; to decide that an order for goods or services will not be carried out.|例句：I had to cancel my trip because I got sick. 我不得不取消我的旅行，因为我生病了。|近义词：abort, call off, revoke, annul, rescind|反义词：approve, confirm, ratify|词性：动词/名词

cancel:/ˈkænsəl/ (US) /ˈkænsl/ (UK)| n.|取消，撤销|The act of cancelling something.|例句：The cancellation of the flight caused a lot of inconvenience. 航班取消造成很多不便。|近义词：abolition, revocation|反义词：approval, confirmation|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *canceller, from Late Latin cancellare "to cross out with lines, delete, make void," from Latin cancelli "lattice work," from cancer "crab, lattice."  The image is of drawing crossed lines like a lattice over writing to delete it.
