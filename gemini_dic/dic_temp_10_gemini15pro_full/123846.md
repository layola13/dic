moralists:/ˈmɔːrəlɪsts/ /ˈmɔːrələsts/| n.|道德家，卫道士|A person who teaches or promotes morality, often in a self-righteous or preachy way.  A person who adheres to strict moral principles or is concerned with moral behavior.|The moralists of the Victorian era condemned many forms of entertainment.|近义词:preacher, puritan, moralizer|反义词:hedonist, libertine|Noun
