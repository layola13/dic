paronomasy:/ˌpærəˈnɒməsi/ /ˌpærəˈnɑːməsi/ | n. |谐音双关，双关语|A rhetorical figure involving a play on words with similar sounds but different meanings.|Example:  "The king is dead; long live the king!" (The similar sounds of "dead" and "live" create a paronomasy, emphasizing the continuity of the monarchy despite the change in rulers.)

Example: "Atheism is a non-prophet institution." (This uses the similar sounds of "prophet" and "profit" to make a humorous point about atheism.) |近义词：pun, wordplay, paranomasia |反义词:  (No direct antonym, but one might consider "literal language")|词性:noun
