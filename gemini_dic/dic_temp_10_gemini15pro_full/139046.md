oarlike:/ˈɔːrlaɪk/| adj.|像桨的，桨状的|Resembling an oar in shape or function.  Having a flat, elongated shape suitable for rowing.|The duck's feet were oarlike, propelling it easily through the water. 鸭子的脚像桨一样，很容易推动它在水中前进。|similar: paddle-like, flat, elongated|opposite: narrow, pointed|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From oar + -like.  "Oar" comes from Old English *ār, from Proto-Germanic *ōrōm, from Proto-Indo-European *erh₁- (“to row, to paddle”).  "-like" is a common suffix used to form adjectives indicating resemblance.
