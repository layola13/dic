indias:/ˈɪndiəz/| n. |印度（通常指英属印度）|India, often specifically referring to British India.  It is an archaic plural form, rarely used in modern English. |In the days of the British Raj, people often referred to the indias.| 近义词:India, British India (historically)|反义词: N/A |名词
Etymology-> From Latin India, from Greek India, ultimately from Sanskrit Sindhu, meaning "river," specifically the Indus River. The plural form "Indias" arose during the British colonial period, likely reflecting the diverse regions and administrative divisions within British India.
