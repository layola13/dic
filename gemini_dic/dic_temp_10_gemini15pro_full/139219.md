Ojibway /oʊˈdʒɪbweɪ/ | n. | 奥吉布瓦人；奥吉布瓦语 | A member of a Native American people inhabiting the region around Lake Huron and Lake Superior.  The Algonquian language of the Ojibwa people. | Example: The Ojibway have a rich tradition of storytelling. 奥吉布瓦人有着丰富的讲故事的传统。 | Example:  He is learning to speak Ojibway. 他正在学习说奥吉布瓦语。 | Synonym: Chippewa, Anishinaabe | Antonym: N/A | Noun

Ojibways /oʊˈdʒɪbweɪz/ | n. | 奥吉布瓦人（复数） | Plural form of Ojibway, referring to multiple members of the Ojibway people. | Example: The Ojibways gathered for their annual powwow. 奥吉布瓦人聚集在一起参加他们一年一度的帕瓦仪式。| Example: Many Ojibways still live in their traditional territories. 许多奥吉布瓦人仍然生活在他们的传统领地上。 | Synonym: Chippewas, Anishinaabeg | Antonym: N/A | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ojibwe /oːdʒɪbˈweː/, meaning "to roast until puckered up," referring to their moccasin-making technique.
