fainter:/ˈfeɪntər/ (US), /ˈfeɪntə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更微弱的，更模糊的，更昏厥的|Comparative form of faint: more faint.  Less clearly visible, audible, distinct, or perceptible; weaker. Also, closer to losing consciousness.|例句：The sound grew fainter as the car drove away. 汽车开走后，声音越来越微弱了。

例句：The light from the distant star was fainter than expected. 远处恒星的光比预期的要暗淡。

近义词：dimmer, weaker, paler, slighter, less distinct|反义词：brighter, stronger, clearer, more distinct|词性:形容词

fainter:/ˈfeɪntər/ (US), /ˈfeɪntə(r)/ (UK)| n.|昏厥的人，快要昏厥的人|One who faints or is about to faint.|例句：The nurses attended to the fainter. 护士们照顾那个快要昏厥的人。

例句：He felt like a fainter in the sweltering heat. 在酷热中，他感觉自己快要昏厥了。

近义词：swooner|反义词: N/A|词性:名词


词源：From Middle English *feinter, *feyntere, from Old French fainte (“feigned, pretended”), feminine of faint (“feigned, pretended, cowardly”), from feindre (“to feign, pretend”), from Latin fingō (“I shape, form, fashion, mold; I devise, contrive, invent, feign”).  The comparative sense developed later.
