incriminating: /ɪnˈkrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 归罪的，牵连的，显示有罪的 | Making someone appear guilty of a crime. |  The police found incriminating evidence in his car. 警方在他的车里发现了显示他有罪的证据。 |  The discovery of the incriminating emails led to his resignation. 发现有罪的电子邮件导致他辞职。 |  implicating, accusatory, compromising |  exculpatory, exonerating | adjective

incriminate: /ɪnˈkrɪmɪneɪt/ | vt. |  控告，牵连，显示…有罪 | To make someone appear guilty of a crime. | He refused to answer the questions, fearing he might incriminate himself.  他拒绝回答问题，因为他害怕这会让他自己显得有罪。| The witness’s testimony incriminated the defendant. 目击者的证词牵连了被告。| accuse, charge, implicate | exonerate, acquit | verb
