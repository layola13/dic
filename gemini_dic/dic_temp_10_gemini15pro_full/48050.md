declaim:/dɪˈkleɪm/ | v. |慷慨激昂地演说；朗诵；高谈阔论|to speak rhetorically; to make a formal speech in a passionate or theatrical way|He declaimed against the evils of war. 他慷慨激昂地谴责战争的罪恶。|近义词：orate, pronounce, recite, rant |反义词: whisper, murmur |词性:动词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "declamare" (to cry out, speak loudly, declaim), from "de-" (from, away) + "clamare" (to cry out, shout).
