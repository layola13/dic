cheekbone:/ˈtʃiːkbəʊn/ (UK) /ˈtʃiːkˌboʊn/ (US)| n. |颧骨|The bone that forms the prominence of the cheek.|She has high cheekbones, which give her face a striking appearance. 她颧骨很高，使她的脸看起来很醒目。|Synonyms: zygomatic bone, malar bone|Antonyms: None|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cēakbān (attested only as cēakbana "cheekbone, jawbone"), from cēace "cheek, jaw" + bān "bone".  The word "cheek" ultimately derives from Proto-Germanic *kekwō and "bone" from Proto-Germanic *bainam.
