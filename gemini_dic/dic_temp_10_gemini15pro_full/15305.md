backplane:/ˈbækpleɪn/| n.|底板；背板|A backplane is a printed circuit board that connects several connectors in parallel with each other, so that each pin of each connector is connected to the same relative pin on all the other connectors. It is used as a backbone to connect several printed circuit boards together to make up a complete computer system.|The server's backplane allows for hot-swapping of hard drives.|Synonyms: motherboard, baseboard|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps component)|Noun
