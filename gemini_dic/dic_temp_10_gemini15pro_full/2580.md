admonishes: /ədˈmɑːnɪʃɪz/
verb
劝诫，警告，责备
to warn or reprimand someone firmly.  To advise or urge earnestly.
The teacher admonished the students for being late. = 老师责备学生迟到。

The doctor admonished him to quit smoking. = 医生劝告他戒烟。

近义词: reprimand, rebuke, scold, warn, caution, advise, counsel
反义词: praise, commend, encourage
词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin admonēre, from Latin ad- + monēre "to warn."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
She admonishes her children to be polite. = 她劝诫她的孩子们要有礼貌。
He admonishes his employees to work harder. = 他告诫他的员工要更加努力工作。


一般过去时:
The coach admonished the team for their poor performance. = 教练因队伍表现不佳而责备他们。
My mother admonished me for not cleaning my room. = 我妈妈因为我没有打扫房间而责备我。


现在完成时:
The principal has admonished the students several times about their behavior. = 校长已经多次就学生们的行为提出警告。
I have admonished him not to repeat the mistake. = 我已经警告过他不要再犯同样的错误。


过去完成时:
He had admonished her about the dangers before she left. = 在她离开之前，他已经警告过她其中的危险。
The teacher had admonished the class about cheating before the exam. = 老师在考试前已经警告过全班不要作弊。


将来时:
I will admonish him if he does that again. = 如果他再那样做，我会警告他。
She will admonish them about the consequences of their actions. = 她会警告他们其行为的后果。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would admonish him privately. = 如果我是你，我会私下劝诫他。
I wish I had admonished her sooner. = 我希望我早点警告她。


被动语态:
He was admonished by his boss for his carelessness. = 他因为粗心而被老板责备。
The students were admonished for talking during the assembly. = 学生们因为在集会期间讲话而受到警告。


疑问句:
Why did she admonish him? = 她为什么警告他？
Has he been admonished before? = 他以前被警告过吗？


否定句:
I didn't admonish him, I praised him. = 我没有警告他，我表扬了他。
She hasn't admonished them yet. = 她还没有警告他们。



PHRASE->
admonish someone against something = 劝诫某人不要做某事
admonish someone to do something = 劝诫某人去做某事
admonish someone for something = 因某事而责备某人
sternly admonish = 严厉地警告
gently admonish =  温和地劝诫
