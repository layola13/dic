hoodwinking:/hʊdˈwɪŋkɪŋ/
n.|欺骗，蒙蔽|The act of deceiving or tricking someone.
|He made his fortune by hoodwinking investors. 他通过欺骗投资者发了财。
|近义词: deceiving, tricking, duping, swindling, defrauding|反义词: enlightening, informing, being honest|词性: Noun (Gerund or present participle)

hoodwink:/hʊdˈwɪŋk/
vt.|欺骗，蒙蔽|To deceive or trick someone.
|The magician hoodwinked the audience with his sleight of hand. 魔术师以巧妙的手法欺骗了观众。
|近义词: deceive, trick, dupe, swindle, defraud|反义词: enlighten, inform, be honest|词性: Verb


词源:
From Middle English *hodwynke*, from *hod* (“hood”) + *wynke* (“to close one's eyes, wink”), thus originally meaning “to cover someone's eyes with a hood.”
