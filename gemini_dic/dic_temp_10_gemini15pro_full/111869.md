logjamming:/ˈlɒɡdʒæmɪŋ/ /ˈlɑːɡdʒæmɪŋ/
noun
阻塞；僵局
The deliberate blocking of logs in a river, typically as a form of protest.  Also used metaphorically to describe any blockage or deadlock.
Environmentalists resorted to logjamming to protest the logging of old-growth forests.  环保主义者采取河流阻塞木材的方式来抗议砍伐原始森林。
blockade, obstruction, deadlock, stalemate
flow, progress, advancement
n.
