debilitants:/dɪˈbɪlɪtənts/| n.| 衰弱剂，虚弱因素 |Substances or factors that weaken or impair strength, vitality, or ability. | The debilitating effects of the illness kept him bedridden for weeks.  这种疾病使他身体虚弱，卧床数周。|Synonyms: weakeners, enervants, impairing agents| Antonyms: tonics, invigorants, strengtheners | noun

debilitating:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪŋ/| adj.|使人衰弱的，使人虚弱的 |Making someone or something physically weak. |  The heat was debilitating, making it hard to concentrate.  酷热让人虚弱，难以集中注意力。|Synonyms: weakening, enervating, sapping| Antonyms: invigorating, strengthening, fortifying | adjective

词源:  From the Latin word "debilis" meaning "weak," and the English suffix "-ant" which forms nouns and adjectives referring to agents or causing actions.
