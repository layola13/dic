deconstructions:/ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkʃənz/
|n.|解构，拆解|The process of analyzing something (e.g., a text, an idea, or a social structure) by examining its underlying assumptions and exposing its inherent contradictions and instabilities.|Literary criticism is full of deconstructions of classic texts. 文学批评充满了对经典文本的解构。|synonyms: analyses, critiques, examinations|antonyms: constructions, syntheses, establishments|noun
