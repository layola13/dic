ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *does, *dooth, from Old English dēþ, third-person singular present indicative of dōn (“to do”). Cognate with Scots daes, does (“does”), Saterland Frisian deet (“does”), West Frisian docht (“does”), Dutch doet (“does”), Low German deiht, deit (“does”), German tut (“does”), Danish gør (“does”), Swedish gör (“does”), Icelandic gerir (“does”).
