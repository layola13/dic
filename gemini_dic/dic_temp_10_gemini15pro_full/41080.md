coppers:/ˈkɒpəz (UK), ˈkɑːpərz (US)| n.|铜币；警察|Small denomination coins, typically made of copper or a copper alloy. Also slang for police officers.|例句：He had a few coppers in his pocket. 他的口袋里有一些铜币。

例句：The coppers were on the scene quickly. 警察很快就赶到了现场。|近义词：police, cops (slang), pennies, change|反义词: (For coins) banknotes, bills|词性:名词

copper:/ˈkɒpə(r) (UK), ˈkɑːpər (US)| n. adj.|铜；铜币；铜色的|A reddish-brown metallic element, used in plumbing and electrical wiring. Also, a small denomination coin traditionally made of copper or a copper alloy. As an adjective, it describes something having the color of copper.|例句：Copper is a good conductor of electricity. 铜是良好的电导体。

例句：The roof was made of copper and had turned green with age. 屋顶是用铜做的，年代久远已经变成了绿色。|近义词：(n.) metal, bronze, brass; (adj.) bronze, reddish-brown|反义词: (n.)  (No direct antonym for the element); (adj.)  (Various color opposites, e.g., blue, green)|词性:名词，形容词
