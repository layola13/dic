climacteric:/klaɪˈmæk.tər.ɪk/
adjective
更年期的，转折点的
Relating to the menopause or marked by a change of life; relating to a critical period or turning point.
例句：She experienced some climacteric symptoms, such as hot flashes. 她经历了一些更年期的症状，例如潮热。

例句：The company is at a climacteric stage in its development. 该公司正处于发展的转折点。
synonym: menopausal, critical, crucial
antonym: unimportant, insignificant
词性: adjective


climacter:/ˈklaɪˌmæk.tər/
noun
更年期，转折点
A period of life characterized by physiological and psychological change, especially in women; a critical period or turning point.
例句：Women experience many hormonal changes during the climacter. 女性在更年期会经历许多荷尔蒙变化。

例句：The nation is at a political climacter.  这个国家正处于政治转折点。
synonym: menopause, turning point, crisis
antonym: stability, normalcy
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin climactericus "critical point in human life," from Greek klimaktēr "rung of a ladder," from klinein "to lean, slope."  Originally referred to critical periods in life supposedly determined by the positions of the stars.
