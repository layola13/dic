hesychast:/ˈhɛsɪkæst/| n.|静修者，寂静主义者|A person who practices hesychasm, a mystical tradition of contemplative prayer in the Eastern Orthodox Church.  It involves focusing the mind on a short prayer and seeking inner stillness to experience the divine light.|例句：The hesychast retreated to his isolated cell for prayer and contemplation.  这位静修者退到他僻静的斗室进行祈祷和沉思。

例句：Hesychasm played a significant role in the spiritual development of Eastern Orthodoxy.  寂静主义在东正教的精神发展中扮演了重要的角色。|近义词：mystic, contemplative, ascetic|反义词：materialist, worldling|词性:noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *hesychastes, from Ancient Greek ἡσυχαστής (hēsychastēs, “quiet, still”), from ἡσυχάζω (hēsycházō, “I rest, keep quiet”), from ἡσύχιος (hēsychios, “quiet, still”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ē- (“to sit”).
