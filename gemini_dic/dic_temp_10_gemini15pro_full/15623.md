bailpiece:/ˈbeɪlˌpiːs/| n.|保释单，保释状|A document or certificate proving that bail has been paid and acknowledging the obligation to appear in court.|The lawyer presented the bailpiece to secure his client's release.|Synonyms: bail bond, recognizance|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider:  arrest warrant, detention order)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From bail (security given for release from custody) + piece (a document).  "Bail" ultimately comes from Old French "baillier" meaning "to deliver, to control."
