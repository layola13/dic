furloughing:/ˈfɝːloʊɪŋ/ (US) /ˈfɜːləʊɪŋ/ (UK)
n.|休假，暂时解雇|The act of placing an employee on furlough.  A period of unpaid leave from work, typically for a specific period of time, often due to economic reasons.|The company is furloughing 100 employees due to the economic downturn.  由于经济低迷，公司将暂时解雇100名员工。|layoff, temporary unemployment|employment, hiring
词性: 名词 (derived from the verb "furlough")


furlough:/ˈfɝːloʊ/ (US) /ˈfɜːləʊ/ (UK)
n.|休假，暂时解雇|A period of unpaid leave from work, typically for a specific period of time, often due to economic reasons.|He took a furlough from his job to travel. 他休假去旅行了。|leave of absence, layoff|employment, hiring
词性: 名词

furlough:/ˈfɝːloʊ/ (US) /ˈfɜːləʊ/ (UK)
v.|准许休假，暂时解雇|To grant a period of unpaid leave from work.|The company furloughed its employees for three months. 公司让员工休假三个月。|lay off, temporarily dismiss|hire, employ
词性: 动词
词源: From the Dutch "verlof," meaning "permission" or "leave."  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*farlaubjaną," meaning "to allow to go."
