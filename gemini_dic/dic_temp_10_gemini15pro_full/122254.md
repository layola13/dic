mitering:/ˈmaɪtərɪŋ/| n.|斜接，斜切|The act of joining two pieces of material at an angle, typically 45 degrees, to form a corner.|He perfected the art of mitering crown molding. 他精通了斜接顶角线工艺。|近义词:beveling, joining|反义词:butt joint|noun

mitre:/ˈmaɪtər/| n. v.|斜接, 斜接接头;主教冠, 斜切|1.(n.) A joint made by beveling two surfaces to be joined, usually at a 45-degree angle. 2.(v.) To join two pieces of material by mitering.  3.(n.) A tall, pointed headdress worn by bishops. |1. The mitre on the picture frame was perfectly aligned. 画框的斜接接头完美对齐。 2. He mitred the two pieces of wood to create a seamless corner. 他斜切两块木头，创造了一个无缝的角落。 3. The bishop wore his mitre during the ceremony. 主教在仪式上戴着主教冠。 |近义词:(n. v.) bevel, join (n.) headdress|反义词:butt joint|noun, verb


词源: From Middle English *mitre, from Old French mitre, from Late Latin mitra, from Ancient Greek μίτρα (mítra, “headband, turban”), of uncertain origin.
