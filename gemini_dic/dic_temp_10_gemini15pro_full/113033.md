illuminate:/ɪˈluːmɪneɪt/ |v.|照亮；阐明；启发|to light up something; to make something clearer or easier to understand|The streets were illuminated with colourful lights. 街道上灯火通明，五彩缤纷。|synonyms: light up, brighten, clarify, elucidate|antonyms: darken, obscure|verb

illuminate:/ɪˈluːmɪneɪt/ |adj.(rare, literary)|照亮的；光明的|lit up; bright|The manuscript was illuminated with gold leaf.  这份手稿用金箔装饰得很光亮。|synonyms: bright, shining, radiant|antonyms: dark, dim|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin illuminatus, past participle of illuminare "to light up, enlighten," from Latin in- "in" + luminare "to light up," from lumen (genitive luminis) "light."
