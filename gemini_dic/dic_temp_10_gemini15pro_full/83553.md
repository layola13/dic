haemocyanin:/ˌhiːməˈsaɪənɪn/ | n. |血蓝蛋白|A type of respiratory pigment found in the blood of some invertebrates, notably mollusks and arthropods. It contains copper and appears blue when oxygenated.|例句：Haemocyanin is less efficient at carrying oxygen than haemoglobin. 血蓝蛋白携带氧气的效率低于血红蛋白。

例句：The horseshoe crab's blood contains haemocyanin, giving it a blue color.  鲎的血液中含有血蓝蛋白，使其呈现蓝色。|近义词：respiratory pigment|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek αἷμα (haîma, “blood”) + κυανός (kuanós, “dark blue”).
