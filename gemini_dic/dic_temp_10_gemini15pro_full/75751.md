fulmar:/ˈfʊlmɑːr/ (US), /ˈfʊlmə(r)/ (UK)| n. |管鼻鹱|A seabird of northern oceans, related to the petrels, with a stout body, long narrow wings, and a tube-like nostril on top of the bill.|The fulmar circled the fishing boat, hoping for scraps.|近义词：petrel, shearwater|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

fulmar: /ˈfʊlmɑːr/ (US), /ˈfʊlmə(r)/ (UK)| n. |管鼻鹱|From Old Norse *fúlmárr, from fúll "foul" + márr "gull".
