chatelaine:/ˈʃætəleɪn/ | n. | 女管家；（旧时挂在腰带上的）钥匙链；锁扣 | A woman in charge of a large house and its servants; a set of short chains attached to a woman's belt, used for carrying keys or small items. |  The chatelaine directed the servants with a firm hand.  女管家用坚定的手段指挥仆人。| synonyms: housekeeper, matron, key chain, key holder | antonyms: servant, maid, employee | noun

chatelaine:/ˈʃætəleɪn/ | adj. | 女管家的；像女管家的 |  Resembling or characteristic of a chatelaine. | She had a chatelaine air about her, always organized and efficient. 她身上有一种女管家的气质，总是井井有条，效率很高。 | synonyms: housewifely, matronly | antonyms: disorganized, inefficient | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *chastelaine*, from Medieval Latin *castellana*, feminine of *castellanus*, "castellan," from *castellum*, "castle" ultimately from Latin *castrum*, "fort."
