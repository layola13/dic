bodeful: /ˈboʊdfəl/ (US) /ˈbəʊdfʊl/ (UK)| adj. |预兆的；凶兆的；不祥的|Predictive of something bad; ominous.  Suggesting that something unpleasant is likely to happen.|The dark clouds were bodeful of a coming storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨即将来临。|The raven's croak was a bodeful sound. 渡鸦的叫声是不祥之兆。|近义词：ominous, portentous, inauspicious|反义词：auspicious, promising, propitious|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From bode (verb) + -ful (suffix).  Bode comes from Old English *bodian "to proclaim, announce, foretell," related to Old Norse boda "to announce." The suffix -ful forms adjectives meaning "full of" or "characterized by."
