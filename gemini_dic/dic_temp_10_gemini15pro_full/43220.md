crackdown:/ˈkrækdaʊn/| n.|压制，取缔，严厉打击|A sudden increase in official activity to stop or control something that is considered wrong or harmful.|The government launched a crackdown on illegal gambling. 政府对非法赌博展开严厉打击。|近义词：suppression, clampdown, repression|反义词:leniency, permissiveness|词性:名词
Etymology: From the verb phrase "to crack down," meaning to take severe measures against something. "Crack" implies a sharp, decisive action, and "down" emphasizes the forceful nature of the action.
