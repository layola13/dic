overpolish:/ˌoʊvərˈpɑːlɪʃ/ |v. |过度抛光，过分修饰|To polish something excessively, often to the point of detracting from its natural beauty or character.|He overpolished the car, and now it looks artificial. 他把车擦得太过头了，现在看起来很假。|The writer overpolished his prose, losing its original freshness.  这位作家过度润色了他的散文，失去了原有的清新感。|synonyms: over-refine, overdo, overwork|antonyms: understate, neglect, roughen|verb

overpolish:/ˌoʊvərˈpɑːlɪʃ/ |adj. |过度抛光的，过分修饰的|Excessively polished, often to the point of appearing artificial or losing its natural character.|The overpolished floors were slippery. 过度抛光的地板很滑。|Her overpolished presentation lacked sincerity. 她过于雕琢的演讲缺乏诚意。|synonyms: over-refined, overdone, overworked|antonyms: understated, natural, rough|adjective
