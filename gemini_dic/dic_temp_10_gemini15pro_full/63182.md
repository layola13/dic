entangle:/ɪnˈtæŋɡl/ | v. | 纠缠，缠绕；使…卷入，使…陷入 | To cause something to become twisted together with something else in a complicated way; to involve someone in a difficult situation. | The fishing line became entangled in the seaweed. 鱼线缠绕在了海藻里。|  近义词: tangle, snarl, involve, implicate | 反义词: disentangle, extricate | 词性: 动词

entanglement:/ɪnˈtæŋɡlmənt/ | n. | 纠缠，缠绕；牵连，卷入 | The state of being entangled; a complicated or compromising relationship or situation. |  The deer struggled to free itself from the entanglement of branches. 鹿挣扎着想摆脱树枝的缠绕。 | 近义词: tangle, complication, involvement | 反义词: disentanglement, order | 词性: 名词

词源：
From en- + tangle.  "Tangle" comes from the Middle English "tanglen," from a Scandinavian source akin to Old Norse "þongull" (seaweed).  The prefix "en-" adds the sense of intensifying or causing the action of the verb.
