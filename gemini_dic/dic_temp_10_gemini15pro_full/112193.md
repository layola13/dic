loonies:/ˈluːniz/| n.|疯子；怪人；(尤指)加元|A slang term for Canadian one-dollar coins, so called because of the image of a common loon on them.  Also used informally to refer to people perceived as eccentric or crazy.|例句：He paid for the coffee with a handful of loonies. 他用一把硬币付了咖啡钱。

例句：They think I'm a bit of a loonie for collecting rubber ducks.  他们觉得我收集橡皮鸭子有点疯疯癫癫的。|近义词：lunatics, crazies, nuts, eccentrics|反义词:sane people, rational people|词性:noun (plural)


loonie:/ˈluːni/| n.|疯子；怪人；(尤指)加元|A slang term for the Canadian one-dollar coin, so called because of the image of a common loon on it.  Also used informally to refer to a person perceived as eccentric or crazy.|例句：Can you lend me a loonie for the parking meter? 你能借我一个加元付停车费吗？

例句：He's a bit of a loonie, but harmless. 他有点疯疯癫癫的，但没恶意。|近义词：lunatic, crazy, nut, eccentric|反义词:sane person, rational person|词性:noun (singular)


词源：From the image of a common loon (a type of aquatic bird) on the Canadian one-dollar coin.
