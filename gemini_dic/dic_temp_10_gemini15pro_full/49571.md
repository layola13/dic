dementedness:/dɪˈmɛntɪdnəs/| n. |痴呆，精神错乱|The state of being demented; a state of severe mental impairment, characterized by loss of cognitive function, especially memory, judgment, and reasoning.|His dementedness made it difficult for him to recognize his own children. 他的痴呆使他难以认出自己的孩子。|近义词：insanity, madness, dementia|反义词：sanity, lucidity|词性: noun


dementedness:/dɪˈmɛntɪdnəs/| n. |失智|A state of serious mental decline, often associated with old age and marked by memory loss and confusion.|Her dementedness progressed rapidly, and she soon required full-time care. 她的失智症进展迅速，很快就需要全天候的照顾。|近义词：senility, Alzheimer's|反义词：mental acuity, sharpness|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dēmentātus* (past participle of *dēmēntāre*, "to drive mad"), from Latin *dē* ("out of, from") + *mēns* ("mind").
