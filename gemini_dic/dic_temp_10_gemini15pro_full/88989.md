holists:/ˈhoʊlɪsts/| n.|整体论者|People who believe in holism, a philosophy that emphasizes the importance of the whole and the interdependence of its parts.|Holistic medicine treats the whole person, not just the disease. 整体医学治疗的是整个人，而不仅仅是疾病。|synonyms: wholistic thinkers, system thinkers|antonyms: reductionists, atomists|noun


holists:/ˈhoʊlɪsts/| n.|整体主义者|People who advocate for holism, a theory that parts of a whole are in intimate interconnection, such that they cannot exist independently of the whole, or cannot be understood without reference to the whole, which is thus regarded as greater than the sum of its parts.|Many holists believe that education should focus on developing the whole child. 许多整体主义者认为教育应该注重培养孩子的全面发展。|synonyms: wholistic proponents, system proponents|antonyms: reductionists, atomists|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "holism" + "-ist"
"Holism" comes from the Greek word "holos" meaning "whole, entire, complete."
"-ist" is a suffix used to form nouns denoting a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or theory.
