fast-forwarded:/ˌfæstˈfɔːrwərdɪd/
| v. |快进 | To move (a recording) forward rapidly.| I fast-forwarded through the commercials. 我快进了广告。|Synonyms: skip ahead, advance rapidly |Antonyms: rewind, play back slowly|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "fast" (meaning quick) + "forward" (meaning to move ahead) + "-ed" (past participle suffix, also used to form adjectives).  The term originates from the operation of fast-forwarding a tape or video recording.
