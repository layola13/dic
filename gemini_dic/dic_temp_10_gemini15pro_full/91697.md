hyperconjugated:/ˌhaɪpərˈkɒndʒʊɡeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 超共轭的 |  Relating to or exhibiting hyperconjugation, a stabilizing interaction that results from the interaction of the electrons in a sigma bond (usually C-H or C-C) with an adjacent empty or partially filled p-orbital or a pi orbital, to give an extended molecular orbital that increases the stability of the system. | The stability of carbocations is often explained by hyperconjugation.  碳正离子的稳定性通常用超共轭来解释。| 近义词:  resonance stabilized (somewhat) | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "unstabilized" in a specific context) | 词性: adjective

hyperconjugation:/ˌhaɪpərˈkɒndʒʊɡeɪʃən/ | n. | 超共轭 | The delocalization of electrons by the overlap of a sigma orbital with an adjacent empty or partially filled p-orbital or a pi orbital. | Hyperconjugation contributes to the stability of alkyl radicals. 超共轭作用有助于烷基自由基的稳定性. | 近义词:  no-bond resonance | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From *hyper-* (meaning "over, beyond") + *conjugation* (referring to the interaction of orbitals).  The term was introduced by Robert Mulliken.
