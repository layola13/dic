mueres:/ˈmwɛɾes/| v.|你死 (Informal, second person singular present indicative of "morir")|You die. This is an informal, second person singular form used primarily in Spain and parts of Latin America.  It translates directly to "you die" but carries a more familiar, less formal tone. |例句：Si no te cuidas, mueres. 如果你不照顾好自己，你会死的。|近义词：falleces (more formal), te mueres (reflexive, adds emphasis)|反义词:vives (you live)|词性:Verb

被动语态:
"Morir" is intransitive and does not have a passive voice.


疑问句:
¿Mueres? = Are you dying? (Informal, can be perceived as blunt or insensitive)
¿Te estás muriendo? = Are you dying? (Informal, reflexive, slightly softer)


否定句:
No mueres. = You are not dying/You don't die. (Informal, context-dependent)
No te mueras. = Don't die. (Informal, imperative, more common)

注意事项:
The word "mueres" is informal and can be considered blunt, especially when talking about death.  Use with caution and consider the context.  The reflexive form "te mueres" adds emphasis.  It's important to understand the nuances of the verb "morir" and its various conjugations in Spanish to use it appropriately.
