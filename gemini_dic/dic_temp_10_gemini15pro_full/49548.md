dematerialized: /ˌdiːməˈtɪəriəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 非物质化的，无实体的 |  Converted from physical to electronic form, or existing only in electronic form.  No longer existing in physical form but rather as electronic data or information. |  Example 1:  The company dematerialized its share certificates, making them easier to trade.  公司将其股票凭证非物质化，使其更容易交易。

Example 2: Many documents are now dematerialized and stored electronically. 许多文件现在都已非物质化并以电子方式存储。 | 近义词: digitalized, electronic, intangible, virtual | 反义词: physical, tangible, materialized | 词性: 形容词

dematerialize: /ˌdiːməˈtɪəriəlaɪz/ | v. | 非物质化，使无实体化 |  To convert from physical to electronic form, or to cause something to exist only in electronic form.  To cease to exist in physical form and become electronic data or information. | Example 1:  The bank dematerialized its stock certificates to reduce paperwork.  银行将其股票凭证非物质化以减少文书工作。

Example 2:  The rise of cloud computing has allowed many businesses to dematerialize their operations.  云计算的兴起使许多企业能够将其运营非物质化。 | 近义词: digitize, electronify | 反义词: materialize, physicalize | 词性: 动词

dematerialization: /ˌdiːməˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 非物质化 | The process of converting assets from physical to electronic form.  The act of becoming or causing something to become intangible or virtual. | Example 1: The dematerialization of stock certificates has made trading more efficient.  股票凭证的非物质化使交易更加高效。

Example 2: The dematerialization of physical media like CDs and DVDs is almost complete.  CD 和 DVD 等物理介质的非物质化几乎已经完成。 | 近义词: digitization, electronification | 反义词: materialization | 词性: 名词


词源:  "de-" (prefix meaning "removal or reversal") + "materialize" (from "material" + "-ize"). The word "material" ultimately comes from Latin "materia" (matter).
