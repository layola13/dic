overaffirm:/ˌoʊvərəˈfɜːrm/ | v. | 过分肯定；过度确认 | To affirm something excessively or beyond what is warranted.  It implies an exaggerated or insincere expression of agreement or support. | He overaffirmed his loyalty to the company, raising suspicions about his true motives. 他过分强调自己对公司的忠诚，反而让人怀疑他的真实动机。| 近义词: overstate, exaggerate, overemphasize | 反义词: understate, downplay | 词性: 动词

overaffirm:/ˌoʊvərəˈfɜːrm/ | v. | 过分肯定；过度确认 | To strongly and repeatedly state something, often to an excessive degree, suggesting a lack of genuine conviction or an attempt to compensate for underlying insecurity. | She overaffirmed her love for him, which made him feel uneasy. 她过分强调对他的爱，这让他感到不安。| 近义词: protest too much, overdo, gush | 反义词: downplay, understate | 词性: 动词
