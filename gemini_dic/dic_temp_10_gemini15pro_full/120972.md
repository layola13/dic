mischievous:/ˈmɪstʃɪvəs/| adj.|恶作剧的；淘气的；有害的|Behaving in a way, or describing behaviour, that is slightly bad but is not intended to cause serious harm or damage. |例句：That mischievous boy tied my shoelaces together. 那个淘气的男孩把我的鞋带系在一起了。

例句：She has a mischievous grin on her face. 她脸上带着恶作剧的笑容。


近义词：naughty, playful, impish, roguish|反义词:well-behaved, obedient|词性:adjective

mischiefful: /ˈmɪstʃɪvfʊl/ | adj. |充满恶作剧的; 调皮的 | (especially of children) enjoying playing tricks and causing slight trouble.  (This word is less common than mischievous.) |例句：The mischiefful child hid his mother's keys.  这个调皮的孩子藏起了妈妈的钥匙。

例句：He gave a mischiefful wink and ran off. 他调皮地眨了眨眼，然后跑掉了。


近义词：naughty, mischievous, playful|反义词:well-behaved, serious|词性:adjective


词源: From Middle English *mischevos, from mis- + chevance ("bargain, agreement, dealing"). More at mis-, cheap.
