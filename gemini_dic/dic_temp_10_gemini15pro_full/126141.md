mutineers:/ˌmjuːtɪˈnɪərz/
n.
叛变者，哗变者
A person, especially a sailor or soldier, who rebels or refuses to obey the orders of a person in authority.
The mutineers took control of the ship. 叛变者控制了这艘船。
The captain had the mutineers flogged. 船长下令鞭打叛乱者。

近义词: rebels, insurgents, traitors
反义词: loyalists, conformists
词性: noun (plural)

Etymology:
From Middle French mutin, from Old French mutin, from meute (“mutiny, riot, pack of hounds”), probably from Vulgar Latin *movita (“movement, commotion”), from Latin movēre (“to move”).
