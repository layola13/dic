civilized: /ˈsɪvɪlaɪzd/ | adj. | 文明的，有教养的 | Having a high state of culture and development both socially and technologically; polite and well-mannered. |  Ancient Greece is often considered a civilized society. 古希腊通常被认为是一个文明的社会。

She spoke in a civilized manner, even when provoked. 即使被激怒了，她说话也很有教养。 | 近义词: cultured, refined, sophisticated, polite, courteous | 反义词: uncivilized, barbaric, primitive, rude, impolite | 词性: adjective

Etymology: From the Late Middle English *civilise*, from French *civiliser*, from Latin *civilis* "of or belonging to citizens, civil," from *civis* "citizen."
