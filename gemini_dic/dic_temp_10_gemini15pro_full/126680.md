namby-pambies:/ˈnæmbi ˈpæmbiz/| n. |娘娘腔；懦弱的人；胆小鬼|A derogatory term for someone considered weak, timid, or effeminate.|He called the other boys namby-pambies because they wouldn't climb the tree.|近义词：wimp, weakling, milksop, coward|反义词: hero, brave person|Noun

Etymology->
"namby-pamby" is believed to be a satirical coinage by Henry Carey in 1725, mocking the poet Ambrose Philips's overly sentimental and childlike verse.  It's based on a childish pronunciation of "Ambrose."
