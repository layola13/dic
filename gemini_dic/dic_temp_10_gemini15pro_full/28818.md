carouse: /kəˈraʊz/ | vi. | 畅饮；狂欢 | to engage in a noisy, drunken revel; to drink large quantities of alcohol and enjoy oneself in a boisterous way | They caroused until the early hours of the morning. 他们狂欢作乐到清晨。| 近义词：revel, celebrate, party | 反义词：abstain, mourn | verb

caroused: /kəˈraʊzd/ | v. | 畅饮；狂欢（carouse的过去式和过去分词） | past tense and past participle of carouse |  They had caroused all night. 他们整夜狂欢。 | 近义词：reveled, celebrated, partied | 反义词：abstained, mourned | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *carouser*, from Old French *carous*, literally "tilt a carouse," from German *gar aus* "entirely out," referring to emptying one's glass.
