ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *orizon (Modern French horizon), from Late Latin horizontem, accusative of horizon, from Ancient Greek ὁρίζων (horizōn, “limiting, bounding”), present participle of ὁρίζω (horizō, “I limit, define, separate”), from ὅρος (horos, “boundary, limit, landmark”).

PHRASE->
1. on the horizon:  即将发生  A new opportunity is on the horizon. 一个新的机会即将出现。
2. broaden/expand one's horizons: 开阔眼界  Traveling can broaden your horizons. 旅行可以开阔你的眼界。
3. new horizons: 新领域，新天地  Scientists are constantly exploring new horizons. 科学家们不断地探索新领域。
4. limited horizons: 狭隘的视野  Growing up in a small town can sometimes lead to limited horizons. 在小镇长大有时会导致眼界狭隘。
5. beyond the horizon: 在地平线之外，比喻超出视野范围或理解范围  What lies beyond the horizon is unknown. 地平线之外是什么是未知的。
