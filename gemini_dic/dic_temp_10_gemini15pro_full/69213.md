fearfuler:/ˈfɪərfələr/ (less common, sometimes considered non-standard)| adj.|更害怕的，更可怕的|The comparative form of "fearful," though "more fearful" is generally preferred. It means exhibiting or causing a greater degree of fear.  While grammatically correct, it's less frequently used than "more fearful".|Example:  The path seemed fearfuler as the darkness deepened.  这条路随着夜色的加深显得更加可怕。|Example: The fearfuler dog hid behind its owner.  那只更害怕的狗躲在主人身后。|Synonyms: more fearful, more afraid, more terrified, scarier|Antonyms: braver, bolder, more courageous, less afraid|adjective

fearful:/ˈfɪərfəl/| adj.|害怕的，可怕的|Feeling or inspiring fear; causing fear; extremely unpleasant or bad.|Example: She was fearful of walking home alone at night. 她害怕晚上一个人走回家。|Example: The storm was a fearful sight.  暴风雨是可怕的景象。|Synonyms: afraid, frightened, scared, terrified, dreadful, awful|Antonyms: brave, bold, courageous, fearless, unafraid|adjective
