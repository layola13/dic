infixion:/ɪnˈflɪkʃən/ | n. | 词尾变化，屈折变化 | The process of adding affixes to a word stem to create different grammatical forms, such as tense, number, or case. | Example: The "-ed" ending in "walked" is an infixion indicating past tense.  词尾“-ed”在“walked”中表示过去式，是一种屈折变化。 | Example:  Latin is a language rich in infixions. 拉丁语是一种屈折变化丰富的语言。 | Synonyms: inflection | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps derivation or compounding could be considered conceptually opposite processes) | noun
