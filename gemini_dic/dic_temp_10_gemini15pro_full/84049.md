half-fictitiousnesses:/ˌhæf fɪkˈtɪʃəsnɪsɪz/
|n.| 半虚构性；半虚构的事物 |  The book is full of half-fictitiousnesses, blending fact and fantasy.  这本书充满了半虚构的内容，融合了事实和幻想。|
|English Explanation: The state or quality of being partly fictitious; things that are partly fictitious.|
|Example 1:  The historian uncovered the half-fictitiousnesses in the supposedly factual account. 历史学家在所谓的真实记载中发现了半虚构的内容。|
|Example 2: Her memoirs were criticized for their half-fictitiousnesses, blurring the lines between reality and imagination. 她的回忆录因其半虚构性而受到批评，模糊了现实与想象之间的界限。|
|Synonym: semi-fiction, part-fiction, embellished truth|
|Antonym: fact, reality, truth|
|Part of Speech: noun|
