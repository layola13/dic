njai:/ˈnjaɪ/
noun
女佣；保姆；（尤指旧时东南亚地区的）土生华人妇女；
A female domestic servant, especially in Southeast Asia, of mixed local and Chinese ancestry.
She worked as a njai in a wealthy household. 她在一个富裕的家庭里当女佣。
njai is derived from the Chinese word  娘姨 (niáng yí), meaning "maidservant" or "auntie". It became adopted in Malay and Indonesian to refer specifically to female domestic servants, often of mixed Chinese and local descent. These women played a significant role in colonial Southeast Asian societies.
Synonyms: maidservant, servant, domestic helper, housekeeper, ayah (specifically for a children's nurse or maid)
Antonyms: employer, master, mistress
