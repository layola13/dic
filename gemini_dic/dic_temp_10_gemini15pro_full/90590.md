hulled:/hʌld/
adjective
去壳的；去皮的
Having had the hulls removed.
The recipe calls for hulled strawberries.  这个食谱需要去蒂的草莓。

hulled:/hʌld/
verb (past participle of hull)
去壳；去皮
To remove the hulls of.
The sunflower seeds were hulled before roasting.  葵花籽在烘烤前去壳了。


Etymology:
From Middle English *hull*, from Old English *hyll* (“husk, pod, shell”), from Proto-Germanic *hulją (“covering, shell”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kel- (“to cut, cleave”).  Cognate with Scots *hull* (“husk, shell”), West Frisian *húl* (“hull, husk”), Dutch *hul* (“chaff”), German *Hülle* (“cover, covering, jacket, case”), Swedish *hölje* (“covering, sheath”).  Related also to hell.

Synonyms:
shelled, husked, dehusked

Antonyms:
unhulled, unshelled
