albatross:/ˈæl.bə.trɒs/ (US) /ˈæl.bə.trɒs/ (UK)| n. |信天翁；沉重的负担，难以摆脱的苦恼|A large seabird with long narrow wings.  Figuratively, something that causes persistent worry or anxiety.|例句：The albatross around his neck was his gambling debt.  他赌博欠下的债务成了他的心头之患。|例句：The team carried the albatross of last year's defeat into the new season.  这支队伍带着去年失败的包袱进入了新赛季。|近义词：burden, obstacle, impediment, handicap|反义词:advantage, benefit|词性:名词
Etymology: From Portuguese *alcatraz* (gannet, pelican), from Arabic *al-ḡaṭṭās* (the diver), referring to pelicans and frigatebirds. The word was later applied to albatrosses.
