heartburn:/ˈhɑːrtbɜːrn/| n. |胃灼热，烧心|A burning sensation in the chest, caused by acid refluxing from the stomach into the esophagus. | I ate too much spicy food and now I have terrible heartburn. 我吃了太多辛辣的食物，现在胃灼热得厉害。|  I get heartburn after eating greasy food. 我吃完油腻的食物后会烧心。 | Synonyms: acid indigestion, pyrosis | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical sensation) | noun

heartburns:/ˈhɑːrtbɜːrnz/| n. |胃灼热，烧心 (复数，较少使用)|Multiple instances of a burning sensation in the chest, caused by acid refluxing from the stomach into the esophagus. |The heartburns I experienced last night were the worst I've ever had. 我昨晚经历的胃灼热是我有史以来最严重的一次。|  Recurring heartburns can be a sign of a more serious underlying condition. 反复出现的烧心可能是更严重的潜在疾病的征兆。| Synonyms: acid indigestions, pyroses (less common) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical sensation) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *heortebren*, equivalent to *heart* + *burn*.  The term reflects the sensation of burning felt in the chest, near the heart.
