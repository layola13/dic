partisan: /ˈpɑːrtɪzæn/ (US) /ˈpɑːtɪzan/ (UK)| adj.|党羽，支持者；偏袒的，盲目拥护的|Showing strong and often uncritical support for a particular person, group, or cause.|例句：The reporting of the event was highly partisan.  对该事件的报道非常偏颇。|近义词：biased, prejudiced, one-sided|反义词：impartial, unbiased, objective, neutral|词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *partisan*, from Italian *partito* "divided, party," past participle of *partire* "to divide, depart," from Latin *partiri* "to divide, share, distribute," from *pars* "part, piece, share."
