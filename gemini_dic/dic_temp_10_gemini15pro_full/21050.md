blind:/blaɪnd/ | adj. n. v. | 瞎的，失明的；盲目的；遮蔽的；百叶窗；使失明；蒙蔽 |Unable to see.  A covering for a window.  To deprive of sight or understanding.|
She is blind in one eye. 她一只眼睛失明了。

He walked blindly into the trap. 他盲目地走进了陷阱。

The blinds were drawn to block out the sun. 百叶窗被拉下来遮挡阳光。

The sudden flash of light blinded him temporarily. 突如其来的闪光使他暂时失明。

His rage blinded him to the consequences. 他的愤怒使他看不到后果。

近义词：sightless, unseeing, visually impaired, unaware, oblivious | 反义词：sighted, seeing, aware, conscious | 词性：adjective, noun, verb

blind:/blaɪnd/ | n. | 掩蔽物；借口；幌子 | Something that conceals or disguises something else. |
His apparent generosity was just a blind for his ulterior motives. 他表面上的慷慨只是他别有用心的幌子。|
近义词：cover, screen, pretext, disguise | 反义词：exposure, revelation | 词性：noun


词源：From Old English *blind, from Proto-Germanic *blindaz, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰel- (to shine, gleam).  The original meaning likely referred to a dazzling or blinding light, which then evolved to mean loss of sight.
