flagellata:/ˌflædʒəˈleɪtə/ | n. |  鞭毛虫 |A large group of single-celled organisms, mostly found in freshwater, characterized by one or more whip-like appendages called flagella, used for locomotion. Some flagellates are parasitic. | Example:  Flagellata play a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems. 鞭毛虫在水生生态系统中起着至关重要的作用。

Example:  Certain species of flagellata can cause diseases in humans. 某些种类的鞭毛虫会导致人类疾病。 | 近义词: mastigophora | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

flagellate:/ˈflædʒəˌleɪt/ |  vt. | 鞭打；鞭笞 |To whip or flog; to punish by whipping. | Example:  The prisoner was flagellated for his disobedience.  囚犯因不服从而被鞭打。

Example:  Some religious sects practice self-flagellation as a form of penance. 一些宗教派别实行自我鞭笞作为一种忏悔形式。 | 近义词: whip, scourge, flog | 反义词: N/A | 词性: verb

flagellate:/ˈflædʒələt/ | adj. |  鞭毛状的; 拥有鞭毛的 |Having or resembling a flagellum; whip-like. |Example:  The flagellate bacteria moved rapidly through the water. 鞭毛状细菌在水中快速移动。

Example:  The organism's flagellate appendage allowed it to propel itself forward. 该生物的鞭毛状附属物使其能够向前推进。| 近义词: flagelliform | 反义词: N/A | 词性: adjective
