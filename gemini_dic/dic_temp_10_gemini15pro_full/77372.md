gault:/ɡɔːlt/| n. |高岭土，泥灰岩|A stiff clay, intermediate between clay and marl, of variable color, typically blue-gray.  It is used for making bricks and cement.|The house was built of local gault brick.|Synonyms: clay, marl|Antonyms: sand, gravel|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old North French *gaut, of uncertain origin.
