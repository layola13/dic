enslaving:/ɪnˈsleɪvɪŋ/
|adj. |使人沦为奴隶的； enslaving
|Making someone a slave; causing someone to become a slave. |
|The enslaving of millions of Africans was a horrific crime against humanity.  数百万非洲人被奴役是反人类的可怕罪行。

|The system was designed to be enslaving, keeping workers in perpetual debt. 这个系统的设计目的是奴役工人，让他们永远负债。

|近义词：oppressive, subjugating, enslaving, tyrannical, domineering
|反义词：liberating, freeing, emancipating
|词性:  adjective (形容词)


enslave:/ɪnˈsleɪv/
|vt. |奴役；使沦为奴隶； enslaving
|To make someone a slave; to cause someone to become a slave. |
|The colonists enslaved the indigenous population. 殖民者奴役了土著居民。

|He was enslaved by his addiction to gambling. 他被赌瘾所奴役。

|近义词：subjugate, oppress, dominate, control
|反义词：liberate, free, emancipate
|词性: verb (动词)


词源:
From en- + slave, from Medieval Latin sclavus "Slav," originally "captive, slave" (see Slav).
