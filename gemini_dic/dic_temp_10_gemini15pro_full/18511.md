benedict:/ˈbɛnɪdɪkt/| n.|本尼迪克特；一种结婚的男人；新郎；（Benedictine 的简称）本尼迪克特会修士|A man who has married; a newly married man.  It can also refer to a Benedictine monk (shortened form).|例句：He was a happy benedict after the wedding. 他婚礼后成了幸福的新郎。

例句：The benedict enjoyed his honeymoon in Hawaii.  这位新婚男士在夏威夷享受了他的蜜月。|近义词：bridegroom, newlywed, husband|反义词:bachelor, single man|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "benedictus" (blessed), past participle of "benedicere" (to bless, speak well of), from "bene" (well) + "dicere" (to speak).  Originally a given name, it came to be used generally for a newly married man, possibly influenced by the character Benedick in Shakespeare's "Much Ado About Nothing."
