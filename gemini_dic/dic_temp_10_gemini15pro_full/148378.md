overspill:/ˈəʊvəˌspɪl/ (UK) /ˈoʊvərˌspɪl/ (US)| n. |溢出；过剩人口；超额|An excess amount of something, especially people, that cannot be accommodated in the intended place. |The overspill from the overcrowded city moved into the surrounding towns. 城市人口过剩外溢到周边城镇。|近义词：excess, surplus, overflow|反义词：shortage, deficit|词性:名词

overspill:/ˈəʊvəˌspɪl/ (UK) /ˈoʊvərˌspɪl/ (US)| vi. |溢出；涌出|To flow over or spread beyond the intended limits. |The crowd overspilled into the streets. 人群涌入街道。|近义词：overflow, spill over|反义词：contain|词性:动词


词源：The word "overspill" is formed from the prefix "over-" (meaning "excessive" or "beyond") and the verb "spill" (meaning "to flow over").  Its usage as a noun dates back to the mid-20th century, referring to the relocation of population from overcrowded urban areas.
