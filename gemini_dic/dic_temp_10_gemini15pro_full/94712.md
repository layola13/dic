improperia:/ɪmˈproʊpəriə/ | n. | 责备，斥责；（尤指在耶稣受难日唱的）圣歌 | Reproaches, rebukes; specifically, a series of antiphons and responses sung during the Good Friday liturgy, recalling the ingratitude of the Jewish people towards God. |  The improperia are a moving part of the Good Friday service.  耶稣受难日圣歌是耶稣受难日仪式中感人的一部分。| synonyms: reproaches, rebukes, censures, upbraidings | antonyms: praise, commendation, acclaim | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *improperia, plural of *improperium “reproach,” from Latin in- “not, opposite of” + properus “proper, fitting.”
