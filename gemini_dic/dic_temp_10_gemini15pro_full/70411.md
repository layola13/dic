fight-shy-of:/ˈfaɪt ʃaɪ əv/
adjective
回避；不愿面对；躲避
Reluctant to engage in or confront something challenging or unpleasant.
He's always been fight-shy-of public speaking. 他一直回避公开演讲。
The government is fight-shy-of making any difficult decisions. 政府不愿做出任何艰难的决定。
近义词: averse to, reluctant, hesitant, unwilling, afraid of
反义词: eager for, keen on, enthusiastic about
词性: adjective

fight:/faɪt/
verb/noun
打架；战斗；争论
Struggle physically or verbally against someone or something.
They fought bravely for their country. 他们为国家英勇作战。（verb）
He was involved in a street fight. 他卷入了一场街头斗殴。（noun）

shy:/ʃaɪ/
adjective
害羞的；胆怯的；腼腆的
Nervous or timid in the company of other people.
She is a shy girl. 她是一个害羞的女孩。
He was too shy to ask her out. 他太害羞了，不敢约她出去。

of:/əv/ /ʌv/
preposition
属于…的；…的；表示数量或范围
Expressing belonging, origin, or possession; indicating amount or extent.
The history of China is long and rich. 中国历史悠久而丰富。
A group of students went on a trip. 一群学生去旅行了。


词源:
"Fight-shy" originated in the context of boxing, referring to a boxer who is reluctant to engage in a fight.  "Of" is a very common preposition with a long history, ultimately deriving from Old English.
