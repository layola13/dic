discomfiting:/dɪsˈkʌmfɪtɪŋ/
adjective
令人不安的，令人尴尬的
Making someone feel uneasy or embarrassed.
The silence after his joke was discomfiting. 他讲完笑话后的沉默令人尴尬。

discomfiting:/dɪsˈkʌmfɪtɪŋ/
adjective
挫败的，使窘迫的
Thwarting the plans of; frustrating.
The constant rain was discomfiting to our picnic plans.  连绵不断的雨阻碍了我们的野餐计划。


近义词: unsettling, disturbing, embarrassing, disconcerting, flustering
反义词: comforting, reassuring, encouraging, emboldening
词性: adjective
词源: From Old French desconfit, past participle of desconfire  "to defeat, rout, undo," from des- "opposite of" + con- "with" + faire "to do." Originally meant "to defeat utterly," but the meaning weakened to "disconcert, embarrass" by the 17th century.
