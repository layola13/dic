authotype:/ˈɔːθoʊˌtaɪp/| n.|自體型，原版|A specimen, such as a plant or insect, used by the author of a scientific name as the type for the species or other taxon.  A specimen designated as the type by the author of the original description.|The authotype of this beetle is housed in the British Museum.|Synonym: holotype|Antonym: (No direct antonym, possibly paratype or neotype)|noun
