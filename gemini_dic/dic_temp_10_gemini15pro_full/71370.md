flagellator:/ˈflædʒəˌleɪtər/ | n. |鞭毛虫；鞭毛|A slender threadlike structure, especially a whiplike appendage that enables many protozoa, bacteria, spermatozoa, etc., to swim.|The flagellator propels the bacterium through the liquid. 鞭毛推动细菌在液体中前进。|The paramecium, a single-celled organism, uses its flagellator for movement. 草履虫，一种单细胞生物，用它的鞭毛来移动。|Synonyms: cilium, flagellum|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps non-motile)|noun

flagellum:/fləˈdʒɛləm/ | n. |鞭毛|A slender threadlike structure, especially a whiplike appendage that enables many protozoa, bacteria, spermatozoa, etc., to swim.|Bacterial flagella are used for locomotion. 细菌鞭毛用于运动。|Sperm cells use their flagella to swim towards the egg. 精子细胞用它们的鞭毛游向卵子。|Synonyms: cilium, flagellator|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps non-motile)|noun
