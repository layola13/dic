overslavishly:/ˌoʊvərˈsleɪvɪʃli/
adverb
过于奴隶般地；过于顺从地
imitating or following someone or something too closely, often in a way that seems excessive or lacking in originality.
He followed the instructions overslavishly, afraid to deviate even slightly.  他过于奴隶般地遵循指示，不敢有任何偏差。
She overslavishly copied the style of her favorite author. 她过于顺从地模仿了她最喜欢的作者的风格。
Synonyms: excessively, rigidly, subserviently, unquestioningly
Antonyms: independently, creatively, originally


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (excessively) + "slavish" (like a slave) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).  "Slavish" ultimately comes from "Slav," referring to the Slavic peoples who were often enslaved in the past.  The word has come to mean showing excessive obedience or imitation.
