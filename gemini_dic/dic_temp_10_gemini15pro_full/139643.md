occulter:/əˈkʌltər/ | n. |掩星器，掩星盘|A device used to block light from a celestial object, especially to allow observation of a fainter object nearby.  It creates an artificial eclipse. |The occulter blocked the glare of the star, allowing astronomers to study the exoplanet. 掩星器挡住了恒星的强光，使天文学家能够研究系外行星。|近义词:coronagraph, mask |反义词:N/A |词性:noun
occulter:/əˈkʌltər/ | v. |掩蔽，遮挡|To obscure or hide from view, especially a celestial object. |The moon occulted the sun during the eclipse. 在日食期间，月亮遮住了太阳。|近义词:eclipse, obscure, conceal |反义词:reveal, expose |词性:verb
