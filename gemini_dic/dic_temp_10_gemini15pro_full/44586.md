croupous:/ˈkruːpəs/| adj.|  渗出性的；格鲁布性肺炎的；假膜性的 |Relating to or affected with croup, an inflammatory condition of the larynx and trachea, characterized by a harsh cough and difficulty breathing; also, relating to a membrane formed during such inflammation, or resembling such a membrane.  More specifically, relating to a form of pneumonia characterized by the formation of a fibrous exudate in the air passages. |  The doctor diagnosed a croupous pneumonia. 医生诊断为格鲁布性肺炎。|Synonyms: membranous, fibrinous, pseudomembranous | Antonyms: non-membranous| adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *croupeux*, from croupe (“rump, crupper”).  Ultimately from Old Norse *kryppa* (“hump, crooked position”).  The connection to the disease croup likely arises from the crouched posture often adopted by sufferers.
