infarction:/ɪnˈfɑːrkʃən/ | n. |梗死，梗塞|The obstruction of a blood vessel, typically by a blood clot or a foreign body, that deprives tissue of oxygen and nutrients, causing local death of the tissue.| He suffered a myocardial infarction. 他患了心肌梗死。|Synonyms: blockage, obstruction, occlusion |Antonyms: perfusion, patency |noun

infarction:/ɪnˈfɑːrkʃən/ | n. |梗塞|Localized necrosis resulting from obstruction of the blood supply.|  A pulmonary infarction can be life-threatening. 肺梗塞可能危及生命。|Synonyms: ischemia, necrosis |Antonyms: reperfusion, viability |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin infarctus, past participle of infarcire (“to stuff, cram”), from in- (“in, into”) + farcire (“to stuff”).
