abominably:/əˈbɒmɪnəbli/| adv.|讨厌地；非常地；极坏地|in an abominable manner; very badly; extremely unpleasantly.|The weather was abominably cold. 天气非常寒冷。

The food at the hotel was abominably prepared. 酒店的食物做得非常难吃。

|近义词：horribly, terribly, awfully, dreadfully, appallingly|反义词：wonderfully, excellently, splendidly, delightfully|词性：副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *abominablement*, from Late Latin abominabilis ("detestable") + -ment.  Ultimately from Latin abōminārī ("to deprecate as an ill omen") + -bilis.

USAGE->
Describing weather:
The weather was abominably cold during our trip. = 我们旅行期间天气非常寒冷。
It rained abominably all day. = 一整天都下着瓢泼大雨。


Describing quality:
He behaved abominably at the party. = 他在聚会上表现得非常糟糕。
The film was abominably acted and directed. = 这部电影的表演和导演都非常糟糕。


Modifying other adverbs:
He sang abominably badly. = 他唱得非常难听。
She played the piano abominably poorly. = 她弹钢琴弹得非常糟糕。


With verbs of feeling or perception:
I felt abominably ill after eating that seafood. = 我吃了那个海鲜后感觉非常不舒服。
The room smelled abominably of stale cigarette smoke. = 房间里弥漫着令人作呕的陈旧香烟味。


With past participles:
The house was abominably decorated. = 这房子装修得非常糟糕。
The report was abominably written. = 这份报告写得非常糟糕。
