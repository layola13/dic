administer-to:/ədˈmɪnɪstər tuː/| v.|给予，施予；照顾，照料|To provide or apply something, especially something beneficial or necessary, to someone or something; to care for or attend to someone's needs.|The nurse administered to the patient's wounds. 护士处理了病人的伤口。

The Red Cross administered food and medical supplies to the refugees. 红十字会向难民提供食物和医疗用品。|近义词：give, provide, supply, tend, care for|反义词：withhold, neglect|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *administren*, from Latin *administrāre* ("to manage, serve, direct"), from *ad* ("to, towards") + *ministrāre* ("to serve, attend"), from *minister* ("servant, attendant").  The "-to" preposition specifies the recipient of the administration.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The doctor administers the vaccine to the children. = 医生给孩子们接种疫苗。
The charity administers aid to those in need. =  慈善机构向需要帮助的人提供援助。

一般过去时:
The nurse administered pain medication to the patient. = 护士给病人服用了止痛药。
The organization administered funds to the disaster victims. = 该组织向灾民提供了资金。

现在完成时:
The government has administered several programs to boost the economy. = 政府已经实施了几项刺激经济的计划。
They have administered first aid to the injured hiker. = 他们已经给受伤的徒步旅行者进行了急救。

过去完成时:
After the paramedics had administered first aid, they transported him to the hospital. =  医护人员进行了急救后，把他送到了医院。
She had administered the medicine before the doctor arrived. = 在医生到达之前，她已经服用了药。

将来时:
The hospital will administer the new treatment to eligible patients. = 医院将对符合条件的患者实施新的治疗方案。
The school will administer the exam next week. = 学校将于下周举行考试。


虚拟语气:
If I were to administer the funds, I would prioritize education. = 如果由我来管理这些资金，我会优先考虑教育。
I wish they would administer the policy more fairly. = 我希望他们能更公平地执行这项政策。


被动语态:
The antidote was administered to the victim immediately. = 解毒剂立即被给予了受害者。
Food and water are being administered to the survivors. = 食物和水正在被提供给幸存者。

疑问句:

否定句:
The doctor did not administer the correct dosage. = 医生没有给予正确的剂量。
They haven't administered the survey yet. = 他们还没有进行调查。
