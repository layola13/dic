flounced: /flaʊnst/ | v. | 猛地移动；气冲冲地走；| to move with exaggerated jerky or bouncy movements, typically in a way intended to express annoyance or disapproval. | She flounced out of the room, slamming the door behind her. 她气冲冲地走出房间，砰地一声关上了门。|  He flounced into the meeting, clearly in a bad mood.  他气冲冲地走进会议室，显然心情不好。| stomp, stride, swagger | tiptoe, creep | verb

flounce: /flaʊns/ | n. | 褶边；荷叶边；气冲冲地走；| a strip of gathered or pleated material sewn to a garment, typically at the hem or cuff, or around the neckline; an act of flouncing. | The dress had a pretty flounce at the hem. 这件衣服的裙摆有一圈漂亮的褶边。| She left the room with a dramatic flounce. 她气冲冲地离开了房间。| frill, ruffle, furbelow | plainness, simplicity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *floncer (compare Old French floncer "to flow, drip," also "to adorn with flounces"), of uncertain origin.
