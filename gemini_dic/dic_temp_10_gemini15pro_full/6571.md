anabaptistical:/ˌænəbæpˈtɪstɪkəl/
adjective
再洗礼派的；激进的宗教改革的
Of or relating to Anabaptists, a radical Protestant sect that emerged during the Reformation.  Characterized by adult baptism, pacifism, and often communal living.
The anabaptistical movement spread rapidly throughout Europe in the 16th century.  再洗礼运动在16世纪迅速传遍了整个欧洲。
His anabaptistical views led him to be ostracized by the mainstream church. 他再洗礼派的观点导致他被主流教会排斥。
近义词: radical, sectarian, dissident, nonconformist
反义词: orthodox, conventional, conformist
词性: adjective

Etymology:
From Anabaptist + -ical.  Anabaptist comes from the Greek ana (again) + baptizein (to baptize).
