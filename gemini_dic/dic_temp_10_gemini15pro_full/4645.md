aleatory: /ˈeɪliətɔːri/ (US), /ˈæliətəri/ (UK) | adj. |  偶然的; 随机的;  | Depending on chance; random. |  The composer used aleatory techniques, allowing the performers to improvise sections of the piece.  作曲家使用了偶然性技巧，允许演奏者即兴创作作品的某些部分。|  random, chance, haphazard, accidental |  planned, predetermined, fixed, predictable | adjective

aleatory: /ˈeɪliətɔːri/ (US), /ˈæliətəri/ (UK) | n. |  偶然性音乐;  | Music in which some element of the composition is left to chance, and/or some primary element of a composed work's realization is left to the determination of its performer(s). | The piece was an example of aleatory, with the musicians choosing which notes to play. 这首曲子是偶然音乐的一个例子，由音乐家选择演奏哪些音符。| randomness, chance, indeterminacy |  determinism, premeditation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *aleatorius (“pertaining to a dice player”), from aleator (“dice player”), from alea (“die; game of dice; chance, risk”).
