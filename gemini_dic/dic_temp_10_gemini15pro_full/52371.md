ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dictionarium "a collection of words or phrases," noun use of neuter of dictionarius "of words," from dictio "a saying, word, diction," from dicere "to say, speak" (from PIE root *deik- "to show," also "to pronounce solemnly").  The English word is attested from c. 1225, referring to a collection of Latin words and phrases.
