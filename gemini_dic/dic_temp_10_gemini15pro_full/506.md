abolished:/əˈbɒlɪʃt/ /əˈbɑːlɪʃt/| v. |废除，取消|To formally put an end to a system, practice, or institution.|Slavery was abolished in the US in 1865.  美国在1865年废除了奴隶制。

Capital punishment has been abolished in many countries. 许多国家已经废除了死刑。|近义词：eliminate, eradicate, extinguish, terminate|反义词：establish, institute, introduce, restore|词性:动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government abolishes outdated laws. = 政府废除过时的法律。
The company abolishes the old dress code. = 公司废除了旧的着装规定。


一般过去时:
They abolished the monarchy in 1918. = 他们在1918年废除了君主制。
The school abolished corporal punishment years ago. = 学校多年前就废除了体罚。


现在完成时:
Several countries have abolished the death penalty. = 一些国家已经废除了死刑。
They have abolished this unnecessary procedure. = 他们已经废除了这个不必要的程序。


过去完成时:
By the time I arrived, they had already abolished the rule. = 到我到达的时候，他们已经废除了这条规则。
He realized the policy had been abolished years before. = 他意识到这项政策多年前就已经被废除了。


将来时:
The committee will abolish the outdated regulations. = 委员会将废除过时的规章制度。
They will abolish this tax next year. = 他们明年将取消这项税收。


被动语态:
The law was abolished in 1964. = 这项法律在1964年被废除。
The practice has been abolished by many organizations. = 这种做法已被许多组织废除。
