interceders:/ˌɪntərˈsiːdərz/ | n. |调解人，说情者，代祷者|A person who intervenes on behalf of another, especially by prayer or entreaty. |例句：The interceders prayed for peace between the warring nations. 调解员们为交战国之间的和平祈祷。

例句：She acted as an interceder between the parents and their rebellious teenager. 她充当了父母和叛逆青少年之间的调解人。|近义词：mediator, advocate, negotiator, pleader |反义词:opponent, adversary |词性: Noun

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *intercedour, from Old French intercedeor, intercesseur, from Latin intercessor, from intercedere (“to intercede”), from inter- (“between”) + cedere (“to go”).
