inconceivably:/ˌɪnkənˈsiːvəbli/
adverb
难以置信地，不可思议地
in a way that is difficult to imagine or believe.
The task was inconceivably difficult. 这项任务难度不可思议。
Near synonyms: unimaginably, unbelievably, incredibly
Antonyms: conceivably, possibly, imaginably
词性: 副词

inconceivable:/ˌɪnkənˈsiːvəbl/
adjective
难以置信的，不可思议的
not capable of being imagined or grasped mentally; unbelievable.
It seemed inconceivable that they could lose. 他们竟然会输，这似乎不可思议。
Near synonyms: unimaginable, unbelievable, incredible
Antonyms: conceivable, possible, imaginable
词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From in- (not) + conceivable (capable of being conceived or imagined).  Conceivable comes from the Late Latin  "concipibilis," from Latin "concipere" (to conceive), from "com-" (together) + "capere" (to take).
