graphite: /ˈɡræfaɪt/ (US), /ˈɡræfaɪt/ (UK)| n. |石墨|A crystalline form of carbon, soft, dark gray or black, and greasy, used as a solid lubricant, in pencils, and as a moderator in nuclear reactors.|Pencils contain graphite. 铅笔含有石墨。|Synonyms: plumbago, black lead|Antonyms: diamond (in terms of crystalline structure)|noun
