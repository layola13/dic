acropoles: /əˈkrɒpəlɪs/ /əˈkrɑːpəlɪs/
n. |卫城，城堡|A citadel or fortified part of an ancient Greek city, typically built on a high rocky outcrop.  The most famous acropolis is the one in Athens. |The Acropolis of Athens dominates the city skyline. 雅典卫城雄踞城市天际线。

The Parthenon is the most famous building on the Acropolis.  帕特农神庙是雅典卫城最著名的建筑。|近义词: citadel, fortress, stronghold|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "lowlands" in a contrasting sense)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *akropolis*, from *akros* "highest, topmost" + *polis* "city".  The word literally means "high city".

The view from the Acropolis is breathtaking. = 从卫城看到的景色令人叹为观止。

The ancient Acropolis is remarkably well-preserved. = 古老的卫城保存得非常好。

The majestic Acropolis stands as a symbol of Greek civilization. = 雄伟的卫城是希腊文明的象征。


Plural Form:

Many Greek cities had acropolises. = 许多希腊城市都有卫城。
