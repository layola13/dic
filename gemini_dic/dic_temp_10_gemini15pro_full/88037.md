highbrowish:/ˈhaɪbraʊɪʃ/| adj.|有点高雅的，有点附庸风雅的|Slightly intellectual or culturally refined, often in a pretentious or affected way.  Suggesting an interest in serious art, literature, etc., often to impress others.|She made some highbrowish remarks about the latest opera, but I doubt she actually understood it. 她对最新的歌剧发表了一些附庸风雅的评论，但我怀疑她是否真的理解。|近义词：pretentious, intellectual, snobbish, affected|反义词：lowbrow, unpretentious, down-to-earth|词性:adjective

highbrow:/ˈhaɪbraʊ/| n. adj.|知识分子，高雅的|An intellectual; of or relating to superior intellectual or cultural interests or to intellectuals.|He considers himself a highbrow and looks down on popular culture. 他自认为是一个知识分子，看不起流行文化。|近义词：intellectual, sophisticated|反义词：lowbrow, philistine|词性:noun, adjective

-ish:/ɪʃ/| suffix.|有点...的，稍微...的|Forms adjectives meaning "somewhat," "rather," or "having the quality of."|It's a greenish-blue color. 它是有点偏绿的蓝色。|近义词：-like, somewhat|反义词: (No direct antonym for a suffix)|词性:suffix
