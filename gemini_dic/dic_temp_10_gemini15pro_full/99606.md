interjection:/ˌɪntərˈdʒekʃən/| n.|感叹词|A word or phrase that expresses a strong feeling or emotion, such as surprise, joy, or anger. It is often followed by an exclamation mark.|Example: "Ouch! That hurt!"  哎哟！好痛！

Example: "Wow! That's amazing!" 哇！太棒了！|Synonym: exclamation|Antonym: (No direct antonym for a part of speech)|part of speech:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *interjectiōn- (stem of interjectiō ), from interjectus, past participle of intericere (“to throw between”), from inter- (“between”) + jacere (“to throw”).

USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
What a beautiful day! = 多么美好的一天！
How interesting! = 真有趣！


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
(Interjections are typically not used within the grammatical structure of a continuous tense, but can precede or follow such a sentence.)
Ouch! I'm burning myself! = 哎哟！我烫到自己了！
Look! It's snowing! = 看！下雪了！


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
(Similar to continuous tenses, interjections don't fit directly into perfect tenses.)
Wow! I've never seen anything like that! = 哇！我从来没见过这样的东西！
Alas! He has gone. = 唉！他走了。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
(Same as above.)
Gosh, I've been working all day! = 天哪，我一直工作了一整天！
Phew! I've been running for hours. = 呼！我跑了好几个小时了。


一般过去时 (Simple Past):

Damn! I missed the bus. = 该死！我错过了公共汽车。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
