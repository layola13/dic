diction:/ˈdɪkʃən/ | n. | 措辞，用语; 发音; |  The choice and use of words and phrases in speech or writing.  |  She has impeccable diction. 她措辞完美无瑕。| synonyms: articulation, pronunciation, enunciation, phrasing, intonation, inflection, delivery, locution | antonyms: inarticulateness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin dictionem (nominative dictio) "a speaking, expression, word," from dicere "to say, speak," frequentative of dicare "proclaim, dedicate," related to Greek deiknynai "to show, point out," Sanskrit disati "shows, points out."
