freak:/friːk/
n.|怪人，怪胎；狂热爱好者；畸形生物|a person or animal that is markedly unusual or deformed|例句：He was a fitness freak. 他是一个健身狂人。|近义词：oddity, eccentric, enthusiast, fanatic, anomaly|反义词：normality, regular|词性:名词

freak:/friːk/
v.|（使）发生畸变；（使）强烈反应；吓坏|to become or cause someone to become extremely emotional, especially because of fear, shock, or excitement|例句：The accident freaked me out.  这起事故把我吓坏了。|近义词：frighten, scare, startle, shock, disturb|反义词：calm, soothe|词性:动词

freak:/friːk/
adj.|奇异的，反常的|very unusual or unexpected|例句：A freak storm hit the coast.  一场异常的风暴袭击了海岸。|近义词：bizarre, strange, odd, unusual, abnormal|反义词：normal, usual, typical|词性:形容词

词源：
From Middle English *freken (“to dance wildly”), of uncertain origin. Perhaps related to Old English frēc (“greedy, bold, audacious”), or to frician (“to dance, jump, caper”).  More at frantic.
来自中古英语 *freken（“疯狂地跳舞”），来源不确定。可能与古英语 frēc（“贪婪的、大胆的、无畏的”）或 frician（“跳舞、跳跃、雀跃”）有关。更多信息参见 frantic。
