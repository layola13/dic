blotchier:/ˈblɒtʃɪə(r)/ (US) /ˈblɑːtʃɪər/
adjective
更斑点的，污迹更多的
More blotchy; having more or larger blotches.
The patient's skin became blotchier as the allergic reaction worsened.  病人的皮肤随着过敏反应的加剧变得更加斑驳。
The old wall was blotchier than I remembered.  这面老墙比我记忆中的斑驳更多。
近义词:spottier, patchier, more mottled
反义词:clearer, smoother, more even
词性:adjective

blotchy:/ˈblɒtʃi/ (US) /ˈblɑːtʃi/
adjective
斑点的，有污迹的
Marked with or characterized by blotches.
Her face was blotchy from crying. 她的脸因为哭泣而变得斑驳。
The painting had a blotchy appearance due to the uneven application of paint.  由于油漆涂抹不均匀，这幅画看起来斑驳不堪。
近义词:spotted, patchy, mottled
反义词:clear, smooth, even
词性:adjective

blotch:/blɒtʃ/ (US) /blɑːtʃ/
noun
斑点，污点
An irregular mark or discolored area, typically on skin.
The ink left a blotch on the paper. 墨水在纸上留下了一个污点。
He had a blotch of red paint on his shirt. 他的衬衫上有一块红色的油漆污渍。
词性:noun

blotch:/blɒtʃ/ (US) /blɑːtʃ/
verb
弄脏，涂污
To mark or stain with blotches.
The spilled coffee blotched the tablecloth. 洒出的咖啡弄脏了桌布。
Tears blotched her makeup. 眼泪弄花了她的妆容。
词性:verb


词源:  From Middle English *blocche, from Old Norse *blottr (“bare, naked”), from Proto-Germanic *blōtaz (“bare, naked”). Related to Old English blāt (“pale, livid”). The original sense was "a bare patch on the skin," later extended to any discolored area.
