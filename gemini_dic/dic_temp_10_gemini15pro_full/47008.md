dashingly:/ˈdæʃɪŋli/| adv.|潇洒地，有气派地，华丽地|In a dashing manner; with flamboyant style and confidence.|He strode into the room dashingly, dressed in a tailored suit. 他穿着剪裁得体的西装，潇洒地走进房间。|Synonyms: stylishly, smartly, elegantly, gallantly|Antonyms: awkwardly, clumsily, plainly, dully|Part of Speech: Adverb

Etymology:
From dashing + -ly.  "Dashing" itself originates from the verb "dash," which initially meant "to strike violently," later evolving to imply a sense of speed and vigorous movement.  This sense of energetic movement then developed into the connotations of boldness and stylishness associated with the word today.
