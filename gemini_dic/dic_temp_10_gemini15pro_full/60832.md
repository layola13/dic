electroshocks:/ɪˈlɛktroʊʃɑːks/ | n. | 电击 | The sudden passage of a burst of electric current through the body. | He was treated with electroshocks for severe depression. 他因严重抑郁接受了电击治疗。|  The patient received a series of electroshocks. 病人接受了一系列电击治疗。 | synonyms:  electroconvulsive therapy (ECT), shock therapy, electrical stimulation | antonyms: grounding, earthing | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
electro- + shock

* electro-: from Greek *ēlektron* meaning "amber," referencing the static electricity produced by rubbing amber.
* shock: From Old French *choquer*, "to strike against," ultimately from a Vulgar Latin source.
