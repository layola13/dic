betacism:/ˈbeɪtəˌsɪzəm/
n.
betacism(语音学)
The change of other consonants, especially *p, *ph, to *b. The excessive use of the sounds represented by the letters B and V, resulting in the confusion of those sounds.
The phenomenon in some languages whereby other sounds change into the sounds represented by the letters b or v.  For example, in Spanish, the Latin 'v' became a 'b'.
Example: Betacism is a common feature in the evolution of Romance languages from Latin.  西班牙语从拉丁语演变而来的一个共同特征是浊化。
Synonym: lenition, weakening
Antonym: fortition, strengthening
