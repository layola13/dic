amarant:/ˈæmərænt/ | n. |苋菜，不凋花| An annual plant of the genus *Amaranthus*, with clusters of small greenish or reddish flowers. Some species are cultivated as vegetables or for their seeds, while others are considered weeds. | The ancient Greeks believed that amaranth had special powers and could grant immortality. 古希腊人认为苋菜具有特殊的力量，可以赋予人永生。|Synonyms: pigweed, love-lies-bleeding | Antonyms: N/A | noun

amarant:/ˈæmərænt/ | adj. |不凋谢的，紫红色的| Of a deep purplish-red colour; unfading. |  She wore an amaranth gown to the ball. 她穿着一件紫红色的礼服去参加舞会。| Synonyms: unfading, everlasting, magenta | Antonyms: fading, ephemeral | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *amarante*, from Latin *amaranthus*, from Greek *amarantos* (unfading), from *a-* (not) + *marainein* (to wither).
