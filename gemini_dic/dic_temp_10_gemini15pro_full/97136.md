infiltration:/ˌɪnfɪlˈtreɪʃən/ | n. | 渗透，浸润 |The act of entering or gaining access to a place or organization surreptitiously, especially for hostile purposes.  |  Example 1: The infiltration of enemy agents into the government was a serious security breach. 敌方特工渗透进政府是一个严重的安保漏洞。

Example 2:  The doctor was concerned about the infiltration of fluid in the lungs. 医生担心肺部积液。 | Synonyms: penetration, permeation, seepage | Antonyms: expulsion, exclusion | noun

infiltrations:/ˌɪnfɪlˈtreɪʃənz/ | n. | 渗透，浸润（复数）|  The plural form of "infiltration," referring to multiple instances of entering or gaining access surreptitiously. | Example 1: The report detailed several infiltrations of the rebel group into the city. 报告详细描述了叛军几次渗透进城市的情况。

Example 2: The repeated infiltrations of rainwater caused damage to the building's foundation.  反复的雨水渗透损坏了建筑物的地基。| Synonyms: penetrations, permeations, seepages | Antonyms: expulsions, exclusions | noun
