abseils: /ˈæbseɪlz/ (UK), /æbˈseɪlz/ (US)| n. vi. vt.|绳降，绕绳下降|The act of descending a rock face or other near-vertical surface by using a doubled rope coiled round the body and fixed at a higher point.|The climbers abseiled down the cliff face. 登山者们用绳子从悬崖面上滑了下来。

The team abseiled into the cave.  队员们用绳索下降进入洞穴。

They abseiled the injured climber to safety.  他们用绳索把受伤的登山者送到了安全的地方。|近义词：rappel (especially US), descend, lower|反义词：ascend, climb|词性：名词(通常复数), 动词(不及物动词, 及物动词)

ETYMOLOGY->
From German *abseilen, from ab- 'down' + Seil 'rope'.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often abseils down mountains. = 他经常从山上绳降。
She abseils for fun. = 她以绳降为乐。

一般过去时:
They abseiled down the waterfall yesterday. = 他们昨天从瀑布上绳降下来。
We abseiled from the helicopter. = 我们从直升机上绳降下来。


现在完成时:
I have abseiled many times before. = 我以前绳降过很多次。
She has never abseiled before. = 她以前从未绳降过。

过去完成时:
He had abseiled down the cliff before the rescue team arrived. = 在救援队到达之前，他已经从悬崖上绳降下来了。
They had abseiled into the canyon before nightfall. = 他们在天黑前就绳降进了峡谷。


将来时:
We will abseil down the building tomorrow. = 我们明天将从楼上绳降。
She will abseil into the cave next week. = 她下周将绳降进洞穴。

虚拟语气:
If I were braver, I would abseil down that mountain. = 如果我更勇敢一些，我会从那座山上绳降下来。
I wish I could abseil like a professional. = 我希望我能像专业人士一样绳降。


被动语态:
The injured climber was abseiled to safety by the rescue team. = 受伤的登山者被救援队用绳索送到了安全的地方。
The equipment is being abseiled down to the climbers. = 设备正在被绳索送到登山者手中。


疑问句:

否定句:

They didn't abseil because of the bad weather. = 由于天气不好，他们没有绳降。
