droughtier:/ˈdraʊtiər/ | adj. | 更干旱的 | More affected by drought than something else; experiencing a more severe or prolonged period of dryness. | The droughtier regions of the country are facing severe water shortages.  这个国家更干旱的地区正面临严重的水资源短缺。 | Synonyms: drier, arid, parched | Antonyms: wetter, moister, humid | adjective

drought:/draʊt/ | n. | 干旱 | A prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall, leading to a shortage of water. |  The drought has severely impacted crop yields this year. 今年的干旱严重影响了农作物产量。 | Synonyms: aridity, dryness | Antonyms: flood, deluge | noun


Etymology: From Middle English *drought, *drougth, from Old English *drugaþ (“dryness, drought”), from Proto-Germanic *drugaz (“dry”). Cognate with Dutch droogte (“drought”), German Duerre (“drought”).
