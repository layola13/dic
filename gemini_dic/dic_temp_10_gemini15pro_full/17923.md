beeswing:/ˈbiːzwɪŋ/ | n. | 酒渣，酒脚，陈酒的沉淀物 |  A deposit of fine particles, especially of tartar, that gradually forms in wine, especially port, as it matures, and is considered a sign of quality.  It is usually filtered out before bottling but sometimes left in older vintages. | The old port had a thick layer of beeswing.  这瓶老波特酒有一层厚厚的酒渣。| Synonym: sediment, deposit, lees | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, possibly "clarity" in the context of wine appearance) | noun
