contumaciously:/ˌkɒntjʊˈmeɪʃəsli/ | adv. | 顽固地，任性地，不服从地 | In a stubbornly disobedient manner; rebelliously. | He contumaciously refused to answer the judge's questions. 他顽固地拒绝回答法官的问题。 | The defendant contumaciously ignored the court order.  被告不服从地无视了法院的命令。 | 近义词: rebelliously, defiantly, obstinately, perversely | 反义词: obediently, submissively, respectfully | 词性: 副词

contumacious:/ˌkɒntjʊˈmeɪʃəs/ | adj. | 顽固的，任性的，不服从的 | Stubbornly disobedient; rebellious. | His contumacious behavior led to his expulsion from school.  他顽固的行为导致他被学校开除。| The contumacious witness refused to cooperate with the investigation.  不服从的证人拒绝配合调查。| 近义词: rebellious, defiant, obstinate, perverse | 反义词: obedient, submissive, respectful | 词性: 形容词

contumacy:/ˈkɒntjʊməsi/ | n. | 顽固，任性，不服从 | Stubborn disobedience; rebelliousness. | The judge held him in contempt for his contumacy. 法官因他的不服从而判他藐视法庭。| Her contumacy towards her parents only worsened the situation. 她对父母的反抗只会使情况恶化。| 近义词: rebellion, defiance, obstinacy, perversity | 反义词: obedience, submission, respect | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contumacia "stubbornness, arrogance," from Latin contumax "stubborn, insolent," perhaps from com- "together" (see com-) + tumēre "to swell" (see thumb).  The notion is perhaps of swelling with pride or arrogance.
