collectivization:/kəˌlɛktɪvɪˈzeɪʃən/
n.|集体化|The process of organizing something, such as agriculture or industry, on the basis of collective ownership and control.|The collectivization of farms in the Soviet Union resulted in widespread famine.|collectivization of agriculture|privatization|noun
collectivize:/kəˈlɛktɪvaɪz/
v.|集体化|To organize something, such as agriculture or industry, on the basis of collective ownership and control.|Stalin collectivized Soviet agriculture in the 1930s.|combine, consolidate|privatize|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *collectiviser*, from *collectif* (collective) + *-iser* (suffix forming verbs from adjectives).  *Collectif* is from Latin *collectivus* from *collectus* (past participle of *colligere* - to gather together), from *com-* (together) + *legere* (to gather).
