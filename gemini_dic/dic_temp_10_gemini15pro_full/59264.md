eachest:/ˈiːtʃɪst/ | determiner, pronoun | 每人，每个 | each + est (superlative suffix, no longer productive); Archaic or dialectal form meaning "each one" or "each of two or more considered individually."  It emphasizes individuality within a group.  While grammatically correct in some older texts, it's generally considered obsolete or non-standard in modern English and is rarely used. | The oldest form, found in some dialects, is eachist.  | 近义词: each, every, apiece | 反义词: none, neither | 词性: determiner, pronoun
