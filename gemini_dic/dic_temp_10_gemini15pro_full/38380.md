conceptions:/kənˈsɛpʃənz/
n.
概念，观念，想法
an abstract idea; a general notion.
She had a vague conception of what she wanted to do. 她对自己想做什么只有一个模糊的概念。

conceptions:/kənˈsɛpʃənz/
n.
怀孕，受孕
the action of conceiving a child or of a child being conceived.
The couple were overjoyed at the news of her conception. 这对夫妇听到她怀孕的消息欣喜若狂。


Etymology:
From Late Latin *conceptionem (nominative conceptio), noun of action from past participle stem of concipere "to take in, conceive, become pregnant," from con- "together" (see con-) + capere "to take, seize" (from PIE root *kap- "to grasp").

Synonym:
idea, notion, concept, thought, perception, understanding, image, view, belief, opinion, impression (for the first meaning)
pregnancy, fertilization, inception, insemination (for the second meaning)

Antonym:
misconception, misunderstanding, misinterpretation (for the first meaning)
