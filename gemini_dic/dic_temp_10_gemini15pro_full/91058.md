hustled:/ˈhʌsəld/ | v. | 催促；赶紧；忙碌；(非正式) 欺骗；兜售 | To force someone to move quickly; to work quickly and energetically; to obtain by forceful action or persuasion, often illegally or unscrupulously. |  He hustled us out of the room. 他把我们从房间里赶了出去。| synonyms: hurried, rushed, hastened, pressured, scammed, swindled | antonyms: dawdled, lingered, delayed, stalled | verb

hustled:/ˈhʌsəld/ | n. | 忙碌；喧嚣；奔忙；欺骗 | Energetic activity. | There was a lot of hustle and bustle in the market. 市场里熙熙攘攘。| synonyms: activity, commotion, flurry, scam, swindle | antonyms: quiet, stillness, calm, inactivity | noun
