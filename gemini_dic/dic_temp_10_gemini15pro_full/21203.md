blockheadedly:/ˌblɒkˈhɛdɪdli/ /ˌblɑːkˈhɛdɪdli/| adv.|愚蠢地，笨拙地|In a stupid or obtuse manner; like a blockhead.  This describes actions performed in a way that demonstrates a lack of intelligence or common sense.|He blockheadedly walked into the meeting without his presentation. 他愚蠢地走进了会议室，却没带演示文稿。|Synonyms: stupidly, foolishly, obtusely, densely, doltishly|Antonyms: intelligently, wisely, cleverly, astutely|Adverb
Etymology: From "blockhead" (a stupid person) + "-ed" (forming an adjective) + "-ly" (forming an adverb). "Blockhead" itself comes from "block" (a solid piece of wood) + "head".
