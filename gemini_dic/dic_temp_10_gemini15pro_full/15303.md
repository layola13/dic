backpedaled: /ˌbækˈpɛdəld/ (US), /ˌbækˈpɛdld/ (UK)| v. |后退；改变立场；收回前言；退缩|To reverse one's previous position or opinion; to retreat from a commitment or statement.| Example:  He backpedaled furiously when he realized he had offended his boss. 他意识到冒犯了老板后，拼命地往回找补。

Example:  After initially supporting the proposal, the politician backpedaled when it proved unpopular.  在最初支持该提议后，这位政治家在提议不受欢迎时改变了立场。| 近义词: retreat, retract, withdraw, recant| 反义词: advance, persist, maintain|词性:动词

backpedal: /ˈbækˌpɛdəl/ (US), /ˈbækˌpɛdl/ (UK)| n. |后退；改变立场；退缩|The act of reversing one's previous position or opinion.| Example: The government's backpedal on the new tax policy was met with criticism. 政府在新税收政策上的退缩遭到了批评。

Example:  Her quick backpedal saved her from further embarrassment.  她迅速的收回前言避免了进一步的尴尬。| 近义词: retreat, retraction, withdrawal| 反义词: advance, persistence|词性:名词
