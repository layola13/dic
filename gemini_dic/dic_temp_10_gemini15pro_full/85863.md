head-over-heels:/ˌhed ˈoʊvər ˈhiːlz/ | adj. | 神魂颠倒的；深深地爱上的 | Deeply in love; completely infatuated. |  例句：He's head-over-heels in love with her. 他对她神魂颠倒。

例句：She fell head-over-heels for him the moment she saw him. 她一见到他就深深地爱上了他。

近义词：infatuated, smitten, besotted | 反义词：indifferent, apathetic | 词性：形容词


head over heels:/ˌhed ˈoʊvər ˈhiːlz/ | adv. |  翻筋斗地；完全地；彻底地 | In a somersaulting motion; completely; utterly. | 例句：The car went head over heels down the embankment. 汽车翻滚着冲下了路堤。

例句：He's head over heels in debt. 他完全负债累累。

近义词：completely, totally, utterly | 反义词：partially, slightly | 词性：副词


词源：This idiom likely originated from the literal act of tumbling head over heels, which suggests a loss of control and disorientation, similar to the feeling of being deeply in love.  最初可能源于字面意义上的翻筋斗动作，表示失去控制和方向感，类似于深深爱上一个人的感觉。
