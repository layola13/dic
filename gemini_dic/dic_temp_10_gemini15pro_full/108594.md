lead-ins:/ˈliːdɪnz/ | n. | 开场白；引子；导入语 |  Something that introduces something else, especially a piece of music or a section of a broadcast. | The DJ played some catchy lead-ins to get the crowd excited.  DJ播放了一些吸引人的开场白来调动人群的情绪。 | synonyms: introduction, preamble, preface, prelude | antonyms: conclusion, ending, epilogue | noun

lead-in:/ˈliːdɪn/ | n. | 引线；引入线；导入 | A wire that carries electric current to a device. (This meaning is less common for "lead-ins" in the plural form.) | The lead-in to the appliance was frayed.  这个电器的引线磨损了。 | synonyms: wire, cable, conductor | antonyms: output, outlet | noun
