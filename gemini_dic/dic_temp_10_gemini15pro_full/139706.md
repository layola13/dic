词源：
ocean (n.)  from Old French ocean, occean "ocean," from Latin Oceanus, the great outer sea encompassing the world (as distinguished from the Mediterranean), also the god of this sea, from Greek Okeanos (genitive Okeanou), name of the god of the river that encircles the earth and also of the earth-encircling river itself, of unknown origin.

-logy from Greek -logia "speaking, discourse," related to legein "to speak."  Used in forming the names of sciences, especially those dealing with abstract entities.
