Alzheimer's: /ˈɔːltshaɪmərz/ | n. | 阿尔茨海默病；老年痴呆症 | A progressive mental deterioration that can occur in middle or old age, due to generalized degeneration of the brain. It is the most common cause of premature senility. | My grandmother suffers from Alzheimer's disease. 我的祖母患有阿尔茨海默病。| Synonyms: dementia, senility | Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for a disease, but one might consider "mental acuity," "cognitive health") | Noun


Etymology: Named after Alois Alzheimer, a German psychiatrist who first described the disease in 1906.  Derived from the proper noun "Alzheimer" + the possessive suffix "-s."
