ETYMOLOGY->
From flax + -y.  Flax comes from Old English *fleax, from Proto-West Germanic *flahs, from Proto-Germanic *flahsaz, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *pleḱ- (“to plait, weave”). The suffix -y is a common adjectival suffix meaning "characterized by" or "full of".
