numismatist:/njuːˈmɪzmətɪst/| n.|钱币学家，钱币收藏家|A numismatist is a person who studies or collects coins, medals, and paper money.|例句：The numismatist displayed his rare coin collection at the museum.  这位钱币学家在博物馆里展示了他珍稀的钱币收藏。

例句：She became a numismatist after inheriting her grandfather's coin collection.  她继承了祖父的钱币收藏后成了一名钱币收藏家。|近义词：coin collector, philatelist (for stamps)|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *numismate*, from Latin *numisma* ("coin"), from Ancient Greek *νόμισμα* (*nómisma*, "current coin; usage, custom, law"), from *νομίζω* (*nomízō*, "hold as customary, use customarily, practice, be in the habit of, be wont; hold as a current view, believe, think, deem; consider right, make current, give currency to, introduce the custom of; establish by law, enact, decree"), from *νόμος* (*nómos*, "usage, custom, law").
