cajolements:/kəˈdʒoʊlmənts/
|n.|
|劝诱；哄骗；甜言蜜语|
|The act of persuading someone to do something they might not otherwise do, by flattery or gentle urging.  It usually implies a degree of insincerity or manipulation.|
|He eventually agreed to help, after much cajolement.  他经过一番劝诱后最终同意帮忙。|
|She used all her cajolements to get him to agree. 她用尽了所有的甜言蜜语让他同意。|
|近义词: blandishments, coaxing, persuasion, flattery, wheedling|
|反义词: coercion, threats, intimidation, force|
|词性: noun|


cajole:/kəˈdʒoʊl/
