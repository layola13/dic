hold court:/hoʊld kɔːrt/
verb phrase
主持；主宰；长时间侃侃而谈
To hold court means to talk at length to a group of people who listen attentively.  It suggests a dominant speaker and a receptive audience.
The professor held court in the coffee shop, surrounded by admiring students. 教授在咖啡店里滔滔不绝地讲着，周围都是崇拜他的学生。

The CEO held court in the boardroom, outlining his vision for the company's future. 首席执行官在会议室里主持会议，概述了他对公司未来的展望。

Synonyms: preside, dominate, lecture, expound, pontificate
Antonyms: listen, follow, be quiet, be silent
