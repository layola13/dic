blenchingly:/ˈblɛntʃɪŋli/
adverb
令人畏缩地，令人胆怯地
in a way that suggests someone is frightened and shocked, so that they become pale
She blenchingly described the horrific accident. 她脸色苍白地描述了那场可怕的事故。
Synonyms: fearfully, timidly, apprehensively
Antonyms: bravely, boldly, courageously
词性: 副词


Etymology:
From blanch + -ing + -ly.  Blanch comes from Old French blanchir "to whiten, make pale," from blanc "white."
