gimmickery's:/ˈɡɪmɪkəriːz/ | n. |  噱头，花招，伎俩 |  The possessive form of "gimmickry," referring to the tricks, gadgets, or devices used to attract attention or interest, often in a deceptive or insincere way.  It highlights the ownership or association of these gimmicks with someone or something. | The magician's gimmickery's success depended on misdirection.  魔术师的伎俩的成功取决于误导。| synonyms:  tricks, contrivances, shenanigans, ruses, subterfuges | antonyms:  honesty, sincerity, authenticity, genuineness | noun


Etymology:  From "gimmick" + "-ery," a suffix forming nouns denoting a trade, art, or practice.  "Gimmick" itself is of uncertain origin, possibly related to "magic" or "game."
