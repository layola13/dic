cut-away:/ˈkʌtəˌweɪ (US), ˈkʌtəweɪ (UK)| adj. n.|剖开的，剖面图；燕尾服|A cutaway is a garment, especially a tailcoat, cut so as to slope from the front waistline toward the back; A cutaway is also something, such as a drawing or model, that has part of its outside removed to show the inside.|The museum displayed a cutaway of a jet engine. 博物館展出了一个喷气发动机的剖面模型。|近义词：cross-section, sectional|反义词: whole, complete|词性: adjective, noun


cut-away:/ˈkʌtəˌweɪ (US), ˈkʌtəweɪ (UK)| n.|插播，切换镜头|A brief scene in a film, television programme, etc. that interrupts the main action to show something else.|The documentary included a cutaway to footage of the president's speech.  这部纪录片插播了一段总统讲话的片段。|近义词：insert, interlude|反义词: main sequence|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb phrase "cut away," meaning to remove a part by cutting.  The noun usage developed from this, referring to the thing that has been cut away.
