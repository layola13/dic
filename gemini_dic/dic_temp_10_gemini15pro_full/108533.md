lazaroni:/ˌlɑːtsəˈroʊni/| n.|乞丐，游民|A derogatory term for the lower classes, especially beggars or homeless people.|The lazaroni of Naples were known for their poverty and desperation. 那不勒斯的乞丐以他们的贫困和绝望而闻名。|Synonyms: beggars, paupers, vagrants|Antonyms: aristocracy, wealthy|noun

lazaroni:/ˌlɑːtsəˈroʊni/| n.|游民，无业游民|A term for people who are unemployed and without a fixed home, often living on the streets.|The economic downturn led to an increase in the number of lazaroni in the city. 经济衰退导致城市游民数量增加。|Synonyms: homeless, vagrants, tramps|Antonyms: employed, housed, settled|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *lazzaroni*, plural of *lazzaro*, meaning "leper," from Lazarus, the beggar in the biblical parable.  The term originally referred to a charitable institution in Naples for the poor and sick. Over time, it became a derogatory term for beggars and the lower classes.
