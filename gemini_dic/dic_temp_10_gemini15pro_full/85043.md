harbour:/ˈhɑːrbər/ (US) /ˈhɑːbə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.| 港口，避难所，窝藏|A place on the coast where ships may moor in shelter, especially one protected from rough water by piers, jetties, and other artificial structures.  A place of shelter.|例句：The ship sailed into the harbour. 船驶进了港口。|近义词：port, haven, shelter, refuge|反义词：exposure|词性:名词,动词

harbour:/ˈhɑːrbər/ (US) /ˈhɑːbə(r)/ (UK)| v.| 窝藏，怀有（感情）|To give shelter or refuge to. To keep (a thought or feeling, typically a negative one) in one's mind, especially secretly.|例句：She harboured a secret desire to be a writer. 她心中暗藏着想成为作家的愿望。|近义词：shelter, conceal, cherish, entertain|反义词：expel, banish|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *herebeorg, from here "army" + beorg "protection, shelter."  Cognate with Old Norse herbergi "lodging."  The meaning shifted from "army shelter" to "shelter for ships."
