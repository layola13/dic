extravagation: /ɪkˌstrævəˈɡeɪʃən/ | n. | 挥霍；过度；溢出；逸出 | An instance of spending money excessively or going beyond reasonable limits; an escape of blood or other fluid from a vessel into the tissues. |  The billionaire's extravagant lifestyle included a private jet and a yacht.  这位亿万富翁的奢侈生活包括私人飞机和游艇。

|  The doctor was concerned about the extravasation of the IV fluid into the patient's arm.  医生担心静脉输液溢出到病人的手臂里。 | 近义词：excess, lavishness, prodigality, effusion | 反义词：economy, thrift, moderation, containment | 词性: noun


extravagate: /ɪkˈstrævəɡeɪt/ | v. | 挥霍；过度；溢出；逸出（血液等） | To spend money excessively or go beyond reasonable limits; (of blood or other fluid) to escape from a vessel into the tissues. | The company extravagated on its Christmas party, spending thousands of dollars on decorations.  公司在圣诞派对上挥霍无度，花了数千美元用于装饰。

| Blood extravagated into the surrounding tissue after the injury. 受伤后血液溢出到周围组织。 | 近义词：splurge, squander, overspend | 反义词：save, economize, conserve | 词性: verb



ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin extravasationem, noun of action from past-participle stem of extravasare "to force out of the veins," from extra- "outside" + vas "vessel."
