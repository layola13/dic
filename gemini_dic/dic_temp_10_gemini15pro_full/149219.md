oxymoronic:/ˌɒksɪməˈrɒnɪk/ /ˌɑːksɪməˈrɑːnɪk/| adj.|矛盾修饰法的，包含矛盾的|Containing or seeming to contain a contradiction in terms.|Her oxymoronic statement about "silent screams" intrigued the audience.|近义词:contradictory, paradoxical, ironic|反义词:consistent, harmonious, logical|词性:adjective

oxymoron:/ˌɒksɪˈmɔːrɒn/ /ˌɑːksɪˈmɔːrɑːn/| n.|矛盾修饰法，逆喻|A figure of speech in which apparently contradictory terms appear in conjunction.| "Jumbo shrimp" is a classic oxymoron.|近义词:contradiction, paradox, antithesis|反义词:tautology, pleonasm|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *oxymōrum*, from Ancient Greek ὀξύμωρον (oxýmōron, “pointedly foolish”), from ὀξύς (oxús, “sharp, keen”) + μωρός (mōrós, “foolish, dull”).
