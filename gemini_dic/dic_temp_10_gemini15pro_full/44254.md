croftland: /ˈkrɒftlænd/ (UK) /ˈkrɑːftlænd/ (US)| n. |小块耕地，小农田|A small piece of arable land, usually attached to a cottage or small farm.  It's typically used for growing crops or keeping a few animals.|The croftland provided enough vegetables for the family.|Synonyms: smallholding, paddock, allotment|Antonyms: estate, plantation|noun
