differentiabilities:/ˌdɪfərɛnʃiəˈbɪlətiz/| n. |可微性|The mathematical property of a function being differentiable, meaning it has a derivative at every point in its domain.  It refers to the existence and properties of derivatives of functions, especially in calculus.|The differentiabilities of this function are complex and require further investigation.|近义词：derivability, smoothness|反义词: non-differentiability|词性:noun

differentiable:/ˌdɪfərɛnʃiəbl/| adj. |可微的|Able to be differentiated; having a derivative.|The function f(x) = x^2 is differentiable everywhere.|近义词：derivable|反义词: non-differentiable, non-smooth|词性:adjective
