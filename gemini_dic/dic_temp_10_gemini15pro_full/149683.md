pajock:/ˈpeɪdʒɒk/ | n. | 花花公子，纨绔子弟 | A vain and ostentatious man who gives exaggerated attention to his clothes and appearance. | He was known as a pajock around town, always flaunting his expensive suits and sports cars. 他在这个镇上被称为花花公子，总是炫耀他昂贵的西装和跑车。 | synonyms: dandy, fop, coxcomb | antonyms: modest man, unassuming person | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From obsolete French *pajocke, probably from Italian *pajocco, related to pazzo (“fool”). Ultimately from Latin *patior (“to suffer, endure”).
