backdraught: /ˈbækdrɑːft/ (UK) /ˈbækdræft/ (US)| n. |逆火; 回燃;  |A sudden rush of air or smoke into a room or other confined space, caused by a change in pressure, often following an explosion or fire.  A reverse flow of air, gases, or smoke.| The firefighters were injured by a backdraught. 消防员被逆火烧伤。| The sudden opening of the door caused a backdraught, blowing smoke back into the room. 突然打开门导致回燃，烟雾被吹回房间。| synonyms: backdraft, blowback | antonyms: updraft | noun
