decumbently:/dɪˈkʌmbəntli/
adverb
仰卧地，伏卧地
lying down, especially in a position of comfort or rest
The cat lay decumbently on the sofa, basking in the sunlight.  这只猫舒服地趴在沙发上，沐浴在阳光下。
Synonyms:  prostrate, recumbent, supine, prone
Antonyms:  upright, erect, standing, vertical
词性: 副词


decumbent:/dɪˈkʌmbənt/
adjective
仰卧的，伏卧的; (植物)匍匐的，蔓生的
lying down, especially in a position of comfort or rest; (of a plant) trailing or creeping along the ground but with tips ascending
The weary traveler assumed a decumbent posture on the soft grass.  疲惫的旅行者在柔软的草地上躺了下来。
The decumbent stems of the plant spread across the garden.  这种植物匍匐的茎蔓遍了整个花园。
Synonyms:  prostrate, recumbent, supine, prone (for lying down); trailing, creeping (for plants)
Antonyms:  upright, erect, standing, vertical (for posture); erect, ascending (for plants)
词性: 形容词

词源:  From Latin decumbens, present participle of decumbere "to lie down," from de- "down" + -cumbere "to lie."
