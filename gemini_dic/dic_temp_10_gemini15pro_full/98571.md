institutional:/ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl/ | adj. |制度的；机构的；收容机构的| Of, relating to, or characteristic of an institution.  Established as or constituting a convention or custom.  Confined to an institution, especially a hospital or prison.|
例句：The report criticized the institutional failings that led to the crisis.  这份报告批评了导致这场危机的制度缺陷。
例句：She spent many years in institutional care. 她在收容机构待了很多年。
近义词：organizational, established, conventional, customary, official, formal |
反义词：individual, personal, informal, unconventional |
词性：adjective


institutional:/ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl/ | n. |机构投资者|An organization, especially one dedicated to education, public service, or culture.|
例句：Large institutional investors can influence the stock market. 大型机构投资者可以影响股票市场。
例句：He works for a large financial institution. 他在一家大型金融机构工作。
近义词：organization, establishment, corporation, institute |
反义词：individual |
词性：noun


词源: Late Middle English: from late Latin institutionalis, from Latin institutus ‘established’ (see institute).
