decoyed:/dɪˈkɔɪd/ |v. |诱骗，引诱|to lure into a trap or away from something, by means of a decoy|The hunters decoyed the ducks into the net. 猎人用诱饵把鸭子引到网里。| lured, enticed, trapped| freed, released| verb


decoyed:/dɪˈkɔɪd/ |adj.|被诱骗的|having been lured into a trap or away from something by a decoy|The decoyed ducks were unable to escape. 被诱骗的鸭子无法逃脱。|lured, trapped, ensnared|free, liberated, untouched| adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From the noun "decoy," which comes from Middle Dutch "de kooi" (the cage).  Originally referring to a cage or trap used for catching waterfowl, it evolved to mean the method of luring birds into the trap, and eventually broadened to encompass any act of luring into a trap.
