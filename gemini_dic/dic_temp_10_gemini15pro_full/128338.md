neutral-zones:/ˈnjuːtrəl zoʊnz/ |n.| 中立区；缓冲区 |An area between two opposing forces in which military activity is forbidden.  |例句：The two armies agreed to establish a neutral-zones between their front lines.  两军同意在前线之间建立一个中立区。

|例句：The demilitarized zone between North and South Korea serves as a neutral-zones.  南北韩之间的非军事区是一个中立区。|近义词：buffer zone, no-man's-land, demilitarized zone|反义词：war zone, battleground|词性:名词

neutral:/ˈnjuːtrəl/ |adj.| 中立的；中性的|Not supporting or helping either side in a conflict, disagreement, etc.; impartial.|例句：Switzerland remained neutral during World War II.  瑞士在二战期间保持中立。

|例句：The journalist tried to maintain a neutral tone in his reporting. 记者试图在报道中保持中性的语气。|近义词：impartial, unbiased, objective|反义词：biased, partisan, subjective|词性:形容词


zone:/zoʊn/ |n.| 地区，区域；地带|An area or stretch of land having a particular characteristic, purpose, or use, or subject to particular restrictions.|例句：The city is divided into residential, commercial, and industrial zones.  这个城市被划分为住宅区、商业区和工业区。

|例句：This is a no-parking zone.  这是一个禁止停车区。|近义词：area, region, district|反义词: (没有明确的反义词，取决于具体的zone)|词性:名词
