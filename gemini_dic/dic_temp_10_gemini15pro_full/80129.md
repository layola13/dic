gobsmack:/ˈɡɒbˌsmæk/ | v. n. | 使…大吃一惊；震惊；令人震惊的事 | To utterly astonish or overwhelm someone.  It can also be a noun referring to the state of being gobsmacked. | The news of the lottery win completely gobsmacked him.  他中了彩票的消息让他大吃一惊。 | synonyms: astonish, amaze, astound, stun, shock, flabbergast | antonyms: bore, tire, unexcite | verb, noun

gobsmacked:/ˌɡɒbˈsmækt/ | adj. | 震惊的，目瞪口呆的 | Utterly astonished or overwhelmed. |  I was absolutely gobsmacked when I heard the news. 我听到这个消息时完全惊呆了。| synonyms: astonished, amazed, astound, stunned, shocked, flabbergasted | antonyms: unfazed, unimpressed, bored | adjective

词源：
Gobsmack is British slang. It originated in the late 19th or early 20th century. "Gob" refers to the mouth, and "smack" refers to a sharp blow or hit.  So, the word literally means to hit someone in the mouth (figuratively) with surprise.
