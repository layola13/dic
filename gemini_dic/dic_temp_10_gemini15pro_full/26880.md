词源：cakewalk (n.) "a simple, easy task," 1889, American English, from cakewalk, a Black American dance contest in which a cake was awarded to the couple judged to have performed the most graceful steps, from cake + walk (n.).  The dance itself is attested from 1877. As an adjective from 1901.
