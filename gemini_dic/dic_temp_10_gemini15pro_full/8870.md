antifilibuster:/ˌæntiˈfɪlɪbʌstər/| adj. n.|反阻挠议事的，阻挠议事反对者|Someone or something that opposes or works to prevent a filibuster.  A filibuster is a tactic used in legislative bodies to delay or block a vote on a bill or other measure. |The senator was a staunch antifilibuster, believing that all votes should be brought to the floor.  这位参议员是坚定的阻挠议事反对者，认为所有投票都应该进行。|The group launched an antifilibuster campaign to pressure the Senate to change its rules. 该组织发起了一场反阻挠议事的运动，向参议院施压，要求其修改规则。|近义词：obstructionist, opponent of filibusters|反义词:filibusterer, proponent of filibusters|词性:adjective, noun


Etymology:
anti- (prefix meaning "against, opposed to") + filibuster (from Dutch vrijbuiter, meaning "freebooter," later applied to pirates and then to obstructionist legislators)
