intermittently:/ˌɪntərˈmɪtəntli/| adv.|间歇地，断断续续地|occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous or steady.|The rain fell intermittently throughout the day.  一整天都断断续续地下着雨。|Synonyms: sporadically, occasionally, periodically, from time to time|Antonyms: continuously, constantly, steadily|adverb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *intermittent- (stem of intermittens), present participle of intermittere "to discontinue, intermit," from Latin inter- "between" + mittere "to send, let go."
