词源：duplex (n.) 1540s, "twofold, double," from Latin duplex "twofold, double," from duo "two" (from PIE root *dwo- "two") + -plex "-fold," from PIE root *plek- "to plait." Meaning "two-family house" is from 1924, American English.  The possessive form "duplex's" is simply the standard English possessive marker added to the noun "duplex."
