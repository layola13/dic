marginalization:/ˌmɑːrdʒɪnələˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 边缘化；排斥；忽视 | The process of treating a person, group, or concept as insignificant or peripheral. |  Many indigenous communities experience marginalization within the dominant society. 许多原住民社群在主流社会中遭受边缘化。| synonyms: exclusion, alienation, sidelining, ostracism | antonyms: inclusion, integration, acceptance, centralisation | noun

marginalize:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəlaɪz/ | v. | 使边缘化；排斥；忽视 | To treat (a person, group, or concept) as insignificant or peripheral. | The new policy marginalized the contributions of experienced teachers. 这项新政策边缘化了经验丰富的教师的贡献。| synonyms: exclude, alienate, sideline, ostracize | antonyms: include, integrate, accept, centralize | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From late 19th century: from marginal + -ize.  The word "marginal" comes from the Latin "marginalis," meaning "pertaining to a margin or border."
