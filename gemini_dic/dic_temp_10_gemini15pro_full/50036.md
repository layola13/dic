denotational:/ˌdiːnəʊˈteɪʃənəl/ |adj.|指称的，表示意义的|Relating to or concerned with the meaning of something, especially in logic or linguistics.  It focuses on the literal, explicit meaning of a word or expression, rather than its implied or associated meanings.|The denotational semantics of a programming language describes the meaning of its programs.|近义词：semantic, significative, indicative|反义词：connotative|词性: adjective
denotation:/ˌdiːnəʊˈteɪʃən/ |n.|指称，意义|The literal or primary meaning of a word, in contrast to the feelings or ideas that the word suggests.|The denotation of "home" is a place where one lives, but its connotation can include warmth and family.|近义词：meaning, signification, sense|反义词：connotation|词性: noun
denote:/dɪˈnəʊt/ |v.|表示，指示|Be a sign of; indicate.|A very high temperature often denotes a serious illness.|近义词：indicate, signify, represent, mean|反义词：connote|词性: verb
