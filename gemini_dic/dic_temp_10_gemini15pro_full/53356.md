disaffect:/ˌdɪsəˈfɛkt/ | vt. | 使疏远；使不满意；使不忠 | To cause (someone) to lose affection or loyalty; to alienate. |  The new policies disaffected many voters. 新政策使许多选民不满。

The constant criticism disaffected the team from their coach.  不断的批评使队员们与教练疏远了。| 近义词: alienate, estrange, antagonize | 反义词: reconcile, conciliate, appease | 词性: 动词

disaffected:/ˌdɪsəˈfɛktɪd/ | adj. | 不满的；不忠的 |  No longer satisfied or loyal; discontented. | Many disaffected voters stayed home on election day. 许多不满的选民在选举日呆在家里。

The disaffected soldiers plotted a mutiny.  心怀不满的士兵们策划了一场兵变。| 近义词: alienated, estranged, disgruntled | 反义词: loyal, contented, satisfied | 词性: 形容词

disaffection:/ˌdɪsəˈfɛkʃən/ | n. | 不满；不忠 |  The state or feeling of being disaffected; discontent. |  There is growing disaffection with the government. 对政府的不满情绪日益增长。

The disaffection among the workers led to a strike. 工人们的不满导致了罢工。| 近义词: alienation, estrangement, discontent | 反义词: loyalty, contentment, satisfaction | 词性: 名词
