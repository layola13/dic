maudlinwort:/ˈmɔːdlɪnwɜːt/ | n. |  醉鱼草;珍珠菜 | A plant of the genus Lysimachia, especially the yellow loosestrife (Lysimachia vulgaris). |  The banks of the river were thick with maudlinwort. 河岸上长满了醉鱼草。 | 近义词: yellow loosestrife, garden loosestrife | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for a plant name) | 词性: noun


Etymology:  "Maudlin" comes from Mary Magdalene, often depicted weeping in religious art.  The plant's drooping yellow flowers were associated with tears and thus with Mary Magdalene.  "Wort" is an Old English word meaning "plant" or "herb."
