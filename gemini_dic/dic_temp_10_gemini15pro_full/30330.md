censurer: /ˈsɛnʃərər/ | n. | 批评者，责难者 | A person who expresses strong disapproval or criticism. | The politician faced a barrage of questions from censurers after the scandal broke.  这位政治家在丑闻曝光后，面临来自批评者的一连串质问。|  Example:  He is a harsh censurer of the government's policies. 他是政府政策的严厉批评者。| Synonyms: critic, detractor, faultfinder | Antonyms: supporter, admirer, advocate | noun

censurer: /ˈsɛnʃərər/ | n. | 审查者，检查者 |  A person who examines books, films, etc., to remove anything considered offensive or harmful. |  In some countries, censurers control what can be published in newspapers. 在一些国家，审查者控制着报纸上可以出版的内容。| Example: The censurer deleted several scenes from the movie. 审查者从电影中删除了几个场景。| Synonyms: censor, examiner, inspector | Antonyms:  promoter, publisher | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English censurer, from Anglo-Norman censour, from Old French censeur, from Latin censor (“judge, critic, censor”), from censēre (“to assess, judge, give opinion”).
