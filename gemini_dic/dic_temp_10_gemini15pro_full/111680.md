lockdowns:/ˈlɒkdaʊnz/ (British) /ˈlɑːkdaʊnz/ (American)| n.|封锁，禁闭|The imposition of stringent restrictions on movement and activity within a community, usually in response to a public health crisis or other emergency.|例句：The city was placed under strict lockdowns to control the spread of the virus.  这座城市被置于严格的封锁之下以控制病毒的传播。

例句：Multiple lockdowns have impacted businesses and the economy. 多次封锁影响了企业和经济。|近义词：closures, shutdowns, quarantines, restrictions, curfews|反义词:reopening, lifting of restrictions|词性:名词

lockdown:/ˈlɒkdaʊn/ (British) /ˈlɑːkdaʊn/ (American)| n.|封锁，禁闭|The state of being in lockdown.|例句：The lockdown was difficult for many people. 封锁对很多人来说很困难。

例句：The prison was placed on lockdown after a riot. 骚乱发生后，监狱被封锁。|近义词：confinement, isolation|反义词:freedom, liberty|词性:名词

PHRASE->

词源：lock (锁) + down (向下)，最初指监狱等场所的紧急封锁状态。后来引申为对社区或区域的行动限制。
