overscruple: /ˌoʊvərˈskruːpəl/| v. | 过分顾虑；过于拘泥；|To hesitate or have moral qualms about doing something, often unnecessarily.| She overscrupled about taking the last piece of cake. 她对拿最后一块蛋糕感到过分顾虑。 |He overscrupled about accepting the gift, fearing it might be seen as a bribe. 他对接受礼物感到过分拘泥，担心这会被视为贿赂。| 近义词：hesitate, waver, vacillate, demur| 反义词： disregard, ignore,  |词性:动词

overscrupulous: /ˌoʊvərˈskruːpjələs/ | adj. | 过分谨慎的；过于拘泥的；| Excessively scrupulous; tending to have qualms about things others might consider unimportant. | An overscrupulous editor, she checked every fact multiple times. 作为一位过分谨慎的编辑，她反复核对了每一个事实。| He was so overscrupulous that he wouldn't even tell a white lie. 他过于拘泥，甚至连善意的谎言都不肯说。| 近义词：conscientious, meticulous, fastidious| 反义词：careless, reckless| 词性:形容词

词源: From over- + scruple (from Latin scrupulus ‘small sharp stone,’ figuratively ‘anxiety, uneasiness’.)
