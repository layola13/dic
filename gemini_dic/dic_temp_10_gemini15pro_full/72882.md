follow-the-leader:/ˈfɑːloʊ ðə ˈliːdər/ | n. |  领头羊游戏，盲从 | A children's game in which one child leads the others, who must copy every action.  Also used figuratively to describe blindly following someone or something. | The children played follow-the-leader in the park. 孩子们在公园里玩老鹰捉小鸡游戏。| The company's marketing strategy was simply follow-the-leader, copying whatever the biggest competitor did.  这家公司的营销策略只是盲目跟风，抄袭最大竞争对手的做法。 |  synonyms: mimicry, imitation, emulation | antonyms:  leadership, originality, independence | noun

follow-the-leader:/ˈfɑːloʊ ðə ˈliːdər/ | adj. |  模仿的，盲从的 |  Descriptive of an action or strategy that involves mimicking someone or something else, often without critical thinking. |  His follow-the-leader approach to investment led to significant losses. 他这种跟风的投资方式导致了巨大的损失。|  The government's follow-the-leader foreign policy was criticized for its lack of originality. 政府亦步亦趋的外交政策因缺乏独创性而受到批评。 | synonyms: imitative, unoriginal, derivative | antonyms: original, innovative, independent | adjective
