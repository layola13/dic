nutgall:/ˈnʌtˌɡɔːl/| n.|五倍子，没食子，橡树瘿|A hard abnormal growth on a plant, especially an oak tree, caused by a gall wasp, formerly used in making ink and dyes.|The ink was made from nutgalls and gum arabic.|Synonym: gall, oak apple|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps healthy plant tissue)|Noun
