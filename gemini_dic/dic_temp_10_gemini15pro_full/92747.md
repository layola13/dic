ichthyol:/ˈɪkθiɒl/ | n. | 鱼石脂，鱼石醇 | A dark brown, viscous liquid with a strong odor, obtained by the destructive distillation of bituminous schists containing fossil fish remains. Used as an antiseptic and in ointments for skin diseases. |  The doctor prescribed ichthyol ointment for her eczema. 医生给她开了鱼石脂软膏治疗湿疹。| synonym:  bituminosulfonate | antonym: None | noun
