contumely: /ˈkɒntjʊmɪli/ (UK) /ˈkɑːntʃəˌmeli/ (US)| n.| 侮辱；傲慢；无礼|  Rudeness or insulting behavior that causes shame or disgrace.  It is characterized by arrogance and disdain. |  He endured years of contumely and abuse.  他忍受了多年的侮辱和虐待。|Synonyms: insolence, disdain, scorn, abuse, disrespect, contempt,  |Antonyms: respect, reverence, honor, praise, admiration| noun


contumelious: /ˌkɒntjʊˈmiːliəs/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntʃəˈmiːliəs/ (US)| adj.| 侮辱的；傲慢的；无礼的 | Showing or expressing contempt or disdain; insolent. |  Her contumelious remarks deeply offended him.  她傲慢无礼的言辞深深地冒犯了他。|Synonyms: insolent, disdainful, scornful, abusive, disrespectful, contemptuous |Antonyms: respectful, reverent, honorable, praising, admiring| adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contumelia, from Latin contumelia (“insult, affront”), from con- + tumeō (“to swell”).  Originally meaning "swelling, puffiness," the sense evolved to signify the "swelling of pride" that leads to insulting behavior.
