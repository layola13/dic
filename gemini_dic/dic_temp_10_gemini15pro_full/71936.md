flickermouse:/ˈflɪkərmˌaʊs/ | n. | 蝙蝠 | A bat, particularly one that flies erratically, like a flickering flame.  This word is considered archaic or dialectal and rarely used in modern English. | The old man described seeing a flickermouse flitting through the twilight. 老人描述看到一只蝙蝠在暮色中飞来飞去。| 近义词: bat | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for an animal) | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->

From 'flicker' (to move in a quick, unsteady way) + 'mouse' (due to its small, furry body and nocturnal habits).  The term likely originated from observing the bat's flight pattern, which can appear erratic and flickering in dim light.
