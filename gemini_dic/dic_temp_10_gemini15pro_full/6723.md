analogue:/ˈænəlɒɡ (UK), ˈænəlɔːɡ (US)| n. adj.|类似物；模拟的|Something that is similar to something else, especially in function or purpose.  Relating to or using signals or information represented by a continuously variable physical quantity such as spatial position or voltage.|例句：The teacher used the analogue of a family to explain the organization of the company. 老师用家庭的比喻来解释公司的组织结构。|近义词：equivalent, parallel, similar, comparable|反义词：digital, different|词性: noun, adjective
analogue:/ˈænəlɒɡ (UK), ˈænəlɔːɡ (US)| adj.|模拟的|Relating to or using signals or information represented by a continuously variable physical quantity such as spatial position or voltage.|例句：He still uses an analogue watch. 他仍然使用指针式手表。|近义词：non-digital|反义词：digital|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *analogus, from Ancient Greek ἀνάλογος (análogos, “proportionate”), from ἀνά (aná, “up”) + λόγος (lógos, “ratio”).
