immolation:/ˌɪməˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 牺牲，祭献；毁灭 | The act of sacrificing something, especially by burning; destruction. | The ritual immolation of the lamb was a central part of the ancient ceremony.  古代仪式上的核心部分是羔羊的祭祀。|  The monk's self-immolation was a protest against the war.  僧侣的自焚是对战争的抗议。| synonyms: sacrifice, offering, holocaust, destruction | antonyms: preservation, salvation | noun

immolate:/ˈɪməleɪt/ | v. | 牺牲，祭献；毁灭 | To kill or destroy, especially by burning, as a sacrifice. | The ancient pagans would immolate animals to appease their gods. 古代异教徒会焚烧动物来安抚他们的神。|  The dictator immolated his enemies, both real and imagined.  独裁者杀害了他的敌人，包括真实的和想象的。| synonyms: sacrifice, kill, destroy | antonyms: save, preserve | verb
