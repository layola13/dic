anachoret:/ˈænəkərˌaɪt/ /ˈænəkəˌrɛt/| n.|隐士，遁世者|A person who withdraws from society for religious or spiritual reasons; a hermit.|The ancient anachoret lived in a cave, subsisting on berries and roots.|近义词：hermit, recluse, ascetic|反义词：socialite, extrovert|词性：名词
Etymology: From Middle English *anachorete*, from Old French *anachorete*, from Late Latin *anachorēta*, from Ancient Greek *ἀναχωρητής* (*anakhōrētḗs*, “one who withdraws, hermit”), from *ἀναχωρέω* (*anakhōréō*, “I withdraw, retire”), from *ἀνά* (*aná*, “up, back”) + *χωρέω* (*khōréō*, “I give way, withdraw”).
