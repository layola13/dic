demurred: /dɪˈmɜːrd/ | v. | 反对；提出异议；犹豫 | to express disagreement or hesitation; to object |  She demurred at the idea of working late on Friday. 她反对星期五晚上加班。| synonyms: object, protest, hesitate, refuse | antonyms: agree, accept, consent, comply | verb

demurred: /dɪˈmɜːrd/ | adj. (obsolete) | reserved, modest | shy or modest |  She gave a demurred smile. 她腼腆地笑了笑。 | synonyms: shy, modest, reserved | antonyms: bold, forward, immodest | adjective (This usage is now obsolete and rarely encountered)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *demorer (“to linger, tarry”), from Latin dēmorārī (“to delay, linger, tarry”), from dē (“down, away”) + morārī (“to delay, linger, tarry”).
