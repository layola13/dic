corinthians: /kəˈrɪnθiənz/ | n. | 哥林多人 | People from the ancient Greek city of Corinth, or members of the Christian church there to whom Paul addressed two epistles.  Also refers to either of the two books of the New Testament (First and Second Corinthians) containing the epistles of Paul to the church at Corinth. |  The Corinthians were known for their wealth and worldliness.  哥林多人以其财富和世俗而闻名。| The pastor preached a sermon on the letters to the Corinthians. 牧师就保罗写给哥林多人的书信布道。 | synonyms:  Corinthian Christians, recipients of Paul's letters | antonyms: N/A | noun
