gannets:/ˈɡænɪts/ | n. |塘鹅；鰹鸟|Large seabirds that plunge-dive into the water to catch fish. |例句：A flock of gannets circled overhead, searching for fish. 一群塘鹅在头顶盘旋，寻找鱼群。

例句：The gannets plummeted into the sea with incredible speed and accuracy. 塘鹅以惊人的速度和准确度冲入大海。|近义词：seabirds, seafowl |反义词: (无直接反义词，可考虑land birds 陆地鸟类) |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *gani, akin to Old High German gano "gosling."  The shift in meaning from "gosling" to "gannet" is unclear, possibly influenced by the bird's white plumage.
