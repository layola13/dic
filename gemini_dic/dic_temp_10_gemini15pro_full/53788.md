discomposedness:/ˌdɪskəmˈpoʊzɪdnəs/
|n.|
|不安；慌乱；失态|
|The state of being discomposed; agitation; perturbation.|
|Her discomposedness was evident in her trembling hands. 她的双手颤抖着，显得很不安。|
|His discomposedness at the unexpected news was palpable.  他听到这个意外的消息后，明显的慌乱了起来。|
|anxiety, uneasiness, perturbation, agitation|
|calmness, composure, tranquility|
|noun|


discompose:/ˌdɪskəmˈpoʊz/
|v.|
|使不安；使慌乱；扰乱|
|To disturb the composure of; agitate; ruffle.|
|The loud noise discomposed the speaker. 巨大的噪音使演讲者感到不安。|
|The sudden change of plans discomposed her. 突然的计划改变让她感到慌乱。|
|unsettle, disturb, ruffle, agitate|
|calm, soothe, compose|
|verb|


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation, reversal) + compose (to make calm, to settle).  Ultimately from Latin componere (to put together).
