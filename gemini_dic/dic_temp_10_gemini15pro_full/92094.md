hyperresonances:/ˌhaɪpərˈrɛzənənsɪz/| n. |过度共振，亢进音|A sound produced during percussion of a body part that indicates an abnormal amount of air in the underlying area.|On percussion of the chest, hyperresonances were noted, suggesting a pneumothorax.|近义词：hyperresonance (singular), tympany|反义词：dullness, flatness|词性:Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hyper-" (Greek: ὑπέρ, hupér, "over, above") + "resonance" (from Latin resonantia, "echo")
