clangour:/ˈklæŋər/ (US) /ˈklæŋɡə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 铿锵声，喧嚣声，叮当声|A loud ringing metallic sound, like that produced by clashing metal objects; a din.|The clangour of the bells echoed through the square. 钟声的铿锵声回荡在广场上。|Synonyms: din, clamor, racket, uproar, cacophony|Antonyms: silence, quiet, hush, stillness|noun


clangorous:/ˈklæŋərəs/ (US) /ˈklæŋɡərəs/ (UK)| adj.| 铿锵的，喧嚣的|Characterized by or producing a clangour.|The clangorous bells announced the start of the festival. 铿锵的钟声宣告了节日的开始。|Synonyms: noisy, loud, resounding, resonant|Antonyms: quiet, silent, hushed, still|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *clangour*, from Old French *clangor*, from Latin *clangōr*, from *clangō* (“to sound, resound, clang”).
