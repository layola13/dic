backdoor:/ˌbækˈdɔːr/ /ˌbækˈdɔr/| n. adj.|后门，秘密通道；不正当手段|A method of bypassing security controls, often secretly and without authorization.  A metaphorical backdoor can also refer to a covert or indirect method of achieving something.|The hackers gained access to the system through a backdoor.|Synonyms: loophole, secret entrance, unauthorized access, covert means|Antonyms: front door, main entrance, authorized access|noun

backdoor:/ˌbækˈdɔːr/ /ˌbækˈdɔr/| adj.|秘密的，不正当的|Relating to or involving a backdoor, typically in computing or security contexts.|The company discovered a backdoor program installed on their servers.|Synonyms: clandestine, covert, surreptitious, unauthorized|Antonyms: overt, authorized, legitimate|adjective
