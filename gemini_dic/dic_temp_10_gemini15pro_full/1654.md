acknowledged:/əkˈnɒlɪdʒd/ /əkˈnɑːlɪdʒd/
verb|承认，确认，答谢|To accept or admit the truth or existence of something.  To express gratitude or appreciation for something.|She acknowledged his presence with a nod. 她点点头，表示注意到他。
The company acknowledged the receipt of the complaint. 公司确认收到了投诉。|近义词：admit, recognize, accept, confirm, concede|反义词：deny, dispute, reject|词性:v.

acknowledged:/əkˈnɒlɪdʒd/ /əkˈnɑːlɪdʒd/
adjective|被承认的，公认的|Widely accepted or recognized.|He is an acknowledged expert in the field. 他是该领域的公认专家。
It is acknowledged that the climate is changing. 气候正在变化是公认的事实。|近义词：recognized, accepted, established, reputed|反义词：undisputed, unacknowledged|词性:adj.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *knowlechen*, from Old English *cnāwlǣcan* (“to acknowledge, confess, admit; get to know, recognize”), from Proto-Germanic *knāulejaną* (“to acknowledge”), from *knāwą* (“to know”) (from Proto-Indo-European *ǵneh₃-* (“to know”)).  Cognate with Dutch *kénnen* (“to know”), German *kennen* (“to know”).  The prefix *ac-* is from Latin *ad-* ("to").
