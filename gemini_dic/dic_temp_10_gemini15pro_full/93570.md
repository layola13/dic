imagist:/ˈɪmɪdʒɪst/| n.|意象派诗人；意象主义者|A member of a school of early 20th-century poets who favored precise imagery and clear, sharp language.|Ezra Pound was a leading figure among the Imagists.  埃兹拉·庞德是意象派诗人的领军人物。|Synonyms:  modernist, symbolist|Antonyms: traditionalist, classicist|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *imagiste*, from *image* (image)  + *-iste* (a suffix forming agent nouns). Ultimately from Latin *imago* (image).
