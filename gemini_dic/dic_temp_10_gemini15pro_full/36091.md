coelum: /ˈsɛləm/ (Classical Latin) | n. | 天空，天国，天堂 |  The word "coelum" is a Latin noun meaning "sky," "heaven," or "the heavens." It is the origin of several English words related to the sky or heaven, such as "celestial."  It was commonly used in classical Latin literature and scientific texts. |  "Deinde ex coelo delapsa, arbores in terra creverunt." (Then, having fallen from the sky/heaven, trees grew on the earth.) | Synonyms: caelum (variant spelling),  firmamentum (firmament) | Antonyms: terra (earth), infernus (hell, underworld) | noun
