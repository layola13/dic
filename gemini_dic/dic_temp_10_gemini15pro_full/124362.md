motivate:/ˈmoʊtɪveɪt/ (US) /ˈməʊtɪveɪt/ (UK)| v. | 激励；激发；使…有动机 | To provide (someone) with a motive for doing something. | The coach motivated the team to win the championship. 教练激励球队赢得了冠军赛。 |  His inspiring words motivated me to pursue my dreams. 他鼓舞人心的话激励我去追求我的梦想。 | inspire, encourage, stimulate | discourage, dissuade | verb

motivable:/ˈmoʊtɪveɪtəbl/ (US) /ˈməʊtɪveɪtəbl/ (UK)| adj. | 可激发的 | Capable of being motivated. |  The students are motivable and eager to learn. 这些学生很容易被激励，渴望学习。 | The team is highly motivable and responds well to positive reinforcement.  这个团队很容易被激励，对积极的强化反应良好。 |  inspirable,  encourageable | unmotivated, apathetic | adjective

motivation:/ˌmoʊtɪˈveɪʃn/ (US) /ˌməʊtɪˈveɪʃn/ (UK)| n. | 动机；积极性 | The reason or reasons one has for acting or behaving in a particular way. |  His motivation for learning English is to get a better job. 他学习英语的动机是为了找到一份更好的工作。 |  Lack of motivation is a common problem among students. 缺乏动力是学生中的一个常见问题。| inspiration, drive, incentive | apathy, indifference | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *motivatus, past participle of *motivare “to set in motion,” from Latin motus, past participle of movere "to move."
