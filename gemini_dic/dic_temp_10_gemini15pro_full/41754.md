corrupt:/kəˈrʌpt/ | adj. v. | 腐败的，堕落的；贿赂，腐蚀 | morally unsound or debased;  cause to become morally unsound or debased. | The corrupt official was sentenced to prison.  腐败的官员被判入狱。| 近义词：dishonest, crooked, contaminated, rotten | 反义词：honest, upright, uncorrupted, pure | 词性: adjective, verb
corrupt:/kəˈrʌpt/ | v. | 贿赂，腐蚀 |  cause to become morally unsound or debased;  destroy the integrity of (data, a text, etc.). | The company tried to corrupt the witness. 这家公司试图贿赂证人。 | 近义词：bribe, subvert, pervert | 反义词：purify, cleanse | 词性: verb

词源: From Middle English *corrupten*, from Old French *corrupt*, from Latin *corruptus*, past participle of *corrumpere* (“to break up, destroy, spoil, corrupt”), from *com-* (“together, with”) + *rumpere* (“to break”).
