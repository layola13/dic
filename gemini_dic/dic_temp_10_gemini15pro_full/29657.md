catfished:/ˈkætˌfɪʃt/| v. n. adj.|欺骗；网络钓鱼；用虚假身份诱骗 (v.)；网络钓鱼的人；被网络钓鱼欺骗的人 (n.)；被网络钓鱼的 (adj.)|To lure someone into a relationship by means of a fictional online persona.  Creating a fake identity online to trick someone into an emotional or romantic relationship.|例句：He catfished her for months, pretending to be a successful lawyer.  他假扮成一个成功的律师，欺骗了她好几个月。

例句：She was catfished by a man who stole photos from a model's Instagram account. 她被一个男人骗了，这个男人从一个模特的Instagram账户上偷了照片。

近义词：deceive, trick, mislead, dupe|反义词:be honest, be truthful|词性:verb (primarily), also noun and adjective


catfish:/ˈkætˌfɪʃ/| n.|鲶鱼|A type of fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth.|例句：We caught a huge catfish in the river. 我们在河里抓到了一条巨大的鲶鱼。

例句：Catfish are bottom-feeders, scavenging for food in the mud. 鲶鱼是底栖动物，在泥里寻找食物。

近义词：bottom-feeder (figuratively for the online deception sense)|反义词:N/A|词性:noun
