mesmerizers:/ˈmɛzməˌraɪzərz/| n. |催眠师；令人着迷的人或物|People or things that mesmerize; those who hypnotize.|The stage magician was a true mesmerizer, captivating the audience with his illusions.  舞台魔术师是一位真正的催眠大师，用他的幻觉吸引了观众。|近义词：hypnotists, charmers, fascinators|反义词：bores, dullards|Noun

Etymology: From the verb "mesmerize," which comes from the name of Franz Mesmer (1734-1815), an Austrian physician who developed a theory of "animal magnetism" and practiced hypnotism.
