multiplicatively:/ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪtɪvli/
| adv. |乘法地，倍数地|In a multiplicative manner; by multiplication.  Refers to a relationship where changes are proportional and based on multiplication, not addition.|The bacteria population grew multiplicatively, doubling every hour.|近义词: exponentially|反义词: additively|词性:副词
词源: From "multiplicative" + "-ly". "Multiplicative" comes from Late Latin "multiplicativus", from "multiplicat-" (past participle stem of "multiplicare", "to multiply") + "-ive".  Ultimately from Latin "multus" (many) + "plicare" (to fold).
