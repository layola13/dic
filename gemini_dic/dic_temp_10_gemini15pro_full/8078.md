anthropomorphology:/ˌænθrəpəʊmɔːrˈfɒlədʒi/ | n. | 人类形态学 | The study of the human body's form and structure, often with a focus on variations and comparisons across populations or through time.  It can also encompass the study of human-like forms in other contexts, such as mythology or art. |  Anthropologists use anthropomorphology to understand human evolution and adaptation. 人类学家利用人类形态学来理解人类的进化和适应。

Anthropomorphology helps us understand the physical diversity of humans. 人类形态学帮助我们理解人类身体的多样性。 | 近义词：human morphology, physical anthropology | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but one could consider areas like physiology or behavioral science as contrasting focuses.) | 词性: noun
