flakily:/ˈfleɪkɪli/| adv.|成片地，薄片状地，不稳定地，古怪地|in a way that breaks or separates easily into small, thin pieces; unreliably; eccentrically|The pastry crumbled flakily.  这糕点酥脆易碎。|近义词：erratically, inconsistently, capriciously|反义词:reliably, consistently, stably|词性:副词

flaky:/ˈfleɪki/| adj.|成片的，薄片的，易剥落的，不稳定的，古怪的|tending to break or separate easily into small, thin pieces; unreliable; eccentric|The pie crust was flaky and delicious.  馅饼皮酥脆可口。|近义词：erratic, inconsistent, capricious|反义词:reliable, consistent, stable|词性:形容词

flake:/fleɪk/| n. vi. vt.|薄片，小片，雪花，古怪的人|a small, thin piece of something, especially one that has broken off a larger piece; to come off in flakes|Snowflakes fell gently. 雪花轻轻地飘落。|近义词：chip, shaving, fragment|反义词:lump, chunk|词性:名词，动词


词源:  From *flakō, from Proto-Germanic *flakō ("piece, fragment"). Cognate with Middle Low German flake (“flake, piece”), Middle Dutch vlacke (“spot, stain”), Old High German flah (“flat”), German flach (“flat”).
