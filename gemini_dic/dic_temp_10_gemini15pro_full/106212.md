knot:/nɑːt/ (US) /nɒt/ (UK)| n. v.|结，绳结；（速度单位）节；难事；（树木的）节疤|A knot is a fastening made by tying a piece of string, rope, cloth, etc. to itself or to another piece.|The sailor tied a knot in the rope. 水手在绳子上打了个结。|Synonyms: tie, bond, hitch|Antonyms: untie, loosen|noun

knot:/nɑːt/ (US) /nɒt/ (UK)| v.|打结；缠绕；使纠结|To knot something means to tie it in a knot.|She knotted the scarf around her neck. 她把围巾绕在脖子上打了个结。|Synonyms: tie, fasten, bind|Antonyms: untie, loosen|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cnotta (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *knuttan, from Proto-Indo-European *genut- (“knot”). Cognate with Old Norse knútr, Old Frisian knot, Middle Dutch cnotte, Dutch knot, Old High German chnodo, German Knoten.
