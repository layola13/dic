cotting:/ˈkɒtɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkɑːtɪŋ/| n.|小屋；棚屋；小别墅；村舍 (尤指渔民或猎人的)|A small, simple dwelling, typically one used by fishermen or hunters, often located in a remote area. |The old cotting by the lake was used as a fishing cabin. 湖边的小屋被用作钓鱼小屋。|Synonyms: cabin, hut, shed, cottage|Antonyms: mansion, palace|Noun


cotting:/ˈkɒtɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkɑːtɪŋ/| n.|棉屑；短纤维|Short fibers of cotton that are separated from the longer fibers during processing. |The cotting was used for making low-grade textiles. 棉屑被用来制作低档纺织品。|Synonyms: lint, fluff, fuzz|Antonyms: long-staple cotton|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English "cot" (small house, hut) + "-ing" (suffix forming nouns). Related to "cottage."
