hardball:/ˈhɑːrdbɔːl/| n. adj. adv.|强硬手段，强硬路线；(棒球)硬式棒球|Uncompromising and ruthless methods or dealings, especially in politics or business.  Also refers to a baseball made with a hard core.  The adjective form describes such tactics or the person employing them. The adverb describes how such tactics are employed.|例句：They're playing hardball in these negotiations. 他们在这些谈判中采取强硬态度。|近义词：aggressive, ruthless, uncompromising, no-nonsense|反义词:softball, conciliatory, compromising|词性: noun, adjective, adverb

hardball:/ˈhɑːrdbɔːl/| adj.|强硬的|Uncompromising and ruthless.|例句：He has a reputation for being a hardball negotiator. 他以强硬的谈判者而闻名。|近义词：tough, aggressive|反义词:soft, lenient|词性: adjective

hardball:/ˈhɑːrdbɔːl/| adv.|强硬地|In an uncompromising and ruthless manner.|例句：The company negotiated hardball to get the best deal.  公司强硬地谈判以获得最佳交易。|近义词：aggressively, ruthlessly|反义词:gently, softly|词性: adverb
