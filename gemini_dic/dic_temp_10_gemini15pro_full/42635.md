counterseal: /ˈkaʊntərˌsiːl/ (US), /ˈkaʊntəˌsiːl/ (UK)| n. vt.| 副印，加封，反签 | An additional seal, often impressed over another seal, especially for authentication or security.  A counterseal can also refer to the act of applying such a seal.| The document was countersealed by the notary public.  该文件经公证人加封。 | The king countersealed the treaty. 国王在条约上加盖了副印。 | synonyms: co-sign, endorse, ratify, authenticate | antonyms: invalidate, reject, repudiate | noun, verb
