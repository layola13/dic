inconstant:/ɪnˈkɑːnstənt/ | adj. | 易变的，反复无常的 | Not constant; changeable; fickle.  |  Her inconstant affections made it difficult to maintain a relationship. 她的反复无常的感情使得维持一段关系变得很困难。 | Example: The weather has been inconstant lately, shifting from sunshine to rain in a matter of minutes. 最近天气变化无常，几分钟内就从晴天转为雨天。 | Synonyms: fickle, capricious, erratic, mercurial, volatile | Antonyms: constant, steady, steadfast, unwavering | adjective

inconstant:/ɪnˈkɑːnstənt/ | n. |  反复无常的人 | A person who is fickle or changeable. | He was known as an inconstant, flitting from one interest to the next. 他以反复无常而闻名，兴趣爱好总是变化不定。| Example: The inconstants in the group made it difficult to reach a consensus.  群体中反复无常的人使得达成共识变得困难。 | Synonyms: waverer, shifter | Antonyms: stalwart, diehard | noun
