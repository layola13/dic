almahad:/ˌæl.məˈhæd/| n.|阿尔摩哈德|A member of a Berber Muslim dynasty that ruled in North Africa and southern Spain in the 12th and 13th centuries. |The Almohads built many impressive mosques and palaces.| 近义词:dynasty, rulers| 反义词:subjects, ruled| 词性:noun

Etymology: From Arabic الموحدون (al-Muwaḥḥidūn), meaning "the Unitarians" or "those who affirm the unity of God".
