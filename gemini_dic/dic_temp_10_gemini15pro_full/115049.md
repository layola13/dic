mandrake:/ˈmændreɪk/ | n. |曼德拉草；茄参|A Mediterranean plant (Mandragora officinarum) in the nightshade family, having a fleshy, often forked root somewhat resembling the human form. The root contains alkaloids that are narcotic and hallucinogenic and was used medicinally in ancient times. The plant figures prominently in superstition and folklore, being thought to shriek when pulled from the ground and to bring misfortune to those who hear it.| The witch used mandrake in her potion.  女巫在她的药水中使用了曼德拉草。| Synonyms: mandragora | Antonyms: N/A | noun
