mouthy:/ˈmaʊði/ (US), /ˈmaʊθi/ (UK)| adj.|多嘴的，爱顶嘴的，放肆的|Talkative in a rude or disrespectful way.  Given to back talk or insolence.|例句：The mouthy child was scolded by his teacher.  这个多嘴的孩子被老师责骂了。|例句：He's gotten very mouthy lately, always questioning my decisions. 他最近变得很爱顶嘴，总是质疑我的决定。|近义词：talkative, garrulous, impertinent, impudent, sassy|反义词：respectful, polite, quiet, reserved|词性:形容词

Etymology: From mouth + -y, meaning "characterized by." The suffix "-y"  forms adjectives meaning 'full of', 'characterized by', or 'like'.  The word "mouth" comes from Old English "mūþ,"  from Proto-Germanic "*munþaz,"  from Proto-Indo-European "*ment-," meaning "mouth, chin, jaw."
