diddles:/ˈdɪdəlz/| v. n.| 欺骗，骗取；摆弄；闲混| To cheat or swindle someone out of something, often a small amount of money. Also, to waste time or trifle with something.  It can also be a noun referring to the act of diddling or things of little value.|
例句：He diddled the old lady out of her savings. 他骗走了老太太的积蓄。
例句：Stop diddling around and get to work! 别闲混了，快去工作！|近义词：cheat, swindle, defraud, trifle, fiddle, dawdle|反义词：be honest, work diligently|词性:动词，名词
