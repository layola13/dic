hemming:/ˈhemɪŋ/ | n. v. |缝边，锁边; 包围，限制 |The act of folding and sewing the edge of a piece of cloth to prevent it from fraying; the act of surrounding or restricting someone or something. |She spent the afternoon hemming the curtains. 她一下午都在给窗帘缝边。|Synonyms: stitching, bordering, enclosing, confining |Antonyms: unraveling, releasing, freeing |noun

PHRASE->
hem in: 包围，限制  The soldiers hemmed the enemy in. 士兵们包围了敌人。
hem and haw: 支支吾吾 He hemmed and hawed before answering the question. 他在回答问题前支支吾吾。
hem stitch:  锁边缝 She used a hem stitch to finish the edge of the scarf. 她用锁边缝完成了围巾的边缘。
double hem: 双层卷边 The skirt had a double hem. 这条裙子有双层卷边。
blind hem:  暗缝边  She sewed a blind hem on the dress. 她在裙子上缝了一个暗缝边。


词源：From Old English *hemmian, from Proto-Germanic *hamjaną, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kem- (“to cover”).
