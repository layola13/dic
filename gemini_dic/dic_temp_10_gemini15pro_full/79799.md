glycerol:/ˈɡlɪsərɒl/ (UK) /ˈɡlɪsəˌrɔl/ (US)| n.|甘油；丙三醇|A sweet, syrupy liquid obtained from animal fats and oils or by the fermentation of glucose. It is used as a solvent, sweetener, and antifreeze and in making dynamite and cosmetics.|Glycerol is commonly used in skin care products.|Synonyms: glycerine, propanetriol|Antonyms: None|Noun

glycerine:/ˈɡlɪsəriːn/ (UK) /ˈɡlɪsəˌrin/ (US) |n.|甘油；丙三醇|Another name for glycerol, a sweet, viscous liquid.|Glycerine is a humectant, drawing moisture to the skin.|Synonyms: glycerol, propanetriol|Antonyms: None|Noun
