glicke:/ˈɡlɪkə/| n.|好运，幸运；幸福|A Yiddish word meaning "luck" or "fortune," often used to express a wish for good luck or to describe a fortunate event.|例句：May you have much glicke in your new venture.  祝你在新的事业中好运连连。

例句：He had the glicke to be in the right place at the right time. 他很幸运，在正确的时间出现在正确的地点。|近义词：luck, fortune, happiness, prosperity|反义词：misfortune, bad luck, hardship|词性:noun
Etymology: From Yiddish גליק (glik), from Middle High German gelücke (“luck, fortune, happiness”), from Old High German gilukki, from Proto-Germanic *ga-lukōn (“to close, lock, conclude successfully”).  Ultimately related to English "lock."
