against the grain:/əˈɡɛnst ðə ɡreɪn/
|idiomatic expression|违背意愿；格格不入；|Going against the grain means doing something that is difficult or unpleasant because it contradicts one's natural inclinations or beliefs.|Working on weekends goes against the grain for me. 周末工作违背我的意愿。|against one's nature, against one's better judgment|with the grain, naturally|phrase


Etymology:
"Grain" originally referred to the direction of the fibers in wood.  Working "with the grain" is easier, while working "against the grain" is more difficult.  This physical concept was extended metaphorically to refer to going against one's natural inclinations or the accepted way of doing things.
