mucilage:/ˈmjuːsɪlɪdʒ/| n.|胶水；黏液；植物黏质|A thick, sticky substance produced by plants or animals.  A gluey substance used for sticking things together.|The cactus stores water in its fleshy stems and mucilage. 仙人掌在其肉质茎和粘液中储存水分。|Synonyms: glue, adhesive, gum, paste|Antonyms: solvent|noun


mucilage:/ˈmjuːsɪlɪdʒ/| n.|胶水；黏液；植物黏质|A thick, sticky substance, often used as an adhesive, found in some plants.|Okra is known for its mucilage, which thickens gumbo. 秋葵以其粘液而闻名，这种粘液可以使秋葵浓稠。|Synonyms: slime, goo, gel|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider "dryness")|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mucilago (“mucus, mold”), from Latin mucus (“mucus, nasal discharge”) + -lago, from Latin -ligo.
