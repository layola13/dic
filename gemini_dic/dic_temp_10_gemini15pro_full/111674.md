lock-up: /ˈlɒkʌp/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkʌp/ (US)| n.  | 锁定，监禁，扣留；关押处 |A place where people are locked in, especially a prison or police station. Also, the act of locking something or someone up.  | The police took him to the lock-up for questioning. 警方把他带到拘留所审问。 |Synonyms: jail, prison, detention, confinement | Antonyms: release, freedom | noun


lock-up: /ˈlɒkʌp/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkʌp/ (US)| adj.  |  锁定的，封闭的 | Designed or intended to be locked.  |  A lock-up garage. 一个带锁的车库。 |Synonyms: secured, locked, fastened | Antonyms: open, unlocked | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrasal verb "lock up," combining "lock" (from Old English *loc, related to German Loch "hole, opening") and "up" (from Old English up).
