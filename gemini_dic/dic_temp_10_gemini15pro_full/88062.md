highjack:/ˈhaɪdʒæk/ (US) /ˈhaɪdʒæk/ (UK)| vt. n.|劫持，强占|To seize or commandeer (a vehicle, aircraft, or ship) in transit, especially by force. To take control of something and use it for one's own purposes, often without permission. |The terrorists highjacked the plane. 恐怖分子劫持了飞机。|Synonyms: hijack, seize, commandeer, usurp, appropriate|Antonyms: release, surrender, relinquish|verb
