discordance: /dɪˈskɔːrdəns/ (US), /dɪˈskɔːdəns/ (UK)| n.|不一致，不协调，不和谐|lack of agreement or harmony; inconsistency between two or more things.|There is a discordance between his words and his actions. 他的言行不一致。|synonyms: disagreement, conflict, disharmony, incongruity, variance, discrepancy|antonyms: agreement, harmony, concord, consistency|noun

discordant: /dɪˈskɔːrdənt/ (US), /dɪˈskɔːdənt/ (UK)| adj.|不一致的，不协调的，不和谐的|not in agreement or harmony; conflicting.|The discordant sounds of the construction site made it difficult to concentrate. 建筑工地不和谐的声音让人很难集中注意力。|synonyms: disagreeing, conflicting, disharmonious, incongruous, jarring, clashing|antonyms: agreeing, harmonious, concordant, consistent|adjective

Etymology: From Late Middle English *discordaunce*, from Old French *discordance*, from Latin *discordantia*, from *discordans* ("discordant"), present participle of *discordare* ("to disagree"), from *dis-* ("apart, away") + *cor*, *cordis* ("heart").
