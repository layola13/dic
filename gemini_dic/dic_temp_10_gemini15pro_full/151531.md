parsimony:/ˈpɑːrsɪməni/ | n. | 节俭，吝啬；简约性 | Extreme unwillingness to spend money or use resources.  The quality of being careful with money or resources. |  She was known for her parsimony, never wasting a penny. 她以节俭闻名，从不浪费一分钱。|  The design of the product reflects a parsimony of materials.  该产品的设计体现了材料的简约性。 |  frugality, thrift, economy | extravagance, prodigality, lavishness | noun

parsimonious:/ˌpɑːrsɪˈməʊniəs/ | adj. | 节俭的，吝啬的；简约的 | Unwilling to spend money or use resources; stingy or mean. | He is parsimonious in giving praise to his employees. 他很少表扬他的员工。 | The company was parsimonious in its spending on research and development.  该公司在研发方面的支出很吝啬。 | frugal, thrifty, economical, miserly | extravagant, prodigal, lavish, generous | adjective
