abrenunciation: /əˌbrɛnʌnsiˈeɪʃən/
noun
放弃；脱离关系；宣布放弃
The act of renouncing or rejecting something, especially one's religion, country, or family.
He made a public abrenunciation of his former beliefs. 他公开宣布放弃他以前的信仰。

She made an abrenunciation of her claim to the throne. 她宣布放弃对王位的要求。

近义词: renunciation, rejection, repudiation, disclaimer, abandonment
反义词: acceptance, embrace, affirmation
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *abrenuntiatio, from Latin abrenuntiare ("to renounce, disown"), from ab- ("from, away from") + renunciare ("to bring back word, report, announce, proclaim").

There are not many common usages of "abrenunciation" in modern English. It's a formal and somewhat archaic word.  Here are a few examples demonstrating its use in different contexts:

Formal Declaration:

The king issued an abrenunciation of his claim to the conquered territories. = 国王宣布放弃对被征服领土的所有权。
The document contained a formal abrenunciation of all previous agreements. = 该文件包含正式放弃所有先前协议的声明。


Historical Context:

The abrenunciation of pagan gods was a key aspect of early Christianity. = 放弃异教神是早期基督教的一个关键方面。
His abrenunciation of his family name brought shame upon his ancestors. = 他放弃了家族姓氏，给他的祖先带来了耻辱。

Literary Context:

The protagonist's abrenunciation of his former life was a central theme of the novel. = 主人公放弃以前的生活是这部小说的中心主题。
The poet's abrenunciation of worldly pleasures reflected his spiritual journey. = 诗人对世俗享乐的放弃反映了他的精神之旅。



PHRASE->
Due to the infrequent use of "abrenunciation", it doesn't have many common phrases associated with it.  It's typically used standalone to describe the act of renouncing something.
