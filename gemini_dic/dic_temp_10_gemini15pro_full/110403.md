lik:/laɪk/ | v. | 喜欢；想要；愿意 | to find something agreeable or enjoyable; to wish for or want something | I like ice cream. 我喜欢冰淇淋。| Synonyms: enjoy, love, prefer, fancy | Antonyms: dislike, hate, detest | verb

lik:/laɪk/ | prep. | 像；如同 | similar to; resembling | He looks like his father. 他看起来像他的父亲。| Synonyms: similar to, resembling, akin to | Antonyms: unlike, dissimilar | preposition

lik:/laɪk/ | adj. | 相似的；相同的 | having the same characteristics or qualities | The two sisters have like minds. 这两姐妹想法相似。| Synonyms: similar, alike, identical | Antonyms: unlike, dissimilar, different | adjective

lik:/laɪk/ | n. | 相似的人或物；喜好；种类 | something that is similar; a preference; a type or kind | We'll never see his like again. 我们再也看不到像他这样的人了。 | Synonyms: counterpart, equivalent, preference | Antonyms: opposite, contrast | noun

lik:/laɪk/ | adv. | 很可能；大概 | probably; likely | It's like to rain this afternoon. 今天下午很可能会下雨。(This usage is more common in some dialects, especially Scottish and Northern English.) | Synonyms: probably, likely, possibly | Antonyms: unlikely, improbably | adverb

词源：From Old English *līc (adjective) "like, similar, equal," from Proto-Germanic *līkaz (source also of Old Saxon līk, Old Frisian līk, Dutch gelijk, Old High German līh, German gleich, Gothic leik), from PIE root *leig- "to bend, twist."  The verb developed from the adjective.  The preposition and adverb developed from the adjective and verb.  The noun developed from the adjective.
