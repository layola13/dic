bowdlerised: /ˈbaʊdləraɪzd/ | adj. |  删节的，净化过的 |  Edited to remove material considered offensive or inappropriate, especially sexual content.  Derived from Thomas Bowdler, who published an expurgated edition of Shakespeare's works in the 19th century. | The play was bowdlerised for television. 这部戏剧为了在电视上播放而被删改了。 |  censored, expurgated | uncensored, unexpurgated | adjective

bowdlerise: /ˈbaʊdləraɪz/ | vt. | 删节，净化 | To edit by omitting or modifying parts considered indelicate or offensive. |  They bowdlerised the book before publishing it. 他们在出版前删改了这本书。 | censor, expurgate | publish uncut | verb

bowdlerisation: /ˌbaʊdləraɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 删节，净化 | The act of bowdlerising something. |  The bowdlerisation of the text removed all references to sex and violence.  对文本的删减删除了所有关于性和暴力的内容。 | censorship, expurgation |  inclusion | noun
