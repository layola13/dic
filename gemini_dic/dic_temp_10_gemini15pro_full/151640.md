particularist: /pərˈtɪkjələrɪst/ /pɑːrˈtɪkjələrɪst/
n.| 特殊主义者；细致的人|A person who focuses on specific details or adheres to a particular set of rules or beliefs, often at the expense of broader considerations. |He was accused of being a particularist, more concerned with the details than the overall strategy. 他被指责是一个特殊主义者，更关注细节而不是整体战略。|stickler, formalist|generalist, universalist|noun
particularist: /pərˈtɪkjələrɪst/ /pɑːrˈtɪkjələrɪst/
adj.| 特殊主义的；细致的|Relating to particularism or a particularist. |Her particularist approach to the problem made it difficult to find a common solution. 她对这个问题的特殊主义方法使得很难找到一个共同的解决方案。|specific, meticulous|general, broad|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From "particular" + "-ist."  "Particular" comes from Late Middle English: from Old French particulier, from Latin particularis ‘of a part, private,’ from pars, part- ‘part.’  The suffix "-ist" forms agent nouns and denotes adherence to a doctrine or system.
