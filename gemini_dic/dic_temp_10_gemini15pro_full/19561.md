big-timer:/ˈbɪɡ ˈtaɪmər/| n.|大人物，要人；成功人士|A person of importance or influence, especially in a particular field or organization.  Someone who is successful and important.|He thinks he's a big-timer now that he's got a promotion.  他现在升职了，觉得自己是个大人物了。|Synonyms: VIP, heavyweight, big shot, high-roller, big wheel, bigwig, important person|Antonyms: nobody, small fry, unimportant person|noun

big-timer:/ˈbɪɡ ˈtaɪmər/| adj.|(informal) Important; influential.|Used informally to describe something related to or characteristic of a big-timer.|He made some big-timer deals in the real estate market. 他在房地产市场做了一些大买卖。|Synonyms: important, influential, high-powered|Antonyms: insignificant, unimportant, low-level|adjective
