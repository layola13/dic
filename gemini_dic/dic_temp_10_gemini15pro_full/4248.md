airplanes:/ˈɛərˌpleɪnz/ (US), /ˈɛəˌpleɪnz/ (UK)| n. |飞机|A powered flying vehicle with fixed wings and a weight greater than that of the air it displaces.|Many airplanes fly in and out of this airport every day. 每天都有很多飞机在这个机场起降。|近义词：aircraft, plane, aeroplane (UK)|反义词: N/A|noun

Etymology:
From aero- + plane.  "Aero" comes from Greek ἀήρ (aēr, “air”). "Plane" in this context refers to a surface, specifically a wing.
