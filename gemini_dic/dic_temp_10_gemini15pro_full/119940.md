milksopping:/ˈmɪlkˌsɑːpɪŋ/| adj.|乳臭未干的，懦弱的，幼稚的|Soaked or saturated with milk (literal); weak, unmanly, cowardly or effeminate (figurative).  The literal meaning is rarely used. It usually implies a lack of courage or strength of character.|He's such a milksopping coward, he wouldn't even stand up for himself. 他真是个乳臭未干的懦夫，连为自己辩护都不敢。|近义词：timid, cowardly, spineless, weak, puerile, immature|反义词：brave, courageous, strong, mature|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From "milk" + "sop", where "sop" refers to something dipped or soaked in liquid.  The figurative meaning developed from the association of milk with babies and therefore immaturity and weakness.
