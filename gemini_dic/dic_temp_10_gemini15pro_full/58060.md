dromon: /ˈdrɒmən/ | n. | 军舰，桨帆战舰 | A large, swift warship of the Byzantine Empire, propelled by both oars and sails. | The dromon was a formidable warship in its time, capable of both speed and maneuverability.  拜占庭时代的桨帆战舰在其时代是一艘令人生畏的战舰，兼具速度和机动性。| The emperor's dromon led the naval attack. 皇帝的桨帆战舰率领海军进攻。 | Near synonyms: warship, galley, bireme | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "merchant ship" as a contrasting vessel) | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Medieval Greek *δρόμων (drómōn), from Ancient Greek δρόμος (drómos, “running, course, race”), from the verb δραμεῖν (drameîn, “to run”).
