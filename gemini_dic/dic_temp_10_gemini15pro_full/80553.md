goodship:/ˈɡʊdˌʃɪp/| n.|美好事物，优秀品质；（古）美德，善良|Something positive or excellent, often referring to a quality or trait. Formerly, it referred to virtue or goodness.|His goodship was evident in his charitable work. 他的优秀品质体现在他的慈善工作中。|近义词：virtue, merit, excellence, kindness|反义词：badness, wickedness, evil|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gōdshipe*, from Old English *gōdscipe* (“goodness, virtue”), from *gōd* (“good”) + *-scipe* (suffix forming abstract nouns of quality or state). Cognate with Scots *gudschip* (“virtue, excellence”).
