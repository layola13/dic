myrtlewood: /ˈmɜːrtlˌwʊd/ (US) /ˈmɜːtlˌwʊd/ (UK)| n.|桃金娘木|The hard, close-grained wood of the California laurel (Umbellularia californica), used in cabinetmaking.|The craftsman used myrtlewood to create a beautiful bowl.|近义词:bay laurel wood, Oregon myrtle wood|反义词: (No direct antonym for a specific type of wood)|noun


Etymology:  From myrtle + wood.  Myrtle refers to the plant genus Myrtus, and by extension, other plants with fragrant evergreen foliage, like the California laurel.
