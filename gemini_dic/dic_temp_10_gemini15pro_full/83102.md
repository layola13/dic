gust:/ɡʌst/| n.|一阵强风；突发的一阵；（感情的）迸发|A sudden, strong rush of wind; a sudden burst of something, such as rain, sound, or emotion.|The wind blew in gusts, making the trees sway wildly.  风一阵阵地吹，使树木剧烈摇摆。|Synonyms: blast, squall, surge|Antonyms: lull, calm|noun

gust:/ɡʌst/| v.|（风）猛烈地吹|To blow strongly in gusts.|The wind gusted through the valley. 风一阵阵地吹过山谷。|Synonyms: blast, squall|Antonyms:  breeze, zephyr|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *gustr, related to Old English gūþ "war, battle" and gyst "guest". The connection between these meanings may lie in the sudden and violent nature of both a gust of wind and an attack in battle.
