chrematistics: /ˌkrɛmətɪˈstɪks/ | n. |  赚钱术; 理财术;  | The branch of political economy relating to the acquisition of wealth; the art of money-making.  It considers ways of acquiring goods and services, including production, but gives special attention to trade as a source of wealth. Chrematistics differs from economics in not limiting its considerations to activities that create utility, and differs from value theory in not considering how the pursuit of wealth affects human welfare. |  He studied chrematistics, hoping to learn the secrets of accumulating wealth. 他学习赚钱术，希望学习积累财富的秘诀。|  Synonyms: moneymaking, finance, economics (in older usage) | Antonyms: philanthropy, charity | noun
