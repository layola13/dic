clear-off:/ˈklɪər ɒf/ (British English), /ˈklɪr ɔːf/ (American English)
verb
离开；走开；消散
To leave a place or to make someone leave a place. Also, to become fine or bright.
"Clear off!" he shouted. “他大喊：‘滚开！’”
The rain clouds finally cleared off. 雨云终于消散了。
synonyms: go away, leave, depart, disperse, clear up
antonyms: arrive, stay, remain, gather, cloud over
词性: 动词

clear-off:/ˈklɪər ɒf/ (British English), /ˈklɪr ɔːf/ (American English)
noun
命令或要求某人离开
An instruction or request for someone to leave.
He gave them a clear-off. 他让他们滚蛋。
synonyms: dismissal, eviction, ejection
antonyms: invitation, welcome
词性: 名词

词源:  'Clear' comes from Old French 'cler' meaning 'bright, shining'. 'Off' is from Old English 'of' indicating separation or departure. The combination emphasizes a complete departure or clearing of a space.
