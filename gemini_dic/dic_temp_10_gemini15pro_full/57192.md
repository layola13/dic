down-line:/ˈdaʊnˌlaɪn/| adj. n.|下线的，下游的；下线，下游|A person or group that is at a lower level in an organization or system.  Relating to or involved in the later stages of a process or system.|例句：She has a large down-line of distributors working for her. 她有一个庞大的下线分销商团队为她工作。

例句：The down-line effects of this decision could be disastrous.  这项决定的下游影响可能是灾难性的。|近义词：subordinate, downstream, dependent|反义词:upstream, up-line|词性:形容词, 名词

downline:/ˈdaʊnˌlaɪn/| n.|下线|A group of distributors in a multi-level marketing organization who are below another distributor in the organization's hierarchy|例句：Building a strong downline is key to success in network marketing.  建立强大的下线是网络营销成功的关键。

例句：He recruited several new members into his downline. 他招募了几名新成员加入他的下线。|近义词：team, network, organization|反义词:upline|词性:名词
