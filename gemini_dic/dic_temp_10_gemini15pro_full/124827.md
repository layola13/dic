muckerish:/ˈmʌkərɪʃ/| adj.|邋遢的，不整洁的；卑鄙的，下流的|Characterized by dirt or filth; slovenly. Also, characterized by low, vulgar, or base behavior.|He had a muckerish appearance after working in the garden all day. 他在花园里工作了一整天，显得邋遢不堪。|近义词：dirty, filthy, slovenly, messy, vulgar, base, mean|反义词：clean, tidy, neat, respectable, noble|词性:形容词

mucker:/ˈmʌkər/| n.|清除粪便的人；卑鄙的人|A person who removes muck, especially dung; a person regarded as low, vulgar, or contemptible.|He called him a mucker for cheating on the test. 他骂他是卑鄙小人，因为他考试作弊. |近义词：scoundrel, rogue, villain|反义词：gentleman, hero|词性:名词

词源: From "muck" (dirt, filth) + "-er" (one who deals with) + "-ish" (having the quality of).  Ultimately from Middle Dutch "mokke" (heap, pile).
