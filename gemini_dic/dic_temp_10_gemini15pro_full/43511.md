cravenness:/ˈkreɪvənnɪs/
n.
懦弱，胆怯
The state of being craven; abject cowardice.
His cravenness prevented him from speaking up against the injustice. 他的懦弱使他不敢对不公正的事情直言不讳。
近义词: cowardice, timidity, pusillanimity
反义词: bravery, courage, valor
词性: 名词

Etymology:
From craven + -ness.  Craven itself comes from Middle English *cravant, from Old French cravante "defeated, overthrown," past participle of cravanter "to defeat, overthrow,"  probably from a Vulgar Latin *crepare "to break, crack." Ultimately related to Latin crepare "to rattle, crack, creak."
