crooked:/ˈkrʊkɪd/ (US), /ˈkrʊkɪd/ (UK)| adj.|弯曲的，歪斜的；不诚实的，欺诈的|bent, twisted, or not straight; dishonest or illegal|例句：The picture hung crooked on the wall.  这幅画歪挂在墙上。

例句：He got involved in some crooked business deals. 他卷入了一些不正当的商业交易。|近义词：bent, twisted, deformed, dishonest, corrupt, fraudulent|反义词：straight, honest, upright|词性:形容词

crook:/krʊk/ (US), /krʊk/ (UK)| n.|钩；弯曲部分；骗子|a bend or curve; a dishonest person, especially a criminal|例句：The shepherd used a crook to pull the sheep back. 牧羊人用一根钩子把羊拉了回来。

例句：That salesman is a real crook. 那个推销员是个十足的骗子。|近义词：thief, robber, swindler, con artist, criminal, bend, curve|反义词：policeman, law enforcement officer, straight part|词性:名词

crook:/krʊk/ (US), /krʊk/ (UK)| v.|使弯曲|to bend or curve something|例句：He crooked his finger to beckon her closer. 他弯曲手指示意她靠近些。

例句：The path crooked sharply to the left. 小路突然向左急转弯。|近义词：bend, curve, twist|反义词：straighten|词性:动词
