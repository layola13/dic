nervily:/ˈnɜːvɪli/ | adv. | 紧张地；胆怯地；不安地 | In a nervous or timid manner; showing signs of nervousness or anxiety. | She nervily adjusted her glasses before the interview.  她面试前紧张地调整了一下眼镜。| nervously, timidly, anxiously, apprehensively | calmly, confidently, composedly | adverb

nervily:/ˈnɜːrvɪli/ | adv. |  大胆地；鲁莽地 |  In a bold or audacious manner, often despite potential negative consequences. |  He nervily challenged the authority of the CEO. 他大胆地挑战了首席执行官的权威。 | boldly, audaciously, daringly, impudently | cautiously, timidly, hesitantly | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From nerve + -ly.  "Nerve" originally referred to sinews or tendons, later expanding to include nerves in the biological sense. The psychological meaning of "courage" or "boldness" developed from the physical sense. The suffix "-ly" forms adverbs.
