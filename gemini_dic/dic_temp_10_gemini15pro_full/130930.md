nonaspirate: /ˌnɒnˈæspɪrət/ (British) /ˌnɑːnˈæspərət/ (American)| adj. n.|非送气的，非送气音|A nonaspirate consonant is one that is produced without a noticeable puff of air.  A nonaspirate sound is a speech sound, usually a consonant, produced without aspiration.|The sounds /b/, /d/, and /ɡ/ are nonaspirates in Spanish.|近义词:unaspirated|反义词: aspirated, aspirate|词性:adjective, noun

Etymology:
From non- + aspirate.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "aspirate" comes from Latin *aspirare*, meaning "to breathe on."
