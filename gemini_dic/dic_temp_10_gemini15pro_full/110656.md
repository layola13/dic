linchpin: /ˈlɪntʃpɪn/ | n. | 关键人物；关键事物；核心；主轴 |  A person or thing vital to the success of an enterprise or organization. Something that holds the various elements of a complicated structure together. | The CEO is the linchpin of the company's success.  首席执行官是公司成功的关键人物。

The axle is the linchpin of the wheel.  轴是轮子的关键。| synonyms: cornerstone, keystone, kingpin, backbone, essential, vital, crucial | antonyms: unimportant, insignificant, trivial | noun


linchpin: /ˈlɪntʃpɪn/ | adj. | 关键的；核心的 |  Crucial to the success or operation of something.  (This usage is less common but becoming more prevalent.) |  He played a linchpin role in the negotiations.  他在谈判中扮演了关键角色。

Her testimony was the linchpin evidence in the case. 她的证词是案件中的关键证据。 | synonyms: key, crucial, central, pivotal, essential | antonyms: unimportant, insignificant, peripheral | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "lynch pin," a pin inserted through the end of an axle to prevent a wheel from coming off.  The "lynch" part is of uncertain origin, possibly related to a word meaning "to bend."
