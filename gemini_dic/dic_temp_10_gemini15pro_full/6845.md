anathematizer:/əˈnæθəməˌtaɪzər/
n.
咒逐者；革除教籍者；强烈谴责者
One who anathematizes; one who pronounces an anathema upon another.  A person who formally curses or condemns someone or something.
The king, acting as anathematizer, banished the traitor from the kingdom. 国王以革除教籍者的身份将叛徒驱逐出境。
The priest acted as the anathematizer, condemning the heretic's teachings.  牧师扮演了咒逐者的角色，谴责异教徒的教义。
近义词:condemner, denouncer, critic, censurer
反义词:supporter, defender, advocate, praiser
词性: Noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *anathematizātor, from Latin anathematizāre (to anathematize) + -tor.  Anathema itself comes from Greek *anáthema, meaning "something devoted to evil," from anatithénai "to set up, dedicate," from ana- "up" + tithénai "to put, place."
