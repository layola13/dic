druid:/ˈdruːɪd/ | n. | 德鲁伊 | A member of the learned class among the ancient Celts of Gaul, Britain, and Ireland, who acted as priests, teachers, judges, and advisors. |  The druids held a powerful position in Celtic society. 德鲁伊在凯尔特社会中拥有强大的地位。 |  priest, shaman, mage | antagonist, enemy | noun
