buttresses:/ˈbʌtrəsɪz/| n.|支撑物，扶壁；支持，加强|A structure, usually of brick or stone, built against a wall to strengthen or support it.  Something that supports or strengthens something else.|The cathedral walls are supported by huge stone buttresses. 大教堂的墙壁由巨大的石质扶壁支撑。|Synonyms: support, prop, reinforcement, brace, shore|Antonyms: weaken, undermine|Noun


buttress:/ˈbʌtrəs/| v.|支撑，加固；支持，加强|To support or strengthen something.|The government's policy was buttressed by strong public support. 政府的政策得到了强有力的公众支持。|Synonyms: support, reinforce, strengthen, bolster, fortify|Antonyms: weaken, undermine|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *buteres*, alteration (influenced by *bouter* "to push, thrust") of *bouterez* "supports, flying buttresses," from *bouter* "to push, thrust."
