insinuate: /ɪnˈsɪnjuːeɪt/ | v. | 暗示；含沙射影；使潜入；迂回地潜入 | To suggest or hint something bad or unpleasant in an indirect way. | He insinuated that she was lying. 他暗示她在撒谎。 |  She insinuated herself into his life. 她潜入了他的生活。 | Synonyms: imply, suggest, intimate, hint | Antonyms: declare, state, assert | verb

insinuation: /ɪnˌsɪnjuˈeɪʃn/ | n. |  暗示；含沙射影；影射 | An indirect or subtle suggestion or hint, especially of something bad or unpleasant. |  His insinuation that she was dishonest angered her. 他暗示她不诚实，这让她很生气。 | Synonyms: implication, innuendo, suggestion, hint | Antonyms: declaration, statement, assertion | noun
