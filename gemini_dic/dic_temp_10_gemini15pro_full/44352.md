cross-bedding:/ˈkrɒsˌbɛdɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌbedɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |交错层理，斜层理|The arrangement of layers of sediment deposited at an angle to the main bedding plane, indicative of current or wave action.| Geologists studied the cross-bedding in the sandstone to determine the direction of ancient river flow. 地质学家研究砂岩中的交错层理，以确定古代河流的流向。|Synonyms: cross-stratification|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but uniform bedding or horizontal bedding could be considered contrasting concepts)|noun

cross-bedding:/ˈkrɒsˌbɛdɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌbedɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |交错层理，斜层理|Cross-bedding is a sedimentary structure consisting of inclined layers within a larger bed.  It forms when sediments are deposited by flowing water or wind.| The cross-bedding in the dunes provided clues about the prevailing wind direction. 沙丘中的交错层理提供了盛行风向的线索。|Synonyms: cross-stratification|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but uniform bedding or horizontal bedding could be considered contrasting concepts)|noun
