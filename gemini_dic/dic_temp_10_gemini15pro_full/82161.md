groover:/ˈɡruːvər/| n.| 沉迷于某种音乐的人；在…开槽的人；开槽机|A person who enjoys and appreciates a particular type of music, especially jazz, soul, or funk.  Someone who makes grooves in something. A tool that makes grooves.|He's a real groover, always listening to the latest funk tracks. 他是一个真正的音乐爱好者，总是听最新的放克音乐。 The carpenter used a groover to create a decorative pattern on the wood. 木匠用开槽机在木头上刻出一个装饰图案。|近义词：fan, enthusiast, aficionado, devotee|反义词: critic, detractor|词性:名词

词源: From "groove" (n.) + "-er" (suffix forming agent nouns).  "Groove" itself originates from Middle Dutch "groeve," meaning "ditch," "furrow."
