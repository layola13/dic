intermediator:/ˌɪntərˈmiːdiˌeɪtər/| n. |中间人，调解人|A person who acts as a link between people or things, especially to try to bring about an agreement or reconciliation.|例句：The lawyer acted as an intermediator between the two parties in the dispute.  律师在争议双方之间充当了调解人。

例句：The United Nations served as an intermediator in the peace talks. 联合国在和平谈判中充当了调解人。|近义词：mediator, negotiator, go-between, broker, middleman|反义词：opponent, adversary|词性: noun

intermediary:/ˌɪntərˈmiːdiˌɛri/| adj. |中间的，媒介的|Acting as a mediator or link between people or things.|例句：The intermediary role of the ambassador was crucial in preventing war. 大使的中间人角色对于防止战争至关重要。

例句：An intermediary stage in a process.  过程中一个中间阶段。|近义词：intermediate, mediating, intervening|反义词：direct|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English intermediat, from Latin intermediātus, past participle of intermediō (“I stand between, intervene”), from inter (“between”) + mediō (“I divide, halve, stand in the middle”).
