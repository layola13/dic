brainstorm:/ˈbreɪnstɔːrm/| n. v.|头脑风暴，集体讨论；突然想到|A sudden, clever idea; a group discussion to produce ideas or solve problems.|We had a brainstorm session to come up with a new marketing campaign. 我们进行了一次头脑风暴会议，想出一个新的营销方案。|synonyms: idea generation, discussion, collaboration|antonyms: inactivity, stagnation|noun, verb

brainstorms:/ˈbreɪnstɔːrms/| n. v. (plural form of brainstorm)|头脑风暴，集体讨论；突然想到（复数形式）|Multiple instances of sudden, clever ideas or group discussions to produce ideas or solve problems.|The team held several brainstorms before finalizing the project proposal.  团队在最终确定项目提案之前举行了几次头脑风暴会议。|synonyms: idea generations, discussions, collaborations|antonyms: inactivities, stagnations|noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From brain + storm.  Coined in the 1940s by Alex Faickney Osborn, an advertising executive.  Originally referred to a creative problem-solving technique in groups.
