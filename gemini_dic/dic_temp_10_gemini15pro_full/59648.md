echelonment:/ˈeʃəlɒnmənt/ |n.|梯次配置，阶梯式布置|The echelonment of troops ensured a steady supply line to the front. 部队的梯次配置确保了前线的稳定补给线。|例句：

The echelonment of the aircraft allowed for a coordinated attack.
飞机的梯次配置使得协同攻击成为可能。

The company's echelonment of management created a clear hierarchy of authority.
公司管理层的梯次配置创造了一个清晰的权力等级。|近义词：arrangement, disposition, layering, staging|反义词: concentration, centralization|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *échelonnement*, from *échelonner* "to arrange in echelons," from *échelon* "rung of a ladder."  Ultimately from Old French *eschiele* "ladder, scale," from Latin *scala* "ladder."
