baron:/ˈbærən/ | n. | 男爵；大亨；巨头 | A nobleman of varying rank, especially a member of the lowest grade of the British peerage.  A powerful or wealthy businessman or industrialist. |  例句：The baron lived in a grand castle. 男爵住在一座宏伟的城堡里。

例句：He was a baron of industry, controlling a vast empire. 他是一位工业巨头，控制着一个庞大的帝国。 | 近义词：nobleman, peer, magnate, tycoon | 反义词：commoner | 词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *baron* "man; warrior; husband" (12c.), from Frankish *baro* "free man; warrior," or a similar Germanic source, from Proto-Germanic *baro* "man; free man," perhaps from PIE root *bʰer- (2) "to bear, carry" (source also of Sanskrit *bhartr* "supporter, husband," Greek *phérō* "I carry," Latin *fero* "I bear").
