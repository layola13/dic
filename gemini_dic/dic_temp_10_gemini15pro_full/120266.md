mini-roundabout:/ˈmɪni ˈraʊndəbaʊt/| n.|小型环岛，迷你环岛|A small roundabout, often marked by a painted circle on the road, where drivers must give way to traffic approaching from the right.|Drivers approaching a mini-roundabout should slow down and give way to traffic from the right.  驾驶员接近小型环岛时应减速并让路给右侧来车。|Synonyms: traffic circle, rotary, small roundabout|Antonyms: intersection, crossroads|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
"mini-" + "roundabout". "Mini-" is a prefix meaning small, derived from Latin "minus". "Roundabout" comes from the phrase "to go roundabout", meaning to go in a circular path.
