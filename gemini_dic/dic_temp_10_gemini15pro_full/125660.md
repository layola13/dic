mumper:/ˈmʌmpər/| n. |乞丐；抱怨者；装可怜的人|A person, especially a beggar, who mumbles or speaks indistinctly, often while complaining or seeking sympathy. |He was a mumper, always begging for money on the street corner. 他是一个乞丐，总是在街角乞讨钱。|The old mumper sat on the steps, muttering to himself.  那个老乞丐坐在台阶上，自言自语地抱怨着。|近义词：beggar, panhandler, mendicant, complainer, whiner|反义词：philanthropist, donor|词性:noun
Etymology->
From the verb "mump," meaning to "speak indistinctly or mutter," often with a sense of complaining or sulking. The origin of "mump" is uncertain, possibly related to Dutch "mompen" (to cheat, deceive) or Low German "mummeln" (to mumble).
