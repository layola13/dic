disheritance:/dɪsɪnˈhɛrɪtəns/ | n. |剥夺继承权，取消继承资格|The act of preventing someone from inheriting property or money. |Example 1: His disheritance from his father's will was a shock to everyone.  他被剥夺了父亲遗嘱中的继承权，这令所有人震惊。

Example 2: The disheritance caused a rift in the family. 取消继承资格导致了家庭的分裂。|Synonym: exclusion, disqualification|Antonym: inheritance, bequeathal|Noun


disherit:/dɪsɪnˈhɛrɪt/ | vt. |剥夺继承权，取消继承资格|To prevent someone from inheriting property or money. |Example 1: He disinherited his son for marrying against his wishes.  他因为儿子违背他的意愿结婚而剥夺了他的继承权。

Example 2:  She was disinherited after a falling out with her mother.  她和母亲闹翻后被剥夺了继承权。|Synonym: exclude, cut off|Antonym: bequeath, endow|Verb
