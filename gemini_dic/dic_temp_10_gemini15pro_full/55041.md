disquietment:/dɪsˈkwaɪətmənt/
n.
不安; 焦虑; 忧虑
A feeling of anxiety or worry.
The news of the layoffs caused widespread disquietment among the employees. 裁员的消息在员工中引起了广泛的不安。
The disquietment she felt about the upcoming exam was evident. 她对即将到来的考试感到焦虑，这是显而易见的。
近义词: unease, anxiety, worry, concern, apprehension
反义词: calmness, tranquility, peace, serenity
词性: 名词

词源:
From dis- (expressing negation, reversal) + quietment (from quiet + -ment).  Ultimately from Latin quies (rest, quiet).
