neolocally:/ˌniːəʊˈləʊkəli/ | adv. | 新居制地，从夫居地 |  Refers to a type of post-marital residence where a newly married couple establishes their own household separate from both the husband's and wife's families. |  The couple decided to live neolocally after their wedding. 这对夫妇决定在婚礼后新居而住。 |  Synonym: independently | Antonym: patrilocally, matrilocally | Adverb

Etymology: From Greek *neos* ("new") + Latin *locus* ("place") + -ly (adverbial suffix).
