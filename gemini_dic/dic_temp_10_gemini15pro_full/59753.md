economic: /ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk/ /ˌɛkəˈnɑːmɪk/ | adj. | 经济的，经济学的 | Relating to the economy or economics.  |  The country is facing a severe economic crisis.  这个国家正面临严重的经济危机。 | financial, monetary | extravagant, wasteful | adjective

economic: /ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk/ /ˌɛkəˈnɑːmɪk/ | n. | 经济学 | (Often used in plural "economics") The study of how societies use scarce resources to produce valuable commodities and distribute them among different people. | He studied economics at university. 他在大学学习经济学。| finance | - | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *économique*, from Latin *oeconomicus*, from Ancient Greek *οἰκονομικός (oikonomikós)*, from *οἰκονομία (oikonomía, “household management”)*, from *οἶκος (oîkos, “house”)* + *νέμω (némō, “I manage, distribute”)*.
