ascetic:/əˈsɛtɪk/| adj. n.|禁欲的；苦行的；禁欲主义者；苦行者|A person who practices severe self-discipline and abstention from all forms of indulgence, typically for religious reasons. |例句：He lived an ascetic life, devoid of all luxury. 他过着苦行僧般的生活，没有任何奢侈享受。|近义词：austere, abstemious, spartan, self-denying|反义词：hedonistic, indulgent, luxurious, self-indulgent|词性:形容词/名词

ascetic:/əˈsɛtɪk/| n.|禁欲主义者；苦行者|A person who practices severe self-discipline and abstention from all forms of indulgence, typically for religious reasons. |例句：The ascetics lived in a remote cave, meditating for hours each day.  苦行者们住在偏远的洞穴里，每天冥想数小时。|近义词：monk, hermit, recluse|反义词：hedonist, sybarite|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *asceticus, from Greek askētikos "athletic, rigorous," from askētēs "athlete, one who exercises," from askein "to exercise, train."  The meaning shifted from "athletic training" to "spiritual discipline" in early Christian times.
