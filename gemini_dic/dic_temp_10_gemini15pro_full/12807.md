assistantships:/əˈsɪstəntʃɪps/| n.|助教职位；研究生助学金|A financial aid package offered to graduate students, often in exchange for teaching or research duties.  It typically includes a tuition waiver and a stipend.|She received a generous assistantship that covered her tuition and living expenses. 她获得了一份丰厚的助学金，足以支付她的学费和生活费。|Synonyms: fellowship, scholarship, grant|Antonyms: loan, debt|noun

assistantship:/əˈsɪstəntʃɪp/| n.|助手的工作；助教的职位|The position or work of an assistant, especially a teaching or research assistant in a university. |The assistantship provided valuable experience and helped him pay for his studies.  这份助教工作提供了宝贵的经验，并帮助他支付了学习费用。|Synonyms: assistantship, fellowship, studentship|Antonyms: professorship, lectureship|noun
