gorge:/ɡɔːrdʒ/ (US) /ɡɔːdʒ/ (UK)| n. v.|峡谷；狼吞虎咽|A narrow valley between hills or mountains, typically with steep rocky walls and a stream running through it. (n.) To eat a large amount greedily. (v.)|The hikers marveled at the beauty of the gorge.  徒步旅行者对峡谷的美丽感到惊叹。 He gorged himself on pizza. 他狼吞虎咽地吃了披萨。|近义词：canyon, ravine (n.), devour, stuff (v.)|反义词: n/a|词性:noun, verb
词源:From Old French *gorge (“throat, gullet”), from Latin gurges (“whirlpool, abyss”).
