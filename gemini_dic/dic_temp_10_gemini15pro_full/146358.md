overfixed:/ˌoʊvərˈfɪkst/| adj.|过度固定的，过度矫正的|Fixed or corrected to an excessive degree.  This term is often used in psychology or medicine to describe a condition where something has been stabilized or corrected beyond what is necessary or beneficial.|The therapist believed the patient's posture was overfixed, leading to stiffness and pain.  治疗师认为病人的姿势过度固定，导致僵硬和疼痛。|Example: His smile seemed overfixed, almost unnatural. 他的笑容似乎过于僵硬，几乎不自然。|Synonyms: overcorrected, overstabilized, rigid, inflexible|Antonyms: flexible, adaptable, loose|adjective
