hard-liner:/ˈhɑːrdˌlaɪnər/ (US), /ˈhɑːdˌlaɪnə/ (UK)| n.|强硬派；强硬路线者|A person who adheres rigidly to a set of beliefs or policies, unwilling to compromise.|He was known as a hard-liner in his political views.|近义词：diehard, extremist, reactionary, stalwart|反义词：moderate, compromiser|Noun

Etymology:
Compound of "hard" (firm, unyielding) and "line" (a course of action or policy), with the suffix "-er" denoting a person who follows a particular line. The term gained prominence during the Cold War, referring to those who favored an aggressive stance against the Soviet Union.
