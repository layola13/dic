monogenism: /ˌmɒnəˈdʒɛnɪzəm/ /ˌmɑːnəˈdʒɛnɪzəm/
noun
单一起源论
The doctrine or belief that all human races have descended from a single created pair or from a single ancestral type.
Monogenism was a widely held belief before Darwin's theory of evolution.  单一起源论在达尔文的进化论出现之前是一个被广泛接受的观点。
Most scientists now subscribe to the theory of polygenism.  现在大多数科学家都赞同多起源论。
近义词: monogenesis
反义词: polygenism
词性: n.

Etymology:
From mono- (one) + Greek genesis (origin, creation)
