messing:/ˈmesɪŋ/ (US), /ˈmesɪŋ/ (UK)| v. | messing | the act of playing or fiddling with something, often without a specific purpose; behaving in a silly or mischievous way; interfering with something or someone, often causing trouble |  He was messing with the radio and broke it. 他摆弄收音机把它弄坏了。


messing:/ˈmesɪŋ/ (US), /ˈmesɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. | unpleasant, dirty, or uncomfortable | This room is a complete mess! 这个房间真是一团糟！

messing (as in "messing around"):/ˈmesɪŋ əˈraʊnd/ (US), /ˈmesɪŋ əˈraʊnd/ (UK)| v. | wasting time; behaving in a silly or playful way | Stop messing around and get your work done! 别再胡闹了，快去做你的工作！

messing (as in "messing with someone"):/ˈmesɪŋ wɪð ˈsʌmwʌn/ (US), /ˈmesɪŋ wɪð ˈsʌmwʌn/ (UK)| v. |  provoking or annoying someone; interfering with someone's plans or activities |  Don't mess with me! I'm in a bad mood.  别惹我！我心情不好。

词源: From the noun "mess," from Old French "mes," meaning "portion of food," later "a dish of soft food," which evolved to mean a state of disorder or untidiness.  Ultimately from Latin "missus," past participle of "mittere," meaning "to send" or "to put."


近义词: fiddling, playing, tampering, interfering, provoking, annoying
反义词: organizing, cleaning, tidying, respecting, leaving alone
词性: verb, adjective (depending on context)
