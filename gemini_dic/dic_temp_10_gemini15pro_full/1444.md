ace up your sleeve:/eɪs ʌp jɔr slɪːv/ | idiom | 暗藏的王牌，秘密武器 |  A secret advantage or resource that can be used if needed.  It refers to a card player hiding an ace in their sleeve to cheat, but now it generally refers to any hidden advantage. |  He had an ace up his sleeve – he knew the chairman of the selection committee. 他有一个秘密武器——他认识选拔委员会的主席。

She still has an ace up her sleeve, so don't count her out yet. 她还有个秘密武器，所以现在还不能把她排除在外。 | 近义词: secret weapon, hidden advantage, trump card | 反义词: disadvantage, weakness | 词性: idiom

ace in the hole:/eɪs ɪn ðə hoʊl/ | idiom | 底牌，王牌 | A hidden advantage or resource kept in reserve until needed. Similar to "ace up your sleeve," but often implies a more significant or decisive advantage.  Originates from stud poker, where the "hole card" is dealt face down. |  The prosecutor had an ace in the hole – a surprise witness. 检察官手里有个王牌——一个出人意料的证人。

Knowing the company's financial struggles was his ace in the hole during the negotiations.  了解公司的财务困境是他谈判中的王牌。 | 近义词: secret weapon, hidden advantage, trump card, ace up your sleeve | 反义词: disadvantage, weakness | 词性: idiom

I knew I had an ace up my sleeve when I walked into the negotiation. 当我走进谈判室时，我知道我手中有一个王牌。 | 近义词: have a secret weapon, have a hidden advantage | 反义词: be at a disadvantage | 词性: idiom
