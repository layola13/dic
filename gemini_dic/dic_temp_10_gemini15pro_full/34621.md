ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cleft, past participle of clēofan "to cleave, split," from Proto-Germanic *kleubaną, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kelə- "to cut, split."  Cognate with Dutch kloven "to cleave," German klieben "to cleave," Old Norse kljúfa "to cleave."
