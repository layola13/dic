fractiousness:/ˈfrækʃəsnəsnəs/| n.| 易怒，暴躁 |The child's fractiousness wore down his parents' patience.  这孩子的暴躁磨光了他父母的耐心。| English Explanation:  The quality of being easily irritated or quarrelsome; unruly behavior.  | Example: The fractiousness of the crowd made it difficult for the police to maintain order.  人群的暴躁情绪使警方难以维持秩序。| Synonyms: irritability, peevishness, crossness, cantankerousness | Antonyms: docility, agreeableness, placidity | Noun

fractious:/ˈfrækʃəs/| adj.| 易怒的，暴躁的 |The fractious horse refused to be ridden.  这匹暴躁的马拒绝被骑。| English Explanation:  Easily irritated or annoyed; readily angered; unruly.  | Example:  Negotiations were difficult because of the fractious relationship between the two parties. 谈判很困难，因为双方关系不和。| Synonyms: irritable, peevishness, cross, grumpy, quarrelsome | Antonyms: docile, agreeable, placid, even-tempered | Adjective
