hackney:/ˈhækni/| n.  adj. v.|出租马车；出租汽车；陈腐的；雇佣文人；出租；使…庸俗化|A hackney was originally a type of horse, and later a horse-drawn carriage or coach for hire. Now it can refer to a taxicab or something overused and commonplace.|例句：The writer's hackneyed prose lacked originality.  这位作家的陈腐散文缺乏创意。|近义词：cab, taxi, commonplace, trite, banal|反义词：original, fresh, new|词性:noun, adjective, verb
hackneyed:/ˈhæknid/| adj.|陈腐的；老套的|Hackneyed refers to something that is overused and unoriginal.|例句：The plot of the movie was hackneyed and predictable.  这部电影的情节老套且可预测。|近义词：trite, banal, clichéd, stale|反义词：original, fresh, innovative|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hakene*, from Old French *haquenée* (“ambling horse, nag”), from *Hakenei* (a placename, now Hackney in London), where such horses were bred.
