cudgeled:/ˈkʌdʒəld/ | v. | 棍棒殴打；苦思冥想 | To beat with a cudgel; to think hard about something. |  He cudgeled his brains trying to remember where he had left his keys. 他绞尽脑汁想记起他把钥匙丢在哪里了。| synonyms: beat, batter, club, pummel, thrash; ponder, contemplate, deliberate | antonyms: caress, soothe; ignore, neglect | verb

cudgeled:/ˈkʌdʒəld/ | adj. | 经过仔细考虑的 | Carefully considered or thought out. | The board presented a cudgeled plan to the investors. 董事会向投资者提交了一份经过深思熟虑的计划。| synonyms: considered, deliberate, reasoned, thoughtful | antonyms: impulsive, rash, unconsidered, thoughtless | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *kugelen*, from Old French *coigner* "to strike," from Latin *cuneus* "wedge."  The original sense referred to beating with a club or cudgel. The figurative sense of "thinking hard" arose later, likely from the image of beating one's brains.
