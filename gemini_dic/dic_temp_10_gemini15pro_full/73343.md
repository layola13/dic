forefending:/fɔːrˈfɛndɪŋ/ | adj. | 预防的，保护的，防止的 | Preventing, guarding against, or averting (something undesirable).  It's often used in the phrase "God forefend." | Example:  "Forefending any further accidents, they implemented stricter safety regulations."  他们为了防止再次发生事故，实施了更严格的安全规定。| Synonym: preventing, averting, precluding, obviating | Antonym: causing, inviting, encouraging |  
Etymology: From Middle English *forfenden*, from Old English *forfēon* (“to despise, disdain, renounce, refuse, abandon, prohibit, forbid”), from *for-* (“for, against, away”) + *fēon* (“to hate, abhor”).  The sense shifted from "prohibiting" to "preventing something from happening."
