distractibility:/dɪˌstræktɪˈbɪləti/| n. |分心，注意力分散|The state of being easily distracted; inability to maintain focus.|Her distractibility made it difficult for her to study. 她的注意力难以集中，学习起来很困难。|Synonyms: inattentiveness, absentmindedness, flightiness|Antonyms: attentiveness, concentration, focus|noun

distractible:/dɪˈstræktəbl/| adj. |易分心的，注意力不集中的|Easily distracted; having one's attention drawn away easily.|He was very distractible and couldn't concentrate on his work. 他很容易分心，无法集中精力工作。|Synonyms: inattentive, unfocused, easily diverted|Antonyms: attentive, focused, concentrated|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *distractus (past participle of distrahere "to pull apart, divide, distract"), from dis- "apart" + trahere "to draw, pull". The suffix "-ibility" denotes the capacity or quality of being distractible.
