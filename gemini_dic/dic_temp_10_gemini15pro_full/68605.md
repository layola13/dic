fantasizing:/ˈfæntəsaɪzɪŋ/ | n. | 幻想，空想 | The act of imagining or creating unrealistic or improbable scenarios in one's mind. | She spent hours fantasizing about winning the lottery. 她花了几个小时幻想自己中了彩票。| Synonyms: daydreaming, imagining, visualizing | Antonyms:  facing reality, being practical | Verb (present participle)

fantasize:/ˈfæntəsaɪz/ | v. | 幻想，空想 | To indulge in fantasies; to create unrealistic or improbable scenarios in one's mind. | He fantasized about traveling the world. 他幻想环游世界。| Synonyms: daydream, imagine, visualize | Antonyms:  face reality, be practical | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fantasier*, from Late Latin *phantasiari*, from Greek *phantazesthai* "to make visible, present to the mind," from *phantazein* "to make visible," from *phantos* "visible," from *phainein* "to show, bring to light."
