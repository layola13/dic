covenant:/ˈkʌvənənt/| n. vi.|契约，约定；盟约|An agreement between two or more parties, especially one that is documented legally or morally binding.  A formal, solemn, and binding agreement.|例句：They made a covenant to share the profits. 他们立约共享利润。

例句：The two companies entered into a covenant not to compete.  两家公司签订了互不竞争的协议。|近义词：agreement, contract, pact, treaty|反义词：disagreement, breach|词性:n. vi.
covenant:/ˈkʌvənənt/| vi.|立约，订立契约|To enter into a formal and binding agreement.|例句：They covenanted to build a new school.  他们立约要建一所新学校。

例句：The landlord covenanted with his tenants to provide heat. 房东与房客约定提供暖气。|近义词：agree, contract, pledge|反义词：renege, break|词性:vi.

词源：
From Old French *covenant (Modern French convenant), present participle of convenir "to agree, be suitable," from Latin convenīre "to come together, assemble, agree," from com- "together" (see com-) + venīre "to come" (see come).
