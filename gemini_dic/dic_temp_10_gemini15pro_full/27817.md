cannula:/ˈkæn.jʊ.lə/ (US) /ˈkæn.jʊ.lə/ (UK)| n.|插管，套管|A thin tube inserted into a vein or body cavity to administer medicine, drain off fluid, or insert a surgical instrument.|The doctor inserted a cannula into the patient's vein to administer fluids.|Synonyms: catheter, tube|Antonyms: None|noun

cannulae:/ˈkæn.jʊ.liː/ (US) /ˈkæn.jʊ.liː/ (UK)| n.|插管，套管（cannula的复数形式）|Plural form of cannula, referring to multiple thin tubes inserted into a vein or body cavity.|The surgeon used several cannulae during the laparoscopic procedure.|Synonyms: catheters, tubes|Antonyms: None|noun
