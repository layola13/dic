fogeys:/ˈfoʊɡiz/| n. |老古董，守旧的人|Old-fashioned or conservative people, often used humorously or disapprovingly.  It refers to people who are seen as out of touch with modern trends and ideas.|Those fogeys wouldn't know a good app if it bit them. 那些老古董根本不懂什么是好应用。|synonyms: old-timers, dinosaurs, conservatives, traditionalists|antonyms:  modernists, progressives, innovators|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain. Possibly related to dialectal *fogie* (an invalid soldier), from *fog* (moss), referring to the musty smell of old age.  Another theory connects it to *fogram,* an old Scots word for a fussy or timid person.
