besiege:/bɪˈsiːdʒ/| vt.|围攻，包围；困扰|To surround a place, especially with an army, in order to capture it; to crowd around; to overwhelm (someone) with requests, demands, etc.|The city was besieged by enemy forces for months.  这座城市被敌军围困了数月。

The celebrity was besieged by reporters wanting an interview.  这位名人被想要采访的记者团团围住。|近义词：surround, beleaguer, beset, harass|反义词：abandon, desert|动词


besiege:/bɪˈsiːdʒ/| n.|围攻，包围 (较少用作名词)|The act of besieging.|The siege was finally lifted after three months. (这里siege是名词)  围攻三个月后终于解除了。 |近义词：siege, blockade|反义词：relief|名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *besieger*, from be- "thoroughly" + sieger "to besiege," from Latin sedēre "to sit."  The original sense was "to sit down before" a fortified place, hence "to besiege."
