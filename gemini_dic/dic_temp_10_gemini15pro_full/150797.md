paradoxes: /ˈpærədɒksɪz/ | n. |悖论，似是而非的说法|A statement or proposition that seems self-contradictory or absurd but in reality expresses a possible truth.|例句：The statement "I always lie" is a classic paradox.  这句话“我总是撒谎”是一个经典的悖论。|近义词：contradiction, enigma, anomaly|反义词: truism, orthodoxy|词性: 名词
paradox: /ˈpærədɒks/ | n. |悖论，似是而非的说法|A statement or proposition that seems self-contradictory or absurd but in reality expresses a possible truth.|例句：It's a paradox that standing is more tiring than walking.  站着比走路更累，真是矛盾。|近义词：contradiction, enigma, anomaly|反义词: truism, orthodoxy|词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin paradoxum, from Greek paradoxon "contrary opinion," neuter of paradoxos "contrary to expectation, incredible," from para- "contrary to" + doxa "opinion," from dokein "to seem, to think."
