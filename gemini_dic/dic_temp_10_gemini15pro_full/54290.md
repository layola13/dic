disgustedly:/dɪsˈɡʌstɪdli/
adverb
厌恶地，憎恶地
In a manner showing strong dislike or disapproval.
She turned away disgustedly. 她厌恶地转过身去。
He spat disgustedly on the ground. 他厌恶地往地上吐了口痰。
近义词:revoltedly, nauseously, distastefully
反义词: approvingly, admiringly, appreciatively
词性:adverb

disgust:/dɪsˈɡʌst/
noun, verb
厌恶，反感；使厌恶
A feeling of revulsion or strong disapproval aroused by something unpleasant or offensive. / To cause (someone) to feel revulsion or strong disapproval.
The smell of the rotten meat filled her with disgust. 腐肉的气味让她充满了厌恶。
The violence in the movie disgusted me. 电影中的暴力让我感到厌恶。
近义词:revulsion, nausea, aversion, distaste, sicken, repel
反义词:admiration, approval, appreciation, delight, please
词性:noun, verb

词源:
From Old French *desgouster (Modern French dégoûter), from des- "dis-" + gouster "to taste," from Latin gustare "to taste."  The sense evolution went from "dislike the taste of" to "have a strong aversion to."
