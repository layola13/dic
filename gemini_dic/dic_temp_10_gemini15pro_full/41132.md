copses:/ˈkɒpsiːz/| n. |矮树林，小灌木丛|A thicket of small trees or bushes, especially one regularly cut back to produce poles or firewood.| We walked through the copses, enjoying the dappled sunlight. 我们穿过矮树林，享受着斑驳的阳光。|The copses provided shelter for the small animals. 这些矮树林为小动物提供了庇护所。|近义词：thicket, grove, undergrowth|反义词: clearing, open field|词性: 名词

词源:
From Middle English *copice, from Old French copeiz, from Late Latin colpare (“to cut”), from Latin colaphus (“a blow, cuff”).  The original meaning referred to a wood where trees were periodically cut back to ground level for coppicing (a method of woodland management).
