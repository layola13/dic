cynicism: /ˈsɪnɪˌsɪzəm/ | n. | 愤世嫉俗；玩世不恭 | An attitude of distrust and negativity towards human sincerity and motives.  The belief that people are motivated purely by self-interest; skepticism about the motives of others. |  He displayed a deep cynicism about politicians. 他对政治家表现出深深的愤世嫉俗。 |  pessimism, skepticism, negativity, distrust, misanthropy | optimism, idealism, faith, trust, hope | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French cynisme, from Latin cynismus, from Greek κυνισμός (kunismos) "dog-like behavior," from κύων (kuōn) "dog."  Originally referred to the philosophy of the Cynics, an ancient Greek school that emphasized self-sufficiency and rejection of social conventions.  Over time, the term evolved to its modern meaning of distrust and negativity towards human motives.
