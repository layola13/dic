draft:/drɑːft/ /dræft/| n. vt. adj.|草稿，汇票，征兵，通风;起草，征募，选秀|A draft is a preliminary version of a piece of writing.  草稿是文章的初步版本。|例句：I finished the first draft of my novel. 我完成了我的小说的初稿。|近义词：sketch, outline, version, draught (British)|反义词：final copy, finished product|词性:名词

draft:/drɑːft/ /dræft/| vt.|起草，征募，选秀|He was drafted into the army. 他被征召入伍。|例句：The lawyer drafted the contract. 律师起草了合同。|近义词：compose, write, recruit, conscript|反义词：reject, discharge|词性:动词

draft:/drɑːft/ /dræft/| adj.|初步的，草拟的|This is just a draft version of the report. 这只是报告的草稿版本。|例句：The draft proposal was sent to the committee for review.  委员会收到了草案提案以供审查。|近义词：preliminary, provisional, tentative|反义词：final, definitive|词性:形容词

词源：Middle English: from Old Norse *draught, from *draga ‘to draw, pull’. The original sense was ‘drawing’, hence ‘something drawn’, giving rise to the senses ‘drawing of liquid’, ‘drawing up of soldiers’, and ‘drawing up of a document’.
