overrationalization: /ˌoʊvərˌræʃənələˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 过度理性化 | The act of applying rational thought to an excessive or inappropriate degree, often to justify decisions based on emotions or biases rather than logic.  | He engaged in overrationalization to explain his impulsive purchase of a sports car, claiming it was a "good investment." 他过度理性化地解释了他冲动购买跑车的行为，声称这是一项“好的投资”。 | Overthinking, overanalyzing, rationalizing | Underthinking, intuition | noun
