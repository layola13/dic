attrition:/əˈtrɪʃən/
n.
损耗，磨损，消耗
a gradual reduction in the number of people who work for an organization, that is achieved by not replacing those who leave
The company is trying to reduce staff through natural attrition. 公司正试图通过自然减员来减少员工。

attrition:/əˈtrɪʃən/
n.
摩擦，冲突
the act of weakening or damaging something by constant attack or pressure
The long war of attrition exhausted both countries. 长期的消耗战使两国都筋疲力尽。


Etymology: From Latin attritionem (nominative attritio) "a rubbing against," noun of action from past-participle stem of atterere "to rub against, rub away," from ad- "to" (see ad-) + terere "to rub" (from PIE root *tere- (1) "to rub, turn").

Synonym:
erosion, depletion, weakening, decline, abrasion, friction, conflict

Antonym:
growth, increase, strengthening, accession, harmony, peace
