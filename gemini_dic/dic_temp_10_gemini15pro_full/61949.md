emulsified: /ɪˈmʌlsɪfaɪd/ | adj. | 乳化的 | Made into or resembling an emulsion; a mixture of two or more liquids that are normally unmixable. | The emulsified fat in the milk made it easier to digest. 牛奶中乳化的脂肪使其更容易消化。 | synonyms: blended, mixed, homogenized | antonyms: separated, immiscible | adjective

emulsify: /ɪˈmʌlsɪfaɪ/ | v. | 乳化 | To make a liquid into an emulsion. |  The chef emulsified the oil and vinegar to make a salad dressing.  厨师将油和醋乳化成沙拉酱。| synonyms: blend, mix, homogenize | antonyms: separate | verb

emulsification: /ɪˌmʌlsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 乳化作用 | The process of making an emulsion. | The emulsification of the ingredients is crucial for the recipe's success.  原料的乳化对于食谱的成功至关重要。 | synonyms: blending, mixing, homogenization | antonyms: separation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Latin word "emulgere" meaning "to milk out,"  combined with the suffix "-ify" to create the verb "emulsify."  The noun "emulsification" and the adjective "emulsified" are derived from the verb.
