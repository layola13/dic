immortalization:/ɪˌmɔːrtəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/
n.
永生化，不朽化
The act of making something or someone immortal; the state of being immortalized.
His immortalization in the Hall of Fame cemented his legacy. 他在名人堂的永生化巩固了他的遗产。
The artist achieved immortalization through his iconic paintings. 这位艺术家通过他标志性的绘画作品获得了不朽的声誉。
近义词: perpetuation, eternalization
反义词: oblivion, extinction
词性: 名词

immortalize:/ɪˈmɔːrtəlaɪz/
vt.
使永生，使不朽
To make someone or something famous or remembered for a long time, especially after their death.
Shakespeare immortalized Romeo and Juliet through his play.  莎士比亚通过他的戏剧使罗密欧与朱丽叶永垂不朽。
The photographer’s images immortalized the horrors of war. 摄影师的照片使战争的恐怖永存。
近义词: commemorate, memorialize
反义词: forget, obscure
词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *immortalizatus, past participle of immortalizare "make immortal," from Latin immortalis "immortal" (see immortal) + -izare.
