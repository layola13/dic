chutzpa:/ˈxʊtspə, ˈhʊtspə/| n. |厚颜无耻，胆大妄为| extreme self-confidence or audacity (usually used disapprovingly).  It suggests a remarkable nerve and a disregard for conventional social norms.| Example: "He had the chutzpah to ask for a raise after missing a week of work." 他竟然有脸在旷工一周后要求加薪。| Example:  She showed real chutzpah by challenging the CEO's decision. 她挑战CEO的决定，真是胆大妄为。 |Synonyms: audacity, nerve, boldness, temerity, gall, effrontery| Antonyms: timidity, shyness, modesty, humility| noun

词源:
From Yiddish חוצפּה (khutspe), from Hebrew חֻצְפָּה (ḥuṣpā), from חָצַף (ḥāṣaf, “to be insolent, to be impudent”). Ultimately from Akkadian *ḫaṣāpu (“to peel, strip off”).
来自意第绪语 חוצפּה (khutspe)，来自希伯来语 חֻצְפָּה (ḥuṣpā)，来自 חָצַף (ḥāṣaf，“傲慢无礼，厚颜无耻”)。最终来自阿卡德语 *ḫaṣāpu（“剥皮，剥离”）。
