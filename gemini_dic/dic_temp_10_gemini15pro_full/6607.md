anacoenosis:/ˌænəkəˈnoʊsɪs/ | n. | 反问; 设问 | A rhetorical figure in which a speaker or writer poses a question to an audience or reader, often with the implication that the answer is obvious or self-evident. It's a form of soliciting the opinions or feelings of those being addressed, even though the speaker or writer already knows the answer or expects a particular response. | "Don't you think it's time we put an end to this madness?" (Implying that the audience likely agrees that it is time to end the madness) | synonyms: rhetorical question, communicatio | antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, but could contrast with direct assertion or statement) | noun
