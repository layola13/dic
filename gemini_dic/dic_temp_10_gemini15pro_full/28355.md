caravan:/ˈkærəvæn/ (US), /ˈkærəvæn/ (UK)| n.| 旅行队，大篷车；房车|A group of people, especially traders or pilgrims, traveling together across a desert or through hostile territory. A vehicle equipped for living in, typically towed by a car and used for holidays.|We saw a caravan of camels crossing the desert. 我们看到一支骆驼商队穿过沙漠。|synonyms: convoy, procession, motorhome, trailer, RV|antonyms:  (For motorhome meaning) house, building|noun


caravan:/ˈkærəvæn/ (US), /ˈkærəvæn/ (UK)| v.|  组成旅行队，用房车旅行|To travel in or with a caravan.|They caravanned across Europe last summer. 去年夏天他们开着房车游遍了欧洲。|synonyms:  tour, journey, trek|antonyms: stay, remain|verb
