loophole:/ˈluːphoʊl/ (US) /ˈluːpˌhəʊl/ (UK)| n. |漏洞，空子|An ambiguity or inadequacy in the law or a set of rules.|The company exploited a loophole in environmental regulations.  这家公司利用了环境法规中的一个漏洞。|synonyms:  gap, opening, flaw, crack, escape clause, technicality|antonyms: closure, seal, blockage|noun

loophole:/ˈluːphoʊl/ (US) /ˈluːpˌhəʊl/ (UK)| n. |射孔，窥视孔 (less common)|A small hole in a wall or door, often for looking through.|The soldiers fired through loopholes in the fortress walls. 士兵们透过堡垒墙上的射孔开火。|synonyms: aperture, slit, peephole|antonyms:  N/A|noun
