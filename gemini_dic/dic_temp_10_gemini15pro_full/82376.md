groyne:/ɡrɔɪn/| n.|防波堤，堤坝，突堤|A low wall or barrier built out into the sea from a beach to check erosion and drifting.|例句：The groynes protect the coastline from erosion. 这些防波堤保护海岸线免受侵蚀。|例句：Several groynes were constructed along the beach to trap sand.  沿着海滩建造了几个突堤来拦截沙子。|近义词：breakwater, jetty, seawall|反义词:|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *groine, from Old North French groign, groin (“snout, pig's snout, projecting point”), from Late Latin grunium (“snout”), from Latin grunnīre (“to grunt”). Cognate with Old French groin (“snout”), French groin (“groin”), Italian grugno (“snout”), Spanish gruño (“grunt”).
