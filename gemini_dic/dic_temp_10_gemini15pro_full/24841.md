词源： "brownfield" is a compound of "brown" and "field".  "Brown" in this context refers to the presence of industrial or commercial activity, which may have left behind pollution or other undesirable remnants. "Field" simply denotes an area of land.  The term emerged in the late 20th century, gaining popularity in the context of urban renewal and sustainable development.
