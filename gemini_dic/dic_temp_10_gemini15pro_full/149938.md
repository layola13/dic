palliator:/ˈpæliˌeɪtər/| n.|姑息疗法者，缓和者|Someone who provides palliative care, which focuses on relieving the symptoms and stress of a serious illness. |The doctor acted as a palliator for the patient in the final stages of cancer, focusing on pain management and comfort.|近义词:comforter, reliever, soother|反义词:aggravator|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *palliator, from Latin palliatus (cloaked, disguised, wearing a pallium), past participle of palliare (to cloak, conceal, excuse, mitigate), from pallium (cloak, coverlet).
