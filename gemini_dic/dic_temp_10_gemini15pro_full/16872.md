basilica:/bəˈsɪlɪkə/ /bəˈsɪlɪkə/| n.|教堂, 长方形基督教堂|A large oblong hall or building with double colonnades and a semicircular apse, used in ancient Rome as a court of law or for public assemblies. Also, an early Christian church building resembling this and a Roman Catholic church granted special privileges by the pope. |The basilica was filled with worshippers for the Christmas Eve service. 圣诞夜弥撒时，教堂里挤满了礼拜者。|Synonym: cathedral, church, sanctuary|Antonym: chapel|noun

basilica:/bəˈsɪlɪkə/ /bəˈsɪlɪkə/| adj.|教堂的|Relating to or having the characteristics of a basilica.  (Less common usage) |The basilica structure of the building gave it a grand and imposing appearance.  这座建筑的教堂式结构使其外观宏伟壮观。|Synonym: cathedral-like, church-like|Antonym: N/A|adjective
