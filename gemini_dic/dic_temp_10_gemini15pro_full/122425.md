moanfully:/ˈmoʊnfəli/ | adv. | 悲叹地，哀伤地 | In a way that expresses sorrow or suffering, typically through a low, mournful sound. |  She sighed moanfully and turned away. 她悲叹地叹了口气，转过身去。|  The wind howled moanfully through the trees. 风悲鸣着穿过树林。 | sadly, mournfully, plaintively | cheerfully, happily | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From moan (v.) + -fully (adv. suffix).  "Moan" comes from Old English *mānian, of Germanic origin, related to Dutch *menen "to mean, think," German meinen "to think, mean."  "-fully" is a common suffix forming adverbs of manner.
