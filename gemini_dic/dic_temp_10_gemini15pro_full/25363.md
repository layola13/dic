buk:/bʊk/ | n. | 雄鹿; 公羊; 公兔; 公鹿皮 | a male deer; a male sheep, goat, or hare; leather made from deerskin |  The hunter tracked the buk through the forest. 猎人追踪着雄鹿穿过森林。 | synonyms: stag, buck, hart (for deer); ram (for sheep); billy goat (for goat); buck (for hare/rabbit) | antonyms: doe (for deer); ewe (for sheep); nanny goat (for goat) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bucca, from Proto-Germanic *bukkô (“male deer, goat”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰug-. Cognate with Dutch bok (“buck, billy goat”), German Bock (“buck, billy goat”), Icelandic bokkur (“male goat, ram”), Gothic 𐌱𐍉𐌺𐍃 (bokks, “male goat”).
