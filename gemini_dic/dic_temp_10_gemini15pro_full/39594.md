consecrator:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪtə(r)/ /ˈkɑːnsɪkreɪtər/ | n. | 主祭人；祝圣者；授圣职者 | A person who performs a ceremony of consecration, dedicating something to a sacred purpose. | The bishop acted as the consecrator at the ordination of the new priests.主教在新神父的晋铎礼上担任主礼人。 | Synonyms: ordainer, dedicator | Antonyms: desecrator, profaner | noun

consecrate:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/ /ˈkɑːnsɪkreɪt/ | v. | 使神圣；祝圣；奉献 | To make or declare something sacred; to dedicate formally to a religious or divine purpose. | The church was consecrated in 1850. 这座教堂于1850年祝圣。 | Synonyms: sanctify, hallow, bless | Antonyms: desecrate, profane, defile | verb
