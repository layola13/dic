efflorescent:/ˌɛfləˈrɛsənt/ | adj. | 开花的；风化的；（比喻）繁荣的 | Losing moisture and crumbling to a powder; (of a substance) becoming covered with a powdery coating as a result of losing water of crystallization; (figuratively) reaching a highly developed or flourishing stage. | The efflorescent crystals on the cave walls shimmered in the lamplight.  洞穴墙壁上风化的晶体在灯光下闪烁。| The Renaissance was an efflorescent period for the arts. 文艺复兴时期是艺术繁荣的时期。 | synonyms: blooming, blossoming, flourishing, thriving | antonyms: decaying, withering, declining | adjective

efflorescence:/ˌɛfləˈrɛsəns/ | n. |  风化；开花；繁荣 | The action or process of efflorescing. | The efflorescence of algae in the lake was a sign of pollution.  湖中藻类的盛开是污染的迹象。| The cultural efflorescence of the Tang Dynasty is still admired today.  唐朝的文化繁荣至今仍令人钦佩。| synonyms: bloom, blossom, flowering, thriving | antonyms: decay, decline, deterioration | noun
