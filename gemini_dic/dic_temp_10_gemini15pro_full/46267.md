czardom:/ˈzɑːrdəm/| n. |沙皇统治；沙皇俄国|The territory ruled by a czar; the period of rule of the czars in Russia.  A system of government or a state ruled by a czar.|The Romanov czardom lasted for over 300 years.  罗曼诺夫王朝的沙皇统治持续了300多年。|Synonyms: empire, kingdom, monarchy|Antonyms: republic, democracy|noun


Etymology->
From Russian *царство (carstvo, “kingdom, realm”), from царь (carʹ, “tsar”). Ultimately from Latin Caesar.
