anchors:/ˈæŋkərz/ (US), /ˈæŋkəz/ (UK)| n.|锚;  依靠;  新闻节目主持人|A heavy object attached to a rope or chain and used to moor a vessel to the seabed; a source of stability or security; a person who presents and coordinates a television or radio program.|The ship dropped its anchors in the bay. 船在海湾抛锚了。|Synonyms: moorings, mainstay, linchpin, host, presenter|Antonyms: adrift, unstable, insecure|Noun

anchors:/ˈæŋkərz/ (US), /ˈæŋkəz/ (UK)| v.|抛锚;  使固定;  主持|To secure (a vessel) by means of an anchor; to fix firmly; to act as an anchor for (a television or radio program).|She anchored the boat to the dock. 她把船停泊在码头上。|Synonyms: moor, secure, fix, attach, present|Antonyms: detach, release, unmoor, drift|Verb
