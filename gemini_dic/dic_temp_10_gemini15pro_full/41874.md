cosenage:/ˈkʌzənɪdʒ/
n.
欺骗；诈骗
The act of deceiving or tricking someone, typically by gaining their trust and persuading them to do something that benefits the deceiver.
He was convicted of cosenage after tricking elderly people out of their savings. 他因欺骗老年人拿出他们的积蓄而被判犯有欺诈罪。
Synonyms: fraud, deceit, trickery, deception, chicanery, guile, subterfuge
Antonyms: honesty, integrity, truthfulness


Etymology:
From Middle English *cosinage*, from Old French *cousinage*, from *cousin* (“cousin, kinsman”), from Latin *consobrinus*.  The original meaning referred to falsely claiming kinship to defraud someone of an inheritance. The meaning broadened over time to encompass any kind of fraudulent deception.
