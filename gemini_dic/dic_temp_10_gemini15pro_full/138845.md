nuraghe:/ˈnʊrɑːɡe/ /nuˈrɑːɡe/
|n.|努拉吉|A nuraghe (plural nuraghi, Sardinian pronunciation: [nuˈɾaɡi]) is the main type of megalithic edifice found in Sardinia, Italy, developed during the Nuragic Age between 1900 and 730 BC. Today more than 7,000 nuraghi remain, though archaeologists believe that originally there were tens of thousands more.|The Nuragic civilization built thousands of nuraghe throughout the island.  努拉吉文明在整个岛屿上建造了成千上万的努拉吉。

The nuraghe is a symbol of Sardinia's ancient past. 努拉吉是撒丁岛古老过去的象征。|近义词:megalith, tower, fortress|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "modern building")|词性:noun
