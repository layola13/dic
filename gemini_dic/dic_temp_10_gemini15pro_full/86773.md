helter-skelteriness:/ˌheltər ˈskeltərnəs/ | n. | 混乱，杂乱无章 | The helter-skelteriness of the evacuation made it difficult to account for everyone. 撤离的混乱使得难以统计所有的人数。 | The room was in a state of utter helter-skelteriness. 房间里一片狼藉，杂乱无章。| synonyms: disorder, chaos, confusion, disarray | antonyms: order, organization, tidiness | noun

helter-skelter:/ˌheltər ˈskeltər/ | adj. adv. | 仓促的，杂乱的；匆忙地，乱糟糟地 | The children ran helter-skelter down the hill. 孩子们乱哄哄地跑下山坡。 |  They packed their belongings helter-skelter and left in a hurry. 他们胡乱地打包行李，匆匆离开了。| synonyms: haphazard, chaotic, disorderly | antonyms: orderly, organized, methodical | adjective, adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the reduplication of *skelter, perhaps related to Middle Dutch *skelteren "to run fast," frequentative of *skelen "to squint, to go astray." The sense evolution might have been from "running wildly" to "disorderly haste." First recorded in the 16th century.
