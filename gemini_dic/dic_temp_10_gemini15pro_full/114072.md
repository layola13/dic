magnetometer:/mæɡnɪˈtɒmɪtər/ |/mæɡnəˈtɑːmɪtər/| n. |磁力计，磁强计 |An instrument used for measuring the strength and/or direction of magnetic fields. |例句：The magnetometer detected a significant change in the Earth's magnetic field. 磁力计探测到地球磁场发生了显著变化。

|例句：Geophysicists use magnetometers to explore for mineral deposits. 地球物理学家使用磁力计勘探矿藏。|近义词：magnetic sensor, Teslameter, Gaussmeter |反义词: N/A |词性: noun

magnetometry:/ˌmæɡnɪˈtɒmɪtri/ |/ˌmæɡnəˈtɑːmətri/| n. |磁力测量法，磁强测量法 |The science and technique of measuring magnetic fields. |例句：Magnetometry plays a vital role in understanding the Earth's core. 磁力测量法对于理解地核至关重要。

|例句：Archaeologists use magnetometry to locate buried structures. 考古学家使用磁力测量法来定位埋藏的建筑物。|近义词：magnetic field measurement |反义词: N/A |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *magnet* + *-o-* + *-meter*. *Magnet* from Old French *magnete*, from Latin *magnes*, from Greek *Μάγνης (Lithos)* (Magnes (stone)), from *Μαγνησία (Magnēsia)* (a region in Thessaly, Greece). *-meter* from French *-mètre*, from Greek *-μέτρον (-metron)*, meaning "measure".
