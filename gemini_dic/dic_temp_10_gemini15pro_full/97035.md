infectivities:/ɪnˌfɛkˈtɪvɪtiz/
noun
传染性，感染力
The plural form of "infectivity," referring to the ability of a pathogen to establish an infection.  It can be used to compare the relative abilities of different strains or variants of a pathogen to infect a host.
The study compared the infectivities of several different strains of the flu virus.  这项研究比较了几种不同流感病毒株的传染性。
Researchers are studying the infectivities of new COVID-19 variants. 研究人员正在研究新冠病毒新变种的传染性。
Synonyms: contagiousness, transmissibility, virulence (sometimes)
Antonyms: resistance, immunity
