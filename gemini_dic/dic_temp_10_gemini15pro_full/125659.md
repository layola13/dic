mump:/mʌmp/ | n. | 腮腺炎; (尤指儿童患的)流行性腮腺炎 | an infectious disease that causes painful swelling in the neck and cheeks, most commonly affecting children |  Example: He had the mumps as a child. 他小时候得过腮腺炎。 | Example: The school had an outbreak of mumps. 学校爆发了腮腺炎。 | Synonyms: parotitis | Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a disease) | Noun


mump:/mʌmp/ | v. | 绷着脸; 闷闷不乐 | to have a sullen or sulky expression | Example: She mumped around the house all day after the argument. 争吵过后，她整天闷闷不乐地待在家里。 | Example: He was mumping because he didn't get the toy he wanted. 他因为没有得到想要的玩具而闷闷不乐。 | Synonyms: sulk, pout, mope | Antonyms: smile, grin, beam | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch mompen "to mumble, grumble," of imitative origin.  The sense of "sulk" developed from the facial expression associated with mumbling.  The meaning "mumps" arose from the swelling of the cheeks characteristic of the disease.
