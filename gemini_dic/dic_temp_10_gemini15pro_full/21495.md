blucher:/ˈbluːtʃər/ (US), /ˈbluːtʃə/ (UK)| n.| 布鲁彻鞋，一种系带低帮鞋|A man's leather shoe that laces over the instep.  A style of shoe or boot characterized by open lacing with side flaps (quarters)  that are stitched on top of the vamp.|He wore a pair of brown bluchers. 他穿着一双棕色的布鲁彻鞋。|Synonyms: oxford, derby|Antonyms: boot, slip-on|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Gebhard Leberecht von Blücher (1742–1819), Prussian field marshal, after whom the shoe was named because it resembled a boot worn by his troops.
