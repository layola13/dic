coxcomically: /ˌkɒksˈkɒmɪkli/ (UK) /ˌkɑːksˈkɑːmɪkli/ (US)| adv.|滑稽地，可笑地，愚蠢地|In a foolish and conceited manner, often in a way that is meant to be amusing.|He strutted around coxcomically, boasting about his latest conquest. 他滑稽地昂首阔步，吹嘘着他最新的征服。|Synonyms: ridiculously, foolishly, absurdly, preposterously, ludicrously|Antonyms: seriously, sensibly, rationally, wisely|词性:副词

coxcomb: /ˈkɒkskəʊm/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkskəʊm/ (US)| n.|纨绔子弟，花花公子，(旧时)帽子的顶饰|A conceited and foolish young man, especially one who dresses extravagantly. Historically, it also referred to the crest of a rooster or a jester's cap.|He was a coxcomb, always preening himself and showing off his expensive clothes. 他是个花花公子，总是打扮自己，炫耀他昂贵的衣服。|Synonyms: dandy, fop, popinjay|Antonyms: modest person|词性:名词
