nonformidablenesses:/ˌnɒnfɔːˈmɪdəblnəsɪz/
n.
lack of formidability; the quality or state of not being formidable
The nonformidableness of the opponent surprised the team. 对手的不足为惧令队伍感到惊讶。
nonformidability, harmlessness
formidability, terribleness
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
non- + formidable + -ness
non-:  prefix meaning "not"
formidable: from French formidable, from Latin formidabilis "causing fear," from formidare "to dread, fear," from formido "fear, dread."
-ness: suffix forming nouns from adjectives, denoting a state or quality.
