charcoal:/ˈtʃɑːrkoʊl/ (US) /ˈtʃɑːkəʊl/ (UK)| n. |木炭; 炭笔|A black porous form of carbon obtained from the partial burning of wood or other organic matter.  It is used as a fuel, a filter, an artist's material, and for other purposes.|She sketched the portrait in charcoal. 她用炭笔画了这幅肖像。|Synonyms: carbon, coal, cinder|Antonyms: diamond (in terms of crystalline structure and properties)|noun

charcoal:/ˈtʃɑːrkoʊl/ (US) /ˈtʃɑːkəʊl/ (UK)| adj. |炭黑色的; 木炭的|Of the color of charcoal; dark gray or black.|She wore a charcoal gray suit. 她穿着一套炭灰色的西装。|Synonyms: dark gray, black, sooty|Antonyms: light, white, bright|adjective
