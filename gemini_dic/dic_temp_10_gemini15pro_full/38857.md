confederacy:/kənˈfɛdərəsi/| n.|联盟，邦联，共谋|A union of states or groups, especially one formed for a particular purpose; a conspiracy.|The Southern Confederacy seceded from the United States in 1861. 南方邦联于1861年脱离美国。|Synonyms: alliance, league, coalition, federation, union|Antonyms: division, separation, disunity|noun

confederacies:/kənˈfɛdərəsiz/| n.|联盟，邦联，共谋（复数）|Plural form of "confederacy," referring to multiple unions of states or groups.|Throughout history, there have been many confederacies formed for various reasons. 历史上，由于各种原因，形成了许多邦联。|Synonyms: alliances, leagues, coalitions, federations, unions|Antonyms: divisions, separations, disunities|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *confœderatia*, from Latin confœderātus (past participle of confœderāre ‘join by treaty’), from con- ‘together’ + fœderāre (from fœdus ‘treaty’).
