metropolitan: /ˌmetrəˈpɒlɪtən/ (US) /ˌmetrəˈpɒlɪtən/ (UK)| adj. n.|大都市的，大都会的；大主教；大主教区的居民|Relating to a large city, its surrounding suburbs, and their inhabitants.  A person who lives in a large city.|例句：London is a metropolitan city. 伦敦是一座大都市。|近义词：urban, cosmopolitan, city|反义词：rural, provincial|词性：形容词, 名词

metropolitan: /ˌmetrəˈpɒlɪtən/ (US) /ˌmetrəˈpɒlɪtən/ (UK)| n.|大都市居民|A person who lives in a large city.|例句：He considers himself a metropolitan and enjoys the city life. 他认为自己是个大都市居民，享受城市生活。|近义词：city dweller, urbanite|反义词：countryman, rustic|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *metropolitanus, from Greek μητροπολίτης (mētropolítēs, “citizen of a metropolis, bishop of a capital city”), from μήτηρ (mḗtēr, “mother”) + πόλις (pólis, “city”).  The "mother city" was the original city of a colony, or the city that was the seat of a metropolitan bishop.
