cesarism:/ˈsiːzərɪzəm/| n.|凯撒主义，专制统治|A system of government by a dictator, especially one who has seized power by force, characterized by the concentration of power in the hands of a single ruler, often involving suppression of opposition and curtailment of civil liberties.  It derives its name from Julius Caesar, the Roman general and statesman who seized power and became dictator for life.|例句：The rise of cesarism in the 20th century led to many brutal dictatorships. 20世纪凯撒主义的兴起导致了许多残酷的独裁统治。

例句：Many feared that the new leader's policies were a step towards cesarism. 很多人担心新领导人的政策是走向凯撒主义的一步。|近义词：dictatorship, autocracy, tyranny, despotism, authoritarianism|反义词：democracy, republicanism|词性:noun
