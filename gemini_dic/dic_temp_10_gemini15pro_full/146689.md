overhears:/ˌoʊvərˈhɪrz/ | v. | 无意听到，偷听到 | To hear (something) without the speaker's intention or knowledge. |  I overheard them talking about the surprise party. 我无意中听到他们在谈论惊喜派对。| Synonyms: eavesdrop, listen in | Antonyms: ignore, disregard | verb

overhears:/ˌoʊvərˈhɪrz/ | v. | 偷听；偶然听到 | To hear (a conversation) that one is not meant to hear | She overheard a conversation between her boss and a client. 她无意中听到她老板和一个客户之间的谈话。| Synonyms: eavesdrop, listen in | Antonyms: ignore, disregard | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *overheren, from Old English oferhīeran (“to overhear, learn by hearsay”), from ofer (“over”) + hīeran (“to hear”). Cognate with Dutch overhoren (“to mishear, overhear”), German überhören (“to ignore, overhear, not hear”), Swedish överhöra (“to examine, interrogate”).
