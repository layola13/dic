bumming:/ˈbʌmɪŋ/| adj. v.| 令人沮丧的；烦人的；（向人）乞讨；流浪；蹭（烟、酒等）| Feeling depressed or annoyed; Asking someone for something (especially money or food); Travelling around without a permanent home or job; Getting something (like a cigarette or drink) from someone else for free.|例句：The constant rain is really bumming me out.  不停的雨真让我沮丧。|近义词：depressing, annoying, scrounging, begging, cadging|反义词：cheering, uplifting, giving|词性：adjective, present participle

bumming:/ˈbʌmɪŋ/| v.| （向人）乞讨；流浪；蹭（烟、酒等）| Asking someone for something (especially money or food); Travelling around without a permanent home or job; Getting something (like a cigarette or drink) from someone else for free.|例句：He was bumming cigarettes from strangers. 他向陌生人讨烟。|近义词：scrounging, begging, cadging|反义词：giving|词性：verb, present participle

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "bum," which likely originated from German "bummeln" (to loaf, stroll) or Dutch "bommelen" (to dangle, hang around).  The original sense of "bum" was to wander aimlessly.  The meaning evolved to include begging and then to the current sense of feeling down or depressed.
