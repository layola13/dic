dismayfully:/dɪˈsmeɪfəli/
adv. | 沮丧地，失望地 | In a manner expressing or filled with dismay; showing consternation and disappointment. |  He shook his head dismayfully at the news. 他听到这个消息沮丧地摇了摇头。|Synonyms: dejectedly, despondently, despairingly | Antonyms: cheerfully, hopefully, joyfully
词性: 副词


dismay:/dɪˈsmeɪ/
n. | 沮丧，失望，惊慌 |  A feeling of sudden or complete loss of courage; consternation. | The news of the defeat filled them with dismay.  战败的消息使他们充满了沮丧。| Synonyms: consternation, alarm, shock | Antonyms:  joy, delight, happiness
词性: 名词


dismay:/dɪˈsmeɪ/
v. | 使沮丧，使失望，使惊慌 | Cause (someone) to feel consternation and distress. | The sudden drop in sales dismayed investors. 销售额的突然下降使投资者感到沮丧。 | Synonyms: appall, horrify, shock | Antonyms: encourage, hearten, reassure
词性: 动词

词源:
From Old French *desmayer (Modern French désmayer) "lose courage, be dismayed," from des- "opposite of" + mayer "to be able" (from a Germanic source, related to Old English magan "to be able, have power," Gothic magan "to be able," German mögen "to like").
