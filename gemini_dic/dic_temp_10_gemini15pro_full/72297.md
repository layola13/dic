flounderer:/ˈflaʊndərər/| n.|挣扎者，笨拙者，不知所措的人|Someone who struggles or acts clumsily, especially in a situation where they don't know what to do.|He was a complete flounderer in social situations. 他在社交场合完全是个不知所措的人。|Synonyms: struggler, blunderer, bungler|Antonyms: expert, adept, professional|noun

flounder:/ˈflaʊndər/| v.|挣扎，笨拙地行动，不知所措|To struggle or act clumsily, especially in a situation where one doesn't know what to do.|She floundered through the interview, unable to answer the questions.  她在面试中磕磕绊绊，无法回答问题。|Synonyms: struggle, stumble, falter|Antonyms: succeed, thrive, excel|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *floundren, frequentative of *flounden, "to flounder," of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *flyndra, "flounder" (the fish), referring to its awkward movements.
