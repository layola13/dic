disquisitionist:/ˌdɪskwɪˈzɪʃənɪst/
n.
专题论文作者；长篇大论者
Someone who writes or delivers long, formal speeches or treatises, often on a scholarly or religious topic.
He was known as a disquisitionist, delivering lengthy lectures on obscure theological matters. 他以长篇大论而闻名，经常就晦涩的神学问题发表冗长的演讲。
synonyms:  lecturer, orator, expounder,  scholar, pedant
antonyms:  listener,  student, layman
noun


Etymology:
From Latin *disquisitio*  "inquiry, investigation," from *disquirere* "to seek out, inquire into," from *dis-* "apart, thoroughly" + *quaerere* "to seek, ask."
