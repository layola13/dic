masquerade:/ˌmæskəˈreɪd/ | n.  v. | 化妆舞会；伪装；掩饰 | A gathering of people wearing masks and costumes, typically for amusement.  To pretend to be someone or something one is not. |  例句：The annual masquerade ball was a grand affair. 年度化妆舞会是一场盛大的活动。  He masqueraded as a police officer to gain entry to the building. 他伪装成警察进入大楼。 | 近义词：disguise, pretense, pose, charade | 反义词：reality, truth, honesty | 词性: noun, verb

masquerades:/ˌmæskəˈreɪdz/ | n. | 化妆舞会 (plural) |  Plural form of masquerade. Refers to multiple masked gatherings or instances of disguise. | 例句：She attended several masquerades during the carnival season.  她在狂欢节期间参加了几场化妆舞会。 The city was known for its elaborate masquerades. 这座城市以其精心制作的化妆舞会而闻名。 | 近义词：disguises, pretenses | 反义词：realities, truths | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French "mascarade," from Italian "mascherata," from "maschera" (mask), from Arabic "maskhara" (buffoonery, mockery).
