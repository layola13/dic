nova:/ˈnoʊvə/ (US) /ˈnəʊvə/ (UK)| n. |新星|A star that suddenly becomes much brighter and then gradually returns to its original brightness.  A nova is caused by a nuclear explosion on the surface of a white dwarf star.|Astronomers observed a nova in the Andromeda galaxy.|Synonyms: supernova (although much brighter and rarer), cataclysmic variable|Antonyms:  No direct antonym, perhaps "dwarf star" in a contrasting sense.|Noun
