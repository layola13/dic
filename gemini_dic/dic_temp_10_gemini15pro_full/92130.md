hyperscrupulousness:/ˌhaɪpərˈskruːpjələsnəs/ | n. | 过分谨慎；过分小心；过于拘泥 |  The state of being excessively scrupulous; extreme conscientiousness and meticulousness, often to the point of being overly cautious or worried about moral or ethical matters. | His hyperscrupulousness prevented him from taking any risks in business. 他的过分谨慎使他在生意上不敢冒险。 | 近义词: conscientiousness, meticulousness, punctiliousness, fastidiousness | 反义词: carelessness, negligence, recklessness | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From hyper- (meaning "excessive," from Greek) + scrupulous (from Latin scrupulus, meaning "small sharp stone," hence "anxiety, uneasiness") + -ness (suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
