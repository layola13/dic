PHRASE->
"Hadn't" doesn't typically form set phrases.  It is primarily used as part of verb conjugations.  However, here are some examples within sentences demonstrating common usage:

I hadn't a clue what he was talking about. 我完全不知道他在说什么。

She hadn't the slightest idea where to go. 她一点儿也不知道该去哪里。

He hadn't the heart to tell her the bad news.  他不忍心告诉她这个坏消息。

I hadn't the time to finish the project. 我没有时间完成这个项目。


She hadn't the nerve to ask for a raise. 她没有勇气要求加薪.


词源: "hadn't" is a contraction of "had not". "Had" comes from Old English *hæfde, the past tense of *habban "to have".
