inconsecutiveness:/ˌɪnkənˌsɛkjʊˈtɪvnəs/ | n. |不连续性，不连贯性 | The state of being inconsecutive; lack of connection or continuity. | The inconsecutiveness of his speech made it difficult to follow. 他的讲话不连贯，很难理解。 |  近义词：discontinuity, disjointedness, incoherence | 反义词：continuity, coherence, consecutiveness | 词性: noun

Etymology: From in- (not) + consecutive + -ness.  "Consecutive" comes from Late Latin *consecutivus, from Latin consequi "to follow closely," from com- "together" + sequi "to follow."
