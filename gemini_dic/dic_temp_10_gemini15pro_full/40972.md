coopt:/koʊˈɑːpt/ | vt. | 指派；吸收；任命 | To appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members. To adopt (an idea, plan, etc.) for one's own use, often without the original intent. |  The rebels were eventually coopted into the government.  叛军最终被吸收到政府中来。

The advertising industry coopted the peace sign, turning it into a marketing tool. 广告业盗用了和平标志，将其变成了营销工具。| 近义词: appropriate, elect, appoint, select, enlist, incorporate, absorb | 反义词: reject, exclude, oust | 词性: 动词

coopt:/koʊˈɑːpt/ | n. |  (指派；吸收；任命)的行为 | The act of coopting. | The coopt of new members increased the diversity of the committee. 新成员的加入增加了委员会的多样性。 | 近义词: appointment, selection, incorporation | 反义词: rejection, exclusion | 词性: 名词 (较少使用)

coopt for: 为...而吸收/指派  (coopt members for a specific task 为一项特定任务吸收成员)

coopt from: 从...中吸收 (coopt ideas from other sources 从其他来源吸收想法)
