biland: /ˈbaɪlənd/ | n. | 双边; 两国; |  A biland is a historical term referring to a small merchant ship with two masts, common in Northern Europe. It was primarily used for coastal trade and short sea voyages. | The biland sailed swiftly along the coast. 这艘双桅小商船沿着海岸快速航行。 | The biland transported goods between neighboring ports. 这艘双桅小商船在邻近港口之间运输货物。| 近义词: sloop, ketch | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun


词源: From Middle Dutch *bieland, from bi- (meaning "two") + land.
