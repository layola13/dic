debilitated: /dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪd/ | adj. | 衰弱的，虚弱的 |  Made weak and less able to do things; enfeebled. |  He was severely debilitated by the illness. 他因病重而身体虚弱。| synonyms: weakened, enfeebled, incapacitated, frail, infirm | antonyms: strong, healthy, robust, vigorous | adjective

debilitate: /dɪˈbɪlɪteɪt/ | verb | 使衰弱，使虚弱 | To make someone or something physically weak. |  The stroke debilitated him. 中风使他身体衰弱。 | synonyms: weaken, enfeeble, incapacitate | antonyms: strengthen, invigorate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin debilitatus, past participle of debilitare (“to weaken”), from de- (“from, down from”) + habilis (“able, apt, fit”)
