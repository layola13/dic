bottle-feed:/ˈbɒtlˌfiːd/ (UK), /ˈbɑːtlˌfiːd/ (US)| v. n.|用奶瓶喂养|To feed a baby or young animal with milk from a bottle, not directly from the mother.|She had to bottle-feed her baby because she couldn't breastfeed. 她不得不奶瓶喂养她的宝宝，因为她无法母乳喂养。

bottle-feed:/ˈbɒtlˌfiːd/ (UK), /ˈbɑːtlˌfiːd/ (US)| v. n.|奶瓶喂养|The act of feeding a baby or young animal with milk from a bottle.|The bottle-feed was prepared at the right temperature. 奶瓶里的奶准备好了合适的温度。

bottle-feed:/ˈbɒtlˌfiːd/ (UK), /ˈbɑːtlˌfiːd/ (US)| n.|用奶瓶喂养的婴儿/动物|A baby or young animal that is fed with milk from a bottle.|The bottle-feed was sleeping soundly.  用奶瓶喂养的婴儿睡得很香。


Etymology:  Compound of "bottle" and "feed". "Bottle" comes from Old French "botele," diminutive of "bot," meaning "cask." "Feed" comes from Old English "fēdan," meaning "to nourish."

Synonyms: formula-feed (v.), hand-rear (v.)

Antonyms: breastfeed (v.), nurse (v.)
