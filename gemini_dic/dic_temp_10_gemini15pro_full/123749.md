moonwort:/ˈmuːnwɝt/| n. |月蕨，药蕨|A fern of the genus *Botrychium*, believed to have magical properties, especially to open locks or unshoe horses.  It is also known as grape fern due to the grape-like clusters of sporangia.|例句：Ancient folklore suggested that moonwort could unlock any door. 古代民间传说认为月蕨可以打开任何门。

例句：The alchemist searched for moonwort, believing it held the key to transmutation.  炼金术士寻找月蕨，认为它掌握着嬗变的关键。|近义词：grape fern|反义词: N/A |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *monewort*, from Old English *mōnanwyrt* (moon plant), composed of *mōna* (moon) + *wyrt* (plant, wort, herb). The name derives from the shape of the leaflets, resembling a crescent moon.
