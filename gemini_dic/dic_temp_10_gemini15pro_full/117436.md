mediocrity: /ˌmiːdiˈɒkrəti/ (US) /ˌmiːdiˈɔːkrəti/ (UK)| n.| 平庸；平凡；平庸之才|The state or quality of being average or ordinary; lacking distinction or excellence.|His acting career descended into mediocrity after his initial success.  他最初的成功之后，他的演艺事业就变得平庸了。|Synonyms: averageness, ordinariness, commonplaceness|Antonyms: excellence, brilliance, superiority|noun

mediocre: /ˌmiːdiˈoʊkər/ (US) /ˌmiːdiˈəʊkə(r)/ (UK)| adj.| 平庸的；平凡的；不够好的|Of only average quality; not very good.|The restaurant served mediocre food at high prices. 这家餐馆价格昂贵，但食物却很一般。|Synonyms: average, ordinary, undistinguished|Antonyms: excellent, outstanding, exceptional|adjective
