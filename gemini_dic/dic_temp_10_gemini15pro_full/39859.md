constantine:/ˈkɑːnstənˌtiːn/ /ˈkɒnstəntiːn/| n.|君士坦丁|A male given name, derived from the Roman family name Constantinus, meaning "steadfast" or "constant".  It is most famously associated with Constantine the Great, the Roman emperor who legalized Christianity.|Constantine was the first Roman emperor to convert to Christianity.|近义词:Constantius|反义词: N/A|词性:Noun
