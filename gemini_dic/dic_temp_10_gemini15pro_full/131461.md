noncohabitation: /ˌnɒnkəʊˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 未同居 | The state of not living together, especially of an unmarried couple. |  The increasing rates of noncohabitation among young couples reflect changing societal norms. 年轻夫妇中未同居率的上升反映了不断变化的社会规范。

Noncohabitation can be a practical choice for couples who are geographically separated. 对于地理上分开的夫妇来说，不同居可能是一个实际的选择。  | 近义词：living apart, separate residence | 反义词：cohabitation, living together | 词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + cohabitation.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "cohabitation" comes from the Latin "cohabitare," meaning "to dwell together," from "co-" (together) + "habitare" (to dwell).
