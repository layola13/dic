antinora:/ˌæn.tɪˈnɔːr.ə/ |n.| 安蒂诺拉 |The lowest circle of Cocytus, the ninth and final circle of Hell in Dante's Inferno, reserved for those who betrayed their benefactors.| Dante describes Antinora as a frozen lake in his Inferno, where traitors to their country or political party are encased in ice up to their necks. |Example 1:  In Dante's *Inferno*, Count Ugolino is found gnawing on the head of Archbishop Ruggieri in Antinora. 在但丁的《地狱篇》中，乌戈利诺伯爵被发现在安蒂诺拉啃咬着鲁杰里大主教的头。

Example 2:  The suffering in Antinora is a stark reminder of the consequences of betrayal. 安蒂诺拉的苦难是对背叛后果的严厉提醒。|Synonym: Cocytus (partially synonymous, as Antinora is part of Cocytus)|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, as it is a specific place in a fictional work.)|noun
