hail-mary-pass:/ˌheɪl ˈmɛəri ˈpæs/ | n. | 孤注一掷，冒险一搏 | A long, high-risk pass in American football, typically made in desperation near the end of a game. It is named after the Catholic prayer "Hail Mary". |  The quarterback threw a Hail Mary pass into the end zone as time expired. 四分卫在比赛时间结束时向端区扔出了一记孤注一掷的传球。| synonym: last-ditch effort, desperate attempt, long shot | antonym: safe bet, sure thing | noun
