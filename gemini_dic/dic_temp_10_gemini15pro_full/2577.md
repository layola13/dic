admonish: /ədˈmɒnɪʃ/ (UK) /ˌædˈmɑːnɪʃ/ (US)| vt.| 告诫，劝诫，警告 | To warn or advise someone firmly, especially about their behaviour. | The teacher admonished the students for being late. 老师告诫学生不要迟到。

The librarian admonished him for talking too loudly. 图书管理员警告他说话太大声了。

近义词: reprimand, rebuke, scold, caution, warn | 反义词: praise, commend, encourage | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *admonester, from Latin admonēre ("to remind, advise, warn"), from ad- ("to") + monēre ("to warn").

USAGE->
一般现在时:
I admonish him every time he misbehaves. = 每当他行为不当时，我都会告诫他。
She admonishes her children to be polite to strangers. = 她告诫她的孩子们要对陌生人有礼貌。


一般过去时:
The teacher admonished the student for cheating on the test. = 老师因为学生考试作弊而告诫他。
My mother admonished me for not cleaning my room. = 我妈妈因为我没有打扫房间而告诫我。


现在完成时:
I have admonished him several times about his tardiness. = 我已经多次告诫他不要迟到。
They have admonished her against associating with known criminals. = 他们告诫过她不要与已知的罪犯来往。


过去完成时:
He had admonished her about the dangers of driving too fast before she left. = 在她离开之前，他已经告诫过她开车太快的危险。
The police had admonished them to stay away from the area. = 警方曾告诫他们远离该地区。


将来时:
I will admonish him if he does that again. = 如果他再这样做，我会告诫他。
The coach will admonish the team if they don't improve their performance. = 如果球队不提高他们的表现，教练会告诫他们。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would admonish him privately. = 如果我是你，我会私下告诫他。
I wish I had admonished her sooner. = 我希望我早点告诫她。


被动语态:
He was admonished by his boss for missing the deadline. = 他因为错过截止日期而被老板告诫。
The children were admonished not to play near the road. = 孩子们被告诫不要在路边玩耍。


疑问句:
Why did you admonish her so harshly? = 你为什么这么严厉地告诫她？
Has he been admonished before? = 他以前被告诫过吗？


否定句:
I didn't admonish him, I just offered some advice. = 我没有告诫他，我只是提供了一些建议。
She hasn't been admonished yet. = 她还没有被告诫过。


PHRASE->
admonish someone against something = 告诫某人不要做某事
admonish someone for something = 因某事告诫某人
admonish someone to do something = 告诫某人做某事
be admonished by someone = 被某人告诫
severely admonish = 严厉告诫
