brattice: /ˈbrætɪs/ (US), /ˈbrætɪs/ (UK)| n. vt.|隔板，挡板；用隔板隔开|A partition or wall, typically temporary, used to regulate ventilation in a mine or to enclose a dangerous area.  To divide or separate with a brattice.|

例句：
The miners erected a brattice to seal off the area where the gas leak had occurred.
矿工们竖起隔板，封锁了发生瓦斯泄漏的区域。

The team bratticed the tunnel to control airflow.
团队用隔板隔开隧道来控制气流。


近义词：partition, barrier, bulkhead, screen|
反义词: opening|
词性: noun, verb|


词源:
From Old French *bretesche "wooden tower, fortification," from Old North French bretesche, from Frankish *brettia "plank, board," from Proto-Germanic *bruthjon-, from PIE root *bher- (2) "to cut, split."  The sense shifted from a defensive structure to a mine partition.
