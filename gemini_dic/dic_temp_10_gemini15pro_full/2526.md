admirable:/ˈædmərəbl/| adj.|令人钦佩的，值得赞赏的|Deserving respect and approval.|Her dedication to her students is admirable. 她对学生的奉献精神令人钦佩。

His courage in the face of danger was admirable. 面对危险时的勇气令人敬佩。


近义词:praiseworthy, commendable, laudable, excellent, wonderful|反义词:deplorable, despicable, contemptible|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from late Latin *admirabilis, from Latin admirari ‘to wonder at’.

USAGE->
Attributive Use (modifying a noun):

He showed admirable courage. = 他展现了令人钦佩的勇气。
She has an admirable work ethic. = 她有令人钦佩的职业道德。

Predicative Use (following a linking verb):

His behavior was admirable. = 他的行为令人钦佩。
The team's performance was admirable, considering the circumstances. = 考虑到具体情况，团队的表现令人钦佩。

With "find" or "consider":

I find her dedication admirable. = 我觉得她的奉献精神令人钦佩。
We consider his achievements admirable. = 我们认为他的成就令人钦佩。

In exclamations:

How admirable! = 多么令人钦佩！
That's admirable! = 那真是令人钦佩！

With "most" or "very":

It was a most admirable achievement. = 那是一项非常令人钦佩的成就。
Her patience is very admirable. = 她的耐心非常令人钦佩。


PHRASE->
an admirable quality = 一个令人钦佩的品质
an admirable person = 一个令人钦佩的人
admirable restraint = 令人钦佩的克制
truly admirable = 真正令人钦佩的
most admirable = 最令人钦佩的
