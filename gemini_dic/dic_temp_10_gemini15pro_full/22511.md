boomerangers:/ˈbuːməræŋərz/
n.
回归者；回飞镖者；自食其果者
People who return to their original place or situation, often after leaving for a period of time; also refers to those whose actions have unintended negative consequences for themselves.
Many boomerangers returned to their hometowns after failing to find success in the big city. 许多回归者在大城市闯荡失败后回到了家乡。
After insulting his boss, John's career took a hit, proving that his actions were like a boomerang.  约翰侮辱了老板后，事业受到了打击，证明了他的行为就像回飞镖一样。
近义词:returnees, expats returning home
反义词:emigrants, expatriates
词性:名词

boomerang:/ˈbuːməræŋ/
n.
回飞镖；自食其果的行为
A curved wooden weapon that can be thrown so as to return to the thrower; also figuratively refers to actions that have unintended negative consequences for the doer.
The hunter threw the boomerang with skill. 猎人熟练地扔出了回飞镖。
His attempt to sabotage his colleague backfired like a boomerang. 他试图破坏同事的计划适得其反，就像回飞镖一样。
近义词:self-defeating action, backlash
反义词:advantage, benefit
词性:名词

词源:
From Dharug (Australian Aboriginal language) *bumarin, *bumarin, "curved throwing stick."
