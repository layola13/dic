biases: /ˈbaɪəsɪz/ | n. | 偏见，偏爱，倾向 |  A particular tendency, trend, inclination, feeling, or opinion, especially one that is preconceived or unreasoned.  |  The judge was accused of having biases against the defendant.  法官被指控对被告有偏见。| synonyms: prejudices, preconceptions, leanings | antonyms: impartiality, objectivity | noun

biases: /ˈbaɪəsɪz/ | v. | 使有偏见 | To cause someone or something to have a bias; to influence in an unfair way. | The media coverage biased public opinion against the candidate. 媒体的报道使公众对这位候选人产生了偏见。| synonyms: influence, sway, predispose | antonyms: neutralize, balance | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *biais* "slant, oblique line," of uncertain origin, possibly from Provençal *biais*, from a Vulgar Latin word *bigassius*.
