naivete:/naɪˈiːvteɪ/ /nɑːˈiːvteɪ/ | n. | 天真;幼稚;单纯 | The state or quality of being naive; lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment. | Her naivete about the dangers of the city was alarming. 她对城市危险的无知令人担忧。| synonyms: innocence, ingenuousness, artlessness, credulity | antonyms: sophistication, worldliness, cynicism, shrewdness | noun

naive:/naɪˈiːv/ /nɑːˈiːv/ | adj. | 天真的;幼稚的;单纯的 | Showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment. |  He was naive enough to believe her lies. 他太天真了，竟然相信了她的谎言。| synonyms: innocent, ingenuous, artless, credulous | antonyms: sophisticated, worldly, cynical, shrewd


ETYMOLOGY->
From French naïveté, from naïf "naive," from Latin nativus "native, natural, innate."
