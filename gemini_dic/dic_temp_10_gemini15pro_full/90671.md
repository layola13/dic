humblers:/ˈhʌmblərz/| n. |使人谦卑的人或事物；挫折；打击|People or things that cause someone to feel less proud or arrogant.  Something that makes you realize your limitations.|The recent defeat was a humbler for the team.  最近的失败让团队受到了打击。|Synonyms:  chastening experience, setback, blow, comeuppance|Antonyms:  boost, encouragement, triumph|Noun

humble:/ˈhʌmbəl/| adj. |谦逊的；卑微的；简陋的|Having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance. Of low social, administrative, or political rank.  Plain and unpretentious.|He was a humble man, despite his great achievements. 尽管他取得了巨大的成就，他仍然是一个谦虚的人。|Synonyms: modest, unpretentious, meek, unassuming|Antonyms: arrogant, proud, haughty, conceited|Adjective

humble:/ˈhʌmbəl/| v. |使谦卑；使感到卑微；降低…的地位|Lower (someone) in dignity or importance. To defeat or subdue (someone).|The loss humbled the champion.  这场失利使冠军感到谦卑。|Synonyms:  humiliate, abase, subdue, defeat|Antonyms:  exalt, elevate, promote|Verb
