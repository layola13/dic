hagiography:/ˌhæɡiˈɒɡrəfi/ /ˌheɪdʒiˈɑːɡrəfi/
n.|圣徒传记；理想化的传记|An idealized biography, especially of a saint or religious figure.  A biography that treats its subject with undue reverence.|The campaign biography was pure hagiography.|eulogy, panegyric, encomium|criticism, exposé|noun
hagiographic:/ˌhæɡiəˈɡræfɪk/ /ˌheɪdʒiəˈɡræfɪk/
adj.|圣徒传记的；理想化的|Relating to hagiography; excessively flattering or idealized.|His campaign speeches were filled with hagiographic accounts of his accomplishments.|laudatory, adulatory, worshipful|critical, objective|adjective
hagiographer:/ˌhæɡiˈɒɡrəfər/ /ˌheɪdʒiˈɑːɡrəfər/
n.|圣徒传记作者|A writer of hagiographies.|Many early hagiographers embellished the lives of the saints with miraculous events.|biographer, chronicler, historian|critic, detractor|noun
