commonplace:/ˈkɒmənpleɪs/ (UK), /ˈkɑːmənpleɪs/ (US)| adj. n.| 平凡的，普通的；司空见惯的事；陈词滥调 |  Lacking originality or novelty; trite; ordinary. |  Her poetry is full of commonplace observations about nature.  她的诗歌充满了关于自然界的陈词滥调。 | synonyms: ordinary, mundane, banal, trite, clichéd, pedestrian | antonyms: original, novel, unusual, extraordinary, remarkable | adjective

commonplace:/ˈkɒmənpleɪs/ (UK), /ˈkɑːmənpleɪs/ (US)| n.| 平凡的事；司空见惯的事 | Something that is ordinary or unremarkable. | Air travel has become commonplace.  空中旅行已变得司空见惯。 | synonyms: cliché, banality, platitude | antonyms: novelty, rarity, phenomenon | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From common + place.  Meaning "ordinary, of frequent occurrence" from mid-16th century.  Originally as a noun meaning "an ordinary topic of conversation."
