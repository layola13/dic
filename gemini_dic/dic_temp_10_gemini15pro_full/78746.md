gigs:/ɡɪɡz/| n.|演出; (尤指临时性的) 工作;|A gig is a live performance by a musician or group of musicians, especially playing modern or pop music; or a job, especially one that is temporary or that has a short-term contract.|例句：He's got a gig in a pub tonight. 他今晚在酒吧有场演出。

例句：She's trying to find a gig as a waitress. 她正试图找一份服务员的工作。|近义词：performance, concert, job, assignment|反义词: unemployment, leisure|词性:noun


gigs:/ɡɪɡz/| v. (third-person singular simple present gigs, present participle gigging, simple past and past participle gigged)|演奏; 做零工|To play a gig; to work at a series of temporary jobs.|例句：The band is gigging at the club tomorrow night.  乐队明晚在俱乐部演出。

例句：He's been gigging around the country, trying to make ends meet. 他一直在全国各地做零工，勉强维持生计。|近义词：perform, play, work|反义词: rest, relax|词性:verb
