black-jacking: /ˌblækˈdʒækɪŋ/ | n. |敲诈；勒索；强取；胁迫；(尤指)以暴力威胁|The act of extorting money from someone by threats or violence, especially by threatening to reveal discreditable information.| He was accused of black-jacking the businessman. 他被指控勒索那位商人.| 近义词: extortion, blackmail, intimidation | 反义词: donation, charity | 词性: 名词

词源:  The term likely originates from the late 19th/early 20th century American underworld slang. "Black Jack" was a name for a bludgeon or blackjack, a small, easily concealed club.  The verb "to blackjack" then evolved to mean attacking someone with a blackjack, and later, figuratively, to extort or coerce someone.
