noncustomarily: /ˌnɑːnkʌstəˈmɛrəli/ /ˌnɒnkʌstəˈmɛrɪli/ | adv. | 非惯常地，不寻常地 | Not in a customary manner; unusually. |  He noncustomarily arrived early for work. 他非惯常地早早来上班了。 |  She noncustomarily wore a bright dress; she usually prefers dark colors. 她不寻常地穿了一件鲜艳的连衣裙；她通常更喜欢深色。 | 近义词: unusually, atypically, uncommonly, exceptionally | 反义词: customarily, usually, typically, habitually | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  Meaning "not," from Latin *non < *noenum (ne oinom, not one).
customary (adjective):  From Old French coustumier, from coustume "custom," from Latin consuetudinem (nominative consuetudo) "habit, usage, way, intimacy, acquaintance," from consuetus, past participle of consuescere "accustom," from com-, intensive prefix (see com-), + suescere "become accustomed," related to suus "one's own."
-ly (suffix): Forms adverbs from adjectives.
