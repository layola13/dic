ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + feebleness (weakness).  "Feebleness" comes from the Middle English "feble," from Old French "feble," ultimately from Latin "flebilis" (lamentable, mournful), from "flere" (to weep). The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives.
