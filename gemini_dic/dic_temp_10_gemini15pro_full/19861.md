bine:/baɪn/ | n. | 藤蔓，攀缘植物；啤酒花藤蔓 |A climbing plant, especially a hop vine. |The farmer carefully cultivated the bine so the hops would grow well. 农民精心培育啤酒花藤蔓，以使其啤酒花长势良好。|Synonyms: vine, creeper, climber |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "tree" or "shrub" as they are self-supporting) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bīan, from Proto-Germanic *bīną (“bee”).  Related to Old High German bīna (“bee”), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *bʰey- (“bee”).  The connection to bees is likely due to the climbing, clinging nature of the plant, similar to how bees cling to flowers.
