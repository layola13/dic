acclimature: /əˈklaɪmətʃər/ |n. v. |适应，驯化；适应环境|The process of becoming accustomed to a new climate or environment.  The acclimature of the new recruits took several weeks. 新兵的适应环境过程需要数周。|例句：The climbers needed time to acclimature to the high altitude. 登山者需要时间来适应高海拔。

The zookeepers are trying to acclimature the panda to its new enclosure.  动物园管理员正试图让熊猫适应它的新围栏。|近义词：acclimatize, adapt, adjust, habituate|反义词: unaccustomed |词性:名词, 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The plant acclimates well to different temperatures. =  这种植物能很好地适应不同的温度。
She acclimates quickly to new situations. = 她很快就能适应新环境。


一般过去时:
It took him a while to acclimate to the city life. = 他花了一段时间才适应城市生活。
The animals acclimated to their new surroundings surprisingly fast. = 动物们以惊人的速度适应了新的环境。


现在完成时:
The explorers have acclimated to the harsh conditions of the desert. = 探险家们已经适应了沙漠的恶劣条件。
They have acclimated themselves to the local customs. = 他们已经适应了当地的风俗习惯。


过去完成时:
By the time the expedition began, they had acclimated to the cold weather. = 到探险开始时，他们已经适应了寒冷的天气。
She had acclimated to the high altitude before attempting the climb. = 她在尝试攀登之前已经适应了高海拔。


将来时:

We will acclimate ourselves to the new time zone within a few days. = 我们会在几天内适应新的时区。


虚拟语气:
If I were to move to a colder climate, I would need time to acclimate. = 如果我要搬到一个更冷的地方，我需要时间来适应。
I wish I had acclimated to the heat before the marathon. = 我希望我在马拉松比赛前就适应了炎热的天气。
