customs:/ˈkʌstəmz/ | n. | 海关；关税；习俗 | the place at a port, airport, or border where travelers' bags are examined for illegal or taxable goods; taxes, tariffs, or duties imposed by a government on imported or exported goods; established socially accepted practices or ways of behaving |  例句：It took us ages to get through customs. 我们花了很长时间才通过海关。 | 近义词：traditions, habits, practices, duties, tariffs | 反义词：N/A | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *custume* (modern French *coutume*), from Vulgar Latin *costuma*, a variant of Latin *consuetudo* "custom, habit, usage, familiarity, intimacy, acquaintance," from *consuescere* "accustom," from *con-* "with" + *suescere* "become accustomed."
