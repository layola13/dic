micmacs:/ˈmɪkmæks/| n. |诡计，阴谋，复杂而混乱的局面|complicated schemes or intrigues; a confused or chaotic state of affairs|The investigation uncovered a whole series of political micmacs. 调查揭露了一系列政治阴谋。|近义词：machinations, intrigues, shenanigans, skullduggery|反义词: straightforwardness, honesty|noun

词源:
From Mi'kmaq (pronounced [miːkmax]), the name of a First Nations people indigenous to Canada's eastern Maritime provinces.  Originally referred to their complex and often confusing (to outsiders) political and social structures. Over time, the term evolved to refer to intricate plots and schemes in general.
