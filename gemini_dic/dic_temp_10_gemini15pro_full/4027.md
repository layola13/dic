ahull:/əˈhʌl/| adj.| 船体在水上; (尤指船) 搁浅 | With the tide out, the boat lay ahull. | The ship was ahull in the storm. | adrift, grounded | afloat | adjective
Etymology:  From a (on) + hull (body of a ship).  The term literally means "on the hull," implying resting on the hull, out of the water or barely afloat.
