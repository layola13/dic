duty-bound:/ˈdjuːti baʊnd/ | adj. | 义不容辞的，有义务的 |  Compelled by a sense of duty or obligation to do something.  Feeling that one must do something because it is the right or moral thing to do, even if it is difficult or unpleasant.|  I felt duty-bound to help my elderly neighbor. 我觉得有义务帮助我年迈的邻居。 | Synonyms: obligated, compelled, required, responsible | Antonyms:  unobligated, free, exempt | adjective

duty-bound:/ˈdjuːti baʊnd/ | adj. | 必须的，有责任的 | Required by one's sense of duty or responsibility. Feeling a strong moral or ethical obligation to perform a particular action. | As a doctor, she felt duty-bound to report the suspected child abuse. 作为一名医生，她觉得有义务报告疑似虐待儿童的行为。 | Synonyms: morally obligated, ethically bound, honor-bound | Antonyms: irresponsible, negligent, unaccountable | adjective
