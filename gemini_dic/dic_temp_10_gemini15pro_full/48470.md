deep:/diːp/
adjective|深的，深奥的，强烈的|extending far down from the top or surface. Also, profound, intense.|The well is very deep. 这口井很深。|近义词：profound, intense, submerged, bottomless|反义词：shallow, superficial|词性:adjective
deep:/diːp/
adverb|深深地，在深处|far down or in.|He breathed deep. 他深深地吸了一口气。|近义词：deeply, profoundly|反义词：shallowly, superficially|词性:adverb
deep:/diːp/
noun|深处，深海|the deepest part.|The ship sank to the deep. 船沉到了海底。|近义词：abyss, depths|反义词：surface, shallows|词性:noun

词源:
From Middle English *dēp*, from Old English *dēop* (“deep, profound, low”), from Proto-Germanic *dēupaz* (“deep”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰewbh- (“deep, hollow”). Cognate with West Frisian *djip* (“deep”), Dutch *diep* (“deep”), German *tief* (“deep”), Danish *dyb* (“deep”), Swedish *djup* (“deep”), Icelandic *djúpur* (“deep”).
