elevatedly: /ˈɛləˌveɪtɪdli/ | adv. | 高尚地，崇高地，傲慢地 | In a manner that is raised or lofty, either literally or figuratively; also, in a haughty or aloof manner. | She spoke elevatedly about the importance of art. 她高谈阔论艺术的重要性。 | He dismissed the idea elevatedly, as if it were beneath him. 他傲慢地否定了这个想法，好像它配不上他。 | 近义词：loftily, haughtily, pompously, arrogantly | 反义词：humbly, modestly, simply | 词性: 副词

词源:  From elevate + -ed + -ly.  "Elevate" comes from Latin *elevatus, past participle of elevare "to lift up," from ex- "out, up" + levare "to lift."
