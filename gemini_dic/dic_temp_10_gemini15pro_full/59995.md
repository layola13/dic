editorialised: /ˌedɪˈtɔːriəlaɪzd/ | v. | 编辑化; 加上评论 | To present news or information in a way that expresses a particular opinion or bias, rather than just reporting the facts objectively. | The news report was heavily editorialised, making it clear which side the network supported. 这篇新闻报道被严重地编辑化了，清楚地表明了该网络支持哪一方。| The journalist editorialised the story to reflect his own political views. 记者编辑了这个故事以反映他自己的政治观点。 |  近义词: slanted, biased, opinionated, subjective | 反义词: objective, impartial, unbiased, neutral | 词性: 动词

词源:  From "editorial" + "-ise/-ize" (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become like," "to subject to the action or process of"). "Editorial" itself comes from "editor," ultimately from Latin "editor" (one who publishes).
