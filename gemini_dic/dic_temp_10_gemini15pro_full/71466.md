flameproofs:/ˈfleɪmpruːfs/
| n. |防火材料，阻燃剂|Materials or substances used to make something resistant to catching fire or burning.|The curtains were made of heavy flameproofs.|近义词：fire retardants, fireproofing materials|反义词：flammable materials|词性:名词
flameproof:/ˈfleɪmpruːf/
| adj. |防火的，阻燃的|Resistant to catching fire or burning.|Flameproof clothing is essential for firefighters.|近义词：fire-resistant, non-flammable|反义词：flammable, combustible|词性:形容词
flameproof:/ˈfleɪmpruːf/
| v. |使…防火，使…阻燃|To treat (something) with a substance to make it resistant to catching fire or burning.|The workers flameproofed the costumes before the performance.|近义词：fireproof, fire-retard|反义词：ignite|词性:动词


词源:  "flame" + "proof".  "Flame" comes from Old French "flamme," ultimately from Latin "flamma". "Proof" comes from Old French "prove," from Latin "probare" (to test, prove).
