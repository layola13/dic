idler:/ˈaɪdlər/| n.|懒汉；闲人；游手好闲的人|A person who habitually avoids work or is lazy.|He was known as an idler who never lifted a finger to help. 他是个出了名的懒汉，从来不帮忙。|Synonyms: loafer, slacker, lounger|Antonyms: worker, busybody|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *idler*, from *idle* + *-er*, from Old English *īdel* ("empty, vain, useless, futile, barren, trifling") and *-ere*.  Cognate with German *eitel* ("vain, futile").
