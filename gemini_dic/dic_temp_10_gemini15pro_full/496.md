abundance:/əˈbʌndəns/| n. |丰富，充裕|A very large quantity of something.|The garden yielded an abundance of fruits and vegetables this year. 今年花园里产出了丰富的水果和蔬菜。|Synonyms: plenty, profusion, wealth, copiousness|Antonyms: scarcity, lack, dearth, deficiency|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *abondance*, from Latin *abundantia*, from *abundans* (“overflowing, abundant”), present participle of *abundare* (“to overflow, abound”), from *ab* (“from”) + *undare* (“to surge, overflow”), from *unda* (“wave”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *hewnd-* (“water, wave”).

USAGE->
There is an abundance of evidence to support this theory. = 有大量的证据支持这一理论。

The country has an abundance of natural resources. = 这个国家拥有丰富的自然资源。


PHRASE->
in abundance = 大量地；丰富地
an abundance of = 大量的；丰富的
abundance of the heart =  (发自内心的)真诚；热情洋溢
abundance of caution = 谨慎起见; 为了慎重起见
live in abundance =  生活富裕
