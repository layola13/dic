circumspectively:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈspektɪvli/ | adv. | 谨慎地，小心地 | In a cautious and prudent manner, considering all possible consequences. |  He acted circumspectively, weighing each decision carefully. 他行事谨慎，仔细权衡每一个决定。|  She circumspectively approached the unfamiliar dog. 她小心翼翼地靠近那只不熟悉的狗。 | cautiously, prudently, carefully, discreetly | rashly, recklessly, impulsively | adverb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *circumspectīvus, from Latin circumspectus (“looked around, cautious”), past participle of circumspicere (“to look around, be cautious”), from circum- (“around”) + specere (“to look”).
