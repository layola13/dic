denization:/ˌdiːnaɪˈzeɪʃən/ |n.|入籍；赋予公民权；居民权|The act of granting the rights and privileges of a citizen to a foreigner; the state of being admitted to residence and certain rights in a foreign country. |He applied for denization after living in the country for five years. 他在该国居住五年后申请了入籍。|Synonyms: naturalization, citizenship, enfranchisement|Antonyms: expatriation, deportation, banishment|noun

denize:/ˈdiːnaɪz/ |v.|授予…居民权；准许…入籍；归化|To grant the rights and privileges of a citizen to; to admit to residence and certain rights in a foreign country.|The king denized the refugees, allowing them to live and work in his kingdom. 国王给予难民居住权，允许他们在他的王国生活和工作。|Synonyms: naturalize, enfranchise|Antonyms: expatriate, deport, banish|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *denizen, from Anglo-Norman denisein, Old French denezein, from denzier “to grant the rights of a denizen,” from Vulgar Latin *denaizare, from Late Latin deintus “within.” Ultimately from Latin de- "from" + intus "within."
