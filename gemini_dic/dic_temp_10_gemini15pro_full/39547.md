conscience's:/ˈkɒnʃənsɪz/ | n. |良心的, 道德心的|The possessive form of "conscience," referring to something that belongs to or is associated with one's sense of right and wrong.|  Her conscience's voice told her to confess.| 近义词: moral compass, inner voice | 反义词: immorality, wickedness | 词性: 名词

词源: From Old French *conscience, from Latin conscientia (“knowledge within oneself, consciousness”), from con- (“with”) + scire (“to know”).
