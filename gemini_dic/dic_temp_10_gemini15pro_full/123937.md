morglay:/ˈmɔːɡleɪ/| n. |魔剑，宝剑|A legendary sword, especially that of Bevis of Hampton.  It is often depicted as possessing magical properties. |Sir Bevis drew forth Morglay, his trusty morglay, and prepared for battle. 贝维斯爵士拔出魔剑莫格莱，他信赖的莫格莱，准备战斗。|近义词：sword, blade, brand|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "sheath")|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *`Morgelay`*, from Old French `Morcley`, perhaps ultimately from Vulgar Latin *`*mauri gladius*`*  ("Moorish sword").
