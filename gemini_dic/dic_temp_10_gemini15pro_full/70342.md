fieldworkers:/ˈfiːldˌwɜːrkərz/ (US), /ˈfiːldˌwɜːkəz/ (UK)| n. |野外工作人员；现场工作人员|People who work in the field, conducting research, collecting data, or performing other tasks.|The fieldworkers collected samples of the local flora. 野外工作人员收集了当地植物群的样本。|The fieldworkers interviewed residents about their experiences.  现场工作人员采访了居民，了解他们的经历。|近义词：field staff, researchers, investigators, surveyors|反义词: office workers, desk workers|词性:名词

Etymology:
field (n.) Old English feld "open country," of West Germanic origin.
work (n.) Old English weorc (noun), wyrcan (verb) "to do, perform," of West Germanic origin.
-er (suffix) forming nouns designating persons from verbs or other nouns.
-s (suffix) forming plural nouns.
