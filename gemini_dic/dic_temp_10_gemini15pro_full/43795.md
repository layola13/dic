cremor:/ˈkriːmɔːr/ (US), /ˈkriːmɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. | 奶油，乳膏；精华，精髓 | A thick, rich, yellowish-white fatty substance obtained by skimming the top layer of milk or by separating the fatty component of milk by centrifugal force. It is used in cooking, as a spread, and as a base for making ice cream, butter, and cheese.  It can also figuratively refer to the best part of something. |  The chef added a dollop of cremor to the sauce.  厨师在酱汁里加了一团奶油。| Synonyms: cream, richness, essence, best | Antonyms: dregs, worst, residue | noun

cremor:/ˈkriːmɔːr/ (US), /ˈkriːmɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. |  (古语)  浓汁，汤 | A thick broth or soup.  (This usage is archaic.) | The medieval feast featured a cremor of mushrooms.  中世纪的宴会上有一道蘑菇浓汤。(古语用法) | Synonyms: broth, soup, consommé | Antonyms: thin soup, watery broth | noun
