mucking:/ˈmʌkɪŋ/| v.|清除；挖掘；弄脏；闲逛|  The act of cleaning, digging, making something dirty, or messing around. |例句：They were mucking out the stables. 他们在清理马厩。|近义词：cleaning, digging, messing, dawdling|反义词:organizing, tidying|verb
mucking:/ˈmʌkɪŋ/| n.|清除；挖掘；弄脏| The material (usually manure) that is removed when cleaning a stable. |例句：The mucking from the stables was used as fertilizer.  马厩里的粪便被用作肥料。|近义词：manure, dirt, filth|反义词:cleanliness|noun
mucking:/ˈmʌkɪŋ/| adj.|糟糕的；讨厌的 (Informal, chiefly British)|  Awful; unpleasant. |例句："That's a mucking nuisance!" he exclaimed. “真是讨厌！”他 exclaimed.|近义词：awful, terrible, dreadful|反义词:wonderful, excellent|adjective
