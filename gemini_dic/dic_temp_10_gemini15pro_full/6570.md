anabaptistic:/ˌænəbæpˈtɪstɪk/ | adj. | 再洗礼派的；激进的改革的 | Of or relating to Anabaptists, a radical Protestant sect that emerged during the Reformation.  Characterized by adult baptism, pacifism, and often communal living. |  例句：His anabaptistic views on baptism caused quite a stir in the community. 他对洗礼的再洗礼派观点在社区引起了不小的轰动。

例句：The community adopted an anabaptistic approach to property, sharing everything.  这个社区对财产采取了再洗礼派的方式，共享一切。 | 近义词：radical, sectarian, nonconformist | 反义词：orthodox, conventional, conformist | 词性: adjective

Etymology: From Anabaptist + -ic.  Anabaptist comes from the Greek ana (again) + baptizein (to baptize).
