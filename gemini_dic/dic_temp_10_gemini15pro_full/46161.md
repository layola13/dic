cynicalness:/ˈsɪnɪkəlnəs/| n.|愤世嫉俗，玩世不恭|The state or quality of being cynical; cynicism.  A distrustful and pessimistic view of human nature and motives.|Her cynicalness about politics made her reluctant to vote.|近义词：cynicism, pessimism, misanthropy, distrust, skepticism|反义词：optimism, idealism, trust, faith, naiveté|词性:noun

Etymology:
From cynical + -ness.  Cynical comes from the Greek word *kunikos* meaning "dog-like," referring to the Cynic philosophers of ancient Greece, known for their unconventional and often critical views.
