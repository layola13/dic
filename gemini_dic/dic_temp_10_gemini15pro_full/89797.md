hormone:/ˈhɔːrmoʊn/ (US), /ˈhɔːməʊn/ (UK)| n. |激素|A regulatory substance produced in an organism and transported in tissue fluids such as blood or sap to stimulate specific cells or tissues into action.|The doctor checked her hormone levels. 医生检查了她的激素水平。|Synonyms: endocrine, secretion|Antonyms: inhibitor|noun
