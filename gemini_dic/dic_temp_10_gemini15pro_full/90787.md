humoured:/ˈhjuːməd/ (British) /ˈhjuːmərd/ (American)| adj.|幽默的，有幽默感的；迁就的，顺从的|Easily amused and tending to laugh; willing to please or comply with the wishes of others. |例句：He's a good-humoured fellow who is always ready with a joke. 他是一个脾气好的人，总是准备好讲笑话。

例句：She humoured her grandfather by pretending to listen to his long stories. 她假装听她祖父的长篇大论来迁就他。|近义词：amiable, cheerful, jovial, obliging, indulgent|反义词：ill-humoured, grumpy, irritable, uncooperative|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the noun "humour" + "-ed" (suffix forming adjectives).  "Humour" itself comes from Old French "humour" and ultimately from Latin "humor" meaning "moisture, fluid".  Originally, "humour" referred to one of the four bodily fluids believed to determine temperament.
