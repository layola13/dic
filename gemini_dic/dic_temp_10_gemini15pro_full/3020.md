aerie: /ˈɛəri/ (US) /ˈɪəri/ (UK) | n. |  高处的巢穴，尤指鹰巢；高处房屋 | A large nest of a bird of prey, especially an eagle, typically built high in a tree or on a cliff. Also, a house or other dwelling built on a high place. | The eagle returned to its aerie high on the mountain crag.  这只鹰回到了它在山崖上的巢穴。

The poet lived in a small aerie overlooking the sea. 这位诗人住在一个可以俯瞰大海的小屋里。 | 近义词：nest, eyrie, perch, roost | 反义词: ground | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *erie, from Old French *airie, from Vulgar Latin *aria, from Latin ager (“field”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eǵros (“field”).  The spelling change from "eyrie" to "aerie" is a relatively modern Americanism.

USAGE->
The word "aerie" is primarily used as a noun to refer to a bird's nest, especially that of an eagle or other bird of prey, located at a high altitude.  It can also be used metaphorically to describe a dwelling or other structure built in a high place.

Examples:

1.  The climbers spotted an aerie perched precariously on the cliff face. = 登山者发现了一个岌岌可危地坐落在悬崖峭壁上的巢穴。

2.  The millionaire built his aerie atop the highest skyscraper in the city. = 百万富翁在城市最高的摩天大楼顶上建造了他的住所。

3.  From their aerie, the eagles had a commanding view of the valley below.  = 从它们的巢穴，老鹰可以俯瞰下面的山谷。

4.  The writer sought solitude in his mountain aerie. = 作家在他的山间小屋里寻求孤独。


PHRASE->
While "aerie" doesn't typically appear in fixed phrases, it can be used in descriptive phrases such as:

1.  eagle's aerie = 鹰巢
2.  mountain aerie = 山间小屋
3.  cliffside aerie = 悬崖边的巢穴
4.  high aerie = 高处的巢穴
5.  solitary aerie = 僻静的住所
