lukewarmths:/ˈluːkwɔːrmθs/
noun
微温，不冷不热，缺乏热情
lack of enthusiasm; the state of being lukewarm.
The lukewarmths of the audience's response was disheartening.  观众反应的冷淡令人沮丧。
近义词:apathy, indifference, coolness
反义词:enthusiasm, fervor, passion
词性:n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From lukewarm + -th (suffix forming nouns of quality or condition).  Lukewarm itself comes from Old English *hlēowcearm, from hlēow (warm) + cearm (grief, sorrow).  The original sense was "moderately warm," later extending to signify lack of zeal or enthusiasm.
