dibses: /ˈdɪbzɪz/
noun
预订；拥有权；权利；要求
A claim or right to something.
例句：I have dibses on the front seat! 我要预订前排座位！

例句：She called dibses on the last slice of pizza. 她对最后一片披萨声明所有权。

近义词：claim, right
反义词：forfeiture, waiver
词性：n.

词源:  "Dibs" likely originated from the children's game of "dibstones" (also known as jacks or knucklebones), where small objects were thrown and caught. Calling "dibs" was a way to claim the next turn or a particular object.  This evolved into the more general meaning of claiming something.
