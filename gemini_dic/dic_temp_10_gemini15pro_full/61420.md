embolden:/ɪmˈboʊldən/| v. |使大胆，使有胆量；鼓励|To make someone feel more confident and willing to take risks. |She was emboldened by the support of her colleagues and decided to apply for the promotion. 她受到了同事们的鼓励，决定申请晋升。| The positive feedback emboldened him to pursue his artistic dreams.  积极的反馈使他更有勇气去追求他的艺术梦想。| 近义词：encourage, inspire, hearten | 反义词：discourage, intimidate, daunt |词性：动词

词源:
From em- + bold + -en.  "Em-" acts as an intensifier, "bold" refers to courage and confidence, and "-en" is a suffix that transforms an adjective into a verb meaning "to make or become."
