corack:/ˈkɔːræk/| n.|渡船;平底船|A flat-bottomed boat used for transport, especially in shallow waters. |例句：The coracle bobbed gently on the still water.  小圆舟在静水中轻轻地漂浮着。

例句：Fishermen used coracles to navigate the shallow river. 渔民们使用平底船在浅水河中航行。|近义词：boat, skiff, punt|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps 'ship' for contrast in size)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Welsh *cwrwgl, diminutive of cwrwg (“boat, coracle”), from Proto-Celtic *kʷorwos (“round”), from Proto-Indo-European *kʷel- (“to turn, bend”). Cognate with Irish and Scottish Gaelic curach, Manx currach, Breton kouruk.
