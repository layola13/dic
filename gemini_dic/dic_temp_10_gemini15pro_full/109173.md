legitimised: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmaɪzd/ | v. | 使合法化，使正当化 | To make something legal or acceptable; to justify something. | The government legitimised the use of force. 政府将使用武力合法化。|  The regime tried to legitimize its seizure of power. 该政权试图使其夺取政权的行为合法化。 | validate, justify, authorize, legalize |  criminalize, outlaw, prohibit | verb

legitimate: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət/ | adj. | 合法的，正当的 | Conforming to the law or rules; justifiable; reasonable. |  Is his business legitimate? 他的生意合法吗？| It's not legitimate to avoid paying taxes.  逃避纳税是不合法的。| lawful, legal, valid, justifiable | illegal, unlawful, invalid, unreasonable | adjective

legitimacy: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi/ | n. | 合法性，正当性 | The state or quality of being legitimate. | The legitimacy of the election was questioned. 选举的合法性受到了质疑。 | The government's legitimacy is derived from the consent of the governed. 政府的合法性来自于被统治者的同意。| lawfulness, legality, validity | illegality, unlawfulness, invalidity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French legitimer, from Latin legitimare, from legitimus ‘lawful’, from lex, leg- ‘law’.
