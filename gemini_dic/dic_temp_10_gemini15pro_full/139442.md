obsolesce:/ˌɒbsəˈlɛs/ | v. | 变得过时，废弃 | To become obsolete; to go out of use. | Example 1: Typewriters have largely obsolesced in the digital age. 打字机在数字时代已经很大程度上过时了。

Example 2: Many traditional skills are obsolescing due to technological advancements. 许多传统技能由于科技进步而逐渐过时。 | 近义词: become outdated, become outmoded, lapse, expire | 反义词: modernize, update, revive | 词性: 动词


obsolescence:/ˌɒbsəˈlɛsəns/ | n. | 过时，废弃 | The process of becoming obsolete or the state of being obsolete. | Example 1: Planned obsolescence is a strategy used by some manufacturers. 计划性报废是一些制造商使用的策略。

Example 2: The rapid obsolescence of technology is a challenge for many businesses.  技术的快速过时对许多企业来说是一个挑战。 | 近义词: outmodedness, antiquity, disuse | 反义词: currency, modernity, relevance | 词性: 名词


obsolete:/ˈɒbsəliːt/ | adj. | 过时的，废弃的 | No longer produced or used; out of date. | Example 1:  Floppy disks are now obsolete. 软盘现在已经过时了。

Example 2: The company's equipment is obsolete and needs to be replaced. 该公司的设备已经过时，需要更换。 | 近义词: outdated, outmoded, archaic, antiquated | 反义词: modern, current, contemporary, up-to-date | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Latin obsolescere "to fall into disuse," from ob- "away" + solescere "to become accustomed," related to solere "to be accustomed."
