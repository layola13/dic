abil:/ˈeɪbəl/| adj.| 有能力的，能干的 | having the power, skill, means, or opportunity to do something | She is an able lawyer. 她是一位能干的律师。

He is able to speak three languages. 他能说三种语言。| 近义词: capable, competent, skilled, talented | 反义词: unable, incapable, incompetent, inept | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Old French *hable (modern French habile "able, skillful"), from Latin habilis "easily held, manageable, apt, skillful," from habēre "to have, hold."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is able to play the piano. = 他会弹钢琴。
She is able to solve complex problems. = 她能够解决复杂的问题。

一般过去时:
I was able to finish the project on time. = 我按时完成了项目。
They were able to find a solution. = 他们找到了解决方案。

现在完成时:
We have been able to achieve our goals. = 我们已经实现了我们的目标。
She has been able to overcome many challenges. = 她已经克服了许多挑战。

过去完成时:
He had been able to learn English before he moved to the US. = 在他搬到美国之前，他已经学会了英语。
They had been able to gather enough evidence before the trial. = 在审判之前，他们已经收集了足够的证据。

将来时:
I will be able to help you next week. = 我下周可以帮助你。
She will be able to drive after she gets her license. = 她拿到驾照后就能开车了。


疑问句:
Are you able to come to the party? = 你能来参加派对吗？
Is he able to swim? = 他会游泳吗？

否定句:
I am not able to attend the meeting. = 我无法参加会议。
She was not able to complete the task. = 她没能完成任务。


PHRASE->
be able to = 能够
able-bodied = 体格健全的
not able to = 不能
more than able = 完全能够，绰绰有余
less able = 能力较差的
