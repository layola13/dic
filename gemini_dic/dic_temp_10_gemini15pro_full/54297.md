disgustingly:/dɪsˈɡʌstɪŋli/
adverb|令人作呕地，极其讨厌地|in a way that causes disgust; extremely unpleasantly|The bathroom was disgustingly dirty. 浴室脏得令人作呕。|近义词：revoltingly, sickeningly, nauseatingly, appallingly|反义词：pleasingly, delightfully, attractively
词性: 副词

disgust:/dɪsˈɡʌst/
noun|厌恶，憎恶|a strong feeling of disapproval and dislike|She felt a deep disgust at his behavior. 她对他的行为感到深恶痛绝。|近义词：revulsion, aversion, repugnance, loathing|反义词：admiration, liking, approval
词性: 名词

disgust:/dɪsˈɡʌst/
verb|使厌恶，使作呕|cause someone to feel revulsion or strong disapproval|His rudeness disgusted everyone. 他的粗鲁让每个人都感到厌恶。|近义词：sicken, revolt, repel, nauseate|反义词：please, delight, attract
词性: 动词

词源：From Middle French *desgouster* (modern French *dégouter*), from *des-* (expressing reversal) + *gouster* "to taste."  Ultimately from Latin *gustare* "to taste."
