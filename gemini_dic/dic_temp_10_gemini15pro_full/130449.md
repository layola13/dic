nonabsolutists:/ˌnɒnˈæbsəluːtɪsts/
noun
非绝对主义者
Those who do not adhere to absolutist principles, believing that morality or truth is not fixed and unchanging.
Nonabsolutists believe that context and circumstances should be considered when making moral judgments.  非绝对主义者认为，在做出道德判断时应该考虑背景和环境。
Many modern philosophers are nonabsolutists, rejecting the idea of universal moral truths. 许多现代哲学家是非绝对主义者，他们反对普遍道德真理的观点。
synonyms: relativists, pragmatists
antonyms: absolutists, dogmatists
