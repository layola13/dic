echinococcus:/ɪˌkaɪnəˈkɒkəs/| n. |棘球绦虫；棘球蚴|A small tapeworm of the genus Echinococcus, whose larval stage forms hydatid cysts in the liver or lungs of various mammals, including humans.|The dog acted as a definitive host for the echinococcus parasite.|近义词：tapeworm, cestode|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


echinococcus:/ɪˌkaɪnəˈkɒkəs/| n. |棘球蚴；棘球蚴病|Infection with the larval stage of the tapeworm Echinococcus, typically forming large cysts in the liver or lungs.|Echinococcus is a zoonotic disease that can be transmitted from animals to humans.|近义词：hydatid disease, hydatidosis|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From New Latin, from Greek *echinos* (hedgehog, sea urchin) + *kokkos* (grain, berry).  Referring to the appearance of the scolex (head) of the worm.
