backscratching:/ˈbækˌskræʧɪŋ/
n.|互相吹捧；相互奉承；挠背|the act of scratching someone's back, often used figuratively to describe the exchange of favors or compliments.|Politicians are often accused of backscratching to get what they want. 政客们经常被指责互相吹捧以达到目的。|近义词：logrolling, mutual admiration, favor-trading|反义词：criticism, condemnation|词性:名词

backscratcher:/ˈbækˌskræʧər/
n.|用来挠背的工具；互相吹捧的人|a tool used to scratch one's back; a person who engages in mutual flattery or favor-trading.|He used a backscratcher to reach that itchy spot. 他用挠背工具挠到了那个痒的地方。 |近义词: (for tool) scratching post, (for person) flatterer, yes-man|反义词: (for person) critic|词性:名词
