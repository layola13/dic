co-option:/ˌkoʊˈɑːpʃən/
n.|共同选择，纳入，吸收|The process of adding members to a group, especially a governing body, by the votes of members already in the group, rather than by election by those outside the group.  The appropriation or adoption of something for one's own use.|例句：The co-option of radical ideas by the mainstream.  主流对激进思想的吸收。|例句：The club rules allow for co-option of new members.  俱乐部规则允许吸纳新成员。|近义词：appropriation, adoption, absorption, incorporation|反义词：exclusion, rejection|词性：名词

co-opt:/ˌkoʊˈɑːpt/
vt.|拉拢，吸收，指派|To appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members. To appropriate or adopt something for one's own use.|例句：The rebels were co-opted into the government.  叛军被拉拢加入了政府。|例句：They co-opted several ideas from other companies. 他们借鉴了其他公司的一些想法。|近义词：appropriate, adopt, absorb, incorporate|反义词：exclude, reject|词性：动词
