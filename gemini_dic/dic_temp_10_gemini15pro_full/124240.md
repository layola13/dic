mossier:/ˈmɔːsiər/ (US), /ˈmɒsiə/ (UK)| n.| 苔藓, 苔藓植物 |A small, flowerless, green plant that lacks true roots, growing in low carpets or rounded cushions in damp habitats and reproducing by means of spores released from stalked capsules.|The rocks were covered in mossier.  岩石上覆盖着苔藓。|Synonyms: moss, lichen (sometimes)|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a plant, could consider "bare" for the surface it grows on)|noun
