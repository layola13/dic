addictions:/əˈdɪkʃənz/| n.|成瘾，癖好|The state of being enslaved to a habit or practice or to something that is psychologically or physically habit-forming, as narcotics, to such an extent that it impairs or destroys one's life.|He struggled with addictions to alcohol and gambling. 他与酗酒和赌博的瘾好作斗争。|近义词：dependencies, habits, compulsions|反义词:aversions, dislikes|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *addictiō*  "a giving over, awarding," from *addictus*, past participle of *addīcere* "to award, assign," from *ad-* "to" + *dicere* "to say, pronounce." Originally referring to a person legally awarded to another as a slave or debtor. The modern sense of psychological or physiological dependence developed later.

USAGE->
Plural Form:
His addictions were ruining his life. = 他的各种瘾好正在毁掉他的生活。
Many people suffer from various addictions. = 许多人饱受各种成瘾的折磨。

Possessive Form:
The addict's addictions controlled his every move. =  瘾君子的成瘾控制了他的一举一动。
Her family worried about her son's addictions. = 她的家人担心她儿子的成瘾问题。

Prepositional Phrases:
He finally overcame his addiction to drugs. = 他最终克服了毒瘾。
She spoke openly about her addiction to shopping. = 她公开谈论她对购物的成瘾。

As an Object:
He admitted his addictions to his family. = 他向家人承认了自己的成瘾问题。
The therapist treated his addictions with a combination of therapy and medication. = 治疗师用治疗和药物相结合的方法治疗他的成瘾问题。

With Adjectives:
He had multiple, serious addictions. = 他有多种严重的成瘾问题。
She developed a crippling addiction to painkillers after her accident. = 事故发生后，她对止痛药产生了严重的依赖。
