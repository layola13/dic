demotic: /dɪˈmɒtɪk/ (UK) /dɪˈmɑːtɪk/ (US) | adj. n.| 通俗的；民众的；通俗体；世俗体 |  Relating to or denoting the form of ancient Egyptian script that followed hieratic, derived from it and ultimately from hieroglyphic; of or relating to the common people; popular or colloquial. |  The demotic script was used for everyday writing in ancient Egypt.  古埃及的世俗体文字用于日常书写。 The play used demotic language to appeal to a wider audience. 这部戏剧使用通俗的语言来吸引更广泛的观众。| 近义词: popular, vernacular, colloquial, common, everyday, informal | 反义词:  formal, learned, literary, refined, aristocratic | 词性: adjective, noun


Etymology:
From Ancient Greek δημοτικός (dēmotikós, “of the people”), from δῆμος (dêmos, “people”).
