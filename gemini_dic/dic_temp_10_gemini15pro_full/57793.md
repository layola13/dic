dredge:/drɛdʒ/| v. n.|挖掘，疏浚；捞取|To remove mud, silt, or other material from the bottom of a body of water.  Also, to search thoroughly, often laboriously.|The workers dredged the harbor to make it deeper. 工人们疏浚了海港以使其更深。|The police dredged the river for the missing weapon. 警方在河里打捞失踪的武器。|synonyms: excavate, scoop, unearth|antonyms: fill, bury|verb

dredged:/drɛdʒd/| adj.|疏浚过的，挖掘过的|Having been dredged; covered or coated with something (usually something unpleasant).|The dredged river bottom was now deeper. 疏浚过的河底现在更深了。|His clothes were dredged in flour after he baked the cake. 他烤蛋糕后衣服上沾满了面粉。|synonyms: excavated, scooped|antonyms: filled, buried|adjective
