obtundent:/ɑbˈtʌndənt/ | adj. | 减弱的，迟钝的; 使减弱的，使迟钝的 | Reducing or lessening the intensity of something, especially sensation or feeling; blunting.  | The drug had an obtundent effect on her pain.  这种药物对她的疼痛有减轻作用。 | Synonyms:  soothing, alleviating, mitigating, dulling, numbing | Antonyms:  aggravating, exacerbating, intensifying, stimulating | adjective

obtund:/ɑbˈtʌnd/ | vt. | 使减弱，使迟钝 | To reduce or lessen the intensity of something, especially sensation or feeling; to blunt. | The medication obtunded the sharp edges of the pain.  药物减轻了剧烈的疼痛。 | Synonyms: dull, blunt, deaden, numb | Antonyms: sharpen, intensify, stimulate | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *obtundere*, from *ob-* (against, towards) + *tundere* (to beat, to strike).  The notion is of beating something until it becomes dull or less sensitive.
