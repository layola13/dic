lean-in:/ˌliːn ˈɪn/ | n. | 积极主动，向前一步 |  The act of actively engaging or participating in something, often to assert oneself or take advantage of an opportunity.  It's often used in the context of career advancement, particularly for women.  Derived from the book "Lean In" by Sheryl Sandberg. |  She decided to lean in and ask for a promotion. 她决定积极主动，争取升职。 |  assertiveness, engagement, participation | passivity, withdrawal | noun

lean-in:/ˌliːn ˈɪn/ | v. | 积极主动，向前一步 | To actively engage or participate in something, often to assert oneself or take advantage of an opportunity.  It often implies a physical posture of leaning forward, symbolizing engagement and interest. |  He leaned in to hear the speaker better. 他向前倾斜身子以便更好地听到演讲者。|  engage, participate, assert oneself |  disengage, withdraw, hold back | verb
