generalized:/ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 普遍的，广义的，概括的 |  Made more general or applicable to a wider range of situations; not specific.  |  The study's findings were too generalized to be of much use.  这项研究的发现过于笼统，用处不大。 |  Generalized anxiety disorder is a condition characterized by persistent and excessive worry. 广泛性焦虑症是一种以持续和过度担忧为特征的疾病。 | general, broad, universal, widespread | specific, particular, localized, limited | adjective

generalise (British English):/ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz/ | v. | 概括，归纳，推广 | To form a general conclusion from particular instances; to apply a general principle to a particular case. |  It's dangerous to generalize from a single example. 从单个例子进行概括是危险的。 |  He tried to generalize the results of his experiments. 他试图概括他的实验结果。 |  infer, deduce, extrapolate, conclude | specialize, specify, restrict, limit | verb
