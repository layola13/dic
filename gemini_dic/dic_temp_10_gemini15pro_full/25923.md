burnish:/ˈbɜːrnɪʃ/ (US) /ˈbɜːnɪʃ/ (UK)| v. n.|擦亮，磨光; 润色; 改进|To polish (something, especially metal) by rubbing. To enhance or improve something.|例句：He burnished the silver until it gleamed. 他把银器擦得闪闪发亮。|近义词：polish, shine, buff, refine, improve|反义词：dull, tarnish, dim|词性:动词, 名词

burnish:/ˈbɜːrnɪʃ/ (US) /ˈbɜːnɪʃ/ (UK)| n.|光泽，光亮|The shine on a surface that has been burnished.|例句：The burnish on the antique furniture was impressive.  古董家具上的光泽令人印象深刻。|近义词：gloss, sheen, luster|反义词：dullness|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French burnir, from Frankish *brunnon, from Proto-Germanic *brunjaną (“to make brown, to polish”). Cognate with Middle High German brünnen (“to make brown, to polish”), Old English brūnian (“to make brown”).
