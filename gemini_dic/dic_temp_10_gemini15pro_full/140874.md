on-the-spur-of-the-moment:/ɒn ðə spɜːr əv ðə ˈməʊmənt/ /ɑːn ðə spɝːr əv ðə ˈmoʊmənt/
adv.
一时冲动地；心血来潮地
Done or decided suddenly and without planning.
例句：We decided to go to the beach on the spur of the moment. 我们一时兴起决定去海滩。

例句：He bought the car on the spur of the moment, and now he regrets it. 他一时冲动买了这辆车，现在后悔了。

近义词：impulsively, spontaneously, extemporaneously
反义词：deliberately, planned, premeditated
词性：副词


ETYMOLOGY->
"spur" originally referred to a spiked device used to urge a horse forward.  The phrase "on the spur of the moment" evokes the image of acting quickly and decisively, as if spurred into action. The phrase first appeared in the late 16th century.
