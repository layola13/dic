clasper:/ˈklɑːspər/ (US), /ˈklɑːspə/ (UK)| n. | 扣子，钩子；（雄性软骨鱼的）鳍脚|A clasper is a modified pelvic fin found on male sharks, rays, and chimaeras. It is used to transfer sperm to the female during mating.  It can also refer to a device or part that clasps or fastens something.| The male shark uses its claspers to hold onto the female during mating. 雄鲨鱼在交配时用鳍脚抓住雌鲨鱼。|Synonyms: fastener, holdfast, grip, clamp|Antonyms: release, unfasten|Noun
