aramaic:/ˌærəˈmeɪɪk/
n.|亚拉姆语|Aramaic is a Northwest Semitic language that was the lingua franca of much of the Near East from the 8th century BCE until the rise of Islam in the 7th century CE.  It is still spoken by small communities, mostly in Syria. |The Aramaic inscription on the stele is difficult to decipher.|近义词:Syriac (related language)|反义词: (None - languages don't have direct antonyms)|noun
