huddles:/ˈhʌdəlz/ (US), /ˈhʌdlz/ (UK)| n. v.| 聚集；拥挤；杂乱的一堆；秘密会议|A huddle is a closely packed group of people or things. As a verb, it means to crowd together.|The penguins huddled together for warmth.  企鹅挤在一起取暖。|
The team huddled together before the game. 球队在比赛前挤作一团。|
synonyms: crowd, gather, cluster, throng|
antonyms: disperse, scatter|
noun, verb


huddles:/ˈhʌdəlz/ (US), /ˈhʌdlz/ (UK)| v.|  蜷缩；拥抱|To huddle means to curl up or crouch close to something for warmth or protection.|She huddled under the blankets on the cold night.  在寒冷的夜晚，她蜷缩在毯子下。|He huddled against the wall, trying to stay out of the wind. 他紧靠着墙，试图避开风。|synonyms: crouch, snuggle, curl up|antonyms: stretch out, sprawl|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Middle English word *huderen*, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to *hide* or *hood*.
