hemothorax:/ˌhiːmoʊˈθɔːræks/| n. |血胸|The presence of blood in the pleural cavity, the space between the lungs and the chest wall.  It's typically caused by trauma, such as a rib fracture or a penetrating chest injury, but can also result from medical conditions like cancer or bleeding disorders.|The patient presented with a hemothorax following a car accident.  病人车祸后出现血胸。|Synonyms:  haemothorax (British spelling)|Antonyms: None (absence of blood would simply be a normal pleural cavity)|noun
