accusations:/ˌækjʊˈzeɪʃənz/| n.|指控，控告|a statement saying that someone has done something morally wrong, illegal, or unkind|The accusations of corruption led to his resignation.  腐败指控导致他辞职。

He denied the accusations against him. 他否认了对他的指控。|近义词：allegations, charges, claims|反义词：defenses, denials|词性:名词(plural)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *accusacioun*, from Old French *acusation*, from Latin *accusātiō*, from *accusātus*, past participle of *accusāre* ("to accuse"), from *ad* ("to, towards") + *causa* ("cause, reason").  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱews-eh₂- ("to seek, examine, test").

USAGE->
Making accusations:
The company faces accusations of unfair labor practices. =  该公司面临不公平劳动行为的指控。
He made serious accusations against his former employer. = 他对他的前任雇主提出了严重的指控。

Responding to accusations:
She denied all the accusations made against her. = 她否认了所有对她的指控。
He vehemently refuted the accusations of plagiarism. = 他强烈反驳了剽窃的指控。

Investigating accusations:
The police are investigating the accusations of fraud. = 警方正在调查欺诈指控。
An independent inquiry will examine the accusations of misconduct. =  一个独立的调查将审查不当行为的指控。

Withdrawing accusations:
He later withdrew his accusations, admitting they were false. = 他后来撤回了他的指控，承认它们是假的。
She publicly apologized for making the unfounded accusations. = 她公开为提出毫无根据的指控道歉。


Dealing with accusations:
He's struggling to cope with the accusations leveled against him. = 他正在努力应对针对他的指控。
She hired a lawyer to help her defend herself against the accusations. = 她聘请了一名律师来帮助她为自己辩护，对抗这些指控。


PHRASE->
false accusations = 虚假指控
serious accusations = 严重指控
unfounded accusations = 毫无根据的指控
face accusations = 面临指控
deny accusations = 否认指控
