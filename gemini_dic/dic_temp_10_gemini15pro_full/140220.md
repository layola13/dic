officiary: /əˈfɪʃəri/ | n. | 官员；全体官员 | A group of officials, especially sports officials.  A person who holds an office, especially in a religious organization. | The officiary of the church met to discuss the new building project. 教会的官员们开会讨论新的建筑项目。|  officials, office-bearers, administrators | laymen, rank and file | noun

Etymology:  From Late Latin *officiarius*, from Latin *officium* "duty, service."
