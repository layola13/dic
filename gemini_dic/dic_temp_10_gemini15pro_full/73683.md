forgetters:/fərˈɡetərz/ | n. |健忘的人; 忘性大的人 |People who tend to forget things. |  The forgetters left their umbrellas on the bus. 健忘的人把他们的伞落在公交车上了。| Those forgetters always misplace their keys. 那些忘性大的人总是把钥匙放错地方。 | 近义词:absent-minded people,  scatterbrained individuals | 反义词: people with good memories | 词性:名词

forget:/fərˈɡet/ | v. |忘记|To cease having in mind; be unable to recall. | I forgot my keys at home. 我把钥匙忘在家里了。| Don't forget to call your mother. 别忘了给你妈妈打电话。| 近义词: misplace, overlook | 反义词: remember, recall | 词性:动词

-er:/ər/ | suffix | ...的人; ...者 | A suffix that forms a noun indicating a person or thing that performs a certain action.  | It is used to form nouns denoting people who do a particular thing. 用于构成表示做某事的人的名词。| This example demonstrates the usage of the suffix "-er." 这个例子演示了后缀“-er”的用法。|近义词: or, ist|反义词: 无|词性:后缀


词源:

From Old English *forgietan, from for- "away, without" + gietan "to get."  Meaning shifted from "lose" to "fail to remember."  The suffix "-er" comes from Old English "-ere," denoting an agent or doer of an action.
