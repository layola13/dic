auto-intoxication: /ˌɔːtəʊɪnˌtɒksɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 自体中毒；肠中毒 |  A historical medical concept where undigested food in the intestines produces toxins that are absorbed into the bloodstream, causing various illnesses.  This concept is largely discredited by modern medicine. |  In the early 20th century, auto-intoxication was blamed for a wide range of ailments.  20世纪初，自体中毒被认为是众多疾病的罪魁祸首。| Synonyms: toxemia, intestinal toxemia | Antonyms: detoxification | noun
