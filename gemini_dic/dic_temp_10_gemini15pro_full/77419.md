gavel:/ˈɡævəl/| n.|小木槌，议事槌|A small hammer used by a judge or other presiding officer in a court, auction, or meeting, especially to signal for attention or order or to mark the conclusion of a transaction.|The judge banged his gavel to silence the courtroom. 法官敲响了他的小木槌，让法庭安静下来。|Synonyms: mallet, hammer|Antonyms: None|noun

gavel:/ˈɡævəl/| v.|敲响议事槌|To strike with or as if with a gavel.|The chairman gaveled the meeting to order. 主席敲响议事槌宣布会议开始。|Synonyms: hammer, strike|Antonyms: None|verb
