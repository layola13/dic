mollify: /ˈmɒlɪfaɪ/ (US) /ˈmɒlɪfaɪ/ (UK)| v. | 平息，抚慰，缓和 | To reduce the anger or anxiety of someone; to appease. |  The manager tried to mollify the angry customer by offering a refund. 经理试图通过提供退款来安抚愤怒的顾客。

The soft music mollified the crying baby.  轻柔的音乐使哭闹的婴儿平静下来。

近义词: appease, soothe, placate, pacify | 反义词: aggravate, provoke, incite, exacerbate | 词性: verb

mollified: /ˈmɒlɪfaɪd/ (US) /ˈmɒlɪfaɪd/ (UK)| adj. | 平息的，抚慰的 | Having been appeased; calmed. | The mollified customer finally accepted the apology.  被安抚的顾客最终接受了道歉。

He felt mollified after a long, hot bath.  洗了个长时间的热水澡后，他感到平静了。


近义词: appeased, soothed, placated, pacified | 反义词: aggravated, provoked, incited, exacerbated | 词性: adjective


词源: From Late Latin mollificāre, from Latin mollis (“soft”) + -ficāre, combining form of facere (“to make, do”).
