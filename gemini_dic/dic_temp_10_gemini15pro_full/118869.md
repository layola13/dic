metascientifically:/ˌmɛtəsaɪənˈtɪfɪkəli/
adverb
元科学地
in a way that relates to metascience (the study of the methods, assumptions, and conclusions of science).  Metascience examines the practices and principles of science itself, rather than the specific content of scientific discoveries.
The philosopher examined the issue metascientifically, questioning the underlying assumptions of the experiment. 哲学家从元科学的角度审视了这个问题，质疑了实验的基本假设。
She metascientifically analyzed the reproducibility crisis in psychology. 她从元科学的角度分析了心理学中的可重复性危机。

近义词: philosophically of science, epistemologically of science
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps) scientifically, empirically
