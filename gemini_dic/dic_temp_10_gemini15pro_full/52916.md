dimple:/ˈdɪmpl/ | n. | 酒窝；小凹陷 | A small natural indentation in the flesh, especially in the cheek or chin. |  She has a dimple in her left cheek. 她左脸颊上有一个酒窝。 |  indentation, hollow |  bulge, bump | noun

dimpled:/ˈdɪmpld/ | adj. | 有酒窝的；有凹陷的 | Having dimples. |  Her dimpled smile charmed everyone. 她酒窝浅笑迷住了所有人。| indented, hollowed | smooth, flat | adjective

dimple:/ˈdɪmpl/ | v. |  形成酒窝；使起涟漪 | To form dimples or ripples. | The wind dimpled the surface of the water. 风使水面起了涟漪。| indent, hollow | flatten, smooth | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dimplen, frequentative of *dimpen (“to indent”), from Old English dympelle. Related to Dutch dempel (“puddle”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *dumpalaz (“pit, hollow”).
