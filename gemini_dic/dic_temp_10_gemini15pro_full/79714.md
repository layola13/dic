glucosinolate:/ˌɡluːkoʊˈsɪnəleɪt/| n. |芥子油苷|A class of organic compounds derived from glucose and an amino acid that occur in certain plants, especially cruciferous vegetables.  They are responsible for the pungent flavors and aromas of these plants and have been studied for their potential health benefits and anticarcinogenic properties.|Broccoli and other cruciferous vegetables are rich in glucosinolates.|Near Synonyms:  glucoside, thioglucoside|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a specific chemical compound)|Noun
