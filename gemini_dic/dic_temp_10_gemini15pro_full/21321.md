bloodwite:/ˈblʌdwaɪt/
noun
血价，赎命金
A fine paid by a killer to the family of the victim, especially in Anglo-Saxon law.
The bloodwite was a substantial sum, often determined by the victim's social standing.  血价是一笔可观的金额，通常由受害者的社会地位决定。
The king imposed a heavy bloodwite on the murderer. 国王对凶手处以重金血价。
Synonym:wergild, blood money
Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but concepts like "impunity" or "lack of accountability" represent the opposite of paying for a life taken.)
