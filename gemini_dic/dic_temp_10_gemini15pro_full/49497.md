demagogue: /ˈdɛməɡɑɡ/ (US) /ˈdɛməɡɒɡ/ (UK) | n. | 煽动者，蛊惑民心的政客 | a political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument. |  The demagogue rallied the crowd with his fiery rhetoric.  煽动者用他激烈的言辞煽动人群。| synonym: agitator, rabble-rouser, firebrand | antonym: statesman, conciliator | noun

demagogic: /ˌdɛməˈɡɒdʒɪk/ (US) /ˌdɛməˈɡɒɡɪk/ (UK)| adj. | 煽动性的，蛊惑人心的 |  relating to or characteristic of a demagogue. | His demagogic speeches incited violence. 他的煽动性言论煽动了暴力。| synonym: inflammatory, rabble-rousing | antonym: conciliatory, statesmanlike | adjective

demagoguery: /ˈdɛməˌɡɑɡəri/ (US) /ˈdɛməˌɡɒɡəri/ (UK) | n. | 煽动，蛊惑人心 | the practice of appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument. |  The politician's demagoguery gained him many supporters.  这位政客的煽动性言论为他赢得了许多支持者。| synonym: rabble-rousing, agitation | antonym: diplomacy, statesmanship | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek dēmagōgos (δημαγωγός, “leader of the people”), from dēmos (δῆμος, “people”) + agōgos (ἀγωγός, “leading, leader”).
