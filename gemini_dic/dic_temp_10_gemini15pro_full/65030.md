etacism:/ˈiːtəsɪzəm/ | n. |语音同一化；伊塔化| The excessive use of the vowel sound /iː/ or /ɪ/, or the letter eta (η) in a language, especially in Greek.  It can also refer to the change of other vowel sounds to /iː/ or /ɪ/. |  Modern Greek pronunciation exhibits etacism, where several formerly distinct vowels are now all pronounced as /i/. 现代希腊语发音表现出语音同一化，其中几个以前不同的元音现在都发音为 /i/。 |  The etacism in this dialect makes it difficult to understand for speakers of other Greek dialects.  这种方言中的语音同一化使得其他希腊方言的使用者难以理解。 | syn: vowel shift, sound change | ant: N/A | noun
