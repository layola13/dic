mutisms: /ˈmjuːtɪzəmz/ | n. | 缄默症，沉默症 | A condition in which a person is unable or unwilling to speak, often due to psychological reasons. |  She suffered from selective mutism as a child, only speaking to her immediate family. 她小时候患有选择性缄默症，只和她直系亲属说话。

The mutisms observed in the patients were attributed to trauma. 在病人身上观察到的沉默症被认为是创伤造成的。| 近义词: silence, speechlessness | 反义词: talkativeness, loquacity | 词性: 名词

词源: From Latin *mūtus (“mute, silent”) + -ism.
