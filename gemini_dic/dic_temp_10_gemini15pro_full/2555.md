admissibly:/ədˈmɪsɪbli/| adv.|可容许地，可接受地|In a way that is considered acceptable or allowable.  It modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb, indicating the manner in which something is done or the degree to which something is true.  Derived from "admissible," which comes from the Latin "admittere" (to admit).|The evidence was admissibly obtained.  证据是合法取得的。

The judge ruled that the testimony was admissibly presented. 法官裁定证词的提交是可接受的。|近义词：acceptably, legitimately, properly, validly|反义词：inadmissibly, unacceptably, improperly, invalidly|词性:副词


USAGE->
在句子中修饰动词，形容词或其他副词，表示行为方式或程度。

例句1:  He admissibly argued his case. 他以可接受的方式陈述了他的案件。
例句2:  The data was admissibly collected according to the research protocol.  根据研究方案，数据收集是可接受的。


注意事项:
admissibly 常用于正式场合，尤其在法律或学术环境中。
