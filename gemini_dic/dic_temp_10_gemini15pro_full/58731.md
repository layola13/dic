dunderheads:/ˈdʌndərˌhɛdz/ | n. |笨蛋；傻瓜|A derogatory term for stupid or foolish people.  A group of people considered to be stupid or foolish. |Those dunderheads lost the map and got us all lost in the woods.| 近义词:idiots, fools, morons, nitwits, blockheads | 反义词:geniuses, intellectuals, scholars | 词性:名词

词源:
Originates from the early 19th century, combining "dunder" (meaning "thunder," often used in euphemisms for "damn" or "hell") with "head."  So, originally, it might have implied someone with a "head full of thunder" or noisy, empty thoughts.
