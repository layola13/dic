anaks: /ˈænæks/
n.
children (archaic or poetic)
An archaic or poetic term for children.
例句：The anaks played merrily in the meadow. 孩子们在草地上快乐地玩耍。
例句：Hear the laughter of the anaks echoing through the halls. 听到孩子们在大厅里回荡的笑声。
近义词：children, offspring, kids, youngsters
反义词：adults, elders
词性：名词

词源:
From Middle English *anak(e)s, plural of *anak, from Old English *anace (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *anakon, diminutive of *an-, *anak “child, son”. Cognate with Old Norse annarr “other, second”.  The singular form has been lost in English.  The plural form survives only in archaic or poetic use.
