helenus:/ˈhɛlɪnəs/| n. |海伦努斯|A son of Priam and Hecuba, a Trojan seer who after the Trojan War was captured by Pyrrhus, king of Epirus, and married Andromache, Hector's widow.  He founded a kingdom in Epirus.|Helenus prophesied that Aeneas would find a new Troy in Italy.|Synonyms: prophet, seer, soothsayer|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a person with prophetic abilities)|Noun
