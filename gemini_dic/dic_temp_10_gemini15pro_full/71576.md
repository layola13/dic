flashingly:/ˈflæʃɪŋli/
adverb
闪耀地，炫耀地
In a way that is bright and attracts attention.
She smiled flashingly at the cameras. 她对着镜头灿烂一笑。
Synonyms: brightly, dazzlingly, glitteringly
Antonyms: dimly, dully
Part of Speech: adverb

flashingly:/ˈflæʃɪŋli/
adverb
炫耀地，招摇地
In a way that is intended to impress or attract attention, often in a showy or ostentatious manner.
He dressed flashingly in a bright red suit. 他穿着一套鲜红色的西装，十分招摇。
Synonyms: ostentatiously, showily, gaudily
Antonyms: modestly, subtly, plainly
Part of Speech: adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "flashing" + "-ly."  "Flashing" comes from the verb "flash," which has roots in Old Norse "flasa" (to blaze, rush) and is related to Old English "flǣsc" (flesh). The suffix "-ly" transforms adjectives into adverbs.
