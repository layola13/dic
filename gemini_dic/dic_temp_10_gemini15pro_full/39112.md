confrontation:/ˌkɑːnfrənˈteɪʃn/ | n. | 对抗，冲突，对峙 | An open or direct clash between two opposing groups or individuals. |  The two armies stood in silent confrontation. 两支军队默默对峙。

The confrontation between the protestors and the police turned violent. 抗议者和警察之间的冲突变得暴力了。 |  clash, conflict, encounter, showdown | avoidance, compromise, cooperation | noun

confront:/kənˈfrʌnt/ | vt. | 面对，遭遇；对抗；使对质 | To face or deal with (something hostile or difficult) boldly or aggressively; to bring face to face. |  He confronted his accusers in court. 他在法庭上与原告对峙。

She was confronted with a difficult decision. 她面临着一个艰难的决定。 | face, encounter, challenge, oppose | avoid, evade, dodge | verb
