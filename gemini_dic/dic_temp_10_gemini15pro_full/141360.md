operation:/ˌɒpəˈreɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃn/ (US)| n.| 操作；手术；运算；经营；行动|An operation is a series of actions carried out to achieve a particular result.  A surgical operation involves cutting into someone's body in order to repair or remove a damaged or diseased part.|The military operation was successful. 这次军事行动很成功。|synonyms: procedure, process, action, maneuver, undertaking|antonyms: inaction, cessation, inactivity|noun

operation:/ˈɒpəreɪt/ (UK) /ˈɑːpəreɪt/ (US)| v.| 操作；运转；经营；动手术|To operate something means to control its functioning or cause it to function.  To operate on someone means to perform surgery.|He operates the machinery with skill. 他熟练地操作机器。|synonyms: function, work, manage, run, control|antonyms: malfunction, fail, close, stop|verb
