conflate:/kənˈfleɪt/ /ˈkɑːnfleɪt/| vt.|合并，混合，混淆|To combine two or more things into one, often in a way that blurs the distinctions between them.|例句：The author conflates several different issues in his argument. 作者在他的论证中混淆了几个不同的问题。

例句：The two versions of the story have become conflated over time.  随着时间的推移，这个故事的两个版本已经融合在一起了。|近义词：merge, combine, blend, fuse, meld|反义词：separate, distinguish, differentiate|词性：动词

conflated:/kənˈfleɪtɪd/ /ˈkɑːnfleɪtɪd/| adj.|合并的，混合的|Combined or blended, often in a confusing way.|例句：The report presented a conflated account of the events.  报告提供了一个对事件的混淆描述。

例句：His memories of the two events were conflated. 他对这两件事的记忆混淆了。|近义词：merged, combined, blended|反义词：separate, distinct|词性：形容词

词源: From Latin conflātus, past participle of conflāre (“to blow together, melt together”), from con- (“together”) + flāre (“to blow”).
