groundswell:/ˈɡraʊndˌswɛl/| n. |激增；浪潮；民情汹涌|A groundswell of opinion/support etc is a sudden increase in a particular feeling among a large group of people.  A groundswell is also a large, rolling wave in the sea.|例句：There is a groundswell of opinion against the new law.  公众舆论对新法律的反对声浪高涨。

例句：The surfer rode the groundswell all the way to shore.  冲浪者乘着涌浪一直冲到岸边。|近义词：surge, upsurge, wave, increase, rise|反义词:decline, decrease, fall|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From ground + swell.  "swell" refers to the rising movement, and "ground" refers to its origin from a large group of people or from the depths of the sea.
