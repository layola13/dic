ergativities:/ɜːrɡətɪˈvɪtiz/ | n. | 作格性（复数） | The grammatical property of a language in which the subject of an intransitive verb and the object of a transitive verb are marked in the same way, while the subject of a transitive verb is marked differently.  It refers to the system of marking grammatical relations in which the subject of a transitive verb is treated differently from the subject of an intransitive verb and the object of a transitive verb. | Basque is well-known for its ergativity. 巴斯克语以其作格性而闻名。|  Many Australian Aboriginal languages exhibit ergativity. 许多澳大利亚原住民语言都表现出作格性。 |近义词: (No direct synonym, but related concepts include) nominative-accusative alignment |反义词: (No direct antonym, but contrasting concept is) nominative-accusative alignment |词性: Noun
