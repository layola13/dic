defoliating:/ˌdiːˈfoʊliˌeɪtɪŋ/
adjective
落叶的; 使落叶的; 脱叶的
causing leaves to fall off
例句：The defoliating spray killed the weeds but left the grass unharmed.  脱叶剂杀死了杂草，但没有损害草坪。

例句：Caterpillars are defoliating insects. 毛毛虫是食叶昆虫。
近义词：leaf-shedding, deciduous
反义词：evergreen
词性: adjective


defoliate:/ˌdiːˈfoʊliˌeɪt/
verb
使落叶; 脱叶
cause (a tree or plant) to lose its leaves prematurely, typically as a result of disease or chemicals.
例句：The trees were defoliated by the strong winds.  强风使树木落叶。

例句：Some insects can defoliate entire forests. 一些昆虫可以使整片森林落叶。
近义词：strip, denude
反义词：grow leaves
词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *defoliatus, past participle of defoliare ("to shed leaves, deprive of leaves"), from de- + folium ("leaf").
