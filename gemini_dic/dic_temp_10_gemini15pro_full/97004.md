infarct:/ˈɪnfɑːrkt/ (US), /ˈɪnfɑːkt/ (UK)| n.|梗塞，梗死|An area of tissue death caused by an obstruction of the blood supply, typically by a thrombus or embolus.|The patient suffered a myocardial infarct.  病人患有心肌梗塞。|Synonyms: infarction, blockage, obstruction|Antonyms: perfusion, flow|noun

infarct:/ˈɪnfɑːrkt/ (US), /ˈɪnfɑːkt/ (UK)| v.|使梗塞，使梗死|To cause an infarct in (an organ or tissue).|The blood clot infarcted the lung tissue. 血凝块导致肺组织梗死。|Synonyms: obstruct, block|Antonyms: perfuse, irrigate|verb
