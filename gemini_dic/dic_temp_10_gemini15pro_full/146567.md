overgraduated: /ˌoʊvərˈɡrædʒueɪtɪd/
adjective
过度毕业的；学历过高的
Having more education than is necessary for a particular job or situation.  This often implies the person is overqualified and may be struggling to find appropriate employment.
She was overgraduated for the entry-level position, holding a Master's degree when a Bachelor's was sufficient. 她学历过高，不适合这个入门级职位，她拥有硕士学位，而学士学位就足够了。
He felt overgraduated working as a barista, having earned a PhD in astrophysics. 他觉得做咖啡师大材小用，因为他拥有天体物理学博士学位。
近义词: overqualified
反义词: underqualified
词性: adjective

词源:
The word "overgraduated" is a combination of the prefix "over-" (meaning "excessive" or "too much") and the past participle of the verb "graduate" (meaning "to complete an academic course").
