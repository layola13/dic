consecraters:/ˈkɒn.sɪ.kreɪ.təz/
n.
祝圣者，圣职授予者
Those who perform the rite of consecration, especially bishops.
The consecraters laid their hands upon the new bishop.  祝圣者将他们的手放在新主教的头上。
近义词:ordainers, inaugurators
反义词:None.
词性:Noun (Plural)


consecrate:/ˈkɒn.sɪ.kreɪt/
v.
祝圣，奉献，使神圣
To make or declare sacred; dedicate formally to a religious or divine purpose.
The church was consecrated in 1850. 这座教堂于1850年祝圣。
近义词:hallow, sanctify, dedicate
反义词:desecrate, profane
词性:Verb


consecrated:/ˈkɒn.sɪ.kreɪ.tɪd/
adj.
祝圣的，神圣的
Having been made or declared sacred.
The consecrated ground was treated with reverence.  这片祝圣的土地受到了崇敬的对待。
近义词:sacred, hallowed, sanctified
反义词:profane, secular
词性:Adjective

词源:
From Late Latin *consecratus, past participle of consecrare "to hallow, devote," from com- "together" (see com-) + sacrare "to make sacred" (see sacred).
