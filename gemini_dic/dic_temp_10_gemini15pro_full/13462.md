atriums: /ˈeɪtriəmz/ (US), /ˈeɪtriəmz/ (UK)| n. |中庭，天井|A large open space inside a building, often with a glass roof, typically found in shopping malls, hotels, or office buildings.  It can also refer to each of the two upper chambers of the heart.|The hotel has a beautiful atrium filled with plants.  这家酒店有一个充满植物的美丽中庭。|The blood flows from the atria to the ventricles. 血液从心房流向心室。| synonyms: courtyard, concourse, lobby, hall, forecourt (for buildings); auricle (for heart) | antonyms:  chamber, cubicle (partial antonyms, implying enclosed spaces) | noun
