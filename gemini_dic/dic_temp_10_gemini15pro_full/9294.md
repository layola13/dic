antiphrastical:/ˌæntɪˈfræstɪkəl/
adj.
反语的，讽刺的
Using words in a sense opposite to their proper meaning; ironic.
例句：He used the term 'genius' antiphrastically to describe the clumsy waiter. 他反讽地用“天才”一词来形容那个笨手笨脚的服务员。

例句：Her antiphrastical praise made it clear she disapproved of his actions. 她讽刺的赞扬清楚地表明了她不赞成他的行为。

近义词：ironic, sarcastic, sardonic
反义词：literal, sincere, straightforward
词性：adjective

词源：From Late Latin *antiphrasticus, from Ancient Greek ἀντιφραστικός (antiphrastikós, “expressing the opposite of what is meant”), from ἀντίφρασις (antiphrasis, “use of an expression in the opposite sense”), from ἀντί (antí, “against, opposite”) + φράσις (phrásis, “speech, diction”).
