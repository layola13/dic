eustyle:/ˈjuːstaɪl/| adj.|优美的，柱间距合适的|Having columns spaced two and a quarter times their diameter apart, considered by Vitruvius to be the most beautiful arrangement.|The temple was built in the eustyle manner, its columns perfectly proportioned. 这座寺庙以优美的柱式建造，其柱子的比例非常完美。|近义词:harmonious, well-proportioned, balanced|反义词:disproportionate, inharmonious|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Greek *eus* (good, well) + *stulos* (column).  Comes from Vitruvius' *De architectura*, where he describes different intercolumniations. Eustyle was considered the best, being visually pleasing and allowing for practical movement between the columns.
