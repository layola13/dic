corsac:/ˈkɔːrsæk/ /ˈkɔːrsɑːk/ | n. |柯萨克狐;草原狐|A small, long-legged fox (Vulpes corsac) with large ears and a pointed muzzle, native to the steppes of Central Asia.|The corsac is well adapted to the harsh conditions of the steppe.|近义词: fox, steppe fox|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

Etymology: From Russian корса́к (korsák), from Turkic *korsak, perhaps ultimately from Proto-Mongolic *kürsäg (“weasel”).
