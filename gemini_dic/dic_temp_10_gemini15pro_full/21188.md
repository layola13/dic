blockaders:/blɒˈkeɪdəz/ | n. |封锁者，阻挠者|People or forces that blockade a place, preventing entry or exit.| The blockaders prevented any ships from entering the port. 封锁者阻止任何船只进入港口。|blockading force, besiegers| defenders, allies| noun
blockade:/blɒˈkeɪd/ | n. vt.|封锁，阻塞|The isolating, closing off, or surrounding of a place, such as a port, a harbor, or a city, by enemy troops or ships to prevent entrance or exit or to cut off supplies.| The navy imposed a blockade on the enemy's coast. 海军对敌人的海岸实施了封锁。|siege, obstruction| opening, access| noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *blocus*, from Dutch *blokhuis* "blockhouse," from *blok* "block" + *huis* "house."  Originally referred to a fortified building blocking a passage.  The meaning extended to the act of blocking a passage and then to any obstruction.
