al·lec·ta·tion /ˌælɛkˈteɪʃən/
|n.|引诱；诱惑；吸引|The allectation of power can corrupt even the most virtuous. 权力带来的诱惑即使是最正直的人也会腐化。|例句：The allectation of a large salary persuaded him to take the job.  高薪的诱惑使他接受了这份工作。

例句：The bright lights of the city held a strong allectation for the young man.  城市璀璨的灯光对这个年轻人有着强烈的吸引力。|近义词：attraction, lure, enticement, temptation|反义词：repulsion, deterrence|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *allectatio, from Latin allectāre (“to entice, allure”), frequentative of adlicere (“to attract”), from ad- (“to, towards”) + lacere (“to entice”).
