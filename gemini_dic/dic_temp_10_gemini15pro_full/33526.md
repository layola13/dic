ciclatoun: /ˈsɪkləˌtuːn/ | n. | 短上衣，骑兵穿的短外衣 | A rich cloth of gold, often interwoven with coloured silks, originally from the Middle East, used especially for ecclesiastical vestments and favoured for secular garments in 13th- and 14th-century Europe.  It was also used for banners and other ceremonial purposes.|  The king wore a magnificent ciclatoun embroidered with pearls. 国王穿着一件绣有珍珠的华丽短上衣。 | 近义词: tunic, surcoat | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "undergarment" in contrast to its outer-wear function) | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Old French ciclaton, siglaton, from Medieval Latin ciclatonus, probably from Arabic siklātūn, ultimately from Persian Saqlatun (name of a town).
