antimetathesis:/ˌæntiˌmɛˈtæθɪsɪs/ | n. |逆修辞，交错对照 | A rhetorical device in which words are repeated in transposed order, often with a change of meaning; also called counterchange, commutatio.  It involves repeating words in reverse grammatical order. | Example 1: "Ask not what your country can do for you; ask what you can do for your country." - John F. Kennedy.  肯尼迪: 不要问你的国家能为你做什么，而要问你能为你的国家做什么。

Example 2: "Fair is foul, and foul is fair." - Shakespeare's Macbeth.  莎士比亚的麦克白: 美即是丑，丑即是美。 | Synonym: chiasmus, counterchange, commutatio | Antonym: (No direct antonym exists for this rhetorical device.) | noun
