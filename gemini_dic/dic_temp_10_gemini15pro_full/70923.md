firebombing:/ˈfaɪərbɒmɪŋ/ (US) /ˈfaɪərbɒmɪŋ/ (UK)| n.|燃烧弹轰炸;纵火爆炸|The act of attacking a place with firebombs.|The city suffered devastating firebombing during the war.  这座城市在战争期间遭受了毁灭性的燃烧弹轰炸。|Example: The firebombing of Dresden was a controversial act. 德累斯顿的燃烧弹轰炸是一个有争议的行为。|Synonyms: incendiary bombing, bombing, air raid|Antonyms: firefighting, extinguishing|noun

firebomb:/ˈfaɪərbɒm/ (US) /ˈfaɪərbɒm/ (UK)| n. vt.|燃烧弹;纵火;用燃烧弹袭击|A bomb designed to cause fires, or the act of using such a bomb.|The protesters firebombed several government buildings. 抗议者用燃烧弹袭击了几座政府大楼。|Example:  He was arrested for making a firebomb. 他因制造燃烧弹而被捕。|Synonyms: incendiary device, Molotov cocktail|Antonyms: fire extinguisher|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From fire + bomb.  "Bomb" comes from the Greek word "bombos" meaning a deep, resonant sound, referring to the explosion.
