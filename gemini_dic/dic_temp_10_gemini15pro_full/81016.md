gradualist:/ˈɡrædʒuəlɪst/| n. adj.|渐进主义者；渐进的|A person who believes in gradualism, the belief that change should happen slowly and in small steps.  A gradual process or change.|例句：He is a gradualist in his approach to political reform. 他在政治改革方面是一位渐进主义者。|近义词：evolutionist, reformer|反义词:revolutionary, radical|词性:noun, adjective

gradual:/ˈɡrædʒuəl/| adj.|逐渐的；逐步的|Happening or changing slowly over a long period of time.|例句：The country experienced a gradual shift towards democracy. 这个国家经历了向民主的逐渐转变。|近义词：progressive, incremental|反义词:sudden, abrupt|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *gradualis "arranged by steps," from Latin gradus "step, degree" + -alis -al (1).  The sense of "proceeding by steps or degrees" is from 1660s.
