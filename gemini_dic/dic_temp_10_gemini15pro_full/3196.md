affectability:/əˌfɛktəˈbɪləti/
n.
易受影响性；感情脆弱
The quality of being easily affected; susceptibility to emotional influence.
Her affectability made her prone to tears at even the slightest provocation. 她容易受影响的性格使她即使受到最轻微的挑衅也会流泪。
impressionability, sensitivity, responsiveness
resistance, impassivity
noun

affect:/əˈfɛkt/
v.
影响；感染；感动；假装
To have an influence on; to touch or move the emotions of; to pretend to have or feel (something).
The new law will affect small businesses. 新法律将影响小型企业。
influence, impact, touch
leave unaffected, be immune to
verb

affect:/ˈæfɛkt/
n.
情感；心情；表情
Emotion or feeling, especially as manifested or expressed.
He displayed a flat affect, showing no emotion. 他面无表情，没有流露任何情感。
feeling, emotion, mood
apathy, indifference
noun
