importunateness:/ɪmˌpɔːrˈtjuːnɪtnəs/ | n. | 持续纠缠；强求；无理要求 | The quality of being importunate; persistence in solicitation or demand, especially to an annoying degree. |  His importunateness finally wore her down and she agreed to help. 他不停地纠缠她，最终她妥协了，答应帮忙。

| Synonyms: persistence, insistence, pertinacity, pushiness | Antonyms: reluctance, hesitancy, reserve, diffidence | noun


importunate:/ɪmˈpɔːrtʃənɪt/ | adj. | 持续纠缠的；强求的；无理要求的 | Persistent, especially to the point of annoyance or intrusion. |  He was importunate in his demands for an answer.  他不停地追问答案，让人厌烦。

| Synonyms: persistent, insistent, demanding, pressing | Antonyms: reserved, hesitant, reluctant, diffident | adjective

词源:  From Late Latin *importunus "unfit, inconvenient, troublesome," from in- "not" + portunus "favorable, fit, suitable" (from portus "harbor"). The original sense in English was "inconvenient, untimely," later shifting to "persistent in requests or demands."
