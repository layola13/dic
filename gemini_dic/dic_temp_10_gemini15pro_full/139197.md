objectionist: /əbˈdʒɛkʃənɪst/ | n. | 反对者，异议者 | A person who habitually or persistently raises objections. |  The objectionist held up the proceedings with endless questions. 反对者无休止地提出问题，阻碍了会议的进行。

|  She gained a reputation as an objectionist, always finding fault with new proposals. 她以反对者而闻名，总是对新的提案吹毛求疵。| 近义词: opponent, dissenter, protestor | 反义词: supporter, proponent, advocate | 词性: noun

objectionistic: /əbˈdʒɛkʃənˈɪstɪk/ | adj. | 反对的，爱反对的 |  Relating to or characteristic of an objectionist; inclined to object. | His objectionistic attitude made it difficult to reach a consensus. 他爱反对的态度使得很难达成共识。

| The objectionistic tone of her email was unnecessary. 她邮件中反对的语气是不必要的。 | 近义词: contrary, quarrelsome, contentious | 反义词: agreeable, amenable, cooperative | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From object (v.) + -ion + -ist.  The verb "object" comes from the Latin "objectus," past participle of "obicere," meaning "to throw against, to oppose."
