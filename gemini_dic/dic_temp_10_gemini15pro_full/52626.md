digged-out:/ˌdɪɡd ˈaʊt/| adj.|挖出的，挖掘出来的|Something that is dug-out is excavated or unearthed from the ground. It can also describe something that has been retrieved from storage or a hidden place after a long time.|例句：They found a dug-out canoe in the bog. 他们在沼泽里发现了一艘独木舟。|例句：She wore a dug-out dress from the back of her closet. 她穿了一件从衣柜后面翻出来的连衣裙。|近义词：excavated, unearthed, exhumed, retrieved|反义词：buried, hidden, concealed|词性:形容词

digged out:/ˌdɪɡd ˈaʊt/|  v. |挖出，挖掘出|Past participle of "dig out," meaning to remove something from the ground or a confined space by digging. It can also mean to retrieve something from storage or a hidden place after a long time.|例句：The archaeologists dugged out the ancient ruins. 考古学家挖出了古代遗迹。|例句：He dugged out his old yearbook from the attic. 他从阁楼里翻出了他的旧年鉴。|近义词：excavated, unearthed, exhumed, retrieved|反义词：buried, hidden, concealed|词性:动词
