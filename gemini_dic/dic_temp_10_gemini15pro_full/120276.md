miniaturizing:/ˈmɪnɪətʃəˌraɪzɪŋ/
|v.|小型化；微型化|The process of making something much smaller than its usual size.|Scientists are miniaturizing electronic devices. 科学家们正在使电子设备小型化。|miniaturize, shrinking, reducing|enlarging, expanding|verb

miniaturization:/ˌmɪnɪətʃəraɪˈzeɪʃən/
|n.|小型化；微型化|The act or process of miniaturizing something.|The miniaturization of computers has revolutionized the electronics industry. 计算机的小型化彻底改变了电子工业。|miniaturizing, shrinking, reduction|enlargement, expansion|noun

miniature:/ˈmɪnɪtʃər/
|adj.|微型的；小型的|A small version of something.|She bought a miniature replica of the Eiffel Tower. 她买了一个埃菲尔铁塔的微型复制品。|small, tiny, diminutive|large, huge, giant|adjective
miniature:/ˈmɪnɪtʃər/
|n.|微型复制品；缩影|A small version of something, often a painting.|He collects miniatures of vintage cars. 他收集老爷车的微型复制品。|replica, model, scale model|-|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *miniature*, from Italian *miniatura*, from Medieval Latin *miniatura* "picture in an illuminated manuscript," from Latin *miniare* "to color with red lead," from *minium* "red lead."
