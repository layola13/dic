overbookishly:/ˌoʊvərˈbʊkɪʃli/
|adv.|
书呆子气地；学究式地；过于卖弄学问地|
Excessively concerned with books or scholarship, often in a way that is pedantic or ostentatious.  Showing or suggesting an excessive fondness for books or study, often in a way that appears pretentious.|
例句：He spoke overbookishly, using obscure vocabulary that no one understood. 他说话太书生气了，用了一些没人懂的晦涩词汇。

例句：She delivered her presentation overbookishly, citing numerous academic sources that seemed irrelevant to the topic.  她做演讲时过于卖弄学问，引用了许多与主题无关的学术资料。
|
近义词：pedantically, pretentiously, academically, scholastically|
反义词：practically, simply, plainly|
词性: 副词
