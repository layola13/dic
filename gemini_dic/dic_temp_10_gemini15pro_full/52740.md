diktat:/dɪkˈtɑːt/ | n. |  命令；指令；规定 | An order or decree imposed by someone in power without popular consent. | The coach’s diktat was that all players must attend practice every day. 教练的命令是所有球员必须每天参加训练。|  The fashion magazine's diktats influence what many people wear.  时尚杂志的规定影响着许多人的穿着。 | synonyms: decree, edict, mandate, order, fiat, command | antonyms:  negotiation, compromise, democracy,  plebiscite | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Diktat*, from Latin *dictatum* ("something dictated"), neuter past participle of *dictare* ("to dictate"), frequentative of *dicere* ("to say").
