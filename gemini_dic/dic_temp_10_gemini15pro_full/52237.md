diazeuctic:/ˌdaɪəˈzuktɪk/
adjective
分开连接的；相隔的；分离的
Relating to or characterized by diazeuxis, a rhetorical device involving the separation of grammatically coordinate elements by other intervening words.
Diazeuctic pauses can create a sense of drama and anticipation in a speech.  分开连接的停顿可以在演讲中营造一种戏剧性和期待感。
The poet used diazeuctic phrasing to emphasize the contrast between life and death. 诗人用分开连接的短语来强调生与死的对比。
近义词: disjunctive, separative
反义词: conjunctive, connecting
词性: adjective

词源: From Greek *diazeuktikós, from diazeugnynai 'to disjoin', from dia 'apart' + zeugnynai 'to yoke'.
