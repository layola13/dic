inoculative:/ɪˈnɒkjʊlətɪv/ | adj. | 接种的；灌输的 | Introducing or tending to introduce something, especially an idea or attitude, into the mind of another. |  The teacher's inoculative methods helped the students develop a love for reading. 老师的循循善诱的方法帮助学生培养了阅读的热爱。| 近义词:  instilling, implanting,  influential, inspiring | 反义词:  suppressive, deterrent | 词性: adjective

inoculate:/ɪˈnɒkjʊleɪt/ | vt. | 接种；灌输 |  To introduce (something, such as an idea or attitude) into the mind of another.  To introduce a serum, vaccine, or antigenic substance into (a person, animal, or plant) to produce or increase immunity to a particular disease. | The doctor inoculated the children against measles. 医生给孩子们接种了麻疹疫苗。 The government tried to inoculate the public against the spread of misinformation. 政府试图让公众对错误信息的传播免疫。|近义词: vaccinate, immunize, imbue, infuse | 反义词: infect, contaminate | 词性: verb

**词源:**

From Late Latin *inoculātus*, past participle of *inoculāre* "to implant a bud or graft," from *in-* "in" + *oculus* "eye, bud."  Originally referring to grafting plants, the meaning was extended to medical inoculation in the 18th century and later to figurative uses of introducing ideas.
