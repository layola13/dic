non-leveraged:/ˌnɒnˈliːvərɪdʒd/ /ˌnɑːnˈlevərɪdʒd/| adj.|无杠杆的，无负债的|Not using borrowed capital or debt financing.  Referring to investments or businesses that operate without the use of borrowed money.|例句：The company pursued a non-leveraged growth strategy.  这家公司奉行无杠杆增长战略。|例句：Non-leveraged investments are generally considered less risky. 无杠杆投资通常被认为风险较低。|近义词：debt-free, unlevered, ungeared|反义词: leveraged, geared|词性:形容词

词源:
* non-:  Latin prefix meaning "not"
* leveraged: From "leverage," ultimately from Latin "levis" (light), referring to the ability to lift heavy objects with relatively little force.  In finance, leverage refers to the use of borrowed money to increase potential returns (but also potential losses).
