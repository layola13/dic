childishness:/ˈtʃaɪldɪʃnəs/ /ˈtʃaɪldɪʃnɪs/| n. |幼稚，孩子气|The state of being childish; immature behavior or thinking.|His childishness is sometimes endearing, but often frustrating. 他的孩子气有时很可爱，但经常令人沮丧。|近义词：immaturity, puerility, naiveté|反义词：maturity, sophistication, worldliness|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From childish + -ness.  Childish comes from Middle English *childisch*, from Old English *cildisc* ("childish"), from *cild* ("child") + *-isc* (suffix forming adjectives).  The *-ness* suffix forms nouns denoting a state or quality.
