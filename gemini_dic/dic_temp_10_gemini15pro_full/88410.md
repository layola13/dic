hirudine:/ˈhɪrʊdiːn/| n. |水蛭素|A peptide, present in the salivary glands of leeches, that inhibits blood coagulation by specifically binding to thrombin.|Hirudin is used medically as an anticoagulant. 水蛭素在医学上用作抗凝血剂。|Synonyms: anticoagulant, antithrombin|Antonyms: coagulant, procoagulant|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hirūdīna, from Latin hirūdō (“leech”).
