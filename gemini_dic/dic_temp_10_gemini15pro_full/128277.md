neuroplasticity: /ˌnʊərəʊplæˈstɪsɪti/ /ˌnʊroʊplæˈstɪsɪti/ | n. | 神经可塑性 | The brain's ability to reorganize itself by forming new neural connections throughout life.  This allows the brain to compensate for injury and disease and to adjust its activities in response to new situations or to changes in their environment. |  Research has shown that neuroplasticity plays a crucial role in learning and memory. 研究表明，神经可塑性在学习和记忆中起着至关重要的作用。|  Neuroplasticity allows the brain to recover after a stroke. 神经可塑性使大脑在中风后得以恢复。 | Synonyms: brain plasticity, neural plasticity | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but a concept relating to lack of change might be *neural rigidity* although not a formally recognized term) | noun
