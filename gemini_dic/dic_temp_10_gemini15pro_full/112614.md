low-church:/ˌloʊˈtʃɜːrtʃ/| adj.|低教会派的，不拘礼仪的|Favoring a simple and informal style of worship, doctrine, and church organization, with little emphasis on ritual or ceremony.|The new vicar was known for his low-church views, preferring simple services to elaborate rituals.|近义词:informal, simple, evangelical|反义词:high-church, Anglo-Catholic|词性:形容词
词源:  "low" (referring to a less elaborate style) + "church" (referring to the Church of England). The term originated in the 18th century to describe those within the Church of England who advocated for simpler forms of worship and less emphasis on ceremony and tradition, often associated with Puritan and evangelical influences.
