ace-up-sleeve:/ˌeɪs ʌp wʌnz ˈsliːv/ | n. | 秘密武器；锦囊妙计；王牌 | A secret advantage or resource that can be used when needed. |  He had an ace up his sleeve – he knew the chairman of the selection committee. 他有一个秘密武器——他认识遴选委员会的主席。

He kept his ace up his sleeve until the final round of negotiations. 他把他的王牌留到最后一轮谈判才用。| 近义词: secret weapon, hidden advantage, trump card | 反义词: disadvantage, weakness | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From card games, specifically poker. An "ace" is the highest-value card, and keeping it hidden "up one's sleeve" (literally or figuratively) implies a secret advantage that can be used to win.  源于纸牌游戏，特别是扑克。“ace”是价值最高的牌，把它藏在“袖子里”（字面上或比喻上）意味着一个可以用来获胜的秘密优势。
