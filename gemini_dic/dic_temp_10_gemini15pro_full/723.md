absentment:/ˈæbsəntmənt/
n.
心不在焉；出神；疏忽
The state of being absent-minded or preoccupied.
His absentment during the meeting led to him missing crucial information.  他在会议期间心不在焉，导致错过了关键信息。

Her absentment caused her to burn the dinner. 她的心不在焉导致她把晚餐烧糊了。

近义词:abstraction, preoccupation, inattention, forgetfulness
反义词:attentiveness, mindfulness, alertness
词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From absent + -ment (suffix forming nouns of action or result).  由absent（缺席的） + -ment（构成动作或结果的名词的后缀）组成。


USAGE->
This word is considered archaic and rarely used in modern English.  这个词被认为是古语，在现代英语中很少使用。  It's more common to use phrases like "absent-mindedness" or "preoccupation."  更常见的用法是“absent-mindedness”或“preoccupation”。


PHRASE->
Due to its infrequent use, "absentment" doesn't have common phrases associated with it. 由于其不常用的性质，“absentment”没有相关的常用短语。
