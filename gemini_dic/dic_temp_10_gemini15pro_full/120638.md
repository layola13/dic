mis-measures:/ˌmɪsˈmeʒərz/
| n. | 措施失当；失策；错误的衡量标准 | Incorrect or inappropriate actions or decisions; faulty standards of judgment. | The government's mis-measures led to a severe economic crisis. 政府的措施失当导致了严重的经济危机。 |  The company's mis-measures in marketing resulted in significant losses.  公司的营销失策导致了重大损失。 | 近义词：blunders, mistakes, errors, miscalculations, misjudgements | 反义词: accurate measures, correct actions, appropriate decisions | 词性: noun


mis-measure:/ˌmɪsˈmeʒər/
| v. |  错误地测量；错误地判断；错误地估计 | To measure, judge, or estimate incorrectly. |  He mis-measured the ingredients, and the cake was a disaster. 他错误地测量了配料，蛋糕做得很糟糕。 | The tailor mis-measured the customer and the suit didn't fit.  裁缝错误地测量了顾客的身材，导致西装不合身。 | 近义词：miscalculate, misjudge, underestimate, overestimate | 反义词: measure accurately, judge correctly, estimate correctly | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From the prefix "mis-" (Old English "mis-", meaning "wrongly, badly, amiss") + "measure" (Old French "mesure", from Latin "mensura", meaning "a measuring, measure").
