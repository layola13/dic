eosin:/ˈiːəsɪn, ˈiːəzɪn/| n. adj.|曙红，伊红|A reddish fluorescent dye resulting from the action of bromine on fluorescein; used in textile dyeing and as a biological stain.|The biologist used eosin to stain the tissue sample. 生物学家用曙红给组织样本染色。|Near synonyms: rose bengal, erythrosine|Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for the dye itself; perhaps consider contrasting colors like "methylene blue")|noun, adjective
