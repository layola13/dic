newsily:/ˈnjuːzɪli/ | adv. | 好事地；爱传闲话地；爱报道新闻地 | In a way that is characteristic of newspapers, especially in focusing on sensational or trivial matters. |  She newsily reported every detail of the office gossip. 她好事地报道了办公室八卦的每一个细节。|  He newsily announced the engagement before the couple had told their families. 他在 pasangan 告诉他们的家人之前就急吼吼地宣布了他们的订婚。| 近义词: gossipingly, chattily, blabbermouthly | 反义词: discreetly, privately, confidentially | 词性: 副词

词源:  From news + -ily (adverbial suffix).  The word "news" itself comes from Middle English "newes," plural of "newe," meaning "new thing," from Old French "noveles" and ultimately from Latin "nova," plural of "novus" meaning "new."
