flurrying:/ˈflʌriɪŋ/ | adj. | 纷飞的，一阵忙乱的 | A sudden, brief, and light fall of snow or rain; a short period of intense activity. | Snow was flurrying through the air. 雪花在空中纷飞。|近义词：rushing, bustling, swarming |反义词: calm, quiet |词性: 形容词

flurry:/ˈflʌri/ | n. | 一阵，一连串，忙乱 | A sudden, brief, and light fall of snow or rain; a short period of intense activity. | There was a flurry of activity in the kitchen as the chefs prepared for the dinner rush. 厨房里一阵忙乱，厨师们正准备迎接晚餐高峰。|近义词：burst, rush, spate |反义词: lull, calm |词性: 名词

flurry:/ˈflʌri/ | v. |  使慌乱，使激动，快速移动 | To move in a quick and confused way; to cause a flurry. | The sudden noise flurried the birds. 突然的噪音使鸟儿惊慌失措。|近义词：agitate, excite, rush |反义词: calm, soothe |词性: 动词
