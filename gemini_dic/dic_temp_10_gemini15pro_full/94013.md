impalpably:/ɪmˈpæl.pə.bli/
adjective
难以察觉地；无法感知地
Not easily perceived by the senses, especially the sense of touch; intangible.
His influence on the team was subtle, almost impalpable. 他对团队的影响很微妙，几乎难以察觉。
The atmosphere in the room was thick with an impalpable tension. 房间里的气氛弥漫着一种难以察觉的紧张感。
Near synonyms: intangible, imperceptible, indefinable, subtle
Near antonyms: tangible, perceptible, palpable, obvious
word class: adjective

Etymology:
From Late Latin *impalpabilis, from in- "not" + palpabilis "tangible," from palpare "to touch, stroke."
