gowden:/ˈɡoʊdən/ (Scots)| adj.|金色的，美好的|A Scots word meaning "golden" or "made of gold," often used poetically or figuratively to describe something beautiful or precious.  Derived from the Old English "gylden."|The gowden rays of the setting sun illuminated the loch.  落日的金色光线照亮了湖泊。|Synonyms: golden, aureate, resplendent|Antonyms: dull, drab|Part of Speech: adjective

gowden:/ˈɡoʊdən/ (Scots)| n.|黄金|A Scots word meaning "gold."  Less common than the adjectival use.|She wore a necklace of gowden. 她戴着一条金项链。|Synonyms: gold, bullion|Antonyms: none|Part of Speech: noun
