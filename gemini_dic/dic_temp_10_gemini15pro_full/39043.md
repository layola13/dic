confluent:/ˈkɒnfluːənt/ (US) /ˈkɑːnfluːənt/| adj.|汇合的，融合的，合流的|Flowing together; merging.  Coming together to form a larger whole.  Used especially of streams or rivers.|The Missouri and Mississippi rivers are confluent at St. Louis. 密苏里河和密西西比河在圣路易斯汇合。|The confluent streams created a powerful river. 汇合的溪流形成了一条强大的河流。|近义词：merging, converging, joining, uniting|反义词：diverging, separating, branching|词性:形容词

confluence:/ˈkɒnfluːəns/ (US) /ˈkɑːnfluːəns/| n.|汇合，融合，合流|The point where two rivers or streams flow together; a flowing together.  A merging or flowing together of two or more things.|The confluence of the two rivers created a fertile delta. 两条河流的汇合处形成了一个肥沃的三角洲。|The conference was a confluence of artists and scientists. 这次会议是艺术家和科学家们的聚会。|近义词：junction, meeting, convergence, union|反义词：divergence, separation|词性:名词


USAGE->词源: From Latin confluentem (nominative confluens), present participle of confluere "to flow together," from com- "together" (see com-) + fluere "to flow" (see fluent).
