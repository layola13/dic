hamburgers:/ˈhæmbɜːrɡərz/ (US) /ˈhæmbɜːɡəz/ (UK)
n.
汉堡包
A ground beef patty, usually grilled, served on a bun or round roll and often garnished with various ingredients, such as lettuce, tomato, onion, and cheese.
I ate two hamburgers for lunch. 我午饭吃了两个汉堡包。

hamburger:/ˈhæmbɜːrɡər/ (US) /ˈhæmbɜːɡə(r)/ (UK)
n.
汉堡肉饼
Ground beef, often shaped into a patty.
He formed the hamburger into patties. 他把汉堡肉做成了肉饼。

Etymology:  From Hamburg, Germany, where a similar dish originated.  Originally referred to a patty made from beef from Hamburg.

Synonyms:  burger, beefburger (more common in UK)

Antonyms:  None (as it's a specific food item, it doesn't have a direct opposite)
