meta-ethics:/ˈmɛtəˌɛθɪks/ | n. | 元伦理学 |  The branch of ethics that seeks to understand the nature of ethical properties, statements, attitudes, and judgments. It doesn't prescribe moral principles or rules but analyzes the meaning of moral language and the metaphysics of moral facts. |  Meta-ethics explores questions like "What is goodness?" rather than "What is good?" | 近义词: philosophical ethics | 反义词: normative ethics, applied ethics | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Greek *meta* (beyond, after, with, among, behind) + ethics.
