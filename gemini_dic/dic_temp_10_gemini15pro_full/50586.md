derailments:/dɪˈreɪlmənts/
|n.|脱轨；出轨；失败|An instance of a train being derailed.  A sudden and complete failure of a plan, process, or institution.|
|例句：Several derailments have occurred on this stretch of track.  这段铁轨上已经发生了几起脱轨事故。|
|例句：The economic derailments caused widespread hardship. 经济崩溃造成了广泛的苦难。|
|近义词：wrecks, crashes, failures, disruptions|
|反义词：successes, achievements, stability|
|词性：名词（plural noun）|

PHRASE->
train derailment = 火车脱轨
a series of derailments = 一系列脱轨事故
prevent derailments = 防止脱轨
investigate a derailment = 调查脱轨事故
the cause of the derailment = 脱轨的原因

词源:  From de- + rail + -ment.  De- 表示 "离开" 或 "除去"，rail 指的是铁轨，-ment 是名词后缀，表示动作或结果。
