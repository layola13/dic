blab:/blæb/| v. n.|泄露秘密，闲聊，胡扯|To reveal secrets or talk too much, often indiscreetly.|He blabbed everything to the police. 他把一切都告诉了警察。|近义词：chatter, gossip, prattle, divulge|反义词:conceal, withhold|词性:动词, 名词
blabs:/blæbz/| v. (third-person singular simple present) |泄露秘密，闲聊，胡扯（第三人称单数）|Third-person singular simple present form of "blab".|He blabs secrets to anyone who will listen. 他向任何愿意听的人泄露秘密。|近义词：chatters, gossips, prattles, divulges|反义词:conceals, withholds|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *blabben*, of imitative origin.  Relates to other words like "babble."

PHRASE->
blab something out:  不经意地说出  He blabbed out the secret in his sleep. 他在睡梦中说出了秘密。
blab on (about something): 喋喋不休地说  She blabbed on about her vacation for hours. 她喋喋不休地谈论她的假期好几个小时。
blab it all: 全盘托出  He got drunk and blabbed it all to the bartender. 他喝醉了，把一切都告诉了酒保。
don't blab: 不要泄露秘密 Don't blab, but I'm getting a promotion. 不要泄露秘密，但我快要升职了。
blabbermouth:  爱说闲话的人 He's such a blabbermouth; you can't trust him with a secret. 他真是个爱说闲话的人；你不能把秘密告诉他。
