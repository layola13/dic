amalgamative:/əˈmælɡəˌmeɪtɪv/ | adj. | 合并的，融合的 | Tending to unite or combine; uniting or merging elements into a single whole. | The teacher employed an amalgamative approach to teaching, blending different learning styles into her lessons. 老师采用了一种融合的教学方法，将不同的学习风格融入到她的课程中。| The new company represents an amalgamative effort of several smaller businesses. 这家新公司代表了数家小企业的合并努力。|  近义词： unifying, combining, merging, integrating | 反义词： divisive, separating, segregating | 词性：形容词

amalgamate:/əˈmælɡəˌmeɪt/ | v. | 合并，融合 | To combine or unite to form one organization or structure. |  The two companies amalgamated to form a larger corporation.  两家公司合并成一个更大的公司。| The artist amalgamated various styles of painting in his work. 这位艺术家在他的作品中融合了各种绘画风格。 | 近义词： merge, combine, unite, consolidate | 反义词： separate, divide, disintegrate | 词性：动词

amalgam:/əˈmælɡəm/ | n. | 混合物，汞合金 | A mixture or blend of different elements. | The city is an amalgam of old and new buildings. 这座城市是新旧建筑的混合体。|  The final product was an amalgam of several different metals. 最终产品是几种不同金属的混合物。| 近义词： mixture, blend, combination, composite | 反义词： component, element | 词性：名词
