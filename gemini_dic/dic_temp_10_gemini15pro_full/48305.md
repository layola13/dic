decrepit:/dɪˈkrɛpɪt/| adj.|衰老的，破旧的|Weakened by old age; worn out or ruined.  Characterized by deterioration or decay, especially because of age or long use.|The decrepit old house stood on a lonely hill. 这座破旧的老房子孤零零地立在山上。|Synonyms:  infirm, feeble, dilapidated, ramshackle|Antonyms:  robust, sturdy, sound, new|adjective

decrepitate:/dɪˈkrɛpɪteɪt/| v.|（使）衰老，（使）破旧|To make or become decrepit; weaken or cause to deteriorate.|Exposure to the sun can decrepitate rubber. 阳光暴晒会使橡胶老化。|Synonyms:  decay, deteriorate, decline, age|Antonyms:  rejuvenate, restore, renew|verb
