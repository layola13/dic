eyelet:/ˈaɪlɪt/| n.|孔眼；金属扣眼；小孔|A small hole in leather or cloth, often edged with metal, for threading a lace or cord through.  A short length of metal tube inserted into such a hole to strengthen or protect it.|The shoes have eyelets for the laces.  鞋子上有穿鞋带的孔眼。|Synonyms: grommet, hole, perforation|Antonyms: solid, blockage|noun

eyelet:/ˈaɪlɪt/| v.|给…打孔眼|To provide (something) with eyelets.|She eyeletted the curtains so they could be hung on the rod. 她给窗帘打了孔眼，这样就可以挂在杆子上了。|Synonyms: grommet, perforate|Antonyms: seal, close|verb
