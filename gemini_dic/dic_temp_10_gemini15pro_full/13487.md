attaboy:/ˌætəˈbɔɪ/
interjection
好小子！/干得好！/加把劲！
An exclamation of encouragement or approval, typically addressed to a male.  Used to express praise or encouragement, especially to a boy or man.
Attaboy, Johnny, you hit that ball a mile! 好小子，约翰尼，你把球打得很远！
Attaboy, team, keep up the good work!  加把劲，团队，继续努力！
近义词:well done, good job, that's the boy, good for you
反义词: (No direct antonym, but expressions of disapproval like "boo," "shame," or "that's terrible" could be considered opposites in context.)
词性: interjection

注意事项:
"Attaboy" is informal and primarily used in American English.  It is generally considered more appropriate for encouraging males.  While sometimes used with females, it can sound slightly outdated or patronizing in such cases.  A more gender-neutral alternative would be "good job" or "well done."
