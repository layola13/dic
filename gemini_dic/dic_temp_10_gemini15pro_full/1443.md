ace-up-your-sleeve:/ˌeɪs ʌp jɔːr ˈsliːv/| idiom | 袖中王牌；秘计；锦囊妙计 |  A secret advantage, plan, or piece of information that can be used if needed.  It refers to hiding an ace (a high-value playing card) in your sleeve to cheat at cards, but is now more commonly used figuratively. |  He had an ace up his sleeve – he knew the chairman of the selection committee. 他有一个锦囊妙计——他认识遴选委员会的主席。

He always seems to have an ace up his sleeve when negotiating deals. 他在谈判交易时似乎总有锦囊妙计。| 近义词: secret weapon, hidden advantage, trump card | 反义词: disadvantage, weakness | 词性: idiom


ETYMOLOGY->
The phrase originates from card games, particularly poker, where hiding an ace up one's sleeve would guarantee a win. This practice was, of course, cheating.  Over time, the phrase evolved to mean any hidden advantage or secret plan.
