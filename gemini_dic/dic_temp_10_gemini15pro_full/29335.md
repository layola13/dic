castillo:/kæˈstijoʊ/ | n. | 城堡；堡垒 | A large building, typically of the medieval period, fortified against attack with thick walls, battlements, towers, and often a moat. |  The king lived in a grand castillo overlooking the valley. 国王住在一座俯瞰山谷的宏伟城堡里。| synonyms: castle, fortress, citadel | antonyms: house, hut | noun

castillo:/kæˈstijoʊ/ | n. | （纸牌游戏） 纸牌 | (in card games) a playing card | He played the castillo and won the hand. 他出了那张牌，赢得了这一局。| synonyms: card | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *castillo*, diminutive of *castro* "fort, castle", from Latin *castrum* "fort". Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱes- "to cut, scratch, scrape."  Relating to the idea of a fortified enclosure.
