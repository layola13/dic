词源:  From "downright" + "-ness". "Downright" originates from the adverb "downright," meaning "straight down" or "thoroughly,"  from Old English *dūnrīht (recorded as adūnrīht) "straight down," from "dūn" (down) + "rīht" (right).  The "-ness" suffix forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state, quality, or condition.
