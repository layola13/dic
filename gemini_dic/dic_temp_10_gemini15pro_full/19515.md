bierbalk:/ˈbiːrˌbɑːlk/| n.|啤酒梁，顶梁|A large beam, typically used in traditional Dutch houses to support the upper floors and roof, sometimes decorated with carved symbols of the brewing trade.|The bierbalk in the old brewery was elaborately carved with hops and barley.|Synonym: beam, girder, rafter|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps support column)|noun

Etymology:
From Dutch *bier* (beer) + *balk* (beam).  The term likely originates from breweries or houses where beer was brewed and stored, and the main beam might have been particularly strong to support the weight.
