antichronical:/ˌæntiˈkrɒnɪkəl/ | adj. | 逆时序的，时代错误的 | Going or occurring against the correct order of time; anachronistic.  |  His anticronical costume, a Roman toga at a Renaissance fair, caused much amusement. 他那时代错误的服装，一件在文艺复兴集市上穿的罗马长袍，引起了很多人的乐趣。 |  The author's placement of a cell phone in Victorian England is an anticronical detail that detracts from the story's realism. 作者将一部手机置于维多利亚时代的英格兰，这是一个时代错误的细节，有损于故事的真实性。| 近义词: anachronistic, out of date, out of place, out of time | 反义词: chronological, timely, contemporary | 词性: 形容词

词源: From anti- (against) + chronical (relating to time). Ultimately from Greek anti- (against) + khronos (time).
