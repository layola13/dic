deescalating: /ˌdiːˈɛskəleɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 减少，降低，缓和 | Reducing the intensity or seriousness of something; causing something to become less dangerous or difficult. |  The police were successful in deescalating the tense situation. 警察成功地缓和了紧张的局势。| Synonyms: mitigating, lessening, alleviating, moderating | Antonyms: escalating, intensifying, aggravating, exacerbating | Verb (present participle)

deescalate: /ˌdiːˈɛskəleɪt/ | v. | 减少，降低，缓和 | To reduce the intensity or seriousness of something; to cause something to become less dangerous or difficult. | The negotiators worked to deescalate the conflict.  谈判人员努力缓和冲突。| Synonyms: mitigate, lessen, alleviate, moderate | Antonyms: escalate, intensify, aggravate, exacerbate | Verb


词源:  "De-" (meaning "down" or "reverse") + "escalate" (from "escalade," meaning "to scale a wall using ladders").  "Escalate" itself comes from the Latin "scala," meaning "ladder" or "stairs."
