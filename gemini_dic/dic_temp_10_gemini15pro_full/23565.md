brainwash: /ˈbreɪnwɒʃ/ (UK) /ˈbreɪnwɑːʃ/ (US) | n. vt. | 洗脑 |  to force someone to accept your ideas by repeating them and preventing the person from hearing different opinions| The cult leader tried to brainwash his followers. | 近义词: indoctrinate, propagandize, condition | 反义词: deprogram, enlighten | 词性: noun, verb

Etymology:  A calque (loan translation) of Chinese 洗腦 (xǐnǎo, literally "wash brain"), first attested in English in the 1950s during the Korean War.
