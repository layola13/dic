brutalization:/ˌbruːtələˈzeɪʃn/
|n.|野蛮化，残酷化|The brutalization of war leaves lasting scars on both individuals and societies. 战争的残酷化给个人和社会都留下了持久的伤疤。|The constant exposure to violence led to the brutalization of the community.  持续接触暴力导致社区变得野蛮。|近义词：dehumanization, barbarization|反义词：civilization, humanization|词性:名词

brutalize:/ˈbruːtəlaɪz/
|v.|使残暴，使野蛮化|The prison system can brutalize inmates. 监狱制度会使囚犯变得残暴。|Years of abuse had brutalized him. 多年的虐待使他变得野蛮。|近义词：dehumanize, barbarize|反义词：civilize, humanize|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From brutal + -ize.  Brutal comes from the Latin word "brutalis," meaning "of beasts," derived from "brutus," meaning "dull, insensible, irrational, brute, beast."
