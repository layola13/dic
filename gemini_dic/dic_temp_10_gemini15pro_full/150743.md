paracmastic:/ˌpærækˈmæstɪk/| adj.|处于顶峰的，最盛期的|Relating to or denoting the period of greatest vigor or maturity in the life cycle of an organism, especially before the onset of decline.|The athlete was in his paracmastic phase, breaking records left and right.|近义词: culminating, peak, climaxing, optimal, prime|反义词: declining, waning, deteriorating|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Greek *parakmḗ*, "decline," from *pará*, "beside," + *akmḗ*, "point, edge."  While the Greek root refers to decline, the English word ironically refers to the period *before* the decline, the peak.
