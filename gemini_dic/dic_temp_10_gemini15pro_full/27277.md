calumniation: /kəˌlʌmniˈeɪʃn/ | n. | 诽谤，中伤 | the making of false and defamatory statements about someone in order to damage their reputation; slander. |  He was the target of calumniation by his political rivals. 他成为了政治对手诽谤的目标。

The newspaper article was a vicious calumniation of the actor's character. 这篇报纸文章是对这位演员人格的恶意诽谤。 | 近义词：slander, defamation, libel, vilification, aspersion, smear | 反义词：praise, commendation, acclaim, exoneration | 词性: 名词

calumniate: /kəˈlʌmniˌeɪt/ | vt. | 诽谤，中伤 | to make false and defamatory statements about someone in order to damage their reputation. |  They calumniated her in the press. 他们在媒体上诽谤她。

His opponents calumniated him relentlessly. 他的对手不断地诽谤他。 | 近义词：slander, defame, libel, vilify, smear | 反义词：praise, commend, acclaim, exonerate | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *calumniationem, accusative singular of calumniatio (“false accusation, slander”), from Latin calumnior (“to accuse falsely, misrepresent, slander”), from Latin calumnia (“trickery, chicanery, false accusation, slander”), probably from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kel- (“to cut, divide”).
