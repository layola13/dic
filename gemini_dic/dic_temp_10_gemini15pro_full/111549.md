lobbies:/ˈlɑbiz/ (US) /ˈlɒbiz/ (UK)| n.|大厅，休息室，游说团体|A lobby is a large entrance hall or waiting area in a building, such as a hotel or theater. It can also refer to a group of people who try to influence politicians on a particular issue.|例句：The hotel lobby was grand and impressive.  酒店大堂富丽堂皇，令人印象深刻。

例句：The environmental lobby is pressuring the government to pass stricter laws.  环保游说团体正在向政府施压，要求通过更严格的法律。|近义词：foyers, entrance halls, pressure groups, interest groups|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


lobbies:/ˈlɑbiz/ (US) /ˈlɒbiz/ (UK)| v.|游说|To lobby is to try to persuade a politician or government official to support a particular cause or policy.|例句：Several organizations are lobbying for stricter gun control laws.  几个组织正在游说更严格的枪支管制法。

例句：She's been lobbying Congress to increase funding for education. 她一直在游说国会增加教育经费。|近义词：persuade, influence, petition|反义词: N/A|词性:verb
