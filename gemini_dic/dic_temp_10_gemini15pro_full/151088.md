paras:/ˈpærəs/ | n. | 寄生虫；食客；谄媚者 | An organism that lives in or on another organism (its host) and benefits by deriving nutrients at the other's expense.  | Example: The dog was infested with paras.  这条狗身上长满了寄生虫。| Example: He's a bit of a para, always sponging off his friends. 他有点像个寄生虫，总是依赖他的朋友。 | Synonyms:  scrounger, freeloader, leech, sponge | Antonyms: host, benefactor | Noun


Etymology: From Latin *parāsītus*, from Greek *παράσιτος* (parásitos, “one who eats at the table of another”), from *παρά* (pará, “beside, near”) + *σῖτος* (sîtos, “food, grain”).
