courtling: /ˈkɔːrtlɪŋ/ | n. | 求爱者；献殷勤的人 | A person who courts another; a wooer, especially a man courting a woman. |  He was a persistent courtling, sending her flowers every day. 他是一位执着的追求者，每天都给她送花。|  The young courtling nervously awaited her response.  这位年轻的求爱者紧张地等待着她的回应。| Synonyms: suitor, wooer, admirer | Antonyms: rival, competitor | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *curteling, *courteling, equivalent to court + -ling.  Ultimately from Latin cohors (“enclosed yard, retinue”).
