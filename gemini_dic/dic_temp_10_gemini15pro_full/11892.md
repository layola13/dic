arrogative: /ˈærəɡətɪv/ | adj. | 傲慢的，自大的 | Claiming or seeming to claim unwarranted importance or superiority; overbearingly proud.  |  He spoke in an arrogative tone that offended many.  他说话的语气很傲慢，冒犯了许多人。 | synonyms: haughty, supercilious, presumptuous, disdainful | antonyms: humble, modest, unassuming | adjective


arrogative: /ˈærəɡətɪv/ | n. | 傲慢的人 |  A person who is arrogative. (Less common usage) | He's such an arrogative!  他真是个傲慢的人！（不太常用） | synonyms: snob | antonyms: none (for noun form) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *arrogativus, from Latin arrogatus past participle of arrogare "to claim for oneself, appropriate, assume," from ad- "to" (see ad-) + rogare "to ask" (see rogation). Related: Arrogatively.
