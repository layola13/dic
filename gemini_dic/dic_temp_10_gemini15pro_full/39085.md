confounding:/kənˈfaʊndɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人困惑的；混淆的；惊人的；讨厌的 |  Causing surprise or confusion, especially by acting against one's expectations. | The confounding results of the study left the researchers puzzled.  这项研究令人困惑的结果让研究人员感到不解。 | synonyms: perplexing, bewildering, confusing, baffling, surprising, astonishing | antonyms: clarifying, elucidating, expected, predictable | adjective

confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/ | v. | 使困惑；混淆；挫败；诅咒 |  To cause surprise or confusion in (someone), especially by acting against their expectations. To prove (a theory, expectation, or prediction) wrong. | His sudden change of heart confounded everyone. 他的突然改变主意使每个人都感到困惑。  The team confounded all predictions by winning the championship.  该队赢得了冠军，这与所有预测都相反。 | synonyms: confuse, bewilder, perplex, baffle, frustrate, thwart | antonyms: clarify, elucidate, enlighten, explain, confirm | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French confondre, from Latin confundere "to pour together, mix up, confuse, confound," from com- "together" (see com-) + fundere "to pour" (see found (v.2)).
