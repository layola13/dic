bumkin:/ˈbʌmkɪn/| n. |乡巴佬，土包子|A person from the countryside who is considered to be awkward and unsophisticated.|The city slickers laughed at the bumkin's outdated clothes. 城市里的时髦人嘲笑这个乡巴佬过时的衣着。|近义词：yokel, rustic, peasant|反义词:sophisticate, cosmopolitan|noun

bumkin:/ˈbʌmkɪn/| n. |笨蛋，傻瓜|A naive or foolish person.|He's such a bumkin, he believes everything he reads on the internet. 他真是个笨蛋，他相信在网上读到的所有东西。|近义词：simpleton, naive|反义词:smart, clever|noun

词源：Likely from Dutch *bomke "little tree," or Middle Low German *bumkin "small tree trunk."  The original sense was likely "blockhead, dolt," perhaps from the image of a thick piece of wood.  Later, the meaning shifted to refer to an unsophisticated country person.
