abridge:/əˈbrɪdʒ/| vt.| 缩短，节略| To shorten or condense, especially a written work, without losing the sense.|  The editor abridged the manuscript for publication. 编辑为了出版而删节了手稿。

The author abridges his lengthy novel for younger readers. 作者将冗长的小说删节为适合年轻读者阅读的版本。

|近义词：shorten, condense, curtail, reduce|反义词：expand, lengthen, extend, amplify|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English abregen, abreggen, from Old French abregier, from Latin abbreviare (“to shorten”), from ad (“to”) + brevis (“short”).

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The author abridges the story for younger readers. = 作者为年轻读者删节了故事。
She often abridges long articles for her blog. = 她经常为她的博客删节长篇文章。

一般过去时:
He abridged the original text by half. = 他将原文删减了一半。
They abridged the documentary for television broadcast. = 他们为电视广播删节了纪录片。

现在完成时:
The publisher has abridged the novel for a new edition. = 出版商为新版本删节了这部小说。
The committee has abridged its report to make it more concise. = 委员会删节了报告，使其更加简洁。

过去完成时:
The editor had abridged the manuscript before sending it to the printer. = 编辑在将手稿送到印刷厂之前已经将其删节了。
She had abridged the play before the first rehearsal. = 她在第一次彩排前已经删节了剧本。

将来时:

The editor will abridge the book for the paperback edition. = 编辑将为平装本删节这本书。


被动语态:
