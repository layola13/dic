condescension:/ˌkɒndɪˈsɛnʃən/ (British) /ˌkɑːndɪˈsɛnʃən/ (American)| n.|傲慢；屈尊；俯就|An attitude of patronizing superiority; disdain.|例句：He spoke to me with such condescension in his voice. 他用一种居高临下的语气跟我说话。

例句：She offered to help with an air of condescension. 她带着一副屈尊俯就的样子提出帮忙。


近义词：patronizing, disdain, superciliousness, haughtiness|反义词:humility, respect, modesty|词性:noun


condescend:/ˌkɒndɪˈsɛnd/ (British) /ˌkɑːndɪˈsɛnd/ (American)| v.|屈尊；俯就|Show feelings of superiority; patronize.|例句：He condescended to explain the situation to us. 他屈尊向我们解释了情况。

例句：Would you condescend to join us for dinner?  您肯赏光和我们一起吃晚饭吗？

近义词：deign, stoop, patronize|反义词:respect, honor|词性:verb
