crag:/kræɡ/
n.|悬崖，峭壁|A steep, rugged rock face, especially one that is part of a cliff or mountain.|The climbers scaled the crag. 攀登者攀登了悬崖。|cliff, precipice, bluff|slope, plain|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cragge*, from Old Northern French *cragge* (compare modern French *crac*), of uncertain origin, perhaps from a Celtic source.
