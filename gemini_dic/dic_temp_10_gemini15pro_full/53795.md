discomposure:/dɪskəmˈpəʊʒə(r)/ | n. |不安；慌乱；窘迫；失态 |A state of being flustered, agitated, or unsettled; loss of composure. |例句：His sudden question caused her a moment of discomposure. 他的突然提问让她一时不知所措。

例句：The unexpected news threw her into a state of discomposure.  这个突如其来的消息让她陷入了不安的状态。 |近义词：unease, anxiety, agitation, perturbation, distress |反义词：composure, calmness, tranquility, serenity |词性:名词

Etymology: From dis- (expressing negation, reversal) + composure (from French composer "to compose, arrange," from Latin componere "to put together").  字源：来自dis-（表示否定、反转）+ composure（来自法语composer“组成、安排”，源自拉丁语componere“放在一起”）。
