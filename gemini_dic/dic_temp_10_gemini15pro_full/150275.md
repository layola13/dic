panegyric:/ˌpænɪˈdʒɪrɪk/| n.|颂词，赞美词，赞颂|A formal speech or piece of writing that praises someone or something highly.|The CEO delivered a panegyric at the company's anniversary celebration.|Synonyms: eulogy, encomium, tribute, accolade|Antonyms: diatribe, denunciation, criticism|noun

panegyric:/ˌpænɪˈdʒɪrɪk/| adj.|颂扬的，赞美的|Expressing high praise.|He wrote a panegyric poem for the retiring professor.|Synonyms: laudatory, eulogistic, complimentary|Antonyms: critical, derogatory, defamatory|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *panegyricus, from Greek panēgyrikos (of or for a festival assembly), from pan- (all) + agyris (assembly).
