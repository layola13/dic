in-the-short-run:/ɪn ðə ʃɔːrt rʌn/ |adv.| 短期内 | In economics, "in the short run" refers to a period of time where at least one factor of production is fixed, usually capital. This means businesses can adjust their output by changing variable inputs like labor, but not by increasing their factory size or buying new equipment. | The price increase might be beneficial in the short run, but detrimental in the long run. 短期来看，价格上涨可能是有益的，但从长远来看是有害的。|  The company decided to cut costs in the short run to improve profitability.  为了提高盈利能力，公司决定在短期内削减成本。 | synonyms: short-term, near-term | antonyms: in-the-long-run, long-term | adverb
