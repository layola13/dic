obtrusion:/əbˈtruːʒən/ | n. | 突出；强加；闯入 |  The act of obtruding or the state of being obtruded; an unwelcome thrusting of oneself or one's opinions upon others. |  His constant questions were an obtrusion on our conversation. 他不停地提问，打扰了我们的谈话。 |  intrusion, imposition, interference | discretion, tact | noun

obtrude:/əbˈtruːd/ | v. |  突出；强加；闯入 | To thrust (something) forward or upon a person, especially without warrant or invitation; to intrude or impose (oneself) upon another. | He obtruded his opinions upon everyone he met.  他把自己的观点强加于他遇到的每一个人。| impose, inflict, intrude |  withdraw, recede | verb
