flamenco: /fləˈmɛŋkoʊ/ (US) /fləˈmɛŋkəʊ/ (UK) | n. adj. |  弗拉门戈舞；弗拉门戈风格的 | A style of Spanish music and dance, characterized by vigorous strumming, singing, and dancing. |  Example: She's a passionate flamenco dancer. 她是一位充满激情的弗拉门戈舞者。| Example: The guitarist played a lively flamenco piece.  吉他手演奏了一首活泼的弗拉门戈曲子。| Synonym:  fandango (similar Spanish dance style) | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but could be contrasted with other dance genres like ballet or waltz) | Noun, Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
The precise origin of the word "flamenco" is uncertain, with various theories proposed. Some believe it may derive from the Spanish word "flamenco" meaning "Flemish," possibly referring to Flemish immigrants in Spain. Others suggest a connection to the word "flama" meaning "flame," alluding to the passionate nature of the dance. Still others propose a link to the Romani people, historically associated with flamenco. The word's etymology remains a topic of debate.
