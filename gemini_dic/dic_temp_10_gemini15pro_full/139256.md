obligednesses:/əˈblaɪdʒɪdnɪsɪz/
n.
义务；恩惠；责任
The state of being obliged or indebted to someone.  A feeling of gratitude and a sense of duty to repay a kindness.
I felt a deep sense of obligedness to my parents for their support. 我对父母的支持深感感激和责任。
She felt no obligedness to help him after the way he had treated her. 在他那样对待她之后，她觉得没有义务帮助他。
近义词: obligations, duties, responsibilities, debts
反义词: ingratitude, disregard, indifference
词性: noun (plural)


obligedness:/əˈblaɪdʒɪdnɪs/
n.
义务；恩惠；责任
The state of being obliged or indebted to someone.  A feeling of gratitude and a sense of duty to repay a kindness.
He felt an obligedness to help her, after she had helped him so much. 在她帮了他这么多之后，他感到有义务帮助她.
I repaid his kindness out of a sense of obligedness. 出于感激之情，我报答了他的好意。
近义词: obligation, duty, responsibility, debt
反义词: ingratitude, disregard, indifference
词性: noun (singular)



词源: From "obliged" + "-ness." "Obliged" comes from the Old French "obligier,"  meaning "to bind, compel," ultimately from the Latin "obligare,"  meaning "to bind, tie up."
