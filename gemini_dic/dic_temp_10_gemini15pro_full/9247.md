antipathists:/ænˈtɪpəθɪsts/
|n.| 反感者；厌恶者；敌视者|People who feel strong antipathy or aversion towards something or someone.|The antipathists of the new policy voiced their concerns loudly. 新政策的反对者大声表达了他们的担忧。|
|近义词：opponents, adversaries, foes, enemies|反义词：supporters, proponents, advocates, allies|词性: noun (plural)|

antipathy:/ænˈtɪpəθi/
|n.| 反感；厌恶；憎恶|A deep-seated feeling of aversion or dislike.|She felt a strong antipathy towards her boss. 她对她的老板非常反感。|
|近义词：aversion, dislike, hostility, animosity|反义词：affection, fondness, liking, sympathy|词性: noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *antipathie*, from Late Latin *antipathia*, from Ancient Greek *ἀντιπάθεια (antipátheia)*, from *ἀντί (antí, “against, opposite”) + πάθος (páthos, “feeling, suffering, experience”)*.
