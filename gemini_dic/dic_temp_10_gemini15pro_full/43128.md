coyly:/ˈkɔɪli/ | adv. | 害羞地，腼腆地，忸怩作态地 | In a shy or flirtatious manner, often pretending to be more innocent or naive than one really is. | She smiled coyly and looked down at her feet. 她害羞地笑了笑，低头看着自己的脚。|例句：He coyly suggested that he might be the best candidate for the job. 他腼腆地暗示，他可能是这项工作的最佳人选。| 近义词: shyly, demurely, bashfully, flirtatiously | 反义词: boldly, brazenly, forwardly | 词性: 副词

词源:
From coy + -ly.  "Coy" itself comes from Middle English *coi, from Old French coi, quieted, calm, from Latin quietus, quiet.
