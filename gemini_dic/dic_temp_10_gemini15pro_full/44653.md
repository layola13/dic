croziered:/ˈkroʊʒɪərd/| adj.| 持牧杖的，主教的 |Carrying a crozier, shaped like a crozier; symbolic of a bishop or episcopal authority. |The archbishop, croziered and mitered, led the procession.  大主教手持牧杖，头戴主教冠，引领着队伍。|The stained-glass window depicted a croziered bishop. 彩色玻璃窗描绘了一位手持牧杖的主教。|近义词：episcopal, pontifical |反义词:lay, secular|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From crozier + -ed.  Crozier comes from Old French *crocier, from Medieval Latin crocia, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Old High German krukke "crutch."
