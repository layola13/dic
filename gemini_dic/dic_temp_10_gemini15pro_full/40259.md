contingency:/kənˈtɪndʒənsi/| n.| 偶然性，意外事件，可能性；意外事故；[军] 临时补给品 |A contingency is a future event or circumstance which is possible but cannot be predicted with certainty.|We must consider all possible contingencies. 我们必须考虑所有可能发生的意外事件。|Synonyms: eventuality, incident, possibility, emergency, accident|Antonyms: certainty, predictability|noun

contingent:/kənˈtɪndʒənt/| adj.| 依情况而定的；偶然发生的；可能的；有条件的| Contingent means subject to chance or unseen circumstances.| Our plans are contingent on the weather. 我们的计划取决于天气。|Synonyms: conditional, dependent, subject to, provisional|Antonyms: certain, definite, independent, unconditional|adjective

contingent:/kənˈtɪndʒənt/| n.| (代表某一组织或国家的)代表团；(军队的)分遣队| A contingent is a group of people united by some common feature, forming part of a larger group.|The university sent a large contingent to the conference. 大学派出了一个庞大的代表团参加会议。|Synonyms: group, delegation, detachment, squad|Antonyms: individual|noun
