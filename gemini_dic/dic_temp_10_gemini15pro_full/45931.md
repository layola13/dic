cuttlefish:/ˈkʌtəlˌfɪʃ/| n. |乌贼，墨鱼|A marine mollusk with eight arms and two tentacles, typically able to change color.  It releases an inky fluid when threatened.|The cuttlefish quickly camouflaged itself against the coral.  乌贼迅速地把自己伪装成珊瑚的样子。|The diver watched the cuttlefish change color. 潜水员看着乌贼改变颜色。|Synonyms: sepia, squid|Antonyms: None|Noun


cuttlebone:/ˈkʌtəlˌboʊn/| n. |乌贼骨，墨鱼骨|The internal shell of a cuttlefish, composed of calcium carbonate. It is often used as a dietary supplement for birds.|The pet store sells cuttlebone for birds.  宠物店出售鸟类的乌贼骨。|I gave my parrot a piece of cuttlebone. 我给我的鹦鹉一块乌贼骨。|Synonyms: None|Antonyms: None|Noun


cuttle:/ˈkʌtəl/| n. |乌贼，墨鱼 (This usage is less common than "cuttlefish")|A cuttlefish.|The cuttle darted away, leaving a cloud of ink. 乌贼迅速游开，留下了一团墨汁。 (Less common usage)|He caught a cuttle in his fishing net. 他在渔网里捕到了一只乌贼。(Less common usage)|Synonyms: sepia, squid|Antonyms: None|Noun
