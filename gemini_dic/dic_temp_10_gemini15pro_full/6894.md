anchoretish: /ˈæŋkəˌrɛtɪʃ/ | adj. | 隐士般的，遁世的 | Like an anchorite; characterized by seclusion or solitude. |  He led an anchoretish existence, far from the bustle of the city. 他过着隐居的生活，远离城市的喧嚣。|  The anchoretish writer rarely left his cabin in the woods.  这位隐居的作家很少离开他在树林里的小屋。 | Near synonyms:  hermitic, recluse, solitary, ascetic | Antonyms:  gregarious, sociable, outgoing | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *ancretisch, from Old English ancra "anchorite, hermit," + -ish.  Ultimately from Late Latin *anachorēta, from Greek anachōrētēs "one who has retired, recluse," from ana- "back, away" + chōrein "to withdraw, give place."
