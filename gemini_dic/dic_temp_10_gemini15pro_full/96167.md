independents:/ˌɪndɪˈpendənts/
n.|独立人士，无党派人士|People who are not affiliated with any political party are called independents.  没有加入任何政党的被称为无党派人士。|Many independents voted in the last election. 许多无党派人士在上次选举中投票。|近义词：nonpartisans, freelancers (if referring to work)|反义词：partisans, members|词性:名词

independent:/ˌɪndɪˈpendənt/
adj.|独立的，自主的|An independent person can take care of themselves. 一个独立的人可以照顾自己。|She's very independent and likes to travel alone. 她非常独立，喜欢独自旅行。|近义词：autonomous, self-reliant, free|反义词：dependent, reliant|词性:形容词

independent of:  独立于；不受…支配
The judiciary is independent of the government.  司法机关独立于政府。

Many former colonies became independent from European powers. 许多前殖民地脱离欧洲列强而独立。

declare independent: 宣布独立
The colonies declared independent from British rule. 殖民地宣布脱离英国统治而独立。

financially independent: 经济独立
She became financially independent after starting her own business.  她创办了自己的企业后经济独立了。

independent thinking: 独立思考
Independent thinking is crucial for innovation. 独立思考对于创新至关重要。
