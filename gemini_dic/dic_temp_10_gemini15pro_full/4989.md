alkarsin: /ælˈkɑːrsɪn/
noun
烷基胂
A highly toxic and volatile liquid, (CH3)2As-As(CH3)2, formerly used as a source of arsenic in medicine.
Alkarsin was one of the first organometallic compounds to be discovered.  It emits a highly unpleasant garlicky odor.
近义词: cacodyl, tetramethyldiarsine
反义词:  (No direct antonym for a specific chemical compound)
词性: n.

Etymology:
From German *Alkarsin*, coined by Robert Bunsen from alcohol + arsenic.
