corposant: /kɔːrˈpəzənt/ /ˈkɔrpəˌzɑːnt/ | n. | 圣艾尔摩之火（航海传说中，暴风雨时出现在船桅杆顶端的淡蓝色火焰） | A luminous discharge of electricity appearing as a bluish flame on the extremities of masts and yards of ships, aircraft, or other pointed objects in stormy weather.  It is also known as St. Elmo's fire. | Example 1: Sailors used to consider the appearance of a corposant a good omen. 水手们过去常常认为圣艾尔摩之火的出现是好兆头。

Example 2: The corposant flickered eerily on the wing tip of the plane. 圣艾尔摩之火在飞机的翼尖上闪烁着诡异的光芒。 | Near synonyms: St. Elmo's fire, electric discharge, glow | Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but could be considered the opposite of darkness) light | Noun
