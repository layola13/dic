headier:/ˈhɛdiər/| adj.|更令人兴奋的；更令人陶醉的；更强烈的；更轻率的|Making you feel excited and slightly out of control, especially because of drinking alcohol or success; stronger or more intense than usual; tending to act without thinking carefully|The champagne made her feel headier than usual.  香槟让她比平时更兴奋。|Synonyms: intoxicating, exhilarating, potent, impetuous|Antonyms: sobering, calming, mild, cautious|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From head + -y + -er (comparative suffix).  The original sense referred to affecting the head, causing dizziness or lightheadedness.  The meaning evolved to include the intoxicating effects of alcohol and, by extension, other exhilarating experiences.
