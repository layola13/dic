grill:/ɡrɪl/| n. v.|烧烤架，烤架；烤肉，烧烤|A framework of metal bars used for cooking food over an open fire.  To cook food over direct heat, as on a grill.|We cooked the steaks on the grill. 我们在烤架上烤了牛排。|Synonyms: barbecue, broil, charbroil|Antonyms: boil, stew|noun
grill:/ɡrɪl/| v.|烧烤，炙烤；拷问，盘问|To cook food over direct heat, as on a grill. To subject to intense questioning or interrogation.|The police grilled the suspect for hours. 警方盘问了嫌疑人几个小时。|Synonyms: barbecue, broil, charbroil, interrogate, question|Antonyms: boil, stew|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "greille" (grid, grating), from Medieval Latin "gratila" (gridiron), ultimately from Latin "cratis" (wickerwork, hurdle).
