deconcoct:/ˌdiːkənˈkɒkt/ | v. | 编造，捏造，虚构；调制，配制 | To invent or devise (a story, plan, etc.), especially fraudulently; to prepare by combining ingredients, especially in cookery. |  He deconcocted a story about being robbed. 他编造了一个关于被抢劫的故事。 |  She deconcocted a delicious soup from leftover vegetables. 她用剩菜调制了一碗美味的汤。 | synonyms: fabricate, invent, devise, concoct, formulate | antonyms: discover, reveal, expose | verb

deconcoction:/ˌdiːkənˈkɒkʃən/ | n. | 编造的故事，虚构的事物；调制品，混合物 | A story, plan, etc. that has been invented or devised, especially fraudulently; something prepared by combining ingredients, especially a medicinal or magical potion. | The whole alibi was a deconcoction. 整个不在场证明都是编造的。 |  He gave me a strange deconcoction to drink. 他给了我一种奇怪的混合物让我喝。 | synonyms: fabrication, invention, concoction, mixture | antonyms: reality, truth | noun
