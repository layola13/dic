hecaton:/ˈhɛkətɒn/ (US) /ˈhɛkətən/ (UK)| n.|百, 一百个, 百人队|A group of one hundred; specifically, in ancient Greece, a division of the Spartan army, consisting of one hundred men.  It derives from the Greek word "ἑκατόν" (hekaton), meaning "hundred".|The Spartan army was divided into hecatons. 斯巴达军队被分成百人队。| The general addressed the hecaton. 将军向百人队发表讲话。 |synonyms: century, hundred |antonyms:  N/A (There isn't a direct antonym for a specific number.) | noun
