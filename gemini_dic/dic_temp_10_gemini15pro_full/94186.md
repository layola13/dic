imperativist:/ɪmˈpɛrətɪvɪst/
n.|命令主义者；绝对命令论者|Someone who believes in or follows imperativism, a philosophical theory emphasizing the importance of moral commands or duties.  This theory often focuses on the inherent rightness or wrongness of actions, regardless of their consequences.  In linguistics, it refers to someone who studies or focuses on the imperative mood.| Kant was a prominent imperativist. 康德是一位著名的命令主义者。|synonyms:  moralist, deontologist|antonyms: consequentialist, utilitarian|noun
