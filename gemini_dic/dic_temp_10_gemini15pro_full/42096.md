cotransport:/ˈkoʊˌtrænsˌpɔːrt/ | n. |协同转运，共转运| The simultaneous transport of two substances across a cell membrane by a single transporter.  One substance moves down its concentration gradient, providing the energy to move the other substance against its concentration gradient. | Example: Glucose cotransport with sodium ions allows for efficient absorption of glucose in the intestines. 葡萄糖与钠离子的协同转运使得肠道能够有效地吸收葡萄糖。

Example:  The cotransport system helps maintain the balance of ions within the cell. 协同转运系统有助于维持细胞内离子的平衡。 | Synonym: coupled transport, symport (when both substances move in the same direction), antiport (when substances move in opposite directions)| Antonym: uniport (transport of a single substance) | noun
