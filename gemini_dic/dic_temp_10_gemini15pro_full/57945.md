drip:/drɪp/ | n. v. | 水滴；滴落；点滴；静脉滴注 | a small drop of liquid that falls or leaks slowly | The tap was dripping. 水龙头在滴水。 | synonyms: trickle, dribble, drop | antonyms: gush, pour, flood | noun

drip:/drɪp/ | v. | 滴下；漏下；充满；浸透 | to fall or let fall small drops of liquid | Water dripped from the ceiling. 水从天花板上滴下来。 | synonyms: trickle, dribble, ooze | antonyms: soak up, absorb | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dryppan, from Proto-Germanic *druppaną (“to drip”), from Proto-Indo-European *dhrebh- (“to make muddy, disturb”). Cognate with Dutch druppen (“to drip”), German tropfen (“to drip”), Icelandic dryppa (“to drip”).
