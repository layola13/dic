词源:

non- (prefix):  Meaning "not," from Old French non-, from Latin non "not," related to nec, neque "and not, nor," from PIE root *ne- "not."
radical (adjective/noun): From Late Latin radicalis "of roots, pertaining to the root," from Latin radix (genitive radicis) "root." The political sense of "thoroughgoing, fundamental" is from 1802, originally especially of democratic reformers in England.
-ness (suffix): Forming nouns of quality or state, from Old English -nes, -nis, common Germanic suffix (cognates: Old Saxon, Old Frisian -nissi, Old Norse -neskja, Dutch -nis, German -nis, Gothic -inassus).
