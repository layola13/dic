freshwaters:/ˈfreʃˌwɔːtərz/ /ˈfreʃˌwɑːtərz/ | n. | 淡水水域；淡水生物 |  Areas of water that are not salty, such as lakes, rivers, and ponds; creatures living in such environments. | The freshwaters of the Great Lakes teem with life.  五大湖的淡水充满了生命。|  近义词: inland waters, sweet waters | 反义词: saltwaters, marine waters | 词性: 名词

freshwater:/ˈfreʃˌwɔːtər/ /ˈfreʃˌwɑːtərz/ | adj. | 淡水的 | Relating to, living in, or consisting of water that is not salty. |  This is a freshwater fish. 这是一条淡水鱼。 | 近义词: lacustrine, riverine | 反义词: saltwater, marine | 词性: 形容词


词源:  "freshwater" comes from the Old English "fersc water," literally meaning "not salty water." "fersc" (fresh) evolved from the Proto-Germanic "*friskaz" meaning "fresh, vigorous," and "water" from Proto-Germanic "*watar."  The plural form "freshwaters" simply adds the plural "-s" to denote multiple bodies of freshwater.
