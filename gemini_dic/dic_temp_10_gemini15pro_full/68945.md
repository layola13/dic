fathom:/ˈfæðəm/| n. v. | 英寻（长度单位，等于6英尺或1.83米），理解，领会 |A fathom is a unit of length equal to six feet (1.83 meters), chiefly used in reference to the depth of water.  To fathom something is to understand it thoroughly.|The ship sank in over thirty fathoms of water. 这艘船沉没在三十多英寻深的水中。|近义词：understand, comprehend, grasp, gauge|反义词：misunderstand, misinterpret|词性:名词, 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English fæðm "outstretched arms, embrace," also a measure of length, from Proto-Germanic *fathmaz (source also of Old Norse faðmr, Old Frisian fethm, Dutch vadem, German Faden "fathom, thread"), from PIE root *pet- "to spread, stretch." The verb meaning "to measure the depth of (water)" is from late 14c.; the figurative sense of "to penetrate to the truth of, comprehend" is from c. 1600.
