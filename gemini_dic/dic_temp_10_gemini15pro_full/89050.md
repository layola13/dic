holograph:/ˈhɒləɡrɑːf/ (US) /ˈhɒləɡræf/ (UK)| n. adj.|全息图；亲笔信；亲笔文件|A document written entirely in the handwriting of the person whose signature it bears.  A three-dimensional image formed by the interference of light beams from a laser or other coherent light source.|例句：He left a holograph will. 他留下了一份亲笔遗嘱。

例句：The museum displayed a holograph of a famous inventor.  博物馆展出了一个著名发明家的全息图。|近义词：autograph, manuscript, hologram (for the image sense)|反义词: copy, forgery|词性:noun, adjective
