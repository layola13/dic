ETYMOLOGY->
From "dug out," past participle of "dig out," meaning to excavate or hollow out.  The word's application to canoes reflects their construction by hollowing out logs.  Its use for military shelters and baseball benches stems from the idea of a space dug out of the ground or built into a slope.
