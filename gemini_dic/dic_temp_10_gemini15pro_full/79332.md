glebosity:/ɡliːˈbɒsɪti/
n.|油嘴滑舌，口若悬河|The quality of being glib or fluent, often insincerely so.  Smooth or fluent talk, often of little substance.|His glebosity made it difficult to trust him. 他的油嘴滑舌让人很难相信他。|近义词：fluency, volubility, loquacity|反义词：reticence, taciturnity|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *glebōsitās, from Latin glebōsus (“fertile, rich in soil”), from gleba (“clod, lump of earth”).  The semantic shift is from "rich soil" to "richness of speech," then to "superficial fluency."
