methodistically:/ˌmɛθəˈdɪstɪkli/
| adv. |有条理地，系统地|In a methodical and systematic manner; according to a fixed plan or system.|She methodistically cleaned the entire house, room by room. 她有条不紊地打扫了整栋房子，一间一间地打扫。|例句：He methodistically worked through the problem, step by step. 他有条不紊地逐步解决了这个问题。|近义词：systematically, orderly, precisely|反义词：haphazardly, randomly, carelessly|词性: 副词

methodical:/məˈθɒdɪk(ə)l/
| adj. |有条理的，系统的|Done according to a systematic or established procedure.|He has a very methodical approach to his work. 他工作很有条理。|例句：She is a methodical worker and always finishes her tasks efficiently. 她工作很有条理，总是高效地完成任务。|近义词：systematic, organized, orderly|反义词：disorganized, chaotic, haphazard|词性: 形容词

method:/ˈmɛθəd/
| n. |方法，方式|A particular form of procedure for accomplishing or approaching something, especially a systematic or established one.|The new teaching methods encourage student participation. 新的教学方法鼓励学生参与。|例句：There are several methods of solving this problem. 有几种方法可以解决这个问题。|近义词：way, technique, procedure|反义词：disorder, chaos|词性: 名词


词源：
From Late Latin methodicus "arranged in regular order," from Greek methodos "way of inquiry, method of investigation," from meta- "after, among, with" + hodos "way, road, journey."  The adverb "methodistically" is formed by adding the suffix "-ically" to the adjective "methodical."
