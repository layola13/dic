cherry-pick: /ˈtʃɛriˌpɪk/ | v. | 挑选；精选 | To select the best or most desirable items from a larger group, often ignoring the less desirable ones. |  He cherry-picked the best strawberries from the basket. 他从篮子里挑了最好的草莓。

cherry-pick: /ˈtʃɛriˌpɪk/ | v. | 择优挑选（尤指不公平地） | To select the most favorable data or items to support a particular point, often ignoring unfavorable data or items. |  The company was accused of cherry-picking data to support its claims.  该公司被指责择优挑选数据来支持其主张。


cherry-pick: /ˈtʃɛriˌpɪk/ | n. | 精选；择优挑选 | The act of selecting the best or most desirable items from a larger group. | The cherry-pick of the best athletes for the national team sparked controversy. 国家队对优秀运动员的择优挑选引发了争议。


近义词: select, handpick, cull, choose | 反义词: accept all, take everything | 词性: verb, noun


词源: From the literal act of picking cherries, where only the ripest and best are chosen.  源自字面上的摘樱桃的行为，只挑选最成熟和最好的樱桃。
