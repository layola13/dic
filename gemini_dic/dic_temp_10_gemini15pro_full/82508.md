gryphite:/ˈɡrɪfaɪt/ | n. | 钩菊石; 箭石 | A fossil of a gryphaea, an extinct oyster-like mollusk.  |  Example 1:  The paleontologist found a large gryphite embedded in the cliff face.  古生物学家在悬崖面上发现了一块巨大的钩菊石化石。

Example 2: Gryphites are common fossils in Jurassic and Cretaceous rocks. 钩菊石是侏罗纪和白垩纪岩石中常见的化石。 | Synonym:  Gryphaea (genus name), devil's toenail (informal) | Antonym:  N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Modern Latin *gryphites*, from Ancient Greek γρυπός (grupós, “curved, hooked”) + -ite (suffix indicating a fossil or rock).
