calamanco:/ˌkæləˈmæŋkoʊ/| n. |卡拉曼科，一种光滑的羊毛或羊毛和丝绸混纺织物|A glossy woolen or worsted and silk fabric, often glazed, plain, or patterned. It was popular in the 17th and 18th centuries.|She wore a dress of rich calamanco. 她穿着一件昂贵的卡拉曼科连衣裙。|Synonym:  worsted, serge, damask|Antonym: None (Specific fabric, no direct opposite)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *calamanque*, from Italian *calamanco*, perhaps from Catalan *calamany*, from Arabic *qalamani* 'from Kalamata' (Greece), referring to a type of fine linen.
