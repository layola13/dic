crystalloid:/ˈkrɪstəlɔɪd/| n. adj.|晶体；晶质|A substance that, when dissolved, forms a true solution and is able to pass through a semipermeable membrane.  A substance resembling a crystal.|Crystalloids are often used in intravenous fluids to replace lost fluids and electrolytes. 晶体通常用于静脉输液，以补充丢失的液体和电解质。| As an adjective: The rock had a crystalloid structure. 这块岩石具有晶体结构。|近义词: crystalline|反义词: colloid|词性: noun, adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From crystal + -oid.  "Crystal" comes from the Greek "krystallos," meaning "ice" or "clear ice," later used for quartz. "-oid" is a suffix meaning "resembling" or "having the form of."
