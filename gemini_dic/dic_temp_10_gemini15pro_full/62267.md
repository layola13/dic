encyclopaedically: /ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpiːdɪkəli/ /ɪnˌsaɪkloʊˈpiːdɪkəli/
| adv. | 百科全书式地，全面地 | In a way that resembles an encyclopedia; comprehensively, exhaustively. |  He described the event encyclopaedically, leaving out no detail. 他对这件事进行了百科全书式的描述，没有遗漏任何细节。| comprehensively, exhaustively, thoroughly | superficially, briefly, cursorily | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From encyclopedia + -ically.  Encyclopedia comes from  Late Latin encyclopaedia, from Ancient Greek ἐγκύκλιος παιδεία (enkýklios paideía, “general education”), from ἐν κύκλῳ (en kýklō, “in a circle”) + παιδεία (paideía, “education, rearing of a child”).
