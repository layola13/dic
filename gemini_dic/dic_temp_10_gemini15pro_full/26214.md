butterers:/ˈbʌtərərz/
n.
奉承者，拍马屁的人
People who flatter others excessively, especially to gain favor.
He's surrounded by a crowd of butterers who constantly praise his every move. 他身边围着一群奉承者，不断地赞扬他的一举一动。
Synonyms: flatterers, sycophants, yes-men
Antonyms: critics, dissenters

Etymology:
From the verb "butter up," meaning to flatter or coax someone.  "Butter" itself comes from Old English "butere," from Latin "butyrum," and ultimately from Greek "boutyron."
