minioning:/ˈmɪnjənɪŋ/
verb
充当爪牙；卑躬屈膝；奴颜婢膝
acting subserviently, like a minion
He was accused of minioning to the CEO to get a promotion. 他被指责为了升职而对CEO卑躬屈膝。
近义词:fawning, kowtowing, groveling, bootlicking
反义词: defying, resisting, challenging
词性:v.

词源: From "minion" (meaning a servile follower or subordinate) + "-ing" (present participle suffix).  "Minion" itself comes from Old French "mignon" meaning "darling" or "favorite."
