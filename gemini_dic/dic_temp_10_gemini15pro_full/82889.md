gulping:/ˈɡʌlpɪŋ/ | adj. n.| 吞咽的，大口地喝；吞咽，大口地喝 |  The act of swallowing something quickly or in large amounts, often audibly.  A gulping sound can indicate nervousness, thirst, or eagerness. | He was gulping down water after his run. 他跑完步后大口地喝水。 |  She took a few gulping breaths, trying to calm down. 她深吸了几口气，试图让自己平静下来。 |近义词：swallowing, chugging, guzzling | 反义词：sipping, nibbling | 词性: adjective, noun (present participle of *gulp*)

gulp:/ɡʌlp/ | v. n.|  吞咽，大口地喝；吞咽，一大口 | To swallow something quickly or in large amounts, often audibly.  A gulp can also refer to the amount swallowed at once. |  He gulped the last of his coffee and rushed out the door. 他喝完最后一口咖啡，冲出门外。 | She took a deep gulp of air before diving into the pool.  她在跳入泳池前深深地吸了一口气。 | 近义词：swallow, chug, guzzle | 反义词：sip, nibble | 词性: verb, noun
