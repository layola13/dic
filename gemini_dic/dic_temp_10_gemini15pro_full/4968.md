alkalimeter: /ˌælkəˈlɪmɪtər/ | n. | 碱度计，酸碱计 | An instrument used to measure the amount of alkali in a solution.  It is typically used to determine the strength of an alkaline solution by titration with a standard acid solution. |  The chemist used an alkalimeter to determine the concentration of the sodium hydroxide solution.  化学家使用碱度计测定氢氧化钠溶液的浓度。| synonyms: burette, titrator | antonyms: acidimeter | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From alkali + -meter. Alkali comes from Arabic *al-qalīy*, meaning "ashes of saltwort". -meter comes from Greek *métron*, meaning "measure".
