basilisk: /ˈbæsɪlɪsk/ | n. |  (带冠毛的)小蜥蜴 | A small crested lizard (genus *Basiliscus*) of tropical America, able to run across the surface of water. | The basilisk lizard can run on water for short distances.  蛇蜥可以在水面上短距离奔跑。 | synonyms:  Jesus Christ lizard,  plumed basilisk | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *basilisc*, from Latin *basiliscus*, from Greek *basilískos* meaning "little king",  from *basileús* meaning "king".  The name refers to the crown-like crest on the head of some species.
