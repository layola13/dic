adjunct:/ˈædʒʌŋkt/| n. adj.|附属物，附件；助手；修饰语|Something added to something else as a supplementary rather than an essential part.  An adjunct professor is one who is hired temporarily.|He works at the university as an adjunct professor. 他在大学里担任兼职教授。|The map is an adjunct to the travel guide. 这张地图是旅行指南的附件。|synonyms: addition, supplement, accessory, attachment|antonyms: essential, core, integral|noun

adjunct:/ˈædʒʌŋkt/| adj.|附属的，附加的|Added or connected in a subordinate capacity; attached to a faculty or staff in an auxiliary capacity.|He held an adjunct position at the university. 他在大学担任兼职职位。|She took an adjunct course over the summer.  她在夏天选修了一门附加课程。|synonyms: supplementary, auxiliary, additional, subordinate|antonyms: primary, main, principal|adjective
