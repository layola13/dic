glamorize: /ˈɡlæməraɪz/ (US) /ˈɡlæməraɪz/ (UK) | vt. | 使...更有魅力；美化；赞美；吹捧 | To make something seem more attractive or exciting than it really is, often by not showing the negative aspects. | The movie glamorized violence, making it seem exciting and heroic.  这部电影美化了暴力，使暴力看起来激动人心，像英雄一样。

The advertisement glamorizes smoking by associating it with wealth and success.  这则广告通过将吸烟与财富和成功联系起来，从而美化了吸烟。 | 近义词: romanticize, idealize, glorify | 反义词: vilify, denigrate, disparage | 词性: verb

Etymology:  From glamour (魅力) + -ize (动词后缀，表示“使成为…”). Glamour comes from grammar, via Scottish dialect, through the association of literacy and learning with magic and enchantment.
