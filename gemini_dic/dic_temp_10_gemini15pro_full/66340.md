exculpating: /ɪkˈskʌlpeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 免罪的，开脱的 | Showing that someone is not guilty of wrongdoing. | The lawyer presented exculpating evidence that proved his client's innocence. 律师出示了免罪的证据，证明了他的当事人的清白。 |  The newly discovered evidence was exculpatory in nature, clearing the suspect of all charges. 新发现的证据本质上是免罪的，洗清了嫌疑人的所有指控。 | Synonyms: absolving, clearing, vindicating | Antonyms: incriminating, accusing, condemning | adjective

exculpate: /ˈɛkskʌlˌpeɪt/ | v. |  开脱，使免罪 | To show or declare that someone is not guilty of wrongdoing. | The evidence exculpated the defendant from all charges. 证据使被告免于所有指控。 |  He tried to exculpate himself by blaming others. 他试图通过责怪别人来开脱自己。 | Synonyms: absolve, acquit, exonerate | Antonyms: incriminate, accuse, convict | verb
