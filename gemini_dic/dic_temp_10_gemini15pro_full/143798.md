词源:  "Out of office" is a simple compound phrase formed from the preposition "out of" and the noun "office."  "Out of" indicates a location outside or away from something, while "office" refers to a place of business or work.  The phrase gained prominence with the rise of email and the need to inform colleagues and clients of one's absence.
