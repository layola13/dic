contingently:/kənˈtɪndʒəntli/| adv.|偶然地，视情况而定地|depending on something else that might or might not happen|The trip is contingently scheduled for next week, depending on the weather.|Synonyms: conditionally, provisionally, subject to|Antonyms: absolutely, unconditionally, definitely|adverb

contingent:/kənˈtɪndʒənt/| adj. n.|偶然的，视情况而定的；小分队|
(adj.) occurring or existing only if (or unless) a particular circumstance or condition is the case;
(n.) a group of people united by some common feature, forming part of a larger group|
(adj.) Our success is contingent upon everyone's hard work. 我们的成功取决于每个人的努力。
(n.) The French contingent of the peacekeeping force arrived yesterday. 维和部队的法国分队昨天抵达。|
(adj.) Synonyms: conditional, dependent, provisional|Antonyms: independent, absolute, certain|
(n.) Synonyms: detachment, group, squad|Antonyms: whole, entirety|adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contingentia, from contingere "to touch," from com- "together" + tangere "to touch."
