outbrazen: /ˌaʊtˈbreɪzən/
verb
厚颜无耻地胜过; 比…更厚颜无耻
surpass in boldness or impudence
He outbrazened his rivals and secured the deal. 他比他的对手更厚颜无耻，从而 secured the deal.

近义词: outface,  surpass,  exceed
反义词: cower,  recoil,  be intimidated
词性: v.


ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + brazen.  Out- acts as an intensifier, meaning "to surpass or exceed." Brazen comes from the Old English "bræs" meaning brass, and later evolved to mean "made of brass," and then figuratively to "shameless" or "impudent," likely due to the bold, bright nature of brass.
