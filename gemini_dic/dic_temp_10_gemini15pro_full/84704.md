handicapped: /ˈhændikæpt/ (US) /ˈhændikæpt/ (UK)| n.|残疾人|A person with a physical or mental disability.|例句：Facilities for the handicapped are improving.  残疾人设施正在改善。|近义词：disabled person, person with a disability|反义词：able-bodied person|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From hand in cap, an old game where players drew lots from a cap to determine the terms of a wager.  Originally referred to a disadvantage or handicap imposed on a superior contestant to make the competition fairer. Over time, it came to refer to a physical or mental disability.

注意事项:

*  The term "handicapped" is now considered outdated and potentially offensive by some.  "Disabled" or "person with a disability" are generally preferred.  When referring to specific disabilities, use precise language such as "person with a visual impairment" or "person with a learning disability."
