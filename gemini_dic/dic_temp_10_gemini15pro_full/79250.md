glassful:/ˈɡlæs.fʊl/ (US), /ˈɡlɑːs.fʊl/ (UK)| n. |一满杯|The quantity that fills a glass. |She drank a glassful of water. 她喝了一满杯水。|I poured myself a glassful of juice. 我给自己倒了一满杯果汁。|近义词：cupful, glass|反义词:sip, drop|词性:名词

Etymology:
From "glass" + "-ful" (suffix indicating fullness).  "Glass" comes from Old English "glæs," ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*glasam," meaning "amber, shining stone."  The suffix "-ful" comes from Old English "-full," meaning "full."
