diglossia:/daɪˈɡlɒsiə/ (US) /daɪˈɡlɒsɪə/ (UK)| n.|双语制，双言制|A situation in which two distinct varieties of a language are used in a community, one considered 'high' (formal) and the other 'low' (informal).|Example 1: Diglossia is common in many Arabic-speaking countries, where Classical Arabic is used for formal writing and a local dialect for everyday speech.  在许多阿拉伯语国家，双语制很常见，古典阿拉伯语用于正式写作，而地方方言用于日常对话。

Example 2: The diglossia in this region involves using the standard language in education and the vernacular at home.  该地区的双语制涉及在教育中使用标准语言，在家中使用方言。|近义词:bilingualism, multilingualism|反义词:monolingualism|词性:noun
