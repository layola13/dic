inoculate: /ɪˈnɒkjʊleɪt/ /ɪˈnɑːkjuleɪt/
verb
接种，灌输
introduce (an idea or habit) into the mind of; vaccinate against a disease with a modified or attenuated pathogen
例句：The doctor inoculated the children against measles. 医生给孩子们接种了麻疹疫苗。

例句：The teacher inoculated her students with a love of learning. 老师向学生们灌输了对学习的热爱。

近义词：vaccinate, immunize, inject, introduce, instill
反义词：infect, contaminate
词性：v.


inoculates: /ɪˈnɒkjʊleɪts/ /ɪˈnɑːkjuleɪts/
verb
接种，灌输
Third-person singular present tense form of "inoculate"
例句：He inoculates the patients with a new vaccine. 他给病人接种了新疫苗。

例句：The program inoculates young people with important life skills. 这个项目向年轻人灌输重要的生活技能。

近义词：vaccinates, immunizes, injects, introduces, instills
反义词：infects, contaminates
词性：v.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin inoculatus, past participle of inoculare "to engraft, implant," from in- "in" + oculus "eye, bud" (see eye (n.)).  Originally of grafting buds onto trees; extended sense of "communicate a disease by inserting infected matter" is from 1706. Figurative sense is from 1766.
