palliative: /ˈpæliətɪv/ | adj. n. | 缓和的，治标的；缓和剂，姑息疗法 | relieving pain without dealing with the cause of the condition |  The doctor prescribed palliative care for the terminally ill patient.  医生为这位晚期病人开了姑息治疗。 | synonyms: soothing, alleviating, mitigating, remedial | antonyms: aggravating, exacerbating | adjective

palliative: /ˈpæliətɪv/ | n. | 缓和剂，治标的措施 | something that makes a problem seem less serious but does not solve it |  The government offered tax cuts as a palliative for the economic recession. 政府提供减税作为缓解经济衰退的措施。 | synonyms: remedy, relief, solace | antonyms: aggravation, exacerbation | noun


Etymology: From Late Latin *palliativus, from Latin palliatus (“cloaked, covered”), past participle of palliare (“to cloak”), from pallium (“cloak”).
