moneywort:/ˈmʌniwɜːrt (US), ˈmʌniwɜːt (UK)| n. |金钱薄荷，过路黄|A creeping perennial plant (Lysimachia nummularia) with rounded leaves and cup-shaped yellow flowers, often grown as a ground cover.  It is also known as creeping Jenny. |例句：The moneywort spread quickly across the garden. 金钱薄荷很快蔓延到整个花园。

例句：Moneywort is a popular choice for hanging baskets. 金钱薄荷是吊篮的热门选择。|近义词：creeping Jenny, herb twopence, twopenny grass|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *monewort, from Old English *mynetwyrt, from mynet (“mint; coin”) + wyrt (“herb, plant”).  The name likely refers to the rounded, coin-like shape of the leaves.
