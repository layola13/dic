likelier:/ˈlaɪkliər/
adjective
更可能的
more likely; more probable
It is likelier that he will arrive tomorrow than today. 他明天比今天来的可能性更大。
Rain is likelier in the afternoon. 下午更有可能下雨。
近义词:more probable, more likely
反义词:less likely, less probable
词性:adjective

词源:
From Middle English *liklier, comparative of likely (“probable”), from Old Norse líkligr (“likely”), from líkr (“like”) + -ligr (suffix forming adjectives). Equivalent to like +‎ -lier.

PHRASE->
(Likelier doesn't typically form set phrases.  It's grammatically possible, but not common usage. Here are some examples showing how it *could* be used, but more natural alternatives):

1.  Instead of: "What's the likelier outcome?"  Use: "What's the most likely outcome?"
2.  Instead of:  "Which option is likelier to succeed?"  Use: "Which option is more likely to succeed?" or "Which option has a better chance of succeeding?"
3.  Instead of: "He's likelier to agree than disagree." Use: "He's more likely to agree than disagree."  or "He's more inclined to agree."
4. Instead of: "Is it likelier to rain today?" Use: "Is it more likely to rain today?" or "Is there a high chance of rain today?"
5. Instead of: "A likelier scenario is..."  Use: "A more likely scenario is..." or "A more probable scenario is..."
