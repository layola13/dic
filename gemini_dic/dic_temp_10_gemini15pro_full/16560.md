barnabite:/ˈbɑːrnəˌbaɪt/| n.| 巴纳比特会修士；巴纳比特会修女|A member of a Roman Catholic religious order, the Clerks Regular of St. Paul, founded in 1530 and devoted to missionary and educational work. |例句：The barnabite missionaries worked tirelessly in India. 巴纳比特会的传教士不知疲倦地在印度工作。

例句：She joined the order of the barnabites. 她加入了巴纳比特会。|近义词：cleric, religious, missionary|反义词: layman, secular|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *Barnabiti*, from *Sant'Ambrogio ad Nemus*, the name of their first church in Milan, dedicated to St. Ambrose. The church was located near a grove (*nemus* in Latin) of elm trees.
