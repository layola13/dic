coerced:/koʊˈɜːrst/ | adj. | 被胁迫的 | persuaded or compelled to do something by using force or threats |  He felt coerced into signing the confession. 他感觉被迫签署了供词。 | synonyms: forced, compelled, pressured, intimidated | antonyms: voluntary, willing, unforced | adjective

coerce:/koʊˈɜːrs/ | vt. | 胁迫，强迫 | persuade (an unwilling person) to do something by using force or threats | The government coerced him into spying on his friends. 政府胁迫他监视他的朋友。 | synonyms: force, compel, pressure, intimidate | antonyms: persuade, encourage | verb

Etymology:
From Latin *coarcere "to enclose, confine, restrain," from com- "together" (see com-) + arcere "to ward off, enclose."
