apartheid:/əˈpɑːrtaɪt/ |n.|种族隔离|A system of institutionalised racial segregation and discrimination that existed in South Africa and South West Africa (now Namibia) from 1948 until the early 1990s.|Nelson Mandela fought against apartheid in South Africa. 纳尔逊·曼德拉在南非与种族隔离作斗争。|Synonyms: segregation, discrimination, prejudice, racism|Antonyms: integration, equality, tolerance|Noun


apartheids:/əˈpɑːrtaɪts/ |n.|种族隔离（复数）|Plural form of "apartheid," referring to multiple instances or systems of racial segregation.|While apartheid is most commonly associated with South Africa, similar systems of apartheids have existed in other parts of the world. 虽然种族隔离通常与南非联系在一起，但世界其他地区也存在类似的种族隔离制度。|Synonyms: segregations, discriminations (less common in plural form)|Antonyms: integrations, equalities (less common in plural form)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Afrikaans *apartheid* meaning "separateness," from *apart* "separate" (from Dutch *apart*) + *-heid* (equivalent to English *-hood*).
