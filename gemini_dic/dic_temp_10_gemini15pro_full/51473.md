detour:/ˈdiːtʊr/ (US) /ˈdiːtʊə(r)/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.|绕道，绕行；迂回；改道|A deviation from a direct course or the usual procedure.|例句：The road was closed, so we had to take a detour.  这条路封闭了，所以我们不得不绕道而行。

例句：Heavy snow forced them to make a detour. 大雪迫使他们绕道而行。|近义词：bypass, diversion, indirect route|反义词：direct route, straight path|词性:名词, 动词


detours:/ˈdiːtʊrz/ (US) /ˈdiːtʊəz/ (UK)| n. v.|绕道，绕行；迂回；改道（detour的复数形式）|Plural form of detour, meaning deviations from a direct course or the usual procedure.|例句：Road closures caused several detours. 道路封闭导致了几个绕行路线。

例句：We encountered numerous detours on our journey.  我们在旅途中遇到了许多绕行路线。|近义词：bypasses, diversions, indirect routes|反义词：direct routes, straight paths|词性:名词, 动词

词源:  From French détour, from détourner "to turn aside," from de- "away" + tourner "to turn."
