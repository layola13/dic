Etymology:
From faulty + -ness.  "Faulty" comes from the Old French "faultif," meaning "guilty," "culpable," which in turn derives from the Latin "fallere," meaning "to deceive," "to fail," or "to err." The suffix "-ness" is a common English suffix used to form nouns expressing a state or quality.
