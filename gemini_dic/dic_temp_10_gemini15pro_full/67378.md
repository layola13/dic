extenuating:/ɪkˈstɛnjuˌeɪtɪŋ/| adj.|情有可原的，可减轻的| lessening the seriousness of something by providing partial excuses |The court reduced his sentence because of extenuating circumstances. 法院因情有可原而减轻了他的刑罚。|近义词：mitigating,  palliating, excusing|反义词：aggravating, incriminating|词性:形容词
词源: Late Middle English: from Latin extenuat- ‘made thin, weakened’, from the verb extenuare, from ex- ‘out, from’ + tenuare ‘make thin’, from tenuis ‘thin’.
