bailiff: /ˈbeɪlɪf/ | n. | 法警；执行官；地主代理人 | An officer of the court who is employed to execute writs and processes and make arrests, and who may perform other duties such as keeping order in the court.  A steward or manager of an estate, especially one who collects rents and supervises tenants. | The bailiff seized the debtor's property. 法警扣押了债务人的财产。

The bailiff evicted the tenants for non-payment of rent. 地主代理人因租户未付租金而将其驱逐。 |  Synonyms: sheriff, officer, marshal, agent, steward  | Antonyms: tenant, renter | noun


bumbailiff: /ˈbʌmbˌbeɪlɪf/ | n. | 催债人；执达吏 | A bailiff, especially one employed to collect debts.  This term is often considered derogatory. | The bumbailiff came knocking at the door, demanding payment. 催债人敲门要求付款。

He dreaded the visit of the bumbailiff. 他很害怕催债人上门。| Synonyms: debt collector, process server | Antonyms: debtor | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"bumbailiff" comes from "bum" (meaning "worthless" or "undesirable") + "bailiff."  The term "bailiff" ultimately derives from Old French "baillif," meaning "administrator," from Late Latin "bajulivus," from "bajulus," meaning "porter," from Greek "βασιλεύς" (basileus), meaning "king."
