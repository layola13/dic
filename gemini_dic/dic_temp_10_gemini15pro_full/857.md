abstractively:/æbˈstræktɪvli/
adv.
抽象地
in a way that expresses only the essential qualities or characteristics of something, without including concrete details or specific examples.
He thought abstractively about the problem, focusing on the underlying principles. 他抽象地思考这个问题，专注于其基本原则。

She described the painting abstractively, talking about color and form rather than the specific subject matter. 她抽象地描述了这幅画，谈论的是色彩和形式，而不是具体的主题。

近义词:theoretically, conceptually, ideally
反义词:concretely, specifically, practically
词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From abstract (adjective) + -ly (adverb suffix).  "Abstract" comes from the Latin "abstractus," the past participle of "abstrahere," meaning "to draw away" or "to separate."
