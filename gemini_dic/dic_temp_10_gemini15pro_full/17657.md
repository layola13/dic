beckon:/ˈbɛkən/ (US), /ˈbɛkən/ (UK)| v. n.|招手，示意；吸引，诱惑|To make a gesture with the hand, arm, or head to encourage someone to come nearer or follow. Also, to seem to attract or invite.|
He beckoned me over with a smile. 他笑着向我招手。

The flashing lights of the city beckoned her.  都市闪烁的灯光吸引着她。

|近义词：signal, gesture, summon, attract, lure, entice|反义词：repel, dismiss, deter|词性: verb, noun

beckon:/ˈbɛkən/ (US), /ˈbɛkən/ (UK)| n.|招手，示意|A gesture used to summon someone.|
With a beckon, he called the waiter over. 他招手叫服务员过来。

|近义词：gesture, signal, summons|反义词：dismissal, repulsion|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *biecnan "to make a sign, indicate," from *beacen "sign, beacon," related to bēacen "beacon," from Proto-Germanic *baukn- "beacon, sign."
