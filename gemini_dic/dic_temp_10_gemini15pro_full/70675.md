finagling:/fɪˈneɪɡlɪŋ/
finagle:/fɪˈneɪɡl/ | v. | 欺骗；哄骗；用计谋获得 | to obtain something by indirect or involved manipulation | He finagled a free ticket to the concert. 他骗到了一张免费的音乐会门票。 | synonyms: wangle, manipulate, scheme, contrive | antonyms: earn, deserve | verb
finagling: /fɪˈneɪɡlɪŋ/ | n. | 欺骗；哄骗；用计谋获得（的行为）| The act of obtaining something by indirect or involved manipulation |  The whole deal felt like a finagling operation.  整个交易感觉像是一场骗局。 | synonyms: manipulation, scheming, maneuvering | antonyms: honesty, forthrightness | noun

词源：
The origin of "finagle" is uncertain, but it likely emerged in American English in the mid-19th century. Some speculate a connection to German "fainaigue," meaning "to evade," or Yiddish "faynikl," referring to someone clever or tricky.  However, these connections are not definitively proven.
