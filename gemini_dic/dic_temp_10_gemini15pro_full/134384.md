nonintersectional:/ˌnɒnɪntərˈsɛkʃənəl/| adj.|非交叉的，非交织的，不考虑交叉性的|Not involving or relating to intersectionality; considering different aspects of social and political identities separately, without acknowledging their combined effects.|Her analysis of the issue was criticized for being nonintersectional, failing to consider the experiences of women of color.|近义词：single-axis, isolated, separate, fragmented|反义词：intersectional, holistic, integrated|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + intersectional.  "Intersectional" comes from "intersection," which traces back to the Latin "intersectio," from "intersecare" (to cut between, divide).  "Non-" is a prefix indicating negation or absence.
