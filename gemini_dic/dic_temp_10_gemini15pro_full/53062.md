diorama:/ˌdaɪəˈrɑːmə/ /ˌdaɪəˈræmə/| n. |透视模型，立体模型|A model representing a scene, often in miniature, with three-dimensional figures, either in a large case or as a small, portable object.| We visited the museum and saw a diorama of a prehistoric forest. 我们参观了博物馆，看到了一个史前森林的立体模型。| The children created a diorama of the solar system for their science project. 孩子们为他们的科学项目制作了一个太阳系的透视模型。|近义词：model, miniature, exhibit, display|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *diorama*, from Greek *dia* ("through") + *orama* ("that which is seen, a sight").  Coined in 1822 by Louis Daguerre and Charles Marie Bouton, inventors of the original diorama, a theatrical device.
