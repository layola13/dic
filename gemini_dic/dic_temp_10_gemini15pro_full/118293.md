mercurialist:/mərˈkjʊəriəlɪst/
noun| 性格反复无常的人；善变的人|A person whose moods or behavior change quickly and unpredictably.|He was a mercurialist, prone to fits of both elation and despair. 他是一个反复无常的人，容易一会儿兴高采烈，一会儿又陷入绝望。|Synonyms: fickle person, capricious person, erratic person, volatile person|Antonyms: steady person, stable person, consistent person|词性: 名词


mercurial:/mərˈkjʊəriəl/
adjective|反复无常的；变化莫测的|Characterized by rapid and unpredictable changeableness of mood or mind.|Her mercurial temperament made it difficult to predict her reactions. 她反复无常的性情使人难以预料她的反应。|Synonyms: fickle, capricious, erratic, volatile, temperamental|Antonyms: steady, stable, consistent, even-tempered|词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Mercurialis*, from Mercurius (Mercury), the Roman god of commerce, eloquence, messages/communication, travelers, trickery, and thieves.  Mercury was also associated with swiftness and changeability, hence the adjective's meaning.
