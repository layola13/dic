词源： "Harum-scarum" is of uncertain origin, but likely emerged in the 16th century.  It might be imitative, suggesting hurried and scattered movement, or possibly related to dialectal words implying disorder or confusion.  The suffix "-ness" transforms the adjective into a noun, signifying the state or quality of being harum-scarum.
