hogh:/hɒɡ/ | n. | 母猪;  阉割过的公猪 (主要用于苏格兰和英格兰北部)| A female pig, especially a breeding sow; a castrated male pig (chiefly Scotland and northern England). |例句：The farmer had a pen full of hoghs.  农夫有一圈母猪。

例句：The hogh was ready for market. 这头阉割过的公猪可以上市了。| 近义词：sow, pig, hog | 反义词: boar (指未阉割的公猪) | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Middle English *hog*, *hogge*, from Old English *hogg*, *hoh*.  Cognate with Scots *hog*, *hogg*. Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *huʒʒą (“young pig”)*. Related to Dutch *hoen* (“hen”), German *Huhn* (“hen”), and Old Norse *haukr* (“hawk”). The original meaning was likely a young animal, later specialized to pigs.
