gove: /ɡoʊv/
| n. | (古语，罕用) a ditch; a small stream | A narrow channel for water, especially one used to drain or irrigate land. | The farmer dug a gove to irrigate his crops. 农民挖了一条沟渠来灌溉他的庄稼。 | 近义词：ditch, channel, furrow, trench | 反义词: ridge, mound | 词性: 名词

gove: /ɡoʊv/
| v. | (古语，罕用) to dig or make a gove | To excavate a small channel or ditch.  | They goved a trench to divert the rainwater. 他们挖了一条沟渠来疏导雨水。 | 近义词：dig, excavate, channel | 反义词: fill, bury | 词性: 动词


词源:
From Middle English *gove, from Old English *gōfa (“a gove”), related to Old Norse gǫf (“sea”).  The exact etymology is uncertain.  This word is now largely obsolete and rarely encountered.
