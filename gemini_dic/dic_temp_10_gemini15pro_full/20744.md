blanch:/blɑːntʃ/ /blæntʃ/| v. | 焯水，使变白，使(脸色)变苍白 | To briefly immerse food, usually vegetables or nuts, in boiling water, then in cold water, to stop the cooking process, preserve color, or remove skins.  Also, to make something white or pale. | Example 1:  She blanched the almonds before slicing them. 她把杏仁焯水后再切片。

Example 2:  His face blanched when he heard the bad news. 听到坏消息后，他的脸色变得苍白。 | 近义词：scald, parboil, whiten, pale | 反义词：darken, color |词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *blanchir* "to whiten, make white," from *blanc* "white."  Ultimately from Frankish or other Germanic source related to Old High German *blank* "shining, white."
