ETYMOLOGY->
"Out of turn" is a phrase that combines the adverb "out" and the noun "turn."  "Out" indicates a state of being away from the usual or expected position or condition. "Turn" in this context refers to an allotted time or opportunity to do or say something, often implying a sequence or order.  The phrase literally means "outside of one's allotted time or opportunity."
