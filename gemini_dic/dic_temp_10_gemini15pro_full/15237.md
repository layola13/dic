backfiring:/ˌbækˈfaɪərɪŋ/ | n. |适得其反，事与愿违| (of a plan or action) having the opposite result to what was intended.|
The company's new marketing campaign backfired, resulting in a drop in sales.
这家公司的新营销活动适得其反，导致销量下降。

His attempt to impress the boss backfired when he accidentally deleted important files.
他试图给老板留下好印象，结果却不小心删除了重要文件，弄巧成拙。
|近义词：rebounding, boomeranging, misfiring|反义词：succeeding, working|词性:名词


backfire:/ˌbækˈfaɪər/ | v. |适得其反，事与愿违|(of a plan or action) have an unintended and unwelcome result that is the opposite of what was intended.|
Her plan to get revenge backfired badly.
她的复仇计划严重地适得其反。

The tax cuts backfired, leading to a decrease in government revenue.
减税措施事与愿违，导致政府收入减少。
|近义词：rebound, boomerang, misfire|反义词：succeed, work|词性:动词


Etymology: From *back* + *fire*. Originally referred to a firearm's premature explosion in the breech.  The figurative sense of having an unintended negative consequence developed later.
