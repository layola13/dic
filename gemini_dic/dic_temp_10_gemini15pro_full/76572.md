galliardise:/ˌɡæliˈɑːrdaɪz/ | n. |  欢快；嬉戏；活泼 |Liveliness and gaiety of manner; merriment.|  He danced with galliardise and charm. 他舞姿活泼迷人。 |Synonyms: exuberance, vivacity, animation, sprightliness, cheerfulness | Antonyms: apathy, dullness, listlessness, gloom | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *galliardise*, from Italian *gagliardìa* "gallantry, bravery, sturdiness", from *gagliarde*, feminine of *gagliardo* "strong, valiant", from Old Occitan *galhart* "valiant, joyful", from *gal* "joy" + *-hart* "hard".
