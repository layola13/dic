inshrine:/ɪnˈʃraɪn/| vt.|珍藏；奉为神圣；铭记|to enclose in or as if in a shrine, especially : to preserve or cherish as sacred|The Constitution enshrines the basic rights of all citizens. 宪法 enshrines 所有公民的基本权利。|The memory of his heroism is enshrined in our hearts.  他的英雄事迹铭记在我们心中。|近义词：cherish, preserve, treasure, sanctify|反义词：desecrate, profane|词性：动词

Etymology:
From Middle English *enshrinen, from en- + shrine (“receptacle for sacred relics”), from Old French escrin (“chest, box”), from Frankish *skrīn (“chest, box”), from Latin scrīnium (“box, case”), from scribere (“to write”), originally referring to a case for books or papers.
