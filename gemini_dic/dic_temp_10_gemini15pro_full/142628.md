osteozoa:/ˌɒstiəˈzoʊə/ | n. | 骨骼动物 | A major clade of animals that includes vertebrates and several invertebrate groups, all characterized by the presence of a skeleton composed of calcium phosphate.  These animals share a common ancestor that possessed a mineralized skeleton. |  Osteozoa encompasses a wide range of animals, from starfish to humans. 骨骼动物包含了从海星到人类的广泛动物。|  近义词:  (No direct synonym, as it's a taxonomic classification)  Perhaps "skeletal animals" as a descriptive phrase. | 反义词: (No direct antonym exists) | Noun
