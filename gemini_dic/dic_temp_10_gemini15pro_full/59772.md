economizing:/ɪˈkɒnəmaɪzɪŋ/ (UK) /ɪˈkɑːnəmaɪzɪŋ/ (US)| v. (present participle)|节省；节约；有效利用|The act of saving money or using resources carefully. |Example: They are economizing on fuel by using public transport. 他们为了节省燃料而使用公共交通工具。|Synonyms: saving, conserving, budgeting, scrimping|Antonyms: wasting, squandering, splurging|verb (present participle)

economize:/ɪˈkɒnəmaɪz/ (UK) /ɪˈkɑːnəmaɪz/ (US)| v.|节省；节约；有效利用|To save money or use resources carefully. |Example: We have to economize on electricity. 我们必须节约用电。|Synonyms: save, conserve, budget, scrimp|Antonyms: waste, squander, splurge|verb

ECONOMY:/ɪˈkɒnəmi/ (UK) /ɪˈkɑːnəmi/ (US)| n.|经济；节约|The wealth and resources of a country or region, especially in terms of the production and consumption of goods and services. Also, careful management of available resources.| Example: The global economy is facing many challenges. 全球经济面临许多挑战。 | Synonyms: thrift, frugality| Antonyms: extravagance, waste|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *économiser*, from Ancient Greek *οἰκονομία* (oikonomía, “household management”), from *οἶκος* (oîkos, “house”) + *νέμω* (némō, “I distribute, manage”).
