acclaim:/əˈkleɪm/| n. vt.| 称赞，赞扬；欢呼，喝彩| To praise enthusiastically and publicly.  To greet with loud approval.| The film has been acclaimed as a masterpiece. 这部电影被誉为杰作。

The young author was acclaimed for her debut novel.  这位年轻作家因其处女作而受到赞誉。|近义词：praise, applaud, commend, laud|反义词：criticize, condemn, denounce|词性: noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin acclamare "to cry out at, applaud," from Latin ad- "to" + clamare "to cry out, shout."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
Critics acclaim the new play. = 评论家们称赞这部新剧。
The audience acclaims the performance. = 观众为表演喝彩。


一般过去时:
The novel was acclaimed by critics. = 这部小说受到了评论家的赞扬。
They acclaimed him as a hero. = 他们称赞他为英雄。


现在完成时:
Her work has been acclaimed worldwide. = 她的作品在世界范围内受到赞誉。
The director has been acclaimed for his innovative films. = 这位导演因其创新电影而受到赞誉。


过去完成时:
The book had been acclaimed as a masterpiece before it was even published. = 这本书甚至在出版之前就被誉为杰作。
He had been acclaimed for his bravery during the war. = 他因在战争中的英勇而受到赞誉。


将来时:
The film will be acclaimed for its stunning visuals. = 这部电影将因其惊人的视觉效果而受到赞誉。
I'm sure her next book will be acclaimed by critics and readers alike. = 我相信她的下一本书将受到评论家和读者的赞誉。


被动语态:
The artist was acclaimed for his unique style. = 这位艺术家因其独特的风格而受到赞誉。
The singer is being acclaimed for her powerful voice. = 这位歌手因其强大的嗓音而受到赞誉。


PHRASE->
widely acclaimed = 广受赞誉
critically acclaimed = 受到评论界的好评
publicly acclaimed = 公开赞扬
to be acclaimed as = 被誉为
to acclaim someone for something = 因某事而赞扬某人
