panderingly:/ˈpændərɪŋli/
adv.
迎合地，讨好地
in a way that tries to please other people by doing or saying what you think they want, even though you might not agree with it.
The politician spoke panderingly to the crowd, promising lower taxes and better healthcare. 这位政客迎合群众，承诺降低税收和改善医疗保健。
He panderingly agreed with everything she said. 他一味地迎合她所说的每一句话。
近义词:obsequiously, subserviently, fawningly
反义词:forthrightly, honestly, frankly
词性:副词

Etymology:
From pander (v.) + -ing + -ly.  "Pander" comes from the character Pandarus in Chaucer's *Troilus and Criseyde*, who acts as a go-between for the lovers.  Over time, the word evolved to mean someone who caters to the base desires of others.
