concretional:/kɒŋˈkriːʃənəl/ /kɑːŋˈkriːʃənəl/| adj.|结核状的，凝固成的|Formed into a concretion; characterizing a concretion. |The geologist discovered a concretional mass of iron oxide in the sedimentary rock. 地质学家在沉积岩中发现了一块结核状的氧化铁。|The cave walls were covered in concretional formations. 洞穴墙壁上覆盖着结核状的构造。|近义词：nodular, consolidated, agglomerated|反义词：diffuse, dispersed|词性:形容词

concretion:/kɒŋˈkriːʃən/ /kɑːŋˈkriːʃən/| n.|结核，凝结物|A hard, compact mass formed by the local accumulation of matter, typically mineral matter, within a rock or other medium. |The concretion was found to contain fossilized remains. 发现这个结核包含着化石遗骸。|Kidney stones are a type of concretion. 肾结石是一种结石。|近义词：nodule, calculus, mass|反义词：solution, dispersion|词性:名词


词源: From Latin "concretus" (grown together, condensed), past participle of "concrescere" (to grow together), from "con-" (together) + "crescere" (to grow).
