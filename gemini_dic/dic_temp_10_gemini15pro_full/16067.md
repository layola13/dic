banditos:/bænˈdiːtoʊz/
bandito (singular):/bænˈdiːtoʊ/
|n.|土匪，强盗，歹徒|A bandit, especially one in Mexico or the southwestern US.|The banditos robbed the stagecoach. 土匪抢劫了驿马车。|Synonyms: outlaw, robber, thief, highwayman, desperado|Antonyms: lawman, sheriff, police officer|Noun
banditos (plural):/bænˈdiːtoʊz/
|n.|土匪们，强盗们，歹徒们|Plural of bandito.  Multiple bandits, especially in Mexico or the southwestern US.|The banditos were pursued by the posse.  土匪们被民兵队追赶。|Synonyms: outlaws, robbers, thieves, highwaymen, desperados|Antonyms: lawmen, sheriffs, police officers|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *bandido*, past participle of *bandir* "to banish, proclaim as an outlaw," from Old French *bandir*, from Frankish *bannjan* "to proclaim, summon," related to *ban* "proclamation, decree."  The suffix *-ito* is a Spanish diminutive.
