confetti:/kənˈfeti/| n. |五彩纸屑，糖果（尤指婚礼、狂欢节中抛撒的）|Small pieces of brightly colored paper, thrown at celebrations, especially weddings and carnivals. |例句：The bride and groom were showered with confetti as they left the church. 新郎新娘离开教堂时，人们向他们抛洒五彩纸屑。

例句：Confetti rained down on the parade. 五彩纸屑像雨点般落在游行队伍上。

近义词：streamers, sprinkles|反义词: None|词性:noun

词源：From Italian *confetti*, plural of *confetto* "small sweet," from Latin *confectum* "something prepared, preserved," past participle of *conficere* "to make ready, complete," from *com-* "together" (see com-) + *facere* "to do, make" (see fac-). Originally sugared almonds, by 1875 referring also to small bits of paper thrown about during carnivals.
