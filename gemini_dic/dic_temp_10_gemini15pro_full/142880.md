out-of-sorts:/ˌaʊt əv ˈsɔːrts/ | adj. | 心情不佳；身体不适 | Slightly unwell or irritable. |  I've been feeling out-of-sorts all day. 我一整天都感觉不舒服。| Example: He woke up feeling out-of-sorts and decided to stay home from work. 他醒来时感觉不舒服，决定待在家里不上班。 | Synonyms: unwell, ill, grumpy, irritable, moody | Antonyms: well, healthy, happy, cheerful | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"Out of sorts" originates from the 16th century. "Sorts" in this context refers to a proper or usual condition or order.  Therefore, "out of sorts" literally means "out of one's usual or proper condition," referring to both physical and mental states.
