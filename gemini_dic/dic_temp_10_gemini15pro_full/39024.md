conflagration:/ˌkɒnfləˈɡreɪʃn/ | n. | 大火灾，火灾；冲突 | a large and destructive fire that threatens human life and property.  A situation of escalating conflict and violence. | The conflagration destroyed several city blocks. 这场大火摧毁了几个街区。 The political unrest quickly escalated into a conflagration. 政治动荡迅速升级为一场冲突。 | 近义词: inferno, blaze, firestorm | 反义词: deluge (flood - focusing on the extinguishing aspect) | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Latin conflagrātiō (“burning, conflagration”), from conflagrō (“I burn up, burn completely”), from con- (“together”) + flagrō (“I burn, blaze”).
