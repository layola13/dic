curdly:/ˈkɜːrdli/| adj.|凝乳状的，像凝乳的|Resembling curds; like curds in appearance or consistency.|The sauce had separated and become slightly curdly.|近义词：coagulated, lumpy, clabbered|反义词:smooth, creamy|词性:形容词
Etymology: From curd + -ly.  "Curd" comes from the Old English "crude," meaning "raw" or "uncooked," likely related to the Dutch "kruid" (herb) and the German "Kraut" (cabbage). The suffix "-ly" forms adjectives meaning "like" or "having the qualities of."
