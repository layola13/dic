having:/ˈhævɪŋ/| v.|有；拥有；持有；经历；吃；喝；进行；举办；生(孩子)；得到；显示(某种感情或品质) |A present participle form of the verb "have".  It can function as a verb in a progressive tense, an adjective, or introduce an adverbial clause.|例句：Having finished his work, he went home. 完成工作后，他回家了。|近义词：possessing, experiencing, undergoing, holding|反义词:lacking|词性:动词 (present participle)

Etymology: From Middle English *haven, from Old English habban, from Proto-Germanic *habjaną, from Proto-Indo-European *keh₂p- (“to seize, hold, take”).

I have a book. = 我有一本书。
She has a cat. = 她有一只猫。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
I am having a good time. = 我玩得很开心。
They are having dinner. = 他们在吃晚饭。

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
I have had a busy day. = 我今天很忙。
He has had a headache all day. = 他整天都头疼。

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
I have been having trouble sleeping. = 我一直有睡眠问题。

一般过去时 (Simple Past):
I had a dream last night. = 我昨晚做了个梦。
She had a meeting yesterday. = 她昨天开了一个会。

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):

被动语态 (Passive Voice - technically applies to "have" not "having"):
The meeting was had yesterday. = 会议昨天举行了。
A good time was had by all. = 大家都玩得很开心。

疑问句 (Interrogative sentences - applies to "have"):
Do you have any questions? = 你有什么问题吗？
Did she have a good time? = 她玩得开心吗？

否定句 (Negative sentences - applies to "have"):
I don't have any money. = 我没有钱。
She didn't have a car. = 她没有车。
