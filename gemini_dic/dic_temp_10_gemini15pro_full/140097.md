off-label:/ˌɒfˈleɪbl/ /ˌɔːfˈleɪbl/ | adj. | 非标签的，标签外的 |  Used to describe the use of a drug or medical device for a condition that it has not been officially approved for by the relevant regulatory body (e.g., the FDA in the US).  It means using a medication or product in a way not specified in the approved packaging label. | The doctor prescribed the drug off-label to treat my migraines. 医生开了标签外用药来治疗我的偏头痛。|  The off-label use of this medication has shown promising results for certain types of cancer.  这种药物的标签外使用对某些类型的癌症显示出有希望的结果。| Synonyms: unapproved, unlicensed | Antonyms: approved, licensed | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"off-" (prefix meaning "away from, detached from, not on") + "label" (noun referring to a tag or sticker attached to something, providing information about it)
