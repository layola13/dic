amanitin:/əˈmænɪtɪn/ | n. | 鹅膏毒素 | A cyclic peptide of eight amino acids extracted from the poisonous mushroom *Amanita phalloides* (death cap) and related species, causing fatal liver damage unless treated promptly. |  Exposure to amanitin can cause severe liver damage. 接触鹅膏毒素会导致严重的肝损伤。 |Synonyms: toxin, poison | Antonyms: antidote, remedy | noun

amanitins:/əˈmænɪtɪnz/ | n. | 鹅膏毒素（复数）|  A group of related toxic cyclic peptides found in certain mushrooms, especially the death cap (Amanita phalloides).  They are potent inhibitors of RNA polymerase II, leading to severe liver damage and potentially death if ingested. | The amanitins in the death cap mushroom are responsible for its high toxicity.  死帽蕈中的鹅膏毒素是其高毒性的原因。 | Synonyms: toxins, poisons | Antonyms: antidotes, remedies | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *amanitine*, from *amanite* ("amanita") + *-ine* (suffix forming names of chemical substances, especially alkaloids).  Ultimately from Latin *amanita*.
