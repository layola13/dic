coreferentiality:/ˌkɔːrɛfərɛnʃiˈælɪti/ | n. | 共指性 | The state or property of referring to the same entity or concept.  It describes the relationship between linguistic expressions that refer to the same thing in the real world or in a text. |  The pronouns "he" and "John" exhibit coreferentiality in the sentence "John said he was tired."  约翰说他累了。（这里的“他”和“约翰”指的是同一个人）| synonyms: coreference, co-reference | antonyms: non-coreference | noun

coreference:/ˌkɔːˈrɛfərəns/ | n. | 共指 |  The act or state of referring to the same entity or concept. | The coreference between "the president" and "he" is clear in this context. 总统和他指的是同一个人，在这个语境下很清晰。| synonyms: coreferentiality, co-reference | antonyms: non-coreference | noun

co-reference:/ˌkəʊˈrɛfərəns/ | n. | 共指 | The act or state of referring to the same entity or concept. |  The use of "it" co-refers to "the book" mentioned earlier.  “它”指的是前面提到的“书”。 | synonyms: coreferentiality, coreference | antonyms: non-coreference | noun
