effrontery: /ɪˈfrʌntəri/ /ɛˈfrʌntəri/ | n. | 厚颜无耻的行为；放肆的行为；无礼的行为 | An act of insolent or impertinent behavior; an instance of audacity. |  Her effronteries to the CEO eventually cost her the job. 她对首席执行官的无礼行为最终让她丢了工作。 | Synonyms: audacity, impudence, insolence, nerve, presumption, temerity | Antonyms: humility, modesty, diffidence, respect | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French effronterie, from effronté "shameless, impudent," from Old French esfronte, past participle of esfronter "to make shameless," from es- (variant of ex-) "out" + front "forehead."  The notion is of having lost the blush of shame from one's forehead.
