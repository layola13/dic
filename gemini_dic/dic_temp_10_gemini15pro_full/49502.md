demagogical:/ˌdeməˈɡɒdʒɪkəl/ (US) /ˌdeməˈɡɑːdʒɪkəl/ (UK)| adj.|煽动性的，蛊惑人心的| Appealing to the emotions and prejudices of people in order to gain power or support, rather than using rational argument. |  The politician's demagogical speeches riled up the crowd.  这位政客煽动性的演讲激怒了人群。 | Example: His demagogical rhetoric incited violence. 他的煽动性言论煽动了暴力。|Synonyms: inflammatory, rabble-rousing, populist, manipulative|Antonyms: reasoned, logical, rational|word type:adjective

demagogue:/ˈdeməɡɒɡ/ (US) /ˈdeməɡɔːɡ/ (UK)| n.|煽动者，蛊惑民心的政客| A political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument. | The demagogue stirred up the crowd with hateful rhetoric.  这个煽动者用充满仇恨的言辞煽动人群。| Example: History is full of demagogues who rose to power through fear-mongering. 历史上充满了通过散布恐惧而掌权的煽动者。|Synonyms: agitator, firebrand, rabble-rouser|Antonyms: statesman, diplomat|word type:noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *dēmagōgicus, from Ancient Greek dēmagōgikós (“of or pertaining to a demagogue”), from dēmagōgós (“leader of the people, popular leader,” later “leader who panders to passions of the mob”). From dēmos (“people”) + agōgos (“leading, leader”).
