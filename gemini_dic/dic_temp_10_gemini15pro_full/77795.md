generalizability:/ˌdʒenərəˌlaɪzəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 普遍性，概括性 | The extent to which research findings can be applied to other settings or populations. | The study's small sample size raises concerns about the generalizability of its findings. 这项研究的样本量小，引发了对其研究结果普遍性的担忧。|  general applicability, universality, external validity | specificity, particularity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From generalize + -ability. Generalize comes from Late Latin *generalizare, from Latin generalis (“general”). -ability is a suffix forming nouns meaning "quality or state of being able."
