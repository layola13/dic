brigade:/brɪˈɡeɪd/| n.|旅，大队，组|A brigade is a large group of soldiers, or a group of people who are organized for a particular purpose.|The fire brigade arrived quickly at the scene. 消防队迅速赶到现场。|The cleaning brigade was hard at work. 清洁队正在努力工作。|synonyms: unit, corps, team, group, band|antonyms: individual, single|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *brigade*, from Italian *brigata* "company, troop," from *brigare* "to brawl, fight," of uncertain origin.
