chordata:/ˈkɔːrdeɪtə/ (US), /kɔːˈdeɪtə/ (UK)| n. |脊索动物门|Animals belonging to the phylum Chordata, possessing a notochord, a dorsal hollow nerve cord, pharyngeal slits, an endostyle, and a post-anal tail at some point during their development.|例句：Humans are members of the chordata phylum. 人类是脊索动物门的一员。

例句：The chordata phylum includes a wide range of animals, from fish to mammals. 脊索动物门包括了从鱼类到哺乳动物的各种动物。|近义词：vertebrates (脊椎动物，虽然并非所有脊索动物都是脊椎动物，但脊椎动物是脊索动物门中最主要的一支) |反义词: invertebrate (无脊椎动物)|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Modern Latin Chordata, neuter plural of chordatus (“having a notochord”), from Latin chorda (“cord, string”), from Greek khordḗ (“gut, string of a musical instrument”).
