overflours:/ˌoʊvərˈflaʊərz/| v.|过度加面粉；加太多面粉|To add too much flour to something, especially dough or batter, resulting in a dry or tough finished product.|The cake was dry and crumbly because I overfloured it. 蛋糕又干又碎，因为我加了太多面粉。|The recipe said to lightly flour the surface, but I think I overfloured it. 食谱上说要在表面轻轻地撒上面粉，但我想我撒得太多了。|synonyms: overmix, add too much flour|antonyms: underflour, use too little flour|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + flour.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively," "too much." "Flour" comes from Old French "flor," meaning "flower, blossom, best part," from Latin "flos," meaning "flower." Originally, flour referred to the finest part of the meal, as if it were the "flower" of the grain.
