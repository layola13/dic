go-fer: /ˈɡoʊfər/ (American English) | n. | 杂工，跑腿 |  A person whose job involves running errands and doing small tasks, often menial ones.  Derived from the phrase "go for," implying someone who is sent to "go for" things. | Example: He started as a go-fer at the studio, but he worked his way up to become a producer. 他从工作室的跑腿开始，后来一路晋升为制片人。 | Example:  She was tired of being the office go-fer, fetching coffee and making copies for everyone. 她厌倦了做办公室杂工，为每个人取咖啡和复印文件。| Synonym: errand boy/girl, dogsbody, lackey | Antonym: boss, manager, executive | Noun
