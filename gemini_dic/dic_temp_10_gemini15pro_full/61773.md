empawn:/ɪmˈpɔːn/ | v. |典当，抵押|To give something valuable to someone as a guarantee that you will repay borrowed money, with the understanding that they can keep the item if the money is not repaid.|She had to empawn her jewelry to pay the rent. 她不得不典当她的珠宝来付房租。|Synonyms: pledge, pawn, mortgage|Antonyms: redeem, reclaim|verb

empawn:/ɪmˈpɔːn/ | v. |使负有义务；使担负责任（较正式，罕用）|To put someone in a position where they are obliged to do something.|He felt empawned by his promise to help. 他感到自己被承诺帮忙的义务所束缚。|Synonyms: obligate, commit|Antonyms: release, absolve|verb
