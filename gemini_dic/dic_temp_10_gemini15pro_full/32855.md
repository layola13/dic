cholesterol:/kəˈlɛstərɔːl/ | n. | 胆固醇 | A waxy, fat-like substance that's found in all the cells of the body.  It's essential for making hormones, vitamin D, and substances that help you digest foods. Your body makes all the cholesterol it needs. | High cholesterol can increase your risk of heart disease. 高胆固醇会增加患心脏病的风险。|  High levels of cholesterol can clog your arteries. 高水平的胆固醇会堵塞你的动脉。 | synonyms: lipid, sterol | antonyms:  (Cholesterol doesn't have a direct antonym.  Instead, one might refer to medications or lifestyle changes that *lower* cholesterol.) | noun
