like-a-house-on-fire:/laɪk ə haʊs ɒn ˈfaɪə(r)/ | adv. | 迅速地，快速地；非常成功地 |  This idiom describes something happening very rapidly or successfully. It evokes the image of a house burning quickly and intensely. | The business is doing like a house on fire. 生意做得红红火火。| 近义词：rapidly, quickly, successfully, like wildfire, like a bat out of hell | 反义词：slowly, gradually, poorly | 词性: 副词

like-a-house-on-fire: /laɪk ə haʊs ɒn ˈfaɪə(r)/ | adj. |  (informal) very successful or popular | Describes something that is very successful or popular. |  Her new book is selling like a house on fire. 她的新书卖得非常火爆。| 近义词：successful, popular, booming, thriving | 反义词：unsuccessful, unpopular, failing | 词性: 形容词
