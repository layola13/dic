broach:/broʊtʃ/| v.|提出；开启；钻孔；穿透|To raise (a sensitive or difficult subject) for discussion.|He finally broached the subject of divorce. 他终于提出了离婚的话题。|近义词：raise, introduce, mention, open, tap|反义词：avoid, ignore, conceal|词性:动词
broach:/broʊtʃ/| n.|钻孔器；拉刀；胸针|A pointed tool, such as a tapered bit, reamer, or drift, for enlarging or shaping a hole.|The jeweler used a broach to enlarge the hole for the diamond. 珠宝商用拉刀扩大了钻石的孔。|近义词：pin, brooch|反义词：None|词性:名词

词源：From Old French *brochier "to spur, prick, goad," from broche "a spit," from Vulgar Latin *brocca "pointed instrument," perhaps of Gaulish origin.
