multiple-choice:/ˈmʌltɪpl ˌtʃɔɪs/| adj.|多项选择的|A type of test or question where the test-taker is given a number of possible answers and must choose the correct one.|The exam was multiple-choice.  考试是多项选择的。|alternative, selective|N/A|adjective

multiple-choice:/ˈmʌltɪpl ˌtʃɔɪs/| n.|多项选择题|A test or question that is multiple-choice.|She got all the multiple-choices right. 她所有的多项选择题都答对了。|alternative, selective|N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "multiple" (meaning many or various) + "choice" (the act of choosing). "Multiple" comes from the Latin "multiplex" (many-fold), and "choice" comes from the Old French "chois" (choice, thing chosen).
