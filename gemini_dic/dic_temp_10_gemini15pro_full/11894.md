arrogators: /ˈærəɡeɪtərz/ | n. | 僭越者；擅取者；霸占者 | People who arrogate to themselves power or privileges they do not rightfully possess.  Those who claim or seize something without justification. |  The arrogators of the throne were eventually overthrown. 这些王位的僭越者最终被推翻了。|  The company's arrogators expanded their control by acquiring smaller businesses. 该公司的霸占者通过收购较小的企业扩大了他们的控制权。 | Synonyms: usurpers, appropriators,  seizers | Antonyms:  yielders, relinquishes, abdicators | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *arrogator, from Latin arrogare (“to claim for oneself, appropriate, assume”), from ad- (“to”) + rogare (“to ask”).
