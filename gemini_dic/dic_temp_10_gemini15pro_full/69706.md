fenner:/ˈfɛnər/ | n. | 矿工；装煤工人；芬纳（姓氏） | A person who works in a coal mine, especially one who loads coal.  Primarily a British term, now largely historical. Also a surname. |  例句：The fenner filled the cart with coal. 装煤工人把煤装满了车。

例句：Many fenners lost their jobs when the mine closed. 矿山关闭时，许多矿工失业了。 | 近义词：miner, collier | 反义词： N/A | 词性：noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *fenere, from Old English *fennere, meaning "marsh-dweller". This likely refers to the location of some early mines, often in marshy areas.  Later, the term evolved to specifically refer to coal miners.
