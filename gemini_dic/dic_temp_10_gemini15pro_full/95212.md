inboard:/ˈɪnbɔːrd/| adj. adv.|内部的，舷内的；在船内；向内；|Located or used within the hull or fuselage of a vessel or aircraft; towards the centre of a vessel or aircraft.|The inboard engine provides more cabin space. 舷内发动机提供了更多舱内空间。|The boat's inboard motor was quiet and efficient. 这艘船的舷内发动机安静而高效。|近义词：internal, inside, inner|反义词:outboard, external|词性:形容词,副词

inboard:/ˈɪnbɔːrd/| adv.|向内，在内|Towards the centre, especially of a ship or aircraft.|He told the passengers to move inboard. 他告诉乘客们向船内移动。|Store the life jackets inboard. 把救生衣存放在船舱内。|近义词：inwards|反义词:outboard|词性:副词

inboard:/ˈɪnbɔːrd/| n.|内部构件，内部系统|The internal structure or systems of a vessel or aircraft.|The inboard of the plane was extensively damaged. 飞机的内部结构受到了广泛的损坏。|The ship's inboard was inspected thoroughly.  这艘船的内部系统被彻底检查了。|近义词：interior, internals|反义词:exterior|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From in + board. "In" meaning within, and "board" referring to the side of a ship.  The term originally referred to being within the ship's boards or hull.
