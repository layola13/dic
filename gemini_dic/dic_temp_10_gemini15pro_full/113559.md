macaronian:/ˌmækəˈroʊniən/| adj. n.|意大利式的，纨绔子弟，花花公子|Of or relating to macaroni; a dandy or fop, especially in the 18th century, characterized by affected elegance and extravagance in dress and manners.|He was a macaronian, always dressed in the latest fashions. 他是个花花公子，总是穿着最新的时装。|近义词：dandy, fop, coxcomb|反义词：sloven, unfashionable|词性：形容词，名词

macaronian:/ˌmækəˈroʊniən/| n.|通心粉|A type of pasta made from durum wheat and shaped into short tubes.|She made a delicious macaronian and cheese for dinner. 她晚饭做了美味的通心粉和奶酪。|近义词：pasta|反义词： (No direct antonym for a type of food)|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *maccarone* (plural *maccheroni*), probably from Greek *makaria*, a kind of barley broth.  The sense of "dandy" arose in the 18th century, referring to young Englishmen who had traveled to Italy and adopted Italian fashions and manners, which were considered affected and extravagant.
