distemperment:/dɪsˈtɛmpəmənt/
|n.|
|精神错乱，疾病，紊乱|
|A state of mental or physical disorder; a disturbance of normal functioning.|
|The dog's distemperment was evident in its erratic behavior. 这只狗的精神错乱从它不稳定的行为中可以明显看出。|

|The recent political upheavals have led to a general distemperment in the country. 最近的政治动荡导致了该国普遍的混乱。|
|近义词：illness, sickness, disorder, malady, ailment, affliction|
|反义词：health, wellness, order, stability|
|词性: 名词|


distemperment:/dɪsˈtɛmpəmənt/
|n.|
|坏脾气，易怒|
|A state of being bad-tempered or irritable.|
|His distemperment made it difficult to work with him. 他的坏脾气使人很难与他共事。|

|She was prone to fits of distemperment when she was tired. 她疲惫的时候容易发脾气。|
|近义词：irritability, bad temper, irascibility, grumpiness, peevishness|
|反义词：good temper, calmness, equanimity, placidity|
|词性: 名词|


词源:  From Middle English *distemperement,  from Anglo-Norman destemprement, from Old French destemprer ("to disturb the temperature of, to upset the balance of humors, to disorder"),  from des- (expressing reversal) + temprer ("to temper, to moderate").
