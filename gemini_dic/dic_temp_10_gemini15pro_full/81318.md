grapeshot: /ˈɡreɪpʃɒt/ (UK) /ˈɡreɪpʃɑːt/ (US) | n. | 霰弹，葡萄弹 | A cluster of small iron balls fired from a cannon. | The cannon fired a volley of grapeshot into the advancing enemy.  大炮向前进的敌人发射了一排霰弹。 | Example: The grapeshot tore through the enemy ranks. 霰弹撕裂了敌人的队伍。| Synonyms: canister shot, case shot | Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but perhaps single cannonball) | Noun

grapeshot: /ˈɡreɪpʃɒt/ (UK) /ˈɡreɪpʃɑːt/ (US) | adj. (Figurative) |  猛烈的，密集的 | Resembling or suggestive of grapeshot in its effects; delivered in a rapid and concentrated manner, like a volley of grapeshot. | His criticism was like grapeshot, relentless and devastating. 他的批评像霰弹一样，无情而具有毁灭性。 | Example:  He unleashed a grapeshot criticism of the government's policies. 他对政府的政策展开了猛烈的抨击。| Synonyms:  intense, concentrated, fierce, withering | Antonyms:  mild, gentle, sporadic | Adjective
