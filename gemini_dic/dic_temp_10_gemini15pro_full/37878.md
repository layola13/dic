complacent:/kəmˈpleɪsnt/ | adj. | 自满的，得意的 | Showing smug or uncritical satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements. |  He had become complacent after years of success.  他多年成功后变得自满了。 | synonyms: smug, self-satisfied, pleased with oneself | antonyms: dissatisfied, discontented, humble | adjective

complacential:/kəmˌpleɪˈsɛnʃəl/ | adj. |  自满的，得意的 | Expressing or characterized by complacency.  | His complacential smile irritated me. 他那自鸣得意的笑容激怒了我。 | synonyms: smug, self-satisfied, complacent | antonyms: dissatisfied, discontented, humble | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin complacent-, from complacere "to please greatly," from com- "together" (see com-) + placere "to please" (see please). The -ial suffix forms adjectives.
