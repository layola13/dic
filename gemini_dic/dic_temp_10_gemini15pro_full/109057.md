legalitarianism: /ɪˌɡælɪˈtɛəriənɪzəm/ | n. | 平等主义；平均主义 |  A belief system that promotes the idea that all people are equal and deserve equal rights and opportunities. |  Example: Legalitarianism is a core principle of many modern democracies. 平等主义是许多现代民主国家的核心原则。| Example:  The movement championed legalitarianism and social justice.  这场运动拥护平等主义和社会正义。 | Synonyms: egalitarianism, equality, fairness | Antonyms: elitism, hierarchy, inequality | noun

legalitarian: /ɪˌɡælɪˈtɛəriən/ | adj. | 平等主义的；平均主义的 | Relating to or believing in the principle that all people are equal and deserve equal rights and opportunities. | Example:  They advocate for a more legalitarian distribution of wealth. 他们主张更平等的财富分配。 | Example:  She holds a legalitarian view of society. 她对社会持有平等主义的观点。 | Synonyms: egalitarian, equitable, fair | Antonyms: elitist, hierarchical, unequal | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *égalitaire*, from *égal* (“equal”), from Latin *aequalis*, from *aequus* (“level, even, equal”) + *-arian* (suffix indicating a person who advocates a particular belief or system).
