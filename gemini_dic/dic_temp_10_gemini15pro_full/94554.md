imposture:/ɪmˈpɒstʃə(r)/ /ɪmˈpɑːstʃər/ | n. | 欺骗；冒名顶替；骗局 |  An act of pretending to be someone else in order to deceive others. |  He was eventually exposed as an imposture, a charlatan who had faked his credentials. 他最终被揭露是一个骗子，一个伪造证件的江湖骗子。

The painting was an imposture, a clever forgery worth only a fraction of the asking price. 这幅画是一幅赝品，一幅巧妙的伪造品，其价值仅为要价的一小部分。 | 近义词：fraud, deception, sham, charade | 反义词：honesty, authenticity, genuineness | 词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *imposture*, from Latin *impostūra* ("imposition, deceit"), from *impositus*, past participle of *imponere* ("to impose, deceive"), from *in-* ("in, on") + *ponere* ("to put, place").
