glamour:/ˈɡlæmər/ (US) /ˈɡlæmə(r)/ (UK)|n.|魅力，诱惑力，迷人的魔力|A quality that makes something or someone seem attractive, exciting, and special, often in a mysterious or magical way.|例句：The city of Paris has a certain glamour. 巴黎这座城市有一种独特的魅力。

例句：She was captivated by the glamour of Hollywood. 她被好莱坞的魅力所吸引。

近义词：charm, allure, enchantment, magic, fascination|反义词：drabness, dullness|词性:名词

glamorous:/ˈɡlæmərəs/ (US) /ˈɡlæmərəs/ (UK)|adj.|迷人的，魅力四射的|Full of glamour; exciting and attractive.|例句：She led a glamorous life as a fashion model. 她作为时装模特过着光鲜亮丽的生活。

例句：The glamorous actress arrived at the premiere in a stunning gown.  这位魅力四射的女演员穿着令人惊艳的礼服出席了首映式。


近义词：charming, alluring, fascinating, attractive|反义词：drab, dull, unglamorous|词性:形容词

glamorize:/ˈɡlæməraɪz/ (US) /ˈɡlæməraɪz/ (UK)|vt.|使…富有魅力，美化|To make something seem more glamorous than it really is.|例句：The movie glamorized the life of a gangster. 这部电影美化了黑帮的生活。

例句：Don't glamorize smoking; it's dangerous. 不要美化吸烟；这很危险。


近义词：romanticize, idealize, glorify|反义词：vilify, denigrate|词性:动词
