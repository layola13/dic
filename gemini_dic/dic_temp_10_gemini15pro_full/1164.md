acclaiming: /əˈkleɪmɪŋ/
verb
称赞；赞扬；给予高度评价
Praising enthusiastically and publicly.
The critics are acclaiming her performance as the best of the year. = 评论家们称赞她的表演是年度最佳。
The crowd was acclaiming the winning team. =  人群正在为获胜的队伍欢呼喝彩。
近义词: praising, applauding, lauding, cheering, hailing
反义词: criticizing, condemning, denouncing
词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin acclamare "to cry out at, applaud," from ad- "to" (see ad-) + clamare "to cry out" (see claim (v.)).  The present participle form "acclaiming" signifies the ongoing action of the verb.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The audience acclaims the performers after every show. = 观众在每场演出后都为表演者喝彩。
He always acclaims his students' hard work. = 他总是称赞他的学生的努力。

一般过去时:
The critics acclaimed the movie as a masterpiece. = 评论家们称赞这部电影是杰作。
They acclaimed him as a hero. = 他们称赞他为英雄。

现在完成时:
The author has been acclaimed for her insightful writing. =  这位作家因其富有洞察力的写作而受到赞誉。
They have acclaimed the new policy as a major success. = 他们称赞新政策取得了重大成功。

过去完成时:
The scientist had been acclaimed for his groundbreaking research before he received the Nobel Prize. = 在获得诺贝尔奖之前，这位科学家就因其开创性的研究而受到赞誉。
She had acclaimed his bravery before she knew the full story. = 在她了解全部情况之前，她称赞了他的勇敢。

将来时:
The critics will acclaim this performance as legendary. = 评论家们将会称赞这场表演是传奇性的。
I believe future generations will acclaim his contributions to science. = 我相信子孙后代会称赞他对科学的贡献。

虚拟语气:
If I were to write a novel, I would hope it would be acclaimed by critics. = 如果我要写一本小说，我希望它能受到评论家的赞扬。
I wish I had a talent that would be acclaimed by others. = 我希望我有一种能被别人称赞的才能。


被动语态:
The young artist was acclaimed for his innovative style. =  这位年轻艺术家因其创新风格而受到赞誉。
Her latest novel has been acclaimed by critics worldwide. = 她的最新小说受到了世界各地评论家的赞誉。

疑问句:
Why is this book being acclaimed so highly? = 为什么这本书受到如此高的赞誉？
Was his performance acclaimed by the audience? = 他的表演受到观众的欢迎吗？


否定句:
His latest film wasn't acclaimed by the critics. = 他的最新电影没有受到评论家的赞扬。
The performance wasn't universally acclaimed. = 这场演出并没有得到普遍的赞扬。
