half-rights:/ˌhɑːf ˈraɪts/ | n. | 部分权利；不完全的权利 |  A situation where someone has some, but not all, of the rights or privileges they should have.  This term often carries a negative connotation, implying that the rights granted are insufficient or discriminatory. |  Black people in the Jim Crow South had half-rights; they could work, but they couldn't vote. | 近义词: limited rights, partial rights, second-class citizenship | 反义词: full rights, equal rights | 词性: 名词

half-rights: /ˌhɑːf ˈraɪts/ | adj. (less common) |  Characterized by having limited or incomplete rights. | The half-rights system in place kept many from true equality. | 近义词:  limited, restricted | 反义词:  unrestricted, complete | 词性: 形容词


词源: "Half" (Old English healf) + "Rights" (Middle English right, from Old Norse réttr). The term is a compound noun formed by combining the adjective "half" with the noun "rights".
