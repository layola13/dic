importunacy:/ˌɪmpɔːrˈtjuːnəsi/ |n.| 持续不断的请求；纠缠不休；强求| Persistent and unwelcome requests or demands; insistent solicitation. |  His importunacy finally wore her down and she agreed to help.  他不断地请求最终使她妥协并同意帮忙。| Synonyms: persistence, insistence, pestering, harassment | Antonyms: acquiescence, resignation, deference | noun

importunate:/ɪmˈpɔːrtʃənət/ |adj.| 持续不断的请求的；纠缠不休的；强求的 | Persistent and unwelcome in requests or demands; insistent in solicitation. |  He was an importunate beggar, always asking for money. 他是一个纠缠不休的乞丐，总是要钱。| Synonyms: persistent, insistent, pestering, harassing | Antonyms: reserved, reticent, unobtrusive | adjective

importune:/ˌɪmpɔːrˈtjuːn/ |v.| 持续不断的请求；纠缠不休；强求 | To make persistent and unwelcome requests or demands; to solicit insistently. |  She importuned him for a loan, even after he had refused. 即使在他拒绝之后，她仍然不断地请求他借钱。| Synonyms:  pester, badger, harass, beseech | Antonyms: desist, forbear, abstain | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *importuner, from Late Latin importunāre "to be troublesome," from Latin importūnus "unfit, inconvenient, troublesome," originally "having no harbor" (of a coast), from in- "not" + portus "harbor."
