hydage:/ˈhaɪdɪdʒ/
n.
水坝使用费；水力发电量；（尤指）运河通行费
A toll or tax payable for the use of water, especially for the passage of boats through a canal or for the use of a wharf. The amount of power generated by water.
The barge owner had to pay hydage for using the canal.  驳船所有者必须支付使用运河的水坝使用费。
The hydage generated by the dam was substantial.  大坝产生的水力发电量很大。
Synonyms:  water toll, canal toll, wharfage, water power, hydropower
Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but could consider) free passage, free access
Etymology:  From Middle English, from Old French *hydage, from hyd- water (from Latin aqua) + -age.
