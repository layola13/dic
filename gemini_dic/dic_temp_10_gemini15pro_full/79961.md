go-along:/ɡoʊ əˈlɒŋ/ /ɡoʊ əˈlɔːŋ/ | phrasal verb | 同意，赞同；陪同，一起去；进展，进行 | To agree with someone or something; to accompany someone; to proceed or progress |  例句：He decided to go along with their plan. 他决定同意他们的计划。| 近义词：agree, concur, accompany, proceed | 反义词：disagree, object, refuse, oppose | 词性: 动词短语

go-along:/ɡoʊ əˈlɒŋ/ /ɡoʊ əˈlɔːŋ/ | noun |  赞同；配合 | Agreement or cooperation | 例句：She gave her go-along to the proposal. 她同意了这项提议。 | 近义词：agreement, approval, consent | 反义词：disagreement, disapproval, objection | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
"Go" comes from Old English "gān," meaning "to go, walk, advance." "Along" comes from Old English "andlang," meaning "along, lengthwise."  The combination "go along" emerged as a phrasal verb, acquiring various meanings related to movement and agreement.
