overlook: /ˌōvərˈlʊk/ (US), /ˌəʊvəˈlʊk/ (UK)| vt. n.| 忽视；俯瞰；远眺 | To fail to notice or consider something; to have a view from a higher position. |例句：He overlooked a crucial detail in the contract. 他忽略了合同中的一个关键细节。|近义词：miss, ignore, neglect, disregard |反义词：notice, observe, scrutinize |词性：动词

overlook: /ˈōvərˌlʊk/ (US), /ˈəʊvəˌlʊk/ (UK)| n.| 忽视；高处俯瞰的地方 | An act of overlooking or failing to notice something; a place from which one can look down on something else. |例句：The hotel had a balcony overlooking the sea. 这家酒店有一个可以俯瞰大海的阳台。|近义词：vantage point, vista |反义词：N/A |词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *oferlocian "look over, look down upon, despise," from ofer "over" + locian "to look."
