gland:/ɡlænd/
noun
腺体
An organ in the body that secretes particular chemical substances for use in the body or for discharge into the surroundings.
The pituitary gland is a small gland located at the base of the brain. 脑垂体是一个位于大脑底部的小腺体。

The thyroid gland produces hormones that regulate metabolism. 甲状腺产生调节新陈代谢的激素。

synonym: organ, secretory organ
antonym:  (No direct antonym for a physical organ.  Perhaps "pore" in some contexts, but not a true opposite)
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "glande," from Latin "glans, glandis" meaning "acorn, nut; any gland resembling an acorn."  The resemblance is due to the shape of some glands.
