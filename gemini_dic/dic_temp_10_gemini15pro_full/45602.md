curricula's:/kəˈrɪkjələz/
noun
课程的
The possessive form of "curricula," which is the plural of "curriculum."  It refers to something belonging to or associated with more than one curriculum.
The curricula's aims were diverse, reflecting the varied needs of the students. 这些课程的目标多种多样，反映了学生不同的需求。
Near Synonyms:  courses', programs', syllabi'
Antonyms:  N/A (Possessive forms don't typically have antonyms)
Part of Speech:  Possessive noun


Etymology:
From Latin "curriculum" meaning "a running, course," from "currere" meaning "to run."
