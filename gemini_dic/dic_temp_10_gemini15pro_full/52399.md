didascalic:/ˌdɪdəˈskælɪk/ | adj. | 教诲的，说教的 | Intended to teach, especially in a moralizing way; instructive. | The novel is highly didascalic, aiming to impart important life lessons to the reader. 这部小说具有很强的教诲意味，旨在向读者传授重要的人生教训。 |  近义词: instructive, pedagogic, didactic, preceptive | 反义词: uninformative, unenlightening | 词性: adjective

didascalia:/ˌdɪdəˈskeɪliə/ | n. | 舞台说明 |  A note in the text of a play, especially one concerning stage direction. |  The didascalia indicated that the actor should enter from stage left. 舞台说明指示演员应该从舞台左侧进入。 | 近义词: stage direction, instruction | 反义词: (No clear antonym) | 词性: noun

词源:
From Greek διδασκαλικός (didaskalikos, “fit for teaching”), from διδάσκαλος (didaskalos, “teacher”), from διδάσκω (didaskō, “I teach”).
