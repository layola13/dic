monetarism:/ˈmʌnɪtərɪzəm/| n. |货币主义|A school of economic thought that emphasizes the role of the money supply in controlling inflation and economic growth.  It argues that governments should primarily focus on maintaining price stability through controlling the money supply, rather than intervening directly in the economy.|Monetarism became influential in the 1970s and 1980s. 货币主义在20世纪70年代和80年代变得有影响力。|Synonyms: monetary policy,  supply-side economics|Antonyms: Keynesianism, fiscal policy|noun
