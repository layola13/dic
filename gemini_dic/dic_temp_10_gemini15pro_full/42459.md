counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/
| adj. n.|反事实的，反事实命题|Relating to or expressing what has not happened or is not the case. |例句：Counterfactual history is a fascinating but ultimately futile exercise. 反事实历史引人入胜，但最终是徒劳的。|近义词：hypothetical, conjectural, speculative|反义词：factual, real, actual|词性:形容词
counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/
|n.|反事实命题|A counterfactual statement or proposition. |例句：It's interesting to consider counterfactuals, like what if the South had won the Civil War? 思考反事实的情况很有趣，例如，如果南方赢得了内战会怎样？|近义词：hypothesis, conjecture, speculation|反义词：fact, reality, actuality|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- (meaning "against" or "opposite") + factual.  The term emerged in the mid-20th century in philosophy and logic.
