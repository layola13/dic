bandagers:/ˈbændɪdʒərz/
n.
绷带员；包扎员
People who apply bandages.  Those who dress wounds.
The bandagers quickly attended to the injured athletes. 绷带员迅速照料受伤的运动员。
The bandagers worked tirelessly in the emergency room. 绷带员在急诊室不知疲倦地工作。
近义词:dressers, medics, first-aiders
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "patients" in a contextual sense)
词性:noun


bandage:/ˈbændɪdʒ/
n.
绷带
A strip of material used to bind a wound or injury.
He wrapped a bandage around his injured ankle. 他在受伤的脚踝上缠了一条绷带。
The nurse applied a clean bandage to the cut. 护士在伤口上贴了一块干净的绷带。
vt.
用绷带包扎
To wrap a bandage around something.
She bandaged his arm carefully. 她小心翼翼地包扎了他的手臂。
He bandaged the wound to stop the bleeding. 他用绷带包扎伤口以止血。
近义词:dressing, wrap
反义词: (No direct antonym)
词性:noun, verb

词源: From Old French "bandage" (noun and verb), from "bande" (strip, band), ultimately from Frankish or other Germanic source. Related to "bind".
