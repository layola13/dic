clamoring:/ˈklæmərɪŋ/ | adj.  | 吵闹的，喧嚷的，大声要求的 |Characterized by a loud outcry or insistent demands.  | The clamoring crowd demanded to see the king. 喧闹的人群要求见国王。 | Synonyms: insistent, vociferous, boisterous | Antonyms: quiet, silent, peaceful | adjective

clamor:/ˈklæmər/ | n. vi. | 喧闹，吵闹；大声要求 |A loud and confused noise, especially that of people shouting vehemently.  To shout loudly and insistently. | The clamor of the city streets made it hard to sleep. 城市街道的喧闹声让人难以入睡. The protesters clamored for change. 抗议者大声疾呼要求变革. | Synonyms: uproar, din, outcry, yell | Antonyms: silence, quiet, hush | noun, verb

词源: From Middle English *clamouren*, from Old French *clameur, clamour* "outcry, loud complaint," from Latin *clamor* "shout, uproar," from *clamare* "to cry out."
