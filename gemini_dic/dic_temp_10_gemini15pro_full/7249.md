anglo-normans:/ˌæŋɡləʊˈnɔːrmənz/
|n.|盎格鲁-诺曼人|The Anglo-Normans were the descendants of the Normans who conquered England in 1066 and subsequently ruled the country.  盎格鲁-诺曼人是1066年征服英格兰的诺曼人的后裔，随后统治了这个国家。|The Anglo-Normans introduced French language and culture to England. 盎格鲁-诺曼人将法语和法国文化引入了英格兰。|近义词:Normans|反义词:Anglo-Saxons|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Anglo- (prefix meaning "English" or "related to England") + Norman (referring to the people of Normandy, France).  来自 Anglo-（前缀，意为“英语”或“与英格兰有关”）+ Norman（指法国诺曼底人）。
