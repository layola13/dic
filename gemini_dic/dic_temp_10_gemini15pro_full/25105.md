buccaneering:/ˌbʌkəˈnɪərɪŋ/
n./
海盗行为；冒险精神；大胆进取
/
The act of being a buccaneer; engaging in piracy.  Also used figuratively to describe ruthless, adventurous, or entrepreneurial behavior.
/
The buccaneering spirit of the early explorers led to the discovery of new lands. 早期探险家的海盗精神导致了新大陆的发现。

His buccaneering business tactics led to rapid growth, but also alienated many competitors.  他大胆进取的商业策略带来了快速增长，但也疏远了许多竞争对手。

/
近义词: piracy, freebooting, adventuring, daring, enterprising, audacious
/
反义词: cautious, timid, conservative, law-abiding
/
词性: noun


buccaneer:/ˌbʌkəˈnɪər/
n./
海盗；冒险家；大胆进取的人
/
A pirate, especially one operating in the Caribbean during the 17th and 18th centuries.  Also used figuratively to describe a ruthless, adventurous, or entrepreneurial person.
/
Sir Francis Drake was a famous buccaneer.  弗朗西斯·德雷克爵士是一位著名的海盗。

He's a real buccaneer in the business world. 他是商界真正的冒险家。
/
近义词: pirate, freebooter, adventurer, daredevil, entrepreneur
/
反义词: coward, conformist, conservative
/
词性: noun


词源:

From French *boucanier*, from *boucan* "wooden frame for smoking meat," from Tupi *mokam*.  Buccaneers were originally hunters who smoked meat on boucans in the Caribbean.  They later turned to piracy.
