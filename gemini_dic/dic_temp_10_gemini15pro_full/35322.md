cnidoblast:/ˈnaɪdəblæst/ | n. | 刺细胞 | An explosive cell, characteristic of Cnidaria (e.g., jellyfish, corals, sea anemones), containing a giant secretory organelle called a cnidocyst that delivers a sting. |  Jellyfish use cnidoblasts to paralyze their prey. 水母用刺细胞来麻痹它们的猎物。 |Synonyms: stinging cell, nematocyst | Antonyms: None | Noun
