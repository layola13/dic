dignifiedly: /ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪdli/
adverb
庄严地；有尊严地
In a manner showing dignity and self-respect.
She walked dignifiedly across the stage to accept the award. 她庄严地走过舞台去领奖。
He bore the criticism dignifiedly. 他庄严地承受了批评。
近义词: nobly, stately, regally, majestically, grandly
反义词: humbly, abjectly, servilely, shamefacedly
词性: adverb

dignified: /ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪd/
adjective
有尊严的；高贵的
Having or showing a composed or serious manner that is worthy of respect.
He maintained a dignified silence throughout the trial. 在整个审判过程中，他保持着庄严的沉默。
She has a dignified bearing. 她举止庄重。
近义词: stately, noble, majestic, imposing, august
反义词: undignified, common, vulgar, low
词性: adjective

dignify: /ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪ/
verb
使高贵；使有尊严
To give dignity or distinction to.
The mayor dignified the ceremony with her presence. 市长的出席使仪式倍增光彩。
I wouldn't dignify his rude remarks with a response. 我不会理会他的粗鲁言论。
近义词: ennoble, exalt, elevate, honor, grace
反义词: demean, degrade, debase, humble, humiliate
词性: verb

词源: From Late Middle English *dignified, from Latin dignificātus, past participle of dignificāre (“to honor, esteem”), from dignus (“worthy”) + -ficāre (a suffix forming verbs, “to make”).
