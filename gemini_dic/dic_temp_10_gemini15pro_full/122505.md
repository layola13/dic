mocked-up:/ˈmɑkt ʌp/ /ˈmɒkt ʌp/ | adj. | 模拟的，伪造的，虚构的 |  Made to look like something real but is not; simulated.  |  They used a mocked-up airplane for the film. 他们用一架模拟飞机进行拍摄。 | Synonyms: simulated, imitation, dummy, fake, feigned,  | Antonyms: real, authentic, genuine | adjective

mocked-up:/ˈmɑkt ʌp/ /ˈmɒkt ʌp/ | n. | 模型，样机 | A model or prototype of something, used for testing or demonstration. |  The designers presented a mocked-up of the new car. 设计师们展示了这款新车的模型。 | Synonyms: model, prototype, mockup, mock-up | Antonyms: final product, finished article | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "mock" (verb, meaning "to imitate or mimic, often in derision") + "up" (adverb, indicating completion or thoroughness). The term originated in the printing industry, referring to a preliminary layout or arrangement of type and illustrations.
