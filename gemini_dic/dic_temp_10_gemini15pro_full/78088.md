geoglyph: /ˈdʒiːoʊɡlɪf/ | n. | 地画 | A large design or motif (generally longer than 4 metres) produced on the ground and typically formed by rocks or similarly durable elements of the landscape, such as stones, stone fragments, gravel pavements, or earth. |  The Nazca Lines in Peru are perhaps the most famous geoglyphs. 秘鲁的纳斯卡线条也许是最著名的地画。|  petroglyph, earthwork, earth figure | N/A | noun
