monoplanes:/ˈmɒnəpleɪnz/ (UK) /ˈmɑːnəpleɪnz/ (US)| n. |单翼机|An airplane with only one main set of wings, as opposed to biplanes or triplanes.|The Wright brothers' first successful aircraft was a biplane, but monoplanes became more common later.|近义词:aircraft, airplane, plane|反义词:biplane, triplane|词性:名词

词源: "mono-" (Greek: μόνος, mónos, 'single, alone') + "plane" (short for aeroplane).
