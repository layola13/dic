melodrama: /ˈmɛləˌdrɑːmə/ (US), /ˈmɛləˌdrɑːmə/ (UK) | n. |情节剧；音乐剧；通俗剧|A play, film, or other narrative characterized by extravagant theatricality and by the predominance of plot and physical action over characterization.  It typically includes stereotyped characters, exaggerated emotions, and exciting events intended to appeal to the emotions.| The movie was a pure melodrama, full of improbable coincidences and over-the-top acting. 这部电影纯粹是一部情节剧，充满了不可能的巧合和夸张的表演。|  近义词: soap opera, drama, play | 反义词: documentary, realism |词性: noun

melodramatic: /ˌmɛlədrəˈmætɪk/ (US), /ˌmɛlədrəˈmætɪk/ (UK) | adj. | 情节剧的；夸张的；煽情的 | Characteristic of melodrama, especially in being exaggerated, sensational, or overemotional. |  He reacted in a melodramatic way, throwing himself on the floor and sobbing. 他以夸张的方式做出反应，倒在地上抽泣。| 近义词: theatrical, histrionic, overwrought | 反义词: understated, subtle, realistic |词性: adjective

词源：melodrama (n.) 1802, "play with songs interspersed," from French mélodrame (1781), from Greek melos "song" (see melody) + French drame "drama."  The sense evolved by 1860 to "play characterized by sensational incident and violent appeals to emotion, but with a happy ending."
