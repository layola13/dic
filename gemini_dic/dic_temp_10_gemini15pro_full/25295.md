ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *buggebear*, equivalent to bug + bear.  "Bug" in this context likely refers to a hobgoblin or bogeyman, rather than an insect.  The word originally referred to a frightening imaginary creature, and later evolved to mean a source of annoyance or worry.
