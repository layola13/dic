decameron:/ˌdekəˈmɛrən/ | n. |十日谈；十日物語 | A collection of one hundred tales told over a period of ten days by a group of young people fleeing Florence to escape the Black Death, written by Giovanni Boccaccio in the 14th century. |  Example 1:  Boccaccio's Decameron is a masterpiece of Italian literature.  薄伽丘的《十日谈》是意大利文学的杰作。

Example 2:  The stories in the Decameron range from tragic to comic. 《十日谈》中的故事从悲剧到喜剧都有。 | synonym:  story collection, anthology | antonym:  (No direct antonym, could consider "monograph" for a single, focused work) | noun
