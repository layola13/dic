carf:/kɑːrf/
n.|雕刻品；切片；切下的肉块|A carved piece of wood, meat, or other material.  A slice or a cut piece of meat.|He showed me a small carf of wood he had been working on. 他给我看他一直在雕刻的一小块木头。|slice, piece, carving|chunk, whole|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *carven*, from Old Norse *karfa* "to cut, carve," related to Old English *ceorfan*.  The noun form likely developed from the verb, referring to the result of carving.
