devil-like:/ˈdevəlˌlaɪk/| adj.|恶魔般的，魔鬼似的|Resembling or characteristic of a devil, especially in being wicked, cruel, or malevolent.|His devil-like laughter echoed through the halls. 他的恶魔般的笑声回荡在大厅里。|The dictator ruled with a devil-like grip on power.  独裁者以恶魔般的控制力掌握着权力。|Synonyms: demonic, satanic, fiendish, infernal, hellish|Antonyms: angelic, saintly, godly, virtuous|Etymology: From "devil" + "-like". "Devil" comes from Old English "dēofol", ultimately from Late Latin "diabolus", from Greek "diabolos" ("slanderer, accuser"). "-like" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "resembling" or "characteristic of".
