malpractice: /ˌmælˈpræktɪs/ | n. |渎职；玩忽职守；医疗事故| Improper, illegal, or negligent professional activity or treatment, especially by a medical practitioner, lawyer, or public official. | The doctor was sued for malpractice after the patient died during a routine operation.  这位医生在病人死于常规手术后被起诉玩忽职守。|  The lawyer was accused of malpractice for failing to adequately represent his client.  这位律师因未能充分代表其客户而被指控渎职。| synonyms: negligence, dereliction of duty, misconduct, wrongdoing | antonyms:  due diligence, proper conduct, professionalism | noun

malpractice: /ˌmælˈpræktɪs/ | n. |失职；不法行为| Failure to fulfil or carry out professional duties or responsibilities, especially in a way that causes harm or damage.| The accountant's malpractice resulted in significant financial losses for the company.  会计的失职给公司造成了重大经济损失。| The teacher's malpractice led to the student's failing grade. 老师的失职导致了学生的不及格。| synonyms:  incompetence, misconduct, negligence | antonyms: competence, diligence, professionalism | noun
