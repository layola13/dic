high-browish:/ˌhaɪˈbraʊɪʃ/ | adj. | 高傲的，自以为有学问的 |  Suggesting intellectual pretension or snobbery.  Having or affecting a highbrow attitude. |  She made some high-browish remark about the novel, making everyone else feel uncultured. 她对这部小说发表了一些自以为高明的评论，让其他人感到自己没文化。 | sophisticated, pretentious, intellectual, snobbish |  lowbrow, unpretentious, down-to-earth | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From highbrow + -ish.  "Highbrow" originated in the late 19th century, referring literally to a high forehead, then figuratively to intellectual superiority (often perceived as pretentious).  The suffix "-ish" adds a sense of approximation or slightness to the quality.
