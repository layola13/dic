charqui: /ˈtʃɑːrki/ | n. | 干肉，牛肉干 |  A form of dried, preserved meat, typically beef, originating in South America. The process involves salting and drying thin strips of meat, often in the sun or with smoke. This method preserves the meat for extended periods without refrigeration.  | The gauchos sustained themselves on charqui during their long journeys across the pampas.  牛仔们在穿越潘帕斯草原的长途旅行中靠牛肉干维持生计。| Synonym: jerky, biltong | Antonym: fresh meat | Noun
