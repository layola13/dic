armed:/ɑːmd/ | adj. | 武装的；有扶手的；有防卫器官的 | furnished with weapons; having arms or weapons of offense or defense; equipped |  The police confronted the armed robbers. 警方与武装劫匪对峙。 | synonyms: equipped, fortified, weaponed | antonyms: unarmed, defenseless, vulnerable | adjective

armed:/ɑːmd/ | v. | 武装；配备 | past tense and past participle of "arm" - to furnish with weapons |  They armed themselves with rifles. 他们用步枪武装自己。 | synonyms: equip, outfit, fortify | antonyms: disarm, demilitarize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *armed*, *armyd*, from Old French *armé*, past participle of *armer* (“to arm”), from Latin *armātus*, past participle of *armāre* (“to arm”), from *arma* (“weapons, arms”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ermos* (“joint, shoulder”).  Cognate with Spanish *armado*, Italian *armato*, Portuguese *armado*.
