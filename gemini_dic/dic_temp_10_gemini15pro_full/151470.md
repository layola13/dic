paroxysms:/ˈpærəkˌsɪzəmz/ /pəˈrɒksɪzəmz/| n. |阵发，突发，发作|A sudden attack or violent expression of a particular emotion or activity.|He broke into paroxysms of laughter. 他突然大笑起来。|近义词：spasms, fits, convulsions, outbursts|反义词:calm, peace, tranquility|词性:名词

词源: From Late Latin paroxysmus, from Greek paroxusmos “irritation, exasperation,” from paroxynein “to irritate, provoke,” from para- “beside” + oxys “sharp, acute.”
