panniers:/ˈpænɪəz/ | n. |驮篮，侧挂篮 |A pair of bags or baskets, typically of canvas or wicker, hung across the back of a pack animal or bicycle. |The donkey carried panniers laden with supplies. 驴子驮着装满补给的驮篮。| synonyms: baskets, bags, packs | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "container" in a broad sense if considering its function is to *not* distribute weight) | noun

panniers:/ˈpænɪəz/ | n. |裙撑，衬裙 |A woman's skirt, or part of a skirt, puffed out at the sides by hoops or padding, fashionable especially in the 18th century. |Her panniers made it difficult to fit through the narrow doorway. 她的裙撑让她很难穿过狭窄的门口。| synonyms: hoopskirt, bustle, farthingale | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "slim skirt") | noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *paniers*, plural of *panier* "bread basket," from Latin *panarium* "bread basket," from *panis* "bread."
