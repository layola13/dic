词源:  From Middle English *faunen, from Old French faonner (“to fawn”), from Vulgar Latin *fatuāre, from Latin fatuus (“foolish, silly”).  Originally referring to the behavior of a young deer towards its mother, the meaning extended to include servile behavior towards humans.
