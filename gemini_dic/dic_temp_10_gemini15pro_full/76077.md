furore:/ˈfjʊərɔːr/ (US) /ˈfjʊərɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. |骚动；轰动；狂怒；喧闹|An outbreak of public anger or excitement.|The book caused a furore in the literary world. 这本书在文学界引起了轰动。|近义词：uproar, commotion, outcry, stir, disturbance|反义词：calm, peace, tranquility|词性:名词

furore:/ˈfjʊərɔːr/ (US) /ˈfjʊərɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. |(尤指因某事不公正而引起的) 公愤，群情激愤|Public anger or excitement, especially caused by something that is thought to be unfair.|There was a public furore over the proposed tax increases.  公众对拟议中的增税方案表示强烈愤慨。|近义词：outrage, indignation, anger, resentment|反义词：acceptance, approval, contentment|词性:名词
