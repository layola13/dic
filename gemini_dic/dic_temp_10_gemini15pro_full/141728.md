oralist:/ˈɔːrəlɪst/| n.| 口语主义者；口语教学法倡导者|A person who advocates for the education of deaf or hard-of-hearing individuals through oral methods, emphasizing lip reading and speech, rather than sign language.|The oralist believed that sign language hindered the integration of deaf individuals into mainstream society.|近义词:speech therapist, audiologist|反义词:signer, manualist|词性:noun
