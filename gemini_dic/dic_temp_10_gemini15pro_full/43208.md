crabsidle:/ˈkræbˌsaɪdəl/ | adj. | 闲逛的，懒散的 | Moving sideways or in a halting or inefficient way, like a crab. Also refers to idling or wasting time, particularly in a leisurely, aimless manner. | Example: He spent the afternoon crabsidling along the beach. 他下午在海滩上闲逛。
Example: Stop crabsidling and get to work! 别闲着了，快去工作！| 近义词: dawdle, loiter, meander, procrastinate | 反义词: hasten, hurry, rush | 词性: adjective

词源:  A blend of "crab" and "idle."  "Crab" refers to the sideways movement of crabs, while "idle" means lazy or inactive. This word evokes a sense of aimless, meandering movement or inactivity.
