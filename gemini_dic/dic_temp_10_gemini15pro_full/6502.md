amulius: /əˈmjuːliəs/
noun
阿穆利乌斯
Amulius, in Roman mythology, was the younger brother of Numitor, king of Alba Longa. He seized the throne from his brother, killed Numitor’s male heirs, and forced Rhea Silvia, Numitor’s daughter, to become a Vestal Virgin so that she would not bear children who might challenge his rule.
Amulius usurped the throne from his brother Numitor. 阿穆利乌斯从他哥哥努米托尔手中篡夺了王位。
Rhea Silvia defied Amulius and gave birth to Romulus and Remus. 瑞亚·西尔维娅违抗了阿穆利乌斯，生下了罗慕路斯和雷姆斯。
synonym: usurper, tyrant
antonym: rightful king, legitimate ruler
