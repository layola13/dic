noumenal:/ˈnuːmɪnəl/| adj.| 本体论的；可感知的；直觉的| Relating to a thing as it is in itself, as distinct from a phenomenon or any other appearance.|  Kant's distinction between the phenomenal world and the noumenal world is central to his philosophy.  康德对现象世界和本体世界的区分是其哲学的核心。

The artist attempted to depict the noumenal realm in his abstract paintings.  这位艺术家试图在他的抽象画中描绘本体领域。|近义词：intelligible, a priori, transcendental|反义词：phenomenal, empirical, a posteriori|词性: adjective

词源:  From German *noumenal*, from Ancient Greek *νοούμενον* (noúmenon, “that which is apprehended by thought, object of thought”), neuter participle of *νοέω* (noéō, “I perceive, think, understand”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵneh₃- (“to know”).
