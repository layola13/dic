concelebrate:/kənˈsɛlɪbreɪt/
verb
共同庆祝；联合庆祝
to celebrate together, especially a religious rite.
The priests concelebrated the Mass. 神父们共同举行了弥撒。
近义词: celebrate jointly, co-officiate
反义词: mourn, grieve
词性: verb

concelebrate:/kənˈsɛlɪbreɪt/
词源:  From Late Latin *concelebrare, from con- + celebrare “to celebrate”
中文翻译: 共同庆祝；联合庆祝
英文解析: To celebrate (a Mass or other religious rite) jointly with other priests or ministers.  It emphasizes a shared celebration, particularly in a religious context.
例句: Several priests concelebrated the Mass.  几位神父共同举行了弥撒。
例句:  They concelebrated the anniversary of the church's founding. 他们共同庆祝了教堂成立周年纪念日。
近义词: co-officiate, celebrate together
反义词:  mourn, grieve
词性: verb
