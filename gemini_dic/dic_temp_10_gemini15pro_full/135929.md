nonpungency:/ˌnɒnpʌndʒənsi/ | n. | 无辛辣味；不刺激性 | The nonpungency of the pepper made it suitable for the mild salsa.  This pepper's lack of pungency makes it ideal for those with sensitive palates. | 例句：The nonpungency of the bell pepper was surprising.  这个甜椒竟然一点也不辣。 The chef appreciated the nonpungency of the oil, as it didn't overpower the delicate flavors of the dish.  厨师很欣赏这种油的不刺激性，因为它不会掩盖菜肴的精致风味。 | 近义词：mildness, blandness | 反义词：pungency, spiciness | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
non- (prefix): not, lacking  (from Latin)
pungency (noun): the quality of having a strong, sharp smell or taste that gives a burning sensation  (from Latin *pungentia, from pungent-, pungens 'pricking', present participle of pungere 'to prick')
-cy (suffix): forming nouns of quality or state  (from French -cie, -ce, from Latin -tia)
