hocked:/hɑːkt/ | v. (past tense and past participle of *hock*) | 典当，抵押 | to pawn or pledge something as security for a loan | I hocked my watch to get some cash. 我典当了我的手表来换些现金。 | pawn, pledge | redeem, reclaim | verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *hokken*, from Middle Dutch *hocken* (“to sit, crouch, perch”), related to *hake* (“hook”). The sense of "to pawn" likely arose from the image of being "hooked" or "caught" by a debt.
