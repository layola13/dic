nonchalance:/ˌnɒnʃəˈlɑːns/ (US) /ˌnɑːnʃəˈlɑːns/| n. | 冷漠；若无其事；冷静 | the state of being nonchalant; a casual lack of concern |  He greeted the news of his dismissal with surprising nonchalance. 他对被解雇的消息反应 surprisingly 冷静。| 近义词: indifference, apathy, coolness, composure | 反义词: anxiety, eagerness, excitement, nervousness | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From French *nonchalant*, present participle of *nonchaloir* "to be indifferent," from *non-* "not" + *chaloir* "to matter, concern," from Latin *calēre* "to be warm."
