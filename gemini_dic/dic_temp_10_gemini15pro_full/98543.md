instillation:/ˌɪnstəˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 安装；灌输；就任；滴注 | The act of installing something or the condition of being installed. The process of slowly introducing a liquid into a body.  |  The instillation of new software took several hours. 新软件的安装花了几个小时。 The instillation of new ideas into the students' minds was a slow process. 将新思想灌输到学生的头脑中是一个缓慢的过程. The instillation ceremony for the new president was held yesterday. 新总统的就任仪式于昨天举行。 The instillation of eye drops can be tricky. 滴眼液的滴注可能很棘手。 | 近义词: installation, inauguration, infusion, introduction | 反义词: removal, extraction | 词性: 名词

instil:/ɪnˈstɪl/ | vt. | 逐渐灌输；慢慢滴入 | To gradually introduce something into someone's mind. To put a liquid into something drop by drop. |  The coach instilled confidence in his players. 教练向他的球员灌输了信心。  She tried to instil a love of reading in her children. 她试图在她孩子心中灌输对阅读的热爱。 The doctor instilled the medication into the patient's ear. 医生将药物滴入病人的耳朵。 He carefully instilled the dye into the fabric. 他小心地将染料滴入织物中。 | 近义词: implant, infuse, inculcate, introduce, inject | 反义词: remove, extract | 词性: 动词
