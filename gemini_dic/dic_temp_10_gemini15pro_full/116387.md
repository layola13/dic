mastodonsaurus:/ˌmæstəˌdɒnəˈsɔːrəs/
|n.|乳齿螈|A genus of extinct temnospondyl amphibians that lived during the Middle Triassic period, characterized by their large size and flattened heads.|The Mastodonsaurus was a formidable predator in its time. 乳齿螈在其时代是一个可怕的掠食者。|近义词:temnospondyl|反义词:None|词性:noun

Etymology: From  *mastodon*  +  *-saurus*

*mastodon*: From Ancient Greek *μαστός* (mastós, “breast, nipple”) + *ὀδούς* (odoús, “tooth”).  Refers to the nipple-like projections on the crowns of their teeth.
*saurus*: From Ancient Greek *σαῦρος* (saûros, “lizard”). A common suffix used in the names of prehistoric reptiles and amphibians.
