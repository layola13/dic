counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/ (US) /ˈkaʊntəfɪt/ (UK)| adj. n. vt.|伪造的；仿造的；假冒的；赝品；伪造；仿造|Made in exact imitation of something valuable with the intention to deceive or defraud. |例句：The police seized a large quantity of counterfeit money. 警方缴获了大量假币。|近义词：fake, forged, spurious, bogus|反义词：genuine, authentic|词性: adjective

counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/ (US) /ˈkaʊntəfɪt/ (UK)| n.|赝品；伪造品|A fraudulent imitation of something else. |例句：The painting was a counterfeit. 这幅画是赝品。|近义词：fake, forgery, sham|反义词：original, genuine|词性: noun

counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/ (US) /ˈkaʊntəfɪt/ (UK)| vt.|伪造；仿造|To make a fraudulent copy of something. |例句：He counterfeited the signature on the check. 他伪造了支票上的签名。|近义词：forge, fake, imitate|反义词：verify, authenticate|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French contrefait, past participle of contrefaire "to imitate, copy," from contre- "against" + faire "to make, do."
