occupy: /ˈɒkjupaɪ/ (UK) /ˈɑːkjupaɪ/ (US)| v. | 占据，占用；居住；使忙碌 | to fill or use a space, an area, or an amount of time; to live or work in a building or place; to keep someone busy or interested | The building is occupied by a large insurance company. 这栋大楼被一家大型保险公司占据。| synonyms: inhabit, reside in, fill, take up | antonyms: vacate, abandon | verb

occupy: /ˈɒkjupaɪ/ (UK) /ˈɑːkjupaɪ/ (US)| v. | 攻占，占领 | to enter a place and take control of it by military force |  Enemy troops occupied the city. 敌军占领了这座城市。 | synonyms: seize, conquer, capture | antonyms: liberate, free | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *occuper*, from Latin *occupare* ("to seize, take possession of, occupy"), from *ob* ("towards, against") + *capere* ("to take").
