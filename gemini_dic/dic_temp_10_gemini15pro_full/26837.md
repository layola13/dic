caimacam:/kaɪˈmækəm/ | n. | 地方行政长官；代理官员 | A government official, especially in Turkey and some other countries, who acts as a deputy or substitute. | The caimacam ruled the province in the sultan's absence.  地方行政长官在苏丹不在的时候统治着这个省。

The young caimacam was known for his fairness.  这位年轻的地方行政长官以公平著称。 | Synonyms: deputy, lieutenant, governor, prefect | Antonyms: ruler, sovereign | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Turkish *kaymakam*, from Arabic قائم مقام (*qāʾim maqām*) "one who stands in place (of another), deputy".
