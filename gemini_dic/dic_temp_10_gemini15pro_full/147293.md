overmodest:/ˌoʊvərˈmɑːdɪst/ | adj. | 过分谦虚的，过分谦逊的 | Excessively modest; unduly humble.  |  She was overmodest about her achievements, attributing her success to luck. 她对自己的成就过分谦虚，把成功归因于运气。 |  例句：He was overmodest about his contribution to the project. 他对他在项目中的贡献过分谦虚。 | 近义词：humble, self-effacing, unassuming | 反义词：boastful, arrogant, conceited | 词性：形容词

overmodest:/ˌoʊvərˈmɑːdɪst/ | adj. | 过于谦逊的 | Too humble or shy about one's abilities or achievements. | He gave an overmodest account of his own contribution. 他过于谦虚地描述了他自己的贡献。 | 例句：It would be overmodest to say I played a small part; in fact, I was central to the project. 说我只扮演了一个小角色就太谦虚了；事实上，我是这个项目的核心人物。 | 近义词：humble, self-deprecating | 反义词：arrogant, boastful | 词性：形容词


Etymology:
From over- + modest.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much." "Modest" comes from the Latin word "modestus," meaning "moderate" or "keeping within measure."
