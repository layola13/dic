oversteadiness:/ˌoʊvərˈstɛdinəs/| n.| 过于稳定; 过于沉稳; 过于坚定|A state of being excessively steady, calm, and unwavering, often to the point of being inflexible or unresponsive to changing circumstances.|His oversteadiness in the face of crisis became a liability, preventing him from adapting to the rapidly evolving situation. 他在危机面前过于沉稳，反而成了累赘，使他无法适应迅速变化的局势。|近义词：inflexibility, rigidity, stubbornness|反义词：adaptability, flexibility, responsiveness|词性:名词

Etymology:
"over-" (prefix):  excessive, beyond normal limits. From Old English *ofer-, from Proto-Germanic *uberi (over, above, beyond).
"steadiness":  the quality of being steady; firmness of character or purpose. From "steady" + "-ness".  "Steady" comes from Old English *stede (firm, fixed, constant), from Proto-Germanic *stadiz.
