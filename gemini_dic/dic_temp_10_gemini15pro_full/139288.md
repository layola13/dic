obliterate:/əˈblɪtəreɪt/ | v. | 消灭，抹去，摧毁 | to remove or destroy all traces of; do away with completely | The bombing raid obliterated the entire city block.  轰炸袭击摧毁了整个街区。| synonyms: destroy, erase, annihilate, expunge | antonyms: create, build, preserve, establish | verb
obliteration:/əˌblɪtəˈreɪʃn/ | n. | 消灭，抹杀，摧毁 | the act or process of obliterating something | The obliteration of the rainforest is a global tragedy.  雨林的消失是一场全球性的悲剧。| synonyms: destruction, erasure, annihilation | antonyms: creation, preservation, establishment | noun
obliterator:/əˈblɪtəreɪtər/ | n. | 毁灭者，清除器 | a person or thing that obliterates something; a device that destroys documents by shredding or incinerating them | The company uses a high-security obliterator for sensitive documents.  公司使用高安全性销毁器来处理敏感文件。| synonyms: destroyer, eraser, annihilator | antonyms: creator, preserver, builder | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin obliteratus, past participle of obliterare (“to erase, blot out, efface”), from ob- (“over, upon”) + literatus (“lettered, written upon”), from litera (“letter”).
