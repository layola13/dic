contempt:/kənˈtempt/ | n. | 轻蔑，蔑视；藐视法庭 | The feeling that a person or a thing is beneath consideration, worthless, or deserving scorn. | He showed his contempt for the rules by ignoring them. 他对这些规则不屑一顾，直接无视了它们。 | synonyms: scorn, disdain, disrespect | antonyms: respect, admiration | noun

contempts:/kənˈtempts/ | n. | 藐视法庭（ contempt 的复数）| Instances of the offense of being disobedient to or disrespectful of a court of law. |  He was found in contempt of court for refusing to testify.  他因拒绝作证而被判藐视法庭。| synonyms: disobediences,  violations (in legal context)| antonyms:  obediences, compliances (in legal context) | noun
