clamour:/ˈklæmə(r)/ (US) /ˈklæmə(r)/ (UK)| n. vi. |喧嚷，吵闹；强烈要求 |A loud and persistent outcry, especially in demand or protest; to make a loud and persistent outcry.| There was a great clamour for tickets.  大家强烈要求买票。| The crowd clamoured for his release.  人群大声疾呼要求释放他。|近义词：outcry, uproar, commotion, demand, protest |反义词: silence, quiet |词性: noun, verb

词源: From Middle English *clamouren, from Anglo-Norman clamur, clamourer, from Latin clamor, from Proto-Indo-European *kele- (“to shout”). Cognate with Old French clamur, clamor, Italian clamore, Spanish clamor.
