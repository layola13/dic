over-grades:/ˌoʊvərˈɡreɪdz/ | v. | 给予过高的评价；过高估计；高估 | To assign a higher grade or value to something than it deserves. | The teacher over-graded the student's essay because she liked his writing style. 老师因为喜欢这个学生的写作风格，所以给他的作文打了过高的分数。|  The antique dealer over-graded the value of the vase, hoping to make a larger profit. 古董商高估了花瓶的价值，希望能赚取更大的利润。 | overestimate, overvalue, inflate | underestimate, undervalue, downgrade | verb

over-grades:/ˌoʊvərˈɡreɪdz/ | n. | 过高的评价；过高的等级 |  A grade or rank that is too high. |  The over-grades given by the professor resulted in grade inflation. 教授给出的过高分数导致了分数膨胀。 | The company's stock price reflected the over-grades it had received from analysts. 该公司的股价反映了分析师对其过高的评价。 | overestimation, overvaluation, inflation | underestimation, undervaluation, downgrade | noun
