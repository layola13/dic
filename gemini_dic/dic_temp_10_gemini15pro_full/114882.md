mammy:/ˈmæmi/| n.|妈妈；(尤指黑人小孩称呼的)保姆|A term of endearment for mother, or, historically, a Black woman who worked as a nanny or servant, especially in the Southern US. This latter usage is now considered offensive due to its association with slavery and racist stereotypes.|例句：The child called for his mammy. 孩子叫他的妈妈。

例句：In the past, some white families employed Black women as mammies. 过去，一些白人家庭雇佣黑人妇女当保姆。(This example demonstrates the historical usage but highlights its problematic nature.)

|近义词：mom, mother, momma, (historically and offensively) nanny, nursemaid|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性: noun
