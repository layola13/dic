manipulate:/məˈnɪpjuleɪt/ /məˈnɪpjəleɪt/
| vt. | 操纵，控制；处理，运用 | To control or influence something or someone, often in a skillful or devious way; to handle or use something skillfully. | He manipulated the stock market to his advantage. 他操纵股市为自己牟利。 |  control, influence, exploit, maneuver | assist, help, support | verb

manipulative:/məˈnɪpjələtɪv/ /məˈnɪpjələtɪv/
| adj. |  善于操纵的，控制欲强的 |  Exercising or involving manipulation; tending to control or influence others in a skillful or devious way. | She's very manipulative and always gets what she wants. 她很会操纵别人，总是能得到她想要的。 | controlling, scheming, exploitative | honest, straightforward, forthright | adjective

manipulation:/məˌnɪpjʊˈleɪʃn/ /məˌnɪpjəˈleɪʃn/
| n. | 操纵，控制；处理，运用 | The action of manipulating something in a skillful manner; the action of controlling or influencing someone or something, often in a dishonest way. | The manipulation of public opinion is a key factor in election campaigns.  操纵公众舆论是竞选活动中的一个关键因素。 | control, influence, exploitation | honesty, fairness, support | noun
