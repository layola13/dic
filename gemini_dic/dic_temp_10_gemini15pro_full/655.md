abrogate:/ˈæbrəɡeɪt/| vt.|废除，取消|to formally put an end to (a law, agreement, or custom)|The treaty was abrogated in 1998. 该条约于1998年被废除。

The government abrogated the agreement. 政府废除了这项协议。


|近义词：repeal, revoke, annul, nullify, abolish|反义词: enact, establish, institute, ratify|verb|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin abrogare ("to repeal, annul a law"), from ab- ("away from") + rogare ("to ask, propose a law").  The original sense was "to propose the repeal of a law," later simply "to repeal."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government abrogates outdated laws. = 政府废除过时的法律。
He abrogates his responsibility as a father. = 他放弃了作为父亲的责任。

一般过去时:
The king abrogated the decree. = 国王废除了法令。
They abrogated the contract last year. = 他们去年废除了合同。

现在完成时:
The company has abrogated several agreements this year. = 今年公司已经废除了几项协议。
Congress has abrogated the old tax law. = 国会已经废除了旧的税法。

过去完成时:
The law had been abrogated before the incident occurred. = 该法律在事件发生之前已被废除。
They had abrogated the treaty before the war began. = 他们在战争开始前废除了条约。

将来时:
The council will abrogate the bylaw next month. = 理事会下个月将废除该细则。
They will abrogate the contract if the terms are not met. = 如果不满足条件，他们将废除合同。


被动语态:
The law was abrogated by the government. = 该法律被政府废除了。
The treaty has been abrogated. = 该条约已被废除。


疑问句:

否定句:
The court did not abrogate the law. = 法院没有废除该法律。
They haven't abrogated the contract yet. = 他们还没有废除合同。


PHRASE->
