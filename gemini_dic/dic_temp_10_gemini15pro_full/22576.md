bootie:/ˈbuːti/ (US) /ˈbuːti/ (UK)| n. |短靴; 赃物; 战利品|A short boot, often worn by women or children. Can also refer to spoils or loot, especially in a playful or humorous context.|She bought a new pair of suede booties for the fall. 她秋天买了一双新的绒面革短靴。|The pirates divided their booties after raiding the ship.  海盗们洗劫了船只后分了赃物。|近义词:boot, ankle boot, shootie|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "sandal" for a contrasting footwear type)|noun
