bacteria:/bækˈtɪəriə/ (plural) /bækˈtɪəriəm/ (singular)| n.|细菌|Microscopic single-celled organisms that exist in vast numbers in virtually all environments, including soil, water, and the bodies of plants and animals. Some bacteria cause disease, while others are beneficial.|例句：Bacteria can cause food poisoning. 细菌会导致食物中毒。|例句：Beneficial bacteria are essential for healthy digestion. 有益细菌对健康消化至关重要。|近义词：microbes, germs|反义词:None (Bacteria are a specific type of organism, not a concept with a direct opposite.)|词性:Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From New Latin bacterium, from Ancient Greek baktērion (βακτήριον, “small staff, rod”), diminutive of baktron (βάκτρον, “staff, cane, club”).  The name was chosen because the first bacteria observed were rod-shaped.
