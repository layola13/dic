anthracite-coals:/ˈænθrəsaɪt koʊlz/| n.|无烟煤|A hard, compact variety of coal that has a high luster and burns with little flame or smoke. It is the highest rank of coal.|The blacksmith used anthracite coals in his forge because they burned hot and clean.|Synonyms: hard coal, black diamond, anthracite|Antonyms: lignite, peat|Noun
