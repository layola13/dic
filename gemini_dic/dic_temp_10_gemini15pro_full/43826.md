creolized:/ˈkriːəlaɪzd/| adj.|克里奥尔化的，混合的|Relating to or denoting a language or culture that has emerged from the mixing of different languages and cultures, typically with a European language as a base.|例句：Creole languages often have simplified grammar compared to their parent languages.  克里奥尔语的语法通常比其母语简单。

例句：Jamaican Patois is a well-known creolized form of English.  牙买加方言是英语的一种著名的克里奥尔化形式。|近义词：mixed, hybrid, blended|反义词：pure, unmixed|词性:形容词
Etymology: From French *créoliser*, from *créole* (see Creole).
