ort:/ɔːrt/| n.| scrap, fragment, leftover |残羹剩饭，碎屑，零碎| The pigs ate the orts from the table. 猪吃了桌子上的残羹剩饭。| synonyms: scraps, leftovers, remnants | antonyms: whole, complete | noun

Etymology: From Middle English *ort(e), from Old English *ort, from Proto-Germanic *uzdaz (“that which sticks out, point”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ews- (“out”). Cognate with Old Frisian ort (“point, place”), Old Saxon ord (“point, beginning, origin”), Old High German ort (“point, place, beginning”), Old Norse oddr (“point of a weapon”), Gothic 𐌰𐌿𐌳𐍃 (aúds, “wealth, goods”). More at odd.
