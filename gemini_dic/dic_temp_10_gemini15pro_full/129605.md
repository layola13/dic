non-concur:/ˌnɑːnkənˈkɜːr/ | v. | 不一致，不同意，不赞成 | To disagree or not approve, especially formally. | The judge non-concurred with the jury's verdict. 法官不同意陪审团的裁决。

The board non-concurred with the CEO's proposal. 董事会不同意首席执行官的提案。

| 近义词：disagree, dissent, object | 反义词：agree, concur, assent | 词性: 动词

Etymology: From the Latin "non" (not) + "concurrere" (to run together, agree).  "Non-" is a prefix indicating negation, and "concurrere" is formed from "com-" (together) + "currere" (to run).  Thus, the literal meaning is "not to run together" or "not to agree."
