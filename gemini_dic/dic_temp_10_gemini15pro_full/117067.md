me-tooer:/miːˈtuːər/
|n.|我也是（性骚扰或性侵犯的）受害者；参与“我也是”运动的人|A person who comes forward with an allegation of sexual harassment or assault, often in response to similar allegations made by others.|例句：Many me-tooers shared their stories online. 许多“我也是”运动的参与者在网上分享了他们的故事。|例句：The me-tooer bravely spoke out against her abuser.  这位“我也是”运动的参与者勇敢地站出来指控了她的施暴者。|近义词：victim, survivor, accuser|反义词:perpetrator, abuser|词性:名词

Etymology:
From the phrase "me too," used to express solidarity with victims of sexual harassment and assault, which gained widespread popularity as a hashtag (#MeToo) on social media in 2017. The suffix "-er" is added to form a noun referring to a person who identifies with the movement.
