devious:/ˈdiːviəs/| adj.|迂回的，曲折的；不光明正大的，狡猾的|straying from a straight course; not straightforward; shifty or crooked|He took a devious route to avoid the traffic. 他绕道而行以避开交通拥堵。|synonyms: cunning, sly, deceitful, underhanded, indirect|antonyms: straightforward, honest, direct|adjective

devious:/ˈdiːviəs/| adj.|狡猾的，诡计多端的|showing a skillful use of underhanded tactics to achieve goals.|The spy used devious methods to infiltrate the enemy's headquarters.  这个间谍用狡猾的方法潜入了敌人的总部。|synonyms:  crafty, wily, scheming, tricky|antonyms:  artless, guileless, ingenuous, straight|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *deviōsus, from Latin devius (“out of the way, remote”) +‎ -ōsus. Equivalent to de- +‎ via (“way, road”) +‎ -ous.
