hypermetropia:/ˌhaɪpərˈməˈtrəʊpiə/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpərˈmiːtroʊpiə/ (US)| n. |远视|A condition in which distant objects are seen clearly but near objects appear blurred because the image comes to a focus behind the retina.|She was diagnosed with hypermetropia and needs glasses to read.|Nearsightedness (myopia)|Presbyopia|noun
hyperopia:/ˌhaɪpərˈoʊpiə/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈrəʊpɪə/ (UK)| n. |远视|Same as hypermetropia.  A condition in which distant objects are seen clearly but near objects appear blurred.|He has hyperopia and wears contact lenses.|Nearsightedness (myopia)|Presbyopia|noun
