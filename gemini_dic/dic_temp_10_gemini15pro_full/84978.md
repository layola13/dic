happiest:/ˈhæpiɪst/ | adj. | 最幸福的 |  Superlative form of "happy," meaning experiencing the greatest degree of joy or contentment. |  This is the happiest day of my life. 这是我一生中最快乐的一天。| My daughter looked happiest when she was playing with her friends.  我女儿和朋友们玩耍时看起来最开心。 |  Most joyful, most cheerful, most content | Saddest, most miserable | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *happiest*, superlative of *happie*, from Old Norse *heppinn* ("fortunate, lucky"), ultimately from Proto-Germanic *happuz*.

将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
This tense is rarely used with "happiest" because it describes an ongoing state leading up to a future point, and "happiest" refers to a peak state.  It's hard to imagine a natural context for this.

被动语态:  "Happiest" is not typically used in the passive voice.
