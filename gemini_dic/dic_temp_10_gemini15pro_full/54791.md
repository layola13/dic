dispensation:/ˌdɪspenˈseɪʃn/ | n. | 分配；豁免；免除；特许；（上帝的）安排；| An act of dispensing with a rule, law, or other requirement; Exemption from a rule or usual requirement;  A system of order, government, or organization of a nation, community, etc., especially by God.|  The teacher gave me a dispensation from homework because I was sick. 老师因为我生病免除了我的家庭作业。| synonyms: exemption, immunity, exception, remission, release| antonyms: obligation, requirement, restriction, duty| noun

dispensation:/ˌdɪspenˈseɪʃn/ | n. | 分配；分发；施予；| The act of distributing something or giving something out; | The government is responsible for the dispensation of justice. 政府负责司法的执行。| synonyms: distribution, allocation, delivery, issuance| antonyms: collection, gathering, withholding, retention| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English dispensacioun, from Old French dispensacion, from Late Latin dispensātiō, dispensātiōnem, from Latin dispensāre (“to dispense”), from dis- (“apart, away”) + pendere (“to weigh, pay”).
