annexing:/əˈnɛksɪŋ/
v.
吞并，兼并
The larger country annexed the smaller one after the war.
战后，较大的国家吞并了较小的国家。
Taking possession of (something, especially territory) by force or without justification.
通过武力或无正当理由占有（某物，尤指领土）。
Russia's annexing of Crimea was widely condemned.
俄罗斯吞并克里米亚的行为受到广泛谴责。
近义词:seizing, appropriating, conquering, grabbing
反义词:ceding, relinquishing, surrendering
词性:动词

annex:/ˈænɛks/
n.
附件，附属物
The annex to the building provided extra office space.
大楼的附属建筑提供了额外的办公空间。
A building joined to or associated with a main building, providing additional space or accommodations.
连接到主建筑物或与主建筑物相关的建筑物，提供额外的空间或住宿。
The historical documents are kept in an annex to the library.
历史文献保存在图书馆的附属建筑中。
近义词:addition, extension, appendage, supplement
反义词:main building, core
词性:名词

词源: From Latin *annexus*, past participle of *annectere* "to bind to," from *ad-* "to" + *nectere* "to bind."
