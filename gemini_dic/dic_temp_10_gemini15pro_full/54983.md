disprovable:/dɪsˈpruːvəbl/ |adj.|可证伪的，可反驳的|Able to be proven false or wrong.| The scientist proposed a disprovable theory, allowing for rigorous testing.  这位科学家提出了一个可证伪的理论，允许进行严格的测试。| Example:  His claim was easily disprovable with a simple experiment. 他的说法很容易用一个简单的实验来反驳。|Synonyms: refutable, falsifiable|Antonyms: irrefutable, unassailable|adjective

disprove:/ˌdɪsˈpruːv/ |v.|反驳，证明…是虚假的|To prove that something is false or wrong.|The lawyer disproved the witness's testimony. 律师反驳了证人的证词。| Example:  New evidence disproved the initial theory. 新证据反驳了最初的理论。|Synonyms: refute, debunk, invalidate|Antonyms: prove, confirm, validate|verb

词源: dis- (not) + prove (to establish the truth of)
