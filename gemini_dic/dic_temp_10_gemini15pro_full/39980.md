consul:/ˈkɒnsəl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsəl/ (US)| n.|领事;  (古罗马的)执政官|An official appointed by a government to live in a foreign city and protect and promote the government's citizens and interests there.  A chief magistrate of the Roman Republic.|The American consul in Paris helped me when my passport was stolen.  巴黎的美国领事在我护照被盗时帮助了我。|Synonyms: diplomat, envoy, representative|Antonyms: (For Roman consul) citizen, plebeian|noun
