hocus-pocussing:/ˌhoʊkəsˈpoʊkəsɪŋ/
|n.| 故弄玄虚，花招，欺骗|The act of performing hocus-pocus; deceptive tricks or practices, often involving meaningless rituals or words.|He was accused of hocus-pocussing the investors out of their money. 他被指控用花言巧语骗取投资者的钱。|近义词：trickery, deception, chicanery, sleight of hand, conjuring|反义词：honesty, truthfulness|词性：名词 (Gerund or present participle of the verb "hocus-pocus")


hocus-pocus:/ˌhoʊkəsˈpoʊkəs/
|n. | 花招，戏法，咒语|Meaningless words or rituals used in conjuring or trickery.|The magician's hocus-pocus amazed the children. 魔术师的戏法使孩子们惊叹。|近义词：trickery, deception, magic, sleight of hand, conjuring|反义词：reality, truth|词性：名词


hocus-pocus:/ˌhoʊkəsˈpoʊkəs/
|vt.| 用花招欺骗|To deceive or trick someone using meaningless words or rituals.|He hocus-pocused the crowd into believing he had magical powers. 他用花招欺骗人群相信他拥有魔力。|近义词：trick, deceive, cheat, swindle, con|反义词：be honest, tell the truth|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the pseudo-Latin *hocus pocus*, probably a mock ecclesiastical formula invented by jugglers. First recorded in the early 17th century.
