clinkstone:/ˈklɪŋkˌstoʊn/| n. |响石，火山岩的一种|A hard, grayish-green or bluish-gray volcanic rock that rings when struck.  It is a type of phonolite.|The geologist identified the sample as clinkstone due to its distinctive ringing sound. 地质学家根据其独特的响声将样品鉴定为响石。|Synonyms: phonolite, basalt, trachyte|Antonyms: N/A|noun

Etymology: From "clink" (referring to the sound) + "stone."  "Clink" comes from Middle Dutch "klinken," meaning "to ring."
