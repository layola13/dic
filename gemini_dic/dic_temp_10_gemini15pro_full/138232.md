nosophobia:/ˌnoʊsəˈfoʊbiə/ | n. | 恐病症，疾病恐惧症 | An abnormal and persistent fear of disease or illness. It can manifest in various ways, from excessive handwashing to avoiding medical appointments. |  She suffered from nosophobia and was constantly worried about catching a cold. 她患有恐病症，总是担心会感冒。

| Example:  He developed nosophobia after witnessing a serious illness in his family.  他目睹了家人患上重病后，患上了恐病症。


| Synonym:  pathophobia,  hypochondria,  disease phobia | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but consider)  fearlessness,  bravery | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *nosos* (disease) + *-phobia* (fear)
