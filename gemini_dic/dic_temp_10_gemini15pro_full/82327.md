groveler:/ˈɡrɒvlə(r) (British), ˈɡrɑːvlər (American)| n. |卑躬屈膝的人，阿谀奉承者|Someone who behaves in a subservient or demeaning way, often to gain favor or avoid punishment.  A person who grovels.|The emperor was surrounded by grovelers, all vying for his favor. 皇帝身边都是些阿谀奉承的人，每个人都争先恐后地想要得到他的宠幸。|Synonyms: sycophant, flatterer, toady, fawner, lickspittle|Antonyms: rebel, nonconformist, individualist|noun
