freneticism:/frəˈnɛtɪsɪzəm/
n.
狂热，疯狂，极度兴奋
A state of intense activity and excitement.
The freneticism of the stock market during the boom years led to many unwise investments.  股市在繁荣时期的狂热导致许多不明智的投资。
The freneticism of the city never ceases to amaze me.  这座城市的喧嚣让我惊叹不已。
frenzy, mania, hysteria, wildness
calm, peacefulness, tranquility


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *frénétique* "frantic," from Late Latin *phreneticus*, from Greek *phrenitikos* "suffering from delirium, insane," from *phren* "mind, heart, diaphragm."  The suffix *-ism* forms nouns of condition or doctrine.
