disenfranchise: /ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ | vt. |剥夺公民权，使失去权利| To deprive someone of the right to vote, or more generally, to deprive someone of a right or privilege. |例句：The new law disenfranchised many voters.  新法律剥夺了许多选民的投票权。

例句：  They felt disenfranchised by the political system. 他们感到被政治体制剥夺了权利。


近义词： marginalize, dispossess, deprive |反义词: empower, enfranchise |词性: verb


disenfranchising: /ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzɪŋ/ | adj. |剥夺公民权的，使人失去权利的| Depriving of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote. |例句：The disenfranchising effects of the new policy were felt immediately. 这项新政策的剥夺权利的影响立即显现出来。

例句：It's a disenfranchising feeling to have no say in decisions that affect your life.  在影响你生活的决策中没有发言权，这种感觉让人感到被剥夺了权利。

近义词： marginalizing, dispossessing, depriving |反义词: empowering, enfranchising |词性: adjective


词源: From dis- "not, opposite of" + enfranchise (from Old French enfranchir "to set free").
