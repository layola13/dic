harrowers:/ˈhærəʊərz/ | n. | 耙地者；折磨者；掠夺者 | People who harrow, either literally (as with a farm implement) or figuratively (causing distress). | The harrowers worked tirelessly in the fields. 耙地者不知疲倦地在田里工作。| The harrowers of the conquered lands took everything they could. 征服土地的掠夺者拿走了他们所能拿走的一切。 | Synonyms: tormentors, oppressors, plunderers, raiders | Antonyms: comforters, protectors, benefactors | noun

harrowing:/ˈhærəʊɪŋ/ | adj. | 悲惨的；令人痛苦的 | Extremely distressing or agonizing. |  It was a harrowing experience to witness the accident. 目睹这场事故是一次痛苦的经历。 |  The harrowing tales of war survivors moved the audience to tears. 战争幸存者悲惨的故事使观众感动得流下了眼泪。| Synonyms: distressing, agonizing, traumatic, torturous | Antonyms: comforting, soothing, pleasant, enjoyable | adjective
