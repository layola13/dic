bluffing:/ˈblʌfɪŋ/ | n. | 虚张声势，吓唬 | The act of deceiving someone by pretending to be stronger or more confident than one actually is. | He won the game by bluffing. 他靠虚张声势赢得了比赛。| synonyms:  deception, feint, pretense | antonyms: honesty, truthfulness | noun
bluff:/blʌf/ | v. | 虚张声势，吓唬 | To deceive someone by pretending to be stronger or more confident than one actually is. | She bluffed her way through the interview. 她靠虚张声势蒙混过关了面试。| synonyms: deceive, feign, pretend | antonyms: be honest, tell the truth | verb
bluff:/blʌf/ | adj. |  直率的，坦率的;陡峭的 |  Direct and outspoken in manner; rising steeply with a broad flat front. | He has a bluff and hearty manner. 他举止直率而热情。  The coastline is characterized by bluff cliffs. 海岸线的特点是陡峭的悬崖。 | synonyms: frank, candid, abrupt; steep, sheer | antonyms: subtle, indirect; sloping, gradual | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From earlier bluff (n.) "act of deceiving," of uncertain origin, perhaps from Dutch bluffen "to boast, brag," related to Middle Dutch blaffen "to bark, brag."
