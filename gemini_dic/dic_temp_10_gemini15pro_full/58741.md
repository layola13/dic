dungan:/ˈdʌŋɡən/| n. | 东干人；东干语 |A member of a group of Muslims of Chinese origin, living mainly in Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, and Uzbekistan, or their language, a form of Mandarin Chinese written in Cyrillic.|  The Dungans have a rich cultural heritage. 东干人有着丰富的文化遗产。|  He is studying the Dungan language. 他正在学习东干语。| 近义词: Hui (sometimes used interchangeably but not entirely accurate) | 反义词:  (No direct antonym exists for an ethnic group or language.) | 词性:noun


Etymology->
From Russian дунга́не (dungáne),  ultimately from Turkic *dōŋgan (“east”), related to Uyghur تۇڭقان‎ (tungghan, “east”).  The term originally referred to Chinese Muslims who migrated to Central Asia, primarily from Gansu and Shaanxi provinces, in the 19th century.
