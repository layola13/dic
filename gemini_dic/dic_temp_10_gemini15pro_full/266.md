abductions: /əbˈdʌkʃənz/
n.
绑架，劫持，诱拐
the action or an instance of forcibly taking someone away against their will.
The abductions of several prominent businessmen have sparked fear in the community.  社区内几名杰出商人的绑架事件引发了恐慌。

The police are investigating a series of abductions in the area. 警方正在调查该地区发生的一系列绑架事件。

近义词: kidnappings, seizures, snatchings
反义词: releases, liberations
词性: 名词(plural)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin abductionem, accusative of abductio (“a carrying away”), from abducere (“to lead away”), from ab- (“away from”) + ducere (“to lead”).

USAGE->
There have been several abductions reported in the news recently. 最近新闻报道了几起绑架事件。

The abductions of the children remain unsolved.  孩子们的绑架案仍未破案。

The number of abductions has increased in recent years. 近年来绑架案数量有所增加。

The government is taking steps to prevent future abductions. 政府正在采取措施防止未来发生绑架事件。

The abductions were carried out by a well-organized criminal gang. 这些绑架事件是由一个组织严密的犯罪团伙实施的。

The victims of the abductions were released unharmed. 绑架案的受害者安然无恙地获释。

PHRASE->
a series of abductions 一系列绑架事件
the victims of the abductions 绑架案的受害者
