augur:/ˈɔːɡər/ (US) /ˈɔːɡə(r)/ (UK)| v. |预示，预兆 |To be a sign of something that will probably happen in the future. |例句：The high turnout at the rally augured well for the election. 集会上高投票率预示着选举结果会很好。

例句：Dark clouds augur a storm. 乌云预示着暴风雨。|近义词：portend, presage, foretell, foreshadow|反义词：belie|词性：动词

augured:/ˈɔːɡərd/ (US) /ˈɔːɡəd/ (UK)| v. |预示，预兆（augur的过去式和过去分词） |Past tense and past participle of "augur". |例句：The economic downturn augured a difficult year ahead. 经济衰退预示着来年将是艰难的一年。

例句：The sudden silence augured ill. 突如其来的沉默预示着不祥。|近义词：portended, presaged, foretold, foreshadowed|反义词：belied|词性：动词
