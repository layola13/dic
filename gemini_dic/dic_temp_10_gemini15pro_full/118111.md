mennonites:/ˈmɛnəˌnaɪts/ | n. | 门诺派教徒 | Members of a Protestant Christian sect originating in the 16th century during the Radical Reformation.  They are known for their pacifism, simple living, and emphasis on community. |  The Mennonites in this community are known for their beautiful quilts. 这个社区的门诺派教徒以他们美丽的被子而闻名。|  Synonyms: Anabaptists, Amish (partially), Brethren | Antonyms: None (Difficult to have a direct antonym for a religious group) | noun

mennonite:/ˈmɛnəˌnaɪt/ | adj. | 门诺派的 | Relating to or characteristic of Mennonites. |  She wore traditional Mennonite clothing. 她穿着传统的门诺派服装。| Synonyms: Anabaptist | Antonyms: None | adjective
