moonshine:/ˈmuːnʃaɪn/ | n. | 私酿酒；月光 | Illicitly distilled or smuggled liquor. Also, moonlight. | The police raided the shack and confiscated gallons of moonshine. 警察突袭了小屋，没收了几加仑的私酿酒。

moonshine:/ˈmuːnʃaɪn/ | v. |  从事走私活动；发出月光 | To engage in the illicit distillation or smuggling of liquor. Also, to shine like moonlight. | He moonshined to make ends meet during the Depression.  他在大萧条时期靠走私酒为生。


moonshine:/ˈmuːnʃaɪn/ | n. | 空话；废话 | Foolish or empty talk. | His promises were all moonshine. 他的承诺都是空话。

moonshine:/ˈmuːnʃaɪn/ | v. |  说空话，说废话 | To talk foolishly or emptily. | Stop moonshining and get to work! 别胡扯了，开始工作吧！



近义词: (For liquor) hooch, white lightning, bootleg. (For moonlight)  luster, glow. (For nonsense)  balderdash, claptrap.
反义词: (For nonsense) sense, truth.
词性: noun, verb

词源: From moon + shine, referring to the practice of making illegal liquor at night.
