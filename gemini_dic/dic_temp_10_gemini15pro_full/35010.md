cloned:/kloʊnd/ (US), /kləʊnd/ (UK)| adj.|克隆的；复制的|Produced asexually from a single ancestor, such that the offspring is genetically identical to the parent.|例句：The scientists cloned the sheep. 科学家们克隆了这只羊。|例句：The cloned plant grew quickly. 克隆植物生长迅速。|近义词：copied, duplicated, replicated|反义词:original, natural|词性:形容词

cloned:/kloʊnd/ (US), /kləʊnd/ (UK)| v. |克隆；复制|To create a copy of (a gene, DNA fragment, cell, or organism).|例句：They cloned the gene responsible for the disease. 他们克隆了导致这种疾病的基因。|例句：Scientists are working on cloning organs for transplant. 科学家们正在研究克隆器官用于移植。|近义词：copy, duplicate, replicate|反义词:|词性:动词

cloned:/kloʊnd/ (US), /kləʊnd/ (UK)| n. |克隆体；复制品|An organism or cell, or group of organisms or cells, produced asexually from one ancestor or stock, to which they are genetically identical.|例句：The clone was identical to its parent. 这个克隆体和它的母体一模一样。|例句：The clones were grown in a laboratory. 这些克隆体是在实验室里培育的。|近义词：copy, duplicate, replica|反义词:original|词性:名词
