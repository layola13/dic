chromogen:/ˈkroʊmədʒən/| n. |色原, 色素原|A substance that can be converted into a dye or pigment.|The pathologist used a chromogen to stain the tissue sample so it could be viewed under a microscope. 病理学家使用色原对组织样本进行染色，以便在显微镜下观察。|近义词：precursor, chromophore |反义词: N/A |词性: noun

Etymology:
From  chromo- (color) + -gen (producing). Ultimately from Greek *khrōma* (color) and *genos* (birth, race, kind).
