acquit:/əˈkwɪt/| vt.|宣判无罪，表现；履行；清偿|to free someone from a criminal charge by a verdict of not guilty; to conduct oneself or perform in a specified way|The jury acquitted the defendant of all charges. 陪审团宣判被告所有罪名不成立。

He acquitted himself well in the difficult situation. 他在困境中表现出色。|近义词：absolve, exonerate, clear, vindicate|反义词:convict, condemn, incriminate|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *acquiter, from a- (intensive prefix) + quite (free, clear). Ultimately from Latin quietus (at rest, quiet).

USAGE->
Present Tense:
The jury acquits the defendant. = 陪审团宣判被告无罪。
She always acquits herself with grace. = 她总是举止优雅。

Past Tense:
The judge acquitted the suspect due to lack of evidence. = 由于缺乏证据，法官宣判嫌疑人无罪。
He acquitted himself bravely in battle. = 他在战斗中表现勇敢。

Present Perfect Tense:
The court has acquitted several people in this case. = 法院在此案中宣判了几个人无罪。
She has acquitted herself admirably in her new role. = 她出色地完成了她的新角色。

Past Perfect Tense:
The jury had acquitted the man before new evidence emerged. = 陪审团在新的证据出现之前已经宣判了该男子无罪。
He had acquitted himself honorably in previous assignments. = 他在之前的任务中表现出色。

Future Tense:
The lawyer believes the jury will acquit his client. = 律师相信陪审团会宣判他的客户无罪。
I hope I will acquit myself well in the upcoming competition. = 我希望我在即将到来的比赛中表现出色。

Subjunctive Mood:
If they were to acquit him, there would be public outcry. = 如果他们宣判他无罪，公众将会强烈抗议。
I wish the court would acquit the innocent man. = 我希望法院能宣判这个无辜的人无罪。

Passive Voice:
The suspect was acquitted of all charges. = 嫌疑人被宣判所有罪名不成立。
He was acquitted by the jury despite the evidence. = 尽管有证据，他还是被陪审团宣判无罪。

Interrogative Sentence:
Did the jury acquit the defendant? = 陪审团是否宣判被告无罪？
How did he acquit himself in the interview? = 他在面试中表现如何？

Negative Sentence:

She did not acquit herself well during the presentation. = 她在演讲中的表现不佳。
