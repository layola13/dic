metaphor:/ˈmetəfɔːr/ /ˈmetəˌfɔːr/ | n. | 比喻; 隐喻 | A figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable.  A comparison without using like or as. |例句： "Life is a journey" is a common metaphor.  “人生是一场旅程”是一个常见的比喻。

例句：He used the metaphor of a chess game to describe the political situation. 他用象棋比赛的比喻来形容政治局势。|近义词：simile, analogy, figure of speech |反义词: literalism |词性: noun

PHRASE->
mixed metaphor = 混杂比喻
dead metaphor = 死比喻 (不再具有比喻意义的比喻)
extended metaphor = 延伸比喻 (在一段文字中持续使用的比喻)
root metaphor = 根比喻 (一个文化或社会中基本的比喻)
controlling metaphor = 支配性比喻 (一个影响整个作品或论述的比喻)


词源:  From Late Latin *metaphora, from Greek metaphora  "a transfer," from metapherein "to transfer, carry over; change," from meta- "over, across" + pherein "to carry."
