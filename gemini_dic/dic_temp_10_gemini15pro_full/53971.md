discrepancy:/dɪˈskrepənsi/| n.| 差异，不符，不一致 |A difference or inconsistency between two or more facts, figures, or claims.|There's a discrepancy between the two accounts of the accident. 这两份事故报告之间存在差异。|近义词：difference, inconsistency, variation, divergence, disagreement|反义词：agreement, consistency, conformity, similarity|词性: 名词

discrepance:/dɪˈskrepəns/| n.| 差异，不符，不一致 |A difference or inconsistency between two or more facts, figures, or claims.  This spelling is less common than "discrepancy".|There was a marked discrepance in their testimonies.  他们的证词有明显的出入。|近义词：difference, inconsistency, variation, divergence, disagreement|反义词：agreement, consistency, conformity, similarity|词性: 名词

词源:  From Late Latin *discrepantia*, from Latin discrepans, present participle of discrepare "to sound differently, disagree," from dis- "apart" + crepare "to rattle, creak, crack."
