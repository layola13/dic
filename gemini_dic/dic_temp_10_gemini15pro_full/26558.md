cable-cars:/ˈkeɪbəl kɑːrz/
n.
缆车
A system of transportation that moves cars along suspended cables, typically used in hilly or mountainous areas.
We took a cable-car up to the top of the mountain. 我们乘坐缆车到达了山顶。

cable-cars:/ˈkeɪbəl kɑːrz/
n.
缆车
A system of transportation that moves cars along suspended cables.
San Francisco is famous for its cable-cars. 旧金山以其缆车而闻名。

cable-cars:/ˈkeɪbəl kɑːrz/
Etymology:  cable + car. "cable" from Old North French *cable, from Late Latin capulum "halter, rope," from Latin capere "to take, seize."  "car" from Middle English carre, from Old North French carre, of Gaulish origin.
词源: cable + car。“cable”来自古北法语*cable，源于晚期拉丁语capulum“缰绳，绳索”，来自拉丁语capere“拿，抓住”。“car”来自中古英语carre，源于古北法语carre，源于高卢语。

cable-cars:/ˈkeɪbəl kɑːrz/
Synonyms:  tram, funicular, aerial tramway
近义词：tram（有轨电车）, funicular（缆索铁路）, aerial tramway（空中缆车）

cable-cars:/ˈkeɪbəl kɑːrz/
Antonyms:  bus, train, car
反义词：bus（公共汽车）, train（火车）, car（汽车）
