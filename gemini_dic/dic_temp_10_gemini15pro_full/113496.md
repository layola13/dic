lystrosaurus:/ˌlɪstroʊˈsɔːrəs/
noun
水龙兽
A genus of extinct dicynodont therapsids from the late Permian and early Triassic periods.  Lystrosaurus was a herbivore with a beak-like mouth and two tusks, and is notable for surviving the Permian-Triassic extinction event.
Lystrosaurus fossils have been found in Antarctica, India, and South Africa.  水龙兽化石已在南极洲、印度和南非被发现。

Lystrosaurus was a common animal during the Early Triassic. 水龙兽是早三叠世时期的一种常见动物。
