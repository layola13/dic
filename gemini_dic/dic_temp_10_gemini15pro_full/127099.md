nationalist:/ˈnæʃ(ə)nəlɪst/| n. adj.|民族主义者；国家主义者；民族主义的；国家主义的|A person who strongly identifies with their own nation and vigorously supports its interests, especially to the exclusion or detriment of the interests of other nations.  A person advocating political independence for a country.|例句：He was a fervent nationalist. 他是一个狂热的民族主义者。

例句：The nationalist movement gained momentum. 民族主义运动势头增强。|近义词：patriot, chauvinist|反义词：cosmopolitan, internationalist|词性: noun, adjective

nationalistic:/ˌnæʃ(ə)nəˈlɪstɪk/| adj.|民族主义的；国家主义的|Showing strong patriotism, especially to the exclusion or detriment of the interests of other nations.|例句：Nationalistic fervor swept the country. 民族主义热情席卷全国。

例句：His nationalistic sentiments were evident in his speech. 他的民族主义情绪在他的演讲中很明显。|近义词：patriotic, chauvinistic|反义词：cosmopolitan, internationalist|词性: adjective

nationalism:/ˈnæʃ(ə)nəlɪzəm/| n.|民族主义；国家主义|Identification with one's own nation and support for its interests, especially to the exclusion or detriment of the interests of other nations.|例句：Nationalism can be a powerful force for both good and bad. 民族主义可以成为一股强大的力量，有利也有弊。

例句：The rise of nationalism led to conflict. 民族主义的兴起导致了冲突。|近义词：patriotism, jingoism|反义词：internationalism, globalism|词性: noun
