barbule:/ˈbɑːrbjuːl/ (US), /ˈbɑːbjuːl/ (UK)| n. |小羽枝，羽绒毛|A small filament or barb branching from the barbs of a feather, forming part of the interlocking structure that gives feathers their strength and shape.|The barbules of the down feathers interlock to trap air and provide insulation. 羽绒羽毛的小羽枝相互交织以留住空气并提供隔热。|Synonyms: filament, barb, plumule|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, could consider "shaft" as a contrasting part of the feather)|Noun
