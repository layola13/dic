noodleism: /ˈnuːdlɪɪzəm/
n.
面条主义; (尤指政治上的)实用主义;机会主义
A pragmatic or opportunistic approach, especially in politics, characterized by a lack of strong principles and a willingness to compromise or change position for personal gain.  It suggests a flexible, sometimes even slippery, stance that bends to circumstances like noodles.
The opposition party accused the government of noodleism in its foreign policy.  反对党指责政府外交政策实用主义。
pragmatism, opportunism, expediency, realpolitik
principled stance, ideological purity
noun
