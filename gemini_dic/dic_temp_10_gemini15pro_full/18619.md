benthamism: /ˈbɛnθəmɪzəm/ | n. | 功利主义；效益主义 |  The ethical theory of Jeremy Bentham, holding that actions are morally right if they promote the greatest happiness for the greatest number of people. | Benthamism is often summarized as "the greatest good for the greatest number." 功利主义常被概括为“为最多的人谋求最大的利益”。 |  近义词: utilitarianism | 反义词: deontology, altruism | 词性: noun

Etymology: From the name of Jeremy Bentham (1748-1832), English philosopher and jurist, + -ism.
