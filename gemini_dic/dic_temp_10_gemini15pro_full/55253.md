dissimulation:/dɪˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 掩饰，伪装，假装 | The act of concealing one's true feelings or intentions. |  He was a master of dissimulation, hiding his true motives behind a charming smile. 他是个掩饰高手，用迷人的微笑掩盖了他真正的动机。| synonyms: pretense, deception, feigning | antonyms: honesty, candor, frankness | noun

dissimulations:/dɪˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃnz/ | n. | 掩饰，伪装，假装（复数） | Acts of concealing one's true feelings or intentions. | Her dissimulations were so convincing that no one suspected her true intentions. 她的伪装如此令人信服，以至于没有人怀疑她的真实意图。 | synonyms: pretenses, deceptions, feignings | antonyms: honesties, candors, franknesses | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin dissimulationem, accusative of dissimulatio (“dissembling, concealing”), from dissimulare (“to disguise, conceal”), from dis- (“not, un-”) + simulare (“to resemble, feign”), from similis (“like”).
