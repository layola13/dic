fuddy-duddy:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi/| n. |老顽固，守旧的人|A person who is old-fashioned and fussy, unwilling to accept new ideas or ways of doing things.|例句：He's such a fuddy-duddy, he still uses a typewriter!  他真是个老顽固，居然还在用打字机！

例句：Don't be such a fuddy-duddy, try something new!  别那么守旧，尝试一些新鲜事物吧！|近义词：conservative, traditionalist, stick-in-the-mud, old fogey|反义词:modernist, progressive, innovator|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Originated in the early 20th century, possibly as a reduplication of "fuddy," meaning "fussy" or "old-fashioned." The exact etymology is uncertain.
