intercrop: /ˈɪntərkrɒp/ (UK), /ˈɪntərkrɑːp/ (US)| n. v.|间作，套种|The practice of growing two or more crops in proximity.  The crops are planted close enough for biological interaction, such as pest control or increased yields.|Example:  The farmer decided to intercrop corn with beans to improve soil fertility.  农民决定将玉米和豆类间作以提高土壤肥力。

Example: They intercropped the cabbages with onions. 他们将卷心菜和洋葱间作。 |近义词：companion plant, mixed cropping|反义词: monoculture|词性: 名词, 动词
