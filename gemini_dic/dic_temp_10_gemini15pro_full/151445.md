paronomasia:/ˌpærənoʊˈmeɪʒə/ /ˌpærənəˈmeɪzɪə/ | n. |  双关语，谐音双关 | A rhetorical device that involves using words that are similar in sound but have different meanings, often for humorous or poetic effect. |  Example:  "Atheism is a non-prophet institution." This uses the similar sounds of "prophet" and "profit" to create a pun. | Synonym: pun, wordplay | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but one could consider "literal language" a contrasting concept) | noun
