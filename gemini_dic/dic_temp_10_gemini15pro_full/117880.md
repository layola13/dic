melodramatics: /ˌmɛlədrəˈmætɪks/ | n. |夸张的言行；装腔作势|Exaggerated or overly emotional behavior or actions, often intended to create a dramatic effect.|She reacted to the news with melodramatics, weeping and wailing loudly. 她对这个消息的反应很夸张，大声哭喊。| The actor's melodramatics made the play seem ridiculous.  这个演员的夸张表演让这出戏显得荒谬。|近义词：histrionics, theatrics, dramatics|反义词：understatement, restraint|词性: 名词

melodramatic: /ˌmɛlədrəˈmætɪk/ | adj. |夸张的；戏剧性的；感情过分渲染的|Characteristic of melodrama, especially in being exaggerated, sensationalized, or overemotional.|The soap opera was full of melodramatic plot twists.  这部肥皂剧充满了戏剧性的情节转折。| He gave a melodramatic account of his trip, making it sound much more exciting than it actually was. 他对他的旅行进行了夸张的描述，使它听起来比实际情况更令人兴奋。|近义词：histrionic, theatrical, dramatic|反义词：understated, restrained|词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mélodramatique*, from *mélodrame* (“melodrama”) + *-ique*.  *mélodrame* is from Greek *μέλος* (*mélos*, “song, melody”) + French *drame* (“drama”).  Ultimately from Ancient Greek *μέλος* (*mélos*, “limb, member; song, strain, melody”) + *δρᾶμα* (*drâma*, “deed, act; play”).
