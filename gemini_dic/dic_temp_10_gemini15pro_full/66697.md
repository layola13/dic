exon:/ˈeksɒn/ (UK), /ˈeksɑːn/ (US)| n. |外显子|A segment of a DNA or RNA molecule containing information coding for a protein or peptide sequence. Exons are transcribed to messenger RNA and, following any necessary processing, are translated into a protein. |The exons are joined together to form the mature mRNA molecule.| synonym: coding sequence | antonym: intron | noun
