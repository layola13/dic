coenurus:/siːˈnjʊərəs/| n. |多头蚴|A larval stage of certain tapeworms, characterized by a large fluid-filled bladder containing multiple scolices (heads). It typically develops in the brain or spinal cord of intermediate hosts, such as sheep and rabbits.|The veterinarian diagnosed the sheep with coenurus cerebralis.  兽医诊断这只羊患有多头蚴脑病。

The coenurus can cause serious neurological problems in the infected animal.  多头蚴会导致感染动物出现严重的神经系统问题。|synonym:  larval stage, metacestode|antonym: adult tapeworm|noun
