onomatopoeia: /ˌɒnəˌmætəˈpiːə/ (UK) /ˌɑːnəˌmætəˈpiːə/ (US)| n. |拟声词，象声词|The formation of a word by imitation of a sound made by or associated with its referent. |Example: "Buzz" is an onomatopoeia for the sound a bee makes.  “嗡嗡”是蜜蜂发出的声音的拟声词。

|Example:  The "sizzle" of the steak on the grill was mouthwatering.  牛排在烤架上“滋滋”的声音令人垂涎。|Synonyms:  sound word, imitation word|Antonyms: N/A|noun

PHRASE->
There isn't really a standard "phrase" that uses onomatopoeia, as by definition it is a single word mimicking a sound.  However, here are some examples of how onomatopoeia is *used* within phrases:

The bees buzzed around the hive. 蜜蜂在蜂巢周围嗡嗡作响。
The steak sizzled on the grill. 牛排在烤架上发出滋滋声。
The rain pattered against the window. 雨滴拍打着窗户。
The dog barked loudly at the mailman. 狗对着邮递员大声吠叫。
The clock ticked softly in the background. 时钟在背景中轻轻地滴答作响。
