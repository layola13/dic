overponderously: /ˌoʊvərˈpɑːndərəsli/ | adv. | 过于沉重地；过于深思熟虑地；笨重地 |  In a way that is too ponderous; in a heavy, labored, or excessively thoughtful manner.  It suggests slowness, clumsiness, and often an excessive seriousness or formality. |  He walked overponderously across the stage, each step a labored effort. 他沉重地走过舞台，每一步都显得吃力。 |  The professor overponderously explained the simple concept, losing the students' attention. 教授过于沉重地解释了这个简单的概念，失去了学生的注意力。 |  laboriously, heavily, clumsily, ponderously | lightly, nimbly, quickly | adverb

Etymology:

From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "ponderous" (adjective meaning "heavy, weighty, or clumsy") + "-ly" (adverb suffix).  The word "ponderous" itself comes from the Latin "ponderosus," meaning "weighty," from "pondus," meaning "weight."
