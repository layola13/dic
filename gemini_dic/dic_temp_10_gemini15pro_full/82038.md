grislier:/ˈɡrɪzlɪər/ (US), /ˈɡrɪzliə/ (UK)| adj.|更加灰白的，更加斑白的|More gray or grizzled (especially of hair); more austere and forbidding in appearance.  Showing the effects of age or hardship.  |例句：His grislier beard made him look older than his years. 他的更加斑白的胡须使他看起来比实际年龄老。

例句：The mountain peaks, grislier in the fading light, seemed to frown down upon the valley.  在逐渐消失的光线下，山峰显得更加灰白，似乎在俯视着山谷。|近义词：grayer, more grizzled, hoarier,  more austere|反义词：younger-looking, more youthful, less austere|词性:adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *grislier, comparative of *grisli (“grizzled, gray”), from Old English grīsle (“gray hair”), from Proto-Germanic *grisl- (“gray hair”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵreys- (“gray”).  The -er suffix forms the comparative adjective.
