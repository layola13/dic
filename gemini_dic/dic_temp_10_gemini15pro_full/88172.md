hilltopper:/ˈhɪlˌtɒpər/ (US) /ˈhɪlˌtɑːpər/| n. |山頂居民；住在山上的人；山顶的房子|A person who lives on top of a hill, or a house located on a hilltop. |例句：The hilltoppers enjoyed breathtaking views. 山顶的居民享有令人叹为观止的景色。

例句：The hilltopper was a quaint cottage overlooking the valley. 山顶的房子是一座可以俯瞰山谷的古雅小屋。|近义词：mountaineer, highlander|反义词:lowlander, valley dweller|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From hill + top + -er.  "Hill" comes from Old English "hyll," meaning "hill, mound." "Top" comes from Old English "topp," meaning "highest point, summit." "-er" is a suffix forming nouns denoting persons or things associated with a particular place or activity.
