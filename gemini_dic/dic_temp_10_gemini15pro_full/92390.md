hypocrystalline:/ˌhaɪpoʊˈkrɪstəlɪn/| adj.|半晶质的，部分结晶的|Partially crystalline; composed of crystals of microscopic or submicroscopic size, embedded in a glassy groundmass.  Relating to igneous rock that contains both crystals and glass.|The rock sample was identified as hypocrystalline, with visible crystals interspersed within the glassy matrix.|近义词：hemicrystalline, partly crystalline|反义词：holocrystalline, amorphous|词性:形容词

Etymology:  From hypo- (Greek 'under', 'less than') + crystalline.
