attitude:/ˈætɪtjuːd/ (US), /ˈætɪtʃuːd/ (UK)| n.|态度，看法；姿势|a feeling or opinion about something or someone, or a way of behaving that is caused by this|She has a positive attitude towards work. 她对工作持积极的态度。|synonyms: viewpoint, perspective, stance, outlook, position, opinion, feeling, frame of mind|antonyms: indifference, apathy|noun

attitude:/ˈætɪtjuːd/ (US), /ˈætɪtʃuːd/ (UK)| n.|姿态|the way a person positions their body|He adopted a defiant attitude. 他摆出一副挑衅的姿态。|synonyms: posture, pose, stance|antonyms: slump|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *actitudo, from Latin aptus (“fit, suitable”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ep- (“to work, be busy; to attain”).
