cosmorama:/ˌkɒzməˈrɑːmə/ | /ˌkɑːzməˈræmə/ | n. | 全景画；世界万象；西洋景|A picture, or series of pictures, representing various parts of the world, which, being wound round and viewed through lenses in a particular way, produce a striking resemblance to reality; also, an exhibition of this kind, or the place where it is exhibited.| We visited a cosmorama showcasing the wonders of the ancient world. 我们参观了一个展示古代世界奇观的全景画展览。|  The cosmorama offered a captivating glimpse into different cultures.  这个全景画展览提供了对不同文化迷人的一瞥。| synonyms: panorama, diorama, cyclorama | antonyms:  None (It's a specific type of display, not a concept with a direct opposite.) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cosmorama*, from *cosmo-* (combining form of Greek *κόσμος* (kósmos, “world, universe, order, ornament”)) + *-orama* (from Greek *ὅραμα* (hórama, “sight, spectacle”)).  First attested in 1820.
