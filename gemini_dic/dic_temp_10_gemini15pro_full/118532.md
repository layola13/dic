mesogloea:/ˌmɛzəˈɡliːə/ /ˌmiːzəˈɡliːə/
| n. | 中胶层；中胚层 |The transparent, jelly-like substance found between the two epithelial cell layers in the bodies of cnidarians and sponges.  It acts as a kind of connective tissue. | Cnidarians like jellyfish have a mesogloea that gives them their structure and buoyancy. 水母之类的刺胞动物有一个中胶层，赋予它们结构和浮力。|Synonyms: mesenchyme | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific anatomical feature) | noun
