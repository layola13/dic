baum:/baʊm/ | n. | 树 |A woody perennial plant with a single main stem or trunk, typically taller than a shrub, and bearing branches and leaves. | The children climbed the baum in the park. 孩子们爬上了公园里的树。| synonyms: tree, sapling, | antonyms: shrub, bush | noun

Etymology: From Middle High German *boum, from Old High German boum, from Proto-Germanic *baumaz, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰew- (“to grow, become”). Cognates include Dutch boom, English beam, and Gothic 𐌱𐌰𐌿𐌼𐍃 (baums).
