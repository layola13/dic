gisarm:/ˈdʒɪsɑːrm/
noun
戟；钩镰枪
A gisarm (sometimes spelled gisarme, guisarme, or giserme) is a pole weapon used in Europe primarily during the medieval period. It is characterized by a top spike and a hook or blade protruding from the side, typically used for hooking and pulling opponents from horseback or disrupting enemy formations.
The knight wielded his gisarm with deadly efficiency, unhorsing the enemy champion. 那位骑士挥舞着他的戟，以惊人的效率将敌方冠军挑落马下。

The gisarm was particularly effective against mounted knights.  这种戟对骑马的骑士特别有效。
