expansionists:/ɪkˈspænʃənɪsts/
n.|扩张主义者；主张扩张者|People who advocate or support expansion, especially territorial expansion of a country's influence and power.|The expansionists believed their nation had a right to conquer neighboring territories.|近义词：imperialists, aggressors|反义词:pacifists, isolationists|词性:名词

Etymology:
From "expansionist," derived from "expansion" + "-ist."  "Expansion" comes from Late Latin "expansio," from "expandere" (to spread out), from "ex-" (out) + "pandere" (to spread).  "-ist" is a suffix forming nouns that denote a person who practices or is occupied with something, or holds a particular doctrine.
