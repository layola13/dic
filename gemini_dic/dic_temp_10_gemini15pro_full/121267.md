misexplained:/ˌmɪsɪkˈspleɪnd/| v. |错误地解释；解释不清|The concept was misexplained, leading to confusion. 这个概念被错误地解释了，导致了混乱。|例句：The teacher misexplained the theory, confusing the students. 老师错误地解释了这个理论，让学生们感到困惑。

例句：He misexplained his intentions, causing a misunderstanding. 他错误地解释了他的意图，导致了误解。

|近义词：misinterpreted, misconstrued, distorted|反义词: explained clearly, elucidated|词性：动词

Etymology:
mis- (prefix): wrongly, badly, unfavorably (from Old English, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *missa-)
explain (verb):  make (an idea, situation, or problem) clear to someone by describing it in more detail or revealing relevant facts or ideas. (from Old French explainer, from Latin explanare ‘make smooth or level, explain’, from ex- ‘out’ + planare ‘to make plain’)
