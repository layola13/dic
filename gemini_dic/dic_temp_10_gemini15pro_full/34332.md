clash:/klæʃ/| n. v.|冲突，撞击声，不协调|A loud, harsh, metallic sound, like that of cymbals being struck together.  A mismatch of colors or styles. To come into conflict or disagreement.|The cymbals clashed loudly.铙钹发出响亮的声音。 The bright pink clashed with the orange. 亮粉色和橙色不协调。  Their personalities clashed. 他们的性格不合。|近义词：conflict, disagree, collide, crash|反义词：harmonize, agree, match|词性:名词，动词

clash:/klæʃɪz/| n.|冲突，撞击声，不协调（复数）|Plural form of "clash," referring to multiple instances of conflict, disagreement, or loud noises.|There were several clashes between the protesters and the police. 抗议者和警察之间发生了多次冲突。  The clashes of color in her outfit were distracting. 她衣服上的颜色冲突让人分心。|近义词：conflicts, disagreements, collisions|反义词：harmonies, agreements, matches|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *clascher, probably of imitative origin.  Related to Middle Dutch klassen (“to clap, crash”).
