词源:
From in- (not) + excusable + -ity (noun suffix).  "Excusable" comes from Late Latin excusabilis, from excusare "to excuse," from ex- "out" + causa "cause, reason."  Essentially, something inexcusable is something for which no valid reason or cause can be given to remove blame.
