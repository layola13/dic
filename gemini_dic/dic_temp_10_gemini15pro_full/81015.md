gradualisms:/ˈɡrædʒuəlɪzəmz/
|n.|渐进主义，渐变论|The theory or practice of gradual or slow changes, especially in political or social matters.  A policy of gradual reform. |The government's approach has been marked by gradualisms, preferring slow and steady change to radical action. 政府的做法以渐进主义为特征，倾向于缓慢而稳定的变革，而不是激进的行动。

The museum's collection reflects the gradualisms of artistic development over the centuries.  博物馆的藏品反映了几个世纪以来艺术发展的渐进性。|近义词：gradualness, gradation, progression, evolution|反义词：revolution, upheaval, suddenness, abruptness|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "gradual" + "-ism."  "Gradual" comes from Late Latin "gradualis" meaning "by degrees," from "gradus" meaning "step" or "degree."  The suffix "-ism" forms nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, principles, or practices.
