hard-line:/ˈhɑːrdlaɪn/ /ˈhɑːrdˌlaɪn/| adj. n.|强硬路线的，强硬派的；强硬路线，强硬派|Uncompromising and strict; a firm, uncompromising stance or position.|例句：The government took a hard-line stance on immigration. 政府对移民问题采取了强硬立场。|近义词：uncompromising, inflexible, firm, strict, rigid|反义词：flexible, moderate, compromising, lenient|词性:形容词

hard-line:/ˈhɑːrdlaɪn/ /ˈhɑːrdˌlaɪn/| n.|强硬路线，强硬派|An uncompromising and strict approach or the people who advocate it.|例句：The hard-liners in the party refused to negotiate. 党内的强硬派拒绝谈判。|近义词：uncompromising position, inflexible stance|反义词：moderate position, flexible stance|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "hard" (meaning firm or unyielding) + "line" (referring to a position or stance).  The term gained prominence during the Cold War, referring to those who favored an aggressive stance against the Soviet Union.
