alburnum:/ælˈbɜːrnəm/| n. |边材|The soft, outer layers of wood in a tree, between the bark and the heartwood, containing living cells and functioning primarily in water transport.  It is lighter in color than the heartwood.|The carpenter explained that the alburnum of the oak tree was more susceptible to rot.  木匠解释说，橡树的边材更容易腐烂。|Synonyms: sapwood|Antonyms: heartwood|Noun
