disinheriting:/dɪsɪnˈhɛrɪtɪŋ/
|v.|剥夺继承权|The act of preventing someone from inheriting property or money that they would normally be entitled to.|He threatened to disinherit his son if he married that woman.  他威胁说如果他儿子娶那个女人，就剥夺他的继承权。|
|近义词：depriving, cutting off|
|反义词：bequeathing, endowing|
|词性：动词|

disinherit:/ˌdɪsɪnˈhɛrɪt/
|v.|剥夺继承权|To prevent someone, especially a child, from inheriting one’s property, especially by stating this in a will.|She was disinherited for marrying against her father's wishes. 她因为违背父亲的意愿结婚而被剥夺了继承权。|
|近义词：cut off, deprive|
|反义词：bequeath, endow|
|词性：动词|
