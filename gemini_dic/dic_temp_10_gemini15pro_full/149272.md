词源:
Old English *wōs "juice, sap," from Proto-Germanic *wōz- (source also of Old Saxon wos, Old High German waz "ooze, moisture," Old Norse vos "dampness"). Related to wet. The original sense of "juice, sap" is now obsolete. The meaning "to exude moisture" is from mid-14c. Figurative sense of "to exude a quality" is from 1828. Related: Oozed; oozing.
