high-security:/ˌhaɪ sɪˈkjʊərəti/
|adj.|高度安全的；戒备森严的|Having or involving a high level of security measures to prevent unauthorized access, activity, or danger. | The prison is a high-security facility.  这个监狱是一个戒备森严的设施。|
| |The president's motorcade had a high-security escort. 总统的车队有高度安全的护送。|
| |Synonyms: maximum-security, heavily guarded, fortified, impenetrable|
| |Antonyms: low-security, unprotected, vulnerable, accessible|
| |Adjective|


ETYMOLOGY->
"high" (adjective) from Old English "heah," meaning "tall, lofty, elevated."
"security" (noun) from Late Latin "securitas," meaning "freedom from care," from Latin "securus" ("free from care, safe").
