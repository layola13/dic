dandyishly:/ˈdændɪɪʃli/ | adv. | 花花公子般地，衣着华丽地 | In a manner characteristic of a dandy; foppishly. | He strolled dandyishly down the street, twirling his cane.  他沿着街道花花公子般地漫步，旋转着手杖。| Synonym: foppishly, smartly, stylishly | Antonym: sloppily, plainly, unfashionably | 词性: 副词

dandy:/ˈdændi/ | n. | 花花公子，纨绔子弟 | A man unduly devoted to style, smartness in dress and appearance. | He was a dandy, always impeccably dressed. 他是个花花公子，总是衣着无可挑剔。| Synonym: fop, coxcomb, popinjay | Antonym: slob | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the late 18th century, the origin of "dandy" is uncertain. It may be a diminutive of "Andrew," or it may be related to the Scottish word "dander," meaning "to wander aimlessly."  The suffix "-ishly" transforms the noun "dandy" into an adverb.
