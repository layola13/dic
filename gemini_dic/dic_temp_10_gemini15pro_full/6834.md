anastomotic:/ˌænəstəˈmɒtɪk/ /ˌænəstəˈmɑːtɪk/| adj.|吻合的；连接的|Relating to or characterized by anastomosis, a connection or opening between two things (especially cavities or passages) that are normally diverging or branching, such as between blood vessels, leaf veins, or streams.|The surgeon performed an anastomotic procedure to reconnect the severed artery.  外科医生进行了吻合手术以重新连接断裂的动脉。|The anastomotic network of rivers in the delta provides a rich habitat for wildlife. 三角洲河流的吻合网络为野生动物提供了丰富的栖息地。|近义词：connecting, joining, linking|反义词：separating, dividing|词性: adjective

词源: From Late Latin *anastomoticus, from Greek ἀναστόμωσις (anastómōsis, “outlet, opening”), from ἀναστομόω (anastomóō, “furnish with a mouth, open”), from ἀνά (aná, “up, upon”) + στόμα (stóma, “mouth”).
