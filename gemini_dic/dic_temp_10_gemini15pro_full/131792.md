nonconscientiousness:/ˌnɒnkɒnʃiˈɛnʃəsnəs/
|n.|缺乏责任心,不认真,不尽责|Lack of conscientiousness; the trait of being careless, irresponsible, and not diligent.|His nonconscientiousness led to several errors in the report. 他的不尽责导致报告中出现了一些错误。|Synonyms: irresponsibility, carelessness, negligence, recklessness|Antonyms: conscientiousness, responsibility, diligence, carefulness|noun

Etymology:
From non- (not) + conscientiousness (the quality of wishing to do one's work or duty well and thoroughly).  Conscientiousness, in turn, comes from  conscientious (from Latin conscientia "knowledge within oneself," from consciens, present participle of conscire "be conscious," from com- "with" + scire "to know").
