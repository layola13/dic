overinclusion:/ˌoʊvərɪnˈkluːʒən/ | n. |过度概括，过度包含|The act of including too much or too many things, especially in a category or group.  This is often observed in children with cognitive or developmental challenges.|  Example: The child's overinclusion of all furry animals as "dogs" shows a lack of differentiation.  这个孩子把所有毛茸茸的动物都归为“狗”，显示出缺乏区分能力。| Example: Her overinclusion of irrelevant details made her essay confusing. 她过度包含了不相关的细节，使她的文章令人困惑。 |Synonyms: overgeneralization, overextension| Antonyms:  underinclusion, exclusion | noun
