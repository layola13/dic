anaesthetic:/ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk/ | n. adj.| 麻醉剂；麻醉药；麻醉的 | A drug that causes temporary loss of bodily sensations, especially pain.  Can also be used as an adjective to describe something that numbs or causes insensitivity. |  The surgeon administered the anaesthetic before the operation. 外科医生在手术前施用了麻醉剂。 | The cream has an anaesthetic effect.  这种药膏有麻醉作用。| Synonyms: anesthetic (American spelling), narcotic, opiate | Antonyms: stimulant | noun, adjective


Etymology:  From Greek ἀναισθητικός (anaisthetikos, “without sensation”), from ἀν- (an-, “without”) + αἴσθησις (aisthēsis, “sensation”).
