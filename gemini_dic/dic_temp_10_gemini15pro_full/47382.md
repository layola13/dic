deada:/ˈdɛdə/| adj.|死的，无生命的|Dead refers to something that is no longer living. It can also be used informally to describe something that is no longer functioning or active. |The tree is dead after the storm.  这棵树在暴风雨后死了。|近义词：deceased, lifeless, inanimate|反义词:alive, living|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Old English *dēad, from Proto-Germanic *daudaz, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰewh₂- (“to die”). Cognate with Old Frisian dād, Old Saxon dōd, Old High German tōt, Old Norse dauðr, Gothic 𐌳𐌰𐌿𐌸𐍃 (dauþs).
