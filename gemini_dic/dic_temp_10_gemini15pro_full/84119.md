half-lunatic: /ˌhæf ˈluːnətɪk/ | adj. | 半疯的；有点疯狂的 |  Partially insane; exhibiting some degree of madness or irrationality. It suggests someone who behaves eccentrically or erratically, but not to the extent of being fully clinically insane. |  He had some half-lunatic scheme to climb Mount Everest in his pajamas. 他有个半疯狂的计划，想穿着睡衣去爬珠穆朗玛峰。| Synonyms: eccentric, erratic, unconventional, quirky, daft | Antonyms: sane, rational, sensible | adjective

half-lunatic: /ˌhæf ˈluːnətɪk/ | n. |  怪人；行为古怪的人 | A person who is partially insane or exhibits eccentric or erratic behavior. |  He was considered a half-lunatic by his neighbors due to his strange habits. 由于他奇怪的习惯，邻居们都认为他是个怪人。 | Synonyms: crank, eccentric, oddball, weirdo | Antonyms: conformist, conventional person | noun


Etymology:  "half-" (Old English healf) + "lunatic" (from Latin lunaticus, "moonstruck," from luna, "moon"). The belief that the moon influenced mental states led to the term "lunatic" for someone deemed insane. "Half-lunatic" then emerged to describe a lesser degree of this perceived madness.
