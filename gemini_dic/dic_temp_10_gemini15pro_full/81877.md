grievance: /ˈɡriːvəns/ (US), /ˈɡriːvəns/ (UK)| n. |委屈，不满，抱怨，申诉|a real or imagined wrong or other cause for complaint or protest, especially unfair treatment.|例句：He has been nursing a grievance against his boss for months.  他几个月来一直对他的老板心怀不满。|例句：The trade union representative listened to the workers' grievances. 工会代表听取了工人们的抱怨。|近义词：complaint, injustice, wrong, beef, grouse|反义词：satisfaction, contentment|词性: noun

词源: From Old French *grevance "difficulty, hardship, pain, grief," from grever "to burden, aggravate, injure," from Late Latin gravare "to burden," from Latin gravis "heavy."
