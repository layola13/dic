impertinence: /ˈɪmpɜːrtɪnəns/ /ɪmˈpɜːrtnəns/ | n. | 无礼，鲁莽；不恰当，不相干 | Lack of respect; rudeness;  irrelevance. |  Her impertinence made me angry. 她的无礼让我很生气。|  The judge deemed the lawyer's question impertinence and told him to move on. 法官认为律师的问题不恰当，让他继续。 | Synonyms: rudeness, insolence, impudence, cheek, audacity | Antonyms: politeness, courtesy, respect, deference | noun

Etymology:
From Late Middle English: impertinence, from Old French impertinence, from Latin impertinentia, from impertinent- ‘not pertinent’ (see impertinent).
