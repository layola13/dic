bedding-down: /ˈbedɪŋ daʊn/ (UK) /ˈbedɪŋ daʊn/ (US)
verb
安顿下来；（使）适应新环境；（使）为…作准备
The process of becoming established or settling into a new situation or routine; preparing something for a particular purpose.
The new CEO is bedding down in her role and making some important changes. 新任首席执行官正在适应她的角色，并做出一些重要的改变。
The team is bedding down the plans for the new product launch. 团队正在为新产品发布计划做准备。
settle in, settle down, acclimatize, establish, prepare
disrupt, unsettle, uproot

bedding-down: /ˈbedɪŋ daʊn/ (UK) /ˈbedɪŋ daʊn/ (US)
noun
（动物或人的）寝具；（植物的）护根覆盖物
Material used as a bed, especially for animals or plants; the act of providing such material.
The farmer spread fresh bedding-down for the cows. 农民为奶牛铺上了新鲜的寝具。
The gardener used straw as bedding-down for the roses. 园丁用稻草作为玫瑰的护根覆盖物。
bedding, litter, mulch
uncovering, exposure
