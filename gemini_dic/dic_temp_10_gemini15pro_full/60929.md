elevate: /ˈɛlɪveɪt/ (US) /ˈɛlɪveɪt/ (UK) | vt. | 提升，提高，升高；使情绪高昂；举起 | To raise to a higher position, level, or rank; to improve morally, intellectually, or culturally. |  They elevated him to the position of CEO. 他们把他提升到了首席执行官的职位。 | synonyms: raise, lift, hoist, boost, upgrade, promote, exalt  | antonyms: lower, depress, degrade, demote | verb


elevated: /ˈɛlɪveɪtɪd/ (US) /ˈɛlɪveɪtɪd/ (UK) | adj. | 高的；高层的；高尚的；兴奋的 | Being at a higher level or rank than normal; lofty, noble; exhilarated. | The house is situated on an elevated platform. 这座房子坐落在一个高台上。 | synonyms: raised, high, lofty, exalted, noble  | antonyms: low, depressed, base | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *elevatus*, past participle of elevare ("to lift up, raise"), from e- ("out, up") + levare ("to lift").
