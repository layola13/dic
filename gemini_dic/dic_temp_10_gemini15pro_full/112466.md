lought: /lɔːt/ | v. (archaic, past tense of laugh) | 笑，大笑 |  This is an archaic past tense form of "laugh," meaning to express mirth, amusement, or derision with an audible expulsion of air from the lungs. | He lought heartily at the jester's antics. 他被小丑的滑稽动作逗得哈哈大笑。 | 近义词: laughed, chuckled, guffawed | 反义词: cried, sobbed, wept | 词性: verb

词源: From Middle English *laughten, *lauwen, from Old English hliehhan (“to laugh”), from Proto-Germanic *klakhjaną (“to laugh”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱlegʰ- (“to make a shrill sound, laugh”). Cognate with Scots lauch (“to laugh”), West Frisian laitsje (“to laugh”), Dutch lachen (“to laugh”), German lachen (“to laugh”), Danish le (“to laugh”), Swedish le (“to laugh, smile”), Icelandic hlæja (“to laugh”).
