epics:/ˈɛpɪks/ | n. | 史诗; 壮举;  |A long poem, typically one derived from ancient oral tradition, narrating the deeds and adventures of heroic or legendary figures or the history of a nation.  A series of events considered to be particularly impressive or momentous.|  Homer's epics, the Iliad and the Odyssey, tell of the Trojan War. 荷马史诗，《伊利亚特》和《奥德赛》，讲述了特洛伊战争的故事。|  saga, legend, narrative| antonym: None (lack of a single direct antonym for a genre)| noun
