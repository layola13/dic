beknave:/bɪˈneɪv/
verb
欺骗，哄骗
to treat (someone) dishonestly or unfairly
He was beknaved out of his inheritance. 他被骗走了遗产。
近义词:deceive, cheat, trick, defraud, swindle
反义词:be honest, treat fairly
词性:verb

词源:
From be- + knave.  "Be-" is a prefix meaning "to make" or "to cause to be," while "knave" originally meant "boy" or "servant," but later evolved to mean "dishonest man" or "rogue."  Thus, "beknave" literally means "to make into a knave" or "to treat someone like a knave."
