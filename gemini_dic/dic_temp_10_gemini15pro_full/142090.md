oriental: /ˌɔːriˈentl/ (British) /ˌɔːrˈiɛntl/ (American)| adj. n.|东方的；东方人|Relating to the countries of East and Southeast Asia.  Often considered outdated or offensive when used to refer to people.|

例句：
The oriental rug added a touch of elegance to the room.
东方地毯为房间增添了一丝优雅。

She studied oriental languages in college.
她在大学学习东方语言。

近义词：Eastern, Asian|
反义词：Western, Occidental|
词性：adjective, noun|


oriental: /ˌɔːriˈentl/ (British) /ˌɔːrˈiɛntl/ (American)| n.|东方人|A person from East and Southeast Asia.  This term is now widely considered offensive.|

例句：
The museum has a large collection of oriental art.  (Use of "oriental" in this context, referring to objects rather than people, is less problematic but "East Asian art" is generally preferred.)
博物馆收藏了大量的东方艺术品。

Many orientals immigrated to the United States in the 19th century. (This usage is offensive; use "East Asian people" or be more specific e.g., "Chinese people," "Japanese people," etc.)
许多东方人在19世纪移民到美国。

近义词：Easterner, Asian (though "Asian" can be too broad and also sometimes carries negative connotations depending on context)|
反义词：Westerner, Occidental|
词性：noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *oriental*, from Latin orientālis (“eastern”), from oriēns (“east; rising sun”), present participle of orīrī (“to rise”).
