ace-up-their-sleeve:/ˈeɪs ʌp ðeər sliːv/| idiom|锦囊妙计；秘诀；王牌|A secret advantage or resource that can be used when needed.  It refers to a card player concealing a winning card, ensuring victory.|He had an ace up his sleeve – he knew the chairman of the selection committee. 他有锦囊妙计——他认识遴选委员会的主席。|近义词:secret weapon, hidden advantage, trump card|反义词:disadvantage, weakness|noun phrase

ETYMOLOGY->
From card games, specifically the practice of cheating by hiding a winning card (an ace) up one's sleeve to be produced at a strategic moment.  This practice dates back to at least the 17th century.
