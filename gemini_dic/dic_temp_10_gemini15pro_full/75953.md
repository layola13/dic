funiculus:/fjuːˈnɪkjʊləs/| n. |索，带，脐带|A slender cord or band of fibers, especially in a plant or animal body.  Often refers to a bundle of nerve fibers.|

例句：The spinal cord is a major funiculus of the central nervous system. 脊髓是中枢神经系统的主要神经索。

例句：The doctor carefully examined the umbilical funiculus. 医生仔细检查了脐带。

近义词：cord, strand, fiber, filament|

词性: noun|

funiculus:/fjuːˈnɪkjʊləs/| n. | (植物)珠柄|The stalk that attaches an ovule to the placenta in a plant's ovary.|

例句：The funiculus of the orchid flower is delicate and threadlike.  兰花的花珠柄纤细如线。

例句：Damage to the funiculus can prevent the ovule from developing into a seed. 珠柄受损会阻止胚珠发育成种子。

近义词：stalk, pedicle|
反义词: N/A|
词性: noun|
