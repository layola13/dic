connive: /kəˈnaɪv/ | v. | 纵容；默许；共谋；串通 | Secretly allow (something considered wrong) to occur. |  The guards connived at the prisoner's escape. 警卫纵容囚犯逃跑。

The government was accused of conniving with the rebels. 政府被指控与叛军勾结。


近义词: conspire, collude, plot, scheme | 反义词: oppose, prevent, obstruct | 词性: 动词

conniving: /kəˈnaɪvɪŋ/ | adj. | 纵容的；默许的；共谋的；串通的 |  Given to or involved in conspiring to do something immoral, illegal, or harmful. | He gave her a conniving smile. 他给了她一个阴险狡诈的微笑。

She suspected a conniving plot to oust her from the committee. 她怀疑有人密谋要把她赶出委员会。


近义词: scheming, deceitful, underhand, crafty | 反义词: honest, forthright, aboveboard | 词性: 形容词


词源:  Late 16th century (in the sense ‘close the eyes to, overlook’): from French conniver, from Latin connivere ‘close the eyes, wink at’, from con- ‘together’ + nictare ‘to wink’.
