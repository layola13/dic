expulsions:/ɪkˈspʌlʃənz/
noun
驱逐，开除，排出
the act of forcing someone to leave a place, school, organization, etc.
The government ordered the expulsions of several diplomats. 政府下令驱逐数名外交官。
synonyms: ejection, removal, dismissal, discharge
antonyms: admission, acceptance, inclusion
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English expulsion, from Late Latin expulsiō, from Latin expellō (“I drive out”).
