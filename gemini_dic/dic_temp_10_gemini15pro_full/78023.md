gentrification:/ˌdʒɛntrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ |n.| 中产阶级化；贵族化；士绅化|The process of renovating and improving a house or district so that it conforms to middle-class taste. This often results in increased property values and the displacement of lower-income residents.|Example 1: The gentrification of the neighborhood has led to a sharp increase in rents. 这个社区的中产阶级化导致租金急剧上涨。

Example 2:  The rapid gentrification of the area has displaced many long-term residents. 该地区的快速贵族化导致许多长期居民流离失所。|近义词：urban renewal, revitalization, upgrading|反义词：urban decay, blight|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From gentry + -fication.  "Gentry" refers to people of good social position, specifically below the nobility in rank. "-fication" is a suffix forming nouns denoting action or process.  The term emerged in the mid-20th century, coined by British sociologist Ruth Glass.
