embloom:/ɪmˈbluːm/ | v. | 使开花；使繁荣；使焕发青春 | to cause to blossom; to decorate with blossoms; to brighten or enhance | The warm spring weather embloomed the cherry trees. 春季的温暖天气使樱花树开花了。 | 近义词: blossom, flourish, thrive, prosper | 反义词: wither, fade, decline | 词性: 动词

词源:  From em- (meaning "to cause to be in" or "to put into") + bloom (meaning "flower" or "state of flowering"). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *blōmō.
