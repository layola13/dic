cog:/kɒɡ/ (British) /kɑːɡ/ (American)| n.|齿轮; 齿; （尤指大机器上的）嵌齿轮; （组织、体制等的）小齿轮，小部件|A cog is a wheel with teeth around its edge, used to turn another wheel or part in a machine.  Figuratively, it refers to a person or thing that plays a minor role in a large organization or system. |The cogs in the clock mechanism were intricate and fascinating.  时钟机制中的齿轮复杂而迷人。|  He felt like a small cog in a vast corporate machine. 他觉得自己像一个庞大企业机器中的一个小齿轮。 |Synonym: gear, tooth, component|Antonym: whole, entirety|noun


cog:/kɒɡ/ (British) /kɑːɡ/ (American)| v.|给…装齿轮; 齿合|To furnish with cogs; to fit together. |They cogged the wheels to create a complex mechanism. 他们给轮子装上齿轮，创造了一个复杂的机制。|The gears cogged smoothly. 齿轮平稳地啮合。 |Synonym: mesh, interlock|Antonym: disengage, separate|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English cogge, from Old Norse kaggi (“cog, peg, wedge”), from Proto-Germanic *kagjō (“peg, wedge”), from Proto-Indo-European *k₃eg̑ʰ- (“to stick, project”).
