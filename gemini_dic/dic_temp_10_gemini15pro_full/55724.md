divarication: /daɪˌværɪˈkeɪʃən/ /daɪˌvæɹɪˈkeɪʃən/
n.|分叉，分歧，岔开|The divarication of the branches made it difficult to climb the tree. 树枝的分叉使得爬树很困难。|The political divarication between the two parties seems unbridgeable.  这两个政党之间的政治分歧似乎无法弥合。|近义词：divergence, separation, forking, branching|反义词：convergence, union, junction|词性：名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *divaricatio*, from Latin divaricatus, past participle of divaricare "to spread apart," from di- "apart" + varicare "to straddle," from varus "bent, knock-kneed."
