alcyonic: /ælˈsaɪənɪk/ | adj. | 平静的，宁静的；繁荣的，幸福的 | Of or relating to the halcyon (kingfisher), a mythical bird said to calm the sea during the winter solstice; hence, peaceful, tranquil, prosperous, or happy. |  The alcyonic days of her childhood were filled with laughter and joy. 她童年平静的日子充满了欢声笑语。 |  The company enjoyed an alcyonic period of growth.  公司经历了一段繁荣的增长期。 | Synonyms: tranquil, peaceful, serene, calm, prosperous, golden, blissful | Antonyms: turbulent, chaotic, troubled, difficult, unhappy | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *alcyonius, from Latin alcyon, from Ancient Greek ἀλκυών (alkuṓn, “kingfisher”).  The myth held that the halcyon, identified with the kingfisher, built its nest on the sea and calmed the waves for seven days before and after the winter solstice.
