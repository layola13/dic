infirmed:/ɪnˈfɜːrmd/ | adj. |  衰弱的，病弱的 | Weakened or disabled, especially by old age or illness. |  The infirmed elderly man required assistance to walk.  这位体弱多病的老人需要帮助才能行走。 | Synonyms: frail, debilitated, weak, feeble, enfeebled | Antonyms: strong, healthy, robust | adjective

infirm:/ɪnˈfɜːrm/ | v. | 使衰弱，使病弱 | To weaken or disable, especially by old age or illness.  (This usage is less common than the adjective form.) |  Long periods of bed rest can infirm a person. 长时间卧床休息会使人虚弱。 | Synonyms: weaken, debilitate, enfeeble | Antonyms: strengthen, invigorate | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *enfermer*, from Latin *in-* (not) + *firmus* (strong, firm).
