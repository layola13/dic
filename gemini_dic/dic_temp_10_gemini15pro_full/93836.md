immiscible:/ɪˈmɪsɪbl/| adj.|不混溶的，不能混合的|Not capable of being mixed; tending to remain in separate phases.|(Of liquids) not forming a homogeneous mixture when added together.|例句：Oil and water are immiscible. 油和水是不混溶的。

例句：The two liquids are immiscible, forming distinct layers. 这两种液体不混溶，会形成明显的层。|近义词：non-blending, unmixable, incompatible|反义词：miscible, blendable, mixable|词性:形容词

immiscibility:/ˌɪmɪsɪˈbɪlɪti/| n.|不混溶性|The inability of two or more substances to mix together to form a homogeneous solution.|例句：The immiscibility of oil and water is a common scientific principle. 油和水的互不相溶性是一个常见的科学原理。

例句：The experiment demonstrated the immiscibility of the two chemicals. 实验演示了这两种化学物质的不混溶性。|近义词：incompatibility, non-blending, unmixability|反义词：miscibility, blendability|词性:名词
