censures:/ˈsɛnʃərz/ | n. | 谴责，责备 |  An expression of strong disapproval or harsh criticism. | The politician faced censures from his colleagues for his unethical behavior.  这位政治家因其不道德的行为而面临同事们的谴责。 | synonyms: reprimands, rebukes, condemnations, criticisms | antonyms: praise, commendation, approval | noun

censures:/ˈsɛnʃərz/ | v. | 谴责，责备 | To express strong disapproval of (someone or something). | The committee censured the senator for accepting bribes. 委员会谴责了参议员接受贿赂的行为。 | synonyms: reprimand, rebuke, condemn, criticize | antonyms: praise, commend, approve | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *censuren*, from Old French *censurer* "to judge, assess, estimate," from Latin *censēre* "to assess, judge, estimate, give one's opinion, hold a census."
