idolist: /ˈaɪdəlɪst/ | n. | 理想主义者；空想家；偶像崇拜者 | A person who is guided more by ideals than by practical considerations; a person who cherishes or pursues high or noble principles, purposes, goals, etc.; a devoted admirer of an idol. |  He's a young idealist who believes he can change the world. 他是一位年轻的理想主义者，相信自己可以改变世界。 | Synonyms: dreamer, visionary, utopian, romantic, enthusiast | Antonyms: pragmatist, realist, cynic | Noun

Etymology:
From French *idoliste*, from idole (“idol”) + -iste (“-ist”).  Ultimately from Greek *εἴδωλον* (eídōlon, “phantom, image, idol”).
