assimilating:/əˈsɪməleɪtɪŋ/
|v.|吸收; 同化; 消化|The process of adapting or adjusting to the culture of a group or nation, or the state of being so adapted.|Immigrants are often faced with the challenge of assimilating into a new culture. 移民常常面临融入新文化的挑战。|Synonyms: integrating, adapting, absorbing, incorporating, blending in|Antonyms: segregating, isolating, differentiating, rejecting|Verb

assimilate:/əˈsɪməleɪt/
|v.|吸收; 同化; 消化|To take in and incorporate as one's own; absorb.|She assimilates information quickly. 她吸收信息很快。|Synonyms:  integrate, adapt, absorb, incorporate, internalize|Antonyms: segregate, isolate, reject|Verb

assimilation:/əˌsɪməˈleɪʃn/
|n.|吸收; 同化; 消化|The process of taking in and fully understanding information or ideas.|The assimilation of facts and figures takes time and concentration. 对事实和数据的吸收需要时间和专注。|Synonyms: integration, absorption, incorporation, internalization|Antonyms: segregation, isolation, rejection|Noun
