clergeon: /ˈklɜːrdʒən/ | n. | 唱诗班的男童；助手 | A boy who assists in religious services, especially in a choir. |  The young clergeon carried the censer during the procession.  年轻的唱诗班男童在游行队伍中拿着香炉。| synonyms: choirboy, acolyte | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps layman or unbeliever in a broader sense) | noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *clerjoun, from Old French clergeon, from Late Latin *clericionem, accusative of *clericio, from Latin clericus (“cleric”).
