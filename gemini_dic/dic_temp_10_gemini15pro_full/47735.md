decagynian:/ˌdekəˈɡɪniən/
adjective
十雌蕊的
Having ten pistils or carpels.
The decagynian flower had ten distinct styles arising from the ovary.  这朵十雌蕊的花有十个从子房中伸出的不同的花柱。
decagynian:/ˌdekəˈɡɪniən/
noun
十雌蕊植物
A plant having ten pistils or carpels.
The botanist identified the specimen as a decagynian.  植物学家将该标本鉴定为十雌蕊植物。


Etymology:
From Greek *δέκα (déka, “ten”) +  *γυνή (gunḗ, “woman, female”)  + -ian.  The pistil is the female reproductive part of a flower.

Synonyms:
ten-pistilled, ten-carpelled

(No direct antonyms exist, as it's a specific descriptive term.  One could contrast it with other numerical prefixes related to pistil number, e.g., monogynous, digynous, etc.)
