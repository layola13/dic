adulator:/ˈædjʊleɪtər/| n.|阿谀奉承者，拍马屁的人|A person who uses flattery or excessive praise, especially to gain an advantage. |He is a shameless adulator of the rich and powerful. 他是一个无耻的阿谀奉承富人和有权势的人。

The CEO was surrounded by adulators who constantly praised his every decision. 首席执行官周围都是奉承者，他们不断地称赞他的每一个决定。

|近义词：flatterer, sycophant, yes-man|反义词：critic, detractor|词性：noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *adulator*, from Latin adulatus, past participle of adulari ("to fawn upon, flatter"), from ad- ("to") + ulari ("to bark, fawn").

USAGE->
He's a notorious adulator, always praising people to get what he wants. = 他是个臭名昭著的马屁精，总是奉承别人以得到他想要的东西。
She was uncomfortable with the adulator's constant compliments. = 她对奉承者不断的恭维感到不舒服。


PHRASE->
a shameless adulator =  一个无耻的奉承者
a fawning adulator =  一个谄媚的奉承者
an obsequious adulator =  一个卑躬屈膝的奉承者
a servile adulator = 一个奴颜婢膝的奉承者
a sycophantic adulator = 一个阿谀奉承的拍马屁者
