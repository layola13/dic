dramatical:/drəˈmætɪkl/ | adj. | 戏剧性的，夸张的，引人注目的 | Relating to drama or the theater; exaggerated or artificial in behavior or manner; striking in appearance or effect.  | The sunset was dramatic, with vibrant hues of orange and purple.  日落景色壮观，橙色和紫色的色调充满活力。 |  The actor gave a dramatic reading of the poem. 这位演员朗诵这首诗的方式很戏剧化。 | synonyms: theatrical, histrionic, exaggerated, striking, spectacular | antonyms: understated, subtle, low-key, ordinary, unremarkable | adjective

dramatically:/drəˈmætɪkli/ | adv. | 戏剧性地，夸张地，显著地 | In a dramatic manner; to a striking degree. |  The weather changed dramatically, from sunshine to a downpour. 天气急剧变化，从阳光明媚变成了倾盆大雨。| The company's profits have increased dramatically this year. 公司的利润今年大幅增长。 | synonyms: theatrically, histrionically, exaggeratedly, strikingly, spectacularly | antonyms: subtly, understatedly, slightly, insignificantly | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dramaticus, from Latin dramaticus (“pertaining to plays”), from Greek δραματικός (dramatikós, “of or for plays”), from δρᾶμα (drâma, “drama, play, action”), from δράω (dráō, “I do, act”).
