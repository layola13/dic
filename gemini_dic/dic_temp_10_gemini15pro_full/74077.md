fossilized:/ˈfɒsəlaɪzd/ (UK) /ˈfɑːsəlaɪzd/ (US)| adj.|僵化的，石化的，成为化石的|Transformed into a fossil; preserved or unchanging over a long period, often in a rigid or outdated form.|The dinosaur bones were fossilized over millions of years.  恐龙骨骼在数百万年的时间里变成了化石。

The company's management structure has become fossilized and resistant to change.  这家公司的管理结构已经僵化，抵制变革。|Synonyms: petrified, ossified, antiquated, outdated|Antonyms:  modern, contemporary, flexible, adaptable|adjective


fossilize:/ˈfɒsəlaɪz/ (UK) /ˈfɑːsəlaɪzd/ (US)| v.|使…石化，使…僵化|To convert into a fossil; to become rigid or outdated.|The remains of the ancient tree had fossilized over time. 古树的遗骸随着时间的推移而石化了。

Their thinking has fossilized; they're unable to adapt to new ideas. 他们的思想僵化了；他们无法适应新的想法。|Synonyms: petrify, ossify|Antonyms:  modernize, adapt, evolve|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fossiliser*, from *fossile* ("fossil"), from Latin *fossilis* ("dug up"), from *fodere* ("to dig").
