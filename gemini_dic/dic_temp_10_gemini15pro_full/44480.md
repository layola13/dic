crossbowmen: /ˈkrɒsbəʊmən/ (UK) /ˈkrɔːsbəʊmən/ (US) | n. |弩兵；弩手 | A soldier armed with a crossbow. |  The crossbowmen took aim at the approaching enemy. 弩兵瞄准了逼近的敌人。|  The king's army boasted a regiment of skilled crossbowmen. 国王的军队拥有一支技艺精湛的弩兵团。| 近义词: archer, bowman | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "rifleman" as a contrasting modern soldier) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *crosbowman*, equivalent to *crossbow* + *-man*.  The word "crossbow" itself comes from Old French *crosbog*, from *cros* ("cross") + *bog* ("bow"). This refers to the bow-like shape of the weapon mounted across a stock.
