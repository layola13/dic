nondistinguished:/ˌnɒndɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt/ | adj. | 无特征的；普通的；不杰出的 | Lacking distinguishing features or characteristics; ordinary; not prominent or eminent. | The nondistinguished building blended in with the rest of the street. 这栋不起眼的建筑与街道上的其他建筑融为一体。|  The speaker gave a nondistinguished speech that failed to capture the audience's attention. 演讲者发表了一场平淡无奇的演讲，未能抓住观众的注意力。 | 近义词: ordinary, common, unexceptional, unremarkable, undistinguished | 反义词: distinguished, prominent, notable, remarkable, exceptional | 词性:形容词

词源:
non- (prefix): not, opposite of  (例如: non-essential, non-profit)
distinguished (adjective):  having a special quality, character, or reputation
