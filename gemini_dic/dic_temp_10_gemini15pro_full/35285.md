cluttering:/ˈklʌtərɪŋ/ | n. | 杂乱，混乱 | The act of filling a space or time with too many things or activities, making it disorganized and difficult to use or manage. |  The cluttering of the room made it hard to find anything. 房间的杂乱使人很难找到任何东西。 | synonyms: disorder, mess, chaos | antonyms: order, tidiness, organization | noun

clutter:/ˈklʌtər/ | v. | 使凌乱，塞满 | To fill or cover with scattered or disordered things that impede movement or reduce effectiveness. | He cluttered his desk with papers. 他把桌子堆满了文件。 | synonyms: litter, mess up, cram | antonyms: tidy, organize, declutter | verb

cluttered:/ˈklʌtərd/ | adj. | 凌乱的，杂乱的 | Filled with or covered in scattered or disordered things that impede movement or reduce effectiveness. | Her desk was cluttered with papers. 她的桌子堆满了文件。 | synonyms: messy, disordered, chaotic | antonyms: tidy, organized, neat | adjective
