deliverances:/dɪˈlɪvərənsɪz/
|n.|解救，释放，解脱；（宗教）神助，拯救|
|The Israelites celebrated their deliverance from slavery in Egypt. 以色列人庆祝他们从埃及的奴隶制中解脱出来。|
|The mountain climbers prayed for deliverance from the blizzard. 登山者祈祷能从暴风雪中获救。|
|近义词：rescue, liberation, salvation, redemption|
|反义词：captivity, imprisonment, oppression|
|词性：名词|


deliverance:/dɪˈlɪvərəns/

|His deliverance from the burning building was a miracle. 他从燃烧的建筑物中逃脱是一个奇迹。|
|She spoke of her deliverance from addiction with great emotion. 她满怀激情地讲述了她摆脱毒瘾的经历。|
|近义词：rescue, liberation, salvation, redemption|
|反义词：captivity, imprisonment, oppression|
|词性：名词|


词源:
From Old French delivrance, from delivrer "to free, deliver," from Latin deliberare "to free from," from de- "from" + liberare "to free," from liber "free."
