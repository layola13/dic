gradiated:/ˈɡreɪdieɪtɪd/ /ˈɡrædʒueɪtɪd/| adj.|分级的，渐变的，刻度的|arranged in a series of stages, levels, or degrees, or having markings that indicate these. |The beaker contained a gradiated cylinder filled with a blue liquid. 烧杯里有一个盛满蓝色液体的刻度量筒。|The artist used a gradiated wash of color to create a subtle effect. 艺术家用渐变的色彩渲染创造出一种微妙的效果。|近义词：graduated, tiered, stepped|反义词：uniform, consistent|词性: adjective

gradiate:/ˈɡreɪdieɪt/ /ˈɡrædʒueɪt/| v.|分级，渐变，授予学位|to arrange in a series of stages, levels, or degrees, or to mark with degrees or other units of measurement; to confer an academic degree or diploma on.|The colors in the sunset gradiated from orange to pink. 日落时的颜色从橙色渐变为粉红色。|He gradiated from Harvard University in 2020. 他于2020年毕业于哈佛大学。|近义词：graduate, grade, rank|反义词：mix, blend|词性: verb
