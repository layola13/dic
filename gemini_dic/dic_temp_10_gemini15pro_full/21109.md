bliss's:/blɪsɪz/
noun
极乐；狂喜；天赐之福
The possessive form of "bliss," indicating ownership or association with bliss.
She found bliss's embrace in the quiet forest. 她在这片宁静的森林中找到了极乐的拥抱。
近义词:joy, happiness, ecstasy, rapture
反义词:misery, sorrow, despair
词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bliþs, from Proto-Germanic *bliþiz (“gentle, kind, merciful, friendly, gracious, blithe”). More at blithe. The original sense was "kindness, grace, mercy". The current sense "supreme happiness, spiritual joy" is from c. 1200.
