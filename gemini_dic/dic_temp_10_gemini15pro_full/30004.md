cavorting:/kəˈvɔːrtɪŋ/
verb
嬉戏;欢跃
Jumping or dancing around excitedly
The lambs were cavorting in the fields. 羊羔在田野里嬉戏。
frolicking, gamboling, romping
sulking, moping
v.

ETYMOLOGY->
From the French "cavourter," frequentative of "cavorer" (to prance, curvet), perhaps ultimately from Italian "cavare" (to take out, dig).
