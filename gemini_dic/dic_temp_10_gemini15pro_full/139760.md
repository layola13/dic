octad:/ˈɒktæd/ (UK) /ˈɑːktæd/ (US)| n. | 八个一组；八度音阶；八人小组 | A group or set of eight.  Specifically in chemistry, a group of eight valence electrons in an atom. | The noble gases have a stable octet (or for helium, a stable duet) of valence electrons. 贵气体有稳定的八隅体（或氦的稳定二重态）价电子。|  The octet rule explains the tendency of atoms to form chemical bonds. 八隅体规则解释了原子形成化学键的趋势。 | group, set, eight, octet | N/A | noun
