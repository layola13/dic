forbade:/fərˈbæd/| v.|禁止，不许|past tense of forbid.  It means to command against or prohibit (something).|My father forbade me to go to the party. 我父亲禁止我去参加派对。|近义词：prohibited, banned, outlawed|反义词：allowed, permitted|verb

forbade:/fərˈbeɪd/|v.|禁止，不许|American pronunciation of the past tense of forbid. It means to command against or prohibit (something).|The school forbade students from bringing cell phones to class. 学校禁止学生带手机上课。|近义词：prohibited, banned, outlawed|反义词：allowed, permitted|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *forbede*, from Old English *forbēodan* (“to forbid, prohibit, command against, renounce”), from *for-* (prefix indicating prohibition or removal) + *bēodan* (“to command, offer, bid”). Cognate with Dutch *verbieden*, German *verbieten*, Gothic *𐍆𐌰𐍂𐌱𐌹𐌿𐌳𐌰𐌽* (farbiudan).
