overstaying:/ˌoʊvərˈsteɪɪŋ/
|n.|逾期居留，逗留过久|The act of staying longer than permitted, especially in a foreign country.|He was fined for overstaying his visa. 他因签证逾期被罚款。|Synonyms: outstaying, remaining too long|Antonyms: leaving on time, departing promptly|noun

overstay:/ˌoʊvərˈsteɪ/
|v.|逾期居留，逗留过久|To stay longer than permitted, especially in a foreign country.|Many tourists overstay their visas unintentionally. 许多游客无意中超过了签证期限。|Synonyms: outstay, remain too long|Antonyms: leave on time, depart promptly|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (meaning "excessive" or "beyond") + "stay" (meaning "remain"). The word "stay" ultimately comes from the Old French "estai," meaning "support" or "prop."  The prefix "over-" adds the sense of exceeding a limit or boundary.
