handicapper:/ˈhændɪˌkæpər/| n.|让分员；差点评定员；障碍赛马预测者；|A person who assesses the relative merits of competitors, especially in horse racing, and assigns handicaps or weights to equalize their chances of winning.  |例句：The handicapper gave the favorite horse a five-pound weight penalty. 让分员给最受欢迎的马加了五磅的负重。

例句：He works as a golf handicapper for the club. 他在俱乐部担任高尔夫差点评定员。|近义词：oddsmaker, assessor, evaluator|反义词: N/A |词性:noun

handicap:/ˈhændɪˌkæp/| n. vt.|障碍；不利条件；残疾；|Something that makes it more difficult for you to do something. To give someone a disadvantage. |例句：Her lack of experience was a handicap in applying for the job. 她缺乏经验，这在申请这份工作时是一个障碍。

例句：The injury handicapped him throughout his career.  这次受伤在他的整个职业生涯中都对他造成了障碍。|近义词：disadvantage, impediment, disability|反义词:advantage, benefit |词性:noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hand in cap," a kind of lottery or game of chance where forfeits were placed in a cap.  Originally referred to a system of equalizing chances in a contest, later evolving to mean a disadvantage or disability.
