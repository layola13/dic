lie-in-state:/ˌlaɪ ɪn ˈsteɪt/| n.|公众瞻仰遗容|The practice of placing a coffin with the body of a deceased person of national importance in a public building so that people can pay their respects.|The Queen's coffin lay in state at Westminster Hall for four days.|Synonyms: ceremonial lying in repose, public viewing|Antonyms: private burial, private funeral|noun

lie-in-state:/ˌlaɪ ɪn ˈsteɪt/| v.|（遗体）停放供公众瞻仰|For a deceased person of national importance to have their coffin placed in a public building so that people can pay their respects.|The former president will lie in state at the Capitol building.|Synonyms: be displayed, be on view|Antonyms: be buried, be cremated|verb
