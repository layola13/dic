lootings: /ˈluːtɪŋz/
n.
掠夺，抢劫（loot的名词复数形式）
The act of stealing goods by force, typically during a riot or other civil disorder.
After the hurricane, there were widespread lootings of abandoned stores.  飓风过后，废弃的商店遭到 widespread 抢劫。
The lootings following the earthquake left many people destitute. 地震后的抢劫使许多人一贫如洗。
Synonyms: pillaging, plundering, ransacking
Antonyms:  donating, giving, restoring
词性: 名词

loot: /luːt/
n.
战利品，赃物，掠夺物
Stolen goods, especially those taken during a war or riot.
The soldiers divided the loot among themselves. 士兵们把战利品分了。
The pirates buried their loot on a deserted island.  海盗们把他们的赃物埋在一个荒岛上。
vt.
掠夺，抢劫
To steal goods by force, typically during a riot or other civil disorder.
Rioters looted shops and set fire to cars. 暴徒洗劫商店并放火烧汽车。
The invading army looted the city of its treasures. 入侵的军队掠夺了这座城市的宝藏。
Synonyms: plunder, pillage, ransack
Antonyms: protect, safeguard, preserve
词性: 名词，动词


词源: Hindi lūt, from Sanskrit loptram 'stolen property'.
