malapert:/ˈmæləpɜːrt/ | adj. | 无礼的，鲁莽的 |  Impudently bold; saucy.  Characterized by a bold and disrespectful, often playful, impertinence. |  The malapert child stuck his tongue out at the teacher.  这个无礼的孩子向老师吐舌头。 |  insolent, impudent, impertinent, saucy, cheeky |  respectful, courteous, polite | adjective
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mal apert "ill-behaved," from mal "badly, ill" (from Latin male "badly, ill") + apert "open, evident, expert, skilled" (from Latin apertus, past participle of aperire "to open"). The sense shifted from "unskillful, clumsy" to "impudent" in the 15th century.
