lurching:/ˈlɜːrtʃɪŋ/ | adj. | 蹒跚的，摇晃的，突然倾斜的 | Moving in a sudden and uncontrolled way, typically forwards or sideways. | The bus was lurching violently as it went down the steep hill. 公共汽车下山时剧烈地摇晃。

The drunk man was lurching down the street. 醉汉在街上踉踉跄跄地走着。 | 近义词:staggering, swaying, reeling, faltering | 反义词:steady, smooth, balanced | 词性:形容词

lurch:/lɜːrtʃ/ | n. vi. | 突然倾斜，蹒跚，踉跄 | An abrupt uncontrolled movement, especially an unsteady tilt or roll.  | The sudden lurch of the train threw me off balance. 火车突然的倾斜让我失去了平衡。

With a lurch, the boat capsized. 船猛地一晃，翻了。 | 近义词:sway, stagger, reel | 反义词:steady, balance | 词性:名词/动词


词源:
From Middle English *lurchen, *lorken (“to lie hidden, lurk”), of uncertain origin. Perhaps related to lurk.
