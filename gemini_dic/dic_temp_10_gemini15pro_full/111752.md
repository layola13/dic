lode:/lőd/ | n. |矿脉；大量；丰富|A rich source of something desirable. | The California Gold Rush began after the discovery of a rich lode of gold. 加州淘金热始于发现丰富的金矿脉。| synonyms: vein, deposit, abundance, wealth | antonyms: scarcity, lack, dearth | noun


lode:/loʊd/ | v. |装载；充满 | To load or burden heavily. | The ship was loaded with cargo. 这艘船装满了货物。| synonyms: fill, pack, burden, charge | antonyms: unload, empty, discharge | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lād (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *laþuz (“way, course, invitation”). Cognate with Old Saxon lādha (“leading, guidance”), Old Frisian lēde (“canal, watercourse”), Middle Dutch lēde (“watercourse, canal”), Dutch leide (“towpath”), German Leite (“guidance, conduct; pasture”), Old Norse leið (“way, journey, course”). More at lead.  The sense evolved from "course of a vein of ore" to "vein of ore itself" to "any rich supply."
