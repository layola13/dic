colonialists: /kəˈloʊniəlɪsts/ | n. | 殖民者，殖民主义者 | People who settle in a colony and establish political control over it, often exploiting the indigenous population and resources. | The colonialists exploited the natural resources of the conquered land. 殖民者掠夺了被征服土地的自然资源。

The colonialists imposed their own laws and customs on the local population. 殖民者将他们自己的法律和习俗强加于当地居民。 | 近义词: settlers, colonizers, imperialists | 反义词: natives, indigenous people | 词性: noun

Etymology: From "colony" + "-ist".  "Colony" comes from the Latin "colonia" meaning "farm, estate, settlement".  The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who practices or adheres to something.
