fraternism:/ˈfrætərnɪzəm/| n.|兄弟情谊，博爱|The spirit of brotherhood and comradeship; a sense of solidarity and support among people with shared interests or goals.  A specifically male camaraderie.|例句：The fraternity house was known for its strong sense of fraternism.  这个兄弟会以其强烈的兄弟情谊而闻名。

例句：The soldiers felt a deep sense of fraternism after fighting together in the war.  士兵们在战争中一起战斗后，产生了深厚的兄弟情谊。|近义词：brotherhood, camaraderie, fellowship, solidarity|反义词:enmity, hostility|词性:noun
fraternal:/frəˈtɜːrnl/| adj.|兄弟般的，友爱的|Of or relating to brothers; showing a warm, friendly feeling like that between brothers.|例句：They shared a fraternal bond that lasted a lifetime.  他们分享着持续一生的兄弟情谊。

例句：He greeted his fraternal twin with a warm hug. 他热情地拥抱了他的孪生兄弟。|近义词：brotherly, amicable, friendly|反义词:unfriendly, hostile|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *fraternitas,* from Latin *frater* ("brother").
