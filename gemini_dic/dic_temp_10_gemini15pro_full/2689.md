adrift:/əˈdrɪft/| adj.| 漂流的，漂泊的；漫无目的的，迷茫的 |  Floating or drifting freely, especially on water; lacking aim or direction. | The boat was adrift at sea. 这艘船在海上漂流。

The young man felt adrift after graduating from college. 这个年轻人在大学毕业后感到迷茫。 | 近义词: afloat, drifting, aimless, lost | 反义词: anchored, moored, grounded, purposeful | 词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From a- + drift.  "A-" is a prefix meaning "on" or "in" in this context. "Drift" comes from Old English *drifan, meaning "to drive, push, or move by a current." So, "adrift" literally means "on the drift."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The boat is adrift. = 船漂流着。
He feels adrift in his life. = 他在生活中感到迷茫。

一般过去时:
The ship went adrift during the storm. = 船在暴风雨中漂流了。
She felt adrift after her husband died. = 她丈夫去世后，她感到迷茫。


现在完成时:
The raft has been adrift for days. = 木筏已经漂流了好几天了。
He has felt adrift ever since he lost his job. = 自从失业以来，他一直感到迷茫。


过去完成时:
The sailors had been adrift for weeks before they were rescued. =  水手们漂流了数周后才获救。
She had felt adrift for years before finding her passion. = 在找到自己的热情之前，她已经迷茫了多年。


将来时:
If the anchor breaks, the yacht will be adrift. = 如果锚断了，游艇就会漂流。
Without a clear goal, he will feel adrift. = 没有明确的目标，他会感到迷茫。


虚拟语气:
If I were adrift at sea, I would be terrified. = 如果我在海上漂流，我会很害怕。
I wish I weren't so adrift in my career. = 我希望我的职业生涯不要如此迷茫。


被动语态:(Adrift is rarely used in the passive voice, as it describes a state of being rather than an action. However, a sentence could be constructed like this:)
The lifeboat was set adrift. = 救生艇被放逐漂流。
The refugees were left adrift at sea. = 难民被遗弃在海上漂流。



疑问句:
Is the boat adrift? = 船漂流了吗？
Why do you feel so adrift? = 你为什么感到如此迷茫？


否定句:
The boat is not adrift, it's anchored. = 船没有漂流，它抛锚了。
He doesn't feel adrift anymore. = 他不再感到迷茫了。



PHRASE->

cut adrift =  漂流，脱离
turn adrift =  使漂流，遗弃
go adrift =  漂流，迷失方向
be adrift = 漂流，迷茫
