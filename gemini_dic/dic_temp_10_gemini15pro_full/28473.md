carcass:/ˈkɑːrkəs (US)/ /ˈkɑːkəs (UK)/
n.|尸体；残骸；（建筑物、汽车等的）骨架，框架|The carcass of a deer lay in the forest. 一只鹿的尸体躺在森林里。|
n.|The fire gutted the building, leaving only a charred carcass. 大火烧毁了这栋建筑，只留下了一个烧焦的骨架。|
synonym:corpse, remains, skeleton, frame
antonym:living being, whole
词性:名词


carcass:/ˈkɑːrkəs (US)/ /ˈkɑːkəs (UK)/
n.|（屠宰后供食用的）畜体|The butcher prepared the carcass of the pig. 屠夫准备好了猪的尸体。|
n.|Supermarkets sell dressed carcasses of lamb. 超市出售处理好的羔羊胴体。|
synonym:body, corpse
antonym:live animal

词源：From Old North French *carquois "quiver" (source of Old French carquois), of uncertain origin.  The semantic development from "quiver" to "skeleton, carcass" is perhaps due to the resemblance of ribs to arrows in a quiver.
