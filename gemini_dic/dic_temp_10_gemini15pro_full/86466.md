hegemony: /hɪˈdʒɛməni/ /ˈhɛdʒɪmoʊni/ | n. | 霸权，支配地位，领导权 |  Leadership or dominance, especially by one country or social group over others. | The United States' cultural hegemony is visible worldwide. 美国的文化霸权在世界范围内显而易见。|  dominance, supremacy, leadership, authority, control | subordination, subjection, dependence | noun

hegemonic: /ˌhɛdʒɪˈmɒnɪk/ /ˌhɛdʒɪˈmɑːnɪk/ | adj. | 霸权的，支配的 | Relating to or constituting hegemony. | The hegemonic power of the ruling class. 统治阶级的霸权。| dominant, controlling, ruling, leading | subordinate, dependent | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From late 18th century: from French hégémonie, from Greek hēgemonia ‘leadership’, from hēgemōn ‘leader’.
