archilute:/ˈɑːrkɪˌluːt/ (US), /ˈɑːkɪˌljuːt/ (UK)| n. |大低音琵琶，弓形琵琶|A large bass lute, popular in the 17th century, with extended bass strings. It's similar to a theorbo but larger.|The archilute produced a deep, resonant sound that was favored in Baroque music.  大低音琵琶发出深沉而洪亮的音响，在巴洛克音乐中很受欢迎。|Synonyms: theorbo, chitarrone|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a specific instrument. Perhaps "small lute" for contrasting size.)|noun
