hardbeam:/ˈhɑːrdbiːm/ | n. | 硬束，硬射线 | A beam of radiation, such as X-rays or gamma rays, that has high penetrating power. |  The hardbeam from the X-ray machine allowed the doctors to see the fractured bone clearly.  硬射线机器发出的硬射线使医生能够清晰地看到骨折的骨头。| synonyms: high-energy beam, penetrating radiation | antonyms: soft beam, low-energy radiation | noun

hardbeam:/ˈhɑːrdbiːm/ | adj. | 硬束的，硬射线的 | Relating to or characteristic of a hardbeam. | The hardbeam treatment was effective in shrinking the tumor. 硬束治疗有效地缩小了肿瘤。 | synonyms: high-energy, penetrating | antonyms: soft, low-energy | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"hard" + "beam"
"hard" Old English heard "firm, solid, resistant, strong, vigorous," also "severe, rigorous, harsh," from Proto-Germanic *harduz (source also of Old Saxon hard, Old Frisian herd, Old Norse harðr, Dutch hard, German hart "hard"), from PIE root *kar- "hard."
"beam" Old English beam "tree, wood, log, timber, ray of light," from Proto-Germanic *baumaz (source also of Old Saxon, Old Frisian, Dutch boom "tree," Old High German boum, German Baum "tree, beam," Old Norse baðmr "tree," Gothic bagms "tree"), from PIE root *bhew- "to grow, exist."
