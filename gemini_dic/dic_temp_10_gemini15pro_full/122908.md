molt:/moʊlt/ (US), /məʊlt/ (UK)| v. n.|蜕皮; 换毛|To shed periodically part or all of a coat or an outer covering, such as feathers, cuticle, or skin, which is then replaced by a new growth.|The snake molted its skin. 蛇蜕了皮。|近义词：shed, cast off, exuviate|反义词: retain, keep|词性:动词, 名词

molted:/ˈmoʊltɪd/ (US), /ˈməʊltɪd/ (UK)| v. adj.|蜕皮的; 换毛的|(past tense and past participle of molt) Having shed periodically part or all of a coat or an outer covering, such as feathers, cuticle, or skin, which is then replaced by a new growth.|The bird had a molted appearance after the breeding season.  繁殖季节过后，这只鸟看起来像是蜕了皮。|近义词：shed, cast off|反义词: unshed|词性:形容词, 动词(过去式/过去分词)
