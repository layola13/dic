interlocutory:/ˌɪntərˈlɒkjʊtəri/ (US) /ˌɪntərˈlɑːkjətɔːri/| adj.|中间的，暂时的，非最终判决的|Of, relating to, or consisting of dialogue or conversation.  Given provisionally during the course of a legal action; not final or definitive.|The judge issued an interlocutory injunction to prevent the company from selling the property until the lawsuit was resolved. 法官发布了临时禁令，阻止该公司在诉讼解决之前出售该房产。|Synonyms: provisional, temporary, interim|Antonyms: final, definitive|adjective

interlocutory:/ˌɪntərˈlɒkjʊtəri/ (US) /ˌɪntərˈlɑːkjətɔːri/| n.|中间裁定，非最终判决|A non-final ruling by a court.|The interlocutory decree addressed only the issue of child custody. 中间裁定只涉及了孩子的监护权问题。|Synonyms: interim order, provisional decision|Antonyms: final judgment, definitive ruling|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *interlocutorius, from Latin interlocūtor ("one who speaks between, mediator") + -orius (suffix forming adjectives).  Interlocūtor is from inter- ("between") + loquor ("to speak").
