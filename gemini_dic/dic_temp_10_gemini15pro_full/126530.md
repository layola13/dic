nabob:/ˈneɪbɒb/ (US) /ˈneɪbɑːb/| n. | 巨富，权贵，阔佬 | A person of great wealth or prominence, especially one who acquired it in India or another part of the East during the period of British rule.  Originally, a nabob was a provincial governor of the Mogul empire in India. |  He lived like a nabob, with a huge house, servants, and expensive cars. 他过着像巨富一样的生活，拥有巨大的房子、仆人和昂贵的汽车。 |  Example: The retired nabob spent his days playing golf and traveling the world.  这位退休的阔佬每天打高尔夫球和环游世界。 | Synonyms: magnate, tycoon, plutocrat | Antonyms: pauper, beggar | noun
