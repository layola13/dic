disruptors:/dɪsˈrʌptərz/| n.|颠覆者，破坏者|People or things that cause major changes in an industry or market, often by introducing new technology or ideas.  A person or thing that prevents something, especially a system, process, or event, from continuing as usual or as expected.|例句：The rise of online streaming services has been a major disruptor in the entertainment industry. 在线流媒体服务的兴起是娱乐业的主要颠覆者。

例句：New technologies are the biggest disruptors in the modern business world. 新技术是现代商业世界中最大的颠覆者。|近义词：revolutionaries, innovators, game-changers, insurgents|反义词：conformists, traditionalists|词性: Noun (Plural)

disruptor:/dɪsˈrʌptər/| n.|颠覆者，破坏者|A person or thing that causes disruption.|例句：The company is a disruptor in the telecommunications industry. 这家公司是电信行业的颠覆者。


例句：The loud noise was a disruptor to the class.  巨大的噪音扰乱了课堂。|近义词：troublemaker, agitator|反义词：stabilizer, maintainer|词性: Noun (Singular)



ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "disrupt," from Latin *disruptus*, past participle of disrumpere "break apart, break asunder," from dis- "apart, asunder" + rumpere "to break."  The suffix "-or" denotes an agent or one who performs the action of the verb.
