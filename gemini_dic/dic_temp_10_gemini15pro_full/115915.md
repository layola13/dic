marooning:/məˈruːnɪŋ/
|n.|遗弃；使孤立无援|The act of leaving someone trapped and isolated in an inaccessible place, especially an island.|例句：The sailors feared marooning on the deserted island.  水手们害怕被困在荒岛上。|近义词：abandonment, stranding, isolation|反义词:rescue, saving|noun
maroon:/məˈruːn/
|v.|使孤立；把…困住|To leave someone trapped and isolated in an inaccessible place, especially an island.|例句：The pirates marooned their captain on a small island. 海盗把他们的船长困在一个小岛上。|近义词：strand, abandon, desert|反义词:rescue, save|verb
maroon:/məˈruːn/
|adj.|栗色的；紫褐色的|A dark brownish-red color.|例句：She wore a maroon dress to the party. 她穿着一件栗色连衣裙去参加聚会。|近义词：burgundy, brownish-red|反义词:(No direct antonym for the color)|adjective
