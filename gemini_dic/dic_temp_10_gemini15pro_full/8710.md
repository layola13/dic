antics: /ˈæn.tɪks/ | n. | 滑稽动作；古怪行为 |  Usually foolish, playful, or mischievous actions or behavior. |  The clown's antics had the children in stitches. 小丑的滑稽动作让孩子们笑得前仰后合。| synonyms: pranks, capers, shenanigans, escapades | antonyms: seriousness, solemnity | noun

Etymology: Late 16th century: from antic, adjective, ‘grotesque, fantastic’ (influenced by Italian antico ‘ancient’), from French antique, from Latin antiquus ‘old’.  The noun originally meant ‘a grotesque representation of a person or thing’, hence ‘a ludicrous gesture or action’.
