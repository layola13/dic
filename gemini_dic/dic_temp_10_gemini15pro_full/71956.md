flightier:/ˈflaɪtɪər/| adj.|更轻浮的，更反复无常的，更易变的|More frivolous, more fickle, more likely to change suddenly.|She's become flightier with age, flitting from one hobby to another.  她随着年龄的增长变得更加轻浮，从一个爱好跳到另一个爱好。|He made the flightier decision to quit his job and travel the world. 他做出了一个更轻浮的决定，辞掉工作去环游世界。|近义词：fickle, capricious, erratic, frivolous, whimsical|反义词：stable, steady, constant, steadfast, serious|词性:形容词

flightiness:/ˈflaɪtinəs/| n.|轻浮，反复无常|The quality of being flighty; fickleness.|Her flightiness made it difficult to rely on her. 她的轻浮使得很难依赖她。|The flightiness of the stock market makes investing a risky proposition. 股市的反复无常使得投资成为一项冒险的提议。|近义词：fickleness, capriciousness, volatility|反义词：stability, steadiness, constancy|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *flighti*, from Old English *flyhtig* ("timid, fearful"), from *fleogan* ("to flee, fly").  The meaning shifted from "timid" to "skittish" and then to "frivolous and fickle".
