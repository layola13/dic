clam:/klæm/| n. vi. vt.|蛤蜊；沉默；<美俚>美元|A marine bivalve mollusk with shells of equal size.|例句：We dug for clams at the beach. 我们在海滩挖蛤蜊。|近义词：mollusk, shellfish|反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑 noisy 对应 clam up)|词性:名词, 动词

clam:/klæm/| vi. vt.|闭嘴；沉默；<美俚>捞蛤|To become silent suddenly.|例句：He clammed up when asked about the money. 当被问到钱的事情时，他闭口不言。|近义词：shut up, be quiet|反义词: speak, talk|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clamme, from Old Norse *klamma (“to pinch, squeeze”), from Proto-Germanic *klammijaną (“to press, squeeze”), from Proto-Indo-European *glembh- (“to stick, cleave”). Related to clamp.  The noun is derived from the verb, referring to the clam's tightly closed shell.
