co-morbidity:/ˌkoʊmɔːrˈbɪdɪti/ | n. | 共病，并发症 | The simultaneous presence of two or more diseases or medical conditions in a patient. |  Example 1: The patient's co-morbidities, including diabetes and heart disease, made his treatment more complex. 病人的合并症，包括糖尿病和心脏病，使他的治疗更加复杂。

Example 2:  Researchers are studying the impact of co-morbidities on COVID-19 outcomes. 研究人员正在研究合并症对 COVID-19 结果的影响。 |  Synonyms: coexisting condition, concurrent illness, accompanying illness | Antonyms:  health, wellness | Noun

comorbid: /ˌkoʊmɔːrˈbɪd/ | adj. | 共病的，并发存在的 |  Relating to or denoting the presence of two or more diseases or medical conditions at the same time. | Example 1:  The study focused on patients with comorbid anxiety and depression.  这项研究的重点是患有共病焦虑和抑郁症的患者。

Example 2: Comorbid conditions can complicate the diagnosis and treatment of a primary disease. 并发疾病会使原发疾病的诊断和治疗复杂化。 | Synonyms: co-occurring, concomitant | Antonyms: singular, isolated | Adjective


Etymology:
From co- (together) + morbidity (the condition of being diseased).  The term "morbidity" comes from the Latin word "morbiditas," meaning "sickliness" or "disease."
