holoblastic:/ˌhɒləˈblæstɪk/ | adj. | 全裂的 | (of an egg) undergoing complete cleavage during early embryonic development.  Characterized by complete or total cleavage dividing the whole egg into distinct blastomeres. | The eggs of mammals, including humans, undergo holoblastic cleavage. 哺乳动物的卵，包括人类，经历全裂卵裂。|  The frog embryo exhibits holoblastic cleavage. 青蛙胚胎表现出全裂卵裂。|  meroblastic, total cleavage | partial cleavage | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
holo- (Greek) whole, entire + -blastic (Greek) pertaining to a blastomere (a cell formed by cleavage of a fertilized ovum)
