mordication:/ˌmɔːrtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ |n.| 苦行；克制；屈辱；坏疽 |The act of mortifying the flesh, subduing the body by abstinence or self-inflicted pain as a religious discipline. Also refers to a feeling of humiliation or shame, or the decay of flesh.|He practiced mortification by fasting for days. 他通过禁食数日来进行苦修。|Near Synonyms: asceticism, austerity, self-denial, humiliation, shame, chagrin|Antonyms: indulgence, gratification, pride, honor|noun


mordicate:/ˈmɔːrtɪkeɪt/ |v. |克制；抑制；使屈辱；使坏死|To subdue (the body or its needs and desires) by self-denial or discipline. Also, to cause (someone) to feel shame or humiliation, or to affect with gangrene.|He tried to mortify his desire for sweets. 他试图克制自己对甜食的渴望。|Near Synonyms: subdue, suppress, repress, humble, humiliate, shame|Antonyms: indulge, gratify, encourage, praise|verb
