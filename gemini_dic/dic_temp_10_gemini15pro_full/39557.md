conscientiously:/ˌkɒnʃɪˈɛnʃəsli/ /ˌkɑːnʃiˈenʃəsli/| adv.|认真地，一丝不苟地，凭良心|In a manner that is careful and thorough, guided by a sense of duty or morality.|She conscientiously completed all her homework assignments. 她认真地完成了所有的家庭作业。|Synonym: diligently, meticulously, scrupulously|Antonym: carelessly, negligently, irresponsibly|词性:副词

conscience:/ˈkɒnʃəns/ /ˈkɑːnʃəns/| n.|良心，道德心|An inner feeling or voice viewed as acting as a guide to the rightness or wrongness of one's behavior.|His conscience told him to return the wallet. 他的良心告诉他要归还钱包。|Synonym: morals, ethics, scruples|Antonym: immorality, dishonesty|词性:名词

conscientious:/ˌkɒnʃɪˈɛnʃəs/ /ˌkɑːnʃiˈenʃəs/| adj.|认真的，尽责的，有良心的|Wishing to do what is right, especially to do one's work or duty well and thoroughly.|He is a conscientious worker who always puts in extra effort. 他是一个认真负责的员工，总是付出额外的努力。|Synonym: diligent, meticulous, responsible|Antonym: careless, negligent, irresponsible|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin conscientia (“knowledge with,” “joint knowledge,” “consciousness”), from consciens, present participle of conscire (“to be conscious,” “to be aware of guilt”), from com- (“with,” “together”) + scire (“to know”).
