glamorizers:/ˈɡlæməˌraɪzərz/ | n. |  美化者；魅力赋予者 | People or things that make something or someone seem more glamorous than it/they actually are. | The magazine's glamorizers often portray unrealistic lifestyles. 这本杂志的美化者经常描绘不现实的生活方式。|  glamorizer is a noun. | glamorizers is the plural form of glamorizer. |  glamorizer is derived from the verb "glamorize," which in turn comes from "glamour" (meaning enchantment, magic) + "-ize" (a suffix forming verbs that mean "to make or cause to be"). The "-er" suffix transforms the verb "glamorize" into a noun denoting a person or thing that performs the action.


Synonyms: romanticizers, idealizers, embellishers
Antonyms: detractors, critics, realists
Word type: Noun
