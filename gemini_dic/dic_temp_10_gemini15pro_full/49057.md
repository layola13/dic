dehumanised:/ˌdiːhjuːmənaɪzd/ | adj. |非人化的，失去人性的 | Deprived of human qualities, such as compassion, individuality or dignity; made to seem less than human. | The refugees were living in dehumanised conditions.  难民们生活在不人道的环境中。| The constant surveillance made the workers feel dehumanised.  持续的监视让工人们感到没有人性。| 近义词: debased, degraded, brutalized | 反义词: humanized, personalized, individualized | 词性: adjective

dehumanize:/ˌdiːhjuːmənaɪz/ | v. |使非人化，使失去人性 | To deprive of human qualities, such as compassion, individuality or dignity; to make seem less than human. |  War can dehumanize even the most compassionate people. 战争即使是最有同情心的人也会变得麻木不仁。| The prison system can dehumanize inmates. 监狱系统会使犯人失去人性尊严。|近义词: degrade, brutalize, depersonalize | 反义词: humanize, personalize | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- (prefix meaning "remove" or "reverse") + humanize + -ed (past participle suffix).  The root word "human" ultimately comes from Latin "humanus".
