hibernacle:/ˈhaɪbərnækl/ | n. |冬眠处，藏匿处|A place of shelter or refuge, especially during winter; a place where something is hidden or stored.|Bears retreat to their hibernacle for the winter. 熊冬天会躲进它们的冬眠处。|Synonyms: shelter, refuge, den, lair|Antonyms: exposure, vulnerability|noun


hibernacle:/ˈhaɪbərnækl/ | n. |(圣餐)柜，圣龛|A receptacle for the reserved Eucharist in Catholic and some other Christian churches.|The priest placed the Eucharist in the hibernacle. 神父把圣餐放进了圣龛。|Synonyms: tabernacle, aumbry|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hibernaculum "winter quarters", from Latin hibernus "of winter", from hiems "winter".
