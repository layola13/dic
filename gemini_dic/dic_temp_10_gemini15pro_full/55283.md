dissociable:/dɪˈsoʊʃiəbəl/ /dɪˈsəʊʃəbəl/| adj.|可分离的；可解离的；不相关的|Capable of being dissociated or separated.  Able to be considered separately.|This study examines whether cognitive processes involved in language production are dissociable from those involved in comprehension. 这项研究检验了语言产生中涉及的认知过程是否可以与理解中涉及的认知过程分离。|The effects of the drug are dissociable from the side effects.  这种药物的疗效与其副作用是可以区分的。|近义词：separable, detachable, independent|反义词：inseparable, indivisible|词性:形容词

dissociation:/dɪˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən/ /dɪˌsəʊʃiˈeɪʃən/| n.|分离；解离；分裂|The act of dissociating or separating; the state of being dissociated. A disconnection or separation.|The dissociation of the mind from the body is a common theme in Eastern philosophy.  身心分离是东方哲学中的一个常见主题。|There is increasing evidence of a dissociation between economic growth and social progress.  越来越多的证据表明，经济增长与社会进步之间存在脱节。|近义词：separation, detachment, disconnection|反义词：connection, association, integration|词性:名词

dissociate:/dɪˈsoʊʃieɪt/ /dɪˈsəʊʃɪeɪt/| v.|分离；解离；使脱离|To separate or disconnect (something) from something else; to cease to associate with (someone).|He tried to dissociate himself from the scandal. 他试图将自己与丑闻撇清关系。|The company decided to dissociate itself from the controversial product. 该公司决定停止与备受争议的产品的关联。|近义词：separate, detach, disconnect|反义词：connect, associate, integrate|词性:动词
