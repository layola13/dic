martingale:/ˈmɑːrtɪŋɡeɪl/| n.|鞅，马丁格尔，(防止马抬头的)嚼环，(女用)束胸带|A system in gambling in which the gambler doubles the stakes after each loss, continuing until a win occurs, the assumption being that eventually a win will cover all previous losses. Also, a strap on a horse's harness that connects the girth to the noseband, preventing the horse from raising its head too high. In addition, it can refer to a part of a woman's bodice or corset.|

He tried the martingale system at the casino but quickly lost a fortune. 他在赌场尝试了鞅系统，但很快就损失了一大笔钱。


The rider adjusted the martingale to better control the horse.  骑手调整了嚼环以便更好地控制马匹。


Her 18th-century gown featured a tightly laced martingale. 她那件18世纪的长袍的特点是有一个紧身束胸带。


|近义词：(for gambling system) doubling-up system|反义词: (no direct antonym)|noun
