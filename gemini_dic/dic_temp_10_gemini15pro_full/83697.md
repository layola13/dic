hagglers:/ˈhæɡlərz/| n. |讨价还价者；讲价的人|People who bargain aggressively, especially over the price of something.|The market was full of hagglers trying to get the best deals. 市场里挤满了想买到最划算商品的讨价还价者。| The hagglers finally agreed on a price for the rug. 这些讨价还价的人最终就地毯的价格达成了协议。 |近义词：bargainers, negotiators|反义词:buyers, sellers|词性:noun

haggler:/ˈhæɡlər/| n. |讨价还价者；讲价的人|A person who bargains aggressively, especially over the price of something.|My aunt is a fierce haggler and can get a discount on anything. 我阿姨是个厉害的讨价还价者，任何东西都能拿到折扣。|He's a shrewd haggler and always gets a good deal. 他是个精明的讨价还价者，总是能得到很好的交易。|近义词：bargainer, negotiator|反义词:buyer, seller|词性:noun

词源:
From Middle English *hagelen, frequentative of haggen (“to hack, chop”), from Old Norse höggva (“to hew, cut”).  The sense development is likely through the idea of "cutting down" the price.
