fullerenes:/ˈfʊləˌriːnz/| n.|富勒烯|Large carbon molecules shaped like hollow spheres, ellipsoids, or tubes. They are similar in structure to graphite, which is composed of stacked graphene sheets of linked hexagonal rings, but may also contain pentagonal or heptagonal rings.|Researchers are exploring the potential applications of fullerenes in nanotechnology.|Synonyms: buckyballs (specifically C60), carbon nanotubes (for tubular fullerenes)|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific class of molecules)|Noun
