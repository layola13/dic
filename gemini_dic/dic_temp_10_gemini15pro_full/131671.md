nonconceptually:/ˌnɑːnkənˈsɛptʃuəli/ | adv. | 非概念地；不从概念上 | Not in terms of concepts; without involving or relating to concepts. |  He approached the problem nonconceptually, focusing on the practical aspects rather than the theoretical framework. 他非概念地处理这个问题，关注实际方面而不是理论框架。|  He reacted nonconceptually, driven by pure instinct. 他非概念地做出反应，纯粹是出于本能。 | empirically, practically | conceptually, theoretically | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
non- (prefix): from Latin non "not"
conceptual (adjective): from concept + -ual
concept (noun): from Late Latin conceptus "a thought, notion, idea," from Latin concipere "to take in, conceive," from com- "together" + capere "to take"
