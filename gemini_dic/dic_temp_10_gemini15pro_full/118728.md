metageometry:/ˌmɛtədʒiˈɒmɪtri/ /ˌmɛtədʒiˈɑːmətri/ | n. | 元几何，超几何 | The study of geometries that differ from Euclidean geometry, particularly in relation to the parallel postulate.  It explores alternative systems where the parallel postulate does not hold. |  Metageometry played a crucial role in the development of non-Euclidean geometries. 元几何在非欧几何的发展中起到了至关重要的作用。

Metageometry challenges the traditional assumptions of Euclidean geometry. 元几何挑战了欧几里得几何的传统假设。 | 近义词: non-Euclidean geometry | 反义词: Euclidean geometry | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From meta- (beyond, after, transcending) + geometry.  "Meta-" is a Greek prefix. "Geometry" comes from the Greek "geōmetria,"  meaning "earth measurement."
