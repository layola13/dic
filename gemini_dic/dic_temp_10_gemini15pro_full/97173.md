infirmest:/ɪnˈfɜːrmɪst/
adjective
最虚弱的
Superlative adjective of infirm; most weak or feeble.
He was the infirmest of the group, barely able to walk. 他是这群人中最虚弱的，几乎走不动路。
Near Synonyms: weakest, frailest, feeblest
Antonyms: strongest, healthiest, most robust
词性: 形容词


Etymology:
From Middle English *infirme*, from Old French *infirme*, from Latin *infirmus* "not strong, weak," from *in-* "not" + *firmus* "strong, firm."  The *-est* ending is the superlative suffix.
词源：
源自中古英语 *infirme*，来自古法语 *infirme*，来自拉丁语 *infirmus* “不强壮，虚弱”，来自 *in-* “不” + *firmus* “强壮，坚定”。 *-est* 结尾是最高级后缀。
