lymph-gland:/ˈlɪmf ɡlænd/ /ˈlɪmf ɡlɑːnd/
n.
淋巴结
A small, bean-shaped organ that filters lymph, a fluid containing white blood cells that fight infection.
The doctor checked the lymph glands in my neck. 医生检查了我脖子上的淋巴结。

lymph-gland:/ˈlɪmf ɡlænd/ /ˈlɪmf ɡlɑːnd/
noun
淋巴结
Part of the body's immune system, filtering lymph and producing lymphocytes.
Swollen lymph glands can be a sign of infection. 淋巴结肿大可能是感染的迹象。


ETYMOLOGY->
From lymph + gland.  "Lymph" comes from Latin "lympha" meaning "water," referring to the clear fluid. "Gland" comes from Latin "glans" meaning "acorn," due to the shape of some glands.

SYNONYMS->
lymph node

ANTONYMS->
(No direct antonym for a physical structure. The opposite of its function might be "immunosuppression," but this is not a direct antonym for the word itself.)
