chanam:/ˈtʃɑːnəm/ | n. | 灰泥，熟石灰 | A lime-based plaster traditionally used in South and Southeast Asia, particularly in construction and as a finishing coat for walls. It is made by burning seashells or limestone and mixing the resulting quicklime with water and other ingredients like sand, clay, or jaggery. |  The walls of the ancient temple were covered in chanam. 古老寺庙的墙壁上覆盖着灰泥。 | Synonyms: lime plaster, stucco, render | Antonyms: N/A | noun
