overtortured: /ˌoʊvərˈtɔːrtʃərd/ | adj. | 过度折磨的，受尽折磨的 |  Having been tortured excessively; subjected to extreme physical or mental pain.  It implies a state of being deeply affected by prolonged and severe torment. |  The overtortured prisoner finally confessed.  过度折磨的囚犯终于招供了。

The overtortured protagonist in the novel finally found peace. 小说中饱受折磨的主人公终于找到了平静。 |  Synonyms: tormented, agonized, distressed, afflicted | Antonyms: soothed, comforted, relieved, healed | adjective

Etymology:

"over-" (prefix): Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi (meaning "over, above, beyond").
"torture" (verb/noun):  From Old French torture, from Latin tortura ("torment, pain, twisting"), from tortus, past participle of torquēre ("to twist, turn, wrench").
