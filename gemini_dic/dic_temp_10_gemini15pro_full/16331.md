barbarian: /bɑːrˈbeəriən/ (UK), /bɑːrˈbɛəriən/ (US) | n. adj. | 野蛮人，未开化的人；野蛮的，未开化的 | A person considered by those of another culture to be uncivilized, primitive, or brutish; savagely cruel; unrelentingly harsh. |  The barbarians attacked the city walls. 野蛮人攻击了城墙。 | synonyms: savage, brute, vandal, philistine | antonyms: civilized, cultured, refined | noun

barbarian: /bɑːrˈbeəriən/ (UK), /bɑːrˈbɛəriən/ (US) | adj. | 野蛮的，未开化的 |  A person considered by those of another culture to be uncivilized, primitive, or brutish; savagely cruel; unrelentingly harsh. | The barbarian hordes swept across the land. 野蛮部落席卷了这片土地。 | synonyms: savage, brutal, uncivilized | antonyms: civilized, humane, refined | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek βάρβαρος (bárbaros), originally referring to anyone who did not speak Greek, perceived as speaking gibberish ("bar-bar").
