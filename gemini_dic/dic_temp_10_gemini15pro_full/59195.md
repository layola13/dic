dyspeptic:/dɪsˈpɛptɪk/ | adj. n. | 消化不良的；脾气暴躁的；易怒的 |  Suffering from or relating to dyspepsia (indigestion); bad-tempered and irritable. | He often felt dyspeptic after eating rich food. 他吃了油腻的食物后经常感到消化不良。 | Synonyms: irritable, grumpy, crabby, indigestion,  | Antonyms: good-humored, cheerful, amiable, digestion | adjective


dyspeptic:/dɪsˈpɛptɪk/ | n. | 消化不良的人；脾气坏的人 | A person who suffers from dyspepsia or indigestion; a bad-tempered person. | The old dyspeptic complained about everything.  那个脾气坏的老家伙什么都抱怨。 | Synonyms: grouch, grumbler, curmudgeon | Antonyms: optimist | noun


Etymology: From Late Latin *dyspepticus, from Greek δυσπεπτος (duspeptos, “hard to digest”), from δυσ- (dus-, “bad, difficult”) + πέπτειν (peptein, “to digest”).
