frayed:/freɪd/| adj.| 磨损的，磨破的，（神经等）紧张的| worn away or tattered along the edge; strained or irritated | The rope was frayed at the end. 绳子末端磨损了。|近义词：worn, tattered, ragged, frazzled |反义词: new, pristine, intact |词性:形容词

fray:/freɪ/| n. | 磨损处，争吵，打斗| a fight or a heated argument; a place where cloth is worn away | The fray at the edge of the sleeve showed how old the jacket was. 袖子边缘的磨损处表明这件夹克有多旧了。|近义词：fight, brawl, scuffle, argument, conflict |反义词: peace, calm, agreement |词性:名词

fray:/freɪ/| v. |（使布料、绳子等）磨损，磨破；（使神经等）变得紧张 | (cause a fabric, rope, etc. to) unravel or become worn at the edge; to strain or irritate | The constant stress began to fray his nerves.  持续的压力开始让他神经紧张。|近义词： unravel, wear, chafe, strain |反义词: mend, repair, strengthen |词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *freien, from Old French *freier, frayer (“to rub, fray”), from Vulgar Latin *frictāre, frequentative of Latin fricāre (“to rub”). Related to friction.
