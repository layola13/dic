commission:/kəˈmɪʃən/ /kəˈmɪʃn/| n. vt.|佣金，委托，授权|An amount of money that is paid to someone for selling goods or services.  The act of officially asking someone to do something. |She gets a 10% commission on every sale. 她每笔销售都能得到10%的佣金。|Synonyms: fee, payment, charge, percentage, cut,  delegation, assignment, mandate |Antonyms: salary, wage|noun

commission:/kəˈmɪʃən/ /kəˈmɪʃn/| vt.|委托，任命|To officially ask someone to do something, especially to produce something or to carry out a piece of work.  To give someone the official authority to be an officer in the armed forces. |They commissioned a study into the problem. 他们委托进行了一项关于这个问题的研究。|Synonyms: authorize, appoint, engage, hire, contract |Antonyms: dismiss, fire, discharge|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "commission" and directly from Latin "commissio", meaning "a committing, entrusting, a commission," from past participle stem of "committere"  "to commit, entrust," from "com-" (together) + "mittere" (to send).
