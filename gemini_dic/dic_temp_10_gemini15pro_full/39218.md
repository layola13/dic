conglomerating: /kənˈɡlɒməreɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 聚集，凝聚，结块 |  The process of separate things coming together to form a mass or group. |  Dust particles were conglomerating in the corners of the room.  房间角落里的灰尘颗粒正在结块。|  aggregating, accumulating, amassing | dispersing, separating | verb

conglomerate: /kənˈɡlɒmərət/ | n. | 聚集体，企业集团 | A mass formed by separate things sticking together; a large corporation formed by merging smaller, often unrelated companies. | The rock was a conglomerate of different minerals. 这块岩石是由不同的矿物聚集而成的。|  aggregation, cluster, combination | division, separation | noun

conglomerate: /kənˈɡlɒmərət/ | adj. | 聚集的，混合的 |  Formed from a number of different things or parts. | The conglomerate rock was made up of various pebbles and stones. 这块聚集岩是由各种各样的卵石和石头组成的。|  combined, composite, mixed | separate, individual | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin conglomeratus, past participle of conglomerare ("to roll together, heap up"), from com- ("together") + glomerare ("to wind into a ball"), from glomus ("ball").
