papistical: /pəˈpɪstɪkəl/ | adj. |  教皇的；天主教的（贬义） | Relating to or characteristic of the Roman Catholic Church, often used in a derogatory or critical way, suggesting excessive devotion to the Pope or Catholic doctrines. |  He condemned what he called the papistical tendencies of the church leadership.  他谴责了他所谓的教会领导层的教皇倾向。 |  The writer satirized the papistical rituals and ceremonies.  作者讽刺了教皇的仪式和典礼。 | 近义词: Catholic, Roman Catholic (neutral), popish (derogatory) | 反义词: Protestant, secular | 词性: adjective

词源: From "papist" + "-ical."  "Papist" (1530s) is a derogatory term for a Roman Catholic, from French "papiste," ultimately from Late Latin "papa" (pope).
