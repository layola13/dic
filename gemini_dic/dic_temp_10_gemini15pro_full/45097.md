cuckold:/ˈkʌkəld/ (US), /ˈkʌkɒld/ (UK)| n. v. |戴绿帽子者；使戴绿帽子|A man whose wife has committed adultery.  (noun) To make a cuckold of (a man). (verb)|例句：He was a cuckold, unaware of his wife's infidelity. 他是个戴绿帽子的人，不知道他妻子的不忠。|例句：She cuckolded her husband with his best friend. 她和丈夫最好的朋友给他戴了绿帽子。|近义词：cheated on, betrayed|反义词:faithful, loyal|词性:noun, verb

cuckoldry:/ˈkʌkəldri/ (US), /ˈkʌkɒldri/ (UK)| n. |戴绿帽子的行为；通奸|The act of adultery on the part of a married woman.|例句：The play explored the themes of cuckoldry and revenge. 这部戏剧探讨了戴绿帽子和复仇的主题。|例句：Cuckoldry was a common theme in Restoration comedies.  戴绿帽子是复辟时期喜剧的常见主题。|近义词：infidelity, adultery|反义词:fidelity, faithfulness|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French cucuault, from cucu "cuckoo," referring to the cuckoo's habit of laying its eggs in other birds' nests.  The cuckoo metaphor suggests that the cuckold is raising another man's child as his own.
