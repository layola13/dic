extrapolative:/ɪkˈstræpələtɪv/ | adj. | 外推的，推断的 | Based on extending known information or data beyond the area where it is certain, to infer values in an unobserved interval. |  The scientist made extrapolative predictions about future climate trends. 科学家对未来的气候趋势做出了外推性的预测。| Example: Based on the current growth rate, the company made extrapolative projections for the next quarter. 基于目前的增长率，公司对下一季度的业绩做出了外推预测。| Synonyms: projected, inferred, deduced, conjectural | Antonyms: interpolated, factual, observed | adjective

extrapolate:/ɪkˈstræpəleɪt/ | v. | 外推，推断 | To infer or estimate by extending or projecting known information. |  We can extrapolate from current trends to predict future sales. 我们可以根据目前的趋势推断未来的销售额。| Example:  Researchers extrapolated the results of the animal study to humans. 研究人员将动物研究的结果外推到人类身上。| Synonyms: project, infer, deduce, conjecture | Antonyms: interpolate | verb
