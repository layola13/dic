convictive: /kənˈvɪktɪv/ | adj. |  令人信服的；有说服力的 | Having the power to convince; persuasive. |  The lawyer presented convictive evidence that proved the defendant's guilt. 律师提出了令人信服的证据，证明了被告有罪。 | Synonyms: persuasive, compelling, convincing, cogent | Antonyms: unconvincing, weak, ineffective | adjective

convictive: /kənˈvɪktɪv/ | adj. |  有定罪效力的；可定罪的(法律) | Relating to or involving conviction in a court of law. (Less common usage) | The convictive proof led to the suspect's imprisonment.  可定罪的证据导致了嫌疑人被监禁。 | Synonyms: incriminating, damning | Antonyms: exculpatory, exonerating | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *convictivus, from Latin convictus (past participle of convincere "to prove wrong, refute, convict"), from com- "together" + vincere "to conquer."
