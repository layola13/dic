macroeconomists:/ˌmækroʊɪˈkɑːnəmɪsts/ | n. |宏观经济学家|Experts who study macroeconomics, focusing on the overall performance and behavior of the economy as a whole, rather than individual markets or consumers. | Macroeconomists are currently debating the effectiveness of government spending in stimulating the economy. 宏观经济学家目前正在讨论政府支出刺激经济的有效性。|Synonyms: economic analysts,  |Antonyms: microeconomists| Noun
macroeconomist (singular): /ˌmækroʊɪˈkɑːnəmɪst/

ETYMOLOGY->
From "macro-" (large, overall) + "economist" (one who studies economics).  "Macro-" comes from the Greek "makros" (long, large). "Economist" comes from "economy" which ultimately derives from the Greek "oikonomia" (household management).
