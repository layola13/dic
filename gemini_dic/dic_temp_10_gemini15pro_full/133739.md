nonfundamentalists:/ˌnɑːnˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪsts/
|n.|非原教旨主义者|People who do not adhere strictly to the fundamental principles of a religion or ideology, especially in a religious context. They are generally more open to modern interpretations and societal changes.|例句：Nonfundamentalists often embrace a more metaphorical understanding of religious texts. 非原教旨主义者通常对宗教文本采用更具隐喻性的理解。

|例句：The rise of nonfundamentalists within the church caused some friction with the traditionalists. 教会内部非原教旨主义者的兴起与传统主义者之间造成了一些摩擦。|近义词：moderates, liberals, reformers|反义词：fundamentalists, traditionalists, conservatives|词性:noun
