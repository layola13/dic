lithophyse:/ˈlɪθəˌfaɪz/| n. |石泡; 石花;|A hollow, usually spheroidal or ovoid, structure found in volcanic and some plutonic rocks. Lithophysae are typically lined with crystals of quartz, feldspar, or other minerals and are thought to form from trapped gas bubbles.|The geologist discovered a large lithophyse containing amethyst crystals within the rhyolite flow.|近义词:vug, geode, miarolitic cavity|反义词: (No direct antonym exists.  One might consider solid rock as a conceptual opposite.)|noun
