fret:/frɛt/| v. n. |烦躁，焦急；磨损；琴格|
Etymology: From Old English *fretan "to devour, consume," from Proto-Germanic *fretaną "to gnaw, eat," from Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰer- "to rub, grind."  The sense of "worry" developed from the idea of "gnawing anxiety."|
He fretted about the upcoming exam. 他对即将到来的考试感到焦虑。|
The guitar has twenty-two frets. 这把吉他有二十二个琴格。|
Synonyms (v.): worry, agonize, stew|
Synonyms (n.): ridge, bar|
Antonyms (v.): relax, calm down|
Antonyms (n.): (no direct antonym for the musical sense)|
word class: verb, noun
