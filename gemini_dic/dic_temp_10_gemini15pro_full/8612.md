anticoagulations:/ˌænˌtɪˌkoʊˌæɡjəˈleɪʃənz/
|n.|抗凝；抗凝治疗|The prevention of blood clotting by the use of drugs.  It is often necessary after surgery or for patients at risk of stroke or heart attack.|The doctor prescribed anticoagulations to prevent blood clots after the surgery. 医生开了抗凝药以防止术后形成血栓。|Synonyms:  anticoagulant therapy, thinning the blood|Antonyms: coagulation, clotting|noun
