nonfragility:/ˌnɒnfrəˈdʒɪləti/ | n. | 非脆弱性；反脆弱性 | The ability to not just withstand shocks but to benefit from them.  It's more than resilience or robustness. |  His investment strategy is built on the principle of nonfragility, seeking out opportunities that thrive in volatile markets. | 近义词: robustness, resilience, antifragility | 反义词: fragility, vulnerability, weakness | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  Derived from "non-" (not) + "fragility" (the quality of being easily broken or damaged). "Fragility" comes from the Latin "fragilis" (breakable). The concept of "nonfragility" was popularized by Nassim Nicholas Taleb in his book "Antifragile."
