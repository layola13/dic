airy:/ˈɛəri/ (US), /ˈeəri/ (UK)| adj.|通风的，空气的，轻快的，空想的|Open to the air; breezy. Light and delicate; ethereal. Not practical or realistic; fanciful.|The room was light and airy.  房间明亮通风。|Synonyms: breezy, light, ethereal, delicate, spacious, open, well-ventilated|Antonyms: stuffy, close, heavy, solid, cramped|adjective

airy:/ˈɛəri/ (US), /ˈeəri/ (UK)| adj.|轻盈的，优雅的|Graceful and effortless in movement or style.|She had an airy manner about her. 她举止轻盈优雅。|Synonyms: graceful, elegant, effortless, flowing|Antonyms: clumsy, awkward, heavy|adjective

Etymology: From Middle English *airi, from Old English *ǣrig (exposed to the air), from *ǣr (air). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *aiwaz and Proto-Indo-European *h₂éh₂ews- (to breathe).
