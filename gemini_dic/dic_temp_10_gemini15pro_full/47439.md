deafened:/ˈdɛfənd/ (US), /ˈdɛfənd/ (UK)| adj. | 震耳欲聋的；使变聋的 | Made temporarily or permanently unable to hear, typically by excessive noise. | The explosion deafened him for several days. 爆炸声让他好几天都听不见。 | synonyms: stunned, dazed, numbed | antonyms: hearing, unimpaired | adjective

deafened:/ˈdɛfənd/ (US), /ˈdɛfənd/ (UK)| v. | 使变聋 |  past participle of the verb "deafen".  | He was deafened by the loud music.  他被大声的音乐震聋了。| synonyms: make deaf, deprive of hearing | antonyms:  enable to hear, cure deafness | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *devenen, from Old English *deafian, from deaf (“deaf”). Cognate with Old Frisian deva (“to deafen”), Middle Dutch doven (“to deafen, extinguish”), German töten (“to kill”) (originally “to make dead”). More at deaf.
