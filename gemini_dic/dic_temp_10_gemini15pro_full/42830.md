courrier:/ˈkʊriər/ (US), /ˈkʌriə(r)/ (UK)| n.|信使，快递员，导游，情报员|A person or company employed to carry messages, packages, etc., especially between businesses or between government officials.|The company sent a courier to deliver the important document. 这家公司派了一名信使递送重要文件。|Synonyms: messenger, carrier, envoy, runner|Antonyms: recipient, receiver|noun


courrier:/ˈkʊriər/ (US), /ˈkʌriə(r)/ (UK)| n.|导游|A guide, especially one who accompanies and looks after tourists.|The courier showed us around the ancient ruins. 导游带我们参观了古代遗址。|Synonyms: guide, escort|Antonyms: tourist, traveler|noun


courrier:/ˈkʊriər/ (US), /ˈkʌriə(r)/ (UK)| n.|情报员|A spy, especially one who carries secret information.|The courier delivered the intelligence to headquarters. 情报员把情报送到了总部。|Synonyms: spy, agent, informant|Antonyms: none|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
