elitist:/ɪˈliːtɪst/ /əˈliːtɪst/
n.|精英主义者|A person who believes that a society or system should be led by an elite.|例句：The elitist policies of the government only benefited the wealthy. 政府的精英主义政策只使富人受益。|近义词：snobbish, highbrow, aristocratic|反义词：egalitarian, populist, democratic|词性:名词

elitist:/ɪˈliːtɪst/ /əˈliːtɪst/
adj.|精英主义的|Relating to or supporting the view that a society or system should be led by an elite.|例句：Many consider private schools to be elitist institutions. 许多人认为私立学校是精英主义机构。|近义词：exclusive, snobbish, selective|反义词：inclusive, egalitarian, democratic|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *élite* (elite), from past participle of *élire* (to elect), from Latin *eligere* (to choose out, elect).  The suffix *-ist* denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system.
