hexateuch:/ˈhɛksətjuːk/| n.|摩西五经|The first six books of the Old Testament (Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy, and Joshua) considered as a unit.|The Hexateuch offers a foundational narrative for the Jewish and Christian faiths.| synonym: Pentateuch (often used interchangeably, though technically the Pentateuch only refers to the first five books)| antonym:  (No direct antonym exists, perhaps one could consider secular literature)|noun
