ETYMOLOGY->

From flap (to move like a bird's wings) + dragon (perhaps referring to the snapping action of the flower or the perceived danger of the game).  The connection with dragons likely comes from the flower's resemblance to a dragon's mouth, which appears to open and close when squeezed.
