meanly:/ˈmiːnli/| adv.|卑鄙地；吝啬地；简陋地|In a mean or ungenerous manner; poorly; shabbily.|He meanly refused to help his own sister. 他卑鄙地拒绝帮助自己的妹妹。|Synonyms: ungenerously, selfishly, basely, niggardly, stingily, poorly, shabbily|Antonyms: generously, kindly, nobly, lavishly|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *menely, from Old English *mǣnlīċe (“meanly, commonly, generally, publicly”), equivalent to mean +‎ -ly. Cognate with Dutch meniglijk (“frequently, commonly”). More at mean, -ly.
