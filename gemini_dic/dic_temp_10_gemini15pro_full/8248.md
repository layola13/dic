anti-inductively:/ˌæntiɪnˈdʌktɪvli/
| adv. | 反归纳地 |  Reasoning or proceeding in a way that opposes or contradicts inductive methods.  Inductive reasoning typically moves from specific observations to general conclusions. Anti-inductive reasoning would then move from general principles to specific conclusions that defy common observed patterns. |  He presented his case anti-inductively, starting with a grand theory and then using cherry-picked examples. 他反归纳地陈述了他的案例，从一个宏大的理论开始，然后使用精心挑选的例子。| The scientist, known for his anti-inductive approach, dismissed the experimental results as mere anomalies.  这位以反归纳方法闻名的科学家将实验结果斥为仅仅是异常现象。 | deductively, axiomatically | inductively, empirically | adverb
