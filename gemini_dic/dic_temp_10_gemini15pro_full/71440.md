flambeau:/ˈflæmˌboʊ/ (US), /ˈflɒmbəʊ/ (UK)| n.|火炬；火把； flambeaux (plural)|A flaming torch, especially one made of several thick wicks dipped in wax and carried in processions.|The procession wound through the streets, each participant carrying a flambeau.|Synonyms: torch, flare, brand, beacon|Antonyms: darkness, extinguisher|Noun
