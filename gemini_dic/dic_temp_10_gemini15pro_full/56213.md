dodoism:/ˈdoʊdoʊɪzəm/ |n.|渡渡鸟主义；愚蠢的行为；顽固不化；墨守成规 |The practice of clinging to outdated ideas or practices, especially in the face of overwhelming evidence to the contrary.  It refers to the extinct dodo bird, known for its inability to adapt and survive. |His dodoism in sticking with the old software cost the company dearly. 他墨守成规地坚持使用旧软件，给公司带来了巨大的损失。|Synonyms:  obstinacy, rigidity, inflexibility, intransigence |Antonyms: adaptability, flexibility, open-mindedness|noun
