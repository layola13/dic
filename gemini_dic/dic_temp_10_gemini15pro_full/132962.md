nonempiricism:/ˌnɒnɪmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 非经验主义 | The doctrine that knowledge is not derived solely from sensory experience.  It emphasizes the role of reason, intuition, or revelation in acquiring knowledge. |  Philosophers debate the merits of empiricism and nonempiricism. 哲学家们争论经验主义和非经验主义的优点。 | Synonyms: rationalism, apriorism, intuitionism | Antonyms: empiricism, positivism | noun
