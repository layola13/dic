implicature:/ˈɪmplɪkətʃər/| n.|含义；暗指；言外之意|An implicature is something the speaker suggests or implies with an utterance, even though it is not literally expressed.  It's what is meant, but not explicitly said.| A: "Are you going to John's party?" B: "I have to work." (Implicature: B is not going to the party.)  A：“你会去约翰的派对吗？” B：“我得工作。” （含义：B不会去派对。）

|Example:  "It's cold in here." (Implicature: Could someone close the window?) “这里很冷。”（言外之意：有人能关上窗户吗？）|Synonym: insinuation, innuendo, hint, suggestion|Antonym: explicit statement, direct statement|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the late 20th century.  Derived from the verb "to imply" (from Old French "implier" and ultimately Latin "implicare" - to enfold, entangle, involve), with the suffix "-ature" (denoting a result or product of an action).
