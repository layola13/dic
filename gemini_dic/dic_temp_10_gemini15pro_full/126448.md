mystifies:/ˈmɪstɪfaɪz/| v. |使迷惑，使困惑|To utterly bewilder or perplex someone; to make something obscure or difficult to understand.|The magician's tricks mystified the audience. 魔术师的戏法使观众迷惑不解。|synonyms: puzzles, bewilders, confounds, perplexes|antonyms: clarifies, explains, elucidates|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *mystifier*, from mysti-, variant stem of mystique (“mystic”) + -fier, from Latin -ficāre. Ultimately from Ancient Greek μυστικός (mustikós, “initiated person, mystic”).
