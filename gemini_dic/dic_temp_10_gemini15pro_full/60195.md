effigial: /ɪˈfɪdʒɪəl/
adjective
肖像的；雕像的；模拟的
Representing or symbolizing something, especially a person, by means of a likeness, portrait, or image.
The effigial representation of the queen was placed in the town square. 女王的肖像被放置在城镇广场上。

effigial: /ɪˈfɪdʒɪəl/
noun
肖像；雕像
A likeness, portrait, or image, especially of a person.
The artist created an effigial of the king.  艺术家创作了国王的肖像。


Etymology:
From Late Latin *effigiālis, from Latin effigies (“likeness, image, portrait”), from ex- (“out”) + fingō (“I form, shape, fashion”).

Synonyms:
representative, symbolic, emblematic, iconic, portraying

Antonyms:
non-representational, abstract, formless
