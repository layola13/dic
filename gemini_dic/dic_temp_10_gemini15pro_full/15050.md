baby boom:/ˈbeɪbi buːm/| n.|婴儿潮|A period marked by a significant increase in the birth rate.|The post-World War II baby boom led to a surge in school enrollment.|synonym: population explosion, birth surge|antonym: population decline, birth dearth|noun

Etymology->
"baby" (Old English "baba," of imitative origin) + "boom" (probably imitative of a loud, resonant sound, mid-19th century).  The term "baby boom" gained popularity after World War II to describe the significant increase in births.
