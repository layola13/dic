ommateum:/ɒˈmeɪtiəm/ /ɑːˈmeɪtiəm/
n.|复眼|A compound eye, found in insects and crustaceans, made up of many individual light-detecting units called ommatidia.|The fly's ommateum allows it to see in multiple directions at once.|compound eye, facetted eye| simple eye|noun
ommatidia:/ˌɒməˈtɪdiə/ /ˌɑːməˈtɪdiə/
n.|小眼|Each of the optical units that make up a compound eye in an insect or crustacean.|Each ommatidium contributes a small part of the overall image seen by the insect.|facet, lens| - |noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ὀμματίδιον (ommatídion, “small eye”), diminutive of ὄμμα (ómma, “eye”).
