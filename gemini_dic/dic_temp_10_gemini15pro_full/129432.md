nomology:/nɒˈmɒlədʒi/ | n. | 法律学；法则学；规律学 | The study of laws, especially the general principles underlying the making of laws.  Also, the science of the mind; the branch of philosophy that investigates the necessary laws of thought. | Example 1: His dissertation explored the nomology of contract law in medieval England. 他在论文中探讨了中世纪英格兰合同法的法理学。

Example 2: Kantian nomology attempts to establish the universal principles of moral action. 康德的法则学试图建立普遍的道德行为原则。 | Near Synonyms: jurisprudence, legislation, lawmaking | Antonyms: lawlessness, anarchy | noun
