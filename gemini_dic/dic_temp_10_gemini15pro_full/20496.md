bivalve:/ˈbaɪvælv/ | n. adj.| 双壳贝类；双瓣的 | An aquatic mollusk (such as a clam, oyster, or mussel) that has a compressed body enclosed within a hinged shell consisting of two valves.  |  例句：Oysters are a type of bivalve. 牡蛎是一种双壳贝类。

例句：The bivalve shells were scattered along the beach. 双壳贝壳散落在海滩上。| 近义词：clam, mussel, oyster, scallop | 反义词: univalve | 词性: 名词, 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *bivalvis (“having two valves”), from bi- (“two”) + valva (“leaf of a folding door, valve”).
