chicken:/ˈtʃɪkɪn/ (US), /ˈtʃɪkɪn/ (UK)| n. adj.|鸡，鸡肉；胆小鬼|A domestic fowl kept for its eggs or meat, especially a young one.  Also used to describe someone cowardly. |例句：We had roast chicken for dinner. 我们晚餐吃了烤鸡。|近义词：fowl, poultry (for the animal); coward, wimp (for the cowardly sense)|反义词:brave, courageous (for the cowardly sense)|noun, adjective

chicken:/ˈtʃɪkɪn/ (US), /ˈtʃɪkɪn/ (UK)| adj.|胆怯的，懦弱的|Afraid to do something seen as risky or dangerous.  |例句：He was too chicken to ask her out. 他太胆小了，不敢约她出去。|近义词：cowardly, timid, fearful|反义词:brave, courageous|adjective
