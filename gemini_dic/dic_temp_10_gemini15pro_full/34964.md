cloddish:/ˈklɑːdɪʃ/ /ˈklɒdɪʃ/| adj.|笨拙的，粗鲁的，土气的|Lacking grace or refinement; awkward and clumsy in behavior or appearance.  Derived from "clod," meaning a lump of earth, suggesting someone who is as unrefined as dirt.|He gave a cloddish bow, nearly knocking over the vase. 他笨拙地鞠了一躬，差点把花瓶碰倒。|Synonyms: awkward, clumsy, gauche, ungainly, oafish, boorish|Antonyms: graceful, elegant, refined, sophisticated|Adjective
