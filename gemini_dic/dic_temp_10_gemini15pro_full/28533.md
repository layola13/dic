cardinalism: /ˈkɑːrdɪnəlɪzəm/| n. |基数主义；数量主义|A system of economic thought that emphasizes the importance of quantifiable measurements in economic analysis, especially focusing on utility and welfare as measurable and comparable entities.|The economist argued against cardinalism, preferring ordinal utility theory.|近义词: utilitarianism, welfarism|反义词: ordinalism|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From cardinal (fundamental) + -ism (doctrine, belief)
