occultnesses:/əˈkʌltnɪsɪz/
n.
神秘；隐秘；奥秘
The state or quality of being occult; mysteriousness; secrecy.
The occultnesses surrounding the ancient ritual intrigued the historians.  围绕着古代仪式的神秘感激起了历史学家们的兴趣。
The book explored the occultnesses of ancient civilizations. 这本书探讨了古代文明的神秘之处。
近义词:secrecy, mystery, mystique, enigma, abstruseness
反义词: clarity, openness, obviousness,  transparency
词性:名词(plural)

occultness:/əˈkʌltnɪs/
n.
神秘；隐秘；奥秘
The state or quality of being occult; mysteriousness; secrecy.
The occultness of the ceremony added to its power.  仪式的隐秘性增加了它的力量。

词性:名词(singular)

occult:/əˈkʌlt/
adj.
神秘的；玄妙的；隐秘的
Of, relating to, or dealing with supernatural influences, agencies, or phenomena.
She studied occult practices in ancient cultures. 她研究古代文化中的神秘习俗。
The group explored occult knowledge. 这个小组探索神秘的知识。
n.
神秘的事物；玄学
Supernatural agencies, phenomena, or practices.
He delved into the occult. 他钻研玄学。
近义词: mysterious, secret, hidden, esoteric, supernatural
反义词: known, obvious, revealed, apparent, exoteric
词性:形容词, 名词


词源: From Late Latin occultus (“hidden, secret”), past participle of occulere (“to cover over, conceal”), from ob (“over, against, before”) + calere (“to hide”).
