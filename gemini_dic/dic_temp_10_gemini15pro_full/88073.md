highlight:/ˈhaɪˌlaɪt/ | v. n. adj. | 突出；强调；加亮；最精彩的部分 | To emphasize something, making it more noticeable or prominent.  |

例句：
The report highlights the need for improved safety measures.
这份报告强调了加强安全措施的必要性。

例句：
Use yellow marker to highlight the important sentences.
用黄色记号笔把重要的句子标出来。

近义词：emphasize, stress, feature, spotlight
反义词：downplay, obscure, de-emphasize
词性：verb, noun, adjective


highlight:/ˈhaɪˌlaɪt/ | n. | 最精彩的部分 | The most interesting or exciting part of something. |

例句：
The highlight of the trip was our visit to the Grand Canyon.
这次旅行最精彩的部分是我们参观大峡谷。

例句：
Scoring the winning goal was the highlight of his career.
打进制胜球是他职业生涯中最精彩的时刻。

近义词：best part, high point, climax
反义词：low point, nadir
词性：noun


highlight:/ˈhaɪˌlaɪt/ | adj. |  突出的；加亮的 |  Made noticeable or prominent, often with color or light. |

例句：
The highlighted text was easy to find.
加亮的文字很容易找到。

例句：
Her highlighted hair looked stunning.
她挑染的头发看起来很漂亮。

近义词：emphasized, marked, prominent
反义词：unmarked, dull, inconspicuous
词性：adjective


词源：From the verb phrase "high light," meaning to illuminate brightly.
