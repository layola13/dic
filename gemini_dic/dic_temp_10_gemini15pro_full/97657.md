inhibited:/ɪnˈhɪbɪtɪd/ | adj. | 害羞的，拘谨的，压抑的 |  Held back or restrained, often due to self-consciousness or fear.  Characterized by a reluctance to express one's feelings or desires openly. |  She felt inhibited from speaking freely in front of her boss.  她在老板面前不敢畅所欲言。 | synonyms: shy, reserved, reticent, repressed, constrained | antonyms: uninhibited, outgoing, extroverted, expressive, confident | adjective


inhibit:/ɪnˈhɪbɪt/ | verb | 抑制，阻止 | To restrain, hinder, or prevent (an action or process). |  The drug inhibits the growth of cancer cells.  这种药物抑制癌细胞的生长。 | synonyms: restrain, hinder, impede, suppress, curb | antonyms: encourage, promote, stimulate, facilitate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin inhibitus, past participle of inhibere "to restrain, hold back," from in- "in, on" + habere "to hold."
