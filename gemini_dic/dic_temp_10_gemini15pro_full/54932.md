dispositive:/dɪˈspɒzɪtɪv/ /dɪˈspɑːzɪtɪv/ | adj. n.| 处置性的，最终的；决定因素；配置 |Having the power or authority to settle an issue; decisive.  A factor, thing, or event that brings about a result or is decisive.|
例句：The judge's ruling was dispositive of the case. 法官的裁决对案件具有最终决定性。

例句：Her testimony was the dispositive factor in the jury's decision. 她的证词是陪审团做出决定的决定性因素。

近义词：conclusive, decisive, definitive, determinative |
反义词：inconclusive, indecisive, ambiguous|
词性：adjective, noun


dispositive:/dɪˈspɒzɪtɪv/ /dɪˈspɑːzɪtɪv/| n. |配置；最终裁决；处置权| Something that settles a matter; the power to settle an issue. |
例句：The will contained a dispositive clause regarding the estate. 遗嘱中包含一项关于遗产的处置条款。

例句：The CEO has dispositive authority over the company's assets. 首席执行官拥有对公司资产的处置权。

近义词：decree, mandate, order, ruling |
反义词： N/A (As a noun, it doesn't readily have antonyms) |
词性：noun


词源: From Late Latin *dispositivus, from Latin disponere "to arrange," from dis- "apart" + ponere "to put, place."
