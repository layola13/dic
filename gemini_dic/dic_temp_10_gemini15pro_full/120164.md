mind-numbingly:/ˈmaɪndˌnʌmɪŋli/| adv.|极其枯燥地，令人麻木地|Extremely boring and tedious, causing mental numbness.|The lecture was mind-numbingly dull.  这讲座枯燥得令人麻木。|Synonyms: tedious, boring, monotonous, dull, insipid|Antonyms: exciting, stimulating, interesting, engaging|Adverb

Etymology:
From "mind-numbing" + "-ly".  "Mind-numbing" is a compound adjective formed from "mind" (referring to mental processes) and "numbing" (causing a loss of sensation).  "Numbing" ultimately derives from the Old English word "numen," meaning "taken, seized."
