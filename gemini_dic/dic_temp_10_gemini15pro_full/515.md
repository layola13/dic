abolitionism: /ˌæbəˈlɪʃənɪzəm/
n.
废奴主义
The principle or policy of abolition, especially of slavery or of the death penalty.  The doctrine that calls for the abolition of slavery.
The abolitionism movement played a crucial role in ending slavery in the United States.  美国的废奴运动在结束奴隶制方面发挥了至关重要的作用。

Abolitionism gained momentum in the 19th century. 废奴主义在19世纪获得了发展势头。

近义词: anti-slavery
反义词: pro-slavery
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French abolitionnisme, from abolition + -isme.  Ultimately from Latin abolitio.

USAGE->
The rise of abolitionism in the 19th century led to significant social and political change. 19世纪废奴主义的兴起导致了重大的社会和政治变革。

Her strong belief in abolitionism prompted her to join the movement. 她对废奴主义的坚定信念促使她加入了这场运动。
