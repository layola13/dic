clinching:/ˈklɪntʃɪŋ/| adj.| 决定性的，确凿的，关键性的 |Serving as a decisive factor; conclusive.  A clinching argument settled the debate. |例句：The clinching evidence secured the conviction.  确凿的证据确保了定罪。

例句：Her clinching performance in the final round won her the gold medal.  她在最后一轮的决定性表现为她赢得了金牌。|近义词：decisive, conclusive, confirming, definitive|反义词：inconclusive, ambiguous, uncertain|词性:形容词

clinching:/ˈklɪntʃɪŋ/| n.| 钉牢，敲弯钉头，拥抱|The act of securing something, often by bending a nail or other fastener. Also, the act of embracing or holding tightly.  The clinching of the deal brought relief to all parties.|例句：The clinching of the nails secured the boards together.  钉牢钉子将木板固定在一起。

例句：Their clinching embrace showed their deep affection.  他们紧紧的拥抱表明了他们深厚的感情。|近义词：securing, fastening, embracing, hugging|反义词：releasing, loosening|词性:名词


Etymology: From the verb "clinch," from Middle English "clenchen," from Old English "clencan" (to make fast, to bend).
