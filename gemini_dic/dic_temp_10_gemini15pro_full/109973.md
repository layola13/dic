liberta:/liˈbɛrtə/ | n. | freedom, liberty | The state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions imposed by authority on one's way of life, behavior, or political views. |  The people fought for liberta from the tyrannical ruler. 人民为摆脱暴君的统治而争取自由。|  She longed for the liberta to express her artistic vision. 她渴望自由地表达她的艺术愿景。 | synonyms: freedom, liberty, independence, autonomy | antonyms: captivity, confinement, oppression, subjugation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin lībertās (“freedom, liberty”), from līber (“free”).
