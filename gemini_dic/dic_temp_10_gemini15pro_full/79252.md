glasshouse:/ˈɡlɑːshaʊs/ /ˈɡlæsˌhaʊs/| n. |温室，玻璃暖房; (易受批评或攻击的)不安全地位|A glasshouse is a building with walls and a roof made of glass, used for growing plants that need warmth and protection.  Figuratively, it refers to a situation where someone is vulnerable to criticism or attack. |例句：The tomatoes are growing well in the glasshouse.  西红柿在温室里长得很好。

例句：Politicians live in a glasshouse and should be careful about criticizing others. 政客们生活在玻璃房子里，批评别人时应该小心谨慎。|近义词：greenhouse, conservatory, hothouse|反义词:  (无直接反义词，可考虑outdoor, open air)|词性: noun
