chowed-down:/tʃaʊd daʊn/
|v.|狼吞虎咽地吃；大快朵颐|To eat enthusiastically and quickly, often a large amount.  Origin: "Chow" is slang for food, likely derived from Chinese pidgin English. "Down" emphasizes the act of consuming.| We chowed down on pizza after the game. 我们赛后狼吞虎咽地吃了披萨。|近义词：gobble, devour, wolf down|反义词: nibble, pick at
词性: 动词
