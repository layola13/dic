coelacanth: /ˈsiːləkænθ/ (US), /ˈsiːləkæntθ/ (UK)| n. |腔棘鱼|A rare order of fish (Coelacanthiformes) that includes two extant species and was thought to have been extinct since the Cretaceous Period, but living examples were discovered in 1938.  It is sometimes called a "living fossil".|例句：The discovery of the coelacanth astonished the scientific world. 腔棘鱼的发现震惊了科学界。

例句：Coelacanths are deep-sea fish that can live for decades. 腔棘鱼是生活在深海中, 可以存活数十年的鱼类。|近义词：living fossil|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider "extinct species")|词性:noun
