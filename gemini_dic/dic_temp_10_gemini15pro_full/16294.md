baptizing:/ˈbæptaɪzɪŋ/
| v.|施洗，命名|The priest is baptizing the baby.  牧师正在给婴儿施洗。|
|English Explanation: The present participle form of "baptize," meaning the act of performing a baptism. It can refer to the religious ceremony of admitting someone into the Christian Church, typically by immersing them in water or sprinkling water on them. It can also refer to giving something a name, especially for the first time.|
|例句：They are baptizing their newborn daughter next Sunday. 他们下周日要为他们刚出生的女儿施洗。|
|例句：The ship was christened, or baptized, the "Seafarer." 这艘船被命名，或者说受洗，为“航海家”。|
|近义词：christening, naming, dedicating|
|反义词:|
|词性: Verb (present participle)|


baptize:/ˈbæptaɪz/
| vt.|施洗，给…施洗礼；命名，给…命名|The minister baptized the baby with holy water.  牧师用圣水为婴儿施洗。|
|English Explanation: To perform the religious ceremony of baptism. It can also mean to give a name to something, especially for the first time.  Derived from Greek "baptizein" meaning "to dip, immerse."|
|例句：John the Baptist baptized Jesus in the River Jordan.  施洗约翰在约旦河里为耶稣施洗。|
|例句：They baptized the new ship "Ocean Explorer." 他们将新船命名为“海洋探险者”。|
|近义词：christen, name, dedicate|
|反义词:|
|词性: Verb|
