disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/
n.|幻灭，失望|Disillusion is a feeling of disappointment resulting from the discovery that something is not as good as one believed it to be.  幻灭是一种发现某事物不如自己想象中那么好而产生的失望感。|The corruption scandal led to widespread disillusion with the government. 腐败丑闻导致人们对政府普遍感到失望。|disappointment, disenchantment|hope, optimism
disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/
vt.|使醒悟，使不再抱幻想|To disillusion someone is to cause them to realize that something they believed to be true or good is not. 使某人醒悟是指使他们意识到他们认为真实或好的事情实际上并非如此。|The harsh realities of war quickly disillusioned the young soldiers. 战争的残酷现实很快让年轻的士兵们不再抱有幻想。|disenchant, undeceive|delude, deceive
