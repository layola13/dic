froth:/frɒθ/ (UK) /frɔːθ/ (US)| n. v.|泡沫，起泡沫|A mass of small bubbles formed on or in liquid, typically by agitation or fermentation.  As a verb, it means to produce or form froth.|
The beer had a thick head of froth. 啤酒上有一层厚厚的泡沫。
He frothed the milk for his cappuccino. 他把牛奶打成泡沫做卡布奇诺。|
近义词：foam, lather, bubbles, spume|
反义词:flat (for drinks)|
词性:noun, verb|
词源:From Old Norse *froða, from Proto-Germanic *frōþōn, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)prewdʰ- (“to spurt, scatter”). Cognate with Icelandic froða (“froth”), German fraß (“food for animals, fodder”).
