abbatess:/ˈæbətɪs/| n.|女院长，女修道院院长|A woman who is the head of an abbey of nuns.|The abbess presided over the convent for many years.  这位女院长管理修道院很多年了。
The abbess welcomed the new novices into the order. 女院长欢迎新来的修女加入修会。|近义词：prioress, mother superior|反义词:monk, abbot|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *abatesse*, from Latin *abbatissa*, feminine of *abbas* "abbot," from Aramaic *abba* "father."  Ultimately from the same source as Hebrew *ab* "father."  The word reflects the role of the abbess as a spiritual mother to the nuns in her abbey.

USAGE->
The word "abbess" primarily functions as a noun and doesn't have verb, adjective, or adverbial forms.  It's used in sentences to refer to the female head of an abbey.
