ghettoing:/ˈɡɛtəʊɪŋ/
| n. |隔离；限制；使边缘化|The act of forcing or confining a particular group of people to live in a certain area, often with poor living conditions and limited opportunities, or treating them in a way that restricts their freedom and opportunities.|The government has been accused of ghettoing refugees in overcrowded camps.|近义词：segregating, isolating, marginalizing|反义词:integrating, including|词性:名词(动词的ing形式，用作名词)

ghettoize:/ˈɡɛtəʊaɪz/
| vt. |隔离；限制；使边缘化|To force or confine a particular group of people to live in a certain area, often with poor living conditions and limited opportunities, or to treat them in a way that restricts their freedom and opportunities.|Critics argue that the new housing policy will further ghettoize low-income families.|近义词：segregate, isolate, marginalize|反义词:integrate, include|词性:动词

词源: From ghetto + -ize or -ing.  Ghetto itself comes from Venetian Italian *ghèto, perhaps from Venetian *gèto “foundry” (site of the first ghetto in Venice), or from Late Latin jactare “to throw,” in the sense of “rubbish dump.”
