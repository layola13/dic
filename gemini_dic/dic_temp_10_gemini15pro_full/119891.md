militarisms:/ˈmɪlɪtərɪzəmz/
n.|军国主义|The belief or desire of a government or people that a country should maintain a strong military capability and be prepared to use it aggressively to defend or promote national interests.|The rise of militarisms in the region led to an arms race.|近义词：bellicosity, warmongering, jingoism|反义词：pacifism, peacefulness|词性:名词

Etymology: From French *militarisme*, from *militaire* "military" (see military) + *-isme*.
