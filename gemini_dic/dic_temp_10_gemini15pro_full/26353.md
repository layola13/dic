bwanas:/ˈbwɑːnəz/
noun
主人；老爷；先生 (plural)
A term of respect used to address or refer to a white man in colonial East Africa.  Derived from Swahili, ultimately from Arabic. Originally meant "master".  Often carries colonial connotations and considered offensive in modern usage.
The bwanas inspected the tea plantation.  老爷们视察了茶园。
The safari was arranged for the bwanas.  为老爷们安排了狩猎之旅。
synonyms: masters, bosses, lords, sirs (though these don't carry the same colonial baggage)
antonyms: servants, slaves (in a historical context)
