blindingly:/ˈblaɪndɪŋli/| adv.|令人眼花缭乱地，耀眼地，极其地|So bright as to make one unable to see properly.  Used figuratively to mean extremely or excessively. |The sun was blindingly bright. 太阳耀眼得令人睁不开眼。|Synonyms: dazzlingly, overwhelmingly, extremely, intensely|Antonyms: dimly, faintly|Adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From blind (adjective) + -ing (suffix forming present participles and adjectives) + -ly (suffix forming adverbs).  The word "blind" comes from Old English "blind," of Germanic origin.
