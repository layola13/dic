furies:/ˈfjʊəriːz/
noun
复仇女神；狂怒；暴怒
The Furies, also known as the Erinyes, are figures from Greek mythology, goddesses of vengeance who pursued those guilty of heinous crimes, particularly matricide.  They are often depicted as winged creatures with snakes for hair.
The furies of the storm lashed the coast.  暴风雨的狂怒席卷了海岸。
Synonyms: rage, wrath, vengeance, anger, frenzy
Antonyms: calmness, peace, tranquility
