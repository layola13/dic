homely:/ˈhoʊmli/ (US) /ˈhəʊmli/ (UK)| adj.|家常的；朴素的；相貌平平的；不好看的|Simple and unpretentious; plain or unattractive in appearance.  It suggests a lack of sophistication or elegance, and can sometimes be perceived as negative when referring to appearance.  In American English, it can also mean comfortable and cozy, like home.|例句：She wore a homely dress. 她穿着一件朴素的连衣裙。|例句：The restaurant had a homely atmosphere. 这家餐厅有一种家常的氛围。|近义词：plain, simple, unpretentious, unattractive (appearance), cozy, comfortable (American English)|反义词：stylish, elegant, sophisticated, beautiful, attractive|词性:形容词

词源: From Old English *hāmlic "belonging to a house, domestic," from hām "home" + -lic (cognate with German -lich). The sense of "ugly" developed in the late 16th century, perhaps from the notion of one who rarely leaves home and thus lacks polish.
