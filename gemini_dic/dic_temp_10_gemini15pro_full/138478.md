novelty:/ˈnɒvəlti/ (UK) /ˈnɑːvəlti/ (US)| n.|新奇; 新奇的事物; 新颖小巧而廉价的物品|Something new or unusual.  The quality of being new, original, or unusual.|例句：The novelty of the experience soon wore off.  这种体验的新鲜感很快就消失了。|近义词：originality, freshness, innovation|反义词: staleness, banality, familiarity|词性:noun

novelty:/ˈnɒvəlti/ (UK) /ˈnɑːvəlti/ (US)| adj.|(only before noun) 新奇的；新颖的|Of a new kind or design.|例句：Novelty mugs are a popular souvenir.  新奇的马克杯是一种很受欢迎的纪念品。|近义词：new, original, unusual|反义词: old, familiar, common|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *noveleté, from Latin novellitas ("newness"), from novellus ("new, young, recent").  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *néwos ("new").
