grouchier:/ˈɡraʊtʃɪər/| adj.|更爱抱怨的，更易怒的|Comparative form of grouchy: more grouchy.  Meaning even more inclined to complain or be irritable.|He seemed even grouchier than usual this morning. 他今天早上看起来比平时更暴躁。|近义词：grumpier, more irritable, crankier|反义词：happier, more cheerful, more pleasant|词性:形容词

grouch:/ɡraʊtʃ/| n. vi.|抱怨，发牢骚；脾气坏的人|A person who complains frequently or is often bad-tempered; the act of complaining.  Derived from the verb "to grouch," meaning to complain or grumble.  Ultimate origin uncertain, possibly related to Middle Dutch "grūwelick" meaning "horrible," or Old French "grochier" meaning "to grumble."|He's such a grouch in the mornings. 他早上脾气真坏。|近义词：grump, complainer, curmudgeon|反义词：optimist, cheerful person|词性:名词,动词
