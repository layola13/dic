nonvitriolic: /ˌnɒnvɪtriˈɒlɪk/ | adj. | 温和的，不刻薄的 | Not containing or expressing harsh or corrosive criticism. |  Her nonvitriolic response surprised everyone, given the harsh criticism she had received.  她温和的回应让每个人都感到惊讶，考虑到她受到了严厉的批评。| Synonyms: gentle, mild, kind, inoffensive, conciliatory | Antonyms: vitriolic, caustic, acrimonious, bitter, scathing | adjective

Etymology:
From non- + vitriolic.  "Vitriolic" comes from the Latin "vitriolum" (vitriol, sulfuric acid), referring to the corrosive nature of the substance, which was extended metaphorically to describe harsh language.  The prefix "non-" simply negates the meaning.
