distractible:/dɪˈstræktəbl/| adj.|易分心的，注意力不集中的|Easily diverted from the task at hand; easily distracted.|He's very distractible and finds it hard to concentrate in noisy environments. 他很容易分心，很难在嘈杂的环境中集中注意力。|The open office plan made her even more distractible. 开放式办公室让她更容易分心。|近义词：inattentive, unfocused, easily distracted|反义词：focused, attentive, concentrated|词性:形容词
distractibility:/dɪˌstræktəˈbɪləti/| n.|分心，注意力不集中|The quality of being easily distracted.|Her distractibility is affecting her schoolwork. 她的注意力不集中影响了她的学业。|The study investigated the relationship between distractibility and academic performance. 这项研究调查了注意力不集中与学业成绩之间的关系。|近义词：inattentiveness, lack of focus|反义词：focus, attention, concentration|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From distract + -ible.  Distract comes from the Latin "distractus," past participle of "distrahere," meaning "to draw apart, divide, separate, distract." "-ible" is a suffix meaning "capable of being."
