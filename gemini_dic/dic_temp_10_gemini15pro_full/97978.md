inoceramus:/ˌɪnəˈsɛrəməs/| n. |菊石|An extinct marine bivalve mollusk of the Cretaceous period, having a pearly shell.  |例句：Inoceramus fossils are common in Cretaceous rocks. 菊石化石在白垩纪岩石中很常见。

例句：The museum displayed a large Inoceramus shell.  博物馆展出了一个大型菊石壳。|近义词：bivalve, mollusk |反义词: N/A |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *īnōcerāmus*, from *īnōcerus* (“hornless”) + Ancient Greek κέρας (*kéras*, “horn”).  The name refers to the lack of a prominent beak or "horn" compared to some other bivalves.
