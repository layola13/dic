concealments:/kənˈsiːlmənts/
n.
隐藏，隐瞒；隐蔽处
the act of hiding something or the state of being hidden
例句：The concealments of the spy were eventually discovered.  间谍的藏身之处最终被发现了。

例句：The government was accused of concealments of vital information. 政府被指控隐瞒重要信息。

近义词：cover-ups, disguises, secrets, hiding places
反义词：revelations, disclosures, exposures
词性：名词(plural)


concealment:/kənˈsiːlmənt/
n.
隐藏，隐瞒；隐蔽处
the act of hiding something or the state of being hidden
例句：The witness went into concealment to avoid intimidation.  证人躲藏起来以避免恐吓。

例句：The thief's concealment in the shadows was effective.  小偷藏在阴影里很有效。

近义词：hiding, secrecy, cover-up
反义词：disclosure, revelation, exposure
词性：名词(singular)
