grille:/ɡrɪl/| n.|格栅，烤架|A grating or screen of metal bars or wires, placed in front of something as protection or to allow ventilation/air to pass through.  A frame of metal bars for cooking food over a fire.|The car’s grille was damaged in the accident. 汽车的格栅在事故中受损了。|synonyms: grating, grid, mesh, screen|antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider "solid" or "unobstructed")|noun

grille:/ɡrɪl/| v.|烧烤，拷问|To cook food over direct heat, usually on a grill. To subject someone to intense questioning.|I grilled the steaks to perfection. 我把牛排烤得恰到好处。  The police grilled the suspect for hours. 警方对嫌疑人进行了数小时的审问。|synonyms: broil, barbecue, interrogate, question|antonyms: chill, freeze (for cooking);  exonerate, release (for questioning)|verb
