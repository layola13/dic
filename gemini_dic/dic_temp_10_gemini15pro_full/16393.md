barble: /ˈbɑːrbəl/ (US) /ˈbɑːbl/ (UK) | n. |倒刺，芒刺，触须| A slender, whisker-like sensory organ near the mouth of some fish.  A sharp projection pointing backward, as on a fishhook or arrow. | The catfish uses its barbels to locate food in murky water.  The barb on the fishhook made it difficult to remove. | 近义词: whisker, spine, thorn | 反义词: smoothness | noun

barble: /ˈbɑːrbəl/ (US) /ˈbɑːbl/ (UK) | v. |(鱼)用触须探索；带刺地说；批评|To explore with barbels; to speak in a critical or barbed manner. | The fish barbled along the bottom of the tank.  He barbled a sarcastic remark about her new dress. | 近义词: probe, criticize, carp | 反义词: praise, compliment | verb
