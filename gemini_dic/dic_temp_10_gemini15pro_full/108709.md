leaned-on:/ˈliːnd ɒn/ | adj. | 依赖的，依靠的 |  Relied on heavily; overused or worn out.  Something or someone that is "leaned-on" is depended upon so much that it's almost overused or overburdened. It can also imply a sense of being tired or worn out due to this reliance. |  That strategy is a bit leaned-on; we need to explore some new approaches.  那个策略有点被过度依赖了；我们需要探索一些新的方法。 |  The charity is leaned-on by many families in the community. 这个慈善机构被社区里的许多家庭所依赖。 |  overused, over-relied upon, dependable, trusted | independent, self-sufficient | adjective

leaned-on:/ˈliːnd ɒn/ | v. | 倚靠，依靠（lean on的过去分词） | Past participle of "lean on," meaning to depend on someone or something for support, either physically or emotionally. | He leaned-on his family during the difficult time. 在困难时期，他依靠他的家人。 | She had leaned-on her best friend for years. 她多年来一直依靠她最好的朋友。 |  depended on, relied on, counted on |  independent | verb
