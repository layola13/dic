backwardation:/ˌbæk.wərˈdeɪ.ʃən/ | n. |  现货溢价; 逆价差 | A market situation in which the price of a commodity for future delivery is lower than the spot price. This typically occurs when there is a shortage of the commodity in the spot market, leading buyers to be willing to pay a premium for immediate delivery. |  Example:  The current backwardation in the gold market suggests a strong near-term demand. 黄金市场当前的现货溢价表明近期需求强劲。

Example:  Backwardation can be an indicator of supply chain disruptions. 逆价差可能是供应链中断的一个指标。 | 近义词:inverted market | 反义词:contango | 词性:名词


Etymology: From "backward" + "-ation" (suffix forming nouns of action or process).  The term implies a price structure that is "backward" from the normal expectation of futures prices being higher than spot prices.
