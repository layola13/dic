long-livednesses:/ˌlɔːŋˈlɪvdnɪsɪz/ | n. | 长寿；长命 | The state or quality of being long-lived.  | The tortoise is known for its long-livedness. 乌龟以其长寿而闻名。 | Synonyms: longevity, durability, permanence | Antonyms: brevity, transience, ephemerality | Noun

Etymology:  From long-lived + -ness.  "Long-lived" dates back to the late 14th century, from "long" + "lived" (past participle of "live").  "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being."
