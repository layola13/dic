mellowest:/ˈmɛloʊɪst/| adj.|最醇厚的，最柔和的，最成熟的|Superlative form of mellow: most mellow.  Having reached the highest degree of mellowness.  This describes something that is extremely soft, smooth, rich, and pleasant, often due to ripening or aging.|The mellowest wine in the cellar was reserved for special occasions.  酒窖里最醇厚的葡萄酒是为特殊场合预留的。

The band played their mellowest tunes during the encore. 乐队在返场演出时演奏了他们最柔和的曲子。|近义词：softest, smoothest, richest, gentlest|反义词：harshest, sharpest, roughest, bitterest|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mellowe*, from Old English *mearu* (“soft, tender, delicate, mellow”), from Proto-Germanic *mairwaz* (“soft”), from Proto-Indo-European *mṛHw- (“soft, tender”). Cognate with Old Frisian *merwe* (“soft, tender”), Dutch *murw* (“tender, mellow, fragile”), Old High German *muruwi* (“soft, tender”), German *mürbe* (“tender, mellow, fragile”), Old Norse *mjúkr* (“soft”), Gothic *𝗺𝗮𝗿𝘇𝘂𝘀* (“soft”).
