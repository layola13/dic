nehushtan:/nɪˈhʌʃtən/| n. |銅蛇; 無價值之物|A piece of brass shaped like a serpent, originally made by Moses at God's command to heal the Israelites from snakebites (Numbers 21:4-9), but later destroyed by King Hezekiah because it became an object of idolatrous worship (2 Kings 18:4).  Figuratively, anything that is worthless or becomes an object of misplaced reverence.|The old computer had become a nehushtan, gathering dust in the corner.|Synonyms: idol, fetish, false god, worthless thing|Antonyms: treasure, valuable, sacred object|noun
