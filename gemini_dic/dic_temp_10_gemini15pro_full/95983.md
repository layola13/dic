incumbrous:/ɪnˈkʌmbrəs/ | adj. | 笨重的；累赘的；妨碍活动的 | Burdensome or hindering, especially because of weight or bulk; cumbersome. |  The knight's armor, while protective, was also incredibly incumbrous. 骑士的盔甲虽然能起到保护作用，但也非常笨重。

The antique desk, beautiful as it was, proved incumbrous when they tried to move it.  这古董桌子虽然漂亮，但在搬运时却显得笨重。| 近义词: cumbersome, unwieldy, awkward, bulky | 反义词: lightweight, manageable, portable | 词性: adjective


Etymology:
From Late Latin *incumbrosus, from incumbere "to lie or lean upon" (from in- "in, on" + -cumbere "to lie down"),  + -osus "-ous."  The original sense was "oppressive," later developing into "burdensome" and then "clumsy."
