ly:/li/ | adv. | -ly | adverbial suffix; forms adverbs from adjectives. | She sang beautifully. 她唱得优美。| 近义词: (varies depending on the adjective) | 反义词: (varies depending on the adjective)| 词性: 后缀

Etymology: Old English -līce, from Germanic *-līkō, related to Old High German -līhho, Gothic -leiko "like, similar."  Originally meant "in a ... manner" or "having the qualities of."

(The suffix "-ly" itself doesn't form phrases. It combines with adjectives to create adverbs.  Here are examples of common adverbs formed with "-ly":)

frequently 经常地
quickly 快速地
slowly 缓慢地
carefully 小心地
easily 容易地
