cowards:/ˈkaʊərdz/| n. |懦夫，胆小鬼|A person who lacks the courage to do or endure dangerous or unpleasant things.|Those cowards abandoned their posts and ran away. 那些懦夫放弃了他们的岗位逃跑了。|近义词：craven, poltroon, dastard, timid, fearful|反义词: hero, brave, courageous|noun
Etymology: From Middle English *coward*, from Old French *coart*, *couard*, from *coe*, *coue* (“tail”) + *-ard*, from Late Latin *-ardus*.  The original sense was "with the tail between the legs," like a frightened dog.
