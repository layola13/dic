ginman: /ˈɡɪnmən/ | n. | 酒保；卖杜松子酒的人 | A person who sells or serves gin. |  He worked as a ginman in a local pub. 他在当地一家酒吧当酒保。|  bartender, barman, publican |  customer, patron | noun

Etymology:
gin (n.) + man (n.)

gin (n.) from genever, shortened from Dutch geneverbes "juniper berry," from Old French genevre (see juniper).

man (n.) Old English mann "human being, person (male or female); brave man, hero;" also "servant, vassal," from Proto-Germanic *mann- (source also of Old Saxon, Old Frisian, Old High German man, Old Norse maðr, Danish mand, Dutch man, German Mann, Gothic manna "man"), from PIE root *man- (1) "man, human being."
