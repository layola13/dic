nonrationalisms:/ˌnɒnˈræʃənəlɪzəmz/
|n.|非理性主义|The rejection of reason as the primary source of knowledge or justification.  It emphasizes intuition, feeling, or experience over logic and reason.|His philosophy embraces nonrationalisms, valuing emotional responses as highly as intellectual ones.|近义词: irrationalities, mysticisms|反义词: rationalisms, logic|词性: 名词

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  from Latin *non "not"
rationalism (noun): from Late Latin *rationalismus, from Latin rationalis "of or belonging to reason, reasonable," from ratio "reckoning, calculation, reason," related to reor "I think, suppose, judge, calculate"
