bonnie:/ˈbɒni/ (UK) /ˈbɑːni/ (US)| adj.|美丽的，漂亮的；健康的，强壮的；快乐的，愉快的|From the Scottish Gaelic word "bonach" meaning "good-natured, cheerful, pleasant". Originally used to describe attractive women, particularly in Scotland, and later extended to describe pleasant or agreeable things. Primarily used as an adjective to describe someone's appearance or disposition, often implying a cheerful and healthy attractiveness. It can also be used as a name.|例句：She has a bonnie face. 她有一张漂亮的脸蛋。

例句：The bonnie baby gurgled happily.  可爱的宝宝高兴地咯咯叫。

近义词：beautiful, pretty, attractive, lovely, charming, pleasant, agreeable|反义词：ugly, unattractive, unpleasant, disagreeable|词性：adjective
