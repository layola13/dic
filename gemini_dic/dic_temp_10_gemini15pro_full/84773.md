hands-down:/ˌhændz ˈdaʊn/| adv. adj.|轻松地，毫无疑问地，绝对地|Easily and decisively; without question.  Originally from horse racing, where a jockey so far ahead could drop his hands and loosen the reins.|She won the election hands-down. 她轻松地赢得了选举。|近义词：easily, decisively, undoubtedly, absolutely, definitely|反义词:doubtfully, questionably|词性:副词, 形容词


词源:  From horse racing. A jockey who is so far ahead that victory is assured can afford to drop his hands and loosen the reins.
