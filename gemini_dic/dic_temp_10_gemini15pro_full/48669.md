defer:/dɪˈfɜːr/ (US), /dɪˈfəːr/ (UK)| v. | 推迟，延期；遵从，听从 | to postpone something to a later time; to yield to someone's wishes, opinion, or authority |  I decided to defer my university entry for a year.  我决定将我的大学入学推迟一年。| synonyms: postpone, delay, adjourn, put off | antonyms: expedite, hasten, accelerate | verb

defer:/dɪˈfɜːr/ (US), /dɪˈfəːr/ (UK)| n. | 推迟，延期 (较少用作名词) | the act of deferring something | The deferment of his military service allowed him to finish college. 他兵役的延期让他能够完成大学学业。 | synonyms: postponement, delay, adjournment | antonyms: expedition, hastening, acceleration | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *deferer, from Latin differre (“to carry in different directions, scatter, spread, put off, delay”), from dis- (“apart, in different directions”) + ferre (“to carry, bear”).
