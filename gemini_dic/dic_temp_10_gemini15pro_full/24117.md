brewhaha:/ˈbruːhɑːhɑː/| n. |骚动，喧闹，吵闹|An uproar or commotion; a noisy quarrel or excited fuss about something unimportant.|例句：There was a big brewhaha over the new dress code.  关于新的着装规定，大家议论纷纷。

例句：The media created a brewhaha about the politician's personal life. 媒体对这位政治家的私生活大肆渲染。|近义词：commotion, uproar, fuss, hubbub, hullabaloo|反义词:calm, peace, quiet|词性:noun

词源: From Scottish Gaelic *brochan*, meaning "broth, porridge," + *tathaich*, meaning "stirring frequently, urging on."  Originally referred to a spell or incantation recited over a cauldron, later evolving to describe the noise and excitement surrounding such a ritual, and eventually any noisy commotion.
