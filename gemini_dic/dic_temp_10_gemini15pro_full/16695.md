barricaded:/ˌbærɪˈkeɪdɪd/ | adj. | 被封锁的，设有路障的 | Blocked or defended with a barricade.  |  The protesters barricaded the street with burning tires. 抗议者用燃烧的轮胎封锁了街道。 |  Synonyms: blocked, obstructed, fortified | Antonyms: open, accessible | adjective

barricade:/ˈbærɪkeɪd/ | n. | 路障，障碍物 | A barrier hastily constructed, as in a street, to stop an enemy's advance. | The police set up a barricade to control the crowd. 警察设置了路障来控制人群。 | Synonyms: barrier, obstacle, blockade | Antonyms: opening, passage | noun

barricade:/ˌbærɪˈkeɪd/ | vt. | 封锁，设路障 | To obstruct or fortify with a barricade. | They barricaded themselves in the house. 他们把自己封锁在房子里。 | Synonyms: block, obstruct, fortify | Antonyms: open, clear | verb

Etymology:
From French "barricade," from Old French "barrique" (barrel) + "-ade" (suffix forming nouns).  The original barricades were often made of barrels filled with earth or other materials.
