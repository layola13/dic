depatriate:/diːˈpeɪtriˌeɪt/ /diːˈpætriˌeɪt/
|v. |遣返；使（某人）离开祖国|To force someone to leave their own country.|The government repatriated the refugees back to their homeland. 政府将难民遣返回他们的祖国。|
|n. |被遣返者；侨民|A person who has been forced to leave their own country, or who lives in a foreign country.|Many expatriates found it difficult to adjust to the new culture. 许多侨民发现很难适应新的文化。


Etymology->
From French *dépatrié*, past participle of *dépatrier* "to expatriate," from *dé-* "out of, from" + *patrie* "native land," from Latin *patria* "native country, fatherland."
