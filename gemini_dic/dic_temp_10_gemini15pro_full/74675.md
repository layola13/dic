freebooting:/ˈfriːbuːtɪŋ/
n.
掠夺;海盗行为;盗版
The act of piracy or plundering, especially on the sea.  Also refers to the illegal copying and distribution of copyrighted material.
Freebooting was rampant in the Caribbean during the 17th century. 17世纪，加勒比海盗猖獗。
Software freebooting is a serious crime. 软件盗版是一项严重的犯罪。
synonyms: piracy, plundering, looting, buccaneering, privateering, copyright infringement
antonyms: lawful trade, legitimate business, respecting intellectual property
noun

freeboot:/ˈfriːbuːt/
n.
海盗;掠夺者
A pirate or plunderer, especially one operating at sea.
The freebooters seized the merchant ship and stole its cargo. 海盗劫持了商船并偷走了货物。
He lived the life of a freeboot, sailing the seas and taking what he wanted. 他过着海盗的生活，在海上航行，随心所欲地掠夺。
synonyms: pirate, buccaneer, corsair, privateer, raider, plunderer
antonyms: merchant, trader, law enforcement officer
noun

freeboot:/ˈfriːbuːt/
v.
掠夺;从事海盗活动;盗版
To act as a freebooter; to engage in piracy or plundering.  Also, to illegally copy and distribute copyrighted material.
They freebooted along the coast, attacking unsuspecting villages. 他们沿着海岸掠夺，袭击毫无戒备的村庄。
He was accused of freebooting copyrighted software and selling it online. 他被指控盗版受版权保护的软件并在网上销售。
synonyms: pirate, plunder, loot, pillage, steal, infringe copyright
antonyms: trade legally, respect intellectual property
verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Dutch *vrijbuit* (free booty), from *vrij* (free) and *buit* (booty, loot).  Entered English in the late 16th century.
