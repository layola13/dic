hucksterer:/ˈhʌkstərər/| n.|叫卖小贩；江湖骗子；吹嘘者|A person who sells small items, either door-to-door or from a stall, often using aggressive or showy tactics.  Also, someone who promotes something in a dishonest or exaggerated way.|The hucksterer at the fair promised his elixir would cure all ills.  集市上的那个叫卖小贩承诺他的万能药可以治愈所有疾病。|Synonyms: peddler, hawker, salesman, promoter, charlatan, fraudster|Antonyms: philanthropist, benefactor|noun

huckster:/ˈhʌkstər/| n. v.|叫卖小贩；兜售；吹嘘|A person who sells small items, often using aggressive tactics. As a verb, it means to promote or sell something aggressively.|The huckster was shouting about the amazing bargains to be had. 那个小贩在大声叫卖着令人惊叹的便宜货。  He was huckstering his wares on the street corner. 他在街角兜售他的商品。|Synonyms: peddle, hawk, tout, promote|Antonyms: donate, give|noun, verb
