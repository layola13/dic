congenial:/kənˈdʒiːniəl/
adjective
意气相投的；适宜的
(of a person) having the same nature, tastes, and interests as another; (of a thing) pleasant or agreeable
例句：She found a congenial group of people at the book club.  她在读书俱乐部找到了一群志趣相投的人。

例句：The warm, sunny weather was congenial to a picnic.  温暖晴朗的天气很适合野餐。
近义词：agreeable, compatible, kindred, sympathetic
反义词：disagreeable, incompatible, uncongenial
词性：adjective


congenial:/kənˈdʒiːniəl/
adjective
(of a thing) pleasant or agreeable; suitable or appropriate.
适宜的；合适的；宜人的
例句：The library provides a congenial atmosphere for studying. 图书馆提供了一个适宜学习的氛围。

例句：The climate is congenial to growing grapes. 这种气候适合种植葡萄。
近义词：favorable, suitable, appropriate, pleasant
反义词：unfavorable, unsuitable, inappropriate, unpleasant
词性：adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *congenialis*, from *con-* (together) + *genialis* (of the same genius, kindred), from *genius* (guardian spirit, inborn nature).
