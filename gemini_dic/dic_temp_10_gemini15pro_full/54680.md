disordered:/dɪsˈɔːdəd/ | adj. | 混乱的，紊乱的，失调的 |  Lacking order or organization; irregular or jumbled.  Characterized by mental or emotional disturbance. |  Her desk was incredibly disordered, covered in papers and pens. 她的桌子乱得不可思议，上面堆满了文件和笔。| The patient exhibited disordered thinking and hallucinations. 病人表现出思维混乱和幻觉。 | messy, chaotic, disorganized, disturbed, unruly | ordered, organized, tidy, calm, stable | adjective

disorder:/dɪsˈɔːdə(r)/ | n. | 混乱，紊乱，失调 |  A state of confusion or lack of organization. A physical or mental condition that is not normal or healthy. | The room was in a state of disorder after the party. 派对结束后，房间里一片混乱。 |  He suffers from a sleep disorder. 他患有睡眠障碍。| chaos, disorganization, mess, ailment, illness | order, organization, tidiness, health, stability | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Old French word "desordene,"  derived from the prefix "des-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of") and "ordene" (meaning "order").  The prefix "dis-" in English performs the same function.
