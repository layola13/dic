nonamenably:/ˌnɒnəˈmiːnəbli/
| adv. |不顺从地，难驾驭地，倔强地|Not easily controlled or persuaded; intractable. |The horse behaved nonamenably, refusing to take the jump. 这匹马表现得很不顺从，拒绝跳跃。|Synonyms: intractably, stubbornly, uncooperatively, recalcitrantly, rebelliously|Antonyms: obediently, submissively, cooperatively, tractably|词性:副词

Etymology:
From non- (not) + amenable (willing to comply) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Amenable" comes from Old French "amener" (to bring to), from Latin "ad" (to) + "minare" (to drive).
