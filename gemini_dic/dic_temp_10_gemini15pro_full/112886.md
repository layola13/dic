luddism:/ˈlʌdɪzəm/
|n.|卢德主义；反对技术进步|Opposition to new technologies, especially industrial or technological advances.  It often involves destroying machinery.  The term comes from the Luddites, textile workers who protested against newly introduced machinery in the early 19th century in England.|The resurgence of luddism in certain sectors reflects a fear of job displacement due to automation.|近义词:anti-technologism, technophobia|反义词:modernism, futurism|词性:名词

luddite:/ˈlʌdaɪt/
|n.|卢德派分子；反对技术进步的人|A person opposed to increased industrialization or new technology.  Originally, a member of any of the bands of English workers who destroyed machinery, especially in cotton and woolen mills, that they believed was threatening their jobs (1811–16).|He was branded a Luddite for his opposition to the introduction of computers in the workplace.|近义词:reactionary, anti-progress|反义词:progressive, innovator|词性:名词
