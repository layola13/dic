lying-in-state:/ˈlaɪɪŋ ɪn steɪt/
noun
公共瞻仰; Lying in state is when the body of a dead important person is put in a public place so that people can go and look at it.
The queen's lying in state lasted for four days. 女王的公众瞻仰持续了四天。
public viewing, ceremonial display
private burial, private funeral

词源：From "lie" (to be in a horizontal position) + "in" + "state" (ceremonial formality).  The phrase originally referred to a woman's confinement after childbirth.  Later, it evolved to describe the ceremonial display of a deceased person's body before burial.
