gloom:/ɡluːm/| n. |忧郁，阴暗，昏暗|A state of partial or total darkness; dimness.  A feeling of sadness; low spirits.|The room was filled with gloom.  房间里充满了阴暗。 He was filled with gloom about the future. 他对未来充满了忧虑。|近义词：darkness, sadness, depression, melancholy|反义词：brightness, happiness, cheer, joy|名词

gloom:/ɡluːm/| v. |变阴暗，变忧郁|To become dark or overcast. To appear or become sullen or despondent.|The sky gloomed over. 天空变得阴暗。  He gloomed at the news. 他听到这个消息后变得忧郁。|近义词：darken, sadden, depress|反义词：brighten, cheer up, lighten|动词

glooms:/ɡluːmz/| n. |(gloom 的复数) 忧郁，阴暗，昏暗|Multiple instances of partial or total darkness or feelings of sadness.|The glooms of winter descended upon the town. 冬天的阴暗笼罩着小镇。 His glooms seemed to deepen with each passing day. 他的忧郁似乎一天比一天加深。|近义词：darknesses, sadnesses, depressions|反义词：brightnesses, happinesses, joys|名词
