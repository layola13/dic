orgal: /ˈɔːrɡəl/ | n. | 风琴; (某些植物的)气孔 | A keyboard instrument consisting of a series of pipes supplied with wind by bellows and controlled by keyboards and pedals.  A minute breathing pore in the epidermis of the leaf or stem of a plant. | The church organ filled the hall with music. 教堂的管风琴使大厅充满了音乐。 | Synonyms: harmonium, pipe organ, wind instrument (for musical instrument); stoma, pore (for plant part) | Antonyms: None | noun

orgal: /ˈɔːrɡəl/ | adj. | 器官的; 有机的 | Relating to an organ or organs. Of, relating to, or derived from living organisms. |  The heart is a vital orgal. 心脏是一个重要的器官。 | Synonyms: organic, visceral | Antonyms: inorganic | adjective
