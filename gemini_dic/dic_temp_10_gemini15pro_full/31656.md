chatter:/ˈtʃætər/ (US), /ˈtʃætə(r)/ (UK)| n.|喋喋不休，啁啾声|Rapid, incessant talk, especially about unimportant matters; a series of short, quick sounds, like those of a bird or monkey.|The constant chatter of the children made it difficult to concentrate. 孩子们不停的叽叽喳喳让集中注意力变得很困难。|近义词：babble, prattle, jabber, twittering|反义词：silence, quiet|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *chateren*, frequentative of *chaten* (“to chat”), of imitative origin.
