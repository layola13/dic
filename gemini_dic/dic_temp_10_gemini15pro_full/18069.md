beguin:/ˈbeɪɡɪn, bəˈɡɪn/| n. |贝居安，女修道院成员；贝居安女修道会会员；头巾；一种女式紧身帽|A woman, especially in the 13th–16th centuries in the Low Countries, who lived in a religious community without taking vows or withdrawing from the world.  A close-fitting cap or hood worn by women, especially in the 14th–16th centuries.|例句：She wore the simple beguin of a novice. 她戴着见习修女的简朴头巾。

例句：The beguinage offered a haven for women seeking spiritual fulfillment outside of traditional monastic life.  贝居安女修道院为那些在传统修道院生活之外寻求精神满足的妇女提供了一个避风港。|近义词：lay sister, devotee, beguine|反义词: monk|词性:noun
