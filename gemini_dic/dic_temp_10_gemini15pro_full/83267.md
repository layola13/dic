gymnophthalmata:/ˌdʒɪmnɒfˈθælmətə/
n.
gymnophthalmids; spectacled tegus
A subfamily of microteiid lizards characterized by reduced or absent limbs.  These lizards are often called "spectacled tegus" or "gymnophthalmids."
Gymnophthalmata are a diverse group of lizards found in Central and South America.  它们是一组在中美洲和南美洲发现的蜥蜴。
Synonym:  Microteiidae (in part - as Gymnophthalmata is sometimes considered a family within Microteiidae)
No direct antonym.
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek γυμνός (gumnós, “naked, bare”) + ὀφθαλμός (ophthalmós, “eye”), referring to the transparent lower eyelid which allows them to see with their eyes closed.
