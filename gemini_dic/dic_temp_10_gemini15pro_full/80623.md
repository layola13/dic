goose-steps:/ˈɡuːs stɛps/| n.|正步，鹅步|A rigidly formal and exaggerated marching step, in which the legs are swung high and kept straight.|The soldiers performed goose-steps across the square. 士兵们在广场上迈着正步。|Synonyms: lock-step, march, parade step|Antonyms: casual walk, stroll|noun


goose-step:/ˈɡuːs stɛp/| v.|迈正步|To march with a rigidly formal and exaggerated step, in which the legs are swung high and kept straight.|The troops goose-stepped past the reviewing stand. 部队迈着正步走过检阅台。|Synonyms: march, parade|Antonyms: stroll, amble|verb
