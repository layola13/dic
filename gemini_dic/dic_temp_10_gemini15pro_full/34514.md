cleaned-out:/ˌkliːndˈaʊt/ | adj. | 清空的，被洗劫的，破产的 |  Completely emptied, robbed of everything valuable, or financially ruined. | The burglars left the house cleaned-out. 盗贼把房子洗劫一空。| 近义词: empty, depleted, broke, bankrupt | 反义词: full, rich, wealthy | 词性: 形容词

cleaned-out:/ˌkliːndˈaʊt/ | adj. |  筋疲力尽的，疲惫不堪的 |  Exhausted, extremely tired. | I felt cleaned-out after running the marathon. 跑完马拉松后，我感到筋疲力尽。| 近义词: exhausted, drained, spent | 反义词: energetic, refreshed, invigorated | 词性: 形容词

词源:  "Cleaned" comes from the Old English *clæne (akin to Old High German kleini "fine, small, delicate, pure"). "Out" comes from Old English ūt (akin to Old High German ūz "out"). The combination emphasizes the complete removal or emptying implied by "clean."
