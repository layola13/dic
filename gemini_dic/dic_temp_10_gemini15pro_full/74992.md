fridstol:/ˈfrɪdstɔːl/ | n. | peace chair; mediator's chair | A chair designated for peaceful negotiations or mediation, often used symbolically.  |  The fridstol was placed in the center of the room, ready for the warring factions to begin talks.  和平椅被放置在房间中央，准备供交战派系开始谈判。 | synonyms: mediator's seat, negotiation table | antonyms: war room, battlefield | noun


Etymology: From Old Norse *friðarstóll, composed of friðr ("peace," "protection") and stóll ("stool," "chair").
