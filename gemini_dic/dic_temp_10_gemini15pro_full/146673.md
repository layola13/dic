overhaul:/ˈoʊvərˌhɔːl/ (US), /ˈəʊvəhɔːl/ (UK)| n. vt.|彻底检修，大修；全面改革|A thorough examination and repair of a machine or system; a major change or improvement.|
The car is due for an overhaul. 这辆车该大修了。
The government is planning a complete overhaul of the education system. 政府正在计划对教育系统进行全面改革。
|近义词：renovate, repair, revamp, restore, refurbish|反义词：neglect, damage|词性: 名词, 动词

overhauls:/ˈoʊvərˌhɔːlz/ (US), /ˈəʊvəhɔːlz/ (UK)| n. vt.|彻底检修，大修；全面改革 (复数)|Thorough examinations and repairs of machines or systems; major changes or improvements. (Plural form of overhaul)|
The factory scheduled major overhauls for all its machinery. 工厂安排了对其所有机器进行大修。
Several departments are undergoing overhauls to improve efficiency.  几个部门正在进行改革以提高效率。
|近义词：renovations, repairs, revamps, restorations, refurbishments|反义词：N/A (plural of neglect/damage doesn't fit contextually)|词性: 名词, 动词


词源:  From "over" + "hale" (to pull, drag).  Originally meant to haul a ship or boat over for cleaning and repairs.
