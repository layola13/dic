bleed:/bliːd/
verb
流血
lose blood from the body as a result of injury or illness.
He was bleeding from a cut on his head. 他头部伤口在流血。

verb
渗出; 褪色
(of a dye or paint) spread beyond a desired area when wet, or (of colour) become less bright.
The red dye bled into the white fabric.  红色的染料渗入了白色的织物。

verb
榨取; 勒索
extort money from (someone), typically by intimidation.
The blackmailers bled him dry. 勒索者榨干了他的钱。

noun
流血; 失血
the loss of blood.
Apply pressure to the wound to stop the bleeding.  按压伤口止血。

noun
褪色
the running of a dye, paint, or other coloured liquid into an adjacent area or onto an underlying surface.
The photograph suffered from colour bleed. 这张照片有褪色问题。

Etymology: Old English *blēdan (attested only in past participle blēden), of Germanic origin; related to Dutch bloeden and German bluten.

Synonyms: hemorrhage, gush, ooze, seep, leak, discharge, exude, weep

Antonyms: clot, coagulate

词性:  v. & n.
