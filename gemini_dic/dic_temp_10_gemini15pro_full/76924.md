gaoler:/ˈdʒeɪlər/ (US) /ˈɡeɪlər/ (UK)| n.|狱卒，看守|A person in charge of a jail or of prisoners.|The gaoler brought the prisoner his meal. 狱卒给犯人送饭。|Synonyms: jailer, prison guard, warden|Antonyms: prisoner, inmate|noun

gaol:/dʒeɪl/ (US) /ɡeɪl/ (UK) |n.|监狱|A place of confinement, especially for those awaiting trial or convicted of minor offenses.  While gaol is the original and etymologically correct spelling, jail is now the more common spelling, especially in American English.  Both spellings refer to the same thing. |The gaol held prisoners awaiting trial.  监狱里关押着等待审判的囚犯。|Synonyms: jail, prison, penitentiary|Antonyms: freedom, liberty|noun
