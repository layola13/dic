falconoid:/ˈfɔːlkənɔɪd/| adj. |像隼的，隼形的|Resembling a falcon; falcon-like.|例句：The bird had a distinct falconoid appearance, with its sharp beak and powerful talons.  这只鸟有着明显的隼形外观，有着锋利的喙和强有力的爪子。

例句：The newly discovered species exhibited several falconoid characteristics. 这个新发现的物种展现出几种类似隼的特征。|近义词：falcon-like, raptorial|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider prey-like depending on context) |词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From *falcon* + *-oid*, a suffix meaning "resembling" or "having the form of."  Falcon itself comes from Old French *faucon*, from Late Latin *falconem*, ultimately from Latin *falx*, *falcis* meaning "sickle," referring to the bird's curved claws.
