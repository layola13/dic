dauphin:/ˈdɔːfɪn (UK), ˈdɔːfən (US)| n.|法国皇太子，海豚(旧用法)|The Dauphin of France was the heir apparent to the French throne. 法国皇太子是法国王位的继承人。|The book described the life of the dauphin. 这本书描述了皇太子的生平。|近义词：crown prince, heir apparent|反义词:|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *dalfin, from Medieval Latin *dalphinus, from Late Latin dalphinus (“dolphin”), from Ancient Greek δελφίς (delphís, “dolphin”). The use of the word to refer to the heir apparent to the French throne originated in 1349, when Humbert II, Dauphin of Viennois, sold his lands, the Dauphiné, to King Philippe VI of France on the condition that the heir to the French throne would bear the title of Dauphin.
