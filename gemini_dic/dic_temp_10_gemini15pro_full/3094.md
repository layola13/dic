aeroplanes:/ˈɛəroʊpleɪnz/ (US), /ˈɛərəpleɪnz/ (UK)| n. |飞机|A powered flying vehicle with fixed wings and a weight greater than that of the air it displaces.|Two aeroplanes collided on the runway. 两架飞机在跑道上相撞。|近义词：airplane, aircraft, plane|反义词: N/A| Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *aéroplane*, from *aéro-* (from Greek *aḗr* "air") + *plane* (from Latin *planus* "flat, level").
