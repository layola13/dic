lumos:/ˈluːmɒs/ | interj. | 照明咒 | A charm used to produce light, originating from the Harry Potter series.  It is derived from the Latin word "lumen" meaning light. | Example 1: "Lumos!" Harry whispered, and the tip of his wand glowed. 哈利低声说道：“荧光闪烁！”，他的魔杖尖端发出了光芒。

Example 2:  She muttered "lumos" and her wand illuminated the dark corridor. 她低声念道“荧光闪烁”，魔杖照亮了黑暗的走廊。 | Synonym: ignite, illuminate, light | Antonym:  nox (the counter-charm, extinguishing the light), darken, extinguish | Part of Speech: Interjection
