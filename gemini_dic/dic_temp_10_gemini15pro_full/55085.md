disputes:/dɪˈspjuːts/
|n. (plural)|争议，争论|A dispute is an argument or disagreement between people or groups.|The two countries have been engaged in a territorial dispute for years. 这两个国家多年来一直卷入领土争端。|
|v. (third-person singular simple present disputes, present participle disputing, simple past and past participle disputed)|
|vt.|质疑，否认；争夺|To dispute something means to argue against it or deny its truth or validity.  To dispute something also means to compete for it.|He disputed the referee's decision. 他对裁判的判决表示质疑。  The two teams disputed the championship.  两支队伍争夺冠军。|
|近义词:|arguments, disagreements, quarrels, debates, contests, challenges|
|反义词:|agreements, accords, harmonies, concessions|
|词性:|noun, verb|


Etymology:
From Old French *desputer, from Latin disputare (“to discuss, examine”), from dis- (“apart, in different directions”) + putare (“to think, reckon, believe”).
