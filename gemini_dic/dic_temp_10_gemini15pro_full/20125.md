bipartisanism: /ˌbaɪˈpɑːrtɪzənɪzəm/ /ˌbaɪˈpɑːr.t̬ə.zən.ɪ.zəm/ | n. |两党合作；跨党派合作|Agreement or cooperation between two political parties that usually oppose each other's policies.|Example:  Bipartisanism is essential to pass important legislation.  两党合作对于通过重要立法至关重要。

Example: The bill received bipartisan support in Congress.  该法案在国会获得了 bipartisan 的支持。|Synonym:  collaboration, cooperation, unity, consensus|Antonym:  partisanship, division, discord, polarization|noun

bipartisan: /ˌbaɪˈpɑːrtɪzən/ /ˌbaɪˈpɑːr.t̬ə.zən/ | adj. |两党的；跨党派的|Involving or supported by two political parties, especially two major parties that generally oppose each other's policies.|Example:  A bipartisan committee was formed to investigate the scandal.  成立了一个两党委员会来调查这起丑闻。

Example:  The bill passed with bipartisan support.  该法案在两党支持下获得通过。|Synonym:  joint, collaborative, cooperative, united|Antonym:  partisan, divisive, factional|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From bi- (two) + partisan (supporter of a party).  The term gained prominence in American politics in the mid-20th century.
