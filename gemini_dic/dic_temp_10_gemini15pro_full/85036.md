harbor:/ˈhɑːrbər/ (US) /ˈhɑːbə(r)/ (UK)| v. n.|庇护，藏匿; 港口|To give shelter or refuge to. A place on the coast where ships may moor in shelter.|例句：The fugitives were harbored by sympathetic villagers. 逃亡者被同情的村民藏匿起来。|近义词：shelter, conceal, protect, port, haven|反义词:expose, expel|词性：动词，名词

harbor:/ˈhɑːrbər/ (US) /ˈhɑːbə(r)/ (UK)| v. n.|怀有，心怀(尤指不好的想法); 港口|To keep (a thought or feeling, typically a negative one) in one's mind, especially secretly.|例句：She harbored a deep resentment towards her former employer. 她对以前的雇主怀恨在心。|近义词：cherish, entertain, nurse, port, haven|反义词:release, relinquish|词性：动词，名词
