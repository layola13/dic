high-tension:/ˌhaɪ ˈtɛnʃən/ | adj. | 高压的，高度紧张的 |  Carrying or transmitting a high voltage of electricity; characterized by a high degree of tension, anxiety, or excitement. | The high-tension wires carry a lethal current.  高压电线带有致命的电流。| synonyms: high-voltage, tense, strained, stressed | antonyms: low-tension, relaxed, calm | adjective

high-tension:/ˌhaɪ ˈtɛnʃən/ | n. | 高电压，高度紧张 |  A high voltage of electricity; a state of high tension, anxiety, or excitement. | There was a palpable high-tension in the room before the announcement.  宣布之前，房间里弥漫着明显的紧张气氛。| synonyms: high voltage, stress, strain | antonyms: low tension, relaxation, calmness | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "high" + "tension". "Tension" comes from the Old French "tension" and the Latin "tensionem," meaning "stretching, a stretching."  In the electrical sense, it refers to the high electromotive force or potential difference. In the figurative sense, it refers to a state of emotional or mental strain.
