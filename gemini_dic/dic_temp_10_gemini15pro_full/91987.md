hypernormal:/ˌhaɪpərˈnɔːrməl/ | adj. | 超常的，极度正常的（指表面正常，但实际隐藏着危机或不稳定） | Appearing superficially normal, but hiding underlying instability or crisis.  A situation where something is exaggerated or amplified to the point where it seems normal, even though it is not. |  例句：The calm before the storm felt hypernormal. 暴风雨前的平静感觉极度正常（却又暗藏危机）。

例句：The dictator maintained a hypernormal facade while the country crumbled beneath him.  独裁者维持着表面上的极度正常，而国家却在他统治下崩溃。| 近义词：artificial, simulated, unnatural, surreal, bizarre | 反义词：normal, natural, genuine, authentic | 词性：形容词

词源：
hyper- (prefix):  from Greek, meaning "over, beyond, excessive"
normal (adjective): from Latin normalis "made according to a carpenter's square," hence "standard, regular," from norma "carpenter's square, rule, pattern"
