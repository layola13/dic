impecuniously:/ˌɪmpɪˈkjuːniəsli/
adverb|贫穷地；拮据地|in a way that suggests having very little money; poorly.|He lived impecuniously, often relying on the generosity of friends. 他生活贫困，经常依赖朋友的慷慨。|例句：She dressed impecuniously, but with a certain flair. 她穿着寒酸，但颇有几分潇洒气质。|近义词：poorly, indigently, penuriously|反义词：richly, affluently, wealthily|词性:副词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *impecuniosus, from Latin in- (“not”) + pecunia (“money”).
