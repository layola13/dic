areole: /ˈæriːoʊl/ | n. | 小孔，小区域 | A small space or cavity, often used in botany to describe the cushioned area on a cactus from which spines, hairs, and flowers grow. |  The areole of the cactus was covered in fine hairs. 仙人掌的小孔上覆盖着细毛。 | Example 2:  New spines emerged from the areole as the cactus grew.  随着仙人掌的生长，新的刺从小孔中冒出来。 | synonyms: pit, cavity, compartment | antonyms: surface, exterior | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Latin *areola*, diminutive of *area* ("open space, courtyard").  The botanical sense, referring specifically to the structures on cacti, developed later.
