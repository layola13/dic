farmed:/fɑːrmd/
|v.|耕种的；养殖的；农业的| cultivated or used for agriculture. |The land was intensively farmed.|这片土地被集约化耕种。|近义词：cultivated, agricultural|反义词：wild, uncultivated|词性: Verb (past participle of "farm")
Etymology: From Old English *feormian "to supply with food, provision," from feorm "provisions, food, supplies," from Proto-Germanic *firmiz (source also of Old Frisian farma "provision," Old Saxon ferm "supplies," Dutch vorm, German Vormund "guardian"), from PIE root *per- (1) "to traffic in, sell." The original sense was to rent or lease land in return for a share of the produce.
