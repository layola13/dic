cave-in:/ˈkeɪvɪn/| n.|塌方，坍塌，倒塌；（计划等的）突然失败，放弃|A sudden collapse or sinking, as of a roof or the surface of the earth; a yielding to pressure or persuasion.|The heavy rains caused a cave-in in the mine. 大雨导致矿井塌方。|近义词：collapse, subsidence, caving in|反义词: buildup, construction|名词

cave-in:/ˈkeɪvɪn/| v.|塌陷，坍塌；屈服，让步|To collapse or sink inward; to yield to pressure or persuasion.|The roof of the tunnel caved in. 隧道顶部塌陷了。|近义词：collapse, crumble, give in|反义词: resist, withstand|动词
