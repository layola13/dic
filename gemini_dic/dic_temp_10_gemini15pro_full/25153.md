buckra: /ˈbʌkrə/ | n. | 白人（贬义，尤指白人男性） | A derogatory term, especially in some Caribbean creoles, referring to a white person, particularly a white man.  Historically used by enslaved people and their descendants.  It carries connotations of oppression, exploitation, and racial superiority assumed by white people. |  "The old buckra wouldn't let us sing our songs."  老白人不让我们唱我们的歌。 | Synonyms: white man (neutral), cracker (derogative), honky (derogative) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but terms like "brother" or "sister" might be used in-group to contrast with the out-group term "buckra") | Noun

buckra: /ˈbʌkrə/ | adj. | 白人的（贬义）|  A derogatory adjective, used in some Caribbean creoles, referring to something belonging to or associated with white people. Carries the same negative connotations as the noun form. | "That's buckra business, not ours." 那是白人的事，不是我们的事。 | Synonyms: white (neutral),  of the white man (neutral) | Antonyms: (No direct antonym, potentially "ours," "of our people," depending on context) | Adjective
