gimmal:/ˈdʒɪməl/| n. |联结物，铰链，机关|A device consisting of two or three rings interlocked in such a way that they can move independently but cannot be separated.  Originally, a finger ring made of two or three interlocked hoops. Also, something made with interlocking parts, a mechanism. |The lovers exchanged gimmal rings as a symbol of their bond.|近义词：joint, hinge, link, coupling|反义词:separation, disconnection|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gemel, from Latin gemellus "twin," diminutive of geminus "twin."
