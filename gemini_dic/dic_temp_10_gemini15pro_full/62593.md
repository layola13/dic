enfeeblers:/ɪnˈfiːblərz/| n. |使人衰弱的东西；削弱者|Something that weakens or makes feeble. |The constant stress and lack of sleep were enfeeblers that gradually wore down her health.  持续的压力和睡眠不足是使她健康逐渐衰弱的因素。| The government's policies were seen as enfeeblers of the economy. 政府的政策被认为是削弱经济的因素。|近义词：weakeners, debilitators|反义词:strengtheners, invigorators|词性:名词

词源: From enfeeble + -er.  Enfeeble comes from the Old French  *enfeblir*, from *en-* (meaning "in, into") + *feble* ("weak"), which ultimately derives from Latin *flebilis* ("lamentable").
