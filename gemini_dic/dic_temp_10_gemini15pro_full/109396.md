lenting:/ˈlɛntɪŋ/ | n. | 四旬斋, 大斋期 | The period of forty days, not including Sundays, between Ash Wednesday and Easter, observed in the Christian Church as a time of penitence and fasting.  |  During Lenting, many people give up certain foods or habits.  在四旬斋期间，许多人戒掉某些食物或习惯。|  近义词: Lent, fast | 反义词: feast, indulgence | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Middle English *lenten, from Old English *lencten “spring,” from Proto-Germanic *langatīnaz “lengthening (of days),” from *langaz “long.”  The religious observance of Lent takes place during this time of year because of the lengthening days as spring approaches.
