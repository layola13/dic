licentiate: /laɪˈsɛnʃɪɪt/ /laɪˈsɛnʃiət/ | n. | 持有执照者；准予…执照 | A person who has been granted a license, especially in a profession or to carry out a particular activity.  |  The licentiate in medicine is allowed to practice under supervision.  这位持有医学执照的人可以在监督下行医。 | synonyms: licensee, graduate, diploma holder | antonyms: amateur, unqualified | noun

licentiate: /laɪˈsɛnʃɪɪt/ /laɪˈsɛnʃiət/ | vt. | 准许…执业；授予…执照 | To grant a license to someone.  |  The board licentiates doctors to practice medicine.  该委员会授权医生行医。 | synonyms: authorize, certify, accredit | antonyms: disqualify, revoke, prohibit | verb
