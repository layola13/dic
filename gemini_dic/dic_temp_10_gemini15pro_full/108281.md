laurel:/ˈlɔːrəl/ (US) /ˈlɒrəl/ (UK)| n. |月桂树；桂冠；荣誉；胜利|A small evergreen tree with smooth, shiny leaves. Its leaves are sometimes used in cooking.  A crown of laurel leaves, given as a prize in ancient Greece, is a symbol of victory or distinction.|The chef added a laurel leaf to the stew. 厨师在炖菜里加了一片月桂叶。|Synonym: bay, garland, wreath, triumph, victory|Antonym: defeat, failure|noun

laureate:/ˈlɔːriət/ (US) /ˈlɒriət/ (UK)| n. adj. |获奖者；桂冠诗人|A person who has been honored for achieving something special, especially in the arts or literature.|The Nobel laureate gave a moving acceptance speech. 诺贝尔奖获得者发表了感人的获奖感言。|Synonym: winner, victor, champion|Antonym: loser|noun, adjective
