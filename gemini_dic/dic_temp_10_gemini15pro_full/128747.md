niggardly:/ˈnɪɡərdli/| adj.|吝啬的，小气的|Not generous; stingy.  Reluctant to give or spend; mean.| He was niggardly with praise, rarely offering a kind word. 他吝于赞美，很少说一句好话。|Synonyms: stingy, miserly, parsimonious, mean, tight-fisted, penny-pinching|Antonyms: generous, liberal, lavish, prodigal|adjective

niggardliness:/ˈnɪɡərdlinəs/| n.|吝啬，小气|The quality of being unwilling to spend money; stinginess.|Her niggardliness prevented her from enjoying life's simple pleasures. 她的吝啬让她无法享受生活中的简单乐趣。|Synonyms: stinginess, miserliness, parsimony, meanness|Antonyms: generosity, liberality, lavishness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *nigard*, from Old Norse *hnǫggr* (stingy), from Proto-Germanic *knugaz* (stingy, sparing).  Related to Old English *hneaw* (stingy).  The suffix *-ly* transforms the noun or adjective into an adverb.
