cocoons:/kəˈkuːnz/ | n. |茧; 庇护; 保护层 |A silky case spun by the larva of some insects, such as moths and butterflies, for protection during the pupal stage. Also used metaphorically to describe a state of safety or isolation.| The caterpillars spun their cocoons and waited to transform. 毛毛虫结茧等待蜕变。 | synonyms: shells, chrysalises, cases, coverings, envelopes | antonyms: exposure, vulnerability | noun

cocoons:/kəˈkuːnz/ | v. | 把…包围起来; 裹在茧里; 处于保护状态 | To wrap or enclose in a cocoon, either literally or figuratively. |  She cocooned herself in blankets on the cold night.  寒冷的夜晚，她用毯子把自己裹得严严实实的。 | synonyms: envelop, protect, shelter, insulate, shield | antonyms: expose, uncover, reveal | verb
