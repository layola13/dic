hype-up:/ˈhaɪp ʌp/
noun|炒作；宣传；刺激；兴奋剂|An activity or campaign designed to generate enthusiasm and publicity for something.|The media hype-up before the fight was intense.|promotion, publicity, buildup|downplaying|n.
hype-up:/ˈhaɪp ʌp/
verb|大肆宣传；使兴奋；夸张|To promote or publicize excessively, especially to generate excitement.|They hyped up the movie so much that I was disappointed when I finally saw it.|promote, publicize, boost|downplay|v.


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hype," meaning exaggerated publicity, combined with "up," intensifying the action.  "Hype" itself likely originated as shortened form of "hypodermic," referring to an injection of excitement.
