ETYMOLOGY->
life (n.) Old English līf "life, lifetime, way of life, livelihood," from Proto-Germanic *libam (source also of Old Saxon lif "life, body," Old Norse líf "life, body," Dutch lijf "body," Old High German līb "life," German Leib "body"), from PIE root *leip- "to remain, adhere, stick," hence "to continue, live."

threatening (adj.) early 15c., "indicating or containing a threat," present participle adjective from threaten (v.).
