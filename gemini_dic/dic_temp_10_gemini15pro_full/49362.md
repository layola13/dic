deliquescence:/ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛsəns/ | n. | 潮解; 溶解 |The process by which a substance absorbs moisture from the atmosphere until it dissolves in the absorbed water and forms a solution. |The deliquescence of the salt made it unusable.  这种盐因潮解而无法使用。|  hygroscopy, absorption | desiccation, drying | noun

deliquescent:/ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛsənt/ | adj. | 潮解的; 易潮解的 |(of a solid) Becoming liquid by absorbing moisture from the air. |Calcium chloride is a deliquescent substance. 氯化钙是一种潮解性物质。| hygroscopic, absorbent | desiccated, dry | adjective

deliquate:/ˈdɛlɪkweɪt/ | v. | 潮解; 溶解 |(of a solid) Become liquid by absorbing moisture from the air. |The sodium hydroxide pellets deliquated overnight. 氢氧化钠颗粒在一夜之间潮解了。| dissolve, melt | dry, solidify | verb
