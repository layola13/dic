gape:/ɡeɪp/| vi. n.|张口结舌；目瞪口呆；裂开；豁开|To stare with an open mouth, typically in amazement or wonder; a wide opening.|例句：The children gaped at the magician's tricks. 孩子们目瞪口呆地看着魔术师的表演。

The earthquake left a gaping hole in the road. 地震在路上留下了一个大裂缝。|近义词：stare, gawk, yawn, chasm, crack|反义词：close, shut|词性:动词, 名词

Etymology: From Old Norse *gapa "to open wide," related to Old English geapan.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *gapaną.
