connotative:/ˈkɑːnəteɪtɪv/ /kɒnətətɪv/| adj.|隐含的，含蓄的|Suggesting or implying a meaning in addition to the literal or primary meaning.|The word "home" has warm connotative meanings. “家”这个词有温暖的隐含意义。|Synonyms: suggestive, evocative, allusive, implicit, implied|Antonyms: denotative, explicit, literal|adjective

connotative:/ˈkɑːnəteɪtɪv/ /kɒnətətɪv/| adj.|内涵的，引申义的|Relating to the implied or associated meanings of a word.|The connotative power of language is important for poets. 语言的内涵力量对诗人很重要。|Synonyms: suggestive, evocative, allusive, implicit, implied|Antonyms: denotative, explicit, literal|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *connotativus, from Latin connotare ("to mark in addition"), from com- ("together") + notare ("to mark").
