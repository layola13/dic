church-goer: /ˈtʃɜːrtʃˌɡəʊər/ (UK) /ˈtʃɜːrtʃˌɡoʊər/ (US)
n.
常去教堂的人；虔诚的教徒
A person who regularly attends church services.
例句：She's a regular church-goer and attends services every Sunday. 她是一位经常去教堂的人，每个星期天都去做礼拜。

例句：The church-goers gathered for the Sunday sermon. 教堂的常客们聚集在一起聆听周日的布道。

近义词：worshipper, church member, congregant
反义词：non-believer, atheist, agnostic
词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
church (n.) + -goer (n.)
church: Old English cirice, circe "church," from Proto-Germanic *kirika (source also of Old Saxon kirika, Old Frisian zerke, Old Norse kirkja, Dutch kerk, Old High German kirihha, German Kirche), borrowed via West Germanic from Late Latin *chiriaca, from Greek kyriakon doma "the Lord's house," from kyriakos "of the lord," from kyrios "lord, master."
-goer: from go + -er, denoting a person who frequently goes to a specified place or event.
