adornment:/əˈdɔːrnmənt/ /əˈdɔːrnmənt/| n. |装饰；装饰品|Something decorative; an embellishment.|The dancer's wrists and ankles jingled with silver adornments.  舞者的腕部和脚踝上戴着叮当作响的银饰。

Her simple dress needed no adornment. 她简朴的连衣裙不需要任何装饰。|近义词：decoration, embellishment, ornament, trinket|反义词：plainness, simplicity|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "adornement," from "adorner" (to adorn), from Latin "ad-" (to) + "ornare" (to furnish, equip, adorn).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*or-no-" (to equip).

USAGE->
With possessive pronouns:
Her favorite adornment was a pearl necklace. = 她最喜欢的装饰品是一条珍珠项链。
His beard was his only facial adornment. = 他的胡须是他唯一的胡须装饰。

With adjectives:
The colorful adornments brightened up the room. = 五彩缤纷的装饰品照亮了房间。
She wore simple adornments to complement her elegant dress. = 她佩戴了简单的装饰品来搭配她优雅的连衣裙。

As a direct object:
They added adornments to the Christmas tree. = 他们在圣诞树上添加了装饰品。
The architect chose minimal adornments for the building's facade. = 建筑师为建筑物的外立面选择了最少的装饰。

As an indirect object:
She gave the room a festive air with bright adornments. = 她用明亮的装饰品给房间增添了节日气氛。
He brought her a small adornment as a gift. = 他给她带了一个小装饰品作为礼物。

In prepositional phrases:
The walls were bare except for a few small adornments. = 除了几件小装饰品外，墙壁是光秃秃的。
The room was filled with adornments of every kind. = 房间里摆满了各种各样的装饰品。
