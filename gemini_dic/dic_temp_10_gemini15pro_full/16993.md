bastionary: /ˈbæstʃənəri/ (US) /ˈbɑːstʃənəri/ (UK) | n. |  堡垒, 要塞; 坚固的防御工事 | A bastionary is a projecting part of a fortification built at an angle to the line of a wall, so as to allow defensive fire in several directions. | The old city walls and bastionary still stand today.  古老的城墙和堡垒至今依然屹立。| 近义词: fortress, citadel, stronghold, rampart | 反义词: weak point, vulnerability | 词性: 名词


Etymology: From Middle French *bastionaire*, from Italian *bastionario*, from *bastione* "bastion". Ultimately from Late Latin *bastire* "to build".
