daguerreotyped:/dəˈɡɛrəˌtaɪpt/ | adj. | 达盖尔照相法的，银版照相的 | Photographed by the daguerreotype process, an early photographic method that produced a unique image on a silver-coated copper plate. | The museum exhibited a collection of daguerreotyped portraits from the 19th century.  博物馆展出了一批19世纪的银版肖像。|Synonyms: photographed (in an old-fashioned sense), pictured, imaged | Antonyms: modernly photographed, digitally captured | Word type: Adjective

daguerreotype:/dəˈɡɛrəˌtaɪp/ | n. | 达盖尔照相法；银版照片 |  An early photographic process using an iodine-sensitized silvered plate and mercury vapor. Also, a photograph made by this process. |  The daguerreotype was an important step in the development of photography.  达盖尔照相法是摄影发展史上的重要一步。| Synonyms: photograph (archaic), image | Antonyms: digital photo, modern photograph | Word type: Noun

词源:  From Louis-Jacques-Mandé Daguerre (1787-1851), French artist and inventor of the process.
