clatter:/ˈklætər/ (US), /ˈklætə(r)/ (UK)| v. n.|发出咔嗒声，咔嗒声|To make a series of short, sharp, hard sounds by knocking together; a rattling sound.|The dishes clattered as they were stacked. 盘子堆叠时发出咔嗒声。|近义词：rattle, bang, clang|反义词:silence, quiet|verb, noun

clattered:/ˈklætərd/ (US), /ˈklætəd/ (UK)| v.|发出咔嗒声|Past tense of clatter, indicating a completed action of making a series of short, sharp sounds.|The cart clattered down the cobblestone street.  马车咔嗒咔嗒地沿着鹅卵石街道行驶。|近义词：rattled, banged, clanged|反义词:silenced, quieted|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch klateren, frequentative of klaten (“to rattle”).
