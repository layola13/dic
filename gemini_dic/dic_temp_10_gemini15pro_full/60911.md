elephantiasis: /ˌɛlɪfənˈtaɪəsɪs/ | n. | 象皮病 | A parasitic disease characterized by the thickening of the skin and underlying tissues, especially in the legs and genitals.  It is caused by obstruction of the lymphatic vessels, which results in chronic edema. |  Elephantiasis can be a debilitating condition. 象皮病可能是一种使人衰弱的疾病。  His legs were swollen due to elephantiasis. 他的腿因为象皮病而肿胀。 | 近义词: lymphatic filariasis | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for a specific disease) | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Greek *elephas*, elephant + *-iasis*, diseased condition.  The name derives from the resemblance of the affected limbs to the legs of an elephant.
