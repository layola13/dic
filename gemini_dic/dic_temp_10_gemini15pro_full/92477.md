hypotaxis:/ˌhaɪpəˈtæksɪs/| n.|从属关系，从属结构|A syntactic structure where clauses and phrases are subordinated to one another. This creates a hierarchical relationship between the different parts of a sentence, with one main clause and dependent clauses modifying it.|The author's use of hypotaxis creates a sense of formality and complexity in his writing. 作者使用从属结构使其写作显得正式和复杂。|The lengthy sentence, filled with hypotaxis, conveyed the weight of the situation.  这个充满从属结构的长句表达了情况的严重性。|subordination, dependency|parataxis|noun
