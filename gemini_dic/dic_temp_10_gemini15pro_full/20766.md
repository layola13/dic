From blandness + -'s (possessive suffix).  Blandness itself comes from bland (mild, gentle, soothing) + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing state or quality).  The ultimate origin of "bland" can be traced back to the Latin word "blandus," meaning flattering, alluring, or coaxing.
