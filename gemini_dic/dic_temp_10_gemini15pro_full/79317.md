gleam:/ɡliːm/| n. vi.|微光，闪光；闪现，流露|A faint or brief light, or a brief or slight manifestation of something (such as a feeling or quality).|The moonlight cast a gleam on the water. 月光在水面上投下一道微光。|近义词：glitter, glimmer, shimmer, shine, sparkle|反义词：dullness, darkness|词性:名词,动词

gleams:/ɡliːmz/| n. vi.|微光，闪光；闪现，流露（gleam的第三人称单数形式）|Plural form of gleam, referring to multiple faint lights or brief manifestations of something.|Her eyes gleamed with excitement. 她的眼睛里闪耀着兴奋的光芒。|近义词：glitters, glimmers, shimmers, shines, sparkles|反义词：dullness, darkness|词性:名词,动词


词源: From Old English *glǣm, from Proto-Germanic *glaimaz, from Proto-Indo-European *gʰlei- (“to shine, glitter”). Cognate with Old Norse gleima (“to gleam”), Dutch glimmen (“to gleam”), German glimmen (“to glow”), Lithuanian glėmžti (“to hug, embrace”).
