lobotomised:/ˌləʊbɒtəmaɪzd/ | adj. | 脑叶切除术后的；被切除脑叶的 |  Having undergone a lobotomy, a surgical procedure that severs connections in the brain's prefrontal lobe. |  He became withdrawn and apathetic after being lobotomised.  他被切除脑叶后变得孤僻冷漠。 |  Synonyms:  brain-damaged, mentally impaired | Antonyms:  neurotypical, unimpaired | adjective


lobotomize:/ˌləʊˈbɒtəmaɪz/ | vt. | 对…施行脑叶切除术 | To perform a lobotomy on. |  They lobotomized him in an attempt to cure his schizophrenia.  为了治疗他的精神分裂症，他们对他进行了脑叶切除术。 | Synonyms:  perform a lobotomy on, operate on the brain | Antonyms:  heal, cure (in a non-invasive way) | verb


lobotomy: /ləˈbɒtəmi/ | n. | 脑叶切除术 | A surgical operation in which a lobe of the brain (typically the prefrontal lobe) is severed from the rest of the brain, formerly used to treat mental illness. | The lobotomy left him with severe cognitive deficits. 脑叶切除术给他留下了严重的认知缺陷。| Synonyms:  leucotomy, psychosurgery | Antonyms:  therapy, counseling (non-invasive treatments) | noun
