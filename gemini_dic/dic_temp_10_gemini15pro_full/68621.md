fantasy: /ˈfæntəsi/ (US) /ˈfæntəzi/ (UK) | n. | 幻想，空想；想象的产物 | Something that is produced by the imagination; an idea that is not based on reality. | She indulged in fantasies of being a famous actress. 她沉迷于成为著名女演员的幻想中。 |  He retreated into a world of fantasy to escape the pressures of work. 他躲进幻想世界以逃避工作的压力。 | synonyms: daydream, illusion, dream, vision | antonyms: reality, actuality, truth | noun

fantasy: /ˈfæntəsi/ (US) /ˈfæntəzi/ (UK) | adj. | 幻想的，虚幻的 (Less common as an adjective) | Relating to or characterized by fantasy. |  The child created a fantasy world with her toys. 孩子用她的玩具创造了一个幻想世界。 | The book describes a fantasy kingdom filled with magical creatures. 这本书描述了一个充满神奇生物的幻想王国。 | synonyms: imaginary, unreal, fanciful | antonyms: real, actual, true | adjective
