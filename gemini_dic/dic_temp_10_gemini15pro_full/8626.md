anticommercialists:/ˌænti kəˈmɜːrʃəlɪsts/ | n. | 反商业主义者 | People who oppose the values and practices of commercialism, often advocating for alternative economic and social systems.  They believe that commercialism negatively impacts society, culture, and the environment. | The anticommercialists protested the new shopping mall, arguing it would destroy local businesses. 反商业主义者抗议新的购物中心，认为它会摧毁当地企业。

The rise of anticommercialists is a reaction against the pervasive influence of advertising and consumer culture. 反商业主义者的兴起是对广告和消费文化普遍影响的一种反击。 | 近义词: activists, critics, dissenters | 反义词: capitalists, commercialists, proponents of free markets | 词性: Noun
