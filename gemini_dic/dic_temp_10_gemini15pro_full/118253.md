merchandiser:/ˈmɜːrtʃəndaɪzər/ (US) /ˈmɜːtʃəndaɪzə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 商家，批发商，采购员| A person or company whose job is to buy and sell goods, especially in large quantities.  A person who promotes or sells goods, especially by organizing displays in shops.| 例句：The merchandiser visited the factory to inspect the quality of the goods.  商家参观了工厂以检查商品的质量。

例句：The experienced merchandiser secured a profitable deal for the company.  经验丰富的采购员为公司争取到了一笔有利可图的交易。| 近义词：vendor, retailer, seller, distributor, trader, salesman, saleswoman| 反义词: buyer, customer, consumer| 词性: Noun


merchandise:/ˈmɜːrtʃəndaɪs/ (US) /ˈmɜːtʃəndaɪz/ (UK)| n. v.| 商品，货物；推销，销售| Goods that are bought and sold. To promote the sale of (goods), especially by their presentation in retail outlets.| 例句：The shop sells a wide range of merchandise.  这家商店出售各种各样的商品。

例句：The company is planning to merchandise its new product line aggressively.  该公司计划积极推销其新的产品线。| 近义词：goods, wares, commodities, products, stock, inventory;  market, promote, sell, retail| 反义词: services (as opposed to goods)| 词性: Noun, Verb
