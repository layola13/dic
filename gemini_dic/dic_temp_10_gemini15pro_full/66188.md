exchequers:/ˈɛksˌtʃɛkəz/
n.|国库，财政部|A government department responsible for collecting and managing public revenue.|The exchequer announced new tax cuts. 财政部宣布了新的减税政策。|treasury, coffers|funds, assets
n.|财源，资金|The resources of a country, organization, or individual.|The club's exchequer was depleted after the expensive renovations.  俱乐部昂贵的装修耗尽了它的资金。|funds, resources|debts, liabilities

词性: 名词 (Noun)

词源:  From Old French eschequier "chessboard," then "counting table," from Medieval Latin scaccarium, from scaccus "chess piece," from Arabic *šaqq, from Persian šah "king." The original meaning referred to the checkered cloth used on counting tables, similar to a chessboard, which was used by the Exchequer in England for financial calculations.
