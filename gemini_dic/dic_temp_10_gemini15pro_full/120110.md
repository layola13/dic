mimic:/ˈmɪmɪk/ (US), /ˈmɪmɪk/ (UK)| v. n. adj.|模仿，模仿者，模仿的|to imitate or copy someone or something, especially in order to entertain or ridicule|例句：The comedian mimicked the president's voice and gestures.  喜剧演员模仿了总统的声音和姿态。

例句：Parrots can mimic human speech. 鹦鹉可以模仿人类说话。|近义词：imitate, copy, ape, mock, impersonate|反义词：originate, invent|词性：verb

mimic:/ˈmɪmɪk/ (US), /ˈmɪmɪk/ (UK)|n.|模仿者|a person or animal that mimics|例句：He's a talented mimic and can impersonate almost anyone. 他是个有才华的模仿者，几乎可以模仿任何人。

例句：The child was a mimic, copying everything his older brother did.  这个孩子是个模仿者，会模仿他哥哥做的每一件事。|近义词：imitator, impersonator|反义词：original|词性：noun


mimic:/ˈmɪmɪk/ (US), /ˈmɪmɪk/ (UK)|adj.|模仿的，拟态的|imitating something or someone else|例句：The insect's mimic coloration protected it from predators. 这种昆虫的拟态颜色保护它免受捕食者的侵害。

例句：She wore a mimic Rolex watch. 她戴着一块仿劳力士手表。|近义词：imitative, mimetic|反义词：original, genuine|词性：adjective
