doubtably:/ˈdaʊtəbli/| adv.|可疑地，怀疑地|In a manner open to doubt or suspicion.  It means "in a way that can be doubted".  While grammatically correct, it's uncommon and often considered awkward. "Doubtfully" is generally preferred.|He doubtably claimed to have seen the Loch Ness Monster. 他声称看到了尼斯湖水怪，这很令人怀疑。|近义词：doubtfully, questionably, suspiciously|反义词:certainly, undoubtedly, definitely|词性:副词

Etymology:
From "doubt" (from Old French "douter," from Latin "dubitare") + "-able" (suffix meaning "capable of being") + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).
