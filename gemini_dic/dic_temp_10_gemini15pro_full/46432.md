dagging:/ˈdæɡɪŋ/
n.|垂饰，下垂物；(尤指衣服或窗帘的)尖角形饰边|A hanging piece, point, or fringe, especially on a garment or curtain.|The dagging on her medieval gown trailed on the floor. 她中世纪礼服上的垂饰拖在地板上。|Synonyms: fringe, trimming, pendant, scallop|Antonyms: smooth edge, straight edge|noun

dagging:/ˈdæɡɪŋ/
v.|（尤指窗帘或衣服）装饰以尖角形饰边；下垂，悬挂|(especially of a curtain or garment) adorn with dagging; hang down, dangle|The designer dagged the edges of the scarf with beads.  设计师用珠子装饰了围巾的边缘。|Synonyms: festoon, drape, droop, hang|Antonyms: straighten, tighten|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *daggen*, from Old Norse *dagg* (“dew, dewdrop”), from Proto-Germanic *dagwaz* (“dew”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰegʷʰ-* (“to drip, flow”).  The sense development is from "dewdrop" to "something hanging like a dewdrop" to "a hanging point or flap."
