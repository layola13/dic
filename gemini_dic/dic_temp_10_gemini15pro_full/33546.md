ciff: /sɪf/
n.|骗局，欺诈|A trick or deception; a swindle.|He ran a ciff where he sold fake antiques.|近义词: scam, fraud, hoax, con, deception|反义词: honesty, truth, legitimacy|词性: 名词

词源:  Likely a variant of "chif" or "chiff," both slang terms for a petty theft or swindle, possibly originating from the French "chiffer," meaning "to calculate" or "to cipher," implying a clever or calculated deception.  Its origins are uncertain and it's considered primarily colloquial or slang.
