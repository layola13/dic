indorsation:/ɪnˌdɔːrˈseɪʃən/ | /ɪnˌdɔːrsˈeɪʃən/
n.|背书，认可，签注|An act of endorsing; approval or support.  The act of writing one's name on the back of a document.  |Example: The check required his indorsation before it could be cashed.  这支票需要他的背书才能兑现。

|Example: The candidate received the indorsation of several major newspapers.  这位候选人得到了几家主要报纸的认可。
|Synonym: endorsement, approval, sanction, support, backing
|Antonym: disapproval, opposition, rejection
|noun

indorse:/ɪnˈdɔːrs/ | /ɪnˈdɔːrs/
vt.|背书，认可，签注|To write one's signature on the back of (a document, especially a check). To give one's public approval or support to. |Example:  He indorsed the check and handed it to the cashier. 他在支票上背书后交给了收银员。

|Example:  Several celebrities have indorsed the new product. 几位名人为这款新产品代言。
|Synonym: endorse, approve, sanction, support, back
|Antonym: disapprove, oppose, reject
|verb
