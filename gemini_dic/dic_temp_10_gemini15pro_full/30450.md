Etymology: From "centred" + "-ness". "Centred" comes from "centre" (from Old French "centre," from Latin "centrum," from Greek "kentron," meaning "sharp point, stationary point of a pair of compasses") + "-ed" (suffix forming past participles and adjectives).  "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns expressing state or quality.
