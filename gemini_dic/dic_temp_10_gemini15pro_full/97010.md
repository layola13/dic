infatuated: /ɪnˈfætʃueɪtɪd/ | adj. | 迷恋的，着迷的 | Filled with a foolish or extravagant passion. | She was completely infatuated with the pop star. 她完全迷恋上了那个流行歌星。 | synonyms: besotted, smitten, obsessed | antonyms: indifferent, apathetic | adjective

infatuate: /ɪnˈfætʃueɪt/ | verb | 使迷恋，使着迷 | To inspire with an extravagant or foolish love or admiration. |  The exotic dancer infatuated many men.  这位异国舞者迷住了许多男人。 | synonyms: captivate, enchant, charm | antonyms: repel, disgust | verb

Etymology:  From Late Latin *infatuatus, past participle of *infatuare (“to make foolish”), from Latin in- (“in, into”) + fatuus (“foolish, silly”).
