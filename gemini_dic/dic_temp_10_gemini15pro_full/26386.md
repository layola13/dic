by-passers:/ˈbaɪˌpæsərz/
n.
路人；旁观者
People who happen to be present at an event but are not directly involved.
Several by-passers witnessed the accident.
几个路人目睹了这起事故。

by-passers:/ˈbaɪˌpæsərz/
n.
局外人
Those who are not part of a particular group or situation.
The by-passers had no idea what the argument was about.
局外人不知道这场争论是关于什么的。


近义词: onlookers, witnesses, spectators
反义词: participants, insiders


词源:
From "by-passer," a combination of "by" (meaning "near" or "aside") and "passer" (one who passes).  The suffix "-er" denotes a person who performs the action.
