dardanian:/dɑːrˈdeɪniən/
n.|达达尼昂人，达达尼昂式的|A native or inhabitant of Dardania, an ancient region in the Balkans.  Also, relating to or characteristic of Dardanus, the mythical ancestor of the Trojans, or his descendants.|D'Artagnan, the protagonist of Alexandre Dumas' "The Three Musketeers," was a Gascon, not a Dardanian, but the term is sometimes used to refer to brave and skilled swordsmen like him.|The dardanian spirit of the fencers was evident in their fierce competition.|近义词:  valiant, courageous, chivalrous|反义词: cowardly, timid|词性: 名词/形容词

dardanian:/dɑːrˈdeɪniən/
adj.|达达尼昂式的，勇敢的|Relating to or characteristic of Dardanus or Dardania; also, brave and skilled in combat, often in reference to the character D'Artagnan from "The Three Musketeers".|His dardanian skills with a rapier were unmatched.|近义词:  valiant, courageous, chivalrous|反义词: cowardly, timid|词性: 形容词
