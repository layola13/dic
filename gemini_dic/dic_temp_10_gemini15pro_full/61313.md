embarrasses:/ɪmˈbærəsɪz/| v.|使尴尬，使窘迫|To cause someone to feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed.|Her constant questions embarrassed me. 她的不断提问让我很尴尬。|synonyms: humiliates, mortifies, shames|antonyms:  flatters, praises|verb

embarrasses:/ɪmˈbærəsɪz/| v.|使困窘；使为难|To cause someone to feel awkward or uncomfortable.|Lack of money embarrassed him. 缺钱使他窘迫。|synonyms: discomfits, troubles|antonyms:  encourages, supports|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *embarrasser, from Spanish embarazar "to impede, perplex," from Portuguese embaraçar "to entangle," from Vulgar Latin *imbarricare "to block with bars," from Latin in- "in" + Late Latin barra "bar."
