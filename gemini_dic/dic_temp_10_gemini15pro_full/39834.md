conspire:/kənˈspaɪər/ | v. | 密谋，共谋，阴谋策划 | to secretly plan with someone else to do something bad or illegal |  The rebels conspired to overthrow the government. 叛乱分子密谋推翻政府。

They conspired against him. 他们合谋反对他。 | 近义词: plot, scheme, collude, intrigue | 反义词: disagree, oppose | 词性: 动词

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *conspiren*, from Old French conspirer, from Latin cōnspīrāre (“to agree, unite, plot together”), from com- (“together”) + spīrāre (“to breathe”).
