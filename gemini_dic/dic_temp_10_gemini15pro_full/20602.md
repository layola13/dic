blackguarding:/ˈblækɡɑːrdɪŋ/
n.|诽谤，污蔑，辱骂|The act of abusing or vilifying someone.
|His constant blackguarding of his opponents was despicable.|他不断诽谤他的对手，令人不齿。
|近义词：slandering, defaming, vilifying, reviling, abusing|
|反义词：praising, commending, lauding|

blackguard:/ˈblækɡɑːrd/
n.|恶棍，流氓|A person, particularly a man, who behaves in a dishonorable or contemptible way.
|He was known as a blackguard and a cheat.|他臭名昭著，是个恶棍和骗子。
|近义词：scoundrel, villain, rogue, rascal|

blackguard:/bləˈɡɑːrd/
v.|诽谤，污蔑，辱骂|To abuse or vilify someone.
|He blackguarded his ex-wife in the press.|他在媒体上诽谤他的前妻。
|近义词：slander, defame, vilify, revile, abuse|
|反义词：praise, commend, laud|
|词性：动词|


词源:

From "black guard," originally referring to the lowest menials in a large household, such as kitchen help, who were often dirty from their work.  Over time, the term evolved to denote a scoundrel or villain, and the verb form followed, meaning to act like a blackguard, or to speak abusively.
