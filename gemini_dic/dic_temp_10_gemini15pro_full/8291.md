anti-malthusian:/ˌæntiˈmælθuːziən/
anti-malthusian: |adj. n.|反马尔萨斯主义的；反马尔萨斯主义者| Opposing or disagreeing with the theories of Thomas Robert Malthus, particularly his theory that population growth will outstrip food supply.|  Many economists today are anti-Malthusian, believing that technological advancements can solve food scarcity issues. | 近义词: cornucopian, optimistic (regarding population growth) | 反义词: Malthusian, pessimistic (regarding population growth) | adj.
anti-malthusian: |n.|反马尔萨斯主义者| A person who opposes or disagrees with the theories of Thomas Robert Malthus. |  He's an anti-Malthusian who believes human ingenuity will always find solutions. |近义词: cornucopian | 反义词: Malthusian | n.
