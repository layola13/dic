dodecaphonies:/ˌdoʊˌdekəˈfoʊniz/| n. |十二音音乐，十二音体系|A method of musical composition in which the twelve notes of the chromatic scale are organized into a series (row) and none can be repeated until all the others have been sounded.|Schoenberg's later works are mostly dodecaphonies.勋伯格的后期作品大多是十二音音乐。|Synonyms: twelve-tone music, serialism|Antonyms: tonality, diatonicism|Noun
