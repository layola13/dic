fig-leafed:/ˈfɪɡˌliːft/ | adj. | 遮掩的，掩饰的 | Covered or concealing something embarrassing or shameful, like a fig leaf covering Adam and Eve's nakedness. |  The politician gave a fig-leafed explanation for his scandalous behavior.  这位政客对他的丑闻行为作了一个掩饰性的解释。 | synonyms: disguised, camouflaged, veiled, concealed | antonyms: exposed, revealed, unconcealed | adjective

fig-leafed:/ˈfɪɡˌliːft/ | adj. |  敷衍的，象征性的 |  Minimal and insufficient, offering only a token gesture of concealment or compliance.  | The company made a fig-leafed attempt to address environmental concerns. 该公司做出了敷衍的姿态来回应环境担忧。 | synonyms: token, perfunctory, superficial, nominal | antonyms: thorough, genuine, substantial | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "fig leaf" + "-ed." "Fig leaf" refers to the biblical story of Adam and Eve using fig leaves to cover their nakedness after eating the forbidden fruit (Genesis 3:7). This has led to the figurative use of "fig leaf" to describe anything used to conceal something shameful or embarrassing.
