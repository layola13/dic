beard-the-lion-in-its-den:/bɪrd ðə ˈlaɪən ɪn ɪts den/| idiom |直捣黄龙;  在太岁头上动土;  老虎头上拍苍蝇|To confront a powerful or dangerous person on their own territory.  This idiom emphasizes the bravery and audacity required to challenge someone in a place where they hold the most power and control. |Example 1: He decided to beard the lion in its den and confront his boss directly about the unfair treatment. 他决定直捣黄龙，直接与老板对峙不公平的待遇。

Example 2:  She knew it was risky, but she bearded the lion in its den and presented her innovative, yet controversial, proposal to the board of directors. 她知道这很冒险，但她还是在太岁头上动土，向董事会提出了她创新但备受争议的提案。|近义词：confront, challenge, take on, tackle|反义词：avoid, evade, shy away from|idiom
