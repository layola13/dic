imperiously:/ɪmˈpɪəriəsli/ | adv. | 专横地，傲慢地 | In a manner showing arrogant superiority and a tendency to command without justification.  It suggests a domineering and haughty attitude, often expressed through tone of voice or mannerisms. |  She imperiously demanded that he fetch her a drink. 她傲慢地要求他给她拿一杯饮料。

The queen imperiously gestured for her servants to leave. 女王傲慢地示意她的仆人离开。


| 近义词：domineeringly, haughtily, arrogantly, dictatorially, overbearingly | 反义词：humbly, modestly, submissively, respectfully | 词性: 副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *imperiousli*, from Anglo-Norman *imperious*, from Latin *imperiōsus* (“powerful, commanding”), from *imperium* (“command, power, empire”).
