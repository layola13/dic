on-the-wrong-tack:/ɒn ðə rɒŋ tæk/ | adj. phr. | 走错路；方法错误；用错误的方式 | Being on the wrong tack means pursuing a course of action that is unlikely to be successful. | Example:  They were on the wrong tack in their negotiations and failed to reach an agreement. 他们在谈判中采取了错误的方式，未能达成协议。

Example: We've been on the wrong tack with this project; we need to rethink our strategy.  我们在这个项目上走错了路；我们需要重新思考我们的策略。 | Synonyms: misguided, mistaken, off-track, off-base, errant | Antonyms: on the right track, correct, accurate | phrase


ETYMOLOGY->

From nautical terminology.  "Tack" refers to the direction a sailing ship travels relative to the wind.  Being "on the wrong tack" meant sailing in a direction that wouldn't lead to the desired destination.
