determinism:/dɪˈtɜːmɪnɪzəm/ | n. | 决定论 | The doctrine that all events, including human action, are ultimately determined by causes external to the will. |  Some philosophers believe in determinism, arguing that free will is an illusion. 一些哲学家相信决定论，认为自由意志是一种幻觉。

| Example 2:  The novel explores the concept of determinism and its implications for moral responsibility. 这部小说探讨了决定论的概念及其对道德责任的意义。 | synonym:  fatalism, predestination, necessity | antonym: free will, indeterminism, chance | noun
