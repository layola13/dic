blowsy: /ˈblaʊ.zi/ (US), /ˈblaʊ.zi/ (UK) | adj. |  邋遢的，慵懒的，蓬乱的；(脸色)发红的，红润的 |  Disheveled, untidy, or messy in appearance; having a flushed or ruddy complexion.  |  She looked blowsy after her nap. 她午睡后看起来很邋遢。|  Synonyms:  untidy, messy, rumpled, disheveled, frowzy, flushed, ruddy | Antonyms: tidy, neat, groomed, pale | word property: adjective


Etymology:  From the earlier word "blowzy," which likely originated in the 16th century.  Its precise origin is uncertain, but it may be related to "blow" in the sense of being windblown and disheveled, or to "blossom" suggesting a full, slightly overblown appearance.
