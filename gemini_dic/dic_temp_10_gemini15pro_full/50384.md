deportment:/dɪˈpɔːrtmənt/| n. |举止，行为，风度|A person's behavior or manners. |例句：Her deportment was impeccable, always polite and respectful. 她的举止无可挑剔，总是彬彬有礼，充满敬意。

例句：The headmaster praised the students for their excellent deportment during the assembly. 校长表扬了学生们在集会上的出色表现。|近义词：behavior, conduct, demeanor, bearing|反义词：misbehavior, misconduct|词性: noun

deportment:/dɪˈpɔːrtmənt/| n. |(旧)驱逐出境|The act of deporting someone.|例句：(Archaic) His deportment from the country was due to his illegal activities. 他被驱逐出境是因为他的非法活动。

例句：(Archaic) The government ordered the deportment of all enemy aliens. 政府下令将所有敌国侨民驱逐出境。|近义词：deportation, expulsion, exile|反义词：repatriation|词性: noun

词源:
From Old French deportement "behavior, carriage," from deporter "to behave, carry oneself," from Latin deportare "to carry away," from de- "away" + portare "to carry."  The meaning shifted from the act of carrying oneself to the manner of carrying oneself.
