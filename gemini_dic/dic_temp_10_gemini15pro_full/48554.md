defame:/dɪˈfeɪm/ | vt. | 诽谤，中伤 | To harm someone's reputation by saying or writing bad things about them that are not true. | The article defamed the politician by accusing him of corruption. 这篇文章指控该政客腐败，损害了他的名誉。

He was defamed in the press. 他在媒体上被诽谤了。

| 近义词：slander, libel, disparage, vilify, calumniate | 反义词：praise, commend, laud, extol | 词性：动词

defamation:/ˌdefəˈmeɪʃn/ | n. | 诽谤，中伤 | The act of defaming someone. | The lawsuit claimed defamation of character. 这场诉讼声称原告的性格受到了诽谤。

He sued the newspaper for defamation. 他起诉了这家报纸诽谤。

| 近义词：slander, libel, calumny | 反义词：praise, commendation | 词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French diffamer, based on Latin diffamare (from dis- ‘expressing reversal’ + fama ‘reputation’).
