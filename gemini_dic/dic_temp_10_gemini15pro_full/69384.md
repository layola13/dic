feebly:/ˈfiːbli/| adv.|微弱地，无力地|In a weak and ineffective way.  Lacking strength or force.|She smiled feebly. 她无力地笑了笑。|weakly, faintly, poorly|strongly, powerfully|adverb
ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *febly, from Old French feble (Modern French faible), from Late Latin flebilis (“lamentable”), from Latin flēbilis (“that is to be wept over”), from flēō (“I weep, cry”).
