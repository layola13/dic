idealisms: /aɪˈdiːəlɪzəmz/ | n. | 理想主义 |  A system of thought or belief emphasizing ideals, especially unrealistically high or impractical ones.  It refers to the pursuit or advocacy of ideals, often without regard to practical considerations. |  His idealisms led him to believe that war could be abolished. 他的理想主义使他相信战争可以被废除。| synonyms: principles, beliefs, doctrines, ethics, morals | antonyms: pragmatism, realism, cynicism | noun

idealisms: /aɪˈdiːəlɪzəmz/ | n. | 理想化 | The practice of idealizing; the act of representing something as perfect or better than in reality.  | The novel's idealisms of rural life ignored the harsh realities of farm labor. 这部小说对农村生活的理想化忽略了农场劳动的残酷现实。 | synonyms: romanticization, glorification, exaltation | antonyms:  vilification, denigration, disparagement | noun
