limping:/ˈlɪmpɪŋ/ | adj. | 跛行的，一瘸一拐的 | walking with difficulty because of an injured leg or foot | The dog was limping badly after it hurt its paw. 这只狗爪子受伤后一瘸一拐地走着。| synonyms: halting, hobbing, stumbling | antonyms: running, sprinting, striding | adjective

limping:/ˈlɪmpɪŋ/ | n. | 跛行 | the act of walking with difficulty because of an injured leg or foot |  His limping made it difficult for him to climb the stairs. 他的跛行使他爬楼梯很困难。 | synonyms: hobble, claudication | antonyms: running, sprinting, striding | noun

Etymology: From Middle English *limpen, from Old English *limpian (“to limp”), from Proto-Germanic *limpaną (“to limp”), of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Proto-Germanic *lam (“lame”).
