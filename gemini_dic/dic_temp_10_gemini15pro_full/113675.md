macintosh:/ˈmækɪntɒʃ/ (UK) /ˈmækɪntɑːʃ/ (US)| n. |Macintosh电脑；麦金塔电脑；雨衣；防水布|A personal computer designed, developed, and marketed by Apple, or the waterproof fabric originally used to make raincoats.|She bought a new Macintosh. 她买了一台新的Macintosh电脑。  He wore a macintosh to stay dry in the rain. 他穿了一件雨衣以防雨淋。|Synonym: Mac, Apple computer, raincoat|Antonym: PC (often referring to Windows-based computers)|noun


Etymology: Named after Charles Macintosh, the Scottish chemist who invented the waterproof fabric.  The Apple computer is named after the McIntosh apple, though the spelling was intentionally changed.
