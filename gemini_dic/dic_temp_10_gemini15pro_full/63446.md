entremets:/ˌɒ̃trəˈmeɪ/ (US), /ˌɒ̃trəˈmeɪ/ (UK)| n. |餐间甜点;一道菜|A sweet dish served between courses, especially in a formal meal. It can also refer to a small savory dish. |The chef prepared a refreshing sorbet as an entremets.  厨师准备了一份清爽的雪葩作为餐间甜点。

The restaurant's entremets were miniature works of art. 这家餐厅的餐间甜点都是微型的艺术品。|近义词:dessert, sweet, confection, intermezzo|反义词:main course, appetizer|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *entremet (Modern French entremets), from entre- "between" + met- "put, placed," past participle of mettre "to put, place".  Originally referred to any dish served between courses, including savory dishes. Over time, the term has come to primarily denote sweet dishes.
