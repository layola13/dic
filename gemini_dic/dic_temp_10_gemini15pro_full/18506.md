bene:/ˈbeni/ /ˈbiːni/ | prefix | good, well |  Comes from Latin "bene," meaning "well" or "good." It's used as a prefix in English words to indicate something beneficial or positive. |  Example: **Benefactor** (someone who gives money to a good cause) - 慈善家/捐助者; **Beneficial** (helpful or advantageous) - 有益的 | Synonym:  (as a prefix) eu-, well- | Antonym: (as a prefix) mal-, dis- | Prefix

PHRASE->
bene- (prefix) - used to form words meaning 'good' or 'well' 例如：benefactor, beneficial
PHRASE->
benevolent (adj.) - well meaning and kindly = 仁慈的
benediction (n.) - the utterance or bestowing of a blessing, especially at the end of a religious service = 祝福
beneficiary (n.) - a person who derives advantage from something, especially a trust, will, or life insurance policy = 受益人
beneficence (n.) - the quality or state of doing or producing good = 仁慈；善行


注意事项:

bene 本身不是一个独立的单词，而是一个前缀。 因此，没有独立的动词、名词或形容词词性，只有作为前缀的词性。

虽然bene来源于拉丁语，但在现代英语中，它主要作为一个前缀使用，而不是一个独立的单词。
