coot:/kuːt/| n.| 傻瓜，笨蛋；(尤指)白骨顶鸡 |A foolish or eccentric person.  A water bird of the rail family, with dark plumage, lobed toes, and a white bill and frontal shield.| There's no way he'd fall for that; he's not a complete coot. 他不可能上当的；他不傻。| We saw several coots swimming on the lake. 我们看到几只白骨顶鸡在湖里游泳。| 近义词：fool, idiot, simpleton, dunce (for person); moorhen, gallinule (for bird)| 反义词：genius, clever person (for person) | 词性：名词

词源：
The word "coot" comes from the Middle English "cote", which in turn is derived from the Old English "cōte".  The ultimate origin is uncertain, but it may be related to a Germanic root imitating the bird's call.
