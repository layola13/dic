hawse:/hɔːz/ | n. | 船首锚链孔，锚链孔 |The hawse is the opening in the bow of a ship through which the anchor chain passes.  船首锚链孔是船首的一个开口，锚链穿过它。 | The ship's hawse was damaged in the storm. 这艘船的锚链孔在风暴中受损了。| synonyms: anchor hole, hawsehole, hawse pipe | antonyms: N/A | noun

hawse:/hɔːz/ | n. | (比喻)困境，艰难处境 |  Being stuck between two opposing forces is like being caught in the hawse.  被困在两股对立的力量之间就像被困在锚链孔里。 |  He felt caught in the hawse between his family and his career.  他感觉自己陷入了家庭和事业的两难境地。| synonyms: predicament, dilemma, quandary | antonyms: advantage, solution | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hause, from Old Norse háls (“neck”), from Proto-Germanic *halsaz (“neck”). Cognate with Danish hals (“neck”), Swedish hals (“neck”), Icelandic háls (“neck”), German Hals (“neck”).  The term originally referred to the part of a ship's bow where the anchor cable passed.

注意事项:  hawse 作为航海术语使用较多，日常生活中很少用到。其比喻义也相对少见。
