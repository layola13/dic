greenhornism:/ˈɡriːnhɔːrnɪzəm/| n.|幼稚的行为；缺乏经验的表现|The investor's greenhornism led him to make several poor decisions.  这个投资者的幼稚行为导致他做了几个糟糕的决定。|例句：His greenhornism was evident in his clumsy attempts at negotiation. 他笨拙的谈判尝试显露出他的缺乏经验。

His greenhornism cost him dearly in the stock market. 他的缺乏经验让他在股市中付出了高昂的代价。|近义词：naivete, inexperience, awkwardness|反义词:sophistication, expertise, experience|词性:noun

词源:  From greenhorn (a newcomer, especially one who is easily fooled) + -ism (a suffix forming nouns denoting action, practice, or a system of principles). "Greenhorn" itself likely originated from the idea of a young animal whose horns haven't yet fully developed.
