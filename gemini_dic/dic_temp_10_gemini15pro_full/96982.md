infantilising:/ˈɪnfəntaɪˌlaɪzɪŋ/ (British), /ˈɪnfəntəˌlaɪzɪŋ/ (American)
|v.|使幼稚化；把…当小孩看待|treating someone as if they are much younger than they are, not giving them responsibilities, and not letting them make their own decisions.|He accused the government of infantilising the public.|近义词: patronizing, condescending, belittling|反义词: empowering, respecting, validating|verb (present participle)

infantilisation:/ɪnˌfæn.tɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (British) /ɪnˌfæn.tɪ.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (American)
|n.|幼稚化；婴儿化|the act of treating someone as if they are much younger than they are|The infantilisation of popular culture is a worrying trend.|近义词: patronization, condescension|反义词: empowerment, respect|noun
