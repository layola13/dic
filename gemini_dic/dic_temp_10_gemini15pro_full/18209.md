beldam:/ˈbeldəm/| n.|老妇人；丑老太婆；女巫|An offensive term for a woman, especially an old woman, considered to be unpleasant or malicious.  A beldam is typically portrayed as ugly, spiteful, and possibly having magical powers (often for evil).  The word carries strong negative connotations and is considered archaic and derogatory.|例句：The children were frightened by the stories of the wicked beldam who lived in the woods. 孩子们被住在森林里的邪恶老巫婆的故事吓坏了。

例句：In the fairytale, the beldam cast a spell on the princess. 在童话故事中，老巫婆对公主施了咒语。|近义词：hag, crone, witch|反义词:young lady, maiden|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *beldame*, from Old French *bel dame* "fine lady," which ironically became pejorative in English.  The Old French term itself is from *belle dame* "beautiful lady."
