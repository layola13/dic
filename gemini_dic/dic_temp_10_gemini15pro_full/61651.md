emissaryship:/ˈɛmɪsərɪʃɪp/ |n.|使节的职务；使者的身份|The role or status of an emissary.  An emissary is a person sent on a special mission, usually diplomatic.|  She undertook the emissaryship with a sense of grave responsibility. 她以高度的责任感承担了使节的职务。| synonym: delegation, mission | antonym: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From emissary + -ship.
"emissary" comes from Late Latin *emissarius "scout, spy," from Latin emissus, past participle of emittere "send forth, let go, discharge," from ex- "out" (see ex-) + mittere "to send" (see mission).
"-ship" is an Old English suffix meaning "state, condition of being," also "office, dignity, skill."
