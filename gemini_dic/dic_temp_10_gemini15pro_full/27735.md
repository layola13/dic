canines:/ˈkeɪnaɪnz/| n.|犬齿；犬科动物|Pointed teeth, especially the four sharp teeth of a dog, wolf, or other meat-eating mammal. Also refers to members of the Canidae family (dogs, wolves, foxes, etc.).|The veterinarian examined the dog's canines. 兽医检查了狗的犬齿。

The zoo has a large exhibit of canines. 动物园里有一个大型犬科动物展览。|近义词：cuspids, dog teeth (for the teeth); Canidae, dog family (for the animals)|反义词：(No direct antonym, could consider herbivores as a contrasting group for the animal meaning)|noun


canine:/ˈkeɪnaɪn/| adj.|犬的；犬科的|Relating to or characteristic of dogs or other members of the Canidae family.|Canine behavior is fascinating to study. 研究犬类行为很有趣。

The wolf's canine howl echoed through the forest. 狼的犬吠声在森林中回荡。|近义词：doglike, lupine|反义词：(No direct antonym)|adjective



ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *caninus*, from Latin *canīnus* (“of a dog, canine”), from *canis* (“dog”).
