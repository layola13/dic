demobilise: /ˌdiːˈməʊbəlaɪz/ (UK) /ˌdiːˈmoʊbəlaɪz/ (US)| v. |遣散；复员；使（工厂、工业等）转为和平时期生产|To release (someone) from military service.  To convert (industry) from wartime to peacetime production.| The government demobilised the troops after the war.  战争结束后，政府遣散了军队。| The factory was demobilised and began producing consumer goods. 工厂转为和平时期生产，开始生产消费品。| synonyms: discharge, muster out, decommission| antonyms: mobilize, enlist, call up|verb

demobilization: /ˌdiːˌməʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (UK) /ˌdiːˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (US)| n. |遣散；复员|The act of demobilizing.|The demobilization of the army took several months.  军队的复员工作花了几个月的时间。|The rapid demobilization led to high unemployment. 快速的复员导致了高失业率。| synonyms: discharge, disbandment| antonyms: mobilization, recruitment|noun
