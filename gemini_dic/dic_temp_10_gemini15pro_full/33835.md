circumlocutionist: /ˌsɜːrkəmləˈkjuːʃənɪst/ /ˌsɝːkəmləˈkjuːʃənɪst/
n.
说话迂回的人；拐弯抹角的人
A person who speaks in a roundabout way, using many words where fewer would do.
The politician was a master circumlocutionist, skilled at avoiding direct answers to difficult questions.  这位政客是一个说话迂回的大师，善于避免直接回答难题。
He was such a circumlocutionist that it took him a full hour to explain what could have been said in five minutes. 他真是个拐弯抹角的人，用了一个小时才解释完五分钟就能说完的事情。
近义词:  equivocator, waffle, prevaricator
反义词:  straight-talker, direct person
词性: noun

circumlocutory: /ˌsɜːrkəmləˈkjuːtəri/ /ˌsɝːkəmləˈkjuːtɔːri/
adj.
迂回的；拐弯抹角的
Expressed in a roundabout way; wordy and indirect.
Her circumlocutory explanation left the audience confused and impatient. 她拐弯抹角的解释让听众感到困惑和不耐烦。
His circumlocutory style of writing made it difficult to understand his main point. 他拐弯抹角的写作风格让人很难理解他的主要观点。
近义词: periphrastic, roundabout, indirect
反义词: direct, concise, succinct
词性: adjective

词源: From Latin circumlocutio, from circum- "around" + locutio "speech," from loqui "to speak."
