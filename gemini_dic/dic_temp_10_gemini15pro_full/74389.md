framer:/ˈfreɪmər/| n.| 制作者，构筑者，设计者|Someone who makes frames, especially for pictures or mirrors.  A person who creates or constructs something, such as a plan, system, or constitution.| The framer carefully joined the pieces of wood to create a sturdy picture frame.  这位制作者小心翼翼地将木片拼接起来，制作了一个结实的画框。| synonyms: maker, builder, constructor, architect, designer | antonyms: destroyer, demolisher | noun

framer:/ˈfreɪmər/| n.| 制定者，构成者|Someone who creates or formulates something, such as a law, policy, or theory. |  James Madison is often called the "Father of the Constitution" as he was the principal framer of the document.  詹姆斯·麦迪逊通常被称为“宪法之父”，因为他主要起草了这份文件。| synonyms: author, creator, originator, developer | antonyms: opponent, critic | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fremere, from Old English *fremere, *fremman (“to frame, perform, do, make”), from Proto-Germanic *framjaną (“to perform, do, make”).
