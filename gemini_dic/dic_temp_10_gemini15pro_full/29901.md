cautery: /ˈkɔːtəri/ (US) /ˈkɔːtəri/ (UK)| n.|烧灼; 烧灼术; 烧灼器|The process of burning tissue with a hot instrument or caustic substance, typically to stop bleeding or remove a diseased part. |The doctor used cautery to stop the bleeding. 医生用烧灼术来止血。|Synonyms: burning, searing, branding|Antonyms: freezing, cooling|Noun

cauterize: /ˈkɔːtəraɪz/ (US) /ˈkɔːtəraɪz/ (UK)| v.|烧灼; 止血|To burn tissue with a hot instrument or caustic substance. |The wound was cauterized to prevent infection.  伤口被烧灼以防止感染。|Synonyms: sear, burn, brand|Antonyms: freeze, cool|Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cauterium, from Latin cauterizare "to burn or brand," from Greek kauteriazein, from kauter "branding iron," from kaiein "to burn."
