bewilderment:/bɪˈwɪldərmənt/| n.|困惑，茫然，不知所措|A state of being confused and puzzled; a feeling of being lost and disoriented.|She stared at the complex instructions in utter bewilderment. 她完全茫然地盯着复杂的说明书。|The sudden change in plans left him in a state of bewilderment.  计划的突然改变让他不知所措。|近义词：confusion, perplexity, disorientation, puzzlement|反义词：clarity, understanding, comprehension|词性:noun

bewilder:/bɪˈwɪldər/| v.|使迷惑，使茫然，使不知所措|To confuse or puzzle someone greatly; to cause someone to feel lost and disoriented.|The city's maze of streets bewildered the tourists. 城市街道的迷宫让游客们感到困惑。|The complicated instructions bewildered her. 复杂的指示让她感到茫然。 |近义词：confuse, perplex, disorient, puzzle|反义词：clarify, enlighten, explain|词性:verb


词源:
From be- + wilder "lead astray, cause to lose one's way," from wild (adj.) + -er (agent suffix).  The original sense was "to lead into the wilds, cause to lose one's way." The figurative sense of "to confuse mentally" developed later.
