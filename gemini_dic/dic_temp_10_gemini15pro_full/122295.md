mitre: /ˈmaɪtər/ (US) /ˈmɑɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 斜接；主教冠；斜接接合| A tall, pointed hat worn by bishops and some other senior clergy as a symbol of their office. A joint made between two pieces of wood or other material at an angle, usually 45 degrees, to form a corner.| The carpenter used a mitre saw to cut the wood for the picture frame. 木匠用斜切锯切割木料制作画框。|Synonyms:  bevel, bias, chamfer|Antonyms: straight, square| noun

mitres: /ˈmaɪtərz/ (US) /ˈmɑɪtəz/ (UK)| n.| 斜接；主教冠；斜接接合（mitre 的复数形式）| Plural form of mitre, referring to more than one mitre joint or bishop's hat.| The mitres on the corners of the frame were perfectly aligned.  框架角落的斜接完美对齐。|Synonyms: bevels, biases, chamfers|Antonyms: straights, squares| noun
