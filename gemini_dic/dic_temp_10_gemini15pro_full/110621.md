limpet:/ˈlɪmpɪt/ (US), /ˈlɪmpɪt/ (UK)| n. |帽贝；坚守岗位的人；粘附物|A marine mollusk with a low conical shell, which clings tightly to rocks. Also used figuratively to describe someone who clings stubbornly to a position or possession.|例句：The limpets clung to the rocks despite the crashing waves.  帽贝们无视海浪的拍打，紧紧地贴在岩石上。

例句：He's a real limpet in his job; he's been there for 30 years and refuses to retire. 他真是个工作狂，在那里干了30年，拒绝退休。|近义词：barnacle, clingfish, stick-in-the-mud (figurative)|反义词: rolling stone (figurative)|词性: noun
