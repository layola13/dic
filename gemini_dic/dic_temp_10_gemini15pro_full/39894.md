constitutionalisms:/ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəlɪzəmz/
n.
宪政主义，立宪主义
Theories or systems of government in which power is limited by law and the rights of citizens are protected.  These theories often focus on the importance of a written constitution.
The conference explored the rise of constitutionalisms in post-colonial Africa.  这次会议探讨了后殖民时代非洲宪政主义的兴起。
The various constitutionalisms of Europe offer a rich ground for comparative study. 欧洲不同的宪政主义为比较研究提供了丰富的基础。
近义词: constitutional government, constitutional democracy
反义词: authoritarianism, totalitarianism
词性: noun


constitution:/ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
n.
宪法；体质；构成；建立
The basic principles and laws of a nation, state, or social group that determine the powers and duties of the government and guarantee certain rights to the people in it.  Also refers to the physical health and condition of a person or animal, or the way in which something is made up.
The US Constitution is the supreme law of the land. 美国宪法是国家的最高法律。
He has a strong constitution and rarely gets sick. 他体质强壮，很少生病。

-ism:/ɪzəm/
suffix.
主义；学说；制度；特性；状态
A suffix that forms nouns and indicates a doctrine, belief, system, practice, or characteristic.
Marxism is a social, political, and economic theory originated by Karl Marx. 马克思主义是由卡尔·马克思提出的社会、政治和经济理论。
Alcoholism is a serious problem. 酒精中毒是一个严重的问题。


词源:
From "constitution" + "-ism." "Constitution" ultimately derives from Latin "constituere" (to establish, constitute).  "-ism" is a suffix of Greek origin.
