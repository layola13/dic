nonbeliefs:/ˌnɑːnbɪˈliːfs/
|n.|不信；缺乏信仰；怀疑|The state of not believing something, especially religious doctrines.  Absence of belief.|His nonbeliefs extended to all organized religions.|近义词:disbelief, skepticism, doubt, unbelief|反义词:belief, faith, conviction|词性:名词

Etymology:
non- (prefix): from Latin *nōn* "not"
belief (noun): from Middle English *beleave*, from Old English *gelēafa* "belief, faith," from *lēaf* "belief, permission."  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *laubiz* "dear, beloved," suggesting the original sense of "trust, confidence."
