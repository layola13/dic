capstan:/ˈkæpstən/ | n. |绞盘，起锚机|A rotating drum or cylinder with a vertical axis, used for winding a rope or cable, especially one on a ship for weighing anchor or raising heavy weights. |The sailors used the capstan to raise the anchor. 水手们用绞盘升起锚。|Synonyms: winch, windlass|Antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French cabestan, from Old Provençal capestan, from Late Latin capistellum, diminutive of capistrum "halter," from caput "head."  The sense evolution is from a device that fits over the head to a device that turns.
