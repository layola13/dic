acerbate:/ˈæsərbeɪt/ /əˈsɜːrbeɪt/| v. adj.|使加剧，恶化；尖刻的，辛辣的|To make something worse or more bitter; sharply or bitingly critical.|His acerbate remarks about the other driver made the situation worse. 他对另一位司机的尖刻评论使情况恶化。|近义词：aggravate, worsen, exacerbate, bitter, caustic, sharp, sarcastic|反义词: soothe, alleviate, mild, gentle|词性:动词, 形容词


acerbate:/ˈæsərbeɪt/ /əˈsɜːrbeɪt/| adj.|尖刻的，辛辣的|Sharply or bitingly critical.|Her acerbate wit made her a popular but feared guest at parties. 她尖刻的机智使她成为聚会上受欢迎但又令人害怕的客人。|近义词：bitter, caustic, sharp, sarcastic|反义词: mild, gentle|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *acerbatus*, past participle of *acerbare* "to make sour, bitter," from *acerbus* "sour, bitter, harsh."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
His constant complaining acerbates the situation. = 他不断的抱怨使情况恶化。
Her acerbate comments often offend people. = 她尖刻的评论经常冒犯别人。

一般过去时:
The politician's speech acerbated the already tense relations between the two countries. = 这位政治家的讲话加剧了两国之间已经紧张的关系。
His acerbate tone acerbated her feelings of resentment. = 他尖刻的语气加剧了她的怨恨。

现在完成时:
The new law has acerbated the housing crisis. = 新法律加剧了住房危机。
His refusal to apologize has acerbated the situation. = 他拒绝道歉加剧了局势。

过去完成时:
The incident had acerbated the conflict before the peace talks even began. =  事件在和平谈判开始之前就加剧了冲突。
Her earlier remarks had acerbated his anger. = 她之前的言论加剧了他的愤怒。

将来时:
Further sanctions will only acerbate the economic crisis. =  进一步的制裁只会加剧经济危机。
Ignoring the problem will acerbate it in the long run. = 从长远来看，忽视这个问题只会使它恶化。


被动语态:
The situation was further acerbated by the media's coverage. =  媒体的报道进一步加剧了局势。
Relations between the two groups were acerbated by the incident. =  两组之间的关系因这一事件而恶化。



PHRASE->
acerbate the situation = 使情况恶化
acerbate feelings = 加剧感情（通常指负面情绪）
acerbate a conflict = 加剧冲突
acerbate tensions = 加剧紧张局势
acerbate a problem = 使问题恶化


注意事项:
acerbate 既可以作动词也可以作形容词。

作为动词时，通常接名词或名词短语作宾语。

作为形容词时，通常修饰名词。
