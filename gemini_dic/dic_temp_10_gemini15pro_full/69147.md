favouring:/ˈfeɪvərɪŋ/ | adj. |偏袒的，有利的| showing or giving an advantage to someone or something, especially in an unfair way | The referee was accused of favouring the home team. 裁判被指责偏袒主队。|  favorable, advantageous, preferential |  unfavorable, disadvantageous, impartial | adjective


favouring:/ˈfeɪvərɪŋ/ | n. |偏袒，偏爱| the act of showing or giving an advantage to someone or something |  His favouring of his eldest son caused resentment among his other children. 他偏爱长子，引起其他孩子的怨恨。 | favoritism, preference, bias | impartiality, fairness, neutrality | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *favoryng*, from Anglo-French *favourer* (to favor), from Latin *favor* (favor, kindness).
