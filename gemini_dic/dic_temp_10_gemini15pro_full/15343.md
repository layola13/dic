backstair:/ˈbækˈstɛər/ (US) /ˈbækˈsteə(r)/ (UK)| adj.| 后楼梯的；秘密的；卑鄙的|Relating to or conducted by means of back stairs; hence, secret, underhand, indirect.  Often used figuratively to describe actions or dealings that are clandestine or devious.|The backstair negotiations led to a surprising outcome. 后楼梯的谈判导致了令人惊讶的结果。|Synonyms: clandestine, covert, furtive, secret, surreptitious, underhand|Antonyms: open, overt, public|adjective

backstairs:/ˈbækˈstɛəz/ (US) /ˈbækˈsteəz/ (UK)| n.| 后楼梯；秘密手段|A back staircase; also, in the plural, secret means or influence.|He gained access to the confidential information through backstairs channels. 他通过秘密渠道获得了机密信息。|Synonyms: backdoor, private, secret |Antonyms: main, public, front|noun
