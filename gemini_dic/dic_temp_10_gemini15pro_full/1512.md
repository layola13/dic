acetonemia: /ˌæsɪtəˈniːmiə/ /ˌæsɪtoʊˈniːmiə/
noun
酮血症
The presence of abnormally high levels of acetone bodies in the blood.  It's a sign that the body is burning fat for energy instead of glucose, often due to uncontrolled diabetes or starvation.
Acetonemia is a common complication of diabetes. 酮血症是糖尿病的常见并发症。

The child's acetonemia was caused by a prolonged period of vomiting and inability to eat.  这个孩子的酮血症是由长时间的呕吐和无法进食引起的。

近义词: ketosis
反义词: (No direct antonym, consider euglycemia for normal blood sugar)
词性: noun
