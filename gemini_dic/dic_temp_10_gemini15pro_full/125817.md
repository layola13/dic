murmurings:/ˈmɜːrmərɪŋz/ | n. | 窃窃私语，低语声，怨言 | a low, continuous, indistinct sound, like that of people talking quietly or of water flowing gently; a complaint uttered in a low voice |  The murmurings of the crowd grew louder as the politician began to speak.  人群的低语声随着政治家开始讲话而变得越来越大。 | synonyms: whispers, mutterings, grumbles, complaints | antonyms: shouts, yells, proclamations, silence | noun

murmur:/ˈmɜːrmər/ | v. | 低语，小声说，发出连续而低沉的声音 | to say something in a low, soft, or indistinct voice; to make a low, continuous, indistinct sound | He murmured an apology before leaving. 他在离开前低声说了一句道歉。 | synonyms: whisper, mutter, mumble | antonyms: shout, yell, proclaim | verb

murmur:/ˈmɜːrmər/ | n. | 低语，低沉的声音 | a low, continuous, indistinct sound, like that of people talking quietly or of water flowing gently |  The murmur of the stream was soothing. 小溪的潺潺声令人舒缓。 | synonyms: whisper, mutter, mumble | antonyms: shout, yell, proclamation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *murmuryng, *murmurynge, from Anglo-Norman murmurer, from Latin murmurāre (“to murmur, grumble”), present active infinitive of murmurō (“to murmur, grumble”).
