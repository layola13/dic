archaeologist:/ˌɑːrkiˈɒlədʒɪst/ | n. | 考古学家 | A person who studies human history and prehistory through the excavation of sites and the analysis of artifacts and other physical remains. |  The archaeologist carefully brushed the dirt off the ancient pottery. 考古学家小心翼翼地把泥土从古代陶器上刷掉。

The archaeologist discovered a new tomb in the Valley of the Kings. 考古学家在帝王谷发现了一座新的陵墓。

| synonym: excavator, antiquarian, paleontologist (for those studying fossils) | antonym:  N/A (no direct opposite, perhaps "destroyer of history" could be considered a loose antonym in a figurative sense) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin archaeologia "antiquities, study of antiquities," from Greek arkhaiologia, from arkhaios "ancient" + -logia "-logy."
