melopoeia:/ˌmɛləˈpiːə/ | n. | 声调优美; 悦耳的诗歌;  | The use of words for their auditory effect, focusing on sound patterns and rhythm to create a musical or pleasing effect in poetry or prose. |  Ezra Pound emphasized melopoeia in his poetry, using assonance and alliteration to create a rich tapestry of sound.  庞德在他的诗歌中强调了声调优美，使用谐音和押头韵创造了丰富的音响效果。

| 近义词: euphony, music, harmony, cadence | 反义词: cacophony, dissonance | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *μελοποιία* (melopoiia) "song-making," from *μέλος* (melos) "song, melody" + *ποιέω* (poieō) "I make, produce."
