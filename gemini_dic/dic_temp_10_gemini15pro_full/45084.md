cubital: /ˈkjuːbɪtəl/| adj.| 肘的，尺骨的|Relating to the elbow or the forearm; specifically, the ulna bone.|The cubital fossa is a triangular area on the anterior surface of the elbow joint.|Near synonyms: ulnar, antecubital|Opposite:  (No direct opposite exists, perhaps consider terms relating to other body parts, e.g., carpal (wrist),  popliteal (knee))|Adjective


cubitus: /ˈkjuːbɪtəs/| n.| 尺骨，前臂|The inner and longer of the two bones of the human forearm, extending from the elbow to the wrist; also, the corresponding bone in other vertebrates.|A fracture of the cubitus can be a serious injury.|Near synonyms: ulna|Opposite: radius (the other forearm bone)|Noun
