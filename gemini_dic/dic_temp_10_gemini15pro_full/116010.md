marshing:/ˈmɑːʃɪŋ/ | n. | 行进，跋涉; 沼泽地带 | The arduous marshing of the troops through the swamp took its toll. 部队艰难地穿过沼泽地带，付出了沉重的代价。 |  Marshing describes the act of moving, especially of troops, in a deliberate and organized manner. It can also refer to an area of marsh.  Derived from "marsh" (n.) which comes from Old English *mersc and Old French maresche. | 例句：The marshing of the soldiers towards the front lines began at dawn. 士兵们向  前线行进始于黎明。

例句：The constant marshing had exhausted the soldiers.  不断的行军使士兵们筋疲力尽。 | 近义词：trekking, hiking, marching, slogging | 反义词: resting, staying | 词性: 名词
