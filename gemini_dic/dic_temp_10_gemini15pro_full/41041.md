copes:/koʊps/ (US) /kəʊps/ (UK)| v. (third-person singular simple present copes, present participle coping, simple past and past participle coped)|  应对; 处理; 挣扎着应付|  Deals with and attempts to overcome problems and difficulties. |She copes well with stress. 她能很好地应对压力。|Synonyms: manages, handles, deals with, contends with|Antonyms: succumbs, fails, struggles, crumbles|Verb

copes:/koʊps/ (US) /kəʊps/ (UK)| n. (plural)|  (通常指教士的)斗篷式长袍，披肩| A long cloak or mantle, often worn by clergy. |The bishop wore his ceremonial copes. 主教穿着他的礼服披肩。|Synonyms: cloak, mantle, cape|Antonyms: N/A|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *coper, from Vulgar Latin *cuppare “to strike, chop,” related to Latin colaphus “a blow with the fist.”  The sense evolved from "to meet in combat" to "to contend with difficulties."  The noun "cope" meaning a cloak is from a different origin, from Old French cape.
