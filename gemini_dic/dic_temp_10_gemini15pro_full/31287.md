chaperone: /ˈʃæpəroʊn/ (US) /ˈʃæpərəʊn/ (UK)| v.| 陪伴，护送|To accompany someone, especially a young unmarried woman, in public as a chaperone.|例句：She chaperoned her younger sister to the concert. 她陪她妹妹去听音乐会。|近义词：escort, accompany, attend|反义词: N/A|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French "chaperon" (hood), later referring to a married lady's hooded cloak and then to the lady herself acting as an escort.  The word then shifted to refer to any person acting as an escort, particularly for young unmarried women.
