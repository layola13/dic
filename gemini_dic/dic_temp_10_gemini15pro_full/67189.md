expressers:/ɪkˈsprɛsərz/| n.|表达者，表示者|People who express themselves, often through art, music, or writing.  Those who convey thoughts, feelings, or information.|The artists were true expressers of emotion through their vibrant paintings. 这些艺术家通过他们充满活力的绘画作品真切地表达了情感。|近义词：communicators, conveyors, articulators|反义词: suppressors, concealers|noun


expresser:/ɪkˈsprɛsərz/| n.|表达者，表示者|A device or tool used to express something, like a juice expresser.  Something that serves as a means of expression.|The lemon expresser made quick work of juicing the citrus.  这个柠檬榨汁机很快就把柑橘榨出了汁。|近义词：tool, device, instrument|反义词: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "express" + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person or thing that performs the action of the verb). "Express" itself comes from the Latin "expressus," past participle of "exprimere" (to press out, portray), from "ex-" (out) + "premere" (to press).
