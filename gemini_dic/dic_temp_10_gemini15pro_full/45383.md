cunningnesses:/ˈkʌnɪŋnɪsɪz/| n. |狡猾，诡诈|The plural form of cunningness, referring to multiple instances of the quality of being cunning or sly.  It implies a skill in deception or trickery.|His cunningnesses were legendary, allowing him to outsmart his opponents every time.|近义词：guile, craftiness, slyness, trickery|反义词：honesty, frankness, sincerity, artlessness|词性:noun

词源: From Middle English *cunnyngnesse, from Old English *cunnungnes, from cunnung (“knowledge, skill, experience; ability, capacity; cunning, craft”) + -nes, equivalent to cunning + -ness.


注意事项:  "Cunningnesses" is grammatically correct, but "cunning" itself can often convey the same meaning without the need for the plural form.  Using the singular form "cunning" is generally preferred.
