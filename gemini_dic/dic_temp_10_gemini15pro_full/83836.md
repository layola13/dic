hakka: /ˈhɑːkə/ (US), /ˈhækə/ (UK)| n. adj.|客家人，客家语；客家的|A Han Chinese subgroup originating in northern China, now largely concentrated in Guangdong, Fujian, Jiangxi, Guangxi, Sichuan, Hunan, and Taiwan, and also found in various Southeast Asian countries.  Also refers to their language, which is a Sinitic language of the Chinese branch of the Sino-Tibetan language family.|Hakka people are known for their earthen houses. 客家人以其土楼而闻名。|The Hakka language is spoken in many parts of Southeast Asia. 客家话在东南亚许多地区都有使用。|synonyms:  Hakka Chinese,  Kèjiā|antonyms: N/A|noun, adjective
