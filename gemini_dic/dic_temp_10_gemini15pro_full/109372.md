lensings:/ˈlɛnzɪŋz/
|n.|透镜效应；引力透镜效应；|The process by which light from a distant object is bent by the gravity of a massive object between the distant object and the observer. This bending can magnify and distort the image of the distant object.|Astronomers observed multiple lensings of the same distant quasar. 天文学家观察到同一个遥远类星体的多重透镜效应。|Synonyms: gravitational lensing, lens effect|Antonyms: N/A|noun

lensings:/ˈlɛnzɪŋz/
|n.|透镜；镜片|A piece of transparent material, usually glass, with curved surfaces that refract light, used singly or combined in eye glasses, telescopes, cameras, etc. (This is the base form "lens," pluralized.)|The telescope's lensings were carefully cleaned. 望远镜的镜片被仔细地清洁了。|Synonyms: lenses|Antonyms: N/A|noun
