coyer:/ˈkɔɪ.ər/ (US), /ˈkɔɪ.ə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更害羞的，更腼腆的|Comparative form of coy: more coy; most coy.  Meaning more reluctant to give details about something, especially something regarded as sensitive.|She gave a coy smile and refused to answer the question. 她腼腆地笑了笑，拒绝回答这个问题。|近义词：shyer, more reserved, more bashful|反义词：bolder, more forward, more outgoing|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Middle English *coye, from Old French coi (“quiet, still”), from Latin quietus.
