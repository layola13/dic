insinuators:/ɪnˈsɪnjuːeɪtərz/| n. |暗讽者；造谣者；潜入者|People who subtly introduce an idea or feeling into someone's mind, often with a negative connotation.  They might spread rumors or suggest something unpleasant without directly saying it.|The insinuators spread rumors about the company's financial stability. 这些造谣者散播关于公司财务稳定性的谣言。

The political insinuators worked to undermine the opponent's reputation. 这些政治上的暗讽者致力于破坏对手的名誉。|近义词：gossipmongers, scandalmongers, rumourmongers, backbiters, detractors|反义词：supporters, defenders, advocates|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin insinuator, agent noun from insinuare “to introduce by windings and turnings, wind or worm one's way into,” from in- “into” + sinuare “to bend, curve,” from sinus “curve, fold.”
