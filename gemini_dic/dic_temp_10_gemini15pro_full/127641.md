needleworker:/ˈniːdlˌwɜːrkər/| n. | 刺绣者；针线活工人 | A person who does needlework, especially sewing, knitting, or embroidery. |  My grandmother was a skilled needleworker and made beautiful quilts. 我的祖母是一位技艺精湛的针线活工人，她制作了漂亮的被子。|  seamstress, sewer, knitter, embroiderer | - | noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From needle + worker.  Needle comes from Old English *nǣdl, from Proto-Germanic *nēdlō, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *snēh₂- (“to sew”). Worker comes from Old English wyrcan (“to work”), from Proto-Germanic *wurkijaną, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *werǵ- (“to do, to work”).
