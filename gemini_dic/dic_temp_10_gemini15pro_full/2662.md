adorn:/əˈdɔːrn/ (UK) /əˈdɔːrn/ (US)| vt.|装饰，装扮|To make something more beautiful or attractive by adding something to it.|She adorned her hair with flowers. 她用鲜花装饰她的头发。

The Christmas tree was adorned with lights and ornaments. 圣诞树上装饰着彩灯和饰品。

|近义词：decorate, embellish, ornament, beautify, garnish|反义词：strip, mar, disfigure|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *adornen*, from Old French *adorner*, from *a-* (to) + *orner* (to adorn, furnish), from Latin *ōrnāre* (to adorn, equip, furnish), from *ōrnus* (decoration, ornament). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*wer-* (to cover, enclose).


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She adorns her house with fresh flowers every week. = 她每周都用鲜花装饰她的房子。
He always adorns his essays with literary quotes. = 他总是在他的文章中引用文学名言。

一般过去时:
They adorned the hall with balloons for the party. = 他们用气球装饰大厅来举办派对。
She adorned herself with jewelry for the wedding. = 她为了婚礼佩戴了珠宝。

现在完成时:
The artist has adorned the walls with murals. = 艺术家在墙上装饰了壁画。
They have adorned the cake with fresh berries. = 他们在蛋糕上装饰了新鲜的浆果。

过去完成时:
She had adorned the room with candles before the guests arrived. = 在客人到来之前，她已经用蜡烛装饰了房间。
He had adorned his uniform with medals before the ceremony. = 在仪式开始前，他已经在制服上佩戴了奖章。

将来时:

被动语态:
