mis-sampled:/ˌmɪsˈsæmp(ə)ld/ | adj. | 错误采样的，采样错误的 | Incorrectly sampled, referring to a statistical sample that does not accurately represent the population from which it was drawn. | The mis-sampled data led to inaccurate conclusions.  错误的采样数据导致了不准确的结论。 | The survey was mis-sampled, leading to biased results.  这项调查的样本有误，导致结果有偏差。 | 近义词: biased, skewed, unrepresentative | 反义词: representative, unbiased, accurate | 词性: 形容词

mis-sample:/ˌmɪsˈsæmp(ə)l/ | v. | 错误采样 | To sample incorrectly, resulting in a sample that does not accurately represent the population. | The researchers mis-sampled the population, leading to flawed results. 研究人员对人口进行了错误的采样，导致结果有缺陷。 | They mis-sampled the soil, so the test results were invalid. 他们对土壤进行了错误的采样，因此测试结果无效。 | 近义词: sample incorrectly | 反义词: sample correctly, sample accurately | 词性: 动词


词源:

"mis-"  is a prefix of Old English and Germanic origin meaning "wrong," "bad," "wrongly," or "badly."
"sample" comes from the Old French word "essample," meaning "example" or "specimen," ultimately derived from the Latin "exemplum."
