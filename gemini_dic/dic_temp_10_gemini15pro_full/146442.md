overfrailty: /ˌoʊvərˈfreɪlti/ | n. | 过度虚弱，极度脆弱 |  A state of extreme frailty, beyond normal aging.  It refers to a heightened vulnerability to stressors and a decreased ability to withstand them, leading to adverse health outcomes. | The overfrailty of the patient made him susceptible to infections. 病人的过度虚弱使他容易感染。 |  Near synonyms:  extreme frailty, severe debility, profound weakness | Antonym: robustness | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"Over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi) meaning "above, beyond, excessively" + "frailty" (from Old French frailete, from Latin fragilitatem, meaning "brittleness, weakness").
