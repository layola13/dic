occasionalism: /əˈkeɪʒənəlɪzəm/ | n. | 机缘主义; 偶然主义 | The philosophical doctrine that mind and body do not interact directly, but only through the intervention of God.  It asserts that on each occasion of apparent interaction, God creates a corresponding mental event for a physical event, or vice versa.  |  Descartes' theory of mind-body interaction is sometimes interpreted as a form of occasionalism.  笛卡尔的“心物互动论”有时被解读为一种机缘主义的形式。| synonyms: dualism, interactionism | antonyms: monism, materialism | noun

occasional: /əˈkeɪʒənəl/ | adj. | 偶尔的;  偶然的;  临时的 |  He makes occasional visits to his family. 他偶尔会去看望他的家人。| synonyms: infrequent, sporadic, intermittent | antonyms: frequent, regular, constant | adjective
