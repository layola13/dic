lute:/luːt/
n.|鲁特琴；琵琶|A stringed musical instrument with a long neck, a pear-shaped body, and a fretted fingerboard, played by plucking.|He played a beautiful melody on the lute. 他用鲁特琴演奏了一曲优美的旋律。|Synonyms:archlute, theorbo|Antonyms: None.|noun

lute:/luːt/
vt.|用油灰封|To seal or coat with lute.|They luted the pipes to prevent leakage.  他们用油灰封住管道以防泄漏。|Synonyms:seal, caulk|Antonyms:unseal, open.|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *lut*, from Arabic *al-‘ūd* meaning "the wood," referring to the instrument's wooden soundboard. The Arabic article *al-* became agglutinated to the noun *‘ūd*, resulting in the various European forms.
