lowlander:/ˈləʊləndə(r)/ (UK) /ˈloʊləndər/ (US)| n.|低地人；居住在低地的人|A person who lives in a lowland area, especially as distinct from a highlander.|The lowlands were populated by farmers and lowlanders, while the highlands were home to shepherds and clans.|近义词：plainsman, valley dweller|反义词：highlander|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From low + land + -er.  "Low" comes from Old English *lēage, meaning "lying low." "Land" comes from Old English land, meaning "land, territory." The suffix "-er" forms agent nouns, indicating someone who lives in or is associated with a particular place.
