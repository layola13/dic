heavy-handed:/ˈheviˈhændɪd/ | adj. | 高压的，粗暴的，笨拙的 | Clumsy, insensitive, or overly forceful.  Characterized by excessive control or severity, often without finesse or subtlety. | The police were criticized for their heavy-handed tactics during the protest. 警方因在抗议期间采取高压策略而受到批评。

The teacher's heavy-handed approach to discipline alienated the students. 老师粗暴的纪律方法疏远了学生们。 | 近义词: clumsy, awkward, oppressive, tyrannical, overbearing, dictatorial | 反义词: delicate, subtle, tactful, gentle, diplomatic | 词性: 形容词

heavy-handed:/ˈheviˈhændɪd/ | adj. | 笨手笨脚的 | Lacking skill or grace in movement or handling; awkward. |  He was heavy-handed with the delicate china and broke a cup.  他笨手笨脚地摆弄着精致的瓷器，打碎了一个杯子。

She's a bit heavy-handed in the kitchen and tends to overmix the batter. 她在厨房里有点笨手笨脚，经常把面糊搅拌过度。 | 近义词: clumsy, awkward, ungainly | 反义词: deft, skillful, adroit | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "heavy" + "hand" + "-ed."  "Heavy hand" figuratively refers to oppressive or forceful control, dating back to the 16th century. The "-ed" suffix creates the adjective form.
