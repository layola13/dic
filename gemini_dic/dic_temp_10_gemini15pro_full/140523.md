olefiant:/oʊˈlɪfiənt/ /ˈəʊlɪfɪənt/ | adjective, noun |  产油的；乙烯的；油质的；乙烯 | A historical term for ethene or ethylene, a hydrocarbon gas (C2H4) that is the simplest alkene. It is flammable and has a sweet odor.  As an adjective, it describes something that produces oil or ethylene. |

Example 1: Olefiant gas was used in early street lighting. (乙烯气体曾用于早期的街道照明。)

Example 2: The olefiant properties of the substance made it useful in certain industrial processes. (该物质的产油特性使其在某些工业过程中很有用。)


Synonym: ethylene, ethene | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but one could consider substances that absorb or consume ethylene as functional opposites.) | Word Type: Primarily adjective, historically also used as a noun.
