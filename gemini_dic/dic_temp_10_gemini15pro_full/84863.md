hanger-on:/ˈhæŋər ɒn/ (US), /ˈhæŋər ɒn/ (UK)| n. | 攀附权贵者；奉承者；食客 |A person who associates with another person, especially someone of higher status, in a sycophantic or parasitic manner, hoping to gain some advantage.| He has a reputation for attracting hanger-ons wherever he goes. 他走到哪儿都有一群趋炎附势的人围着他转。|synonyms: follower, sycophant, leech, parasite, toady | antonyms: leader, independent | noun

hanger-on:/ˈhæŋər ɒn/ (US), /ˈhæŋər ɒn/ (UK)| n. | 剩余物；残留物|Something that remains after the main part has gone or been used.| The last few apples were just hanger-ons from last year's crop. 最后几个苹果是去年收成的残留物。|synonyms: remnant, leftover, residue| antonyms: new, fresh| noun
