cannonballs:/ˈkænənˌbɔːlz/ | n. |炮弹；实心弹|A round, solid projectile fired from a cannon. |The ship fired its cannonballs at the fort.  这艘船向堡垒发射了炮弹。|
cannonballs:/ˈkænənˌbɔːlz/ | n. |（非正式）猛冲；快速移动| (Informal) To move very fast or rush. |He cannonballed down the hill on his bike. 他骑着自行车猛冲下山。|
cannonballs:/ˈkænənˌbɔːlz/ | n. |炮弹式跳水| A dive in which the diver tucks their knees into their chest. |He did a cannonball into the pool, splashing everyone. 他做了炮弹式跳水，溅了每个人一身水。|


词源: From "cannon" + "ball".  "Cannon" comes from the Old Italian word "cannone," meaning "large tube." "Ball" comes from the Old Norse word "ballr."

近义词: projectile, shot, shell (for the ammunition sense); plunge, dive (for the movement sense)
反义词: None (no direct opposite for this word)
词性: Noun
