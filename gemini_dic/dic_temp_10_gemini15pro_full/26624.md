cack-handed:/ˈkækˈhændɪd/| adj.|笨手笨脚的；左撇子的(旧义)|Awkward and clumsy; (formerly) left-handed.  The term originally referred to left-handedness, which was often seen as sinister or awkward. Now it primarily means clumsy or inept.|He made a cack-handed attempt to fix the shelf and ended up breaking it. 他笨手笨脚地试图修理架子，结果把它弄坏了。|Synonyms: clumsy, awkward, inept, maladroit, ham-fisted|Antonyms: skillful, dexterous, adept, adroit|adjective

cack-handedness:/ˌkækˈhændɪdnəs/| n.|笨拙；不灵巧|The state of being cack-handed; clumsiness or ineptness.|Her cack-handedness in the kitchen resulted in a burnt dinner. 她在厨房里的笨拙导致了晚餐烧焦。|Synonyms: clumsiness, awkwardness, ineptness, maladroitness|Antonyms: skill, dexterity, adeptness, adroitness|noun
