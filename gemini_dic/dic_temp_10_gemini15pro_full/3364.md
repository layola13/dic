Etymology:
From Middle English *affrighten*, from Old English *āfyrhtan* "to frighten," from *ā-* (intensive prefix) + *fyrhtan* "to frighten," from Proto-Germanic *furkhtijaną*, from *furkhtą* "fear." Related to Old High German *forhten* "to fear," Gothic *faurhtjan* "to be afraid."
