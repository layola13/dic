overmitigated: /ˌōvərˈmɪtɪɡeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 过度减轻的，过度缓和的 |  Reduced or lessened to an excessive degree, often to the point of ineffectiveness.  It implies that the attempt to mitigate something, such as a risk or a punishment, has gone too far and might even have made the situation worse. | The judge's sentence was criticized as being overmitigated, given the severity of the crime. 法官的判决被批评为过度减轻，考虑到罪行的严重性。|  over-softened, excessively lenient | under-mitigated, harsh | adjective
