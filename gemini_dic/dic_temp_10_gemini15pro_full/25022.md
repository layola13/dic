brutism:/ˈbruːtɪzəm/| n.|野兽主义，粗野主义|A style of architecture characterized by massive, angular, and blocky forms, often made of concrete, and a lack of ornamentation.  It flourished from the 1950s to the mid-1970s, particularly in public buildings.|The Barbican Centre in London is a prime example of brutism. 伦敦的巴比肯中心是野兽主义的典型例子。|Near synonyms: brutalism (often used interchangeably), concrete architecture|Near antonyms:  ornate architecture, decorative architecture|noun
