glamorized: /ˈɡlæməˌraɪzd/ | adj. | 被美化的，被赋予魅力的 |  Made to seem more attractive or exciting than it really is, often by exaggerating its positive aspects and ignoring its negative ones. | The movie glamorized the life of a gangster. 这部电影美化了黑帮的生活。| synonyms: romanticized, idealized, glorified | antonyms: deglamorized, realistic, unglamorous | adjective

glamorize: /ˈɡlæməˌraɪz/ | v. | 美化，赋予魅力 | To make something seem more attractive or exciting than it really is. | The media tends to glamorize violence. 媒体倾向于美化暴力。| synonyms: romanticize, idealize, glorify | antonyms: deglamorize, expose, reveal | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From glamour + -ize.  Glamour originally meant "magic, enchantment," influenced by grammar, due to the association of learning with magic.
