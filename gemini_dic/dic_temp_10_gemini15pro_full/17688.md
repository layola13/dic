bed-down:/ˌbed ˈdaʊn/| phrasal verb |安顿下来；（动物）卧下；铺床|To settle down or sleep somewhere, often temporarily or in makeshift accommodations;  (of animals) to lie down to sleep. |例句：The hikers bed-down in their sleeping bags for the night.  徒步旅行者晚上睡在睡袋里。

例句：The farmer bedded the cows down for the night. 农民把牛安顿好过夜。|近义词：settle down, bunk down, sleep, rest|反义词：get up, rise|词性：动词短语

bed-down:/ˌbed ˈdaʊn/| n. |（临时或简陋的）铺位；（动物的）窝|A temporary or makeshift place to sleep; a place where an animal sleeps. |例句：The hay bale served as his bed-down for the night.  干草捆成了他晚上的铺位。

例句：The dog found a comfortable bed-down in the leaves. 狗在树叶中找到了一个舒适的窝。|近义词：bed, nest, lair|反义词：N/A|词性：名词
