frenulum:/ˈfrɛnjʊləm/ (US), /ˈfrɛn.jə.ləm/ (UK)| n. |系带，小带|A small fold of membrane that restricts the movement of a mobile organ or part.|The frenulum of the tongue is a small fold of mucous membrane extending from the floor of the mouth to the midline of the underside of the tongue.|Synonyms: frenum|Antonyms: N/A|noun


frenula:/ˈfrɛnjʊlə/ (US), /ˈfrɛn.jə.lə/ (UK)| n. |系带（复数）|Plural of frenulum.|The patient presented with multiple frenula in the oral cavity.|Synonyms: frenums|Antonyms: N/A|noun


Etymology: From Latin *frenulum*, diminutive of *frenum* ("bridle, rein").
