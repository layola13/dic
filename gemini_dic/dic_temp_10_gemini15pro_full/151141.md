parenthesis: /pəˈrɛnθəsɪs/ /pəˈrɛnθəˌsis/ | n. | 括号，插入语，插曲 | An explanatory or qualifying word, clause, or sentence inserted into a passage with which it doesn't necessarily have any grammatical connection, and marked off by round brackets, dashes, or commas.  A remark or passage that departs from the theme of a discourse; a digression. | The sentence, "My dog (a West Highland terrier) is ten years old," contains a parenthesis.  句子“我的狗（一只西部高地白梗）十岁了”包含一个插入语。 | synonyms: digression, aside, interpolation, interjection | antonyms: main clause, main point | noun

parentheses: /pəˈrɛnθəˌsiz/ | n. | 括号，插入语，插曲（parenthesis的复数形式） | The plural form of parenthesis. Round brackets used to enclose additional information or an afterthought. |  Use parentheses to enclose the dates. 使用括号括起日期。 | synonyms: brackets, round brackets | antonyms: N/A | noun

parenthetical: /ˌpærənˈθɛtɪkəl/ /ˌpɛrənˈθɛtɪkəl/ | adj. | 括号的，插入的，附带说明的 | Relating to, or inserted as a parenthesis. Of the nature of a parenthesis; qualifying or explanatory. | The author added a parenthetical remark about the character's past. 作者添加了一句关于角色过去的附带说明。 | synonyms: incidental, digressive, explanatory, supplementary | antonyms: essential, integral, main | adjective
