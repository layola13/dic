bridestake: /ˈbraɪdˌsteɪk/ | n. |  新娘彩礼，聘礼 | A payment or gift traditionally given by a groom or his family to the bride's family as part of a marriage agreement.  It can also refer to the property or wealth a bride brings to a marriage. |  The bridestake was a considerable sum, reflecting the bride's high social standing. 新娘的彩礼数额巨大，反映了新娘的社会地位很高。| dowry, bride price |  - | noun
