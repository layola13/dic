expose:/ɪkˈspoʊz/ (US) /ɪkˈspəʊz/ (UK)| v. n.|暴露，揭露；使曝光；使面临，使遭受|To reveal or unmask something hidden, or to subject something to an action or influence.|例句：The newspaper exposed the corruption of the politician. 这家报纸揭露了这位政客的腐败行为。|近义词：reveal, uncover, divulge, subject|反义词：conceal, hide, protect, shield|词性:动词, 名词


expose:/ɪkˈspoʊz/ (US) /ɪkˈspəʊz/ (UK)| n.|暴露；揭露；揭发|The act of revealing or unmasking something hidden.|例句：The expose of the company's illegal activities led to its downfall. 对该公司非法活动的揭露导致了它的垮台。|近义词：revelation, disclosure|反义词：cover-up, concealment|词性:名词

词源: From Latin *expausare*, from *ex-* ("out") + *pausare* ("to pause, rest").  The original meaning was "to leave without shelter."
