anabaptist:/ˌænəˈbæptɪst/ | n. | 再洗礼派教徒；重浸派教徒 | A member of a radical Protestant sect that arose in Europe during the Reformation, emphasizing adult baptism and rejecting infant baptism.  |  例句：The Anabaptists were persecuted for their beliefs. 再洗礼派教徒因其信仰而受到迫害。

例句：Many Anabaptists fled Europe to find religious freedom. 许多再洗礼派教徒逃离欧洲以寻求宗教自由。| 近义词：rebaptizer | 反义词： (No direct antonym, potentially "paedobaptist" which refers to those who practice infant baptism) | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *anabaptista, from Ancient Greek ἀναβαπτίζω (anabaptizō, “I rebaptize”), from ἀνά (aná, “again”) + βαπτίζω (baptízō, “I baptize, dip, plunge”).
