extort:/ɪkˈstɔːrt/ /ɪkˈstɔːrt/| vt.|勒索，敲诈|To obtain something, especially money, by force, threats, or other unfair means.|The criminals extorted money from the shop owner by threatening to burn down his store. 犯罪分子威胁要烧毁店主人的商店，以此敲诈钱财。|近义词：coerce, wring, extract|反义词:donate, give|verb

extortion:/ɪkˈstɔːrʃn/ /ɪkˈstɔːrʃn/| n.|勒索，敲诈|The act of extorting something, especially money.|The businessman was accused of extortion and blackmail.  这位商人被指控犯有敲诈勒索罪。|近义词：coercion, blackmail|反义词:donation, charity|noun

extortionate:/ɪkˈstɔːrʃənət/ /ɪkˈstɔːrʃənət/| adj.|勒索的，敲诈的；过高的，过分的|Unreasonably high; exorbitant.|The hotel charges extortionate prices for phone calls. 这家酒店的电话收费过高。|近义词：exorbitant, excessive, outrageous|反义词:reasonable, fair, moderate|adjective
