drugged-up:/ˈdrʌɡd ˈʌp/| adj.|吸毒的；昏昏沉沉的|Under the influence of drugs, often to a significant and noticeable degree.  Implies a negative state. | He stumbled into the room, looking drugged-up and disoriented. 他跌跌撞撞地走进房间，看起来像是吸毒了，而且迷失了方向。|Example: The drugged-up driver caused a serious accident.  那个吸毒的司机造成了一起严重事故。|Synonyms: intoxicated, high, stoned, wasted|Antonyms: sober, clear-headed|adjective


drugged:/drʌɡd/| adj.|被下药的；麻醉了的|Having been given drugs, often secretly or against one's will.  Can also describe the feeling of being under the influence of drugs. |The victim was drugged and robbed. 受害者被下药抢劫了。|Example: She felt drugged after taking the prescribed medication. 服用处方药后，她感觉昏昏沉沉的。|Synonyms: narcotized, sedated, doped|Antonyms: unaffected, unsedated|adjective
