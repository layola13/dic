estoppel:/ɪˈstɒpəl/ (US) /ɪˈstɑːpəl/ (UK)| n.|禁止反言；不容反悔|The principle which prevents a person from asserting something contrary to what is implied by a previous action or statement of that person or by a previous pertinent judicial determination.|He was estopped from denying the paternity of the child because he had previously signed a birth certificate.|近义词：preclusion, bar, prohibition|反义词：permission, allowance|noun
