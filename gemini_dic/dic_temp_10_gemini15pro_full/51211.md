destitutely:/ˈdɛstɪtjutli/ | adv. | 贫困地，赤贫地 | In a manner characterized by utter poverty and lacking the basic necessities of life. |  They were living destitutely, with barely enough food to eat. 他们生活贫困，几乎没有足够的食物吃。|  The earthquake victims were left destitutely wandering the streets. 地震灾民赤贫地流落街头。 |  impoverishedly, indigently, penuriously |  affluently, richly, opulently | adverb

词源:
From Late Middle English *destitutli, from Old French destitut (“abandoned, forsaken”), past participle of destituer (“to abandon, forsake”), from Latin dēstituō (“I abandon, forsake, desert, leave, deprive, leave destitute”), from dē (“away, from”) + statuō (“I set, place, establish”).
