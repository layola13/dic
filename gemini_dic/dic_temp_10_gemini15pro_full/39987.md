consul:/ˈkɒnsəl/ (US) /ˈkɑːnsəl/| n. |领事；执政官|An official appointed by a government to live in a foreign city and protect and promote the government's citizens and interests there.  A chief magistrate in the Roman Republic.|The British consul in New York helped us with our lost passports. 英国驻纽约领事帮助我们找回了丢失的护照。|Synonyms: diplomat, envoy, representative|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a job title, possibly: citizen, national)|noun
