eschevin:/ˈɛʃəvɪn/| n.|市政官，市议员|A municipal magistrate or alderman in some European countries, particularly in the Low Countries (Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg).|例句：The eschevin was responsible for overseeing the town's finances.  市政官负责监督该镇的财政。

例句：The eschevins met to discuss the new building project. 市议员们开会讨论新的建筑项目。|近义词：alderman, councilor, magistrate|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *schepen (modern Dutch schepen, "alderman"), from Old Dutch *skepino, from Proto-Germanic *skapjaną ("to shape, create, ordain"), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kep- ("to cut, scrape").  Cognate with Old English sceapan ("to shape, create").  The word originally referred to a judge or magistrate appointed by a ruler.
