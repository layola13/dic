oversusceptibilities: /ˌoʊvər səˌsɛptɪˈbɪlɪtiz/
| n. | 过度敏感，过度易感性 | The state of being excessively or unduly susceptible (to something).  This refers to an increased or exaggerated likelihood of being influenced or affected by something, often in a negative way, such as an illness, allergen, or emotional stimulus. |  Their oversusceptibilities to colds and flus made them frequent visitors to the doctor. 他们对感冒和流感的过度敏感使他们经常去看医生。


The child's oversusceptibilities to certain foods required a carefully planned diet.  这个孩子对某些食物过度敏感，需要精心计划饮食。| 近义词: sensitivities, vulnerabilities | 反义词: resistances, immunities | 词性: 名词 (plural)


Etymology:
From over- + susceptibility + -ities (plural suffix). Susceptibility ultimately comes from the Late Latin *susceptibilis, from Latin suscipere "to undertake, receive," from sub- "up" + capere "to take."
