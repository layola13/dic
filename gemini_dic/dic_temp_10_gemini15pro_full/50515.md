deprivers:/dɪˈpraɪvərz/| n. |剥夺者；使人丧失者|People who deprive others of something. Those who take away rights, privileges, or possessions.|The deprivers took away their land and livelihood. 剥夺者夺走了他们的土地和生计。|近义词：robbers, thieves, oppressors|反义词: providers, givers|词性:noun

词源: From the verb "deprive," which comes from Old French "depriver," and ultimately from Latin "deprivare" (to dispossess, bereave).  The "-er" suffix denotes the agent or person performing the action.
