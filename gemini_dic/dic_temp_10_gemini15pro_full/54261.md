disgrace:/dɪsˈɡreɪs/| n. vt.|耻辱，丢脸; 使丢脸|A disgrace is a state of being dishonored, or something that causes dishonor.  To disgrace someone means to bring shame or dishonor upon them.|His behavior brought disgrace upon his family. 他的行为给他的家庭带来了耻辱。|Synonyms: shame, dishonor, ignominy, scandal|Antonyms: honor, glory, credit|noun

disgrace:/dɪsˈɡreɪs/| vt.|使丢脸，使蒙羞|To disgrace means to cause someone to lose honor or respect.|He disgraced himself by his actions. 他的行为让他自己蒙羞。|Synonyms: shame, dishonor, humiliate|Antonyms: honor, glorify, exalt|verb
