canary:/ˈkænəri/ | n. |金雀，女歌手，告密者|A small, brightly colored finch, originally from the Canary Islands, often kept as a cage bird. Also refers to a female singer, especially in a nightclub, or an informer, especially one placed in a situation to detect wrongdoing. |例句：The canary in the cage sang beautifully.  笼中的金丝雀唱得很动听。

例句：She was a popular canary in the local nightclub. 她是当地夜总会里很受欢迎的女歌手。|近义词：songbird, informer, informant, rat (slang) |反义词: (No direct antonym, depends on the meaning used. For bird: predator. For singer: listener. For informer: loyalist.)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *canary*, from *Canary Islands*, from Latin * Canariae Insulae* "Islands of the Dogs",  because of the large dogs kept there. The bird association came later.
