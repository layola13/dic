marchionesses:/ˌmɑːrʃəˈnɛsɪz/
n.
侯爵夫人（marchioness 的复数）
The wives or widows of marquesses are called marchionesses. 侯爵的妻子或寡妇被称为侯爵夫人。
The marchionesses attended the royal ball. 侯爵夫人参加了皇家舞会。
Synonym: peeresses
Antonym: N/A (no direct antonym for a title)
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *marchionesse (Modern French marquise), from Italian marchesa, feminine of marchese (“marquis”), from marca (“frontier district”), from Proto-Germanic *markō (“borderland, horse”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *merǵ- (“border, edge”).
