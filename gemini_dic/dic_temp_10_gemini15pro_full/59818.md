ecstasy: /ˈɛkstəsi/ (US) /ˈɛkstəsi/ (UK)| n.| 狂喜；入迷；忘形|A state of overwhelming emotion, especially joy; rapturous delight.|She was in an ecstasy of delight when she heard the news. 她听到这个消息欣喜若狂。|Synonyms: rapture, bliss, euphoria, elation|Antonyms: despair, misery, depression|Noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin extasis, from Greek ekstasis "entrancement, astonishment, insanity," literally "a displacement, a standing outside oneself," from existanai "displace, put out of place," from ex "out" (see ex-) + histanai "to cause to stand," from PIE root *sta- "to stand, make or be firm."
