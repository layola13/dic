bursts:/bɜːrsts/ /bɝːsts/
n.|爆发，突发，一阵|A sudden, brief, and intense occurrence of something.|The audience erupted in bursts of laughter. 观众爆发出阵阵笑声。|outbreaks, explosions, eruptions|lulls, calms
v.|（使）爆裂，（使）爆炸，突然出现|To break open or apart suddenly and violently, or to cause something to do this; to appear suddenly.|The balloon burst when I touched it. 气球我一碰就爆了。|explode, rupture, erupt|implode, mend, disappear
词性:名词(plural), 动词(third-person singular present)
词源:From Middle English *bersten, from Old English berstan, from Proto-Germanic *brestaną (“to burst, break, crack”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰreg- (“to break”).
