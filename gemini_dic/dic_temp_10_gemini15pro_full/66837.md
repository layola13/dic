expatiators:/ɪkˈspeɪʃieɪtərz/ |n.| 详细解说者；详述者 | People who expatiate on a subject speak or write about it in detail. | The expatiators on the subject of ancient Roman history captivated the audience. 这些详细解说古罗马历史的人吸引了观众。| synonyms: elaborators, expanders, commentators | antonyms: summarizers, abridgers | noun

expatiate:/ɪkˈspeɪʃieɪt/ |v.| 详细解说；详述 | To speak or write at length or in detail. |  He expatiated on the benefits of a vegetarian diet. 他详细阐述了素食的好处。| synonyms: elaborate, expand, enlarge | antonyms: summarize, abridge | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *expatiatus, past participle of expatiari "to wander, digress," from ex- "out" + patiari "to wander," related to spatium "space." The original sense was "to wander from the subject," later evolving to "to speak or write at length."
