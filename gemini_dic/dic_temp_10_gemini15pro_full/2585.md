admonition:/ˌædməˈnɪʃən/
n.
警告，劝告，忠告
an act of admonishing; authoritative counsel or warning.
The teacher gave the students an admonition about being late to class. 老师告诫学生不要迟到。

He received a stern admonition from his boss. 他受到了老板严厉的警告。

近义词: warning, caution, advice, counsel, reproof
反义词: praise, commendation, encouragement
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *admonicioun*, from Old French *admonicion*, from Latin *admonitiō*, from *admonēre* ("to remind, advise, warn"), from *ad- ("to") + *monēre* ("to warn").

USAGE->
She gave him a gentle admonition to be more careful. = 她温和地劝告他要更加小心。
The judge delivered a stern admonition to the defendant. = 法官向被告发出了严厉的警告。

PHRASE->
a word of admonition = 一句忠告
heed an admonition = 注意警告
ignore an admonition = 忽视警告
issue an admonition = 发出警告
a stern admonition = 严厉的警告
