ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *abominablenes*, from Old French *abominablete*, from Late Latin *abōminābilis* (“abominable”) + *-tās* (suffix forming abstract nouns of quality).  The English suffix *-ness* replaced the original French/Latin suffix.  Ultimately from Latin *abōminor* (“to deprecate as an ill omen, abhor, detest”) + *-bilis* (suffix forming adjectives).

词源: 来自中古英语 *abominablenes*，源自古法语 *abominablete*，来自晚期拉丁语 *abōminābilis*（“可憎的”）+ *-tās*（构成性质抽象名词的后缀）。英语后缀 *-ness* 取代了原来的法语/拉丁语后缀。最终来自拉丁语 *abōminor*（“将……贬为不祥之兆，憎恶，厌恶”）+ *-bilis*（构成形容词的后缀）。
