dissent:/dɪˈsent/ | n. vi.| 异议，不同意 |  Disagreement with a majority opinion, especially in voting or a matter of belief. |  Two members dissented from the majority decision. 两名成员对多数决定表示异议。| synonyms: disagreement, objection, opposition | antonyms: agreement, consent, assent | noun

dissent:/dɪˈsent/ | vi.| 持异议，不同意 | To hold or express an opinion different from that of the majority. |  Several senators dissented from the party line on the new bill. 几位参议员对新法案的党派立场表示异议。| synonyms: disagree, object, oppose | antonyms: agree, consent, assent | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "dissentir" (to disagree, be of a different opinion), from Latin "dissentire" (to disagree, be of a different sentiment), from "dis-" (apart, away) + "sentire" (to feel, perceive).
