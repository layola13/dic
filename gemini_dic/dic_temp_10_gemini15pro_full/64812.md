esoterics:/ˌɛsəˈtɛrɪks/ | n. | 秘传的事物；奥秘 | Intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people with a specialized knowledge or interest. |  He was drawn to the esoterics of ancient religions. 他被古代宗教的奥秘所吸引。| synonyms: mysteries, arcana, secrets | antonyms: exoterics, common knowledge | noun

esoteric:/ˌɛsəˈtɛrɪk/ | adj. | 秘传的；深奥的 | Intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people with a specialized knowledge or interest. |  The professor's lectures were filled with esoteric allusions that few students could grasp.  教授的讲座充满了很少有学生能理解的深奥典故。 | synonyms: abstruse, obscure, arcane | antonyms: exoteric, common, accessible | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *esotericus, from Greek esōterikos (“inner”), from esō (“within”), from en (“in”).
