deceiving:/dɪˈsiːvɪŋ/
adjective
欺骗性的，误导性的
giving a false or misleading impression
The advertisement was deceiving, the product was not as good as it claimed to be. 这广告是骗人的，产品并不像它声称的那么好。
Her seemingly innocent appearance was deceiving; she was a skilled con artist. 她看似天真的外表是骗人的；她是一个老练的骗子。
Synonyms: misleading, deceptive, fallacious, treacherous, dishonest
Antonyms: honest, truthful, genuine, sincere, candid
词性: 形容词


Etymology:
From Middle English *deceiven*, from Old French *deceivre*, from Latin *dēcīpere* (“to deceive, beguile, ensnare, betray”), from *de-* + *capere* (“to take, seize”).
