opportunism:/ˌɒpətˈjuːnɪzəm/ /ˌɑːpərˈtuːnɪzəm/| n.|机会主义；投机主义|The act of taking advantage of opportunities without regard for moral principles or the interests of others.|He seized the opportunity with a mixture of opportunism and sheer desperation. 他带着机会主义和纯粹的绝望抓住了这个机会。|近义词：expediency, pragmatism|反义词：principle, integrity|词性:名词

opportunist:/ˌɒpətˈjuːnɪst/ /ˌɑːpərˈtuːnɪst/| n.|机会主义者；投机分子|A person who exploits opportunities with little regard for principles or ultimate consequences.|He's a ruthless opportunist who will stop at nothing to get what he wants. 他是一个无情的机会主义者，为了得到他想要的东西，他会不择手段。|近义词：exploiter, self-seeker|反义词：idealist, principled person|词性:名词

opportunistic:/ˌɒpətjuːˈnɪstɪk/ /ˌɑːpərtuːˈnɪstɪk/| adj.|机会主义的；投机的|Exploiting opportunities as they arise without moral regard or planning.|The company took an opportunistic approach to acquisitions. 该公司采取了机会主义的收购方式。|近义词：exploitative, pragmatic|反义词：principled, planned|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French opportunisme, from opportun (convenient, opportune), from Latin opportunus (suitable, fit, convenient, timely), from ob- (toward) + portus (harbor, port).  The notion is of a favorable wind blowing toward port.
