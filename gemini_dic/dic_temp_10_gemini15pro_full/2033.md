acuation: /ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃən/
n.
疏散；撤离；排空
The evacuation of the city was orderly and efficient.  这座城市的疏散工作井然有序，效率很高。

The evacuation of the building was necessary due to the fire. 由于火灾，该建筑物必须疏散。

English Explanation:  The process of moving people from a dangerous place to somewhere safe.  Also refers to the emptying of something (like a stomach or a building).

Example Sentence:
The hospital ordered the evacuation of all patients due to the approaching hurricane.  由于飓风即将来临，医院下令疏散所有病人。

The evacuation of the flood zone was completed before the river overflowed its banks. 在河水漫堤之前，洪水区的疏散工作已经完成。


Synonyms:
clearance, exodus, retreat, withdrawal

Antonyms:
occupation, influx, entry

词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *evacuationem,  from Latin evacuare ("to empty out"), from ex- ("out") + vacuus ("empty").
