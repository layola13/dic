being-as:/ˈbiːɪŋ æz/ | conj. | 既然；因为 |  Used informally to mean "since" or "because".  It introduces a reason or explanation. | Being-as it's raining, we'll stay indoors. 既然下雨，我们就待在室内。| 近义词: since, because, as, seeing that | 反义词: although, though | 词性: 连词

being-as:/ˈbiːɪŋ æz/ | conj. | 鉴于；考虑到 | Considering that; since. | Being-as you're new here, let me show you around. 鉴于你是新来的，让我带你四处看看。| 近义词: considering, given that, inasmuch as | 反义词: despite, in spite of | 词性: 连词


词源:  "Being as" is a colloquial conjunction that originated from the longer phrase "being as how." The "how" eventually dropped out of common usage.  It's generally considered informal or dialectal, particularly in American English.

注意事项:
"Being-as" is considered informal and is more common in spoken English than in formal writing.  While acceptable in casual conversation, it's generally best to avoid it in formal contexts or academic writing.  Use "since" or "because" instead.
