concentrate:/ˈkɒnsəntreɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsəntreɪt/ (US)| v. n.|集中；浓缩|to focus all one's attention on a particular object or activity; to increase the strength of a solution by reducing its volume|例句：I need to concentrate on my work. 我需要集中精力工作。|近义词：focus, center, direct|反义词：scatter, disperse, distract|词性:动词,名词

concentrate:/ˈkɒnsəntreɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsəntreɪt/ (US)| n.|浓缩物|a substance made by removing most of the liquid from another substance|例句：Orange juice concentrate is convenient. 浓缩橙汁很方便。|近义词：extract, essence|反义词：dilution|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin concentratus, past participle of concentrare (“to join in a center, concentrate”), from com- (“together”) + centrum (“center”).
