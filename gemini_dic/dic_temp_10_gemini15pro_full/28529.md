cardigans:/ˈkɑːrdɪɡənz/| n. |羊毛开衫|A type of knitted garment, usually a sweater or jacket, that opens down the front and is typically made of wool.|He layered a cardigan over his shirt for extra warmth. 他在衬衫外面套了一件羊毛开衫以保暖。|近义词：pullover, jumper, jacket|反义词:|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From James Thomas Brudenell, 7th Earl of Cardigan (1797–1868), a British Army officer who allegedly wore such a garment during the Crimean War.  The name became associated with the style of knitted jacket he wore.
