incuse:/ɪnˈkjuːs/ /ɪnˈkjuːz/| adj. v.|雕刻的，压印的；雕刻，压印|A design or inscription that is incised or stamped, especially into a coin or medal. To stamp or impress (a design or inscription) into a surface, especially a coin or medal.|The coin was incused with the king's portrait. 这枚硬币上刻有国王的肖像。|近义词：engraved, impressed, stamped|反义词：raised, embossed|词性:形容词，动词
incuse:/ɪnˈkjuːs/ /ɪnˈkjuːz/| v.|雕刻，压印|To stamp or impress (a design or inscription) into a surface, especially a coin or medal.|The artist incused a delicate pattern onto the silver plate. 艺术家在银盘上刻上了精美的图案。|近义词：engrave, impress, stamp|反义词：raise, emboss|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin incudere "to forge, stamp," from in- "in, on" + cudere "to strike, beat."
