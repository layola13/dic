blighty: /ˈblaɪti/ | n. | 英国本土；英国（尤指士兵在海外服役时对英国的称呼）; (old-fashioned, informal) 愉快的地方; 伤口; 受伤 |  A term used (especially by soldiers serving overseas) to refer to Britain. /  A pleasant place or situation. / A wound or injury. |  He was sent home with a blighty to his leg. 他腿部受伤被送回了国。/  That little cottage is a real blighty. 那间小别墅真是个好地方。| 近义词: Britain, UK, England (though less specific) / wound, injury | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Hindi word "vilāyatī" (विलायती), meaning "foreign," specifically referring to Britain during the British Raj. Over time, the meaning shifted to refer specifically to Britain itself, especially from the perspective of someone abroad.
