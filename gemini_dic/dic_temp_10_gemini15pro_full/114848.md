mammifer:/ˈmæməfər/ | n. | 哺乳动物 | An animal that is characterized by the possession of mammary glands in females, which secrete milk for the nourishment of young, and typically by the presence of hair or fur, the birth of live young, and a warm-blooded physiology. |  Example 1: Whales are marine mammals. 鲸鱼是海洋哺乳动物。

Example 2:  The platypus is a unique mammifer, laying eggs instead of giving birth to live young.  鸭嘴兽是一种独特的哺乳动物，产卵而不是产下幼崽。 | 近义词: mammal | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but could consider animals of other classes like reptiles, birds, etc.) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mammifère*, from Latin *mammifer* "breast-bearing," from *mamma* "breast, udder" + *-fer* "bearing," from *ferre* "to carry."
