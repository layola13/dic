endrudge:/ɪnˈdrʌdʒ/ |v. | 使辛劳，强迫做苦工 | To make someone work hard and continuously at unpleasant or tiring tasks. |  He endrudged himself with tedious chores all day. 他整天让自己辛劳地做着繁琐的家务。

She was endrudged by her boss with endless paperwork.  她的老板让她没完没了地做文书工作，使她疲惫不堪。| 近义词: toil, slave, drudge | 反义词: relax, idle | 词性: 动词

词源:  From en- + drudge.  "En-" acts as a causative prefix, meaning "to cause to be," combined with "drudge," meaning a person who does menial, tedious, or unpleasant work.
