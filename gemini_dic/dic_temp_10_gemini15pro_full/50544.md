deput:/ˈdepjut/ (US), /ˈdɛpjut/ (UK)| n.|代理人，代表，副手|A person appointed to act as a substitute for another; a deputy.|The sheriff's deput was responsible for serving warrants.  警长的副手负责送达逮捕令。|Synonyms:  substitute, representative, agent, delegate, assistant|Antonyms:  principal, leader, chief|noun

deput:/ˈdepjut/ (US), /ˈdɛpjut/ (UK)| v.|委派…为副手，代理|To appoint as a deputy.|He was deputed to represent the company at the conference. 他被委派代表公司出席会议。|Synonyms:  appoint, delegate, commission, authorize|Antonyms:  dismiss, discharge|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *deputé*, past participle of *deputer* "to depute," from Latin deputatus, past participle of deputare "to assign, allot, select," from de- "from" + putare "to think, reckon, consider, prune."
