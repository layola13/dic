altruism:/ˈæltruɪzəm/| n.|利他主义；无私|Disinterested and selfless concern for the well-being of others.|Mother Teresa was known for her altruism.  特蕾莎修女以其利他主义而闻名。|synonyms: selflessness, benevolence, charity|antonyms: selfishness, egoism, greed|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *altruisme*, from Italian *altrui* "of others, somebody else's," from Latin *alteri huic* "to this other."  Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European root *al- (1) "beyond."
