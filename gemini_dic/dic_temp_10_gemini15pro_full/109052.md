legalism: /ˈliːɡəlɪzəm/ | n. | 法律主义，墨家 |  A philosophy emphasizing strict adherence to law or rules, often to the exclusion of other considerations like morality or compassion.  | The Qin dynasty's reliance on legalism led to a highly structured but also oppressive society.  秦朝对法家思想的依赖导致了一个高度结构化但也具有压迫性的社会。| synonyms: formalism, literalism, by-the-book | antonyms:  anarchism, liberalism, leniency | noun
