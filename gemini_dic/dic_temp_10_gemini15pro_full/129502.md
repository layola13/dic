non-Aryan:/ˌnɒn ˈɛərɪən/ | adj. |非雅利安人的|Not belonging to the Aryan peoples, a term used in Nazi ideology to refer to people considered racially inferior, particularly Jews, Roma, and Slavs.  The term "Aryan" itself is a misnomer based on flawed linguistic and racial theories.| The Nazis persecuted non-Aryans during the Holocaust.| Synonyms: non-Indo-European (in the original linguistic sense, though not in the Nazi usage),  | Antonyms: Aryan (though this term's usage is problematic and should be avoided) | adjective
