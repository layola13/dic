hypermutability:/ˌhaɪpərˌmjuːtəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 超突变性 | The increased rate of mutation, often in specific regions of the genome. | The hypermutability of certain viruses makes them difficult to treat. 某些病毒的超突变性使它们难以治疗。| 近义词: hypermutation | 反义词: stability, immutability | 词性: noun

hypermutability:/ˌhaɪpərˌmjuːtəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 超变异性 | The quality or state of being hypermutable; unusually high mutation rate. | The hypermutability of this bacterial strain allows it to quickly adapt to new antibiotics.  这种细菌菌株的超变异性使其能够快速适应新的抗生素。| 近义词: hypermutation | 反义词: stability, immutability | 词性: noun

Etymology:  "hyper-" (Greek: ὑπέρ, hupér, "over, beyond") + "mutability" (from Latin "mutabilis" meaning "changeable," from "mutare" meaning "to change").
