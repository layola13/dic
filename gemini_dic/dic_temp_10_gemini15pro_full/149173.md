oxyacid:/ˈɒksiˌæsɪd/ | n. |含氧酸|An acid containing oxygen. An oxyacid is an acid that contains oxygen. Specifically, it is a compound that contains hydrogen, oxygen, and at least one other element, with at least one hydrogen atom bound to oxygen that can dissociate to produce the H+ cation and the anion of the acid.|Sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄) is a strong oxyacid.|近义词:acid|反义词:base|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From oxy- +‎ acid.  Oxy- comes from the Greek word "oxys" meaning "sharp, acid". Acid comes from the Latin word "acidus" also meaning "sour, sharp".
