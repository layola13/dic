outbidder:/ˌaʊtˈbɪdər/| n.|出价更高者；胜过他人出价者|A person who offers a higher price than someone else at an auction or in a competitive bidding situation. |例句：The outbidder offered $10,000 more than the original bid. 出价更高者比最初的出价高出1万美元。

例句：The painting was eventually sold to the outbidder for a record price. 这幅画最终以创纪录的价格卖给了出价更高的人。|近义词：higher bidder, overbidder|反义词: underbidder, losing bidder|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + bidder.  "Out-" acts as a prefix meaning "to surpass or exceed,"  and "bidder" refers to someone who makes a bid.  The combination creates the meaning of someone who surpasses other bidders.
