hamitic:/ˈhæmɪtɪk/ | adj. n.| 含米特人的，含米特语的；含米特人，含米特语 | Relating to or denoting a group of languages spoken across North Africa and the Horn of Africa, now usually regarded as a branch of the Afro-Asiatic family.  Also relating to the peoples speaking these languages, or their characteristic features.  |  Example: Hamitic languages include Berber, Cushitic, and Egyptian. 含米特语包括柏柏尔语、库什特语和埃及语。

Example: Some older anthropological classifications referred to "Hamitic peoples." 一些较旧的人类学分类提到了“含米特人”。| 近义词: Afro-Asiatic | 反义词:  (No clear antonym exists) | 词性: adjective, noun
