blockout:/ˈblɑːkaʊt/ (US), /ˈblɒkaʊt/ (UK)| n.|停电，封锁，中断，(节目单上的)预留时段，(印刷中的)遮挡|A period of time unavailable for appointments, broadcasts, or other activities because it has been reserved for something else.|The theatre has scheduled a blockout for rehearsals next week. 剧院已安排下周预留时间段进行排练。|近义词：blackout, outage, closure, reservation|反义词: availability, opening|名词

blockout:/ˈblɑːkaʊt/ (US), /ˈblɒkaʊt/ (UK)| v.|遮挡，封锁，中断，预留(时间)|To reserve a period of time so that it is unavailable for other activities.|They blocked out three hours for the meeting. 他们预留了三个小时开会。|近义词：reserve, schedule, block off|反义词: open, free up|动词
