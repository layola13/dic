interadaptions:/ˌɪntərˌædæpˈteɪʃənz/ | n. |相互适应|  Reciprocal adaptations between two or more species, where the evolution of one species influences the evolution of the other(s).|  Example: The interadaptions between flowering plants and their pollinators have led to a remarkable diversity of floral forms and pollinator behaviors.  开花植物和它们的传粉者之间的相互适应导致了花卉形态和传粉者行为的显著多样性。| Example: The complex interadaptions within a coral reef ecosystem are crucial for its stability. 珊瑚礁生态系统中复杂的相互适应对其稳定性至关重要。|Synonyms: coadaptation, coevolution| Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps maladaptation in a broader context) | Noun
