imposture:/ɪmˈpɒstʃə(r)/ (US: /ɪmˈpɑːstʃər/)
|n.|欺骗；冒名顶替；诈骗|an act of deceiving someone by pretending to be someone else|He was eventually exposed as an imposture. 他最终被揭穿是一个骗子。|
|synonyms: fraud, deception, trickery, charade, hoax, sham|
|antonyms: honesty, truthfulness, genuineness, authenticity|
|noun|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *imposture*, from Latin *impostūra* (“a deceiving, a cheat”), from *impositus*, past participle of *imponere* (“to impose, to put upon”), from *in-* (“in, on”) + *ponere* (“to place, to put”).
