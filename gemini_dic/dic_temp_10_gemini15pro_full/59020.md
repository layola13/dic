dwale:/dweɪl/| n.|麻醉剂，昏迷，蒙汗药；毒芹|A potion, herb, or other substance that induces a trance, stupor, or deep sleep, especially one used formerly as an anesthetic or to dull the senses.  Historically, often referring to a concoction made from deadly nightshade.|He drank a dwale and fell into a deep slumber. 他喝了一种麻醉剂，陷入了沉睡。|Synonyms: narcotic, opiate, anesthetic, soporific|Antonyms: stimulant, invigorant|noun
