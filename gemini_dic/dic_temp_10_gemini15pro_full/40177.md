contention:/kənˈtɛnʃən/| n.|争论，论点，竞争|a heated disagreement; an assertion, especially one maintained in argument; the act of competing|例句：The main contention of the article is that climate change is real. 这篇文章的主要论点是气候变化是真实的。

例句：There's been much contention about the new policy. 关于这项新政策有很多争议。|近义词：argument, dispute, debate, conflict, rivalry, competition|反义词：agreement, harmony, peace, accord|词性:noun

contention:/kənˈtɛnʃən/| n.|争夺，竞争|the act or process of competing|例句：The team is in contention for the championship.  这个队伍在争夺冠军。

例句：Three runners are in serious contention for the gold medal. 三名选手在认真争夺金牌。|近义词：competition, rivalry, struggle, contest|反义词：surrender, concession|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English contencioun, from Old French contention, from Latin contentiō (“a straining, exertion, struggle”), from contendō (“I strive, contend”), from com- (“together”) + tendō (“I stretch, extend”).
