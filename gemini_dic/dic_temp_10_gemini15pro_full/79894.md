gnatling: /ˈnæt.lɪŋ/ | n. | 小蚊蚋；小烦人 | A small gnat; an insignificant but annoying person. |  The air was thick with gnatlings. 空气中到处都是小蚊蚋。|  He was a gnatling, always buzzing around and causing trouble. 他就像个小蚊蚋，总是嗡嗡地飞来飞去，惹是生非。 | synonyms: midge, mosquito, pest | antonyms: giant | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From gnat + -ling (diminutive suffix).  The word "gnat" comes from Old English "gnæt," which is related to Old High German "knat," and ultimately from a Proto-Germanic root meaning "to bite" or "to gnaw."
