discontentedness's:/ˌdɪskənˈtɛntɪdnɪsɪz/ | n. | 不满足；不满意 | The state of being discontented; lack of contentment.  This word is not commonly used and can often be replaced with simpler terms.  It indicates a possessive form, belonging to the discontentedness.  However, due to its awkward construction, it's highly recommended to rephrase sentences to avoid its usage.|  The discontentedness's root lay in the unfair treatment they received. (Awkward and uncommon - better to rephrase: The root of their discontent lay in the unfair treatment they received.) | 近义词: dissatisfaction, unhappiness, displeasure | 反义词: contentment, satisfaction, happiness | 词性: 名词

词源:
From discontented + -ness. Discontented from dis- + contented.  Ultimately from Latin contentus, past participle of continēre "to hold together, contain."
