incrustment:/ɪnˈkrʌstmənt/| n. |结壳，包壳；镶嵌物|An incrustment is a hard coating or deposit of something, like minerals or dirt, on a surface. It can also refer to the act of covering something with a hard layer.  It also can refer to something embedded in a surface. |The ancient vase was covered in an incrustment of dirt.  这古老的花瓶覆盖着一层污垢。|The incrustment of jewels on the crown made it sparkle. 王冠上镶嵌的珠宝使它闪闪发光。|近义词：coating, covering, deposit, layer, inlay|反义词: (For the sense of covering): erosion, stripping |词性:noun

incrust:/ɪnˈkrʌst/| vt. |使结壳，包壳；镶嵌|To incrust something means to cover it with a hard layer or to embed something in a surface. |The walls of the cave were incrusted with crystals. 洞穴的墙壁上结满了晶体。 |She incrusted the box with precious stones.  她在盒子上镶嵌了宝石。|近义词：coat, cover, embed, inlay|反义词:(For the sense of covering): strip, remove|词性:verb
