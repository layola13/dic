breathe:/briːð/ (US) /briːð/ (UK)| v. n.|呼吸，吸气，呼气；吐露，散发|To inhale and exhale air; to live.  To express (a feeling or idea).|I can hardly breathe in this smoky room. 在这烟雾弥漫的房间里我几乎无法呼吸。|Synonyms: inhale, exhale, respire, live|Antonyms: suffocate, stifle|verb, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *brēthan, from Proto-Germanic *brēthijaną "to smell, exhale," related to Old English brǣth "odor, breath."  Cognate with Old High German brādam "to breathe," Old Norse bræða "to smell."
