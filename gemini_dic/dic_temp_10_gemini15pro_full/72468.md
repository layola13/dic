flunkey: /ˈflʌŋki/ | n. | 仆人，走狗，马屁精 | A servant, especially a liveried one, who performs menial tasks.  A person who behaves servilely to someone important. |  He treated his staff like flunkeys. 他把他的员工当作仆人使唤。The dictator surrounded himself with flunkeys. 独裁者身边围绕着一群马屁精。 | Synonyms: lackey, servant, toady, sycophant | Antonyms: master, boss, leader | noun

flunkey: /ˈflʌŋki/ | v. |  拍马屁，卑躬屈膝 | To behave servilely; to fawn. | He flunkeyed around the CEO, hoping for a promotion. 他围着CEO拍马屁，希望能升职。She refused to flunkey to anyone. 她拒绝向任何人卑躬屈膝。 | Synonyms: fawn, grovel, kowtow | Antonyms: defy, resist, challenge | verb
