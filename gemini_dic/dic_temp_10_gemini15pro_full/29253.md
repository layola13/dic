cashier:/kæˈʃɪər/ /kæˈʃɪr/| n.|收银员|A person who handles payments and receipts in a shop, bank, or other business.|例句：The cashier scanned the groceries.  收银员扫描了杂货。|例句：She worked as a cashier at the supermarket during the summer. 她夏天在超市当收银员。|近义词：teller, clerk|反义词:customer|词性:noun

cashier:/kæˈʃɪər/ /kæˈʃɪr/| vt.|撤职; 解雇 (尤指军官)|To dismiss from a position of authority or trust, especially a military officer.|例句：The captain was cashiered for disobeying orders. 船长因不服从命令而被解职。|例句：The bank cashiered him for embezzlement. 银行因他挪用公款而解雇了他。|近义词：dismiss, fire, sack|反义词:hire, employ|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cassier*, from *casse* "money box," from Italian *cassa* "chest, box," from Latin *capsa* "box, case."
