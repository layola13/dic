Montcalm: /mɒntˈkɑːm/ | n. | 蒙卡尔姆 |  Louis-Joseph de Montcalm-Gozon, Marquis de Saint-Véran (1712-1759), a French soldier and general, most famous for his role in the Seven Years' War, particularly his victory at the Battle of Carillon (also known as the Battle of Ticonderoga) and his death at the Battle of the Plains of Abraham.  The name also refers to places named after him. | The Montcalm Hotel is a luxurious establishment. 蒙卡尔姆酒店是一家豪华酒店。| Near synonyms: general, commander, marquis | Antonyms: civilian, private | noun
