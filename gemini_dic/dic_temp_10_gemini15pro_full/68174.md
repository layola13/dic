faint:/feɪnt/| vi. adj. n.|昏倒，微弱的，昏厥|To lose consciousness briefly.  Lacking clarity or strength. A brief loss of consciousness.|He fainted from the shock. 他震惊得昏了过去。|近义词：swoon, pass out, black out|反义词:revive, recover|词性:动词, 形容词, 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *feint, past participle of feindre “to feign, pretend,” from Latin fingere “to shape, form, invent, pretend.”  The sense of "lose consciousness" developed from the idea of "pretending to be dead."
