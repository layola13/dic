controversially: /ˌkɒntrəˈvɜːʃəli/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntrəˈvɜːrʃəli/ (US) | adv. | 有争议地，引起争议地 | In a way that causes disagreement or argument. |  The book controversially claims that Shakespeare did not write his own plays. 这本书有争议地声称莎士比亚并没有写他自己的戏剧。| synonyms: disputably, contentiously, polemically | antonyms: indisputably, uncontroversially | adverb

Etymology:  From "controversial" + "-ly". "Controversial" comes from Late Latin "controversia" meaning "debate, dispute", from "controversus" meaning "turned against, disputed," from "contra" (against) + "versus" (turned).
