degrading:/dɪˈɡreɪdɪŋ/
adjective
降低身份的；丢脸的；有辱人格的
causing someone to feel that they have lost respect for themselves or others; humiliating.
例句：It was degrading to have to ask for money.  不得不乞讨钱财真是丢脸。

例句：She found the work degrading. 她觉得这份工作很低贱。

近义词：humiliating, shameful, demeaning, debasing, lowering
反义词：uplifting, ennobling, elevating
词性: adjective


degrade:/dɪˈɡreɪd/
verb
降低…的身份；使…丢脸；贬低；降低…的质量；退化；降解
(with object) treat or regard (someone) with contempt or disrespect.
(without object) (of a substance) break down or deteriorate chemically.
例句：The report degrades the educational standards of the college. 这份报告贬低了这所大学的教育水准。

例句：The use of such language degrades all women.  使用这样的语言贬低了所有女性。

近义词：humiliate, shame, demean, debase, lower, deteriorate, decompose
反义词：elevate, enhance, improve
词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin degradare "reduce in rank," from de- "down" + gradus "step, rank."
