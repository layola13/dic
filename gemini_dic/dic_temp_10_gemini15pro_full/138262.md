not-so-much:/ˌnɒt səʊ ˈmʌtʃ/ (British), /ˌnɑːt soʊ ˈmʌtʃ/ (American)| adv.| 不完全是；与其说…不如说…；没那么…| Used to make a statement weaker or less definite. It often implies a comparison or contrast.|  I don't dislike him, not so much dislike as find him rather dull. 我并不讨厌他，与其说讨厌他，不如说我觉得他很无聊。 | Near synonyms: not really, not exactly, not quite, not particularly | Antonyms: very much, definitely, absolutely | adverb

not-so-much:/ˌnɒt səʊ ˈmʌtʃ/ (British), /ˌnɑːt soʊ ˈmʌtʃ/ (American)| adj.| 没那么…的 | Used to describe something that is less significant or important than something else (often implied). |  It was a not-so-much a vacation as a business trip. 这与其说是一个假期，不如说是一次商务旅行。| Near synonyms: less, not as, not quite as | Antonyms: more, very, extremely | adjective
