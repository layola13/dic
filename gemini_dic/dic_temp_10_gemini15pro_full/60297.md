eggplant: /ˈɛɡˌplænt/ (US) /ˈɛɡˌplɑːnt/ (UK)| n.|茄子|A purple-skinned fruit used as a vegetable.  It's called "eggplant" because some early varieties were white or yellowish and resembled goose or hen's eggs.|I made eggplant parmesan for dinner. 我晚饭做了茄子帕尔玛干酪。|Synonyms: aubergine (chiefly British), melongene|Antonyms: None (It's a specific fruit/vegetable; there isn't a direct opposite.)|Noun
