biceps's:/ˈbaɪˌsɛpsɪz/| n.|二头肌|The possessive form of "biceps," referring to something belonging to or associated with the biceps muscle.  Derived from the Latin words "bi" (two) and "caput" (head), referring to the two-headed muscle in the upper arm.|Example: The biceps's strength is impressive.  他的二头肌力量惊人。|Example:  He injured his biceps's tendon. 他伤到了二头肌的肌腱。|Synonym: biceps (often used interchangeably in the possessive)|Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a body part.  Could consider antagonist muscle: triceps)|noun
