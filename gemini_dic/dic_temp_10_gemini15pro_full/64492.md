erodability:/ɪˌrəʊdəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可蚀性，侵蚀性 | The susceptibility of soil or rock to erosion.  The ease with which it can be eroded by wind, water, or other natural agents. | The erodability of the coastline is a major concern for local residents.  海岸线的可蚀性是当地居民的主要担忧。| susceptibility to erosion, vulnerability to erosion | resistance to erosion | noun

erode:/ɪˈroʊd/ | v. | 侵蚀，腐蚀 | (of wind, water, or other natural agents) gradually wear away (soil, rock, or other matter). Gradually destroy or be destroyed. | The cliffs are being eroded by the sea. 悬崖正被海水侵蚀。| wear away, abrade, corrode | build up, strengthen | verb
