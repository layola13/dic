artery:/ˈɑːrtəri/ (US), /ˈɑːtəri/ (UK)| n. |动脉|A blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart to other parts of the body.|Blood is pumped from the heart into the arteries.|近义词: blood vessel,  main line (figurative)|反义词: vein|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *arterie*, from Old French *arterie*, from Latin *arteria*, from Ancient Greek *ἀρτηρία (artēría)*, meaning "windpipe, artery". Originally, arteries were thought to carry air, as they are often found empty after death. The connection with carrying blood was later established.
