full-bloodedly:/ˌfʊlˈblʌdɪdli/
adv.
全心全意地；热情地；彻底地
With all one's energy and enthusiasm; completely.
He threw himself full-bloodedly into the project. 他全心全意地投入到这个项目中。
Synonyms: enthusiastically, wholeheartedly, passionately, vigorously, intensely
Antonyms: halfheartedly, apathetically, listlessly

Etymology:
From "full-blooded" + "-ly."  "Full-blooded" means having strong, healthy blood; vigorous. It originates from "full" + "blood" + "-ed."  "Full" traces back to Old English "full,"  "blood" to Old English "blōd," and "-ed" is a suffix forming adjectives from nouns. "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives.
