gerrymanderer:/ˈdʒɛrɪˌmændərər/ /ˈɡerɪˌmændərər/ | n. | 不公正划分选区者；操纵选区的人 | A person who gerrymanders (manipulates the boundaries of an electoral constituency) so as to favor one party or class. |  The gerrymanderer carved up the district to ensure his party's victory.  这个划分选区者为了确保他所在的党派获胜，对选区进行了划分。| synonym: manipulator, cheat | antonym: impartial arbiter | noun

gerrymander:/ˈdʒɛrɪˌmændər/ /ˈɡerɪˌmændər/ | vt. | 不公正地划分选区；操纵选区 | To divide (a geographic area) into voting districts in a way that gives one party an unfair advantage. | The opposition party accused the ruling party of gerrymandering the state. 反对党指责执政党操纵州的选区划分。| synonym: manipulate, rig | antonym: apportion fairly | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Elbridge Gerry + salamander.  Elbridge Gerry (1744-1814) was the governor of Massachusetts when a redistricting plan was approved that created a district resembling a salamander in shape.  The term was coined in reaction to this plan in 1812.
