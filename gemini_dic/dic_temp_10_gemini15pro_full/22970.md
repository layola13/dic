bottoms-up:/ˈbɒtəmz ʌp/ (UK) /ˈbɑːtəmz ʌp/ (US)| adj. adv. | 干杯；一口喝干；尽情的; 自下而上的 |  Used as a toast before drinking, encouraging one to empty their glass. Also refers to a strategy or approach that starts with the details or basic elements and builds upward to a larger concept or plan. | Let's raise our glasses. Bottoms up! 让我们举起酒杯。干杯！| Synonyms: cheers, down the hatch | Antonyms: sip, savor | phrase
