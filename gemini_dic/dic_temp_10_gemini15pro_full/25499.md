bullheadedness:/ˌbʊlˈhɛdɪdnəs/| n. |固执，顽固，倔强|The quality of being unwilling to change one's mind or consider other people's opinions, even when it is clear that they are wrong. |His bullheadedness prevented him from seeing the other side of the argument. 他的固执使他看不到争论的另一面。|Synonyms: stubbornness, obstinacy, pigheadedness, pertinacity,  willfulness |Antonyms: flexibility, open-mindedness, compliance, docility|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From bull +‎ headed +‎ -ness.  "Bull-headed" refers to the image of a bull stubbornly lowering its head and refusing to budge.
