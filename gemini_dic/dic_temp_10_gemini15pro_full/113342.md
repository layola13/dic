luxuriating:/lʌɡˈʒʊəriˌeɪtɪŋ/
|v.|尽情享受；沉溺于；|To indulge in something that provides pleasure or comfort.  Allowing oneself to enjoy something without restraint.|She was luxuriating in a long, hot bath. 她正尽情享受着一次长时间的热水浴。|Synonyms: indulging, reveling, basking, wallowing|Antonyms: abstaining, refraining, foregoing|verb


luxuriate:/lʌɡˈʒʊərieɪt/
|v.|尽情享受；沉溺于；|To indulge in something that provides pleasure or comfort.  Allowing oneself to enjoy something without restraint.|They luxuriated in the warmth of the tropical sun. 他们在热带阳光的温暖中尽情享受。|Synonyms: indulge, revel, bask, wallow|Antonyms: abstain, refrain, forgo|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *luxuriari*, from *luxuria* "luxury, abundance," related to *luxus* "dislocation, excess," perhaps from PIE root *leug- (1)* "to bend, to be loose, to lie."
