dithyramb:/ˈdɪθɪræm/ | n. |狂热赞美诗；酒神赞歌；狂热兴奋的言辞| A wild, enthusiastic speech or piece of writing, usually in praise of something.  Originally, a choral hymn in ancient Greece dedicated to Dionysus, the god of wine and revelry. |  Example 1: The poet's latest work was a dithyramb to the beauty of nature.  诗人最新的作品是对自然之美的热情赞歌。

Example 2:  His acceptance speech was a dithyramb, full of praise for his supporters and colleagues. 他的获奖感言是一篇热情洋溢的颂词，充满了对支持者和同事的赞美。 | 近义词: eulogy, panegyric, encomium, accolade, tribute | 反义词:  criticism, condemnation, denunciation | 词性: noun

词源: From Greek *dithyrambos*, of unknown origin.
