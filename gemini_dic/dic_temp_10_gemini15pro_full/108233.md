laughing: /ˈlæfɪŋ/ (US), /ˈlɑːfɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. n.|笑的，笑声|The act of laughing; characterized by laughter.  The sound of laughter.|例句：The laughing children enjoyed the clown.  笑着的孩子们很喜欢小丑。|例句：We heard laughing coming from the next room. 我们听到隔壁房间传来笑声。|近义词：chuckling, giggling, guffawing|反义词：crying, sobbing|词性: adjective, noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *laughinge*, *lauhwinge*, present participle of *laughen*, *laughe* (“to laugh”), from Old English *hliehhan* (“to laugh”), from Proto-Germanic *klakʰjaną* (“to laugh, clang, clatter”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱlegʰ- (“to make a sharp noise”). Cognate with Dutch *lachen* (“to laugh”), German *lachen* (“to laugh”), Swedish *le* (“to smile, laugh”), Latin *clārāre* (“to make clear, bright”), Ancient Greek *καλός* (*kalós* - “beautiful, good”).
