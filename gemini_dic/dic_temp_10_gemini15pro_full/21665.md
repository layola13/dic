blusterer:/ˈblʌstərər/ | n. |  吹牛大王；大声嚷嚷的人 |  A person who talks loudly and aggressively, but whose words are usually empty and not to be taken seriously. |  He's a blusterer, all talk and no action. 他是个吹牛大王，光说不练。|  boaster, braggart, loudmouth |  quiet person, meek person | noun


bluster:/ˈblʌstər/ | v. | 猛烈地吹；咆哮；恐吓 | To blow or speak in a loud, blustering manner. |  The wind blustered around the house. 风绕着房子呼啸。|  roar, rage, storm |  whisper, murmur | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *blustren*, probably from Old Norse *blystra* (“to blow, puff”).
