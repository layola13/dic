Etymology:

* non-:  prefix meaning "not" (from Latin)
* concentrate: from Late Latin concentratus, past participle of concentrare "to bring to a common center," from com- "together" (see com-) + centrum "center" (see center (n.)).
* -ive: suffix forming adjectives meaning "tending to; having the nature or quality of" (from Latin -ivus).
* -ness: suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being."
* -es: plural suffix.
