exasperatedly:/ɪɡˈzæspəreɪtɪdli/| adv.|恼怒地，愤怒地|In a manner that shows irritation or annoyance.  Stems from the verb "exasperate," meaning to irritate intensely.|She sighed exasperatedly and threw her hands up in the air. 她恼怒地叹了口气，并将双手举向空中。|近义词：angrily, furiously, irritably|反义词：calmly, patiently, peacefully|词性:副词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin exasperatus, past participle of exasperare "to roughen, irritate," from Latin ex- "out" + asperare "to make rough," from asper "rough, harsh."
