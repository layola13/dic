accoucheur: /ˌækʊˈʃɜːr/ (UK), /ˌɑːkuːˈʃɜːr/ (US)
n.
男接生员；产科男医生
A male midwife or obstetrician.
The accoucheur arrived just in time for the delivery.  男接生员正好赶上分娩。

The accoucheur skillfully delivered the baby.  这位产科男医生熟练地接生了婴儿。

近义词: obstetrician, midwife (if female)
反义词:  None (This word describes a specific profession; there isn't a direct opposite.)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French accoucheur, from accoucher "to deliver a child," from à "to" + coucher "to put to bed."

USAGE->
This word is somewhat archaic and not commonly used in modern English.  "Obstetrician" is generally preferred.  However, it can still be found in older texts or in very formal contexts.
