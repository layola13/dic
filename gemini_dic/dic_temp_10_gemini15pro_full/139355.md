obscure's:/əbˈskjʊərz/ | adj. | 模糊的，晦涩的，昏暗的 |  Belonging to or associated with something obscure; making unclear or unintelligible.  This is the possessive form of the adjective "obscure", implying ownership or association. | The obscure's meaning was lost on the audience. 这晦涩的意义让观众感到困惑。 | 近义词: unclear, vague, ambiguous, indistinct, cryptic | 反义词: clear, obvious, distinct, lucid, transparent | 词性: 形容词 (Possessive)

obscure:/əbˈskjʊər/ | adj. | 模糊的，晦涩的，昏暗的 | Not discovered or known about; uncertain.  |  The origins of the custom are obscure.  这个习俗的起源很模糊。 | 近义词: unclear, vague, ambiguous, indistinct, cryptic | 反义词: clear, obvious, distinct, lucid, transparent | 词性: 形容词

obscure:/əbˈskjʊər/ | vt. |  使模糊，使晦涩，遮掩 | Keep from being seen; conceal.  |  Clouds obscured the moon. 云遮住了月亮。 | 近义词: conceal, hide, cover, mask, veil | 反义词: reveal, expose, uncover, clarify, illuminate | 词性: 动词


词源:  From Late Latin obscurus "dark, dim," from Latin obscurus "covered, dark, shadowy, indistinct, unknown," figuratively "hidden, secret, unintelligible," from ob "over" (see ob-) + scurus "covered," from PIE root *(s)keu- "to cover, conceal."
