lavished: /ˈlævɪʃt/ | v. (past tense) | 慷慨地给予；挥霍；滥用 |  past tense of lavish: to give or bestow something generously or excessively | He lavished attention on his new girlfriend. 他对他的新女友关怀备至。| synonyms: showered, bestowed, heaped, poured, splurged | antonyms: stinted, scrimped, withheld | verb

lavish: /ˈlævɪʃ/ | adj. v. | 奢华的；丰富的；慷慨的；浪费 | adj. sumptuously rich, elaborate, or luxurious. v. to bestow something in generous or extravagant quantities upon. |  The hotel was lavishly decorated. 这家酒店装饰奢华。They lavished praise on the chef. 他们对厨师赞不绝口。| synonyms: adj. luxurious, opulent, extravagant, profuse; v. shower, bestow, heap | antonyms: adj. meager, sparse, simple; v. stint, withhold | adjective, verb


Etymology:
From Middle French *lavasse (“deluge, downpour”), from laver (“to wash”), from Latin lavō (“I wash”).  The sense development is from "washing, drenching" to "profuse, overflowing".
