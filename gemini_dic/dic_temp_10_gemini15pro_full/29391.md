cat-nap: /ˈkætˌnæp/ | n. | 小睡，打盹 | A short sleep, typically taken during the day. |  I took a cat-nap after lunch. 我午饭后打了个盹。| synonyms: doze, siesta, nap | antonyms:  deep sleep, all-nighter | noun

Etymology:  The compound "cat-nap" originated in the early 18th century, likely referencing the way cats sleep lightly and often. "Cat" refers to the feline animal known for its short periods of sleep. "Nap" comes from the Old English "hnappian" meaning "to doze."
