disconcert:/ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrt/ /ˌdɪskənˈsɝːt/| v. |使不安；使困惑；使仓皇失措|To disturb the composure of; to ruffle; to frustrate; to throw into disorder.|The sudden question disconcerted her. 突如其来的问题让她不知所措。|Synonyms: unsettle, perturb, confuse, ruffle, discompose|Antonyms: calm, soothe, reassure, comfort|verb

disconcerting:/ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrtɪŋ/ /ˌdɪskənˈsɝːtɪŋ/| adj. |令人不安的；令人困惑的|Causing one to feel unsettled, perturbed, or embarrassed.|His disconcerting silence made her nervous. 他令人不安的沉默让她感到紧张。|Synonyms: unsettling, disturbing, troubling, perplexing|Antonyms: calming, soothing, reassuring, comforting|adjective
