bickle:/ˈbɪkəl/| n.|腌菜，酸黄瓜;困境，麻烦|A cucumber or other vegetable preserved in vinegar or brine. Also, a difficult situation or predicament.|例句：He ate a bickle with his sandwich. 他和三明治一起吃了一根腌黄瓜。

例句：They found themselves in a real bickle when their car broke down in the middle of nowhere. 他们的车在荒郊野外抛锚了，这让他们陷入了困境。|近义词：pickle, gherkin, predicament, dilemma|反义词:solution, advantage|词性:noun

bickle:/ˈbɪkəl/| v.|腌制|To preserve something in vinegar or brine.|例句：She bickled the cucumbers from her garden. 她把园子里摘的黄瓜腌了起来。

例句：They bickled the onions and carrots along with the cucumbers. 他们把洋葱和胡萝卜和黄瓜一起腌制。|近义词：pickle, preserve, marinade|反义词:N/A|词性:verb

词源:  "bickle" 源自中古荷兰语 "pekel"，意思是 "brine"（盐水）。 这也与现代荷兰语 "pekel" 和德语 "Pökel" 相关，它们都与腌制或腌渍有关。
