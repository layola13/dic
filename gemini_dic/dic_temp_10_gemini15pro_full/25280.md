buffoonly: /bəˈfuːn(ə)li/ | adv. | 滑稽地；丑角般地 | In a comical or ludicrous way, resembling a buffoon. | He danced buffoonly around the room, making everyone laugh. 他滑稽地在房间里跳舞，逗得大家都笑了。 |  He acted buffoonly, tripping over his own feet. 他笨手笨脚地表演，还被自己的脚绊倒了。 | 近义词：comically, ludicrously, clownishly | 反义词：seriously, solemnly, gracefully | 词性: 副词

buffoon: /bəˈfuːn/ | n. | 小丑；丑角 | A person who amuses others by clowning, joking, and antics. | The king's buffoon entertained the court with his silly jokes. 国王的小丑用他愚蠢的笑话娱乐了朝廷。 | The circus buffoon juggled flaming torches while riding a unicycle. 马戏团的小丑骑着独轮车玩耍着燃烧的火把。 | 近义词：clown, jester, fool | 反义词：dignitary, nobleman | 词性: 名词


词源: From Italian *buffone* "jester, clown," from *buffa* "jest, joke." Ultimately of uncertain origin, possibly from Late Latin *buffare* "to puff, blow, swell the cheeks."
