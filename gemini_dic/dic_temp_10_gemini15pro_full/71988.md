flinchingly:/ˈflɪntʃɪŋli/| adv.|畏缩地，退缩地|In a way that shows you are unwilling to do something or are nervous about doing it; in a flinching manner.  This word describes an action done hesitantly or with a sense of fear or reluctance.|She flinchingly agreed to the surgery, terrified of the potential complications. 她害怕可能的并发症，畏缩地同意了手术。|Synonyms: hesitantly, reluctantly, timidly, nervously, apprehensively|Antonyms: boldly, bravely, confidently, resolutely, fearlessly|词性: 副词

flinch:/flɪntʃ/| vi.|畏缩，退缩|To make a sudden, small movement because of pain or fear.|He flinched when the doctor touched his wound. 当医生碰到他的伤口时，他畏缩了一下。|Synonyms: wince, recoil, shrink, cower|Antonyms:  stand firm, brace oneself|词性: 动词
