hawing:/ˈhɔːɪŋ/ | n. | 支吾; 吞吞吐吐; 犹豫 | The act of hesitating or stammering in speech, especially when trying to think of what to say. | He stood there hawing and hawing, unable to make up his mind. 他站在那里支支吾吾，拿不定主意。 | 近义词: hesitating, stammering, faltering, dithering | 反义词: decisiveness, fluency | 词性: 名词

hawing:/ˈhɔːɪŋ/ | v. | 支吾; 吞吞吐吐; 犹豫 | To speak with hesitation and pauses, especially when trying to find the right words. |  "Well," he hawed, "I'm not quite sure." “嗯，”他吞吞吐吐地说，“我不太确定。” | 近义词: hesitate, stammer, falter, dither | 反义词: articulate, declare, pronounce | 词性: 动词


词源: From the Middle English word *hawien, of imitative origin.  It mimics the sound of someone hesitating in speech.
