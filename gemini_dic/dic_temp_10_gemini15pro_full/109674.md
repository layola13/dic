词源:  From Greek *leukophlegmatos* "having white phlegm," from *leukos* "white" + *phlegma* "phlegm."  In ancient Greek medicine, phlegm was believed to be one of the four humors, and an excess of white phlegm was thought to cause apathy and sluggishness.
