Etymology:
Old English *æfentid*, from *æfen* (evening) + *tīd* (time).  The word is a compound of "often" (meaning "frequently") and "tide" (meaning "time" or "season"). However, this is a folk etymology. The actual origin is from the Old English word "æfentīd," which meant "evening time."
