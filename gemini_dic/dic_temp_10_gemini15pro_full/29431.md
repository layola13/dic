catalepsy: /ˈkætəˌlɛpsi/ | n. | 僵住症，木僵 | A medical condition characterized by a trance-like state with loss of voluntary motion and usually of sensation. |  The patient experienced a sudden onset of catalepsy during the stressful event.  这位病人在压力事件中突然出现了僵住症。| Example: During a hypnotic state, the performer induced catalepsy in his subject.  在催眠状态下，表演者使他的对象进入了僵住状态。 | Synonym: trance, rigidity, immobility, stupor | Antonym: mobility, flexibility, responsiveness | noun

cataleptic: /ˌkætəˈlɛptɪk/ | adj. | 僵住症的，木僵的 | Relating to or affected by catalepsy. |  The cataleptic patient remained motionless for hours. 僵住症患者一动不动地保持了几个小时。| Example:  His cataleptic state was induced by hypnosis. 他的僵硬状态是由催眠引起的。 | Synonym: trance-like, rigid, immobile, stuporous | Antonym: mobile, flexible, responsive | adjective
