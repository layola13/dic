hobbled:/ˈhɑːbəld/ (US) /ˈhɒbəld/ (UK)| v. adj.|蹒跚，跛行；阻碍，限制|To walk with difficulty, typically because of an injury to the leg or foot; To hamper the progress or effectiveness of something or someone.|例句：The injured horse hobbled across the field. 受伤的马蹒跚地穿过田野。|近义词：limped, shuffled, hampered, hindered|反义词:ran, sprinted, facilitated, aided|词性:动词，形容词
hobbled:/ˈhɑːbəld/ (US) /ˈhɒbəld/ (UK)| adj.|跛行的，受阻的|Having difficulty walking, typically because of an injury to the leg or foot; Hampered or constrained.|例句：The hobbled economy struggled to recover. 举步维艰的经济难以复苏。|近义词：lame, crippled, hampered, constrained|反义词:healthy, sound, unhindered, free|词性:形容词

词源: From hobble (n.), from Middle English hobelen, probably frequentative of hobben "to hop, limp," of uncertain origin.
