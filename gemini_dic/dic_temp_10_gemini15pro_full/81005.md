gradin:/ˈɡreɪdɪn/ /ˈɡrædɪn/| n.|梯级，等级，分级，斜坡, 阶梯教室|A series of steps or seats arranged in tiers, especially in an amphitheater or stadium. Also refers to the act of arranging something in grades or levels, or a slope.|The gradin offered a perfect view of the stage.  梯级提供了观看舞台的完美视野。|synonyms: steps, tiers, seating, terracing, slope, incline, bank|antonyms: level, flat, plain|noun

gradin:/ˈɡreɪdɪn/ /ˈɡrædɪn/| n.|分级，等级划分|The act of sorting or arranging items into different categories based on quality, size, etc.|The gradin of the apples ensures consistent quality in each bag. 苹果的分级确保每袋苹果的质量一致。|synonyms: sorting, classifying, categorizing, ranking, grading|antonyms: mixing, combining, jumbling|noun
