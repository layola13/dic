overcivilizing: /ˌoʊvərˈsɪvɪlaɪzɪŋ/
|v.|过度文明化；过分教化|The process of imposing excessive or inappropriate civilizing influences upon a person or group, often resulting in the suppression of natural instincts or traditional ways of life.| Some anthropologists argue that overcivilizing indigenous populations leads to the loss of valuable cultural practices. 一些人类学家认为，过度文明化土著居民会导致宝贵的文化习俗的丧失。|over-cultivating, over-refining|undercivilizing|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "civilizing" (present participle of "civilize," from "civil" + "-ize").  "Civil" ultimately derives from Latin "civis" (citizen).
