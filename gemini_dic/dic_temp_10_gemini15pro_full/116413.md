matachin:/ˌmɑːtʃəˈtʃiːn/
|n.|
|Matachin舞；Matachin舞蹈者|
|A traditional masked dance performed in parts of Spain and Latin America, often involving mock combat with swords or sticks.  It is also used to refer to a performer of this dance.|
|The matachines danced in the town square during the festival.| 节日期间，Matachin舞者在镇上的广场上跳舞。

|The colorful costumes of the matachines were a highlight of the celebration.| Matachin舞者色彩鲜艳的服装是庆典的一大亮点。
|Synonyms: dancer, performer|
|Antonyms: audience, spectator|
|noun|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *matachín*, possibly from Arabic *mutawajjihin* (متوجّهين), plural participle of *tawajjaha* (توجّه) "to turn towards, to face", or perhaps of Berber origin.
