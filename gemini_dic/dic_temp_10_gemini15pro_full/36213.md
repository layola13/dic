cogitation:/ˌkɒdʒɪˈteɪʃən/ /ˌkɑːdʒɪˈteɪʃən/| n. | 沉思，思考，冥想 |The process of thinking deeply and seriously about something. |She was lost in cogitation, trying to solve the puzzle. 她陷入了沉思，试图解开这个谜题。| After hours of cogitation, he finally had a solution. 经过数小时的思考，他终于找到了解决办法。 |近义词：contemplation, reflection, deliberation, musing |反义词：inattention, heedlessness |词性:noun

cogitation:/ˌkɒdʒɪˈteɪʃən/ /ˌkɑːdʒɪˈteɪʃən/| n. | 思想，想法 |A thought or idea. |The cogitations of great philosophers have shaped human history.  伟大哲学家的思想塑造了人类历史。| His latest cogitations on the subject are quite profound. 他对这个主题的最新思考相当深刻。| 近义词：thought, idea, notion, concept |反义词：- |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cogitationem, the accusative singular of cogitatio, from Latin cogitare (“to think”), frequentative of cogere (“to compel, collect, bring together”).
