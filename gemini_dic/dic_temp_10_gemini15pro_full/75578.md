fuddy-duddy:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi/ | n. | 老顽固，守旧的人 | A person who is old-fashioned and fussy, especially about their clothes or opinions. |  He's such a fuddy-duddy, he still wears a bowler hat. 他真是个老顽固，他还戴着圆顶硬礼帽。 | synonyms: conservative, traditionalist, diehard, stick-in-the-mud | antonyms: modernist, progressive, radical, innovator | noun


Etymology:
Originated in the late 19th century.  The exact etymology is uncertain, but it is believed to be imitative, perhaps suggesting a fussy or hesitant manner of speaking.
