dichromatism: /daɪˈkroʊməˌtɪzəm/
noun
二色性；二色视觉
The condition of having two types of functioning color receptors, called cone cells, in the eyes. This results in a limited range of color vision.
Dichromatism is a deficiency in color vision.
Some animals, like dogs, exhibit dichromatism. 一些动物，例如狗，表现出二色性视觉。
Dichromatic vision allows them to see blues, yellows, and shades of gray. 二色视觉使它们能够看到蓝色、黄色和灰色阴影。

近义词: color blindness, color deficiency
反义词: trichromacy (三色视觉)
词性: noun

dichromatic: /ˌdaɪkrəˈmætɪk/
adjective
二色的；二色性的
Having or exhibiting two colors.  Relating to dichromatism.
A dichromatic bird has feathers of two distinct colors.  一只二色的鸟有两色分明的羽毛。
This species of fish is dichromatic, with males and females displaying different coloration.  这种鱼是二色的，雄性和雌性显示不同的颜色。

近义词: two-colored, bicoloured, bicolored
反义词: monochromatic, trichromatic
词性: adjective
