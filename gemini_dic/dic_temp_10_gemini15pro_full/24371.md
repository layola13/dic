bring-to-terms:/ˈbrɪŋ tə tɜːrmz/ | phrasal verb | 使接受；使妥协；使顺从 | To cause someone to accept a difficult or unpleasant situation, often through reasoning or persuasion. |  After his diagnosis, it took him months to bring himself to terms with his illness.  在他确诊之后，他花了几个月的时间才接受自己的病情。

bring-to-terms:/ˈbrɪŋ tə tɜːrmz/ | phrasal verb | 使接受；使妥协；使顺从 | To negotiate an agreement or settlement with someone. | The company finally brought the union to terms after a long and difficult strike.  经过长期而艰难的罢工，公司最终与工会达成了协议。

bring-to-terms:/ˈbrɪŋ tə tɜːrmz/ | phrasal verb | 使接受；使妥协；使顺从 | (Less common) To subdue or control someone or something. | The police brought the rioters to terms with tear gas and batons. 警察用催泪瓦斯和警棍制服了暴徒。


近义词: reconcile, accept, adjust, come to grips with, cope with
反义词: resist, deny, fight, reject
