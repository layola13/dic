old-worldly:/ˌoʊldˈwɜːrldli/
|adj.|老派的；旧世界的；古色古香的|Belonging to or characteristic of the past, especially in being traditional or conservative. It often implies a charm or quaintness associated with older times and customs.|The hotel had an old-worldly charm with its antique furniture and fireplaces. 这家酒店的古董家具和壁炉散发出一种旧世界的魅力。|近义词: quaint, traditional, conservative, formal, old-fashioned|反义词: modern, trendy, contemporary|词性:adjective


词源:
From "old world" + "-ly" (adverbial suffix) + "-ness" (noun suffix) forming "old-worldliness" and its plural "old-worldlinesses". "Old world" refers to Europe, Asia, and Africa, continents known to Europeans before the discovery of the Americas.
