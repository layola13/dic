barracked:/ˈbærəkt/ (UK), /ˈbærɪkt/ (US)| v. |起哄，喝倒彩；(为…)欢呼，加油|to shout loudly, especially to show support for or opposition to a speaker, performer, or team|The crowd barracked the visiting team relentlessly.  观众不停地对客队喝倒彩。|The home fans barracked their team to victory. 主场球迷为他们的球队加油助威，最终球队获胜。|Synonyms: jeer, heckle, cheer, root (for)|Antonyms: applaud, praise|verb

barracked:/ˈbærəkt/ (UK), /ˈbærɪkt/ (US)| adj. |被关押在营房中的|confined to barracks as a punishment|The soldiers were barracked for a week after the incident.  事件发生后，士兵们被关在营房里一周。|The rebellious troops were barracked until further notice.  叛乱的军队被关押在营房里，直到另行通知。|Synonyms: confined, imprisoned, restricted|Antonyms: free, liberated|adjective
