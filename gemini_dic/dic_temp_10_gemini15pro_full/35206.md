clubs:/klʌbz/ (UK) /klʌbz/ (US) | n. | 俱乐部；社团；(纸牌)梅花 |A group of people organized for a joint purpose, typically in a particular field of study, or the building used by such a group; also, one of the four suits in a deck of playing cards, represented by a black trefoil. |  She joined several clubs at university. 她加入了大学里的几个社团。 | synonyms: societies, associations, organizations, groups, cliques, gangs, (for playing cards) trefoils | antonyms: (for groups) individuals, loners; (for playing cards - no direct opposite) | noun

clubs:/klʌbz/ (UK) /klʌbz/ (US) | n. |  棍棒; 球杆| A heavy stick, typically made of wood, used as a weapon; also, a long, thin stick used in golf to hit the ball. |  He defended himself with a club. 他用一根棍棒自卫。 | synonyms: (as weapon) bludgeon, cudgel, stick, truncheon; (as golf club) driver, iron, putter, wood | antonyms: (no direct opposite for either sense) | noun
