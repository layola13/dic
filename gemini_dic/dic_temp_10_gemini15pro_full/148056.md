oversale: /ˌoʊvərˈseɪl/ (US), /ˌəʊvəˈseɪl/ (UK)
verb
超额销售；过分吹嘘
to sell more of something, such as tickets, than is available or to praise something too much, making it seem better than it really is.
The airline oversold the flight, and some passengers had to be bumped. 这家航空公司超售了机票，一些乘客不得不被拒载。
The salesman oversold the car's features, and I was disappointed when I drove it. 销售员过分夸大了这辆车的性能，我开的时候很失望。
近义词: overbook, exaggerate, hype
反义词: undersell, downplay
词性: verb

oversale: /ˈoʊvərseɪl/ (US), /ˈəʊvəseɪl/ (UK)
noun
超额销售；过分吹嘘
the act of selling more of something than is available or the act of praising something too much
The oversale of tickets led to chaos at the concert. 门票的超售导致了音乐会的混乱。
The oversale of the product eventually hurt its reputation. 对该产品的过分吹嘘最终损害了它的声誉。
近义词: overbooking, exaggeration, hype
反义词: underselling, downplaying
词性: noun


词源: "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "sale" (noun)
