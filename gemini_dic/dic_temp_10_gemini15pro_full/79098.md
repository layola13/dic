glad-handed:/ˌɡlædˈhændɪd/
adjective
热情友好的；虚情假意的
Marked by hearty, effusive, and often insincere friendliness.  Given to shaking hands readily and warmly.
He's a glad-handed politician, always eager to please. 他是一位热情友好的政治家，总是渴望取悦他人。
The glad-handed salesman greeted every customer with a wide smile.  这位热情洋溢的推销员笑容满面地迎接每一位顾客。
近义词:affable, cordial, gregarious, outgoing
反义词:aloof, reserved, cold, distant
词性:adjective

glad-hand:/ˈɡlædˌhænd/
verb
热情地握手；虚情假意地打招呼
To greet or welcome someone in an effusive, often insincere manner, typically involving shaking hands.
The mayor glad-handed the visitors, hoping to win their votes. 市长热情地与来访者握手，希望能赢得他们的选票。
The salesman glad-handed potential clients at the conference.  推销员在会议上热情地与潜在客户打招呼。
近义词:schmooze, butter up
反义词:snub, ignore
词性:verb
