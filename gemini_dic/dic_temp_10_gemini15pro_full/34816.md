clinched:/klɪntʃt/| adj. v. | 确定的；稳操胜券的；紧握的 | Confirmed or settled conclusively; secured firmly.  From the verb "clinch." |  He clinched the victory with a final goal. 他以最后一球锁定了胜局。 | Synonyms: secured, confirmed, guaranteed, sealed | Antonyms:  uncertain, lost,  tenuous | verb

clinched:/klɪntʃt/| adj. v. | 紧握的；咬紧的 | Held or grasped tightly. From the verb "clinch." | She clinched her fists in anger. 她愤怒地握紧了拳头。 | Synonyms: gripped, grasped, clenched | Antonyms: released, loosened, open | verb

clinched:/klɪntʃt/| adj. v. | (of a nail or bolt) bent over or hammered in at the end to secure it | （指钉子或螺栓）弯曲或敲打末端以固定 | The carpenter clinched the nails to secure the board. 木匠把钉子敲弯以固定木板。| Synonyms: fastened, secured, fixed | Antonyms: loosened, detached | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English clenchen, clinchen, from Old English *clencan (“to bend, twist”), from Proto-Germanic *klinkaną (“to clink, ring, clang”), *klankjaną (“to make a sharp sound”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ḱleng- (“to resound, ring”). Cognate with Dutch klemmen (“to pinch, jam, grip”), German klemmen (“to pinch, jam, grip”). Related to clink.
