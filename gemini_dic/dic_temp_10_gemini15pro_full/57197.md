down-scaled:/ˌdaʊnˈskeɪld/ | adj. |  缩小规模的，降低规模的 | Reduced in size or scale. |  The company down-scaled its operations due to the economic downturn.  公司因经济衰退而缩减了业务规模。| synonyms: reduced, smaller, miniaturized | antonyms: upscaled, enlarged, expanded | adjective

down-scaled:/ˌdaʊnˈskeɪld/ | v. | 缩小规模，降低规模 | To reduce in size or scale. | They down-scaled the project after realizing it was too ambitious.  他们意识到项目过于雄心勃勃后缩减了规模。| synonyms: reduce, shrink, decrease | antonyms: upscale, enlarge, expand | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
"down-" (adverb and prefix) Old English "dūn," meaning "from a higher to a lower place."
"scale" (noun)  Old Norse "skal," meaning "bowl, scale of a balance."
"scale" (verb)  from the noun, with the meaning evolving to "climb, ascend," and later "to measure or adjust according to a scale."
"down-scaled" (adjective/verb) a combination of "down" and "scaled," implying a reduction in scale or size.
