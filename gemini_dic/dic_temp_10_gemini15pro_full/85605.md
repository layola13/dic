hauberk:/ˈhɔːbɜːrk/| n.|锁子甲，锁甲衣|A long, sleeveless shirt of mail or scale armor, usually reaching to about mid-thigh, worn as defensive armor from the 12th to the 14th century.|The knight donned his hauberk before riding into battle.  骑士在骑马出战前穿上了他的锁子甲。|Synonyms: chainmail, mail shirt, byrnie|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps civilian clothing)|Noun
