paradoxically:/ˌpærəˈdɒksɪkli/ /ˌpærəˈdɑːksɪkli/| adv. |似矛盾地，反常地|In a seemingly contradictory way; contrary to expectations.|例句：Paradoxically, the less he slept, the more energetic he appeared. 他睡眠越少，看起来反而越有活力。|例句：Paradoxically, the new tax laws, designed to help small businesses, actually hurt them.  反常的是，旨在帮助小型企业的新税法实际上损害了它们。|近义词：ironically, surprisingly, counterintuitively, unexpectedly|反义词: predictably, expectedly|词性: 副词

Etymology:
From paradox + -ical + -ly.  "Paradox" comes from the Greek "paradoxon," meaning "contrary to expectation," from "para-" (beside, contrary to) + "doxa" (opinion, belief).
