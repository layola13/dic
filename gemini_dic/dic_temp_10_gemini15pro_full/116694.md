maturers:/məˈtʊərərz/
n.
成熟者；使成熟的人或物
People or things that cause something to mature or become ripe.
The warm sun and rich soil are the maturers of the grapes. 温暖的阳光和肥沃的土壤使葡萄成熟。
近义词：developers, ripeners
反义词：immaturers, retardants
词性：名词


mature:/məˈtʃʊr/
v.
成熟；使…成熟
To become fully developed or ripe; to bring to full development or ripeness.
The apples matured on the tree. 苹果在树上成熟了。
近义词：ripen, develop, age
反义词：decay, rot, regress
词性：动词

mature:/məˈtʃʊr/
adj.
成熟的；成年人的；深思的；到期的；(of cheese, wine, etc.) fully developed in flavour and ready to eat or drink
Fully developed physically; behaving in a sensible way like an adult.
She has a mature attitude towards her work. 她对工作态度成熟。
近义词：ripe, grown-up, adult, developed
反义词：immature, childish, undeveloped
词性：形容词

词源：From Old French *matur, from Latin maturus "ripe, timely, early," figuratively "perfect, complete," from PIE root *meH-(2) "to mow, reap, gather." The notion is of "having reached the point of gathering."
