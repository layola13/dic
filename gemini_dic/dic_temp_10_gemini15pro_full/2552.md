admissible:/ədˈmɪsɪb(ə)l/ /ədˈmɪsəbəl/| adj.|可接受的；可容许的；可采纳的|Allowable or acceptable, especially in a court of law.|Her testimony was ruled admissible by the judge. 法官裁定她的证词可采纳。

The evidence was deemed admissible in court.  该证据被认为在法庭上可接受。|近义词：acceptable, allowable, valid, legitimate, permissible|反义词：inadmissible, unacceptable, invalid, prohibited|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin admissibilis, from Latin admiss- ‘admitted’, from the verb admittere, from ad- ‘to’ + mittere ‘send’.

USAGE->
Formal writing:
The admissible evidence clearly pointed to the defendant's guilt. = 可接受的证据清楚地表明了被告有罪。
Only admissible data will be included in the study. = 只有可接受的数据才会被纳入研究。

Legal contexts:
The judge ruled that the evidence was admissible. = 法官裁定证据可受理。
Admissible hearsay is a complex area of law. = 可采纳的传闻证据是法律中一个复杂的领域。

General usage:
Is this behavior admissible in your culture? = 在你们的文化中，这种行为可以接受吗？
The noise levels were barely admissible. = 噪音水平几乎难以忍受（勉强可接受）。

Describing options or solutions:
Several admissible solutions to the problem were proposed. =  针对这个问题提出了几个可行的解决方案。
Of the three options, only two were deemed admissible. = 三个选项中，只有两个被认为是可行的。


PHRASE->
admissible evidence = 可采纳的证据
admissible standards = 可接受的标准
deemed admissible = 被认为可接受
ruled admissible = 裁定可接受
barely admissible = 勉强可接受
