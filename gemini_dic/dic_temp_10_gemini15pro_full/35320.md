cnida:/ˈnaɪdə/| n. |刺细胞|A stinging cell, characteristic of cnidarians (e.g., jellyfish, corals, sea anemones). These cells contain nematocysts, which are specialized organelles that eject a barbed or venomous thread.|Jellyfish use their cnida to paralyze prey.|Synonyms: nematocyst, stinging cell|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "sensory cell" as a contrasting cell type)|Noun
