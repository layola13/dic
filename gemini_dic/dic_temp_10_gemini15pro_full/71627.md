flatlined: /ˈflætˌlaɪnd/ | v. | 变得毫无生气；(尤指心跳)停止；失败；毫无进展 | To become inactive or unproductive; to fail or cease to make progress, often used in medical contexts to refer to a cessation of heartbeat. | The patient's heart flatlined, but doctors were able to revive him. 病人的心跳停止了，但医生们成功地使他苏醒过来。 | The project flatlined due to lack of funding.  由于缺乏资金，该项目毫无进展。| synonyms: crashed, stalled, failed, died, expired | antonyms: revived, recovered, improved, progressed | verb

flatlined: /ˈflætˌlaɪnd/ | adj. | (心跳)停止的；毫无生气的；失败的；毫无进展的 | Inactive, unproductive, failed, or showing no progress, especially in a medical context referring to a stopped heartbeat. | The flatlined economy needs urgent intervention. 停滞不前的经济需要紧急干预。|  The doctors worked frantically to save the flatlined patient. 医生们疯狂地工作以拯救心跳停止的病人。| synonyms: stagnant, moribund, dead, unsuccessful | antonyms: thriving, vibrant, successful, progressing | adjective
