graving:/ˈɡreɪvɪŋ/ | n. | 雕刻；雕刻品；雕刻术 | The act or art of carving designs, letters, etc., into a hard surface like wood or stone. graving:/ˈɡreɪvɪŋ/ | adj. | 渴望的; 严重的; 重要的 |  Feeling a strong desire or need for something. Serious and important. |  He had a graving look in his eyes as he stared at the food. 他盯着食物，眼中流露出渴望的神情。  The doctor informed her of the graving nature of her illness. 医生告诉她病情严重。 | Synonyms: yearning, longing, serious, grave | Antonyms:  apathetic, indifferent, trivial, minor | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *grafung, verbal noun from grafan "to dig, carve," from Proto-Germanic *grabanan, from Proto-Indo-European *ghrebh- "to dig, scratch, scrape."
