exultance:/ɪɡˈzʌltəns/| n. |欣喜；欢欣；狂喜|A feeling of triumphant joy and jubilation.|She danced with exultance after winning the race. 她赢了比赛后欣喜若狂地跳起了舞。|
例句：His exultance at their victory was infectious. 他对他们胜利的喜悦很有感染力。|
例句：The team's exultance was palpable after their stunning victory. 球队在取得惊人胜利后，欣喜之情溢于言表。|
近义词：joy, elation, jubilation, rapture, delight|
反义词：despair, sorrow, grief, misery|
词性:noun


exultant:/ɪɡˈzʌltənt/| adj. |欣喜若狂的；欢欣鼓舞的|Feeling or expressing great joy or triumph.|An exultant cheer went up from the crowd.  人群中爆发出欢呼声。|
例句：They were exultant at the news of their engagement. 他们听到订婚的消息欣喜若狂。|
例句：The exultant fans poured onto the field after the game. 欣喜若狂的球迷在比赛结束后涌入球场。|
近义词：joyful, jubilant, triumphant, elated|
反义词：despondent, dejected, miserable|
词性:adjective
