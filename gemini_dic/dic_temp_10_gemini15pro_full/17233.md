bawler:/ˈbɔːlər/| n.|大声哭叫的人；爱哭鬼|A person who cries loudly or frequently, especially a child.|The child was a terrible bawler, crying at the slightest provocation.  这个孩子是个十足的爱哭鬼，稍微一激怒就哭。|例句：The little bawler finally stopped crying when he got his candy.  这个小哭包终于拿到糖果后停止了哭泣。

例句：He's such a bawler; he cries over everything! 他真是个爱哭鬼，什么事都哭！|近义词：crybaby, whiner, complainer|反义词:stoic|词性:noun

bawl:/bɔːl/| v.|大声哭；放声痛哭|To cry loudly and unrestrainedly.|The baby bawled for hours. 婴儿哭了几个小时。|例句：She bawled her eyes out when she heard the bad news. 她听到坏消息后痛哭流涕。

例句：The coach bawled at the players for their poor performance. 教练因球员表现不佳而大声斥责他们。|近义词：cry, weep, sob, yell, shout|反义词:whisper, murmur|词性:verb


词源: From Middle English *baullen, *bawlen, of uncertain origin; compare Middle Dutch baullen (“to bark, bay”), German bellen (“to bark, bay”). Perhaps imitative.
