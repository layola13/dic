leak-out:/ˈliːk aʊt/
verb phrase
泄露；透露
(of secret information) to become known
例句：News of the merger leaked out before it was officially announced.  合并的消息在正式宣布之前就泄露了。

例句：Details of the confidential report leaked out to the press.  机密报告的细节泄露给了媒体。
近义词：get out,  transpire,  emerge,  become known,  come out
反义词：conceal,  suppress, hide
词性：动词短语


leak:/liːk/
noun
泄漏；漏洞
a hole or crack through which a liquid or gas can escape
例句：There's a leak in the roof. 屋顶漏水了。

例句：The leak in the pipe was causing a lot of damage.  管道泄漏造成了很大的损害。
近义词：hole, crack,  fissure,  aperture,  opening
反义词：seal, closure
词性：名词

leak:/liːk/
verb
泄漏；透露
(of a container or covering) accidentally allow a liquid or gas to escape
例句：The pipe is leaking. 管道漏水了。

例句：The roof leaks when it rains.  下雨时屋顶会漏水。
近义词：escape,  ooze,  seep,  dribble,  trickle
反义词：contain, hold
词性：动词


out:/aʊt/
adverb
在外；向外
away from the inside or middle of something
例句：He went out.  他出去了。

例句：She looked out the window.  她向窗外看去。
近义词：outside, exterior, external
反义词：in, inside, internal
词性：副词
