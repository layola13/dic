counterfoil:/ˈkaʊntərfɔɪl/ | n. | 存根，票根 | A part of a check, ticket, or other document that is kept as a record after the main part has been given to someone else. |  I kept the counterfoil of the train ticket as proof of purchase. 我保留了火车票的存根作为购买凭证。| synonym: stub, duplicate, receipt | antonym: original, whole | noun

counterfoil:/ˈkaʊntərfɔɪl/ | n. | (比喻) 副本，对应物 | Something that corresponds to or complements something else. |  The two halves of the torn document served as counterfoils to each other.  撕裂文件的两半互为对应物。| synonym: counterpart, complement | antonym: opposite, contrast | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *contrefoil*, from *contre* (against) + *foil* (leaf).  The original meaning referred to a leaf of parchment or paper that was folded in two, with one half serving as a record and the other as the main document.
