ill-concealed:/ˌɪlkənˈsiːld/
|adj.|
|难以掩饰的，不加掩饰的|
|Meaning: poorly hidden; not effectively concealed.  Formed from "ill" (badly) + "concealed" (hidden).|
|例句：Her ill-concealed anger made everyone uncomfortable. 她难以掩饰的愤怒让每个人都感到不舒服。|

|例句：He cast an ill-concealed glance at the expensive watch. 他不加掩饰地瞥了一眼那块昂贵的手表。|

|近义词：obvious, apparent, evident, transparent, unconcealed|
|反义词：concealed, hidden, disguised, masked|
|词性: adjective|


ETYMOLOGY->
From ill- + concealed, past participle of conceal (v.).  "Conceal" comes from Old French "conceler," from Latin "con-" (with, together) + "celare" (to hide).
