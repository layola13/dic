beghard: /ˈbɛɡhɑːrd/ | n. | 贝加尔德; 乞丐修士 | A member of a lay religious order of men, originating in the Netherlands in the 12th century, who lived by begging and devoted themselves to good works.  They were similar to the Beguines, a female order. |  The beghards were often persecuted for their unorthodox beliefs. 贝加尔德教派经常因其非正统的信仰而受到迫害。 |  The beghard travelled from town to town, relying on the charity of others. 这个贝加尔德修士辗转于各个城镇之间，依靠他人的施舍生活。 | 近义词: mendicant, friar | 反义词: None | 词性: noun
