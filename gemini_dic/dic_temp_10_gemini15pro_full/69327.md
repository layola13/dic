fecula:/ˈfɛkjʊlə/ | n. |淀粉；绿末|Starchy substance obtained by washing the pulverized root or seeds of certain plants, like potatoes, arrowroot, or cassava. It is a fine powder, white or slightly yellowish, used for thickening sauces and soups, in making industrial starches, and in pharmacy.|The recipe calls for a tablespoon of fecula to thicken the gravy.|近义词:starch, amylum|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "liquid" or "solution" in specific contexts)|noun
