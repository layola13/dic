gallstone:/ˈɡɔːlˌstoʊn/ (US), /ˈɡɔːlˌstəʊn/ (UK)| n. |胆结石|A small, hard crystalline mass formed abnormally in the gallbladder or bile ducts, consisting chiefly of cholesterol, calcium bilirubinate, and calcium carbonate.|My grandmother had her gallstones removed surgically.|Synonym: biliary calculus|Antonym: None|Noun
