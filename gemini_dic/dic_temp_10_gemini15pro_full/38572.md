concomitance:/kənˈkɒmɪtəns/ /ˌkɑːnkɑːˈmɪtəns/| n. |伴随，共存|The concomitance of poverty and crime is a complex social issue. 贫困和犯罪的伴随是一个复杂的社会问题。|The study explored the concomitance of stress and illness. 这项研究探讨了压力与疾病的伴随关系。|近义词：coexistence, accompaniment, concurrence|反义词：dissociation, separation|词性: 名词

concomitant:/kənˈkɒmɪtənt/ /ˌkɑːnkɑːˈmɪtənt/| adj. |伴随的，共存的|There are concomitant risks associated with this type of investment. 这种类型的投资伴随有风险。|The increase in tourism brought concomitant economic benefits. 旅游业的增长带来了随之而来的经济效益。|近义词：accompanying, attendant, concurrent|反义词：separate, independent|词性: 形容词

concomitantly:/kənˈkɒmɪtəntli/ /ˌkɑːnkɑːˈmɪtəntli/| adv. |伴随地，共存地|The patient's blood pressure and heart rate increased concomitantly. 病人的血压和心率同时升高。|The economy grew, and concomitantly, unemployment fell. 经济增长，随之而来的是失业率下降。|近义词：concurrently, simultaneously|反义词：separately, independently|词性: 副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin concomitans, present participle of concomitari "to accompany," from com- "together" + comitari "to accompany," from comes "companion."
