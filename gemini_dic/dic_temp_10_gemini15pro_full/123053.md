monetarism: /ˈmɒnɪtərɪzəm/ (UK) /ˈmɑːnɪtərɪzəm/ (US) | n. | 货币主义 | A school of economic thought that emphasizes the role of the money supply in controlling inflation and economic growth.  It holds that changes in the money supply have major influences on national output in the short run and on the price level over longer periods. |  Example: Monetarism was a dominant economic theory in the 1980s.  货币主义在20世纪80年代是一种主流的经济理论。

Example:  The government's policies reflect a belief in monetarism. 政府的政策反映了对货币主义的信仰。 | 近义词: monetary policy, quantitative easing (in some contexts) | 反义词: Keynesianism, fiscalism | 词性: noun
