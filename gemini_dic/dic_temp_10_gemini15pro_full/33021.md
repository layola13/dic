chortled:/ˈtʃɔːrt(ə)ld/ | v. |  轻声地欢笑；咯咯地笑 | To chuckle gleefully.  It suggests a combination of chuckling and snorting. |  He chortled with delight at his own joke. 他为自己开的玩笑高兴地轻声笑了。| synonyms: chuckled, giggled, guffawed | antonyms: sobbed, cried, mourned | verb

chortle:/ˈtʃɔːrt(ə)l/ | n. | 轻声的笑；咯咯的笑 | A gleeful chuckle. | He let out a chortle of amusement. 他发出一声愉快的轻笑。| synonyms: chuckle, giggle, guffaw | antonyms: sob, cry, wail | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

Coined by Lewis Carroll in his book "Through the Looking-Glass, and What Alice Found There" (1871), blending "chuckle" and "snort."
