over-righteous:/ˌoʊvərˈraɪtʃəs/| adj.|自以为是，假正经，道貌岸然|Characterized by excessive or ostentatious righteousness; sanctimonious.|He gave me an over-righteous lecture about the dangers of drinking.|近义词：sanctimonious, self-righteous, hypocritical, pietistic|反义词：humble, modest, unpretentious|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From over + righteous.  "Over" acts as an intensifier, indicating an excess or extreme of righteousness. "Righteous" comes from Middle English *rightwise*, from Old English *rihtwīs* "just, upright," composed of *riht* "right" and *wīs* "wise, manner."
