balancer:/ˈbælənsər/ | n. | 平衡器，平衡装置，镇流器 | A device or system that maintains or restores equilibrium or stability. | The tightrope walker used a long pole as a balancer. 走钢丝的人用一根长杆作为平衡器。| synonym: stabilizer, regulator, counterweight | antonym: destabilizer | noun

balancer:/ˈbælənsər/ | n. | 平衡者，协调者 | A person who balances things or acts as a mediator. |  He's the balancer in the group, always mediating disputes. 他是小组中的协调者，总是调解纠纷。 | synonym: mediator, arbitrator, conciliator | antonym: agitator | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "balance," which comes from Old French "balancer" (to swing, waver, hesitate), derived from Vulgar Latin "*balancium" (balance, pair of scales), from Late Latin "bilanx" (having two scales), from "bis" (twice) + "lanx" (plate, dish).
