accurst: /əˈkɜːrst/ /əˈkɜːːst/ | adj. |被诅咒的，可憎的|Under an accurst influence; doomed to evil, misery, or misfortune.  Cursed, damned.|The accurst monster haunted the village. 被诅咒的怪物在村庄里出没。

The king banished the accurst prince. 国王放逐了被诅咒的王子。|近义词：cursed, damned, doomed, ill-fated|反义词：blessed, fortunate, lucky|词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *accursed, *acursed, from Old English ācursod “accursed,” past participle of ācursian “to curse,” equivalent to ā- “intensive prefix” + cursian “to curse” (from Proto-Germanic *kursōjaną, from *kursaz “heavy; oppressive, burdensome; severe”). More at a- (1), curse.

词源：
来源于中古英语 *accursed, *acursed，来自古英语 ācursod “被诅咒的”，是 ācursian “诅咒”的过去分词，相当于 ā- “加强前缀” + cursian “诅咒”（来自原始日耳曼语 *kursōjaną，来自 *kursaz “沉重的；压迫的，繁重的；严厉的”）。更多信息请参见 a- (1), curse。


USAGE->
由于accurst是一个较为古老且正式的词汇，现在使用频率较低，主要出现在文学作品中，尤其在诗歌或具有戏剧性的场景中。以下是一些用法示例：

描述被诅咒的人或物：
The accurst ship sank to the bottom of the ocean. = 被诅咒的船沉到了海底。
The villagers feared the accurst forest. = 村民们害怕那片被诅咒的森林。

表达强烈的厌恶或憎恨：
"Accurst be the day I met him!" she cried. = “我诅咒遇见他的那一天！”她哭喊道。
He muttered an accurst phrase under his breath. = 他低声咒骂了一句。

文学作品中的用法示例：
Shakespeare frequently used “accurst” in his plays. 例如，在《麦克白》中，女巫们被称为“accursed”：
"The weird sisters, hand in hand, / Posters of the sea and land, / Thus do go to cursed hell."
