godfather:/ˈɡɒdfɑːðə(r)/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːdfɑːðər/ (US)| n. |教父; 干爹|A man who presents a child at baptism and promises to take responsibility for their religious education; a male sponsor or protector. Also, (especially in the Mafia) a powerful friend or mentor, especially one in a position of authority.|He asked his closest friend to be his son's godfather. 他请求他最亲密的朋友做他儿子的教父。

He was known as the godfather of soul music. 他被称为灵魂音乐教父。|近义词：sponsor, protector, mentor, patron|反义词: N/A|词性:Noun (singular)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *godfæder (attested as godfædera), from god "god" + fæder "father."  Cognate with Dutch godfather, German Gottvater.  The extended meaning of "a powerful friend or mentor" arose in the 20th century, popularized by Mario Puzo's novel "The Godfather."
