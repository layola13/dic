overabstracting:/ˌoʊvəræbˈstræktɪŋ/
|v.|过度抽象化|The process of abstracting something too much, removing too many specific details and making it difficult to understand or apply in a practical context.|He was overabstracting the issue, making it seem more complicated than it really was. 他把这个问题过度抽象化了，使它看起来比实际情况更复杂。|overgeneralizing, oversimplifying|contextualizing, specifying|verb
overabstract:/ˌoʊvəræbˈstrækt/
|adj.|过度抽象的|Excessively abstract; lacking in concrete details or practical application.|His overabstract theories were difficult for the students to grasp. 他过于抽象的理论让学生们难以理解。|overly theoretical, too general|concrete, specific|adjective
overabstraction:/ˌoʊvəræbˈstrækʃən/
|n.|过度抽象|The act or process of overabstracting; an excessively abstract concept or idea.|The overabstraction in his argument made it lose its connection to reality.  他论证中的过度抽象使其失去了与现实的联系。|overgeneralization, oversimplification|concretization, specification|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
over- (prefix): from Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi, meaning "above, over, beyond."
abstract (verb): from Late Latin abstractus, past participle of abstrahere "to draw away," from Latin abs- "away" + trahere "to draw."
-ing (suffix): forms the present participle and gerund of verbs.
