equivocatory:/ɪˈkwɪvəkətɔːri/
|adj.|模棱两可的，含糊其辞的，故意含糊的|Using equivocatory language, the politician avoided giving a direct answer to the journalist's question.|The lawyer's equivocatory response left the jury confused. |近义词:ambiguous, evasive, misleading, deceptive|反义词:clear, straightforward, direct, unambiguous|词性:形容词

equivocate:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪt/
|v.|含糊其辞，支吾其词|She equivocated when asked about her involvement in the scandal.|He tends to equivocate rather than give a straight answer. |近义词:prevaricate, hedge, fudge|反义词:be forthright, be direct|词性:动词

equivocation:/ɪˌkwɪvəˈkeɪʃn/
|n.|含糊其辞，模棱两可的话|Her testimony was filled with equivocations and half-truths.|The contract was voided due to the equivocation in its wording. |近义词:ambiguity, evasion, prevarication|反义词:clarity, directness|词性:名词
