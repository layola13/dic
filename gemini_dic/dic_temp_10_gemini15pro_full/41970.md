cosset:/ˈkɒsɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːsɪt/ (US)| n. vt.|娇惯的人；宠儿|A person or animal that is pampered or treated with excessive indulgence.|She was treated like a cosset, never having to lift a finger.|近义词：pamper, indulge, spoil, coddle|反义词:neglect, ignore|词性:noun, verb

cosset:/ˈkɒsɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːsɪt/ (US)| vt.|宠爱；娇惯|To pamper or treat with excessive indulgence.|The parents cosseted their only child.|近义词：pamper, indulge, spoil, coddle|反义词:neglect, ignore|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cosset, probably from Old Norse *kot-sæti (“cot-sitter”), which refers to a lamb brought up in the house.
