neuroticism:/njʊˈrɒtɪsɪzəm/ /nʊˈrɑːtɪsɪzəm/| n.|神经质，神经过敏|A personality trait characterized by a tendency to experience negative emotions, such as anxiety, anger, and depression.  It also refers to emotional instability and a tendency to overreact to stimuli.|She displayed high levels of neuroticism, worrying constantly about minor things.|近义词：anxiety, instability, negativity, emotional lability|反义词：stability, emotional stability, resilience|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From neuro- +‎ -ticism.  "Neuro-" relates to nerves or the nervous system,  and "-ticism" is a suffix forming nouns meaning 'state or condition of being'.
