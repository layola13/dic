amphisbaenian:/ˌæmfɪsˈbiːniən/ | n. |蚓蜥 |A limbless, burrowing lizard of the order Amphisbaenia. |Example 1: The amphisbaenian uses its head to burrow through the earth. 蚓蜥用它的头在地下挖洞。

Example 2:  Amphisbaenians are often mistaken for snakes or worms. 蚓蜥经常被误认为是蛇或蠕虫。 |Synonym: worm lizard| Antonym: (No direct antonym, could consider limbed lizard) |noun


Etymology: From Greek 'amphisbaena' (walking both ways), from 'amphi-' (both) + 'bainein' (to go).
