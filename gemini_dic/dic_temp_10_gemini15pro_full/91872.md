hyperinsulinaemia: /ˌhaɪpərɪnsjʊlɪˈniːmiə/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpərˌɪnsəˈliːniə/ (US)| n. |高胰岛素血症|A condition in which there are excess levels of insulin circulating in the blood relative to the level of glucose.  This can be a consequence of insulin resistance or a tumor of the pancreas.|Example:  Hyperinsulinaemia can be a precursor to type 2 diabetes. 高胰岛素血症可能是2型糖尿病的前兆。|Example: The doctor suspected hyperinsulinaemia based on the patient's symptoms and lab results. 医生根据病人的症状和实验室结果怀疑是高胰岛素血症。|近义词: hyperinsulinism|反义词: hypoinsulinemia|词性: noun


Etymology:
hyper- (Greek):  prefix meaning "over, above, excessive"
insulin (Latin, insula, "island"):  hormone produced in the islets of Langerhans in the pancreas
-aemia (Greek, haima, "blood"):  suffix denoting a condition of the blood
