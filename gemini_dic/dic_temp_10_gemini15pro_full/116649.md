matter-of-factnesses: /ˌmætər əv ˈfæktnəsəz/ | n. | 实事求是；平淡无奇；就事论事 |  The plural form of matter-of-factness, referring to instances or qualities of being straightforward and unemotional, sticking to the facts.  |  Her matter-of-factnesses about the accident were unsettling. 她对事故的实事求是令人不安。|  He recounted the story with a series of matter-of-factnesses that made it seem even more unbelievable. 他平淡无奇地讲述了这个故事，反而让它显得更加难以置信。|Synonyms: practicality, pragmatism, realism,  | Antonyms: emotionalism, sentimentality, subjectivity | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "matter-of-fact" + "-ness".  "Matter-of-fact" originated in the late 16th century, meaning "concerned with fact." It combines "matter" (meaning "subject, topic") and "fact."  The suffix "-ness" transforms adjectives into nouns, indicating a state or quality.
