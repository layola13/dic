mordanting:/ˈmɔːrdəntɪŋ/
noun
媒染; 媒染法
The process of treating a textile material with a mordant to fix dyes.
The mordanting process helps the dye adhere to the fabric. 媒染过程有助于染料附着在织物上。

mordanting:/ˈmɔːrdəntɪŋ/
adjective
媒染的
Treated or used as a mordant.
Mordanting agents are crucial for natural dyeing. 媒染剂对于天然染色至关重要。

mordant:/ˈmɔːrdənt/
noun
媒染剂
A substance, typically an inorganic oxide, that combines with a dye or stain and thereby fixes it in a material.
Alum is a common mordant used in dyeing. 明矾是染色中常用的媒染剂。


Etymology: From Old French *mordant, present participle of mordre "to bite," from Latin mordēre "to bite."  The mordant "bites" into the fiber, allowing the dye to bind.

Synonyms: fixing, setting (for the process)
Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for the process.  For the noun "mordant", one could consider "release agent" in specific contexts, though this doesn't perfectly capture the opposite meaning.)
