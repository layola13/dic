gone:/ɡɒn/ (UK) /ɡɔːn/ (US)| adj.| 离开的，过去的，用完的，死了的 |  No longer present; departed. Also used to indicate something that has been used up or is no longer available. Can also mean dead. | The milk is all gone. 牛奶都喝完了。| Synonyms: departed, absent, used up, finished, deceased | Antonyms: present, here, remaining, extant, alive | word type: adjective

gone:/ɡɒn/ (UK) /ɡɔːn/ (US)| past participle of "go"| 去了，走了 | Past participle of the verb "go."  Indicates completion of the action of going. | He's gone to the store. 他去商店了。| Synonyms: departed, left | Antonyms: arrived, returned | word type: past participle

The money is gone. 钱用完了。

Present Continuous:

My patience is going. 我的耐心快要耗尽了.


Present Perfect:
The guests have gone home. 客人们都回家了。
He has gone to the market. 他去市场了。


Present Perfect Continuous:
(Generally not used with "gone")


Simple Past:
He went to the store, and when he returned, the cake was gone. 他去了商店，当他回来时，蛋糕不见了。
She went to bed early last night. 她昨晚很早就睡了。 (While this uses "went," it shows the relation to "gone")


Past Continuous:
The snow was going fast when the sun came out. 太阳出来时，雪融化得很快。(Again, related but uses "going")
He was going to the store when he got the call. 他正要去商店时接到了电话。(Related but uses "going")


Past Perfect:
The cake had been gone for hours before anyone noticed. 蛋糕不见了几个小时才有人注意到。
He had gone to school before she woke up. 他在她醒来之前就去上学了。


Past Perfect Continuous:
(Generally not used with "gone")


Simple Future:
By next week, the snow will be gone. 到下周，雪就会融化了。
He will be gone by the time you arrive. 你到达时他将已经离开。


Future Continuous:
He will be going to the store later. 他晚些时候会去商店。(Related but uses "going")
She will be going home after work. 她下班后会回家。(Related but uses "going")


Future Perfect:
He will have gone by the time you get there. 你到那里的时候他将已经走了。
The food will have been gone long before the party ends.  食物会在派对结束前很久就吃完了。


Future Perfect Continuous:
(Generally not used with "gone")

Subjunctive Mood:
If he were gone, I would be sad. 如果他走了，我会很伤心的。
I wish he were gone. 我希望他走了。

Passive Voice:
(Not applicable, as "gone" cannot be used in the passive voice.)

Interrogative sentences:
Is the food gone? 食物吃完了吗？
Are they gone yet? 他们走了吗？

Negative sentences:
The food isn't gone yet. 食物还没吃完。
They aren't gone yet. 他们还没走。
