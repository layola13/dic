oxidizing:/ˈɒksɪdaɪzɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈɑːksɪdaɪzɪŋ/ (US)| adj.|氧化的；氧化性的|Causing oxidation; combining with oxygen.|例句：Rust is formed by the oxidizing process of iron. 铁锈是由铁的氧化过程形成的。

例句：The oxidizing agent accepts electrons in a chemical reaction. 氧化剂在化学反应中接受电子。|近义词：corrosive, rusting|反义词：reducing|词性：形容词

oxidize:/ˈɒksɪdaɪz/ (UK) /ˈɑːksɪdaɪz/ (US)| v.|氧化；生锈|To combine or become combined chemically with oxygen.|例句：Iron oxidizes in the presence of air and moisture. 铁在空气和水分存在的情况下会氧化。

例句：The apple quickly oxidized after being cut. 苹果切开后迅速氧化。|近义词：corrode, rust|反义词：reduce|词性：动词

oxidation:/ˌɒksɪˈdeɪʃən/ (UK) /ˌɑːksɪˈdeɪʃən/ (US)| n.|氧化；氧化作用|The process or result of oxidizing or being oxidized.|例句：The oxidation of iron produces rust. 铁的氧化会产生铁锈。

例句：The slow oxidation of wine improves its flavor. 葡萄酒的缓慢氧化可以改善其风味。|近义词：corrosion, rusting|反义词：reduction|词性：名词
