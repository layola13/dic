Karakoram:/ˌkærəˈkɔːrəm/ | n. | 喀喇昆仑山脉 | A large mountain range spanning the borders of Pakistan, India, and China, and containing K2, the second highest peak in the world. |  The Karakoram Highway connects Pakistan and China through the Karakoram mountains. 喀喇昆仑公路穿过喀喇昆仑山脉连接巴基斯坦和中国。| Synonym: mountain range | Antonym: plain, valley | Noun


Etymology:
From Turkic *qara qorum "black gravel," referring to the dark scree slopes of the range.
