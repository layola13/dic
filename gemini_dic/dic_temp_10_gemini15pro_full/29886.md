caustic:/ˈkɔːstik/ (US) /ˈkɔːstɪk/ (UK)| adj. n.| 腐蚀性的；苛性的；刻薄的；焦性的 | (adj.) Able to burn or corrode organic tissue by chemical action;  bitterly sarcastic. (n.) A caustic substance.|

例句：
The cleaner contains a caustic substance.
这种清洁剂含有腐蚀性物质。

The critic's caustic remarks offended the artist.
评论家刻薄的言辞冒犯了艺术家。


近义词：(adj.) corrosive, acrid, biting, mordant, sarcastic, scathing |(n.) alkali, base
反义词：(adj.) mild, gentle, kind |(n.) acid
词性：adjective, noun

caustics:/ˈkɔːstɪks/| n.| 腐蚀剂；苛性碱；焦散线| The plural form of caustic, referring to caustic substances or, in optics, to the curves or surfaces formed by the focusing of light rays reflected or refracted by a curved surface. |

例句：
Strong caustics can cause severe burns.
强腐蚀剂会导致严重的灼伤。

The artist used caustics in his photography to create interesting effects.
这位艺术家在他的摄影作品中使用了焦散线来创造有趣的效果。

近义词：corrosives, alkalis
反义词:  (For the chemical meaning) neutralizers
词性: noun


词源: From Late Latin *causticus, from Greek kaustikos "burning," from kaiein "to burn".
