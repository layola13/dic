counterguard:/ˈkaʊntərɡɑːrd/
n.|反卫；外护墙；对开的防卫物|A defensive structure or force positioned to protect against attack from a specific direction, especially one designed to counter an enemy's flanking maneuver.|The general positioned a counterguard on the hill to protect against a flanking attack.|近义词:bulwark, rampart, defense, protection|反义词:vulnerability, weakness|词性:名词

Etymology:
From counter- + guard.  "Counter-" acts as a prefix indicating opposition or reciprocal action. "Guard" originates from the Old North French word "garder" meaning "to protect" or "to keep."
