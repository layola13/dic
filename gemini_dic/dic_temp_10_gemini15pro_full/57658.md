drawbore: /ˈdrɔːbɔːr/
noun
榫钉孔；拉穿榫眼
A hole drilled through a tenon and its corresponding mortise, offset so that a pin driven through the holes will draw the joint tight.
The carpenter used a drawbore to secure the joint tightly.  木匠用榫钉孔将接头牢牢固定。

verb
用榫钉固定
To fasten with a drawbore.
He drawbored the two pieces of wood together. 他用榫钉将两块木头固定在一起。

Etymology:  From 'draw' (pull) + 'bore' (hole).  The term refers to the action of the pin drawing the joint tight.

Synonyms:  dowel joint (though not exactly the same), pinned joint
Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "loose joint" in a practical sense)
Word Type:  Both noun and verb
