diachroneity:/ˌdaɪəkrəˈnɪsɪti/ | n. |历时性| The study of language development through time; historical linguistics.  The change in language over time.|  Example: The diachroneity of English pronunciation is evident in the Great Vowel Shift.| Synonym: historical development, evolution, temporal change | Antonym: synchroneity | noun


diachronic:/ˌdaɪəˈkrɒnɪk/ | adj. |历时的|  Concerned with the way in which something, especially language, has developed and changed through time.| Example: A diachronic study of the word "nice" reveals its surprising origins. | Synonym: historical, evolutionary | Antonym: synchronic | adjective
