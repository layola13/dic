brain-washes:/ˈbreɪnˌwɒʃɪz/ (UK) /ˈbreɪnˌwɑːʃɪz/ (US)| v. | 洗脑 |  pressurizes (someone) into adopting radically different beliefs by using systematic and often forcible means. | The cult leader brain-washes his followers into believing he is a god. | 近义词: indoctrinates, propagandizes, conditions | 反义词: educates, enlightens | 词性: 动词

brain-washing:/ˈbreɪnˌwɒʃɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈbreɪnˌwɑːʃɪŋ/ (US)| n. | 洗脑 | The process of pressurizing someone into adopting radically different beliefs by using systematic and often forcible means. | The government's use of propaganda was a form of brain-washing. | 近义词: indoctrination, propaganda | 反义词: education, enlightenment | 词性: 名词

词源:  "Brainwashing" is a calque of the Chinese term 洗腦 (xǐnǎo), which literally translates to "wash brain".  It gained popularity during the Korean War when it was used to describe the methods used by the Chinese to indoctrinate American prisoners of war.
