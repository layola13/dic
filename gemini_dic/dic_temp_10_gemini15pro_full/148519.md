oversuperstitiously: /ˌoʊvərˌsuːpərˈstɪʃəsli/
| adv. | 过于迷信地 | Excessively or unduly superstitious; in a way that is marked by an extreme belief in superstitions. |  He oversuperstitiously avoided walking under ladders, convinced it would bring bad luck. 他过于迷信地避免走在梯子下面，确信这会带来厄运。 | Synonyms: excessively superstitious, unduly superstitious | Antonyms: rationally, skeptically | 词性: 副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- (prefix meaning "excessively") + superstitious (adjective) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  Superstitious comes from Latin superstitio, meaning "excessive fear of the gods; religious awe."
