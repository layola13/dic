ingraft:/ɪnˈɡræft/ /ɪnˈɡrɑːft/
verb
嫁接；灌输；移植
to insert or fix (a shoot, bud, etc) as a graft
例句：
The gardener ingrafted a new branch onto the old apple tree. 园丁把一根新枝嫁接到老苹果树上。

The teacher tried to ingraft good manners into her students. 老师试图将良好的举止灌输给她的学生。

近义词：graft, implant, instill, imbue
反义词：excise, remove, uproot
词性:v.


ingraft:/ˈɪnɡræft/ /ˈɪnɡrɑːft/
noun
嫁接；移植
the act or process of ingrafting
例句：
The ingraft of the new tissue was successful. 新组织的移植很成功。

The ingraft of new ideas into the company culture is essential for growth. 将新思想融入公司文化对发展至关重要。

近义词：graft, implantation, transplant
反义词：removal, excision
词性:n.
