distraught:/dɪˈstrɔːt/ | adj. | 心烦意乱的，发狂的 | Extremely worried, upset, and confused. | She was distraught after hearing the news of her husband's accident. 她听到丈夫出事的消息后心烦意乱。|
近义词: agitated, frantic, distressed, upset | 反义词: calm, composed, serene | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *distraught*, past participle of *distraughte*, *distracte*, from Old French *distract*, past participle of *distraire*, from Latin *distractus*, past participle of *distrahere* ("to pull asunder, divide, separate, distract"), from *dis-* ("apart, asunder") + *trahere* ("to draw, pull").
