bromine:/ˈbroʊmiːn/ (US) /ˈbrəʊmiːn/ (UK)| n.|溴|A chemical element, a nonmetallic liquid halogen element, that is a deep reddish-brown, fuming liquid with a suffocating odor.|Bromine is used in many areas, such as agricultural chemicals, dyestuffs, insecticides, pharmaceuticals and chemical intermediates.|Bromine is a highly reactive element.|Synonym: halogen|Antonym: None (Elements don't have direct antonyms)|noun
