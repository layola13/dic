mesmerizingly:/ˈmezməˌraɪzɪŋli/| adv.| 迷人地；令人着迷地|in a way that completely holds your attention|Her performance was mesmerizingly beautiful. 她的表演美得令人着迷。|Synonyms: captivatingly, enchantingly, fascinatingly, hypnotically|Antonyms: boringly, dully, tediously|Word type: Adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From mesmerize + -ing + -ly.  "Mesmerize" comes from Franz Anton Mesmer (1734-1815), an Austrian physician who developed a theory of animal magnetism, which he called "mesmerism."  The theory was later discredited, but the word "mesmerize" remained, meaning to hypnotize or fascinate.
