cognitivity:/ˌkɒɡnɪˈtɪvɪti/ | n. | 认知能力，认知度 | The state or quality of being cognitive; mental processes involved in knowing, learning, and understanding. |  His cognitivity declined sharply after the accident.  他的认知能力在事故后急剧下降。|  The drug improved the patient's cognitivity.  这种药物提高了病人的认知能力。| 近义词：cognition, understanding, comprehension, awareness | 反义词：incomprehension, unawareness | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Late Latin *cognitivus (from Latin cognitus, past participle of cognoscere “to learn, to get to know”), equivalent to cognit(us) + -ive.  来自于晚期拉丁语 *cognitivus（源自拉丁语 cognitus，cognoscere 的过去分词，意为“学习，了解”），相当于 cognit(us) + -ive。
