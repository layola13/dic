indiscretion:/ˌɪndɪˈskreʃn/
n.
轻率；不谨慎；言行失检
Lack of discretion; imprudence.
He committed an indiscretion that cost him his job. 他犯了一个轻率的错误，让他丢了工作。

indiscretion:/ˌɪndɪˈskreʃn/
n.
不慎重的言行；失言；失态
An indiscreet act or remark.
The governor made an indiscretion about his opponent's personal life. 州长对对手的私生活发表了不慎重的言论。


Synonyms: imprudence, folly, recklessness, carelessness, lapse, blunder, gaffe, slip, error, mistake, transgression, offense
Antonyms: prudence, caution, discretion, circumspection, wisdom, foresight

Etymology:  From Late Latin indiscretio, from Latin indiscretus "undistinguishing," from in- "not" + discretus "separated, distinguished" (see discreet).
