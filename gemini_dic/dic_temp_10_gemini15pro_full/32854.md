cholesterins:/kəˈlɛstərɪnz/ | n. | 胆固醇 | A plural form of cholesterol, a waxy, fat-like substance that's found in all the cells in your body.  While technically a plural, it's often used interchangeably with the singular "cholesterol" in informal contexts. |  High levels of cholesterins can lead to heart disease. 高胆固醇会导致心脏病。| synonym: cholesterol (generally preferred) | antonym:  (No direct antonym for a substance.  One might consider medications that *lower* cholesterol as functional antonyms.) | noun
