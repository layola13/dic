civilised:/ˈsɪvəlaɪzd/ (US) /ˈsɪvɪlaɪzd/ (UK)| adj.|文明的，有教养的|Having a high state of social and cultural development; polite and well-mannered.|例句：They considered themselves more civilised than the barbarians. 他们认为自己比野蛮人更文明。|近义词：cultured, refined, sophisticated|反义词：barbaric, uncivilised, savage|词性:形容词
civilised:/ˈsɪvəlaɪzd/ (US) /ˈsɪvɪlaɪzd/ (UK)| v. |使文明化，教化|To bring (a person or people) to a more highly developed state of social organization; to refine or polish (someone's manners).|例句：The Romans civilised many parts of Europe. 罗马人使欧洲的许多地区文明化。|近义词：educate, enlighten, refine|反义词：barbarise|词性:动词
