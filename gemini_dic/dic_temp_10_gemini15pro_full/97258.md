inflicters:/ɪnˈflɪktərz/| n. |施加者；使遭受…的人|People who inflict something (usually unpleasant) on others.  Those who cause something to be endured.|The inflicters of the punishment showed no mercy.  这些施刑者没有表现出任何怜悯。|Synonyms: perpetrators, causers, agents|Antonyms: sufferers, victims|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "inflict," which comes from the Latin "infligere" (to strike, dash against), formed from "in-" (in, on, against) + "fligere" (to strike).  The "-er" suffix denotes the agent or one who performs the action.
