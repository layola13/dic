chadar's:/ˈtʃɑːdɑːrz/| n.|查达尔(复数形式), 盖在头上和肩膀上的大围巾(尤指穆斯林和印度妇女)|A chadar is a large piece of cloth traditionally worn by women in South Asia, particularly in India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh, as a headcovering and shawl. The word "chadar's" implies multiple chadars.|She brought several chadars back from her trip to India. 她从印度旅行回来带了几条查达尔。|Synonyms: shawl, veil, dupatta|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "unveiled attire")|noun
