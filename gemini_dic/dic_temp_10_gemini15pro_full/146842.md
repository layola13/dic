overindex:/ˌōvərˈɪndeks/| v.|过度索引，过分重视；|To overindex on something means to give it more importance or emphasis than it deserves, often in comparison to other factors. This can lead to skewed results or decisions.|The company overindexed on social media marketing, neglecting other important channels.|近义词：overemphasize, overvalue, prioritize excessively|反义词：underemphasize, undervalue, neglect|词性:动词

Etymology: "Over-" (prefix indicating excess) + "index" (from Latin "indicare," meaning "to point out").  The term gained popularity in the context of market research and investment.
