ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *o*, from Old English *ō* (also *ā*), from Proto-Germanic *ō*/*ā*, from Proto-Indo-European *h₁oḱḱō* (“eye, to see”). Cognate with Scots o, West Frisian oo (“eye”), Dutch oo (“eye”), German Auge (“eye”), Icelandic auga (“eye”), Latin oculus (“eye”), Ancient Greek ὄσσος (óssos, “eye”), Sanskrit अक्षि (ákṣi, “eye”).
