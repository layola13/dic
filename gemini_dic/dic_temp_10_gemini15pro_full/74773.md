freeze:/friːz/ | v. | 冻结，凝固；感到寒冷；惊呆；停止，僵住 | to become so cold that a liquid changes into a solid; to preserve food by freezing; to stop moving suddenly; to become very cold |  The lake froze over in winter.  湖在冬天结冰了。 |  solidify, congeal, chill, halt | thaw, melt, move | verb

freeze:/friːz/ | n. | 冻结；冰冻期；停止；冻结状态 | a period of very cold weather; the act of freezing something | There's a freeze on hiring at the moment. 目前招聘冻结了。 | halt, standstill | thaw | noun

PHRASE->
freeze over:  结冰  The pond froze over last night. 池塘昨晚结冰了。
freeze up:  (因寒冷) 冻住；(因紧张) 变得僵硬 He froze up when he saw the police. 他看到警察就僵住了。
freeze someone out:  排挤某人  They tried to freeze her out of the conversation. 他们试图把她排除在谈话之外。
freeze frame:  定格画面  The director used a freeze frame to emphasize the actor's expression. 导演用定格画面来强调演员的表情。
deep freeze:  深度冷冻  The meat was kept in a deep freeze. 肉被保存在深度冷冻库里。
