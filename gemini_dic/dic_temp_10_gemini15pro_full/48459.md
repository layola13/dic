deemer:/ˈdiːmər/| n.|裁定者，认为者，判断者|Someone who judges or regards something in a particular way.  It often appears in legal contexts referring to someone who makes a judgment or determination.|The judge, acting as the deemer in this case, ruled against the defendant.|近义词: judge, arbiter, considerer, estimator|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


Etymology:
From the verb "deem,"  Middle English demen, from Old English dēman "to judge, pronounce a judgment, decree, doom; consider, think; suppose, imagine," from Proto-Germanic *domjan "to form a judgment," from *domoz "judgment."
