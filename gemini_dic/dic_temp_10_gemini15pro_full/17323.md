be-big-on:/biː bɪɡ ɒn/ | adj. phrasal verb | 热衷于，非常喜欢 | To be very enthusiastic about or interested in something. |  例句：She's be-big-on  yoga recently. 她最近非常喜欢瑜伽。| 近义词：keen on, fond of, enthusiastic about | 反义词：dislike, hate | 词性: phrasal verb


Etymology:
"Be" comes from Old English "bēon," meaning "to be, exist, happen."
"Big" comes from Middle English "bigge," of uncertain origin.
"On" comes from Old English "on," meaning "on, in, into, at, toward, among."  The phrasal verb combines these words to convey a strong sense of enthusiasm or interest.
