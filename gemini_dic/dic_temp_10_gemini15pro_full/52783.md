dilettante:/ˌdɪləˈtænti/ /ˌdɪləˈtɑːnt/ | n. adj. | 业余爱好者；浅尝辄止的人 | A person who cultivates an area of interest, such as the arts, without real commitment or knowledge. |  He's a bit of a dilettante as far as wine is concerned – he knows the labels but not the grapes. 就葡萄酒而言，他有点像个门外汉——他知道酒的牌子，但不知道葡萄的种类。|  She dabbles in painting, but she's really just a dilettante. 她涉猎绘画，但她实际上只是一个业余爱好者。| 近义词:amateur, dabbler, superficial, nonprofessional | 反义词:professional, expert, connoisseur | 词性:名词，形容词

dilettante:/ˌdɪləˈtænti/ /ˌdɪləˈtɑːnt/ | adj. | 业余的；浅尝辄止的 | Showing superficial interest, lacking in depth of knowledge. | His dilettante approach to the subject annoyed the professor. 他对这门学科浅尝辄止的态度惹恼了教授。|  He gave a dilettante performance, clearly not having practiced enough. 他的表演很业余，显然没有充分练习。| 近义词:amateurish, superficial | 反义词:professional, expert, masterful | 词性:形容词
