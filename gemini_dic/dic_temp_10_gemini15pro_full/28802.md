carom:/ˈkærəm/ (US), /ˈkærəm/ (UK)| vi. vt.| 撞击；碰开；（台球）连击；|To carom means to hit something and then bounce off it at an angle, often hitting something else.  撞击的意思是撞到某个东西，然后以一个角度反弹，通常会撞到其他东西。| The car swerved to avoid the deer and caromed off a tree. 汽车为了避开鹿而转向，撞到了一棵树上。| synonyms: rebound, glance, ricochet | antonyms: stick, adhere | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *carommer*, from Spanish *carambola*, from Portuguese *carambola*, ultimately from Sanskrit *karmaphala* (“fruit of action”).
