come your way:/kʌm jʊr weɪ/ | idiom |  降临到你身上；来到你面前；被你得到 | Something that comes your way happens to you or becomes available to you. |  A lot of opportunities have come my way recently. 最近我遇到了很多机会。|  Good fortune will come your way if you persevere. 如果你坚持不懈，好运终会降临到你身上。| 近义词: happen to you, come to you, fall into your lap | 反义词: avoid, miss out on | phrase

come:/kʌm/ | v. | 来；来到；出现；发生 | To move or travel toward the speaker or the place where the speaker is. | He came to my house yesterday. 他昨天来我家了。 |  She will come to the party tonight. 她今晚会来参加派对。 | 近义词: arrive, approach | 反义词: go, leave | verb

your:/jʊr/ | pron. | 你的；你们的 |  Belonging to or associated with the person or people that the speaker is addressing. | This is your book. 这是你的书。 | Is this your car? 这是你的车吗？ | 近义词: (none - possessive pronoun) | 反义词: (none - possessive pronoun) | pronoun

way:/weɪ/ | n. | 路；方法；方式 | A route, direction, or path. A method, style, or manner of doing something. |  I don't know the way to the station. 我不知道去车站的路。 | There are many ways to solve this problem. 解决这个问题有很多方法。 | 近义词: road, path, method, manner | 反义词: (none - depends on context) | noun
