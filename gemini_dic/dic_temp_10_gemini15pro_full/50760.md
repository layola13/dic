dervish:/ˈdɜːrvɪʃ/| n.|托钵僧，苦行僧|A member of a Muslim (especially Sufi) religious order, known for their ascetic practices and sometimes for performing whirling dances as a form of religious ecstasy.|The dervish whirled around and around, chanting rhythmically.|近义词:ascetic, monk, fakir|反义词:materialist, hedonist|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Turkish *derviş*, from Persian *darvīsh* "beggar, mendicant", from *dar* "door" + *vīsh* "face, front".  The implication is one who begs door to door.
