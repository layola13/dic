herdsman:/ˈhɜːrdzmən/ | n. | 牧人；牧牛人 | A person, especially a man, who takes care of a herd of grazing animals. | The herdsman led his cattle to the high pastures for the summer. 牧人夏天把牛赶到高处的牧场。 |  synonyms: shepherd, cowherd, stockman, drover | antonyms: farmer (a broader term, not necessarily focused on grazing animals) | noun

herdswoman:/ˈhɜːrdzwʊmən/ | n. | 女牧人；女牧牛人 | A woman who takes care of a herd of grazing animals. | The herdswoman skillfully guided the sheep through the narrow mountain pass. 女牧人熟练地引导羊群穿过狭窄的山路。 | synonyms: shepherdess, cowgirl (in some contexts), stockwoman | antonyms: farmer (a broader term, not necessarily focused on grazing animals) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hierde + mann (herd + man).  The word "herd" itself comes from Proto-Germanic *herdo, meaning "herd, flock."
