fretter:/ˈfrɛtər/| v. n. |磨损；焦躁；烦躁；金属条|To wear away or corrode by rubbing or gnawing. A person who worries or frets constantly. A bar of metal, especially one across the fingerboard of a stringed instrument. |例句1：The constant dripping of water fretted a hole in the stone.  不断滴落的水把石头磨出了一个洞。

例句2：He fretted about the upcoming exam. 他对即将到来的考试感到焦虑。|近义词：worry, chafe, erode, gnaw|反义词：soothe, calm, comfort|词性：动词, 名词


fretter:/ˈfrɛtər/| n. | 焦躁不安的人 |Someone who habitually worries or frets. |例句1：She's such a fretter; she worries about everything. 她真是个爱操心的人；她担心一切。

例句2：Don't be such a fretter, everything will be alright.  别这么焦虑，一切都会好起来的。|近义词：worrier, fusser|反义词： optimist, carefree person|词性：名词



ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fretan "devour, consume," from Proto-Germanic *fratanan "gnaw, eat," from Proto-Indo-European *gʷrē- "to rub, grind." Related to Old High German frezzan "to eat greedily," Old Norse freta "to devour." The meaning "to worry" developed from the sense of "gnawing anxiety."
