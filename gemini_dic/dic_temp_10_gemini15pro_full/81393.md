grass-widowhood: /ɡræsˈwɪdəʊhʊd/ (British) /ɡræsˈwɪdoʊhʊd/ (American) | n. | 草寡妇身份，丈夫长期不在身边的妻子 | The state of a woman whose husband is away for a prolonged period, often for work or other commitments. | She experienced a period of grass-widowhood while her husband was working overseas. 她丈夫在海外工作期间，她经历了一段草寡妇生活。| 近义词: separation, absence | 反义词: togetherness, cohabitation | 词性: noun

Etymology:
"grass" (Old English græs, gærs) + "widow" (Old English widewe) + "-hood" (Old English -hād, denoting state or condition).  The term likely arose from the notion that a wife whose husband was frequently absent was free to "graze" in new pastures, like a widow.
