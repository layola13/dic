interdiction:/ˌɪntərˈdɪkʃn/| n.|禁止，阻止，阻断|The act of prohibiting or forbidding something, especially by law or official decree; the action of intercepting and preventing the movement of a prohibited commodity or person.|The government imposed an interdiction on the sale of firearms. 政府禁止出售枪支。|近义词：prohibition, ban, embargo, blockage|反义词:allowance, permission|noun

interdict:/ˌɪntərˈdɪkt/| vt.|禁止，阻止，阻断|To prohibit or forbid something, especially by law or official decree; to intercept and prevent the movement of a prohibited commodity or person.|The court interdicted the company from selling the product. 法院禁止该公司销售该产品。|近义词：prohibit, forbid, ban, bar|反义词:allow, permit|verb
