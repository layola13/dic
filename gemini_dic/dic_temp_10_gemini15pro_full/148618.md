overtheorization: /ˌoʊvərˌθiːəraɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 过度理论化; 过于理论化 | The act of developing theories that are excessively complex or abstract, often lacking practical application or empirical support.  It implies an imbalance between theory and evidence. |  His overtheorization of the problem made it seem more complicated than it actually was. 他对这个问题的过度理论化使它看起来比实际情况更复杂。| synonyms: over-intellectualization, abstractionism, speculation,  | antonyms: pragmatism, empiricism, practicality | noun

overtheorize: /ˌoʊvərˈθiːəraɪz/ | v. | 过度理论化; 过于理论化 | To develop theories that are excessively complex or abstract, often lacking practical application or empirical support. | Some critics say that economists overtheorize and fail to account for real-world complexities. 一些评论家说，经济学家过度理论化，未能考虑到现实世界的复杂性。 | synonyms: over-intellectualize, speculate, abstract | antonyms:  practice, apply, implement | verb
