cloying:/ˈklɔɪɪŋ/ | adj. |甜得发腻的；令人腻烦的 | Excessively sweet, rich, or sentimental, to the point of causing disgust.  | The dessert was so cloying that I couldn't finish it. 这甜点太腻了，我吃不完。| 近义词：sickly, saccharine, sugary, oversweet | 反义词：tart, tangy, refreshing | 词性: 形容词

cloying:/ˈklɔɪɪŋ/ | adj. |令人腻烦的 | Excessively sentimental or flattering to the point of being annoying. |  His cloying compliments made her uncomfortable. 他那令人腻烦的恭维让她感到不舒服。| 近义词：fulsome, excessive, insincere | 反义词：genuine, sincere, understated | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Middle English *cloien, from Anglo-Norman cluer (“to nail, to stick, to stop up, to clog”), from Latin claudere (“to close, to shut”).  More at close.  Originally meaning "to clog," sense development was via "to satiate" to "to cause disgust by excess of sweetness, richness, etc."
