druse:/druːs/
n.
晶簇；晶族
A group of crystals.
The geologist found a beautiful druse of quartz crystals in the cave.  地质学家在山洞里发现了一簇美丽的石英晶体。
The druse of amethyst sparkled in the light. 紫水晶晶簇在灯光下闪闪发光。
cluster, aggregation
individual crystal

n.
（医学）玻璃疣，玻璃体疣
(medicine) A small, hard, granular deposit, often of uric acid crystals, on the inner lining of the eyelid.
The ophthalmologist diagnosed the patient with a druse on her eyelid. 眼科医生诊断病人眼睑上有一个玻璃疣。
He had a druse removed from his eyelid. 他眼睑上的一个玻璃疣被切除了。
deposit, concretion

Etymology: From German Druse, from Middle High German drūse ‘gland, geode,’ of uncertain origin.
