impecuniousnesses:/ɪmˌpɛkjuːˈniəsnəsɪz/
|n.|身无分文；贫困；穷困潦倒|The state of being impecunious; poverty.|His impecuniousnesses forced him to sell his family heirlooms.|Synonyms: poverty, indigence, penury, destitution|Antonyms: wealth, affluence, prosperity|Noun

Etymology:
From impecunious + -ness.  Impecunious comes from Late Latin impecuniosus "without money," from in- "not, opposite of" + pecuniosus "rich, wealthy," from pecunia "money, property, wealth."  Ultimately from Latin pecu "cattle, flock," from Proto-Italic *pekū "cattle," from Proto-Indo-European *peḱu- "cattle, livestock."
