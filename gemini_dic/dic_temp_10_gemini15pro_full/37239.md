comfortful:/ˈkʌmfərtfəl/| adj.| 令人感到安慰的，舒适的| While not strictly standard English, "comfortful" is occasionally used to describe something that provides comfort. It's formed by adding the suffix "-ful" to "comfort," similar to how "joyful" is formed from "joy." | The soft blanket was comfortful on a cold night.  柔软的毯子在寒冷的夜晚令人感到舒适。| Near synonyms: comforting, cozy, soothing | Near antonyms: uncomfortable, distressing | adjective

comfortful:/ˈkʌmfərtfəl/| adj.| 感到舒适的|Although less common and sometimes considered non-standard, it can also describe someone *feeling* comfort. | She felt comfortful in her grandmother's arms.  她在祖母的怀里感到舒适。|Near synonyms: comfortable, relaxed, at ease|Near antonyms: uncomfortable, uneasy, restless| adjective
