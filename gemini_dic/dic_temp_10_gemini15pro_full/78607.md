gibbets:/ˈdʒɪbɪts/| n.|绞刑架; 耻辱柱|A structure for hanging criminals, typically a wooden frame from which a noose is suspended.  It also refers to a thing that causes public disgrace.|The bodies of the executed pirates were left hanging from the gibbets as a warning to others.|Synonyms: gallows, scaffold|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, consider "memorial," "honor," "celebration")|Noun


gibbet:/ˈdʒɪbɪt/| vt.|吊死; 公开谴责|To hang someone on a gibbet; to expose someone to public scorn or ridicule.|The rebels were gibbeted after the failed uprising.  |His actions were gibbeted in the press.|Synonyms: hang, execute, denounce, pillory|Antonyms: praise, commend, celebrate|Verb
