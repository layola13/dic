fiery:/ˈfaɪəri/ (US), /ˈfaɪəri/ (UK)| adj.|火热的，燃烧的；激烈的；暴躁的|Consisting of fire or burning intensely; passionate or easily angered.|The fiery sunset painted the sky in vibrant hues. 火红的夕阳将天空染上了鲜艳的色彩。|The politician delivered a fiery speech. 这位政治家发表了慷慨激昂的演讲。|Synonyms: burning, blazing, passionate, fervent, impetuous|Antonyms: calm, peaceful, tranquil, subdued, apathetic|adjective

fiery:/ˈfaɪəri/ (US), /ˈfaɪəri/ (UK)| n.|火红色|Red like fire|The fiery hue of the autumn leaves was breathtaking. 秋叶的火红色令人叹为观止|Not commonly used as a noun; usually used in poetic or descriptive contexts. |Synonyms: redness, crimson, scarlet|Antonyms: paleness, dullness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fyri*, *firie*, from Old English *fȳrig* (“fiery, on fire”), equivalent to *fȳr* (“fire”) + *-ig* (adjective suffix). Cognate with West Frisian *fierich* (“fiery”), Dutch *vurig* (“fiery”), German *feurig* (“fiery”).
