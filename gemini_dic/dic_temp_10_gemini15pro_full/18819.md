berth: /bɜːrθ/ (UK) /bɜːrθ/ (US)| n. | 卧铺; 停泊处; (议会等的)席位 | A sleeping place on a train or ship; a place where a ship stays in port; a position or seat in a legislative body. |
The train was full, so I had to share a berth with a stranger. 火车上人满了，所以我不得不和一个陌生人共用一个卧铺。

We reserved a berth on the overnight ferry. 我们在夜间渡轮上预订了一个卧铺。

He lost his berth in Parliament after the scandal.  丑闻之后，他失去了在议会的席位。

| 近义词： bunk, bed, dock, mooring, seat, position | 反义词: None | 词性: noun


berth: /bɜːrθ/ (UK) /bɜːrθ/ (US)| vt. |  (使船)停泊; 为…提供卧铺 | To moor a ship; to provide a sleeping place for someone. |
The captain berthed the ship alongside the quay. 船长把船停泊在码头边。

The hotel berthed us in a comfortable room. 旅馆为我们提供了一个舒适的房间。

| 近义词： dock, moor, accommodate, lodge | 反义词: None | 词性: verb
