antistyle:/ˈæntiˌstaɪl/| n.|反风格，刻意粗糙的风格|A deliberate avoidance of or rebellion against traditional artistic styles, often characterized by crudeness or a lack of polish.  It can also refer to a style that embraces the ordinary or mundane.|例句：The artist's antistyle was a reaction against the over-refined aesthetics of the time. 这位艺术家的反风格是对当时过度精致美学的反叛。

例句：Her photographs embrace an antistyle, focusing on the everyday and the seemingly insignificant. 她的照片拥抱了一种反风格，专注于日常和平凡的事物。|近义词：unconventional style, iconoclasm, deconstruction|反义词: classicism, traditional style|词性:noun
