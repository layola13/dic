cramping:/ˈkræmpɪŋ/ /ˈkrɑːmpɪŋ/| n. |痉挛，抽筋|A painful, involuntary contraction of a muscle or muscles, typically lasting from a few seconds to several minutes.|例句：I woke up with terrible leg cramping. 我醒来时腿部严重抽筋。

例句：She experienced cramping in her stomach after eating the shellfish. 她吃了贝类后胃痉挛。|近义词：spasm, contraction, pang|反义词: relaxation, looseness|词性:名词

cramping:/ˈkræmpɪŋ/ /ˈkrɑːmpɪŋ/| adj. |狭窄的，局促的|Confining; restricting.|例句：The cramping living conditions made them feel claustrophobic. 狭窄的居住条件让他们感到幽闭恐惧。

例句：The small, cramping office was difficult to work in.  狭小局促的办公室很难工作。|近义词：confining, restricting, limiting|反义词: spacious, roomy|词性:形容词
