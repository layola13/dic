allegro:/əˈlɛɡroʊ/ |adj. n. |快乐的，活泼的；快板|Characterized by a brisk and lively tempo, typically between 120 and 168 beats per minute.  It can also refer to a piece of music played in this tempo.|The symphony's final movement was a vibrant allegro. 交响曲的最后乐章是充满活力的快板。|Synonyms: brisk, lively, rapid, swift, quick, fast|Antonyms: slow, adagio, lento|adjective, noun

allegros:/əˈlɛɡroʊz/ |n. |快板乐章（复数）|The plural form of "allegro," referring to multiple pieces of music or movements played in a fast tempo.|The composer included two allegros in his sonata. 作曲家在他的奏鸣曲中加入了两段快板。|Synonyms: rapid movements, quick sections|Antonyms: slow movements, adagios|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *allegro*, literally "cheerful," from Latin *alacris* "cheerful, brisk, lively."
