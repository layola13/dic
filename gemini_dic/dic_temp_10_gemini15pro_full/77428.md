gavotte:/ɡəˈvɒt/ | n. |加沃特舞；加沃特舞曲|A moderate tempo dance of French peasant origin, in duple time and usually beginning on the third beat of the bar. It became popular in the 17th and 18th centuries.|The orchestra played a lively gavotte.  管弦乐队演奏了一首活泼的加沃特舞曲。|Synonyms: dance, jig, reel|Antonyms: N/A|noun


gavotte:/ɡəˈvɒt/ | v. |跳加沃特舞|To dance the gavotte.|They gavotted gracefully across the ballroom floor.  他们优雅地在舞厅的地板上跳着加沃特舞。|Synonyms: dance, jig, reel|Antonyms: N/A|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *gavotte*, from *gavot*, inhabitant of the *Pays de Gap* in Dauphiné, southeast France.
