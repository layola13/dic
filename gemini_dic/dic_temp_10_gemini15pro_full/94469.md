imponderableness:/ɪmˈpɒndərəblənəs/ |n.| 不可称量性；难以理解的事物；无重量 |The imponderableness of the situation made it difficult to plan.  情况的难以捉摸使得计划难以制定。| English Explanation: The state or quality of being imponderable; something that is difficult or impossible to understand or assess. | Example: The imponderableness of his motives made her uneasy.  他动机的难以捉摸让她感到不安。|Synonyms: intangibility,  incalculability,  unpredictability| Antonyms: tangibility, calculability, predictability | Noun

imponderable:/ɪmˈpɒndərəbl/ |adj. | 不可称量的；难以理解的；无重量的| The future is imponderable. 未来是不可预测的。 | English Explanation: Difficult or impossible to estimate, assess, or understand.  Also, lacking weight or substance. | Example:  There are many imponderable factors affecting the outcome of the election. 许多难以预测的因素会影响选举结果。| Synonyms:  unmeasurable,  incalculable,  unfathomable| Antonyms: measurable, calculable, fathomable | Adjective
