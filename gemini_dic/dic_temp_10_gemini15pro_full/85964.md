headrace:/ˈhedˌreɪs/ | n. | 水头渠，引水渠 | An artificial channel conveying water to a water mill or other hydraulic device. A headrace is used to increase the elevation of water before it enters a turbine or water wheel, thereby increasing the potential energy available to generate power.  | The headrace channeled water towards the mill. 水头渠将水引向磨坊。| The headrace was carefully engineered to maximize the water's energy. 水头渠经过精心设计，以最大限度地利用水的能量。 | synonyms: millrace, flume, raceway, canal, channel | antonyms: tailrace | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From 'head' + 'race'.  'Head' refers to the elevated position of the water, and 'race' refers to a channel for water, derived from Old Norse *rās 'running, current, race'.
