admit-of:/ədˈmɪt ʌv/| v.| 容许; 承认|To allow or be capable of; to permit.  The situation admits of no other explanation.  这种情况不容许有其他解释。|例句：The rules don't admit of any exceptions. 规则不容许有任何例外。

The evidence admits of only one interpretation.  证据只有一种解释。|近义词：allow, permit, tolerate|反义词：exclude, prevent, prohibit|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *admitten*, from Anglo-Norman *admettre*, from Latin *admittere* (“to send to, let go to, insert, introduce, receive, admit”), from *ad- (“to”) + *mittere* (“to send, let go”).

USAGE->
This phrase is generally used in formal or literary contexts.  It often appears with abstract nouns as subjects.

一般现在时:
The problem admits of several solutions. = 这个问题有几种解决方法。
His behavior admits of no excuse. = 他的行为没有任何借口。


一般过去时:
The situation admitted of no delay. = 情况刻不容缓。
The evidence admitted of two interpretations. = 证据有两种解释。


现在完成时:
The question has admitted of much debate. = 这个问题引起了很多争论。
His statement has admitted of some doubt. = 他的陈述引起了一些怀疑。


过去完成时:
The circumstances had admitted of no other course of action. =  当时的情况不允许采取其他行动方案。
The facts had admitted of no other conclusion. = 事实不容许得出其他结论。


将来时:
The new policy will admit of greater flexibility. = 新政策将允许更大的灵活性。
This development will admit of further investigation. = 这一进展将允许进一步调查。


疑问句:
Does this situation admit of any other explanation? =  这种情况还有其他解释吗？
Did the evidence admit of any doubt? = 证据有任何疑问吗？


否定句:
The facts do not admit of any other interpretation. = 事实不容许任何其他解释。
His behavior did not admit of any excuse. = 他的行为没有任何借口。



PHRASE->
(It's not typically used in set phrases. However, common collocations include:)
admit of no doubt = 毫无疑问
admit of no delay = 刻不容缓
admit of no excuse = 没有借口
admit of several interpretations = 有几种解释
admit of a solution = 容许有一个解决方案


注意事项:
"Admit of" is a rather formal and somewhat archaic expression. While grammatically correct, it might sound stilted in everyday conversation. Consider using simpler alternatives like "allow for," "permit," or "be capable of" in most cases.  It’s more common in written than spoken English.
