overargumentatively:/ˌōvərˌärɡyəˈmentətivlē/ | adv. | 过于争辩地，过于论证地 |  In a manner characterized by excessive argumentation; arguing or debating too much or in a way that is unnecessarily detailed or insistent. | He responded overargumentatively, citing obscure legal precedents. 他反应过度，引用了晦涩的法律先例。| Synonyms: contentiously, disputatiously, polemically | Antonyms: agreeably, amicably, harmoniously | word property: adverb

Etymology:

* "over-" (prefix): Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond, across."
* "argumentative": from "argument" (late 14c.), from Old French "argument" (13c.), from Latin "argumentum" "evidence, proof, token, subject, contents," from "arguere" "to make clear, make known, prove, accuse."
* "-ly" (suffix): forming adverbs from adjectives.
