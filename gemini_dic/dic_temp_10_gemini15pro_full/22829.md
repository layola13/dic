bossest:/ˈbɒsɪst/ | adj. | 最像老板的，最专横的 |  A humorous and informal superlative form of "bossy," meaning extremely domineering or controlling.  It's not considered standard English and is rarely used in formal writing. |  "She was the bossest person I'd ever met, always telling everyone what to do." 她是我见过的最专横的人，总是告诉每个人该做什么。 | 近义词: bossiest, most domineering, most controlling | 反义词: submissive, meek, compliant | 词性:形容词

bossest:/ˈbɒsɪst/ | n. | 最专横的人 |  A humorous and informal noun form of "bossy," referring to the most domineering person. Like the adjective form, it's not standard English. | "He's the bossest in the whole group." 他是整个小组中最专横的人。 | 近义词: dictator, tyrant, autocrat | 反义词: follower, subordinate | 词性:名词


词源:  Derived from "bossy," which originated in the 19th century, likely from "boss" (meaning a supervisor or manager). The "-est" suffix is a standard English superlative ending.
