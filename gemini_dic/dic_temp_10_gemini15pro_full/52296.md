dichroic:/daɪˈkroʊɪk/| adj.|二色的，双色的，二向色的|Exhibiting dichroism; having two colors, appearing of two different colors, especially when viewed from different directions or by transmitted and reflected light.|The gemstone displays dichroic properties, appearing blue from one angle and green from another.|近义词：birefringent, dichromatic|反义词: monochromatic|词性:形容词

dichroism:/ˈdaɪkroʊɪzm/| n.|二色性，双色性|The property of some crystals and other materials of exhibiting different colors, especially two different colors, when viewed from different directions or by transmitted and reflected light.|The dichroism of the mineral allowed it to be identified.|近义词：birefringence|反义词: monochromatism|词性:名词

dichroscope:/ˈdaɪkrəˌskoʊp/| n.|二色镜，二色检查器|An instrument for examining the dichroism of crystals.|The gemologist used a dichroscope to confirm the stone's dichroism.|近义词：polariscope|反义词: N/A|词性:名词

dichromatic:/ˌdaɪkrəˈmætɪk/| adj.|双色的，二色的|Having two colors; exhibiting dichromatism.|Some species of birds are sexually dichromatic, with males and females displaying different plumage colors.|近义词：dichroic|反义词: monochromatic|词性:形容词

dichromatism:/daɪˈkroʊməˌtɪzəm/| n.|二色性，二色视觉|A deficiency of color vision in which only two of the three primary colors can be distinguished.|Dichromatism affects the ability to perceive certain color combinations.|近义词：color blindness|反义词: trichromacy|词性:名词
