maori:/ˈmaʊri/ | n. adj.| 毛利人；毛利语 | A member of the indigenous Polynesian people of New Zealand. Also, the language of the Maori people. |  Example: The Maori culture is rich in tradition. 毛利文化富有传统。

Example:  He is learning to speak Maori. 他正在学习说毛利语。 | Synonym: (for the people)  indigenous, native | Antonym: (for the people) immigrant, foreigner | Noun, Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Maori word *māori*, meaning "normal", "ordinary", or "indigenous".  Originally used by the Maori to distinguish themselves from Europeans.
