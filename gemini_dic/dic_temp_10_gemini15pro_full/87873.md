hideyoshi:/hiːdeɪˈjoʊʃiː/ | n. |  丰臣秀吉 (Toyotomi Hideyoshi) |  A Japanese daimyo (feudal lord) and politician of the late Sengoku period who unified Japan and initiated the invasion of Korea.  | Hideyoshi rose from humble origins to become the most powerful man in Japan. 丰臣秀吉出身卑微，最终成为日本最有权势的人。 | synonym: none (as a proper noun) | antonym: none (as a proper noun) | proper noun


hideyoshi:/hiːdeɪˈjoʊʃiː/ | n. |  丰臣秀吉式的 (Hideyoshi-like or Hideyoshi-esque)  |  Used to describe something reminiscent of Toyotomi Hideyoshi's policies, strategies, or character, such as his ambitious nature, ruthlessness, or social mobility. |  His hideyoshi-esque ambition led him to pursue ever greater power. 他丰臣秀吉式的野心驱使他追求更大的权力。| synonym: ambitious, ruthless, cunning | antonym: humble, compassionate, merciful | adjective (informal, rare usage)
