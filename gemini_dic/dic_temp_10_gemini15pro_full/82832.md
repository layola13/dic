gulch:/ɡʌltʃ/
n.
深谷；峡谷；冲沟
A deep, narrow ravine, especially one formed by heavy rains or a stream.
The hikers carefully descended into the steep gulch.  徒步旅行者小心翼翼地走下陡峭的峡谷。
The flash flood carved a new gulch through the landscape. 暴洪在地貌上冲刷出一条新的沟壑。
近义词: ravine, canyon, gorge
反义词: ridge, peak, summit
词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From a dialectal variant of *gulk*, likely related to *gulp* and referring to the swallowing action of a ravine.  Possibly influenced by Dutch *kolkgat*, meaning "whirlpool."
