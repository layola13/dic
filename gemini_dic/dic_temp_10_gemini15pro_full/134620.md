nonlyrically:/ˌnɒnˈlɪrɪkli/ /ˌnɑːnˈlɪrɪkli/| adv.| 非抒情地；没有歌词地；散文式地 |  In a manner that is not lyrical; without lyrics; in prose. | He spoke nonlyrically about the beauty of the sunset, focusing on the scientific aspects of the light refraction. 他用非抒情的语言描述日落的美丽，重点讲述了光线折射的科学方面。 | prosaically, plainly, matter-of-factly | lyrically, poetically | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + lyrically.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of." "Lyrically" is an adverb derived from "lyrical," which relates to expressing emotions in an imaginative and beautiful way, often through poetry or song.  The word "lyrical" ultimately comes from the Greek word "lyrikos," meaning "sung to the lyre."
