buy-in:/ˈbaɪ.ɪn/| n.|买进; 入股; 支持; 赞同|An agreement to participate in something, especially by giving money or time; Acceptance of and willingness to actively support and participate in something.|例句：We need to get buy-in from all the stakeholders before we proceed.  我们需要在继续进行之前获得所有利益相关者的支持。

例句：The company secured buy-in from the employees for the new restructuring plan. 公司获得了员工对新重组计划的支持。|近义词：agreement, acceptance, approval, support, endorsement|反义词：opposition, dissent, rejection|词性:noun


buy-ins:/ˈbaɪ.ɪnz/| n.|买进; 入股; 支持; 赞同（复数）|Agreements to participate in something, especially by giving money or time; Instances of acceptance and willingness to actively support and participate in something.|例句：Multiple buy-ins from investors allowed the project to move forward.  多个投资者的加入使得项目得以推进。


例句：The team leader sought buy-ins from individual members to ensure project success. 团队领导者寻求每个成员的支持，以确保项目成功。|近义词：agreements, acceptances, approvals, supports, endorsements|反义词：oppositions, dissents, rejections|词性:noun
