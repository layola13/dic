obloquious: /əˈbloʊkwiəs/ | adj. | 谄媚的，奉承的 | Excessively eager to please or obey; fawning. Characterized by or exhibiting servile compliance; subservient. |  He was so obloquious to his boss that his colleagues found him repulsive. 他对老板如此谄媚，以至于他的同事都觉得他令人反感。

The obloquious waiter hovered around the table, anticipating the diners' every need.  那个谄媚的侍者在桌子旁徘徊，预料着用餐者的一切需求。

| 近义词：fawning, servile, subservient, sycophantic, flattering | 反义词：independent, assertive, forthright, rebellious | 词性: adjective

Etymology:  From Latin *obliquus* ("slanting, indirect") + *-ious*.  Originally meant "slanted, indirect," then "devious," and finally "servile, fawning."
