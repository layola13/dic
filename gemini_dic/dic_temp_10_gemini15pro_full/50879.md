desert:/ˈdɛzərt/ (US), /ˈdɛzət/ (UK)| n. |沙漠，荒漠|A barren or desolate area, especially one with little or no vegetation.|The Sahara is the largest hot desert in the world.  撒哈拉沙漠是世界上最大的热沙漠。

desert:/dɪˈzɜːrt/ (US), /dɪˈzɜːt/ (UK)| vt. |抛弃，离弃|To abandon or leave someone or something.|He deserted his wife and children. 他抛弃了他的妻子和孩子。


desert:/ˈdɛzərt/ (US), /ˈdɛzət/ (UK)| adj. |荒芜的，无人居住的|Uninhabited and desolate.|The desert landscape stretched for miles. 荒凉的景色绵延数英里。


Etymology:
From Old French *desert, from Latin desertum "abandoned place," neuter past participle of deserere "to abandon," from de- "away" + serere "to join, link."

Synonyms (noun): wasteland, wilderness, barren
Synonyms (verb): abandon, forsake, leave
Antonyms (noun): oasis, paradise, fertile land
Antonyms (verb): support, stay with, remain

词性:  noun, verb, adjective
