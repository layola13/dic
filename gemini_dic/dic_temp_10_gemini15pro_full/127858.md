neo-hellenic:/ˌnɪːoʊˈhɛlɪnɪk/
adjective|新希腊的，现代希腊的|Relating to or denoting the form of the Greek language used since the end of the Byzantine period.|例句：Neo-Hellenic literature often explores themes of national identity. 现代希腊文学经常探讨民族认同的主题。|例句：The neo-Hellenic state was established in 1821. 新希腊国家成立于1821年。|近义词：Modern Greek|反义词：Ancient Greek|词性:adjective

Etymology:
neo- + Hellenic
neo-:  prefix meaning "new," from Greek *neos*
Hellenic:  from Greek *Hellēnikos* "Greek," from *Hellēn* "Greek"
