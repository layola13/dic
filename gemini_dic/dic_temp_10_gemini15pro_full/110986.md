liquidize:/ˈlɪkwɪdaɪz/ /ˈlɪkwɪˌdaɪz/| vt. & vi. n.|液化，清算，消除|To convert (something) into cash, typically by selling it.  To kill or destroy (someone).|例句：The company was forced to liquidize its assets to pay off its debts.  公司被迫清算资产以偿还债务。

例句：The dictator ordered his troops to liquidize the opposition leaders.  独裁者命令他的军队消灭反对派领导人。|近义词：dissolve, liquefy, kill, eliminate|反义词:solidify, freeze, establish|词性:verb, noun


liquidize:/ˈlɪkwɪdaɪz/ /ˈlɪkwɪˌdaɪz/| n.|液化，清算|The process of converting assets into cash, or the state of being liquid.|例句：The company went into liquidation after failing to secure further funding. 公司在未能获得进一步融资后进入清算阶段。

例句：The liquidation of the company's assets took several months. 公司资产的清算花了几个月的时间。|近义词：dissolution, liquefaction|反义词:solidification, freezing|词性:noun
