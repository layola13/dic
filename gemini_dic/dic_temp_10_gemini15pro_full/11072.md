archaeologist: /ˌɑːrkiˈɒlədʒɪst/ (US) /ˌɑːkiˈɒlədʒɪst/ (UK)| n. |考古学家|A person who studies human history and prehistory through the excavation of sites and the analysis of artifacts and other physical remains.|The archaeologist carefully brushed the dirt from the ancient pottery. 考古学家小心翼翼地把泥土从古代陶器上刷掉。|Near synonyms: excavator, anthropologist, historian|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could consider "fabricator" in a context of falsifying history)|Noun

archaeologue: /ˌɑːrkiˈɒləɡ/ (US) /ˌɑːrkiˈɒlɒɡ/ (UK)| n. |考古学家|(Less common, chiefly British) A person who studies human history and prehistory through the excavation of sites and the analysis of artifacts and other physical remains.|The archaeologue unearthed a remarkable mosaic. 考古学家挖掘出了一幅非凡的马赛克。|Near synonyms: excavator, anthropologist, historian|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, could consider "fabricator" in a context of falsifying history)|Noun
