grown-ups:/ˈɡroʊnˌʌps/ (US) /ˈɡrəʊnˌʌps/ (UK)| n. |成年人|Adults, people who are no longer children.  Mature individuals.|例句：The grown-ups are talking in the living room. 成年人在客厅里谈话。

例句：The movie is not suitable for children, it's for grown-ups. 这部电影不适合儿童，它是为成年人准备的。|近义词：adults, matures|反义词:children, kids|词性:名词

grown-up:/ˈɡroʊnˈʌp/ (US) /ˈɡrəʊnˈʌp/ (UK)| adj. |成年的; 长大的|Having reached adulthood; fully developed.|例句：She has a grown-up son who lives in another city. 她有一个成年的儿子住在另一个城市。

例句：He has a very grown-up attitude for his age.  就他的年龄而言，他的态度非常成熟。|近义词：adult, mature|反义词:childish, immature|词性:形容词
