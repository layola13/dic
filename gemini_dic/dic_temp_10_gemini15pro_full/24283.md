brigandages:/ˈbrɪɡəndɪdʒɪz/
noun
抢劫, 匪盗行为
Acts of robbery and violence, typically committed by bandits.
The brigandages committed by the outlaw gang terrorized the countryside. 这伙歹徒犯下的抢劫行为恐吓了整个乡村。
The museum was the target of several brigandages over the years. 多年来，该博物馆是多次抢劫的目标。
synonyms: robberies, banditry, outlawry, plundering, pillaging
antonyms: lawfulness, order, peace, security
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From French "brigandage," from "brigand" (bandit), of uncertain origin. Possibly from Italian "brigante" (bandit), ultimately perhaps from a Germanic source.
