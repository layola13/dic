old-maidishly:/ˌoʊldˈmeɪdɪʃli/ | adv. | 老处女似地；古板地；拘谨地 | In a way that is considered typical of an old maid, often characterized by excessive neatness, fussiness, primness, or prudishness. | She arranged the flowers old-maidishly, obsessing over every petal's placement. 她老处女似地插花，对每个花瓣的位置都斤斤计较。|  She smoothed her skirt old-maidishly before sitting down. 她坐下前老处女似地抚平了裙子。 | Synonyms: primly, fussily, prudishly, meticulously | Antonyms:  carelessly, sloppily, casually | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From old maid + -ish + -ly.  "Old maid" refers to an unmarried woman, typically older, and carries connotations of primness and fussiness.  The suffix "-ish" transforms it into an adjective, and "-ly" further transforms it into an adverb.
