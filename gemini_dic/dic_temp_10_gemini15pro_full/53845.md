discontent's:/dɪskənˈtɛnts/
noun
不满；不满足
The possessive form of "discontent," referring to the dissatisfaction or unhappiness belonging to someone or something.
The worker's discontent's stemmed from low wages and poor working conditions. 工人的不满源于低工资和恶劣的工作条件。
Near Synonyms: dissatisfaction, unhappiness, disaffection, resentment
Antonyms: contentment, satisfaction, happiness, pleasure
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *discontent*, borrowed from Anglo-Norman *discontentement* and Old French *descontentement*, from *descontenter* (“to displease”)  + *-ment* (suffix forming nouns of action or result), from Latin *discontentus* (“discontented”), from *dis-* (“not, un-”) + *contentus* (“content, satisfied”).
