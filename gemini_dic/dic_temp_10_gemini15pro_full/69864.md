ferrotype: /ˈfɛrəˌtaɪp/ | n. | 铁板照片，锡版照片 | A photograph taken on a thin iron plate coated with a dark enamel or lacquer and used as the support for a collodion photographic emulsion. | Example:  The antique shop had a collection of Victorian-era ferrotypes. 古董店里收藏了一些维多利亚时代的铁板照片。

Example: My grandmother showed me her ferrotype portrait from when she was a child. 我祖母给我看了她小时候的铁板照片。 | Near synonyms: tintype, melainotype | Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps digital photograph) | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From ferro- (iron) + -type (impression, image)
