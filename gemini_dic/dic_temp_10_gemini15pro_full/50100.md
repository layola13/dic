dentilabial:/ˌdɛntiˈleɪbiəl/
adjective
齿唇音的
A dentilabial consonant is one that is produced by placing the lower lip against the upper teeth.
The sound /f/ in "five" is a dentilabial consonant.  /f/在"five"中是一个齿唇音。

She struggled to pronounce the dentilabial sounds in the foreign language. 她很难发出这门外语中的齿唇音。
synonym: labiodental (sometimes used interchangeably, though technically refers to upper teeth touching lower lip)
antonym:  (No direct antonym, as it describes a specific place of articulation.  Could consider contrasting terms like velar, glottal, etc. which describe different articulation points.)
词性: adjective
