borlaug: /ˈbɔːrlɔːɡ/ | n. | 博洛格 | Norman Borlaug (1914-2009), an American agronomist and humanitarian who is considered the "Father of the Green Revolution" for his work in developing high-yielding and disease-resistant wheat varieties that significantly increased food production, especially in developing countries.
