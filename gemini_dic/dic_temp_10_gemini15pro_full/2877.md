advert: /ˈædvɜːt/ (UK) /ˈædvɜːrt/ (US)| n. |广告|An advertisement; a notice or announcement in a public medium promoting a product, service, or event.|We placed an advert in the local newspaper. 我们在当地报纸上刊登了一则广告。|Synonyms: advertisement, commercial, announcement, promotion, publicity, notice|Antonyms: None|Noun

advert: /ədˈvɜːt/ (UK) /ædˈvɜːrt/ (US)| vi. |提及，谈到|Refer to in speaking or writing.|The chairman adverted to the issue of funding in his speech. 主席在他的讲话中提到了资金问题。|Synonyms: refer, mention, allude, touch upon, comment on|Antonyms: ignore, avoid, neglect|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *advertere* "to turn towards," from *ad-* "to" + *vertere* "to turn."  The noun form shortened from *advertisement*.


USAGE->

(As a Noun)

Countable Noun:

The adverts for the new car are everywhere. =  这辆新车的广告到处都是。

They placed several adverts in the Sunday paper. = 他们在周日报纸上刊登了几个广告。

(As a Verb)

Simple Present:

He adverts to the problem in the report. = 他在报告中提到了这个问题。
She rarely adverts to her past experiences. = 她很少提及她过去的经历。


Simple Past:

The speaker adverted briefly to the recent economic downturn. = 演讲者简要地提到了最近的经济衰退。
During the meeting, they adverted to the budget constraints. = 在会议期间，他们提到了预算限制。


Present Perfect:

The report has adverted to the potential risks of the project. = 该报告提到了该项目的潜在风险。
Several experts have adverted to the importance of early intervention. = 几位专家提到了早期干预的重要性。


Past Perfect:

He had adverted to the issue several times before the final decision was made. = 在最终决定做出之前，他已经多次提到了这个问题。
She had adverted to the possibility of failure, but no one listened. = 她曾提到过失败的可能性，但没有人听。


Future:

He will advert to the matter in his next speech. = 他将在下次演讲中提及此事。
The committee will advert to the proposed changes at the next meeting. = 委员会将在下次会议上提及拟议的变更。


PHRASE->
(Primarily applies to the noun form)

an online advert = 一个在线广告
a classified advert =  一个分类广告
