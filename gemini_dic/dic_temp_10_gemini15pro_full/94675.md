imprinted:/ɪmˈprɪntɪd/ |adj.|印记的；铭刻的；被牢记的|Marked or impressed upon a surface, as if by printing; fixed firmly in the mind.|The horrifying images of the war were imprinted on his memory.  战争的恐怖画面深深地印在了他的记忆里。|Synonyms: engraved, etched, stamped, fixed, ingrained|Antonyms: forgotten, erased|Adjective

imprint:/ɪmˈprɪnt/ |v.|印；刻；铭记；使铭记|To mark or impress (something) on a surface; to fix (something) firmly in the mind.|He tried to imprint the scene on his memory. 他试图把这个场景铭记在心。|Synonyms: impress, stamp, engrave, fix, instill|Antonyms: erase, forget|Verb

imprint:/ˈɪmˌprɪnt/ |n.|印记；痕迹；深刻的影响|A mark or impression made by pressure; a lasting effect or influence.|The experience left a deep imprint on his personality. 这段经历对他的人格留下了深刻的印记。|Synonyms: mark, impression, stamp, trace, vestige|Antonyms: blankness, void|Noun
