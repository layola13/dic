choreography: /ˌkɔːriˈɒɡrəfi/ (US) /ˌkɒriˈɒɡrəfi/ (UK)| n.| 舞蹈设计；编舞| the art of creating and arranging dances or other movements, especially for performances on stage, film, or television| The choreography for the ballet was breathtaking.  芭蕾舞的编舞令人叹为观止。|The elaborate choreography made the performance captivating. 精心的编舞使表演引人入胜。| synonyms: dance composition, dance arrangement | antonyms: improvisation, extemporization| noun

choreograph: /ˈkɔːriəˌɡræf/ (US) /ˈkɒriəˌɡrɑːf/ (UK)| v.| 编舞；设计舞蹈动作| to create and arrange the steps and movements of a dance or other performance| She choreographed the opening ceremony for the Olympics. 她为奥运会开幕式编排了舞蹈。|He choreographed a stunning piece that blended classical and modern dance. 他编排了一支融合古典和现代舞蹈的精彩作品。| synonyms: compose, arrange, devise, stage, direct | antonyms: improvise, extemporize| verb
