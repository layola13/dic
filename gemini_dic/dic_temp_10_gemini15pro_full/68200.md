fair-to-middling:/ˌfer tə ˈmɪdlɪŋ/ | adj. | 中等的；一般的；过得去的 |  Moderately good; average; neither very good nor very bad.  | The food at the restaurant was fair-to-middling.  这家餐馆的食物一般。 | Synonyms: average, mediocre, so-so, passable | Antonyms: excellent, superb, outstanding, terrible, awful | adjective


fair-to-middling:/ˌfer tə ˈmɪdlɪŋ/ | n. | 中等水平；中等质量 |  A level or quality that is moderate or average.  |  His performance was fair-to-middling. 他的表现中等。| Synonyms: mediocrity | Antonyms: excellence, superiority | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From "fair" (meaning moderately good) and "middling" (meaning average or moderate). The term became common in the 19th century.
