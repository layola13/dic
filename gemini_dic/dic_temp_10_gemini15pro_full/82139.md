grommet:/ˈɡrɑːmɪt (US), ˈɡrɒmɪt (UK)| n. |索环，金属扣眼，垫圈|A metal ring or edge that is used to reinforce a hole in a material, such as cloth or leather, and prevent it from tearing or fraying. |Example 1: The grommets in the shower curtain allow you to easily hang it on the rod.  淋浴帘上的索环可以让你轻松地把它挂在杆子上。

Example 2:  My new boots have metal grommets for the laces. 我的新靴子有金属扣眼用来穿鞋带。|Synonym: eyelet, ring, bushing|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "unreinforced hole") |noun


grommets:/ˈɡrɑːmɪts (US), ˈɡrɒmɪts (UK)| n. |索环，金属扣眼，垫圈 (plural of grommet)|Plural form of grommet, referring to more than one grommet. |Example 1: The grommets on the sail are rusting.  船帆上的索环正在生锈。

Example 2:  She bought a package of grommets to repair the tarpaulin. 她买了一包索环来修理防水布。|Synonym: eyelets, rings, bushings|Antonym:  (No direct antonym) |noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "grommet" (a ship's rigging ring), diminutive of "gromet" (boy, groom), of Germanic origin.
