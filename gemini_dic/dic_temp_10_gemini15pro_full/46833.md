daphnia:/ˈdæfniə/| n. |水蚤|A small freshwater crustacean of the order Cladocera, characterized by a transparent carapace and jerky movements.|Daphnia are often used in scientific studies. 水蚤常用于科学研究。|Synonyms: water flea|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

daphnia:/ˈdæfniə/| n. |水蚤|A small planktonic crustacean of the order Cladocera. They are commonly called water fleas because of their jerky swimming motions.|Many fish species feed on daphnia. 许多鱼类以水蚤为食。|Synonyms: water flea|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From New Latin *Daphnia, from Ancient Greek δάφνη (dáphnē, “laurel, bay tree”), because of the supposed resemblance of some species to a laurel leaf.
