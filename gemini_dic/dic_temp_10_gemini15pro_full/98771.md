intangiblenesses: /ɪnˈtændʒəbəlɪnɪsɪz/ | n. | 无形资产，无形性 | The state or quality of being intangible; numerous intangible things.  It refers to things that cannot be touched or easily defined, often abstract qualities. |  The company's intangiblenesses, such as its brand reputation and customer loyalty, are difficult to quantify but crucial to its success.  这家公司的无形资产，例如品牌声誉和客户忠诚度，难以量化，但对其成功至关重要。| synonyms: impalpabilities, abstractnesses | antonyms: tangibilities, concretenesses | noun

词源: From intangible + -ness + -es (plural).  Intangible comes from Late Latin *intangibilis, from in- 'not' + tangibilis 'tangible'.
