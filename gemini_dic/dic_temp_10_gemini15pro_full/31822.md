chedda:/ˈtʃɛdə/| n.|切达干酪|A firm smooth-textured cheese, originally made in Cheddar, Somerset, England, from cow's milk. It is now made worldwide and varies in color from white to deep yellow.|例句：I like my sandwich with cheddar. 我喜欢我的三明治加切达干酪。|近义词：cheese|反义词:|名词

chedda:/ˈtʃɛdə/| adj. (俚语)|很棒的，极好的|Excellent; great.  This usage is primarily slang.|例句：That concert was chedda! 那场音乐会真是太棒了！|近义词：awesome, great, excellent|反义词：terrible, awful|形容词

词源:  The word "cheddar" comes from the village of Cheddar in Somerset, England, where the cheese was originally made in the caves of the Cheddar Gorge. The name "Cheddar" itself is derived from the Old English word ceodor, meaning "deep valley" or "ravine."
