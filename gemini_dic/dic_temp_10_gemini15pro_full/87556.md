heteronomy:/ˌhɛtərˈɑːnəmi/ |n.| 他律，异律|The state of being under the control of another, especially in respect to moral principles; the opposite of autonomy.  Being governed by external forces or laws.|  Children often display heteronomy in their moral development, accepting rules without questioning them. 孩子们在道德发展中经常表现出他律性，不加质疑地接受规则。|Synonyms: subjection, dependence, other-direction|Antonyms: autonomy, self-government, independence|noun
