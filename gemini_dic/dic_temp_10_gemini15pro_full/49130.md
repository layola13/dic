deist:/ˈdiːɪst/ | n. | 自然神论者 | A person who believes in the existence of a supreme being, specifically of a creator who does not intervene in the universe.  A deist believes in God, but rejects organized religion and supernatural events like miracles, believing that God created the universe and then allowed it to operate according to natural laws. |  Thomas Paine was a prominent deist. 托马斯·潘恩是一位著名的自然神论者。

He considered himself a deist, believing in a creator but not in divine intervention. 他认为自己是一个自然神论者，相信造物主，但不相信神圣干预。| 近义词: freethinker, rationalist | 反义词: theist, atheist | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *déiste*, from Latin *deus* "god". Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*dyeu-* "to shine," in the sense of "sky" or "heaven".
