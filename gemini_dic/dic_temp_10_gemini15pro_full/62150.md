enclose:/ɪnˈkloʊz/ (US) /ɪnˈkləʊz/ (UK)| vt.|包围，围绕；附上，装入|To surround or close off on all sides; to include something with a letter or package.|Please enclose a check with your application. 请随申请书附上支票。|Synonyms: surround, encompass, include, attach|Antonyms: open, release, exclude, detach|Verb


enclose:/ɪnˈkloʊz/ (US) /ɪnˈkləʊz/ (UK)| n.|围场，圈地 (Rarely Used as a Noun)|An enclosed area.  (This noun form is less common)|The sheep were kept in a small enclose. 羊被圈在一个小围场里。|Synonyms: enclosure, compound, paddock|Antonyms: open space|Noun (rare)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "enclore," from Latin "includere" (to shut in), from "in-" (in) + "claudere" (to shut).
