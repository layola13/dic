fossil's:/ˈfɑːsəlz/ /ˈfɒsɪlz/ | n. | 化石的；化石 |  The possessive form of "fossil," meaning belonging to a fossil or fossils. | Example: The fossil's age was determined by carbon dating.  化石的年龄是用碳测年法测定的。| Synonym: fossilized | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a possessive) | Possessive noun


fossil:/ˈfɑːsəl/ /ˈfɒsɪl/ | n. adj.| 化石；僵化的，老式的 | The remains or impression of a prehistoric plant or animal embedded in rock and preserved in petrified form. Can also be used to describe something outdated. | Example:  They found a dinosaur fossil. 他们发现了一个恐龙化石。 | Synonym: relic, remnant | Antonym: modern, contemporary | Noun, Adjective
