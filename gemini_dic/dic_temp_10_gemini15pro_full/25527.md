bullrush:/ˈbʊlrʌʃ/ | n. | 香蒲, 蒲草, 灯芯草; 猛攻, 突袭 | A tall marsh plant (Typha latifolia or T. angustifolia) with long flat leaves and a dark brown, velvety cylindrical head of densely packed flowers. Also, a sudden, vigorous attack or effort. |  The lake's edge was thick with bullrushes. 湖边长满了香蒲。|  The army launched a bullrush on the enemy position.  军队对敌人的阵地发动了猛攻。 | synonyms: reedmace, cattail, rush, assault, onslaught | antonyms: retreat, withdrawal | noun


bullrush:/ˈbʊlrʌʃ/ | v. |  (使)快速通过; 猛攻, 突袭 | To force or move through something quickly and impetuously. | The police bullrushed the suspect into the courtroom. 警察把嫌疑人迅速带进了法庭。| The team bullrushed their opponents into submission. 该队猛攻对手，使其屈服。| synonyms: hustle, rush, charge, storm | antonyms: dawdle, linger | verb
