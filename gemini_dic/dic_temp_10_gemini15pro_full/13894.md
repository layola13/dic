augustan:/ɔːˈɡʌstən/ | adj. n. |奥古斯都式的，奥古斯都时代的人|Relating to the Roman emperor Augustus or his age; marked by a classical elegance and refinement.|The Augustan age was a golden age for Roman literature. 奥古斯都时代是罗马文学的黄金时代.|近义词：classical, neoclassical, refined, elegant|反义词：barbaric, unrefined, crude|词性:形容词, 名词

augustan:/ɔːˈɡʌstən/ | n. |奥古斯都时代的人|A person who lived during the reign of Augustus or a writer of that period.|Virgil and Horace were prominent Augustans. 维吉尔和贺拉斯是杰出的奥古斯都时代人物。|近义词：classicist, neoclassicist|反义词： - |词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Augustus*, title of the first Roman emperor, Gaius Octavius (63 BC – AD 14), originally named Gaius Octavius Thurinus,  grandnephew and adopted son of Julius Caesar.  The title *Augustus*  meaning "venerable," "majestic,"  was conferred on him by the Roman Senate in 27 BC.
