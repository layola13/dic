nonelasticities:/ˌnɒnɪˌlæˈstɪsɪtiz/| n. |非弹性|The plural form of nonelasticity, referring to the lack of responsiveness of one variable to changes in another.  It often applies in economics to describe situations where demand or supply doesn't change significantly with price fluctuations.|The nonelasticities of demand for essential goods like insulin make pricing strategies complex.|Synonym:inelasticities|Antonym:elasticities|noun
