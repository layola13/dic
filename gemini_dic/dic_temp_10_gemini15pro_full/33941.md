cirrobranchiata:/ˌsɪroʊbræŋˈkiːətə/
|n.|羽鳃类|A subclass of brachiopods characterized by a pair of spiralled, tentacle-bearing arms used for filter feeding, and a short, fleshy stalk or pedicle for attachment.|Cirrobranchiata are exclusively marine animals found in deep-sea environments.|近义词: Brachiopoda (broader classification)|反义词: None (specific subclass)|词性: noun

Etymology:
cirro- (Latin, from cirrus, meaning "curl") + branchiata (Latin, neuter plural of branchiatus, meaning "having gills")
