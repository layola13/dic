banyan:/ˈbænjən/ | n. | 印度榕树，榕树 | A large East Indian tree (Ficus benghalensis) that puts out aerial roots that grow down into the soil forming additional trunks.  It is the national tree of India, revered for its longevity and its large, spreading canopy that offers shade. |  We sat in the shade of a giant banyan tree.  我们坐在一棵巨大的榕树的树荫下。

banyan:/ˈbænjən/ | n. | (比喻)提供庇护或支持的东西；像榕树一样不断扩展的事物 | Something that provides shelter or support, or that grows and spreads like a banyan tree.  | The university has become a banyan for scientific research in the region. 这所大学已经成为该地区科学研究的庇护所。

ETYMOLOGY->
From Portuguese *baniano*,  from Gujarati *baṇiyõ* "merchant, grocer,"  because the tree was often used as a marketplace by traders, especially Bania merchants.
