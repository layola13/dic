declamatorily:/dɪˌklæməˈtɔːrɪli/ | adv. | 演说般地，雄辩地，慷慨激昂地 | In a manner resembling a formal speech or oration, often with exaggerated expression or emotion.  Characterized by a pompous or theatrical delivery. | He spoke declamatorily, his voice booming across the hall. 他慷慨激昂地说着，声音在整个大厅回响。 | 近义词：rhetorically, oratorically, dramatically, bombastically | 反义词：simply, plainly, quietly, subtly | 词性: 副词

詞源: From Late Latin *declamatorius, from Latin declamat-, past participle stem of declamare (“to declaim”).
