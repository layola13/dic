exag:/ɪɡˈzædʒ/ | abbreviation | 夸张; 夸大 |  Short for "exaggerate". |  "He told me he caught a fish *this* big! Total exag." 他告诉我他钓到了一条这么大的鱼！完全是夸张。| 近义词: overstatement, hyperbole | 反义词: understatement, minimization | n.

exaggerate: /ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪt/ | v. | 夸大; 夸张 | To represent something as larger, better, or worse than it really is. | She tends to exaggerate when she tells stories. 她讲故事时往往会夸大其词。 | 近义词: overstate, amplify, embellish | 反义词: understate, downplay | v.

exaggerated: /ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪtɪd/ | adj. | 夸大的; 夸张的 | Enlarged or altered beyond normal proportions; overstated. | He walked with an exaggerated swagger. 他走路 swagger 的样子很夸张。 | 近义词: overstated, inflated, amplified | 反义词: understated, minimized | adj.

exaggeration: /ɪɡˌzædʒəˈreɪʃn/ | n. | 夸张; 夸大之词 | A statement that represents something as better or worse than it really is. | It's an exaggeration to say that I'm a great singer. 说我是个很棒的歌手，有点夸张了。 | 近义词: overstatement, hyperbole | 反义词: understatement | n.
