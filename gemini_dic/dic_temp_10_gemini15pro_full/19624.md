bigy: /ˈbɪɡi/
adjective
大的；重要的；自负的 (Informal, often humorous)
Bigy is not a standard English word. It seems to be a colloquial or slang term, possibly derived from "big," meaning large or significant, and used to describe someone or something considered important, or perhaps self-important, often in a humorous or ironic way.
"He acts all bigy now that he's got a promotion."  他升职后就变得很自负。
"That's a bigy car you've got there!"  你的车真大！
近义词: important, significant, pompous, arrogant, conceited
反义词: small, insignificant, humble, modest
词性: adjective
