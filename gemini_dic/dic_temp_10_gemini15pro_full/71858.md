fleshier:/ˈflɛʃɪər/ | adj. | 更肉的，更丰满的 | Having more flesh; plumper.  More fleshy than something else being compared to. | The grapes this year are fleshier than last year's. 今年的葡萄比去年的更肉。| synonyms: plumper, meatier, fuller, thicker | antonyms: thinner, leaner, bonier | adjective

fleshier:/ˈflɛʃɪər/ | adj. | 更多肉质的，更鲜嫩的 (指水果蔬菜) | Having more pulp; juicier.  Used to describe fruits or vegetables that have a richer, more succulent texture. |  The peaches at this orchard are fleshier and sweeter. 这个果园的桃子更肉质更甜。| synonyms: juicier, pulpier, succulenter, riper | antonyms: drier, tougher, stringier | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From flesh + -ier (comparative suffix).  Flesh comes from Old English *flæsc, from Proto-Germanic *flaiska-.
