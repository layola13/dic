flittern:/ˈflɪtərn/ | n. | 碎屑，碎片，小片 | Small, thin pieces, fragments, or flakes of something. |  The flittern of fallen leaves covered the path. 落叶的碎片覆盖了小路。| synonyms: flakes, fragments, scraps, shards | antonyms: whole, complete | noun


flittern:/ˈflɪtərn/ | v. |  飘动，轻快地飞，快速移动 | To move swiftly and lightly, like a butterfly or bird; to flutter. | Butterflies flitterned among the flowers. 蝴蝶在花丛中飞舞。| synonyms: flutter, dart, flit, whisk | antonyms: plod, trudge | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fliternen*, frequentative of *flitren* (“to flutter”), from Old English *flyttrian* (“to flutter”), related to *fleotan* (“to float, swim”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *fleutjaną* (“to float”), from Proto-Indo-European *plewd- (“to flow, float, fly”).
