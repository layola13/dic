gingerbread:/ˈdʒɪndʒərbred/| n. adj.|姜饼, 姜饼色的|A type of cookie or cake flavored with ginger and spices, often made in decorative shapes, especially during Christmas. Also used as an adjective to describe something that resembles gingerbread in color or decoration.|例句：The children decorated gingerbread houses for Christmas. 孩子们为了圣诞节装饰了姜饼屋。

例句：She wore a gingerbread-colored dress. 她穿了一件姜饼色的连衣裙。|近义词：ginger snap, gingersnap, cookie|反义词: (No direct antonym, potentially "plain cookie/cake")|词性:noun, adjective

gingerbread:/ˈdʒɪndʒərbred/| adj.|姜饼色的|Having a warm brownish color like that of gingerbread.|例句：The house had gingerbread trim around the windows.  房子窗户周围有姜饼色的装饰。

例句：He bought a gingerbread-colored teddy bear. 他买了一个姜饼色的泰迪熊。|近义词：ginger, cinnamon, tan|反义词: (No direct antonym, potentially "white", "pale")|词性:adjective

词源:
From Middle English *gingbred, from Old French gingebras, from Medieval Latin gingiber, from Latin zingiberi, from Ancient Greek ζιγγίβερις (zingíberis), from Prakrit *siṃgivera, from Sanskrit शृङ्गवेर (śṛṅgavera, “ginger root”).

注意事项:
Gingerbread can be both a noun and an adjective.
