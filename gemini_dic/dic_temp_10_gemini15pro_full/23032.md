boulevarded:/ˈbuːləvɑːrdɪd/
adjective
林荫大道化的；有林荫大道的
lined with boulevards; having boulevards
The boulevarded streets of Paris are famous. 巴黎林荫大道化的街道很有名。
The city became more boulevarded after the renovations.  翻新之后，这座城市有了更多的林荫大道。
Synonyms: tree-lined, leafy, grand
Antonyms: barren, desolate

Etymology:
From boulevard + -ed.  "Boulevard" originates from Middle Dutch  bolwerc, meaning "bulwark" or "rampart." Over time, the term shifted to refer to the open spaces created when city walls were dismantled, which were often planted with trees and became promenades.
