oxygenator:/ˈɒksɪdʒəneɪtər/ (UK) /ˈɑːksɪdʒəneɪtər/ (US)| n. |氧合器；人工肺|A device that adds oxygen to and removes carbon dioxide from the blood, used during heart surgery or when the lungs are unable to function properly.|The surgeon carefully monitored the oxygenator during the bypass procedure. 外科医生在搭桥手术中仔细监测氧合器。|Synonyms: heart-lung machine, artificial lung|Antonyms: N/A|noun
