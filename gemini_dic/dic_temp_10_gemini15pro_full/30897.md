chagatai:/tʃɑːɡəˈtaɪ/
n.|察合台语|A Turkic language of Central Asia, spoken in parts of Uzbekistan and Xinjiang. It flourished as a literary language from the 15th to the 20th centuries.|Babur, founder of the Mughal Empire, wrote his memoirs, the Baburnama, in Chagatai.|近义词:Turkic, Jagatai|反义词: (No direct antonym for a language)|词性:名词
