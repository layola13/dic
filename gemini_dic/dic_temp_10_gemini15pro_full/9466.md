antirenter:/ˌæntiˈrɛntər/
n.|反租佃农；反对地租者|A person who opposes or refuses to pay rent, especially a tenant farmer protesting against high rents or unfair tenancy conditions.|The antirenters of the 19th century fought for land ownership.|近义词:protestor, objector, dissident|反义词:landlord, tenant (who pays rent)|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + renter.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposed to."  "Renter" refers to someone who pays rent for the use of property. The term gained prominence during the Anti-Rent War in New York in the mid-19th century.
