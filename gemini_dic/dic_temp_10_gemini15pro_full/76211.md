fusty:/ˈfʌsti/ | adj. |  霉味的；陈腐的；守旧的 | Smelling stale, damp, and stuffy, as from being long closed; old-fashioned and out-of-date in attitude or style. | The room had a fusty smell, like old books and dust.  房间里有一股霉味，像是旧书和灰尘的味道。 | The library was dark and fusty. 图书馆里光线昏暗，充满霉味。 | 近义词: musty, stale, old-fashioned, stuffy, antiquated | 反义词: fresh, modern, airy | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:  From Middle English *fouste, *fuste (“tasting stale”), from Old French fust (“wine cask; wood”), from Latin fustis (“staff, club, cudgel”). The sense development is likely from wine casks becoming moldy or musty.
