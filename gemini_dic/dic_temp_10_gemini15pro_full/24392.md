brinkmanship: /ˈbrɪŋkmənʃɪp/ | n. | 边缘政策 | The art or practice of pursuing a dangerous policy to the limits of safety before stopping, especially in politics. |  The two nations engaged in dangerous brinkmanship over the territorial dispute.  两国在领土争端上采取了危险的边缘政策。 | synonyms:  gambling, daredevilry, risk-taking, adventurism, saber-rattling | antonyms: caution, prudence, diplomacy, conciliation, compromise | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From brink (edge) + -manship (skill in handling).  Coined in the 1950s, popularized by US Secretary of State John Foster Dulles.
