dollishly:/ˈdɒlɪʃli/ | adv. | 娇滴滴地，像洋娃娃般地 | In a manner resembling a doll, especially in being pretty and delicate, but sometimes also suggesting artificiality or lack of substance.  | She smiled dollishly at the camera. 她对着镜头娇滴滴地一笑。 | 近义词：sweetly, prettily, coyly, demurely | 反义词：awkwardly, clumsily, gracelessly | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From doll + -ish + -ly.  "Doll" originates from the Middle Dutch "dolle," a pet name or nickname, ultimately from "Adolf."  The suffix "-ish" creates adjectives meaning "like" or "somewhat," while "-ly" forms adverbs.
