hypermodernity:/ˌhaɪpərˌməˈdɜːrnɪti/ |n.| 超现代性 |A cultural condition characterized by an intensification of modernity, marked by features like globalization, information overload, technological advancement, and a fragmented sense of self. It builds upon and extends the concepts of modernity, often highlighting its paradoxes and anxieties.| The constant bombardment of information in hypermodernity can be overwhelming.  在超现代社会中，持续的信息轰炸可能会让人难以承受。| Hypermodernity has led to a decline in traditional social structures. 超现代性导致了传统社会结构的衰落。| 近义词: postmodernity, late modernity | 反义词: premodernity, tradition | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From hyper- + modernity.  "Hyper-" is a Greek prefix meaning "over, above, beyond," and "modernity" refers to the historical period marked by a break from traditional ways of life and the embrace of reason, science, and progress.
