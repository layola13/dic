lock-off: /ˈlɒk ɒf/ (UK) /ˈlɑːk ɔːf/ (US)| n.| 锁定；固定点；锁止|A lock-off is a climbing technique where a climber establishes a stable position on the rock face, usually by wedging themselves in a crack or using a good handhold, and then uses this position as a rest point or to clip into protection without letting go.  In filmmaking, it refers to a static shot where the camera remains fixed in one position. | In climbing: "The climber used a lock-off to rest and place protection."  In filmmaking: "The director used a lock-off to create a sense of stillness and observation."|Near synonyms: hold, stance, fixed shot, static shot|Opposite: dynamic shot, moving shot|noun


lock-off: /ˈlɒk ɒf/ (UK) /ˈlɑːk ɔːf/ (US)| v.| 锁定；固定|To secure something in a fixed position. In climbing, to maintain a hold without changing hand position. In filmmaking, to fix the camera in a specific position. |"He locked off the camera to capture the entire scene without movement." "The climber locked off his left arm while reaching for the next hold."|Near synonyms: secure, fix, stabilize|Opposite: release, loosen|verb
