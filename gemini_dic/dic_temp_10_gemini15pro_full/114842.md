mammed: /ˈmæmd/ | adj. | 喂饱的，吃饱了的 | Fed to satiety; full.  Having eaten a large meal.  |  The mammed guests lounged contentedly after the feast.  吃饱了的客人们在宴会后心满意足地 lounging着。 | Synonyms: satiated, full, replete, gorged | Antonyms: hungry, starving, empty | adjective

Etymology: Likely derived from the verb "mamm" (now obsolete), meaning "to eat greedily" or "to cram with food," possibly of imitative origin.  Similar to "munch" or "champ."
