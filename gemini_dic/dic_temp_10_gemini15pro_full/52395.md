didal: /ˈdɪdəl/ | n. |顶针; 指套| A protective cap worn on the finger, usually made of metal or leather, used in sewing to push the needle through fabric. | She used a didal to protect her finger while sewing the thick leather. 她用顶针保护手指，缝制厚厚的皮革。|  She pricked her finger with the needle even though she was wearing a didal. 即使戴着顶针，她的手指还是被针扎破了。 |Synonyms: thimble | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym) | Noun
