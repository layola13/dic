indo-saracenic:/ˌɪndoʊˌsærəˈsɛnɪk/| adj.| 印度-撒拉逊式的|A style of architecture that blended Islamic and Indian elements, particularly prevalent in 19th-century British India.|The Gateway of India is a prime example of Indo-Saracenic architecture.  印度门是印度-撒拉逊式建筑的典型例子。|Indo-Islamic, Mughal-Gothic| None| adjective
Etymology->
From *Indo-* (indicating India) + *Saracenic* (referring to Islamic architecture, derived from a historical term for Muslims).  源自 *Indo-* (指印度) + *Saracenic* (指伊斯兰建筑，源自历史上对穆斯林的称呼).
