flirtish: /ˈflɜːrtɪʃ/ (US) /ˈflɜːtɪʃ/ (UK) | adj. | 轻浮的，调情的 |  Suggestive of flirting; playful and romantic or sexual in a lighthearted way.  | He gave her a flirtish glance across the room. 他隔着房间给了她一个轻浮的眼神。 | 近义词: playful, teasing, coquettish, flirtatious | 反义词: serious, reserved, modest, shy | 词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From flirt + -ish.  "Flirt" originally referred to a quick, jerky movement, later evolving to mean a person who behaves amorously without serious intent, and finally to the act of behaving in such a way.  The suffix "-ish" adds the meaning "somewhat" or "having the qualities of."
