chromid: /ˈkrəʊmɪd/ | n. | 染色体外DNA，染色粒 | A bacterial chromosome-like structure that carries essential genes but replicates independently of the main chromosome. |  Example 1: Some bacteria have chromids that contain antibiotic resistance genes. 一些细菌拥有含有抗生素抗性基因的染色粒。

Example 2: The presence of a chromid can provide a selective advantage to bacteria in certain environments.  染色粒的存在可以为细菌在某些环境中提供选择优势。 | Synonym: megaplasmid, secondary chromosome | Antonym: chromosome | noun
