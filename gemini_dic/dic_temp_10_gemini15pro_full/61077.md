elliptic:/ɪˈlɪptɪk/ /iˈlɪptɪk/| adj.|椭圆的；省略的|Oval or oblong, with rounded ends. Of, relating to, or having the form of an ellipse. Also, containing an ellipsis; marked by ellipsis.|例句：The planet follows an elliptic path around the sun. 这颗行星绕太阳运行的轨道是椭圆形的。

例句：Her speech was elliptic, jumping from one topic to another. 她的讲话是省略的，从一个话题跳到另一个话题。|近义词：oval, oblong, elliptical|反义词:circular, round|词性:形容词

ellipse:/ɪˈlɪps/ /iˈlɪps/| n.|椭圆；省略|A regular oval shape, traced by a point moving in a plane so that the sum of its distances from two other points (the foci) is constant; an elongated circle.  The omission from a sentence or other construction of one or more words that would complete or clarify the construction.|例句：Draw an ellipse with a major axis of 6 cm and a minor axis of 4 cm. 画一个长轴为6厘米，短轴为4厘米的椭圆。

例句：The use of ellipses in the text created a sense of mystery. 文本中省略号的使用营造了一种神秘感。|近义词：oval|反义词:circle|词性:名词

elliptical:/ɪˈlɪptɪkəl/ /iˈlɪptɪkəl/| adj.|椭圆的；省略的|Having the form of an ellipse; elliptic. Using or involving ellipsis.|例句：The elliptical shape of the stadium made it unique. 体育场的椭圆形状使其独一无二。

例句：His elliptical writing style can be difficult to understand. 他省略的写作风格可能很难理解。|近义词：oval, oblong, elliptic|反义词:circular, round|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *ellipticus, from Ancient Greek ἐλλειπτικός (elleiptikós, “defective, failing”), from ἐλλείπω (elleípō, “I fall short, leave out, omit”), from ἐν (en, “in, on”) + λείπω (leípō, “I leave”).
