USAGE->

词源: From Latin *fervidus*, from *fervere* "to boil, glow."

用法说明: "Fervidities" is the plural form of "fervidity," and less commonly used than "fervid" or "fervidity." It refers to multiple instances of intense and passionate feelings.  While grammatically correct, it can sound somewhat formal or literary.  It's often used in more poetic or elevated contexts.  In most cases, using synonyms like "passions" or "enthusiasms" would be more natural and readily understood.
