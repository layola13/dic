career: /kəˈrɪr/ (UK) /kəˈrɪr/ (US)| n. |职业，事业；生涯；快速的运动|A profession for which one trains and which is pursued as a permanent calling.|She had a long and distinguished career in medicine. 她有着漫长而卓越的医学事业。|近义词：profession, occupation, vocation, calling|反义词: unemployment, idleness, inactivity|词性: noun

Etymology: From the French "carrière" meaning "racecourse, career," from the Italian "carriera," from "carro" meaning "car," ultimately from the Gaulish "karros." Originally referred to a racecourse for chariots, later broadened to mean a general course of action or progress through life.
