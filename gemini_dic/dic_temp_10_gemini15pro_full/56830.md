dormouse:/ˈdɔːrmaʊs/ (UK) /ˈdɔːrˌmaʊs/ (US)| n. |睡鼠|A small rodent that hibernates in the winter, characterized by its furry tail and large eyes.  It belongs to the family Gliridae.|The dormouse curled up in a ball and went to sleep. 睡鼠蜷成一团睡着了。|Synonyms: Hazel dormouse, garden dormouse, edible dormouse|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for an animal, but perhaps "active rodent" in the context of hibernation)|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dormous*, from unrecorded Old English *dormūs*, equivalent to *dorm* (“sleep”) + *mūs* (“mouse”). Cognate with Dutch *dormmuis*, German *Dormmaus*, Swedish *dormus*.
