manichean:/ˌmænɪˈkiːən/ | adj. n.|摩尼教的；二元论的；善恶二元论的| Of or relating to Manichaeism, a religion founded in the 3rd century AD by Mani, which viewed the world as a struggle between good and evil; characterized by a dualistic worldview that sees things in terms of absolute good and evil.  Also used as a noun to refer to a follower of Manichaeism. |例句：His worldview was surprisingly Manichean, dividing everything into stark categories of right and wrong. 他的世界观令人惊讶地带有摩尼教色彩，将一切都简单粗暴地划分为对与错。

|例句：The Manichean scriptures describe a cosmic struggle between light and darkness. 摩尼教经文描述了光明与黑暗之间的宇宙斗争。|近义词：dualistic, absolutist|反义词： nuanced, relativistic|词性: adjective, noun
