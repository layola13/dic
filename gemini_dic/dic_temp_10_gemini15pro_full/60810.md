electropermanent:/ɪˌlɛktroʊˈpɜːrmənənt/| adj.|电永磁的|A type of magnet that can be switched on and off electronically, combining the strengths of permanent magnets and electromagnets. It uses a small electric pulse to change its magnetic state.|Electropermanent magnets are used in robotic grippers, allowing them to pick up and release objects precisely. 电永磁磁铁用于机器人夹爪，使其能够精确地拾取和释放物体。

Electropermanent brakes offer high holding force and fail-safe operation. 电永磁制动器提供高保持力和故障安全操作。|近义词：switchable magnet, controllable magnet|反义词：permanent magnet, electromagnet|词性：adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
electro- (prefix): relating to electricity (from Greek "ēlektron" meaning "amber")
permanent: lasting or intended to last or remain unchanged indefinitely (from Latin "permanens," present participle of "permanere" meaning "to endure")
