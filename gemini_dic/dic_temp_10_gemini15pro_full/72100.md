floccule:/ˈflɒkjuːl/ (US) /ˈflɑːkjuːl/| n. |絮状物；小绒毛|A floccule is a small clump or cluster, especially of a woolly or fluffy substance, or a loosely aggregated mass of fine particles.|The floccules of snow drifted gently down.|Synonym: flake, clump, cluster, aggregate|Antonym: dispersion|noun

flocculate:/ˈflɒkjʊleɪt/ (US) /ˈflɑːkjʊleɪt/| v. |絮凝；结块|To flocculate means to form or cause to form floccules; to aggregate or clump together.|The chemical caused the suspended particles to flocculate.|Synonym: agglomerate, coagulate, clump, cluster|Antonym: disperse, dissolve|verb
