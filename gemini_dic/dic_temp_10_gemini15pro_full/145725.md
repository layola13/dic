overcredulously:/ˌoʊvərˈkrɛdʒələsli/| adv.|过于轻信地|In a manner that is too ready to believe; excessively gullible.|He overcredulously accepted the stranger's story. 他过于轻信地接受了陌生人的故事。|She overcredulously invested in the scheme and lost all her money. 她轻信地投资了这个计划，结果赔光了所有的钱。|近义词：naively, gullibly, unsuspectingly|反义词：skeptically, cynically, suspiciously|词性:副词

词源:
From over- + credulous + -ly.  "Over-"  is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much". "Credulous" comes from the Latin "credulus," meaning "believing easily," from "credere," meaning "to believe."  "-ly" is a suffix that forms adverbs.
