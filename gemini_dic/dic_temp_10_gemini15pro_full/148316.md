oversophisticated:/ˌōvər səˈfɪstɪˌkeɪtɪd/| adj.| 过于复杂 sophisticated; 过于精致 sophisticated; 过于世故 sophisticated| excessively sophisticated; having or showing an excessive amount of worldly experience and knowledge of fashion and culture.| Her writing style is oversophisticated and difficult to understand. 她的写作风格过于复杂，难以理解。| The decor was a bit oversophisticated for my taste.  这装潢对我来说有点过于精致了。 | 近义词：pretentious, affected, elaborate, complex, overrefined | 反义词：simple, natural, unsophisticated, artless | 词性: adjective

词源:
From over- + sophisticated.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much."  "Sophisticated" comes from the Late Latin word "sophisticatus," past participle of "sophisticare," meaning "to alter, adulterate, or make artificial," derived from the Greek "sophistēs," meaning "wise man" or "sophist."
