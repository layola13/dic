greenage:/ˈɡriːneɪdʒ/| n.|青壮年时期，青春|A period of youth, vigor, or freshness.|例句：He was in the greenage of his life, full of energy and ambition. 他正值青春年华，充满活力和抱负。

例句：The greenage of the forest was evident in the vibrant new growth. 森林的青春活力在充满生机的嫩芽中显而易见。|近义词：youth, prime, adolescence|反义词：old age, senescence|词性:名词

词源:  From "green" (referring to freshness and new growth) + "age" (a period of time).  The term draws a parallel to the vibrant green of new plant growth, symbolizing youth and vitality.
