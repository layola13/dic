embolus:/ˈɛmbələs/ (US), /ˈɛmbələs/ (UK)| n.| 栓子；栓塞物|A blood clot, air bubble, piece of fatty deposit, or other object which has been carried in the bloodstream to lodge in a vessel and cause an embolism.|The doctor feared the embolus would travel to his lungs. 医生担心栓子会跑到他的肺里。|Synonyms: clot, plug, thrombus, blockage|Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, but consider: solvent (for dissolving clots))|noun
