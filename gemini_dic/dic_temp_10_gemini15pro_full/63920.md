epiphyte:/ˈɛpɪˌfaɪt/| n. |附生植物|A plant that grows on another plant, especially one that is not parasitic, deriving its moisture and nutrients from the air and rain and sometimes from debris accumulating around it.|The orchid is an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree branches high in the rainforest canopy.|Synonyms: air plant|Antonyms: terrestrial plant|noun
