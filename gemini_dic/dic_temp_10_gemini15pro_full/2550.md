admissibility:/ədˌmɪsɪˈbɪləti/| n.|可采纳性，可接受性|The admissibility of evidence is a matter for the judge to decide.  证据的可采纳性由法官决定。|The lawyer challenged the admissibility of the confession. 律师对供词的可采纳性提出了质疑。|近义词：acceptability, allowability|反义词：inadmissibility|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *admissiblete*, from Late Latin *admissibilitās*, from Latin *admissibilis* ("admissible") + *-itās* (suffix forming abstract nouns of quality).  *admissibilis* from *admittō* ("admit") + *-bilis* (suffix forming adjectives meaning “able to be”).

USAGE->
The admissibility of the evidence will be determined by the judge. = 法官将决定证据的可采纳性。

The defense questioned the admissibility of the witness's testimony. = 辩方质疑证人证词的可采纳性。

The admissibility of certain types of evidence can vary depending on the jurisdiction. = 某些类型证据的可采纳性可能因司法管辖区而异。

The lawyer argued for the admissibility of the photographs. = 律师主张照片的可采纳性。
