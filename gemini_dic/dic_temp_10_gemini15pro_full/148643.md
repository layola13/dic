overthriftinesses: /ˌoʊvərˈθrɪftɪnəsɪz/
| n. |过度节俭，极度吝啬 | The state of being excessively frugal or stingy. |  His overthriftinesses bordered on miserliness, refusing even to buy new socks when his old ones had holes. 他的过度节俭近乎吝啬，即使旧袜子破了洞也不愿买新的。| Synonyms: parsimony, miserliness, stinginess, frugality | Antonyms: extravagance, generosity, prodigality | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over" (excessive) + "thriftiness" (the quality of being thrifty).  "Thrift" comes from Old Norse "þrif" meaning prosperity, success.  The suffix "-iness" transforms the adjective "thrifty" into the noun "thriftiness."  The plural suffix "-es" indicates multiple instances of this quality.
