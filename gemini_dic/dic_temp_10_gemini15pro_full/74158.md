founds:/faʊndz/
verb
建立，创立
establishes or creates (an institution, organization, etc.), typically by providing an endowment.
He founded a school for underprivileged children. 他为贫困儿童创办了一所学校。
近义词:establishes, creates, sets up, institutes
反义词:dissolves, abolishes, terminates
词性:verb

founds:/faʊndz/
verb (third-person singular simple present founds, present participle founding, simple past and past participle founded)
(often followed by "on" or "upon") to base something firmly on; establish on a solid basis.
The company was founded on the principles of innovation and customer satisfaction. 这家公司建立在创新和客户满意度的原则之上。
近义词:bases, grounds, roots
反义词:undermines
词性:verb


词源：From Middle English *founden, from Anglo-Norman funder, from Old French fonder, from Latin fundāre (“to found, lay the bottom, establish, build”), from fundus (“bottom, foundation”).
