admonish: /ədˈmɒnɪʃ/ (UK) /ˌædˈmɑːnɪʃ/ (US)
verb
劝诫；警告；责备
to warn someone of something to be avoided; to gently scold or reprimand someone.
The teacher admonished the students for talking during the exam. 老师告诫学生们考试期间不要讲话。

The librarian admonished him for being too loud. 图书管理员警告他不要太大声。

近义词: reprimand, rebuke, caution, warn, advise, counsel
反义词: praise, commend, encourage
词性: verb

USAGE->
一般现在时:
I admonish my children to be polite to others. = 我劝诫我的孩子们要对别人有礼貌。
He admonishes his employees to arrive on time. = 他告诫他的员工要准时到达。

一般过去时:
The coach admonished the team for their poor performance. = 教练因队伍表现不佳而责备他们。
My mother admonished me for not cleaning my room. = 我妈妈因为我没有打扫房间而责备我。

现在完成时:
The principal has admonished the student body several times about the dress code. = 校长已经多次就着装规范告诫学生了。
I have admonished him about his careless driving. = 我已经警告过他关于他粗心驾驶的问题了。

过去完成时:
She had admonished him about his spending habits before he went bankrupt. = 在他破产之前，她曾告诫过他关于他的消费习惯。
The teacher had admonished the class about cheating before the exam. = 考试前，老师已经警告过全班关于作弊的问题。

将来时:
I will admonish him if he continues to be late. = 如果他继续迟到，我会警告他。
The manager will admonish the employees about the new safety regulations. = 经理将就新的安全规定告诫员工。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would admonish him about his behavior. = 如果我是你，我会告诫他关于他的行为。
I wish I had admonished her about the dangers of that neighborhood. = 我希望我曾警告过她那个街区的危险。

被动语态:
He was admonished by his boss for missing the deadline. = 他因为错过截止日期而被老板责备。
The students were admonished for their disruptive behavior. = 学生们因为他们的破坏性行为而受到警告。

疑问句:
Why did the teacher admonish the student? = 老师为什么告诫那个学生？
Have you admonished him about the risks involved? = 你警告过他相关的风险吗？

否定句:
I did not admonish him, I praised him. = 我没有告诫他，我表扬了他。
She hasn't admonished them yet. = 她还没有告诫他们。


PHRASE->

admonish someone for something = 因某事而告诫某人
admonish someone to do something = 劝诫某人去做某事
be admonished by someone = 被某人告诫
severely admonish = 严厉地告诫
