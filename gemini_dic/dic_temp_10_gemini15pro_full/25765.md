burden:/ˈbɜːrdən/ (US) /ˈbɜːdn/ (UK)| n. v.|负担，重担;  加重压于，烦扰|A burden is a heavy load, often a difficult responsibility.  As a verb, it means to load heavily or to trouble someone with something difficult. |例句：The burden of proof lies with the prosecution.  举证责任在于控方。|近义词：load, weight, encumbrance, onus, responsibility|反义词: relief, advantage, asset|n.

burden:/ˈbɜːrdən/ (US) /ˈbɜːdn/ (UK)| v.|加重压于，烦扰|To burden someone is to weigh them down with obligations or difficulties.|例句：I don't want to burden you with my problems. 我不想用我的问题来烦扰你。|近义词：load, encumber, oppress, tax, trouble|反义词: unburden, relieve, ease|v.
