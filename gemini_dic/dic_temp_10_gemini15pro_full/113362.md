lycanthrope:/ˈlaɪkənˌθroʊp/ (US), /ˈlaɪkənˌθrəʊp/ (UK)| n.|狼人；变狼狂患者|A mythical or folklore figure who transforms into a wolf, either purposely or involuntarily.|The villagers feared the lycanthrope that roamed the forest at night. 村民们害怕晚上在森林里游荡的狼人。|Synonyms: werewolf, wolfman|Antonyms: human|noun

lycanthropy:/laɪˈkænθrəpi/ (US), /laɪˈkænθrəpi/ (UK)| n.|变狼狂；狼人状态|The supernatural transformation of a human into a wolf, or a belief in such a transformation.|Lycanthropy is a common theme in horror literature. 变狼狂是恐怖文学中的常见主题。|Synonyms: werewolfism|Antonyms: None|noun

lycanthropist:/laɪˈkænθrəpɪst/ (US), /laɪˈkænθrəpɪst/ (UK)| n.|狼人；变狼狂患者|A person afflicted with or believed to be afflicted with lycanthropy.|The lycanthropist was said to prowl the streets under the full moon. 据说这个狼人在满月时会在街上徘徊。|Synonyms: werewolf, wolfman|Antonyms: human|noun
