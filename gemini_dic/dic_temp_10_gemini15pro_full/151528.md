parsimoniously:/ˌpɑːrsɪˈməʊniəsli/ | adv. | 吝啬地，节俭地 | In a way that is very unwilling to spend money or use resources. | She parsimoniously used the last of the flour to make a small loaf of bread. 她节俭地用最后一点面粉做了一个小面包。|  He dressed parsimoniously, wearing old, patched clothes. 他穿着朴素，穿着打补丁的旧衣服。 | 近义词: stingily, frugally, economically | 反义词: extravagantly, lavishly, generously | 词性: 副词

parsimonious:/ˌpɑːrsɪˈməʊniəs/ | adj. | 吝啬的，节俭的 | Unwilling to spend money or use resources; stingy or frugal. |  He was parsimonious in his praise of the project. 他对这个项目的赞扬很吝啬。| The company's parsimonious approach to employee benefits led to low morale. 公司对员工福利的吝啬态度导致士气低落。| 近义词: stingy, frugal, thrifty | 反义词: generous, extravagant, prodigal | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Late Latin *parsimoniosus, from Latin parsimonia (“frugality”), from parcere (“to spare”) + -monia (suffix forming abstract nouns).
