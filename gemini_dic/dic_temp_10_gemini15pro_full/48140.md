decollectivizing: /diːkəˈlɛktɪvaɪzɪŋ/
verb
非集体化，去集体化
The process of dismantling or reversing collectivization, typically referring to the transfer of ownership and control of land or businesses from collective or state ownership to private individuals.
The government began decollectivizing agriculture in the 1990s. 政府在20世纪90年代开始农业非集体化。
Many farmers resisted decollectivizing, fearing they would lose the support of the collective. 许多农民抵制非集体化，担心他们会失去集体的支持。
近义词: privatizing, individualizing
反义词: collectivizing, nationalizing
词性: verb (present participle)

词源:  de- (prefix meaning "reverse" or "remove") + collectivize + -ing (present participle suffix).  "Collectivize" comes from "collective" + "-ize" (suffix meaning "to make or become").  Ultimately from Latin "colligere" (to gather together).
