observer:/əbˈzɜːrvər/ (US) /əbˈzɜːvə(r)/ (UK)| n.|观察者；观察员；奉行者；遵守者|A person who watches or notices something; someone who attends something as a spectator; someone who follows or adheres to something (a law, custom, etc.).|例句：The UN sent observers to monitor the elections. 联合国派遣观察员监督选举。|例句：He is a keen observer of human nature. 他是一个敏锐的人性观察者。|近义词：spectator, onlooker, witness, monitor, follower|反义词:participant, perpetrator|noun

observer:/əbˈzɜːrvər/ (US) /əbˈzɜːvə(r)/ (UK)| n.|观察家|Someone who is skilled at noticing things, especially small details.|例句：She's a shrewd observer of the political scene. 她是一位精明的政治观察家。|例句：As a casual observer, I noticed a subtle change in her demeanor. 作为一个不经意的观察者，我注意到她举止上的细微变化。|近义词：analyst, commentator, pundit|反义词:ignoramus, oblivious person|noun
