inthrall:/ɪnˈθrɔːl/ /ɪnˈθrɑːl/ | vt. | 迷住，吸引 | to hold the complete attention of (someone) as if by magic; fascinate | The magician's performance inthralled the audience. 魔术师的表演迷住了观众。 |  The storyteller inthralled the children with his tales of adventure.  讲故事的人用他的冒险故事吸引了孩子们。 | 近义词：captivate, fascinate, mesmerize, enchant | 反义词：bore, repel, disgust | 词性：动词

词源:
Late Middle English: from en- ‘in’ + thrall ‘slave’. The original sense was ‘enslave’, later ‘hold spellbound’.
