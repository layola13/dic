co-operative:/kəʊˈɒpərətɪv/| n. adj.|合作社，合作的|An organization or business owned and run jointly by its members, who share the profits or benefits.  A co-operative approach involves people working together to achieve a common goal.|例句：The local farmers formed a co-operative to sell their produce. 当地的农民成立了一个合作社来销售他们的产品。

|近义词：collective, joint, shared, communal|反义词:private, individual|词性:名词, 形容词

co-operatives:/kəʊˈɒpərətɪvz/| n. |合作社|The plural form of "co-operative".|例句：Many co-operatives are based on the principles of democratic control and shared ownership. 许多合作社都建立在民主控制和共有所有权的原则之上。


|近义词：collectives|反义词:corporations|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the late 18th century, from co- + operative.  The word "operative" itself comes from Latin "operari" meaning "to work".
