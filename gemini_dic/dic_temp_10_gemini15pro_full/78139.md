geometries:/dʒiˈɒmɪtriz/
noun
几何学(复数)
The branch of mathematics concerned with the properties and relations of points, lines, surfaces, solids, and higher dimensional analogs.
Non-Euclidean geometries are used in Einstein's theory of relativity. 非欧几里得几何学用于爱因斯坦的相对论。
Synonyms:  configurations, shapes, forms
Antonyms: shapelessness, formlessness
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *geometria, from Ancient Greek γεωμετρία (geōmetría, “geometry, land-measuring”), from γῆ (gê, “earth, land”) + μέτρον (métron, “measure”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *méh₁-trom (“instrument for measuring”).
