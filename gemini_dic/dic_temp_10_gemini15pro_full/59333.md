earl:/ɜːrl/ | n. |伯爵|A British nobleman ranking above a viscount and below a marquess.|例句：The Earl of Essex was a favorite of Queen Elizabeth I. 埃塞克斯伯爵是伊丽莎白一世女王的宠臣。|例句：He was given the title of Earl after his distinguished service to the crown.  由于他对王室的杰出贡献，他被授予了伯爵爵位。|近义词：nobleman, peer, count |反义词: commoner| noun

earlet:/ˈɜːrlɪt/ | n. |小伯爵（伯爵的幼子或继承人）|A young earl, or the son of an earl.|例句：The young earlet was known for his mischievous behavior.  这位小伯爵以他顽皮的行为而出名。|例句：The earlet accompanied his father to the royal court. 小伯爵陪同他的父亲去了王室法庭。|近义词：young nobleman, heir |反义词: N/A| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *eorl, from Old Norse jarl, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *erlaz, meaning "man, warrior, nobleman."
