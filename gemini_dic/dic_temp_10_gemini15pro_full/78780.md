gillers:/ˈɡɪlərz/| n.| 鳃, (尤指鱼的)呼吸器官; 峡谷;  (蘑菇的)菌褶;  (某些植物的)裂片 |Gillers are the respiratory organs of fish, allowing them to extract oxygen from water. 鱼鳃是鱼类的呼吸器官，使它们能够从水中提取氧气。|例句：The fish opened its gillers to breathe. 鱼张开鳃呼吸。

例句：The deep gillers of the mushroom were clearly visible. 蘑菇的深褶清晰可见。|近义词：branchiae (technical), lamellae (for mushrooms)|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider lungs in a broader biological context) |词性:noun


gillers:/ˈɡɪlərz/| n.| 骗子; 欺诈者; 诡计多端的人| Gillers are people who deceive others for personal gain. 骗子是为个人利益欺骗他人的人。|例句：The gillers tricked the old lady out of her savings. 骗子骗走了老太太的积蓄。


例句：Beware of gillers who promise easy money.  小心那些承诺轻松赚钱的骗子。|近义词：fraudsters, swindlers, cheats, con artists|反义词: honest person, trustworthy individual |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *gilleres*, from Old English *gilla*,  meaning "gill" (the respiratory organ). The slang meaning of "deceiver" or "trickster" is of uncertain origin, possibly related to the idea of catching someone like a fish with gills.
