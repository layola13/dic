didacticism:/ˌdɪdˈæktɪsɪzəm/
n.
教导主义；教训主义
Designed or intended to teach people something, especially a moral lesson.  Focusing on instruction often at the expense of entertainment.
The didacticism in Aesop's fables is obvious, but children still enjoy them.  伊索寓言中的教诲意味很明显，但孩子们仍然喜欢它们。
近义词：moralizing, preachiness, instructiveness
反义词：amusement, entertainment, lightheartedness
词性: 名词

didactic:/daɪˈdæktɪk/
adj.
教诲的；说教的
Intended to teach, particularly in having moral instruction as an ulterior motive.
The professor's lectures were didactic, focusing on conveying information and principles.  教授的讲座很教条，注重传授信息和原则。
近义词：instructive, educational, pedagogic
反义词：uninformative, entertaining, lightheartedness
词性: 形容词
