anticoagulative:/ˌænˌtɪkoʊˈæɡjʊlətɪv/
adjective
抗凝的，抗凝血的
Preventing or reducing coagulation of the blood.
例句：The doctor prescribed an anticoagulative medication to prevent blood clots. 医生开了抗凝药物来预防血栓。

例句：Patients undergoing surgery often receive anticoagulative therapy. 接受手术的病人通常会接受抗凝治疗。

近义词：antithrombotic, anticlotting
反义词：coagulant, procoagulant
词性：adjective


anticoagulant:/ˌænˌtɪkoʊˈæɡjʊlənt/
noun
抗凝剂，抗凝血剂
A substance that prevents or reduces coagulation of the blood.
例句：Warfarin is a commonly used anticoagulant. 华法林是一种常用的抗凝剂。

例句：He was prescribed an anticoagulant after his heart attack. 他在心脏病发作后被开了抗凝剂。


近义词：antithrombotic, anticlotting agent
反义词：coagulant, procoagulant
词性：noun


词源：anti- (against) + coagulant (causing clotting)  From Latin 'coagulāre' meaning to curdle or clot.
