demonised:/ˈdiːmənaɪzd/| adj.|被妖魔化的|Having been portrayed as wicked or threatening.|The media demonised the striking workers. 媒体把罢工工人妖魔化了。|近义词：vilified, slandered, denigrated|反义词：praised, lauded, glorified|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From demon + -ise.  "Demon" comes from the Greek "daimon," originally referring to a spirit or divine power, later acquiring negative connotations in Christian contexts. "-ise" (or "-ize") is a suffix used to form verbs and, subsequently, adjectives, meaning "to make or become like."
