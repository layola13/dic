acrobat: /ˈækrəˌbæt/ | n. | 杂技演员；特技演员；立场善变的人 | a skilled performer of gymnastic feats, typically one who works in a circus.  A person who holds varying or inconsistent opinions. | The acrobat wowed the crowd with his daring feats.  这位杂技演员以其大胆的技艺使观众惊叹。

The political acrobat shifted his stance yet again. 这位立场善变的政客又一次改变了他的立场。 | 近义词：gymnast, tumbler, aerialist | 反义词：couch potato, layman | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *acrobate*, from Greek *akrobates* "rope-dancer, one who walks on tiptoe," from *akron* "extremity, summit" + *bainen* "to go, walk, step."  The sense of "one who shifts opinions" is first recorded in 1860.
