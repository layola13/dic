displaced:/dɪsˈpleɪst/ | adj. | 流离失所的，被取代的 | forced to leave their home, typically by war, persecution, or natural disaster; removed from its usual or proper place | Many people were displaced by the flood. 洪水使许多人流离失所。 | synonyms: uprooted, exiled, refugee, evacuee | antonyms: settled, established | adjective

displaced:/dɪsˈpleɪst/ | v. | 取代，置换 | take over the place, position, or role of (someone or something). | The new technology displaced the old methods. 新技术取代了旧方法。| synonyms: replace, supersede, supplant | antonyms: reinstate, restore | verb

词源：
From dis- + place + -ed.  "Dis-" prefix indicating reversal or negation, and "place" meaning to put in a particular position.


注意事项:
displaced 既可以用作动词的过去分词形式，也可以用作形容词。上下文语境有助于区分其用法。
