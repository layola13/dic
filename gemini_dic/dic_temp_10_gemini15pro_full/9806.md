anxiety:/æŋˈzaɪəti/ /æŋˈzaɪɪti/| n.|焦虑，不安，担忧|A feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease, typically about an imminent event or something with an uncertain outcome.|She felt a surge of anxiety before the exam. 她考试前感到一阵焦虑。|近义词：worry, concern, apprehension, angst, nervousness, unease, distress, dread|反义词：calm, tranquility, peace, serenity, composure|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *anxietatem, from Latin anxietas (“uneasiness, anxiety”), from anxius (“solicitous, uneasy, troubled”), from angere (“to cause distress, torment, strangle”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂enǵʰ- (“narrow, tight; distress”). Cognate with Old English enge (“narrow”).
