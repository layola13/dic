citadel: /ˈsɪtədəl/ | n. |城堡；要塞；堡垒|A fortress, typically on high ground, protecting or dominating a city.|The citadel overlooked the entire valley.  城堡俯瞰着整个山谷。|Example: The soldiers retreated to the citadel for safety. 士兵们撤退到城堡寻求安全。|Synonyms: fortress, stronghold, bastion, fort|Antonyms:  none (lack of a direct antonym, could consider "vulnerable point")|Noun


citadel: /ˈsɪtədəl/ | n. |核心；中心；大本营 (比喻义)|A place or situation in which something is most strongly established or defended.|The university is a citadel of learning. 这所大学是学习的中心。|Example: The company's headquarters served as its citadel of power. 公司总部是其权力中心。|Synonyms: center, hub, core, headquarters|Antonyms: periphery, outskirts|Noun
