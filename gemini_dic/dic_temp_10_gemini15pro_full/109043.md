legal-tender:/ˈliːɡl ˈtɛndər/ | n. |法定货币；法币|A legal tender is a medium of payment recognized by a legal system to be valid for meeting a financial obligation.  It is anything recognized by law as a means to settle a public or private debt or meet a financial obligation, including tax payments, contracts, and legal judgments.|U.S. dollars are legal tender in the United States. 美元在美国是法定货币。|Synonyms: legal currency, lawful money|Antonyms: counterfeit money,  unofficial currency|noun
