layabout:/ˈleɪəbaʊt/
n.|懒汉，游手好闲的人|A person who habitually avoids work; an idler.|He's a layabout who never does a day's work. 他是个懒汉，从来不工作。|近义词：loafer, idler, slacker|反义词：workaholic, hard worker|词性: 名词

词源:
From the verb phrase "lie about," meaning to spend time idly or doing nothing. "Lie" in this context retains its older meaning of "to recline" or "to remain in a particular place."
