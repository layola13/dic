emulsifying: /ɪˈmʌlsɪfaɪɪŋ/
| adj. | 乳化的 |  Making or becoming an emulsion; capable of causing emulsification. |  This emulsifying agent helps to combine oil and water.  这种乳化剂有助于油和水的结合。 | emulsifying, mixing, blending | separating, dividing | adjective

emulsify: /ɪˈmʌlsɪfaɪ/
| vt. | 乳化 | To make a liquid into an emulsion. |  The chef emulsified the oil and vinegar to make a salad dressing. 厨师将油和醋乳化制成沙拉酱。 | mix, blend, combine, homogenize | separate, divide | verb

emulsion: /ɪˈmʌlʃən/
| n. | 乳状液 | A mixture of two or more liquids that are normally immiscible (unblendable). | Milk is an emulsion of fat and water. 牛奶是脂肪和水的乳状液。 | mixture, blend, suspension | solution, separation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *émulsion*, from Latin *ēmulsiō* (“a milking out”), from *ēmulgēre* (“to milk out”), from *ex* (“out”) + *mulgēre* (“to milk”).
