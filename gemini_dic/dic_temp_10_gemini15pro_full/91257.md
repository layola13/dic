hydrofluorocarbons:/ˌhaɪdroʊˈflʊrəˌkɑːrbənz/ | n. | 氢氟烃 |  A group of organic compounds that contain hydrogen, fluorine, and carbon atoms. They are often used as refrigerants and propellants, replacing chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) which deplete the ozone layer. | Hydrofluorocarbons are potent greenhouse gases. 氢氟烃是强效的温室气体。| Synonyms: HFCs | Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, but alternatives include natural refrigerants like propane and ammonia) | noun
