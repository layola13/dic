logophile: /ˈlɒɡəfaɪl/ (British) /ˈlɑːɡəfaɪl/ (American)| n. | 爱词者；词汇爱好者 | A lover of words.  A person who finds pleasure in words and language. | She's a true logophile, always reading the dictionary for fun. 她是一位真正的词汇爱好者，总是以阅读词典为乐。 |  He impressed everyone with his logophilic tendencies, effortlessly weaving intricate words into his conversation. 他毫不费力地将复杂的词汇融入到他的谈话中，用他热爱词汇的倾向给每个人都留下了深刻的印象。| 近义词: word lover, lexicographer (more formal, denotes a dictionary compiler) | 反义词: (No direct antonym, but one could consider someone who dislikes or avoids complex language) | 词性: noun
