decolonization:/diːˌkɒlənaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 去殖民化；非殖民化 | The process by which colonies become independent or self-governing. |  The decolonization of Africa occurred largely in the mid-20th century.  非洲的非殖民化主要发生在20世纪中叶。| synonyms: liberation, independence, emancipation | antonyms: colonization, imperialism | noun


decolonize:/diːˈkɒlənaɪz/ | v. | 去殖民化；使非殖民化 | To free a colony from dependent status. |  Many countries in Asia were decolonized after World War II.  二战后，亚洲许多国家摆脱了殖民统治。| synonyms: liberate, free, emancipate | antonyms: colonize, subjugate | verb


decolonizing:/diːˈkɒlənaɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 去殖民化的；非殖民化的 | Relating to the process of decolonization. | The decolonizing struggles of the 20th century reshaped the global political landscape. 20世纪的非殖民化斗争重塑了全球政治格局。 | synonyms: liberating, freeing, emancipating | antonyms: colonizing, subjugating | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- + colonize + -ation.  "De-" is a prefix meaning "remove" or "reverse". "Colonize" comes from the Latin "colonia" meaning "farm, settlement". "-ation" is a suffix forming nouns of action or process.
