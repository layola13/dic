exaggeratedly:/ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪtɪdli/
adverb
夸张地
in an exaggerated manner; to an excessive degree.
She laughed exaggeratedly at his joke. 她夸张地大笑他的笑话。
近义词: dramatically, excessively, hyperbolically, overly
反义词: moderately, subtly, understatedly
词性: 副词

exaggerate:/ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪt/
verb
夸大；夸张
to represent (something) as being larger, greater, better, or worse than it really is.
Don't exaggerate – it wasn't that bad. 不要夸大其词——没那么糟。
近义词: overstate, amplify, embellish, inflate
反义词: understate, downplay, minimize
词性: 动词

Etymology: From Late Latin exaggeratus, past participle of exaggerare "to heap up, increase," from Latin ex- "out" + aggerare "to heap up," from agger "heap."
