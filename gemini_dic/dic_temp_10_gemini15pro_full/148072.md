oversaturating:/ˌoʊvərˈsætʃəreɪtɪŋ/
verb
过度饱和，过分渗透
causing something to become oversaturated.
例句：The market is oversaturating with new products.  市场上充斥着新产品。

例句：Oversaturating the soil with water can damage the roots.  过量浇水会损害植物根部。

近义词：flooding, glutting, drenching
反义词：undersupplying, starving
词性：v. (present participle of oversaturate)


oversaturated:/ˌoʊvərˈsætʃəreɪtɪd/
adjective
过度饱和的，过分渗透的
too full of something; containing more of a substance than can be absorbed or dissolved.
例句：The oversaturated job market makes it difficult for recent graduates to find work.  过度饱和的就业市场让应届毕业生找工作很困难。

例句：The colors in the photograph were oversaturated, making it look artificial.  照片中的颜色过度饱和，看起来很不自然。

近义词：saturated, soaked, glutted
反义词：unsaturated, dry
词性：adj.


oversaturate:/ˌoʊvərˈsætʃəreɪt/
verb
使过度饱和，过分渗透
supply beyond the point of saturation.
例句：They oversaturated the market with cheap imports.  他们用廉价进口商品充斥了市场。

例句：Be careful not to oversaturate the sponge.  小心不要让海绵吸水过饱。

近义词：saturate, flood, glut
反义词：undersupply, starve
词性：v.



词源:
From over- + saturate.  Saturate comes from Latin saturatus, past participle of saturare "to fill full, sate," from satur "full, sated."
