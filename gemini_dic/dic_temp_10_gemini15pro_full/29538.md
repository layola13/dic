catch:/kætʃ/ | v. n. | 接住，抓住；赶上；感染；理解；吸引 | To grasp or seize something, often in motion; to become infected with a disease; to understand something; to be in time for something. | The dog caught the ball in mid-air. 狗在半空中接住了球。| grab, seize, capture, contract, apprehend | miss, lose, release | verb

catch:/kætʃ/ | n. | 抓住；捕获物；锁扣；隐患 | The act of catching; something that is caught; a fastener or latch; a hidden problem or disadvantage. | The fisherman had a good catch today. 渔夫今天的收获不错。| capture, haul, take | loss, release | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English cacchen, from Anglo-Norman cachier, from Late Latin *captiāre, frequentative of Latin capere (“to take, seize”).
