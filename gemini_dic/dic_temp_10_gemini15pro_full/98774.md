intangle:/ɪnˈtæŋɡl/ | v. | 缠住，使纠缠；卷入，使陷入 | To twist or twine together in a confused mass; to involve in a difficult or complicated situation. | The kite string became intangled in the branches of the tree. 风筝线缠在了树枝上。|  The company was intangled in a lengthy legal battle.  这家公司卷入了一场漫长的法律纠纷。 | synonyms: entangle, snarl, tangle | antonyms: disentangle, unravel | verb


intangle:/ɪnˈtæŋɡl/ | n. | 纠缠；混乱 | A confused or complicated state; a tangle. |  The intangle of wires behind the television was a fire hazard. 电视机后面的电线缠成一团，有火灾隐患。 | The political intangle made it difficult to find a solution. 政治上的纠纷使得很难找到解决方案。| synonyms: tangle, mess, confusion | antonyms: order, clarity | noun
