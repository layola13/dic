delusional:/dɪˈluːʒənəl/
adjective
中文翻译：妄想的；错觉的
英文解析：Characterized by or holding delusions, which are fixed, false beliefs that are not based in reality.
例句：He had delusional beliefs about being a secret agent. 他妄想自己是特工。

例句：Her delusional thinking led her to believe she could fly. 她错觉的思维让她相信自己能飞。

近义词：psychotic, deranged, hallucinatory, paranoid, fanciful
反义词：rational, sane, lucid, realistic
词性：adjective

delusion:/dɪˈluːʒən/
noun
中文翻译：妄想；错觉
英文解析：A fixed, false belief that is not based in reality and is held despite evidence to the contrary.
例句：He suffered from delusions of grandeur. 他患有夸大妄想症。

例句：The patient experienced delusions of persecution. 病人经历了被害妄想。

近义词：illusion, misconception, fallacy, fantasy
反义词：reality, truth, fact
词性：noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dēlūsiōn-, from Latin dēlūdō (“I deceive”), from dē- (“down, completely”) + lūdō (“I play, mock”).
