mercantilism: /ˈmɜːrkəntɪlɪzəm/ | n. | 重商主义 | An economic theory and practice common in Europe from the 16th to the 18th centuries that promoted governmental regulation of a nation's economy for the purpose of augmenting state power at the expense of rival national powers. It was characterized by the belief that national wealth and power were best served by increasing exports and collecting precious metals in return. |  The age of exploration was driven in large part by mercantilism.  大航海时代很大程度上是由重商主义驱动的。

Mercantilism was the dominant economic system in Europe for centuries. 重商主义几个世纪以来都是欧洲占主导地位的经济体系。| 近义词: commercialism, protectionism | 反义词: free trade, laissez-faire | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mercantilisme*, from *mercantile* "commercial," from Italian *mercantile*, from *mercante* "merchant," from Latin *mercans* (genitive *mercantis*) "merchant," present participle of *mercari* "to trade," from *merx* (genitive *mercis*) "merchandise."
