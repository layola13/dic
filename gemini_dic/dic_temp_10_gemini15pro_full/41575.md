corporatism: /ˈkɔːrpərətɪzəm/ (UK), /ˈkɔːrpərətɪzəm/ (US)| n.|社团主义; 行会主义|A political ideology or system in which economic and social interests are organized into major interest groups (such as business, labor, and agriculture) and these groups collaborate with the state to regulate the economy and other aspects of public life.|Italy under Mussolini was an example of corporatism. 墨索里尼统治下的意大利是社团主义的一个例子。|Synonyms: syndicalism, guild socialism|Antonyms: individualism, laissez-faire|noun
