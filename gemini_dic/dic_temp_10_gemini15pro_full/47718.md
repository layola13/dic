decadences:/ˈdɛkədənsɪz/
n.
堕落，颓废
moral or cultural decline as characterized by excessive indulgence in pleasure or luxury
The Roman Empire's decline is often attributed to decadences within its ruling class.  罗马帝国的衰落通常归因于其统治阶级的腐朽堕落。

The city was filled with all sorts of decadences. 这个城市充满了各种各样的堕落现象。

近义词: corruption, depravity, dissipation, degeneration
反义词: morality, virtue, uprightness, integrity
词性: 名词 (Noun)

decay:/dɪˈkeɪ/
n. vi. vt.
腐烂；衰败
the state or process of rotting or decomposition
The old house was falling into decay.  这栋老房子正在腐朽。

Tooth decay can be prevented by regular brushing.  经常刷牙可以防止蛀牙。

近义词: rot, decomposition, deterioration
反义词: growth, improvement, development
词性: 名词 (Noun), 动词 (Verb)
