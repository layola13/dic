bearing-up:/ˌbɛərɪŋ ˈʌp/
|phrasal verb|
|振作起来；承受住；顶住|
|To withstand stress or difficulty with strength and resilience.  It implies facing adversity with courage and not giving in to despair.|
|She's bearing up remarkably well under the circumstances. 她在这种情况下表现得非常坚强。|

|He bore up bravely during his illness. 他在生病期间勇敢地承受着。|

|近义词：cope, endure, persevere, withstand|
|反义词：succumb, collapse, break down|
|词性：动词短语|


bearing-up:/ˌbɛərɪŋ ˈʌp/
|noun (rare)|
|振作；坚强；承受力|
|The act of enduring hardship or stress with fortitude.|
|Her bearing-up through the ordeal inspired everyone. 她经受住考验的坚强鼓舞了所有人。|

|His bearing-up in the face of adversity was admirable. 他面对逆境的坚强令人钦佩。|

|近义词：resilience, fortitude, endurance|
|反义词：weakness, fragility, vulnerability|
|词性：名词|


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "bear" (meaning "to carry, endure") + "up" (adverb indicating continuation or support).  "Bear" has roots in Old English "beran," and "up" comes from Old English "up" or "upp."
