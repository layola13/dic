drift:/drɪft/
n.|漂流，漂移；趋势，动向；堆积物|a continuous slow movement from one place to another; a gradual change or development; a mass of something, such as snow, piled up by the wind|The boat drifted down the river. 船顺着河流漂流。|near synonyms: float, wander, trend, tendency|near antonyms: anchor, fix, remain|noun

drift:/drɪft/
vi.|漂流，漂移；漫无目的地移动；逐渐变化|to be carried along by currents of air or water; to move about without a clear purpose or direction; to change gradually|The snow drifted against the wall. 雪堆积在墙边。|near synonyms: float, wander, stray, meander|near antonyms: anchor, fix, remain|verb

drift:/drɪft/
vt.|使漂流，使堆积|to cause something to be carried along by currents of air or water; to pile something up in a mass|The wind drifted the leaves into piles. 风把树叶吹成一堆一堆的。|near synonyms: waft, heap, pile|near antonyms: scatter, disperse|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *drifa (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *drifaną, from Proto-Indo-European *dhreybʰ- (“to turn, to whirl”). Cognate with Icelandic drífa (“to drive, drift”), Faroese dríva (“to drive”), Norwegian Bokmål drive (“to drive”), Norwegian Nynorsk driva (“to drive”), Swedish driva (“to drive, float, drift”), Danish drive (“to drive”).
