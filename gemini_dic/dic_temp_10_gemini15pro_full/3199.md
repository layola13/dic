affectation:/ˌæfɛkˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 做作，矫揉造作 | An unnatural or artificial behavior or mannerism, usually intended to impress. | She spoke with a ridiculous affectation of a French accent. 她说话时带着一种可笑的假法国口音。 |例句：His use of big words was just an affectation to make himself sound smarter. 他使用大词只是为了让自己听起来更聪明的一种矫揉造作。

例句：Her constant sighing was an affectation that annoyed everyone around her. 她不停地叹气，这是一种做作，惹恼了周围的每个人。


|近义词：pretense, artificiality, mannerism, pose |反义词: genuineness, naturalness, sincerity |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *affectatio, from Latin affectatus, past participle of affectare "to strive after, aspire to, aim at, affect," frequentative of afficere "to influence, act on," from ad- "to" + facere "to do, make."
