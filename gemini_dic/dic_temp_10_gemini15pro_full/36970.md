colt:/kəʊlt/ (British), /koʊlt/ (American)|n.|小马；新手；轻机枪|A young male horse, typically less than four years old. Also can refer to an inexperienced person, especially a young athlete or performer, or a type of firearm.|The farmer proudly showed off his prize-winning colt.|The team's star quarterback is a promising young colt.|He carried a Colt .45.|近义词:foal, yearling, novice, rookie|反义词:mare, stallion, veteran, expert|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *colt, from Proto-Germanic *kulþaz. Related to Old Norse kultr.
