neatest:/ˈniːtɪst/| adj.|最整洁的，最整齐的，最干净的|superlative of neat: arranged in a tidy way; in a good order|Her room is always the neatest in the house. 她的房间总是家里最整洁的。|synonyms: tidiest, cleanest, most orderly|antonyms: messiest, dirtiest, most disorderly|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *nete*, from Old English *nēat*, from Proto-Germanic *nautaz* (“useful, of use, advantageous”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵn̥h₂-tó-s* (“known”).  Cognate with Old Frisian *nēte*, Old Saxon *nōti*, Old High German *nuozi* (“useful, diligent”), Gothic *𐌽𐌴𐌸𐍃* (nēþs, “easy, comfortable”). More at need. The sense development is from “suitable, fitting” to “well-arranged, tidy.”
