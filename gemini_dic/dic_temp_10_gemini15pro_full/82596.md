guardianship: /ˈɡɑːrdiənʃɪp/ | n. | 监护权；监护 |The legal right and responsibility of taking care of someone who cannot take care of themselves, such as a child whose parents have died or a person who is mentally incapacitated. | She was granted guardianship of her niece after her sister's death. 她姐姐去世后，她获得了侄女的监护权。|The court awarded him guardianship of his children. 法院授予了他孩子的监护权。| custody, care, protection | neglect, abandonment | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gardein + -ship.  Ultimately from Frankish *warding, from Proto-Germanic *wardōną (“to watch, guard”). Related to warden.
