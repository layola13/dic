chagrin:/ʃəˈɡrɪn/| n. |懊恼，失望，悔恨|A feeling of disappointment and humiliation.|例句：He felt a deep chagrin at having failed the exam. 他考试不及格，感到非常懊恼。

例句：Much to her chagrin, she wasn't invited to the party. 令她懊恼的是，她没有被邀请参加聚会。|近义词：annoyance, vexation, disappointment, humiliation|反义词：delight, joy, satisfaction|词性:noun

词源:From French chagrin, from Old French chagreigner "to make sad, sadden," of unknown origin. Some sources suggest a connection with Old North French chagrin "rough skin, shagreen," perhaps from the notion of having one's skin irritated.
