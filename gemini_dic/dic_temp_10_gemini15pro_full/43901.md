cretinous:/ˈkriːtɪnəs/ | adj. | 愚蠢的，白痴的 |  Suffering from cretinism; very stupid. Showing a lack of intelligence. |  His cretinous behavior embarrassed everyone at the party. 他的愚蠢行为让聚会上的每个人都感到尴尬。 | Synonyms: stupid, idiotic, imbecilic, moronic, foolish | Antonyms: intelligent, smart, bright, clever, wise | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *crétin*, from Franco-Provençal *cretin*, from Vulgar Latin *christianus* (“Christian”), literally meaning "Christian, human being," used euphemistically for "poor afflicted person not responsible for their actions."  The condition of cretinism was common in areas of iodine deficiency, and sufferers were often taken in by monasteries.
