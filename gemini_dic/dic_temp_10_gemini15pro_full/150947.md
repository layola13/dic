paralytic:/ˌpærəˈlɪtɪk/ | adj. n. |瘫痪的；麻痹的；瘫痪病人|Affected by paralysis; unable to move or feel all or part of the body.  Also, a person affected by paralysis.|例句：The venom of the spider can be paralytic. 这种蜘蛛的毒液会导致瘫痪。|例句：He was a paralytic after the accident. 事故发生后，他瘫痪了。|近义词：disabled, crippled, immobile, incapacitated |反义词：mobile, able-bodied|词性:形容词, 名词

paralytic:/ˌpærəˈlɪtɪk/ | adj. |极度紧张的；吓呆的|Informal; extremely nervous or frightened.|例句：I was paralytic with fear. 我吓得动不了了。|例句：The thought of public speaking made her paralytic. 一想到要公开演讲，她就紧张得不行。|近义词：terrified, petrified, scared stiff|反义词：calm, composed|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin paralyticus, from Greek paralytikos, from paralyein "to disable," from para- "beside" + lyein "to loosen."
