lifebelt:/ˈlaɪfbelt/| n.|救生圈，救生带|A ring-shaped or cylindrical inflatable device, typically made of cork or other buoyant material, used to keep a person afloat in water.|The swimmer grabbed the lifebelt and was pulled to safety. 游泳者抓住了救生圈，被拉到了安全的地方。|Synonyms: life preserver, life buoy, life ring, life jacket|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps 'anchor', 'weight')|noun

lifebelt:/ˈlaɪfbelt/| n.|救命稻草，指能帮助人摆脱困境的事物|Something that saves a person, organization, or system from failure or difficulty.|The government loan was a lifebelt for the struggling company. 政府贷款对这家苦苦挣扎的公司来说是一根救命稻草。|Synonyms: lifeline, safety net, last resort|Antonyms: burden, hindrance|noun
