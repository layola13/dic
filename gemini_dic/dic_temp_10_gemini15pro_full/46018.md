cyberpunks:/ˈsaɪbərˌpʌŋks/| n. |赛博朋克（们）|A subgenre of science fiction, usually set in a dystopian future characterized by advanced technology and a breakdown of social order. Often features marginalized individuals, such as hackers and rebels, who use technology to fight against oppressive systems.|The movie Blade Runner 2049 portrays a world of cyberpunks struggling to survive.|近义词：technopunks, futurists|反义词:traditionalists, conservatives|词性:Noun


Etymology:
Cyberpunk is a portmanteau of "cybernetics" and "punk." "Cybernetics" refers to the science of communication and control systems in both machines and living things, while "punk" suggests a rebellious and anti-establishment attitude.  The term was coined by Bruce Bethke as the title of his short story in 1983.
