fopping:/ˈfɒpɪŋ/ | adj. | 浮华的，纨绔子弟的 | Characteristic of or resembling a fop; vain and affected in dress or manners. | He had a fopping manner, constantly adjusting his cravat and preening himself. 他举止浮华，不停地调整领结，打扮自己。 |  He struck a fopping pose, hand on hip, and surveyed the room. 他摆出一个纨绔子弟的姿势，手叉着腰，环视着房间。 | 近义词: dandyish, pretentious, affected | 反义词: unpretentious, modest, unaffected | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From the noun "fop," which originated in the late 16th century, possibly as a shortening of "fopdoodle," a foolish or insignificant person.
