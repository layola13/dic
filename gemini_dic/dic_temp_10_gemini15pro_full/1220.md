accommodatory: /əˈkɒməˌdeɪtəri/ (US) /əˈkɑːmədeɪtɔːri/
adjective
 accommodating, obliging
willing to help and do things for other people
The hotel staff were very accommodatory and allowed us to check in early.  酒店工作人员非常乐于助人，允许我们提前入住。

The host was accommodatory, offering us extra blankets and pillows. 主人很热情，给我们提供了额外的毯子和枕头。

近义词: accommodating, obliging, cooperative, helpful, considerate
反义词: unaccommodating, unhelpful, inconsiderate, obstructive
词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: from late Latin accommodatorius, from accommodare ‘accommodate’.

USAGE->
This word is less commonly used than "accommodating."

Formal contexts:
The accommodatory nature of the new policy has been praised by many. = 新政策的包容性受到了许多人的赞扬。
The government took an accommodatory stance in the negotiations. = 政府在谈判中采取了迁就的立场。

Describing people:
She has always been an accommodatory colleague, willing to go the extra mile. = 她一直是一位乐于助人的同事，愿意付出额外的努力。
He was known for his accommodatory nature, always willing to help those in need. = 他以乐于助人的天性而闻名，总是愿意帮助那些需要帮助的人。

Describing policies or actions:
The bank offered accommodatory loan terms to the struggling business. = 银行向这家苦苦挣扎的企业提供了优惠的贷款条件。
The company's accommodatory return policy made it easy for customers to shop with confidence. = 该公司通融的退货政策使客户能够放心地购物。

Describing facilities:
The hotel offered accommodatory services such as laundry and room service. = 酒店提供洗衣和客房服务等便利服务。
The conference center had accommodatory meeting rooms with state-of-the-art equipment. = 会议中心拥有设备先进的会议室。


PHRASE->
Not commonly used in set phrases.  It's generally used directly as a descriptive adjective.
