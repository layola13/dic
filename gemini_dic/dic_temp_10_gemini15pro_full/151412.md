parochial: /pəˈroʊkiəl/ /pəˈrɒkiəl/
| adj. | 狭隘的；地方性的；教区的 | Having a limited or narrow outlook or scope; relating to a church parish. | The politician's parochial views prevented him from seeing the bigger picture.  这位政客狭隘的观点使他无法看到全局。 |  limited, narrow, provincial, insular | broad-minded, cosmopolitan, liberal | adjective

parochial: /pəˈroʊkiəl/ /pəˈrɒkiəl/
| n. | 教区居民 | A parishioner; an inhabitant of a parish.  |  The parochials gathered for the annual church picnic. 教区居民聚集在一起参加一年一度的教堂野餐。 | parishioner, inhabitant | outsider | noun (This usage is less common than the adjective form.)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *parochialis, from Latin parochia "parish," from Greek paroikia "sojourning abroad," from paroikos "dwelling beside, stranger, sojourner," from para- "beside" + oikos "house."
