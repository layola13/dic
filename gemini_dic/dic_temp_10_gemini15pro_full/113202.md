lurking:/ˈlɜːrkɪŋ/ (US), /ˈlɜːkɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.  v. (present participle)|潜伏的，暗藏的；埋伏的|being or remaining hidden so as to wait in ambush|例句：A crocodile was lurking in the water.  一条鳄鱼潜伏在水中。|近义词：hiding, skulking, prowling|反义词：emerging, appearing|词性：形容词, 动词的现在分词

lurking:/ˈlɜːrkɪŋ/ (US), /ˈlɜːkɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |潜伏，埋伏|the act of lurking; lying in wait|例句：The lurking danger was not immediately apparent.  潜伏的危险并没有立即显现。|近义词：ambush, hiding, concealment|反义词：exposure, revelation|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lurken, from Old Norse *lurka (“to lie in wait, lurk”), from Proto-Germanic *lurkōną (“to crouch, stoop”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)lerg- (“to bend, turn”).
