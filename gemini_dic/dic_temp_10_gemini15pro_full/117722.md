melancholy: /ˈmɛlənkɒli/ (UK) /ˈmɛlənˌkɑːli/ (US)| adj. n.|忧郁的，忧伤的；忧郁，悲哀|A feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause. |例句：A deep melancholy settled over him.  一种深深的忧郁笼罩着他。|近义词：sadness, dejection, gloom, depression|反义词：happiness, joy, cheerfulness|词性: adjective, noun

melancholy: /ˈmɛlənkɒli/ (UK) /ˈmɛlənˌkɑːli/ (US)| n.|忧郁，悲哀|A state of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause. |例句：He succumbed to melancholy. 他陷入了忧郁。|近义词：sadness, dejection, gloom, depression|反义词：happiness, joy, cheerfulness|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *melancolie*, from Late Latin *melancholia*, from Ancient Greek *μελαγχολία (melankholía)*, from *μέλας (mélas, “black”) + χολή (kholḗ, “bile”)*.  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholy.
