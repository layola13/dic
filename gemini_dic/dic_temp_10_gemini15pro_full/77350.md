gaudiness:/ˈɡɔːdinəs/
noun
俗丽；艳丽；炫耀
The state or quality of being gaudy; excessive showiness or ornamentation.
The gaudiness of the decorations was overwhelming.  装饰的艳丽让人眼花缭乱。
近义词:garishness, flashiness, tawdriness
反义词:simplicity, understatement, elegance
词性:noun

gaudily:/ˈɡɔːdəli/
adverb
俗丽地；艳丽地；炫耀地
In a gaudy manner; showily or flashily.
She was gaudily dressed in a bright pink dress and heavy makeup. 她穿着亮粉色的连衣裙，化着浓妆，打扮得花枝招展。
近义词:garishly, flashily, tawdrily
反义词:simply, subtly, elegantly
词性:adverb

gaudy:/ˈɡɔːdi/
adjective
俗丽的；艳丽的；炫耀的
Extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless.
The carnival was a riot of gaudy colors and loud music.  嘉年华充满了俗艳的色彩和喧闹的音乐。
近义词:garish, flashy, tawdry
反义词:simple, understated, elegant
词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the late 16th century.  Ultimately from the Latin *gaudium* meaning "joy," perhaps influenced by the Old French *gaudir* "to rejoice," which itself also comes from Latin *gaudium*. The connection may lie in the bright colors associated with festivities and celebrations.
