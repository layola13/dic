flusteration: /ˌflʌstəˈreɪʃən/ | n. | 慌乱，不安，紧张 | a state of nervous excitement or confusion |  She felt a surge of flusteration when she realized she was late for the interview. 她意识到面试迟到了，感到一阵慌乱。 | 近义词: agitation, anxiety, nervousness, confusion, disquiet | 反义词: calmness, composure, tranquility | 词性: 名词

词源:  From the verb "fluster," which likely originated from a Scandinavian source, possibly related to Old Norse *flysja "to hurry."
