ordalian:/ɔːrˈdiːliən/ | adj. |  考验的；磨难的；如同神判法的 | Resembling or characteristic of an ordeal; subjecting to or involving a severe test or trial. |  The climb up the mountain was an ordalian experience.  爬山是一次痛苦的经历。 |  trying, difficult, arduous, grueling, strenuous, taxing, challenging | easy, simple, straightforward | adjective

ordalian:/ɔːrˈdiːliən/ | n. |  经历神判法的人 | A person who undergoes an ordeal. (This usage is less common.) | The ordalian emerged from the fiery trial unscathed.  经历神判法的人毫发无损地从烈火考验中走了出来。 | sufferer, victim, survivor |  - | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *ordeillier, from ordalie "ordeal," from Late Latin ordalium "judgment of God," of uncertain origin. Possibly from Frankish *urdaili "that which is decreed," from *uz- "out" + *dailjan "to deal, divide, judge," from Proto-Germanic *dailjanan.
