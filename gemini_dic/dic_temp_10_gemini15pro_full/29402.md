catachrestical: /ˌkætəˈkrɛstɪkəl/ | adj. | 滥用的，比喻不当的 |  (of language) Involving an incorrect use of words; strained or far-fetched; misused.  It refers to a figure of speech in which a word is applied in a way that departs from its strict or literal meaning, often creating a forced or unusual effect. |  Her catachrestical use of the word "obsequious" made her sound pretentious. 她对“谄媚”一词的滥用让她听起来很做作。 |  inappropriate, misused, strained, forced, far-fetched, tortured,  malapropism | appropriate, correct, apt, fitting, proper | adjective
