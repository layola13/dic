drawls:/drɔːlz/ (British) /drɔːlz/ (American)| n. v.|慢吞吞地说; 拖长音调地说|A slow way of speaking, typically associated with a Southern US accent.  The act of speaking in this way.|

例句：
He drawls his words in a lazy Southern accent.
他用慵懒的南方口音慢吞吞地说着话。

Her drawl made it difficult to understand her at first.
她拖长的语调一开始让我很难听懂她的话。


近义词：elongates, stretches, slurs|
反义词:clips, hastens, rushes|
词性: Noun (plural), Verb (third person singular present tense)|

词源: From *drawl*, from *draw* + *-le* (frequentative suffix).  Essentially means "to draw out" one's speech.
