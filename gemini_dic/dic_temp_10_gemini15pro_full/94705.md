impromptu:/ɪmˈprɒmptjuː/ (UK) /ɪmˈprɑːmptuː/ (US)| adj. adv. n.|即兴的，即席的；即兴曲；即席演出|Done without being planned, organized, or rehearsed.|She gave an impromptu performance at the party. 她在聚会上即兴表演了一段。|近义词：spontaneous, unrehearsed, extemporaneous, off-the-cuff|反义词：planned, prepared, rehearsed|词性: adjective, adverb, noun
impromptu:/ɪmˈprɒmptjuː/ (UK) /ɪmˈprɑːmptuː/ (US)| adv.|即席地，即兴地|In an impromptu manner.|He spoke impromptu at the meeting. 他在会议上即兴发言。|近义词：extemporaneously, spontaneously|反义词：deliberately, planned|词性: adverb
impromptu:/ɪmˈprɒmptjuː/ (UK) /ɪmˈprɑːmptuː/ (US)| n.|即兴曲；即席演出|A short piece of music, especially for the piano, that is performed without preparation.|He played a delightful impromptu on the piano. 他用钢琴演奏了一曲令人愉快的即兴曲。|近义词：improvisation|反义词：prepared piece|词性: noun

词源: From French *impromptu*, from Latin *in promptu* "in readiness," from *in* "in" + *promptus* "ready."
