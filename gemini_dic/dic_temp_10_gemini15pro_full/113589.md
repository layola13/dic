mace:/meɪs/| n.|权杖，狼牙棒，催泪喷雾|A heavy club, often spiked or flanged, used as a weapon; a spray used by police to subdue violent individuals.|The king carried a golden mace as a symbol of his authority. 国王手持一根金权杖作为他权威的象征。

mace:/meɪs/| n.|肉豆蔻皮，肉豆蔻粉|The dried outer covering of the nutmeg seed, used as a spice.|Mace has a warm, slightly sweet flavor. 肉豆蔻皮有一种温暖、略带甜味的味道。


Etymology->
From Old French *mace*, from Latin *mattea*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Arabic *mattha*.

Synonyms->
(weapon) club, bludgeon, staff
(spice) nutmeg

Antonyms->
(weapon) shield, armor  (These are not direct opposites but represent opposing concepts)
(spice)  There's no direct antonym for a spice like mace.
