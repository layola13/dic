obfuscate:/ˈɒbfəskeɪt/ (US) /ˈɑːbfəskeɪt/ (UK)| vt.|使模糊；使混乱；使难懂|To make something difficult to understand, usually deliberately.|The government was accused of obfuscating the issue. 政府被指责故意混淆这个问题。|Synonyms: confuse, obscure, bewilder, muddle, complicate|Antonyms: clarify, elucidate, explain, simplify|Verb

obfuscater:/ˈɒbfəskeɪtər/ (US) /ˈɑːbfəskeɪtər/ (UK)| n.|模糊处理者；混淆者；使人困惑的人或事物|A person or thing that obfuscates.|The lawyer acted as an obfuscater, making the case more complex than it needed to be.  这位律师像个混淆者，把案子弄得比实际需要的更复杂。|Synonyms:  confuser, obscurer|Antonyms: clarifier, elucidator|Noun

obfuscation:/ˌɒbfəˈskeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌɒbfʌsˈkeɪʃn/ (UK)| n.|模糊；混乱；困惑|The act of obfuscating; the state of being obfuscated.|The contract was filled with legal obfuscation.  合同里充满了法律上的含糊不清之处。|Synonyms: confusion, obscurity, bewilderment|Antonyms: clarity, elucidation|Noun
