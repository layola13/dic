narrower:/ˈnæroʊər/| adj.|更窄的，更狭隘的|Comparative form of "narrow," meaning more limited in width, scope, or point of view.|The path became narrower as we went deeper into the woods.  这条小路在我们越往森林深处走就越窄。|The politician had a narrower view of the issue than the economist.  这位政治家对这个问题的看法比经济学家更狭隘。|近义词:thinner, slimmer, tighter, restricted, limited|反义词:wider, broader, larger, expansive|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Old English *nearwa (attested only in comparative nearra "narrower"), from Proto-Germanic *narwaz "narrow, pressed, close," from PIE root *ner- (1) "on the left; below," also "under, near."  The semantic development likely progressed from "being under" to "constrained," and thence to "narrow."
