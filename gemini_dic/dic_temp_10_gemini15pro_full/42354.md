counteraggressions:/ˌkaʊntərəˈɡreʃənz/| n. |反击，对抗性攻击|Actions taken in response to aggression, often with the intention of neutralizing or stopping the initial aggressive behavior.  These actions can be physical, verbal, or psychological.|Example:  Her counteraggressions were swift and effective, silencing her bully. 她迅速而有效的反击让欺负她的人哑口无言。

Example:  The country's counteraggressions included economic sanctions and diplomatic pressure.  这个国家的反击包括经济制裁和外交压力。|近义词：retaliation, reprisal, counterattack, defense, resistance|反义词: aggression, attack, offense|词性:名词

词源：
"Counter-" comes from the Old French "contre-" meaning "against". "Aggression" comes from the Latin "aggressio" meaning "attack".  Therefore, "counteraggression" literally means "attack against (an attack)".
