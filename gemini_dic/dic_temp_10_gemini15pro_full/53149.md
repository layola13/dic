diplomats:/ˈdɪpləmæts/| n.|外交官|Officials who represent their country in foreign countries and deal with international relations.|The diplomats negotiated a peace treaty.|Synonyms: envoys, ambassadors, consuls, representatives|Antonyms: civilians, natives, nationals|Noun

diplomatic:/ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk/| adj.|外交的，圆滑的|Relating to diplomacy; tactful and skillful in dealing with people.|He took a diplomatic approach to the sensitive issue.|Synonyms: tactful, discreet, polite, courteous|Antonyms: blunt, rude, undiplomatic, tactless|Adjective

diplomacy:/dɪˈploʊməsi/| n.|外交，外交手腕|The art and practice of conducting negotiations between nations; skill in handling affairs without raising hostility.|Diplomacy is often the best way to resolve international disputes.|Synonyms: negotiation, statesmanship, tact, finesse|Antonyms: hostility, aggression, belligerence|Noun
