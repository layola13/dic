macadam:/məˈkædəm/ | n. | 碎石路 | A road surface made of small broken stones. |  The old macadam road was bumpy and dusty.  这条古老的碎石路坑坑洼洼，尘土飞扬。 | synonyms:  gravel road, crushed stone road | antonyms: paved road, asphalt road | noun

macadamize:/məˈkædəˌmaɪz/ | v. | 用碎石铺路 | To pave or surface a road with small broken stones. | They macadamized the driveway leading to the house.  他们用碎石铺设了通往房屋的车道。 | synonyms: pave, surface, gravel | antonyms: unpave | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
Named after John Loudon McAdam (1756–1836), a Scottish engineer who pioneered the method of road construction using broken stone.
