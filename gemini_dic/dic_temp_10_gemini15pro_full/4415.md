alarm: /əˈlɑːrm/ (US) /əˈlɑːm/ (UK)| n. vt.| 警报，惊恐，闹钟 |A sudden fear caused by the realization of danger; a warning of danger.| The fire alarm went off. 火警响了。| synonyms: warning, alert, signal | antonyms: calmness, tranquility | noun

alarm: /əˈlɑːrm/ (US) /əˈlɑːm/ (UK)| vt.| 使惊恐，使担心 |To fill with sudden fear or apprehension.| The news alarmed the public. 这条新闻使公众感到恐慌。| synonyms: frighten, scare, terrify | antonyms: calm, soothe, reassure | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *alarme* "call to arms," from Italian *all'arme* "to arms!" Ultimately from Latin *ad* "to" + *arma* "arms."
