a matter of life and death:/ə ˈmætər əv laɪf ænd dɛθ/ | n. |生死攸关的事情; 极其重要的事情 |It was a matter of life and death for the trapped miners. 对于被困的矿工来说，这是生死攸关的事情。| The surgery was a matter of life and death for him.  这个手术对他来说是生死攸关的。| 近义词：critical, crucial, vital, essential | 反义词: trivial, unimportant | 词性:名词短语

ETYMOLOGY->
This phrase is a straightforward combination of the words "life" and "death," emphasizing the ultimate importance of a situation. The use of "matter" highlights that the situation is an issue or concern of the highest importance.  The phrase likely originated in common usage, possibly gaining prominence in literature and storytelling where dramatic situations of survival were often depicted.

USAGE->
There's no specific grammatical rule tied to this phrase as it functions as a noun phrase. It can be used in various grammatical contexts.

陈述句:
It was a matter of life and death to get him to the hospital. =把他送到医院是生死攸关的事。
Finding a source of clean water became a matter of life and death for the stranded survivors. = 对被困的幸存者来说，找到干净的水源成了生死攸关的大事。


条件句:
If it's a matter of life and death, I'll do whatever it takes. = 如果是生死攸关的事，我会不惜一切代价。
He would only call her in the middle of the night if it were a matter of life and death. = 他只有在生死攸关的情况下才会在半夜给她打电话。


疑问句:
Is it really a matter of life and death? = 这真的是生死攸关的事吗？
Was it a matter of life and death to leave immediately? = 立即离开是生死攸关的吗？



否定句:
It wasn't a matter of life and death, but it was still very important. = 这不是生死攸关的大事，但仍然很重要。
Don't exaggerate; it's not a matter of life and death. = 不要夸大其词；这不是生死攸关的事。

注意事项:

"A matter of life and death" is generally used for serious situations, though sometimes it can be used hyperbolically for emphasis.  Avoid overuse, as it can lose its impact if used too casually.
