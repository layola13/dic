dissipatedness:/ˈdɪsɪpeɪtɪdnəs/
|n.|
|放荡；挥霍；消散|
|The state of being dissipated; a tendency towards dissipation, especially of energy, money, or moral restraint.|
|His dissipatedness led him to squander his fortune on gambling and extravagant parties. 他的放荡让他把自己的财产挥霍在赌博和奢华的派对上。|
|近义词：debauchery, profligacy, licentiousness, dissoluteness|
|反义词：frugality, temperance, moderation, abstinence|
|词性：名词|
|词源：From dissipate + -ed + -ness. Dissipate comes from Latin dissipatus, past participle of dissipare "to scatter," from dis- "apart" + sipare "to throw, scatter."|
