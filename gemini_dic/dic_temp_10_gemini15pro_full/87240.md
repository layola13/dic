heretic:/ˈherətɪk/ (US), /ˈhɛrətɪk/ (UK)| n.|异教徒，异端邪说者|A person believing in or practicing religious heresy.|例句：The church condemned him as a heretic. 教会谴责他为异教徒。

例句： Galileo was considered a heretic for his scientific beliefs. 伽利略因为他的科学信仰被认为是异端。|近义词：dissenter, nonconformist, schismatic, apostate|反义词：orthodox, believer, conformist|词性:noun

heretics:/ˈherətɪks/ (US), /ˈhɛrətɪks/ (UK)| n.|异教徒们，异端邪说者们|Plural form of "heretic".  People believing in or practicing religious heresy.|例句：The heretics were excommunicated from the church. 异教徒被逐出教会。

例句：Throughout history, heretics have often been persecuted for their beliefs. 纵观历史，异端分子经常因为他们的信仰而受到迫害。|近义词：dissenters, nonconformists, schismatics, apostates|反义词：orthodox believers, conformists|词性:noun

词源：From Old French *eretique*, from Late Latin *haereticus*, from Greek *hairetikos* "able to choose," from *haireisthai* "to take, choose."  Originally referred to one who chooses his own philosophical or religious beliefs, later specifically to one whose beliefs deviate from established doctrine.
