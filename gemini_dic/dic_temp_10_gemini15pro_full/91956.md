hypermodernities:/ˌhaɪpərməˈdɜːrnɪtiz/ | n. | 超现代性（复数） |  A term used in social theory to describe the intensified and accelerated aspects of modernity, often characterized by globalization, technological advancements, and the compression of time and space. It signifies a heightened state of modernity, where its defining features are amplified and intensified. | The hypermodernities of global capitalism have created new forms of social inequality. 全球资本主义的超现代性造成了新的社会不平等形式。 |  Late modernity, postmodernity, supermodernity |  -- | noun
