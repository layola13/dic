heroic couplets:/hɪˈroʊɪk ˈkʌplɪts/ | n. | 英雄双韵体 |  A pair of rhyming lines in iambic pentameter. |  Example:  Alexander Pope's "The Rape of the Lock" is written in heroic couplets.  亚历山大·蒲柏的《夺发记》是用英雄双韵体写成的。 | Synonym: rhyming couplets, closed couplets | Antonym: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "heroic" (referring to epic poetry where this form was common) + "couplet" (a pair of rhyming lines). "Heroic" comes from the French "héroïque" ultimately from the Greek "hērōikos" meaning 'of a hero'. "Couplet" comes from the Old French "couplet," diminutive of "couple" meaning 'pair'.
