fumbling:/ˈfʌmblɪŋ/| adj. |笨手笨脚的；摸索的|Doing something clumsily or hesitantly, often involving the hands.|He made a fumbling attempt to tie his shoelaces. 他笨手笨脚地试图系鞋带。|Synonyms: clumsy, awkward, inept, bumbling|Antonyms: dexterous, skillful, adept|adjective

fumble:/ˈfʌmbl/| v. n.| 笨拙地摸索；弄乱；失球|1.(v.) To handle something clumsily or hesitantly, often dropping or failing to grasp it. 2.(n.) An act of fumbling.|She fumbled in her purse for her keys. 她在钱包里摸索着找钥匙。| The quarterback fumbled the ball, and the other team recovered it.  四分卫失球了，对方球队抢到了球。|Synonyms: grope, fumble around|Antonyms: grasp, seize|verb, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fomblen*, frequentative of *fomen* "to try, attempt, grope," of uncertain origin.
