cockied:/ˈkʊkiːd/ | adj. | 自以为是的，骄傲自满的 | Having an inflated sense of one's own abilities or importance, often because of a perceived advantage or accomplishment.  Like someone who feels superior after successfully completing something, especially when that success is attributed more to luck or circumstance than actual skill. | He got a little cockied after winning the local chess tournament, even though he knew he'd gotten lucky in a couple of key matches. 他在赢得当地象棋锦标赛后有点沾沾自喜，即使他知道自己在几场关键比赛中很幸运。|  例句：She became quite cockied after receiving a promotion, acting superior to her former colleagues.  她升职后变得非常自以为是，对以前的同事表现得高人一等。| 近义词：smug, arrogant, conceited, self-satisfied, complacent | 反义词：humble, modest, unassuming | 词性:形容词
