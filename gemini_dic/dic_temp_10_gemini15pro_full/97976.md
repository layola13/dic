inocarpin:/ɪnoʊˈkɑːrpɪn/ | n. | 印加果素 | A toxic isoflavone compound found in the seeds of the tara tree (Caesalpinia spinosa). It is structurally similar to rotenone and has insecticidal and piscicidal properties. | The traditional use of inocarpin in South America involves grinding the seeds and using them as a fish poison. | 近义词: rotenone, piscicide, insecticide | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but could consider "growth stimulant" for plants or "antidote" in a toxicological context) | 词性: noun
