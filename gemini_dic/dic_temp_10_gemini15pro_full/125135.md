multi-partyism:/ˌmʌltiˈpɑːrtiɪzəm/ | n. | 多党制 | A system in which multiple political parties compete for power and hold significant seats in government. |  India is a vibrant example of multi-partyism. 印度是多党制的生动例子。| synonyms: multi-party system, pluralism | antonyms: one-party system, single-party rule | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From multi- (many) + party + -ism (doctrine or system).  "Multi-" comes from Latin "multus" meaning "much" or "many."  "Party" originates from Old French "partie" meaning "part" or "side." "-ism" comes from Greek "-ismos," indicating a doctrine, belief, or practice.
