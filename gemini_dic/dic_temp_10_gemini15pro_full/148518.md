oversuperstitious:/ˌoʊvərˌsuːpərˈstɪʃəs/
|adj.| 过于迷信的 | Excessively or unduly superstitious; believing in too many superstitions or interpreting things as omens too readily. | She's so oversuperstitious that she won't walk under ladders or leave her house on Friday the 13th. 她太迷信了，不会从梯子下走过，也不会在13号星期五出门。 | He was oversuperstitious about the number 13 and refused to stay in room 1313. 他对数字13过于迷信，拒绝住在1313号房间。 | superstitious, credulous | rational, skeptical | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + superstitious.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively," "too much."  "Superstitious" comes from the Latin word "superstitio," meaning "excessive fear of the gods" or "religious awe."
