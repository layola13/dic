euthanising:/ˈjuːθəˌnaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 安乐死 | The act of painlessly killing a suffering person or animal to relieve them from further suffering. |  The vet recommended euthanising the dog as it was in considerable pain. 兽医建议对这只狗实施安乐死，因为它非常痛苦。

The family made the difficult decision to euthanise their beloved pet. 这家人做出了一个艰难的决定，对他们心爱的宠物实施安乐死。 | 近义词: putting down, putting to sleep | 反义词: resuscitating, reviving | 词性: 动词

euthanize:/ˈjuːθəˌnaɪz/ | v. | 安乐死 | To put to death humanely, usually to end suffering. | The vet euthanized the injured horse. 兽医对受伤的马实施了安乐死。

They decided to euthanize the dog rather than let it suffer any longer. 他们决定对这只狗实施安乐死，而不是让它继续受苦。 | 近义词: putting down, putting to sleep | 反义词: resuscitating, reviving | 词性: 动词


词源:  From Greek *eu* "good" + *thanatos* "death".
