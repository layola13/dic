faburden: /ˈfeɪbərˌdən/ | n. | 衬音；福伯顿；福布尔登| A compositional technique of the late Middle Ages and Renaissance, in which a plainchant melody is sung in the middle voice, accompanied by two other voices a perfect fourth above and below.  The upper voice follows the plainchant rhythmically while the lower voice moves mostly in parallel perfect fourths below the plainchant, creating a series of first inversions.|  The choir sang a beautiful faburden of the "Ave Maria."  唱诗班演唱了一首优美的“圣母颂”福伯顿。 | Synonyms: fauxbourdon, faulx bourdon | Antonyms: N/A | Noun

Etymology: From Old French *faux bourdon ("false drone," literally "false bass"), perhaps influenced by Latin *burdo ("mule," hence "low voice")
