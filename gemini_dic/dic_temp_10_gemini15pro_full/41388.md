corms:/kɔːrmz/
|n.|球茎；块茎|A rounded underground storage organ present in plants such as crocuses and gladioli, consisting of a swollen stem base covered with scale leaves.|Crocuses grow from corms. 番红花由球茎生长而来。|Synonyms:bulb, tuber|Antonyms: |noun
