anti-jesuit: /ˌæntiˈdʒɛzjuɪt/ | adj. n. | 反耶稣会的；反对耶稣会的人 | Opposed to the Jesuits, a Roman Catholic religious order. Can refer to a person holding such views or to actions and policies motivated by such opposition. |  The anti-jesuit sentiment was strong in 18th-century Europe.  十八世纪的欧洲反耶稣会情绪高涨。|  The king implemented several anti-jesuit policies. 国王实施了几项反耶稣会的政策。 | 近义词: anti-Catholic, Jesuit-baiting | 反义词: pro-Jesuit, Jesuit-supporting | 词性: 形容词，名词

Etymology:
anti- (prefix): from Greek _anti_, meaning "against," "opposite of"
Jesuit: from Late Latin _Iesuitēs_, short for _Societās Iēsū_ "Society of Jesus"
