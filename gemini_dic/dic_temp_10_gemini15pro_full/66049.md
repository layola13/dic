exasperated:/ɪɡˈzæspəreɪtɪd/ | adj. | 恼怒的，激怒的 |  Intensely irritated and frustrated. | She was exasperated by his constant interruptions. 她被他不断的打断弄得很恼火。 |  annoyed, frustrated, irritated, infuriated | calm, patient, pleased, soothed | adjective

exasperate:/ɪɡˈzæspəreɪt/ | v. | 激怒，使恼怒 |  Irritate intensely; infuriate. |  His constant complaining exasperated her. 他不断的抱怨让她很恼火。 | annoy, irritate, provoke, infuriate | calm, soothe, placate, appease | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin exasperatus, past participle of exasperare (“to roughen up, irritate”), from ex (“out”) + asper (“rough”).
