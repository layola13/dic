lunge:/lʌndʒ/| n. vi. | 刺，跃进，猛冲；弓步，刺剑|A sudden forward movement of the body, often to attack someone or seize something. Also, a position with one leg forward and bent at the knee and the other leg straight behind.|He made a lunge for the ball. 他猛扑向球。|近义词：thrust, pounce, leap, dive|反义词：retreat, withdraw|词性: noun, verb|词源: From Old French *longier “to lengthen,” from Latin longus “long.”  Originally referred to the extension of a horse's body in gait.
