centralism: /ˈsɛntrəlɪzəm/| n. |中央集权制，集权主义| The concentration of control of an activity or organization under a single authority.|  The government's policy of centralism has led to resentment in the provinces. 政府的中央集权政策引起了各省的不满。|  Centralism can be efficient, but it often stifles local initiative. 中央集权可以提高效率，但往往会扼杀地方的积极性。| 近义词：unitarism, centralization | 反义词: decentralization, federalism | 词性: noun

centralism: /ˈsɛntrəlɪzəm/| n. |中央集权|The principle or policy of concentrating power and control in a central organization or authority, especially in government.|  The party advocated for greater centralism in economic planning. 该党主张在经济规划中加强中央集权。|  The country's centralism meant that all major decisions were made in the capital. 该国的中央集权意味着所有主要决策都在首都做出。| 近义词：unitarianism, centralization | 反义词: decentralization, localism | 词性: noun
