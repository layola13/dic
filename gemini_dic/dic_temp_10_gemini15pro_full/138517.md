nowal:/ˈnoʊəl/| adj.| 无头的, 没有头的 |Lacking a head; headless.  |The nowal statue was unsettling.  无头的雕像令人不安。 | headless, acephalous | headed | adjective
Etymology: From Middle English *nowel, from Old French *noel, from Latin *nōdālis, meaning "knotty," related to nōdus ("knot").  The semantic shift likely occurred through association with the idea of a knot as a head or top.
