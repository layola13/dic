nonvirtuousness:/ˌnɒnˈvɜːtʃuəsnəs/
|n.|不道德；邪恶；无德|Lack of virtue; wickedness.|His nonvirtuousness led him down a dark path. 他的不道德让他走上了一条黑暗的道路。|Synonyms: immorality, wickedness, depravity, vice, iniquity|Antonyms: virtue, morality, righteousness, goodness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + virtuousness (the quality of being virtuous).  Virtuousness comes from virtuous, from Old French vertuos, from Latin virtuosus ("full of virtue"), from virtus ("manliness, excellence, worth, virtue").
