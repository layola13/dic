nonimpulsivenesses:/ˌnɑːnɪmˈpʌlsɪvnəsɪz/
|n.|非冲动性；缺乏冲动；克制冲动|The state or quality of not being impulsive; a lack of impulsiveness.|Her nonimpulsivenesses served her well in the high-stakes negotiations. 她的沉着冷静在高风险的谈判中发挥了很好的作用。|  His nonimpulsivenesses are remarkable considering his age. 考虑到他的年龄，他的沉着冷静令人瞩目。|Synonyms: self-control, restraint, composure, level-headedness|Antonyms: impulsiveness, rashness, recklessness|noun


Etymology:
From non- (not) + impulsiveness + -es (plural suffix).  Impulsiveness derives from impulsive, from impulse (a sudden urge) + -ive (forming adjectives). Impulse ultimately comes from Latin *impulsus, past participle of impellere "to push against, urge."
