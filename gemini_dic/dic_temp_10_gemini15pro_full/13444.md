atrabilarian:/ˌætrəbɪˈlɛəriən/| adj. n.|忧郁的，阴郁的；忧郁质的人|Affected with, characterized by, or causing atrabiliousness; melancholic; gloomy.  Also, a person so affected.|例句：He had an atrabilarian temperament, always prone to melancholy. 他性情忧郁，总是容易伤感。|例句：The atrabilarian poet locked himself in his room for days. 这位忧郁的诗人把自己关在房间里好几天。|近义词：melancholic, morose, gloomy, saturnine, splenetic|反义词：cheerful, optimistic, sanguine|词性：adjective, noun

atrabilious:/ˌætrəˈbɪliəs/| adj.|忧郁的，坏脾气的|Characterized by melancholy or ill temper, especially as formerly believed to be caused by an excess of black bile.|例句：His atrabilious mood darkened the entire room. 他忧郁的情绪让整个房间都暗了下来。|例句：She gave him an atrabilious glare. 她狠狠地瞪了他一眼，充满了怒气。|近义词：melancholy, morose, gloomy, irritable, choleric|反义词：cheerful, good-natured, pleasant|词性：adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *atrabilarius, from Latin atra bilis (“black bile”).  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholy and ill temper.
