disgustedness:/dɪsˈɡʌstɪdnəs/
n.
厌恶，憎恶
A feeling of strong dislike or revulsion.
She expressed her disgustedness at the sight of the rotting food. 她对看到腐烂的食物表示厌恶。
近义词:revulsion, repugnance, aversion, nausea
反义词:attraction, liking, pleasure
词性:名词

disgust:/dɪsˈɡʌst/
n.
厌恶，憎恶
A feeling of strong dislike or revulsion.
The smell of the garbage filled him with disgust. 垃圾的气味让他感到恶心。
vt.
使厌恶，使作呕
To cause a feeling of strong dislike or revulsion.
The violent scenes in the movie disgusted many viewers.  电影中的暴力场面让许多观众感到恶心。
近义词:repulsion, repugnance, aversion, nausea, sicken, revolt
反义词:attraction, liking, pleasure, delight
词性:名词，动词

disgusting:/dɪsˈɡʌstɪŋ/
adj.
令人厌恶的，令人作呕的
Causing a feeling of strong dislike or revulsion.
The food was disgusting and I couldn't eat it.  食物令人作呕，我吃不下。
近义词:repulsive, revolting, sickening, nauseating, foul, offensive
反义词:pleasant, agreeable, delightful, appealing
词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *desgouster (meaning "to displease the taste"), from des- (meaning "opposite of, away") + gouster (meaning "to taste").  The noun "disgustedness" is formed by adding the suffix "-edness" to the past participle "disgusted," which comes from the verb "disgust."
