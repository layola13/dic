desecrater:/ˈdɛsɪˌkreɪtər/| n.|亵渎者，污损圣物者|A person who desecrates something, especially a sacred place or thing.|The desecraters of the ancient tombs were condemned by archaeologists and historians.|近义词：vandal, profaner, defiler|反义词：protector, guardian|词性:noun
Etymology: From Late Latin *desecrator, from Latin desecrat-, past participle stem of desecrare  "to unconsecrate," from de- "un-, remove" + sacrare "to consecrate," from sacer "sacred."
