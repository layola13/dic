betrayers:/bɪˈtreɪərz/| n. |背叛者，叛徒|People who betray someone's trust or are disloyal to a cause, country, or group.  |The betrayers were punished for their treachery. 叛徒因其背叛行为受到了惩罚。|
The general was furious with the betrayers who had leaked vital information to the enemy. 将军对那些向敌人泄露重要信息的叛徒非常愤怒。|
近义词：traitors, deserters, double-crossers, turncoats|反义词:loyalists, patriots|词性:名词

betray:/bɪˈtreɪ/| vt. |背叛，出卖；辜负；泄露|To be disloyal or unfaithful to someone or something. To reveal something that should have been kept secret. To unintentionally reveal (an emotion or quality).|His trembling hands betrayed his nervousness. 他颤抖的双手暴露了他的紧张。|She betrayed her friend by revealing her secret. 她泄露了朋友的秘密，背叛了朋友。|
近义词：deceive, mislead, double-cross|反义词:support, defend|词性:动词
