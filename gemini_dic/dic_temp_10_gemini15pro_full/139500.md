obstructionist:/əbˈstrʌkʃənɪst/
n.
阻挠者，妨碍者
A person who deliberately delays or prevents progress, especially in politics.
The senator was accused of being an obstructionist by his opponents.  这位参议员被他的对手指责为阻挠者。
The obstructionist tactics employed by the minority party stalled the bill's passage. 少数党采用的阻挠策略拖延了法案的通过。

近义词:opponent, antagonist, blocker, thwarter
反义词:supporter, advocate, facilitator

词性: noun

词源: From obstruct + -ion + -ist.  Obstruct comes from Latin obstructus, past participle of obstruere "to build in front of, block up," from ob "against" + struere "to pile, build."
