noncovetousness:/ˌnɒnkʌvɪtəsnəs/
|n.|无贪欲，不贪婪|The state of not coveting or desiring what belongs to others; lack of greed.|Her noncovetousness was evident in her simple lifestyle. 她的简朴生活方式表明她不贪婪。|Near Synonyms: contentment, generosity, unselfishness|Antonyms: greed, avarice, covetousness|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + covetousness (desire for possessions belonging to others).  The root word "covet" comes from the Old French "coveiter," meaning "to desire eagerly."  Ultimately, the word traces back to the Latin "cupiditas," meaning "desire" or "passion."
