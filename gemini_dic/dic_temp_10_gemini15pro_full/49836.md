demonstratorship:/ˌdemənˈstreɪtərʃɪp/
|n.| 助教职位；示范员职位
|The position or role of a demonstrator, typically in a university or college setting.|
|She secured a demonstratorship in the physics department. 她获得了物理系的助教职位。|
|He held a demonstratorship while completing his PhD. 他在攻读博士学位期间担任助教。|
|近义词：assistantship, instructorship|
|反义词：professorship|
|词性: noun|


demonstrate:/ˈdemənstreɪt/
|v.|展示，演示；证明；示威
|To show or explain how something works or is done; to prove something clearly; to take part in a public meeting or march, usually to show support for or opposition to something.|
|He demonstrated the new software to the team. 他向团队演示了新软件。|
|The study demonstrated a clear link between smoking and lung cancer. 这项研究证明了吸烟和肺癌之间存在明确的联系。|
|近义词：show, explain, prove, exhibit, display|
|反义词：conceal, hide|
|词性: verb|


demonstrator:/ˈdemənstreɪtər/
|n.|示范者，演示者；助教；示威者
|A person who demonstrates something, such as a product or a skill; a person who teaches practical skills, typically in a science subject, at a university or college; a person who takes part in a public demonstration.|
|The demonstrator explained the features of the new car. 演示员讲解了新车的特点。|
|She worked as a demonstrator in the chemistry lab. 她在化学实验室担任助教。|
|近义词：assistant, instructor, teacher, exhibitor|
|反义词：student, learner|
|词性: noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *demonstratif*, from late Latin *dēmōnstrātīvus*, from Latin *dēmōnstrāre* "to point out, show,"  from *dē-* "fully" + *monstrāre* "to show." The suffix "-ship" indicates a state, condition, or office.
