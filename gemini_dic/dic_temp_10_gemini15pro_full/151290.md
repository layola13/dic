pargetory: /ˈpɑːrdʒɪtəri/ /ˈpɑːrɡətɔːri/ | n. | 炼狱；涤罪；苦难的经历 | A place or state of suffering inhabited by the souls of sinners who are expiating their sins before going to heaven.  Figuratively, any place or state of temporary suffering or expiation. |  例句：He felt his job was a pargetory, a tedious and unpleasant experience he had to endure before reaching better things. 他觉得他的工作就是炼狱，一个在他到达更好的境地之前必须忍受的乏味而不愉快的经历。

例句：After the scandal, the politician spent several years in political pargetory, ostracized by his colleagues and the public.  丑闻之后，这位政治家在政治炼狱中度过了几年，被他的同事和公众排斥。| 近义词：purgatory, ordeal, trial | 反义词: heaven, paradise | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *pargatoire (modern French purgatoire), from Late Latin purgatorium, neuter of purgātōrius (“cleansing, purging”), from Latin purgāre (“to cleanse, purge”). Ultimately from Latin purus (“pure”) + agere (“to do, drive”).
