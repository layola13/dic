depression:/dɪˈprɛʃən/ /dɪˈprɛʃn/ | n. | 沮丧，抑郁，萧条 | A state of low mood and aversion to activity that can affect a person's thoughts, behavior, feelings, and sense of well-being.  A period of economic decline. A hollow or low place in a surface.|  He suffered from severe depression after losing his job. 他失业后患上了严重的抑郁症。 The country is in a deep economic depression.  这个国家正处于严重的经济萧条之中。 There was a noticeable depression in the ground where the meteorite had struck. 陨石撞击的地方地面有一个明显的凹陷。 | 近义词：sadness, gloom, melancholy, recession, slump, hollow | 反义词：happiness, joy, elation, boom, prosperity, elevation | 词性: 名词

depression:/dɪˈprɛʃən/ /dɪˈprɛʃn/ | n. | 压下，降低 | The act of pressing something down. | The depression of the brake pedal stopped the car.  刹车踏板的压下使汽车停了下来。| 近义词：lowering, sinking | 反义词：raising, lifting | 词性: 名词

词源：From Late Latin *depressionem (nominative depressio) "a pressing down," noun of action from past-participle stem of deprimere "press down" (see depress).  Meaning "lowering of spirits" is from 1660s. Economic sense is from 1849.
