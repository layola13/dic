alamani:/ˌæləˈmɑːni/ | n. | 阿拉曼尼人; 阿拉曼尼语 |  The Alamanni were a Germanic people who lived in the region of the Upper Rhine in the 3rd century AD. They are considered the ancestors of the modern-day Alemannic German dialects spoken in parts of Switzerland, Germany, France, and Austria. | The Alamanni established a kingdom in the region, frequently clashing with the Roman Empire. |  Synonyms: Suebi, Germani | Antonyms: Romans (in a historical context) | noun
