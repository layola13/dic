hyperglycaemia: /ˌhaɪpərɡlaɪˈsiːmiə/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpərɡlaɪˈsiːmiə/ (US)| n. |高血糖|A condition in which an excessive amount of glucose circulates in the blood plasma.  This is generally a blood glucose level higher than 11.1 mmol/L (200 mg/dL).|Example 1:  The doctor diagnosed her with hyperglycaemia after the blood test. 医生在验血后诊断她患有高血糖。

Example 2:  Untreated hyperglycaemia can lead to serious complications.  未经治疗的高血糖会导致严重的并发症。|Synonym: high blood sugar, hyperglycemia (US spelling)|Antonym: hypoglycaemia, hypoglycemia (US spelling)|noun
