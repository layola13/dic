inconsisting:/ˌɪnkənˈsɪstɪŋ/ | adj. | 不一致的，不协调的，矛盾的 | Not staying the same throughout; containing elements that disagree with each other or with the whole. | Her testimony was inconsistent with the evidence. 她的证词与证据不符。| synonyms: contradictory, conflicting, incompatible, discrepant, discordant | antonyms: consistent, compatible, harmonious, uniform, congruent | adjective

inconsistency:/ˌɪnkənˈsɪstənsi/ | n. | 不一致，不协调，矛盾 | The state of being inconsistent; lack of uniformity or steadiness. | There's an inconsistency between his words and his actions. 他的言行不一致。 | synonyms: contradiction, conflict, incompatibility, discrepancy, discordance | antonyms: consistency, compatibility, harmony, uniformity, congruence | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From in- (not) + consistent (standing firm; agreeing).  Consistent comes from Latin consistere (to stand firm), from con- (together) + sistere (to stand).
