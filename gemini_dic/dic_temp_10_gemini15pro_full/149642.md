painterly:/ˈpeɪntərli/| adj.|绘画般的，画意的|Suggestive of painting, especially in the application of paint or the overall effect. Characterized by qualities that are associated with painting, such as visible brushstrokes, rich color, and an emphasis on texture or light. |Her painterly style is reminiscent of the Impressionists. 她的绘画风格让人想起印象派画家。|近义词：artistic, picturesque, aesthetic |反义词：graphic, photographic, realistic|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From *painter* + *-ly*.  *Painter* comes from Old French *peintre*, ultimately from Latin *pinctor*, "painter," from *pingere*, "to paint."  *-ly* is a suffix forming adjectives and adverbs, meaning "like," "characteristic of," or "in a manner of."
