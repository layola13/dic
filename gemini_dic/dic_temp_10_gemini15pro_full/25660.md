bunfight:/ˈbʌnˌfaɪt/| n.|混乱；争吵；争论；(尤指)派系斗争|A heated argument or dispute, especially one involving factions or cliques.|There was a real bunfight at the meeting over the proposed budget cuts.  会议上就拟议的预算削减问题发生了激烈的争论。|The team had a bit of a bunfight about tactics.  团队内部就战术问题发生了一些争执。|近义词：quarrel, argument, dispute, squabble, row|反义词：agreement, harmony, concord|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
Originating in British slang, likely from the image of a chaotic tea party or social gathering where buns are served, implying a messy or disorderly situation.  The word evokes a sense of informal, often petty, conflict.
