词源：From Middle Dutch *dunnen (“to make a loud noise”), from Proto-Germanic *dunnaną (“to resound, roar”). Cognate with Old English dynnan (“to make a din, resound”). Related to din.  The meaning shifted from "to make a din" to "to demand payment noisily and persistently".
