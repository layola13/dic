drudge:/drʌdʒ/| n. vi.|苦工，做苦工；单调乏味的工作|A drudge is a person who does tedious, menial, or unpleasant work.  A drudge can also refer to the tedious work itself.|She felt like a drudge, constantly cleaning up after others. 她觉得自己像个苦力，不停地帮别人收拾残局。

He drudged away at his homework all evening. 他整个晚上都在辛苦地做作业。|Synonyms: toiler, laborer, menial, workhorse|Antonyms: idler, loafer|noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *drucche*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *drukkna* "to drown," suggesting a person overwhelmed by work.
