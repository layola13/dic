cuticle:/ˈkjuːtɪkl/ | n. |角质层；表皮 |A protective layer of overlapping cells, mostly made of keratin, covering the outer surface of hair shafts and the epidermis at the base of nails. | Example 1: The manicurist pushed back the cuticles on my fingernails.  美甲师把我指甲上的角质层往后推了。

Example 2:  Dry cuticles can be painful and unsightly.  干燥的角质层会很痛，而且不好看。| Synonym: epidermis,  skin | Antonym:  hypodermis (although not a direct opposite, it refers to a deeper layer of skin) | noun

cuticles:/ˈkjuːtɪklz/ | n. |(复数)角质层；表皮 |The plural form of cuticle, referring to multiple protective layers of overlapping cells. | Example 1:  My cuticles are always dry in the winter.  我的角质层在冬天总是很干燥。

Example 2:  Properly moisturizing your cuticles can prevent hangnails.  适当地滋润你的角质层可以防止倒刺。| Synonym: epidermis, skin | Antonym: (same as singular form) hypodermis | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cuticula,* diminutive of *cutis* "skin."
