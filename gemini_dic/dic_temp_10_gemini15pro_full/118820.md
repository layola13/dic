metanauplius: /ˌmɛtəˈnɔːpliəs/
noun
无尾类幼体
A larval stage in the development of some crustaceans, following the nauplius stage and characterized by the appearance of additional appendages.
The metanauplius larva swims freely and feeds on plankton. 无尾类幼体自由游动并以浮游生物为食。
The metanauplius stage is crucial for the development of many crustacean species. 无尾类幼体阶段对许多甲壳类物种的发育至关重要。
Synonyms:  zoea (though not always equivalent), postnauplius
Antonyms:  adult, mature (representing opposite ends of the developmental spectrum)
