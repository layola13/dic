hypersuggestibly:/ˌhaɪpər səˈdʒɛstɪbli/ | adv. | 过于容易受暗示地，极易被暗示地 |  In a manner that is excessively susceptible to suggestion, easily influenced by the opinions or ideas of others.  | The hypnotist found her hypersuggestibly receptive to his commands.  催眠师发现她极易接受他的指令。| 近义词: readily, easily, | 反义词: skeptically, resistantly | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
hyper- (prefix): from Greek, meaning "over, beyond, excessive"
suggest (verb): from Latin suggerere "to bring under, supply," from sub- "under" + gerere "to carry, bear"
-ible (suffix): from Latin -ibilis, meaning "capable of, worthy of"
-ly (suffix): forms adverbs from adjectives
