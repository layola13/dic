needlewomen: /ˈniːdlˌwɪmɪn/
n.
女裁缝，女针线工
Women who sew, especially as a profession or skilled hobby.
The needlewomen of the village were known for their intricate embroidery.  这个村庄的女裁缝以其复杂的刺绣而闻名。
The guild of needlewomen produced exquisite tapestries.  女裁缝协会制作了精美的挂毯。
近义词: seamstresses, sewers
反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps one could consider someone unskilled with a needle)
词性: noun

词源:  From "needle" + "woman" + plural suffix "-en" (an older form, now usually replaced by "-s"). "Needle" comes from Old English "nǣdl," ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*nēdlō," related to Old Norse "nál." "Woman" comes from Old English "wīfmann," literally "wife-man."
