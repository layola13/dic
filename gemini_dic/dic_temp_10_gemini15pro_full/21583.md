bluffest:/ˈblʌfɪst/ | adj. | 最虚张声势的 | Superlative form of bluff; most given to bluffing; most pretentious. | He was the bluffest poker player I'd ever seen, but he actually had a terrible hand. 他是我见过的最虚张声势的扑克玩家，但实际上他的牌很差。| 近义词：most pretentious, most deceptive | 反义词：most honest, most sincere | 词性: 形容词

bluff:/blʌf/ | n. | 悬崖；虚张声势 | A steep cliff, bank, or hill.  An attempt to deceive someone into believing that one can or will do something. | We hiked to the edge of the bluff and looked out at the ocean. 我们徒步走到悬崖边，眺望大海。 He called their bluff and they had to back down. 他识破了他们的虚张声势，他们不得不退缩。| 近义词：cliff, pretense, deception | 反义词：honesty, sincerity | 词性: 名词

bluff:/blʌf/ | v. | 虚张声势；吓唬 | To deceive someone by pretending to have more strength, confidence, or resources than one actually possesses. | He was bluffing when he said he had a royal flush. 当他说他有同花大顺时，他是在虚张声势。  She tried to bluff her way through the interview, but the interviewer saw right through her. 她试图蒙混过关，但面试官一眼就看穿了她。| 近义词：deceive, pretend, feign | 反义词：be honest, tell the truth | 词性: 动词
