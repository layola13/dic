murdress: /ˈmɜːrdrəs/ (often pronounced /ˈmɜːdrɪs/ in casual speech) | n. | 谋杀（尤指女性）, 残杀 | The act of killing someone, especially a woman, unlawfully and with premeditation.  It also can refer to brutal and indiscriminate killing. |  The murdress of the young heiress shocked the city.  年轻的女继承人的谋杀震惊了全城。| Synonyms: homicide, manslaughter, assassination, slaughter, carnage | Antonyms:  preservation, protection, rescue, saving | Noun

Etymology:  From the Middle English *murdresse*,  derived from Old French *murdriss-,* the stem of *murdrer* "to murder,"  ultimately from Late Latin *mordrum* "secret killing," of Germanic origin.
