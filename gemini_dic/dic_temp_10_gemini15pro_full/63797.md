epidemic: /ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk/ (US) /ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk/ (UK)| adj. n.| 流行性的，流行病 |A widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time.|  The flu epidemic of 1918 was devastating. 1918年的流感非常具有破坏性。|  The city was struggling to cope with the cholera epidemic.  这个城市正努力应对霍乱的流行。| 近义词: widespread, rampant, pandemic | 反义词: isolated, sporadic, endemic | 词性: adjective, noun

epidemics: /ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪks/ (US) /ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪks/ (UK)| n. | 流行病（复数） | Instances of a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time. |  Epidemics of measles are becoming less common thanks to vaccinations.  由于疫苗接种，麻疹的流行变得越来越少见。 |  History books are filled with accounts of devastating epidemics. 历史书中充满了对毁灭性流行病的描述。 | 近义词: outbreaks, plagues | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for the plural form, but singular antonym could be "isolated cases") | 词性: noun

词源: From Greek *epidēmios*  "prevalent among the people," from *epi* "upon, among" + *dēmos* "people."
