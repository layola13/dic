bellarmine: /ˈbɛlɑːrˌmaɪn/ | n. |  贝尔拉明啤酒壶; 贝尔拉明罐 | A stoneware jug with a rounded body and a short neck, often bearing a bearded face, popular in the 17th century and named after Cardinal Bellarmine, a Jesuit theologian who opposed the Protestant Reformation. | The museum displayed a collection of antique bellarmines. 博物馆展出了一系列古董贝尔拉明啤酒壶。|  Synonyms: Bartmann jug, greybeard jug, stone jug | Antonyms: N/A | noun
