antimodernism:/ˌæntiˈmɒdə(r)nɪzəm (British), ˌæntiˈmɑːdərnɪzəm (American)/
| n. |反现代主义|A movement or tendency opposed to modernism, especially in the arts. |Antimodernism gained traction in the early 20th century as a reaction against rapid societal changes. 反现代主义在20世纪初获得了吸引力，作为对社会快速变化的一种反应。 |The rise of antimodernism reflected a nostalgia for simpler times. 反现代主义的兴起反映了对更简单时代的怀念。|近义词：traditionalism, conservatism |反义词：modernism, avant-gardism |词性: 名词


Etymology:  "anti-" (prefix meaning "against, opposed to") + "modernist" (from "modern" + "-ist" (suffix denoting a follower of a doctrine or movement)).  Ultimately derived from Latin "modernus" (recent, new).
