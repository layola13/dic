licentiation:/ˌlaɪsɛnˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 授权，许可，特许 | The act of granting a license or the state of being licensed. |  The licentiation of the software allows users to install it on multiple computers.  该软件的授权允许用户将其安装在多台计算机上。| synonyms: authorization, permission, granting, concession | antonyms: prohibition, restriction, forbiddance | noun


licentiate:/laɪˈsɛnʃiət/ | n. | 获许可者，被授予执照者| A person who has been granted a license, especially in a particular profession or area of expertise. |  He is a licentiate of the Royal College of Physicians. 他是皇家内科医学院的获许可者。 | synonyms: licensee, graduate, diploma holder | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, consider unlicensed individual/practitioner) | noun
