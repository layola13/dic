humbly:/ˈhʌmbli/| adv.|谦逊地，谦恭地|In a way that shows you do not think you are better than other people; in a way that shows you are not proud.|She humbly accepted the award. 她谦逊地接受了奖项。|Synonyms: modestly, respectfully, unpretentiously|Antonyms: arrogantly, proudly, haughtily|adverb
humbly:/ˈhʌmbli/| adv.|卑微地，低微地|In a way that shows you have a low social position.|He came from humble beginnings. 他出身卑微。|Synonyms: low, lowly, mean, simple|Antonyms: high, noble, grand|adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *humbely, humbly*, from Old French *humblement*, from *humble* + *-ment*.  Ultimately from Latin *humilis* (“low, humble”), from *humus* (“ground”).
