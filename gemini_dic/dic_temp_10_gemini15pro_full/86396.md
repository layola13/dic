hedging:/ˈhedʒɪŋ/| n. v.|套期保值，规避，避免明确表态|The act of protecting oneself against financial loss by making balancing or compensating transactions. Also, the act of avoiding commitment by leaving open a way of retreat. |Example: The company used currency hedging to protect itself against fluctuations in the exchange rate. 这家公司使用货币对冲来保护自己免受汇率波动的影响。

hedging:/ˈhedʒɪŋ/| n. v.|套期保值，规避，避免明确表态|Avoiding making a firm commitment or expressing a strong opinion. |Example:  He was hedging his bets by applying to several universities. 他为了保险起见，申请了多所大学。

hedging:/ˈhedʒɪŋ/| n. v.|套期保值，规避，避免明确表态|Planting shrubs or other plants to form a hedge.|Example: The gardener was hedging the property with privet. 园丁正在用女贞树篱围住这处房产。


近义词: mitigating, avoiding, evading, qualifying, circumventing
反义词: speculating, embracing, committing, endorsing
词性: noun, verb

词源: From Middle English hegge, from Old English hecg "hedge," from Proto-Germanic *hagjo- "enclosure," from PIE *kagh-yo-, from root *kagh- "to catch, seize." The financial sense of "protecting against loss" arose from the idea of enclosing or limiting risk.
