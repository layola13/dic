faulter:/ˈfɔːltər/ (US) /ˈfɔːltə(r)/ (UK)| n. vi. | 犹豫者，支吾者； 蹒跚； 衰弱； 结巴；|A person who hesitates or stammers.  To speak hesitantly or brokenly. To move unsteadily or stumble. To lose strength or momentum.|
His voice faltered as he spoke about the accident. 他谈到事故时，声音颤抖了。
She faltered for a moment, then continued walking. 她犹豫了一下，然后继续走。|
近义词：hesitate, stumble, waver, stammer|
反义词：persist, persevere, continue, proceed|
词性: noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *falteren, frequentative of *fallen (“to fall”), from Old English *fallan, feallan (“to fall”), from Proto-Germanic *fallaną (“to fall”), from Proto-Indo-European *ph₂l- (“to fall”). Cognate with Scots faulter (“to falter”), West Frisian falterje (“to falter”), Dutch falteren (“to falter”), German faltern (“to falter”), Danish falde (“to fall”), Swedish falla (“to fall”), Icelandic falla (“to fall”).
