oversteadinesses: /ˌoʊvərˈstɛdɪnɪsɪz/ | n. | 过于稳定；过度稳态 |  The state of being excessively steady or unwavering, often to a fault. It implies a rigidity or inflexibility that can be detrimental. | His oversteadinesses in sticking to the plan, despite changing circumstances, ultimately led to the project's failure. 他过于坚持计划，尽管情况发生了变化，最终导致了项目的失败。| 近义词: inflexibility, rigidity, stubbornness | 反义词: flexibility, adaptability, dynamism | 词性: 名词

词源: "oversteadinesses" comes from "oversteady" + "-ness" + "-es". "Oversteady" is formed by the prefix "over-" (meaning "excessive" or "too much") and "steady" (meaning firm, unwavering).  "-ness" is a suffix used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality. "-es" is a plural suffix.
