gendarmes:/ˈʒɑːndɑːrmz/ | n. |宪兵，警察| French armed police, similar to military police; often used in English to refer to police, especially in a rural or paramilitary context. | The gendarmes patrolled the countryside. 宪兵在乡间巡逻。| The gendarmes investigated the robbery. 宪兵调查了这起抢劫案。|Synonyms: police, officers, constabulary | Antonyms: criminals, lawbreakers | noun

gendarme:/ˈʒɑːndɑːrm/ | n. |宪兵，警察| A member of the gendarmerie, a French armed police force. | The gendarme directed traffic. 宪兵指挥交通。| The gendarme questioned the witness. 宪兵询问了目击者。|Synonyms: policeman, officer, constable | Antonyms: criminal, lawbreaker | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gent d'armes ("people of arms"), plural of gent d'arme, from Latin gentem ("people, race, tribe, nation, clan, family") + de ("of") + arma ("arms, weapons").
