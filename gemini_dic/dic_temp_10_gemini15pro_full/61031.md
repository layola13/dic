elitism:/ɪˈliːtɪzəm/ /əˈliːtɪzəm/ | n. |精英主义；杰出人物统治论|The belief that a society or system should be led by an elite.  The attitude that society should be governed by a select group of individuals considered superior by virtue of their intellect, talent, or social standing.|  Many critics see elitism in the selection process for top universities. 许多评论家认为顶尖大学的招生过程中存在精英主义。|  The club is known for its elitism and exclusivity.  这家俱乐部以其精英主义和排他性而闻名。 | 近义词：snobbishness, exclusiveness | 反义词：egalitarianism, populism | 词性: noun

elitist:/ɪˈliːtɪst/ /əˈliːtɪst/ | adj. n. |精英主义的；精英主义者| Relating to or supporting the view that a society or system should be led by an elite; a person who believes that a society or system should be led by an elite. |  The elitist policies of the government alienated many voters. 政府的精英主义政策疏远了许多选民。 | He's been accused of being an elitist. 他被指责为精英主义者。| 近义词：snobbish, exclusive | 反义词：egalitarian, populist | 词性: adjective, noun
