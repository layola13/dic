paralyse:/ˈpærəlaɪz/| vt.|使瘫痪，使麻痹|The accident paralysed him from the waist down.  事故导致他腰部以下瘫痪。|近义词：disable, cripple|反义词:enable, rehabilitate|动词
paralytic:/ˌpærəˈlɪtɪk/| adj.|瘫痪的，麻痹的|He suffers from a paralytic illness. 他患有瘫痪性疾病。|近义词：disabled, crippled|反义词:able-bodied, healthy|形容词
paralysation (or paralysis, US): /ˌpærəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ /pəˈrælɪsɪs/| n.|瘫痪，麻痹（英式拼写，美式常用paralysis）|The paralysation of the transport network caused chaos. 交通网络的瘫痪造成了混乱。|近义词：numbness, immobility|反义词:mobility, movement|名词

词源: From Late Latin *paralysationem, from Latin paralyticus (“of or pertaining to paralysis”), from Ancient Greek παράλυσις (parálusis, “loosening, relaxing, enfeeblement”), from παραλύω (paralúō, “I disable, loosen, relax”), from παρά (pará, “beside, from, against”) + λύω (lúō, “I loosen, release, dissolve”).
