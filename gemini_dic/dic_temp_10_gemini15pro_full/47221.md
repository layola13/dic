daywoman:/ˈdeɪˌwʊmən/ | n. |女佣，白天做家务的女工|A woman who is employed to do housework during the day. |例句：The daywoman comes in three times a week to clean.  这个女佣每周来三次打扫卫生。

例句：My grandmother employed a daywoman to help her with the chores. 我祖母雇了一个女佣帮她做家务。|近义词：charwoman, cleaning lady, maid, housekeeper |反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps employer) |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From 'day' + 'woman'.  'Day' indicating the work is performed during daytime hours, and 'woman' referring to the gender of the worker. This compound word is a straightforward combination of its constituent parts.
