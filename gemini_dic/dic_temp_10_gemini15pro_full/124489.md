mouldiest:/ˈməʊldiɪst/ | adj. | 最发霉的 | Covered with mould to the greatest degree; most affected by mould.  |  The bread in the back of the cupboard was the mouldiest I'd ever seen.  橱柜后面的面包是我见过的最发霉的。| The fruit at the bottom of the bowl was mouldiest.  碗底的水果最发霉。 | Synonyms: fustiest, musty, dankest, stalest | Antonyms: freshest, cleanest, newest | adjective

mouldy:/ˈməʊldi/ | adj. | 发霉的 | Covered with or smelling of mould. | The cheese was mouldy so I threw it away. 奶酪发霉了，所以我把它扔掉了。| The old books in the attic smelled mouldy. 阁楼里的旧书闻起来有霉味。 | Synonyms: musty, fusty, mildewy, stale | Antonyms: fresh, clean, new | adjective

mould:/məʊld/ | n. | 霉菌；模具 | A furry growth of minute fungal hyphae forming on the surface of organic matter, especially in damp conditions; a hollow container used to give shape to molten or hot liquid material when it cools and hardens. | There was mould growing on the wall. 墙上长了霉菌。|  The jelly was set in a mould. 果冻在模具中凝固。 | Synonyms: mildew, fungus, template, cast | Antonyms:  (For the fungus sense) sterility, cleanliness (For the mould/cast sense)  N/A | noun

mould:/məʊld/ | v. | 塑造；浇铸 | To shape (something) in a mould; to influence the formation or development of. | He moulded the clay into a pot. 他用粘土塑成一个罐子。|  Her experiences have moulded her character. 她的经历塑造了她的性格。| Synonyms: shape, form, fashion, influence | Antonyms: deform, distort, misshape | verb
