muckworm:/ˈmʌkwɜːrm/| n. |吝啬鬼，守财奴；卑鄙小人|A person who is obsessed with money, often seen as greedy and miserly; a despicable person.|He's a real muckworm, hoarding all his wealth and never spending a dime on anyone. 他真是个吝啬鬼，囤积所有财富，从不在任何人身上花一分钱。|近义词：miser, skinflint, cheapskate, scoundrel|反义词：philanthropist, spendthrift|词性:名词

词源:
From muck + worm.  "Muck" referring to dirt, filth, or manure, suggesting someone who grovels in or is obsessed with lowly things (like money). "Worm" adds a connotation of being a lowly, insignificant creature.
