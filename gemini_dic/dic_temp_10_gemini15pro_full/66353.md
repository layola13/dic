excursive:/ɪkˈskɜːrsɪv/ | adj. |  离题的；散漫的；游览的 | Passing from one topic to another; digressing; rambling. Also, relating to a digression or excursion. |  His lectures were entertaining but rather excursive, covering a wide range of unrelated topics. 他的演讲很有趣，但内容太散漫，涵盖了各种不相关的主题。 | Example: The professor's excursive lecture covered everything from ancient history to modern physics.  教授离题的演讲涵盖了从古代历史到现代物理的方方面面。| synonyms: digressive, rambling, wandering, desultory | antonyms: focused, concise, succinct, relevant | adjective

excursive:/ɪkˈskɜːrsɪv/ | n. |  离题；远足；游览 | A digression or deviation from the main subject.  | His book contained several interesting excursus on related subjects.  他的书中包含几个关于相关主题的有趣的附录。 | Example: The author's excursive into the history of the region provided valuable context for the novel. 作者对该地区历史的探讨为小说提供了宝贵的背景。| synonyms: digression, deviation, excursion, tangent | antonyms: focus, main point, central theme | noun
