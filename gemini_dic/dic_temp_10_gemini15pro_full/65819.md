eviler:/ˈiːvɪlər/ | adj. | 更邪恶的，更坏的 | Comparative form of "evil," meaning more wicked or morally wrong. | He was an eviler man than his brother. 他比他哥哥更坏。|  The eviler of the two villains plotted to overthrow the king.  两个恶棍中较邪恶的那个密谋推翻国王。 | Near Synonyms: wickeder, more corrupt, more malevolent | Near Antonyms: better, kinder, more virtuous | Word Type: Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English "yfel" (evil), from Proto-Germanic "*ubilaz" (evil), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*up-elo-" (bad, over). The comparative "-er" suffix is a standard Germanic ending indicating a higher degree.
