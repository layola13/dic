amaranthus: /ˌæməˈrænθəs/ | n. | 苋菜；不凋花 | A genus of flowering plants, many of which are cultivated for their colorful foliage and edible seeds. |  The ancient Greeks associated amaranthus with immortality. 古希腊人把苋菜与永生联系在一起。

amaranthus: /ˌæməˈrænθəs/ | n. | 苋菜籽 | The seeds of the amaranthus plant, often used as a grain. | Amaranthus seeds are a good source of protein and fiber. 苋菜籽是蛋白质和纤维的良好来源。


近义词: pigweed (especially for weedy species), love-lies-bleeding (for some ornamental species)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *amaranthus*, from Ancient Greek ἀμάραντος (amárantos, “unfading”), from ἀ- (a-, “not”) + μαραίνω (maraínō, “I wither, fade”).
