opisthoglypha:/əˌpɪsθəˈɡlaɪfə/ | n. | 后沟牙蛇类 | A suborder of snakes characterized by having enlarged, grooved teeth positioned near the rear of the maxilla (upper jaw). These teeth are used to inject venom into prey. | Opisthoglyphous snakes, like the boomslang, possess rear fangs. 后沟牙蛇类，例如非洲树蛇，拥有后沟牙。 | 近义词: rear-fanged snakes | 反义词: proteroglypha (前沟牙蛇类), solenoglypha (管牙蛇类), aglypha (无毒蛇) | noun
