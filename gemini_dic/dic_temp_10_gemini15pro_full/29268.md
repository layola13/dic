cassonade:/ˌkæsəˈneɪd/ /ˌkæsəˈnɑːd/
|n.|
|红糖，赤砂糖|
|A coarse, brown sugar that is similar to turbinado sugar, but contains more molasses. It's less refined than granulated sugar and has a moist texture and a deep caramel flavor.|
|I added a sprinkle of cassonade to the top of the crème brûlée for a crunchy, caramelized crust.| 我在焦糖布丁的顶部撒上了一点红糖，使其形成酥脆的焦糖外壳。

|She used cassonade in the apple pie filling to give it a richer flavor.| 她在苹果派馅料中使用了红糖，使其味道更浓郁。
|近义词：brown sugar, turbinado sugar, demerara sugar|
|反义词：white sugar, granulated sugar|
|词性：名词|

词源:
From French *cassonade*, from *casse* (“husk, shell, peel”) (from Occitan *cassa*, from Latin *capsa* (“box, case”)), + *-onade* (suffix forming nouns denoting products made from the base word). Literally “product made from husks/shells”.  The term originally referred to the outer crust of a sugar loaf.
