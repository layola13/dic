barrens:/ˈbærənz/| n. |荒地，贫瘠之地|A barren tract of land, typically treeless.|例句：The pioneers struggled to survive in the barrens.  拓荒者们在荒地上挣扎求生。

例句：The lunar barrens stretched out before the astronauts.  月球荒原在宇航员面前延伸开来。|近义词：wasteland, desert, badlands|反义词：fertile land, oasis|词性: 名词

barren:/ˈbærən/| adj. |贫瘠的，不毛的；不育的|Too poor to produce much or any vegetation; unproductive; infertile.|例句：The farmer struggled to cultivate the barren soil.  农民努力耕种贫瘠的土地。

例句：The barren landscape offered little hope for the travelers. 贫瘠的景象给旅行者带来渺茫的希望。|近义词：infertile, sterile, unproductive|反义词：fertile, productive, fruitful|词性: 形容词


词源: From Middle English *baren, from Old English *bǣr, from Proto-Germanic *barjaz (“bare”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰosós (“bare, naked”).  Cognate with German bar (“bare”), Dutch baar (“bare”), Icelandic ber (“bare”).  The sense development from “bare” to “barren” is through the notion of “bare of vegetation.”
