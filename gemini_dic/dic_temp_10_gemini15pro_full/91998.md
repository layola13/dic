hypernymy:/ˌhaɪpərˈnɪmi/ | n. |上位词，上义词| A semantic relationship between a more general term (the hypernym) and a more specific instance of it (the hyponym). For example, "animal" is a hypernym of "dog," and "dog" is a hyponym of "animal." |The word "furniture" is a hypernym, encompassing terms like chair, table, and bed.| 近义词: superordinate | 反义词: hyponymy | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Ancient Greek ὑπέρ (hupér, “over, above”) + ὄνυμα (ónuma, “name”).
