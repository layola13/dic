caryatid: /ˌkærɪˈætɪd/| n. |女像柱|A sculpted female figure serving as an architectural support, typically an Ionic column.|例句：The caryatids supporting the porch roof were intricately carved.  门廊屋顶的女像柱雕刻精美。

例句：The museum featured several ancient Greek caryatids, each with a unique pose and expression.  博物馆展出了几尊古希腊女像柱，每一尊都有独特的姿势和表情。|近义词：atlant, telamon (male equivalent)|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *caryatide*, from Latin *Caryatides*, plural of *Caryatis*, from Greek *Καρυάτις* (Karyatis), a woman of Caryae (Κάρυαι), a town in Laconia, ancient Greece. The women of Caryae were famed for their beauty and grace.  According to the Roman architect Vitruvius, they were enslaved by the Greeks after siding with the Persians, and their image was used as columns as a symbol of their burden.
