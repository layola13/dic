magnifico:/mæɡˈnɪfɪkoʊ/ (US), /mæɡˈnɪfɪkəʊ/ (UK)| adj.|宏伟的，壮丽的；极好的，精彩的|Magnifico is used to describe something that is extremely good, beautiful, or impressive, often used in a slightly dramatic or exaggerated way.  It comes from the Italian word "magnifico," meaning "magnificent."|The fireworks display was absolutely magnifico.  烟火表演绝对精彩。|Synonyms: magnificent, splendid, superb, wonderful, excellent|Antonyms: awful, terrible, dreadful, poor, mediocre|adjective

magnifico:/mæɡˈnɪfɪkoʊ/ (US), /mæɡˈnɪfɪkəʊ/ (UK)| n.|要人，大人物 (主要用于意大利语，在英语中较少见)|In Italian, "magnifico" can also be a noun referring to a person of high rank or importance.
