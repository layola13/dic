motleyer:/ˈmɒtliər/ (US) /ˈmɒt.li.ər/ (UK)| n. | 小丑，穿着花哨衣服的人 | A person dressed in motley, especially a jester or fool. | The king's motleyer entertained the court with jokes and juggling. 国王的弄臣用笑话和杂耍娱乐宫廷。| synonyms: jester, fool, clown, harlequin | antonyms: serious person, king, queen | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *motleyer*, from *motley* (multicolored cloth) + *-er* (agent suffix).  Ultimately from Old French *motelé*, past participle of *moteler* "to variegate," of uncertain origin.
