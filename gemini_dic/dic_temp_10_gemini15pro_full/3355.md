affricates:/ˈæfrɪkɪts/ | n. | 塞擦音 | A sound produced by stopping the airflow, then releasing it with friction, like the 'ch' in 'church' or the 'j' in 'judge'. | Example: The sounds /tʃ/ and /dʒ/ are affricates.  塞擦音/tʃ/和/dʒ/是塞擦音。| Example:  Many languages use affricates in their phonology. 许多语言在其音系中使用塞擦音。 | Synonym: affricate consonant | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but could be contrasted with fricatives or plosives) | noun
