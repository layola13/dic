connivingly:/kəˈnaɪvɪŋli/
| adv. | 诡诈地，阴险地 |  in a secret and dishonest way, with the intention of deceiving or tricking someone |  He connivingly plotted to steal his brother's inheritance. 他阴险地密谋窃取他兄弟的遗产。|  deceitfully, craftily, slyly, schemingly | honestly, openly, frankly | adverb

connive:/kəˈnaɪv/
| v. | 串通，共谋，纵容 | secretly allow (something considered immoral, illegal, wrong, or harmful) to continue | The guards connived at the prisoner's escape. 警卫纵容了囚犯的逃跑。|  conspire, collude, plot | obstruct, prevent, expose | verb

ETYMOLOGY->

From Latin *conivēre*, from *com-* (together) + *nīvēre* (to wink, close the eyes).  The original sense was "to close one's eyes to something wrong," hence "to give tacit consent to wrongdoing."
