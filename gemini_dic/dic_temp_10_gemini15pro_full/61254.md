emancipating:/ɪˈmænsɪpeɪtɪŋ/
|v. |解放，使摆脱束缚|Freeing someone from slavery or other forms of oppression; liberating.| The Emancipation Proclamation emancipating enslaved people in the Confederate states.  解放黑奴宣言解放了邦联各州的奴隶。|Synonyms: liberating, freeing, releasing, delivering|Antonyms: enslaving, oppressing, restricting, confining|Verb

emancipated:/ɪˈmænsɪpeɪtɪd/
|adj.|被解放的，获得自由的|Freed from social, political, or legal restrictions.|  Emancipated women played a key role in the suffrage movement.  获得解放的妇女在争取选举权运动中发挥了关键作用。|Synonyms: liberated, freed, unrestricted, autonomous|Antonyms: enslaved, oppressed, restricted, confined|Adjective

emancipation:/ɪˌmænsɪˈpeɪʃn/
|n.|解放，摆脱|The fact or process of being set free from legal, social, or political restrictions; liberation.|The emancipation of slaves marked a turning point in American history.  奴隶的解放标志着美国历史的一个转折点。|Synonyms: liberation, freedom, release, deliverance|Antonyms: enslavement, oppression, restriction, confinement|Noun
