acids: /ˈæsɪdz/ | n. | 酸 |  A substance that in solution tastes sour, reacts with a base to form a salt, and turns litmus paper red.  A substance that can donate a proton (hydrogen ion) to another substance. |  Hydrochloric acid is a strong acid. 盐酸是一种强酸。

Many fruits contain natural acids. 许多水果含有天然酸。 | 近义词: sours | 反义词: bases, alkalis | 词性: 名词

词源: From Middle French *acide* (modern French *acide*), from Latin *acidus* "sour, sharp, tart," figuratively "unpleasant, disagreeable," from *acēre* "to be sour."
