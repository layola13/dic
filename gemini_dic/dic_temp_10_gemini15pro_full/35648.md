coaxed:/koʊkst/| v. (past tense)|哄骗，劝诱|Persuaded someone gently and patiently to do something.|She coaxed the shy child out from behind the sofa. 她哄着那个害羞的孩子从沙发后面出来。|
He coaxed a smile from his grieving friend. 他劝慰他悲伤的朋友，使他露出了一丝笑容。|
Synonyms: persuaded, cajoled, wheedled, enticed, urged|Antonyms: forced, compelled, coerced, intimidated|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cokesen*, from *cokes* ("simpleton, fool"), perhaps a pet-form of *cock*.  The original meaning was "to make a fool of, pet, fondle," which gradually shifted to "persuade by flattery."
