half-informing:/ˌhæf ɪnˈfɔːrmɪŋ/ | adj. | 半知半解的，提供不完整信息的 | Providing information that is incomplete and therefore potentially misleading. | The article was half-informing, leaving the reader with more questions than answers. 这篇文章信息不完整，给读者留下了比答案更多的问题。| The government released a half-informing statement about the incident. 政府发布了一份关于该事件的含糊其辞的声明。 | Near Synonyms: misleading, incomplete, vague, partial | Antonyms: comprehensive, thorough, complete, informative | Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
"Half-" is an Old English word meaning "half."  "Informing" comes from the Old French "enformer," ultimately from the Latin "informare," meaning "to shape, train, instruct."  The combination "half-informing" signifies the act of informing only partially or incompletely.
