ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *couardise*, from Old French *coardise*, from *coart*, *couart* (“coward”), from Vulgar Latin *cōdārius* (“one who drops his tail”), from Latin *cauda* (“tail”).  The suffix *-ice* derives from the French *-ice*, from Latin *-itia*.  The notion is of an animal tucking its tail between its legs in fear.
