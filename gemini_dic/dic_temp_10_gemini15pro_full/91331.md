hydrophoby:/ˌhaɪdrəˈfoʊbiə/ | n. | 恐水症；狂犬病；憎水性 | An abnormal or unnatural dread of water.  A morbid dread of water, especially as a symptom of rabies in humans. Also, the property of not readily absorbing or mixing with water. |  Example 1: The dog exhibited signs of hydrophobia, foaming at the mouth and avoiding water. 这只狗表现出恐水症的迹象，口吐白沫，避水。

Example 2:  Certain fabrics have hydrophoby built in to repel water. 某些织物内置了憎水性以防水。 | Synonym: aquaphobia (for the fear), rabies (for the disease), water-repellence | Antonym:hydrophilia (for the property) | noun
