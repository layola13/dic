obliteration:/əˌblɪtəˈreɪʃn/
|n.|消除，抹去，毁灭|The obliteration of the evidence made it impossible to solve the crime.  证据的抹去使得这起犯罪无法侦破。|
|n.|涂抹，删除|The obliteration of the inscription made it difficult to date the artifact.  碑文的涂抹使得难以确定文物的年代。|
|n.|忘却，湮没|Time has caused the obliteration of many childhood memories. 时间流逝抹去了许多童年的记忆。|
Synonyms:destruction, eradication, annihilation, erasure, deletion
|Antonyms:preservation, creation, construction, restoration|
Etymology:From Latin obliterare "to erase, blot out, efface," from ob "over" + literare "to smear with letters," from litera "letter."
