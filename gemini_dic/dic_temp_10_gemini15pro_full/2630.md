adopted: /əˈdɑːptɪd/ (US) /əˈdɒptɪd/ (UK)| adj.|被收养的；采用的|Taken into one's family as a relation, especially as a son or daughter; or accepted, used, or followed.|例句：They adopted a child from China. 他们从中国收养了一个孩子。

She has adopted a vegan lifestyle. 她采用了一种纯素的生活方式。|近义词：foster, accepted, chosen, embraced|反义词：biological, natural, rejected|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *adoptāre*, frequentative of *adōptāre* "to choose, select, elect," from *ad-* "to" + *optāre* "to choose, wish, desire."

USAGE->

Present Tense:
The adopted child is now a teenager. =  这个被收养的孩子现在是青少年了。
My family has adopted several stray cats. = 我的家庭收养了几只流浪猫。

Past Tense:
He was adopted when he was a baby. = 他在婴儿时期就被收养了。
The company adopted a new marketing strategy last year. =  这家公司去年采用了一种新的营销策略。

Present Perfect Tense:
They have adopted two children from overseas. = 他们从海外收养了两个孩子。
The school has adopted a new curriculum. = 这所学校采用了新的课程。

Past Perfect Tense:
She had been adopted before she could remember. = 她记事之前就被收养了。
The committee had already adopted the proposal before the meeting. = 委员会在会议之前就已经采纳了这项提议。

Future Tense:
They will adopt a dog once they move to a bigger house. = 他们搬到更大的房子后就会养一只狗。
The government will adopt new measures to combat inflation. = 政府将采取新措施来对抗通货膨胀。

Subjunctive Mood:
If I were to adopt a child, I would adopt a baby girl. = 如果我要收养一个孩子，我会收养一个女婴。
I wish they had adopted a different approach. = 我希望他们采用不同的方法。

Passive Voice:
The new policy was adopted by the board. = 新政策被董事会采纳了。
A more sustainable approach is being adopted by many companies. = 许多公司正在采用更可持续的方法。
