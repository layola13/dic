manichees:/ˌmænɪˈkiːz/
noun
摩尼教徒; 绝对二元论者
Followers of Manichaeism, a religion founded by Mani in the 3rd century AD, characterized by a dualistic cosmology of light and darkness, good and evil.  Also used to describe someone who sees the world in strictly black and white terms.
The Manichees believed in a cosmic struggle between good and evil. 摩尼教徒相信善恶之间存在着宇宙斗争。
Some politicians take a Manichee view of international relations, seeing everything as a conflict between "us" and "them." 一些政治家对国际关系持摩尼教式的观点，将一切视为“我们”和“他们”之间的冲突。
synonyms: dualist, absolutist
antonyms: relativist, pragmatist
