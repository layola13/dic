anaesthetists: /əˈniːsθətɪsts/ (UK) /ænəsˈθiːtɪsts/ (US)| n. |麻醉师|A doctor who specializes in administering anesthetics.|例句：The anaesthetists carefully monitored the patient's vital signs during the surgery. 麻醉师在手术期间仔细监测病人的生命体征。

例句：Experienced anaesthetists are essential for safe and successful surgical procedures.  经验丰富的麻醉师对于安全和成功的手术至关重要。

近义词：anesthesiologists|反义词: None|词性: noun

Etymology:  From Greek "anaisthesia," meaning "lack of sensation,"  composed of "an-" (without) + "aisthesis" (sensation).  The "-ist" suffix denotes a person who practices or is skilled in a particular field.
