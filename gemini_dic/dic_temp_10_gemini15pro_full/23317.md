boycotts:/ˈbɔɪkɒts/ (UK), /ˈbɔɪkɑːts/ (US)| n. v.|抵制，联合抵制|An act of voluntarily abstaining from using, buying, or dealing with a person, organization, or country as an expression of protest, usually for political or social reasons.  A refusal to buy or use goods and services.|例句：Many people boycotted the company's products due to their unethical labor practices. 许多人因为该公司不道德的劳工行为而抵制其产品。

例句：The students boycotted the school cafeteria to protest the poor quality of food. 学生们抵制学校食堂，抗议食物质量差。|近义词：ban, embargo, sanction, shun, ostracize|反义词:patronize, support|词性:名词, 动词
