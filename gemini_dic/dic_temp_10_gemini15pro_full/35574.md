coalpit: /ˈkoʊlˌpɪt/ (US), /ˈkəʊlˌpɪt/ (UK)| n.|煤坑，煤矿|A pit from which coal is mined.  A coal mine, especially a deep one.|The miners spent their days toiling in the dark and dangerous coalpit.|mine, colliery|quarry|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *colpitte*, from Old English *colpytt* (“coal pit”), equivalent to *col* (“coal”) + *pytt* (“pit”). Cognate with Middle Low German *kolput* (“coal pit”).
