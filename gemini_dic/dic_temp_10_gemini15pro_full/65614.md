evangelicalism: /ˌiːvænˈdʒɛlɪkəlɪzəm/ | n. |福音主义，福音派基督教|A worldwide transdenominational movement within Protestant Christianity maintaining that the essence of the gospel consists in the doctrine of salvation by grace through faith in Jesus Christ's atonement. | Evangelicalism is a growing movement in many parts of the world. 福音主义在世界许多地方都是一个正在发展的运动。|  The preacher's sermon focused on the core tenets of evangelicalism. 牧师的布道集中在福音主义的核心原则上。| Synonyms: revivalism, evangelism | Antonyms: secularism, atheism | noun

evangelical: /ˌiːvænˈdʒɛlɪk(ə)l/ | adj. | 福音的，福音派的；热衷传福音的 | Of or relating to a Christian sect or group that stresses the authority of the Bible, the importance of personal conversion and faith as the means of salvation, and the obligation to evangelize. |  He attends an evangelical church. 他参加一个福音派教会。|  She has a strong evangelical faith. 她有坚定的福音信仰。| Synonyms:  gospel, fundamentalist | Antonyms: liberal, secular | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *evangelicalis, from Ancient Greek εὐαγγελικός (euangelikós, “of or pertaining to the gospel”), from εὐαγγέλιον (euangélion, “good news, gospel”). Doublet of evangelistic.
