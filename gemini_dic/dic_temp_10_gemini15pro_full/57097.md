ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *doughti*, from Old English *dōhtig* (“valiant, doughty, useful”), from *dugan* (“to be strong, to avail”), from Proto-Germanic *duganą* (“to be strong, to avail”). Cognate with Old Frisian *duchtich* (“excellent, honest, worthy”), Dutch *deugdelijk* (“virtuous, excellent, sound”), German *tüchtig* (“efficient, capable, fit”).
