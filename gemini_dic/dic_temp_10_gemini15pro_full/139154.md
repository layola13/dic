obfuscate:/ˈɒbfəskeɪt/ (UK) /ˈɑːbfəskeɪt/ (US)| v. |使模糊；使混乱；使困惑|To make something unclear, obscure, or unintelligible.|The government tried to obfuscate the issue with complex legal jargon. 政府试图用复杂的法律术语来混淆这个问题。|The writer's style is deliberately obfuscated.  作者的风格故意晦涩难懂。|近义词：obscure, confuse, bewilder, muddle, confound|反义词：clarify, elucidate, illuminate|词性:动词

obfuscation:/ˌɒbfəˈskeɪʃən/ (UK) /ˌɑːbfəˈskeɪʃən/ (US)| n. |模糊；混乱；困惑|The act of making something unclear, obscure, or unintelligible.|The report was filled with deliberate obfuscation. 这份报告充满了故意的含糊其辞。|Her testimony was marked by obfuscation and evasion. 她的证词充满了混淆和回避。|近义词：obscurity, confusion, bewilderment|反义词：clarity, elucidation|词性:名词

词源: From Latin *obfuscatus*, past participle of *obfuscare* "to darken," from *ob* "over" + *fuscare* "to darken," from *fuscus* "dark, dusky."
