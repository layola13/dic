blench:/blɛntʃ/ | v. |  变白；退缩；畏缩 | To suddenly become pale, usually because you are frightened or shocked; to flinch or shrink back in fear. |  He blenched at the sight of the blood. 他看到血就脸色发白。 | synonyms: blanch, flinch, recoil, quail, wince | antonyms:  flush, redden, face, confront | verb

blenched:/blɛntʃt/ | adj. |  变白的；畏缩的 | Having become pale, usually from fear or shock.  | His blenched face showed his terror. 他苍白的脸显示了他的恐惧。 | synonyms: pale, white, ashen, wan | antonyms: flushed, ruddy, rosy | adjective
