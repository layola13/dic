embracer:/ɪmˈbreɪsər/| n.|拥抱者；信奉者；接受者|A person who embraces something (literally or figuratively).|She was an early embracer of new technology. 她很早就接受了新技术。|近义词：adopter, supporter, advocate|反义词：opponent, resister|noun

embracing:/ɪmˈbreɪsɪŋ/| adj.|拥抱的；包容的；环绕的|Characterized by embracing or acceptance.|The community had an embracing attitude towards newcomers. 这个社区对新来的人持包容的态度。|近义词：accepting, welcoming, inclusive|反义词：rejecting, excluding|adjective

embrace:/ɪmˈbreɪs/| vt.|拥抱；欣然接受；信奉；包含|To hold closely in one's arms, typically as a sign of affection; to accept or support (a belief, theory, or change) willingly and enthusiastically.|He embraced his daughter warmly. 他热情地拥抱了他的女儿。
She embraced the opportunity to study abroad. 她欣然接受了出国留学的机会。|近义词：hug, accept, adopt, support|反义词：reject, oppose, resist|verb
