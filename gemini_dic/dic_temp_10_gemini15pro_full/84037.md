half-farthing:/ˌhɑːf ˈfɑːrðɪŋ/ | n. | 半法寻 (一种旧时的英国硬币，值四分之一便士) | A former British coin worth a quarter of a farthing, and one-eighth of a penny. |  In Victorian times, a half-farthing was the smallest denomination of currency. 在维多利亚时代，半法寻是最小的货币单位。

|  Half-farthings were not commonly used and were primarily for trade with Britain's colonies. 半法寻不常用，主要用于与英国殖民地的贸易。| 近义词: (in terms of small value) mite, pittance | 反义词: (in terms of large value) fortune, king's ransom | 词性: noun

词源:  "Half" + "farthing". "Farthing" comes from Old English *fēorþing, from *fēorþa (fourth) + -ing (diminutive suffix).  Therefore, a half-farthing is literally half of a fourth of a penny.
