incenser:/ˈɪnsɛnsər/| n.|香炉，焚香器; 激怒者|A container in which incense is burned.  Something that provokes strong feelings, especially anger or indignation.|The monk lit the incenser and the temple filled with a sweet aroma.  和尚点燃了香炉，寺庙里充满了甜蜜的香气。|Synonyms: censer, thurible, burner, provoker, instigator|Antonyms: soother, pacifier|noun


incense:/ˈɪnsɛns/| n. vt.|香; 激怒|A substance that produces a fragrant odor when burned. To make (someone) very angry.|The incense filled the air with a calming scent. 香充满了空气，散发出一种令人平静的香味。  The politician's insensitive remarks incensed the public.  这位政客的麻木不仁的言论激怒了公众。|Synonyms: fragrance, perfume, aroma; enrage, infuriate, anger|Antonyms: stench, stink; soothe, placate|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *encenser* (noun) and *encensier* (verb), derived from Late Latin *incensarium* (noun) and *incensare* (verb), from Latin *incendere* "to set on fire," from *in- "into" + *candere* "to glow."
