mesmerised:/ˈmezməraɪzd/ (UK) /ˈmɛzməˌraɪzd/ (US)| adj.|着迷的，被迷住的|Being completely fascinated or hypnotized by something or someone.|例句：I was mesmerised by the dancer's graceful movements. 我被舞者优雅的动作迷住了。

例句：The audience was mesmerised by the magician's performance. 观众被魔术师的表演迷住了。

|近义词：captivated, enthralled, fascinated, hypnotized|反义词：bored, uninterested, indifferent|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the name of Franz Mesmer (1734-1815), an Austrian physician who popularized hypnotism (then called "mesmerism").  The word originally referred to being in a hypnotic trance but broadened to mean "fascinated" or "captivated."
