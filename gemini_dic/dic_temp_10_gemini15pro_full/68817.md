fascinate:/ˈfæsɪneɪt/ | v. | 使着迷，深深吸引 | To attract and hold the attention and interest of someone completely. | The magician's tricks fascinated the children. 魔术师的戏法使孩子们着迷。|  captivate, enthrall, mesmerize | repel, bore | verb

fascinating:/ˈfæsɪneɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 迷人的，极有吸引力的 | Extremely interesting. | The documentary about ancient Egypt was fascinating.  关于古埃及的纪录片非常引人入胜。| captivating, intriguing, engaging | boring, dull | adjective

fascination:/ˌfæsɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. |  着迷，魅力 |  The power or ability to strongly attract and hold someone's attention. |  He has a fascination with trains. 他对火车很着迷。|  captivation, allure, enchantment |  disinterest, apathy | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin fascinatus, past participle of fascinare "to bewitch," from Latin fascinum "spell, witchcraft."
