breadlines:/ˈbredlaɪnz/| n. |救济粮领取队伍；面包线|A line of people waiting to receive free food, typically during a time of widespread unemployment and poverty.  A symbol of economic hardship. |During the Great Depression, breadlines were a common sight in many American cities. 在大萧条时期，领取救济粮的队伍在美国许多城市都很常见。

The government's economic policies have led to long breadlines in the poorest neighborhoods. 政府的经济政策导致最贫困社区出现了长长的领取救济粮的队伍。|近义词：soup kitchens, food banks|反义词: prosperity, affluence|词性:noun
