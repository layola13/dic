civil-law:/ˈsɪvl lɔː/ (US), /ˈsɪvl lɔː/ (UK)| n.|民法;大陆法系|The system of law predominant in continental Europe and other parts of the world that is based on a comprehensive code of written laws, as opposed to common law.|例句：He specializes in civil-law cases. 他专攻民法案件。|近义词：Roman law, codified law|反义词：common law|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *civile lawe, from Latin *civilis lex, from civis (“citizen”) + lex (“law”).
