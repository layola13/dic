/ˈhɛlm/ | n. | 舵, 头盔, 掌舵 | A covering that protects the head.  A position of leadership. The wheel or tiller by which a ship is steered. | He wore a helmet for protection. 他戴着头盔以保护自己。 | synonyms: headgear, helmet, hard hat | antonyms:  (For the protective sense, there isn't a direct antonym. Perhaps vulnerability?) | noun


/hɛlm/ | v. | 指挥，掌舵 | To steer (a ship or boat). To be in charge or in control of something. |  She helmed the ship through the storm. 她掌舵带领船只度过了风暴。 | synonyms: steer, guide, navigate, command, lead | antonyms: follow, drift | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *helm (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *helmaz (“helmet, covering”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱel- (“to cover, conceal, hide”). Cognate with Old Norse hjálmr, Old Frisian helm, Old High German helm, German Helm, Gothic 𐌷𐌹𐌻𐌼𐍃 (hilms).
