menstruations:/ˌmɛnstruˈeɪʃənz/ | n. |月经，行经|The physiological discharge of blood and mucosal tissue from the inner lining of the uterus through the vagina, occurring approximately monthly from puberty to menopause in nonpregnant females.|She experiences painful menstruations. 她经期疼痛。| 近义词：periods, menses | 反义词: None (This is a biological process without a direct opposite.) | noun

注意事项:
The word "menstruations" is grammatically correct but sounds quite formal. Native speakers almost always use "periods" or "menses" in everyday conversation.  "Menstruation" (singular) is more common when referring to the overall process.
