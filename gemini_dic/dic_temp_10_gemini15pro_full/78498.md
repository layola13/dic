ghastly:/ˈɡɑːstli/ /ˈɡæstli/| adj.|可怕的，恐怖的；惨白的，憔悴的|Causing great horror or fear; extremely unwell.  Pale and ill-looking.|The scene of the accident was ghastly. 事故现场惨不忍睹。|近义词：horrible, terrible, dreadful, frightful, awful, appalling, gruesome, hideous, macabre, eerie, sinister, lurid|反义词：pleasant, delightful, lovely, beautiful|词性:形容词

ghastly:/ˈɡɑːstli/ /ˈɡæstli/| adv.|可怕地，恐怖地；惨白地，憔悴地|In a ghastly manner. |She looked ghastly pale. 她看起来惨白得吓人。|近义词：horribly, terribly, dreadfully, frightfully, awfully, appallingly|反义词：pleasantly, delightfully, lovely|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English gastly, from Old English gæstlic (“spiritual, ghostly”), from gāst (“ghost, spirit”) + -lic (suffix forming adjectives).  The meaning shifted from "ghostly" to "horrible" over time.
