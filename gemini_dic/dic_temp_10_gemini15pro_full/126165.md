muttoned:/ˈmʌtənd/ | adj. | 静音的，无声的；（尤指男人）沉默寡言的 |Silenced; deprived of the power of speech; figuratively, subdued, repressed; specifically (of a man) taciturn, uncommunicative.  | The muttoned bells of the church echoed the somber mood of the town. 教堂静默的钟声回应了小镇忧郁的气氛。 |  The muttoned reply suggested he wasn't comfortable discussing the topic. 沉默的回答表明他不愿意讨论这个话题。 | 近义词: silent, hushed, quiet, taciturn | 反义词: loud, vocal, talkative | 词性: 形容词
词源:  From *mutten* (to make quiet, subdue), from French *mutiner* (to mutiny, revolt), and ultimately from Latin *movere* (to move).  The sense shift occurred through the idea of suppressing a mutiny or rebellion, leading to silencing.
