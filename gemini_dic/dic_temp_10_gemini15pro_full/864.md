abstracts:/ˈæbstrækts/ (US) /ˈæbstrækts/ (UK)| n. |摘要，概要；抽象概念|A short summary of a longer piece of writing, such as an article or book. Also, an idea or concept that is not concrete or physical.|例句：The abstracts of the conference papers are available online. 会议论文的摘要可以在线获取。

She has difficulty grasping abstract concepts. 她难以理解抽象的概念。|近义词：summaries, synopses, epitomes, outlines, précis, digests|反义词：expansions, elaborations|词性:noun


abstracts:/əbˈstrækts/ (US) /əbˈstrækts/ (UK)| v. |提取；摘要；使……抽象化|To remove something from somewhere else; to make a summary of a longer piece of writing; to consider something theoretically or separately from something else.|例句：The chemist abstracted the essential oils from the plant. 化学家从植物中提取了精油。

He abstracted the key findings from the report. 他从报告中提取了主要发现。|近义词：extract, remove, summarize, epitomize|反义词：insert, add, expand|词性:verb


USAGE->

The journal abstracts all submitted articles. = 这份期刊会为所有提交的文章撰写摘要。
She abstracts the main points from each chapter. = 她从每一章中提取要点。


一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

He had abstracted the main arguments before presenting the case. = 在陈述案件之前，他已经提取了主要论点。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
