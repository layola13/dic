murderers:/ˈmɜːrdərərz/ (US) /ˈmɜːdəɾəz/ (UK)| n. | 杀人犯；凶手 |  A person who commits murder. | The murderers were apprehended after a lengthy investigation. 凶手在经过 lengthy 的调查后被逮捕了。| killers, assassins, homicides | victims | noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *murd(e)reres*, from Anglo-Norman *murd(e)rers*, plural of *murd(e)rer*,  from Old French *murdrere*, *murderres*, agent noun from *murdrer* (“to murder”),  from Frankish *murþrōn* (compare Old High German *murthrean* (“to strangle, choke, smother, kill”)), of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Proto-Germanic *murþrą (“murder”),  from Proto-Indo-European *mer- (“to die”).
