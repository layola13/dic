namby-pamby: /ˈnæmbi ˈpæmbi/ | adj. | 幼稚的, 柔弱的, 婆婆妈妈的 | Weak, sentimental, and ineffectual.  Characterized by excessive or affected sweetness. | He wrote nambyspamby poetry that no one took seriously. 他写了一些婆婆妈妈的诗，没有人当真。 | 近义词: sentimental, maudlin, saccharine, effeminate | 反义词: strong, masculine, decisive, unsentimental | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the name "Ambrose Philips" (1674-1749), an English poet ridiculed by Henry Carey for his overly sentimental verses about children.  The name "Ambrose" was first changed to "Namby" and then lengthened to "Namby-Pamby."
