barbacan: /ˈbɑːrbɪkən/ (US), /ˈbɑːbəkən/ (UK) | n. |  城楼，外郭，炮楼 | A fortified outpost or gateway, especially one protecting a bridge or city gate.  |  The barbican offered a strategic advantage to the defenders of the city.  城楼为城市的防御者提供了战略优势。 |  Example: The soldiers stationed at the barbican kept watch for approaching enemies.  驻扎在城楼的士兵密切注视着逼近的敌人。| Synonyms: gatehouse, outwork, bastion | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "inner keep" or "citadel" as the opposite in terms of location within fortifications) | noun
