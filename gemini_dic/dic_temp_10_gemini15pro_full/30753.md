cespitine:/ˈsɛspɪˌtiːn/| n. |腐胺|A colorless, oily, poisonous ptomaine, C₅H₁₄N₂, formed by the putrefaction of animal tissue.|The cespitine was isolated from the decaying fish.|近义词: cadaverine, putrescine|反义词: None|词性:noun
Etymology: From Latin *caespes, caespit-* ("turf, sod") + *-īnē* (suffix forming abstract nouns).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰes- (“to sprout, grow”).
