old-mannish:/ˌoʊldˈmænɪʃ/ | adj. | 老气横秋的，老成的 | Resembling or characteristic of an old man, especially in a negative way, suggesting premature aging or excessive seriousness and conservatism. |  He had an old-mannish way of speaking, even though he was only in his thirties.  他说话老气横秋的，尽管他才三十多岁。 | elderly, prematurely aged, grave | youthful, lively | adjective

Etymology:
From "old" + "man" + "-ish." "Old" comes from Old English "eald." "Man" comes from Old English "mann." "-ish" is a suffix forming adjectives from nouns, meaning "of the nature of," "like," or "somewhat."
