gladiator:/ˈɡlædiˌeɪtər/| n.|角斗士；奋斗者|A Roman fighter, often a slave or captive, who was armed with a sword or other weapon and compelled to fight to the death in a public arena against another person or a wild animal for the entertainment of the spectators.|The gladiators fought bravely in the Colosseum. 角斗士们在斗兽场英勇作战。|Synonyms: fighter, warrior, combatant|Antonyms: pacifist, coward|Noun
