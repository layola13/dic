bias:/ˈbaɪəs/| n. vt.|偏见，偏爱；倾向；斜纹；偏差|A particular tendency, trend, inclination, feeling, or opinion, especially one that is preconceived or unreasoned.|The judge was accused of bias in favor of the defendant.  法官被指控偏袒被告。|synonyms: prejudice, partiality, inclination, predisposition|antonyms: impartiality, fairness, objectivity|noun
bias:/ˈbaɪəs/| vt.|使有偏见；使偏心|To influence in an unfair way; to cause prejudice in (someone).|The media coverage biased public opinion against the candidate. 媒体的报道使公众对这位候选人产生了偏见。|synonyms: influence, sway, predispose, prejudice|antonyms: enlighten, inform objectively|verb
