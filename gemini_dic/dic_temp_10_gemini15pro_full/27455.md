camouflaged:/ˈkæməˌflɑːʒd/ | adj. | 伪装的，被掩蔽的 | disguised or concealed by camouflage |  The soldiers were camouflaged in the jungle. 士兵们在丛林中伪装了起来。|  The sniper was so well camouflaged that we couldn't see him. 狙击手伪装得很好，我们都看不到他。| Synonyms: disguised, concealed, hidden | Antonyms: exposed, visible, revealed | adjective

camouflage:/ˈkæməˌflɑːʒ/ | n. v. | 伪装，掩饰 | the disguising of military personnel, equipment, and installations by painting or covering them to make them blend in with their surroundings;  the act of concealing the identity of something | The soldiers used camouflage to blend in with the forest. 士兵们用伪装与森林融为一体。|  They camouflaged the tank with branches and leaves. 他们用树枝和树叶伪装坦克。| Synonyms: disguise, concealment, masking | Antonyms: exposure, revelation | noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French camouflage, from camoufler "to disguise," literally "to put smoke in the face," from Italian camuffare "to disguise, to deceive," perhaps from dialectal French camus "muzzle," or Italian muffola "muff" + -are (infinitive suffix).
