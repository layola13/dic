off-screen:/ˌɒfˈskriːn/ /ˌɔːfˈskriːn/| adj. adv.| 幕后的，不在屏幕上的；银幕外的|Something that happens off-screen is not seen by the audience because it does not happen on the stage or is not shown on the television or cinema screen.| The director shouted instructions off-screen. 导演在幕后大声喊着指示。|  The actor's off-screen life was as dramatic as his roles. 这位演员的银幕外生活和他扮演的角色一样戏剧化。|近义词：behind the scenes, backstage|反义词：on-screen|词性:形容词，副词


off-screen:/ˌɒfˈskriːn/ /ˌɔːfˈskriːn/| n.| 幕后，银幕外|The world or activities that are not seen on a television or cinema screen, or that do not happen on a stage.|  There was a lot of laughter off-screen during the filming of that scene.  拍摄那一场戏时，幕后有很多笑声。|  The off-screen romance between the two actors eventually became public.  两位演员的银幕外恋情最终被公开了。|近义词：backstage|反义词：on-screen|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "off" + "screen". "Off" indicating separation or detachment and "screen" referring to the display surface of a television, cinema, or stage.
