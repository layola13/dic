discombobulated:/ˌdɪskəmˈbɑːbəleɪtɪd/ /ˌdɪskəmˈbɒbjʊleɪtɪd/| adj.| 困惑的，糊涂的，扰乱的|Confused and disoriented; bewildered.|例句：I felt completely discombobulated after the long flight. 长途飞行后，我感到十分晕头转向。|近义词：confused, bewildered, disoriented, flustered, rattled|反义词：calm, composed, collected, organized|词性:形容词

discombobulate:/ˌdɪskəmˈbɑːbəleɪt/ /ˌdɪskəmˈbɒbjʊleɪt/| vt.| 使困惑，使糊涂，使扰乱|To confuse or disorient someone; to bewilder.|例句：The sudden change of plans completely discombobulated him. 突然的计划改变让他彻底不知所措。|近义词：confuse, bewilder, disorient, fluster, rattle|反义词：calm, compose, collect, organize|词性:动词

词源:  The origin of "discombobulate" is uncertain, but it likely originated in the United States in the mid-19th century. It is thought to be a humorous, fanciful formation, possibly influenced by words like "discomfit" and "bamboozle."
