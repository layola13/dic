abruptly: /əˈbrʌptli/ (US) /əˈbrʌptli/ (UK)
adverb
突然地，唐突地
Suddenly and unexpectedly.
The meeting ended abruptly.  会议突然结束了。

The bus stopped abruptly, throwing the passengers forward. 公共汽车突然停下，乘客都向前倾倒。

近义词: suddenly, unexpectedly, sharply, hastily
反义词: gradually, slowly, smoothly
词性: adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin abruptus (broken off), past participle of abrumpere (to break off), from ab- (off, away from) + rumpere (to break).

USAGE->

The weather changes abruptly in the mountains. = 山区的天气变化无常。
He abruptly ends the conversation. = 他突然结束对话。


一般过去时:
The car swerved abruptly to avoid the deer. = 汽车突然转向以避开鹿。
She abruptly left the party without saying goodbye. = 她没说再见就突然离开了派对。


现在完成时:
The music has abruptly stopped. = 音乐突然停止了。
He has abruptly changed his mind. = 他突然改变了主意。


过去完成时:
The train had abruptly stopped due to a signal failure. = 由于信号故障，火车突然停了下来。
He had abruptly resigned from his position. = 他突然辞去了职务。


将来时:
If the wind picks up, the boat will turn abruptly. = 如果风力增强，船会突然转向。
The meeting will end abruptly at 3 p.m. = 会议将于下午3点突然结束。


虚拟语气:
If he were to abruptly leave, we would be in trouble. = 如果他突然离开，我们会有麻烦。
I wish he wouldn't speak so abruptly. = 我希望他说话不要那么唐突。


被动语态:
