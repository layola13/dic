disowning:/dɪsˈoʊnɪŋ/
|v.|否认，断绝关系|The act of refusing to acknowledge or accept someone as one's own; rejecting any connection or association.|He disowned his son after he was convicted of the crime. 他在儿子被判有罪后与他断绝了关系。|
|近义词：rejecting, renouncing, repudiating|反义词：acknowledging, accepting, embracing|词性:动名词(gerund)


disown:/dɪsˈoʊn/
|vt.|否认，断绝关系|To refuse to acknowledge or accept someone as one's own; to reject any connection or association.|She disowned her family and changed her name. 她否认了自己的家庭并改了名字。|
|近义词：reject, renounce, repudiate|反义词：acknowledge, accept, embrace|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + own.  The prefix "dis-" comes from Latin and means "apart," "asunder," "away," or "not."  "Own" comes from Old English "āgan," meaning "to possess."
