companionhood:/kəmˈpænjənˌhʊd/| n.|友谊；陪伴；同伴关系|The state of being a companion; fellowship. |例句：They enjoyed years of companionhood, sharing adventures and supporting each other. 他们享受了多年的友谊，分享冒险经历，互相支持。

例句：She valued the companionhood of her dog during long walks in the countryside.  在乡间长途散步时，她很珍惜与她的狗的陪伴。|近义词：fellowship, friendship, camaraderie, companionship|反义词:isolation, loneliness|词性:noun

Etymology:
From companion + -hood.  "Companion" comes from Old French "compagnon" meaning "one who shares bread," from Late Latin "companionem," accusative of "companio,"  from Latin "com-" (with) + "panis" (bread).  "-hood" is an Old English suffix denoting "state, condition, quality."
