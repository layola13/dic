n.
Danu, Danu (Hinduism)
In Hinduism, Danu is a primordial goddess, often associated with the waters and considered the mother of the Danavas. Danu is mentioned in the Vedas as a powerful and benevolent force. However, in later Puranic texts, her offspring, the Danavas, are often portrayed as antagonists to the Devas.
Danu is the mother of Vritra, who withheld the waters of the world. Indra, the king of the gods, slew Vritra and released the waters.
Synonym: Primordial Goddess, Mother Goddess
Antonym: N/A
