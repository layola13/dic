organophosphorus compounds:/ɔːrˌɡænəʊˈfɒsfərəs ˈkɒmpaʊndz/ | n. |有机磷化合物|A class of chemical compounds containing carbon and phosphorus, often used as pesticides, nerve agents, and flame retardants.  These compounds are characterized by the presence of a phosphorus atom bonded to an organic group. |Organophosphorus compounds are widely used in agriculture. 有机磷化合物广泛用于农业。|Synonyms:  organophosphates,  phosphorus-containing organic compounds |Antonyms:  None (no direct chemical antonym)|noun
