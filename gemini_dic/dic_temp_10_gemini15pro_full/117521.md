meerschaum: /ˈmɪərʃɔːm/ | n. | 海泡石；海泡石烟斗 | A white mineral, hydrous magnesium silicate, used for making pipes and ornaments. | He smoked his favorite meerschaum pipe. 他抽着他最喜欢的海泡石烟斗。 | synonyms: sepiolite | antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Meerschaum*, literally "sea foam," from *Meer* ("sea") + *Schaum* ("foam").  So called because the light, porous mineral sometimes floats on water.
