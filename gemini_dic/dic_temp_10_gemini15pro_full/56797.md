dorda:/ˈdɔːrdə/ | n. |疼痛,酸痛|(non-standard, informal) Pain, ache, soreness.  A blend of "dull" and "sore." |  My back's giving me a real dorda today. 我的背今天真疼。| 近义词: ache, pain, soreness, throb | 反义词: comfort, relief | 词性: 名词

词源:  "Dorda" is a relatively recent colloquialism, likely originating in the late 20th or early 21st century. It's a blend of "dull" and "sore," combining the sense of a persistent, low-grade pain with the feeling of tenderness or discomfort. It doesn't appear in standard dictionaries and is primarily used in informal spoken English.
