microburin:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌbʊrɪn/| n. |微石锛; 小型石锛|A small, chisel-like flint flake produced as a by-product of making other tools, often used as a diagnostic artifact in archaeology.  It is a characteristic piece of the Mesolithic period.|Archaeologists found numerous microburins at the site, indicating Mesolithic occupation.|Synonym: burin spall (more technical)|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps "core" as the source material from which microburins are detached)
词性: noun
