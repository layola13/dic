non-compete: /ˌnɒn kəmˈpiːt/ (British), /ˌnɑːn kəmˈpiːt/ (American) | adj. n. | 非竞争的；非竞争协议 |  Relating to or being an agreement in which one party agrees not to enter into or start a similar business in competition with another party (often for a specified period of time and within a specific geographic area). | Example: She signed a non-compete clause as part of her severance package. 她作为离职补偿的一部分签署了竞业禁止条款。 | Synonym: non-competition, restrictive covenant | Antonym: competition, open competition | adjective, noun
