ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *neien, *neighen, *neghen, from Old English hnǣgan (“to neigh”), from Proto-Germanic *hnaigijaną (“to neigh”), from Proto-Indo-European *kneigh-. Cognate with West Frisian neige (“to neigh”), Dutch neighen (“to neigh”), German wiehern (“to neigh”).
