hold-with:/hoʊld wɪð/ | phrasal verb | 赞同，同意；容忍，忍受 | To agree with or approve of something; to tolerate or put up with something. | She doesn't hold with smoking. 她不赞成吸烟。

| 近义词: agree with, approve of, condone, tolerate | 反义词: disagree with, disapprove of, condemn, oppose | verb phrase


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *holden with*, from Old English *healdan* (to hold, keep, guard, observe) + *with* (together, against, in company with). The sense of "agree with" developed from the earlier sense of "side with."
