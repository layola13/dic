gypsied:/ˈdʒɪpsid/ |v. |欺骗; 诈骗; (尤指) 骗钱;  |To cheat or swindle someone, especially out of money. Often implies a nomadic or transient lifestyle associated with Romani people, historically stereotyped as deceptive.  |They gypsied him out of his life savings. 他们骗走了他一辈子的积蓄。|Synonyms: cheated, swindled, defrauded, conned |Antonyms:  rewarded, compensated, paid fairly|verb

gypsied:/ˈdʒɪpsid/ |adj. |被欺骗的; 受骗的;  |Having been cheated or swindled.  |He felt gypsied after buying the faulty car. 他买了那辆有问题的车后觉得自己被骗了。|Synonyms: cheated, swindled, defrauded, conned|Antonyms: fortunate, lucky, rewarded|adjective
