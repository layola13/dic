fust:/fʌst/ | vi. | 发霉；腐朽；变得闷热；变得不新鲜 | To become stale, musty, or moldy. To become dull or listless from inactivity. | The bread fusted in the humid air.  面包在潮湿的空气中发霉了。| 近义词: mold, mildew, rot, decay, spoil, wither | 反义词: flourish, thrive | 动词

fust:/fʌst/ | n. | 霉味；腐臭味；不新鲜 | A strong musty smell; the state of being musty. | The old books had a fust about them.  这些旧书有一股霉味。 | 近义词: mustiness, mold, mildew | 反义词: freshness | 名词

fust:/fʌst/ | n. | 烦躁；恼怒 | A state of irritation or annoyance. | He was in a fust because he couldn't find his keys. 他因为找不到钥匙而烦躁不安。| 近义词: irritation, annoyance, frustration | 反义词: calmness, contentment | 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fusten, from Old French fust "wine cask; staff, stick," from Late Latin fustis "club, staff; wood," probably of Gaulish origin.  The sense of "moldy smell" developed from the smell of moldy casks.
