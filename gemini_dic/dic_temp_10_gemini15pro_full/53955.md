discredibility:/ˌdɪsˌkrɛdəˈbɪləti/
n.
不可信，不值得信任
the state of being not credible; the state of being unworthy of belief.
The witness's conflicting accounts led to his discredbility. 证人前后矛盾的叙述导致了他的不可信。
近义词：unreliability, untrustworthiness, implausibility
反义词：credibility, believability, plausibility
词性：名词

Etymology:
From dis- + credibility.  Dis- is a prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of". Credibility comes from the Late Latin credibilitatem, from credibilis "worthy of belief," from credere "to believe."
