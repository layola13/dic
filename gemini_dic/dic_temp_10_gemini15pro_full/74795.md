fremd:/frɛmd/| adj.|陌生的，外国的；冷淡的|Feeling out of place; unfamiliar; alien; estranged.  Derived from Old High German *framjaną "to put forward, promote," later "to further, support,"  then in Middle High German "to be of advantage," also "to be strange," perhaps because what is advantageous is often unfamiliar.  Cognate with Old English fremede "foreign," Old Norse framandi "strange."|She felt fremd in the new city, not knowing anyone. 她在这个新城市感到陌生，一个人也不认识。|The customs seemed fremd and unsettling. 这些习俗看起来陌生而令人不安。|近义词：strange, foreign, alien, unfamiliar, aloof, distant|反义词：familiar, known, native, friendly, close|词性:形容词
