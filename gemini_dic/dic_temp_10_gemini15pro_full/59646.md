echeloned:/ˈeʃəlɒnd/ | adj. | 梯形的，阶梯状的；部署呈梯形的 | Arranged in a series of staggered rows or levels, like the steps of a ladder; deployed in a formation with overlapping flanks, especially of military units. | 例句：The troops were echeloned along the ridge. 部队沿着山脊呈梯队部署。| 例句：The planes flew in echelon formation. 飞机以梯队队形飞行。 | 近义词：staggered, tiered, layered | 反义词：aligned, linear | 词性：形容词

echelon:/ˈeʃəlɒn/ | n. | 梯队；等级；阶层 | A level or rank in an organization, profession, or society. Also, a formation of troops, ships, aircraft, or vehicles in a staggered arrangement. | 例句：The upper echelons of management. 管理层的高层。 | 例句：The attack was led by the first echelon of troops. 攻击由第一梯队的部队发起。 | 近义词：level, rank, tier, grade | 反义词：none | 词性：名词
