aberrances: /əˈbɛrənsɪz/
n.
偏差，异常，反常行为
a departure from what is normal, usual, or expected, typically one that is unwelcome.
His aberrances from the strict party line are tolerated. 他对严格的党的路线的偏离是可以容忍的。
These minor aberrances in his character are entirely forgivable. 他性格上的这些小偏差完全可以原谅。
近义词: deviations, anomalies, irregularities, abnormalities
反义词: norms, standards, regularities
词性: 名词(plural)

aberrance: /əˈbɛrəns/
n.
偏差，异常，反常行为
a departure from what is normal, usual, or expected, typically one that is unwelcome.
A temporary aberrance from the usual routine.  暂时偏离了通常的惯例。
Her outburst in the meeting was an aberrance from her normally calm demeanor. 她在会议上的爆发是她通常平静举止的一次异常。
近义词: deviation, anomaly, irregularity, abnormality
反义词: norm, standard, regularity
词性: 名词(singular)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin aberrantia, from Latin aberrans, present participle of aberrare "to stray," from ab- "away" + errare "to wander."
