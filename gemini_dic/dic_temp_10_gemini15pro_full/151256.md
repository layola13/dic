parenthetically:/ˌpærənˈθetɪkli/ | adv. | 附带地，顺便提一句 | In an incidental or casual way, as a digression or afterthought.  It is often used to qualify a statement, adding information that is related but not essential to the main point.  Derived from "parenthesis," meaning a word, clause, or sentence inserted as an explanation or afterthought into a passage that is grammatically complete without it. |  He added, parenthetically, that he would be late for the meeting.  他顺便提了一句，他会开会迟到。

He mentioned, parenthetically, that he had once met the Queen. 他顺便提到他曾经见过女王。 | 近义词：incidentally, by the way, in passing | 反义词：explicitly, directly | 词性：副词
