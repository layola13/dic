crimsoned:/ˈkrɪmzənd/ | adj. | 深红色的，染成深红色的 | turned a deep red, as if stained with blood |  The setting sun crimsoned the clouds. 落日把云染成了深红色。 | Synonyms: blood-red, scarlet, ruby, vermilion | Antonyms: pale, bleached, ashen | adjective

Etymology: From crimson + -ed.  Crimson itself comes from the Old Spanish "cremesín," ultimately from the Arabic "qirmiz," referring to kermes, a red dye derived from insects.
