highfaluting: /ˌhaɪfəˈluːtɪŋ/ | adj. | 夸夸其谈的，言过其实的 | Pompous or pretentious, especially in language.  Characterized by or given to pretentious, inflated, or bombastic language.  |  He delivered a highfaluting speech that impressed no one.  他发表了一篇夸夸其谈的演讲，却没能让任何人留下深刻印象。 |  The critic dismissed the novel as highfaluting and derivative.  评论家认为这部小说言过其实，缺乏创意。 | 近义词：pompous, pretentious, bombastic, inflated | 反义词：simple, modest, unpretentious, down-to-earth | 词性: adjective

Etymology:
From high + obsolete falute "to salute," from Middle French faluter, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Old French flater "to flatter".  Originally meaning "to greet in a very showy or ostentatious manner," the sense shifted to "using lofty language" in the 16th century.
