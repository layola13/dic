mourning:/ˈmɔːrnɪŋ/ (US) /ˈmɔːnɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |哀悼，悲痛|The period of time following a death during which grief is expressed and the deceased is remembered.  The expression of sorrow for someone's death.|例句：She was in deep mourning after her husband's death. 她丈夫去世后，她陷入了深深的悲痛之中。|例句：The whole nation observed a period of mourning for the late president.  举国上下为已故总统举行了哀悼。|近义词：grief, sorrow, bereavement|反义词：celebration, rejoicing|词性:noun

mourning:/ˈmɔːrnɪŋ/ (US) /ˈmɔːnɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |悲痛的，哀悼的|Expressing or suggesting grief or sorrow.|例句：She wore a mourning dress to the funeral. 她穿着丧服参加了葬礼。|例句：The mourning dove is known for its melancholic coo. 哀鸠以其忧郁的叫声而闻名。|近义词：sorrowful, grieving, bereaved|反义词：joyful, celebratory|词性:adjective
