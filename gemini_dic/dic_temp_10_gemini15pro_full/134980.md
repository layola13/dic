nonnaturalists:/ˌnɒnˈnætʃrəlɪsts/ | n. |非自然主义者| Someone who believes that moral properties are not natural properties, meaning they are not reducible to or identifiable with properties studied by the natural sciences. | Philosophers like G.E. Moore were prominent nonnaturalists, arguing that goodness is a simple, non-analyzable property.  非自然主义哲学家如G.E.摩尔认为善是一种简单的、不可分析的属性。 | Synonyms: moral realists, intuitionists | Antonyms: naturalists, subjectivists | noun

nonnaturalist:/ˌnɒnˈnætʃrəlɪst/ | adj. |非自然主义的| Relating to or characteristic of nonnaturalism. |  His nonnaturalist approach to ethics emphasized the importance of intuition. 他对伦理学的非自然主义方法强调了直觉的重要性。 | Synonyms: intuitionist, moral realist | Antonyms: naturalist, subjectivist | adjective
