overwealth:/ˈoʊvərˌwelθ/| n. |过度财富，过剩财富|An excessive accumulation of wealth, often considered to be more than what is necessary or morally justifiable.  A state of possessing significantly more wealth than is needed for a comfortable and secure life, often raising ethical questions about distribution and inequality.|The family's overwealth allowed them to live a life of extravagant luxury.  这个家庭的巨额财富使他们过着奢侈的生活。|近义词：excess wealth, superfluous wealth, surplus wealth|反义词: poverty, destitution|词性:名词

词源: "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much") + "wealth" (noun meaning "a large amount of money or valuable possessions").  由表示“过度”或“太多”的前缀“over-”和表示“大量金钱或贵重物品”的名词“wealth”组成。
