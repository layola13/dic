dismay:/dɪsˈmeɪ/| n. vt.|沮丧，惊慌；使沮丧，使惊慌|A feeling of unhappiness and disappointment.|The news of the defeat filled them with dismay.  战败的消息使他们充满了沮丧。|近义词：discouragement, consternation, alarm|反义词：encouragement, joy, delight|词性:名词,动词
dismay:/dɪsˈmeɪ/| v. |使沮丧，使惊慌|To cause (someone) to feel discouraged or disappointed.|The team was dismayed by the sudden injury of their star player.  明星球员的突然受伤使球队感到沮丧。|近义词：dishearten, daunt, appall|反义词：encourage, hearten, cheer|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desmayer "lose courage," from des- "away" + Vulgar Latin *exmagare "lose one's power," from Latin ex- "out of" + *magare, of unknown origin.
