Etymology:
graphic (adj.) + -ness (suffix)

graphic: from Late Latin graphicus "of painting or drawing," from Greek graphikos "of or for painting, suited for drawing, descriptive," from graphein "to write, draw, paint," from graphe "writing, drawing, painting."

-ness: Old English -nes(s), -nis(s), a Germanic suffix forming abstract nouns from adjectives, originally in concrete senses of "state, condition, quality."
