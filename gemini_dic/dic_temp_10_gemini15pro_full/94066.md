impassion:/ɪmˈpæʃən/ | v. |使充满激情，激起热情 | To fill with passion or intense feeling. |  The speaker's words impassioned the crowd and moved them to tears. 演讲者的话语激起了群众的热情，使他们感动得流下了眼泪。

The coach's speech impassioned the team before the big game. 教练在比赛前的讲话激发了球队的热情。| 近义词: excite, inflame, arouse, inspire | 反义词: calm, subdue, quell | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *impassionare, from Latin in- + passio (“suffering, passion”).  Essentially meaning "to inflict suffering," which evolved to mean "to arouse strong feelings."

PHRASE->
While "impassion" isn't commonly used in set phrases, it can be combined with adverbs for emphasis: deeply impassion, strongly impassion, greatly impassion. It can also be followed by prepositions like "about" or "with."  It's more common to see the adjective "impassioned" used in phrases like "impassioned plea" or "impassioned speech."
