dislodge:/dɪsˈlɒdʒ/ (British) /dɪsˈlɑːdʒ/ (American)| v. | 移开；取出；逐出 | to remove something or someone, especially by force, from a fixed position | The earthquake dislodged several boulders from the cliff face. 地震使几块巨石从悬崖面上滚落下来。 | remove, displace, extricate | fix, secure, embed | verb

dislodged:/dɪsˈlɒdʒt/ (British) /dɪsˈlɑːdʒt/ (American)| adj. | 被移位的；被取出的 | removed from a fixed position | The dislodged rock tumbled down the mountain. 被移位的岩石滚下山。| displaced, removed | fixed, secured | adjective


Etymology:
From dis- + lodge, from Old French logier "to lodge, house," from Vulgar Latin *laubia "shelter," of Germanic origin. The prefix dis- adds the sense of reversal or removal.
