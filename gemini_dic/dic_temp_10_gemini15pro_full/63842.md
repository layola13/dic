epiglottis:/ˌɛpɪˈɡlɒtɪs/ (US) /ˌɛpɪˈɡlɒtɪs/ (UK)| n. |会厌|A flap of cartilage at the root of the tongue, which depresses over the larynx to prevent food from entering the trachea during swallowing.|The epiglottis prevents food from going down the wrong pipe.|Synonyms:  epiglottic cartilage|Antonyms: None|Noun

epiglottides:/ˌɛpɪˈɡlɒtɪdɪz/ (US) /ˌɛpɪˈɡlɒtɪdɪz/ (UK)| n. |会厌（复数）|The plural form of epiglottis, referring to more than one of these cartilaginous flaps.|While less common, the term epiglottides can be used when discussing multiple epiglottises, such as in a comparative anatomy context.|Synonyms: None (plural of epiglottis)|Antonyms: None|Noun
