melancholize:/ˈmɛlənˌkɒlaɪz/ /ˈmɛlənˌkɑːlaɪz/| v. |使忧郁，使伤感|To make someone feel melancholic; to indulge in melancholy.|She tends to melancholize about the past. 她常常伤感地回忆过去。|Synonyms: sadden, depress, deject|Antonyms: cheer, gladden, elate|verb

melancholy:/ˈmɛlənˌkɒli/ /ˈmɛlənˌkɑːli/| n. adj.|忧郁，伤感；忧郁的，伤感的|A feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause; characterized by or causing or expressing sadness.|A deep melancholy settled over him.  一种深深的忧郁笼罩着他。|Synonyms: sadness, dejection, gloom|Antonyms: happiness, cheerfulness, joy|noun, adjective
