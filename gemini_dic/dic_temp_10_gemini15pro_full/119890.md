militarism:/ˈmɪlɪtərɪzəm/| n.|军国主义|A belief or desire of a government or people that a country should maintain a strong military capability and be prepared to use it aggressively to defend or promote national interests.|The rise of militarism in the region led to an arms race.|近义词：bellicosity, warmongering, jingoism|反义词：pacifism, peacefulness|词性:noun

Etymology: From French *militarisme*, from *militaire* ("military") + *-isme*
