fielders:/ˈfiːldərz/| n. |外野手；守备员|People who catch or stop the ball in sports like cricket, baseball, or softball.  They are positioned in the field, not near the batsman/batter. |The fielders were quick to react to the fly ball. 外野手对飞球反应迅速。|The cricket team had excellent fielders. 这支板球队拥有优秀的外野手。|Synonyms: outfielders, defenders|Antonyms: batters, hitters|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From 'field' (Old English 'feld', meaning open land) + '-er' (suffix denoting a person or thing that performs an action).  So, a fielder is someone who 'fields' (catches or retrieves) the ball in the field.
