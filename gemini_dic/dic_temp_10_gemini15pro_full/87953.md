high-concept:/ˌhaɪ ˈkɒnsept/ (US) /ˌhaɪ ˈkɑːnsept/ (UK)| adj.|高概念的|Relating to or denoting a film or other work of fiction that can be easily summarized in a succinct and appealing way, often relying on a striking central idea rather than complex character development or narrative.|The studio was looking for a high-concept thriller to attract a wide audience. 这家工作室正在寻找一部高概念的惊悚片来吸引广大观众。|近义词： marketable, commercial,  easily understood, simple,  |反义词: complex, intricate, nuanced, low-concept|adjective

high-concept:/ˌhaɪ ˈkɒnsept/ (US) /ˌhaɪ ˈkɑːnsept/ (UK)| n.|高概念|A film or other work of fiction that can be easily summarized in a succinct and appealing way, often relying on a striking central idea rather than complex character development or narrative.|The writer pitched a high-concept about a world where people could swap bodies.  这位作家提出了一个关于人们可以交换身体的世界的高概念。|近义词：premise, idea, hook|反义词: - |noun
