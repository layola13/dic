conjurer:/ˈkʌndʒərər/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒərə(r)/ (UK)| n.|魔术师；变戏法的人；巫师|A person who performs magic tricks; someone who seems to make things appear or disappear as if by magic. Also, someone who evokes a feeling, memory, or image.|The conjurer pulled a rabbit out of his hat. 魔术师从帽子里 pulling出一只兔子。|Synonyms: magician, illusionist, sorcerer, wizard|Antonyms: None|Noun

conjure:/ˈkʌndʒər/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒə(r)/ (UK)| v.|变戏法；使…魔法般出现（或消失）；使想起；恳求|To make something appear or disappear as if by magic; to evoke a feeling, memory, or image; to implore or beseech.|He conjured a dove out of thin air. 他凭空变出一只鸽子。|Synonyms: evoke, summon, materialize, implore|Antonyms: banish, dispel|Verb
