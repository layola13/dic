gadding:/ˈɡædɪŋ/| n.|闲逛，游荡|The act of going from place to place for pleasure, often without any real purpose.|She's always gadding about, never at home. 她总是四处闲逛，从不待在家里。|近义词：roaming, wandering, gallivanting|反义词: staying, remaining|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "gad," which originated in the late 16th century.  "Gad" itself comes from the Middle English "gadden," meaning "to rush about," likely of Scandinavian origin.
