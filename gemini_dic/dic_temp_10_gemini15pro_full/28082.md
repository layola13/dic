capillaries:/ˈkæpɪˌlɛriz/| n.|毛细血管|Very fine blood vessels that connect arteries and veins.  They are the smallest blood vessels in the body and allow for the exchange of oxygen, nutrients, and waste products between the blood and the surrounding tissues.|Oxygen and carbon dioxide are exchanged between the blood and body tissues in the capillaries.|近义词:blood vessels|反义词: (No direct antonym for a physical structure)|词性:noun


capillary:/ˈkæpɪˌlɛri/| adj.|毛细管的，毛细作用的|Relating to or resembling a capillary; involving capillarity.|The capillary action of the sponge soaked up the spilled water. |近义词:minute, fine, hair-like|反义词:large, wide|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *capillaris* "hair-like," from Latin *capillus* "hair".
