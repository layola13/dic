melanism: /ˈmɛlənɪzəm/ | n. | 黑色素沉着；黑变病 | The increased production of melanin in the skin, feathers, or hair of an animal, resulting in a darker coloration. |

Example 1: Melanism in peppered moths is a classic example of natural selection.  胡椒蛾的黑化是自然选择的一个典型例子。

Example 2:  Researchers are studying melanism in different species of birds. 研究人员正在研究不同鸟类的黑化现象。

Synonym: hyperpigmentation, darkening | Antonym: albinism, leucism | noun
