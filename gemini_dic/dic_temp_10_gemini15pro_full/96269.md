indicter:/ɪnˈdaɪtər/| n.|控告者；起诉者；大陪审团|a person, especially a member of a jury, who formally accuses someone of a crime. |The grand jury indicter listened carefully to the evidence.|近义词：accuser, prosecutor|反义词：defendant, accused|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Anglo-French *enditer, from Old French enditer, endicter (“to pronounce, declare, relate, accuse”), from en- (“in, on”) + dit (“said”), past participle of dire (“to say”), from Latin dīcere.
