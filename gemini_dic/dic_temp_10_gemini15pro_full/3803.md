agnomen:/ægˈnəʊmən/ /ægˈnoʊmən/
noun
附加名; 绰号; 别名
An agnomen is an additional name given to a person, often to commemorate an achievement or mark a personal characteristic.  It was common practice in ancient Rome.
Publius Cornelius Scipio Africanus, "Africanus" being his agnomen earned after his victories in Africa.  普布利乌斯·科尔内利乌斯·西庇阿·阿非利加努斯，“阿非利加努斯”是他在非洲取得胜利后获得的绰号。

Marcus Tullius Cicero was known by the agnomen "Tully". 马库斯·图利乌斯·西塞罗以绰号“图利”而闻名。

synonym: nickname, sobriquet, epithet
antonym: (No direct antonym, but could be considered opposite to given name or birth name)
