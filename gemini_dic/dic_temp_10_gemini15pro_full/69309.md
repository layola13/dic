febrility:/fɛˈbrɪlɪti/| n. |狂热，兴奋，激动|A state of nervous excitement or energy, often associated with illness or anxiety.|The febrility of the stock market worried investors. 股市的狂热令投资者担忧。|Synonyms: agitation, excitement, fervor, frenzy|Antonyms: calmness, composure, tranquility|noun

febrile:/ˈfiːbraɪl/| adj. |发烧的，狂热的|Characterized by fever; feverish. Also, marked by intense emotion or activity.|His febrile energy suggested he was not as sick as he claimed.  他狂热的精力表明他并没有他声称的那么病重。  Her febrile imagination produced a vivid and fantastical world. 她狂热的想象力创造了一个生动而奇幻的世界。|Synonyms: feverish, heated, fervent|Antonyms: calm, cool, collected|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *febrilitas, from Latin febris (“fever”).
