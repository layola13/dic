abear:/əˈbɛər/| vt.|忍受，容忍；[律]举止，行为|To endure, tolerate, or behave (usually negatively, in a legal context).|He could not abear the thought of losing her. 他无法忍受失去她的想法。

The defendant did not abear himself well in court.  被告在法庭上的举止不佳。|近义词：tolerate, endure, withstand, bear, abide|反义词:reject, refuse, oppose|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *aberen*, from Old English *āberan* "to endure, tolerate," from *ā-* (prefix indicating intensive action) + *beran* "to bear, carry."  Cognate with German *ertragen* "to endure."

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He abears the pain stoically. = 他默默地忍受着痛苦。
She abears his constant complaining. = 她忍受着他不断的抱怨。

一般过去时:
They abeared the harsh conditions of the journey. = 他们忍受了旅途中的艰苦条件。
He abeared the insults without a word. = 他默默地忍受了侮辱。

现在完成时:
She has abeared his bad behavior for far too long. = 她已经忍受了他的不良行为太久了。
We have abeared many hardships. = 我们已经忍受了许多苦难。

过去完成时:
He had abeared the noise for hours before he finally complained. = 他忍受了几个小时的噪音才终于抱怨了。
She had abeared his criticism for years. = 她忍受了他的批评多年。

将来时:
I will not abear this injustice any longer. = 我不会再忍受这种不公正了。
He will abear the consequences of his actions. = 他将承担他行为的后果。

虚拟语气:
If I could abear the cold, I would go with you. = 如果我能忍受寒冷，我会和你一起去。
I wish I could abear this situation better. = 我希望我能更好地忍受这种情况。

被动语态 (较少使用，通常用主动语态):
Such behavior will not be abeared. = 这样的行为是不能容忍的。
His constant interruptions were not abeared by the judge. = 法官不能容忍他不断的打断。


疑问句:
How can you abear such rudeness? = 你怎么能忍受这种粗鲁的行为？
Could you abear the heat in the desert? = 你能忍受沙漠里的高温吗？

否定句:
I cannot abear his arrogance. = 我无法忍受他的傲慢。
She could not abear to see him suffer. = 她不忍心看他受苦。



PHRASE->
(由于abear较为正式和古旧，词组搭配较少)
not abear = 不能忍受
abear the consequences = 承担后果
abear oneself well/poorly = 表现良好/糟糕
abear the burden = 承受负担
abear the pain/suffering = 忍受痛苦/折磨


注意事项:

abear是一个比较正式和古旧的词，现在很少使用，在现代英语中通常用bear, tolerate, endure, stand, put up with等词代替。
