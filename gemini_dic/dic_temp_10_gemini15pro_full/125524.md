multisiliquous:/ˌmʌltiˈsɪlɪkwəs/
adjective
多荚的；多果的
Having many siliques (seed pods).  A silique is a type of dry fruit that is long and slender, typically containing multiple seeds.  It is characteristic of plants in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).
The botanist identified the plant as a multisiliquous species due to its numerous seed pods.  这位植物学家根据其众多的种子荚，将该植物鉴定为多荚植物。
Near synonyms: multicapsular, multifollicular
Antonyms:  unilocular (single-chambered fruit), bicarpellary (two-carpellate fruit)
word type: adjective

Etymology:
From Latin "multi-" (many) + "siliqua" (pod, husk) + "-ous" (adjective-forming suffix).
