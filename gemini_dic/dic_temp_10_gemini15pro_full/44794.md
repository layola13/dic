crunchingness:/ˈkrʌntʃɪŋnɪs/| n. |嘎吱作响，松脆 |The quality of being crunchy; crispness.  The sound or sensation produced by something crunchy.| The crunchingness of the fresh snow under my boots was satisfying.  靴子下新鲜的雪发出令人满意的嘎吱声。|Synonyms: crispness, crunchiness, brittleness|Antonyms: softness, chewiness, sogginess|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From crunch + -ing + -ness.  "Crunch" originates from imitative roots, suggesting the sound it describes.  The "-ing" suffix transforms it into a present participle, describing the action of crunching, and the "-ness" suffix further transforms it into a noun representing the quality or state of being crunchy.
