domicile:/ˈdɒmɪsaɪl/ (UK) /ˈdɑːməsaɪl/ (US)| n. v.|住所，定居地；(法律)户籍|A person's domicile is the place where they live and consider to be their permanent home.  A person can have only one domicile at a time.|例句：He changed his domicile from London to Paris. 他把户籍从伦敦迁到了巴黎。

|近义词：residence, abode, dwelling, home|反义词: N/A|词性: noun, verb

domicile:/ˈdɒmɪsaɪl/ (UK) /ˈdɑːməsaɪl/ (US)| v. |(使)定居，(使)落户|To domicile someone or something means to establish a domicile for them. |例句：The company is domiciled in Delaware. 这家公司注册在特拉华州。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *domicilium (unattested), from Latin domicilium "dwelling, abode, residence, house," from domus "house" + -ilium (suffix forming nouns of place).
