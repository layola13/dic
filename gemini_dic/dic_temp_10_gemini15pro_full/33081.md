chrismal: /ˈkrɪzməl/ | adj. n.| 圣油的；圣油；圣化油 |Relating to chrism, a consecrated oil used in various Christian ceremonies. Also, the chrism itself, or a white robe worn by newly baptized Christians. | The chrismal Mass is celebrated by the bishop during Holy Week. 主教在圣周期间举行圣油弥撒。 |The priest anointed the baby with chrismal oil. 神父用圣油为婴儿涂油。|  synonyms: consecrated, holy, sacred | antonyms: profane, secular | adjective, noun

chrismal: /ˈkrɪzməl/ | n.| 圣衣 |A white robe worn by newly baptized Christians. | After her baptism, she wore a chrismal robe. 她受洗后，穿着一件圣衣。| synonyms: robe, garment | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "everyday clothes") | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *chrismale, from Latin chrisma (“anointing oil”), from Ancient Greek χρῖσμα (khrîsma, “anointing”), from χρίω (khríō, “I anoint”).
