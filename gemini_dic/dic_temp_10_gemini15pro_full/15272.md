backlasher:/ˈbæklæʃər/| n. |强烈反对者；反冲者|A person who reacts against recent social or political change.|The politician faced a backlash from voters after raising taxes.  这位政治家在提高税收后遭到选民的强烈反对。|近义词: reactionary, opponent, critic |反义词: supporter, advocate |词性: noun
Etymology:  backlash (n.) + -er (suffix forming agent nouns)
backlash (n.): from back (adv.) + lash (v.)  "recoil of a whipped object," 1837, originally of machinery; figurative sense of "adverse reaction" is from 1954.
lash (v.): Old English *læscan (implied in læsc "flash, lightning"), from Proto-Germanic *laskijanan "to flash" (source also of Old Norse leiskr "flash, lightning," Middle Dutch leschen "to flame, flash," Old High German leisc "lightning," German leuchten "to shine, flash"), from PIE root *leuk- "light, brightness."
