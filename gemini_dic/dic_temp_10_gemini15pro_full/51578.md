deutosulphuret:/ˌdjuːtoʊˈsʌlfaɪd/| n.|二硫化物|A compound containing two sulfur atoms bonded together, often to a metal or other element.  Formed from the archaic "deuto" (meaning second or two) and "sulphuret" (an older spelling of sulfide).  This term is now largely archaic and replaced by disulfide.|Iron deutosulphuret (iron disulfide) is a common mineral. 二硫化铁（二硫化铁）是一种常见的矿物。|Synonym: disulfide|Antonym: (No direct antonym, potentially a monosulfide if considering sulfur count)|noun
