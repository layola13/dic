cardiosclerosis: /ˌkɑːrdioʊskləˈroʊsɪs/ /ˌkɑːdiəʊskləˈrəʊsɪs/
|n.|心肌硬化|Hardening of the heart muscle, usually due to fibrosis (scarring).|Cardiosclerosis can lead to heart failure. 心肌硬化会导致心力衰竭。|Synonyms: myocardial fibrosis, cardiac fibrosis|Antonyms:  N/A (A direct antonym doesn't exist.  One might consider a healthy, flexible heart muscle the opposite state.)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
cardio- (from Greek kardia, meaning "heart") + -sclerosis (from Greek sklēros, meaning "hard" and -osis, meaning "condition" or "process")
