fluffing:/ˈflʌfɪŋ/| n. |抖松，拍松，弄蓬松|The act of making something light and airy, especially by shaking or patting it.|She was fluffing the pillows on the bed. 她正在拍打床上的枕头，使其蓬松。|近义词：plumping, shaking, airing|反义词:flattening, smoothing|noun

fluff:/flʌf/| v. |抖松，拍松，弄蓬松; 搞砸，弄糟|To make something light and airy, especially by shaking or patting it; to make a mistake or blunder.|He fluffed his lines in the play. 他在剧中把台词念错了。|近义词：mess up, bungle|反义词:perfect, succeed|verb

词源:  From the noun "fluff," which likely originated as an imitative word, suggesting the sound of something light and airy.
