detortion:/dɪˈtɔːrʃən/| n.| 歪曲，扭曲；曲解|the act of twisting or altering something from its true, natural, or original state. Distortion can apply to physical objects, sounds, images, or even facts and information.|The media's detortion of the facts led to widespread misunderstanding. 媒体对事实的歪曲导致了广泛的误解。|synonyms: misrepresentation, falsification, perversion, twisting, warping|antonyms: accuracy, truth, fidelity, clarity|noun

detortion:/dɪˈstɔːrʃən/| n.| 失真（尤指声音或图像）|In electronics, distortion refers to any unwanted alteration of an audio or video signal during processing, amplification, or transmission.|The guitar amplifier was turned up so loud that the sound was heavily distorted. 吉他音箱开得太大了，声音失真很严重。|synonyms: garbling, fuzziness, static, interference|antonyms: clarity, fidelity, purity|noun
