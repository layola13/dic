foredispose:/ˌpriːdɪˈspoʊz/ /ˌpriːdɪˈspəʊz/| vt. |使预先倾向于；使易受感染|To make someone more likely to suffer from a particular illness or condition, or to make someone more likely to behave in a particular way.|Her genetic background predisposes her to diabetes. 她的基因背景使她容易患糖尿病。|

His upbringing predisposed him to distrust authority. 他的成长环境使他不信任权威。|近义词：influence, incline, make susceptible|反义词：immunize, protect, shield|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *praedisponere, from Latin prae ("before") + disponere ("to arrange").  The prefix pre- emphasizes the sense of "beforehand."
