middlebrowism: /ˈmɪdəlˌbraʊɪzəm/ | n. | 中庸之道；中庸趣味；中等文化水平 | An intellectual and cultural attitude associated with an interest in moderately challenging and accessible forms of art and literature, often seen as a compromise between highbrow and lowbrow tastes. |  Middlebrowism was prevalent in the book clubs of the 1950s.  50年代的读书俱乐部盛行中庸之道。|  The museum's latest exhibition caters to middlebrowism, offering something for everyone but not pushing any boundaries.  博物馆最新的展览迎合了中庸趣味，为每个人都提供了一些东西，但并没有突破任何界限。| 近义词: philistinism, mediocrity | 反义词: highbrowism, lowbrowism | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From middlebrow + -ism. Middlebrow (adjective and noun) is first attested 1925, from middle + brow (in reference to forehead height, supposedly correlating with intelligence).
