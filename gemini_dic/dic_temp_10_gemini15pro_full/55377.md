distemper:/dɪˈstɛmpər/ | n. | 犬瘟热；油彩画；心情不好 | A viral disease of some animals, especially dogs, causing fever, coughing, and weakness. Also, a type of paint made by mixing powdered pigments with water and glue, used for painting walls and ceilings.  Figuratively, it can mean a state of disorder or unrest. | The puppy died of distemper. 这只小狗死于犬瘟热。


The artist used distemper to create a mural.  这位艺术家用油彩画创作了一幅壁画。


Political distemper gripped the nation. 政治动荡席卷了全国。 | 近义词: disease, illness (for the animal disease sense), paint (for the art sense), unrest, turmoil (for the figurative sense) | 反义词: health, order, calm | 词性: noun


distemper:/dɪˈstɛmpər/ | vt. | 使（心情）烦乱，扰乱 | To disturb or agitate the temper or mind of; to upset emotionally. | The news distempered him greatly.  这个消息使他非常不安。 | 近义词: upset, disturb, agitate | 反义词: calm, soothe, pacify | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *destemprer*, from *des-* (prefix indicating reversal) + *temprer* "to temper," from Latin *temperare* "to mix, blend, moderate."  The original meaning referred to an imbalance of bodily humors, thought to cause illness.
