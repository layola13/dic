blusterous: /ˈblʌstərəs/ (US), /ˈblʌstərəs/ (UK)| adj. | 狂暴的；喧闹的；吓唬人的 |  Noisy and aggressive; characterized by strong winds and stormy weather.  | The blusterous wind howled through the trees. 狂风呼啸着穿过树林。 | The politician's blusterous speech didn't impress many voters.  这位政客咆哮式的演讲并没有给很多选民留下深刻印象。 |  noisy, loud, boisterous, clamorous, tumultuous | quiet, calm, peaceful, serene, tranquil | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *blustren*, from Old Norse *blystra* ("to blow, puff"), from Proto-Germanic *blustjaną* ("to blow, blast"), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰlew- ("to blow, swell").  Related to *blast*.
