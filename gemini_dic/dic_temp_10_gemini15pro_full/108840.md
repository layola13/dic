leaven:/ˈlɛvən/| n. v. |酵母，使发酵|A substance, such as yeast, that causes fermentation and expansion of dough or batter.|The baker added leaven to the dough. 面包师在面团里加了酵母。|Synonyms: yeast, ferment, raising agent|Antonyms: inhibitor|noun

leaven:/ˈlɛvən/| v. |使发酵，潜移默化地影响|To cause to ferment or rise by or as if by adding a leavening agent; to permeate or influence subtly or gradually.|Her presence leavened the meeting. 她的出现使会议气氛活跃起来。|Synonyms: permeate, imbue, influence|Antonyms: stagnate, suppress|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *leven*, from Old French *levain*, from Latin *levāmen*, from *levāre* "to raise," from Proto-Indo-European *lewbʰ- "to lift, to be light."
