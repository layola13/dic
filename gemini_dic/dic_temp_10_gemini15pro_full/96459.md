individualism:/ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlɪzəm/| n.|个人主义|A social theory favoring freedom of action for individuals over collective or state control.  The habit or principle of being independent and self-reliant.|Example:  She prized individualism and independence above all else. 她最珍视个人主义和独立。|Synonyms: independence, self-reliance, autonomy, liberty|Antonyms: collectivism, conformity, socialism|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *individualisme*, from *individuel* (“individual”) + *-isme* (“-ism”).  *Individuel* comes from Late Latin *individualis*, from *individuus* (“indivisible”), from *in-* (“not”) + *dividuus* (“divisible”).
