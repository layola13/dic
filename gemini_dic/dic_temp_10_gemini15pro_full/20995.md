blenny:/ˈblɛni/ | n. |  鳚鱼 | A small, often brightly colored fish that lives in shallow coastal waters, typically hiding among rocks or seaweed. |  The blenny darted out from its hiding place to snatch a passing shrimp.  鳚鱼从它的藏身之处冲出来，抓住一只路过的虾。 | synonyms:  rockfish, tidepool fish | antonyms: None | noun

blenny:/ˈblɛni/ | n. | 呆子;笨蛋 | (Informal, somewhat dated) A stupid or foolish person. | He was such a blenny, he locked his keys in the car again. 他真是个呆子，又把钥匙锁在车里了。 | synonyms:  idiot, fool, dolt | antonyms: genius, intellectual | noun
