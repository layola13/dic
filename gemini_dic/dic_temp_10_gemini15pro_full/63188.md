entanglement:/ɪnˈtæŋɡlmənt/ | n. | 纠缠，缠绕；牵连，卷入 | A complicated or compromising relationship or situation.  The state of being entangled.  In physics, a phenomenon in which two or more particles become linked together in a way that they share the same fate, even when separated by vast distances. |  The wire became an entanglement around his ankle. 这根电线缠绕在他的脚踝上。

| 近义词: complication, involvement, tangle, snarl | 反义词: simplicity, freedom, disentanglement, separation | 词性: noun


entangle:/ɪnˈtæŋɡl/ | vt. |  使纠缠，使缠绕；使卷入，使牵连 | Cause to become twisted together with or caught in.  Involve (someone) in difficulties or complicated circumstances. | The fishing line became entangled in the seaweed. 鱼线缠绕在了海藻里。 He became entangled in a messy lawsuit. 他卷入了一场棘手的官司。 | 近义词: enmesh, ensnare, involve, implicate | 反义词: disentangle, extricate, free, liberate | 词性: verb

From Old French *entangler (unattested), from en- "in" + tangler "to tie," of Germanic origin.
