lisper:/ˈlɪspər/ | n. | 咬舌者，口齿不清的人 | Someone who speaks with a lisp, a speech impediment where /s/ and /z/ sounds are pronounced like /θ/ and /ð/ respectively. | Example:  The little girl was a lisper, pronouncing "sister" as "thister".  小女孩说话口齿不清，把“sister”发成“thister”。

Example: He overcame his lisp with the help of a speech therapist. 在语言治疗师的帮助下，他克服了口齿不清的毛病。 | Synonym:  tongue-tied (informal) | Antonym:  eloquent speaker | noun
