mansarded:/ˈmænsɑːrdɪd/| adj.|有老虎窗的，屋顶为曼萨尔德式的|Having a mansard roof; a roof with two slopes on each side, the lower slope being steeper than the upper.|The mansarded roof provided extra living space on the top floor.|近义词:sloping, pitched|反义词:flat|词性:形容词

Etymology: From French *mansardé*, past participle of *mansarder* "to build with a mansard roof," from the name of François Mansart (1598–1666), a French architect credited with popularizing this roof design, + *-ed*.
