freakier:/ˈfriːkiər/| adj.|更古怪的，更奇特的|Comparative form of "freaky," meaning stranger or more unusual than usual.  It implies a greater degree of eccentricity, unconventionality, or even disturbing strangeness.|例句：His new art is even freakier than his last exhibition. 他的新艺术作品比他上次展览的还要古怪。

例句：The weather is getting freakier and freakier with each passing year.  天气一年比一年古怪。|近义词：stranger, weirder, odder, more bizarre, more eccentric|反义词：normaler, more conventional, more ordinary|词性:形容词

词源: From "freaky" + "-er" (comparative suffix). "Freaky" originates from "freak" (n.), which has uncertain origins, possibly related to Old English "freca" (a bold warrior, a greedy person), evolving to mean a person or thing that is markedly unusual or abnormal.
