ethnarch:/ˈeθnɑːrk/ | n. |民族统治者，地方长官|A ruler or governor of a people or province.  Specifically, a ruler of a nation or province within a larger empire, often appointed by the emperor.  The term often implies a degree of autonomy. | Herod was appointed ethnarch of Judea by the Romans. 希律被罗马人任命为犹太的民族统治者。|Synonyms: tetrarch, governor, ruler, prefect, satrap | Antonyms: subject, citizen, commoner | noun

etymology: From Ancient Greek ἔθνάρχης (éthnárkhēs), from ἔθνος (éthnos, “nation”) + ἄρχων (árkhōn, “ruler”).| 词源：来自古希腊语 ἔθνάρχης (éthnárkhēs)，由 ἔθνος (éthnos, “民族”) + ἄρχων (árkhōn, “统治者”) 组成。
