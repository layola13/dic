bigly:/ˈbɪɡli/| adv.|大大地，广泛地，夸大地|A nonstandard adverb meaning "in a big way" or "hugely." While not traditionally considered a proper word, it gained notoriety through its use by Donald Trump during the 2016 US presidential campaign. It's often interpreted as a humorous or informal intensification of "big."|例句：He claimed to have won bigly. 他声称自己大获全胜。|近义词：greatly, enormously, hugely, immensely|反义词: slightly, insignificantly, minimally|词性:副词

词源:
Likely a humorous or informal derivation from the adjective "big."  While its usage can be traced back to the 19th century, with sporadic appearances in literature, its modern usage is primarily attributed to Donald Trump.
