hoodednesses:/ˈhʊdɪdnəsɪz/
n.
戴兜帽的状态；戴兜帽的性质
The state or quality of being hooded; the extent to which something is hooded.
The hoodednesses of the figures added to the mystery of the scene.  这些身影戴着兜帽的状态增添了场景的神秘感。
The different hoodednesses of the cloaks indicated different ranks within the order.  斗篷不同程度的遮盖表明了教团内的不同等级。
近义词: concealment, covering
反义词: exposure, openness
词性: 名词 (Noun)


ETYMOLOGY->
From hooded + -ness.  Hooded comes from hood + -ed. Hood ultimately derives from Old English *hōd (akin to Old High German hōt "hood, hat").  The suffix -ness forms nouns denoting state or quality.
