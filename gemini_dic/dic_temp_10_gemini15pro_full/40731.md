convexness:/kənˈvɛksnɪs/ | n. |凸性，凸度|The state or quality of being convex; the degree to which something is convex. |The convexness of the lens helps to focus the light. 镜片的凸度有助于聚焦光线。|近义词：bulge, curvature, prominence|反义词：concavity, hollowness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From convex + -ness.  Convex comes from Late Latin convexus "vaulted, arched," from Latin convexus "curved, arched," literally "carried together," past participle of convehere "to carry or bring together," from com- "together" (see com-) + vehere "to carry" (from PIE root *wegh- "to go, move, transport").
