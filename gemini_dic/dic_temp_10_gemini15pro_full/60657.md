electiveness:/ɪˈlɛktɪvnɪs/ |n.|可选性，自主性|The quality of being elective; the fact of being chosen by election.  The state of being optional or non-required. |Students appreciate the electiveness of the courses offered in their final year. 学生们很感激他们在最后一年可以选择自己想学的课程。|Many colleges are increasing the electiveness of their curricula, allowing students more freedom to pursue their interests. 许多大学正在提高课程的可选性，让学生有更多自由去追求自己的兴趣。|近义词：optionality, choice, voluntariness|反义词：compulsion, obligation, requirement|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From elective + -ness.  "Elective" comes from Late Latin *electīvus, from Latin electus (past participle of eligere "to choose out, elect"), from ex- "out" + legere "to choose, gather, read".  "-ness" is a native English suffix forming nouns expressing state or quality.
