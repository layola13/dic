conestoga:/ˌkɒnɪˈstoʊɡə/ /ˌkɑːnɪˈstoʊɡə/| n.|康内斯托加马车；大篷车|A broad-wheeled covered wagon, used for hauling freight across the prairies of North America, especially in the 19th century.|The Conestoga wagon played a vital role in westward expansion. 康内斯托加马车在西进运动中发挥了至关重要的作用。

The museum displayed a meticulously restored Conestoga wagon. 博物馆展出了一辆精心修复的康内斯托加马车。|近义词：covered wagon, prairie schooner|反义词:|名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Conestoga, the name of a creek and a town in Lancaster County, Pennsylvania, USA, where these wagons were first built.  The name Conestoga is thought to derive from a Susquehannock Indian word meaning "place of the immersed poles."
