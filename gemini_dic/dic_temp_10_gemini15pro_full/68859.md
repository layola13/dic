fassaite:/ˈfæsəˌaɪt/
n.
fassait; 铁钙辉石;  透辉石
A dark green to black pyroxene mineral containing calcium, iron, magnesium, and aluminum.  It is a variety of augite.
Fassaite is a common mineral in metamorphic rocks.  铁钙辉石是一种常见的变质岩矿物。
Fassaite can be found in skarns. 铁钙辉石可以在矽卡岩中找到。
Synonyms: augite, pyroxene
Antonyms: None (Minerals don't typically have antonyms)
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Val di Fassa (Fassa Valley) in the Dolomites of northern Italy, where it was first discovered, + -ite (suffix forming names of minerals).
