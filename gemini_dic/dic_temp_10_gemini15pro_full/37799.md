compelling:/kəmˈpɛlɪŋ/ | adj. | 引人注目的；令人信服的；非常强烈的；不可抗拒的 |Evoking interest, attention, or admiration in a powerfully irresistible way.  Creating a strong sense of necessity or obligation. | The prosecutor presented a compelling case against the defendant. 检察官提出了针对被告的令人信服的案件。 |  persuasive, convincing, cogent, compelling, irresistible, captivating  | uninteresting, weak, unconvincing, unpersuasive | adjective

compel:/kəmˈpɛl/ | vt. |强迫；迫使；使必须 |To force or oblige (someone) to do something. To drive or urge forcefully or irresistibly. |  The evidence compels me to believe he is guilty. 证据迫使我相信他有罪。| force, oblige, require, coerce, constrain | dissuade, discourage | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin compellere "to drive together, force, compel," from com- "together" (see com-) + pellere "to drive" (see pulse (n.)).
