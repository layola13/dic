exundation:/ˌɛksʌnˈdeɪʃən/ | n. | 溢出；涌出；泛滥 | An overflowing abundance; copious discharge.  The act of overflowing or the state of being overflowed. | The exundation of lava from the volcano destroyed the nearby village. 火山熔岩的喷涌摧毁了附近的村庄。 |  overflow, outpouring, effusion, discharge, flood |  lack, scarcity, dearth | noun

exude:/ɪɡˈz(j)uːd/ | v. | 渗出；流出；散发 |  To discharge or emit (a liquid or gas, for example) gradually. To display (an emotion or quality) strongly and openly. | The pine tree exuded sticky resin. 松树渗出粘稠的树脂.  She exuded confidence. 她浑身散发着自信。 |  emit, radiate, ooze, leak, secrete | absorb, conceal, suppress | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin exundationem, accusative of exundatio (“overflowing”), from exundare (“to overflow, surge”), from ex (“out of, from”) + undare (“to surge, wave, flow”), from unda (“wave”).
