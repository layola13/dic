cadi:/ˈkɑːdiː/| n.|卡迪，法官，地方行政官|A judge or magistrate in some Muslim countries.|The cadi heard the case and made his judgment. 卡迪听取了案件并作出了判决。|Synonyms: judge, magistrate, jurist|Antonyms: defendant, accused|noun

Etymology->
From Arabic *qāḍī* meaning "judge". Ultimately from the verb *qada* meaning "to judge" or "to decide".
