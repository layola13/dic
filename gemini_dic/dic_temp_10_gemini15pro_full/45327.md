cummerbund: /ˈkʌmərˌbʌnd/ (US), /ˈkʌməbʌnd/ (UK)| n. | 腰带，腰封 | A broad sash worn around the waist, typically over a man's formal evening attire, usually made of silk or satin and often pleated or folded. | He wore a black cummerbund with his tuxedo. 他穿着一件燕尾服，系着黑色的腰封。| Synonym: sash, waistband, girdle | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps "belt" in casual contexts) | Noun

cummerbund: /ˈkʌmərˌbʌnd/ (US), /ˈkʌməbʌnd/ (UK)| n. | 腰带，腰封 |  Origin: From Hindi kamarband, from kamar "waist, loins" + band "band." |  The cummerbund originated in Persia and India and was adopted by British military officers in colonial India.
