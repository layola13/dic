oxbird:/ˈɒksbɜːrd/ | n. | 牛椋鸟 | A type of bird (of the family Buphagidae) that feeds on parasites and dead skin found on large mammals, especially cattle.  |  The oxbird perched on the rhino's back, picking at its skin.  牛椋鸟栖息在犀牛的背上，啄食它的皮肤。 |  Synonym: tickbird | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, could consider a predator bird like an eagle) | Noun
