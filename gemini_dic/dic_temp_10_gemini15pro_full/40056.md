consumptives:/kənˈsʌmptɪvz/
| n. |肺痨患者；痨病患者|A person suffering from pulmonary tuberculosis (consumption).  This term is now largely outdated and considered insensitive.|In the 19th century, consumptives were often sent to sanatoriums for treatment. 19世纪，肺结核患者经常被送到疗养院治疗。|Synonyms: TB patients, sufferers of consumption (outdated) |Antonyms: healthy individuals|Noun

consumptive:/kənˈsʌmptɪv/
| adj. |患肺痨的；消费的|Relating to or affected with pulmonary tuberculosis (consumption). Also, tending to consume; destructive.|Her consumptive cough echoed through the room. 她的肺痨咳嗽在房间里回荡。  The fire was consumptive, quickly devouring the entire forest. 大火迅速吞噬了整片森林，具有毁灭性。|Synonyms: tubercular, phthisical (medical, outdated); wasteful, devouring |Antonyms: healthy; productive, conserving|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *consumptivus, from Latin consumere (“to consume”), from com- (“together”) + sumere (“to take”).
