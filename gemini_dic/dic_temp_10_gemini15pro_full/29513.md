catastrophic:/ˌkætəˈstrɒfɪk/ (US) /ˌkætəˈstrɑːfɪk/ (UK)| adj.|灾难性的，毁灭性的|Involving or causing sudden great damage or suffering.|
The earthquake had catastrophic consequences for the city.  地震对这座城市造成了灾难性的后果。

The oil spill was an environmental catastrophe.  石油泄漏是一场环境灾难。

近义词:disastrous, calamitous, devastating, ruinous|
反义词:beneficial, advantageous, fortunate|
词性:adjective|
词源:From French *catastrophique*, from Late Latin catastrophē, from Greek katastrophḗ "overturn, sudden turn, a down-turning," from katastréphein "to overturn," from kata- "down" + stréphein "to turn."|
