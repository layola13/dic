angstrom:/ˈæŋstrəm/ |n.|埃（长度单位）|A unit of length equal to one ten-billionth of a meter (10⁻¹⁰ meter). Used chiefly in expressing wavelengths of light and interatomic distances.|The wavelength of visible light is typically measured in angstroms.|Synonym: Å (symbol), one ten-billionth of a meter|Antonym: N/A|noun

angstroms:/ˈæŋstrəmz/ |n.|埃（长度单位，复数）|Plural form of angstrom.  A unit of length equal to one ten-billionth of a meter (10⁻¹⁰ meter). Used chiefly in expressing wavelengths of light and interatomic distances.|The diameter of a hydrogen atom is about 1.06 angstroms.|Synonym: Ås (symbol), one ten-billionths of a meter|Antonym: N/A|noun
