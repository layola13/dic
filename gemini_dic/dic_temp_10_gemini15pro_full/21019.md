bletchley:/ˈblɛtʃli/| n.|布莱切利|Bletchley is a town in Milton Keynes, Buckinghamshire, England. It is famous for its role in World War II, housing the Government Code and Cypher School (GC&CS), which regularly penetrated the secret communications of the Axis Powers – most importantly the German Enigma and Lorenz ciphers.|Bletchley Park played a crucial role in World War II. 布莱切利园在二战中发挥了至关重要的作用。|近义词: (As a place name, it has no direct synonyms.)|反义词: (As a place name, it has no direct synonyms.)|词性:noun
