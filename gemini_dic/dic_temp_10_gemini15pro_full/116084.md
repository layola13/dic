martyr:/ˈmɑːrtər/ (US) /ˈmɑːtə(r)/ (UK)| n. |烈士；殉道者；(因病等)长期受苦者|A person who is killed because of their religious or political beliefs, or someone who suffers very much for a long time.|Joan of Arc was a martyr for her faith.  圣女贞德是为信仰而死的烈士。|synonyms: victim, sufferer, saint |antonyms: oppressor, persecutor, tormentor|noun


martyr:/ˈmɑːrtər/ (US) /ˈmɑːtə(r)/ (UK)| v. |使殉难；折磨；杀害(尤指因宗教或政治信仰)|To kill someone because of their religious or political beliefs.|He was martyred for his beliefs. 他因其信仰而殉难。|synonyms:  execute, sacrifice, persecute |antonyms: protect, save, liberate|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin martyr, from Greek martur, meaning "witness."  Originally, a martyr was a witness to an event, particularly a religious one.  The meaning evolved to refer to someone who suffers or dies for their beliefs.
