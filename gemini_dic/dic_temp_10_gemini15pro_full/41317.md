coreferentialities:/ˌkɔːrɛfərɛnʃiˈælɪtiz/ | n. | 共指性 | The state or condition of being coreferential; a set of coreferential expressions.  It refers to the linguistic phenomenon where two or more expressions in a text refer to the same entity or concept in the real world. | The coreferentialities between "the author" and "Jane Austen" are clear throughout the biography.  整部传记中，“作者”和“简·奥斯汀”之间的共指关系很明显。 |  The coreferentialities in the legal document ensured there was no ambiguity about the parties involved. 这份法律文件中的共指关系确保了所涉及的各方没有歧义。 | synonyms: coreferences, co-references | antonyms: N/A | noun
