elongate:/ɪˈlɒŋɡeɪt/ (UK), /ɪˈlɔːŋɡeɪt/ (US)| v. adj.|加长，延长；细长的|To make something longer, or to become longer.  To extend the length of something.|The artist elongated the legs of the figure in the painting to create a sense of elegance.  艺术家拉长了画中人物的腿，以营造优雅的感觉。|lengthen, extend, stretch, protract|shorten, contract, curtail, abbreviate|verb

elongate:/ɪˈlɒŋɡeɪt/ (UK), /ɪˈlɔːŋɡeɪt/ (US)| adj.|细长的，瘦长的|Long and thin.|The giraffe's elongated neck allows it to reach high into the trees. 长颈鹿细长的脖子使它能够够到树的高处。|lengthy, extended, stretched|short, stubby, squat|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin elongatus, past participle of elongare "to lengthen," from Latin e- "out" + longus "long."
