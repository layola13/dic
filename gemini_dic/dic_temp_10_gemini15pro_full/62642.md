enfranchisement:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
n.
赋予公民权；解放；特许经营权
The act of giving a group of people the right to vote, or a particular right.  The fact of being given the right to vote, or a particular right.
The 19th Amendment to the Constitution gave women the enfranchisement to vote.  美国宪法第十九修正案赋予了妇女投票权。
Women's enfranchisement was a long and hard-fought battle.  妇女的选举权是一场漫长而艰苦的斗争。
Synonyms: suffrage, empowerment, liberation, emancipation
Antonyms: disenfranchisement, subjugation, oppression
noun
