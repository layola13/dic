oscillation:/ˌɒsɪˈleɪʃn/ (US) /ˌɑːsɪˈleɪʃn/ (UK)| n.| 振荡，波动，摆动|The regular variation in magnitude or position about a central point, such as the oscillation of a pendulum or the oscillation of prices.|The pendulum's oscillation was hypnotic. 钟摆的摆动令人着迷。|fluctuation, vibration, swing, wavering|stability, constancy|noun

oscillate:/ˈɒsɪleɪt/ (US) /ˈɒsɪleɪt/ (UK)| v.| 振荡，波动，摆动，犹豫|To swing back and forth with a regular rhythm, or to vary between two states or extremes.|Her feelings oscillated between hope and despair. 她的心情在希望和绝望之间摇摆不定。|fluctuate, vibrate, swing, waver|stabilize, remain constant|verb
