low-key:/ˌloʊˈkiː/| adj. adv.|低调的，不张扬的；秘密的，不公开的|Not very intense, exciting, or obvious; restrained.|例句：They had a low-key wedding ceremony with only close family and friends. 他们举行了一个低调的婚礼，只有亲朋好友参加。

例句：She wanted to keep the party low-key, so she only invited a few people. 她想让派对保持低调，所以只邀请了几个人。|近义词：understated, subtle, quiet, discreet, private|反义词：ostentatious, flamboyant, public, high-profile|词性:形容词，副词

Etymology:  "Low-key" originated in the early 20th century as a musical term referring to a subdued or quiet tone or passage. This meaning was then extended figuratively to describe other things that were similarly restrained or understated.
