inoculate:/ɪˈnɒkjʊleɪt/ /ɪˈnɑːkjuleɪt/ | vt. |接种，嫁接;灌输(思想等)| To introduce something, such as a serum, vaccine, or an antigen, into the body of a person or an animal, especially in order to produce or boost immunity to a specific disease. To introduce an idea, attitude, or habit into someone's mind. | Doctors routinely inoculate children against measles. 医生通常给儿童接种麻疹疫苗。

The teacher tried to inoculate his students with a love of learning. 老师试图向学生灌输热爱学习的思想。 | 近义词：vaccinate, immunize, inject, implant, introduce, instill | 反义词: infect, contaminate | 词性:动词

inoculation:/ɪˌnɒkjʊˈleɪʃn/ /ɪˌnɑːkjʊˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 接种; 嫁接; 预防注射|The act or process of inoculating or of being inoculated; vaccination. The introduction of a microorganism, serum, or vaccine into a living organism to prevent or treat a disease. |  His inoculation against the flu protected him during the epidemic. 他接种了流感疫苗，在疫情期间得到了保护。

The inoculation of new ideas into the company culture was met with resistance. 将新的想法引入公司文化遇到了阻力。| 近义词：vaccination, immunization, injection | 反义词: infection, contamination | 词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *inoculātus, past participle of *inoculāre (“to engraft, implant”), from in (“in, on”) + oculus (“eye, bud”). Originally referred to grafting the "eye" or bud of one plant onto another.
