foretaster:/ˈfɔːrteɪstər/| n.|先驱者；预言者；预尝者|Someone or something that precedes and indicates the approach of something else; a harbinger.  A person employed to taste food or drink, especially wine, before it is served to others to ensure that it is palatable and safe.|The canary in the coal mine served as a foretaster of the dangers to come.  金丝雀在煤矿中充当着危险来临的预警者。|The king employed a foretaster to check for poison in his meals. 国王雇佣了一名试毒者来检查他的膳食中是否有毒。|Synonyms: precursor, harbinger, herald, forerunner, pioneer, vanguard|Antonyms: follower, successor|noun
