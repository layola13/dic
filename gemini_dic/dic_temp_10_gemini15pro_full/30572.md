ceratobranchial: /ˌsɛrətəˈbræŋkiəl/ | adj. n.| 角鳃的；角鳃骨 |Relating to or designating the ceratobranchials, which are a series of paired bones or cartilages forming the posterior part of the branchial arches in fish and some amphibians. They support the gills. Also used as a noun to refer to a ceratobranchial bone or cartilage. | The ceratobranchial bones are important for gill support in fish.  鱼的角鳃骨对鳃的支撑很重要。| The fish's ceratobranchials were clearly visible during the dissection. 解剖过程中，鱼的角鳃骨清晰可见。| Synonyms: branchial (as an adjective, relating to gills),  gill arch (for the structure). | Antonyms: None specific.
词性: adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *keras, keratos* "horn" + *branchia* "gills".
