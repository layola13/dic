cauliculus: /kɔːˈlɪkjʊləs/ | n. |  胚茎；子叶柄；上丘 | The part of a plant embryo or seedling below the cotyledons and above the radicle, connecting the radicle to the plumule.  It is the stem of the embryo, destined to develop into the main stem of the plant. | The cauliculus of the bean sprout emerged first.  豆芽的胚茎首先出现。 | synonyms: hypocotyl | antonyms: plumule (sometimes considered the opposite as it develops into the shoot) | noun

cauliculi: /kɔːˈlɪkjʊlaɪ/ | n. | 胚茎（复数）; 上丘（复数）| Plural form of cauliculus, referring to multiple plant embryos' stems or to the structures in the brain. | The cauliculi of the seedlings were all healthy.  这些幼苗的胚茎都很健康。 | synonyms: hypocotyls | antonyms: plumules | noun
