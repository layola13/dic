expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/| adj. n.|权宜之计的，方便的；权宜之计|Suitable or practical for a particular purpose, but not necessarily morally right.  A means to an end.|例句：It might be expedient to ignore the rules sometimes. 有时候，忽略规则可能是权宜之计。|近义词：convenient, practical, advantageous, useful, politic|反义词:inconvenient, impractical, inexpedient, unwise|adjective
expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/| n.|权宜之计|A means to an end; a way of achieving something quickly and easily, even if it is not morally right.|例句：Using a back door was an expedient to get into the locked building. 使用后门是进入锁着的建筑物的一种权宜之计。|近义词：measure, resort, strategy, tactic|反义词:|noun
