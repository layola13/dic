elephantiasis:/ˌelɪfənˈtaɪəsɪs/| n.|象皮病|A chronic disease characterized by the thickening and enlargement of skin and tissues, typically in the legs and genitals, caused by obstruction of the lymphatic vessels, often by filarial worms.|Elephantiasis can be a debilitating condition. 象皮病可能是一种使人衰弱的疾病。|近义词:lymphedema, filariasis|反义词: (No direct antonym for a disease)|词性:noun
