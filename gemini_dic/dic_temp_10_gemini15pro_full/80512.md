good-ol-boy:/ˌɡʊd oʊlˈbɔɪ/ |n.| 老好人；哥们儿；(尤指美国南部)仗势欺人的地方政客 |A man, typically from the southern U.S., who is part of a close-knit, often exclusive, group that traditionally holds political power. Often implies cronyism and favoritism. |He's a good-ol-boy who always gets his way. 他是个老好人，总是能随心所欲。| 近义词: crony, insider, buddy | 反义词: outsider | 词性: noun

good-ol-boy:/ˌɡʊd oʊlˈbɔɪ/ |adj.| 老好人的；哥们儿的；(尤指美国南部)仗势欺人的地方政客的 |Characteristic of or relating to good-ol-boys. |The good-ol-boy network controlled the local government.  老好人关系网控制了当地政府。| 近义词: cronyistic, insider | 反义词: outsider | 词性: adjective
