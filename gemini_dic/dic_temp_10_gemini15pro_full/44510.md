crossly:/ˈkrɒsli/ (UK) /ˈkrɔːsli/ (US)| adv.|生气地，暴躁地|In a bad-tempered or irritable way; angrily.|She answered him crossly. 她生气地回答他.|近义词：angrily, irritably, grumpily, peevishly|反义词：pleasantly, cheerfully, amiably, politely|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From cross (adjective) + -ly (adverbial suffix). The adjective "cross" meaning "ill-tempered" comes from Old Norse "kross," meaning "affliction, trial, cross."  The sense evolved from the idea of bearing a burden or suffering, which can lead to irritability.
