assimilators:/əˈsɪməˌleɪtərz/
|n.|同化者，吸收者|People who adopt and incorporate new ideas, information, or cultures into their own understanding or way of life. |例句：The assimilators quickly adopted the customs of their new country.  同化者很快就接受了他们新国家的习俗。

| | |例句：Good language learners are often skilled assimilators of new vocabulary.  优秀的语言学习者通常能熟练地吸收新词汇。
|近义词：adopters, integrators, absorbers|反义词：resisters, rejecters|词性：名词

assimilate:/əˈsɪməˌleɪt/
|v.|同化，吸收|To absorb and integrate new ideas, information, or cultures into one's own understanding or way of life.|例句：Children assimilate new information quickly. 孩子们吸收新信息很快。

| | |例句：It takes time to assimilate into a new culture. 融入一种新文化需要时间。
|近义词：absorb, integrate, incorporate, adapt|反义词：reject, resist|词性：动词

assimilation:/əˌsɪməˈleɪʃən/
|n.|同化，吸收|The process of absorbing and integrating new ideas, information, or cultures into one's own understanding or way of life.|例句：Cultural assimilation can be a challenging process. 文化同化可能是一个充满挑战的过程。

| | |例句：The assimilation of immigrants into society is an ongoing debate. 移民融入社会是一个持续的辩论话题。
|近义词：integration, absorption, incorporation, adaptation|反义词：rejection, resistance|词性：名词
