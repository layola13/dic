mobbing: /ˈmɒbɪŋ/ (British) /ˈmɑːbɪŋ/ (American)
|n.|欺凌，围攻，聚众滋扰|The act of a group of people harassing, attacking, or persecuting an individual.  This can occur in various settings, such as the workplace, school, or online.|He was the victim of relentless mobbing at work. 他在工作中遭受了无情的欺凌。|
|近义词: bullying, harassment, persecution, intimidation|反义词: supporting, protecting, defending|词性: 名词|
mobbing: /ˈmɒbɪŋ/ (British) /ˈmɑːbɪŋ/ (American)
|v.|欺凌，围攻，聚众滋扰|To harass, attack, or persecute (someone) as a group.|His colleagues mobbed him with constant criticism and gossip.  他的同事不断地批评和八卦他，对他进行了围攻。|
|近义词: bully, harass, persecute, intimidate|反义词: support, protect, defend|词性: 动词|



词源: From the verb "mob," meaning to crowd around or attack in a disorderly mass.  Ultimately derived from Latin "mobile vulgus," meaning "fickle crowd."
