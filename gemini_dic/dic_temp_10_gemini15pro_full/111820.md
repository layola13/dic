loger:/ˈloʊɡər/ (US), /ˈlɒɡə(r)/ (UK)| n.|居住者，寄宿者;伐木工人|A person who lives in a particular place or who stays somewhere for a period of time; A person whose job is cutting down trees.|例句：The lodger paid his rent weekly.  寄宿者每周支付房租。


例句：The lodger in the attic room was very quiet.  住在阁楼里的房客很安静。


近义词：resident, occupant, tenant, boarder, lumberjack, logger (for the tree-cutting sense)|反义词:landlord, homeowner (in the sense of residency) |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *loggere, from loggen (“to lodge”), from Old North French logier, from Frankish *laugi (“lodging”), from Proto-Germanic *lauhō (“cave, den, shelter”). Cognate with Middle Dutch logeren (“to lodge”), German dialectal lugen (“to lie”). More at lodge.
