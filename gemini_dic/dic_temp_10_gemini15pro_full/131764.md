nonconjugality:/ˌnɒnkɒndʒʊˈɡælɪti/ | n. | 非共轭性 | The state or quality of not being conjugate.  This term is often used in mathematics, particularly in group theory and linear algebra, to describe matrices or groups that are not related by a similarity transformation. It can also be used more broadly to describe things that are not paired or coupled in a specific way. | The nonconjugality of the two matrices made the calculation more complex. 这两个矩阵的非共轭性使得计算更加复杂。 |  The professor explained the concept of nonconjugality in group theory. 教授解释了群论中非共轭性的概念。 | Near synonyms: dissimilarity, difference, distinctness | Antonyms: conjugacy, similarity, relatedness | Noun
