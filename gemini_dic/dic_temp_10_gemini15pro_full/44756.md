crumblingnesses:/ˈkrʌmblɪŋnəsɪz/ |n. |崩塌；破碎；崩溃|The state of something crumbling; the tendency to crumble.  The crumblingnesses of the ancient ruins were evident. 这些古代遗址的破败显而易见。|例句：The crumblingnesses of the old building were a safety concern. 这栋老建筑的破败状况令人担忧安全问题。

例句：The crumblingnesses of the empire were apparent to all.  这个帝国的崩溃对所有人来说都是显而易见的。|近义词：fragility, decay, deterioration, dilapidation|反义词：stability, soundness, strength|词性: 名词

crumbling:/ˈkrʌmblɪŋ/ |adj. |崩溃的；摇摇欲坠的；衰落的|Falling apart; disintegrating. The crumbling walls of the castle. 城堡摇摇欲坠的墙壁。|例句：The crumbling economy is a major concern for the government. 崩溃的经济是政府的主要担忧。

例句：The crumbling infrastructure needs immediate attention. 破败的基础设施需要立即关注。|近义词：decaying, deteriorating, disintegrating, dilapidated|反义词：stable, sound, strong, robust|词性: 形容词

crumble:/ˈkrʌmbəl/ |v. |崩溃；破碎；弄碎|Break or fall apart into small fragments. The cake crumbled as I cut it. 我切蛋糕的时候，蛋糕碎了。|例句：The empire began to crumble under the weight of its own corruption.  这个帝国在自身腐败的重压下开始崩溃。

例句：She crumbled the bread into small pieces for the birds. 她把面包捏碎成小块喂鸟。|近义词：disintegrate, break apart, fall apart, fragment|反义词：solidify, strengthen, hold together|词性: 动词
