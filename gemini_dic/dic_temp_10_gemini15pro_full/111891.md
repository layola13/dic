logos:/ˈloʊɡɒs/ (US), /ˈlɒɡɒs/ (UK)| n.|逻各斯; 理念; 论证; 道|A word (from Greek) meaning "word," "speech," "account," "reason."  In rhetoric, logos refers to the internal consistency of the message—the clarity of the claim, the logic of its reasons, and the effectiveness of its supporting evidence. The impact of logos on an audience is sometimes called the argument's logical appeal.  It can also refer to a symbol or other design adopted by an organization to identify its products, uniform, vehicles, etc.|The company updated its logos to appeal to a younger audience. 公司更新了标识以吸引更年轻的受众。|synonyms: reason, logic, rationale, argument, principle, doctrine, creed|antonyms: illogic, irrationality, unreason, absurdity|noun
