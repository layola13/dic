lay-off:/ˈleɪˌɔːf/ (US), /ˈleɪˌɒf/ (UK)| n.|裁员，解雇；停工期；失业期|A lay-off is a temporary or permanent discharge of a worker or workers.|The company announced a lay-off of 100 employees due to declining sales. 由于销售额下降，该公司宣布裁员100人。|Synonyms: redundancy, discharge, dismissal|Antonyms: hiring, recruitment|noun

lay-off:/ˈleɪˌɔːf/ (US), /ˈleɪˌɒf/ (UK)| n.|临时解雇；停工期|A lay-off is a period of time when someone is not working, usually because their employer has no work for them.|He's on lay-off from the factory. 他被工厂暂时解雇了。|Synonyms: furlough, downtime, unemployment|Antonyms: employment, work|noun
