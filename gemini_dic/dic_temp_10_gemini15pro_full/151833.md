passes-as:/ˈpæsɪz æz/| v.|被接受为；冒充；|Something passes as something else if it is accepted or regarded as that thing, although it may not really be that thing.|He passes as a doctor, but he's really a fraud. 他冒充医生，但他实际上是个骗子。|近义词：goes for, qualifies as, serves as, is accepted as|反义词: is rejected as, is exposed as|verb phrase


passes-as:/ˈpæsɪz æz/| v.|充当；起…作用；|Something passes as something else if it functions or serves in the capacity of that thing.|This old shed passes as a workshop.  这个旧棚子充当了车间。|近义词：serves as, functions as, acts as|反义词: fails as, doesn't work as|verb phrase

词源: "passes" comes from Old French "passer" meaning "to step, walk, go, pass." "As" comes from Old English "alswaz," meaning "all so," which evolved into "also" and then "as."  The combination "passes as" developed to convey the meaning of something being accepted or regarded in a certain way.
