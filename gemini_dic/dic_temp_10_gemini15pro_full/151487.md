parrhesia:/pɑːrˈreɪʒə/ /pɑːrˈriːʒə/| n.|坦率直言，直言不讳|Frankness of speech; fearless outspokenness; free speech.  It implies not only freedom of speech, but also the duty to speak freely for the common good, even at personal risk.|Foucault admired the ancient Greek concept of parrhesia. 福柯很欣赏古希腊的坦率直言的概念。|Synonyms: candor, outspokenness, forthrightness, plain speaking|Antonyms: reticence, evasiveness, dissimulation|noun
