bechamel:/ˈbeɪʃəmel/ (US), /ˈbɛʃəmɛl/ (UK)| n.|白汁，奶油沙司|A rich white sauce made with butter, flour, and milk, often flavored with nutmeg, onion, or other seasonings.  It's one of the mother sauces of French cuisine.|The lasagna was layered with a rich bechamel and ground beef.|Synonyms: white sauce, cream sauce|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps a contrasting sauce like a tomato sauce)|noun
