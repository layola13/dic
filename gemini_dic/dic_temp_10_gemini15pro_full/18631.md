benumbment:/bɪˈnʌm.mənt/| n.|麻木；麻痹；失去感觉|A state of physical or emotional numbness; a lack of sensation or feeling.|The trauma of the accident left her in a state of benumbment.|近义词：numbness, insensibility, stupor, torpor|反义词：awareness, sensitivity, feeling|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From be- (prefix meaning "thoroughly") + numb + -ment (suffix forming nouns of action or process).  "Numb" itself comes from Old English *numen, past participle of *nimman "to take," related to German nehmen "to take," suggesting the original sense of having one's senses or feelings "taken away."
