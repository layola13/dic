词源:
From a (on) + cock (竖起). Originally referred to the cocked hammer of a firearm, then extended to other objects held in a similar position, and finally to a person's posture.

USAGE->
This word is primarily used as an adverb to describe a posture or the way something is held.

He walked acock through the town. = 他昂首阔步地走过城镇。

The dog's ears stood acock. = 狗的耳朵竖了起来。


PHRASE->
This word is not typically used in phrases. It functions primarily as a standalone adverb.


注意事项:

"Acock" is an older and somewhat less common word.  It can sometimes be perceived as slightly humorous or archaic.  While not inherently vulgar, its similarity to "cock" (penis) can make it sound suggestive in some contexts, so use with caution.
