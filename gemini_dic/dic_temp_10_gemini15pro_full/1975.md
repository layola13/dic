activism: /ˈæktɪvɪzəm/
n.
行动主义；积极行动
The doctrine or practice of vigorous action or involvement as a means of achieving political or other goals, especially in support of or opposition to one side of a controversial issue.
She is known for her activism in environmental protection. 她以在环境保护方面的积极行动而闻名。

Student activism played a key role in the protests. 学生行动主义在抗议活动中扮演了关键角色。

近义词: advocacy, campaigning, militancy
反义词: apathy, inaction, passivity
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From active + -ism.  Active traces back to the Latin word "actus," meaning "a doing, a driving, an impulse."  The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice.  Therefore, activism literally refers to the doctrine or practice of being active, particularly in pursuing a cause.
