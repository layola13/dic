mulishly:/ˈmjuːlɪʃli/ | adv. | 顽固地，倔强地 | In a stubborn and unyielding manner, like a mule. | He mulishly refused to apologize, even though he knew he was wrong. 他顽固地拒绝道歉，即使他知道自己错了。| stubbornly, obstinately, persistently, doggedly | compliantly, submissively | adverb

Etymology:
From mule + -ish + -ly.  "Mule" comes from Old French "mul," from Latin "mulus."  The suffix "-ish" forms adjectives meaning "like" or "having the qualities of," and "-ly" forms adverbs.
