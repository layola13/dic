cothurnate: /kəˈθɜːrneɪt/
adjective
高贵的，悲壮的，像穿厚底靴的
Of or relating to tragedy; tragic.  Elevated in style; pompous or lofty.  Worn or made for tragedy; buskined.
例句：The actor delivered his cothurnate lines with gravitas. 演员庄严地念着悲壮的台词。

例句：Her cothurnate pronouncements about the state of the nation seemed out of touch with reality. 她关于国家状况的高谈阔论似乎与现实脱节。
近义词：tragic, lofty, pompous, grandiloquent
反义词：comic, commonplace, humble, modest
词性：adjective


cothurnus: /kəˈθɜːnəs/
noun
厚底靴，高筒靴，悲剧
A thick-soled boot or buskin worn by actors in Greek tragedy, hence, tragedy in general.
例句：The actor donned the cothurnus for his role as Oedipus. 演员穿上厚底靴扮演俄狄浦斯。

例句：She embraced the cothurnus of her situation with stoic resolve. 她以坚忍的决心接受了她悲惨的处境。
近义词：buskin, tragedy
反义词：comedy
词性：noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cothurnatus*, from cothurnus (“buskin, thick-soled boot worn by tragic actors”). Ultimately from Greek κοθόρνος (kothórnos, “buskin”).
