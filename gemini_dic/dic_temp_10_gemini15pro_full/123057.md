monetarists:/ˈmʌnɪtərɪsts/
monetarists|n.|货币主义者|Advocates or supporters of monetarism, a school of economic thought that emphasizes the role of the money supply in controlling inflation and economic growth.|Monetarists argue that controlling the money supply is the key to a stable economy. 货币主义者认为，控制货币供应是稳定经济的关键。|
monetarists|n.|货币主义者|Many economists in the 1980s were monetarists. 20世纪80年代的许多经济学家都是货币主义者。|
monetarists|近义词:|monetary economists, supporters of monetarism|
monetarists|反义词:|Keynesians, fiscalists|
monetarists|词性:|noun (plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From monetary + -ist.  "Monetary" comes from the Latin "monetarius" (relating to money), which in turn derives from "moneta" (mint, money).  The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system.
