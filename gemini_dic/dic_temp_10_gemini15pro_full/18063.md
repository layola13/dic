beguilements:/bɪˈɡaɪlmənts/
|n.|诱惑；魅力；哄骗|The act of beguiling; charm or enchantment that deceives or deludes. |Her beguilements were legendary, captivating men with a single glance. 她有着极富传奇色彩的魅力，只需一眼就能让男人为之倾倒。|
| |Synonyms: allure, enticements, charms, attractions, temptations|
| |Antonyms: repulsions, deterrents, warnings, discouragement|
|noun|


beguilement:/bɪˈɡaɪlmənt/
|n.|诱惑；魅力；哄骗|The act of beguiling; charm or enchantment that deceives or deludes. |He used flattery and beguilement to get what he wanted. 他用奉承和哄骗来达到目的。|
| |Synonyms: allure, enticements, charms, attractions, temptations|
| |Antonyms: repulsions, deterrents, warnings, discouragement|
|noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *begylen*, from be- + gylen (“to deceive, beguile”).  The suffix -ment forms nouns of action or process. Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *gīlijaną* (“to enchant, delude”).
