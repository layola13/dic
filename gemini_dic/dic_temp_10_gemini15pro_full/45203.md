cull:/kʌl/ | v. n. |挑选，剔除; 扑杀; 精选; 剔除的东西|To select from a group, typically for a specific purpose (often involving removal or destruction).  |例句：The farmer culled the weakest lambs from the flock. 农夫从羊群中剔除了最弱的小羊。

|例句：She culled the best photographs from her trip to display. 她从旅行照片中挑选了最好的照片进行展示。|近义词：select, choose, pick, glean, weed out, thin out, winnow |反义词: include, keep, gather, amass |词性: verb, noun

cull:/kʌl/ | n. |剔除; 扑杀; 精选|The act of culling or the things that have been culled.|例句：The annual deer cull helps to control the population.  每年的鹿群扑杀有助于控制鹿群数量。

|例句：The cull from the library's collection included many outdated books. 图书馆藏书的剔除包括许多过时的书籍。|近义词：selection, choice, harvest |反义词: collection, whole |词性: noun

词源：From Latin *colligere "to collect," from com- "together" + legere "to gather, choose, read."  The original meaning was "to collect," but the sense shifted to "select and remove" likely through the practice of gathering the best and discarding the rest.
