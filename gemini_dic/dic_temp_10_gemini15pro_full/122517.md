mockle:/ˈmɒkəl/| adj.|大量的，许多的|A Scots word meaning "much," "many," or "large." It's often used to emphasize the quantity of something.|例句：There was a mockle crowd at the festival. 节日上有很多人。

例句：He inherited a mockle fortune from his uncle. 他从叔叔那里继承了一大笔财产。|近义词：much, many, large, considerable, substantial, ample, copious|反义词：little, few, small, meager, scant|词性:形容词
词源:From Middle English *mokel, from Old English *myċel, from Proto-Germanic *mikilaz, from Proto-Indo-European *meǵh₂- (“great, big”). Cognate with Scots meikle, Dutch mijkel (“large”), German michel (“big, much”), Old Norse mikill (“big”).
