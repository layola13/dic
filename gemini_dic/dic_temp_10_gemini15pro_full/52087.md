diamagnetism:/ˌdaɪəˈmæɡnɪtɪzəm/
n.|抗磁性|A property of materials that causes them to create a magnetic field in opposition to an externally applied magnetic field. |Bismuth exhibits strong diamagnetism. 铋表现出很强的抗磁性。|
近义词:antiferromagnetism|反义词:paramagnetism, ferromagnetism|词性:noun

diamagnetic:/ˌdaɪəˌmæɡˈnɛtɪk/
adj.|抗磁性的|Relating to or exhibiting diamagnetism; repelled by a magnet. |A diamagnetic substance is repelled by a magnet. 抗磁性物质会被磁铁排斥。|
近义词:non-magnetic|反义词:paramagnetic, ferromagnetic|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From dia- (Greek: through, across) + magnetic.  The term "diamagnetismus" was coined by Michael Faraday in 1845.
