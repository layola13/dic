diggers:/ˈdɪɡərz/| n.|挖掘者；挖掘机；淘金者|People who dig, especially for gold or other minerals.  Also, a colloquial term for Australian soldiers, originating from World War I.|The diggers worked tirelessly in the hot sun. 这些挖掘者在炎热的阳光下不知疲倦地工作着。|The ANZAC diggers fought bravely at Gallipoli.  澳新军团的士兵们在加里波利英勇作战。|近义词：miners, excavators, prospectors (for gold), troops, soldiers (for Australian soldiers)|反义词: - |noun

digging:/ˈdɪɡɪŋ/| n.|挖掘；挖掘出来的东西|The act of digging or the thing that is dug up.|The archaeological dig unearthed some ancient pottery. 考古挖掘出土了一些古代陶器。|The digging in the garden took all afternoon.  在花园里挖土花了一下午的时间。|近义词：excavation, unearthing|反义词:burying, filling|noun

dig:/dɪɡ/| v.|挖，掘；探究|To break up and move earth, etc., with a tool, machine, or with hands and claws; to search deeply into something.|They dug a hole for the new tree. 他们为新树挖了一个坑。|He's digging into the history of his family. 他正在钻研他的家族史。|近义词：excavate, delve, burrow, investigate|反义词:fill, bury|verb
