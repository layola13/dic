antiutilitarians:/ˌæntijuːˌtɪlɪˈteəriənz/
|n.|反功利主义者|Those who oppose utilitarianism, a moral philosophy that holds that actions are right if they promote happiness or pleasure and wrong if they produce unhappiness or pain.|The antiutilitarians argued that maximizing happiness wasn't the only moral consideration. 反功利主义者认为，最大化幸福并非唯一的道德考量。|Opponents of utilitarianism, critics of utilitarianism|Utilitarians, proponents of utilitarianism|noun
