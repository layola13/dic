dakir: /ˈdɑːkɪr/ | n. | 达基尔 (一种北非长袍) | A dakir is a long, loose-fitting robe traditionally worn by men in North Africa.  It is often made of wool or cotton and can be plain or decorated. | He wore a simple white dakir. 他穿着一件简单的白色达基尔长袍。|  Synonyms: djellaba, gandoura | Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, perhaps Western clothing) | noun


Etymology->

The word "dakir" likely comes from the Arabic word "djellaba" or a related term, referring to a similar type of robe.  The exact etymology and variations in spelling and pronunciation across North African dialects are complex.
