lent: /lent/ | v. | 借出；贷出; |To allow someone to use something that you expect will be returned. | I lent my car to my brother. 我把我的车借给了我弟弟。 | He lent her his book. 他把他的书借给了她。 | 近义词：loan, advance | 反义词：borrow, receive | 词性: 动词

lent: /lent/ | n. |  四旬斋; |The period of forty days before Easter, a time of fasting and repentance in the Christian Church. |  We gave up chocolate for Lent.  我们在四旬斋期间放弃了吃巧克力。|  During Lent, many Christians abstain from meat. 在四旬斋期间，许多基督徒戒肉。| 近义词：fasting period | 反义词：None | 词性: 名词

lent: /lent/ | adj. |  (古语) 借出的 |  Given temporarily for use by another. (Archaic) | This usage is now largely archaic. 这个用法现在基本已经过时了。 | No contemporary example available. 没有当代例句。 | 近义词：loaned | 反义词：borrowed | 词性: 形容词


词源:

From Middle English *lenten*, from Old English *lænan* (“to lend, grant, give”), from Proto-Germanic *lēnaną* (“to lend”), from Proto-Indo-European *leh₂-n-eh₂* (“to lend, borrow”). Cognate with Old Frisian *lēna* (“to lend”), Old Saxon *lēnan*, Old High German *lihan* (“to lend”), Old Norse *lána* (“to lend”), Gothic *leihwan* (“to lend”).  The noun meaning "Lent" comes from the Old English *lencten* ("spring"), referencing the time of year when Lent falls.
