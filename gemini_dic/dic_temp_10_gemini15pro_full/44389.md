cross-fertilise: /ˌkrɒs ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz/ (UK) /ˌkrɔːs ˈfɜːrtɪlaɪz/ (US)
| v. | 交叉施肥，互相影响，相互促进 | To fertilize a plant with pollen from another plant of a different variety, or to exchange ideas or influences between different groups or disciplines. | The company hopes to cross-fertilise ideas between its design and engineering teams. 该公司希望在其设计和工程团队之间相互交流思想。 | intermingle, hybridize, integrate | isolate, segregate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "cross-" + "fertilize". "Cross-" indicates a mixing or interaction between different things, and "fertilize" originally referred to making soil fertile but broadened to encompass the idea of making something productive or stimulating growth and development.
