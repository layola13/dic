ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lesen, from Old English *lēas, from Proto-Germanic *lausaz (“pasture, grazing”).  Cognate with Old High German lōs (“pasture”), Old Norse lauss (“loose”).  Related to the verb "to lease."  The original sense referred to the right to pasture animals on land, later extending to the gleanings or what was left on the land after harvesting.
