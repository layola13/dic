glyptodont:/ˈɡlɪptəˌdɑːnt/| n. |雕齿兽|An extinct, heavily armored mammal, resembling a giant armadillo, that lived in the Americas during the Pleistocene epoch.|The museum displayed a complete glyptodont skeleton. 博物馆展出了一具完整的雕齿兽骨骼。|近义词:armadillo (distant relative)|反义词:None (extinct animal, no direct opposite)|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek *γλυπτός (gluptós, “carved, engraved”) + *ὀδούς (odoús, “tooth”).  Referring to the sculpted appearance of the animal's teeth.
