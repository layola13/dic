intergraft: /ˈɪntərɡræft/
verb
嫁接；移植；（使）紧密结合
To graft (tissue or an organ) from one part of the body to another or from one individual to another.  To join closely as if by grafting.
例句：
The surgeon intergrafted skin from the patient's leg onto the burn wound.  外科医生将病人腿上的皮肤移植到烧伤处。

例句：
The two communities intergrafted over time, sharing traditions and customs.  随着时间的推移，这两个社区紧密结合，分享传统和习俗。

近义词：graft, transplant, implant, integrate
反义词：separate, detach
词性：v.

intergraft: /ˈɪntərɡræft/
noun
嫁接物；移植体
A graft of tissue or an organ from one part of the body to another or from one individual to another.
例句：
The intergraft showed signs of rejection after a few weeks.  几周后，移植体出现排斥反应的迹象。

例句：
The intergraft from the donor was successful.  来自捐赠者的移植很成功。

近义词：graft, transplant, implant
反义词：N/A
词性：n.
