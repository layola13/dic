coarse:/kɔːrs/ (US) /kɔːs/ (UK)| adj.|粗糙的，粗俗的，粗劣的|Rough or harsh in texture.  Rude or vulgar.|The beach was covered in coarse sand.  海滩上覆盖着粗糙的沙子。 He made a coarse joke that offended everyone. 他讲了个粗俗的笑话，冒犯了所有人。|近义词：rough, harsh, crude, vulgar, unrefined|反义词：fine, smooth, refined, polite, sophisticated|词性:形容词

Etymology: From Middle English *cors*, from Old French *cors*, from Latin *cursus* ("course, running"). The original meaning related to something not properly finished or having irregularities, like a race interrupted.  Over time, the meaning shifted to describe textures and then behavior.
