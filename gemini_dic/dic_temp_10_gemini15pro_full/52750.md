dilapidator:/dɪˈlæpɪdeɪtər/
|n.|破败者； dilapidator 指的是导致某物（通常是建筑物）破败的人|A dilapidator is someone who causes something, usually a building, to fall into a state of disrepair.|The dilapidator of the historic mansion was brought to court.  这座历史悠久的宅邸的破败者被带上了法庭。|Synonyms: vandal, ruiner, wrecker|Antonyms: restorer, renovator|noun
dilapidated:/dɪˈlæpɪdeɪtɪd/
|adj.|破败的；荒废的；破旧的|Dilapidated describes something, especially a building, in a state of disrepair or ruin due to neglect or age.|The old house was dilapidated and in need of serious repairs. 这栋老房子破败不堪，需要大修。|Synonyms: ramshackle, derelict, ruined|Antonyms: pristine, renovated, restored|adjective
