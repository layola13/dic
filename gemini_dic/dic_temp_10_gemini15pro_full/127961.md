neorealism:/ˌniːoʊˈriːəlɪzəm/ | n. |新现实主义|A theory of international relations that emphasizes the role of power and national interest in shaping state behavior, rejecting the idealistic notions of international cooperation and harmony.  It posits that states are primarily motivated by self-preservation and security in an anarchic international system. | Neorealism influenced US foreign policy during the Cold War. 新现实主义影响了冷战时期的美国外交政策。 | 近义词: realpolitik, power politics | 反义词: idealism, liberal internationalism | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From neo- (new) + realism.  The "neo" distinguishes it from classical realism, though they share core principles.  Coined in the mid-20th century. 由 neo-（新的）+ realism 组成。“neo” 将其与古典现实主义区分开来，尽管它们具有共同的核心原则。  20世纪中期创造。
