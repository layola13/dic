In chaos theory, a set of numerical values or points in a phase space towards which a system tends to evolve regardless of the starting conditions of the system. |例句：The new shopping mall is a major attractor for the town.  这个新的购物中心是小镇的主要吸引物。

ETYMOLOGY->
From the late 16th century, "attract" comes from the Latin word "attrahere,"  meaning "to draw to oneself."  "Attract" became a noun form with the suffix "-or," creating "attractor" in the mid 20th century, especially in scientific contexts like chaos theory.
