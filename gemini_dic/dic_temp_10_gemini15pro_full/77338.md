gaucherie:/ˈɡoʊʃəri/| n.|笨拙，不圆滑，失礼|Awkwardness or tactlessness in social behavior.  A lack of social grace or ease; clumsiness.|Her gaucherie at the formal dinner was evident when she spilled wine on the guest of honor.|Synonym: awkwardness, clumsiness, ineptness, ineptitude, ungainliness|Antonym: grace, elegance, poise, tact, finesse|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From French *gaucherie*, from *gauche* (left, awkward), from Old Frankish *gawi* (left).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *winkaz* (left, weak).
