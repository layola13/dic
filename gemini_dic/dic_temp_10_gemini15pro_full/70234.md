ficklest:/ˈfɪk(ə)ləst/| adj.|最易变的；最反复无常的|Changing frequently, especially as regards one's loyalties, interests, or affection.|例句：Fashion is the ficklest industry. 时尚是最善变的行业。|例句：He was accused of being the ficklest of lovers. 他被指责是最薄情的恋人。|近义词：capricious, erratic, mercurial, volatile, whimsical|反义词：constant, steadfast, loyal, steady|词性：形容词

词源：From Middle English *fikelest, superlative of *fikel, from Old English *ficol (“deceitful, treacherous, false”), from Proto-Germanic *fīkaz (“deceitful”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)peyg- (“to curse, to offend”). Cognate with Dutch fiek (“sly”), German feige (“cowardly”). Doublet of fig.  More at fickle.
