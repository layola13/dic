domiciliar:/ˌdɒmɪˈsɪliəri/ (US) /ˌdɒmɪˈsɪliəri/ (UK)| adj.| domiciliary | relating to the provision of healthcare or other services in a patient's home | Example:  Domiciliary care is provided to those who are unable to leave their homes.  为那些无法离开家的人提供上门护理。

Example: The domiciliary visit by the nurse was much appreciated. 护士的上门探访非常受欢迎。


|近义词: home-based, in-home, residential, house-call (for medical services) |反义词: hospital, clinical, institutional |词性: adjective

domicile: /ˈdɒmɪsaɪl/ (US) /ˈdɒmɪsaɪl/ (UK) | n. |住所，定居地 | A person's fixed, permanent, and principal home for legal purposes. |Example:  He changed his domicile from London to Paris. 他把住所从伦敦搬到了巴黎。

Example: The company's domicile is in Delaware.  该公司的注册地是特拉华州。|近义词: residence, abode, dwelling, home |反义词:  N/A |词性: noun

domicile: /ˈdɒmɪsaɪl/ (US) /ˈdɒmɪsaɪl/ (UK) | vt. |使定居，安置 | To establish in a domicile. | Example: The refugees were domiciled in temporary housing. 难民被安置在临时住所。

Example: The company is domiciled in the Cayman Islands for tax purposes.  该公司出于税务目的在开曼群岛注册。|近义词: settle, establish, reside |反义词:  N/A |词性: verb
