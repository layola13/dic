disfranchise:/ˌdɪsˈfrænˌtʃaɪz/ | vt. |剥夺公民权；剥夺选举权；剥夺权利|To deprive of a franchise, a legal right, or a privilege, especially the right to vote.|Many people were disfranchised because of their criminal records. 很多人因为犯罪记录而被剥夺了公民权。|The new law disfranchised thousands of voters. 新法律剥夺了数千选民的选举权。|Synonyms: disenfranchise, deprive, strip|Antonyms: enfranchise, empower|Verb
