albyn:/ˈalbɪn/
noun
Scotland
A poetic name for Scotland, derived from the Gaelic *Alba*.
From the misty heights of Albyn, the warrior looked down upon the valley.
近义词: Caledonia, Scotia
反义词: (None - as it's a proper noun for a specific place)
词性: n.

Etymology: From Scottish Gaelic *Alba*, meaning "Scotland".  Ultimately from a Brittonic or Pictish source, perhaps related to the Latin *albus* "white", possibly referring to the white cliffs of Dover or other coastal features.
