battledoor:/ˈbætlˌdɔːr/ (US), /ˈbætlˌdɔː(r)/ (UK)| n.|羽毛球拍；板羽球；（旧时）洗衣用的木板|A bat with a flat, solid surface used for hitting a shuttlecock in the game of battledore and shuttlecock, a precursor to badminton.  Also, a wooden implement used for washing clothes by beating them.|He brought out his old battledore and shuttlecock set for a game in the garden. 他拿出他旧的板羽球拍在花园里玩游戏。|Synonyms: bat, paddle, racket|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, could consider "ball" as the object being hit)|noun

battledoor:/ˈbætlˌdɔːr/ (US), /ˈbætlˌdɔː(r)/ (UK)| adj.|（旧时）像板羽球似的来回的|Resembling the back-and-forth motion of battledore and shuttlecock.|The argument went back and forth in a battledore manner.  这场争论像板羽球一样来回进行。|Synonyms: reciprocating, alternating, oscillating|Antonyms: static, stationary, unidirectional|adjective
