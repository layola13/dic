fukuyama:/ˌfuːkuˈjɑːmə/ | n. | 福山, 福山哲學 |  Refers to Yoshihiro Francis Fukuyama, a Japanese-American political scientist, political economist, and author.  Known for his book "The End of History and the Last Man," which argued that the spread of liberal democracies may signal the endpoint of humanity's sociocultural evolution and the final form of human government. |  Fukuyama's thesis sparked widespread debate.  福山的论点引发了广泛的争论。| synonym:  (no direct synonym for a person, could use "political theorist," "philosopher") | antonym: (no direct antonym for a person) | noun


fukuyamaism:/ˌfuːkuˈjɑːməɪzəm/ | n. | 福山主义 | The theory, popularized by Francis Fukuyama, positing that the worldwide spread of liberal democracies and free-market capitalism of the West and its lifestyle may signal the endpoint of humanity's sociocultural evolution and the final form of human government.  | Fukuyamaism has been both praised and criticized. 福山主义受到了赞扬和批评。 | synonym: (no direct synonym, could use "end of history thesis") | antonym: (no direct antonym) | noun
