gaslighted:/ˈɡæsˌlɪtɪd/
v. |被操纵的，被灌输错误信息的|Manipulated into questioning one's own sanity or perceptions, often by being persistently presented with false information.|
例句：
She felt gaslighted after her partner repeatedly denied things she knew to be true. 她感觉被操纵了，因为她的伴侣反复否认她知道是真实的事情。

He claimed he was being gaslighted by the media. 他声称他被媒体操纵了。

近义词：manipulated, brainwashed, deceived
反义词：empowered, validated, supported
词性：动词的过去分词形式，用作形容词

gaslight:/ˈɡæsˌlaɪt/
v. |操纵，灌输错误信息|To manipulate someone into questioning their own sanity or perceptions, often by persistently presenting them with false information.|
例句：
He tried to gaslight her by pretending he didn't remember the conversation. 他假装不记得谈话内容，试图操纵她。

The abuser will often gaslight the victim into believing they are the problem. 施虐者经常会操纵受害者，让他们相信自己是问题所在。

近义词：manipulate, brainwash, deceive
反义词：support, validate, empower
词性：动词


词源: From the 1938 play and subsequent films *Gas Light*, in which a husband manipulates his wife into believing she is going insane by dimming the gas lights and then denying it. 源于1938年的戏剧及其后续电影《煤气灯下》，剧中丈夫通过调暗煤气灯然后否认来操纵妻子，让她相信自己疯了。
