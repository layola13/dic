datives:/ˈdeɪtɪvz/| n.|与格|A grammatical case used to indicate the noun or pronoun that receives the indirect object of a verb.  It often corresponds to the word "to" or "for" in English.|She gave the book to him. (him is the indirect object, the dative case in some languages) 她把书给了他。|近义词: indirect objects|反义词: (No direct antonym for a grammatical case)|noun

dative:/ˈdeɪtɪv/| adj.|与格的|Relating to or in the dative case.|The dative case is used frequently in German.  与格在德语中经常使用。|近义词: indirect object|反义词: (No direct antonym for a grammatical case)|adjective
