disciplinarianism:/ˌdɪsɪplɪˈnɛəriənɪzəm/
|n.|纪律主义;  严厉的纪律作风;  笃信纪律主义|The belief in or practice of strict discipline.|The headmaster's disciplinarianism created a climate of fear in the school. 校长的纪律主义在学校制造了一种恐惧的气氛。|近义词: authoritarianism, rigorism|反义词: permissiveness, leniency|词性: noun

Etymology:
From disciplinarian + -ism.  "Disciplinarian" comes from "discipline," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "disciplina," meaning "instruction," "knowledge," "teaching," or "learning."  The "-ism" suffix denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice.
