condemners:/kənˈdɛmərz/| n.|谴责者，责难者|People who express strong disapproval or pronounce a negative judgment on someone or something.  Those who condemn.|例句：The condemners of the new policy were vocal in their opposition.  新政策的谴责者公开表示反对。

例句：The judge listened patiently to the condemners before passing sentence.  法官在宣判前耐心地听取了谴责者的意见。|近义词：critics, detractors, censurers|反义词：supporters, defenders, advocates|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From condemn + -er.  "Condemn" comes from the Old French condemner, from Latin condemnare "to sentence, doom, condemn," from com- "together" + damnare "to inflict loss upon, condemn," from damnum "loss, damage."  The suffix "-er" denotes a person or thing that performs the action of the verb.
