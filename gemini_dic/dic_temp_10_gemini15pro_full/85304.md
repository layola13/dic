harnessing:/ˈhɑːrnɪsɪŋ/ /ˈhɑːrnəsɪŋ/
verb
利用，治理，控制
the process of controlling and making use of natural resources, especially to produce energy
例句：Harnessing the power of the wind is becoming increasingly important.  利用风能正变得越来越重要。

例句：They are harnessing the sun's energy to heat their home.  他们正在利用太阳能为他们的房子供暖。

近义词：utilizing, exploiting, employing, channeling
反义词：wasting, squandering, neglecting, ignoring
词性：v.


harness:/ˈhɑːrnɪs/ /ˈhɑːrnəs/
noun
马具，挽具
a set of straps and fittings by which a horse or other draught animal is fastened to a cart, plough, etc.
例句：The farmer put the harness on the horse.  农民给马套上马具。

例句：The leather harness was worn and cracked.  皮革马具已经磨损并开裂了。

近义词：tack, gear, equipment
反义词：(没有直接的反义词，可以考虑用 "freedom" 或 "release" 表示从harness中解放出来)
词性：n.


harness:/ˈhɑːrnɪs/ /ˈhɑːrnəs/
verb
套上马具，利用，控制
put a harness on (a horse or other draught animal). control and make use of (natural resources), especially to produce energy.
例句：He harnessed the horses to the wagon.  他把马套到马车上。

例句：We need to harness the power of the river.  我们需要利用河流的力量。

近义词：utilize, exploit, employ, channel, yoke
反义词：release, free, unharness, waste, squander
词性：v.



词源：From Old French *harneis* (Modern French *harnais*), of Germanic origin.
