moderates:/ˈmɒdərəɪts/ (UK) /ˈmɑːdərɪts/ (US)| n. |温和派；稳健派|People who hold moderate political views, avoiding extreme positions. |The moderates in the party called for compromise.  该党中的温和派呼吁妥协。|近义词：centrists, conservatives (sometimes)|反义词:extremists, radicals|noun
moderates:/ˈmɒdərəɪts/ (UK) /ˈmɑːdərɪts/ (US)| v. |缓和；使适中；主持|To make something less extreme or intense; to preside over a meeting or discussion. |The chairman moderates the debate. 主席主持辩论。|近义词：lessens, mitigates, chairs, mediates|反义词:intensifies, aggravates, incites|verb
moderate:/ˈmɒdərət/ (UK) /ˈmɑːdərət/ (US)| adj. |温和的；适度的；中等的|Not extreme in degree, amount, strength, or quality. |The weather was moderate, not too hot or too cold. 天气温和，不太热也不太冷。|近义词：mild, temperate, reasonable|反义词:extreme, excessive, immoderate|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *moderātus, past participle of moderārī (“to regulate, restrain”), from Latin modus (“measure, limit”).
