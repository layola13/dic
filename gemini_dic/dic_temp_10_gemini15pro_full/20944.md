bleeder resistor:/ˈbliːdər rɪˈzɪstər/| n.|泄放电阻，分压电阻|A resistor used to discharge a capacitor when the circuit is powered off, for safety reasons. It also can be used to establish a specific voltage level by providing a path for current to flow.|The bleeder resistor prevents dangerous voltages from building up on the capacitor after the power is turned off.|Synonym: discharge resistor|Antonym: N/A (no direct antonym)|noun

bleeder:/ˈbliːdər/| n.|出血的人/物，放血器|Someone or something that bleeds, or a device used for bleeding.  In electronics, it refers to a component that drains current. |The bleeder resistor discharges the capacitor.|Synonym: drainer (in electronics context)|Antonym: N/A|noun

resistor:/rɪˈzɪstər/| n.|电阻器|An electrical component that opposes the flow of current.|The resistor limits the current in the circuit.|Synonym: resistance (can be used in some contexts)|Antonym: conductor|noun
