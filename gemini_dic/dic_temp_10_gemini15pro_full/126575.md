naffer:/ˈnæfə(r)/ | adj. | 笨拙的，愚蠢的，无能的 |Someone who is naffer is awkward, foolish, or incompetent.  It's primarily used in British slang, particularly in the Midlands and northern England.  |  "He's a bit naffer, isn't he? He can't even boil an egg." 他有点笨，不是吗？他连鸡蛋都不会煮。 | 近义词:  clumsy, awkward, inept, foolish, silly | 反义词:  skilled, adept, competent, clever, intelligent | 词性: 形容词

词源:  The etymology of "naffer" is uncertain, but it's thought to possibly derive from dialectal variations of "knacker," meaning "to tire out" or "exhaust," which could extend metaphorically to someone who is incompetent or worn out mentally.
