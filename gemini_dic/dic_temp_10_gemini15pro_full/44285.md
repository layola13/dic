cronyism:/ˈkroʊniɪzəm/| n.|裙带关系；任人唯亲；徇私|The practice of favoring one's friends, especially in political appointments.|The mayor's administration was riddled with cronyism, with key positions going to unqualified friends and family.|近义词：favoritism, nepotism, patronage|反义词:impartiality, meritocracy|词性:名词

Etymology:  From crony + -ism. Crony (meaning a close friend or companion) dates back to the 17th century, possibly originating from student slang at Cambridge University, from Greek *chronios "long-lasting".
