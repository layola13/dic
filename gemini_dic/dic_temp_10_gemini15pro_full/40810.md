convolution:/ˌkɒnvəˈluːʃən/ /ˌkɑːnvəˈluːʃən/| n. |(数学) 卷积|In mathematics, convolution is a mathematical operation on two functions (f and g) that produces a third function expressing how the shape of one is modified by the other. |The convolution of the two functions yielded a new function.  这两个函数的卷积产生了一个新的函数。|近义词：(In specific mathematical contexts) cross-correlation|反义词:deconvolution|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *convolutio, from Latin convolvere "to roll together, roll up," from com- "together" (see com-) + volvere "to roll" (see voluble).
