bowgrace: /ˈboʊɡreɪs/ | n. | 优雅的鞠躬，优美的屈膝礼 | An act of bowing with grace and elegance.  A graceful and elegant bow or curtsy. |  She offered a bowgrace to the queen. 她向女王行了一个优雅的屈膝礼。| 近义词: curtsy, obeisance | 反义词: snub, slight | 词性: 名词


词源:  "Bowgrace" is a relatively modern word, likely formed by combining "bow" (meaning to bend the head or body as a sign of respect) and "grace" (meaning elegance and refinement of movement). It doesn't have a long etymological history like some older words.
