carbon:/ˈkɑːrbən/
noun
碳
A nonmetallic chemical element with atomic number 6 that readily forms compounds with many other elements and is a constituent of all organic compounds.
Carbon is essential for life. 碳是生命之源。
synonyms:  graphite, charcoal, coal, diamond (different forms)
antonyms: (None for carbon specifically, perhaps elements lacking carbon-based life forms like silicon?)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French carbone and Latin carbō (“coal, charcoal”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱerh₂- (“to burn”).
