cleave:/kliːv/| v.|劈开；粘着；坚持|To split or divide by force; to adhere firmly; to remain faithful to.|The butcher cleaved the bone in two. 屠夫把骨头劈成了两半。|近义词：split, adhere, stick, cling|反义词:separate, detach|词性:动词

cleave:/kliːv/| v.|粘着；坚持|To adhere firmly; to remain faithful to.|The two surfaces cleaved together. 两个表面粘在一起。|近义词：adhere, stick, cling, hold|反义词:separate, detach|词性:动词

词源: Old English *clēofan (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *kleubaną (“to cleave, stick”). Cognate with Dutch klieven (“to cleave, split”), German klieben (“to cleave, split”), Old Norse kljúfa (“to cleave”).  Doublet of climb.  This verb exists in two forms with opposite meanings due to divergent development from the same root.
