overconscientious:/ˌoʊvərˌkɑːnʃiˈenʃəs/| adj.|过度认真的，过分谨慎的|Excessively careful and diligent; overly concerned with doing things correctly or perfectly.|She was so overconscientious that she worried about every little detail.|近义词：meticulous, scrupulous, painstaking|反义词：careless, negligent|词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From over- + conscientious.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively," and "conscientious" comes from Latin *conscientia* "knowledge within oneself," especially of right and wrong, from *com-"together" + *scire* "to know."
