believability:/bɪˌliːvəˈbɪləti/| n. |可信性，可靠性|The believability of her story was questioned. |The witness's shaky testimony damaged the believability of the prosecution's case. |credibility, plausibility|unbelievability, implausibility|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From believe + -ability.  "Believe" comes from Middle English *bileven*, from Old English *belīefan*  (meaning "to hold dear, value, trust"),  composed of *be-* (meaning "about, around, by") and *lēof* (meaning "dear, beloved"). "-ability" is a suffix meaning "capable of being."
