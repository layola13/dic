condescend:/ˌkɒndɪˈsend/ (UK), /ˌkɑːndɪˈsend/ (US)| v. |屈尊；俯就；摆架子|To behave as if one is of a higher social class or rank than others, often by doing something considered beneath one's dignity.|The professor condescended to speak to the freshman. 教授屈尊与新生交谈。|
近义词：patronize, deign, stoop|反义词：respect, admire|词性:动词

condescending:/ˌkɒndɪˈsendɪŋ/ (UK), /ˌkɑːndɪˈsendɪŋ/ (US)| adj. |居高临下的；恩赐的；屈尊的|Having or showing an attitude of patronizing superiority.|She gave him a condescending smile. 她给了他一个居高临下的微笑。|
近义词：patronizing, supercilious, haughty|反义词：humble, respectful, egalitarian|词性:形容词
