golyardeys:/ˌɡɒlɪˈɑːrdiːz/
n.
欢乐的伙伴; 饮酒作乐的诗人; 放荡不羁的人;
A person, typically a medieval poet or jester, known for revelry, ribaldry, and satirical or bawdy verse.  These were often wandering scholars or clerics who celebrated the joys of life, often in defiance of conventional morality.
例句：The golyardeys of the Middle Ages were known for their irreverent songs and poems. 中世纪的欢乐的伙伴以其不敬的歌曲和诗歌而闻名。

例句：The tavern was filled with the boisterous laughter of golyardeys. 酒馆里充满了欢乐的伙伴们喧闹的笑声。

近义词：reveler, carouser, merrymaker, jester, rogue, bohemian
反义词：puritan, ascetic, moralist


词性: noun

词源: From Anglo-Norman *goliard(eis), from Old French goliart, of uncertain origin. Possibly from a personal name, perhaps ultimately from Germanic *gaillard “joyful, lively.”
