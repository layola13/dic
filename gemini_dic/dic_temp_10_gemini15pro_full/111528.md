loathing:/ˈloʊðɪŋ/| n.|厌恶，憎恨|An intense feeling of disgust or aversion.|She felt a deep loathing for her abuser.|disgust, hatred, aversion, revulsion, abhorrence|affection, love, liking|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lothing*, from Old English *lāðung* (“hatred, disgust, insult”), from *lāð* (“hateful, hostile, loathsome”), from Proto-Germanic *laiþaz* (“hateful, loathsome”), from Proto-Indo-European *leyt- (“to be sticky, slimy”). Cognate with Old High German *leid* (“suffering, sadness, injury”), Old Norse *leiðr* (“loathsome, unpleasant”), Gothic *leiþs* (“evil”).
