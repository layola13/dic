aegean:/ɪˈdʒiːən/| adj. n.|爱琴海的；爱琴海|Relating to the Aegean Sea, an arm of the Mediterranean Sea located between Greece and Turkey.  It can also refer to the Bronze Age civilization that flourished in the Aegean region.|The Aegean Sea is known for its beautiful islands. 爱琴海以其美丽的岛屿而闻名。

The Aegean civilization was a precursor to classical Greece. 爱琴海文明是古典希腊的前身。|近义词：Mediterranean, Greek (when referring to the culture)|反义词: (No direct antonym exists)|词性: adjective, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Aegaeum (mare)* "Aegean (sea)," from Greek *Aigaion (pelagos)*, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to the Greek *aigis* "storm" or to *Aigeus*, a mythical king of Athens who drowned himself in the sea believing his son Theseus had died.
