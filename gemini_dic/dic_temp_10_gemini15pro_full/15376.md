backtracks:/ˈbæktræks/ (US), /ˈbæktræks/ (UK)| v.|后退，折返；取消（诺言、声明等）|To go back over the same route; to reverse one's position or actions, often metaphorically. |The hiker backtracked when he realized he was lost.  这徒步旅行者意识到自己迷路了，于是原路返回。

The politician backtracked on his promise to raise taxes. 这位政治家取消了他要增税的承诺。|近义词：retreats, withdraws, reverses, recants|反义词：advances, proceeds, continues|词性：动词


backtrack:/ˈbæktræk/ (US), /ˈbæktræk/ (UK)| n.|回溯；倒退的路线|The act of going back over the same route, literally or metaphorically. |The detective followed the backtrack of the thief.  侦探沿着小偷的足迹追寻。

There was no backtrack available once the decision was made. 一旦做出决定，就没有回旋的余地了。|近义词：retreat, reversal|反义词：advance, progression|词性：名词
