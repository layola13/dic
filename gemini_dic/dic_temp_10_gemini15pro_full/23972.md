breastwork:/ˈbrɛstwɜːrk/| n.|胸墙，防壁|A low, temporary wall built for defense, typically made of earth, sandbags, or other materials.  It provides protection from enemy fire.|The soldiers quickly erected a breastwork of sandbags.|Synonyms: parapet, rampart, bulwark, barricade|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, consider words like opening, gap)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From breast (chest) + work (fortification).  The idea is that the wall is about chest-high.
