demoralizingly:/dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪzɪŋli/
adverb
令人沮丧地， demoralizing 的副词形式
in a way that causes someone to lose confidence or hope
The team played demoralizingly, losing by a large margin.  这个队伍的比赛令人沮丧，惨败。
Synonyms: dishearteningly, disappointingly, discouragingly
Antonyms: encouragingly, inspiringly, upliftingly
词性: 副词


demoralize:/dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪz/
verb
使士气低落，使沮丧
to cause (someone) to lose confidence or hope
The loss demoralized the team.  这场失利使队伍的士气低落。
Synonyms: dishearten, discourage, dispirit
Antonyms: encourage, hearten, inspire
词性: 动词


demoralized:/dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪzd/
adjective
士气低落的，沮丧的
having lost confidence or hope
The demoralized troops retreated. 士气低落的军队撤退了。
Synonyms: disheartened, discouraged, dispirited
Antonyms: encouraged, heartened, inspired
词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *dé-* (expressing reversal) + *moral* (from Latin *moralis*, relating to character) + *-iser* (to make) + *-ing* (present participle suffix) + *-ly* (adverbial suffix)
