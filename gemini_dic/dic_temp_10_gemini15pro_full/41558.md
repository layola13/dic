corporale: /kɔːrˈpɔːrəli/ /kɔːrˈporəl/ | n. | 圣餐布; 体罚 | A linen cloth on which the consecrated Host and chalice are placed during the celebration of the Eucharist, especially in the Roman Catholic Church.  Also, historically, corporal punishment. |  The priest carefully unfolded the corporale on the altar.  神父小心翼翼地在祭坛上展开圣餐布。| Synonym: pall, communion cloth (for Eucharist sense); physical punishment (for historical sense) | Antonym: (No direct antonym, could consider spiritual discipline for the historical sense) | noun

corporale: /kɔːrˈpɔːrəli/ /kɔːrˈporəl/ | adj. | 肉体的; 身体的; 物质的 | Relating to the body, especially as opposed to the spirit. | Corporale punishment was common in schools in the past. 过去学校里体罚很普遍。 | Synonym: bodily, physical, fleshly | Antonym: spiritual, incorporeal | adjective
