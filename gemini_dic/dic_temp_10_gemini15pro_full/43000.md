coward:/ˈkaʊərd/| n.|懦夫，胆小鬼|A person who lacks courage and is easily intimidated.|He was too much of a coward to stand up to the bully. 他太胆小了，不敢反抗那个恶霸。|近义词：chicken, wimp, craven|反义词: hero, brave|名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *coward*, from Old French *coart*, *couard*, from *coe*, *coue* "tail" (from Latin *cauda*) + *-ard* (pejorative suffix).  The original sense was of an animal with its tail between its legs, hence, timid.
