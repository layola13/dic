acquiet:/əˈkwaɪɪt/| vt.|宣判无罪；使平静；履行（义务）；偿还（债务）|to free someone from a criminal charge by a verdict of not guilty; to behave in a specified way;  to discharge (an obligation); to pay (a debt)|The jury acquitted the defendant of all charges. 陪审团宣判被告所有罪名不成立。

He acquitted himself well in his first job. 他在第一份工作中表现出色。|近义词：absolve, exonerate, clear, discharge, fulfill|反义词：convict, incriminate, condemn|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *aquieter, from a- (from Latin ad 'to') + quiet 'quiet' (from Latin quietus 'at rest').  The sense development is from 'to put at rest,' to 'to settle (a claim),'  to 'to free from a charge.'
