hyphomycetes:/ˌhaɪfəˈmaɪsiːtiːz/ | n. | 半知菌 |A large, artificial class of fungi characterized by the formation of their spores (called conidia) on exposed hyphae or aggregations of hyphae, not enclosed within a fruiting body.|  Hyphomycetes play a vital role in decomposition in many ecosystems. 半知菌在许多生态系统中扮演着重要的分解角色。|Synonym:  conidial fungi, Deuteromycetes (obsolete), Fungi Imperfecti (obsolete)| Antonym: None (as it's a classification, not a characteristic)| Noun
