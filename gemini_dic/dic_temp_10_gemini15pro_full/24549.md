broadsided:/ˈbrɔːdsaɪdɪd/
verb
猛烈抨击，攻击; 用船的舷侧撞击
To attack or criticize someone fiercely and unexpectedly.  To hit the side of something, especially a ship, with the broadside of another ship.
The candidate was broadsided by accusations of corruption just days before the election.  候选人在选举前几天遭到腐败指控的猛烈抨击。
The small fishing boat was broadsided by a freighter and capsized.  这艘小型渔船被一艘货船撞上侧面后倾覆了。
近义词:attacked, criticized, slammed, bombarded, rammed
反义词:praised, defended, supported
词性:v.


broadside:/ˈbrɔːdsaɪd/
noun
舷侧齐射;  猛烈抨击
The simultaneous firing of all the guns on one side of a warship. A strong verbal attack.
The warship delivered a devastating broadside.  军舰进行了一次毁灭性的舷侧齐射。
He launched a broadside against the government's economic policies.  他对政府的经济政策进行了猛烈抨击。
近义词:attack, barrage, volley, salvo
反义词:defense, praise
词性:n.


词源: From "broad" (wide) + "side" (side of a ship). Originally referred to the simultaneous firing of all guns on one side of a warship.  源自“broad”（宽的）+“side”（船侧）。最初指的是军舰一侧所有火炮的同时射击。
