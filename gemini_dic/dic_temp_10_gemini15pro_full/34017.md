citizenish:/ˈsɪtɪzənɪʃ/ | adj. | 平民的；市民的；有公民意识的；像市民的 | Resembling, characteristic of, or appropriate to a citizen; suggestive of the attitudes, behavior, or status of a private citizen. | He gave a citizenish speech about the importance of voting. 他发表了一篇关于投票重要性的、很有公民意识的演讲。 | 近义词：civic, civil, public-spirited | 反义词：uncivil, unpatriotic | 词性：形容词

Etymology: citizen + -ish.  From "citizen" (meaning a legally recognized subject or national of a state or commonwealth) + "-ish" (a suffix forming adjectives from nouns, with the sense ‘having the nature or qualities of’, ‘somewhat resembling’, ‘given to’, ‘of the nature of’).
