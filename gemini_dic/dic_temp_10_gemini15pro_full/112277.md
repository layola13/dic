lopped:/lɑːpt/ | v. |砍掉，修剪，砍伐 |cut off (a part of something, typically a tree or plant) | The gardener lopped the branches off the tree. 园丁砍掉了树上的枝条。|
lopped:/lɑːpt/ | adj. |被砍掉的，修剪过的 | having been cut off | The lopped branches lay scattered on the ground. 被砍掉的树枝散落在地上。|
lopped:/lɑːpt/ | v. |垂下，耷拉着 | hang loosely; droop | The dog's ears lopped dejectedly. 狗的耳朵沮丧地垂了下来。|
lopped:/lɑːpt/ | adj. |下垂的，耷拉着的 | hanging loosely; drooping | The rabbit had long, lopped ears. 兔子有长长的、耷拉着的耳朵。|


Etymology: From Middle English *loppen, from Old English *loppe (“spider”), of uncertain origin.  The verb sense of "to cut off" likely developed from the image of a spider's legs being cut off.


Synonyms: trim, prune, clip, chop, sever, lop off (verb); drooping, hanging, pendulous (adjective)
Antonyms: grow, extend, lengthen (verb); erect, upright (adjective)
Word type: Verb, Adjective
