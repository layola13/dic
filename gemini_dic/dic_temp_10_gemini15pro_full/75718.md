full-on:/ˌfʊl ˈɒn/ (UK) /ˌfʊl ˈɑːn/ (US)| adv.|全力以赴地，彻底地|Completely, absolutely, with maximum effort or intensity.|Example: They went full-on into the competition. 他们全力以赴地参加了比赛。|Example: The band performed full-on for two hours. 乐队全力以赴地表演了两个小时。|近义词：completely, totally, absolutely, intensely|反义词：mildly, moderately, restrainedly|词性:副词


词源:  "Full-on" originated in the late 20th century, possibly from the idea of something being "fully on," as in operating at maximum capacity or intensity.  There's no clear single source, but its usage spread through colloquial English.
