noun

matters, business, concerns

English Explanation:  "Affaire's" is not standard English. The correct form is "affairs," which is the plural of "affair."  It refers to matters or concerns, often related to business, personal life, or public matters. It can also refer to a romantic or sexual relationship, especially one that is secret or considered scandalous.


Example:
The CEO is busy with company affairs.
首席执行官忙于公司事务。

He's been having an affair with his secretary.
他一直和他的秘书有染。


Synonym:
business, matter, concern, issue, event, circumstance, relationship (when referring to a romantic or sexual relationship)

Antonym:
(No direct antonym for the general sense of "affairs."  Antonyms would depend on the specific context. For example, if "affairs" refers to problems, an antonym might be "solutions.")
