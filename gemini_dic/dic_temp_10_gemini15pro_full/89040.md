holoblast:/ˈhɒləˌblæst/ /ˈhoʊləˌblæst/ | n. | 全裂卵，全裂胚 | An egg or embryo in which the entire cytoplasm divides into blastomeres during cleavage.  The entire zygote is cleaved into smaller cells, as opposed to meroblastic cleavage. |  The holoblast of the sea urchin is a classic example of radial cleavage. 海胆的全裂胚是辐射卵裂的典型例子。 |  The holoblast undergoes rapid cell division in the early stages of development.  全裂胚在发育早期经历快速细胞分裂。 |  blastomere, complete cleavage | meroblastic cleavage | noun
