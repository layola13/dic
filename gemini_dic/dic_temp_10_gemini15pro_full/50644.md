derisiveness:/dɪˈraɪsɪvnəs/| n. |嘲笑, 嘲弄, 奚落|The state of being derisive; mockery or ridicule.|His derisiveness towards her ideas made her feel small. 他对她的想法的嘲笑让她感到自己很渺小。|近义词：mockery, ridicule, scorn, contempt|反义词:respect, admiration, praise|noun

derisive:/dɪˈraɪsɪv/| adj. |嘲笑的, 嘲弄的, 奚落的|Expressing or characterized by derision; mocking.|He gave a derisive laugh. 他发出一声嘲笑。|近义词：mocking, scornful, contemptuous|反义词:respectful, admiring, complimentary|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dērīsīvus, from Latin dērīdeō (“I mock, ridicule”), from dē- + rīdeō (“I laugh, smile”).
