charm:/tʃɑːrm/ (US) /tʃɑːm/ (UK)| n. v.| 魅力，吸引力；护身符；咒语|  A quality that attracts or fascinates; an object believed to bring good luck; a magic spell.| She has a natural charm that makes her popular. 她有一种天生的魅力，使她很受欢迎。 | synonyms: appeal, charisma, allure, magic, amulet | antonyms: repulsion, distaste, ugliness | noun

charm:/tʃɑːrm/ (US) /tʃɑːm/ (UK)| v.|  吸引，迷住；施魔法于| To attract or fascinate; to influence by magic. | He charmed the audience with his witty stories. 他用诙谐的故事迷住了观众。| synonyms: attract, enchant, bewitch, captivate | antonyms: repel, disgust, disenchant | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "charme" meaning "song, incantation, charm," from Latin "carmen" meaning "song, verse, poem, oracle, prophecy."
