pamperize:/ˈpæmpəˌraɪz/| v. |纵容；娇惯；娇养|To treat with excessive indulgence; coddle.|She pamperizes her dog with gourmet treats and a luxurious bed. 她用美味的食物和豪华的床娇惯她的狗。|近义词：spoil, indulge, coddle, baby|反义词：discipline, neglect|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From pamper + -ize.  "Pamper" comes from Middle English *pampren*, perhaps of imitative origin, related to Dutch *pamperen* "to cram, indulge," German *pämpern* "to cram, stuff."  The suffix "-ize" forms verbs meaning "to make or cause to be," from Greek *-izein*.
