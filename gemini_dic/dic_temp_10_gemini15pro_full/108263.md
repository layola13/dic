launder:/ˈlɔːndər/ (US) /ˈlɔːndə(r)/ (UK)| v. n.|洗涤；清洗；洗黑钱|to wash and iron clothes, sheets, etc. / to move money that has been obtained illegally through banks and other businesses, so that it seems to have been obtained legally|例句：He launders his shirts every week. 他每周洗他的衬衫。

例句：They were accused of laundering drug money. 他们被指控洗黑钱。|近义词：wash, clean, launder, sanitize|反义词:soil, dirty|词性:动词, 名词

launder:/ˈlɔːndər/ (US) /ˈlɔːndə(r)/ (UK)| n.|洗衣店; 洗衣设备|a place where clothes, sheets, etc. are washed and ironed, or the machines used for this purpose|例句：The hotel has a laundry service. 这家酒店提供洗衣服务。

例句：We took our dirty clothes to the launderette. 我们把脏衣服送到自助洗衣店。|近义词：laundromat, laundry room|反义词: None|词性:名词
