bugbane:/ˈbʌɡbeɪn/| n. |升麻，类叶升麻; |A tall North American plant of the buttercup family, whose roots are traditionally used as an insecticide.  A perennial herb (Actaea racemosa or Cimicifuga racemosa, family Ranunculaceae, syn. Cimicifuga racemosa) of the buttercup family, native to eastern North America. It has large, much-divided leaves and long racemes of small, white flowers. The rootstock is poisonous but yields a medicinal extract.|The bugbane grew tall in the shaded garden. 升麻在阴凉的花园里长得高大。|The settlers used bugbane to repel insects.  殖民者用升麻来驱除昆虫。|Synonyms: black cohosh, black snakeroot, rattleweed|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a plant, could consider words like attractant)|Noun
