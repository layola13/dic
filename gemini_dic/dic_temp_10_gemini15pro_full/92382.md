hypocrisies:/hɪˈpɑːkrəsiːz/
|n.|虚伪，伪善|The act or practice of pretending to hold beliefs, feelings, standards, qualities, opinions, behaviours, virtues, motivations, or other characteristics that one does not actually hold, especially the pretense of virtue or piety.|The politician's hypocrisies were well known. 这位政客的虚伪行为是众所周知的。|近义词：dishonesty, insincerity, falseness, duplicity|反义词：honesty, sincerity, genuineness|词性:名词 (plural of hypocrisy)

hypocrisy:/hɪˈpɑːkrəsi/
|n.|虚伪，伪善|The practice of claiming to have moral standards or beliefs to which one's own behavior does not conform; pretense.|He condemned the hypocrisy of those who preached against gambling but played the lottery themselves. 他谴责了那些自己买彩票却宣扬反对赌博的人的虚伪。|近义词：dishonesty, insincerity, falseness, duplicity|反义词：honesty, sincerity, genuineness|词性:名词

词源: From Middle English *ipocrisie*, from Old French *ypocrisie*, from Late Latin *hypocrisis*, from Ancient Greek *ὑπόκρισις* (*hypókrisis*, “acting of a theatrical part, pretense, dissimulation”), from *ὑποκρίνομαι* (*hypokrínomai*, “I answer, I play a part, I pretend”), from *ὑπό* (*hypó*, “under”) + *κρίνομαι* (*krínomai*, “I decide, I judge”).
