exaggerative:/ɪɡˈzædʒərətɪv/ /ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪtɪv/| adj.|夸张的，夸大的|Representing something in an excessive or overstated manner; enlarging beyond bounds or the truth.|He has an exaggerative way of telling stories, making every minor incident sound like a grand adventure. 他讲故事的方式很夸张，把每个小事件都说得像一次大冒险。|近义词：hyperbolic, overstated, inflated|反义词：understated, moderate, realistic|词性:形容词

exaggeration:/ɪɡˌzædʒəˈreɪʃn/| n.|夸张，夸大|The act of exaggerating; a statement that represents something as better or worse than it really is.|Her claim that she could speak five languages fluently was a gross exaggeration. 她声称自己能流利地说五种语言，这完全是夸大其词。|近义词：hyperbole, overstatement|反义词：understatement|词性:名词

exaggerate:/ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪt/| v.|夸大，夸张|To represent something as larger, better, or worse than it really is.|Don't exaggerate the importance of this event; it's not as significant as you think. 不要夸大这件事的重要性；它不像你想的那么重要。|近义词：overstate, amplify, inflate|反义词：understate, downplay, minimize|词性:动词
