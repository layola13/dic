frenzying:/ˈfrɛnziɪŋ/ | adj. | 使狂乱的，使疯狂的 | Driving someone into a state of frenzy; causing wild excitement or agitation. | The frenzying music made the crowd go wild.  使人狂乱的音乐让观众疯狂起来。|  近义词:  maddening, frantic,  infuriating | 反义词: calming, soothing, tranquilizing | 词性: 形容词

frenzy:/ˈfrɛnzi/ | n. | 狂乱，疯狂，狂暴 | A state of wild excitement or agitation. | He was in a frenzy of activity. 他处于疯狂的活动状态中。| 近义词: madness, rage, fury | 反义词: calmness, tranquility, peace | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *frenesie*, from Late Latin *phrenesis*, from Greek *phrenîtis* "inflammation of the brain," from *phrḗn* "mind, diaphragm". The shift in sense from "delirium" to "wild excitement" occurred in French.
