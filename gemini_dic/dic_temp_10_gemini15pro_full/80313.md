goldbricked:/ˈɡoʊldˌbrɪkt/| v. |逃避工作；偷懒；磨洋工|To evade work or duty, especially by pretending to be busy.  Origin: From the image of a worthless brick painted gold to appear valuable, thus deceiving someone.|He goldbricked his way through the entire project, contributing very little. 他在整个项目中都在偷懒，几乎没有做出任何贡献。|Synonyms: shirk, loaf, malinger, dodge, slack off|Antonyms: work hard, be diligent, be productive|verb

goldbricking:/ˈɡoʊldˌbrɪkɪŋ/| n. |逃避工作；偷懒；磨洋工|The act of evading work or duty, especially by pretending to be busy.|His goldbricking became obvious when the project deadlines were missed. 当项目截止日期被错过时，他的偷懒行为变得显而易见。|Synonyms: shirking, loafing, malingering, dodging, slacking|Antonyms: hard work, diligence, productivity|noun
