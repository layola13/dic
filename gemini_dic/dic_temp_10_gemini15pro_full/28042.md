caparisoned:/kəˈpærɪsən/| adj.|披着华丽马衣的，盛装打扮的|dressed in rich decorative coverings, typically used in reference to horses but can be extended to other things or people.|The knight's warhorse, caparisoned in blue and gold, pranced proudly.  骑士的战马披着蓝金相间的华丽马衣，骄傲地腾跃着。|The actress was caparisoned in jewels for the premiere.  这位女演员在首映式上佩戴着珠宝，盛装出席。|近义词：adorned, ornamented, decorated, bedecked|反义词：plain, unadorned, simple|词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *caparasson* (Modern French *caparaçon*), from Spanish *caparazón* "saddle cover," ultimately from Late Latin *cappa* "cape, hooded cloak." The connection is likely due to the resemblance of a horse's caparison to a cape.
