atropous:/ˈætrəpəs/| adj.|萎缩的，退化的|Characterized by atrophy; wasted; degenerated. |The doctor noted the patient's atropous muscles. 医生注意到病人肌肉萎缩。|例句：The long period of disuse left his arm muscles atropous. 长期不用使他的手臂肌肉萎缩了。|近义词：wasted, shrunken, withered, degenerate|反义词：developed, healthy, robust|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Late Latin *atropus, from Greek ἄτροπος (átropos, “inflexible, unyielding, inexorable”), from ἀ- (a-, “not”) + τρέπω (trépo, “to turn”). Originally used in medical contexts to describe organs or tissues that had diminished in size or function due to disease or lack of use.
