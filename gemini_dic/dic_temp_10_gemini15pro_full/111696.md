lockjawed:/ˈlɒkdʒɔːd/ (British) /ˈlɑːkdʒɔːd/ (American)| adj.| 牙关紧闭的；沉默寡言的；紧张的|Unable to speak or move the jaw because of stiffness in the jaw muscles, often due to tetanus infection. Also used figuratively to describe someone unable to speak because of nervousness, shock, etc.| The lockjawed victim was rushed to the hospital. 牙关紧闭的受害者被紧急送往医院。|  He stood lockjawed, unable to utter a word in his defense. 他牙关紧闭地站着，无法说出一个字来为自己辩护。|近义词：silent, speechless, tight-lipped, inarticulate|反义词：loquacious, talkative, garrulous|词性:形容词

lockjaw:/ˈlɒkdʒɔː/ (British) /ˈlɑːkdʒɔː/ (American)| n.| 牙关紧闭；破伤风|A disease, also called tetanus, that causes painful muscle spasms, particularly of the jaw and neck.| Lockjaw is a serious disease that can be prevented with vaccination. 破伤风是一种严重的疾病，可以通过接种疫苗来预防。| Lockjaw can make it difficult to swallow or breathe. 破伤风会使吞咽或呼吸困难。|近义词：tetanus|反义词: 无|词性:名词

PHRASE->
There are not many common phrases using "lockjawed" in everyday English. It's primarily a medical term.  "lockjaw" is more commonly used when discussing the disease tetanus.


词源:  "Lockjaw" is a compound word formed from "lock" (referring to the fixed or closed position of the jaw) and "jaw."  The term vividly describes the primary symptom of tetanus.
