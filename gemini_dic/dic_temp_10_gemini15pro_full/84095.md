half-insinuated:/ˌhæf ɪnˈsɪnjuːeɪtɪd/
|adj.|半暗示的，含沙射影的|Implied or suggested indirectly, only partially expressed.|Her half-insinuated criticism stung more than outright accusations. 她半遮半掩的批评比直接的指责更伤人。|例句：His half-insinuated threat left her feeling uneasy. 他半暗示的威胁让她感到不安。|近义词：implied, suggested, intimated, hinted|反义词：explicit, stated, overt|词性:形容词

half:/hæf/
|n. adj. adv.|一半，半个|One of two equal parts of something.|He ate half the pizza. 他吃了一半的披萨。|例句：Half of the students failed the exam. 一半的学生考试不及格。|近义词： moiety, part, portion|反义词：whole, total, entire|词性:名词, 形容词, 副词

insinuate:/ɪnˈsɪnjuːeɪt/
|vt.|暗示，旁敲侧击地说; 巧妙地潜入; 使迂回地潜入|To suggest or hint something bad or unpleasant in an indirect way; to introduce (something, such as an idea) gradually or in a subtle, indirect, or covert way.|He insinuated that she was lying. 他暗示她在撒谎。|例句：She insinuated herself into his life. 她巧妙地融入了他的生活。|近义词：imply, suggest, intimate, hint|反义词：declare, state, assert|词性:动词
