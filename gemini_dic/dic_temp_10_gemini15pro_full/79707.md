glucogen:/ˈɡluːkədʒən/ | n. |胰高血糖素|A hormone formed in the pancreas that promotes the breakdown of glycogen to glucose in the liver. | Example 1: Glucagon helps regulate blood sugar levels. 胰高血糖素有助于调节血糖水平。

Example 2:  The doctor explained the role of glucogen in the body's metabolism. 医生解释了胰高血糖素在人体新陈代谢中的作用。|Synonym:  hyperglycemic factor, hyperglycaemic factor | Antonym: insulin | noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From gluco- (glucose) + -gen (generating).  Ultimately from Greek *gleukos* (sweet) + -gen (generating).
