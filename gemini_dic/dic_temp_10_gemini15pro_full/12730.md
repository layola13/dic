asset-stripping:/ˈæsɛt ˌstrɪpɪŋ/
n.
资产剥离
The practice of buying a company and selling off its assets for profit, often with little regard for the company's long-term viability or the jobs of its employees.
The corporate raider engaged in asset-stripping, leaving the company a mere shell of its former self.
近义词：liquidation, divestiture
反义词：investment, development
词性: 名词


asset-stripping:/ˈæsɛt ˌstrɪpɪŋ/
v.
剥离资产
To strip a company of its assets.
The new owners asset-stripped the company, selling off its valuable real estate and leaving behind a mountain of debt.
近义词：liquidate, divest
反义词：invest, develop
词性: 动词 (作为动名词使用，本身并非动词)

词源:  "asset" (meaning possessions, property) + "stripping" (present participle of "strip," meaning to remove covering or parts). The term gained prominence in the 1980s during a wave of corporate takeovers.
