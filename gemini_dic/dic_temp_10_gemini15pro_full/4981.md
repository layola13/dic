alkalizer:/ˈælkəˌlaɪzər/ | n. | 碱化剂; 抗酸剂 | A substance that reduces acidity, especially in the body. |  She took an alkalizer to relieve her heartburn. 她服用抗酸剂来缓解胃灼热。| Synonyms: antacid, base, neutralizer | Antonyms: acid, acidifier | noun

alkalize:/ˈælkəˌlaɪz/ | v. | 使碱化; 中和酸性 | To make something alkaline or less acidic. |  Adding baking soda to the soil will alkalize it. 在土壤中加入小苏打可以使其碱化。| Synonyms: neutralize, deacidify | Antonyms: acidify, acidulate | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From alkali + -ize/-izer.  Alkali ultimately comes from Arabic *al-qalī* "ashes of saltwort," a plant used to produce soda ash.  The suffix *-ize* forms verbs meaning "to make or become," and *-izer* forms nouns referring to agents that cause such changes.
