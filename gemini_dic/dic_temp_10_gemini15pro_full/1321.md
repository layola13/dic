accredits: /əˈkrɛdɪts/
verb
认可，授权，委派
officially recognizes someone or something as having a particular status or being qualified to perform a particular activity.
The university accredits several online programs.  这所大学认可几个在线课程。

This agency accredits hospitals. 这个机构授权医院。

近义词: authorize, certify, license, recognize, endorse
反义词: disqualify, reject, revoke

词性: verb (动词)
词源: From Late Latin accredere, from ad- + credere "to believe."


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The organization accredits schools that meet its standards. =  该组织认可符合其标准的学校。
She accredits her success to hard work and dedication. = 她将她的成功归功于努力工作和奉献精神。

一般过去时:
The agency accredited the program last year. = 该机构去年认可了这个项目。
They accredited him with inventing the device. = 他们认为是他发明了这个装置。


现在完成时:
The university has accredited several new programs this year. = 这所大学今年已经认可了几个新项目。
The board has accredited her with extensive experience in the field. = 董事会认可她在该领域的丰富经验。


过去完成时:
The committee had accredited the institution before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，委员会已经认可了该机构。
They had accredited him with exceptional skills before he joined the team. = 在他加入团队之前，他们认为他拥有卓越的技能。

将来时:
The association will accredit the new hospital next month. = 该协会将于下个月认可这家新医院。
They will accredit him with the discovery once the research is complete. = 一旦研究完成，他们就会把这一发现归功于他。


被动语态:
The school is accredited by a national organization. = 这所学校是由一个国家组织认可的。
The doctor was accredited with saving the patient's life. =  这位医生被认为挽救了病人的生命。

否定句:
The agency does not accredit programs that fail to meet its requirements. = 该机构不认可不符合其要求的课程。
They did not accredit him with the discovery. = 他们没有把这项发现归功于他。



PHRASE->
