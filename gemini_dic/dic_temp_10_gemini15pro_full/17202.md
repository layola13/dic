baumol:/ˈbaʊmɒl/| n.|鲍莫尔|Referring to William Baumol, an American economist known for his work on entrepreneurship, innovation, and the performing arts.  Specifically, it often refers to "Baumol's cost disease," which describes the rising costs in labor-intensive service sectors like the performing arts, even when productivity gains are minimal.  It can also refer to his theories on contestable markets.|Baumol's cost disease explains why ticket prices for live theater keep rising. 鲍莫尔成本病解释了为什么现场戏剧的票价持续上涨。

Baumol's work on entrepreneurship has influenced modern business practices. 鲍莫尔关于创业的研究影响了现代商业实践。|近义词: None (as it refers to a specific person and concept)|反义词: None|词性: Noun (proper)
