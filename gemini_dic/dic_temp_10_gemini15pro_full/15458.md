badder:/ˈbædər/ | adj. | 更坏的，更差的 | Comparative form of "bad," although considered nonstandard. It implies a greater degree of badness or unpleasantness.  While grammatically correct in some dialects, "worse" is generally preferred in formal English. |  He's a badder man than I thought. (他比我想象的更坏。) | worse, meaner, more unpleasant | better, nicer, kinder | adjective


worse:/wɜːrs/ (US) /wɜːs/ (UK) | adj. | 更坏的，更糟糕的；更严重的；更恶劣的 | The comparative form of "bad". Used to describe something of lower quality, more unpleasant, or more severe. | The weather is getting worse. (天气越来越糟了。)  His condition is worse than yesterday. (他的情况比昨天更糟了。) | poorer, inferior, more severe | better, superior, improved | adjective
