heterodoxy:/ˈhɛtərəˌdɒksi/ (US) /ˈhɛtərəˌdɒksi/ (UK)| n. |异端，异端邪说|Any opinions or doctrines at variance with an official or orthodox set of beliefs.| Galileo was condemned for his heterodoxy by the Catholic Church.  伽利略因其异端邪说而受到天主教会的谴责。

The heterodoxy of his views made him unpopular. 他非正统的观点使他不受欢迎。|近义词：heresy, unorthodoxy, dissent, dissidence|反义词：orthodoxy, conformity, conventionality|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *heterodoxia*, from Ancient Greek ἑτεροδοξία (heterodoxía, “difference of opinion”), from ἕτερος (héteros, “other, different”) + δόξα (dóxa, “opinion, glory”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰes- (“to grasp”).
