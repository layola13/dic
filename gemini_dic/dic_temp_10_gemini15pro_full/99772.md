intermediating:/ˌɪntərˈmiːdieɪtɪŋ/
|v.|调解；斡旋；作为中间人|acting as a mediator or agent between parties|The bank is intermediating the loan between the two companies.  这家银行正在为两家公司之间的贷款进行斡旋。|
| |The government is intermediating in the dispute between the union and the company. 政府正在调解工会和公司之间的纠纷。|
| | 近义词: mediating, negotiating, arbitrating|
| | 反义词: separating, dividing|
| |词性: verb (present participle)|


intermediate:/ˌɪntərˈmiːdiət/
|adj.|中间的；中级的|being or occurring between two extremes or stages|He's taking an intermediate level English course. 他正在学习中级英语课程。|
| |The intermediate stage of the project is now complete.  项目的中间阶段现已完成。|
| | 近义词: middle, in-between, median|
| | 反义词: extreme, beginning, end|
| |词性: adjective|


intermediary:/ˌɪntərˈmiːdiəri/
|n.|中间人；调解人|a person who acts as a link between people or things|The lawyer acted as an intermediary between the buyer and the seller.  律师充当了买卖双方的中间人。|
| |The United Nations is often asked to act as an intermediary in international disputes. 联合国经常被要求在国际争端中充当调解人。|
| | 近义词: mediator, agent, go-between|
| | 反义词: principal, direct party|
| |词性: noun|



词源: From Latin intermedius "intermediate," from inter "between" + medius "middle".
