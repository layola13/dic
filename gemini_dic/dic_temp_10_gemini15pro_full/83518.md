hadder:/ˈhædər/ |n.|  heather; ling | A low-growing evergreen shrub with small purple, pink, or white flowers, found on moorland and heathland. |  The hills were covered in a carpet of purple hadder.  山丘上覆盖着一层紫色的石南花。| synonyms: heather, ling | antonyms: None | noun

Etymology->
From Middle English *haddir, *hathir, from Old English hæðre (“heath, heather”), from Proto-Germanic *haþiz (“heath, heather”). Cognate with Scots hadder (“heather”), West Frisian heide (“heath”), Dutch heide (“heath, heather”), German Heide (“heath, heather”), Danish hede (“heath”), Swedish hed (“heath”), Icelandic heiði (“heath, heather”).
