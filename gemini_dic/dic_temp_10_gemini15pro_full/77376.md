gaunter:/ˈɡɔːntər/| adj.|更瘦的，更憔悴的|More lean and haggard, thin and bony, often due to suffering, hunger, or age.|He looked gaunter than the last time I saw him. 他看起来比我上次见到他时更憔悴了。|Synonyms: thinner, leaner, haggard, emaciated|Antonyms: fatter, plumper, healthier|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *ganter, gauntre, probably from Old North French *galtre (“emaciated, scabby”), from Old Norse *galtr (“boar”).  Related to dialectal English galt (“boar”). The original sense likely referred to the leanness of a boar, especially after the rutting season.
