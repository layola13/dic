misgive:/mɪsˈɡɪv/| v. |使担忧；使怀疑；使害怕|To cause to feel doubt or suspicion; to fill with foreboding.|I had a sudden misgiving about the whole venture. 我对整个冒险事业突然感到不安。|近义词：worry, doubt, suspect, apprehend|反义词：trust, believe, reassure|词性：动词

词源:
From Old English *misgiefan (unattested), from mis- + giefan (“to give”).  The original sense was "to give wrongly or amiss," later "to bestow wrongly or sparingly," hence "to grudge" (obsolete).  The current meaning developed from "to feel misgivings."
