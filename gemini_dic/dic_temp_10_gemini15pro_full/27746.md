cankeredly:/ˈkæŋkərdli/
adverb
恶毒地，充满仇恨地
in a cankered manner; spitefully and maliciously.
His words, spoken cankeredly, dripped with venom. 他恶毒地说出的话充满了恶意。
She glared cankeredly at her rival. 她充满敌意地瞪着她的对手。
Synonyms: spitefully, maliciously, venomously, bitterly, resentfully
Antonyms: kindly, benevolently, charitably, generously, affably
Word type: adverb

Etymology:
From "cankered" + "-ly."  "Cankered" comes from "canker," from Old North French "cancre," related to Dutch "kanker" and Old English "cancer," ultimately from Latin "cancer" (crab, ulcer).  The metaphorical sense of a corrupting influence developed from the image of a spreading sore.
