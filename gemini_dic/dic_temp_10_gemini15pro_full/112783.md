lubrications:/ˌluːbrɪˈkeɪʃənz/
|n.|润滑，润滑剂|The engine requires regular lubrications. 发动机需要定期润滑。|
|例句：Regular lubrications are essential for the smooth operation of machinery.  定期润滑对于机器的平稳运行至关重要。|
|例句：The mechanic applied several lubrications to the moving parts. 机械师对活动部件涂抹了几种润滑剂。|
|近义词：oiling, greasing, lubricant|
|反义词：friction, abrasion|
|词性：名词|


lubricate:/ˈluːbrɪkeɪt/
|v.|润滑，使润滑|We need to lubricate the gears. 我们需要润滑齿轮。|
|例句：You should lubricate the door hinges to stop them squeaking. 你应该给门铰链上油，以免它们发出吱吱声。|
|例句：The cream is used to lubricate the skin.  这种乳霜用来润滑皮肤。|
|近义词：oil, grease, anoint|
|反义词：dry, desiccate|
|词性：动词|


lubricant:/ˈluːbrɪkənt/
|n.|润滑剂|This lubricant is suitable for high-speed machinery. 这种润滑剂适用于高速机械。|
|例句：Oil is a common lubricant used in car engines.  油是汽车发动机中常用的润滑剂。|
|例句：Apply a lubricant to the bearings to reduce friction. 在轴承上涂抹润滑剂以减少摩擦。|
|近义词：oil, grease, lubrication|
|反义词：desiccant, abrasive|
|词性：名词|


词源: From Late Latin *lubricationem, from Latin lubricatus, past participle of lubricare "to make slippery," from lubricus "slippery."
