cavillation:/ˌkævɪˈteɪʃən/
n.
空化；空蚀；气蚀
The formation of vapour cavities in a liquid, small pockets of gas, especially as caused by the local reduction of pressure in a fast-moving liquid, as around a propeller.
The pump suffered damage from cavitation.  这个泵受到了气蚀的损害。

cavitation:/ˌkævɪˈteɪʃən/
n.
空穴作用；空穴现象
The action of forming cavities in an organ or tissue.
Cavitation can occur in the brain after a stroke. 中风后脑部可能会发生空穴现象。


近义词:erosion, pitting, corrosion
反义词:solidification


词源: From Latin cavitat- ‘hollowed out’, from the verb cavare.
