metasounds:/ˈmɛtəˌsaʊndz/ | n. |元音|Sounds that are perceived as belonging to a particular language or dialect, often subconsciously influencing how we hear and interpret speech.  These sounds are not individual phonemes, but rather patterns and characteristics of sound that contribute to the overall "sound" of a language.|  The metasounds of Mandarin Chinese are very different from those of English.  普通话的元音与英语的元音非常不同。 | synonyms: prosody, intonation, acoustic characteristics | antonyms: N/A | noun
