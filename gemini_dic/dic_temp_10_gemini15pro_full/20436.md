bittacle:/ˈbɪnəkl/ (US), /ˈbɪtəkl/ (UK)| n.|罗经柜;罗经座|A housing for a ship's compass, typically located near the helm.|The captain checked the bittacle to ensure the ship was on course. 船长查看了一下罗经柜，以确保船舶航行方向正确。|Synonyms: binnacle|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Portuguese *bitacola*, from Late Latin *habitaculum* ("dwelling place"), from Latin *habitare* ("to dwell").  The term originally referred to a small housing or shelter on deck, later specifically for the compass.
