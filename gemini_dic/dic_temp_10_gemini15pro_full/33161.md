chromatophore: /ˈkrəʊmətəfɔːr/ (UK) /ˈkroʊmətəfɔːr/ (US)
chromatophore: n.
chromatophore: 色素体；载色体
chromatophore: A pigment-containing cell or group of cells, especially one that can change color, found in amphibians, fish, reptiles, crustaceans, and cephalopods.
