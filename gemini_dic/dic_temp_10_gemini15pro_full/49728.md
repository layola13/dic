democratized: /dɪˈmɒkrətaɪzd/ | v. | 使民主化；使大众化 | To make something accessible to everyone, removing restrictions or limitations previously in place. |  例句：The internet has democratized access to information. 互联网使获取信息变得大众化。| 近义词：popularized, broadened, expanded, opened up | 反义词：restricted, limited, controlled, monopolized | 词性：动词

democratizing: /dɪˈmɒkrətaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使民主化；使大众化(现在分词) | The act of making something accessible to everyone, removing restrictions or limitations. | 例句：The democratizing effect of social media is undeniable. 社交媒体的民主化效应不可否认。 | 近义词：popularizing, broadening, expanding, opening up | 反义词：restricting, limiting, controlling, monopolizing | 词性：动词

democratic: /ˌdɛməˈkrætɪk/ | adj. | 民主的；民主化的 | Relating to or supporting democracy; characterized by equal rights and participation for all. | 例句：A democratic government is elected by the people. 民主政府是由人民选举产生的。 | 近义词： egalitarian, representative, popular | 反义词：autocratic, totalitarian, dictatorial | 词性：形容词

democratization: /dɪˌmɒkrətɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 民主化；大众化 | The process of making something accessible to everyone. | 例句：The democratization of education is a key goal in many countries. 教育民主化是许多国家的主要目标。 | 近义词： popularization, expansion, liberalization | 反义词： restriction, limitation, control | 词性：名词
