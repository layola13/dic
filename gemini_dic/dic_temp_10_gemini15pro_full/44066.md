crinkle:/ˈkrɪŋkəl/| n. v.|皱纹，褶皱；使起皱纹，使起褶皱|A small fold or wrinkle; to form small folds or wrinkles.|The crinkle of the fabric added a nice texture to the dress.  这种织物的褶皱给裙子增添了很好的质感。|She crinkled her nose in disgust. 她厌恶地皱起了鼻子。|wrinkle, crease, crumple|smooth, flatten|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *crinklen, frequentative of *crincen, from Old English *crincan (“to bend, curve, twist”), from Proto-Germanic *krenkaną (“to bend, turn, wind”), from Proto-Indo-European *grengʰ- (“to bend, turn”). Cognate with Dutch krenken (“to offend, violate, hurt”), German kränken (“to offend, mortify”), Danish krænke (“to violate, offend”).
