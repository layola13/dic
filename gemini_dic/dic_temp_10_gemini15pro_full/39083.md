confounder:/kənˈfaʊndər/ | n. | 混杂因素，干扰因素 |  A confounder is a variable that influences both the dependent variable and independent variable, causing a spurious association.  It makes it difficult to determine the true causal relationship between the variables of interest. |  Smoking is a confounder in the relationship between coffee drinking and heart disease.  吸烟是喝咖啡和心脏病之间关系的混杂因素。| synonym: extraneous variable, lurking variable, confounding factor | antonym:  N/A (no direct antonym, perhaps controlled variable) | noun

confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/ | v. | 使迷惑，使混淆，挫败 |  To confound means to cause surprise or confusion in (someone), especially by acting against their expectations.  It can also mean to prove (a theory, expectation, or prediction) wrong. | The sudden change in policy confounded investors.  政策的突然变化使投资者感到困惑。 | synonym: confuse, bewilder, baffle, frustrate, thwart | antonym: clarify, enlighten, explain | verb
