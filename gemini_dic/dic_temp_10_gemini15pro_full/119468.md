micrometre: /ˈmaɪkrəʊmiːtər/ (UK) /ˈmaɪkroʊˌmiːtər/ (US)| n. |微米; 测微计|A unit of length equal to one millionth of a metre.  A device used for making precise measurements of small distances.|例句：The diameter of the fibre was measured using a micrometre.  这根纤维的直径是用测微计测量的。

例句：Bacteria are typically a few micrometres in length.  细菌通常有几微米长。

|近义词：micron, micrometer (US spelling)|反义词: kilometer, meter (larger units)|词性:noun


micrometer: /maɪˈkrɒmɪtər/ (UK) /maɪˈkrɑːmɪtər/ (US)| n. |测微计|A device incorporating a calibrated screw, used for making precise measurements of small distances.|例句：He used a micrometer to measure the thickness of the sheet metal. 他用测微计测量了金属板的厚度。

例句：The machinist checked the tolerances with a micrometer.  机械师用测微计检查了公差。

|近义词：micrometer caliper, screw gauge|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider contrasting tools like rulers or calipers for larger measurements)|词性:noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *mikros* (small) + *metron* (measure).
