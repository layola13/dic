oakum: /ˈoʊkəm/ | n. | 填絮，麻絮 | Loose fibre obtained by untwisting and picking apart old ropes, used especially in caulking wooden ships. |  The ship's carpenter used oakum to caulk the seams of the deck.  船上的木匠用麻絮填塞甲板的缝隙。

The workers filled the gaps in the wall with oakum. 工人们用麻絮填补了墙上的缝隙。 | 近义词: caulking, filling | 反义词: None | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *okum*, from Old English *ācumba* (“tow”), from *ā-* (“out”) + *cemban* (“to comb”).  Cognate with Dutch *aakem* (“tow”).
