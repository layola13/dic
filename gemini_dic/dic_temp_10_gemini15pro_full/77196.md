gasses:/ˈɡæsɪz/| n.|气体；毒气|A substance or matter in a state in which it will expand freely to fill the whole of a container, having no fixed shape or volume.|The greenhouse gasses are causing global warming. 温室气体正在导致全球变暖。|近义词：vapors, fumes|反义词:solids, liquids|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From gas + -es (plural suffix).  The word "gas" itself has a complex etymology, possibly coined by the 17th-century Flemish chemist J.B. van Helmont, perhaps from the Greek word "chaos."
