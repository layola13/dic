pantiles:/ˈpænˌtaɪlz/| n.|西班牙瓦，筒状瓦 |Curved roofing tiles, often S-shaped in cross-section, giving a channelled appearance.|The roof of the old house was covered in pantiles. 那栋老房子的屋顶铺满了西班牙瓦。|Synonyms: roof tiles, S-tiles|Antonyms: None|Noun

pantile:/ˈpænˌtaɪl/| n.|西班牙瓦，筒状瓦 |A curved roofing tile, often S-shaped in cross-section, giving a channelled appearance.|Many traditional houses in this area have pantile roofs.  这个地区的许多传统房屋都有西班牙瓦屋顶。|Synonyms: roof tile, S-tile|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "pan" + "tile". "Pan" refers to the curved shape resembling a pan.  The word "tile" comes from Old English "tigel" from Latin "tegula", meaning covering.
