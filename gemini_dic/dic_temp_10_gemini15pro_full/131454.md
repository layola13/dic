noncognate:/nɑːnˈkɑɡneɪt/ | adj. n.| 非同源词 |A word in one language that is not derived from the same root as a similar-sounding word in another language, even though the two words may share some similarities in meaning or form. |  English "night" and French "nuit" are cognates, but English "much" and Spanish "mucho" (meaning "a lot") are noncognates, despite their similarity.| 近义词: unrelated, dissimilar | 反义词: cognate | 词性: adjective, noun
