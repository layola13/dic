drill: /drɪl/ (US) /drɪl/ (UK) | v. | 钻孔; 训练; 练习 | To make a hole in something using a drill. To teach someone something by making them repeat it or do it again and again. | They drilled the students in fire safety procedures. 他们对学生进行了消防安全程序的训练。| synonyms:  bore, pierce, practice, exercise, rehearse | antonyms: neglect, idle | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *drillen, frequentative of *dran, from Proto-Germanic *draną (“to turn, twist”).
