make bricks without straw:/meɪk brɪks wɪˈðaʊt strɔː/ | idiom |  无米之炊；巧妇难为无米之炊 | To attempt to do something without the necessary resources or materials.  It refers to a difficult or impossible task.  This idiom originates from the biblical story of the Israelites' enslavement in Egypt, where they were forced to make bricks without being provided with straw. |  The manager asked us to increase sales by 20% this quarter, but with the current economic downturn, it's like making bricks without straw. 经理要求我们本季度销售额增长 20%，但在当前经济低迷的情况下，这无异于巧妇难为无米之炊。 |  Synonyms:  attempt the impossible,  fight a losing battle,  mission impossible | Antonyms: have all the tools,  have the necessary resources,  be well-equipped | idiom
