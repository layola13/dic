figurehead:/ˈfɪɡjərˌhɛd/ (US), /ˈfɪɡəhɛd/ (UK)| n. | 名义领袖；傀儡；船首像 |A person who appears to be the leader of an organization but who has no real power or authority.  A carved figure, usually of a woman or mythical creature, fixed to the prow of a ship.| The king was merely a figurehead; the real power lay with the military. 国王只是一个傀儡；真正的权力掌握在军队手中。| synonym: puppet, nominal leader, front, token, ornament | antonym: leader, authority, decision-maker | noun

PHRASE->
mere figurehead =  有名无实的领导  (a leader with no real power)
act as a figurehead = 担任傀儡 (to serve as a puppet leader)
serve as a figurehead =  担任名义领袖 (to function as a nominal leader)
a symbolic figurehead = 象征性的傀儡  (a puppet leader with symbolic importance)
install a figurehead =  安插傀儡 (to put a puppet leader in place)


词源：
From figure (n.) + head (n.). Originally nautical, referring to the carved figure on the prow of a ship.  The figurative sense of "nominal leader" developed from this in the mid-19th century.  来自figure（名词）+ head（名词）。最初是航海术语，指的是船首的雕刻人物。“名义领袖”的比喻意义是从19世纪中期发展而来的。
