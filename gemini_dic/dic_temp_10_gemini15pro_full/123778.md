moot:/muːt/
adjective
无实际意义的；不值得讨论的
Subject to debate, dispute, or uncertainty, and typically not admitting of a final decision.
The question of whether or not to wear a mask is now moot, since the mandate has been lifted.  戴口罩与否的问题现在已经没有实际意义了，因为强制令已经取消了。

moot:/muːt/
verb
提出…供讨论
Raise (a question or topic) for discussion; suggest (an idea or possibility).
She mooted the idea of a vacation in Europe. 她提出了去欧洲度假的想法。

moot:/muːt/
noun
(尤指在盎格鲁-撒克逊时代)乡民大会；假定的案件(尤指法律系学生为练习而辩论的)
(especially in Anglo-Saxon England) an assembly held for discussion, especially of judicial or legislative matters. / A hypothetical case argued by law students as an exercise.
The moot court was a valuable learning experience.  模拟法庭是一次宝贵的学习经验。



Etymology->
From Old English *mōt (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *mōtam (compare Old Frisian mōt “meeting, council, court,” Old Saxon mōt “meeting, court,” Old High German muoz “meeting, market, court,” German Muth “courage, mettle,” Gothic 𐌼𐍉𐍄𐍃 (mōts) “custom, toll, duty”), from Proto-Indo-European *mē- (1) “to measure, distribute, allot.”

近义词:debatable, arguable, disputable (adjective); propose, suggest, raise (verb); discussion, debate, forum (noun)
反义词:undisputable, incontestable, irrefutable (adjective)
词性:adjective, verb, noun
