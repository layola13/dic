non-Malthusian:/ˌnɒn mælˈθjuːziən/ | adj. | 非马尔萨斯主义的 | Relating to or characteristic of a theory that rejects Malthus's predictions, especially his prediction that population growth will outstrip food supply. |  Many economists today hold non-Malthusian views on population growth. 许多当今的经济学家对人口增长持有非马尔萨斯主义的观点。 |  The government implemented non-Malthusian policies to encourage larger families. 政府实施了非马尔萨斯主义政策来鼓励生育更多孩子。 | 近义词: neo-Malthusian (in some contexts, as both relate to population), cornucopian | 反义词: Malthusian | 词性: adjective

Etymology:
non- (prefix meaning "not") + Malthusian (referring to Thomas Robert Malthus and his theories)
