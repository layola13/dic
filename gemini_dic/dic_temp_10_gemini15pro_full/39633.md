consequentialist:/ˌkɒnsɪˈkwɛnʃəlɪst/| adj. n.|后果论的，后果论者|Relating to or characteristic of consequentialism; a person who believes in or practices consequentialism. Consequentialism is the doctrine that the morality of an action is to be judged solely by its consequences.|例句：As a consequentialist, she believes that the ends justify the means. 作为一名后果论者，她认为结果证明手段是正当的。

|例句：Consequentialist ethics are often contrasted with deontological ethics.  后果论伦理经常与道义论伦理进行对比。|近义词：teleological, result-oriented|反义词：deontological, principled|词性: adjective, noun

consequentialism:/kənˌsɛkwɛnʃ(ə)lɪzəm/| n.|后果论|The doctrine that the morality of an action is to be judged solely by its consequences.|例句：Consequentialism is a complex philosophical theory with many variations. 后果论是一个复杂的哲学理论，有很多变体。

|例句：Utilitarianism is a form of consequentialism. 功利主义是后果论的一种形式。|近义词：teleology|反义词：deontology|词性: noun
