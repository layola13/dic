marshal: /ˈmɑːrʃəl/ (US), /ˈmɑːʃəl/ (UK)| v. |  整理；排列；召集；引导 | To gather and arrange (things) in order; to assemble and organize (troops, etc.); to lead or guide ceremonially. |  She marshaled her arguments for the debate. 她整理了辩论的论点。 | Synonyms: arrange, organize, assemble, gather, muster | Antonyms: disorganize, scatter, disperse | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mareschal, from Frankish *marhskalk "horse servant," from *marha- "horse" + *skalk- "servant." The word's meaning evolved from "stable keeper" to "military commander" and then to various other official roles.
