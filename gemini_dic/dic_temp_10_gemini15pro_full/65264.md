eucalyptol:/ˌjuːkəˈlɪptɒl/ (UK) /ˌjuːkəˈlɪptɔːl/ (US)| n.|桉树脑，桉叶素|A colorless, oily liquid with a strong, camphoraceous odor, obtained from eucalyptus oil and used as an antiseptic and flavoring.|Eucalyptus oil, from which eucalyptol is derived, is commonly used in vapor rubs to relieve chest congestion.|Synonyms: cineole, 1,8-cineole|Antonyms: N/A|noun

eucalyptus:/ˌjuːkəˈlɪptəs/ (UK) /ˌjuːkəˈlɪptəs/ (US)| n.|桉树|A fast-growing evergreen Australasian tree that has aromatic leaves yielding an oil used in medicine and perfumes.|The koala's diet consists mainly of eucalyptus leaves.|Synonyms: gum tree|Antonyms: N/A|noun
