drudging:/ˈdrʌdʒɪŋ/| n. | 苦工；单调乏味的工作 |  Hard, monotonous, and tiring work. |  The drudging of data entry made her want to quit. 数据录入的苦工让她想辞职。 |  toil, labor, grind |  leisure, relaxation | noun

drudge:/drʌdʒ/| v. | 做苦工；从事单调乏味的工作 | To work hard at tedious or monotonous tasks. | He drudged away at his homework all weekend. 他整个周末都在辛苦地做作业。 | toil, slave, slog | relax, rest | verb
drudge:/drʌdʒ/| n. | 做苦工的人；苦力 |  A person who does tedious, menial, or unpleasant work. |  She felt like a drudge, constantly cleaning up after everyone. 她觉得自己像个苦力，不停地跟在大家后面打扫卫生。 |  laborer, toiler, workhorse |  idler, loafer | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *druggen*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *draga* (to draw, drag), suggesting the sense of heavy pulling or laborious work.  Another possibility is a connection to Old English *dreogan* (to endure, suffer).
