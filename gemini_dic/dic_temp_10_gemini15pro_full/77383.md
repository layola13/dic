gauntly:/ˈɡɔːntli/ | adv. | 憔悴地，消瘦地 |  In a thin and bony way, often due to suffering, hunger, or age.  |  He stared gauntly across the room, his eyes hollow and dark. 他眼神空洞而阴暗地凝视着房间的另一头。 |  thinly, haggardly, leanly, emaciatedly |  robustly, healthily, plumply | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From gaunt + -ly.  Gaunt itself comes from Middle English *gaunt*, from Old North French *gant* and ultimately of uncertain Germanic origin.
