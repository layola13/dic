cupel:/ˈkjuːpəl/ (US), /ˈkʌpəl/ (UK)| n.|灰皿，试金灰皿|A small, porous cup, typically made of bone ash, used in assaying to separate precious metals, such as gold and silver, from base metals by oxidation at high temperatures.|The goldsmith used a cupel to refine the silver.|Synonyms: assay cup, scorifier|Antonyms: N/A|noun

cupel:/ˈkjuːpəl/ (US), /ˈkʌpəl/ (UK)| v.|用灰皿精炼|To refine or assay (precious metals) in a cupel.|He cupeled the ore to determine its silver content.|Synonyms: assay, refine, purify|Antonyms: contaminate, pollute|verb
