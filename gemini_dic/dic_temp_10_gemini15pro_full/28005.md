caoutchoucin:/ˌkaʊˈtʃuːsɪn/ | n. |橡胶素；橡胶毒素| A crystalline glycoside obtained from the bark of several species of Malaysian Apocynaceae, notably Urceola elastica, and used as an arrow poison. It is related chemically to ouabain.|The indigenous people used caoutchoucin, extracted from the bark of certain trees, as a potent arrow poison. 土著居民使用从某些树皮中提取的橡胶毒素作为强效的箭毒。|Synonyms: urceolin, |Antonyms: N/A| noun
