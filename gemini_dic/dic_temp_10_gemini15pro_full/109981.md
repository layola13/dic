libertine:/ˈlɪbərtɪn/ | n. adj. | 放荡者，放纵的人；放荡的，放纵的 | a person, especially a man, who behaves without moral principles or is sexually immoral; unrestrained by convention or morality |  He was known as a libertine and a gambler. 他是一个出了名的放荡者和赌徒。 |  synonyms: debauchee, profligate, rake, reprobate | antonyms: ascetic, puritan, moralist | noun, adjective

libertine:/ˈlɪbərtɪn/ | adj. | 放荡的，放纵的 | unrestrained by convention or morality | She led a libertine lifestyle, indulging in every excess. 她过着放荡的生活，尽情享受各种过度行为。 | synonyms: dissolute, licentious, wanton | antonyms: chaste, virtuous, moral | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *libertin*, from Latin *lībērtīnus* "freedman," from *līber* "free." Originally referred to freed slaves in ancient Rome, later evolving to describe freethinkers who challenged religious dogma, and ultimately acquiring its modern sense of one who is morally or sexually unrestrained.
