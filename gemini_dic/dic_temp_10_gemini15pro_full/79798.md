glycerol:/ˈɡlɪsərɒl/ (UK) /ˈɡlɪsəˌrɔl/ (US)| n.|甘油；丙三醇|A sweet, syrupy liquid obtained from fats and oils during saponification, or by the fermentation of glucose. It is used in making explosives and antifreeze, and as a solvent, sweetener, and preservative.|Glycerol is commonly used in cosmetics.|Synonym: glycerine|Antonym: None|noun
