cracowes:/ˈkrækaʊz/ |n. | 厚底鞋，一种中世纪的尖头鞋 | A type of medieval pointed shoe, often with a very long toe, sometimes ridiculously long.  These were fashionable among the aristocracy. | The nobleman strutted about in his extravagant cracowes, the toes so long they had to be tied to his knees. 贵族穿着他那奢华的厚底鞋昂首阔步，鞋尖长到必须绑在膝盖上。|  Synonyms:  poulaines, pointed shoes | Antonyms: flats, slippers | noun
