apagoge:/əˈpæɡədʒi/ | n. | 反证法，归谬法 |A method of proof by contradiction, where the impossibility of the contradictory of a proposition is demonstrated in order to prove the proposition itself.  It is also known as reductio ad absurdum. |  Example: The lawyer used apagoge to demonstrate his client's innocence. 律师用反证法证明了他的当事人的清白。 | Example:  By apagoge, we can prove that the square root of 2 is irrational. 通过反证法，我们可以证明2的平方根是无理数。 | Synonym: reductio ad absurdum, proof by contradiction | Antonym: direct proof | noun

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek ἀπαγωγή (apagōgḗ, “a leading away, abduction”), from ἀπάγω (apágō, “I lead away, abduct”), from ἀπό (apó, “away from”) + ἄγω (ágō, “I lead”).
