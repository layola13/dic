erect:/ɪˈrekt/ (US), /ɪˈrɛkt/ (UK)| adj. v. |直立的，竖立的；建造，建立|Characterized by uprightness; to construct or build something.|The soldiers stood erect. 士兵们直立站着。 They erected a statue in his honor. 他们在那里竖立了一座雕像来纪念他。|近义词：upright, vertical, build, construct|反义词:prone, horizontal, demolish, dismantle|词性:形容词, 动词
erection:/ɪˈrɛkʃən/ (US), /ɪˈrɛkʃən/ (UK)| n. |直立，竖立；建造，建筑物；勃起|The act of erecting something or the state of being erect; a building or structure; physiological phenomenon of engorgement of erectile tissue.|The erection of the new building took several months. 新大楼的建造花了几个月的时间。 He got an erection. 他勃起了。|近义词：construction, building|反义词:demolition, destruction|词性:名词

词源: From Latin *ērigere* (to raise up), from *ē- (ex-)* (out) + *regere* (to guide, rule).
