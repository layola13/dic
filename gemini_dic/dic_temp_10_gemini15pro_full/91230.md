hydrocephaloid:/ˌhaɪdrəˈsɛfələɪd/ | adj. n. | 脑积水样的；假性脑积水 | Resembling hydrocephalus; a condition, especially in infants, resembling hydrocephalus, characterized by depression, dehydration, and other symptoms but without increased intracranial pressure. | The infant presented with symptoms suggestive of hydrocephaloid, including lethargy and a sunken fontanelle.  这个婴儿表现出类似假性脑积水的症状，包括嗜睡和囟门凹陷。| The doctor diagnosed the baby with hydrocephaloid. 医生诊断婴儿患有假性脑积水。 |  Near synonyms:  hydrocephalic-like, pseudohydrocephalus | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider) healthy, normal development | noun, adjective

Etymology:  hydro- (water) + cephal- (head) + -oid (resembling)  From Greek "hydro-" (water) + "kephalē" (head) + "-oid" (like).
