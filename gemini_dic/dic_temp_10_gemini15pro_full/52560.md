disfranchisement:/ˌdɪsfrænˈtʃaɪzmənt/ | n. |剥夺公民权; 剥夺权利|The act of depriving a person or group of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.|  The disfranchisement of black voters was a major setback for civil rights.  剥夺黑人选民的权利是民权运动的一大挫折。 |Synonyms: disenfranchisement, deprivation, forfeiture| Antonyms: enfranchisement, empowerment| noun


disfranchise:/ˌdɪsfrænˈtʃaɪz/ | vt. |剥夺公民权; 剥夺权利| To deprive a person or group of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.|  The new law effectively disfranchised many poor and minority voters.  这项新法律实际上剥夺了许多贫穷和少数族裔选民的权利。|Synonyms: disenfranchise, deprive, strip| Antonyms: enfranchise, empower| verb
