aquilon:/ˈækwɪlɒn/ (UK) /ˈækwɪlɑːn/ (US)| n.|北风；北风之神|A cold northerly wind, especially in literary contexts.  The north wind personified as a god (Roman mythology).|例句：The aquilon blew fiercely across the plains. 北风猛烈地吹过平原。

例句：The poet invoked the aquilon in his verses. 诗人在他的诗句中祈求北风之神。|近义词：north wind, boreal wind|反义词:south wind, austral wind|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *aquilō* (north wind), from *aquilus* (dark, dusky, blackish), possibly related to *aquila* (eagle). The connection may be due to the dark clouds often associated with northerly winds.
