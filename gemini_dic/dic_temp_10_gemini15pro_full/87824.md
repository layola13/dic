hickory:/ˈhɪkəri/ (US), /ˈhɪkəri/ (UK)| n.|山核桃; 山核桃木|A North American tree with hard wood and edible nuts.  A hard, tough, heavy wood of a hickory tree.|The squirrels were burying hickorys for the winter. 松鼠正在为冬天埋藏山核桃。|Synonyms: pecan, walnut (for the nut), ash, oak (for the wood)|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

hickories:/ˈhɪkəriz/ (US), /ˈhɪkəriz/ (UK)| n.|山核桃树; 山核桃木（复数）|Plural form of hickory, referring to multiple hickory trees or pieces of hickory wood.|There are several species of hickories native to North America.  北美有几种本地山核桃树。|Synonyms: pecan trees, walnut trees (for the nut-producing trees)|Antonyms: N/A|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Powhatan (Virginia Algonquian) word *pawcohiccora*, referring to a milk-like drink made from pounded hickory nuts.
