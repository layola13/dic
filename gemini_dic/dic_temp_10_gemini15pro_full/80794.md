goth:/ɡɒθ/ (UK) /ɡɑːθ/ (US)| n. adj.|哥特人，哥特式的|A member of a Germanic people who conquered parts of the Roman Empire between the 3rd and 5th centuries.  Also, relating to or characteristic of the Goths or their language.  More recently, a person who follows a style characterized by black clothing, dark makeup, and an interest in gothic rock music and subculture. |例句：She's always dressed in goth clothing. 她总是穿着哥特式服装。

He's a big fan of goth music. 他是哥特音乐的忠实粉丝。|近义词：dark, gloomy, morbid (adj.)|反义词： cheerful, bright, preppy (adj.)|词性: 名词, 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *Gotan (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *Gutans. Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰedʰ- (“to unite”). Related to Old Norse Gautar, Danish *Gøter, Swedish Götar, Old High German Goti, German Goten, and Gothic 𐌲𐌿𐍄𐌸𐌹𐌿𐌳𐌰 (gutþiuda, “Goth”).
