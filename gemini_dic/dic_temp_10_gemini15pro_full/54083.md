disembarked:/ˌdɪsɪmˈbɑːrkt/ /ˌdɪsɪmˈbɑːrkt/ | v. |下船，登陆|to leave a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle|The passengers disembarked from the cruise ship in Miami. 乘客们在迈阿密下了游轮。|synonyms: debark, land, go ashore|antonyms: embark, board, go aboard|verb

disembarkation:/ˌdɪsɪmbɑːrˈkeɪʃn/ /ˌdɪsɪmbɑːrˈkeɪʃn/ | n. |下船，登陆|the act of leaving a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle|The disembarkation process was smooth and efficient. 下船过程顺利而高效。|synonyms: debarkation, landing|antonyms: embarkation, boarding|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desembarquer, from des- (prefix meaning "off, down, from") + embarquer ("to embark"), from en- ("in, on") + barque ("barge").
