displant:/dɪsˈplænt/ | vt. | 移栽；移植；迫使…离开家园；取代 | To remove a plant from the ground or from a pot. To force people to leave their home. To replace something or someone. |  The refugees were displanted by the war. 战争迫使难民们背井离乡。 The new technology is displanting the old methods. 新技术正在取代旧方法。 | 近义词: uproot, displace, remove, supplant | 反义词: plant, settle, establish | 词性: 动词

displant:/dɪsˈplænt/ | n. | (植物的)移植 | The act of displanting something, especially a plant. | The displant of the seedlings was successful. 幼苗的移栽很成功。 The displant of the community caused much hardship. 社区的迁移造成了很多困难。 | 近义词: transplantation, removal, displacement | 反义词: planting, establishment | 词性: 名词
