debilitations:/dɪˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃənz/| n. | 衰弱，虚弱，弱化|  The plural form of debilitation, referring to multiple instances of weakening or enfeebling.  It describes a state of reduced strength or capability, often due to illness or injury.|  The prolonged illness led to a number of debilitations, including muscle weakness and fatigue. 长期患病导致了许多衰弱，包括肌肉无力和疲劳。|  weaknesses, impairments, infirmities | strengths, enhancements, improvements | noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin dēbilitātiō, from Latin dēbilitāre, from dēbilis "weak".
