followers:/ˈfɒləʊəz (UK), ˈfɑːloʊərz (US)| n. |追随者，拥护者，信徒|People who follow a particular person, belief, or interest.  Those who subscribe to or support someone, especially on social media.|例句：The politician has a large number of followers on Twitter.  这位政治家在推特上有很多粉丝。|例句：She gained many followers after her TED talk went viral.  她的TED演讲走红后，她收获了很多粉丝。|近义词：supporters, fans, adherents, disciples|反义词：opponents, critics, detractors|词性:noun


Etymology:
From the verb "follow" + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person or thing that performs a specified action).  "Follow" comes from Middle English *folowen*, from Old English *folgian*, of Germanic origin.
