fetishization:/ˌfɛtɪʃɪˈzeɪʃən/ /ˌfiːtɪʃɪˈzeɪʃən/
n.
物化；恋物癖化；迷恋
The excessive focus on a particular object or trait, often imbuing it with special power or significance, sometimes in a sexual context.
The fetishization of youth in Western culture is often criticized.  西方文化中对年轻的迷恋经常受到批评。
近义词: obsession, idolization, fixation
反义词: indifference, apathy
词性: noun

fetishize:/ˈfɛtɪʃaɪz/ /ˈfiːtɪʃaɪz/
v.
迷恋；把…当成恋物对象
To treat (something) as a fetish; to invest with excessive or irrational significance.
Some argue that the media fetishizes violence.  一些人认为媒体过度渲染暴力。
近义词: obsess over, idolize, fixate on
反义词: disregard, ignore
词性: verb
