archduke:/ˈɑːrtʃˌduːk/ | n. |大公|A nobleman of very high rank, especially (formerly) the chief noble of Austria or a son of the Holy Roman Emperor.|The archduke was known for his philanthropy.|Synonyms: prince, grand duke|Antonyms: commoner|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *archeduc*, from Medieval Latin *archidux*, from *archi-* (from Greek *arkhi-*, "chief, ruler") + *dux* ("leader, duke").
