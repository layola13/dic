gaudily: /ˈɡɔːdɪli/ | adv. | 花哨地；俗丽地 | In a way that is bright and overly decorated, typically so as to appear vulgar or tasteless. |  She was dressed gaudily in a bright pink dress and heavy makeup. 她穿着一件亮粉色的连衣裙，化着浓妆，打扮得花枝招展。|  The Christmas tree was gaudily decorated with tinsel and flashing lights. 圣诞树上装饰着金箔和闪光灯，显得俗不可耐。 | 近义词: garishly, flashily, showily, ostentatiously | 反义词: simply, plainly, subtly, tastefully | 词性: 副词

gaudiness: /ˈɡɔːdinəs/ | n. | 花哨；俗丽 | The quality of being bright and overly decorated, typically so as to appear vulgar or tasteless. | The gaudiness of the decorations was overwhelming. 装饰的俗丽让人难以忍受。 | The gaudiness of her outfit was inappropriate for the occasion. 她的穿着过于花哨，不适合这个场合。 | 近义词: garishness, flashiness, showiness, ostentation | 反义词: simplicity, plainness, subtlety, tastefulness | 词性: 名词

gaudy: /ˈɡɔːdi/ | adj. | 花哨的；俗丽的 | Extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless. |  She wore a gaudy dress to the party. 她穿着一件花哨的连衣裙去参加聚会。 | The decorations were a bit too gaudy for my taste.  对我来说，这些装饰有点过于花哨了。 | 近义词: garish, flashy, showy, ostentatious | 反义词: simple, plain, subtle, tasteful | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Middle English *gaude, from Old North French gaudir (“to rejoice, be joyful”), ultimately from Latin gaudere.
