fluster:/ˈflʌstər/| v. n.|使慌乱，使不安；慌乱，不安|To make someone nervous and confused, or the state of being nervous and confused. |The unexpected question flustered her.  突如其来的问题让她慌了神。|近义词:unnerve, agitate, confuse, disconcert|反义词:calm, compose, soothe|词性:动词, 名词
flustered:/ˈflʌstərd/| adj.|慌乱的，不安的|Feeling or appearing nervous and confused. |She seemed flustered by the sudden attention. 她似乎被这突如其来的关注弄得不知所措。|近义词:nervous, agitated, confused|反义词:calm, composed, collected|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the now obsolete *flusterate, probably a frequentative of an Old Norse word *flaustr, meaning "hurry, haste."
