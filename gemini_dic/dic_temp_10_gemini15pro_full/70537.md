filibusterous:/ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstərəs/| adj.|冗长演说的，阻挠议事的|Characterized by or resembling a filibuster; obstructive, dilatory, especially in a legislative assembly.|The senator's filibusterous speech lasted for over 12 hours, effectively blocking the vote.|Synonyms: obstructive, dilatory, delaying, procrastinating, time-wasting|Antonyms: expeditious, efficient, swift, concise, brief|adjective

Etymology->
From filibuster (n.) + -ous. Filibuster comes from Dutch vrijbuiter "freebooter," from vrij "free" + buiter "plunderer." Originally referring to pirates, the term was later applied to adventurers who engaged in unauthorized military expeditions, and eventually to those who obstruct legislative proceedings.
