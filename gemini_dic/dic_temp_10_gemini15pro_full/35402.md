co-opt:/koʊˈɑːpt/ |v. |吸收；拉拢；指派 |To appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members.  To adopt (an idea, course of action, etc.) for one's own use, especially without the consent of the originator.|The rebels co-opted several villagers into their army. 叛军把几个村民拉进了他们的军队。|Synonyms: appropriate, assimilate, enlist, incorporate|Antonyms: exclude, reject, oust|verb

co-opt:/koʊˈɑːpt/ |n. |(指被吸收的人) |A person who is co-opted.|The new co-opts quickly learned the ropes. 新被吸收的成员很快学会了窍门。|Synonyms: member, recruit|Antonyms: outsider|noun
