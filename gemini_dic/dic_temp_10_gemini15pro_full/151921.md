pasteurization's:/ˌpæstərɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. | 巴氏灭菌法；巴氏消毒法 |  The process of heating a liquid, such as milk or beer, to a specific temperature for a specific period to kill microorganisms that could cause spoilage or disease without altering the flavor or nutritional value.  It is derived from the name of Louis Pasteur, the French scientist who developed the process. |  Pasteurization's impact on public health has been immense. 巴氏灭菌法对公共卫生的影响是巨大的。| synonyms: sterilization, disinfection | antonyms: contamination, infection | noun
