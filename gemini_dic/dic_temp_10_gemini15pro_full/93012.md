idiosyncratically:/ˌɪdiəsɪŋˈkrætɪkli/ | adv. | 特别地；古怪地； idiosyncratically means in a way that is peculiar to a person or thing. It emphasizes a distinctive or unusual characteristic. | He dresses idiosyncratically, always wearing mismatched socks. 他穿着很古怪，总是穿不配对的袜子。 | She plays the piano idiosyncratically, adding her own unique flourishes to every piece. 她弹钢琴的方式很独特，每首曲子都加入了她自己独特的装饰音。 | synonym: peculiarly, distinctively, eccentrically | antonym: typically, conventionally, normally | adverb

idiosyncratic:/ˌɪdiəsɪŋˈkrætɪk/ | adj. | 特有的；独特的；古怪的； idiosyncratic means peculiar to a particular individual or thing; distinctive. | His teaching methods are idiosyncratic but very effective. 他的教学方法很独特，但非常有效。 |  The building has an idiosyncratic charm. 这栋建筑有一种独特的魅力。 | synonym: peculiar, distinctive, eccentric | antonym: typical, conventional, normal | adjective

Etymology: From Greek *idiosynkrasia*, from *idios* ("one's own") + *syn* ("with") + *krasis* ("mixture").
