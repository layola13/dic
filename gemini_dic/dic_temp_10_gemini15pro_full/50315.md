deplorabilities:/dɪˌplɔːrəˈbɪlɪtiz/
|n.|可悲之人（或事物）; 糟糕的人（或事物）|The state of being deplorable; wretchedness. A group or collection of deplorable people or things.  It often implies a sense of disdain or contempt for the people or things being described. |Hillary Clinton referred to some of Donald Trump's supporters as a "basket of deplorables." 希拉里·克林顿将唐纳德·特朗普的一些支持者称为“一篮子可悲的人”。|Synonyms: undesirables, reprobates, miscreants|Antonyms:  admirables, commendable people/things|noun

deplorable:/dɪˈplɔːrəbl/
|adj.|可悲的；应受谴责的；糟糕的|Deeply regrettable; unfortunate. Deserving strong condemnation.  Wretched; bad.|The working conditions in the factory were deplorable. 工厂的工作条件非常糟糕。|Synonyms: lamentable, regrettable, disgraceful, appalling|Antonyms: admirable, commendable, excellent|adjective
