monologue: /ˈmɒnəlɒɡ/ (UK), /ˈmɑːnəlɔːɡ/ (US) | n. | 独白；个人长篇演说 | A long speech by one actor in a play or film, or as part of a theatrical or broadcast programme. |  He launched into a monologue about the evils of modern society. 他开始长篇大论地讲述现代社会的弊端。| synonym: soliloquy, speech, address | antonym: dialogue, conversation | noun

monologuize: /məˈnɒləɡaɪz/ (UK), /məˈnɑːləɡaɪz/ (US) | v. |  发表长篇大论；滔滔不绝地说 | To talk at length, especially about oneself or one's interests, without allowing other people to speak. | He tends to monologuize rather than have a conversation. 他往往是滔滔不绝地说，而不是进行对话。| synonym: lecture, preach, rant | antonym: converse, discuss, listen | verb
