epanadiplosis:/ˌɛpənədɪˈploʊsɪs/
n.
首尾重复（修辞手法）
A rhetorical device where a word or phrase is repeated at the beginning and end of a clause or sentence.
"Rejoice in the Lord always: and again I say, Rejoice." - Philippians 4:4  在主里常常喜乐，我再说，常常喜乐。

"The king is dead, long live the king!" 国王驾崩了，国王万岁！

synonym: epanalepsis, inclusio
antonym:  (No direct antonym for a rhetorical device.  Consider contrasting it with asyndeton or polysyndeton.)
