dyspraxic: /dɪsˈpræksɪk/ | adj. | 失用症的，运动障碍的 | Relating to or affected by dyspraxia, a developmental coordination disorder affecting physical coordination. |  Example 1:  The dyspraxic child struggled to tie his shoelaces.  这个患有运动障碍的孩子难以系鞋带。

Example 2: Dyspraxic individuals may have difficulty with fine motor skills. 失用症患者可能在精细运动技能方面有困难。 | Near synonyms: clumsy, awkward, uncoordinated | Antonym: coordinated | Word type: Adjective


Etymology:  From dys- (Greek, meaning “bad,” “difficult,” or “abnormal”) + praxis (Greek, meaning “doing,” “act,” or “practice”).
