grafters: /ˈɡrɑːftərz/ (UK), /ˈɡræftərz/ (US)
| n. | 嫁接者; 贪污者; 辛苦工作的人; |  A person who grafts, especially plants or skin. Also (informally and often disapprovingly) someone who works hard, or someone who uses their position or power in a dishonest way to get money or advantages.|
例句：The grafters in the orchard were busy preparing the trees for the new season. 果园里的嫁接工人正忙着为新季节准备树木。

例句：The investigation uncovered a network of grafters who had been skimming money off public funds. 调查揭露了一个贪污网络，他们一直在从公共资金中捞钱。

近义词：worker, hustler, swindler, embezzler
反义词：idler, loafer
词性：名词


graft: /ɡrɑːft/ (UK), /ɡræft/ (US)
| n. | 嫁接; 贪污; 辛苦的工作; | The process of joining a shoot or bud of one plant to another plant so that they grow together. Also (informally) hard work, or the practice of using your position or power in a dishonest way to get money or advantages. |
例句：The gardener expertly performed the graft, ensuring a healthy union between the two plants. 园丁熟练地进行了嫁接，确保了两株植物的健康结合。

例句：He got the job through graft and corruption, not through merit. 他是通过贪污腐败而不是靠能力得到这份工作的。

近义词：work, bribery, corruption, transplant
反义词：honesty, integrity
词性：名词

graft: /ɡrɑːft/ (UK), /ɡræft/ (US)
| v. | 嫁接; 贪污; 努力工作; | To join a shoot or bud of one plant to another plant so that they grow together. Also (informally) to work hard, or to use your position or power in a dishonest way to get money or advantages.|
例句：They grafted skin from her leg onto the burn. 他们从她的腿上取下皮肤移植到烧伤处。

例句：He's been grafting away at that project for months. 他几个月来一直在努力做那个项目。

近义词：work, hustle, transplant, embezzle
反义词：idle, loaf
词性：动词


词源: From Old French *graffe, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Greek graphion 'stylus' (for making incisions in the bark).
