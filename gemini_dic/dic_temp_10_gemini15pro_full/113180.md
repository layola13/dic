lure:/lʊr/ | v. n. | 诱惑；引诱；诱饵 | To tempt or attract with the promise of something pleasing. |  The fisherman used worms to lure the fish. 渔夫用虫子引诱鱼。 | synonyms: entice, tempt, attract, seduce | antonyms: repel, deter, discourage | verb

lured:/lʊrd/ | v. | 诱惑；引诱（lure的过去式和过去分词）| Tempted or attracted with the promise of something pleasing (past tense). | The flashing lights lured the moths. 闪烁的灯光吸引了飞蛾。 | synonyms: enticed, tempted, attracted, seduced | antonyms: repelled, deterred, discouraged | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *luerre "bait, decoy," of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Old French loire "bridle, rein" (from Latin lorum "strap, thong"), or perhaps a Germanic word related to Old English belucan "to lock up, enclose."
