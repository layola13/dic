desolaters:/ˈdesəˌleɪtərz/
n.
破坏者；使荒芜的人；劫掠者
Those who cause destruction, ruin, or devastation, leaving a place barren or desolate.
The desolaters pillaged the village, leaving it a smoldering ruin.  这些破坏者洗劫了村庄，把它变成了一个冒烟的废墟。
The desolaters left the once fertile land barren and lifeless. 这些劫掠者使曾经肥沃的土地变得荒芜和毫无生气。
近义词: destroyers, devastators, ravagers, pillagers
反义词: creators, builders, protectors, preservers
词性: 名词(plural)


词源:
From the verb "desolate" + "-er" (agent suffix).  "Desolate" comes from the Latin word "desolatus," the past participle of "desolare," meaning "to abandon, forsake, lay waste."  The root is "solus," meaning "alone."
