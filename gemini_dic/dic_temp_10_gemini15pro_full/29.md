a-matter-of-life-and-death:/ə ˈmætər əv laɪf ənd dɛθ/| adj.|生死攸关的，极其重要的|A situation that is extremely serious and could result in someone living or dying.|The surgery was a matter of life and death for him.  对他来说，这次手术是生死攸关的。

The doctors worked frantically; it was a matter of life and death. 医生们疯狂地工作；这是生死攸关的事情。|近义词：critical, crucial, vital, essential, urgent|反义词：trivial, unimportant, insignificant|词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
"matter" from Old French "matere" (subject matter, material) from Latin "materia" (timber, material).
"life" from Old English "līf" (life, lifetime).
"and" from Old English "and" (and).
"death" from Old English "dēaþ" (death).

USAGE->
This phrase usually functions as an adjectival phrase modifying a noun, or as a predicate adjective following a linking verb.

Example 1:  Finding a compatible blood donor was a matter of life and death for the patient. = 为病人找到合适的献血者是生死攸关的事情。

Example 2:  The situation was a matter of life and death. = 情况危急。


PHRASE->
This phrase is often used on its own and doesn't typically form part of other set phrases.  However, it can be modified by adverbs like "literally" or "truly."

Example 1:  It was literally a matter of life and death. = 这真的是生死攸关的事情。

Example 2: The search for the missing child was truly a matter of life and death. = 寻找失踪儿童真的是生死攸关的事情。
