arrantly:/ˈærəntli/| adv.|完全地，彻底地，极恶劣地|Completely and utterly; in an egregious manner.  It emphasizes the negative aspect of the modified adjective or adverb.|He was arrantly dishonest in his dealings. 他在他的交易中完全不诚实。|Synonyms: utterly, completely, totally, absolutely, downright, flagrantly, egregiously|Antonyms: slightly, moderately, marginally|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *araunt, from Old French *arrant, present participle of errer (“to err, wander, stray”), from Latin iterō (“to repeat, reiterate”), from iter (“a journey, way, road”). More at iterate. The sense development appears to have been influenced by or merged with errant.
