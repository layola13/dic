agitato:/ˌædʒɪˈtɑːtoʊ/ /ˌædʒɪˈtɑːtəʊ/
| adj. | 激动地，不安地 |  Characterized by agitation and excitement; performed in a restless or hurried manner. | The musician played the agitato passage with frantic energy. 音乐家以狂乱的能量演奏了那段激动人心的乐段。| synonyms: restless, disturbed, uneasy, agitated, excited, frantic, frenetic | antonyms: calm, peaceful, tranquil, serene | adjective

agitato:/ˌædʒɪˈtɑːtoʊ/ /ˌædʒɪˈtɑːtəʊ/
| adv. | 激动地，不安地 | In a restless or agitated manner; with agitation. | The strings played agitato, reflecting the character's inner turmoil.  弦乐激动地演奏，反映了角色内心的混乱。 | synonyms: restlessly, excitedly, frantically | antonyms: calmly, peacefully, tranquilly | adverb

Etymology:
From Italian *agitato*, past participle of *agitare* "to agitate," from Latin *agitare* "to drive, urge forward, stir up," frequentative of *agere* "to do, act, drive."
