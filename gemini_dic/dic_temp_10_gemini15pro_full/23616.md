branches:/ˈbrɑːntʃɪz/ (US) /ˈbrɑːnʧɪz/ (UK)| n.|分支，分部，树枝|A part of something larger, such as a part of a tree, a company, or a family. |The bank has branches all over the country.  这家银行在全国各地都有分行。|synonyms: limbs, offshoots, divisions, sections |antonyms: trunk, root, origin|noun


branches:/ˈbrɑːntʃɪz/ (US) /ˈbrɑːnʧɪz/ (UK)| v.|分支，分叉|To divide into separate parts. |The road branches off to the left. 这条路向左分叉。|synonyms: diverge, fork, separate |antonyms: converge, join, unite|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "branche," ultimately from Vulgar Latin *branca, of uncertain origin. Related to Old Occitan "branca" and Catalan "branca."
