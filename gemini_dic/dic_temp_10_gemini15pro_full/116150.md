mascagnin:/mæskəˈniːn/| n. |马斯卡尼石，硫酸铵矿|A naturally occurring mineral form of ammonium sulfate, (NH4)2SO4. It is typically found as a white to yellowish efflorescence or crust in volcanic fumaroles and around burning coal seams.|Mascagnin is a common mineral found near active volcanoes. 马斯卡尼石是一种常见于活火山附近的矿物。

The miners discovered a vein of mascagnin. 矿工们发现了一条马斯卡尼石矿脉。|近义词:ammonium sulfate |反义词: N/A |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *mascagnino*, from the name of Paolo Mascagni (1755–1815), the Italian anatomist who first described it.
