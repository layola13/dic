hit-or-miss:/ˌhɪt ər ˈmɪs/| adj.|时好时坏的；碰运气；随便的|Something that is hit-or-miss is sometimes successful and sometimes not, often because of a lack of skill or planning.|The restaurant's food is hit-or-miss – sometimes it's great, other times it's awful. 这家餐馆的菜时好时坏——有时很棒，有时很糟糕。|His attendance at meetings was hit-or-miss. 他开会出席的情况时好时坏。|近义词：random, haphazard, unpredictable, inconsistent|反义词：consistent, reliable, dependable|词性:形容词

hit-or-miss:/ˌhɪt ər ˈmɪs/| n.|碰运气的事情|A situation or event whose success is uncertain.|Starting a new business can be a real hit-or-miss.  创业是一件碰运气的事。|It was a hit-or-miss whether he would show up. 他是否出现完全是碰运气。|近义词：gamble, lottery, chance|反义词：certainty, sure thing|词性:名词
