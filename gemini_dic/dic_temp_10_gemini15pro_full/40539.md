controversialism:/ˌkɒntrəˈvɜːʃəlɪzəm/ | n. |争议性，引起争论的倾向 |The book's controversialism guaranteed its place on the bestseller list. 这本书的争议性保证了它在畅销书排行榜上的位置。|  The politician's controversialism made him a popular, albeit divisive, figure. 这位政治家的争议性使他成为一个受欢迎的、尽管有争议的人物。 | synonyms: contentiousness, disputatiousness, polemicalness | antonyms: agreeableness, amiability, harmony | noun

Etymology:  From controversial + -ism.  "Controversial" comes from "controversy," which ultimately derives from the Latin "controversia," meaning "a turning against," from "contra" (against) and "vertere" (to turn).  The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice.
