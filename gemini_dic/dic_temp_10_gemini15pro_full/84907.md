hanking:/ˈhæŋkɪŋ/ | n. | 渴望，热望; 痛打; 纺纱; |A strong desire or craving, especially for something illicit or unhealthy. Also, a beating or thrashing. In a textile context, it refers to the process of forming yarn from fibres.| He had a hanking for a cigarette. 他很想抽根烟。|近义词：yearning, longing, craving, beating, thrashing |反义词：aversion, dislike, indifference |词性：名词

词源:

From the verb "hank," meaning to fasten or secure.  The "hanking" for something likely developed from the idea of being tied to or held by a desire. The sense of a beating may come from the image of yanking or pulling something repeatedly.  The textile meaning relates to the handling of hanks (coils) of yarn.
