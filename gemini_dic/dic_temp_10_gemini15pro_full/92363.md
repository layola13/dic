hypochondria:/ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndriə/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪə/ (UK)| n.|疑病症，臆想病|A psychological disorder characterized by excessive worry about having a serious illness, despite medical reassurance to the contrary.|She suffered from hypochondria and was always convinced she had a terrible disease.|近义词: health anxiety, illness anxiety disorder|反义词:  (No direct antonym, consider terms like robust health, well-being)|词性:noun

hypochondriac:/ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndriæk/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪæk/ (UK)| n. adj.|疑病症患者，疑病的|A person who suffers from hypochondria, or exhibiting characteristics of hypochondria.|He's a terrible hypochondriac; every little ache or pain sends him running to the doctor. /  Her hypochondriac tendencies led her to believe a common cold was pneumonia.|近义词: valetudinarian|反义词:  (No direct antonym)|词性: noun, adjective
