double-talk:/ˈdʌbəlˌtɔːk/ (US), /ˈdʌblˌtɔːk/ (UK)| n.|模棱两可的话；含糊其辞；遁辞|Language that appears to be meaningful but in fact is a mixture of sense and nonsense, designed to mislead or to evade the truth. |He gave a speech full of double-talk, and nobody understood what he meant. 他发表了一通充满模棱两可的讲话，没人明白他的意思。|Synonyms: ambiguity, equivocation, evasion, prevarication |Antonyms: clarity, directness, honesty, straightforwardness|noun


double-talk:/ˈdʌbəlˌtɔːk/ (US), /ˈdʌblˌtɔːk/ (UK)| v.|说模棱两可的话；含糊其辞|To speak evasively or ambiguously. |The politician double-talked his way through the interview, avoiding any direct answers.  这位政客在采访中含糊其辞，避免任何直接的回答。|Synonyms: equivocate, prevaricate, hedge|Antonyms: articulate, clarify, explain clearly|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From *double* + *talk*. First recorded in the 1940s.
