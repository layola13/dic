movingly:/ˈmuːvɪŋli/| adv.|动人地，感人地|In a way that causes strong feelings of sadness or sympathy.  It describes the manner in which something is done, specifically in a way that evokes emotion, particularly sadness or sympathy.|She spoke movingly about her experiences during the war. 她动情地讲述了她在战争期间的经历。|近义词：touchingly, poignantly, emotionally, affectingly|反义词：unemotionally, coldly, indifferently|词性:副词

Etymology: From "moving" (present participle of "move," from Old French "movoir," from Latin "movēre" - to move) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).
