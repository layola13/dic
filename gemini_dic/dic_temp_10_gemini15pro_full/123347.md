monolithic:/ˌmɒnəˈlɪθɪk/ | /ˌmɑːnəˈlɪθɪk/ | adj. | 巨大的，庞大的；单一的，统一的；整体式的 | Formed of a single large block of stone.  (Of a building) large, impressive, and made of stone. (figurative) Large, powerful, and indivisible. |  The company is trying to break down its monolithic structure.  这家公司正试图打破其庞大的结构。|  The government was a monolithic entity, resistant to change. 政府是一个庞大的实体，抵制变革。 | massive, huge, gigantic, colossal, imposing | diverse, varied, heterogeneous | adjective

monolith:/ˈmɒnəlɪθ/ | /ˈmɑːnəlɪθ/ | n. | 巨石；庞然大物；单一庞大的组织 | A large single upright block of stone, especially one shaped into or serving as a pillar or monument.  A large and impersonal political, corporate, or social structure regarded as indivisible and slow to change. | Stonehenge is a prehistoric monument made up of monoliths. 巨石阵是由巨石组成的史前遗迹。 |  The corporation was a monolith, difficult to influence.  这家公司是一个庞然大物，难以影响。 | obelisk, pillar, column, megalith | fragment, piece | noun
