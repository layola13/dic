disaccommodate:/dɪsəˈkɑːmədeɪt/| v.|使不便；拒绝提供住宿；|To cause inconvenience or discomfort to someone; to refuse or be unable to provide lodging for someone.|The hotel was overbooked and had to disaccommodate several guests.|inconvenience, disturb, upset|accommodate, assist, help|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + accommodate (from Late Latin accommodatus, past participle of accommodare, "to make fit," from Latin ad- ("to") + commodus ("suitable, convenient")).
