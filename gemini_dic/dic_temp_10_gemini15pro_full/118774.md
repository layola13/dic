metallograph: /məˈtæləɡrɑːf/ /məˈtæləɡræf/
noun
金相显微镜；金相图谱
An optical microscope designed for observing the structure of metals, typically using reflected light.  A photographic record made with such a microscope.
例句：The metallograph revealed the grain structure of the steel. 金相显微镜揭示了钢的晶粒结构。

例句：The metallograph showed the presence of inclusions in the alloy. 金相照片显示合金中存在夹杂物。

近义词：metallographic microscope
反义词:  (No direct antonym for a physical instrument)
词性: n.


metallography: /məˈtælɒɡrəfi/ /məˈtælɑːɡrəfi/
noun
金相学
The study of the structure of metals and alloys, typically using microscopy.
例句：Metallography is crucial for understanding the properties of materials.  金相学对于理解材料的性能至关重要。

例句：He specialized in the metallography of ancient artifacts. 他专门研究古代文物的金相学。

近义词：materialography
反义词: (No direct antonym for a field of study)
词性: n.
