disgust:/dɪsˈɡʌst/
n.|厌恶，憎恶|A feeling of strong dislike or revulsion.|The smell of rotten eggs filled me with disgust.|repugnance, aversion, nausea|pleasure, liking
v.|使厌恶，使作呕|To cause a feeling of strong dislike or revulsion.|The violent scenes in the movie disgusted many viewers.|repel, sicken, nauseate|please, attract
词性:名词,动词

词源:
From Old French *desgouster (Modern French dégoûter), from des- "dis-" + gouster "to taste," from Latin gustare.  The original meaning was "to cause to lose one's taste (for something)," hence "to cause aversion to."
