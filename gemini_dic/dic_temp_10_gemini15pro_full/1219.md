accommodators: /əˈkɒmədeɪtəz/ (UK) /əˈkɑːmədeɪtərz/ (US)| n.|调节者，适应者，提供住宿者|People who adjust their behavior or thinking to suit others or new situations, or those who provide lodging. |例句：The hotel accommodators were very friendly and helpful.  酒店的住宿服务员非常友好，乐于助人。

The accommodators made every effort to ensure the guests felt comfortable.  调节者尽一切努力确保客人感到舒适。|近义词：adapters, adjusters, hosts, providers|反义词: nonconformists, dissenters|词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "accommodate," which comes from the Latin "accommodare," meaning "to make fit, adapt."  "Accommodate" is formed from "ad-" (to) + "commodare" (to make suitable), which itself derives from "commodus" (convenient, suitable).
