counterpointing:/ˌkaʊntərˈpɔɪntɪŋ/ | n. | 对位法；对比；衬托 |  The act or technique of setting two or more independent melodies in such a way that they harmonize when played or sung simultaneously;  The act of presenting contrasting ideas or viewpoints. | The composer's skillful counterpointing created a rich and complex musical texture. 作曲家娴熟的复调技巧创造了丰富而复杂的音乐质感。

The article counterpointed the benefits of technology with its potential dangers. 这篇文章将科技的益处与其潜在的危险进行了对比。 | 近义词: contrasting, juxtaposing, setting against | 反义词: harmonizing, blending, conforming | 词性: Noun (derived from the verb "counterpoint")

counterpoint:/ˈkaʊntərˌpɔɪnt/ | v. |  构成对位；形成对比；衬托 | To set in counterpoint; to contrast or offset. |  The bright colors of the flowers counterpointed the dark green of the foliage.  鲜艳的花朵与深绿色的叶子形成对比。

His calm demeanor counterpointed her frantic energy. 他平静的风度与她狂乱的活力形成了对比。 | 近义词: contrast, offset, juxtapose | 反义词: harmonize, blend, conform | 词性: Verb

PHRASE->
counterpoint to something:  与某物形成对比/对位  Her optimistic outlook served as a counterpoint to his pessimism. 她的乐观态度与他的悲观形成了对比。
counterpoint something with something: 将某物与某物形成对比/对位 The artist counterpointed the vibrant colors with the muted tones. 艺术家将鲜艳的色彩与柔和的色调进行了对比。
in counterpoint to: 与…形成对比/对位 Her actions were in counterpoint to her words. 她的行为与她的言语形成了对比。
as a counterpoint to: 作为对…的对比/对位  The documentary presented the personal stories as a counterpoint to the dry statistics. 纪录片以个人故事作为对枯燥统计数据的对比。
