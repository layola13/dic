democracies: /dɪˈmɒkrəsiːz/ (UK) /dɪˈmɑːkrəsiːz/ (US)| n. |民主国家，民主政体（democracy 的复数形式）|A system of government in which power is vested in the people and exercised by them directly or through elected representatives. |Many Western European countries are democracies. 许多西欧国家是民主国家。|synonyms: republics, self-governments| antonyms: dictatorships, autocracies| noun

democracy: /dɪˈmɒkrəsi/ (UK) /dɪˈmɑːkrəsi/ (US)| n. |民主，民主主义|A system of government by the whole population or all the eligible members of a state, typically through elected representatives. |The country transitioned to democracy after the revolution.  这个国家在革命后过渡到民主制度。|synonyms:  republicanism, self-government, popular sovereignty| antonyms: dictatorship, autocracy, tyranny, despotism| noun

词源：From Middle French *democratie*, from Late Latin *dēmocratia*, from Greek *dēmokratiā*, from *dēmos* "people" + *kratos* "power, rule".
