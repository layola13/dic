monocondyla: /ˌmɒnəˈkɒndɪlə/ | n. | 单髁类 |  A taxonomic group (subclass) of early mammals characterized by a single condyle on the lower jaw.  This feature distinguishes them from more derived mammals which have two condyles. |  Monocondyla were a diverse group that included egg-laying mammals. 单髁类是一组多样化的哺乳动物，包括卵生哺乳动物。 | Near Synonyms: Prototheria (although not perfectly equivalent, as Prototheria sometimes includes Morganucodontidae which may or may not be monocondylar) | Antonyms:  None (a taxonomic group doesn't have a direct antonym) | Noun

monocondylar: /ˌmɒnəˈkɒndɪlər/ | adj. | 单髁的 | Possessing a single condyle on the lower jaw.  This is a characteristic of the subclass Monocondyla. |  The monocondylar jaw articulation is a primitive feature. 单髁颌关节是一个原始特征。 | Near Synonyms: single-condyled | Antonyms:  bicondyled, double-condyled | Adjective
