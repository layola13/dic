agonise: /ˈæɡənaɪz/ | v. | 感到极度痛苦；苦恼；焦虑 | To suffer extreme physical or mental pain; to worry greatly about something. | He agonised over the decision for weeks.  他为这个决定苦恼了几个星期。|  worry, fret, stress | rejoice, celebrate | verb

词源: From Late Middle English *agonisen, *agonizen, from Old French agoniser, agonisier (“to struggle, agonize, be in agony”),  from Late Latin agōnizāre, from Ancient Greek ἀγωνίζεσθαι (agōnízesthai, “to contend for a prize, struggle, agonize”), from ἀγωνίζομαι (agōnízomai), from ἀγών (agṓn, “contest, struggle”).
