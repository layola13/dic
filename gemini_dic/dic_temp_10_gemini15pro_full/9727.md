antiutilitarianisms: /ˌæntijuːˌtɪlɪˈtɛəriənɪzəmz/ | n. | 反功利主义（复数）|  Philosophies opposed to utilitarianism, rejecting the idea that the morality of an action is determined solely by its consequences and maximizing overall happiness. |  Many antiutilitarianisms emphasize the importance of individual rights and duties, regardless of their impact on overall happiness. 许多反功利主义强调个人权利和义务的重要性，无论它们对整体幸福的影响如何。|  His antiutilitarianisms stem from a deep belief in the sanctity of human life. 他的反功利主义源于对生命神圣性的深信不疑。 | synonyms: non-utilitarianisms, deontological ethics, virtue ethics | antonyms: utilitarianisms, consequentialism | noun
