disappendant:/dɪsəˈpɛndənt/ | adj. | 附属的，从属的 |  Formerly used in legal contexts to describe something or someone that is appendent or appurtenant (belonging to something else, especially as a right or privilege). It suggests a subordinate or dependent relationship. The word is archaic and rarely used in modern English. |  The disappendant lands were transferred to the new owner along with the main estate.  附属土地连同主要地产一起转让给了新的业主。| Near synonyms:  dependent, subordinate, ancillary, appurtenant | Antonyms: independent, autonomous, principal | word attribute: adjective
