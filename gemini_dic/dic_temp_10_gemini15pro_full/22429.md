booked:/bʊkt/|/ v.|预订，预约；登记；（非正式）逮捕|The past tense and past participle of "book." It means to reserve something, like a ticket or a room, or to register someone's name. It can also informally mean to arrest someone.|We booked a flight to Paris for next month. 我们预订了去下个月去巴黎的航班。|近义词：reserve, schedule, engage|反义词:cancel|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *boken*, from Old English *bōcian* (“to grant by charter, to assign by writing”), from *bōc* (“book, writing, charter”).  The modern sense of reserving something, like a hotel room, arose from the practice of writing names in a register or "book."
