momentum:/məˈmɛntəm/ (US), /məʊˈmɛntəm/ (UK)| n.| 动量，势头，动力|The force or speed with which something moves.|The car gained momentum as it rolled down the hill. 汽车顺着山坡向下滚动，速度越来越快。|synonyms: impetus, impulse, drive, thrust|antonyms: inertia, stagnation|noun

momentum:/məˈmɛntəm/ (US), /məʊˈmɛntəm/ (UK)| n.| 气势，冲劲|The strength or force gained by motion or by a series of events.|The campaign gained momentum in the final weeks before the election.  竞选活动在选举前的最后几周势头强劲。|synonyms: energy, power, force|antonyms: weakness, lethargy|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *movimentum*, from movēre "to move."
