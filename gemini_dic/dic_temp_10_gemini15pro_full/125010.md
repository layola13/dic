mugwumpery:/ˈmʌɡwʌmpəri/
noun
n.
独立改革运动;清廉正直的政治;高尚的政治
A political movement in the United States in the 1880s that advocated non-partisanship and opposed corruption in government. It refers to a high-minded but often self-righteous attitude to politics.

Example 1: His mugwumpery prevented him from engaging in the usual backroom deals.
他的清廉正直的政治态度使他无法参与通常的幕后交易。

Example 2: The candidate's mugwumpery alienated some party loyalists who saw it as naive.
这位候选人高尚的政治态度疏远了一些认为他天真的党内忠实拥护者。

Synonym:  reformer,  idealist,  independent
Antonym:  partisan,  hack,  corrupt
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Algonquian (Natick) *mugquomp, a word meaning "important person," "kingpin," or literally “war leader.”  It was used derisively by the New York Sun to refer to Republican reformers who refused to support their party's nominee, James G. Blaine, in the 1884 presidential election.
