nonmonistically:/ˌnɒnˌmɒnɪˈstɪkəli/
adv.|非一元论地|In a way that is not monistic; rejecting monism.  The theory rejects monism and embraces a nonmonistically view of reality.|He argued nonmonistically, suggesting that both mind and matter are fundamental.|Near Antonyms: monistically
Etymology:  "non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "monistic" (relating to monism) + "-ally" (adverbial suffix). "Monism" comes from Greek "monos" (one).
