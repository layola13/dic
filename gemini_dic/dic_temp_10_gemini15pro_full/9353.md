antiprohibitionists:/ˌæntiˌproʊɪˈbɪʃənɪsts/
|n.| 反禁酒主义者|People who opposed the prohibition of alcohol, particularly in the United States during the period of national prohibition (1920-1933).  They believed that prohibiting alcohol was ineffective, infringed on personal liberties, and led to increased crime and corruption.|The antiprohibitionists campaigned tirelessly for the repeal of the Eighteenth Amendment.|近义词:wets, repealists|反义词:prohibitionists, drys|词性:名词

Etymology:
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to (from Greek anti-)
prohibition: the action of forbidding something, especially by law (from Latin prohibitus, past participle of prohibere 'to prevent, hinder')
-ist: (suffix) forming nouns denoting an adherent of a doctrine, system, or party (from Greek -istes)
