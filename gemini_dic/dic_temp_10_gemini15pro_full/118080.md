-mendous: /ˈtrem.ən.dəs/ | adj. | 巨大的，惊人的，极好的 |  Used to emphasize the great amount, size, or degree of something.  Originates from Latin "tremendus," meaning "dreadful,"  "awesome," inspiring awe or fear.  Over time, the negative connotation lessened, and it came to mean "immense" or "extraordinary." |  The party was a tremendous success.  派对非常成功。 | Synonyms: huge, enormous, immense, gigantic, colossal, vast, massive, prodigious, stupendous, monumental, extraordinary, remarkable, exceptional, outstanding, wonderful, fantastic, great, terrific | Antonyms: tiny, small, minuscule, insignificant, negligible | 词性: adjective
