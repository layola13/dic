crypto-calvinist:/ˌkrɪptoʊˈkælvɪnɪst/ | n. | 秘密加尔文主义者 | A person who outwardly conforms to the doctrines and practices of a dominant church (usually Lutheranism) while secretly adhering to Calvinist beliefs. |  Example 1:  Historians suspect that many German princes in the 16th century were crypto-Calvinists, publicly supporting Lutheranism while privately favoring Calvin's teachings.  历史学家怀疑16世纪的许多德国王子都是秘密加尔文主义者，公开支持路德教，私下里却赞成加尔文的教义。

Example 2:  He was accused of being a crypto-Calvinist and forced to recant his beliefs. 他被指控为秘密加尔文主义者，并被迫放弃他的信仰。 | 近义词: Nicodemite | 反义词:  Openly Calvinist,  Lutheran | 词性: 名词
