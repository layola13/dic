cocket:/ˈkɒkɪt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːkɪt/ (US)| adj. | 俏皮的；活泼的；自负的 | Flirtatiously confident; pert.  Characterized by a playful boldness, often combined with a touch of vanity or arrogance. | She gave him a cocket smile. 她给了他一个俏皮的微笑。| He strutted around with a cocket air. 他昂首阔步，神气十足。| 近义词: pert, saucy, cheeky, flirty | 反义词: shy, timid, reserved | 词性: 形容词

词源:
From Middle English *coket*, probably from Old North French *coquet*, diminutive of coq "rooster," referring to the rooster's strutting gait and behavior.
