hold-up:/ˈhəʊldʌp/ (US) /ˈhoʊldʌp/| n.|抢劫; 阻碍; 延误|A robbery at gunpoint.  A situation causing delay.|例句：The hold-up caused a massive traffic jam. 这起抢劫造成了严重的交通堵塞。

hold-up:/ˈhəʊldʌp/ (US) /ˈhoʊldʌp/| n.|抢劫; 阻碍; 延误|A situation that prevents something from continuing as expected.|例句：There was a hold-up in the production process due to a shortage of parts.  由于零件短缺，生产过程出现了延误。|近义词：delay, robbery, heist, obstruction|反义词:advancement, progress|词性:名词

词源:  "hold-up" comes from the phrasal verb "to hold up," meaning to delay or obstruct.  The noun form developed from this verb, initially referring to highway robbery (literally stopping someone and holding them up at gunpoint).
