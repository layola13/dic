dishonouring:/dɪsˈɒnərɪŋ/ (UK) /dɪsˈɑːnərɪŋ/ (US)| v. |使丢脸；玷污；拒付|The act of bringing shame or disgrace upon someone or something, or refusing to pay a check or other financial obligation.|He dishonoured his family by his criminal behaviour. 他因犯罪行为使家人蒙羞。|Synonyms: disgracing, shaming, humiliating, defaming, discrediting, refusing payment|Antonyms: honouring, respecting, upholding, paying|Verb

dishonour:/dɪsˈɒnər/ (UK) /dɪsˈɑːnər/ (US)| n. |耻辱；丢脸的事情|Lack of honour or respect; shame or disgrace.|Bringing dishonour to the family name. 给家族带来耻辱。|Synonyms: shame, disgrace, ignominy, humiliation, disrepute|Antonyms: honour, respect, glory, dignity|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *deshonorer*, from des- (expressing negation) + honorer (to honour). Ultimately from Latin honor (honour).
