greynesses:/ˈɡreɪnɪsnɪsɪz/
noun
灰暗，阴沉；灰色调
The state or quality of being grey or greyish.  The various shades or intensities of grey.
The photograph captured the greynesses of the winter sky. 这张照片捕捉到了冬日天空的灰暗。
The artist skillfully used greynesses to create a sense of depth.  这位艺术家巧妙地运用灰色调来营造一种深度感。
synonyms: dullness, drabness, gloominess, cloudiness
antonyms: brightness, vibrancy, colorfulness
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From grey + -ness. Grey (meaning the color) comes from the Old English grǣg. The suffix -ness is used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state, quality, or condition.
