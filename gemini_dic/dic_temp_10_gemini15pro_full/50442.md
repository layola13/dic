deprecator:/ˈdɛprɪkeɪtər/| n.|反对者，弃用者|Someone who expresses disapproval of something.  One who deprecates something.|The library has become a deprecator of outdated technology, actively removing old computers and software.  图书馆已经成为过时技术的反对者，积极移除旧电脑和软件。|近义词：critic, objector, naysayer|反义词：supporter, advocate, proponent|词性:noun
词源:From the verb "deprecate" + "-or" (agent suffix). "Deprecate" comes from Latin "deprecatus," past participle of "deprecari," meaning "to pray against" or "to avert by prayer," from "de-" (down, away) + "precari" (to pray).
