intermodification:/ˌɪntərˌmɑːdɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/ | n. |相互修饰，互为修饰| The process by which two or more words modify each other. | Example: In the phrase "bright red," "bright" modifies "red" and "red" also implicitly modifies "bright," suggesting a specific kind of brightness.  红色鲜艳，“鲜艳”修饰“红色”，“红色”也含蓄地修饰“鲜艳”，暗示一种特定类型的鲜艳。| Synonym: reciprocal modification, mutual modification | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but consider "independent modification") | Noun
