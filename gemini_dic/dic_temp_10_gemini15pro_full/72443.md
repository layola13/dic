fluke:/fluːk/| n.|侥幸；意外的成功；意外的挫折；意外；吸虫|An unlikely chance occurrence, especially a surprising piece of luck.  A stroke of luck, a surprising bit of good fortune. A parasitic flatworm, especially one that infects the livers of vertebrates.|例句：He passed the exam by a fluke. 他侥幸通过了考试。

例句：Catching such a big fish was a real fluke. 钓到这么大一条鱼真是意外的收获。|近义词：accident, chance, coincidence, stroke of luck, windfall|反义词：intention, plan, design|词性:noun


fluke:/fluːk/| v.|侥幸成功；意外发生|To achieve or gain something by luck rather than skill or planning.|例句：He fluked a hole-in-one on the seventh. 他在第七洞侥幸打出一杆进洞。

例句：She fluked the exam despite not studying. 她虽然没有学习，却侥幸通过了考试。|近义词：chance, luck out|反义词：plan, intend|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
Possibly from Old Norse *flōki, a flatfish, from Proto-Germanic *flaukiz.  Related to Old English flōc (flounder). The sense of “accidental stroke of luck” likely arose from the idea of a lucky catch in fishing.
