notidanian: /ˌnəʊtɪˈdeɪniən/ | adj. n. | 六鳃鲨目的，六鳃鲨科的 | Relating to or denoting a shark of the order Hexanchiformes, which includes the cow sharks and sixgill sharks. These sharks are characterized by having six or seven gill slits, unlike most other sharks which have five. |  Example: The notidanian shark is an ancient species. 六鳃鲨是一种古老的物种。| Example:  Notidanian sharks are found in deep ocean waters. 六鳃鲨生活在深海中。 | 近义词:  cow shark, sixgill shark (these are more specific examples within the order) | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it refers to a specific taxonomic group.  Could possibly contrast with "modern shark" in some contexts) | 词性: adjective, noun


Etymology: From  *Notidanus*, the genus name (now *Hexanchus*) + -ian (suffix forming adjectives and nouns). *Notidanus* comes from Greek *nōton* (back) + *idanus* (tooth-like),  referring to the shape of the teeth.
