old-womanism:/ˌoʊld ˈwʊmənɪzəm/ | n. | 老妪主义，女性长老主义 | A term coined by Alice Walker to describe the wisdom, strength, and influence of older Black women, particularly in their communities and families.  It emphasizes their role as mentors, leaders, and sources of cultural knowledge, often drawing on African and African American traditions. | Example: The concept of old-womanism recognizes the valuable contributions of elder Black women.  老妪主义的概念认可了年长黑人女性的宝贵贡献。

Example: Her grandmother, a strong advocate for social justice, embodied the principles of old-womanism. 她的祖母是一位坚定的社会正义倡导者，体现了老妪主义的原则。 | 近义词: womanism, feminism, matriarchy | 反义词: patriarchy | 词性: noun
