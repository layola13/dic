melt:/mɛlt/ | v. n. | 融化，熔化；感动，软化 | to change from a solid to a liquid state, usually by heat; to become softened in feeling | The snow began to melt as the sun came out.  太阳出来后雪开始融化。| liquefy, dissolve, thaw | freeze, solidify | verb
melt:/mɛlt/ | n. | 融化；熔化物 | the process of changing from a solid to a liquid; the substance that results from melting | The melt from the glaciers formed a river. 冰川融化形成了一条河。| liquefaction, thawing | freezing, solidification | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *meltan (attested only in the past participle molten), from Proto-Germanic *meltaną (source also of Old Norse melta "to melt, digest," Old Frisian melta, Middle Dutch smelten, Dutch smelten, Old High German smelzan, German schmelzen), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)meld- "to render liquid, soft."
