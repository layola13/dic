hedonist:/ˈhiːdənɪst/ | n. |享乐主义者；快乐主义者|A person who believes that pleasure is the most important thing in life. |例句：He is a hedonist who lives for pleasure. 他是一个为了享乐而活的享乐主义者。

例句：She was a hedonist at heart, always seeking new experiences. 她骨子里是个享乐主义者，总是寻求新的体验。|近义词：pleasure-seeker, sensualist, epicurean, sybarite |反义词：ascetic, puritan |词性:noun

hedonistic:/ˌhiːdəˈnɪstɪk/ | adj. |享乐主义的；快乐主义的|Characterized by the pursuit of pleasure; sensually self-indulgent. |例句：They lead a hedonistic lifestyle, filled with parties and expensive vacations. 他们过着享乐主义的生活方式，充满了派对和昂贵的假期。

例句：His hedonistic tendencies led him to make some poor choices. 他的享乐主义倾向导致他做出了一些糟糕的选择。|近义词：pleasure-seeking, self-indulgent, luxurious, decadent |反义词：ascetic, austere |词性:adjective

hedonism:/ˈhiːdənɪzəm/ | n. |享乐主义；快乐主义|The ethical theory that pleasure (in the sense of the satisfaction of desires) is the highest good and proper aim of human life. |例句：Hedonism is a philosophy that emphasizes the pursuit of pleasure. 享乐主义是一种强调追求快乐的哲学。

例句：The book explored the dangers of unchecked hedonism. 这本书探讨了无节制享乐主义的危险。|近义词：pleasure-seeking, sensuality, epicureanism |反义词：asceticism, puritanism |词性:noun


词源: From Greek *hēdonē* "pleasure"
