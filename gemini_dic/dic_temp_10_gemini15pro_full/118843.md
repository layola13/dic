metaphors:/ˈmɛtəfɔːrz/ (US), /ˈmɛtəfɔːz/ (UK)| n.| 比喻; 隐喻 |A figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable.  A comparison without using like or as.|例句： "The snow is a white blanket." (雪是白色的毯子。)  This metaphor compares snow to a blanket, highlighting its covering quality.|近义词：figures of speech, analogies, similes, comparisons|反义词：literal language|词性: noun
