congregativeness:/kənˌɡrɛɡəˈtɪvnəs/| n. |群居性，聚集性|The tendency to congregate or assemble; the quality of being congregative.  The congregativeness of the species is evident in their large flocks.|例句：The congregativeness of starlings is remarkable, forming massive murmurations in the sky.  椋鸟的群居性非常显著，它们会在天空中形成巨大的鸟群。

例句：The congregativeness of the monks was reflected in their communal living arrangements.  僧侣们的群居性体现在他们的集体生活安排中。|近义词：sociability, gregariousness|反义词：solitariness, isolation|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From congregative + -ness.  Congregative from Latin congregātus, past participle of congregāre  "to flock together," from com- "together" + greg- (stem of grex "flock") + -āre (frequentative suffix).
