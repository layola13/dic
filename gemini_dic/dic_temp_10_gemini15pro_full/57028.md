doublespeak:/ˈdʌbəlˌspiːk/| n.|双重语言，模棱两可的话|Language deliberately used to disguise, distort, or reverse the meaning of what is being said.  It is often used by governments and organizations to make unpleasant or controversial actions sound acceptable.|The government used doublespeak to describe the invasion as a "liberation." 政府用双关语将入侵描述为“解放”。|synonyms: euphemism, jargon, gobbledygook, ambiguity, equivocation|antonyms: plain speaking, clarity, straightforwardness|noun
