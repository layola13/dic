impressionabilities:/ɪmˌprɛʃənəˈbɪlɪtiz/| n.|易受影响性，可塑性|The quality of being easily impressed or influenced. This refers to the susceptibility of someone, especially a young person, to new ideas and influences.|Her impressionabilities made her vulnerable to peer pressure. 她的易受影响性使她容易受到同伴的压力。|近义词：suggestibility, plasticity, receptiveness|反义词：resistance, imperviousness|词性: Noun (plural)

Etymology:
From impressionable + -ity.  Impressionable comes from French impressionnable, from impressionner "to impress," from Latin impress-, past participle stem of imprimere "to press into, imprint," from in- "in" + premere "to press."
