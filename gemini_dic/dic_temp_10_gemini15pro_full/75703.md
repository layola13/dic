full-blooded:/ˌfʊlˈblʌdɪd/ | adj. | 纯血统的；血气方刚的；精力充沛的 | Of unmixed ancestry; vigorous and hearty. |  He was a full-blooded Cherokee Indian. 他是一个纯血统的切罗基印第安人。|  She threw herself into the project with full-blooded enthusiasm. 她以饱满的热情投入到这个项目中。 | Synonyms: thoroughbred, purebred, spirited, passionate | Antonyms: half-blooded, mixed-breed, apathetic, listless | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From "full" + "blooded". "Full" meaning complete or total, and "blooded" referring to bloodline or ancestry, implying pure lineage.  Later extended to describe someone with vigorous energy and passion, as if having "full blood" circulating.
