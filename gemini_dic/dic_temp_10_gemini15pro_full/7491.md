anlace: /ˈænlɪs/ | n. |  短剑，匕首 | A medieval dagger with a broad, two-edged blade. |  The knight drew his anlace and prepared for close combat.  骑士拔出匕首，准备近身搏斗。 |  dagger, knife | sword, longsword | noun

Etymology:
From Old French *anlas, probably from Anglo-Norman *anelas, from Old English æt þǣm læsan "at the leash," referring to the carrying of the knife at the side, attached to a leash.
