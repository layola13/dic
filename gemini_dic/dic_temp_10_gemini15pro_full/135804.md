nonprolixities:/ˌnɒnprəˈlɪksɪtiz/
| n. | 简洁，简练 |  The nonprolixity of his writing style made it easy to understand.  His speeches were remarkable for their nonprolixities, conveying complex ideas with remarkable conciseness. |  The quality or state of being concise and not wordy; briefness. The nonprolixities of the legal document were appreciated by all parties involved.  It is a welcome change to encounter such nonprolixities in academic writing. | 近义词：brevity, conciseness, terseness | 反义词：prolixity, verbosity, wordiness | 词性: noun (plural)

词源:
From non- + prolixity.  Prolixity comes from Latin *prolixitas, from prolixus "extended, long," from pro- "forth" + -lixus (akin to liquere "to be fluid").
