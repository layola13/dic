fasted:/ˈfɑːstɪd/ |v. adj.|禁食的，斋戒的；|Having abstained from food or drink, typically for religious or health reasons. |例句：She fasted for three days as part of a religious observance. 她为了宗教仪式禁食了三天。

例句：He fasted before his medical procedure. 他在医疗手术前禁食了。|近义词：abstained, refrained|反义词：feasted, gorged|词性:动词(过去式/过去分词), 形容词

词源: From Old English *fæstan "to fast," from Proto-Germanic *fastanan, from *fastu- "firm, fixed." Related to fast (adj.). The original sense was "to hold firmly, to keep, observe," later "to abstain from food."
