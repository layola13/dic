counteract:/ˌkaʊntərˈækt/ | v. | 抵消，中和，对抗 | to act against something in order to reduce its force or neutralize it. |  Drinking plenty of water counteracts the dehydrating effects of the medication. 多喝水可以抵消药物的脱水作用。 | Synonyms: neutralize, offset, negate, thwart | Antonyms: enhance, augment, reinforce, aid | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the late 16th century.  From counter- + act.  "Counter-" signifies "against" or "opposite." "Act" refers to the action taken.  Therefore, "counteract" literally means to "act against."
