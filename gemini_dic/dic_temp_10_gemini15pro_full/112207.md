loopholed: /ˈluːphəʊld/ (UK) /ˈluːphoʊld/ (US) | adj. |有漏洞的，钻空子的 | Having a loophole or loopholes; characterized by loopholes.  A loophole is an ambiguity or inadequacy in the law or a set of rules. |  The contract was loopholed, allowing the company to avoid paying taxes.  合同有漏洞，允许公司避免缴税。 | Synonyms: flawed, defective, exploitable | Antonyms: airtight, sound, impeccable | adjective

loophole: /ˈluːphəʊl/ (UK) /ˈluːphoʊld/ (US) | n. |漏洞，空子 | An ambiguity or inadequacy in the law or a set of rules. |  They found a loophole in the tax law. 他们在税法中找到了一个漏洞。 | Synonyms: escape clause, technicality, ambiguity | Antonyms: closure, clarity, precision | noun
