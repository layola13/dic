fast-tracking:/ˌfæst ˈtrækɪŋ/ | n. |快速通道，快速处理|The process of accelerating a project or task by overlapping phases that would normally be sequential.  This often involves taking on more risk.|The project was put on a fast track to meet the deadline.  这个项目被快速推进以赶上最后期限。|Synonyms: expediting, accelerating, rushing |Antonyms: delaying, slowing down |Noun

fast-track:/ˈfæst træk/ | v. |快速推进，使…加快进展|To accelerate the progress of something, typically a project or career.|The company fast-tracked the development of the new drug. 公司快速推进了新药的研发。|Synonyms: expedite, accelerate, rush |Antonyms: delay, slow down |Verb

fast-tracked:/ˌfæst ˈtrækt/ | adj. |快速处理的，快速晋升的|Having been accelerated or expedited.|The fast-tracked project was completed in record time.  这个快速处理的项目在创纪录的时间内完成了。|Synonyms: expedited, accelerated, rushed |Antonyms: delayed, slowed |Adjective



ETYMOLOGY->
"Fast track" originated in American English in the mid-20th century, combining the words "fast" (meaning quick) and "track" (referring to a course or path). The metaphorical sense of a quicker route or process developed from the literal sense of a designated lane for faster movement.
