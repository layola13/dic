euphuism:/ˈjuːfjuːɪzəm/
n.
 Euphuism, an elaborately ornate style of writing or speaking
A high-flown, affected, or ornate style of writing or speaking, characterized by antithesis, alliteration, and frequent use of similes drawn from mythology and nature.  It takes its name from John Lyly's *Euphues* (1579).
例句：The writer's prose was filled with euphuism, making it difficult to understand.  这位作家的散文充满了华丽的辞藻，令人难以理解。

例句：Her euphuism made her sound pretentious rather than intelligent. 她的浮夸的语言让她听起来很做作，而不是聪明。

近义词：ornateness, elaborateness, grandiloquence, bombast
反义词：plainness, simplicity, directness
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *euphuia* (eloquence), from Greek *euphuia* (grace of language, elegance of style), from *euphues* (well-grown, shapely, witty), from *eu* (well) + *phuein* (to grow, bring forth).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *bʰuH- (to become, exist, grow).
