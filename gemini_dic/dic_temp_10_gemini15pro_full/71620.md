flatfishes:/ˈflætˌfɪʃɪz/| n. |比目鱼，扁平鱼|A general term for a variety of fish species characterized by their flattened bodies, asymmetrical eyes, and bottom-dwelling habits.  They typically lie on one side of their body, often camouflaged to match the seabed.|Flatfishes are often found camouflaged on the ocean floor. 比目鱼经常伪装在海底。|synonyms:  flounders, soles, plaice|antonyms:  roundfish|noun


flatfish:/ˈflætˌfɪʃ/| n. |比目鱼，扁平鱼|A single fish belonging to the flatfish group.|The fisherman caught a large flatfish. 渔夫捕到了一条大比目鱼。|synonyms:  flounder, sole, plaice|antonyms:  roundfish|noun
