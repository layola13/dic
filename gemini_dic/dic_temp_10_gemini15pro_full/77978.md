gentil:/ʒɑ̃ti/ (French), /dʒɛnˈtiːl/ (Anglicized)| adj.|亲切的，和蔼的，有礼貌的|Kind, courteous, polite; of noble rank or birth (archaic).  Originates from Old French and ultimately Latin "gentilis," meaning "of the same clan or family."  In modern French, it primarily means "kind" or "nice." The Anglicized pronunciation is less common and retains a somewhat archaic meaning related to nobility.|She gave me a gentil smile. 她给了我一个亲切的微笑。|He had a gentil manner about him, always opening doors for others. 他举止彬彬有礼，总是为别人开门。|Synonyms: kind, courteous, amiable, polite, gracious, affable|Antonyms: rude, unkind, impolite, discourteous, churlish|adjective
