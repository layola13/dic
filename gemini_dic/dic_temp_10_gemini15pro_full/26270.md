buttonholer: /ˈbʌtnˌhoʊlər/ (US), /ˈbʌtnˌhəʊlə(r)/ (UK) | n. | 扣眼机；强留别人说话的人 | A person who detains someone in conversation, especially against their will.  A machine for making buttonholes. | He's a terrible buttonholer - I could hardly get away from him! 他真是个话痨，我几乎无法脱身！ | The tailor used a buttonholer to create perfect buttonholes on the jacket.  裁缝用扣眼机在夹克上开了完美的扣眼。 | Synonyms: bore, pest, chatterbox (for person); buttonhole machine (for machine) | Antonyms: listener (for person) | noun


buttonhole: /ˈbʌtnˌhoʊl/ (US), /ˈbʌtnˌhəʊl/ (UK) | v. |  用扣眼机开扣眼；强留别人说话 | To make buttonholes in (something); To detain (someone) in conversation, especially against their will. | He buttonholed me as I was leaving the meeting.  会议结束后，他把我拦住说话。 |  She buttonholed the fabric to create a neat edge. 她用扣眼机给布料锁边。 | Synonyms: accost, detain, corner (for detaining someone) | Antonyms: release, free (for detaining someone) | verb


Etymology: From "buttonhole" (n.) + "-er" (suffix forming agent nouns). The verb sense developed from the noun, referring to the act of using a buttonholer (machine) or the act of detaining someone as if holding onto their buttonhole.
