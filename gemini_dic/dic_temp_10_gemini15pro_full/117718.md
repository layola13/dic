melancholy: /ˈmɛlənkɒli/ (UK) /ˈmɛlənˌkɑːli/ (US)| n. | 忧郁，悲哀 | A state of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause. |  A sense of melancholy pervaded the room. 房间里弥漫着忧郁的气氛。| 近义词: sadness, dejection, gloom | 反义词: happiness, joy, cheerfulness | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *melancolie*, from Late Latin melancholia, from Greek melankholía, from melas (black) + kholé (bile).  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholy.
