low-level:/ˈloʊˌlɛvəl/| adj.|低级的，底层的，基础的|Relating to the basic or fundamental operations of a computer or system, often involving direct interaction with hardware or software at a detailed level; simple and unsophisticated.|Example: Low-level programming languages are closer to machine code. 低级编程语言更接近机器代码。|Example:  He started with a low-level job in the company. 他在公司从基层工作做起。|近义词: basic, fundamental, elementary, rudimentary, simple|反义词: high-level, advanced, sophisticated|词性:形容词
low level:/ˈloʊ ˌlɛvəl/| n.|低水平，低层次|A low or basic position or rank within an organization or hierarchy.|Example: The talks broke down at a low level.  会谈在低级别破裂了。|Example:  Air pollution remains at a low level. 空气污染仍然处于低水平。|近义词: bottom, base|反义词: top, high level|词性:名词
