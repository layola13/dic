cured:/kjʊərd/ | adj. | 治愈的；腌制的；矫正的 |  freed from a disease or illness; preserved by drying, salting, smoking, or other methods | The doctor confirmed that the patient was completely cured. 医生确认病人已完全康复。 | synonyms: healed, recovered, remedied, preserved | antonyms: sick, ill, diseased, raw, fresh | adjective

cured:/kjʊərd/ | v. | 治愈；腌制；矫正（cure的过去分词） | past participle of "cure" which means to restore to health or soundness; to preserve by drying, salting, smoking, etc.; to correct or remove a fault or defect | The bacon was cured with salt and smoke. 培根是用盐和烟熏制成的。 | synonyms: healed, recovered, remedied, preserved | antonyms: harmed, worsened, aggravated, spoiled | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *curen*, from Anglo-Norman *curer*, from Old French *curer*, from Latin *cūrāre* (“to take care of, trouble oneself about, attend to, heal”), from *cūra* (“care, attention, concern, anxiety, grief”).
