heterophoric:/ˌhɛtərəˈfɔːrɪk/ | adj. |斜视的，隐斜视的 | Relating to heterophoria, a condition in which the eyes have a tendency to deviate from their normal position for binocular vision, but are able to maintain alignment through the effort of the eye muscles. |  Example: The doctor diagnosed him with a heterophoric condition. 医生诊断他患有隐斜视。| Example: Heterophoric patients often experience eye strain and headaches. 隐斜视患者经常感到眼睛疲劳和头痛。 | Synonyms: strabismic, squinting | Antonyms: orthophoric | Adjective

Etymology:
From hetero- (Greek: different, other) + -phoria (Greek: carrying, bearing).  Relates to the eyes bearing or carrying themselves in different directions.
