confessionalist:/kənˈfɛʃənəlɪst/
adjective|忏悔的；告解的；教派主义的|Relating to or characteristic of a confession, especially a religious one; adhering to a particular confession of faith, often with strong emphasis on its distinctiveness and importance; relating to or advocating confessionalism (a system where different religious communities have official recognition and a degree of autonomy).|The confessionalist nature of the political system led to frequent tensions between different religious groups.|近义词：sectarian, denominational, dogmatic|反义词：secular, non-denominational, ecumenical|词性：adjective
confessionalist:/kənˈfɛʃənəlɪst/
noun|忏悔者；教派主义者|A person who adheres to a particular confession of faith, often with strong emphasis on its distinctiveness and importance; a person who advocates confessionalism.|The confessionalist argued that his religious community deserved greater autonomy.|近义词：sectarian, partisan|反义词：secularist, pluralist|词性：noun
