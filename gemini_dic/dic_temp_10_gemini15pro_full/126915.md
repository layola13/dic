nare:/ˈnɛər/ /ˈneɪr/ | n. |鼻孔|Nostril; either of the two external openings of the nasal cavity in vertebrates that admit air to the lungs and smells to the olfactory nerves. |The horse flared its nares. 马张大了鼻孔。|近义词:nostril |反义词: N/A |词性:名词


Etymology->
From Old English *nare, from Proto-Germanic *nasō, from Proto-Indo-European *nós-. Cognate with Dutch neer "nostril," German Nase "nose," Latin nāris "nostril," Greek ῥίς (rhis) "nose," Sanskrit नासिका (nāsikā) "nose."
