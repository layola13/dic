deconstructive:/ˌdɪːkənˈstrʌktɪv/ | adj. | 解构的，解构主义的 |  Characterized by deconstruction, a philosophical and critical movement that questions traditional assumptions about certainty, identity, and truth. It often involves analyzing texts to reveal their internal contradictions and hidden biases. |  Her deconstructive approach to literary criticism challenged established interpretations of the novel. 她对文学批评的解构方法挑战了对这部小说的既定解释。 | synonyms: analytical, critical, subversive, questioning | antonyms: constructive, synthetic, traditional, affirming | adjective

deconstruct:/ˌdɪːkənˈstrʌkt/ | vt. | 解构，拆解 | To analyze (a text or idea) by breaking it down into its constituent parts, especially to expose its underlying assumptions and contradictions.  | The professor deconstructed the poem, revealing its hidden meanings. 教授解构了这首诗，揭示了它隐藏的含义。 | synonyms: analyze, dissect, dismantle, unpack | antonyms: construct, synthesize, build, establish | verb
