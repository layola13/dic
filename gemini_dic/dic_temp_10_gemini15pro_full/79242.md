glass-jawed: /ˈɡlæs ˌdʒɔːd/ | adj. | 玻璃下巴的，意志薄弱的 | Easily knocked out or defeated, especially in boxing; figuratively, vulnerable to criticism or setbacks. |  He proved to be glass-jawed and was knocked out in the first round. 他被证明是个玻璃下巴，第一回合就被击倒了。|  The politician was glass-jawed and couldn't handle the media scrutiny. 这位政客意志薄弱，无法应对媒体的审查。 | 近义词: vulnerable, weak, fragile | 反义词: resilient, tough, strong | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
Compound of "glass" and "jawed."  "Glass" in this context refers to the fragility of glass.  "Jawed" refers to the jaw, a common target in boxing.  The term originated in boxing slang, describing a fighter who is easily knocked out by a blow to the jaw.
