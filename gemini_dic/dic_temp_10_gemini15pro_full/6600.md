anachronistic:/əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪk/ | adj. |时代错误的；年代失调的 | Belonging or appropriate to an earlier period than that in which it exists, especially so as to seem conspicuously old-fashioned. |  The writer included some deliberate anachronisms in the novel to create a sense of dislocation.  作者在小说中加入了一些故意的时代错误，以营造一种错位感。| anachronous, outmoded, outdated | contemporary, modern | adjective

anachronism:/əˈnækrəˌnɪzəm/ | n. |时代错误；年代失调 | A thing belonging or appropriate to a period other than that in which it exists, especially a thing that is conspicuously old-fashioned. | The inclusion of a cell phone in a movie set in the 1920s is a glaring anachronism.  在一部设定在20世纪20年代的电影中出现手机是一个明显的时代错误。 | archaism, incongruity, solecism |  modernity, contemporaneity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *anachronique*, from Ancient Greek *ἀνά* (aná, “up, against”) + *χρόνος* (khrónos, “time”).
