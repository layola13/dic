disenfranchise:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ | vt. |剥夺权利；使失去公民权；使失去选举权|To deprive someone of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.|例句：The new law disenfranchised many voters. 新法律剥夺了许多选民的权利。

例句：Felons are disenfranchised in many states. 在许多州，重罪犯被剥夺了选举权。|近义词：dispossess, deprive, strip|反义词：enfranchise, empower|词性：动词
