conspiring:/kənˈspaɪərɪŋ/
verb
密谋；共谋；协力
acting or working together secretly, especially to commit a crime or achieve something harmful or illegal.
They were accused of conspiring against the government. 他们被指控密谋反对政府。
Synonyms: plotting, scheming, intriguing, colluding
Antonyms: cooperating (for good), collaborating (for good)
verb

conspire:/kənˈspaɪər/
verb
密谋；共谋；协力
make secret plans jointly to commit an unlawful or harmful act.
They conspired to overthrow the government. 他们密谋推翻政府。
Synonyms: plot, scheme, intrigue, collude
Antonyms: cooperate (for good), collaborate (for good)
verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French conspirer, from Latin conspirare ‘agree, combine, plot’, from con- ‘together’ + spirare ‘breathe’.
