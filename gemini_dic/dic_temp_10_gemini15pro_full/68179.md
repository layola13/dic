fainting:/ˈfeɪntɪŋ/ | n. | 昏厥，晕倒 |A sudden and temporary loss of consciousness. | She felt a wave of nausea and then fainted. 她感到一阵恶心，然后晕倒了。|  synonym: syncope, blackout, swoon | antonym: consciousness, alertness | noun


faint:/feɪnt/ | adj. | 微弱的，模糊的，虚弱的 |Lacking brightness, clarity, or strength. | There was a faint smell of smoke in the air. 空气中有一股淡淡的烟味。| synonym: weak, dim, slight | antonym: strong, bright, clear | adjective

faint:/feɪnt/ | v. | 昏倒，晕倒 | To lose consciousness briefly. | He fainted from the heat. 他因高温而晕倒了。 | synonym: collapse, pass out, black out | antonym: revive, awaken | verb

Etymology: From Old French *feint, past participle of feindre "to feign, pretend," from Latin fingere "to shape, form, invent, devise, feign."  The sense of "lose consciousness" developed from the idea of "pretending to be dead."
