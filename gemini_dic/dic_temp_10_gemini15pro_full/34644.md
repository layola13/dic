clenches:/ˈklɛntʃɪz/| v.| 紧握，攥紧，咬紧（牙关）|He clenches his fists in anger. 他愤怒地攥紧了拳头。|近义词：grips, grasps, holds tightly|反义词:releases, loosens|verb (third-person singular simple present clenches, present participle clenching, simple past clenched, past participle clenched)

clenches:/ˈklɛntʃɪz/| n.| 紧握，咬紧|The clench of his jaw showed his determination. 他紧咬的下巴显示了他的决心。|近义词：grip, grasp|反义词:release|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English clenchen, from Old English *clencan (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *klankjaną (“to make clink, clang”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)klang- (“to resound, ring”). Cognate with Middle Dutch clenken (“to clench, rivet”), German klingen (“to sound, ring”).
