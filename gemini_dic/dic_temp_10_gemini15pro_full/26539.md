cabin:/ˈkæbɪn/ (US), /ˈkæbɪn/ (UK)| n.|小木屋，客舱，机舱|A small, simple dwelling, usually made of wood; a private room on a ship or aircraft.|例句：They rented a cozy cabin in the mountains for their vacation. 他们租了一间舒适的山间小屋度假。

例句：He booked a first-class cabin on the cruise ship. 他在游轮上预订了一个头等舱。|近义词：hut, cottage, shed, compartment|反义词:mansion, palace|词性:noun

cabin:/ˈkæbɪn/ (US), /ˈkæbɪn/ (UK)| v.|限制，禁闭|To confine or restrict.|例句：The passengers were cabined in their rooms during the storm.  暴风雨期间，乘客被限制在房间里。

例句：He felt cabined by his job. 他觉得他的工作束缚了他。|近义词：confine, restrict, imprison|反义词:liberate, free|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *cabine, from Old North French *cabine, from Late Latin *capanna (“hut, cabin”), from Latin capanna (“cart with a covered top”). Doublet of capanna.
