disturb:/dɪˈstɜːrb/ (US) /dɪˈstɜːb/ (UK)| v.|打扰，扰乱，使不安|To interrupt the quiet, peace, or order of something or someone.|例句：The loud noise disturbed my sleep. 吵闹的声音打扰了我的睡眠。|近义词：interrupt, bother, upset, disrupt|反义词：soothe, calm, comfort|verb

disturbed:/dɪˈstɜːbd/ (US) /dɪˈstɜːbd/ (UK)| adj.|不安的，心烦意乱的，精神错乱的|Mentally or emotionally unstable or upset.|例句：He seemed disturbed by the news. 他似乎被这个消息弄得很不安。|近义词：upset, troubled, agitated|反义词：calm, peaceful, serene|adjective

disturbance:/dɪˈstɜːrbəns/ (US) /dɪˈstɜːbəns/ (UK)| n.|骚乱，干扰，打扰|An interruption of peace, quiet, or order.|例句：There was a disturbance outside the building. 大楼外发生了骚乱。|近义词：interruption, disruption, commotion|反义词：peace, quiet, order|noun
