forestaff:/ˈfɔːrstæf/| n. |前杆，十字测天仪|A simple instrument used for taking the altitude of the sun or stars, consisting of a staff with a crosspiece that slides along it.|The mariner used a forestaff to determine the ship's latitude.|Near Synonyms: cross-staff, Jacob's staff, baculum|Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for a specific instrument. Could consider modern navigational tools as functional antonyms, e.g., sextant, GPS)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From fore- + staff. Fore meaning "front" or "anterior" and staff referring to the rod or stick.  The cross-piece was held "before" the eye.
