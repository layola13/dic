cross-gartering: /ˈkrɒs ˌɡɑːtərɪŋ/ | n. | 十字形袜带；(尤指男性)交叉绑腿 | The practice of wearing garters crossed below the knee, often associated with historical fashion or theatrical costumes. | He affected a flamboyant style, complete with cross-gartering and a feathered hat. 他刻意追求一种华丽的风格，包括交叉绑腿和羽毛帽子。 | 近义词: gartering | 反义词: None | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From "cross" + "gartering".  "Cross" indicating the crossed arrangement, and "gartering" referring to the act of wearing or fastening garters. Garter comes from Old North French "gartier" from "garret," meaning "bend of the knee."
