crengle:/ˈkrɛŋɡəl/ | n. | 环;圈;绳环 | A loop or eye of rope, especially one spliced into the bolt-rope of a sail for fastening it to a stay, mast, or spar. |  The sail was ripped, tearing the crengle from the mast.  帆被撕裂了，绳环从桅杆上扯了下来。|Synonyms: loop, eye, grommet | Antonyms: knot (loosely) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *crengyll, possibly related to Old Norse *kringla (“circle, ring”), from Proto-Germanic *kringlō (“circle, ring”). Cognate with Icelandic kringla (“circle, ring, disc, globe”).
