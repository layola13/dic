fichu:/ˈfiːʃuː/ | n. | 三角形披肩，围巾 | A lace or muslin scarf worn by women to fill in the low necklines of dresses. | She wore a delicate fichu to complement her gown. 她戴着一条精致的三角形披肩来搭配她的礼服。 |  Synonyms: scarf, shawl | Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps bare neck/chest) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fichu*, past participle of *ficher* "to fix, stick, drive in," from Vulgar Latin *ficcare*, from Latin *figere* "to fix, fasten."  Originally referred to the way the scarf was "fixed" or pinned in place.
