back-yonder:/ˌbæk ˈjɑːndər/ | adj.| 遥远的，远处的 | Located far away, especially in a rural or isolated area.  It often carries a connotation of nostalgia or a sense of being old-fashioned or quaint.|  He grew up in a back-yonder town in the Appalachian Mountains. 他在阿巴拉契亚山脉的一个偏远小镇长大。 | synonyms: remote, distant, far-flung, out-of-the-way, backwoods | antonyms: nearby, close, adjacent | 词性: adjective

Etymology:  "Back" meaning far away and "yonder" meaning "over there," both from Old English.
