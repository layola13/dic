cabinets:/ˈkæbɪnɪts/| n.|橱柜；内阁|A cabinet is a piece of furniture with shelves and doors used for storing things.  A cabinet can also refer to a group of advisors to a head of state.|例句：The dishes are kept in the kitchen cabinets.  盘子放在厨房的橱柜里。

例句：The Prime Minister and his cabinet discussed the new policy. 首相和他的内阁讨论了这项新政策。|近义词：cupboard, closet, locker|反义词: (No direct antonym)|noun


cabinet:/ˈkæbɪnɪt/ | n. | 橱柜；内阁 | A cabinet is a cupboard-like piece of furniture used for storing items. It can also refer to a group of advisors to a head of state.| 例句：She keeps her china in a glass-fronted cabinet. 她把瓷器放在一个玻璃门的橱柜里。

例句：The cabinet met to discuss the budget. 内阁开会讨论预算。| 近义词：cupboard, closet (for furniture); council, ministry (for government) | 反义词：None (for furniture); opposition (loosely, for government) | noun
