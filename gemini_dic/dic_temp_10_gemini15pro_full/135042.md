nonnoble:/nɒnˈnəʊbəl/ | adj. | 非贵族的，卑贱的 | Not noble; not of high social rank; common; base. |  The nonnoble classes often struggled for basic rights. 非贵族阶级经常为基本权利而斗争。|  The play explored the lives of nonnoble citizens in ancient Rome.  这出戏探讨了古罗马非贵族公民的生活。| 近义词: common, base, ignoble, plebeian | 反义词: noble, aristocratic, highborn | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From non- + noble. "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Noble" comes from Old French, ultimately from Latin "nobilis" meaning "knowable, well-known, illustrious."  Therefore, "nonnoble" literally means "not well-known" or "not illustrious."
