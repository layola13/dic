anachronic:/əˈnækrənɪk/ | adj. | 过时的，时代错误的，年代失调的 | Belonging or appropriate to an earlier period, especially so as to seem conspicuously old-fashioned. |  The writer included some deliberate anachronisms in his historical novel to add a touch of humor.  作者在他的历史小说中故意加入了一些时代错误的元素，以增加一些幽默感。 | Example: The use of a cell phone in a movie set in ancient Rome is a glaring anachronism.  例如，在一部以古罗马为背景的电影中使用手机是一个明显的时代错误。 | Synonyms: outdated, antiquated, archaic, obsolete | Antonyms: modern, contemporary, current | adjective

anachronism:/əˈnækrənɪzəm/ | n. | 时代错误，过时的事物 | A thing belonging or appropriate to a period other than that in which it exists, especially a thing that is conspicuously old-fashioned. | The play was full of anachronisms, like characters using modern slang. 这部戏剧充满了时代错误，比如人物使用现代俚语。 | Example:  The inclusion of a computer in a story set in the Middle Ages is an anachronism.  在一个发生在中世纪的故事中加入电脑是一个时代错误。 | Synonyms:  misplacement,  incongruity,  solecism | Antonyms: contemporaneity | noun
