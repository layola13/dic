over-hard:/ˈoʊvərˌhɑːrd/| adj.|煎得过老的（通常指鸡蛋）|Cooked until the yolk is solid, usually referring to eggs.  The white is fully cooked and firm, and the yolk is cooked through and solid, not runny.|例句：I ordered my eggs over-hard. 我点的鸡蛋要煎得老一点。

|例句：She prefers her eggs over-hard, with no runny yolk. 她喜欢吃煎得老一点的鸡蛋，蛋黄不能流黄。|近义词：well-done, hard-cooked, hard-boiled (for whole eggs)|反义词: over-easy, sunny-side up, soft-boiled|词性:形容词
词源: "over-" (prefix indicating excessive or thorough action) + "hard" (adjective indicating firmness or solidity). This compound describes the extent of cooking, specifically to a hard consistency.
