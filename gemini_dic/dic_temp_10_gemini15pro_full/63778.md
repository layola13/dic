epicureanism: /ˌɛpɪkjʊˈriːəˌnɪzəm/ | n. | 享乐主义; 伊壁鸠鲁哲学 |  A philosophy that holds that pleasure is the chief good.  It emphasizes not only sensual pleasures but also mental and spiritual enjoyments, advocating for a life of moderation and tranquility, free from pain and fear. |  He lived a life of epicureanism, indulging in fine food and wine. 他过着享乐主义的生活，沉迷于美食和美酒。|  The restaurant catered to epicureanism, offering a menu of exquisite dishes. 这家餐厅迎合享乐主义，提供一份精致的菜肴菜单。| 近义词: hedonism, sensualism, indulgence | 反义词: asceticism, self-denial, abstinence | 词性: noun
Etymology: From Late Latin *Epicurēus, from Greek Epikouros (Ἐπίκουρος), the philosopher Epicurus + -ism.
