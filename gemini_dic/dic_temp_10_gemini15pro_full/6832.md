anastomose:/əˈnæstəˌmoʊz/ | v. | 吻合，连接 | To surgically connect two tubular structures, such as blood vessels or intestines.  | The surgeon anastomosed the two sections of the intestine.  外科医生将两段肠吻合在一起。 | connect, join, unite | separate, divide | verb

anastomosis:/əˌnæstəˈmoʊsɪs/ | n. | 吻合，连接，吻合口 | A surgical connection between two tubular structures, such as blood vessels or intestines. | The surgeon created an anastomosis between the artery and the vein.  外科医生在动脉和静脉之间建立了一个吻合口。 | connection, junction, union | separation, division | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *anastōmōsis*, from Ancient Greek ἀναστόμωσις (anastómōsis, “outlet, opening, mouth”), from ἀναστομόω (anastomóō, “to furnish with a mouth, to open”), from ἀνά (aná, “up, upon, throughout”) + στόμα (stóma, “mouth”).
