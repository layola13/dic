impeach:/ɪmˈpiːtʃ/ | v. | 弹劾，控告 | To charge (a public official) with misconduct in office. |  The House of Representatives voted to impeach the president. 众议院投票弹劾总统。| synonyms: accuse, indict, charge | antonyms: exonerate, acquit | verb

impeached:/ɪmˈpiːtʃt/ | adj. | 被弹劾的 | Having been formally accused of misconduct in office. | The impeached governor resigned from office. 被弹劾的州长辞职了。| synonyms: accused, indicted, charged | antonyms: exonerated, acquitted | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *empeechier, from em- (variant of en- 'in, into') + pechier 'to hinder, entrap', from Latin impedicare 'to shackle the feet of', from in- 'in' + pedica 'fetter, shackle'.
