augustan-age:/ɔːˈɡʌstən eɪdʒ/ | n. | 奥古斯都时代 |  The period of Roman literature during the reign of the Emperor Augustus (27 BC – AD 14), marked by a flourishing of literature and the arts, particularly under the patronage of Maecenas. This is often considered the Golden Age of Roman literature. |  The Augustan age saw the rise of great poets like Virgil and Ovid. 奥古斯都时代见证了维吉尔和奥维德等伟大诗人的崛起。 | Golden age, classical period | Silver age, Dark Ages | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Augustus, the first Roman emperor, + age.  The term is modeled on the "Golden Age" of Pericles in Athens and signifies a period of great cultural achievement.
