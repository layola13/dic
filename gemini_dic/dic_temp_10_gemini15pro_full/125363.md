multiloquy: /ˌmʌltɪˈlɒkwi/ /ˌmʌltiˈlɑːkwi/
| n. |冗长的讲话，长篇大论|A long speech by one person, especially one containing too many words and not enough action.  A prolonged, tedious speech or discussion, often by one person dominating a conversation.|The professor launched into a multiloquy on the history of philosophy, leaving the students bewildered.|近义词：monologue, soliloquy, harangue, tirade, diatribe|反义词: dialogue, conversation|词性: noun

Etymology: From Latin *multiloquium*, from *multus* ("much, many") + *loqui* ("to speak").
