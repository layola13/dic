chalder:/ˈtʃɔːldər/| n.|一查尔德（旧时干量单位，通常指煤炭，等于36蒲式耳）|A chalder was a dry measure, typically for coal, equal to 36 bushels.|The coal merchant delivered a chalder of coal to the house.  煤炭商向这所房子运送了一查尔德的煤。|近义词:bushel (although a smaller unit), measure|反义词:None (as it's a unit of measurement)|词性:名词

chalder:/ˈtʃɔːldər/| n.|大量；许多（非正式）|A large quantity or abundance of something (informal usage).  This meaning is less common.|He had a chalder of money. 他有很多钱。|近义词:abundance, lot, heap, pile|反义词:lack, scarcity|词性:名词

词源:
From Old English *cealdor, from Proto-Germanic *kaldrą (“cold, cool”), from Proto-Indo-European *gel- (“cold”).  Originally referring to a measure of cold things, such as fish. Later generalized to other dry goods, especially coal.
