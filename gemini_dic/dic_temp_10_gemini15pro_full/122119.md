mistral:/ˈmɪstrəl, mɪˈstrɑːl/| n. |密史脱拉风；西北风|A strong, cold, dry, northwesterly wind that blows through the Rhône valley and southern France to the Mediterranean coast, especially in winter.|The mistral whipped through the valley, carrying dust and leaves. 密史脱拉风掠过山谷，卷起尘土和树叶。|近义词：northwester, wind, gale|反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑 southerly wind 南风)|词性:noun
Etymology: From Provençal *mistral, from Late Latin *magistralis (“masterful”), from Latin magister (“master”). The sense development is from "master wind" to "northwest wind".
