lean-on: /ˈliːn ɒn/ (British) /ˈliːn ɑːn/ (American)| phrasal verb |依靠，依赖；精神支柱|To depend on someone or something for support, especially emotional support.  To use something or someone as a crutch.|例句：He leans on his family for emotional support. 他在情感上依赖他的家人。

例句：She leans on her faith during difficult times.  她在困境中依靠她的信仰。|近义词：depend on, rely on, count on|反义词：be independent, be self-sufficient|词性：phrasal verb

lean-on: /ˈliːn ɒn/ (British) /ˈliːn ɑːn/ (American)| noun |精神支柱；依靠的对象|Someone or something that a person depends on for emotional support.|例句：Her best friend is her main lean-on. 她最好的朋友是她的主要精神支柱。

例句：His faith has always been a lean-on for him. 他的信仰一直是他的精神支柱。|近义词：support, rock, anchor|反义词：burden|词性：noun
