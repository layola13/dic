exonerative:/ɪɡˈzɑːnəreɪtɪv/
adjective
免罪的，免责的
tending or serving to exonerate;
例句：The lawyer presented exonerative evidence that proved his client's innocence. 律师出示了免罪的证据，证明了他的当事人的清白。

例句：The report was exonerative, clearing the company of any wrongdoing.  这份报告是免罪的，洗清了该公司的不法行为。
近义词：absolving, clearing, vindicating, exculpatory
反义词：incriminating, accusatory, condemning
词性: adjective

exonerate:/ɪɡˈzɑːnəreɪt/
verb
免除责任；宣布无罪
(especially of an official body) absolve (someone) from blame for a fault or wrongdoing, especially after due consideration of the case.
例句：The jury exonerated the defendant. 陪审团宣布被告无罪。

例句：The investigation exonerated the mayor from any involvement in the scandal. 调查免除了市长与丑闻的任何牵连。
近义词：absolve, acquit, clear, vindicate, exculpate
反义词：incriminate, accuse, condemn, convict
词性: verb

exoneration:/ɪɡˌzɑːnəˈreɪʃn/
noun
免罪；免责
the action of officially absolving someone from blame; vindication.
例句：The lawyer sought the exoneration of his client. 律师寻求为他委托人免罪。

例句：His exoneration came after years of wrongful imprisonment. 多年冤狱之后，他终于得到了昭雪。
近义词：absolution, acquittal, clearance, vindication, exculpation
反义词：incrimination, accusation, condemnation, conviction
词性: noun
