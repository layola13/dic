flinch:/flɪntʃ/ | v. | 畏缩，退缩 | To make a sudden, small movement because of pain or fear. | He flinched when the doctor touched his wound. 他在医生碰到他的伤口时畏缩了一下。 |  | wince, recoil, shrink | stand firm, brave | verb

flinch:/flɪntʃ/ | n. | 畏缩，退缩（的动作） | A sudden, small movement because of pain or fear. |  His flinch betrayed his nervousness. 他的畏缩暴露了他的紧张。 | wince, recoil |  stand, firmness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *flenchir*, a variant of *flanchir* "to bend, turn aside," from Frankish *flankōn* "to bend" (compare Old High German *hlanc* "limp, flabby"), from Proto-Germanic *hlankiz* "flexible," from PIE *kl̥eng- "to bend, turn, curve."
