accomplicity: /əkəmˈplɪsɪti/
n.
共谋，同谋，串通
The act of helping someone else commit a crime or do something wrong.  Involvement with others in an illegal activity or wrongdoing.

He was charged with aiding and abetting the robbers and complicity in the robbery.
他被控协助和教唆抢劫犯以及参与抢劫。

There's no proof of her complicity in the murder.
没有证据证明她参与了谋杀。

近义词: collusion, connivance, conspiracy, involvement
反义词: innocence, non-involvement
词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *complicité*, from Late Latin *complicitatem*, from Latin *complicem* ("accomplice"), from *com-* ("with") + *plicāre* ("to fold").  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*pleḱ-* ("to plait").
