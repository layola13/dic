freemason:/ˈfriːmeɪsən/| n. |共济会会员；石匠| A member of a secret society, often involved in charitable work, with rituals and symbols derived from stonemasonry. | My grandfather was a freemason. 我的祖父是一名共济会会员。 | He became a freemason to network with other professionals. 他成为共济会会员是为了与其他专业人士建立联系。|Synonyms: Mason| Antonyms: None | Noun



ETYMOLOGY->

From "free" (referring to the medieval practice of allowing skilled masons to travel freely) + "mason" (a stoneworker). The organization evolved from guilds of stonemasons in the Middle Ages.
