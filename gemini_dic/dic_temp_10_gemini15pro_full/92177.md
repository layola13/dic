hyperspeculativeness:/ˌhaɪpərˌspɛkjʊlətɪvnəs/
| n. |过度推测，过度臆测|The state or quality of being excessively speculative; characterized by conjecture or hypothesis based on little or no evidence.|His hyperspeculativeness about the future of the company made his colleagues uneasy. 他对公司未来的过度推测让他的同事们感到不安。|Near Synonyms:  conjecturalness, supposition, speculation, theorizing|Antonyms:  certainty, factuality, pragmatism|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
hyper- (prefix): from Greek, meaning "over, above, beyond"
speculative (adjective): from Latin speculativus, from speculatus, past participle of speculari "to look at, observe, examine, spy out," from specula "watchtower, lookout," from specere "to look at."
-ness (suffix): forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality
