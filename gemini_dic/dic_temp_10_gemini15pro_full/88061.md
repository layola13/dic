hijacking:/ˈhaɪdʒækɪŋ/
n.
劫持, 绑架
The act of illegally seizing an aircraft, ship, or vehicle while in transit and forcing it to go to a different destination or using it for one's own purposes.
The hijacking of the plane ended tragically.  这架飞机的劫持以悲剧告终。
近义词：seizure, commandeering
反义词：release, liberation
词性：noun

hijack:/ˈhaɪdʒæk/
v.
劫持, 绑架
To illegally seize (an aircraft, ship, or vehicle) while in transit and force it to go to a different destination or use it for one's own purposes.
The terrorists hijacked the plane and demanded a ransom. 恐怖分子劫持了飞机并索要赎金。
近义词：seize, commandeer
反义词：release, surrender
词性：verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "high jack," likely influenced by "highwayman."  "High jack" originally meant to rob someone at gunpoint, especially on a highway. This term later evolved to refer to the seizing of vehicles, including aircraft.
