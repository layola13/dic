hearkeners:/ˈhɑːrkənərz/ | n. |倾听者；听众|People who listen attentively.  Those who heed a message or call.|例句：The hearkeners gathered around the storyteller, captivated by his tales. 倾听者聚集在讲故事的人周围，被他讲的故事迷住了。

例句：The prophet's words resonated with the hearkeners. 先知的话引起了听众的共鸣。|近义词：listeners, audience, followers|反义词：speakers, talkers, ignorers|词性:Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From hearken + -er. Hearken comes from Middle English *herknen*, from Old English *heorcn(i)an*, from Proto-Germanic *hauzijan-*.  Related to hear. The suffix *-er* denotes someone who performs the action of the verb.
