dehortation:/ˌdiːhɔːrˈteɪʃn/ | n. |劝阻，告诫，阻止|The act of dissuading or attempting to dissuade someone from a course of action.|His dehortation from joining the gang was unsuccessful. 他劝阻我不要加入帮派，但没有成功。|近义词：dissuasion, admonition, caution, warning|反义词：persuasion, encouragement, exhortation|词性: noun
Etymology: From Late Latin *dehortātiō*, from Latin *dehortārī* (“to urge away from, dissuade”), from *de-* (“away from”) + *hortārī* (“to urge, encourage”).
