deflation:/dɪˈfleɪʃən/
|n.|通货紧缩；放气；泄劲|A reduction in the general level of prices in an economy.  A decrease in the amount of air or gas in something.|例句：The government is trying to combat deflation. 政府正在努力应对通货紧缩。

例句：The deflation of the balloon was slow.  气球慢慢地漏气了。|近义词：contraction, recession, slump (for economic deflation); emptying, release (for air/gas deflation)|反义词：inflation (for economic deflation); inflation, expansion (for air/gas deflation)|词性:名词

deflations:/dɪˈfleɪʃənz/
|n.|通货紧缩（复数）；放气（复数）|Plural form of "deflation", referring to multiple instances of price decreases or air/gas releases.|例句：Several deflations in the housing market have impacted the economy. 房地产市场几次通货紧缩对经济造成了影响。

例句：Repeated deflations of the tires suggested a puncture. 轮胎反复漏气表明有刺破。|近义词：contractions, recessions, slumps (for economic deflations); emptyings, releases (for air/gas deflations)|反义词：inflations (for economic deflations); inflations, expansions (for air/gas deflations)|词性:名词
