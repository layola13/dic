gappier:/ˈɡæpiər/| adj.|更张口结舌的，更吃惊的|Comparative form of gappy: more gappy.  Describes something with more or larger gaps. |The audience was even gappier after the magician's final trick.|近义词：more surprised, more astonished, more open-mouthed|反义词：less surprised, less astonished|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From gap + -y + -er.  "Gap" originates from Old Norse "gap" meaning chasm or opening.  "-y" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "full of" or "characterized by."  "-er" is a comparative suffix.
