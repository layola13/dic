derogation:/ˌdɛrəˈɡeɪʃən/ | n. | 毁损，减损，贬低 |  the act of taking away a right or power; damaging the value or reputation of something or someone |  The new law represents a derogation of their civil rights. 这项新法律代表着对他们公民权利的损害。| synonyms: detraction, depreciation, lessening, belittling | antonyms:  enhancement, elevation, improvement | noun


derogatory:/dɪˈrɒɡət(ə)ri/ | adj. | 贬低的，诽谤的 | showing a critical or disrespectful attitude | He made some derogatory remarks about her appearance. 他对她的外貌发表了一些贬低的言论。| synonyms: disparaging, belittling, insulting, denigrating | antonyms: complimentary, flattering, praising | adjective


Etymology:  From Late Latin *dērōgātiō, from Latin dērogāre (“to repeal partly, diminish, detract”), from dē- (“from, away from”) + rogāre (“to ask, propose a law”).
