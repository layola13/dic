bandwagon:/ˈbændˌwæɡən/| n.|花车，潮流，浪潮|a current or fashionable trend or activity that a large number of people are or begin to become involved in.|例句：Everyone's jumping on the vegan bandwagon these days.  如今，每个人都赶上了纯素食的潮流。

例句：The politician jumped on the environmental bandwagon. 这位政治家赶上了环保的浪潮。|近义词：trend, fad, craze, vogue|反义词:unpopular, unconventional|词性:noun

bandwagon:/ˈbændˌwæɡən/| n.|乐队花车|a wagon carrying a band, especially in a parade or procession.|例句：The circus parade featured a colorful bandwagon. 马戏团游行队伍中有一辆色彩鲜艳的乐队花车。

例句：The band played lively music from their bandwagon. 乐队在他们的花车上演奏了欢快的音乐。|近义词：float, platform|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "band" (a group of musicians) + "wagon" (a four-wheeled vehicle). Originally referred to a wagon that carried a band in a parade. The figurative sense of joining a popular trend developed later, implying that people "climb aboard" the bandwagon as it gains momentum.
