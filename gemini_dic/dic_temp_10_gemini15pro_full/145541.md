overcommercializes: /ˌoʊvər kəˈmɜːrʃəlaɪzɪz/
|v.|过度商业化|To exploit or promote excessively for commercial gain, often to the detriment of quality or integrity.|The city has overcommercialized its historic waterfront, replacing charming local shops with generic chain stores. 这个城市过度商业化了其历史悠久的海滨地区，用普通的连锁店取代了迷人的当地商店。|overexploits, overmarkets|understates, downplays|verb


overcommercialized: /ˌoʊvər kəˈmɜːrʃəlaɪzd/
|adj.|过度商业化的|Excessively exploited or promoted for commercial gain.|The once-unique festival has become overcommercialized and lost its original charm.  这个曾经独一无二的节日已经变得过度商业化，失去了原有的魅力。|overexploited, overmarketed|understated, authentic|adjective


overcommercialization: /ˌoʊvər kəˌmɜːrʃələˈzeɪʃən/
|n.|过度商业化|The act or process of overcommercializing something.|The overcommercialization of Christmas has led to a focus on consumerism rather than the holiday's true meaning. 圣诞节的过度商业化导致人们关注消费主义，而不是节日的真正意义。|overexploitation, overmarketing|understatement|noun


Etymology:  "over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi "over, above") + "commercialize" (from "commercial" (from French, from Latin *commerciālis) + "-ize").
