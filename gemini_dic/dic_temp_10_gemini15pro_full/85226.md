harlequin:/ˈhɑːrlɪkwɪn/ | n. adj.|丑角，滑稽角色；小丑服；五颜六色的|A comic character in commedia dell'arte, typically masked, dressed in a chequered costume, and carrying a wooden sword or bat. Also refers to a pattern of diamond-shaped or lozenge-shaped figures in contrasting colors.|The harlequin skipped across the stage, juggling balls. 丑角在舞台上跳跃着，玩着杂耍球。|Synonyms: clown, jester, buffoon|Antonyms: serious person, tragedian|Noun, Adjective

harlequin:/ˈhɑːrlɪkwɪn/ | adj.|五颜六色的，色彩斑斓的|Having a pattern of brightly colored diamond or lozenge shapes.|She wore a harlequin dress to the party. 她穿着一件五颜六色的连衣裙去参加聚会。|Synonyms: multicolored, variegated, motley|Antonyms: monochrome, plain, simple|Adjective
