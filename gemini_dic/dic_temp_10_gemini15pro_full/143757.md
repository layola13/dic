outmans:/ˈaʊtmənz/ | n. |歹徒，暴徒; 逃犯 | A group of criminals, especially bandits or fugitives. |例句：The outmans hid in the mountains after robbing the stagecoach.  这群歹徒抢劫了驿站马车后躲藏在山中。

例句：The sheriff and his posse were pursuing the outmans across the desert. 警长和他的队伍正在沙漠中追捕逃犯。 |近义词：bandits, robbers, criminals, fugitives, outlaws |反义词: lawmen, police, officers |词性: 名词

词源:  Likely a variant or misspelling of "outmen," which itself comes from "out" + "men," referring to men outside the law or society.
