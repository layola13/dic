lecture: /ˈlɛktʃər/ (US) /ˈlɛktʃə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.| 讲座，演讲；教训，训斥|a formal talk on a serious subject given to a group of people, especially students; a serious talk given to someone in order to criticize their behaviour|
例句：The professor gave a fascinating lecture on the history of art. 教授做了一个关于艺术史的精彩讲座。

例句：My father gave me a lecture on the importance of saving money. 我父亲教训我要节约用钱。

近义词：speech, address, talk, presentation, sermon, discourse, lesson, scolding, reprimand|
反义词：discussion, conversation, chat|
词性：noun, verb


lecture: /ˈlɛktʃər/ (US) /ˈlɛktʃə(r)/ (UK)| v.| 讲课，演讲；教训，训斥|to give a formal talk on a serious subject; to criticize someone angrily for something they have done wrong|
例句：She lectures on 19th-century literature at the university. 她在大学讲授19世纪文学。

例句：He lectured me on the dangers of smoking. 他教训我吸烟的危害。


近义词：speak, talk, address, present, preach, scold, reprimand|
反义词：listen, converse, chat|
词性：verb


词源：From Middle French *lecture*  "reading,"  from Latin *lectura* "a reading aloud," from *lectus*, past participle of *legere* "to read, gather."
