confiscators:/ˈkɒnfɪskeɪtəz/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnfɪskeɪtərz/ (US)| n. |充公者，没收者|People who officially take possession of someone's property, often as a punishment.|The confiscators seized the illegal goods. 充公者没收了非法货物。|The government appointed confiscators to seize the assets of the criminal organization. 政府指派充公者扣押犯罪组织的资产。|近义词：seizers, appropriators|反义词: restorers, returners|词性: 名词

confiscate: /ˈkɒnfɪskeɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnfɪskeɪt/ (US)| vt. |没收，充公|To officially take possession of someone's property, often as a punishment.|The police confiscated the drugs. 警察没收了毒品。|The teacher confiscated the student's phone. 老师没收了学生的手机。|近义词：seize, impound, appropriate|反义词: return, restore|词性: 动词

confiscation: /ˌkɒnfɪˈskeɪʃən/ (UK) /ˌkɑːnfɪˈskeɪʃən/ (US)| n. |没收，充公|The act of confiscating something.|The confiscation of the weapons was necessary for public safety. 没收武器对于公共安全是必要的。|The confiscation of his passport prevented him from leaving the country.  没收他的护照使他无法离开该国。|近义词：seizure, appropriation|反义词: restoration, return|词性: 名词
