paleotherium:/ˌpeɪliəˈθɪəriəm/ | n. | 古兽，古哺乳动物 | An extinct genus of perissodactyl mammals related to both the horse and the rhinoceros, that lived in Europe during the Eocene and Oligocene epochs. |  The paleotherium, an ancestor of the horse, roamed the ancient forests. 古兽，一种马的祖先，曾在古老的森林中漫游。

Paleotherium fossils are commonly found in European Eocene deposits.  古兽化石常见于欧洲始新世沉积物中。| 近义词: Brontotherium, Titanotherium (broadly similar extinct mammals) | 反义词: N/A (extinct animals don't have direct antonyms) | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *paléothérium*, from Ancient Greek *παλαιός* (palaios, “ancient”) + *θηρίον* (thērion, “wild beast”).
