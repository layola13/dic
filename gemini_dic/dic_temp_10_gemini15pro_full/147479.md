overoptimists:/ˌoʊvərˈɒptɪmɪsts/ | n. |过度乐观者| People who are excessively optimistic, often unrealistically so.  They tend to overestimate the likelihood of positive outcomes and underestimate the potential for negative ones. | The overoptimists predicted a swift victory, ignoring the warnings of experienced analysts. 过度乐观者预测会迅速获胜，忽略了经验丰富的分析师的警告。

Overoptimists often underestimate the risks involved in starting a new business. 过度乐观者常常低估创业的风险。| 近义词: Pollyannas, utopians | 反义词: pessimists, realists | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
From 'over-' (prefix meaning "excessive") + 'optimist' (from Latin 'optimus' meaning "best").  The suffix '-ist' denotes a person who adheres to a particular belief or practice.
