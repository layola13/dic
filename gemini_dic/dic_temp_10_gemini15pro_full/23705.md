brasser:/bʁɑ.se/| v. |抛光, 磨光; 擦亮; (比喻)润色, 改进|To polish or burnish, especially metal.  Figuratively, to refine or improve something.|Example 1: Il a brassé les poignées de porte en cuivre. (He polished the brass door handles.) 他擦亮了铜门把手。

Example 2: Elle a passé des heures à brasser son discours. (She spent hours polishing her speech.) 她花了几个小时润色她的演讲。|近义词: polir, lustrer, fourbir, peaufiner|反义词: ternir, détériorer|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "brasser" (to brew), from Frankish "*brasōn" (to stir, mix).  The sense of polishing likely developed from the idea of stirring or rubbing.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*brasaną."  The modern French word retains the original meaning of "to brew," while the sense of "to polish" is now mostly archaic or regional.
