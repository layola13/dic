icebergs:/ˈaɪsbɜːrɡz/
noun
冰山
A large floating mass of ice detached from a glacier or ice sheet and carried out to sea.
The Titanic sank after hitting an iceberg.  泰坦尼克号撞到冰山后沉没了。

icebergs:/ˈaɪsbɜːrɡz/
noun
潜在的危险或问题
A large, potentially dangerous or problematic situation that is not immediately obvious.
The report warned of the icebergs that lie ahead for the economy. 报告警告说，经济发展面临着潜在的危险。


近义词:glacier, ice floe
反义词:(无直接反义词，可考虑warm water, open sea)
词性:noun
