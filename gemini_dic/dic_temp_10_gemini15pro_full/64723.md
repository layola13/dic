escheator:/ɪˈʃiːtər/ | n. | 遗产管理官；（旧时）负责调查及没收无合法继承人遗产的官员 | An officer formerly appointed to look into escheats.  An escheator was a medieval English Crown officer responsible for administering escheats – property that reverted to the Crown because the property owner died without an heir. | The escheator was responsible for managing the king's lands. 遗产管理官负责管理国王的土地。 |  The escheator determined that the property would revert to the crown. 遗产管理官裁定该财产将归还王室。 | Synonyms: administrator, executor | Antonyms: heir, inheritor | noun

escheat:/ɪsˈtʃiːt/ | n. vi. vt.| 充公；归还；（因无人继承而）归属 | The reversion of property to the state, or (in feudal law) to a lord, on the death of the owner without legal heirs. To become an escheat. To forfeit by escheat. |  His lands escheated to the Crown. 他的土地被充公。 | The property escheated to the state because there were no heirs. 因为没有继承人，这处房产被充公了。| Synonyms: forfeit, confiscate | Antonyms: inherit, receive | noun, verb transitive, verb intransitive
