controverts:/ˈkɒntrəvɜːts/ (UK), /ˈkɑːntrəvɜːrts/ (US)| v.| 反驳，争论，否认|To argue against something; dispute or deny. |The lawyer controverts the witness's testimony. 律师反驳了证人的证词。|近义词：dispute, challenge, deny, contradict, rebut|反义词：confirm, support, corroborate, verify|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin controversia, from controversus "turned against," from contra "against" + versus, past participle of vertere "to turn."
