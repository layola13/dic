bromoiodism:/ˌbroʊmoʊˈaɪədɪzəm/| n.|溴碘疗法；溴碘中毒|A method of treatment involving the administration of bromine and iodine, sometimes resulting in poisoning.|The doctor prescribed bromoiodism, but the patient developed an adverse reaction. 医生开了溴碘疗法，但病人出现了不良反应。|Synonym: iodine therapy, bromine therapy|Antonym: None.|noun

Etymology->
From bromo- + iod- + -ism.  "Bromo-" refers to bromine, a chemical element. "Iod-" refers to iodine, another chemical element. "-ism" is a suffix used to form nouns denoting a condition, practice, or doctrine.
