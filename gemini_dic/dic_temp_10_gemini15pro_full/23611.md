branch-like:/ˈbrɑːntʃˌlaɪk/| adj.|像树枝的，分叉的|Resembling a branch, especially in being slender and forked or subdivided.|The lightning struck the tree, leaving branch-like scorch marks on the trunk.|近义词：branched, dendritic, ramified, forked|反义词:straight, unbranched|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From branch + -like.  "Branch" comes from Old French "branche," ultimately from Vulgar Latin *branca, of uncertain origin, possibly related to a word meaning "paw" or "claw." "-like" is a native English suffix meaning "similar to," "resembling," from Old English "-lic."
