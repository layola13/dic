overacute:/ˌoʊvərəˈkjuːt/| adj.|过锐的，过于尖锐的|An angle greater than 90 degrees but less than 180 degrees is called an obtuse angle. An angle less than 90 degrees is called an acute angle.  An overacute angle is a somewhat informal term sometimes used to describe an angle that is very close to 0 degrees, extremely sharp.  It emphasizes the sharpness of the angle.|The architect's design featured several overacute angles, giving the building a distinctive look. 建筑师的设计以几个过锐角为特色，赋予了建筑独特的外观。| The needles on the pine tree were overacute and sharp. 松树上的针又尖又细。|近义词：sharp, pointed, acute|反义词:obtuse, blunt|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (meaning "excessive, beyond") + "acute" (from Latin "acutus," meaning "sharpened").
