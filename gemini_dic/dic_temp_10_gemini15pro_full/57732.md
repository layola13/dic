dreadnought:/ˈdrɛdnɔːt/| n.|无畏舰，主力舰|A type of battleship introduced in the early 20th century, characterized by its all-big-gun armament.|The dreadnought revolutionized naval warfare.|Synonym: battleship|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "small warship")|Noun

dreadnoughts:/ˈdrɛdnɔːts/| n.|无畏舰，主力舰（复数）|Plural of dreadnought, referring to multiple battleships of this type.|The dreadnoughts clashed in the Battle of Jutland.|Synonym: battleships|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "small warships")|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From "dread" (meaning fear or awe) + "nought" (meaning nothing, implying that the ship feared nothing).  Coined after the name of the first ship of this class, HMS Dreadnought, launched in 1906.
