ballast: /ˈbæləst/ | n. v. |压舱物，稳定因素，使稳定|Heavy material, such as gravel, sand, or iron, placed low in a vessel to improve its stability.  Something that gives stability, strength, or steadiness.  To provide or fill with ballast. |The ship took on ballast before setting sail.  这艘船在启航前装上了压舱物。

The ballast kept the hot air balloon steady.  压舱物使热气球保持稳定。

He considered his family to be his emotional ballast. 他认为他的家庭是他的情感支柱。

The ship was ballasted with rocks.  这艘船用岩石压舱。|近义词：counterweight, stabilizer, anchor |反义词：instability |词性：名词, 动词
