misdoubtful:/ˌmɪsˈdaʊtfʊl/ | adj. | 怀疑的，不信任的 | Feeling or expressing doubt or suspicion.  |  I am misdoubtful of his promises. 我怀疑他的承诺。 | Synonym: suspicious, distrustful, skeptical | Antonym: trusting, confident, certain | word property: adjective

misdoubtful:/ˌmɪsˈdaʊtfʊl/ | adj. | 可疑的，令人怀疑的 | Arousing doubt or suspicion. | The circumstances surrounding his disappearance were misdoubtful. 他失踪的周围情况很可疑。| Synonym: dubious, questionable, suspect | Antonym: trustworthy, reliable, credible | word property: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- + doubtful. "Mis-" is a prefix of Old English origin meaning "wrong," "badly," "wrongly," or expressing the opposite of the base word. "Doubtful" comes from the Old French "douteus" and ultimately from the Latin "dubitare" meaning "to hesitate" or "to waver in opinion."
