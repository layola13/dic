moors:/mʊrz/ | n. | 荒野，沼泽；摩尔人 |  A tract of open uncultivated upland, typically covered with heather.  Often refers to the upland areas of northern England and Scotland. Can also refer to the Muslim inhabitants of North Africa and Spain in the Middle Ages. | The hikers got lost on the moors in the heavy fog.  徒步旅行者在大雾中迷失在荒野里。

The Moors invaded Spain in the 8th century. 摩尔人在8世纪入侵了西班牙。 | synonyms: heathland, wasteland, uplands,  Muslims (historical) | antonyms:  lowlands, valleys, cultivated land | noun


moor:/mʊr/ | v. | 停泊（船只）；使（船只）停泊 | To secure (a vessel) in a particular place, as by cables or anchors. | We moored the boat to the dock. 我们把船停泊在码头上。

The ship was moored offshore. 船停泊在近海。 | synonyms: anchor, berth, dock | antonyms: unmoor, cast off | verb
