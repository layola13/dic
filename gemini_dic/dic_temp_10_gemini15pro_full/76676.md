galvanocautery: /ˌɡælvənoʊˈkɔːtəri/ /ˌɡælvənoʊˈkɔːtəri/ | n. | 烧灼术，电烙术 | The destruction of tissue by electric current.  It involves using a heated instrument or an electric current to burn or destroy abnormal tissue, such as warts, polyps, or tumors. | The doctor used galvanocautery to remove the wart. 医生用电烙术去除了疣。 |  electrosurgery, cauterization | cryosurgery | noun
Etymology:  From "galvano-" (relating to galvanism, a direct current of electricity) + "cautery" (burning or searing of tissue).  Ultimately from Greek "kautērion" (branding iron).
