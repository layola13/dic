barium:/ˈbeəriəm/| n.|钡|A soft, silvery-white metallic element of the alkaline-earth group, occurring only in combination with other elements, especially in barite.  It is used in some alloys, and its compounds are used in medicine, in fireworks, and in the manufacture of glass. |Barium sulfate is used as a contrast agent for X-rays.|synonyms:  alkaline earth metal|antonyms: non-metal|noun
