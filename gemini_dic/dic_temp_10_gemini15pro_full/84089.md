half-ingeniousnesses:/ˌhæf ˌɪnˈdʒiːniəsnəsɪz/
|n.|
|半灵巧; 不够精巧(通常用复数形式)|
|A state of being only partially ingenious; a collection of ideas or inventions that are somewhat clever but not fully developed or effective.  It emphasizes a lack of complete ingenuity.|
|His half-ingeniousnesses were amusing, but ultimately impractical for solving the complex problem.|他的那些小聪明虽然有趣，但最终对于解决这个复杂的问题来说并不实用。|
|partial cleverness, semi-ingenuity, limited resourcefulness|
|complete ingenuity, brilliance, genius|
|noun|
