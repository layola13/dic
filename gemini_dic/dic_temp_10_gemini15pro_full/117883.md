melodrame: /ˈmɛləˌdrɑːmə/ (US), /ˈmɛləʊˌdrɑːmə/ (UK)| n.| 情节剧; 通俗剧;  夸张的事件或行为|A play, film, or other work characterized by exaggerated emotions, stereotypical characters, and interpersonal conflicts.|The movie was a pure melodrama, full of improbable plot twists and over-the-top acting.  这部电影纯粹是一部情节剧，充满了难以置信的情节转折和夸张的表演。|近义词: drama, soap opera, theatrics|反义词: understatement, realism|词性: noun

melodromatic: /ˌmɛlədrəˈmætɪk/ (US), /ˌmɛləʊdrəˈmætɪk/ (UK)| adj.| 情节剧的; 通俗剧的; 夸张的|Characteristic of melodrama, especially in being exaggerated, sensationalized, or overemotional.|Her reaction was melodramatic, as if she were in a play. 她的反应很夸张，好像在演戏一样。|近义词: theatrical, histrionic, overwrought|反义词: understated, restrained, realistic|词性: adjective

Etymology: From French *mélodrame*, from Greek *melos* "song" + French *drame* "drama."
