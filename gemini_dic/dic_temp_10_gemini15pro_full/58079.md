drooling:/ˈdruːlɪŋ/ | adj. | 流口水的；垂涎的 | Letting saliva flow from the mouth; showing excessive desire for something. | The dog was drooling over the steak.  狗对着牛排流口水。| synonyms: salivating, slavering, yearning, coveting | antonyms: indifferent, uninterested | adjective

drool:/druːl/ | vi. n. | 流口水；垂涎 | 1.(vi.) To let saliva flow from the mouth. 2.(vi.) To show excessive desire for something. 3.(n.) Saliva running from the mouth. | 1. The baby was drooling in its sleep. 婴儿在睡梦中流口水。 2. He was drooling over the new sports car. 他对那辆新的跑车垂涎三尺。 3. Wipe the drool off your chin. 把你下巴上的口水擦掉。 | synonyms: salivate, slaver, yearn, covet (v.); saliva, spittle (n.) | antonyms: none | verb, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *drūlen, frequentative of *drūwen "to be sad, troubled," related to Old English drēorig "sad, bloody," from Proto-Germanic *dreuzijaz. The sense shift from "sad" to "drooling" likely occurred through the association of sadness with a downturned mouth.
