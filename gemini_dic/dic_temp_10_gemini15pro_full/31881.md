cheerly:/ˈtʃɪrli/| adv. adj. (archaic)|高兴地，欢快地；兴高采烈的|Cheerly, she greeted her guests. 她兴高采烈地迎接客人。|近义词：happily, merrily, joyfully, blithely|反义词：morosely, gloomily, sadly|词性：副词，形容词 (古体)

词源: From Middle English *cherli, from Old English *ċēorliċe (“clearly, brightly, famously, nobly”), equivalent to *ċēor (“mood, disposition, cheer”) +‎ -ly.  Cognate with Scots cheirlie (“cheerfully”). Related to cheer.

用法说明:

"Cheerly" primarily functions as an adverb, meaning "in a cheerful way." While it can also be used as an adjective, this usage is now considered archaic. It is more commonly found in older literature and less frequent in modern English.

例句：

He cheerly waved goodbye.
他高兴地挥手告别。


With a cheerly smile, she accepted the gift.
她带着愉快的笑容接受了礼物。
