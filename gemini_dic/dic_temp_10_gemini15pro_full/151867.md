passions:/ˈpæʃənz/| n. |激情，热情，酷爱；盛怒| strong and barely controllable emotions.|  She spoke with passion about the need for change. 她充满激情地谈到变革的必要性。

|近义词：enthusiasms, fervor, zeal, ardor, love, infatuation|反义词：apathy, indifference, detachment|词性:名词(plural)


passions:/ˈpæʃənz/| n. |爱好，嗜好| something that someone likes to do very much.|  His passions include playing the guitar and collecting stamps. 他的爱好包括弹吉他和集邮。

|近义词：hobbies, interests, pursuits|反义词：dislikes, aversions|词性:名词(plural)


passions:/ˈpæʃənz/| n. |（基督教）耶稣受难| the sufferings of Jesus Christ between the night of the Last Supper and his death.|  The Passion of Christ is commemorated during Holy Week. 基督受难是在圣周期间纪念的。

|近义词：suffering, agony, torment|反义词：joy, bliss|词性:名词(plural)


词源:
From Old French passion, from Late Latin passiō (“suffering, enduring; passion”), from Latin passus, past participle of patī (“to suffer, endure”).
