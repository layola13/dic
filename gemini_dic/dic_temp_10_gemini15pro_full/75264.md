frondeur:/frɔ̃ˈdœʁ/ (French pronunciation)| n.|投石党人；叛逆者；捣乱分子|A frondeur is someone who rebels against authority or causes disruption, often for political reasons.  The word originates from the French "Fronde," a series of uprisings against the French monarchy in the 17th century.|The politician acted like a frondeur, constantly challenging the party line. 这位政治家表现得像个捣乱分子，不断挑战党的路线。|Synonyms: rebel, insurgent, dissident, nonconformist, malcontent|Antonyms: conformist, loyalist|noun

frondeur:/frɔ̃ˈdœʁ/ (French pronunciation) | adj.|叛逆的；捣乱的|Characterized by rebelliousness and a tendency to cause disruption.|His frondeur attitude made him unpopular with the management. 他叛逆的态度让他不受管理层的欢迎。|Synonyms: rebellious, defiant, unruly, disruptive, insubordinate|Antonyms: obedient, compliant, submissive|adjective
