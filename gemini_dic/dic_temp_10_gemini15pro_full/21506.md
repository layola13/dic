bludge:/blʌdʒ/
bludges:/ˈblʌdʒɪz/
bludge: |v. n.| 逃避工作；偷懒；诈骗；(澳/新西兰俚语)吃白食；|to avoid work or responsibility; to live off others; to sponge off someone. (Often considered informal or slang, especially in Australian and New Zealand English.)|He's always bludging off his friends. 他总是占朋友的便宜。|近义词：shirk, loaf, sponge, freeload|反义词：work, toil, graft|词性: 动词/名词
bludges:|n.| 逃避工作的人；诈骗行为；(澳/新西兰俚语)吃白食的人；|Acts of avoiding work; sponging off others; people who avoid work.  (Often considered informal or slang, especially in Australian and New Zealand English.)|He called them a bunch of bludges. 他称他们是一群懒汉。|近义词：shirkers, loafers, spongers, freeloaders|反义词：workers, toilers, grafters|词性: 名词

词源:
Origin uncertain, possibly from 19th-century British slang.
