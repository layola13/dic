nerve:/nɜːrv/| n.|神经；勇气；胆量；厚颜无耻|A bundle of fibers that transmits impulses of sensation to the brain or spinal cord, and impulses from these to the muscles and organs.|She touched a raw nerve when she mentioned his ex-wife. 她提到他的前妻，触到了他的痛处。|Synonyms: courage, bravery, audacity, guts|Antonyms: timidity, cowardice|noun

nerve:/nɜːrv/| vt.|使有勇气；使振作；鼓起勇气|To give someone the courage or confidence to do something.|He nerved himself to ask her out. 他鼓起勇气约她出去。|Synonyms: embolden, strengthen, fortify|Antonyms: discourage, weaken, undermine|verb
