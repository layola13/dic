conductor:/kənˈdʌktər/ (US), /kənˈdʌktə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 指挥家；售票员；导体 |A person who directs the performance of an orchestra or choir. A person who collects fares or tickets on a bus or train. A substance that allows heat or electricity to pass through it.|
例句：The conductor led the orchestra with great passion. 指挥家充满激情地指挥着乐队。

例句：The bus conductor asked me for my ticket. 公共汽车售票员要我买票。


近义词：director (for orchestra), driver, operator (for bus/train), leader, guide|
反义词：insulator (for electricity/heat)|
词性：noun|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *conductor, from Latin conducere "to lead together, bring with one," from com- "together" (see com-) + ducere "to lead" (see duke (n.)).
