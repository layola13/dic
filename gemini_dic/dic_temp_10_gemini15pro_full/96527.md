indol:/ˈɪndɒl/ (UK), /ˈɪndoʊl/ (US)| n.|吲哚|A crystalline organic compound containing a benzene ring fused to a pyrrole ring.  It is found in coal tar and feces and has a strong fecal odor. In highly diluted solutions, however, it has a flowery odor and is used in perfumery.|The chemist detected a faint odor of indol in the sample.|Synonyms:  benzopyrrole, ketole|Antonyms: None specific to indol.|noun
