individuator:/ɪnˌdɪvɪdʒuˈeɪtər/ | n. | 个体化者，赋予个体性的事物 |Something that individuates or distinguishes something or someone as an individual.  |The artist's unique style acts as an individuator, setting their work apart from others. 艺术家独特的风格使其作品与众不同，成为个体化的标志。|  The experience of grief can be a powerful individuator, forcing us to confront our own mortality. 悲伤的经历可以是一个强大的个体化因素，迫使我们面对自身的死亡。 |近义词: differentiator, distinguisher | 反义词: homogenizer, unifier | 词性: noun

individuator:/ɪnˌdɪvɪdʒuˈeɪtər/ | n. |赋予个体性的事物，个体化者|Something or someone that confers individuality; that distinguishes as an individual.|例句：Jung believed the 'Self' to be the central archetype and the individuator of the psyche. 荣格认为“自我”是核心原型和心灵的个体化者。|例句:  Education can be a powerful individuator, allowing people to develop their unique talents and perspectives. 教育可以是一个强大的个体化因素，使人们能够发展自己独特的才能和视角。 |近义词: distinguisher, differentiator |反义词: homogenizer, conformer |词性: noun
