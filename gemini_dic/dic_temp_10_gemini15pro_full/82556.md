guano:/ˈɡwɑːnoʊ/ | n. | 鸟粪，海鸟粪，蝙蝠粪 | A natural fertilizer composed chiefly of the excrement of seabirds, cave-dwelling bats, or seals, found especially on islands near the Peruvian coast. |  The farmers used guano to fertilize their fields.  农民们用鸟粪给田地施肥。| Synonyms: fertilizer, manure, dung | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "barren soil" in a metaphorical sense) | noun


guanos:/ˈɡwɑːnoʊz/ | n. | 鸟粪（guano的复数形式）|  Plural form of guano.  |  The guanos were shipped from the islands to the mainland.  这些鸟粪从岛屿运到大陆。| Synonyms: fertilizers, manures, dungs | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *guano*, from Quechua *wanu* (dung, manure).
