bind-out:/ˈbaɪnd aʊt/ | n. | 契约佣工; 约束; 绑定输出 | An agreement by which a child is bound to service, especially as an apprentice. Historically, this often referred to poor or orphaned children being placed in servitude until they reached adulthood. |  The bind-out system was often exploitative, offering little opportunity for education or advancement. 契约佣工制度通常具有剥削性，很少提供教育或晋升的机会。|  The historical records show many examples of children being bound out to work on farms. 历史记录显示了许多儿童被契约到农场工作的例子。 | synonym: indenture, apprenticeship (historical context) | antonym: freedom, emancipation | noun


bind-out:/ˈbaɪnd aʊt/ | adj. | 绑定的 |  Bound by agreement or contract, particularly in the context of a service or obligation. |  A bind-out contract often stipulated the terms of service.  契约佣工合同通常规定了服务条款。 | The bind-out child was expected to work long hours. 契约佣工的儿童被期望长时间工作。 | synonym: contracted, obligated (in context) | antonym: free, unbound | adjective
