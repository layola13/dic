gallant:/ˈɡælənt/ (US), /ˈɡælənt/ (UK)| n.|英勇的人；对女子献殷勤的男人|a brave or chivalrous man; a man who is exceptionally attentive and courteous to women |例句：The gallants of the court vied for the queen's favor. 宫廷里的绅士们争相获得女王的青睐。|近义词：suitor, admirer, beau, courtier|反义词：cad, boor|词性:名词

gallants:/ˈɡælənts/ (US), /ˈɡælənts/ (UK)| n.|（尤指旧时的）衣着华丽的男子；勇敢的人| (especially in former times) a dashing and stylishly dressed man; a brave person|例句：The gallants rode their horses through the town square.  那些衣着华丽的男子骑着马穿过城镇广场。|近义词：beaux, dandies, sparks|反义词：cowards|词性:名词复数


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *galant, from galer "to rejoice," of Germanic origin.
