disguiser:/dɪsˈɡaɪzər/ | n. |伪装者，掩饰者|A person or thing that disguises. Someone or something that changes the appearance or nature of something or someone so that it is not easily recognized. |He was a master disguiser, able to blend in anywhere. 他是一个伪装大师，能够融入任何地方。|The heavy makeup acted as a disguiser. 厚重的妆容起到了伪装的作用。|Synonyms: camouflage, masker, cloak|Antonyms: revealer, exposer|noun

disguise: /dɪsˈɡaɪz/ | vt.  n. | 伪装，掩饰；伪装物，掩饰物 | To change the appearance or nature of something or someone so that it is not easily recognized; something that is used to change someone's appearance or nature so that they are not easily recognized. |He disguised himself as a policeman. 他把自己伪装成一个警察。|She wore a disguise to the party.  她在派对上戴了伪装。|Synonyms: conceal, mask, camouflage | Antonyms: reveal, expose | verb, noun
