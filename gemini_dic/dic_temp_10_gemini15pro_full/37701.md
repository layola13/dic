companionship:/kəmˈpæn.jən.ʃɪp/
n.
友谊；陪伴；伴侣关系
the condition of being a companion or companions; fellowship.
例句：She found companionship in her dog after her husband died.  丈夫去世后，她从她的狗身上找到了陪伴。

例句：The elderly often suffer from a lack of companionship. 老年人经常遭受缺乏陪伴的痛苦。

近义词：friendship, fellowship, camaraderie, company
反义词：isolation, loneliness, solitude
词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *company*, from Old French *compagnie* (modern French *compagnie*), from Medieval Latin *companio*, ultimately from Latin *com-* (“with”) + *panis* (“bread”).  Originally referring to people who eat bread together, thus companions, company. The suffix "-ship" denotes a state, quality, or condition.
