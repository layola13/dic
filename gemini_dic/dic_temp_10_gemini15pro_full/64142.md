equalise:/ˈiːkwəlaɪz/ /ˈikwəˌlaɪz/ | v. | 使相等，使均等，补偿 | To make equal or uniform. To make the same in size, degree, amount, etc. | The government is trying to equalise educational opportunities. 政府正在努力使教育机会均等。 |  synonyms: level, balance, even out | antonyms:  differentiate, vary,  unbalance | verb

equalised:/ˈiːkwəlaɪzd/ /ˈikwəˌlaɪzd/ | v. | 使相等，使均等，补偿（过去式和过去分词）|  Past tense and past participle of "equalise".  To have made equal or uniform. To have made the same in size, degree, amount, etc. | The scores were equalised in the final minutes of the game. 比分在比赛的最后几分钟被扳平了。 | synonyms: levelled, balanced, evened out | antonyms: differentiated, varied, unbalanced | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *equalisen*, from late Latin *aequalizare*, from Latin *aequalis* (“equal”).
