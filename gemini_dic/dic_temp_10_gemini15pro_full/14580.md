avicularium:/ævɪˈkjʊləriəm/ | n. | 鸟头体；鸟嘴状结构 | A small, beak-shaped structure found on some bryozoans (moss animals), used for defense or capturing food. | The avicularia of this bryozoan species are particularly large and mobile.  这种苔藓虫的鸟头体特别大而且活动自如。 | mandible, beak | N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *avicula* ("small bird") + *-aris* (adjective suffix forming words denoting resemblance or belonging). *Avicula* is a diminutive of *avis* ("bird").
