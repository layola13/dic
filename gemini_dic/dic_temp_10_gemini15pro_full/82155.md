gloomy: /ˈɡluːmi/ | adj. | 忧郁的，阴暗的，沮丧的 | Dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing or frightening; causing or suggestive of gloom. | The gloomy weather reflects my mood.  阴沉的天气反映了我的心情。 | Synonyms: dark, dim, depressing, dismal, somber, bleak, dreary, mournful, sullen | Antonyms: bright, cheerful, sunny, light, optimistic | adjective

gloomier: /ˈɡluːmiər/ | adj. | 更忧郁的，更阴暗的，更沮丧的 (gloomy的比较级) | More gloomy. | The weather today is even gloomier than yesterday. 今天的天气比昨天更阴沉。 | Synonyms: darker, dimmer, more depressing | Antonyms: brighter, cheerier, sunnier | adjective

gloomiest: /ˈɡluːmiɪst/ | adj. | 最忧郁的，最阴暗的，最沮丧的 (gloomy的最高级) | Most gloomy. | This is the gloomiest room in the house. 这是房子里最阴暗的房间。 | Synonyms: darkest, dimmest, most depressing | Antonyms: brightest, cheerfullest, sunniest | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *glommy*, from Old English *glom*, meaning "twilight, dusk."
