diapir:/ˈdaɪəpaɪər/ | n. |底辟，挤入构造|A dome-shaped intrusion of rock, such as salt or igneous rock, that has pierced through overlying rock layers.|The salt diapir rose slowly through the sedimentary layers.  盐底辟缓慢地穿过沉积层。|Synonym: intrusion, dome |Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps subsidence?) |noun

diapirs:/ˈdaɪəpaɪərz/ | n. |底辟（复数）|Dome-shaped intrusions of rock, such as salt or igneous rock, that have pierced through overlying rock layers.|The geologists mapped several diapirs in the region. 地质学家绘制了该地区几个底辟的地图。|Synonym: intrusions, domes |Antonym: (No direct antonym) |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *diapir*, from Greek *diapeirein* "to pierce through," from *dia-* "through" + *peirein* "to pierce."
