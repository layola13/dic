ETYMOLOGY->
From the Latin "gratus," meaning "pleasing," ironically, the word "grating" has evolved to describe something unpleasant. This shift in meaning likely comes from the sense of something being repeatedly scraped or rubbed, which can be irritating.  The noun form comes from the verb "grate."
