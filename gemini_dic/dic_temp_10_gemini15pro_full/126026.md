mussing:/ˈmʌsɪŋ/
v.|弄乱，揉乱|Making something untidy or disordered, typically hair.|She was mussing her hair in frustration.|Synonyms: disheveling, rumpling, tousling|Antonyms: smoothing, straightening, tidying|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "muss," which originated in the late 16th century. "Muss" likely comes from the Middle Dutch word "musse," meaning "mess" or "confusion."
