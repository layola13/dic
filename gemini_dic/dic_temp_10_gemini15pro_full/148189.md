overshadowers:/ˌoʊvərˈʃædərz/ | n. | 使黯然失色的人或事物 |  People or things that overshadow others; those that cause others to seem less important or impressive. |  Their success was overshadowed by the scandal. 他们的成功被丑闻蒙上了一层阴影. | synonyms: eclipsers, outshiners | antonyms:  underdogs, inferiors | noun

overshadow:/ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊ/ | vt. |  (1)遮蔽，使阴暗; (2)使显得逊色，使黯然失色 | (1) To cast a shadow over; darken. (2) To make (something) seem less important; to dominate or surpass. | (1) The tall buildings overshadowed the park. 高楼遮蔽了公园。(2) Her early success was overshadowed by her later failures. 她早期的成功被后来的失败所掩盖。 | synonyms: dim, obscure, eclipse, dwarf | antonyms: highlight, illuminate, emphasize | verb
