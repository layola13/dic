cuskin:/ˈkʌskɪn/| n.|酒桶，酒桶的底座；（尤指）威士忌酒桶|A large cask, especially one for whisky.|The distillery had rows and rows of cuskins filled with aging whisky. 酒厂里一排排的酒桶装着正在陈酿的威士忌。|Synonyms: cask, barrel, tun|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Scottish Gaelic *cuisgein*, diminutive of *cuisge*  "vessel, cask," related to Irish *coisgin*. Ultimately from Latin *cuspis* "point, spear."  The connection likely arises from the pointed staves used in barrel construction.
