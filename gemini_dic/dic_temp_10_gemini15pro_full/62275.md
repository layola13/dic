encyclopedism:/ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpiːdɪzəm/| n. | 百科全书主义；百科全书式知识；|A system of organizing knowledge that resembles an encyclopedia, often characterized by superficiality or an excessive focus on factual information.|His encyclopedism allowed him to speak on a wide range of topics, though not always with great depth. 他的百科全书式知识使他能谈论各种话题，尽管并不总是很深入。|近义词：comprehensiveness, polymathy|反义词：specialization, ignorance|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *encyclopédisme*, from *encyclopédie* (encyclopedia) + *-isme* (-ism).  The word "encyclopedia" itself comes from Late Latin *encyclopaedia*, from Greek *enkyklios paideia* meaning "general education," from *enkyklios* "circular, recurring" + *paideia* "education, rearing of a child."
