chamade:/ʃəˈmɑːd/ /ʃəˈmeɪd/
n.|信号, 喇叭声，休战信号|A signal by drum or trumpet, or the sound itself, formerly used to request a parley or truce.|The besieged fortress sounded a chamade to request negotiations.|近义词:parley, truce, ceasefire|反义词:attack, assault|词性:名词
Etymology: From French *chamade*, from Spanish *llamada* "call," from *llamar* "to call," from Late Latin *clamare* "to cry out."
