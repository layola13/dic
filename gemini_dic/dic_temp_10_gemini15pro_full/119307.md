microatoll:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌætɒl/ | n. |微环礁|A small, low-lying, ring-shaped island or reef structure, formed from coral, that resembles a miniature atoll.  Microatolls are particularly sensitive to sea-level changes and can be used to reconstruct past sea levels.|The researchers studied the microatolls in the Maldives to reconstruct past sea level changes.  研究人员研究了马尔代夫的微环礁，以重建过去的海平面变化。|Synonym: miniature atoll|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps 'deep-sea coral')|noun
