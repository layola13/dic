gnawing:/ˈnɔːɪŋ/| adj.  v.| 持续的；折磨人的；咬的 |  persistently and painfully affecting one's conscience or emotions; biting or chewing something persistently.|  A gnawing sense of guilt kept her awake at night. 一种持续的负罪感让她夜不能寐。|Synonyms:  biting, chewing, corroding, eroding, nagging, persistent, troubling | Antonyms: soothing, comforting| adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *gnāgan, from Proto-Germanic *knāganą (compare West Frisian gnaegje, Dutch knagen, German nagen), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵneh₁- (“to gnaw”).
