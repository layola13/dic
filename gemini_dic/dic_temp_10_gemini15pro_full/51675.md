devilfish: /ˈdevəlˌfɪʃ/ | n. | 恶魔鱼，章鱼，鱿鱼，蝠鲼 |  A sea creature, such as an octopus, squid, or ray, often considered dangerous or frightening.  It comes from the notion that these creatures are monstrous or demonic. | The old sailor told tales of a giant devilfish that dragged ships to their doom.  老水手讲述了一个关于巨型恶魔鱼将船只拖入毁灭的故事。|  The devilfish lurked in the dark depths, waiting for its prey.  恶魔鱼潜伏在黑暗的深处，等待着它的猎物。 | Synonyms: octopus, squid, ray, manta ray | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, as it's a descriptive term. One could consider small, harmless fish as an opposite concept.) | Noun
