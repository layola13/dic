countervallation:/ˌkaʊntərˌvæˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 对垒；围城工事; 包围圈 | An artificial rampart raised by besiegers around a fortified place.  A line of fortifications, consisting of a wall and trench, built by the besieging army around the besieged city. | The Roman army built a countervallation around the city to prevent supplies from reaching the defenders.  罗马军队在城市周围建造了围城工事，以防止补给到达守军。| synonym: circumvallation, siegeworks | antonym: sally port | noun
