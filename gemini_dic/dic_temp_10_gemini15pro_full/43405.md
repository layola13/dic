ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cranke*, probably from Old Norse *krankr* (“crooked, bent”), from Proto-Germanic *kranką* (“crooked, bent”). Cognate with German *krank* (“sick, ill”). The sense of "eccentric person" arose from the notion of something being "bent out of shape" or "twisted."
