infuscation:/ɪnˌfʌsˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 模糊；混淆；昏暗 | The act of darkening or obscuring something.  It can refer to a physical darkening, like the infuscation of the sky by clouds, or a metaphorical darkening, like the infuscation of a meaning by unclear language. | The dense fog caused an infuscation of the landscape. 浓雾使景色变得模糊不清。| Synonyms: darkening, obscuring, obfuscation, clouding | Antonyms: clarification, illumination, elucidation | noun

infuscate:/ɪnˈfʌsˌkeɪt/ | v. | 使模糊；使混淆；使昏暗 | To make dark or obscure; to confuse. | The writer's convoluted prose infuscated the meaning of the poem.  作者晦涩难懂的散文使诗歌的意义变得模糊不清。 His attempts to explain only further infuscated the issue. 他试图解释，反而使问题更加混乱。| Synonyms: darken, obscure, obfuscate, cloud | Antonyms: clarify, illuminate, elucidate | verb
