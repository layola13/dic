monasticism:/məˈnæstɪˌsɪzəm/ | n. |修道主义，禁欲主义|  The renunciation of worldly things and the adoption of a religious life, especially by living in a monastery or convent.  A way of life characterized by prayer, contemplation, and often self-denial, typically in a secluded community. |  The rise of monasticism in the Middle Ages had a profound impact on European society. 中世纪修道主义的兴起对欧洲社会产生了深远的影响。| asceticism, contemplation, religious life | worldliness, materialism | noun
