nebulose:/ˈnɛbjʊləs/ /ˈnɛbjʊloʊs/ | adj. | 模糊的，星云状的 | Cloudy or hazy; resembling a nebula. |  The meaning of the ancient text remained nebulose, despite years of study.  古代文献的含义尽管经过多年的研究仍然模糊不清。

The photographer captured a nebulose image of the distant galaxy. 摄影师捕捉到了遥远星系的模糊图像。 | 近义词：hazy, vague, indistinct, obscure, amorphous | 反义词：clear, distinct, precise, sharp, definite | 词性: 形容词

nebula:/ˈnɛbjʊlə/ /ˈnɛbjʊlə/ | n. | 星云 | A cloud of gas and dust in outer space, visible in the night sky either as an indistinct bright patch or as a dark silhouette against other luminous matter. | The Orion Nebula is a popular target for amateur astronomers. 猎户座星云是业余天文学家的热门目标。

Astronomers study nebulae to learn more about star formation. 天文学家研究星云以了解更多关于恒星形成的知识。 | 近义词：cloud | 反义词: (No direct antonym, potentially "void" or "empty space") | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin nebula ("mist, vapor, fog, cloud, exhalation"), from Proto-Italic *nebhōlā, from Proto-Indo-European *nébʰōlā ("cloud"). Related to Old English nifol ("dark, gloomy, misty"), German Nebel ("fog").
