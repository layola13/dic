hybridising:/ˈhaɪbrɪdaɪzɪŋ/ |v.| 杂交，混合 |The process of creating a hybrid, often by crossbreeding different species or varieties. |Scientists are hybridising different strains of wheat to create a disease-resistant variety. 科学家们正在将不同的小麦品种杂交，以创造一种抗病品种。|
|近义词：crossbreeding, interbreeding, mixing, blending|反义词：purifying, isolating|词性: 动词(gerund or present participle of hybridise)


hybridise:/ˈhaɪbrɪdaɪz/ |v.| 杂交，混合 |To create a hybrid, often by crossbreeding different species or varieties.  |These two species of plants can hybridise naturally. 这两种植物可以自然杂交。| They hybridised the roses to get a new color. 他们将玫瑰杂交以获得新的颜色。|近义词：crossbreed, interbreed, mix, blend|反义词：purify, isolate|词性: 动词

词源：From hybrid + -ize.  From French hybride, from Latin hybrida, offspring of a tame sow and wild boar.
