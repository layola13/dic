heterotopia:/ˌhɛtərəˈtoʊpiə/ | n. | 异位；异位性 |  The displacement of an organ or body part from its normal location.  A concept developed by Michel Foucault referring to spaces that are somehow 'other': sacred or forbidden, utopian or dystopian. |  Example 1:  The child was born with intestinal heterotopia, a condition where part of the intestine was found outside its usual place. 孩子出生时患有肠异位，一部分肠子位于其通常位置之外。

Example 2:  Foucault described the ship as a "heterotopia," a space existing outside of conventional social structures. 福柯将船描述为“异托邦”，一个存在于传统社会结构之外的空间。 | Near Synonyms: displacement, malposition, abnormality, anomaly | Antonyms: normal position, eutopia | noun


heterotopism:/ˌhɛtərəˈtoʊpɪzəm/ | n. | 异位性；异位症 | The condition or state of being heterotopic.  The presence of a particular tissue type in an abnormal location. | Example 1:  Heterotopism of the gray matter can cause seizures. 灰质异位会导致癫痫发作。

Example 2:  Gastric heterotopism in the esophagus is a relatively common finding. 食管中的胃异位是一种相对常见的发现。 | Near Synonyms: heterotopia, ectopia, displacement | Antonyms: normal placement | noun
