billion:/ˈbɪljən/ | n. | 十亿 | A number equal to 1,000 million (1,000,000,000). |  The company is worth billions of dollars.  这家公司价值数十亿美元。 | near synonyms: milliard, thousand million | antonym: none (specific numerical antonyms like million are not truly opposites) | noun

Etymology: From Old French *billion (unattested), from byllion, an augmentation of million, itself from Old Italian milione ("a great thousand"). Cognate with Italian bilione. In American English, the meaning shifted to "a thousand millions" in the early 20th century, and this has become the standard meaning.
