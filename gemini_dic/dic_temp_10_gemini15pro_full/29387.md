casuistry: /ˈkæʒuɪstri/ | n. | 诡辩术，决疑法 |  the use of clever but unsound reasoning, especially in relation to moral questions; sophistry. |  The lawyer's casuistry was impressive, but ultimately unconvincing.  律师的诡辩很精彩，但最终没有说服力。| Synonym: sophistry, quibbling, equivocation | Antonym:  straightforwardness, honesty, directness | noun

casuistry: /ˈkæʒuɪstri/ | n. |  决疑论,个案推理 | the application of general ethical principles to particular cases (especially of conscience). | Studying casuistry can help one navigate complex moral dilemmas. 学习决疑论可以帮助人们应对复杂的道德困境。 | Synonym:  moral reasoning, ethical analysis | Antonym: moral absolutism | noun
