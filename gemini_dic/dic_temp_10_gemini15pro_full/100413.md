intertextuality:/ˌɪntərˌtɛkstʃuˈælɪti/ | n. |互文性|The relationship between texts, especially literary ones. It refers to the way that texts are shaped by other texts, either through direct reference, allusion, or broader cultural influences.  |Example 1: The intertextuality between James Joyce's Ulysses and Homer's Odyssey is well documented. 詹姆斯·乔伊斯的《尤利西斯》和荷马的《奥德赛》之间的互文性已被充分记录。

Example 2:  The movie's intertextuality, with its references to classic horror films, adds another layer of meaning for cinephiles.  这部电影的互文性，以及它对经典恐怖电影的引用，为影迷增添了另一层含义。|近义词：allusion, reference, echo|反义词: originality, novelty |词性: noun
