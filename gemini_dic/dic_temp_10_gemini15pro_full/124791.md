mty:/ˈɛmti/| adj.|空的，空虚的|Empty refers to something containing nothing or lacking its usual or expected contents.  It can also describe a feeling of lacking purpose or meaning.|The bottle is empty. 瓶子是空的。|synonyms: vacant, void, bare, unfilled, hollow|antonyms: full, occupied, replete|adjective

mty:/ˈɛmti/| v.|倒空，腾空|To empty something means to remove all its contents.  It can also mean to discharge or unload something.|Please empty the trash can. 请把垃圾桶倒空。|synonyms: evacuate, clear, drain, unload|antonyms: fill, load|verb

mty:/ˈɛmti/| n.|空的东西，空车|An empty can refer to a container that is empty, such as an empty bottle or an empty train carriage. This usage is less common than the adjective or verb forms.|The train pulled into the station with several empties. 火车拉着几节空车厢进站了。|synonyms: void|antonyms: full|noun
