gestour: /ˈdʒɛstər/ | n. | storyteller, historical narrator; a professional reciter of romances, tales, etc., especially one attached to a court or noble household | A gestour would entertain the court with tales of bravery and adventure. | The gestour captivated his audience with a thrilling tale of a knight's quest. | synonym: minstrel, bard, storyteller, narrator | antonym: listener, audience | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Anglo-Norman gestour, Old French gestour (“storyteller”), from geste (“deed, exploit, story, tale”), from Latin gesta (“things done, deeds, exploits”). Ultimately from the verb gerere (“to carry, to bear, to manage, to conduct”).
