courtesan: /ˈkɔːrtɪzæn/ (US), /ˈkɔːtɪzæn/ (UK)| n. |妓女；高级妓女 (尤指旧时受过良好教育，有艺术才能，出入于上流社会的妓女)|A woman, especially in the past, who had sexual relationships with rich and powerful men.|The courtesan was known for her wit and charm, captivating the hearts of many noblemen.|近义词: prostitute, harlot, geisha (in a specific cultural context)|反义词: nun, chaste woman|词性: noun

Etymology:
From Old French *corteysane*, feminine of *corteysan*, from Old Italian *cortigiana*, feminine of *cortigiano*, meaning "courtier," from *corte* meaning "court." Ultimately from Latin *cohors* meaning "enclosed yard," hence "retinue, cohort."
