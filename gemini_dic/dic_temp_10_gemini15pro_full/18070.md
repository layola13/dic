beguinage:/ˈbeɡɪnɪdʒ/ /bɛɡɪˈnɑʒ/ | n. |女修道院，贞节妇人院;寡妇院|A community of small houses occupied by beguines, especially in the Netherlands and Belgium. A beguinage is typically enclosed by a wall and includes a chapel and communal facilities.  |The beguinage in Bruges is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. 布鲁日的贞节妇人院是联合国教科文组织世界遗产。|

The city of Amsterdam still has several well-preserved beguinages. 阿姆斯特丹市仍然有几个保存完好的贞节妇人院。

|Synonyms: convent, cloister, religious community|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps secular community) | noun |
