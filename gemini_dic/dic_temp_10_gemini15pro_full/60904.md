elenchus:/ɪˈlɛŋkəs/ | n. | 辩驳法，苏格拉底式诘问法 | A method of questioning used by Socrates to elicit truth by exposing contradictions in the interlocutor's arguments.  It involves a series of questions and answers, leading the person being questioned to ultimately contradict themselves and thus reveal their lack of true understanding. |  Socrates employed the elenchus to challenge the beliefs of his students. 苏格拉底运用辩驳法来挑战他学生的信念。

The elenchus is a powerful tool for critical thinking. 辩驳法是批判性思维的有力工具。| 近义词：cross-examination, refutation, interrogation | 反义词：confirmation, agreement | 词性: noun
