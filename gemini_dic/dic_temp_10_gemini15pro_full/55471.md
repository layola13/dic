distortion:/dɪˈstɔːrʃn/
n.| 扭曲，变形；失真；曲解|The earthquake caused a distortion of the railway lines. 地震导致铁路扭曲变形。|
Etymology:  From Late Latin *distortionem,  from Latin distortus (past participle of distorquere 'to twist different ways'), from dis- 'apart, away' + torquere 'to twist'.
Synonyms: deformation, contortion, warping, misrepresentation, perversion
Antonyms: accuracy, truth, clarity, precision
Word type: Noun

distort:/dɪˈstɔːrt/
v.| 扭曲，使变形；歪曲；使失真|The media distorted the facts about the incident. 媒体歪曲了事件的真相。|
Etymology:  From Latin distortus (past participle of distorquere 'to twist different ways'), from dis- 'apart, away' + torquere 'to twist'.
Synonyms: twist, deform, warp, misrepresent, falsify
Antonyms: clarify, represent truthfully, maintain
Word type: Verb
