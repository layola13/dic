log-of-wood:/lɔːɡ əv wʊd/ | n. |原木，木头|A section of a tree trunk that has been felled and trimmed, ready for sawing into lumber. |例句：The pioneers built their cabins from logs-of-wood.  拓荒者用原木建造他们的小屋。

例句：He chopped several logs-of-wood for the fireplace. 他砍了几根原木准备生火。|近义词：log, timber, wood|反义词: (无直接反义词，可以考虑processed wood 加工木材)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *logge, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to lie (as in something lying on the ground). The "of wood" specification is added for clarity, as "log" can also refer to other things (e.g., ship's log, logarithm).
