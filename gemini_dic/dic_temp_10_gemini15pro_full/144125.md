outscout:/ˌaʊtˈskaʊt/
verb
胜过侦察；侦察方面胜过
to surpass in scouting; to be better at scouting than someone else.
The experienced team easily outscouted their opponents and knew their every move.  经验丰富的团队轻松地胜过对手的侦察，知道他们的一举一动。
The home team was completely outscouted and lost the game badly. 主队在侦察方面完全被胜过，惨败。
近义词: outmaneuver, surpass, outwit
反义词: be outsmarted, be outwitted
词性: verb

outscout:/ˌaʊtˈskaʊt/
noun
更优秀的侦察员
a person who is better at scouting than another.
He proved to be a valuable outscout, providing crucial information about the enemy's position. 他被证明是一位很有价值的侦察员，提供了关于敌人位置的关键信息。
The team hired a renowned outscout to help them prepare for the playoffs.  该队聘请了一位著名的侦察员来帮助他们备战季后赛。
近义词: superior scout, better scout
反义词: inferior scout, poorer scout
词性: noun


词源:  "out-" (prefix meaning "exceeding, surpassing") + "scout" (verb/noun related to reconnaissance and exploration).  由前缀"out-"（表示“超过，胜过”）和"scout"（与侦察和探索相关的动词/名词）组成。
