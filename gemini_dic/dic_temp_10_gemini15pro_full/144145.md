outshame:/ˌaʊtˈʃeɪm/
verb
使（某人）感到羞愧胜过；使（某人）羞愧得不敢（做某事）
To shame someone more than they shame you; to make someone so ashamed that they do not do something.
She outshamed him into returning the stolen money. 她让他羞愧难当，只得把偷来的钱还了回去。
近义词:humiliate, mortify, embarrass
反义词:honor, praise, commend
词性:v.

Etymology:
From out- + shame.  "Out-" acts as a prefix indicating exceeding or surpassing, as in "outdo" or "outperform".
