furlough:/ˈfɜːrloʊ (US), ˈfɜːləʊ (UK)| n. v.|休假，暂时解雇|A period of time when an employee is told not to come to work, often because there is not enough work, but they are kept on the company’s payroll and are expected to return to work later.|例句：Many companies were forced to furlough employees during the pandemic. 许多公司在大流行期间被迫让员工休假。

例句：He took a furlough to care for his sick mother. 他休假去照顾他生病的母亲。|近义词：leave of absence, layoff, break, sabbatical|反义词：work, employment|词性: noun, verb


furlough:/ˈfɜːrloʊ (US), ˈfɜːləʊ (UK)| v.|准假，暂时解雇|To grant a leave of absence to an employee, often temporarily because of lack of work.|例句：The company furloughed 100 workers due to the economic downturn. 由于经济低迷，公司暂时解雇了100名工人。

例句：She was furloughed for three months but then recalled. 她被停薪留职三个月，然后被召回。|近义词：lay off, dismiss, suspend|反义词：hire, employ, recall|词性: verb
