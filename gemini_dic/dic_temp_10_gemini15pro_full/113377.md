lycopod:/ˈlaɪkəˌpɑːd/ | n. |石松，石松类植物 | Any of various vascular plants of the class Lycopodiopsida, having branching stems covered with small overlapping leaves and reproducing by spores. These plants are among the oldest living vascular plants. |  Example 1: The lycopod fossils found in the coal mine date back millions of years.  煤矿中发现的石松化石可以追溯到数百万年前。

Example 2: Some species of lycopod are used in herbal medicine.  某些种类的石松被用于草药。 | Synonyms: club moss, ground pine | Antonyms: N/A | noun

lycopod:/ˈlaɪkəˌpɑːd/ | adj. |石松类的 | Relating to or characteristic of lycopods. | Example 1: The lycopod forests of the Carboniferous period were much larger than they are today. 石炭纪的石松森林比现在大得多。

Example 2: Scientists study lycopod anatomy to understand the evolution of vascular plants. 科学家研究石松的解剖结构以了解维管植物的进化。| Synonyms: lycopodian | Antonyms: N/A | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *lycopode*, from New Latin *Lycopodium*, from Ancient Greek *λυκόποδιον* (lukópodion, “wolf's foot”), from *λύκος* (lúkos, “wolf”) + *πούς* (poús, “foot”). The name refers to the resemblance of the plant's root to a wolf's paw.
