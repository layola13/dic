faulcon:/ˈfɔːlkən/ | n. |猎鹰|A bird of prey with long pointed wings and a notched beak, known for its swift flight and keen eyesight.  It is often trained for falconry.|The falcon soared high above the mountains.  猎鹰在群山上空翱翔。|近义词:hawk, raptor|反义词:prey|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *faucon*, from Late Latin *falconem*, accusative of *falco* ("falcon"), from Latin *falx, falcis* ("sickle, scythe"), referring to the bird's hooked claws.
