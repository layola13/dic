dialectician:/ˌdaɪəlekˈtɪʃən/ | n. | 辩证法家；辩证论者 | A person skilled in dialectic; a logician, especially one who studies or teaches dialectic. | He was a brilliant dialectician, adept at dissecting arguments and exposing fallacies. 他是一位杰出的辩证法家，擅长剖析论点和揭露谬误。 |  The dialectician skillfully refuted his opponent's claims. 这位辩证论者巧妙地驳斥了对手的主张。 | Synonyms: logician, debater, arguer, reasoner | Antonyms: dogmatist | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dialecticus, from Ancient Greek διαλεκτικός (dialektikós, “of or for conversation, dialectical”), from διάλεκτος (dialektos, “conversation, dialect, language”).
