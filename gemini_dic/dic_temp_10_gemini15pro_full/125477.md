multipole:/ˈmʌltɪpoʊl/ (US), /ˈmʌltɪpəʊl/ (UK)| n.|多极子|A system in physics and mathematics characterized by having multiple poles, such as a dipole (two poles), quadrupole (four poles), octupole (eight poles), etc., where each pole represents a source of a physical field like an electric or magnetic field.|The antenna is designed as a multipole array to improve its directivity.|Synonyms: multipole moment, multipole expansion|Antonyms: monopole (arguably)|Noun
