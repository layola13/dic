bonafide:/ˌboʊnə ˈfaɪdi/ /ˌbɒnə ˈfaɪdi/| adjective| 真实的，真诚的；合法的|Genuine; real; not counterfeit.  Made or carried out in good faith. |She had a bonafide offer of a job in Paris. 她有一份在巴黎工作的真正录取通知。|Synonyms: authentic, genuine, real, legitimate, valid|Antonyms: fake, counterfeit, spurious, sham|词性:形容词

bonafide:/ˌboʊnə ˈfaɪdi/ /ˌbɒnə ˈfaɪdi/| noun| 真实；真诚；善意|Something that is genuine, real, or done in good faith. (Often used in the plural.)|The museum curator examined the artifacts to determine their bonafides.  博物馆馆长检查了这些文物，以确定它们的真伪。|Synonyms: authenticity, genuineness, validity|Antonyms: falsehood, deception, fraud|词性:名词

bonafide:/ˌboʊnə ˈfaɪdi/ /ˌbɒnə ˈfaɪdi/| adverb| 真诚地；合法地| Genuinely; authentically; without deception. | He acted bonafide in all his dealings with the company. 他在与公司的所有交易中都表现得真诚。|Synonyms: genuinely, truly, sincerely, legitimately|Antonyms: falsely, deceptively, fraudulently|词性:副词
