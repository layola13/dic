grovelled:/ˈɡrɒvəld/ (UK) /ˈɡrɑːvəld/ (US)| v. |卑躬屈膝，匍匐；奴颜婢膝|To behave in a subservient or demeaning manner, especially to gain favor or avoid punishment. |He grovelled at the feet of the king, begging for mercy. 他跪在国王脚下，乞求怜悯。|Synonyms:  crawl, cringe, fawn, kowtow|Antonyms: defy, resist, stand up to|verb

grovel:/ˈɡrɒvəl/ (UK) /ˈɡrɑːvəl/ (US)| v. |卑躬屈膝，匍匐；奴颜婢膝|To behave in a subservient or demeaning manner, especially to gain favor or avoid punishment. |She had to grovel to her boss to get the promotion. 她不得不向老板卑躬屈膝才能得到晋升。|Synonyms: crawl, cringe, fawn, kowtow|Antonyms: defy, resist, stand up to|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *grovelen, *grufelen (“to lie prone, grovel”), probably from Old Norse *grufla (“to feel with the hands, grope”), related to grufe (“pit, trench”).

USAGE->
