nationalizers:/ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪzərz/
n.
国有化支持者；实行国有化的人
People who support or carry out the act of nationalization.
The nationalizers argued that key industries should be under state control. 国有化支持者认为关键产业应该由国家控制。
The government was full of nationalizers who believed in state ownership of industry. 政府里有很多国有化支持者，他们相信工业应该国有。
近义词:statists, advocates of nationalization
反义词:privatizers, proponents of privatization
词性:noun


nationalize:/ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz/
v.
国有化
To transfer (a major branch of industry or commerce) from private to state ownership or control.
The government decided to nationalize the railways. 政府决定将铁路国有化。
Several countries nationalized their oil industries in the 1970s.  几个国家在20世纪70年代将石油工业国有化。
近义词:expropriate, socialize
反义词:privatize, denationalize
词性:verb

nationalization:/ˌnæʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
n.
国有化
The act or process of nationalizing.
The nationalization of the banks caused a lot of controversy.  银行国有化引起了很多争议。
The country underwent a period of nationalization after the war. 战后，这个国家经历了一段国有化时期。
近义词:state ownership, collectivization
反义词:privatization, denationalization
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "nationalize" + "-er" (agent suffix). "Nationalize" comes from "national" + "-ize" (verb-forming suffix). "National" ultimately derives from Latin "natio" (nation, race, stock, kind, species).
