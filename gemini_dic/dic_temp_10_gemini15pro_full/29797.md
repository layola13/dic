caucus:/ˈkɔːkəs/ (US) /ˈkɔːkəs/ (UK)| n. vi.| 政治会议；核心小组会议；预选会议| A meeting of members of a political party or movement, especially in the US, to choose candidates or decide policy.  A group of people with shared concerns within a larger group, especially in politics.|

例句：
The Iowa caucuses are the first major contest of the presidential election season.
爱荷华州的预选会议是总统选举季的第一场重要竞赛。

The Democratic caucus met to discuss strategy for the upcoming election.
民主党核心小组开会讨论即将到来的选举的策略。

近义词：meeting, conference, gathering, assembly
反义词:  N/A
词性: noun, verb


caucuses:/ˈkɔːkəsɪz/ (US) /ˈkɔːkəsɪz/ (UK)| n. | 政治会议（复数）；核心小组会议（复数）；预选会议（复数）| The plural form of "caucus". Refers to multiple meetings of members of a political party or movement to choose candidates or decide policy. |

例句：
Several candidates emerged from the various state caucuses.
几位候选人从各州的预选会议中脱颖而出。

The party caucuses are often dominated by activists.
党的核心小组会议通常由积极分子主导。

近义词：meetings, conferences, gatherings, assemblies
反义词:  N/A
词性: noun


词源:  From Algonquian (probably Natick) *cau·cau·as·u 'one who advises'.
