pasty:/ˈpeɪsti/ (US), /ˈpɑːsti/ (UK)| adj. n.|苍白的；病态的；黏糊糊的；肉馅饼| Pale and unhealthy in appearance; like paste in texture; a baked pastry filled with meat and vegetables.|例句：Her face was pasty and she looked unwell. 她的脸色苍白，看起来不舒服。|近义词：pale, wan, pallid, doughy, sickly|反义词：ruddy, healthy, robust|词性:形容词

pasty:/ˈpeɪsti/ (US), /ˈpɑːsti/ (UK)| n.|肉馅饼|A baked pastry filled with meat and vegetables.|例句：He bought a Cornish pasty for lunch. 他午饭买了一个康沃尔肉馅饼。|近义词：pie, turnover|反义词:|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *pastee*, from Old French *paste*, from Late Latin *pasta* ("paste, dough"), from Greek *πάστα* (*pasta*, "barley porridge").  The adjective sense "pale" likely arose from the resemblance of such a complexion to dough.
