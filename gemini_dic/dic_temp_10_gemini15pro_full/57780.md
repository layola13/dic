dreariment: /ˈdriːrɪmənt/ | n. | 凄凉，沉闷，阴郁 | A state of dreariness; gloominess. |  The long winter brought a sense of dreariment to the village. 漫长的冬天给村庄带来了一种沉闷的感觉。|  The dreariment of the abandoned house was palpable. 这栋废弃房屋的阴郁气氛是显而易见的。 | synonyms: gloom, dreariness, desolation, bleakness | antonyms: cheerfulness, brightness, joy, vibrancy | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From dreary + -ment (a suffix forming nouns of action or process or result). Dreary itself comes from Old English *dreorig, meaning "sorrowful," "bloody," related to Old High German trurag "mournful."
