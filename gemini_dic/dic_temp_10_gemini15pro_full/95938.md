incubation:/ˌɪŋkjuˈbeɪʃən/ | n. | 孵化；潜伏期；培养；发展 |The process of keeping something at a suitable temperature and humidity for it to develop; the period between infection and the appearance of symptoms; the development of something over time. |The eggs require a period of incubation before they hatch.  这些蛋需要一段时间的孵化才能孵出。|Synonyms: brooding, hatching, gestation, development, cultivation |Antonyms: dormancy, inactivity, suppression |noun

incubation:/ˌɪŋkjuˈbeɪʃən/ | n. | 孵化；潜伏期；培养；发展 |The virus has a long incubation period.  这种病毒潜伏期很长。|Synonyms: latency, quiescence |Antonyms: manifestation, outbreak |noun

incubation:/ˌɪŋkjuˈbeɪʃən/ | n. | 孵化；潜伏期；培养；发展 |The project is still in its incubation stage.  该项目仍处于孵化阶段。|Synonyms:  germination,  nurturing |Antonyms: stagnation, decline |noun
