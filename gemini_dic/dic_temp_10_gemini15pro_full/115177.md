manichaean:/ˌmænɪˈkiːən/| adj. n.|摩尼教的；二元论的；摩尼教徒|Of or relating to Manichaeism, a dualistic religious system originating in Persia in the 3rd century AD.  Also used more broadly to describe anything characterized by a dualistic worldview, seeing the world as a struggle between good and evil.|The ancient manuscript contained complex Manichaean diagrams depicting the battle between light and darkness.| 近义词:dualistic, gnostic|反义词:monistic, unified|词性:adjective, noun

manichaean:/ˌmænɪˈkiːən/| n.|摩尼教徒|A follower of Manichaeism.|Historical records suggest that there were many Manichaean communities throughout the Roman Empire.| 近义词:dualist, gnostic|反义词:n/a|词性:noun
