(third-person singular simple present douses, present participle dousing, simple past and past participle doused)|浇灭，熄灭；弄湿，浸泡|To extinguish something, typically a fire, with water or other liquid; to soak or drench something or someone with liquid.|He doused the campfire with water before leaving. 他离开前用水浇灭了篝火。|synonyms: extinguish, quench, soak, drench|antonyms: ignite, kindle, light|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dousen, from Anglo-Norman *duscher, *douser, perhaps from Vulgar Latin *ducāre, from Latin dūcō (“I lead, draw, bring”).  The original sense was likely "to plunge into water," which evolved to "extinguish with water" and then to "drench."
