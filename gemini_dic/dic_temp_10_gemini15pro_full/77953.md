genpaku:/ɡenˈpɑːkuː/ |n.|  兰学家;  |A Japanese scholar of Western sciences during the Edo period, especially medicine and botany.  Sugita Genpaku is a famous example. | Sugita Genpaku translated a Dutch anatomical text, Kaitai Shinsho.  杉田玄白翻译了一本荷兰解剖学著作《解体新书》。|  Rangaku scholar, Dutch studies scholar | N/A | noun
Etymology: From Japanese 玄白 (genpaku), a combination of 玄 (gen, "mysterious," "profound," "dark") and 白 (paku/haku, "white," "clear"). This was the personal name of Sugita Genpaku (1733–1817), a prominent Rangaku scholar.  His influence led to the word "genpaku" being associated with Rangaku scholars in general.
