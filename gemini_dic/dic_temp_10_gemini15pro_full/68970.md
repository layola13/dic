fathom:/ˈfæðəm/ | n. | 英寻（水深单位，等于6英尺或1.8288米）; 理解，领会 | A fathom is a unit of length equal to six feet (1.8 meters), chiefly used in reference to the depth of water.  It can also mean to understand something thoroughly. | The ship sank in over 100 fathoms of water. 这艘船沉没在100多英寻深的水中。

fathom:/ˈfæðəm/ | v. |  理解，领会；测水深 | To fathom something is to understand it completely. | I can't fathom why she would do such a thing. 我无法理解她为什么 would 做这样的事。

Etymology: From Old English *fæðm, from Proto-Germanic *faþmaz (embrace, fathom), from Proto-Indo-European *pet- (to spread, stretch).  The original sense was "outstretched arms".
