britt:/brɪt/| n.|不列颠人，英国人|A person from Britain, especially England or Wales. |例句：He's a Britt, but he's lived in the US for years. 他是英国人，但在美国生活了很多年。

例句：Many Britts enjoy a cup of tea in the afternoon. 许多英国人喜欢在下午喝杯茶。|近义词：Briton, Englishman, Englishwoman (if female)|反义词:None (nationality has no direct opposite)|词性:noun


brittleness:/ˈbrɪtlnəs/| n.|脆性，易碎性；脆弱性|The quality of being easily broken or damaged; fragility.|例句：The brittleness of the material made it unsuitable for the project.  这种材料的脆性使其不适合该项目。

例句：The bone's brittleness increased with age. 骨头的脆性随着年龄的增长而增加。|近义词：fragility, friability, breakability|反义词:toughness, resilience, durability|词性:noun


brittle:/ˈbrɪtl/| adj.|易碎的，脆弱的；(声音)尖厉的；(态度)冷淡的|Hard but liable to break or shatter easily; (of a sound) sharp and clear; (of a person or behavior) appearing or sounding aloof and unfriendly.|例句：The old vase was very brittle.  那个旧花瓶很易碎。

例句：Her voice was brittle with anger. 她的声音因愤怒而变得尖厉。|近义词：fragile, frail, breakable, crisp (for sound), curt (for manner)|反义词:tough, resilient, flexible, warm (for manner)|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English "Bryttas," plural of "Brytt," meaning "Briton," ultimately from Latin "Brittones" (inhabitants of Britain).  Related to Welsh "Brython."
