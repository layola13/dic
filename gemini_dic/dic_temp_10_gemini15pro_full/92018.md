hyperoxygenation:/ˌhaɪpərˌɒksɪdʒəˈneɪʃən/ /ˌhaɪpərˌɑːksɪdʒəˈneɪʃən/| n.|高氧；过度氧合|The process of increasing the oxygen content of the blood or tissues beyond normal levels.  This can be achieved by breathing pure oxygen or oxygen-enriched air. |The diver underwent hyperoxygenation before the deep dive. 潜水员在深潜前进行了过度氧合。|Synonyms: oxygen enrichment, superoxygenation|Antonyms: hypoxia, anoxia|noun

hyperoxygenate:/ˌhaɪpərˈɒksɪdʒəneɪt/ /ˌhaɪpərˈɑːksɪdʒəneɪt/| vt.|使氧合过度|To increase the oxygen content of the blood or tissues beyond normal levels.|The patient was hyperoxygenated before surgery. 病人在手术前被过度氧合。|Synonyms: oxygenate excessively|Antonyms: deprive of oxygen|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- (Greek: "over, excessive") + oxygen + -ation (suffix forming nouns of action or process).
