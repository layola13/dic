delinquent:/dɪˈlɪŋkwənt/| adj. n.|拖欠的，违法的；失职者，违法者|Failing to fulfill an obligation, especially to pay a debt; guilty of a minor offense.|例句：The delinquent account has been sent to collections.  这个拖欠的账户已经被送去催收了。|近义词：overdue, late, remiss, negligent, culpable|反义词：prompt, responsible, law-abiding|词性:形容词

delinquent:/dɪˈlɪŋkwənt/| n.|失职者，违法者|A person, especially a young person, who fails to fulfill an obligation or commits a minor offense.|例句：Juvenile delinquents are often given a second chance.  青少年犯罪者通常会被给予第二次机会。|近义词：offender, culprit, wrongdoer|反义词：law-abider, conformist|词性:名词

词源: From Latin *dēlinquēns*, present participle of *dēlinquere* (“to fail in duty, offend”), from *dē-* (“down, away from”) + *linquere* (“to leave”).

注意事项:  "delinquent" can be used as both an adjective and a noun.  As an adjective, it describes something that is overdue or unpaid. As a noun, it refers to a person, usually a minor, who has committed a crime.
