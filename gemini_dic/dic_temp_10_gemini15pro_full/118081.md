mendregal:/mɛnˈdreɪɡəl/ | n. | 曼德拉草 | A plant of southern Europe and North Africa with a forked root formerly thought to have magical powers and to shriek when pulled from the ground.  A Mediterranean plant (Mandragora officinarum) of the nightshade family, having a fleshy, often forked root somewhat resembling a human form, to which magical properties were formerly ascribed. |  Legend has it that the mendregal screams when pulled from the earth.  传说曼德拉草被拔出地面时会尖叫。 |Synonyms: mandrake | Antonyms: N/A | Noun
