blackbox:/ˈblækˌbɑːks/| n. |黑匣子；暗箱；秘密|A device, system, or object which can be viewed in terms of its inputs and outputs (or transfer characteristics), without any knowledge of its internal workings. A theoretical construct used to represent a complex system whose precise internal workings are unknown or irrelevant to the problem being considered.|The algorithm worked like a blackbox, no one knew how it selected the winners.|近义词：mystery, enigma, closed book|反义词:transparent, open, clear|词性:名词

blackbox:/ˈblækˌbɑːks/| adj. |黑匣子的；暗箱操作的|Relating to or resembling a black box, especially in being opaque or mysterious in operation.|The company's decision-making process was a blackbox, leaving employees in the dark.|近义词：opaque, obscure, hidden|反义词:transparent, open, clear|词性:形容词
