obdiplostemony: /ˌɒbdɪplɒˈstɛməni/
noun
反向二强雄蕊
A condition in flowers where the stamens are arranged in two whorls, with the outer whorl opposite the petals and the inner whorl opposite the sepals.
The unusual floral structure of obdiplostemony is thought to have evolved from diplostemony.
近义词: diplostemony (somewhat)
反义词: (No direct antonym.  Perhaps haplostemony or other specific stamen arrangements could be considered contrasting)
词性: n.

Etymology:  From Greek *ob* (against, opposite) + *diplo* (double) + *stemon* (thread, stamen)
