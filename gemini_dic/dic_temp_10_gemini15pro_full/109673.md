leucophlegmacy: /ˌluːkəˈflɛɡməsi/ | n. | 粘液过多；白痰；淡漠 | A condition characterized by a pale, flabby, and watery condition of the body, often associated with phlegmatic temperament.  It refers to an excess of phlegm, especially white phlegm, or to a general sluggishness or apathy. |  His leucophlegmacy made him seem indifferent to the crisis. 他的粘液过多症使他看起来对危机漠不关心。 |  Synonyms: sluggishness, apathy, inertia, languor, torpor | Antonyms: energy, vigor, activity, liveliness, enthusiasm | noun

leucophlegmatic: /ˌluːkəflɛɡˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 粘液过多的；白痰的；淡漠的 |  Characterized by or exhibiting leucophlegmacy; sluggish and apathetic. | He had a leucophlegmatic temperament, rarely showing any strong emotion.  他性格淡漠，很少表现出强烈的情感。| Synonyms: phlegmatic, sluggish, apathetic, indifferent, listless | Antonyms: energetic, vigorous, enthusiastic, passionate, lively | adjective
