donatism:/ˈdɒnətɪzəm/ | n. | 多纳图斯派; 多纳徒主义 | The heresy that the validity of sacraments depends on the moral character of the minister.  A schismatic movement in the early Christian church (chiefly in North Africa) beginning in 311 with the election of Caecilian as bishop of Carthage. The Donatists, holding that those who had lapsed from the faith during persecution could not administer the sacraments, set up a rival bishopric under Majorinus, later succeeded by Donatus. |  The Donatist schism weakened the church in North Africa for centuries. 多纳图斯教派的分裂使北非教会衰弱了几个世纪。|  The Donatist controversy highlighted the issue of the church's purity. 多纳图斯派的争论突出了教会纯洁性的问题。 | 近义词: schism, heresy | 反义词: orthodoxy | 词性: noun
