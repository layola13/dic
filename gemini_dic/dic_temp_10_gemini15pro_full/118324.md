meretriciously:/ˌmɛrɪˈtrɪʃəsli/ | adv. |俗艳地；华而不实地|In a way that appears attractive but has no real value or integrity. | She dressed meretriciously in a sequined gown and heavy makeup. 她穿着一件亮片礼服，化着浓妆，显得俗不可耐。|  The politician meretriciously promised tax cuts he knew he couldn't deliver.  这位政客用华而不实的手段承诺减税，他知道自己无法兑现。 | 近义词：gaudy, tawdry, flashy, ostentatious | 反义词：simple, understated, genuine, authentic | 词性: 副词

meretricious:/ˌmɛrɪˈtrɪʃəs/ | adj. |俗艳的；华而不实的|Seemingly attractive but having in reality no value or integrity. | The meretricious glitter of the casino attracted many tourists. 赌场俗丽的闪光吸引了许多游客。 |  The movie's plot was meretricious, relying on special effects rather than substance. 这部电影的情节华而不实，依靠特效而不是实质内容。| 近义词：gaudy, tawdry, flashy, ostentatious | 反义词：simple, understated, genuine, authentic | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Latin *meretricius, from meretrix "prostitute," from merēre, mereri "to earn, deserve, gain, acquire."  最初指妓女的，引申为“吸引人的但没有真正价值的”。
