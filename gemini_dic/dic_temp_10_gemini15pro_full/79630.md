glossator:/ˈɡlɒseɪtər/ (US) /ˈɡlɑːseɪtər/ (UK)| n.|注释者，注解者|A scholar who wrote glosses, which were annotations or explanations of difficult words or passages in texts, especially legal or biblical texts.|The glossator meticulously explained the obscure legal terms in the ancient manuscript. 注释者一丝不苟地解释了古代手稿中晦涩的法律术语。|近义词：commentator, annotator, expounder, interpreter|反义词:|noun

gloss:/ɡlɒs/ (US) /ɡlɑːs/ (UK)| n. v.|光泽，注释；给…加注释|a superficially attractive appearance or impression.  To provide an explanation or interpretation, often in the margin of a text.|The gloss on the furniture made it look expensive. 家具上的光泽使它看起来很昂贵。
He glossed the difficult passages in the poem for his students. 他为学生们注释了诗中难懂的段落。 |近义词：shine, luster, sheen, annotation, explanation|反义词:dullness|noun, verb
