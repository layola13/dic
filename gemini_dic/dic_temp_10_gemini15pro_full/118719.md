metafictions: /ˌmetəˈfɪkʃənz/
| n. |元小说|Fiction that consciously examines the nature of fiction itself, often by drawing attention to its own artificiality.  It explores the relationship between fiction and reality, blurring the lines between the two.|The novel is full of metafictions, with characters constantly questioning their own existence within the narrative.|近义词: self-reflexive fiction, postmodern fiction |反义词: realist fiction |词性: noun

Etymology:
"meta-" (Greek): beyond, after, or about + "fiction" (Latin: fictio, from fingere, to shape, feign): a literary work based on the imagination and not necessarily on fact.
