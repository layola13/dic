cheerfulnesses:/ˈtʃɪərfəlnəsɪz/
n.
快乐，高兴，欢快
The state of being cheerful; happiness and good spirits.
Her cheerfulnesses were infectious, brightening everyone's day. 她的快乐很有感染力，照亮了每个人的一天。
The room was filled with cheerfulnesses. 房间里充满了欢乐。
Synonyms: joy, happiness, gaiety, mirth, merriment, lightheartedness
Antonyms: sadness, gloom, dejection, melancholy, depression
词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From cheerfulness + -es (plural suffix).  Cheerfulness itself comes from cheerful + -ness. Cheerful traces back to Middle English *cherful, from chere "face, countenance" (from Old French chiere, ultimately from Latin cara "face") + -ful "full of."
