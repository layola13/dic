noneconomy:/ˌnɒnɪˈkɒnəmi/ | n. | 非经济 |  Lack of economy; wastefulness or extravagance.  A situation or activity that is not profitable or cost-effective. | The company's noneconomy in purchasing led to significant losses.  这家公司在采购方面的非经济性导致了重大损失。|Near Synonyms: waste, extravagance, uneconomicalness | Antonyms: economy, thrift, frugality | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + economy.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not" or "the opposite of." "Economy" comes from the Old French "economie," ultimately from the Greek "oikonomia," meaning "household management."
