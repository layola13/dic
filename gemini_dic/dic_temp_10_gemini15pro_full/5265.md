allomorph:/ˈæləmɔːrf/ | n. | 同位异音 | One of two or more alternative forms of a morpheme. In English, the plural morpheme has three allomorphs: /-s/, /-z/, and /-ɪz/, as in cats, dogs, and horses. | The word "cats" contains two morphemes, "cat" and the plural allomorph "-s". | synonyms: variant, variation, form | antonyms: (No direct antonyms for a technical linguistic term like allomorph) | noun
