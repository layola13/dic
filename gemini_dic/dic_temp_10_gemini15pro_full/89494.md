honeypots:/ˈhʌnipɒts/ (UK), /ˈhʌnipɑːts/ (US)| n.|蜜罐；诱惑物|A honeypot is a computer system or network site that appears to be vulnerable to attack but is actually closely monitored and designed to attract and trap potential attackers, gathering information about their methods and motives.  A honeypot acts as a decoy, diverting attackers from real targets and allowing security personnel to study their techniques.|例句：The company set up a honeypot to lure hackers and learn about their tactics.  公司设置了一个蜜罐来引诱黑客并了解他们的策略。

例句：The honeypot successfully captured data about the attacker's malware.  蜜罐成功捕获了有关攻击者恶意软件的数据。|近义词：decoy, trap, lure|反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "legitimate target")|词性: noun


honeypot:/ˈhʌnipɒt/ (UK), /ˈhʌnipɑːts/ (US)| n.|蜜罐（单数形式）|Same definition as above, but singular form.|例句：A honeypot can be a valuable tool for cybersecurity research.  蜜罐可以成为网络安全研究的宝贵工具。

例句：He configured the honeypot to mimic a vulnerable server.  他将蜜罐配置为模仿易受攻击的服务器。|近义词：decoy, trap, lure|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性: noun
