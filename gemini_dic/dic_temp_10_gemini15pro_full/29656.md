catfish:/ˈkætˌfɪʃ/ (US), /ˈkætˌfɪʃ/ (UK)| n. v.|鲶鱼；欺骗感情的人；用鲶鱼捕鱼|A fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth, typically bottom-dwelling. Also, a person who creates a false online persona to trick or deceive others.|例句：I caught a huge catfish in the river. 我在河里钓到了一条大鲶鱼。

|近义词：bottom-feeder, swindler (for the deceptive sense), |反义词: (No direct antonym.  Could consider "honest person" for the deceptive sense)|n.

catfish:/ˈkætˌfɪʃ/ (US), /ˈkætˌfɪʃ/ (UK)| v.|用鲶鱼捕鱼；欺骗（感情）|To fish for catfish. Also, to lure someone into a relationship by means of a fictional online persona.|例句：He was catfished by someone pretending to be a model. 他被一个假扮模特的人骗了感情。

|近义词：deceive, trick, mislead (for the deceptive sense)|反义词: (No direct antonym for the fishing sense. For the deceptive sense, consider "be truthful")|v.
