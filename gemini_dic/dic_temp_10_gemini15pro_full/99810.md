intermigrating:/ˌɪntərˈmaɪɡreɪtɪŋ/
| adj. |互相迁移的，互相移居的|Moving from one place to another and mixing with other groups, often referring to populations or species.|例句：The intermigrating populations exchanged cultural practices. 互相迁移的人群交换了文化习俗。|例句：Scientists studied the intermigrating herds of wildebeest and zebra. 科学家们研究了互相迁徙的牛羚和斑马群。|近义词：migrating, commingling, integrating|反义词：segregating, isolating|词性：形容词

Etymology:
From inter- (between) + migrating (present participle of migrate, from Latin migrare "to change one's abode, depart," from migratus, past participle of migrare, perhaps related to the Greek ameibein "to change," or perhaps a compound of *mi- "away" + *ag- "to drive, lead.")
