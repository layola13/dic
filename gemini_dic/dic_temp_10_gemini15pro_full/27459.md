camp-follower:/ˈkæmp ˌfɒləwə(r)/ (US) /ˈkæmp ˌfɑːləwər/| n.|随军人员，营妓|A person who follows a military camp, especially to sell goods or services or, historically, to engage in prostitution.|During the war, many camp-followers trailed the army, hoping to make a living by selling supplies.  战争期间，许多随军人员跟随军队，希望能通过出售物资来谋生。|Synonyms: hanger-on, tagalong, straggler|Antonyms: soldier, combatant|Noun

camp-follower:/ˈkæmp ˌfɒləwə(r)/ (US) /ˈkæmp ˌfɑːləwər/| n.|追随者，攀附者|A person who follows or attaches themselves to someone or something, often in a servile or opportunistic way.|He had a small band of camp-followers who agreed with everything he said. 他有一小群追随者，同意他所说的一切。|Synonyms: sycophant, flatterer, groupie|Antonyms: leader, independent|Noun

词源:  From "camp" + "follower".  The term originally referred to civilians who followed armies, providing services such as laundry, cooking, and sometimes prostitution.  Over time, it has broadened to include anyone who follows a group or person in a servile or opportunistic manner.
