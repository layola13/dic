eclipse: /ɪˈklɪps/ (US), /ɪˈklɪps/ (UK)| n. vt.|日食，月食；黯然失色，衰落|An eclipse occurs when one celestial body obscures another.|The lunar eclipse was visible across much of North America.|近义词：obscuration, darkening, decline|反义词: illumination, brightening, ascendance|词性:名词,动词

eclipse: /ɪˈklɪps/ (US), /ɪˈklɪps/ (UK)| vt.|使黯然失色，使相形见绌；遮蔽|Her performance eclipsed all the others.|The new building eclipsed the old one in size.|近义词：outshadow, surpass, obscure|反义词: highlight, emphasize|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *eclipser*, from Latin *eclipsis*, from Ancient Greek ἔκλειψις (ékleipsis, “eclipse, abandonment”), from ἐκλείπω (ekleípō, “I abandon, forsake, leave out, vanish”), from ἐκ (ek, “out”) + λείπω (leípō, “I leave”).
