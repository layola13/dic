endoglossic:/ˌɛndoʊˈɡlɒsɪk/ | adj. | 内部的，内部语言的 | Relating to a language or dialect that originates within a community, specifically referring to words borrowed from one's own language rather than from another language.  It describes words formed from elements already present within the language. | Example: The word "smog" is an endoglossic creation in English, formed by combining "smoke" and "fog."  烟雾（smog）这个词是英语中的一个内部语言创造，由“烟雾（smoke）”和“雾（fog）”组合而成。

| Example:  Many technical terms in linguistics are endoglossic, derived from Greek and Latin roots already present in academic English. 许多语言学中的技术术语是内部语言的，源自学术英语中已经存在的希腊语和拉丁语词根。| 近义词: native, indigenous | 反义词: exoglossic, borrowed | 词性: adjective
