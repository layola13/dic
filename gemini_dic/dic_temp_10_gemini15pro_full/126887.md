narcissism:/ˈnɑːrsɪsɪzəm/| n. |自恋；自我陶醉|Excessive self-love and admiration, often combined with a lack of empathy for others.  A psychological condition characterized by self-preoccupation, an exaggerated sense of self-importance, and a need for admiration.|His narcissism prevented him from seeing his own flaws. 他的自恋让他看不到自己的缺点。|Synonyms: vanity, self-love, egotism, conceit|Antonyms: humility, modesty|noun

narcissist:/ˈnɑːrsɪsɪst/| n. |自恋者|A person who has an excessive admiration of themselves. Someone who suffers from narcissism.|He's such a narcissist; he only talks about himself. 他真是个自恋狂，他只谈论他自己。|Synonyms: egotist, self-admirer, braggart|Antonyms:  altruist, humanitarian|noun

narcissistic:/ˌnɑːrsɪˈsɪstɪk/| adj. |自恋的；自我陶醉的|Having or showing characteristics of excessive self-love and admiration.|Her narcissistic tendencies made her difficult to work with. 她的自恋倾向让她很难相处。|Synonyms: vain, self-centered, egotistical, conceited|Antonyms: humble, modest, selfless|adjective
