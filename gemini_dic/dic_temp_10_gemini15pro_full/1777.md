acquitted: /əˈkwɪtɪd/
verb
宣告无罪，开释
to free someone from a criminal charge by a verdict of not guilty
The jury acquitted the defendant of all charges. 陪审团宣告被告所有罪名不成立。

He was acquitted due to lack of evidence.  他因证据不足被无罪释放。

近义词: absolve, exonerate, clear, discharge
反义词: convict, condemn, incriminate
词性: past participle of "acquit"


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *acquiter*, from a- "to" + quite "free, clear". Ultimately from Latin quietus "at rest, quiet".

USAGE->
Simple Past:
The jury acquitted him last week. = 陪审团上周宣告他无罪。
She was acquitted of the murder charge. = 她被宣告谋杀罪名不成立。


Past Perfect:
He had been acquitted before the new evidence emerged. = 在新的证据出现之前，他已经被宣告无罪。
They had been acquitted of all charges by the time the media caught wind of the story. = 在媒体得知此事之前，他们已经被宣告所有罪名不成立。


Present Perfect:
He has been acquitted of all charges. = 他已被宣告所有罪名不成立。
She has finally been acquitted after a long trial. = 经过漫长的审判，她终于被宣告无罪。


Passive Voice:
The defendant was acquitted by the jury. = 被告被陪审团宣告无罪。
He was acquitted on all counts. = 他被宣告所有罪名不成立。


PHRASE->
be acquitted of (a charge/crime) = 被宣告(指控/罪行)无罪
acquit oneself well/badly = 表现出色/糟糕 (This refers to how someone performs in a challenging situation. It's related to the idea of discharging a duty or responsibility.)
be honorably acquitted = 光荣地被宣告无罪
acquittal on all charges = 所有指控均被宣告无罪
acquittal of the defendant = 被告被宣告无罪
