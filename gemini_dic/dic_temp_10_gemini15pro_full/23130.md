ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *burne, *borne, from Old English burna (“stream, brook, fountain, spring, well”), from Proto-Germanic *brunnō (“spring, well”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰrew- (“to boil, bubble, effervesce, spring”). Cognate with Scots burn (“stream”), North Frisian burn (“spring”), Saterland Frisian Burne (“spring”), West Frisian boarne (“source, spring”), Dutch born (“spring, well”), Low German Born (“spring, well”), German Brunnen (“well, fountain”), Danish borne (“spring, well”), Swedish brunn (“well”), Icelandic brunnr (“well”). Related also to Old English bryne (“burning, conflagration”). Doublet of burn.
