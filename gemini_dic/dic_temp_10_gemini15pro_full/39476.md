connives:/kəˈnaɪvz/
verb
纵容；默许；串通
secretly allow (something considered immoral, illegal, wrong, or harmful) to occur.
She connived with her boyfriend to rob the bank. 她和她的男朋友串通抢劫银行。
synonyms: conspire, collude, intrigue
antonyms: expose, obstruct, prevent
词性: 动词

connive:/kəˈnaɪvz/
verb
假装没看见；佯装不知
pretend not to know or notice something.
The government connived at the illegal activities of the company. 政府对这家公司的非法活动视而不见。
synonyms: overlook, ignore, disregard
antonyms: acknowledge, recognize, confront
词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *connivēre, present active infinitive of conniveō (“I close the eyes, wink, overlook, connive at”), from con- (“with, together”) + *nīvēre, related to nicō (“I wink, beckon”).
