aged:/ˈeɪdʒd/ (US) /eɪdʒd/ (UK)| adj. | 年迈的；陈年的；老化的 | Having lived for a long time; old.  Also, matured or developed over a period of time. |  The aged man sat on the park bench.  那位年迈的老人坐在公园的长椅上。| elderly, old, mature | young, new | adjective

aged:/ˈeɪdʒd/ (US) /eɪdʒd/ (UK)| n. | 老年人 | People who are old, considered collectively. | The aged require special care. 老年人需要特殊照顾。| elderly, seniors | youngsters, youth | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *aged*, *agede*, from Old French *aage*, *ege* ("age"), from Latin *aetās* ("age, lifetime"), from Proto-Italic *aetas*, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eyt- (“life, eternity”).
