faciend:/fæsˈiɛnd/ | n. |  (古体或法律)  地租; 租金; 收入 |  An amount paid as rent or a source of revenue, especially from land.  Derived from the Latin "faciendum" (something to be done),  referring to duties or services owed. Now mostly used in legal or historical contexts.| The lord collected his faciend from the tenants. 领主向佃户收取地租。 |Synonyms: rent, revenue, income, proceeds |Antonyms: expenditure, expense, cost | noun
