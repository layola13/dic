PHRASE->
There are not many common set phrases using "deridingly" as it's an adverb describing the *manner* of an action. It usually modifies verbs of communication like "say," "laugh," "comment," etc.  It's not typically part of a fixed phrase.


词源:
From "deride" + "-ingly".  "Deride" comes from Latin "deridere" (to laugh down, mock), from "de-" (down) + "ridere" (to laugh).
