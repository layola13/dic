anachronically:/əˌnæk.rəˈnɪk.li/ | adv. | 过期地；时代错误地；年代颠倒地 |  In a way that is chronologically misplaced. It refers to something that is out of its proper time in history. |  He anachronically wore a powdered wig to the disco. 他时代错误地戴着假发去迪斯科舞厅。| 近义词: unseasonably, untimely, inappropriately | 反义词: appropriately, timely, seasonably | 词性: adverb

anachronism:/əˈnæk.rə.nɪ.zəm/ | n. | 时代错误；过时现象 | Something (such as a word, an object, or an event) that is mistakenly placed in a time where it does not belong in a story, movie, etc.  | The movie is full of anachronisms. 这部电影充满了时代错误。| 近义词: misplacement, misdating, solecism | 反义词: contemporaneity | 词性: noun

anachronistic:/əˌnæk.rəˈnɪs.tɪk/ | adj. | 时代错误的；过时的 |  Belonging or appropriate to an earlier period, especially so as to seem conspicuously old-fashioned.  |  His anachronistic views on race were offensive. 他关于种族的过时观点令人反感。| 近义词: outdated, antiquated, obsolete | 反义词: modern, contemporary, current | 词性: adjective
