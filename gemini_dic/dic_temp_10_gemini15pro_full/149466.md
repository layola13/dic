paddock:/ˈpædək/ (US), /ˈpædək/ (UK)| n.|围场，小牧场|A small field near a stable or barn, used for exercising horses or for containing them before or after a race.  A fenced-in area, often near a racetrack, where horses are saddled and paraded before a race.|The horses were led around the paddock before the race. 赛马在比赛前被牵着绕围场走了一圈。

The champion horse was paraded in the paddock after the race.  冠军马在赛后在围场中亮相。|近义词：field, enclosure, pen, corral|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider) open field, wilderness|词性:noun


paddock:/ˈpædək/ (US), /ˈpædək/ (UK)| n.|（澳大利亚）指用于农业的土地，尤指被围起来放牧牲畜的土地|In Australia, a paddock refers to a field or piece of land used for agriculture, especially one enclosed for grazing livestock. |We drove past vast paddocks of sheep and cattle. 我们开车经过了广阔的绵羊和牛牧场。

The drought has left the paddocks dry and barren. 干旱使牧场干涸贫瘠。|近义词：field, pasture, land|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider) urban area, city|词性:noun
