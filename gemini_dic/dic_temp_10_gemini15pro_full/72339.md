词源: From Middle English *flour, flur, from Old French flor, flour (“flower, blossom; best part, prime; fine flour; menstruation”), from Latin flōs (genitive flōris) (“flower, blossom; youth, bloom of life; fine flour; surface; choice part; ornament”), from Proto-Italic *flōs, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰlēw- (“to blow, bloom, blossom”). Cognate with Italian fiore, Spanish flor, Portuguese flor. Related also to Latin flōreō (“to blossom, flourish”).  The spelling with -ow- emerged in the 15th century.
