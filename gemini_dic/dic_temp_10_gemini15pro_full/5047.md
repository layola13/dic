all-or-nothing:/ˌɔːl ɔːr ˈnʌθɪŋ/| adj.|孤注一掷的，要么全有要么全无的|Characterized by a situation where one either achieves complete success or total failure, with no intermediate outcome.|He took an all-or-nothing gamble on the stock market. 他在股市上孤注一掷。|近义词：do-or-die, high-stakes, desperate|反义词:cautious, moderate, balanced|词性:adjective
all-or-nothing:/ˌɔːl ɔːr ˈnʌθɪŋ/| n.|孤注一掷的做法|A strategy or approach where one either achieves complete success or total failure.|The all-or-nothing approach to dieting rarely works. 这种要么全有要么全无的节食方法很少奏效。|近义词：last-ditch effort, Hail Mary pass|反义词:compromise, gradual approach|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrase "all or nothing," combining "all" (meaning everything) and "nothing" (meaning the absence of anything). This phrase likely originated in the early 20th century, becoming more common with the rise of high-stakes gambling and investment.
