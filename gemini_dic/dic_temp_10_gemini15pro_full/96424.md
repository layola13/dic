indissolublenesses:/ˌɪndɪˈsɒljəblnəsɪz/ |n. |不可溶解性；牢不可破；永久性|The quality or state of being indissoluble; inability to be dissolved or broken up.|The indissolublenesses of their marriage vows were paramount to them. 他们婚姻誓言的不可分割性对他们至关重要。|近义词：permanence, stability, inseparability|反义词：dissolubility, fragility, separability|词性: 名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *indissolubilitatem, from indissolubilis (“indissoluble”) + -itatem (suffix forming abstract nouns).  The English word is formed by adding the suffix "-ness" to "indissoluble," which itself comes from the Latin "in-" (not) + "dissolubilis" (dissoluble), from "dis-" (apart) + "solvere" (to loosen, dissolve).
