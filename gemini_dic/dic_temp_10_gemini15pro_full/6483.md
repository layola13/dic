amputator:/ˈæmpjəˌteɪtər/
|n.|截肢者，截肢手术医生|A person who performs amputations; a surgeon who amputates limbs.|The amputator skillfully removed the damaged limb.  截肢医生熟练地切除了受损的肢体。|Synonyms: surgeon, operating doctor|Antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *amputator, from Latin amputatus, past participle of amputare (“to cut off, prune, lop off”), from ambi- (“around”) + putare (“to prune, cleanse”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *pyeuh₂- (“to rot, purify”).
