collimation:/ˌkɒlɪˈmeɪʃn/ /ˌkɑːlɪˈmeɪʃn/| n.|准直，校准|The process of making light rays parallel or nearly parallel.  Making a beam of radiation (light, electrons, etc.) parallel.|The telescope requires careful collimation. 这架望远镜需要仔细校准。|近义词：alignment, adjustment|反义词：divergence|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "collineāre,"  meaning "to direct in a straight line."  "Col-" (together) + "linea" (line) + "-ation" (forming a noun of action).
