half-deprecating:/ˌhæf ˈdɛprɪkeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 半自嘲的；略带自谦的 | Expressing mild disapproval of oneself, often humorously.  It suggests a modest or slightly self-critical attitude, often used to downplay one's achievements or abilities. | He gave a half-deprecating smile as he accepted the award.  他接受奖项时带着略带自谦的微笑。 |  She made a half-deprecating joke about her cooking skills. 她自嘲地开了个玩笑，说自己的厨艺不怎么样。 | Near synonyms: self-effacing, modest, unassuming | Near antonyms: boastful, arrogant, self-aggrandizing | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
"half-" (Old English healf) + "deprecating" (from Latin deprecatus, past participle of deprecari "to avert by prayer," from de- "down" + precari "to pray").  The "half-" prefix modifies the intensity of "deprecating," suggesting a milder form of self-disapproval.
