neuronavigation:/ˌnʊroʊˌnævɪˈɡeɪʃən/| n. |神经导航|The process of using computer-generated images and instruments to guide surgical procedures in the nervous system.  It involves creating a virtual map of the patient's brain or spine and using it to precisely target surgical instruments.|The surgeon used neuronavigation to remove the tumor. 外科医生使用神经导航来切除肿瘤。

Neuronavigation allowed for a minimally invasive approach to the surgery. 神经导航允许以微创方式进行手术。|Synonyms: image-guided surgery, computer-assisted surgery, stereotactic surgery|Antonyms: conventional surgery|noun
