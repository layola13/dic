geeking:/ˈɡiːkɪŋ/ | v. |沉迷于，痴迷于；像个极客一样行事 | Engaging in or obsessed with a particular interest, especially one related to technology or a specific hobby; behaving like a geek. | He was geeking out over his new computer. 他对他的新电脑非常着迷。| 近义词: nerding, obsessing | 反义词: ignoring, neglecting | 词性: 动词

词源:  Derived from the noun "geek," which originated in the late 19th century with uncertain etymology, possibly related to Low German "geck" (fool, freak). The "-ing" suffix transforms it into a present participle/gerund, indicating the act of being a geek.
