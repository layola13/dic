firestorm:/ˈfaɪərstɔːrm/| n.|火风暴；舆论风暴；强烈抗议|A large and destructive fire that creates and sustains its own wind system.  A sudden outburst of widespread, usually negative, public reaction.|The politician's insensitive remarks ignited a firestorm of criticism.  这位政治家麻木不仁的言论引发了舆论的强烈批评。|近义词：conflagration, inferno, uproar, outcry|反义词:calm, peace|词性:名词

firestorm:/ˈfaɪərstɔːrm/| n.|火风暴；舆论风暴；强烈抗议|A situation that is extremely turbulent or controversial.  A sudden violent outburst of something.|The release of the controversial film sparked a firestorm of debate.  这部备受争议的电影一上映就引发了激烈的争论。|近义词：controversy, furore, maelstrom|反义词:harmony, agreement|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From fire + storm.  First recorded in the early 20th century, initially referring to literal large fires.  The figurative sense of a sudden, intense public reaction developed later.
