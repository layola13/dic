disenfranchise:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/
|vt.|剥夺权利；剥夺公民权|To deprive someone of the right to vote, or other privileges of citizenship.|The new law disenfranchised many poor people from voting.  这项新法律剥夺了许多穷人的投票权。|deprive of a right, dispossess|enfranchise, empower|verb


disenfranchisement:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
|n.|剥夺权利；剥夺公民权|The act of depriving someone of the right to vote or other privileges of citizenship.|The disenfranchisement of black voters was a major issue during the Civil Rights Movement.  黑人选民被剥夺选举权是民权运动期间的一个主要问题。|deprivation of rights, dispossession|enfranchisement, empowerment|noun
