ambury:/ˈæmbəri/ | n. | 内脏, 下水 | The butcher prepared the ambury for sale. 肉贩准备出售内脏。| The ambury was discarded after the animal was butchered.  动物被屠宰后，内脏被丢弃了。 |  offal, entrails, viscera | guts, innards | noun

Etymology:  From Middle English *ambury, from Anglo-Norman anbur, aumbure (“entrails, intestines”), and Old French andui (“entrails”), probably from Late Latin *indualia, plural of *indualis, from Latin induo (“to put on, clothe”), because they are inside the body like clothing.
