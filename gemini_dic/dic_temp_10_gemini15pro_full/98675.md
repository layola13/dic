insulator:/ˈɪnsjəleɪtər/ (US), /ˈɪnsjʊleɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n.|绝缘体；隔离者；隔热体|A material that does not readily allow the passage of heat, electricity, or sound.|The plastic coating on the wires acts as an insulator.|近义词:nonconductor, dielectric|反义词:conductor|词性:名词

insulate:/ˈɪnsjəleɪt/ (US), /ˈɪnsjʊleɪt/ (UK)| v.|使绝缘；隔离；使隔热|To protect something with a material that prevents heat, electricity, or sound from passing through.|The house is well insulated against the cold.|近义词:protect, shield|反义词:expose|词性:动词

insulating:/ˈɪnsjʊleɪtɪŋ/ (US), /ˈɪnsjəleɪtɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|绝缘的；隔热的|Preventing the passage of heat, electricity, or sound.|Insulating materials are used to reduce energy loss.|近义词:protective, shielding|反义词:conductive|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *insulator, from Latin insulare "to make into an island, isolate," from insula "island."  The notion is of something being set apart or isolated, like an island.
