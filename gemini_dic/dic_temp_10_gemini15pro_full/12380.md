ashs:/ˈæʃɪz/ | n. | 灰烬；骨灰；遗骸 | The remains of something that has been burned, typically wood or other organic matter. Also used figuratively to represent the remains of something destroyed or lost. |  The fire reduced the building to ashes.  大火把这栋建筑烧成了灰烬。| synonyms: cinders, embers, dust, remains | antonyms: whole, intact | noun


Etymology: From Old English *æsc, from Proto-Germanic *askō, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eHs- (“ashes”). Cognate with Old Norse aska, Old Frisian aska, Old Saxon aska, Old High German asca, Gothic azgō.
