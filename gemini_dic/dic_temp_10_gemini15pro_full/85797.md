hazard:/ˈhæzərd/ (US), /ˈhæzəd/ (UK)| n. v.|危险，危害；冒险，尝试；|A hazard is something that is dangerous and likely to cause damage.  A hazard can also be a risk or a chance of danger.|例句：The icy roads are a hazard to drivers.  结冰的道路对司机来说是一种危险。|近义词：danger, risk, peril, threat|反义词：safety, security|词性:名词

hazard:/ˈhæzərd/ (US), /ˈhæzəd/ (UK)| v.|冒险，尝试；使遭受危险|To hazard something means to risk losing it or to put it in danger. It can also mean to venture a guess or opinion.|例句：He hazarded his life to save the drowning child. 他冒着生命危险去救溺水的孩子。|近义词：risk, venture, gamble|反义词：protect, safeguard|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *hasart "game of dice," of uncertain origin. Possibly from Arabic *az-zahr (الزهر)  meaning "the die."
