emboss:/ɪmˈbɒs/ (UK), /ɪmˈbɔːs/ (US)| v. n.|浮雕，凸印；使…突出|To carve, mold, or stamp a design on (a surface) so that it stands out in relief.|She embossed the stationery with the company logo. 她用公司标志凸印了信纸。|近义词：raise, carve, mold, stamp|反义词:flatten, smooth|词性:动词, 名词

embossment:/ɪmˈbɒsmənt/ (UK), /ɪmˈbɔːsmənt/ (US)| n.|浮雕，凸印；凸出物|The act or process of embossing or the state of being embossed; a raised design or figure.|The embossment on the wedding invitation added a touch of elegance.  婚礼请柬上的浮雕增添了一丝优雅。|近义词：relief, projection, protuberance|反义词:indentation, depression|词性:名词
