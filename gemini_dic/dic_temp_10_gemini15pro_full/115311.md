mannerist:/ˈmænərɪst/| n. adj.|风格主义者；矫揉造作的人；|An artist who deliberately exaggerates or distorts elements of style, often resulting in artificiality.  A person who behaves in an affected or artificial way.|The painting was clearly the work of a Mannerist, evident in the elongated figures and dramatic poses. 这幅画显然是一位风格主义者的作品，从拉长的 figures 和戏剧性的姿势可以明显看出。  He's such a mannerist, always striking dramatic poses and using overly elaborate language. 他真是个矫揉造作的人，总是摆出戏剧性的姿势，使用过于华丽的语言。|近义词：affected, artificial, stylized, pretentious|反义词：natural, unaffected, simple, unpretentious|词性: 名词, 形容词

Etymology:
From Italian *manierista*, from *maniera* "manner," from Latin *manus* "hand".  The original sense referred to an artist's distinctive "hand" or style.
