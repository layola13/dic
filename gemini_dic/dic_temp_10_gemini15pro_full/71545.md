flaring:/ˈfleərɪŋ/ /ˈflerɪŋ/ | adj. v. n. | 燃烧的，闪耀的，喇叭形的；爆发，突然发怒；爆发，闪光 | Burning with a sudden, unsteady flame; widening or spreading out from a narrow base; a sudden outburst of anger or emotion; a sudden, brief burst of bright light or energy. | The flaring nostrils of the bull indicated its anger. 公牛张开的鼻孔表明了它的愤怒。 | 近义词：blazing, flashing, erupting, bursting, widening | 反义词：shrinking, narrowing, diminishing, subsiding | 词性: adjective, verb, noun

flaring:/ˈfleərɪŋ/ /ˈflerɪŋ/ | n. |  闪光; 耀斑; (信号弹的)照明; (短暂的)加剧 | A sudden, brief burst of bright light or energy |  Solar flares can disrupt communications. 太阳耀斑会干扰通讯。 | 近义词：flash, blaze, burst, eruption | 反义词：diminishment, decrease | 词性: noun

flaring:/ˈfleərɪŋ/ /ˈflerɪŋ/ | v. | (火焰)闪耀; (短暂地)烧旺; (衣裙等)呈喇叭形展开; (鼻孔)张大 | Burn with a sudden, unsteady flame; become wider at one end; (of nostrils) widen, especially as a sign of anger | The fire flared up suddenly. 火突然烧了起来。 | 近义词：blaze, flash, erupt, burst, widen | 反义词：shrink, narrow, diminish, subside | 词性: verb


词源:  From Middle English *flaren, from Old Norse *flāra (“to waver”), from Proto-Germanic *flārōną (“to blow, waver”). Cognate with Old High German flāran (“to blow”).
