loathe:/ləʊð/| v. |憎恶，厌恶|To feel intense dislike or disgust for something or someone.|He loathed having to get up early. 他讨厌不得不早起。|
近义词：detest, abhor, despise, hate|反义词:love, adore, like|词性:动词

词源:
From Middle English *lothen, from Old English lāðian (“to invite, summon; be hateful, be displeasing”), from Proto-Germanic *laþōną (“to invite, summon, call together”), from Proto-Indo-European *lēdʰ- (“to invite, summon”). More at loath.  The meaning shifted from “to be hateful” to “to hate” in Middle English.
