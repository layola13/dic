mirage: /məˈrɑːʒ/ (məˈräʒ)/| n. |海蜃楼；幻景；幻想|An optical illusion caused by atmospheric conditions, especially the appearance of a sheet of water in a desert or on a hot road caused by the refraction of light from the sky by heated air.  Something illusory or insubstantial.|  例句：The shimmering mirage in the desert tempted the lost travelers.  沙漠中 shimmering 的海市蜃楼诱惑着迷路的旅行者。

例句：His dream of becoming a millionaire was just a mirage. 他想成为百万富翁的梦想只是一个幻景。|近义词：illusion, hallucination, delusion, fantasy|反义词：reality|词性：名词

mirages: /məˈrɑːʒɪz/ (məˈräʒɪz)/| n. |海蜃楼（复数）；幻景（复数）；幻想（复数）|The plural form of "mirage".|例句：The travelers saw shimmering mirages on the horizon. 旅行者在地平线上看到了闪闪发光的海市蜃楼。

例句：Many people chase mirages of wealth and fame. 很多人追逐着财富和名名的幻影。|近义词：illusions, hallucinations, delusions, fantasies|反义词：realities|词性：名词
