commercialize: /kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪz/ (UK) /kəˈmɜːrʃəlaɪz/ (US)
|v.|商业化，商品化|To make something suitable for sale or exploit it to make a profit.|The company successfully commercialized the new technology.  这家公司成功地将新技术商业化了。|synonyms: marketize, merchandise, monetize|antonyms: decommercialize|verb

commercialize: /kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪz/ (UK) /kəˈmɜːrʃəlaɪz/ (US)
|n.|商业化|The process of making something suitable for sale or exploiting it to make a profit.|The commercialization of the internet has changed the world. 互联网的商业化改变了世界。|synonyms: marketing, merchandising|antonyms:  -|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *commercialiser*, from *commercial* + *-iser* (suffix forming verbs from adjectives). *Commercial* is from Late Latin *commercialis* "of or pertaining to trade," from Latin *commercium* "trade, commerce," from *com-* (together) + *merx* (merchandise).
