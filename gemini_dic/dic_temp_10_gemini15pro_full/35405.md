co-optation:/koʊˌɑːpˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 收编，纳入；拉拢 | The co-optation of community leaders into the political system can be a way to quell dissent.  将社区领导人纳入政治体系可以成为平息异议的一种方式。 |  The co-optation of local customs by large corporations can lead to the loss of cultural identity. 大公司对当地习俗的吸收会导致文化认同的丧失。 | synonyms: absorption, assimilation, incorporation, appropriation | antonyms: exclusion, rejection, expulsion | noun


co-optations:/koʊˌɑːpˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 收编，纳入；拉拢（co-optation的复数形式）|  Several co-optations of smaller companies have allowed the corporation to expand its market share.  对几家小公司的收购使该公司扩大了市场份额。 |  The co-optations of radical ideas into mainstream culture often leads to their dilution. 将激进思想纳入主流文化通常会导致它们被稀释。 | synonyms: absorptions, assimilations, incorporations, appropriations | antonyms: exclusions, rejections, expulsions | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cooptatio, from Latin cooptāre "to elect together, choose," from co- "together" + optāre "to choose, wish, desire."
