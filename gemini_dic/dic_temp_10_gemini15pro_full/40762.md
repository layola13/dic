convicts:/ˈkɒnvɪkts/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnvɪkts/ (US)| n.|罪犯|A person found guilty of a crime and sentenced by a court.|The convicts were transported to Australia. 这些罪犯被流放到澳大利亚。|synonyms: prisoner, inmate, criminal, felon, offender|antonyms: law-abiding citizen, innocent person|noun


convicts:/kənˈvɪkts/ (UK) /kənˈvɪkts/ (US)| vt.|证明…有罪；使…确信|To prove or declare (someone) to be guilty of a criminal offense by the verdict of a jury or the decision of a judge in a court of law; to persuade someone of the truth of something.|The jury convicted him of murder. 陪审团裁定他犯有谋杀罪。|synonyms: condemn, sentence, incriminate, indict|antonyms: acquit, exonerate, absolve|verb
