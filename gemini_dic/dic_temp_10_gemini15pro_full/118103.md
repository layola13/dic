meniscus: /məˈnɪskəs/ (US), /məˈnɪskəs/ (UK)| n. |半月板|A C-shaped piece of cartilage that acts as a shock absorber in the knee joint.|He tore his meniscus playing football. 他踢足球时撕裂了半月板。|Synonyms: cartilage (partially)|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for an anatomical part)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mēniscus*, from Ancient Greek μηνίσκος (mēnískos, “crescent”), diminutive of μήνη (mḗnē, “moon”).
