nonmarketabilities:/ˌnɒnˌmɑːkɪtəˈbɪlətiz/ | n. | 不可销售性；非市场性；不适销性 |  The quality of being difficult or impossible to sell or trade in a market.  This often applies to assets or skills that are specific to a particular context or lack broad appeal. | The company's specialized equipment had significant nonmarketabilities after the factory closed.  这家工厂关闭后，该公司的专用设备具有严重的不可销售性。

The employee's highly specialized skills in a now-obsolete technology resulted in nonmarketabilities when seeking new employment.  这名员工在现已过时的技术方面拥有高度专业化的技能，导致在寻找新工作时不适销。 | 近义词: unmarketability, unsalability | 反义词: marketability, salability | 词性: noun (plural)

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  Meaning "not," from Latin non "not."
market (noun): From Old French market, ultimately from Latin mercatus "trade, market," past participle of mercari "to trade, traffic, earn."
-able (suffix): Meaning "capable of being," "suitable for being," "tending to," "giving occasion to," from Latin -abilis.
-ity (suffix):  Forms abstract nouns of quality or condition, from Latin -itas, -itatis.
