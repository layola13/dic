concretionary:/ˌkɒŋˈkriːʃənəri/ | adj. | 结核的，凝固的 | Relating to or resembling a concretion; formed by coalescing or solidifying. | The concretionary limestone formed interesting patterns in the cave. 这些结核灰岩在洞穴中形成了有趣的图案。 | The concretionary structure of the rock made it difficult to break.  岩石的结核结构使其难以破碎。 | 近义词：nodular, consolidated, solidified | 反义词：diffuse, dispersed, unconsolidated | 词性: adjective

concretion:/kɒŋˈkriːʃ(ə)n/ | n. | 凝结物，结石 | A hard solid mass formed by the local accumulation of matter, especially within the body or within a mass of sediment. | Kidney stones are a common type of concretion. 肾结石是一种常见的凝结物。 | The geologist examined the concretions found in the sedimentary rock. 地质学家检查了沉积岩中发现的凝结物。| 近义词：calculus, deposit, mass | 反义词：solution, dispersion | 词性: noun
