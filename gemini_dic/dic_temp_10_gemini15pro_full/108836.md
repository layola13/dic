leaving: /ˈliːvɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈliːvɪŋ/ (US) | n. adj. | 离开，离去；剩余物；休假 | The act of departing or going away from a place; something that remains; time off from work. |  She was sad about leaving home. 她很伤心要离开家。| synonyms: departure, exit, going, remaining, remnant, residue, vacation, leave | antonyms: arrival, entrance, coming, staying | noun

leaving: /ˈliːvɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈliːvɪŋ/ (US) | adj. | 离开的，即將離開的 | Departing or about to depart. |  The leaving train was delayed.  即将离开的火车晚点了。| synonyms: departing, outgoing, exiting | antonyms: arriving, incoming, entering | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lēofan (to allow, permit, leave behind), from Proto-Germanic *laubijaną, from Proto-Indo-European *leubh- (to love, care, desire).  The sense of "departing" developed from the idea of "leaving something behind."
