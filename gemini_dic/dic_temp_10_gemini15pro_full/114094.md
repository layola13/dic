magnifier:/ˈmæɡnɪfaɪər/| n.|放大镜，放大器|A device, such as a lens, that makes an object appear larger.|She used a magnifier to examine the tiny inscription on the ring. 她用放大镜检查戒指上细小的铭文。|synonyms: lens, glass, loupe|antonyms: reducer, minimizer|noun

magnify:/ˈmæɡnɪfaɪ/| v.|放大，扩大，夸大|To make (something) appear larger than it is, especially with a lens or other optical instrument. To increase the importance or effect of something.|The telescope magnified the stars a hundred times. 望远镜将星星放大了100倍。 The media tends to magnify the dangers of travel. 媒体倾向于夸大旅行的危险。|synonyms: enlarge, amplify, expand, exaggerate|antonyms: reduce, diminish, minimize|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English magnifien, from Old French magnifier, from Latin magnificāre (“to extol, praise highly, magnify”), from magnus (“great, large, important”) + -ficāre (from facere (“to make, do”)).
