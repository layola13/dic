crunchier:/ˈkrʌntʃiər/ | adj. | 更脆的 | Comparative form of crunchy; having a firmer, crispier texture and producing a louder sound when bitten or crushed. | The second batch of cookies was crunchier than the first. 第二批饼干比第一批更脆。| The apples were crisp and crunchier than I expected. 这些苹果很脆，比我想象的还要脆。| 近义词: crispier, firmer | 反义词: softer, chewier | 词性: 形容词

crunch:/krʌntʃ/ | n. v. |嘎吱作响，咬嚼，处理数据 | From imitative origin. |  n. The crunch of leaves underfoot was satisfying. 脚下树叶嘎吱作响的声音令人满足。| v.  She crunched on a carrot. 她嘎吱嘎吱地嚼着胡萝卜。 | 近义词: crush, grind (v.); crispness (n.) | 反义词: softness, chewiness | 词性: 名词，动词

-y:/i/ | suffix |  构成形容词，表示“充满...的”, “具有...特性的” | Added to nouns to form adjectives. |  例句: A crunchy apple. 一个脆脆的苹果。 | 词性: 后缀

词源:  crunch + -y + -er
"crunch" comes from imitative origin, mimicking the sound. "-y" is a suffix that forms adjectives meaning "full of" or "characterized by". "-er" is a suffix that forms the comparative degree of adjectives.
