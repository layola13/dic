commotion:/kəˈmoʊʃən/ (US) /kəˈməʊʃən/ (UK)| n. |骚动，混乱，喧闹|A state of confused and noisy disturbance.|
例句：There was a great commotion when the fire alarm went off. 火警响起时，一片混乱。

例句：His sudden arrival caused quite a commotion. 他的突然到来引起了一阵骚动。

近义词：disturbance, uproar, tumult, chaos, pandemonium, hubbub|
反义词：calm, peace, quiet, order, tranquility|
词性：noun


commotion:/kəˈmoʊʃən/ (US) /kəˈməʊʃən/ (UK)| n. |激动，不安|A state of agitated or nervous excitement.|
例句：She felt a commotion in her chest as she waited for the results.  她等待结果时感到胸口一阵激动。

例句：The news of the election caused a commotion throughout the country.  选举的消息在全国引起了骚动。

近义词：agitation, excitement, anxiety, perturbation|
反义词：calm, composure, serenity|
词性：noun



词源: Late Middle English: from Old French commotion, from Latin commotio(n-), from commovēre ‘move violently,’ from com- (intensive force) + movēre ‘to move.’
