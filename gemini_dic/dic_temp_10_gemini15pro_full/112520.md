louvring:/ˈluːvrɪŋ/| n.|卢浮宫式屋顶；天窗；百叶窗|A roof having a series of parallel ridges and furrows, resembling the roof of the Louvre museum. A skylight or a series of openings in a roof or ceiling for ventilation or light. Also refers to louvers, which are a series of angled slats or blades used to control airflow or light.|The architect incorporated louvring into the design to allow for natural light and ventilation.|Synonyms: skylight, vent, ventilator, louver, shutter|Antonyms: solid roof, closed window|noun
