functionalism:/ˈfʌŋkʃənəlɪzəm/
n.|功能主义|A theory that explains something based on its purpose or role, particularly in social sciences, emphasizing the interdependence of its parts and their contribution to the overall system.|Functionalism emphasizes the interdependence of parts within a system.|近义词:pragmatism, utilitarianism|反义词:structuralism, intentionalism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From function + -al + -ism. "Function" comes from the Late Latin "functio" meaning "performance, execution". "-al" forms adjectives meaning "relating to". "-ism" forms nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, or practices.
