outwatering:/ˌaʊtˈwɔːtərɪŋ/
n.|排水，脱水|The process of removing water from an area, such as a construction site or wetland, to allow for work or other activities.|例句：The outwatering of the excavation site was necessary before construction could begin.  施工开始前必须对挖掘现场进行排水。|近义词：dewatering, drainage|反义词:watering, irrigation|词性:名词

Etymology:
out- (prefix):  from Old English *ūt, meaning "out, outside, outwards, from within"
water (noun/verb): from Old English wæter, meaning "water, liquid, sea"
-ing (suffix): forms present participles and gerunds, often indicating an ongoing process or action.
