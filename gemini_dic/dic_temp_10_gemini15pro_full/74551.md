fraud:/frɔːd/ | n. | 欺诈，诈骗 | wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain | He was found guilty of fraud. 他被判犯有欺诈罪。 | synonyms: deception, deceit, trickery, swindle, cheat, hoax | antonyms: honesty, integrity | noun

fraud:/frɔːd/ | n. | 骗子 | a person or thing intended to deceive others, typically by unjustifiably claiming or being credited with accomplishments or qualities | The so-called faith healer was exposed as a fraud.  所谓的信仰治疗师被揭露是个骗子。 | synonyms: imposter, charlatan, fake, sham | antonyms: genuine | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French fraude (“deceit, trickery, dishonesty”), from Latin fraus (“deceit, injury, damage”), from Proto-Italic *froud, of uncertain origin.
