odontophore:/ˈoʊdənˌtəfɔːr/| n. |齿舌|A ribbon-like structure in the mouths of mollusks, except bivalves, used for scraping or cutting food.|The snail extended its odontophore to graze on the algae.|Synonym: radula|Antonym: (No direct antonym, as it's a specific anatomical feature)|noun
