exacuate: /ɪɡˈzækjueɪt/
verb
撤离，疏散
To remove (people) from a dangerous place.
The government ordered the residents to exacuate the coastal areas before the hurricane.  政府命令居民在飓风来临之前撤离沿海地区。
The building was exacuated because of a bomb threat.  由于炸弹威胁，该建筑物被疏散。
近义词: evacuate, clear, remove
反义词: occupy, inhabit

词性: verb
词源: From Latin ex- (out of) + acuere (to sharpen).  The original meaning was "to sharpen thoroughly," but it evolved to mean "to remove completely" and finally "to evacuate."
