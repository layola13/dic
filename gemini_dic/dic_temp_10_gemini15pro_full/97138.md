infiltrator:/ˈɪnfɪltreɪtər/ (US), /ˈɪnfɪltreɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n.|渗透者，潜入者|A person who enters an organization or place secretly in order to spy or cause damage.|The infiltrator managed to gain access to the secret files.  这个渗透者设法获得了机密文件。|Synonyms: spy, mole, agent, interloper|Antonyms: defender, protector|noun

infiltrate:/ˈɪnfɪltreɪt/ (US), /ˈɪnfɪltreɪt/ (UK)| v.|渗透，潜入|To enter or gain access to (an organization, place, etc.) surreptitiously and gradually, especially in order to acquire secret information or bring about an internal change.|Rebel forces infiltrated the government. 叛军渗透了政府。|Synonyms: penetrate, permeate, pervade|Antonyms: exclude, expel|verb
