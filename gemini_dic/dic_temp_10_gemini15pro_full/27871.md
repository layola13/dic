canopy: /ˈkænəpi/ (US) /ˈkænəpi/ (UK)| n.| 天篷；遮篷；树冠层|A protective covering, often of fabric, supported by a frame or posts, or the upper layer of branches and leaves in a forest.| We sat under the canopy, sheltered from the sun. 我们坐在天篷下，遮蔽着阳光。 The rainforest canopy teems with life.  雨林的树冠层充满了生机。|synonyms: awning, covering, roof, shade, shelter, umbrella, vault, ceiling, baldachin (especially a decorative one)|antonyms: floor, base, ground|noun

canopy: /ˈkænəpi/ (US) /ˈkænəpi/ (UK)| v.| 覆盖；遮盖| To cover with a canopy.|  Trees canopied the path.  树木遮盖了小路。  The stars canopied the night sky. 星星覆盖了夜空。|synonyms: cover, overspread, overshadow, veil|antonyms: uncover, expose|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *canope, from Old North French canape ("gauze net, mosquito net"), from Medieval Latin canopeum ("mosquito net"), from Ancient Greek κωνωπεῖον (kōnōpeîon, “canopy, mosquito net”), from κώνωψ (kṓnōps, “gnat, mosquito”).
