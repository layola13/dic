consecratednesses:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪtɪdnɪsɪz/ (US) /ˈkɒnsɪkreɪtɪdnəsɪz/ (UK)
n.
神圣性；献身精神（通常用复数）
The state of being consecrated; the quality of being dedicated to a sacred purpose.  This word is rarely used and considered archaic or overly formal.
The consecratednesses of the ancient temples were palpable.  古代寺庙的神圣感是显而易见的。
Near synonyms: sanctity, holiness, sacredness
Antonym: profanity
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From consecrated + -ness.  "Consecrated" comes from the Latin "consecratus," the past participle of "consecrare" (to hallow, dedicate), from "com-" (together, with) + "sacer" (sacred). "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality.
