conceptualise:/kənˈsɛptʃʊəlaɪz/ | v. | 概念化，构思 | To form a concept or idea of something. | He struggled to conceptualise the sheer scale of the universe. 他难以理解宇宙的浩瀚规模。| envision, imagine, formulate, conceive | - | Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From late 19th century: from concept + -ualize.  "Concept" itself comes from the Latin conceptum, meaning "something conceived," the past participle of concipere "to take in, conceive," from com- "together" + capere "to take."
