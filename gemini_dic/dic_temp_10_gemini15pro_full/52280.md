dichotomist:/daɪˈkɒtəmɪst/ /daɪˈkɑːtəmɪst/| n. |二分法者；非此即彼思考者|A person who sees things in terms of two opposing categories, often overlooking complexities and nuances.|例句：The dichotomist argued that people were either good or evil, with no room for shades of gray.  二分法者认为人要么是善的，要么是恶的，没有中间地带。

例句：As a dichotomist, he struggled to understand the concept of a spectrum. 作为二分法者，他难以理解光谱的概念。

|近义词：dualist, splitter|反义词: synthesist, unifier|词性:noun


dichotomous:/daɪˈkɒtəməs/ /daɪˈkɑːtəməs/| adj. |二分的；对立的|Divided or dividing into two sharply distinguished parts or classifications.|例句：The survey presented a dichotomous choice: agree or disagree.  该调查提出了一个二元选择：同意或不同意。

例句：The dichotomous nature of the debate prevented any real progress. 辩论的二元性质阻碍了任何真正的进展。

|近义词：binary, dualistic|反义词: continuous, non-dual|词性:adjective


dichotomy:/daɪˈkɒtəmi/ /daɪˈkɑːtəmi/| n. |二分法；分裂；对立|A division or contrast between two things that are or are represented as being opposed or entirely different.|例句：There is a false dichotomy between economic growth and environmental protection.  经济增长和环境保护之间存在着错误的二分法。

例句：The dichotomy between theory and practice is a common problem in education. 理论与实践之间的脱节是教育中的一个常见问题。

|近义词：division, contrast, split|反义词: unity, synthesis|词性:noun
