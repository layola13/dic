algonkin:/ælˈɡɒŋkɪn/ /ælˈɡɑːŋkɪn/| n. adj.| 阿尔冈昆人；阿尔冈昆语|A member of any of the North American Indian peoples speaking Algonquian languages and originally living in the region from the Atlantic coast to the Great Lakes and from Labrador to the Carolinas.  Also refers to the family of languages spoken by these peoples.|The Algonkin were skilled hunters and fishermen.|近义词：Algonquian|反义词: (No specific antonym exists for a group of people or a language family.)|词性:noun, adjective

Etymology: From Algonquin, the name of a specific Algonquian-speaking people.
