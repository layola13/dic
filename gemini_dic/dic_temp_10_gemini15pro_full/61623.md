emigrated: /ˈemɪɡreɪtɪd/
verb
移居国外（过去式）
left one's country to settle in another.
My grandparents emigrated from Italy to the United States in the 1920s. 我的祖父母在20世纪20年代从意大利移民到美国。
He emigrated to Canada seeking better job opportunities. 他为了寻找更好的工作机会移民到加拿大。
近义词: migrated, relocated, moved abroad
反义词: immigrated, repatriated
词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *emigratus*, past participle of *emigrare* (“to move out, remove, migrate from”), from *e-* (“out, from”) + *migrare* (“to migrate, depart, change one's abode”).
