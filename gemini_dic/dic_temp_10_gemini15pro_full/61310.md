embarrass:/ɪmˈbærəs/ (US), /ɪmˈbærəs/ (UK)| vt.|使尴尬，使窘迫，使不好意思|To cause someone to feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed.|She was embarrassed by his unexpected question. 她被他突如其来的问题弄得很尴尬。|synonyms: shame, humiliate, mortify, discomfit|antonyms: comfort, reassure, encourage|verb

embarrass:/ɪmˈbærəs/ (US), /ɪmˈbærəs/ (UK)| adj.|尴尬的，窘迫的 (rarely used, usually in the form of "embarrassed")|Feeling awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed.|I felt embarrassed when I realized I had forgotten her name. 当我意识到我忘记了她的名字时，我感到很尴尬。|synonyms: ashamed, uncomfortable, self-conscious|antonyms: comfortable, confident, composed|adjective

embarrassment:/ɪmˈbærəsmənt/ (US), /ɪmˈbærəsmənt/ (UK)| n.|尴尬，窘迫，难堪|The state of feeling awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed.|Her embarrassment was evident as she blushed. 她的尴尬显而易见，因为她脸红了。|synonyms: shame, humiliation, mortification, discomfiture|antonyms: comfort, reassurance, encouragement|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *embarrasser*, from Spanish *embarazar* "to impede, embarrass," from Portuguese *embaraçar* "to entangle, embarrass," from *barra* "bar, obstacle," probably of pre-Roman origin.
