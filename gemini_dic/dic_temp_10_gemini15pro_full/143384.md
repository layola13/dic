outfielders:/ˈaʊtˌfiːldərz/
n.
外野手（复数）
A baseball or cricket player who positions themselves in the outfield.  The outfield is the area of the field furthest from the batsman or batter.
The outfielders were chasing the ball hit deep into left field. 外野手正在追逐打到左外野深处的球。

The coach praised his outfielders for their excellent catches. 教练称赞他的外野手们的出色接球。


近义词:fielders
反义词:infielder, batter, pitcher (These are not true opposites, but represent different roles on the field)
词性:noun (plural)


outfielder:/ˈaʊtˌfiːldər/
n.
外野手
A baseball or cricket player who positions themselves in the outfield.  The outfield is the area of the field furthest from the batsman or batter.
The outfielder made a spectacular diving catch. 外野手做出了一个精彩的飞身接球。

The outfielder threw the ball to the shortstop. 外野手把球扔给了游击手。


近义词:fielder
反义词:infielder, batter, pitcher (These are not true opposites, but represent different roles on the field)
词性:noun


词源：out + field + -er

out:  from Old English *ūt, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *ūt-

field: from Old English feld “open country,” from Proto-Germanic *felþuz 

-er: a suffix forming nouns designating persons from their activity, place of origin, etc., from Old English -ere, from Proto-Germanic *-arjaz.
