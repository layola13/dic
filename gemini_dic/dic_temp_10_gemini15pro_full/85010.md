haranguer:/həˈræŋər/ | n. | 演说者，高谈阔论者 | A person who delivers a harangue; a loud, pompous, or prolonged speech or lecture. | The haranguer spoke for hours, tiring the audience. 演说者连续讲了几个小时，使听众感到疲惫。 |  The haranguer captivated the crowd with his fiery speech.  演说者用他充满激情的演讲吸引了人群。 | synonyms: orator, speaker, lecturer, preacher | antonyms: listener, audience | noun

harangue:/həˈræŋ/ | n. | 长篇大论，高谈阔论 | A lengthy and aggressive speech. |  He delivered a long harangue about the evils of modern society. 他发表了一篇关于现代社会弊端的冗长而激烈的演讲。 | The coach's harangue after the game lasted for an hour. 教练在比赛后的长篇大论持续了一个小时。 | synonyms: tirade, diatribe, rant, lecture | antonyms: discussion, conversation | noun

harangue:/həˈræŋ/ | v. | 长篇大论，高谈阔论，训斥 | To deliver a harangue; to lecture someone at length in an aggressive and critical manner. |  He harangued the crowd about the importance of voting. 他向人群慷慨陈词，讲述投票的重要性。 |  She harangued her son for not cleaning his room. 她斥责她的儿子不打扫房间。| synonyms: lecture, scold, berate, rant | antonyms: praise, commend | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *harangue*, from Old Italian *arangare* "to speak in public," of Germanic origin, related to Old High German *hring* "ring, circle" (as in an assembly of listeners).
