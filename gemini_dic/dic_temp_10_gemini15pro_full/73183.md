forboden:/fərˈboʊdən/| adj.|被禁止的，禁忌的|Forbidden means not allowed, especially by law or rule. It carries a sense of strong prohibition and often implies moral or social disapproval.  It comes from the past participle of the verb "forbid".|例句：The forbidden fruit is always the most tempting.  禁果总是最诱人的。

例句：Entry to this area is strictly forbidden.  严禁进入该区域。|近义词：prohibited, banned, outlawed, taboo, illicit|反义词：permitted, allowed, legal|词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *forboden, from Old English forboden, past participle of forbēodan (“to forbid, prohibit, command against, renounce”), from Proto-Germanic *farbiudaną (“to forbid”), equivalent to for- +‎ bid. Cognate with Dutch verbieden (“to forbid”), German verbieten (“to forbid”), Swedish förbjuda (“to forbid”).  More at forbid.
