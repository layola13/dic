ditsier:/ˈdɪtsiər/ | adj. | 更傻气的，更轻佻的 | More silly, scatterbrained, or frivolous.  Characterized by lightheaded silliness or lack of seriousness. | She was acting ditsier than usual after staying up all night. 她熬了一整夜后，表现得比平时更傻气。| Example: Her ditsier moments were often followed by surprising insights. 她傻乎乎的时刻之后，往往会爆发出惊人的洞察力。| synonyms: flightier, giddier, sillier, frivolous, lightheaded, scatterbrained | antonyms: serious, sensible, sober, grave, thoughtful | adjective

Etymology:
From *ditzy* (meaning "foolish, silly, frivolous") + *-er* (comparative suffix). *Dizzy* itself likely comes from Old English *dysig* meaning "foolish, stupid."
