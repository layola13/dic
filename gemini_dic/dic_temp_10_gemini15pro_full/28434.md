carborundum:/ˌkɑːrbəˈrʌndəm/ | n. |碳化硅；金刚砂|A very hard, black, crystalline compound of silicon and carbon (SiC), used as an abrasive and in refractories.|例句：Carborundum is used in grinding wheels. 碳化硅用于砂轮。

例句：The carborundum blade made quick work of the metal. 金刚砂刀片很快就把金属切开了。|近义词：silicon carbide, abrasive |反义词: N/A |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From carbon + corundum, because it is nearly as hard as corundum (ruby or sapphire). Coined in 1891 by Edward Goodrich Acheson, an American chemist.
