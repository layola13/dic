informing:/ɪnˈfɔːrmɪŋ/| v. |通知，告知；提供信息|The article was very informing, providing a lot of detail about the new technology. 这篇文章信息量很大，提供了很多关于新技术的细节。|例句：

He is informing the police about the accident.
他正在向警察报告事故。

She informed me that the meeting had been cancelled.
她通知我会议取消了。

|近义词：telling, notifying, advising, apprising|反义词：misinforming, deceiving, withholding|词性：动词 (present participle of *inform*)


informing:/ɪnˈfɔːrmɪŋ/| adj. |提供信息的，增进知识的|The documentary was very informing. 这部纪录片信息量很大。|例句：

The lecture was quite informing and thought-provoking.
这个讲座很有启发性，引人深思。

He gave an informing presentation about the current state of the economy.
他做了一个关于当前经济状况的报告，信息量很大。

|近义词：instructive, enlightening, educational|反义词：uninformative, misleading|词性：形容词


Etymology:
From Middle English *enformen*, from Old French *enformer*, from Late Latin *informare* "to shape, train, instruct, educate," from Latin *in-* "into" + *forma* "form, shape."  The sense of "give form to" led to the meaning "give essential character to," and thus "tell," "instruct."
