nation:/ˈneɪʃən/ (US), /ˈneɪʃən/ (UK)| n. |国家，民族|A large body of people united by common descent, history, culture, or language, inhabiting a particular country or territory.|The nation mourned the death of its leader.  这个国家悼念其领导人的逝世。|Example: The United States is a nation of immigrants. 美国是一个移民国家。|Synonyms: country, state, land, people, race|Antonyms: world, globe,  international|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *nacion*  (Modern French *nation*),  from Latin *nationem* (nominative *natio*) "birth, origin; breed, stock, kind, species; race of people, tribe," from *natus*, past participle of *nasci* "to be born."
