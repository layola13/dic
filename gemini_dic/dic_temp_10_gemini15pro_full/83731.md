haiku: /ˈhaɪkuː/ | n. |俳句|A Japanese poem of seventeen syllables, in three lines of five, seven, and five, traditionally evoking images of the natural world.|例句：She wrote a haiku about the falling leaves. 她写了一首关于落叶的俳句。

例句：The haiku captured the essence of the autumn evening. 这首俳句捕捉到了秋夜的精髓。|近义词：poem, verse|反义词: novel, prose|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Japanese 俳句 (haiku), from 俳 (hai, "playful, unorthodox") + 句 (ku, "sentence, phrase").  Originally referred to the opening verse of a longer poem known as a renga.
