belittling:/bɪˈlɪt(ə)lɪŋ/ /bəˈlɪtlɪŋ/ | adj.| 轻视的，贬低的 | Making someone or something seem unimportant. |  His belittling comments made her feel small. 他的轻蔑的评论让她感到自己很渺小。|  The article was full of belittling remarks about the town. 这篇文章充满了对该镇的贬低言论。 |  disparaging, derogatory, condescending, demeaning |  complimentary, praising, flattering | adjective

belittle:/bɪˈlɪt(ə)l/ /bəˈlɪtl/ | vt.| 轻视，贬低 | To make someone or something seem unimportant. |  He belittled her achievements. 他贬低了她的成就。| Don't belittle yourself - you're very talented. 不要贬低自己——你很有才华。 |  disparage, denigrate, deprecate, underestimate |  praise, extol, laud, overestimate | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From be- + little.  Meaning "to make little of, to represent as unimportant" from the 1590s.  "little" comes from Old English *lytel, from Proto-Germanic *lutilaz (also source of Old Saxon luttil, Old Frisian litel, Dutch luttel "little," Old High German luzil, German lützel "small, slight," Gothic leitils).
