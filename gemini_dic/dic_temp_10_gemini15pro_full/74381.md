frame-up:/ˈfreɪm ʌp/
noun
陷害；诬陷
A frame-up is the act of framing someone, which means to falsely accuse someone of a crime and arrange evidence to make it look like they are guilty.
The police found evidence that proved the suspect was the victim of a frame-up. 警方找到了证据，证明嫌疑人是被人陷害的。
The lawyer argued that the entire case was a frame-up designed to ruin his client's reputation. 律师辩称，整个案件都是为了毁掉他客户的名誉而设计的陷害。
synonyms: setup, entrapment, fabrication
antonyms: exoneration, vindication


frame-up:/ˈfreɪm ʌp/
verb
陷害；诬陷
To frame-up someone is to falsely accuse them of a crime and arrange evidence to make it look like they are guilty.
They tried to frame-up the innocent man for a crime he didn't commit. 他们试图陷害这个无辜的人，让他承担他没有犯下的罪行。
The rival company framed him up to steal his clients.  竞争对手公司陷害他以窃取他的客户。
synonyms: set up, entrap, fabricate
antonyms: exonerate, vindicate

词性:  可以作为名词和动词

词源:  "frame-up" originated in the late 19th century as American slang. "Frame" in this context refers to the act of constructing something falsely.  "Up" intensifies the meaning, suggesting a complete and deliberate act of fabrication.
