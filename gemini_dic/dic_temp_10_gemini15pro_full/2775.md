adumbrative:/ˌædʌmˈbreɪtɪv/| adj.|预示的，略述的，概括的|Giving a sketchy outline or a faint indication beforehand; foreshadowing.|The play's opening scene is adumbrative of the tragedy to come.  这出戏的开场预示了即将发生的悲剧。

The dark clouds were adumbrative of the coming storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨即将来临。|近义词：foreshadowing, prefigurative, suggestive, indicative|反义词：explicit, clear, detailed|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *adumbrare* "to sketch, outline," from *ad-* "to" + *umbra* "shadow."  The sense evolution is from "casting a shadow" to "representing faintly" to "foreshadowing."

USAGE->
Attributive Use:
The adumbrative remarks hinted at the true nature of the problem. = 这些概括性的言论暗示了问题的本质。
His adumbrative style of teaching left many students confused. = 他概括性的教学风格让很多学生感到困惑。


Predicative Use:
The speaker's comments were brief and adumbrative. = 演讲者的评论简短而概括。
The introduction to the novel is adumbrative of the major themes. = 小说的引言概括了主要的主题。


With "of":
The early chapters are adumbrative of the novel's tragic ending. =  早期章节预示了这部小说的悲惨结局。
The artist's sketches were adumbrative of the final masterpiece. =  这位艺术家的草图预示了最终的杰作。



PHRASE->
adumbrative of =  预示...
highly adumbrative =  高度概括的
somewhat adumbrative =  略微概括的
adumbrative remarks =  概括性的言论
adumbrative summary =  概括性的总结
