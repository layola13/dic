glimmerless:/ˈɡlɪmərləs/
adjective
无微光的，暗淡的
Lacking any glimmer of light; completely dark or dull.
The moonless night was glimmerless and oppressive.  无月的夜晚一片漆黑，令人感到压抑。
The future seemed glimmerless and hopeless. 未来似乎一片黯淡，毫无希望。
近义词:dark, dim, gloomy, murky
反义词:bright, shining, luminous, radiant
词性:adjective

glimmer:/ˈɡlɪmər/
noun
微光，闪烁
A faint, unsteady light.
I saw a glimmer of hope in his eyes. 我在他的眼中看到了一丝希望的微光。
There was a glimmer of light at the end of the tunnel. 隧道尽头有一丝微光。
verb
闪烁，发出微光
To shine faintly and unsteadily.
The stars glimmered in the night sky.  星星在夜空中闪烁。
A single candle glimmered in the darkness. 一支蜡烛在黑暗中闪烁着微光。
近义词:shine, flicker, twinkle, gleam
反义词:fade, darken, extinguish
词性:noun, verb

词源:
From *glimmer* + *-less*.  *Glimmer* comes from Middle English *glimeren*, from Old English *glimmerian*, of Germanic origin.  *-less* is a suffix meaning "without" or "lacking."
