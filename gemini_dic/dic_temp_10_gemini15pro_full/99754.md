intermeddler: /ˌɪntərˈmedlər/ | n. | 干涉者，爱管闲事的人 | A person who interferes in the affairs of others, often without permission or invitation.  |  He's such an intermeddler, always sticking his nose in other people's business. 他真是个爱管闲事的人，总是把鼻子伸到别人的事情里。 | Synonyms: busybody, meddler, interferer | Antonyms:  noninterfering person, bystander | noun

intermeddle: /ˌɪntərˈmedl/ | v. | 干涉，干预 | To interfere in something that is not one's concern. |  She intermeddled in her sister's marriage, causing a lot of tension. 她干涉她姐姐的婚姻，造成了很多紧张气氛。| Synonyms: interfere, meddle, intrude | Antonyms: ignore, abstain, disregard | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *intermedlen*, from Anglo-Norman *entremedler*, from Old French *entremedler* (“to mix, mingle, meddle”), from *entre-* (from Latin *inter*- (“between, among”)) + *medler* (“to mix, meddle”).
