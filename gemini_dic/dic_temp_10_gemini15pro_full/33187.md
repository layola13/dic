chromolithography: /ˌkroʊməlɪˈθɑːɡrəfi/ /ˌkrəʊməlɪˈθɒɡrəfi/
noun
彩色石版印刷
A method of printing color images by using multiple lithographic stones, each applying a different color ink.
The museum has a collection of antique chromolithographs.  博物馆收藏了古董彩色石版画。
synonyms: color lithography
antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps black and white printing or monochrome printing)
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From chromo- + -lithography.  Chromo- comes from Greek chrōma "color". Lithography comes from Greek lithos "stone" + -graphia "writing".
