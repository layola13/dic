acquiesced: /ˌækwiˈɛst/ | v. | 默许，勉强同意 | to accept something reluctantly but without protest |  He acquiesced to their demands. = 他默许了他们的要求。

She acquiesced in the decision, although she didn't agree with it. = 她勉强同意了这个决定，尽管她并不赞同。

近义词: consent, comply, agree, accede | 反义词: object, resist, oppose, dissent | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *acquiescer*, from Latin *acquiescere* ("to rest in, find repose in, be satisfied with"), from *ad-* ("to, towards") + *quiescere* ("to rest, be quiet").

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He acquiesces to his parents' wishes. = 他顺从父母的意愿。
She always acquiesces without complaint. = 她总是毫无怨言地默许。

一般过去时:
The government acquiesced to the protesters' demands. = 政府默许了抗议者的要求。
They acquiesced in the plan because they had no other choice. = 他们勉强同意了这个计划，因为他们别无选择。

现在完成时:
The company has acquiesced to the union's demands. = 公司已经默许了工会的要求。
He has acquiesced in the decision made by the board. = 他已经默许了董事会的决定。

过去完成时:
They had acquiesced to the changes before I joined the company. = 在我加入公司之前，他们就已经默许了这些变化。
She had acquiesced in his plan even though she thought it was risky. = 即使她认为他的计划有风险，她还是默许了。

将来时:
I doubt they will acquiesce to our proposal. = 我怀疑他们会同意我们的提议。
If we present a strong case, they might acquiesce. = 如果我们提出强有力的论据，他们可能会默许。

虚拟语气:
If he were to acquiesce, it would surprise everyone. = 如果他默许，大家都会感到惊讶。
I wish they would acquiesce to our demands. = 我希望他们会同意我们的要求。

被动语态:
The proposal was acquiesced to by the majority. = 该提案得到了大多数人的默许。
His demands were acquiesced in without further discussion. = 他的要求未经进一步讨论就被默许了。


PHRASE->
acquiesce in (something) = 默许某事
acquiesce to (something) = 默许某事
acquiesce without protest =  默默地默许
tacitly acquiesce = 默认
reluctantly acquiesce =  勉强默许
