harass:/ˈhærəs/ (US), /ˈhərəs/ (UK)| vt. n.|骚扰，烦扰|To persistently annoy or bother someone, often to the point of distress or intimidation.  The act of harassing.|The paparazzi harassed the celebrity relentlessly. 狗仔队不断地骚扰这位名人。|The constant harassment from her coworkers made her dread going to work.  同事们不断的骚扰让她害怕上班。|近义词：pester, bother, torment, persecute, hound|反义词：soothe, comfort, support|词性:动词, 名词|词源: From Old French *harasser "to set a dog on, harry," from Old North French harer (compare Norman harier "to incite, urge on a dog"). Related to Old High German herot "army," literally "war-cry."
