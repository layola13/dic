idealistic:/ˌaɪdiːəˈlɪstɪk/ | adj. | 理想主义的；空想的 | Believing in ideals, often impractical or unrealistic.  Characterized by idealism; unrealistically aiming for perfection. |  She has an idealistic view of marriage. 她对婚姻抱有理想化的看法。

He's young and idealistic and believes he can change the world. 他年轻而理想主义，相信自己可以改变世界。| 近义词: utopian, quixotic, visionary | 反义词: realistic, pragmatic, cynical | 词性: adjective

Etymology:
From ideal + -istic. "Ideal" comes from Late Latin idealis "existing in idea," from idea (see idea). The suffix "-istic" forms adjectives meaning "relating to" or "characteristic of."
