instructions:/ɪnˈstrʌkʃənz/ | n. | 指示，说明 |  Directions or orders given to someone about how to do something. | Please follow the instructions carefully. 请仔细按照说明操作。 | directions, orders, commands, guidance |  None (lack of instructions might be implied by words like "confusion," "disarray") | Noun

/ɪnˈstrʌkt/ | v. | 指导，指示 | To teach someone how to do something; to give someone detailed information about how something should be done. | The teacher instructed the students to complete the exercise. 老师指示学生完成练习。 | teach, educate, guide, direct, train | confuse, mislead | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English instruccioun, instruction, from Old French instruction, from Latin instructiō (“a building, constructing, furnishing, preparing, drawing up; education, instruction”), from īnstruere, from in- (“in, on”) + struere (“to pile, build”).
