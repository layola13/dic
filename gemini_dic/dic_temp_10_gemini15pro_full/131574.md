noncommutativity:/ˌnɒnkɒmjʊtəˈtɪvɪti/ (US) /ˌnɒnkɒmjuːtəˈtɪvɪti/ (UK)| n.|非交换性|The property of an operation where the order of the operands affects the result.  For example, matrix multiplication is noncommutative.|Matrix multiplication exhibits noncommutativity, meaning AB is not necessarily equal to BA.|Near Synonyms: non-abelian, noncommutative property|Antonyms: commutativity, abelian|Noun
