grammaticalization:/ˌɡræmætɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |语法化|The process by which a lexical item or construction becomes grammatical, i.e., part of the grammar of a language.|例句：The grammaticalization of the word "going" to express future tense is a common phenomenon in English. “going”一词的语法化用来表达将来时在英语中是一种常见现象。|近义词：grammaticisation |反义词:lexicalization|词性:noun

grammaticalization:/ˌɡræmætɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |语法化|The development of a grammatical function by a word or phrase that originally had a lexical meaning.|例句：The grammaticalization of "will" from a verb of volition to a future tense marker is a well-documented case.  “will”从一个意愿动词发展成一个将来时标记是一个有充分记录的案例。|近义词：grammaticisation |反义词:lexicalization|词性:noun
