distractability:/dɪˌstræktəˈbɪləti/ | n. |分心，注意力分散|The quality of being easily distracted.  The tendency to be easily drawn away from one's focus. |His distractability made it difficult for him to study. 他的注意力不集中使他很难学习。| Distractability can be a major obstacle to academic success. 注意力分散可能是学业成功的主要障碍。 | 近义词: inattentiveness, flightiness, short attention span | 反义词: focus, concentration, attentiveness | 词性: 名词

distract:/dɪˈstrækt/ | v. |转移(注意力); 使分心|To draw someone's attention away from something. To divert someone's focus. |The noise distracted him from his work. 噪音使他无法专心工作。| The constant interruptions distracted her from finishing the report.  不断的打扰让她无法完成报告。| 近义词: divert, sidetrack | 反义词: focus, concentrate | 词性: 动词

distracted:/dɪˈstræktɪd/ | adj. |心烦意乱的; 注意力分散的|Unable to concentrate because one's attention is drawn to other things. Having one's attention diverted. |He seemed distracted during the meeting.  他在会议期间似乎心不在焉。|She was too distracted to notice the car approaching. 她太分心了，没有注意到汽车驶近。| 近义词: preoccupied, inattentive | 反义词: focused, attentive | 词性: 形容词


词源:
From Late Latin *dīstractus, past participle of dīstrahere (“to pull asunder, divide, separate”), from dis- (“apart, asunder”) + trahere (“to draw, pull”).
