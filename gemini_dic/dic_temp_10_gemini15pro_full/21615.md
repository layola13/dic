bluntish:/ˈblʌntɪʃ/ | adj. | 钝的，有点钝的 | Moderately blunt; not very sharp. | The knife was bluntish and couldn't cut the vegetables properly. 这把刀有点钝，不能 properly把蔬菜切好。|  The scissors are a bit bluntish. 这把剪刀有点钝。| 近义词: dull, obtuse | 反义词: sharp, keen | 词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From blunt + -ish.  Blunt itself comes from Old Norse *bluntr, related to Old English blunt "dull, obtuse," from Proto-Germanic *bluntaz. The suffix "-ish" forms adjectives meaning "somewhat," "rather," or "having the quality of."
