dialogism:/ˈdaɪəˌlɒdʒɪzəm/| n.|对话主义，对话性|A philosophy that emphasizes the importance of dialogue and the interconnectedness of voices and perspectives in understanding meaning and shaping reality. It stresses the social and cultural contexts of language and the role of multiple voices in the construction of knowledge.  It rejects the idea of a single, authoritative voice or perspective and instead emphasizes the importance of understanding multiple viewpoints.|Bakhtin's work on dialogism has had a profound impact on literary theory. 巴赫金关于对话主义的著作对文学理论产生了深远的影响。|近义词：intertextuality, polyphony|反义词：monologism|词性：noun
