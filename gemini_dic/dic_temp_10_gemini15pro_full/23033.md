boulevardier:/ˌbuːləˈvɑːrdjeɪ/ /ˌbuːləˈvɑːrdiər/ | n. |  花花公子，纨绔子弟；浪荡子 | A sophisticated, worldly man, often associated with a lifestyle of leisure and pleasure-seeking. | He was a true boulevardier, always seen at the finest restaurants and clubs. 他是一个真正的花花公子，总是出现在最好的餐厅和俱乐部。 | 近义词：man-about-town, playboy, socialite | 反义词: homebody, recluse | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *boulevardier*, from *boulevard* (“boulevard”), ultimately from Dutch *bolwerk* (“bulwark, bastion”).  The term originally referred to a person who frequented boulevards, often implying a fashionable and leisurely lifestyle.
