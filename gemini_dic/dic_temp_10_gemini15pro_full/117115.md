mealy-mouthedly:/ˈmiːliˌmaʊðɪdli/
| adv. |吞吞吐吐地，含糊其辞地|Speaking indirectly and evasively, unwilling to express oneself plainly or directly.  Characterized by a lack of frankness or straightforwardness, often for fear of giving offense or causing trouble.|He mealy-mouthedly suggested that perhaps there might be a problem.|近义词：evasively, indirectly, vaguely, equivocally|反义词：directly, frankly, plainly, candidly|词性:副词

mealy-mouthed:/ˈmiːliˌmaʊðd/
| adj. |吞吞吐吐的，含糊其辞的|Unwilling to express oneself plainly or directly; speaking indirectly and evasively, often for fear of giving offense or causing trouble.|The politician gave a mealy-mouthed response to the journalist's question.|近义词：evasively, indirect, vague, equivocal|反义词：direct, frank, plain, candid|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "mealy" (soft, bland) + "mouthed" (having a particular kind of mouth).  "Mealy" comes from Old English *mealu (meal, flour). The implication is of someone whose speech is as bland and untextured as flour.
