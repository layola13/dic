compurgation:/ˌkɒmpərˈɡeɪʃən/ | n. |宣誓清除罪名；通过宣誓证明清白|The practice of clearing an accused person of a charge by having a certain number of people swear to their innocence.  This was a common practice in medieval law.| Example:  Compurgation was a common way to avoid trial by ordeal in medieval Europe. 中世纪欧洲，宣誓清除罪名是避免神判的一种常见方式。

Example:  The accused relied on compurgation to prove his innocence, gathering eleven oath-helpers to swear he was telling the truth. 被告依靠宣誓清除罪名来证明自己的清白，召集了十一个宣誓助手来证明他所说的是实话。|近义词：exculpation, acquittal, exoneration |反义词：condemnation, conviction |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *compurgacion*, from Late Latin compurgatio, from compurgare “to purify completely,” from com- “together” + purgare “to cleanse, purge.”
