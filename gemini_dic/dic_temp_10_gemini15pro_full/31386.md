charge:/tʃɑːrdʒ/ /tʃɑːrdʒ/| n. v.|费用，指控，冲锋，掌管|An amount of money that you have to pay for something. To accuse someone formally of a crime. A sudden rapid attack. Responsibility for something or someone.|
例句：The charge for the service is $50. 这项服务的费用是50美元。

例句：He was charged with murder. 他被指控犯有谋杀罪。

例句：The cavalry made a charge at the enemy.  骑兵向敌人发起了冲锋。

例句：She is in charge of the project. 她负责这个项目。

近义词：fee, price, cost (n.); accuse, indict (v.); attack, assault (v.); responsibility, duty (n.)|
反义词：discharge, acquit (v.); retreat (v.)|
词性: 名词, 动词


charge:/tʃɑːrdʒ/ /tʃɑːrdʒ/| n. v.|充电|To store electrical energy in a battery or device.|
例句：I need to charge my phone. 我需要给手机充电。

例句：The electric car takes eight hours to fully charge. 这辆电动汽车需要八小时才能充满电。

近义词：power, energize|
反义词：discharge|
词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French chargier "to load, burden," from Late Latin carricare "to load a car," from Latin carrus "wagon."
