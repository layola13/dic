firebomb:/ˈfaɪərbɒm/ (UK) /ˈfaɪərbɑːm/ (US)| n. vt.|燃烧弹；用燃烧弹袭击|A bomb containing flammable material, such as petrol, often in a glass bottle, designed to cause a fire.  (As a verb) To attack with firebombs.|

例句：
The rioters firebombed several buildings. 暴徒用燃烧弹袭击了几栋建筑物。

The firebomb exploded, setting the car ablaze. 燃烧弹爆炸了，汽车也燃烧了起来。


近义词：incendiary bomb, Molotov cocktail, petrol bomb|
反义词: extinguisher, suppressant (these oppose the action, not the object itself)|
词性: noun, verb|


firebomb:/ˈfaɪərbɒm/ (UK) /ˈfaɪərbɑːm/ (US)| vt.|用燃烧弹袭击|To attack with firebombs.|

例句：
They firebombed the embassy in protest. 他们为了抗议而用燃烧弹袭击了大使馆。

The warehouse was firebombed during the riots. 仓库在暴乱期间遭到燃烧弹袭击。


近义词：attack with firebombs, bomb with incendiaries|
反义词: extinguish, put out (these oppose the action)|
词性: verb|


词源: fire + bomb.  "Fire" from Old English "fyr," meaning "fire." "Bomb" from Middle French "bombe" and ultimately from Latin "bombus" meaning "a humming, buzzing sound," referring to the sound of an explosion. The combination effectively describes a device that creates fire through an explosive action.|
