haematogenesis:/ˌhiːmətoʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs/ /ˌhiːmætəˈdʒɛnɪsɪs/| n. |造血；血细胞生成|The process of blood cell formation, including erythropoiesis (red blood cells), leukopoiesis (white blood cells), and thrombopoiesis (platelets).|The doctor explained that chemotherapy can disrupt haematogenesis, leading to anemia.|Synonym: hematopoiesis|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but consider bone marrow suppression or myelosuppression)|noun

haematopoiesis:/ˌhiːmətoʊpɔɪˈiːsɪs/ /ˌhiːmætəpɔɪˈiːsɪs/| n. |造血；血细胞生成|The process of blood cell formation, including erythropoiesis (red blood cells), leukopoiesis (white blood cells), and thrombopoiesis (platelets).|The bone marrow is the primary site of haematopoiesis in adults.|Synonym: haematogenesis|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but consider bone marrow suppression or myelosuppression)|noun
