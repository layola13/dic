outsiderism:/ˌaʊtˈsaɪdərɪzəm/
n.|外来主义，局外人身份，边缘化|The state or quality of being an outsider; behavior or attitudes characteristic of outsiders, often involving a sense of alienation or rebellion against established norms.|The rise of outsiderism in politics has led to unexpected election results.  政治上的外来主义兴起导致了意外的选举结果。|近义词：alienation, marginalization, nonconformity|反义词：insiderism, establishment, conformity|词性:名词

outsider:/ˌaʊtˈsaɪdər/
n.|局外人，外人，门外汉|A person who does not belong to a particular group or organization; someone who is not familiar with a particular subject or activity.|He felt like an outsider looking in on their close-knit community. 他感觉自己像个局外人，旁观着他们紧密联系的社区。|近义词：stranger, alien, newcomer|反义词：insider, member, native|词性:名词
