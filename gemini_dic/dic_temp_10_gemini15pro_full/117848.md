mellower:/ˈmeləʊər (UK), ˈmeloʊər (US)| adj.|更醇厚的，更柔和的，更成熟的|Comparative form of mellow: more mellow.  It describes something that has become softer, smoother, more relaxed, or more pleasant, often with age or maturity.  It can refer to flavors, sounds, colors, personalities, or moods.|The wine is mellower after aging for a few years.  这葡萄酒陈酿几年后更醇厚了。

The singer's voice has become mellower with time.  这位歌手的声音随着时间的推移变得更加柔和了。|近义词：softer, smoother, gentler, richer, milder|反义词：harsh, sharp, bitter, immature|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mellow, from Old English *mearu "soft, tender," of Germanic origin; related to Old High German maro "soft, tender," Old Norse mjúkr "soft."
