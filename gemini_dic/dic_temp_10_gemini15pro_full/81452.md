gratingly:/ˈɡreɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 刺耳地，令人不快地 |  In a harsh, irritating, or unpleasant way, often referring to sound, but also to manner or behavior. |  He laughed gratingly, making everyone uncomfortable. 他刺耳地笑了，让每个人都很不舒服。 | synonyms: harshly, raspingly, jarringly, stridently, unpleasantly | antonyms: smoothly, pleasantly, melodiously, harmoniously | word type: adverb

gratingly:/ˈɡreɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 摩擦地，刮擦地 | With a harsh scraping sound. | The door opened gratingly on its rusty hinges. 门在生锈的铰链上发出刺耳的摩擦声打开。| synonyms: scrapingly, raspingly, scratchily | antonyms: smoothly, silently | word type: adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "grate" (v.) + "-ing" (present participle suffix) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix). "Grate" comes from Middle English "graten," from Old North French "grater," ultimately from Vulgar Latin "*cratere," frequentative of Latin "cretare"  "to crack, creak," from "creta" "chalk."
