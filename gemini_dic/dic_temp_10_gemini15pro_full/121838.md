misremembers:/ˌmɪsrɪˈmɛmbərz/ | v. |记错，误记| To remember incorrectly; to have a false or inaccurate memory of something. | She misremembers the details of the accident. 她记错了事故的细节。| synonyms: misremembered, misrecollecting, misrecall | antonyms: remembers, recalls, recollects accurately | verb (present tense, 3rd person singular)

misremembers:/ˌmɪsrɪˈmɛmbərz/ | v. |记错，误记| To remember incorrectly; to have a false or inaccurate memory of something. | He often misremembers names and faces. 他经常记错名字和面孔。| synonyms: misremembered, misrecollecting, misrecall | antonyms: remembers, recalls, recollects accurately | verb (present tense, 3rd person singular)


ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (meaning "wrongly" or "badly") + remember.  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old English "mis-", meaning "wrongly, amiss, badly." "Remember" comes from Old French "remembrer," from Latin "rememorari," meaning "to recall to mind, be mindful of."
