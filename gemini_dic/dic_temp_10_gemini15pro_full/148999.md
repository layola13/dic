ovoviviparousness:/ˌoʊvoʊˌvaɪvɪˈpærəsnəs/ | n. | 卵胎生 |  A mode of reproduction in which eggs are retained within the mother's body until they hatch, so that the young are born alive but without placental attachment. |  Many sharks exhibit ovoviviparousness, giving birth to live young. 许多鲨鱼表现出卵胎生，产下活的幼崽。 |  viviparity, aplacental viviparity | oviparity | noun
