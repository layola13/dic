caddies:/ˈkædiz/ (US), /ˈkædiz/ (UK)| n. |球童；球僮；（高尔夫球）球童包；茶叶罐|A person who carries a golfer's clubs and gives advice on the game.|例句：The caddie offered helpful advice about which club to use. 球童就使用哪种球杆提出了有益的建议。

例句：He hired a caddie for his round of golf. 他为他的高尔夫球赛雇了一个球童。|近义词：golf bag carrier, looper |反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "golfer" as the person being served by the caddie) |词性:noun


caddies:/ˈkædiz/ (US), /ˈkædiz/ (UK)| v. |(高尔夫球)当球童；用茶叶罐装|To work as a caddie.|例句：He caddied during the summer to earn extra money.  他在夏天当球童赚外快。

例句：She caddies for a famous golfer. 她为一位著名的高尔夫球手当球童。|近义词：loop, carry clubs |反义词: (No direct antonym) |词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the French word "cadet," meaning younger son, which came to refer to a boy doing errands.  This evolved into "caddie" specifically for those carrying clubs for golfers.
