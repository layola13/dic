countenance:/ˈkaʊntənəns/ (US), /ˈkaʊntɪnəns/ (UK)| v. n.| 支持，赞成; 面容，表情| to support, approve, or tolerate something; a person's face or facial expression |  The school will not countenance cheating. 学校不会容忍作弊行为。| synonyms: tolerate, permit, allow, endorse, sanction | antonyms: oppose, disapprove, condemn | verb

countenance:/ˈkaʊntənəns/ (US), /ˈkaʊntɪnəns/ (UK)| n.| 面容，表情| a person's face or facial expression | He had a sad countenance. 他面带悲伤。| synonyms: face, visage, expression, features | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *contenance* "bearing, demeanor, appearance," from *contenir* "to contain, hold," from Latin *continere* "to hold together, enclose," from *com-* "together" + *tenere* "to hold." The sense of "facial expression" developed from the idea of one's bearing or demeanor.
