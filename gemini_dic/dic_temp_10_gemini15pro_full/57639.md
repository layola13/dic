drave:/dreɪv/| v.| drove 的过去式 |The past tense of drive.  It means to operate and control the movement of a vehicle or animal.|He drave the oxen across the field. 他赶着牛穿过田野。|近义词：drove, steered, guided|反义词: stopped, halted|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *draven, *driven, from Old English drāf (past tense of drīfan (“to drive”)), from Proto-Germanic *drēb (“to drive”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰrebʰ- (“to muddy; to trouble; to drive”). Cognate with West Frisian dreau (“drove”), Dutch dreef (“drove”), German trieb (“drove”), Old Norse dreif (“drove”).
