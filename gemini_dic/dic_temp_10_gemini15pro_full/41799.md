cortes:/ˈkɔːrteɪz/ (US), /ˈkɔːtiːz/ (UK)| adj.| 彬彬有礼的；客气的；殷勤的 |Courteous is an adjective that describes someone who is polite, respectful, and considerate in their behavior towards others. It implies a genuine desire to be pleasant and accommodating.| He was very courteous and helpful. 他非常有礼貌，乐于助人。| The hotel staff were unfailingly courteous.  酒店工作人员始终彬彬有礼。 | synonyms: polite, respectful, civil, gracious, well-mannered | antonyms: rude, impolite, disrespectful, discourteous | adjective

cortes:/ˈkɔːrteɪz/ (US), /ˈkɔːtiːz/ (UK)| n.| 礼貌；客气；殷勤 (通常用复数corteses)| Cortes (plural) refers to acts of politeness, courtesy, or respectful gestures. While less common than the adjective form, it can be used to describe polite behavior in a more general sense.|  He offered her his seat with all due cortes. 他非常有礼貌地给她让座。|  His cortes were impeccable, making everyone feel welcome. 他的礼节无可挑剔，让每个人都感到宾至如归。| synonyms: civilities, courtesies, politeness | antonyms: rudeness, impoliteness, disrespect | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *corteis* (modern French *courtois*), from *cort* "court," from Latin *cohors* "enclosed place, cohort."  The original meaning referred to the refined manners expected in a royal court.
