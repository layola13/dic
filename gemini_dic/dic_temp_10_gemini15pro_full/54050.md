disdainer:/dɪsˈdeɪnər/
n.
轻蔑者；鄙视者
A person who disdains; one who regards something as beneath them.
He is a disdainer of popular culture. 他是一个鄙视流行文化的人。
Synonyms: scorner, despiser, contemner
Antonyms: admirer, respecter
noun


disdainful:/dɪsˈdeɪnfʊl/
adj.
轻蔑的；鄙视的
Showing contempt or lack of respect.
She cast a disdainful glance at the cheap jewelry. 她轻蔑地瞥了一眼那些廉价珠宝。
Synonyms: contemptuous, scornful, disrespectful
Antonyms: respectful, admiring
adjective


disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/
n.
轻蔑；鄙视
The feeling that someone or something is unworthy of one's consideration or respect.
He looked at me with disdain. 他轻蔑地看着我。
v.
轻蔑；鄙视；不屑
Consider to be unworthy of one's consideration or respect.
She disdained to answer his rude question. 她不屑回答他粗鲁的问题。
Synonyms: contempt, scorn, disregard (n.) / scorn, despise, reject (v.)
Antonyms: respect, admiration, esteem (n.) / admire, respect, value (v.)
noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desdeigner, from des- "expressing negation" + deigner "to deign," from Latin dignari "to deem worthy."
