ETYMOLOGY->

From "de-" (prefix meaning "remove" or "deprive of") + "queen".  Modeled after words like "dethrone".  Primarily associated with the city name De Queen, Arkansas, which was named after a railroad stop believed to be named after a Choctaw phrase meaning "yellow pine," misinterpreted by French settlers.
