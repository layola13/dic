neoliberals:/ˌniːoʊˈlɪbərəlz/ |n.| 新自由主义者 | People who believe in and support the principles of neoliberalism, an ideology that emphasizes free markets, deregulation, and limited government intervention. | Many economists criticized the government's neoliberal policies. 许多经济学家批评了政府的新自由主义政策。| The conference attracted neoliberals from around the world.  这次会议吸引了来自世界各地的新自由主义者。|Synonyms: free marketeers, laissez-faire capitalists |Antonyms: socialists, communists, interventionists |noun

neo-/ˈniːoʊ/ |prefix| 新 |  A prefix meaning "new," "recent," or "revived."| Neoliberalism is a relatively recent economic philosophy. 新自由主义是一种相对较新的经济哲学。|  The museum has a collection of neoclassical sculptures.  博物馆收藏了新古典主义雕塑。| Synonyms: new, modern, recent |Antonyms: old, ancient, archaic |prefix

liberal:/ˈlɪbərəl/ |adj. n.| 自由主义的；自由主义者| Favoring individual liberty, free trade, and moderate political and social reform. |  He has a liberal attitude towards immigration. 他对移民持自由主义态度。| She is a member of the Liberal Party. 她是自由党的成员。| Synonyms: progressive, open-minded, tolerant |Antonyms: conservative, reactionary, authoritarian |adjective, noun
