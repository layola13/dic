distractious: /dɪˈstrækʃəs/ | adj. | 易分心的，分散注意力的 | Easily distracted; tending to distract.  |  The child was very distractious and couldn't focus on his homework.  这个孩子很容易分心，无法集中精力做作业。 |  He found the constant noise very distractious. 他觉得持续不断的噪音让他很难集中注意力。| 近义词: distractible, inattentive, unfocused | 反义词: focused, attentive, concentrated | 词性:形容词

distraction: /dɪˈstrækʃən/ | n. | 分心，分散注意力的事物 | Something that prevents someone from giving their attention to something else. | The television was a constant distraction while I was trying to study.  电视是我学习时一直让我分心的事物。 |  There were too many distractions at the party, and I couldn't hear what he was saying. 派对上让人分心的事情太多了，我听不清他在说什么。| 近义词: disturbance, interruption, diversion | 反义词: focus, concentration | 词性:名词

distract: /dɪˈstrækt/ | v. | 分心，分散(注意力) | Prevent (someone) from giving their attention to something else. |  The noise from the street distracted her from her work. 街上的噪音让她无法专心工作。 |  Don't distract me while I'm driving! 我开车的时候别打扰我！ | 近义词: divert, sidetrack, disturb | 反义词: focus, concentrate | 词性:动词


词源:  From Late Latin *distractus, past participle of distrahere "to pull apart, divide, separate," from dis- "apart" + trahere "to draw, pull."
