conjurorship:/ˈkʌndʒərərʃɪp/
n.
魔术师的职业或技艺; 魔法; 巫术
The art or skill of a conjurer; magic; witchcraft.
He amazed the audience with his conjurorship. 他以其精湛的魔术技艺令观众叹为观止。
Synonyms: magic, wizardry, sorcery, witchcraft
Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, consider "inability," "ineptitude," or concepts like "science," "reality")
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From conjurer + -ship.  "Conjurer" comes from Old French "conjureor," ultimately from Latin "conjurare" (to swear together, conspire, implore). "-ship" is an Old English suffix denoting state, condition, or office.
