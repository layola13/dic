paralysing:/ˈpærəlaɪzɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈpærəˌlaɪzɪŋ/ (US)| adj.|使瘫痪的，使麻痹的；令人不知所措的|Causing someone or something to become paralysed or unable to move or function; causing a feeling of fear or shock that makes someone unable to act.|The fear was paralysing. 恐惧令人不知所措。|Synonyms: debilitating, crippling, immobilizing, incapacitating, numbing|Antonyms: invigorating, empowering, energizing|adjective


paralyse:/ˈpærəlaɪz/ (UK) /ˈpærəˌlaɪz/ (US)| v.|使瘫痪，使麻痹；使丧失作用|To make someone unable to move or feel all or part of their body; to make something unable to operate normally.|The accident paralysed him from the waist down. 事故导致他腰部以下瘫痪。|Synonyms: disable, cripple, immobilize, incapacitate|Antonyms: enable, empower, invigorate|verb
