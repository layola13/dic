cruiser:/ˈkruːzər/ (US), /ˈkruːzə(r)/ (UK)| n.|巡洋舰；巡逻车；大型摩托车|A large fast warship.  A police car used for patrolling. A large, comfortable motorcycle.|
The navy deployed a cruiser to the region.|海军向该区域部署了一艘巡洋舰。
He saw the flashing lights of a police cruiser in his rearview mirror.|他从后视镜里看到了警车闪烁的灯光。
They rode their cruisers down the coast highway.|他们骑着大型摩托车沿着海岸公路行驶。
|近义词：warship (for military ships), patrol car (for police cars), motorcycle (for motorcycles)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "cruise," which originated from the Dutch word "kruisen" meaning "to cross."  The sense of leisurely sailing developed later, and from that came the use of "cruiser" to describe ships designed for such travel, then extended to other vehicles used for patrolling or leisurely travel.
