basilicas:/bəˈsɪlɪkəz/
|n.|教堂，圣殿|A large oblong hall or building with double colonnades and a semicircular apse, used in ancient Rome as a court of law or for public assemblies. Later adopted by the Christian Church as a church of similar form, often one of great size and associated with a diocese or with the pope.|The pope celebrated Mass at St. Peter's Basilica.|近义词：cathedral, church, temple|反义词:|名词
