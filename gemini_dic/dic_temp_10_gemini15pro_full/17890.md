been-around:/ˌbɪn əˈraʊnd/ | adj. | 经验丰富的；老练的；见过世面的；流传已久的 | Experienced; worldly-wise; having existed for a long time. | He's been around, so he knows how to handle these situations. 他经验丰富，所以他知道如何处理这些情况。 |  My grandfather's been around the block a few times. 我祖父可是个见过世面的人。 | 近义词: experienced, seasoned, sophisticated, veteran | 反义词: naive, inexperienced, green | 词性:形容词

词源:  "Been" is the past participle of the verb "to be," and "around" indicates presence in a place or experience over time.  The phrase evolved to mean having been in many places and situations, thus gaining experience.

USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
He's been around and knows the best places to eat. 他经验丰富，知道最好的餐馆。
That song's been around for ages. 那首歌已经流行很久了.


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
That style is been-around for a while now. 那种款式已经流行一段时间了。
He's being been-around, acting like he knows everything. 他表现得像个老油条，好像什么都知道。 (Note: This usage is less common and can sound awkward. "Acting like he's been around" is more natural.)


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
She's been around the world twice. 她已经环游世界两次了。
He's been around long enough to know better. 他阅历丰富，应该更懂事才对。



现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
This technology has been been-around for several years. 这项技术已经存在好几年了。（Note: Using "been" twice here is grammatically incorrect. The correct form would be "This technology has been around for several years.")


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
He had been around a bit before settling down. 他在安定下来之前四处游历了一番.
The band had been around since the 70s. 这支乐队从70年代就存在了。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
He was been-around even back then.  (Incorrect usage.  "He was already experienced back then" would be better.)


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
She had been around the world before she turned 30. 她30岁之前就环游过世界了。

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
He had been been-around for years before he finally found success. (Incorrect usage. "He had been working in the industry for years..." would be better.)


一般将来时 (Simple Future):
This trend will be been-around for a while. (Incorrect usage. "This trend will be around for a while" is correct.)


将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
This product will be been-around for years to come. (Incorrect usage.  "This product will be around for years to come" is correct.)
