ablest:/ˈeɪblɪst/| adj.|最有能力的，最能干的|Having or showing the greatest ability; most competent or capable.|She is the ablest lawyer in the firm. 她是最能干的律师。

Marie Curie was one of the ablest scientists of her time. 玛丽·居里是她那个时代最有能力的科学家之一。|近义词：most capable, most competent, most skilled, most talented|反义词：least capable, least competent, most inept|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English ablest, superlative of able (“able, capable”), from Old French habile (“able, suitable, competent”), from Latin habilis (“easily held, manageable, apt, fit, suitable, proper, skillful”), from habēre (“to have, hold”).  The superlative suffix -est comes from Old English -est, from Proto-Germanic *-istaz.
