necropolises: /nəˈkrɒpəlɪsiːz/
n.
墓地，坟场（necropolis 的复数）
A large, designed cemetery with elaborate tomb monuments.  A city of the dead.
The ancient necropolises of Egypt are fascinating to explore.  探索埃及古老的墓地非常引人入胜。

necropolises: /nəˈkrɒpəlɪsiːz/
n.
墓地，坟场（necropolis 的复数）
A place where many people are buried, especially a large and elaborate cemetery.
The Valley of the Kings is one of the most famous necropolises in the world.  帝王谷是世界上最著名的墓地之一。

necropolises: /nəˈkrɒpəlɪsiːz/
n.
墓地，坟场（necropolis 的复数）
Origin:  From Greek nekropolis, from nekros (dead body) + polis (city).
Many ancient civilizations built elaborate necropolises for their deceased rulers. 许多古代文明为他们已故的统治者建造了精心设计的墓地。

necropolises: /nəˈkrɒpəlɪsiːz/
n.
墓地，坟场（necropolis 的复数）
Synonym: cemeteries, burial grounds, graveyards
Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but could consider "living city" as a conceptual opposite)
