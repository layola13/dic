loggerheads:/ˈlɒɡəhedz/ (UK) /ˈlɔːɡərhedz/ (US)| n. |争吵，意见不合；蠵龟|at loggerheads (with someone) (over something): in strong disagreement or dispute with someone about something.  The two countries are at loggerheads over trade.  Also refers to a type of sea turtle. |例句：The two companies have been at loggerheads for months over the terms of the merger.  两家公司就合并条款争执了几个月。

例句：Loggerhead turtles are an endangered species.  蠵龟是一种濒危物种。|近义词：disagreement, dispute, conflict, at odds, variance |反义词:agreement, harmony, accord|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "logger" (a heavy piece of wood) + "head."  Originally referred to a blockhead or stupid person.  The sense of "in conflict" likely arose from the image of two thick-headed people butting heads.  The application to the turtle is due to its large head.
