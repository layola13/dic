hyperresonant:/ˌhaɪpərˈrɛzənənt/| adj.|过共振的，亢进的|Characterized by an exaggerated resonance, typically a lower-pitched and booming sound heard on percussion of a body cavity, such as the chest or abdomen, indicating an abnormal increase in air content.|例句：The patient's chest exhibited a hyperresonant sound on percussion. 病人的胸部叩诊呈现过共振的声音。

例句：Hyperresonance can be a sign of pneumothorax. 过共振可能是气胸的征兆。|近义词：booming, resonant, tympanitic|反义词：dull, flat|词性：形容词

词源:
hyper- (prefix): from Greek, meaning "over, beyond, excessive"
resonant (adjective): from Latin resonantem, present participle of resonare "to resound," from re- "back" + sonare "to sound"
