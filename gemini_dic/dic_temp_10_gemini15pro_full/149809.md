paleocrinoidea:/ˌpeɪliəʊˌkraɪnɔɪˈdiːə/
| n. |古海百合纲|An extinct class of echinoderms related to the crinoids, that existed from the Ordovician to the Permian periods.|Paleocrinoidea fossils are valuable for understanding marine ecosystems of the Paleozoic era. 古海百合纲化石对于理解古生代海洋生态系统很有价值。|Synonyms: (No direct synonyms, but related terms include) crinoids, echinoderms|Antonyms: (No antonyms)|noun
