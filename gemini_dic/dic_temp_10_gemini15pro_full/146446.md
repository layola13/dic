overfreedoms:/ˈoʊvərˈfriːdəmz/
noun
过度自由；过多的自由
An excess of freedoms, often leading to negative consequences or a sense of paralysis.
The sheer number of choices available created a sense of overfreedoms, leaving consumers feeling overwhelmed. 过多的选择反而造成了过度自由的感觉，让消费者不知所措。
近义词: excess of freedoms, overabundance of choices, option overload
反义词: restrictions, limitations, constraints
词性:n.


overfreedoms:/ˈoʊvərˈfriːdəmz/
noun
过度自由；过多的自由
A state where individuals have so many choices and freedoms that it becomes overwhelming or detrimental.
Some argue that overfreedoms can lead to societal breakdown and a lack of shared values.  一些人认为，过度的自由会导致社会崩溃和共同价值观的缺失。
近义词: plethora of choices, excessive liberty, unbounded freedom
反义词: limited choices, constraints, regulations
词性:n.


词源:
"Over-" (prefix) + "freedoms" (noun)

"Over-" denotes an excess or going beyond a normal or desirable limit.
"Freedoms" refers to the power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants without hindrances.

The word is a relatively recent coinage, likely emerging in the late 20th or early 21st century to describe the perceived negative consequences of excessive choice and liberty in modern societies.
