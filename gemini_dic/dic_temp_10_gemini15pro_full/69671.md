fender:/ˈfɛndər/ (US), /ˈfɛndə/ (UK)| n.|挡泥板；防护板；（壁炉的）围栏|A part of a vehicle, such as a car, bicycle or boat, that arches over the wheels to protect it, the rider or the vehicle itself from mud or water sprayed up by the wheels.  It can also refer to the guard around a fireplace.|The car's fender was dented in the accident. 汽车的挡泥板在事故中凹陷了。|Synonyms: mudguard, wing (UK), guard|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "exposed")|noun

fender:/ˈfɛndər/ (US), /ˈfɛndə/ (UK)| v.|抵挡，防卫；避开|To ward off, defend against, or protect from something, often a blow or impact. It can also mean to take care of or provide for.|He used his arm to fender off the blow. 他用手臂挡开了那一击。|Synonyms: defend, protect, shield, guard, ward off|Antonyms: attack, assault, expose|verb
