disme:/daɪm/| n.|十分之一, 什一税, 十分之一银币 (尤指旧时的美国硬币)|A tenth part, especially a tenth part of one's income paid as a contribution (especially for the support of the church) or a tenth part of agricultural produce paid as tithes or rent; also, a tenth part of one's property or possessions paid as a tax to the government; a silver coin of the United States, formerly of the value of ten cents. |The king demanded a disme from all his subjects. 国王要求所有臣民缴纳十分之一的税.|Synonym: tithe, tenth|Antonym: whole|noun
