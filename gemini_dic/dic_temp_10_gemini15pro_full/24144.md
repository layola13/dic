bribe:/braɪb/| n. vt.|贿赂；贿赂物；诱惑物|Something, such as money or a favor, offered or given to someone in a position of trust in order to persuade them to act dishonestly.|He was offered a bribe to keep quiet about the incident.  他被贿赂对事件保持沉默。|The company was accused of offering bribes to government officials.  该公司被指控向政府官员行贿。|近义词：inducement, payoff, kickback|反义词：reward (for meritorious service)|n. v.

bribe:/braɪb/| vt.|向…行贿；用…贿赂|To give or offer a bribe to.|They bribed the guards to let them escape.  他们贿赂警卫让他们逃跑。|He was bribed into revealing confidential information. 他被贿赂泄露机密信息。|近义词：buy off, corrupt|反义词：--|v.
