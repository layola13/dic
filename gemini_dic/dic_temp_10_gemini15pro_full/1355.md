accumulate: /əˈkjuːmjəleɪt/ (US) /əˈkjuːmjʊleɪt/ (UK)
| v. | 积累，积聚 | to gather or collect something in an increasing quantity over a period of time | Dust accumulated on the shelves.  灰尘在架子上积聚。 | synonyms: gather, collect, amass, accrue | antonyms: disperse, dissipate, squander, deplete | verb

accumulate: /əˈkjuːmjəleɪt/ (US) /əˈkjuːmjʊleɪt/ (UK)
| adj. | 累积的 | increasing gradually as more is added or happens | The accumulated evidence suggests he is guilty. 积累的证据表明他有罪。 | synonyms: cumulative, amassed, gathered | antonyms: dispersed, dissipated | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin accumulātus, past participle of accumulāre (“to heap up, amass”), from ad- (“to, towards”) + cumulāre (“to heap”), from cumulus (“heap”).

USAGE->
Present Simple:
They accumulate wealth over time. = 他们随着时间的推移积累财富。
She accumulates a lot of unnecessary items. = 她积攒了很多不必要的东西。

Present Continuous:
He is accumulating evidence for his case. = 他正在为他的案子收集证据。
The snow is accumulating quickly on the ground. = 地上的雪积得很快。

Past Simple:
We accumulated a large amount of debt. = 我们积累了大量的债务。
Over the years, she accumulated a vast collection of books. = 多年来，她积累了大量的书籍。

Past Continuous:
They were accumulating funds for the project. = 他们当时正在为该项目筹集资金。
The water was accumulating behind the dam. = 水在坝后积聚。

Present Perfect:

He has accumulated a substantial fortune. = 他已经积累了相当多的财富。

Past Perfect:
By the time he retired, he had accumulated a considerable pension pot. = 到他退休时，他已经积累了相当可观的养老金。
