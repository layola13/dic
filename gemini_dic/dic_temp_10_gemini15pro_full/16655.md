barracoon:/bærəˈkuːn/
noun
奴隶营，奴隶收容所
An enclosure in which enslaved people were kept before being shipped elsewhere, especially in West Africa.
Example 1: The barracoon was a grim reminder of the horrors of the slave trade.  奴隶营是奴隶贸易恐怖的残酷提醒。

Example 2:  The stench from the overcrowded barracoon was overwhelming.  拥挤不堪的奴隶营散发出的恶臭令人窒息。

Synonym: slave pen, slave quarters
Antonym: freedom, liberty
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *barracón*, augmentative of *barraca* ("hut, shack"), from Arabic *barraka* ("blessed, prosperous").  The semantic shift occurred as these structures were initially used for various purposes before becoming associated primarily with the slave trade.
