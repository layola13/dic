abstractiveness:/æbˈstræktnəs/
n.
抽象性，抽象度
The quality of being abstract; abstractness of the concept makes it difficult to understand.
这个概念的抽象性使得它难以理解。

He discussed the painting's form and abstractiveness.
他讨论了这幅画的形式和抽象性。

近义词: abstraction, generality, vagueness
反义词: concreteness, specificity, clarity
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From abstract + -ness.  Abstract comes from Late Latin abstractus, past participle of abstrahere "to draw away," from Latin abs-, ab- "off, away from" + trahere "to draw."

USAGE->
The noun "abstractiveness" is typically used to describe the level or quality of being abstract. It can be used in various contexts, often in academic or philosophical discussions.

Example 1: The abstractiveness of the theory made it difficult to apply to real-world situations.  该理论的抽象性使其难以应用于现实世界的情况。

Example 2:  The artist explored the abstractiveness of form and color in her latest exhibition.  这位艺术家在她最新的展览中探索了形式和色彩的抽象性。

Example 3: The abstractiveness of the concept made it hard for students to grasp. 这个概念的抽象性使学生难以理解。

Example 4: He appreciated the poem's abstractiveness and ambiguity. 他欣赏这首诗的抽象性和含糊性。
