cunctator:/kʌŋkˈteɪtər/ | n. | 拖延者，迟缓者 |  One who delays or puts things off; a procrastinator.  It derives from the Latin verb *cunctārī*, meaning "to delay, linger, hesitate."  Famous example is Quintus Fabius Maximus Verrucosus,  a Roman general and statesman given the agnomen "Cunctator" for his delaying tactics against Hannibal during the Second Punic War. | Fabius was known as the "Cunctator" for his cautious strategy.  费边以其谨慎的策略被称为“拖延者”。 | The general's cunctatory tactics frustrated the enemy's advance. 将军的拖延战术挫败了敌人的进攻。 | synonyms: procrastinator, delayer, loiterer, lingerer | antonyms: expediter, doer, go-getter | noun
