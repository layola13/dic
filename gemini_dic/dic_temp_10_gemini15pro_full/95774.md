incontestably:/ˌɪnkənˈtestəbli/ | adv. |无可争议地，毋庸置疑地 | Not able to be contested; undoubtedly. | Her victory was incontestably the greatest achievement of her career. 她取得的胜利无疑是她职业生涯中最伟大的成就。|  incontestably proven = 无可争议地证明 |Synonyms: undeniably, indisputably, irrefutably, unquestionably | Antonyms: disputably, arguably, questionably | Adverb

incontestable:/ˌɪnkənˈtestəbl/ | adj. |无可争议的，不容置疑的| Not able to be contested; beyond dispute. | The evidence against him was incontestable.  针对他的证据是无可争议的。| incontestable proof = 确凿的证据 | Synonyms: undeniable, indisputable, irrefutable, unquestionable | Antonyms: disputable, arguable, questionable | Adjective


Etymology:
From Late Latin *incontestabilis, from in- (“not”) + contestari (“to call to witness, to contest”)  (from con- (“together”) + testari (“to bear witness”)).
