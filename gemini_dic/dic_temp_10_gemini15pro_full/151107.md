parasitic:/ˌpærəˈsɪtɪk/ | adj. |寄生的，寄生虫的；依赖他人生活的| living as a parasite; exploiting others |例句：Mistletoe is a parasitic plant that grows on trees. 槲寄生是一种寄生在树木上的寄生植物。

例句：He has a parasitic lifestyle, always relying on others for money and support. 他过着寄生虫般的生活，总是依赖别人的金钱和支持。|近义词：dependent, leeching, bloodsucking |反义词：independent, self-sufficient |词性: adjective

parasitic:/ˌpærəˈsɪtɪk/ | n. |寄生虫| a plant or an animal that lives on or in another plant or animal and gets its food from it | 例句：Fleas and ticks are common parasites of dogs. 跳蚤和蜱虫是狗身上常见的寄生虫。

例句：This type of parasitic worm can infect humans. 这种寄生虫会感染人类。 | 近义词： freeloader, hanger-on | 反义词： host | 词性: noun (rarely used)
