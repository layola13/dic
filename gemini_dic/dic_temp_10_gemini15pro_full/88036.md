highbrowed:/ˈhaɪˌbraʊd/| adj.|高傲的，自以为文化修养高的|Having or showing a disapproval of things that are popular, or an interest in things that are considered to be serious and important.|She gave him a highbrowed look when he mentioned the latest reality TV show. 当他提到最新的真人秀节目时，她给了他一个高傲的眼神。|近义词：pretentious, supercilious, snobbish|反义词：lowbrow, unpretentious, down-to-earth|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From highbrow (n.) + -ed. Highbrow (n. & adj.) is from the notion of a high forehead being considered a mark of intelligence.  First recorded in 1902 (as high-brow).
