glom:/ɡlɒm/ (British) /ɡlɑːm/ (American)| v. n.|凝视，盯着看；抓住，攫取；理解，领会|To stare at; to seize or grab; to understand or grasp.|He glommed onto the idea immediately. 他立刻就抓住了这个想法。|近义词：grasp, seize, snatch, stare, gape|反义词: release, miss, overlook|词性:动词, 名词

glom:/ɡlɒm/ (British) /ɡlɑːm/ (American)| n.|一瞥，一看|A quick look or glance.|He took a glom at the newspaper headlines. 他快速浏览了一下报纸的标题。|近义词：glance, peek|反义词: scrutiny|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the early 20th century, likely of imitative origin, perhaps related to words like "glob" and "clump."  Possibly influenced by Yiddish *glemen* “to peep, steal a glance.”
