gossiping:/ˈɡɑːsəpɪŋ/ /ˈɡɒsɪpɪŋ/| n. adj.|闲聊，八卦|the act of talking about other people's private lives, often in a way that is unkind or untrue|例句：They were gossiping about their neighbours. 他们在谈论邻居的八卦。|近义词：chattering, tattling|反义词:reticence, silence|词性:noun (gerund/present participle of "gossip")

gossiping:/ˈɡɑːsəpɪŋ/ /ˈɡɒsɪpɪŋ/| adj.|爱说闲话的|Given to or engaging in gossip.|例句：She has a gossiping tongue. 她有一张爱说闲话的嘴。|近义词：talkative, chatty|反义词:reserved, discreet|词性:adjective

词源：From *godsibb*, from *God* + *sibb* (“relative, kin”). Originally meant "godparent", later acquiring the sense of a close friend or neighbour (especially a woman), and from there the sense of idle chatter.
