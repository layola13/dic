electrolytic:/ˌɪˌlɛktrəˈlɪtɪk/ | adj. |电解的；由电解产生的|Relating to or using electrolysis (the passage of an electric current through a substance to cause a chemical change).|例句：The electrolytic process is used to refine copper.  电解法用于精炼铜。

例句：Electrolytic capacitors are commonly used in electronic circuits. 电解电容器常用于电子电路中。|近义词：electrochemical|反义词: none|词性: adjective

electrolyte: /ɪˈlɛktrəˌlaɪt/ | n. |电解质；电解液|A substance that produces an electrically conducting solution when dissolved in a polar solvent, such as water.|例句：Sports drinks contain electrolytes to replace those lost through sweating. 运动饮料含有电解质，以补充因出汗而流失的电解质。

例句：Sodium chloride is a common electrolyte. 氯化钠是一种常见的电解质。|近义词：ion conductor|反义词: none |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From electro- + -lytic.  "Electro-" comes from the Greek "ēlektron" meaning "amber," referencing the static electricity produced by rubbing amber. "-lytic" relates to "lysis," meaning "loosening" or "breaking down," referring to the chemical decomposition caused by electrolysis.
