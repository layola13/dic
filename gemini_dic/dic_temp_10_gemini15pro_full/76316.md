gabfests:/ˈɡæbfɛsts/| n.|闲聊；饶舌；聚会聊天|A gabfest is an informal gathering where people engage in extended conversation, often about trivial matters. |例句：We had a gabfest over coffee this morning. 今天早上我们边喝咖啡边闲聊。

例句：The ladies' monthly lunch was always a lively gabfest. 女士们的月度午餐总是一场热闹的闲聊。|近义词：chat, gossip, chitchat, small talk|反义词：silence, quiet|词性：名词

词源：gab (n.) + fest (n.)

*   **gab (n.)**:  meaning "idle talk," dates back to the late 18th century. Its origin is uncertain, possibly related to earlier words meaning "mouth" or "to open the mouth."

*   **fest (n.)**:  short for "festival," comes from German *Fest*, meaning "feast, celebration."  It's often used as a combining form to indicate a gathering or celebration centered around a particular activity or theme (e.g., filmfest, songfest).
