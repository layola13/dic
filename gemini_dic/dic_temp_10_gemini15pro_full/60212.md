effluency: /ˈɛfluənsi/ | n. | 流畅; 雄辩;  | The smooth and effortless flow of something, especially speech or writing.  |  His effluency in French impressed everyone at the meeting. 他在会议上流利的法语给每个人都留下了深刻的印象。 |  She spoke with great effluency and passion. 她讲话非常流利，充满激情。 | 近义词: fluency, eloquence, articulateness | 反义词: hesitancy, faltering, inarticulateness | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Late Latin *effluentia, from Latin effluēns, present participle of effluere “to flow out,” from ex- “out” + fluere “to flow.”

PHRASE->

(It's unusual to use "effluency" in phrases.  It's more commonly used directly as a noun describing someone's skill.  However, here are some possible constructions:)

With great effluency  = 非常流利地
Demonstrate effluency in = 展示...方面的流利程度
Achieve effluency in =  达到...方面的流利程度
Maintain effluency in = 保持...方面的流利程度
Lack of effluency =  缺乏流利性


注意事项:

"Effluency" is less common than "fluency".  While both refer to smooth and easy expression, "fluency" is the more standard and widely accepted term.  "Effluency" might be considered slightly more formal or literary.
