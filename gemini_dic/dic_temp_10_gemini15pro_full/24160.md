brick-walled:/ˈbrɪk wɔːld/
adjective
砖墙的; 遇到阻碍的; 拒绝提供信息的
Made of brick walls; (figuratively) encountering an insurmountable obstacle or refusal to provide information.
The detective hit a brick wall in his investigation.  侦探的调查遇到了阻碍。
The company was brick-walled by the government's refusal to release the documents.  政府拒绝公布文件，公司碰壁了。
近义词: blocked, obstructed, stonewalled
反义词: cooperative, helpful, forthcoming
词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
"brick" (n.) Old English *brice, from Old French brique "piece of baked clay," from Frankish or some other West Germanic source akin to Old High German brihha "brick," related to break (v.). "wall" (n.) Old English weall "wall, rampart, dam," from Proto-Germanic *walluz (source also of Old Saxon wall, Old Norse veggr, Old Frisian wal, Middle Dutch wal, Dutch wal, Old High German wall, German Wall), from PIE root *wel- (3) "to turn, revolve, wind." Meaning "side of a building" is from late Old English.
