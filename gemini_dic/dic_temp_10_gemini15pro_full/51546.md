deuces:/ˈduːsɪz/ /ˈdjuːsɪz/| n. |二，一对二，再见，|A slang term primarily meaning "two," often used in the context of dice or cards. It can also be used as a casual farewell.|例句：He rolled deuces on the dice. 他掷骰子掷出了两个二。

例句："Deuces," he said as he walked out the door. “再见，”他说着走出了门。|近义词：twos, goodbye, later, peace out (slang) |反义词: N/A |词性:noun

deuces:/ˈduːsɪz/ /ˈdjuːsɪz/| interj. |再见|Used as a casual farewell, similar to "peace out."  Often implies a sense of finality or leaving a situation behind.|例句："Deuces, I'm outta here!" he exclaimed after winning the game. “拜拜，我走了！”他在赢得比赛后喊道。

例句："Deuces," she muttered as she hung up the phone after a frustrating conversation. “再见，”她在一次令人沮丧的谈话后挂断电话咕哝道。|近义词：goodbye, later, peace out, see ya |反义词: N/A |词性:interjection
