debilitating:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪŋ/| adj.|使人衰弱的；削弱的|Making someone or something physically weak.|The debilitating effects of the illness kept her bedridden for months.  这种疾病带来的衰弱让她卧床不起好几个月。|Synonyms: weakening, enervating, incapacitating, crippling|Antonyms: strengthening, invigorating, empowering|adjective


debilitate:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪt/| vt.|使衰弱；削弱|To make someone or something physically weak.|The stroke debilitated his left side. 中风使他的左侧身体变得虚弱。|Synonyms: weaken, enfeeble, sap, undermine|Antonyms: strengthen, invigorate, empower|verb

Etymology:
From Latin *dēbilis* "weak" (from *dē* "from, away from" + *habilis* "able, apt"),  + *-itāre*, frequentative suffix of *-itō*, frequentative of *-eō*,  meaning "to weaken repeatedly."
