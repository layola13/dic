claspered:/ˈklæspərd/| adj.|有抱器的，有螯状附肢的|Having claspers, which are modified pelvic fins in male sharks, skates, rays, and chimaeras, used for internal fertilization.|The male shark's claspers aid in reproduction. 雄性鲨鱼的抱器有助于繁殖。|近义词:prehensile|反义词: (No direct antonym, could be considered as "lacking claspers")|词性:adjective
Etymology: From clasp + -ed.  "Clasp" comes from Middle English claspe,  from Old Norse klǫspb, related to Dutch klaps and German Klaps.
