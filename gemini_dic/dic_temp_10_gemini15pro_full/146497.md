overgarrison:/ˌoʊvərˈɡærɪsən/
n.|过度驻军|The act of stationing too many troops in a particular location, making it vulnerable in other areas or placing an undue burden on the local resources.|The overgarrisoning of the small island strained its limited resources.|overdeployment, overmanning|undergarrison|noun
Etymology->From over- + garrison.
