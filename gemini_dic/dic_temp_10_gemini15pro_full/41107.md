coprincipals:/koʊˈprɪnsəpəlz/| n. |共同负责人，共同校长|Two or more people sharing a position of principal authority or responsibility.|Example: The coprincipals of the law firm shared the profits equally. 这家律师事务所的共同负责人平分利润。
Example: The coprincipals of the school worked together to implement new policies.  这所学校的共同校长共同努力实施新政策。|Synonyms: codirectors, joint heads, co-leaders|Antonyms: subordinate, assistant|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "co-" (together, joint) + "principal" (chief, main). "Principal" comes from Old French "principal" and ultimately from Latin "principalis" (chief, principal), derived from "princeps" (first, chief).
