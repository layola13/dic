detach:/dɪˈtætʃ/ /diˈtætʃ/ | v. | 分离，拆卸;  使摆脱;  派遣; | To separate (something) from something else; to become separated from something; to send (a group of soldiers, vehicles, etc.) away from a larger group on a special mission. |  The astronauts detached the lunar module from the command module. 宇航员将登月舱与指令舱分离。| synonyms: separate, disconnect, disengage, uncouple, remove, withdraw | antonyms: attach, connect, join, unite | verb

detach:/dɪˈtætʃ/ /diˈtætʃ/ | adj. |  分离的，超然的 | Emotionally removed; aloof. | He seemed strangely detached from the tragedy. 他似乎对这场悲剧异常冷漠。 | synonyms: aloof, remote, distant, withdrawn, indifferent | antonyms: involved, engaged, attached, connected | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "destacher," from "des-" (apart, away) + "tacher" (to attach).  Ultimately from Frankish "*stakka" (stick, peg).
