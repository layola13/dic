dizzier:/ˈdɪziər/ | adj. | 更晕眩的，更令人眼花缭乱的 | Comparative form of dizzy: more dizzy. Feeling as if everything is spinning around and that you are about to fall. | The higher we climbed, the dizzier I felt. 我们爬得越高，我就感觉越晕。| The rollercoaster made me feel dizzier than I expected.  过山车让我感觉比预期的更晕。 | 近义词: giddier, lightheaded, faint | 反义词: steady, stable | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Old English *dysig, from Germanic *dusigaz "foolish, stupid," related to *dus- "dead, insensible." The meaning shifted from "foolish" to "confused, bewildered" and then to the current sense of vertigo.
