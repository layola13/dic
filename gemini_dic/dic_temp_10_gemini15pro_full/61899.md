empty-headedness:/ˌɛmptiˈhɛdɪdnəs/| n. |愚蠢，轻浮，头脑空虚|The state of being empty-headed; lack of intelligence or good sense. |His empty-headedness caused him to forget his keys.| 近义词：silliness, foolishness, frivolity, thoughtlessness| 反义词：intelligence, wisdom, seriousness, thoughtfulness| 词性: 名词

词源:  From empty-headed + -ness.  Empty (from Old English *æmettig) meaning "containing nothing" + head (from Old English hēafod) referring to the mind or intellect.  The suffix -ness forms nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition.
