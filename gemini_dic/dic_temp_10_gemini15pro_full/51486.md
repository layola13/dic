detractation:/ˌdiːtrækˈteɪʃən/
noun
诋毁，贬低，诽谤
the act of taking away from or lessening the value or importance of something; disparagement.
Her constant detractions from his character made it difficult for anyone to take him seriously. 她不断地诋毁他的性格，使任何人都难以认真对待他。
synonyms: disparagement, denigration, defamation, slander, libel, calumny, vilification, backbiting, muckraking
antonyms: praise, commendation, acclaim, applause, adulation, exaltation

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dētractātiō, from Latin dētractāre (“to disparage”) + -tiō.  Ultimately from Latin trahere ("to draw, pull").  The prefix de- indicates removal or separation.  Therefore, the word literally means "a pulling down" or "a drawing away from."
