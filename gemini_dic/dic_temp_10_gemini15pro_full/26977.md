calciner:/ˈkælsɪnər/| n. |煅烧炉，焙烧炉|A furnace or kiln used for calcination, the process of heating a substance to a high temperature but below its melting point to drive off volatile components, or to cause a chemical or physical change.|The limestone is processed in the calciner to produce lime. 石灰石在煅烧炉中加工成石灰。|Synonyms: kiln, furnace, roaster|Antonyms: N/A|noun


calcine:/ˈkælsaɪn/| v. |煅烧，焙烧|To heat a substance to a high temperature but below its melting point to drive off volatile components, or to cause a chemical or physical change.|The bones were calcined to produce bone ash. 骨头被煅烧成骨灰。|Synonyms: roast, bake, burn|Antonyms: N/A|verb
