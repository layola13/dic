barkentine:/ˈbɑːrkənˌtiːn/
noun
三桅帆船；巴肯廷帆船
A sailing ship with three masts; a square-rigged foremast and fore-and-aft rigged main and mizzen masts.
The barkentine sailed gracefully into the harbor.  这艘三桅帆船优雅地驶入港口。
The old barkentine had seen better days, but she still held the sea well.  这艘老旧的三桅帆船已经过了它的辉煌时期，但它仍然在海上航行得很好。
near synonyms: barquentine, brigantine
antonyms: (No direct antonyms for a specific ship type.  Could consider general antonyms like "steamship" or "motor vessel" for contrasting propulsion methods)
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From barque + brigantine, blending the terms for two different types of sailing vessels.  "Barque" refers to a vessel with three or more masts, square-rigged on all but the aftermost mast.  "Brigantine" refers to a two-masted vessel, square-rigged on the foremast and fore-and-aft rigged on the mainmast.
