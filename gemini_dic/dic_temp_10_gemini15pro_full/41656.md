correlate:/ˈkɔːrəleɪt/ (US) /ˈkɒrəleɪt/ (UK)| v. n. adj.|关联，相关，相关物|To have a mutual relationship or connection, in which one thing affects or depends on another.  (v.) To establish such a relationship. (n.) Something that corresponds to or is related with something else. (adj.) Mutually related or connected.|
例句：
The study correlates smoking with lung cancer. 这项研究将吸烟与肺癌联系起来。

The rise in crime correlates with increased unemployment. 犯罪率的上升与失业率的增加相关。


近义词：correspond, relate, match, link, connect|
反义词：differ, diverge, contrast|
词性：verb, noun, adjective


correlate:/ˈkɔːrəleɪt/ (US) /ˈkɒrəleɪt/ (UK)| n.|相关物，相关联的人或事物|A person or thing that corresponds to or is related with something else.|
例句：
Stress levels and heart disease are often correlates. 压力水平和心脏病通常是相关的。

He found a correlate between poverty and crime rates. 他发现贫困与犯罪率之间存在关联。


近义词：equivalent, counterpart, parallel|
反义词：opposite, antithesis|
词性：noun



correlate:/ˈkɔːrəleɪt/ (US) /ˈkɒrəleɪt/ (UK)| adj.|相关的，关联的|Mutually related or connected.|
例句：
The two variables are strongly correlate. 这两个变量密切相关。

Correlate evidence supports the theory. 相关的证据支持该理论。


近义词：related, connected, corresponding|
反义词：unrelated, unconnected, dissimilar|
词性：adjective


词源：From the Late Latin "correlātus", past participle of "correlāre" ("to bring into mutual relation"), from "com-" (together) + "relātus" (related).
