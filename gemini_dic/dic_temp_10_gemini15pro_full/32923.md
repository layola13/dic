choosinesses:/ˈtʃuːzinɪsɪz/ | n. |挑剔, 讲究|The state of being choosy; fastidiousness in selection.| Her choosinesses about food made it difficult to find a restaurant that pleased her. 她对食物的挑剔使得很难找到一家让她满意的餐厅。| 近义词: fastidiousness, selectivity, discrimination | 反义词: indiscriminateness, acceptance | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  From "choosy" + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns expressing a state, quality, or condition).  "Choosy" itself comes from "choose" + "-y" (suffix forming adjectives).  "Choose" ultimately derives from the Old English *ċēosan.
