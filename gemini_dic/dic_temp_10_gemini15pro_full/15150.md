back-formation:/ˌbæk fɔːrˈmeɪʃən/
|n.|逆构词法|The creation of a new word (usually a verb) from an existing word (usually a noun) by removing a suffix or what looks like a suffix. For example, "edit" is a back-formation from "editor."|The word "donate" is a back-formation from "donation."  捐赠这个词是由捐献逆构而来的。|
|Etymology:  Late 19th century: from back- + formation.|
|Synonyms:  derivation, coinage, neologism|
|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a linguistic process)|
|n.|

PHRASE->
There is no commonly used phrase built around "back-formation" itself, as it's a linguistic term.  However, the *result* of back-formation (the new word created) can become part of phrases. For example:

Edit the document = 编辑文档
Donate to charity = 向慈善机构捐款
Lazing on the beach = 在沙滩上懒洋洋地躺着
Babysit the kids = 照顾孩子
House-sit for a friend = 帮朋友看房子
