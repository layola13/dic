clear-headedly:/ˌklɪrˈhɛdɪdli/
| adv.|头脑清醒地，清晰地|In a way that shows clear thinking; rationally.|She clear-headedly assessed the situation and made a quick decision. 她头脑清醒地评估了情况，并迅速做出了决定。|clearly, rationally, logically, lucidly|confusedly, irrationally, hazily|副词

Etymology:
clear (adj.) + -headed (suffix) + -ly (adverbial suffix)

"clear" comes from Old French "cler" meaning "bright, shining," ultimately from Latin "clarus."
"-headed" is a suffix meaning "having a head of a specified kind."
"-ly" is a common adverbial suffix.
