filibuster:/ˈfɪlɪbʌstər/| n. v.| 冗长演说；阻挠议事；海盗,掠夺者|A prolonged speech or series of speeches made to delay action on legislation.  To obstruct or delay legislative action by making long speeches.|The senator launched a filibuster to prevent a vote on the bill.  参议员发动了冗长演说以阻止对该法案的投票。|近义词：stonewall, obstruct, delay|反义词: expedite, facilitate|词性: noun, verb
filibuster:/ˈfɪlɪbʌstər/| v.|进行冗长演说以阻挠议事|To obstruct or delay legislative action by making long speeches.|Opponents of the bill threatened to filibuster. 该法案的反对者威胁要进行冗长演说以阻挠议事。|近义词：stonewall, obstruct, delay|反义词: expedite, facilitate|词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *filibustero*, "pirate, freebooter," from Dutch *vrijbuiter*, "freebooter," from *vrij*, "free" + *buiter*, "looter." Originally referred to pirates in the Caribbean, later applied to adventurers who attempted to incite revolutions in Central America in the mid-19th century.  The sense of delaying legislative action emerged in the mid-19th century in the United States.
