Orwell: /ˈɔːrwɛl/ | n. | 奥威尔 |  Primarily refers to George Orwell, the pen name of Eric Arthur Blair, a British novelist, essayist, journalist, and critic.  His work is marked by lucid prose, awareness of social injustice, opposition to totalitarianism, and support of democratic socialism.  It can also, by extension, refer to themes or concepts characteristic of his writing, such as dystopia, surveillance, and thought control. |  George Orwell's *Nineteen Eighty-Four* is a chilling depiction of a totalitarian state.  奥威尔的《一九八四》是对极权国家的令人不寒而栗的描述。

Orwellian: /ɔːrˈwɛliən/ | adj. | 奥威尔式的，形容词|  Resembling the totalitarian political and social climate depicted in George Orwell's novels, especially *Nineteen Eighty-Four*. Characterized by pervasive government surveillance, manipulation of language, and suppression of individual thought and freedom. | The government's new surveillance program felt distinctly Orwellian. 政府新的监控项目让人感觉明显具有奥威尔式的特点。 The constant rewriting of history by the ruling party was a truly Orwellian tactic. 执政党不断改写历史是一种真正的奥威尔式策略。 |  dystopian, totalitarian | utopian, libertarian | adjective
