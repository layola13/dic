mast:/mæst/ /mɑːst/| n.|桅杆；旗杆；天线杆；无线电杆；橡树果；大量；许多|A tall pole or spar on a ship or boat that supports the sails and rigging. A tall metal tower that supports an antenna for broadcasting or receiving radio waves. The nuts of forest trees (as beech and oak) accumulated on the ground and often serving as food for animals.|
The ship lost its mast in the storm. 这艘船在暴风雨中失去了桅杆。

The radio mast is located on the hill. 无线电桅杆位于山上。

Squirrels bury acorns for winter mast. 松鼠埋下橡子作为冬天的食物。|
近义词：pole, spar, tower, antenna, post|
反义词:None|
词性:noun


mast:/mæst/ /mɑːst/| v.|给…装桅杆；大量供应；用橡树果喂养|To furnish with a mast.  To feed with or as if with mast.|
They masted the ship before setting sail. 他们在起航前给船装上了桅杆。

The pigs were masted on acorns. 猪被橡子喂肥了。|
近义词：equip, rig, supply, provide|
反义词:None|
词性:verb
