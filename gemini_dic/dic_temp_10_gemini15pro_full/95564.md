incoherency:/ˌɪnkəˈhɪərənsi/ | n. | 不连贯，语无伦次 | the quality of being illogical, inconsistent, or unclear |  His speech was marked by long pauses and incoherency. 他的讲话以长时间的停顿和语无伦次为特征。| incoherence, disjointedness, rambling | coherence, clarity, lucidity | noun


incoherent:/ˌɪnkəˈhɪərənt/ | adj. | 不连贯的，语无伦次的 | expressed in a confusing or illogical way; unable to express yourself clearly | He became incoherent with rage. 他愤怒得语无伦次。| disjointed, rambling, illogical | coherent, articulate, lucid | adjective


Etymology:
From Late Latin *incohaerentia, from Latin in- (“not”) + cohaerentia (“sticking together,” “coherence”), from cohaerēns (“sticking together”), present participle of cohaerēre (“to stick together”), from co- (“together”) + haerēre (“to stick”).
