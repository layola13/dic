menstruum:/ˈmenstruːəm/| n. |溶剂; 月经; (炼金术士用语) 溶媒|A substance, especially a liquid, that dissolves another substance to form a solution.  (Often used in archaic or alchemical contexts.)|The alchemist searched for the perfect menstruum to dissolve gold. 炼金术士寻找完美的溶剂来溶解黄金。|solvent, dissolvent, medium|N/A|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *menstruum*, from Latin *menstruus* (“monthly”), from *mensis* (“month”).  Originally referring to the belief that menstrual blood had solvent properties. Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *mḗh₁n̥s- (“moon, month”).
