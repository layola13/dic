geminately:/ˈdʒɛmɪnɪtli/| adv.|成对地，双双地|Characterized by twins or occurring in pairs; doubled.  Derived from the Latin word "geminus," meaning "twin."|The crystals were arranged geminately, reflecting light in a dazzling display. 晶体成对排列，反射出耀眼的光芒。|The two butterflies flew geminately, their wings mirroring each other's movements. 两只蝴蝶双双飞舞，它们的翅膀互相映照着对方的动作。|Synonym: paired, doubled, coupled|Antonym: singly, individually|adverb

geminate:/ˈdʒɛmɪneɪt/| adj. v.|成对的，双生的；使成双|
1. (adj.) Arranged in pairs; doubled.
2. (v.) To double; to arrange in pairs.
|1. (adj.) The geminate flowers bloomed on a single stem. 成对的花朵开在一根茎上。
2. (v.) The artist geminated the image to create a symmetrical design. 艺术家将图像复制成双，以创造一个对称的设计。|Synonym: paired, doubled, twinned (adj.); double, pair (v.)|Antonym: single, solitary (adj.); separate (v.)|adjective, verb
