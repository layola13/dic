imperativism:/ɪmˈpɛrətɪvɪzəm/| n.|命令主义，指令主义|The doctrine that moral statements are commands or rules.  It emphasizes the importance of commands, directives, and rules in moral decision-making and action. It can also refer to a tendency to favor direct action and giving orders.|His imperativism often alienated his colleagues. 他的命令主义常常疏远他的同事们。|近义词：authoritarianism,  directivism|反义词：libertarianism, permissiveness|Noun
