dibasicity:/ˌdaɪbeɪˈsɪsɪti/
n.
二元性; 二碱性
The property of a chemical compound, particularly an acid, of having two hydrogen atoms that can be replaced by metal atoms to form salts.
Sulfuric acid exhibits dibasicity because it can donate two protons. 硫酸表现出二元性，因为它可以提供两个质子。
The dibasicity of the acid allows it to form two different salts.  这种酸的二元性使其能够形成两种不同的盐。
synonym: diprotic, diacidic
antonym: monobasicity, tribasicity
noun
