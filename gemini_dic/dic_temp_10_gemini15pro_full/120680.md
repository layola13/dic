misadjust:/ˌmɪsəˈdʒʌst/
verb
调整不当；校准错误
to adjust wrongly or improperly.
The mechanic misadjusted the carburetor, causing the engine to stall. 技工把化油器调错了，导致发动机熄火。

misadjust:/ˌmɪsəˈdʒʌst/
adjective
调整不当的；校准错误的
badly or improperly adjusted.
The misadjusted brakes made the car difficult to control.  调整不当的刹车使汽车难以控制。


ETYMOLOGY->
mis- (prefix):  Old English *mis-, from Proto-Germanic *missa- "wrongly, astray,"  related to Old Norse mis-, Old High German missi-, Gothic missa-.
adjust (verb): from Old French ajuster "to arrange, bring together," from à "to" (from Latin ad) + juste "just, right," from Latin iustus.

SYNONYMS-> (verb) mishandle, mismanage, fumble, bungle
SYNONYMS-> (adjective) maladjusted, faulty, defective, improper
ANTONYMS-> (verb) adjust, fine-tune, calibrate, regulate
ANTONYMS-> (adjective) adjusted, well-adjusted, proper, correct
