halloa:/həˈloʊ/ /hɑːˈloʊ/ | int. | 喂；你好 | An exclamation used to attract attention or express greeting. |Halloa! Is anybody there? 喂！有人在吗？|Hi, hello, hey | Goodbye, farewell | Interjection

halloa:/həˈloʊ/ /hɑːˈloʊ/ | n. | 呼喊；喊声 | A loud cry or shout. | We heard a halloa from the other side of the valley. 我们听到山谷另一边传来一声呼喊。| Shout, cry, yell | Whisper, murmur | Noun

halloa:/həˈloʊ/ /hɑːˈloʊ/ | vt. | 呼喊；招呼 | To greet or call out to someone. | He halloaed to his friend across the street. 他向街对面的朋友打招呼。| Call, shout, hail | Whisper | Verb

Etymology:
From Middle English *halloen, *halowen, from Anglo-Norman halloer, haler, haloer, of Germanic origin.  Related to Old High German halōn, halōn (“to fetch, call”), Middle Dutch halloen (“to halloo”), Dutch halen (“to fetch, pull, drag”). More at hale.
