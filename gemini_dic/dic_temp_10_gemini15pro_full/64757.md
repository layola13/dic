escuage:/ˈɛskjuɪdʒ/| n.|盾牌钱，免役税|A monetary payment made in lieu of military service, especially during the feudal era.  It was a form of taxation levied by a lord on his vassals in exchange for exemption from their obligation to provide knight service.|The king levied escuage on his barons who preferred to pay rather than fight. 国王向他的男爵们征收免役税，他们宁愿交钱也不愿打仗。|Synonyms: scutage, shield money|Antonyms: military service|noun
