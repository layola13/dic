fancifulnesses:/ˈfænsifʊlnəsɪz/
n.
奇思妙想；想象力丰富；异想天开
The state or quality of being fanciful; a tendency to indulge in fantasy or whimsical ideas.
Her writing is full of fancifulnesses, creating a world of magical creatures and impossible adventures. 她的作品充满了奇思妙想，创造了一个充满神奇生物和不可能的冒险的世界。
近义词：imaginativeness, creativity, whimsy, fantasticalness
反义词：practicality, realism, dullness, mundanity
词性: 名词

fanciful:/ˈfænsifʊl/
adj.
充满幻想的；异想天开的；不切实际的
Characterized by or showing imagination or invention rather than based on reality.
He told a fanciful tale of dragons and unicorns. 他讲述了一个关于龙和独角兽的奇幻故事。
近义词：imaginative, whimsical, fantastic, unreal
反义词：realistic, practical, down-to-earth, mundane
词性: 形容词


词源:
From fancy (n.) + -ful + -ness.  "Fancy" itself traces back to Old French "fantasie," ultimately from Greek "phantasia" meaning "appearance," "imagination."
