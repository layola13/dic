hobbit:/ˈhɒbɪt/ (UK) /ˈhɑːbɪt/ (US)| n.|霍比特人|A member of an imaginary race of small people, similar to humans but shorter and with hairy feet, who live in comfortable underground houses and are fond of good food and drink.  Originally created by J.R.R. Tolkien.|Bilbo Baggins is a famous hobbit. 比尔博·巴金斯是一位著名的霍比特人。|The hobbits enjoyed a second breakfast. 霍比特人享用了第二顿早餐。|近义词:dwarf, halfling|反义词:giant|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
Coined by J.R.R. Tolkien, author of "The Hobbit" and "The Lord of the Rings". The exact origin is uncertain, but Tolkien himself suggested a possible connection to *hol-bytla* (hole-dweller) in Old English.
