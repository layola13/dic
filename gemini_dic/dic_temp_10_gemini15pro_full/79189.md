glamorous: /ˈɡlæmərəs/ (US) /ˈɡlæmərəs/ (UK)| adj.| 迷人的，富有魅力的，光彩夺目的| Attractive in an exciting and special way.|例句：She led a glamorous life. 她过着光鲜亮丽的生活。|例句：Hollywood is known for its glamorous parties. 好莱坞以其魅力四射的派对而闻名。|近义词：alluring, charming, fascinating, attractive, stylish, elegant|反义词：unglamorous, dull, plain, ordinary|词性:形容词
glamour: /ˈɡlæmər/ (US) /ˈɡlæmər/ (UK)| n.| 魅力，魔力，光彩| An attractive or exciting quality that makes certain people or things seem special.|例句：The city has lost its glamour. 这座城市失去了它的魅力。|例句：She added a touch of glamour to the occasion. 她为这个场合增添了一抹魅力。|近义词：allure, charm, fascination, magic, enchantment|反义词：dullness, ordinariness|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Scottish *glamour*, alteration of *grammar*. Originally, in Scotland, *glamour* meant a magic spell, especially one causing people to see things differently from how they actually are.  The current meaning, a more general sense of allure and fascination, developed later.
