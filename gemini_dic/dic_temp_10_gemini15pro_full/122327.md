mix-ups:/ˈmɪks ˌʌps/ | n. |混乱，错误，混淆|A situation that is confused and full of mistakes, or a mistake in which one thing is mistaken for another.| There were a few mix-ups with the bookings, but everything was sorted out in the end.  预订方面有一些混乱，但最终都解决了。 | 近义词: muddles, confusions, errors, blunders | 反义词: order, organization | 词性: 名词

mix-up:/ˈmɪks ʌp/ | n. |混乱，错误，混淆|A situation that is confused and full of mistakes, or a mistake in which one thing is mistaken for another.| There was a mix-up with our hotel reservation. 我们的酒店预订出了点差错。 | 近义词: muddle, confusion, error, blunder | 反义词: order, organization | 词性: 名词


词源:  From the phrasal verb "mix up," meaning to confuse or to make a mistake. "Mix" comes from Old English "miscian," and "up" from Old English "up" or "uppe."
