embolus: /ˈɛmbələs/ (US), /ˈɛmbələs/ (UK)| n. |栓子; 梗塞|A blood clot, air bubble, piece of fatty deposit, or other object which has been carried in the bloodstream to lodge in a vessel and cause an embolism. |The embolus blocked a major artery in his brain. 栓子阻塞了他大脑中的一条主要动脉。

The doctor identified the embolus as a piece of plaque that had broken off from an artery wall. 医生确定栓子是一块从动脉壁脱落的斑块。|近义词：clot, plug|反义词: N/A|词性: Noun



词源: From Modern Latin embolus, from Greek embolos "stopper, plug," from en- "in" + ballein "to throw."
