consciousnesses:/ˈkɒnʃəsnɪsɪz/ /ˈkɑːnʃəsnɪsɪz/
|n.|意识，知觉，觉悟|The state of being aware of and responsive to one's surroundings.|He slowly regained consciousness. 他慢慢恢复了意识。|Synonyms: awareness, sentience, alertness|Antonyms: unconsciousness, unawareness|noun
consciousnesses:|/ˈkɒnʃəsnɪsɪz/ /ˈkɑːnʃəsnɪsɪz/
|n.|各种意识|Different states or types of awareness.|Their consciousnesses merged into one. 他们的意识融合成一体。|Synonyms: minds, perceptions, sensibilities|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English consciensnes, from consciens +‎ -nes, from Anglo-Norman conscience, from Latin conscientia (“knowledge within oneself, consciousness”), from conscient- (“being privy to”), present participle of conscire (“to be conscious, to know”), from com- (“with”) + scire (“to know”).
