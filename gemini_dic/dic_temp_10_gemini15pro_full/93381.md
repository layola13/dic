illiberalize: /ɪˈlɪbərəlaɪz/
|v. |使不自由；限制；变得不自由|To make less liberal; to restrict freedoms or rights.|The government's new policies seem designed to illiberalize the press. 政府的新政策似乎旨在限制新闻自由。|The new regime illiberalized the country, restricting freedom of speech and assembly. 新政权使国家变得不自由，限制了言论和集会自由。|近义词: restrict, repress, constrain, limit|反义词: liberalize, free, emancipate|词性:动词

illiberal: /ɪˈlɪbərəl/
|adj.|不自由的；狭隘的；偏执的|Not liberal; opposed to liberal principles; narrow-minded; bigoted.|His illiberal views on immigration are offensive. 他在移民问题上狭隘的观点令人反感。|The illiberal regime suppressed dissent and curtailed civil liberties.  这个不自由的政权压制异见，剥夺公民自由。|近义词: narrow-minded, intolerant, bigoted, prejudiced|反义词: liberal, tolerant, open-minded, progressive|词性:形容词

词源:  "illiberalize" comes from the prefix "il-" (meaning "not") added to "liberalize," which itself comes from "liberal" (meaning "favorable to progress or reform; believing in maximum individual freedom").  Ultimately, the roots trace back to the Latin "liber" (meaning "free").
