oversight:/ˈoʊvərˌsaɪt/| n.|疏忽，监管，监督|An unintentional failure to notice or do something.  The act of overseeing something.|例句：The error was due to an oversight in the editing process. 这个错误是由于编辑过程中的疏忽造成的。

例句：The committee has oversight of the project. 委员会负责监督这个项目。|近义词：supervision, inspection, management, control|反义词：neglect, inattention|词性:noun

oversight:/ˈoʊvərˌsaɪt/| n.|失察，疏漏|A mistake resulting from inattention. A failure to notice or consider something.|例句：By an oversight, his name was omitted from the list. 由于疏忽，他的名字被从名单中遗漏了。

例句：It was an oversight on my part. 这是我的失察。|近义词：lapse, blunder, mistake, error|反义词：carefulness, attention|词性:noun

词源：From over + sight.  "Over" implies a position above or encompassing, while "sight" refers to the act of seeing or observing.  Therefore, "oversight" originally meant "watching over" or "supervising."  The meaning of "unintentional failure to notice" developed later.
