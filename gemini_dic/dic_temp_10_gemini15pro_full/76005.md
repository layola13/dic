furbishable:/ˈfɜːrbɪʃəbl/
adj.
可翻新的，可整修的
able to be furbished (cleaned, polished, or repaired).
The old house is furbishable, but it will take a lot of work. 这栋老房子可以翻新，但这需要很多工作。
近义词:repairable, restorable, renovable
反义词:irreparable, beyond repair
词性:形容词

furbishable:/ˈfɜːrbɪʃəbl/
词源:From furbish + -able.  "Furbish" comes from Old French "fourbiss-" (past participle stem of fourbir), meaning "to polish," ultimately from Frankish or another Germanic source.
英文解析:Capable of being furbished; that may be brightened, renovated, or made to look new again.  Essentially means something can be improved or restored to a better condition, especially in appearance.
例句：The antique furniture was furbishable and, with a little work, looked brand new.  这件古董家具可以翻新，稍加修饰就焕然一新。
例句：Although worn, the leather boots were furbishable and could be restored to their former glory. 虽然磨损了，但这双皮靴可以翻新，可以恢复昔日的辉煌。
近义词:restorable, renovable, repairable, refreshable
反义词:irreparable, dilapidated, ruined, broken
词性:形容词
