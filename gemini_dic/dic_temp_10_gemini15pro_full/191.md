Abbasids: /əˈbæsɪdz/ | n. | 阿巴斯王朝 |  A dynasty of caliphs who ruled much of the Islamic Empire from 750 to 1258 CE. They overthrew the Umayyad dynasty and moved the capital from Damascus to Baghdad. | The Abbasids presided over a golden age of Islamic scholarship.  阿巴斯王朝引领了伊斯兰学术的黄金时代。

The Abbasid caliphate was eventually weakened by internal strife and external pressures. 阿巴斯哈里发最终被内部冲突和外部压力削弱了。 | 近义词:Caliphate (in some contexts) | 反义词: Umayyads | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Arabic *`al-‘Abbāsīyūn`*, meaning "descendants of al-Abbas," referring to al-‘Abbās ibn ‘Abd al-Muttalib, the uncle of the Prophet Muhammad.
