miscoordinate:/ˌmɪskɔːˈɔːrdɪneɪt/
|v.|协调失调，配合不当|To fail to coordinate properly; to move or function in a way that lacks proper coordination.|The dancer's arms and legs seemed to miscoordinate, making her movements awkward.  舞者的胳膊和腿似乎配合不当，使她的动作显得笨拙。|mismanage, misdirect|coordinate, synchronize|verb


miscoordinate:/ˌmɪskɔːˈɔːrdɪneɪtɪd/
|adj.|不协调的，配合不良的|Lacking proper coordination; not functioning together smoothly.|Her miscoordinated movements made it difficult for her to walk in a straight line. 她不协调的动作使她难以走直线。|clumsy, awkward|coordinated, synchronized|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
mis- (prefix): from Old English and Old French, meaning "wrongly," "badly," or "not."
coordinate (verb): from Late Latin coordinatus, past participle of coordinare "to arrange in order," from com- "together" + ordinare "to arrange," from Latin ordo "order."
