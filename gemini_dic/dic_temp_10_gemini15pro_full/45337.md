cumulating:/ˈkjuːmjəleɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 累积的，渐增的 | Increasing or growing by successive additions. | The cumulating evidence suggests he is guilty.  越来越多的证据表明他有罪。| Synonyms: accumulating, gathering, increasing, mounting | Antonyms: decreasing, diminishing, lessening | Adjective


cumulate:/ˈkjuːmjəleɪt/ | v. | 累积，积聚 |  To gather or become greater in quantity or amount; to amass. |  The snow cumulated quickly during the blizzard. 暴雪期间，雪很快积了起来。| Synonyms: accumulate, amass, gather, accrue | Antonyms: disperse, dissipate, scatter | Verb


cumulation:/ˌkjuːmjəˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 累积，积聚 | The act or process of cumulating; the state of being cumulated. | The cumulation of stress led to a breakdown.  压力的累积导致了崩溃。| Synonyms: accumulation, aggregation, accretion | Antonyms: decrease, reduction, diminution | Noun


Etymology:
From Latin "cumulare" (to heap up), from "cumulus" (heap).
