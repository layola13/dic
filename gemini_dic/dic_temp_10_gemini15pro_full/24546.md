broadsheet:/ˈbrɔːdʃiːt/
n.
大报；单张印刷品；大幅纸张
a newspaper printed on large sheets of paper, quality newspapers are generally considered to be broadsheets
例句：The broadsheet covered the political scandal in detail. 这份大报详细报道了这起政治丑闻。

例句：He used a broadsheet to announce the upcoming town meeting. 他用一张大幅纸张宣布即将举行的镇民大会。
近义词：newspaper, journal, paper
反义词：tabloid
词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "broad" (wide) + "sheet" (of paper).  Originally referred to single sheets of paper printed on one side, often containing proclamations or ballads.  Later came to refer to newspapers printed on large-format paper.
