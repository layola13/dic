decretorily:/dɪˈkrɛtərɪli/| adv.|断然地，专横地|In a manner that expresses a final decision or authoritative command, often without allowing for discussion or objection.  It implies a tone and manner that is peremptory and assertive.|He decretorily announced his resignation, leaving no room for discussion. 他断然宣布辞职，不容置疑。|近义词:peremptorily, authoritatively, dictatorially, dogmatically|反义词:hesitantly, tentatively, democratically|词性:副词

decretory:/dɪˈkriːtəri/| adj.|决定性的，法令的|Having the force of or pertaining to a decree; definitive and authoritative.|The judge's decision was decretory and could not be appealed. 法官的裁决是决定性的，不能上诉。|近义词:definitive, conclusive, authoritative, decisive|反义词:provisional, tentative, indecisive|词性:形容词

词源:
From Late Latin *decretorius, from Latin decretus (past participle of decernere ‘to decide, decree’), from de- ‘down, completely’ + cernere ‘to separate, distinguish, decide’.
