hebraistically:/ˌhiːbreɪˈɪstɪkli/| adv.| 以希伯来语的方式；根据希伯来语的习惯|In English, the word "hebraistically" means "in a Hebrew way" or "according to the idiom of the Hebrew language." It refers to the adoption of Hebrew expressions, structures, or thought patterns in another language, often English. This adoption might be conscious or unconscious, reflecting an influence from Hebrew culture, literature, or religious texts.|He spoke hebraistically, using metaphors and imagery drawn from the Old Testament.|Synonym: Hebraically|Antonym: Hellenistically|Adverb
