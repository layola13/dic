coached:/koʊtʃt/| v.|辅导，指导，训练|To train or instruct someone in a particular skill or activity.  To prepare someone for a difficult situation or task, often by advising them on what to say or do.|The experienced manager coached the new employee on how to handle difficult clients. 经验丰富的经理指导新员工如何处理难缠的客户。|trained, tutored, mentored| neglected, ignored|verb
coach:/koʊtʃ/| n.|教练，辅导员，长途公共汽车|A person who trains or instructs someone in a particular skill or activity. A long-distance bus.|The basketball coach led his team to victory. 篮球教练带领他的球队取得了胜利。|trainer, instructor, tutor|student, pupil|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Hungarian "kocsi," meaning "carriage" or "coach," named after the town of Kocs where such carriages were first made.  The word later evolved to mean not only the vehicle but also the driver, and eventually, anyone who instructs or guides others.
