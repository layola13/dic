dichotomize: /daɪˈkɑːtəmaɪz/ | v. | 二分，分成两部分 | To divide something into two parts, often seen as mutually exclusive or opposite. |  Researchers dichotomized the participants into two groups: those who exercised regularly and those who did not.  研究人员将参与者分成两组：经常锻炼的人和不经常锻炼的人。

The survey dichotomized responses into "agree" and "disagree," ignoring any nuance.  该调查将回答分为“同意”和“不同意”两类，忽略了任何细微差别。

| 近义词: divide, split, bifurcate | 反义词: unite, combine, merge | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *dikhotomia*, from *dikha* "in two, asunder" + *temnein* "to cut".
