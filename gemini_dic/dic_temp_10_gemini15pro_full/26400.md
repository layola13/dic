byard:/ˈbaɪərd/| n.|畜栏，围场|An enclosure for livestock, typically one near a farmhouse.|The farmer kept his sheep in the byard overnight.|近义词：pen, enclosure, corral, fold|反义词: open field|词性:noun
Etymology: From Middle English *biyard*, from Old English *bēo-geard* ("bee-yard, apiary"), from *bēo* ("bee") + *geard* ("yard, enclosure").  The meaning shifted from a bee enclosure to a general livestock enclosure.
