napierian:/neɪˈpɪəriən/
adj.
纳皮尔的，自然对数的
Relating to or involving logarithms to the base *e* (also called natural logarithms), especially as used in the calculation of powers or roots.
The napierian logarithm of a number x is the power to which e would have to be raised to equal x.
近义词: natural, logarithmic
反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider "common" for logarithms with base 10)
词性: adjective


Etymology:
Named after John Napier (1550–1617), a Scottish mathematician who invented logarithms.
