chock:/tʃɑːk/ (US), /tʃɒk/ (UK)| n. vt.|楔子，垫块，阻碍物；塞紧，楔住|A block or wedge placed against a wheel or other rolling object to prevent it from moving.  Also, the act of wedging something.|
例句：
He put a chock under the wheel to stop the car from rolling. 他在车轮下放了一个楔子，防止车子滚动。

例句：
Chock the wheels before you jack up the car. 在你用千斤顶顶起汽车之前，先用楔子塞住车轮。

近义词：wedge, block, prop, support|
反义词:release, loosen|
词性:noun, verb


chock-full:/ˌtʃɑːkˈfʊl/ (US), /ˌtʃɒkˈfʊl/ (UK)| adj.|塞满的，充满的|Completely full; crammed.|
例句：
The room was chock-full of people. 房间里挤满了人。

例句：
The pantry was chock-full of food for the holidays. 食品储藏室里塞满了节日食物。

近义词：full, crammed, packed, overflowing|
反义词:empty, vacant|
词性:adjective
