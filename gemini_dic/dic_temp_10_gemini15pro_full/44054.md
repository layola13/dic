cringing:/ˈkrɪndʒɪŋ/| adj. v.|畏缩的，尴尬的，奉承的|Shrinking or recoiling, especially in fear or servility.  Experiencing an inward feeling of embarrassment or awkwardness.|例句：I was cringing at his terrible jokes. 我被他糟糕的笑话弄得很尴尬。|近义词：wincing, flinching, recoiling, fawning|反义词:confident, bold|verb (present participle)

cringe:/krɪndʒ/| v. n.|畏缩，感到尴尬，奉承|To shrink or recoil, especially in fear or servility. To experience an inward feeling of embarrassment or awkwardness.|例句：He cringed at the sound of his own voice. 他听到自己的声音感到很尴尬。|近义词：wince, flinch, recoil, fawn|反义词:boast, brag|verb, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cringan "to bend, yield, fall in battle," from Proto-Germanic *kringaną "to bend, turn, twist" (source also of Old Norse kringr "circle," Old High German chranc "bent, crooked"). The sense of "to shrink in fear" developed in Middle English.
