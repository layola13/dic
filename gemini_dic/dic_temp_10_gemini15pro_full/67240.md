expulse:/ɪkˈspʌls/ /ɪkˈspəls/| vt.|驱逐，逐出，排出|To force someone or something to leave a place or organization.|The government expelled the foreign journalists. 政府驱逐了外国记者。|近义词：eject, banish, oust, deport|反义词:admit, welcome|verb

expulse:/ɪkˈspʌls/ /ɪkˈspəls/| n.|驱逐，逐出|The act of expelling someone or something.|The expulse of the diplomats caused an international incident.  外交官的驱逐引发了一场国际事件。|近义词：ejection, banishment, ouster, deportation|反义词:admission, welcome|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin expulsus, past participle of expellere "to drive out," from ex- "out" + pellere "to drive, push."
