indenture:/ɪnˈdɛntʃər/| n.|契约，合同；正式凭证；学徒期|A formal legal agreement, contract, or document.  Historically, it often involved serrated edges that were torn apart to create matching halves as proof of authenticity.|The apprentice signed an indenture binding him to his master for seven years.|近义词：contract, agreement, covenant, deed|反义词：N/A|名词

indenture:/ɪnˈdɛntʃər/| vt.|以契约约束；签订契约|Bind by an indenture (as an apprentice).|He was indentured to a carpenter.|近义词：bind, contract, apprentice|反义词：release, free|动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *endenture (Modern French denture), from Latin indentura (“notch, tooth”), from dens, dentis (“tooth”).  The word originally referred to the practice of cutting a document with jagged edges, so that the two halves could be fitted together to verify authenticity.
