legislative: /ˈledʒɪslətɪv/ (US) /ˈledʒɪslətɪv/ (UK)| adj.| 立法的；立法机关的| Relating to the process of making laws or the body that makes them.|  The legislative branch of government is responsible for making laws. 政府的立法部门负责制定法律。|  The legislative process can be long and complex. 立法过程可能漫长而复杂。|  Synonyms: lawmaking, statutory | Antonyms: executive, judicial | adjective

legislature: /ˈledʒɪsleɪtʃər/ (US) /ˈledʒɪsleɪtʃə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 立法机关；立法机构| The body of government responsible for making laws.| The state legislature passed a new budget. 州立法机关通过了新的预算。|  The legislature is debating a controversial new bill.  立法机关正在辩论一项有争议的新法案。 | Synonyms: parliament, congress, assembly | Antonyms:  executive, judiciary | noun


legislator: /ˈledʒɪsleɪtər/ (US) /ˈledʒɪsleɪtər/ (UK)| n.| 立法者；议员| A member of a legislature.|  The legislators voted in favor of the bill. 立法者投票赞成该法案。|  She is a respected legislator known for her integrity. 她是一位受人尊敬的立法者，以其正直而闻名。 | Synonyms: lawmaker, congressman, parliamentarian | Antonyms: constituent, citizen | noun


**Etymology (词源):**

From Late Middle English: from Old French *legislatif, or from medieval Latin legislativus, from Latin legis, stem of lex ‘law’ + -lativus (from lat- ‘carry, bear’).
