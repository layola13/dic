parageosyncline:/ˌpærəˌdʒiːoʊˈsɪŋklaɪn/ | n. | 副地槽 | A long, relatively narrow and shallow depression of the earth's crust, considered to be a precursor to a geosyncline. It is characterized by accumulating sediments and volcanic rocks. | The parageosyncline accumulated thick layers of volcanic ash. 副地槽积累了厚厚的火山灰层。 | The sediments in the parageosyncline eventually formed folded mountains. 副地槽中的沉积物最终形成了褶皱山脉。 | Synonym: back-arc basin | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, possibly horst or platform) | noun
