discouraged:/dɪˈskʌrɪdʒd/
adjective
沮丧的，灰心的
Feeling or showing a loss of enthusiasm and hope.
She felt discouraged after failing the exam. 她考试不及格后感到很沮丧。

discouraged:/dɪˈskʌrɪdʒd/
verb (past participle)
使灰心，使沮丧
Past participle of "discourage," meaning to cause (someone) to lose confidence or enthusiasm; deter.
He was discouraged by the lack of progress.  他没有取得进展，感到很灰心。

discouraged:/dɪˈskʌrɪdʒd/
adjective
气馁的
Having lost confidence or enthusiasm; disheartened.
The team was discouraged by their recent losses.  球队最近的失利让他们很气馁。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "descoragier," from "des-" (expressing negation) + "corage" (courage, heart). Ultimately from Latin "cor" (heart).

SYNONYMS->
dejected, disheartened, demoralized, dispirited, downhearted, disappointed

ANTONYMS->
encouraged, hopeful, optimistic, motivated, inspired
