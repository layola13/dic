archosaurs:/ˈɑːrkəˌsɔːrz/| n.|主龙类|A group of diapsid amniotes whose living representatives are birds and crocodilians.  Archosaurs are characterized by features such as antorbital fenestrae (openings in the skull in front of the eyes) and mandibular fenestrae (openings in the lower jaw).|Crocodiles and birds are archosaurs.  鳄鱼和鸟类都是主龙类。|synonyms: archosaurian reptiles|antonyms: (no direct antonym, could use non-archosaurian reptiles)|noun
