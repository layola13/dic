mishappy:/mɪsˈhæpi/ | adj. | 不高兴的，不幸的 |  Archaic. Unhappy; wretched.  Formed with the prefix "mis-" indicating "badly" or "wrongly," combined with "happy."  While technically correct in its formation, it's considered outdated and rarely used in modern English. |  He felt mishappy about the turn of events. 他对事态的发展感到不高兴。 |  unhappy, sad, miserable, wretched | happy, joyful, cheerful | adjective
