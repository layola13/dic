fumitory:/ˈfjuːmɪtɔːri/ (US) /ˈfjuːmɪtri/ (UK)| n. |延胡索，紫堇|A delicate European plant (Fumaria officinalis) with purplish flowers and finely divided leaves, formerly used in herbal medicine.  It is considered a weed in some areas.|The farmer tried to eradicate the fumitory from his fields.|近义词：earth smoke, fumewort|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Old French *fumoterre (Modern French fumeterre), literally "smoke of the earth," perhaps because the plant's juice causes the eyes to water, or from the appearance of the foliage emerging from the ground.  Ultimately from Latin fumus ("smoke") + terra ("earth").
