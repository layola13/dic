catalyst:/ˈkætəlɪst/| n.|催化剂；促进因素|A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change.  A person or thing that precipitates an event.|例句：The enzyme acts as a catalyst in the reaction.  这种酶在反应中充当催化剂。

例句：The assassination of the archduke was the catalyst for the war.  大公遇刺是战争的导火索。|近义词：stimulus, impetus, trigger|反义词:inhibitor, deterrent|词性:noun
catalysts:/ˈkætəlɪsts/| n.|催化剂；促进因素（catalyst的复数形式）|The plural form of catalyst, referring to multiple substances or agents that accelerate a process without being consumed themselves.|例句：These enzymes are important catalysts in the digestive process. 这些酶是消化过程中重要的催化剂。

例句：Social unrest and economic hardship were the catalysts for revolution. 社会动荡和经济困难是革命的催化剂。|近义词：stimuli, impetuses, triggers|反义词:inhibitors, deterrents|词性:noun
