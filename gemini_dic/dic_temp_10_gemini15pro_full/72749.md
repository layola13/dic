fogeyism:/ˈfoʊɡiɪzəm/| n. |守旧主义，老古董思想|The tendency to be resistant to change and new ideas, often associated with older people.  A clinging to old-fashioned ways and ideas.|His fogeyism prevented him from appreciating the new music.|近义词：conservatism, traditionalism, old-fashionedness, stodginess|反义词:modernism, progressivism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From fogey (meaning an old-fashioned person) + -ism (suffix forming abstract nouns).  Fogey likely originated in the 18th century, possibly related to the word "fogyish," meaning dull or sluggish.
