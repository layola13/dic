married: /ˈmærɪd/ (US) /ˈmærid/ (UK)
| adj. | 已婚的，结婚的 | Being in a state of matrimony; having a husband or wife. |  She is married to a doctor. 她嫁给了一位医生。| married couple, wedded, united | single, unmarried, divorced | adjective

married: /ˈmærɪd/ (US) /ˈmærid/ (UK)
| v. | 结婚，嫁，娶 | To become husband and wife; to perform the ceremony of marriage. | The priest married them in a small chapel. 牧师在一个小教堂里为他们主持了婚礼。| wed, espouse | divorce, separate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *maried*, *married*, past participle of *marien, marrien* (“to marry”), from Old French *marier*, from Late Latin *maritāre* (“to marry, wed, give in marriage”), from Latin *marītus* (“married, marital, matrimonial; husband”), from *mas, maris* (“male, man”) + *-tus* (adjective suffix).

They are getting married next spring. 他们明年春天结婚。 (Note: "Getting married" is more common than "Being married" in the present continuous tense, as "being married" describes a state, not an action in progress.)
