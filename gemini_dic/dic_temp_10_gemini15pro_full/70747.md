fine:/faɪn/| adj.|好的，美好的；健康的；细致的；晴朗的|Of high quality; excellent.  In good health; well. Consisting of small particles.  (of weather) bright and clear.|例句：The wine is very fine.  这葡萄酒非常好。

例句：I feel fine today. 我今天感觉很好。

近义词：excellent, good, well, healthy, delicate, clear, sunny|反义词：bad, ill, poor, coarse, cloudy, stormy|词性:形容词

fine:/faɪn/| n.|罚款|A sum of money exacted as a penalty for an offense or dereliction.|例句：I had to pay a fine for parking illegally. 我因为违规停车不得不支付罚款。

例句：The court imposed a heavy fine on the company. 法院对该公司处以巨额罚款。

近义词：penalty, sanction|反义词：reward|词性:名词

fine:/faɪn/| v.|处以罚金|Punish by a fine.|例句：The judge fined him $500. 法官罚了他500美元。

例句：They were fined for speeding. 他们因超速行驶被罚款。

近义词：penalize, sanction|反义词：reward|词性:动词


词源：From Old French *fin, from Latin fīnis (“end, boundary, limit”).  The adjective sense ("refined, pure") developed from the idea of something brought to its finished state.
