desolator:/ˈdesəleɪtər/| n.|荒芜者，使荒凉的人或物，劫掠者|(something that causes great destruction or devastation, leaving a place desolate or empty.  Often used in a figurative sense to describe something that causes emotional or spiritual emptiness.)|The war was a desolator of the countryside, leaving only burned fields and empty homes. 战争使乡村荒芜，只留下烧焦的田野和空荡荡的房屋。|近义词：ravager, destroyer, devastator|反义词: creator, builder|词性:名词
Etymology: From Late Latin *desolator, from Latin desolatus (past participle of desolare "to abandon, forsake, lay waste"), from de- "down, completely" + solus "alone."
