leig: /leɪɡ/ | n. | liege |  A liege lord is a feudal superior to whom allegiance and service are owed.  A liege man is a vassal or subject of a liege lord. |  The knight swore fealty to his liege.  骑士向他的君主宣誓效忠。 | 近义词: lord, sovereign, overlord | 反义词: vassal, subject | 词性: 名词

leig: /leɪɡ/ | adj. | liege |  Bound by oath to give service and allegiance to a lord.  Owing feudal allegiance and service. |  He was a liege man of the king. 他是国王的封臣。 | 近义词: loyal, faithful, bound | 反义词: rebellious, disloyal | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Old French *liege, from Frankish *letig "free", from Proto-Germanic *leudijaz "belonging to the people, free". The semantic shift occurred because only free men could bear arms and swear fealty.
