cardamom:/ˈkɑːrdəməm/ (US), /ˈkɑːdəmɒm/ (UK)| n. |小豆蔻|The aromatic seeds of a plant of the ginger family, used as a spice and also in medicine.|例句：Cardamom is a common ingredient in Indian cuisine. 小豆蔻是印度菜肴中常见的配料。

例句：She added a pinch of cardamom to the cake batter. 她在蛋糕糊里加了一撮小豆蔻。|近义词：spice, flavoring|反义词: (No direct antonym for a spice.  Could consider blandness as a contrasting concept.)|词性:noun


cardamums:/ˈkɑːrdəməmz/ (US), /ˈkɑːdəmɒmz/ (UK)| n. |小豆蔻（复数）|The plural form of cardamom, referring to multiple seeds or pods of the plant.|例句：These cardamums are particularly fragrant. 这些小豆蔻特别香。

例句：He bought a bag of green cardamums at the market. 他在市场上买了一袋绿豆蔻。|近义词：spices, flavorings|反义词: (No direct antonym for a spice. Could consider blandness as a contrasting concept.)|词性:noun


词源: From Late Middle English *cardamome, from Old French cardamome, from Latin cardamomum, from Greek kardamomon.
