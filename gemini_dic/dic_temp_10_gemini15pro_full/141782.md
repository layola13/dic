oratorize: /ˈɔːrətəraɪz/ (US) /ˈɒrətəraɪz/ (UK)| v. | 演说；作演说；雄辩地讲话 | To speak in a formal or elaborate manner, often in public; to declaim. | He oratorized about the importance of education. 他慷慨激昂地讲述了教育的重要性。| The politician oratorized for hours, captivating the audience. 这位政治家滔滔不绝地讲了几个小时，吸引了观众。 | 近义词：declaim, speechify, harangue | 反义词：converse, chat, whisper | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *ōrātōriāre, from Latin ōrātor "speaker, orator,"  from ōrāre "to speak, plead, pray,"  from os, or- "mouth."
