hypochondriac: /ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndriæk/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpəˈkɑːndriæk/ (US)| n. |疑病症患者，过分担心健康的人|A person who is abnormally anxious about their health, often believing they have a serious illness despite medical reassurance.|例句：He's a terrible hypochondriac; every time he gets a headache, he's convinced he has a brain tumour. 他是一个严重的疑病症患者；每次头痛，他都确信自己得了脑瘤。

例句：She's such a hypochondriac that she reads medical journals all the time, looking for new diseases she might have. 她疑心病很重，总是阅读医学期刊，寻找自己可能患上的新疾病。

|近义词：valetudinarian, worrier|反义词: optimist (regarding health)|词性:noun


hypochondriacs: /ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndriæks/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpəˈkɑːndriæks/ (US)| n. |疑病症患者（复数）|Plural form of hypochondriac, referring to multiple people who are abnormally anxious about their health.|例句：Hypochondriacs often visit the doctor frequently. 疑病症患者经常去看医生。

例句：Support groups can be helpful for hypochondriacs to share their experiences and learn coping mechanisms.  互助小组可以帮助疑病症患者分享他们的经历并学习应对机制。


|近义词：valetudinarians, worriers|反义词: optimists (regarding health)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hypochondriacus, from Greek hupok hondrion 'under the cartilage (of the breastbone),' from hupo 'under' + khondros 'cartilage.'  The area was believed in ancient medicine to be the seat of melancholy and hypochondria.
