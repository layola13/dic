cunnings:/ˈkʌnɪŋz/
noun
狡猾，诡计
Skill in deception; craftiness.
His cunnings allowed him to escape the trap. 他的狡猾让他逃脱了陷阱。
synonyms:  guile,  trickery,  deceit,  chicanery
antonyms:  honesty,  frankness,  openness,  sincerity
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English connyng, from Old English cunnung (“knowledge, skill, experience”), from Proto-Germanic *kunnaną (“to know, be able”). More at can. The plural form cunnings is less common than cunning, but still attested.
