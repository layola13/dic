clumper:/ˈklʌmpər/| n.|丛生植物；成群生长之物；凝聚物|A plant that grows in clumps. Something that forms clumps or clusters.  A device or substance that causes things to clump together.|The irises were clumpers, and soon formed a thick patch.  这些鸢尾花是丛生的，很快便长成了一片。|近义词：cluster, bunch, clump, mass|反义词:scattered, dispersed|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From clump + -er (suffix forming nouns denoting agents or instruments).  Clump itself likely originates from a Scandinavian source, possibly related to Old Norse klumpr (“lump, mass”).
