groomish: /ˈɡruːmɪʃ/ | adj. |注重仪表的；喜欢打扮的；衣着考究的 |Concerned with one's appearance and neatness of dress; dapper. | He looked very groomish in his new suit. 他穿着新西装，看起来很讲究。 | 近义词: dapper, spruce, smart, stylish | 反义词: scruffy, unkempt, slovenly | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From *groom* (n.) + *-ish* (suffix).  The original meaning of "groom" referred to a servant, especially one who took care of horses.  Over time, the term expanded to encompass care of one's own appearance.  The suffix "-ish" adds the sense of "having the quality of" or "resembling."
