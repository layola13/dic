hern:/hɜːrn/
n.|角; (尤指)雄鹿的角; 鹭;
|A heron is a large bird with long legs, a long neck, and a long beak. |The heron stood motionless in the shallows, waiting for a fish.  这只苍鹭一动不动地站在浅滩里，等待着鱼儿。|Synonyms: egret, crane |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a type of bird) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hearn, from Proto-Germanic *kraznaz, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *gerh₂- (“to cry hoarsely”). Cognate with Dutch reiger, German Reiher, Danish hejre.
