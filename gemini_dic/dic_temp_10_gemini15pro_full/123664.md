moochers:/ˈmuːtʃərz/
n.
乞丐，揩油的人，寄生虫
People who habitually take advantage of the generosity of others without giving anything in return.
Those moochers are always asking for handouts. 那些揩油的人总是伸手要钱。
近义词:scroungers, freeloaders, parasites
反义词:givers, philanthropists
词性:名词(复数)

mooch:/muːtʃ/
v.
蹭，揩油，闲逛
To get something from someone without paying for it; to wander around without a specific purpose.
He's always mooching off his friends. 他总是蹭朋友的吃喝。
近义词:scrounge, freeload, sponge, loiter
反义词:provide, give, work
词性:动词

Etymology:
From 19th century American slang, possibly related to German "mutschen" meaning "to eat secretly" or "to hide something."
