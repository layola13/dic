didgeridoo:/ˈdɪdʒəriːduː/ (US), /ˌdɪdʒəˈriːduː/ (UK)| n. |迪吉里杜管|A wind instrument developed by Indigenous Australians of northern Australia. It is a long, wooden tube that produces a low-droning sound by vibrating the player's lips against the mouthpiece.|He played a haunting melody on the didgeridoo. 他用迪吉里杜管演奏了一曲萦绕心头的旋律。|Synonyms: didjeridu (alternative spelling)|Antonyms: N/A|noun

didgeridoos:/ˈdɪdʒəriːduːz/ (US), /ˌdɪdʒəˈriːduːz/ (UK)| n. |迪吉里杜管（复数）|Plural form of didgeridoo.|The shop sold didgeridoos of varying lengths and decorations. 这家商店出售各种长度和装饰的迪吉里杜管。|Synonyms: didjeridus (alternative spelling)|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From the English imitation of the sound of the instrument.  Not an Aboriginal word.
