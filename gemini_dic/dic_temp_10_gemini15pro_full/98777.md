integer:/ˈɪntɪdʒər/| n.|整数|A whole number (not a fractional number) that can be positive, negative, or zero.|例句：-3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, and 3 are integers. -3，-2，-1，0，1，2和3都是整数。|近义词：whole number|反义词:fraction, decimal|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin integer (whole, complete), from in- (not) + tangere (to touch).  The idea is that an integer is "untouched," meaning not broken into fractions.
