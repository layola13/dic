mealy-mouthednesses:/ˈmiːliˌmaʊðɪdnɪsɪz/
|n.|支支吾吾，吞吞吐吐|The habit of speaking indirectly or evasively, especially for fear of giving offense or being blunt.  A state of being unwilling to speak frankly or directly, often out of fear of causing offense.|His mealy-mouthednesses irritated everyone at the meeting. 他的吞吞吐吐激怒了会议上的每一个人。|近义词: evasiveness, indirectness, prevarication|反义词: directness, frankness, candor|词性: 名词
