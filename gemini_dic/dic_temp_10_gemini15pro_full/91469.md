hyloist:/ˈhaɪləʊɪst/| n.|泛灵论者；万物有灵论者|A person who believes in hyloism (the doctrine that matter is inseparable from life or spirit).|Hyloists believe that all matter, including inanimate objects, possesses a kind of life or soul.|The philosopher argued against the hyloist's position, claiming consciousness is not inherent in all matter.|近义词：animist, panpsychist|反义词：materialist, physicalist|词性:noun

hyloistic:/ˌhaɪləʊˈɪstɪk/| adj.|泛灵论的；万物有灵论的|Relating to hyloism; believing that matter is inseparable from life or spirit.|His hyloistic beliefs led him to see spirits in every tree and rock.|The ancient culture had a hyloistic worldview, believing that all natural objects were imbued with a life force.|近义词：animistic, panpsychistic|反义词：materialistic, physicalistic|词性:adjective
