aether:/ˈiːθər/ (US) /ˈiːθə/ (UK)| n.| 以太，乙醚 |A subtle, rarefied, and elastic medium formerly believed to permeate all space, as well as the upper regions of the atmosphere, and to be the medium through which light and other electromagnetic radiation are transmitted; also, the clear sky; the heavens. In chemistry, a class of organic compounds in which two hydrocarbon radicals are linked by an oxygen atom.|例句：Scientists used to believe light traveled through the aether. 科学家过去认为光在以太中传播。

例句：The poet described the aether as the realm of the gods. 诗人将以太描述为众神的领域。|近义词：sky, heavens, ether|反义词:None|词性:noun

aether:/ˈiːθər/ (US) /ˈiːθə/ (UK)| n.| 乙醚 |A colorless, volatile, highly flammable liquid, C₂H₅OC₂H₅, used as an anesthetic and as a solvent.|例句：The patient was anesthetized with ether before the surgery. 病人在手术前用乙醚麻醉。

例句：Ether is a common solvent in organic chemistry. 乙醚是有机化学中常用的溶剂。|近义词：diethyl ether|反义词:None|词性:noun
