eyeglasses: /ˈaɪˌɡlæsɪz/ | n. | 眼镜 | A pair of lenses mounted in a frame and worn in front of the eyes to correct or assist defective eyesight. |  I need new eyeglasses; I can barely see. 我需要一副新眼镜；我几乎看不见东西。| spectacles, glasses | contacts | noun
etymology: eye + glasses,  from Middle English *ie glasse, singular, "lens for one eye," from eye (n.) + glass (n.). The plural form emerged later, denoting a pair of lenses.
