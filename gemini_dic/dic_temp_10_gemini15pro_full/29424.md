cataian:/kəˈteɪən/| n. adj.|骗子，恶棍; 狡猾的，奸诈的|A term formerly used to denote a native of China, later acquiring derogatory connotations of dishonesty and trickery.|He called him a cataian, implying he was not to be trusted. 他称他为骗子，暗示他不值得信任。|近义词：villain, rogue, scoundrel, knave, cheat, swindler|反义词：saint, hero|词性:名词，形容词

Etymology: From Middle French *Cathaien*, from Old French *Catay*, from Medieval Latin *Cathaya*, ultimately from Khitan, a nomadic people who ruled parts of Manchuria and northern China.  The term originally referred to a person from Cathay (China). Over time, due to trade disputes and prejudice, it acquired negative connotations of deceitfulness and villainy.
