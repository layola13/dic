lairs:/lɛərz/| n. |巢穴，窝点；藏身处|A place where a wild animal lives, especially a predator, or a place where a person hides or secretly does something.|The fox retreated to its lair. 狐狸退回到它的巢穴。|dens, hideouts, retreats|exposures, open areas|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lēger, from Proto-Germanic *legrą (“a lying place, bed”), from Proto-Indo-European *legh- (“to lie down”). Cognate with Old Norse legr (“lair”), Dutch leger (“camp, bed”), German Lager (“camp, store, stock”), Gothic 𐌻𐌹𐌲𐍂𐍃 (ligrs, “bed”).
