czardom:/ˈzɑːrdəm/| n. |沙皇统治；沙皇俄国|A czar's domain or authority; the system of government in Russia before 1917.|The Romanov czardom lasted for over 300 years.|Synonyms: empire, kingdom, monarchy|Antonyms: republic, democracy|noun


czardoms:/ˈzɑːrdəmz/| n. |沙皇统治（复数）；沙皇俄国（复数）|Plural of czardom. Refers to multiple instances of a czar's domain or the Russian empire under czarist rule.|The history books detail the rise and fall of various czardoms.|Synonyms: empires, kingdoms, monarchies|Antonyms: republics, democracies|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Russian *tsarstvo* (царство), meaning "kingdom," from *tsar* (царь), meaning "czar," ultimately from Latin *Caesar*.
