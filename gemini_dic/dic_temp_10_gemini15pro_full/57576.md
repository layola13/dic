dramatizers:/ˈdræməˌtaɪzərz/
n.
戏剧化的人；渲染者；小题大做的人
People who exaggerate situations or emotions, often making them seem more important or serious than they are.  Those who adapt something into a dramatic form, like a play or screenplay.
The media are often accused of being dramatizers, sensationalizing stories to attract viewers. 媒体经常被指责为小题大做，为了吸引观众而夸大事实。
He's such a dramatizer; he makes a big fuss over everything. 他真是个爱小题大做的人；每件事他都要大惊小怪。
近义词: exaggerators, sensationalists, melodramatists
反义词: minimizers, downplayers
词性: noun (plural)

dramatize:/ˈdræməˌtaɪz/
v.
戏剧化；渲染；改编成戏剧
To present or represent something in a dramatic or exaggerated way. To adapt something into a dramatic form, like a play or screenplay.
She tends to dramatize every minor setback. 她倾向于夸大每一个小小的挫折。
They dramatized the novel for television. 他们将这部小说改编成电视剧。
近义词: exaggerate, overstate, sensationalize, adapt
反义词: understate, downplay
词性: verb
