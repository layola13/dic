"Passionate" (from Late Latin passionatus, past participle of passionari "to suffer," from Latin passio "suffering," from pati "to suffer, endure") evolving to mean "having or showing strong feelings".
"-ness" (Old English -nes(s), -nis(s), -nys(s)) a suffix forming nouns of quality or state.
"-es" plural suffix.
