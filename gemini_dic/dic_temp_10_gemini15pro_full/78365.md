gesticulate:/dʒɛˈstɪkjʊleɪt/
verb
做手势；用手势表达
To make gestures, especially when speaking, to emphasize one's words or express an idea.
She gesticulated wildly as she described the accident. 她激动地比划着，描述那场事故。
The speaker gesticulated to emphasize his point. 演讲者做手势来强调他的观点。

近义词: gesture, motion, signal, sign, wave
反义词: be still, freeze

词性: verb

词源:  From Late Latin gesticulatus, past participle of gesticulari "to make gestures," from Latin gestus "gesture, posture, carriage," from gerere "to bear, carry, perform."
