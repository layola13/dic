effortlessnesses:/ˈɛfətlɪsnɪsɪz/
n.
轻松，毫不费力，流畅自然
the quality of requiring or involving little or no effort; ease.
Her effortlessnesses on the stage captivated the audience. 她在舞台上的轻松自如深深吸引了观众。
Synonyms: ease, facility, naturalness, smoothness, fluency
Antonyms: difficulty, awkwardness, clumsiness, strain, struggle
Noun


Etymology:
From effortless + -ness + -es (plural suffix)
Effortless comes from effort + -less (suffix meaning "without")
Effort from Old French effort, from esforcier "to make an effort," from Vulgar Latin *exfortiare "to strengthen," from Latin ex- "out" + fortis "strong."
