conciliator:/kənˈsɪliˌeɪtər/| n.|调解人，和事佬|A person who acts as an intermediary between two disputing people or groups to effect a reconciliation.|The conciliator helped the two sides reach a compromise. 调解员帮助双方达成了妥协。|近义词：mediator, arbitrator, peacemaker|反义词：agitator, instigator|noun

conciliate:/kənˈsɪliˌeɪt/| v.|安抚，调解，调停|To stop someone from being angry or discontented; placate; pacify.|He tried to conciliate his angry wife with flowers. 他试图用鲜花来安抚他愤怒的妻子。|近义词：appease, placate, mollify|反义词：provoke, antagonize|verb

Etymology: From Latin conciliatus, past participle of conciliare "to bring together, unite, recommend, win over," from concilium "council, meeting."
