microclimates:/ˈmaɪkrəʊˌklaɪmɪts/ | n. | 微气候，小气候 |  The climate of a small, specific place within a larger area, which differs from the climate of the surrounding area. |  The valley has its own unique microclimate, which allows for the cultivation of tropical fruits.  这个山谷有其独特的微气候，适宜种植热带水果。| synonyms: microhabitat, local climate | antonyms: macroclimate, global climate | noun

microclimate:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌklaɪmət/ | n. | 微气候，小气候 | The climate of a small, specific place within a larger area, which differs from the climate of the surrounding area. | Cacti thrive in the hot, dry microclimate of the desert. 仙人掌在沙漠炎热干燥的微气候中茁壮成长。| synonyms: microhabitat, local climate | antonyms: macroclimate, global climate | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From micro- + climate. "Micro-" comes from Greek "mikros" meaning "small". "Climate" comes from Latin "climatis" and ultimately from Greek "klima" meaning "inclination, zone".
