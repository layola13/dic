lionizer:/ˈlaɪənaɪzər/| n.|奉承者，吹捧者|A person who lionizes someone, treating them as a celebrity or giving them excessive admiration.|She was surrounded by a crowd of lionizers after her successful performance. 她成功的表演后被一群吹捧者包围。|近义词：adulator, flatterer, sycophant|反义词：critic, detractor|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "lionize," which means "to treat as a celebrity."  "Lionize" comes from "lion," referring to the animal's perceived regal status and power.
