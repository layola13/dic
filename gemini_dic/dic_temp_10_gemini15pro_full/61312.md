embarrassed: /ɪmˈbærəst/ (US), /ɪmˈbærəst/ (UK)| adj.|尴尬的，窘迫的|Feeling awkward, self-conscious, and uncomfortable.|例句：I was embarrassed when I tripped and fell in front of everyone. 我在大家面前绊倒了，感到很尴尬。|近义词：ashamed, mortified, humiliated|反义词：confident, comfortable, proud|词性:形容词

embarrassed: /ɪmˈbærəst/ (US), /ɪmˈbærəst/ (UK)| v. |使尴尬，使窘迫|(Usually used in the passive form) To cause someone to feel awkward, self-conscious, and uncomfortable.|例句：He was embarrassed by his child's behavior. 他孩子的行为让他感到尴尬。|近义词：humiliate, mortify, shame|反义词：embolden, encourage|词性:动词
