braille: /breɪl/ | n. |盲文|A system of writing and printing for blind or visually impaired people, in which raised dots represent letters, numerals, and punctuation marks. The dots are arranged in cells of up to six dots in a 2x3 matrix.|例句：She learned to read braille when she was five. 她五岁时学会了阅读盲文。

|例句：The library has a large collection of books in braille.  图书馆有大量的盲文书籍。|近义词：tactile writing, raised dots|反义词: print (in the context of reading for sighted people)|词性:noun


brailles: /breɪlz/ | n. |盲文（复数）|Plural form of "braille," referring to multiple instances or types of braille systems.|例句：The museum showcased various brailles from around the world. 博物馆展示了来自世界各地的各种盲文。


|例句：Different brailles are used for different languages. 不同的语言使用不同的盲文。 |近义词：tactile writings, raised dots (plural)|反义词: prints (in the context of reading for sighted people)|词性:noun
