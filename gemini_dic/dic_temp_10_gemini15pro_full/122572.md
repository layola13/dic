moderationist:/ˈmɑːdəˈreɪʃənɪst/ | n. | 温和主义者；稳健派；节制主义者 | A person who holds moderate views, especially in politics or religion. |  The moderationist faction within the party gained influence after the election.  该党内的温和派在选举后获得了影响力。 | Synonyms: moderate, centrist | Antonyms: extremist, radical | Noun


moderationist:/ˈmɑːdəˈreɪʃənɪst/ | adj. | 温和的；稳健的；节制的 | Favoring or characterized by moderation. |  He took a moderationist stance on the issue, advocating for a compromise. 他在这个问题上采取了温和的立场，主张妥协。 | Synonyms: temperate, balanced | Antonyms: extreme, immoderate | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From moderation + -ist.  Moderation comes from Late Latin  *moderationem,  from Latin moderari "to moderate, regulate," from modus "measure, manner."
