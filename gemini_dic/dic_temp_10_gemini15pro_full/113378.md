lycopode:/ˈlaɪkəˌpoʊd/| n. |石松，石松粉|A type of plant belonging to the Lycopodiaceae family, also known as clubmoss.  The term can also refer to the fine, flammable powder made from the spores of these plants.|例句：The lycopode powder ignited with a bright flash. 石松粉闪耀着明亮的光芒。

Lycopode plants are often found in damp woodlands. 石松植物经常在潮湿的林地中被发现。|近义词：clubmoss|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *lycopode*, from Late Latin *lycopodium*, from Ancient Greek *λυκόποδιον* (lukópodion, “wolf's foot”), from *λύκος* (lúkos, “wolf”) + *πούς* (poús, “foot”).  The name refers to the resemblance of the plant's branching roots to a wolf's paw.
