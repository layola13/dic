demoniasm: /ˈdiːməˌnaɪzəm/ | n. |着魔；疯狂；恶魔附身|The state of being possessed by demons or evil spirits; a frantic or frenzied state. | He screamed and raved, exhibiting signs of demoniasm. 他尖叫着咆哮，表现出着魔的迹象。| The villagers believed she was suffering from demoniasm. 村民们认为她被恶魔附身了。 | 近义词：possession, madness, frenzy | 反义词：sanity, rationality | 词性: noun

Etymology: Late Middle English: from late Latin daemonismus, from Greek daimōnismos, from daimōnizein ‘be possessed by a demon’.
