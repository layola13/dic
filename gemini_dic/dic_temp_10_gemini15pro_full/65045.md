etchers:/ˈɛtʃərz/| n. |蚀刻师；蚀刻艺术家|A person who creates art by etching, a process of incising a design onto a hard, flat surface, typically metal, by the action of acid. |The etchers displayed their intricate works at the gallery.  蚀刻师在画廊里展示了他们复杂的作品。| The museum hosted a workshop for aspiring etchers.  博物馆为有抱负的蚀刻师举办了一个研讨会。 | 近义词:engravers, printmakers | 反义词: None (No direct opposite for a profession) | 词性: Noun

etch:/ɛtʃ/| v. |蚀刻；铭刻；留下深刻印象|1. To cut into the surface of (something, especially metal, glass, or stone) by the action of acid. 2. (of an acid) to erode or corrode. 3.  To impress deeply (on the memory or mind). | Acid is used to etch the design onto the plate.  酸被用来将图案蚀刻到板上。|His experiences are etched in his memory. 他的经历深深地印在他的记忆里。| 近义词:engrave, carve, inscribe | 反义词: erase, obliterate| 词性: Verb
