eutrophication:/ˌjuːtrəfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |富营养化|Excessive richness of nutrients in a lake or other body of water, frequently due to runoff from the land, which causes a dense growth of plant life and death of animal life from lack of oxygen.|The algal bloom was a clear sign of eutrophication in the lake.  湖中的藻华是湖泊富营养化的明显标志。| Eutrophication is a serious environmental problem. 富营养化是一个严重的环境问题。|近义词：hypertrophication|反义词:oligotrophication|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From eu- (Greek 'well, good') + -troph- (Greek 'nourishment') + -ication (suffix forming nouns of action or process).
