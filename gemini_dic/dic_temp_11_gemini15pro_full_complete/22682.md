borderline:/ˈbɔːrdərlaɪn/| n. adj.|边界线，界限;临界的，边缘的|A line separating two countries, administrative divisions, or other areas.  Also describes something that is at the limit of acceptability or function.|The river forms the borderline between the two states. 这条河是两州的边界线。|Synonyms: boundary, frontier, limit, edge, margin|Antonyms: center, core, middle|noun

borderline:/ˈbɔːrdərlaɪn/| adj.|临界的，边缘的;勉强合格的|Describing something that is at the limit of acceptability or function, often in relation to a medical or psychological condition.|His performance was borderline acceptable. 他的表现勉强合格。|Synonyms: marginal, doubtful, ambiguous|Antonyms: definite, clear, unambiguous|adjective

borderlines:/ˈbɔːrdərlaɪnz/| n.|边界线（borderline的复数形式）|Plural form of borderline, referring to multiple lines separating areas.|The borderlines between these countries are often disputed. 这些国家之间的边界线经常存在争议。|Synonyms: boundaries, frontiers, limits|Antonyms: centers, cores|noun
