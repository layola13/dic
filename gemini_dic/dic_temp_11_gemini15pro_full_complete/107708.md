lantern:/ˈlæntərn/ | n. | 灯笼; 提灯 |A portable light enclosed in a protective case, often made of glass or metal and with a handle for carrying. | The children carried colorful lanterns in the night parade. 孩子们在夜间游行中提着五彩缤纷的灯笼。 | synonyms: lamp, light | antonyms: darkness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "lanterne" and Latin "lanterna"  meaning "lamp, lantern," ultimately from Greek "lampein" meaning "to shine."  The word also has connections to the Latin word "linen," as early lanterns often had linen coverings.
