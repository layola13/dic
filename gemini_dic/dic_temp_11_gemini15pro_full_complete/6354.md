amphibian:/æmˈfɪbiən/ | n. | 两栖动物 | A cold-blooded vertebrate animal of a class that comprises the frogs, toads, newts, salamanders, and caecilians. They are distinguished by having an aquatic gill-breathing larval stage followed (typically) by a terrestrial lung-breathing adult stage. | Frogs are amphibians. 青蛙是两栖动物。| synonyms:  frog, toad, newt, salamander, caecilian | antonyms: mammal, reptile, bird, fish | noun

amphibian:/æmˈfɪbiən/ | adj. | 两栖的 | Relating to or characteristic of amphibians; able to live both on land and in water. |  Amphibian vehicles can travel on both land and water. 两栖车辆可以在陆地和水中行驶。| synonyms: amphibious | antonyms: terrestrial, aquatic | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *amphibium, from Ancient Greek ἀμφίβιος (amphíbios, “living a double life”), from ἀμφί (amphí, “on both sides”) + βίος (bíos, “life”).
