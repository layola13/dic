botches:/ˈbɒtʃɪz/ (British), /ˈbɑːtʃɪz/ (American)| n. |拙劣的工作；笨手笨脚的错误|A botch is a clumsy or badly executed piece of work.|The carpenter made a botch of the bookcase. 木匠把书柜做得很糟糕。|近义词:blunders, messes, screw-ups, foul-ups|反义词:successes, triumphs|词性:名词(复数)

botch:/bɒtʃ/ (British), /bɑːtʃ/ (American)| vt. |笨手笨脚地做；搞砸|To botch something means to spoil it by clumsy work or by dealing with it inefficiently.|He botched the repair job on the car. 他把汽车的修理工作搞砸了。|近义词:bungle, mess up, spoil, ruin|反义词:perfect, complete, succeed|词性:动词

botched:/bɒtʃt/ (British), /bɑːtʃt/ (American)| adj. |搞砸的；拙劣的|Botched describes something that has been done clumsily or badly.|The botched surgery left the patient with permanent damage. 失败的手术给病人留下了永久性的损伤。|近义词:bungled, messed up, spoiled, ruined|反义词:perfect, flawless, impeccable|词性:形容词
