common market:/ˈkɒmən ˌmɑːkɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmən ˌmɑːrkɪt/ (US)| n. |共同市场|A group of countries imposing few or no duties on trade with one another and a common tariff on trade with other countries.|The European Economic Community was a common market.|Synonym: trading bloc, customs union, free trade area|Antonym: closed market|noun

common markets:/ˈkɒmən ˌmɑːkɪts/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmən ˌmɑːrkɪts/ (US)| n. |共同市场（复数）|Plural form of "common market".|Several common markets exist around the world.|Synonym: trading blocs, customs unions, free trade areas|Antonym: closed markets|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"Common" from Old French "comun" ultimately from Latin "communis" meaning "shared by all or many." "Market" from Old French "market" from Latin "mercatus" meaning "trade, marketplace."
