michery:/ˈmɪtʃəri/
noun
恶作剧，捣蛋，鬼把戏
Roguish or mischievous behavior; playful tricks or pranks.
The children were full of michery, hiding their father's glasses and giggling. 孩子们充满了恶作剧，藏起了父亲的眼镜，咯咯地笑着。
Synonyms: mischief, prank, trickery, shenanigans, roguery, deviltry
Antonyms: obedience, good behavior, seriousness
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *micherie*, from Old French *micherie*, from *micher* "to hide, conceal, pilfer," of uncertain origin.
