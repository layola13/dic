grallatores:/ɡræləˈtɔːriːz/ | n. | 涉禽类；长脚鸟类 | An order of birds characterized by long legs, necks, and bills, adapted for wading in shallow water.  These birds are typically found in marshes, swamps, and other wetland habitats. | Example: Herons, cranes, and rails are all members of the grallatores order.  鹭、鹤和秧鸡都是涉禽类。

Example: The grallatores are well adapted for foraging in shallow water. 涉禽类很适应在浅水中觅食。 | 近义词: wading birds, shorebirds | 反义词:  (No direct antonym; perhaps consider contrasting groups like "diving birds" or "raptors") | 词性: noun
