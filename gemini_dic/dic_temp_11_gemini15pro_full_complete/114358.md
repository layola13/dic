majorate:/ˈmeɪdʒəreɪt/ /ˈmeɪdʒəˌreɪt/ | v. |  增大；扩大；提高；超过；占多数 | to increase the amount, size, or importance of something; to be greater in number than another group | The government majorated the tax on cigarettes. 政府提高了香烟税。| 近义词: increase, augment, amplify, enlarge, expand | 反义词: decrease, diminish, reduce, lessen, contract | 词性: 动词

majorate:/ˈmeɪdʒəreɪt/ /ˈmeɪdʒəˌreɪt/ | n. |  多数；过半数 | the greater number or part; more than half | The majorate voted in favor of the new law. 多数人投票赞成新法律。| 近义词: majority, plurality | 反义词: minority | 词性: 名词

词源: From Late Latin *majoratus, past participle of *majorare “to make greater,” from Latin major “greater.”
