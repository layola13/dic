coystrel:/ˈkɔɪstrəl/| n.|卑鄙小人，无赖，懦夫|A base, cowardly person; a knave.|He was known throughout the town as a dishonest coystrel, always looking for ways to cheat others. 他在镇上是出了名的不诚实无赖，总是想方设法欺骗别人。| The coystrel abandoned his comrades in the face of danger.  这个懦夫在危险面前抛弃了他的同伴。|近义词：knave, rogue, scoundrel, villain|反义词：hero, gentleman|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *coystrel*, possibly from Old French *coistre*, meaning "groom" or "servant," later acquiring a derogatory sense. The evolution of meaning likely reflects the low social status associated with servants in that era.
