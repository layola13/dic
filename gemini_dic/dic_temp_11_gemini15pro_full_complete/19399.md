bicaudate:/baɪˈkɔːdeɪt/| adj.|双尾的，具有两个尾状突起的|Having two tails or tail-like processes.  Specifically referring to a structure in the brain, the caudate nucleus, which has a head and a tail on each side of the brain, thus appearing "bicaudate" in some views.|The MRI showed a normal, bicaudate nucleus.|Near Antonyms: unicaudate (though not commonly used)|Near Synonyms: two-tailed (in a general sense, not specific to anatomy)|adjective
Etymology: From Latin "bi-" (two) + "caudatus" (tailed), past participle of "caudare" (to furnish with a tail), from "cauda" (tail).
