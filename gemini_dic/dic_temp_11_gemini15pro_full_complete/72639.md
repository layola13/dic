flypapers:/ˈflaɪˌpeɪpərz/
noun
捕蝇纸
Sticky strips of paper used to trap flies.
The old house was filled with cobwebs and flypapers.  老房子里满是蜘蛛网和捕蝇纸。

flypapers:/ˈflaɪˌpeɪpərz/
noun
(比喻) 难以摆脱的困境
Something that entraps or holds fast.  (Figurative)
His gambling debts became a flypaper he couldn't escape. 他的赌债成了他无法摆脱的困境。


Etymology:  Compound of "fly" (noun referring to the insect) and "paper" (noun referring to the material).  The word's origin is straightforward, describing the paper's function of catching flies.

Synonyms: fly ribbon, fly strip, fly trap, (figurative) quagmire, predicament

Antonyms:  (For the literal meaning, there isn't a direct opposite. For the figurative meaning) freedom, liberation, escape
