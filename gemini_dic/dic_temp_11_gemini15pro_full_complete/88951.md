hole-up:/ˈhoʊl ʌp/ (US) /ˈhəʊl ʌp/ (UK)| v. n.|躲藏，藏匿；窝藏|To stay in a safe place, often hiding from someone or something.  A place where someone hides.|例句：The robbers holed up in a cabin in the woods.  强盗们躲藏在树林里的小屋中。

例句：They found a hole-up in an abandoned warehouse. 他们在废弃的仓库里找到了一个藏身之处。|近义词：hide, conceal, shelter, bunker down|反义词:emerge, come out|词性：动词, 名词

词源: "hole" (洞) + "up" (向上).  Originally referring to animals hibernating in burrows.  Later extended to people hiding in a secure location.
