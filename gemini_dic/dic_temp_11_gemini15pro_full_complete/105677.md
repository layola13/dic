kingship:/ˈkɪŋʃɪp/| n.|王权，君主身份，君主统治|The state or position of being a king.  The fact of being ruled by a king.|He succeeded to the kingship at the age of fifteen. 他十五岁继承王位。|近义词：sovereignty, monarchy, reign, dominion|反义词：republicanism|词性：名词

Etymology:
From Old English *cyningscipe "kingship, reign, royal dignity," from cyning "king" (from Proto-Germanic *kuningaz "king," literally "man of the family/clan") + -scipe (suffix forming abstract nouns of quality, condition, or office).
