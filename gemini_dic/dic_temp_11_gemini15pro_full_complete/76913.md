gantlet:/ˈɡɔːntlət/ (US), /ˈɡɔːntlɪt/ (UK)| n.|考验；磨难；夹道鞭笞|A severe trial or ordeal, often involving running between two rows of people who strike at the person passing through.|The new recruit had to run the gantlet of his fellow soldiers. 新兵必须经受战友们的夹道鞭笞。|近义词：ordeal, trial, test|反义词: ease, comfort|词性: noun

gantlet:/ˈɡɔːntlət/ (US), /ˈɡɔːntlɪt/ (UK)| n.|挑战；批评|A barrage of criticism or hostile questioning.|The CEO faced a gantlet of reporters after the scandal broke.  丑闻爆出后，首席执行官面临记者们的连番质问。|近义词：barrage, onslaught|反义词: praise, acclaim|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Swedish *gatlopp*, from *gata* ("lane, street") + *lopp* ("run, course").  Originally referred to a military punishment where an offender ran between two rows of men who struck him.  Later, it came to mean any difficult or painful experience.
