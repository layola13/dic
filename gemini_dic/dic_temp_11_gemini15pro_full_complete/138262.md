not-so-much:/ˌnɒt səʊ ˈmʌtʃ/ /ˌnɑːt soʊ ˈmʌtʃ/ | adv. | 不太；与其说…不如说… | Used to say that one thing is not true or correct, while another thing is true or correct. It diminishes the importance of one element and emphasizes another.  |  例句：I don't dislike her, not so much dislike as find her boring. 我并不讨厌她，与其说讨厌不如说觉得她无聊。 | 近义词：not really, not exactly, rather than, less so | 反义词: very much, definitely | 词性: 副词

not-so-much: /ˌnɒt səʊ ˈmʌtʃ/ /ˌnɑːt soʊ ˈmʌtʃ/ | adj. |  不太…的 | Used to describe something that is less significant or important than something else. |  例句：It was a not-so-much a vacation as a business trip. 这与其说是度假，不如说是出差。| 近义词：less, not quite, not entirely | 反义词: very, completely | 词性: 形容词


词源:  This phrase is a contraction of "not so much... as...".  It originated from comparing two things and emphasizing one over the other. The "as" is frequently omitted in modern usage.
