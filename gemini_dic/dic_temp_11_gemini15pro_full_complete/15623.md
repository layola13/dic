bailpiece:/ˈbeɪlˌpiːs/| n.|保释单，保释状|A legal document that serves as proof of release on bail.|The bailpiece was handed over to the court clerk. 保释单已交给法院书记员。|Synonyms: bail bond, recognizance|Antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "bail" (security given for release) + "piece" (document).  "Bail" comes from Old French "baillier" (to deliver, give custody of), ultimately from Latin "bajulare" (to carry a burden). "Piece" comes from Old French "piece," ultimately from Vulgar Latin "pecia" (piece, fragment).
