ennobler:/ɪˈnoʊblər/ |n.|使高贵的人或物；赋予贵族称号的人|Something that ennobles someone or something.  It can also refer to a person who grants a noble title.|The patronage of the arts has long been seen as an ennobler of society.  艺术赞助长期以来一直被视为社会的高尚之举。|synonyms: dignify, elevate, exalt, uplift|antonyms: degrade, debase, demean, corrupt|noun

ennoble:/ɪˈnoʊbl/ |v.|使高贵；使崇高；授予爵位|To make someone or something noble or of a higher quality; to give someone a noble title.|Her selfless acts of charity ennobled her in the eyes of the community. 她无私的慈善行为使她在社区民众眼中变得高尚。  The king ennobled him for his bravery in battle. 国王因他在战斗中的英勇而授予他爵位。|synonyms: dignify, elevate, exalt, uplift|antonyms: degrade, debase, demean, corrupt|verb
