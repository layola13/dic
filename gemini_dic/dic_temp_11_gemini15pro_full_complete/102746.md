jansenism:/ˈdʒænsənɪzəm/| n.|詹森主义|A 17th-century theological movement within Catholicism, emphasizing original sin, human depravity, predestination, and the necessity of grace. It arose from the work of Cornelius Jansen.|Jansenism was condemned by the Catholic Church. 詹森主义被天主教会谴责。|近义词:Augustinianism, rigorism|反义词:Jesuitism, laxism|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Cornelius Jansen (1585–1638), Bishop of Ypres, + -ism.
