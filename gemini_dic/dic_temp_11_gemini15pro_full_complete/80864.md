gout:/ɡaʊt/| n. |痛风|A disease in which defective metabolism of uric acid causes arthritis, especially in the smaller bones of the feet, deposition of chalkstones, and episodes of acute pain.| My grandfather suffers from gout. 我的祖父患有痛风。|Example: The pain in his big toe was diagnosed as gout.  他的大脚趾疼痛被诊断为痛风。|Synonyms: arthritis, inflammation, hyperuricemia|Antonyms: health, wellness|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *goute*, from Old French *goute* (“drop”), from Latin *gutta* (“drop”).  So called because the disease was believed to be caused by a poisonous drop of humor falling into the joint.
