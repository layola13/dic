inquisitionary:/ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈtɔːri/| adj.|审讯式的，质疑的，酷似审讯的|Relating to, resembling, or suggestive of an inquisition, especially in being harsh, rigorous, or intrusive.|The police officer's inquisitionary tone made the suspect nervous. 警察审讯式的语气让嫌疑人感到紧张。|Synonyms: questioning, probing, interrogative, scrutinizing, examining, inquiring |Antonyms: lenient, gentle, mild, permissive|adjective

inquisitionary:/ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈtɔːri/| n.|调查者，询问者|A person who conducts an inquisition.|The inquisitionary relentlessly pursued the truth. 调查者不懈地追寻真相。|Synonyms: investigator, examiner, inquirer|Antonyms: defendant, respondent|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *inquisitionarius, from Latin inquisitio (“inquiry, investigation”), from inquirere (“to inquire into, investigate”), from in- (“in, into”) + quaerere (“to seek, ask”).
