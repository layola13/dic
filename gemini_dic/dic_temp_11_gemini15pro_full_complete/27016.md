calculi:/ˈkælkjʊlaɪ/| n. |结石；运算；微积分学|A stone formed in the body, such as a kidney stone, or the branch of mathematics dealing with the finding and properties of derivatives and integrals of functions, by methods originally based on the summation of infinitesimal differences.|The doctor found calculi in his gallbladder.|近义词：stones, concretions (for physical stones), calculus (singular form)|反义词:None|词性:Noun (plural)


calculi:/ˈkælkjʊlaɪ/| n. |计算；演算|The process of calculating something, especially using a calculating machine.|The calculi involved in predicting the weather are complex.|近义词：calculations, computations|反义词:None|词性:Noun (plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin calculus (“small stone, pebble used in reckoning”), diminutive of calx (“limestone, chalk”).  The mathematical sense developed from the use of pebbles for counting.
