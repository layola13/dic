against-the-grain:/əˈɡenst ðə ɡreɪn/ | adj. |违背本性地，格格不入地 | Going against the grain means doing something that is difficult or unpleasant because it contradicts one's natural inclinations or the usual way of doing things. It originates from the woodworking practice where smoothing wood against the direction of its fibers (grain) is more difficult and yields a rougher result. | Working on weekends goes against the grain for me.  周末工作违背我的本性。 |  It went against the grain to accept charity. 接受施舍让我很不舒服。| synonyms: counterintuitive, disagreeable, unnatural, awkward, difficult, unpleasant | antonyms: natural, agreeable, pleasant, easy, intuitive, along-the-grain | adjective
