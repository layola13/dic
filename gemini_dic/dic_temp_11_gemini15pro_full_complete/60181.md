effetenesses:/ɪˈfɛtɪnɪsɪz/
noun
柔弱；颓废；无力
The state or quality of being effete; weakness, decadence, or inability to act effectively.
His effetenesses prevented him from taking decisive action. 他的柔弱使他无法采取果断的行动。
Near synonyms: weakness, feebleness, decadence, enervation, impotence
Near antonyms: strength, vigor, robustness, dynamism

ETYMOLOGY->
From effete + -ness.  Effete from Latin effetus (“worn out, exhausted, barren”), past participle of effētus (“that has brought forth offspring”), from ex- (“out”) + fetus (“that has brought forth”), from ferō (“I bear”).
