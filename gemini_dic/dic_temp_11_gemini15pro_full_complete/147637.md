overpolice:/ˌoʊvərˈpliːs/| v. |过度监管，过度警备|To maintain an excessive police presence in (a place or area); to police too much.|例句：The neighborhood felt overpoliced, with officers on every corner. 这个社区感觉警力过剩，每个角落都有警察。|例句：Critics argue that overpolicing can lead to mistrust between the community and law enforcement. 批评人士认为，过度警备会导致社区和执法部门之间缺乏信任。|近义词：overregulate, oversaturate (with police)|反义词: underpolice, neglect|词性:动词

overpolice:/ˌoʊvərˈpliːsɪŋ/| n. |过度监管，过度警备|The act of maintaining an excessive police presence in a place or area.|例句：The community protested against the overpolicing of their neighborhood. 社区抗议他们社区的过度警备。|例句：Overpolicing can be a form of harassment, particularly for minority groups. 过度警备可能是一种骚扰形式，尤其是对少数群体而言。|近义词：overregulation|反义词: underpolicing, neglect|词性:名词
