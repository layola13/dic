debouchement:/ˌdeɪˈbuːʃmɒ̃/ | n. |河口，出口，出路; (军队)走出工事，出动|An outlet or mouth of a river or other body of water; the act of emerging from a confined space, especially of troops leaving a fortified position. |The army's debouchement from the mountains caught the enemy by surprise. 部队从山区出动，打了敌人一个措手不及。|近义词：outlet, exit, egress, emergence|反义词：entrance, entry, ingress|词性:名词

Etymology:
From French *débouchement*, from *déboucher* "to uncork, unplug, emerge," from *de-* (expressing removal) + *bouche* "mouth," from Latin *bucca* "cheek, mouth."
