fusile:/ˈfjuːzaɪl/ /ˈfjuːsɪl/ | adj. | 可熔的；易熔的；液化的 | Capable of being melted or liquefied; molten.  | The metal became fusible at high temperatures.  这种金属在高温下会熔化。 | Synonyms: meltable, liquefiable, molten | Antonyms: infusible, non-fusible, refractory | adjective

fusile:/ˈfjuːzaɪl/ /ˈfjuːsɪl/ | adj. | (literary) 可铸造的 |  Capable of being molded or shaped; pliable. |  He had a fusible personality, easily influenced by others. 他性格易受影响，容易被他人左右。 | Synonyms: malleable, pliable, ductile | Antonyms: rigid, inflexible, unyielding | adjective

fusile:/ˈfjuːzaɪl/ /ˈfjuːsɪl/ | adj. | (military) 流线型的 | Designed with a streamlined shape to minimize air resistance. | The new aircraft had a fusible body for increased speed. 新型飞机具有流线型机身以提高速度。| Synonyms: streamlined, aerodynamic | Antonyms: blunt, blocky | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *fūsilis, from Latin fūsus (“poured, melted”), past participle of fundere (“to pour, melt”).
