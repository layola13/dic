civilise:/ˈsɪvɪlaɪz/ (UK) /ˈsɪvəˌlaɪz/ (US)| vt.|使文明，使开化；教化|To bring out of a savage, uneducated, or unrefined state, or to refine and improve.|The Romans civilised many of the tribes of Europe.  罗马人教化了许多欧洲部落。|Synonyms: educate, refine, cultivate, enlighten|Antonyms: barbarize, brutalize|verb

civilized:/ˈsɪvɪlaɪzd/ (UK) /ˈsɪvəˌlaɪzd/ (US)| adj.|文明的，开化的；有教养的|Having an advanced or humane culture, society, etc.|We live in a civilized society. 我们生活在一个文明的社会。|Synonyms: cultured, refined, sophisticated, polite|Antonyms: barbaric, uncivilized, savage|adjective
