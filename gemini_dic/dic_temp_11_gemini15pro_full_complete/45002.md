crystallography: /ˌkrɪstəˈlɒɡrəfi/ (US) /ˌkrɪstəˈlɒɡrəfi/ (UK)| n. | 晶体学 | The science that examines crystals, their form, internal structure, and properties.  It involves studying how atoms are arranged in solids and how this arrangement affects the material's physical and chemical properties. | X-ray crystallography is a powerful technique used to determine the structure of molecules.  X射线晶体学是测定分子结构的有效技术。

Crystallography played a crucial role in understanding the structure of DNA.  晶体学在理解DNA结构方面发挥了至关重要的作用。| 近义词: crystal science | 反义词:  (No direct antonym) | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek krýstallos (κρύσταλλος, “ice, crystal”) + -graphy (-γραφία, from γράφω (gráphō, “I write”)).
