on-the-spur-of-the-moment:/ɒn ðə spɜːr əv ðə ˈməʊmənt/ | adv. | 心血来潮；一时冲动；即兴 | Done suddenly, without planning. |  We decided to go to the beach on the spur of the moment. 我们一时兴起决定去海滩。| synonyms: impulsively, spontaneously, extemporaneously | antonyms: deliberately, planned, premeditated | adverb

Etymology:
From the practice of using spurs to urge a horse forward suddenly.  "Spur" in this context refers to a pointed device worn on a rider's heel for urging a horse forward.  The phrase evokes the image of acting quickly and impulsively, just as a horse would react to the spur.
