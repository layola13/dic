eschatologically:/ˌɛskətəˈlɒdʒɪkli/
adverb
末世论地
in a way that relates to the part of theology concerned with death, judgement, and the final destiny of the soul and of humankind.
Some Christians interpret the Book of Revelation eschatologically, believing it predicts the end times.  一些基督徒从末世论的角度解释《启示录》，认为它预言了末日。
The preacher spoke eschatologically about the coming apocalypse.  传教士从末世论的角度谈论即将到来的天启。
