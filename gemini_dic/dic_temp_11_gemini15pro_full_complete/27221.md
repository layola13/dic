callus:/ˈkæləs/ (US) /ˈkæləs/ (UK)| n. |老茧；胼胝；愈合组织|A thickened and hardened part of the skin or soft tissue, especially in an area that has been subjected to friction.|The guitarist had calluses on his fingertips.|近义词：thickening, induration|反义词: softness|词性:名词

callus:/ˈkæləs/ (US) /ˈkæləs/ (UK)| v. |(使)长茧；(使)结痂|To form or cause to form a callus.|The constant digging callused his hands.|近义词：harden, toughen|反义词: soften|词性:动词
