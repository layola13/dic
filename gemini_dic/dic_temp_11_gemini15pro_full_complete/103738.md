jostle: /ˈdʒɒs(ə)l/ (US) /ˈdʒɑːsəl/ (UK)| v.  n.| 推挤，挤开；争夺|To push roughly against someone in a crowd. Also, to compete strongly with someone for something.|
The shoppers jostled each other to get to the sale items. 顾客们互相推搡着去抢购打折商品。
He jostled his way through the crowded street. 他在拥挤的街道上挤过去。
|近义词：shove, push, elbow, bump, vie, contend|反义词：yield, give way|词性:动词,名词

jostle: /ˈdʒɒs(ə)l/ (US) /ˈdʒɑːsəl/ (UK)| n.| 推挤，拥挤|The action of jostling.|
There was a lot of jostle for position at the front of the stage.  舞台前排位置的争夺非常激烈。
The jostle of the crowd made it hard to breathe. 人群的拥挤让人难以呼吸。
|近义词：shoving, pushing, crowding|反义词：order, space|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *joster (unattested), ultimately from Vulgar Latin *juxtare "to join, put together," frequentative of Latin jungere "to join." The sense evolution likely went from "to joust" to "to bump or knock against" to the current meaning of "to push or shove."
