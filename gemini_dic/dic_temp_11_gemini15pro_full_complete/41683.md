correspondentship:/ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːndəntʃɪp/ |n.| 通信；记者工作；通讯员的身份 |The correspondentship in Beijing was a highly sought-after position. 在北京的记者工作是一个备受追捧的职位。|  Maintaining a regular correspondentship with her pen pal brought her much joy.  与笔友保持定期通信给她带来了很多快乐。 | synonyms: communication, correspondence, journalism (when referring to the profession), posting (when referring to the position) | antonyms: silence, non-communication | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "correspondent" + "-ship".  "Correspondent" comes from the Late Middle English "correspondent," from Old French, present participle of "correspondre," from Latin "correspondēre" ("to correspond"), from "com-" ("with, together") + "respondēre" ("to answer, respond").  "-ship" is an Old English suffix denoting "state, condition, quality, character, office, skill."
