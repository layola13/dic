mitigant:/ˈmɪtɪɡənt/ | adj. n. | 减轻的，缓和的；缓和剂 | Something that mitigates or makes less severe, serious, or painful. |  The judge considered her youth as a mitigating factor in sentencing. 法官认为她的年轻是量刑时的一个减轻罪责的因素。 |  She described exercise as a mitigant against depression. 她将运动描述为对抗抑郁症的一种缓解方法。 |  近义词:  alleviating, palliative, assuaging, lessening,  relieving | 反义词: aggravating, exacerbating, intensifying | 词性: adjective, noun

mitigating:/ˈmɪtɪɡeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 减轻的，缓和的 | Making something less severe, serious, or painful. |  There are mitigating circumstances that explain his behavior. 有一些可以解释他行为的减轻情节。 |  His apology had a mitigating effect on her anger. 他的道歉减轻了她的愤怒。 | 近义词: extenuating, justificatory,  excusatory |  反义词: incriminating, aggravating | 词性: adjective


词源:
From Late Latin *mitigantem, present participle of mitigare ("to soften, alleviate"), from Latin mitis ("mild, soft") + -igare (frequentative suffix).
