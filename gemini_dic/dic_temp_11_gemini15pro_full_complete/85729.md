hawk:/hɔːk/ | n. |鹰；掠夺他人的人；鹰派人物|A bird of prey with sharp talons and a hooked beak, known for its keen eyesight and powerful flight.  A person who advocates aggressive policies, especially in foreign affairs. |例句：The hawk circled overhead, searching for prey.  鹰在头顶盘旋，搜寻猎物。

例句：He's known as a hawk on defense spending. 他以在国防开支上持强硬态度而闻名。 |近义词：falcon, eagle, predator (n.), warmonger (n.)|反义词：dove (n.)|词性:noun


hawk:/hɔːk/ | v. |兜售；叫卖；清嗓子；咳出|To sell goods aggressively, often by going from place to place. To clear the throat noisily. |例句：He was hawking his wares on the street corner. 他在街角兜售他的商品。

例句：She hawked and spat on the sidewalk. 她在人行道上清了清嗓子，吐了口痰。|近义词：peddle, vend, sell, cough|反义词：buy|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hafoc, from Proto-Germanic *habukaz, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kep- "to cut, to scrape".  Related to Dutch havik and German Habicht.  The figurative sense of "war hawk" arose in the early 19th century.
