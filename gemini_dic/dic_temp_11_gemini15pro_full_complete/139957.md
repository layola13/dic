odontolite:/ˌoʊdənˈtəˌlaɪt/ | n. |齿磷灰石，蓝松石|A blue or greenish-blue mineral, a form of turquoise, often used as a gemstone.  It is essentially bone or tooth material that has been fossilized and then altered by the addition of iron and copper phosphates. |Example 1: The museum displayed a beautiful necklace made of odontolite beads. 博物馆展出了一条由齿磷灰石珠子制成的漂亮项链。

Example 2: Odontolite was a popular gemstone in Victorian jewelry. 齿磷灰石是维多利亚时代珠宝中流行的宝石。|Synonym: bone turquoise, fossil turquoise, occidental turquoise|Antonym: N/A (No direct antonym for a specific mineral)|Noun
