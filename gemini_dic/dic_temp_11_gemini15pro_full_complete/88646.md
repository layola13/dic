hoagie:/ˈhoʊɡi/ (US), /ˈhəʊɡi/ (UK)| n. |英雄三明治，大号三明治|A large sandwich on a long roll typically filled with meat, cheese, and vegetables.|例句：I ordered a hoagie for lunch. 我午饭点了一个英雄三明治。

例句：He loves Italian hoagies with lots of peppers. 他喜欢加很多辣椒的意大利英雄三明治。|近义词：sub, grinder, hero, wedge, Italian sandwich|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps a small snack or appetizer)|词性:noun

hoagies:/ˈhoʊɡiz/ (US), /ˈhəʊɡiz/ (UK)| n. |英雄三明治（复数）|Plural form of hoagie.|例句：We bought two hoagies to share. 我们买了两个英雄三明治一起吃。

例句：They sell delicious hoagies at that deli. 那家熟食店卖好吃的英雄三明治。|近义词：subs, grinders, heroes, wedges, Italian sandwiches|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性:noun

词源:  The origin of "hoagie" is uncertain, but the most widely accepted theory connects it to Hog Island, Philadelphia, where shipyard workers during WWI were called "hoggies" and ate large sandwiches.  These sandwiches became known as "hoagies."
