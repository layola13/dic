hypocritically:/ˌhɪpəˈkrɪtɪkli/| adv.|虚伪地，伪善地|In a way that suggests one has higher moral standards or beliefs than is the case.|He hypocritically condemned violence while secretly funding terrorist groups. 他虚伪地谴责暴力，同时却秘密资助恐怖组织。|Synonyms: insincerely, falsely, deceptively|Antonyms: sincerely, genuinely, honestly|Part of Speech: Adverb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *hypocritus* + -al + -ly.  Late Latin *hypocritus* comes from Ancient Greek *hypokritḗs* (ὑποκριτής), meaning "actor, pretender," from *hypokrínesthai* (ὑποκρίνεσθαι), "to answer, interpret, play a part, pretend."
