embroil: /ɪmˈbrɔɪl/ | vt. | 使卷入；使纠缠 | to involve someone deeply in an argument, conflict, or difficult situation | The two countries were embroiled in a bitter dispute over territory. 这两个国家卷入了一场关于领土的激烈争端。 |  The CEO found himself embroiled in a scandal. 首席执行官发现自己卷入了丑闻。 | 近义词: involve, entangle, mire | 反义词: extricate, disentangle | 词性: 动词

embroil: /ɪmˈbrɔɪl/ |  vt. |  使卷入纷争;使陷入困境 | to cause someone to become involved in an argument or a difficult situation |  He became embroiled in a dispute with his neighbours. 他和邻居陷入了纠纷。| The company was embroiled in legal battles for years.  这家公司卷入法律纠纷多年。| 近义词: enmesh, implicate | 反义词:  disengage, separate | 词性: 动词
