caddice:/ˈkædɪs/| n. |石蛾，石蚕|The larva of a caddisfly, which lives in water and typically constructs a portable case of leaves, twigs, or small stones.|Caddisflies build intricate cases to protect themselves from predators. 石蛾会建造复杂的壳来保护自己免受捕食者的侵害。|近义词: caddisfly larva|反义词: None|词性: noun
Etymology: From Middle English *cados, from Old Northern French cadis, of uncertain origin.
