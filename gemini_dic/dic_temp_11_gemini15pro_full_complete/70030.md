fetishized:/ˈfɛtɪʃaɪzd/| adj.| 恋物化的；被视为恋物对象的 |Made into a fetish; treated or regarded with fetishistic devotion.  The media has fetishized youth and beauty. 媒体把年轻和美貌恋物化了。| 近义词: idolized, obsessed over, romanticized | 反义词: disregarded, ignored, devalued |词性:形容词

fetish:/ˈfɛtɪʃ/| n.| 恋物；迷恋；癖好 | An inanimate object worshiped for its supposed magical powers or because it is considered to be inhabited by a spirit. He had a fetish for high heels. 他对高跟鞋有特殊的癖好。| 近义词: obsession, fixation | 反义词: aversion, dislike |词性:名词

-ize:/aɪz/| suffix.| 使…化；使成…状态 |  A suffix forming verbs meaning ‘make or become’: familiarize; sterilize.  Used to create verbs from nouns and adjectives. | 近义词: N/A (suffix) | 反义词: N/A (suffix) |词性:后缀

词源: From French *fétiche* (now *fétiche*), from Portuguese *feitiço* "artificial charm, amulet, sorcery", from Latin *facticius* "artificial, made by art", from *facere* "to do, to make".  The original meaning was related to objects used in religious or magical practices.
