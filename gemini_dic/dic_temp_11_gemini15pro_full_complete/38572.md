concomitance:/kənˈkɒmɪtəns/ /kənˈkɑːmɪtəns/| n. |伴随，共存|The state of accompanying or existing together with something else. |例句：The concomitance of poverty and crime is a complex issue. 贫困和犯罪的伴随是一个复杂的问题。

concomitance:/kənˈkɒmɪtəns/ /kənˈkɑːmɪtəns/| n. |伴随，共存|The state of accompanying or existing together with something else. |例句：There is a clear concomitance between smoking and lung cancer. 吸烟和肺癌之间存在明显的伴随关系。

concomitant:/kənˈkɒmɪtənt/ /kənˈkɑːmɪtənt/| adj. |伴随的，共存的|Naturally accompanying or associated. |例句：The concomitant rise in unemployment and crime rates is worrying. 失业率和犯罪率的同时上升令人担忧。

concomitant:/kənˈkɒmɪtənt/ /kənˈkɑːmɪtənt/| adj. |伴随的，共存的|Naturally accompanying or associated. |例句：She experienced the usual concomitant symptoms of the flu. 她经历了流感的常见伴随症状。

concomitant:/kənˈkɒmɪtənt/ /kənˈkɑːmɪtənt/| n. |伴随物，伴随现象|Something that accompanies or is collaterally connected with something else. |例句：Loss of appetite is a frequent concomitant of the disease. 食欲不振是这种疾病的常见伴随现象。


concomitant:/kənˈkɒmɪtənt/ /kənˈkɑːmɪtənt/| n. |伴随物，伴随现象|Something that accompanies or is collaterally connected with something else. |例句：Stress is often a concomitant of modern life.  压力通常是现代生活的伴随现象。


近义词: accompaniment, association, correlation, attendance,  concurrence

反义词:  dissociation, separation, independence


词源: Late Middle English: from late Latin concomitantia, from concomitari ‘accompany’, from con- ‘together’ + comitari, from comes ‘companion’.
