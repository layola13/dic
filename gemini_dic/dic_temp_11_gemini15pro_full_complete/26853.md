cairns:/kɛərnz/ (UK), /kɛrnz/ (US)| n.|石冢，堆石路标|A mound of stones built as a memorial or landmark, typically on a hilltop or skyline.|例句：The hikers followed the cairns up the mountain.  徒步旅行者沿着石冢上山。

例句：Cairns mark the trail through the wilderness.  石冢标示着穿过荒野的小路。|近义词：mounds, markers, beacons|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "path" or "road" in a sense of a clearly marked way)|词性:noun

Etymology->
From Scottish Gaelic *càrn* meaning "heap of stones". Ultimately from Proto-Celtic *karnā* and Proto-Indo-European *ḱerh₂-*.
