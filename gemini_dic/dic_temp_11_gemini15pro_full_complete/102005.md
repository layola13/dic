isodiabatic:/ˌaɪsoʊdaɪəˈbætɪk/
adj.
等熵的; 绝热的
Relating to a thermodynamic process occurring at constant entropy.  In meteorology, it refers to a process where no heat is exchanged between an air parcel and its surroundings.
An isodiabatic process is both adiabatic (no heat exchange) and reversible (no entropy change).
