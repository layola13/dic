bloviating:/ˈbloʊvieɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 夸夸其谈；高谈阔论 | Speaking or writing verbosely and pompously. | He was bloviating about his accomplishments, but no one was impressed. 他一直在吹嘘自己的成就，但没有人留下深刻印象。 | The politician was known for bloviating on any subject, regardless of his actual knowledge.  这位政客以无论对任何话题，不管他实际了解多少，都夸夸其谈而闻名。 | 近义词: ranting, raving, boasting, bragging | 反义词: concise, succinct, reticent | 词性: 动词 (gerund or present participle)

bloviate:/ˈbloʊvieɪt/ | v. | 夸夸其谈；高谈阔论 | To speak or write verbosely and pompously. | He tends to bloviate at meetings, dominating the conversation. 他在会议上总是夸夸其谈，主导谈话。 |  She bloviated for hours about her latest project. 她就她的最新项目夸夸其谈了几个小时。| 近义词: rant, rave, boast, brag | 反义词: summarize, condense, be quiet | 词性: 动词

Etymology: From "blow hard," a slang term meaning "to brag or boast," possibly influenced by the image of someone puffing themselves up with importance.
