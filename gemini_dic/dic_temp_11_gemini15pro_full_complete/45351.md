cumulus: /ˈkjuːmjələs/ (US), /ˈkjuːmjʊləs/ (UK)| n. |积云|A cloud forming rounded masses heaped on each other above a flat base at fairly low altitude.|The sky was filled with white cumulus clouds.|近义词：cloud|反义词: clear sky|词性: 名词

cumuli: /ˈkjuːmjʊlaɪ/ (plural)| n. |积云（复数）|Plural form of cumulus.|The cumuli were building up, promising afternoon showers.|近义词：clouds|反义词: clear skies|词性: 名词

Etymology: From Latin *cumulus* "heap, pile,"  from *cumulare* "to heap up," related to Greek *kyma* "wave, swelling."
