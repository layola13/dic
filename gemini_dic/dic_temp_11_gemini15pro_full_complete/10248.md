apothegmatist:/əˈpɒθɪɡmətɪst/ | n. | 格言家；警句家 | A person who uses or creates apothegms (short, pithy, and instructive sayings).  |  Benjamin Franklin was a prolific writer and apothegmatist, filling his Poor Richard's Almanack with witty observations about life. 本杰明·富兰克林是一位多产的作家和格言家，在他的《穷理查年鉴》中充满了关于生活的机智观察。 |  proverbialist, maxim writer, aphorist | antonym:  None (lack of a direct antonym; perhaps "unwise person" in a loose sense) | noun
