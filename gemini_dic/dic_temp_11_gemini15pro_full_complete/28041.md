caparison: /kəˈpærɪsən/ | n. vt. |  装饰，华丽的服饰；披上华丽的服饰 | A rich covering, especially decorative trappings for a horse; to dress richly or elaborately. | The knight's horse was clad in a magnificent caparison of gold and crimson.  骑士的马披着华丽的金红色装饰。 |  The general caparisoned his horse for the parade. 将军为游行装饰了他的马。 | Synonyms: adornment, ornamentation, decoration, array | Antonyms: plainness, simplicity | noun
caparison: /kəˈpærɪsən/ | vt. |  披上华丽的服饰，装饰 | To dress richly or elaborately. |  They caparisoned the elephants for the festival. 他们为节日装饰了大象。 |  The queen's carriage was caparisoned in velvet and gold. 女王的马车装饰着天鹅绒和黄金。 | Synonyms: adorn, decorate, embellish, ornament | Antonyms: strip, denude | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *caparasson*, from Spanish *caparazón* "large hood," from *capa* "cape, hood."
