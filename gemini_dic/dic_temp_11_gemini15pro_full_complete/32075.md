chervil:/ˈtʃɜːr.vɪl/ | n. |细叶芹；雪维菜；香芹|An aromatic herb (Anthriscus cerefolium) of the parsley family, with delicate, ferny leaves used in salads and as a garnish.|Example 1: The chef sprinkled fresh chervil over the salmon.  厨师在鲑鱼上撒了新鲜的细叶芹。

Example 2:  A simple salad of lettuce, chervil, and chives.  一份简单的生菜、细叶芹和细香葱沙拉。|近义词:parsley,  cerfeuil|反义词: N/A|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *cherfoil, from Late Latin chaerefolium, cerefolium, from Greek χαμαικέρασος (khamailerasos, “ground cherry”), from χαμαί (khamai, “on the ground”) + κέρασος (kerasos, “cherry”). The word refers to the plant's low growth habit and supposed similarity in scent to cherries.
