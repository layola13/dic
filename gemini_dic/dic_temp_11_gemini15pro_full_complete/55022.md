disqualification:/dɪsˌkwɒlɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/ | n. |取消资格，不合格|The act of disqualifying someone or something, or the state of being disqualified.  The athlete faced disqualification for using performance-enhancing drugs.| He received a disqualification from driving for six months. 他因驾驶被取消资格六个月。| 近义词: ineligibility, rejection, exclusion | 反义词: qualification, eligibility | 词性: noun

disqualify:/dɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪ/ | vt. |取消…的资格，使…不合格|To declare someone or something ineligible or unsuitable for something.  His criminal record disqualifies him from holding public office. | The injury disqualified her from competing in the Olympics.  伤病使她无法参加奥运会比赛。|近义词: bar, preclude, prohibit | 反义词: qualify, enable, permit | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + qualification.  The prefix "dis-" comes from Latin and means "apart," "asunder," "away," or "not." "Qualification" comes from Late Latin qualificatio, meaning "a giving of quality or character," from Latin qualis "of what sort" + facere "to make."
