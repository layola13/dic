expansionists:/ɪkˈspænʃənɪsts/
|n.|扩张主义者；主张扩张者|People who advocate or support expansion, especially territorial expansion of a country.|The expansionists believed their nation had a right to conquer neighboring lands.|近义词：imperialists, aggressors|反义词：pacifists, isolationists|词性:名词
Etymology: From "expansionist" + "-s" (plural suffix). "Expansionist" comes from "expansion" + "-ist" (suffix forming agent nouns). "Expansion" derives from Late Latin "expansio," from "expandere" (to spread out).
