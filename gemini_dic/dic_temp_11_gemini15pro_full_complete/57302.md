downstage: /ˌdaʊnˈsteɪdʒ/ | adv. adj. n.| 舞台前方地，舞台前方的；近观众席的 |  Towards or at the front of a stage, closest to the audience. | The actor took a downstage bow. 演员走到舞台前方鞠躬。|  近义词: frontstage | 反义词: upstage | 词性: 副词，形容词，名词

downstage: /ˌdaʊnˈsteɪdʒ/ | vt. | 抢戏，压倒(其他演员) | To draw attention to oneself at the expense of other performers, especially by moving downstage and forcing others to turn their backs to the audience. | He downstaged his co-star by delivering his lines with exaggerated emotion. 他用夸张的情绪念台词，抢了搭档的戏。| 近义词: upstage, overshadow | 反义词: support | 词性: 动词
