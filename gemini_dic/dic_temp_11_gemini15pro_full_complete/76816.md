gamut:/ˈɡæmət/ | n. | 全部，全音阶，整个范围 | The full range or scope of something. | Her emotions ran the whole gamut from joy to despair. 她的情绪经历了从喜悦到绝望的各种变化。| Example: The singer's voice had a remarkable gamut, spanning three octaves.  这位歌手的音域非常广，跨越了三个八度。| Synonyms: range, spectrum, scope, compass, sweep | Antonyms: limitation, restriction | noun

gamut:/ˈɡæmət/ | n. | 色域 | The complete range of colors available on a particular device or in a particular situation. | The printer can reproduce a wide gamut of colors. 这台打印机可以再现很广的色域。 | Example: The painting showcased a vibrant gamut of colors, capturing the beauty of the sunset. 这幅画展示了丰富的色彩，捕捉到了日落的美丽。 | Synonyms: palette, spectrum | Antonyms: monochrome | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Medieval Latin *gamma ut, representing the lowest note of the medieval musical scale (Gamma or G) and the first syllable of the hymn "Ut queant laxis."  Originally referring to the entire musical scale, the word's meaning expanded to encompass any complete range.
