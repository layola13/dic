ne'er-do-well:/ˌnerduːˈwel/
noun
游手好闲的人；败家子
a person who is lazy and irresponsible
例句：He was known as a ne'er-do-well in his youth. 他年轻时是个有名的游手好闲的人。
例句：The family worried that their youngest son would become a ne'er-do-well.  这家人担心他们的小儿子会变成一个败家子。
近义词：good-for-nothing, loafer, idler, wastrel
反义词：hard worker, achiever, responsible person
词性：n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From "ne'er" (never) + "do" + "well."  The term originated in the late 16th century.  "Ne'er" is a poetic contraction of "never."
