coilabilities:/ˌkɔɪləˈbɪlətiz/ | n. | 可绕性，可卷绕性 | The ability of something, typically a wire or other elongated material, to be coiled or wound, often referring to its flexibility and resistance to breaking or cracking during the process.  |  The coilabilities of the copper wire were excellent, allowing it to be tightly wound around the core without any damage.  铜丝的可绕性非常好，可以紧紧地绕在芯上而不会有任何损坏。 |  flexibility, pliability, ductility | stiffness, rigidity | noun
