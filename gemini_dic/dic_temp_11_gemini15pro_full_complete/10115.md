apograph:/ˈæpəɡræf/ | n. | 抄本，誊写本 | A copy, transcript, or excerpt, especially of a manuscript.  A copy made by tracing the original. |  The researcher studied an apograph of the ancient text, as the original was too fragile to handle. 研究人员研究了古代文本的抄本，因为原件太脆弱了，无法处理。 | synonym: copy, transcript, duplicate, facsimile | antonym: original, archetype | noun

apograph:/ˈæpəɡræf/ | n. | 摹本，临摹 | A copy or reproduction of a work of art, especially one made by tracing. | The museum displayed an apograph of the famous painting, allowing visitors to examine the details up close. 博物馆展出了这幅名画的摹本，让参观者可以近距离观察细节。| synonym: replica, reproduction, imitation | antonym: original, master | noun
