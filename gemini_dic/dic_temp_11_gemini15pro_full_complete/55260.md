dissimulour:/dɪˈsɪmjʊleɪtər/ | n. | 伪君子，掩饰者 | A person who conceals their true motives, feelings, or beliefs. | He was a dissimulour, pretending to be their friend while secretly plotting against them. 他是个伪君子，假装是他们的朋友，却暗中密谋反对他们。 | Synonyms: hypocrite, pretender, deceiver | Antonyms: honest person, candid person | noun


dissimulate:/dɪˈsɪmjʊleɪt/ | v. | 掩饰，假装 | To conceal one's true motives, feelings, or beliefs. | She dissimulated her anger with a forced smile. 她强颜欢笑以掩饰她的愤怒。 |  He dissimulated his fear by whistling a cheerful tune. 他用吹口哨来掩饰他的恐惧。| Synonyms: conceal, disguise, mask, feign | Antonyms: reveal, expose, disclose | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dissimulāre, from Latin dissimulāre (“to disguise, conceal, ignore, pretend not to notice”), from dis- (“not, un-”) + simulāre (“to make similar, copy, imitate, feign”).
