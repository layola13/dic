inchoate:/ɪnˈkoʊeɪt/ /ɪnˈkəʊeɪt/
adjective
初步的，未完成的；不成熟的；刚开始的
just begun and so not fully formed or developed; rudimentary
例句：
The painting was still inchoate, a swirl of colors without clear form.
这幅画仍处于初期阶段，只是一团没有清晰形状的色彩漩涡。

Her ideas were inchoate, but full of potential.
她的想法尚不成熟，但充满了潜力。

近义词：rudimentary, nascent, incipient, embryonic, undeveloped
反义词：developed, mature, complete, finished
词性：adjective

inchoate:/ɪnˈkoʊeɪt/ /ɪnˈkəʊeɪt/
noun
inchoateness
未完成；不成熟
The state of being just begun and not fully developed
例句：
The inchoateness of the plan made it difficult to assess its feasibility.
该计划的不成熟性使其难以评估其可行性。

There was a sense of inchoateness about the new government.
新政府有一种不成熟的感觉。

近义词：undevelopment, immaturity, incompleteness
反义词：completion, maturity, development
词性：noun
