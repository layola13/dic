misallocations: /ˌmɪsˌæləˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. | 错误分配；分配不当 | An act of allocating something wrongly or inefficiently.  Resources, funds, or assets being distributed in a way that is not optimal or effective. | The misallocations of resources led to significant economic inefficiencies. 资源的错误分配导致了严重的经济效率低下。

The government's misallocations of funds hindered the development of key infrastructure projects. 政府资金分配不当阻碍了关键基础设施项目的发展。| 近义词: misdistribution, misuse, misapplication | 反义词: proper allocation, efficient distribution | 词性: 名词 (plural)

词源:

mis- (prefix):  Meaning "wrong," "bad," or "lack of." From Old English "mis," meaning "wrongly," "badly," "amiss."

allocation (noun): From the late Middle English "allocacioun," from Old French "allocation," from Late Latin "allocatio," from Latin "allocare"  meaning "to place, allot," from "ad" (to) + "locare" (to place).
