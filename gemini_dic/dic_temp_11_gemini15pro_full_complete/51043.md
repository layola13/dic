desmomyaria:/ˌdɛzməˈmaɪəriə/
| n. | 蚌目; 褶纹蛤目 | A taxonomic order within the class Bivalvia, commonly known as freshwater mussels. These mussels have a unique larval stage called a glochidium, which is parasitic on fish. |  Freshwater mussels of the order Desmomyaria are crucial for maintaining healthy river ecosystems. 淡水蚌目对于维持健康的河流生态系统至关重要。| Synonym: None (This is a specific taxonomic classification) | Antonym: None (This is a specific taxonomic classification) | noun

Etymology:  From Greek *desmos* (bond, ligament) + *mys* (muscle) +  *-aria* (suffix forming nouns denoting groups or assemblages).  The name refers to the ligament and muscle attachments of the shells.
