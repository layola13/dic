dephlegmedness:/ˌdiːˈflɛmdnɪs/
|n.|冷静；沉着；镇定|The state of being calm, composed, and imperturbable.|His dephlegmedness in the face of danger was remarkable. 他在危险面前的镇定令人瞩目。|Synonyms: calmness, composure, equanimity, tranquility, serenity|Antonyms: agitation, anxiety, nervousness, restlessness, excitability|noun


dephlegmed:/diːˈflɛmd/
|adj.|冷静的；沉着的；镇定的|Calm, composed, and imperturbable.|He remained dephlegmed throughout the crisis.  他在整个危机期间保持冷静。|Synonyms: calm, composed, cool, collected, level-headed|Antonyms: agitated, anxious, nervous, restless, excitable|adjective


Etymology:
From de- + phlegm + -ed + -ness.  "Phlegm" in this older sense refers to one of the four humors in ancient medicine, believed to cause sluggishness and apathy.  Removing the phlegm (dephlegming) was thought to result in greater alertness and composure.
