maundy: /ˈmɔːndi/ | n. |濯足节，施舍| A religious rite observed on Maundy Thursday (the Thursday before Easter), commemorating the Last Supper and including the ceremonial washing of the feet of the poor. |  The king performed the traditional maundy ceremony. 国王举行了传统的濯足节仪式。 | synonyms: foot-washing, ceremony, rite | antonyms: N/A | noun

maundy: /ˈmɔːndi/ | adj. |濯足节的| Relating to or characteristic of Maundy Thursday. | The maundy money was distributed to the poor. 濯足节的钱币分发给了穷人。 | synonyms: N/A | antonyms: N/A | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mandé "commandment," from Latin mandatum, neuter past participle of mandare "to entrust, command," related to manus "hand" (source also of English manual, manage, mandate). Originally referring to the commandment of Christ to his disciples to love one another, as expressed in the foot-washing ceremony.
