drolled:/droʊld/| v. |  开玩笑；逗乐； |To make jokes or say funny things, especially in a dry or sarcastic way.| He drolled about his misfortunes. 他拿自己的不幸开玩笑。| Synonyms: joked, jested, quipped | Antonyms: lamented, mourned | Verb

droll:/droʊl/| adj. | 滑稽的；古怪有趣的； | Amusing or funny in a strange or unusual way. | He had a droll sense of humor. 他有一种古怪的幽默感。 | Synonyms: amusing, funny, comical, whimsical | Antonyms: serious, solemn, humorless | Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *droler* (to play pranks), perhaps of Germanic origin. Related to Dutch *drollen* (to be lazy, to trifle) and German *trollen* (to stroll, ramble). The noun *drollery* and adjective *droll* are derived from the verb *droll*.
