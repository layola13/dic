lucubrator:/ˈluːkjʊˌbreɪtər/| n. |刻苦钻研者；苦学者|One who studies or works laboriously, especially at night.  A person who works or studies hard, especially at night.|例句：The lucubrator spent countless nights poring over ancient texts.  这位苦学者花了无数个夜晚钻研古代文献。

例句：Known as a tireless lucubrator, she often worked late into the night.  她以不知疲倦的苦学者而闻名，经常工作到深夜。|近义词：scholar, student, researcher, worker, swot (informal)|反义词：idler, loafer, slacker|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin lūcubrātor ("one who works by lamplight"), from lūcubrō ("I work by lamplight"), from lūx ("light").
