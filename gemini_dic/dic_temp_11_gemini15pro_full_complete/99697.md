interlockers:/ˌɪntərˈlɒkəz (UK), ˌɪntərˈlɑːkərz (US)| n.| 联锁装置；联锁设备；联锁系统|A device or system of devices that prevents conflicting movements or operations, often used in railway signaling or industrial machinery to ensure safety.|The interlockers prevented the two trains from colliding on the same track. 联锁装置防止了两列火车在同一轨道上相撞。|The factory's interlockers shut down the machinery when a malfunction was detected. 当检测到故障时，工厂的联锁装置关闭了机器。|synonyms: safety devices, control systems, interlocking system|antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could consider "disconnected systems" in some contexts)
词性: 名词(plural)

interlocker:/ˌɪntərˈlɒkə(r) (UK), ˌɪntərˈlɑːkər (US)| n.| 联锁装置；联锁设备；(编织)互相交织的东西|A device or system that prevents conflicting movements or operations, often used in railway signaling or industrial machinery to ensure safety. Also refers to something that interlocks or is interlocked, like parts of a machine or threads in a fabric.|The railway interlocker ensures safe train movements. 铁路联锁装置确保火车安全运行。|The intricate interlocker of the knitted fabric created a unique texture.针织物复杂的交织结构创造了独特的质感。|synonyms: safety device, control system, interlocking mechanism|antonyms: (No direct antonym, could consider "disconnected mechanism" in some contexts)
词性: 名词(singular)

词源: From the verb "interlock" + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person or thing that performs a specified action).  "Interlock" itself comes from "inter-" (prefix meaning "between, among, mutually") + "lock" (verb meaning "to fasten or secure").
