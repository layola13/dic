burrow:/ˈbɜːroʊ/ (US) /ˈbʌrəʊ/ (UK)| n. v.|洞穴，挖洞|A hole or tunnel dug by an animal as a dwelling.  To dig a hole or tunnel.|The rabbit disappeared into its burrow. 兔子消失在它的洞穴里。|The moles burrowed deep into the ground.  鼹鼠在地下挖了很深的洞。|近义词：hole, den, tunnel, excavate|反义词: mound, hill|noun, verb

PHRASE->
burrow into something =  钻研某事  She burrowed into her research. 她埋头研究。
burrow down = 躲藏，藏身  The children burrowed down in the hay. 孩子们躲在干草堆里。
burrow deep = 深入挖掘，深入研究 The journalist burrowed deep into the story. 记者深入挖掘了这个故事。
burrow one's head in the sand =  逃避现实 He's just burrowing his head in the sand and hoping the problem will go away. 他只是在逃避现实，希望问题会自行消失。
burrow oneself = 舒适地躺着或坐着 She burrowed herself into the cushions on the sofa. 她舒服地躺在沙发垫上。
