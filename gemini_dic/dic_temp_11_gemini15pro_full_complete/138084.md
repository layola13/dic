normal:/ˈnɔːrml/ (US) /ˈnɔːməl/ (UK)| adj. n.|正常的，标准的；法线；常态|Conforming to a standard; usual, typical or expected.|例句：It's normal to feel nervous before an exam.  考试前感到紧张是很正常的。|近义词：regular, usual, typical, standard, ordinary|反义词：abnormal, unusual, atypical, exceptional|词性:形容词

normal:/ˈnɔːrml/ (US) /ˈnɔːməl/ (UK)| n.|正常；标准；常态；法线|The usual or expected state or condition.|例句：Things soon returned to normal after the storm.  暴风雨过后，一切很快恢复了正常。|近义词：standard, average|反义词：abnormality, anomaly|词性:名词

normals:/ˈnɔːrmlz/ (US) /ˈnɔːməlz/ (UK)| n.|法线（复数）；标准值（复数）|Plural form of "normal," often used in technical contexts, especially in mathematics and computer graphics, referring to lines perpendicular to a surface.|例句：The normals to this surface are all parallel.  这个表面的所有法线都是平行的。|近义词：perpendiculars|反义词：(No direct antonym, depends on context)|词性:名词
