imbecilely:/ˌɪmbəˈsɪlli/
| adv. |
|愚蠢地，痴呆地|
|In a foolish or stupid manner; like an imbecile.|
|He imbecilely grinned at the camera, oblivious to the seriousness of the situation. 他对着镜头傻笑着，完全没有意识到情况的严重性。|

|She imbecilely tried to open the locked door with a carrot. 她愚蠢地试图用一根胡萝卜打开锁着的门。|

|近义词:stupidly, foolishly, idiotically, absurdly|
|反义词:intelligently, wisely, cleverly, sensibly|
|词性:副词|


ETYMOLOGY->
From imbecile + -ly.  Imbecile comes from French imbécile, from Latin imbecillus "weak," from in- "not" + baculum "staff."  Originally meaning physically weak, it later came to mean mentally weak.

PHRASE->
Not commonly used in phrases.  The adverb itself carries the full meaning and doesn't usually combine with other words to form set phrases.  It's more natural to use a phrase like "acted foolishly" or "made a stupid decision" rather than trying to force "imbecilely" into a phrase.
