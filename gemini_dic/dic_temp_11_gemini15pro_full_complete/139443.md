obsolescence:/ˌɒbsəˈlɛsəns/ | n. | 过时，陈旧，淘汰 |The obsolescence of old technology is inevitable. 旧技术的淘汰是不可避免的。|例句：Planned obsolescence is a controversial marketing strategy.  计划性报废是一个有争议的营销策略。

例句：The rapid obsolescence of computers makes it hard to keep up. 电脑的快速更新换代让人很难跟上。

近义词：decline, decay, disuse, depreciation |反义词: modernity, currency,  innovation |词性: noun

obsolescent:/ˌɒbsəˈlɛsənt/ | adj. | 即将过时的，逐渐被淘汰的 | Many of the old machines are now obsolescent. 许多旧机器现在已经过时了。|例句：Obsolescent equipment can pose safety risks. 过时的设备可能会造成安全隐患。

例句：The company invested in new technology to replace its obsolescent machinery. 公司投资新技术来取代过时的机器。

近义词：outdated, outmoded, antiquated, old-fashioned |反义词: modern, current,  advanced |词性: adjective
