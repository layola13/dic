nemertida:/nɛˈmɜːrtɪdə/ | n. | nemertids; members of the phylum Nemertea |Ribbon worms; a phylum of invertebrate animals also known as proboscis worms or rhynchocoels.  They are characterized by their long, slender, unsegmented bodies and their ability to evert a proboscis, a long, tubular organ used for capturing prey.|The biologists discovered a new species of nemertida on the ocean floor. 生物学家在海底发现了一种新的纽形动物。 |Near synonyms: ribbon worm, proboscis worm, rhynchocoel| Antonyms:  N/A (Phyla don't typically have direct opposites.)|Noun
