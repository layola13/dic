kinglessnesses: /ˈkɪŋləsnəsɪz/
n.
无国王状态；无君主状态；缺乏国王或君主的状态
The state of being without a king; lack of kings or a monarch.
The prolonged kinglessnesses in several medieval European states led to periods of instability.  几个中世纪欧洲国家的长期无国王状态导致了不稳定时期。
The historian detailed the various kinglessnesses that plagued the nation throughout its history.  历史学家详细描述了该国历史上困扰它的各种无国王状态。
Synonyms: anarchy, lawlessness, disorder, chaos (in some contexts)
Antonyms: monarchy, kingship, reign, sovereignty
Noun

kinglessness: /ˈkɪŋləsnəs/
n.
无国王状态；无君主状态；缺乏国王或君主的状态
The state of being without a king; lack of a king or monarch.
The kinglessness following the revolution led to a power vacuum.  革命后的无国王状态导致了权力真空。
The country experienced a brief period of kinglessness before a new monarch was chosen.  在新君主被选出之前，这个国家经历了一段短暂的无国王状态。
Synonyms: anarchy, lawlessness, disorder, chaos (in some contexts)
Antonyms: monarchy, kingship, reign, sovereignty
Noun

kingly: /ˈkɪŋli/
adj.
国王般的；帝王般的；高贵的；庄严的
Of, relating to, or befitting a king; regal; noble; majestic.
He maintained a kingly demeanor throughout the ceremony.  在整个仪式中，他保持着国王般的风范。
She wore a kingly robe of purple and gold. 她穿着一件紫色和金色的帝王长袍。
Synonyms: regal, royal, majestic, noble, stately, magnificent
Antonyms: lowly, humble, base, common, ignoble
Adjective

king: /kɪŋ/
n.
国王；君主；最重要的东西；（纸牌、棋子中的）王
A male monarch; sovereign; the most important thing; (in cards and chess) a playing piece.
The king addressed his subjects. 国王向他的臣民讲话。
He is the king of the chessboard. 他是棋盘上的王。
Synonyms: monarch, sovereign, ruler, emperor, tsar
Antonyms: subject, commoner, peasant
Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cyning, from Proto-Germanic *kuningaz ("king"), from *kunją ("kin, family").  Ultimately related to "kin."
