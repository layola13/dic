midshipmanship: /ˌmɪdˈʃɪpmənʃɪp/ | n. | 海军军官候补生身份；海军实习期 | The state or position of being a midshipman; the period during which one is a midshipman; the training or education received as a midshipman.  | He completed his midshipmanship and was commissioned as an officer. 他完成了海军实习期，被委任为军官。 | 近义词: cadetship, apprenticeship | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From mid + ship + man + -ship.  "Mid" meaning middle, referring to their position between the regular officers and the crew. "Ship" referring to the naval vessel. "Man" denoting the individual serving. "-ship" a suffix forming abstract nouns denoting state, condition, or skill.
