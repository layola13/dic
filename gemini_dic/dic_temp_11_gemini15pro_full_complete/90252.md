housebreaker:/ˈhaʊsˌbreɪkə(r)/| n. |破门盗窃者；入室窃贼|A person who breaks into a house or other building to commit a crime, typically theft.|The police caught the housebreaker red-handed. 警察当场抓获了入室窃贼。|Synonyms: burglar, thief, robber|Antonyms: law-abiding citizen|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "house" + "breaker".  "House" refers to a dwelling, and "breaker" refers to someone who forces entry.  The term dates back to the 16th century.
