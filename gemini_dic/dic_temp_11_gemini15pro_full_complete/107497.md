lancewood:/ˈlɑːnsˌwʊd/| n.|矛木，白松木|A tough elastic wood from several trees, especially the New Zealand white pine (Plagianthus regius), formerly used for spear shafts and fishing rods.|The Māori traditionally used lancewood for making spears and tools. 毛利人传统上用矛木制作长矛和工具。|Lancewood is a strong and flexible wood suitable for crafting fishing rods. 矛木是一种坚固而有弹性的木材，适合制作鱼竿。|近义词：white pine, hickory|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

词源: From lance + wood, referring to its traditional use in making lances.
