be-all-ears:/bi ˌɔːl ˈɪərz/
|idiom.|全神贯注地倾听；洗耳恭听|To be very eager and ready to listen to someone.| "Tell me everything, I'm all ears."  告诉我一切，我洗耳恭听。|Synonyms: attentive, listening intently, keen to hear|Antonyms: inattentive, indifferent, uninterested|phrase

Etymology:
This idiom likely originated from the visual image of someone turning both ears towards the speaker, demonstrating their full attention.  The earliest recorded use dates back to the 19th century.
