jabot:/ʒæˈboʊ/ (US), /ˈʒæboʊ/ (UK)| n.|褶边, 胸饰；（鸟类）嗉囊|A decorative frill of lace or other material worn down the front of a shirt or blouse, especially in the 17th and 18th centuries. Also, a pouch of skin hanging from the throat of some birds, such as the pigeon.|The judge wore a jabot with his robes. 法官穿着长袍，戴着褶边胸饰。|Synonyms: frill, ruffle, lace trimming|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, consider plainness or lack of embellishment)|noun

jabot:/ʒæˈboʊ/ (US), /ˈʒæboʊ/ (UK)| n.|嗉囊（鸟类）|A pouch of skin hanging from the throat of some birds, used for storing food.|The pigeon puffed out its jabot.  鸽子鼓起了它的嗉囊。|Synonyms: craw, crop|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|noun
