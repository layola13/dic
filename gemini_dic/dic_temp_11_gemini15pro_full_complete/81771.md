grege:/ˈɡriːdʒ/| n. |畜群，羊群|A flock or herd of animals, especially sheep or goats.  It refers to a group of animals, especially sheep or goats, that live together. While less common, it can also be used to refer to a group of people or things.  It comes from the Latin word "grex," meaning "flock."|The shepherd led his grege to the pasture. 牧羊人把他的羊群带到牧场。|Synonyms: flock, herd, drove|Antonyms: individual, single|Noun
