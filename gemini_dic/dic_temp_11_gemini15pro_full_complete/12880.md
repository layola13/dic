assume: /əˈsuːm/ (US), /əˈsjuːm/ (UK) | v. | 假定，假设；承担；装出，呈现 | to suppose something to be the case, on the basis of probability or plausibility but without proof; to take or begin to have (power or responsibility); to begin to have a particular quality or appearance |  I assumed he was coming, so I was surprised when he didn't show up. 我以为他会来，所以他没来我很惊讶。 | presume, suppose, posit | deny, disavow | verb

assume: /əˈsuːm/ (US), /əˈsjuːm/ (UK) | n. | 假设 | (archaic) An assumption. | His core assume is that all people are inherently good. 他的核心假设是所有人本质上都是善良的。| presumption, supposition | fact, truth | noun (This noun form is archaic and rarely used)
