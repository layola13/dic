melastomaceous:/ˌmɛləˌstoʊˈmeɪʃəs/
adjective
野牡丹科的
Belonging to or relating to the Melastomataceae family of plants, which are characterized by opposite leaves, showy flowers, and often fruit that is a berry or capsule.
The botanist specialized in melastomaceous plants of the Amazon rainforest.  这位植物学家专门研究亚马逊雨林的野牡丹科植物。
Near Antonyms: non-melastomaceous


ETYMOLOGY->
From New Latin Melastomataceae, from Melastoma (genus name), from Greek melas (black) + stoma (mouth), referring to the black stain the fruit leaves on the mouth when eaten.
