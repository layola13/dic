mishmosh:/ˈmɪʃmɑːʃ/ /ˈmɪʃmɒʃ/| n.|混杂物，大杂烩|A confused mixture or jumble of things.|The attic was a mishmosh of old furniture, toys, and clothes.|jumble, hodgepodge, medley, assortment|N/A|noun
Etymology->From Yiddish *מישמאַש* (*mishmash*), from Hebrew *מְשַׁמֵּשׁ* (*məshammeš*), "attendant, sexton".  The original meaning in Yiddish referred to a fruit compote.
