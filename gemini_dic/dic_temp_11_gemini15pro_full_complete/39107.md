confronte:/kənˈfrʌnt/| vt.| 面对；遭遇；对抗|To face someone or something, especially in a challenging or hostile way.  To present evidence or a difficult situation to someone.|例句：
The police confronted the suspect with the evidence. 警察用证据与嫌疑人对质。
She was confronted with a difficult decision. 她面临着一个艰难的决定。

|近义词：face, encounter, challenge, oppose|反义词: avoid, evade, dodge|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French confronter, from Latin confrontare "to compare, bring face to face," from com- "together" + frons "forehead, front."  The original sense was "to compare writings," later "to bring face to face, confront."
