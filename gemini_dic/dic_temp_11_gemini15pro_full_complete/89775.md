hording:/ˈhɔːrdɪŋ/ | n. | 囤积；广告牌 | The act of accumulating and storing a large quantity of something, often excessively. A large outdoor signboard used for advertising. | Example 1: The hording of essential supplies during the pandemic led to shortages.  疫情期间对必需品的囤积导致了短缺。

Example 2: The company invested heavily in hordings to promote their new product.  该公司在广告牌上投入巨资以推广他们的新产品。 | Synonym: hoarding (for act of accumulating): stockpiling, accumulating, amassing. hoarding (for signboard): billboard, signboard, poster board | Antonym: hoarding (for act of accumulating): distributing, sharing, donating | noun


hording:/ˈhɔːrdɪŋ/ | n. | 围板；围栏 | A temporary fence or enclosure, typically made of wooden planks. | Example 1:  A hording was erected around the construction site.  工地周围竖起了一道围栏。

Example 2: The hording protected the newly planted trees from damage. 围栏保护新栽的树木免受损害。 | Synonym: fence, barrier, enclosure | Antonym: opening, gap | noun
