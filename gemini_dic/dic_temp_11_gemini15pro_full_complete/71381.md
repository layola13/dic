flagitate:/ˈflædʒɪteɪt/| v. |恳求，强烈要求|To demand urgently or persistently.|The protesters flagitated for the release of the political prisoners. 抗议者强烈要求释放政治犯。| 近义词：demand, implore, beseech, entreat, importune |反义词: suggest, hint |词性:动词

flagitate:/ˈflædʒɪteɪt/| v. |敦促|To urge earnestly; press.|They flagitated the government to take action against climate change. 他们敦促政府采取行动应对气候变化。| 近义词：urge, exhort, press, push |反义词: discourage, dissuade |词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *flagitatus*, past participle of *flagitare*  "to demand urgently, importune," frequentative of *flagere* "to burn, blaze, glow; be eager, be excited."  The sense evolution is perhaps from the image of a burning desire.
