mooters:/ˈmuːtərz/ | n. | 辩论者；参加模拟法庭的学生 | People who argue or plead a case, especially in a mock court setting.  Participants in a moot court are called mooters. | The mooters presented their arguments clearly and persuasively.  辩论者们清晰而有说服力地提出了他们的论点。

The law school's mooters are preparing for the national competition.  法学院的模拟法庭参赛者正在准备全国比赛。 |  Near Synonyms: debaters, arguers, pleaders, advocates | Antonyms: listeners, audience, judges | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "moot," meaning to raise a question or topic for discussion, from Old English "mōtian" (to meet, discuss, converse).  The noun "mooter" derives from this verb, referring to someone who participates in such a discussion, particularly a legal one.
