martyrized:/ˈmɑːrtəˌraɪzd/ | adj. | 殉难的，折磨的 |  Having been made a martyr, especially having been killed for one's beliefs or having suffered greatly for a cause.  Also, tormented or made to suffer. |  Many early Christians were martyrized for their faith. 许多早期基督徒因其信仰而殉难。

The scientist felt martyrized by the constant criticism of his work.  这位科学家感到他不断受到对其工作的批评而备受折磨。 | Synonyms: persecuted, tormented, tortured, victimized | Antonyms: protected, cherished, comforted | 词性:形容词


martyr:/ˈmɑːrtər/ | n. | 烈士，殉道者 | A person who is killed because of their religious or other beliefs. | Joan of Arc was a martyr.  圣女贞德是一位烈士。| Synonyms: saint, victim, sufferer | Antonyms: persecutor, oppressor, tyrant | 词性:名词


martyr:/ˈmɑːrtər/ | v. |  使殉难，折磨，杀害（尤指因信仰）| To kill someone because of their beliefs. To torment or make someone suffer greatly. |  They were martyred for their faith. 他们因信仰而殉难。| Synonyms: persecute, torture, torment | Antonyms: protect, comfort, cherish | 词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin martyr, from Greek mártyr, meaning "witness". The meaning shifted from "witness" to "one who suffers for a cause" and then to "one who is put to death for their beliefs."
