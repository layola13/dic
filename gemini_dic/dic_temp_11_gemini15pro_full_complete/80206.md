godmother:/ˈɡɒdmʌðə(r)/ | n. | 教母；女性监护人 | A woman who sponsors a child at baptism.  She takes responsibility for the child's religious education and general well-being, especially if anything happens to the parents. | My aunt is my godmother. 我的姑姑是我的教母。

My godmother gave me a beautiful necklace for my confirmation. 我的教母送给我一条漂亮的项链，作为我坚信礼的礼物。


近义词: sponsor, guardian | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun


Etymology:
Old English *godmōdor (unattested), from god "god" + mōdor "mother."  Cognate with Old High German gotmuoter, Old Norse guðmóðir.  The concept predates Christianity, referring to a female sponsor at a non-Christian rite of passage.
