alterate: /ˈɔːltərɪt/ | adj. | 交替的，轮流的 | Occurring or succeeding by turns; alternating. | The trees were planted in an alterate pattern of oak and maple.  橡树和枫树交替种植。| The president and vice-president give speeches in alterate years. 总统和副总统轮流发表演讲。 |  Synonyms: alternating, reciprocal, interchangeable | Antonyms: continuous, constant | adjective


alterate: /ˈɔːltəreɪt/ | v. |  改变，修改（尤指局部地）| To make different in some particular, as size, style, course, or the like; modify: to alter a garment; to alter one's plans. |  She altered her dress to fit better. 她修改了她的衣服，使之更合身。 | He altered the recipe by adding more spices. 他通过添加更多香料来修改食谱。 | Synonyms: modify, change, adjust, amend | Antonyms: maintain, preserve, keep | verb


词源：From Late Latin *alterātus, past participle of alterāre (“to change”), from Latin alter (“other”).
