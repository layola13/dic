mollify:/ˈmɒlɪfaɪ/ (US) /ˈmɒlɪfaɪ/ (UK)| v. |缓和；减轻；安抚|To soothe in temper or disposition; appease: to reduce the severity of (something); mitigate.  |The manager tried to mollify the angry customer. 经理试图安抚愤怒的顾客。|Near Synonyms: appease, assuage, mitigate, placate, alleviate |Antonyms: aggravate, exacerbate, intensify|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin mollificātiō, from Latin mollificāre (“to soften”), from mollis (“soft”) + -ficāre, combining form of facere (“to make, do”).
