groyne:/ɡrɔɪn/ | n. | 防波堤；突堤；海堤 | A low wall or sturdy barrier built out into the sea from a beach to check erosion and drifting. | Example 1:  The groynes along the coast helped to protect the beach from erosion.  海岸线上的防波堤有助于保护海滩免受侵蚀。

Example 2:  Several new groynes were constructed after the severe storms.  几场强风暴过后，建造了几个新的防波堤。 | 近义词: breakwater, jetty, seawall, mole | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps open coast/shore) | 词性: noun


groynes:/ɡrɔɪnz/ | n. | 防波堤；突堤；海堤（复数） | Plural form of "groyne," referring to multiple low walls or barriers built out into the sea from a beach to check erosion and drifting. | Example 1:  The groynes along the coastline are essential for maintaining the beach's width.  海岸线上的防波堤对于维持海滩的宽度至关重要。

Example 2:  The effectiveness of the groynes was evident after the storm, as the beach remained largely intact.  风暴过后，防波堤的有效性显而易见，因为海滩基本完好无损。 | 近义词: breakwaters, jetties, seawalls, moles | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps open coasts/shores) | 词性: noun

词源: From Middle English *groine, from Old French groigne, groin “snout, pig’s snout,” from Late Latin grunium, from Latin grunnire “to grunt.” The sense development is from the projecting shape of a groyne, resembling a snout.
