homini:/ˈhɒmɪnɪ/| n.| (拉丁语) 人; 人类;  (尤指) 男人 | Dative singular of "homo" (man) in Latin.  It is used in phrases like "cui bono?" ("who benefits?" - literally "for what good to whom?") where it represents the indirect object. | Cui bono this crime?  谁会从这桩罪行中受益？| Near synonyms:  viro (man), hominibus (to/for men) | Antonyms:  feminae (to/for women) | Noun (dative singular)
