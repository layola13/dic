crisped:/krɪspt/| adj.|卷曲的，脆的，烤脆的|Made brittle or curly, especially by heat or dryness.  The edges of the leaves were crisped by the sun. |例句：The bacon was perfectly crisped. 培根烤得非常脆。

例句：The crisped edges of the lettuce added a nice texture to the salad.  莴苣脆脆的边缘给沙拉增添了不错的口感。|近义词：crispy, crunchy, brittle, frizzled|反义词：soft, soggy, limp|词性:形容词
crisp:/krɪsp/| v. |使变脆，使卷曲|To make something brittle or curly, especially by heat or dryness.  The cold air crisped the fallen leaves. |例句：The cold wind crisped the leaves. 冷风使树叶变脆了。

例句：I crisped the bacon in the pan. 我在锅里把培根煎脆了。|近义词：toast, sear, char|反义词：soften, moisten|词性:动词
crisp:/krɪsp/| n. |薯片|A thin slice of potato fried until crisp. |例句：I like eating crisps while watching movies. 我喜欢看电影的时候吃薯片。

例句：A bag of crisps is a common snack. 一袋薯片是很常见的零食。|近义词：chip (British English), potato chip (American English)|反义词: 无|词性:名词

词源: From Old English "crisp" and Middle Dutch "crisp," both meaning "curly," ultimately from a Proto-Germanic root meaning "to curl."  The sense of "brittle" developed later.
