/əbˈstruːsər/ | n. | 深奥难懂的事物；故弄玄虚的人 |  Something that is abstruse is difficult to understand because it is very complex and technical. Someone who is abstruse expresses themselves in a way that is difficult to understand. |  The professor's lectures on quantum physics were so abstruse that most students struggled to follow them. 教授关于量子物理学的讲座深奥难懂，大多数学生都难以理解。 He was an abstruse thinker, and his writings were often difficult to decipher. 他是一个深奥的思想家，他的著作通常难以理解。 | 近义词：obscure, esoteric, complex, complicated, recondite | 反义词：clear, simple, straightforward, lucid, perspicuous | 词性: noun

/əbˈstruːs/ | adj. | 深奥的；难懂的 | Difficult to understand. | The subject matter was so abstruse that even the experts struggled to grasp it.  这个主题太深奥了，即使是专家也难以理解。  His abstruse theories on the origin of the universe left the audience bewildered. 他关于宇宙起源的深奥理论让听众感到困惑。 | 近义词：obscure, esoteric, complex, complicated, recondite | 反义词：clear, simple, straightforward, lucid, perspicuous | 词性: adjective

词源:  From Late Latin *abstrusus, past participle of abstrūdō (“hide away, conceal”), from ab (“away, from”) + trūdō (“push, thrust”).
