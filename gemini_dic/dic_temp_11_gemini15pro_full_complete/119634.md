middle-classnesses:/ˈmɪdəl ˈklæsɪznəsɪz/| n.|中产阶级性，中产阶级特质（复数）| The study explored the various middle-classnesses emerging in developing countries. 这项研究探讨了发展中国家正在出现的各种中产阶级特质。| 近义词: middle-class characteristics, bourgeois traits | 反义词: working-class characteristics, lower-class characteristics | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
middle + class + -ness + -es
"middle" (adj.) - Old English middel "middle," from Proto-Germanic *midila- (source also of Old Saxon middil, Old Frisian middel, Old Norse miðr, Dutch middel, Old High German mittil, German mittel, Gothic midjis), from PIE root *medhyo- "middle."
"class" (n.) - late 14c., "category, division, group of students," from Old French classe "category, class, rank" (12c.), from Latin classis "a division of the Roman people; a summons, a calling up," of unknown origin.
"-ness" - Old English -nes(s), -nis(s), -nys(s), a suffix forming abstract nouns from adjectives and participles, from Proto-Germanic *-inassu-, *-nassu- (source also of Old Saxon, Old Frisian -nissi, Dutch -nis, Old High German -nissa, -nissi, German -nis, Gothic -inassus), from PIE *-neuti-, *-noti-, from root *nau- "to die, pretend to be dead" (see naught).
"-es" - plural marker.
