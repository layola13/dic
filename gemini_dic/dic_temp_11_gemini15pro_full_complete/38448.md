concession:/kənˈseʃən/ /kənˈsɛʃən/| n.|让步，妥协；特许权；优惠；承认；租界|Something granted, especially in response to demands; a thing conceded.|The company made several concessions to the workers.  公司对工人做出了几项让步。|synonyms: compromise, allowance, adjustment|antonyms: insistence, refusal|noun

concession:/kənˈseʃən/ /kənˈsɛʃən/| n.|（体育比赛等的）败北；认输|An act of admitting defeat.|The candidate made a concession speech after losing the election.  候选人在败选后发表了败选感言。|synonyms:  admission of defeat, surrender|antonyms: victory, triumph|noun

concession:/kənˈseʃən/ /kənˈsɛʃən/| n.|（尤指在公共场所的）售货摊，营业权；特许经营权|A business operating within a larger enterprise, such as a refreshment stand at a sports stadium.|The airport has a number of fast-food concessions.  机场有许多快餐特许经营店。|synonyms: franchise, license, permit|antonyms: N/A|noun
