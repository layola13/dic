disliking:/dɪsˈlaɪkɪŋ/
n.|不喜欢，厌恶|A feeling of aversion or disapproval.|His disliking of crowds made him avoid busy places. 他不喜欢人群，所以他避开繁忙的地方。|近义词：aversion, distaste, antipathy|反义词：liking, fondness, affection|词性:名词
disliking:/dɪsˈlaɪkɪŋ/
adj.|不喜欢的，讨厌的|Having or showing a feeling of aversion or disapproval.|She had a disliking expression on her face. 她脸上带着厌恶的表情。|近义词：averse, hostile, opposed|反义词：fond, liking, approving|词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *misliken*, from Old English *mislīcian* (“to displease, offend, appear evil”), from *mis-* + *līcian* (“to please, be pleasing”).  The present participle of *dislike*.
