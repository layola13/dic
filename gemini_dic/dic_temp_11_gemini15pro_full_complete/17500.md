bearward:/ˈbeərwərd/| n.|熊管理员，养熊人|A person who looked after and often exhibited a bear, especially in the past.|In the Middle Ages, a bearward often led a muzzled bear through the streets, providing entertainment for the public. 中世纪时期，养熊人通常牵着一只戴着口套的熊穿过街道，为公众提供娱乐。|Synonyms: bear keeper, bear handler|Antonyms: None|Noun

Etymology:
From Old English *berweard*, from *bera* (bear) + *weard* (ward, keeper).
