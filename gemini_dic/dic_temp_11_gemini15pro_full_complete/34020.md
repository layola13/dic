citizenry: /ˈsɪtɪzənri/ /ˈsɪtɪzənri/
noun
公民；国民；市民
The body of citizens of a state or country.  All the citizens of a place considered as a group.

The citizenry is demanding better public services. 公民要求更好的公共服务。
The government has a responsibility to protect the citizenry. 政府有责任保护公民。

Synonyms: citizens, population, inhabitants, residents, public, people
Antonyms:  non-citizens, foreigners, aliens
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From citizen + -ry.  "Citizen" comes from Old French "citézein," ultimately from Latin "civis" (citizen). The suffix "-ry" forms collective nouns, denoting a group or body of people.
