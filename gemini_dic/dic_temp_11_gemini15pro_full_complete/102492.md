jacobin:/ˈdʒækəbɪn/| n.|雅各宾派（成员），激进的民主主义者|A member of a democratic club established in Paris in 1789. The Jacobins were the most radical and ruthless of the political groups formed in the wake of the French Revolution, and they instituted the Reign of Terror.|例句：The Jacobin leader was known for his fiery speeches.  这位雅各宾派领导人以其激烈的演讲而闻名。

例句：He was accused of being a Jacobin and imprisoned. 他被指控为雅各宾派成员而入狱。|近义词：radical, revolutionary, extremist|反义词：conservative, moderate, reactionary|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *Jacobin*, from the Dominican convent of St. Jacques in Paris where the club first met.
