anti-romans:/ˌæntiˈroʊmænz/ | n. | 反浪漫小说；非传统浪漫小说 | Novels that subvert or challenge the conventions of the romance genre, often by focusing on realistic relationships, flawed characters, or unconventional storylines. |  Example 1:  Many contemporary anti-romans explore the complexities of modern relationships, rejecting the idealized portrayals of love found in traditional romances. 许多当代反浪漫小说探讨了现代关系的复杂性，拒绝了传统浪漫小说中对爱情的理想化描绘。

Example 2: The anti-roman often features protagonists who are cynical about love or struggling with personal issues that hinder their ability to form meaningful connections. 反浪漫小说通常以对爱情持愤世嫉俗态度或与阻碍他们建立有意义联系的个人问题作斗争的主人公为特色。 | Near synonyms:  dark romances, unconventional romances, realistic romances | Antonyms: traditional romances, romantic comedies | noun
