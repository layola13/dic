aramaic:/ˌærəˈmeɪɪk/ | adj. n. |阿拉姆语的，阿拉姆语 |A Semitic language, originally of the Arameans, and adopted as the official language of the Achaemenid Persian Empire. It became the common language of Palestine after the exile, and is thought to have been the language spoken by Jesus. |She studied ancient Aramaic inscriptions. 她研究了古代阿拉姆语铭文。|The Aramaic language influenced many other languages in the region. 阿拉姆语影响了该地区的许多其他语言。|Synonyms: Syriac, Chaldean |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a language) |noun, adjective
