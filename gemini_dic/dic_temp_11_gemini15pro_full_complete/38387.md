conceptualising:/kənˈsɛptʃuəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 概念化；将…概念化 | Forming a concept or idea of something. |  She is conceptualising a new marketing strategy. 她正在构想一个新的营销策略。| synonyms: conceiving, envisioning, imagining, formulating, ideating | antonyms:  misunderstanding, misinterpreting | verb (present participle)

conceptualise:/kənˈsɛptʃuəlaɪz/ | v. | 概念化；将…概念化 | To form a concept or idea of something. |  He can conceptualise complex scientific theories. 他能够理解复杂的科学理论。| synonyms: conceive, envision, imagine, formulate, ideate | antonyms: misunderstand, misinterpret | verb

conceptualization:/kənˌsɛptʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 概念化 | The act or process of forming a concept or idea of something. | The conceptualization of the project took several months.  这个项目的概念化花了几个月的时间。| synonyms: conception, formulation, ideation | antonyms: misinterpretation, misunderstanding | noun


Etymology: From late 19th century: from conceptual + -ize.  Ultimately from Latin conceptum ‘something conceived’.
