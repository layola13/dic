dizzies:/ˈdɪziz/ | n. | 头晕，眩晕 | A feeling of dizziness or lightheadedness. |  The roller coaster ride gave me the dizzies. 过山车让我感到头晕。 | 近义词：vertigo, lightheadedness | 反义词: stability, groundedness | 词性: noun (plural)

dizzy:/ˈdɪzi/ | adj. | 头晕目眩的，使人头晕的 | Having or causing a whirling sensation in the head. | The heights made her dizzy. 高度让她头晕目眩。| 近义词：giddy, lightheaded | 反义词: steady, stable | 词性: adjective

dizzy:/ˈdɪzi/ | v. | 使头晕目眩 | To make someone feel dizzy. | The spinning motion dizzied him. 旋转的动作让他感到头晕。 | 近义词：disorient, confuse | 反义词: stabilize, steady | 词性: verb

词源: From Old English *dysig, meaning "foolish, stupid," later evolving to include the sense of disorientation and dizziness.  Related to Old High German tusic "unconscious."
