blowing:/ˈbloʊɪŋ/ (US) /ˈbləʊɪŋ/ (UK)| v. adj.|吹，刮；爆炸；喘气；挥霍|The wind is blowing hard. 风刮得很大。|近义词：gusting, blasting, puffing, squandering (挥霍)|反义词：sucking, inhaling, saving (节约)|verb/adjective


Etymology->
From Old English *blāwian, from Proto-Germanic *blēaną (“to blow”). Cognate with Old High German blājan (“to blow”), Old Norse blása (“to blow”).

PHRASE->
blow out: 吹灭  (e.g., blow out the candles)
blow up: 爆炸;  充气 (e.g., The building blew up. / blow up a balloon)
blow away: 吹走 (e.g., The wind blew away my hat.)
blow over:  (风暴等) 平息; (不愉快的事情) 被淡忘 (e.g., The storm blew over quickly. / The scandal will eventually blow over.)
blow one's mind:  (俚语) 使某人非常兴奋，感到震惊 (e.g., The concert totally blew my mind.)
