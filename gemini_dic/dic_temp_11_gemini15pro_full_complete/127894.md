neogrammarian:/ˌniːoʊˈɡræməriən/ | n. |新语法学家|A linguist who adheres to the principles of neogrammarianism, a school of thought in historical linguistics popular in the late 19th century.  Neogrammarians believed that sound laws operated without exception.|The neogrammarian insisted that sound changes were regular and exceptionless.  这位新语法学家坚持认为语音变化是有规律的，没有例外。|Synonyms: Young Grammarian |Antonyms:  None (lack of a direct antonym, could consider an "anti-neogrammarian") | noun
