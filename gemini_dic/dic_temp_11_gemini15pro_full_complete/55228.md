dissident:/ˈdɪsɪdənt/| n. adj.|持不同政见者，异议人士；持不同政见的|A person who publicly disagrees with and criticizes their government.|例句：The government imprisoned several dissidents. 政府囚禁了几名持不同政见者。

例句：She was a vocal dissident of the regime. 她是一位直言不讳的反对派人士。|近义词：rebel, protester, objector, nonconformist|反义词：conformist, loyalist|词性: 名词, 形容词

dissident:/dɪˈsɪdənt/| adj.|持不同政见的|Publicly disagreeing with and criticizing one's government.|例句：Dissident voices are becoming increasingly common.  异议越来越普遍。

例句：The dissident writer was forced into exile. 持不同政见的那位作家被迫流亡。|近义词：rebellious, critical, opposing|反义词：conformist, supportive, loyal|词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin dissident-, present participle stem of dissidēre "to sit apart, disagree," from dis- "apart" + sedēre "to sit."
