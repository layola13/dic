diameters:/daɪˈæmɪtərz/
|n.|直径；直径长(复数)|A straight line passing from side to side through the center of a body or figure, especially a circle or sphere.|Measure the diameter of the circle.| radii, width, span|circumference|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English diametre, from Old French diametre, from Latin diametrus, from Ancient Greek διάμετρος (diámetros, “diameter”), from διά (diá, “through”) + μέτρον (métron, “measure”).
