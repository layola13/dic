boat:/boʊt/ (US) /bəʊt/ (UK)| n.|船, 小船|A small vessel for travelling over water, propelled by oars, sails, or an engine.|We took a boat trip down the river. 我们乘船沿河而下。|ship, vessel, yacht, canoe|car, airplane|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bāt (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *bōtą (“boat”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰew- (“to swell, grow”). Cognate with Old Frisian bāt, Old Saxon bōt, Old Norse bátr (whence Danish båd, Swedish båt), Dutch boot, German Boot, Gothic 𐌱𐌰𐌿𐍄𐍃 (bauts).
