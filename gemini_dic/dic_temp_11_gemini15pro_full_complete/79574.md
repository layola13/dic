gloomily: /ˈɡluːmɪli/ | adv. | 忧郁地，沮丧地 | In a gloomy manner; sadly and despondently. |  He shook his head gloomily. 他沮丧地摇了摇头。|  She stared gloomily out the window. 她忧郁地望着窗外。 | sadly, despondently, dejectedly, morosely, glumly | cheerfully, brightly, happily | adverb

Etymology:
From *gloom* + *-ly*.  *Gloom* comes from Middle English *glom* "twilight, dusk," likely from a Scandinavian source (compare Old Norse *glāmr* "moonlight").  The *-ly* suffix is a common adverbial ending derived from Old English *-līce*.
