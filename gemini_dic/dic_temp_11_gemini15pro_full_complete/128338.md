neutral-zones: /ˈnjuːtrəl zoʊnz/ | n. | 中立区；缓冲区 | An area between two or more opposing forces in which military activity is forbidden. |  例句：The two armies agreed to establish a neutral-zone between their front lines.  两军同意在前线之间建立一个中立区。

例句：The DMZ between North and South Korea is a well-known neutral-zone.  南北韩之间的非军事区是一个著名的中立区。| 近义词：buffer zone, no man's land, demilitarized zone | 反义词：war zone, battleground | 词性: 名词

neutral-zone: /ˈnjuːtrəl zoʊn/ | n. | (比喻) 中立地带；安全区 | An area or situation where opposing views or forces can coexist peacefully. | 例句：The coffee shop became a neutral-zone for students from different cliques.  这家咖啡店成了不同小团体学生的中立地带。

例句：He tried to maintain a neutral-zone in the family argument. 他试图在家庭争吵中保持中立。 | 近义词：common ground, middle ground | 反义词：contested area, disputed territory | 词性: 名词
