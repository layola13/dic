democratises:/dɪˈmɒkrətaɪzɪz/
verb
使民主化；使大众化
makes something accessible to everyone; removes restrictions based on class or privilege
The internet has democratised information. 互联网使信息大众化。
synonyms: popularizes, makes accessible, opens up
antonyms: restricts, limits, monopolizes
词性:动词

democratise:/dɪˈmɒkrətaɪz/
verb
使民主化；使大众化
makes something accessible to everyone; removes restrictions based on class or privilege
The government is trying to democratise education. 政府正在努力普及教育。
synonyms: popularizes, makes accessible, opens up
antonyms: restricts, limits, monopolizes
词性:动词

democratisation:/dɪˌmɒkrətɪˈzeɪʃən/
noun
民主化；大众化
the action of making something accessible to everyone; the removal of restrictions based on class or privilege
The democratisation of art has led to a wider range of artistic expression. 艺术的民主化导致了更广泛的艺术表达。
synonyms: popularization, liberalization
antonyms: restriction, limitation
词性:名词

democratic:/ˌdɛməˈkrætɪk/
adjective
民主的；民主主义的
relating to or supporting democracy; based on the principle of equality and the right of everyone to participate in decision-making
The country held its first democratic elections in decades.  这个国家几十年来举行了第一次民主选举。
synonyms: egalitarian, representative, participatory
antonyms: authoritarian, totalitarian, dictatorial
词性:形容词
