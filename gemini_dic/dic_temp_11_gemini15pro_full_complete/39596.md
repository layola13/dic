consecratory:/ˈkɒnsɪkrətəri/ | adj. | 奉献的；神圣的；使神圣的 | Relating to the act of consecrating something; making something sacred or dedicating it to a religious purpose. |  The bishop led the consecratory service for the new church. 主教主持了新教堂的奉献仪式。

The consecratory rites involved anointing the altar with holy oil. 奉献仪式包括用圣油涂抹祭坛。

| 近义词：sacred, holy, dedicatory | 反义词：profane, secular | 词性: adjective

consecrate:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/ | v. | 使神圣；奉献；致力于 | To make or declare something sacred; to dedicate something to a religious or divine purpose; to devote oneself to something. | The priest consecrated the new altar.  神父祝圣了新的祭坛。

She consecrated her life to helping the poor. 她一生致力于帮助穷人。

| 近义词：sanctify, hallow, dedicate | 反义词：desecrate, profane | 词性: verb

词源: From Late Latin *consecratorius, from Latin consecrat-, past participle stem of consecrare "to consecrate," from com- "together" (see com-) + sacrare "to make sacred," from sacer "sacred" (see sacred).
