fishwifes:/ˈfɪʃˌwaɪvz/| n. |鱼妇们（fishwife的复数形式）|A fishwife is a woman who sells fish, especially one who is loud and argumentative.  Fishwives were traditionally known for their strong voices and assertive personalities, often used to haggle with customers. The term is now often used figuratively to describe a woman who is loud, boisterous, and quarrelsome.|The fishwives at the market were haggling loudly over prices. 市场上的鱼妇们大声地讨价还价。|近义词：virago, shrew, harridan|反义词:quiet woman, demure woman|词性:noun
