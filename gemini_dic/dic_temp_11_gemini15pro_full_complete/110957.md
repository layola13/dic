liquefacient:/lɪk.wəˈfeɪ.ʃənt/ | adj. n.|液化的，液化剂|Something that is liquefacient causes a solid or gas to become liquid.  As a noun, it refers to a substance that causes liquefaction. |The liquefacient properties of certain gases are crucial for industrial processes. 某些气体的液化特性对工业过程至关重要。| The scientist added a liquefacient to the solid compound. 科学家向固体化合物中添加了液化剂。| 近义词：liquefying, dissolving|反义词：solidifying, congealing|词性: adjective, noun
Etymology: From Latin liquefaciens, present participle of liquefacere (“to liquefy”), from lique- (“liquid”) + facere (“to make”).
