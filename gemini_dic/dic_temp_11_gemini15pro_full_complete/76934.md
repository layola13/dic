gape: /ɡeɪp/ | v. n. | 张口结舌；目瞪口呆；裂开；张开 | to stare with your mouth open, especially because you are shocked or surprised; a wide opening |  The audience gaped at the magician as he performed his trick. 观众张大嘴巴看着魔术师表演他的戏法。 | synonyms: stare, gawk, yawn | antonyms: close, shut | verb

gape: /ɡeɪp/ | n. | 裂口；开口；张口 | a wide opening | There was a wide gape in the fence. 篱笆上有一个很大的裂口。 | synonyms: opening, chasm, crack | antonyms: closure, seal | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *gapa "to open the mouth," from Proto-Germanic *gapaną.
