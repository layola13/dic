hulotheism:/ˌhjuːləˈθiːɪzəm/| n. |万物有灵论；泛灵论|The belief that all things are imbued with a spirit or soul.|Indigenous cultures often practice a form of hulotheism, seeing spirits in trees, rocks, and rivers.|近义词：animism, pantheism|反义词：atheism, materialism|词性:noun
Etymology:  From Greek *hūlos* (ὕλη) "matter, material, wood, timber" + *theos* (θεός) "god".
