curricle: /ˈkɜːrɪkəl/ (US) /ˈkʌrɪkəl/ (UK)
noun
轻便双轮马车；双轮马车
a light, open, two-wheeled carriage pulled by two horses abreast.
The young lord drove his curricle through the park.  年轻的勋爵驾着他的轻便双轮马车穿过公园。
The curricle was a popular mode of transport for the wealthy in the 18th and 19th centuries.  在18和19世纪，轻便双轮马车是富人常用的交通工具。
synonyms: carriage, buggy, cabriolet
antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "walking" or other modes of transport like "sedan chair" in a historical context.)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *curriculus*, diminutive of *currus* ("chariot, car").
