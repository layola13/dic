bugleweed:/ˈbjuːɡəlˌwiːd/| n. |水苏, 野芝麻, 美国薄荷|A low-growing, aromatic plant (Lycopus) of the mint family, with small white flowers. Several species are native to North America and are sometimes used medicinally.|Bugleweed is sometimes used to treat hyperthyroidism. 水苏有时被用来治疗甲状腺功能亢进。|Synonyms:  water horehound, gypsywort|Antonyms: N/A|noun

bugleweed:/ˈbjuːɡəlˌwiːd/| n. |筋骨草|A type of herb used in traditional medicine.|Bugleweed can be made into a tea. 筋骨草可以制成茶。|Synonyms: self-heal, heal-all|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From bugle (a kind of plant) + weed.  The "bugle" part likely refers to the shape of the flowers resembling a bugle horn.
