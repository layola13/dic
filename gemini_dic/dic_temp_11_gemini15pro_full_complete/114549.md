malarias: /məˈlɛəriəz/ | n. |疟疾（复数）| A disease characterized by recurrent attacks of chills and fever, caused by a parasite transmitted by the bite of infected mosquitoes.  It's the plural form of "malaria," but rarely used. | Malaria is a serious health concern in many tropical countries. 疟疾是许多热带国家的严重健康问题。|  The doctor diagnosed him with malaria after he returned from his trip.  医生在他旅行回来后诊断他患有疟疾。 | 近义词: ague (old-fashioned), paludism (technical) | 反义词:  None (diseases don't have direct antonyms) | 词性: Noun


Etymology:  From Italian *mal aria* "bad air," from *mala* "bad" and *aria* "air."  This reflects the former belief that the disease was caused by bad air from swamps.
