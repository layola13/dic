mediacy:/ˈmiːdiəsi/| n.|媒介性，中介性，临场感|The sense of being present or immersed in a mediated experience, as opposed to feeling removed or distanced from it.  It refers to the extent to which a communication medium allows users to feel connected to the other person or the information being conveyed. |The video conferencing software offered a high degree of mediacy, making it feel like we were in the same room.  这套视频会议软件提供了高度的临场感，让我们感觉就像在同一个房间里。|近义词：immediacy, presence, directness|反义词：distance, remoteness, detachment|词性:noun

Etymology:  From "mediate" + "-acy." "Mediate" comes from Latin "mediatus," past participle of "mediare" ("to be in the middle"), from "medius" ("middle").  "-acy" is a suffix forming nouns of state or quality.
