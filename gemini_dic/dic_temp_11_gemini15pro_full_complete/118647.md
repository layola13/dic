messy:/ˈmesi/ (US), /ˈmɛsi/ (UK)| adj.|凌乱的，脏的|Characterized by a state of disorder or untidiness; dirty.|例句：Her room is always messy. 她的房间总是很乱。|例句：He's a messy eater. 他吃饭很邋遢。|近义词：disorderly, untidy, cluttered, chaotic, dirty, slovenly|反义词：tidy, neat, organized, clean|词性:形容词

messy:/ˈmesi/ (US), /ˈmɛsi/ (UK)| n.|邋遢的人; 混乱局面|A person who is habitually untidy or creates disorder; a state of disorder or confusion.|例句：He's such a messy, I can't stand it. 他真是个邋遢鬼，我受不了了。|例句：Cleaning up after the party was a messy.  派对后的清理工作真是混乱。|近义词：slob, slobbery|反义词：neatnik, perfectionist|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *messy, from mess (“a serving of soft food”) + -y (“having the quality of”); related to Old French mes (“portion of food”).  The sense of "untidy" developed later, probably from the association of spilled or dropped food with untidiness.
