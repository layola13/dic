clearstory:/ˈklɪərstɔːri/ (US), /ˈklɪəstɔːri/ (UK)| n.|天窗，高侧窗，透光高窗|A high section of wall containing windows that admits light to the central part of a building, especially a church or hall.  A raised section of roof containing vertical windows.|The church has beautiful stained-glass clearstories.|Synonyms: clerestory, high-level window, skylight|Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, but consider: solid wall, windowless)|noun


clearstory:/ˈklɪərstɔːri/ (US), /ˈklɪəstɔːri/ (UK)| n.|上层建筑，上部结构|A raised structure, such as a railroad track or highway, above the general level of the ground.|The clearstory of the highway offered a great view of the city.|Synonyms: elevated structure, overpass, viaduct|Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, but consider: underpass, subway)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clerestorie, from Old French cler estorie (“clear story”), from cler (“clear”) + estorie (“story, level of a building”).  The spelling shifted from "clerestory" to "clearstory" over time, likely influenced by the literal meaning of "clear story."
