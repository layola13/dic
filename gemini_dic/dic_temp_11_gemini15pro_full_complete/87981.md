high-minded:/ˌhaɪˈmaɪndɪd/| adj.|高尚的，高洁的；眼高于顶的|Having or showing high moral principles or ideals; concerned with noble or lofty thoughts or aims.  Can sometimes imply being impractical or unrealistic.|例句：She was a high-minded woman, always striving to do what was right. 她是一位高尚的女性，总是努力做正确的事。

例句：His high-minded ideals often clashed with the realities of political life. 他高尚的理想常常与政治生活的现实发生冲突。|近义词：noble, virtuous, principled, idealistic|反义词：base, ignoble, mean, selfish|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From high + minded.  "Minded" signifies "having a mind (inclined) to something."  Thus, "high-minded" literally means having a mind inclined towards high or noble things.
