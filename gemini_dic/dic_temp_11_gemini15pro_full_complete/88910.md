hold-from:/ˈhoʊld frʌm/ | phrasal verb | 阻止，扣留，隐瞒 | To refrain from giving or allowing; to withhold. |  He held back the truth from his parents. 他对他父母隐瞒了真相。| synonyms: withhold, keep back, conceal, suppress | antonyms: divulge, reveal, disclose | verb phrase

hold-from:/ˈhoʊld frʌm/ | phrasal verb |  阻止，扣留，隐瞒 | To prevent someone from having or doing something. | The company held back from investing in the new technology.  这家公司没有投资这项新技术。| synonyms: prevent, restrain, hinder, inhibit | antonyms: allow, permit, facilitate, enable | verb phrase


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *healdan from, meaning "to keep from."  "Hold" originates from Proto-Germanic *haldaną, meaning "to hold, keep, guard." "From" comes from Old English fram, meaning "away from, out of."
