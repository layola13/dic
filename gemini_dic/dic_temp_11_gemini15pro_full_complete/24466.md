britishism: /ˈbrɪtɪʃɪzəm/ | n. |英国英语; 英式英语; 英国习语 |A word or phrase that is characteristic of British English.  It is often different from American English or other varieties of English.|  She uses a lot of Britishisms, like "queue" instead of "line". 她使用很多英式英语，比如用“queue”而不是“line”。 | 近义词: Briticism, UK English | 反义词: Americanism | 词性: noun

词源:  From British + -ism.  The suffix "-ism" is used to form nouns meaning a characteristic practice, usage, or expression of a particular group or place.
