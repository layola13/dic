brasseries:/ˈbræsəriz/ | n. |啤酒屋；餐馆|A restaurant serving beer, wine, and other drinks, and usually also offering a variety of simple meals.|The brasseries in Brussels are known for their mussels and fries. 布鲁塞尔的啤酒屋以他们的贻贝和薯条而闻名。|Synonyms: restaurant, café, bistro, pub|Antonyms: None|Noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From French *brasserie*, from *brasser* "to brew," from Old French *bracier*, from Vulgar Latin *brasiare*, from Latin *brasium* "malt."  Ultimately from Gaulish.
