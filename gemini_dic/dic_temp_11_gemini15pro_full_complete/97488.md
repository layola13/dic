ingenuously:/ɪnˈdʒɛnjuəsli/| adv.|天真地；坦率地；真诚地|In a way that is innocent, trusting, and sincere, often without being aware of the potential consequences.  Showing childlike simplicity.|She ingenuously believed that everyone was as honest as she was. 她天真地认为每个人都和她一样诚实。|Synonyms: naively, artlessly, candidly, frankly, innocently|Antonyms: cunningly, deceitfully, slyly, craftily|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *ingenuōsus, from Latin ingenuus (“native, freeborn, noble, frank, honest”) + -ōsus (suffix forming adjectives).  The Latin ingenuus is derived from in (“in”) + gignere, perfect active infinitive genuisse (“to beget, bring forth”).
