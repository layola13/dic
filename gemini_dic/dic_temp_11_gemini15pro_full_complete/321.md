abetting:/əˈbɛtɪŋ/
|n.|教唆；煽动；怂恿|The act of encouraging or assisting someone to do something wrong or illegal.|His abetting of the crime led to his arrest. 他对犯罪的教唆导致了他的被捕。|Synonyms: inciting, instigating, aiding, assisting|Antonyms: hindering, preventing, discouraging|noun
abetting:/əˈbɛtɪŋ/
|v.|教唆；煽动；怂恿|(present participle of abet) To encourage or assist someone to do something wrong or illegal.|He was charged with abetting the thief. 他被指控教唆小偷。|Synonyms: inciting, instigating, aiding, assisting|Antonyms: hindering, preventing, discouraging|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Anglo-French *abeter, from à (to) + beter (to bait).  Ultimately from a Germanic source related to "bite."  The original meaning was to incite (a dog, etc.) to attack by baiting.
