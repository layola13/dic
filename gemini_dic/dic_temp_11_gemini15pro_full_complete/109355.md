一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Minä olen täällä. = I am here. 我在这里。
Hän on opettaja. = He/She is a teacher. 他/她是老师。


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Finnish doesn't have a present continuous tense in the same way as English.  The present tense often conveys ongoing action.
Olen syömässä. = I am eating (literally: I am in the process of eating). 我正在吃。
Hän on lukemassa kirjaa. = He/She is reading a book (literally: He/She is in the process of reading a book). 他/她正在读书。



现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
Olen ollut täällä ennenkin. = I have been here before. 我以前来过这里。
Hän on lukenut kirjan. = He/She has read the book. 他/她读过这本书。



现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
Finnish doesn't have a direct equivalent.  The perfect tense often implies continuous action leading up to the present.
Olen lukenut kirjaa koko päivän. = I have been reading a book all day. 我读了一整天的书。
Hän on työskennellyt projektin parissa viikkoja. = He/She has been working on the project for weeks. 他/她在这个项目上工作了好几个星期。



一般过去时 (Simple Past):
Olin siellä eilen. = I was there yesterday. 我昨天在那里。
Hän oli kotona. = He/She was at home. 他/她在家里。



过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
Similar to present continuous, Finnish uses present participles with past tense "olla" to convey ongoing past actions.
Luin kirjaa, kun hän soitti. = I was reading a book when he/she called. 当他/她打电话时，我正在读书。
Hän katsoi televisiota. = He/She was watching television. 他/她正在看电视。



过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
Olin ollut siellä ennenkin. = I had been there before. 我以前去过那里。
Hän oli lukenut kirjan ennen elokuvaa. = He/She had read the book before the movie. 他/她在电影之前读过这本书。



过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
Finnish doesn't have a direct equivalent.
Olin lukenut kirjaa tuntikausia ennen kuin hän tuli. = I had been reading the book for hours before he/she came. 在他/她来之前我已经读了几个小时的书。
Hän oli työskennellyt projektin parissa kuukausia ennen esitystä. = He/She had been working on the project for months before the presentation. 在演讲前他/她在这个项目上工作了好几个月。



一般将来时 (Simple Future):
Olen siellä huomenna. = I will be there tomorrow. 我明天会在那里。
Hän lukee kirjan. = He/She will read the book. 他/她会读那本书。


将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
Finnish expresses this with present participle and future auxiliary.
Olen lukevinani kirjaa huomenna. = I will be reading a book tomorrow. 我明天会在读书。
Hän on katsovinansa televisiota. = He/She will be watching television. 他/她会在看电视。


将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
Olen lukenut kirjan ensi viikkoon mennessä. = I will have read the book by next week. 我下周之前会读完这本书。
Hän on valmistunut ensi vuoteen mennessä. = He/She will have graduated by next year. 他/她明年会毕业。



将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
No direct equivalent in Finnish.  The future perfect often implies ongoing action.
Olen opiskellut suomea kymmenen vuotta ensi vuonna. = I will have been studying Finnish for ten years next year. 明年我就学了十年芬兰语了。
Hän on työskennellyt yrityksessä viisi vuotta ensi kuussa. = He/She will have been working at the company for five years next month. 下个月他/她就在公司工作五年了。


PHRASE->
(Phrases with "lenni" are less common as it primarily functions as a verb. Here are some uses of the infinitive "olla" which is more flexible):

olla oikeassa = to be right  是对的
olla väärässä = to be wrong 是错的
olla paikalla = to be present  在场
olla poissa = to be absent  缺席
olla hiljaa = to be quiet 安静

注意事项:
Finnish grammar is significantly different from English.  These are simplified explanations.  Word order and other grammatical features heavily influence meaning.  The verb "lenni" is often omitted in the present tense as it is implied by the subject.
