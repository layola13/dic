onychophora:/ˌɒnɪˈkɒfərə/ (US) /ˌɑːnɪˈkɑːfərə/| n. |有爪动物门|A phylum of small, segmented animals, also known as velvet worms, characterized by their soft bodies, numerous pairs of legs, and slime glands. |Onychophora are found in moist habitats, primarily in the Southern Hemisphere. 有爪动物门发现于潮湿的栖息地，主要在南半球。|近义词:velvet worms|反义词: None|词性: Noun

Etymology: From Ancient Greek ὄνυξ (ónux, “claw, nail”) +‎ -φόρος (-phóros, “-bearing”).
