cronian:/ˈkroʊniən/| adj.| 克洛诺斯的，土星的 |Relating to the god Cronus or the planet Saturn.  Cronus was a Titan and the father of Zeus in Greek mythology. Saturn is named after the Roman equivalent of Cronus.  Therefore, "Cronian" can refer to anything associated with either of these figures, often implying age, time, or a slow, ponderous nature.|例句：The Cronian rings shimmered in the distant light.  土星环在远处的光线下闪烁。

例句：He had a Cronian temperament, slow and deliberate in all his actions. 他性情沉稳，做事缓慢而从容。|近义词：Saturnian, Saturnine|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider "Jovian" - relating to Jupiter - as a planetary contrast)|词性: adjective
