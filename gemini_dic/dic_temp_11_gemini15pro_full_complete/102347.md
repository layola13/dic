ixtli:/ˈiʃtli/ | n. |  脸，面容; 眼睛，视觉 | From Nahuatl *iš-tli, meaning "face, countenance," derived from the verb *iši "to see."  It refers to the face or countenance, especially as expressive of character or emotion. In some contexts, it can also mean "eyes" or "vision." | The ancient Aztecs believed the ixtli revealed a person's soul.  古代阿兹特克人相信，ixtli展现了一个人的灵魂。|  The warrior's ixtli was grim and determined.  战士的面容严肃而坚定。 | synonyms: face, countenance, visage, features, look, expression, eyes, vision | antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for a physical feature.  Could consider antonyms for related concepts like "expression," e.g., blankness, impassivity) | noun
