garrulousnesses:/ˌɡærələsnəsəz/| n. |喋喋不休，饶舌，啰嗦|The state or quality of being excessively talkative, especially on trivial matters.  This word emphasizes the habit of talking too much. |His garrulousnesses bored everyone at the party. 他的喋喋不休让派对上的每个人都感到厌烦。|Synonyms: loquacity, talkativeness, volubility|Antonyms: reticence, taciturnity, silence|Noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *garrulusness*, from Latin garrulus (talkative) + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing state or quality).  The root of "garrulus" is related to the sound of chattering or babbling.
