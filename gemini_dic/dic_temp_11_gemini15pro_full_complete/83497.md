hackle:/ˈhækəl/| n. v. |颈羽，骇毛；梳理，激怒|Long, narrow feathers on the neck of a bird, especially a rooster, or the back of a dog; to dress (flax or hemp) by combing out the tow or short fibers; to annoy or irritate someone.|The rooster puffed out his hackles in a display of dominance. 公鸡竖起颈羽以示威风。 He was hackled by the audience for his controversial views.  他因其有争议的观点而被观众诘难。|近义词：plumage, feather, bristle, annoy, irritate|反义词:soothe, calm|词性:名词, 动词

hackle:/ˈhækəl/| n. |(比喻) 脾气，怒气 | State of anger and annoyance. | His hackles rose when he heard the accusation. 他听到指控后怒火中烧。| 近义词：temper, ire, dander | 反义词：calm, composure |词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hakell*, from Old English *hæcele*, from Proto-Germanic *hakelō*, from Proto-Indo-European *kogʰelos* (hook, barb).
