brinkmanship: /ˈbrɪŋkmənʃɪp/ | n. |边缘政策；走到悬崖边缘的策略|The art or practice of pursuing a dangerous policy to the limits of safety before stopping, typically in order to secure an advantage.|The two superpowers came close to war during the Cuban Missile Crisis, a classic example of brinkmanship.|Synonyms: intimidation, coercion, saber-rattling|Antonyms: compromise, negotiation, conciliation|noun
