argues:/ˈɑːrɡjuːz/ (US), /ˈɑːɡjuːz/ (UK)| v. |争论，辩论，论证|gives reasons or evidence to support or oppose something|He argues that the policy will be ineffective. 他辩称这项政策将会无效。|Example: She argued persuasively for her client's innocence. 她为客户的清白进行了有说服力的辩护。|Synonyms: debates, disputes, contends, reasons, asserts|Antonyms: agrees, concedes, accepts|Verb

argues:/ˈɑːrɡjuːz/ (US), /ˈɑːɡjuːz/ (UK)| v. |表明，指示|serves as evidence or proof of something|The high unemployment rate argues for the need for economic reform.  高失业率表明需要进行经济改革。|Example:  His behavior argues a lack of respect. 他的行为表明他不尊重别人。|Synonyms: indicates, suggests, implies, demonstrates|Antonyms: conceals, hides, masks|Verb
