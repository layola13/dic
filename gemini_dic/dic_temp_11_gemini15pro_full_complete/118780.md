metalloid:/ˈmɛt(ə)lɔɪd/| n.|类金属|An element (e.g. arsenic, antimony, or tin) whose properties are intermediate between those of metals and solid nonmetals. They are electrical semiconductors.|Silicon is a classic example of a metalloid. 硅是类金属的典型例子。|Synonym: semimetal|Antonym: metal, nonmetal|noun

metalloids:/ˈmɛt(ə)lɔɪdz/| n.|类金属（复数）|The plural form of metalloid, referring to a group of elements with properties between metals and nonmetals.|Metalloids are useful in the semiconductor industry. 类金属在半导体工业中很有用。|Synonym: semimetals|Antonym: metals, nonmetals|noun
