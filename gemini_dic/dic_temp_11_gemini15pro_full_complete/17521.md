beat-the-air:/biːt ðiː ɛər/| idiom | 白费力气，徒劳无功 | To exert oneself without useful effect; to make futile efforts.  This idiom often implies vigorous but ineffective action, like literally beating the air. | The politician's speech was just beating the air; it didn't address any real issues.  这位政客的演讲只是白费口舌，没有谈及任何实际问题。| 近义词:  futile, ineffective, pointless, useless | 反义词: effective, productive, useful | idiom


beat-the-air:/biːt ðiː ɛər/| v. | 白费力气，徒劳无功 | To make futile efforts; to strive without achieving anything. | He was beating the air trying to convince her to change her mind. 他试图说服她改变主意，但却白费力气。 | 近义词:  struggle in vain,  labour in vain,  spin one's wheels | 反义词:  succeed, achieve, accomplish | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the literal act of striking out at the air, which obviously accomplishes nothing.  The metaphorical meaning developed from this physical image.
