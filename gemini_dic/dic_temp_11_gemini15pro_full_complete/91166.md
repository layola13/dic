hydraemia:/haɪˈdriːmiə/ | n. | 水血症 | A condition in which the blood plasma is abnormally diluted with water, resulting in a reduced concentration of red blood cells and other blood components. |  The patient presented with symptoms of hydraemia after excessive fluid intake.  病人因摄入过多的液体而出现水血症症状。| Synonyms: hemodilution, water intoxication | Antonyms: hemoconcentration, dehydration | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *ὕδωρ (hýdōr, “water”) + *-αιμία (-aimía, "blood condition").
