deniability:/dɪˌnaɪəˈbɪləti/
n.
可否认性
the ability to deny involvement in something, especially something wrong or illegal.
The CIA director insisted on plausible deniability in case the operation went wrong. 中央情报局局长坚持要求在行动出错的情况下有合理的推诿余地。

The politician maintained his deniability in the scandal.  这位政客在丑闻中保持了可否认性。

近义词:disavowability, repudiability
反义词:accountability, responsibility
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From deny + -ability.  Deny comes from Old French denier "deny, refuse," from Latin denegare "to deny, refuse," from de- "away" + negare "to say no."
