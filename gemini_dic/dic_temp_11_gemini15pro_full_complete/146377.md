overflorid: /ˌoʊvərˈflɔːrɪd/ | adj. | 过于华丽的，过于修饰的 | Excessively ornate or flowery in style or language; too elaborate or embellished.  Characterized by an overabundance of ornamentation or figurative language. | Her prose was so overflorid that it was difficult to understand. 她的散文过于华丽以至于难以理解。

The politician's overflorid speech failed to connect with the audience.  这位政客过于华丽的演讲未能与听众产生共鸣。 | 近义词: flamboyant, ornate, flowery, bombastic, rococo | 反义词: plain, simple, austere, understated | 词性: 形容词

词源:  "Over-" (excessively) + "florid" (from Latin "floridus," meaning "flowering, blooming," from "flos," meaning "flower").
