cast-off:/ˈkɑːst ɒf/ (US) /ˈkɑːst ɒf/ (UK)| adj. n.|丢弃的，抛弃的；被遗弃的人/物|Something that has been discarded or rejected.|The charity shop was full of cast-off clothes.  这家慈善商店里满是别人丢弃的衣服。|Synonyms: discarded, rejected, abandoned, secondhand|Antonyms: new, kept, cherished|adjective

cast-off:/ˈkɑːst ɒf/ (US) /ˈkɑːst ɒf/ (UK)| n.|丢弃物，被遗弃的人/物|A person or thing that has been discarded or rejected.|The cast-offs of society often struggle to find housing.  社会遗弃者常常难以找到住所。|Synonyms: outcast, reject, waif, stray|Antonyms:  keeper, beloved|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb phrase "cast off," meaning to throw off or discard.  "Cast" comes from Old Norse *kasta, meaning "to throw."
