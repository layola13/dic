indistinguishing:/ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃɪŋ/| adj.|无区别的，难以区分的|Not distinguishing; making no distinctions; unable to tell the difference.|The twins were so alike that they were indistinguishing to most people. 这对双胞胎长得太像了，大多数人都无法区分他们。|近义词：undifferentiating, indiscriminate, homogenous|反义词：distinguishing, distinctive, characteristic|词性:形容词

indistinguishable:/ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃəbl/| adj.|不能区别的，难以区分的|Not capable of being distinguished or differentiated.|The two shades of blue were almost indistinguishable. 这两种蓝色几乎难以区分。|近义词：undifferentiable, indiscernible|反义词：distinguishable, discernible, distinct|词性:形容词


词源:

in- (not, opposite of) + distinguish (to recognize as different) + -ing/-able (suffixes forming adjectives)

"in-" 源自拉丁语，表示“不，非”；"distinguish" 源自拉丁语 "distinguere"，意为“区分”；"-ing" 和 "-able" 是形容词后缀。
