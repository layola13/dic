overimposing:/ˌoʊvərɪmˈpoʊzɪŋ/ | adj. | 重叠的，叠加的 |  Placing or laying something over something else, especially in such a way that both are still perceptible. It often refers to images or sounds. |  The artist created a unique effect by overimposing two photographs. 艺术家通过将两张照片重叠创造出一种独特的效果。 | Synonyms: superimposing, overlaying | Antonyms: separating, detaching | Word Type: Adjective

overimpose:/ˌoʊvərɪmˈpoʊz/ | v. |  重叠，叠加 | To place or lay (one thing) over another, typically so that both are still apparent. | He overimposed the image of a dove over the cityscape. 他将鸽子的图像叠加在城市景观之上。 | Synonyms: superimpose, overlay | Antonyms: separate, detach | Word Type: Verb

词源：
From over- + impose.  "Over-" signifies "above" or "excessively," and "impose" means "to place upon." Thus, "overimpose" literally means to place something over something else.
