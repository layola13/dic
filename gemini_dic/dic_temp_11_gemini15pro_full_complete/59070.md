dwindle:/ˈdwɪndl/| v. |减少，缩小，衰落|To become smaller and smaller; to decrease gradually.|The town's population has dwindled to a mere few hundred.  这个镇的人口已经减少到只有几百人。

The snow dwindled away as the temperature rose. 随着气温上升，雪逐渐融化消失了。|近义词：decrease, diminish, shrink, decline|反义词:increase, grow, expand|词性:动词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dwinen, from Old English *dwinan (“to vanish, fade, waste away”), from Proto-Germanic *dwinaną (“to disappear, fade, waste away”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰwi- (“to disappear, fade away”). Cognate with Old Saxon dwīnōn (“to vanish, fade, waste away”), Old Frisian dwīna (“to vanish, fade, waste away”), Dutch verdwijnen (“to disappear”), German schwinden (“to disappear, dwindle, vanish”), Old Norse dvīna (“to pine away, languish, dwindle”).
