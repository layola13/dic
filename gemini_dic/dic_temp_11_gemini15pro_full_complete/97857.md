inlet's: /ˈɪnlɛts/ | n. (plural) | 入口，进水口 |  "Inlet's" is a non-standard and rarely used contraction of "inlet is" or "inlets are."  It refers to a narrow passage or opening by which water or air enters a larger body.  While grammatically questionable, it might be used informally. | The inlet's blocked with debris. 入口被碎屑堵住了。 |  Synonyms: entrances, openings, passages | Antonyms: outlets, exits | noun
