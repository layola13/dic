intermediating: /ˌɪntərˈmiːdiˌeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 调解；斡旋；作为中间人 | acting as a mediator between two people or groups |  The bank is intermediating the deal between the buyer and the seller. 银行在买方和卖方之间进行交易调解。|  The lawyer intermediated a settlement between the warring parties. 律师在交战各方之间斡旋达成和解。 | synonyms: mediating, negotiating, arbitrating | antonyms: hindering, obstructing | verb

intermediary: /ˌɪntərˈmiːdiˌɛri/ | n. | 中间人；调解人 | a person who acts as a link between people in order to try to bring about an agreement or reconciliation; a mediator |  The intermediary helped the two sides reach a compromise. 中间人帮助双方达成妥协。|  He served as an intermediary between the government and the rebels. 他担任政府和叛军之间的中间人。| synonyms: mediator, negotiator, go-between | antonyms: antagonist, opponent | noun

intermediate: /ˌɪntərˈmiːdiət/ | adj. | 中间的；中级的 | coming between two stages or levels; lying between two extremes |  The intermediate stage of the project is now complete. 项目的中间阶段现已完成。 | He's taking an intermediate English course. 他正在学习中级英语课程。 | synonyms: middle, median, halfway | antonyms: extreme, beginning, end | adjective
