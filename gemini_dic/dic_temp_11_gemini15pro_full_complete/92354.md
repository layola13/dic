hypocarpium:/ˌhaɪpəˈkɑːrpiəm/| n. |子房柄|The stalk-like or fleshy base that supports the ovary in some flowers and subsequently, the fruit.  It develops from the receptacle or the floral axis.|The botanist examined the hypocarpium of the flower to determine its species.|近义词:carpophore, gynophore (depending on the specific structure)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek  *ὑπό* (hupó, “under”) + *καρπός* (karpós, “fruit”).
