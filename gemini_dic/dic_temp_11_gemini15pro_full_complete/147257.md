overmerrily:/ˌoʊvərˈmɛrɪli/| adv.|过度快乐地，非常高兴地|In an excessively merry or cheerful manner.  It emphasizes an extreme degree of merriment, sometimes suggesting boisterousness or even slight recklessness.|They sang overmerrily, oblivious to the approaching storm. 他们过度快乐地唱歌，没有注意到即将来临的风暴。|Synonyms: exuberantly, boisterously, hilariously|Antonyms: gloomily, morosely, sadly|Word Type: Adverb

Etymology:
From "over" (meaning "excessively") + "merrily" (from "merry," meaning "cheerful" or "joyful"). The Old English root for "merry" is "myrge."
