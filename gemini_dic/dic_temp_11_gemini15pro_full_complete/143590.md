outhurl:/ˌaʊtˈhɜːrl/ |v. |大声喊出，大声说出; 驱逐出境|To shout or speak something loudly and forcefully; to hurl something out or away.|He outhurled his defiance at the judge. 他向法官大声表达了他的反抗。|近义词: yell, shout, bellow, proclaim, expel, banish|反义词: whisper, murmur, admit, welcome|词性:动词

词源:  "outhurl" is a combination of "out-" (meaning "outward, forth") and "hurl" (meaning "to throw with great force").  The "hurl" part comes from the Old French word "hurler," meaning "to howl" or "to make a loud noise," and ultimately from the Frankish *hurla, of imitative origin.
