enclosure:/ɪnˈkləʊʒə(r)/ (UK) /ɪnˈkloʊʒər/ (US)| n.| 附件；围场；封闭物|Something that is enclosed, such as a document or photograph included with a letter, or a piece of land surrounded by a fence or wall.|The letter came with several enclosures. 这封信附带了几个附件。|The enclosure kept the animals safe from predators.  围栏保护动物免受捕食者的侵害。|synonyms: attachment, appendix, supplement, compound, paddock, field|antonyms: exclusion, release, opening|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *enclos, from Anglo-Norman enclos, past participle of enclore (“to enclose”), from Latin inclūdere (“to shut in, enclose”), from in- (“in, into”) + claudere, clūdere (“to shut, close”).
