deport:/dɪˈpɔːrt/ (US) /dɪˈpɔːt/ (UK)| vt.|驱逐出境，放逐|to force someone to leave a country, usually because they have broken the law or their permission to stay has expired|The government deported the illegal immigrants. 政府驱逐了非法移民。

The journalist was deported for writing articles critical of the government. 这名记者因撰写批评政府的文章而被驱逐出境。

|近义词：expel, banish, exile|反义词：admit, allow entry|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *deporter, from Latin deportare "to carry away, remove, banish," from de- "away" + portare "to carry."
