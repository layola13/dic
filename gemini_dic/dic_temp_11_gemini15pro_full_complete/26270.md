buttonholer: /ˈbʌtnˌhoʊlər/ (US), /ˈbʌtnˌhəʊlə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 扣眼机；强留别人说话的人|A person who detains someone in conversation, especially about a tedious subject. |My uncle is a terrible buttonholer – he trapped me for half an hour talking about his stamp collection.|Synonym: bore, pest |Antonym: listener |noun


buttonholer: /ˈbʌtnˌhoʊlər/ (US), /ˈbʌtnˌhəʊlə(r)/ (UK)| v.|  用扣眼机缝制；强留别人说话|To detain someone in conversation, especially about a tedious subject. |He buttonholed me as I was trying to leave the party. |Synonym: accost, detain, corner |Antonym: release, free |verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "buttonhole" (n.) + "-er" (suffix forming agent nouns). "Buttonhole" refers to the slit in fabric for fastening a button, from "button" + "hole."
