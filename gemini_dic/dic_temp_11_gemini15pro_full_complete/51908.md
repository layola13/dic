dhoti:/ˈdoʊti/ | n. |  缠腰布，兜裆布 | A loincloth worn by men in India, Bangladesh, and some other parts of South Asia, consisting of a long piece of cloth wrapped around the waist and legs. |  He wore a crisp white dhoti. 他穿着一件干净的白缠腰布。| Synonym: loincloth, lunghi | Antonym:  trousers, pants | noun

dhooties:/ˈdoʊtiz/ | n. | 缠腰布（dhoti的复数形式） | Plural form of dhoti.  |  Many men in the village still wear dhooties. 村里很多男人仍然穿缠腰布。 | Synonym: loincloths, lungis | Antonym: trousers, pants | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Hindi धोती (dhotī), from Sanskrit धौती (dhautī), feminine past participle of धावति (dhāvati, “to wash, cleanse”).  The garment was originally meant to be washed frequently for hygienic purposes.
