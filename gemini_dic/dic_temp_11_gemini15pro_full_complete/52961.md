dingy:/ˈdɪŋi/ (US), /ˈdɪŋɡi/ (UK)| n. |小艇，橡皮艇；肮脏的，邋遢的(主要指地方)|A small open boat, often carried or towed by a larger vessel; Shabby and dismal (referring primarily to a place).|例句：The sailors used a dingy to get ashore. 水手们用一艘小艇上岸。

例句：The apartment was small and dingy. 这间公寓又小又脏。|近义词：(boat) skiff, rowboat, inflatable; (shabby) grimy, grubby, seedy|反义词：(boat) ship, yacht; (shabby) clean, spotless, pristine|词性:名词


dingies:/ˈdɪŋiz/ (US), /ˈdɪŋɡiz/ (UK)| n. |小艇，橡皮艇(复数)；肮脏的，邋遢的地方(复数)|Plural of dingy. Small open boats, often carried or towed by a larger vessel; Shabby and dismal places (plural).|例句：Several dingies bobbed in the harbor. 港口里漂着几艘小艇。


例句：They avoided the dingies down by the docks. 他们避开了码头边那些脏乱的地方。|近义词：(boats) skiffs, rowboats, inflatables; (shabby places) slums, dives|反义词：(boats) ships, yachts; (shabby places) mansions, palaces|词性:名词


词源: From Hindi  डिंगी (ḍingī), from Marathi  डिंगी (ḍingī).  Ultimately probably of Dravidian origin.
