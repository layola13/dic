landlubberlinesses:/ˌlændˈlʌbərlɪnəsɪz/ | n. | 陆地人的笨拙，不谙航海 | The extreme awkwardness and unfamiliarity with seafaring matters, characteristic of a landlubber.  |  His landlubberlinesses were obvious as he stumbled around the deck of the ship.  他笨手笨脚地在甲板上蹒跚而行，一看就知道是个旱鸭子。 |  clumsiness, awkwardness, ineptitude | seamanship, nautical skill | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From landlubber + -liness + -es (plural suffix).  Landlubber is a compound of land and lubber (a clumsy or lazy person).
