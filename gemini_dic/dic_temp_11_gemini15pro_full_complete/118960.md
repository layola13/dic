meteors:/ˈmiːtiərz/ (US), /ˈmiːtiɔːz/ (UK)| n. |流星|A small body of matter from outer space that enters the earth's atmosphere, becoming incandescent as a result of friction and appearing as a streak of light.|We watched the meteors shower across the night sky. 我们看着流星雨划过夜空。|Near synonyms: shooting star, falling star|Antonyms: N/A (Meteors don't have a direct opposite)|Noun
