elegist:/ˈɛlɪdʒɪst/
n.
挽歌诗人；写挽歌的人
A writer of elegies, especially one who writes a mournful poem to commemorate someone who has died.
The elegist captured the profound grief of the nation in his poem. 这位挽歌诗人用他的诗歌捕捉了整个国家的深切悲痛。

Thomas Gray is considered one of the greatest elegists of English literature. 托马斯·格雷被认为是英国文学史上最伟大的挽歌诗人之一。

近义词:poet of elegies, writer of dirges, lamenter
反义词:None (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑celebrant, eulogist)
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin elegista, from Latin elegia (“elegy”) + -ista (“-ist”).  The Latin word elegia comes from Greek ἐλεγεία (elegeia), from ἔλεγος (elegos, “song of mourning”).
