loather:/ˈloʊðər/| n.|憎恨者，厌恶者|A person who intensely dislikes someone or something.|He is a loather of all things modern. 他厌恶所有现代的东西。|近义词：hater, detester, abhorrer|反义词:lover, admirer|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lothere*, from Old English *lāðere* (“one who hates”), from *lāð* (“hateful, hostile; loathsome, disgusting”) + *-ere* (agent suffix). Cognate with Old Frisian *lāthere* (“hater”), Old Saxon *lēthari* (“hater”), Old High German *lēidari* (“hater”), Old Norse *leiðari* (“hater”). Related to loath.
