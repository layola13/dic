crabbier:/ˈkræbiər/ | adj. | 更暴躁的，更易怒的 |Comparative form of "crabby," meaning more irritable, grumpy, or ill-tempered. |  He's been much crabbier since he lost his job.  他自从失业后变得更加暴躁了。|  She gets crabbier when she's tired. 她累的时候脾气更暴躁。 | Synonyms: grumpier, moodier, more irritable, more irascible | Antonyms: happier, more cheerful, more pleasant, more agreeable | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From "crabby" + "-er" (comparative suffix). "Crabby" likely originated from the perceived sideways, awkward movement of crabs, which was metaphorically extended to describe a person's irritable or grumpy demeanor.
