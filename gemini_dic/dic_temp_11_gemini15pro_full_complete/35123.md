cloudily:/ˈklaʊdəli/| adv.|阴沉地；模糊地；朦胧地|In a cloudy manner; dimly; obscurely.  It describes a state or condition that is characterized by clouds, either literally in the sky or figuratively in terms of understanding or perception. |The sun shone cloudily through the mist. 太阳阴沉地透过薄雾照耀着。|近义词：overcastly, dimly, vaguely|反义词：brightly, clearly|词性:副词
Etymology: From cloud + -y + -ly.  "Cloud" comes from Old English clūd, meaning "mass of rock, hill."  The meaning shifted to "rain cloud" likely due to the resemblance of cumulus clouds to mountains.  The suffix "-y" forms adjectives, and "-ly" forms adverbs.
