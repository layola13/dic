bolthole:/ˈbəʊlthəʊl/ (UK) /ˈboʊlˌhoʊl/ (US)| n. | 藏身处；避难所；安全屋|A place of refuge or safety, especially one that is secret or secluded.| He kept a bolthole in the countryside in case of emergency. 他在乡下有一个藏身处，以防万一。|Example: The dictator fled to his bolthole in the mountains. 独裁者逃到了他在山中的藏身处。

Example:  She found a bolthole in the library where she could study in peace. 她在图书馆里找到了一个可以安静学习的藏身之处。|近义词：hideout, refuge, sanctuary, retreat|反义词:  exposure, vulnerability|词性: 名词

bolthole:/ˈbəʊlthəʊl/ (UK) /ˈboʊlˌhoʊl/ (US)| n. | (动物的)洞穴，巢穴|A hole or burrow used by an animal as a refuge.|The rabbit darted into its bolthole. 兔子冲进了它的洞穴。|Example: The fox retreated to its bolthole after being chased by hounds. 狐狸被猎犬追赶后，退回到它的洞穴里。

Example: The badger had a network of boltholes throughout the woods. 獾在整个树林里都有一个洞穴网络。 |近义词：burrow, den, lair|反义词:  openness, exposure|词性: 名词
