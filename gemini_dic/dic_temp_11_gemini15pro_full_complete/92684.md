iceberg:/ˈaɪsbɜːrɡ/| n.|冰山|A large mass of ice floating in the sea, the greater part of which is below the surface.|The Titanic sank after hitting an iceberg. 泰坦尼克号撞到冰山后沉没了。|近义词：glacier, ice floe|反义词: (no direct antonym, perhaps 'molten ice' or 'water')|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Dutch *ijsberg*, from ijs "ice" + berg "mountain". Cognate with German Eisberg.
