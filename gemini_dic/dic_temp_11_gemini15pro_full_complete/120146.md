minced:/mɪnst/| adj.| 切碎的；剁碎的；委婉的；矫揉造作的|cut up or ground into very small pieces; (of speech or manner) affectedly dainty or refined|  The recipe calls for minced garlic.  这道菜需要蒜末。|Synonyms: chopped, ground, pulverized, diced, shredded, comminuted; (for speech) affected, artificial, precious, euphemistic|Antonyms: whole, intact; (for speech) natural, genuine, sincere|adjective

mince:/mɪns/| v.| 切碎；剁碎；碎步走；装腔作势地说|cut up or grind (food, especially meat) into very small pieces; walk with short quick steps in an affectedly dainty way; speak affectedly or indirectly| She minced the garlic finely. 她把大蒜切得很细。|Synonyms: chop, grind, pulverize, dice, shred, comminute; (for walking) tiptoe, teeter, totter; (for speaking) euphemize, prevaricate|Antonyms: (for food) leave whole; (for walking) stride; (for speaking) be direct, be frank|verb
