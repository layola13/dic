ingrain:/ɪnˈɡreɪn/ /ɪnˈɡreɪn/ |v. adj.|使根深蒂固；根深蒂固的|To fix or establish (a habit, belief, or attitude) deeply and firmly; firmly fixed or established.|例句：His prejudices are deeply ingrained. 他的偏见根深蒂固。|近义词：embed, implant, instill, root|反义词：eradicate, uproot|词性: 动词, 形容词

ingrain:/ˈɪnɡreɪn/ /ˈɪnɡreɪn/ |n.|固有性质；原纱染色|An ingrained habit, belief, or attitude; yarn dyed before weaving.|例句：The ingrain of his personality made it difficult for him to change. 他根深蒂固的个性使他难以改变。|近义词：nature, essence|反义词：-|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From in- + grain (noun).  Originally referred to dyeing yarn before weaving, hence deeply ingrained color.  Figurative sense of "firmly fixed" developed from this.
