assimilationist: /əˌsɪməˈleɪʃənɪst/
n. adj.|同化主义者；同化主义的|A person who advocates or participates in cultural assimilation.  Someone who believes in or promotes the policy of absorbing a minority group into the dominant culture, often at the expense of the minority group's own traditions and identity.|The government's assimilationist policies led to the suppression of the native language.|近义词：integrationist, conformist|反义词：separatist, multiculturalist|词性: noun, adjective

assimilationist: /əˌsɪməˈleɪʃənɪst/
adj.|同化主义的|Relating to or supporting assimilation, especially cultural assimilation.|The assimilationist agenda of the boarding schools aimed to erase Native American cultures.|近义词：integrationist, conformist|反义词：separatist, multiculturalist|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From assimilation + -ist.  Assimilation comes from Late Latin assimilātiōn- (stem of assimilātiō), from assimilāre "to make similar," from ad- "to" + similāre "to make like," from similis "like."
