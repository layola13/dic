catarrhal:/kəˈtɑːrəl/ (US), /kəˈtɑːr(ə)l/ (UK)| adj.|卡他性的，粘膜炎的|Relating to or characterized by catarrh, an excessive discharge or build-up of mucus in the nose or throat.|The child had a catarrhal cough.  这孩子咳嗽有痰。|Synonyms: mucous, phlegmy, congested|Antonyms: dry, clear|Adjective


catarrhally:/kəˈtɑːrəli/ (US), /kəˈtɑːrəli/ (UK)| adv.|卡他地，患粘膜炎地|In a catarrhal manner; with catarrh.|He was coughing catarrhally. 他咳嗽带痰。|Synonyms: mucusly, phlegmily|Antonyms: dryly, clearly|Adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *catarrhālis*, from Greek katárrhous "flowing down," from katá "down" + rhein "to flow."
