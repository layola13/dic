deontologist:/ˌdiːɒnˈtɒlədʒɪst/ /ˌdiːɑːnˈtɑːlədʒɪst/
n.
道义论者，义务论者
A person who believes in deontology, a system of ethics that judges the morality of an action based on the action's adherence to a rule or rules.
A deontologist would argue that lying is always wrong, regardless of the consequences.  道义论者会认为说谎总是错的，不管后果如何。
The deontologist refused to sanction the mission, even though it could save lives, because it involved torture.  即使这项任务可以拯救生命，但因为它涉及酷刑，这位道义论者拒绝批准。
Synonyms: moralist, ethicist
Antonyms: consequentialist, utilitarian
