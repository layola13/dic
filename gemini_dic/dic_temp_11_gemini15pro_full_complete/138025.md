noosed:/nuːst/ | adj. | 被套索的，被绞死的 | Having a noose around something, especially the neck; hanged with a noose. | The noosed prisoner awaited his fate.  被套索的囚犯等待着他的命运。|  Synonym: hanged, strangled | Antonym: freed, released | adjective

noosed:/nuːst/ | v. | 用套索套住 | To secure or catch with a noose. | The cowboy noosed the calf. 牛仔用套索套住了小牛。 | Synonym: lassoed, roped | Antonym: released, freed | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From noose + -ed.  Noose comes from Middle English *noose, from Old French *nos, ultimately from Latin *nodus "knot."
