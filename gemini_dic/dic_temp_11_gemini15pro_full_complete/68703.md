farers:/ˈfɛərərz/| n.|旅客，航海者|People who travel, especially by sea or air. |例句：The ancient mariners were skilled farers. 古代水手是熟练的航海者。

例句：The farers disembarked at dawn. 旅客们在黎明时分下船。|近义词：travelers, voyagers, sailors, passengers|反义词:stay-at-homes, residents|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *faran "to journey, travel," from Proto-Germanic *faranan (source also of Old Saxon, Old Frisian faran "to travel, fare," Dutch varen "to sail, travel," Old High German faran "to go, travel, behave, lead," German fahren "to drive, travel, go"), from PIE root *per- (2) "to lead, pass over." The -er suffix denotes an agent or one who performs the action.
