empire: /ˈempaɪər/ (US) /ˈempaɪə(r)/ (UK)| n. |帝国；大企业|A group of nations or peoples ruled over by an emperor, empress, or other powerful sovereign or government: usually a territory of greater extent than a kingdom, as the former British Empire.|The Roman Empire was vast and powerful.| 近义词: kingdom, realm, domain, dominion | 反义词: colony, dependency | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From Old French *empire* "authority, power, rule," from Latin *imperium* "command, power, authority," from *imperare* "to command," literally "to procure, obtain."
