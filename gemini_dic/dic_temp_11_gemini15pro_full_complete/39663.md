conservator:/kənˈsɜːrvətər/ | n. | 保护者，管理员，文物修复师 | a person who protects something, especially something of historical or artistic value  |  The museum hired a conservator to restore the ancient scrolls.  博物馆聘请了一位文物修复师来修复古代卷轴。 | synonyms: guardian, custodian, protector, preserver | antonyms: destroyer, vandal | noun

conservator:/kənˈsɜːrvətər/ | n. | (法律) 保护人，监护人 | a person appointed by a court to manage the financial affairs of someone who is legally incapable of doing so themselves  | The court appointed a conservator to oversee the elderly woman's finances.  法院指定了一名监护人来监督这位老妇人的财务。 | synonyms: guardian, trustee | antonyms: ward | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *conservator, from Latin conservare (“to preserve”), from con- (“together”) + servare (“to keep, guard”).
