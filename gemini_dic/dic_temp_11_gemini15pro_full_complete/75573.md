fuddies:/ˈfʌdiz/| n.|老古董，守旧派（通常指老年人）|People who are old-fashioned and resistant to change, often used humorously or affectionately to refer to older people.  It implies a gentle teasing or affectionate disapproval of their outdated views or habits.| My grandparents are such fuddies; they still think email is a newfangled invention. 我的祖父母真是老古董；他们仍然认为电子邮件是一种新奇的发明。|synonyms: conservatives, traditionalists, stick-in-the-muds| antonyms: modernists, progressives, radicals| noun

Etymology:  Likely a playful alteration of "fusty," which means stale-smelling or old-fashioned. "Fusty" itself comes from the Old French word "fust," meaning "tasting of the cask." This suggests a connection to the idea of something being old and stored away for too long.
