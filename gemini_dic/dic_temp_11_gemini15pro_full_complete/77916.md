genitival: /dʒɪˈnɪtɪvəl/ | adj. |属格的，所有格的 |Of, relating to, or designating a grammatical case that expresses possession, origin, or source. |  The genitival form of "dog" is "dog's." 狗的属格形式是“dog's”。|  The apostrophe-s ('s) is a common marker of the genitival case in English.  撇号s ('s)是英语中属格的常见标记。| synonyms: possessive | antonyms: N/A | word type: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *genitivus, from Latin genitivus (casus) “(case) of origin or birth,” from genitus “born, produced,” past participle of gignere “to beget.”
