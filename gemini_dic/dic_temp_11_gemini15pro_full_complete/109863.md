/wɒt/ (UK) /wʌt/ (US) |pron.|什么，多么，多少|Used to ask for information about people or things.|What is your name? 你叫什么名字？|Which, who | |pronoun|

/hwɒt/ (UK) /hwʌt/ (US) |det. |什么，哪一个，哪一些|Used with nouns to ask questions.|What color is your car? 你的车是什么颜色的？|Which | |determiner|

/wɒt/ (UK) /wʌt/ (US) |int. |什么？|Expressing surprise or disbelief.|What! You won the lottery? 什么！你中了彩票？| | |interjection|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hwæt, neuter singular nominative and accusative of hwā "who," from Proto-Germanic *hwat, from Proto-Indo-European *kwod. Cognate with Old Frisian hwet, Old Saxon hwat, Old High German hwaz, Old Norse hvat, Gothic hva, Latin quod "what," quod "which, that," quid "what," Sanskrit kad.
