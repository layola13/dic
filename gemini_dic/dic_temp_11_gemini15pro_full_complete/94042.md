imparters:/ɪmˈpɑːrtərz/
| n. |传授者，赋予者|People or things that impart something, such as knowledge, qualities, or characteristics. |The imparters of knowledge played a crucial role in shaping young minds. 知识的传授者在塑造年轻人的思想方面发挥了至关重要的作用。|Synonyms: teachers, instructors, educators, givers, bestowers |Antonyms: receivers, takers, recipients|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "impart," which comes from the Old French "impartir" and ultimately from the Latin "impartire," meaning "to share," "to give a part of."  The suffix "-er" denotes a person or thing that performs the action of the verb.
