conformer:/kənˈfɔːrmər/ | n. |构象异构体，构象体| A conformer is one of the several isomers of a molecule that can be interconverted exclusively by rotations around formally single bonds.  It refers to a specific three-dimensional arrangement of atoms in a molecule that results from rotation about single bonds. |The different conformers of butane have different energies.| 近义词: isomer, rotamer | 反义词:  (No direct antonym) | noun

conformation:/ˌkɒnfɔːˈmeɪʃn/ | n. |构象，构造| The three-dimensional arrangement of atoms in a molecule, especially of a large or flexible molecule.  It also refers to the general shape or structure of something. |The protein's conformation is essential for its function.| 近义词: structure, shape, form | 反义词:  (No direct antonym) | noun

conformational:/ˌkɒnfɔːˈmeɪʃənl/ | adj. |构象的| Relating to or involving the conformation of a molecule. | Conformational changes in proteins can affect their activity.| 近义词: structural, steric | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | adjective
