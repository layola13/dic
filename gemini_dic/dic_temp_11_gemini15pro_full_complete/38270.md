conversing:/kənˈvɜːrsɪŋ/ | v. | 交谈，谈话 | engaging in conversation; talking. | They were conversing quietly in the corner. 他们在角落里安静地交谈。|  We were conversing about the latest news. 我们正在谈论最新的新闻。 |近义词：talking, chatting, discussing, speaking |反义词：silent, mute | 词性: 动词 (present participle)

Etymology:
From Old French *converser, from Latin conversārī (“to live with, keep company with, dwell, abide, stay, sojourn, remain, tarry; to associate, frequent; to be occupied or busied; to conduct oneself, behave, act; to be versed in, be familiar with, be engaged in; to spend time, pass or employ time”), present active infinitive of conversor (“I turn around, turn about, turn back, return; I move about, dwell, live; I associate with, frequent; I am occupied or busied; I conduct myself, behave, act; I am versed in, am familiar with; I spend time, pass or employ time”), from con- +‎ versor (“I turn frequently, turn myself about, twist, roll, revolve; I am engaged or occupied”), frequentative of vertō (“I turn”).
