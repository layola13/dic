bandolier:/ˌbændəˈlɪr/ | n. | 子弹带，弹药带 | A belt worn over the shoulder and across the chest, typically used to carry ammunition. | The soldier wore a bandolier across his chest, loaded with bullets. 士兵胸前挎着一条装满子弹的子弹带。 | The cowboy adjusted his bandolier before the gunfight. 牛仔在枪战前调整了他的子弹带。 | synonyms: ammunition belt, cartridge belt | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could consider "holster" as it holds a single weapon rather than many rounds) | noun

bandolier:/ˌbændəˈlɪr/ | n. | (旧)绶带 | A shoulder sash worn as a badge of office or membership. (This meaning is now largely archaic.) |  The mayor wore a bandolier as a symbol of his authority. 市长佩戴绶带作为其权威的象征。(古旧用法) |  The members of the secret society were recognized by their distinctive bandoliers. 秘密社团的成员可以通过其独特的绶带被识别出来。(古旧用法) | synonyms: sash, ribbon, baldric | antonyms: (No direct antonym) | noun
