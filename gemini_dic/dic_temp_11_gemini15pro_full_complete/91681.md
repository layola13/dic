hypercoagulability:/ˌhaɪpərkoʊˌæɡjələˈbɪləti/
n.
高凝状态
A condition in which there is an increased tendency for the blood to clot.
The patient's hypercoagulability put him at risk for a stroke. 病人的高凝状态使他面临中风的风险。

hypercoagulable:/ˌhaɪpərkoʊˌæɡjələbəl/
adj.
高凝的
Characterized by an increased tendency of the blood to clot.
The doctor prescribed blood thinners because the patient was hypercoagulable. 医生开了血液稀释剂，因为病人血液高凝。

Etymology:
hyper- (Greek): over, excessive
coagul- (Latin coagulare): to curdle, clot
-able (suffix): capable of, susceptible of
-ity (suffix): state, quality

Synonyms:
thrombophilia, prothrombotic state

Antonyms:
hypocoagulability, bleeding tendency
