cozy-up:/ˈkoʊzi ʌp/| phrasal verb |  亲近；依偎；巴结；奉承 | To make oneself close and comfortable with someone, often for personal gain.  To act in a friendly way towards someone, especially someone important, so that they will do what you want. | The politician cozied up to the union leader in hopes of winning the election. 这位政客为了赢得选举而巴结工会领袖。| synonyms: snuggle up, cuddle up, ingratiate oneself with | antonyms: distance oneself from, alienate | verb phrase

cozy-up:/ˈkoʊzi ʌp/| phrasal verb |  变得舒适；使舒适 | To make a place or situation warm and comfortable. | I cozied up by the fire with a good book. 我拿着一本好书依偎在火炉旁，感到很舒适。 | synonyms: nestle, settle in | antonyms:  - | verb phrase
