ostracod:/ˈɒstrəkɒd/ | n. |介形虫；介形类动物|A small crustacean of the class Ostracoda, which has a bivalved carapace and typically lives in water. |例句：Ostracods are tiny crustaceans that inhabit both freshwater and marine environments. 介形虫是生活在淡水和海洋环境中的微小甲壳类动物。

例句：Fossil ostracods are useful for biostratigraphy. 介形虫化石对生物地层学很有用。|近义词：seed shrimp, mussel shrimp|反义词: N/A |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Modern Latin *Ostracoda, from ostrac- + -oda.  Ostrac- comes from Greek óstrakon "shell, tile," related to óstreon "oyster." -oda is a suffix used in zoology to form the names of classes and orders, ultimately from Greek -ōdēs, adjectival suffix denoting likeness or resemblance.
