mutineer:/ˌmjuːtɪˈnɪər/| n. |叛变者，反抗者|A person who takes part in a mutiny; a rebel.|例句：The mutineers took control of the ship. 叛变者控制了这艘船。

例句：The captain had the mutineers thrown in the brig. 船长把叛变者关进了禁闭室。

|近义词：rebel, insurgent, revolutionary|反义词:loyalist, conformist|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mutinier*, from *mutin* ("mutinous"), from Middle French *meute* ("mutiny, riot, pack of hounds"), from Late Latin *motita* ("movement, motion, riot"), from Latin *motus*, past participle of *movēre* ("to move"). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *mewh- ("to push away").
