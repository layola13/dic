flyblow:/ˈflaɪbləʊ/ (UK) /ˈflaɪbloʊ/ (US)| n. |蝇卵；蛆；讨厌的东西|The eggs laid by a fly, typically on meat or decaying matter; also, the maggots that hatch from these eggs. Figuratively, something foul or unpleasant.|The butcher had to discard the meat because it was covered in flyblow. 肉贩不得不丢弃这块肉，因为它上面爬满了蛆。|近义词：maggot, worm, pest|反义词: (No direct antonym, consider "cleanliness" or "purity" for the figurative meaning)|noun

flyblow:/ˈflaɪbləʊ/ (UK) /ˈflaɪbloʊ/ (US)| vt. |(苍蝇)在…上产卵，使…生蛆|To deposit fly eggs on something, causing it to become infested with maggots.|The warm weather flyblew the carcass quickly.  温暖的天气使尸体很快生蛆了。|近义词：infest|反义词: (No direct antonym)|verb
