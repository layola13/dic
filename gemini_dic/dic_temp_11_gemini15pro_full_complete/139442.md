obsolesce:/ˌɒbsəˈlɛs/ (US) /ˌɒbsəˈliːs/ (UK)| v. | 变得过时，废弃 | To become obsolete; to go out of use. |  Typewriters have largely obsolesced in the digital age. 打字机在数字时代已基本过时。

The company's main product line is obsolescing rapidly. 该公司的主要产品线正在迅速过时。 | 近义词：become outdated, become outmoded, age,  deprecate | 反义词：modernize, update, innovate | 词性: 动词

obsolescence:/ˌɒbsəˈlɛsəns/ (US) /ˌɒbsəˈliːsəns/ (UK)| n. | 过时，废弃 | The process of becoming obsolete. | Planned obsolescence is a controversial strategy employed by some manufacturers. 计划性报废是一些制造商采用的有争议的策略。

The rapid obsolescence of technology is a challenge for many businesses.  技术的快速过时对许多企业来说是一个挑战。| 近义词：outdatedness, antiquity | 反义词：modernity, currency | 词性: 名词

obsolete:/ˈɒbsəliːt/ (US/UK) | adj. | 过时的，废弃的 | No longer produced or used; out of date. | Many of the old accounting practices are now obsolete. 许多旧的会计做法现在已经过时了。

The introduction of the internet made dial-up modems obsolete. 互联网的引入使得拨号调制解调器过时了。| 近义词：outdated, outmoded, antiquated, archaic | 反义词：modern, current, contemporary, up-to-date | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Latin obsolescere "to fall into disuse," from ob- "away" + solescere "to become accustomed," from the adjective solus "alone, accustomed."
