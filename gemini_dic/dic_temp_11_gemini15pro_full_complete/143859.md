outplod: /aʊtˈplɒd/ | v. |  跋涉；沉重缓慢地走 | to walk slowly and heavily, especially for a long distance |  The weary travelers outplodded the last few miles.  疲惫的旅行者们沉重缓慢地走完了最后几英里。 | synonyms: trudge, plod, slog | antonyms: sprint, dart, race | verb

outplod: /aʊtˈplɒd/ | v. |  坚持不懈地工作；埋头苦干 | to work slowly and steadily, especially on a difficult or tedious task | He outplodded his rivals and eventually won the contract. 他比他的竞争对手更坚持不懈地工作，最终赢得了合同。 | synonyms: persevere, toil, labor | antonyms:  idle, dawdle, procrastinate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + plod.  "Out-" intensifies the meaning of "plod," suggesting a more arduous or prolonged effort.
