hypersuggestibility:/ˌhaɪpər səˌdʒɛstəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 过度暗示性，高度暗示性 | The state of being excessively responsive to suggestions.  A heightened state of suggestibility in which an individual is unusually susceptible to accepting and acting on suggestions from others, even if those suggestions are illogical or contrary to their own beliefs or experiences. | The hypnotist took advantage of the subject's hypersuggestibility.  催眠师利用了受试者的过度暗示性。
