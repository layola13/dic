distilling:/dɪˈstɪlɪŋ/ | n. | 蒸馏; 提炼; 萃取 | The process of purifying a liquid by heating it until it vaporizes, then cooling and condensing the vapor back into a liquid form.  This process separates substances based on their different boiling points. |  The distilling of whiskey requires careful temperature control. 威士忌的蒸馏需要精确的温度控制。| synonyms: refining, purification, extraction | antonyms: contamination, adulteration | noun

distil:/dɪˈstɪl/ | v. | 蒸馏; 提炼; 萃取(精华) | To purify a liquid by heating it until it vaporizes, then cooling and condensing the vapor back into a liquid form; to extract the essential meaning or most important aspects of something. | They distil the whiskey in copper stills. 他们用铜制蒸馏器蒸馏威士忌。  | synonyms: refine, purify, extract, concentrate | antonyms: contaminate, adulterate, dilute | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *distiller*, from Latin *destillare* "to drip down," from *de-* "down" + *stillare* "to drip," from *stilla* "a drop."
