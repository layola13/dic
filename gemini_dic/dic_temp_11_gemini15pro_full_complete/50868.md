desensitised: /diːˈsɛnsɪtaɪzd/ | adj. | 脱敏的，失去敏感性的 | Made less sensitive.  Having reduced sensitivity to something (e.g., a drug, an allergen, violence, etc.) as a result of repeated exposure. | After years of working in the emergency room, the doctor had become desensitised to the sight of blood. 多年在急诊室工作后，这位医生对看到血已经麻木了。| 近义词: numb, indifferent, hardened, inured | 反义词: sensitive, responsive, susceptible | 词性: 形容词

desensitize: /diːˈsɛnsɪtaɪz/ | vt. | 使脱敏，使失去敏感性 | To make someone less sensitive to something.  To reduce someone's responsiveness to something through repeated exposure. | The therapist tried to desensitize the patient to their fear of spiders. 治疗师试图让病人对蜘蛛的恐惧脱敏。 | 近义词: numb, dull, harden | 反义词: sensitize, sharpen | 词性: 动词

词源:  de- (prefix meaning "remove" or "reduce") + sensitize (from sense + -ize, meaning "to make sensitive"). Ultimately from Latin *sensus* "feeling, sense."
