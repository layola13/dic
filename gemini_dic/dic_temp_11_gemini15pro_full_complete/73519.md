foreshore:/ˈfɔːrʃɔːr/
n.
前滩；海滩；潮间带
The part of a shore between high-water and low-water marks, or between the water and cultivated or developed land.
The children were playing on the foreshore, looking for shells. 孩子们在前滩上玩耍，寻找贝壳。

The hotel is located right on the foreshore.  这家酒店就坐落在前滩上。

Synonym:
beach, seashore, waterfront, tideland, intertidal zone

Antonym:
hinterland, backshore

ETYMOLOGY->
From fore- + shore.  "Fore-" signifies "situated in front of or at the front" while "shore" refers to the land bordering a body of water.
