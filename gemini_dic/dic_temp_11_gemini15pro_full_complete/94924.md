in-love:/ɪn ˈlʌv/| adj.|热恋的，陷入爱情的|Being deeply enamored or infatuated with someone.|例句：They are deeply in love and plan to get married soon. 他们深爱着对方，打算很快结婚。|近义词：enamored, infatuated, smitten|反义词：indifferent, uninterested|词性:形容词
in love (Note: sometimes written without the hyphen, especially in American English) comes from the Old English "in lufe," with "lufe" meaning "love." The preposition "in" indicates a state of being. Over time, the phrase evolved to its current form, expressing the condition of being deeply affected by love.
