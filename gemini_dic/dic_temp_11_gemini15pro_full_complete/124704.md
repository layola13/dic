mown:/moʊn/ | adj. | 已割的，已修剪的 |  Cut down with a mower; also used figuratively. | The lawn was neatly mown. 草坪修剪得很整齐。| synonyms: cut, cropped, trimmed, shorn | antonyms: overgrown, uncut, unkempt | adjective

Etymology: From Middle English *mowen*, past participle of *mowen*, from Old English *māwan* (“to mow”), from Proto-Germanic *māaną* (“to mow”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂meh₁- (“to cut, reap”). Cognate with Dutch *maaien* (“to mow”), German *mähen* (“to mow”).
