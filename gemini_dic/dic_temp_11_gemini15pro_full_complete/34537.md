ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "cleanse," which comes from Middle English "clensen," derived from Old French "clenser," ultimately from Latin "clensus," the past participle of "clēnsis" meaning "clear" or "pure".  The "-er" suffix denotes an agent or instrument that performs the action of the verb.
