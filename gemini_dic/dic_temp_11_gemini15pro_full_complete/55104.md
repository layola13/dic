disrober:/dɪsˈroʊbər/
|n.|脱衣者；使人脱衣的人或物|a person or thing that disrobes someone or something. |The nurse acted as a disrober for the patient before the surgery.  护士在病人手术前帮他脱了衣服。|Synonym: undresser|Antonym: dresser|noun

disrobe:/dɪsˈroʊb/
|v.|脱衣服；使脱光衣服|to remove clothes from; undress.|The king disrobed before entering the bath. 国王在进入浴池前脱去了衣服。|Synonym: undress, strip|Antonym: dress, clothe|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (prefix meaning "reversal, removal") + robe (noun meaning "a long, loose garment").  Ultimately from Latin *disrobare*, from dis- + roba ("garment").
