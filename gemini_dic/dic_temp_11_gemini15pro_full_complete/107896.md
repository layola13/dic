larkspur: /ˈlɑːrkspɜr/ (US) /ˈlɑːkspɜː(r)/ (UK)| n. |飞燕草；翠雀|A plant with spurred flowers, typically of a bluish-purple colour, popular in gardens.  It's also known as delphinium.|The border was ablaze with colour, thanks to the vibrant larkspurs.|近义词：delphinium,  consolida|反义词: (No direct antonym for a plant name, could consider contrasting colours/shapes, e.g., marigold, rose)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "lark" (referring to the resemblance of the flower's spur to a lark's claw) + "spur"
