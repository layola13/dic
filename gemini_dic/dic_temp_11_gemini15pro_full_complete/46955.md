darrow:/ˈdæroʊ/
n.|达罗|Clarence Seward Darrow (1857-1938), a famous American lawyer, best known for his defense of John T. Scopes in the Scopes "Monkey" Trial (1925), which challenged a Tennessee law against teaching evolution in public schools. He was also a leading member of the American Civil Liberties Union and a prominent advocate for labor rights and social justice.|Clarence Darrow was a brilliant lawyer. 克拉伦斯·达罗是一位杰出的律师。|Synonym:  advocate, defender|Antonym: prosecutor|noun

darrow:/ˈdæroʊ/
adj.|狭窄的，似峡谷的|Resembling a narrow valley or gorge; steep-sided and confined.|The trail led through a darrow canyon. 这条小径穿过一个狭窄的峡谷。|Synonym: narrow, constricted, tight|Antonym: wide, broad, expansive|adjective
