fratricelli:/ˌfrɑːtrɪˈtʃɛli/ | n. | 小兄弟派教徒；精神上的兄弟 | Members of a strict Franciscan sect of the 13th and 14th centuries that advocated absolute poverty. | The fratricelli were persecuted by the established church. 小兄弟派教徒受教会迫害。 | synonyms: Spiritual Franciscans,  | antonyms:  | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *fratricelli*, diminutive of *fratricello*, diminutive of *frate* ("friar, brother"), from Latin *frāter*. Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*bʰréh₂tēr* (“brother”).
