mislead:/ˌmɪsˈliːd/ | vt. | 误导，欺骗 | To cause someone to believe something that is not true. | The advertisement was intended to mislead consumers about the product's effectiveness. 这则广告旨在误导消费者对产品功效的认知。

The politician misled the public with false promises.  这个政客用虚假的承诺误导了公众。

Synonyms: deceive, delude, trick, fool, dupe
Antonyms: guide, enlighten, inform, direct
词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *misleden*, from Old English *mislēdan* (“to mislead, misguide, lead astray, seduce”), from *mis-* (“wrongly, amiss”) + *lēdan* (“to lead”).
