ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *inquinātus, past participle of inquināre (“to stain, soil, pollute”), from Latin in- (“in, into, on, upon, against”) + *coenos (“dirt, filth”) (akin to Ancient Greek κοινός (koinós, “common, shared, public”), Latin cēnum (“mud, mire”), and English hind).
