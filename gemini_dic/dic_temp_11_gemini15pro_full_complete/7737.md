anoplotherium:/əˌnɒpləˈθɪəriəm/ | n. | 无角兽 | An extinct genus of even-toed ungulate mammals, resembling a cross between a pig and a camel, that lived in Europe during the Eocene and Oligocene epochs.  | Example: The anoplotherium, with its unique combination of pig-like and camel-like features, is a fascinating subject for paleontologists. 无角兽，兼具猪和骆驼的特征，是古生物学家们感兴趣的研究对象。

Example:  Museum exhibits showcase fossilized remains of the anoplotherium, providing a glimpse into prehistoric life. 博物馆展出了无角兽的化石遗骸，让人们得以一窥史前生命。 | 近义词:  Xiphodon (a related genus) | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun


Etymology:  From Greek *ἀνοπλόθηρ* (anoplothēr),  from *ἄνοπλος* (anoplos, "unarmed") + *θήρ* (thēr, "beast").  This refers to the animal's lack of canine teeth or tusks.
