dog-eared:/dɒɡˈɪəd/ /dɔːɡˈɪrd/| adj.|狗耳朵的，卷角的，翻旧的|Having the corners of pages turned or curled up, typically from frequent use or handling.  It suggests the book has been well-loved and read often.|The book was dog-eared and worn, a testament to how many times she'd read it. 这本书卷角磨损，证明她读过很多遍。|近义词：worn, tattered, battered, thumbed|反义词：pristine, mint, new|词性:形容词

dog-eared:/dɒɡˈɪəd/ /dɔːɡˈɪrd/| n.|卷角|A turned-down corner of a page. |He smoothed the dog-ear on the page before closing the book. 他在合上书之前抚平了书页上的卷角。|近义词：crease, fold|反义词：flatness|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->

From "dog's ear," referring to the resemblance of a turned-down page corner to a dog's ear. First recorded in the late 16th century.
