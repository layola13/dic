endurably:/ɪnˈdʊrəbli/ | adv. | 可忍受地，持久地 |  In a way that can be endured; bearably. | The pain was endurably sharp. 疼痛是尖锐的，但还可以忍受。| Synonyms: bearably, tolerably, sufferably | Antonyms: unbearably, intolerably, insufferably | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From endure (v.) + -ably (adv. suffix).  Endure comes from Old French endurer, from Latin indurare "to harden," from in- "in" + durare "to harden, last," from durus "hard."
