ghostwrite:/ˈɡoʊstˌraɪt/ (US), /ˈɡəʊstˌraɪt/ (UK)| v. |代笔，为人捉刀|To write for someone else, who will then put their name to it as if they had written it themselves. |例句：He ghostwrote the autobiography of the famous athlete. 他为那位著名的运动员代笔自传。|例句：She makes a living ghostwriting novels for celebrities. 她靠为名人代笔小说谋生。|近义词：author for, write for, pen for|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps) self-author, write oneself|词性: verb


ghostwriter:/ˈɡoʊstˌraɪtər/ (US), /ˈɡəʊstˌraɪtər/ (UK)| n. |代笔人，捉刀人|A person who writes for someone else, who will then put their name to it as if they had written it themselves. |例句：The celebrity's memoir was actually written by a ghostwriter.  这位名人的回忆录实际上是由一位代笔人写的。|例句：He hired a ghostwriter to help him finish his novel. 他雇了一个代笔人来帮他完成他的小说。|近义词：amanuensis, scribe|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps) author, writer|词性: noun


ghostwritten:/ˈɡoʊstˌrɪtn/ (US), /ˈɡəʊstˌrɪtn/ (UK)| adj. |代写的，捉刀的|Written by a ghostwriter. |例句：The ghostwritten biography became a bestseller. 这次代写的传记成了畅销书。|例句：Many celebrities have ghostwritten autobiographies. 许多名人的自传都是代写的。|近义词：authored by another, penned by another|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps) self-written, originally authored|词性: adjective
