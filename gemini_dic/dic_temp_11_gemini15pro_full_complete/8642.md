anticonfederationists:/ˌæntiˌkənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪsts/
| n. | 反联邦主义者 |  Those who opposed the creation of a stronger U.S. federal government under the Constitution proposed in 1787, favoring instead greater state sovereignty. |  The Anticonfederationists argued that the Constitution gave too much power to the central government. 反联邦主义者认为宪法赋予中央政府过多的权力。 |  opponents of the Constitution, anti-federalists |  federalists, supporters of the Constitution | noun
