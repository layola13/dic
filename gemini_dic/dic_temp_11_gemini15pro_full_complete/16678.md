barrel:/ˈbærəl/| n.|桶；枪管；一桶的量|A barrel is a large, cylindrical container, usually made of wood, metal, or plastic, with flat ends and, typically, slightly bulging sides. It is used for storing or transporting liquids, such as oil or wine, or other goods, such as flour or apples.  It can also refer to the metal tube of a gun through which the bullet travels.|The wine was stored in oak barrels.  这种酒储存在橡木桶里。|Synonyms: cask, keg, drum, cylinder|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for the container itself.  Perhaps "containerless" in a specific context)|noun

barrel:/ˈbærəl/| v.|飞驰；快速移动|To barrel means to move very fast, often in a somewhat uncontrolled way.|The car barreled down the highway. 汽车在高速公路上飞驰。|Synonyms: speed, rush, hurtle, zoom|Antonyms: crawl, creep, dawdle|verb
