ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *grōnen*, from Old English *grānian* (“to groan, lament, murmur”), from Proto-Germanic *grōnaną* (“to groan”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵrēh₂-n-*, *ǵreh₂-* (“to roar, snarl”). Cognate with Dutch *gronen* (“to groan”), German *greinen* (“to whine, cry”), Icelandic *grœna* (“to smile, grin”). The noun is derived from the present participle of the verb.
