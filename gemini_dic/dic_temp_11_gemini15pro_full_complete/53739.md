discolorate:/dɪsˈkʌlərət/ /dɪsˈkʌl.ɚ.eɪt/ | v. | 使变色，使褪色 | To change the color of something, usually in a way that makes it look less attractive. |  The intense sunlight has discolored the curtains.  强烈的阳光使窗帘褪色了。 |  Exposure to the elements can discolorate wood.  暴露在风雨中会使木头变色。 | 近义词: stain, fade, bleach, tarnish | 反义词: brighten, color, dye | 词性: 动词

discoloration:/dɪsˌkʌl.əˈreɪ.ʃən/ /dɪsˌkʌl.ɚˈeɪ.ʃən/ | n. | 变色，褪色 | A change in the color of something, typically one that makes it look less attractive. | The discoloration of the old photographs made them difficult to identify.  旧照片的褪色使得难以辨认。 | There was a noticeable discoloration on the wall where the water had leaked.  在漏水的地方，墙上有一个明显的变色痕迹。 | 近义词: stain, blemish, fading | 反义词: brightness, coloring | 词性: 名词


词源: dis- (Latin prefix meaning "apart," "asunder," "reversal") + color + -ate (suffix forming verbs)
