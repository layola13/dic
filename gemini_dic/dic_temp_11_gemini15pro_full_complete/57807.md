dreichly:/ˈdriːxli/| adv.|沉闷地，阴郁地，凄凉地|Drearily; gloomily; miserably.  It describes weather that is dull, gloomy, and often wet.  It evokes a sense of dreariness and unpleasantness. |The rain fell dreichly all day. 雨沉闷地下了一整天。|近义词：drearily, gloomily, miserably, dismally|反义词：brightly, cheerfully, sunnily|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Scots *dreich* ("dreary, tedious, overcast"), from Old Norse *draugr* ("phantom, ghost").  The sense of "dreary weather" likely developed from the association of overcast skies with a sense of foreboding or gloom.
