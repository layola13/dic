ectomere:/ˈɛktəˌmɪr/| n. |外胚层节|The outer, segmented portion of the mesoderm in a developing embryo, especially in vertebrates.  It gives rise to the body wall and limbs. |The researchers studied the development of the ectomeres in chick embryos. 研究人员研究了鸡胚胎中外胚层节的发育。|Synonym:  somite (though not perfectly synonymous, as somites encompass more than just the ectomere), epimere|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific anatomical structure)|noun
