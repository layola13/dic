carvings: /ˈkɑːrvɪŋz/
n.
雕刻品，雕刻物
Figures or designs carved from wood, stone, or other materials.
The museum displayed intricate carvings made from ivory.  博物馆展出了由象牙制成的复杂雕刻品。
sculptures, engravings, etchings
paintings, drawings

carvings: /ˈkɑːrvɪŋz/
n.
切下的肉
Slices of meat carved from a larger piece, especially a roast.
He enjoyed the Thanksgiving turkey carvings. 他很喜欢感恩节的火鸡切片。
slices, portions, cuts
whole, uncut


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *carving*, from Old English *ceorfan* (to cut, carve), from Proto-Germanic *kerfaną* (to cut, notch), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰerbh-* (to scratch, scrape).  The "-ing" suffix indicates a gerund or present participle, which has become nominalized to mean "the result of carving."
