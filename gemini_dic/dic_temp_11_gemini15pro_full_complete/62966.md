ennoblingly:/ɪˈnoʊblɪŋli/
adverb
高尚地；崇高地
in a way that makes something or someone seem noble or of a high moral standard.
Her father spoke ennoblingly of her work with the poor. 她父亲高度赞扬了她为穷人所做的工作。
Synonyms: nobly, loftily, grandly, majestically, admirably
Antonyms: basely, ignobly, dishonorably
词性: 副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From ennoble + -ing + -ly.  "Ennoble" comes from the Old French "ennoblir,"  meaning "to make noble," from "en-" (meaning "in" or "into") + "noble" (from Latin "nobilis").  "-ing" forms the present participle and "-ly" creates the adverb form.
