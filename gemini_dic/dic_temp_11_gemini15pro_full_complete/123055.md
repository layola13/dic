monetarist:/ˈmʌnɪtərɪst/ | n. adj. |货币主义者; 货币主义的| An economist who believes that the amount of money in circulation is the main factor affecting economic activity, and that control of the money supply is the chief way to influence it.  A person who believes in monetarism. (As an adjective, relating to monetarism)| Example:  The monetarist argued that inflation could be controlled by restricting the growth of the money supply. 这位货币主义者认为，可以通过限制货币供应量的增长来控制通货膨胀。|Synonyms:  Friedmanite (referring to Milton Friedman, a prominent monetarist), supply-sider (partially overlapping), conservative economist (often, but not always)| Antonyms: Keynesian, fiscalist | noun, adjective

monetarism: /ˈmʌnɪtərɪzəm/ | n. |货币主义| The theory or practice of controlling the supply of money as the chief method of stabilizing the economy. | Example: Monetarism was a dominant economic philosophy in the 1980s. 货币主义是20世纪80年代的主流经济哲学。 | Synonyms:  supply-side economics (partially overlapping) | Antonyms: Keynesianism, fiscalism | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "money" + "-ist" (denoting a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or theory)  "Money" ultimately derives from Latin "moneta", referring to the Roman goddess Juno Moneta, in whose temple coins were minted.
