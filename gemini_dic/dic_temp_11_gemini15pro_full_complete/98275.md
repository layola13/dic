insessores:/ɪnsɛˈsɔːriːz/ | n. | 雀形目鸟类 | Perching birds; an obsolete taxonomic group that included most birds with three toes pointing forward and one pointing backward, now considered largely equivalent to the order Passeriformes. |  The insessores are known for their diverse songs. 雀形目鸟类以其多样化的歌声而闻名。| synonym: passerines | antonym:  (No direct antonym as it's a taxonomic group) | noun
