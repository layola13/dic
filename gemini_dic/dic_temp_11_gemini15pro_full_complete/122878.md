mollusc:/ˈmɒləsk/ (UK) /ˈmɑːləsk/ (US)| n. |软体动物|Any of a large phylum (Mollusca) of invertebrate animals (such as snails, clams, or squids) with a soft unsegmented body usually enclosed in a calcareous shell.|Snails and clams are molluscs.  蜗牛和蛤蜊都是软体动物。|Synonyms: shellfish, invertebrate|Antonyms: vertebrate|noun

mollusk:/ˈmɑːləsk/ (US)| n. |软体动物|American spelling of mollusc.|Oysters are a type of mollusk.  牡蛎是一种软体动物。|Synonyms: shellfish, invertebrate|Antonyms: vertebrate|noun
