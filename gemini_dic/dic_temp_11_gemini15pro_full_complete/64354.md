erastian:/ɪˈræstiən/ | adj. n. | 伊拉斯提派的，伊拉斯提派信徒 | Relating to or characteristic of Erastianism, the doctrine that the state should have supreme authority over the church in ecclesiastical matters. |  The king's erastian policies angered many church leaders. 国王的伊拉斯提政策激怒了许多教会领袖。|  The erastian argued for the supremacy of the state in religious affairs.  这位伊拉斯提派信徒主张国家在宗教事务中的至高无上地位。 |近义词: secularist, statist | 反义词: theocratic, ultramontane | 词性: adjective, noun


Etymology:
From Erastus + -ian.  Erastus refers to Thomas Erastus (1524-1583), a Swiss physician and theologian who opposed the use of excommunication by the church.
