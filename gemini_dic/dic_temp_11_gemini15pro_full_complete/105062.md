kerb:/kɜːb/ (British English), /kɜːrb/ (American English)| n.|路缘，路边|A stone or concrete edging to a pavement or raised path. |例句：The car was parked close to the kerb. 汽车停靠在路边。

例句：She tripped over the kerb and fell. 她被路缘绊倒了。

近义词：curb (American English), edge, rim, verge|反义词:|名词

kerb:/kɜːb/ (British English), /kɜːrb/ (American English)| vt.|控制，约束|To restrain or keep in check.|例句：He struggled to kerb his anger. 他努力抑制自己的愤怒。

例句：The government introduced measures to kerb inflation. 政府采取措施控制通货膨胀。

近义词：restrain, control, check, limit|反义词: unleash, release|动词


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *curb*, from Old North French *curbe*, from Late Latin *curva*, feminine of *curvus* (“curved”).  Doublet of *curve*.  Cognate with Old French *corbe* (“curved; crooked”).
