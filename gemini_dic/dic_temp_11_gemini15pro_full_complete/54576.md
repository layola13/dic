dislustre:/dɪsˈlʌstər/ (US) /dɪsˈlʌstə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.| 失去光泽; 黯淡无光; 耻辱; 使…失去光泽; 使…黯然失色|The scandal brought dislustre to his career. 这桩丑闻使他的事业蒙上阴影。|近义词：tarnish, dullness, disgrace, shame, dishonor|反义词：lustre, shine, brilliance, honor, glory|词性:名词/动词

Etymology:
From dis- + lustre.  "Dis-" is a prefix meaning "apart," "asunder," "away," or having a privative, negative, or reversing force. "Lustre" comes from Old French "lustre," ultimately from Latin "lustrare" meaning "to purify, make bright."
