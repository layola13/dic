mahomedan:/məˈhɒmɪdən/| adj. n.|穆罕默德的信徒，伊斯兰教徒|An outdated and often offensive term for a Muslim.  This term is considered pejorative due to its historical association with colonialism and misrepresentation of Islamic beliefs.  Prefer the term "Muslim."|例句：The term "Mahomedan" was commonly used in the 19th century but is now considered offensive.  19世纪，“Mahomedan”一词很常用，但现在被认为具有冒犯性。

例句：Using "Mahomedan" instead of "Muslim" can be seen as disrespectful.  用“Mahomedan”代替“Muslim”会被视为不尊重。|近义词：(Outdated and offensive) Mohammedanist, Moslem|反义词: None (The appropriate antonym would depend on the context and might be "non-Muslim")|词性:adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Medieval Latin *Mahometanus, derived from the name Mahomet (a variant of Muhammad), the prophet of Islam. The suffix -an denotes "pertaining to" or "a follower of."
