live-ins:/ˈlɪv ɪnz/ | n. |同居者，住家佣人|People who live together, especially in a sexual relationship without being married, or employees who live at their place of work.  |例句：The live-ins at the mansion included a cook and a housekeeper.  这座大厦的住家佣人包括一名厨师和一名管家。

|例句：Many young couples choose to be live-ins before marriage. 许多年轻夫妇选择在结婚前同居。|近义词：cohabitants, roommates (for non-romantic relationships), domestic staff (for employees)|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider) homeowners, landlords|词性:名词


live-in:/ˈlɪv ɪn/ | adj. |同居的，住家的|Describing someone who lives where they work, or describing a partner in a cohabiting relationship. |例句：She has a live-in nanny to care for the children. 她有一个住家保姆来照顾孩子们。


|例句：They have a live-in relationship. 他们有同居关系。|近义词：resident, cohabiting|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider) commuting, non-resident|词性:形容词
