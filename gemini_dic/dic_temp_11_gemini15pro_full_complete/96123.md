indelicatenesses:/ɪnˌdɛlɪkətɪsnəsɪz/
|n.|不文雅；粗俗；失禮|The act of being indelicate; lack of sensitivity and refinement; something that is indelicate.|Her indelicatenesses at the dinner table shocked the guests. 她在餐桌上的粗俗行为让客人们感到震惊。|近义词：rudeness, vulgarity, impropriety, coarseness, gaucherie|反义词：tact, politeness, refinement, delicacy, sensitivity|词性: 名词

词源:
From in- + delicate + -ness + -es (plural).  The prefix "in-" negates the meaning of "delicate," which means refined and sensitive.  "-ness" turns the adjective "indelicate" into a noun, representing the quality of being indelicate. "-es" marks the plural form, indicating multiple instances of indelicate behavior or remarks.
