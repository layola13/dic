damnably:/ˈdæmnəbli/ | adv. |该死地，非常地，极其地 |  In a damnable manner; execrably; hatefully; outrageously. |  The traffic was damnably slow. 交通慢得要死。 | 近义词：awfully, terribly, extremely, exceedingly | 反义词：blessedly, divinely | 词性: 副词

Etymology: From *damnable* + *-ly*.  *Damnable* comes from Old French *damnable*, from Late Latin *damnabilis*, from Latin *damnare* "to condemn, damage," from *damnum* "damage, loss."
