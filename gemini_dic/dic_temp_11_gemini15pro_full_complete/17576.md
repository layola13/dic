beauseant: /ˈboʊsɪənt/| n.|黑白旗; 圣殿骑士团的战旗;|A banner divided vertically into black and white, used as the battle standard of the Knights Templar.|The beauseant flew high above the battlefield, inspiring the Templar knights.|Synonym: banner, standard, flag|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "surrender flag")|noun


Etymology:
From Old French *bausant, probably from bauz “piebald” (of a horse) + -ant, present participle suffix.  The original meaning referred to a piebald horse.
