nongracefully:/ˌnɒnˈɡreɪsfʊli/ | adv. |不优雅地，笨拙地 | Not in a graceful or elegant manner; awkwardly or clumsily. |  He tripped and fell nongracefully down the stairs. 他绊了一跤，笨拙地摔下了楼梯。|Synonyms: awkwardly, clumsily, ungracefully, inelegantly | Antonyms: gracefully, elegantly, smoothly | Word Type: Adverb

Etymology:
* non-:  a prefix meaning "not," from Latin non "not."
* graceful: from Middle English graceful, from Old French gracieux, from Late Latin gratiosus "pleasing, agreeable," from Latin gratia "grace, favor, thanks."
* -ly: adverbial suffix.  Essentially, it's "graceful" with a negating prefix and an adverbial suffix.
