deconsecration:/ˌdiːˌkɒnsɪˈkreɪʃn/ | n. | 去圣化，去神圣化 | The act of depriving something of its sacred character; the removal of a religious blessing from a person, place, or object. | The deconsecration of the church shocked the community. 教堂的去圣化震惊了社区。 |  The deconsecration ceremony involved removing the altar and other religious symbols. 去圣化仪式包括移除祭坛和其他宗教符号。 | 近义词: desecration, secularization | 反义词: consecration, sanctification | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
From de- (prefix meaning "removal, reversal") + consecration (from Latin consecrat-, past participle stem of consecrare "to make sacred, dedicate," from com- "together" + sacrare "to make sacred").
