grandific:/ˈɡrændɪfaɪ/ | adj. | 夸大的，夸张的 | Making something appear grander or more important than it actually is; exaggerating. |  He delivered a grandific speech about his minor accomplishments.  他对自己微不足道的成就发表了夸大的演讲。 | Synonyms: exaggerated, inflated, pompous, bombastic, grandiose | Antonyms: understated, modest, humble, simple | Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *grandificare*, from *grandis* (large, great) + *-ficare* (making, causing).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *gʷr̥h₂n̥t-* (big, great).
