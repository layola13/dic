one-off:/ˈwʌnˌɒf/ (UK), /ˈwʌnˌɔːf/ (US)| n. adj.|一次性的，特例的|Something that happens or is made or done only once.|例句：This is a one-off opportunity. 这是一个一次性的机会。|近义词：unique, single, exceptional|反义词：regular, recurring, frequent|词性：名词/形容词

one-off:/ˈwʌnˌɒf/ (UK), /ˈwʌnˌɔːf/ (US)| n. adj.|一次性的，特例的|A single item of something that is not part of a regular series.|例句：They made a one-off payment of £1,000. 他们支付了一次性款项1000英镑。|近义词：nonrecurring, isolated, special|反义词：common, typical, standard|词性：名词/形容词

词源：Compound formed from one and off.  First recorded in the mid 20th century.  Originally (and still often) hyphenated.
