clangour:/ˈklæŋər/ (US), /ˈklæŋɡə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 铿锵声， clang, din, loud noise |A continuous loud banging or ringing sound.|The clangour of the bells announced the start of the festival. 钟声的铿锵声宣布了节日的开始。|Synonyms: clang, din, racket, uproar, commotion|Antonyms: silence, quiet, hush, stillness|noun


clangour:/ˈklæŋər/ (US), /ˈklæŋɡə(r)/ (UK)| v.|发出铿锵声|To make a continuous loud banging or ringing sound.|The bells clangoured throughout the city. 钟声在整个城市回响。|Synonyms: clang, ring, resound, reverberate|Antonyms: be silent, be quiet|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *clangor, from Latin clangor "a loud cry, shriek," from clangere "to sound, resound, clang."
