carped:/ˈkɑːrpt/| v. | 抓住机会；及时行乐 | to seize an opportunity; to make the most of the present moment; to enjoy oneself while one can |  They carped the diem and went on a spontaneous road trip. 他们抓住机会，进行了一次说走就走的公路旅行。| Synonyms: seize, grasp, take advantage of, enjoy, revel in | Antonyms: waste, squander, miss, ignore | verb

carped:/ˈkɑːrpt/| adj. (rare, archaic) |  (Of speech or writing) Characterized by seizing on trivial points or raising petty objections; quibbling |  picking apart; nitpicking | His carped criticism of the presentation focused on minor typos. 他对演讲吹毛求疵的批评集中在一些小的拼写错误上。 | Synonyms:  nitpicking, caviling, quibbling, captious | Antonyms:  uncritical, accepting, tolerant, broad-minded | adjective
