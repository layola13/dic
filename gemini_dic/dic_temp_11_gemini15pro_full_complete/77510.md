gazley: /ˈɡeɪzli/ | adj. | 凝视的，目不转睛的 |  Gazely describes the act of looking intently or steadily at something or someone. It implies a fixed and unwavering gaze, often suggesting deep thought, fascination, or scrutiny.  | He gave her a long, gazely look. 他久久地凝视着她。 | 近义词: staring, fixed, intent, intense | 反义词: glancing, fleeting, cursory | 词性: 形容词

词源:  Likely a back-formation from the noun "gaze" + "-ly" (adverbial suffix forming adjectives).  "Gaze" itself comes from Old North French "gaser," meaning "to gape" or "stare."
