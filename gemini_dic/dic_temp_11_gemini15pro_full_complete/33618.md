cincinnatus: /ˌsɪnsɪˈnætəs/ | n. |辛辛纳图斯;  |A Roman statesman and military leader of the early Republic who gained fame for his selfless devotion to the Republic, twice being called from his farm to assume dictatorial powers, and each time retiring to his farm once the crisis had abated.  Cincinnatus is a figure often cited as an example of civic virtue, simple living, and selfless leadership.|  George Washington was often compared to Cincinnatus for his willingness to relinquish power.  乔治·华盛顿因其愿意放弃权力而经常被比作辛辛纳图斯。|  Synonyms: patriot, hero | Antonyms: tyrant, dictator | noun
