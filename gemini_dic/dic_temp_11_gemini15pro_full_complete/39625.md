consequence:/ˈkɒnsɪkwəns/ (UK), /ˈkɑːnsəkwɛns/ (US)| n.|结果，后果；重要性；推论|a result or effect of an action or condition.|例句：The consequence of his actions was a loss of trust. 他的行为导致了信任的丧失。|例句：She understands the consequences of her decision. 她明白她决定的后果。|近义词：result, outcome, effect, ramification|反义词：cause, origin|词性:名词

consequent:/ˈkɒnsɪkwənt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːnsəkwɛnt/ (US)| adj.|作为结果的，随之发生的|following as a result or effect.|例句：The consequent loss of jobs caused hardship for many families.  随之而来的失业给许多家庭带来了困难。|例句：His resignation and the consequent reorganization of the company took several months. 他的辞职以及随之而来的公司重组花了几个月的时间。|近义词：resulting, ensuing, subsequent|反义词：preceding, prior|词性:形容词

consequently:/ˈkɒnsɪkwəntli/ (UK), /ˈkɑːnsəkwɛntli/ (US)| adv.|因此，所以|as a result; therefore.|例句：He was injured and consequently couldn't play. 他受伤了，因此不能上场。|例句：She lost her job and consequently had to move. 她失业了，因此不得不搬家。|近义词：therefore, thus, hence, accordingly|反义词：nevertheless, however|词性:副词
