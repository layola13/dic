asafoetida: /ˌæsəˈfɛtɪdə/ | n. | 阿魏，阿魏胶 | A hard, resinous gum with a pungent, unpleasant odor, obtained from the roots of several species of Ferula, used in medicine and as a seasoning, especially in Indian cuisine. |  Asafoetida is a common ingredient in Indian vegetarian cooking. 阿魏是印度素食烹饪中常见的配料。

Asafoetida has a strong smell that some people find offensive.  有些人觉得阿魏的味道很刺鼻。 | 近义词: hing, devil's dung, stinking gum | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps pleasant-smelling spices like cardamom or cinnamon could be considered loosely antonymous in a culinary context.) | 词性: noun
