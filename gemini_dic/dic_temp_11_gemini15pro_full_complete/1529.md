Achaean:/əˈkiːən/
adjective|阿凯亚人的；阿凯亚的|Relating to Achaea, an ancient region of Greece.|The Achaean League was a confederation of Greek city-states. 阿凯亚同盟是希腊城邦的联盟。
noun|阿凯亚人|A native or inhabitant of Achaea.|The Achaeans fought bravely in the Trojan War. 阿凯亚人在特洛伊战争中英勇作战。


Etymology->
From Latin *Achaicus*, from Ancient Greek *Ἀχαιός (Akhaios)*.  Ultimately from Proto-Hellenic *Akʰāwós*.

Synonyms->
Greek (more general)
Hellenic (more formal)
Argive (specifically relating to Argos, a major city in Achaea)
Mycenaean (referring to the Mycenaean civilization, which predated the classical Achaean period)

Antonyms->
(No direct antonyms.  One could consider "Dorian," "Ionian," or other Greek tribal names as contrasting terms, but they are not truly opposites.)
