footrope: /ˈfʊtrəʊp/ (UK) /ˈfʊtroʊp/ (US) | n. | 脚绳，踏索 | A rope or bar fastened horizontally to the shrouds of a sailing ship's lower mast and used as a foothold by sailors when furling or reefing sails. | The sailors clung to the footrope as they reefed the mainsail in the gale.  水手们在大风中收缩主帆时紧紧抓住踏索。| 近义词:  ratline | 反义词: None | 词性: noun


footrope: /ˈfʊtrəʊp/ (UK) /ˈfʊtroʊp/ (US) | n. | 踏板，踏杆 | A horizontal bar or platform for standing on, especially in a circus or other performance setting. | The acrobat balanced precariously on the footrope high above the crowd.  杂技演员在高高的踏板上危险地保持着平衡，俯视着人群。| 近义词:  pedal, platform | 反义词: None | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fotrop, from Old English *fōtrop (attested only as fōt-rǣp "foot-rope"), from fōt "foot" + rāp "rope."
