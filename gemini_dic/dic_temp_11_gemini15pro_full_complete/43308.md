crags:/kræɡz/
n.
悬崖，峭壁
A steep, rugged rock; a rough, broken cliff or projecting point of rock.
The climbers scaled the treacherous crags. 攀岩者攀登了险峻的峭壁。

cliff, precipice, bluff, peak
slope, plain

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *kragr (“rock”), from Proto-Germanic *kraguz. Cognate with Norwegian and Danish krag (“cliff, crag”), Swedish kråka (“crow, rook”), Icelandic kráka (“crow, rook”). The original meaning is thought to have been "projecting rock".
