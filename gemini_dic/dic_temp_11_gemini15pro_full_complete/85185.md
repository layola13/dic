harem:/ˈhɛərəm/ (US), /ˈhɑːriːm/ (UK)| n. | 后宫; 妻妾;  حرم |A group of women associated with one man, especially the wives or concubines of a polygamous man in a Muslim household.  It can also refer to the part of a Muslim household reserved for these women.|  The sultan's harem was legendary for its beauty and intrigue. 苏丹的后宫以其美貌和阴谋而闻名。

The tourists were not allowed to enter the harem. 游客不允许进入后宫。|近义词：seraglio, zenana|反义词: None|词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Arabic word "harim" (حريم), meaning "forbidden" or "sacred," referring to the women's quarters which were off-limits to men outside the family.
