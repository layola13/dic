giddiest:/ˈɡɪdiɪst/ | adj. | 最头晕的；最轻浮的；最令人眼花缭乱的 |  Superlative form of giddy: most giddy.  It describes a state of extreme dizziness, lightheadedness, or excitement. | The giddiest heights of the rollercoaster made my stomach drop. 过山车最令人眩晕的高度让我的胃都掉了下去。

The giddiest rumours were circulating about the pop star's love life. 关于这位流行歌星的爱情生活，流传着最令人眼花缭乱的谣言。 | 近义词：dizziest, lightheadedest, flightiest | 反义词: serious, solemn, sober | 词性: adjective

词源: From Middle English *gidi, from Old English *gydig, from Proto-Germanic *gidigaz (“possessed by a god, insane”).  Related to Old Norse gýðja (“priestess”). The original meaning was "possessed by a god," later evolving to "insane" and then to the modern senses of "dizzy" and "frivolous."
