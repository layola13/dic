demoralising:/dɪˈmɒrəlaɪzɪŋ/ (UK) /dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪzɪŋ/ (US)| adj.| 令人沮丧的，使人泄气的| Causing someone to lose confidence or hope; disheartening.| The team suffered a demoralising defeat.  这个队伍遭受了一场令人沮丧的失败。|Synonyms: disheartening, discouraging, dispiriting, sapping, undermining|Antonyms: encouraging, uplifting, inspiring, heartening|adjective

demoralise:/dɪˈmɒrəlaɪz/ (UK) /dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪz/ (US)| vt.| 使士气低落，使泄气| To cause someone to lose confidence or hope.| Constant criticism can demoralise employees.  不断的批评会使员工士气低落。|Synonyms: dishearten, discourage, dispirit, undermine|Antonyms: encourage, hearten, inspire|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *dé-* (expressing reversal) + *moraliser* (to moralize), from Late Latin *moralizare*, from Latin *moralis* (moral).  The sense shifted from "to corrupt the morals of" to "to discourage, dispirit."
