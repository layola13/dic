legalism:/ˈliːɡəlɪzəm/| n. |律法主义，法律主义|A strict adherence to law or formula.  A dependence on moral law rather than on personal religious faith.  Excessive adherence to the letter of the law.|例句：The Pharisees' legalism often conflicted with Jesus' teachings. 法利赛人的律法主义经常与耶稣的教导相冲突。

例句：Confucianism, with its emphasis on ritual and propriety, sometimes bordered on legalism. 儒家思想强调礼仪和得体，有时近乎律法主义。|近义词：formalism, literalism, dogmatism|反义词：antinomianism, liberalism, grace|词性:noun
