hookworms:/ˈhʊkwɜːrmz/
n.|钩虫|Small parasitic worms (genus *Ancylostoma* and *Necator*) that live in the intestines of humans and other mammals, causing anemia and other health problems.  They enter the body through the skin, usually by walking barefoot on contaminated soil.|The doctor diagnosed him with hookworms after he returned from his trip to the tropics.  医生在他从热带旅行回来后诊断他患有钩虫病。|synonyms:  Ancylostoma, Necator|antonyms:  None (no direct antonym for a parasite)|noun

hookworm:/ˈhʊkwɜːrm/
n.|钩虫|A single specimen of the parasitic worm known as a hookworm.|He found a hookworm in the dog's stool sample. 他在狗的粪便样本中发现了一条钩虫。|synonyms:  Ancylostoma, Necator|antonyms:  None (no direct antonym for a parasite)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hook" + "worm."  "Hook" refers to the hooked shape of the worm's mouthparts. "Worm" refers to its elongated, worm-like body.
