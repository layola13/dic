ekes out:/iks aʊt/
|v.|补充；勉强维持；使…延长|to make a small supply of something such as food or money last longer by using very small amounts of it|She eked out a living by selling flowers.|近义词:stretch, supplement, augment|反义词:squander, waste|词性:动词

Etymology:
From Middle English *eken out*, from Old English *ēacan ūt* (“to eke out, increase, add to”), from *ēacian* (“to increase, augment, add to”), from *ēaca* (“an addition, increase, augmentation”).
