noncomplacency:/ˌnɒnkəmˈpleɪsənsi/ | n. | 不自满；警觉；居安思危 | The state of not being complacent; a lack of complacency.  Being aware of potential dangers and challenges and remaining vigilant. |  The team's noncomplacency was key to their continued success. 该团队的不自满是他们持续成功的关键。

Their noncomplacency about the virus allowed them to react quickly.  他们对病毒的警觉使他们能够迅速做出反应。| 近义词: vigilance, alertness, watchfulness | 反义词: complacency, satisfaction, smugness | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + complacency.  "Complacency" comes from the Latin "complacēre" (to please greatly), from "com-" (with, together) + "placēre" (to please).  The prefix "non-" simply negates the meaning.
