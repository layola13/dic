disenfranchise: /ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ | vt. | 剥夺权利；剥夺公民权；使失去选举权 | To deprive someone of the right to vote or other rights of citizenship. |  Many people were disenfranchised because of their criminal records. 许多人因为犯罪记录而被剥夺了权利。

The new law disenfranchised thousands of voters.  这项新法律剥夺了数千名选民的投票权。


|近义词: dispossess, deprive, marginalize |反义词: empower, enfranchise | 词性: verb(动词)


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *desfranchiser, from des- (expressing reversal) + franchiser “to make free,” from franc “free.”  最终源自法兰克语，意为“自由”。
