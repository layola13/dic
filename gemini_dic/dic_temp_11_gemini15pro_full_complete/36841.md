colonize:/ˈkɑːlənaɪz/ /ˈkɒlənaɪz/ | vt.|殖民，拓殖，移植，(生物)移生于，(细菌)在…繁殖|To establish a colony in; to settle; to migrate to and establish control over (a territory or place); to establish a colony of (a plant or animal) in a new place. |European powers colonized much of Africa in the 19th century. 欧洲列强在19世纪殖民了非洲的大部分地区。| The scientists colonized the island with a new species of bird. 科学家们在这个岛上移植了一种新的鸟类。 |近义词：settle, populate, inhabit, occupy|反义词: decolonize, evacuate|词性:动词

colonize:/ˈkɑːlənaɪz/ /ˈkɒlənaɪz/ | vi.|建立殖民地，形成菌落|To form or establish a colony; (of bacteria) to grow and multiply on or within a surface or tissue.|The pilgrims colonized New England. 清教徒在纽西兰建立了殖民地。|Bacteria can colonize the gut and cause illness. 细菌可以在肠道内繁殖并引起疾病。|近义词：settle, multiply, proliferate|反义词: decolonize, disappear|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *colonizāre, from Latin colōnus (“farmer, husbandman, settler, colonist”), from colō (“I till, cultivate, inhabit”).
