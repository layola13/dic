dewfall:/ˈdjuːfɔːl/| n.|露水；露水的降落|Moisture condensed from the atmosphere, especially at night, and deposited in the form of small drops upon any cool surface.|The dewfall was heavy this morning, soaking the grass.|Synonyms: dew, condensation|Antonyms: evaporation, dryness|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dēawfeall, from dēaw (dew) + feall (fall).  The word literally means "dew fall".
