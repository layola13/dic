carburizer:/ˈkɑːrbəˌraɪzər/ | n. |渗碳剂；增碳剂|A substance used to introduce carbon into the surface of steel or other metals in order to harden it.|The carburizer was used to increase the surface hardness of the gears.|Synonyms: case hardening compound, cementing agent|Antonyms: decarburizer|noun
