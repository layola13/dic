diker:/ˈdaɪkər/| n. |小羚羊，堤坝，筑堤者|A small antelope found in Africa and Asia. Also, a ditch or water channel, and one who constructs dikes.|例句：The diker grazed peacefully in the tall grass. 小羚羊在高高的草丛中安静地吃草。

|例句：The farmer built a diker to irrigate his fields. 农民修建了一条堤坝来灌溉他的田地。|近义词：antelope (for the animal), levee, embankment (for the structure)|反义词: (No direct antonym) |词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *dīker, from *dīken  "to make a dike," from Proto-Germanic *dīkijaną "to make a dike," from *dīkaz "dike, ditch."  The animal sense emerged later, likely due to the antelope's preference for habitats near water.
