myelins:/ˈmaɪəlɪnz/ | n. |髓磷脂（复数）| A mixture of proteins and phospholipids forming a whitish insulating sheath around many nerve fibers, increasing the speed at which impulses are conducted. |  Myelins are essential for the proper functioning of the nervous system. 髓磷脂对神经系统的正常运作至关重要。|  Damage to myelins can lead to serious neurological problems. 髓磷脂损伤会导致严重的神经系统问题。| synonym: myelin sheaths | antonym: None (Myelin is a necessary component; there's no direct opposite.) | noun
