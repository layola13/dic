locusting:/ˈloʊkəstɪŋ/ | n. |蝗灾，蝗虫群的侵袭；（尤指短期大量的）涌入，聚集|The act of swarming or migrating in large numbers like locusts; a sudden influx or concentration, especially of people, in a particular place. |The locusting of tourists during the summer months overwhelms the small town. 夏天几个月里游客的大量涌入让这个小镇不堪重负。| 近义词：swarming, influx, invasion, inundation | 反义词：emigration, departure, exodus |词性:名词


locusting:/ˈloʊkəstɪŋ/ | adj. |像蝗虫一样移动的；破坏性极大的|Resembling the movement or destructive behavior of locusts. |The locusting hordes of shoppers stripped the shelves bare in minutes.  像蝗虫一样的购物者大军几分钟内就把货架上的东西抢购一空。| 近义词：destructive, rapacious, voracious | 反义词：constructive, beneficial |词性:形容词

词源: From locust + -ing.  Derived from the noun "locust," referring to the short-horned grasshopper known for swarming and devouring vegetation. The "-ing" suffix transforms it into a noun or adjective describing the action or characteristic of locusts.
