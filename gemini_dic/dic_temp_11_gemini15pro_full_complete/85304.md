harnessing:/ˈhɑːrnɪsɪŋ/
verb
利用，控制
controlling and making use of (natural resources), especially to produce energy
例句：They are harnessing the power of the sun to generate electricity. 他们正在利用太阳能发电。

例句：Scientists are working on harnessing the power of nuclear fusion. 科学家们正致力于控制核聚变的力量。

近义词：utilizing, exploiting, employing, channeling
反义词：squandering, wasting, neglecting
词性：v.


harness:/ˈhɑːrnɪs/
noun
马具，挽具
a set of straps and fittings by which a horse or other draught animal is fastened to a cart, plough, etc.
例句：The farmer put the harness on the horse. 农民把马具套在马上。

例句：The leather harness was worn and cracked. 皮革马具破旧开裂。

近义词：tack, gear, equipment
反义词：(None - no direct opposite for a physical object)
词性：n.


harness:/ˈhɑːrnɪs/
verb
给…上挽具；控制，利用
put a harness on (a horse or other draught animal); control and make use of (natural resources), especially to produce energy.
例句：He harnessed the horses to the wagon. 他把马套到马车上。

例句：We must harness the power of the wind and waves. 我们必须利用风和海浪的力量。

近义词：utilize, exploit, employ, channel
反义词：waste, neglect
词性：v.


词源：From Old French *harneis "armor, equipment," from Old Norse *hernest "military equipment”.
