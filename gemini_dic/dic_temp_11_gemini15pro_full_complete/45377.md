cunninger:/ˈkʌnɪŋər/ | adj. | 更狡猾的，更精明的 | Comparative form of "cunning," meaning more skilled in deception or more clever in achieving one's ends. | The cunninger fox outwitted the hounds.  更狡猾的狐狸智胜了猎犬。|  He devised a cunninger plan to escape. 他想出了一个更巧妙的逃跑计划。 | 近义词: craftier, slyer, wilier | 反义词:  naive, guileless | 词性: 形容词

词源:
From Middle English *cunninge*, from Old English *cunnung* (“knowledge, skill, experience”), from Proto-Germanic *kunnaną* (“to know, be able”). Related to can. The comparative *cunninger* is formed by adding *-er*, and the superlative *cunningest* by adding *-est*.
