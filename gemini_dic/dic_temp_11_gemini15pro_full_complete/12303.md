ascocarp: /ˈæskəˌkɑːrp/ | n. | 子囊果 | The fruiting body of an ascomycete fungus, typically containing asci (sac-like structures) which in turn contain ascospores. | Example 1: The ascocarp of the morel mushroom is highly prized by gourmets.  羊肚菌的子囊果很受美食家推崇。

Example 2:  Scientists studied the development of the ascocarp to understand the fungus's reproductive cycle. 科学家研究了子囊果的发育以了解真菌的繁殖周期。 | Synonym: fruiting body, sporocarp | Antonym: N/A | Noun


Etymology: From Modern Latin *ascocarpus, from ascus + -carpus ("fruit").  Ascus comes from Greek askos meaning "sac" or "bag".
