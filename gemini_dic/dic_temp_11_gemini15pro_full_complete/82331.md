groveling:/ˈɡrɒvəlɪŋ/ (US) /ˈɡrɑːvəlɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |卑躬屈膝的，奴颜婢膝的；匍匐的|Acting in a subservient manner, behaving with undue humility, often to gain favor or avoid punishment; crawling on the ground.|例句：He gave a groveling apology to his boss. 他向他的老板卑躬屈膝地道歉。|近义词：fawning, servile, obsequious, abject|反义词：dignified, proud, upright|词性:形容词

grovel:/ˈɡrɒvəl/ (US) /ˈɡrɑːvəl/ (UK)| vi. |卑躬屈膝，奴颜婢膝；匍匐前进|To behave in a subservient or demeaning manner; to crawl on the ground.|例句：He groveled at the feet of the king. 他匍匐在国王的脚下。|近义词：crawl, cringe, fawn|反义词：stand tall, resist|词性:动词

词源: From Old Norse *grufla ("to feel with the hands, grope"), from Proto-Germanic *grub-. Related to grave (v.).
