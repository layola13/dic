mediatization:/ˌmiːdiətaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 媒介化 | The process by which the media come to play a central role in shaping social processes and individual experience.  It refers to the increasing influence of media in various aspects of society, including politics, culture, economics, and daily life. | The mediatization of politics has led to a focus on image and sound bites rather than substantive policy debates. 政治的媒介化导致人们关注形象和简短的口号，而不是实质性的政策辩论。

The increasing mediatization of society is changing how we interact with each other and the world around us.  日益增长的社会媒介化正在改变我们彼此互动以及与周围世界互动的方式。 | 近义词：media influence, media saturation | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, consider perhaps:  isolation, face-to-face interaction) | 词性: noun
