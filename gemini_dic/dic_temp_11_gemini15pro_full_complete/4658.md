alectoromancy:/ˌælɪktərˈoʊmænsi/ | n. | 鸡卜 | Divination by means of a rooster or cock pecking at grain. |  A form of divination in which a rooster is presented with grains of wheat, each representing a letter of the alphabet, and the letters pecked are interpreted to reveal a message. | The ancient Romans sometimes practiced alectoromancy to guide their decisions. 古代罗马人有时会用鸡卜来指导他们的决策。

The villagers resorted to alectoromancy to identify the thief. 村民们 resorted to alectoromancy to identify the thief. | 近义词: divination, augury | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, could consider "scientific method") | 词性: noun
