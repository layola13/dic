nonabsolutes:/nɒnˈæbsəluːts/ | n. | 非绝对事物；非绝对真理 |  Things that are not absolute; principles or values that are not fixed or universally applicable. They are often context-dependent and subject to change. |  Many moral principles are nonabsolutes, shifting according to cultural norms. 许多道德原则并非绝对真理，会根据文化规范而变化。| synonyms: contingencies, variables | antonyms: absolutes, constants | noun

nonabsolute:/nɒnˈæbsəluːt/ | adj. | 非绝对的；不完全的 | Not absolute; not complete or perfect.  |  The success of the project is nonabsolute, depending on various external factors.  这个项目的成功并非绝对的，取决于各种外部因素。 | synonyms: relative, conditional, qualified | antonyms: absolute, unconditional, complete | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "non-" (not) + "absolute" (perfect, complete, unrestricted).  "Absolute" comes from Latin "absolutus," past participle of "absolvere" (to set free, complete).
