flood:/flʌd/| n. v.|洪水，泛滥，涌入|A large amount of water covering an area that is usually dry.  An overflowing of a large expanse of water over land not usually submerged. A large number or amount of something.|The town was devastated by the flood.  这个城镇被洪水摧毁了。|The river flooded the valley. 河水淹没了山谷。|近义词：deluge, inundation, torrent|反义词:drought, ebb|noun
flood:/flʌd/| v.|淹没，涌入，充满|To cover or become covered with a large amount of water. To fill or be filled to overflowing.|The heavy rains flooded the streets.  大雨淹没了街道。|Memories flooded back.  记忆涌上心头。|近义词：inundate, deluge, overwhelm|反义词:drain, dry|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *flōd*, from Proto-Germanic *flōduz* (compare West Frisian *floed*, Dutch *vloed*, German *Flut*, Old Norse *flóð*), from Proto-Indo-European *plew- (“to flow, swim, sail”). Cognate with flow.
