ETYMOLOGY->
From incomparable + -ness. Incomparable stems from Late Middle English, borrowed from Old French incomparable, from in- "not" + comparable "comparable," from Late Latin comparabilis, from comparare "to compare."  The suffix -ness forms nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition.
