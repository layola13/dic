magnetometer:/ˌmæɡnɪˈtɒmɪtər/ (US) /ˌmæɡnɪˈtɑːmɪtər/| n. |磁力计，磁强计|An instrument used for measuring the strength and/or direction of magnetic fields.|例句：The magnetometer detected a significant change in the Earth's magnetic field. 磁力计探测到地球磁场发生了显著变化。

例句：Scientists use magnetometers to study the magnetic properties of rocks. 科学家使用磁力计来研究岩石的磁性。|近义词：magnetic sensor, Gaussmeter|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

magnetometry:/ˌmæɡnɪˈtɒmətri/ (US) /ˌmæɡnɪˈtɑːmətri/| n. |磁力测量法|The technique of measuring magnetic fields.|例句：Magnetometry is used in archaeological surveys to detect buried objects. 磁力测量法用于考古调查以探测埋藏的物体。

例句：Spacecraft use magnetometry to study the magnetic fields of planets.  航天器使用磁力测量法来研究行星的磁场。|近义词：magnetic field measurement|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

词源:  From magnet + -o- + -meter.  "Magnet" ultimately derives from the Greek Μαγνήτης λίθος (Magnētēs lithos), meaning "Magnesian stone," referring to lodestone found in Magnesia, a region in ancient Greece. "-meter" comes from the Greek μέτρον (metron), meaning "measure."
