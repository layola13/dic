implacable:/ɪmˈplækəbəl/| adj.|无情的，不妥协的，无法平息的|not capable of being appeased, significantly changed, or mitigated|例句：His implacable hatred for his brother led to a lifelong feud. 他对他兄弟的 implacable 仇恨导致了终生的不和。|近义词：unyielding, relentless, inexorable, ruthless|反义词：merciful, compassionate, forgiving, yielding|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Late Latin *implacabilis, from Latin in- (“not”) + placabilis (“able to be appeased”), from placare (“to appease”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *plāk- (“to be flat, spread out, soothe”).
