brigs:/brɪɡz/ | n. | 双桅横帆船;监狱(尤指船上的); |A two-masted square-rigged sailing ship; a prison (especially on board a ship). | The pirates seized the merchant's brigs and plundered their cargo. 海盗劫持了商人的双桅船并掠夺了他们的货物。| The mutineers were confined to the brigs below deck. 叛变者被关押在甲板下的监狱里。 | synonyms: ship, vessel, jail, prison | antonyms: freedom, liberty | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English brigge, from Old North French brigue, of uncertain origin, possibly from Old Provençal briga "fight, strife," or from a Germanic source akin to Middle Low German brigge "bridge," referring to the raised quarterdeck of such ships. The sense of "prison" arose from the use of such ships as prison hulks.
