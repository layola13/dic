cardinal: /ˈkɑːrdɪnl/ (US) /ˈkɑːdɪnəl/ (UK)| adj. n.|主要的；基本的；深红色的；枢机主教|A cardinal rule or quality is one that is considered to be the most important or basic.  A cardinal is a priest of very high rank in the Roman Catholic Church.|例句：Honesty is a cardinal virtue.  诚实是一项重要的美德。|近义词：principal, main, chief, fundamental, essential, basic, primary|反义词：minor, secondary, unimportant|词性: adjective, noun

词源：From Old French cardinal and directly from Latin cardinalis “principal, chief, essential,” from cardo (genitive cardinis) “hinge, pivot, turning point.”  The sense development is from “that on which something turns or depends” to “principal, chief.”
