conflux:/ˈkɒnflʌks/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnflʌks/ (US)| n.|汇合；汇流；汇集；（人或事物的）聚集，合流|A flowing together of two or more streams. A coming together of people or things.|例句：The annual music festival becomes a conflux of different cultures.  一年一度的音乐节成了不同文化的交汇点。

例句：The city is a conflux of rivers and canals. 这座城市是河流和运河的汇集处。|近义词：convergence, junction, meeting, confluence, intersection, merging|反义词：divergence, separation, parting|词性: noun


confluence:/ˈkɒnfluəns/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnfluəns/ (US)| n.|（河流的）汇合处，交汇处；汇流点；（人或事物的）聚集，合流|The place where two rivers flow together, or the flowing together of two rivers.  A situation in which two things join or come together.|例句：The confluence of the two rivers creates a fertile valley. 两条河流的交汇处形成了一个肥沃的山谷。

例句：The conference was a confluence of great minds. 这次会议是伟大思想的汇聚。|近义词：convergence, junction, meeting, conflux, intersection, merging|反义词：divergence, separation, parting|词性: noun

词源: From Latin confluentem (nominative confluens) "flowing together," present participle of confluere "to flow together," from com- "together" (see com-) + fluere "to flow" (see fluent).
