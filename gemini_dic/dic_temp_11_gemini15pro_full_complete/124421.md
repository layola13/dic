motorcades:/ˈmoʊtərkeɪdz/ (US), /ˈməʊtəkeɪdz/ (UK)| n. |车队，汽车行列|A procession of motor vehicles, typically carrying an important person.|例句：The president's motorcade made its way slowly through the crowded streets. 总统的车队缓缓穿过拥挤的街道。

例句：Large motorcades are often used for transporting heads of state.  大型车队通常用于运送国家元首。|近义词：procession, convoy, caravan |反义词: N/A |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "motor" + "-cade" (a suffix denoting a procession or parade). "Motor" ultimately comes from the Latin "movēre" meaning "to move."  "-Cade" comes from the French "-cade" and ultimately the Italian "-cata," which is a feminine past participle suffix.
