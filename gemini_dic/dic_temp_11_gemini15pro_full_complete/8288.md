anti-malthusian:/ˌæntiˈmælθuːziən/
anti-malthusian: |adj. n.| 反马尔萨斯主义的；反马尔萨斯主义者 |Relating to or characteristic of a theory that opposes Malthus's pessimistic view of population growth, especially his prediction that population will always tend to outrun the food supply.|  The economist held an anti-Malthusian perspective, believing that technological advancements could outpace population growth. 这位经济学家持反马尔萨斯主义的观点，认为科技进步可以超过人口增长。|Near synonyms:  Cornucopian, optimistic (regarding population growth) | Antonyms: Malthusian, pessimistic (regarding population growth)|adjective, noun


anti-malthusian:/ˌæntiˈmælθuːziən/
anti-malthusian:|n.|反马尔萨斯主义者| A person who believes that Malthus's predictions about population growth are incorrect. |He's an anti-Malthusian, arguing that human ingenuity will always find solutions to resource scarcity. 他是一个反马尔萨斯主义者，认为人类的智慧总能找到解决资源短缺的办法。 |Near synonyms: optimist, cornucopian | Antonyms: Malthusian, pessimist| noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + Malthusian, named after Thomas Robert Malthus (1766–1834), an English economist and demographer who argued that population growth would eventually outstrip food supply, leading to widespread famine and misery.
