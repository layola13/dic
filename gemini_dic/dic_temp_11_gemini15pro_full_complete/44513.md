crossopterygian: /ˌkrɒsəpˈtɛrɪdʒɪən/ /ˌkrɔːsəpˈtɛrɪdʒiən/ | n. adj. |  总鳍鱼；总鳍鱼类的 | A bony fish of a largely extinct group having fleshy lobed paired fins which are thought to have been used for crawling.  A group of primitive bony fishes possessing lungs and fleshy, lobed fins, believed to be ancestral to amphibians. |  The coelacanth is a living crossopterygian.  腔棘鱼是一种现存的总鳍鱼。 | Synonyms: lobe-finned fish, rhipidistian | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could potentially list a specific fish group that is not closely related, like "ray-finned fish") | noun, adjective
