designate:/ˈdɛzɪɡneɪt/| v. adj.|指定；指派；标明；称呼|To officially give someone or something a particular name, title, or status; to mark or point out clearly.|The area has been designated as a national park.  这个区域已被指定为国家公园。|appoint, assign, name, nominate|reject, refuse|verb
designate:/ˈdɛzɪɡneɪt/| adj.|指定的；选定的|Chosen or appointed for a particular purpose.|He is the designated driver for the night. 他是被指定的今晚的司机。|appointed, chosen, selected|unselected, unappointed|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *designatus*, past participle of designare "to mark out, indicate, choose," from Latin de- "out" + signare "to mark," from signum "mark, sign."
