caligula:/kəˈlɪɡjʊlə/| n. |卡利古拉（罗马帝国皇帝）|Gaius Caesar Augustus Germanicus (AD 12 – 41), Roman emperor AD 37–41, nicknamed Caligula ("Little Boots") by the soldiers in his father's army. He was known for his cruelty, extravagance, and supposed insanity.|Caligula is considered one of the most tyrannical Roman emperors. 卡利古拉被认为是罗马帝国最残暴的皇帝之一。|Synonym: tyrant, despot|Antonym:  benevolent ruler|Noun
