marquee: /mɑːrˈkiː/ | n. |  大帐篷；华盖；遮篷；（剧院等的）大型入口处的遮篷 | A large tent or a structure with a canvas roof, especially one used for a social event.  Also, a canopy projecting over the entrance to a theater, hotel, etc. | The wedding reception was held under a large marquee. 婚礼招待会在一个大帐篷下举行。

The hotel had a grand marquee over its entrance. 这家酒店的入口上方有一个宏伟的遮篷。 | synonyms: canopy, awning, tent | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps "building", "interior") | noun


marquee: /mɑːrˈkiː/ | adj. |  (figurative) Leading; preeminent; outstanding. |  A prominent or leading position.  Often used in phrases like "marquee name" or "marquee player". | They signed a marquee player to boost their team's performance.  他们签下了一名明星球员来提升球队的表现。

The conference featured several marquee speakers from the tech industry. 这次会议邀请了几位科技行业的杰出演讲者。 | synonyms: prominent, leading, star, top, chief | antonyms: minor, insignificant, unknown | adjective
