accommodate: /əˈkɑːmədeɪt/ (US) /əˈkɒmədeɪt/ (UK)| v.| 容纳；提供住宿；适应；顾及|To provide lodging or sufficient space for; to adapt to; to oblige or help someone; to consider and adjust plans accordingly. |The hotel can accommodate up to 500 guests. 这家酒店最多可容纳500位客人。

The company accommodated the employee's request for a flexible schedule. 公司同意了员工弹性工作时间的请求。|近义词：house, lodge, adapt, adjust, oblige|反义词：displace, reject, refuse|词性：动词

accommodation: /əˌkɑːməˈdeɪʃn/ (US) /əˌkɒməˈdeɪʃn/ (UK)| n.| 住宿；膳宿；适应；和解|A place to live or stay, especially on holiday; the process of adapting or adjusting to something; a settlement or compromise.|We found comfortable accommodations at a small bed and breakfast. 我们在一个小型的住宿加早餐旅馆找到了舒适的住处。

The two parties reached an accommodation after hours of negotiation. 双方经过数小时的谈判达成了和解。|近义词：lodging, housing, adaptation, adjustment, compromise|反义词：disagreement, conflict|词性：名词

PHRASE->
accommodate to: 适应 (accommodate to the new environment 适应新环境)
accommodate with: 向…提供；供应 (accommodate someone with a loan 向某人提供贷款)
accommodate oneself to: 使自己适应于 (accommodate oneself to the local customs 使自己适应当地风俗)
make accommodations for: 为…提供方便；为…做出安排 (make accommodations for disabled guests 为残疾客人提供方便)
comfortable accommodations: 舒适的住宿 (We booked comfortable accommodations for our trip. 我们为旅行预订了舒适的住宿。)


词源：From Latin accommodatus, past participle of accommodare ("to fit to, adjust to"), from ad- ("to") + commodare ("to make fit"), from commodus ("suitable, convenient").
