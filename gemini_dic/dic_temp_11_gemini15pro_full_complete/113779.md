macrosociological:/ˌmækroʊˌsoʊsiəˈlɒdʒɪkl/ | adj. |宏观社会学的| Relating to macrosociology, the study of large-scale social systems and processes. | Macrosociological theories attempt to explain societal changes. 宏观社会学理论试图解释社会变革。| Macrosociological perspectives offer valuable insights. 宏观社会学的视角提供了宝贵的见解。 | large-scale, societal, global | microsociological | adjective

Etymology:
"macro-" + "sociological"
"macro-" from Greek "makrós" meaning "long, large"
"sociological" from "sociology"  + "-ical"
"sociology" from French "sociologie," coined by Auguste Comte from Latin "socius" (companion) + Greek "lógos" (word, study)
"-ical" a suffix forming adjectives from nouns ending in "-ic" or "-ics"
