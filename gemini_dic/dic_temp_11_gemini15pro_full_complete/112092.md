longirostres:/ˌlɒŋɪˈrɒstriːz/ | n. |长喙类 |A taxonomic group (usually a suborder) of birds characterized by having long beaks.  Specifically refers to the Longirostres, a former suborder that included snipes, woodcocks, and sandpipers. Now, these birds are classified under the suborder Scolopaci within the order Charadriiformes. |  The longirostres, such as the woodcock, are known for their probing beaks. 长喙类动物，如丘鹬，以其探测用的喙而闻名。 | synonyms: long-billed birds,  Scolopaci (more accurate modern term) | antonyms:  brevirostres (short-beaked) | noun
