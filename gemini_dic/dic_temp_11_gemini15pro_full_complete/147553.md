overpenalized: /ˌoʊvərˈpiːnəlaɪzd/
|adj.|过度惩罚的，处罚过重的|Being punished too harshly or excessively for a particular offense or error.|The defendant argued that he was overpenalized for a minor infraction.|Synonyms: overpunished, excessively penalized|Antonyms: underpunished, leniently penalized|adjective


overpenalize: /ˌoʊvərˈpiːnəlaɪz/
|verb (vt.)|过度惩罚，处罚过重|To punish someone too harshly or excessively for a particular offense or error.|The judge was accused of overpenalizing first-time offenders.|Synonyms: overpunish, excessively penalize|Antonyms: underpunish, leniently penalize|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "penalize" (from "penal" ultimately derived from Latin "poena" meaning "punishment").
