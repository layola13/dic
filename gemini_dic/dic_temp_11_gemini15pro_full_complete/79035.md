give free rein to:/ɡɪv friː reɪn tuː/ |v. phr.|放任；充分发挥；使自由发展|to allow someone or something complete freedom of action or expression|He gave free rein to his imagination and wrote a brilliant fantasy novel. 他充分发挥自己的想象力，写了一部精彩的奇幻小说。|近义词：indulge, allow, unleash, unbridle|反义词: restrict, control, curb, restrain|词性: 动词短语

give free rein to:/ɡɪv friː reɪn tuː/ |v. phr.|放任；充分发挥；使自由发展|The artist gave free rein to her creativity and produced a stunning masterpiece.  这位艺术家充分发挥了她的创造力，创作出了一幅令人惊叹的杰作。|近义词：indulge, allow, unleash, unbridle|反义词: restrict, control, curb, restrain|词性: 动词短语


ETYMOLOGY->
From the equestrian practice of loosening the reins to allow a horse to run freely.  "Rein" itself comes from Old French "resne," ultimately from Latin "retinere" (to hold back). The imagery is of releasing control or restraint.
