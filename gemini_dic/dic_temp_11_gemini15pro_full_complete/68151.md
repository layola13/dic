failing:/ˈfeɪlɪŋ/| n. |失败；缺点；不足之处|a weakness or fault in someone or something| Her biggest failing is her inability to listen. 她最大的缺点就是不会倾听。|synonyms: weakness, shortcoming, fault, defect, flaw | antonyms: strength, virtue, merit|noun
failing:/ˈfeɪlɪŋ/| prep. |如果没有；若非|in the absence of; without| Failing her help, we shall have to abandon the project. 如果没有她的帮助，我们将不得不放弃这个项目。|synonyms: without, lacking, in the absence of | antonyms: with, having|preposition
failing:/ˈfeɪlɪŋ/| adj. |衰退的；衰弱的；失败的|becoming weaker; unsuccessful| a failing business 衰败的企业 | synonyms: declining, deteriorating, weakening, unsuccessful | antonyms: thriving, flourishing, successful|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *failinge, present participle of failen (“to fail”), from Old French faillir (“to fail, miss, err, be lacking”), from Vulgar Latin *fallire, from Latin fallere (“to deceive, trick, disappoint, escape the notice of”).
