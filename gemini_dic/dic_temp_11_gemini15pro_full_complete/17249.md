bayardly:/ˈbeɪərdli/| adj.|卑鄙的，怯懦的|Showing or characterized by contemptible timidity or lack of courage.  Resembling or characteristic of a false knight.  |He committed a bayardly act of betrayal, abandoning his comrades in the face of danger.|近义词: cowardly, craven, dastardly, pusillanimous|反义词: brave, courageous, valiant, heroic|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Bayard, the name of the magic horse belonging to the four sons of Aymon in medieval French romance, later applied ironically to a spiritless or cowardly horse, and then to cowardly people.
