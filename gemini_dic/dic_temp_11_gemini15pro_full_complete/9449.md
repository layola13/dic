antireductive:/ˌæntiɹɪˈdʌktɪv/ | adj. | 反还原论的; 不可简化的 | Characterized by the belief that something cannot be explained by reducing it to its constituent parts; opposing reductionism. |  Her antireductive approach to literature emphasized the importance of interpreting texts as wholes. 她反还原论的文学研究方法强调将文本作为一个整体来解读的重要性。

The scientist took an antireductive stance, arguing that consciousness couldn't be fully explained by brain chemistry alone.  这位科学家采取了反还原论的立场，认为意识不能仅仅用大脑化学来完全解释。 | 近义词: holistic, synthetic | 反义词: reductionist, analytic | 词性: adjective

Etymology:  "anti-" (prefix meaning against, opposing) + "reductive" (from "reduce," ultimately from Latin "reducere" - to lead back, bring back).
