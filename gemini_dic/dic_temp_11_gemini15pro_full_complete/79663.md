glottals:/ˈɡlɒtəlz/ (UK), /ˈɡlɑːtəlz/ (US)| n.| 声门音|Sounds produced by the glottis, the opening between the vocal cords. |Example: The glottals in Arabic can be challenging for English speakers to master.  阿拉伯语中的声门音对于英语母语者来说很难掌握。

Example:  The linguist studied the various glottals used in different languages.  这位语言学家研究了不同语言中使用的各种声门音。|近义词: guttural sounds|反义词: (No direct antonym, as it refers to a specific type of sound)|词性: noun
词源:  From "glottis" + "-al" (suffix forming adjectives).  "Glottis" comes from Greek "glōttis" (tongue, windpipe).
