despisedness:/dɪˈspaɪzɪdnəs/| n. |被鄙视；被憎恶|The state of being despised; the condition of being regarded with contempt or disgust.|His despisedness made him a social outcast. 他被人鄙视，成了社会弃儿。|近义词：contempt, disdain, scorn, odium|反义词：admiration, respect, esteem|词性:名词
Etymology: From despise (v.) + -ed (suffix forming past participles and adjectives) + -ness (suffix forming nouns from adjectives).  Despise ultimately comes from the Old French *despiser*, from Latin *despicere* "look down on," from *de-* "down" + *specere* "to look."
