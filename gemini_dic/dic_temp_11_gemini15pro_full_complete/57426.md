ETYMOLOGY->
From drab (adjective) + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing a state or quality).
The adjective "drab" originates from the Old French word "drape" meaning "cloth" or "woollen fabric," specifically referring to undyed or natural-colored cloth, which was typically dull and brownish. This sense of dullness in color extended to a more general meaning of dullness or dreariness.
