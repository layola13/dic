compellative:/kəmˈpelətɪv/| adj. n.|称呼的，称呼语|Used to or intended to compel; compelling.  Also, a word or form of address.|例句：He used a compellative tone to get the children's attention. 他用一种命令式的语气来引起孩子们的注意。

例句： "Sir" is a common compellative. “先生”是一个常见的称呼。|近义词：vocative, appellative|反义词: None|词性: adjective, noun

Etymology:  From Late Latin *compellātīvus, from Latin compellāre (to address, accost), from com- (with, together) + appellāre (to call, name). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *pel- (to push, drive, strike).
