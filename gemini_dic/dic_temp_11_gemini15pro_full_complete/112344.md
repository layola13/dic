lorica:/ləˈraɪkə/ | n. | 胸甲；(某些原生动物的)壳 |A protective case, shell, or covering, as of a rotifer, infusorian, or crustacean.  Also, a corselet of leather or metal, as worn by Roman soldiers.| The Roman soldier's lorica protected him from enemy blows.  罗马士兵的胸甲保护他免受敌人打击。 |Synonyms: armor, cuirass, breastplate, shell, casing |Antonyms: vulnerability, exposure | noun

lorica:/ləˈraɪkə/ | n. | (某些原生动物的)壳 | A protective outer covering, especially of certain protozoans. | The lorica of the paramecium helps it maintain its shape. 草履虫的壳 helps maintain its shape. | Synonyms: test, theca, shell | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical structure, but conceptually: softness, fragility) | noun
