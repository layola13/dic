kaleidoscope:/kəˈlaɪdəˌskoʊp/| n. |万花筒；千变万化|A tube containing mirrors and pieces of colored glass or paper, whose reflections produce changing patterns when the tube is rotated.  A constantly changing pattern or sequence of objects or elements.|例句：The changing political landscape is a kaleidoscope of different ideologies.  不断变化的政治格局就像一个由不同意识形态组成的万花筒。

例句：Looking through the kaleidoscope brought back childhood memories.  透过万花筒看，勾起了童年的回忆。|近义词：mirage, display, spectacle|反义词:monotony, uniformity|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *kalos* "beautiful" + *eidos* "form, shape" + *-skopos* "watcher, observer".  Essentially, a "viewer of beautiful forms".
