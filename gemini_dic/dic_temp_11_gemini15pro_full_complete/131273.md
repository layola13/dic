noncausative:/ˌnɒnˈkɔːzətɪv/ | adj. | 非使役的，非致使的 |  Describing a verb or construction where the subject undergoes or experiences the action rather than causing it.  | The window broke. (The window experienced the breaking, it didn't cause it.) 窗户破了。|  intransitive, nonagentive | causative | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->

From non- + causative.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of." "Causative" refers to causing something to happen.

PHRASE->

(While "noncausative" itself doesn't typically appear in phrases, it describes verbs used in common constructions. Here are examples of intransitive verbs often used in a noncausative sense:)

The lights went out. 灯灭了。
The car broke down. 车坏了。
The door blew open. 门被风吹开了。
The window cracked. 窗户裂开了。
The food spoiled. 食物变质了。
