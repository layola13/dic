bos'ns:/ˈboʊsənz/ /ˈbəʊsnz/| n.| 水手长；舵手| A petty officer on a merchant ship or a warrant officer or chief petty officer in a navy, having charge of the anchors, ropes, rigging, and deck crew.| The bos'n piped all hands on deck. 水手长吹哨召集全体船员到甲板上。| 近义词：boatswain, deck officer | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps landlubber as a humorous contrast) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *botsweyn, from Old English *bātswegen (“boatman, boatswain”), from bāt (“boat”) + swegen (“swain, servant”). Cognate with Dutch bootsman.
