kerfuffle:/kərˈfʌf(ə)l/
noun
混乱；骚动；小题大做
a commotion or fuss, especially one caused by conflicting views
例句：There was a bit of a kerfuffle over the seating arrangements.  座位安排上有点小题大做。

例句：The sudden change in policy caused a kerfuffle among the staff. 政策的突然改变在员工中引起了一阵骚动。

近义词：fuss, commotion, disturbance, uproar, hubbub, brouhaha
反义词：calm, peace, tranquility
词性：n.


ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain, perhaps imitative. First recorded in the mid 19th century. Possibly related to Scottish *carfuffle, of uncertain origin.
