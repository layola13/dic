antistrophe:/ænˈtɪstrəfi/ | n. | 反strophe; 回旋歌; |  In classical Greek drama, the antistrophe is the second section of an ode, sung by the chorus as they move in the opposite direction from the strophe. It corresponds metrically to the strophe. | The chorus sang the antistrophe with grace and power. 合唱团优雅而有力地演唱了回旋歌。| synonym: counterstrophe | antonym: strophe | noun

antistrophe:/ænˈtɪstrəfi/ | n. | 反复; 反句; |  In rhetoric, antistrophe refers to the repetition of the same word or phrase at the end of successive clauses, sentences, or paragraphs.  | His speech used antistrophe to emphasize the urgency of the situation. 他的演讲用反复强调了情况的紧迫性。| synonym: epistrophe | antonym: anaphora | noun
