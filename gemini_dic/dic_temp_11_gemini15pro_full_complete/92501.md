hypothesised:/haɪˈpɒθəsaɪzd/ (UK) /haɪˈpɑːθəsaɪzd/ (US)| v. |假设的，假定的| put (something) forward as a hypothesis.|例句：It is hypothesised that the universe is expanding.  有人假设宇宙正在膨胀。|例句：Scientists have hypothesised a link between the two phenomena. 科学家们假设这两种现象之间存在联系。|近义词：theorized, postulated, supposed, assumed|反义词：proven, demonstrated|词性：动词

hypothetical:/ˌhaɪpəˈθetɪk(ə)l/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpəˈθetɪk(ə)l/ (US)| adj. |假设的，假定的| of, based on, or serving as a hypothesis.|例句：A hypothetical situation was presented to the students for discussion.  向学生们提出了一个假设的情况以供讨论。|例句：Let's consider a hypothetical case.  让我们考虑一个假设的案例。|近义词：theoretical, conjectural, supposed, assumed|反义词：actual, real, factual|词性：形容词

hypothesising:/haɪˈpɒθəsaɪzɪŋ/ (UK)  /haɪˈpɑːθəsaɪzɪŋ/ (US) | v. |假设，假定 (hypothesise的现在分词形式)| the act of putting something forward as a hypothesis |例句：They are hypothesising about the cause of the fire. 他们正在假设火灾的原因。|例句：He was hypothesising a link between diet and disease. 他在假设饮食和疾病之间的联系。|近义词：theorizing, postulating, supposing, assuming |反义词：proving, demonstrating|词性：动词


词源:
From Late Latin *hypothesizare, from Ancient Greek ὑποτίθεσθαι (hupotíthesthai, “to suppose”), from ὑπό (hupó, “under”) + τίθεσθαι (títhesthai, “to put, place”).
