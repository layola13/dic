heart:/hɑːrt/ (US), /hɑːt/ (UK)| n. v.| 心脏；中心；要点；感情；勇气|The core, central part of something, often associated with emotions, courage, and the seat of life.  Also used as a verb to encourage or love something deeply.|
She listened to her heart. 她听从了自己的内心。
His heart was pounding in his chest. 他的心脏在胸腔里怦怦直跳。
He hearted the photo on Instagram. 他在Instagram上给这张照片点了赞。
|近义词：core, center, essence, feeling, courage, spirit|反义词:exterior, periphery, cowardice|词性:noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English "heorte," from Proto-Germanic "*hertō," from Proto-Indo-European "*ḱḗr," meaning "heart."  Cognates include German "Herz," Dutch "hart," and Latin "cor" (whence "cardiac").
