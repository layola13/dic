ganoidei:/ˌɡæˈnɔɪdiˌaɪ/ | n. | 硬鳞鱼类 | A group of ray-finned fishes characterized by ganoid scales, which are thick, bony, rhombic, and non-overlapping.  They include gars, bichirs, and sturgeons. | Example: Ganoidei are an ancient group of fish. 硬鳞鱼类是一种古老的鱼类。 | Example:  Scientists study Ganoidei to understand fish evolution. 科学家研究硬鳞鱼类以了解鱼类的进化。 | Synonym: ganoid fishes | Antonym:  (No direct antonym; could possibly use teleosts (真骨鱼类) as a contrasting group, but not a true opposite) | Noun
