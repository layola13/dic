floaty:/ˈfloʊti/ (US), /ˈfləʊti/ (UK)| adj.|轻飘飘的，飘浮的|Light and airy; tending to float or drift on air or water.  Often used to describe fabrics or sensations.|The dress was made of a floaty, sheer material. 这条裙子是用轻飘飘的透明材料制成的。|The medication made her feel floaty and disconnected.  这种药让她感觉轻飘飘的，与现实脱节。|近义词：airy, buoyant, ethereal, diaphanous|反义词：heavy, weighty, substantial|词性:形容词

floaty:/ˈfloʊti/ (US), /ˈfləʊti/ (UK)| n.|轻飘飘的东西 (less common)|Something that floats, or has a floaty quality (This usage is less common than the adjective form).|The floaty caught the wind and carried the fishing line downstream.  浮标顺着风，把鱼线带到下游去了。(This example uses "floaty" as a noun referring to a fishing float, which is a specific object, not the general sense of "something floaty")|She wore several floatys in her hair for the festival. 她为了节日在头发上戴了几个轻飘飘的装饰。(This example uses "floatys" as a somewhat informal plural noun.)|近义词：buoyant, driftwood (if referring to floating objects)|反义词：anchor, weight (if referring to objects that sink)|词性:名词
