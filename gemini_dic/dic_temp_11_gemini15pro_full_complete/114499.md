malacozoa:/ˌmæləˈkəʊzə/ | n. | 软体动物 |A large phylum of invertebrate animals (as snails, clams, and mussels) that have a soft unsegmented body usually enclosed in a shell containing calcium carbonate, a mantle, and a radula. | Mollusks like snails and clams are classified under the phylum Malacozoa.  蜗牛和蛤蜊等软体动物被归类于软体动物门。|Synonyms: Mollusca, mollusks |Antonyms: Vertebrata (vertebrates)| noun

malacozoa:/ˌmæləˈkəʊzə/ | n. | 软体动物 |The phylum includes snails, slugs, clams, mussels, octopuses, and squid.  该门包括蜗牛、蛞蝓、蛤蜊、贻贝、章鱼和鱿鱼。|Synonyms: Mollusca, mollusks |Antonyms: Vertebrata (vertebrates)| noun
