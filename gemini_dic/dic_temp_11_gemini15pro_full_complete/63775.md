epicure:/ˈɛpɪkjʊr/ /ˈɛpɪkjər/| n.|美食家；享乐主义者|A person who takes particular pleasure in fine food and drink; a connoisseur.|He was an epicure, savoring every bite of the exquisite meal. 他是一位美食家，细细品味着这顿佳肴。|Synonyms: gourmand, gourmet, connoisseur|Antonyms: ascetic, abstainer|noun

epicurean:/ˌɛpɪˈkjʊəriən/ /ˌɛpɪˈkjʊriːən/| adj. n.|享乐主义的；享乐主义者|Devoted to the pursuit of pleasure; relating to Epicurus or his philosophy.|He led an epicurean lifestyle, indulging in every luxury. 他过着享乐主义的生活，沉溺于各种奢侈品。  The restaurant offered an epicurean experience, with its elegant decor and delectable cuisine. 这家餐厅环境优雅，菜肴美味，提供了一种享乐主义的体验。|Synonyms: hedonistic, luxurious, sybaritic|Antonyms: ascetic, austere|adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *épicurien*, from Latin *Epicūreus*, from Greek *Epikouros* (Ἐπίκουρος), the name of the Greek philosopher Epicurus (341-270 BC), who advocated a philosophy of pleasure based on freedom from pain and anxiety.
