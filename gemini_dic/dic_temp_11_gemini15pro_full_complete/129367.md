nomad:/ˈnoʊmæd/ (US), /ˈnəʊmæd/ (UK)| n.| 游牧民；流浪者|A member of a people or tribe that has no permanent abode but moves about from place to place, usually seasonally and often following a traditional route or circuit according to the availability of grazing, etc.|The nomads travelled across the desert seeking fresh pasture for their animals. 这些游牧民穿越沙漠，为他们的牲畜寻找新鲜的牧场。|近义词：wanderer, migrant, rover, itinerant|反义词：settler, resident|词性:名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *nomas, nomad-, from Greek nomas (genitive nomados) "roaming, wandering, pasturing," from nemein "to pasture, distribute, allot."
