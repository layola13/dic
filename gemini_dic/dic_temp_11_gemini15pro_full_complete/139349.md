obscurantism: /əbˈskjʊrənˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 蒙昧主义，愚民政策 | The practice of deliberately preventing the facts or full details of something from becoming known. |  The government's policy of obscurantism is hindering the progress of scientific research. 政府的愚民政策阻碍了科学研究的进展。

The historian accused the church of obscurantism in suppressing scientific discoveries that challenged its doctrines.  历史学家指责教会压制挑战其教义的科学发现，实行蒙昧主义。 | 近义词:  obfuscation, mystification, suppression | 反义词: enlightenment, clarity, transparency | 词性: 名词

obscurantisms: /əbˈskjʊrənˌtɪzəmz/ | n. | 蒙昧主义（复数） |  Plural form of obscurantism, referring to multiple instances or forms of the practice. | The various obscurantisms of the past have often led to periods of stagnation and decline. 过去的各种蒙昧主义常常导致停滞和衰退时期。

Throughout history, different obscurantisms have attempted to control the flow of information and manipulate public opinion. 纵观历史，不同的蒙昧主义试图控制信息流动和操纵公众舆论。 | 近义词: obfuscations, mystifications, suppressions | 反义词: enlightenments, clarities, transparencies | 词性: 名词
