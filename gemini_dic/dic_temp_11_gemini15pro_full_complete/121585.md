misobserver:/ˌmɪsəbˈzɜːrvər/ | n. | 错误观察者；观察错误的人 |  Someone who observes incorrectly or makes a mistake in their observation. | The misobserver reported seeing a UFO, but it turned out to be a weather balloon.  错误的观察者报告说看到了不明飞行物，但结果证明那是一个气象气球。| 近义词: inaccurate observer, faulty witness | 反义词: accurate observer, careful observer | 词性: noun

Etymology:
mis- (prefix): wrongly, badly (from Old English and Old French)
observe (verb): watch attentively, notice (from Latin observare, from ob- "toward" + servare "to watch, keep")
-er (suffix): denoting a person or thing that performs a specified action (from Latin -or, -tor)
