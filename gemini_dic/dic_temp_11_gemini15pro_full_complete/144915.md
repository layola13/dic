over-talkative: /ˌoʊvərˈtɔːkətɪv/ | adj. | 过于健谈的，话多的 | excessively talkative; tending to talk too much | He's a nice guy, but a bit over-talkative at times. 他是个好人，但有时有点太话多了。|  She's so over-talkative that it's hard to get a word in edgewise. 她太健谈了，很难插上一句话。 | 近义词: loquacious, garrulous, voluble | 反义词: reticent, taciturn, laconic | 词性: adjective

词源:  From "over-" (meaning "excessively") + "talkative" (inclined to talk a great deal). "Talkative" comes from "talk" + "-ative" (forming adjectives).  Ultimately from Middle English *talken*, from Old English *talian* (to talk).
