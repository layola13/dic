isolationist: /ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪst/ | n. adj. | 孤立主义者；孤立主义的 | A person who advocates isolationism, a policy of remaining apart from the affairs or interests of other groups, especially the political affairs of other countries. |  The senator was labeled an isolationist for his opposition to the treaty.  这位参议员因反对该条约而被贴上孤立主义者的标签。|  近义词：noninterventionist, separatist | 反义词：internationalist, interventionist | 词性: noun, adjective

isolationist: /ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪst/ | adj. | 孤立主义的 | Favoring or characterized by isolationism. | The country's isolationist policies hindered its economic growth.  该国孤立主义政策阻碍了其经济增长。 | 近义词：protectionist, exclusionary | 反义词：globalist, internationalist | 词性: adjective

Etymology: From isolation + -ist.  Isolation from isolate, from French isoler, from Italian isolare, from isola "island," from Latin insula "island."
