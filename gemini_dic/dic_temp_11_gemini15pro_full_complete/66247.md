excludability:/ɪksˌkluːdəˈbɪləti/| n. |排他性，可排除性|The quality of being excludable; the ability to prevent someone from consuming a good or service if they do not pay for it.|例句：The excludability of private goods allows businesses to charge for their consumption. 私人物品的排他性允许企业对其消费收费。

例句：The lack of excludability in public goods makes it difficult to charge for them. 公共物品缺乏排他性使得难以对其收费。|近义词：restrictability, limitability|反义词：non-excludability, inclusivity|词性:noun
