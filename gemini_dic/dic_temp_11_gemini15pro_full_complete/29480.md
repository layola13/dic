cataphora:/kəˈtæfərə/| n.|回指|Cataphora is a figure of speech where a word or phrase refers to something that appears later in the text.|After he had finished his work, John went home. (Here, "he" refers to "John" which comes later.)|近义词：forward reference, anticipation|反义词: anaphora|词性:noun

PHRASE->
Examples are hard to come by for cataphoric phrases.  Cataphora is more commonly used with pronouns.  Here are some examples illustrating the concept, but they aren't set phrases:

1. Here they are:  John and Mary. (Here "they" refers to "John and Mary") = 他们在这里：约翰和玛丽。
2. These are the winners: first place, Susan; second place, David. (These refers to the list of winners) = 这些是获胜者：第一名，苏珊；第二名，大卫。
3.  The problem is this: nobody wants to work on Saturday. (this refers to the following explanation of the problem) = 问题是这样的：没人想在周六工作。
4.  His reasoning was as follows: the evidence was weak and the witness unreliable. (as follows introduces the reasoning that comes after) = 他的推理如下：证据薄弱，证人不可靠。
5.  This is what I believe: everyone deserves a second chance.  (This refers to the belief stated afterwards) = 我相信的是：每个人都应该有第二次机会。
