burly:/ˈbɜːrli/ | adj. | 粗鲁的，粗暴的 |  (of a person's appearance or manner) rough and somewhat aggressive. |  He had a burly and intimidating demeanor. 他有着粗鲁而吓人的举止。 | Synonyms: gruff, rough, brusque, surly | Antonyms: gentle, kind, polite, refined | word


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *burly*, from Old English *borlice* (“splendidly, excellently”), from *bor* (“noble, excellent”) + *-lice* (adverbial suffix). Related to Old High German *burlih* (“noble, distinguished”).  The meaning shifted from "noble" to "large and strong" over time.
