Dejima:/ˈdɛʒɪmə/ | n. | 出岛；长崎出岛 | A small artificial island, formerly built in the bay of Nagasaki, Japan, and used as a trading post by the Dutch, the only Europeans allowed in Japan during the Edo period (1603-1867). |  The Dutch traders were confined to Dejima, a small, fan-shaped island in Nagasaki harbor. 荷兰商人被限制在长崎港一个扇形小岛——出岛上。| Synonym: trading post, factory (historical), enclave | Antonym: mainland, open country | noun
