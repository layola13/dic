numbing:/ˈnʌmɪŋ/ | adj. | 麻木的；使失去感觉的 |  Causing a loss of feeling, either physical or emotional. | The dentist gave me a numbing injection before he started the procedure. 牙医在开始手术前给我打了一针麻药，让我感觉麻木。 |  The tragic news had a numbing effect on her.  这悲惨的消息使她麻木了。 | 近义词:  anesthetizing, deadening, benumbing | 反义词: stimulating, invigorating | 词性: 形容词

numbing:/ˈnʌmɪŋ/ | n. | 麻木；麻痹 | The state of being numb. |  The numbing of his fingers made it difficult to play the guitar.  他的手指麻木了，很难弹吉他。| 近义词: numbness, paralysis | 反义词: sensitivity, feeling | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From numb + -ing.  "Numb" comes from Old English *num*, meaning "seized, taken, captured," later developing the sense of "benumbed, torpid."
