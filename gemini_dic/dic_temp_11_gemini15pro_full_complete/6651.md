anaglyph:/ˈænəɡlɪf/| n. |立体照片，浮雕|A stereoscopic picture consisting of two superimposed images of different colors that appear three-dimensional when viewed through eyeglasses having lenses of corresponding colors.|I viewed the anaglyph with red and blue 3D glasses. 我用红蓝3D眼镜观看了立体照片。|Synonyms: stereogram, 3D image|Antonyms: 2D image|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From French *anaglyphe*, from Ancient Greek ἀνάγλυφος (anágluphos, “carved in relief”), from ἀνά (aná, “up”) + γλύφω (glúphō, “carve”).
