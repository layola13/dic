furnishing:/ˈfɜːrnɪʃɪŋ/ (US) /ˈfɜːnɪʃɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |(usually furnishings) the act of putting furniture in a place. | the act of supplying something needed | The furnishings of the new office took several weeks.  新办公室的布置花了几个星期。 | synonyms: equipping, outfitting, supplying, providing | antonyms: stripping, emptying | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *furnischen*, from Old French *furniss-,* stem of *furnir* (“to furnish, provide, supply”), from Frankish *frumjan,* from Proto-Germanic *frumjaną* (“to perform, accomplish, promote, further”), from Proto-Indo-European *per- (“forward”). Cognate with Old High German *frummen* (“to perform, do, benefit”), Old English *fremman* (“to perform, do, make, build”). More at frame.
