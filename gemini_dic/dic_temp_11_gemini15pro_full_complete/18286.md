bell-hopped:/ˈbelˌhɒpt/ /ˈbelˌhɑːpt/| adj.|在酒店当过行李员的；像行李员一样殷勤的|Having worked as a bellhop; resembling or characteristic of a bellhop in behavior, especially in being eager to please. |He had a bell-hopped eagerness to please. 他像个行李员一样殷勤地取悦他人。|近义词：servile, obsequious|反义词：imperious, haughty|词性：adjective

Etymology:  "bell-hop" (noun) is a compound of "bell" and "hop," where "hop" means a short quick jump or movement.  Bellhops were originally called "bellboys," and the term evolved to "bellhop" likely due to their quick movements in responding to guests' needs.  The adjective "bell-hopped" is derived from the noun.
