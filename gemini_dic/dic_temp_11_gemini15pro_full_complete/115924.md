marquee:/mɑːrˈkiː/
n.|大帐篷，华盖；（电影院等的）遮檐；跑马灯|A large tent, often used for outdoor events; a canopy projecting over an entrance, especially of a theater or hotel; a lighted display sign with moving messages.|The wedding reception was held under a large marquee. 婚礼招待会在一个大帐篷下举行。|Synonyms: canopy, awning, tent, pavilion|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps: indoor space, building)|Noun

marquee:/mɑːrˈkiː/
adj.|显著的，重要的；（尤指演员）主演的，领衔的|Prominent, leading, especially of a performer.|The marquee player signed a multi-million dollar contract.  这位主要球员签了一份数百万美元的合同。|Synonyms: leading, star, principal, chief, main, headline, flagship, top-billed|Antonyms: minor, supporting, secondary|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *marquise* ("marquee," literally, "marquis's tent"), feminine of *marquis*, from Old French *marchis*
