overmast:/ˈoʊvərˌmæst/ /ˈəʊvəˌmɑːst/ | adj. | 过高的，过大的，过度的 | Excessively tall or large, surpassing a reasonable or desirable limit. | The ship's overmast sails caught the wind and propelled it forward. 这艘船过高的帆抓住了风，推动它前进。| The company's overmast ambition led to its downfall.  这家公司过度的野心导致了它的衰败。| 近义词: excessive, exorbitant, immoderate | 反义词: moderate, reasonable, appropriate | 词性: 形容词

词源:  "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "mast" (noun referring to a ship's mast). The word's origin lies in nautical terminology, referring to sails or rigging that were too high or large for a ship's mast, leading to instability.
