mopeful: /ˈmoʊpfəl/ | adj. | 闷闷不乐的；沮丧的 | Characterized by or expressing dejection or listlessness. | She sat mopefully in the corner after her team lost the game.  她输了比赛后闷闷不乐地坐在角落里。 |  Synonyms: dejected, gloomy, downcast, glum, despondent | Antonyms: cheerful, happy, joyful, optimistic | word property: adjective

mopefully: /ˈmoʊpfəli/ | adv. | 闷闷不乐地；沮丧地 | In a mopeful manner; dejectedly or listlessly. |  He stared mopefully out the window, lost in thought. 他闷闷不乐地凝视着窗外，陷入沉思。 | Synonyms: dejectedly, gloomily, downcastly | Antonyms: cheerfully, happily, joyfully | word property: adverb

Etymology:
From "mope" (verb meaning "to be gloomy or dejected") + "-ful" (suffix meaning "full of" or "characterized by").  "Mope" is of uncertain origin, possibly related to Dutch "moppen" (to pout).
