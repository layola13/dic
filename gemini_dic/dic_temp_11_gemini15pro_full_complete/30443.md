centre-in:/ˈsɛntər ɪn/ | adj. | 中心进入的，向中心聚集的 |  Moving or directed towards the centre.  Refers to something that focuses or converges towards a central point. | The centre-in design of the new park allows for easy access from all sides.  这个新公园的中心入口设计方便从各个方向进入。

The company adopted a centre-in marketing strategy, focusing all efforts on the core customer base.  这家公司采取了以中心为导向的营销策略，将所有精力集中在核心客户群上。| 近义词:  centralized, converging | 反义词:  decentralized, diverging | 词性: adjective

Etymology:  A compound word formed from "centre" and "in." "Centre" comes from the Old French "centre," ultimately from the Latin "centrum" meaning "center point." "In" is a native English preposition with Germanic roots, indicating direction or location within a space.
