microfracturing: /ˈmaɪkroʊˌfræktʃərɪŋ/ | n. | 微骨折术；微创骨折术 |  A minimally invasive surgical procedure used to treat articular cartilage defects in joints, particularly the knee. It involves creating tiny fractures in the underlying bone to stimulate the growth of new cartilage-like tissue. | The surgeon recommended microfracturing for his damaged knee cartilage.  外科医生建议对他受损的膝关节软骨进行微骨折术。 | Microfracturing is a common treatment for cartilage defects. 微骨折术是治疗软骨缺损的常见方法。 |  synonyms: subchondral drilling, bone marrow stimulation | antonyms: cartilage transplantation, autologous chondrocyte implantation | noun
