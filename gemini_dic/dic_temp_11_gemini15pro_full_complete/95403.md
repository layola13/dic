inchoatively: /ɪnˈkoʊətɪvli/
adverb
开始地，起初地
in a way that is just beginning; incipiently.
The project is inchoatively underway.  这个项目刚刚开始进行。
The feeling of love was inchoatively present. 爱的感觉刚刚萌生。
Synonyms: incipiently, beginning, nascently, initially
Antonyms: completely, fully, maturely
词性: adverb

inchoate: /ɪnˈkoʊət/ 
adjective
刚开始的，未发展的，不完整的
just begun and so not fully formed or developed; rudimentary.
an inchoate mass of ideas  一堆不成熟的想法
Symptoms of the disease are often inchoate and difficult to spot. 这种疾病的症状通常不明显，难以发现。
Synonyms: rudimentary, undeveloped, nascent, incipient
Antonyms: developed, complete, mature
词性: adjective

inchoateness: /ɪnˈkoʊət.nəs/
noun
未完成，不发达的状态
The state of being just begun and not fully formed or developed.
The inchoateness of the plan made it difficult to implement.  计划的不成熟使其难以实施。
There's a certain inchoateness to the script, as if it's still a work in progress.  剧本有一定的不完整性，就好像它仍在创作中。
Synonyms:  incipiency,  underdevelopment,  immaturity
Antonyms: completion, maturity, development
词性: noun


词源:  From Late Latin *inchoativus, from Latin inchoare (“to begin”), from in- (“in, on”) + cohum (“strap, thong”).  The original sense was "to begin work by putting on one's harness."
