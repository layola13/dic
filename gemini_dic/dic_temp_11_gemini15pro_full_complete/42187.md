couloir:/kuːˈlwɑːr/ (US), /ˈkuːlwɑː(r)/ (UK)| n.|狭窄陡峭的峡谷；山沟；（尤指剧院或其他公共建筑的）走廊，通道|A steep, narrow gully on a mountainside, especially one filled with snow or ice. Also, a corridor or passage, especially in a theater or other public building.|The skiers carefully navigated the treacherous couloir.|近义词：gully, ravine, channel, corridor, passage|反义词:plain, open field|词性:noun

Etymology:
From French *couloir*, from *couler* "to flow, run, glide," from Latin *cōlere* "to cultivate, inhabit, till, protect, worship."  Originally referred to a channel or gutter for water.
