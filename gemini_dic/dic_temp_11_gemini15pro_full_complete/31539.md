chartist: /ˈtʃɑːtɪst/ (US), /ˈtʃɑːtɪst/ (UK)| n. |宪章运动者，图表分析师|A person who supported the People's Charter in 19th-century Britain, advocating for political reforms including universal male suffrage.  A person who uses charts and graphs to analyze market trends, especially in the stock market.|例句：The Chartists held large meetings and demonstrations to pressure the government. 宪章运动者举行大型集会和示威来向政府施压。|例句：The chartist predicted a rise in stock prices based on technical analysis.  图表分析师根据技术分析预测股价将上涨。|近义词：reformer, advocate (for the first meaning); analyst, technician (for the second meaning)|反义词：opponent, reactionary (for the first meaning)|词性: noun
