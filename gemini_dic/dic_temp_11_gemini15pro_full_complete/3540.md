against-the-grain:/əˈɡenst ðə ɡreɪn/| adj. adv.|逆着潮流的；违反意愿的；格格不入的|Going against the grain means doing something that is difficult or unpleasant, or that is different from what is usual or expected.  It can also refer to the direction in which wood fibers grow, making it harder to work with in that direction. |Sanding against the grain can damage the wood. 逆着木纹打磨会损坏木材。|unnatural, difficult, awkward, contrary|with the grain, along with the grain|adjective, adverbial phrase
