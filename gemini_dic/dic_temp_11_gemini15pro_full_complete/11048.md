arch-abbey:/ɑːrtʃˈæbi/
n.
总修道院，大修道院
A large and important abbey, often the chief abbey of an order.
The arch-abbey of Beuron is a Benedictine monastery in southern Germany. 博伊龙总修道院是德国南部的一座本笃会修道院。
近义词:monastery, abbey
反义词:None
词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From arch- +‎ abbey.  "Arch-" is a prefix derived from Greek meaning "chief" or "principal". "Abbey" comes from Old French abbeie, ultimately from Late Latin abbatia "abbey, abbacy", from Latin abbas "abbot".
