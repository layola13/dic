nephew: /ˈnefjuː/ (US) /ˈnevjuː/ (UK)| n. |侄子；外甥|A son of one's brother or sister, or of one's brother-in-law or sister-in-law.|My nephew is graduating from college next year. 我的侄子明年大学毕业。|Synonym:  None (precisely).  Relatives like cousin are broader. |Antonym:  None (precisely).  Niece is the female counterpart. |Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *neveu (modern French neveu), from Latin nepōtem (nominative nepōs), meaning "grandson, nephew".  The Latin word is related to Sanskrit napāt ("grandson").
