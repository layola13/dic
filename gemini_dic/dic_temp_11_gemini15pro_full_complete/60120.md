fascinate:/ˈfæsɪneɪt/| v. |使着迷，深深吸引|To attract and hold the attention and interest of someone completely.|Her beauty fascinated him. 她的美貌深深地吸引了他。|近义词：captivate, enthrall, mesmerize, charm, allure|反义词：bore, repel, disgust|词性:动词

fascinating:/ˈfæsɪneɪtɪŋ/| adj. |迷人的，极有吸引力的|Extremely interesting and attractive.|The book offers a fascinating glimpse into the lives of the rich and famous. 这本书引人入胜地展现了富人和名人的生活。|近义词：captivating, enthralling, intriguing, compelling|反义词：boring, dull, tedious, uninteresting|词性:形容词

fascination:/ˌfæsɪˈneɪʃn/| n. |着迷，极大的兴趣|The state of being fascinated; extreme interest.|He has a fascination with ancient history. 他对古代历史有着极大的兴趣。|近义词：captivation, enchantment, allure, obsession|反义词：disinterest, apathy, indifference|词性:名词


词源: From Late Latin fascinatus, past participle of fascinare "to bewitch," from Latin fascinum "spell, witchcraft."
