crowbarred:/ˈkroʊˌbɑːrd/| v. |撬开；强行加入|To force something open or into a place using a crowbar; to force something (an idea, a policy, etc.) into a situation where it doesn't fit naturally. |He crowbarred the door open. 他用撬棍撬开了门。

The new regulations were crowbarred into the existing legislation. 新规章被强行纳入现行法律。|近义词：force, pry, lever, wedge, shoehorn|反义词: N/A|词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From crow + bar. A crowbar is a metal bar with a flattened end, used as a lever.  The "crow-" part likely refers to the bar's resemblance to a crow's beak or foot.
