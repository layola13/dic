alexandrian: /ˌælɪɡˈzændriən/ | adj. n. | 亚历山大的；亚历山大人 | Relating to the city of Alexandria in Egypt, especially its Hellenistic culture.  A native or inhabitant of Alexandria. |  例句：The Alexandrian library was one of the greatest in the ancient world. 亚历山大图书馆是古代世界最伟大的图书馆之一。

例句：He was an Alexandrian scholar. 他是一位亚历山大学者。 | 近义词：Egyptian (in a broader sense), Hellenistic | 反义词: N/A | 词性: adjective, noun

Etymology: From Latin *Alexandrīnus*, from Greek *Ἀλεξανδρεὺς* (Alexandréus), from *Ἀλεξάνδρεια* (Alexándreia, “Alexandria”). Ultimately from the name Alexander.
