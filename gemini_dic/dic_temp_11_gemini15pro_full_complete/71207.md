fit-to-be-tied:/fɪt tə bi taɪd/ | adj. | 暴怒的，非常生气的 | Extremely angry; furious. |  例句：She was fit to be tied when she found out her car had been towed. 她发现她的车被拖走了，气得暴跳如雷。

例句：He was fit to be tied when his flight was delayed for six hours.  他的航班延误了六个小时，他气得要命. | 近义词：furious, enraged, livid, indignant | 反义词：calm, peaceful, tranquil | 词性: adjective


词源:  This idiom likely originates from the practice of tying up animals, particularly horses or oxen, when they became agitated or difficult to control.  The image evokes a state of restrained fury.
