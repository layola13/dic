diamond-in-the-rough:/ˈdaɪəmənd ɪn ðə rʌf/
noun
璞玉；未经雕琢的钻石；潜力股
Someone or something with potential but lacking refinement or polish.  A person who is basically good hearted but lacking social graces and polish.  Something valuable that is not yet finished or perfect, but has great potential.
例句：He's a diamond in the rough; he just needs some training. 他是个璞玉，只需要一些训练。

例句：This old car is a diamond in the rough - a little work and it'll be a classic.  这辆旧车是块璞玉 -  稍加修理就会成为经典之作。
近义词：unpolished gem, undiscovered talent, potential star, sleeping giant
反义词：polished gem, finished product, failure, mediocrity
词性：n.
