junketer: /ˈdʒʌŋkɪtər/ | n. |  (公费)游玩者，(尤指)考察旅行者；寻欢作乐者 |  A person who goes on a junket, especially a politician or journalist. | The junketers enjoyed a lavish banquet at the taxpayers' expense. 这些(公费)游玩者用纳税人的钱享受了一场奢华的宴会。| 近义词：tripper, excursionist, traveler, reveler | 反义词: worker, homebody | 词性: 名词

junket: /ˈdʒʌŋkɪt/ | n. | (尤指官员的)外出考察，(公费)旅游；野餐；(用牛奶、甜酒等制成的)凝乳食品 |  A trip, often by a government official, that appears to be for pleasure or entertainment but is really for business or political purposes; a picnic or feast; a sweet, custard-like food made with milk, rennet, and sugar. | The senator went on a junket to Hawaii. 参议员到夏威夷进行了公费旅行。 | 近义词: trip, excursion, outing, feast | 反义词: work, business trip (if the junket is disguised as one) | 词性: 名词

junket: /ˈdʒʌŋkɪt/ | vi. |  去(公费)旅游，寻欢作乐 | To go on a junket. |  They were junketing around Europe at the company's expense. 他们以公司的名义在欧洲四处游玩。 | 近义词: travel, tour, revel | 反义词: work | 词性: 动词

词源:
From Middle English *joncate, from Old French joncade "rush basket," from jonc "rush," from Latin juncus. Originally referred to a basket made of rushes, then to a cream cheese dish served in such a basket, and eventually to a feast or picnic. The modern sense of a pleasure trip, often at public expense, developed in the 19th century.
