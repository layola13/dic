clenches:/ˈklɛnʃɪz/ /ˈklɛntʃɪz/ | v. n.| 握紧，咬紧，攥紧 |  Third-person singular present tense form of the verb "clench." It describes the action of closing or tightening something, often a hand or fist, or the jaw. As a noun, it refers to the act of clenching or the state of being clenched. | He clenches his fists in anger. 他愤怒地攥紧拳头。

The boxer clenches his jaw as he prepares for the next round.  拳击手准备下一回合时咬紧牙关。

clenches: /ˈklɛnʃɪz/ /ˈklɛntʃɪz/|n.| 握紧，咬紧 |The clenches of his fists showed his frustration. 他紧握的拳头表明了他的沮丧。

His jaw clenches were visible as he endured the pain. 他忍受痛苦时，咬紧牙关的样子清晰可见。|近义词：grips, grasps, tightens, clinches |反义词：releases, loosens, relaxes |词性：动词(第三人称单数现在式), 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clenchen, from Old English *clenċan, frequentative of clincan (“to shrink, clink”).  Related to cling.
