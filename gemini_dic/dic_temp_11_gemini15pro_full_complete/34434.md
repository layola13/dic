claustrophobically:/ˌklɔːstrəˈfəʊbɪkli/ | adv. | 幽闭恐惧地 | In a way that relates to or suggests claustrophobia.  Experiencing or inducing a fear of enclosed spaces. |  She felt claustrophobically trapped in the elevator. 她感觉被困在电梯里，幽闭恐惧症发作了。| The small, windowless room was claustrophobically decorated.  这个小而无窗的房间的装饰让人感到幽闭恐惧。 | nearly, tightly, restrictively | spaciously, openly, airily | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From claustrophobia + -ically.  Claustrophobia comes from Latin claustrum (a bolt, bar, barrier, enclosed place) +  -phobia (from Greek phobos, meaning "fear").
