amphitheatres: /ˈæmfɪˌθiːətərz/ (US), /ˈæmfɪˌθɪətəz/ (UK)
n.
圆形剧场，露天剧场
An oval or circular building with tiers of seats rising from a central open space or arena, used for public performances, especially in ancient Rome.
The gladiators fought in the amphitheatre. 角斗士在圆形剧场搏斗。
近义词: arena, stadium, coliseum
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "confined theater" or "indoor venue")
词性: Noun


amphitheatre: /ˈæmfɪˌθiːətər/ (US), /ˈæmfɪˌθɪətər/ (UK)
n.
圆形剧场，露天剧场
An oval or circular building with tiers of seats rising from a central open space or arena, used for public performances, especially in ancient Rome.
The concert was held in a large amphitheatre.  这场音乐会在一个大型露天剧场举行。
近义词: arena, stadium, coliseum
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "confined theater" or "indoor venue")
词性: Noun


词源: From Latin amphitheatrum, from Greek amphitheatron, from amphi- (around) + theatron (a place for viewing).
