Etymology:
From appeasable + -ness.  Appeasable comes from appease (v.) + -able. Appease comes from Old French apaisier "pacify, calm, soothe," from a- "to" (see ad-) + pais "peace," from Latin pacem (nominative pax) "peace, compact, treaty," related to pangere "to fix, fasten" (see pact).
