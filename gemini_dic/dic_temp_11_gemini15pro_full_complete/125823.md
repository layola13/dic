murmurs:/ˈmɜːrmərz/ (US), /ˈmɜːməz/ (UK)| n. |低语声，咕哝声，小声抱怨|a low, continuous, indistinct sound; a complaint uttered in a low and indistinct tone.|例句：The murmurs of the crowd grew louder as the politician approached. 人群的低语声随着政客的靠近而变大。

例句：There were murmurs of discontent among the employees. 员工中有一些不满的抱怨声。|近义词：whispers, mutterings, grumbles, complaints|反义词：shouts, yells, roars|词性:名词(plural)

murmur:/ˈmɜːrmər/ (US), /ˈmɜːmə(r)/ (UK)| v. |低声说，咕哝，小声抱怨|to say something in a low, soft, or indistinct voice; to complain in a low voice.|例句：He murmured an apology and left the room. 他低声说了句道歉，然后离开了房间。

例句：She murmured something about the unfairness of it all. 她低声抱怨着这一切的不公平。|近义词：whisper, mutter, grumble, complain|反义词：shout, yell, roar|词性:动词


词源: From Old French *murmurer, from Latin murmurare "to murmur, roar, purl," of imitative origin.
