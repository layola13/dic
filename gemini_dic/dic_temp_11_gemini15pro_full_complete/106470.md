kowtow:/ˌkaʊˈtaʊ/ |/ˌkaʊˈtoʊ/| vi. n.|叩头，磕头；卑躬屈膝；唯命是从|To act in an excessively subservient manner.  To kneel and touch the ground with the forehead in worship or submission.|He kowtowed to the emperor. 他向皇帝叩头。|synonyms: grovel, fawn, truckle,  bootlick,  apple-polish|antonyms: defy, resist, stand up to|verb

kowtow:/ˌkaʊˈtaʊ/ |/ˌkaʊˈtoʊ/| n.|叩头，磕头|An act of kowtowing. A deep bow expressing respect or submission.|The kowtow was a traditional gesture of respect in China. 叩头是中国传统的表示尊敬的礼节。|synonyms: obeisance,  prostration|antonyms: defiance, resistance|noun
