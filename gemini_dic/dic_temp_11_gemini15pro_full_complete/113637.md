machination:/ˌmækɪˈneɪʃən/
|n.|阴谋，诡计|a plot or scheme, especially one that is elaborate and secret and intended to achieve an evil or illegal end.|The president was the victim of a machination by his own advisors. 总统成了自己顾问们阴谋的受害者。|
|近义词:plot, scheme, conspiracy, intrigue|
|反义词:fair play, honesty, openness|
|词性:noun|


machinations:/ˌmækɪˈneɪʃənz/
|n.|阴谋，诡计（复数）|plots or schemes, especially ones that are elaborate and secret and intended to achieve an evil or illegal end.|His machinations to take over the company were ultimately unsuccessful. 他接管公司的阴谋最终失败了。|
|近义词:plots, schemes, conspiracies, intrigues|
|反义词:fair play, honesty, openness|
|词性:noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *machinatio*, from Latin machinari "to devise, contrive," from machina "machine, engine, device, contrivance."
