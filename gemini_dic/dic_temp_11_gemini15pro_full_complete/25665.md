bungalow:/ˈbʌŋɡəloʊ/ (US) /ˈbʌŋɡələʊ/ (UK)| n. |平房，小屋|A small house, usually with a single story and sometimes with an attic or upper rooms built into a sloping roof.|We stayed in a charming bungalow near the beach. 我们住在海滩附近一个迷人的小屋里。|synonyms: cottage, cabin, lodge, villa|antonyms: mansion, skyscraper, high-rise|noun

bungalows:/ˈbʌŋɡəloʊz/ (US) /ˈbʌŋɡələʊz/ (UK)| n. |平房，小屋（复数）|Plural form of bungalow, referring to more than one small, single-story house.|The development consists of several modern bungalows.  这个开发项目由几座现代平房组成。|synonyms: cottages, cabins, lodges, villas|antonyms: mansions, skyscrapers, high-rises|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Hindi बंगला (baṅglā), meaning "belonging to Bengal," used elliptically to refer to houses built in the Bengal style.  These houses were typically low-rise and thatched. The word entered English in the 17th century.
