catcall: /ˈkætˌkɔːl/ (US) /ˈkætˌkɔːl/ (UK)| n.|嘘声，(尤指男性对女性的)口哨声，喊叫|A shout of a rude and sexually suggestive remark at someone, especially a woman, who is passing by in a public place.|Example: She ignored the catcalls from the construction site. 她无视了建筑工地传来的口哨声。

Example:  The catcalls made her feel uncomfortable and unsafe.  这些口哨声让她感到不舒服和不安全。|近义词：jeer, taunt|反义词: compliment, praise|词性:名词

词源:  "cat" + "call,"  "cat" likely referring to a cat's screech, and "call" to a vocalization.  This combination suggests a harsh, unpleasant cry, which evolved to specifically refer to the type of harassment described above.
