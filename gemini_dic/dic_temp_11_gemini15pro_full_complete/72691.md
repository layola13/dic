focalised:/ˈfəʊkəlaɪzd/ | adj. |聚焦的，集中的|Directed towards a narrow or specific area or purpose; concentrated.  It describes something that has been made more focused or specific. |The company's marketing campaign was highly focalised on a specific demographic. 该公司的营销活动高度集中于特定的人群。|The artist's focalised gaze captured every detail of the scene. 艺术家专注的目光捕捉到了场景的每一个细节。|近义词: concentrated, focused, targeted, specialized |反义词: diffused, scattered, unfocused, generalized |词性:形容词


focalize:/ˈfəʊkəlaɪz/ | v. |聚焦，集中|To direct attention or resources towards a narrow or specific area or purpose; to concentrate. |The company decided to focalize its resources on developing new products. 公司决定将资源集中在新产品开发上。|The photographer focalized the camera lens on the flower. 摄影师将相机镜头对准了花朵。|近义词: focus, concentrate, direct, target |反义词: diffuse, scatter, broaden |词性:动词

词源: From French *focaliser*, from *focal* (focal), from Latin *focus* (hearth, fireplace, point of convergence). The "-ize" suffix is a common way to form verbs from adjectives or nouns, and it often indicates the process of making something possess the quality described by the root word.  In this case, "focalize" means to make something focal, or focused.
