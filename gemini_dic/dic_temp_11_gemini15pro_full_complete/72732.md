foes:/foʊz/
|n.|敌人，仇敌|plural of foe;  a person who feels enmity, hatred, or malice toward another|His foes plotted his downfall. 他的敌人们密谋要他垮台。|
|近义词：enemies, adversaries, opponents, rivals|
|反义词：friends, allies, supporters|
|词性：名词（复数）|


Etymology:
From Middle English *foos, fos, foes, plural of foo, fo, from Old English fā (“foe, enemy, adversary, opponent in a contest, stranger, devil, fiend”), from Proto-Germanic *fēhugaz (“enemy”), from Proto-Indo-European *peh₂i- (“to blame, condemn, despise”). Cognate with West Frisian fijân (“enemy”), Dutch vijand (“enemy”), German Feind (“enemy”), Danish fjende (“enemy”), Swedish fiende (“enemy”), Icelandic fjándi (“enemy”), Latin foedus (“foul, loathsome, ugly, disgusting; shameful, disgraceful, base”). Related also to Latin faex (“dregs, lees, sediment”). Doublet of fiend.
