exhilarated:/ɪɡˈzɪləreɪtɪd/| adj.|兴高采烈的；兴奋的；激动的|Feeling very happy, animated, and lively. |例句：I felt exhilarated after the roller coaster ride. 坐过山车后我感到很兴奋。|例句：She was exhilarated by the news of her promotion.  她晋升的消息让她欣喜若狂。|近义词：thrilled, elated, excited, invigorated|反义词：depressed, dejected, downcast|词性:形容词
exhilarate:/ɪɡˈzɪləreɪt/| vt.|使高兴；使兴奋；使激动|To make someone feel very happy, animated, and lively. |例句：The fresh air and sunshine exhilarated us. 清新空气和阳光让我们心旷神怡。|例句：The victory exhilarated the entire team. 胜利让整个团队兴奋不已。|近义词：thrill, elate, excite, invigorate|反义词：depress, deject, discourage|词性:动词
exhilaration:/ɪɡˌzɪləˈreɪʃn/| n.|高兴；兴奋；激动|A feeling of great happiness, excitement, or animation.|例句：The exhilaration of winning the championship was overwhelming. 赢得冠军的喜悦之情难以言表。|例句：She felt a sense of exhilaration as she reached the mountain summit. 当她到达山顶时，她感到一阵兴奋。|近义词：thrill, elation, excitement, invigoration|反义词：depression, dejection, despondency|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin exhilaratus, past participle of exhilarare (“to gladden, cheer”), from Latin ex- (“out, from”) + hilarare (“to make cheerful”), from hilaris (“cheerful”).
