irreconcilables:/ɪˌrɛkənˈsaɪləblz/
|n.|不可调和的人（或事物）|People or things that are irreconcilable are so strongly opposed to each other that it is not possible for them to reach an agreement.|The two factions within the party remained irreconcilables, constantly battling for control.  党内两派仍然水火不容，不断争夺控制权。|synonyms: antagonists, adversaries, opponents|antonyms: allies, supporters, compatibles|noun


irreconcilable:/ɪˌrɛkənˈsaɪləbl/
|adj.|不可调和的；无法协调的|Not able to be brought into agreement or harmony; incompatible.|His views on religion were irreconcilable with his scientific beliefs. 他对宗教的看法与他的科学信仰格格不入。|synonyms: incompatible, conflicting, clashing|antonyms: compatible, harmonious, consistent|adjective


Etymology:
From Late Latin *irreconciliabilis, from Latin in- (“not”) + reconciliabilis (“reconcilable”), from reconciliare (“to reconcile”), from re- (“again”) + conciliare (“to bring together, unite”).
