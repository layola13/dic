chaperone:/ˈʃæpəroʊn/ (US) /ˈʃæpərəʊn/ (UK)| v.|陪伴；监护|to accompany and supervise someone, especially a young unmarried woman, on social occasions.|例句：He chaperoned his daughter to the prom. 他陪同女儿参加了毕业舞会。|例句：Several teachers chaperoned the students on the field trip. 几位老师陪同学生进行了实地考察。|近义词：escort, accompany, supervise|反义词: N/A|词性: 动词


USAGE->

词源: From French chaperon, originally meaning "hood," later referring to a married woman's hood, and then to the woman herself.  The sense of accompanying and supervising developed because older women traditionally accompanied younger unmarried women in social settings.
