baby boomer:/ˈbeɪbi ˈbuːmər/| n. |婴儿潮一代|A person born during the post–World War II baby boom, roughly between the years 1946 and 1964.  This period was marked by a significant increase in birth rates.|The politician focused his campaign on the concerns of baby boomers.|Synonyms: boomer|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but one might consider Gen X, Gen Y, or Millennial as contrasting generations)|Noun
