counter-coloured:/ˈkaʊntərˈkʌlərd/| adj.|反色的，保护色的|Having a coloration designed to camouflage by blending with the background.  The animal's fur was counter-coloured to match the surrounding rocks and vegetation.|The arctic fox's winter coat is counter-coloured, making it nearly invisible against the snow.|近义词:camouflaged, cryptic|反义词:conspicuous, bright|词性:形容词

Etymology:
"counter-" (prefix): from Old French "contre-" and Latin "contra" meaning "against, opposite"
"coloured": past participle of the verb "colour," from Old French "colorer" and Latin "colorare" meaning "to color"
