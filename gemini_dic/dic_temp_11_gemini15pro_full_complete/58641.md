dumbfoundering:/dʌmˈfaʊndərɪŋ/
| adj. | 令人惊愕的，使人目瞪口呆的 | Extremely surprising or shocking; astonishing. | The news of his sudden death was absolutely dumbfoundering.  他突然去世的消息绝对令人震惊。|  astonishing, astounding, bewildering, shocking | unsurprising, expected | adjective

dumbfounded:/dʌmˈfaʊndɪd/
| adj. | 惊呆的，目瞪口呆的 | So surprised and shocked that one is unable to speak. | I was dumbfounded by her audacity. 我被她的大胆吓呆了。| astonished, speechless, stunned, amazed | unexcited, unfazed | adjective

词源:

From the verb "dumbfound," which originated in the late 16th century.  "Dumb" in this context means "unable to speak" and "found" (from "confound") means "to confuse or perplex."  Thus, the original meaning was to be so amazed that one is unable to speak.
