ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cirice, circe, from Proto-West Germanic *kirkja, from Proto-Germanic *kirika, borrowed from Late Latin *chērica, *cīrica, from Ancient Greek κυριακόν (kuriakon, “the Lord's [house]”), neuter of κυριακός (kuriakos, “of the Lord”), from κύριος (kurios, “lord, master”). Cognate with West Frisian tsjerke, Dutch kerk, German Kirche, Scots kirk, Swedish kyrka.
