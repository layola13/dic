legitimist:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmɪst/| n. adj.|正统主义者，拥护正统的|A person who supports a legitimate ruler, especially a monarch, or a system of government based on the hereditary right of rulers.  A legitimist believes in the principle of legitimacy. |例句：The legitimist faction fought to restore the monarchy. 正统派奋力恢复君主制。

例句：He held legitimist views and opposed the revolution. 他持有正统主义观点，反对革命。|近义词：royalist, monarchist, traditionalist|反义词：rebel, revolutionary, usurper|词性: noun, adjective

legitimate:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmət/| adj.|合法的，正当的，正统的|Conforming to the law or to rules.  Able to be defended with logic or justification; valid.|例句：The government is the legitimate representative of the people. 政府是人民的合法代表。

例句：Is his claim legitimate? 他的主张合理吗？|近义词：lawful, legal, valid, justifiable|反义词：illegal, illegitimate, invalid, unjustified|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *légitimiste*, from *légitime* (“legitimate”), from Latin *legitimus* (“lawful”), from *lex* (“law”).
