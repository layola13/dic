bribe: /braɪb/ | n. vt. |贿赂;贿赂物|Something, such as money or a favor, offered or given to someone in a position of trust to persuade them to act dishonestly.|The official was accused of accepting bribes.  这位官员被指控接受贿赂。|He bribed the guard to let him in. 他贿赂警卫让他进去。|近义词: payoff, kickback, inducement|反义词: reward (for meritorious service) |词性: noun, verb

bribes: /braɪbz/ | n. |贿赂(bribe的复数)|Plural form of bribe, referring to multiple instances of offering or giving something to persuade someone to act dishonestly.|The company paid bribes to win the contract. 这家公司为了赢得合同而行贿。|Several officials were indicted on charges of accepting bribes. 几名官员因被控受贿而遭到起诉。|近义词: payoffs, kickbacks, inducements|反义词: rewards (for meritorious service)|词性: noun

词源: From Old French *briber "to beg, ask for," from a Germanic source, from Proto-Germanic *breban- "to break, to break pieces of bread."  The sense shifted from "begging" to "offering a gift to influence someone" and finally to "offering money or goods to corrupt someone."
