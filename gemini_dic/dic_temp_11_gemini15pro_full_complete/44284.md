cronyism:/ˈkroʊnizəm/| n.|裙带关系；任人唯亲；徇私|The practice of favoring one's friends, especially in political appointments, regardless of their qualifications.|His administration was riddled with cronyism and corruption. 他的政府充斥着裙带关系和腐败。|近义词：favoritism, nepotism, patronage|反义词：meritocracy, impartiality|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From crony + -ism.  Crony (meaning a close friend or companion) dates back to the 17th century, possibly originating from university slang at Cambridge.  The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, practice, or characteristic.
