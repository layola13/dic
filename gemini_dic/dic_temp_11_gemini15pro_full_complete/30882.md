chafferer:/ˈtʃæfərər/| n. |讨价还价者；讲价的人|Someone who barters or haggles, especially persistently.|The chafferer spent hours at the market trying to get the lowest price for the rugs.|Synonyms: bargainer, haggler, trader|Antonyms: buyer, customer|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *chaffaren*, from Old North French *chaf(f)rer* (compare Old French *chafarer*, *chalopper* (“to warm oneself; to chat, gossip, chatter”)), of uncertain origin.
