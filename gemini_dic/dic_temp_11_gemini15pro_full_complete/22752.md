borracho:/boˈratʃo/| adj.|醉的; 酗酒的|Drunk; intoxicated by alcohol.  Derived from the Spanish word "borracho," ultimately from the Vulgar Latin *burrāceus "made of flocks," referring to the woolliness of mind associated with drunkenness.|He stumbled into the bar, completely borracho. 他跌跌撞撞地走进酒吧，烂醉如泥。|Synonyms: drunk, intoxicated, inebriated, tipsy, plastered|Antonyms: sober, temperate|adjective

borracho:/boˈratʃo/| n.|醉汉; 酒鬼|A drunkard; a habitual drinker.|The borracho stumbled down the street. 那个醉汉在街上踉踉跄跄地走着。|Synonyms: drunkard, alcoholic, sot, inebriate|Antonyms: teetotaler|noun
