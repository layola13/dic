commensurability:/kəˌmenʃərəˈbɪləti/ |n.|可公度性，相称性|The state of being commensurable; having a common measure.  Two quantities are commensurable if their ratio is a rational number.|The commensurability of the two projects allowed for efficient resource allocation. 这两个项目的可公度性使得资源分配更加高效。|Synonyms:  proportionality, comparability, compatibility|Antonyms:  incommensurability, disparity, disproportion|noun


commensurate:/kəˈmenʃərət/ |adj.|相称的，相当的|Corresponding in size or degree; proportionate.|Her salary is commensurate with her experience. 她的薪水与她的经验相称。|Synonyms:  proportionate, corresponding, equivalent, appropriate|Antonyms:  disproportionate, inadequate, insufficient|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *commensurabilitas, from Latin commensurabilis  "measurable together," from com- "together" + mensurabilis "measurable," from mensurare "to measure."
