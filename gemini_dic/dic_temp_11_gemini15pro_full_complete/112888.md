luddite:/ˈlʌdaɪt/| n.| 卢德派；反对技术进步的人|A member of any of the bands of English workers who destroyed machinery, especially in cotton and woolen mills, that they believed was threatening their jobs (1811–16).|例句：He was labelled a Luddite for opposing the introduction of computers in the workplace.  他因为反对在工作场所引入电脑而被称为卢德派。

|例句：Some people see the rejection of new technologies as a Luddite attitude. 一些人将拒绝新技术视为卢德主义的态度。|近义词：reactionary, conservative, opponent of progress|反义词:progressive, innovator, modernist|词性:noun
