fingol:/ˈfɪŋɡɒl/ | n. | 肿块；淤青；（尤指眼部）被打青的地方 | A bruise, especially a black eye. | He had a nasty fingol after the fight. 他打架后眼眶乌青了一大块。 | Synonyms: black eye, shiner, bruise, contusion | Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, could consider "unblemished skin," "clear complexion") | Noun

Etymology: From Irish and Scottish Gaelic  *fingal*, meaning "white stranger" or "foreigner," later associated with the hero Fingal in James Macpherson's Ossianic poems.  The connection to bruising likely arises from the idea of a foreigner being beaten up.
