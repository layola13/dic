mortise: /ˈmɔːrtɪs/ (US), /ˈmɔːtɪs/ (UK)| n. v. |榫眼；榫孔；嵌入；开榫眼|A hole or recess cut into a piece of timber, etc., to receive a tenon or corresponding projection.|  The carpenter carefully cut a mortise in the door frame. 木匠小心地在门框上开了一个榫眼。|  He mortised the two pieces of wood together. 他将两块木头榫接在一起。|近义词: slot, groove, recess|反义词: tenon|词性: 名词, 动词

mortised: /ˈmɔːrtɪst/ (US), /ˈmɔːtɪst/ (UK)| adj. |榫接的；有榫眼的|Joined by a mortise and tenon.| The table legs were mortised into the tabletop. 桌腿是用榫眼和榫头连接到桌面上的。 | 近义词: jointed, connected | 反义词: unjoined, separated | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mortaise (modern mortaise), perhaps from Arabic murtazz ‘fixed, implanted’.
