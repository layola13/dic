carmelite:/ˈkɑːməlaɪt/ | n. adj.| 加尔默罗会的修士（或修女）；加尔默罗会的 |A member of the Roman Catholic Order of Carmelites, or relating to this order. |例句：She entered a Carmelite convent at a young age. 她年轻时就进入了一家加尔默罗会的修道院。

例句：The Carmelite monastery is known for its peaceful atmosphere. 这座加尔默罗会修道院以其宁静的氛围而闻名。|近义词：monk, nun, friar (for male Carmelites) |反义词: None (lacks a direct opposite) |词性: Noun, Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *carmelite*, from Medieval Latin *Carmelita*, from Mount Carmel (*Carmelus* in Latin), the location of the order's first monastery.
