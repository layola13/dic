mesmerism:/ˈmɛzmərɪzəm/ | n. |催眠术；迷惑；魅力|Hypnotic induction of a state of altered consciousness in which the subject is more susceptible to suggestion.  The compelling attractiveness of something. |例句：The audience was held in a state of mesmerism by the speaker's powerful words. 演讲者充满力量的话语使听众们听得如痴如醉。

例句：The mesmerism of the fire drew her closer. 火的魅力吸引着她靠近。|近义词：hypnosis, fascination, charm, allure |反义词：repulsion, aversion |词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Franz Anton Mesmer (1734-1815), a German physician who developed a theory of animal magnetism, which he used to treat illnesses. The word "mesmerism" comes from his name.
