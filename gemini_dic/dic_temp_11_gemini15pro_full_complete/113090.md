lunation:/ˈluːneɪʃən/| n.|朔望月，阴历月|The time between one new moon and the next, approximately 29.5 days.  The cycle of phases of the moon.|例句：The rocket launch was timed to coincide with a specific lunation. 火箭发射的时间被安排与特定的朔望月一致。

例句：Ancient cultures often based their calendars on lunations. 古代文化通常根据朔望月制定日历。|近义词：synodic month, lunar month|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *lunationem, accusative singular of lunatio (“performing the course of a month”), from Latin luna (“moon”) + -ationem, accusative singular of -atio, suffix forming nouns of action from verbs ending in -are.|
