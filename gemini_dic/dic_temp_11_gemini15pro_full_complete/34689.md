clerkliness:/ˈklɜːrklinɪs/
n.|办事员气质；呆板；公式化|The excessive clerkliness of the report made it tedious to read.  这份报告过分的办事员气质使它读起来很乏味。|例句：His clerkliness made him ill-suited for a leadership role. 他的呆板使他不适合担任领导角色。|近义词：bureaucracy, formality, rigidity|反义词：flexibility, dynamism, creativity|词性:名词

Etymology:
clerk (n.) + -liness (suffix)

*   clerk (n.): From Old French *clerc, from Latin clericus "clergyman," from Greek klerikos "of the clergy." Originally referred to a member of the clergy, later broadened to mean a scholar or a person who keeps records or accounts.
*   -liness (suffix):  Forms nouns meaning "state or quality of being," from Old English -licnes.
