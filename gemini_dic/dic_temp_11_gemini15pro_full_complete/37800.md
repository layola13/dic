compellingly:/kəmˈpɛlɪŋli/
adverb
令人信服地，引人注目地
in a way that makes you feel you must do something or believe something
The evidence was presented compellingly.  证据被令人信服地提出来了。
近义词:persuasively, convincingly, forcefully
反义词:weakly, unconvincingly
词性:adverb

compel:/kəmˈpɛl/
verb
强迫，迫使
to force someone to do something
The law compels employers to provide health insurance.  法律强制雇主提供健康保险。
近义词:force, oblige, require
反义词:persuade, encourage
词性:verb

compelling:/kəmˈpɛlɪŋ/
adjective
引人入胜的，令人信服的
If a reason, argument, etc. is compelling, it makes you believe it or accept it because it is so strong.
The prosecutor presented a compelling case.  检察官提出了一个令人信服的案件。
近义词:persuasive, convincing, forceful
反义词:weakly, unconvincingly
词性:adjective
