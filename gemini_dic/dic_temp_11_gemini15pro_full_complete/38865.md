confederationism:/kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪzəm/
n.
邦联主义；联邦主义
A system of government in which sovereign states delegate certain powers to a central government for specific purposes, while retaining ultimate authority.
The Southern states’ belief in confederationism led to the American Civil War.  南方各州对邦联主义的信仰导致了美国内战。
近义词:federalism, unionism
反义词:centralism, unitary system
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From confederation + -ism.  Confederation from Late Latin confoederatio, from confoederare "to unite by league," from com- "together" + foederare "to make a treaty," from foedus "treaty, league."
