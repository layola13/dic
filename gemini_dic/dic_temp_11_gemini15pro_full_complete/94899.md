in for it:/ɪn fɔːr ɪt/ | adj. phrasal verb |  惹上麻烦；注定倒霉；将受惩罚 | Being in a difficult or unpleasant situation that one cannot avoid. |  "If you lie to her, you're in for it." 如果你对她撒谎，你就会有麻烦了。 |  in trouble, facing consequences, due for a reprimand |  safe, out of the woods | idiom
Etymology: The phrase likely originates from the early 19th century, possibly from nautical slang.  While the exact origin is uncertain, the "in" likely implies being "in" a situation and "for it" suggesting a negative outcome or punishment "for" some action.
