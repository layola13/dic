jacaranda:/ˌdʒækəˈrændə/ | n. | 蓝花楹 | A tropical American tree with blue-violet flowers. |  The jacarandas are in full bloom, painting the streets purple. 蓝花楹盛开，把街道染成了紫色。

jacaranda:/ˌdʒækəˈrændə/ | n. | 蓝花楹 |  The wood of any of these trees, used in cabinetmaking. | The craftsman used jacaranda wood to create a beautiful table.  工匠用蓝花楹木做了一张漂亮的桌子。

jacaranda:/ˌdʒækəˈrændə/ | n. | 蓝花楹 | (South African informal) A five-rand banknote. | He paid for the coffee with a jacaranda. 他用一张五兰特纸币付了咖啡钱。


Etymology->
From Portuguese *jacarandá*, probably from a Tupi-Guaraní language of Brazil.

Synonyms->
(for the tree): blue jacaranda, black poui
(for the wood): rosewood (loosely)

Antonyms->
None (as it refers to a specific type of tree and wood)

Word Type->
Noun
