mazer:/ˈmeɪzər/| n.|  大酒杯; 木碗 | A large drinking bowl, typically made of wood or metal, without a handle or foot, especially one used in medieval times. | He drank deeply from the ornate mazer. 他从华丽的大酒杯里痛饮。| synonyms: bowl, goblet, tankard | antonyms: cup (due to handle, often smaller) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *maser*, *mazer*, from Old French *mazre*, *masere*, from *maser* "maple tree", from Late Latin *macer*, from Gaulish *masaros*. Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *medʰu-*. Related to maple.
