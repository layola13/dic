burlesque:/bərˈlɛsk/| n. adj.|滑稽模仿；讽刺作品；滑稽的；讽刺的|A burlesque is a literary, dramatic or musical work intended to cause laughter by caricaturing the manner or spirit of serious works, or by ludicrous treatment of their subjects.  It is a form of satire.|The play was a burlesque of Victorian melodrama. 这部戏是对维多利亚时代情节剧的滑稽模仿。|Synonyms: parody, satire, caricature, lampoon, travesty|Antonyms: serious, respectful, reverent|noun, adjective

burlesques:/bərˈlɛsks/| n.|滑稽模仿；讽刺作品|The plural form of "burlesque," referring to multiple instances of this type of performance or work.|The theater put on a series of burlesques of popular movies.  这家剧院上演了一系列模仿流行电影的滑稽剧。|Synonyms: parodies, satires, caricatures, lampoons, travesties|Antonyms: serious works, respectful pieces, reverent productions|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *burlesco*, from *burla* "joke, mockery," probably ultimately of Germanic origin.
