dracin:/ˈdreɪsɪn/| n.| 龙涎香; 龙涎香酊|A fragrant, waxy substance secreted by the sperm whale, used in perfumery.|The perfumer used a small amount of dracin to give the fragrance a unique depth.  调香师使用少量龙涎香为香水增添独特的深度。|Synonyms: ambergris|Antonyms: None|Noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *dracin, alteration (influenced by Latin draco (“dragon”)) of ambre gris (“gray amber”), from Old French ambre gris, from Arabic عنبر‎ (ʿanbar, “ambergris”) + gris (“gray”).
