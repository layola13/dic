bore:/bɔːr/(UK) /bɔːr/(US)| n. v.| 令人厌烦的人(或事物); 孔; 钻孔; 内径|A bore is someone or something that is tedious and uninteresting. To bore means to make someone feel tired and impatient by being uninteresting.|
The lecture was a complete bore.  这讲座真是无聊透顶。
He bored us all with his endless stories. 他无休止地讲故事，让我们都厌烦了。
|近义词：dullard, nuisance, pest|反义词：delight, thrill|词性:名词，动词


bore:/bɔːr/(UK) /bɔːr/(US)| v.| 钻(孔); 挖(洞); 盯着看|To bore means to make a hole in something using a tool. It can also mean to stare at someone intently.|
They bored a tunnel through the mountain.  他们在山中凿了一条隧道。
He bored into me with his intense gaze. 他目不转睛地盯着我。
|近义词：drill, pierce, stare|反义词：fill, plug|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *borian, from Proto-Germanic *borōną (“to bore, pierce”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰer- (“to cut, split, pierce”).
