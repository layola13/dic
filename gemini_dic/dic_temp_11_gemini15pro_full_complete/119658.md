middler:/ˈmɪdlər/| n.|中间的人，中等生|A person who is in the middle of a group, scale, or series, especially a student in the middle grades of school (typically grades 6-8 in the US).|He was a middler in his class, neither at the top nor the bottom. 他在班里中等，既不是顶尖的也不是垫底的。|Synonyms: average student, middle-schooler|Antonyms: high achiever, low achiever|noun


middler:/ˈmɪdlər/| n.|夹心层|Someone who is caught in the middle of a difficult situation or conflict.|He felt like a middler between his parents who were constantly arguing.  他感觉自己像夹在他不断争吵的父母中间的夹心饼干。|Synonyms: intermediary, go-between|Antonyms: protagonist, antagonist|noun
