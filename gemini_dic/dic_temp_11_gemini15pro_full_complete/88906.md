hold court:/hoʊld kɔːrt/
verb phrase
主持；吸引注意；像法官一样发表意见；滔滔不绝地说话
To speak at length to a group of people who listen attentively.  To act as the center of attention, often by telling stories or expressing opinions.
The professor held court at the coffee shop, surrounded by students eager to hear his anecdotes. 教授在咖啡店里滔滔不绝地讲着，周围围满了渴望听他讲故事的学生。
The celebrity held court at the party, charming everyone with her stories.  这位名人在聚会上吸引了所有人的注意，用她的故事迷住了每个人。
Synonyms: dominate the conversation, be the center of attention, monopolize the conversation
Antonyms: be quiet, listen attentively, be reserved
