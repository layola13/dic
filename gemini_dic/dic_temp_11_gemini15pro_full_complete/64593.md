eruditely:/ˈerʊdaɪtli/ /ˌerjʊˈdaɪtli/| adv.|博学地，渊博地|In a scholarly or learned manner; with erudition.  Demonstrating extensive knowledge.|She spoke eruditely on the subject of ancient Roman history. 她博学地谈论了古罗马历史这个话题。|近义词：learnedly, scholarly, knowledgeably|反义词：ignorantly, superficially|词性:副词
Etymology: From Late Latin *eruditē*, ablative of *eruditus* (“learned, accomplished”), past participle of *erudire* (“to educate, to instruct, to polish”), from *e-* (“out”) + *rudis* (“rough, unpolished, untrained”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *rewd-* (“to break, to split”).
