dight:/daɪt/| v. (archaic)|打扮，装饰，装备|To dress, adorn, or equip, especially in a fine or stately manner.|The knight dighted himself in shining armor. 骑士穿上了闪亮的盔甲。|近义词：adorn, array, deck, dress, equip|反义词：undress, disrobe|verb

词源: From Middle English *dihten*, from Old English *dihtan* (“to arrange, order, compose, write, dictate”), from Proto-Germanic *dihtaną* (“to arrange, order, compose, set in order, ordain, appoint, determine, direct, rule, govern”), from Proto-Indo-European *deh₃- (“to put, place, arrange”). Cognate with Old Saxon *dihton* (“to order, arrange”), Old High German *tihton* (“to order, arrange, compose, prepare, make”), Old Norse *díta* (“to dictate”), Gothic *𐌳𐌹𐌷𐍄𐌰𐌽* (dihtan, “to set in order, ordain”).
