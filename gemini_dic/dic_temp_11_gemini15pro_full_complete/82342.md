grow-a-pair:/ˌɡroʊ ə ˈpɛr/| idiom |拿出勇气；拿出胆量；硬气一点 | To show courage, bravery, or assertiveness, especially in a situation where one has previously been timid or hesitant.  It is often used in a condescending or mocking way to challenge someone to be more decisive or take a risk.| Example 1:  "Just grow a pair and ask her out already!" (拿出点勇气，去约她出去吧！)

Example 2: "He needs to grow a pair and stand up to his boss." (他需要拿出胆量，勇敢地反抗他的老板。)| Near synonyms:  man up, toughen up, show some backbone, get some guts | Antonyms:  chicken out, back down, wimp out |idiom
