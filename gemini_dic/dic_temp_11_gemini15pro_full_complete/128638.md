nickels-and-dimes:/ˈnɪkəlz ənd daɪmz/| idiom |斤斤计较；为小钱操心；吝啬| To be concerned with or preoccupied with small amounts of money; to be frugal or stingy.  It implies being excessively careful or miserly with money, often to the point of pettiness.|  He's always nickels-and-diming his employees, refusing to pay for even basic office supplies.| 近义词: penny-pinching, frugal, thrifty, miserly | 反义词: extravagant, generous, spendthrift | idiom

nickels:/ˈnɪkəlz/| n. |五分镍币（美国和加拿大的货币单位）| A five-cent coin in the US and Canada.  | He paid for the candy with nickels. | 近义词: five-cent piece | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but could consider larger denominations like dimes, quarters, dollars) | noun
