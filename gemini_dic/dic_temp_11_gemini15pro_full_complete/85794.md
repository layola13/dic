haywire:/ˈheɪwaɪər/| adj.|混乱的，故障的，失控的|Out of control; in a state of disorder or malfunction.|例句：The computer system went haywire after the power surge.  电脑系统在电源浪涌后失控了。|例句：My plans went haywire when my flight was cancelled. 我的航班取消后，我的计划全乱了。|近义词：chaotic, disordered, malfunctioning, amuck, awry|反义词：orderly, functional, controlled|词性：形容词

Etymology:
From haywire (n.) "wire used for binding bales of hay," from hay + wire (n.).  Originally used to describe something repaired or improvised with this readily available material, often resulting in a messy or unreliable fix.  The figurative sense of "disordered, malfunctioning" developed from this.
