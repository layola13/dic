maypole:/ˈmeɪpoʊl/| n. |五月柱|A tall pole, decorated with ribbons and flowers, around which people dance on May Day.|例句：Children danced around the maypole on May Day. 孩子们在五朔节那天绕着五月柱跳舞。

例句：The maypole was a central part of the village's May Day celebrations. 五月柱是村里五朔节庆祝活动的中心部分。|近义词：May Day pole|反义词: None|词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *maypole*, from *may* + *pole*.  The "may" refers to the month of May, and "pole" refers to a long, slender, rounded piece of wood or metal.
