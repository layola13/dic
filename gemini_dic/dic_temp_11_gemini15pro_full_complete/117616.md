megapode:/ˈmɛɡəˌpoʊd/ | n. |冢雉| A large-footed bird of the family Megapodiidae, found in Australasia, that incubates its eggs in mounds of earth, sand, and decaying vegetation. | The male megapode diligently tends the nest mound. 雄冢雉会勤奋地照料巢穴土堆。| synonym:incubator bird, mound builder | antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps birds that use traditional nests like robins) | noun

megapode:/ˈmɛɡəˌpoʊd/ | n. | 巨足鸟 | A bird known for its large feet and unique incubation method. |  Megapodes are fascinating creatures to study. 巨足鸟是值得研究的迷人生物。 | synonym:brush turkey | antonym: (No direct antonym) | noun
