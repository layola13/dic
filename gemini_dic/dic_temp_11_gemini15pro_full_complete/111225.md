lithotomical:/ˌlɪθəˈtɒmɪkl/ | adj. |  切石术的；截石位的 | Relating to lithotomy, a surgical incision for removing stones, especially from the bladder, or the position in which a patient is placed for this operation. | The patient was placed in the lithotomical position for the surgery. 病人被置于截石位进行手术。 | The surgeon used a lithotomical knife.  外科医生使用了切石刀。|Synonyms: surgical, operative | Antonyms: non-surgical, therapeutic | adjective

lithotomy:/lɪˈθɒtəmi/ | n. | 切石术；截石位 | Surgical incision for removing stones, especially from the bladder, or the position in which a patient is placed for this operation. | The patient underwent a lithotomy to remove kidney stones.  病人接受了切石术以去除肾结石。 | The lithotomy position can be uncomfortable for patients. 截石位对病人来说可能不舒服。 | Synonyms: lithotomy position, stone removal surgery | Antonyms: N/A | noun
