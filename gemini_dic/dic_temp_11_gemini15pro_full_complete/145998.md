overdomesticating:/ˌoʊvərdoʊˈmɛstɪˌkeɪtɪŋ/
|v.|过度驯化|The act of domesticating something excessively, to the point where it loses its natural abilities or characteristics.|We have overdomesticated our pets, to the point where some breeds can barely survive without human intervention.|近义词: over-taming, over-civilizing|反义词: wilding, rewilding|词性: Verb (present participle)

overdomesticate:/ˌoʊvərdoʊˈmɛstɪˌkeɪt/
|v.|过度驯化|To domesticate something excessively, to the point where it loses its natural abilities or characteristics.|Some argue that we have overdomesticated dogs, making them dependent and less resilient.|近义词: over-tame, over-civilize|反义词: wild, rewild|词性: Verb


词源:
"Over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "domesticate" (from Latin "domesticus" meaning "belonging to the house").
