conceivably:/kənˈsiːvəbli/
adverb
可以想象地，可能地
It is conceivable that she might be wrong. 她可能是错的，这是可以想象的。
It’s conceivable that they knew about the plan all along. 可以想象，他们一直都知道这个计划。
conceivable: /kənˈsiːvəbl/ | adj. | 可以想象的，可能的 |  capable of being imagined or grasped mentally; possible | It is conceivable that he could win the election.  可以想象他会赢得选举。| imaginable, possible, plausible | inconceivable | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *conceivable, from Anglo-Norman concevable, conceyvable, from conceivre (“to conceive”) + -able (“-able”).  conceivre from Latin concipere (“to take in, conceive”), from con- (“together”) + capere (“to take, seize”).
