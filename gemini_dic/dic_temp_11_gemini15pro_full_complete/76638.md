gally:/ˈɡæli/| n.|厨房, 舰船上的炊事室, 印刷厂的排字房|A galley is the kitchen on a ship or boat. It can also refer to a compartment on an aircraft where food is prepared. In older usage, it could also mean the kitchen of a house or a room in a printing house where type is set.  The word derives ultimately from the Latin word *galea*, meaning "galley" (the ship).|The ship's galley was small and cramped. 船上的厨房又小又挤。

The galley was hot and stuffy. 印刷厂的排字房又热又闷。|synonyms: kitchen, cookhouse, scullery|antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps dining room or mess hall in certain contexts)|noun


gally:/ˈɡæli/| adj. (informal, chiefly nautical) |(of a ship or boat) unsteady or easily overturned; cranky|A gally boat is one that is unstable and prone to tipping. This usage is primarily nautical and informal.|The small sailboat was gally in the high winds.  这艘小帆船在大风中很不稳。

Be careful, that canoe is a bit gally. 小心点，那独木舟有点不稳。|synonyms: unstable, rickety, unsteady, wobbly|antonyms: stable, steady, secure|adjective
