nonaspirates:/ˌnɒnˈæspɪrɪts/ | n. | 非送气音 |  Consonant sounds produced without a noticeable puff of air, in contrast to aspirated sounds. |  In English, /p/, /t/, and /k/ are nonaspirates after /s/ at the beginning of a syllable, as in "spin," "stick," and "skill." | 近义词: unaspirated consonants | 反义词: aspirates, aspirated consonants | 词性: noun

词源:  From "non-" (not) + "aspirate" (to pronounce with an audible breath).  "Aspirate" ultimately derives from Latin *aspirare*, from *ad-* (to) + *spirare* (to breathe).
