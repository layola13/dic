overimitation: /ˌəʊvərɪmɪˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 过度模仿 | The action of copying another person's actions or behavior, even when those actions are unnecessary or irrelevant to achieving a goal. | Young children often exhibit overimitation, meticulously copying every step of a demonstration, even if some steps are clearly pointless.  孩子们经常表现出过度模仿，一丝不苟地复制示范的每一步，即使有些步骤显然毫无意义。| synonym: mimicry, emulation, aping | antonym: innovation, originality | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From 'over-' (prefix meaning 'excessive' or 'too much') + 'imitation' (from Latin 'imitatio', meaning 'a copying, imitation').
