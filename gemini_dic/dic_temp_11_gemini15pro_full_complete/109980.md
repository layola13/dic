libertinage:/ˌlɪbərˈtiːnɪdʒ/ | n. | 放荡，淫荡 | The state or practice of unrestrained indulgence in sensual pleasures; debauchery. |  The king was known for his libertinage.  国王以他的放荡闻名。| Synonyms: debauchery, licentiousness, promiscuity, profligacy | Antonyms: chastity, morality, virtue | noun

libertine:/ˈlɪbərtɪn/ | n. | 放荡者，浪荡子 | A person, especially a man, who behaves without moral principles or a sense of responsibility, especially in sexual matters. | The play featured a charming libertine who seduced innocent women. 这部戏以一个迷人的浪荡子为主角，他引诱无辜的妇女。 | Synonyms: rake, playboy, debauchee, profligate | Antonyms: prude, ascetic | noun

libertine:/ˈlɪbərtɪn/ | adj. | 放荡的，浪荡子的 | Characterized by a disregard of morality, especially in sexual matters. | He led a libertine lifestyle, indulging in every pleasure imaginable. 他过着放荡的生活，沉迷于各种可以想象的享乐。| Synonyms: dissolute, licentious, debauched, profligate | Antonyms: chaste, moral, virtuous | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *libertinage*, from *libertin* ("freethinker, libertine"), from Italian *libertino*, from Latin *lībērtīnus* ("freedman"), from *līber* ("free").  Originally referred to freed slaves in ancient Rome, then to freethinkers who rejected conventional religious beliefs, and eventually to those who disregarded moral restraints, especially in sexual matters.
