chiaroscurist:/ˌkjɑːrəˈskjʊərɪst/
n.|明暗对照法画家|An artist who uses chiaroscuro. | Caravaggio was a master chiaroscurist, famous for his dramatic use of light and shadow. 卡拉瓦乔是一位明暗对照法大师，以其对光影的戏剧性运用而闻名。| synonym: painter of light and shade, tenebrist | antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "colorist" in a loose sense, as it emphasizes color over light and shade) | noun

chiaroscuro:/ˌkjɑːrəˈskjʊəroʊ/
n.|明暗对照法; 光影 |The treatment of light and shade in drawing and painting. | The chiaroscuro in Rembrandt's paintings is truly breathtaking. 伦勃朗绘画中的明暗对比令人叹为观止。| synonym: light and shade, contrast, shading | antonym: flat lighting, even illumination | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *chiaroscuro*, from *chiaro* ("clear, light") + *oscuro* ("obscure, dark").  Ultimately from Latin *clarus* ("clear") and *obscurus* ("dark").
