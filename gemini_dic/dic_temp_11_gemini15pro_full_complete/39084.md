confounder:/kənˈfaʊndər/ | n. | 混杂因素，干扰因素 | Something that confuses or makes results unclear; a variable that influences both the dependent variable and independent variable, causing a spurious association. | Example 1:  Researchers tried to control for confounders like age and socioeconomic status in the study.  研究人员试图控制研究中的年龄和社会经济地位等混杂因素。

Example 2: The study failed to account for several key confounders, making its conclusions questionable.  这项研究未能考虑到几个关键的混杂因素，使其结论值得怀疑。 |Synonyms:  confusing factor, extraneous variable, lurking variable, intervening variable | Antonyms:  control variable | Noun


confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/ | v. | 使迷惑，使混淆，挫败 | To cause surprise or confusion in (someone), especially by acting against their expectations. To prove (a theory, expectation, or prediction) wrong. To defeat (a plan, aim, or hope). | Example 1: The sudden change in the data confounded the researchers.  数据突然的变化使研究人员感到困惑。

Example 2:  His recovery confounded the doctors who had predicted he wouldn't walk again.  他的康复让预言他再也无法行走的医生们感到困惑。 | Synonyms:  bewilder, perplex, puzzle, frustrate, thwart | Antonyms:  clarify, enlighten, explain | Verb
