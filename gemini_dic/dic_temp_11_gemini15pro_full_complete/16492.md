bargeman: /ˈbɑːdʒmən/ | n. | 驳船夫；船夫 | A man who works on or operates a barge. | The bargeman skillfully navigated the canal. 驳船夫熟练地在运河上航行。|  The bargeman steered the barge through the lock. 驳船夫驾驶驳船通过船闸。 |Synonyms: boatman, waterman, bargee | Antonyms: landsman | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From *barge* + *-man*. *Barge* comes from Old French *barge*, from Vulgar Latin *barca*, from Late Latin *barca*, of uncertain origin. *-man* is a common suffix denoting a person associated with a particular activity or thing.
