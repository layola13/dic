high-grading:/haɪˈɡreɪdɪŋ/
n.|高品位开采; 精选; 挑肥拣瘦|The practice of extracting only the most valuable part of a resource, such as ore or timber, leaving the rest behind.  It can also refer to selectively choosing the best employees from a company.|The company was accused of high-grading the forest, taking only the oldest and tallest trees. 这家公司被指控对森林进行高品位开采，只砍伐最古老、最高的树木。|
近义词:cherry-picking, cream-skimming, selective mining
反义词:low-grading
