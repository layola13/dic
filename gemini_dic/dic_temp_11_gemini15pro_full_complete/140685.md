ominousnesses:/ˈɑmɪnəsnɪsɪz/ | n. | 不祥之兆，凶兆 | The quality or state of being ominous; ominous character.  |The ominousnesses of the dark clouds gathering overhead suggested a storm was brewing.  头顶乌云密布的不祥之兆预示着一场暴风雨即将来临。|Synonyms: foreboding, portentousness, threateningness | Antonyms: auspiciousness, hopefulness, promise | Noun

Etymology: From ominous + -ness. Ominous comes from Latin *ominōsus, from ōmen (“omen”) + -ōsus (“full of”).
