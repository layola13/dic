counter-salient:/ˈkaʊntər ˈseɪliənt/| n.|反突出部，突出部|A counter-salient is a bulge or protrusion in a military line that extends into enemy territory, creating a vulnerable point susceptible to attack from multiple sides.  It is essentially the opposite of a salient, which is an outward bulge in one's own lines.|The enemy attacked the counter-salient with heavy artillery.|近义词:bulge, protrusion, pocket|反义词:salient|词性:名词

counter-salient:/ˈkaʊntər ˈseɪliənt/| adj.|反突出的|Extending into enemy territory, forming a counter-salient.|The counter-salient position made them vulnerable.|近义词:protruding, projecting|反义词:recessed, indented|词性:形容词
