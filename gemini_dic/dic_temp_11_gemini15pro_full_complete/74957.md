friars:/ˈfraɪərz/ | n. | 修士；男修士（friar 的复数） | Members of certain religious orders, especially the four mendicant orders (Augustinians, Carmelites, Dominicans, and Franciscans). | The friars lived a simple life of prayer and service.  修士们过着简朴的祈祷和服务的生活。

The friars traveled from town to town preaching. 修士们走遍城镇布道。 | 近义词: monks, brothers | 反义词: nuns, sisters | 词性: 名词

词源：
From Old French frere, from Latin frāter (“brother”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *bʰréh₂tēr (“brother”).
