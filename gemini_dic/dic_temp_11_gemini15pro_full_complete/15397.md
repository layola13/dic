backwater:/ˈbækwɔːtər/ (US), /ˈbækwɔːtə(r)/ (UK)| n. adj.|死水，回水；闭塞落后的地方|A place or condition in which no development or progress is taking place.|The town felt like a backwater, untouched by modern technology.|近义词: backcountry, hinterland, boondocks, stagnant, undeveloped|反义词: metropolis, hub, center, progressive, developed|词性: 名词，形容词

backwater:/ˈbækwɔːtər/ (US), /ˈbækwɔːtə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|闭塞落后的|Remote and undeveloped.|She grew up in a backwater town with limited opportunities.|近义词: backward, provincial, isolated|反义词: modern, advanced, cosmopolitan|词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "back" + "water." "Back" in this context refers to water held back or flowing backward, as in an eddy.  First recorded in the 1590s.
