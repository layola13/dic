deaconships:/ˈdiːkənʃɪps/
n.
执事职务；执事身份
The office or position of a deacon.  The period of time served as a deacon.
He fulfilled his deaconship with humility and grace. 他以谦逊和优雅履行了他的执事职责。
The church recognized his years of service during his deaconship. 教会认可了他在担任执事期间多年的服务。
近义词: deaconry, diaconate
反义词: None (No direct opposite for an office/position)
词性: Noun


Etymology:
From Late Old English *dēaconhād, from dēacon "deacon" + -hād "-hood."  The word "deacon" ultimately derives from the Greek diákonos, meaning "servant" or "minister."
