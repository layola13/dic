familiarised:/fəˈmɪliəraɪzd/ |v. | 使熟悉，使了解 | To make someone familiar with something; to accustom someone to something. |  She familiarised herself with the company's procedures before her first day. 她第一天上班前先熟悉了公司的流程。

The museum offers tours to familiarise visitors with its collection. 博物馆提供导览，让游客熟悉其馆藏。|Synonyms: acquainted, accustomed, versed | Antonyms: unfamiliar, unacquainted | Verb (past participle)


ETYMOLOGY->
From familiar + -ise/-ize.  "Familiar" comes from the Latin "familiaris," meaning "of or belonging to a family or household," from "familia" meaning "family."  The suffix "-ise/-ize" is used to form verbs meaning "to make or become."
