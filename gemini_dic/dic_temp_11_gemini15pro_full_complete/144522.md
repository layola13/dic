outtyrannizing: /ˌaʊtˈtɪrəˌnaɪzɪŋ/
|v.| 过于残暴地统治；专横跋扈地对待|To tyrannize to a greater degree than another tyrant; to exceed in tyranny; to surpass in cruelty or oppression.|His reign was marked by outtyrannizing all previous rulers. 他的统治以超越所有前任统治者的暴政为标志。|Synonyms: domineering, overbearing, oppressive, despotic|Antonyms: benevolent, compassionate, merciful|verb

Etymology:

* out- (prefix):  meaning "beyond," "more than," "outside," "excessively." From Old English *ūt, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *ūt, *ūz.
* tyrannize (verb): From Late Latin tyrannizare, from Greek tyrannizein, from tyrannos "tyrant."
