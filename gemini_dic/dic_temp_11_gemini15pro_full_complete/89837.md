horning:/ˈhɔːrnɪŋ/ | n. | 鸣笛；按喇叭；（旧时）不名誉的公开示众 |The constant horning of cars in the city is deafening.  城市里汽车不断的鸣笛声震耳欲聋。| English Explanation: The act of sounding a horn, especially a car horn. Historically, it also referred to a public display of someone in disgrace. | 例句：The driver was ticketed for excessive horning. 司机因过度鸣笛被开了罚单。

horning:/ˈhɔːrnɪŋ/ | n. | 驱逐；通缉 |He was declared a rebel and letters of horning were issued against him. 他被宣布为叛乱分子，并对他发出了通缉令。 | English Explanation:  A legal term, primarily in Scots law, for a writ or warrant issued to enforce a decree of a court. | 例句：Letters of horning were used to seize his property. 通缉令被用来没收他的财产。


近义词：honking, tooting, beeping |
反义词: silence |
词性: noun |
词源: From the Old English "hornung," meaning "the sounding of a horn." Related to the noun "horn."|
