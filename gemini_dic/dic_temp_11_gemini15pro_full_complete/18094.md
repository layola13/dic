behaviourism: /bɪˈheɪvjərɪzəm/ (US) /bɪˈheɪvjərɪzəm/ (UK)
n.|行为主义|A school of psychology that takes the objective evidence of behavior (such as measured responses to stimuli) as the only concern of its research and the only basis of its theory without reference to conscious experience.|Pavlov's experiments with dogs are a classic example of behaviourism. 巴甫洛夫的狗实验是行为主义的经典例子。|近义词: learning theory|反义词: cognitivism|词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From *behave* + *-iour* + *-ism*.  *Behave* comes from the Middle English *behaven*, from *be-* + *haven* "to have, hold, keep". *-iour* is a suffix forming nouns from verbs. *-ism* is a suffix forming nouns signifying a doctrine, belief, or practice.
