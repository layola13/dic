oversales:/ˌoʊvərˈseɪlz/ | n. | 超额销售；过量销售 | the practice of selling more of something, such as tickets for a flight or seats at a concert, than are actually available, in the hope that some people will not turn up or will cancel their booking | The airline apologized for the oversales and offered compensation to the affected passengers. 航空公司为超售机票道歉，并向受影响的乘客提供赔偿。 | 近义词: overbooking | 反义词: undersales | 词性: 名词

oversold:/ˌoʊvərˈsoʊld/ | adj. |  超额销售的；过分吹嘘的 | (of something) having been sold in excess of the available supply; (of an idea, etc.) promoted or praised excessively, especially in an attempt to persuade people | The concert was oversold, and many fans were turned away at the door. 音乐会门票超额销售，许多歌迷被拒之门外。 | The benefits of the new product were oversold, and it failed to live up to expectations. 新产品的好处被过分夸大了，它没有达到预期。 | 近义词: overhyped, exaggerated | 反义词: undersold | 词性: 形容词


词源:  "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much") + "sale" (noun referring to the act of selling).
