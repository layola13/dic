alms-house: /ˈɑːmzˌhaʊs/ (US), /ˈɑːmzˌhaʊs/ (UK)| n. |救济院，贫民院|A house built and endowed for the lodging and support of poor persons.|The old alms-house provided shelter for the destitute. 这座古老的救济院为贫困者提供住所。|Synonyms: poorhouse, workhouse|Antonyms: mansion, palace|noun


alms-house: /ˈɑːmzˌhaʊs/ (US), /ˈɑːmzˌhaʊs/ (UK)| n. |施舍，救济| (Archaic) The giving of alms, charity.|The alms-house relied on the alms-house of the wealthy.  (Archaic usage) 这家救济院依靠富人的施舍。|Synonyms: charity, donation|Antonyms: greed, selfishness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English ælmeshūs, from ælmesse ("alms") + hūs ("house").  The word "alms" ultimately derives from the Greek ἐλεημοσύνη (eleēmosynē), meaning "pity, mercy, alms."
