eclipses:/ɪˈklɪpsɪz/
n.
日蚀，月蚀；黯然失色，衰退
An eclipse occurs when one celestial body obscures another.
例句：A total solar eclipse is a rare and spectacular event. 日全食是一种罕见而壮观的事件。

例句：The popularity of the band eclipsed after their lead singer left.  乐队主唱离开后，乐队的人气就黯然失色了。
近义词：occultation, darkening, decline, waning
反义词：illumination, brightening
词性：名词


eclipse:/ɪˈklɪps/
vt.
遮蔽，使黯然失色；超越，胜过
The moon eclipsed the sun.
例句：The young player's skill eclipsed that of his older teammates.  这位年轻球员的技术超过了他的老队友。

例句：His recent successes have eclipsed all his previous achievements. 他最近的成功使他以前的所有成就都黯然失色。
近义词：outshine, surpass, exceed, obscure
反义词：be inferior to, be overshadowed by
词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *eclipser, from Latin eclipsis, from Ancient Greek ἔκλειψις (ékleipsis, “eclipse, abandonment”), from ἐκλείπω (ekleípō, “I abandon, I forsake, I fail to appear”), from ἐκ (ek, “out”) + λείπω (leípō, “I leave”).
