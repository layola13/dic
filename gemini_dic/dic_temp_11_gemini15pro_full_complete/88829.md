hogging:/ˈhɒɡɪŋ/ (UK), /ˈhɑːɡɪŋ/ (US)| n. v. |拱起，翘曲; (车辆、船只)首尾下垂，中间拱起|A curvature of a ship or vehicle in which the ends droop and the middle arches upward. |The ship exhibited hogging after running aground.  该船搁浅后出现了拱起现象。|Synonyms: buckling, warping, arching |Antonyms: sagging, drooping|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From hog (v.) + -ing.  The original sense referred to taking more than one's share, like a hog greedily eating. The nautical sense likely arose from the resemblance of a hog's arched back to the shape of a ship experiencing hogging.
