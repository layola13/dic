furnitures: /ˈfɜːrnɪtʃərz/
n.
(Incorrect usage) 家具
Furniture is an uncountable noun and does not have a plural form.  The correct word is "furniture."
*INCORRECT*:  The furnitures in the room were antique.
*CORRECT*: The furniture in the room was antique.
furniture (singular) 家具
Old English *furnitura, -ture*, from *furnian* "to furnish, provide, supply,"  from Latin *furnire*, *fornire* "to equip, furnish, provide," from *fornus* "oven," literally "that which provides heat." The original sense was "furnishings, equipment."
My grandmother left me all her furniture. 我的祖母把所有的家具都留给了我。
Near Synonyms: furnishings, fittings, appointments
No antonym for furniture exists as it's a concrete object.
