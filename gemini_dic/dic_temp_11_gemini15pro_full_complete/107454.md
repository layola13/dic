lampoon:/læmˈpuːn/
n.
讽刺文章；讽刺诗；嘲弄
A piece of writing, a cartoon, etc. that criticizes somebody/something in a humorous way, especially by making them/it seem ridiculous
例句：The magazine is known for its merciless lampoons of political figures. 这本杂志以其对政治人物无情的讽刺而闻名。

例句：His latest play is a cruel lampoon of the fashion industry.  他最新的戏剧是对时尚界的残酷讽刺。

近义词：satire, parody, mockery, caricature
反义词：praise, eulogy, tribute
词性：noun


lampoon:/læmˈpuːn/
vt.
讽刺；嘲弄
To criticize somebody/something publicly in a humorous way, especially by making them/it seem ridiculous
例句：The show lampooned the pretensions of the upper classes.  这个节目讽刺了上层阶级的虚荣。

例句：The newspaper lampooned the government's new policy. 这家报纸讽刺了政府的新政策。


近义词：satirize, mock, ridicule, caricature
反义词：praise, commend, extol
词性：verb


词源: From French *lamponner* "to guzzle, carouse," of uncertain origin, perhaps from *lampons*, plural of *lampon* "drinking song," from Old French *lamper* "to drink" (perhaps imitative). The sense shifted to "satirical song" then to "satire" in general.
