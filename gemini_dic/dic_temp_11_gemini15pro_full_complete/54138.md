disenfranchise: /ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ | vt. | 剥夺权利；剥夺公民权 | To deprive someone of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote. |  The new law disenfranchised many minority voters. 新法律剥夺了许多少数族裔选民的权利。

The government disenfranchised people who had committed serious crimes. 政府剥夺了犯有严重罪行的人的权利。
