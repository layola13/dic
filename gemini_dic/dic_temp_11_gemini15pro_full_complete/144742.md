over-articulate:/ˌōvər ärˈtɪkyəˌlāt/ | adj. | 过分清晰发音的；咬字过重的 |  Speaking more clearly and distinctly than is necessary, often to an unnatural or exaggerated degree.  Characterized by excessive precision in pronunciation. |  She over-articulates her words when she's nervous, making her sound unnatural.  她紧张的时候会过度清晰地发音，听起来很不自然。|  precise, pedantic, affected |  mumbled, slurred, indistinct | adjective

over-articulate:/ˌəʊvər ɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪt/ | v. |  过分清晰地发音；咬字过重 | To pronounce words with excessive clarity, often to an unnatural or exaggerated degree. | He tends to over-articulate when giving presentations, which can be distracting. 他在做演讲时往往会过度清晰地发音，这可能会让人分心。 | enunciate excessively, pronounce too precisely | mumble, slurred, mutter | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (excessive) + "articulate" (to pronounce distinctly).  "Articulate" comes from Latin "articulatus," past participle of "articulare" (to joint distinctly), from "articulus" (a small joint), diminutive of "artus" (joint).
