overprogrammed:/ˌoʊvərˈproʊɡræmd/
adjective
过度编程的，程序过多的
Having too much programming or too many scheduled activities.  Overburdened with planned activities, often leaving little room for spontaneity or flexibility.
例句：
The children were overprogrammed with extracurricular activities and had no time to just play.
孩子们课外活动安排得太多，没有时间玩耍。

The overprogrammed schedule left the employees feeling stressed and burnt out.
过度安排的日程让员工感到压力很大，精疲力竭。

近义词：overscheduled, overloaded, overbooked
反义词：under-scheduled, free, relaxed
词性：adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "programmed" (past participle of "program," meaning "to create a program or schedule"). The word reflects the increasing prevalence of structured activities in modern life.
