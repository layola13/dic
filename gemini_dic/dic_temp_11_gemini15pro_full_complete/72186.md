floppier:/ˈflɒpiər (UK), ˈflɑːpiər (US)| adj.|更软的，更松软的|Comparative form of "floppy."  Means more flexible and less rigid. |The old computer used a floppier disk than the new one. 旧电脑使用的磁盘比新电脑的软盘更软。|The lettuce was old and floppier than usual. 这棵生菜很老，比平时更蔫。|近义词：softer, limper, looser|反义词:stiffer, firmer, harder|词性:形容词

floppy:/ˈflɒpi (UK), ˈflɑːpi (US)| adj.|软的，松软的|Flexible and not stiff. Often used to describe things that hang loosely.|The dog had floppy ears.  这只狗的耳朵耷拉着。|The hat was too big and floppy. 这顶帽子太大了，松松垮垮的。|近义词：limp, droopy, flexible|反义词:stiff, rigid, firm|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "flop," meaning to fall or hang loosely, with the suffix "-y" added to make it an adjective.  "Flop" likely originates from a Scandinavian source.  "Floppier" is simply the comparative form of "floppy," created by adding "-er."
