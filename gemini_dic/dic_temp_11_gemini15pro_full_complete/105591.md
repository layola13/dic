kindling:/ˈkɪndlɪŋ/| n. |引火物，点火材料；(感情、兴趣等的)激起，煽动|Small pieces of dry wood or other flammable material used for starting a fire. Also, the act of arousing or stirring up (feelings, interest, etc.).|例句：She used some paper and kindling to start the fire in the fireplace. 她用了一些纸和引火物在壁炉里生火。

例句：The music was the kindling for a renewed interest in dance. 音乐重新燃起了人们对舞蹈的兴趣。|近义词：tinder, fuel, incitement|反义词：extinguisher, deterrent|词性:noun

kindling:/ˈkɪndlɪŋ/| adj. |（常用于构成复合词）易燃的，引火的|Used to describe something that easily catches fire or helps start a fire.|例句：Kindling wood is essential for starting a campfire. 引火柴对于生篝火至关重要。

例句：The dry leaves provided kindling material for the blaze. 干燥的树叶为大火提供了引火材料。|近义词：flammable, combustible|反义词：nonflammable, fire-resistant|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *kindlen, frequentative of *kinden, from Old Norse kynda (“to kindle”), from Proto-Germanic *kundōną (“to set fire to”), from Proto-Indo-European *gʷendʰ- (“to kindle, ignite”).
