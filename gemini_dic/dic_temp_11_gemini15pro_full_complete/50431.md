depravingly:/dɪˈpreɪvɪŋli/
| adv. |堕落地，腐败地|in a way that corrupts or makes morally bad|His actions were judged as depravingly influencing young people. 他的行为被认为对年轻人产生了堕落的影响。|
|The media was accused of depravingly sensationalizing the tragedy. 媒体被指责为了博眼球而堕落地渲染了这场悲剧。|
|Synonyms: corruptly, wickedly, sinfully, perversely|
|Antonyms: virtuously, righteously, morally|
|Adverb|

词源:  From "deprave" + "-ing" + "-ly".  "Deprave" comes from the Latin "dēpravāre"  (to make crooked, distort, corrupt), from "dē-" (down, away) + "pravus" (crooked, perverse).
