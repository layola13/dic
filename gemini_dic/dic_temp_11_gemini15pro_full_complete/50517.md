depriving:/dɪˈpraɪvɪŋ/
|v.|剥夺；使丧失|Taking away something from someone.  The new law is depriving many people of their basic rights.|例句：He was deprived of food and water for three days.  他被剥夺了食物和水三天。

例句：Depriving children of love and attention can have serious consequences.  剥夺孩子们的爱和关注会造成严重的后果。

|近义词：divesting, stripping, bereaving|反义词：giving, granting, providing|词性：动词(present participle of deprive)

deprive:/dɪˈpraɪv/
|vt.|剥夺；使丧失|To take something away from someone. The new law will deprive many people of their rights.|例句：The drought deprived the region of its vital water supply. 干旱使该地区失去了重要的水源。

例句：He was deprived of his freedom after the trial. 审判后他被剥夺了自由。

|近义词：dispossess, rob, divest|反义词：give, grant, endow|词性：动词
