logical-positivisms:/ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈpɒzɪtɪvɪzəmz/
n.
逻辑实证主义（复数）
A philosophy asserting the only meaningful philosophical problems are those which can be solved by logical analysis.  It rejected metaphysics and asserted that verifiable statements about the physical world or about logic and mathematics were the only meaningful ones.
Logical positivisms attempted to unify all sciences by reducing their statements to a common logical structure. 逻辑实证主义试图通过将其陈述简化为共同的逻辑结构来统一所有科学。

Logical positivisms were a dominant force in early 20th-century philosophy. 逻辑实证主义是20世纪早期哲学的主导力量。

Synonym: logical empiricism, neopositivism
Antonym: metaphysics, idealism
