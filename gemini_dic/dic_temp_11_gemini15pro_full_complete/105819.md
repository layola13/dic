kists:/kɪsts/
n.
chests, boxes (especially made of wicker or wood)
Chests or boxes, typically made of wicker or wood, used for storage.
The family's heirlooms were stored in several old kists in the attic.  家族的传家宝被存放在阁楼里的几个旧箱子里。
Old documents were found in the kist. 在箱子里发现了旧文件。
Synonym: chests, boxes, coffers
Antonym:  (No direct antonym; perhaps "open shelves" as a contrast to storage)
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Scots *kist*, from Middle Dutch *kiste*  "chest, box," related to Old English *cist*, *cest* "chest, box, coffin" (from Proto-Germanic *kista-), from Latin *cista* "box, basket," of uncertain origin.
