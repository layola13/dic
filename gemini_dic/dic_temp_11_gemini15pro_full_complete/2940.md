advocators:/ˈædvəkɪtərz/ | n. | 拥护者，提倡者 | A person who publicly supports or recommends a particular cause or policy. |  Example:  Advocates for free speech rallied against the new law. 言论自由的拥护者们集会反对这项新法律。

Example:  She is a strong advocate for animal rights. 她是一位坚定的动物权利倡导者。 | 近义词:supporters, proponents, champions | 反义词: opponents, adversaries, critics | 词性: noun


词源:
From Late Latin *advocator, from Latin advocatus ("one called to aid; advocate"), past participle of advocare ("to summon, call to one's aid"), from ad- ("to, toward") + vocare ("to call").
