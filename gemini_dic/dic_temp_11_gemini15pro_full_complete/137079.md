nonstructure:/nɒnˈstrʌktʃə(r)/ |n.| 非结构；非结构体；非结构件 |A nonstructure is a component of a building that is not part of the main structural system. It doesn't contribute to the overall stability of the building but serves other functions.  For example, cladding, partitions, and ceilings are considered nonstructural elements. |The earthquake damaged the building's nonstructure, causing the facade to fall off. 地震损坏了建筑物的非结构部分，导致外墙脱落。| synonyms: non-structural element, non-structural component | antonyms: structure, structural member | noun

nonstructural:/nɒnˈstrʌktʃərəl/ |adj.| 非结构的；非结构性的 |Nonstructural refers to elements of a building that do not contribute to its primary structural support. These components are often added for aesthetic or functional purposes, such as partitions, finishes, or cladding. |The nonstructural damage to the building was extensive, but the main frame remained intact.  建筑物的非结构性损坏很严重，但主体框架完好无损。| synonyms: non-load-bearing, secondary | antonyms: structural, load-bearing | adjective
