morosely: /məˈroʊsli/ (US) /məˈrəʊsli/ (UK) | adv. | 闷闷不乐地，阴郁地 | In a gloomy or sullen manner; sadly. | She stared morosely out the window at the rainy day. 她闷闷不乐地望着窗外阴雨绵绵的天。| He walked morosely through the empty streets. 他阴郁地走过空荡荡的街道。 | 近义词：sullenly, gloomily, glumly, despondently | 反义词：cheerfully, happily, joyfully | 词性：副词

词源: From the adjective "morose" + "-ly". "Morose" comes from the Latin word "mōrōsus", meaning "peevish" or "fastidious," ultimately from "mōs" ("custom" or "manner") and the suffix "-osus."
