balkanisms:/ˈbɔːlkənɪzəmz/ | n. | 巴尔干化，巴尔干地区特有的事物 |The political fragmentation and instability characteristic of the Balkan states, especially in the early 20th century. Also refers to the attitudes, ways of life, or other characteristics typical of the Balkan region. |  The collapse of Yugoslavia led to a resurgence of balkanisms, with ethnic tensions and territorial disputes flaring up across the region.  南斯拉夫的解体导致巴尔干地区冲突死灰复燃，整个地区的种族紧张关系和领土争端再次爆发。| synonyms: fragmentation, division, factionalism | antonyms: unity, integration, harmony | noun

balkanize:/ˈbɔːlkənaɪz/ | v. | 巴尔干化，使分裂 |To divide (a region or body) into smaller, often hostile, units. | The Austro-Hungarian Empire was balkanized after World War I.  奥匈帝国在第一次世界大战后被分裂成许多小国。| synonyms: divide, fragment, partition | antonyms: unite, integrate, consolidate | verb

balkanization:/ˌbɔːlkənaɪˈzeɪʃn/ | n. | 巴尔干化 |The process of dividing a region or body into smaller, often hostile, units. | The balkanization of the Middle East is a complex and ongoing process. 中东的巴尔干化是一个复杂且持续进行的过程。| synonyms: fragmentation, division, partition | antonyms: unification, integration, consolidation | noun
