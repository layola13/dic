imbrication:/ˌɪmbrɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. |叠瓦状，覆瓦状，交叠，镶嵌|The arrangement of something overlapping, like tiles or shingles on a roof.  The process of overlapping or being overlapped in this way.|The imbrication of the fish scales helps to protect it from predators. 鱼鳞的叠瓦状排列有助于保护它免受捕食者的侵害。|Synonyms: overlapping, overlaying, superposition|Antonyms: separation, disjunction|noun


imbricate:/ˈɪmbrɪkeɪt/| adj. |叠瓦状的，覆瓦状的|Having edges that overlap, like tiles or shingles on a roof.|The roof was made of imbricate slate tiles. 屋顶是用叠瓦状的石板瓦制成的。|Synonyms: overlapping, overlaying, shingled|Antonyms: separate, disjunct|adjective


imbricate:/ˈɪmbrɪkeɪt/| v. |使成叠瓦状，使交叠|To arrange something so that it overlaps, like tiles or shingles on a roof.|The artist imbricated the pieces of wood to create a textured surface. 艺术家将木片交叠起来，创造出有纹理的表面。|Synonyms: overlap, overlay, shingle|Antonyms: separate, disjoin|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *imbricare*, from *imbrex* "tile," related to *imber* "rain."  The idea is of tiles overlapping to shed rain.
