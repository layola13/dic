cadge:/kædʒ/ | v. | 乞讨，索取，蹭（饭、车等） | To ask for or obtain something (such as food or money) from someone without intending to pay for it or return it. | He cadged a cigarette from a stranger. 他向一个陌生人要了一根烟。|  synonyms: beg, sponge, scrounge, mooch | antonyms: give, provide, donate | verb

cadge:/kædʒ/ | n. | 乞讨，索取 | The act of cadging. |  His constant cadging annoyed his friends. 他不停地乞讨让他的朋友很恼火。 | synonyms: begging, sponging, scrounging | antonyms: giving, providing, donating | noun

词源:
The origin of "cadge" is uncertain, but it's thought to be related to the Scottish word "cad," meaning a traveling salesman or peddler, often one with a reputation for dishonesty. Over time, the meaning shifted to describe the act of begging or obtaining something by dubious means.
