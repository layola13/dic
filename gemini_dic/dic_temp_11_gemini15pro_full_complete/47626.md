debilitants:/dɪˈbɪlɪtənts/ | n. | 削弱物；使人衰弱的东西 | Substances or factors that weaken or impair strength, vitality, or function. |  Chronic stress and lack of sleep can be major debilitants. 长期压力和睡眠不足是主要的虚弱因素。|  The illness acted as a debilitant, leaving him weak and unable to work. 这种疾病削弱了他的身体，使他虚弱无力，无法工作。 | synonyms: weakeners, enervators, impairers | antonyms: fortifiers, strengtheners | noun


debilitating:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 使人衰弱的；削弱的 | Tending to weaken or impair strength, vitality, or function. | The debilitating effects of the disease left her bedridden. 这种疾病使她衰弱，卧床不起。 | He suffered a debilitating injury that ended his athletic career. 他遭受了使人衰弱的伤，结束了他的运动生涯。 | synonyms: weakening, enervating, sapping, draining | antonyms: invigorating, strengthening, fortifying | adjective


debilitate:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪt/ | v. | 使衰弱；削弱 | To make someone or something weak and infirm. | The chemotherapy debilitated his immune system. 化疗使他的免疫系统衰弱。|  A prolonged fever can severely debilitate a person. 长期发烧会严重削弱一个人的身体。 | synonyms: weaken, enfeeble, enervate, sap, drain | antonyms: strengthen, invigorate, fortify | verb


Etymology:  From Late Latin *dēbilitāre, from Latin dēbilis “weak” (from de- “away” + habilis “able”) + -itāre (frequentative suffix).
