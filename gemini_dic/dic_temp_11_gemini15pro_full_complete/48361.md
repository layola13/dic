decumbent:/dɪˈkʌmbənt/| adj.| 卧倒的；斜倚的；（尤指官员）不积极的，怠惰的 |Lying down, often in a relaxed or comfortable position; (especially of an official) failing to act with sufficient energy or determination.| The decumbent figure of a sleeping man lay on the park bench. 公园长椅上躺着一个睡觉的男人的身影。| The decumbent mayor faced criticism for his inaction during the crisis.  这位不作为的市长因在危机期间无所作为而面临批评。| 近义词: reclining, prostrate, supine, inactive, negligent | 反义词: upright, erect, active, diligent | 词性:形容词

decumbent:/dɪˈkʌmbənt/| n.|  (植物)匍匐茎 |(botany) A stem that lies flat on the ground with the tip turned upward.| The plant's decumbent stems spread across the ground. 这种植物的匍匐茎在地面上蔓延开来。|  Many creeping plants have decumbent stems that root at the nodes. 许多匍匐植物都有在节处生根的匍匐茎。| 近义词: procumbent, trailing | 反义词: erect | 词性:名词
