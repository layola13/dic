mercantilism:/ˈmɜːrkəntɪlɪzəm/ | n. | 重商主义 | An economic theory and practice common in Europe from the 16th to the 18th century that promoted governmental regulation of a nation's economy for the purpose of augmenting state power at the expense of rival national powers.  It was characterized by the belief that national wealth and power were best served by increasing exports and collecting precious metals in return. |  Mercantilism was the dominant economic system in Europe for centuries. 重商主义在欧洲几个世纪以来都是占主导地位的经济体系。

The British Empire's colonial policies were heavily influenced by mercantilism. 大英帝国的殖民政策深受重商主义的影响。 | 近义词: protectionism, commercialism | 反义词: free trade, laissez-faire | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mercantilisme*, from *mercantile* "merchant,"  from Italian *mercantile*, from *mercante* "merchant," from Latin *mercans* (genitive *mercantis*), present participle of *mercari* "to trade," from *merx* "merchandise."
