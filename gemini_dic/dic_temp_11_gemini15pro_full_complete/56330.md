dogmatisms: /ˈdɒɡməˌtɪzəmz/ (UK) /ˈdɔːɡməˌtɪzəmz/ (US)| n. |教条主义，武断；独断|The tendency to lay down principles too arbitrarily or dogmatically.  The practice of expressing personal opinions or beliefs as if they were certainly true and cannot be doubted.|His dogmatisms on art made him unpopular. 他在艺术上的武断让他不受欢迎.|Synonyms: doctrines, tenets, dogma, rigidity, orthodoxy|Antonyms: flexibility, open-mindedness, skepticism|Noun
