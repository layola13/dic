confederationists:/kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪsts/
|n.|邦联主义者；支持联邦制的人|Supporters or advocates of a confederation, a system of government in which several states or political units retain considerable independence while united for certain common purposes.|The confederationists argued for a weaker central government.|近义词：federalists, unionists|反义词:centralists, separatists|词性: 名词

confederationist:/kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪst/
|adj.|邦联的；联邦制的|Relating to or supporting a confederation.|The confederationist movement gained momentum in the 19th century.|近义词：federal, unionist|反义词:centralized, separatist|词性: 形容词
