anchorite:/ˈæŋkəˌraɪt/| n. |隐士，遁世者|A person who lives in seclusion, usually for religious reasons, often in a solitary cell or hermitage.|例句：The anchorite lived a simple life of prayer and contemplation in the desert.  这位隐士在沙漠中过着简朴的祈祷和沉思的生活。

例句：She chose the life of an anchorite, seeking spiritual solace away from the world. 她选择了隐士的生活，在远离尘世的地方寻求精神慰藉。|近义词：hermit, recluse, ascetic|反义词:socialite, extrovert|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *ancherite*, from Late Latin *anchorita*, from Greek *anachōrētēs* "one who has retired," from *anachōrein* "to retire, withdraw," from *ana* "back, up" + *chōrein* "to give way, withdraw."
