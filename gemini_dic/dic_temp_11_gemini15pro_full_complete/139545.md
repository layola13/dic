obtrusion:/əbˈtruːʒən/| n.|强行闯入，打扰，突出|An unwelcome intrusion into private life. 对私人生活的无礼干扰|The obtrusion of advertising billboards on the landscape. 广告牌对景观的突兀存在。|Synonyms: intrusion, imposition, interference|Antonyms: withdrawal, retreat, seclusion|noun

obtrude:/əbˈtruːd/| v.|强加，打扰，闯入|She didn't want to obtrude on their privacy. 她不想打扰他们的隐私。|He obtruded his opinions on everyone. 他把自己的意见强加于每个人。|Synonyms: impose, intrude, interfere|Antonyms: withdraw, retreat, abstain|verb

obtrusive:/əbˈtruːsɪv/| adj.|冒昧的，突兀的，显眼的|The logo was small and unobtrusive.  标志很小，并不显眼。|His loud laugh was obtrusive in the quiet library. 他响亮的笑声在安静的图书馆里显得格格不入。|Synonyms: intrusive, conspicuous, noticeable|Antonyms: inconspicuous, subtle, discreet|adjective
