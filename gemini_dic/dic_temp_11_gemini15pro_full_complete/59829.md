ectocuniform:/ˌɛktoʊˈkjuːnɪfɔːrm/
noun
外楔骨
The ectocuniform is one of the tarsal bones in the foot, specifically located on the lateral side of the tarsus.  It articulates with the metatarsals and other tarsal bones.
The ectocuniform bone is an important part of the foot's structure, providing stability and facilitating movement. 外楔骨是足部结构的重要组成部分，提供稳定性并促进运动。
The fourth metatarsal articulates with the cuboid and ectocuniform bones. 第四跖骨与骰骨和外楔骨连接。
Fractures of the ectocuniform, while uncommon, can occur from high-impact injuries.  虽然不常见，但外楔骨骨折可能由高冲击损伤引起。
Synonyms:  lateral cuneiform, third cuneiform (sometimes used, though technically distinct)
