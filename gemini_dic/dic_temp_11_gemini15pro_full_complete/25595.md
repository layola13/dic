bumming:/ˈbʌmɪŋ/
bumming| adj.| 令人沮丧的；烦人的；无聊的 |Causing or characterized by a feeling of disappointment, annoyance, or boredom. |The constant rain was bumming me out.  不停的雨让我很沮丧。|Synonyms: depressing, annoying, tedious, boring |Antonyms: uplifting, exciting, enjoyable|adjective

bumming:/ˈbʌmɪŋ/
bumming| n.| 乞讨；流浪 |The practice of begging or living as a vagrant. |He resorted to bumming to survive. 他为了生存下去不得不乞讨。|Synonyms: begging, panhandling, vagrancy |Antonyms: working, earning |noun

bumming:/ˈbʌmɪŋ/
bumming| v.| 乞讨；闲逛；蹭（烟、酒等）|(1) To beg or obtain something by asking for it. (2) To spend time idly or without purpose. (3) To obtain something (such as a cigarette or a drink) from someone else for free.  |He was bumming cigarettes from strangers. 他向陌生人讨烟抽。|Synonyms: begging, cadging, scrounging, loafing, idling |Antonyms: giving, working, earning |verb
