monocarbonate:/ˌmɒnəʊˈkɑːbənət/ /ˌmɑːnəʊˈkɑːrbənət/
noun
碳酸一价盐; 一碳酸盐
A salt containing the radical HCO3-. It is formed by replacing one of the hydrogen atoms in carbonic acid with a metal or other positive ion.
Sodium monocarbonate is commonly known as baking soda. 碳酸一钠通常被称为小苏打。
synonym: bicarbonate, hydrogen carbonate
antonym: (No direct antonym, but perhaps consider carbonate as a related but different compound)
n.
