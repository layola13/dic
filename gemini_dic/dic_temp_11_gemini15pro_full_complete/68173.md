faint-heartedness:/ˌfeɪntˈhɑːrtɪdnəs/| n.|胆怯，懦弱|The faint-heartedness of the soldiers led to their defeat. 士兵们的胆怯导致了他们的失败。|例句：His faint-heartedness prevented him from asking her out. 他的胆怯使他不敢约她出去。

例句：She overcame her faint-heartedness and spoke up at the meeting. 她克服了胆怯，在会议上发言。

近义词：timidity, cowardice, fearfulness|反义词：bravery, courage, boldness|词性:名词

Etymology:
From faint-hearted + -ness.  Faint-hearted from faint + hearted.  Faint from Old French *feint, past participle of feindre “to feign, pretend,” from Latin fingere "to shape, form, invent, feign." Hearted from heart + -ed.
