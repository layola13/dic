dreynte:/ˈdreɪntə/| adj.| drenched, soaked (obsolete)|  Meaning "thoroughly soaked, drenched." This word is archaic and no longer in common use.| He was dreynte to the bone after falling in the river. 他掉进河里后浑身湿透了。|近义词：soaked, drenched, saturated|反义词:dry, arid|词性:adjective
Etymology: From Middle English *dreint, past participle of drenchen, from Old English *drencan (“to drench”), from Proto-Germanic *drankjaną (“to cause to drink; to give to drink; to drown”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰrengʰ- (“to draw, pull, drag”). Cognate with Old High German trenkan (“to give to drink; to drown”), Old Norse drekka (“to drink”), Dutch drenken (“to water, soak, steep”), German tränken (“to give to drink; to saturate”).
