headest:/ˈhɛdɪst/| adj.|最主要的，最重要的 (Non-standard, archaic or dialectal)|This usage is considered non-standard, archaic, or dialectal. It's an archaic superlative form of "head,"  meaning "chief" or "most important."  It is very rarely used in modern English and would generally be considered incorrect.|He was the headest of the clan.
