flake:/fleɪk/| n. |薄片，碎片；古怪的人|A small, thin piece of something, typically one that has broken away from a larger piece.  Also, an informal term for an unreliable or eccentric person.|The paint was coming off the wall in flakes.  油漆正一片片地从墙上脱落。 He's a bit of a flake, he's always changing his mind. 他有点古怪，总是改变主意。|Synonyms: chip, shaving, fragment, eccentric, unreliable|Antonyms: whole, stable, reliable|noun

flake:/fleɪk/| v. |剥落，成片脱落|To come off in flakes.|The paint is flaking off the wall. 油漆正从墙上剥落。|Synonyms: peel, chip, crumble|Antonyms: adhere, stick|verb
