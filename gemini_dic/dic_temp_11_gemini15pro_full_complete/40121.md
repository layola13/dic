contemplate: /ˈkɒntɛmpleɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːntəmˌpleɪt/ (US)| v. |沉思；仔细考虑；思忖；考虑；注视；凝视|To think profoundly and at length; to look at or think about something attentively.|He contemplated the meaning of life. 他沉思着生命的意义。|She contemplated the painting for a long time. 她凝视着这幅画很长时间。|近义词：consider, ponder, meditate, reflect, muse|反义词：ignore, disregard|词性: 动词

词源: From Latin contemplatus, past participle of contemplari (“to gaze attentively, observe, contemplate”), from con- (“together”) + templum (“temple, place for observation”). Originally referred to the practice of augurs observing the flight of birds from a consecrated space (templum) in order to interpret omens.
