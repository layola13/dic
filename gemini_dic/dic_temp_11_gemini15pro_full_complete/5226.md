alliteration:/əˌlɪtəˈreɪʃn/ | n. |头韵，押头韵|The repetition of the same sounds—usually initial consonants of words or of stressed syllables—in any sequence of neighboring words. |Example 1: "Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers" is a classic example of alliteration. 彼得·派珀摘了一小撮腌辣椒（这是一个典型的头韵的例子）。

Example 2:  She sells seashells by the seashore. 她在海边卖贝壳。|Synonym:  assonance, consonance |Antonym:  N/A (No direct antonym for a literary device) |noun
