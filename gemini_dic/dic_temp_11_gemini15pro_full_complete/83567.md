haemapod:/ˌhiːməˈpɒd/ | n. | 血足 | An appendage modified for walking, typical of certain insect larvae.  Specifically refers to the fleshy, leg-like structures on the abdomen of some insect larvae, particularly those of certain butterflies and moths. | The caterpillar moved across the leaf using its haemapods and prolegs. 毛毛虫用它的血足和腹足爬过叶子。 | synonym: proleg, pseudopod | antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "true leg") | noun
