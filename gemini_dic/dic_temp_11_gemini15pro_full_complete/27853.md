canonize:/ˈkænənaɪz/| vt.|使成为经典；使成为圣徒；推崇；承认…为圣典|To declare (a deceased person) to be a saint, especially by the Roman Catholic Church; to regard as being above reproach or beyond criticism.|例句：The church canonized Joan of Arc in 1920. 教会在1920年将圣女贞德封为圣徒。

例句：His works have been canonized by literary critics. 他的作品已被文学评论家奉为经典。|近义词：deify, exalt, glorify, sanctify|反义词：condemn, denounce, vilify|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *canonizare, from Latin canon "rule, standard," from Greek kanōn "straight rod, measuring rod; rule, standard."
