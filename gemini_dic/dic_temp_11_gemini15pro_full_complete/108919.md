leech:/liːtʃ/| n. v.|水蛭；吸血鬼；榨取他人钱财的人；依附他人生活的人|A bloodsucking worm that lives in water.  A person who extorts money from others or lives off others. |例句：The leech clung to his leg. 水蛭粘在他的腿上。|例句：He accused her of being a leech on her family. 他指责她是家里的吸血鬼。|近义词：parasite, bloodsucker, freeloader|反义词:host, benefactor|词性:名词, 动词

leech:/liːtʃ/| v.|吸血；榨取|To suck blood; to extort money or other resources from someone. |例句：Leeches were used in medicine to leech blood from patients. 水蛭在医学上被用来给病人放血。|例句：He leeches off his wealthy friends. 他榨取他有钱朋友的钱财。|近义词：bleed, exploit, sponge|反义词:give, donate|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lǣce, from Proto-Germanic *lāikaz, meaning "physician, healer".  Ironically, the word came to refer to the worm due to its use in medieval medicine.  The metaphorical sense of "someone who clings to another for personal gain" developed later.
