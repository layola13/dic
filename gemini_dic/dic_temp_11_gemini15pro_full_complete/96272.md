indiction:/ɪnˈdɪkʃən/
n.
指示;指定;宣布;征兆;起诉
a formal announcement or proclamation
The president's speech contained an indiction of his plans for the next four years. 总统的演讲包含了他未来四年计划的声明。

a period of fifteen years, used in dating historical documents, especially in the Middle Ages
Medieval scholars often used indictions to date documents. 中世纪学者经常使用指示周期来确定文件的日期。

a charge or accusation, especially in connection with a legal process
The grand jury returned an indiction against the suspect. 大陪审团对嫌疑人提出了起诉。

a sign or indication
The dark clouds were an indiction of an approaching storm. 乌云是暴风雨即将来临的征兆。

indictment (尤指正式的)控告，起诉;迹象，征兆
Synonyms: proclamation, declaration, announcement, decree, edict, ordinance, command, order, instruction, directive, mandate, pronouncement, signification, indication, sign, symptom, portent, omen, presage, augury, forewarning, warning
Antonyms: concealment, suppression, hiding, cover-up, veiling, masking, disguising, cloaking, camouflaging
