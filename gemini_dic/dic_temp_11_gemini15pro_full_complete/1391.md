accusative:/əˈkjuːzətɪv/| adj. n.|宾格的，宾格|Of, relating to, or being the grammatical case of a noun or pronoun that is typically the direct object of a verb.  Marking the direct object of a transitive verb.|The accusative case marks the direct object of the verb. 宾格标志着动词的直接宾语。|She placed the book (accusative) on the table. 她把书（宾格）放在桌子上。|近义词：objective|反义词：nominative|词性: adjective, noun

Etymology->
From Late Latin *accusativus, from Latin accusare (“to accuse”).  The connection is that the accusative case often marks the person or thing *affected* by the verb's action.  The term is a calque of Greek αἰτιατική (aitiatikḗ, “accusative”).
