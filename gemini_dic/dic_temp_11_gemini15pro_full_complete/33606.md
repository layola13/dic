cinches:/ˈsɪntʃɪz/| n. |肚带；束带；确定的事情|A cinch is a strong strap, usually made of leather or canvas, used to fasten something tightly, especially a saddle to a horse's back. It can also figuratively refer to something that is very easy or certain. |The saddle slipped because the cinches were loose.  马鞍滑落了，因为肚带松了。|近义词：strap, belt, girth, sure thing, easy task |反义词:uncertainty, difficulty|noun


cinches:/ˈsɪntʃɪz/| v. |用肚带系紧；确定；确保|To cinch something means to fasten it securely with a cinch. Figuratively, it means to make certain of something or to guarantee it. |He cinched the saddle tightly before mounting the horse. 他上马前紧紧地系好了马鞍。|近义词：fasten, secure, guarantee, ensure |反义词: loosen, unfasten, jeopardize|verb
