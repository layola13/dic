charlatanical: /ˌʃɑːrləˈtænɪkəl/ | adj. |江湖骗子的；装腔作势的；招摇撞骗的 | Relating to or characteristic of a charlatan; fraudulently claiming to have more knowledge or skill than one possesses. |  He gave a charlatanical lecture on the benefits of snake oil. 他做了一个关于蛇油益处的江湖骗子式的演讲。| synonyms: fraudulent, deceptive, bogus, phony, sham, spurious | antonyms: genuine, authentic, sincere, honest | adjective

charlatan: /ˈʃɑːrlətən/ | n. |江湖骗子；装腔作势的人；招摇撞骗的人 | A person falsely claiming to have a special knowledge or skill; a quack. | The charlatan sold fake cures to desperate people.  这个江湖骗子把假药卖给绝望的人们。| synonyms: quack, fraud, imposter, swindler, con artist | antonyms: expert, professional, authority | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *charlatan*, from Italian *ciarlatano*, from *ciarlare* "to prattle, chatter," of imitative origin. Ultimately from the Latin word *garrire* meaning "to chatter".
