cognovit:/kɒɡˈnəʊvɪt/|/kɑːɡˈnoʊvɪt/| n. |认罪书；认债书|A written confession of a debt, by which a debtor authorizes a creditor to enter judgment against him or her without a formal legal proceeding.|The debtor signed a cognovit, allowing the creditor to seize his assets.|Synonym: confession of judgment, acknowledgment of debt|Antonym: denial, defense|noun
