gentler:/ˈdʒɛntlə(r)/| adj.|更温柔的，更柔和的|Comparative form of gentle: more kind or friendly; less harsh or severe.|She spoke in a gentler tone to the frightened child. 她用更温和的语气对受惊的孩子说话。|近义词：milder, softer, calmer|反义词：harsher, rougher, sterner|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Middle English *gentler, comparative of gentil, from Old French gentil, from Latin gentilis (“of the same clan or family; of the same race or nation; belonging to the same people”), from gens (“clan, race, nation, people”).  The semantic shift from "noble" to "kind, gentle" occurred in both French and English.
