appeasers:/əˈpiːzərz/
|n.|安抚者；绥靖者；姑息者|A person who appeases someone or something, especially a potential aggressor.|例句：The appeasers believed that giving in to Hitler's demands would prevent war. 绥靖主义者认为，满足希特勒的要求可以避免战争。|例句：History has judged the appeasers harshly.  历史对绥靖主义者作出了严厉的评判。|近义词：conciliators, pacifiers, mediators|反义词：aggressors, warmongers|词性: 名词

词源:
From the verb "appease" + "-er" (agent suffix).  "Appease" comes from Old French "apaisier"  meaning "to pacify," ultimately from Latin "pac-" (peace).
