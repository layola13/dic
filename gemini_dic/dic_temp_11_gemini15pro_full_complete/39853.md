constabulatory:/kənˈstæbjʊlətɔːri/ | adj. |巡逻的，巡查的| Relating to a police force organized for patrolling a specific area. | The town had a small but effective constabulatory force.  小镇有一支规模虽小但效率很高的巡逻警察部队。| 近义词: patrolling, policing | 反义词:  N/A | 词性: adjective

Etymology: Late 16th century (in the sense ‘office of a constable’): from Latin constabulatus, from constabularius ‘stable-master’, later ‘governor of a castle, marshal’, from comes stabuli (see constable). The current sense dates from the mid 19th century.
