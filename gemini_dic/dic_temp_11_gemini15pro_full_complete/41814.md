cortile:/kɔːrˈtiːleɪ/ /kɔːrˈtaɪleɪ/
noun
庭院，天井
An open courtyard, especially of a large building such as a palazzo or villa, typically paved and surrounded by colonnades or arcades.
The hotel's central cortile was filled with lush plants and a tinkling fountain.  酒店中心的庭院里种满了茂盛的植物，还有一个叮咚作响的喷泉。
courtyard, patio, piazza
garden, field
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian cortile, from Vulgar Latin *cortile, from Latin cohors (“enclosure, yard”). Doublet of court.
