candle:/ˈkændl/ (US) /ˈkændəl/ (UK)| n. |蜡烛|A cylindrical or tapered stick of wax or tallow with a wick in the center, which is burned to provide light.|The soft glow of the candles created a romantic atmosphere. 蜡烛柔和的光芒营造出浪漫的气氛。|近义词：taper, light|反义词：darkness, electric light|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *candel (unattested), from Late Latin candela, from Latin candēre "to shine, glow," related to candidus "white, bright."
