augurial: /ɔːˈɡjʊəriəl/ | adj. | 预兆的，占卜的 | Relating to augury; of the nature of an augury; predicting; ominous. | The augurial signs pointed towards a successful venture. 预兆显示这是一次成功的冒险。 |  The ancient Romans looked for augurial signs before making important decisions. 古罗马人在做重要决定之前会寻找预兆。 | 近义词: prophetic, ominous, portentous | 反义词: unprophetic, auspicious | 词性: adjective

Etymology: From Latin *auguriālis*, from augur (“augur, diviner”) +‎ *-ālis*.  The word "augur" itself comes from the Latin word *augur*, meaning "diviner" or "soothsayer", ultimately possibly related to *avis* (“bird”) because the flight of birds was a common method of divination.
