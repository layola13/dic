gentiles:/ˈdʒɛntaɪlz/ | n. |非犹太人，外邦人|People who are not Jewish. |例句：The Apostle Paul was sent to preach the gospel to the Gentiles. 使徒保罗被派去向外邦人传福音。

例句：In ancient Rome, Gentiles often viewed Jewish customs with suspicion. 在古罗马，外邦人常常对犹太人的习俗抱有怀疑态度。|近义词：non-Jews, heathens (贬义), pagans (贬义)|反义词：Jews, Israelites|词性:名词

Etymology: From Old French *gentil* "noble, high-born, of good family, kind, gentle," from Latin *gentilis* "of the same clan or race," from *gens* "clan, race, nation." The religious sense developed as Jews used the term to distinguish themselves from other nations.
