narcotical: /nɑːrˈkɒtɪkl/ | adj. | 麻醉的；催眠的；麻醉剂的 | Relating to or producing narcosis or stupor; inducing sleep.  | The doctor prescribed a narcotical pain reliever after the surgery.  医生在手术后开了麻醉止痛药。 |  The effects of the drug were narcotical. 这种药物的作用是麻醉性的。 | Synonyms:  soporific, hypnotic, opiate, anesthetic | Antonyms:  stimulating, invigorating | adjective


narcotic: /nɑːrˈkɒtɪk/ | n. | 麻醉剂；麻醉药 | A drug that relieves pain and induces drowsiness, stupor, or insensibility. |  The police found a large quantity of narcotics in the car. 警方在车里发现了大量的麻醉药品。 | Morphine is a powerful narcotic. 吗啡是一种强效麻醉剂。 | Synonyms:  opiate, analgesic, painkiller | Antonyms:  stimulant | noun


Etymology: From Late Latin *narcoticus, from Greek narkōtikos “benumbing, narcotic,” from narkoun “to benumb,” from narkē “numbness, stupor.”
