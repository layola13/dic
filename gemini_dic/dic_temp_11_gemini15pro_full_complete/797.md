absorb:/əbˈzɔːrb/ /əbˈsɔːrb/| v. |吸收，理解，使全神贯注|To take in or soak up (liquids or other substances); to occupy the full attention of; to incorporate or assimilate.|The sponge absorbed the spilled water. 海绵吸收了洒出的水。|Synonyms: soak up, take in, imbibe, assimilate, incorporate, engross, captivate|Antonyms: exude, emit, release, repel, distract|verb

absorb:/əbˈzɔːrb/ /əbˈsɔːrb/| adj. |(常用于科学)可吸收的|Capable of absorbing something.|Absorbent paper towels are useful for cleaning up spills. 吸水纸巾对于清理溢出物很有用。|Synonyms: porous, permeable|Antonyms: impermeable, nonporous|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin absorbēre, from Latin ab- (“from, away from”) + sorbēre (“to suck up, swallow”).
