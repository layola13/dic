niobe:/ˈnaɪoʊbiː/| n.|尼俄柏, 骄傲自负的人|A character in Greek mythology, the queen of Thebes, whose children were slain by Apollo and Artemis because she boasted of her superiority to their mother Leto.  Now used to refer to someone who is excessively proud or arrogant, especially someone whose pride leads to their downfall.|例句：She was a niobe, weeping over the loss of her fortune. 她像尼俄柏一样，为失去的财富而哭泣。

例句：His niobe-like pride led to his downfall in the company. 他尼俄柏式的骄傲导致了他在公司的垮台。|近义词：braggart, show-off|反义词：humble person|词性:noun
