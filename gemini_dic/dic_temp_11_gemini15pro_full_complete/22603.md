boottopping:/ˈbuːtˌtɒpɪŋ/ (US) /ˈbuːtˌtɒpɪŋ/ (UK)| n.|船底涂漆，水线漆|The area of a ship's hull that is between the waterline and the topsides, which is usually painted a different color.|The ship's boottopping was a bright red.|Synonyms: waterline paint, antifouling paint|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "hull" which is the whole structure the boottopping is a part of)|noun

boottopping:/ˈbuːtˌtɒpɪŋ/ (US) /ˈbuːtˌtɒpɪŋ/ (UK)| n.|水线与上层建筑之间的部分|The part of a ship's hull that lies between the waterline and the upper works.|The boottopping needed a fresh coat of paint.|Synonyms: topsides, freeboard (related terms, not exact)|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "keel" which is the bottom of the hull)|noun
