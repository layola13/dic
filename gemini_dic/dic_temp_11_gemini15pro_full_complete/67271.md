exquisite:/ˈɛkskwɪzɪt, ɪkˈskwɪzɪt/| adj.|精致的；精美的；剧烈的；敏锐的|delicately beautiful and pleasing, especially in a refined or elaborate way; intense; acutely sensitive|The necklace was made of exquisite pearls. 这条项链是用精美的珍珠制成的。|Synonyms: delicate, elegant, refined, superb, perfect, choice, dainty, flawless, impeccable, exquisite, divine, ethereal, magnificent, lovely, beautiful, gorgeous, stunning, breathtaking|Antonyms: crude, rough, coarse, clumsy, ugly, hideous, awful, terrible, dreadful|adjective

exquisites:/ˈɛkskwɪzɪts, ɪkˈskwɪzɪts/| n.|精美的东西；精致的人|things of exquisite beauty or delicacy; people with refined tastes|She surrounded herself with exquisites – fine art, rare books, and beautiful jewelry. 她周围都是精美的东西——精美的艺术品、珍贵的书籍和漂亮的珠宝。|Synonyms: delicacies, rarities, treasures, gems, jewels, masterpieces, connoisseurs, aesthetes|Antonyms: junk, trash, rubbish, riffraff, philistines|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English exquisit, exquesit, from Old French exquisit, exquis, from Latin exquisītus, past participle of exquīrere (“to search out, seek diligently, examine carefully, inquire into”), from ex- (“out, thoroughly”) + quaerere (“to seek, ask”).
