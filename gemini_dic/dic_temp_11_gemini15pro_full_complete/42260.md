counter-argument:/ˈkaʊntərˌɑːrɡjʊmənt/ (US), /ˈkaʊntərˌɑːɡjʊm(ə)nt/ (UK)| n. |反驳；反对理由|An argument made to oppose another argument. |例句：She presented a strong counter-argument to his proposal. 她对他的提议提出了强有力的反驳。

|例句：His counter-argument was well-researched and persuasive. 他的反驳经过充分的研究，很有说服力。

|近义词：rebuttal, objection, confutation|反义词：argument, support, agreement|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + argument.  "Counter-" comes from the Old French "contre-" meaning "against," ultimately derived from the Latin "contra." "Argument" comes from the Old French "argument," from the Latin "argumentum," meaning "proof, evidence, reason."
