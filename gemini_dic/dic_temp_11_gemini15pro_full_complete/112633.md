low-profile:/ˌloʊ ˈproʊfaɪl/| adj.|低调的，不引人注目的|Avoiding attention or publicity; not attracting attention to oneself.  Characterized by a low physical height or projection.|He tried to keep a low profile after the scandal. 他试图在丑闻之后保持低调。|近义词：unobtrusive, discreet, inconspicuous, humble, modest|反义词：high-profile, ostentatious, flamboyant|adjective


low-profile:/ˌloʊ ˈproʊfaɪl/| n.|低调，不张扬|The state or quality of being low-profile; a deliberate avoidance of public attention.|The company adopted a low profile during the investigation.  这家公司在调查期间保持低调。|近义词：inconspicuousness, discretion, modesty|反义词：showiness, ostentation, prominence|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"low" + "profile"
"low" (adj.) Old English *laȝ, from Proto-Germanic *laga- (source also of Old Norse lagr "low," Dutch laag, Old High German lach, German niedrig "low"), from PIE root *leg- (1) "to lie, lay." 
"profile" (n.) mid-15c., "outline of a face," from Italian profilo "an outline," from profilare "to draw in outline," from pro "forth" (see pro-) + filare "to spin, draw out threads," from Latin filum "thread" (see file (n.2)).
