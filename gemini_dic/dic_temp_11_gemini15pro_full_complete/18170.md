beknave:/bɪˈneɪv/
verb
欺骗，诱骗，哄骗
to treat (someone) dishonestly : to deceive or trick (someone)
He was beknighted by his supposed friends. 他被那些所谓的“朋友”欺骗了。
Synonyms: deceive, trick, cheat, defraud, swindle, dupe, hoodwink, bamboozle
Antonyms: help, assist, support, aid, protect
词性: v.

Etymology:
From be- + knave.  "Be-" is a prefix meaning "to make" or "to cause to be."  "Knave" originally meant "boy" or "servant," but later evolved to mean "dishonest man" or "rogue."  Therefore, "beknave" literally means "to make someone a knave" or "to cause someone to become dishonest."
