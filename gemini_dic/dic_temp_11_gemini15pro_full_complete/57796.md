dredge:/drɛdʒ/ | n. vt. | 疏浚机，挖泥机；(用挖泥机)疏浚，挖掘 | A machine used for deepening or clearing harbors, rivers, etc. | The harbor was dredged to allow larger ships to enter. 港口被疏浚，以便更大的船只能够进入。 | Synonyms: excavator, digger | Antonyms: fill, backfill | noun

dredge:/drɛdʒ/ | vt. | (用挖泥机)疏浚，挖掘；搜寻，翻找 | To remove mud, weeds, etc. from the bottom of a river, lake, etc.; to search thoroughly | They dredged the river to make it deeper.  他们疏浚了河流，使它更深。 | Synonyms: excavate, scoop, search, rummage | Antonyms: bury, conceal | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dreccan "to trouble, vex," from Proto-Germanic *draggijan "to draw, pull, drag," from Proto-Indo-European *dheregh- "to draw, pull." The sense shifted from "to drag a net" to "to dredge with a drag-net" and then to "to dredge" in general.
