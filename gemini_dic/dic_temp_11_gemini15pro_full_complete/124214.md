moslem's:/ˈmʊzləmz/ | n. | 穆斯林的（所有格） |  An outdated and often offensive term referring to something belonging to a Muslim.  It is now generally considered more respectful to use "Muslim's". | This is the moslem's prayer rug. (This sentence uses outdated and potentially offensive language. It’s better to say: This is the Muslim’s prayer rug.) | 近义词: Muslim's | 反义词: N/A | 词性:  possessive noun


词源:
From Middle English *musulman, from Old French musulman, mousselin, from Medieval Latin musalmanus, from Arabic مسلم‎ (muslim, “Muslim”).  The spelling with -o- is now archaic and often considered offensive.

用法说明:

USAGE->
This word is outdated and often considered offensive.  It is preferable to use "Muslim's".  Using "Moslem" can be perceived as disrespectful due to its historical association with colonialist and orientalist views of Islam and Muslims.
