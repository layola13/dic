depose:/dɪˈpoʊz/ /dɪˈpɒz/ | vt. | 免职；罢免；作证；宣誓作证 | To remove someone from a position of power, especially a king or queen; To make a statement under oath. | The king was deposed by his own son. 国王被他自己的儿子废黜了。

The witness was called to depose before the court.  证人被传唤到法庭作证。 | 近义词：overthrow, oust, testify, declare | 反义词：install, reinstate | 词性: 动词

deposer:/dɪˈpoʊzər/ /dɪˈpɒzər/ | n. | 废黜者；罢免者；宣誓作证者 |  One who deposes, especially one who removes someone from a position of power or one who gives testimony under oath. | The deposer of the king was his ambitious son.  废黜国王的是他雄心勃勃的儿子。

The deposer provided crucial evidence in the case. 作证者提供了此案的关键证据。 | 近义词：dethroner, witness | 反义词：supporter | 词性: 名词

词源：
From Old French *deposer "to lay down, place, put; depose (a ruler)," from Latin depositus, past participle of deponere "lay down, put down, deposit, entrust; resign," from de- "down, away" (see de-) + ponere "to put, place" (from PIE root *apo- "off, away"). Meaning "to testify under oath" from mid-15c. Related: Deposed; deposing.
