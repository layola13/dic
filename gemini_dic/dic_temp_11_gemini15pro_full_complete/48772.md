deflater:/dɪˈfleɪtər/| n.|压缩器；放气装置|A device or substance that deflates something, especially a tire.|The deflater quickly emptied the air mattress.|Synonym: compressor, pump (for inflating)|Antonym: inflator|noun


deflate:/dɪˈfleɪt/| v.|放气；泄气；使缩小；使灰心|To release air or gas from something, making it smaller or less full; to reduce the importance or effectiveness of something; to make someone feel less confident or important.|The sudden drop in sales deflated the company's optimism. |He deflated the tires before storing the car for the winter. |Synonym: flatten, collapse, diminish, discourage|Antonym: inflate, encourage, boost|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Latin word *deflāre*, meaning "to blow away" or "to deflate,"  composed of *de-* (down, away from) and *flāre* (to blow).
