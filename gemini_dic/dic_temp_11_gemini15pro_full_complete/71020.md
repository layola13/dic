firmest:/ˈfɜːrmɪst/| adj.|最坚定的，最牢固的，最坚实的|Superlative form of firm: most firm.  Meaning most resolute, unyielding, or unwavering; most stable, solid, or secure.|The coach's firmest supporters stayed loyal even after the losing season. 教练最坚定的支持者即使在失利的赛季后仍然保持忠诚。|The firmest ground for the tent was found near the riverbank.  帐篷最坚实的地面是在河岸附近找到的。|近义词：strongest, most stable, most resolute, most solid|反义词：weakest, loosest, shakiest, least stable|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fermest*, superlative of *ferm*, from Old English *feorm*, *fyrm* (“firm, fixed, constant, strong, steadfast, unshakeable”), from Proto-Germanic *firmą (“firm”), from Proto-Indo-European *perkʷ- (“strong”).  Cognates include Dutch *verm* (“fine”), German *firm* (“firm”), Latin *firmus* (“strong, firm, stable”).
