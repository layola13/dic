词源:
From Old English *līf (attested only in compounds), from Proto-Germanic *lībam (source also of Old Frisian līf, Old Saxon līf, Old Norse líf, Dutch lijf "body", German Leib "body", Gothic 𐌻𐌹 Leib "flesh, body"), from Proto-Indo-European *leip- "to stick, adhere; fat, grease".  The sense development in Germanic is from "body, flesh" to "life, lifetime".
