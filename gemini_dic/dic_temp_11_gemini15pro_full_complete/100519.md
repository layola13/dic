interviewers:/ˈɪntərvjuːərz/| n.|面试官，采访者|People who conduct interviews.  Those who ask questions to assess a candidate for a job or to gather information for a news story, article, or research.|The interviewers were impressed with her qualifications. 面试官对她的资历印象深刻。|synonyms: assessors, examiners, questioners|antonyms: interviewees, candidates, applicants|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From interview + -er (agent suffix) + -s (plural suffix).  "Interview" comes from French "entrevue," meaning "a meeting between," from "entre-" (between) and "vue" (sight).
