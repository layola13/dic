coal:/koʊl/ (US), /kəʊl/ (UK)| n.|煤，煤块|A black or brownish-black sedimentary rock that is combustible and used as a fuel.|The power plant burns coal to generate electricity.  发电厂燃烧煤炭发电。|synonyms: fuel, anthracite, lignite|antonyms:  (Coal doesn't have a direct opposite.  Perhaps "renewable energy" could be considered an antonym in a context discussing energy sources.)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *col (recorded only in compounds such as colpytt "coal pit"), from Proto-Germanic *kula- (source also of Middle Dutch kole, Old High German chol, German Kohle), from Proto-Indo-European *g(e)u-lo-, from root *g(e)u- "to burn."
