bluffer:/ˈblʌfər/| n.| 虚张声势的人；吹牛者 |Someone who bluffs; a person who pretends to have knowledge or confidence that they do not possess, especially in order to deceive someone.| He was a bluffer, pretending to know more about the subject than he actually did. 他是个爱虚张声势的人，假装对这个话题比实际上更了解。| synonyms: imposter, charlatan, fraud, fake, pretender | antonyms: honest person, straight shooter | noun

bluff:/blʌf/| v.| 虚张声势；吓唬；蒙骗|To deceive someone by pretending to be stronger or more confident than one really is.|He bluffed his way through the interview, even though he wasn't qualified for the job.  他虽然没有资格胜任这份工作，但却靠着虚张声势蒙混过关了面试。|synonyms: deceive, pretend, feign, fake, sham |antonyms: be honest, tell the truth | verb

bluff:/blʌf/| adj.| 直率的；坦率的|Direct and outspoken; blunt.|His bluff manner can sometimes be mistaken for rudeness. 他直率的举止有时会被误认为是粗鲁。|synonyms: frank, direct, blunt, outspoken, forthright |antonyms: subtle, indirect, evasive | adjective

bluff:/blʌf/| n.| 陡峭的悬崖；绝壁|A steep cliff or bank.|The house stood on a bluff overlooking the ocean. 这栋房子坐落在俯瞰大海的悬崖上。|synonyms: cliff, precipice, escarpment, bank, ridge |antonyms: valley, plain | noun
