outspokenly:/ˌaʊtˈspoʊkənli/
adverb
坦率地，直言不讳地
In a frank and unreserved manner; candidly.
She outspokenly criticized the government's policies. 她坦率地批评了政府的政策。
He outspokenly expressed his disapproval. 他直言不讳地表达了他的反对意见。
近义词:candidly, frankly, bluntly, directly
反义词:reservedly, cautiously, discreetly
词性:adverb

outspoken:/ˌaʊtˈspoʊkən/
adjective
坦率的，直言不讳的
Frank and unreserved in speech; candid.
He was an outspoken critic of the regime. 他是一位对政权直言不讳的批评家。
She is outspoken in her views on animal rights.  她在动物权利方面的观点非常坦率。
近义词:frank, candid, blunt, direct
反义词:reserved, cautious, discreet
词性:adjective

speak:/spiːk/
verb
说话，讲话
Say something in order to convey information or express a feeling.
He spoke about his experiences in the war. 他讲述了他在战争中的经历。
She spoke to her boss about a raise. 她和她的老板谈了加薪的事情。
近义词:talk, say, utter, express
反义词:listen, be silent
词性:verb

词源:
From "outspoken" + "-ly". "Outspoken" comes from "out-" + "speak" + "-en".  "Out-" is an Old English prefix meaning "out, outside, beyond". "Speak" comes from Old English "sprecan". "-en" is a suffix meaning "to make or become".  "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives.
