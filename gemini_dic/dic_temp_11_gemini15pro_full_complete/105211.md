keynotes:/ˈkiːnəʊts/ (UK), /ˈkiːnoʊts/ (US)| n.|主题演讲；主旨；基调；要旨|A keynote is the central idea or theme of something, typically a speech or a piece of writing. It can also refer to the main speech given at a conference or other formal gathering.|The keynote of the conference was innovation in technology.  这次会议的主题是技术创新。|Synonyms: theme, main point, central idea, essence, gist, core, heart, nub|Antonyms: detail, triviality, periphery, insignificant point|noun

keynotes:/ˈkiːnəʊts/ (UK), /ˈkiːnoʊts/ (US)| v.|发表主题演讲；确定基调|To keynote means to give the main speech at a conference or other formal gathering, or to set the central theme or tone of something.|The CEO keynoted the annual sales conference. 首席执行官在年度销售会议上发表了主题演讲。|Synonyms: address, speak, present, deliver a speech, set the tone|Antonyms: listen, attend, observe|verb
