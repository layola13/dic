deflation:/dɪˈfleɪʃən/ | n. | 通货紧缩，物价下跌 | A reduction of the general level of prices in an economy. | Example 1: The government is trying to combat deflation. 政府正在努力对抗通货紧缩。 | Example 2:  Deflation can lead to a decrease in consumer spending. 通货紧缩会导致消费者支出减少。 | Synonym: disinflation, price decrease | Antonym: inflation, price increase | noun


deflate:/dɪˈfleɪt/ | v. |  （使）瘪；（使）泄气；降低（价格或工资）| To release air or gas from something, making it smaller; to reduce the amount or value of something. | Example 1: The tires deflated slowly after the puncture. 轮胎被刺破后慢慢漏气了。| Example 2: The government's policies deflated the economy. 政府的政策导致经济萎缩。| Synonym: collapse, shrink, reduce | Antonym: inflate, expand, increase | verb
