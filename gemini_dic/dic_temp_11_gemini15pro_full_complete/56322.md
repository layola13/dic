dogmatic:/dɒɡˈmætɪk/ /dɑːɡˈmætɪk/| adj.|教条的，武断的|Inclined to lay down principles as incontrovertibly true.|He has a dogmatic belief in the literal truth of the Bible. 他对圣经字面真理的信仰十分教条。|近义词：doctrinaire, opinionated, peremptory, authoritarian|反义词：open-minded, flexible, receptive|词性:形容词

dogmatic:/dɒɡˈmætɪk/ /dɑːɡˈmætɪk/| n.|教条，信条|A principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true.|The politician's speech was filled with empty dogmatics. 这位政治家的演讲充满了空洞的教条。|近义词：doctrine, tenet, dogma|反义词：- (通常不用反义词)|词性:名词 (较少使用)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dogmaticus*, from Greek dogmatikos "pertaining to doctrines," from dogma (genitive dogmatos) "opinion, tenet, that which seems true," from dokein "to seem, think, imagine."
