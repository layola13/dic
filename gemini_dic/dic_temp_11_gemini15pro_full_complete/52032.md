dialectician:/ˌdaɪəlekˈtɪʃən/| n.|辩证法家，辩论家|A person skilled in dialectic; a logician. Someone who studies or uses dialectic.|例句：He was a skilled dialectician, able to argue any point convincingly. 他是一位技艺娴熟的辩证法家，能够令人信服地论证任何观点。

例句：The dialectician skillfully exposed the flaws in his opponent's argument. 这位辩证法家巧妙地揭露了对手论点中的缺陷。

|近义词：logician, debater, arguer, reasoner|反义词:dogmatist|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dialecticus*, from Ancient Greek διαλεκτικός (dialektikós, “of or for conversation, discussion, debate”), from διάλεκτος (dialektos, “conversation, dialogue, dialect”).  Ultimately from the verb διαλέγομαι (dialégomai, “I converse, discuss”).
