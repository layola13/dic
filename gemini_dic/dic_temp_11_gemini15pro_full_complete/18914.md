besot:/bɪˈsɒt/ /bɪˈsɑːt/| vt.|使沉迷，使糊涂，使痴 infatuate|He was completely besotted with her. 他完全被她迷住了。|Synonyms: infatuate, captivate, enchant |Antonyms: disenchant, disillusion |verb

besot:/bɪˈsɒt/ /bɪˈsɑːt/| adj.(old-fashioned, usually passive) |沉迷的，糊涂的  infatuated|He was besotted with grief after his wife's death.  妻子去世后，他悲痛欲绝。|Synonyms: infatuated, obsessed, preoccupied |Antonyms: clear-headed, sober |adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From be- + sot (a fool, a habitual drunkard).  The original meaning was "to make a sot of," hence "to stupefy with drink."
