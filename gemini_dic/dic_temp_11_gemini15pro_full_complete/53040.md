diogenes:/daɪˈɒdʒɪˌniːz/ /daɪˈɑːdʒɪˌniːz/
n.|第欧根尼|A Greek philosopher and one of the founders of Cynic philosophy. He is known for his extreme asceticism and rejection of social conventions. |Diogenes lived in a barrel and famously searched for an honest man with a lantern in broad daylight.|近义词:cynic, ascetic|反义词:conformist, hedonist|词性:noun
Etymology: From Latin Diogenes, from Ancient Greek Διογένης (Diogénēs).
