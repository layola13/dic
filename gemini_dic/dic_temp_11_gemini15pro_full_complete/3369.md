affronted: /əˈfrʌntɪd/ | adj. | 受到冒犯的，感到侮辱的 | Feeling or showing offense or indignation at having been treated disrespectfully. |  She looked affronted when I questioned her judgment. 她当我质疑她的判断时，看起来很不高兴。| Synonyms: insulted, offended, aggrieved, indignant, piqued | Antonyms: pleased, delighted, flattered, honored | word property: adjective

affront: /əˈfrʌnt/ | n. vt. | 冒犯，侮辱 | An action or remark that causes outrage or offense. / To offend the dignity or character of. | His refusal to shake hands was a deliberate affront. 他拒绝握手是故意的侮辱。/  I did not mean to affront you. 我不是有意要冒犯你。| Synonyms: insult, offense, slight, indignity | Antonyms: compliment, praise, honor | word property: noun, verb

Etymology: From Old French *afronter "to strike on the forehead, confront, insult," from a- "to, at" + front "forehead."  Ultimately from Latin frons "forehead, brow, front."
