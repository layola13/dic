downcast:/ˈdaʊnkæst/| adj. n.|沮丧的，垂头丧气的；下垂的；（目光）向下投射的；沮丧的神情|A downcast look/expression. 沮丧的表情|Her eyes were downcast. 她的目光向下投射。|Synonyms: dejected, depressed, gloomy, glum, sad, despondent|Antonyms: cheerful, happy, optimistic, upbeat|adjective

downcasts:/ˈdaʊnkæsts/| n.|沮丧的神情；垂头丧气的人|The downcasts of the team after the loss were evident. 球队输球后垂头丧气的样子显而易见。|The shelter was filled with downcasts who had lost everything. 收容所里挤满了失去一切的沮丧之人。|Synonyms: dejected people, depressed individuals|Antonyms: cheerful people, optimistic individuals|noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *douncasten*, from *doun* (down) + *casten* (to cast).  The original meaning was "to cast down" literally, such as casting one's eyes downwards.  The figurative meaning of being emotionally cast down (depressed) developed later.
