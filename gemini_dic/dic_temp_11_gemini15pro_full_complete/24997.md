brutalization:/ˌbruːtələˈzeɪʃn/ | n. | 野蛮化；残酷化；兽性化 | The act of making someone brutal or the process of becoming brutal.  | The brutalization of war can leave lasting psychological scars. 战争的残酷化会留下持久的心理创伤。 |  The brutalization of the prisoners of war was horrific. 战俘遭受的残酷待遇骇人听闻。 | 近义词：dehumanization, barbarization | 反义词：civilization, humanization | 词性: noun

brutalize:/ˈbruːtəlaɪz/ | vt. | 使残暴；使丧失人性；残酷对待 | To make someone brutal or treat someone with brutality. | The constant violence brutalized the soldiers.  持续的暴力使士兵们变得残暴。 | The regime brutalized its opponents. 该政权残酷对待其反对者。| 近义词：batter, abuse, mistreat | 反义词：humanize, civilize | 词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From brutal + -ize.  Brutal comes from the Late Latin brutalis, meaning "of beasts," from Latin brutus "dull, senseless, irrational, brutish," figuratively "of beasts."
