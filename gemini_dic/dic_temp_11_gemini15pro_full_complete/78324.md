gerrymander: /ˈdʒɛrimændər/ (US), /ˈdʒɛrimændə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.| 不公正地划分选区；操纵选区 | The act of manipulating the boundaries of an electoral constituency so as to favor one party or class.  |  The opposition party accused the government of gerrymandering to maintain its power. 反对党指责政府为了维持其权力而操纵选区。| Synonyms: manipulate, rig, fix | Antonyms: fair apportionment, equitable distribution | noun, verb

gerrymander: /ˈdʒɛrimændər/ (US), /ˈdʒɛrimændə(r)/ (UK)| v.| 不公正地划分选区；操纵选区 | To divide (a geographic area) into voting districts so as to give unfair advantage to one party in elections.  | The state legislature gerrymandered the districts to ensure a Republican majority. 州立法机关为了确保共和党占多数而操纵选区。| Synonyms: manipulate, rig, fix | Antonyms: fair apportionment, equitable distribution | verb
