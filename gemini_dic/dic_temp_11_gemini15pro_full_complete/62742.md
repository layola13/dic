englishry:/ˈɪŋɡlɪʃri/
n.
英国国民;英国特性;英国文化
The state or condition of being English; English customs and characteristics.
The Norman conquerors looked upon the conquered Saxons with contempt, and punished the slightest breach of the forest laws with barbarous severity, and every transgression of the game laws was punished with loss of eyes or hands; the practice of "Englishry," or presentment of the body of a murdered person, and requiring the hundred or township in which the body was found to pay a heavy fine, unless they could prove that the murdered person was English, was another cruel invention of Norman tyranny.
近义词: Englishness, Anglicism
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps foreignness or alienness in a broad sense)
