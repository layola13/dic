alibis: /ˈælɪbaɪz/ (UK), /ˈælɪbaɪz/ (US)| n. |不在场证明；托辞，借口|A claim or piece of evidence that one was elsewhere when an act, typically a criminal one, is alleged to have taken place. | He had a solid alibi for the night of the murder. 他在谋杀案发生当晚有确凿的不在场证明。| synonyms: excuse, pretext, defense| antonyms: confession, admission | noun

alibis: /ˈælɪbaɪz/ (UK), /ˈælɪbaɪz/ (US)| v. | 为…提供不在场证明；辩解|To offer an excuse or defense for (someone or something), especially by providing an account of one's whereabouts at the time of an alleged act. | She tried to alibi her husband's absence. 她试图为丈夫的缺席辩解。| synonyms: excuse, justify, vindicate| antonyms: accuse, incriminate | verb
