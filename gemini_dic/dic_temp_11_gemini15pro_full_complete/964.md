acadian:/əˈkeɪdiən/ | adj. n. | 阿卡迪亚的；阿卡迪亚人 | Relating to Acadia (a region of eastern Canada) or its inhabitants. |例句：Acadian French is a dialect spoken in some parts of Canada.  阿卡迪亚法语是加拿大某些地区的一种方言。

例句：Many Acadians were deported by the British in the 18th century.  许多阿卡迪亚人在18世纪被英国人驱逐出境。|近义词：Nova Scotian (sometimes), Maritime (general region) |反义词: N/A |词性: adjective, noun

Etymology: From Acadia, the former French colony in eastern Canada.  The name Acadia is believed to derive from the Mi'kmaq word *akatik*, meaning "place where things abound."
