durometer:/duːˈrɒmɪtər/ /duˈrɑːmɪtər/| n. |硬度计；硬度测试器|An instrument for measuring the hardness of materials, especially rubber and plastics.  |例句：The technician used a durometer to test the hardness of the rubber seal.  技术员用硬度计测试橡胶密封圈的硬度。

|例句：The durometer reading indicated that the plastic was too soft for the application. 硬度计读数表明该塑料对于此应用来说太软了。|近义词：hardness tester, sclerometer|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


durometer:/duːˈrɒmɪtər/ /duˈrɑːmɪtər/| n. |硬度|The hardness of a material as measured by a durometer. (Less common usage) |例句：The durometer of the tire was checked for wear and tear.  轮胎的硬度被检查了磨损情况。(不太常用)

|例句：The specified durometer for the gasket was 70 Shore A.  垫片的规定硬度为70肖氏A硬度。(不太常用) |近义词：hardness, firmness|反义词:softness|词性:noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From *dur(o)-* (combining form of Latin *dūrus* "hard") + *-meter* (suffix meaning "measuring instrument").
