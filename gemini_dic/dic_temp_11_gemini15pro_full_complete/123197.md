monochlamydeous:/ˌmɒnəʊkləˈmɪdiəs/
adjective
单被的，单轮被的
Having a single floral envelope, i.e., the perianth consisting of either calyx or corolla only, or the parts all similar and not distinguishable as calyx or corolla.
Example: Monochlamydeous flowers, unlike dichlamydeous ones, have only one whorl of perianth segments.  单被花与双被花不同，只有一个轮生的花被片。

Example:  The botanist identified the plant as monochlamydeous due to its single, undifferentiated perianth.  植物学家将这种植物鉴定为单被植物，因为它只有一个未分化的花被。

Synonym: apetalous, haplochlamydeous
Antonym: dichlamydeous

Etymology:  From mono- (one) + Greek chlamys (cloak, mantle) + -ous (adjective suffix).
