bigoted: /ˈbɪɡətɪd/ | adj. | 固执己见的，偏执的，顽固的 | Showing strong, unreasonable prejudice against a particular group of people, especially on the basis of their race, religion, or sexual orientation. |  He made bigoted remarks about immigrants. 他对移民发表了偏执的言论。 | prejudiced, biased, intolerant, narrow-minded, dogmatic | tolerant, open-minded, unprejudiced, unbiased | adjective

bigoted: /ˈbɪɡətɪd/ | adj. | 顽固的，抱有成见的 |  Obstinately attached to a particular creed, opinion, practice, or ritual; devoted to one's own opinions and prejudices, especially : blindly or intolerantly devoted. |  She is bigoted against anyone who doesn't share her political views. 她对任何与她政治观点不同的人都抱有成见。| prejudiced, biased, intolerant, narrow-minded, dogmatic | tolerant, open-minded, unprejudiced, unbiased | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From bigot (n.), from Old French bigot (12c.), of unknown origin. Perhaps from Visigoth, or from a Germanic oath by God.
