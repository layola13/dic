decompiling:/ˌdiːkəmˈpaɪlɪŋ/ | n. | 反编译 | The process of converting executable code (like a .exe file) back into a more human-readable form, such as higher-level source code. This is often done to understand how a program works, or to modify it. |  Example: Decompiling the game allowed modders to add new features. 反编译游戏允许修改者添加新功能。 | Synonyms: reverse engineering, disassembling | Antonyms: compiling | noun
