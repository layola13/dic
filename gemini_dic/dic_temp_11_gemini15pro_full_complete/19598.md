blighted:/ˈblaɪtɪd/ | adj. | 枯萎的；衰败的；毁坏的；使人扫兴的 | Damaged, spoiled, or harmed.  Affected by blight; decaying or deteriorated. Bringing unhappiness, misery, or disappointment. | The blighted crops were a sign of the coming famine. 枯萎的庄稼预示着饥荒即将到来。 | Synonyms: withered, ruined, decayed, spoiled, cursed, jinxed | Antonyms: flourishing, thriving, healthy, prosperous | adjective

blight:/blaɪt/ | n. | 枯萎病；祸害；不良影响 | A disease that causes plants to wither and die. Something that spoils or damages something. | The potato blight caused widespread famine in Ireland. 马铃薯枯萎病在爱尔兰造成了 widespread 的饥荒。 | Synonyms: affliction, curse, plague, scourge | Antonyms: blessing, boon, benefit | noun

blight:/blaɪt/ | vt. | 使枯萎；破坏；摧残 | To cause (a plant) to wither and die. To spoil, harm, or destroy. | The frost blighted the orange crop. 霜冻使柑橘作物枯萎。| Synonyms: ruin, spoil, destroy, impair, mar | Antonyms: nourish, improve, enhance | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *blighte*, from Old English *blīhtan* (“to shine, gleam”), of uncertain origin. The sense shifted from "to make pale or sickly" to "to cause to wither and decay."
