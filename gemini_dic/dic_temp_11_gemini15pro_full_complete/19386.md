bicameralism:/ˌbaɪˈkæmərəlɪzəm/| n.|两院制|The principle of having two separate legislative chambers or houses, such as a Senate and a House of Representatives.|The United States operates under bicameralism, with the Senate and the House of Representatives.|Synonyms: dual-chamber system, two-chamber system|Antonyms: unicameralism|noun
