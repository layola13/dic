bonanza:/bəˈnænzə/
n.
大量；富矿带；好运；意外的收获
A sudden, large amount of something pleasant or valuable.
The recent decrease in oil prices has been a bonanza for consumers.  最近油价下跌对消费者来说是一件好事。
The discovery of gold in California created a bonanza for many people. 在加州发现黄金为许多人创造了财富。
近义词: windfall, boon, godsend, jackpot
反义词: dearth, scarcity, lack, deficiency
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *bonanza "fair weather, prosperity," from Vulgar Latin *(bona) bonacia "good weather," from Latin bonus "good" + Late Latin aura "breeze." Originally a nautical term referring to favorable winds, it later broadened to mean general prosperity and good fortune.  源于西班牙语 *bonanza “好天气，繁荣”，来自通俗拉丁语 *(bona) bonacia “好天气”，源自拉丁语 bonus “好” + 晚期拉丁语 aura “微风”。最初是一个航海术语，指顺风，后来扩展到指普遍的繁荣和好运。
