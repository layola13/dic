eliminant:/ɪˈlɪmɪnənt/| n.|消除剂，消元式|A substance that eliminates something, especially a poison or toxin from the body; in mathematics, a function of the coefficients of a set of simultaneous polynomial equations whose vanishing is a necessary and sufficient condition for the set to have a nontrivial solution.|The eliminant of the two equations is zero, indicating a common solution.|近义词:determinant, resultant|反义词:N/A|词性:noun
Etymology:  Late 19th century: from eliminate + -ant.
