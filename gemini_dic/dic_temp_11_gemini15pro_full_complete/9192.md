antioch: /ˈæntiɑːk/ | n. | 安提阿 | An ancient city, formerly in Syria and now in Turkey, important in early Christianity.  It was one of the earliest centers of Christianity and is mentioned several times in the New Testament. |  Antioch was an important city in the Roman Empire. 安提阿是罗马帝国的一座重要城市。


Antioch features prominently in the Acts of the Apostles. 安提阿在使徒行传中占有突出地位。 | 近义词: city, metropolis | 反义词: village, hamlet | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek Ἀντίοχος (Antíokhos), ultimately from ἀντί (antí, “against”) + ἔχω (ékho, “to hold, have”). Several cities were named Antioch, typically after kings named Antiochus. The most famous Antioch was Antioch on the Orontes, also known as Antiochia ad Orontem.
