kinetograph:/ˌkaɪˈnɛtəˌɡræf/ /ˌkɪˈniːtəɡrɑːf/
noun
活动电影放映机；电影摄影机
An early movie camera invented by Thomas Edison and William Kennedy Laurie Dickson that used celluloid film to record a sequence of images.
The Kinetograph was a groundbreaking invention in the history of cinema. 活动电影放映机是电影史上的突破性发明。
The first Kinetograph parlor opened in 1894. 第一家活动电影放映机影院于1894年开业。
Synonym: movie camera, film camera
Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps projector as an opposite function)
词性: 名词
