ordalian:/ɔːrˈdiːliən/ /ɔːrˈdiːljən/
adjective|考验的，磨难的|Resembling an ordeal; difficult and painful.|例句：The climb to the summit was an ordalian experience. 登顶是一次痛苦的考验。|近义词：trying, arduous, grueling, difficult, challenging|反义词：easy, simple, painless|词性: adjective
Etymology: From Medieval Latin *ordalium*, from Old High German *urteili*, from *ur-* "out" + *teil* "part, share." Originally referring to a medieval form of trial by fire or water, to determine guilt or innocence. The meaning later broadened to encompass any severe test or experience.
