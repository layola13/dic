jettison:/ˈdʒɛtɪsən/ | v. n. | 抛弃，丢弃 | To throw or drop (something) from an aircraft or ship |  The crew jettisoned the cargo to lighten the plane.  机组人员抛弃了货物以减轻飞机的重量。|  The captain was forced to jettison the fuel. 船长被迫抛弃燃料。| synonyms: discard, dump, eject | antonyms: retain, keep, preserve | verb
jettison:/ˈdʒɛtɪsən/ | n. | 抛弃，丢弃 | The act of jettisoning something | The jettison of the cargo saved the ship. 抛弃货物拯救了这艘船。| The jettison of the fuel was a necessary precaution.  抛弃燃料是一项必要的预防措施。| synonyms: discarding, dumping | antonyms: retention, keeping | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "getaison" meaning "act of throwing," from "geter" meaning "to throw," ultimately from Latin "jactare," frequentative of "jacere" meaning "to throw."
