enervator:/ˈɛnərveɪtər/| n.|使人衰弱的人或物；泄气的人或物|Something that weakens or drains of energy. |The long, hot summer proved to be a terrible enervator.|近义词：weakening, debilitating, exhausting|反义词:invigorating, energizing, strengthening|noun


enervate:/ˈɛnərveɪt/| vt.|使衰弱；使无力|To deprive of force or strength; weaken. |The humid heat enervated everyone. |近义词：weaken, debilitate, exhaust|反义词:invigorate, energize, strengthen|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *ēnervātus*, past participle of ēnervāre (“to remove the sinews or nerves from, weaken”), from ē- (“out of, from”) + nervus (“sinew, nerve”).
