divaricator:/ˈdaɪvərɪˌkeɪtər/| n. |展肌；扩张器；分叉器|A muscle that draws a part away from the median line of the body or expands an organ.  Something that spreads or forces apart.|The surgeon used a divaricator to hold the incision open.  外科医生用扩张器撑开切口。|Synonyms: retractor, spreader, expander|Antonyms: constrictor, compressor|noun

divaricate:/daɪˈværɪkeɪt/| v. |分叉；岔开；使分开|To spread apart, branch out, or diverge.|The branches of the tree divaricated widely.  树枝岔开得很宽。|Synonyms: diverge, separate, branch, fork|Antonyms: converge, unite, join|verb

divaricate:/daɪˈværɪkeɪt/| adj. |分叉的；岔开的|Spread apart, branched, or divergent.|The plant had divaricate stems.  这种植物的茎是分叉的。|Synonyms: diverging, branching, forking, spreading|Antonyms: converging, uniting, joining|adjective
