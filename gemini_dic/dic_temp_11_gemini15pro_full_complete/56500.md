domesticate: /dəˈmestɪkeɪt/ /dəˈmɛstɪˌkeɪt/
verb
驯养；驯化
To adapt (a plant or animal) to life in intimate association with and to the advantage of humans.
例句：
Humans domesticated the dog thousands of years ago.
人类在几千年前驯化了狗。

The farmers domesticated wild boars and created pigs.
农民驯化了野猪，创造了家猪。


adjective
家养的；驯化的
Adapted to life in intimate association with and to the advantage of humans.
例句：
Domesticated animals are often dependent on humans for food and shelter.
家养动物通常依赖人类提供食物和住所。

The domesticated cat purred contentedly on its owner's lap.
这只家猫心满意足地在主人腿上发出呼噜声。


近义词: tame, cultivate, train
反义词: wild, feral
词性: verb, adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin domesticatus, past participle of domesticare "to tame, domesticate," from Latin domesticus "belonging to the house," from domus "house."
