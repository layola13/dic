lampooners:/læmˈpuːnərz/
noun
讽刺作家；嘲弄者
People who write lampoons; satirists who publicly criticize someone or something by using ridicule, irony, or sarcasm.
The lampooners mercilessly mocked the politician's latest gaffe. 这些讽刺作家无情地嘲笑了这位政客最近的失态。
synonyms: satirists, mockers, caricaturists, parodists
antonyms: admirers, flatterers, praisers, eulogists
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From French "lampon" (drinking song), likely from "lampons" (let's drink), ultimately from "lamper" (to gulp).  Originally, lampoons were satirical poems sung during drinking parties. The meaning later broadened to include any form of harsh satire.
