Fatimite: /ˈfɑːtɪˌmaɪt/ | n. adj. | 法蒂玛王朝的（人）；法蒂玛王朝的 |  Relating to the Fatimid dynasty, a Shi'ite Muslim caliphate that ruled over North Africa, Egypt, and parts of the Middle East from 909 to 1171 CE. | The Fatimite caliphate was known for its patronage of the arts and sciences. 法蒂玛王朝以其对艺术和科学的赞助而闻名。|  The Fatimite army conquered Egypt in 969 CE. 法蒂玛王朝的军队在公元 969 年征服了埃及。 | Synonyms: Fatimid (adj.) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could potentially list opposing dynasties like Abbasid or Umayyad depending on context) | Noun, Adjective
