hermitries:/ˈhɜːmɪtriz/
n.
隐居处；修道院；隐居生活
Places of seclusion or solitude, often associated with religious hermits or monks.  A hermitage can also refer to the state of living as a hermit.
The monk retreated to the hermitries in the mountains for prayer and contemplation.  这位僧侣退到山里的隐居处祈祷和沉思。
The hermit lived a life of prayer and contemplation in the hermitries. 这位隐士在隐居处过着祈祷和沉思的生活。
近义词：monasteries, cloisters, retreats, sanctuaries
反义词：cities, society, urban areas
词性：名词

hermitry:/ˈhɜːmɪtri/
n.
隐居处；修道院；隐居生活
A place of seclusion or solitude, often associated with religious hermits or monks.  It can also refer to the state of living as a hermit.
The hermit built his hermitry deep in the forest. 隐士在森林深处建造了他的隐居处。
She sought hermitry in a remote cabin in the woods. 她在树林里一个偏僻的小屋里寻求隐居。
近义词：monastery, cloister, retreat, sanctuary
反义词：city, society, urban area
词性：名词

词源: From Old French *ermitage*, from Late Latin *eremitagium*, from Latin *eremita* "hermit," from Greek *erēmítēs* "desert dweller," from *érēmos* "desert," related to *erēmaō* "I make desolate."
