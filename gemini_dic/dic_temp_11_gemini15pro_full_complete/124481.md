moulded:/ˈməʊldɪd/ (UK) /ˈmoʊldɪd/ (US)| adj.|模塑的，塑造的|Shaped or formed in a mould; influenced or formed by a particular person or experience. |The toy soldiers were moulded from plastic. 这些玩具士兵是用塑料模塑的。|
The child's character was moulded by his strict upbringing.  这个孩子的性格是由他严格的教养塑造的。|近义词:shaped, formed, cast, fashioned|反义词:unformed, natural|词性:形容词

mold:/moʊld/ (US) /məʊld/ (UK)| n. v. |模具，霉菌；塑造|A hollow container used to give shape to molten or hot liquid material when it cools and hardens; a furry growth of minute fungal hyphae occurring typically in moist warm conditions, especially on food or other organic matter; to shape or form something in a mould; to influence the development or formation of something.|The jelly was set in a heart-shaped mold. 果冻被放在心形模具里凝固。|Bread left uneaten will soon grow mold.  没吃完的面包很快就会发霉。|She molded the clay into a small bowl. 她把黏土塑成一个小碗。|His experiences have molded his character. 他的经历塑造了他的性格。|近义词:(n.)form, matrix, die, cast; (v.)shape, form, fashion, cast|反义词:(n.)(for mold as fungus) sterility, cleanliness; (v.)deform, distort|词性:名词，动词
