bloodbath:/ˈblʌdˌbɑːθ/ /ˈblʌdˌbæθ/| n. |大屠杀，血洗，惨败|A massacre; a scene of carnage. A disastrous loss or defeat.|The stock market experienced a bloodbath yesterday. 股票市场昨天经历了一场大屠杀。|近义词：massacre, slaughter, carnage, rout|反义词:victory, triumph|词性:名词

词源: "blood" + "bath,"  a straightforward compound word first recorded in the early 20th century, literally meaning a bath of blood.  Its figurative use for disastrous defeats or losses became common later.
