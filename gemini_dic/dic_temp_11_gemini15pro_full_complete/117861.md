melodeon:/məˈloʊdiən/ | n. |簧风琴，手风琴 |A small portable reed organ, played by pumping a bellows with one hand while pressing keys or buttons with the other. | He played a lively jig on his melodeon. 他用他的簧风琴演奏了一段轻快的吉格舞曲。|  The travelling musician entertained the crowd with his melodeon.  这位旅行音乐家用他的簧风琴娱乐了人群。| synonyms: accordion, concertina, squeezebox | antonyms: None (lack of a direct opposite for a specific musical instrument) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mélodèon*, from Greek *melos* (song) + *ode* (song).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *wed- (to sing, to speak solemnly).
