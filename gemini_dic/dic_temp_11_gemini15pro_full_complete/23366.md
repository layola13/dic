bracer:/ˈbreɪsər/| n.|支架，牙套，护腕|A device that gives support or firmness; something that braces or strengthens.|She wore a bracer on her wrist after the injury. 她受伤后手腕上戴了一个护腕。|近义词:support, brace, splint|反义词: |noun

bracer:/ˈbreɪsər/| n.|一杯烈酒|An alcoholic drink, especially one taken as a stimulant.|He needed a bracer after the long day.  漫长的一天过后，他需要一杯烈酒提提神。|近义词:stimulant, tonic|反义词: |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *brasere, from Anglo-Norman bracer "to embrace", from Old French brace "arms", from Late Latin bracchia "arms, claws".  The meaning shifted from "something that embraces or holds firmly" to "a support" and later to "a stimulant".
