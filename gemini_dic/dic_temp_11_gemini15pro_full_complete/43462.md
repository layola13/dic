crashers:/ˈkræʃərz/| n.| 闯入者，不速之客|People who attend a party or other event without being invited. | Many wedding crashers were seen enjoying the free food and drinks. 许多婚礼上的不速之客被看到在享用免费的食物和饮料。| The club was full of crashers who hadn't paid to get in. 俱乐部里挤满了没付钱就进来的不速之客。|Synonyms: gatecrashers, intruders, uninvited guests |Antonyms: invitees, guests| noun


Etymology->
From the verb "crash," meaning to enter without permission or invitation.  The "-er" suffix turns the verb into a noun, denoting a person who performs the action.  "Crash" in this sense originated in the early 20th century, likely influenced by the idea of forcefully breaking into something.
