kebaya:/kəˈbaɪə/| n.|卡巴雅服，娘惹服|A long-sleeved, translucent blouse worn by women in Indonesia, Malaysia, and some other Southeast Asian countries, usually made of fine fabrics like silk, lace, or cotton, and often intricately embroidered. It is traditionally paired with a sarong or batik skirt.|The kebaya is often considered a symbol of feminine grace and elegance. 卡巴雅服通常被认为是女性优雅的象征。|Near synonyms: blouse, tunic, traditional dress|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "Western attire")|noun
