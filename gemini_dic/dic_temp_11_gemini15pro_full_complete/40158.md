contend:/kənˈtend/ | v. | 争夺，竞争；声称，主张 | to struggle in opposition; to assert something as a position in an argument | He contended that the judge's decision was wrong. 他声称法官的判决是错误的。| compete, argue, assert | yield, surrender | verb

contested:/kənˈtestɪd/ | adj. | 有争议的 | Disputed or challenged; not agreed upon. | The election results were contested. 选举结果存在争议。 | disputed, challenged, debatable | undisputed, unchallenged, agreed upon | adjective

contented:/kənˈtentɪd/ | adj. | 满足的，满意的 | happy and satisfied | She felt contented and relaxed by the fire. 她感到满足和放松，坐在火炉旁。 | satisfied, happy, pleased | dissatisfied, unhappy, discontented | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "contendere" (to stretch, strive, contend), from "com-" (together) + "tendere" (to stretch).
