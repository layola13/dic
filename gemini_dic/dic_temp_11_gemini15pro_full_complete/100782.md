intrenches:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃɪz/
|v. |使牢固，巩固；根深蒂固；挖壕沟防护|To establish firmly or strengthen, especially a belief, attitude, or habit.  To dig trenches for defense.|The government's policies are now deeply entrenched. 政府的政策现在根深蒂固。|Synonyms: fortify, reinforce, solidify, establish, embed|Antonyms: weaken, undermine, dislodge|verb


intrenched:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃt/
|adj. |牢固的；根深蒂固的| Firmly established and difficult to change.|The company's entrenched position in the market makes it difficult for competitors.  该公司在市场上根深蒂固的地位使其竞争对手难以匹敌。|Synonyms: established, ingrained, rooted, fixed, firm|Antonyms: weak, unstable, precarious|adjective
