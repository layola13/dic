dichroism:/ˈdaɪkroʊɪzəm/| n.|二色性|The property of exhibiting different colors by transmitted light when viewed in two different directions, or, more generally, of selectively absorbing one of the polarized components of an incident beam of light.|The tourmaline crystal shows strong dichroism.|Synonyms: birefringence, double refraction|Antonyms: monochromism|noun

dichroic:/daɪˈkroʊɪk/| adj.|二色的|Exhibiting two colors, especially relating to a crystal that exhibits dichroism.|The dichroic mirror reflected blue light and transmitted red light.|Synonyms: birefringent, doubly refracting|Antonyms: monochromatic|adjective
