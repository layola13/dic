off-shore:/ˌɔːfˈʃɔːr/, /ˌɑːfˈʃɔːr/
adjective
离岸的，近海的
Located or operating away from the shore or coast, but within the maritime boundaries of a country.
Many companies register their businesses in off-shore tax havens. 许多公司在离岸避税天堂注册公司。
The off-shore wind farm is expected to generate a significant amount of electricity. 近海风电场预计将产生大量电力。
近义词：coastal, maritime, seaward
反义词：onshore, inland
词性：adjective

off-shore:/ˌɔːfˈʃɔːr/, /ˌɑːfˈʃɔːr/
adverb
离岸地，近海地
Away from the shore or coast, but within the maritime boundaries of a country.
The oil rig was located off-shore. 石油钻井平台位于近海。
They decided to invest off-shore to reduce taxes. 他们决定进行离岸投资以减少税收。
近义词：seaward
反义词：onshore, inland
词性：adverb

off-shore:/ˌɔːfˈʃɔːr/, /ˌɑːfˈʃɔːr/
noun
近海区域
The area of sea relatively close to the shore.
The off-shore is rich in marine life. 近海区域海洋生物丰富。
Pollution in the off-shore can impact coastal ecosystems. 近海污染会影响沿海生态系统。
近义词：coastal waters
反义词：inland
词性：noun


词源: off + shore.  "Off" indicating separation or distance, and "shore" referring to the land bordering a body of water.
