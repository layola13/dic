moxa:/ˈmɒksə/ | n. | 艾, 艾绒; 艾灸 | A soft woolly mass prepared from the leaves of mugwort (Artemisia vulgaris), burned on the skin as a counterirritant in traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine. | Example 1: The practitioner applied moxa to specific acupuncture points on the patient's back.  中医师在病人背部的特定穴位上施灸。

Example 2: Moxa is believed to stimulate blood flow and promote healing. 艾被认为可以促进血液循环和愈合。 | Synonym: mugwort, artemisia | Antonym: (No direct antonym, could consider conventional medicine as a contrasting concept) | noun
