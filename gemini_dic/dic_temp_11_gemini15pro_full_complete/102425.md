jackarooing:/ˌdʒækəˈruːɪŋ/ | n. | 打零工;  在澳大利亚内陆地区做临时工,尤指在牧场做工 | The act of working as a jackaroo, typically on a sheep or cattle station in outback Australia.  It involves gaining practical experience in farm work. |  He spent a year jackarooing in the outback. 他在澳大利亚内陆地区做了一年的临时工。 | 近义词: farm work, ranching, working holiday | 反义词: unemployment, idleness | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From jackaroo + -ing. Jackaroo itself is of uncertain origin, possibly related to "jack" (a general term for a man) and the name of a bushranger called "Kangaroo Jack".  It emerged in the mid-19th century.
