collocated:/ˈkɒləkeɪtɪd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləkeɪtɪd/ (US)| adj.|共址的，同位的，并置的|Placed or located together; existing in the same place or position. |例句：The server and the database are collocated for better performance. 服务器和数据库共址以获得更好的性能。|近义词：adjacent, juxtaposed, alongside, together, combined|反义词：separate, dispersed, remote, distant|词性:形容词

collocate:/ˈkɒləkeɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləkeɪt/ (US)| v.|搭配，并置|Place side by side or in a particular relation.|例句：The artist collocated contrasting colors to create a striking effect. 艺术家并置对比色来创造一种引人注目的效果。|近义词：juxtapose, combine, arrange, position|反义词：separate, disperse|词性:动词

collocation:/ˌkɒləˈkeɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌkɑːləˈkeɪʃn/ (US)| n.|搭配，组合，并置|The habitual juxtaposition of a particular word with another word or words with a frequency greater than chance.|例句："Heavy rain" is a common collocation in English. “Heavy rain”在英语中是一个常见的搭配。|近义词：combination, juxtaposition, pairing, grouping|反义词：separation, division|词性:名词
