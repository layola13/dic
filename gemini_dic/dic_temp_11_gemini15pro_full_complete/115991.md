marshaler:/ˈmɑːrʃələr/ (US) /ˈmɑːʃələr/ (UK)| n.|编组程序，调度员，整理者|A marshaler is a person or a piece of software that arranges or manages things in a specific order, especially data or code in computer science.  It can also refer to someone who directs and guides the movement of aircraft, vehicles, or people.|The marshaler directed the plane to the gate.  调度员将飞机引导至登机口。|The data marshaler converts the data into a format suitable for transmission. 数据编组程序将数据转换为适合传输的格式。|synonyms: organizer, arranger, director, coordinator|antonyms: disorganizer|noun


marshaler:/ˈmɑːrʃələr/ (US) /ˈmɑːʃələr/ (UK)| v.|整理，安排，编组|To marshal means to arrange or assemble something in a specific order, especially troops or ideas. In computer science, it refers to the process of transforming the memory representation of an object into a data format suitable for storage or transmission, and vice versa.|He marshaled his arguments carefully before the debate. 在辩论前，他仔细地整理了自己的论点。|The program marshals the data before sending it over the network. 程序在通过网络发送数据之前对其进行编组。|synonyms: organize, arrange, assemble, gather|antonyms: disperse, scatter|verb
