diesis:/ˈdaɪɪsɪs/ /ˈdiːsɪs/| n.|升音号，半音|A sign (♯) used in musical notation to indicate that a note is to be raised by a semitone.|The diesis raises the pitch of a note by a half step.|近义词：sharp|反义词：flat|词性:noun
Etymology->
From Late Latin *diesis, from Ancient Greek δίεσις (díesis, “a letting through, passing through; a quarter tone”), from διίημι (diíēmi, “I send through, let go, dismiss”), from διά (diá, “through”) + ἵημι (híēmi, “I send, throw”). Doublet of diasyrm.
