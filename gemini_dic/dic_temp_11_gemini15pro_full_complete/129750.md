non-event:/ˌnɒn ɪˈvent/ | n. | 无聊的事情；平淡无奇的事情；没发生什么大事 | An occasion that was expected to be significant or exciting but which turns out to be disappointing or uneventful. | The party was a complete non-event.  这个派对真是无聊透顶。

The much-hyped product launch turned out to be a non-event. 大肆宣传的产品发布结果却平淡无奇。| 近义词: disappointment, anticlimax, flop, dud | 反义词: success, triumph, hit | 词性: 名词

Etymology: non- + event.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "event" refers to something that happens.  Therefore, a "non-event" is literally something that did *not* happen in a meaningful or impactful way, even if it technically took place.
