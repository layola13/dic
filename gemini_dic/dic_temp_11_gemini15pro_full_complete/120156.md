ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ġemynd (attested only in compounds like cearigemynd "anxiety, care"), from Proto-Germanic *ga-mundiz (source also of Old Frisian gemend "memory", Old Saxon gimund "memory, remembrance", Old High German gimunt "memory, remembrance", German Gemüt "mind, feeling, disposition", Gothic gamunds "remembrance"), from Proto-Indo-European *mn̥-tis (source also of Sanskrit matiḥ "thought, opinion, devotion", Greek mémona "I remember", Latin mēns "mind", mentis (genitive), Old Church Slavonic pamęti "memory").
