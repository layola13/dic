offset:/ˈɒfset/ (UK) /ˈɔːfset/ (US)| n. v. adj.|抵消，补偿，偏置，支管|a consideration or amount that diminishes or balances the effect of an opposite one, something that counterbalances or compensates for something else; to counteract something by having an opposing force or effect |例句：The company's losses were offset by gains in other areas. 公司在其他领域的收益抵消了亏损。|近义词：counterbalance, compensate, neutralize|反义词：augment, enhance, exacerbate|词性:noun, verb, adjective

offset:/ˈɒfset/ (UK) /ˈɔːfset/ (US)| n. v. adj.|偏移量，位移，弯管，旁支|the amount or distance by which something is out of line or position |例句：The printing is offset slightly to the left. 印刷略微偏左。|近义词：displacement, shift, deviation|反义词：alignment, centering|词性:noun, verb, adjective

词源: From the verb phrase "set off," meaning to place against as a counterbalance.  First recorded in the late 18th century.
