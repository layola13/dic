chalice: /ˈtʃælɪs/ (US) /ˈtʃælɪs/ (UK)| n.|圣杯，酒杯|A large cup or goblet, often used for ceremonial purposes, especially in religious services. It is often made of precious metal and highly decorated. |The priest raised the chalice and offered a blessing.  牧师举起圣杯并给予祝福。

The museum displayed a beautiful chalice from the 12th century.  博物馆展出了一个12世纪的精美圣杯。|近义词：goblet, cup, vessel|反义词: None|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *calice*, from Latin *calix* meaning "cup, goblet". Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kel-*, meaning "to hide, cover".
