cnns:/ˌsiːˌɛnˈɛns/| n.| 卷积神经网络 (Convolutional Neural Networks)| Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are a specialized type of artificial neural network that uses a mathematical operation called convolution in place of general matrix multiplication in at least one of its layers. CNNs are designed to process pixel data and are used in image recognition and processing, natural language processing, and other areas.|  CNNs are widely used in image classification tasks. 卷积神经网络广泛用于图像分类任务。| synonyms: ConvNets| antonyms: N/A| abbreviation
