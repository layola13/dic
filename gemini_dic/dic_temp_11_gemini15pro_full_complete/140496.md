old-fashioned:/ˌoʊldˈfæʃənd/ | adj. |老式的，过时的，守旧的 | characteristic of or adhering to customs or ideas of the past, especially in a way regarded as no longer fashionable or appropriate |  She wore an old-fashioned dress. 她穿着一件老式的连衣裙。|  He has some old-fashioned ideas about women's roles. 他对女性的角色有一些过时的想法。 | 近义词: outdated, antiquated, archaic, vintage, retro | 反义词: modern, contemporary, fashionable, trendy | 词性:形容词

Etymology:
From old + fashioned, past participle of fashion (“to make, shape, mold”).  The word "fashion" itself comes from Old French "façon" meaning "manner, shape, form," ultimately from Latin "factio" meaning "a making, a doing."
