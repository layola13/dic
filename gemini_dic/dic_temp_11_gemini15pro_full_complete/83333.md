gypsums:/ˈdʒɪpsəmz/| n. |石膏（复数）|A soft white or gray mineral consisting of hydrated calcium sulfate.  It is used in making plaster of Paris and fertilizers, and occurs in various forms. |The gypsums were mined and used in the construction industry.|Synonym: plaster, sulfate|Antonym: N/A (Minerals typically don't have direct antonyms)|noun
