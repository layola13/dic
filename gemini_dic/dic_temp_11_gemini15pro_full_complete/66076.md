excandescence:/ˌɛkskænˈdɛsəns/| n.|白热化；炽热；光辉|the state of being white or glowing with heat; brilliance|The excandescence of the molten lava was mesmerizing. 熔岩的白热化状态令人着迷。|近义词：glow, brilliance, radiance, luminosity|反义词：dullness, darkness|名词

excandescent:/ˌɛkskænˈdɛsənt/| adj.|白热的；炽热的；光辉的|emitting light as a result of being heated; passionate|The excandescent filament in the light bulb glowed brightly. 灯泡里炽热的灯丝发出明亮的光芒。|近义词：glowing, brilliant, radiant, luminous|反义词：dull, dark|形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *excandescens, present participle of excandescere (“to grow white-hot”), from ex- (“out, thoroughly”) + candescere (“to glow, to become white”), from candēre (“to shine, to glow”), from Proto-Italic *kandēō, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱend- (“to shine, to glow”).
