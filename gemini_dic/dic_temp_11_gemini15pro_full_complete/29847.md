causationist:/kɔːˈzeɪʃənɪst/
n.|因果论者，原因论者|A philosopher or scientist who believes in causation, especially as a fundamental principle.|The causationist argued that every event has a specific cause.|近义词:determinist|反义词:indeterminist|词性:名词
Etymology: From "causation" + "-ist".  "Causation" comes from Late Latin "causationem" (nominative causatio), noun of action from past-participle stem of causare "to plead a cause, allege," from causa "cause, reason, motive."
