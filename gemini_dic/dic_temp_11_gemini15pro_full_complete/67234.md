expropriator:/ɪkˌsproʊpriˈeɪtər/ | n. |  expropriator | A person or entity that expropriates something, especially property. | The government acted as the expropriator, seizing land for the new highway. 政府充当征用者，为修建新高速公路征用了土地。| Example: The expropriator offered little compensation for the seized land.  征用者对被征用的土地提供的补偿很少。 | Synonyms: confiscator, seizer, appropriator | Antonyms:  giver, donor, restorer | noun

expropriate:/ɪkˈsproʊprieɪt/ | v. | 征用，没收 | To take property from its owner for public use or benefit, often with little or no compensation.  | The government expropriated the land to build a new airport. 政府征用了这块土地来建造一个新机场。 | Example:  The regime expropriated the assets of several foreign companies. 该政权没收了几家外国公司的资产。 | Synonyms: seize, confiscate, commandeer | Antonyms: return, restore, give | verb
