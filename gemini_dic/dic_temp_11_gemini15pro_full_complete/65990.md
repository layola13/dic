exalters:/ɪɡˈzɔːltərz/
| n.| 赞美者，颂扬者 | People who exalt or praise someone or something highly are exalters. | The king's exalters filled the court, singing his praises. 国王的赞美者挤满了宫廷，歌颂着他的功绩。 |  The poet's work attracted many exalters. 诗人的作品吸引了很多赞美者。 | admirers, praisers, lauders | critics, detractors | noun

exalts:/ɪɡˈzɔːlts/
| vt.| 提升，赞扬，使高贵 | To exalt someone or something means to raise them to a higher rank or position, or to praise them highly. | The new leader was exalted by his followers. 新领导人受到了追随者的拥戴。| The poem exalts the beauty of nature. 这首诗歌颂了大自然的美丽。| praise, glorify, elevate |  degrade,  humiliate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *exalten*, from Old French *exalter*, from Latin *exaltare* ("to raise high, elevate"), from *ex-* ("out, up") + *altus* ("high").
