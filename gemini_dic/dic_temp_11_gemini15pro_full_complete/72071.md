flitting:/ˈflɪtɪŋ/ /ˈflɪdɪŋ/
verb
轻快地飞来飞去，掠过；一闪而过
moving swiftly and lightly
Butterflies were flitting from flower to flower. 蝴蝶在花丛中飞来飞去。

adjective
一闪而过的；短暂的
lasting for a short time
a flitting thought  一闪而过的念头

Etymology:
From Middle English flitten, from Old English flitan (“to remove, migrate, depart”), from Proto-Germanic *flitan (“to strive, contend, dispute, quarrel”). Cognate with Scots flit (“to move house, change one's residence”), West Frisian flitte (“to move, flit”), Dutch vlieden (“to flee”), German flitzen (“to flit, dart, whiz”).

Synonyms:
(v.) dart, flutter, flash, skim, dance
(adj.) fleeting, transient, ephemeral, momentary

Antonyms:
(v.) stay, remain, linger
(adj.) permanent, lasting, enduring

Word Type:
Verb, Adjective
