freewheeled:/ˈfriːwiːld/| adj.|随心所欲的，无拘无束的；自由放任的|acting in a relaxed and informal way, without feeling limited or constrained|He has a freewheeling style of management. 他有一种自由放任的管理风格。|Synonyms: uninhibited, relaxed, easygoing, casual, informal|Antonyms: restrained, inhibited, formal, rigid, controlled|adjective

freewheel:/ˈfriːwiːl/| v.|自由滑行；轻松应对；自由发展|to move forward easily without effort or control, often used figuratively to describe a relaxed or uninhibited approach to something|The company freewheeled through the early years, enjoying rapid growth. 公司在早期轻松发展，享受着快速增长。|Synonyms: coast, glide, drift, sail|Antonyms: struggle, toil, labor, strive|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From free + wheel.  The literal meaning refers to a bicycle mechanism that allows the rear wheel to continue turning even when the pedals are not being moved. This concept was then extended figuratively to describe actions or behaviors that are similarly unrestrained and effortless.
