overscrupulous: /ˌoʊvərˈskruːpjələs/ | adj. | 过分谨慎的，过于拘泥的 | Excessively conscientious or careful; tending to worry about small details or behaving in an excessively moral way.  Derived from "over" + "scrupulous" (from Latin "scrupulus," a small sharp stone, hence a cause of uneasiness).  It implies an excessive concern with minor details of conscience or morality, often to the point of being impractical or counterproductive. | He was so overscrupulous in his work that he never finished anything on time. 他工作过于谨慎，以至于什么都做不完。| She's overscrupulous about cleanliness. 她对清洁过于拘泥。 | 近义词: meticulous, conscientious, fastidious, punctilious | 反义词: careless, negligent, remiss, lax | 词性: adjective
