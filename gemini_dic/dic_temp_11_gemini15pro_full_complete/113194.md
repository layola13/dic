luridnesses:/ˈlʊrɪdnɪsɪz/
noun
耸人听闻；骇人听闻；可怕
The quality or state of being lurid; sensational or shocking character; gruesome or excessively vivid details.
The luridnesses of the crime scene shocked even the seasoned detectives. 犯罪现场的骇人听闻即使是经验丰富的侦探也感到震惊。
Near Synonyms: ghastliness, gruesomeness, hideousness, macabre, sensationalism
Antonyms:  pleasantness, attractiveness, beauty, normalcy


ETYMOLOGY->
From lurid + -ness.  Lurid itself comes from the Latin "luridus," meaning pale yellow, ghastly, or wan. Over time, the meaning shifted to encompass the sense of something shocking or sensational, likely due to the association of the pale or ghastly color with death and decay.
