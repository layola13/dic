bibles: /ˈbaɪbəlz/ | n. | 圣经（复数） | The holy book of Christianity, comprising the Old and New Testaments. |  He studies the Bibles in different languages. 他学习不同语言版本的圣经。| Synonym: scriptures, Holy Writ | Antonym: None | Noun

Etymology: From Middle English *biblis*, from Old French *bibles*, plural of *bible*, from Late Latin *biblia*, from Greek *ta biblia* "the books," plural of *biblion* "book," diminutive of *byblos* "papyrus," from *Byblos*, a Phoenician city from which papyrus was exported.
