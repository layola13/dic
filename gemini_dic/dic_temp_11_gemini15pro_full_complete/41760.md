corrupters:/kəˈrʌptərz/| n. |腐败者，堕落者，败坏者|People who corrupt; those who engage in corrupt practices, destroying the integrity of something or someone.  They may bribe officials, manipulate systems, or spread harmful ideologies.|The corrupters were brought to justice after years of investigation. 这些腐败分子经过多年的调查后被绳之以法。|Synonyms: debauchers, perverters, suborners|Antonyms: reformers, purifiers|noun


corrupt:/kəˈrʌpt/| v. adj.|腐败的，堕落的；败坏，腐蚀| (v.) To cause to become dishonest, immoral, or impure; to spoil. (adj.) Dishonest, immoral, or impure.|The corrupt official accepted bribes.  这位腐败的官员接受贿赂。  He was accused of corrupting the youth. 他被指控败坏青年。|Synonyms: (v.) contaminate, defile, pervert; (adj.) dishonest, unscrupulous|Antonyms: (v.) purify, cleanse; (adj.) honest, upright|verb, adjective
