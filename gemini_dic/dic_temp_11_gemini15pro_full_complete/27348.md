camblet:/ˈkæmlɪt/| n.|坎布里奇呢，一种轻质羊毛织物或类似的织物|A fine, smooth, and glossy fabric, typically woven from wool or silk and cotton, historically used for clothing and upholstery.  It was often mixed with other fibers like silk or linen to create different textures and sheens.|The queen wore a gown of shimmering camblet.| 近义词:fabric, cloth, textile, material|反义词: None (lack of a specific antonym for a type of fabric)|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *camelin, ultimately from  Medieval Latin *camelīnus (“made of camel's hair”),  from Late Latin camelus (“camel”). While initially thought to be made of camel hair, it was more commonly woven from wool, silk, and later, cotton and linen blends.
