acanthodians: /ˌækænˈθəʊdiənz/ | n. |棘鱼类| An extinct group of primitive jawed fishes, characterized by spines in front of their fins. |  Acanthodians flourished during the Silurian and Devonian periods. 棘鱼类在志留纪和泥盆纪时期繁盛。| synonyms: spiny sharks (informal), gnathostomes (broader term) | antonyms: None (as they are an extinct group, there isn't a direct opposite) | noun
