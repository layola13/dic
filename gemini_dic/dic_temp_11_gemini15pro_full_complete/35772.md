cockade:/kɒˈkeɪd/ | /kɑːˈkeɪd/ | n. |帽章；徽章；花结| A knot of ribbons, or other circular or oval-shaped symbol of office or party, worn on the hat. | The soldier wore a cockade on his hat to show his regiment.  士兵的帽子上戴着帽章以表明他的所属团。|  emblem, badge, rosette |  None | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cocarde*, from *coq* "cock," because early cockades resembled a cock's comb.  源自法语 *cocarde*，来自 *coq* “公鸡”，因为早期的帽章类似于公鸡的鸡冠。
