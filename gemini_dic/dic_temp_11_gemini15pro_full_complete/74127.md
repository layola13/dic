Etymology->
From Middle English *foulness*, equivalent to *foul* + *-ness*. *foul* comes from Old English *fūl*, from Proto-Germanic *fūlaz* (“rotten, putrid, foul”), from Proto-Indo-European *puH- (“to rot, stink”).  The suffix *-ness* is a common Old English suffix used to form nouns from adjectives, denoting a state, quality, or condition.
