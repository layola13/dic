callot:/ˈkælət/ | n. | 招募；召集（尤指服兵役）；应召入伍者 | A call-up, especially for military service; a person called up for military service. |  The general issued a callot for all able-bodied men. 将军发出号召，所有身体健全的男子都要应召入伍。 | synonyms: conscription, draft, levy, muster | antonyms: discharge, release, demobilization | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *callot*, diminutive of *calle*, "skull, head" (from Latin *calva*, "bald head").  The sense shift from "head" to "person" and then to "conscript" likely occurred because new recruits were often counted or "headed."
