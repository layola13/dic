brood:/bruːd/
n.|一窝; 孵蛋; (雏鸡等)一窝; 一群(孩子); 沉思|A family of young animals, especially birds, that are produced at the same time; a number of things of the same kind coming at the same time; a person's children; a group of related things; to think for a long time about something that makes you worried or angry.|The hen was sitting on a brood of eggs. 母鸡正坐在一窝蛋上。|synonyms: clutch, litter, offspring, family|antonyms: individual, single|noun

brood:/bruːd/
v.|孵蛋; 沉思; 念念不忘; 郁郁不乐|To think for a long time about something that makes you worried or angry; (of a bird) to sit on eggs in order to keep them warm until they hatch.|She brooded over the insult for days. 她对那次侮辱耿耿于怀了好几天。|synonyms: ponder, dwell on, mull over, fret|antonyms: ignore, forget|verb
