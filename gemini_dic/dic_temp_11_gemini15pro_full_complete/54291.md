disgustedness:/dɪsˈɡʌstɪdnɪs/| n. |厌恶，憎恶|The state of feeling disgust; extreme dislike or aversion.|The smell of the rotten food filled her with disgustedness.|近义词：revulsion, repugnance, nausea, abhorrence, loathing|反义词：attraction, liking, fondness, pleasure|词性:名词

词源: From "disgust" + "-ed" (forming the past participle and adjective) + "-ness" (forming nouns expressing a state or quality).  "Disgust" comes from the Old French "desgouster" meaning "to distaste," ultimately from the Latin "gustus" meaning "taste."
