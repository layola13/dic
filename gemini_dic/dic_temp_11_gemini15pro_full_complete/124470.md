mouflon:/ˈmuːflɒn/ | n. | 野绵羊，摩弗仑羊 | A wild sheep (Ovis musimon or Ovis orientalis) native to Corsica and Sardinia, the ancestor of domestic sheep. |  The mouflon roamed the rocky slopes. 野绵羊在岩石坡上漫步。| Synonym: wild sheep, argali | Antonym: domestic sheep | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French mouflon, from Italian muflone, perhaps from Medieval Latin musmon, from Latin musmo, from Ancient Greek μούσμων (moúsmon).
