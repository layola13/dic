arabber's:/ˈærəbərz/
noun
流动小贩的（东西）; 街头小贩的（东西）
belonging to or associated with an arabber (a street vendor, especially of fruits and vegetables, who typically uses a horse-drawn cart).  Primarily used in the Baltimore, Maryland area.
The arabber's cart was filled with fresh produce. 流动小贩的推车里装满了新鲜农产品。
The arabber's call echoed through the streets. 街头小贩的叫卖声在街道上回荡。
synonyms: street vendor's, peddler's, hawker's
antonyms: shopkeeper's, store's, retailer's
词性: noun (所有格)

词源：
From arabber + -'s (possessive suffix).  "Arabber" itself is of uncertain origin, possibly from a mispronunciation of "Arab" or a word related to "barrow," a handbarrow or cart.  The term is primarily associated with Baltimore.
