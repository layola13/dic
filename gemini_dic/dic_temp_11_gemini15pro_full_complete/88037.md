highbrowish:/ˌhaɪˈbraʊɪʃ/| adj.|略高雅的，有点附庸雅致的，假装有学问的|Suggesting intellectual pretension or snobbery.|She made some highbrowish remarks about the art exhibit, which made her sound pretentious.|近义词：pretentious, intellectual, snobbish, elitist|反义词：lowbrow, unpretentious, down-to-earth|词性:形容词
Etymology:  From "highbrow" + "-ish." "Highbrow" originated in the late 19th century, referring literally to a high forehead, supposedly a sign of intelligence, and later figuratively to intellectual or cultural interests.  The suffix "-ish" adds the sense of "somewhat" or "slightly."
