cirriped:/ˈsɪrɪped/| n. | 藤壶；蔓足类动物 |A marine crustacean with an external shell, which attaches itself permanently to a variety of surfaces, including rocks, ships' hulls, and whales.  They are typically hermaphroditic and filter-feed using their feathery appendages.| Barnacles are common cirripeds found on rocks along the coast. 藤壶是海岸边岩石上常见的蔓足类动物。|Synonym: barnacle| Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps mobile marine creature) | noun
