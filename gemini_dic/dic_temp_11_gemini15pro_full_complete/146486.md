overfussy:/ˌoʊvərˈfʌsi/| adj.|过于讲究的，过分挑剔的|Too concerned with unimportant details and prone to excessive worry or meticulousness.|例句：He's a bit overfussy about his clothes. 他对自己的穿着有点过于讲究。

例句：She's an overfussy eater and rarely finishes a meal. 她吃饭很挑剔，很少能吃完一顿饭。|近义词：fastidious, meticulous, picky, finicky|反义词：easygoing, tolerant, undemanding|词性:形容词


Etymology->
From over- + fussy.  "Fussy" ultimately comes from the obsolete verb "fust" meaning to become moldy or stale, which developed a sense of being ill-tempered or irritable.  The prefix "over-" intensifies the meaning, implying an excessive degree of fussiness.
