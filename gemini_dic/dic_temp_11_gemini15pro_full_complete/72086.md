floatier:/ˈfləʊ.ti.ər/ (US) /ˈfloʊ.ti.ər/ (UK)| adj.|更轻浮的，更飘浮的|Comparative form of floaty: lighter and more buoyant; tending to drift on the surface of a liquid or in the air.|The new dress has a floatier skirt than the old one. 这条新裙子的裙摆比旧裙子更飘逸。|近义词：lighter, airier, more buoyant|反义词：heavier, denser|词性:形容词

floaty:/ˈfləʊ.ti/ (US) /ˈfloʊ.ti/ (UK)| adj.|轻浮的，飘浮的|light and buoyant; tending to drift on the surface of a liquid or in the air.|She wore a floaty dress. 她穿了一件飘逸的连衣裙。|近义词：light, airy, buoyant|反义词：heavy, dense|词性:形容词

float:/floʊt/ (US) /fləʊt/ (UK)| v. n.|漂浮，浮动，提出，上市|1.(v.) rest or move on or near the surface of a liquid without sinking. 2. (v.) suggest an idea or plan. 3. (n.) a thing that floats on a liquid.|The boat floated on the lake. 船漂浮在湖面上。|近义词：drift, waft|反义词：sink|词性:动词，名词

词源: From Middle English *floten, from Old English flotian, from Proto-Germanic *flotōjaną (“to float”), from Proto-Indo-European *plewd- (“to flow, float, swim”). Cognate with Scots flet (“to float”), West Frisian floeie (“to flow, to float”), Dutch vlotten (“to raft”), German flößen (“to float, to raft”), Swedish flyta (“to float”). Related to flow.
