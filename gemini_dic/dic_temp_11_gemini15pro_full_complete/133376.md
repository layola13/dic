nonextrication:/ˌnɒnˌɛkstrɪˈkeɪʃn/
n.|无法解脱，无法摆脱|The state of being unable to extricate oneself or something else from a difficult situation or position. |The firefighters worked tirelessly, but the nonextrication of the victims trapped beneath the rubble seemed inevitable.| 近义词: entrapment, entanglement, imprisonment | 反义词: liberation, release, freedom | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
nonextrication is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the word "extrication" (from Latin "extricare," meaning "to disentangle").  "Ex-" means "out" and "tricae" refers to "perplexities" or "hindrances."
