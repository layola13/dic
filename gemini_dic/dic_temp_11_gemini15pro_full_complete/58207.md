drudging:/ˈdrʌdʒɪŋ/
drudge:/drʌdʒ/
| n. |苦工；单调乏味的工作| a person who has to do hard, menial, or dull work |  He felt like a drudge, doing the same boring tasks every day. | 他感觉自己像个苦工，每天做着同样无聊的工作。 |  menial worker, laborer, toiler |  skilled worker, professional | noun
| v. |做苦工；辛勤工作| to work hard and steadily at boring or unpleasant tasks |  She drudged away at her homework all evening. | 她整个晚上都在辛勤地做作业。 | toil, slave, grind | relax, idle | verb
drudging:| adj. |单调乏味的；辛苦的| involving or characterized by hard, menial, or dull work |  He found the drudging work exhausting. | 他觉得这单调乏味的工作令人筋疲力尽。 | tedious, monotonous, laborious | stimulating, exciting, enjoyable | adjective
