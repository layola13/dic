cajoling:/kəˈdʒoʊlɪŋ/ | adj.  v. | 劝诱的；哄骗的 | Persuading someone to do something by sustained coaxing or flattery. |  He cajoled his parents into letting him go to the concert. 他劝诱他的父母让他去听音乐会。 | Synonyms: coaxing, persuading, wheedling, enticing | Antonyms: dissuading, discouraging, deterring | verb/adjective

cajole:/kəˈdʒoʊl/ | v. | 劝诱；哄骗 | To persuade someone to do something by sustained coaxing or flattery. | She cajoled him into buying her a new dress. 她哄骗他给她买了一件新衣服。| Synonyms: coax, persuade, wheedle, entice | Antonyms: dissuade, discourage, deter | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cajoler*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old French *gaioler* "to cage," implying the idea of entrapping someone with sweet words.
