at one's mercy:/æt wʌnz ˈmɜːrsi/ |phrase|受…支配；任…摆布；在…的掌握中|Being at someone's mercy means being completely under their control and dependent on them, often in a vulnerable or dangerous situation.|The small boat was at the mercy of the storm. 小船在暴风雨中失去控制。|in control of oneself, independent|phrase


Etymology: The phrase originates from the concept of "mercy" meaning compassion or forgiveness.  Being "at someone's mercy" implies being in a position where one's well-being depends entirely on the other person's choice to show compassion or not.
