cathedratic:/ˌkæθəˈdrætɪk/| adj.|教授的，权威的，独断的|Resembling or characteristic of a professor, especially in being pedantic or dogmatic.|He delivered his pronouncements with a cathedratic air. 他以一种教授式的口吻发表了自己的声明。|近义词：professorial, magisterial, dogmatic, authoritative|反义词：humble, modest, unassuming|词性:形容词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cathedraticus*, from Greek *kathedratikos*, from *kathedra* (chair, professorial chair) + *-ikos* (suffix forming adjectives).  The Greek *kathedra* literally means "seat" or "sitting place".
