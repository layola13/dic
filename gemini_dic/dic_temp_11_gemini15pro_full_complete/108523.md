laywoman:/ˈleɪˌwʊmən/| n.|外行女子；俗人；|A woman who is not trained or ordained as a member of the clergy; a woman who does not have expert knowledge of a particular subject.|例句：She explained the complex procedure in terms a layperson could understand. 她用外行人能理解的语言解释了这个复杂的过程。|例句：As a layperson, I can't comment on the technical aspects of the project. 作为外行，我无法评论该项目的专业技术。|近义词：nonprofessional, amateur, non-expert|反义词:professional, expert|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From lay + woman.  "Lay" in this context comes from Old French *lai (meaning "secular") ultimately from Latin *laicus (meaning "of the people"), from Greek *laikos (meaning "of the people").
