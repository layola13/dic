countersigning:/ˈkaʊntərˌsaɪnɪŋ/
n.|副签；连署；会签|The act of signing a document already signed by another person, usually to confirm its authenticity or to assume joint responsibility.|例句：Countersigning of the cheque is required by two authorized signatures. 这张支票需要两个授权签字会签。|近义词：co-signing, endorsing|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps refusal to sign)
v.|副签；连署；会签|To sign a document already signed by another person, usually to confirm its authenticity or to assume joint responsibility.|例句：The manager countersigned the employee's travel request. 经理在员工的出差申请上副签了。|近义词：co-sign, endorse|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps refusing to sign)
