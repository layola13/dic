constructible:/kənˈstrʌktəbl/| adj.|可构造的，可建造的|Able to be constructed or built; capable of being formed or devised.|例句：The architect confirmed that the skyscraper was constructible.  建筑师确认摩天大楼是可建造的。

例句：A constructible polygon can be drawn using only a compass and straightedge.  一个可构造的多边形可以用圆规和直尺绘制。|近义词：buildable, makeable, feasible|反义词:unbuildable, impossible|词性:形容词
construct:/kənˈstrʌkt/| v.|建造，构造|To build or create something.|例句：They plan to construct a new bridge across the river.  他们计划在河上建造一座新桥。


例句：The students were asked to construct a sentence using the new vocabulary.  学生们被要求用新词汇造句。|近义词：build, create, make|反义词:demolish, destroy|词性:动词
construction:/kənˈstrʌkʃn/| n.|建造，建筑物，结构|The act or process of building or creating something.|例句：The construction of the new highway caused significant traffic delays. 新高速公路的建设造成了严重的交通延误。


例句：The building is under construction. 这栋楼正在建设中。|近义词：building, erection, fabrication|反义词:destruction, demolition|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *constructibilis, from Latin constructus (past participle of construere "to build"), from com- "together" + struere "to pile, build."
