convictism: /kənˈvɪktɪzəm/
n.
罪犯制度; 囚犯制度; 流放犯制度
A system of penal transportation, especially the transportation of convicted criminals from Britain to Australia.
The history of Australia is inextricably linked to convictism. 澳大利亚的历史与罪犯制度密不可分。
Transportation was a key part of convictism in 19th-century Britain.  运输是19世纪英国罪犯制度的关键部分。
penal colony, transportation, penal servitude
exile, freedom


ETYMOLOGY->
From convict + -ism.
