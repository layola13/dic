dress: /drɛs/ (US) /dres/ (UK) | v. |  处理; 包扎（伤口）|  To treat or apply a dressing to a wound. | The nurse dressed the wound carefully. 护士仔细地包扎了伤口。| 近义词：bandage, treat | 反义词： | 词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *dresser "to arrange, straighten, prepare," from Vulgar Latin *dīrēctiāre, from Latin dīrēctus "straight, direct." The sense of "to clothe" developed from the idea of "arranging" one's clothing.
