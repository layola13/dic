focussed:/ˈfoʊkəst/ (US) /ˈfəʊkəst/ (UK)| adj.| 集中的，专注的 |  Giving a lot of attention to one particular thing |  She remained focussed on her work despite the distractions. 她尽管受到干扰，仍然专注于工作。 |  The company has a focussed strategy for growth.  这家公司有一个专注的增长战略。 | concentrated, attentive, dedicated | distracted, unfocused, scattered | adjective

focussed:/ˈfoʊkəst/ (US) /ˈfəʊkəst/ (UK)| v.| (focus的过去式和过去分词，主要用于英式英语) 集中(注意力)，聚焦 | Past tense and past participle of "focus," primarily used in British English, meaning to concentrate or direct attention towards something |  He focussed his attention on the problem at hand. 他把注意力集中在眼前的问题上。 | The photographer focussed the camera on the bird. 摄影师把相机对焦在那只鸟上。 | concentrated, fixed, directed | diffused, scattered, unfocussed | verb


Etymology:
From the Latin "focus" meaning "hearth, fireplace."  The meaning shifted to the point where light rays converge, then to a center of activity or interest.
