fossil:/ˈfɒsəl/ (UK) /ˈfɑːsl/ (US)| n. adj.| 化石；老顽固 |The remains or impression of a prehistoric plant or animal embedded in rock and preserved in petrified form. (As an adjective) outdated; old-fashioned.|

例句：
The museum has a large collection of dinosaur fossils.
博物馆收藏了大量的恐龙化石。

She found a small fossil while walking on the beach.
她在海滩上散步时发现了一块小化石。

He has some fossil ideas about women's role in society.
他对女性在社会中的角色抱有一些过时的想法。

The company's fossil management needs to adapt to the changing times.
公司老旧的管理层需要适应时代的变化。


近义词：relic, remnant, petrified, antiquated (adj.)|
反义词：modern, contemporary (adj.)|
词性：noun, adjective|
