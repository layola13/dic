marsupium:/mɑːrˈsuːpiəm/ | n. | 育儿袋 | A pouch on the abdomen of a female marsupial, such as a kangaroo or opossum, in which the young are carried and suckled. | The kangaroo joey peeked out from its mother's marsupium. 袋鼠宝宝从妈妈的育儿袋里探出头来。| synonyms: pouch, pocket | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could consider "placenta" in a biological context) | noun
