hine:/haɪn/| n.|仆人; 农工; 农业工人; (尤指农场中的)雇工|A farm worker, especially one regularly employed on a particular farm. |例句：The farmer hired a new hine to help with the harvest. 农场主雇了一个新的雇工来帮忙收割。

例句：The hines worked long hours in the fields.  雇工们在地里长时间工作。|近义词：farmhand, laborer, worker|反义词：employer, owner|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old English *hīna (attested only as genitive plural hīna), from Proto-Germanic *hīnaz, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱey- (“to lie, rest”). Cognate with Old Norse hión (“family, household”), Old High German hiwo (“husband, spouse”), Gothic ḫeiwa (“wife”). More at hive.  The original meaning may have been "member of the household."
