eked:/ikt/ | v. |  补充; 勉强维持;  | To supplement or make something last longer, typically by adding something small or meager. | She eked out her meager income by taking in laundry. 她靠帮人洗衣服来勉强维持她微薄的收入。|  supplement, stretch, augment | squander, waste | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *eken*, from Old English *ēacan* ("to increase, add to, augment"), from Proto-Germanic *aukjaną* (“to increase, augment, add”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ewg- (“to increase, augment”). Cognate with Dutch *oegen* (“to add, eke out”), German *erhöhen* (“to increase”), Old Norse *auka* (“to increase”).
