isolate:/ˈaɪsəleɪt/ (US), /ˈaɪsələt/ (UK)| v. n. adj.|隔离，孤立，分离|To separate something from other things; to be or become separated from other things.|The government decided to isolate the infected patients to prevent the spread of the disease. 政府决定隔离受感染的病人以防止疾病传播。|近义词：separate, segregate, detach, quarantine|反义词：integrate, combine, unite|verb

isolate:/ˈaɪsələt/ (US), /ˈaɪsələt/ (UK)| n.|隔离体，分离物|Something that has been isolated, especially a pure chemical substance or a microorganism.|Scientists have identified the isolate as a new strain of the virus. 科学家已经确认该分离物是一种新的病毒株。|近义词：isolate (v.) result|反义词：mixture, combination|noun

isolate:/ˈaɪsələt/ (US), /ˈaɪsələt/ (UK)| adj.|孤立的，单独的|Occurring alone; single; separated; remote.|The researchers studied the effects of the drug on isolated cells. 研究人员研究了这种药物对分离细胞的影响。|近义词：isolated, solitary, lonely, remote|反义词：connected, integrated|adjective
