gradualist:/ˈɡrædʒuəlɪst/| n. adj.|渐进主义者；渐进的|A person who believes in gradualism, the policy of introducing changes gradually or in stages. Favoring gradual reform rather than sudden change or revolution.|The gradualist approach to economic reform is preferred by many. 许多人更喜欢渐进的经济改革方法。|近义词：evolutionist, reformer|反义词:revolutionary, radical|词性:noun, adjective
gradual:/ˈɡrædʒuəl/| adj.|逐渐的|Happening or changing slowly over a long period of time.|The recovery from his illness was gradual. 他从病中逐渐康复。|近义词：progressive, steady|反义词:sudden, abrupt|词性:adjective

词源:
From Latin *gradus* ("step, degree") + *-alis* (suffix forming adjectives of relation)  + *-ist* (suffix forming agent nouns).
