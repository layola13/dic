damners:/ˈdæmərz/
n.|诅咒者，谴责者|People who condemn or curse others.  Those who express strong disapproval or pronounce judgment against something or someone.|The damners never stopped criticizing her choices.|近义词: critics, condemners, censurers|反义词: praisers, supporters|词性:名词

Etymology:  From the verb "damn" + "-er" (agent suffix). "Damn" comes from the Old French "damner," meaning "to condemn," ultimately from the Latin "damnare," also meaning "to condemn."

PHRASE->
Not commonly used in set phrases.  The word is generally used directly.  However, one might see phrases like:
"Damners be damned." = 让那些谴责者见鬼去吧。 (Expressing defiance against criticism)


注意事项:
While grammatically correct, "damners" is not a frequently used word in modern English.  "Critics," "condemners," or "censurers" are more common and generally preferred.  "Damn" as a verb is more common than "damners" as a noun.
