linchpin: /ˈlɪntʃpɪn/ | n. | 关键人物；关键要素；核心 |  A person or thing vital to an enterprise or organization.  Something that holds the various elements of a complicated structure together. | The quarterback is the linchpin of the team's offense.  四分卫是球队进攻的关键人物。

| The new factory is the linchpin of the government’s plan to revitalize the region.  新工厂是政府振兴该地区计划的关键。| 近义词: keystone, cornerstone, centerpiece, pillar | 反义词:  nonessential, unimportant, insignificant | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->

From "lynch pin," a pin inserted through the end of an axle to prevent the wheel from slipping off.  The "lynch" part may be related to "link."  Originally a literal mechanical term, it gained its figurative meaning of a crucial element in the mid-19th century.
