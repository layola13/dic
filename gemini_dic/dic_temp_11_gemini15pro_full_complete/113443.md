lynch:/lɪntʃ/
|v.|以私刑处死|To kill someone without legal authority, especially by hanging, often by a mob. |They threatened to lynch him if he didn't leave town. 他们威胁说，如果他不离开小镇，就把他处以私刑。|近义词: execute, murder|反义词: pardon, acquit|词性: verb


Etymology: From Lynch's Law, named after Charles Lynch (1736–1796), a Virginia planter and justice of the peace who headed an extralegal court during the American Revolution.  Originally, the term referred to extrajudicial punishments, not necessarily involving death.
