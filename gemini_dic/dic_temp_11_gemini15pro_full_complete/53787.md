discomposedly:/ˌdɪskəmˈpoʊzɪdli/| adv.|不安地，慌乱地|In a manner that shows someone is upset, disturbed, or lacking composure.|She rose discomposedly and left the room. 她慌乱地站起来离开了房间。|Example:  He stammered discomposedly when he realized his mistake.  他意识到自己的错误时，慌乱地结结巴巴起来。

Example:  After receiving the bad news, she walked discomposedly out of the office.  收到坏消息后，她心烦意乱地走出了办公室。|Synonyms: nervously, agitatedly, anxiously, uneasily|Antonyms: calmly, serenely, composedly, placidly|词性: 副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From discompose (meaning "to disturb the composure of") + -ly (adverbial suffix). Discompose comes from dis- (prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of") + compose (meaning "to form by putting together" or "to calm or settle").  Ultimately, the roots trace back to Latin.
