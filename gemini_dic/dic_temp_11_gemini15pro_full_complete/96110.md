indelectable:/ˌɪndɪˈlɛktəbəl/| adj.|美味的，令人愉悦的|Extremely pleasing to the senses, especially to the taste; delicious.|例句：The chef prepared an indelectable feast.  厨师准备了一顿美味的盛宴。

例句：The aroma from the bakery was indelectable.  面包店飘来的香味令人陶醉。|近义词：delicious, delightful, exquisite, scrumptious, savory, palatable|反义词：unappetizing, distasteful, unpleasant, unsavory|词性：形容词

Etymology:
From Late Latin indelectabilis "not to be charmed away, inescapable," from in- "not" + delectabilis "delightful," from the verb delectare "to delight," from de- intensive prefix + lactare "to entice, allure." The sense shifted in English to "delightful."
