exculpating: /ɪkˈskʌlpeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 开脱罪责的，免罪的 | Showing that someone is not guilty of wrongdoing. | The lawyer presented exculpating evidence that proved his client's innocence. 律师出示了证明其委托人无罪的开脱罪责的证据。|
The newly discovered evidence was exculpating, clearing the suspect of all charges. 新发现的证据具有开脱罪责的作用，洗清了嫌疑人的所有指控。 | Synonyms: absolving, clearing, vindicating | Antonyms: incriminating, condemning | adjective

exculpate: /ˈɛkskʌlˌpeɪt/ | vt. | 为…开脱；证明…无罪 | To show or declare that someone is not guilty of wrongdoing. | The evidence exculpated the defendant from the charges of robbery.  证据证明被告没有犯抢劫罪。|
The security footage exculpated her from the accusations of theft.  监控录像证明她没有偷窃。 | Synonyms: absolve, acquit, clear, exonerate, vindicate | Antonyms: accuse, blame, convict, incriminate | verb

词源:  Late Middle English: from Latin exculpat- ‘freed from blame’, from the verb exculpare, from ex- ‘out, from’ + culpa ‘blame’.
