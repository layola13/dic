counter-balance:/ˈkaʊntərˌbæləns/ | n. vt.| 平衡，抵消；平衡物，抵消力 |A weight that balances another weight; something that offsets or compensates for something else. |The government's spending on education is a counter-balance to the cuts in other areas. 政府在教育上的支出与其他领域的削减相抵消。|Synonyms: offset, compensate, counterpoise, neutralize |Antonyms: intensify, aggravate, exacerbate |noun
counter-balance:/ˌkaʊntərˈbæləns/ | vt.| 使平衡，抵消；补偿 |To offset or compensate for something. |The company's losses were counter-balanced by gains in other areas. 该公司的损失被其他地区的收益所抵消。|Synonyms: offset, compensate, counterpoise, neutralize |Antonyms: intensify, aggravate, exacerbate |verb
