mawkishness:/ˈmɔːkɪʃnəs/| n.|多愁善感；自作多情；无病呻吟|Excessive sentimentality; a display of emotion that is exaggerated or insincere. |Her mawkishness made it difficult to take her seriously. 她的多愁善感让人很难认真对待她。|sentimentality, emotionalism, sappiness|unsentimentality, coldness|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From mawkish + -ness. Mawkish originally meant "sickly sweet" or "nauseating," deriving from the Middle English *mawke* meaning "maggot."  The emotional sense developed later, referring to a kind of sentimentality that is so excessive it becomes unpleasant.
