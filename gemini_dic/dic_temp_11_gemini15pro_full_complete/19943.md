biodegrade:/ˌbaɪəʊdɪˈɡreɪd/ | v. |生物降解|To decompose or break down naturally into harmless substances through the action of living organisms, especially bacteria. |例句：Many plastics do not biodegrade easily. 很多塑料不容易生物降解。

例句：The new packaging is designed to biodegrade quickly in landfill sites.  这种新包装设计成在垃圾填埋场可以快速生物降解。|近义词：decompose, rot, decay|反义词: synthesize, build up|词性:动词


biodegradable:/ˌbaɪəʊdɪˈɡreɪdəbl/ | adj. |可生物降解的|Capable of being decomposed by bacteria or other living organisms. |例句：Biodegradable packaging helps reduce environmental pollution. 可生物降解的包装有助于减少环境污染。

例句： Consumers are increasingly demanding biodegradable products. 消费者越来越需要可生物降解的产品。|近义词：compostable, decomposable|反义词: non-biodegradable, persistent|词性:形容词

biodegradation:/ˌbaɪəʊˌdɛɡrəˈdeɪʃən/ | n. |生物降解（名词）|The process by which organic substances are broken down by living organisms, such as bacteria, into simpler substances such as carbon dioxide, water, and other harmless products. |例句：The biodegradation of oil spills can take many years.  石油泄漏的生物降解可能需要很多年。

例句：Scientists are researching new methods to accelerate biodegradation. 科学家正在研究加速生物降解的新方法。|近义词：decomposition, decay, rotting|反义词: synthesis, build up |词性:名词
