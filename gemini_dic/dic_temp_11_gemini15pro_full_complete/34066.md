civil-law:/ˈsɪvəl lɔː/
adjective|民法的|Relating to the system of law concerned with private relations between members of a community rather than criminal, military, or religious affairs.|例句：He specializes in civil-law cases. 他专攻民法案件。|近义词：private-law|反义词：criminal-law|词性:adjective
civil-law:/ˈsɪvəl lɔː/
noun|民法|The system of law concerned with private relations between members of a community rather than criminal, military, or religious affairs.|例句：Civil law offers remedies for breaches of contract. 民法为违反合同的行为提供补救措施。|近义词：private law|反义词：criminal law|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *civile* (modern *civil*), from Latin *civilis* "relating to a citizen, relating to public life, befitting a citizen, courteous, polite," derived from *civis* "citizen." The legal sense emerged in Late Middle English.
