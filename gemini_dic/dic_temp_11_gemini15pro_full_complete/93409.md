illiterate:/ɪˈlɪtərət/ /ɪˈlɪtrət/ | adj. n. | 文盲的，不识字的；文盲 | Unable to read or write.  |  例句：The government is trying to reduce the number of illiterate adults. 政府正在努力减少成年文盲的数量。| 例句：Many illiterate people rely on oral traditions to pass down knowledge. 许多不识字的人依靠口头传统来传承知识。| 近义词：unlettered, uneducated | 反义词: literate, educated | 词性: 形容词，名词

illiteracy:/ɪˈlɪtərəsi/ /ɪˈlɪtrəsi/ | n. | 文盲，不识字 | The inability to read or write. | 例句：Illiteracy is a major problem in many developing countries. 文盲是许多发展中国家的主要问题。| 例句：The organization is working to combat illiteracy around the world. 该组织正致力于在世界范围内消除文盲。 | 近义词：unletteredness | 反义词: literacy | 词性: 名词

词源: From Late Latin *illiteratus, from Latin in- “not” + litteratus “lettered.”
