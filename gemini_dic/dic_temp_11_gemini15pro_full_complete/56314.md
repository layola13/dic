dogie:/ˈdoʊɡi/| n.|小牛，孤儿，流浪的小牛|A motherless calf or a young steer, especially one kept as a pet or mascot.  A dogie is often seen as symbolic of the American West.|The cowboys were rounding up the dogies. 牛仔们正在把小牛们赶到一起。|Synonyms: calf, orphan, stray, maverick|Antonyms: cow, bull, adult|noun

dogie:/ˈdoʊɡi/| n.|孤儿，无家可归的人，新手，新来的人|A lonely and neglected person, especially a child; a newcomer or novice.|He felt like a dogie in the big city, all alone and lost. 他感觉自己在大城市里像个孤儿，孤单无助，迷失了方向。|Synonyms: orphan, waif, stray, newcomer, novice, greenhorn|Antonyms: veteran, expert, native|noun
