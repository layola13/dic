dingily:/ˈdɪŋɪli/| adv.|昏暗地，阴沉地|In a gloomy, dark, or depressing manner.  Characterized by a lack of cheerfulness or brightness.|The room was lit dingily, with only a single flickering candle.  房间里光线昏暗，只有一支闪烁的蜡烛。|Synonyms: gloomily, darkly, drearily, somberly|Antonyms: brightly, cheerfully, gaily|Adverb

Etymology: From "dingy" + "-ly".  "Dingy" comes from the early 18th century, of uncertain origin, possibly related to "dung."
