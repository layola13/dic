creaky:/ˈkriːki/| adj.|吱吱作响的；摇摇欲坠的；破旧的|Making a harsh, grating sound, typically as a result of old age or poor condition.  Relating to or characteristic of something old and likely to break or fall apart.|The stairs were old and creaky. 楼梯又旧又嘎吱作响。|The old house had creaky floorboards. 这栋老房子地板吱吱作响。|近义词：rickety, squeaky, wobbly|反义词：stable, sturdy, solid|词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *creky, from Old English *crēac, from Proto-Germanic *krekaz (“bent, crooked”). Cognate with Dutch kreek (“creek, inlet”), German krick (“crack, creek”), Icelandic kriki (“corner, nook”).
