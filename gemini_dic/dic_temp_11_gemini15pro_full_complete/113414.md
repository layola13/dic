lymphangitis:/ˌlɪmfænˈdʒaɪtɪs/
n.|淋巴管炎|Inflammation of the lymphatic vessels, typically caused by a bacterial infection.  The affected area becomes red, swollen, and tender.|The patient presented with lymphangitis in his left arm after a minor cut became infected.|Near synonyms: lymphadenitis (inflammation of lymph nodes, often occurs with lymphangitis)|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a pathological condition. One might consider "lymphatic health" as a contrasting state.)
