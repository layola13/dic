demonised:/ˈdiːmənaɪzd/ | adj. |被妖魔化的；被污蔑的 |Portrayed as wicked and threatening.  | The media demonised the politician, portraying him as a threat to national security. 媒体将这位政治家妖魔化，把他描绘成对国家安全的威胁。|  The immigrants were unfairly demonised in the press. 这些移民在媒体上被不公平地妖魔化了。 |vilified, slandered, denigrated | praised, lauded, celebrated |adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From demon + -ise/-ize.  "Demon" comes from the Greek "daimon," originally referring to a divine or supernatural being, later acquiring negative connotations.
