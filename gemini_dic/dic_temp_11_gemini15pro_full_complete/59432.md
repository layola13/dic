earthquake:/ˈɜːθˌkweɪk/ (US) /ˈɜːθkwɛɪk/ (UK)| n.|地震|A sudden and violent shaking of the ground, sometimes causing great destruction, as a result of movements within the earth's crust.|The earthquake destroyed many buildings. 地震摧毁了许多建筑物。|Synonyms: tremor, temblor, quake|Antonyms: stability, stillness|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *erthequake*, equivalent to earth + quake.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *erþō-kwakōną (“earthquake”).  Compare Dutch aardbeving, German Erdbeben, Swedish jordbävning.
