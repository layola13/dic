conciliatory:/kənˈsɪliətɔːri/ /kənˈsɪliəˌtɔri/
adjective
抚慰的；调解的；调和的
Intended or likely to placate or pacify.
例句：
She took a conciliatory approach to the dispute. 她对这场纠纷采取了安抚的态度。

The government made conciliatory gestures to the rebels. 政府向叛军做出了和解的姿态。

近义词：appeasing, placating, mollifying, soothing, pacific, peacemaking
反义词：antagonistic, hostile, aggressive, provocative
词性: adjective

conciliate:/kənˈsɪlieɪt/
verb
安抚；调解；调停；赢得…的好感（或支持）
To stop (someone) from being angry or discontented; placate; to gain (something) by pleasing acts.
例句：
The company tried to conciliate the angry customers. 公司试图安抚愤怒的顾客。

The mediator conciliated the two warring factions. 调解员调解了两个交战派别。

近义词：placate, appease, mollify, pacify, soothe
反义词：aggravate, provoke, antagonize
词性: verb

conciliation:/kənˌsɪliˈeɪʃn/
noun
安抚；调解；调停
The action of stopping someone from being angry or discontented; placation.
例句：
Conciliation is often the best way to resolve disputes. 调解通常是解决争端的最佳方式。

The two sides reached an agreement through conciliation. 双方通过调解达成了协议。

近义词：placation, appeasement, mollification, pacification
反义词：aggravation, provocation, antagonism
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin conciliātōrius, from conciliātus, past participle of conciliāre (“to reconcile, unite, win over”), from concilium (“council”).
