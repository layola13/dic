brandish:/ˈbrændɪʃ/| v.|挥舞，炫耀|To wave or flourish (something, especially a weapon) as a threat or in anger or excitement.  To display or show off something boastfully.|The knight brandished his sword. 骑士挥舞着他的剑。|近义词：wave, flourish, wield, flaunt|反义词：conceal, hide|词性:动词
Etymology: From Old French "brandir," from Frankish "*brand," sword, related to Old High German "brant," burning piece of wood, sword.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*brandaz," burning, brand.
