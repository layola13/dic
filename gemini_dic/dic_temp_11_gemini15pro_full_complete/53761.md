discomfiture:/dɪskəmˈfɪtʃər/ | n. | 沮丧；狼狈；挫败 | A state of being disconcerted, embarrassed, or confused. |  The look of discomfiture on her face was evident after she tripped in front of everyone. 她当着众人的面绊倒后，脸上显露出尴尬的神情。

Her discomfiture at having forgotten his name was obvious. 她忘记了他的名字，显得十分不安，这很明显。 | 近义词: embarrassment, confusion, uneasiness, chagrin | 反义词: composure, confidence, aplomb | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *desconfiture*, from *desconfire* "to defeat, rout," from *des-* "expressing reversal" + *confire* "to make, prepare, preserve; accomplish," from Latin *conficere* "to make together, accomplish, complete."  The sense shifted from "rout, defeat" to "frustration, embarrassment" in the 16th century.
