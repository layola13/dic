balls:/bɔːlz/ (UK) /bɑːlz/ (US)| n.|球；睾丸；勇气；胡说|A round object used in games and sports; testicles; courage; nonsense. |He kicked the balls across the field. 他把球踢过整个球场。|The dog chased after the balls. 狗追着球跑。|synonyms: spheres, orbs, testicles (for the anatomical sense), guts, nerve (for courage), rubbish, nonsense (for lies)|antonyms: cubes, (lack of) courage, sense, truth|noun

balls:/bɔːlz/ (UK) /bɑːlz/ (US)| v. (balls, balling, balled)|使成球形；缠绕；捏成球形|To form into a ball; to wind into a ball.  |She balled up the yarn. 她把毛线绕成球。|He balled his fists in anger. 他愤怒地握紧了拳头。|synonyms: roll, wind, coil|antonyms: unroll, unwind, straighten|verb
