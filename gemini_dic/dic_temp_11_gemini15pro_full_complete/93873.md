immolate: /ˈɪməleɪt/ | vt. | 牺牲；献祭；烧死 | to kill or destroy, especially by fire, as a sacrifice to a god | The ancient Aztecs would immolate prisoners of war to appease their gods. 古代阿兹特克人会烧死战俘来安抚他们的神。|  近义词: sacrifice, kill, destroy | 反义词: save, rescue, protect | 词性: verb

immolate: /ˈɪməleɪt/ | vt. | 毁灭；使受损 | to destroy or damage something greatly |  His reputation was immolated by the scandal. 他的名誉被丑闻毁了。 | 近义词: ruin, destroy, devastate | 反义词: build, create, restore | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *immolare, from in- "in, on" + mola "sacrificial meal, salt cake." Literally "to sprinkle with sacrificial meal."
