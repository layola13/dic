monocotyledons:/ˌmɒnəʊˌkɒtɪˈliːdənz/ | n. |单子叶植物|A class of flowering plants (angiosperms) characterized by having only one embryonic seed leaf or cotyledon.  They also typically have parallel leaf veins, scattered vascular bundles in the stem, the absence of a typical cambium, and adventitious roots.|Examples of monocotyledons include grasses, lilies, orchids, and palms.|近义词:monocot|反义词:dicotyledon|Noun
