indulgent:/ɪnˈdʌldʒənt/ | adj. | 纵容的；溺爱的；宽容的；放纵的 | Having or indicating a tendency to be overly generous to or lenient with someone. | She gave her children an indulgent smile. 她对孩子们露出了溺爱的笑容。|  He has a very indulgent grandfather who gives him everything he wants. 他有一个非常溺爱他的祖父，对他有求必应。 | 近义词：lenient, permissive, tolerant, forgiving | 反义词：strict, severe, austere, harsh | 词性：形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *indulgentem (nominative indulgens), present participle of indulgere "to be kind or tender to, yield to, give free rein to," from in- "in, toward" (from PIE root *en "in") + dulgere "be kind to, give way to," perhaps originally "stroke, caress," of uncertain origin.
