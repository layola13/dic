moss:/mɒs/ (UK), /mɔːs/ (US)| n.|苔藓|A small flowerless green plant that lacks true roots, growing in low carpets or rounded cushions in damp habitats and reproducing by means of spores released from stalked capsules.|The rocks were covered with moss.|Synonyms: lichen, liverwort|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a plant, could consider something like "bare rock" as a contrasting concept)|noun
