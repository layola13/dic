beat-around-the-bush:/ˌbiːt əˈraʊnd ðə ˈbʊʃ/| idiom | 旁敲侧击，拐弯抹角，说话绕圈子 | To avoid speaking directly about a difficult or uncomfortable topic.  To speak indirectly or evasively. |  He kept beating around the bush about the real cost of the project. 他对项目的实际成本一直含糊其辞。 |  equivocate, prevaricate, avoid the issue, hedge, stall | be direct, straightforward, upfront, to the point | idiom
