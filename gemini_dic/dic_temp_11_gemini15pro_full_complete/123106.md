mongrelization:/ˌmʌŋɡrələˈzeɪʃən/ |n.| 杂交，混血，混合|The mongrelization of cultures can lead to both exciting new forms and the loss of distinct traditions. 文化的融合既可能带来令人兴奋的新形式，也可能导致独特传统的丧失。|例句：The mongrelization of architectural styles in the city reflects its diverse history.  这座城市建筑风格的混合反映了其多元的历史。

例句：Some fear the mongrelization of language through the adoption of foreign words.  一些人担心由于采用外来词而导致语言的混杂。|近义词：hybridization, intermingling, mixture, amalgamation, fusion, blending|反义词：purification, segregation, separation|词性: noun
