negligibleness:/ˈnɛɡlɪdʒɪbəlnəs/
n.
可忽略性；微不足道
The negligibleness of the side effects made the drug a viable option.  这种药物的副作用可以忽略不计，使其成为可行的选择。
The negligibleness of the error allowed the experiment to continue.  误差的微不足道使得实验得以继续。
近义词:insignificance, triviality
反义词:importance, significance
词性:noun

negligible:/ˈnɛɡlɪdʒɪbəl/
adj.
可忽略的；微不足道的
The difference in price was negligible. 价格差异微不足道。
The risk of infection is negligible. 感染的风险可以忽略不计。
近义词:insignificant, trivial, minor
反义词:significant, important, major
词性:adjective

词源: From negligible + -ness.  Negligible comes from the Latin negligibilis,  from negligere "to neglect," from nec "not" + legere "to gather, choose, read."
