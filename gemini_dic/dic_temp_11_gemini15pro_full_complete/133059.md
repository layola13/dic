词源:  "nonequabilities" comes from "nonequable" + "-ities". "Nonequable" is formed by the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") added to "equable" (meaning "uniform" or "steady"). "Equable" ultimately derives from the Latin "aequabilis," from "aequus" (meaning "equal").  "-ities" is a suffix that forms plural nouns denoting qualities or states.
