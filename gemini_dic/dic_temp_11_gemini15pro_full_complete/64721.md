escheatable:/ɪˈʃiːtəbl/
adjective
可充公的
Liable to be escheated.  Something that is escheatable can be taken back by the government if the owner dies without a will or legal heir.
The property was escheatable to the state because the owner died intestate and without heirs. 这处房产可归国家所有，因为业主死时没有立遗嘱，也没有继承人。

The king declared the traitor's lands escheatable. 国王宣布叛徒的土地可以充公。
近义词: forfeitable, confiscable
反义词: inheritable
