makeshift:/ˈmeɪkʃɪft/| adj.|权宜之计的；临时凑合的|Serving as a temporary substitute; sufficient for the time being. |We used a large box as a makeshift table. 我们用一个大箱子当作临时桌子。|近义词：temporary, provisional, improvised, jury-rigged|反义词:permanent, durable, robust|词性:形容词
Etymology: From "make" + "shift" (meaning 'contrive,' 'manage').  The original sense was "to contrive, manage," later evolving to describe something contrived or temporarily put together.
