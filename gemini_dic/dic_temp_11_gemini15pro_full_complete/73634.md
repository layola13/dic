forfeitable:/ˈfɔːrfɪtəbl/
adjective
可丧失的，可被没收的
Subject to being forfeited, especially as a penalty for a crime, breach of contract, or other failure to fulfill an obligation.
The deposit is forfeitable if the buyer backs out of the deal.  买方如果退出交易，定金将被没收。
Her driver's license is forfeitable if she gets another speeding ticket. 如果她再收到一张超速罚单，她的驾驶执照将被吊销。
synonyms: confiscable, seizable, losable
antonyms: unlosable, secure, guaranteed
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From forfeit (v.) + -able.  Forfeit itself comes from Old French *forfet, probably from fors- "outside" + faire "to do," hence "to do something outside the law."
