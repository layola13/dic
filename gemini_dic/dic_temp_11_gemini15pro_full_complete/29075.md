carver:/ˈkɑːrvər/ (US) /ˈkɑːvə(r)/ (UK)| n. |雕刻家，雕工；切肉刀|A person who carves wood, stone, or other materials into artistic forms; a large knife used for carving meat.| He is a skilled carver of wooden figures. 他是一位技艺精湛的木雕师。 The Thanksgiving turkey was carved with a large, sharp carver. 感恩节的火鸡是用一把又大又锋利的切肉刀切开的。|近义词：sculptor, engraver, whittler (for wood), butcher knife (for meat)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

carve:/kɑːrv/ (US) /kɑːv/ (UK)| v. |雕刻；切割；开创|To cut (a hard material) in order to produce an artistic shape; to cut (cooked meat) into slices for serving; to create or establish something through painstaking effort.|She carved a beautiful bird from the piece of wood. 她用这块木头雕刻了一只美丽的鸟。 He carefully carved the roast beef. 他小心翼翼地切烤牛肉。  They carved a niche for themselves in the competitive market. 他们在竞争激烈的市场中开创了一片天地。|近义词：sculpt, engrave, whittle, slice, create|反义词: N/A|词性:verb
