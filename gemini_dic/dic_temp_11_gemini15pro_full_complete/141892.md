ordal:/ˈɔːrdiːəl/ (US), /ˈɔːdiːəl/ (UK)| n.|考验；磨难；神判法|A difficult or painful experience, a trial.  Historically, a primitive form of trial to determine guilt or innocence by subjecting the accused to dangerous or painful tests believed to be controlled by a supernatural power.|He went through a terrible ordeal after being captured by the enemy. 他被敌人俘虏后经历了一场可怕的磨难。|Synonyms: trial, tribulation, test, hardship|Antonyms: comfort, ease, pleasure|Noun
