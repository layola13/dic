herdgroom:/ˈhɜːrdˌɡruːm/ (US), /ˈhɜːdˌɡruːm/ (UK)| n.|牧人，牧童|A person who tends a herd of animals, especially cattle.|The herdgroom led the cows to the pasture. 牧童把牛群赶到牧场。|近义词：herdsman, shepherd, cowherd|反义词: None|词性: Noun

词源:
From herd + groom.  "Herd" comes from Old English "heord" meaning a flock or company. "Groom" comes from Middle English "grome" meaning a boy or young man, later specifically a servant who tends horses.
