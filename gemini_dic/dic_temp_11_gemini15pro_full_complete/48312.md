decrepitude:/dɪˈkrɛpɪtjuːd/ /dɪˈkrɛpɪtuːd/| n. |衰老，破旧|The decrepitude of the building was evident in its crumbling facade. 这栋建筑的破败在其摇摇欲坠的正面显而易见。|例句：The decrepitude of the old man was apparent in his slow, shuffling gait.  这位老人的衰老表现在他缓慢的、拖着脚的步态上。

The decrepitude of the once-grand mansion was a sad sight to behold.  这座曾经宏伟的宅邸如今破败不堪，令人唏嘘。|近义词：feebleness, frailty, infirmity, debility, decay|反义词：strength, vigor, robustness, soundness|词性:名词


decrepit:/dɪˈkrɛpɪt/| adj. |衰老的，破旧的|The decrepit car coughed and sputtered before finally dying.  这辆破旧的汽车咳嗽了几声，喷了几下，最后熄火了。|例句：The decrepit bridge was deemed unsafe for travel. 这座破旧的桥被认为不安全，禁止通行。

The decrepit chair collapsed under his weight.  那把破旧的椅子在他的重压下塌了。|近义词：feeble, frail, infirm, debilitated, ramshackle, dilapidated|反义词：strong, vigorous, robust, sound, sturdy|词性:形容词

词源: From Latin *decrepitus*, past participle of decrepare (“to crackle, rattle”), from de- (“down, away”) + crepare (“to creak, crack, rattle”).
