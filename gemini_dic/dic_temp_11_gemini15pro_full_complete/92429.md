hyponatraemia: /ˌhaɪpəʊnəˈtriːmiə/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpoʊnəˈtriːmiə/ (US)| n. |低钠血症|A condition in which the concentration of sodium in the blood is abnormally low.|The patient presented with symptoms of hyponatraemia, including nausea and confusion.|Synonyms: hyponatremia (US spelling), low blood sodium|Antonyms: hypernatraemia (high blood sodium)|noun
