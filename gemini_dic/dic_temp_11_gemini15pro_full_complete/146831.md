overimpressionablenesses:/ˌoʊvərɪmˈprɛʃənəbəlnəsɪz/
|n.| 过度易受影响性；过度敏感性
|The state of being excessively impressionable; undue sensitivity to external influences.|
|Her overimpressionablenesses led her to believe every conspiracy theory she encountered.| 她过度敏感的天性使她相信她遇到的每一个阴谋论。
|近义词: sensitivity, suggestibility, vulnerability|
|反义词: resilience, firmness, resistance|
|词性: noun|

overimpressionable:/ˌoʊvərɪmˈprɛʃənəbəl/
|adj.| 过度易受影响的；过度敏感的
|Easily influenced or impressed; unduly sensitive.|
|He was an overimpressionable young man, easily swayed by the opinions of others.| 他是一个过度敏感的年轻人，很容易被别人的意见左右。
|近义词: sensitive, suggestible, vulnerable|
|反义词: resilient, firm, resistant|
|词性: adjective|

impressionable:/ɪmˈprɛʃənəbəl/
|adj.| 易受影响的；敏感的
|Easily influenced or impressed.|
|Children are at an impressionable age and need good role models.| 儿童处于易受影响的年龄，需要良好的榜样。
|近义词: sensitive, receptive, susceptible|
|反义词: resistant, impervious, unyielding|
|词性: adjective|

impression:/ɪmˈprɛʃən/
|n.| 印象；感想；盖印；压痕
|An effect, feeling, or image retained as a result of experience.|
|My first impression of him was that he was very shy.| 我对他的第一印象是他很害羞。
|近义词: feeling, sense, notion, idea|
|反义词: reality, fact, truth|
|词性: noun|

impress:/ɪmˈprɛs/
|v.| 给…留下深刻印象；使铭记；盖印；压印
|To affect deeply or strongly in mind or feelings; to imprint.|
|She impressed me with her intelligence and wit.| 她的智慧和机智给我留下了深刻的印象。
|近义词: influence, affect, move, touch|
|反义词: bore, disappoint, disillusion|
|词性: verb|
