afforder:/əˈfɔːrdər/| n.|负担得起的人;  |Someone who can afford something.  |例句：The new apartments are only for high-income afforders. 这些新公寓只针对高收入人群。|近义词：buyer, purchaser, consumer|反义词: non-buyer, defaulter| noun


afforded:/əˈfɔːrdɪd/| v. (afford的过去式和过去分词)|提供;给予;供应得起|to be able to buy or do something because you have enough money or time |例句：We can't afford a new car. 我们买不起新车。  例句：I can't afford the time to go on vacation right now. 我现在没时间去度假。 |近义词：provide, supply, furnish, manage|反义词: refuse, deny, withhold| verb

affording:/əˈfɔːrdɪŋ/| v. (afford的现在分词)|提供;给予;供应得起|to be able to buy or do something because you have enough money or time |例句：Affording a house in this city is becoming increasingly difficult. 在这个城市买房子越来越难了。 例句：He's affording his children a good education. 他正在为他的孩子们提供良好的教育。|近义词：providing, supplying, furnishing, granting|反义词: refusing, denying, withholding| verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *affordan, *afforden, from Old English geāfordian (“to afford, provide, perform, accomplish, carry out, execute, commit”), from Proto-Germanic *aforþōjaną (“to perform, carry out”), equivalent to a- + forth + -do. Cognate with Dutch veroorloven (“to afford, permit”), German verordnen (“to ordain, prescribe”).
