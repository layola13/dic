meganucleus:/ˌmɛɡəˈnjuːkliəs/| n. |巨核; 大核|A large, often polyploid nucleus, characteristic of certain cells, especially megakaryocytes.  It's formed by repeated rounds of DNA replication without cell division.|Megakaryocytes, the precursors to platelets, contain a characteristic meganucleus.|Synonyms: polytene nucleus, giant nucleus|Antonyms: micronucleus|noun
