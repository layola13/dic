acuity:/əˈkjuːɪti/| n. |敏锐，锐度|Sharpness or keenness of thought, vision, or hearing.|例句：The acuity of his vision allowed him to spot the distant ship. 他的视力敏锐，使他能够发现远处的船只。|例句：The lawyer had remarkable mental acuity. 这位律师思维异常敏锐。|近义词：sharpness, keenness, acuteness|反义词:dullness, bluntness|词性:名词

acuities:/əˈkjuːɪtiz/| n. |敏锐度（复数）|The plural form of "acuity," referring to multiple instances of sharpness or keenness.|例句：The surgeon's acuities of vision and touch were essential to the success of the operation.  外科医生的视觉和触觉敏锐度对这次手术的成功至关重要。|例句：As we age, our various acuities, both physical and mental, may decline. 随着年龄的增长，我们的各种敏锐度，包括身体和精神上的，都可能会下降。|近义词：sharpnesses, keennesses, acutenesses|反义词:dullnesses, bluntnesses|词性:名词
