ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *bord (unattested) or Old North French bord, from Frankish *bort "board, plank, ship's side," from Proto-Germanic *burdą (“board, plank”). Cognate with Old English bord (“board, plank, shield, side of a ship”), Old High German bort (“board, plank”), Old Norse borð (“board, plank, table, ship's side”). More at board.
