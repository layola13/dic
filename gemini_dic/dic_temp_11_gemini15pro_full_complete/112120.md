long-term:/ˌlɔːŋˈtɜːrm/ | adj. | 长期的 |  Extending over or involving a relatively long period of time. |  They have a long-term plan for expansion. 他们有一个长期的扩张计划。|  She's looking for a long-term relationship. 她在寻找一段长期的恋爱关系。 | 近义词:  long-lasting, enduring, protracted | 反义词: short-term, temporary | 词性: 形容词

long-term:/ˌlɔːŋˈtɜːrm/ | n. | 长期 |  A long period of time. | In the long-term, this investment will pay off. 从长远来看，这项投资将会得到回报。 |  The long-term effects of the medication are still unknown.  这种药物的长期影响仍然未知。 | 近义词: future, long run | 反义词: short term | 词性: 名词


词源:  From "long" + "term."  "Long" traces back to Old English "lang," ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*langaz." "Term" comes from Old French "terme," ultimately from Latin "terminus" meaning "limit, boundary, end."
