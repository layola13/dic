astate:/əˈsteɪt/ | adj. | 惊愕的，惊呆的 |  Shocked and bewildered; stunned.  |  He stood astate, unable to speak. 他惊呆了，说不出话来。 | The news left her astate.  这消息让她惊呆了。 | astonished, amazed, stunned, shocked, bewildered, dumbfounded |  calm, composed, unfazed | adjective

Etymology:
From Old French *estat, from Latin status "a standing, position, posture, condition,"  past participle of stare "to stand."  The meaning "bewildered, amazed" arose from the literal sense of being "stupefied as if rooted to the spot."
