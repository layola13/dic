microbrewer:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌbruːər/ | n. | 小型酿酒厂；精酿啤酒商 | A microbrewer is a brewery that produces a small amount of beer, typically for sale on-site or in local markets.  |  Example: The local microbrewer has a tasting room where you can sample their latest creations.  这家本地小型酿酒厂有一个品酒室，你可以在那里品尝他们最新的作品。

Example:  Many microbrewers focus on crafting unique and flavorful beers. 许多小型酿酒厂专注于酿造独特而美味的啤酒。 | Synonyms: craft brewer, independent brewer | Antonyms: macrobrewery, large-scale brewery | noun



ETYMOLOGY->

From *micro-* (small) + *brewer* (one who brews). The term gained popularity in the late 20th century with the rise of the craft beer movement.
