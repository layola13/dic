choleric: /ˈkɑːlərɪk/ (US), /kəˈlɛrɪk/ (UK)
| adj. | 易怒的，暴躁的 | bad-tempered; easily angered |  He has a choleric temperament and easily flies off the handle. 他脾气暴躁，很容易发火。| synonyms: irascible, irritable, short-tempered, fiery, hot-tempered, hot-headed | antonyms: calm, placid, even-tempered, affable | word type: adjective

choleric: /ˈkɑːlərɪk/ (US), /kəˈlɛrɪk/ (UK)
| n. | 暴躁的人 | a person who is easily angered |  He's such a choleric, always yelling about something. 他真是个暴躁的人，总是大喊大叫。| synonyms: hothead, firebrand | antonyms: pacifist | word type: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: colerik, via Old French from Late Latin cholericus, from Greek kholerikos, from kholē 'bile'.  The word's meaning stems from the ancient Greek theory of the four humors, where choler (yellow bile) was believed to cause anger and irritability.
