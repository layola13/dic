ETYMOLOGY->
From lousy + -ness.  "Lousy" originally referred to being infested with lice, but over time broadened in meaning to encompass anything unpleasant or of poor quality.  The suffix "-ness" transforms the adjective "lousy" into a noun, indicating the state or quality of being lousy.
