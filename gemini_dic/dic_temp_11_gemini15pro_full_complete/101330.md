iodines:/ˈaɪəˌdaɪnz/ | n. |碘（复数）|A nonmetallic halogen element, occurring at ordinary temperatures as a grayish-black crystalline solid that sublimes to a dense violet vapor.  Used in medicine (especially as an antiseptic) and in photography. |The doctor used iodines to disinfect the wound.|Synonyms: iodine (singular form is generally preferred), halogen|Antonyms: None (as it's a specific element)|noun
