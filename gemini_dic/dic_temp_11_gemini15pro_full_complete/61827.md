empiricist:/ɪmˈpɪrɪsɪst/ /ɛmˈpɪrɪsɪst/
n.|经验主义者; 经验论者|A person who believes that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience.|John Locke was a prominent empiricist.  约翰·洛克是一位杰出的经验主义者。|synonyms: experimentalist, pragmatist|antonyms: rationalist, theorist|noun
empirical:/ɪmˈpɪrɪkl/ /ɛmˈpɪrɪkl/
adj.|经验主义的; 以经验为依据的|Based on, concerned with, or verifiable by observation or experience rather than theory or pure logic.|Empirical evidence supports the theory. 经验证据支持该理论。|synonyms: observed, factual, practical|antonyms: theoretical, hypothetical, conjectural|adjective
empiricism:/ɪmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ /ɛmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/
n.|经验主义; 经验论|The theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience.|Empiricism emphasizes the role of experience in acquiring knowledge. 经验主义强调经验在获取知识中的作用。|synonyms:  experimentalism, pragmatism|antonyms: rationalism, idealism|noun
