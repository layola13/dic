obligednesses:/əˈblaɪdʒɪdnɪsɪz/
n.
义务; 责任; 恩惠;
The state of being obliged or indebted to someone or something.  A feeling of being bound to do or pay something.
I felt a deep sense of obligedness to my parents for their sacrifices. 我对父母的牺牲感到深深的责任感。
She helped him out of a sense of obligedness. 她出于责任感帮助了他.
近义词:obligations, duties, responsibilities
反义词:freedom, independence, irresponsibility
词性:名词

obligedness:/əˈblaɪdʒɪdnɪsɪz/
词源: From obliged + -ness.  "Obliged" comes from the Old French "obligier" (to bind, compel), ultimately from the Latin "obligare" (to tie up, bind).
用法说明: This word is not commonly used in modern English. "Obligations" is generally preferred.  It refers to the state of being bound or indebted to someone, often due to a favor or service they have provided.
