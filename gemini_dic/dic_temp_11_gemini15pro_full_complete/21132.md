blithely:/ˈblaɪðli/ | adv. | 愉快地；无忧无虑地；轻率地 | In a happy or carefree manner; without thought or regard for serious matters. | She blithely ignored the warnings and went ahead with her plans. 她无视警告，继续执行她的计划。 |  He blithely skipped down the street, whistling a merry tune. 他愉快地沿着街道跳着舞，吹着欢快的曲子。 | 近义词: cheerfully, merrily, lightheartedly, casually | 反义词: anxiously, seriously, cautiously | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *blithli*, from Old English *blīþlīce* (“blithely, joyfully, kindly, gently, graciously”), equivalent to *blīþe* (“blithe”) + *-līce* (adverbial suffix). Cognate with Old Saxon *blīðlīko* (“blithely”), Old High German *blīdlihho* (“blithely”), Old Norse *blíðliga* (“blithely”).
