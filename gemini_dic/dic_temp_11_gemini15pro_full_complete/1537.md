ache:/eɪk/ | n. vi. | 疼痛；渴望 | a continuous dull pain; a strong desire for something or someone | My head aches. 我的头疼。|  Synonyms: pain, throb, yearn, long | Antonyms: comfort, ease | noun, verb

ache:/eɪk/ | vi. | 疼痛；渴望 | to feel a continuous dull pain; to have a strong desire for something or someone |  I'm aching all over after that workout. 锻炼后我浑身酸痛。| Synonyms: hurt, smart, yearn, long | Antonyms:  feel well, be indifferent to | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *acan (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *akaną (source also of Old Saxon akan "to ache, pain", Old High German ahhan "to persecute", Gothic agan "to fear, be afraid"). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ogh- (“to be distressed, anxious”).
