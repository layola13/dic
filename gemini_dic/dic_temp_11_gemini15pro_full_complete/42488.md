countergovernment:/ˌkaʊntərˈɡʌvərmənt/ | n. | 反政府；影子内阁；对立政府 | A group or organization that seeks to overthrow or replace the existing government. |  The exiled group formed a countergovernment, hoping to regain power.  流亡团体组建了一个反政府组织，希望重新掌权。 | Synonyms: shadow government, opposition, rebel government | Antonyms: incumbent government, ruling party, administration | noun

countergovernment:/ˌkaʊntərˈɡʌvərmənt/ | n. | 反政府；影子内阁；对立政府 |  A group of people prepared to take control if the existing government falls.  | The opposition party established a countergovernment in preparation for potential elections. 反对党建立了一个影子内阁，为可能举行的选举做准备。 | Synonyms: alternative government, standby government,  rival government | Antonyms: established government, legitimate government,  official government | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
counter- (prefix): against, opposite. From Old French *contre-, from Latin contra ("against").
government (noun): the governing body of a nation, state, or community. From Old French governement, from governer ("to govern"), ultimately from Latin gubernare ("to steer, guide, govern").
