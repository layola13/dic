acquiescence:/ˌækwiˈɛsəns/| n.|默许，顺从，同意|The government met with widespread acquiescence to its new policy. 政府的新政策得到了广泛的默许。|例句：Her acquiescence to their demands was reluctant. 她勉强同意了他们的要求。

例句：He nodded in acquiescence. 他点头表示同意。

|近义词：consent, agreement, compliance, acceptance|反义词：dissent, disagreement, opposition, resistance|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *acquiescentia, from Latin acquiescere "to rest, remain at rest, find rest," figuratively "to be satisfied, comply, agree," from ad- "to" + quiescere "to be quiet, rest" (from quies "rest, quiet," related to Old English cwic "alive," German quick "alive," Gothic qius "alive").
