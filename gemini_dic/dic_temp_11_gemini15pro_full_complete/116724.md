maudlinnesses: /ˈmɔːdlɪnɪsɪz/ | n. | 伤感，多愁善感 | Excessive sentimentality or tearfulness.  |  His maudlinnesses after a few drinks became tiresome.  几杯酒下肚后，他的伤感变得令人厌烦。 | 近义词: sentimentality, tearfulness, emotionalism | 反义词: stoicism, unemotionality | 词性: noun


Etymology:  From maudlin (adjective) + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing a state or quality).  Maudlin itself comes from the Middle English "Maudeleyn," referring to Mary Magdalene, often depicted weeping in paintings.
