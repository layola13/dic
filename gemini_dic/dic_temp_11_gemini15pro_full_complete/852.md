abstractionist:/ˌæbˈstrækʃənɪst/
n.|抽象主义者；抽象派艺术家|An artist who creates abstract art, which is art that does not attempt to represent an accurate depiction of visual reality but instead uses shapes, colours, forms and gestural marks to achieve its effect.|Jackson Pollock was a famous abstractionist. 杰克逊·波洛克是一位著名的抽象主义者。|近义词:non-objective artist, nonrepresentational artist|反义词:realist|词性:noun

abstractionist:/ˌæbˈstrækʃənɪst/
adj.|抽象主义的；抽象派的|Relating to or characteristic of abstractionism, a style of art that does not represent recognizable objects or scenes.|Her abstractionist paintings were full of vibrant colors and dynamic shapes. 她的抽象主义绘画充满了鲜艳的色彩和动态的形状。|近义词:non-objective, nonrepresentational|反义词:realistic, representational|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From abstraction + -ist.  Abstraction comes from Late Latin abstractionem (nominative abstractio), noun of action from past-participle stem of abstrahere "to draw away," from ab- "away" + trahere "to draw."
