fleets:/fliːts/
n.
舰队；车队；机群
A group of ships, aircraft, or vehicles, especially when owned or controlled by one person or organization.
The company owns a large fleet of trucks.  这家公司拥有一支庞大的卡车车队。
The fishing fleet returned to port. 渔船队返回了港口。
synonyms: armada, flotilla, navy, squadron
antonyms: single ship/aircraft/vehicle


fleets:/fliːts/
v. (third-person singular simple present fleets, present participle fleeting, simple past and past participle fleeted)
快速移动；消逝
To move swiftly; to pass quickly.
Time fleets by. 时间飞逝。
A faint smile fleeted across her face.  她脸上掠过一丝淡淡的微笑。
synonyms: flit, flash, dart, rush
antonyms: linger, remain, stay


Etymology: From Old English *flēotan "to float, swim, flow", from Proto-Germanic *fleutaną "to float", from Proto-Indo-European *plew- "to flow, swim".  The sense of "a group of ships" developed from the idea of ships floating together.
