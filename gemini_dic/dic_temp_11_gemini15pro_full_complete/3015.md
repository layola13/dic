aeriality:/ˌɛəriˈælɪti/| n.|空中性，轻盈，飘逸|The quality of being light and graceful in the air, as in ballet or gymnastics.  It refers to the ability to perform movements that give the impression of floating or being suspended in the air.|Her aeriality was breathtaking as she leaped across the stage. 她的轻盈在舞台上跳跃时令人叹为观止。|Synonyms: lightness, grace, airiness, buoyancy|Antonyms: heaviness, clumsiness, awkwardness|noun

Etymology->
From Latin *aeriam* (“airiness”) +‎ *-ity*,  ultimately from Latin *aer* (“air”).
