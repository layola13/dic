bounder:/ˈbaʊndər/| n. |粗鲁的人，无赖|A person who behaves badly or in a way that is not acceptable in society. |例句：He's a real bounder, always pushing in front of people in queues. 他真是个粗鲁的家伙，总是在队伍里插队。

例句：She called him a bounder for his rude remarks. 她因为他粗鲁的言论称他为无赖。|近义词：cad, lout, boor, rotter|反义词:gentleman|词性:名词

bound:/baʊnd/| n. |跳跃，界限|A jump or leap. A limit or boundary.|例句：The dog made a sudden bound towards the door. 狗突然朝门猛地一跳。

例句：There are no bounds to his ambition. 他的野心没有界限。|近义词：leap, jump, limit, boundary|反义词:limitlessness, freedom|词性:名词

bound:/baʊnd/| adj. |肯定的，有义务的，受约束的|Certain to do something, or likely to happen. Obliged to do something by law, duty, or some other form of constraint.|例句：He's bound to be late; he always is. 他肯定会迟到，他总是迟到。

例句：I'm bound by my promise to help him. 我有义务履行我的承诺去帮助他。|近义词：certain, likely, obliged, obligated|反义词:unlikely, free, unobligated|词性:形容词

bound:/baʊnd/| v. |跳跃，限制|To jump or leap. To form the boundary of; enclose.|例句：The deer bounded across the field. 鹿跳跃着穿过田野。

例句：The property is bounded on one side by a river. 这块地产的一侧以一条河为界。|近义词：leap, jump, limit, restrict|反义词:free, release|词性:动词
