excommunicators:/ˌɛkskəˈmjuːnɪkeɪtərz/
|n.| 革除教籍者，逐出教会者
|A person who excommunicates someone from a church or religious community.|
|The excommunicators declared the heretic no longer a member of the church.|革除教籍者宣布异教徒不再是教会成员。

|The medieval excommunicators held significant power over people's lives.|中世纪的革除教籍者对人们的生活拥有巨大的权力。

|近义词：expellers, banishers, ostracizers|
|反义词: welcomers, includers|
|词性: noun|


excommunicate:/ˌɛkskəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt/
|v.| 革除教籍，逐出教会
|To officially exclude someone from participation in the sacraments and services of the Christian Church.|
|The bishop excommunicated the monk for his heretical beliefs.|主教因僧侣的异端信仰而将他革除教籍。

|They were excommunicated for refusing to follow the church's doctrines.|他们因拒绝遵守教会的教义而被逐出教会。

|近义词：expel, banish, ostracize|
|反义词: welcome, include|
|词性: verb|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *excommunicatus*, past participle of excommunicare "to exclude from communion," from ex- "out of" + communicare "to share, communicate," from communis "common."
