词源：
climate (n.) 14c., "region of the earth," from Old French climat "region, slope of a hill," from Latin clima (genitive climatis) "region; slope of the earth," from Greek klima "region, zone; inclination, slope," from klinein "to slope, to lean," related to klitos "sloping."  Meaning "a district with reference to its prevailing weather conditions" is first recorded 1580s.

-ologist:  word-forming element meaning "one who studies or deals with," from French -ologue and directly from Greek -logos "one who deals with a certain subject," from logos "word, speech, discourse, reason" (see logos).
