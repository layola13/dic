disconsolation:/ˌdɪskɒnsəˈleɪʃn/
n.| 沮丧，失落，伤心|A state of profound sadness or dejection, typically caused by the loss of something or someone valued.|She was overcome with disconsolation after the death of her cat.| 近义词: grief, sorrow, sadness, dejection, despair | 反义词: consolation, comfort, joy, happiness
Etymology: From Late Latin disconsolationem, from disconsolari "to discourage, dishearten," from dis- "not, opposite of" + consolari "to console."
