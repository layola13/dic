gradient: /ˈɡreɪdiənt/ (US), /ˈɡreɪdiənt/ (UK)| n. |梯度，坡度，倾斜度|a rate of inclination; a slope|The cyclist struggled up the steep gradient.  骑自行车的人艰难地爬上陡峭的斜坡。|The temperature gradient between the two liquids caused convection. 两种液体之间的温度梯度引起了对流。| synonyms: slope, incline, grade, slant| antonyms: level, flat|noun

gradient: /ˈɡreɪdiənt/ (US), /ˈɡreɪdiənt/ (UK)| adj. |倾斜的，渐变的|increasing or decreasing by regular degrees or stages|a gradient change in color 颜色渐变的变化|The fabric had a gradient pattern from light to dark. 这块布料有一个从浅到深的渐变图案。|synonyms: graded, sloping, inclined|antonyms: level, flat|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *gradiens, present participle of gradi (to walk, step, go).  Related to "grade" and "graduate."
