macerate: /ˈmæsəreɪt/ (US), /ˈmæsəreɪt/ (UK) | v.  | 浸渍；软化；消瘦 | To soften or break down by soaking in a liquid; to become thin and weak. | The chef macerated the strawberries in sugar and balsamic vinegar.  厨师将草莓浸泡在糖和香醋中。

The body had been macerated in the bog for centuries. 这具尸体在沼泽中浸泡了几个世纪。 | 近义词: soak, steep, soften, weaken | 反义词: harden, strengthen | verb

maceration: /ˌmæsəˈreɪʃn/ (US), /ˌmæsəˈreɪʃn/ (UK) | n. | 浸渍；软化 | The process of softening or breaking down by soaking. | The maceration of the fruit made it perfect for the jam. 水果的浸渍使其非常适合做果酱。

Long maceration can weaken the fibers of the fabric.  长时间的浸渍会削弱织物的纤维。| 近义词: soaking, steeping | 反义词: hardening | noun
