lockstep: /ˈlɑːkstɛp/ (US), /ˈlɒkstɛp/ (UK)| n. | 锁步，亦步亦趋 | A way of marching or acting in which a group of people follow each other closely and do exactly the same thing. | The two countries have been in lockstep on this issue for years. 这两个国家在这个问题上多年来一直步调一致。| synonyms: unison, conformity, synchronization | antonyms: discord, disagreement, divergence | noun

lockstep: /ˈlɑːkstɛp/ (US), /ˈlɒkstɛp/ (UK)| adv. |  锁步地，亦步亦趋地 | In a way that involves following someone or something closely and doing exactly the same thing. | The industry has moved in lockstep with the government's policies.  该行业与政府的政策步调一致。 | synonyms: uniformly, consistently, harmoniously | antonyms: independently, divergently, individually | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "lock" (referring to the fixed position of the feet) + "step"
