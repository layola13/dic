closetful:/ˈklɒzɪtfʊl/ (British), /ˈklɑːzɪtfʊl/ (American)| n. |满满一衣橱; 许多; 大量|A closetful refers to the quantity of something that would fill a closet, often used figuratively to denote a large amount.  It implies having a significant supply of something, typically clothing or other items stored in a closet.| She has a closetful of designer shoes. 她有一衣橱的名牌鞋。|Synonyms: abundance, plethora, lots, heaps|Antonyms: lack, scarcity|noun
