fast-forwarded:/ˌfæst ˈfɔːrwərdɪd/ |adj. v. (past participle)|快进的，快速推进的|Advanced or moved forward quickly in time, often used in the context of media playback or a narrative.  Origin: From the verb "fast-forward," which combines "fast" (meaning quickly) and "forward" (meaning to move ahead).  The original meaning related to tape recorders and video players, but the term has expanded metaphorically to any situation involving rapid advancement.|例句：The movie was fast-forwarded through the boring parts.  这部电影快进了无聊的部分。

例句：She fast-forwarded through the commercials. 她快进了广告。|近义词：advanced, accelerated, expedited, skipped|反义词：rewound, reversed, slowed down|词性: adjective, past participle

例句：He fast-forwarded through the tedious introduction. 他快进了冗长的介绍。|近义词：advance, skip ahead|反义词：rewind, go back|词性: verb
