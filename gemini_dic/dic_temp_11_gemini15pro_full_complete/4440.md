alaunt:/əˈlɔːnt/| adj.| 高高在上地；炫耀地；傲慢地；得意洋洋地|in a way that shows that you are very proud and pleased with yourself and your achievements, especially in a way that annoys other people|She walked in alaunt, clearly pleased with her new outfit. 她趾高气扬地走了进来，显然对她的新衣服很满意。|近义词：proudly, haughtily, triumphantly, boastfully|反义词：humbly, modestly|词性：形容词

词源:
From Middle English *alaunt*, from Old French *alaunt* (“hunting (with hounds)”), present participle of *aler* (“to go”), from Latin *alare*, *amblare* (“to walk, amble”). The meaning shifted from "hunting" to "proud, as if hunting" to "proud, boastful" in general.
