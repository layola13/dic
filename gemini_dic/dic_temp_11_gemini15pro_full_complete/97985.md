inoculation:/ɪˌnɒkjuˈleɪʃn/ /ɪˌnɑːkjuˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 接种; 预防注射; 灌输 | The introduction of a pathogen or antigen into a living organism to stimulate the production of antibodies. |  例句：The doctor recommended annual flu inoculations. 医生建议每年接种流感疫苗。

例句：His inoculation against smallpox was successful. 他的天花疫苗接种很成功。

近义词：vaccination, immunization, injection | 反义词: - | 词性: noun


inoculate:/ɪˈnɒkjʊleɪt/  /ɪˈnɑːkjʊleɪt/ | vt. | 接种; 灌输 | To introduce a microorganism, serum, or vaccine into an organism in order to create immunity to a disease. Also, to introduce an idea or attitude into someone's mind. | 例句：The nurse inoculated the children against measles. 护士给孩子们接种了麻疹疫苗。

例句：He was inoculated with the idea that hard work leads to success. 他被灌输了努力工作就能成功的思想。

近义词：vaccinate, immunize, inject, infuse | 反义词: - | 词性: verb
