mellows:/ˈmeləʊz/ (UK), /ˈmeloʊz/ (US)| v. |(使)成熟,(使)柔和,(使)变得醇厚 |becomes softer and more gentle; becomes ripe|The cheese mellows with age.  这种奶酪随着时间的推移会变得更醇厚。|The autumn sun mellowed the apples on the tree. 秋天的阳光使树上的苹果成熟了。|近义词:soften, ripen, mature, mild, sweeten|反义词:harden, sour, worsen, intensify|词性:动词

mellow:/ˈmeləʊ/ (UK), /ˈmeloʊ/ (US)| adj. |成熟的,柔和的,醇厚的 |soft, gentle, and pleasant; ripe|He has a mellow personality.  他性格温和。|The wine has a mellow flavor. 这酒味道醇厚。|近义词:soft, gentle, pleasant, ripe, mature|反义词:harsh, sharp, immature, unripe|词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *mellow*, from Old English *mearu* (“soft, tender, ripe”), from Proto-Germanic *marwaz* (“soft, decayed”), from Proto-Indo-European *mṛh₂wós* (“soft, tender, weak”). Cognate with Old Frisian *merwe*, Old Saxon *marwi*, Old High German *marawi*, *muruwi* (“soft, ripe”), German *mürbe* (“tender, mellow, friable”), Old Norse *mǫrr* (“soft, tender, rotten”), Gothic *𝗺𝗮𝗿𝘄𝘀* (marws, “soft”).
