mortgage:/ˈmɔːrɡɪdʒ/ (US) /ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ/ (UK)| n. vt.|抵押，抵押贷款；抵押借款|A loan obtained through the conveyance of property as security.|例句：They took out a mortgage to buy their first house. 他们办理了抵押贷款来购买他们的第一套房子。|近义词：loan, lien, security|反义词:redemption|词性:名词, 动词

mortgage:/ˈmɔːrɡɪdʒ/ (US) /ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ/ (UK)| vt.|抵押|To convey or pledge (property) as security for a loan.|例句：They mortgaged their house to start a business. 他们抵押了房子来创业。|近义词：pledge, pawn|反义词:redeem|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mortgaige* "dead pledge," from *mort* "dead" (from Latin *mortuus*, past participle of *mori* "to die") + *gage* "pledge" (from Frankish *wadi* "pledge, wager").  A "dead pledge" because the property was effectively dead to the owner until the loan was repaid.
