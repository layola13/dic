aurochs:/ˈɔːrɒks/ (UK) /ˈɔːrɑːks/ (US)| n.| 欧洲野牛（已灭绝）|A large wild ox (Bos primigenius) that inhabited Europe, Asia, and North Africa and is the ancestor of domestic cattle. It is now extinct.|The cave paintings depict aurochs roaming the prehistoric landscape.  洞穴壁画描绘了在史前景观中漫游的欧洲野牛。

The aurochs, ancestor of modern cattle, became extinct in the 17th century.  欧洲野牛，现代牛的祖先，在17世纪灭绝了。|近义词：wild ox, urus|反义词: (No direct antonym, as it's an extinct species.  Perhaps "domestic cattle" could be considered a contrasting term.) |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From German Auerochse, from Auer (meaning "primitive," "primeval," or referring to a floodplain forest) + Ochse (meaning "ox").  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *ūruz.
