hyperdicrotism:/ˌhaɪpərˈdaɪkrətɪzəm/ | n. | 双重脉搏，重脉 | A condition characterized by an exaggerated dicrotic notch on the pulse tracing, giving the impression of a double pulse beat.  It is typically seen in conditions associated with low peripheral resistance and high cardiac output, such as aortic regurgitation. | The patient's hyperdicrotism was a clear sign of aortic insufficiency. 病人的重脉是主动脉瓣关闭不全的明显迹象。 | 近义词: dicrotism, double pulse | 反义词:  N/A | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From hyper- + dicrotism.  Hyper- is a Greek prefix meaning "over, excessive," and dicrotism refers to a double pulse beat, derived from the Greek di- "two" + krotos "beat."
