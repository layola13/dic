gildable:/ˈɡɪldəbl/| adj.|可镀金的，可装饰的|Something that is gildable can be covered with a thin layer of gold or something that looks like gold.  It can also be used figuratively to describe something that can be made to seem better or more attractive than it really is.|The cheap plastic frame was gildable, so I sprayed it with gold paint.  这个廉价的塑料框可以镀金，所以我给它喷上了金漆。|Synonyms: decorable, embellishable|Antonyms: ungildable, unimprovable|adjective


gildable:/ˈɡɪldəbl/| adj.|可改进的，可润色的|Figuratively, something that can be improved or enhanced.|His reputation, though tarnished, was still gildable. 虽然他的名声已受损，但仍有改进的余地。|Synonyms: improvable, refinable|Antonyms: unimprovable, incorrigible|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From gild + -able.  "Gild" comes from the Old English "gyldan," meaning "to cover with gold."  The suffix "-able" forms adjectives meaning "capable of being."
