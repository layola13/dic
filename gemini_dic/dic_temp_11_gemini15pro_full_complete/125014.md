mugwumpisms:/ˈmʌɡwʌmpɪzəmz/
|n.|清高自诩的行为，骑墙态度|The politician's mugwumpisms alienated both parties, leaving him with little support. 这位政客清高自诩的行为疏远了双方，使他几乎没有支持者。|Mugwumpisms are often seen as a form of political posturing. 清高自诩的行为常被视为一种政治姿态。|近义词：fence-sitting, neutrality, indecision|反义词：partisanship, commitment, decisiveness|词性: 名词


Etymology:
From Mugwump + -ism.  Mugwump, from Natick *mugquomp "important person, kingpin," from mugwomp "war leader."
