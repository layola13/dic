derricking:/ˈdɛrɪkɪŋ/
n.|起重;用起重机吊|The act of lifting or moving something with a derrick. |The derricking of the heavy equipment took several hours.|近义词：hoisting, lifting, craning|反义词:lowering|词性:名词
derricking:/ˈdɛrɪkɪŋ/
v.|用起重机吊|To lift or move something with a derrick.|They are derricking the steel beams into place.|近义词：hoisting, lifting, craning|反义词:lowering|词性:动词

词源:  From the proper noun Derrick, ultimately from Theodoric, a Germanic personal name.  The term likely evolved from the name of a 17th-century hangman, Thomas Derrick, whose name became associated with gallows (a type of crane-like structure).
