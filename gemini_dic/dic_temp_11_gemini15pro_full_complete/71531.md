flapperish:/ˈflæpərɪʃ/| adj.|轻佻的，像轻佻女郎的|Resembling or characteristic of a flapper, a young woman in the 1920s who flouted conventional standards of behavior.|She had a flapperish hairstyle, with bobbed hair and a headband.|近义词：flighty, frivolous, giddy, irresponsible|反义词：serious, demure, reserved|词性:形容词
Etymology: From "flapper" + "-ish". "Flapper" originally referred to a young bird learning to fly, then came to mean a young, unconventional woman, especially in the 1920s.  The suffix "-ish" adds the meaning "like" or "having the qualities of".
