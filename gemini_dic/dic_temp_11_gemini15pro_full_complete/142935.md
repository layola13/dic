out-trot:/ˌaʊtˈtrɒt/| v. | 超越，胜过（尤指在小跑比赛中）| to trot faster than someone or something else, especially in a competition | The young horse easily out-trotted the older ones. 这匹小马轻松地超过了那些老马。| 近义词：outrun, surpass, excel | 反义词：lag behind, fall behind | 词性：动词

out-trot:/ˌaʊtˈtrɒt/| v. |胜过，超过（非速度方面）| to surpass or excel someone in some non-speed related aspect | He out-trotted his rivals in fundraising. 他在筹款方面胜过了他的对手。| 近义词：outdo, outstrip, outperform | 反义词：be outdone, be surpassed | 词性：动词

PHRASE->
Not applicable.  "Out-trot" is not commonly used in phrases.  It's typically used as a standalone verb.

词源: out- + trot.  "Out-" is a prefix meaning "to surpass" and "trot" refers to a gait of a horse or other four-legged animal.  Therefore, "out-trot" literally means to trot faster than another.  The meaning has extended to encompass surpassing someone in any competition or endeavor.
