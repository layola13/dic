lionizing:/ˈlaɪənaɪzɪŋ/
verb
奉为偶像；崇拜；吹捧
treating (someone) as a celebrity.
The media is lionizing the young athlete after his Olympic victory. 媒体在年轻运动员奥运夺冠后将他奉为偶像。
The press lionized her after her novel won the Pulitzer Prize.  在她的小说获得普利策奖后，媒体对她大肆吹捧。
近义词: idolizing, glorifying, celebrating, exalting
反义词: criticizing, condemning, vilifying, disparaging
词性: verb (present participle)

lionize:/ˈlaɪənaɪz/
verb
奉为偶像；崇拜；吹捧
treat (someone) as a celebrity.
They lionized him as a hero. 他们把他奉为英雄。
The fans lionized the rock star. 歌迷们把这位摇滚明星奉为偶像。
近义词: idolize, glorify, celebrate, exalt
反义词: criticize, condemn, vilify, disparage
词性: verb

词源: From lion + -ize.  The original sense was "to treat as a lion," hence "to treat as a celebrity or object of great interest."
