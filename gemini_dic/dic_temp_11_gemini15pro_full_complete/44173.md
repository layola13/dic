criticling: /ˈkrɪtɪsɪzɪŋ/ | n. | 批评；评论；实习期 | The act of evaluating or judging something, often in a negative or corrective way. Specifically refers to the period of articling or apprenticeship for lawyers and other professionals. |  The student's criticling period was challenging but rewarding.  这个学生的实习期充满挑战，但收获颇丰。| synonyms:  evaluating, assessing, reviewing, appraising, judging, analyzing, commenting on | antonyms: praising, lauding, commending, extolling | noun


criticling: /ˈkrɪtɪsɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 批评的；评论的| Relating to or involving criticism or critical analysis. | He offered some criticling remarks on her performance. 他对她的表现提出了一些批评性的意见。 | synonyms: critical, analytical, evaluative, judgmental | antonyms: complimentary, laudatory, praising | adjective


Etymology:  From the verb "criticize," which comes from the French "critiquer" and ultimately from the Greek "kritikos" meaning "able to discern or judge."
