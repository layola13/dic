envoys:/ˈɛnvɔɪz/| n. |使节，外交使者|A person delegated by a government to represent it in another country.|The president sent envoys to discuss the peace treaty.|representatives, ambassadors, delegates, emissaries|opponents, adversaries|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *envoié*, past participle of *envoyer* "to send," from Vulgar Latin *inviare*, from Latin *in via* "on the way."
