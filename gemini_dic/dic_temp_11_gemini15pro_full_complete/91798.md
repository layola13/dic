hyperfederalist:/ˌhaɪpərˈfɛdərəlɪst/| n. adj.|极端联邦主义者；极端联邦主义的|A person who advocates for a strong central government with limited state power, often to an extreme degree.  Someone who believes in maximizing the power of the federal government at the expense of the states.|  The senator's hyperfederalist stance alienated many voters in his home state.  这位参议员极端联邦主义的立场疏远了他家乡州的许多选民。|Synonyms: centralist, nationalist|Antonyms: states' rights advocate, secessionist| Noun, Adjective
