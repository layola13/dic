contriturate: /kɒnˈtrɪtʃʊreɪt/ /kənˈtrɪtʃəˌreɪt/
|v. |研碎，磨碎，捣碎|To reduce to powder or fine particles by pounding, grinding, or rubbing.|The pharmacist contriturated the tablets into a fine powder. 药剂师将药片研磨成细粉。|grind, pulverize, crush, pound|N/A|verb
contriturate: /kɒnˈtrɪtʃʊreɪt/ /kənˈtrɪtʃəˌreɪt/
|n. |研碎物，磨碎物|The product of contrituration; a finely ground substance.|The contriturate was then mixed with a liquid to form a paste.  研磨物随后与液体混合形成糊状物。|powder, dust, fine particles|N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin contrītus (past participle of conterere, “to rub together, grind, bruise”), from com- (“together”) + terere (“to rub”).
