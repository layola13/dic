makeshift:/ˈmeɪkʃɪft/| adj. n.|临时替代的；权宜之计；|Serving as a temporary substitute; improvised for emergency use.|We used a cardboard box as a makeshift table. 我们用一个纸箱子当作临时桌子。|近义词:temporary, improvised, provisional, substitute|反义词:permanent, proper, planned|形容词
makeshift:/ˈmeɪkʃɪft/| n.|权宜之计；临时替代品|A temporary substitute or expedient.|The refugees built makeshifts out of salvaged materials.  难民用抢救出来的材料搭建了临时住所。|近义词:substitute, expedient, improvisation|反义词:original, prepared, planned|名词

词源:  From the verb phrase "make shift" (meaning "to manage with the means at hand"), from "make" + "shift" (resource, expedient).  Dates back to the late 16th century.
