grafters:/ˈɡrɑːftərz/
n.|嫁接者; 努力工作的人; 贪污受贿者|People who work hard, especially at a physically demanding job. Can also refer to someone who takes advantage of their position for personal gain, especially through bribery or corruption.|例句：The grafters on the building site worked tirelessly through the heat.  建筑工地的工人们顶着酷暑不知疲倦地工作着。

例句：The investigation uncovered a network of grafters who had been siphoning off public funds for years. 调查揭露了一个多年来一直盗用公款的贪污受贿网络。|近义词：workers, toilers, hustlers, swindlers, grifters|反义词：slackers, idlers, shirkers|词性：名词

grafter:/ˈɡrɑːftər/
n.|嫁接者; 努力工作的人; 贪污受贿者|A person who grafts (plants) or who works hard, or someone who engages in dishonest practices to make money.|例句：He's a real grafter, always the first to arrive and the last to leave. 他真是个努力工作的人，总是第一个来，最后一个走。

例句：The councilor was exposed as a grafter, accepting bribes for planning permission.  这位议员被揭露是一个贪污受贿者，他收受贿赂以换取规划许可。|近义词：worker, laborer, hustler, cheat, fraudster|反义词：loafer, idler, honest person|词性：名词


词源:  From "graft" (v.) + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting agents).  "Graft" itself comes from the Old French "grafe" meaning a stylus or grafting knife.
