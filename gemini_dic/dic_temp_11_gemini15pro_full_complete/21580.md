bluffed:/blʌft/ | v. | 虚张声势，吓唬，蒙骗 | to deceive someone by pretending to be stronger or more confident or knowledgeable than one is | He bluffed his way through the interview, even though he knew nothing about the job. 他在面试中虚张声势，尽管他对这份工作一无所知。 | He bluffed the guards into thinking he was a police officer. 他骗过警卫，让他们以为他是警察。| deceive, pretend, feign | admit, confess | verb

词源：bluff (n.) "steep cliff, headland," 1670s, probably from Dutch blaf "flat, broad," of unknown origin. The verb meaning "to frighten by a show of strength" is from 1882; the noun meaning "act of bluffing" is from 1889.
