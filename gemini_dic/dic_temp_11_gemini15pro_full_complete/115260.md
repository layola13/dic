manipulative:/məˈnɪpjələtɪv/ | adj. n. | 操控的，爱操纵的；(复数)教具 |  (adj.) characterized by unscrupulous control of a situation or person. (n.) (usually plural) An object designed to be handled in order to demonstrate or discover some mathematical or scientific principle. |  例句：He's a manipulative person who always tries to get his own way. 他是一个爱操纵别人的人，总是想尽办法达到自己的目的。| 近义词：(adj.) controlling, scheming, exploitative (n.)  learning resources, educational tools | 反义词：(adj.) forthright, honest, straightforward | 词性: adjective, noun

manipulatives:/məˈnɪpjələtɪvz/ | n. | 教具；操作材料 | Objects or materials that students can physically handle and move to help them learn mathematical or other concepts. | 例句：The teacher used manipulatives to help the children understand fractions. 老师使用教具帮助孩子们理解分数。| 近义词：learning resources, educational tools, hands-on materials, concrete materials | 反义词：abstract concepts | 词性: noun
