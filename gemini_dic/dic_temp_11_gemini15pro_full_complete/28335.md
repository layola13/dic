caramelize:/ˈkærəməlaɪz/ /ˈkɑːrəməlaɪz/| v. |焦糖化，使变成焦糖|To heat sugar until it becomes brown and develops a characteristic flavor.|She caramelized the onions for the soup. 她把洋葱炒成了焦糖色，用来做汤。|The sugar caramelized quickly over the high heat. 糖在高温下迅速焦糖化。|近义词：brown, glaze|反义词:|词性:动词

caramelization:/ˌkærəmələˈzeɪʃən/ /ˌkɑːrəmələˈzeɪʃən/| n. |焦糖化|The process of heating sugar until it browns and develops a characteristic flavor.|The caramelization of the onions added a depth of flavor to the dish. 洋葱的焦糖化使这道菜的味道更加浓郁。|The slow caramelization of the sugar created a rich, golden syrup.  糖的慢速焦糖化产生了一种浓郁的金黄色糖浆。|近义词：browning|反义词:|词性:名词

caramelized:/ˈkærəməlaɪzd/ /ˈkɑːrəməlaɪzd/| adj. |焦糖化的|Coated or cooked with caramelized sugar.|The caramelized apples were a delicious dessert. 焦糖苹果是一道美味的甜点。|The chef added caramelized onions to the burger.  厨师在汉堡里加了焦糖洋葱。|近义词：browned, glazed|反义词:|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *caraméliser*, from caramel + -iser (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make into" or "to treat with"). Caramel ultimately derives from Spanish *caramelo* (meaning "burnt sugar"), probably from Portuguese *caramelo* or Late Latin *cannamella* (meaning "sugarcane"), from Latin *canna* (meaning "reed, cane") + *mella* (meaning "honey").
