muzzle: /ˈmʌzəl/ (US) /ˈmʌzl/ (UK)| n. vt.|口套，枪口，钳制言论|The part of the face of an animal, such as a dog or horse, including the nose and mouth. Also, the open end of a gun.  Figuratively, it can mean to prevent someone from expressing their opinions.|
The dog's muzzle was covered in mud. 狗的口套上沾满了泥巴。

The hunter carefully cleaned the muzzle of his rifle. 猎人小心翼翼地擦拭着他的步枪的枪口。

The government tried to muzzle the press. 政府试图压制新闻界。

|近义词：snout, nose, gag, silence, suppress|反义词:free, liberate, unleash|词性:noun, verb


muzzle: /ˈmʌzəl/ (US) /ˈmʌzl/ (UK)| vt.|给…戴上口套，钳制…的言论|To put a muzzle on an animal. Also, to prevent someone from expressing their opinions.|
They had to muzzle the dog because it was barking aggressively.  他们不得不给狗戴上口套，因为它一直在狂吠。

Attempts to muzzle dissent were unsuccessful. 压制异议的企图没有成功。

|近义词：gag, silence, suppress|反义词:free, liberate, unleash|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *musel (modern French museau), diminutive of muse "snout, muzzle," of uncertain origin.
