go-to-town:/ˌɡoʊ tə ˈtaʊn/| idiom (phrasal verb)|尽情享受；全力以赴；大肆挥霍；大干一场|To do something with great enthusiasm and energy, often to excess.  It can also refer to spending a lot of money freely.|例句：She really went to town on the decorations for the party.  她为了派会的装饰真是煞费苦心。|近义词：go all out, pull out all the stops, spare no expense|反义词：hold back, skimp, be frugal|词性 idiom (phrasal verb)

go-to-town:/ˌɡoʊ tə ˈtaʊn/| idiom (phrasal verb)|痛快地玩，尽情地表演（尤指演奏爵士乐）|To play or perform with great enthusiasm and energy, especially in jazz music.|例句：The band really went to town during their last set. 乐队在最后一组演奏时真是尽情发挥。|近义词：jam, improvise, let loose|反义词：play it safe, hold back|词性 idiom (phrasal verb)
