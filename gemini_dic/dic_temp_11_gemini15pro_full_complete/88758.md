hock: /hɒk/ (UK) /hɑːk/ (US) | n. |跗关节；蹄膀；典当；困境| The joint bending backwards in the hind leg of a four-legged animal, especially a horse, or the corresponding part of a bird's leg. Also, a cut of meat from the lower part of an animal's leg, typically a pig. Can also refer to the act of pawning something or a state of difficulty. | The horse injured its hock in the race.  这匹马在比赛中伤了跗关节。

The butcher prepared the hock for roasting. 肉贩准备好了烤蹄膀。

She had to hock her jewelry to pay the rent. 她不得不典当她的珠宝来付房租。

He found himself in hock to the loan sharks. 他发现自己欠了高利贷。 |近义词:  joint, gammon (for meat), pawn, predicament |反义词: None (for anatomical meaning). redeem (for pawning) |词性: noun
