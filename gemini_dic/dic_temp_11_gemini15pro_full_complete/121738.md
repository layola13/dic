misproportion:/ˌmɪsprəˈpɔːrʃən/ | n. | 不成比例，比例失调 | lack of proper proportion; imbalance |  The painting suffered from a misproportion of the figures. 这幅画中的人物比例失调。| disproportion, imbalance |  proportion, balance | noun


misproportion:/ˌmɪsprəˈpɔːrʃən/ | vt. | 使不成比例，使比例失调 | to make something out of proportion |  The artist misproportioned the head in relation to the body.  这位艺术家把头部与身体的比例弄错了。|  distort, warp | balance, harmonize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (wrong, bad) + proportion (ratio, balance).  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old French "mes-" (wrongly), ultimately from a Germanic source. "Proportion" comes from Old French and Latin "proportio" (comparative relation, analogy), from "pro" (for, in front of) + "portio" (share, part).
