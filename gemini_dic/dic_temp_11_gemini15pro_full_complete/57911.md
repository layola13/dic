drill:/drɪl/| n. v.|钻头，训练，演习；钻孔，训练，操练|A tool or machine used for making holes.  A way of teaching people something by making them repeat it or practise it many times.  An exercise, especially a military exercise.|The dentist used a drill to clean my teeth. 牙医用钻头清洁我的牙齿。

The soldiers performed drills on the parade ground. 士兵们在阅兵场上进行操练。

The teacher made us do spelling drills every day. 老师让我们每天做拼写练习。|近义词：exercise, practice, training, borer, auger|反义词: neglect, idleness|noun

drill:/drɪl/| v.|钻孔，训练，操练|To make a hole in something using a drill. To teach someone something by making them repeat it or practise it many times.|He drilled a hole in the wall for the shelf. 他在墙上钻了一个洞来放架子。

The coach drilled the team on their defensive plays. 教练让队员们反复练习防守战术。|近义词：bore, pierce, train, practice|反义词: neglect|verb
