illaudable:/ˌɪlˈɔːdəbəl/| adj.|不值得称赞的，应受谴责的|Not praiseworthy; deserving of blame or censure.  Derived from the French "illaudable," from the Latin "illaudabilis," meaning "not praiseworthy."  The prefix "il-" signifies negation, and "laudabilis" means "praiseworthy."|His illaudable behavior resulted in his expulsion from school. 他的应受谴责的行为导致他被学校开除。|Synonyms: blameworthy, censurable, reprehensible, disgraceful, shameful|Antonyms: laudable, praiseworthy, commendable, admirable|adjective
