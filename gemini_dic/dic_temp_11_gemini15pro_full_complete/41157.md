copulatively:/ˈkɒpjʊlətɪvli/ /ˈkɑːpjʊlətɪvli/| adv.|连接地，联合地|in a way that connects clauses or sentences with conjunctions, especially "and."  It means joining things together, often in a series or list, emphasizing the combination or accumulation.|He listed the ingredients copulatively: flour, sugar, butter, and eggs. 他连接地列出了配料：面粉、糖、黄油和鸡蛋。|Synonyms: jointly, conjunctively, connectedly|Antonyms: disjunctively, separately|Adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *copulativus, from Latin copulat-, past participle stem of copulare "to join, couple, link, unite,"  from copula "a link, tie, bond."
