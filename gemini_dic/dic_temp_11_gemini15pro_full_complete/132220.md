nondecadents:/ˌnɒndɪˈkeɪdənts/
n.|非颓废者|People who are not decadent; those who do not indulge in excessive pleasure or luxury.|The nondecadents among the artists focused on simple and meaningful themes.|近义词:puritans, ascetics, moralists|反义词:decadents, hedonists|词性:名词

Etymology:
non- (prefix): Latin, meaning "not"
decadent (adjective/noun): From French décadent, from Latin decadentem, present participle of decadere "to fall down, decay," from de- "down" + cadere "to fall."
