bunks:/bʌŋks/| n.|铺位，床铺(通常指双层床的下铺); 躲藏处; 逃匿; (尤指儿童)睡的地方|a narrow bed that is fixed to a wall, especially on a ship or train, or in a prison.  Often used in the plural to refer to a place to sleep.|The children slept in bunks.|近义词：beds, berths, cots|反义词:|noun

bunk:/bʌŋk/| n.|废话，胡说|nonsense; something that is not true|That's a load of bunk!|近义词：nonsense, rubbish, baloney|反义词：truth, fact|noun

bunk:/bʌŋk/| vi.|逃学，旷课; 擅离职守; 躲藏|to stay away from school or work without permission; to sleep somewhere, especially in a temporary or makeshift bed|He bunked off school to go to the football match.|近义词：skip, dodge, play truant|反义词：attend|verb

bunk:/bʌŋk/| vt.|提供铺位; 安排…睡在铺位上|to provide someone with a bed, especially a bunk|The crew were bunked down below deck.|近义词：accommodate, house, lodge|反义词:|verb
