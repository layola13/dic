fudging:/ˈfʌdʒɪŋ/ | v. | 欺骗；敷衍；捏造；回避 |  acting dishonestly or evasively, especially in order to avoid something unpleasant. | He was fudging the figures to make the company look more profitable. 他篡改数字，让公司看起来更有利可图。 |  He fudged his answer to avoid getting into trouble. 他含糊其辞地回答以避免惹上麻烦。 | 近义词: cheating, faking, dodging, evading | 反义词: being honest, being truthful | 词性: 动词

fudge:/fʌdʒ/ | n. | 软糖 | a type of soft creamy candy. |  I love chocolate fudge. 我喜欢巧克力软糖。| She brought a box of fudge to the party. 她带了一盒软糖去参加聚会。 | 近义词: toffee, caramel | 反义词: (No direct antonym for a type of candy) | 词性: 名词

词源:  "fudge" (n.)  likely originated in the 17th century, perhaps as an imitative word. Its meaning as a verb ("to fake or cheat") developed later, probably influenced by the idea of something being soft or pliable, like fudge candy.
