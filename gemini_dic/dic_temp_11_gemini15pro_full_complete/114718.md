malleolar:/məˈliːələ(r)/
adjective
踝的；有关踝的
Relating to the malleolus (the bony prominence on either side of the ankle).
The patient presented with malleolar pain after twisting his ankle. 病人在扭伤脚踝后出现了踝关节疼痛。

malleolar:/məˈliːələ(r)/
noun
踝部
The region of the malleolus.  (This usage is less common than the adjectival form)
Trauma to the malleolar can result in significant pain and immobility. 对踝部的创伤会导致严重的疼痛和行动不便。


Etymology:
From Late Latin malleolus "small hammer," diminutive of malleus "hammer,"  because the bony prominences resemble small hammers.  The anatomical sense emerged in the 16th century.


Synonyms (for adjective):
ankle, perimalleolar

Antonyms (for adjective):  (No direct antonyms exist.  One could consider words relating to other parts of the leg, but these wouldn't be true opposites.)
