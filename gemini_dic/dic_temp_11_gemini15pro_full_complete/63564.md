envies:/ˈɛnviːz/
n.|羡慕，嫉妒|A feeling of discontent or covetousness with regard to another's advantages, success, possessions, etc.|She felt a pang of envy when she saw her friend's new car. 她看到朋友的新车时感到一阵嫉妒。|synonyms: jealousy, covetousness, resentment|antonyms: admiration, goodwill|noun

envy:/ˈɛnvi/
n.|羡慕，嫉妒|A feeling of discontent or covetousness with regard to another's advantages, success, possessions, etc.|He couldn't hide his envy of his brother's promotion. 他无法掩饰对哥哥升职的羡慕。|synonyms: jealousy, covetousness, resentment|antonyms: admiration, goodwill|noun

envy:/ˈɛnvi/
vt.|羡慕，嫉妒|To feel discontent or covetousness toward (someone) because of their advantages, success, possessions, etc.|I envy your ability to speak so many languages. 我羡慕你能说这么多语言。|synonyms: be jealous of, covet, begrudge|antonyms: admire, respect|verb
