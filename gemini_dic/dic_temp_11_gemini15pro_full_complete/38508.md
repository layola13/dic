conciliating: /kənˈsɪlieɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 安抚的，调解的 | Intended to placate or pacify.  Having an effect of soothing or calming anger or distrust. |  Her conciliating tone helped to diffuse the tense situation. 她安抚的语气有助于缓和紧张的局势。 | synonyms: appeasing, pacifying, mollifying, soothing, placatory | antonyms:  antagonistic, provocative, inflammatory, aggravating | adjective

conciliate: /kənˈsɪlieɪt/ | verb | 安抚，调解 | To stop someone from being angry or discontented; placate; to gain (esteem, good will, or the like) by pleasing acts. | He tried to conciliate his angry wife with flowers. 他试图用鲜花来安抚他愤怒的妻子。 | synonyms:  appease, placate, mollify, pacify, soothe | antonyms:  antagonize, provoke, inflame, aggravate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin conciliatus, past participle of conciliare ("to bring together, unite, win over"), from Latin concilium ("council").
