fogyism:/ˈfoʊɡiˌɪzəm/| n.|老年人歧视；守旧；|Discrimination or prejudice against older people, especially based on the belief that they are outdated or resistant to change.  It can also refer to excessively conservative or old-fashioned attitudes and behaviors.|例句：The company's promotion policies reflect blatant fogyism. 这公司的晋升政策明显反映出对老年人的歧视。

例句：His fogyism prevents him from appreciating new music. 他的守旧思想使他无法欣赏新的音乐。|近义词：ageism, conservatism, traditionalism|反义词:modernism, progressivism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From fogy (meaning "old-fashioned person") + -ism (suffix forming nouns denoting doctrines, practices, or characteristics).  Fogy likely originated as a shortening of fogram, a dialectal term for an old man, possibly related to the obsolete fogram, meaning "a dull, inactive fellow."
