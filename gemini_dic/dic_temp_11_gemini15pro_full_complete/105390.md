kidding:/ˈkɪdɪŋ/| adj. n.|开玩笑的，戏弄；开玩笑，戏弄|Used to say that something you have just said is not true or serious, or to tell someone that something is not true or serious.|例句：You're kidding! 你在开玩笑吧！|近义词：joking, teasing, jesting|反义词：serious, earnest|词性：形容词

kidding:/ˈkɪdɪŋ/| n.|开玩笑，戏弄|The action of saying something that is not true as a joke.|例句：No kidding! 不是开玩笑吧！|近义词：joking, teasing, jesting|反义词：seriousness, earnestness|词性：名词

词源：From kid (v.) "to tease, jest," 1590s, which itself possibly comes from a Scandinavian source (compare dialectal Norwegian kida "to tickle, to make small movements," kidda "young goat"), or from a Middle Dutch or Middle Low German word related to kid (n.).
