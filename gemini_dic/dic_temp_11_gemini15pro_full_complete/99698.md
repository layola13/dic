interlocking:/ˌɪntərˈlɒkɪŋ/ (US) /ˌɪntəˈlɒkɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|联锁的，互相扣合的，紧密相连的|Joined together firmly, like the parts of a puzzle; forming a whole by fitting together.|The toy bricks have interlocking pieces.  这些积木的零件是互相扣合的。|
interlocking:/ˌɪntərˈlɒkɪŋ/ (US) /ˌɪntəˈlɒkɪŋ/ (UK)| n.|联锁，互相扣合|The act or process of interlocking, or the state of being interlocked.|The interlocking of the branches made it difficult to climb the tree. 树枝的联锁使得爬树很困难。|
interlocking: /ˌɪntərˈlɒkɪŋ/ (US) /ˌɪntəˈlɒkɪŋ/ (UK)|adj.|连锁的，关联的|Closely related or connected in such a way that each part affects the others.|The company's interlocking departments ensure smooth operation.  该公司相互关联的部门确保了平稳运行。|


近义词:interconnected, intertwined, linked, joined, connected
反义词:separate, detached, independent, disconnected
词性: adjective, noun (rarely)
词源: From inter- + lock + -ing.  Inter- means "between" or "among," lock refers to fastening or joining, and -ing forms the present participle and adjective.
