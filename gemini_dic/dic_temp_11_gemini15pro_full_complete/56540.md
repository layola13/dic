dominatingly:/ˈdɒmɪneɪtɪŋli/ /ˈdɑːmɪneɪtɪŋli/
| adv. | 支配地，统治地，盛气凌人地 | In a way that shows control or power over others; overwhelmingly. |  She dominatingly controlled the conversation, barely letting anyone else speak. 她以一种盛气凌人的方式控制着谈话，几乎不让其他人说话。|
| The team dominatingly won the championship, scoring twice as many points as their opponents.  该队以压倒性的优势赢得了冠军，得分是对手的两倍。|
| 近义词: overwhelmingly, powerfully, forcefully, authoritatively, imperiously |
| 反义词: submissively, passively, meekly, humbly |
| 词性: 副词 (adverb) |


dominate:/ˈdɒmɪneɪt/ /ˈdɑːmɪneɪt/
| v. | 支配，控制，统治，占优势 | To have control or power over someone or something; to be the most important or noticeable feature of something. | The company dominates the market for electric cars.  这家公司在电动汽车市场占据主导地位。 |
| He dominated the conversation, not letting anyone else get a word in edgewise. 他控制了谈话，不让别人插嘴。 |
| 近义词: control, govern, rule, command, lead |
| 反义词: submit, yield, follow |
| 词性: 动词 (verb) |


dominant:/ˈdɒmɪnənt/ /ˈdɑːmɪnənt/
| adj. | 占优势的，支配的，统治的，显著的 | Having power and influence over others; most important, powerful, or influential. | The dominant male in the group gets first choice of food.  群体中占优势的雄性可以优先选择食物。|
| The dominant feature of the landscape is the huge mountain.  这片土地上最显著的特征是那座巨大的山峰。 |
| 近义词: controlling, governing, ruling, leading, main, chief, primary |
| 反义词: subordinate, secondary, minor |
| 词性: 形容词 (adjective) |


词源:  From Latin "dominari" (to rule, govern), from "dominus" (lord, master).
