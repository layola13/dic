knockdown:/ˈnɒkdaʊn/ (UK) /ˈnɑːkdaʊn/ (US)| n. adj.|击倒，拆卸，便宜货|A forceful blow that knocks someone down; something (like a piece of furniture) designed to be easily assembled and disassembled; something sold very cheaply.|The boxer delivered a powerful knockdown in the third round.  拳击手在第三回合给了对手有力的一击。|Synonyms: knockout, demolition, bargain|Antonyms: buildup, construction, expensive|noun

knockdown:/ˈnɒkdaʊn/ (UK) /ˈnɑːkdaʊn/ (US)| adj.|可拆卸的，组装的|Designed to be easily assembled and disassembled.|We bought a knockdown bookshelf for our new apartment. 我们为新公寓买了一个可拆卸的书架。|Synonyms:collapsible, dismountable|Antonyms: fixed, permanent|adjective
