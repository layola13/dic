hanger-on:/ˈhæŋər ɒn/ (US) /ˈhæŋər ɒn/ (UK)| n. |奉承者，攀附者，食客|A person who associates with another person, especially a wealthy or influential one, in a way that is perceived as parasitic or overly dependent.|He has a reputation for attracting hangers-on who hope to benefit from his success. 他因吸引那些希望从他的成功中获益的奉承者而闻名。|Synonyms: follower, leech, sycophant, parasite, sponge |Antonyms: leader, independent |noun


hanger-on:/ˈhæŋər ɒn/ (US) /ˈhæŋər ɒn/ (UK)| n. |追随者，(尤指)一伙人中的不重要人物|A relatively unimportant person who follows around the members of a group.|She was a hanger-on in the fashion world, hoping to become a designer. 她是时尚界的追随者，希望成为一名设计师。|Synonyms: follower, groupie |Antonyms: leader, independent |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "hang on" meaning to linger or remain attached.  The noun form developed in the 17th century.
