nonne:/ˈnɒneɪ/ (UK) /ˈnɑːneɪ/ (US)| adv.|难道不？不是吗？|Used in questions expecting an affirmative answer, equivalent to "is it not so?" or "isn't it true?". It implies that the speaker believes the answer to be yes.|Nonne Romulus et Remus urbem Romam condiderunt?  Didn't Romulus and Remus found the city of Rome?|近义词:num, isn't it true?, is it not so?|反义词:None (as a direct antonym is not applicable, as "nonne" seeks confirmation, not negation).|Adverb

Since it's difficult to provide phrases with "nonne" that are commonly used today, here are examples of how you might see it incorporated into slightly longer expressions:


Nonne hoc verum est?  Isn't this true? (Similar to "Isn't that the truth?")

Nonne id mirum est? Isn't that strange? (Similar to "Isn't that odd?")

Nonne bene intellegis? Don't you understand well? (Rhetorical, expecting agreement)

Nonne mecum venies? Won't you come with me? (A polite invitation with an expectation of acceptance)

Nonne hoc iustum est? Isn't this fair? (Rhetorical, implying that something is indeed fair)


注意事项:

"Nonne" is primarily used in formal or literary contexts, particularly in Latin.  It is rarely encountered in modern spoken or informal written Latin.
