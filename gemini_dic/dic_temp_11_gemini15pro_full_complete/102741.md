janizary: /ˈdʒænɪsəri/ | n. | 近卫军，禁卫军 (尤指旧时土耳其或其他一些回教国家的) | A member of the Turkish infantry forming the Sultan's guard between the 14th and 19th centuries.  They were known for their discipline and loyalty, but also for their occasional political influence and rebellions. |  The janizaries were a formidable fighting force in the Ottoman Empire.  土耳其禁卫军是奥斯曼帝国一支强大的战斗力量。

The janizaries' revolt threatened the stability of the empire.  禁卫军的叛乱威胁到帝国的稳定。 | 近义词：guardsman, soldier, elite troop | 反义词：civilian, rebel (in some contexts) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *janissaire*, from Turkish *yeniçeri*, meaning "new soldier" or "new troop".  The word *yeni* means "new" and *çeri* means "troop" or "soldier."
