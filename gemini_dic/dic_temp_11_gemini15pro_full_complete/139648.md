occultist: /əˈkʌltɪst/ | n. | 神秘学家，玄学家 | A person who studies or practices occult arts, such as magic, alchemy, or astrology. | 例句：The occultist claimed to be able to communicate with the dead.  这位神秘学家声称能够与死者交流。

例句：The occultist predicted the future by reading tarot cards.  这位神秘学家通过解读塔罗牌来预测未来。| 近义词：mystic, esoteric, magician, wizard, sorcerer, witch | 反义词：scientist, skeptic, rationalist | 词性: noun

occult: /əˈkʌlt/ | adj. | 神秘的，玄奥的 |  Of, relating to, or dealing with supernatural influences, agencies, or phenomena. | 例句：The occult practices of the group were shrouded in secrecy.  该团体的玄奥仪式笼罩在神秘之中。

例句：She has a strong interest in occult subjects. 她对神秘事物有着浓厚的兴趣。| 近义词：hidden, secret, esoteric, mystical, supernatural | 反义词：overt, known, public, scientific, rational | 词性: adjective

词源: From Late Latin occultista, from Latin occultus “hidden, secret,” past participle of occulere “to cover over, conceal.”
