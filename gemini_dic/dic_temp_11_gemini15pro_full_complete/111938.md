lollers:/ˈlɒləz/ (British) /ˈlɑːlərz/ (American)| n.|傻笑的人；游手好闲的人；异端|People who loll about, especially in a lazy or indecent way; historically, also used to refer to the Lollards, a pre-Reformation Christian religious movement in England.|The lollers gathered in the tavern, drinking ale and telling bawdy jokes. 那些游手好闲的人聚集在酒馆里，喝着麦芽酒，讲着下流的笑话。|Synonyms: idlers, loafers, loungers|Antonyms: workers, busybodies|Noun
