grounding:/ˈɡraʊndɪŋ/ | n. | 接地；基础；根据；停飞；禁足 | The act of connecting something to the earth, either electrically or for stability; a basis or foundation for something; the act of preventing an aircraft from flying; a punishment for a child in which they are not allowed to go out. |  The electrician checked the grounding of the electrical system. 电工检查了电气系统的接地。The grounding for his argument was shaky. 他的论点的基础不稳固。 The airline announced the grounding of all its flights due to bad weather.  由于天气恶劣，该航空公司宣布所有航班停飞。 He was given a grounding for staying out too late. 他因为在外面待得太晚而被禁足。 | synonyms: earthing, foundation, basis, grounding, confinement | antonyms:  flight, release, liberation | noun

ground:/ɡraʊnd/ | n. | 地面；土地；场所；根据；立场 | The solid surface of the earth; an area of land used for a particular purpose; a reason, basis, or justification; a position or point of view. | The children were playing on the ground. 孩子们在地上玩耍。 The company owns a large ground for its factory.  这家公司拥有一大片工厂用地。 He had no ground for complaint. 他没有理由抱怨。 He stood his ground despite the opposition. 尽管遭到反对，他还是坚持自己的立场。 | synonyms: earth, soil, land, territory, basis, reason | antonyms: sky, air | noun

ground:/ɡraʊnd/ | v. | 使搁浅；使停飞；基于；打基础 | To cause a ship or aircraft to run aground; to prevent an aircraft from flying; to base something on; to establish a basis for. | The ship was grounded on a reef. 这艘船搁浅在礁石上。 The pilot was forced to ground the plane due to engine trouble.  由于发动机故障，飞行员被迫将飞机停飞。  His theory is grounded in scientific research. 他的理论基于科学研究。  She grounded her argument in historical facts.  她将自己的论点建立在历史事实上。 | synonyms: beach, strand, earth, base, establish | antonyms: launch, fly | verb
