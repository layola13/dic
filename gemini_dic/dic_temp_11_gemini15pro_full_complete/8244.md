anti-incumbents:/ˌænti-ɪnˈkʌmbənts/| n.| 反现任者；挑战现任者的人（通常指政治选举中）|  A person who opposes an incumbent in an election.  It refers to a candidate who is running against a current officeholder. |  The anti-incumbents capitalized on public dissatisfaction with the current administration.  挑战现任者的人利用了公众对现任政府的不满。

The election saw a surge of support for anti-incumbents across the country. 这次选举见证了全国各地对挑战现任者的人的支持激增。 | 近义词：challenger, opponent | 反义词：incumbent | 词性: 名词

anti-incumbent:/ˌænti-ɪnˈkʌmbənt/| adj.| 反现任的；反对现任者的 |  Opposing or challenging an incumbent. |  There was a strong anti-incumbent sentiment among voters. 选民中存在着强烈的反现任情绪。

The anti-incumbent movement gained momentum as the economy faltered. 随着经济衰退，反现任运动获得了动力。 | 近义词：oppositional | 反义词：pro-incumbent | 词性: 形容词
