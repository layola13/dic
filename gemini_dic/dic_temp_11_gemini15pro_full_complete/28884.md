carpooling:/ˈkɑːrpuːlɪŋ/ | n. |拼车，合伙用车|The practice of sharing a car with other people, especially for commuting to work or school. |例句：Carpooling can save money and reduce traffic congestion. 拼车可以省钱并减少交通拥堵。

例句：They formed a carpooling group to reduce their carbon footprint. 他们组成了一个拼车小组以减少他们的碳足迹。|近义词：ride-sharing, car sharing, lift sharing|反义词:driving alone, solo driving|词性:noun

carpool:/ˈkɑːrpuːl/ | v. |拼车，合伙用车|To travel to work or school together in a car, sharing the costs and often taking turns driving.|例句：We carpool to work to save on gas. 我们拼车上班以节省汽油。

例句：They decided to carpool to the concert. 他们决定拼车去音乐会。|近义词：ride-share, share a ride|反义词:drive alone|词性:verb

Etymology:  A blend of *car* and *pool* (in the sense of "a common fund").  The term gained popularity in the 1970s during the oil crisis.
