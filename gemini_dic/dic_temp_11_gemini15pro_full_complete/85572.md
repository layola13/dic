hates:/heɪts/ | v. | 憎恨，讨厌 | feels intense dislike or disgust for | She hates spiders. 她讨厌蜘蛛。 | He hates getting up early. 他讨厌早起。 |  loathe, despise, detest, abhor | love, adore, like | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *haten*, from Old English *hatian* (“to hate, persecute, treat with enmity”), from Proto-Germanic *hatōną* (“to hate”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱad-/*(e)ḱoh₁d- (“to be angry, hate”). Cognate with Scots hait (“to hate”), West Frisian hatije (“to hate”), Dutch haten (“to hate”), German hassen (“to hate”), Danish hade (“to hate”), Swedish hata (“to hate”), Icelandic hata (“to hate”).
