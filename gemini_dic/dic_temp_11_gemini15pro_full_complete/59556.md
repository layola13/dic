eavesdrops:/ˈiːvzdrɒps/ (British) /ˈiːvzdrɑːps/ (American)| v.| 偷听，窃听 | Secretly listen to a conversation.|  He eavesdropped on their conversation from behind the door. 他躲在门后偷听他们的谈话.| 近义词：overhear, listen in, snoop | 反义词: ignore, disregard | 词性: 动词

eavesdropping:/ˈiːvzdrɒpɪŋ/ (British) /ˈiːvzdrɑːpɪŋ/ (American)| n.| 偷听，窃听 | The act of secretly listening to a conversation.| Eavesdropping is considered rude and disrespectful. 偷听被认为是粗鲁和不尊重的行为. | 近义词：listening in, snooping | 反义词: - | 词性: 名词

词源:  From "eavesdrip," the dripping of water from the eaves of a house.  Someone standing under the eaves to avoid the drip could overhear conversations inside. 源于“eavesdrip”，指水从屋檐滴落。站在屋檐下躲雨的人可以听到里面的谈话。
