cook:/kʊk/ (UK) /kʊk/ (US)| n. v.|厨师，烹调|A person who prepares and cooks food.  To prepare food by heating it.|
例句：
My mom is a great cook.  我妈妈是一位很棒的厨师。

She cooked a delicious meal for us. 她为我们做了一顿美味的佳肴。

近义词：chef, prepare, make|反义词:eat, consume|词性:noun, verb

PHRASE->
cook something up:  策划，编造
例句：He cooked up a story about missing his train. 他编造了一个错过火车的谎言。

cook the books:  做假账
例句：They were accused of cooking the books to hide their losses.  他们被指控做假账以掩盖损失。

cook someone's goose:  毁掉某人的计划
例句：His constant lateness cooked his goose with his boss.  他经常迟到，毁了他和老板的关系。

what's cooking?:  怎么了？/发生什么事了？
例句：What's cooking? Why is everyone so excited?  发生什么事了？为什么大家都这么兴奋？

too many cooks spoil the broth: 人多坏事
例句：With so many people giving different instructions, it felt like too many cooks spoil the broth.  这么多人给出不同的指示，感觉就像人多坏事。

词源：From Old English *cōc (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *kōkaz (compare West Frisian koek, Dutch kok, German Koch), from Proto-Indo-European *pekʷ- (“to cook, ripen”).
