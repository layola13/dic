accompliceship: /əˈkɒmplɪsʃɪp/
n.
共犯关系；同谋关系
The state of being an accomplice; partnership or involvement in wrongdoing.
He denied any accompliceship in the robbery. 他否认参与了这起抢劫案。
近义词: collaboration, collusion, conspiracy, partnership in crime
反义词: innocence, non-involvement
词性: 名词

Etymology:
From accomplice + -ship.  Accomplice comes from French complice, from Late Latin complex, meaning "partner in crime," from Latin complic-, stem of complicare "to fold together," from com- "together" + plicare "to fold."  The suffix -ship denotes "state or condition of being."
