discomfit:/dɪsˈkʌmfɪt/ | vt. | 使狼狈；使难堪；使窘迫；挫败 | To frustrate the plans of; to thwart or confuse. |  The sudden question discomfited her, and she stammered a reply. 突如其来的问题让她感到尴尬，她结结巴巴地回答。| Synonyms: embarrass, fluster, disconcert, rattle, confound | Antonyms: comfort, reassure, encourage | verb

discomfiture:/dɪsˈkʌmfɪtʃər/ | n. |  狼狈；难堪；窘迫 | A feeling of unease or embarrassment; awkwardness. |  His discomfiture was evident as he fumbled for his notes.  当他慌乱地翻找笔记时，他的窘迫显而易见。| Synonyms: embarrassment, unease, awkwardness, confusion | Antonyms: composure, poise, self-assurance | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French desconfit (past participle of desconfire "to defeat, undo"), from des- "un-, not" + confit "prepared, made ready," past participle of confire "to accomplish, prepare." Ultimately from Latin conficere "to accomplish, complete."
