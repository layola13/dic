oliva:/əˈliːvə/ | n. | 橄榄；橄榄树；橄榄色；橄榄形物 | An olive is a small, oval fruit with a hard stone and a bitter taste. It is usually green when unripe and turns black or purple when ripe. Olives are often preserved in brine or oil and are used in cooking, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine. They are also the source of olive oil. | The chef added a few olives to the salad. 厨师在沙拉里加了几颗橄榄。|Synonyms: olive tree, olive fruit | Antonyms: N/A | noun

olivaceous:/ˌɒlɪˈveɪʃəs/ | adj. | 橄榄色的；橄榄绿的 | Of a dusky yellowish-green colour like that of an unripe olive. | The bird had olivaceous plumage. 这只鸟有橄榄色的羽毛。| Synonyms: olive-green, olive-drab, olive-colored | Antonyms: N/A | adjective
