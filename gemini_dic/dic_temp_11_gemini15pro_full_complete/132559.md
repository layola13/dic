nondialectics:/ˌnɒndaɪəˈlɛktɪks/ | n. |非辩证法 | The theory or practice that rejects or ignores dialectics; the absence or denial of dialectical processes.  Nondialectics often views things as static and unchanging, rather than in a state of constant development and interaction. | Some critics argue that his philosophy embraces nondialectics and thus fails to account for historical change.  一些评论家认为他的哲学包含非辩证法，因此无法解释历史变化。|Near Synonyms:  non-dialectical approach, anti-dialectics, static view, ahistorical perspective | Antonyms: dialectics, dialectical materialism, historical materialism | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + dialectics.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of." "Dialectics" comes from the Greek "dialektikē (tekhnē)," meaning "(the art of) logical argumentation."
