glissando:/ɡlɪˈsændəʊ/ (UK), /ɡlɪˈsɑːndoʊ/ (US)| n.|滑音，滑奏|A rapid slide up or down the musical scale. |The pianist finished the piece with a delicate glissando. 钢琴家用一段轻柔的滑音结束了这首曲子。|Synonyms: slide, sweep, run|Antonyms: staccato|noun


glissando:/ɡlɪˈsændəʊ/ (UK), /ɡlɪˈsɑːndoʊ/ (US)| adv.|滑音地，滑奏地|In a gliding manner. |She played the passage glissando. 她滑奏地演奏了这段乐句。|Synonyms: smoothly, slidingly|Antonyms: abruptly, jerkily|adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *glissando*, present participle of *glissare* "to glide," from French *glisser*. Ultimately from Frankish *glitsan*, from Proto-Germanic *glitsaną*.
