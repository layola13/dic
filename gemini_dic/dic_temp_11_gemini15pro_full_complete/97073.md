inferno:/ɪnˈfɜːrnoʊ/ (US) /ɪnˈfɜːnəʊ/ (UK)| n.|地狱；烈火；阴间；炼狱|A large fire that is very difficult to control, or a place that looks or feels like this.  A situation that causes a lot of suffering.|The whole city was an inferno.|近义词: hell, blaze, conflagration, firestorm|反义词: paradise, heaven|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *inferno*, from Latin *īnfernus* (“below, underground, of the lower world”), from *īnferus* (“low”), from *īnfer* (“below”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*h₁én-dheros*, comparative form of *\*h₁én-dher* (“under”).
