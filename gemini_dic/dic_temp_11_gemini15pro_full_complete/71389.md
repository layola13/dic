flagpole:/ˈflæɡpoʊl/ (US) /ˈflæɡpəʊl/ (UK)| n. |旗杆|A tall pole on which a flag is flown.|The flagpole stood tall against the blue sky. 旗杆高高耸立在蓝天下。|The American flag waved proudly from the flagpole. 美国国旗在旗杆上骄傲地飘扬。|Synonyms: flagstaff, mast|Antonyms: None|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From *flag* + *pole*.  *Flag* comes from Middle English *flagge*, of Scandinavian origin, related to *flagga* (to flap). *Pole* comes from Old English *pāl*, of Germanic origin, related to Dutch *paal* and German *Pfahl*.
