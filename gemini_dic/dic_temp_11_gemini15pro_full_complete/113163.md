lupuline:/ˈluːpjʊlɪn/ | n. | 蛇麻素; 啤酒花苦味酸 |  A yellowish, bitter powder found in the cones of the hop plant, used primarily as a flavoring and bittering agent in beer.  It contains resins and essential oils that contribute to the aroma, flavor, and bitterness of beer. | The lupuline glands in hops are what give beer its characteristic bitterness. 啤酒花中的蛇麻腺赋予了啤酒特有的苦味。 | synonyms: hop resin, hop powder | antonyms: None | noun

lupulin:/ˈluːpjʊlɪn/ | n. | 蛇麻素; 啤酒花苦味酸 | Same as lupuline.  Sometimes used interchangeably, though lupuline is generally preferred. |  Brewers carefully consider the lupulin content when selecting hops. 酿酒师在选择啤酒花时会仔细考虑蛇麻素的含量。 | synonyms: hop resin, hop powder | antonyms: None | noun
