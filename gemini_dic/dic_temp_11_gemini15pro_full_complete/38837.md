confabulation:/kənˌfæbjuˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 虚构，编造；闲谈 | the act of creating and remembering false experiences, often as a result of brain damage |  He suffers from confabulation as a result of his head injury. 他因头部受伤而患有虚构症。 | synonyms: fabrication, invention, falsehood, delusion | antonyms: truth, reality, fact | noun

confabulate:/kənˈfæbjuleɪt/ | v. | 虚构，编造；闲聊 | to have a conversation; to create and remember false experiences | He began to confabulate stories about his childhood. 他开始编造关于他童年的故事。 | synonyms: chat, converse, fabricate, invent | antonyms:  be silent, be quiet | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin confabulatio, from Latin confabulārī ("to chat, talk together"), from con- ("together") + fabulārī ("to talk, tell stories"), from fabula ("story, tale").
