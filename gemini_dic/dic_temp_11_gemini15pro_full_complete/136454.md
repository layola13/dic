nonreversiblenesses:/ˌnɒnˌrɪˈvɜːsɪbəlˈnɛsɪz/ /ˌnɑːnˌrɪˈvɜːrsəbəlˈnɛsɪz/
noun
不可逆性（复数）
The quality or state of being nonreversible; the inability to be reversed or undone.  This term often appears in scientific contexts, particularly thermodynamics and chemistry.
The nonreversiblenesses of the chemical reaction made it impossible to recover the original reactants. 这个化学反应的不可逆性使得无法恢复原始反应物。
The physicists discussed the nonreversiblenesses inherent in certain natural processes. 物理学家们讨论了某些自然过程中固有的不可逆性。
Synonyms: irreversibility, immutability
Antonyms: reversibility, mutability
n.


Etymology->
non- (prefix): from Latin, meaning "not"
reversible (adjective): from Late Middle English, from Old French *reversable, from reverser "to reverse"
-ness (suffix): from Old English -nes(s), forming nouns expressing state or quality.  Essentially turns an adjective into a noun.
