dermises:/ˈdɜːrməsiːz/
n.
真皮层（复数）
The plural form of "dermis," referring to the thick layer of living tissue below the epidermis that forms the true skin, containing blood capillaries, nerve endings, sweat glands, hair follicles, and other structures.
The dermises of burn victims were severely damaged. 烧伤患者的真皮层严重受损。
Synonyms:  skins, integuments
Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a anatomical part)
noun
