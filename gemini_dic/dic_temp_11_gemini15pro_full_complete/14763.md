awkward:/ˈɔːkwərd/ (US), /ˈɔːkwəd/ (UK)| adj.|笨拙的，尴尬的，棘手的|Lacking grace or skill in movement or performance; causing difficulty; embarrassing or uncomfortable.|例句：He felt awkward dancing with a taller partner.  他觉得自己和一个更高的舞伴跳舞很笨拙。

例句：There was an awkward silence after his inappropriate joke. 他讲了个不合时宜的笑话后，出现了尴尬的沉默。|近义词：clumsy, ungainly, embarrassing, uncomfortable|反义词：graceful, skillful, comfortable, convenient|词性:形容词

awkwardly:/ˈɔːkwərdli/ (US), /ˈɔːkwədli/ (UK)| adv.|笨拙地，尴尬地|In an awkward manner.|例句：She awkwardly stumbled over her words during the presentation.  在演讲中，她笨拙地结结巴巴。

例句：He smiled awkwardly when he realized he'd made a mistake.  当他意识到自己犯了错误时，他尴尬地笑了笑。|近义词：clumsily, ungainly|反义词：gracefully, skillfully|词性:副词

awkwardness:/ˈɔːkwərdnɪs/ (US), /ˈɔːkwədnɪs/ (UK)| n.|笨拙，尴尬|The state or quality of being awkward.|例句：The awkwardness of the situation made everyone uncomfortable.  情况的尴尬让每个人都不舒服。

例句：His social awkwardness made it difficult for him to make friends.  他的社交笨拙使他难以交朋友。|近义词：clumsiness, ungainliness, embarrassment|反义词：grace, skill, comfort|词性:名词
