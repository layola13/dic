acquiescing:/ˌækwiˈesɪŋ/
|v.|默许；顺从；勉强同意|The act of accepting something reluctantly but without protest.  Agreeing passively or without enthusiasm.| He acquiesced in the decision, though he didn't personally agree with it. 他默许了这个决定，尽管他个人并不赞同。|Synonyms: consenting, complying, yielding|Antonyms: resisting, opposing, objecting|verb
