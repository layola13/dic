coenobite:/ˈsiːnəbaɪt/ | n. | 修道士；修道院成员 | A person who lives in a religious community, especially a monk or nun.  |  Example: The coenobites devoted their lives to prayer and study within the monastery walls.  修道士们在修道院里献身于祈祷和学习。

Example:  Ancient texts describe the daily routines of the coenobites. 古代文献描述了修道士们的日常生活。 | Synonym: monk, nun, friar, religious | Antonym: hermit, recluse | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *coenobita*, from Greek *koinobitēs* "living in community," from *koinos* "common" + *bios* "life."
