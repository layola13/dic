contingent:/kənˈtɪndʒənt/| adj. n.|偶然的，视情况而定的；(代表某一特定群体或地区的)代表团，小分队|Dependent on or conditioned by something else; a group of people united by some common feature, forming part of a larger group.|例句：Our plans are contingent on the weather. 我们的计划取决于天气。|近义词：dependent, conditional, subject to, accidental, fortuitous; group, delegation, detachment|反义词:independent, unconditional, certain, planned; individual|词性:形容词, 名词

contingent:/kənˈtɪndʒənt/| n.|偶然事件；(代表某一特定群体或地区的)代表团，小分队|A group of people united by some common feature, forming part of a larger group; a future event or circumstance that is possible but cannot be predicted with certainty.|例句：The university sent a large contingent to the conference. 大学派了一个很大的代表团参加会议。|近义词：group, delegation, detachment,  possibility|反义词:individual, certainty|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contingēns*, present participle of contingere (“to touch, befall, happen”), from Latin con- + tangere (“to touch”).
