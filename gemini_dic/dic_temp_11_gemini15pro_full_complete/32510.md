chinook:/tʃɪˈnʊk/ /ʃɪˈnʊk/| n.|奇努克风（一种温暖干燥的风）;奇努克人（北美印第安人的一支）|A warm, dry wind that blows down the eastern slopes of the Rocky Mountains.  A member of an American Indian people originally inhabiting the region around the lower Columbia River in Oregon and Washington. |例句：The chinook melted the snow quickly. 奇努克风很快融化了雪。

例句：The Chinook people were known for their fishing skills. 奇努克人以其捕鱼技巧而闻名。|近义词：foehn (for the wind)|反义词:|n.
