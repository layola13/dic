jitter:/ˈdʒɪtər/| n. v. |紧张不安；抖动；（电子信号的）颤动|A feeling of extreme nervousness.  A rapid variation in the timing of a signal or process.|The speaker suffered from pre-performance jitters. 演讲者在演出前感到非常紧张。|Near synonyms: nervousness, anxiety, unease, apprehension|Near antonyms: calmness, composure, tranquility|noun

jitter:/ˈdʒɪtər/| v. |紧张；抖动；（电子信号）颤动|To behave nervously or act in a restless way.  To experience rapid and irregular variations.|His voice jittered as he spoke. 他说话时声音颤抖。|Near synonyms: tremble, shake, quiver, vibrate|Near antonyms: steady, stabilize, calm|verb
