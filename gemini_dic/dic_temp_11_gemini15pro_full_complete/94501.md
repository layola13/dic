importunacy:/ˌɪmpɔːrˈtjuːnəsi/ | n. | 持续纠缠；强求；胡搅蛮缠 | Persistent and unwelcome demanding; insistent solicitation. |  The man's importunacy finally wore down the shopkeeper, who agreed to give him a refund.  这个男人不断纠缠，最终让店主妥协，同意给他退款。 |  persistence, insistence, harassment |  restraint, reserve, diffidence | noun

importunate:/ɪmˈpɔːrtʃənət/ | adj. | 持续纠缠的；强求的；胡搅蛮缠的 | Persistent, especially to the point of annoyance or intrusion. | He was so importunate in his demands that I finally gave in. 他不停地要求，我最终让步了。 | persistent, insistent, tenacious | reserved, diffident, unintrusive | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *importunitas, from Latin importunus "unfit, inconvenient, troublesome," from in- "not" + portus "harbor, haven."  The original sense was "unseasonable, inconvenient."  The sense of "troublesomely persistent" developed later.
