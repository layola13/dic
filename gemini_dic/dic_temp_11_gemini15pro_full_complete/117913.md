meltdown's:/ˈmɛltˌdaʊnz/| n.| 崩溃，熔毁 |A meltdown is a disastrous event or situation marked by a sudden, catastrophic failure or collapse.|The nuclear power plant experienced a meltdown after the earthquake.|近义词：collapse, crisis, breakdown, debacle|反义词: recovery, breakthrough, success|词性:名词

词源:  "meltdown" comes from the literal meaning of "melting down," referring to the melting of a nuclear reactor core.  Figuratively, it began to be used in the mid-20th century to describe any kind of severe collapse or breakdown. The 's at the end indicates the possessive form, meaning "belonging to the meltdown" or "of the meltdown".
