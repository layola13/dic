kiting:/ˈkaɪtɪŋ/ | n. |放风筝；（金融）开空头支票；（金融）利用支票在账户之间转移资金以获取未经授权的信用 |The act of flying a kite; a fraudulent scheme involving writing checks against nonexistent funds or moving funds between accounts to obtain unauthorized credit. |例句：The children were kiting on the beach. 孩子们在沙滩上放风筝。

kiting:/ˈkaɪtɪŋ/ | v. |放风筝；（金融）开空头支票；（金融）利用支票在账户之间转移资金 |To fly a kite; to engage in check kiting. |例句：They were kiting their new kite in the park. 他们在公园里放新风筝。

例句：He was kiting checks between two accounts to make it look like he had more money than he did. 他在两个账户之间开空头支票，使自己看起来比实际更有钱。|近义词：check kiting (for the financial sense) |反义词: (No direct antonym) |词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From kite (n.), which comes from Old English *cyta, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to a word meaning "belly, womb" due to the kite's swollen appearance in flight.  The financial sense arose from the notion of keeping something afloat artificially, like a kite in the wind.
