amenably:/əˈmiːnəbli/
adjective
易接受地，顺从地
Willing to accept or be influenced by a suggestion or request.
He listened amenably to her suggestions. 他顺从地听取了她的建议。

amenably:/əˈmiːnəbli/
adjective
通情达理地
In a reasonable and agreeable manner.
She responded amenably to the compromise. 她通情达理地接受了妥协方案。


Synonyms:
cooperatively, willingly, readily, agreeably, receptively, compliantly, obediently, submissively
Antonyms:
uncooperatively, unwillingly, stubbornly, resistant, hostile, intractable, recalcitrant, defiant
Etymology:
From Late Latin *amenabilis "liable, responsible," from amenare "to lead, bring to," from a- "to" + men- "lead" (from PIE root *men- (1) "to lead, guide, direct"). The English sense development was influenced by French amène "pleasant, agreeable."
