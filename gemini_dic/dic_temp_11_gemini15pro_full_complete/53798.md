disconcertedly:/ˌdɪskənˈsɜːtɪdli/ | adv. | 慌张地，不安地，狼狈地 | In a manner that shows someone is upset, confused, or embarrassed. | He looked at her disconcertedly. 他慌张地看着她。|  She smiled disconcertedly at him. 她不安地对他笑了笑。 | 近义词: nervously, anxiously, uneasily, awkwardly | 反义词: calmly, confidently, composedly | 词性: 副词

词源:  From the verb "disconcert," which comes from the French "déconcerter," meaning "to upset the composure of."  "Dis-" is a prefix meaning "apart" or "away," and "concert" in this context relates to harmony or agreement, so to disconcert is to break the harmony or composure.  "-edly" is a suffix that forms adverbs.
