boxhauling:/ˈbɒksˌhɔːlɪŋ/
n.
抢风调向; 逆风抢风航行; 抢风掉抢;
A sailing maneuver used to turn a vessel toward the wind in a confined space, typically by alternately backing and filling the sails.  This allows the vessel to make progress to windward in a channel too narrow for conventional tacking.
The captain skillfully executed a boxhaul to avoid the rocks in the narrow strait. 船长熟练地操纵抢风调向以避开狭窄海峡中的岩石。

tacking, wearing
