galatea:/ˌɡæləˈtiːə/ | n. | 盖拉蒂亚 | In Greek mythology, Galatea was a statue brought to life by the gods.  A galatea refers to a statue, especially one representing a female figure, or a woman who is idealized as being beautiful and unattainable. | Pygmalion fell in love with his statue, Galatea. 皮格马利翁爱上了他的雕像盖拉蒂亚。 | The artist sculpted a beautiful galatea from marble.  艺术家用大理石雕刻了一尊美丽的女性雕像。 |  statue, sculpture, figure |  - | noun
