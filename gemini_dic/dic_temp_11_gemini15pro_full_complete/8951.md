antihumanists:/ˌæntiˈhjuːmənɪsts/
n.|反人文主义者|People who oppose or critique humanism, often emphasizing the limitations or dangers of human reason and individual autonomy. | The antihumanists argued that the focus on individual experience neglected the importance of social structures.| 近义词: poststructuralists, deconstructionists | 反义词: humanists | 词性: 名词


antihumanist:/ˌæntiˈhjuːmənɪst/
adj.|反人文主义的|Opposed to or critical of humanism. |The antihumanist philosopher argued that human reason was not sufficient to understand the world.| 近义词: poststructuralist, deconstructionist | 反义词: humanist | 词性: 形容词


词源:
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to. From Greek anti-.
humanist: From Late Latin humanista, meaning “humanist, scholar of the humanities.”  Ultimately from Latin humanus, "human."
-ist (suffix):  forms agent nouns and adjectives.
