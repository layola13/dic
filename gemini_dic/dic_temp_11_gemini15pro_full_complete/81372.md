grappling:/ˈɡræpəlɪŋ/| n.  |扭打；搏斗；格斗；（与难题的）斗争|The act of engaging in a close fight or struggle, especially one that involves holding your opponent.|He was skilled in the art of grappling. 他精通格斗技巧。|Synonyms: wrestling, struggling, contending, combating|Antonyms: yielding, surrendering|noun


grapple:/ˈɡræpəl/| v. |扭打；搏斗；格斗；努力克服（难题）|To engage in a close fight or struggle, especially one that involves holding your opponent; to try hard to deal with or understand something difficult.|The two wrestlers grappled with each other. 两名摔跤选手扭打在一起。  She grappled with the complex problem. 她努力克服这个复杂的问题。|Synonyms: wrestle, struggle, contend, combat|Antonyms: yield, surrender|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *graper or *grappiller (“to seize, grasp, clutch”), from Frankish *krappon, from Proto-Germanic *krappōną (“to seize, grasp”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)krebʰ- (“to seize, hold”). Cognate with Middle Dutch grappelen, German grappeln and Dutch grappelen.
