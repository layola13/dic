bionic:/baɪˈɒnɪk/ | adj. | 仿生学的；具有生物功能的；| Having or denoting artificially constructed organs or other body parts that are electronically or mechanically powered and capable of performing the functions of natural ones. |例句：Scientists are developing a bionic eye to help the blind see. 科学家正在开发一种仿生眼来帮助盲人恢复视力。

例句：The bionic hand allows amputees to regain some dexterity.  仿生手让截肢者恢复了一些灵活性。|近义词：artificial, synthetic, prosthetic |反义词: natural, organic |词性: adjective

Etymology: From Greek *biōnikos* "of or for life" (from *bios* "life") + *-ikos* "-ic." Coined in 1958 by Jack E. Steele.
