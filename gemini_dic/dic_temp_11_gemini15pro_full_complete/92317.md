hypnotised: /ˈhɪpnətaɪzd/ (UK) /ˈhɪpnəˌtaɪzd/ (US)| adj.| 被催眠的；着迷的 | Being in a state of hypnosis; captivated or fascinated.|  The audience was hypnotised by the magician's performance.  观众被魔术师的表演迷住了。 |  The hypnotised subject was unresponsive to external stimuli.  被催眠的对象对外界刺激没有反应。 |  Synonyms: mesmerized, entranced, spellbound, captivated | Antonyms: alert, aware, conscious | adjective

hypnotize: /ˈhɪpnətaɪz/ (UK) /ˈhɪpnəˌtaɪz/ (US)| vt.| 催眠；使着迷 | To induce a state of hypnosis; to fascinate or captivate.| The therapist hypnotized the patient to help them overcome their fear.  治疗师催眠病人以帮助他们克服恐惧。| The shimmering lights hypnotized the children. 闪烁的灯光使孩子们着迷。| Synonyms: mesmerize, entrance, spellbind, captivate | Antonyms: bore, disenchant | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *hypnotiser*, from Greek *hypnos* (sleep) + *-ize*.
