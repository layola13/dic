intrude: /ɪnˈtruːd/ (US) /ɪnˈtruːd/ (UK)| v. |侵入，打扰，闯入|To come or go in without permission or welcome.|She intruded on their privacy by reading their letters. 她未经允许看了他们的信，侵犯了他们的隐私。|He intruded into the conversation. 他插嘴了。|近义词：interfere, meddle, barge in, encroach|反义词：withdraw, retreat, abstain|词性:动词

intruded: /ɪnˈtruːdɪd/ (US) /ɪnˈtruːdɪd/ (UK)| v. |(intrude的过去式和过去分词) 侵入，打扰，闯入|Past tense and past participle of "intrude".  Indicates that the action of intruding has already happened.|He had intruded upon their meeting uninvited. 他未经邀请闯入了他们的会议。|The thought intruded itself upon her mind. 这个想法突然闯入了她的脑海。|近义词：interfered, meddled, barged in, encroached|反义词：withdrawn, retreated, abstained|词性:动词
