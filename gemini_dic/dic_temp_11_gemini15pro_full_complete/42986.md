covetously: /ˈkʌvɪtəsli/
adverb
贪婪地，垂涎地
Having or showing a great desire to possess something, typically something belonging to someone else.
He looked covetously at his neighbor's new car. 他贪婪地看着邻居的新车。
Synonyms: greedily, enviously, jealously, avariciously, desirously
Antonyms: generously, selflessly, altruistically, charitably
词性: 副词


Etymology:
From Late Middle English *coveitousli*, from coveitous + -ly.  Coveitous comes from Old French coveitos, from Latin cupidus (“eager, passionate”), from cupere (“to desire”).
