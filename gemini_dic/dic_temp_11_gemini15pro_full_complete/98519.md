instauration:/ˌɪnstɔːˈreɪʃn/ |n.| 复兴，重建，恢复；(尤指宗教、习俗等的)重新建立|The instauration of democracy after the dictatorship was a long and difficult process.  独裁统治之后民主的重建是一个漫长而艰难的过程。|例句：The instauration of the monarchy brought a period of stability. 君主制的恢复带来了一个稳定时期。

例句：The museum celebrated the instauration of its ancient artifacts collection.  博物馆庆祝其古代文物收藏的重新建立。|近义词：restoration, revival, renewal, re-establishment|反义词：abolition, destruction, termination|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *instauratio*, from Latin instaurare (“to renew, restore”), from in- (“in, on”) + *staurare*, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Greek ἵστημι (hístēmi, “I set up, establish”).
