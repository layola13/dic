deep-sixing:/ˌdiːpˈsɪksɪŋ/| n. v.|丢弃，抛弃，处理掉（通常指秘密地或永久地）|The act of discarding or disposing of something, often secretly or permanently.  It originates from nautical slang, where "deep six" referred to the deepest part of the ocean (six fathoms deep), a traditional burial place at sea.|例句：The company decided on deep-sixing the project due to budget cuts. 公司由于预算削减决定放弃该项目。

例句：He was accused of deep-sixing the evidence. 他被指控销毁了证据。|近义词：discarding, dumping, ditching, scrapping, burying, concealing|反义词:keeping, preserving, saving|词性:noun, verb
