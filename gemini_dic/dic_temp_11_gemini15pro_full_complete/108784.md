leasor:/ˈliːsɔːr/| n.|出租人|The person or entity that grants a lease.  The leasor owns the property and allows the lessee to use it for a specified period in exchange for rent.|The leasor stipulated that the lessee could not sublet the apartment.|Synonym: landlord, lessor|Antonym: lessee, tenant|noun
