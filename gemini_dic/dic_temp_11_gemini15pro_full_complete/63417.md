entrancing:/ɪnˈtrɑːnsɪŋ/ | adj. | 迷人的，令人入迷的 | Filling someone with wonder and delight, captivating. | Her entrancing performance captivated the entire audience. 她迷人的表演吸引了全场观众。 | synonyms: captivating, mesmerizing, spellbinding, fascinating, enchanting | antonyms:  repulsive, boring, dull, tedious, uninteresting | adjective

entrancing:/ɪnˈtrɑːnsɪŋ/ | adj. | 迷人的，令人入迷的 | Holding someone's attention completely; fascinating. | The entrancing beauty of the sunset left us speechless.  令人入迷的日落美景让我们 speechless。 | synonyms: captivating, mesmerizing, spellbinding, fascinating, enchanting | antonyms:  repulsive, boring, dull, tedious, uninteresting | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *entrauncen*, from Anglo-Norman *entrauncer*, from Old French *entraire* "to enter," from Latin *intra* "within" + *ire* "to go."  The sense evolved from "putting into a trance" to "captivating, fascinating."
