cacoxene:/kəˈkɒksiːn/ | n. | 水磷铁矿; 黄磷铁矿 |A hydrated basic iron phosphate mineral, Fe4(PO4)3(OH)3·12H2O, occurring as yellow to brownish-yellow crystals or earthy masses.  It is a secondary mineral formed by the alteration of primary phosphate minerals. |Cacoxene is a relatively rare mineral found in the oxidized zones of iron deposits containing phosphates.  | Synonyms: None (as it's a specific mineral name) | Antonyms:  N/A (Not applicable to mineral names) | Noun
