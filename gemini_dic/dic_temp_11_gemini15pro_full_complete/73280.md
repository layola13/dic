forebodement:/fɔːrˈboʊdmənt/| n. |预感；预兆；凶兆|An intuitive feeling that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen. |例句：A sense of foreboding settled over her. 她心中涌起一种不祥的预感。

例句：The dark clouds were a forebodement of the coming storm. 乌云是暴风雨即将来临的预兆。|近义词：premonition, presentiment, omen, portent|反义词：assurance, hope, optimism|词性:名词

forebode:/fɔːrˈboʊd/| v. |预示；预兆；预感|To act as a warning of (something bad); portend.|例句：The black crows seemed to forebode evil.  乌鸦似乎预示着邪恶。

例句：The sudden silence foreboded trouble. 突如其来的寂静预示着麻烦。|近义词：presage, augur, portend|反义词：reassure|词性:动词

词源：
From Middle English *forboden, from Old English forbēodan (“to forbid, prohibit, command against, renounce”), from Proto-Germanic *farbiudaną (“to forbid”), equivalent to for- +‎ bid.  最初的意思是“禁止”，后来引申为“预示不祥的事情”。
