eulogism: /ˈjuːlədʒɪzəm/ | n. | 颂词，悼词 | A speech or piece of writing that praises someone or something highly, typically someone who has just died. |  The minister delivered a moving eulogism at the funeral. 牧师在葬礼上致了感人的悼词。

| Example:  His eulogism was a touching tribute to his late friend.  他的悼词是对已故朋友的感人致敬。


| Synonyms: tribute, panegyric, encomium, accolade | Antonyms: criticism, condemnation, denunciation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *eulogium*, from Greek *eulogia* "praise, blessing,"  from *eu* "well" + *logos* "word, speech."
