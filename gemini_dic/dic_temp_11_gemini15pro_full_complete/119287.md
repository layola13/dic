micro-electrophoresis: /ˌmaɪkroʊɪˌlɛktroʊfɒˈriːsɪs/ | n. | 微电泳 | The technique of separating and analyzing charged particles, especially proteins and colloids, in a microscopic environment by applying an electric field. | Micro-electrophoresis is a powerful tool for studying the surface properties of materials. 微电泳是研究材料表面性质的有力工具。| 近义词: capillary electrophoresis | 反义词: N/A | 词性: 名词

micro- (from Greek mikrós "small") + electro- (from Greek ēlektron "amber," referring to static electricity) + phoresis (from Greek phórein "to carry")
