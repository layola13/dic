downplayed:/ˌdaʊnˈpleɪd/ | v. | 低估；轻描淡写；淡化|To minimize the significance or importance of something; to represent something as less important than it actually is. | The government downplayed the severity of the economic crisis. 政府淡化了经济危机的严重性。| The coach downplayed the team's loss, focusing instead on the players' efforts. 教练淡化了球队的失利，反而着重强调了球员们的努力。| synonyms: minimize, understate, de-emphasize, soft-pedal | antonyms: exaggerate, overstate, emphasize, play up | verb

downplay:/ˌdaʊnˈpleɪ/ | v. | 低估；轻描淡写；淡化 | To make something seem less important or less serious than it is. | He downplayed his role in the project, but everyone knew he was the key figure. 他淡化了他在项目中的作用，但每个人都知道他是关键人物。|  She downplayed the seriousness of her illness, not wanting to worry her family. 她轻描淡写了病情的严重性，不想让家人担心。 | synonyms: minimize, understate, de-emphasize | antonyms: exaggerate, overstate, emphasize | verb
