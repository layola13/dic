disappointing:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人失望的 | Causing disappointment; failing to fulfill expectations. | The movie was disappointing; it wasn't as good as the reviews said.  这部电影令人失望；它不如评论说的那么好。| The team's performance was disappointing. 球队的表现令人失望。 | 近义词: unsatisfactory, underwhelming, frustrating | 反义词: satisfying, pleasing, encouraging | 词性: adjective

disappoint:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt/ | vt. | 使失望 | Fail to fulfill the hopes or expectations of (someone). |  I hate to disappoint you, but I can't come to the party. 我很不想让你失望，但我不能来参加聚会了。|  He disappointed his parents by failing the exam. 他考试不及格，让父母失望了。 | 近义词: let down, dissatisfy | 反义词: please, satisfy | 词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + appoint (to fix, settle, determine).  The original meaning was "to undo an appointment," later evolving to "frustrate expectations."
