boult:/boʊlt/| v. | 筛； 脱粒；  | To sift or separate (grain) from chaff.  To rush or spring away suddenly. | The farmer boulted the wheat to remove the chaff.  农民簸小麦以去除糠秕。 The startled deer boulted into the forest. 受惊的鹿猛地窜进了森林。 | 近义词：sift, separate, bolt, dash, dart | 反义词: combine, gather | 词性: 动词

词源:  From Old French *bouter, from Vulgar Latin *butare "to push, thrust," from a Germanic source related to English beat.  The original sense of "boult" was "to sift," and the sense of "to rush away" developed from the idea of grain being tossed about in a sieve.
