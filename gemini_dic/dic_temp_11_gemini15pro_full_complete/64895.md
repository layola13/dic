essene:/ˈɛsiːn/
noun
艾赛尼人；艾赛尼教派成员
A member of a Jewish sect or brotherhood that flourished in Palestine from the 2nd century BC to the 2nd century AD.  They lived a communal life, held property in common, and practiced asceticism.
例句：The Essenes lived a simple life, dedicated to prayer and study.  艾赛尼人过着简朴的生活，致力于祈祷和学习。

例句：Some scholars believe that John the Baptist may have been an Essene. 一些学者认为施洗约翰可能是艾赛尼人。
近义词: ascetic, monk
反义词: hedonist
词性: n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin Esseni, from Greek Essēnoi, perhaps from Aramaic asaya, meaning "healers" or "pious ones." The exact origin is uncertain.
