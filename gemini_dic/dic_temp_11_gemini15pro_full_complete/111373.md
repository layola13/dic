livery:/ˈlɪvəri/ (US), /ˈlɪvri/ (UK)| n. adj.| 制服；彩绘；（尤指马的）饲养；（公司或个人的）外观设计 |A special uniform worn by a servant or official, or a distinctive design and colors used on the vehicles, aircraft, etc. of a particular company.| The chauffeur wore a smart livery. 司机穿着整洁的制服。| synonyms: uniform, attire, costume, garb, clothing, outfit, ensemble, suit, regalia, insignia| antonyms: plainclothes, civilian clothes| noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *livrée*, past participle of *livrer* "to deliver, hand over," from Latin *liberare* "to free, liberate," from *liber* "free."  Originally referred to the delivery of provisions or clothing, especially to servants or retainers. The sense of a distinctive uniform or color scheme developed from the practice of providing such clothing to servants for identification.
