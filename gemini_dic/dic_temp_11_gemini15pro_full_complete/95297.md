incarnates:/ɪnˈkɑːrneɪts/ | v. | 体现，使具体化， воплощать | embodies in flesh; represents in a concrete form |  The artist incarnates the spirit of the age in his paintings.  这位艺术家在他的绘画中体现了时代精神。 | embodies, personifies, represents, exemplifies |  abstracts, disembodies | verb

incarnate:/ɪnˈkɑːrneɪt/ | adj. | 化身的， воплощенный | embodied in flesh; personified |  He believed he was talking to an incarnate deity. 他相信他正在与一位化身的上帝交谈。 | embodied, personified, materialized | disembodied, spiritual, immaterial | adjective

incarnate:/ˈɪnkɑːrneɪt/ | n. | 化身， воплощение | a person who embodies in the flesh a deity, spirit, or abstract quality. | The Buddha is considered an incarnate deity. 佛被认为是一位化身的上帝。 | embodiment, personification, manifestation | none | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin incarnatus, past participle of incarnare ("to make flesh"), from in- ("in, into") + caro, carnis ("flesh").
