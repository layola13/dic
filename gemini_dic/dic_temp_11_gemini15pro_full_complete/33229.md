chronographer:/krəˈnɒɡrəfər/ /krəˈnɑːɡrəfər/
n.
计时员，计时器，年代史编者
A person who writes chronicles or historical accounts; a device used for measuring and recording time, often as part of a watch.
The chronograph accurately measured the athlete's time to the hundredth of a second. 计时器精确地测量了运动员的成绩，精确到百分之一秒。
Historians rely on the meticulous records of early chronographers.  历史学家依赖早期年代史编纂者细致的记录。
近义词:timer, recorder, historian, chronologist
反义词:None
词性:Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *chronographe*, from Ancient Greek χρόνος (chrónos, “time”) + -graphe (from γράφω (gráphō, “I write”)).
