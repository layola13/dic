cherry-pickers:/ˈtʃɛriˌpɪkərz/| n.| 采摘樱桃的人；选择性接受信息的人|People who pick cherries from trees, or figuratively, people who select only the best or most desirable items from a larger set.|The cherry-pickers were out in the orchards early this morning. 采摘樱桃的人今天早上很早就到果园去了。|

cherry-pickers:/ˈtʃɛriˌpɪkərz/| n.| 选择性接受信息的人|People who select only the best or most desirable items from a larger set, often ignoring the less favorable ones.|He accused the media of cherry-picking data to support their narrative. 他指责媒体选择性地使用数据来支持他们的叙述。|近义词:selectors, choosers|反义词: N/A|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "cherry" + "picker," literally "one who picks cherries."  The figurative meaning developed from the literal act of selecting the best cherries from a tree.
