nonmeasurably:/ˌnɒnˈmɛʒərəbli/ | adv. | 不可测量地，无法估量地 | Not able to be measured, usually because of being too large, too small, or too abstract.  | The impact of his work is nonmeasurably significant. 他的工作的影响是不可估量的。 |  The universe is nonmeasurably vast. 宇宙浩瀚无垠，无法测量。 | Synonyms: immeasurably, incalculably, infinitely | Antonyms: measurably, calculably, finitely | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + measurable + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Measurable" comes from the Old French "mesurable," ultimately from Latin "mensura" (measure).
