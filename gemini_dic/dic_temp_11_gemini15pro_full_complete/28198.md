capriciously: /kəˈprɪʃəsli/ /kəˈpriːʃəsli/
| adv. |反复无常地，任性地 |In a capricious manner; unpredictably; impulsively.  Characterized by or subject to whim; impulsive and unpredictable. |She acted capriciously, changing her mind every few minutes. 她反复无常，每隔几分钟就改变主意。|
| The weather changed capriciously, going from sunshine to rain in an instant. 天气变化无常，瞬间由晴转雨。|
|Synonyms: erratically, whimsically, impulsively, arbitrarily, fickly |
|Antonyms: consistently, steadily, predictably, rationally |
|词性: 副词(adverb)|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *capriciōsus, from Latin capra (“goat”) +‎ -iōsus (akin to English -ious).  Goats were thought to be prone to sudden, unpredictable movements, hence the association with capricious behavior.
