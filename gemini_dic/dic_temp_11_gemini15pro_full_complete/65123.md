ether:/ˈiːθər/ (US), /ˈiːθə(r)/ (UK)| n.|乙醚; 以太;|A colorless, volatile, highly flammable liquid, (C₂H₅)₂O, used as an anesthetic and as a solvent.|例句：The patient was anesthetized with ether. 病人被乙醚麻醉了。

例句：Scientists once believed in the existence of ether, a substance thought to permeate all space. 科学家曾经相信以太的存在，一种被认为弥漫于所有空间的物质。|近义词：anaesthetic (as an agent), medium (as a pervading substance)|反义词:N/A|词性:noun

ether:/ˈiːθər/ (US), /ˈiːθə(r)/ (UK)| n.|天空,苍穹;|The clear sky; the upper regions of air beyond the clouds.|例句：The rocket soared into the ether. 火箭飞上了天空。

例句：His spirit seemed to ascend into the ether. 他的灵魂似乎升入了天际。|近义词：sky, heavens, firmament|反义词:earth, ground|词性:noun
