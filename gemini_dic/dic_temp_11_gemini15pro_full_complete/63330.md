entitlement:/ɪnˈtaɪtlmənt/ | n. |权利，资格；津贴|The right to have something or do something, often because it is part of a social system or agreement.  A government program providing benefits to members of a specified group.|He has a sense of entitlement. 他有一种优越感。|Synonyms: right, privilege, claim, prerogative |Antonyms: disqualification, ineligibility|noun

entitle:/ɪnˈtaɪtl/ | vt. |给…权利；给…命名|To give someone the right to have or do something. To give a title to a book, film, etc.|This ticket entitles you to a free drink.  这张票可以让你免费喝一杯饮料。|Synonyms: empower, authorize, commission, qualify| Antonyms: disqualify, prohibit, forbid|verb
