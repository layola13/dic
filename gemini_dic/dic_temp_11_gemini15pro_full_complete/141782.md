oratorize: /ˈɔːrəˌtaɪz/ (US), /ˈɒrətəraɪz/ (UK) | v. | 演说；作演说；|to speak in a pompous or inflated style, as if giving a formal speech.| He tended to oratorize rather than engage in genuine conversation. 他倾向于夸夸其谈而不是进行真正的对话。 |Example: The politician oratorized for hours about the importance of his policies.  这位政治家就其政策的重要性滔滔不绝地讲了几个小时。

Example:  She oratorized about the beauty of the sunset, making it sound more like a performance than a simple observation. 她对日落的美丽夸夸其谈，使之听起来更像一场表演，而不是简单的观察。| 近义词: declaim, speechify, pontificate | 反义词:  converse, chat |词性: 动词
