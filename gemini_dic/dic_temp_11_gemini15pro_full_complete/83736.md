hail-mary-pass:/ˌheɪl ˈmɛəri ˈpæs/ | n. | 孤注一掷的尝试，最后一搏 | A long, high-risk pass in American football, typically made in desperation at the end of a game. It refers to a desperate, last-ditch effort to succeed. | The quarterback threw a hail-mary pass into the end zone as the clock ran out.  四分卫在比赛结束时向达阵区投出了一记孤注一掷的长传。| synonyms: last-ditch effort, desperate attempt, long shot | antonyms: safe bet, sure thing | noun

词源：来源于美式橄榄球比赛中，在比赛即将结束时，落后方为了争取胜利而采取的长传战术，这种传球往往成功率很低，如同向上帝祈祷一样，因此被称为“hail mary pass”（圣母玛利亚的祈祷）。
