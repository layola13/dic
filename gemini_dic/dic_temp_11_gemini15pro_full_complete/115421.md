mantis:/ˈmæntɪs/ (US), /ˈmæntɪs/ (UK)| n. |螳螂|A predatory insect with a long body, large eyes, and forelegs adapted for catching and holding prey.  It is known for its characteristic praying posture.|例句：The mantis patiently waited for its prey. 螳螂耐心地等待着它的猎物。

例句：A praying mantis camouflaged itself on a green leaf. 一只螳螂在绿叶上伪装自己。|近义词：mantid|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek μάντις (mántis) meaning "seer, prophet, diviner," perhaps because of its upright posture resembling a person at prayer.
