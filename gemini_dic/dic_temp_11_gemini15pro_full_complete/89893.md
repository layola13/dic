horridly:/ˈhɒrɪdli/ (British), /ˈhɔːrɪdli/ (American)| adv.|可怕地，恐怖地，非常讨厌地|In a horrid manner; to a horrid degree.  Describes something done in a way that is extremely unpleasant or shocking.|The play was horridly acted and the costumes were cheap. 这部戏演得糟透了，服装也很廉价。|The children behaved horridly at the party, spilling juice and throwing food. 孩子们在聚会上表现得非常糟糕，洒了果汁，还扔食物。|近义词：awfully, terribly, dreadfully, frightfully|反义词：beautifully, wonderfully, delightfully|词性:副词

horrid (adjective): From Late Latin horridus "rough, bristling, standing on end (of hair)," from Latin horrere "to bristle, shudder," figuratively "to dread."  The adverb horridly is formed by adding the suffix -ly to the adjective horrid.
