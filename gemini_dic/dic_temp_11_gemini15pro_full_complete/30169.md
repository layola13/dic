celebrators:/ˈsɛləˌbreɪtərz/
n.
庆祝者，庆祝的人们
People who are celebrating something.
The celebrators filled the streets after the team's victory.  庆祝者在球队获胜后挤满了街道。
The celebrators danced and sang all night long. 庆祝者整夜载歌载舞。
synonyms: revelers, partygoers, celebrants
antonyms: mourners, grievers
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From celebrate (v.) + -or (suffix forming nouns denoting an agent or person performing an action).  Ultimately from Latin celebrāre "to frequent, honor, praise, celebrate,"  from celeber "frequented, populous, famous."
