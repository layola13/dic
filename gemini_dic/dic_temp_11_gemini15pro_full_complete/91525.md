hyoscyamine:/ˌhaɪəˈsaɪəmiːn/ | n. |莨菪碱|A crystalline alkaloid, C₁₇H₂₃NO₃, obtained from henbane and other plants of the nightshade family, used as an anticholinergic and antispasmodic.  It is a secondary metabolite of these plants and serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.|Hyoscyamine is used to treat stomach and intestinal cramping. 莨菪碱用于治疗胃和肠痉挛。|Synonyms: duboisine, daturine | Antonyms: None | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From  *Hyoscyamus* (genus name of henbane) + *-ine* (chemical suffix indicating an alkaloid).  *Hyoscyamus* ultimately derives from Greek *hýos* (hog) + *kýamos* (bean), referring to a plant poisonous to swine.
