augur:/ˈɔːɡər/ (US), /ˈɔːɡə(r)/ (UK)| v. |预示，预兆 |to be a sign that something will probably happen, especially something good |例句：The high turnout at the rally augured well for the election. 集会上 turnout很高，预示着选举结果会很好。

例句：Dark clouds augur rain. 乌云预示着要下雨。|近义词：foretell, presage, portend, bode |反义词: N/A |词性: verb


augured:/ˈɔːɡərd/ (US), /ˈɔːɡəd/ (UK)| v. (past tense) |预示了，预兆了| past tense of "augur," meaning it served as a sign of something that was likely to happen |例句：The initial results augured a landslide victory for the incumbent. 初步结果预示着现任官员将以压倒性优势获胜。

例句：The sudden drop in sales augured ill for the company's future. 销售额的突然下降预示着公司的前景不妙。|近义词：foretold, presaged, portended, boded |反义词: N/A |词性: verb


词源: From Latin *augurāri, from augur (“augur, diviner”).
