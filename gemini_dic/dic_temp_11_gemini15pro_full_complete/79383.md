gliddery:/ˈɡlɪdəri/ | adj. | 滑溜的，狡猾的 | Covered with a slippery substance, causing one to slide or lose footing.  Figuratively, it can also mean elusive or tricky to deal with. | The path was gliddery after the rain. 雨后的路很滑。 The politician's gliddery answers made it hard to trust him.  这位政客滑头的回答让人很难相信他。 | 近义词: slippery, slick, treacherous, elusive, shifty | 反义词: rough, grippy, stable, honest, straightforward | 词性: adjective

词源:  From "glide" + "-ery," a suffix forming nouns and adjectives denoting a place or state associated with the root word. "Glide" itself comes from Old English "glidan."
