embarrassing:/ɪmˈbærəsɪŋ/ (UK), /ɪmˈbɛrəsɪŋ/ (US)| adj.|令人尴尬的，使人难堪的|Causing a feeling of self-conscious shame and discomfort.|例句：Tripping in front of everyone was so embarrassing. 在大家面前绊倒真是太尴尬了。|近义词：humiliating, awkward, mortifying|反义词：comforting, reassuring|词性:形容词

embarrass:/ɪmˈbærəs/ (UK), /ɪmˈbɛrəs/ (US)| vt.|使尴尬，使难堪|To cause someone to feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed.|例句：He embarrassed her by asking personal questions in front of her friends. 他在她的朋友面前问她私人问题，让她很尴尬。|近义词：humiliate, shame, mortify|反义词：comfort, reassure|词性:动词

embarrassment:/ɪmˈbærəsmənt/ (UK), /ɪmˈbɛrəsmənt/ (US)| n.|尴尬，难堪|A feeling of self-consciousness, shame, or awkwardness.|例句：She blushed with embarrassment. 她尴尬地脸红了。|近义词：shame, humiliation, mortification|反义词：comfort, reassurance|词性:名词
