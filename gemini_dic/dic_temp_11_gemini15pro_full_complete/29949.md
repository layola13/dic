cave-in:/ˈkeɪvɪn/ | n. |塌方，坍塌，（尤指在压力下）让步，屈服|A sudden collapse or subsidence of land, a roof, or other structure, typically creating a hole or cavity in the ground; also, a yielding to pressure or demands.|The heavy rains caused a cave-in of the old mine shaft.  暴雨导致了旧矿井的坍塌。|Synonyms: collapse, subsidence, caving, giving way|Antonyms:  support, bolster, reinforce|noun

cave-in:/ˈkeɪvɪn/ | v. |塌陷，坍塌，（尤指在压力下）让步，屈服|To collapse or subside suddenly, creating a hole or cavity; also, to yield to pressure or demands.|The roof of the tunnel caved in. 隧道顶部塌陷了。|Synonyms: collapse, crumble, give way|Antonyms:  hold, withstand, resist|verb
