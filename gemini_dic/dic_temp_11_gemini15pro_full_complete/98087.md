inquisitive:/ɪnˈkwɪzətɪv/| adj.|好奇的，爱打听的|Given to asking questions; eager for knowledge; curious.|例句：She has always been an inquisitive child, always asking "why?". 她一直是个好奇的孩子，总是问“为什么？”


例句：The inquisitive journalist investigated the corruption scandal. 好奇的记者调查了腐败丑闻。


近义词：curious, inquiring, prying, nosy|反义词：apathetic, indifferent, incurious|词性：形容词

inquisitively:/ɪnˈkwɪzətɪvli/| adv.|好奇地，爱打听地|In a curious or inquiring manner.|例句：He looked at me inquisitively, trying to understand my motives. 他好奇地看着我，试图理解我的动机。

例句：She inquisitively peeked into the box. 她好奇地偷看盒子里的东西。


近义词：curiously, inquiringly|反义词：apathetically, indifferently|词性：副词

inquisitiveness:/ɪnˌkwɪzətɪvnəs/| n.|好奇心，爱打听|The quality of being inquisitive.|例句：Her inquisitiveness led her to a career in journalism. 她的好奇心使她从事了新闻事业。

例句：Children's natural inquisitiveness should be encouraged. 应该鼓励孩子们天生的好奇心。


近义词：curiosity, inquiry|反义词：apathy, indifference|词性：名词


词源：Late Middle English: from late Latin inquisitivus, from inquirere ‘inquire’.
