globulin:/ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn/ (US) /ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn/ (UK)| n. |球蛋白|A family of globular proteins that are generally insoluble in pure water but dissolve in dilute salt solutions. They are important components of blood plasma and include antibodies, enzymes, and transport proteins.|Blood tests revealed low levels of gamma globulin. 血液测试显示伽马球蛋白水平低。|synonyms:  plasma protein, serum protein|antonyms:  None (Globulins are a class of proteins, not a concept with a direct opposite.)|noun
