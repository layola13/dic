anaesthesia:/ˌænɪsˈθiːʒə/ (UK) /ˌænɪsˈθiːziə/ (US)| n.|麻醉|Loss of sensation, especially of pain, induced by drugs or other means.  The state of being insensible to pain, chiefly as artificially induced by the administration of gases or the injection of drugs before surgical operations.|The patient was given general anaesthesia before the surgery. 病人在手术前进行了全身麻醉。|Synonyms: numbness, insensibility|Antonyms: sensation, consciousness|noun
