humoralist:/ˈhjuːmərəlɪst/ | n. | 体液学家；体液说信奉者 | A believer in the humoral theory of medicine.  A person who believes that the balance of humors in the body determines health and temperament. |  In the Middle Ages, most physicians were humoralists who prescribed bloodletting and other treatments to balance the humors.  中世纪，大多数医生都是体液学家，他们通过放血和其他疗法来平衡体液。|  The humoralist believed the patient's melancholic temperament was caused by an excess of black bile.  这位体液学家认为病人的忧郁气质是由过多的黑胆汁引起的。 |  physician, doctor |  | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *humoralista, from Latin humor ("fluid") + -ista.
