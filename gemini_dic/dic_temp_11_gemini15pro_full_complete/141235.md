oozes:/ˈuːzɪz/ (British), /ˈuːziz/ (American)| v. (third person singular present tense)|渗出，流出；散发，显露|To flow or leak out slowly; to exude or emit something gradually, often something unpleasant or undesirable.  To display a particular quality or feeling very strongly.|The wound oozes pus.  伤口渗出脓液。|近义词：seeps, exudes, leaks, drips, emanates|反义词:absorbs, dries, solidifies|verb

ooze:/uːz/ (British), /uːz/ (American)| n.|软泥，淤泥|Soft, watery mud, especially at the bottom of a lake or river.|The boat sank into the ooze. 船沉入了淤泥。|近义词：mud, slime, sludge|反义词:solid, rock|noun

oozy:/ˈuːzi/ (British), /ˈuːzi/ (American)| adj.|渗出的，泥泞的|Covered or saturated with ooze; resembling ooze.|The oozy ground was difficult to walk on. 泥泞的地面很难行走。|近义词：muddy, slimy, sludgy|反义词:dry, solid|adjective
