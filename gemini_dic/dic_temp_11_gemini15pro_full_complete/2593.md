amortization: /ˌæmərtaɪˈzeɪʃn/ | n. | 摊销，分期偿还 | The gradual reduction of a loan principal over time through regular payments.  The process of gradually writing off the initial cost of an asset. |  Example 1: The amortization schedule shows how much of each payment goes towards principal and interest.  摊销计划表显示了每次付款中有多少用于支付本金和利息。

Example 2:  The company is amortizing the cost of the new equipment over five years.  公司正在将新设备的成本摊销五年。 | Synonyms: depreciation, write-off | Antonyms: appreciation, accrual | noun


amortize: /ˈæmərtaɪz/ | vt. | 摊销，分期偿还 | To gradually reduce or write off the cost or value of an asset over a period of time.  To pay off a debt in regular installments over a period of time. | Example 1: The loan will be amortized over 15 years.  这笔贷款将在15年内分期偿还。

Example 2:  They are amortizing the cost of the new building.  他们正在摊销新建筑的成本。 | Synonyms: depreciate, write off, pay off | Antonyms: appreciate, accrue | verb
