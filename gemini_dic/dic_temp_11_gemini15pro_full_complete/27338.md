camass:/kəˈmæs/ | n. | 卡马斯，北美百合科植物 | A North American plant of the lily family, with blue or white flowers and an edible bulb.  The bulb was a staple food for Native Americans. | The Nez Perce tribe traditionally harvested camass bulbs in large quantities. 内兹佩尔塞部落传统上大量收获卡马斯球茎。

|  The camass fields were a valuable resource for the indigenous people. 卡马斯田野是对土著人民宝贵的资源。 |Synonyms: quamash, wild hyacinth | Antonyms: N/A | Noun
