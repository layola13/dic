emotioned:/ɪˈmoʊʃənd/ | adj. | 充满感情的；激动的 | Filled with or showing emotion; moved. | She was very emotioned after hearing the news. 她听到这个消息后非常激动。 |  He delivered an emotioned speech about his late father. 他发表了一篇充满感情的悼念亡父的演讲。| 近义词: emotional, moved, affected, stirred, passionate | 反义词: unemotional, apathetic, indifferent | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From the verb "emotion" + "-ed" (suffix forming adjectives). The verb "emotion" comes from the French "émouvoir," from Latin "ēmovēre," meaning "to move out, remove, agitate, excite."

While less common as part of a set phrase, "emotioned" can be used in phrases like:

注意事项:

"Emotioned" is less commonly used than "emotional."  "Emotional" is the more frequent and natural choice in most contexts.  "Emotioned" might be considered slightly archaic or overly formal in modern English.
