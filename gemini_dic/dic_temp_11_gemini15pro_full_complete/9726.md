antiutilitarianism:/ˌæntijuːˌtɪlɪˈtɛəriənɪzəm/
|n.|反功利主义|The philosophical doctrine opposing utilitarianism, rejecting the idea that the morally right action is always the one that maximizes happiness or well-being.|John Stuart Mill's work attempted to address some of the critiques of antiutilitarianism.  约翰·斯图尔特·密尔的作品试图回应一些对反功利主义的批评。|
|Antonyms: utilitarianism|
|Synonyms: non-utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics|
|Noun|
