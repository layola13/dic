antidiuretic:/ˌæntidaɪjʊˈrɛtɪk/| n.|抗利尿剂|A substance that reduces or prevents the production of urine. |The patient was given an antidiuretic to manage their condition. 病人服用了抗利尿剂来控制病情。|She needs to take an antidiuretic to help her body retain water. 她需要服用抗利尿剂来帮助身体保持水分。|近义词：vasopressin, desmopressin|反义词：diuretic|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + diuretic.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposite of."  "Diuretic" comes from the Greek "diouretikos," meaning "promoting urine."  Therefore, "antidiuretic" literally means "against promoting urine."
