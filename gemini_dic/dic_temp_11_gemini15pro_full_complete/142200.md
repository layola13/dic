ornerinesses: /ˈɔːrnərinəsɪz/ | n. | 坏脾气，乖戾，倔强 | The state of being ornery; a display of  stubbornness, cantankerousness, or ill temper.  |  His ornerinesses made it difficult to work with him. 他的坏脾气让人很难和他共事。 | Synonyms:  cantankerousness,  cussedness,  irascibility,  perverseness,  contrariness  | Antonyms:  agreeableness,  amenability,  compliance,  docility,  tractability | noun

Etymology: From ornery + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing state, quality, or condition). Ornery itself is of uncertain origin, possibly from ordinary with a meaning shift to "low, common," then to "ill-tempered."
