duboisine: /djuːˈbɔɪziːn/ | n. | 杜波伊辛，东莨菪碱 |  An alkaloid drug, derived from the plant Duboisia myoporoides, similar to atropine and hyoscyamine in its effects, used to dilate pupils and treat motion sickness. |  Duboisine is sometimes used to treat Parkinson's disease.  杜波伊辛有时用于治疗帕金森病。| Synonym: scopolamine, hyoscine | Antonym:  N/A (no direct antonym for a specific chemical compound) | noun
