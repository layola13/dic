forehorse:/ˈfɔːrhɔːrs/| n.|前导马，领头马；先锋，领导者|A person or thing that leads a group or movement or is at the forefront of something.|The company has been a forehorse in developing new technologies.  这家公司一直是新技术开发的先锋。|synonyms: leader, pioneer, vanguard, front-runner|antonyms: follower, laggard|noun


forehorse:/ˈfɔːrhɔːrs/| n.|最重要的；最显著的|The most important or prominent.|Economic issues were forehorse in the election campaign. 经济问题在竞选活动中是最重要的。|synonyms: paramount, primary, chief, principal|antonyms: secondary, minor, insignificant|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *forhors*, from Old English *forhors* (“horse in front”), equivalent to *fore- + horse*. Cognate with Old Frisian *forhors* (“forehorse”), Dutch *voorpaard* (“forehorse, leader”), German *Vorpferd* (“forehorse, leader”).
