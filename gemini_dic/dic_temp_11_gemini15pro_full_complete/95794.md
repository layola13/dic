inconveniencing: /ˌɪnkənˈviːniənsiŋ/ | v. |  使不便；打扰；麻烦 | Causing trouble or difficulty to someone; putting someone out. |  I hope I'm not inconveniencing you by asking you to do this.  我希望我叫你做这件事不会打扰你。 | synonyms: troubling, bothering, disturbing, disrupting, hindering | antonyms: accommodating, assisting, helping, aiding | verb

inconvenience: /ˌɪnkənˈviːniəns/ | n. | 不便；麻烦；打扰 | Trouble or difficulty caused to someone; something that causes this. | The flight delay was a major inconvenience.  航班延误造成很大的不便。| synonyms: trouble, difficulty, bother, nuisance, disruption | antonyms: convenience, ease, comfort | noun

inconvenient: /ˌɪnkənˈviːniənt/ | adj. | 不方便的；麻烦的；打扰的 | Causing trouble or difficulty. | It's very inconvenient for me to have to go out again. 我不得不再次外出，这很不方便。 | synonyms: troublesome, awkward, bothersome, disruptive, unsuitable | antonyms: convenient, suitable, appropriate, handy | adjective
