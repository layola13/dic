chastiser:/ˈtʃæstɪzər/ | n. | 惩罚者；责骂者；惩戒者 |  A person who chastises or punishes someone. | The teacher acted as a chastiser to the unruly student. 老师对不守规矩的学生充当了惩罚者的角色。 |  punisher, disciplinarian, corrector |  rewarder, praiser | noun

chastise:/tʃæˈstaɪz/ | vt. | 惩罚；责骂；严厉批评；责备 | To rebuke or reprimand severely.  To inflict punishment on. | The coach chastised the team for their poor performance. 教练因球队糟糕的表现而严厉批评他们。
He was chastised for his dishonesty. 他因为不诚实而受到惩罚。 |  reprimand, rebuke, scold, punish, discipline, castigate | praise, commend, reward | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French chastier (verb), chastiement (noun), from Latin castigāre "to correct, punish, chastise," from castus "pure, chaste" + -igāre (frequentative suffix).  The notion is of correcting someone to make them morally pure.
