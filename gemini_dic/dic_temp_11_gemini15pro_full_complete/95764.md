inconstantly: /ɪnˈkɑːnstəntli/ | adv. | 不断地，反复无常地 |  Not constantly; in a way that is not steady or dependable; erratically. | Her moods shifted inconstantly, from joy to sadness in the blink of an eye. 她的情绪反复无常，一眨眼就从高兴变成了悲伤。| The wind blew inconstantly, making it difficult to sail. 风刮得很不稳定，使航行很困难。 | 近义词: erratically, capriciously, fitfully, irregularly | 反义词: constantly, steadily, consistently, regularly | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From in- (not) + constant + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Constant" traces back through Old French to Latin "constans," present participle of "constare" (to stand firm).
