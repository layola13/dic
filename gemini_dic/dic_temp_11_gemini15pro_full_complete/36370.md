coindication:/ˌkoʊɪndɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 并发症，合并症；共同适应症 | A coindication is the presence of one or more additional diseases or conditions occurring with a primary disease or condition.  It can also refer to the use of a drug to treat two or more conditions simultaneously. |  The patient presented with depression and anxiety, a common coindication.  该患者表现出抑郁和焦虑，这是一种常见的并发症。

The drug is effective for both pain and inflammation, making it valuable in cases of coindication. 这种药物对疼痛和炎症都有效，使其在并发症病例中很有价值。 | 近义词: comorbidity, concomitant disorder | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, potentially "isolated condition") | 词性: noun
