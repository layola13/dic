galliambic:/ˌɡæliˈæmbɪk/
adjective|奔涌的，狂乱的，高亢激昂的 (尤指诗歌)|A galliambic poem is one written in galliambic meter, a type of verse characterized by a frenzied, excited rhythm.|Her galliambic verses pulsed with wild energy, reflecting the chaotic emotions of the protagonist. 她奔涌的诗句充满了狂野的能量，反映了主人公混乱的情绪。|近义词:frenzied, wild, ecstatic, impassioned|反义词:calm, subdued, tranquil|词性:adjective
galliambic:/ˌɡæliˈæmbɪk/
noun|高卢诗体，奔涌的诗|A galliambic is a verse written in galliambic meter.|His galliambics captured the frenetic energy of the Dionysian rituals. 他的高卢诗体捕捉到了狄俄尼索斯仪式的狂热能量。|近义词:verse, poem|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *galliambus*, from Greek *galliambos*, from *Gallus* "Gaul" + *iambos* "iamb".  The meter was associated with the Galli, priests of Cybele, whose rituals were characterized by frenzied dancing and music.
