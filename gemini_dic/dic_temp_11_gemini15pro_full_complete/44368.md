cross-corner:/ˌkrɒsˈkɔːnə(r)/ /ˌkrɑːsˈkɔːrnər/ | adj. | 对角线的，斜对角的 | Extending or passing from one corner to the opposite corner. |  The architect designed a cross-corner beam to support the ceiling.  建筑师设计了一根对角梁来支撑天花板。

cross-corner:/ˌkrɒsˈkɔːnə(r)/ /ˌkrɑːsˈkɔːrnər/ | n. |  斜对角 | A diagonal line or path. | He threw the ball cross-corner across the room. 他把球斜着扔过房间。


cross-corner:/ˌkrɒsˈkɔːnə(r)/ /ˌkrɑːsˈkɔːrnər/ | adv. | 斜对角地 | In a diagonal direction.  | The painting was hung cross-corner. 这幅画被斜着挂了起来。

Etymology:  "cross-" (from Old English "cros" and Old Norse "kross," meaning "cross, mark") + "corner" (from Old French "cornier," from Latin "cornu," meaning "horn, angle").  The combined meaning refers to a line or direction traversing from one corner to the opposite, forming a cross shape.


近义词: diagonal, oblique, kitty-corner, catty-corner (especially US)
反义词: straight, parallel
词性: adjective, noun, adverb
