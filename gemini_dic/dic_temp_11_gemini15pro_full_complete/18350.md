bellow:/ˈbeloʊ/ (US) /ˈbɛləʊ/ (UK)| v. n.|吼叫，怒吼；发出低沉的声音；风箱|To emit a deep, loud roar, typically of a bull or other large animal; a device that produces a stream of air when squeezed. |The bull bellowed in rage. 公牛愤怒地吼叫。|近义词：roar, yell, shout, bawl|反义词:whisper, murmur|verb

bellow:/ˈbeloʊ/ (US) /ˈbɛləʊ/ (UK)| n.|吼叫声；怒吼声|A deep, loud roar.|The bellow of the foghorn warned ships of the approaching rocks. 雾角的低沉吼叫警告船只注意接近的岩石。|近义词：roar, yell, shout, bawl|反义词:whisper, murmur|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bellan (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *bellanan, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰel- (“to resound, roar”). Cognate with Dutch bellen (“to bark, low”), German bellen (“to bark”), dialectal German bellen (“to bellow, roar”). Related to Old English bellan (“to yell”).
