mitering:/ˈmaɪtərɪŋ/ (US), /ˈmaɪtrɪŋ/ (UK)| n.|斜接，斜切|The act of joining two pieces of material at an angle, typically 45 degrees, to create a corner.  This is often used in woodworking, picture framing, and other construction projects.|He perfected the art of mitering in his carpentry apprenticeship.|近义词:beveling, joining|反义词: (No direct antonym, consider perhaps "butting" - joining edges squarely)|noun

mitre:/ˈmaɪtər/ (US), /ˈmaɪtrə/ (UK)| n. v.|斜接, 斜角;主教冠;斜切|1.(n.) A joint made by beveling each of two parts to be joined, usually at a 45 degree angle, to form a corner, often used for moldings. 2.(v.) To join two pieces of material by mitering.|1.(n.) The picture frame was assembled with tight mitres. 2. (v.) He carefully mitred the pieces of wood before gluing them.|近义词: bevel, chamfer (for the verb)|反义词: (No direct antonym, consider "butt joint")|noun, verb
