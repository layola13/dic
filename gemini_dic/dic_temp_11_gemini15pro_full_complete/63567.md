enviously:/ˈɛnviəsli/| adv.|羡慕地，嫉妒地|Feeling or showing envy; in an envious manner.  Experiencing or expressing a desire for something that someone else has.|She looked enviously at her friend's new car. 她羡慕地看着她朋友的新车。|近义词：jealously, covetously|反义词：admiringly, approvingly|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From envious + -ly.  Envious comes from the Old French envieus, which in turn derives from the Latin invidiosus, meaning "full of envy".  The Latin word is based on invidia, meaning "envy", from invidere "to look askance at, look maliciously at, envy," from in- "upon, against" + videre "to see".
