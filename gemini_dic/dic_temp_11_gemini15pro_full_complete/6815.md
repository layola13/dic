anarchists: /ˈænərkɪsts/ | n. | 无政府主义者 |  A person who believes in or tries to bring about anarchy.  Anarchy is the belief that government and laws are not necessary. |  The anarchists protested against the government's policies.  无政府主义者抗议政府的政策。

The anarchists distributed pamphlets advocating for a stateless society. 无政府主义者散发宣传无国家社会的小册子。 | 近义词: radicals, revolutionaries, rebels | 反义词: conservatives, conformists | 词性: Noun (plural)

Etymology: From the French *anarchiste*, from Ancient Greek *anarkhos* (ἀναρχος) meaning "without a ruler," from *an-* (ἀν-, "without") + *arkhos* (ἄρχος, "ruler, leader").
