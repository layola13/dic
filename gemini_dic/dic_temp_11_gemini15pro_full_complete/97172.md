infirmer:/ɪnˈfɜːrmər/ (US) /ɪnˈfɜːmə(r)/ (UK)| n.|医务室；医院；养老院|A place for the care of the infirm or sick, especially a hospital or other institution.|The school had an infirmer for students who felt unwell. 学校有一个医务室，供感觉不舒服的学生使用。|Synonyms: hospital, clinic, sickbay, sanatorium|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, consider "wellness center" or "health club" for a contrasting concept)|noun

infirm:/ɪnˈfɜːrm/ (US) /ɪnˈfɜːm/ (UK)| adj.|体弱的；不坚固的；意志薄弱的|Not physically or mentally strong, especially through age or illness; (of an object or structure) not strongly made or not stable|My grandmother became increasingly infirm as she got older. 我的祖母随着年龄的增长越来越虚弱了。|Synonyms: frail, weak, feeble, debilitated|Antonyms: strong, robust, healthy, sturdy|adjective
