druidic:/druːˈɪdɪk/ | adj. | 德鲁伊的；与德鲁伊教有关的 | Relating to the Druids, an ancient Celtic priesthood. |  The ancient stones held a druidic significance.  这些古老的石头具有德鲁伊教的意义。 |  The ceremony was steeped in druidic tradition.  这个仪式充满了德鲁伊教的传统。 | 近义词: Celtic, pagan | 反义词: modern, Christian | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Druid + -ic.  Druid comes from Gaulish *druwides, ultimately from Proto-Celtic *dru-wid-es ("very wise"), from *deru- ("oak") + *weid- ("to see, know").  The "-ic" suffix forms adjectives meaning "of or relating to."
