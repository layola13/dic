insidiate: /ɪnˈsɪdieɪt/| v. | 潜伏；暗中进行；伺机潜入 | To introduce or spread something harmful gradually and subtly, or to work oneself into a position of power or influence by subtle and cunning means. | The company insidiated itself into the market by offering free trials.  该公司通过提供免费试用逐渐渗透市场。

The virus insidiated the computer system, corrupting files slowly over time.  病毒潜伏在计算机系统中，随着时间的推移慢慢破坏文件。 | 近义词: infiltrate, insinuate, creep into | 反义词: expose, withdraw, retreat | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *insidiatus*, past participle of *insidiare* "to lie in ambush, plot against," from *insidiae* "ambush, treachery, plot," from *in-* "in, on" + *sedere* "to sit."
