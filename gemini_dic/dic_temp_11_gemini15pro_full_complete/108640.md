leaf:/liːf/ | n. | 叶子 | A flat, usually green part of a plant that grows from a stem or branch.  | The leaves on the trees are changing color. 树上的叶子正在变色。| synonyms: foliage, frond | antonym: root | noun

leaf:/liːf/ | v. | 翻页；浏览 | To turn the pages of a book or magazine quickly. | He leafed through the magazine, looking at the pictures. 他翻阅着杂志，看着图片。 | synonyms: browse, skim, flip | antonym: peruse | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lēaf, from Proto-Germanic *laubą (“leaf”), from Proto-Indo-European *leubʰ- (“to peel, to strip off bark, to pull, to tear”). Cognate with Scots leaf (“leaf”), West Frisian leaf (“leaf”), Dutch loof (“foliage”), German Laub (“foliage”), Danish løv (“foliage”), Swedish löv (“foliage”), Icelandic lauf (“leaf, foliage”). The verb is derived from the noun.
