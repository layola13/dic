half-farthing:/ˌhɑːf ˈfɑːrðɪŋ/ |n.| 半法新（英国旧时货币，等于四分之一便士）|A half-farthing was a coin worth one eighth of a penny, used in Britain from 1843 to 1856. |A half-farthing was not worth very much, even in those days.|Synonym: quarter-farthing (though less common) |Antonym:  (No direct antonym; perhaps pound or shilling as much larger units)|noun
