dumbs:/dʌmz/ | v. | 使…变哑，使…沉默 | Makes silent or unable to speak. |  He dumbs down his speeches so everyone can understand. 他简化了他的演讲，以便每个人都能理解。 |  She dumbed down the report to make it accessible to a wider audience. 她简化了报告，使其更容易被更广泛的读者理解。 | synonyms: silences, hushes, muffles, quiets | antonyms: amplifies, broadcasts, expresses, vocalizes | verb

dumbs:/dʌmz/ | n. | 笨蛋，傻瓜 (非正式，通常用复数形式) | Stupid people (informal, usually plural). |  "Look at those dumbs over there," he whispered. “看看那边那些傻瓜，”他低声说道。 | Only dumbs would go swimming in this weather. 只有傻瓜才会在这种天气去游泳。| synonyms: idiots, fools, morons, imbeciles | antonyms: geniuses, intellectuals, scholars, sages | noun

Etymology: From Middle English *dummen*, from Old English *dumb*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *dom* and German *dumm*.  The original meaning referred to being mute or unable to speak, and the sense of "stupid" developed later.
