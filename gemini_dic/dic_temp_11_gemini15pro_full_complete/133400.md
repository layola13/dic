nonfacts:/nɒnˈfækts/ |n. |非事实; 虚假信息| Things that are not true or accurate; falsehoods. | Many conspiracy theories are based on nonfacts and misinformation. 许多阴谋论都建立在非事实和错误信息之上。| The article was full of nonfacts and distortions. 这篇文章充满了不实信息和歪曲。 | synonyms: falsehoods, untruths, lies, inaccuracies, fabrications | antonyms: facts, truths, realities | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "non-" (not) + "facts" (things that are true).  "Non-" is a prefix of Latin origin meaning "not." "Fact" comes from the Latin "factum," meaning "something done" or "deed," which evolved to mean "something that has actually occurred."
