exaltation:/ˌɛksɔːlˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 兴奋，得意，崇高 |A feeling of intense happiness and elation.  A state of being highly praised or glorified. |  He felt a sense of exaltation as he reached the mountain summit. 他到达山顶时感到一阵兴奋。 The coronation ceremony was a moment of national exaltation. 加冕典礼是一个举国欢腾的时刻。 | 近义词: elation, joy, rapture, bliss, elevation, glorification | 反义词: dejection, depression, humiliation, degradation | 词性: 名词

exalt:/ɪɡˈzɔːlt/ | vt. | 提升，提拔；赞扬，歌颂 | To raise in rank, power, or character. To praise highly; glorify. | The king exalted his loyal knight to the position of general. 国王将他的忠诚骑士提升为将军。 The poem exalts the beauty of nature. 这首诗歌颂了大自然的美丽。 | 近义词: praise, glorify, elevate, promote, laud | 反义词:  debase, degrade, criticize, condemn | 词性: 动词
