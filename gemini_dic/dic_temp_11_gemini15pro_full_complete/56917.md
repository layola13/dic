dote:/doʊt/| vi.| 溺爱；昏聩；衰退 |To be excessively fond of; to show extreme affection. Also, to become mentally feeble, especially with age.|She dotes on her grandchildren, showering them with gifts and attention. 她溺爱她的孙子孙女，给他们大量的礼物和关注。|The old professor, once brilliant, began to dote and forget things. 曾经才华横溢的老教授开始变得昏聩，忘记事情。| synonyms: adore, cherish, idolize, indulge | antonyms: neglect, despise, disregard | verb

dote:/doʊt/| n.| 傻瓜；白痴 (obsolete, rarely used) |A foolish or silly person.|He was such a dote that he believed everything she said.  他真是个傻瓜，竟然相信她说的每一句话。 (This usage is archaic and rarely encountered.) | synonyms: fool, idiot, simpleton | antonyms: genius, sage | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *doten*, from Old English *dotian* "to be foolish," of uncertain origin, possibly related to Dutch *doten* "to rave, be delirious."
