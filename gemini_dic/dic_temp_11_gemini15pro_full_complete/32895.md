chondropterygii: /ˌkɒndrɒˈtɛrɪdʒɪaɪ/ /ˌkɑːndrəˈtɛrɪdʒiaɪ/ | n. |软骨鱼纲|A class of fishes that have skeletons made of cartilage, including sharks, rays, and chimaeras.  This term is now considered outdated and has been largely replaced by Chondrichthyes.|Sharks and rays belong to the class Chondropterygii, characterized by their cartilaginous skeletons.  鲨鱼和鳐鱼属于软骨鱼纲，其特征是它们的软骨骨骼。|Synonym: Chondrichthyes|Antonym: Osteichthyes|Noun

Etymology->
From Ancient Greek  χόνδρος (khóndros, “cartilage”) + πτέρυξ (ptérux, “wing, fin”) + -ii (class suffix).
