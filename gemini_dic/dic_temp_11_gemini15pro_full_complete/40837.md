convulsions:/kənˈvʌlʃənz/| n.|抽搐，痉挛|sudden, violent, and involuntary contractions of a group of muscles. |The child's high fever brought on convulsions.  这个孩子的高烧引起了抽搐。|
convulsions:/kənˈvʌlʃənz/| n.|动乱，骚动|a violent social or political upheaval. |The country was in convulsions after the assassination of the president.  总统遇刺后，国家陷入了动乱。|
convulsion:/kənˈvʌlʃən/| n.|（一阵）大笑|a fit of laughter. |She was in convulsions of laughter. 她笑得前仰后合。|
convulsive:/kənˈvʌlsɪv/| adj.|痉挛的，抽搐的|producing or characterized by convulsions. |He made a convulsive movement with his arm. 他的手臂做了一个痉挛的动作。|

近义词:spasms, seizures, fits, tremors, upheavals, turmoil
反义词:calm, stillness, peace, tranquility
词性: noun (convulsions), adjective (convulsive)

词源: From Late Latin convulsiōn- (stem of convulsiō ), from Latin convellere ‘pull violently together’, from con- ‘together’ + vellere ‘pluck’.
