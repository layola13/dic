comum:/koˈmũ/ (Brazilian Portuguese), /kuˈmũ/ (European Portuguese)| adj.|普通的，常见的，平常的|Common, usual, ordinary; shared, joint.  It describes something that is frequently encountered or widely available, or something that is held or used in common by two or more parties.|Exemplo: É comum chover em São Paulo no verão. (It's common to rain in São Paulo in the summer.)|Synonyms: usual, frequent, ordinary, habitual, normal, regular, standard, typical, general, widespread, prevalent, everyday, familiar, commonplace, run-of-the-mill, garden-variety|Antonyms: rare, unusual, uncommon, atypical, exceptional, extraordinary, infrequent, scarce, unique, singular|Adjective

comum:/koˈmũ/ (Brazilian Portuguese), /kuˈmũ/ (European Portuguese)| n.|Commons, common land|Land or resources belonging to or affecting the whole of a community.|Exemplo: Os animais pastavam no comum da aldeia. (The animals grazed on the village common.)|Synonyms: common land, communal land, shared land|Antonyms: private property|Noun
