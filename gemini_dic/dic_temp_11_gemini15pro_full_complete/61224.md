emacerate: /ɪˈmeɪsiˌeɪt/ /ɪˈmæsəˌreɪt/ | v. | 使消瘦；使衰弱；浸软 | to make or become thin and weak; to soften or break down (something, especially plant fibers) by soaking | The illness emacerated him to the point where he could barely stand.  这种疾病使他消瘦到几乎站不立的程度。| The body had been emacerated in the bog for centuries. 这具尸体在沼泽里浸泡了几个世纪，已经变得软烂。 | synonyms: weaken, enfeeble, debilitate, attenuate, macerate | antonyms: strengthen, invigorate, fortify | verb

emaceration: /ɪˌmeɪsiˈeɪʃən/ /ɪˌmæsəˈreɪʃən/ | n. | 消瘦；衰弱；浸软 | the process of becoming emaciated; the state of being emaciated | His emaciation was a clear sign of the disease's progression. 他的消瘦是疾病恶化的明显迹象。 | The archaeologist studied the emaceration of the ancient textiles. 考古学家研究了古代纺织品的浸软情况。 | synonyms: thinness, weakness, debility, attenuation | antonyms: strength, vigor, robustness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *emaceratus*, past participle of *emacerare* "to make lean, cause to waste away," from *e-* (variant of *ex-*) "out" + *macer* "lean, thin."
