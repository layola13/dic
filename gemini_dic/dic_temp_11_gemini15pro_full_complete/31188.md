changemaker:/ˈtʃeɪndʒˌmeɪkər/| n. |变革者，改革者，创新者|A person who causes significant social, political, or economic change.|例句：She is a true changemaker in the field of education. 她是教育领域的真正变革者。

例句：He was recognized as a changemaker for his innovative approach to business. 他因其创新的商业方法而被公认为变革者。|近义词：reformer, innovator, revolutionary, pioneer, trailblazer|反义词：conservative, traditionalist|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "change" + "maker".  "Change" comes from the Old French "changier" (to change), ultimately from the Latin "cambiare" (to barter, exchange).  "Maker" comes from Old English "maca" (maker, poet), related to "macian" (to make).
