mysterize: /ˈmɪstəˌraɪz/ | v. | 使神秘化，使迷惑 | To make something mysterious or obscure; to involve in mystery or obscurity. | He mysterized his past, never revealing details about his childhood. 他将自己的过去神秘化，从不透露关于童年的细节。 |  She mysterized the ending of her novel, leaving the readers in suspense. 她把小说的结局神秘化，让读者悬念丛生。 | 近义词: mystify, obscure, puzzle, bewilder | 反义词: clarify, explain, elucidate | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From mystery + -ize.  "Mystery" comes from Old French "misterie," ultimately from Latin "mysterium," meaning "secret rite, secret thing."  The suffix "-ize" is derived from Greek "-izein" and forms verbs meaning "to make or become."
