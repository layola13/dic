curts:/kɜːrts/ | adj. | 简慢无礼的；唐突的 | Rudely brief or abrupt, especially in speech or manner. |  She gave a curt reply and turned away. 她简慢地回答后就走开了。| synonyms: abrupt, brusque, terse, blunt, short, sharp, gruff | antonyms: polite, courteous, civil, respectful | adjective

curt:/kɜːrts/ | noun | (obsolete) A short, cutting sword. | A type of sword used in the past, characterized by its short and sharp blade. |  He drew his curt and prepared to defend himself.  他拔出短剑准备自卫。(这个用法已过时) | synonyms: sword, dagger | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a type of weapon) | noun


Etymology: From Middle English *curt, from Old French cort, from Latin curtus (“short”), from Proto-Italic *kurtos, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱr̥tós (“short”).
