/hwɑːt/ |pron. |什么| Used to ask for information specifying something. | What are you doing? 你在做什么？| Which, who | Nothing (as a direct antonym) | pronoun

USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
What do you eat for breakfast? = 你早餐吃什么？
What does she like to do in her free time? = 她空闲时间喜欢做什么？

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
What are you listening to right now? = 你现在在听什么？
What is he working on at the moment? = 他现在正在做什么？

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
What have you learned from this experience? = 你从这次经历中学到了什么？
What has she done to help? = 她做了什么来帮忙？

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
What have you been reading lately? = 你最近一直在读什么？
What has he been doing since he quit his job? = 他自从辞职以来一直在做什么？

一般过去时 (Simple Past):
What did you do yesterday? = 你昨天做了什么？
What did she say about the movie? = 她对这部电影说了些什么？

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
What were you doing when the phone rang? = 电话响的时候你在做什么？
What was she thinking about during the meeting? = 开会的时候她在想什么？

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
What had you done before you went to bed? = 你睡觉前做了什么？
What had they discussed before I arrived? = 在我到达之前他们讨论了什么？

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
What had you been working on before the interruption? = 打断之前你一直在做什么？
What had they been arguing about for so long? = 他们一直在争论什么这么久？

一般将来时 (Simple Future):
What will you do after graduation? = 毕业后你会做什么？
What will happen if we don't act now? = 如果我们现在不行动会发生什么？

将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
What will you be doing this time next week? = 下周这个时候你会在做什么？
What will she be studying next semester? = 她下学期会学习什么？

将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
What will you have achieved by the end of the year? = 到年底你将完成什么？
What will they have built by next summer? = 到明年夏天他们将建成什么？

将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
What will you have been doing for the past five years by then? = 到那时你将已经做了五年什么？
What will she have been studying by the time she graduates? = 到她毕业时，她将已经学习什么多久了？


PHRASE->

What the hell! = 搞什么鬼！
what if = 如果...会怎么样
what for = 为了什么
no matter what = 无论如何


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *what*, *hwat*, from Old English *hwæt*, from Proto-Germanic *hwat*, from Proto-Indo-European *kʷód*.  Cognates include Scots *what*, Saterland Frisian *wat*, West Frisian *wat*, Dutch *wat*, Low German *wat*, German *was*, Danish *hvad*, Swedish *vad*, Icelandic *hvað*.
