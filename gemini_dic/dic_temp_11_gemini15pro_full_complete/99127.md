intercentrum:/ˈɪntərˌsɛntrəm/| n.|间椎体，椎间体|A small, wedge-shaped bone or cartilage located between the centra of adjacent vertebrae, especially in some reptiles and amphibians.  It forms part or all of the vertebral column's ventral surface.|The intercentrum of the Ichthyostega provided support for the spine.|Synonym: hypocentrum|Antonym: pleurocentrum|noun
