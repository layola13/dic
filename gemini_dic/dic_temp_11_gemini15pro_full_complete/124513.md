moundy:/ˈmaʊndi/| adj.|土堆状的；隆起的；坟丘似的|Covered with mounds; resembling a mound.  Characterized by rising ground or mounds.|The landscape was moundy, dotted with small hills.|近义词：hilly, bumpy, undulating|反义词:flat, level|词性:形容词


Etymology: From *mound + -y.  "Mound" comes from Middle English *mount, from Old French *mont, ultimately from Latin *montem, the accusative of *mons (“mountain”).  The suffix "-y" forms adjectives meaning "characterized by" or "full of".
