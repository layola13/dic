goinging:  This is not a standard English word.  It appears to be a misspelling of "going," the present participle of the verb "go."  Therefore, the following analysis pertains to "going."


going: /ˈɡoʊɪŋ/ (US) /ˈɡəʊɪŋ/ (UK) | adj. | 进行的，流行的 |  The meeting is going well. 会议进行得很顺利。 |近义词： proceeding, progressing, current, fashionable | 反义词:  stopped, halted, outdated | 词性: adjective (primarily), also present participle of the verb "go"


going: /ˈɡoʊɪŋ/ (US) /ˈɡəʊɪŋ/ (UK) | n. | 行走，离去 | The going was tough through the deep snow.  在大雪中行走很艰难。| 近义词: departure, leaving | 反义词: arrival, coming | 词性: noun (less common usage)



ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *gānde (Northumbrian gānd), present participle of gān (“to go”), from Proto-Germanic *ganjandz, present participle of *gananą (“to go”). Cognate with West Frisian geande, Dutch gaande, German gehend.
