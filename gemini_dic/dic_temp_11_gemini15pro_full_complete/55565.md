disturb:/dɪˈstɜːrb/ (US) /dɪˈstɜːb/ (UK)| v.|打扰，扰乱，使不安|To interrupt the quiet, peace, or order of something or someone; to worry or upset someone.|例句：The noise disturbed my sleep. 噪音打扰了我的睡眠.|近义词：interrupt, bother, upset, agitate|反义词: soothe, calm, pacify|verb

disturbance:/dɪˈstɜːrbəns/ (US) /dɪˈstɜːbəns/ (UK)| n.|打扰，骚乱，不安|An interruption of peace, quiet, or order; a state of being worried or upset.|例句：There was a disturbance outside the building. 大楼外发生了骚乱.|近义词：interruption, commotion, disorder, unrest|反义词: peace, tranquility, order|noun

disturbed:/dɪˈstɜːrbd/ (US) /dɪˈstɜːbd/ (UK)| adj.|不安的，心烦意乱的，精神失常的|Worried and upset; mentally ill.|例句：She was deeply disturbed by the news. 她被这个消息深深地不安。|近义词：upset, troubled, agitated, distressed|反义词: calm, peaceful, tranquil|adjective
