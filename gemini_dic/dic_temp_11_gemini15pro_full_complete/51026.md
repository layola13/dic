deslum:/desˈlʌm/ |v. | 使眼花缭乱，使目眩 | To dazzle or impress greatly, often in a superficial or deceptive way. |  The fireworks deslumed the children. 烟花使孩子们眼花缭乱。| synonyms: dazzle, bedazzle, blind, fascinate | antonyms: bore, tire, disenchant | verb


Etymology: From Portuguese *deslumbrar*, from des- (meaning "un-" or "removal of") + lumbrar ("to illuminate" or "to shine"), ultimately from Latin lūmen ("light").
