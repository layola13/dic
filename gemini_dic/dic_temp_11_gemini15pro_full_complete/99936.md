internment:/ɪnˈtɜːnmənt/ | n. |拘留，扣押，收容|The state of being confined as a prisoner, especially for political or military reasons.  The act of confining someone in such a way.|例句：The internment of Japanese Americans during World War II was a dark chapter in American history.  二战期间对日裔美国人的拘留是美国历史上黑暗的一章。

例句：The government ordered the internment of enemy aliens. 政府下令拘留敌国侨民。|近义词：confinement, detention, imprisonment, incarceration|反义词：release, liberation, freedom|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *interné*, past participle of *interner* (“to confine, intern”), from Latin *internus* (“internal, inward”), from *inter* (“between, among”).
