intersectional:/ˌɪntərˈsekʃənəl/ |adj.| 交叉的，交叉性的，多重歧视的| Relating to the interconnected nature of social categorizations such as race, class, and gender, regarded as creating overlapping systems of discrimination or disadvantage. |例句：Intersectional feminism examines how various forms of oppression intersect.  交叉性女权主义研究各种形式的压迫是如何相互交叉的。

例句：Her research focuses on the intersectional experiences of Black women. 她的研究重点是黑人女性的交叉经验。| 近义词：interrelated, interwoven, multidimensional | 反义词：isolated, separate, singular | 词性：adjective

Etymology->
From intersection + -al.  Intersection itself comes from Latin *intersectio, from intersectus, past participle of intersecare "to cut in between," from inter- "between" + secare "to cut."
