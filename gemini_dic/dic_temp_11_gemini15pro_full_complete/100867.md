introverts: /ˈɪntrəvɜːrts/ (US) /ˈɪntrəvɜːts/ (UK)| n. |内向的人|A person who tends to be predominantly concerned with their own thoughts and feelings rather than with external things or making social contacts.|She's a bit of an introvert and prefers quiet evenings at home.|近义词: shy person, reserved person, recluse|反义词: extrovert, outgoing person|词性:名词

词源: From Latin intro- "inwardly" + vertere "to turn."  The noun form developed in the early 20th century, based on the adjective "introverted."
