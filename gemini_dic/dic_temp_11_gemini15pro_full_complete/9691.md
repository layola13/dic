antithesis:/ænˈtɪθəsɪs/
n.
对立；对照；反论
a person or thing that is the direct opposite of someone or something else.  A contrast or opposition between two things.  A figure of speech in which an opposition or contrast of ideas is expressed by parallelism of words that are the opposites of, or strongly contrasted with, each other, such as “hatred stirs up strife, but love covers all sins”.
Example: Love is the antithesis of selfishness. 爱是自私的对立面。

Example: His carefree nature is the antithesis of his brother's seriousness. 他无忧无虑的性格与他兄弟的严肃认真形成了鲜明的对比。
synonym: opposite, contrast, reverse, converse
antonym: similarity, resemblance, likeness
noun

antithesis:/ænˈtɪθəsɪs/
n.
对立面
a figure of speech in which an opposition or contrast of ideas is expressed by parallelism of words that are the opposites of, or strongly contrasted with, each other, such as “hatred stirs up strife, but love covers all sins”.
Example: “Give me liberty, or give me death” is a famous example of antithesis.  “不自由，毋宁死”是反衬的著名例子。

Example:  The antithesis between light and darkness is a recurring theme in the novel. 小说中反复出现的主题是光明与黑暗的对立。
synonym: juxtaposition, contrast, foil
antonym: similarity, resemblance
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin antithesis, from Ancient Greek ἀντίθεσις (antíthesis, “opposition, contrast”), from ἀντιτίθημι (antitíthēmi, “I set against, oppose”), from ἀντί (antí, “against”) + τίθημι (títhēmi, “I put, place”).
