dialysis:/daɪˈælɪsɪs/ | n. |透析，血液透析|The process of separating substances in solution by the difference in their rates of diffusion through a semipermeable membrane. It is used medically to remove waste products and excess fluid from the blood when the kidneys are not functioning properly.|The patient was put on dialysis after his kidneys failed. 病人的肾衰竭后开始接受透析。|Synonyms: haemodialysis, renal dialysis|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dialysis, from Greek διάλυσις (diálysis, “separation”), from διαλύειν (dialýein, “to dissolve, separate”), from διά (diá, “through, across”) + λύειν (lýein, “to loosen, dissolve”).
