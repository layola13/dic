goose-step:/ˈɡuːs step/ | n. | 正步，鹅步 | A stiff-legged marching step in which the legs are swung high and kept straight.  A way of marching used by some armies, especially formerly by German soldiers, in which each leg is lifted high and brought down stiffly. | The soldiers goose-stepped across the parade ground. 士兵们在阅兵场上走正步。 | synonyms: march, parade | antonyms: amble, stroll | noun

goose-step:/ˈɡuːs step/ | v. | 走正步 | To march in the goose-step. | The troops goose-stepped past the reviewing stand. 部队正步走过检阅台。 | synonyms: march, parade | antonyms: amble, stroll | verb
