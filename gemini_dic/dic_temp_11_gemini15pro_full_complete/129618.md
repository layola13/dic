non-connotative:/ˌnɒn kɒnˈəʊtətɪv/ | adj. | 非内涵的，无附加意义的 |  Lacking connotative meaning; denoting or relating to the explicit or primary meaning of a word or expression, not its implications or suggestions. | The word "table" is non-connotative; it simply refers to a piece of furniture. “桌子”这个词是非内涵的；它仅仅指一件家具。 | Example:  Scientists strive to use non-connotative language in their reports. 科学家努力在他们的报告中使用非内涵性语言。 | Synonyms: denotative, literal, objective | Antonyms: connotative, suggestive, subjective | word property: adjective
