calcavella:/ˌkælkəˈvɛlə/ | n. |卡尔卡韦拉葡萄酒| A dry white wine, originally from Portugal, made from a blend of local grape varieties. |  We enjoyed a bottle of crisp calcavella with our seafood dinner. 我们晚餐喝了一瓶清爽的卡尔卡韦拉葡萄酒，搭配海鲜。| Synonyms: vinho verde,  Albariño | Antonyms: (No direct antonyms for a specific wine type. Could use "sweet wine" as a general contrast) | Noun


Etymology: From Portuguese *calca velha*  meaning "treads old" or "worn heels", possibly referring to the vineyard workers treading the grapes or the wine being easy to drink and leading to unsteady steps.
