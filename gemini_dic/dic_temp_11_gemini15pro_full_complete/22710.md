bores:/bɔːrz/ /bɔːrz/
n.|钻孔，令人厌烦的人或事物|A bore is something that is tedious and uninteresting.  A person who bores others is also called a bore.|The lecture was a complete bore. 这场讲座真是无聊透顶。|Synonyms: nuisances, pests, dullards|Antonyms: delights, pleasures|Noun

bores:/bɔːrz/ /bɔːrz/
v. (third-person singular simple present bores, present participle boring, simple past and past participle bored)|使厌烦，钻孔|To bore means to make someone feel tired and impatient because something is uninteresting, or to make a hole in something using a tool.|He bored us with stories of his childhood. 他讲童年故事，听得我们厌烦。  The workers bored a tunnel through the mountain. 工人们在山中钻了一条隧道。|Synonyms: tires, irks, drills|Antonyms: excites, interests, amuses|Verb
