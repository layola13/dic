baculite:/ˈbækjuːlaɪt/ | n. |杆菊石|A straight-shelled ammonite, a type of extinct cephalopod mollusk that lived during the Cretaceous period.|The museum displayed a collection of well-preserved baculites.  博物馆展示了一批保存完好的杆菊石。|Synonyms: straight ammonoid, orthoceratite (similar but distinct group)|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps coiled ammonite as a contrasting shape)|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *baculite*, from Latin *baculus* ("staff, stick") + Greek suffix *-itēs* (denoting a mineral or fossil), so called because of its straight, staff-like shell.
