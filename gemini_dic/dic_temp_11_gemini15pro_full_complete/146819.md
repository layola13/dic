overimpose:/ˌoʊvərɪmˈpoʊz/| v. |过度强加；重叠放置|To impose something excessively or to place one thing over another, especially in printing or photography.|The artist overimposed images of nature onto urban landscapes in his work. 艺术家在他的作品中将自然的图像叠加到城市景观上。| The watermark was overimposed on the final image. 水印被叠加在最终图像上。|近义词:superimpose, overlay|反义词:remove, separate|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + impose.  "Over-" acts as a prefix indicating excess or "too much," while "impose" comes from Old French "imposer" and ultimately from Latin "imponere," meaning "to place upon."
