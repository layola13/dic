fold:/foʊld/ (US) /fəʊld/ (UK)| n. v. | 折叠；褶皱；羊圈；信徒 | A bend or crease in a flexible material, such as paper or cloth. To bend (something flexible and relatively flat) over on itself so that one part covers another. | Example: Fold the paper in half. 把纸对折。| Synonyms: crease, wrinkle, pleat, gather | Antonyms: unfold, straighten, flatten | noun
fold:/foʊld/ (US) /fəʊld/ (UK)| v. | 折叠；合拢；包；失败；屈服；拥抱 | To bend (something flexible and relatively flat) over on itself so that one part covers another. | Example: He folded his arms across his chest. 他双手交叉抱在胸前。| Synonyms: bend, double over, tuck, crease | Antonyms: unfold, unbend, straighten | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fealdan, from Proto-Germanic *falþaną, from Proto-Indo-European *pel- (to fold, bend).
