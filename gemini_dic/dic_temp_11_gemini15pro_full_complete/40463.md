contredanse:/ˈkɒntrədɑːns/ (UK), /ˈkɑːntrəˌdæns/ (US)| n. |孔特拉丹斯，对舞|A country dance, originating in France and popular in the 18th and 19th centuries, performed by two lines of couples facing each other.  It evolved into square dancing and other folk dances.|The orchestra struck up a lively contredanse.  乐队奏响了一支活泼的孔特拉丹斯。|Synonyms: country dance, quadrille|Antonyms: N/A|noun
