cockney:/ˈkɒkni/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkni/ (US)| n. adj.|伦敦东区人，伦敦东区方言|A native of East London, traditionally one born within earshot of the Bow Bells (the bells of St. Mary-le-Bow church); also, the dialect or accent traditionally spoken by such people, characterized by rhyming slang and distinctive pronunciation.|He spoke with a broad cockney accent. 他说话带有浓重的伦敦东区口音。|近义词：East Ender, Londoner|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps someone from a different region of England, e.g., a Geordie)|词性: noun, adjective

cockney:/ˈkɒkni/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkni/ (US)| adj.|伦敦东区的，伦敦东区人特有的|Characteristic of or relating to Cockneys or their dialect.|The cockney rhyming slang is quite unique. 伦敦东区押韵俚语非常独特。|近义词：East End|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps something like "upper-class British")|词性: adjective

词源:  From Middle English *coken-ey (“cock’s egg”, a small, malformed egg), from coken (“cock”) + ey (“egg”).  The term was originally derogatory, referring to effeminate city dwellers.
