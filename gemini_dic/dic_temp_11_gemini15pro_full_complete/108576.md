leach's:/liːtʃɪz/| n.| 滤液；沥出液 | Leach's refers to the liquid that has percolated through a substance, often carrying dissolved or extracted components. It's the possessive form of "leach," implying the liquid belongs to or is characteristic of the leached material.  | The leach's color indicated the presence of copper. 滤液的颜色表明存在铜。|Synonyms: filtrate, effluent, percolate | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, consider "solid residue" or "original substance") | noun

leach:/liːtʃ/| n.| 沥滤；滤灰堆 | A leach is a pile of material, typically ore or waste, through which water or other liquid percolates, extracting soluble substances. | The gold mine used a cyanide leach to extract the precious metal. 金矿使用氰化物滤灰堆提取贵金属。|Synonyms: heap, pile | Antonyms: N/A | noun

leach:/liːtʃ/| v.| 沥滤；过滤；浸提 |  To leach means to dissolve out soluble constituents from a solid by percolating a liquid through it. It can also refer to the gradual loss of nutrients or minerals from soil due to rainwater or irrigation.  | Rainwater leached the nutrients from the soil. 雨水从土壤中滤去了养分。|Synonyms: percolate, filter, extract, dissolve | Antonyms:  deposit, accumulate | verb
