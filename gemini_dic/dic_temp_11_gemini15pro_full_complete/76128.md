furzechat:/ˈfɜːrzˌtʃæt/ | n. | 金雀花丛间的闲聊; 无意义的谈话 | A casual, often inconsequential conversation, likened to the light chirping of birds hidden within a furze bush. |  We had a pleasant furzechat about the weather while waiting for the bus. 我们在等公共汽车的时候闲聊了一会儿天气。| 近义词：chit-chat, small talk, gossip, banter | 反义词：serious discussion, debate, formal conversation | 词性: noun

Etymology:
* **furze:** From Old English *fyrs*, related to West Germanic *furz*, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *furχiz*.  Refers to a spiny evergreen shrub with yellow flowers, also known as gorse.
* **chat:** From the verb "to chat," which originated in the late 16th century, likely as a shortening of "chatter."  From imitative origin, suggesting the sound of light, quick talk.
