katakanas:/ˌkɑːtəˈkɑːnəz/| n. |片假名|Katakana is one of two Japanese syllabaries, the other being hiragana. It is used primarily for loanwords, foreign names, onomatopoeia, scientific names, and sometimes for emphasis.|例句：Katakana is essential for reading and writing Japanese properly. 片假名对于正确读写日语至关重要。

例句：Many loanwords in Japanese are written in katakana.  日语中许多外来词是用片假名写的。|近义词：hiragana (in some contexts)|反义词: None |词性:noun

词源: From Japanese カタカナ (katakana), from 片仮名 (kata-kana, “fragmentary kana”),  as opposed to complete Chinese characters.
