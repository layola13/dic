bewares:/bɪˈwɛəz/
| v.| 警惕，小心 | This is an archaic form, rarely used now.  It means "be wary of" or "be cautious of". |  He bewares the dangers of the forest. 他小心森林里的危险。 (Archaic usage - avoid in modern English) | 近义词：be wary of, be cautious of, watch out for, guard against | 反义词：ignore, disregard | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bewarynge*, from Old English *bewarian* (“to guard, watch, keep, protect”), from Proto-Germanic *bewarōną (“to take care, pay attention”), from *be- (“around”) + *warōną (“to perceive, pay attention”); related to Old High German biwarōn (“to protect, defend”), Old Norse vara sik (“to beware”).
