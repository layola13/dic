coaxation:/koʊˈækʃən/| n.|哄骗；劝诱；诱骗|The process of gently persuading someone to do something, often by flattery or promises.|He used coaxation and bribery to get what he wanted. 他用哄骗和贿赂来得到他想要的东西。|Synonyms: persuasion, cajolery, inducement|Antonyms: coercion, force, intimidation|noun

coax:/koʊˈæks/| v.|哄骗；劝诱；诱骗|To persuade someone gently to do something, often by flattery or promises.|She coaxed him into going to the party. 她哄骗他去参加聚会。|Synonyms: persuade, cajole, wheedle|Antonyms: force, coerce, compel|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *coaxāre, frequentative of coaxō ("to urge, force, compel, restrain, curb, soothe, quiet"), probably of imitative origin. Related to coactus ("compelled, constrained").
