glazed:/ɡleɪzd/ | adj. |  光滑的，上釉的；呆滞的，无表情的 | Covered with a smooth, shiny surface, as if with a glaze; having a dull, unfocused look, as if in a daze. | The baker took the glazed doughnuts out of the oven.  面包师把上了釉的甜甜圈从烤箱里拿了出来。

The driver had a glazed look in his eyes after the accident. 事故发生后，司机眼神呆滞。 | 近义词：glossy, shiny, polished, glassy, vacant, blank  | 反义词：dull, matte, rough, alert | 词性: 形容词

glazed:/ɡleɪzd/ | v. |  给...上釉；使...变得光滑；(使)变得呆滞无神 | To cover with a glaze; to make smooth and shiny; to become dull and unfocused. | She glazed the pottery with a beautiful blue color. 她给陶器上了漂亮的蓝色釉。

The heat glazed his eyes.  高温使他目光呆滞。 | 近义词：polish, varnish, coat  | 反义词: roughen | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *glasier, from glas "glass," from Late Latin glacia, alteration of Latin glacies "ice."  The semantic shift from "ice" to "glass" likely occurred due to the visual similarity.  The meaning "to become dull or glassy-eyed" developed later.
