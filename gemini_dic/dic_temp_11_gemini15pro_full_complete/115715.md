margrave: /ˈmɑːrɡreɪv/ | n. | 边疆伯爵；藩侯 | A nobleman of high rank in the Holy Roman Empire, especially one who ruled a border province. | The margrave commanded the troops defending the eastern frontier. 边疆伯爵指挥部队保卫东部边境。| synonym: count, marquis | antonym: serf, peasant | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle High German *marcgrâve, from mark (“border region”) + grâve (“count”). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *markō (“border”) and *grabaz (“count”).
