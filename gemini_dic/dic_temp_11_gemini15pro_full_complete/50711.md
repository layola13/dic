dermopterygii: /ˌdɜːrmɒpˈtɛrɪdʒɪaɪ/ | n. | 皮翼鱼纲 | A class of ray-finned fishes characterized by fleshy, limb-like pectoral and pelvic fins. These fins are used for "walking" on the seabed. | Example: Coelacanths and lungfishes are members of the Dermopterygii.  腔棘鱼和肺鱼是皮翼鱼纲的成员。| Synonym: Sarcopterygii (in some classifications) | Antonym:  N/A | Noun
