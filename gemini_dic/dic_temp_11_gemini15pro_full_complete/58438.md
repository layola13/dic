ducket:/ˈdʌkɪt/| n.| 代币；门票；入场券；（尤指旧时的）罚款单 |A small piece of paper or card used as a ticket, token, or label, especially one used as a substitute for money.  Formerly, a receipt for payment of a fine or tax.|He paid his parking ducket. 他支付了停车罚款单。| synonyms: ticket, token, voucher, coupon, chit, slip, label | antonyms: debt (for fine ducket) | noun

ducket:/ˈdʌkɪt/| n.| （尤指英国旧时的）钱；现金 |Money, especially in British informal use. |He hasn't got a ducket to his name. 他身无分文。| synonyms: cash, money, funds, capital, resources | antonyms: debt, poverty | noun
