graffito:/ɡrəˈfiːtoʊ/ (US), /ɡræˈfiːtəʊ/ (UK)| n. |涂鸦; (尤指) 乱涂乱画|An inscription or drawing made on a wall or other surface, usually without permission and within public view. |例句：The walls were covered in graffiti. 墙上满是涂鸦。|例句：Someone had scrawled a graffito on the bus stop. 有人在公共汽车站乱涂乱画。|近义词：doodle, scribble, inscription|反义词:mural (when done with permission)|词性:noun

graffiti:/ɡrəˈfiːtiː/ (US), /ɡræˈfiːtiː/ (UK)| n. |涂鸦 (复数形式)|Plural form of graffito.  The inscriptions or drawings made on a wall or other surface, usually without permission and within public view. |例句：The artist's graffiti were considered vandalism by the city.  这位艺术家的涂鸦被市政府认为是故意破坏公物。|例句：Colorful graffiti adorned the sides of the abandoned buildings.  五颜六色的涂鸦装饰着废弃建筑物的侧面。|近义词：doodles, scribbles, inscriptions|反义词:murals (when done with permission)|词性:noun
