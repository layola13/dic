ballasters:/ˈbæləstərz/| n.|压舱物，镇流器，道碴|Heavy material, such as gravel, sand, or iron, placed low in a vessel to improve its stability.  Also, an electrical device used to limit the current in a discharge lamp.|The ship used rocks as ballasters. 这艘船用石头作为压舱物。|Synonyms: counterweight, stabilizer, ballast|Antonyms: destabilizer|noun

ballast:/ˈbæləst/| n. vt.|压舱物，镇流器，道碴; 加压舱物|Heavy material, such as gravel, sand, or iron, placed low in a vessel to improve its stability.  Also, an electrical device used to limit the current in a discharge lamp.  As a verb, it means to add ballast to something.|The ship was ballasted with iron ore. 这艘船用铁矿石加压舱物。|Synonyms: counterweight, stabilizer, ballasters|Antonyms: destabilizer|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *barlastʀ, from barr "bare" + last "load."  Originally referred to a load of stones carried by a ship when not carrying cargo.
