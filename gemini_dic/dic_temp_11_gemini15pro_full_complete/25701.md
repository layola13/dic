bunko:/ˈbʌŋkoʊ/ (US) /ˈbʌŋkəʊ/ (UK)| n. |骗局，欺诈；（尤指赌博中的）诈骗；廉价书|A swindle, a confidence trick, especially one involving gambling; a cheap book. |He lost all his money in a bunko game. 他在一次赌博骗局中输光了所有的钱。|Synonyms: scam, fraud, hoax, swindle, cheat, deception, trickery|Antonyms: honesty, truth, legitimacy|noun

bunko:/ˈbʌŋkoʊ/ (US) /ˈbʌŋkəʊ/ (UK)| vt. |欺骗，诈骗|To swindle, cheat. |They bunkoed him out of his life savings. 他们骗走了他一生的积蓄。|Synonyms: swindle, defraud, cheat, deceive, trick, dupe|Antonyms: be honest, deal fairly|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From the American slang "bunko steerer," referring to a con man who steers victims to a rigged gambling game.  The origin of "bunko" itself is uncertain, but it may be related to the Italian word "banco," meaning "bank" or "table," referring to the gambling table.
