hell-bent:/ˈhelˌbent/| adj.|一心想做...的；坚决要...的；执意要...的|Determined to achieve something at all costs; stubbornly pursuing a course of action, often regardless of the risks or consequences. It implies a single-minded focus and a refusal to be dissuaded.|He was hell-bent on revenge. 他一心想要复仇。

|She's hell-bent on getting a promotion. 她一心想得到晋升。
|近义词:determined, resolute, adamant, insistent, fixed, obsessed|反义词:hesitant, reluctant, ambivalent, wavering|词性:adjective

Etymology:
From "hell" + "bent."  "Bent" in this context means "determined" or "inclined." The image is of someone so determined to do something that they are leaning or angled towards hell, suggesting a reckless and unwavering pursuit.
