brooklike:/ˈbrʊklaɪk/| adj.|像小溪的，潺潺的|Resembling a brook, especially in its quiet, gentle flow or its small size.|The brooklike stream flowed gently through the meadow. 这条像小溪一样的小河缓缓流过草地。|近义词：streamlike, rivulet-like|反义词:raging, torrential|词性:形容词

Etymology:
brook (n.) Old English *brōc (implied by brōc "watercress," literally "brook plant"), from Proto-Germanic *bruka- (source also of Old Norse brū, Middle Dutch broke, Old High German bruoh, German Bruch "marsh," Gothic *bruks "useful"), from PIE root *bhreu- "to boil, bubble, effervesce, burn."
-like (suffix) Old English -lic, from Proto-Germanic *-likan (source also of Old Saxon -lik, Old Frisian -lik, Old Norse -ligr, Dutch -lijk, Old High German -lih, German -lich, Gothic -leiks), from PIE *-likos.
