misimprovement:/ˌmɪsɪmˈpruːvmənt/| n.|弄巧成拙，反效果|An action intended to improve something but having the opposite effect.|The new traffic system was a misimprovement, making congestion worse.|近义词:worsening, setback, deterioration|反义词:improvement, betterment, enhancement|词性:名词


Etymology:
mis- (prefix): from Old English, meaning "wrong," "bad," or "astray"
improvement: from Old French empreuvement, ultimately from Late Latin impromere "to improve,"
