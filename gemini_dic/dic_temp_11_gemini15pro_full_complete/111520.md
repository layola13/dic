loath:/loʊθ/ (US), /ləʊθ/ (UK)| adj.|不情愿的，厌恶的|Reluctant; unwilling; averse.  Feeling dislike or disgust.|例句：He was loath to admit his mistake. 他不愿承认自己的错误。

例句：She was loath to leave her home. 她不愿离开她的家。


近义词：reluctant, unwilling, averse, disinclined|反义词：willing, eager, keen|词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *lōth, from Old English lāþ (“hateful, hostile, loathsome; unpleasant, grievous; evil, wicked; slow, sluggish”), from Proto-Germanic *laiþaz (“hateful, loathsome”), from Proto-Indo-European *leyt- (“to stick, adhere, be sticky”). Cognate with Old Frisian lēth (“evil, bad”), Old Saxon leth (“evil, bad”), Old High German leid (“loathsome, troublesome”), Old Norse leiðr (“loathsome, hateful”), Gothic 𐌻𐌰𐌹𐌸𐍃 (laiþs, “evil, bad”). Related also to loathe.
