missionary:/ˈmɪʃənəri/ | n. adj. | 传教士；传教的 | A person sent on a religious mission, especially one sent to promote Christianity in a foreign country.  Also, relating to or characteristic of missionaries or their work. | Example 1: The missionary traveled to a remote village to spread the gospel.  这个传教士前往一个偏远的村庄传播福音。

Example 2:  She had a missionary zeal for helping the poor. 她有一种传教士般的热情去帮助穷人。 | Synonym: evangelist, apostle, preacher | Antonym: nonbeliever, atheist | noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *missionarius*, from Latin missio "act of sending," from mittere "to send."
