antireformer:/ˈæntɪrɪˌfɔːrmər/| n.| 反改革者，反对改革的人|A person who opposes reform or change, especially in politics or society.|The antireformers blocked any attempts to modernize the education system. 反对改革者阻止了任何使教育体系现代化的尝试。|近义词：conservative, reactionary, diehard|反义词: reformer, progressive, radical|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From anti- + reformer.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposed to," derived from Greek "anti." "Reformer" comes from the Late Middle English "reformen,"  meaning "to form again, reshape," from Old French "reformer," ultimately from Latin "reformare,"  from "re-" (again) + "formare" (to form).
