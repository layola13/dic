escapement: /ɪˈskeɪpmənt/| n.|擒纵机构；逃脱；解脱|A mechanism in a mechanical clock or watch that transfers energy to the oscillator (e.g., pendulum or balance wheel) and allows the oscillator to advance at a controlled rate, thus regulating the timekeeping.|The watchmaker carefully adjusted the escapement.|Synonyms: escape, release, liberation|Antonyms: confinement, imprisonment, capture|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "eschapement" (escape, flight), derived from "eschaper" (to escape), which comes from the Vulgar Latin "excappare" (to escape from a cape or cloak).  The specialized horological meaning developed later.
