beckoner: /ˈbɛkənər/ | n. | 召唤者；示意者 | Someone who beckons.  A person who signals or summons someone to approach. | The ferryman was the beckoner of souls across the river Styx. 摆渡人是召唤灵魂穿过冥河斯堤克斯的人。| The flashing lights of the city acted as a beckoner to the weary travelers.  城市的灯光闪烁，仿佛在召唤着疲惫的旅行者。 |  summoner, signaler, caller | dismisser, ignorer | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "beckon" + "-er" (agent suffix). "Beckon" comes from the Old English "beacnian" meaning "to make a sign, signal."
