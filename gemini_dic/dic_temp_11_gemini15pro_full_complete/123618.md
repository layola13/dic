词源：From Old English *mōnaþ (attested only in compounds), from Proto-Germanic *mēnōþs, from Proto-Indo-European *mēnsis (source also of Old Frisian mōnath, Old Saxon mānoth, Old High German mānod, Old Norse mānaðr, Gothic 𐌼𐌴𐌽𐍉𐌸𐍃 (mēnōþs), Latin mēnsis, Old Irish mí, Greek μήν (mēn), Sanskrit मास (māsa)), from *meH₁-n-o-s, from the root *meH₁- (“to measure”). Cognate with moon.  The word is literally “lunation”, the period from one new moon to the next.
