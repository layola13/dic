cholerically:/kəˈlɛrɪkli/ | adv. |暴躁地，易怒地|in a choleric or bad-tempered manner; angrily.|He responded cholerically to the slightest provocation. 他对哪怕最轻微的挑衅都暴跳如雷。|Synonyms: angrily, furiously, irately, wrathfully|Antonyms: calmly, patiently, peacefully|Adverb

choleric:/ˈkɒlərɪk/ | adj. |暴躁的，易怒的|easily angered; bad-tempered.|He has a choleric temperament. 他性情暴躁。|Synonyms: bad-tempered, irascible, irritable, short-tempered|Antonyms: calm, even-tempered, patient|Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *colerik*, from Old French *colerique*, from Late Latin *cholericus*, from Latin *cholera* (“bile”), from Ancient Greek *χολέρα* (*kholéra*).  The Ancient Greeks believed that an excess of yellow bile (one of the four humors) caused anger and irritability.
