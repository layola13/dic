interdict:/ˈɪntərˌdɪkt/ (US) /ˌɪntəˈdɪkt/ (UK)| vt.|禁止，禁令；阻碍，阻止|To prohibit or forbid officially. To impede or stop.|The judge interdicted the company from selling the faulty product. 法官禁止该公司销售有缺陷的产品。|Synonyms: prohibit, forbid, ban, bar, restrain, preclude|Antonyms: permit, allow, authorize|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *interdictum*, noun use of neuter of past participle of Latin interdicere (“to forbid, prohibit, interpose”), from inter (“between”) + dicere (“to say, speak”).
