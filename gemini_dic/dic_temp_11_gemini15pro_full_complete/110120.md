lictor:/ˈlɪktər/| n. |侍从官，执法官|An officer in ancient Rome who attended on a magistrate, bearing the fasces as a symbol of power.|The lictors cleared a path through the crowd for the consul. 侍从官为执政官在人群中开辟了一条道路。|Synonyms: attendant, guard, official|Antonyms: commoner, civilian|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *lictor*, from *ligare* "to bind, tie," referring to the fasces, a bundle of rods bound together around an axe, carried by lictors as a symbol of the magistrate's power to punish.
