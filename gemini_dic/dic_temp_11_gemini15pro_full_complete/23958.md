breastplough: /ˈbrɛstplaʊ/ | n. | 乳沟犁, 胸犁; (在泥泞中)匍匐前进| A method of tillage used in the Middle Ages where a person would be harnessed to a plough and pull it using their chest and shoulders, or the act of moving through mud or snow on one's chest. |  The farmer used a breastplough to prepare the field for planting.  农民用胸犁来准备耕地。  Soldiers breastploughed through the deep snow. 士兵们在深雪中匍匐前进。 | 近义词:  belly-ploughing, crawling | 反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑walking, running) | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From breast + plough.  The word describes both the tool and the action.  "Breast" refers to the chest being used to pull, and "plough" refers to the agricultural implement or the act of tilling the earth.  The term also gained metaphorical usage for any arduous crawling or dragging motion.
