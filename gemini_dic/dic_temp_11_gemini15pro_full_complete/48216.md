deconsecrate:/ˌdiːˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/ | v. | 剥夺神圣性；使不再神圣； | To deprive (a consecrated place or object) of its sacred character. | The church was deconsecrated and turned into a community center. 教堂被剥夺了神圣性，变成了社区中心。| The altar was deconsecrated before being removed from the church. 祭坛在从教堂移走之前被剥夺了神圣性。| 近义词：desecrate, profane | 反义词：consecrate, sanctify | verb

deconsecration:/ˌdiːˌkɒnsɪˈkreɪʃn/ | n. | 剥夺神圣性；撤销祝圣 | The act of depriving a consecrated place or object of its sacred character. | The deconsecration of the church saddened many parishioners. 教堂的去圣化使许多教区居民感到悲伤。| The deconsecration ceremony involved removing the altar and other sacred objects. 去圣化仪式包括移除祭坛和其他圣物。| 近义词：desecration, profanation | 反义词：consecration, sanctification | noun
