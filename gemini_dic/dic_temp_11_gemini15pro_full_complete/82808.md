guised:/ɡaɪzd/| adj.|伪装的，假扮的|dressed or appearing in a different way to hide true form or intentions; disguised as something/someone|例句：He entered the enemy camp guised as a soldier. 他伪装成士兵进入了敌营。|例句：The pickpocket guised himself as a tourist. 扒手把自己伪装成游客。|近义词：camouflaged, masked, cloaked, concealed, veiled|反义词：exposed, revealed, uncovered|词性：形容词

Etymology:
From Middle English *gisen, *gysen (“to clothe, dress, attire, equip”), from Old French guiser (“to fashion, arrange, prepare”), from Frankish *wīsōn (“to clothe, dress”), from Proto-Germanic *wīsōną (“manner, wise, fashion, guise”), from Proto-Indo-European *weh₁y- (“to turn, twist, wind”). Cognate with Old High German wīsan (“to show, exhibit”), Old English wīsan (“to show, guide, direct”).
