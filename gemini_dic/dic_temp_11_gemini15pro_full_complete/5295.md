allotrope:/ˈæləˌtroʊp/ (US), /ˈælətrəʊp/ (UK)| n. |同素异形体|One of two or more different physical forms in which an element can exist.  Different allotropes of an element have different physical properties because their atoms are bonded together in different ways.|Diamond and graphite are allotropes of carbon. 钻石和石墨是碳的同素异形体。|synonym: polymorph, modification|antonym: N/A|noun
