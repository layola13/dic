hematoxylin: /ˌhiːməˈtɒksɪlɪn/ (US) /ˌhiːməˈtɒksɪlɪn/ (UK)| n. |苏木精|A dark purple dye extracted from the heartwood of the logwood tree (Haematoxylum campechianum), used especially as a biological stain. It stains cell nuclei blue-violet due to its affinity for negatively charged substances such as DNA. |The pathologist used hematoxylin and eosin to stain the tissue sample. 病理学家用苏木精和伊红染色组织样本。|Synonyms: haematoxylin|Antonyms: eosin (in the context of staining, as they are often used together in contrasting colours)|noun

hematoxylin: /ˌhiːməˈtɒksɪlɪn/ (US) /ˌhiːməˈtɒksɪlɪn/ (UK)| n. |苏木素|A crystalline compound, C16H14O6, derived from logwood and used as a blue dye, especially in staining microscopic sections.|Hematoxylin is a common component in many histological stains. 苏木素是许多组织学染色剂的常见成分。|Synonyms: haematoxylin|Antonyms: eosin (in the context of staining, as they are often used together in contrasting colours)|noun
