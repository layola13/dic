octant:/ˈɒktənt/ (UK) /ˈɑːktənt/ (US)| n. |八分圆；八分仪；四十五度角；卦限|An octant is one of eight equal parts of a circle or sphere, or a device used for measuring angles, especially the altitude of stars, to navigate at sea. It can also refer to any of the eight regions into which three-dimensional space is divided by three mutually perpendicular planes. |The ship's navigator used an octant to determine their position. 船上的领航员用八分仪来确定他们的位置。
