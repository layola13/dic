nudgers:/ˈnʌdʒərz/
n.|推动者；轻推者|People who encourage or gently persuade others to do something, especially something beneficial to themselves or society.|Governments are increasingly using nudgers to influence public behavior. 政府越来越多地使用推动者来影响公众行为。|Synonyms: influencers, persuaders, prompters, guides|Antonyms: obstructers, hinderers|noun

nudger:/ˈnʌdʒər/
n.|推动者；轻推者|A person who encourages or gently persuades others to do something, especially something beneficial to themselves or society.|The book "Nudge" explores how nudgers can help people make better decisions.  《助推》这本书探讨了推动者如何帮助人们做出更好的决策。|Synonyms: influencer, persuader, prompter, guide|Antonyms: obstructer, hinderer|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "nudge" + "-er" (agent suffix). "Nudge" originates from the late 18th century, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Norwegian "nugga" meaning "to push, rub."
