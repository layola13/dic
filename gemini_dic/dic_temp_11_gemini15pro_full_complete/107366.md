lamellirostres:/ˌlæməˈlɪrɒstriːz/
| n. |  板嘴鸟类 | A group of birds characterized by their flat, broad bills with lamellae (thin, plate-like structures) for filtering food from water.  These birds are also known as "filter-feeding birds". |  Lamellirostres such as ducks and geese are common in wetlands.  板嘴鸟类，如鸭和鹅，在湿地很常见。 |  Anseriformes | N/A | Noun
etymology: From Latin *lamella* (“thin plate”) + *rostrum* (“beak, bill”).
