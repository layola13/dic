brachioganoidei:/ˌbrækiˌoʊɡəˈnɔɪdiˌaɪ/
noun
腕甲鱼目
An extinct order of bony fishes from the Devonian period, characterized by heavily armored bodies and a heterocercal tail.  They are considered a transitional group between placoderms and true bony fishes.
The Brachioganoidei, with their bony head shields, offer a glimpse into the evolution of early vertebrates.  腕甲鱼目凭借其骨质头部护盾，为了解早期脊椎动物的进化提供了一瞥。
Fossil evidence suggests that Brachioganoidei inhabited freshwater environments. 化石证据表明，腕甲鱼目栖息于淡水环境中。
近义词:  Palaeoniscoidea (a broader group to which they belong)
反义词:  N/A (extinct group, no direct opposite)
词性:  n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek βραχίων (brakhíōn, “arm”) + γάνος (gános, “brightness, sheen”) +  -oidea (a taxonomic suffix denoting order).  The name refers to the bony plates covering their bodies, which may have had a shiny appearance.
