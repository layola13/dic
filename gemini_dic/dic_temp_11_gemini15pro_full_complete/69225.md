fearmongers:/ˈfɪəmʌŋɡərz/
noun
散布恐慌的人；危言耸听者
People who spread fear and anxiety, often by exaggerating dangers or predicting calamities.
The fearmongers were predicting economic collapse. 危言耸听者预言经济会崩溃。
近义词:alarmists, scaremongers
反义词:reassurers, optimists
词性:n.

Etymology:
From "fear" + "monger," from Old English "mangere" meaning "trader" or "dealer," ultimately from Latin "mango" meaning "dealer," "monger."  The word implies someone who "deals" in fear.
