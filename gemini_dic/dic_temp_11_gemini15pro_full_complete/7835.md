antanaclasis:/ˌæntənəˈklæsɪs/
n.
antanaclasis (修辞)
A rhetorical device that consists in repeating a word or phrase but with a different meaning.  Using the same word with two different meanings.
例句：The scriptures are written to be read; reading the scriptures writes the scriptures in our hearts. 圣经是为了让人阅读而写的；阅读圣经将圣经写入我们的心中。(read 第一次指“阅读”，第二次指“理解并接受”)

例句：If we don’t hang together, we shall most assuredly hang separately. 如果我们不团结一致，我们肯定会被一个个绞死。(hang 第一次指“团结”，第二次指“绞死”)

近义词：pun, paronomasia, double entendre
反义词: N/A
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin, from Greek *antanáklasis*, from *antanaklán*, to bend back, reflect.
