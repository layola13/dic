cough up:/ˈkɒf ʌp/ (UK), /ˈkɔːf ʌp/ (US)| phrasal verb |咳出；勉强提供（尤指钱）|To expel something from the lungs or throat by coughing. Also, to reluctantly give something, especially money.|例句：He coughed up some phlegm. 他咳出了一些痰。|近义词：fork out, shell out, pay up|反义词: keep, withhold|词性:短语动词

cough up:/ˈkɒf ʌp/ (UK), /ˈkɔːf ʌp/ (US)| phrasal verb |咳出；勉强提供（尤指钱）|To reluctantly provide something, especially money.|例句：I had to cough up $500 for the repairs. 我不得不掏出500美元来修理。|近义词：fork over, hand over|反义词: retain, keep back|词性:短语动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the literal meaning of coughing something up from the throat or lungs, the figurative meaning of reluctantly giving something (usually money) developed.
