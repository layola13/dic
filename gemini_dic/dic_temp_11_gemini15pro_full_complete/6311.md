amortized:/ˈæmərtaɪzd/ /əˈmɔːrtaɪzd/ | adj. | 摊销的，分期偿还的 |  Reduced gradually by periodic payments spread over a term.  |  The cost of the new equipment was amortized over five years.  新设备的成本在五年内摊销。 | synonyms: depreciated, expensed, written off | antonyms: capitalized, accrued | adjective

amortize:/ˈæmərtaɪz/ /əˈmɔːrtaɪz/ | v. | 摊销，分期偿还 |  To gradually reduce or write off the cost of an asset over a period of time. | The company amortized the cost of the new building over 20 years.  公司将新大楼的成本在20年内摊销。 | synonyms: depreciate, expense, write off | antonyms: capitalize, accrue | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *amortir "to kill, deaden, extinguish," from a- "to" + morir "to die," from Latin mori.  The financial sense developed from the idea of "killing" or extinguishing a debt.
