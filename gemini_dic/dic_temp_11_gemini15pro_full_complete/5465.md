alog: /ˈeɪlɒɡ/ (British) /ˈeɪlɔːɡ/ (American)| n.| 后记，结尾，算法|A section or speech at the end of a book or play that serves as a comment on or a conclusion to what has happened.|The play concluded with a poignant alog delivered by the protagonist.|近义词：epilogue, afterword, coda|反义词:prologue, preface|词性:名词

词源：From Greek *álogos* "speech, discourse," related to *légein* "to speak." Originally referred to a speech, especially one delivered at the end of a play. The modern sense, referring to a concluding section of a book, developed later.  The computer science meaning as an abbreviation of "algorithm" is a relatively recent development.
