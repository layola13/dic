murmurish:/ˈmɜːrmərɪʃ/ | adj. | 爱嘀咕的；爱抱怨的 | Inclined to murmur; given to complaining in a low voice. |  The murmurish crowd expressed their dissatisfaction.  爱嘀咕的人群表达了他们的不满。| synonyms: complaining, grumbling, muttering, whiny | antonyms: content, satisfied, cheerful, agreeable | adjective

murmur:/ˈmɜːrmər/ | n. v. | 低语，小声说；抱怨 | a low, continuous sound, like the sound of a stream or the wind; to say something in a low, soft, or indistinct voice; to complain in a low voice. | The murmur of the crowd grew louder. 人群的低语声越来越大。  She murmured a prayer. 她低声祈祷。| synonyms: whisper, mutter, grumble, drone | antonyms: shout, yell, proclaim, declare | noun, verb


Etymology:
From Middle English *murmuren*, from Anglo-Norman *murmurer*, from Latin *murmurāre*, present active infinitive of *murmurō* (“to murmur”).
