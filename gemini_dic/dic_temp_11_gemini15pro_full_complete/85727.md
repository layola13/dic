haw-haw:/hɔːˈhɔː/| n. |嘲笑声，大笑声|A loud, boisterous laugh, often derisive or scornful.|The bully let out a haw-haw when he tripped the smaller boy.|近义词：guffaw, roar, cackle|反义词: sob, whimper |词性:名词
haw-haw:/ˈhɔːhɔː/| adj. | (英国)上流社会的，势利的|Characteristic of the upper class or high society, often in a snobbish or exclusive way.  (Primarily British usage.)|He spoke with a haw-haw accent that made him sound rather pretentious. |近义词：posh, snooty, aristocratic|反义词: common, working-class |词性:形容词

词源:  Imitative of the sound of laughter.  The adjectival use likely arose from the perceived association of this type of laughter with the upper classes.
