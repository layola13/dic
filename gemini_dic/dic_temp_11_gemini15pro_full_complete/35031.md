close-cropped:/kloʊs krɒpt/ /kloʊs krɑːpt/ | adj. | (植被)修剪得很短的 |  cut very short, especially referring to vegetation like grass or hedges.  | The lawn was close-cropped and immaculate. 草坪修剪得很短，一尘不染。| neatly trimmed, short | overgrown, long | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "close," meaning near or tight, and "cropped," the past participle of "crop," meaning to cut short.  The word originally referred to the practice of cutting hair or vegetation very short.
