monologue: /ˈmɒnəlɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈmɑːnəlɔːɡ/ (US)| n. |独白; 独白体作品|A long speech by one person in a play, film/movie, etc., or as part of a theatrical or broadcast programme. A long and typically tedious speech by one person during a conversation.|Hamlet's famous monologue, "To be or not to be..." 哈姆雷特的著名独白，“生存还是毁灭……”|近义词：soliloquy, speech, address|反义词:dialogue, conversation|词性:noun

monolog: /ˈmɒnəlɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈmɑːnəlɔːɡ/ (US)| v. |作独白; 发表冗长的讲话|Speak or give a monologue.|He monologued about his childhood. 他滔滔不绝地讲述了自己的童年。|近义词：soliloquize, speechify, rant|反义词:converse, dialogue|词性:verb
