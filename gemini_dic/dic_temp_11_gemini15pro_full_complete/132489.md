nondescript:/ˈnɒndɪskrɪpt/ (UK) /ˌnɑːndɪˈskrɪpt/ (US)| adj. n.|平凡的，毫无特色的；难以形容的人或物|Lacking distinctive or interesting features or characteristics; dull and ordinary.|The building was a nondescript gray structure.|近义词：unremarkable, ordinary, undistinguished, commonplace, characterless, featureless|反义词: distinctive, remarkable, unique, striking, memorable|词性:形容词

nondescript:/ˈnɒndɪskrɪpt/ (UK) /ˌnɑːndɪˈskrɪpt/ (US)| n.|难以形容的人或物|A person or thing that is difficult to describe because of a lack of distinctive features.|He was a nondescript in a gray suit.|近义词：nonentity, nobody|反义词: somebody, personality|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + descript (from Latin dēscrīptus, past participle of dēscrībere ‘to describe’).
