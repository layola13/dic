curfews: /ˈkɜːrfjuːz/ (US) /ˈkɜːfjuːz/ (UK)
|n.|宵禁；宵禁令|A regulation requiring people to remain indoors between specified hours, typically at night.|Many cities imposed curfews after the riots. 骚乱发生后，许多城市实施了宵禁。|The curfew requires everyone to be indoors by 10 p.m.  宵禁要求每个人晚上10点之前待在室内。|synonyms: night-time ban, restriction, prohibition|antonyms: freedom, liberty|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *cuevrefeu (“cover fire”), from cuevre (“cover”) + feu (“fire”).  Originally referred to the practice of covering fires at a fixed time in the evening as a precaution against fire.
