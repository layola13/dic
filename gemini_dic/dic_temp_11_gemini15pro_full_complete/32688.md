chlamydia:/kləˈmɪdiə/ | n. | 衣原体 | A very small parasitic bacterium that, like a virus, requires the biochemical mechanisms of a host cell in order to reproduce. Several species cause disease in humans, including trachoma, psittacosis, and a sexually transmitted infection that may cause infertility. | Chlamydia is a common sexually transmitted infection. 衣原体是一种常见的性传播感染。|  Synonyms:  STI, STD, venereal disease | Antonyms:  health, wellness | noun

chlamydias: /kləˈmɪdiəz/ | n. | 衣原体（复数）| Plural form of chlamydia, referring to multiple species or infections of the bacterium. | Many different types of chlamydias can infect humans. 许多不同类型的衣原体可以感染人类。| Synonyms:  STIs, STDs, venereal diseases | Antonyms: health, wellness | noun
