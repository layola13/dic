inject:/ɪnˈdʒekt/ | v. | 注射；注入；投入 | to introduce a substance into something, typically by using a syringe or other device; also, figuratively, adding a quality or element to something | The doctor injected the medicine into the patient's arm. 医生把药注射到病人的胳膊里。 |  He injected some humor into his presentation. 他在演讲中加入了一些幽默。 | synonyms: administer, infuse, introduce, insert | antonyms: withdraw, extract, remove | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *inictire, from Latin inicere (“to throw in, insert”), from in- (“in, into”) + iacere (“to throw”).
