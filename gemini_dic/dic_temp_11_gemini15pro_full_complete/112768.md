lubberly:/ˈlʌbərli/| adj.|笨拙的，笨手笨脚的；迟钝的|Clumsy, awkward, and unskilled; slow and heavy in movement.  Referring to someone lacking physical grace and coordination, often in a nautical context, suggesting someone unsuitable for seafaring.|The lubberly sailor kept tripping over ropes and bumping into things.  那个笨手笨脚的水手总是被绳子绊倒，撞到东西。|Near synonyms: clumsy, awkward, ungainly, inept, gawky|Antonyms: graceful, agile, nimble, deft, coordinated|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From *lubber* (a clumsy, awkward person) + *-ly* (adverbial suffix forming adjectives).  *Lubber* is of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle Low German *lubben* (to be clumsy).
