accident: /ˈæksɪdənt/ (US) /ˈæksɪd(ə)nt/ (UK)| n.| 事故，意外，偶然事件|An unfortunate incident that happens unexpectedly and unintentionally, typically resulting in damage or injury.|例句：The car accident caused a lot of traffic. 这场车祸造成了严重的交通堵塞。|近义词：incident, mishap, misfortune, casualty|反义词: intention, plan, design|noun


accident: /ˈæksɪdənt/ (US) /ˈæksɪd(ə)nt/ (UK)| adj. (obsolete)| 偶然的|Happening by chance or unexpectedly; unintentional.|例句：An accident meeting led to a lifelong friendship. 一次偶然的相遇促成了一段终生的友谊. (This usage is now largely obsolete.)|近义词：incidental, chance, fortuitous|反义词: intentional, deliberate, planned|adjective (obsolete)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *accidens*, present participle of *accidere* "to happen, befall, fall to one's lot, come to pass," from *ad* "to" + *cadere* "to fall."
