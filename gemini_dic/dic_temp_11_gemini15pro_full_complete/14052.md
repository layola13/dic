austerity:/ɔːˈstɛrəti/ |n.| 紧缩；朴素；严厉；苦行 |A state of reduced spending and increased frugality, often imposed by governments or institutions in times of economic hardship.  Also refers to a stern or severe manner or appearance. |The government imposed austerity measures to combat the recession. 政府实施紧缩措施以应对经济衰退。

The wartime austerity meant there was little food or fuel. 战时的紧缩意味着食物和燃料都很少。|近义词：severity, strictness, frugality, asceticism |反义词：luxury, extravagance, lavishness|词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *austerite*, from Old French *austerite*, from Latin *aucteritās*, from *aucterus* ("harsh, severe, sour, tart"), probably from *augeo* ("to dry, parch").
