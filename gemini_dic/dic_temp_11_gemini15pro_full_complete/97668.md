inhibitory:/ɪnˈhɪbɪtəri/ |adj.|抑制的，禁止的| hindering or preventing an action, impulse, or feeling|  The drug has an inhibitory effect on the central nervous system. 这种药物对中枢神经系统有抑制作用。| synonyms: repressive, restrictive,  constraining | antonyms: stimulatory, excitatory |Adjective

inhibition:/ˌɪnhɪˈbɪʃən/ |n.|抑制，禁止|a feeling that makes one self-conscious and unable to act in a relaxed and natural way|He overcame his inhibition and spoke out. 他克服了自己的拘谨，大胆地说了出来。| synonyms: restraint, self-consciousness, reserve | antonyms: boldness, confidence |Noun

inhibit:/ɪnˈhɪbɪt/ |v.|抑制，禁止|hinder or prevent (an action or process)| This drug inhibits the growth of cancerous cells. 这种药物抑制癌细胞的生长。| synonyms: restrain, curb, suppress | antonyms: encourage, promote |Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *inhibitorius, from Latin inhibitus, past participle of inhibēre "to restrain, check, prohibit," from in- "in, on" + habēre "to hold."
