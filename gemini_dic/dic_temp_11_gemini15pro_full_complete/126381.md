myrmidon:/ˈmɜːrmɪdɒn/ (UK), /ˈmɜːrmɪdɑːn/ (US)| n. | 爪牙；忠实的追随者；（尤指）盲从的追随者 | A loyal follower, especially one who executes orders without question or moral concern.  Derived from the Myrmidons, a fierce Thessalian tribe in Greek mythology who followed Achilles to the Trojan War. |  The dictator surrounded himself with myrmidons who carried out his every whim. 独裁者身边都是唯命是从的爪牙。| synonyms: follower, henchman, minion, lackey, subordinate, underling | antonyms: leader, chief, boss, head, opponent, rebel | noun

myrmidonian:/ˌmɜːrmɪˈdɒniən/ (UK), /ˌmɜːrmɪˈdoʊniən/ (US) | adj. |  像蚂蚁一样的；（尤指）盲目服从的；关于密尔弥冬人的 |  Resembling or characteristic of the Myrmidons; especially blindly obedient. | His myrmidonian loyalty made him a valuable asset to the corrupt politician. 他盲目忠诚的追随使他成为腐败政客的宝贵资产。 | synonyms: obedient, subservient, submissive, compliant, docile, unquestioning | antonyms: rebellious, disobedient, independent, defiant, insubordinate | adjective
