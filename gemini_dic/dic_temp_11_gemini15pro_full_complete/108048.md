latchkey:/ˈlætʃkiː/
n.|钥匙儿童；（尤指）挂在脖子上的钥匙|A key, especially one kept on a string or chain worn around the neck, used by children to let themselves into their homes. |例句：She was a latchkey kid, coming home to an empty house after school. 她是个钥匙儿童，放学后回到空无一人的家。|例句：He wore his latchkey around his neck on a shoelace. 他把钥匙系在鞋带上挂在脖子上。 |近义词：house key, door key|反义词: None|词性:noun


词源:
From latch (n.) + key (n.). Originally referring to a key for a latch (15c.), the modern sense of "key carried by a child" is first attested 1944, American English. The notion being that children left alone after school let themselves into the house with their own key.
