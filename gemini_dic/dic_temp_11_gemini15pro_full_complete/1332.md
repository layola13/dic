accriminate: /əˈkrɪmɪneɪt/ | vt. | 控告；指控；使负罪 | to make someone appear guilty of a crime or wrongdoing |  The evidence seemed to acriminate the defendant.  证据似乎指控了被告。| incriminate, implicate, blame | exonerate, absolve | verb

accriminate: /əˈkrɪmɪneɪt/ | vt. | 使显得有罪;连累 | to cause someone to appear guilty or to connect someone with a wrongdoing | His angry outburst acriminated him further. 他愤怒的爆发使他更加显得有罪。 | incriminate, implicate, blame | exonerate, absolve | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin  *accriminatus, past participle of *accriminare (“to accuse”),  from Latin ad (“to”) + criminari (“to accuse”), from crimen (“accusation, crime”).
