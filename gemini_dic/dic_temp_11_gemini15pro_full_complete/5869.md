ambassador-at-large:/æmˈbæsədər æt lɑːrdʒ/
noun
特使；大使；无任所大使；巡回大使
An ambassador-at-large is a diplomat of the highest rank who is accredited to represent their country, but often doesn't have a specific post or resides in a particular country and travels widely on special diplomatic assignments.
The ambassador-at-large addressed the UN Security Council.  特使向联合国安理会发表了讲话。
The president appointed a new ambassador-at-large for environmental affairs. 总统任命了一位新的环境事务无任所大使。
Synonym: envoy, diplomat, emissary, representative
Antonym: resident ambassador


Etymology:
From French ambassadeur, from Provençal ambaissador, from Late Latin ambactiator (“servant, messenger”), from ambactus (“vassal, servant, client”), of Gaulish origin. + at large: free, unrestrained; generally
