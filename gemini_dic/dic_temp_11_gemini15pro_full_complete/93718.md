immanation:/ˌɪmæˈneɪʃən/ | n. |内在化，体现，化身|The act or process of immanating or being immanated; inherent existence or indwelling.  The embodiment of a deity or spirit in a human or animal form. |The artist sought to capture the immanation of the divine in his paintings.  艺术家试图在他的画作中捕捉神性的体现。|近义词：incarnation, embodiment, personification, manifestation |反义词: externalization, disembodiment|词性:名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *immanatio*, from Latin immanare “to flow or spread into, dwell in”, from in- “into” + manare “to flow”.
