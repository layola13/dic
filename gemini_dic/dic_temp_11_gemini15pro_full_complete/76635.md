galls:/ɡɔːlz/
n.|胆汁; 恶毒; 粗鲁; 瘿瘤|A bitter greenish-brown alkaline fluid that is secreted by the liver and stored in the gallbladder. It aids digestion by emulsifying fats.|The defeat galled him.|近义词:bile, bitterness, resentment, audacity, impudence, nerve|反义词:kindness, politeness, humility|词性:noun

galls:/ɡɔːlz/
v.|激怒; 擦伤; 使烦恼; 磨损|Make (someone) feel annoyed or resentful.|His tone galled her.|近义词:irritate, annoy, vex, chafe, rub, abrade|反义词:soothe, please, comfort|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *gealla (attested only in compounds), from Proto-Germanic *gallō, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰelh₂- (“yellow, green, gall”). Cognate with Old Frisian galla, Old Saxon galla, Old High German galla, Old Norse gall, Gothic 𐌲𐌰𐌻𐌻𐌰 (galla).
