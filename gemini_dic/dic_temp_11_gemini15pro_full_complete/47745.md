decalcomanie:/ˌdeɪkælˈkəʊməni/ | n. | 贴花；转印画；移印画 | A decorative technique by which prints are transferred from specially prepared paper.  It involves transferring images from paper to another surface (such as glass, porcelain, or fabric) by moistening the paper and pressing it against the surface.  It can also refer to the resulting image. |  She decorated her mirror with a beautiful decalcomanie of flowers. 她用一幅美丽的鲜花贴花装饰了她的镜子。

The artist created a striking decalcomanie on the vase.  这位艺术家在花瓶上创作了一幅引人注目的移印画。 | 近义词: transfer, decal | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "original" in the sense of the source image) | 词性: noun
