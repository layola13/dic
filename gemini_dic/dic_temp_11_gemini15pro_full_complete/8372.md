anti-puritanism:/ˌæntiˈpjʊrɪtənɪzəm/
|n.|反清教主义|Opposition to the beliefs and practices of Puritans, especially their strict moral code and emphasis on religious discipline.|The rise of anti-puritanism in the 17th century led to a more relaxed social atmosphere. 17世纪反清教主义的兴起带来了更轻松的社会氛围。|近义词:libertinism, licentiousness|反义词:Puritanism, asceticism|词性:名词

anti-puritan:/ˌæntiˈpjʊrɪtən/
|adj.|反清教主义的|Opposed to the beliefs and practices of Puritans.|His anti-puritan sentiments were evident in his flamboyant lifestyle. 他反清教主义的情绪体现在他华丽的生活方式中。|近义词:libertine, licentious|反义词:Puritan, ascetic|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix): from Greek anti- "against, opposite of"
Puritan: from Late Latin puritanus "purist,"  from purus "pure, clean"
-ism (suffix):  forms nouns denoting action, practice, doctrines, a state or condition
