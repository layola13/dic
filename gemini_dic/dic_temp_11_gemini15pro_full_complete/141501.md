opisthopulmonate: /ˌɒpɪθɒˈpʌlməneɪt/ /ˌɑːpɪθɑːˈpʊlməneɪt/
| n. | 后鳃亚纲动物 | A subclass of snails and slugs characterized by having the respiratory organs located towards the rear of the body. |  The opisthopulmonate sea slugs are known for their vibrant colors.  后鳃亚纲海蛞蝓以其鲜艳的色彩而闻名。| synonyms:  pulmonate gastropod (more general) | antonyms: prosobranch (a different subclass of gastropods) | noun


opisthopulmonate: /ˌɒpɪθɒˈpʌlməneɪt/ /ˌɑːpɪθɑːˈpʊlməneɪt/
| adj. | 后鳃亚纲的 | Belonging to or relating to the Opisthopulmonata subclass. | Some opisthopulmonate species are terrestrial, while others are marine. 一些后鳃亚纲物种是陆生的，而另一些是海洋的。| synonyms: pulmonate (more general) | antonyms: prosobranch (related to a different subclass) | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *opistho-* (behind, posterior) + Latin *pulmo, pulmon-* (lung) + *-ate* (suffix forming adjectives and nouns).  Indicates having lungs located posteriorly.
