graftage:/ˈɡræftɪdʒ/ | n. |嫁接; 接枝|The act or process of grafting plants.  The state of being grafted. |The gardener used graftage to create a new variety of apple tree. 园丁用嫁接的方法培育了一个苹果新品种。|Synonyms: grafting, implanting, inarching|Antonyms: detachment, separation|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *greffage*, from greffer (to graft), from Old French *grafe* (grafting knife, stylus, pencil), of Germanic origin.
