close-up:/ˈkloʊsˌʌp/ (US) /ˈkləʊsˌʌp/ (UK)| n. adj. adv.|特写镜头；近景；近距离观察；|A photograph, film shot, or view taken at close range.  A close examination or view of something.|The director wanted a close-up of the actor's face. 导演想要一个演员脸部的特写镜头.|Synonyms: close shot, close view, detail shot|Antonyms: long shot, wide shot|noun

close-up:/ˈkloʊsˌʌp/ (US) /ˈkləʊsˌʌp/ (UK)| adj.|近距离的；特写的|Viewed or taken from a short distance.|The close-up photography revealed every detail of the insect's wings.  近距离摄影展现了昆虫翅膀的每一个细节.|Synonyms: close, near, nearby|Antonyms: distant, far, faraway|adjective

close-up:/ˈkloʊsˌʌp/ (US) /ˈkləʊsˌʌp/ (UK)| adv.|近距离地；特写地|From a close range.|The camera zoomed in close-up on the diamond ring. 相机对钻石戒指进行了特写放大。|Synonyms: closely, nearly, intimately|Antonyms: distantly, remotely|adverb
