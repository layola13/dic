cardol: /ˈkɑːrdɒl/ | n. |腰果酚| An oily phenolic liquid found in the shell of the cashew nut (Anacardium occidentale), causing a burning sensation on contact with skin.  It is used in resins and plastics. |  Cardol can cause severe skin irritation. 腰果酚会导致严重的皮肤刺激。| Cashew nutshell liquid contains cardol, anacardic acid, and cardanol. 腰果壳液包含腰果酚、腰果酸和腰果醇。| synonyms: cashew nutshell liquid component (broad term), alkylresorcinol (chemical class)| antonyms:  None (lacks a direct opposite)| noun
