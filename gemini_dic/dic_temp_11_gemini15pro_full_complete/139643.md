occulter:/ɒˈkʌltər/ (UK) /ɑːˈkʌltər/ (US)| n.|掩星器，掩星盤，星掩测量仪|An instrument or device used in astronomy to obscure a celestial body, such as a star, in order to observe or measure something near it.|The astronomer used an occulter to block the light from the bright star so they could observe the faint planet orbiting it.|近义词:coronagraph, mask|反义词:telescope (in a broad sense)|词性:noun

occulter:/ɒˈkʌltər/ (UK) /ɑːˈkʌltər/ (US)| n.| (犁的)切土部件|A sharp blade attached to a plow that cuts a vertical slice into the soil ahead of the plowshare.|The occulter on the plow sliced through the tough sod, making it easier for the plowshare to turn the earth.|近义词:knife coulter, disc coulter|反义词:N/A|词性:noun
