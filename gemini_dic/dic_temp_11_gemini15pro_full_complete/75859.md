functionalism:/ˈfʌŋkʃənəlɪzəm/| n.|功能主义|A doctrine that stresses utility or purpose, especially in architecture and design; the theory that all aspects of a society serve a function and are necessary for the survival of that society.|The building is a classic example of functionalism, prioritizing practicality over ornamentation.|近义词:pragmatism, utilitarianism|反义词:formalism, aestheticism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From function + -al + -ism.  Function comes from the Late Latin *functionem*, meaning "performance, execution".
