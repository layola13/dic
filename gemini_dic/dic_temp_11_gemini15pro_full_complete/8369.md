anti-protestant:/ˌæntiˈprɒtɪstənt/ |n.| 反新教者；敌视新教的人 | A person who is opposed to or hostile towards Protestantism. | He was known as a staunch anti-protestant. 他以坚定的反新教者而闻名。


anti-protestant:/ˌæntiˈprɒtɪstənt/| n. adj.| 反新教的；敌视新教的；反新教者；敌视新教的人 | Opposed to or hostile towards Protestantism; a person who is opposed to Protestantism. |  The anti-protestant laws were eventually repealed. 反新教法律最终被废除了。


近义词: anti-reformation, counter-reformation |
反义词: pro-protestant, pro-reformation |
词性: adjective, noun


词源:
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to. From Greek "anti".
Protestant (noun/adjective): a member or follower of any of the Western Christian churches that are separate from the Roman Catholic Church and follow the principles of the Reformation, including the Baptist, Presbyterian, and Lutheran churches. From late Middle English: from Anglo-Latin protestans, protestant-, present participle of protestari ‘declare publicly’.
