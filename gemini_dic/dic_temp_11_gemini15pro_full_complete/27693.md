candlewaster:/ˈkændəlˌweɪstər/| n.| 浪费蜡烛的人；游手好闲的人；夜猫子|Someone who stays up late, originally implying they are wasting candles by burning them late into the night.  Now more generally used to describe someone who wastes time or resources, often in frivolous pursuits.| He was a bit of a candlewaster in his youth, staying up all night reading and writing poetry. 他年轻时有点游手好闲，整夜不睡地读诗写诗。|近义词：idler, loafer, night owl|反义词：worker, early bird|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From "candle" + "waster."  "Candle" refers to the wax light source, and "waster" refers to someone who squanders or uses something inefficiently.
