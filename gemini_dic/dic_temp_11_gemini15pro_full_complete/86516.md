heist:/haɪst/| n. |抢劫，盗窃|An act of stealing something valuable, typically involving elaborate planning and daring execution. | The thieves pulled off a daring heist at the museum. 盗贼在博物馆进行了一场大胆的抢劫。|近义词：robbery, theft, burglary|反义词:return, give back|noun


heist:/haɪst/| vt. |抢劫，盗窃|To steal something valuable, typically with elaborate planning and daring execution. | They planned to heist the jewels from the heavily guarded vault. 他们计划从戒备森严的金库中盗取珠宝。|近义词：rob, steal, plunder|反义词:protect, defend|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Middle Dutch *heisten "to hoist, lift up," from Proto-Germanic *haistjanan "to seize, grasp." The meaning shifted from "to seize" to "to rob" in the 19th century, perhaps influenced by the idea of lifting or carrying away stolen goods.
