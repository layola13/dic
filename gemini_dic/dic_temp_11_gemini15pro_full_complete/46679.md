dampish:/ˈdæmpɪʃ/ | adj. | 略湿的，微湿的 | Slightly damp; moderately moist. | The towel was dampish after being left out overnight. 毛巾在外面放了一夜后有点潮湿。| The air felt dampish and chilly. 空气感觉潮湿又寒冷。 | 近义词:moist, humid, clammy | 反义词:dry, arid | 词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From damp + -ish.  "Damp" comes from Middle English *dampe, from Old Norse dampi ("vapor, steam"), from Proto-Germanic *dampaz. "-ish" is a suffix forming adjectives from other adjectives, meaning "somewhat," "rather," "somewhat like," "approaching," "of the nature of," from Old English -isc.
