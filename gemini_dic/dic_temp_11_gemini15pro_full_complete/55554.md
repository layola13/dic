disturb:/dɪˈstɜːrb/ |v.|打扰，扰乱，使不安|to interrupt someone or something; to make someone worried or upset|例句：The noise disturbed my sleep. 噪音打扰了我的睡眠。|近义词：interrupt, bother, upset, agitate|反义词：soothe, calm|词性：动词

disturbance:/dɪˈstɜːrbəns/ |n.|扰乱，骚乱，不安|an interruption of a peaceful state; a state of worry or anxiety|例句：There was a disturbance outside the building. 大楼外发生了骚动。|近义词：interruption, commotion, disorder, unrest|反义词：peace, calm, tranquility|词性：名词

disturbed:/dɪˈstɜːrbd/ |adj.|不安的，心烦意乱的，精神错乱的|worried or upset; mentally ill|例句：She was deeply disturbed by the news. 她对这个消息深感不安。|近义词：upset, worried, troubled, agitated|反义词：calm, peaceful, tranquil|词性：形容词

disturbing:/dɪˈstɜːrbɪŋ/ |adj.|令人不安的，令人烦恼的|causing worry or upset|例句：The news was very disturbing. 这个消息令人非常不安。|近义词：upseting, worrying, troubling, alarming|反义词：comforting, reassuring, calming|词性：形容词
