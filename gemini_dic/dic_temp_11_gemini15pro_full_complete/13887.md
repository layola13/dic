augur:/ˈɔːɡər/ (US) /ˈɔːɡə(r)/ (UK)| n. |预言家，占卜师；预兆，征兆|A person who foretells events by omens; something that portends a future event, especially something that suggests a good or bad outcome.|例句：The ancient Romans consulted augurs before making important decisions. 古代罗马人在做出重要决定之前会咨询占卜师。

例句：The dark clouds augur a storm. 乌云预示着暴风雨。|近义词：prophet, soothsayer, omen, portent, sign, forebode, presage|反义词: N/A |词性: noun

augurs:/ˈɔːɡərz/ (US) /ˈɔːɡəz/ (UK)| v. |预示，预兆|To be a sign or omen of; to portend.|例句：The bird's flight augurs well for the journey. 鸟儿的飞行预示着旅程顺利。

例句：These recent developments augur a change in government policy. 这些最近的事态发展预示着政府政策的改变。|近义词：portend, presage, foretell, predict, foreshadow|反义词: N/A |词性: verb
