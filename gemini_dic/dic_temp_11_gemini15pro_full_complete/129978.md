non-Mormon:/ˌnɑːnˈmɔːrmən/ | n. adj. | 非摩门教徒；非摩门教的 |  A person who is not a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (commonly known as the Mormon Church). Also used as an adjective to describe something not related to or affiliated with Mormonism. |  She is a non-Mormon living in Utah. 她是一位住在犹他州的非摩门教徒。 The university offers non-Mormon religious services. 这所大学提供非摩门教的宗教服务。 | 近义词: gentile (in a Mormon context) | 反义词: Mormon, Latter-day Saint | 词性: 名词, 形容词

词源:  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "Mormon" refers to a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.  The term originated in the 19th century as a way to distinguish those outside of the Mormon faith.
