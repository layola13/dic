fundamentalisms: /ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪzəmz/
|n.|原教旨主义|A strict adherence to the basic principles of any subject or discipline, particularly religion, often accompanied by a literal interpretation of texts and a resistance to change or modernization.|The rise of religious fundamentalisms in the 20th century has led to numerous conflicts.|近义词: orthodoxy, dogmatism, traditionalism|反义词: liberalism, modernism|词性: 名词

fundamentalism: /ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪzəm/
|n.|原教旨主义|A movement or attitude stressing strict and literal adherence to a set of basic principles, often religious.|Islamic fundamentalism is a complex and diverse phenomenon.|近义词: orthodoxy, dogmatism, traditionalism|反义词: liberalism, modernism|词性: 名词
