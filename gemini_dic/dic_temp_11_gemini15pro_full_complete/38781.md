conduct:/kənˈdʌkt/ (US) /ˈkɒndʌkt/ (UK)| n. v.| 行为，举动；引导，带领；指挥；传导|  As a noun, it refers to the manner in which a person behaves, especially in a particular place or situation. As a verb, it means to organize and carry out a particular activity.|
例句：
His conduct at the meeting was unacceptable. 
他在会议上的行为是不可接受的。

The experiment was conducted under strict conditions.
这个实验是在严格的条件下进行的。


近义词：behavior, demeanor (n.); manage, direct, lead (v.)|
反义词：misconduct (n.)|
词性：noun, verb|

conduct:/kənˈdʌkt/ (US) /ˈkɒndʌkt/ (UK)| v.| 引导，带领|To lead or guide someone to a particular place. |
例句：
He conducted the tourists through the museum.
他带领游客参观了博物馆。

She conducted herself with grace and dignity.
她举止优雅端庄。


近义词：lead, guide, escort|
反义词：follow|
词性：verb|

conduct:/kənˈdʌkt/ (US) /ˈkɒndʌkt/ (UK)| v.| 指挥（乐队、合唱团等）|To direct the performance of musicians or singers. |
例句：
The orchestra was conducted by a renowned maestro.
这支管弦乐队由一位著名的指挥家指挥。

He conducted the choir beautifully.
他把合唱团指挥得很好。


近义词：direct, lead|
反义词：N/A|
词性：verb|

conduct:/kənˈdʌkt/ (US) /ˈkɒndʌkt/ (UK)| v.| 传导（热、电等）|To allow heat or electricity to pass along or through. |
例句：
Copper conducts electricity well.
铜导电性能好。

The metal conducted heat quickly.
这种金属导热很快。


近义词：transmit, convey|
反义词：insulate|
词性：verb|
