indemnities:/ɪnˈdɛmnɪtiz/| n.|赔偿；保障；补偿|protection against possible damage or loss, especially a promise of payment or the payment itself to cover any losses that someone suffers|The insurance company paid him an indemnity for his losses in the fire.  保险公司赔偿了他在火灾中的损失。|Synonyms: compensation, restitution, recompense, redress|Antonyms: penalty, forfeiture|noun

indemnity:/ɪnˈdɛmnɪti/| n.|赔偿；保障；补偿|protection against possible damage or loss, especially a promise of payment or the payment itself to cover any losses that someone suffers|The company agreed to indemnify its employees against any legal action arising from their work. 公司同意保障其员工免受因工作而引起的任何法律诉讼的影响。|Synonyms: compensation, restitution, recompense, redress|Antonyms: penalty, forfeiture|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *indemnite*  (modern French *indemnité*), from Latin *indemnitas* (“freedom from loss or damage, safety, security”), from *indemnis* (“unharmed, unhurt, safe, secure”), from *in-* (“not”) + *damnum* (“loss, damage”).
