cushier:/ˈkʊʃ.i.ər/ /ˈkuːʃ.ər/ | adj. | 更舒适的，更安逸的 | More comfortable, providing more cushioning.  Relating to a job or situation that is easy and not very demanding. |  The new office chairs are much cushier than the old ones.  新办公室的椅子比旧的舒服多了。 | This job is cushier than my last one. 这份工作比我上一份工作更轻松。| 近义词:  comfortable, softer, easier, less demanding | 反义词:  harder, tougher, more demanding, uncomfortable | 词性: adjective

cushion:/ˈkʊʃ.ən/ /ˈkʊʃ.ən/ | n. vt. | 垫子，缓冲物；缓和，缓冲 | A soft bag filled with something soft, used for sitting or leaning on.  To make the effect or force of something less severe. | I bought a new cushion for my chair. 我买了个新椅垫。| The government took steps to cushion the impact of the recession. 政府采取措施来缓冲经济衰退的影响。 |近义词: pillow, pad, buffer, mitigate | 反义词: aggravate, worsen | 词性: noun, verb


词源:  From the noun "cushion" + the suffix "-er" (comparative adjective). Ultimately from Old French "coissin" (cushion).
