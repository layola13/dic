词源:  "Led-out" is a past participle form of the phrasal verb "lead out," meaning to deplete or exhaust. "Lead" in this context likely comes from an older sense of the verb meaning "to draw or pull," related to the noun "lead" (a metal wire).  "Out" intensifies the sense of complete depletion.
