mawkishly:/ˈmɔːkɪʃli/ | adv. | 伤感地，多愁善感地，自作多情地 | In a sentimental and exaggerated way, often to the point of being sickening or insincere. | She mawkishly described their final parting, with tears streaming down her face. 她伤感地描述了他们最后的离别，泪流满面。| sentimentally, over-sentimentally, cloyingly, sickeningly, drippily | unsentimentally, realistically, practically | adverb

mawkish:/ˈmɔːkɪʃ/ | adj. | 伤感地，多愁善感地，自作多情地 | Excessively sentimental to the point of being sickening or insincere. | The film was mawkish and predictable. 这部电影多愁善感，而且可以预见。| sentimental, over-sentimental, cloying, sickening, drippy | unsentimental, realistic, practical | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the obsolete adjective "mawk" (meaning maggot), via the now also obsolete "mawkish" (meaning squeamish or nauseated).  The current meaning developed from the idea of being overly sentimental, as if sickeningly sweet.
