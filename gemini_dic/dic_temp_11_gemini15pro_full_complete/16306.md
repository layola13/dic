barag: /ˈbæræɡ/ | n. |  障碍物，阻碍，争吵 | A barag is an obstacle or impediment, or a noisy quarrel or fight. This word is archaic and rarely used in modern English. | Example: The fallen tree formed a barag across the road. 倒下的树成了横在路上的障碍物。 | Example:  A loud barag erupted in the tavern. 酒馆里爆发了一场激烈的争吵。| Synonym: obstacle, impediment, obstruction, barrier, altercation, quarrel, fight | Antonym: aid, assistance, help, support | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *barague (unattested), perhaps from Arabic *barāqa 'lightning', via Italian baracca 'hut, shack'. The semantic shift likely occurred through the idea of a sudden, disruptive event.
