looser:/ˈluːsər/| adj.|更宽松的，更松的；输家，失败者|Someone or something that loses, especially in a competition or game. Can also mean not tight or fitting well.  Derived from "loose" with the comparative suffix "-er". From Old Norse *lauss, meaning "loose, free."|The screw was looser than it should have been.  螺丝比它应有的松。 He felt like a loser after failing the exam. 他考试不及格后觉得自己像个失败者。|近义词：baggier, looser-fitting, defeated, unsuccessful|反义词:tighter, winner, successful|词性:形容词

looser:/ˈluːzər/| n.|输家，失败者|A person or thing that loses, especially habitually.  From "loose" (adj.) + "-er" (suffix forming nouns of agency).|He's always been a loser in love. 他在爱情上一直是个失败者。|近义词：failure, also-ran|反义词:winner, victor|词性:名词
