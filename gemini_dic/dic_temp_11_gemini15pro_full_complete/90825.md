hunch:/hʌntʃ/
n.|预感，直觉；肉峰，驼背|A feeling or guess based on intuition rather than known facts.  A hump on the back.|I had a hunch that he was lying. 我预感他在撒谎。|premonition, intuition, suspicion, feeling|certainty, knowledge|noun
hunch:/hʌntʃ/
v.|弓起，耸起（背部）|To raise (one's shoulders) and bend the top of one's body forward.|He hunched over his desk, working late into the night.  他弓着背伏在桌上，工作到深夜。|slouch, stoop, crouch|straighten, stand erect|verb
