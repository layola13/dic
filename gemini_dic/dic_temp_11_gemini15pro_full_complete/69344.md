federalism:/ˈfɛdərəlɪzəm/ | n. | 联邦制 | A system of government in which power is divided between a central authority and constituent political units (such as states or provinces). | The United States operates under a system of federalism. 美国实行联邦制。|  Switzerland's federalism allows for a great deal of autonomy for its cantons. 瑞士的联邦制允许其各州拥有很大的自治权。|  Synonyms: confederation, union | Antonyms: centralism, unitary system | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fédéralisme*, from *fédéral* (“federal”) + *-isme*.  *fédéral* is from Late Latin *foederalis* (“of a treaty, league”),  from Latin *foedus* (“treaty, league, covenant”).
