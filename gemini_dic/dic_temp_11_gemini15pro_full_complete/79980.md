go cold turkey:/ɡoʊ koʊld ˈtɜːrki/| idiom |突然戒掉(尤指毒瘾或坏习惯);|To abruptly stop a bad habit, such as smoking or drinking alcohol, without any gradual reduction.  This often leads to withdrawal symptoms. It is thought to originate from the appearance of someone undergoing withdrawal, with their skin resembling a plucked, cold turkey.|He decided to go cold turkey on smoking and hasn't touched a cigarette since. 他决定彻底戒烟，从此再也没碰过香烟。|近义词:quit abruptly, stop suddenly, withdraw immediately |反义词:gradually reduce, wean off| idiom
