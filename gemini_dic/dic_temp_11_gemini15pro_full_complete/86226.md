heathlands:/ˈhiːθləndz/
noun
石南荒原；欧石南丛生的荒野
A large area of open land covered with small shrubs called heaths.
The heathlands are a haven for wildlife.  这些石南荒原是野生动物的避难所。
synonyms:moor, moorland, heath
antonyms:forest, woodland
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hāþland (unattested), from hāþ (“heath”) + land (“land”). Cognate with Middle Dutch hētlant, hetland (“heathland”).
