grapple-with:/ˈɡræpəl wɪð/| phrasal verb |努力对付，全力应付，与…搏斗|to struggle with or work hard to deal with something difficult|例句：Scientists are grappling with the problem of climate change. 科学家们正在努力应对气候变化问题。|例句：The government is grappling with a serious economic crisis. 政府正在努力应对一场严重的经济危机。|近义词：contend with, wrestle with, tackle, address|反义词：ignore, neglect, avoid|词性:动词短语

grapple:/ˈɡræpəl/| n. v. |抓住；格斗；抓斗| a hook or an iron device with claws for gripping and holding things, especially an anchor; to hold and fight with someone|例句：He grappled with the intruder. 他与入侵者扭打在一起。|例句：The ship's grapple caught the sunken treasure chest. 船上的抓斗抓住了沉没的宝箱。|近义词：grip, grasp, wrestle|反义词：release, let go|词性:名词,动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *grappiller* (to seize, grasp), ultimately from Frankish *krappōn* (hook, grapple), which is related to Old High German *krapfo* (hook) and Old English *crappian* (to seize, pluck).
