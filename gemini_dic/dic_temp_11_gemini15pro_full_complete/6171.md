amioidei:/ˌeɪmiˈɔɪdiˌaɪ/ | n. |  鳉形目 | An order of ray-finned fishes, also called Cyprinodontiformes, that includes killifishes, livebearers, and others. They are characterized by a protrusible premaxilla and usually a rounded caudal fin. | 例句：The amioidei are a diverse group of fishes found in freshwater habitats around the world. 鳉形目鱼类是一个多样化的群体，分布在世界各地的淡水栖息地。

ETYMOLOGY->
From  New Latin, from *Amia (a genus of bowfin) + -oidei (resembling).  Ultimately from Greek *amia (a kind of shark).
