impishness:/ˈɪmpɪʃnəs/| n. |顽皮，淘气|The quality of being impish; mischievous playfulness.|Her impishness always brought a smile to her grandfather's face. 她的顽皮总是让她祖父脸上露出笑容。|Synonyms: mischief, naughtiness, playfulness, prankishness|Antonyms: seriousness, obedience, docility|noun

impish:/ˈɪmpɪʃ/| adj. |顽皮的，淘气的|Inclined to do slightly naughty things for fun; mischievous.|He had an impish grin on his face. 他脸上带着顽皮的笑容。|Synonyms: mischievous, naughty, playful, prankish|Antonyms: serious, obedient, docile|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From imp + -ish + -ness.  Imp originally referred to a small demon or evil spirit.  The suffix "-ish" creates an adjective meaning "like an imp," and "-ness" turns the adjective into a noun referring to the state or quality of being impish.
