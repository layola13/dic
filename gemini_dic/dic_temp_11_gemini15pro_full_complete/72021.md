flip-flopped:/ˌflɪpˈflɑpt/ | v. | 改变主意，出尔反尔，反复无常 | to change your opinion or decision, especially suddenly | The politician flip-flopped on the issue of taxes. 这位政客在税收问题上出尔反尔。| synonyms: vacillate, waver, change one's mind | antonyms:  stand firm, stick to one's guns, remain resolute | verb

flip-flopped:/ˌflɪpˈflɑpt/ | adj. |  (非正式)摇摆不定的，反复无常的 | (informal) characterized by changing one's opinion or decision frequently | His flip-flopped stance on the environment angered many voters. 他在环境问题上摇摆不定的立场激怒了许多选民。| synonyms:  vacillating, wavering, inconsistent | antonyms:  consistent, steadfast, resolute | adjective
