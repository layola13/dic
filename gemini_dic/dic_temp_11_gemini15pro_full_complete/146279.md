overfanciful: /ˌoʊvərˈfænsɪfəl/
adjective
过度想象的；过于花哨的
Excessively fanciful or elaborate; too ornamented or embellished.
Her prose is sometimes overfanciful, making the story hard to follow. 她写的散文有时过于华丽，使得故事难以理解。

The architect's overfanciful design was ultimately rejected as impractical.  这位建筑师过于花哨的设计最终因不切实际而被否决。

synonym:  ornate, flamboyant, extravagant, elaborate, baroque
antonym:  simple, plain, austere, understated, unadorned
词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + fanciful. "Over-" acts as a prefix indicating "excessive" or "too much." "Fanciful" comes from "fancy," which originally meant "fantasy" or "imagination."  Therefore, "overfanciful" literally means "too much imagination" or "excessively imaginative."
