nonsatiations:/ˌnɒnˌseɪʃiˈeɪʃənz/ | n. | 不满足，不饱足 |  The state of not being satiated or satisfied; a lack of satisfaction.  It often refers to a continuing or recurring desire, especially for things like food, power, or knowledge.  |  His nonsatiations for knowledge led him to pursue multiple doctoral degrees. 他对知识的永不满足促使他攻读多个博士学位。 |  The investor's nonsatiations for profit drove him to make risky investments.  这位投资者对利润的贪得无厌驱使他进行高风险投资。 | 近义词：insatiability, unsatiatedness, greed, avarice | 反义词：satisfaction, contentment, satiety | 词性: 名词

词源:  Derived from "nonsatiation," which comes from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and "satiation" (meaning "the state of being full or satisfied").  "Satiation" ultimately comes from the Latin word "satiare," meaning "to satisfy."
