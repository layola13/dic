diaphanotype:/daɪˈæfənətaɪp/| n.|透明幻灯片，透视图|A diaphanotype is a positive transparent photograph printed on a thin support, intended to be viewed by transmitted light.|The museum displayed a collection of 19th-century diaphanotypes showing scenes from daily life.|Synonym: transparency, slide|Antonym: opaque print|noun

diaphaneity:/ˌdaɪəfəˈniːɪti/| n.|透明度，半透明|Diaphaneity is the quality of allowing light to pass through diffusely.|The diaphaneity of the silk scarf gave it an ethereal quality.|Synonym: translucency, transparency|Antonym: opacity|noun

diaphanous:/daɪˈæfənəs/| adj.|透明的，半透明的，轻薄的|Diaphanous materials allow light to pass through diffusely.|She wore a diaphanous gown that shimmered in the candlelight.|Synonym: translucent, sheer, transparent|Antonym: opaque, thick|adjective
