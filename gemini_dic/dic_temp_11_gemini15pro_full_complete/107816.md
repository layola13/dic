ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lappewinge, *lappewynge, from Old English *hlēapewince, from Proto-Germanic *hlaupawingō (“lapwing”), equivalent to *hlaupaną (“to leap, run”) + *wingō (“wing”). Cognate with Scots laipwing (“lapwing”), West Frisian ljipwjuk (“lapwing”), Dutch kievit (“lapwing”), German Kiebitz (“lapwing”).
