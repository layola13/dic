ballistas:/bəˈlɪstəz/
noun
弩炮，石弩
A powerful engine of war used in ancient and medieval times for hurling large missiles.
The Roman army used ballistas to besiege fortified cities. 罗马军队使用弩炮围攻设防的城市。

ballistas:/bəˈlɪstəz/
noun
弩炮，抛石机
A military machine worked by torsion, used for hurling stones, darts, and other missiles.
The defenders relied on their ballistas to repel the attackers.  守军依靠他们的弩炮击退进攻者。

ballista:/bəˈlɪstə/
noun
弩炮，抛石机 (单数形式)
A large crossbow-like weapon used in ancient warfare for throwing stones, spears, etc.
The ballista launched a huge rock towards the enemy walls. 弩炮向敌人的城墙发射了一块巨石。


Etymology:
From Latin ballista, from Greek βαλλίστρα (ballistra), from βάλλειν (ballein, “to throw”).

Synonyms:
catapult, trebuchet, siege engine, artillery piece

Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but consider)
shield, armor, fortification (as defensive measures)

Word Type:
Noun
