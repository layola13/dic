muffier:/ˈmʌfɪər/ (US) /ˈmʌfɪə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更闷的，更低沉的|Comparative form of stuffy.  Used to describe sound as being quieter and less distinct, or a space as being poorly ventilated and lacking fresh air.|The room was much muffier after the windows were closed.  房间窗户关上后变得更闷了。|The singer's voice sounded muffier because of the thick curtains.  由于厚厚的窗帘，歌手的声音听起来更低沉了。|近义词：stuffier, duller, quieter, indistinct|反义词：clearer, brighter, louder, distinct|词性:形容词

muffle:/ˈmʌfl/ (US) /ˈmʌf(ə)l/ (UK)| v. |使声音低沉；裹住；抑制|To make a sound quieter or less distinct; to wrap or cover for warmth or protection.|She muffled her cough with her hand. 她用手捂住嘴咳嗽。|The heavy snow muffled the sound of the traffic.  大雪掩盖了交通的声音。|近义词：stifle, deaden, suppress, mute|反义词：amplify, increase, enhance|词性:动词

muffler:/ˈmʌflər/ (US) /ˈmʌflə(r)/ (UK)| n. |消音器；围巾；头巾|A device used to reduce noise; a scarf worn around the neck for warmth.|The car needs a new muffler. 这辆车需要一个新的消声器。|He wrapped a thick woolen muffler around his neck. 他脖子上围了一条厚厚的羊毛围巾。|近义词：silencer, scarf, wrap|反义词：(For silencer) amplifier|词性:名词
