cutting-edge:/ˈkʌtɪŋ ɛdʒ/| adj.|尖端的；前沿的；领先的|Being at the cutting edge of a field means being at the forefront of new developments and innovations.|例句：This company is at the cutting edge of medical technology. 这家公司处于医疗技术的前沿。|近义词：advanced, innovative, leading-edge, state-of-the-art|反义词：outdated, obsolete, antiquated|词性:形容词

cutting-edge:/ˈkʌtɪŋ ɛdʒ/| n.|前沿；最先进的部分|The cutting edge refers to the most advanced or innovative part of something.|例句：They are working at the cutting edge of artificial intelligence. 他们在人工智能的前沿领域工作。|近义词：forefront, vanguard, leading position|反义词：rear, backwater|词性:名词

词源：
From "cutting edge" (literal), the sharpened edge of a blade, first attested 1826. Figurative sense of "the forefront of progress" first recorded 1958.
来源于字面意义上的“切割边缘”，指刀片的锋利边缘，最早出现在1826年。比喻意义上的“进步的前沿”最早记录于1958年。
