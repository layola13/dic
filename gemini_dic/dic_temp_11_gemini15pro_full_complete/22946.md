bottlenecking:/ˈbɒtlˌnɛkɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈbɑːtlˌnɛkɪŋ/ (US)| n. |瓶颈；瓶颈效应；阻塞；限制|A bottleneck is a point of congestion or blockage, often in a process, system, or flow, that restricts overall progress or capacity.|The construction on the highway is causing a bottleneck during rush hour. 高速公路上的施工在高峰时段造成了交通瓶颈。|近义词：constraint, restriction, obstruction, impediment, blockage|反义词:expedite,  facilitate|词性:名词


bottleneck:/ˈbɒtlˌnɛk/ (UK) /ˈbɑːtlˌnɛk/ (US)| n. vt.|瓶颈；瓶颈效应；阻塞；限制；造成瓶颈|A bottleneck is a point of congestion or blockage.  As a verb, it means to cause a bottleneck.|The lack of skilled workers is bottlenecking the company's growth.  缺乏熟练工人限制了公司的发展。|近义词：constrict, restrict, impede, obstruct|反义词:expand, broaden, widen|词性:名词，动词

词源:  The word "bottleneck" comes from the narrow neck of a bottle, which restricts the flow of liquid. This analogy is used to describe any point in a process where progress is slowed down or limited.
