conventions:/kənˈvɛnʃənz/
| n. |习俗，惯例；会议；公约|a way in which something is usually done, especially within a particular area or activity.  A large formal meeting of people with a shared interest, or a formal agreement between countries.|
例句：The handshake is a social convention in many cultures.  握手是许多文化中的社会习俗。
例句：Diplomatic conventions prevented war. 外交惯例阻止了战争爆发。
|近义词：customs, traditions, practices, norms, agreements, conferences, assemblies|
|反义词：unconventionality, anomaly, peculiarities|
|词性: noun|


conventions:/kənˈvɛnʃənz/
| n. |大会，集会|a large formal meeting of people with a shared interest|
例句：The Republican National Convention was held in Cleveland. 共和党全国代表大会在克利夫兰举行。
例句：The annual convention of science fiction fans drew thousands of people. 年度科幻小说迷大会吸引了数千人。
|近义词：congress, conference, meeting, assembly, gathering|
|反义词：dispersal, scattering|
|词性: noun|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *convencioun*, from Old French *convention*, from Latin *conventiō*, *conventionis* ("meeting, assembly, agreement"), from *conveniō* ("I come together, assemble, agree"), from *com-* ("together") + *veniō* ("I come").
