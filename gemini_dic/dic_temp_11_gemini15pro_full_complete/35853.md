cockpits:/ˈkɒkpɪts/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkpɪts/ (US)| n. |驾驶舱，座舱|The enclosed space in an aircraft or spacecraft where the pilot and crew sit and control the vehicle.  Also, a small enclosed space for a specific activity. |The pilot checked the instruments in the cockpit before takeoff.  飞行员在起飞前检查了驾驶舱内的仪表。|The racing car's cockpit was cramped and uncomfortable. 赛车的驾驶舱狭窄不舒服。|synonyms: cabin, flight deck, control panel, compartment|antonyms: passenger cabin, cargo hold|noun
