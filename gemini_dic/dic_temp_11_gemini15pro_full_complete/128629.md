nickeling-and-diming: /ˈnɪkəlɪŋ ənd ˈdaɪmɪŋ/ | idiom |斤斤计较; 为小钱斤斤计较; 小额收费|Charging someone small amounts of money for many things, which can add up to a considerable sum. It often implies exploiting or taking unfair advantage of someone.|The airline is nickeling-and-diming its customers with fees for checked bags, seat assignments, and even water.|近义词: penny-pinching, chiseling, gouging |反义词: generous, extravagant | idiom

nickel: /ˈnɪkəl/ | n. |镍; 五分镍币 |A hard, silvery-white metallic chemical element, or a five-cent coin in the US and Canada.|The US five-cent coin is called a nickel. | 近义词: (for the coin) five cents | 反义词: (no direct antonym for the metal or coin) | noun

dime: /daɪm/ | n. |十分硬币; 少量 | A ten-cent coin in the US and Canada, or a small amount.|Can I borrow a dime? | 近义词: (for the coin) ten cents | 反义词: (no direct antonym for the coin) | noun
