hyssop:/ˈhɪs.əp/ | n. | 牛膝草；神香草 | A small bushy aromatic plant of the mint family, with blue flowers. It is mentioned in the Bible and was used in Jewish purification rites. |  The priests sprinkled hyssop dipped in the blood of the Passover lamb on the doorposts of the Israelites. 祭司们用牛膝草蘸逾越节羔羊的血洒在以色列人的门楣上。
