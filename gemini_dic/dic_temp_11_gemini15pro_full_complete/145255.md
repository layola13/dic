overblown:/ˌoʊvərˈbloʊn/| adj.|夸大的；过分渲染的；（花）盛开过度的；凋谢的|inflated; exaggerated; overstated; (of a flower) past its prime|The media coverage of the event was overblown. 媒体对该事件的报道过于夸大。|synonyms: exaggerated, inflated, overstated, pompous, pretentious|antonyms: understated, modest, simple, realistic|adjective

overblown:/ˌoʊvərˈbloʊn/| adj.|（花）盛开过度的；凋谢的|past its prime; over-mature|The roses were overblown and starting to drop their petals. 玫瑰花开过了，开始掉落花瓣。|synonyms: withered, faded, wilted|antonyms: blooming, fresh, vibrant|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From over + blown (past participle of blow).  The literal meaning refers to a flower that has blossomed too much and is beginning to decay.  The figurative meaning of "exaggerated" or "inflated" developed from this.
