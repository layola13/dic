canonizing:/ˈkænənaɪzɪŋ/
v.
册封圣徒；使崇高；使神圣化
The act of officially declaring someone a saint.  Also, the act of treating someone or something as being very important or special.
The Church canonized the two nuns who devoted their lives to the poor.  教会将这两位毕生奉献给穷人的修女册封为圣徒。
The media has canonized her as a national hero. 媒体把她捧成了民族英雄。
近义词:glorifying, exalting, deifying, sanctifying
反义词:condemning, vilifying, denouncing
词性:动词 (present participle)

canonize:/ˈkænənaɪz/
vt.
册封圣徒；使崇高；使神圣化
To officially declare someone a saint. Also, to treat someone or something as being very important or special.
The Pope canonized Joan of Arc in 1920.  教皇于1920年册封圣女贞德。
His works have been canonized by literary critics.  他的作品被文学评论家奉为经典。
近义词:glorify, exalt, deify, sanctify
反义词:condemn, vilify, denounce
词性:动词

Etymology:
From Late Latin canonizare "to place in the canon, declare a saint," from Latin canon "rule, standard" (see canon).
