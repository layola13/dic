indecision's:/ˌɪndɪˈsɪʒənz/| n.|犹豫不决，优柔寡断|The possessive form of "indecision," referring to the state of being unable to decide.  It highlights the negative consequences or impact of someone's inability to make a choice.|Her indecision's consequences were severe, costing her the opportunity.|近义词：hesitation's, wavering's, doubt's|反义词: decisiveness's, resolution's|词性:名词


词源:
From Latin *indecisionem*, accusative singular of *indecisio*,  meaning "irresolution, want of decision," from in- "not" + decisio "a decision" (from decidere "to decide").
