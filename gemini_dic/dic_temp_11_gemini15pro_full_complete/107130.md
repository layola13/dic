ladyishly:/ˈleɪdɪʃli/| adv.|女人气地，娇滴滴地|In a way that is considered typical of a woman, often in a delicate or affected manner.|She giggled ladyishly and covered her mouth with her hand. 她娇滴滴地笑着，用手捂住了嘴。|近义词：femininely, daintily, coyly|反义词：masculinely, mannishly|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From lady + -ish + -ly.  "Lady" comes from Old English "hlǣfdīge" (loaf-kneader). "-ish" is a suffix forming adjectives from nouns, meaning "having the character of." "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives.
