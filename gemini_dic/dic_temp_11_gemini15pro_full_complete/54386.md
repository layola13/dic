disillusioning:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒənɪŋ/
adjective
令人失望的，使人幻想破灭的
Causing someone to stop believing in something or someone that they previously thought was good or truthful.
The experience was deeply disillusioning.  这次经历令人深感失望。
It was disillusioning to discover the truth about him. 发现关于他的真相令人 disillusioning。
近义词:disappointing, disheartening, disenchanting
反义词:encouraging, inspiring, heartening
词性:adjective

disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/
verb
使…幻想破灭
To cause someone to stop believing in something or someone that they previously thought was good or truthful.
The scandal disillusioned many voters.  这桩丑闻使许多选民 disillusioned。
He was disillusioned by the realities of political life. 他对政治生活的现实感到 disillusioned。
近义词:disappoint, disenchant
反义词:encourage, inspire
词性:verb

disillusionment:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒənmənt/
noun
幻想破灭
A feeling of disappointment resulting from the discovery that something is not as good as one believed it to be.
There is widespread disillusionment with the government. 人们普遍对政府感到 disillusionment。
The experience led to a growing sense of disillusionment. 这次经历导致了日益增长的 disillusionment。
近义词:disappointment, disenchantment
反义词:hope, faith
词性:noun
