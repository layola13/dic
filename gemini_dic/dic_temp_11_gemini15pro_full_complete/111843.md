logical-positivists: /ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈpɒzɪtɪvɪsts/ /ˈlɑːdʒɪkəl ˈpɑːzɪtɪvɪsts/
noun
逻辑实证主义者
Followers of logical positivism, a philosophical movement that emphasizes empirical verification and the importance of logical analysis.
Logical positivists believed that only statements verifiable through observation or logical proof are meaningful.  They rejected metaphysics and theology as nonsensical.
Many prominent philosophers of the early 20th century, such as Moritz Schlick and Rudolf Carnap, were logical positivists.
synonyms: empiricists, verificationists
antonyms: metaphysicians, idealists
n.
