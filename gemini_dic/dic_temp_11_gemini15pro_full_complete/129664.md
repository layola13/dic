non-defining:/ˌnɒn dɪˈfaɪnɪŋ/ /ˌnɑːn dɪˈfaɪnɪŋ/ | adj. | 非限定性的，非定义性的 |  Giving extra information about something, but not essential to identify it.  | My car, which is blue, is parked outside. 我的车，蓝色的那辆，停在外面。 |  parenthetical, supplementary | essential, restrictive | adjective

non-defining:/ˌnɒn dɪˈfaɪnɪŋ/ /ˌnɑːn dɪˈfaɪnɪŋ/ | n. | 非限定性成分 | A word, phrase, or clause that provides additional, non-essential information. | The phrase "which is blue" is a non-defining relative clause. 短语“which is blue”是一个非限定性关系从句。 |  parenthetical, appositive | essential, restrictive | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + defining,  "defining" from the verb "define" which ultimately comes from the Latin "definire" (to limit, determine).
