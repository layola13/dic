fresco:/ˈfrɛskoʊ/ (US), /ˈfrɛskəʊ/ (UK)| n. adj.|壁画；湿壁画技法|A painting done rapidly in watercolor on wet plaster on a wall or ceiling, so that the colors penetrate the plaster and become fixed as it dries. Also, the technique of producing such a painting.|The Sistine Chapel ceiling is a famous example of a fresco. 西斯廷教堂的天花板是湿壁画的著名例子。|近义词：mural, painting|反义词: (No direct antonym for a specific art form)|noun, adjective

fresco:/ˈfrɛskoʊ/ (US), /ˈfrɛskəʊ/ (UK)| adj.|新鲜的；清新的|Fresh and lively.|The fresco colors were still vibrant after centuries.  几个世纪后，壁画的颜色依然鲜艳。|近义词：fresh, vivid, bright|反义词:dull, faded|adjective
