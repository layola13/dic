doughfaceism:/ˈdoʊfeɪsɪzəm/
n.|迎合南方奴隶主的人；假仁假义|A derogatory term used before the American Civil War to describe Northerners who sympathized with or appeased Southern slaveholders, often for political gain.|Historians often cite doughfaceism as a key factor in delaying the inevitable conflict over slavery.|Synonyms: appeasement, conciliation, truckling|Antonyms: abolitionism, resistance, defiance|noun
