chancels: /ˈtʃænsəlz/ (US), /ˈtʃɑːnsəlz/ (UK)| n. |圣坛围栏，祭坛|The part of a church near the altar, reserved for the clergy and choir, and often enclosed by a screen or railing. | The choir stood in the chancels, their voices echoing through the cathedral.  唱诗班站在圣坛围栏里，他们的歌声在教堂里回荡。|  The bride and groom exchanged vows in front of the chancels. 新娘和新郎在圣坛围栏前交换誓言。|Synonyms: sanctuary, apse, chancel| Antonyms: nave, aisle| Noun
