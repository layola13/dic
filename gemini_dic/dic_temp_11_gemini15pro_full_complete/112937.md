lugger:/ˈlʌɡər/ | n. | 小型帆船；耳环（旧）; 笨重的人或物 |A small sailing vessel with one or more lugsails, or a person or thing that is heavy and awkward. | The old lugger struggled against the strong winds. 那艘老式帆船在强风中艰难前行。| synonyms:  sailing boat, sloop, barge | antonyms: yacht, speedboat | noun

lugger:/ˈlʌɡər/ | v. |  用力拖拉；吃力地搬运 | To carry or pull something with difficulty or effort.  | He lugged the heavy suitcase up the stairs. 他吃力地把沉重的行李箱搬上楼。 | synonyms: haul, drag, heave | antonyms: carry lightly, float | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From probably Low German or Dutch *logger, from loggen (“to pull, drag”). Cognate with lug (verb).
