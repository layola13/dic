flange:/flændʒ/
n.|法兰；凸缘；轮缘|A projecting flat rim, collar, or rib on an object, serving to strengthen or attach or (as on a wheel) to maintain position on a rail.|The pipe has a flange at each end. 管子两端都有法兰。|rim, edge, lip|center, core|noun
flange:/flændʒ/
v.|装上凸缘；给…镶边|To provide with a flange.|The workers flanged the steel beams together. 工人们把钢梁用凸缘连接在一起。|border, edge, rim|disconnect, separate|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *flange (“broad flat piece of iron”), perhaps from Old North French flanche (“flank, side”), from Frankish *hlanka (“flank”), from Proto-Germanic *hlankō (“flank, hip”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ḱelh₂- (“to bend, curve”). Cognate with Old High German hlanca (“flank, loin”), Old English hlanca (“haunch, hip”). More at flank.
