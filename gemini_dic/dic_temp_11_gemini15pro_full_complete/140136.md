off-the-track:/ˌɔːf ðə ˈtræk/ | adj. | 脱离正轨的，偏离主题的 | Deviating from the intended course or subject; irrelevant. |  He was off-the-track in his calculations and got the wrong answer. 他的计算偏离了轨道，得到了错误的答案。 | The project went off-the-track due to poor planning.  由于计划不周，项目偏离了轨道。| Synonyms:  sidetracked, misguided, lost, off-course | Antonyms: on-course, on target, focused, directed | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"Off" meaning away from and "track" referring to a course or path.  The literal meaning refers to something deviating from a physical track, as a train. Figuratively, it refers to deviating from a planned course of action or topic.
