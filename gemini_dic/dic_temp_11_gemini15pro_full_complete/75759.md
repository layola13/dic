fulmination:/ˌfʊlmɪˈneɪʃən/ /ˌfʊlmɪˈneɪʃn/ | n. | 猛烈抨击；怒斥；爆炸；爆鸣 | a sudden loud noise; an expression of vehement protest. |  The senator's speech was filled with fulminations against the proposed law.  参议员的演讲充满了对拟议法律的猛烈抨击。 |  The explosion produced a loud fulmination. 爆炸声震耳欲聋。 | Synonyms: denunciation, condemnation, diatribe, explosion, detonation | Antonyms: praise, commendation, compliment,  implosion | noun

fulminate:/ˈfʊlmɪneɪt/ /ˈfʊlmɪneɪt/ | v. | 猛烈抨击；怒斥；爆炸；爆鸣 |  express vehement protest. | The preacher fulminated against the evils of gambling.  传教士猛烈抨击赌博的罪恶。 | The bomb fulminated, destroying the building. 炸弹爆炸，摧毁了建筑物。 |  Synonyms: denounce, condemn, rail, explode, detonate | Antonyms: praise, commend, compliment, implode | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *fulminatus*, past participle of *fulminare* "to strike with lightning, hurl lightning," from *fulmen* (genitive *fulminis*) "lightning flash, thunderbolt," related to *fulgere* "to shine, flash, gleam."
