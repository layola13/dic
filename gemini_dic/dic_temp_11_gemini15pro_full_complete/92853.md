ictus: /ˈɪktəs/| n.| 中风；（诗歌的）重音；（指挥的）击拍；突然发作|A sudden attack or stroke, as of epilepsy or apoplexy.  Also, the stress or beat in a metrical foot of verse or in music.|He suffered an ictus two years ago.|Synonyms: stroke, seizure, attack, fit, paroxysm|Antonyms: remission, recovery|Noun

Etymology->
From Latin ictus ("a blow, stroke, thrust"), perfect passive participle of īcere ("to strike, hit").
