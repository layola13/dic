butterflies:/ˈbʌtərˌflaɪz/ /ˈbʌtərˌflaɪz/| n.|蝴蝶（复数）|The plural form of "butterfly," referring to multiple of these insects.|Butterflies flitted among the flowers. 蝴蝶在花丛中飞舞。|近义词:Lepidoptera (technical term)|反义词:None|Noun

butterfly:/ˈbʌtərˌflaɪ/ /ˈbʌtərˌflaɪz/| n.|蝴蝶|A flying insect with large, often brightly colored wings.|A monarch butterfly landed on her hand. 一只帝王蝶落在她的手上。|近义词:moth|反义词:None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *butterfleoge*, possibly because butterflies were thought to be attracted to dairy products, or because their excrement resembles butter. Alternatively, it might be related to the pale yellow color of some species.
