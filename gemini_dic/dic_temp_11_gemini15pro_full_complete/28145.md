capitulating:/kəˈpɪtʃəleɪtɪŋ/
| v. |投降，屈服，让步|Ceasing to resist an opponent or demand.|例句：After a long siege, the city finally capitulated.  经过长时间的围困，这座城市最终投降了。

例句：He capitulated to his daughter's demands for a new phone. 他屈服于女儿要一部新手机的要求。

|近义词：surrendering, yielding, submitting, giving in|反义词：resisting, fighting, opposing|词性：动词(Verb)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *capitulātus, past participle of capitulāre (“to draw up in chapters, arrange conditions”), from Latin capitulum (“little head”), diminutive of caput (“head”).  The original meaning referred to drawing up terms of agreement, which evolved into the modern sense of surrendering.
