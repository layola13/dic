maligning:/məˈlaɪnɪŋ/
verb
诽谤，污蔑
The act of saying false and unpleasant things about someone or something, in order to damage their reputation.
He was accused of maligning the company's reputation. 他被指控损害公司名誉。

maligning:/məˈlaɪnɪŋ/
gerund or present participle
诽谤，污蔑
A form of the verb "malign" used as a noun (gerund) or as an adjective (present participle).
Maligning someone's character can have serious consequences. 诽谤某人的性格会造成严重后果。


SYNONYMS->
slandering, defaming, libeling, vilifying, traducing, disparaging, denigrating, badmouthing, besmirching

ANTONYMS->
praising, commending, lauding, extolling, eulogizing


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English malignen, from Old French malignier, from Latin malignari (“to act badly”), from malignus (“ill-disposed, wicked”), from male (“badly”) + genus (“kind, sort, race”).
