deconstructed:/ˌdiːkənˈstrʌktɪd/
adjective
解构的，拆分的
Having been analyzed and interpreted critically, typically in terms of social or cultural assumptions and values.
例句：The novel offers a deconstructed view of heroism. 这部小说提供了一个对英雄主义的解构观点。

例句：The chef served a deconstructed cheesecake, with the crust, filling, and berries presented separately.  厨师端上了一份解构的芝士蛋糕，蛋糕底、馅料和浆果分开摆放。

近义词：analyzed, interpreted, dissected, broken down
反义词：conventional, traditional, orthodox
词性: adjective


deconstruct:/ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkt/
verb
解构，拆分
To analyze (something) in detail, typically in order to reveal its hidden assumptions and contradictions.
例句：The professor deconstructed the poem, revealing its underlying themes of loss and despair. 教授解构了这首诗，揭示了其潜在的失落和绝望主题。

例句：The architect deconstructed the traditional design of the house, creating a more modern and open space.  建筑师解构了房屋的传统设计，创造了一个更现代、更开放的空间。

近义词：analyze, interpret, dissect, break down
反义词：synthesize, construct, build up
词性: verb


词源:
From post-structuralist philosophy, coined in French as *déconstruire* from *dé-* (meaning "undoing, reversal") + *construire* (meaning "to construct").  The English term "deconstruct" emerged in the late 20th century.
