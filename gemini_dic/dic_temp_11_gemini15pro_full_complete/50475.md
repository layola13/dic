depressibilities:/dɪˌprɛsəˈbɪlɪtiz/ | n. | 沮丧；抑郁；萧条 |  The state or quality of being easily depressed or made gloomy.  A susceptibility to depression, especially economic depression. | The economist warned of the depressibilities inherent in the current market.  The depressibilities of the post-war era affected many families. | 近义词: vulnerabilities, weaknesses, susceptibilities | 反义词: resiliencies, strengths, stabilities | 词性: 名词

词源:  From "depressible" + "-ities" (suffix forming plural nouns). "Depressible" comes from "depress" + "-ible" (suffix meaning "capable of being"). "Depress" ultimately derives from Latin "depressus," past participle of "deprimere" ("to press down").
