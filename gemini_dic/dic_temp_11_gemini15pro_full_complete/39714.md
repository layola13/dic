consignification:/kənˌsɪɡnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |共同意义，共同指称；联义|The process by which words or expressions come to share a common meaning or referent, often through metaphorical extension or cultural association.  It refers to the phenomenon where multiple signs contribute to a shared understanding or meaning.|The consignification of "dove" with peace has a long history.  鸽子与和平的联义有着悠久的历史。|Synonyms: connotation, implication, association|Antonyms: denotation, explicit meaning|noun

consignification:/kənˌsɪɡnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |共同赋义|The act of giving a shared meaning or significance to something. This often involves multiple signs or symbols contributing to a unified understanding.|The artist used color and form in consignification to create a sense of unease.  这位艺术家运用色彩和形式的共同赋义来营造一种不安的感觉。|Synonyms: shared meaning, joint signification |Antonyms: individual meaning, separate signification|noun
