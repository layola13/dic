frisson:/ˈfriːsɔːn/ /frɪˈsɑːn/| n.|一阵颤栗，激动，兴奋|A sudden, brief thrill of excitement, fear, or pleasure.|例句：A frisson of excitement ran through the crowd as the music began. 音乐响起时，人群中掠过一阵兴奋的颤栗。

例句：She felt a frisson of fear as she walked through the dark alley. 当她穿过黑暗的小巷时，感到一阵恐惧的颤栗。|近义词：thrill, shiver, shudder, tingle|反义词: apathy, indifference|词性:noun

Etymology: From French frisson, from Old French friçon, from Late Latin *frictiōnem, accusative of *frictiō (“a rubbing, friction”), from Latin frictus, past participle of fricāre (“to rub”).
