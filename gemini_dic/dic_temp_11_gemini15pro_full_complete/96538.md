indomitably:/ɪnˈdɒmɪtəbli/ | adv. |不屈不挠地，顽强地 | In a way that is impossible to subdue or defeat.  Showing courage and unwillingness to give up. |  She fought indomitably against the disease. 她顽强地与病魔作斗争。 | resolutely, stubbornly, persistently, tenaciously | submissively, meekly, passively | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *indomitabilis, from in- (“not”) + domitabilis (“tameable”), from domitare (“to tame”), from domitus, past participle of domare (“to tame, subdue”).
