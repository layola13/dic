chutzpah: /ˈhʊtspə, ˈxʊtspə/| n.|厚颜无耻；胆大妄为；过分的自信|Supreme self-confidence; audacity.  It suggests a kind of nerve or arrogance that allows someone to do or say things that others would find shocking or unbelievable.|He had the chutzpah to ask for a raise after only a week on the job. 他竟然敢在工作才一个星期就要求加薪。|近义词: audacity, nerve, gall, temerity, boldness|反义词: timidity, shyness, modesty, humility|词性: noun

词源:
From Yiddish חוצפּה (khutspe), from Hebrew חֻצְפָּה (ḥuṣpā), from חָצַף (ḥāṣaf, “to be insolent, impudent”).
