emaculate:/ɪˈmækjʊlət/ | adj. | 洁净的，无瑕疵的；完美的 | Perfectly clean, neat, or tidy; free from flaws or mistakes.  | Her apartment was always immaculate. 她的公寓总是 spotless. | 近义词：spotless, pristine, flawless, impeccable | 反义词：dirty, messy, flawed, imperfect | 词性: 形容词

emaculate:/ɪˈmækjʊleɪt/ | v. |  使洁净，使无瑕疵 | To remove flaws or blemishes; to make perfect. |  He emaculated the manuscript before submitting it. 他在提交手稿之前把它修改得 spotless. | 近义词：cleanse, purify, refine | 反义词：soil, stain, corrupt | 词性: 动词

词源: From Latin *emaculatus*, past participle of *emaculare* "to remove spots," from *e-* "out" + *macula* "spot."
