haunts:/hɔːnts/ | v. | 萦绕，缠绕；常去；(鬼魂)出没 | to be persistently in the mind of (someone); to visit (a place) regularly; (of a ghost) to appear in a place repeatedly | The memory of the accident still haunts him.  事故的记忆仍然萦绕着他。| synonyms:  plagues, troubles, torments, frequents, visits | antonyms:  evades, avoids | verb

haunts:/hɔːnts/ | n. | 常去的地方；出没地 | a place frequently visited | This cafe is one of my favorite haunts. 这家咖啡馆是我最喜欢的去处之一。| synonyms:  hangouts,  abodes,  resorts | antonyms:  none | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *haunten*, from Old North French *hanter* (to frequent, practice), of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Frankish *heim* (“home”) (whence also English *home*).
