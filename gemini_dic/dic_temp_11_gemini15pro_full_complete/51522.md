detritus:/dɪˈtraɪtəs/| n.|碎屑，残渣，废墟|Waste or debris of any kind, organic or inorganic; fragments resulting from disintegration or wearing away.|The forest floor was covered with detritus from the trees.|Synonyms: debris, waste, rubble, remains, sediment|Antonyms: wholeness, entirety|noun

detritus:/dɪˈtraɪtəs/| n.|（生态学）腐质|Dead organic matter, especially plant matter, that forms a layer on the ground in forests and other ecosystems, providing nutrients for other organisms.|The detritus on the forest floor provides a habitat for many invertebrates.|Synonyms: humus, leaf litter, organic matter|Antonyms: living matter|noun
