back-stabbing: /ˈbækˌstæbɪŋ/ | adj. n. | 背后捅刀子；暗箭伤人 | The act of harming someone by betraying them secretly. |  She accused her colleagues of back-stabbing to get ahead. 她指责她的同事为了升职而背后捅刀子。

back-stabbing: /ˈbækˌstæbɪŋ/ | adj. n. | 背后捅刀子；暗箭伤人 |  Involving or characterized by the act of betraying someone secretly. | The office was rife with back-stabbing and infighting. 办公室里充满了背后捅刀子和内讧。

back-stabber: /ˈbækˌstæbər/ | n. | 背后捅刀子的人 | A person who harms someone by betraying them secretly. | He was known as a back-stabber and was distrusted by everyone. 他被称为背后捅刀子的人，并且被所有人不信任。


近义词: treacherous, deceitful, two-faced, disloyal, underhanded, perfidious
反义词: loyal, trustworthy, faithful, supportive
词性:  adjective, noun (referring to the act)


词源:  "Back" (referring to the back of a person) + "stabbing" (from the verb "to stab," meaning to wound with a pointed weapon). The term evokes the image of literally stabbing someone in the back, a cowardly and unexpected attack.
