inaniloquent:/ɪnænˈɪləkwənt/ | adj. | 无言的，沉默寡言的 | Unable to express oneself clearly or fluently; not eloquent. |  He became inaniloquent when asked about his feelings.  他被问及感受时变得沉默寡言。| Synonyms: speechless, taciturn, inarticulate | Antonyms: eloquent, articulate, loquacious | word property

Etymology:
From Latin *inaniloquus*, from *in-* (not) + *anilis* (old woman's, foolish) + *loquus* (speaking), from *loqui* (to speak).  The original sense was "speaking foolishly," but it has evolved to mean simply "not speaking."
