obligedly:/əˈblaɪdʒɪdli/
adverb
殷勤地，乐意地，感激地
In a way that shows willingness to do a service or kindness.
"He obligedly answered all of her questions." 他殷勤地回答了她所有的问题。
Synonyms: willingly, gladly, readily, cheerfully, courteously
Antonyms: unwillingly, reluctantly, grudgingly
词性: 副词

obliged:/əˈblaɪdʒd/
adjective
感激的，蒙恩的，义不容辞的
Feeling or showing gratitude.
"I'm much obliged to you for your help." 我非常感谢你的帮助。
Synonyms: grateful, thankful, indebted
Antonyms: ungrateful, unappreciative
词性: 形容词

oblige:/əˈblaɪdʒ/
verb
强制，迫使，帮忙，施恩惠
Make (someone) legally or morally bound to an action or course of action.
"Doctors are obliged by law to keep patients alive while there is a chance of recovery." 法律规定医生有义务在病人有康复机会的情况下使其存活。
Synonyms: compel, force, require, bind, constrain
Antonyms: allow, permit, excuse
词性: 动词

词源:
From Old French obliger (to bind, compel), from Latin obligare (to bind, tie up), from ob- (towards) + ligare (to bind).
