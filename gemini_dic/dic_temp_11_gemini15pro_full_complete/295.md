aberrant:/ˈæbərənt/| adj.|异常的，反常的，偏离正道的|Deviating from the usual or natural type; atypical.  Straying from the right or normal way.|例句：His aberrant behavior led to his dismissal. 他的异常行为导致他被解雇。

例句：The aberrant data point was removed from the study.  这个异常的数据点被从研究中移除。|近义词：anomalous, atypical, deviant, irregular, abnormal|反义词：normal, typical, usual, standard|词性:形容词

aberration:/ˌæbəˈreɪʃn/| n.|异常，反常，偏差|A departure from what is normal, usual, or expected, typically an unwelcome one. A deviation or distortion in the appearance of an image caused by a defect in a lens or mirror.|例句：The recent drop in sales is just a temporary aberration. 最近的销售额下降只是一个暂时的异常现象。

例句：The Hubble telescope was initially plagued by an optical aberration. 哈勃望远镜最初受到光学像差的困扰。|近义词：anomaly, deviation, irregularity, abnormality|反义词：normality, regularity, typicality|词性:名词

aberrate:/ˈæbəreɪt/| v.|偏离，脱离常轨|To deviate from the normal or correct course.|例句：The compass needle began to aberrate wildly. 指南针的指针开始剧烈偏转。

例句：Stress can cause people to aberrate from their usual routines. 压力会导致人们偏离他们通常的日常生活。|近义词：deviate, stray, diverge, digress|反义词：conform, adhere, comply|词性:动词
