limply:/ˈlɪmpli/| adv.|无力地，柔软地|In a way that lacks firmness, strength, or stiffness.|Her hand lay limply in his. 她的手无力地躺在他的手中。|weakly, feebly|firmly, strongly|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From limp (adjective) + -ly (adverb suffix).  The word "limp" comes from Old English *limpian, likely related to Old High German limphan "to be lame," perhaps of imitative origin.
