constitutions:/ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃnz/ (UK), /ˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃnz/ (US)
n.
宪法；章程；体质；构成
A constitution is a set of fundamental principles or established precedents according to which a state or other organization is governed.
The United States Constitution is the supreme law of the land. 美国宪法是国家的最高法律。
The club's constitution outlines the rules and regulations for members.  俱乐部的章程概述了会员的规章制度。

近义词: charters, frameworks, laws
反义词: lawlessness, anarchy
词性: noun


Etymology:
From Late Middle English *constitucioun, from Old French constitucion, from Latin cōnstitūtiō (“establishment, arrangement, order, institution, decree, nature, constitution, temperament”), from cōnstituō (“set up, establish, found, build, construct, arrange, ordain, appoint, create, make, compose”).
