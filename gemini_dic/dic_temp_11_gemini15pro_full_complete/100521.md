interviews:/ˈɪntərˌvjuːz/ (US), /ˈɪntəvjuːz/ (UK)
n.|面试；面谈；采访|A formal meeting in which someone is asked questions to find out if they are suitable for a job, course of study, etc., or to find out their opinions.|
例句：
I had three interviews for the job. 我为了这份工作参加了三次面试。

例句：
The interviews were conducted by telephone. 这些面试是通过电话进行的。

近义词：meetings, discussions, consultations
反义词:| N/A (No direct antonym for interviews)
词性: noun (plural)

interview:/ˈɪntərˌvjuː/ (US), /ˈɪntəvjuː/ (UK)
vt.|面试；面谈；采访|To ask someone questions in a formal meeting in order to find out if they are suitable for a job, course of study, etc., or to find out their opinions.|
例句：
We interviewed over 20 candidates for the position. 我们面试了20多位候选人。

例句：
They interviewed him about his experiences. 他们采访了他关于他的经历。

近义词：question, examine, assess
反义词:| N/A (No direct antonym for interview as a verb)
词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *entrevue*, from *entrevoir* "to see each other, catch sight of," from *entre-* "between" + *voir* "to see."
