half-deafened:/ˌhɑːfˈdɛfənd/ /ˌhæfˈdɛfənd/| adj.|半聋的，听力部分受损的|Partially deafened; having one's hearing impaired.  The loud explosion left him half-deafened.|例句：The concert left me half-deafened.  这场音乐会让我听力部分受损。

例句：He was half-deafened by the explosion. 他被爆炸震得半聋。|近义词：partially deaf, hard of hearing|反义词：hearing clearly, hearing perfectly|词性：形容词

half-:/hɑːf/ /hæf/| n. adj. adv.|一半，半个|One of two equal or approximately equal parts of a divisible whole. |
deafen:/ˈdɛfən/| vt.|使聋，震耳欲聋|To make deaf; deprive of hearing, either permanently or temporarily. Also, to stun or overwhelm with noise.|
-ed:| suffix.|过去分词后缀，形容词后缀|Used to form the past participle of regular verbs, and also used to form adjectives.|
