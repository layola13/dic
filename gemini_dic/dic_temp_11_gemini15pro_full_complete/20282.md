一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Saya bisa berenang. = I can swim.
Dia bisa memasak. = She can cook.

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Saya sedang bisa mengerjakan tugas ini. = I am currently able to work on this assignment. (Note: This is less common usage, as "bisa" usually implies a general ability.)
Dia sedang bisa membantu kita. = He/She is currently able to help us.


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
Saya sudah bisa menyelesaikan tugas itu. = I have been able to finish that task.
Dia sudah bisa membaca buku itu. = He/She has been able to read that book.


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
(Not applicable -  "bisa" generally doesn't work with this tense in Indonesian/Malay)


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
Saya bisa berenang waktu kecil. = I could swim when I was young. (Note: the past tense is often implied by context rather than a change in the word "bisa" itself.)
Dulu dia bisa berbicara bahasa Prancis. = He/She used to be able to speak French.

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):

Dia sudah bisa membaca buku itu sebelum ujian. = He/She had been able to read the book before the exam.

一般将来时 (Simple Future):
Saya akan bisa menyetir mobil tahun depan. = I will be able to drive a car next year.
Dia akan bisa berbicara bahasa Jepang setelah belajar. = He/She will be able to speak Japanese after studying.

(Not applicable)

将来完成时 (Future Perfect):

Dia akan sudah bisa menulis buku itu sebelum akhir tahun. = He/She will have been able to write the book before the end of the year.

虚拟语气:
Seandainya saya bisa terbang, saya akan pergi ke bulan. = If I could fly, I would go to the moon.
Saya berharap bisa berbicara bahasa Spanyol. = I wish I could speak Spanish.


被动语态:
(Not generally applicable in Indonesian/Malay)


疑问句:
Bisakah kamu berenang? = Can you swim?
Bisakah dia memasak? = Can she cook?

否定句:
Saya tidak bisa berenang. = I cannot swim.
Dia tidak bisa memasak. = She cannot cook.
