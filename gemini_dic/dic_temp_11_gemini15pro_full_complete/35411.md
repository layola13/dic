co-optive:/koʊˈɑːptɪv/
adjective
吸收的，拉拢的
tending to assimilate or take in and incorporate as one's own or one's group's
The club has a co-optive policy, meaning that new members are chosen by the existing members.  这个俱乐部采取吸收政策，意味着新成员由现有成员选择。
The government's co-optive strategy aimed to bring potential dissidents into the fold. 政府的拉拢策略旨在将潜在的异议人士纳入其阵营。
近义词: assimilative, incorporative, integrating
反义词: exclusive, rejecting
词性: adjective


co-opt:/koʊˈɑːpt/
verb
吸收，拉拢
appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members.
The board co-opted several community leaders onto the committee. 董事会吸收了几位社区领袖加入委员会。
The rebels were co-opted into the government, effectively ending the uprising. 反叛者被拉拢加入政府，有效地结束了起义。
近义词: absorb, incorporate, enlist
反义词: exclude, reject
词性: verb

词源:
From Latin co-optāre ‘choose as a colleague’, from co- ‘together’ + optāre ‘choose, desire’.
