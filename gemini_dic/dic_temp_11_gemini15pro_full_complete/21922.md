bog-down:/bɒɡ daʊn/ | v. | 陷入困境，停滞不前 | to become stuck in wet ground or to become so involved in something difficult or complicated that you cannot make progress | The car bogged down in the mud. 汽车陷在泥里了。| 近义词：stall, mire, stick, founder | 反义词：expedite, advance, progress | 动词

bog-down:/bɑːɡ daʊn/ | v. | 陷入困境，停滞不前 | to become stuck in wet ground or to become so involved in something difficult or complicated that you cannot make progress | The project bogged down in a mire of bureaucratic paperwork.  该项目陷入了繁琐的官僚文书工作的泥潭。 | 近义词：stall, mire, stick, founder | 反义词：expedite, advance, progress | 动词

词源: From 'bog' (n.) + 'down' (adv.). 'Bog' comes from Irish or Scottish Gaelic 'bogach', meaning 'soft, wet ground'.
