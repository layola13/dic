old-womanism: /ˌoʊldˈwʊmənɪzəm/ | n. | 老妇主义; 老妇人主义 |  A term coined by Alice Walker to describe the wisdom, strength, and influence of older Black women within their communities and families, often drawing on African and African American cultural traditions. It emphasizes the importance of their lived experiences and perspectives in shaping and preserving cultural heritage. |  "Old-womanism is about respecting and valuing the wisdom and experience of older Black women." |  Near synonyms:  womanism, feminism, matriarchy | Antonym: patriarchy | Noun
