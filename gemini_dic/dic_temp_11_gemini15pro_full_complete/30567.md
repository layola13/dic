cerasinous:/sɛrˈæsɪnəs/| adj.|樱桃胶的，似樱桃胶的|Resembling or containing cerasin, a gummy substance found in cherry trees.|The tree exuded a cerasinous substance. 这棵树渗出一种像樱桃胶的物质。|Synonyms: gummy, resinous, viscous|Antonyms: watery, thin, fluid|adjective

Etymology:
From cerasin + -ous.  Cerasin itself comes from French *cérasine, from Latin cerasus "cherry tree," from Greek kerasos "cherry." The "-ous" suffix is a common adjective-forming suffix derived from Latin -osus, meaning "full of" or "having the qualities of."
