dementia:/dɪˈmɛnʃə/| n. |痴呆，失智|A chronic or persistent disorder of the mental processes caused by brain disease or injury and marked by memory disorders, personality changes, and impaired reasoning.|My grandfather suffers from dementia. 我的祖父患有痴呆症。| Alzheimer's disease is the most common cause of dementia. 阿尔茨海默病是痴呆症最常见的原因。|Synonyms: senility, Alzheimer's, cognitive impairment|Antonyms: lucidity, mental sharpness|noun


demented:/dɪˈmɛntɪd/| adj. |疯狂的，精神错乱的|Driven to behave irrationally due to anger, distress, or excitement.|He had a demented look in his eyes. 他的眼神看起来很疯狂。|She was demented with grief after her son's death.  儿子死后，她悲痛欲绝，精神错乱。|Synonyms: insane, mad, crazy, deranged|Antonyms: sane, rational, lucid|adjective
