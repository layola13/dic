obfuscatory:/ˌɒbfʌsˈkeɪtəri/ | adj. | 模糊的，混乱的，故弄玄虚的 | Making something unclear or confusing, especially intentionally. |  The politician's obfuscatory language made it difficult to understand his true position.  这位政客含糊其辞的语言让人难以理解他的真实立场。| synonyms: confusing, unclear, bewildering, misleading, evasive, ambiguous | antonyms: clear, lucid, transparent, straightforward, unambiguous | adjective

obfuscate:/ˈɒbfʌskeɪt/ | v. | 使模糊，使混乱，使困惑 | Render obscure, unclear, or unintelligible. | The company's financial reports were obfuscated by complex accounting practices. 该公司的财务报告因复杂的会计实务而变得模糊不清。 | synonyms: confuse, obscure, bewilder, muddle, complicate | antonyms: clarify, elucidate, explain, simplify | verb

obfuscation:/ˌɒbfʌsˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 模糊，混乱，困惑 | The act of making something obscure, unclear, or unintelligible. |  The lawyer's obfuscation of the facts made it difficult for the jury to reach a verdict. 律师对事实的混淆使得陪审团难以做出裁决。 | synonyms: confusion, obscurity, bewilderment | antonyms: clarity, lucidity, transparency | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *obfuscatus, past participle of obfuscare "to darken," from Latin ob "over, against" + fuscare "to darken," from fuscus "dark."
