blowoff:/ˈbloʊɒf/ (US) /ˈbləʊɒf/ (UK)| n.|释放；排气；轻视；解雇；借口；爽约|a release of pressure; a dismissive action or comment; an excuse; a cancellation|例句：The factory blowoff startled the nearby residents. 工厂的排气吓了附近的居民一跳。

He treated my suggestion as a blowoff. 他对我的建议不屑一顾。|近义词：rejection, dismissal, excuse, snub, slight|反义词: acceptance, embrace|词性:noun


blow off:/ˌbloʊ ˈɔːf/ (US) /ˌbləʊ ˈɒf/ (UK)| v.|释放；排气；轻视；解雇；吹掉|to release pressure; to dismiss or ignore; to fire someone; to blow something away|例句：The pressure cooker needs to blow off steam.  高压锅需要排气。


She blew off her responsibilities and went to the beach. 她对自己的责任置之不理，去了海滩。|近义词：ignore, dismiss, neglect, disregard, skip|反义词: attend to, address, honor|词性:verb


词源: blow + off. "blow" meaning to expel air, and "off" indicating separation or removal.  The noun form is derived from the verb.
