calcination:/ˌkæl.sɪˈneɪ.ʃən/| n.|煅烧，焙烧|The process of heating a substance to a high temperature but below the melting or fusing point, causing loss of moisture, volatile matter, or oxidation.|The limestone undergoes calcination in the kiln. 石灰石在窑中进行煅烧。|近义词：roasting, burning, firing|反义词: hydration|noun
