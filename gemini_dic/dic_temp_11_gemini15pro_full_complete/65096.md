etherealism:/ɪˈθɪərɪəlɪzəm/ | n. | 天上主义;空灵主义;超凡脱俗 | The quality of being ethereal; dreamlike, otherworldly, delicate and light. A style of art that emphasizes the spiritual or otherworldly. |  Her paintings capture a sense of etherealism, transporting the viewer to a realm beyond the everyday. 她的画捕捉到了一种空灵主义的感觉，将观者带到一个超越日常的境界。|  近义词: spirituality, otherworldliness, mysticism, idealism | 反义词: realism, materialism, pragmatism | 词性: noun

ethereal:/ɪˈθɪərɪəl/ | adj. | 天上的;空灵的;飘渺的 | Extremely delicate and light in a way that seems too perfect for this world. | Her voice was ethereal, like the sound of angels singing. 她的声音空灵，像天使在歌唱。 | 近义词: airy, delicate, heavenly, celestial, otherworldly | 反义词: earthly, mundane, material, substantial | 词性: adjective

词源:  From Late Latin aethereus "of the upper air," from Greek aitherios, from aithēr "upper air; bright, pure sky" (see ether). The -al suffix forms adjectives, and the -ism suffix denotes a doctrine, belief or system of thought.
