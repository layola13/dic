hyperpersonal:/ˌhaɪpərˈpɜːrsənəl/ | adj. | 超个人的，超乎寻常的亲密的 |  Relating to online communication that is more intimate than face-to-face interaction, often due to factors like selective self-presentation and asynchronous communication. |  Online relationships can become hyperpersonal, allowing individuals to craft idealized versions of themselves.  网上关系可以变得超乎寻常的亲密，让人们能够塑造理想化的自我形象。|  intimate, close, intense | impersonal, distant, detached | adjective

Etymology->
"Hyper-" (Greek): over, beyond, excessive.  "Personal" (Latin): relating to a particular person. The term was coined by Joseph Walther in the 1990s.
