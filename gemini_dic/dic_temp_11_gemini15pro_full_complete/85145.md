hard-pressed:/ˌhɑːrdˈprɛst/ | adj. | 陷入困境的，拮据的，很难的 |  Being under great difficulty or stress, especially financially or in terms of time or resources.  Having difficulty doing something, especially because there is not enough time or money. | Example: The company is hard-pressed to meet the deadline.  这家公司很难按期完成任务。

Example:  Many families are hard-pressed to pay their bills. 许多家庭难以支付账单。| 近义词: strained, stressed, struggling, under pressure | 反义词: prosperous, affluent, well-off | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From "hard" (meaning difficult) and "pressed" (past participle of "press," meaning to exert force or pressure).  The original meaning referred to being literally pressed hard, but the meaning evolved to encompass figurative pressure.
