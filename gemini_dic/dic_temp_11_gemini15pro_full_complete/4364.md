alabastrum:/æləˈbæstrəm/
n.
花蕾，花芽
A flower bud; specifically, the flower bud of a plant before the petals unfold.
The alabastrum of the rose was just beginning to open. 玫瑰的花蕾才刚刚开始绽放。
synonyms: bud, flower bud
antonyms: bloom, blossom
noun


alabastrum:/æləˈbæstrəm/
n.
(药剂用)小玻璃瓶，药瓶
A small glass or earthenware vessel, usually with a narrow neck, used for holding ointments or perfumes.  (Primarily archaic in this sense.)
The apothecary carefully measured the herbs and placed them in the alabastrum. 药剂师仔细地量好药草，并将它们放入小药瓶中。
synonyms: vial, phial, flask
antonyms: N/A
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *alabastrum, from Greek ἀλάβαστρον (alabastron), a box or vase made of alabaster (a soft, usually white, gypsum), used for holding perfumes and ointments. The term was later extended to the shape of the container regardless of the material, and eventually to flower buds resembling this shape.
