marsupion:/mɑːrˈsuːpiən/ | n. |有袋动物|A marsupial; an animal, such as a kangaroo or opossum, whose young are born incompletely developed and are typically carried and suckled in a pouch on the mother's belly.|Kangaroos and koalas are marsupions found in Australia.  袋鼠和考拉是在澳大利亚发现的有袋动物。|Synonyms: marsupial |Antonyms: placental mammal | Noun
