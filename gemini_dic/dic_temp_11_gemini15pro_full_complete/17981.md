befuddlement:/bɪˈfʌdəlmənt/| n. |困惑，迷惑，糊涂|A state of being confused and unable to think clearly.|The complex instructions caused complete befuddlement among the users. 复杂的说明让用户们完全摸不着头脑。|Synonyms: confusion, bewilderment, perplexity, disorientation|Antonyms: clarity, understanding, lucidity|noun


Etymology->
From the verb "befuddle," which originated in the early 17th century.  "Befuddle" is a combination of "be-" (a prefix meaning "thoroughly" or "completely") and "fuddle," an earlier verb meaning "to act in a clumsy or inefficient way," possibly related to "fondle" or the Dutch word "foppen" (to deceive).
