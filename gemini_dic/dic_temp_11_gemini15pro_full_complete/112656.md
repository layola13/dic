low-cost:/ˌloʊˈkɔːst/| adj.|低成本的，廉价的|Relatively low in price; inexpensive.  Formed by combining "low" and "cost".  "Low" comes from Old English "lah," meaning "not high." "Cost" comes from Old French "couster," ultimately from Latin "constare," meaning "to stand firm, stand at a price."|We flew with a low-cost airline to save money. 我们乘坐廉价航空公司以节省费用。|Synonyms: inexpensive, cheap, budget, affordable|Antonyms: expensive, costly, high-end|adjective

low cost:/ˌloʊ ˈkɔːst/| n.|低成本|The state of being relatively inexpensive. |The low cost of living attracted many people to the area.  该地区的低生活成本吸引了很多人。|Synonyms: inexpensiveness, cheapness|Antonyms: expensiveness, high cost|noun
