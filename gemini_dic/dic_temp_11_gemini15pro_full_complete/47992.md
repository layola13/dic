decimation:/ˌdesɪˈmeɪʃn/ | n. | 大量毁灭，严重削弱 | The decimation of the population by the plague was devastating. | 例句：The decimation of the rainforest is a serious environmental concern.  雨林的大量破坏是一个严重的环境问题。

例句：The company's profits were decimated by the recession.  经济衰退使公司的利润大幅下降。 | 近义词：destruction, devastation, annihilation, slaughter | 反义词：preservation, construction, growth | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *decimatio*, from decem (“ten”). Originally referred to the Roman military practice of killing every tenth man in a mutinous legion.
