chirpiness:/ˈtʃɜːrpɪnɪs/ | n. |快乐，活泼|The state of being cheerful and lively. |Her chirpiness was infectious, brightening everyone's mood. 她的快乐很有感染力，让每个人的心情都好了起来。|Synonyms: cheerfulness, liveliness, lightheartedness, buoyancy, exuberance |Antonyms: gloominess, sullenness, moroseness, dejection, glumness | noun

chirpy:/ˈtʃɜːrpi/ | adj. |活泼的，快乐的|Characterized by a cheerful and lively manner. |He had a chirpy disposition, always whistling and singing. 他性格开朗，总是吹口哨唱歌。|Synonyms: cheerful, lively, merry, jolly, upbeat |Antonyms: gloomy, sullen, morose, dejected, glum | adjective


Etymology:
From "chirp" (imitating the short, sharp sound of a small bird) + "-y" (suffix forming adjectives) + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns from adjectives).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *kirp-.
