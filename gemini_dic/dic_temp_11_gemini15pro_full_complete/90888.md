huns:/hʌnz/
|n.|匈奴人；破坏者；野蛮人|A nomadic people of Central Asia, who raided Europe and Asia in the 4th and 5th centuries.  Also used informally to refer to vandals or destructive people, often in a humorous or derogatory way. |The Huns swept across the plains, leaving destruction in their wake. 匈奴人席卷平原，留下满目疮痍。|synonyms: barbarians, vandals, destroyers|antonyms: protectors, guardians|noun


huns:/hʌnz/
|n.| (informal, often derogatory) A group of boisterous or rowdy women, typically enjoying a social gathering. |The huns were out in force last night, celebrating Sarah's birthday. 昨晚姑娘们成群结队地出去庆祝莎拉的生日。|synonyms: girls, ladies (neutral), lasses (informal)|antonyms: (No direct antonym for this informal usage)|noun
