clerical:/ˈklerɪkəl/| adj.|文书的；牧师的；教士的|Relating to office work or administration, especially routine documentation and paperwork; relating to the clergy.|例句：She works a clerical job at the local council. 她在当地议会做文书工作。

例句：He wears clerical garb. 他穿着牧师的服装。|近义词：administrative, secretarial, priestly, ministerial|反义词：lay, secular|词性:形容词

cleric:/ˈklerɪk/| n.|神职人员；牧师；教士|A member of the clergy, especially in a Christian church.|例句：The cleric delivered a moving sermon. 牧师发表了一篇感人的布道。

例句：He aspired to be a cleric from a young age. 他从小就渴望成为一名神职人员。|近义词：priest, minister, clergyman, parson|反义词：layperson|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clericali*, from Late Latin *clericālis*, from Latin *clericus* (“cleric, clergyman”), from Ancient Greek *κληρικός* (*klērikós*, “of or pertaining to an inheritance, of or pertaining to the clergy”), from *κλῆρος* (*klêros*, “lot, share, inheritance, allotted portion of land, office, duty”). Doublet of *clerk*.
