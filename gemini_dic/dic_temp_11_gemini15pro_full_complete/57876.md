dribbles:/ˈdrɪbəlz/ (US), /ˈdrɪblz/ (UK)| n. v.|运球，滴下，少量|The act of bouncing a ball repeatedly, typically in basketball or soccer. Also, a small quantity of liquid falling slowly.|例句：He dribbles the basketball skillfully. 他熟练地运着篮球。


dribbles:/ˈdrɪbəlz/ (US), /ˈdrɪblz/ (UK)| n. |运球，滴下，少量|A small quantity of liquid falling slowly.|例句：Dribbles of sweat ran down his face.  汗珠顺着他的脸流了下来。|近义词：drops, trickles|反义词:gush, flood|词性:名词


dribble:/ˈdrɪbəl/ (US), /ˈdrɪbl/ (UK)| v. |运球，滴下，流口水|To bounce a ball repeatedly or to let liquid fall slowly in drops.|例句：She dribbled the ball down the court. 她运球沿着球场前进。


dribble:/ˈdrɪbəl/ (US), /ˈdrɪbl/ (UK)| v. |运球，滴下，流口水|To let saliva flow from the mouth.|例句：The baby dribbled milk down its chin. 婴儿的牛奶顺着下巴流了下来。|近义词：drool, slobber|反义词: N/A|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *driblen, frequentative of drippen (“to drip”), from Old English *dryppan, from Proto-Germanic *druppaną (“to drip”).
