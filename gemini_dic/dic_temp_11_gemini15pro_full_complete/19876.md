biniodide:/baɪˈaɪəˌdaɪd/
|n.|二碘化物|a chemical compound containing two iodine atoms per molecule.  A compound of iodine with another element or radical, in which the iodine is the more electronegative element.|Mercury biniodide is a scarlet red crystalline powder.|Synonyms: diiodide|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a specific chemical compound)|noun
