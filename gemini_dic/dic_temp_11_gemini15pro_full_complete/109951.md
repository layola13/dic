liberalizer:/ˈlɪbərəlaɪzər/
n.|自由化者，放松管制者|A person who promotes or implements policies that reduce restrictions and promote greater freedom, often in economic or political contexts.|The government appointed a liberalizer to oversee the deregulation of the banking industry. 政府任命了一位自由化者来监督银行业的放松管制。|Synonyms: reformer, innovator, progressive|Antonyms: conservative, traditionalist, reactionary
词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From liberalize + -er.  "Liberalize" comes from "liberal" (favoring individual freedom) + "-ize" (to make or become). "Liberal" ultimately derives from Latin "liber" (free).
