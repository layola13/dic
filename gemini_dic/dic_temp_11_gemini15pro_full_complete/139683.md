ocd:/ˌoʊ siː ˈdiː/| n. |强迫症(Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder)|Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) is a mental disorder in which a person has certain thoughts repeatedly (obsessions) or feels the need to perform certain routines repeatedly (compulsions) to an extent that generates distress or impairs general functioning.|He was diagnosed with OCD after years of struggling with intrusive thoughts and rituals.|近义词: compulsion, obsession|反义词: (No direct antonym for a medical condition)|词性: noun
