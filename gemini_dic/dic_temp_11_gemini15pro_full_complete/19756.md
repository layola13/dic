billfolds:/ˈbɪlfəʊldz/
n.
钱包（尤指男用）
A folding wallet, especially one used by men, for carrying paper money.
He pulled a thick wad of cash from his billfold. 他从钱包里掏出一大叠现金。
wallets, purses
handbags, backpacks
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From bill + fold.  "Bill" in this context originally referred to a written or printed document, often a bill of exchange or other financial document.  "Fold" simply refers to the act of folding, as billfolds are typically folded.  The term became common in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
