convincing:/kənˈvɪnsɪŋ/| adj.|令人信服的；有说服力的|Able to persuade somebody to believe that something is true or to act.|Her argument was very convincing. 她的论点很有说服力。|近义词：persuasive, compelling, cogent|反义词：unconvincing, weak, dubious|词性:形容词
convince:/kənˈvɪns/| vt.|使确信；使信服|To persuade someone to believe something or to do something.|I managed to convince them of the truth. 我设法使他们相信了真相。|近义词：persuade, assure, satisfy|反义词：dissuade, deter|词性:动词

词源:
From Late Middle English: from Old French convainquant, present participle of convaincre, from Latin convincere ‘prove wrong, overcome’, from con- ‘together’ + vincere ‘conquer’.
