kopkin:/ˈkɒpkɪn/ (British) /ˈkɑːpkɪn/ (American)| n. |(俚语，可能带有贬义) 小孩，家伙，小子| A somewhat derogatory or playful term for a child or young person, similar to "kid" or "brat".  Its origin is uncertain, possibly Yiddish. |Example:  "Look at that kopkin showing off on his bike!" 瞧那小子在自行车上炫耀！|Synonym: kid, brat, urchin, youngster, child |Antonym: adult, grown-up |Etymology: Unknown, possibly from Yiddish. |Part of Speech: Noun
