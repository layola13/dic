cosies: /ˈkəʊziz/ (British English), /ˈkoʊziz/ (American English)
| n. |舒适的地方；保暖衣物（尤指小孩的）；茶壶套|A small and comfortable place; warm coverings, especially for children; a tea cosy.|We spent a cosy evening by the fire. 我们在火炉边度过了一个舒适的夜晚。|Synonyms: snug, comfy, warm|Antonyms: uncomfortable, cold|Noun
cosies: /ˈkəʊziz/ (British English), /ˈkoʊziz/ (American English)
| v. |使舒适；使温暖|To make comfortable and warm.|She cosied up to him on the sofa. 她依偎在他身旁，在沙发上感到很舒服。|Synonyms: snuggle, cuddle|Antonyms: expose, chill|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Scottish word "cosy," meaning sheltered or snug, ultimately of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to the Old Norse "kosa," meaning "to choose," suggesting a chosen and therefore comfortable place.
