hydatid:/ˈhaɪdəˌtɪd/| n.|包蚴，棘球蚴，多房棘球蚴|A cystic larval stage of a tapeworm, typically found in the liver or lungs of domesticated animals, and sometimes in humans.  A hydatid cyst can grow slowly over many years and can cause various health problems if left untreated.|The surgeon removed the hydatid cyst from the patient's liver.|Synonyms: cyst, bladderworm|Antonyms: None|Noun
