intempestively: /ˌɪntɛmˈpɛstɪvli/
adverb
不合时宜地，鲁莽地
Occurring at an inappropriate or inopportune time; untimely. Done in a rash or impulsive way.
He chose an intempestively moment to announce his resignation. 他选择了一个不合时宜的时刻宣布辞职。
She intempestively blurted out the secret. 她鲁莽地说出了秘密。
近义词: untimely, inappropriately, rashly, impulsively
反义词: timely, appropriately, cautiously, deliberately
词性: adverb

词源: From Late Latin *intempestivus, from Latin in- "not" + tempestivus "timely, seasonable," from tempestas "time, season, weather," related to tempus "time."
