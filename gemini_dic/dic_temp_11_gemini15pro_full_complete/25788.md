bureaucrat: /ˈbjʊərəkræt/ (US), /ˈbjʊərəkræt/ (UK)| n. | 官僚; 官僚主义者; | An official in a government department, especially one perceived as being concerned with procedural correctness at the expense of people's needs. |  The bureaucrats insisted on following every rule to the letter.  官僚们坚持要严格遵守每一条规则。| Synonyms: official, administrator, civil servant, functionary | Antonyms: nonconformist, maverick | Noun


bureaucratic: /ˌbjʊərəˈkrætɪk/ (US), /ˌbjʊərəˈkrætɪk/ (UK)| adj. | 官僚主义的; | Relating to bureaucracy or bureaucrats; excessively concerned with procedure at the expense of efficiency or common sense. | The bureaucratic process was slow and frustrating. 官僚主义的程序缓慢而令人沮丧。| Synonyms: administrative, official, governmental, procedural | Antonyms: efficient, streamlined, flexible | Adjective
