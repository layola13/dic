misconducted:/ˌmɪskənˈdʌktɪd/
verb, adjective
行为不端的，处理不当的
behaved badly; managed badly
例句：The investigation found that the police officer had misconducted himself. 调查发现这名警官行为不端。
例句: The company's finances were misconducted, leading to bankruptcy. 公司的财务管理不善，导致破产。
近义词：mismanaged, mishandled, improper, inappropriate
反义词：well-behaved, properly managed, appropriate
词性:  past participle of  misconduct


misconduct:/ˌmɪskənˈdʌkt/
noun, verb
不端行为，处理不当
bad behaviour; the management of something badly
例句：His misconduct led to his dismissal from the company. 他的不端行为导致他被公司解雇。
例句：The lawyer was accused of misconduct in the courtroom.  这名律师被指控在法庭上行为不当。
近义词：mismanagement, wrongdoing, impropriety
反义词：good conduct, proper management, propriety
词性: noun, verb

词源:
From mis- (wrongly) + conduct (to behave or manage).  Ultimately from Latin "mis-" (wrongly) and "conducere" (to lead together).
