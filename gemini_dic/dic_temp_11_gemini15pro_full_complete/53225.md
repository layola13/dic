directionlessnesses:/ˌdɪˈrɛkʃənləsnɪsɪz/| n. | 无方向感；漫无目的；迷茫 | The state of lacking direction or purpose. |  He felt a deep sense of directionlessness after losing his job.  他失业后感到深深的迷茫。| 近义词：aimlessness, purposelessness, lostness | 反义词：purposefulness, direction, focus | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From directionless + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing state or quality). Directionless from direction + -less (suffix meaning "without"). Direction from Old French direction, from Latin directionem (nominative directio) "a straightening, a directing," noun of action from past-participle stem of dirigere "to direct, guide, steer, arrange," from dis- "apart" (see dis-) + regere "to lead straight, guide, rule" (from PIE root *reg- "move in a straight line," with derivatives meaning "to direct in a straight line," thus "to lead, rule").
