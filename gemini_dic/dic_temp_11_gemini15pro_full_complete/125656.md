mummy:/ˈmʌmi/ (UK) /ˈmɑːmi/ (US)| n.|木乃伊；妈咪|A body embalmed or dried for burial, especially one of ancient Egypt.  Informally, mother.|例句：The museum displayed a collection of Egyptian mummies.  博物馆展出了一批埃及木乃伊。

例句：He called out for his mummy when he fell down. 他摔倒时喊着要妈妈。|近义词：mom, mother (informal), preserved body|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


mummy:/ˈmʌmi/ (UK) /ˈmɑːmi/ (US)| vt.|将…做成木乃伊；溺爱|To embalm or dry (a body) as a mummy. To treat with excessive care or protectiveness.|例句：The ancient Egyptians mummified their pharaohs. 古埃及人把他们的法老做成木乃伊。

例句：She tends to mummy her youngest child. 她倾向于溺爱她最小的孩子。|近义词：embalm, coddle, pamper|反义词: neglect|词性: verb
