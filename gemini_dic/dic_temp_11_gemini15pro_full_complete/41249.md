corbel:/ˈkɔːrbl/ (US), /ˈkɔːbəl/ (UK)| n. v. | 悬臂梁，托臂；(v.) 用悬臂梁支撑 | A corbel is a structural piece of stone, wood, or brick jutting from a wall to carry a superincumbent weight, a type of bracket. (n.) To furnish with corbels. (v.) | The gargoyle perched atop the stone corbel. 石像鬼栖息在石制托臂上。|  The arch was corbelled using carefully shaped stones.  拱门是用精心塑造的石头做成悬臂状的。 | 近义词：bracket, support, console | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun, verb

Etymology: From Old French *corbel (modern French corbeau "raven"), from its beak-like shape. Ultimately from Late Latin corvellus, diminutive of corvus "raven".
