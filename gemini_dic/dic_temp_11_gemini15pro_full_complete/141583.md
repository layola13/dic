oppressive:/əˈprɛsɪv/ | adj. | 压迫的；压抑的；沉重的 | Burdensome, unjustly harsh, or tyrannical. Causing discomfort by being excessive, intense, or stuffy. |  The oppressive heat made it difficult to work.  酷热的天气使工作难以进行。

The atmosphere in the office was oppressive. 办公室里的气氛很压抑。| 近义词: tyrannical, harsh, cruel, brutal, unjust, burdensome, stifling, suffocating, heavy | 反义词: liberating, freeing, gentle, mild, light | 词性: adjective

oppress:/əˈprɛs/ | vt. | 压迫；压制；使烦恼 |  To keep down by unjust use of force or authority : govern or rule cruelly or unjustly. To cause to feel worried or unhappy. | The dictator oppressed the people.  独裁者压迫人民。

The thought of the upcoming exam oppressed him. 即将到来的考试让他感到焦虑。| 近义词: subjugate, suppress, repress, tyrannize | 反义词: liberate, free, empower | 词性: verb

oppression:/əˈprɛʃən/ | n. | 压迫；压制；压抑；沉闷 | The exercise of authority or power in a burdensome, cruel, or unjust manner.  A sense of being weighed down physically or mentally : depression. |  The people lived under oppression for many years. 人民在压迫下生活了很多年。

The oppression of the humid weather was unbearable. 闷热的天气让人难以忍受。| 近义词: tyranny, cruelty, brutality, hardship, suppression, repression | 反义词: liberation, freedom, empowerment | 词性: noun


词源: From Late Latin *oppress-, from Latin oppressus, past participle of opprimere “to press against, suppress,” from ob- "against" + premere "to press."
