floodplain:/ˈflʌdpleɪn/| n. |泛滥平原；洪泛区|An area of low-lying ground adjacent to a river, formed mainly of river sediments and subject to flooding.|The houses on the floodplain were evacuated before the hurricane. 泛滥平原上的房屋在飓风来临前被疏散了。|近义词：alluvial plain, bottomland, valley floor|反义词: upland, highland|词性:noun

Etymology: From *flood* + *plain*.  *Flood* comes from Old English *flōd*, meaning "a flowing, stream, flood, mass of water". *Plain* comes from Old French *plain*, from Latin *planus*, meaning "flat, level, even, plain".
