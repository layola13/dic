meddlesomenesses:/ˌmɛdlˈsʌmnɪsɪz/
|n.|爱管闲事，多管闲事|The state of being meddlesome; interference in other people's affairs.|Her meddlesomenesses finally drove her neighbors to move.|近义词: interference, intrusion, meddling|反义词: noninterference, respect, aloofness|词性: Noun

Etymology:
From meddlesomeness, from meddlesome + -ness.  Meddlesome comes from Middle English medelsome, from medel (“to mix, mingle”) + -some.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *mēd (“reward, payment”).
