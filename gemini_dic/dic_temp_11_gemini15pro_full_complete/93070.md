idolizer:/ˈaɪdəˌlaɪzər/| n.|崇拜者，偶像化者|Someone who idolizes another person; an adoring fan.|She's such an idolizer of that movie star, she has posters all over her room. 她非常崇拜那位电影明星，她的房间里贴满了海报。|Synonyms: admirer, devotee, fan, worshipper|Antonyms: critic, detractor|noun

idolize:/ˈaɪdəˌlaɪz/| v.|崇拜，偶像化|To admire, revere, or love someone or something to an excessive degree.|He idolizes his older brother. 他崇拜他的哥哥。|Synonyms: admire, adore, worship, revere|Antonyms: despise, loathe, criticize|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From idol + -ize.  "Idol" comes from the Old French "idole,"  ultimately from the Greek "eidōlon" meaning "image" or "phantom."  The suffix "-ize" forms verbs meaning "to make into" or "to cause to be."
