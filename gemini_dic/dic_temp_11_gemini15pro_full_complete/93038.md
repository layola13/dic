idlers:/ˈaɪdlərz/| n.|闲人，懒汉|People who are idle; those who do not work or are not active. |The factory owner was frustrated with the idlers who stood around all day. 工厂老板对整天无所事事的闲人们感到沮丧。|
idlers:/ˈaɪdlərz/| n.|闲人，懒汉|Those who avoid work or responsibility. |The park was full of idlers on a sunny afternoon.  阳光明媚的下午，公园里到处都是闲人。|
idlers:/ˈaɪdlərz/| n.|闲置的部件，惰轮|A mechanical part that does not transmit power but serves to guide or support other moving parts. |The idler pulley in the engine needed to be replaced.  发动机中的惰轮需要更换。|
idlers:/ˈaɪdlərz/| n.|闲置的部件，惰轮|A gear wheel that meshes with other gears to change speed or direction of motion, often without itself transmitting power.|The conveyor belt's idlers were worn out from constant use.  传送带的惰轮因长期使用而磨损了。|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *idler, from idle + -er.  Idle itself comes from Old English īdel, meaning "empty, vain, useless."

SYNONYMS->
loafer, slacker, dawdler, lounger, good-for-nothing, drone

ANTONYMS->
worker, laborer, toiler, busybody, workaholic
