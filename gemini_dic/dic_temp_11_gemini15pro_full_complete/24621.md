broken:/ˈbroʊkən/ (US) /ˈbrəʊkən/ (UK)| adj.|破碎的，坏掉的，断裂的；不完整的；不连贯的；（精神上）崩溃的；（法律、诺言等）被违背的；（机器）失灵的|Something that is broken is damaged and separated into pieces, or no longer works correctly. |The vase was broken into a hundred pieces. 花瓶碎成了一百块。|
synonyms: damaged, fractured, shattered, smashed, destroyed, ruined, faulty, defective, inoperative, out of order
antonyms: intact, whole, undamaged, functional, working, repaired, fixed|adjective


broken:/ˈbroʊkən/ (US) /ˈbrəʊkən/ (UK)| n.|断裂；破裂；骨折|A break or crack in something.|He suffered a broken leg in the accident. 他在事故中腿骨折了。|synonyms: fracture, crack, rupture|antonyms: repair, mend|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *brokyn, *brokun, past participle of breken (“to break”), from Old English brocen, past participle of brecan (“to break”), from Proto-Germanic *brekaną (“to break”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰrégʰ-. Cognate with Scots broken (“broken”), West Frisian brutsen (“broken”), Dutch gebroken (“broken”), German gebrochen (“broken”), Danish brækket (“broken, fractured”).
