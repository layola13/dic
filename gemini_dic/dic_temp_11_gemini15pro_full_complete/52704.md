digresser:/daɪˈɡrɛsər/ /daɪˈɡresər/ | n. | 离题者，跑题者 | Someone who digresses; a person who deviates from the main subject in speech or writing. | The professor was known as a digresser, often veering off on tangents during lectures.  这位教授以跑题而闻名，在讲课时经常偏离主题。| 近义词： rambler, divagator | 反义词:  focuser | 词性:noun

digress:/daɪˈɡrɛs/ /daɪˈɡres/ | v. |  跑题，离题 | To deviate or wander away from the main subject or topic in speaking or writing. | He digressed from the main point of his presentation to tell a personal anecdote. 他偏离了演讲的主题，讲了一个个人轶事。 | 近义词： deviate, stray, wander, ramble | 反义词: focus, stick to the point | 词性:verb

词源: From Latin digressus, past participle of digredi  "to go aside, depart," from di- "aside" + gradi "to walk, step, go".
