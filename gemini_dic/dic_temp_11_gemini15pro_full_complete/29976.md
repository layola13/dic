caviler:/ˈkævɪlər/| n.|吹毛求疵的人；苛责者|A caviler is someone who raises trivial objections or makes petty criticisms.|He's a caviler who finds fault with everything. 他是一个对每件事都吹毛求疵的人。|The architect was known as a caviler, always finding something to criticize in even the most brilliant designs.  这位建筑师以吹毛求疵而闻名，即使是最杰出的设计，他总能找到一些东西来批评。|近义词：critic, nitpicker, faultfinder, carper|反义词: admirer, supporter, praiser|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *caveilen*, from Anglo-Norman *caveler*, from Old North French *caviller* (“to mock, jest, cavil”), from Latin *cavillārī* (“to jest, mock, banter”), frequentative of *cavillī* (“jests, raillery”).
