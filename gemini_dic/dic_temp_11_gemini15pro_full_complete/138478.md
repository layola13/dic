novelty:/ˈnɒvəlti/ (UK) /ˈnɑːvəlti/ (US)| n.|新奇，新奇的事物；新颖小巧而廉价的物品|Something new or unusual.  A small, cheap, unusual object, often given as a present.|例句：The novelty of the new toy soon wore off. 新玩具的新鲜感很快就消失了。|近义词：innovation, originality, freshness|反义词: staleness, banality, oldness|词性:noun
novelty:/ˈnɒvəlti/ (UK) /ˈnɑːvəlti/ (US)| n.|新颖性，独特性|The quality of being new, original, or unusual.|例句：The product's novelty lies in its unique design.  这款产品的新颖性在于其独特的设计。|近义词：uniqueness, originality, unconventionality|反义词: familiarity, conventionality, staleness|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *noveleté, from Late Latin *novellitas, from Latin novellus "new, young," diminutive of novus "new."
