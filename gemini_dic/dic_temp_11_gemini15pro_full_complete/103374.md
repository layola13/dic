jinglier: /ˈdʒɪŋɡliər/ | adj. | 更令人吃惊的；更令人紧张不安的 |  Comparative form of "jingly," meaning characterized by or causing a clinking or tinkling sound.  Often used figuratively to describe a heightened sense of nervousness or anticipation. | The jinglier the music, the more anxious I felt. 音乐越叮当响，我就越感到焦虑。 |  The atmosphere grew jinglier as the deadline approached. 随着截止日期的临近，气氛变得更加紧张。 | 近义词: more nerve-wracking, more unsettling, more tense, edgier | 反义词: calmer, more relaxing, more soothing | 词性: 形容词

词源:  Derived from "jingle" + "-er" (comparative suffix).  "Jingle" originates from the imitative verb "jinglen," meaning "to ring," from the Middle English "ginglen."
