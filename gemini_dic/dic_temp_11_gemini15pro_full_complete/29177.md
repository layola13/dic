cashed-in:/kæʃtˈɪn/
verb
兑现；变卖；利用（机会）；（因伤病）退出比赛
to exchange something such as chips in a casino for money; to sell an investment; to take advantage of an opportunity, often in a way that is seen as unfair; to stop competing in a race or game because of injury or illness
He cashed in his chips after winning a large sum of money at the poker table.  他在扑克桌上赢了一大笔钱后兑现了筹码。

After the stock market crash, many investors cashed in their stocks. 股市崩盘后，许多投资者抛售了他们的股票。

The politician cashed in on the scandal by writing a tell-all book.  这位政客利用这起丑闻写了一本揭秘的书。

The injured marathon runner cashed in after completing 20 miles.  受伤的马拉松运动员在跑了20英里后退出了比赛。

近义词:redeemed, sold, capitalized on, withdrew
反义词:invested, bought, held on, continued
