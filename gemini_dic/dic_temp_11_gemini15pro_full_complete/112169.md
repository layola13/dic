lookdown:/ˈlʊkdaʊn/ (US) /ˈlʊkdaʊn/ (UK)| n.|轻蔑; 傲慢的态度;  (尤指)居高临下的态度; 封锁|A lookdown is an act of looking down on someone or something, often with disdain or superiority. It can also refer to a lockdown, a restriction on movement or activity, especially during an emergency.|He gave me a lookdown, as if I were beneath him. 他轻蔑地看了我一眼，好像我很低贱。|近义词：condescension, disdain, scorn, lockdown (in the sense of restriction)|反义词：respect, admiration|词性:noun


look down:/lʊk daʊn/ (US) /lʊk daʊn/ (UK)| phrasal verb|俯视;  轻视; 贬低; (股票市场)下跌|To look down means to lower one's gaze or to regard someone or something with contempt.  In finance, it can also refer to a decline in market value.|She looked down at the ant crawling on her shoe. 她低头看着爬在她鞋子上的蚂蚁。

He looks down on anyone who hasn't had a university education. 他瞧不起任何没有受过大学教育的人。

The stock market looked down after the news of the recession. 股票市场在经济衰退的消息传出后下跌了。|近义词：despise, disdain, scorn, condescend|反义词：admire, respect, look up to|词性:phrasal verb

词源: "Look" comes from Old English *lōcian "to see, look at," and "down" comes from Old English *dūne "from a higher to a lower place."  The combined meaning developed naturally from the physical act of looking downwards, extending to metaphorical uses of looking down on someone.
