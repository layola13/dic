hotfoot:/ˈhɒtfʊt/ (UK) /ˈhɑːtfʊt/ (US)| v. | 匆匆忙忙地走；快速行动 | to go or leave somewhere quickly, especially in order to escape or avoid something | He hotfooted it out of there as soon as he saw the police. 他一看到警察就匆匆逃离了那里。| Synonyms: hurry, rush, dash, scamper | Antonyms: dawdle, linger | verb

hotfooting:/ˈhɒtfʊtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːtfʊtɪŋ/ (US)| n. | 匆匆忙忙地走；快速行动 | the act of going or leaving somewhere quickly |  His hotfooting it out the back door suggested he had something to hide. 他从后门匆匆溜走 suggests 他有事要隐瞒。 | Synonyms: hurrying, rushing, dashing, scampering | Antonyms: dawdling, lingering | noun
