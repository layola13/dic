counterconvention:/ˌkaʊntər.kənˈvɛnʃən/| n. |反传统，反常规，打破常规(单数)|An action or idea that deliberately opposes or breaks with an established convention or norm.|  His counterconvention of wearing pajamas to the opera raised eyebrows. 他穿着睡衣去歌剧院的这种反常规行为令人侧目。| synonyms: unconventionality, heterodoxy, nonconformity | antonyms: convention, tradition, norm | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"counter-" (prefix): from Old French "contre-" and Latin "contra" meaning "against"
"convention": from Late Middle English "convencioun," from Old French "convention" and Latin "conventio," meaning "meeting, agreement, covenant."
