entrenching: /ɪnˈtrɛntʃɪŋ/ | v. | 巩固；加强；使处于牢固地位；挖壕沟防护 |  The process of firmly establishing something, making it difficult to change.  | The government's policies are entrenching inequality. 政府的政策正在加剧不平等。 |  Reinforcing, strengthening, consolidating, fortifying | Weakening, undermining, eroding | verb

entrenched: /ɪnˈtrɛntʃt/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；牢固的 | Firmly established and difficult to change. |  These attitudes are deeply entrenched in our society. 这些态度在我们的社会中根深蒂固。| ingrained, established, fixed, rooted | superficial, shallow, weak | adjective
