allophone:/ˈæləfoʊn/ (US), /ˈæləfəʊn/ (UK)| n.|音位变体|One of two or more variants of the same phoneme.  An allophone is a phonetic variant of a phoneme that does not change the meaning of a word.  Allophones occur in different phonetic contexts. |The aspirated 'p' in 'pin' and the unaspirated 'p' in 'spin' are allophones of the phoneme /p/.  这两个p音是音位/p/的音位变体。

The dark 'l' in 'pull' and the clear 'l' in 'lip' are allophones of the phoneme /l/. 在pull中l是dark l, 而在lip中则是clear l,二者都是音位/l/的音位变体。|近义词：variant, variation|反义词:phoneme|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From allo- "other" + -phone "sound".  The term was coined by the Polish linguist Jan Baudouin de Courtenay in the late 19th century.
