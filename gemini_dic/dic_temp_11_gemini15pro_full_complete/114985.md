mancus:/ˈmæŋkəs/| adj. n.|有缺陷的，残缺的；曼卡斯（一种中世纪金币）|Defective, mutilated; an Anglo-Saxon gold coin, current in the late eighth and ninth centuries.|The museum displayed a rare mancus from the reign of King Coenwulf.|近义词：deficient, imperfect, flawed, damaged, mutilated (adj.); coin, currency (n.)|反义词：perfect, flawless, intact (adj.)|词性:形容词, 名词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mancus, from Arabic منقوش (manqūsh, “stamped, engraved, inscribed”). Ultimately from the same Semitic root as the Hebrew  נָקַשׁ  (nāqaš, “to strike, to engrave”).
