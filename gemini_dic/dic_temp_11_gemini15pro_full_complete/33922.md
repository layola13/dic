cirques:/ˈsɜːrks/ (US) /ˈsɜːks/ (UK)| n. |冰斗，圈谷|A bowl-shaped hollow eroded by a glacier, typically located at the head of a valley.|The hikers marveled at the sheer cliffs of the cirques.  徒步旅行者对冰斗陡峭的悬崖感到惊叹。|The valley was dotted with several cirques formed during the last ice age.  山谷中点缀着几个在上一个冰河时代形成的冰斗。|Synonyms: corries, cwms|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, could consider 'arete' which is a sharp ridge between cirques)|Noun
