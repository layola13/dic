by-hook-or-by-crook:/baɪ hʊk ɔːr baɪ krʊk/ | idiom | 不择手段；千方百计 |  This idiom means to achieve something by any means necessary, whether those means are honest or dishonest. It implies a determination to succeed regardless of the methods used. | He was determined to win the election by hook or by crook. 他决心不择手段赢得选举。 | synonyms: by any means necessary, at any cost, no matter what | antonyms: honestly, ethically, fairly | adverbial phrase
