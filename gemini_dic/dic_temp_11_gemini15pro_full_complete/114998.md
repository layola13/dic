mandarining:/ˈmændərɪnɪŋ/| n.|用官话讲话;摆官架子|The act of speaking Mandarin Chinese or behaving in an officious or condescending manner, often associated with bureaucratic or elite circles.|He resented their mandarining and refused to be intimidated. 他反感他们摆官架子，拒绝被吓倒。|近义词:officialese, pomposity|反义词:humility, modesty|词性:名词

Etymology: Derived from "Mandarin," originally referring to a high-ranking official in the Chinese empire.  The term later extended to the language spoken by these officials, and eventually to the behavior associated with their social status.
