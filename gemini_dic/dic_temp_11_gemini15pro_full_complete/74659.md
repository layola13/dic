free-wheel:/ˌfriːˈwiːl/ | vi. n. | 空转，随心所欲; 飞轮 | to move forward in a vehicle without using any power, especially by going downhill; to act without concern for rules or the possible results | Example: The cyclist free-wheeled down the hill. 骑自行车的人沿着山坡滑行。| Synonym: coast, drift | Antonym: pedal, drive | Verb

free-wheel:/ˌfriːˈwiːl/ | vi. n. | 自由行动，不受约束; 飞轮 | to do what one wants without worrying about rules or the possible bad results | Example: The children were left to free-wheel during the summer holidays. 孩子们在暑假期间可以自由活动。| Synonym:  roam, wander | Antonym: restrict, control | Verb

free-wheel:/ˌfriːˈwiːl/ | n. | 飞轮 | a set of teeth on the rear wheel of a bicycle or other vehicle that allows the wheel to turn freely without turning the pedals or other driving mechanism  | Example: The free-wheel on my bike needs to be replaced. 我自行车上的飞轮需要更换。| Synonym: ratchet wheel | Antonym: fixed wheel | Noun
