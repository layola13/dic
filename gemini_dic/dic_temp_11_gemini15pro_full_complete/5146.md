alleluia's:/ˌælɪˈluːjəz/
noun
赞美诗，哈利路亚
A plural form of "alleluia," an exclamation of praise and joy, especially in religious contexts.
The choir sang powerful alleluias during the Easter service.  唱诗班在复活节仪式上唱着强有力的赞美诗。
The air was filled with the joyous alleluias of the congregation.  空气中充满了会众欢乐的赞美诗。
Synonyms: praises, hallelujahs
Antonyms: lamentations, complaints
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *alleluia, from Greek ἀλληλούϊα (allēlouia), from Hebrew הַלְלוּיָהּ (haleluyah), meaning "Praise ye Yah" (Yah being a shortened form of Yahweh, the name of God).
