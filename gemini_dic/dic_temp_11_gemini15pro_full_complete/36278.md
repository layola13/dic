cohabits:/koʊˈhæbɪts/ | v. |同居，姘居|To live together in the same dwelling place, especially as a couple, without being married. |例句：They cohabited for five years before getting married. 他们在结婚前同居了五年。

例句：Many young couples choose to cohabit before marriage these days. 现在很多年轻情侣选择在婚前同居。

近义词：live together, shack up |反义词: live apart, separate |词性: verb

cohabiting: /koʊˈhæbɪtɪŋ/ | adj. |同居的|Living together as an unmarried couple. |例句：A cohabiting couple may face legal challenges regarding property ownership.  同居伴侣可能面临财产所有权方面的法律挑战。


例句：The study focused on the financial habits of cohabiting couples. 这项研究的重点是同居伴侣的财务习惯。

近义词：living together, cohabitual |反义词: married, single |词性: adjective

cohabitation: /koʊˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən/ | n. |同居，姘居|The state of living together as an unmarried couple. |例句：The increasing rate of cohabitation is a significant social trend.  同居率的上升是一个重要的社会趋势。

例句：Cohabitation has become a more accepted lifestyle choice in recent years. 近年来，同居已成为一种更被接受的生活方式选择。

近义词：living together |反义词: marriage, separation |词性: noun
