interventions:/ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃənz/| n. |干预，介入，调停|The act of intervening, especially to prevent something from happening or to alter the course of events. |例句：Early interventions can prevent problems from escalating. 早期干预可以防止问题升级。

例句：Military intervention was necessary to protect civilians. 军事干预对于保护平民是必要的。

近义词：interference, mediation, involvement, intrusion|反义词:noninterference, inaction|词性:名词

词源: From Late Latin *interventionem, accusative of *interventio (“a coming between”), from Latin intervenire (“to come between, interrupt”), from inter- (“between”) + venire (“to come”).
