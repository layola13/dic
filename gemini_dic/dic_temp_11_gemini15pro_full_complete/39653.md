conservatisms:/kənˈsɜːrvətɪzəmz/
|n.|保守主义，保守思想|A set of political beliefs that favors tradition and gradual change.  The belief that society should be based on traditional values and that changes should be made gradually.|  Many different conservatisms are present in modern politics.|Synonyms: traditionalism, orthodoxy|Antonyms: radicalism, progressivism|noun


conservatism:/kənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/
|n.|保守主义，保守思想|The holding of political views that favor free enterprise, private ownership, and socially traditional ideas.|His conservatism is reflected in his voting record.|Synonyms: traditionalism, right-wing politics|Antonyms: liberalism, radicalism|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *conservatisme*, from *conserver* "to preserve," from Latin *conservare*  "to keep, preserve," from *com-* "together" + *servare* "to keep, guard."
