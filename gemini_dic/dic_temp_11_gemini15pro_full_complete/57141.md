dovetails:/ˈdʌvˌteɪlz/ (US), /ˈdʌvteɪlz/ (UK)| n. v. |燕尾榫; 吻合; 咬合 |A type of joint used in woodworking where two pieces of wood are interlocked by wedge-shaped projections (tails) and recesses (pins).  Also used figuratively to describe things that fit together neatly and precisely. |The carpenter skillfully dovetailed the drawers of the dresser. 木匠巧妙地用燕尾榫连接了梳妆台的抽屉。|synonyms: interlocks, meshes, coincides, corresponds| antonyms: clashes, conflicts, diverges| noun

dovetails:/ˈdʌvˌteɪlz/ (US), /ˈdʌvteɪlz/ (UK)| v. |用燕尾榫连接; (使)吻合; (使)咬合 |To join or fit together using a dovetail joint or in a way that resembles a dovetail joint; to cause to fit together neatly and precisely. |Her research dovetails perfectly with our existing project. 她的研究与我们现有的项目完美契合。|synonyms: interlock, mesh, coincide, correspond| antonyms: clash, conflict, diverge| verb
