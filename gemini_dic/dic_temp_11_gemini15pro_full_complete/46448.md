daguerreotyper:/dəˈɡɛrəˌtaɪpər/ | n. | 达盖尔照相师，银版照相师 | A person who takes daguerreotypes (photographs made by an early photographic process). | Louis Daguerre himself was a skilled daguerreotyper, capturing stunning images of Parisian streets. 路易·达盖尔本人就是一位技艺精湛的银版照相师，他拍摄了令人惊叹的巴黎街道图像。 |  The daguerreotyper carefully polished the silver plate before exposing it to light.  这位银版照相师在曝光之前仔细地抛光了银板。 | photographer, camera operator |  None | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Daguerreotype + -er.  Daguerreotype is named after Louis-Jacques-Mandé Daguerre, the French artist and inventor who developed the process in the early 19th century.  The -er suffix denotes a person who performs an action or works with something.
