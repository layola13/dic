blacklisters:/ˈblækˌlɪstərz/
n.
加入黑名单的人;将…列入黑名单的人
People who put others on a blacklist.  Those who blacklist others.
The blacklisters have considerable power within the industry.  这些把别人列入黑名单的人在业内拥有相当大的权力。
Industry blacklisters can ruin careers. 行业黑名单制定者可以毁掉职业生涯。

近义词:banners, excluders
反义词:approvers, includers
词性:名词(plural of blacklister)


blacklist:/ˈblækˌlɪst/
n.
黑名单
A list of people or things that are disapproved of and should be avoided.
He was put on a blacklist for his political activities.  由于他的政治活动，他被列入了黑名单。
The company maintains a blacklist of suppliers known for unethical practices.  该公司维护着一份因不道德行为而臭名昭著的供应商黑名单。
vt.
将…列入黑名单
To put someone or something on a blacklist.
The government blacklisted several companies for violating sanctions. 政府将几家违反制裁的公司列入黑名单。
They blacklisted him from the club for his disruptive behavior.  他们因为他扰乱秩序的行为而把他列入了俱乐部的黑名单。

近义词:ban, bar, exclude
反义词:approve, include, whitelist
词性:名词,动词


词源:
"Blacklist" dates back to the 17th century.  "Black" has long been associated with negativity or evil.  A "list" is simply a record.  Therefore, a "blacklist" is a list of those considered undesirable or dangerous.
