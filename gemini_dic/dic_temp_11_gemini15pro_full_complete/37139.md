come-on:/ˈkʌmɒn/ (UK), /ˈkʌmɑːn/ (US)| n. int.| 诱惑；挑逗；劝诱；邀请；花言巧语；吸引人的特色；(比赛)开始信号|An enticing or alluring quality or feature; an invitation, especially one that is considered insincere or deceptive; a signal to begin a contest or performance.| "The low price was a come-on to get customers into the store."  低价是吸引顾客进店的诱饵。|"Come on, let's go to the movies." 来吧，我们去看电影。|Synonyms: enticement, lure, inducement, invitation, plea|Antonyms: discouragement, deterrent|noun

come-on:/ˈkʌmɒn/ (UK), /ˈkʌmɑːn/ (US)| int.| 加油；快点；得了吧；算了吧|Used to express encouragement, disbelief, or impatience.| "Come on, you can do it!" 加油，你能做到！|"Oh, come on, that's not true!" 哦，得了吧，那不是真的！|Synonyms: hurry up, please, really, get a move on|Antonyms: stop, wait, slow down|interjection
