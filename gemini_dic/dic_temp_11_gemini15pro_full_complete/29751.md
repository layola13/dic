cator:/ˈkeɪtər/| n.| 投合者，迎合者 | someone who provides food and drinks for an event, especially a large or special one | The caterers did a fantastic job with the wedding reception.  婚宴承办商把婚礼招待会办得非常出色。| synonyms: purveyor, supplier, provisioner | antonyms: consumer, client, recipient | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cāptātor*, frequentative of *cāpere* "to take, seize, hold, grasp".  The original meaning was "one who seizes or takes hold of something."  Over time, it evolved to specifically mean one who "takes hold of" the opportunity to provide services, particularly food and drink, for others.
