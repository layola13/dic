equivocator:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪtər/ | n. | 模棱两可的人，含糊其辞的人 | Someone who uses ambiguous language to conceal the truth or avoid committing themselves. |  The politician was an equivocator, never giving a straight answer.  这位政客是个含糊其辞的人，从不给出直接的答案。| Synonyms: prevaricator, deceiver, liar | Antonyms: truth-teller, honest person | Noun

equivocate:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪt/ | v. |  含糊其辞，支吾其词 | To use ambiguous language so as to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself. | He equivocated when asked about his involvement in the scandal. 当被问及他与丑闻的关系时，他含糊其辞。 | Synonyms: prevaricate, fudge, hedge | Antonyms: be forthright, be honest | Verb

equivocation:/ɪˌkwɪvəˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |  含糊其辞，模棱两可的话 | The use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself. | Her equivocation on the subject made it difficult to understand her true position.  她在这个问题上的含糊其辞使得人们难以理解她的真实立场。 | Synonyms: ambiguity, prevarication, evasion | Antonyms: clarity, directness, honesty | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *aequivocator, from Latin aequivocus (“ambiguous”), from aequi- (“equally”) + vocare (“to call”).
