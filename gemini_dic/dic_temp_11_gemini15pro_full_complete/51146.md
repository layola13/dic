despondency:/dɪˈspɒndənsi/ /dɪˈspɑːndənsi/| n. |沮丧，灰心，意气消沉|A state of low spirits caused by loss of hope or courage.|例句：He sank into despondency after failing the exam.  他考试不及格后陷入了沮丧。

例句：Her despondency was evident in her slumped posture and quiet voice. 她的沮丧从她颓丧的姿势和低沉的声音中显而易见。|近义词：dejection, despair, hopelessness, gloom, melancholy|反义词: hope, optimism, cheerfulness, joy|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *despondentia, from Latin despondēre (“to lose heart, give up, despair”), from de- (“down, away”) + spondere (“to promise, engage oneself”).
