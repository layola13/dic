hypnotism:/ˈhɪpnətɪzəm/ | n. |催眠；催眠术；催眠状态|The induction of a state of consciousness in which a person apparently loses the power of voluntary action and is highly responsive to suggestion or direction. Its use in therapy, typically to recover suppressed memories or to allow modification of behavior by suggestion, has been revived but is still controversial.|He used hypnotism to help her quit smoking. 他用催眠术帮助她戒烟。|Synonyms: mesmerism, hypnosis|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, consider 'wakefulness', 'consciousness')|noun

hypnotize:/ˈhɪpnətaɪz/ | vt. |催眠；使着迷|To produce a state of hypnosis in (someone). To capture the whole attention of (someone); fascinate.|The magician hypnotized the audience with his tricks. 魔术师用他的戏法迷住了观众。|Synonyms: mesmerize, entrance, captivate|Antonyms: disenchant, bore|verb
