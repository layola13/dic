creatureliness:/ˈkriːtʃərlɪnɪs/
n.
生物性；受造物性；有限性
The state or quality of being a creature; dependence on God or on nature; mortality and limitations.
His poetry explores themes of creatureliness, mortality, and the human condition. 他的诗歌探索了生物性、死亡率和人类生存状态的主题。

近义词：mortality, finitude, physicality, vulnerability
反义词：divinity, immortality, transcendence
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From creature + -liness.  "Creature" comes from Old French "creature"  (Modern French "créature"), and ultimately from Latin "creatura"  "thing created," from "creare" "to create." "-liness" is a suffix forming abstract nouns of quality or state, from Old English "-licnes," related to "-lice" (see -ly (1)).
