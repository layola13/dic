escheator:/ɪˈʃiːətər/| n.|遗产管理人；（旧时）负责调查并没收无合法继承人遗产的官员|An escheator was a medieval officer responsible for finding and managing property that had reverted to the crown.|The escheator was responsible for managing the deceased nobleman's estate.|Synonyms: administrator, executor|Antonyms: heir, beneficiary|noun

escheat:/ɪsˈtʃiːt/| n. v.|（财产）归还；充公；|n. The escheat of the property to the state occurred because there were no heirs.|v.  The land escheated to the crown.|Synonyms: forfeit, confiscate (v); reversion, forfeiture (n)|Antonyms: inherit, acquire (v)|noun, verb
