cryopreserved:/ˌkraɪoʊprɪˈzɜːrvd/ | adj. | 冷冻保存的 | Preserved by freezing at very low temperatures, typically for storage or future use. | The cryopreserved embryos were thawed and implanted.  冷冻保存的胚胎被解冻并植入。|  The hospital cryopreserved the patient's stem cells. 医院冷冻保存了病人的干细胞。 | 近义词：frozen, refrigerated | 反义词：thawed, unfrozen | 词性: 形容词

cryopreserve:/ˌkraɪoʊprɪˈzɜːrv/ | vt. | 冷冻保存 | To preserve (biological tissue, cells, etc.) by freezing at very low temperatures. |  Scientists can cryopreserve human eggs and sperm. 科学家可以冷冻保存人类的卵子和精子。 | The lab cryopreserved the tissue samples for future research.  实验室冷冻保存了组织样本以供未来研究。 | 近义词：freeze, deep-freeze | 反义词：thaw | 词性: 动词

cryopreservation:/ˌkraɪoʊˌprɛzərˈveɪʃən/ | n. | 冷冻保存 | The process of preserving something by freezing it at very low temperatures. | Cryopreservation allows for the long-term storage of biological materials. 冷冻保存可以长期储存生物材料。 | The cryopreservation of organs is a complex process. 器官的冷冻保存是一个复杂的过程。| 近义词：freezing, deep-freezing | 反义词：thawing | 词性: 名词



词源: cryo- (from Greek kryos, meaning "frost" or "icy cold") + preserve
