nonforbearing:/ˌnɒnfɔːrˈbeərɪŋ/
adjective
不忍耐的，不宽容的
Unwilling to tolerate or show patience; unforgiving or relentless.
He was known for his nonforbearing attitude towards any mistakes, no matter how small. 他以对任何错误都不宽容的态度而闻名，无论多么小。
The nonforbearing teacher punished the student severely for being late.  不宽容的老师严厉地惩罚了迟到的学生。
近义词:impatient, intolerant, unforgiving, unmerciful, relentless
反义词:forbearing, patient, tolerant, forgiving, merciful
词性:adjective

词源:
From non- + forbearing.  Forbearing itself comes from the verb 'forbear,' meaning to refrain from or abstain from something. 'Forbear' has roots in Old English 'forberan,' meaning "to endure patiently."  The prefix "non-" simply negates the meaning.
