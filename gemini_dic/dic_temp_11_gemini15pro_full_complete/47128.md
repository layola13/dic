dauphinate:/ˈdɔːfɪnɪt/ | n. |法国皇太子之位；皇太子领地| The dauphinate was a powerful position in pre-revolutionary France.  法国大革命前，皇太子之位拥有很大的权力。|Near synonyms:  princedom, heirship | Antonyms: N/A | Noun

dauphinate:/ˈdɔːfɪnɪt/ | vt. |立…为皇太子；授予…皇太子之位|He was dauphinated at a young age. 他在很小的时候就被立为皇太子了。|Near synonyms:  invest, appoint | Antonyms:  dethrone, depose | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *dauphiné*, past participle of *dauphiner*, "to make dauphin," from *dauphin*, "dauphin" (heir apparent to the French throne).  Ultimately from Latin *delphinus*, "dolphin," via a heraldic dolphin emblem.
