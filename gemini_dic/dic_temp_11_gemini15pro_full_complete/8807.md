antienthusiastically:/ˌæntiɪnˌθuːziˈæstɪkli/
| adv. | 缺乏热情地，冷淡地 | Showing a lack of enthusiasm; without excitement or interest. | He antienthusiastically agreed to go to the party. 他很不情愿地同意去参加聚会。 |  apathetically, halfheartedly, unenthusiastically | enthusiastically, eagerly | adverb

Etymology:
anti- (prefix) + enthusiastic + -ally (suffix)

"anti-" is a prefix of Greek origin meaning "against," "opposite of," "opposed to."
"enthusiastic" comes from the Greek "enthousiasmos," meaning "inspiration, divine possession," from "enthousiastes," meaning "inspired."
"-ally" is a suffix used to form adverbs from adjectives.
