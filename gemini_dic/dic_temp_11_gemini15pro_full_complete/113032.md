luminary: /ˈluːmɪnəri/ (US); /ˈljuːmɪnəri/ (UK) | n. |  杰出人物， luminary, something that gives light, especially in the sky, such as the sun or moon.  A person who inspires or influences others, especially one prominent in a particular sphere.|  例句：Marie Curie was a luminary in the field of science.  玛丽·居里是科学界的杰出人物。

例句：He is a luminary in the world of finance. 他是金融界的杰出人物。| 近义词：star, leader, expert, notable, pundit | 反义词：nobody, unknown | 词性：名词

luminary: /ˈluːmɪnəri/ (US); /ˈljuːmɪnəri/ (UK) | adj. |  发光的，明亮的, of or relating to a luminary or luminaries; shining; bright.|  例句：The luminary stars illuminated the night sky. 闪耀的星星照亮了夜空。

例句：The luminary glow of the moon cast long shadows. 月亮明亮的光芒投下长长的影子。| 近义词：bright, shining, radiant, luminous | 反义词：dim, dark | 词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: via Old French luminaire or medieval Latin luminare, from Latin luminare ‘to light’, from lumen, lumin- ‘light’.
