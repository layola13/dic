ogam:/ˈoʊɡəm/ |n.| 欧甘字母；欧甘文|An ancient alphabet used in Ireland and Britain, consisting of strokes and notches cut on the edges of a stone or piece of wood. |The inscription on the standing stone was written in ogam.  这块立石上的铭文是用欧甘文写成的。| synonym: early Irish alphabet | antonym: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Irish *ogam, perhaps ultimately from the name of Ogma, the Celtic god of eloquence.
