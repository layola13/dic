aphorismatic:/ˌæfərɪˈzmætɪk/ | adj. | 格言式的；警句般的 | characterized by the use of aphorisms, or concise, memorable sayings expressing a general truth or principle. | His writing style is known for its aphorismatic wit. 他的写作风格以其格言式的智慧而闻名。|  Example:  The professor's lectures were filled with aphorismatic wisdom. 教授的讲座充满了格言式的智慧。| Synonyms: pithy, concise, epigrammatic, sententious | Antonyms: verbose, prolix, rambling | adjective

aphorism: /ˈæfərɪzəm/ | n. | 格言；警句 | a concise statement of a principle or precept. | "Power tends to corrupt, and absolute power corrupts absolutely" is a famous aphorism. “权力导致腐败，绝对的权力导致绝对的腐败”是一句著名的格言。|  Example:  He peppered his speech with well-known aphorisms. 他的演讲中穿插着许多著名的格言。 | Synonyms:  maxim, adage, proverb, saw | Antonyms:  essay, treatise, dissertation | noun
