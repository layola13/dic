cantilever: /ˈkæntɪˌlivər/ (US), /ˈkæntɪˌliːvər/ (UK) | n. | 悬臂，悬梁 | A beam or structure that is fixed at one end and projects horizontally, supporting a load at the free end. | The balcony is a cantilever extending out from the building.  阳台是从建筑物延伸出来的悬臂。| synonyms: overhang, projection, jetty | antonyms: support, pier | noun

cantilever: /ˈkæntɪˌlivər/ (US), /ˈkæntɪˌliːvər/ (UK) | v. | 使成悬臂，悬挑 | To construct or support something with a cantilever. | The architect cantilevered the roof over the patio.  建筑师将屋顶悬挑在露台上方。| synonyms: overhang, project, extend | antonyms: support, brace | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the 18th century, probably a blend of cant and lever.  Cant refers to an angled or sloping surface, while lever refers to a rigid bar used to exert pressure or lift.
