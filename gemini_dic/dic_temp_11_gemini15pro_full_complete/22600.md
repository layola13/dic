bootstrapping:/ˈbuːtˌstræpɪŋ/| n.| 自举，引导程序，白手起家|The process of starting something with limited resources, often by using existing resources to generate more resources.  This can apply to starting a business with little capital or to a statistical method that uses resampling.|He built his business from the ground up through bootstrapping, reinvesting early profits to fuel growth. 他通过自举白手起家，将早期利润再投资以促进增长。|synonyms: self-starting, self-financing, cold-starting, seed funding | antonyms: venture capital, external funding| noun


bootstrapped:/ˈbuːtˌstræpt/| v.|  自举启动，以有限资源启动|Past tense of "bootstrap", referring to something that was started with limited resources.|The company bootstrapped its initial operations, relying on personal savings and ingenuity.  这家公司依靠个人储蓄和独创性启动了最初的运营。|synonyms: self-started, self-financed, cold-started | antonyms: venture-funded, externally-funded| verb
