near:/nɪr/ (US) /nɪə(r)/ (UK)| adj. adv. prep.|近的，附近的；临近；|Close in distance, time, or relationship.|例句：The library is near my house. 图书馆离我家很近。|近义词：close, nearby, adjacent|反义词:far, distant|adjective

near:/nɪr/ (US) /nɪə(r)/ (UK)| adv.|接近地，靠近地；|At or to a short distance away; close.|例句：He came near and whispered in my ear. 他走近我，在我耳边低语。|近义词：closely, almost|反义词:far, distantly|adverb

near:/nɪr/ (US) /nɪə(r)/ (UK)| prep.|靠近，接近|Close to; not far from.|例句：The cat sat near the fire. 猫坐在火炉边。|近义词：close to, by, beside|反义词:far from|preposition


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *nēar (attested only in the comparative nēarra "nearer," superlative nīehst "nearest"), from Proto-Germanic *nēhwaz, from Proto-Indo-European *negh- "near." Cognates include Dutch naar "to, towards," German nahe "near," Latin nequeo "I am unable" (literally "I am not near [being able]"), Greek nekhus "dead body" (literally "lying near").
