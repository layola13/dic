fanatic:/fəˈnætɪk/ | n. | 狂热者，入迷者 | A person filled with excessive and single-minded zeal, especially for a cause or an idea. |  He's a football fanatic and watches every game. 他是个足球狂热爱好者，每场比赛都看。

fanatic:/fəˈnætɪk/ | adj. | 狂热的，入迷的 |  Marked by excessive enthusiasm and often intense unreasoning devotion. |  She's fanatic about keeping her house clean. 她对保持房子清洁非常狂热。


fanatics:/fəˈnætɪks/ | n. | 狂热者们，入迷者们 | Plural form of "fanatic," referring to a group of people filled with excessive and single-minded zeal. | The sports fanatics were cheering wildly.  这些体育狂热爱好者们疯狂地欢呼着。


Etymology:
From Late Latin *fanaticus, from Latin fanaticus (“inspired by a deity, frenzied, enthusiastic”), from fanum (“temple”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁- (“to do, to put, to place”).

Synonyms:
zealot, enthusiast, devotee, extremist, maniac

Antonyms:
moderate, apathetic, indifferent, unenthusiastic
