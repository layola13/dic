beat-ups:/ˈbiːtˌʌps/| n.|破旧的东西；破烂的车辆；(尤指)遭到殴打的人|A beat-up is something that is old and damaged, especially a vehicle. It can also refer to a person who has been badly beaten.|My old car is a real beat-up. 我的旧车真是辆破车。|近义词：wreck, clunker, jalopy (for vehicles); battered, bruised (for people)|反义词:new, pristine|词性:名词

beat-ups:/ˈbiːtˌʌps/| n.|殴打；痛打|A beat-up is an act of violence in which someone is badly beaten.|He got a beat-up after school. 放学后他被痛打了一顿。|近义词：beating, thrashing, walloping|反义词:protection, safety|词性:名词


词源:  From the past participle of "beat up," meaning to assault physically.
