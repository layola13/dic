anachronistically:/əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪkli/ | adv. |时代错误地；年代失调地 | In a way that is chronologically misplaced. Placing something in the wrong historical period. | The film anachronistically features a car in a story set in ancient Rome. 这部电影时代错误地在一出以古罗马为背景的故事中出现了一辆汽车。|Synonyms: unseasonably, untimely, inappropriately, incorrectly, erroneously | Antonyms: appropriately, correctly, chronologically | adverb

anachronism:/əˈnækrənɪzəm/ | n. |时代错误；年代误植| Something (such as a word, an object, or an event) that is mistakenly placed in a time where it does not belong in a story, movie, etc. | The author's use of modern slang in a historical novel is an anachronism. 作者在历史小说中使用现代俚语是一个时代错误。| Synonyms: misplacement, misdating, solecism | Antonyms: accuracy, correctness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *anachronismus*, from Ancient Greek ἀναχρονισμός (anachronismós), from ἀνά (aná, “up, against”) + χρόνος (khrónos, “time”) + -ισμός (-ismós).
