karob:/ˈkɛrəb/ | n. | 角豆，角豆树，角豆荚 | A Mediterranean tree (Ceratonia siliqua) of the legume family, having pinnately compound, evergreen leaves and bearing long, flattened, dark brown pods. Also: the edible pod of this tree, containing a sweet pulp and seeds that yield a gum used as a stabilizer and thickener.  |  The carob tree is native to the Mediterranean region. 角豆树原产于地中海地区。| Synonyms: locust bean, St. John's bread | Antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *carobe*, from Arabic *kharūb*, ultimately from Akkadian *kharūbu*.
