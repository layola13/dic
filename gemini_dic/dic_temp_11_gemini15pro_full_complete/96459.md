individualism:/ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlɪzəm/| n.|个人主义|A social theory favoring freedom of action for individuals over collective or state control.  The habit or principle of being independent and self-reliant.|Example:  She prized individualism and valued her independence. 她珍视个人主义，重视她的独立性。

|Example:  The spirit of individualism is deeply ingrained in American culture. 个人主义精神深深植根于美国文化中。|近义词：independence, self-reliance, autonomy|反义词：collectivism, conformity|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *individualisme*, from *individuel* ("individual") + *-isme* ("-ism").  Ultimately from Latin *individuus* ("indivisible").
