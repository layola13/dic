maiden:/ˈmeɪdn/| n. adj.|少女，处女；首次的，初次的|A young unmarried woman, especially a virgin.  Also, being the first of its kind.|例句：The knight rescued the fair maiden. 骑士救出了美丽的少女。|近义词：girl, virgin, inaugural, first|反义词：married woman, experienced|词性:名词，形容词

maiden:/ˈmeɪdn/| adj.|首次的，初次的|Being the first of its kind.|例句：The aircraft's maiden flight was successful.  飞机的首次飞行很成功。|近义词：inaugural, first, initial|反义词：subsequent, later|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mægden (attested as oblique cases mægdne, mægdene), from Proto-Germanic *magadīnaz, from Proto-Indo-European *méh₂-gʰedʰ- (“unmarried”). Cognate with Old Frisian megith, Old Saxon magath, Old High German magad, Old Norse mey (Danish mø), Dutch maagd, German Magd.
