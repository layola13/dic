enarthrodia:/ɛnˌɑːrˈθroʊdiə/ |n.|杵臼关节|A freely moving joint in which a sphere on the head of one bone fits into a rounded cavity in the other bone, allowing movement in almost any direction; a ball-and-socket joint.|The hip joint is an example of an enarthrodia, allowing for a wide range of motion.|Synonym: ball-and-socket joint|Antonym: synarthrosis|Noun
