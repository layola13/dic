beldam:/ˈbeldəm/ | n. |老妇人；丑老太婆；女巫|An old woman, especially one considered to be unpleasant or malicious. |例句：The beldam cackled gleefully as she stirred her potion.  丑老太婆一边搅拌着她的药水，一边高兴地咯咯笑着。

例句：The children were frightened by the stories of the beldam who lived in the woods. 孩子们被住在森林里的丑老太婆的故事吓坏了。|近义词：hag, crone, witch |反义词: young woman, maiden |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *beldam*, from Old French *bel dame* "fine lady," from *bel* "beautiful" + *dame* "lady."  The meaning shifted over time from "grandmother" to "old woman," and eventually acquired negative connotations of ugliness and malevolence.
