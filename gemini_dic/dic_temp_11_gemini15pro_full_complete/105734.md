kipchaks:/ˈkɪptʃæks/| n.|钦察人，钦察人(突厥语族)|A Turkic people who dominated the western part of the Eurasian Steppe in the 11th and 12th centuries.  They formed a powerful confederation and played a significant role in the history of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.|The Kipchaks were nomadic pastoralists. 钦察人是游牧民族。|Kipchak influence can be seen in various languages. 钦察人的影响可以在各种语言中看到。|Synonyms: Cumans, Polovtsians|Antonyms: N/A|noun
