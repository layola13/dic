obstructionism:/əbˈstrʌkʃənɪzəm/
noun
阻挠主义; 故意阻挠
the practice of deliberately preventing something from happening
The senator was accused of obstructionism for blocking the bill.  这位参议员因阻挠该法案而受到指责。
近义词：obstruction, impedance, hindrance
反义词：cooperation, assistance, facilitation
词性: noun

obstruct:/əbˈstrʌkt/
verb
阻碍; 阻塞; 妨碍
to block or prevent something from happening or making progress
The fallen tree obstructed the road.  倒下的树挡住了道路。
近义词：block, impede, hinder
反义词：aid, assist, facilitate
词性: verb

obstructionist:/əbˈstrʌkʃənɪst/
noun, adjective
阻挠主义者; 阻挠的
a person who deliberately obstructs something; relating to or characterized by obstructionism
The obstructionist tactics of the opposition party delayed the passage of the bill. 反对党的阻挠策略延误了法案的通过。
He was labeled an obstructionist by his colleagues.  他被同事们贴上了阻挠者的标签。
近义词：(n.) opponent, antagonist; (adj.) obstructive, hindering
反义词：(n.) supporter, advocate; (adj.) cooperative, helpful
词性: noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Late Latin *obstructionem, from obstruct-, past participle stem of obstruere "to build against, block up," from ob "against, in the way of" + struere "to pile, build."
