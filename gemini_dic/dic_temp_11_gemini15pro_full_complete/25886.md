burlesquing: /bərˈlɛskɪŋ/ | v. | 滑稽模仿；以夸张手法模仿 |  Imitating in a humorous and exaggerated way, often to mock or ridicule. |  He was burlesquing the pompous pronouncements of the politician. 他正在滑稽地模仿那位政客浮夸的声明。|  The comedian burlesqued the president's speech, exaggerating his gestures and voice.  这位喜剧演员滑稽地模仿了总统的演讲，夸大了他的手势和声音。| synonyms: parodying, satirizing, lampooning, mimicking, mocking | antonyms: imitating seriously, respecting, honoring | verb

burlesque: /bərˈlɛsk/ | n. | 滑稽模仿；滑稽剧 | A humorous and exaggerated imitation; a theatrical entertainment of this kind. | The play was a burlesque of a popular melodrama.  这出戏是对一部流行情节剧的滑稽模仿。 | The burlesque show featured comedians and dancers in elaborate costumes. 滑稽表演以穿着华丽服装的喜剧演员和舞者为特色。 | synonyms: parody, satire, lampoon, caricature | antonyms: serious drama, tragedy | noun

词性:
verb/noun

词源:
From French *burlesque*, from Italian *burlesco*, from *burla* "joke, mockery".
