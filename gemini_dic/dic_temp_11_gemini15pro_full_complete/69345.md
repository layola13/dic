federalist:/ˈfɛdərəlɪst/ | n. adj. | 联邦主义者；联邦制的 | A person who advocates or supports federalism (a system of government in which power is divided between a central authority and constituent political units).  Also used as an adjective to describe things related to federalism. |  Example: James Madison was a prominent Federalist.  詹姆斯·麦迪逊是一位著名的联邦主义者。| Example: The Federalist Papers argued in favor of the new Constitution. 《联邦党人文集》支持新宪法。 |Synonyms: unionist, nationalist (as noun), federal (as adjective) | Antonyms: separatist, states' rights advocate, confederal (as adjective) | noun, adjective

Etymology:
Late 18th century: from federal + -ist.
