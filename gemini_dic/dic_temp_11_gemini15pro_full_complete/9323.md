antipoperies:/ænˌtɪˈpoʊpəriːz/
|n. (plural)|反对教皇的言行；反对罗马天主教的言行；|Opposition to the pope or the doctrines of the Roman Catholic Church; things opposed to the pope or the doctrines of the Roman Catholic Church.|The reformer’s writings were full of antipoperies, vehemently criticizing papal authority.|近义词：heresies, blasphemies|反义词：orthodoxies, pieties|词性:名词

Etymology:
From anti- (against) + popery (the doctrines, practices, and rituals of the Roman Catholic Church). Popery itself comes from pope + -ery (a suffix forming nouns denoting a condition, practice, or place associated with the thing specified).
