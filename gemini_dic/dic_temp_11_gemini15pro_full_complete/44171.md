criticizing: /ˈkrɪtɪsaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 批评，批判 | The act of expressing disapproval of someone or something, finding fault with. | He is always criticizing his colleagues' work. 他总是批评他同事的工作。|  She's been criticizing the government's handling of the crisis. 她一直在批评政府对这场危机的处理方式。 |  synonyms: censuring, condemning, denouncing, reprimanding, reproaching | antonyms: praising, commending, lauding, approving | present participle of criticize

criticize: /ˈkrɪtɪsaɪz/ | v. | 批评，批判 |  To express disapproval of someone or something, find fault with. |  The report criticizes the government for its lack of action. 该报告批评政府缺乏行动。| Don't criticize him too harshly; he's still learning. 不要太苛刻地批评他；他还在学习。 | synonyms: censure, condemn, denounce, reprimand, reproach | antonyms: praise, commend, laud, approve | verb

Etymology: From the French critiquer, from Greek kritikos "able to judge," from krinein "to separate, decide."
