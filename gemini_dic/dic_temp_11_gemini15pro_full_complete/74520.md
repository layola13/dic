fraternalisms:/frəˈtɜːrnəlɪzəmz/
n.
兄弟情谊；博爱精神
The spirit of brotherhood and mutual support.  A system of principles and practices based on this spirit.

The fraternalisms of college life are often remembered fondly. 大学生活的兄弟情谊常常令人怀念。
Secret societies are often built on complex systems of fraternalisms. 秘密社团通常建立在复杂的兄弟情谊体系之上。

近义词：brotherhood, camaraderie, fellowship, solidarity
反义词：enmity, hostility, antagonism
词性: 名词(plural)

fraternalism:/frəˈtɜːrnəlɪzəm/
n.
兄弟情谊；博爱精神
The spirit of brotherhood and mutual support. A system of principles and practices based on this spirit.

College fraternities emphasize fraternalism and bonding.  大学兄弟会强调兄弟情谊和团结。
The fraternalism of the firefighters created a strong sense of community. 消防员之间的兄弟情谊创造了强烈的社区意识。

近义词：brotherhood, camaraderie, fellowship, solidarity
反义词：enmity, hostility, antagonism
词性: 名词(singular)


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fraternel* (brotherly) from Latin *fraternus* (brotherly) from *frater* (brother), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\bʰréh₂tēr* (brother). The suffix *-ism* denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice.
