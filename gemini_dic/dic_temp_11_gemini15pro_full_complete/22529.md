boondoggler:/ˈbuːndɑːɡlər/| n.| 浪费公款的人；工作偷懒的人；骗子|Someone who wastes time or money on unnecessary or unproductive activities, especially using public funds.  A person who engages in useless or trivial work, often as a way to avoid real work. | The mayor was accused of being a boondoggler, wasting taxpayer money on frivolous projects. 市长被指控是一个浪费公款的人，把纳税人的钱浪费在无聊的项目上。

The consultant was a boondoggler, charging exorbitant fees for useless advice.  这位顾问是个骗子，对无用的建议收取过高的费用。| 近义词：idler, loafer, shirker, slacker, time-waster, malingerer, fraudster, swindler| 反义词：hard worker, diligent worker|词性: 名词

词源:  From the noun "boondoggle," which originated in the 1930s in the United States, referring to a type of braided leather craft made by Boy Scouts. The meaning later shifted to refer to wasteful or unproductive activity, often involving government funds.
