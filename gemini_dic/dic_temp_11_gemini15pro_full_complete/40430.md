contrariously:/ˌkɒntrəˈrɛərɪəsli/ | adv. | 矛盾地，相反地，反常地 | In a contrary or opposite manner; perversely or contradictorily. | He contrariously decided to go north, even though everyone advised him to go south. 他反常地决定向北走，尽管每个人都建议他向南走。 |  She contrariously refused the offer, despite its obvious advantages. 她反常地拒绝了这个提议，尽管它有明显的优势。| 近义词: perversely, oppositely, conversely, contradictorily | 反义词: agreeably, compliantly, obediently | 词性: 副词

contrary:/ˈkɒntrəri/ | adj. n. | 相反的，对立的；相反，对立面 | Opposite in nature, direction, or meaning; a thing that is the opposite of something else. |  The results were contrary to expectations. 结果与预期相反。 | He acted contrary to his beliefs. 他的行为与他的信仰相悖。 | 近义词: opposite, opposing, conflicting, adverse | 反义词: similar, alike, consistent, agreeable | 词性: 形容词/名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contrarius, from Latin contrā (“against, opposite”).
