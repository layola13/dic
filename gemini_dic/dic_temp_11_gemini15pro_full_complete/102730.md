janissary: /ˈdʒænɪsəri/ | n. | 近卫军士兵，禁卫军士兵 | A member of the Turkish infantry forming the Sultan's guard between the 14th and 19th centuries, noted for their military effectiveness and loyalty until they became a powerful and unruly force. |  The janissaries were once renowned for their discipline and loyalty to the Sultan.  禁卫军士兵曾以纪律严明和对苏丹的忠诚而闻名。

The janissaries' revolt threatened the stability of the Ottoman Empire. 禁卫军的叛乱威胁着奥斯曼帝国的稳定。 | synonym:  soldier, guardsman, trooper | antonym: civilian, rebel | noun
