just-so-much:/ˈdʒʌst soʊ mʌtʃ/ | adj. | 就这么多；恰好这么多；不多不少 |  Used to emphasize that a limited quantity of something is available and no more. Implies a sense of restriction or insufficiency. |  We only have just-so-much time to complete this project. 我们只有这么点时间来完成这个项目。 | synonyms: only this much, a limited amount, a fixed quantity | antonyms: ample, abundant, plentiful | adjective


just-so-much:/ˈdʒʌst soʊ mʌtʃ/ | adv. |  就这么多；不多不少 |  Used to modify a verb or adjective, emphasizing a limited degree or extent.  |  He was just-so-much helpful, offering only minimal assistance. 他只是稍微帮了点忙，只提供了极少的帮助。 | synonyms: to a limited extent, only slightly, barely | antonyms: greatly, excessively, considerably | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
"Just" comes from Old French "juste" meaning "right, proper, exact." "So" comes from Old English "swa" meaning "in this way, thus." "Much" comes from Old English "mycel" meaning "large, great." The combination "just so much" emphasizes a precise and limited quantity.
