calcinate:/ˈkælsɪˌneɪt/ | v. | 煅烧，烧成灰烬 | To heat a substance to a high temperature but below the melting or fusing point, causing loss of moisture, volatile matter, or oxidation.  | Limestone is calcined to produce lime. 石灰石煅烧制成石灰。| synonyms: burn, roast, bake | antonyms: hydrate, moisten | verb

calcinate:/ˈkælsɪˌneɪt/ | n. | 煅烧物 | A product of calcination. | The calcinate was then leached with acid. 煅烧物然后用酸浸出。| synonyms: residue, ash | antonyms: raw material | noun
