fossilization:/ˌfɒsɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ /ˌfɑːsɪləˈzeɪʃən/| n. |化石化，僵化|The process by which organic matter becomes a fossil.  The process of something becoming fixed and rigid, unable to change.|例句：The fossilization of the dinosaur bones took millions of years. 恐龙骨骼的化石化过程耗时数百万年。

例句：The fossilization of traditional teaching methods can hinder educational progress.  传统教学方法的僵化会阻碍教育进步。|近义词：petrification, mineralization|反义词: decay, decomposition|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French fossilisation, from fossiliser “to fossilize,”  from fossile “fossil.”  Ultimately from Latin fossilis “dug up,” from fodere “to dig.”
