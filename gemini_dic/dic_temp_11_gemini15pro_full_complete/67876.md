fabricator:/ˈfæbrɪkeɪtər/| n.| 制造者，捏造者，装配工 | A person who fabricates things, especially metal parts or a story/lie. | The fabricator welded the steel beams together. 制造者将钢梁焊接在一起。| synonyms: manufacturer, producer, inventor, constructor, smith, forger (especially for lies) | antonyms: destroyer, demolisher | noun

fabricate:/ˈfæbrɪkeɪt/| v.| 制造，捏造，装配 | To construct or manufacture something, especially from metal parts or to invent or concoct something, typically a story or a lie. | The company fabricates components for aircraft. 这家公司为飞机制造零件。 He fabricated a story about being robbed. 他编造了一个关于被抢劫的故事。 | synonyms: manufacture, produce, invent, construct, build, forge (especially for lies) | antonyms: destroy, demolish, dismantle | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *fabricātor, from Latin fabricāre (“to construct, build, forge”), from fabrica (“workshop, forge”).
