eccentric:/ɪkˈsentrɪk/ | adj. n.|古怪的，反常的；古怪的人|Deviating from the recognized or customary character, practice, etc.; peculiar; odd;  a person who has an unusual or odd personality.|
例句：He was known for his eccentric behavior, such as wearing a mismatched socks. 他以古怪的行为而闻名，比如穿着不配对的袜子。

例句：She's a bit of an eccentric, but she's a brilliant artist. 她有点古怪，但她是一位才华横溢的艺术家。
|近义词：peculiar, odd, strange, unusual, quirky, bizarre|反义词：normal, conventional, ordinary, usual|词性:形容词,名词

eccentricity:/ˌeksɛnˈtrɪsɪti/ | n.|古怪；反常；偏心距|The quality of being eccentric; peculiarity; oddity; the state or amount of being off-center.|
例句：The eccentricity of her clothing was matched by her unusual hairstyle. 她古怪的服装和她不同寻常的发型相得益彰。

例句：The architect incorporated eccentricities like spiral staircases and asymmetrical windows into the building's design.  这位建筑师在建筑设计中融入了螺旋楼梯和不对称窗户等古怪元素。
|近义词：oddity, peculiarity, strangeness, quirkiness|反义词：normality, conventionality, ordinariness|词性:名词
