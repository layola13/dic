kick-starting:/ˌkɪkˈstɑːtɪŋ/
|v.|重新启动，启动，促进|The government is trying to kick-start the economy. 政府正试图启动经济。|例句：The new advertising campaign is designed to kick-start sales.  新的广告活动旨在促进销售。

例句：They hope the tax cuts will kick-start the economy. 他们希望减税能刺激经济复苏。|近义词：jump-start, boost, revive, stimulate|反义词:halt, stop, hinder|词性:动词(present participle)

kick-start:/ˈkɪkˌstɑːt/
|n.|启动，促进|The project needs a financial kick-start to get it off the ground.  这个项目需要资金启动才能开始。|例句：The government's investment provided the kick-start the industry needed. 政府的投资为这个行业提供了所需的启动资金。

例句：The loan gave her business the kick-start it needed. 这笔贷款给了她的企业所需的启动资金。|近义词：boost, impetus, stimulus|反义词:hindrance, obstacle|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "kick" (meaning to strike with the foot) + "start" (meaning to begin or set in motion).  Originally referred to the act of starting a motorcycle engine by kicking a lever.  Later extended metaphorically to mean stimulating any kind of activity.  源于“kick”（意为用脚踢）+“start”（意为开始或启动）。最初指的是通过踢动杠杆来启动摩托车发动机。后来被比喻地扩展为刺激任何一种活动。
