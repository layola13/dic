mutacism: /ˈmjuːtɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 缄默症，沉默症 |  A condition characterized by persistent refusal or inability to speak, often associated with psychological factors. |  He suffered from mutacism after the traumatic event. 他在经历了创伤性事件后患上了缄默症。| synonyms: silence, dumbness, aphasia (in some cases) | antonyms: loquacity, talkativeness | noun
