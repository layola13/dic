caretakers: /ˈkɛərˌteɪkərz/
n.
管理员，看护人
A person who takes care of a person, a place, or an organization.
The caretakers of the museum ensure that the exhibits are well-maintained. 博物馆的管理员确保展品维护良好。

The caretakers of the elderly woman made sure she took her medication regularly.  照顾这位老妇人的看护确保她按时服药。

近义词：custodians, guardians, attendants, minders
反义词：neglecter, abandoner
词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "caretaker," a compound of "care" (Old English caru, from Proto-Germanic *karō) and "taker" (from "take" - Old English tacan, of Germanic origin).
