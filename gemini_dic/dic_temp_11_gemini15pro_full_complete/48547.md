defalcate:/ˌdiːfælˈkeɪt/ | v. | 挪用；盗用（尤指公款） | To embezzle or misappropriate funds, especially those entrusted to one's care. | The treasurer was accused of defalcating thousands of dollars from the company.  财务主管被指控盗用公司数千美元。| Synonyms: embezzle, misappropriate, peculate | Antonyms: reimburse, repay | verb

defalcation:/ˌdiːfælˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 挪用；盗用（尤指公款） | The embezzlement or misappropriation of funds, especially those entrusted to one's care. | The defalcation of the company's funds led to its bankruptcy. 公司资金的挪用导致其破产。 | Synonyms: embezzlement, misappropriation, peculation | Antonyms: restitution, reimbursement | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin defalcare "to cut off, deduct," from de- "off" + falx "sickle."  The original meaning referred to deducting a portion of something, but the meaning shifted over time to refer specifically to the fraudulent deduction or misappropriation of funds.
