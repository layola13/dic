aren't:/ɑːrnt/ /ɑːnt/
| v. |不是|Contraction of "are not".  Used to form the negative of the present tense of the verb "to be" for plural subjects and the second person singular.|They aren't coming to the party. 他们不会来参加聚会了。|近义词:ain't (informal)|反义词:are|词性:verb (contraction)

They aren't happy with the results. 他们对结果不满意。
We aren't going to the cinema tonight. 我们今晚不去电影院。

They aren't playing football now. 他们现在没有在踢足球。
We aren't working on that project anymore. 我们不再做那个项目了。

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
They aren't finished with their homework yet. 他们还没有完成作业。
We aren't convinced by his argument. 我们不相信他的论点。

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
They aren't been waiting long. 他们没等多久。
We aren't been working on this project for very long. 我们做这个项目的时间不长。

虚拟语气:
If they weren't so busy, they would help us. 如果他们不那么忙，他们会帮助我们。
I wish they weren't leaving so soon. 我希望他们不要这么快就离开。


被动语态:
These rules aren't enforced very strictly. 这些规则执行得不是很严格。
The details aren't being released to the public yet. 细节尚未向公众发布。


疑问句:
Aren't they coming to the party? 他们不是要来参加聚会吗？
Aren't you feeling well? 你感觉不舒服吗？


否定句:
They aren't my friends. 他们不是我的朋友。
We aren't going to the beach today. 我们今天不去海滩。


PHRASE->
There aren't many options. 没有很多选择。
Why aren't you ready yet? 你怎么还没准备好？

They aren't what they seem. 他们并非表面看起来那样。
Aren't I lucky? 我不是很幸运吗？


词源:  "aren't" is a contraction of "are not."  "Are" comes from Old English "aron," plural of "earun" (are). "Not" comes from Old English "nawiht," meaning "nothing" or "not at all."
