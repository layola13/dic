lectern: /ˈlektərn/ (US), /ˈlektən/ (UK) | n. |讲台|A stand with a sloping top, used to support a book or script when reading aloud, especially in a church or lecture hall.|The speaker placed her notes on the lectern and began her presentation. 演讲者把她的笔记放在讲台上，开始了她的演讲。| 近义词: podium, rostrum, stand | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "floor" in the context of public speaking) | 词性: 名词

词源：From Old French *letrin, from Latin lecternus, lectrinus “of or belonging to a bed or couch,” from lectus “bed, couch.”  The sense shifted from "reading desk in a monastery dormitory" to "reading desk in a church."
