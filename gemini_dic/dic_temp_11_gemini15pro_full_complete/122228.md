misworshiped:/ˌmɪsˈwɜːrʃɪpt/ |v. |错误地崇拜，不当地崇拜| To worship someone or something incorrectly, or to give reverence to something that is not worthy of it. |例句：They misworshiped idols made of wood and stone. 他们错误地崇拜由木头和石头制成的偶像。

例句：He misworshiped power and money, neglecting his family and friends. 他错误地崇拜权力和金钱，忽略了他的家人和朋友。|近义词：idolize falsely, venerate wrongly|反义词：worship correctly, venerate properly|词性：动词

misworshipping:/ˌmɪsˈwɜːrʃɪpɪŋ/ |adj.|错误崇拜的，不当崇拜的| Characterized by incorrect or inappropriate worship. |例句:  Their misworshipping behavior led them astray. 他们错误的崇拜行为使他们误入歧途。

例句: The tribe's misworshipping rituals were condemned by the missionaries. 这个部落错误的崇拜仪式受到了传教士的谴责。 |近义词: idolatrous, misguided in worship |反义词: reverent, pious |词性: 形容词


词源:

"mis-" (Old English *mis-, from Proto-Germanic *missa- "wrongly," related to Old Norse mis-, Old Frisian mis-, Old Saxon mis-, Dutch mis-, Old High German mis-, German miß-, Gothic missa-) + "worship" (Old English weorðscipe "condition of being worthy, dignity, glory, honor, respect; adoration, reverence," from weorð "worthy" + -scipe (see -ship)).  Essentially, it combines the concept of "wrong" with the act of "worship."
