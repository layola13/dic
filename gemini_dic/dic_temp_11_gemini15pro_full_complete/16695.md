barricaded:/ˌbærɪˈkeɪdɪd/
adjective|设有路障的， barricaded 用作形容词，意思是“设有路障的”“被封锁的”，在句中主要用作表语或定语。|The protesters barricaded themselves in the building. 抗议者们把自己封锁在大楼里。|近义词：blocked, obstructed, fortified|反义词：open, accessible|词性:adjective
barricaded:/ˌbærɪˈkeɪdɪd/
verb|用路障围住， barricaded 是 barricade 的过去式和过去分词形式，作动词时意为“用障碍物封锁；设置路障”，指用障碍物阻止人或物通过。|They barricaded the doors and windows to prevent the rioters from entering. 他们用障碍物封锁了门窗，以防止暴徒进入。|近义词：block, obstruct, fortify|反义词：open, uncover|词性:verb

Etymology:
From French *barricader*, from *barrique* "barrel" (from Latin *barrica*) + *-ade*.  Originally referred to fortifications made of barrels filled with earth.
