aprocta:/əˈprɒktə/ | n. | 无肺目 | An order of parasitic nematodes, commonly known as lungworms, characterized by the absence of a pulmonary system. They are typically found in the respiratory or circulatory systems of vertebrates. | The aprocta species *Dictyocaulus viviparus* causes parasitic bronchitis in cattle. 无肺目物种*Dictyocaulus viviparus*会引起牛的寄生性支气管炎。| 近义词: none (as it's a taxonomic classification) | 反义词: none (as it's a taxonomic classification) | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From Greek *a-* (without) + *proktos* (anus).  This refers to the absence of a distinct anus in these organisms.
