die-hard:/ˈdaɪˌhɑːrd/ | adj. n. |顽固的；死硬的；顽固分子；死硬派|Someone who is die-hard is unwilling to change their beliefs or give up their ways, even when there is good reason to do so.  A die-hard is a person who strongly supports a particular cause or political party. | He's a die-hard fan of the team. 他是球队的铁杆球迷。| 近义词:stubborn, resolute, tenacious, inflexible, obstinate | 反义词:flexible, adaptable, open-minded | 词性:形容词, 名词

die-hard:/ˈdaɪˌhɑːrd/ | n. |顽固分子；死硬派 | A person who strongly supports a particular cause or political party and refuses to change their beliefs or actions. |  The die-hards refused to accept the new policy.  死硬派拒绝接受新政策。 | 近义词:fanatic, zealot, extremist | 反义词:moderate, pragmatist | 词性:名词

词源:  "Die-hard" originally referred to soldiers who fought to the death rather than surrender.  It later broadened to describe anyone unwilling to give up their beliefs or ways.  The term gained wider usage in the 19th century.
