make heads roll:/meɪk hɛdz roʊl/
|idiomatic phrase|
|使人丢官；造成震动；引起混乱|
|To cause important people to lose their jobs or positions of power, often due to a scandal or major change.|
|The new CEO came in and made heads roll, firing several senior managers.|新任首席执行官上任后大刀阔斧，解雇了几名高级经理。|
|Synonyms: cause upheaval, shake things up, clean house, bring about change, oust, dismiss, fire, sack|
|Antonyms: maintain the status quo, keep things the same, leave things undisturbed|
|phrase|
