free-for-all:/ˌfriː fər ˈɔːl/ | n. | 自由竞争；混战；多人为所欲为的局面 | A disorganized or unrestricted situation or contest in which everyone is trying to get what they want, often in a chaotic or unfair way. | The Black Friday sale turned into a complete free-for-all, with people grabbing items off the shelves and fighting over discounted merchandise. 黑色星期五的促销变成了一场彻底的混战，人们从货架上抢东西，为打折商品争吵。

The meeting descended into a free-for-all, with everyone shouting and interrupting each other. 会议沦为一场混战，每个人都在大喊大叫，互相打断。| 近义词：brawl, melee, fracas, Donnybrook | 反义词：order, organized event, structured competition | 词性: 名词


词源:  Compound of "free" and "for all," suggesting a situation open to anyone and often lacking rules or control.  Its earliest recorded use is from the mid-19th century, initially referring to a kind of brawl or fight.
