deiparous:/dɪˈɪpərəs/| adj.|神生的，生神的|Bearing or giving birth to a god or deity.  Relating to the bearing of a god, specifically the Virgin Mary.|例句：Early Christian art often depicts Mary as deiparous, emphasizing her role as the Mother of God. 早期基督教艺术经常将玛利亚描绘成生神的，强调她作为上帝之母的角色。

例句：The deiparous nature of Mary is a central tenet of Catholic theology. 玛利亚的生神属性是天主教神学的核心原则。|近义词：theotokos (Greek)|反义词: N/A|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *deiparus, from dei (genitive of deus, "god") + -parus ("bearing," related to parere, "to bear, give birth to").
