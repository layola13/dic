mercenary: /ˈmɜːrsənəri/ (US) /ˈmɜːsənəri/ (UK)| n. adj.|雇佣兵，唯利是图的|A soldier who is paid to fight for a foreign country. Also describes someone motivated solely by personal gain.|例句：He was a mercenary, fighting for whichever side paid the most. 他是个雇佣兵，为出价最高的任何一方作战。

例句：Many companies employ mercenaries to protect their overseas interests. 许多公司雇佣雇佣兵来保护他们的海外利益。|近义词：soldier of fortune, hired gun|反义词：patriot, volunteer|词性：名词, 形容词

mercenary: /ˈmɜːrsənəri/ (US) /ˈmɜːsənəri/ (UK)| adj.|唯利是图的，贪财的|Primarily concerned with making money at the expense of ethics.|例句：She had a mercenary attitude towards relationships, always looking for what she could get out of them. 她对人际关系的态度很唯利是图，总是想着能从中得到什么。

例句：His motives for taking the job were purely mercenary. 他接受这份工作的动机纯粹是出于贪财。|近义词：greedy, avaricious, grasping|反义词：altruistic, selfless|词性：形容词
