crater:/ˈkreɪtər/ (US) /ˈkreɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n. | 火山口；弹坑；陨石坑 | A bowl-shaped depression, usually round, formed by volcanic activity, the impact of a meteorite, or an explosion. |  The meteor left a large crater in the desert. 流星在沙漠中留下了一个大坑。

|Synonyms: cavity, depression, hole, pit, hollow|Antonyms: mound, hill, peak|Noun


crater:/ˈkreɪtər/ (US) /ˈkreɪtə(r)/ (UK)| v. | 形成坑；产生弹坑 | To form a crater in (something). | The explosion cratered the moon's surface. 爆炸在月球表面形成了一个坑。

|Synonyms:  pit, hollow, gouge|Antonyms: fill, level, smooth|Verb
