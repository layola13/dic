offspring:/ˈɔːfsprɪŋ/ (US) /ˈɒfsprɪŋ/ (UK)
|n.|后代，子孙，产物|A person's child or children.  Something that is produced as a result of something else.|The farmer was proud of his healthy offspring.  这位农民为他的健康后代感到自豪。|Synonyms: children, descendants, progeny, issue, young|Antonyms: parent, ancestor|noun

offsprings:/ˈɔːfsprɪŋz/ (US) /ˈɒfsprɪŋz/ (UK)
|n.|后代，子孙，产物 (复数形式，较少使用, offspring本身可以指单数或复数)|A person's children or an animal's young. Something that is produced as a result of something else.  While "offspring" is generally preferred for both singular and plural, "offsprings" can sometimes be encountered, particularly in older or more informal texts.|She dedicated her life to raising her offsprings. 她一生致力于抚养她的孩子们。（较少使用，建议用offspring） The new law is the offspring of a long political battle. 这项新法律是一场漫长政治斗争的产物。|Synonyms: children, descendants, progeny, issue, young|Antonyms: parent, ancestor|noun
