behaviourist:/bɪˈheɪvjərɪst/| n. |行为主义者|A psychologist who adheres to behaviorism, a school of thought that emphasizes the study of observable behavior and its environmental determinants. |例句：The behaviourist conducted experiments to observe the rats' responses to stimuli. 行为主义者进行实验来观察老鼠对刺激的反应。

例句：John B. Watson is considered a prominent behaviourist.  约翰·B·沃森被认为是一位杰出的行为主义者。|近义词：behaviorist (alternative spelling) |反义词:cognitivist, mentalist|noun

behaviourism:/bɪˈheɪvjərɪzəm/| n. |行为主义|A school of psychology that emphasizes the study of observable behavior and its environmental determinants, rather than mental processes. |例句：Behaviourism focuses on how learning occurs through conditioning. 行为主义关注学习是如何通过条件反射发生的。

例句：Behaviourism was a dominant force in psychology during the mid-20th century. 行为主义是20世纪中期心理学领域的主导力量。|近义词：learning theory|反义词:cognitivism, mentalism|noun

behavioural:/bɪˈheɪvjərəl/| adj. |行为的|Relating to or involving behavior. |例句：Behavioral therapy can help people overcome phobias. 行为疗法可以帮助人们克服恐惧症。

例句：The researchers studied the behavioural patterns of primates. 研究人员研究了灵长类动物的行为模式。|近义词：conductual|反义词:cognitive, mental|adjective
