infante:/ɪnˈfænti/ /ɪnˈfɑːnteɪ/ | n. | 婴儿；幼儿；（西班牙和葡萄牙）王子；公主 | A young child, especially one of royal birth. In Spain and Portugal, the title of infante or infanta is given to royal princes and princesses who are not heirs to the throne. |  例句：The infante was baptized in a lavish ceremony.  这位王子/公主在一个奢华的仪式上受洗。

例句：The portrait depicted the infante playing with a toy soldier.  这幅肖像画描绘了这位王子/公主玩玩具士兵的情景。 | 近义词：baby, child, toddler, infant, (for royalty) prince, princess | 反义词：adult, grown-up | 词性：noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *enfant* (modern French *enfant*), from Latin *infans* ("unable to speak, young"), present participle of *infari* ("to speak indistinctly"), from *in-* ("not") + *fari* ("to speak").  Cognate with Spanish *infante*, Portuguese *infante*, Italian *infante*.
