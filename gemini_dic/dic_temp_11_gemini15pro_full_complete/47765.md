decamping:/dɪˈkæmpɪŋ/
| v. | 撤离，突然离开 | Leaving suddenly or secretly, especially without paying one's debts. |  The circus decamped in the middle of the night. 马戏团半夜偷偷离开了。| synonyms: fleeing, absconding, escaping, departing | antonyms: arriving, staying, remaining | verb

decamping:/dɪˈkæmpɪŋ/
| n. | 撤离，突然离开 | The act of leaving suddenly or secretly. |  The decamping of the CEO surprised everyone. 首席执行官的突然离开让每个人都感到惊讶。| synonyms: flight, escape, departure | antonyms: arrival, stay | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French "décamper," from "dé-" (away, from) + "camper" (to encamp).  Ultimately from Latin "campus" (field).
