antisyndicalists:/ˌæntiˌsɪndɪkəlɪsts/
| n. |反工团主义者；反对工会者|People who oppose syndicalism, a movement advocating worker control of industry through the establishment of trade unions.|The government cracked down on the antisyndicalists during the labor unrest.|近义词：anti-unionists, labor opponents|反义词：syndicalists, unionists|Noun

antisyndicalist:/ˌæntiˌsɪndɪkəlɪst/
| adj. |反工团主义的；反对工会的|Opposed to syndicalism or trade unions.|The antisyndicalist policies of the government led to widespread protests.|近义词：anti-union, anti-labor|反义词：pro-union, pro-labor|Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + syndicalist.  Syndicalist comes from French syndicaliste, from syndicat "trade union," from Latin syndicatus "legal representative, defender," from syndicus "procurator, delegate," from Greek syndikos "one who helps in a lawsuit, advocate, representative," from syn- "together" + dike "justice."
