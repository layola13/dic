capriciousness:/kəˈprɪʃəsnəs/| n. |反复无常；善变；任性|The state of being capricious; unpredictable changes in mood or behavior. |Her capriciousness made it difficult to make plans with her. 她的反复无常使得很难和她一起制定计划。|The capriciousness of the weather ruined our picnic. 天气的变化无常毁了我们的野餐。|近义词：fickleness, volatility, whimsy, unpredictability|反义词：steadiness, consistency, stability|词性:noun
capricious:/kəˈprɪʃəs/| adj. |反复无常的；善变的；任性的|Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior. |He was a capricious leader, making decisions based on whims rather than logic. 他是一个反复无常的领导者，做决定基于一时兴起而不是逻辑。|The capricious winds made sailing difficult.  变化无常的风使得航行很困难。|近义词：fickle, volatile, whimsical, unpredictable|反义词：steady, consistent, stable|词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *capriciosus*, from Italian capriccioso (“capricious”), from capriccio (“whim, caprice”), from capo (“head”) + riccio (“hedgehog”).  The notion is of a head full of hedgehog spines, hence prickly, irritable, whimsical.
