launderings:/ˈlɔːndərɪŋz/
n.
洗钱，洗涤，洗熨
the act of washing, ironing, and folding clothes or linen; the processing of converting illegally obtained money into seemingly legitimate funds.
The FBI investigated several international launderings involving millions of dollars.  联邦调查局调查了几起涉及数百万美元的国际洗钱案。
He sent his launderings to the dry cleaners. 他把要洗的衣物送去了干洗店。
Synonyms: cleaning, washing, cleansing (for literal laundering); money laundering (for financial laundering)
Antonyms: soiling, dirtying (for literal laundering)
noun


launder:/ˈlɔːndər/
v.
洗涤，洗钱
to wash, iron, and fold clothes or linen; to conceal the source of illegally obtained money by converting it into seemingly legitimate funds.
She laundered the sheets and towels. 她洗了床单和毛巾。
They were accused of laundering drug money through offshore accounts. 他们被指控通过离岸账户洗钱。
Synonyms: wash, clean, cleanse (for literal laundering); conceal, disguise (for financial laundering)
Antonyms: soil, dirty (for literal laundering); expose, reveal (for financial laundering)
verb
