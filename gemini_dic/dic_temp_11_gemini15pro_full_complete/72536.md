fluster:/ˈflʌstər/ (US); /ˈflʌstə(r)/ (UK)| v. n. |使慌乱，使不安；慌乱，不安|To make someone nervous and confused, typically so that they are unable to react calmly or coherently; A state of nervous excitement or confusion.|

例句：
The unexpected question flustered her.
突如其来的问题让她慌了神。

Being called on to speak in front of the class flustered him.
被叫到在全班同学面前讲话让他很紧张。

Her cheeks flushed with a fluster of shyness.
她的双颊因一阵害羞而泛红。

The sudden noise caused a fluster of activity in the office.
突然的噪音使办公室里一阵忙乱。


近义词：unnerve, disconcert, agitate, confuse, rattle, bewilder
反义词：calm, compose, soothe, reassure
词性：verb, noun
