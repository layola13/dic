marechal:/ˈmɑːrʃəl/ | n. | 元帅，最高指挥官 | A high-ranking military officer, especially the highest rank in the army of some countries. |例句：He was promoted to the rank of marechal. 他被提升为元帅。

例句：The marechal led the troops into battle. 元帅率领部队投入战斗。

近义词：field marshal, general, commander | 反义词: private, soldier | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mareschal, from Frankish *marhskalk "horse servant," from *marha- "horse" + *skalk- "servant." The word originally referred to a person who cared for horses, especially in a royal household, and gradually evolved to denote a high military rank.
