effacers:/ɪˈfeɪsərz/
n.
清除器；消除器；抹去者
People or things that efface something.  Those who obliterate or erase something.
The effacers of history attempted to rewrite textbooks and burn books. 历史的抹去者试图改写教科书和焚烧书籍。
近义词:erasers, obliterators, destroyers
反义词:creators, preservers, restorers
词性:名词

efface:/ɪˈfeɪs/
v.
抹去，消除；使…模糊
To remove something intentionally, often a mark or writing, or to make something less noticeable or important.  Derived from Old French *effacer*, from *ex-* (out) + *face* (face).  The original sense was "to scratch out the face" (of a coin or inscription).
Time had effaced almost all signs of the struggle. 时间几乎抹去了所有斗争的痕迹。
近义词:erase, obliterate, expunge
反义词:preserve, enhance, emphasize
词性:动词
