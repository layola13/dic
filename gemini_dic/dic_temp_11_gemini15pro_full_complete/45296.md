culverin: /ˈkʌlvərɪn/ | n. | 古代一种长管火炮;一种长蛇; | A type of long cannon used from the 14th to the 17th century, typically larger and more powerful than a falcon but smaller than a cannon royal. It had a long barrel and fired solid shot. | The culverin's accuracy and range made it a valuable weapon in siege warfare.  这门culverin的精度和射程使其成为攻城战中的宝贵武器。

The ship's cannons included several culverins, capable of hitting targets far inland.  这艘船的大炮包括几门culverin，能够击中远在内陆的目标。 | 近义词: cannon, bombard | 反义词: (No direct antonym for a specific weapon type) | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *couleuvrine*, from *couleuvre* "snake", from Latin *colubra*.  The name likely refers to the long, snake-like shape of the barrel.
