interminably:/ɪnˈtɜːrmɪnəbli/| adv.|没完没了地，无休止地|Continuing for a very long time and therefore boring or annoying.|The meeting dragged on interminably.  会议没完没了地拖延下去。|The speaker talked interminably about his childhood. 演讲者喋喋不休地谈论他的童年。|近义词: endlessly, incessantly, perpetually|反义词: briefly, shortly, concisely|词性:副词
Etymology: From Late Latin interminabilis, from in- "not" + terminabilis "that can be limited," from terminare "to limit," from terminus "end, limit."
