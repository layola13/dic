ecumenists:/ɪˈkjuːmənɪsts/ | n. | 教会合一主义者；普世教会主义者 |  People who advocate or promote ecumenism, the principle or aim of promoting unity among the world's Christian churches. |  The ecumenists worked tirelessly to bridge the divides between different denominations. 教会合一主义者孜孜不倦地致力于弥合不同教派之间的分歧。| Synonyms:  unifiers, conciliators | Antonyms:  separatists, schismatics | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *oecumenicus*, from Ancient Greek οἰκουμενικός (oikoumenikós, “of the whole world, universal, general”), from οἰκουμένη (oikoumenē, “the inhabited world”), from οἰκέω (oikeō, “I inhabit”), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *weyḱ- (“house, settlement”).
