bungle:/ˈbʌŋɡəl/| v. n.| 笨拙地做；搞砸；失败；拙劣的工作| To carry out (a task) clumsily or incompetently, leading to failure or an unsatisfactory outcome. |  He bungled the presentation and lost the contract. 他搞砸了演示，失去了合同。| synonyms: mishandle, botch, mess up, boggle, fumble, spoil | antonyms: succeed, achieve, accomplish, manage, excel| verb

bungle:/ˈbʌŋɡəl/| n.| 拙劣的工作；失败；失误| A clumsy or incompetently performed task; a failure or mistake. | The entire project was a bungle from start to finish. 整个项目从头到尾都是一团糟。| synonyms: mess, blunder, fiasco, foul-up, cock-up| antonyms: success, achievement, triumph, masterpiece| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain, possibly from a Scandinavian source.  First recorded in the 18th century.
