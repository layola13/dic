connotations:/ˌkɒnəˈteɪʃənz/ /ˌkɑːnəˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 内涵；含义；隐含意义 | An idea or feeling that a word invokes in addition to its literal or primary meaning. |  The word "cheap" has negative connotations of poor quality. “便宜”这个词带有质量差的负面含义。


connotation:/ˌkɒnəˈteɪʃən/ /ˌkɑːnəˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 内涵；含义；隐含意义 |  The implied or associative meaning of a word in addition to its literal meaning. | The word "home" has warm connotations of family and comfort. “家”这个词带有家庭和舒适的温暖含义。


connotation:/ˌkɒnəˈteɪʃən/ /ˌkɑːnəˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 内涵；含义；隐含意义 | From Latin "connotare"  (to mark together, to signify), from "com-" (together) + "notare" (to mark). |  The connotations of the word "politician" can vary depending on one's political views. “政治家”一词的含义会因个人的政治观点而异。
