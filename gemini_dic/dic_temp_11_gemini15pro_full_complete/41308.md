cordwain:/ˈkɔːrdweɪn/| n.|科尔多瓦皮革; 山羊皮; 细皮革|A type of fine, soft leather originally made in Cordoba, Spain, from goatskin or sheepskin, typically used for shoes.  It is also a term for shoemaking.|The cordwainer carefully stitched the cordwain upper to the sole.  鞋匠小心地将科尔多瓦皮革鞋面缝到鞋底上。|Synonyms: leather, goatskin, sheepskin|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a material; perhaps synthetic leather as a conceptual opposite)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *cordoanier, from cordouan "of Cordoba," from Latin Corduba, the Roman name for Córdoba, Spain.
