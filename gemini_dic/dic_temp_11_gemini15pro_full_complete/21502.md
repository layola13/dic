bludgeoning:/ˈblʌdʒənɪŋ/| n. v.| 殴打，重击；（比喻）严厉批评，抨击 |The act of hitting someone or something repeatedly with a heavy object; a forceful and relentless attack (often metaphorical).|He was charged with bludgeoning his neighbor to death. 他被指控用钝器殴打邻居致死。| The constant bludgeoning of negative news can be demoralizing.  不断涌现的负面新闻会让人 demoralizing. |Synonyms: beating, battering, pummeling, lambasting, criticizing|Antonyms: praising, commending, caressing|noun, verb


bludgeon:/ˈblʌdʒən/| n. v.| 棍棒，钝器；用棍棒打，（比喻）强迫，胁迫 |A heavy club or similar weapon; to hit someone or something repeatedly with such a weapon; to force someone to do something.|The robbers used a bludgeon to subdue the security guard.  劫匪用棍棒制服了保安。|  She felt bludgeoned into accepting the deal. 她感到被迫接受了这笔交易。|Synonyms: club, cudgel, cosh, force, coerce|Antonyms: persuade, coax|noun, verb
