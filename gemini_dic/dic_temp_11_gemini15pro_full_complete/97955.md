Inuit: /ˈɪnjuɪt/ (US), /ˈɪnjuːɪt/ (UK)| n.| 因纽特人; 因纽特语 |A member of an indigenous people of northern Canada, Greenland, Alaska, and eastern Siberia, traditionally living by hunting, fishing, and reindeer herding.  The language of the Inuit people. |Example: The Inuit have a deep understanding of the Arctic environment. 因纽特人对北极环境有着深刻的理解。|Example: She is learning to speak Inuit. 她正在学习说因纽特语。|Synonyms: Eskimo (outdated and considered offensive by many), Inuk (singular) |Antonyms: N/A|Noun
