astonishing:/əˈstɒnɪʃɪŋ/ (US) /əˈstɒnɪʃɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|令人惊讶的，惊人的|Causing astonishment; extremely surprising or impressive. |例句：The view from the mountaintop was astonishing.  从山顶望下去的景色令人叹为观止。|近义词：amazing, surprising, astounding, incredible, remarkable|反义词：unremarkable, ordinary, mundane|词性:形容词

astonish:/əˈstɒnɪʃ/ (US) /əˈstɒnɪʃ/ (UK)| vt.|使惊讶，使震惊|Surprise or impress (someone) greatly.|例句：I was astonished by his sudden appearance. 他的突然出现让我大吃一惊。|近义词：surprise, amaze, astound, startle|反义词：bore, tire|词性:动词

astonishment:/əˈstɒnɪʃmənt/ (US) /əˈstɒnɪʃmənt/ (UK)| n.|惊讶，惊奇|Great surprise.|例句：She stared in astonishment at the news. 她惊愕地盯着这则新闻。|近义词：surprise, amazement, awe|反义词：indifference, apathy|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *astonysshen, from Anglo-Norman estoner, astoner, from Old French estoner (“to stun, daze, astound”), from Vulgar Latin *extonare, from Latin ex- + tonare (“to thunder”).
