married: /ˈmærɪd/ (US) /ˈmærid/ (UK)| adj.  v.| 已婚的，结婚的；结婚 |Being in a state of matrimony.  As a verb, it signifies the act of entering into marriage.|例句：They are a happily married couple.  他们是一对幸福的已婚夫妇。|近义词：wedded, espoused |反义词: single, unmarried, divorced |词性: adjective/verb

married: /ˈmærɪd/ (US) /ˈmærid/ (UK)| v.| 结婚，嫁，娶 |To join in marriage.|例句：They married last year. 他们去年结婚了。|近义词：wed, espouse |反义词: divorce, separate |词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *maried, *marred, from Old English *gemarried, past participle of *mariġean (“to marry”), from Proto-West Germanic *marōn, from Proto-Germanic *marōną (“to provide with a husband, give in marriage”), from Proto-Indo-European *mer- (“to provide with, allot, assign”).  Cognate with Old Frisian mariā (“to marry”), Old Saxon marōn (“to marry”), Old High German marōn (“to marry”), Old Norse marka (“to provide with a husband”).

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
She is married to a doctor. 她嫁给了一位医生。
He marries his best friend's sister. 他娶了他最好朋友的妹妹。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
They are getting married next spring. 他们明年春天结婚。
He is marrying his longtime girlfriend. 他正在和他交往多年的女朋友结婚。

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
They have been married for twenty years. 他们已经结婚二十年了。
She has married twice. 她结过两次婚。

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
They have been married happily since 1998. 从1998年开始他们一直婚姻幸福。 (Note: This tense is less common with "married" as a state of being.)

一般过去时 (Simple Past):
They married in a small church. 他们在一个小教堂结婚。
She married him for his money. 她嫁给他为了他的钱。

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
They were planning their wedding when they received the bad news. 当他们收到坏消息时，他们正在计划他们的婚礼。 (Note:  "were marrying" in the past continuous is not typically used.)


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
They had been married for five years before they had their first child. 他们在有第一个孩子之前已经结婚五年了。
She had already married someone else by the time he returned. 当他回来的时候，她已经嫁给了别人。

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
They had been planning their wedding for months before it was postponed. 在婚礼被推迟之前，他们已经筹划了好几个月。(Note:  "had been marrying" in the past perfect continuous is not typically used.)

一般将来时 (Simple Future):
They will marry in June. 他们将在六月结婚。
She will marry him eventually. 她最终会嫁给他的。


将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
They will be getting married this time next year. 明年这个时候他们将要结婚。(Note: "will be marrying" is less common.)


将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
By next year, they will have been married for ten years. 到明年，他们将结婚十年了。

将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
This time next year, they will have been married for a decade.  明年这个时候，他们就已经结婚十年了。


虚拟语气:

否定句:
