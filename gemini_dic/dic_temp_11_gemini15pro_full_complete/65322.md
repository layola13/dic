eugenics:/juːˈdʒɛnɪks/
n.
优生学
the study of how to arrange reproduction within a human population to increase the occurrence of heritable characteristics regarded as desirable.
例句：The history of eugenics is a dark chapter in the 20th century. 优生学历史是20世纪黑暗的一章。

例句：He was a strong advocate for eugenics, believing it could improve society. 他是优生学的坚定拥护者，相信它可以改善社会。

近义词：genetic engineering, gene manipulation
反义词: dysgenics
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *eugenes* "well-born, of noble race," from *eu-* "good" + *genos* "birth, race, kind."  Coined in 1883 by Francis Galton.
