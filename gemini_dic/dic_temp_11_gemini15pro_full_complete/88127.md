hike:/haɪk/
n.|远足，徒步旅行|A long walk, especially in the countryside or mountains.|We went on a hike in the mountains last weekend. 我们上周末去山上徒步旅行了。|walk, trek, tramp|drive, ride
v.|徒步旅行，远足|To go for a long walk, especially in the countryside or mountains.|They hiked to the top of the mountain. 他们徒步旅行到山顶。|walk, trek, tramp|drive, ride
v.|大幅提高（价格等）|To increase (a price, rate, etc.) by a large amount.|The landlord hiked the rent this year.  房东今年大幅提高了租金。|raise, increase, boost|lower, reduce, decrease


ETYMOLOGY->
From earlier *hiken, probably from Middle English hyken "to limp, hobble," of unknown origin.  The sense of "long walk" developed in the 19th century, perhaps influenced by the idea of strenuous effort. The meaning "to increase sharply" is from the 20th century.
