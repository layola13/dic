consols:/ˈkɒnsɒlz/ (UK), /ˈkɑːnsɑːlz/ (US)| n. |公债, 永久公债|A consolidated annuity; a form of British government bond that pays interest in perpetuity, meaning it has no maturity date.  The principal is never repaid, but the interest payments continue indefinitely.|The government issued consols in the 18th century to fund wars.|近义词：perpetual bonds, gilts (more general)|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider) short-term bonds, callable bonds|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
Shortened from "consolidated annuities."  These were formed in 1751 by consolidating several government annuities into one.
