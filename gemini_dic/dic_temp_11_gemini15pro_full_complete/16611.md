barometz: /ˈbærəmɛts/ | n. | 植物羊， Scythian 羊羔 | A mythical zoophyte, said to grow in the form of a lamb attached to the ground by a stalk.  It was believed to devour all vegetation within reach. |  Legend has it that the barometz was a plant-animal hybrid that grew wool. | 近义词: vegetable lamb, Scythian lamb | 反义词: (No direct antonym for a mythical creature) | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Medieval Latin *barometz*, from Old French *barometz*, from Medieval Latin *borametz*, perhaps from a Turkic language.  Ultimately related to the word for "cotton."
