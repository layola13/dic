jaunty:/ˈdʒɔːnti/ | adj. | 轻松活泼的；神气活现的 | Having or expressing a lively, cheerful, and self-confident manner. | She wore her hat at a jaunty angle. 她俏皮地歪戴着帽子。 | Synonyms: cheerful, lively, perky, sprightly, brisk | Antonyms: gloomy, depressed, downcast | adjective

jaunty:/ˈdʒɔːnti/ | adj. | 衣着时髦的；潇洒的 | Stylish and confident in manner. | He strolled into the room with a jaunty air. 他神气地走进房间。 | Synonyms: stylish, chic, dapper, debonair | Antonyms: dowdy, frumpy, shabby | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French gentil ("noble, elegant, graceful"), from Latin gentilis ("of the same clan or family; related by blood"). The semantic shift from "noble" to "stylish" and then to "cheerful and self-confident" occurred over time in English.
