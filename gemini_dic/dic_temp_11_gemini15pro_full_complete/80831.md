gouge:/ɡaʊdʒ/| v. n.| 凿；挖； gouge (out) 欺诈；敲竹杠 |To make a hole or groove in something with a sharp tool or object. Also, to extort money from someone.|He gouged a hole in the wood. 他在木头上凿了个洞。| The store gouged customers by charging exorbitant prices for basic necessities. 这家商店通过对基本必需品收取过高的价格来敲诈顾客。|synonyms: scoop, excavate, extort, overcharge|antonyms: fill, embed, donate, undercharge|verb, noun

gouged:/ɡaʊdʒd/| adj.| 被凿的；被挖的| Having a hole or groove made by a sharp tool or object.  Also, cheated or overcharged. |The gouged surface of the table was unsightly.  桌子被凿过的表面很难看。|  The tourists felt gouged after paying triple the normal price for souvenirs.  游客们在为纪念品支付了三倍于正常价格后感到被敲竹杠了。|synonyms:  hollowed, grooved, cheated, swindled|antonyms: filled, smooth, intact, fairly treated|adjective
