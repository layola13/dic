bushrangers:/ˈbʊʃˌreɪndʒərz/
n.
歹徒，丛林强盗
(especially in Australia) a robber who lives in the bush.
例句：The bushrangers held up the stagecoach and stole the gold.  丛林强盗拦下公共马车，偷走了黄金。

例句：Ned Kelly is perhaps the most famous of Australian bushrangers.  奈德·凯利可能是澳大利亚最著名的丛林强盗。
近义词：outlaw, bandit, highwayman
反义词:lawman, police officer
词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "bush" + "ranger".  The word "bush" refers to the wild, uncleared land, and "ranger" refers to someone who roams or ranges over a particular area.  The term gained prominence in Australia in the 18th and 19th centuries.
