archbutler: /ˈɑːrtʃˌbʌtlər/ | n. | 总管，大管家 | A chief butler, especially in a large household or palace.  The archbutler oversaw all the staff and ensured the smooth running of the household. |  The archbutler announced the arrival of the royal guests. 总管宣布了皇室贵宾的到来。

The archbutler directed the other servants in preparing for the grand ball. 大管家指挥其他仆人为盛大的舞会做准备。 | 近义词：head butler, major-domo | 反义词: servant, underling | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
From arch- + butler.  "Arch-" acts as a prefix meaning "chief" or "principal."  "Butler" comes from Old French *butelier, meaning "bottle bearer," ultimately from Late Latin *butticula, "bottle."
