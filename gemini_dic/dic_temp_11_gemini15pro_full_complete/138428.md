nourished: /ˈnɜːrɪʃt/ | adj. | 滋养的，营养的 |  well-fed and healthy, having had enough food and other things necessary for growth and good health | The children looked healthy and well nourished. 孩子们看起来健康且营养良好。 |  healthy, well-fed, thriving | undernourished, malnourished, starved | adjective

nourished: /ˈnɜːrɪʃt/ | v. (past tense and past participle) | 滋养，养育 | to provide with the food or other substances necessary for growth, health, and good condition |  The plants are nourished by the rich soil. 植物被肥沃的土壤滋养。 | feed, sustain, nurture | starve, deprive, neglect | verb (past participle)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *norisshed, nourisshed*, past participle of *norisshen, nourisshen* (“to nourish, nurture, educate, bring up, rear”), from Anglo-Norman *nurir, norir*, from Old French *norrir* (“to nourish, nurture, educate, bring up, rear”), from Latin *nūtrīre* (“to suckle, nourish, cherish”), from *nūtrīx* (“nurse”), from Proto-Indo-European *\*nuh₂-tr-iks* (“nourisher”), from *\*nuh₂-tér-* (“to suckle, nurse, nourish”). Cognate with Old Spanish *nodrir* (“to nourish”).
