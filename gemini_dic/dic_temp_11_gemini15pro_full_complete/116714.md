maucaco:/məˈkɑːkoʊ/ | n. | 捲尾猴;白脸猴 |A type of monkey found in Central and South America, known for its long, prehensile tail. |  The maucaco swung effortlessly through the trees.  捲尾猴轻松地在树林间荡来荡去。|  Synonym: capuchin, monkey | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for an animal species) | Noun
Etymology: From Portuguese *macaco*, from an indigenous language of Brazil (probably Tupi).
