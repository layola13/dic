aumail:/ɔːˈmeɪl/ | n. | Chain mail; armor made of interlinked metal rings. | A flexible armor made of small metal rings linked together in a pattern, used in medieval times. | The knight wore aumail under his surcoat. 骑士在外衣下穿着锁子甲。 | Near synonyms: chain mail, mail, ring mail, hauberk | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "unarmored") | noun

Etymology:
From Old French *ausberc, halberc, from Frankish *halsberg ("neck protection"), from Proto-Germanic *halsaz ("neck") + *berganą ("to protect, cover").  The "au-" in aumail is a result of sound changes in Old French.
