flow-on:/ˈfloʊ ɒn/| adj. n.|后续的，连锁反应的；后续影响|A flow-on effect is a consequence that follows directly from another event or situation.  It's often used in the context of economics or other social sciences.  Think of it like dominoes – one event triggers the next.|例句：The increased cost of fuel has had a flow-on effect on the price of food. 燃料成本的增加对食品价格产生了连锁反应。

例句：The company's downsizing had a significant flow-on effect on the local economy.  公司的裁员对当地经济产生了重大的后续影响。|近义词：knock-on, ripple, consequential, indirect, secondary|反义词：primary, direct, initial|词性：adjective, noun
