malthusianisms:/mælˈθjuːziənɪzəmz/
|n.| 马尔萨斯主义，人口过剩论|Theories or principles derived from or related to the work of Thomas Robert Malthus, especially his theory that population tends to increase faster than the means of subsistence, leading to checks on population growth such as famine, disease, and war.  Specifically, it often refers to pessimistic or alarmist views about population growth and its consequences.|The politician's malthusianisms about overpopulation were met with criticism.|近义词: overpopulation theories, neo-Malthusianism|反义词: cornucopianism|词性: noun

malthusianism:/mælˈθjuːziənɪzəm/
|n.| 马尔萨斯主义，人口过剩论|The theory that population tends to increase faster than the means of subsistence, leading to checks on population growth such as famine, disease, and war.|His malthusianism led him to advocate for population control.|近义词: overpopulation theory, neo-Malthusianism|反义词: cornucopianism|词性: noun
