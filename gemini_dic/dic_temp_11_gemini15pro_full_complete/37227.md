comfiture:/ˈkʌmfɪtʃər/ |n.|狼狈；窘迫；挫折；失败|A state of confusion and embarrassment; discomfiture.|例句：To his comfiture, he realized he had forgotten his speech. 令他尴尬的是，他意识到自己忘记了演讲稿。|例句：Her comfiture was evident as she stumbled over her words.  当她结结巴巴地说不出话时，她的窘态显而易见。|近义词：discomfiture, embarrassment, humiliation, confusion|反义词：poise, composure, self-assurance|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *confiture, from confire (“to preserve, to defeat”), from Latin conficere (“to accomplish, to finish, to defeat”).  The original meaning was "rout, defeat," later shifting to the feeling of being defeated, or embarrassed.
