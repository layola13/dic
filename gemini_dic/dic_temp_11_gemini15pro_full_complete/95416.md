incidentalist:/ˌɪnsɪˈdɛntəlɪst/ | n. | 附带现象论者；偶然主义者 | A person who believes that historical events are largely determined by accidents or coincidences, rather than by underlying trends or laws. |  Historians often debate whether great figures shape history or are merely incidentalists swept along by events. 历史学家经常争论，伟大人物是塑造了历史，还是仅仅是被事件裹挟的偶然主义者。 | synonyms: accidentalist | antonyms: determinist, inevitabilist | noun

incidental:/ˌɪnsɪˈdɛntəl/ | adj. | 附带的；偶然的；非主要的 | Happening as a minor accompaniment to something else. | The discovery of the new element was incidental to their main research. 新元素的发现是他们主要研究的附带结果。| synonyms: accidental, subsidiary, ancillary, minor, secondary | antonyms: essential, primary, main, crucial, vital | adjective
