impose:/ɪmˈpoʊz/ (US) /ɪmˈpəʊz/ (UK)| v. |强加；征税；施加；利用；打扰|To force something, such as a belief, rule, or tax, to be accepted or put in place; to take unfair advantage of someone|The government imposed a new tax on cigarettes. 政府对香烟征收了新的税。|近义词：levy, inflict, enforce, dictate|反义词：remove, abolish, lift|词性:动词

Etymology:
From Late Middle English imposen, from Old French imposer, from Latin imponere (to place upon, impose), from in- (in, on) + ponere (to put, place).
