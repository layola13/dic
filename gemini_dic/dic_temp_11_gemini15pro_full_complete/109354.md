lenity:/ˈlɛnɪti/| n. |仁慈，宽厚，温和|Mildness or gentleness, especially in punishing or judging someone.|The judge showed lenity in sentencing the first-time offender. 法官对初犯表现出宽大处理。|Near Synonyms: mercy, clemency, compassion, forbearance, indulgence|Antonyms: severity, harshness, strictness, cruelty, ruthlessness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lenyte*, from Old French *lenité*, from Latin *lēnitās* ("gentleness, mildness"), from *lēnis* ("mild, gentle, calm").
