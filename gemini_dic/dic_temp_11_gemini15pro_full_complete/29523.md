catatonia: /ˌkætəˈtoʊniə/ (US) /ˌkætəˈtəʊniə/ (UK)| n. |紧张症，僵住症|A state of immobility and unresponsiveness lasting for long periods of time.|The patient exhibited catatonia, remaining motionless and silent for hours.|Near synonyms: stupor, rigidity, immobility|Antonyms: mobility, responsiveness, activity|noun

catatonias: /ˌkætəˈtoʊniəz/ (US) /ˌkætəˈtəʊniəz/ (UK)| n. |紧张症（复数）|Plural form of catatonia, referring to multiple instances or cases of the condition.|The psychiatrist studied various catatonias in patients with schizophrenia.|Near synonyms: stupors, rigidities, immobilities|Antonyms: mobilities, responsivenesses, activities|noun
