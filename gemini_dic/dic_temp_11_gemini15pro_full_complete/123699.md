moondog:/ˈmuːnˌdɒɡ/ (US), /ˈmuːnˌdɔːɡ/ (UK)| n.| 月旁彩云; 幻月; 伞状云|A relatively bright spot of light sometimes appearing next to the moon, caused by the refraction of moonlight by ice crystals in cirrus clouds.  A moondog is also sometimes called a paraselene (plural paraselenae).|  I saw a moondog last night, it was beautiful.  昨晚我看到一个幻月，很漂亮。| The moondogs were clearly visible on either side of the moon. 月亮两侧的幻月清晰可见。|Synonyms: paraselene, mock moon|Antonyms: N/A|noun


moondog:/ˈmuːnˌdɒɡ/ (US), /ˈmuːnˌdɔːɡ/ (UK)| n.| 流浪汉; 无业游民 (dated slang)|A homeless person, especially one who wanders from place to place.|He lived like a moondog, sleeping under bridges and scavenging for food. 他像个流浪汉一样生活，睡在桥下，四处觅食。|  The old moondog sat on the corner, begging for change.  那个老流浪汉坐在街角乞讨零钱。|Synonyms: vagrant, tramp, hobo|Antonyms: N/A|noun
