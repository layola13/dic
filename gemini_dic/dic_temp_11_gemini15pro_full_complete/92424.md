hypomanic:/ˌhaɪpəˈmænɪk/| adj.|轻躁狂的|A mild form of mania, characterized by an elevated mood, increased activity, and racing thoughts, but without the severe symptoms of mania that cause marked impairment in social or occupational functioning.|She experienced a hypomanic episode last year, marked by increased energy and impulsivity. 她去年经历了一段轻躁狂期，表现为精力充沛和冲动。|近义词:elated, excitable, energetic|反义词:depressed, lethargic, apathetic|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From hypo- (Greek: 'under', 'less than') + manic (from mania, Greek: 'madness', 'frenzy')
