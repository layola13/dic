lazier:/ˈleɪziər/ | adj. | 更懒的 | Comparative form of "lazy," meaning exhibiting a greater degree of unwillingness to work or exert oneself. |  He's lazier than his brother. 他比他哥哥更懒。|  She's become lazier since she started working from home. 自从她开始在家工作以来，她变得更懒了。 | 近义词: more indolent, more slothful | 反义词: more industrious, more diligent | 词性: adjective

词源: From Middle English *lazier, comparative of *lazy, from Old English læsīġ.
