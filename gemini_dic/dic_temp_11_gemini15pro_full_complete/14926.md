azoth:/ˈæzɒθ/ /ˈeɪzɑːθ/| n.|万能药，炼金术士的万能溶剂|A hypothetical universal solvent sought by alchemists. A term used in alchemy to denote the universal spirit or life force, believed to be the essential agent of transformation.  It was often associated with mercury, or the first matter of creation. |The alchemist claimed to have discovered the azoth, the key to transmuting lead into gold.  炼金术士声称发现了万能药，可以将铅转化为金。|Synonyms: elixir, panacea, philosopher's stone|Antonyms: N/A|noun

azothic:/əˈzɒθɪk/ /əˈzoʊθɪk/| adj.|与氮有关的，含氮的|Pertaining to or containing nitrogen. Relating to or containing azoth, the alchemical universal solvent.|Azothic compounds are essential for life. 含氮化合物对生命至关重要。|Synonyms: nitrogenous|Antonyms: N/A|adjective
