discrimination:/dɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪʃn/ | n. | 歧视；区别；辨别力 | The act of treating someone differently based on their race, religion, gender, etc., or the ability to recognize the difference between two things. |  例句：Discrimination based on race is illegal. 基于种族的歧视是非法的。

例句：He showed great discrimination in his choice of wines. 他在选择葡萄酒方面表现出极佳的鉴赏力。 | 近义词：prejudice, bias, bigotry, distinction, differentiation | 反义词：fairness, impartiality, equality | 词性: 名词

discriminations:/dɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪʃnz/ | n. | 歧视行为（复数）；区别（复数）；辨别力（复数）| The plural form of "discrimination," referring to multiple instances of treating someone differently based on their race, religion, gender, etc., or multiple instances of recognizing differences between things. | 例句：The company has been accused of several discriminations against female employees.  这家公司被指控对女性员工有多项歧视行为。

例句：Fine discriminations of color are difficult for some people.  对某些人来说，细微的颜色区别是难以辨别的。| 近义词：prejudices, biases, distinctions, differentiations | 反义词：fairness, impartiality, equality | 词性: 名词
