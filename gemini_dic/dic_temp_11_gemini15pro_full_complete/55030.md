disquiet:/dɪsˈkwaɪət/| n. vt.|不安，焦虑，担忧；使不安，使担忧|A feeling of anxiety or worry.|The news of the layoffs caused widespread disquiet among the employees. 裁员的消息在员工中引起了广泛的不安。|近义词：unease, anxiety, worry, concern, apprehension|反义词：calm, peace, tranquility|词性:名词,动词

disquiet:/dɪsˈkwaɪət/| vt.|使不安，使担忧|To make someone feel anxious or worried.|The strange noises in the night disquieted the residents of the neighborhood.  夜晚奇怪的声音让附近的居民感到不安。|近义词：disturb, upset, worry, trouble, bother|反义词：calm, soothe, reassure|词性:动词

词源: From Old French *desquetier, from des- "dis-" + quetier "to quiet," from Latin quies "rest, quiet."
