disloyalty's:/dɪsˈlɔɪəltiz/
n.|不忠诚，背叛|The disloyalty's sting was sharp and deep.  背叛的刺痛是尖锐而深刻的。|
英文解析: This is the possessive form of "disloyalty," meaning the state or quality of being disloyal. It refers to the act of betraying someone or something, breaking a promise or trust, or failing to support someone or something to which one owes allegiance.
例句：His disloyalty's consequences were severe. 他的不忠行为导致了严重的后果。
例句：The soldier's disloyalty's discovery shocked the entire army.  士兵不忠行为的发现震惊了整个军队。

近义词：treachery, betrayal, perfidy, unfaithfulness
反义词：loyalty, fidelity, faithfulness, allegiance
词性: 名词 (Possessive Noun)
