dauber:/ˈdɔːbər/ (US), /ˈdɔːbə(r)/ (UK)| n.|涂抹者，粗俗的画家，拙劣的艺术家|A person who daubs, especially one who paints crudely or unskillfully.  A person who applies a substance such as plaster, mud, or paint carelessly or roughly.|The dauber slapped the paint onto the canvas with little care. 这个涂抹者毫不在意地把颜料涂在画布上。|Synonyms: plasterer, painter, stainer, smearer|Antonyms: artist, craftsman|noun

daub:/dɔːb/ (US), /dɔːb/ (UK)| v.  n.|涂抹，涂污；涂抹物|To cover with a soft adhesive substance, such as plaster, mud, or paint, in a rough or careless way.  A smear or blob of a soft adhesive substance.|He daubed some mud on the wall to fill the crack. 他在墙上涂了些泥巴来填补裂缝。|Synonyms: smear, plaster, smudge, coat|Antonyms: clean, polish|verb, noun
