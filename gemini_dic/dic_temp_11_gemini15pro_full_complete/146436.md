overfoul:/ˌoʊvərˈfaʊl/| adj.|过犯规的，犯规过度的|An act in a sport that is judged to be a foul that is excessive or unnecessary.|The referee called an overfoul on the basketball player for his aggressive move.|近义词：excessive foul, flagrant foul|反义词：fair play, clean play|词性:形容词

Etymology:
"over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi) + "foul" (from Old English *fūl, meaning "rotten, putrid, unclean, filthy," later extended to moral failings)
