etacism:/ˈiːtəsɪzəm/
noun
元音化, 伊塔化
The excessive use of the sound /iː/ or /ɪ/, especially in Greek.  It refers to a phonological process where various vowels and diphthongs merge into a single vowel sound, often /i/ or a similar sound.
The modern Greek pronunciation is a result of etacism. 现代希腊语的发音是元音化的结果。
近义词: vowel shift, sound change
反义词: (No direct antonym, but perhaps) sound preservation
