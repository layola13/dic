festination:/ˌfɛstɪˈneɪʃən/
n.|步履慌张；慌忙|An involuntary quickening of gait, often seen in Parkinson's disease.|His festination made it difficult for him to navigate crowded sidewalks. 他的步履慌张使他难以在拥挤的人行道上行走。|Synonyms: hurrying, hastening, rushing|Antonyms: slowing, delaying|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *festinatio, from Latin festinare "to hasten," from festīnātus, past participle of festinō "I make haste, hurry."
