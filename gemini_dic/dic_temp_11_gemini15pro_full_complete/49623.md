demiculverin:/ˌdɛmiˈkʌlvərɪn/
noun
一种中型加农炮
A type of medium-sized cannon used from the 16th to the 18th centuries, typically firing a 9-pound ball.
The demiculverin was crucial in several sieges during the English Civil War.  这门半加农炮在英国内战期间的几次围攻中至关重要。
cannon, artillery piece
(No clear antonym for a specific type of cannon)
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *demicoulverine*, from *demi-* (half) + *couleuvrine* (culverin), ultimately from Latin *colubra* (snake).  The name likely refers to the long, slender shape of the early culverins resembling a snake.
