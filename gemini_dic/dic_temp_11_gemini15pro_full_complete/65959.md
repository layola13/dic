exaeresis:/ɪkˈsɪərɪsɪs/ | n. | 省音,省略元音 | The omission of a sound or syllable when speaking, as in "e'er" for "ever." |  An example of exaeresis is pronouncing "coop'rative" instead of "co-operative".  省音的一个例子是读作“coop'rative”而不是“co-operative”。| syn: syncope, elision | ant: epenthesis | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *exhaeresis, from Ancient Greek ἐξαίρεσις (exhairesis, “a taking out, removal”), from ἐξαιρέω (exhaireō, “I take out, remove”), from ἐξ (ex, “out”) + αἱρέω (haireō, “I take, seize”).
