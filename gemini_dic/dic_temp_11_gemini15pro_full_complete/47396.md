deadfall: /ˈdɛdfɔːl/ (US), /ˈdɛdfɔːl/ (UK) | n. | 陷阱，落石，倒木 | A trap for large animals, typically consisting of a heavy log or other object set to fall on and kill or disable the animal when it triggers a mechanism. Also, a tangle of fallen trees or branches. | The hunter set a deadfall in the woods, hoping to catch a deer. 猎人设置了一个陷阱，希望抓到一只鹿。 |  The hikers had to navigate through a dense deadfall after the storm.  暴风雨过后，徒步旅行者不得不穿过一片浓密的倒木。 | synonyms: trap, snare, pitfall | antonyms: shelter, refuge | noun


deadfall: /ˈdɛdfɔːl/ (US), /ˈdɛdfɔːl/ (UK) | n. |  大量; 突然的坠落; 大量堆积的东西 |  A large quantity of something that falls suddenly.  |  A deadfall of snow blocked the mountain pass. 大量的雪堵塞了山口。 | synonyms: avalanche, deluge, landslide | antonyms: trickle, dribble | noun
