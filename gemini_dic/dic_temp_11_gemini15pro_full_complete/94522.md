imposed: /ɪmˈpoʊzd/ (US) /ɪmˈpəʊzd/ (UK)
| adj. | 强加的，施加的，征收的 | forced, established, or applied |  A new tax was imposed on cigarettes.  对香烟征收了一项新税。 | enforced, levied, mandatory | voluntary, optional | adjective

impose: /ɪmˈpoʊz/ (US) /ɪmˈpəʊz/ (UK)
| verb |  强加，施加，征收 | to force something on someone | He imposed his will on the group. 他把自己的意愿强加于小组。 | enforce, inflict, levy |  remove, lift, abolish | verb
Etymology: From Late Middle English *imposen*, from Old French *imposer* (meaning "to lay on, inflict, assess"), from Latin *imponere* (meaning "to place upon, set in, inflict"), from *in-* ("in, on, upon") + *ponere* ("to place, put").

PHRASE->
impose on:  打扰；利用  Can I impose on you for a ride home? 我可以麻烦你载我回家吗？
impose oneself on:  硬缠着；打扰  I don't want to impose myself on them. 我不想打扰他们。
impose a burden on:  加重负担  The new tax imposed a burden on small businesses. 新税增加了小企业的负担。
impose a deadline on:  设定最后期限  The boss imposed a deadline on the project. 老板给项目设定了最后期限。
impose restrictions on:  限制  The government imposed restrictions on travel. 政府限制了旅行。
