affrontednesses:/əˈfrʌntɪdnɪsɪz/
n.
冒犯，侮辱（复数）
The state of being affronted; a collection of affronts or offended feelings.
His repeated affrontednesses finally led to a formal complaint. 他反复的冒犯最终导致了正式的投诉。
近义词: insults, offenses, indignities
反义词: respects, honors, courtesies
词性: 名词 (plural noun)

Etymology: From affronted + -ness.  Affront comes from the Old French afronter, meaning "to strike on the forehead," from a- "to" + front "forehead."  The meaning shifted to "insult" over time. -ness is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, denoting a state or quality.
