bureaucratic: /ˌbjʊərəˈkrætɪk/ /ˌbʊrəˈkrætɪk/
| adj. | 官僚的；官僚主义的 | Relating to a system of government in which most of the important decisions are made by state officials rather than by elected representatives.  |  The process was bureaucratic and time-consuming.  这个过程是官僚主义的，很费时间。 |
| | |  She encountered bureaucratic obstacles when trying to start her business.  她在创业时遇到了官僚障碍。 | 近义词：official, governmental, administrative | 反义词:  democratic, informal, streamlined | 词性: 形容词

| n. | 官僚机构；官僚作风；繁文缛节 | A system of government in which most of the important decisions are made by state officials rather than by elected representatives. |  He complained about the endless bureaucracy. 他抱怨无休止的官僚作风。 |
| | | The bureaucracy is hindering progress. 官僚机构阻碍了进步。 | 近义词：administration, government, red tape | 反义词:  democracy,  simplicity, efficiency | 词性: 名词

bureaucrat: /ˈbjʊərəkræt/ /ˈbʊrəkræt/
| n. | 官僚 | An official in a government department, in particular one perceived as being concerned with procedural correctness at the expense of people's needs. | He's a heartless bureaucrat. 他是一个冷酷无情的官僚。 |
| | | The bureaucrats are resistant to change. 官僚们抵制变革。 | 近义词：official, administrator, civil servant | 反义词:  revolutionary,  reformer,  activist | 词性: 名词
