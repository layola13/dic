agio:/ˈædʒioʊ/ (US), /ˈæʒiːəʊ/ (UK)| n.|溢价，贴水；佣金|A premium or commission charged on an exchange of one currency for another, especially a more valuable one.|Example: The agio for exchanging small bills for large ones was quite high.  将小面额钞票兑换成大面额钞票需要支付相当高的溢价。

Example: There's a slight agio on gold coins at the moment. 目前金币略有溢价。|近义词：premium, surcharge, commission|反义词:discount|词性:noun

Etymology: From Italian *aggio*, from Late Latin *adigium* "surplus, extra," itself from Latin *ad* "to" + a lost word reconstructed as *-iectium*, related to *iacēre* "to lie."  The sense development was likely from the notion of something "lying over" or "in addition to."
