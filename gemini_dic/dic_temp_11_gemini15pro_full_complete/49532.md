demarcation:/ˌdiːmɑːrˈkeɪʃn/ /ˌdɛmɑːrˈkeɪʃn/| n. |界限；分界；划分|The demarcation between the two properties was marked by a fence. 这两处房产的分界线用篱笆标明了。|例句：

The treaty established a clear demarcation line between the two countries.
该条约在两国之间建立了一条清晰的界限。

There's a clear demarcation between church and state.
教会和国家之间有明确的界限。

|近义词：boundary, division, separation, distinction|反义词:connection, union, integration|词性:noun


demarcate:/ˈdiːmɑːrkeɪt/ /ˈdɛmɑːrkeɪt/| vt. |划定界限；区分；划分|The boundary between the two countries is demarcated by a river. 两国之间的边界以一条河流为界。|例句：

The police demarcated the crime scene with tape.
警察用胶带划定了犯罪现场。

The new policy clearly demarcates the roles of different departments.
新政策明确区分了不同部门的职责。

|近义词：define, delimit, separate, distinguish|反义词:combine, merge, confuse|词性:verb
