evangelicalism:/ˌiːvænˈdʒɛlɪkəlɪzəm/
|n.|福音主义|A worldwide transdenominational movement within Protestant Christianity maintaining that the essence of the gospel consists in the doctrine of salvation by grace through faith in Jesus Christ's atonement. This emphasizes the experience of conversion or regeneration and the importance of evangelization.|The rise of evangelicalism in the 18th century marked a significant shift in religious thought.|近义词：evangelism, revivalism|反义词: secularism, modernism|词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From evangelical + -ism.  "Evangelical" ultimately derives from the Greek word euangelion, meaning "good news" or "gospel."
