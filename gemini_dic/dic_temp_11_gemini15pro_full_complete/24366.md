bring-to-bear:/ˌbrɪŋ tə ˈber/ | v. |施加；运用；瞄准；|To apply or exert (influence, pressure, etc.); to focus or direct (something, such as attention or resources) on a particular problem or situation.|The government brought pressure to bear on the company to improve safety standards. 政府向公司施加压力，要求其提高安全标准。|Synonyms: apply, exert, utilize, employ, deploy|Antonyms: withhold, neglect, ignore|Verb

bring-to-bear:/ˌbrɪŋ tə ˈber/ | v. |瞄准（武器）|To aim (a weapon) at a target.|The artillerymen brought their guns to bear on the enemy position. 炮兵将他们的火炮瞄准了敌人的阵地。|Synonyms: aim, target, direct|Antonyms: miss, avoid|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From the nautical term "bring (a gun) to bear," meaning to maneuver a ship so that a gun could be aimed at a target.  This evolved to the more general sense of applying force or influence.
