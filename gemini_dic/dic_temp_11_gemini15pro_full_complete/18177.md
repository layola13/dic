belabour:/bɪˈleɪbər/ /biˈlābər/| v. |反复强调；抨击；痛打|To explain, criticize, or attack (something) repeatedly or at excessive length; to beat severely.|He belaboured the point about the importance of punctuality.|近义词：overemphasize, harp on, dwell on,  lambast, attack, thrash, pummel|反义词: understate, downplay, ignore, praise, commend|词性:动词

Etymology:
From Middle English *belabouren*, from be- + labour (“to work”).  The original sense was "to beat soundly," later extending to figurative senses of attacking verbally or dwelling on excessively.
