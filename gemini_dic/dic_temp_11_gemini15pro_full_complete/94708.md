impromptu:/ɪmˈprɒmptjuː/ (British), /ɪmˈprɑːmptuː/ (American)| n. adj. adv.|即兴演奏；即兴的；即兴地|A performance or speech given without preparation or rehearsal.  It can also be used as an adjective to describe something done without planning, or as an adverb to describe how something is done.|例句：He gave an impromptu performance on the piano. 他即兴演奏了一曲钢琴。|近义词：offhand, extemporaneous, spontaneous, unrehearsed|反义词：planned, prepared, rehearsed|词性: noun, adjective, adverb

impromptus:/ɪmˈprɒmptjuːz/ (British), /ɪmˈprɑːmptuːz/ (American)| n.|即兴演奏(复数)|Plural form of "impromptu," referring to multiple performances or speeches given without preparation.|例句：The concert included several impromptus by the guest artist.  音乐会包括了客座艺术家的一些即兴演奏。|近义词：improvisations|反义词：planned performances|词性: noun

词源: From French *impromptu*, from Latin *in promptu* "in readiness," from *in* "in" + *promptus* "ready."
