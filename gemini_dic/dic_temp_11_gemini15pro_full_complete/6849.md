anatifer:/əˈnætɪfər/ | n. |  鹅颈藤壶 | A type of barnacle, specifically of the genus *Lepas*, commonly found attached to floating objects or ships' hulls. anatifer:/əˈnætɪfər/ | n. | 藤壶鹅 | A mythical bird believed to hatch from goose barnacles. This belief stemmed from the resemblance of the barnacle's stalk to a bird's neck. | The legend of the anatifer was widespread in the Middle Ages. 藤壶鹅的传说在中世纪广泛流传。| Synonyms: barnacle goose | Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a mythical creature) | Noun
