buy-in:/ˈbaɪɪn/| n.|支持，买进|Agreement or support for an idea or plan. The purchase of a company, especially by the existing management.|例句：We need to get buy-in from the team before we proceed.  在我们继续之前，我们需要得到团队的支持。|近义词：support, agreement, acceptance, backing, endorsement|反义词：opposition, resistance, dissent|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
"Buy-in" originates from the business world, specifically the practice of purchasing a significant stake in a company.  The concept later broadened to encompass the idea of securing support or agreement for an initiative. The informal, contracted form "buyin's" likely emerged in casual conversation and implies a collective sense of support or contributions.  It doesn't have a separate, formal etymology.
