feuding:/ˈfjuːdɪŋ/
n.|不和，世仇，长期争斗|a state of prolonged mutual hostility, typically between two families or communities, characterized by acts of violence or aggression. |The two families have been feuding for generations. 这两个家族世代不和。|近义词：quarreling, fighting, bickering, clashing|反义词：reconciling, harmonizing|词性:名词

feud:/fjuːd/
n.|世仇，宿怨|a prolonged and bitter quarrel or dispute.|Their feud goes back to childhood. 他们的宿怨可以追溯到童年时期。|近义词：vendetta, rivalry, conflict|反义词：harmony, friendship, concord|词性:名词

feud:/fjuːd/
v.|长期争斗，结仇|to be engaged in a prolonged and bitter quarrel or dispute.|The two gangs have been feuding for years. 这两个帮派多年来一直在争斗。|近义词：quarrel, fight, bicker, clash|反义词：reconcile, harmonize, make peace|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *feude, *faide "feud, hostility," from Frankish *faida or some other Germanic source, from Proto-Germanic *faihizō "feud, enmity," from *faih "hostile," related to *fijands "enemy" (source of Old English fēond "enemy, fiend, Satan," Gothic fijands "enemy").
