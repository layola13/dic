improvisate:/ˈɪmprəvaɪˌzeɪt/ /ˈɪmprəvɪˌzeɪt/| v. | 即兴创作，即兴表演 | To create or perform (music, drama, or verse) spontaneously or without preparation. |  He had to improvise a speech at the awards ceremony. 他不得不在颁奖典礼上即兴发表演讲。

|  The musicians improvised a jazz piece. 音乐家们即兴演奏了一段爵士乐。 | 近义词：extemporize, ad-lib | 反义词：rehearse, plan, prepare | 词性: verb

improvisate (verb) 词源:  From Late Latin improvisatus, past participle of improvisare "to do something extempore," from Latin in- "not" + provisus, past participle of providere "to foresee, provide."  来源于晚期拉丁语 improvisatus，是 improvisare 的过去分词，“即兴做某事”，来自拉丁语 in- “不” + provisus，是 providere 的过去分词“预见，提供”。
