bipartitely:/baɪˈpɑːrtaɪtli/| adv.|二分地，分成两部分地|In graph theory, a bipartite graph is one whose vertices can be divided into two disjoint and independent sets, U and V, such that every edge connects a vertex in U to one in V.  A problem can be solved bipartitely by dividing it into two interconnected parts.|The researchers bipartitely analyzed the data, separating the control group from the experimental group. 研究人员将数据二分分析，将对照组与实验组分开。|Near synonyms:dichotomously, separately|Antonym:jointly|Part of Speech: Adverb

Etymology->
From "bipartite" + "-ly." "Bipartite" comes from Latin "bipartitus,"  from "bi-" (two) + "partitus" (divided), past participle of "partiri" (to divide).
