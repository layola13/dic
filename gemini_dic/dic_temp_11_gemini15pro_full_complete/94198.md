imperceptibly:/ˌɪmpərˈsɛptɪbli/ | adv. |不知不觉地，难以察觉地 | In a way that is so slight, gradual, or subtle as not to be perceived. | The days grew longer almost imperceptibly. 白昼不知不觉地变长了。 | 近义词：subtly, gradually, invisibly, unnoticeably | 反义词：perceptibly, noticeably, obviously, visibly | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *imperceptibilis, from in- "not" + percept-, past participle stem of percipere "to perceive" + -ibilis "-ible".
