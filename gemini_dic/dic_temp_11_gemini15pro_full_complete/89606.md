hoods:/hʊdz/
n.
兜帽；头巾；引擎盖；街区；黑帮
A covering for the head and neck, often attached to a coat or jacket; the hinged metal covering over the engine of a motor vehicle; a neighborhood, often associated with a particular group or characteristic, sometimes negative; a group of criminals or gangsters.
The monk wore a dark hood.  和尚戴着深色的兜帽。

The car's hood was dented in the accident.  汽车的引擎盖在事故中凹陷了。

He grew up in a tough hood. 他在一个混乱的街区长大。

The hoods were known for their violent activities.  这些黑帮以其暴力活动而闻名。

近义词:cowls, bonnets (for car hoods), districts, neighborhoods, gangs
反义词: (No direct antonyms for all meanings.  Could be "open" for car hood, "affluent area" for negative neighborhood connotation)
词性:noun


hoods:/hʊdz/
v. (third-person singular simple present hoods, present participle hooding, simple past and past participle hooded)
给…戴上兜帽；覆盖
To cover with a hood.
The falconer hooded the bird.  驯鹰者给鸟戴上了兜帽。

She hooded her eyes with her hand. 她用手遮住了眼睛。

近义词:cover, conceal
反义词:uncover, reveal
词性:verb
