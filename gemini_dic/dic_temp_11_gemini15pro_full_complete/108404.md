lawmaker:/ˈlɔːmeɪkər/ | n. | 立法者；议员 | A person who makes laws, especially a member of a legislative body. |  The lawmakers are debating a new bill. 议员们正在辩论一项新的法案。| legislator, congressman, congresswoman, senator, representative | antonym:  Not applicable (It's difficult to have a direct antonym for a person who makes laws) | noun

Etymology:  law + maker.  "Law" comes from Old English *lagu (plural laga), "law, ordinance, rule, regulation; district governed by the same laws."  "Maker" comes from Old English macere "one who makes," agent noun from macian "to make."
