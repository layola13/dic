mountains:/ˈmaʊntənz/ (US), /ˈmaʊntɪnz/ (UK)| n.|山脉，群山|A large natural elevation of the earth's surface rising abruptly from the surrounding level; a large mass of earth and rock rising above the common level of the earth or adjacent land, usually higher than a hill.|We climbed the highest mountain in the range. 我们爬上了该山脉中最高的山峰。|The mountains were covered in snow. 群山上覆盖着白雪。|近义词：ranges, peaks, hills (when smaller)|反义词：valleys, plains|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *mountaynes*, plural of *mountayne*, from Old French *montaigne*, from Vulgar Latin *montanea* (unattested), from Latin *montāneus* (“of a mountain, mountainous”), from *mons*, *montis* (“mountain”).
