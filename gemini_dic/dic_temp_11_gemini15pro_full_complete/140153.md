offend:/əˈfend/ /ɑːˈfend/| v. |冒犯；得罪；使厌恶；违反|To cause someone to feel upset, angry, or annoyed, typically by something considered rude or disrespectful.  To go against a law, rule, or principle.|I didn't mean to offend anyone with my remarks.  我的话并非有意冒犯任何人。|synonyms:  insult, upset, annoy, anger, irritate, displease, affront, pique, vex, provoke, gall, outrage, shock, disgust, repel, revolt, sicken, nauseate |antonyms:  please, delight, charm, flatter, compliment|verb
