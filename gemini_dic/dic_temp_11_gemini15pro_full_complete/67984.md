fact-finding:/ˈfæktˌfaɪndɪŋ/
fact-finding| adj. n.| 事实调查，真相调查 |The process of gathering information or evidence to establish the facts of a situation. |A fact-finding mission was launched to determine the cause of the accident.  一个事实调查团被派去确定事故原因。|Synonyms: investigation, inquiry, research |Antonyms: cover-up, concealment|adjective/noun


fact-finding:/ˈfæktˌfaɪndɪŋ/
fact-finding| n.| 事实调查，真相调查 |The information or evidence gathered during a fact-finding process. |The fact-finding revealed a serious breach of security. 事实调查揭露了严重的安全漏洞。|Synonyms: findings, results, data |Antonyms: misinformation, disinformation|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From fact + finding (present participle of find).  "Fact" comes from Latin "factum" meaning "something done" or "deed". "Find" comes from Old English "findan" meaning "to discover" or "to come upon".
