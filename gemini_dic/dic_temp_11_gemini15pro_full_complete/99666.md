interlibels:/ˌɪntərˈlaɪbəlz/
n.
相互诽谤; 多次诽谤
The ongoing feud resulted in a series of interlibels between the two families.
这场持续的争执导致两个家族之间发生了一系列相互诽谤。
Etymology:  "inter-" (Latin, between) + "libel" (from Old French "libelle," meaning "little book," later used for defamatory writing).
The word combines the prefix "inter-" indicating reciprocity or mutuality, and "libels," the plural of "libel," meaning a defamatory statement expressed in writing or printing.
Example: The rival politicians engaged in a series of interlibels during the election campaign.  两位竞争的政治家在竞选期间进行了一系列相互诽谤。
Synonyms: mutual defamation, reciprocal slander, cross-libels
Antonyms: praise, commendation, accolades
