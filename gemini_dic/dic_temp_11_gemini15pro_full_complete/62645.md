enfranchisers:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzərz/
n.
授予公民权的人;给予选举权的人
People (or a legislative body) who grant the right to vote to a person or group.
The enfranchisers extended voting rights to all citizens over the age of 18.  授予公民权者将投票权扩大到所有18岁以上的公民。
The 19th Amendment's enfranchisers finally granted women the right to vote. 第19条修正案的授权者最终赋予了妇女投票权。
近义词: empowerers, liberators
反义词: disenfranchisers, oppressors
词性: noun (plural)

enfranchise:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/
v.
给予…公民权（或选举权）
Grant the right to vote to.
The movement sought to enfranchise all citizens regardless of race. 该运动旨在赋予所有公民选举权，不论种族。
Many countries did not enfranchise women until the 20th century. 许多国家直到20世纪才赋予妇女选举权。
近义词: empower, liberate
反义词: disenfranchise, oppress
词性: verb

enfranchisement:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
n.
公民权的授予;选举权的授予
The act of granting the right to vote.
The enfranchisement of women was a landmark achievement. 妇女获得选举权是一项里程碑式的成就。
The country celebrated the enfranchisement of its formerly oppressed population.  这个国家庆祝其以前受压迫的人口获得选举权。
近义词: empowerment, liberation
反义词: disenfranchisement, oppression
词性: noun
