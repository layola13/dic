infantries:/ˈɪnfəntriz/| n. |步兵；步兵部队|Soldiers trained to fight on foot; foot soldiers collectively.|The infantries marched for days through the harsh terrain. 步兵们在崎岖的地形中行军了数日。|The general commanded several infantries in the battle. 将军指挥了战斗中的几支步兵部队。|近义词：foot soldiers, infantrymen|反义词: cavalry, armored forces|词性:名词

词源: From Old French *infanterie (whence also Modern French infanterie), from Italian infanteria, from infante "youth, foot soldier," from Latin infans (genitive infantis) "unable to speak, young," literally "not speaking."  The military sense arose from the fact that young men often served as foot soldiers.
