binding:/ˈbaɪndɪŋ/ | adj. n. | 有约束力的，捆绑的；装订；捆绑 | Something that is binding forces people to do what it says.  A binding is something that ties things together, like the cover of a book. | The contract is legally binding.  这合同具有法律约束力。| Synonyms: obligatory, compulsory, mandatory | Antonyms: non-binding, optional | adjective

binding:/ˈbaɪndɪŋ/ | n. | 装订，捆绑，粘合 | The act of tying something together, or the material used to do so. | The binding on the book is starting to come loose. 书的装订开始松动了。 | Synonyms: fastening, tying | Antonyms: loosening, untying | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English "bindan" (to tie, bind), from Proto-Germanic "*bindanan," from Proto-Indo-European "*bhendh-" (to bind, tie).
