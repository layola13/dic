celestialize:/səˈlɛstiəˌlaɪz/
verb
使神圣化，使升天，使超凡脱俗
To make something celestial; to give something a heavenly or spiritual quality.  To exalt or glorify.
The poet celestialized the beauty of nature in his verses. 诗人在他的诗句中将大自然的美神圣化了。

The ancient Egyptians celestialized their pharaohs after death. 古埃及人在法老死后将他们神圣化。

Synonym: deify, exalt, glorify, spiritualize
Antonym: degrade, demean, earthly, secularize
词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From celestial + -ize.  Celestial comes from Late Latin caelestis  "heavenly," from Latin caelum "sky, heaven."
