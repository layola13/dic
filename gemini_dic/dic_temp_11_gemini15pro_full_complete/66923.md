expendables:/ɪkˈspɛndəblz/| n.|消耗品，易耗品；牺牲品|Items that are intended to be used up and then replaced; people considered unimportant and easily sacrificed. |The army needs a constant supply of expendables like ammunition and bandages.  军队需要持续供应弹药和绷带等消耗品。| The company treats its low-wage workers as expendables.  这家公司把低薪工人当作可牺牲的棋子。|synonyms: consumables, disposables, supplies; sacrifices, pawns|antonyms:  essentials, necessities, valuables|noun

expendable:/ɪkˈspɛndəbl/| adj.|可消耗的；可牺牲的|Able to be used up and then replaced; considered unimportant and easily sacrificed. |Office supplies such as pens and paper are considered expendable.  笔和纸之类的办公用品被认为是消耗品。 |In wartime, unfortunately, some soldiers are seen as expendable.  在战争时期，不幸的是，一些士兵被视为可以牺牲的。|synonyms: consumable, disposable, replaceable; dispensable, unimportant|antonyms: essential, necessary, indispensable, valuable|adjective
