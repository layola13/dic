displacement:/dɪsˈpleɪsmənt/ | n. | 位移，取代，排水量 | the act of moving something from its original place or position. The removal of something from its usual place or position by something which then occupies that place or position. | The earthquake caused a displacement of the Earth's crust. 地震导致地壳位移。 | synonyms: shift, move, relocation, removal, substitution | antonyms: replacement, return, reinstatement | noun

displacement:/dɪsˈpleɪsmənt/ | n. | 排水量 | The weight of water that a ship displaces when afloat, equal to the weight of the ship itself. | The displacement of the new aircraft carrier is over 100,000 tons.  这艘新航母的排水量超过10万吨。 | synonyms: tonnage, capacity | antonyms: N/A | noun

displacement:/dɪsˈpleɪsmənt/ | n. | 心理转移 | In psychology, a defense mechanism in which emotions or impulses are redirected from their original target to a less threatening one. | He displayed displacement by yelling at his dog when he was actually angry with his boss. 他把对老板的怒气发泄在了他的狗身上，这是一种心理转移。 | synonyms: transference, redirection | antonyms: N/A | noun
