anglo-saxons:/ˈæŋɡloʊ ˈsæksənz/
noun
盎格鲁-撒克逊人
People who migrated to Britain from continental Europe (mainly Germany, Denmark, and the Netherlands) in the 5th and 6th centuries AD.  They established several kingdoms and contributed significantly to the development of English language and culture.
The Anglo-Saxons left a rich legacy of literature and art.  盎格鲁-撒克逊人留下了丰富的文学和艺术遗产。
Anglo-Saxons were skilled metalworkers. 盎格鲁撒克逊人是技艺精湛的金属工匠。

近义词:Early English, Germanic tribes
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps) Celts, Britons (referring to the pre-Anglo-Saxon inhabitants of Britain)
词性:noun (plural)
