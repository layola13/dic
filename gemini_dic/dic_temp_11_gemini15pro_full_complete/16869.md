basilects: /ˈbeɪsɪlɛkts/ | n. | 地方方言；基层方言 |  A variety of a language characteristically spoken by the least educated members of a speech community, often deviating from the standard language in pronunciation, grammar, and vocabulary.  It is considered the most divergent from the prestige variety. | The basilects spoken in some Caribbean islands retain many features of West African languages.  加勒比海一些岛屿上使用的基层方言保留了许多西非语言的特征。 |  vernacular, dialect | standard language, acrolect | noun
