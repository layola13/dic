co-respondent:/ˌkəʊrɪˈspɒndənt/ (UK), /ˌkoʊrɪˈspɑːndənt/ (US)| n.|共同被告，（尤指离婚案中）与被告通奸的人|A person cited in a divorce case as having committed adultery with the respondent (the person against whom the divorce petition is filed).|The co-respondent in the divorce case denied all allegations of an affair.|Synonym: paramour, lover, mistress (if female)|Antonym: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From co- + respondent.  "Respondent" comes from Latin *respondentem (nominative respondens), present participle of respondere "to answer," from re- "back" + spondere "to promise solemnly, engage oneself." The prefix "co-" comes from Latin com- "with, together."
