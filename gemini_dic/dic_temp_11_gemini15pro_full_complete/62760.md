engraftment:/ɪnˈɡræftmənt/ | n. |移植, 植入, 嫁接|The process by which transplanted tissue, especially bone marrow, becomes established and starts to grow in a recipient.|The patient's engraftment was successful, and the new bone marrow started producing healthy blood cells.| 近义词: implantation, transplantation, rooting | 反义词: rejection | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From en- + graft + -ment.  "Graft" comes from the Old French word "graffe", meaning a cutting or shoot used for grafting. The prefix "en-" signifies "in" or "into," indicating the act of placing the graft into something.  "-ment" is a suffix forming nouns of action or process.
