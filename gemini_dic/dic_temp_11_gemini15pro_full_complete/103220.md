jetteau:/ʒə.tø/| v. |我扔了，我投掷了 |I threw. This is the first-person singular past tense form of the French verb "jeter," meaning "to throw."| J'ai jeté la balle. (I threw the ball.) | 近义词:lançai (I threw - more literary),  | 反义词:attrapai (I caught), gardai (I kept)| verb

USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Je jette la balle. (I throw the ball.)
Il jette des pierres. (He throws stones.)

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Je suis en train de jeter la balle. (I am throwing the ball.) - This tense is less common in French for simple actions.
Il est en train de jeter des pierres. (He is throwing stones.) - This tense is less common in French for simple actions.

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
J'ai jeté la balle. (I have thrown the ball / I threw the ball.)
Il a jeté des pierres. (He has thrown stones / He threw the stones.)

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
French does not have a direct equivalent to this tense. The present perfect is generally used.

一般过去时 (Simple Past):
Je jetai la balle. (I threw the ball.) This tense is less common in spoken French.
Il jeta des pierres. (He threw stones.) This tense is less common in spoken French.

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
Je jetais en train de jeter la balle. (I was throwing the ball.)
Il était en train de jeter des pierres. (He was throwing stones.)

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
J'avais jeté la balle. (I had thrown the ball.)
Il avait jeté des pierres. (He had thrown stones.)

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
French does not have a direct equivalent to this tense. The past perfect is generally used.

一般将来时 (Simple Future):
Je jetterai la balle. (I will throw the ball.)
Il jettera des pierres. (He will throw stones.)

将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
Je serai en train de jeter la balle. (I will be throwing the ball.)
Il sera en train de jeter des pierres. (He will be throwing stones.)

将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
J'aurai jeté la balle. (I will have thrown the ball.)
Il aura jeté des pierres. (He will have thrown stones.)

将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
French does not have a direct equivalent to this tense. The future perfect is generally used.


虚拟语气:
Que je jette la balle. (That I throw the ball - subjunctive)
Il faudrait que je jette la balle. (I should throw the ball - subjunctive)

被动语态:
La balle est jetée par moi. (The ball is thrown by me.) - This is less common in French.
Les pierres sont jetées par lui. (The stones are thrown by him.) - This is less common in French.

疑问句:
As-tu jeté la balle ? (Did you throw the ball?)
A-t-il jeté les pierres ? (Did he throw the stones?)

否定句:
Je n'ai pas jeté la balle. (I didn't throw the ball.)
Il n'a pas jeté les pierres. (He didn't throw the stones.)

注意事项:
*  "jetteau" is specifically a past tense form and doesn't exist in other tenses in isolation. The different tenses use conjugations of the verb "jeter."
* French uses different sentence structures and tenses than English, so direct translations don't always capture the nuance.
* The continuous tenses are less common in French than in English. The simple tenses often convey the same meaning.
*  The passive voice is less frequently used in French than in English.
