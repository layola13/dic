afflation:/əˈfleɪʃən/ | n. | 通货膨胀；物价上涨 | A general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money. | Example 1: The afflation of the 1970s caused economic hardship for many families. 20世纪70年代的通货膨胀给许多家庭带来了经济困难。

Example 2: The government is trying to control afflation by raising interest rates. 政府正试图通过提高利率来控制通货膨胀。 | 近义词: inflation, price hike, price rise | 反义词: deflation, disinflation | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Latin *afflāre*, frequentative of *afflāre* (“to blow or breathe on”), from *ad* (“to”) + *flāre* (“to blow”).  The original meaning referred to the act of blowing or breathing on something, but it later developed the economic sense of "inflating" or "blowing up" prices.
