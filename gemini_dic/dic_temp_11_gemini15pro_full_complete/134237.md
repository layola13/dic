noninfallibly:/ˌnɒnɪnˈfælɪbli/ | adv. | 非绝对可靠地，并非万无一失地 | Not infallibly; in a way that is not absolutely reliable or certain.  | The weatherman predicted sunshine, but noninfallibly, it rained. 天气预报员预测会是晴天，但并非绝对可靠，结果下雨了。 |  The system, while advanced, does not operate noninfallibly.  虽然系统很先进，但运行起来并非万无一失。 | 近义词: imperfectly, faultily, erringly | 反义词: infallibly, perfectly, certainly | 词性: 副词

词源:
From non- (not) + infallibly (in a way that is incapable of making mistakes or being wrong).  "Infallibly" comes from Late Latin infallibilis, from in- (not) + fallere (to deceive, err) + -bilis (capable of being).
