The general formula for paraffins is $C_nH_{2n+2}$, where 'n' represents the number of carbon atoms.  The simplest paraffin is methane ($CH_4$), followed by ethane ($C_2H_6$), propane ($C_3H_8$),butane ($C_4H_{10}$), and so on. As the number of carbon atoms increases, the properties of paraffins change.  Lower molecular weight paraffins are gases at room temperature, while higher molecular weight paraffins are liquids or solids.historical name meaning "little affinity."
