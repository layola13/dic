ebullition:/ˌɛbəˈlɪʃən/ | n. | 沸腾；奔腾；激动 | The ebullition of the crowd after the winning goal was incredible. | The rapid conversion of liquid to vapour, usually with the formation of bubbles.  The crowd was in a state of ebullition after their team won the championship. | 近义词: boiling, effervescence, ferment | 反义词: calmness, tranquility | 词性: noun

词源: From Latin *ēbullitiō*, from ēbullīre (“to boil up, bubble out”), from ex (“out”) + bullīre (“to boil, bubble”).
