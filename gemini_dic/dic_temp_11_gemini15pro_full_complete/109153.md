legislatrix: /ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪtrɪks/
noun
女立法者
A female legislator.  A woman who makes laws.
Example: The newly elected legislatrix championed environmental protection.  新当选的女立法者倡导环境保护。

Example:  The legislatrix introduced a bill to improve public education.  这位女立法者提出了一项改善公共教育的法案。

Synonym: lawmaker, legislator, congresswoman (if in Congress)
Antonym:  N/A (There's no direct opposite of someone who makes laws.)
Etymology:  From Latin *legislatrix*, feminine form of *legislator*, from *legislat-* (past participle stem of *legislare* "to legislate") + *-trix* (feminine agent suffix).  The root *legislare* comes from *lex* "law" + *-lare* (frequentative suffix).
词性: 名词
