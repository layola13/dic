hmg:/eɪtʃ em dʒiː/ |abbr.| human menopausal gonadotropin; 人绝经期促性腺激素|Human menopausal gonadotropin (hMG) is a purified preparation of gonadotropins extracted from the urine of postmenopausal women. It contains follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) and luteinizing hormone (LH). hMG is used to treat infertility in both men and women.|Doctors prescribed hMG to stimulate ovulation.|Synonyms: menotropins|Antonyms: N/A| abbreviation
