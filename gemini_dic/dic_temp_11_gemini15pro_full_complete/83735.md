hail-mary-pass:/ˌheɪl ˈmɛəri ˈpæs/ | n. | 孤注一掷；最后一搏；绝望一试 | A long, high-risk pass in American football, typically made in desperation near the end of a game.  It is a desperate, last-ditch attempt to win. |  With seconds left on the clock, the quarterback threw a Hail Mary pass into the end zone.  比赛还剩几秒钟时，四分卫向端区投出了一记孤注一掷的长传。| synonyms: last-ditch effort, desperate attempt, long shot | antonyms: safe bet, sure thing | noun

hail-mary:/ˌheɪl ˈmɛəri/ | n. | 万福玛利亚；孤注一掷 | (1) A prayer to the Virgin Mary. (2) A desperate, last-ditch attempt. | He muttered a Hail Mary before jumping out of the plane. 他跳下飞机前低声念了一句万福玛利亚。 | synonyms: last resort, desperate plea | antonyms: careful planning, calculated risk | noun
