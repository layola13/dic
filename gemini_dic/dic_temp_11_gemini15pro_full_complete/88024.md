high-wire:/ˈhaɪˌwaɪər/ | n. | 高空钢丝 | A wire or rope stretched high above the ground, used by acrobats. | He walked across the high-wire blindfolded. 他蒙着眼睛走过高空钢丝。 |  The circus high-wire act was breathtaking. 马戏团的高空钢丝表演令人叹为观止。 | 近义词: tightrope | 反义词:  (没有直接的反义词) | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "high" + "wire".  "High" meaning elevated, and "wire" referring to a metal strand.  The compound term "high-wire" specifically refers to a wire suspended high above the ground.
