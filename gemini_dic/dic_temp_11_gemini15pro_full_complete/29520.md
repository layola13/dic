catatonia: /ˌkætəˈtəʊniə/ (British) /ˌkætəˈtoʊniə/ (American)| n. |紧张症，僵直症|A state of immobility and unresponsiveness, often associated with schizophrenia or other mental illnesses.  It can involve stupor, mutism, and bizarre postures.|The patient exhibited catatonia, remaining motionless for hours. 病人表现出紧张症，一动不动地保持了几个小时。|Near synonyms: stupor, rigidity, immobility|Near antonyms: mobility, responsiveness, activity|noun
