coxswain:/ˈkɒksən/ (UK), /ˈkɑːksən/ (US)| n.|舵手；艇长|A coxswain is the person in charge of a boat, especially a racing boat, who steers and gives instructions to the rowers.|The coxswain yelled at the crew to row faster. 艇长大声喊着让队员们划得更快些。|近义词：helmsman, steersman|反义词: crew, rower|词性: noun


coxswains:/ˈkɒksənz/ (UK), /ˈkɑːksənz/ (US)| n.|舵手们；艇长们|Plural form of coxswain, referring to multiple people who steer and command boats.|The coxswains met to discuss race strategies.  艇长们开会讨论比赛策略。|近义词：helmsmen, steersmen|反义词: crews, rowers|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "cock" (small boat) + "swain" (servant, boy). Originally referred to a boatman in charge of a ship's small boat.
