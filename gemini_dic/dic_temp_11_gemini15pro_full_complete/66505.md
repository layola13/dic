exfoliated: /ɛksˈfoʊliˌeɪtɪd/ /ɪksˈfəʊliˌeɪtɪd/
adjective
脱落的; 去角质的
removed in scales or layers; having had the outer layer removed.
例句：Exfoliated skin cells can clog pores.  脱落的皮肤细胞会堵塞毛孔。

例句：The exfoliated rock face revealed layers of different minerals.  剥落的岩壁展现出不同矿物的层理。

近义词：peeled, shed, sloughed
反义词：intact, adhered

词性: adjective


exfoliate: /ɛksˈfoʊliˌeɪt/ /ɪksˈfəʊliˌeɪt/
verb
脱落; 去角质
(of a material) come off in scales or layers.  Remove the outer layer of (something).
例句：The bark of the tree exfoliates in flakes.  这棵树的树皮呈片状剥落。

例句：She uses a scrub to exfoliate her skin. 她用磨砂膏去角质。

近义词：peel, shed, slough
反义词：adhere, stick


exfoliation: /ɛksˌfoʊliˈeɪʃən/ /ɪksˌfəʊliˈeɪʃən/
noun
脱落; 去角质
the process of being exfoliated or the state of being exfoliated.
例句：Exfoliation of the skin helps remove dead cells.  皮肤去角质有助于去除死皮细胞。

例句：The exfoliation of the rock is caused by weathering. 岩石的剥落是由风化作用引起的。

近义词：peeling, shedding, sloughing
反义词：adhesion


词源：From Latin exfoliāre ‘strip of leaves’, from ex- ‘out, from’ + folium ‘leaf’.
