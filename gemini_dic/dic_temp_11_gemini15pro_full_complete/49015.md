degrading:/dɪˈɡreɪdɪŋ/
adjective
降低身份的；丢脸的；有辱人格的
causing someone to feel that they have lost respect for themselves
例句：It was a degrading experience. 这是一次有辱人格的经历。
例句：He found it degrading to have to ask for money. 他觉得不得不乞讨很丢脸。
近义词：humiliating, shameful, demeaning
反义词：uplifting, ennobling
词性：adjective

degrade:/dɪˈɡreɪd/
verb
降低…的身份；使…丢脸；降低…的质量；退化；降解
1. to show or treat someone in a way that makes them seem not worth any respect
2. to make something become worse, especially in quality
3. (of a substance) to change to a simpler chemical form
例句：Pornography degrades women. 色情作品贬低女性。
例句：The landscape has been degraded by years of industrial pollution. 多年的工业污染已经使这片土地退化。
近义词：debase, demean, cheapen, deteriorate, decompose
反义词：elevate, enhance, improve
词性：verb

词源：From Late Latin degradare "to reduce in rank," from de- "down" + gradus "step, rank."
