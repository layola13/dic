intimidation:/ɪnˌtɪmɪˈdeɪʃn/ | n. | 恐吓，威胁 | The act of frightening or threatening someone, usually in order to persuade them to do something that they do not want to do. | The witness refused to testify due to intimidation from the defendant's family.  证人由于受到被告家人的恐吓而拒绝作证。 | synonyms: coercion, bullying, menace, threat | antonyms: encouragement, support, reassurance | noun

intimidate:/ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪt/ | v. | 恐吓，威胁 | To frighten or threaten someone, usually in order to persuade them to do something that they do not want to do. |  He tried to intimidate the other players into passing the ball to him. 他试图恐吓其他球员，让他们把球传给他。 | synonyms: frighten, menace, terrorize, browbeat | antonyms: encourage, reassure, support | verb
