out-of-sorts:/ˌaʊt əv ˈsɔːrts/ | adj. | 心情不佳；身体不适 | Feeling slightly unwell or irritable. | I've been feeling out-of-sorts all day. 我一整天都感觉不舒服。|  I woke up this morning feeling out-of-sorts and decided to stay home. 我今天早上醒来感觉不舒服，决定待在家里。 | 近义词: unwell, grumpy, irritable, moody | 反义词: well, happy, cheerful | 词性: 形容词

词源:  The phrase "out of sorts" originated in the 16th century. "Sorts" in this context refers to a proper or usual condition or order. Thus, "out of sorts" meant not in one's usual or proper condition, either physically or mentally.
