moth-eaten:/ˈmɔθˌiːtn/ | adj. | 虫蛀的，破旧的 | Damaged or worn away by moths or other insects, giving a shabby appearance.  It can also be used figuratively to describe something outdated or obsolete. |  The old house was filled with moth-eaten furniture.  这栋老房子里满是虫蛀的家具。 | synonyms: dilapidated, shabby, threadbare, worn-out | antonyms: pristine, new, immaculate | adjective

moth-eaten:/ˈmɔθˌiːtn/ | adj. | 过时的，陈腐的 | Figuratively used to describe something outdated or no longer relevant. | His moth-eaten ideas about economics are no longer applicable in today's world. 他过时的经济学理念在当今世界已不再适用。| synonyms: outdated, obsolete, antiquated, archaic | antonyms: modern, contemporary, current, up-to-date | adjective
