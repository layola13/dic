ghost:/ɡoʊst/ (US) /ɡəʊst/ (UK)| n. |鬼魂，幽灵；鬼影；一丝，一点儿|a supernatural apparition, specter, or phantom; a faint trace or suggestion|The old house is said to be haunted by a ghost.  这栋老房子据说闹鬼。|synonyms: specter, phantom, apparition, wraith, spirit|antonyms: reality, corporeality|noun

ghost:/ɡoʊst/ (US) /ɡəʊst/ (UK)| vt. |为…捉刀，代笔|to write for someone else, who then takes the credit|He ghosted the autobiography of the famous actress. 他为那位著名女演员代笔自传。|synonyms: author (for someone else), write (for someone else)|antonyms:  (no direct antonym, perhaps "plagiarize" as a contrasting concept)|verb
