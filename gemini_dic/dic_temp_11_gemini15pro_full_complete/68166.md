fainer:/ˈfeɪnər/| adj.|更乐意的，更高兴的，更好的|Happier, more willing, better.  It's a comparative adjective, meaning "more fain."|She was fainer to stay home than go out in the cold. 她宁愿待在家里也不愿出去挨冻。|近义词：keener, more willing, happier, better|反义词：reluctant, unwilling, worse|词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *fainer, comparative of *fayne, from Old English fægen (“glad, joyful, pleasant”), from Proto-Germanic *fagainaz (“glad, joyful”), from Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰai- (“to rejoice”). Cognate with Scots fain (“glad, joyful, eager”), West Frisian fein (“fine, beautiful”), Dutch fijn (“fine, delicate”), German fein (“fine, delicate”).
