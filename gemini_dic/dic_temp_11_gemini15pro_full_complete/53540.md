disavowedly:/ˌdɪsəˈvaʊɪdli/
adverb
公开否认地；不承认地
In a manner that involves disavowal; openly denying responsibility for or connection with.
He disavowedly shrugged, indicating he had nothing to do with the incident. 他公开否认地耸了耸肩，表示他与这件事无关。
The government disavowedly rejected the accusations of corruption. 政府公开否认了腐败指控。
近义词: openly, explicitly, expressly
反义词: tacitly, implicitly, secretly
词性: adverb

词源:
From disavow + -ed + -ly.  Disavow comes from the Old French *desavouer, from des- (expressing reversal) + avouer "to avow, confess," from Latin advocare "to summon, call to one's aid."
