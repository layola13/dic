chromatin:/ˈkrəʊmətɪn/ (US) /ˈkroʊmətɪn/ (UK)| n. |染色质|The material of which the chromosomes of organisms other than bacteria (i.e., eukaryotes) are composed. It consists of protein, RNA, and DNA.|Chromatin condenses to form chromosomes during cell division. 染色质在细胞分裂过程中浓缩形成染色体。|Chromatin structure plays a vital role in gene regulation. 染色质结构在基因调控中起着至关重要的作用。|synonyms:  nucleosome fiber, chromosome material|antonyms: N/A|noun
