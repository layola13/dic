myrosin:/ˈmaɪrəsɪn/
n.
黑芥子酶,硫葡萄糖苷酶
An enzyme found in plants of the family Brassicaceae (such as mustard, cabbage, broccoli, and radishes) that catalyzes the hydrolysis of glucosinolates to produce isothiocyanates, which are responsible for the pungent flavor and aroma of these plants.
Myrosinase plays a crucial role in the plant's defense mechanism against herbivores. 黑芥子酶在植物抵御食草动物的防御机制中起着至关重要的作用。
Myrosin is released when plant tissues are damaged. 当植物组织受损时，黑芥子酶就会被释放出来。
Synonyms: myrosinase, thioglucoside glucohydrolase
Antonyms: None.
