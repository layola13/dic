jud:/dʒʌd/ | n. | 法官，裁判员 | A public official appointed to decide cases in a court of law. |  The jud sentenced the criminal to five years in prison. 法官判处罪犯五年徒刑。| synonym: magistrate, justice, arbiter | antonym: defendant, accused | noun


Etymology:
From Old French juge, from Latin iudex (genitive iudicis) "judge, juror," from ius "right, law" + dicere "to say."
