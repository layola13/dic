knobbling:/ˈnɒblɪŋ/| v.|暗中操纵，搞鬼，贿赂 (尤指为阻止其获胜而对马下药)|To interfere with someone or something in a secret or dishonest way, often involving bribery, especially to prevent them from succeeding. |The police suspect that the racehorse was nobbled before the big race. 警方怀疑这匹赛马在大赛前被人做了手脚。|近义词：fixing, rigging, manipulating, tampering|反义词: promoting, supporting|词性:动词

knobbling:/ˈnɑːblɪŋ/| v.|暗中操纵，搞鬼，贿赂 (尤指为阻止其获胜而对马下药)|To interfere with someone or something in a secret or dishonest way, often involving bribery, especially to prevent them from succeeding. |Someone tried to nobble the key witness before the trial. 有人试图在审判前贿赂主要证人。|近义词：sabotaging, undermining|反义词: assisting, aiding|词性:动词


词源:
From knob (n.) + -le (frequentative suffix) + -ing (present participle suffix). Originally referred to striking a horse on the legs with a knobbed stick to lame it.
