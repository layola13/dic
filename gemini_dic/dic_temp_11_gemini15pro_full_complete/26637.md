cacochymic:/ˌkækəˈkɪmɪk/| adj.|体液失调的；脾气坏的；恶劣的|Having or relating to a disordered state of the body fluids or humors, formerly believed to be the cause of disease; ill-tempered; bad.|The cacochymic patient complained of a variety of ailments.|近义词：dyspeptic, bilious, choleric, irascible, splenetic|反义词：healthy, good-humored, genial|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *cacochymicus*, from Greek *kakochymos* "having bad juices," from *kakos* "bad" + *chymos* "juice."
