lares: /ˈlɛəriːz/, /ˈlɑːreɪz/ | n. | 家庭守护神，家神 | Roman household deities, usually represented by small statues, believed to protect the home and family.  They were often placed near the hearth or in a special shrine. | The family prayed to their lares for protection and good fortune.  这个家庭向他们的家神祈祷以求保护和好运。 |  household gods, deities, penates |  None | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *lares*, plural of *lar* ("home, house, dwelling").  Related to Latin *lases* ("spirits of the dead").
