misbegot:/ˌmɪsbɪˈɡɒt/ (US) /ˌmɪsbɪˈɡɒt/ (UK)| adj.|私生的；孽种；不幸的；畸形的|Born out of wedlock; ill-conceived; wretched; deformed.  It implies something that is badly formed or unlucky, often used in a literary or humorous context.|例句：The misbegot plan was doomed to fail from the start. 这个糟糕的计划从一开始就注定要失败。|近义词：illegitimate, bastard, ill-conceived, unfortunate|反义词:legitimate, fortunate|词性:形容词

misbegotten:/ˌmɪsbɪˈɡɒtn/ (US) /ˌmɪsbɪˈɡɒtn/ (UK)| adj.|私生的；孽种；不幸的；畸形的|(Past participle of misbeget, used more commonly than misbegot)  Having been badly conceived or planned; illegitimate.  Similar in meaning to misbegot, but often used to describe abstract things like plans or ideas.|例句：The project was a misbegotten attempt to merge two incompatible systems.  这个项目是试图合并两个不兼容系统的错误尝试。|近义词：ill-conceived, ill-fated, unfortunate, flawed|反义词:well-conceived, fortunate, successful|词性:形容词


词源:
From Middle English *misbigeten, from Old English misbegietan (“to get wrongly, obtain unlawfully, misbeget”), from mis- (“wrongly”) + begietan (“to get, obtain, beget”).
