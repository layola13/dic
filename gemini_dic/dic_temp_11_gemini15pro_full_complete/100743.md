intransitivenesses:/ˌɪntrænˈsɪtɪvnɪsɪz/ | n. |不及物性；非及物性|The quality or state of being intransitive.  The property of a verb that does not take a direct object.|The intransitivenesses of certain verbs can make them difficult to use in some constructions.|近义词:intransitivity|反义词:transitivity|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From intransitive + -ness.  Intransitive comes from Late Latin intransitivus, from in- "not" + transitivus "passing over," from transitus "a passing over."
