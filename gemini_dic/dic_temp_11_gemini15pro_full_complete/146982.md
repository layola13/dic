overjudicious:/ˌoʊvərˌdʒuːˈdɪʃəs/| adj.|过度判断的，过于审慎的|Excessively judicious; too careful or cautious in making judgments or decisions.  Characterized by an excessive or undue reliance on judgment or discretion.|He was so overjudicious in his investments that he missed out on several lucrative opportunities. 他在投资方面过于谨慎，以至于错过了几个赚钱的机会。|Synonyms: overcautious, overscrupulous, hypercritical, fastidious|Antonyms: rash, impulsive, reckless, careless|Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + judicious.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much."  "Judicious" comes from the Latin "iudiciosus," meaning "prudent" or "wise," derived from "iudicium," meaning "judgment."
