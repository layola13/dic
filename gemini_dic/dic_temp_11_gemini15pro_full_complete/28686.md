cark:/kɑːrk/ | v. n. | (v.) 焦虑，担忧；(n.) 焦虑，担忧 | (v.) To be burdened with anxieties or worries; to worry excessively. (n.) Anxiety; worry; burden of care. | (v.) He carked over his financial problems. 他为自己的经济问题而担忧。  (n.) The cark of responsibility weighed heavily on her. 责任的重担压得她喘不过气。 | (v.) worry, fret, agonize (n.) worry, anxiety, burden | (v.) relax, disregard (n.) peace, tranquility | verb, noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *carken, likely from Old Norse *karkr (“burden, load”), from Proto-Germanic *karkaz (“burden, load”).  Related to Dutch kar (“cart, wagon”), German Karren (“cart, handcart”).  The semantic shift from "burden, load" to "worry, anxiety" is natural.
