oscillative: /ˈɒsɪlətɪv/ (British) /ˈɑːsɪleɪtɪv/ (American)
| adj. | 振荡的，振动的 | Characterized by oscillation; swinging back and forth. | The pendulum's movement was oscillative. 钟摆的运动是振荡的。 | The oscillative motion of the swing was soothing. 秋千的振荡运动令人舒缓。 | 近义词: fluctuating, vibrating, wavering, pulsating | 反义词: steady, stable, fixed, constant | 词性: 形容词

oscillate: /ˈɒsɪleɪt/ (British) /ˈɑːsɪleɪt/ (American)
| v. | 振荡，摆动 | To swing back and forth with a regular rhythm. | The fan oscillated, spreading the cool air around the room. 风扇摆动，将冷空气散布到房间各处。 |  His feelings oscillated between hope and despair. 他的心情在希望和绝望之间摇摆不定。| 近义词: swing, sway, fluctuate, vibrate | 反义词: stabilize, steady, fix | 词性: 动词

oscillation: /ˌɒsɪˈleɪʃn/ (British) /ˌɑːsɪˈleɪʃn/ (American)
| n. | 振荡，摆动 | The act or state of swinging back and forth with a regular rhythm. | The oscillation of the stock market made investors nervous. 股市的震荡让投资者感到紧张。| The regular oscillation of the waves was hypnotic. 波浪有规律的波动令人催眠。 | 近义词: fluctuation, vibration, swing, wavering | 反义词: stability, steadiness, fixity | 词性: 名词


词源: From Latin oscillatus, past participle of oscillare (“to swing”), from oscillum (“swing”).
