gloppier:/ˈɡlɒpiər/ (UK) /ˈɡlɑːpiər/ (US)| adj.|更粘稠的，更糊状的|Comparative form of "gloppy," meaning more sticky, thick, or viscous. It describes a substance that is even less fluid than something already considered gloppy.|The batter was much gloppier than I expected, making it difficult to pour.  面糊比我预料的要粘稠得多，很难倒出来。

The mud after the rain was gloppier than usual, making it hard to walk through. 雨后的泥巴比平时更粘稠，很难走过去。|近义词：stickier, gooier, thicker, more viscous|反义词：thinner, runnier, less viscous|词性:形容词

gloppy:/ˈɡlɒpi/ (UK) /ˈɡlɑːpi/ (US)| adj.|粘稠的，糊状的|Describes a substance that is sticky, thick, or viscous, often unpleasantly so.|The glue was too gloppy to spread evenly. 胶水太粘稠了，无法均匀涂抹。

The pudding had a strange, gloppy texture. 布丁有一种奇怪的糊状质地。|近义词：sticky, gooey, thick, viscous|反义词：thin, runny, watery|词性:形容词


词源:  "Gloppy" likely originated as an imitative word, echoing the sound of a thick substance being moved or manipulated.  It first appeared in the late 19th century.
