galleys:/ˈɡæliz/| n.|船上的厨房; (印刷厂的) 排字房;|A galley is the kitchen on a ship or aircraft.  A galley was also a long, low ship with sails and oars, used in ancient times.|The galley was small and cramped, but the food was surprisingly good. 船上的厨房很小很拥挤，但食物却出奇地好。|The printer's galleys were filled with type. 印刷厂的排字房里摆满了铅字。|synonyms: kitchen (on a ship/aircraft), ship (historical), workspace (printing)|antonyms: (no direct antonyms, could consider "dining room" for ship context, "modern printing press" for historical context)|noun


galley:/ˈɡæli/| n.|单层甲板大帆船|A galley was a type of ship propelled mainly by oars, but also having sails.|Ancient Roman galleys were powerful warships. 古罗马的单层甲板大帆船是强大的战舰。|The galley slaves rowed tirelessly. 划桨奴隶不知疲倦地划船。|synonyms: warship, bireme, trireme|antonyms: (no direct antonyms, perhaps "modern warship")|noun
