buccaneerish:/ˌbʌkəˈnɪərɪʃ/
adjective
海盗似的；冒险的；狂放不羁的
Resembling or suggestive of a buccaneer, especially in being bold, adventurous, or recklessly daring.  Characterized by a swaggering, adventurous, or slightly unscrupulous manner.
He had a buccaneerish grin. 他带着海盗式的笑容。
Her buccaneerish spirit led her to a life of travel and adventure. 她那海盗般冒险的精神引领她走向了充满旅行和冒险的人生。

近义词:piratical, adventurous, daring, swaggering, roguish, dashing
反义词:timid, cautious, conventional, restrained, law-abiding
词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *boucanier*, "pirate,"  originally referring to pirates of the Caribbean who smoked meat on a *boucan* (a wooden frame for smoking meat), from the Taino word *buccan*.
