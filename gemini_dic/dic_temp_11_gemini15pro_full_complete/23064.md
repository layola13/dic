bound's:/baʊndz/
noun
界限；范围
The dog ran to the bounds of the garden. 狗跑到花园的边界。
limit, boundary, extent
constraint, restriction


bound's:/baʊndz/
noun (usually plural)
（尤指某人或组织的）责任，义务
It's within the bounds of possibility that he could win. 他有可能获胜。
duty, responsibility, obligation
freedom, exemption


bound's:/baʊndz/
verb (third person singular present)
跳跃；弹回
The ball bounded across the court. 球弹过球场。
jump, leap, spring
crawl, walk


bound's:/baʊndz/
adjective
肯定的；极有可能的；必定的
It's bound to rain soon.  肯定很快就要下雨了。
certain, sure, definite
unlikely, improbable


Etymology: From Middle English *bounden*, *bunden*, from Old Norse *búinn* (past participle of *búa* (“to prepare, make ready, dwell”)), from Proto-Germanic *būaną* (“to dwell, inhabit”).
