allies: /ˈælaɪz/ | n. | 同盟者，盟友 | People, groups, or nations that have joined together for mutual benefit or to achieve a common purpose. |例句：The US and the UK were allies during World War II. 美国和英国在第二次世界大战期间是盟友。

例句：During the Cold War, NATO allies faced the threat of Soviet expansion. 在冷战期间，北约盟国面临苏联扩张的威胁。

近义词：partners, associates, friends, confederates | 反义词: enemies, adversaries, opponents | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From Old French *alier "to bind, unite," from Latin alligare "to bind to," from ad- "to" + ligare "to bind."
