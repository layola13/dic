knee-jerk:/ˈniː dʒɜːrk/| n. adj.|膝跳反射；下意识的反应；|A sudden involuntary reflex kick of the lower leg in response to a sharp tap on the patellar tendon, located just below the kneecap.  Also used figuratively to describe an automatic, unthinking reaction.|The politician's knee-jerk reaction to the scandal was to deny everything.|Synonyms: automatic, instinctive, reflex, unthinking, spontaneous|Antonyms: considered, deliberate, reasoned, thoughtful|noun, adjective

knee-jerks:/ˈniː dʒɜːrks/| n.|膝跳反射（复数）；下意识的反应（复数）|Plural form of knee-jerk, referring to multiple instances of the reflex or unthinking reactions.|His knee-jerks were tested by the doctor.  The media's knee-jerks to the news were predictable and unhelpful.|Synonyms: automatic reactions, instinctive responses, reflexes|Antonyms: considered responses, deliberate actions|noun
