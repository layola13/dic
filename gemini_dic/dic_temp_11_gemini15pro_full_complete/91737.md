hyperdemocratic:/ˌhaɪpərˌdɛməˈkrætɪk/| adj.|过度民主的；极端民主的|Characterized by an excessive or exaggerated form of democracy, often to the point of inefficiency or instability.  It implies a system where democratic principles are taken to an extreme, potentially undermining the effectiveness of governance. |The political scientist argued that the country's hyperdemocratic system, with its constant referendums and citizen initiatives, had led to political paralysis.|近义词:ultrademocratic, excessively democratic|反义词:authoritarian, undemocratic|词性:形容词

Etymology:
* hyper- (prefix): from Greek *hyper*, meaning "over, beyond, excessive"
* democratic: from French *démocratique*, ultimately from Greek *dēmokratikos* "democratic," from *dēmos* "people" + *kratos* "power."
