chronicle: /ˈkrɒnɪk(ə)l/ (UK) /ˈkrɑːnɪk(ə)l/ (US)| n. vt.| 编年史，记录；记述|A factual written account of important or historical events in the order of their occurrence. |The book chronicles the writer's life during the war. 这本书记录了作者在战争期间的生活。|近义词：record, history, annals, account|反义词: fiction, fabrication|词性:noun, verb

chronicle: /ˈkrɒnɪk(ə)l/ (UK) /ˈkrɑːnɪk(ə)l/ (US)| vt.| 记录，把…载入编年史|To record (a series of events) in a factual and detailed way.|The journalist chronicled the events leading up to the election. 记者记录了导致选举的事件。|近义词：record, document, report, narrate|反义词: ignore, suppress|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *chronique* (Modern French *chronique*), from Late Latin *chronica*, from Greek *khronika* (plural of *khronikon* 'annals'), neuter of *khronikos* 'of time,' from *khronos* 'time.'
