buenos:/ˈbwɛnos/ |adj.| good, fine (masculine plural) | Used to describe masculine plural nouns in Spanish.  It means "good" or "fine." | Los buenos tiempos. (The good times.) | Near synonyms: excelentes (excellent), magníficos (magnificent) | Antonyms: malos (bad), terribles (terrible) | adjective

Etymology: From Latin "bonus" (good).

被动语态:

否定句:
Los tomates no son buenos. (The tomatoes are not good.)

PHRASE->

*Not commonly used in phrases as a standalone word.  It primarily modifies nouns.*  However, it's part of common greetings like:

1. ¡Buenos días! (Good morning!)
2. ¡Buenas tardes! (Good afternoon/evening!)
3. ¡Buenas noches! (Good night!)
4. ¡Buenos Aires! (Buenos Aires - literally "good airs")
5. ¡Qué tengas un buen día! (Have a good day!)


注意事项:
"Buenos" is the masculine plural form.  The feminine plural is "buenas."  The singular forms are "bueno" (masculine) and "buena" (feminine).
