mind-blowing:/ˈmaɪndˌbloʊɪŋ/| adj.|令人极度兴奋的；令人印象深刻的；令人感到震撼的|Extremely impressive, exciting, or shocking.|The special effects in the movie were mind-blowing.  这部电影的特效令人叹为观止。|Synonyms: astonishing, amazing, incredible, phenomenal, stunning|Antonyms: underwhelming, boring, mundane|Adjective

Etymology->
"mind-blowing" is a compound adjective formed from "mind" and "blowing".  "Mind" refers to the intellect or thoughts, while "blowing" in this context suggests something so overwhelming it metaphorically "blows your mind".  The term became popular in the 1960s, likely influenced by the psychedelic drug culture.
