conscript:/kənˈskrɪpt/ |v. adj. n. |征召，应征入伍者，被征召的|To force someone to serve in the army.  Enrolled in compulsory service.|例句：He was conscripted into the army at the age of 18. 他18岁时被征召入伍。|近义词：draft, enlist, recruit|反义词:discharge, release|词性:verb

conscript:/ˈkɒnskrɪpt/ |adj. |被征召的|Enrolled in compulsory service.|例句：Conscript soldiers were often poorly trained. 被征召的士兵通常训练不足。|近义词：drafted, enlisted|反义词:volunteer|词性:adjective

conscript:/ˈkɒnskrɪpt/ |n. |应征入伍者|A person who has been forced to serve in the army.|例句：The conscripts were unhappy about being sent to war. 应征入伍者对被派去打仗很不满。|近义词：draftee, recruit|反义词:volunteer|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin conscriptus, past participle of conscribere (“to enroll, enlist”), from con- (“together”) + scribere (“to write”).
