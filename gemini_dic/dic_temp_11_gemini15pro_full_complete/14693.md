awakeners:/əˈweɪkənərz/
|n.|唤醒者；觉醒者|People or things that cause someone to become aware of something or to feel a particular emotion. |The book served as an awakener for many people, opening their eyes to social injustices. 这本书对许多人来说起到了唤醒的作用，使他们认识到社会的不公。|近义词: catalysts, instigators, enlighteners|反义词: pacifiers, suppressors|词性: 名词

词源:
From awaken + -er.  "Awaken" comes from Old English *awæcnan, from *awæccan "to awake, arouse from sleep," from the Proto-Germanic *wakjanan "to watch, be awake" (source also of Old Saxon wakon, Old Frisian wakia, Old Norse vaka "to be awake," Dutch waken, Old High German wahhen, German wachen "to watch, be awake," Gothic wakan "to wake, watch").  The "-er" suffix is used to form nouns indicating a person or thing that performs the action of the verb.
