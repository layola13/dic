lubricate:/ˈluːbrɪkeɪt/ /ˈluːbrɪˌkeɪt/ |v. |润滑；使顺利进行|To apply a substance such as oil or grease to (something) in order to reduce friction; to make (something) operate more smoothly.|例句：The mechanic lubricated the car's engine.  机修工给汽车发动机上了润滑油。

例句：  He tried to lubricate the deal with a small bribe. 他试图用一小笔贿赂来使这笔交易顺利进行。|近义词：oil, grease, smooth, facilitate|反义词：roughen, obstruct|词性:动词

lubrication:/ˌluːbrɪˈkeɪʃn/ /ˌluːbrɪˈkeɪʃən/|n.|润滑；润滑作用|The action of lubricating something or the state of being lubricated.|例句：Regular lubrication is essential for the smooth running of the engine. 定期润滑对发动机的平稳运转至关重要。

例句： The lubrication of the gears reduced the noise. 齿轮的润滑减少了噪音。 |近义词：oiling, greasing|反义词：drying|词性:名词

lubricant:/ˈluːbrɪkənt/ /ˈluːbrɪkənt/|n.|润滑剂；润滑油|A substance, such as oil or grease, used for lubricating.|例句：This lubricant is specifically designed for high-performance engines. 这种润滑剂是专门为高性能发动机设计的。

例句：  Apply a small amount of lubricant to the hinges. 在铰链上涂抹少量润滑剂。|近义词：oil, grease|反义词：desiccant|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *lubricare, from Latin lubricus (“slippery, smooth”), related to labi (“to slip, glide”).
