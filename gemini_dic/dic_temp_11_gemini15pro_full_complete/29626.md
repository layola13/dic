catenary: /ˈkætənɛri/ (US) /ˈkætɪnəri/ (UK)| n. adj.|悬链线，悬链线的|A curve formed by a flexible cable or chain hanging freely from two fixed points, the shape of which is determined by the uniform force of gravity acting on the cable or chain.|The cable of the Golden Gate Bridge hangs in a graceful catenary.|近义词: curve, arc|反义词: straight line|词性: noun, adjective

catenarian: /ˌkætəˈnɛəriən/ (US) /ˌkætɪˈnɛəriən/ (UK)| adj. n.|悬链线的，悬链线|Relating to or resembling a catenary curve; a curve formed by a flexible cable or chain hanging freely from two fixed points.|The architect admired the catenarian arch of the bridge. | The bridge's catenarian design was both elegant and structurally sound. |近义词:  curvilinear, arched|反义词: straight, linear|词性: adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *catenarius*, from Latin *catena* "chain".
