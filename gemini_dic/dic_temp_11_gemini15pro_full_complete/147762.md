overprolix:/ˌoʊvərˈproʊlɪks/| adj.|冗长的，啰嗦的|Excessively wordy or diffuse; too long.  Characterized by excessive length or detail in speech or writing.|例句：His overprolix style made it difficult to follow his argument. 他冗长的风格使人难以理解他的论点。|近义词：verbose, wordy, prolix, long-winded, diffuse|反义词：concise, succinct, brief, terse|词性:形容词
Etymology->
From over- + prolix, from Latin prolixus "extended, long," from pro- "forth" + liqui "to flow."  Literally, "flowing forth too much."
