inconceivability:/ˌɪnkənˌsiːvəˈbɪləti/| n. | 不可思议，难以想象 |The inconceivability of the situation made it difficult to process. |例句：The sheer inconceivability of winning the lottery makes it exciting.  赢得彩票的绝对不可思议性让它令人兴奋。

The inconceivability of faster-than-light travel is a current scientific barrier.  超光速旅行的不可思议性是目前科学的一大障碍。|近义词：unthinkableness, implausibility, incredibility |反义词：conceivability, plausibility, credibility |词性: noun

inconceivable:/ˌɪnkənˈsiːvəbl/| adj. | 不可思议的，难以想象的 |It's inconceivable that he would do such a thing. |例句：It's inconceivable that humans could ever live on Mars.  人类能够在火星上生活是难以想象的。

It was inconceivable to her that he would betray her trust.  对她来说，他竟然会背叛她的信任是不可想象的。|近义词：unthinkable, implausible, incredible |反义词：conceivable, plausible, credible |词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *inconceyvable, from in- (“not”) + conceivable (“imaginable”), from conceive (“to form an idea of”), from Old French conceveir (“to conceive”), from Latin concipere (“to take in, conceive”), from con- (“together”) + capere (“to take”).
