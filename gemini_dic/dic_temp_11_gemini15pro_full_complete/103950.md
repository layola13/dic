judicially:/dʒuˈdɪʃəli/| adv.|司法上地；公正地；明断地|In a manner relating to the administration of justice; in a way that is appropriate for a judge or court of law.  |The matter was handled judicially and fairly.  这件事得到了司法公正的处理。|Synonyms: legally, lawfully, impartially, objectively|Antonyms: unfairly, biasedly, unjustly|词性:副词

judicially:/dʒuˈdɪʃəli/| adv.|明智地；审慎地|In a way that shows good judgement or wisdom. |He judicially weighed all the options before making a decision. 他明智地权衡了所有选择才做出决定。|Synonyms: wisely, prudently, sagaciously, discreetly|Antonyms: foolishly, imprudently, rashly|词性:副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *iudicialiter, from Latin iudicialis (“judicial”) + -iter (adverbial suffix).  iudicialis is from iudicium (“judgment, trial”) + -alis (adjective suffix).  iudicium is from iudex (“judge”), from ius (“law, right”) + -dex (“doer, agent”).
