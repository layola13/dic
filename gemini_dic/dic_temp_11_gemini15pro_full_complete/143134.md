outcasting: /ˈaʊtkæstɪŋ/ | n. |放逐，排斥；被驱逐，被排斥|The act of excluding someone from a group or society.|His outcasting from the community left him feeling isolated and alone. 他被逐出社区，感到孤立无援。|近义词：ostracization, exclusion, banishment, expulsion|反义词：inclusion, acceptance, integration|词性: 名词

outcast: /ˈaʊtkæst/ | n. adj. |被遗弃者，流浪者；被遗弃的，被排斥的|A person who has been rejected by society or a group.|He lived as an outcast, wandering from town to town. 他像个流浪汉一样生活，从一个镇流浪到另一个镇。  She felt like an outcast in her new school. 在新学校里，她感觉自己像个局外人。|近义词：pariah, exile, outsider|反义词：insider, member|词性: 名词，形容词


词源：From the verb "to outcast," which comes from the Old English "ūtācēan," meaning "to cast out" or "to expel."  “out” (外面) + “cast” (扔, 抛)。
