emaciation:/ɪˌmeɪsiˈeɪʃən/ |n.|消瘦；羸弱；衰弱|The state of being abnormally thin or weak, often because of illness or lack of food.|His emaciation was shocking; he was just skin and bones. 他的消瘦令人震惊，他瘦得皮包骨头。|Synonyms: thinness, wasting, atrophy, gauntness|Antonyms: obesity, plumpness, stoutness|noun

emaciation:/ɪˌmeɪʃiˈeɪʃn/ |n.|消瘦；衰弱|The act of making someone or something abnormally thin and weak.|The long illness resulted in the emaciation of his body.  长期的疾病导致他身体消瘦。|Synonyms: weakening, debilitating|Antonyms: strengthening, invigorating|noun
