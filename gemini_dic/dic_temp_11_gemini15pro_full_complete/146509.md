overgeneralized: /ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪzd/ | adj. | 过度概括的，过分概括的 | Characterized by the application of a grammatical rule or pattern in a situation where it does not apply, or by extending a conclusion from too little evidence. |  He made an overgeneralized statement about women being bad drivers. 他对女性是不良驾驶者这一说法过于概括。| Synonyms: sweeping, indiscriminate, blanket | Antonyms: specific, precise, nuanced | adjective

overgeneralize: /ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪz/ | v. | 过度概括，过分概括 | To draw a conclusion or make a statement about something that is more general than is justified by the available evidence. | Children sometimes overgeneralize grammatical rules, like adding "-ed" to every verb to form the past tense. 孩子们有时会过度概括语法规则，比如在每个动词后面加上“-ed”来构成过去式。| Synonyms: extrapolate, generalize too broadly | Antonyms: specify, particularize | verb

Etymology: The prefix "over-" signifies "too much" or "excessive." It is combined with "generalize," which comes from the Late Latin "generalizare," meaning "to make general."
