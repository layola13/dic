outlaw: /ˈaʊtlɔː/ (US) /ˈaʊtlɔː/ (UK)| n. vt.|歹徒，逃犯；宣布…为不合法|A person who has broken the law and is hiding or running away to avoid punishment; to make something illegal or unacceptable.|例句：The sheriff was determined to hunt down the outlaws.  警长决心追捕逃犯。|近义词：criminal, bandit, fugitive, exile|反义词：law-abider, citizen|词性: noun, verb

outlaw: /ˈaʊtlɔː/ (US) /ˈaʊtlɔː/ (UK)| vt.|宣布…为不合法；禁止|To make something illegal or unacceptable.|例句：The government outlawed smoking in public places. 政府禁止在公共场所吸烟。|近义词：ban, prohibit, forbid|反义词：legalize, permit, allow|词性: verb


词源: From Old Norse *ūtlagr, from *ūt "out" + *lǫg "law."  Literally, "outside the law."
