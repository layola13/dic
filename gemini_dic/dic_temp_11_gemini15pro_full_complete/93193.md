词源：From Latin *ignōrāmus (“we do not know”), the first word of a legal formula, from ignōrō (“I do not know, I am ignorant of”), from in- (“not”) + gnōscō (“I know”).  Originally a legal term referring to a finding of "we ignore it", later came to mean an ignorant person.
