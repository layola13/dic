bohemia:/boʊˈhiːmiə/| n.|波西米亚；放荡不羁的艺术家|A region historically in the Czech Republic, associated with a free-spirited and unconventional lifestyle.|She lived a bohemian life, traveling from country to country and writing poetry.|近义词:unconventionality, nonconformity|反义词:conformity|词性:noun
etymology: From French *Bohême*, from Late Latin *Boiohaemum*, from earlier *Bohemia*, ultimately from the name of the Boii, a Celtic tribe that inhabited the area in ancient times. The association with an unconventional lifestyle arose in 19th-century France, referring to the Romani people (often mistakenly called "Bohemians") who were perceived as wanderers and artists.
