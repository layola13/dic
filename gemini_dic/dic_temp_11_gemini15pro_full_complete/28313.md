carac:/ˈkæræk/| n.| 卡拉克帆船 (一种大型多桅帆船)|A carac was a three- or four-masted ocean-going sailing ship that was developed in Portugal in the 15th century. It was larger than the caravel and was used for trade and exploration.|The carac played a crucial role in the Portuguese voyages of discovery.|近义词：galleon, nao|反义词: (无直接反义词，可考虑小型船只，例如dinghy)|词性:noun
