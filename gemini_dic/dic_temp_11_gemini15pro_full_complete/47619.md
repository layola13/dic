debenture: /dɪˈbɛntʃər/ | n. | 债券；信用债券；无担保债券 | A debenture is a type of bond or other debt instrument that is unsecured by collateral. Debentures are backed only by the creditworthiness and reputation of the issuer. |  Example 1: The company issued debentures to raise capital for expansion.  这家公司发行债券为扩张筹集资金。

Example 2: Investors were wary of purchasing the company's debentures due to its poor financial performance. 由于该公司糟糕的财务业绩，投资者对其债券的购买持谨慎态度。 | Synonym: bond, loan, note | Antonym: equity, stock | Noun


debentured: /dɪˈbɛntʃərd/ | adj. | 负债的；以债券担保的 | Secured by debentures; having debt. | Example 1: The debentured debt was a significant portion of the company's liabilities. 以债券担保的债务是公司负债的很大一部分。

Example 2:  The company's assets were debentured to secure the loan.  该公司的资产以债券作抵押来担保贷款。 | Synonym: indebted, bonded | Antonym: unencumbered, debt-free | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *debenture (unattested), from Latin dēbentūra (“something owed”), neuter plural of dēbentūrus (“owing”), future active participle of dēbeō (“I owe”).
