metafiction:/ˌmɛtəˈfɪkʃən/| n. |元小说|Fiction that consciously and systematically draws attention to its status as an artifact in order to pose questions about the relationship between fiction and reality.  It explores the nature of fiction itself, often blurring the lines between the story and the process of storytelling.|The novel "If on a winter's night a traveler" by Italo Calvino is a prime example of metafiction, constantly reminding the reader that they are reading a book.|近义词：self-reflexive fiction, postmodern fiction|反义词:  realism, traditional fiction|词性:noun
