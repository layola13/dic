castlin: /ˈkɑːslɪn/ | n. | 城堡 | A fortified residence, typically of a lord or noble in the Middle Ages. |  The king lived in a grand castlin overlooking the valley. 国王住在一座俯瞰山谷的宏伟城堡里。| 近义词: castle, fortress, stronghold | 反义词: hut, shack | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Middle English *castelling, *castelyng, from Anglo-Norman castelin, from Old North French *casteleng, from Medieval Latin castellanus, from Latin castellum (“castle”). Equivalent to castle +‎ -ling or castle +‎ -ing.  Ultimately from Latin castrum (“fort”).
