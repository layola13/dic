fairer:/ˈfeərər/ (US), /ˈfɛərər/ (UK)| adj.|更公平的，更美丽的，更白皙的|Comparative form of "fair."  Used to describe something or someone that exhibits a greater degree of fairness, beauty, or lightness in complexion than something/someone else being compared to.|She presented a fairer solution to the problem than her colleague. 她提出了比她同事更公平的解决方案。|The princess was known for her fairer skin.  这位公主以她白皙的皮肤而闻名。|近义词：more just, more equitable, more beautiful, prettier, lighter|反义词：darker, less just, less equitable|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Middle English *fairer*, comparative of *fair*. Ultimately from Old English *fæġer* (“beautiful, fair, pleasant”). Cognate with West Frisian *fair* (“beautiful, fair”), Dutch *vaar* (“capable, clever”), German *feger* (“sweeper”).
