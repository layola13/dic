gailliarde:/ɡælˈjɑːrd/ /ɡaɪˈjɑːrd/ | n. |加利亚德舞曲；活泼奔放|A lively, vigorous Renaissance dance in triple time, often paired with the pavane. | Example 1:  The musicians played a sprightly gailliarde.  乐师们演奏了一曲轻快的加利亚德舞曲。

Example 2: The gailliarde was a popular dance in the courts of Europe.  加利亚德舞曲在欧洲宫廷很受欢迎。 | Synonym: dance, jig, romp | Antonym:  pavane (often considered its opposite in tempo and mood) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *gaillarde*, feminine of *gaillard* "high-spirited, lively," from Old French *gaillard*, of uncertain origin, perhaps from Frankish *gahi* "joyful" or from a Germanic source.
