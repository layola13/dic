glitch:/ɡlɪtʃ/ | n. | 小故障；失灵；短时故障 | A sudden, usually temporary malfunction or irregularity of equipment. | The computer program had a glitch that caused it to crash. 电脑程序出现了一个小故障，导致它崩溃了。| malfunction, bug, error, fault | smooth operation, perfection | noun

glitch:/ɡlɪtʃ/ | v. | 出现小故障；失灵 | To experience a sudden, usually temporary malfunction or irregularity.  | The system glitched and lost all the data. 系统出现故障，丢失了所有数据。 | malfunction, fail, break down | function correctly, work properly | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain. Possibly related to Yiddish *glitsh ‘slippery place,’ or German glitschen ‘to slip.’ First recorded in the 1960s, originally in the context of spaceflight.
