high-profile:/ˌhaɪ ˈproʊfaɪl/| adj.|高调的，引人注目的|Attracting a lot of public attention, usually deliberately.|The celebrity's high-profile wedding was featured in all the magazines.  这位名人的高调婚礼登上了所有杂志。|近义词：prominent, noticeable, conspicuous|反义词：low-profile, inconspicuous, obscure|词性:形容词

Etymology:
"high" (OE hēah) + "profile" (from Italian profilo, from profilare "to draw in profile," from pro- "forth" + filo "thread, line").  Originally referred to a side view or silhouette, later extended to describe something prominent or conspicuous.
