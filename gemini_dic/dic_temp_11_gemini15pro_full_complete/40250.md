continental-crust:/ˌkɑːntɪˈnɛntl ˈkrʌst/ /ˌkɒntɪˈnɛntl ˈkrʌst/| n. |大陆地壳|The relatively thick part of the earth's crust that forms the continents. It is generally older and more complex than the oceanic crust.|The continental crust is less dense than the oceanic crust. 大陆地壳的密度比海洋地壳小。|The earthquake originated in the continental crust. 地震起源于大陆地壳。|近义词：earth's crust, sial|反义词: oceanic crust|词性:noun

continental:/ˌkɑːntɪˈnɛntl/ /ˌkɒntɪˈnɛntl/| adj. |大陆的，大陆性的|Of or relating to a continent.|Continental Europe has a diverse range of climates. 欧洲大陆的气候多种多样。|They prefer a continental breakfast. 他们更喜欢欧陆式早餐。|近义词：mainland|反义词: insular, island|词性:adjective

crust:/krʌst/| n. |地壳，外壳，硬皮|The outermost layer of a planet or moon; a hard outer covering.|The Earth's crust is constantly shifting. 地球的地壳在不断地移动。|The pie had a golden brown crust. 派的表面有一层金黄色的脆皮。|近义词：shell, covering, exterior|反义词: core, interior|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
continental: From Late Latin *continentalis, from continens (present participle of continēre “to hold together, contain”), from com- (“together”) + tenēre (“to hold”).
crust: From Latin crusta (“shell, rind, bark”).
