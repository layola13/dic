flare:/fler/ | n. vi. |闪光; 照明弹; 耀斑; (衣裙等)底部张开;  |A sudden brief burst of bright flame or light. A gradual widening, especially of a skirt or pants leg. To burn with a sudden intensity. To (cause to) become wider at one end.| The skirt flares out from the hips. 这条裙子从臀部向下呈喇叭形展开。|Synonyms: blaze, flash, gleam, glare | Antonyms: diminish, dwindle, fade | noun, verb

flare:/fler/ | v. |(突然)燃烧起来，闪耀; (使)底部张开，(使)呈喇叭形; (短暂地)爆发，突然发怒; |To burn with a sudden intensity. To suddenly become angry. To (cause to) become wider at one end. | Tempers flared after the game. 比赛结束后，人们勃然大怒。|Synonyms: blaze, erupt, explode, flash | Antonyms: calm, die down, lessen | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *flāra (“to flutter, waver”), from Proto-Germanic *flairōną (“to waver”), from Proto-Indo-European *pleh₁- (“to flow, run”). Cognate with Danish flagre (“to flutter”), Norwegian flagre (“to flutter”), Swedish flagra (“to flutter, flap, wave”). Related to flag.
