evacuation:/ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃn/ | n. | 疏散，撤离 | the process of moving people from a dangerous place to somewhere safe | The evacuation of the city was completed before the hurricane hit.  这座城市的疏散工作在飓风来袭前完成。| synonyms: removal, clearance | antonyms:  occupation, influx | noun

evacuate:/ɪˈvækjueɪt/ | v. | 疏散，撤离 | to move people from a dangerous place to somewhere safe |  Residents were evacuated from their homes due to the wildfire. 由于野火，居民们被从家中疏散。| synonyms: remove, clear | antonyms: occupy, populate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *evacuare, from Latin evacuatus, past participle of evacuare "to empty out," from ex- "out" + vacuare "to empty," from vacuus "empty."
