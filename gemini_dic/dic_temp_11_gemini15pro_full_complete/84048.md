half-fictitiousness:/ˌhæf fɪkˈtɪʃəsnəs/| n.|半虚构性|The state or quality of being partially fictional; based partly on fact and partly on imagination.|例句：The novel has a certain half-fictitiousness to it, blurring the lines between reality and imagination. 这部小说带有一定的半虚构性，模糊了现实与想象之间的界限。

例句：The author admitted to the half-fictitiousness of the biography, explaining that some events were embellished for dramatic effect. 作者承认这部传记的半虚构性，解释说一些事件是为了戏剧效果而润色的。|近义词：semi-fiction, part-fiction, quasi-fiction|反义词：factuality, reality, truth|词性:noun
