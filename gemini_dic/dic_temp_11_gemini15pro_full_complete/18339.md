belligerency:/bəˈlɪdʒərənsi/
n.|好战性，交战状态|The state of being at war or being engaged in warfare.  A warlike or hostile attitude or nature.|例句：The country's belligerency led to a swift escalation of the conflict.  这个国家的好战性导致冲突迅速升级。

例句：His belligerency surprised everyone in the room. 他的好战态度让房间里的每个人都感到惊讶。
|近义词：hostility, aggressiveness, warmongering, combativeness|反义词：peacefulness, pacifism, neutrality|词性:名词

belligerent:/bəˈlɪdʒərənt/
adj.|好战的，交战的|Inclined to or exhibiting assertiveness, hostility, or combativeness.  Waging war.|例句：His belligerent attitude made it difficult to negotiate.  他好斗的态度使谈判变得困难。

例句：The two countries were belligerent towards each other for many years. 这两个国家多年来一直处于交战状态。
|近义词：hostile, aggressive, pugnacious, warlike|反义词：peaceful, conciliatory, amicable|词性:形容词


Etymology:
From Late Latin *belligerantia, from Latin belligerant- (stem of belligerans), present participle of belligerare “to wage war,” from bellum “war” + gerere “to wage, carry on.”
