inexpedient:/ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiənt/| adj.|不明智的，不合适的，不利的|Not suitable or advisable; not practical or wise.  It suggests a lack of wisdom or prudence in a particular situation, often because the action would be disadvantageous or counterproductive.|例句：It would be inexpedient to raise taxes at this time.  在这个时候提高税收是不明智的。|例句：She thought it inexpedient to mention the matter to him. 她认为向他提及此事是不合适的。|近义词：inadvisable, unwise, imprudent, impolitic|反义词: advisable, wise, prudent, expedient|词性:形容词

词源:
From Latin *inexpediēns*, present participle of *inexpedīre* "to hinder, entangle," from *in-* "not" + *ex-* "out" + *pedis* "foot, fetter."  The original sense was "having one's feet entangled, hampered," hence "inconvenient, disadvantageous."
