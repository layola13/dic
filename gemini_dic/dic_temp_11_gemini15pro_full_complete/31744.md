词源:  "Cheapskate" originated in the late 19th or early 20th century. "Cheap" meaning inexpensive combined with "skate," likely referring to a worn-out or inexpensive ice skate, implying someone who cuts corners and avoids spending money.  "Cheapskating" is the gerund or present participle form of the verb "to cheapskate," although the verb form is less common.
