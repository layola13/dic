abidingly:/əˈbaɪdɪŋli/
| adv. |持久地，永久地；始终如一地|In a manner that endures or lasts; permanently; constantly. |She abidingly believed in the power of love. 她始终坚信爱情的力量。|Synonyms: enduringly, lastingly, permanently, constantly, perpetually|Antonyms: temporarily, fleetingly, transiently|词性:副词

abide:/əˈbaɪd/
| v. |容忍，忍受；遵守；居留|1. Tolerate or put up with (someone or something). 2. Act in accordance with (a rule, decision, or recommendation). 3. (of a feeling or memory) continue to exist.  | 1. I can't abide his rudeness. 我无法忍受他的粗鲁。 2. Both sides must abide by the judge's decision. 双方都必须遵守法官的裁决。 3.  The memory of that day still abides with me. 那天的记忆仍然萦绕在我的心头。 |Synonyms: tolerate, endure, bear, stand, obey, comply with, follow, remain, stay, reside, dwell, live|Antonyms: disallow, reject, disobey, defy, leave|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English abiden, from Old English ābīdan (“to await, wait for, remain, continue, last, endure, experience, stand firm”), from a- (“on, in, at”) + bīdan (“to abide, remain, wait, stay, dwell”). Related to German abwarten (“to wait for, await”).
