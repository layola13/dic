at all:/æt ɔːl/| adv.| 根本；丝毫；究竟；到底| Used for emphasis, especially in negative sentences, or to express the smallest amount.  Used in questions to express surprise or disbelief. | I haven't seen him at all. 我根本没见过他。| Synonyms: whatsoever, in the least, in the slightest | Antonyms: entirely, completely | adverb

at all:/æt ɔːl/| adv.|完全地，全然|Used to emphasize a positive statement, often in response to a negative question or suggestion.|Did you enjoy the movie? At all? 你喜欢这部电影吗？完全喜欢吗？|Synonyms: completely, entirely |Antonyms: not at all |adverb
