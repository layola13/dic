levitate: /ˈlɛvɪteɪt/ (US) /ˈlɛvɪteɪt/ (UK)| v. | 漂浮，悬浮；使…漂浮 | To rise or cause to rise and float in the air, especially by means of supernatural power. | The magician levitated the table. 魔术师使桌子漂浮起来。| The guru claimed he could levitate. 那位大师声称他可以漂浮。 | 近义词: float, hover, rise, ascend | 反义词: descend, fall, drop | 词性: 动词

levitation: /ˌlɛvɪˈteɪʃən/ (US) /ˌlɛvɪˈteɪʃən/ (UK)| n. | 漂浮，悬浮 | The act or process of levitating. | The levitation of the object defied gravity. 物体的漂浮违反了重力。|  He studied the levitation of saints in religious texts. 他研究了宗教文本中圣徒的漂浮。 | 近义词: floating, hovering | 反义词: falling, descent | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *levitatus, past participle of levitare "to lift up, lighten", from Latin levis "light (in weight)".
