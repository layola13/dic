carving:/ˈkɑːrvɪŋ/ (US), /ˈkɑːvɪŋ/ (UK)| n. | 雕刻；雕刻品；雕刻艺术 | The act or art of shaping wood, stone, or other materials by cutting or chipping away parts of it.  A carved object or design.| He admired the intricate carvings on the church doors. 他欣赏教堂门上复杂的雕刻。 | synonyms: sculpture, engraving, etching | antonyms: casting, molding | noun

carving:/ˈkɑːrvɪŋ/ (US), /ˈkɑːvɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. | 雕刻的 | Used for carving; suitable for being carved.|  A carving knife. 一把雕刻刀。| synonyms: sculptural, engraved | antonyms: cast, molded | adjective


carve:/kɑːrv/ (US), /kɑːv/ (UK)| v. | 雕刻；切开 | To cut (a hard material) in order to produce an object, design, or inscription.| She carved a statue out of wood. 她用木头雕刻了一尊雕像。| synonyms: sculpt, engrave, etch | antonyms: cast, mold | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ceorfan (attested only in combined forms such as for-ceorfan "cut off"), from Proto-Germanic *kerfaną (source also of Old Frisian kerva, Middle Dutch kerven, Dutch kerven "to notch, carve", Old High German kerban "to cut, notch", German kerben "to notch, indent"), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kerb- (source also of Lithuanian skrebti "to scrape, scratch", Latvian skrāpēt "to scratch, scrape", Albanian shkrab "scratch").
