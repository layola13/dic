overestimated: /ˌoʊvərˈɛstɪmeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 高估的 | Having estimated something to be larger, better, or more important than it actually is. |  The impact of the new law was overestimated. 新法律的影响被高估了。| overvalued, overrated | underestimated, undervalued | adjective

overestimate: /ˌoʊvərˈɛstɪmeɪt/ | v. | 高估 | To estimate something to be larger, better, or more important than it actually is. | I overestimated the amount of food we would need for the party. 我高估了聚会需要的食物量。| overvalue, exaggerate | underestimate, undervalue | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + estimate.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "too much" or "excessively". "Estimate" comes from the Latin word "aestimatus," the past participle of "aestimare," meaning "to value, assess."
