gladhand:/ˈɡlædˌhænd/| n. v.|热情握手；虚情假意的欢迎；拉拢|A hearty handshake, often given as a gesture of superficial cordiality, especially to win favor or votes.|The politician gladhanded everyone at the rally.|近义词：welcome, greet, schmooze|反义词:snub, ignore|词性:名词, 动词

gladhand:/ˈɡlædˌhænd/| v.|热情握手；虚情假意的欢迎；拉拢|To greet or welcome someone with excessive cordiality, often insincerely, especially to win favor or votes.|The salesman gladhanded potential customers as they entered the store.|近义词：welcome, greet, schmooze|反义词:snub, ignore|词性:动词

词源:  Compound of *glad* (happy, pleased) and *hand* (the body part).  It emerged in the late 19th century, initially in American English, to describe the enthusiastic handshakes common in politics and business.
