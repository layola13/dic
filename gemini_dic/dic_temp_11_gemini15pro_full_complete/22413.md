bookend: /ˈbʊkˌɛnd/ | n. | 书挡；支撑物；结尾；首尾呼应的事件或事物 | A support placed at the end of a row of books to keep them upright. Also used figuratively to describe something that comes at the beginning and end of something else, creating a sense of enclosure or completion. |  The author bookended his novel with scenes of the protagonist's childhood.  作者用主人公童年的场景作为小说的开头和结尾。 | Synonyms: support, bracket, brace, prop, border, frame | Antonyms: center, middle | noun

bookend: /ˈbʊkˌɛnd/ | vt. | 用书挡支撑；首尾呼应 | To support or enclose something with bookends; to mark the beginning and end of something. | The concert was bookended by two of the band's most popular songs.  这场音乐会的开头和结尾都是乐队最受欢迎的两首歌。 | Synonyms: frame, bracket, enclose, border | Antonyms:  separate, divide | verb
