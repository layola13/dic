crowder:/ˈkraʊdər/| n.|拥挤者；挤入者；群集者|Someone who crowds or pushes into a space.|The crowder pushed his way onto the already full bus. 挤满了人，这人还硬挤上了公共汽车。|近义词：thronger, packer, crammer|反义词: disperser, spreader|noun

crowded:/ˈkraʊdɪd/| adj.|拥挤的；挤满的|Full of people; packed.|The market was crowded with shoppers. 市场里挤满了购物者。|近义词：packed, jammed, thronged|反义词: empty, deserted|adjective

crowd:/kraʊd/| n. vi. vt.|人群；群众；拥挤；挤满|A large number of people gathered together; to gather in large numbers; to fill a space by pressing together.|A crowd gathered to watch the street performer.  一群人聚集起来观看街头艺人的表演。|近义词：throng, multitude, swarm|反义词: scattering, few|noun, verb

词源:
From Middle English *crowden, from Old English crūdan (“to press, urge, hasten, drive”), from Proto-Germanic *kreutaną (“to press, crowd”), from Proto-Indo-European *grewd- (“to press, push, crowd”). Cognate with Dutch kruien (“to wheel, barrow, push”), German kräuen (“to scratch, crawl, curry”).
