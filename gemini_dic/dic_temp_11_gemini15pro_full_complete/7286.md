angry: /ˈæŋɡri/ (US) /ˈæŋɡri/ (UK)| adj.|生气的，愤怒的|Feeling or showing strong displeasure, annoyance, or hostility.|例句：He was angry about the delay. 他对延误很生气。|例句：She gave him an angry look. 她怒视着他。|近义词：furious, enraged, indignant, mad|反义词：calm, happy, pleased|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Middle English *angri, from Old Norse *angr (n.) "grief, sorrow, anxiety," and *angr (adj.) "distressed, sorrowful," both from Proto-Germanic *angraz, from Proto-Indo-European *angh- "tight, painful." The sense shifted from "distressed" to "angry" in Middle English.
