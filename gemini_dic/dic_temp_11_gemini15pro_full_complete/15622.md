bailouts: /ˈbeɪlaʊts/
| n. |紧急财政援助；紧急救助；保释金| An act of giving financial assistance to a failing business or economy to save it from collapse.  A payment to secure someone's release from jail.|The government provided bailouts to several failing banks. 政府向几家濒临倒闭的银行提供紧急财政援助。| synonyms: rescue, lifeline, aid, assistance | antonyms: refusal, denial, rejection | noun


bailout: /ˈbeɪlaʊt/
| n. |紧急财政援助；紧急救助；保释金| An act of giving financial assistance to a failing business or economy to save it from collapse.  A payment to secure someone's release from jail.|He paid the bailout for his friend who was arrested. 他支付了被捕朋友的保释金。| synonyms: rescue, lifeline, aid, assistance | antonyms: refusal, denial, rejection | noun



Etymology:  "bail" (to clear water from a boat) + "out" (completely).  Originally referred to helping a boat from sinking, later extended to financial rescues.
