flagrantly:/ˈfleɪɡrəntli/
ad.|明目张胆地，公然地|In a blatant and obvious way, showing a shocking disregard for rules or decency.|He flagrantly disregarded the rules.|近义词：blatantly, openly, brazenly, conspicuously, shamelessly|反义词：secretly, discreetly, subtly, covertly|词性:副词
Etymology: From Late Latin *flagrant-, present participle stem of flagrāre "to burn," from Latin flagrare "to blaze, glow, burn."  The sense evolution is from "burning" to "glowing" to "conspicuous" to "glaring" and finally "outrageous."
