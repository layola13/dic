hole: /hoʊl/ (US) /həʊl/ (UK) | n. |洞，孔，漏洞|An opening or empty space in a solid object or surface. |例句：There's a hole in my sock. 我的袜子上有个洞。

例句：They dug a hole in the ground to plant the tree. 他们在土里挖了个洞来种树。

近义词：cavity, pit, opening, gap, void |反义词: fullness, solid, whole |词性: noun


hole: /hoʊl/ (US) /həʊl/ (UK) | v. | 打洞，穿孔|To make a hole in something. |例句：He holed the paper with a pencil. 他用铅笔在纸上戳了个洞。

例句：The golfer holed the ball in one shot. 高尔夫球手一杆进洞。

近义词：pierce, puncture, perforate, penetrate |反义词: fill, cover, seal |词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hol, from Proto-Germanic *hulaz, from Proto-Indo-European *kel- (to cover, conceal, save). Related to Old High German hol (cave, hollow), Old Norse hol (cave), and Latin celare (to hide). The original sense might have been "cave" or "hollow place."
