imaginationalism:/ɪˌmædʒɪˈneɪʃənəlɪzəm/
n.
想象主义，想象力至上主义
The belief in or reliance on imagination as the primary source of knowledge or truth.  A philosophy or artistic movement that emphasizes the power and importance of imagination.
His writings are steeped in imaginationalism, creating worlds that defy logic. 他的作品充满了想象主义，创造了违背逻辑的世界。
近义词：fantasy, creativity, idealism, romanticism
反义词：realism, pragmatism
词性：名词


Etymology:
From imagination + -al + -ism.  "Imagination" comes from Late Middle English: from Old French imaginacion, or from late Latin imaginatio(n-), from imaginari ‘picture to oneself’, from the noun imago, imagin- ‘image’.
