jockeys:/ˈdʒɒkiz/ /ˈdʒɑːkiz/| n.| 骑师；职业赛马骑师|A jockey is a person who rides horses in horse races, especially as a profession. |The jockey urged his horse towards the finish line.  骑师催促他的马冲向终点线。

The jockey weighed in before the race.  骑师在比赛前称了体重。|近义词：rider, horseman|反义词:None|词性:noun


jockey:/ˈdʒɒki/ /ˈdʒɑːki/| v. |  用不正当手段谋取；耍花招；操控；骑（马）；驾驶|To jockey is to try to get something by using clever or dishonest methods, or to maneuver skillfully, especially in a vehicle.  It can also mean to ride a horse, especially as a jockey. |Several candidates are jockeying for the top position.  几个候选人正在争夺最高职位。

He jockeyed the car into a tight parking space. 他巧妙地把车停在一个狭窄的停车位里。|近义词：maneuver, manipulate, scheme, ride|反义词:None|词性:verb
