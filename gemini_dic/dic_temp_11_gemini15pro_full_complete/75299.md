frontiersman:/ˌfrʌnˈtɪərzmən/ | n. | 边疆居民；拓荒者 | A person who lives on the frontier, especially in the American West during the period of westward expansion. |  Example 1:  The frontiersman built a log cabin for his family.  边疆居民为他的家人建造了一间小木屋。

Example 2:  Frontiersmen faced many dangers, including wild animals and hostile Native American tribes.  拓荒者面临许多危险，包括野生动物和敌对的美洲原住民部落。 | 近义词: pioneer, settler, homesteader | 反义词: city dweller, urbanite | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From frontier + -s + man.  Frontier comes from Old French frontiere meaning "border," ultimately from Latin frons "forehead, front."
