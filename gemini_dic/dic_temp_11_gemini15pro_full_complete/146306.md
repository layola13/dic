overfeast: /ˌoʊvərˈfiːst/ | v. n. |暴饮暴食；过度宴饮；盛宴 | to eat too much; a feast where too much food is eaten |  After the wedding, we overfeasted and felt sick. 婚礼后，我们暴饮暴食，感到恶心。|  We had such an overfeast at Thanksgiving that we couldn't move. 感恩节我们吃得太多了，动弹不得。| 近义词：gorge, stuff, overeat, overindulge | 反义词：starve, diet, fast | 词性: 动词, 名词

overfeast: /ˌoʊvərˈfiːst/ | n. |盛宴；宴会 | a large meal, especially a celebratory one, often characterized by excessive eating | The king held an overfeast to celebrate his victory. 国王举行盛宴庆祝胜利。 | The annual overfeast was a tradition in the village.  一年一度的盛宴是村里的传统。| 近义词：banquet, feast, celebration | 反义词：fast, diet | 词性: 名词

词源： "over-" (Old English *ofer, meaning "above, beyond, over") + "feast" (from Old French feste, ultimately from Latin festa "holiday, feast day")
