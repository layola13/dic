fall-over-backwards:/ˌfɔːl ˈəʊvə ˈbækwədz/| idiom |竭尽全力；费尽心思；百依百顺 |To make a great effort to please someone or to do something.| Example:  She fell over backwards to help him get the job. 她竭尽全力帮助他得到那份工作。| Example: The hotel staff fell over backwards to make sure our stay was enjoyable. 酒店员工费尽心思确保我们住得愉快。| 近义词: bend over backwards, go out of one's way, go the extra mile | 反义词: do the bare minimum, lift a finger | 词性: idiom

fall-over-backwards:/ˌfɔːl ˈəʊvə ˈbækwədz/| idiom | 过分殷勤；过分热情；矫揉造作 | To behave in an excessively enthusiastic or ingratiating manner. | Example: He fell over backwards to impress his new boss, which seemed insincere. 他为了给新老板留下好印象而过分殷勤，这显得不真诚。| Example:  She's so eager to please that she falls over backwards, which can be a bit much. 她太渴望取悦别人了，以至于表现得矫揉造作，这有点过分。| 近义词:  overdo it, go overboard, try too hard | 反义词: be aloof, be reserved | 词性: idiom
