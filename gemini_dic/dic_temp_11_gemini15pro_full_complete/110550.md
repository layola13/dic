limerick: /ˈlɪmərɪk/ | n. | 打打油诗; Limerick市(爱尔兰西部城市) | A five-line poem with a specific rhyming pattern (AABBA) and usually humorous and often ribald subject matter.  It is named after the city of Limerick in Ireland. |  Example 1: He wrote a limerick about a flea. 他写了一首关于跳蚤的打油诗。

Example 2:  Limericks are a fun way to play with words. 打油诗是一种有趣的文字游戏方式。 | synonym:  nonsense verse, light verse, doggerel | antonym:  epic poem, serious poetry | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the city of Limerick, Ireland, perhaps from a chorus or refrain that included the words "Will you come up to Limerick?"  The exact origin is uncertain.
