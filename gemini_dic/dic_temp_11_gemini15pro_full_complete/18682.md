bequeathment:/bɪˈkwiːðmənt/
n.
遗产；遗赠；遗赠物
The act of bequeathing something (property, a sum of money, etc.) in a will or the thing bequeathed.
His father's will contained a generous bequeathment to his children. 他父亲的遗嘱中给他的孩子们留下了丰厚的遗产。

My grandmother left me a small bequeathment in her will. 我的祖母在遗嘱中给我留下了一小笔遗产。

Synonyms: legacy, inheritance, bequest, endowment
Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms exist for the act of bequeathing.  One could consider concepts like "confiscation" or "seizure" as opposite actions regarding the fate of property.)
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *biquemen, from Old English bi-cweman (“to commit, entrust, commend”). Equivalent to be- +‎ queath.
