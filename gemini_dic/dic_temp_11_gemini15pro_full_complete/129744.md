non-establishment:/ˌnɒn ɪˈstæblɪʃmənt/ | adj. n. | 非建制派的；非主流的；反传统的 | Not belonging to the established system; unconventional; opposed to the traditional power structure. |  The non-establishment candidate unexpectedly won the election.  非建制派候选人意外赢得了选举。

| Example:  She prefers non-establishment art. 她更喜欢非主流艺术。| Synonym: unconventional, unorthodox, rebellious, outsider,  | Antonym: establishment, conventional, mainstream, orthodox, insider | adjective

non-establishment:/ˌnɒn ɪˈstæblɪʃmənt/ | n. | 非建制派人士；非主流群体 |  A group or individual not belonging to the established system; unconventional individuals or groups. | The non-establishment voiced their concerns about the new policy. 非建制派人士表达了他们对新政策的担忧。

| Example: The rise of the non-establishment has changed the political landscape. 非建制派的崛起改变了政治格局。| Synonym:  unconventional group, fringe group, outsiders | Antonym: the establishment, the mainstream, insiders | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "establishment" (from the verb "establish," meaning "to set up permanently"). The term gained prominence in the mid-20th century, particularly in the context of politics and social movements.
