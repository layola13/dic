ETYMOLOGY->
From the practice of concealing flasks of alcohol in the leg of a high boot, especially during Prohibition in the United States (1920-1933).  The term "bootleg" originally referred to the act of concealing something in a boot leg, and later extended to any illegal trade, particularly alcohol.
