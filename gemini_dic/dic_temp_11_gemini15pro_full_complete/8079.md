anthropomorphosis:/ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːrfəsɪs/
|n.|拟人化，人格化|The attribution of human characteristics or behavior to a god, animal, or object.|The children's book is filled with anthropomorphosis, with animals talking and wearing clothes.  这本童书充满了拟人化，动物会说话，还会穿衣服。|
|近义词：personification, humanization|反义词：dehumanization|词性：noun|


anthropomorphic:/ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːrfɪk/
|adj.|拟人化的|Relating to or characterized by anthropomorphism.|The ancient Egyptians had many anthropomorphic gods, depicted with human bodies and animal heads. 古埃及人有许多拟人化的神，被描绘成人的身体和动物的头。|近义词：personified, humanlike|反义词：zoomorphic|词性：adjective|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἄνθρωπος (ánthrōpos, “human being”) + μορφή (morphḗ, “form, shape”) + -osis.
