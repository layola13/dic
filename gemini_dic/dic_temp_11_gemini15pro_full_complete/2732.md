adulating: /ˈædʒəˌleɪtɪŋ/
Verb
中文翻译：奉承；谄媚；过分称赞
英文解析：Praising someone excessively or obsequiously; flattering.  Often used in a humorous or ironic way to describe acting like a responsible adult.
例句：I was adulating my boss, hoping for a promotion. 我一直在奉承我的老板，希望能升职。

例句：She's constantly adulating her favorite celebrities on social media. 她一直在社交媒体上吹捧她最喜欢的名人。

近义词：flattering, fawning, praising, extolling, lauding
反义词：criticizing, condemning, disparaging
词性：Verb (present participle)

adulting: /əˈdʌltɪŋ/
Noun, Verb
中文翻译：(n.) 成年人的行为；(v.) 做成年人做的事情
英文解析：(n.) The practice of behaving in a way characteristic of a responsible adult, especially the accomplishment of mundane but necessary tasks.; (v.)  Engaging in activities typically associated with being a responsible adult.
例句：I hate adulting, especially paying bills. 我讨厌做成年人的事，尤其是付账单。

例句：She’s been adulting so hard lately, working two jobs and taking care of her family. 她最近一直在努力扮演成年人的角色，做两份工作并照顾她的家人。

近义词：(n.) responsibility, maturity; (v.)  managing, coping
反义词：(n.) immaturity, childishness
词性：Noun, Verb

词源:
From adult + -ing.  The use of "adult" as a verb emerged in the late 20th/early 21st century, primarily in informal contexts.  "Adulting" as a noun followed shortly after.
