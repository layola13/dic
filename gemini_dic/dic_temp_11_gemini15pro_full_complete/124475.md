mould:/məʊld/ (UK) /moʊld/ (US)| n. v.|霉菌; 模具; 类型; (性格或品质的)类型|A hollow container used to give shape to molten or hot liquid material when it cools and hardens.  A furry growth of minute fungal hyphae occurring typically in moist warm conditions, especially on food or other organic matter.|例句：There was mould growing on the cheese. 奶酪上长了霉。|近义词：mold (US), fungus, mildew, form, cast, type, character|反义词: -(none clear antonym)|noun

mould:/məʊld/ (UK) /moʊld/ (US)| v.|塑造; 模制; 对…产生影响; 使形成|To shape (something) in a mould. To influence the development or formation of (something).|例句：He moulded the clay into a pot. 他用黏土塑造了一个罐子。|近义词：shape, form, fashion, influence|反义词: deform, distort|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *moldre, from Latin modulus ("small measure, standard"), diminutive of modus ("measure, manner, fashion"). Doublet of model and module.
