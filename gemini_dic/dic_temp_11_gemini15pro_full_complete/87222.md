heren:/ˈheːrən/ (Dutch)| n.| gentlemen, sirs (plural)|This archaic word is the plural of "heer" (lord, master) and is used as a polite form of address for multiple men. It is primarily used in the Netherlands and Belgium, and is less common in modern usage. | Heren, welkom in ons restaurant. (Gentlemen, welcome to our restaurant.)|近义词: gentlemen, sirs|反义词: N/A|Noun

PHRASE->

(Since "heren" is a form of address, it doesn't typically form set phrases in the same way other words do. Instead, it's incorporated into polite expressions.)

1. Goedemorgen, heren. (Good morning, gentlemen.)
2. Goedenavond, heren. (Good evening, gentlemen.)
3. Welkom, heren. (Welcome, gentlemen.)
4. Heren, mag ik uw aandacht? (Gentlemen, may I have your attention?)
5. Dank u wel, heren. (Thank you, gentlemen.)


注意事项:
While "heren" is grammatically plural, it can sometimes be used with a singular verb in informal Dutch, especially in dialects.  However, formal usage generally requires plural verb agreement.  The word is becoming less common in modern Dutch, with "mannen" (men) becoming a more frequent, though less formal, alternative.
