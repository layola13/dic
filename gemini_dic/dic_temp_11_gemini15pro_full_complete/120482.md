minted:/ˈmɪntɪd/| adj.|新造的，新铸的；刚获得的；富有的|Having just been created or produced; recently acquired (especially a qualification or award); wealthy.|例句：He's a minted millionaire after selling his company. 他卖掉公司后成了个腰缠万贯的百万富翁。|例句：She's just minted a PhD in astrophysics. 她刚刚获得了天体物理学博士学位。|近义词：new, fresh, brand-new, newly-acquired, wealthy, rich, affluent|反义词：old, worn, established, poor, impoverished|词性:形容词

minted:/ˈmɪntɪd/| v.|铸造（货币）；创造（新词语）|(of a government or bank) to make coins; to invent or create (a new word or phrase).|例句：The Royal Mint minted a new coin to commemorate the Queen's Jubilee. 皇家铸币厂铸造了一枚新硬币以纪念女王的登基庆典。|例句：He minted a new term for this phenomenon. 他为这种现象创造了一个新术语。|近义词：coin, create, invent, forge|反义词：destroy, abolish|词性:动词
