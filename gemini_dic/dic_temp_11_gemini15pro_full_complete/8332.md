anti-philosophies:/ˌænti fɪˈlɒsəfiz/ | n. | 反哲学，非哲学 | Systems of thought or beliefs that oppose or reject the fundamental principles of philosophy, often emphasizing practicality, skepticism, or religious dogma over rational inquiry. |  Many religious fundamentalists hold anti-philosophies, believing that faith trumps reason. 许多宗教原教旨主义者持有反哲学的观点，认为信仰胜过理性。

Anti-philosophies can also be seen in some political ideologies that prioritize action over reflection. 在一些优先考虑行动而非反思的政治意识形态中也可以看到反哲学。 | 近义词: counter-philosophies, unphilosophical doctrines | 反义词: philosophies, philosophical doctrines | 词性: 名词
