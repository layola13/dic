babbled:/ˈbæbəld/ | v. | 喋喋不休，胡言乱语 | to talk quickly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way | The baby babbled happily in her crib. 婴儿在婴儿床里高兴地牙牙学语。| synonyms: chattered, prattled, gabbled | antonyms: whispered, muttered | verb

babble:/ˈbæbəl/ | n. | 喋喋不休，胡言乱语 | the sound of many people talking at the same time; foolish or confused talk | I couldn't hear anything but the babble of the crowd. 我除了人群的嘈杂声什么也听不见。 | synonyms: chatter, prattle, gabble | antonyms: silence, quiet | noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *bablen, from Old French babler, of imitative origin.
