ablaqueate: /ˈæbləkwɪˌeɪt/
verb
剥离树根周围的土壤; 疏松树根周围的土壤
To uncover the roots of (a tree) and expose them to the air, especially as a method of cultivation.
The gardener ablaqueated the roots of the old oak tree to improve its health. 园丁剥离了老橡树周围的土壤以促进其生长。
近义词: unearth, expose
反义词: bury, cover
词性: verb

Etymology: From Latin *ablaqueāre*  "to lay bare by removing earth", from *ab-* "away" + *laqueus* "noose, snare".  The original meaning likely referred to removing earth around a tree's roots as if loosening a snare.
