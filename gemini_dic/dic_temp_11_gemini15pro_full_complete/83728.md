haight:/heɪt/
n.
Haight-Ashbury (often just "The Haight")
海特-阿什伯里（通常简称为“海特”）
Haight-Ashbury is a district of San Francisco, California, named for the intersection of Haight and Ashbury streets. It is known as the birthplace of the hippie counterculture of the 1960s.
海特-阿什伯里是加利福尼亚州旧金山的一个地区，以海特街和阿什伯里街的交叉口命名。它被称为20世纪60年代嬉皮士反主流文化的诞生地。
例句：
He lived in the Haight during the Summer of Love. 他在“爱之夏”期间住在海特区。

例句：
The Haight has changed a lot since the 60s.  自60年代以来，海特区发生了很大的变化。


近义词：
Haight-Ashbury, the Haight
反义词：
(No direct antonym, perhaps a contrasting district like the Financial District)
词性：
noun

词源:
Named after two intersecting streets: Haight Street (named after early San Francisco banker and civic leader Henry Haight) and Ashbury Street (named after Munroe Ashbury, a member of the San Francisco Board of Supervisors from 1864 to 1870).
