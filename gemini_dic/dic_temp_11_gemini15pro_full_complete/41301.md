cordon:/ˈkɔːrdən/ (UK), /ˈkɔːrdn/ (US)| n. v. |警戒线，封锁线；饰带，绶带|A line or circle of police, soldiers, or guards preventing people from entering or leaving a place; a thin rope or ribbon worn around the neck as a decoration or part of a uniform. |The police set up a cordon around the crime scene. 警方在犯罪现场设置了警戒线。|Synonyms: barrier, barricade, line, rope, ribbon|Antonyms: access, entrance, opening|noun

cordon:/ˈkɔːrdən/ (UK), /ˈkɔːrdn/ (US)| v. |设置警戒线，包围，封锁|To enclose or surround an area with a line of police, soldiers, or guards to prevent people from entering or leaving.|The area was cordoned off after the explosion. 爆炸发生后，该地区被封锁了。|Synonyms: surround, enclose, block off, seal off|Antonyms: open, free, release|verb
