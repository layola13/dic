enallage:/ɪˈnælədʒiː/
n.
换位; 转移; 修辞格的一种,指语法形式的改变,尤指时态、人称、数或格的变化
A figure of speech in which one grammatical form is substituted for another, such as a different tense, person, number, or case.
The poet used enallage, substituting past tense for present tense to create a sense of immediacy. 诗人运用换位修辞法，用过去时态代替现在时态，以营造一种即时感。

近义词:metonymy, catachresis
反义词:N/A
词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *enallagē, from Ancient Greek ἐναλλαγή (enallagḗ, “interchange, exchange”), from ἐναλλάσσω (enallássō, “I exchange”).
