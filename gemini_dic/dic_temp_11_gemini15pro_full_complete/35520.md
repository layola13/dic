coagmentation:/koʊˌæɡjʊˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 凝结，凝固 | The process of changing from a liquid to a thickened or solid state. |  The coagmentation of blood is essential for stopping bleeding. 血液的凝结对于止血至关重要。

The coagmentation of the protein occurred when the temperature was increased.  当温度升高时，蛋白质发生了凝固。 | 近义词：coagulation, clotting, thickening | 反义词：liquefaction, thinning | 词性：名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *coagmentationem, derived from Latin coagulatus, past participle of coagulare "to curdle," from co- "together" + agere "to drive, to do."
