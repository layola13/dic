hemapophysis:/ˌhiːməˈpɒfɪsɪs/ /ˌhiːməˈpɑːfɪsɪs/
noun
血突，椎体侧突
A lateral process of a vertebra, especially one of the transverse processes of a dorsal vertebra, that arises from the vertebral centrum.  It represents a rib element.
The thoracic hemapophyses are small and fused to the transverse processes. 胸椎的血突很小，并与横突融合。
The fossil shows well-developed hemapophyses. 这块化石显示了发育良好的血突。
Synonyms:  pleurapophysis, transverse process (in broader context)
Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but could consider the vertebral body/centrum as a contrasting structure.)
