articulately:/ɑːrˈtɪkjələtli/ | adv. | 清晰地；明白地；表达流畅地 |  In a way that is clearly expressed and easy to understand.  | She spoke articulately about the complex issue. 她清晰地阐述了这个复杂的问题。 |  He presented his arguments articulately and persuasively. 他流畅而有说服力地陈述了他的论点。| clearly, eloquently, distinctly, fluently | inarticulately, vaguely, unclearly | adverb

articulate:/ɑːrˈtɪkjəleɪt/ | adj. |  善于表达的；清晰的 | Able to express thoughts and feelings easily and clearly; expressed clearly. |  She is an articulate and persuasive speaker. 她是一位口才流利、极具说服力的演讲者。 | The child is unusually articulate for his age.  这个孩子对于他这个年龄来说表达能力异乎寻常地好。 | eloquent, fluent, expressive, lucid | inarticulate, unclear, mumbled, incoherent | adjective
articulate:/ɑːrˈtɪkjəleɪt/ | v. |  清楚地表达；清晰地发音 | Express (an idea or feeling) fluently and coherently; pronounce (something) clearly. | He struggled to articulate his feelings. 他难以表达自己的感受。 |  She articulated each syllable carefully. 她仔细地发出每一个音节。 | express, voice, utter, pronounce | mumble, slur, mutter, stumble | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *articulatus*, past participle of articulare (“to utter distinctly, pronounce clearly, divide into joints”), from Latin articulus (“a small joint”), diminutive of artus (“joint, limb”).
