Etymology:
newslessness (n.) + -'s (possessive suffix)

newslessness:
news (n.) + -less (suffix indicating absence) + -ness (suffix forming nouns from adjectives)

news: from Middle English *newes, plural of newe (“new thing”), from Old French noveles, plural of novel (“new, fresh, recent, early”), from Latin novellus (“new, young, recent”), diminutive of novus (“new”).

-less: from Old English -lēas, from Proto-Germanic *-lausaz.

-ness: from Old English -nes(s), -nis(s), -nys(s), from Proto-Germanic *-inassuz.

-'s: possessive suffix.
