loathingly:/ˈloʊðɪŋli/ | adv. | 厌恶地，不情愿地 | In a manner expressing strong dislike or reluctance. | He loathingly agreed to do the chores. 他不情愿地同意做家务。| She looked at the dirty dishes loathingly. 她厌恶地看着脏盘子。 | reluctantly, unwillingly, grudgingly | eagerly, willingly, readily | adverb

Etymology:
From *loath* (adjective) + *-ing* (present participle suffix) + *-ly* (adverbial suffix).  The word *loath* comes from Old English *lāþ*, meaning "hateful, hostile, unpleasant."
