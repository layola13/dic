methane:/ˈmɛθeɪn/ | n. | 甲烷 | A colorless, odorless flammable gas which is the main constituent of natural gas.  It is the simplest alkane, and is used as a fuel. |  Landfills are a major source of methane emissions. 垃圾填埋场是甲烷排放的主要来源。| synonyms:  firedamp | antonyms: None | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French méthane, coined by French chemist Auguste Laurent (1807–1853) from methyl + -ane,  with methyl from German Methyl, from Greek methy (wine) + hyle (wood). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *medhu- (“honey; sweet drink”) + *ḱey-lós (“firewood”).
