contortionate:/kənˈtɔːrʃəneɪt/
contortionate: |adj.| 扭曲的，歪曲的 | Twisted or strained out of shape, often violently; also, figuratively, highly complex or strained.|  Her face was contorted in a grimace of pain. 她的脸因痛苦而扭曲变形。 | Synonyms: distorted, twisted, warped, deformed | Antonyms: straight, symmetrical, unbent, natural
contortionate:|v.| 扭曲，歪曲 | To twist or bend out of its normal shape.| He contortionated his body to fit through the narrow opening. 他扭曲身体以穿过狭窄的开口。 | Synonyms: distort, twist, warp, deform | Antonyms: straighten, align, smooth

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contortionatus*, past participle of *contortionare*, from Latin contortus (past participle of contorquēre ‘twist violently’), from con- ‘together’ + torquēre ‘to twist’.
