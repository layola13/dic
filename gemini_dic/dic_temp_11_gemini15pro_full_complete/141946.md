ordovian:/ɔːrˈdoʊviən/
adjective|奥陶纪的|Relating to or denoting the second period of the Paleozoic era, between the Cambrian and Silurian, lasting from 505 to 438 million years ago.|The Ordovician period saw a great diversification of marine life.|近义词: Paleozoic, early Paleozoic|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider periods like "Precambrian" or "Silurian")|词性: adjective

ordovian:/ɔːrˈdoʊviən/
noun|奥陶纪|The second period of the Paleozoic era, between the Cambrian and Silurian, lasting from 505 to 438 million years ago.|Trilobites were abundant during the Ordovician.|近义词: (No direct synonym, but can be referred to as an "era" or "period")|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider periods like "Precambrian" or "Silurian")|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *Ordovices*, a Celtic tribe of North Wales, where rocks of this age are found + *-ian*.
