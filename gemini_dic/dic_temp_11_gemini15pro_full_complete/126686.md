namby-pambyisms:/ˌnæmbi ˈpæmbiɪzəmz/
|n.|幼稚的言行；故作多情的行为；扭捏作态|Weak, sentimental, or affected behavior or language.|His writing is full of namby-pambyisms that make it hard to take seriously. 他的作品充满了幼稚的言行，让人难以认真对待。|近义词：sentimentality, affectation, mawkishness, effeminacy|反义词：strength, decisiveness, masculinity, robustness|词性:名词

namby-pamby:/ˈnæmbi ˈpæmbi/
|adj.|幼稚的；故作多情的；扭捏作态的|Weak, sentimental, or affected.|He gave a namby-pamby apology that didn't sound sincere at all. 他做了一个软弱无力的道歉，听起来一点也不真诚。|近义词：sentimental, affected, mawkish, effeminate|反义词：strong, decisive, masculine, robust|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the name "Ambrose", perhaps influenced by "namby", an 18th-century word for a weak or effeminate man.  Originally used to satirize the weak verses of Ambrose Philips, a contemporary of Alexander Pope.
