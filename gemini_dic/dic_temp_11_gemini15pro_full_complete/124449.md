motors:/ˈmoʊtərz/ (US), /ˈməʊtəz/ (UK)| n.|发动机，马达|A machine, especially an electric one, that supplies motive power for a vehicle or for some other device with moving parts.|The electric motors whirred as the assembly line started up.|engines, machines| brakes (as a device that stops motion) |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *motor*, from Old French *moteur*, from Latin *motor*, "mover," from *movēre*, "to move."
