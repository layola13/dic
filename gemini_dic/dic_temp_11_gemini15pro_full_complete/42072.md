cothurn:/ˈkɒθɜːn/ (US) /ˈkoʊθɜːrn/| n. |厚底靴；悲剧; 悲剧风格；高腔；高谈阔论|A thick-soled boot or buskin worn by tragic actors in ancient Greek theatre, hence, symbolically, tragedy or the tragic style.|The actor donned the cothurn and prepared to deliver his lines.  演员穿上了厚底靴，准备说出他的台词。|Synonyms: buskin, boot, tragedy|Antonyms: comedy, lightness|noun

cothurn:/ˈkɒθɜːn/ (US) /ˈkoʊθɜːrn/| n. |高腔；高谈阔论|Elevated or pompous language or style, often associated with tragedy.|His speech was full of cothurn and dramatic pronouncements. 他的演讲充满了高腔和戏剧性的宣言。|Synonyms: bombast, grandiloquence, pomposity|Antonyms: simplicity, humility, understatement|noun
