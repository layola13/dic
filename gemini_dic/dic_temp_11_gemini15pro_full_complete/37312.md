commatism: /ˈkɒmətɪzəm/ (US) /ˈkɒmətɪzəm/ (UK)| n. | 共栖，共栖现象 |A symbiotic relationship between two organisms in which one organism benefits and the other is unaffected.|The clownfish and the sea anemone exhibit commatism; the clownfish gains protection, while the anemone is neither harmed nor helped. 小丑鱼和海葵表现出共栖关系；小丑鱼获得保护，而海葵既没有受到伤害也没有得到帮助。|Example: The relationship between cattle egrets and cattle is a classic example of commatism. 牛背鹭和牛之间的关系是共栖关系的典型例子。|Synonyms: commensalism|Antonyms: parasitism, mutualism|Noun
