kist:/kɪst/
n.
chest, coffin, box
A chest or box, especially one for holding valuables or relics; also, a coffin.
The old woman kept her jewels in a kist. 老妇人把她的珠宝放在一个箱子里。
kist:/kɪst/
n.
coffin
A coffin.
His body was laid in the kist. 他的遗体被安放在棺材里。


Etymology: From Middle Scots *kist, from Old Norse kista (“chest, box”), from Proto-Germanic *kistō (“basket, box”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱístis (“basket, woven container”). Cognate with Old English cīst (“chest, box, coffin”).
