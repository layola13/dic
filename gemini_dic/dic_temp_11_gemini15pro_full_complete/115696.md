marginalization:/ˌmɑːrdʒɪnələˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 边缘化；排斥；忽视 | The process of making a group or class of people less important or powerful in society. |  Many minority groups experience marginalization in the workplace. 许多少数群体在工作场所经历边缘化。| synonym: exclusion, sidelining, ostracism | antonym: inclusion, integration, empowerment | noun

marginalize:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəlaɪz/ | v. | 边缘化；排斥；忽视 | To treat someone or a group as if they are not important. |  Society tends to marginalize the elderly. 社会往往会边缘化老年人。| synonym:  exclude, sideline, ostracize | antonym: include, integrate, empower | verb

marginal:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəl/ | adj. | 微小的；边缘的；不重要的 |  Relating to or situated at the edge or margin of something. Small and not very important. | The increase in profits was only marginal. 利润的增长微乎其微。| synonym: slight, minimal, insignificant, peripheral | antonym: significant, central, important | adjective
