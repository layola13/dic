overmerciful: /ˌoʊvərˈmɜːrsɪfəl/ | adj. | 过于仁慈的，过度宽容的 | Excessively merciful; too lenient or forgiving.  Showing more mercy or compassion than is considered appropriate or wise. |  The overmerciful judge let the repeat offender off with a warning.  过于仁慈的法官只是警告了惯犯。|  The king, known for his overmerciful nature, pardoned even the most heinous criminals.  这位国王以其过于仁慈的本性而闻名，即使是最凶恶的罪犯也会得到赦免。 | synonyms:  too lenient, excessively forgiving, overindulgent,  | antonyms: merciless, ruthless, unforgiving, harsh, strict | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "merciful" (from "mercy" + "-ful").  "Mercy" comes from Old French "merci" (meaning "grace, pity, thanks"), ultimately from Latin "merces" (meaning "reward, wages").
