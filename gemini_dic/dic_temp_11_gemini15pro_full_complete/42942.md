coventry: /ˈkʌvəntri/ | n. | 考文垂（英国城市名）;  被排斥，被集体孤立 | a state of being ostracized or sent to Coventry; a city in central England |例句：He was sent to Coventry by his colleagues for betraying their trust.  他因为背叛了同事们的信任而被他们孤立。

例句：Coventry is famous for its cathedral. 考文垂以其大教堂而闻名。| 近义词：ostracism, isolation, boycott | 反义词：inclusion, acceptance | 词性: 名词

词源:  From the name of the English city of Coventry.  One theory suggests that during the English Civil War, Royalist prisoners were sent to Coventry, which was a Parliamentarian stronghold, leading to the association of the city with exclusion. Another theory posits that soldiers captured in Coventry were particularly badly treated.
