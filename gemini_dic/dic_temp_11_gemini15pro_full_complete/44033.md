crimsoning:/ˈkrɪmzənɪŋ/ | v. | 变红，脸红 |  The sky was crimsoning as the sun set. | The sunset was crimsoning the clouds. | blushing, reddening | paling, blanching | verb

Etymology:
From crimson + -ing. Crimson itself comes from the Old Spanish "cremesin," ultimately derived from the Arabic "qirmiz," referring to the kermes insect, from which a red dye was obtained.
