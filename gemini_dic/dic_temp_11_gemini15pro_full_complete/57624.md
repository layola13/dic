draughtily:/ˈdrɑːf.tɪ.li/ | adv. | 有穿堂风地；冷飕飕地 | In a way that allows currents of cold air to flow through. | The old house was draughtily ventilated, making it quite chilly. 这栋老房子通风不好，冷飕飕的。| The windows were ill-fitting and the room was draughtily cold. 窗户不合缝，房间里冷飕飕的。| 近义词: airily, coldly, breezily | 反义词: warmly, snugly | 词性: 副词

draught:/drɑːft/ | n. |  气流，通风；草稿；汇票 | A current of cool air in a room or other enclosed space; a preliminary version of a piece of writing; an order for a bank to pay a stated sum from the drawer's account. | I felt a draught and closed the window. 我感到一阵冷风，就把窗户关上了。| She's working on the first draught of her novel. 她正在写她小说的初稿。 | 近义词: breeze, current, draft (US) | 反义词: stillness | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Middle English *draught, *draȝt, from Old English *draht, *droht (“a pull, a draught, act of drawing, a stroke, a team of draught animals”), from Proto-Germanic *drahtiz (“a pull, a draught”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰregʰ- (“to pull, to draw”). Cognate with Dutch draft (“draft, drawing”), German Tracht (“load, costume, fashion”), Icelandic dráttr (“draught, drawing”). Related to draw.  The adverbial form "draughtily" is formed by adding the suffix "-ily" which is a variant of "-ly" used after words ending in -y.
