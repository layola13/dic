lenis:/ˈliːnɪs/| adj.|弱的，不送气的|Characterized by weakness or lack of force, especially in the articulation of consonants; unaspirated.  A lenis consonant is produced with relatively little muscular tension and breath force.|The sounds /b/, /d/, and /ɡ/ are lenis consonants in English.|近义词：weak, soft, lax|反义词：fortis, strong, tense|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Latin *lēnis, from *lēn-, seen also in lēvis "smooth."  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *lē- "to stick, adhere, be sticky," whence also "smooth, soft."
