ostracizing: /ˈɑːstrəsaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 排斥，孤立 |  The act of excluding someone from a group or society by common consent. |  They were ostracizing her because of her beliefs.  他们因为她的信仰而排斥她。 | synonyms: excluding, shunning, banishing, boycotting, blackballing, cold-shouldering | antonyms: including, welcoming, embracing, accepting | verb

ostracize: /ˈɑːstrəsaɪz/ | v. | 排斥，孤立 | To exclude from a group or society by common consent. | He was ostracized by his colleagues for his controversial views.  由于他备受争议的观点，他被同事们排斥了。| synonyms: exclude, shun, banish, boycott, blackball | antonyms: include, welcome, embrace, accept | verb


Etymology:  From Greek ostrakizein, from ostrakon 'shell, potsherd'.  In ancient Athens, ostracism was a procedure by which citizens could vote to banish a person deemed dangerous to the state.  The votes were cast on potsherds.
