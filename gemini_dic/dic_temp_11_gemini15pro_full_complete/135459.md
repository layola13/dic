|n.| 非普遍性；非渗透性；不普及 |The nonpervasivenesses of certain technologies in developing countries hinders their economic growth.  某些技术在发展中国家的非普遍性阻碍了它们的经济增长。| The report highlighted the nonpervasivenesses of digital literacy across various demographics.  该报告强调了不同人群中数字素养的非普及性。 |absence, rarity, scarcity| pervasiveness, prevalence, ubiquity| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + pervasiveness + -es.
* **non-**: a prefix meaning "not," "opposite of," "lack of," freely used as an English formative, usually with native or Latin-derived elements.
* **pervasiveness**: the quality of being pervasive; spreading widely throughout an area or a group of people. From pervasive + -ness.
* **pervasive**: spreading widely throughout an area or a group of people. From pervade + -ive.
* **pervade**:  spread through and be perceived in every part of. From Latin *pervadere* "to go through, traverse, penetrate," from *per* "through" + *vadere* "to go."
* **-ness**: a native English suffix attached to adjectives and participles, forming abstract nouns denoting quality or state (goodness, kindness, obligingness, preparedness).
* **-es**: plural suffix.
