clamourer:/ˈklæmərər/ (US) /ˈklæmərə(r)/ (UK)| n.|喧闹者；大声疾呼者；强烈要求者|A person who clamours; someone who shouts loudly and persistently, especially in demand or protest.|The clamourers outside the government building demanded immediate action. 政府大楼外的抗议者要求立即采取行动。|Near synonyms: protester, agitator, shouter, advocate, campaigner|Near antonyms: conformist, quietist|noun


clamor:/ˈklæmər/ (US) /ˈklæmə(r)/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.|喧闹；大声疾呼；强烈要求|1.(n.) A loud and persistent outcry from many people. 2.(vi.) To shout loudly and insistently. 3.(vt.) To demand something insistently.|The clamor of the crowd grew louder as the team scored. 球队得分时，人群的欢呼声越来越大。  The protesters clamored for change. 抗议者大声疾呼要求改变。 The fans clamored the band to play their hit song. 歌迷们强烈要求乐队演奏他们的热门歌曲。|Near synonyms: uproar, din, outcry, hubbub (n.); yell, shout, bellow (v.)|Near antonyms: silence, quiet, stillness (n.); whisper, murmur (v.)|noun, verb intransitive, verb transitive
