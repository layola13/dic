conceitedness's:/kənˈsiːtɪdnəsɪz/ | n. |自负；骄傲自大| The quality or state of being conceited; excessive pride in oneself.  |  His conceitedness's evident in his constant boasting. 他的自负表现在他不断的吹嘘上。 |  Synonyms: vanity, arrogance, egotism, pride, self-importance | Antonyms: humility, modesty, humbleness | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From conceited + -ness.  Conceited comes from Middle French conceu, past participle of concevoir  "to conceive, understand," from Latin concipere  "to take in, conceive,"  from com- "together" + capere "to take."  The suffix -ness forms nouns from adjectives.
