mortise: /ˈmɔːrtɪs/ (British) /ˈmɔːrtɪs/ (American)| n. vt.|榫眼，榫孔；开榫眼；接合|A hole or recess cut into a piece of wood or other material, designed to receive a corresponding projection (a tenon) so as to form a joint.|The carpenter carefully cut a mortise in the wood.  木匠小心地在木头上凿了一个榫眼。|He mortised the two pieces of wood together. 他把两块木头用榫卯接合。|近义词：slot, hole, recess, socket|反义词：tenon|词性: noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mortaise*, from *morteis*, past participle of *morteir* "to bite," from Latin *mordēre* "to bite."
