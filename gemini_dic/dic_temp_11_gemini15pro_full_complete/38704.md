condescending:/ˌkɒndɪˈsendɪŋ/ /ˌkɑːndɪˈsendɪŋ/| adj.|居高临下的，屈尊的|Showing or characterized by a patronizing or superior attitude.|例句：He spoke in a condescending tone, as if he were explaining something to a child. 他说话的语气很 condescending，好像在跟小孩解释什么。|例句：She gave me a condescending smile, making me feel small and insignificant. 她 condescending 地笑了笑，让我觉得自己很渺小和微不足道。|近义词：patronizing, supercilious, haughty|反义词：humble, respectful, egalitarian|词性:adjective

condescend:/ˌkɒndɪˈsend/ /ˌkɑːndɪˈsend/| v.|屈尊，俯就|Do something that one considers to be below one's dignity.|例句：He condescended to help me with my homework, even though he thought it was very easy. 即使他认为我的作业很简单，他还是 condescending 地帮我做。|例句：The CEO rarely condescended to speak with the lower-level employees. 首席执行官很少 condescending 地与低级别员工交谈。|近义词：deign, stoop, patronize|反义词：respect, admire|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *condēscendere, from Latin condēscendō (“I descend, stoop, condescend”), present active of condēscendō, from con- (“together, with”) + dēscendō (“I climb down, descend”).
