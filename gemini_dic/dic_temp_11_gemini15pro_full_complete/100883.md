intrusiveness:/ɪnˈtruːsɪvnəs/
|n.|侵扰性；打扰性；唐突|The intrusiveness of the paparazzi made her life miserable. 狗仔队的侵扰使她的生活很痛苦。|
|近义词:interference, intrusion, imposition, presumptuousness|
|反义词:politeness, discretion, respect, reserve|
|词性:noun|

intrude:/ɪnˈtruːd/
|v.|侵入；闯入；打扰|Don't intrude on their privacy. 不要侵犯他们的隐私。|
|近义词:interfere, interrupt, impose, infringe|
|反义词:respect, withdraw, abstain|
|词性:verb|

intrusive:/ɪnˈtruːsɪv/
|adj.|侵入的；打扰的；唐突的|The constant phone calls were intrusive and annoying.  不断的电话让人烦扰不安。|
|近义词:interfering, interrupting, imposing, presumptuous|
|反义词:polite, discreet, respectful, reserved|
|词性:adjective|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin intrusus (“thrust in, obtruded”), past participle of intrudere (“to thrust in”), from in- (“in, into”) + trudere (“to thrust, push”).
