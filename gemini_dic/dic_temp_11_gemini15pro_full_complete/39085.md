confounding:/kənˈfaʊndɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人困惑的；混淆的；使人惊慌的 |  Causing surprise or confusion, especially in a way that makes you feel less confident or less sure about something. |  The confounding results of the study left the researchers puzzled.  这项研究令人困惑的结果让研究人员感到不解。| synonyms: puzzling, bewildering, perplexing, baffling | antonyms: clarifying, elucidating | adjective

confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/ | vt. | 使困惑；混淆；挫败 | To surprise or confuse someone by acting against their expectations. To prove someone or something wrong. |  His recovery confounded the doctors. 他的康复使医生们感到困惑。 The strategy confounded their opponents. 这个策略挫败了他们的对手。| synonyms: confuse, bewilder, perplex, frustrate | antonyms: clarify, enlighten | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *confondre*, from Latin confundere "to pour together, mix up, confuse, confound," from com- "together" (see com-) + fundere "to pour, melt" (see found (v.2)).
