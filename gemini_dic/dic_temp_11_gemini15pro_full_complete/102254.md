iterator:/ˈɪtəreɪtər/| n.|迭代器|An object that enables a programmer to traverse a container, particularly lists, and access each element sequentially without needing to know the underlying implementation of the container.|The iterator made it easy to process each item in the linked list.|近义词:enumerator, cursor|反义词:N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *iteratus*, past participle of *iterare* "to repeat," from *iterum* "again."  Coined in the context of computer programming.
