contracture:/kənˈtræktʃər/| n. |挛缩|A permanent shortening of a muscle or joint, often resulting from injury or disease.|The patient developed a contracture in his leg after the accident.  病人事故后腿部发生了挛缩。|Synonyms: shortening, constriction, tightening|Antonyms: extension, lengthening, relaxation|noun

contracture:/kənˈtræktʃər/| n. |挛缩|The abnormal shortening of muscle tissue, rendering the muscle highly resistant to passive stretching.|She suffered from a hand contracture that made it difficult to write. 她患有手挛缩，导致难以书写。|Synonyms: rigidity, stiffness, inflexibility|Antonyms: flexibility, suppleness, pliability|noun
