doctrinist:/ˈdɒktrɪnɪst/ | n. | 教条主义者；学说拥护者 | A person who adheres rigidly to a doctrine or set of beliefs, especially in religion or politics. |  Example 1: The doctrinist refused to consider any viewpoints that challenged his established beliefs.  这位教条主义者拒绝考虑任何挑战他既定信仰的观点。

Example 2:  He was known as a doctrinist, unwilling to compromise on even the smallest details of his ideology. 他以教条主义者闻名，即使是他意识形态中最小的细节也不愿妥协。 |  Synonyms: dogmatist, ideologue, rigid believer | Antonyms: freethinker, pragmatist, skeptic | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *doctriniste*, from doctrine + -iste. Ultimately from Latin *doctrina* (teaching, instruction).
