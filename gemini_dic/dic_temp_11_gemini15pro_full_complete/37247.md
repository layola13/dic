comfrey: /ˈkʌmfri/ (US), /ˈkʌmfri/ (UK) | n. | 紫草科聚合草 | A perennial herb (Symphytum officinale) of the borage family, native to Eurasia, having hairy leaves and small bell-shaped flowers, typically purple, pink, or white, and thick, black, rhizomatous roots. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine. | Comfrey is known for its ability to soothe burns and promote healing. 聚合草以其舒缓烧伤和促进愈合的能力而闻名。 | Comfrey poultices were traditionally used to treat broken bones.  聚合草膏药传统上用于治疗骨折。 | synonyms: knitbone, boneset, bruisewort | antonyms: N/A | noun
