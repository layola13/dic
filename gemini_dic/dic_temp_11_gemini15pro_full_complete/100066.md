interpolater:/ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪtər/ | n. |内插器，插值器|A person or device that interpolates.  Someone or something that estimates unknown values between two known values.|The interpolater smoothly connected the two data points on the graph. 内插器平滑地连接了图表上的两个数据点。|interpolator, estimator|extrapolator|noun


interpolate:/ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪt/ | v. |插入，插值；篡改|Insert (something of a different nature) into something else; introduce (words) into a text or conversation, typically with the intention of falsifying it.|He interpolated a few remarks while I was speaking. 我讲话时，他插了几句话。|insert, introduce, add|delete, remove, erase|verb


interpolation:/ɪnˌtɜːrpəˈleɪʃn/ | n. |插入，插值；内插法|The action of interpolating; a remark or passage interpolated into a text.|The interpolation of the data allowed for a more accurate prediction. 数据的插值允许更精确的预测。|insertion, introduction, addition|deletion, removal, erasure|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin interpolatus, past participle of interpolare (“to refurbish, polish up, alter”), from inter (“between”) + polire (“to polish”).
