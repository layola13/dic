eugen:/ˈjuːdʒən/ |n.|优生；优生学|The science of improving a human population by controlled breeding to increase the occurrence of desirable heritable characteristics. Developed largely by Francis Galton as a method of improving the human race, it fell into disfavor only after the perversion of its doctrines by the Nazis.|Eugenics was a popular social movement in the early 20th century. 优生学在20世纪早期是一个流行的社会运动。|  He studied the history of eugenics. 他研究了优生学史。|Synonyms:  genetic engineering, genetic modification|Antonyms: dysgenics|Noun
