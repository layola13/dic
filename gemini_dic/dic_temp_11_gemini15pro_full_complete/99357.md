interdependability:/ˌɪntərˌdɪpɛndəˈbɪləti/| n.|相互依存性，互相依赖性|The quality or state of being interdependent; the degree to which two or more things rely on each other.|The global economy highlights the interdependability of nations.|近义词：interdependence, reliance, dependency|反义词:independence, autonomy|词性:名词

interdependent:/ˌɪntərˌdɪˈpɛndənt/| adj.|相互依存的，互相依赖的|Mutually reliant; depending on each other.|The two companies are interdependent; one supplies the raw materials, and the other manufactures the final product.|近义词：interrelated, interconnected, mutually dependent|反义词:independent, autonomous|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From inter- + depend + -able + -ity.  "Inter-" is a Latin prefix meaning "between, among, mutually." "Depend" comes from the Latin "dependere," meaning "to hang down from."  "-able" is a suffix meaning "capable of being," and "-ity" is a suffix forming nouns of quality or state.
