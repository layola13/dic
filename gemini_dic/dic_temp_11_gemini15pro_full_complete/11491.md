arianism:/ˈɛəriənɪzəm/ | n. | 阿里乌斯教; 阿里乌主义 | A heresy denying the divinity of Christ, originating with Arius, a presbyter of Alexandria (died 336), who held that Christ was created by God and was therefore neither coeternal with the Father nor consubstantial. |  Arianism was condemned as heresy by the Council of Nicaea in 325.  阿里乌斯教在325年的尼西亚会议上被定为异端。 |  The spread of Arianism caused significant upheaval in the early Church. 阿里乌斯主义的传播引起了早期教会的巨大动荡。 | Near synonyms: heresy, heterodoxy | Antonyms: orthodoxy, Trinitarianism | noun
