diversionist:/daɪˈvɜːrʒənɪst/ 
n.
转移注意者；扰乱者
someone who uses diversionary tactics
The diversionist planted a bomb threat to draw attention away from the real robbery.  转移注意的人放置了炸弹威胁，以转移人们对真正抢劫的注意力。

diversionary:/daɪˈvɜːrʃənəri/
adj.
转移注意力的；牵制的
intended to distract attention from something more important
The government launched a diversionary attack to cover up its economic failures. 政府发动了一次牵制性攻击，以掩盖其经济上的失败。

diversion:/daɪˈvɜːrʒən/
n.
转移；消遣；绕道
the act of turning something aside from its course; an activity that diverts the mind; a different route used because a road is closed
The magician's sleight of hand was a clever diversion.  魔术师的手法是一种巧妙的转移注意力的方式。
Reading is a relaxing diversion. 阅读是一种放松的消遣。
There's a diversion in place because of roadworks. 因为道路施工，现在有绕行路线。

词源: From Late Latin *dīversionem, accusative of *dīversio (“a turning aside”), from dīvertere (“to turn aside”), from dis- (“apart”) + vertere (“to turn”).

近义词:saboteur, agitator, troublemaker, disruptor
反义词:conformist, loyalist
