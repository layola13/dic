maverick: /ˈmævərɪk/ (US) /ˈmævərɪk/ (UK)| n. adj.| 特立独行的人，持不同意见的人；不墨守成规的，标新立异的|An independent individual who does not go along with a group or party; unconventional, independent-minded.|例句：He was a maverick in the Senate, often voting against his party. 他在参议院是个特立独行的人，经常投票反对自己的党派。|近义词：nonconformist, rebel, dissident, individualist, iconoclast|反义词：conformist, follower, traditionalist|词性: 名词，形容词

maverick: /ˈmævərɪk/ (US) /ˈmævərɪk/ (UK)| adj.| 不墨守成规的，标新立异的|Unconventional, independent-minded.|例句：She took a maverick approach to the problem, ignoring conventional wisdom. 她用一种不墨守成规的方法来解决这个问题，忽略了传统的智慧。|近义词：unconventional, unorthodox, independent, innovative|反义词：conventional, orthodox, traditional|词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Samuel Augustus Maverick (1803-1870), a Texas rancher who didn't brand his cattle.
