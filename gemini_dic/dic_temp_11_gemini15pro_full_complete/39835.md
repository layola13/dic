conspirer:/kənˈspaɪərər/| n.|共谋者，阴谋者|A person who takes part in a conspiracy; a plotter.|例句：The conspirers were arrested before they could carry out their plan. 阴谋者在实施计划之前被逮捕了。

例句：The conspirers met in secret to discuss their plot.  阴谋分子秘密会面，讨论他们的阴谋。|近义词：plotter, schemer, collaborator|反义词:loyalist, opponent|词性:noun

conspire:/kənˈspaɪər/| v.|密谋，共谋；协力促成|To secretly plan with others to do something illegal or harmful; to work together to bring about a particular result, typically unintentionally.|例句：They conspired to overthrow the government. 他们密谋推翻政府。

例句：The weather conspired to ruin our picnic. 天气合力破坏了我们的野餐。|近义词：plot, scheme, collude|反义词:expose, reveal|词性:verb

Etymology:
From Old French conspirer, from Latin conspirare "to agree, unite, plot," literally "to breathe together," from com- "together" (see com-) + spirare "to breathe" (see spirit (n.)).
