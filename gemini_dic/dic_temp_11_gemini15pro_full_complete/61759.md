empathise:/ˈɛmpəθaɪz/ (UK) /ˈɛmpəˌθaɪz/ (US)| v. |同情，感同身受|To understand and share the feelings of another.|I can empathise with your situation, having been through something similar myself. 我能对你的处境感同身受，因为我自己也经历过类似的事情。|近义词：sympathize, understand, relate, share|反义词：disregard, ignore|词性：动词

empathize:/ˈɛmpəθaɪz/ (UK) /ˈɛmpəˌθaɪz/ (US)| v. |移情，神入|To project one's personality into (and so fully comprehend) the object of contemplation.|He empathized with the fictional character. 他对那个虚构的角色产生了移情作用。|近义词：identify, connect|反义词：detach, alienate|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From modern Latin *empathia*, from Greek empatheia, from em- 'in' + pathos 'feeling'.
