indentureships:/ɪnˈdɛntʃəʃɪps/| n.|契约劳工制度；包身工制度|A system of bonded labor, where a person signs a contract (indenture) to work for a particular employer, often in another country, for a fixed period of time, typically in exchange for passage to the new country. The system was often exploitative and akin to slavery.|Indentureships were common in the 19th and early 20th centuries, particularly for laborers from India, China, and other Asian countries working in colonies throughout the world.|近义词：bonded labor, servitude, forced labor|反义词:freedom, liberty|词性:noun
