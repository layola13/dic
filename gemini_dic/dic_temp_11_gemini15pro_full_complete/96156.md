indentureship:/ɪnˈdɛntʃəʃɪp/| n. |契约劳工制；包身工制|A system of labor in which a person is bound to work for another for a specified period of time, often in exchange for passage to a new country or the learning of a trade.| Many Indians came to the Caribbean under indentureship in the 19th century. 许多印度人在19世纪以契约劳工的身份来到加勒比地区。|Synonyms: servitude, bondage, apprenticeship |Antonyms: freedom, liberty | noun
