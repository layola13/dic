jingoistically:/ˌdʒɪŋɡoʊˈɪstɪkli/ | adv. | 侵略好战地；沙文主义地 | In a manner characterized by extreme patriotism, especially in the form of aggressive or warlike foreign policy.  |  He spoke jingoistically about the need to defend the nation's honor. 他以沙文主义的口吻谈论捍卫国家荣誉的必要性。 | Examples: The politician jingoistically called for war against the neighboring country.  这位政客狂热地叫嚣要对邻国开战。 The newspaper published a jingoistically editorial supporting the invasion. 这家报纸发表了一篇侵略好战的社论，支持入侵。 | 近义词：chauvinistically, patriotically (excessively), belligerently, aggressively | 反义词：peacefully, diplomatically, pacifistically | 词性: adverb

词源: From jingoist + -ically.  "Jingoist" originated in Britain during the Russo-Turkish War (1877-78) from a popular music-hall song with the refrain "By Jingo!".  The song expressed strong support for British intervention against Russia.
