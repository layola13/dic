atropine:/ˈætrəpiːn/| n.|阿托品|A poisonous crystalline alkaloid extracted from deadly nightshade and other related plants. It is used as a muscle relaxant, to dilate the pupil of the eye, and as an antidote for some poisons.|Atropine is used to treat certain types of nerve agent and pesticide poisonings.|Synonyms: hyoscyamine, daturine|Antonyms:  physostigmine, pilocarpine|noun
