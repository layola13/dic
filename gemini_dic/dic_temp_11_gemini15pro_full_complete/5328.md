alloxan:/ˈælɒksæn/ (UK) /əˈlɑːksæn/ (US)| n.|四氧嘧啶|A crystalline compound, C₄H₂N₂O₄, formed by the oxidation of uric acid. It is used to induce diabetes in experimental animals.|Alloxan is often used in research to induce diabetes in laboratory animals. 四氧嘧啶常用于研究中，以诱导实验动物患糖尿病。|Synonyms:  tetraoxo-pyrimidine |Antonyms: None|Noun
