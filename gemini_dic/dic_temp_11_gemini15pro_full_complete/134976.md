nonnaturalism:/ˌnɒnˈnætʃrəlɪzəm/
n.|非自然主义|The philosophical view that moral properties exist but are not natural properties, often understood as not being reducible to or identifiable with properties studied by the natural sciences.  It often involves the idea that moral properties are sui generis, or of a unique kind.|Moore's work is considered a landmark contribution to nonnaturalism in ethics.|近义词:moral realism, ethical objectivism|反义词:naturalism, moral skepticism|词性:noun

Etymology:
non- (prefix meaning "not") + naturalism (from natural + -ism)
