overembellishing:/ˌoʊvərɪmˈbɛlɪʃɪŋ/
verb
过度装饰；过分修饰
Adding too many decorations or details to something, making it seem excessive or fussy.
The writer was accused of overembellishing the story to make it more exciting.  作者被指责为了使故事更刺激而过度渲染。
His account of the meeting was so overembellished that no one believed him. 他对会议的描述过于夸张，以至于没有人相信他。
近义词: exaggerating, overstating, embroidering, gilding the lily
反义词: understating, downplaying, simplifying
词性: verb (present participle)

overembellishment:/ˌoʊvərɪmˈbɛlɪʃmənt/
noun
过度装饰；过分修饰
The act of adding too many decorations or details, resulting in something excessive or fussy.
The overembellishment of the wedding cake made it look gaudy.  婚礼蛋糕的过度装饰使它看起来很俗气。
Her prose suffered from overembellishment, making it difficult to read. 她的散文因过度修饰而显得晦涩难懂。
近义词: exaggeration, overstatement, embroidery
反义词: understatement, simplification
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + embellish.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much". "Embellish" comes from the Old French "embellir," meaning "to make beautiful," ultimately from the Latin "bellus," meaning "beautiful."
