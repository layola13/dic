fjord:/fjɔːrd/ (British) /fiˈɔːrd/ (American)| n. |峡湾|A long, narrow, deep inlet of the sea between high cliffs, typically formed by submergence of a glaciated valley.|The Geirangerfjord is one of Norway's most visited fjords. 盖朗厄尔峡湾是挪威最受欢迎的峡湾之一。

The ship sailed into the fjord. 船驶入了峡湾。


|近义词：inlet, bay, cove|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps open sea)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Norwegian *fjǫrðr*, from Proto-Germanic *ferþuz (“fjord, firth”), from Proto-Indo-European *pr̥tús (“ford, passage”). Cognate with Old English *fiord*, *fyrþ* (“firth, inlet”), West Frisian *fjoerd* (“inlet, bay”), Dutch *voorde* (“ford”), German *Furt* (“ford”), Latin *portus* (“harbor, port”), Ancient Greek *πόρος* (*póros*, “ford, passage, way”).
