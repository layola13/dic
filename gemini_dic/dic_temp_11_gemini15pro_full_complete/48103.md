declinatory:/dɪˈklɪnətɔːri/ /dɪˈklɪnəˌtɔri/
adjective
拒绝的，推辞的；（语法）变格的
Relating to or expressing refusal or declination. In grammar, relating to the declension of nouns, pronouns, and adjectives.
He gave a declinatory response to the invitation. 他拒绝了邀请。
The committee took a declinatory stance on the proposed changes. 委员会对提议的修改持拒绝态度。
近义词:refusal, rejecting, negative, dismissive
反义词:accepting, affirmative, positive
词性:adjective

declinatory:/dɪˈklɪnətɔːri/ /dɪˈklɪnəˌtɔri/
noun
推辞；拒绝；（律）异议
An act of declining or refusing something; a statement of refusal. In law, a plea declining the jurisdiction of a court.
The defendant filed a declinatory plea. 被告提出了异议的抗辩。
His declinatory was unexpected. 他的拒绝出乎意料。
近义词:refusal, rejection, denial
反义词:acceptance, agreement
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin declīnātōrius, from Latin declīnāre  "to bend down, turn aside," from de- "down, away" + clināre "to bend, lean."
