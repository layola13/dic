alternate:/ˈɔːltərneɪt/ (US) /ˈɒltəneɪt/ (UK)| adj. v.| 交替的，轮流的；交替，轮流 |Occurring in turn repeatedly; to happen or exist one after the other repeatedly. |The flags are alternate red and white. 旗帜是红白相间的。|synonyms: rotating, successive, intermittent| antonyms: continuous, constant| adjective

alternate:/ˈɔːltərneɪt/ (US) /ˈɒltəneɪt/ (UK)| v.| 交替，轮流 |To do, use, or happen in successive turns; to make something do this. |She alternates between cheerfulness and deep despair. 她一会儿兴高采烈，一会儿又深陷绝望。|synonyms: rotate, interchange, switch| antonyms: persist, continue| verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *alternatus, past participle of alternare (“to do by turns”), from Latin alternus (“one after the other, by turns”), from alter (“other”).
