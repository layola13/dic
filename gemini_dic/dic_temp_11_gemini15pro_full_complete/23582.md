brake-off:/ˈbreɪk ɒf/ (UK) /ˈbreɪk ɔːf/ (US)
n.|中断，中止，决裂|A sudden ending or cessation, especially of a relationship or discussion.|例句：There was a sudden brake-off in the negotiations. 谈判突然中止了。|近义词：break, rupture, cessation, discontinuation|反义词：continuation, resumption|词性:名词

brake-off:/ˈbreɪk ɒf/ (UK) /ˈbreɪk ɔːf/ (US)
v.|中断，中止，决裂|To suddenly end or cease, especially a relationship or discussion.|例句：The two countries broke off diplomatic relations.  这两个国家断绝了外交关系。|近义词：break, sever, discontinue, terminate|反义词：continue, resume, establish|词性:动词

Etymology:
From the verb "break" + the adverb/preposition "off".  "Break" has a long history, originating from Proto-Germanic *brekaną, meaning "to shatter, burst".  "Off" comes from Old English "of," meaning "away from, off."
