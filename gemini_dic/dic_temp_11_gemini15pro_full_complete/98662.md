insularisms:/ˈɪnsjələrɪzəmz/ | n. | 狭隘性，岛民性，偏狭观念 |  The narrow-mindedness or peculiarities characteristic of isolated communities or people.  Features or qualities that are considered characteristic of islanders, especially a narrowness of viewpoint resulting from isolation or lack of contact with other people or cultures. |  The islanders' insularisms made it difficult for them to adapt to life on the mainland. 岛民的狭隘性使他们难以适应大陆的生活。| 近义词：narrow-mindedness, provincialism, parochialism | 反义词：cosmopolitanism, worldliness, open-mindedness | 词性: noun

词源: From insular + -ism.  "Insular" comes from the Latin "insularis," meaning "of an island," from "insula," meaning "island."  The suffix "-ism" forms nouns denoting a doctrine, belief, or practice.
