encrustations: /ɪnˌkrʌˈsteɪʃənz/
| n. |结壳；硬壳；外壳；包覆物|A hard coating or deposit of minerals or other substances on a surface.|The ancient vase was covered in encrustations of dirt and grime. 古老的花瓶上覆盖着污垢和尘垢的硬壳。|Synonyms: coating, covering, crust, deposit, layer, film, scale|Antonyms: core, interior, inside|Noun

encrustation: /ɪnˌkrʌˈsteɪʃən/
| n. |嵌入；镶嵌|The process of becoming covered with or forming a crust or hard coating.|The encrustation of the pipes with minerals reduced water flow. 管道上矿物质的积聚减少了水流量。|Synonyms: accretion, accumulation, deposition|Antonyms: erosion, dissolution, removal|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *encrustacioun*, from Late Latin *incrustātiō*, from Latin *incrustātus*, past participle of *incrustāre* (“to encrust”), from *in-* + *crusta* (“crust, rind, bark, shell”).
