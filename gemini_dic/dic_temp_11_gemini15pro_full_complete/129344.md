noisomely:/ˈnɔɪsəmli/| adv.|恶臭地；令人厌恶地；有害地|In an offensive, disgusting, or harmful manner, typically referring to smell but also applicable to other unpleasant qualities.|The garbage dump noisomely filled the air with the stench of decay. 垃圾场恶臭地弥漫着腐烂的臭味。|The factory noisomely discharged pollutants into the river.  这家工厂令人厌恶地向河里排放污染物。|近义词：offensively, disgustingly, repulsively, objectionably|反义词：pleasantly, agreeably, delightfully|词性:副词

词源:
From Middle English *noisomli, from noisom +‎ -ly.  "Noisome" itself comes from Anglo-Norman *noisum, noisous (“harmful, annoying”), and Old French *nuis-, nuis-, nois- (“harm, injury, annoyance”), from Latin nocēre (“to hurt, harm, injure”).
