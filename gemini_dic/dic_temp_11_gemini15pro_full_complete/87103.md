heralding:/ˈhɛrəldɪŋ/| v. |预示，预告|Be a sign that (something) is about to happen.|例句：The robin is heralding the arrival of spring. 知更鸟预示着春天的到来。

例句：The cooler weather heralded the beginning of autumn.  凉爽的天气预示着秋天的开始。|近义词：announcing, proclaiming, signaling, foreshadowing|反义词：concealing, hiding, suppressing|词性:动词(present participle of herald)

herald:/ˈhɛrəld/ | n. vt.| 预兆，使者；预示，预报|A person or thing viewed as a sign that something is about to happen.|例句：The president's speech heralded a new era of economic growth. 总统的讲话预示着经济增长的新时代。

例句：The dark clouds heralded a coming storm. 乌云预示着暴风雨的来临。|近义词：messenger, harbinger, announce, proclaim|反义词：conceal, suppress|词性:名词, 动词
