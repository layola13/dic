mistrusters:/ˌmɪsˈtrʌstərz/
n.
不信任者，怀疑者
People who mistrust others; those who are suspicious or lack confidence in someone or something.
The mistrusters questioned the motives of the new CEO.  不信任者质疑新任首席执行官的动机。
The group of mistrusters voiced their concerns about the proposed changes.  这群不信任者表达了他们对拟议变更的担忧。
近义词: skeptics, doubters, cynics
反义词: believers, trusters, optimists
词性: noun (plural)

mistrust:/ˌmɪsˈtrʌst/
v.
不信任，怀疑
To have doubts about the honesty or reliability of someone or something.
She mistrusts anyone who offers help too readily. 她不信任任何太容易提供帮助的人。
He had learned to mistrust his own judgment. 他已经学会不相信自己的判断了。
近义词: doubt, suspect, disbelieve
反义词: trust, believe, confide
词性: verb

mistrust:/ˌmɪsˈtrʌst/
n.
不信任，怀疑
Lack of trust or confidence.
There is a deep mistrust between the two communities. 这两个社群之间存在着深深的不信任。
His actions bred mistrust among his colleagues. 他的行为在他同事之间滋生了不信任。
近义词: suspicion, doubt, distrustfulness
反义词: trust, confidence, faith
词性: noun
