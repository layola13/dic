disreputably: /dɪsˈrɛpjətəbli/ | adv. | 败坏名声地，声名狼藉地 |  In a manner that damages one's reputation; dishonorably. | He acted disreputably and was ostracized by the community. 他行为不端，被社区排斥。|  disgracefully, dishonorably, shamefully | respectably, honorably | adverb

Etymology:
From dis- + reputable + -ly.  "Dis-" is a prefix indicating negation or reversal. "Reputable" comes from the Latin word "reputare" meaning "to think over, consider, reckon."  "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs.
