injudiciousnesses:/ˌɪndʒʊˈdɪʃəsnəsɪz/
| n. |不明智；欠考虑；轻率|The state or quality of being injudicious; lack of judgment or discretion. |His injudiciousnesses led to a series of unfortunate events.|近义词：foolishness, recklessness, imprudence|反义词：wisdom, prudence, judiciousness|词性:名词

Etymology:
From injudicious + -ness.  Injudicious itself comes from in- (not) + judicious (wise, showing good judgment), from Latin iudicium "judgment."
