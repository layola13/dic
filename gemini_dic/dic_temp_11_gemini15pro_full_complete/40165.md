contend:/kənˈtend/ | v. | 争夺，竞争；声称，主张 | to strive in opposition or against difficulties; to assert something as a position in an argument |  He contended that the judge's decision was wrong. 他声称法官的判决是错误的。 | Synonyms: argue, assert, compete, vie, maintain | Antonyms: concede, yield, surrender, acquiesce | verb

contends:/kənˈtendz/ | v. | (第三人称单数)争夺，竞争；声称，主张 |  (Third-person singular simple present form of "contend") | She contends that the theory is flawed. 她认为该理论是有缺陷的。 | Synonyms: argues, asserts, competes, vies, maintains | Antonyms: concedes, yields, surrenders, acquiesces | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contendere, frequentative of Latin contendere  "to stretch out, strive, contend," from com- "together" (see com-) + tendere "to stretch" (from PIE root *ten- "to stretch").
