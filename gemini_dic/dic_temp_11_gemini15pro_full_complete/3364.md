ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *affrighten*, from Old English *āfyrhtan*, from *ā-* (intensive prefix) + *fyrhtan* (“to frighten”), from Proto-Germanic *furhtōną* (“to frighten”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰerǵʰ-* (“high; to be excited, agitated”). Cognate with Dutch *vervrichten* (“to perform, accomplish”), German *fürchten* (“to fear”), Gothic *𐍆𐌰𐌿𐍂𐌷𐍄𐌰𐌽* (faurhtan, “to be afraid”). More at fright.
