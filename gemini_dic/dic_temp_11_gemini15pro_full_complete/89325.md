homogenised: /həˈmɒdʒənaɪzd/ /həˈmɑːdʒənaɪzd/
|adj.|均质的，均匀的|Made uniform or consistent throughout a given space or substance.  A mixture that has been homogenized has had its different parts mixed together thoroughly so that they are evenly distributed.|The milk is homogenised to prevent the cream from separating.|Synonyms: uniform, consistent, standardized, unvarying|Antonyms: heterogeneous, varied, diverse|adjective

homogenize: /həˈmɒdʒənaɪz/ /həˈmɑːdʒənaɪz/
|v.|使均匀，使均质|To make something uniform or consistent throughout.|The company aims to homogenize its global operations.|Synonyms: standardize, blend, mix, unify|Antonyms: diversify, vary, differentiate|verb

homogenization: /həˌmɒdʒənaɪˈzeɪʃən/ /həˌmɑːdʒənaɪˈzeɪʃən/
|n.|均质化，均匀化|The process of making something uniform or consistent throughout.|The homogenization of culture can lead to a loss of local traditions.|Synonyms: standardization, blending, mixing, unification|Antonyms: diversification, variation, differentiation|noun
