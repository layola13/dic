overromanticized: /ˌoʊvərroʊˈmæntɪsaɪzd/ /ˌəʊvərroʊˈmæntɪsaɪzd/ | adj. | 过于浪漫化的，过度理想化的 |  Having portrayed or thought of something as more romantic or idyllic than it actually is.  It implies a distorted or unrealistic view, often ignoring negative aspects or complexities. | The movie offers an overromanticized view of war, ignoring the brutality and suffering involved. 这部电影对战争进行了过于浪漫化的描述，忽略了战争的残酷和苦难。


The novel presents an overromanticized portrait of rural life, conveniently omitting the hardships and poverty. 这部小说描绘了过于浪漫化的乡村生活，有意地忽略了其中的艰辛和贫困。

近义词: idealized, glamorized, sentimentalized
反义词: realistic, pragmatic, cynical
词性: adjective


词源:
"Over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "romanticized" (past participle of "romanticize"). "Romanticize" comes from "romantic" + "-ize" (suffix meaning "to make or become"). "Romantic" ultimately derives from the Old French word "romanz," referring to a vernacular narrative.
