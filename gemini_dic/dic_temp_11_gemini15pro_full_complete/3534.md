against the grain:/əˈɡenst ðə ɡreɪn/ | idiom |违背本性；格格不入|Going against the grain means doing something that is difficult or uncomfortable because it contradicts one's natural inclinations or beliefs.|例句：It goes against the grain to accept charity. 接受施舍违背我的本性。|例句：Asking for help goes against the grain for him.  对他来说寻求帮助是违反他本性的。|近义词：against one's nature, contrary to one's principles, reluctantly, unwillingly |反义词: naturally, willingly, easily | idiom


词源: This idiom originates from carpentry and woodworking.  The "grain" refers to the direction of the wood fibers.  Working "against the grain" makes the process more difficult and can result in a rough, splintered finish. The metaphorical meaning developed from this literal sense.
