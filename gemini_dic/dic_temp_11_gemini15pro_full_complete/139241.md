obligation: /ˌɒblɪˈɡeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌɒblɪˈɡeɪʃən/ (UK)| n.|义务，责任，债务|A duty or commitment.|例句：She felt a strong obligation to help her family. 她感到有责任帮助她的家人。

例句：He has a legal obligation to pay taxes. 他有依法纳税的义务。

近义词：duty, responsibility, commitment, liability|反义词：exemption, freedom|词性：名词

obligate: /ˈɒblɪɡeɪt/ (US) /ˈɒblɪɡeɪt/ (UK)| vt.|使负有义务，强制|To bind morally or legally.|例句：The contract obligates him to pay $10,000.  合同规定他有义务支付1万美元。

例句：I felt obligated to help them after they helped me.  在他们帮助我之后，我感到有义务帮助他们。

近义词：compel, bind, require, force|反义词：exempt, release|词性：动词

obligated: /ˈɒblɪɡeɪtɪd/ (US) /ˈɒblɪɡeɪtɪd/ (UK)| adj.|有义务的，被强制的|Bound by a legal, moral, or other duty.|例句：I felt obligated to return the favor. 我觉得有义务回报这份恩情。

例句：She is obligated to care for her elderly parents. 她有义务照顾年迈的父母。

近义词：bound, compelled, required, duty-bound|反义词：exempt, free|词性：形容词
