contradict:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkt/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkt/ (US)| v. |反驳，驳斥；与…矛盾，与…相抵触|To assert the opposite of (a statement). To be in conflict with.|His account of the accident contradicts hers.  他对于事故的描述与她的说法相矛盾。|Synonyms: deny, dispute, gainsay, oppose|Antonyms: confirm, support, agree, corroborate|verb

contradiction:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkʃn/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkʃn/ (US)| n. |矛盾，反驳|The act of contradicting; a denial.|It's a contradiction to say you love someone and then treat them badly.  说你爱一个人却又对他们不好，这是自相矛盾的。|Synonyms: denial, disagreement, inconsistency|Antonyms: agreement, consistency, harmony|noun

contradictory:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪktəri/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪktəri/ (US)| adj. |互相矛盾的，对立的|Mutually opposed or inconsistent.|The witnesses gave contradictory accounts of the incident. 目击者对事件的描述互相矛盾。|Synonyms: conflicting, opposed, incompatible|Antonyms: consistent, compatible, harmonious|adjective
