grandstanding:/ˈɡrændˌstændɪŋ/ | n. |哗众取宠；作秀；炫耀 |The act of behaving in a way intended to impress or attract attention, especially by saying or doing things that are dramatic or unusual. |He was accused of political grandstanding. 他被指责玩弄政治作秀。|近义词：showboating, attention-seeking, posturing |反义词：modesty, humility |词性:名词

grandstand:/ˈɡrændˌstænd/ | v.i. |哗众取宠；作秀；炫耀|To behave in a way intended to impress or attract attention, especially by saying or doing things that are dramatic or unusual. |The senator was grandstanding for the cameras. 这位参议员对着摄像机哗众取宠。|近义词：show off, play to the gallery |反义词：be modest, be humble |词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "grandstand" (n.), originally referring to the main seating area in a stadium, often offering the best view.  The verb sense developed from the idea of performing or showing off in front of the grandstand.  "Grand" here means large or impressive.
