deadness's:/ˈdɛdnɪsɪz/
noun
死亡的状态；麻木；沉闷
The possessive form of "deadness," indicating belonging or association with the state of being dead, numb, or inactive.
The deadness's grip on the town was palpable.  这个城镇弥漫着 palpable 的死气沉沉。
The artist tried to capture the deadness's of the winter landscape.  艺术家试图捕捉冬日景象的死寂。
近义词: lifelessness, inactivity, dullness
反义词: liveliness, vibrancy, activity
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From dead + -ness + -'s (possessive).  Dead comes from Old English *dēad.  The suffix -ness forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.  The -'s is the possessive marker.
