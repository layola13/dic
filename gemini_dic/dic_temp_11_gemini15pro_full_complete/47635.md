debilities:/dɪˈbɪlɪtiz/
n.
虚弱，衰弱；缺陷
physical or mental weaknesses
His increasing debilities made it difficult for him to live independently. 他日益衰弱的身体使他难以独立生活。
Synonyms: weaknesses, frailties, infirmities, ailments
Antonyms: strengths, abilities, capabilities
noun

Etymology:
Late Middle English: from Old French debilite or late Latin debilitas, from Latin debilitare ‘weaken’, from de- ‘away, down’ + habilis ‘able’.
