karyoplasma: /ˌkæriəˈplæzmə/ | n. | 核质，核浆 | The fluid substance of a cell nucleus, excluding the nucleolus and chromatin.  The karyoplasm contains various enzymes, proteins, and other molecules involved in nuclear functions. | The karyoplasm plays a vital role in DNA replication and transcription. 核质在DNA复制和转录中起着至关重要的作用。 |  The researchers stained the karyoplasm to observe the distribution of specific proteins. 研究人员对核质进行染色以观察特定蛋白质的分布。 | synonym: nucleoplasm | antonym: cytoplasm | noun
