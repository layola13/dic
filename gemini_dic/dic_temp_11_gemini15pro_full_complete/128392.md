new-wave: /ˈnjuː weɪv/ (US), /ˈnjuː weɪv/ (UK)| n. adj.|新浪潮|A genre of rock music popular in the late 1970s and early 1980s, characterized by electronic sounds and often unconventional lyrics.  Can also be used more generally to describe any new trend or movement that breaks with tradition.|
例句：
The band's sound is reminiscent of 80s new-wave.  这支乐队的音乐让人想起80年代的新浪潮。

Many consider Blondie to be a quintessential new-wave band. 许多人认为金发女郎乐队是典型的新浪潮乐队。

近义词：post-punk, synth-pop|
反义词:traditional, mainstream|
词性: noun, adjective
