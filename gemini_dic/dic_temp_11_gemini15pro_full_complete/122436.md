mob:/mɑːb/ (US) /mɒb/ (UK)| n. |暴徒，乌合之众；暴民；(尤指鸟类或昆虫的)群；民众，群众|A large crowd of people, especially one that is disorderly and intent on causing trouble or violence; a flock or swarm of animals, especially birds or insects; the common people; the masses.|例句：The angry mob surged forward.  愤怒的暴徒向前冲去。

|例句：A mob of reporters surrounded the politician. 一群记者围住了这位政治家。


|近义词：crowd, horde, throng, swarm, mass|反义词:individual, few, elite|词性:名词


mob:/mɑːb/ (US) /mɒb/ (UK)| v. |围攻；成群围住|Crowd around (someone) in an unruly and intimidating way.|例句：Fans mobbed the singer as he left the concert hall. 歌迷们围攻了这位歌手，因为他离开了音乐厅。

|例句：The protesters mobbed the government building. 抗议者围攻了政府大楼。


|近义词：crowd, surround, besiege|反义词:disperse, scatter|词性:动词
