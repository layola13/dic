operatic:/ˌɒpəˈrætɪk/ (US) /ˌɒpəˈrætɪk/ (UK)| adj.|歌剧的；夸张的；做作的|Relating to or characteristic of opera; melodramatic, exaggerated, or theatrical in style.|Her reaction was quite operatic, complete with dramatic sighing and fainting. 她的反应非常戏剧化，充满了夸张的叹息和昏厥。|例句：The singer has an operatic voice. 这位歌手拥有歌剧般的嗓音。|近义词：dramatic, theatrical, histrionic|反义词：understated, subtle, restrained|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From opera + -ic.  "Opera" ultimately derives from the Latin word "opera," meaning "work, effort, care." The suffix "-ic" forms adjectives meaning "having to do with," "of the nature of," or "caused by."
