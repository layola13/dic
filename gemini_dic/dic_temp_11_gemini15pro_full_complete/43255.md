cradle:/ˈkreɪdl/ (US), /ˈkreɪdl/ (UK)| n. v.|摇篮，发源地，支架|A baby's bed, typically one that rocks; the place or region in which something began or was nurtured; a framework or support.|The baby slept soundly in its cradle. 婴儿在摇篮里睡得很香。|Synonyms: bassinet, crib, birthplace, origin, support|Antonyms: grave, end, destruction|noun

cradle:/ˈkreɪdl/ (US), /ˈkreɪdl/ (UK)| v.|轻轻地抱，抚育，保护|To hold gently and protectively; to nurture or protect in its early stages.|She cradled the newborn baby in her arms. 她轻轻地把新生儿抱在怀里。|Synonyms: hold, rock, nurture, protect, support|Antonyms: drop, neglect, abandon|verb
