muckworm:/ˈmʌkwɜːrm/| n.|卑鄙小人，吝啬鬼|A person who is considered despicable or contemptible, often because they are perceived as greedy or miserly.  It can also refer to someone who enjoys or is accustomed to dirty work.|He called his opponent a muckworm during the debate.  他在辩论中称他的对手为卑鄙小人。|近义词：scoundrel, villain, miser, skinflint|反义词：philanthropist, benefactor|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From muck + worm.  "Muck" refers to dirt, filth, or manure, while "worm" is used in a derogatory sense to refer to a weak or insignificant creature.  The combination suggests someone who grovels in or is associated with filth, either literally or figuratively.
