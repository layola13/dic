gadfly:/ˈɡædˌflaɪ/| n. |牛虻；讨厌的人；惹人讨厌的人|A gadfly is a fly that bites livestock, especially cattle.  Figuratively, a gadfly is a person who persistently challenges the status quo or those in authority. |The journalist was a gadfly, constantly questioning the government's policies.  这个记者像个牛虻，不断质疑政府的政策。|Synonyms: nuisance, pest, provocateur, agitator|Antonyms: conformist, yes-man|noun


gadflies:/ˈɡædˌflaɪz/| n. |牛虻（复数）；讨厌的人；惹人讨厌的人（复数）|Plural form of gadfly. |The gadflies of the media relentlessly pursued the scandal.  媒体像牛虻一样穷追不舍地报道这桩丑闻。|Synonyms: nuisances, pests, provocateurs, agitators|Antonyms: conformists, yes-men|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *gaut, *gad "goad, spike" + fly.  Originally referred to the fly's sharp proboscis.
