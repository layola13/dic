deaned:/diːnd/
|v.|免除…的院长职务；革除…的院长职务|To remove someone from the position of dean.|The university deaned him for misconduct. 大学因他的不当行为免除了他的院长职务。|removed, dismissed, ousted|appointed, reinstated|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From *dean* (n.) + *-ed* (verb suffix forming past participles).  The noun "dean" comes from Old French *deien*, from Medieval Latin *decanus*, ultimately from Latin *decem* "ten," originally referring to a leader of ten monks or soldiers.
