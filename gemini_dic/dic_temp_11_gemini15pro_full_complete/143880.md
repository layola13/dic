outpolitick: /ˌaʊtˈpɒlətɪk/ (US) /ˌaʊtˈpɒlɪtɪk/ (UK)
verb
以更精明的政治手段胜过；在政治手腕上胜过
to defeat someone by being more clever and skillful in political matters
The mayor outpoliticked his opponent in the election. 市长在选举中以更精明的政治手段战胜了对手。
近义词: outmaneuver, outsmart, outwit
反义词: be outmaneuvered, be outsmarted, be outwitted
词性: verb

outpolitick: /ˌaʊtˈpɒlətɪk/ (US) /ˌaʊtˈpɒlɪtɪk/ (UK)
词源:  "out-" (prefix meaning "surpassing, exceeding") + "politick" (verb, meaning "to engage in political activity")  The word "politick" itself comes from the Middle French "politique" and ultimately from the Greek "politikos," meaning "of citizens or the state."
中文翻译: 以更精明的政治手段胜过；在政治手腕上胜过
英文解析: To defeat or surpass someone in political maneuvering or strategy; to be more astute or skillful in political matters.
例句:
The young senator outpoliticked his more experienced rival.  年轻的参议员在政治手腕上胜过了他更有经验的对手。

She outpoliticked everyone in the negotiations and got exactly what she wanted. 她在谈判中以更精明的政治手段胜过所有人，并得到了她想要的一切。
