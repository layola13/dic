extractabilities:/ɪkˌstræktəˈbɪlɪtiz/| n.|可提取性；可萃取性|The extractabilities of different compounds vary depending on the solvent used. 不同化合物的可提取性取决于所使用的溶剂。|例句：The study focused on the extractabilities of bioactive compounds from plant material.  这项研究的重点是从植物材料中提取生物活性化合物的可提取性。

例句：The extractabilities of these metals from the ore were quite low. 这些金属从矿石中的可提取性相当低。|近义词：solubility, dissolvability|反义词: insolubility|词性:名词

extract:/ɪkˈstrækt/| v. n.|提取，萃取；摘录|To extract something is to remove it, often with some effort. 提取某物是指将其移除，通常需要一些努力。|例句：They extracted the oil from the olives. 他们从橄榄中提取油。

例句：The dentist extracted my wisdom tooth. 牙医拔掉了我的智齿。|近义词：remove, obtain, derive|反义词: insert, implant|词性:动词/名词

extractable:/ɪkˈstræktəbl/| adj.|可提取的；可萃取的|Something that is extractable can be removed or taken out. 可提取的东西是可以移除或取出的。|例句：The valuable minerals are extractable from the rock. 这些有价值的矿物可以从岩石中提取出来。

例句：The data is extractable from the database.  数据可以从数据库中提取。|近义词：removable, obtainable|反义词: non-extractable|词性:形容词
