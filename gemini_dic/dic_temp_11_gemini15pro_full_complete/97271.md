influence:/ˈɪnfluəns/ (US), /ˈɪnfluənts/ (UK)| n. vt.|影响；势力；感化；有影响的人或事物|The ability to affect someone or something, especially in an indirect but important way.  |Her parents’ influence made her decide to study medicine. 她父母的影响使她决定学医。|influence, impact, effect, sway|resistance, opposition|noun, verb

influence:/ˈɪnfluəns/ (US), /ˈɪnfluənts/ (UK)| vt.|影响；感化|To affect or change someone or something, especially in an indirect but important way. |Social media influences the way teenagers think. 社交媒体影响青少年的思维方式。|affect, impact, shape, modify|ignore, neglect|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *influence*, from Old French *influence* (noun and verb), from Latin *influentia* (“a flowing in”), from *influō* (“flow into”), from *in* (“in, into”) + *fluō* (“flow”).
