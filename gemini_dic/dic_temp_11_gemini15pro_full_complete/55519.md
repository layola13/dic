ETYMOLOGY->
From distress (n.) + -ing (suffix forming present participles and adjectives).  Distress itself comes from Old French destresse "hardship, oppression" from Vulgar Latin *districtia, from Latin districtus "pressed together, narrow," past participle of distringere "draw tight, stretch apart, pull asunder, distract," from dis- "apart" + stringere "to draw tight."
