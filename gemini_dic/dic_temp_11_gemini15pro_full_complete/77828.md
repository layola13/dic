generativenesses:/ˌdʒɛnərəˈtɪvnɪsɪz/| n. | 生成性，创造性（复数）|  The quality or capacity of being generative; productivity, especially in producing offspring or new ideas.  It refers to the ability to create, produce, or generate something new. In psychology, it often refers to Erik Erikson's theory of psychosocial development, where generativity is a stage characterized by a concern for establishing and guiding the next generation. |  Her artistic generativenesses seem boundless, constantly producing new and exciting works. 她的艺术创造力似乎无穷无尽，不断创作出令人兴奋的新作品。|  creativity, productivity, fertility, fecundity |  sterility, barrenness | noun
