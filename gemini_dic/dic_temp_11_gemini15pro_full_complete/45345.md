cumulonimbus:/ˌkjuːmjʊləˈnɪmbəs/| n. |积雨云|A type of cloud that is tall, dense, and involved in thunderstorms and other intense weather. It is characterized by its dark, towering appearance and often brings heavy rain, hail, lightning, and strong winds.|The cumulonimbus clouds darkened the sky, signaling an impending storm. 积雨云使天空变暗，预示着即将到来的暴风雨。

The pilot avoided flying through the cumulonimbus because of the risk of severe turbulence. 飞行员避开积雨云飞行，因为有严重湍流的风险。|近义词：thunderhead, storm cloud|反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑晴朗天空相关的词，例如：clear sky)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin cumulus (“heap, pile”) + nimbus (“rain cloud, rain storm”).
