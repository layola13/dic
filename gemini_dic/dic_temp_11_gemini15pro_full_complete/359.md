abideth:/əˈbaɪdɛθ/| v.| 持续；忍受；居住；遵守；信守|This archaic form of "abide" means to continue, endure, dwell, or remain faithful to something.  It carries a sense of permanence and steadfastness. |例句：He abideth in me, and I in him. 他常在我里面，我也常在他里面。|近义词：remains, stays, endures, persists, obeys|反义词: departs, leaves, abandons, breaks, transgresses |词性:动词 (verb)

PHRASE->

Due to its archaic nature, "abideth" is not commonly used in modern phrases.  It primarily appears in religious texts or literature mimicking older styles of English.  Therefore, providing modern phrases is not applicable.  However, for the modern form "abide," common phrases include:

abide by (遵守):  You must abide by the law. = 你必须遵守法律。
abide in (居住于):  He abides in peace. = 他安居乐业。
can't abide (无法忍受): I can't abide his rudeness. = 我无法忍受他的粗鲁。


词源：
From Middle English *abiden, from Old English ābīdan (“to remain, continue, await, expect, endure, experience”).  The "-eth" ending is an archaic third-person singular present indicative ending.
