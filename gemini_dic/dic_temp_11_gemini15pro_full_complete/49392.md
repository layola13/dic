delitescence:/ˌdɛlɪˈtɛsəns/ | n. | 潮解；溶解 |The process by which a substance absorbs moisture from the atmosphere until it dissolves in the absorbed water and becomes a solution. |The delitescence of the fertilizer caused it to clump together. 肥料的潮解导致它结块了。| Near synonyms: deliquescence, dissolving, liquefaction | Near antonyms: desiccation, dehydration, drying | noun

delitescent:/ˌdɛlɪˈtɛsənt/ | adj. | 潮解性的；易潮解的 |Capable of absorbing moisture from the atmosphere and dissolving in it. |Calcium chloride is a delitescent material. 氯化钙是一种潮解性物质。|Near synonyms: deliquescent, hygroscopic | Near antonyms: non-hygroscopic, anhydrous | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin dēlītēscere ("to hide away, vanish"), from dē- ("away, from") + latēscere ("to lie hidden").  The scientific usage arose from the apparent "vanishing" of a substance as it absorbs water and dissolves.
