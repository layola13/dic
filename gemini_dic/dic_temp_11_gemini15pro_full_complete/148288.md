oversocialized: /ˌoʊvərˈsoʊʃəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 过度社会化的 | Excessively or inappropriately socialized; having learned social rules and expectations to the point of inhibiting spontaneity, creativity, or individuality.  Describes someone who conforms too rigidly to social norms, often at the expense of their own personal expression. | He seemed oversocialized, unable to relax and be himself around others. 他似乎过度社会化了，无法在别人面前放松做自己。 |  The oversocialized child always said "please" and "thank you," even when it felt forced.  这个过度社会化的孩子总是说“请”和“谢谢”，即使感觉很勉强。 | 近义词: conformist, conventional, inhibited | 反义词: unconventional, individualistic, uninhibited | 词性: adjective

Etymology:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "socialized" (past participle of "socialize," meaning "to make fit for living in companionship with others"). The word implies an excessive degree of socialization, suggesting a negative connotation.
