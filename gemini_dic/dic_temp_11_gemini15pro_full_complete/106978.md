lacklustre:/ˈlæklʌstər/ (US) /ˈlækˌlʌstə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|缺乏光泽的；黯淡的；无生气的|Lacking in vitality, force, or conviction; uninspired or uninspiring.  Dull; not shining.|Her lacklustre performance disappointed the audience. 她毫无光彩的表演令观众失望。|Synonyms: dull, flat, uninspired, lifeless, vapid, insipid|Antonyms: vibrant, dynamic, energetic, spirited, brilliant, dazzling|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From lack + lustre.  Lustre itself comes from Old French lustre "brightness, brilliance," from Latin lustrare "to brighten, purify."
