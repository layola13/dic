nonjurancy:/nɒnˈdʒʊərənsi/ /nɑːnˈdʒʊrənsi/| n. |拒绝宣誓效忠；不宣誓|The state of being a nonjuror, or refusing to take the required oaths.|His nonjurancy cost him his position at the university. 他的拒绝宣誓让他失去了大学的职位。|近义词：recusancy, dissent|反义词：allegiance, loyalty|词性:名词

nonjuror:/nɒnˈdʒʊərər/ /nɑːnˈdʒʊrər/| n. |拒絕宣誓者|A person who refuses to swear an oath, especially an oath of allegiance or supremacy.|Many nonjurors were forced into exile after the Glorious Revolution.  许多拒絕宣誓者在光荣革命后被迫流亡。|近义词：recusant, dissenter|反义词：loyalist|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + juror, from Anglo-French jurour, from Old French jureor "swearer," agent noun from jurer "to swear," from Latin jurare "to swear," from jus (genitive juris) "law, right."  The suffix -ancy is from Old French -ance.
