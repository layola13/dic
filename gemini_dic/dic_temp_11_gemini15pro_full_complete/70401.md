fig-leafed:/ˈfɪɡˌliːft/ | adj. | 遮掩的，掩饰的；羞羞答答的|Covered or disguised, often in a superficial or inadequate way, like using a fig leaf to cover nudity; also implying a sense of shame or embarrassment.|  The report was a fig-leafed attempt to justify their actions. 这份报告只是为了掩饰他们的行为.| Synonyms: disguised, veiled, camouflaged, concealed, pretense | Antonyms: exposed, revealed, overt, transparent | word property: Adjective

fig-leafed:/ˈfɪɡˌliːft/ | adj. |  遮掩的，掩饰的；羞羞答答的 |  His fig-leafed explanation did little to convince anyone of his innocence. 他遮遮掩掩的解释几乎无法使任何人相信他的清白。| Synonyms: evasive, coy | Antonyms: forthright, candid | word property: Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->

From "fig leaf" + "-ed".  The term originates from the biblical story of Adam and Eve, who used fig leaves to cover their nakedness after eating the forbidden fruit (Genesis 3:7). This act has become symbolic of attempts to conceal shame or wrongdoing.
