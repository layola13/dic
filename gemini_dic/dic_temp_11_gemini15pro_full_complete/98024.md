inordinacy:/ˌɪnˈɔːrdɪnəsi/ |n.| 过度；无节制；放肆 |The inordinacy of his demands shocked everyone.  他的过分要求震惊了所有人。|Example: The inordinacy of her spending habits led to massive debt. 她无节制的消费习惯导致了巨额债务。|Synonyms: excess, immoderation, extravagance, intemperance |Antonyms: moderation, temperance, restraint |noun


inordinate:/ɪnˈɔːrdɪnət/ |adj.| 过度的；过分的；无节制的 |She spent an inordinate amount of time on social media. 她花了过多的时间在社交媒体上。|Example: He took an inordinate amount of pride in his work. 他对自己的工作感到无比自豪。|Synonyms: excessive, immoderate, extravagant, unreasonable |Antonyms: moderate, reasonable, temperate |adjective


Etymology:
From Late Latin *inordinatus, from Latin in- "not" + ordinatus "ordered, arranged," past participle of ordinare "to set in order," from ordo "order."
