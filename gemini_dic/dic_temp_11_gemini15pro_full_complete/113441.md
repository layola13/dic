lyncher's:/ˈlɪntʃərz/| n. |私刑者拥有的东西|Something that belongs to a lyncher (a person who participates in lynching, the act of killing someone without legal trial, usually by hanging).|The lyncher's rope was still hanging from the tree. 私刑者的绳子还挂在树上。|The lyncher's motives were never fully understood.  私刑者的动机从未被完全理解。|近义词:vigilante's, mob's|反义词:victim's, law enforcer's|词性:名词所有格

词源: From "lyncher" + "'s" (possessive suffix). "Lyncher" comes from "lynch," likely derived from the name of Charles Lynch (1736–1796), a Virginia planter and justice of the peace who presided over extralegal courts during the American Revolution. However, the precise origin remains uncertain.
