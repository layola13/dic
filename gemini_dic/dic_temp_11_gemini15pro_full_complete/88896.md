hokums:/ˈhoʊkəmz/| n. |胡说八道，废话，荒唐话|nonsense, claptrap, or humbug, especially of a sentimental nature.  Often used in the phrase "selling hokum".|The politician's speech was pure hokum, full of empty promises and emotional appeals.|近义词：nonsense, claptrap, humbug, bunk, baloney|反义词：truth, fact, reality|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain. Possibly from a fanciful alteration of hocus-pocus. First recorded in the early 20th century, originally in US theatrical slang.
