destabilization:/diːˌsteɪbɪləˈzeɪʃən/| n.|不稳定，动摇|The destabilization of the government caused widespread unrest. 政府的不稳定引起了广泛的社会动荡。|例句：The political destabilization led to a civil war. 政治动荡导致了内战。|近义词：undermining, weakening, disruption, subversion|反义词: stabilization, strengthening, reinforcement|词性:名词

destabilize:/diːˈsteɪbɪlaɪz/ | vt.|使不稳定，使动摇|Foreign interference is destabilizing the region. 外部干涉正在破坏该地区的稳定。|例句：The sudden change in leadership destabilized the company. 领导层的突然变化使公司陷入了不稳定。|近义词：undermine, weaken, disrupt, subvert|反义词: stabilize, strengthen, reinforce|词性:动词

词源:  From de- + stabilize + -ation.  "Stabilize" comes from the Late Latin word "stabilis" meaning "firm, steadfast."  The prefix "de-" indicates reversal or removal.
