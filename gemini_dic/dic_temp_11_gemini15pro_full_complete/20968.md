blench:/blɛntʃ/| v. | 畏缩，退缩；变白 | To shrink or flinch from pain, danger, or disgust; to turn pale. |  She blenched at the sight of the blood. 她看到血就吓得脸色发白。| Synonyms: flinch, recoil, blanch, quail | Antonyms: confront, face, embrace|verb


blench:/blɛntʃ/| v. | (使)变白，(使)褪色| To whiten; to bleach. | The sun blenched the fabric. 阳光使织物褪色。| Synonyms: bleach, whiten, fade | Antonyms: darken, color|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *blanchir* (to whiten), from Frankish *blankjan*, from Proto-Germanic *blangan* (to shine, gleam, blink). Related to blink.
