nonprolixly:/ˌnɒnprəˈlɪksli/| adv.|简洁地，简明地|In a concise and succinct manner; without unnecessary length or wordiness.  It emphasizes brevity and efficiency in communication or writing.  It suggests getting straight to the point without excessive elaboration.|He explained the complex issue nonprolixly, getting straight to the core of the matter.|近义词：concisely, succinctly, briefly, tersely|反义词：prolixly, verbosely, lengthily|词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + prolix + -ly.  "Non-" is a prefix indicating negation. "Prolix" comes from Latin "prolixus," meaning "extended," from "pro-" (forth, forward) + "liquere" (to be fluid, to flow).  Thus, "nonprolixly" literally means "not extendedly" or "not flowingly."
