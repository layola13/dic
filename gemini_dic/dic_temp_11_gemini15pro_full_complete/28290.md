capturing:/ˈkæptʃərɪŋ/| adj. v.| 捕获的，迷人的 |  present participle of "capture" which means to take into one's possession or control by force, skill, or artifice, or to record accurately in words or pictures. | The photographer was capturing the beauty of the sunset. 摄影师正在捕捉日落的美丽。 | synonyms: seizing, arresting, recording, fascinating, enthralling | antonyms: releasing, freeing, liberating, boring, dull | verb/adjective

capturing:/ˈkæptʃərɪŋ/| n.| 捕获，俘获 | the act of taking something into one's possession or control by force, skill, or artifice. | The capturing of the escaped convict was the top story on the news. 逃犯的落网是新闻头条。| synonyms: seizure, arrest, acquisition | antonyms: release, liberation | noun
