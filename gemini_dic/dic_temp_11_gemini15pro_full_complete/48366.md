decurionate:/dɪkjʊəriənət/
n.
十人团; 市议员职位;
The office or position of a decurion, a member of a local council in the Roman Empire.  A decurionate was a position of some power and prestige in Roman municipal government.
The decurionate was a significant position in Roman society.  十人团在罗马社会中是一个重要的职位。
The burdens of the decurionate often fell on the wealthy. 十人团的负担通常落在富人身上。
Synonyms: council, municipality, magistracy
Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but consider) peasantry, proletariat
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *decurionātus*, from *decurio* (decurion), from *decem* (ten).
