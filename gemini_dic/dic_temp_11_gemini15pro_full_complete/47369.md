deactivating: /diːˈæktɪveɪtɪŋ/
verb, adjective|使失效，使失去作用；停用；使无效|The process of making something inactive or no longer functional.  It can also describe something in the process of becoming inactive.|The company is deactivating old accounts.  该公司正在停用旧帐户。|disabling, shutting down|activating, enabling|verb, adjective
deactivation: /diːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən/
noun|失效，停用；去活化|The act of deactivating something, making it inactive or no longer functional.|The deactivation of the alarm system took several minutes.  警报系统的停用花了数分钟。|disablement, shutdown|activation, enablement|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From de- (meaning "reverse" or "remove") + activate + -ing (present participle suffix). The root "activate" comes from Late Latin activātus, past participle of activāre, meaning "to make active," from Latin activus "active."
