overliberalizes: /ˌoʊvərˈlɪb(ə)rəˌlaɪzɪz/
| v. |过度自由化|To liberalize to an excessive degree.  To make something too free or open, often resulting in negative consequences.|
The government overliberalized the banking sector, leading to a financial crisis.|
近义词: deregulate excessively, loosen controls too much|
反义词: restrict, regulate, control|
词性: verb


overliberalized: /ˌoʊvərˈlɪb(ə)rəˌlaɪzd/
| v. |过度自由化(过去式/过去分词)|Past tense and past participle of "overliberalize".  Indicates that the action of overliberalizing happened in the past.|
The previous administration had overliberalized the market, making it vulnerable to manipulation.|
近义词: deregulated excessively, loosened controls too much|
反义词: restricted, regulated, controlled|
词性: verb


overliberalizing: /ˌoʊvərˈlɪb(ə)rəˌlaɪzɪŋ/
| v. |过度自由化(现在分词)|Present participle of "overliberalize".  Indicates the ongoing action of overliberalizing.|
They are overliberalizing the economy, which could have unintended consequences.|
近义词: deregulating excessively, loosening controls too much|
反义词: restricting, regulating, controlling|
词性: verb


词源:  "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "liberalize" (from "liberal" meaning "favoring individual liberty").  Ultimately from Latin "liber" (free).
