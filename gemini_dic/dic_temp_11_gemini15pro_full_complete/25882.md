burlesquely: /bɜːrˈlɛskli/ /bərˈleskli/
| adv. | 滑稽地；嘲弄地；以滑稽剧的风格 | In a comical, exaggerated, and often mocking way, typically involving caricature and parody. |  He burlesquely imitated the politician's pompous speech. 他滑稽地模仿了那个政客浮夸的演讲。| 近义词：comically, mockingly, satirically, farcically | 反义词：seriously, respectfully, solemnly | 词性: 副词

burlesque: /bɜːrˈlɛsk/ /bərˈlesk/
| n. | 滑稽剧；嘲弄性模仿；滑稽作品 | A literary or dramatic work that makes fun of something by imitating it in a humorous and exaggerated way. | The play was a burlesque of a popular melodrama.  这出戏是对一部流行情节剧的滑稽模仿。 | 近义词：parody, satire, caricature, lampoon | 反义词：serious drama, tragedy | 词性: 名词

burlesque: /bɜːrˈlɛsk/ /bərˈlesk/
| adj. | 滑稽的；嘲弄的 | Relating to or characteristic of burlesque. |  The burlesque performance had the audience in stitches. 滑稽的表演让观众捧腹大笑。 | 近义词：comical, mocking, satirical, farcical | 反义词：serious, respectful, solemn | 词性: 形容词

burlesque: /bɜːrˈlɛsk/ /bərˈlesk/
| vt. | 以滑稽剧的形式模仿；嘲弄 | To represent or imitate in a humorous and exaggerated way; to parody. | The comedian burlesqued the president's mannerisms.  喜剧演员滑稽地模仿了总统的举止。| 近义词：parody, satirize, caricature, lampoon | 反义词：honor, respect, praise | 词性: 动词


词源: From Italian *burlesco*, from *burla* "joke, mockery."
