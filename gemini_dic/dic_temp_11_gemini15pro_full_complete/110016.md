libration:/laɪˈbreɪʃən/ | n. | 天平动；振动；摇动 | An apparent oscillatory motion of a celestial body, especially of the Moon or a satellite, as seen from the body it revolves around. | The libration of the Moon allows us to see slightly more than half of its surface. 月球的天平动使我们能看到略多于一半的月球表面。

The satellite's libration made it difficult to track.  卫星的振动使得追踪它变得困难。


近义词：oscillation, wobble, fluctuation | 反义词：stability, stillness | 词性：noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *librātiōn- (stem of librātiō), from Latin librāre (“to weigh, balance”), from libra (“balance, scales”).
