destabilised:/diːˈsteɪbəlaɪzd/| v. | 使不稳定，使动摇 |To make something less stable or secure, to upset the stability of; to undermine. |The sudden change in leadership destabilised the country. 突然的领导层变动使国家动荡不安。| The rumors destabilised the stock market. 谣言使股市动荡。|  synonyms: unsettle, weaken, undermine, disrupt, dislocate| antonyms: stabilize, strengthen, secure, reinforce| verb

destabilizing:/diːˈsteɪbəlaɪzɪŋ/| adj. |  不稳定的，动摇的 | Having the effect of making something less stable or secure. |The destabilizing effects of the war were felt throughout the region. 战争带来的不稳定影响波及整个地区。| The constant fighting has a destabilizing influence on the region.  持续不断的战斗对该地区有不稳定的影响。| synonyms: disruptive, unsettling, undermining | antonyms: stabilizing, strengthening, securing | adjective
