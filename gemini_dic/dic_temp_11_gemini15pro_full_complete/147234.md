overmedicating: /ˌoʊvərˈmɛdɪkeɪtɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
过度用药
Giving someone too much medication.  The act of administering an excessive amount of medication, often beyond what is medically necessary or recommended.
He was concerned about overmedicating his elderly mother. 他担心给他的老母亲服药过量。
Overmedicating children with ADHD can have serious side effects.  给患有多动症的儿童过度用药会有严重的副作用。
近义词: overdosing, overprescribing
反义词: undermedicating
词性: verb (present participle)

overmedicate: /ˌoʊvərˈmɛdɪkeɪt/
verb
过度用药
To give someone too much medication. To administer an excessive amount of medication, often beyond what is medically necessary or recommended.
Doctors are careful not to overmedicate their patients. 医生们小心翼翼地避免给病人过度用药。
It's easy to accidentally overmedicate infants.  很容易不小心给婴儿过度用药。
近义词: overdose, overprescribe
反义词: undermedicate
词性: verb


词源:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "medicate" (verb meaning "to treat with medicine").  "Medicate" comes from the Latin "medicatus," past participle of "medicare" ("to heal, to treat medically"), from "medicus" ("physician").
