moton:/ˈmoʊtən/ (US), /ˈməʊtən/ (UK)| n.|运动; 动机; 提议; 议案|A statement or suggestion put forward for consideration or discussion, especially by a legislative body.  A reason for doing something, especially one that is hidden or not obvious.  The process of moving or the way something moves.| The moton to adjourn the meeting was passed unanimously.  会议休会的动议获得一致通过。 His real moton was to gain control of the company. 他真正的动机是为了获得公司的控制权。  The smooth moton of the dancers was mesmerizing. 舞者流畅的动作令人着迷。 |近义词：motion, proposal, suggestion, motive, impetus|反义词：inaction, stillness|词性：名词


词源:
From Old French *moton, from Latin mōtiō (“a moving, motion”), from mōtus (“moved”), past participle of movēre (“to move”). Doublet of motion.
