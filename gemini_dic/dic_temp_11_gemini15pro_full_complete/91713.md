hyperconstitutionalism:/ˌhaɪpərˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃənəlɪzəm/ | n. | 超宪政主义；极端宪政主义 |  A political philosophy that emphasizes strict adherence to a literal interpretation of a constitution, often to the point of opposing any changes or interpretations that might expand governmental power. It prioritizes individual liberty and limits government intervention. |  Some critics argue that hyperconstitutionalism can lead to gridlock and prevent necessary government action.  一些评论家认为，极端宪政主义会导致政治僵局，并阻碍必要的政府行动。| Synonyms: strict constructionism, originalism | Antonyms:  loose constructionism, living constitutionalism | noun
