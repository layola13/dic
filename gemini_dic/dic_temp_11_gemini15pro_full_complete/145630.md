overconstructed:/ˌoʊvər.kənˈstrʌktɪd/ | adj. | 过于复杂化的，过度设计的 |  Having too much construction or detail; overly elaborate or complicated. | The plot of the movie was overconstructed, making it difficult to follow.  这部电影的情节过于复杂，让人难以理解。 | overwrought, overdone, overelaborate | simple, straightforward | adjective

overconstructed:/ˌoʊvər.kənˈstrʌktɪd/ | adj. | 过于刻意,矫揉造作的 | Built or contrived in a way that seems artificial or labored.  | Her performance felt overconstructed, lacking naturalness and spontaneity. 她的表演感觉过于刻意，缺乏自然和自发性。| contrived, artificial, affected | natural, spontaneous, genuine | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + constructed, past participle of construct.  Over- as a prefix means "excessively" or "too much". Construct comes from Latin *constructus*, past participle of construere "to build," from com- "together" + struere "to pile, build."
