bakshali: /bɑːkˈʃɑːli/
n. |巴克沙利手稿|An ancient Indian mathematical manuscript written on birch bark, notable for containing an early form of decimal notation and a symbol for zero.|The Bakhshali manuscript is a key source for understanding the development of mathematics in ancient India. 巴克沙利手稿是了解古印度数学发展的关键来源。|Synonyms: manuscript, text, document |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a specific manuscript) |Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
Named after the village of Bakhshali (near Peshawar, present-day Pakistan) where it was discovered in 1881.  From the place name "Bakhshali" + "-i" (forming an adjective).
