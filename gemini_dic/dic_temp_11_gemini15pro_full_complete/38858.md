confederate:/kənˈfɛdərit/ (US) /kənˈfɛdərɪt/ (UK)| adj. n. v.| 同盟的；联邦的；共谋的；同盟者；同伙；结盟；勾结|A person, group, or nation united with others for a common purpose, especially in a war or conspiracy.  |The Confederate states seceded from the Union in 1861.  南方邦联于1861年脱离联邦。|近义词：ally, accomplice, associate|反义词：enemy, opponent|词性: adjective, noun, verb

confederate:/kənˈfɛdərit/ (US) /kənˈfɛdərɪt/ (UK)| n.| 同盟者；同伙|A person who helps someone else do something wrong or illegal. |He was arrested along with his confederates.  他和他的同伙一起被捕了。|近义词：accomplice, collaborator, partner in crime|反义词：opponent, adversary|词性: noun

confederate:/kənˈfɛdərit/ (US) /kənˈfɛdərɪt/ (UK)| adj.| 联盟的；联邦的|Joined together by an agreement or treaty. |The confederate states fought against the Union.  邦联各州与联邦作战。|近义词：allied, federated, united|反义词：independent, separate|词性: adjective

confederate:/ˈkɒnfɪdəreɪt/ (verb, often passive)| v. |结盟；勾结|To join in a league or alliance. |Several smaller parties confederated to form a larger political force.  几个小党派联合起来组成了一股更大的政治力量。|近义词：ally, unite, join forces|反义词：separate, divide|词性: verb
