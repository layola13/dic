commensurateness:/kəˌmenʃərətnəs/| n. | 相称，相称性，相当，相等|The commensurateness of the punishment to the crime is important. 惩罚与罪行相称很重要。|例句：The commensurateness of his salary with his experience is questionable. 他的薪水与其经验是否相称值得怀疑。

例句：There's a lack of commensurateness between the resources allocated and the needs of the project.  分配的资源与项目的需求之间缺乏相称性。|近义词：proportionality,  appropriateness,  suitability,  congruity|反义词：disproportion,  inadequacy,  unsuitability,  incongruity|词性: 名词


commensurate:/kəˈmenʃərət/ | adj. | 相称的，相当的，相等的|A commensurate salary is important for attracting qualified candidates. 相称的薪水对于吸引合格的候选人很重要。|例句：Her salary is commensurate with her skills and experience. 她的薪水与她的技能和经验相称。

例句：The punishment should be commensurate with the crime. 惩罚应该与罪行相称。|近义词：proportional,  appropriate,  suitable,  equivalent,  corresponding|反义词：disproportionate,  inadequate,  unsuitable,  unequal|词性: 形容词

词源: Late Middle English: from late Latin commensuratus, past participle of commensurare, from com- ‘together’ + mensurare ‘to measure’ (from mensura ‘measure’).
