boughs:/baʊz/
|n.|树枝; 大树枝|Main branches of a tree.|The boughs of the apple tree were laden with fruit. 苹果树的枝头上挂满了果实。|branches, limbs|trunk, roots|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bowes, *boghes, from Old English *bōgas, plural of bōg (“bough, branch, shoulder, arm, bow”), from Proto-Germanic *bōguz (“bow, shoulder, arm”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰewǵ- (“to bend, curve”). Cognate with Scots bou (“bough”), West Frisian bôge (“bow, arc, arch”), Dutch boog (“bow, arc, arch”), German Bug (“bow, shoulder, bend, curve”), Old Norse bogi (“bow”), Gothic 𐌱𐌰𐌿𐌲𐍃 (baugs, “bow”).  Related to bow.
