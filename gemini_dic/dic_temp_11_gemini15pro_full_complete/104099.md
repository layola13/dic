jump-start:/ˈdʒʌmp stɑːrt/| n. v.|启动，发动；促进，推动|To start (a vehicle) by connecting its battery to the battery of another vehicle with jumper cables.  To give a boost or impetus to something.|I had to jump-start my car this morning because the battery was dead. 我今天早上不得不启动我的车，因为电池没电了。 The government is hoping the tax cuts will jump-start the economy. 政府希望减税能刺激经济。|近义词：boost, revive, kick-start|反义词:halt, stop|n. v.

jump-start:/ˈdʒʌmp stɑːrt/| n.|启动，发动；促进，推动|The act of jump-starting a vehicle or giving a boost to something.|The car needed a jump-start. 这辆车需要启动。|近义词：boost, impetus|反义词:hindrance|n.

词源: "jump-start" originated in the mid-20th century, combining "jump," referring to the sudden surge of power, and "start," relating to the act of starting an engine.
