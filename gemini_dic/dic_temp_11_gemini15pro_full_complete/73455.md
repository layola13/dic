forensic:/fəˈrɛnsɪk/ | adj. | 法医的，法庭的，适于法庭的 | Relating to or denoting the application of science to legal matters, especially in a court of law. | The police used forensic evidence to convict the criminal. 警方利用法医证据将罪犯定罪。 | Synonyms: legal, judicial, medicolegal | Antonyms:  non-forensic, non-legal | adjective

forensic:/fəˈrɛnsɪk/ | n. | 法医学，辩论术 | The scientific study of crime scene investigation. |  He studied forensics in college. 他在大学学习法医学。 | Synonyms:  criminology | Antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *forensis*, meaning "of the forum, public," from *forum* ("forum, marketplace, public place").  Originally referred to Roman forums where legal matters were discussed and debated. The modern meaning related to legal proceedings and scientific investigation developed later.
