jim-jams:/ˈdʒɪmˌdʒæmz/| n.|睡衣，睡裤|Pajamas, especially for children.  They often have a whimsical or childish pattern.|The little boy was snug in his dinosaur jim-jams.|近义词：pajamas, nightclothes|反义词:daywear|词性:noun

Etymology:
Origin uncertain, perhaps imitative. First recorded in the late 19th century.  Possibly related to the earlier term "jim-crow," a derogatory term for a Black person, which sometimes referred to a ragged garment. This connection is speculative and not definitively proven.
