liberals:/ˈlɪbərəlz/| n.|自由主义者；开明人士|People who hold liberal views, typically advocating individual rights, civil liberties, and government intervention to address social and economic inequality.  Those who are open to new behavior or opinions and willing to discard traditional values.|例句：The liberals are pushing for healthcare reform. 自由主义者正在推动医疗改革。

Liberals often advocate for social justice. 自由主义者经常倡导社会正义。|近义词：progressives, reformers, leftists|反义词:conservatives, reactionaries, right-wingers|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *liberalis "of freedom, befitting a free man," from Latin liber "free."  The political sense emerged in the early 19th century.
