outshame:/ˌaʊtˈʃeɪm/
verb
使（某人）感到羞愧胜过；羞辱；
To cause someone to feel shame by behaving in a way that is perceived as more virtuous or moral.
She tried to outshame him by donating a larger sum to the charity. 她试图通过向慈善机构捐赠更大一笔钱来让他感到羞愧。
She outshammed him into donating to the charity. 她使他感到羞愧从而捐款给慈善机构。
近义词:humiliate, disgrace, mortify
反义词:honor, praise, commend
词性:v.

Etymology:
From out- + shame.  The prefix "out-"  often indicates exceeding or surpassing something.
