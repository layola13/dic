intrusively:/ɪnˈtruːsɪvli/
adverb|侵入地，打扰地|in a way that affects someone's private life or activities in a way that they do not want|例句：He stared intrusively at her. 他冒昧地盯着她看。|例句：The photographer intrusively snapped pictures of the grieving family. 摄影师唐突地拍下了悲痛的家庭的照片。|近义词：obtrusively, invasively, officiously|反义词：discreetly, respectfully|词性:副词
intrude:/ɪnˈtruːd/
verb|侵入，闯入|to come or go into a place where you are not wanted or welcome|
intrusive:/ɪnˈtruːsɪv/
adjective|侵入的，打扰的|affecting someone's private life or interrupting them in an unwanted and annoying way|
intrusion:/ɪnˈtruːʒən/
noun|侵入，闯入|the act of intruding|

词源:
From Late Latin intrusus, past participle of intrudere “to thrust in,” from in- “in” + trudere “to thrust, push.”
