maran:/ˈmærən/ (US), /ˈmɑːrən/ (UK)| n.| 主，我主（对耶稣基督的称呼）| From Aramaic "maran," meaning "our Lord." A title of reverence and adoration used for Jesus Christ, particularly in early Christian writings. | Example:  "Maran atha!" which means "Our Lord comes!"  主来了！| Example: The early Christians often used "maran" in their prayers. 早期基督徒经常在祷告中使用“maran”。 | Synonym: Lord, Master | Antonym: N/A | Part of Speech: Noun
