diminutive:/dɪˈmɪnjʊtɪv/| adj. n.|小的，微小的；爱称|Something diminutive is extremely small.  A diminutive word, name, or form of a word is shortened or modified to express affection, etc.|The bonsai tree was a diminutive version of the giant redwood. 这棵盆栽是巨型红杉的缩小版。|  Her diminutive stature made her seem younger than she was. 她娇小的身材使她看起来比实际年龄小。|synonyms: tiny, small, miniature, minuscule|antonyms: huge, enormous, gigantic|adjective, noun

diminuent:/dɪˈmɪnjʊənt/| n. adj.|指小词；微小的|A diminutive word, name, or form of a word, often used to express affection, smallness, or triviality.  As an adjective, it means very small.| "-let" is a common diminutive suffix in English, like in "booklet." “-let”是英语中常见的指小后缀，例如“booklet”。|The kitten, a diminutive creature, curled up in my hand.  这只小猫，一个微小的生物，蜷缩在我的手中。|synonyms: diminutive (as adj.), small, tiny, mini, slight|antonyms: augmentative, magnifying, enlarging|noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *diminuent- (stem of diminuens), present participle of diminuere "to lessen," from Latin de- "from, down" + minuere "to lessen" (from PIE root *mei- (2) "small").
