cephalopodous:/ˌsefələˈpɒdəs/ |adj.| 头足纲的，头足类的 | Belonging to the class Cephalopoda, which includes octopuses, squid, cuttlefish, and nautiluses. These creatures are characterized by their prominent head and tentacles. |  Example: Octopuses are cephalopodous mollusks known for their intelligence. 章鱼是著名的头足纲软体动物，以其智力而闻名。 | Example: The cephalopodous animal quickly changed color to blend in with its surroundings.  这只头足类动物迅速改变颜色以融入周围环境。 | Synonym: cephalopodan | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, as it's a classificatory term. One could perhaps use "non-cephalopodous" informally.) | Word type: Adjective

cephalopod:/ˈsefələpɒd/ |n.| 头足纲动物 |Any mollusk of the class Cephalopoda, such as an octopus, squid, cuttlefish, or nautilus. | Example: Cephalopods are highly intelligent invertebrates. 头足类动物是高智商的无脊椎动物。| Example: The giant squid is one of the largest cephalopods. 巨型乌贼是最大的头足类动物之一。| Synonym: (No single perfect synonym, but depending on context: octopus, squid, cuttlefish, nautilus) | Antonym: (No direct antonym, as it's a classificatory term.) | Word type: Noun


Etymology:
From Greek kephalē (head) + pous (foot), via New Latin Cephalopoda.
