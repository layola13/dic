estreat:/ɪˈstriːt/ | n. vt.| 摘录；取出；征收；提取 | An official copy of a record of a court, especially of fines or forfeitures imposed, authorizing the collection of the sum.  The act of extracting such a copy. To extract (a part of a document) and put into a different form. To levy (a fine or amercement) by estreat. | The court ordered the estreat of the bond when the defendant failed to appear. 法院在被告未出庭时下令提取保证金。 | Synonyms: extract, copy, levy, fine | Antonyms: return, refund, remit | noun, verb

estreat:/ɪˈstriːt/ | vt.| 征收；提取 | To extract and certify (a recognizance, fine, etc.) from a court record, usually for the purpose of enforcing payment. | The judge estreated the bail bond after the defendant missed several court appearances.  法官在被告多次缺席庭审后征收了保释金。 | Synonyms:  extract, levy, collect, impose | Antonyms:  remit, pardon, forgive | verb
