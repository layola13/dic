high-hat:/ˈhaɪˌhæt/| n. |踩镲, 高礼帽 (指傲慢的态度)|A pair of cymbals fastened together, played by striking them together, or by operating a foot pedal. Also refers to a snobbish or condescending attitude. |例句：The drummer's high-hat kept a steady beat. 鼓手的踩镲保持着稳定的节奏。

例句：He gave me the high-hat when I asked for help. 当我寻求帮助时，他对我摆出一副高傲的态度。|近义词：(for cymbal) cymbals; (for attitude) snobbish, condescending, arrogant |反义词:(for attitude) humble, modest |词性:noun

high-hat:/ˈhaɪˌhæt/| v. |冷落, 怠慢|To treat someone in a condescending or supercilious manner; snub. |例句：She high-hatted her old friends after she became famous. 她成名后对老朋友们不屑一顾。

例句：He was high-hatted by the wealthy club members. 他被富有的俱乐部成员冷落了。|近义词：snub, ignore, condescend to, slight |反义词: welcome, embrace |词性:verb
