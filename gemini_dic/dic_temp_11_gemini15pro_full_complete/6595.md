anachronic:/əˈnækrənɪk/ | adj. |时代错误的；过时的 | Belonging to a period other than that being portrayed; chronologically misplaced. |例句：The movie was set in ancient Rome, but the characters' use of cell phones was quite anachronistic.  这部电影以古罗马为背景，但角色使用手机就显得时代错误了。

例句：Wearing a powdered wig to a modern-day party would be considered anachronistic.  戴着假发参加现代派对会被认为是时代错误的。 |近义词：outdated, obsolete, antiquated |反义词：contemporary, modern, current |词性: adjective

anachronism:/əˈnækrənɪzəm/ | n. |时代错误；过时的人或物 | A thing belonging or appropriate to a period other than that in which it exists, especially a thing that is conspicuously old-fashioned. |例句：The inclusion of a computer in a film set in the 1800s is a glaring anachronism. 在一部以19世纪为背景的电影中出现电脑是一个明显的时代错误。

例句：His ideas about social etiquette are anachronisms in today's world.  他关于社交礼仪的观念在当今世界已经过时了。 |近义词：misplacement, incongruity |反义词：chronological accuracy |词性: noun
