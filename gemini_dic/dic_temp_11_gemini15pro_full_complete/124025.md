morosenesses:/ˌmɔːrəʊsnɪsɪz/ /ˌmɔːˈroʊsnəsɪz/
noun
郁闷，闷闷不乐，阴郁
The state of being sullen and ill-tempered.
His moroseness made him unpleasant to be around. 他的郁闷让他周围的人感到不愉快。
近义词：sullenness, gloominess, dejection, glumness
反义词：cheerfulness, happiness, joyfulness
词性: noun

Etymology:
From morose + -ness.  Morose comes from Late Latin *morosus "peevish, fastidious," from Latin morosus "capricious, whimsical," from mos (plural mores) "custom, habit, usage, way, manner,"  The sense evolution is from "habituated to a certain way" to "fixed in one's habits" to "difficult to please" to "sullen."
