categoricalness:/ˌkætəˈɡɔːrɪkəlnəs/ | n. |绝对性，明确性| The state or quality of being categorical; explicitness and directness. |  The categoricalness of his denial left no room for doubt. 他的断然否认不容置疑。|  certainty, definiteness, explicitness | ambiguity, vagueness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From categorical + -ness.  Categorical comes from Late Latin categoricus, from Greek katēgorikos, from katēgorein "to accuse, assert, predicate," from kata- "down, against" + agoreuein "to speak in the assembly," from agora "assembly."
