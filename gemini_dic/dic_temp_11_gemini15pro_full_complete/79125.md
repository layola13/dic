gladiatorism:/ˈɡlædɪeɪtərɪzəm/
n.
角斗士制度; 角斗士的行為;  残酷的斗争
A system of combat between armed gladiators for public entertainment in ancient Rome.  Also refers to any fierce or ruthless struggle for survival or supremacy.
The Roman Empire was known for its gladiatorism, which often resulted in death.  罗马帝国以其角斗士制度而闻名，这种制度通常会导致死亡。
Gladiatorism in the business world can be cutthroat. 商业世界的角斗士式竞争可能非常残酷。
近义词: combat, fighting, struggle, contention, competition
反义词: peace, cooperation, harmony
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *gladiator* (swordsman), from *gladius* (sword).  The suffix "-ism" denotes a practice, system, or doctrine.  源自拉丁语 *gladiator*（剑士），来自 *gladius*（剑）。后缀“-ism”表示实践、制度或学说。
