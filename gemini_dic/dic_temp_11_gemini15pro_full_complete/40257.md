contingency:/kənˈtɪndʒənsi/ | n. | 偶然性，意外事故，可能性 | a future event or circumstance which is possible but cannot be predicted with certainty | The company has a contingency plan in case of a power outage. 公司有停电时的应急方案。| synonyms: possibility, eventuality, chance, accident, incident | antonyms: certainty, inevitability | noun

contingent:/kənˈtɪndʒənt/ | adj. | 依情况而定的，偶然的，可能的 | occurring or existing only if (certain circumstances) are the case; dependent on | Payment is contingent on fulfilling the terms of the contract. 付款取决于合同条款的履行。| synonyms: dependent, conditional, subject to, provisional | antonyms: independent, unconditional, certain | adjective

contingent:/kənˈtɪndʒənt/ | n. |  (代表某一组织或国家的)代表团，小分队 | a group of people united by some common feature, forming part of a larger group | The California contingent at the convention was large. 大会上加州的代表团规模很大。| synonyms: group, delegation, detachment, squad | antonyms: N/A | noun
