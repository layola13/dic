hybodont:/ˈhaɪbəˌdɒnt/ (US) /ˈhaɪbəˌdɒnt/ (UK)| adj. n.|弓鲛齿型的，弓鲛目动物|Relating to or denoting Hybodontiformes, an extinct order of shark-like fishes that existed from the Devonian to the Cretaceous periods.  Also used as a noun to refer to a member of this order.|The hybodont teeth were well adapted for crushing shellfish.|近义词:shark-like, elasmobranch|反义词: (No direct antonym,  perhaps "modern shark")|词性:adjective, noun

Etymology: From Greek *hýbos* (humpbacked) + *odoús, odóntos* (tooth).  Referring to the humped shape of some hybodont teeth.
