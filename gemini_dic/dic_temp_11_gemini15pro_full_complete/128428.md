newish:/ˈnjuːɪʃ/| adj.|较新的；近来的|Relatively new; recently made or acquired.  Something that is not brand new, but has been around for a short time.|例句：I bought a newish car last year. 我去年买了一辆较新的车。|近义词：recent, modern, contemporary|反义词：old, ancient, outdated|词性:adjective
Etymology:  From 'new' + '-ish' (a suffix indicating somewhat or approximately). The suffix "-ish" is of Germanic origin and is used to form adjectives from adjectives or nouns, often with the sense of "somewhat," "approximately," or "having the quality of."
