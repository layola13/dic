monad:/ˈmɒnæd/ (UK) /ˈmoʊnæd/ (US)| n.|单子，单元，单体， монада (Russian)|A single unit or entity, especially in philosophy or programming.  In philosophy, it refers to an indivisible and fundamental constituent of reality. In functional programming, it's a design pattern that allows sequencing operations within a specific context.|

例句：
In Leibniz's philosophy, the universe is composed of countless monads.
在莱布尼茨的哲学中，宇宙是由无数个单子组成的。

The IO monad in Haskell helps manage side effects.
Haskell 中的 IO 单子有助于管理副作用。

近义词：unit, entity, atom
反义词:dyad, plurality
词性:noun
