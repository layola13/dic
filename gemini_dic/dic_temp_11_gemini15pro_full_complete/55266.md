dissipatedness:/ˈdɪsɪpeɪtɪdnəs/
|n.|放荡，挥霍，消散|The state of being dissipated; a tendency towards dissipation, especially of energy, money, or moral restraint.  指消散的状态；一种消散的倾向，尤指精力、金钱或道德约束的消散。|His dissipatedness led to his financial ruin. 他的挥霍无度导致了他的经济破产。|近义词：debauchery, profligacy, licentiousness|反义词：frugality, abstinence, moderation|词性: noun

dissipate:/ˈdɪsɪpeɪt/
|v.|消散，驱散，浪费|To scatter in various directions; disperse; to spend or use wastefully or recklessly.  向各个方向散开；分散；浪费或不计后果地花费或使用。|The fog dissipated quickly in the morning sun. 晨曦中的雾很快消散了。|近义词：scatter, disperse, squander|反义词：gather, collect, conserve|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dissipatus, past participle of dissipare "to scatter," from dis- "apart" + sipare "to throw, scatter."
