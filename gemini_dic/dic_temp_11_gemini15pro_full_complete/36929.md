colossus:/kəˈlɒsəs/ (US) /kəˈlɑːsəs/ (UK)| n. | 巨像；巨人；庞然大物 |A statue of gigantic size; a person or thing of immense size or power. |例句：The Empire State Building is a colossus of steel and concrete. 帝国大厦是钢铁和混凝土的巨像。

例句：He was a colossus in the world of finance. 他是金融界的巨擘。|近义词：giant, behemoth, titan, mammoth |反义词:dwarf, pygmy |词性:noun


colossal:/kəˈlɒsəl/ (US) /kəˈlɑːsəl/ (UK)| adj. | 巨大的；庞大的 |Extremely large or great. |例句：They made a colossal mistake. 他们犯了一个巨大的错误。

例句：The project was a colossal undertaking. 这个项目是一项巨大的工程。|近义词：huge, enormous, gigantic, immense |反义词:tiny, minuscule |词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *colossus*, from Greek *kolossos* "gigantic statue," of unknown origin.
