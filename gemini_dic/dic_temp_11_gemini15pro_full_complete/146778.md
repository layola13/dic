overidealizing:/ˌoʊvər aɪˈdiːəlaɪzɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
过度理想化
The act of portraying or viewing something as perfect or better than it actually is, often ignoring flaws or negative aspects.
He was overidealizing the relationship, blind to her obvious faults. 他过度美化了这段关系，对她的明显缺点视而不见。
Overidealizing the past can prevent us from appreciating the present. 过度理想化过去会阻碍我们欣赏现在。
近义词: romanticizing, glorifying, extolling
反义词: vilifying, denigrating, criticizing
词性: v. (present participle)

overidealize:/ˌoʊvər aɪˈdiːəlaɪz/
verb
过度理想化
To view or portray something as perfect or better than it actually is.
She tends to overidealize her childhood. 她倾向于过度理想化她的童年。
Many young people overidealize celebrities. 许多年轻人过度理想化名人。
近义词: romanticize, glorify, extol
反义词: vilify, denigrate, criticize
词性: v.

词源:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "idealize" (from "ideal" + "-ize," a suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or cause to be").  Ultimately from Greek "idea" (form, pattern).
