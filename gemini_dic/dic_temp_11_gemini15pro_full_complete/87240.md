heretics:/ˈherətɪks/| n.|异教徒，异端邪说者（复数）|Plural form of "heretic," referring to multiple people believing in or practicing religious heresy.|The heretics were persecuted for their beliefs. 这些异教徒因其信仰而受到迫害。|synonyms: dissenters, nonconformists, apostates, schismatics|antonyms: believers, conformists|noun

词源:
From Old French *eretique* (Modern French *hérétique*), from Latin *haereticus*, from Ancient Greek *αἱρετικός (hairetikós)* "able to choose," from *αἱρέομαι (hairéomai)* "I choose."  The original meaning referred to one who chooses his own beliefs, particularly in religion.
