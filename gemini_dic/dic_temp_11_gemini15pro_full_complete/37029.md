comae:/ˈkəʊmiː/ (plural of coma)|n.|彗发|The luminous envelope around the nucleus of a comet, formed by vaporization of part of the nucleus when the comet is near the sun.|The comet's comae was a brilliant spectacle.|Near-synonyms: atmosphere, halo|Antonym: nucleus|noun


coma:/ˈkoʊmə/|n.|昏迷|A state of deep unconsciousness from which one cannot be awakened; a prolonged state of deep unconsciousness caused especially by severe injury or illness.|He was in a coma for several weeks after the accident.|Near-synonyms: unconsciousness, stupor|Antonym: consciousness|noun
