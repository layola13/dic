contextualized:/kənˈtekstʃuəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 置于语境中的，根据上下文理解的 | Placed or studied in context, especially so as to be understood or interpreted in light of surrounding information or circumstances. | The teacher contextualized the historical event by discussing its social and political background. 老师通过讨论其社会和政治背景，将这一历史事件置于语境中。|  synonyms: situated, placed, embedded, grounded, interpreted, explained | antonyms: decontextualized, abstract, isolated | adjective

contextualized:/kənˈtekstʃuəlaɪzd/ | v. | 置于语境中，根据上下文理解 | To place (something, such as a word or activity) in a context. |  The author contextualized the novel by describing the historical period in which it was set. 作者通过描述小说所设定的历史时期，将其置于语境中。|  synonyms: situate, place, embed, ground, interpret, explain | antonyms: decontextualize, abstract, isolate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From context + -ual + -ize.  "Context" comes from the Latin "contextus," meaning "a joining together," from "con-" (together) and "texere" (to weave).  "-ual" forms adjectives from nouns, and "-ize" forms verbs meaning "to make or become."
