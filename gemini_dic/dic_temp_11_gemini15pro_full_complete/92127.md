hyperscrupulosity:/ˌhaɪpərˌskruːpjəˈlɒsɪti/ | n. | 过度谨慎；过度拘泥；洁癖 |  A psychological condition characterized by excessive worry and guilt about moral or religious issues, often involving minor transgressions. |  Example: Her hyperscrupulosity led her to confess even the smallest of sins. 她的过度谨慎让她即使是最小的罪过也去忏悔。

Example:  He suffered from hyperscrupulosity, constantly questioning the morality of his actions. 他患有过度谨慎，不断质疑自己行为的道德性。 | 近义词: conscientiousness, scrupulosity, anxiety, OCD | 反义词: carelessness, recklessness, nonchalance | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- (Greek: 'over, beyond') + scrupulosity (from Latin scrūpulus 'small sharp stone', hence 'anxiety, uneasiness', figuratively referring to a stone in one's shoe causing discomfort).
