authoritarians: /əˌθɔːrɪˈtɛəriənz/
| n. |独裁者；专制主义者；权力主义者|People who favor authoritarianism, a political system characterized by strong central power and limited political freedoms.| Many historians believe that Hitler and Stalin were authoritarians. 许多历史学家认为希特勒和斯大林是独裁者。| Synonyms: dictators, despots, tyrants, autocrats | Antonyms: democrats, liberals, libertarians | Noun (plural)

authoritarian: /əˌθɔːrɪˈtɛəriən/
| adj. |独裁的；专制的；权力主义的|Favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority, especially that of the government, at the expense of personal freedom.| The country is ruled by an authoritarian regime.  这个国家由一个专制政权统治。| Synonyms: dictatorial, despotic, tyrannical, autocratic | Antonyms: democratic, liberal, libertarian | Adjective


authoritarianism: /əˌθɔːrɪˈtɛəriənɪzəm/
| n. |独裁主义；专制主义；权力主义|The enforcement or advocacy of strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom.  |There is a growing concern about the rise of authoritarianism in the world.  人们越来越担心世界范围内独裁主义的抬头。| Synonyms: dictatorship, despotism, tyranny, autocracy | Antonyms: democracy, liberalism, libertarianism | Noun


Etymology: From Late Latin *authoritativus, from Latin auctoritas (“authority”).
