corroborative:/kəˈrɒbəreɪtɪv/ | adj. | 确证的，证实的 | serving to support or confirm; tending to corroborate | The witness provided corroborative evidence that strengthened the prosecution's case.  证人提供了确凿的证据，加强了控方的论点。| synonyms: confirmatory, supporting, substantiating, validating | antonyms: contradictory, conflicting, opposing, refuting | adjective

corroborate:/kəˈrɒbəreɪt/ | v. | 证实，确证 | to confirm or give support to (a statement, theory, or finding) |  The forensic evidence corroborates the witness's testimony.  法医证据证实了证人的证词。 | synonyms: confirm, verify, substantiate, validate | antonyms: contradict, refute, disprove, challenge | verb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *corroborativus, from Latin corroboratus (past participle of corroborare "to strengthen"), from com- "together" + roborare "to strengthen," from robur "strength, oak."
