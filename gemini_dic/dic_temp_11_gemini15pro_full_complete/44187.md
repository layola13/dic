croaker:/ˈkroʊkər/| n.| 呱呱叫的动物; 抱怨者;  石首鱼|A fish that makes a croaking noise.  Also, a person who complains or predicts doom.|The old croaker was always complaining about the weather.  We caught a few croakers while fishing in the bay. |近义词：complainer, pessimist, grumbler,  drum (fish)|反义词:optimist|词性:noun

croak:/kroʊk/| n. vi. vt.|呱呱叫声; 抱怨; 死亡; 说出（尤指声音沙哑）|The sound a frog makes. Also, to complain or predict doom, or to die.  Can also mean to speak hoarsely.|The frogs croaked loudly in the pond.  He croaked about the state of the economy.  The old man croaked last night.  "I need some water," she croaked.|近义词：complain, grumble, moan, die, expire|反义词:live, thrive, rejoice|词性:noun, verb
