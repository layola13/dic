ambushing:/ˈæmbʊʃɪŋ/| n. v.|埋伏，伏击|The act of waiting in a concealed position to surprise and attack someone or something.|The soldiers were ambushing the enemy troops. 士兵们正在埋伏敌军。|近义词：waylaying, trapping, ensnaring|反义词：exposing, revealing|词性: 名词/动词-ing形式

ambush:/ˈæmbʊʃ/| n. v.|埋伏，伏击|A concealed position from which to attack someone or something by surprise; the act of lying in wait to attack by surprise.|The troops lay in ambush, waiting for the enemy to pass. 部队埋伏起来，等待敌人经过。  They planned an ambush on the enemy patrol. 他们计划伏击敌军的巡逻队。|近义词：trap, snare, pitfall|反义词：exposure, revelation|词性: 名词/动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *embuscher, from en- "in" + busche "bush, wood," from Vulgar Latin *boscus (source also of Italian bosco, Spanish and Portuguese bosque), of uncertain origin.
