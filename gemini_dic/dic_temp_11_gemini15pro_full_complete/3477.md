aftercast:/ˈæftərˌkæst/
n.
后续预测；事后评估；预后
A forecast made after an event has occurred, especially in meteorology.
Example: The aftercast of the hurricane showed that it was more powerful than initially predicted.  飓风过后的事后评估表明，它比最初预测的威力更大。

Example:  The aftercast of the election results revealed a surprising shift in voter demographics. 选举结果的事后评估揭示了选民人口结构的惊人变化。

近义词: hindsight, post-mortem, review, analysis
反义词: forecast, prediction

词性: noun

词源: "after" + "cast."  "After" indicates something happening later, and "cast" refers to a prediction or projection.
