outheard: /ˌaʊtˈhɜːrd/ | v. |胜过听力；听得比…更清楚| to hear something more clearly or effectively than someone else |  I managed to outheard the other bidders at the auction and secured the antique vase. 我在拍卖会上成功地听得比其他竞标者更清楚，并获得了那件古董花瓶。| outspeak, outdo |  - | verb
Etymology: out- + heard (past participle of hear)
out- : Old English ūt ‘out, outside’.
heard: Old English hīeran (West Saxon, Anglian), hēran (Kentish) ‘perceive by ear’, of West Germanic origin; related to Dutch horen and German hören .
