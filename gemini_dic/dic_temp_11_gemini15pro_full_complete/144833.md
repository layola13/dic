over-logically:/ˌoʊvərˈlɑːdʒɪkli/
adjective| 过于逻辑地，过度理性地|  Reasoning or analyzing something in an excessively logical manner, often to the point of neglecting other important factors like emotions or practical considerations.| He approached the problem over-logically, failing to consider the human element. 他处理这个问题过于逻辑化，没有考虑到人的因素。|  over-analytical, excessively rational| intuitive, emotional| adjective
Etymology: "over-" (Old English *ofer) + "logic" (from Old French logique, from Latin logica, from Greek logikē) + "-al" (suffix forming adjectives) + "-ly" (suffix forming adverbs, here used to create an adjective with the sense of 'in an ... manner'). 词源：“over-”（古英语 *ofer）+ “logic”（源自古法语 logique，源自拉丁语 logica，源自希腊语 logikē）+ “-al”（构成形容词的后缀）+ “-ly”（构成副词的后缀，此处用于构成具有“...方式”含义的形容词）。
