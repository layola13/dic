hold-the-bag:/ˌhoʊld ðə ˈbæɡ/ | idiom | 被迫承担责任；背黑锅 | To be left with the responsibility for something, often unpleasant or disadvantageous, that others have abandoned. | Example 1: When the company went bankrupt, the small investors were left holding the bag.  公司破产时，小投资者被迫承担了损失。

Example 2:  He promised to help with the project, but at the last minute he backed out, leaving me holding the bag. 他答应帮忙做这个项目，但在最后一刻他退缩了，让我承担了所有责任。 | Synonyms:  left in the lurch,  carrying the can,  take the rap,  bear the brunt | Antonyms:  pass the buck,  shift the blame,  get off scot-free | idiom
