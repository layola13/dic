calade:/kəˈlɑːd/ |n.|队列，行列；游行 |A procession, especially one of horsemen; a cavalcade. |The king led the calade through the city streets. 国王率领队伍穿过城市街道。|Example: The festival featured a colorful calade of floats and marching bands.  节日以彩车和游行乐队的游行队伍为特色。|Synonym: procession, cavalcade, parade, march|Antonym: dispersal, scattering|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French "cavalcade," from Italian "cavalcata," from "cavalcare" (to ride on horseback), from "cavallo" (horse), from Late Latin "caballus" (horse). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*kebalos" (horse).
