barbiturates: /bɑːrˈbɪtʃərɪts/ | n. | 巴比妥类药物 | A group of drugs that act as central nervous system depressants, and can therefore produce a wide spectrum of effects, from mild sedation to total anesthesia. They are also effective as anxiolytics, hypnotics, and anticonvulsants. |  Barbiturates were once commonly used as sleeping pills but are now rarely prescribed due to their high potential for addiction and overdose. 巴比妥类药物曾被广泛用作安眠药，但由于其成瘾性和过量服用的高风险，现在很少被开处方。

| Synonyms: sedatives, hypnotics, downers | Antonyms: stimulants | noun
