loxodromics:/ˌlɒksəˈdrɒmɪks/ | n. | 斜航学；等角航线；斜航线 | The study of loxodromic curves, also known as rhumb lines, which maintain a constant bearing or compass direction.  It's a branch of navigation concerned with sailing along such lines. |  Loxodromics is essential for long-distance navigation. 斜航学对于长途航行至关重要。 | Synonyms: rhumb-line sailing, constant bearing sailing | Antonyms: great-circle sailing | noun
