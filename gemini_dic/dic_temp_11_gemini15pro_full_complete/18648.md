benzol:/ˈbɛnzɒl/ (UK) /ˈbɛnzɔːl/ (US)| n.|苯|A colorless volatile liquid hydrocarbon present in coal tar and petroleum, used in chemical synthesis.  It is a known carcinogen.|Benzol was once widely used as a solvent.|近义词：benzene|反义词: N/A|词性:noun
Etymology: From German *Benzol*, from *Benz(oe)* (benzoic acid) + *-ol* (chemical suffix denoting alcohols and other hydroxyl compounds). Ultimately from Arabic *lubān jāwī* (لبان جاوي, “Javan frankincense”), via Medieval Latin *benjuinum*.
