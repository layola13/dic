badgered:/ˈbæʤərd/ | v. | 纠缠，烦扰 | to repeatedly ask (someone) to do something; to pester; to harass persistently |  The salesman badgered me into buying a new car. 售货员缠着我买了一辆新车。 |  pester, harass, annoy, bother | leave alone, ignore | verb
badger:/ˈbæʤər/ | n. | 獾 | a stout, burrowing mammal with short legs and thick, coarse fur |  We saw a badger cross the road last night.  昨晚我们看到一只獾穿过马路。 |  brock | None | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *baggere, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to badge, referring to the white markings on the badger's head.
