imperdibility:/ˌɪmpərˈdɪbɪlɪti/ | n. | 不可信性，难以置信 | The imperdibility of his story made it difficult to believe. |  His claims had an air of imperdibility about them. | 近义词: incredibility, unbelievability | 反义词: credibility, believability | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *imperdibilis, from in- "not" + perdibilis "losable," from perdere "to lose."  The suffix "-ity" transforms the adjective into a noun denoting a state or quality.
