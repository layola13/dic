gages:/ˈɡeɪdʒɪz/| n.|测量仪器，规格，抵押品|A device used for measuring something, such as the thickness of wire or the amount of rain that has fallen; a standard measure or scale; something deposited as security for the fulfillment of a condition.|The mechanic used several gages to ensure the parts were the correct size. 技工使用了几种规格来确保零件尺寸正确。|Synonyms: gauge, measure, instrument, security, pledge|Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for the broad meaning of "gages". Specific antonyms depend on the context.  For example, if "gage" refers to a security deposit, an antonym might be "debt". If it refers to a measuring instrument, an antonym might be "estimate".)|Noun


gages (alternative spelling of gauges):/ˈɡeɪdʒɪz/| v.|测量，估计，判断|To measure precisely, to estimate or judge|He tried to gage the depth of the river with a stick. 他试图用一根棍子测量河的深度。|Synonyms: measure, estimate, assess, judge, evaluate|Antonyms: misjudge, miscalculate|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old North French *gauge (Modern French jauge), a measuring rod. Ultimately from Late Latin *gaugia, of uncertain origin.
