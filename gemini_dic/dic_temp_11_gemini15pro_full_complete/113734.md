macroeconomicses: /ˌmækroʊˌiːkəˈnɑːmɪksɪz/ | n. |宏观经济学（复数，不常用）| The study of the overall aspects and workings of a national economy, such as inflation, growth, unemployment, and economic policies.  It looks at the economy as a whole rather than focusing on individual markets or businesses. | Macroeconomicses is not standard English; the word "macroeconomics" is itself treated as both singular and plural.  It would be unusual to add an "es" to it.  Instead, refer to "various fields of macroeconomics" or "multiple macroeconomics theories." | 近义词: macroeconomy | 反义词: microeconomics | 词性: noun

词源:  From Greek *makros* (large) + *oikos* (house) + *nomos* (law).  The term "macroeconomics" emerged in the 20th century, distinguishing it from microeconomics, which focuses on individual markets.
