Olmecs: /ˈɒlmeks/ | n. | 奥尔梅克人 |  A member of a people of ancient Mexico, who flourished between 1200 and 400 BC along the southern Gulf Coast. They are known for their monumental stone sculptures, including colossal heads. | The Olmecs are considered the mother culture of Mesoamerica. 奥尔梅克人被认为是中美洲的母文化。|  Example 2: The Olmecs developed a complex system of writing and mathematics. 奥尔梅克人发展了一套复杂的书写和数学系统。 | Synonyms: Mesoamericans (broader term) | Antonyms: N/A | Noun
