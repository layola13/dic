misrepeat:/ˌmɪsrɪˈpiːt/ | v. | 错误地重复；说错；背错 | To repeat something incorrectly, often a word or phrase. |  She misrepeated the instructions, causing confusion. 她把指示说错了，造成了混乱。| The actor misrepeated his lines and had to start the scene again. 演员背错了台词，不得不重新开始这场戏。 | mispronounce, misstate, misquote | repeat correctly, articulate clearly | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- + repeat.  "Mis-" is a prefix of Old English and Germanic origin meaning "wrongly," "badly," or "incorrectly." "Repeat" comes from the Old French "repeter" and ultimately from the Latin "repetere," meaning "to seek again," "to attack again," or "to repeat."
