mechanizer:/ˈmɛkəˌnaɪzər/| n. |机械化者，机械化推广者|A person or thing that mechanizes something, especially farming or industry.  Someone who introduces machines or automatic devices to replace manual labor. |The farm was transformed by the mechanizer who introduced modern tractors and harvesters.  这个农场被引入现代拖拉机和收割机的机械化推广者改变了。|近义词：automaton,  innovator, modernizer|反义词:traditionalist, luddite|词性:noun

Etymology: From the verb "mechanize," which is derived from "mechanism" + "-ize." "Mechanism" comes from the Greek "mēkhanē," meaning "machine" or "contrivance."
