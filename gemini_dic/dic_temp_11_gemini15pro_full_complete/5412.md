alme:/ɑːlm/ | n. |  灵魂; 心灵; 精髓; 要旨 |  The innermost part of a person's being, their spirit or soul.  It can also refer to the essence or most important part of something. |  The alme of the book is its message of hope. 这本书的精髓在于它传递的希望。|  Synonyms: soul, spirit, essence, core | Antonyms: body, exterior, superficiality | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *alme (unattested), from Latin anima (“breath, soul, life”), from Proto-Italic *anəmā, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂enh₁- (“to breathe”). Cognate with Ancient Greek ἄνεμος (ánemos, “wind”). Related to animate.
