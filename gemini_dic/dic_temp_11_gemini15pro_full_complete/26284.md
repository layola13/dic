buttressing:/ˈbʌtrəsɪŋ/
|n.|支撑；扶壁；支持|The act of supporting or reinforcing something, like a wall or an argument.|The buttressing of the old cathedral walls prevented further collapse.  旧教堂墙壁的支撑防止了进一步的倒塌。|support, reinforcement, strengthening|undermining, weakening|noun
buttress:/ˈbʌtrəs/
|vt.|支持；加强|To support or reinforce something.|The lawyer buttressed his argument with compelling evidence. 律师用令人信服的证据支持他的论点。|support, reinforce, strengthen|weaken, undermine|verb
buttress:/ˈbʌtrəs/
|n.|扶壁；支撑物|A structure built against a wall to support or strengthen it.|The flying buttresses of the cathedral are a marvel of engineering.  大教堂的飞扶壁是工程学的奇迹。|support, prop, brace|none|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *butrecer* "to push, thrust," from *bouter* "to push, thrust" and *-ecer* (suffix forming frequentative verbs).  Ultimately from Vulgar Latin *buttare* "to thrust," perhaps from a Germanic source.
