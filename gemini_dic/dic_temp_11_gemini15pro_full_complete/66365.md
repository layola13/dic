excused: /ɪkˈskjuːzd/ | adj. | 免除的；被原谅的；有理由的 |  freed from an obligation or excused for a fault; forgiven. | He was excused from attending the meeting due to illness. 他因病被免于参加会议。| excused from jury duty 免除陪审团义务 |  obligated, responsible | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *excusen*, from Old French excuser, from Latin excusare (“to free from blame, make an excuse for”), from ex- (“out of, from”) + causa (“cause, reason”).

excuse oneself =  为自己辩解
