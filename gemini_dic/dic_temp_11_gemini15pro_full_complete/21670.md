blusterously:/ˈblʌstərəsli/ | adv. | 喧闹地；狂暴地；咆哮地 |  In a loud, noisy, and boastful manner, often with little effect. | He blusterously proclaimed his innocence, but no one believed him. 他喧闹地声称自己无辜，但没有人相信他。|  The wind blew blusterously, rattling the windows.  风狂暴地吹着，窗户嘎嘎作响。 |noisily, loudly, boisterously, clamorously | quietly, calmly, softly | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From bluster (v.) + -ously (adv. suffix).  "Bluster" comes from Middle English "blustren," likely of imitative origin, suggesting the sound of a strong wind or forceful speech.
