infatuate: /ɪnˈfætʃueɪt/ | vt. | 使迷恋；使着迷 | To inspire or possess with a foolish or extravagant passion or admiration. | He was completely infatuated with her. 他完全被她迷住了。 |  enamor, captivate, obsess | disenchant, disillusion | verb

infatuated: /ɪnˈfætʃueɪted/ | adj. | 迷恋的；着迷的 | Filled with or marked by foolish or extravagant passion. | She was infatuated with the handsome actor. 她迷恋上了那位英俊的演员。 | smitten, obsessed, enamored | indifferent, apathetic | adjective

infatuation: /ɪnˈfætʃueɪeɪʃn/ | n. | 迷恋；着迷 | A foolish or extravagant passion. |  His infatuation with her lasted only a few weeks. 他对她的迷恋只持续了几个星期。 | crush, obsession, passion | indifference, apathy | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *infatuatus, past participle of infatuare (“to make foolish”), from in (“in”) + fatuus (“foolish”).
