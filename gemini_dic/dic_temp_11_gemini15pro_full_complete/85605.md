hauberk:/ˈhɔːbɜːrk/| n.|锁子甲，铠甲|A long, sleeveless shirt of chain mail worn as defensive armor.|The knight donned his hauberk before riding into battle. 骑士在骑马出战前穿上了他的锁子甲。|Synonyms: chain mail, byrnie, mail shirt|Antonyms: None|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *hauberc*, from Frankish *halsberg* (neck protection), from *hals* (neck) + *berg* (protection).  Cognate with Old High German *halsberg* (neck protection).
