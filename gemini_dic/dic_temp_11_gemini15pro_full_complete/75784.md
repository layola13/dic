fumarate:/ˈfjuːməreɪt/| n.|富马酸盐；反丁烯二酸盐|A salt or ester of fumaric acid.  It plays a crucial role in the Krebs cycle, a series of chemical reactions used by all aerobic organisms to generate energy through the oxidation of acetate derived from carbohydrates, fats, and proteins into carbon dioxide and water.|Sodium fumarate is used as a food additive. 富马酸钠用作食品添加剂。|Synonyms: butenedioate|Antonyms: None|Noun
