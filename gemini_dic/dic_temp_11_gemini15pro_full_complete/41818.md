cortisones: /ˈkɔːrtɪˌzoʊnz/ | n. | 皮质激素 | A group of steroid hormones produced by the adrenal cortex or manufactured synthetically.  They are used medically to treat inflammation, allergies, and other conditions. |  She was prescribed cortisone injections for her arthritis. 她被开了皮质激素注射剂来治疗关节炎。 | steroids, glucocorticoids |  | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From cortisone + -s (plural suffix).  Cortisone itself comes from cortex (outer layer, especially of the brain or an organ) + -one (chemical suffix denoting a ketone). Ultimately from Latin cortex (bark, rind).
