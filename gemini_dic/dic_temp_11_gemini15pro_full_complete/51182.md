destabilise:/ˌdiːˈsteɪbəlaɪz/| v.| 使不稳定，动摇| to make something less firm or steady, especially a political situation | The economic crisis destabilised the government. 经济危机动摇了政府。|synonyms: undermine, weaken, unsettle| antonyms: stabilize, strengthen, secure | verb

Etymology:  From de- (meaning "reverse" or "remove") + stabilise (from stable, ultimately from Latin stabilis "standing firm").
