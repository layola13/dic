ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hwæt, neuter singular nominative and accusative of hwā (“who”, interrogative pronoun), from Proto-Germanic *hwat, from Proto-Indo-European *kʷód. Cognate with Scots what (“what”), West Frisian wat (“what”), Dutch wat (“what”), German was (“what”), Danish hvad (“what”), Swedish vad (“what”), Icelandic hvað (“what”), Latin quod (“what, which; because”), Sanskrit कत् (kat, “who, which, what”).
