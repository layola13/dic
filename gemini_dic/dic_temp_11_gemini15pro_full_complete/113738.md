macrofauna:/ˈmækroʊfɔːnə/ | n. | 大型动物群 | Animals large enough to be seen with the naked eye, especially those living in the soil or a body of water. | Example 1: The macrofauna of the coral reef includes fish, crabs, and starfish. 珊瑚礁的大型动物群包括鱼、螃蟹和海星。

Example 2:  Scientists studied the impact of pollution on the river's macrofauna. 科学家研究了污染对河流大型动物群的影响。| Synonym: megafauna, large animals | Antonym: microfauna | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From macro- (large) + fauna (animal life).  "Macro-" comes from the Greek "makros" meaning "large" or "long."  "Fauna" comes from the Latin "Fauna," the Roman goddess of animals and fertility, and by extension, the animal life of a region.
