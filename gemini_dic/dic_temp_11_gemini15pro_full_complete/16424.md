bardship:/ˈbɑːrdʃɪp/ |n.| 吟游诗人的身份或职业；诗人的使命；诗歌创作的艰难|The life of a bard, especially the hardships associated with it.  The difficulties and challenges faced by a poet or bard. |He bore the bardship with stoic grace, never complaining about the lack of patronage. 他以坚忍的优雅承受着诗人的艰辛，从不抱怨缺乏赞助。|  The young bard struggled with the bardship of finding his voice in a world saturated with noise.  这位年轻的吟游诗人努力在充满喧嚣的世界中找到自己的声音，这对他来说是一种挑战。 | near synonyms: poetic vocation, poetic calling, poetic struggles | antonyms:  prose, silence, comfort, ease| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From 'bard' (poet) + 'ship' (a suffix indicating state, condition, or office).  'Bard' ultimately comes from the Old Celtic *bardos.
