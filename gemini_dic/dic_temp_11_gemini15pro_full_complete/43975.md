crier:/ˈkraɪər/| n.|叫卖者，传令员，哭泣的人|A person who shouts out news or announcements in a public place; a person who weeps easily.|The town crier announced the birth of the princess. 城镇传令员宣布了公主的诞生。|synonyms: announcer, herald, proclaimer, weeper|antonyms: stoic (for weeper)|noun

词源:
From Middle English *criour, from Anglo-Norman criour, from Old French criere, from crier (“to shout, cry, proclaim”), from Latin quiritare (“to call for help, complain”), frequentative of quirī (“to complain”).
