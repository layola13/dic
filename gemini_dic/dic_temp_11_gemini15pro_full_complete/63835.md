epigenesis:/ˌɛpɪˈdʒɛnɪsɪs/ | n. | 后成论，渐成论 | The theory that an embryo develops progressively from an undifferentiated egg cell.  The development of an organism's form by successive differentiation of an initially undifferentiated structure rather than by the unfolding of a preformed miniature. | Example 1:  The theory of epigenesis contrasts with preformationism. 后成论与先成论形成对比。

Example 2:  Modern biology supports the theory of epigenesis in embryonic development. 现代生物学支持胚胎发育中的后成论。| Synonyms:  development, evolution, formation, morphogenesis | Antonyms: preformation, preformationism | noun
