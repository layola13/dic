name-calling:/ˈneɪmˌkɔːlɪŋ/
n.|辱骂，骂人;污蔑|The act of using abusive or insulting language to describe someone. |He resorted to name-calling when he couldn't win the argument.  他无法赢得辩论时就 resorted to name-calling.|近义词：abuse, insults, slurs, defamation, vilification|反义词：praise, compliment|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From name + calling, a verbal noun from the verb call.  The sense of "giving someone a bad name" dates from the late 19th century.
