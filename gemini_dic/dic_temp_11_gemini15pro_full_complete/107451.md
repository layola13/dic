lampooning:/læmˈpuːnɪŋ/
n.|讽刺；嘲讽|The act of criticizing someone or something publicly in a humorous way, often by exaggerating their faults. |His latest play is a lampooning of the political elite.  他最新的戏剧是对政治精英的讽刺。|近义词：satirizing, mocking, ridiculing|反义词：praising, commending|词性:名词(gerund)
lampoon:/læmˈpuːn/
n.|讽刺文章；讽刺诗|A piece of writing, a cartoon, etc. that criticizes someone or something publicly in a humorous way, often by exaggerating their faults.|The magazine published a lampoon of the president. 这家杂志刊登了一篇讽刺总统的文章。|近义词：satire, parody, caricature|反义词：eulogy, panegyric|词性:名词
lampoon:/læmˈpuːn/
v.|讽刺；嘲弄|To criticize someone or something publicly in a humorous way, often by exaggerating their faults.|The show lampooned the pretensions of the upper class.  这个节目讽刺了上流社会的虚荣。|近义词：satirize, mock, ridicule|反义词：praise, commend|词性:动词
