frontbencher:/ˈfrʌntˌbɛntʃər/| n. |前座议员；前排议员|A member of Parliament who sits on the front benches of the House of Commons or other legislative body, typically a senior member of the government or opposition. |The Prime Minister addressed the frontbenchers directly. 首相直接向坐在前排的议员们发表讲话。|  Example 2: The opposition frontbencher challenged the government's policy. 反对党前座议员挑战了政府的政策。| synonym: leading member, senior politician| antonym: backbencher | noun
