haguebut:/ˈheɪɡbʌt/| n.|钩銃，火绳枪|A heavy firearm, an early type of portable arquebus, used from the 15th to the 17th centuries.|The soldiers were armed with haguebuts and pikes. 士兵们配备了钩銃和长矛。|Synonyms: arquebus, handgonne, matchlock|Antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *haquebute*, from Old Dutch *hakebusse* (literally "hook gun"), probably from *hake* ("hook") + *busse* ("box," "gun").  The "hook" likely refers to a hook used to steady the weapon when firing.
