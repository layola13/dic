criminalize: /ˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪz/ | vt. |使…成为犯罪；宣布…为非法|To make something illegal or a crime.|The government criminalized the possession of certain drugs. 政府将持有某些毒品定为犯罪。|The act criminalized drug use. 这项法令将吸毒定为犯罪。|synonyms: outlaw, prohibit, ban|antonyms: legalize, decriminalize, permit|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From crime + -ize.  "Crime" comes from the Latin "crimen," meaning "accusation, charge, indictment, crime."  The suffix "-ize" comes from the Greek "-izein," and is used to form verbs meaning "to make or become."
