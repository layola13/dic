canaille:/kəˈnaɪ/ /kəˈneɪ/| n.|rabble, riffraff; the masses, the common people (often used derogatorily)|A term of contempt for the common people, implying that they are vulgar, base, or disorderly.|The king looked down upon the canaille with disdain. 国王轻蔑地看着这些贱民。|The writer satirized the political aspirations of the canaille.  这位作家讽刺了这些平民的政治抱负。|近义词：rabble, mob, riffraff, dregs, scum|反义词：aristocracy, elite, nobility|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *canaille*, from Old Italian *canaglia*, “pack of dogs,” from *cane*, “dog,” from Latin *canis*, “dog.”  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱwṓn-,* "dog".
