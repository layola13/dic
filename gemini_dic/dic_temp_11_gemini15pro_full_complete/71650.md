flatter:/ˈflætər (US)/ /ˈflætə(r) (UK)/
v.|奉承，谄媚；使显得更漂亮；感到荣幸|To praise someone excessively or insincerely, often to gain favor. To make someone appear more attractive or favorable than they actually are.  To feel honored or pleased.|He flattered her with compliments about her appearance. 他用恭维她外貌的话奉承她。|synonyms: compliment, praise, adulate, charm, coax, blandish|antonyms: criticize, insult, disparage|verb

flatter:/ˈflætər (US)/ /ˈflætə(r) (UK)/
adj.|（尤指比实际）平坦的|Smoother or more level than is actually the case.|The photo made her face look flatter than it really is. 这张照片使她的脸看起来比实际更平。|synonyms: level, even, smooth|antonyms: uneven, bumpy, rough|adjective
