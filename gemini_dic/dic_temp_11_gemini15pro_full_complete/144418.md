outsucks:/ˌaʊtˈsʌks/| verb (informal)| 比...更糟糕，胜过...（通常指负面方面）| It means to be worse than, to surpass in negative qualities.  Often used in a comparative sense, implying something is significantly worse than another thing.| That new restaurant really outsucks the old one.  那家新餐馆比老餐馆差多了。|  outstinks, overshadows (negatively)| outshines, surpasses (positively)| verb
Etymology:  A combination of "out-" (meaning exceeding or surpassing) and "sucks" (slang for being bad or unpleasant).  The term likely originated in online communities and gaming culture.
