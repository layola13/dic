bioethicists: /ˌbaɪoʊˈɛθɪsɪsts/
n.
生物伦理学家
Experts who study and address the ethical implications of biological research and advancements, especially in medicine and genetics.
Bioethicists debated the ethical implications of cloning. 生物伦理学家们就克隆的伦理意义进行了辩论。

近义词: medical ethicists, ethical consultants
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps someone who disregards bioethics)
词性: noun (plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
bio-: from Greek bios "life"
ethics: from Greek ēthikē (tekhnē) "(practical) science of morals"
-ist: suffix indicating a person who practices or is an expert in a particular field
