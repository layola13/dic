outscout:/ˌaʊtˈskaʊt/
verb
侦察得比…更仔细；胜过…的侦察
to surpass in scouting; to gather information more effectively than another scout.
The experienced hunter outscouted the enemy troops and gained valuable intel.  经验丰富的猎人比敌军侦察得更仔细，获得了宝贵的情报。
The team outscouted their opponents and found their weakness.  这个队比他们的对手侦察得更仔细，发现了他们的弱点。
近义词:outmaneuver, surpass, outwit
反义词:be outsmarted, be outmaneuvered
词性:v.

outscout:/ˌaʊtˈskaʊt/
noun
胜过他人侦察者
one who surpasses others in scouting
He was known as the best outscout in the region, always one step ahead of the enemy. 他是该地区最著名的侦察高手，总是比敌人领先一步。
She proved herself a valuable outscout, consistently providing critical information. 她证明了自己是一个很有价值的侦察者，不断地提供关键信息。
近义词:expert scout, superior scout
反义词:poor scout, ineffective scout
词性:n.
