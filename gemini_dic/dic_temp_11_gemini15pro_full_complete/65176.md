ethnolinguists: /ˌɛθnoʊˈlɪŋɡwɪsts/ | n. | 民族语言学家 |  Experts who study the relationship between language and culture. They investigate how language influences culture and vice versa, exploring topics such as how different languages categorize the world, how language reflects social structures, and how language changes over time in response to cultural shifts. | Ethnolinguists have documented how the language spoken by a community can reflect their traditional practices and beliefs. 民族语言学家记录了社群使用的语言如何反映他们的传统习俗和信仰。

Ethnolinguists are studying the impact of globalization on indigenous languages. 民族语言学家正在研究全球化对土著语言的影响。 |  sociolinguists, anthropological linguists |  (No clear antonym) | noun
