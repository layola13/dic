ironstone-chinas:/ˈaɪərnˌstoʊn ˈtʃaɪnəz/| n.|铁石瓷器|A type of inexpensive, opaque, and durable ceramic tableware, often white or cream-colored, made in England from the early 19th century.  It's stronger than porcelain but less translucent.|My grandmother had a beautiful set of ironstone-chinas. 我祖母有一套漂亮的铁石瓷器。|Synonyms: ironstone ware, earthenware|Antonyms: porcelain, bone china|Noun
