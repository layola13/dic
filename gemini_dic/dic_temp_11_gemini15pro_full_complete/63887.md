epimachus:/ɪˈpɪməkəs/| n. |披肩，斗篷; (尤指古希腊的) 骑兵披风|A short cloak worn by Greek soldiers and horsemen. |Alexander the Great wore an epimachus in battle.  亚历山大大帝在战斗中穿着一件披风。|cloak, cape, mantle| - |noun
Etymology->
From Ancient Greek ἐπίμαχος (epímakhos, “fit for fighting, warlike”), from ἐπί (epí, “upon, on”) + μάχη (mákhē, “battle, fight”).
