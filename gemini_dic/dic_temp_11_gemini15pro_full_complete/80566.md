goody-goody:/ˌɡʊdiˈɡʊdi/
adjective|伪善的，假正经的，过分规矩的|A goody-goody person often acts excessively virtuous or moralistic, sometimes to an irritating degree. They may be seen as insincere or self-righteous.|例句：She's such a goody-goody, always telling the teacher when someone breaks the rules. 她真是个假正经，总是向老师告状有人违反规定。|近义词：sanctimonious, self-righteous, priggish|反义词：rebellious, mischievous, bad|词性:adjective

goody-goody:/ˌɡʊdiˈɡʊdi/
noun|伪君子，假正经的人|A goody-goody is a person who is excessively or affectedly virtuous.|例句：He was a goody-goody in school, always following the rules and never getting into trouble.  他在学校里是个乖乖仔，总是循规蹈矩，从不惹麻烦。|近义词：prig, prude|反义词：rebel, scoundrel|词性:noun
