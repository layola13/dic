artboards:/ˈɑːrtbɔːrdz/
n.
画板
A drawing surface, typically on a computer, that represents a single screen or page in a design.
The designer arranged the different screens of the app on separate artboards.  设计师将应用程序的不同屏幕排列在单独的画板上。
canvases, panels, worksheets

noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"artboard" is a compound word made up of "art" and "board."  "Art" refers to the creative work being done, and "board" refers to the surface on which it is done.  It's analogous to a traditional artist's drawing board. The term became widely used with the advent of computer graphics software.
