embarkment:/ɪmˈbɑːrkmənt/| n. |堤防，筑堤；上船，登机|A structure built to protect a shoreline from erosion or flooding; the act of boarding a ship or aircraft.|例句：The embarkment protected the town from the rising tide. 堤坝保护了小镇免受上涨的潮水侵袭。

例句：The embarkment of the passengers onto the cruise ship was smooth and efficient. 乘客登上游轮的过程顺利而高效。|近义词：bank, levee, boarding, embarkation|反义词:disembarkation, debarkation|词性:noun

词源: From the French embarquement, ultimately from the verb embarquer "to embark," from en- "in" + barque "bark" (small ship).
