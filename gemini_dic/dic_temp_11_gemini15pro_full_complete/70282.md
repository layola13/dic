fiddly:/ˈfɪdli/ | adj. | 难弄的，繁琐的，需要技巧的 | Awkward or complicated to do or use because of many small parts or details. |  This toy is too fiddly for young children. 这个玩具对小孩子来说太难弄了。

The clasp on this necklace is very fiddly. 这条项链的扣子很难扣。


fiddly:/ˈfɪdli/ | adj. | 爱摆弄的，好拨弄的 |  Having a tendency to fiddle with things. |  He's a fiddly person, always touching and adjusting things. 他是个爱摆弄的人，总是摸来摸去，调整东西。

She has fiddly fingers and is good at knitting. 她手指灵活，擅长编织。


ETYMOLOGY->
From fiddle (verb), meaning to make small movements with the hands, often restlessly.  Likely related to Middle Dutch *fidelen, frequentative of an unattested verb meaning "to move quickly or lightly."

SYNONYMS->
fussy, finicky, tricky, awkward, cumbersome, fiddlesome

ANTONYMS->
simple, easy, straightforward
