burlapping: /ˈbɜːrlæpɪŋ/ | n. |粗麻布，麻袋布|The rough material used for making burlap sacks.  A coarse, woven fabric made from jute or hemp, typically used for sacks and wrapping.| The artist used burlapping to create a textured background for his painting.  艺术家用粗麻布为他的画作创作了一个有纹理的背景。| 近义词:hessian, sackcloth, gunny | 反义词: silk, satin | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From burlap + -ing (present participle suffix).  Burlap itself comes from the town of Burlap, in India, where the fabric was originally made.
