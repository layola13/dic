capriccioso:/kəˌprɪtʃiˈoʊsoʊ/ /kəˌprɪtʃiˈəʊsəʊ/
| adj. |反复无常的；任性的；随心所欲的| Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior. |The capricious weather made it difficult to plan a picnic. 反复无常的天气使得难以计划野餐。|近义词：whimsical, fickle, erratic, impulsive, mercurial|反义词：steady, consistent, reliable|词性:形容词
capriccioso:/kəˌprɪtʃiˈoʊsoʊ/ /kəˌprɪtʃiˈəʊsəʊ/
| adv. |反复无常地；任性地；随心所欲地|In a capricious manner; impulsively. |She played the piece capriccioso, letting her emotions guide her fingers. 她随性地演奏了这首曲子，让情感引导着她的手指。|近义词：whimsically, erratically, impulsively|反义词：steadily, consistently, reliably|词性:副词
capriccioso:/kəˌprɪtʃiˈoʊsoʊ/ /kəˌprɪtʃiˈəʊsəʊ/
| n. |随想曲；狂想曲|A musical composition free in form and often whimsical or fanciful. |The composer's capriccioso was full of unexpected twists and turns. 作曲家的随想曲充满了意想不到的曲折。|近义词：fantasia, rhapsody|反义词: (No direct antonym for a musical form)|词性:名词
