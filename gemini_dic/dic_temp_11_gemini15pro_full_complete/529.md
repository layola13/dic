abominate:/əˈbɒmɪneɪt/
|v.|憎恶，厌恶，痛恨|To feel intense hatred or loathing for something.|She abominates cruelty to animals. 她痛恨虐待动物。|
|近义词：detest, loathe, abhor, despise|
|反义词：adore, love, cherish, admire|
|词性：动词|

abomination:/əˌbɒmɪˈneɪʃn/
|n.|令人憎恶的事物，可憎之物|Something that is regarded with intense aversion or loathing.|His cruelty to animals was an abomination. 他对动物的残忍令人憎恶。|
|近义词：atrocity, horror, outrage, evil|
|反义词：delight, pleasure, joy, blessing|
|词性：名词|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin abominari "deprecate as an ill omen, detest," from Latin ab- "away from" + ominari "foretell, prophesy," related to omen.  The original sense was "to turn away from something as from an evil omen," hence "to deprecate, dislike strongly, hate, detest."
