coronilla:/ˌkɒrəˈnɪlə/ (US) /ˌkɒrəˈnɪlə/ (UK)| n. |冠花；念珠；花冠|A genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae, characterized by their umbrella-like clusters of small flowers. Also refers to a small crown or chaplet, especially one used in religious ceremonies.  Sometimes used poetically to refer to a garland or wreath.|The coronilla bloomed brightly in the spring sunshine. 春天的阳光下，冠花开得灿烂。|Synonyms: crown, chaplet, wreath, garland|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

coronilla:/ˌkɒrəˈnɪlə/ (US) /ˌkɒrəˈnɪlə/ (UK)| n. |念珠|A string of beads used in reciting prayers, especially in the Roman Catholic Church.  Derived from the resemblance of the beads to the small flowers of the Coronilla plant.|She held her coronilla and prayed silently. 她拿着念珠默默地祈祷。|Synonyms: rosary, prayer beads|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
