mobocracy:/mɒˈbɒkrəsi/ | n. |暴民统治；暴民政治| Rule by a mob; control of a government by those outside the conventional or lawful structures, typically involving violence. | The French Revolution, at its height, descended into mobocracy. 法国大革命在其鼎盛时期沦为暴民统治。| The mobocracy threatened to overthrow the newly elected government. 暴民政治威胁要推翻新当选的政府。 | 近义词: anarchy, ochlocracy | 反义词: democracy, republic | 词性: noun

Etymology: From French *mobocratie*, from English *mob* + *-cratie* (from Greek *-kratia*, "power, rule"). 最初来自法语 *mobocratie*，由英语 *mob* 和 *-cratie*（来自希腊语 *-kratia*，意为“权力，统治”）组成。
