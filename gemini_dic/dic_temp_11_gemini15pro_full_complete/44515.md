crossover:/ˈkrɒsˌoʊvər/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌəʊvə(r)/ (UK)| n. adj. v.| 交叉；跨界；跨越；转乘站| A crossover is a point or place where two things, such as roads or subjects, cross each other. | The band's music is a crossover between jazz and rock.  这支乐队的音乐融合了爵士乐和摇滚乐。| synonyms: blend, hybrid, fusion | antonyms: separation, division | noun

crossover:/ˈkrɒsˌoʊvər/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌəʊvə(r)/ (UK)| adj.| 交叉的；跨界的；跨越的 | Crossover describes something that involves two different groups or areas of activity. | This is a crossover hit that appeals to both young and old audiences.  这是一首老少咸宜的跨界热门歌曲。| synonyms: interdisciplinary, multidisciplinary | antonyms: specialized, niche | adjective

crossover:/ˈkrɒsˌoʊvər/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌəʊvə(r)/ (UK)| v.| 交叉；跨界；跨越 | To crossover means to move from one area of activity to another. | The athlete decided to crossover from track and field to basketball.  这位运动员决定从田径转到篮球。| synonyms: transition, shift, branch out | antonyms: remain, stay | verb
