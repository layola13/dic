cosied:/ˈkəʊ.zid/ /ˈkoʊ.zid/ | v. | 使舒适；依偎；蜷伏；| to make oneself or someone else warm and comfortable, often by sitting close together; to settle oneself in a comfortable or safe place |  We cosied up by the fire. 我们依偎在火炉旁。| synonyms: snuggled, cuddled, nestled | antonyms: separated, distanced | verb (past tense)

cosy:/ˈkəʊ.zi/ /ˈkoʊ.zi/ | adj. | 舒适的；惬意的；亲密的 | giving a feeling of warmth, comfort, and relaxation | The room was cosy and inviting. 房间很舒适温馨。| synonyms: comfortable, snug, warm | antonyms: uncomfortable, cold, unwelcoming | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Scots *cosie, from Dutch koesen (“to talk, chat, gossip”), from Middle Dutch kosen. Related to German kosen (“to caress, fondle”), Old English cēosan (“to choose”).  The connection may be via the notion of a chosen or select companion with whom one can be intimate.
