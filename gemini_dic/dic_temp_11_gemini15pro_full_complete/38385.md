conceptualised:/kənˈsɛptʃuəlaɪzd/ | v. | 使形成概念；构思；将…概念化 |  To form a concept or idea of something; to think of something in terms of its abstract or theoretical meaning. | The architect conceptualised the building as a giant wave.  这位建筑师将这座建筑构想成一个巨浪。 | conceptualize, envision, imagine, formulate |  - | verb

conceptualize:/kənˈsɛptʃuəlaɪz/ | v. | 使形成概念；构思；将…概念化 |  To form a concept or idea of something; to think of something in terms of its abstract or theoretical meaning. |  She struggled to conceptualize the sheer scale of the universe. 她难以理解宇宙的浩瀚规模。 | conceptualise, envision, imagine, formulate | - | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From conceptual + -ize.  Conceptual comes from concept + -ual. Concept comes from Late Latin conceptus, meaning "a thought, notion, idea," from Latin concipere "to take in, conceive, become pregnant," from com- "together" + capere "to take."
