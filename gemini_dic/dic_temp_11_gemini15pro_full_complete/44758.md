crumbs:/krʌmz/ | n. |碎屑，面包屑|small pieces of bread, cake, or other food that have broken off a larger piece| The floor was covered in crumbs after the children had finished their cookies. 孩子们吃完饼干后，地板上满是碎屑。| synonyms: fragments, bits, scraps, particles | antonyms: whole, complete | noun

crumbs:/krʌmz/ | int. |哎呀，糟糕（表示轻微的恼怒或失望）| (informal) used to express mild annoyance or disappointment | "Crumbs! I've forgotten my keys." “哎呀！我忘了带钥匙了。” | synonyms:  drat, shoot | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, expresses a feeling rather than a concrete thing) | interjection

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cromme, from Old Norse krumma (“crumb, fragment”), from Proto-Germanic *krummǭ (“crumb”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)krembh- (“to bend, curve”). Cognate with Danish krumme (“crumb”), Swedish krumma (“crumb”). Related to crumble.
