eviction: /ɪˈvɪkʃən/ | n. | 驱逐，逐出 | The act of expelling someone, especially a tenant, from a property. | The family faced eviction after failing to pay rent. 这家人因未能支付租金而面临被驱逐的困境。|synonyms: expulsion, removal, ouster | antonyms: admittance, acceptance, welcoming | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *eviccioun*, from Anglo-Norman eviction, evictioun, from Latin ēvictiō (“a dispossessing, ejection”), from ēvictus, past participle of ēvincō (“I conquer completely, prevail upon, evict”), from ex (“out of, from”) + vincō (“I conquer”).
