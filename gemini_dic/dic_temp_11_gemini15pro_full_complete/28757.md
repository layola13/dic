carnification:/ˌkɑːrnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/
n.
肉化，肉样变，(肺)肉质变
The process of becoming fleshy or like flesh.  The pathological conversion of tissue into a substance resembling flesh.
The carnification of the lung tissue made it difficult for the patient to breathe. 肺组织的肉质变使得病人呼吸困难。
近义词：induration, hardening
反义词：softening
词性：noun

Etymology: From Late Latin carnificātiō, from Latin carnificāre (“to make flesh”), from carō (“flesh”) + -ficāre (akin to faciō (“I make, I do”)).
