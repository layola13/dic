diapirism:/ˌdaɪəˈpaɪrɪzəm/| n. |底辟作用，刺穿褶皱作用|The process by which a mobile mass, such as salt or magma, intrudes into overlying rocks.|Example 1: Diapirism of salt domes is a common geological phenomenon.  盐丘的底辟作用是一种常见的地质现象。

Example 2: The formation of mud volcanoes is often associated with diapirism. 泥火山的形成通常与底辟作用有关。|Synonym: intrusion, piercing, uplift|Antonym: subsidence, sinking|noun
