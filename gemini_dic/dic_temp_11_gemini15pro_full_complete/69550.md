fellowfeel:/ˈfɛləʊfiːl/| n.|同情，共鸣，感同身受|A feeling of sharing another's emotions, especially sorrow or pain. |She felt a deep fellowfeel for the refugees fleeing the war. 她对逃离战争的难民深感同情。|Synonyms: empathy, compassion, sympathy|Antonyms: apathy, indifference|noun

fellowfeel:/ˈfɛləʊfiːl/| v.|同情，共鸣，感同身受|To share the feelings of another, especially sorrow or pain. |He fellowfeels with those who have lost loved ones. 他对那些失去亲人的人感同身受。|Synonyms: empathize, sympathize, commiserate|Antonyms: disregard, ignore|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From *fellow* (companion, sharer) + *feel* (to experience emotion).  The word dates back to the late 16th century.
