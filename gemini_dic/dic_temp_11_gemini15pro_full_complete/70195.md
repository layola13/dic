fibrins:/ˈfaɪbrɪnz/| n. |纤维蛋白|Insoluble fibrous proteins formed from fibrinogen by the action of thrombin, forming the essential portion of a blood clot.|A mesh of fibrin traps red blood cells and platelets, forming a clot to stop bleeding. 纤维蛋白网捕获红细胞和血小板，形成凝块以止血。| The fibrin network strengthens the clot and provides a framework for tissue repair. 纤维蛋白网络加强了凝块并为组织修复提供了框架。|Synonym:  None (Fibrin is a specific protein)|Antonym: None (There isn't a direct opposite of a specific protein)|noun
