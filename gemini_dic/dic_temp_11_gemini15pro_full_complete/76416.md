gaggling:/ˈɡæɡlɪŋ/| n. |咯咯声，傻笑声|The sound of a flock of geese. Also used to describe high-pitched, often silly or nervous laughter, especially of a group of women.|The room was filled with the gaggling of teenage girls. 房间里充满了少女们的咯咯笑声。|近义词：cackling, giggling, chattering|反义词: silence, quiet|词性:noun

词源: From the verb "gaggle," which imitates the sound geese make.  Ultimately of imitative origin.
