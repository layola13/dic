flack:/flæk/ | n. | 公关人员，新闻发言人；吹捧；高射炮；严厉批评 | A person whose job is to represent an organization, famous person, etc. to the public;  publicity or promotion, especially of a favorable kind; an anti-aircraft gun; strong criticism | The movie star's flack denied the rumors about her divorce.  这位电影明星的公关人员否认了关于她离婚的谣言。 | synonyms: publicist, press agent, spokesman, promoter | antonyms: critic, detractor | noun

flack:/flæk/ | v. | 做公关工作；吹捧；严厉批评 | To act as a publicist; to promote with excessive enthusiasm; to criticize harshly |  He flacked for the new band, trying to get them airplay on the radio. 他为这支新乐队做宣传，试图让他们在电台播放他们的歌曲。| synonyms: publicize, promote, hype, criticize | antonyms: criticize, condemn | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Yiddish *flak, from German *Flak, abbreviation of *Fliegerabwehrkanone, literally “aircraft-defense gun.”
