dandyise:/ˈdændɪˌaɪz/ | v. | 把…打扮成花花公子；使…纨绔化 | To make someone or something like a dandy; to give someone or something a dandyish appearance or character. |  He dandified himself with a brightly colored scarf and a top hat. 他用一条鲜艳的围巾和一顶大礼帽把自己打扮得像个花花公子。 | synonyms: embellish, adorn, spruce up | antonyms: simplify, roughen, unadorn | verb

dandyism:/ˈdændɪɪzəm/ | n. | 花花公子作风；纨绔习气 |  The behavior or style of a dandy; excessive concern with appearance and fashion. | The dandyism of the 18th century was characterized by elaborate clothing and affected manners. 18世纪的花花公子作风以精致的服装和做作的举止为特征。| synonyms: foppishness, affectation, pretension | antonyms: modesty, simplicity, naturalness | noun
