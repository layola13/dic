codependent:/ˌkoʊdɪˈpɛndənt/| adj. n.|相互依赖的，共依存的；共依赖者|Characterized by excessive emotional or psychological reliance on a partner, typically one with an illness or addiction.  A relationship where one person supports or enables another person's addiction, poor mental health, immaturity, irresponsibility, or under-achievement.|She was in a codependent relationship with her alcoholic husband.|近义词：interdependent, mutually dependent, enabling|反义词：independent, autonomous|词性: adjective, noun

codependency:/ˌkoʊdɪˈpɛndənsi/| n.|相互依赖，共依存|The state of being codependent.|Her codependency made it difficult for her to leave the abusive relationship.|近义词：interdependence, mutual dependence|反义词：independence, autonomy|词性: noun


Etymology: co- (together) + dependent.  "Co-dependent" emerged in the late 20th century, originally used in the context of addiction, referring to the relationship between the addict and a person who facilitates or enables the addiction.
