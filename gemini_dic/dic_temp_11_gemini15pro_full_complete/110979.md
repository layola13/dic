liquidator:/ˈlɪkwɪdeɪtər/| n.| 清算人； ликвидатор|A liquidator is a person appointed to wind up the affairs of a company or an estate, especially one that is insolvent. |The liquidator sold the company's assets to pay off its debts. 清算人出售了公司的资产以偿还债务。

|The court appointed a liquidator to oversee the bankruptcy proceedings. 法院指定了一名清算人来监督破产程序。|Synonyms: receiver, administrator, trustee|Antonyms: founder, establisher|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin liquidator, from Latin liquidus (“liquid, clear, evident”), from liquere (“to be fluid, clear”).  The sense development is from "making clear (accounts)" to "settling (debts)."
