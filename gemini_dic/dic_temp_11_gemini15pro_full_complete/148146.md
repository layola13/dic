oversensitized: /ˌoʊvərˈsɛnsɪtaɪzd/ | adj. | 过度敏感的 | Excessively sensitive; easily irritated or offended.  Made to react to stimuli more strongly than is usual or desirable. |  She became oversensitized to criticism after the incident.  她在那件事之后变得对批评过度敏感。 | hypersensitive, touchy, irritable | insensitive, thick-skinned | adjective

oversensitize: /ˌoʊvərˈsɛnsɪtaɪz/ | vt. | 使过度敏感 | To make someone or something excessively sensitive. | The media has oversensitized the public to this issue. 媒体使公众对这个问题过度敏感。 |  sensitize, stimulate | desensitize, numb | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + sensitize.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much". "Sensitize" comes from the Late Latin "sensibilis", meaning "capable of sensation", ultimately from the Latin "sentire", "to feel".
