fertilizing:/ˈfɜːrtɪlaɪzɪŋ/
v. |施肥，使肥沃，使受精|The act of applying fertilizer to soil to enrich it and promote plant growth.  Also refers to the union of male and female gametes in sexual reproduction.|He is fertilizing the garden. 他正在给花园施肥。|Synonyms: enriching, nourishing, manuring|Antonyms: depleting, impoverishing|Verb

fertilize:/ˈfɜːrtɪlaɪz/
v. |施肥，使肥沃，使受精|To supply with fertilizer; to make fertile or productive; to impregnate or pollinate.|Farmers fertilize their fields to improve crop yields.  农民给田地施肥以提高作物产量。|Synonyms: enrich, nourish, manure|Antonyms: deplete, impoverish|Verb

fertilizer:/ˈfɜːrtɪlaɪzər/
n. |肥料|A substance added to soil to improve its fertility.|The farmer used organic fertilizer on his crops. 农民对他的作物使用了有机肥料。|Synonyms: manure, compost, plant food|Antonyms: pollutant|Noun

fertile:/ˈfɜːrtaɪl/
adj. |肥沃的，能生育的，多产的|Producing or capable of producing abundant vegetation or offspring; intellectually productive.|The fertile valley produced a bountiful harvest. 肥沃的山谷带来了丰收。|Synonyms: productive, fruitful, rich|Antonyms: barren, infertile, sterile|Adjective

fertility:/fərˈtɪləti/
n. |肥沃，生育力，繁殖力|The ability to produce offspring or vegetation; the quality of being fertile.|The fertility of the soil is essential for good crops.  土壤的肥沃性对于获得好收成至关重要。|Synonyms: fecundity, productiveness, richness|Antonyms: sterility, barrenness, infertility|Noun
