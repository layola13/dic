Manichean:/ˌmænɪˈkiːən/ | adj. n.| 摩尼教的；二元论的；善恶二元论者| relating to or resembling Manicheanism, characterized by dualism, especially regarding good and evil |  His worldview is rather Manichean, seeing everything as a struggle between good and evil. 他的世界观相当摩尼教式，将一切视为善恶之间的斗争。 | synonyms: dualistic, dichotomous | antonyms: nuanced, complex | adjective

Manichean:/ˌmænɪˈkiːən/ | n.| 摩尼教徒；二元论者 | a follower of Manicheanism |  He was considered a Manichean for his belief in the constant battle between light and darkness.  他因相信光明与黑暗之间的持续斗争而被认为是摩尼教徒。 | synonyms: dualist | antonyms:  (No single direct antonym, perhaps "monist") | noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Manichaeus*, from Greek *Μανιχαῖος* (Manikhaîos), from *Μάνης* (Manēs), the founder of Manicheanism.  Ultimately from Aramaic *Mānī* "Mani."
