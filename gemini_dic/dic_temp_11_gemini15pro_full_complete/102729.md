janissaries:/ˈdʒænɪsɛriz/ | n. |禁卫军，近卫军|Elite infantry units that formed the Ottoman Sultan's household troops, bodyguards and the first modern standing army in Europe. They were known for their strict discipline, loyalty (initially), and distinctive uniforms.|例句：The janissaries were once the most feared soldiers in Europe. 禁卫军曾经是欧洲最令人生畏的士兵。

例句：The janissaries’ revolt led to significant political instability. 禁卫军的叛乱导致了严重的政治动荡。|近义词：bodyguards, elite troops, soldiers|反义词: civilians, rebels|词性: noun
