enslaved: /ɪnˈsleɪvd/ | adj. | 被奴役的，受控制的 | Made a slave; forced into servitude; controlled or dominated. |  Many Africans were enslaved and brought to America. 许多非洲人被奴役并带到美洲。|  The people were enslaved by the dictator. 人民被独裁者奴役。| 近义词: captive, oppressed, subjugated, enthralled | 反义词: free, liberated, independent | 词性: 形容词

词源: From en- + slave + -ed.  "En-" acts as a prefix meaning "to put into or onto," and "-ed" creates the past participle form of the verb "enslave." Ultimately deriving from the Medieval Latin word "sclavus," meaning "Slav," as many Slavic peoples were enslaved during the Middle Ages.
