burnt-out:/ˌbɜːnt ˈaʊt/ | adj. |精疲力竭的，耗尽的| (of a person) exhausted and demoralized as a result of prolonged stress or excessive work |  The teachers are feeling burnt out after a long school year.  老师们在漫长的学年后感到精疲力竭。|  exhausted, drained, spent, worn out | energetic, refreshed, invigorated | adjective

burnt-out:/ˌbɜːnt ˈaʊt/ | adj. |烧毁的| (of a building or vehicle) destroyed or badly damaged by fire | The burnt-out shell of the car was a stark reminder of the accident. 烧毁的汽车外壳是事故的鲜明提醒。 | destroyed, gutted, ruined | intact, undamaged | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb phrase "burn out," which combines "burn" (from Old English *bærnan, related to German brennen) and "out" (from Old English ūt).
