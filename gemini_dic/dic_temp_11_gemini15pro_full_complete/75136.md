frisks:/frɪsk/| v. n.|搜身，轻快地跳跃 |To pass the hands quickly over (someone) in a search for concealed weapons, drugs, or other items. Also, to move about in a playful and energetic way. |The police officer frisked the suspect. 警察搜查了嫌疑犯的身。The lambs frisked in the meadow. 羊羔在草地上嬉戏。|近义词：search, pat down, frolic, gambol|反义词：trudge, plod|词性：动词，名词

词源：From Old French *frisque (“fresh, lively”), from Frankish *frisk (“fresh”), from Proto-Germanic *friskaz (“fresh, strong”). Cognate with Old English fersc (“fresh”). The noun is derived from the verb.
