conjecturably:/kənˈdʒɛktʃərəbli/| adv.|推测地，臆想地|Based on conjecture; by guesswork.  It means something is possibly true based on an educated guess or inference, but without definitive proof.|Conjecturably, the new policy will increase unemployment.  推测起来，这项新政策会增加失业率。|Synonyms: presumably, supposedly, hypothetically, possibly, arguably|Antonyms: definitely, certainly, undoubtedly, provably|adverb

conjecture:/kənˈdʒɛktʃər/| n. v.|推测，臆测|A guess or inference based on incomplete or inconclusive information. As a verb, it means to form an opinion or supposition about (something) on the basis of incomplete information.|Her conjecture about the election results proved correct. 她对选举结果的推测被证明是正确的。 He conjectured that the painting was a forgery. 他推测这幅画是赝品。|Synonyms: guess, supposition, speculation, surmise, theory|Antonyms: fact, proof, certainty, knowledge|noun, verb
