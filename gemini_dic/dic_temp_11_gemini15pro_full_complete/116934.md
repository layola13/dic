mazard:/ˈmæzərd/| n.| 头，脑袋; (尤指)头顶; 头盖骨|A blow to the mazard. 对头部的打击。|He received a blow on his mazard. 他头部挨了一击。|近义词：head, skull, pate, noggin|反义词: foot (opposite end of the body)|noun

词源: From Old French *maser* "to crush, smash," from Vulgar Latin *masicare*. Related to macerate.

用法说明:
Mazard is an archaic and somewhat humorous term for the head, particularly the top of the head or skull. It is not commonly used in modern English but may be encountered in older literature or used for stylistic effect.
