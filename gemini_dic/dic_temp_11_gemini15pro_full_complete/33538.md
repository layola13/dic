ciderkin:/ˈsaɪdərkɪn/| n.|苹果酒（尤指弱的或未发酵的）|A weak or unfermented cider.|They enjoyed a refreshing ciderkin on a warm summer's day. 他们在温暖的夏日享用了一杯清爽的苹果酒。|近义词:cider, perry|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "strong liquor")|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *ciderkin*, a diminutive of *cider*, from Old French *cidre*, from Late Latin *sicera*, from Greek *sikera* (strong drink), from Semitic (cf. Akkadian *šikaru*, Aramaic *ܫܟܪܐ* (*šikra*), Hebrew *שיכר* (*šēḵār*)). Ultimately from a Proto-Semitic root.
