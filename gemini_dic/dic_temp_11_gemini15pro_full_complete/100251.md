interruption:/ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən/ | n. | 中断，打扰，干扰 | An act of interrupting the flow of something; a break in continuity. | The constant interruptions made it difficult to concentrate.  不断的打扰使得很难集中注意力。| synonyms: disruption, interference, intrusion, obstruction | antonyms: continuation, progression | noun

interruption:/ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən/ | n. | 中断，打扰，干扰 | The act of stopping something from continuing or being finished; a break in continuity. |  There was an interruption in the electricity supply.  电力供应中断了。 | synonyms: break, pause, hiatus, cessation | antonyms: continuance, resumption | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *interruptionem, from Latin interruptus (past participle of interrumpere ‘break off, interrupt’), from inter- ‘between’ + rumpere ‘break’.
