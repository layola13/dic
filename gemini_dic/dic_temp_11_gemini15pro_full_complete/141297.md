open-jaw:/ˈoʊpənˌdʒɔː/| adj. n.|开口的，张开的;开口航程|An open-jaw flight is a type of round-trip air ticket where the departure city and arrival city of the outbound leg and the departure city and arrival city of the inbound leg are different.  It's like a triangle or a horseshoe shape, rather than a simple loop.|I booked an open-jaw ticket, flying into Rome and out of Paris.|近义词：round-trip, multi-city|反义词:direct, one-way|词性:形容词, 名词
Etymology:  "Open" from Old English "open"; "Jaw" from Old English "ceowan" (to chew).  The combined term "open-jaw" in aviation context is a metaphorical usage, referring to the shape of the travel itinerary resembling an open mouth.
