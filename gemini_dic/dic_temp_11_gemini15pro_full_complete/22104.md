bolsheviki:/ˌbɒlʃəˈvɪki/ /ˌboʊlʃəˈvɪki/ | n. | 布尔什维克（党员）， Bolsheviks的复数形式 | Members of the majority faction of the Russian Social Democratic Party, which seized power in the October Revolution of 1917 and formed the Communist Party.  |  The Bolsheviki, led by Lenin, overthrew the provisional government. 布尔什维克在列宁的领导下推翻了临时政府。| synonyms: communists, revolutionaries | antonyms: Tsarists, Mensheviks | noun

bolshevik:/ˌbɒlʃəˈvɪk/ /ˌboʊlʃəˈvɪk/ | n. adj. | 布尔什维克（党员）| A member of the majority faction of the Russian Social Democratic Party, which seized power in the October Revolution of 1917 and formed the Communist Party. Also used as an adjective meaning relating to or characteristic of the Bolsheviks. | Lenin was a key Bolshevik leader. 列宁是布尔什维克的主要领导人。 The Bolshevik revolution changed Russia forever. 布尔什维克革命永远地改变了俄罗斯。| synonyms: communist, revolutionary | antonyms: Tsarist, Menshevik | noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Russian большевик (bol'ševik), literally "member of the majority," from больший (bol'šij, “bigger; greater”), ultimately from Proto-Slavic *bolьji (“bigger”).  The Bolsheviks were originally the majority faction in the Russian Social Democratic Labour Party.
