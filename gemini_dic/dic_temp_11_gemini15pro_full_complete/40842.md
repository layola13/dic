cony:/ˈkəʊni/ (UK), /ˈkoʊni/ (US)| n.|兔子；野兔|A rabbit, especially a young one.  A term of endearment, especially for a woman.|The hunter caught a cony in his snare.|兔子被猎人用陷阱抓住了。|Synonyms: rabbit, hare, bunny|Antonyms: predator|noun

cony:/ˈkəʊni/ (UK), /ˈkoʊni/ (US)| n.|（古）骗子；易受骗的人|Someone easily tricked or deceived. (archaic)|The cony was quickly parted from his money by the charlatan.|那个容易受骗的人很快就被江湖骗子骗走了钱。|Synonyms: dupe, fool, gull|Antonyms: skeptic, cynic|noun
