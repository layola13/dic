billows:/ˈbɪloʊz/ (US), /ˈbɪləʊz/ (UK)| n. | 巨浪；波涛汹涌；翻腾 | large waves; masses of something that rise and fall; to swell out or puff up | The ship pitched and rolled in the heavy billows. 船在巨浪中颠簸摇晃。| synonyms: waves, surges, swells, breakers | antonyms: ripples, calms | noun

billows:/ˈbɪloʊz/ (US), /ˈbɪləʊz/ (UK)| v. | 翻腾；汹涌；鼓起 | to rise and fall like waves; to swell out or puff up | Smoke billowed from the burning building.  浓烟从燃烧的建筑物中滚滚而出。| synonyms: surge, swell, puff up, bulge | antonyms: deflate, shrink, flatten | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *bylgja, from Proto-Germanic *bulgijō ("bag, bulge, billow"). Cognate with Old English bylge ("billow, wave, bag"), Old Saxon bulgia ("leather bag"), Old High German bulga ("bag, sack").
