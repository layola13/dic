jesuitical: /ˌdʒɛzjuˈɪtɪkəl/ | adj. | 耶稣会的；诡辩的；狡猾的 |  Of or relating to the Jesuits, often used to imply casuistry, sophistry, or equivocation.  Derived from "Jesuit," referring to the Society of Jesus, a Catholic religious order.  The term gained a negative connotation due to accusations of using complex arguments to justify questionable actions. |  His jesuitical reasoning allowed him to justify almost any behavior. 他的诡辩推理让他几乎可以为任何行为辩护。| The politician gave a jesuitical answer, avoiding a direct response to the question. 这位政客给出了一个狡猾的答案，避免直接回答这个问题。|Synonyms: casuistic, sophistical, equivocal, cunning, devious, wily | Antonyms:  straightforward, honest, candid, frank, artless | adjective
