cosy-up: /ˈkəʊzi ʌp/ (British), /ˈkoʊzi ʌp/ (American) | phrasal verb | 巴结；奉承；套近乎 | To act in a friendly way towards someone, especially in order to gain an advantage. |  He's been cosying up to the boss recently, hoping for a promotion. 他最近一直在巴结老板，希望能升职。

cosy-up: /ˈkəʊzi ʌp/ (British), /ˈkoʊzi ʌp/ (American) | phrasal verb |  依偎；亲热 | To get physically close to someone for warmth or affection. | The couple cosyed up by the fire on a cold winter's night.  寒冷的冬夜，这对夫妇依偎在火炉旁。
