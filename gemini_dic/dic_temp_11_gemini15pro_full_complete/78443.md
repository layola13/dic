get-your-Irish-up:/ɡet jɔːr ˈaɪrɪʃ ʌp/ | idiom |  激怒，发怒；变得好斗 | To become angry or roused to a fighting spirit. This idiom alludes to the stereotypical view of Irish people as quick-tempered.  | Example 1: He got his Irish up when they accused him of cheating. 他被指责作弊时勃然大怒。

Example 2: Don't get your Irish up! Let's talk about this calmly. 别生气！我们冷静地谈谈这件事。 | Synonym: get angry, lose one's temper, fly off the handle, see red | Antonym: calm down, cool off | idiom
