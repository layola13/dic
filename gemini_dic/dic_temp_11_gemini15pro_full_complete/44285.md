cronyism:/ˈkroʊniˌɪzəm/
n.|裙带关系；任人唯亲；徇私|The practice of favoring one's friends, especially in political appointments.|The mayor was accused of cronyism after he appointed his brother-in-law to a key position. 市长任命他的内兄担任要职后被指责为任人唯亲。|近义词：favoritism, nepotism, patronage|反义词：impartiality, meritocracy|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From crony + -ism.  "Crony" itself likely originated in the 17th century from Greek *khronios* meaning "long-lasting," referring to long-time companions.  The pejorative sense of favoritism developed later.
