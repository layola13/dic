hand-me-down:/ˈhænd miː daʊn/ | n. adj. | 传给我的旧物；旧衣服 | Something, typically clothing, that has been passed down from another person, usually an older sibling. | My younger brother always wears my hand-me-downs. 我弟弟总是穿我穿过的旧衣服。 |  She was grateful for the hand-me-down clothes from her cousins. 她很感激表姐们给她的旧衣服。 | 近义词: secondhand, used | 反义词: new, brand-new | 词性:名词，形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
"Hand-me-down" is a compound noun formed from the verb "hand down" (to give or pass something to someone, especially something that has belonged to an older relative) and the pronoun "me." The term suggests something passed down by hand to another person.
