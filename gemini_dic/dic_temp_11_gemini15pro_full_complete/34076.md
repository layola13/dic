civilised:/ˈsɪvɪlaɪzd/ /ˈsɪvəlaɪzd/| adj.|文明的，开化的|Having a high state of culture and development both socially and technologically. Showing evidence of moral and intellectual advancement; humane, ethical.  Marked by refinement in taste and manners.|例句：They considered themselves more civilised than the neighbouring tribes. 他们认为自己比邻近部落更文明。|例句：The civilised world condemned the barbaric act. 文明的世界谴责了这种野蛮的行为。|近义词：cultured, refined, sophisticated, educated|反义词：barbaric, uncivilised, primitive, savage|词性:形容词
civilize:/ˈsɪvɪlaɪz/ /ˈsɪvəlaɪz/| vt.|使文明，使开化|To bring out of a savage, uneducated, or unrefined state, or to a less primitive condition. To instruct in the arts of life; to enlighten; to refine and polish.|例句：Education can civilize people. 教育可以使人文明。|例句：The Romans civilized much of Europe. 罗马人使欧洲的大部分地区变得文明。|近义词：educate, refine, cultivate, enlighten|反义词：barbarize|词性:动词
civilisation:/ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ /ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃn/| n.|文明，文化|A society in an advanced state of social development (e.g., with complex legal and political and religious organizations). The stage of human social development and organization that is considered most advanced.|例句：Ancient Greece was the cradle of Western civilisation. 古希腊是西方文明的摇篮。|例句：The civilisation of the Incas was highly developed. 印加文明高度发达。|近义词：culture, society|反义词：barbarism|词性:名词
