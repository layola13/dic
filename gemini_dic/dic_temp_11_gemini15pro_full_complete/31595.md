chastener:/ˈtʃeɪsənər/ (US), /ˈtʃeɪsənə(r)/ (UK)| n.|惩罚者；磨炼者|A person or thing that chastens or corrects.  A chastener can be a person who disciplines someone, or an experience that teaches a valuable lesson.|The coach acted as a chastener, pushing the athletes to their limits. 教练扮演着惩罚者的角色，把运动员逼到极限。

The difficult experience served as a chastener, teaching him humility. 这段艰难的经历磨练了他，教会了他谦逊。|近义词：disciplinarian, corrector, punisher, reformer|反义词: encourager, rewarder|词性: noun

Etymology: From Middle English *chastener*, from Anglo-French *chastiener*, from Late Latin *castigāre* (“to chastise, correct”), from Latin *castīgāre*, from *castus* (“pure”).

注意事项:
Although *chastener* can be used literally to refer to a person who administers punishment, it's more commonly used figuratively to describe an experience or situation that has a corrective or humbling effect.  It often implies a process of learning and growth through hardship.
