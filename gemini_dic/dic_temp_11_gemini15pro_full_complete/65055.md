eternalism:/ɪˈtɜːrnəlɪzəm/ | n. |永恒主义| A philosophical theory that posits that all points in time are equally "real," as opposed to the idea that only the present exists.  It suggests that past, present, and future coexist. |Einstein's theory of relativity lends credence to eternalism, suggesting that time is a dimension like space, and all moments exist simultaneously.|Synonyms: block universe theory, B-theory of time|Antonyms: presentism, A-theory of time|noun
