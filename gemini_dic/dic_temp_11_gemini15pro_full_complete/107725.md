laocoön:/ˌlaʊəˈkəʊən/ | n. | 拉奥孔 | A Trojan priest who warned his people not to bring the wooden horse of the Greeks into Troy; he and his two sons were killed by two sea serpents sent by a god (either Athena or Poseidon, depending on the version of the story).  Also refers to a famous Hellenistic sculpture depicting this scene. |  The museum's most prized possession is a replica of the Laocoön. 该博物馆最珍贵的藏品是拉奥孔的复制品。 | synonyms: none (the name is unique) | antonyms: none | noun
