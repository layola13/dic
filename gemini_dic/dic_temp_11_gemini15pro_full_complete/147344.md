overmultiply:/ˌoʊvərˈmʌltɪplaɪ/| v. |过度相乘，过度增加|To multiply to an excessive degree, often resulting in a value exceeding the expected or desired range, particularly in the context of digital image processing.|The software seems to overmultiply the color channels, making the image look too saturated.|近义词：oversaturate, amplify, exaggerate|反义词: reduce, diminish|词性:动词

Etymology:
"over-" (Old English *ofer*, meaning "above, beyond, across") + "multiply" (from Late Latin *multiplicare*, meaning "to increase in number")
