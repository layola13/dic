nympholepsy: /ˌnɪmfəˈlɛpsi/ | n. | 迷狂，神魂颠倒；对自然美的狂热 | A state of rapture supposedly caused by the sight of beautiful nature, especially by a glimpse of a nymph.  A strong emotional response, often a feeling of overwhelming joy or ecstasy, inspired by something beautiful or awe-inspiring. |  He was overcome with nympholepsy at the sight of the waterfall cascading through the lush green forest. 他看到瀑布穿过茂密的绿色森林，欣喜若狂。

|  Her nympholepsy for the untouched wilderness led her to a career in conservation. 她对原始荒野的狂热使她从事了自然保护事业。 | 近义词: ecstasy, rapture, bliss, euphoria | 反义词: apathy, indifference, boredom | 词性: noun


词源: From Ancient Greek νυμφόληπτος (numphólēptos, “seized by nymphs”), from νύμφη (númphē, “nymph”) + λαμβάνω (lambánō, “to take, seize”).
