fettles:/ˈfɛtəlz/ (British English)| v.|整理；修补；使处于良好状态|To make or repair something, especially in a skillful or clever way.  To put something into working order.  Also, to prepare something, often food.|He fettled the engine until it purred like a kitten. 他把发动机修整到像小猫一样咕噜叫。|Synonyms: mend, repair, fix, overhaul, restore, prepare, arrange|Antonyms: break, damage, ruin|verb

fettles:/ˈfɛtəlz/ (British English)| n.|状态；良好状态|The state or condition of being in good working order or repair.  Often used in the phrase "in fine fettle."|The garden was in fine fettle after he had weeded and pruned it.  整理和修剪之后，花园状况良好。|Synonyms: condition, state, shape, order|Antonyms: disrepair, disorder|noun
