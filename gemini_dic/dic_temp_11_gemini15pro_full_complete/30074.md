cDNA:/ˌsiː diː ɛn ˈeɪ/ |n.| 互补DNA | Complementary DNA, a DNA molecule made in vitro using mRNA as a template and the enzyme reverse transcriptase. cDNA libraries are useful for identifying protein-coding genes, as they contain only the expressed genes of an organism.|  Scientists used cDNA to clone the human insulin gene. 科学家利用cDNA克隆了人类胰岛素基因。|  近义词: copy DNA | 反义词: genomic DNA | 词性: noun
