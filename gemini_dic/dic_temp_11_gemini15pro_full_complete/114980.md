manciple:/ˈmænsɪpl/| n.|采办员，伙食管理员|An official who buys provisions for a college, monastery, or other institution.|The manciple was responsible for purchasing all the food for the college.|近义词：steward, caterer, purveyor|反义词:|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *manciple*, from Latin *mancipium* ("slave, purchase"), from *manus* ("hand") + *-cipium* (suffix forming nouns related to taking or holding).  The sense evolved from "purchase" to "purchaser."
