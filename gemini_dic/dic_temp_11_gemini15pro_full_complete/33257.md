chrysaline:/ˈkrɪsəˌlaɪn/ /ˈkrɪsəlɪn/| adj.|金色的，透明的，如蛹的|Resembling a chrysalis; made of or similar to a chrysalis.  Also, by extension, translucent or golden.|The chrysaline wings of the butterfly shimmered in the sunlight. 蝴蝶金色的翅膀在阳光下闪闪发光。|Synonyms: golden, translucent, shimmering, iridescent|Antonyms: opaque, dull, dark|adjective

chrysaline:/ˈkrɪsəˌlaɪn/ /ˈkrɪsəlɪn/| n.|蛹|The protective covering of a butterfly pupa.|The butterfly emerged from its chrysaline after several weeks. 几周后，蝴蝶从它的蛹中羽化出来。|Synonyms: pupa, cocoon|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "adult" or "imago" in the context of insect development)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *chrysalide*, from Latin *chrysallis*, from Greek *khrysallis* (χρυσαλλίς), meaning "gold-colored pupa," from *khrysos* (χρυσός), meaning "gold."  The "-ine" suffix denotes "of or pertaining to," or "like."
