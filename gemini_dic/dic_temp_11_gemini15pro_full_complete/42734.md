countess:/ˈkaʊntɪs/| n.|女伯爵；伯爵夫人|A woman holding the rank of countess, especially the wife or widow of a count.|The countess presided over the grand ball.|近义词:noblewoman, peeress|反义词:commoner|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *contesse*, from Late Latin comitissa, feminine form of comes (“count”). Ultimately from Latin comit-, present participle stem of comitari (“to accompany”).
