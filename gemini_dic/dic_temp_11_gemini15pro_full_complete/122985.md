monan: /ˈmoʊnən/ | n. | 莫南（苏格兰盖尔语，指泥炭沼泽，尤指生长苔藓的沼泽） | A monan is a type of peat bog, especially one where moss grows, common in Scotland and areas where Gaelic is spoken.  It derives from the Scottish Gaelic word *monadh*, meaning 'moor' or 'mountainous area'.  The word is not commonly used in modern English. |  The sheep grazed on the rough grasses of the monan.  羊群在莫南粗糙的草地上吃草。|  bog, marsh, mire, moor |  --- | noun
