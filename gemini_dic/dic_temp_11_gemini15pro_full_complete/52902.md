dimmer:/ˈdɪmər (US) / ˈdɪmə(r) (UK)| n. |调光器；变暗器|A device for changing the brightness of an electric light. |Example:  I installed a dimmer switch in the living room. 我在客厅安装了一个调光开关。

Example: The dimmer allows you to create the perfect mood lighting.  这个调光器可以让你营造完美的氛围灯光。

|近义词：light control, rheostat|反义词:brightener|词性:noun


dim:/dɪm/| adj. |昏暗的；模糊的；暗淡的|Not bright or clear; faint. |Example: The room was dimly lit. 房间里光线昏暗。

Example:  He had a dim memory of the event. 他对这件事的记忆很模糊。

|近义词：faint, weak, obscure, dusky|反义词:bright, clear, distinct|词性:adjective


dim:/dɪm/| v. |使变暗；使模糊|To make or become less bright or clear. |Example: The lights dimmed as the movie began. 电影开始时，灯光变暗了。

Example: The fog dimmed the landscape. 雾使景色变得模糊。

|近义词：darken, obscure, fade|反义词:brighten, illuminate, clarify|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dimmen, from Old English *dimian ("to grow dark, become dusk"), from Proto-Germanic *dimmą (“dark, dim”), from Proto-Indo-European *dhem- (“dark, cloudy”).
