canton:/ˈkæntɒn/ (UK), /ˈkæntɑːn/ (US)| n.|州；行政区；（瑞士的）州；（纹章学中的）方块|A canton is a subdivision of a country, established for political or administrative purposes. In Switzerland, a canton is a member state of the federal republic. In heraldry, a canton is a square division occupying the upper left-hand corner of a shield.|Switzerland is divided into 26 cantons. 瑞士被划分为26个州。|Synonyms: district, region, province, state|Antonyms: whole, entirety|noun

canton:/ˈkæntɒn/ (UK), /ˈkæntɑːn/ (US)| vt.|把…划分成州；把…分派到营房|To canton is to divide a country or region into cantons or to assign troops to quarters.|The army was cantoned in the nearby town. 军队被分派到附近的城镇驻扎。|Synonyms: quarter, billet, lodge|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "centralize")|verb
