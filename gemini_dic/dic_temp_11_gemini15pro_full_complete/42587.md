counterpoint:/ˈkaʊntərˌpɔɪnt/ | n. v. | 对位法；对位乐曲；对比物；反驳 | A musical technique involving the combination of two or more independent melodic lines; something that forms a contrast to something else. | The composer skillfully used counterpoint to create a rich and complex texture.  这位作曲家巧妙地运用对位法，创作出丰富而复杂的音乐织体。 | contrast, antithesis, juxtaposition | harmony, agreement | noun, verb

Etymology:
From Middle French *contrepoint*, from Italian *contrappunto*, literally "point against point," from *contra* ("against") + *punto* ("point").  The musical sense arose from the practice of writing musical notes "against" (i.e., above or below) other notes on a staff.
