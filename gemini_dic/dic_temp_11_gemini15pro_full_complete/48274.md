decouple:/diːˈkʌpəl/| v. |分离，解耦|to separate two or more things or activities, or to become separated|The government is trying to decouple the economy from excessive reliance on exports. 政府正试图让经济摆脱对出口的过度依赖。|The two economies are now effectively decoupled. 这两个经济体现在已有效分离。|近义词：separate, disconnect, disengage|反义词：couple, link, connect|词性:动词

decoupling:/diːˈkʌplɪŋ/| n. |分离，解耦|the act of separating two or more things or activities, or the state of being separated|The decoupling of the two economies has had a significant impact on trade. 两个经济体的分离对贸易产生了重大影响。|There has been a decoupling of economic growth and environmental damage in recent years.近年来，经济增长与环境破坏已经脱钩。|近义词：separation, disconnection, disengagement|反义词：coupling, linking, connection|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- + couple.  "De-" is a prefix meaning "off, away from, down." "Couple" comes from Old French "coupler," meaning "to join, link."
