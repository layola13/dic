cheapishly:/ˈtʃiːpɪʃli/
adverb|吝啬地，小气地|in a cheap or stingy manner; in a way that suggests a lack of generosity or quality. | He furnished his apartment cheapishly, buying mostly secondhand furniture. 他很吝啬地布置了他的公寓，大部分家具都是二手的。|Synonyms: stingily, meanly, parsimoniously, frugally (negative connotation)|Antonyms: generously, lavishly, extravagantly|adverb

cheapishly:/ˈtʃiːpɪʃli/
adverb|廉价地|at a low cost; inexpensively. (This meaning is less common and can sometimes overlap with "shabbily.")|The costumes were made cheapishly and looked it. 这些服装制作得很廉价，看起来也很廉价。|Synonyms: inexpensively, economically, affordably|Antonyms: expensively, lavishly|adverb

词源: cheap + -ish + -ly

cheap (adj.): from Old English *cēap (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *kaupą (“bargain, price, business transaction”). Related to Dutch koop (“bargain, purchase”), German Kauf (“purchase”), Swedish köp (“purchase”).

-ish (suffix): forms adjectives from other adjectives or nouns, meaning "somewhat like" or "having the quality of." From Old English -isc.

-ly (suffix): forms adverbs from adjectives. From Old English -līce.
