monopolizer:/məˈnɑːpəˌlaɪzər/| n. |垄断者;独占者|A person or company that monopolizes something, especially a market or industry.  Someone who controls the entire supply of a commodity or service.| The company became a ruthless monopolizer, driving smaller competitors out of business. 这家公司变成了一个残酷的垄断者，把较小的竞争对手赶出了市场。|Synonyms: cornerer, controller, dominator, hog, trust|Antonyms: competitor, sharer|noun


monopolizes:/məˈnɑːpəˌlaɪzɪz/| v. |垄断;独占|To obtain exclusive possession or control of.| The company monopolizes the banana trade in this region.  这家公司垄断了该地区的香蕉贸易。|Synonyms: dominate, corner, control, hog |Antonyms: share, distribute|verb


monopolizing:/məˈnɑːpəˌlaɪzɪŋ/ | adj. |垄断的;独占的 |Having exclusive control or possession of something.| The monopolizing behavior of the company stifled innovation. 该公司的垄断行为扼杀了创新。|Synonyms: dominating, controlling, cornering |Antonyms: sharing, distributing|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From monopolize + -er/-or.  Monopolize comes from monopoly + -ize. Monopoly originates from the Greek "monos" (alone, single) and "polein" (to sell).
