arjuna:/ˈɑːrdʒʊnə/| n.|阿周那|Arjuna is a central character in the Hindu epic Mahabharata. He is considered to be an incarnation of the god Indra and is renowned for his skill in archery and his unwavering devotion to dharma.  The name derives from Sanskrit, meaning "bright," "shining," or "silver."|Arjuna is one of the Pandavas and a key figure in the Kurukshetra War. 阿周那是一位般度族王子，也是俱卢之战中的关键人物。|synonyms: Partha, Dhananjaya, Gudakesha|antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a proper noun.  One could consider Karna a rival, but not an antonym.)|noun
