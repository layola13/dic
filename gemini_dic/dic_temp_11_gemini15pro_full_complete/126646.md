naivety:/naɪˈiːvɪti/ /nɑːˈiːvɪti/| n.|天真，幼稚，单纯|The naivety of her question surprised everyone. 她的问题天真得让每个人都感到惊讶。|例句：His naivety led him to believe the stranger. 他的天真使他相信了那个陌生人。

例句：She displayed a certain naivety about the dangers of the city. 她对城市的危险表现出某种天真。|近义词：innocence, ingenuousness, artlessness, credulity|反义词：sophistication, cynicism, worldliness|词性:noun

naïve:/naɪˈiːv/ /nɑːˈiːv/| adj.|天真的，幼稚的，单纯的|It was naive of her to think that she could change his mind. 她认为自己可以改变他的主意，这很天真。|例句：He was naive enough to believe her lies. 他很天真，竟然相信了她的谎言。

例句：It was a naive question, but I understood why he asked it. 这是一个天真的问题，但我理解他为什么问。|近义词：innocent, ingenuous, artless, credulous|反义词：sophisticated, cynical, worldly|词性:adjective
