bambocciade:/ˌbæmbɒˈtʃɑːdeɪ/ |n. |喧闹的场景; 粗俗的绘画;  低级趣味的娱乐 | Paintings or other representations of low-life subjects, often humorous or satirical, typically depicting scenes of peasant life, tavern brawls, or other boisterous activities.  Derived from the nickname of the Dutch painter Pieter van Laer (1599-1642), known as "Il Bamboccio" (meaning "big baby" or "puppet" due to his deformed appearance), who specialized in such works. | The museum's collection includes several bambocciade depicting lively street scenes in 17th-century Rome.  博物馆的收藏包括几幅描绘 17 世纪罗马热闹街景的喧闹场景画。| The artist's bambocciade offered a glimpse into the everyday lives of common people.  这位艺术家的喧闹场景画展现了普通人的日常生活。 | synonyms: genre painting, low-life scenes, scenes of everyday life | antonyms:  idealized scenes, historical painting | noun
