harvestability's:/ˌhɑːrvɪstəˈbɪlətiz/ | n. | 可收获性，可收割性 | The quality or state of being harvestable; the ease with which something can be harvested.  | The harvestability's of the wheat crop this year is excellent due to the favorable weather conditions. 今年小麦作物的可收获性非常好，因为天气条件有利。 | Synonyms: reap-ability, gather-ability | Antonyms: unharvestability, non-harvestability | noun

Etymology:
From "harvest" (from Old English *hærfest "autumn") + "-able" (suffix meaning "capable of being") + "-ity" (suffix forming abstract nouns) + "-'s" (possessive suffix).
