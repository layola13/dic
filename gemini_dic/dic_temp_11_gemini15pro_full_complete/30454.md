centrepiece:/ˈsentəpiːs/| n.|中心装饰品，核心，|A decorative object placed in the centre of a table, often used for formal occasions.  A main or most important feature. |The elaborate floral centrepiece dominated the dining table.  精心制作的花卉中心装饰品在餐桌上占据了主要位置。

The reduction of the national debt was the centrepiece of the government's economic policy.  削减国债是政府经济政策的核心。|synonyms: centerpiece, focal point, centerpiece, highlight|antonyms: periphery, edge, minor detail|noun

centrepieces:/ˈsentəpiːsəz/| n.|中心装饰品（复数），核心（复数）|Plural form of "centrepiece."  Refers to multiple decorative objects placed in the center of tables, or multiple main or most important features. |The centrepieces at each table were unique and beautifully crafted.  每张桌子上的中心装饰品都是独一无二的，制作精美。

The centrepieces of the museum's new exhibition are two recently discovered Renaissance paintings.  博物馆新展览的核心是两幅最近发现的文艺复兴时期绘画。|synonyms: centerpieces, focal points, main features|antonyms: peripheries, edges, minor details|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the French word "centre de table" meaning "center of the table". This evolved to "centrepiece" in English.
