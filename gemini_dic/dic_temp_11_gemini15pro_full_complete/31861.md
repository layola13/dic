cheeriest:/ˈtʃɪriɪst/| adj.|最高兴的，最愉快的|Superlative form of cheery: most cheery.  It describes someone or something as being in the highest spirits, most joyful, or most optimistic.|例句：She had the cheeriest smile I'd ever seen. 她拥有我见过的最灿烂的笑容。

例句：The cheeriest room in the house was the kitchen, filled with sunlight and the smell of baking. 房子里最令人愉快的房间是厨房，充满阳光和烘焙的香味。|近义词：jolliest, happiest, merriest, most joyful|反义词：gloomiest, saddest, most miserable|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Middle English *cherisshen, from Old French cherir (“to cherish”), from Late Latin cariscus (“dear, precious”), from a Germanic source akin to Old High German chërisōn (“to lament, complain”).  The modern meaning developed from the sense of holding someone dear and treating them with kindness and affection, which naturally leads to a cheerful atmosphere.
