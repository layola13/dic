mockable:/ˈmɑːkəbl/ (US) /ˈmɒkəbl/ (UK)| adj.|可嘲笑的，可模仿的|Something that is mockable is easy or deserving to be mocked.  It invites ridicule or imitation.|His attempts at seriousness were mockable. 他一本正经的样子很滑稽.|Near synonyms:  ridiculous, absurd, laughable, derisible|Near antonyms:  serious, respectable, admirable|Adjective

Etymology->

From mock (verb) + -able (suffix).  "Mock" comes from Middle French "mocquer,"  ultimately from Vulgar Latin "*moccare," of uncertain origin, perhaps imitative.  "-able" is a suffix of French or Latin origin meaning "capable of being, fit to be, worthy of being."
