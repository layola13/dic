fatuously: /fəˈtʃuːəsli/ | adv. | 愚蠢地，傻乎乎地 | In a foolish and self-satisfied way.  Characterized by foolishness and complacency. | He fatuously believed he could fly. 他愚蠢地相信自己能飞。| foolishly, stupidly, idiotically, absurdly, vacuously | sensibly, wisely, intelligently, prudently | adverb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *fatuōsus, from Latin fatuus (“foolish, silly, simple”).
