nominated:/ˈnɒmɪneɪtɪd/ (UK) /ˈnɑːmɪneɪtɪd/ (US)| adj. |被提名的；被任命的|Having been proposed as a candidate for election or for an honor or award.|She was nominated for an Oscar. 她被提名奥斯卡奖。|近义词：proposed, designated, appointed|反义词：rejected, unnominated|词性:形容词

nominate:/ˈnɒmɪneɪt/ (UK) /ˈnɑːmɪneɪt/ (US)| vt. |提名；任命；指定|To propose or formally enter as a candidate for election or for an honor or award.|We nominated her to represent us. 我们提名她作为我们的代表。|近义词：propose, designate, appoint|反义词：reject|词性:动词

nomination:/ˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌnɑːmɪˈneɪʃn/ (US)| n. |提名；任命；指定|The act of formally proposing someone as a candidate for election or for an honor or award.|His nomination to the Supreme Court was controversial. 他被提名到最高法院是有争议的。|近义词：appointment, designation, proposal|反义词：rejection|词性:名词


词源: From Latin nominatus, past participle of nominare "to name," from nomen "name."
