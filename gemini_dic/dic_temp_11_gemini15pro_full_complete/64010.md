episyllogism:/ˌɛpɪˈsɪləˌdʒɪzəm/
|n.|
|附加三段论; 后续三段论|
|An episyllogism is a syllogism in which the conclusion of a preceding syllogism becomes the premise of the subsequent one.|
|The first syllogism concluded that all mammals are warm-blooded.  The episyllogism then used this conclusion as a premise to argue that since whales are mammals, they must also be warm-blooded.|

|inference, deduction, reasoning|

|noun|
