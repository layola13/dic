dishorse:/dɪsˈhɔːrs/
n.
卸下马匹; 使下马
The act of removing a rider from a horse, either intentionally or unintentionally.
The knight was dishorsed during the joust. 骑士在马上长矛比武中被摔下马。

The king was dishorsed by the rebel soldiers. 国王被叛军士兵拉下马。

v.
使下马; 卸下马匹
To remove a rider from a horse, either intentionally or unintentionally.

He was dishorsed by a low-hanging branch. 他被低垂的树枝从马上撞了下来。


She dishorsed her opponent in the competition. 她在比赛中把对手从马上摔了下来。

近义词: unmount, unhorse
反义词: mount

词性:  动词 (v.)  名词 (n.)
