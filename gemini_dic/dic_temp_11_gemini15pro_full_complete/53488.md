disarticulate:/dɪsɑːrˈtɪkjʊleɪt/
verb
使脱节，使分离
to separate at the joints; to take apart
The butcher disarticulated the chicken.  屠夫把鸡切块。

disarticulate:/dɪsɑːrˈtɪkjʊleɪt/
verb
(指说话)不连贯的，支离破碎的
to speak unclearly or incoherently
His speech was disarticulated and difficult to follow. 他的讲话断断续续，很难理解。

disarticulate:/dɪsɑːrˈtɪkjʊleɪt/
adjective
脱节的，分离的
separated at the joints
The skeleton was disarticulated for easier study.  为了便于研究，这具骨骼被拆开了。

disarticulate:/dɪsɑːrˈtɪkjʊleɪt/
adjective
(指说话)不连贯的，支离破碎的
lacking connection or coherence
Her disarticulated sentences made it hard to understand her point. 她断断续续的句子让人难以理解她的观点。


Etymology: From Latin dis- (“apart, asunder”) + articulatus (“jointed”), past participle of articulare (“to joint”).

Synonyms: disconnect, separate, detach, disjoin, disjoint (v.); incoherent, rambling, disconnected, disjointed (adj.)

Antonyms: connect, join, unite, articulate (v.); coherent, connected, fluent (adj.)
