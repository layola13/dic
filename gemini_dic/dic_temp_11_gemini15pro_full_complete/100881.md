intrusive:/ɪnˈtruːsɪv/ | adj. | 侵入的，打扰的，唐突的 | Forcing itself or its way in without welcome; interrupting or disturbing. |  The intrusive reporter kept asking personal questions.  这个爱管闲事的记者不断地问私人问题。 |例句：The constant notifications from the app were intrusive and annoying.  这个应用程序不断发来的通知很烦人，打扰到我了。| 近义词：invasive, obtrusive, meddling, pushy | 反义词：unobtrusive, discreet, respectful | 词性: 形容词

intrusive:/ɪnˈtruːsɪv/ | adj. | 侵入的，地质学中指侵入岩 |  (geology) Of rock formed from magma that has been forced into pre-existing rock.  | The intrusive granite formed a dramatic cliff face. 侵入的花岗岩形成了壮观的悬崖面。 |例句：The geologists studied the intrusive rock formations in the valley. 地质学家研究了山谷中侵入的岩层。| 近义词：igneous, plutonic | 反义词：extrusive | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin intrusus, past participle of intrudere "to thrust in," from in- "in" + trudere "to thrust, push."
