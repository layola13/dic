birrus:/ˈbɪrəs/| n.|粗毛斗篷，粗羊毛披风|A coarse woolen cloak or hooded cape worn by peasants and shepherds in ancient Rome.|The shepherd wore a heavy birrus to protect himself from the cold wind. 牧羊人穿着一件厚重的粗毛斗篷来抵御寒风。|Near synonyms: cloak, cape, mantle|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps light clothing)|Noun
