epistolary: /ɪˈpɪstəˌlɛri/ /ɪˈpɪstəˌleri/
adjective
书信体的；用书信写成的
Relating to or written in the form of letters.
Example:  The novel is epistolary, told entirely through letters exchanged between the two main characters. 这部小说是书信体的，完全通过两个主要人物之间的书信往来讲述故事。

Example:  His epistolary style makes the story feel very personal and intimate.  他书信体的写作风格使故事感觉非常个人化和亲密。
synonym: letter-based, correspondence-based
antonym: non-epistolary

epistolaries: /ɪˈpɪstəˌlɛriz/ /ɪˈpɪstəˌleriz/
noun
书信体小说；书信集
A novel or other literary work written in the form of letters.  Also refers to a collection of letters.
Example:  Early American epistolaries offer a fascinating glimpse into the lives of ordinary people. 早期的美国书信体小说让我们得以一窥普通人的生活。

Example:  The professor assigned the class to read a selection of 18th-century epistolaries. 教授布置学生阅读一些18世纪的书信集。
synonym:  letter novels, correspondence novels
antonym:  (No direct antonym, but could consider genres like "narratives" or "biographies" as contrasting forms)
