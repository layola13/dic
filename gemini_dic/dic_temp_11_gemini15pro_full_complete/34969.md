clodhoppers:/ˈklɑːdˌhɑːpərz/ /ˈklɒdˌhɒpəz/ | n. |笨重的鞋子；粗俗之人|Large, heavy shoes, often associated with farmworkers or rural people; also used informally and often humorously to refer to a person considered unsophisticated or awkward. |He wore a pair of muddy clodhoppers. 他穿着一双沾满泥巴的笨重鞋子。| synonyms: work boots, clunkers, stompers | antonyms: elegant shoes, slippers | noun

clodhopper:/ˈklɑːdˌhɑːpər/ /ˈklɒdˌhɒpəz/ | n. |乡巴佬；笨蛋|An unsophisticated or awkward person, often from a rural area.  |He's a bit of a clodhopper, but he means well. 他有点傻气，但他的本意是好的。| synonyms: bumpkin, yokel, oaf | antonyms: sophisticate, cosmopolitan | noun
