anorexia:/ˌænəˈreksiə/ | n. |厌食症；食欲缺乏|A serious mental illness and eating disorder characterized by an abnormally low body weight through restriction of food intake, often driven by an intense fear of gaining weight and a distorted perception of body shape and size.  It can have severe physical and psychological consequences.|She was diagnosed with anorexia after losing a significant amount of weight.|Synonyms: anorexia nervosa, self-starvation|Antonyms: bulimia, binge eating disorder|noun


anorexic:/ˌænəˈreksɪk/ | adj. |厌食的；患厌食症的|Suffering from or relating to anorexia nervosa.|The anorexic patient refused to eat anything.|Synonyms: emaciated, undernourished|Antonyms: obese, overweight|adjective
