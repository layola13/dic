jibb:/dʒɪb/| n. |三角帆；吊杆；(起重机的)悬臂；(尤指突然地)改变主意；(尤指感情上的)突然转变|A triangular sail set forward of the foremast on a sailing vessel. Also, a projecting arm of a crane or derrick. A sudden change of mind, especially an emotional one.|The yacht's jibb filled with wind as it turned.  这艘游艇转弯时，三角帆鼓满了风。|Synonyms: staysail, foresail,  shift,  change,  swerve|Antonyms: constancy,  stability|noun

jibb:/dʒɪb/| v. |(使)改变主意；(使)突然转变|(Of a horse) to stop abruptly and refuse to go on. To shy away from something; balk. To change one's mind suddenly.|The horse jibbed at the jump. 这匹马在跳跃时停了下来。 He jibbed at the last minute and refused to sign the contract. 他在最后一刻改变了主意，拒绝签署合同。|Synonyms: balk, recoil,  hesitate,  waver|Antonyms:  persist,  continue,  persevere|verb
