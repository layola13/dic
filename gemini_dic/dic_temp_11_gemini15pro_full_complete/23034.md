boulevard: /ˈbuːləvɑːrd/ (US) /ˈbuːlvɑːd/ (UK)| n. |林荫大道；大马路|A wide street, often tree-lined, and usually in a town or city.|We strolled along the boulevard, enjoying the evening air. 我们沿着林荫大道漫步，享受着夜晚的空气。|Synonyms: avenue, promenade, thoroughfare|Antonyms: alley, lane|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French boulevard, originally meaning "bulwark," a defensive rampart. Over time, the term shifted to refer to the broad open space on top of city walls, which were often converted into promenades.  Eventually, the term came to mean any broad, tree-lined street.
