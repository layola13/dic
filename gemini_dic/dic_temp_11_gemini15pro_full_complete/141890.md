ordainments: /ɔːrˈdeɪnmənts/ | n. | 仪式, 任命, 法令, 规定 |  The act of ordaining, or the state of being ordained; especially, the conferring of holy orders; consecration. Also, an established rite or ceremony; an ordinance; a decree. | The bishop performed the ordainments of the new priests. 主教执行了新神父的任命仪式。| 近义词: ordinances, decrees, rites, ceremonies, appointments | 反义词: prohibitions, cancellations | 词性: 名词

词源: From Middle English *ordeinement, *ordeynament, from Anglo-Norman ordeinement, ordeynement, from Old French ordenement, ordoinement (“arrangement, order, ordinance”), from ordener (“to arrange, order”), from Latin ōrdināre (“to arrange, order”), from ōrdō (“row, rank, order”).
