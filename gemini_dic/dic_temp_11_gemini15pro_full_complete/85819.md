hazelwort:/ˈheɪzəlˌwɜːrt/| n. |榛草, 夏枯草; 疗肺草; 黄花九轮草;|A perennial herb (Asarum europaeum) of the birthwort family, having brownish flowers and aromatic roots formerly used medicinally.|The hazelwort was used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory ailments.|近义词：asarabacca, wild ginger, European wild ginger|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

词源: From Middle English *haselwort, equivalent to hazel +‎ wort (“plant”).  The name refers to the resemblance of the leaves to those of the hazel tree.
