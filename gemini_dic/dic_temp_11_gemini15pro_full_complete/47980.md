decimalised:/ˈdesɪməlaɪzd/ | adj. | 十进制的，小数化的 | Converted to a decimal system, especially in currency.  Expressing a quantity in the decimal system. |  Example: The UK decimalised its currency in 1971. 英国于1971年将其货币十进制化。


Example:  Decimalised figures are easier to work with in calculations.  十进制数字在计算中更容易使用。 | Synonym: decimalized, metricated | Antonym: non-decimalised | adjective


decimatize:/ˈdesɪmətaɪz/ | v. |  大批杀死，大量毁灭 | To kill, destroy, or remove a large percentage or part of. Originally referring to the Roman practice of killing every tenth soldier in a mutinous legion. | Example:  The disease decimated the rabbit population. 这种疾病使兔子数量锐减。


Example: The invading army decimated the local population. 入侵的军队大量屠杀了当地居民。| Synonym: destroy, devastate, ravage | Antonym:  preserve, protect, save | verb
