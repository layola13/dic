gouger:/ˈɡaʊdʒər/| n.| 敲竹杠的人，奸商；骗子|A person who charges exorbitant prices for something, especially someone who exploits a shortage of goods or a situation of emergency.|The gougers were selling bottled water for $10 after the hurricane.|近义词：profiteer, extortionist, swindler, cheat|反义词: philanthropist, benefactor|noun

gouge:/ɡaʊdʒ/| v.| 敲竹杠；诈骗； gouge out 挖出|To overcharge; to extort.|They gouged tourists by charging triple the normal price for souvenirs.  他们向游客收取三倍于正常价格的纪念品，以此敲诈。|近义词：overcharge, extort, swindle, cheat|反义词: donate, give|verb
gouge:/ɡaʊdʒ/| v.| 挖出；凿出|To scoop or force something out, especially with a sharp tool.|He gouged out a chunk of wood with his chisel. 他用凿子凿出一块木头。|近义词：scoop, excavate, dig out|反义词: fill, insert|verb
gouge:/ɡaʊdʒ/| n.| 凿子； gouge mark 凿痕|A chisel with a concave blade, used for scooping or cutting grooves.|The carpenter used a gouge to create a decorative groove in the wood. 木匠用凿子在木头上刻出一道装饰性的凹槽。|近义词：chisel, groove|反义词: N/A|noun
