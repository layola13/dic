heliotypy:/ˌhiːliˈɒtɪpi/
n.
日光胶版术，日光照相术
A photomechanical process for producing printing plates by exposing a bichromated gelatin film under a photographic negative, hardening the gelatin where light strikes it, and then using the relief image to print copies.
The heliotypy process produced high-quality images that rivaled traditional photographs. 日光胶版术能够制作出可以与传统照片相媲美的高质量图像。
