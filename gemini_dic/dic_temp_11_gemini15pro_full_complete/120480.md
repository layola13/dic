mint:/mɪnt/| n. vt.| 薄荷；造币厂；巨款；新鲜事物 |A place where coins are made; a large amount of money; something new and fresh; an aromatic herb.|She had a mint of money.|她有一大笔钱。|近义词:spearmint, peppermint, fortune, heap, wad (for money), brand-new, fresh (for new)|反义词:stale, old|词性:名词, 动词

mint:/mɪnt/| vt.| 铸造（货币）|To manufacture (coins); to invent or create (something new).|The Royal Mint produces all British coins.|英国皇家造币厂生产所有英国硬币。|近义词:coin, forge, create, invent|反义词:withdraw, destroy|词性:动词

词源: From Old English *mynet, from Latin moneta "mint, money," originally a surname of Juno, in whose temple coins were struck.
