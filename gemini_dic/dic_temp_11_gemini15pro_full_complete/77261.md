gastropodan: /ɡæˈstrɒpədən/ | n. adj. | 腹足动物；腹足纲的 |  A mollusk of the large class Gastropoda, such as a snail, slug, or whelk, having a single shell (often coiled) or no shell at all, a flattened muscular foot for locomotion, and a head bearing stalked eyes. | Snails are common gastropods.  蜗牛是常见的腹足动物。| The garden snail is a terrestrial gastropodan. 花园蜗牛是一种陆生腹足动物。 |Synonyms: univalve, snail, slug | Antonyms: bivalve (although not a direct opposite, it represents a different class of mollusks) | noun, adjective
