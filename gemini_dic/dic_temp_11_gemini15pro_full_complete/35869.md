词源: From Middle English *cokesswayne, *cokswayne, from Old English *cōcswēin (“boat-servant, boatswain”), from cōc (“cook”) + swēin (“servant, boy”).  Originally referred to a sailor responsible for the captain's boat and later specialized to the person steering a smaller boat.
