kerb:/kɜːrb/ (British) /kɜːrb/ (American)| n. |路缘，路边|A stone or concrete edging to a pavement or raised path. |例句：
The car parked close to the kerb. 汽车停得很靠近路边。

She tripped over the kerb and fell. 她被路边绊倒了。


近义词：curb (American), edge, rim, verge|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "center of the road") |词性:noun

kerbs:/kɜːrbz/ (British) /kɜːrbz/ (American)| n. |路缘（复数）|Plural form of "kerb."|例句：

The kerbs were painted white to improve visibility at night. 路缘被漆成白色以提高夜间能见度。

The city council is planning to replace the damaged kerbs. 市议会正计划更换损坏的路缘。

近义词：curbs (American), edges, rims, verges|反义词: (No direct antonym) |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *curb*, from Old North French *curbe*, from Late Latin *curva*, feminine of *curvus* (“curved”). Cognate with Old French *corbe* (“curve, bend”), Italian *curva*.  Doublet of *curve*. The spelling with <k> is a later respelling influenced by the word *curb* (meaning "restrain").
