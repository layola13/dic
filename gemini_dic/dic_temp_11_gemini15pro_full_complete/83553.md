haemocyanin:/ˌhiːməˈsaɪənɪn/ | n. |血蓝蛋白|A copper-containing respiratory pigment found in the blood of mollusks and some arthropods, analogous to hemoglobin in vertebrates.  It appears blue when oxygenated and colorless when deoxygenated.|The horseshoe crab's blood contains haemocyanin, which makes it appear blue.| 近义词: respiratory pigment | 反义词: hemoglobin | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek αἷμα (haîma, “blood”) + κυανός (kuanós, “dark blue”) + -in.
