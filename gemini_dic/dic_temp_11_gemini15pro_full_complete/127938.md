neonomianism:/ˌnɪːoʊˈnoʊmiənɪzəm/
n.
新律法主义
The doctrine that the gospel abrogates the moral law contained in the Old Testament.
Neonomianism was a controversial theological doctrine in 17th-century England.  新律法主义是17世纪英格兰一个有争议的神学教义。
Neonomianism is often contrasted with antinomianism. 新律法主义常与反律法主义对比。
近义词: None (It's a specific theological term)
反义词: antinomianism


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *neos* (new) + *nomos* (law) + *-ian* (adherent of) + *-ism* (doctrine).
