old-maidy:/ˌoʊldˈmeɪdi/ | adj. | 老处女的，古板的，拘谨的 | Resembling or characteristic of an old maid, especially in being fussy, prim, or overly fastidious.  |  She has such old-maidy ways, always fussing about the tidiness of the house. 她有一些老处女的习惯，总是对房子的整洁度大惊小怪。 | Example: Her apartment was decorated in an old-maidy style, with doilies and floral prints everywhere. 她的公寓装饰得很老气，到处都是桌布和碎花图案。 | Synonyms: prim, prissy, fussy, fastidious, proper | Antonyms:  easygoing, casual, relaxed, slovenly | adjective


old-maid:/ˌoʊldˈmeɪd/ | n. | 老处女 | An older woman who has never married. | She was considered an old maid because she was still single in her forties. 她被认为是老处女，因为她四十多岁仍然单身。 | Example: In those days, a woman who remained unmarried past a certain age was often labeled an old maid. 在那个年代，一个过了某个年龄仍然未婚的女人通常会被贴上老处女的标签。 | Synonyms: spinster | Antonyms: wife, married woman | noun
