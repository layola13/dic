catfish: /ˈkætˌfɪʃ/ (US), /ˈkætˌfɪʃ/ (UK)| n. v. |鲶鱼；欺骗；网络钓鱼|A freshwater or marine fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth, typically bottom-dwelling.  As a verb, it refers to the act of luring someone into a relationship by means of a fictional online persona.|

例句：
He caught a large catfish in the river.  他在河里钓到了一条大鲶鱼。

She catfished him for months before he realized the truth. 她用假身份在网上欺骗了他好几个月，他才意识到真相。

近义词：(n.) bullhead, mudcat (v.) deceive, trick, dupe
反义词:  (v.) be honest, be truthful
词性:  noun, verb

catfished: /ˈkætˌfɪʃt/ (US), /ˈkætˌfɪʃt/ (UK)| v. |被网络钓鱼欺骗；被（用假身份）引诱|Past tense and past participle of "catfish".  Refers to having been deceived by someone online who created a fake persona.|

例句：
He was catfished by a woman pretending to be a model. 他被一个假扮模特的女子网络钓鱼欺骗了。

She felt devastated after being catfished for over a year.  被网络钓鱼欺骗了一年多之后，她感到非常崩溃。


近义词：deceived, tricked, duped, fooled
反义词: None (as it is a passive verb form)
词性: verb
