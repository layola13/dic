degenerationist:/dɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃənɪst/
n.|退化论者；堕落论者|A person who believes in the theory of degeneration, especially regarding social or moral decline.|The degenerationist argued that modern society was in a state of decline.|近义词:decadent, reactionary|反义词:progressive, reformist|词性:名词

Etymology:
From degeneration + -ist.  "Degeneration" comes from Late Latin *degeneratio, from Latin degenerare "to depart from one's kind, deteriorate," from de- "down, away from" + genus "race, kind."
