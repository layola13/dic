co-operator:/koʊˈɑːpəreɪtər/| n. |合作者，合作社社员|A co-operator is someone who works together with others for a common purpose, especially in a business or other organization. |例句：The co-operators in the farming cooperative shared the profits.  农场合作社的社员们分享了利润。

例句：The project was successful thanks to the efforts of all the co-operators.  由于所有合作者的努力，该项目取得了成功。|近义词：collaborator, partner, associate|反义词：competitor, opponent|词性:noun

co-operate:/koʊˈɑːpəreɪt/| v. |合作，协作|To co-operate is to work together with others for a common purpose or benefit. |例句：The two companies agreed to co-operate on the new project. 两家公司同意在新项目上合作。

例句：We need to co-operate if we want to achieve our goals. 如果我们想实现目标，就需要合作。|近义词：collaborate, work together, join forces|反义词：compete, oppose, obstruct|词性:verb

co-operation:/koʊˌɑːpəˈreɪʃn/| n. |合作，协作|Co-operation is the act of working together with others for a common purpose or benefit. |例句：International co-operation is essential to address global challenges. 国际合作对于应对全球挑战至关重要。

例句：The success of the project depends on the co-operation of all team members.  项目的成功取决于所有团队成员的合作。|近义词：collaboration, teamwork, partnership|反义词：competition, opposition, conflict|词性:noun

co-operative:/koʊˈɑːpərətɪv/| adj. |合作的，协作的|Co-operative describes someone or something that is willing to work together with others for a common purpose or benefit. |例句：The witness was very co-operative with the police.  证人非常配合警方。

例句：We have a co-operative relationship with our suppliers. 我们与供应商保持着合作关系。|近义词：collaborative, helpful, accommodating|反义词：uncooperative, unhelpful, obstructive|词性:adjective
