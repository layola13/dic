ingratiating:/ɪnˈɡreɪʃɪeɪtɪŋ/
adjective
讨好的；巴结的；迎合的
Intended to gain approval or favor; sycophantic.
He had a particularly ingratiating manner. 他有一种特别讨人喜欢的方式。
近义词:charming, flattering, fawning, obsequious
反义词:off-putting, repellent, unappealing
词性:adjective


ingratiate:/ɪnˈɡreɪʃɪeɪt/
verb
使…讨人喜欢；使…迎合
Bring oneself into favor with someone by flattering or trying to please them.
He ingratiated himself with the boss by offering to do extra work. 他主动加班来讨好老板。
近义词:charm, flatter, win over
反义词:alienate, offend, repel
词性:verb


词源:
From Late Latin ingratiat- ‘brought into favour’, from the verb ingratiare, from in- ‘into’ + gratia ‘grace, favour’.
