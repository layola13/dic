knobbly:/ˈnɒbli/ (US: /ˈnɑːbli/)| adj.|多节的；多瘤的；粗糙的|Having knobs; gnarled or misshapen. Prominent or pronounced (of features).|The branches of the tree were knobbly and twisted. 树枝疙疙瘩瘩，扭曲变形。|近义词：gnarled, knotty, lumpy, bumpy|反义词:smooth, even|词性:形容词

词源: From *knob* (a rounded lump or protuberance) + *-ly* (forming adjectives indicating a quality or characteristic) + *-er* (comparative suffix). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *knubban* (to rub, strike).
