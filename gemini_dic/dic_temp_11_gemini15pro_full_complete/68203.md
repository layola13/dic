fair-trader:/ˈfeə(r) ˌtreɪdə(r)/ | n. | 公平贸易者；公平交易商 | A person or business that trades in products that are produced ethically and sustainably, ensuring fair prices and working conditions for producers in developing countries. |  Example 1: The fair-trader sourced coffee directly from the farmers in Guatemala.  这个公平贸易商从危地马拉的农民那里直接采购咖啡。

Example 2:  She always buys her chocolate from fair-traders to support ethical production. 她总是从公平贸易商那里购买巧克力以支持道德生产。 | synonym: ethical trader, responsible trader | antonym: exploitative trader, unethical trader | noun


fair-trade:/ˈfeə(r) treɪd/ | adj. | 公平贸易的 | Relating to or produced through fair trade. |  Example 1: The shop sells a range of fair-trade products, including coffee, tea, and chocolate. 这家商店出售一系列公平贸易产品，包括咖啡、茶和巧克力。

Example 2:  They advocate for fair-trade practices to ensure better living standards for farmers. 他们倡导公平贸易实践，以确保农民有更好的生活水平。 | synonym: ethical, responsible, sustainable | antonym: exploitative, unethical, unsustainable | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"fair" (Old English "fæger," meaning "beautiful, pleasing, attractive") + "trade" (Middle English "trade," from Middle Low German "trade," meaning "track, course") - The combined meaning emphasizes trade that is "pleasing" and "attractive" because it is ethical and beneficial for all parties involved.
