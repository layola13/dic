calf: /kæf/ (US), /kɑːf/ (UK)| n.| 小牛；(鲸、象、鹿等大型哺乳动物的)幼崽；小腿肚；(尤指小牛的)皮革 |A young bovine animal, especially a domestic cow or bull in its first year.|The farmer sold his calves at the market.|例句：The calf stayed close to its mother. 小牛紧紧依偎着它的母亲。

calf: /kæf/ (US), /kɑːf/ (UK)| n.| 小腿肚 |The fleshy back part of the human leg below the knee.|He strained a muscle in his calf while running. |例句：She has shapely calves. 她有匀称的小腿。


calf: /kæf/ (US), /kɑːf/ (UK)| n.| (尤指小牛的)皮革 |Leather made from the skin of a calf.|Calf leather is soft and supple.|例句：My new shoes are made of calf. 我的新鞋是用小牛皮做的。

近义词: young, baby (for animal); lower leg, shin (for body part)
反义词: cow, bull (for animal); thigh (for body part)
词性: noun
