disarrangement:/ˌdɪsəˈreɪndʒmənt/ | n. |扰乱，混乱，杂乱|The disarrangement of the furniture made the room look messy. 家具的杂乱摆放使房间看起来很乱。|例句：The disarrangement of his papers frustrated him. 他的论文被打乱了，这让他很沮丧。

The sudden disarrangement of the bookshelves caused several books to fall to the floor. 书架突然被打乱，导致几本书掉到地上。|近义词：disorder, chaos, mess, confusion|反义词：order, arrangement, organization|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation, reversal) + arrangement (from Old French arengement, from arenger "to arrange").  The prefix "dis-" negates the meaning of "arrangement," indicating a lack of order or a state of being disordered.
