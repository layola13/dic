groomishly:/ˈɡruːmɪʃli/
adverb
精心打扮地，修饰整齐地
in a way that suggests someone has taken great care with their appearance
He was groomishly dressed in a tailored suit. 他穿着剪裁得体的西装，打扮得十分整洁。
近义词:smartly, neatly, dapperly
反义词:slovenly, untidily, messily
词性:副词

词源:
From groom (v.) + -ish + -ly.  "Groom" originally meant "boy" or "young man," and later came to mean "servant who cares for horses."  The sense of tending to one's appearance is an extension of the idea of caring for something.
