词源：From Latin *cumbentem (nominative cumbens) "lying down," present participle of *cumbere "to lie down," from PIE root *ḱemb- "to bend, to turn, to lie down."  Related to succumb.  Originally meant "lying in a particular place" and evolved to refer to someone "holding an office."
