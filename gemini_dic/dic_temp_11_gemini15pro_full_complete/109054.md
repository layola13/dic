legalistic: /ˌliːɡəˈlɪstɪk/ | adj. | 墨守成规的，拘泥于法律条文的 | Excessively concerned with or adhering to the letter or formal interpretation of the law, often to the detriment of its spirit or purpose. | The lawyer's legalistic arguments failed to impress the jury, who were looking for justice, not just technicalities.  律师墨守成规的论点未能打动陪审团，陪审团寻求的是正义，而不仅仅是技术细节。 |  例句：The contract was very legalistic and difficult to understand.  合同非常拘泥于法律条文，难以理解。 | 近义词：formal, by-the-book, technical, rigid | 反义词：flexible, lenient, pragmatic | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From legal + -istic.  "legal" comes from Old French "legal," ultimately from Latin "lex" (law).  "-istic" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "relating to" or "characteristic of."
