alibi: /ˈælɪbaɪ/ (US), /ˈælɪbaɪ/ (UK)| n.|不在场证明；托辞，借口|A claim or piece of evidence that one was elsewhere when an act, typically a criminal one, is alleged to have taken place.|He had a perfect alibi for the night of the murder. 他有不在场证明，证明谋杀案发生当晚他不在现场。|Synonyms: excuse, pretext, defense|Antonyms: confession, admission|noun

alibi: /ˈælɪbaɪ/ (US), /ˈælɪbaɪ/ (UK)| vt.| 为...提供不在场证明；辩解|To offer an excuse or defense for (someone or something).|He tried to alibi his absence from the meeting. 他试图为缺席会议找借口。|Synonyms: excuse, justify, vindicate|Antonyms: accuse, blame, incriminate|verb
