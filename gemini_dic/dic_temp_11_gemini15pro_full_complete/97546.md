ingratiatingly:/ɪnˈɡreɪʃɪeɪtɪŋli/
adverb
讨好地，谄媚地
In a manner intended to gain approval or favor; charmingly or flatteringly, often in an excessive or insincere way.
He smiled ingratiatingly at his boss, hoping to get a promotion. 他讨好地对老板笑着，希望能升职。
近义词:charmingly, flatteringly, obsequiously, fawningly
反义词:abrasively, offensively, rudely
词性:adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "ingratiate," which comes from the Latin "in" (into) + "gratia" (grace, favor).  The suffix "-ingly" transforms the verb into an adverb.
