improvised:/ˈɪmprəvaɪzd/ | adj. | 即兴的，临时的 | Done or made using whatever is available, without advance planning. |  The musicians improvised a bluesy tune.  音乐家们即兴演奏了一曲布鲁斯调。| synonyms:  ad-lib, extemporaneous, off-the-cuff, unrehearsed, makeshift, impromptu | antonyms: planned, prepared, rehearsed | adjective

improvise:/ˈɪmprəvaɪz/ | v. | 即兴创作，临时凑合 | Create and perform (music, drama, or verse) spontaneously or without preparation.  To make or do something using whatever is available. | He improvised a song on his guitar. 他用吉他即兴创作了一首歌。| synonyms:  ad-lib, extemporize, wing it, make do, concoct | antonyms: plan, prepare, rehearse | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *improviser*, from Italian *improvvisare*, from *improvviso* "unexpected, sudden," from Latin *in- + provisus*, past participle of *providēre* "to foresee."
