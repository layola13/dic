enthronization:/ɪnˌθroʊnɪˈzeɪʃən/ |n.| 登基；就任； enthronement|The enthronization of the new king was a grand ceremony. 新国王的登基典礼非常隆重。|Synonyms: inauguration, investiture, coronation |Antonyms: deposition, abdication| noun

enthrone:/ɪnˈθroʊn/ |vt.| 使登基；使就任； enthrone|The bishop was enthroned in the cathedral. 主教在大教堂就任。|Synonyms: install, inaugurate, invest |Antonyms: depose, dethrone| verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From en- + throne + -ization.  "Throne" comes from Old French "trone"  and ultimately from Latin "thronus" meaning "seat of state".  The prefix "en-" adds the sense of "putting onto".  "-ization" is a suffix forming nouns of action or process.
