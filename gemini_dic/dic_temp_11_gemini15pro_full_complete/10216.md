apostolic: /ˌæpəˈstɒlɪk/ (US) /ˌæpəˈstɑːlɪk/ (UK)| adj.|使徒的；使徒时代的；宗徒的|Relating to the apostles or their teachings; adhering to the doctrines or practices of the apostles.|例句：The apostolic church traces its origins back to the first apostles. 使徒教会将其起源追溯到第一批使徒。|近义词：evangelical, missionary|反义词：secular, worldly|词性:形容词

apostolic:/ˌæpəˈstɒlɪk/ (US) /ˌæpəˈstɑːlɪk/ (UK)| n.|使徒继承人; 主教|A bishop, especially one claiming a direct line of succession from the apostles.|例句：He claimed to be an apostolic, possessing special authority. 他声称自己是使徒继承人，拥有特殊权威。|近义词：bishop, patriarch|反义词：layperson, nonbeliever|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *apostolicus*, from Greek *ἀποστολικός* (apostolikos), from *ἀπόστολος* (apostolos, “apostle”), from *ἀποστέλλειν* (apostellein, “to send away”).
