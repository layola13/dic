counterfeiting:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪtɪŋ/
n.|伪造；仿造|The act of making something false or an imitation, especially money, documents, or goods, with the intent to deceive.|The counterfeiting of banknotes is a serious crime. 伪造钞票是严重的犯罪。|synonyms: forgery, imitation, faking, copying|antonyms: authenticity, genuineness|noun

counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/
n.|赝品；伪造品|A fraudulent imitation or copy of something valuable.|The police seized a large number of counterfeit handbags. 警方查获了大量假冒手袋。|synonyms: fake, forgery, sham, imitation|antonyms: genuine, authentic, real|noun
counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/
vt.|伪造；仿造|To make a fraudulent imitation or copy of something valuable.|He was arrested for counterfeiting money. 他因伪造货币而被捕。|synonyms: forge, fake, imitate, copy|antonyms: authenticate, verify|verb
counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/
adj.|伪造的；仿造的|Made in imitation of something else with intent to deceive.|The painting was discovered to be a counterfeit. 这幅画被发现是赝品。|synonyms: fake, forged, sham, imitation|antonyms: genuine, authentic, real|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French contrefait, past participle of contrefaire “to imitate, copy, forge,” from contre- “against” + faire “to make, do.”
