hoity-toity:/ˌhɔɪtiˈtɔɪti/
adjective
傲慢的，自负的
Behaving in a way that shows you think you are very important and better than other people.
例句：She gave me a hoity-toity look. 她傲慢地看了我一眼。
例句：He has a hoity-toity attitude. 他态度傲慢。
近义词：arrogant, haughty, snobbish, supercilious, condescending
反义词：humble, modest, down-to-earth
词性: adjective


hoity:/ˈhɔɪti/
adjective, noun, adverb (obsolete)
傲慢的; 傲慢; 傲慢地(已过时)
(Adjective)  Haughty or supercilious.  (Noun)  Haughty or supercilious behavior. (Adverb - obsolete)  In a haughty or supercilious manner.
例句: "Don't get hoity with me!"  “别对我这么傲慢！”
例句:  She showed a lot of hoity after she got the promotion. 她升职后表现得很傲慢。
近义词：(adj) arrogant, haughty, snobbish, supercilious, condescending, pompous
反义词：(adj) humble, modest, unassuming
词性: adjective, noun, adverb (obsolete)

词源:
From the obsolete adjective "hoit", meaning "frisky, wanton," probably from Middle Dutch "hoit" meaning "romp".  The reduplication "hoity-toity" emerged in the 19th century.
