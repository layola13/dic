levitators:/ˈlevɪteɪtərz/
n.
悬浮者；使悬浮的人或物
People or things that levitate (cause something to rise and float in the air, especially by means of supernatural power).
The levitators amazed the audience by seemingly making the objects float in mid-air.  悬浮者让观众惊叹，他们似乎能让物体漂浮在半空中。
Magicians are sometimes referred to as levitators, though their tricks are based on illusion. 魔术师有时被称为悬浮者，尽管他们的技巧是基于幻觉。
近义词:floaters
反义词:sinkers
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "levitate," which comes from the Latin "levitatus," the past participle of "levitare" (to lighten, lift up), from "levis" (light in weight).  源自动词“levitate”，该词来自拉丁语“levitatus”，是“levitare”（使变轻，举起）的过去分词，源自“levis”（重量轻）。
