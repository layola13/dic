nonpragmatics:/ˌnɒnprægˈmætɪks/ | n. | 非语用学 |  The study of language without consideration of its use in context, focusing solely on the literal meaning and grammatical structure.  This contrasts with pragmatics, which explores how context influences meaning. |  His analysis of the sentence was purely nonpragmatic, ignoring the speaker's intention and the social setting. 他对句子的分析纯粹是非语用学的，忽略了说话者的意图和社会背景。| Near synonyms: non-contextual linguistics, literal semantics | Antonyms: pragmatics | noun
