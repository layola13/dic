词源：
"Naik" originates from Malay and Indonesian, where it carries the primary meaning of "to go up," "ascend," or "rise."  It can also refer to mounting (e.g., a horse) or increasing in value or quantity.  It is related to other Austronesian languages.

While "naik" is commonly used as a verb and an adjective in Malay and Indonesian, its usage as an adjective is less frequent in English contexts.
