embarrass:/ɪmˈbærəs/ (US), /ɪmˈbærəs/ (UK)| vt.|使尴尬，使窘迫|To cause someone to feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed.|He was embarrassed by his child's bad behavior. 他对孩子的糟糕行为感到尴尬。|Synonyms: shame, humiliate, mortify, disconcert|Antonyms: comfort, reassure, encourage|Verb

embarrassed:/ɪmˈbærəst/ (US), /ɪmˈbærəst/ (UK)| adj.|尴尬的，窘迫的|Feeling awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed.|She felt embarrassed when she realized she had forgotten his name.  当她意识到自己忘记了他的名字时，她感到很尴尬。|Synonyms: ashamed, uncomfortable, self-conscious, mortified|Antonyms: comfortable, confident, composed|Adjective

embarrassment:/ɪmˈbærəsmənt/ (US), /ɪmˈbærəsmənt/ (UK)| n.|尴尬，窘迫|A feeling of awkwardness, self-consciousness, or shame.|Her embarrassment was evident as she blushed. 她的尴尬显而易见，因为她脸红了。|Synonyms: shame, humiliation, mortification, awkwardness|Antonyms: comfort, confidence, composure|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *embarrasser (verb), from en- "in" + barre "bar." Originally meant "to obstruct, impede," later evolving to "to perplex" and finally to "to make self-conscious, ashamed."
