anti-puritanisms:/ˌæntiˈpjʊrɪtənɪzəmz/| n. |反清教主义，对清教主义的反对|Opposition to or rejection of the beliefs and practices of Puritanism, often characterized by a focus on strict moral codes and religious discipline.|The rise of anti-puritanisms in the 17th century led to a greater emphasis on personal freedom and enjoyment.| 近义词：libertinism, licentiousness, worldliness | 反义词：Puritanism, piety, asceticism | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
anti- (prefix): from Greek *antí*, meaning "against," "opposite of"
Puritanism: from Puritan, a member of a group of English Protestants of the late 16th and 17th centuries who regarded the Reformation of the Church of England under Elizabeth I as incomplete and sought to simplify and regulate forms of worship.
-ism (suffix):  a suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine, etc., as in baptism, criticism, despotism.
