brochure:/broʊˈʃʊr/ (US), /ˈbrəʊʃə(r)/ (UK)| n. |小册子，宣传册|A small booklet or pamphlet, often containing advertising material or information about a product, service, or place.|Example: The travel agent gave me a brochure about vacation packages to Hawaii. 旅行社给了我一本关于夏威夷度假套餐的小册子。

Example:  I picked up a brochure for the new museum exhibit. 我拿了一份新博物馆展览的小册子。|Synonym: pamphlet, leaflet, booklet, flyer, handout|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but consider)  tome, volume (for size contrast)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *brochure*, from *brocher* "to stitch," from Old French *brochier*, based on *broche* "an awl, pointed tool."  Originally referred to a stitched work, evolving to mean a pamphlet.
