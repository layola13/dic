mugwort:/ˈmʌɡˌwɜːrt/ (US), /ˈmʌɡwɜːt/ (UK)| n. | 艾草；洋艾 |Aromatic plant of the daisy family, used in traditional medicine and sometimes in cooking.  It has silvery-green leaves and small yellowish flowers.| Mugwort tea is believed to have medicinal properties. 艾草茶被认为具有药用价值。| Artemisia vulgaris, common wormwood, felon herb | N/A | noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mucgwyrt, from mucg "midge" + wyrt "plant, herb".  So named because it was believed to repel insects.
