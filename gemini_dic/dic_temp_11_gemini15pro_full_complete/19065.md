betokening:/bɪˈtoʊkənɪŋ/
| adj. |预示的，象征的|Indicating or signifying something; being a sign of.|The dark clouds betokening rain. 乌云预示着要下雨。|Synonyms: presaging, foreshadowing, portending, auguring, indicating, signifying|Antonyms: concealing, hiding, masking|adjective

Etymology->
From betoken + -ing.  Betoken comes from Middle English *bitokenen*, from Old English *betācnian*  "to signify, indicate, show; demonstrate, prove; assign, allot," from *be-* "by" + *tācen* "sign, token."  The suffix *-ing* forms the present participle and gerund.
