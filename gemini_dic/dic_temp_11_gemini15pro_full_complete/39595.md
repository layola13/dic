consecrators:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪtəz/ | n. | 主教,祝圣者 | People who perform the act of consecration, especially of bishops.  They officially declare something sacred or dedicate it to a religious purpose. | The consecrators laid their hands upon the new bishop. 祝圣者们把手按在新主教身上。|The new cathedral was consecrated by the archbishop and his consecrators.  新主教座堂由大主教和他的祝圣者们祝圣。 | synonyms: ordainers, inaugurators | antonyms: desecrators, profaners | noun


consecrate: /ˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/ | v. | 祝圣，奉献 | To declare something sacred or dedicate it to a religious purpose; to devote something to a special purpose. | The church was consecrated in 1857. 这座教堂于1857年祝圣。| He consecrated his life to the study of medieval history. 他毕生致力于研究中世纪历史。| synonyms: hallow, sanctify, dedicate, devote | antonyms: desecrate, profane, defile | verb
