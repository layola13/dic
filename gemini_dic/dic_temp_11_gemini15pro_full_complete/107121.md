ladybug:|/ˈleɪdiˌbʌɡ/|n.|瓢虫|A small beetle, often brightly colored and spotted, that is believed to bring good luck. It feeds primarily on aphids and other plant-eating insects.|例句：A ladybug crawled across my hand.一只瓢虫爬过我的手。|近义词：ladybird, lady beetle|反义词：N/A|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English ladybryʒʒe, from Old English lēodiflȳ ("ladyfly"), where lēodig refers to Our Lady, the Virgin Mary. The beetle is said to have been dedicated to the Virgin Mary and is sometimes called Our Lady's bird or the beetle of Our Lady.
