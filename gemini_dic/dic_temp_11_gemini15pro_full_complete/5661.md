altocumulus:/ˌæltəʊˈkjuːmjʊləs/ | n. | 高积云 | A type of mid-level cloud characterized by white or gray patches or layered sheets, often with a rippled or dappled appearance.  They are formed by rising air and often indicate fair weather, although they can sometimes precede thunderstorms. |  Example: The altocumulus clouds drifted lazily across the sky.  高积云懒洋洋地飘过天空。| Example:  We saw beautiful altocumulus formations during our hike.  我们在徒步旅行时看到了美丽的高积云层。 | Synonym:  cloud, mackerel sky (when in a specific formation) | Antonym: clear sky | Noun
