cithern:/ˈsɪθərn/| n. |西塞琴，齐特琴|A stringed instrument of the lute family, with a flat-backed soundbox and a short neck, popular in Europe from the Renaissance to the 18th century.  It is typically played by plucking the strings with a plectrum.|He played a lively jig on the cithern. 他用西塞琴演奏了一首轻快的吉格舞曲。|Synonyms: cittern, gittern|Antonyms: None|Noun
