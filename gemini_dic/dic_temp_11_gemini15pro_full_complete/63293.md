enthusiasm:/ɪnˈθuːziæzəm/| n.|热情，热心，热忱|A feeling of energetic interest in a particular subject or activity and an eagerness to be involved in it.|She has a great enthusiasm for music. 她对音乐充满热情。|例句：He approached the task with enthusiasm. 他满怀热情地投入到这项任务中。|近义词：passion, zeal, fervor, eagerness|反义词：apathy, indifference, coldness|noun


enthusiast:/ɪnˈθuːziæst/| n.|热衷者，爱好者|A person who is very interested in and involved with a particular subject or activity.|He is a model train enthusiast. 他是一位火车模型爱好者。|例句：She is an enthusiast of classical music. 她是一位古典音乐爱好者。|近义词：fan, aficionado, devotee|反义词：critic, skeptic|noun


enthusiastic:/ɪnˈθuːziˈæstɪk/| adj.|热情的，热心的|Having or showing intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval.|He gave an enthusiastic welcome to the visitors. 他热情地欢迎来访者。|例句：She was enthusiastic about the project. 她对这个项目充满热情。|近义词：eager, ardent, passionate, zealous|反义词：apathetic, indifferent, unenthusiastic|adjective


enthusiastically:/ɪnˈθuːziˈæstɪkli/| adv.|热情地，热心地|In a way that shows intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval.|The audience applauded enthusiastically. 观众热情地鼓掌。|例句：He greeted me enthusiastically at the door. 他在门口热情地迎接我。|近义词：eagerly, ardently, passionately, zealously|反义词：apathetically, indifferently, unenthusiastically|adverb



ETYMOLOGY->
From French *enthousiasme*, from Late Latin *enthusiasmus*, from Greek *enthousiasmos* "divine inspiration, enthusiasm," from *enthousiazein* "be inspired or possessed by a god, be in ecstasy," from *en- "in" + *theos* "god".
