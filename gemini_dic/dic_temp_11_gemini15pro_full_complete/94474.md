impose:/ɪmˈpəʊz/ (UK) /ɪmˈpoʊz/ (US)| v. |强加；征税；利用；打扰|To force something, such as a rule, tax, or punishment, to be accepted or obeyed; to take unfair advantage of someone's kindness; to be noticeable in a way that is not wanted or necessary.|The government imposed a new tax on fuel. 政府对燃料征收了新的税。 |synonyms: enforce, levy, inflict, obtrude| antonyms: remove, abolish, lift, repeal |verb

impose:/ɪmˈpəʊz/ (UK) /ɪmˈpoʊz/ (US)| v. |施加；强迫；征(税)；加(负担)于|To establish or apply by authority; to force someone to accept something; to require someone to do something; to take advantage of someone by demanding their attention or commitment.|Don't impose your beliefs on others. 不要把你的信仰强加于人。 |synonyms: dictate, mandate, foist| antonyms: liberate, free, exempt |verb
