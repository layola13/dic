fanatically:/fəˈnætɪkli/ | adv. | 狂热地；极端地 | In a way that shows excessive enthusiasm and often unreasonable beliefs. | He worked fanatically, often pulling all-nighters to finish projects. 他工作狂热，经常熬通宵完成项目。| She fanatically collects antique dolls. 她狂热地收集古董娃娃。 | 近义词：obsessively, zealously, enthusiastically, fervently | 反义词：moderately, reasonably, calmly, apathetically | 词性:副词

fanatic:/fəˈnætɪk/ | n. | 狂热者；极端分子 | A person filled with excessive and single-minded zeal, especially for an extreme religious or political cause. | He's a fanatic about cleanliness. 他是个清洁狂。| The group was comprised of religious fanatics. 这个团体由宗教狂热分子组成。| 近义词：extremist, zealot, devotee | 反义词：moderate | 词性:名词

fanatical:/fəˈnætɪkl/ | adj. | 狂热的；极端入迷的 | Marked by excessive enthusiasm and often intense devotion. | He's a fanatical supporter of the local football team. 他是当地足球队的狂热支持者。|  She has a fanatical devotion to her work. 她对工作极其投入。 | 近义词：obsessive, zealous, enthusiastic, fervent | 反义词：moderate, reasonable, calm, apathetic | 词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fanatique* (now *fanatique*), from Latin *fanaticus* "inspired by a deity, frenzied, enthusiastic," from *fanum* "temple."  The original sense in English was "inspired by a god," but it gradually shifted to describe excessive and often misguided enthusiasm.
