old-world:/ˌoʊld ˈwɜːrld/| adj.|旧世界的，古色古香的，保守的|Characteristic of the Old World, especially Europe; traditional or conservative in style or manners.  It can refer to physical things (like furniture or architecture) or to attitudes and values.|The hotel had an old-world charm that I found very appealing. 这家酒店有一种我非常喜欢的旧世界魅力。|近义词：traditional, classic, antique, vintage, conservative|反义词：modern, contemporary, new-fangled, progressive|词性:形容词
Etymology: From "old" + "world". The term "Old World" geographically refers to the parts of the world known to Europeans before the discovery of the Americas, typically including Europe, Asia, and Africa.  This meaning extends metaphorically to describe styles, customs, and attitudes that originated in or are characteristic of these regions, often implying a sense of history, tradition, and established conventions.
