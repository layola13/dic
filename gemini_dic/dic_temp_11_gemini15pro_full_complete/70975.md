fireproofed:/ˈfaɪərpruːft/| adj.|防火的，耐火的|Treated or constructed with a material that makes it resistant to catching fire or burning.|The curtains were fireproofed to comply with safety regulations.  这些窗帘经过防火处理，符合安全规定。|Example:  All the materials used in building the library were fireproofed. 图书馆建筑中使用的所有材料都经过了防火处理。|Synonyms: fire-resistant, non-flammable|Antonyms: flammable, combustible|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
fire (n.) + proof (n.) + -ed (suffix)

fire (n.): from Old English *fyr, from Proto-Germanic *fūr- (source also of Old Saxon fiur, Old Frisian fiur, Old Norse fýrr, Middle Dutch vuer, Old High German fiur, German Feuer, Gothic funins "fire"), from PIE *pūr- (source also of Sanskrit pu-, Greek pyr, Latin pir, Old Irish ar "fire").

proof (n.): from Old French prueve "proof, evidence, test," from Latin proba "proof, test," related to probare "to test, prove good, approve" (see prove).

-ed (suffix): forms the past participle of regular verbs and many adjectives.
