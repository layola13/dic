chaha: /ˈtʃɑːhɑː/ | n. | 茶，奶茶 (Specifically a spiced milk tea beverage popular in South Asia) |  A beverage made by boiling black tea in milk and water with spices like cardamom, ginger, and cinnamon.  It's especially popular in India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh. |  I had a delicious cup of chaha this morning. 我今天早上喝了一杯美味的奶茶。 | Synonyms: chai, masala chai | Antonyms: None (It's a specific type of beverage, not a quality) | noun


chaha: /ˈtʃɑːhɑː/ | v. (past tense: chahed) | 想要,渴望 (This usage is less common and primarily found in certain dialects or poetic contexts. It's derived from the Hindi/Urdu verb "chahna" meaning "to want" or "to love")  | To want or desire something intensely. |  My heart chahed for her presence. 我的心渴望她的出现.  | Synonyms: desired, longed for, yearned for, craved | Antonyms: disliked, abhorred | verb
