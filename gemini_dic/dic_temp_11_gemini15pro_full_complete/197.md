abbess:/ˈæbɪs/| n.|女院长，女修道院院长|a woman who is the head of an abbey of nuns|The abbess welcomed the visitors to the abbey. 女院长欢迎游客来到修道院。|近义词：prioress, mother superior|反义词:monk (This is a loose antonym, focusing on gender and sometimes leadership within a religious order.)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *abbesse*, from Late Latin *abbatissa*, feminine form of *abbas* ("abbot"), from Greek *abbas* ("father," "monk," title of respect), from Aramaic *abba* ("father"). Ultimately from Proto-Semitic *ʼab* ("father").
