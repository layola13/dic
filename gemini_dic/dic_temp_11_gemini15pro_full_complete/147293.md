overmodest: /ˌoʊvərˈmɒdɪst/ (US) /ˌəʊvəˈmɒdɪst/ (UK)
|adj.| 过分谦虚的；过分谦逊的|Excessively modest; unduly humble.  Characterized by an exaggerated or excessive lack of self-assertion.  It implies a minimizing of one's own abilities or achievements to a degree that is considered unwarranted or insincere.|She was overmodest about her achievements, attributing her success to luck. 她过分谦虚地谈论自己的成就，把成功归因于运气。|
| |He gave an overmodest account of his contribution to the project. 他过分谦虚地讲述了他对这个项目的贡献。|
| |Synonyms: excessively modest, unduly humble, self-deprecating, unassuming|
| |Antonyms: boastful, arrogant, conceited, immodest|
| |word class: adjective|


Etymology:
over- (prefix meaning "excessive," "too much") + modest (from Latin modestus "moderate, keeping due measure," from modus "measure, manner")
