consolate:/ˈkɒnsəleɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsəleɪt/ (US)| v. |安慰，慰藉|To comfort someone who is sad or disappointed.|She tried to console him after his dog died. 她试图在他失去爱犬后安慰他。|例句：
The mother consoled her crying child. 母亲安慰她哭泣的孩子。
He tried to console her with a hug. 他试图用拥抱来安慰她。

|近义词：comfort, soothe, solace|反义词:distress, upset|词性:verb

consolation:/ˌkɒnsəˈleɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌkɑːnsəˈleɪʃn/ (US)| n. |安慰，慰藉|Something that provides comfort or solace in a time of grief or disappointment.|Her only consolation was that she still had her family. 她唯一的安慰是她还有家人。|例句：
The news brought little consolation. 这个消息并没有带来多少安慰。
His presence was a great consolation to her. 他的出现对她来说是莫大的安慰。

|近义词：comfort, solace, relief|反义词:aggravation, distress|词性:noun
