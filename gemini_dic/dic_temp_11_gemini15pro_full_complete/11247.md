archosaurs:/ˈɑːrkəˌsɔːrz/ | n. | 始龙类，祖龙类 |A group of diapsid amniotes whose living representatives consist of birds and crocodilians.  Archosaurs are characterized by features such as antorbital fenestrae (openings in the skull in front of the eyes) and a modified ankle joint.|Crocodiles and birds are archosaurs. 鳄鱼和鸟类都是始龙类。|Synonyms:  archosaurian (adjective form) | Antonyms:  None (no single antonym for a taxonomic group) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek  ἀρχός (arkhós, “leader, ruler”) + σαῦρος (saûros, “lizard”).
