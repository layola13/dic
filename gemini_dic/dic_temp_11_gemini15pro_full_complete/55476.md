distractability:/dɪˌstræktəˈbɪləti/| n. |分心；注意力分散|The state of being easily distracted.  It refers to the difficulty in maintaining focus and attention, often shifting between tasks or thoughts.|His distractability made it hard for him to study. 他的注意力容易分散，这使他很难学习。|Synonyms: inattentiveness, flightiness, short attention span|Antonyms: focus, concentration, attentiveness|noun

distractible:/dɪˈstræktəbl/| adj. |易分心的；注意力不集中的|Easily diverted; susceptible to distraction.|He's very distractible and can't work with the TV on. 他很容易分心，电视开着就没法工作。|Synonyms: inattentive, restless, fidgety|Antonyms: focused, attentive, concentrated|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *distractus (past participle of distrahere "to pull apart, divide") + -ibility.  Distrahere is formed from dis- "apart" + trahere "to draw, pull".
