federationist:/ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪst/
|n.|联邦主义者|A person who advocates or supports federation, especially a supporter of the federal principle of government.|例句：The federationist argued for a stronger central government. 这位联邦主义者主张建立一个更强大的中央政府。

|例句：She was a staunch federationist, believing in the power of unity. 她是一位坚定的联邦主义者，相信团结的力量。
|近义词：federalist, unionist|反义词：separatist, secessionist|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From federation + -ist.  Federation comes from Late Latin *foederatio, from Latin foederare "to make a treaty, league," from foedus "treaty, league, covenant."
