inkhornism:/ˈɪŋkˌhɔːrnɪzəm/| n.|迂腐的词语；矫揉造作的文风|An inkhornism is a word or expression that is unusual or obscure, often used to show off learning.  It refers to a style of writing that is overly elaborate and artificial, characterized by the use of such words.|例句：The writer's prose was filled with inkhornisms, making it difficult to understand.  这位作家的散文充满了迂腐的词语，使人难以理解。

例句：Using "perambulate" instead of "walk" is an example of an inkhornism.  用“perambulate”代替“walk”是迂腐词语的一个例子。|近义词：pedantry, affectation, bombast, purple prose|反义词:simplicity, naturalness, plainness|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "ink" + "horn" + "-ism."  Originally referred to an inkwell made from a horn, which became associated with scholarly pursuits.  The term evolved to denote the excessive use of learned or obscure words, often associated with those who carried such inkhorns.
