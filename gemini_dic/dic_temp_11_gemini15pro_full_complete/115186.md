manichees:/ˌmænɪˈkiːz/
noun
摩尼教徒; 绝对善恶二元论者
followers of Manichaeism, a religion founded by Mani in the 3rd century AD, characterized by a dualistic cosmology of light and darkness, good and evil.  Also used more broadly to refer to those who see the world in starkly black and white terms.
The Manichees believed in a cosmic struggle between good and evil. 摩尼教徒相信善恶之间的宇宙斗争。
The author criticizes politicians who take a Manichee view of international relations. 作者批评那些对国际关系持绝对善恶二元论观点的政治家。
Synonyms: dualists, absolutists
Antonyms: relativists, pragmatists
