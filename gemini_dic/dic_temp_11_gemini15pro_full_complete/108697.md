leal:/liːl/ | adj. | 忠诚的，忠实的 | Loyal and faithful; true in allegiance. |  He remained leal to the king even in exile.  他即使在流亡中也仍然忠于国王。 | Synonyms: faithful, loyal, constant, true, steadfast, devoted | Antonyms: disloyal, faithless, treacherous, perfidious | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *leal*, from Latin *lēgālis* "legal, lawful," from *lēx* "law."  The sense shifted from "lawful, according to law" to "faithful to one's oath or duty."
