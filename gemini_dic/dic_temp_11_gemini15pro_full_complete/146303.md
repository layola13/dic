overfearfully: /ˌoʊvərˈfɪrfəli/ | adv. | 过度害怕地，过分胆怯地 | Excessively fearfully; with too much fear.  This adverb describes actions or states performed with an exaggerated sense of fear. It combines "over-" (meaning "excessively") with "fearfully" (meaning "in a fearful manner"). |  He tiptoed overfearfully across the creaky floorboards. 他过度害怕地踮着脚尖走过嘎吱作响的地板。|Synonyms: timidly, apprehensively, nervously, cautiously | Antonyms: boldly, bravely, courageously, confidently | Word Type: Adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi, meaning "above, beyond, over") + "fearfully" (from "fear" (Old English fær, from Proto-Germanic *feraz, meaning "danger, sudden calamity") + "-ful" (Old English -ful, meaning "full of") + "-ly" (Old English -līċe, forming adverbs).
