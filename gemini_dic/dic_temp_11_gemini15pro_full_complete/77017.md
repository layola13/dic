gargil:/ˈɡɑːrɡɪl/ | n. |  滴水嘴兽，怪兽状的排水口 | A carved grotesque figure, usually of a human or animal, projecting from the gutter of a building and designed to throw rainwater clear of the walls. | The gargoyles on the cathedral stared down at the city below.  教堂上的滴水嘴兽俯视着下面的城市。| Synonym: grotesque, chimera | Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "decoration" in a broad sense) | Noun
