confrontations: /ˌkɑːnfrənˈteɪʃənz/ /ˌkɒnfrənˈteɪʃənz/
n.
对抗，冲突
a situation in which people or groups are in strong disagreement or opposition
The two countries are on the brink of armed confrontation. 这两个国家处于武装冲突的边缘。

confrontations: /ˌkɑːnfrənˈteɪʃənz/ /ˌkɒnfrənˈteɪʃənz/
n.
对峙，遭遇
a hostile or argumentative meeting or situation between opposing parties.
He got into a confrontation with the police. 他与警方发生了冲突。

confrontations: /ˌkɑːnfrənˈteɪʃənz/ /ˌkɒnfrənˈteɪʃənz/
n.
正视，面对
the act of confronting or being confronted.
Confrontation with the truth is often painful.  直面真相通常是痛苦的。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin confrontatio, from Latin confrontare "to compare, bring face to face," from com- "together" + frontem (nominative frons) "forehead, front."
