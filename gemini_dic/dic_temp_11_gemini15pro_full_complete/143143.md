outcavilled: /ˌaʊtkəˈvɪld/
| v. |驳倒，以诡辩胜过|To surpass or defeat in caviling; to raise more captious objections than.|He outcavilled his opponent in the debate, leaving them speechless. 他在辩论中驳倒了对手，让他们哑口无言。|Synonyms: outargue, refute, confute|Antonyms: concede, acquiesce|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + cavil.  "Out-" is a prefix meaning "to surpass or exceed." "Cavil" comes from the Old French "caviller" meaning "to mock, jest," ultimately from the Latin "cavillari"  meaning "to jeer, joke, quibble."
