humbling:/ˈhʌmblɪŋ/| adj.|令人谦卑的，使人感到卑微的|Making you feel aware of your shortcomings; causing you to feel less important or proud.|The experience of losing the competition was humbling.|Synonyms: chastening, humbling, mortifying|Antonyms: exalting, elevating, aggrandizing|adjective


humbling:/ˈhʌmblɪŋ/| n.|使人谦卑的经历|An experience that makes you feel humble.|Losing the election was a humbling experience for him.|Synonyms: humiliation, comedown|Antonyms: triumph, victory|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "humble,"  from Middle English *humblen*, from Old French *humilier* "to humiliate,"  from Late Latin *humiliare* "to humble," from Latin *humilis* "low, lowly, humble," literally "on the ground," from *humus* "ground, earth."
