lead-on:/ˌliːdˈɒn/| n.|欺骗；引诱；虚假的希望|A deceptive action or promise that encourages false hopes.|He gave her a lead-on about a promotion, but it never materialized. 他给了她一个关于升职的虚假承诺，但这从未实现。|例句：She accused him of giving her a lead-on about their relationship. 她指责他给了她关于他们关系的虚假希望。

例句：The job advertisement was a total lead-on; it didn't even exist. 这则招聘广告完全是骗人的；这个工作根本不存在。|近义词：deception, enticement, false hope, allurement|反义词：honesty, truthfulness|词性:名词
