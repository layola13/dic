d-day:/ˈdiː deɪ/
noun
D-日，决战日，关键时刻
The day on which a planned operation is to begin or a planned event is to take place.  Specifically, June 6, 1944, the day of the Allied invasion of Normandy in World War II.
The general refused to set a D-day for the attack. 将军拒绝为进攻设定一个D日。
The company's D-day for the product launch is next Monday. 公司产品发布的D日是下周一。
synonyms:deadline, due date, target date, zero hour
antonyms: N/A
