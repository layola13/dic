freemasonry: /ˈfriːˈmeɪsənri/ | n. | 共济会，兄弟会 |  A fraternal organization with elaborate rituals and a system of secret signs; the craft or principles of Freemasons.  | Example:  He joined freemasonry in his early thirties. 他三十出头就加入了共济会。

Example: The principles of freemasonry emphasize brotherhood and charity. 共济会的原则强调兄弟情谊和慈善。 | 近义词: fraternity, brotherhood, lodge | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps secrecy vs. openness) | 词性: noun

Etymology: From *free* + *mason* + *-ry*.  *Free* here originally meant having the freedom of a guild, referring to the medieval stonemasons' guilds from which some aspects of Freemasonry are thought to derive.  *Mason* refers to a stone worker.  *-ry* is a suffix forming nouns denoting a practice, art, or occupation.
