intolerancy:/ɪnˈtɑːlərəns/ |ɪnˈtɒlərəns/| n. |不容忍，偏狭|Lack of tolerance; unwillingness or refusal to tolerate or respect contrary opinions or beliefs, persons of different races, etc. |  She showed intolerance towards people of different religions. 她对不同宗教信仰的人表现出不容忍。| bigotry, prejudice, discrimination | tolerance, acceptance, open-mindedness | noun


intolerant:/ɪnˈtɑːlərənt/ |ɪnˈtɒlərənt/| adj. |不容忍的，偏狭的|Not tolerant of views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own. | He is intolerant of any opinion that differs from his. 他不容忍任何与他不同的意见。| biased, prejudiced, discriminatory | tolerant, accepting, open-minded | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From in- (not) + tolerant (bearing, enduring).  The word "tolerant" comes from the Latin "tolerare" meaning "to bear, endure."
