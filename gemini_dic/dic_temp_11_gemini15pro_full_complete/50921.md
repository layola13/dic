deshabille:/ˌdeɪzəˈbiːj/ /ˌdɛzəˈbiːj/ | n. |便服，家居服，穿着随便的状态|The state of being dressed casually or partially undressed, typically in nightclothes or lounging attire.  A loose, flowing garment for informal wear.|She answered the door in deshabille, clearly not expecting visitors. 她穿着家居服开了门，显然没想到会有客人来访。|近义词：negligee, undress, nightclothes |反义词:formal wear, full dress |词性:noun

词源:
From French déshabillé, past participle of déshabiller (“to undress”), from des- (prefix meaning "un-") + habiller (“to dress”). Ultimately from Frankish *habiliōn, from Proto-Germanic *habilianą (“to make able, equip”).
