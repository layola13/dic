mirthful:/ˈmɜːrθfəl/ (US), /ˈmɜːθfʊl/ (UK)| adj.|欢乐的，高兴的|Full of mirth; merry or amusing.|Her mirthful laughter filled the room. 她欢乐的笑声充满了整个房间。|Example: The party had a mirthful atmosphere. 派对的气氛很欢乐。|Synonyms: cheerful, jolly, merry, joyous, jovial|Antonyms: sad, gloomy, somber, morose|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mirthful, from mirth + -ful.  Mirth itself comes from Old English *myrgþ, meaning "joy, pleasure, happiness," related to Old High German *murgi (modern German *Munterkeit* "cheerfulness").  The suffix *-ful* comes from Old English *-full*, meaning "full of."
