nautilus:/ˈnɔːtɪləs/ (US) /ˈnɔːtɪləs/ (UK)| n. |鹦鹉螺|A cephalopod mollusk of the genus Nautilus, having a spiral, chambered shell, pearly on the inside, that contains gas which enables the animal to maintain buoyancy. |The nautilus is a living fossil. 鹦鹉螺是一种活化石。|Synonym: chambered nautilus|Antonym: None|noun

nautiluses:/ˈnɔːtɪləsiz/ (US) /ˈnɔːtɪləsɪz/ (UK)| n. |鹦鹉螺（复数）|Plural form of nautilus.  Multiple cephalopod mollusks of the genus Nautilus. |The nautiluses gracefully navigated the coral reef.  这些鹦鹉螺优雅地在珊瑚礁中航行。|Synonym: chambered nautiluses|Antonym: None|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *nautilus*, from Ancient Greek ναυτίλος (nautílos, “sailor, paper nautilus, nautilus”), from ναύτης (naútēs, “sailor”), from ναῦς (naûs, “ship”).
