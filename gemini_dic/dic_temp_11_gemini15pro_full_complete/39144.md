confusion's:/kənˈfjuːʒənz/| n.|困惑，混乱，混淆|This is the possessive form of "confusion," indicating ownership or belonging. It refers to something that belongs to or is associated with a state of confusion.|The confusion's source was a miscommunication. 这个混乱的根源是沟通不畅。|Synonyms: bewilderment's, disorientation's, perplexity's|Antonyms: clarity's, order's|noun (possessive)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: confusioun, from Old French confusion, from Latin confusio(n-), from confusus ‘poured together, mingled’, past participle of confundere (see confound).
