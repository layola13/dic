just-so:/ˈdʒʌst ˈsoʊ/ | adj. | 恰到好处的；整齐的；过于完美的；牵强的 | Appearing contrived or too perfect, often in a way that seems unrealistic or superficial.  Also, neat and tidy. Derived from Rudyard Kipling's "Just So Stories," which offered fanciful explanations for natural phenomena. | The room was decorated in a just-so manner, everything perfectly placed. 房间装饰得恰到好处，一切都摆放得井井有条。|  The explanation seemed a little just-so, too neat to be true. 这个解释似乎有点牵强，太完美了，不像是真的。 | 近义词: perfect, tidy, neat, contrived, artificial | 反义词: messy, disordered, chaotic, natural, realistic | 词性: 形容词

just-so:/ˈdʒʌst ˈsoʊ/ | adv. | 恰好如此；正是这样 | In precisely this manner; exactly so. |  "He wanted it done just so," she explained. “他希望事情就这么办，”她解释道。 | The story unfolded just so, with no surprises. 故事就这样展开了，没有任何惊喜。 | 近义词: exactly, precisely, perfectly | 反义词: differently, otherwise | 词性: 副词
