flicker:/ˈflɪkər/ (US) /ˈflɪkə(r)/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.|闪烁，摇曳，颤动|A flicker is a brief, unsteady light or flame.  As a verb, it means to burn or shine unsteadily, or to appear briefly.|The candle flickered in the wind.  蜡烛在风中摇曳。|synonyms: glimmer, shimmer, flutter, waver|antonyms: blaze, shine steadily|noun, verb

flicker:/ˈflɪkər/ (US) /ˈflɪkə(r)/ (UK)| n. vi. vt.|一闪而过，(感情) 瞬间显露|A flicker can also refer to a brief, temporary feeling or expression. As a verb, it means to appear briefly or to show a fleeting emotion.|A flicker of fear crossed her face.  她脸上闪过一丝恐惧。|synonyms: glimpse, flash, hint|antonyms: persist, remain|noun, verb
