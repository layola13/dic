distractedly: /dɪˈstræktɪdli/ | adv. | 心不在焉地，分心地 | In a manner that shows one's attention is not focused; absentmindedly. | She looked at me distractedly, her mind clearly elsewhere. 她心不在焉地看着我，显然心不在焉。| He tapped his pen distractedly against the table. 他心不在焉地用笔敲着桌子。| 近义词: absentmindedly, inattentively, vaguely | 反义词: attentively, carefully, focusedly | 词性: 副词

distract: /dɪˈstrækt/ | v. | 使分心，转移(注意力) | Prevent (someone) from giving full attention to something. | The noise from the street distracted me from my work. 街上的噪音让我无法专心工作。|  Don't distract her while she's driving. 她开车时不要打扰她。| 近义词: divert, sidetrack | 反义词: focus, concentrate | 词性: 动词

distracted: /dɪˈstræktɪd/ | adj. | 心烦意乱的，分心的 | Unable to concentrate because one's mind is preoccupied. | He seemed distracted and didn't hear what I said. 他似乎心不在焉，没听到我说的话。| She was too distracted by her own thoughts to notice what was happening. 她太专注于自己的想法，没有注意到发生了什么。| 近义词: preoccupied, inattentive | 反义词: attentive, focused | 词性: 形词


词源:  From Late Latin *distractus, past participle of distrahere (“to pull apart, divide, distract”), from dis- (“apart, away”) + trahere (“to draw, pull”).
