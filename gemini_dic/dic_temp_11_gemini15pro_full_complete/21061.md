blindfolding:/ˈblaɪndˌfoʊldɪŋ/| n. |蒙眼，眼罩|The act of covering someone's eyes with a cloth or bandage so they cannot see. |The magician started the trick by blindfolding the volunteer. 魔术师蒙住志愿者的眼睛开始了魔术。|近义词：covering, obscuring, veiling|反义词：uncovering, exposing|noun

blindfold:/ˈblaɪndˌfoʊld/| v. |蒙住…的眼睛|To cover someone's eyes with a cloth or bandage so they cannot see. |She blindfolded him and led him into the surprise party. 她蒙住他的眼睛，把他带到惊喜派对上。|近义词：cover, obscure, veil|反义词：uncover, expose|verb

blindfold:/ˈblaɪndˌfoʊld/| adj. |被蒙住眼睛的|Having one's eyes covered with a cloth or bandage so they cannot see. |The blindfold contestant had to guess the object by touch. 被蒙住眼睛的参赛者必须通过触摸来猜测物体。|近义词：covered, obscured, veiled|反义词：uncovered, exposed|adjective

词源:  From "blind" (unable to see) + "fold" (a layer of something).  Essentially, a "fold" over the eyes that makes one "blind."
