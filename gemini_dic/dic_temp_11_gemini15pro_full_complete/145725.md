overcredulously: /ˌoʊvərkrɪˈdʒuːləsli/ | adv. | 过于轻信地 | In a manner that is too ready to believe things, especially things that are unlikely to be true. |  He overcredulously accepted the stranger's story. 他过于轻信地接受了陌生人的故事。| Example: She overcredulously believed the email promising her a million dollars. 她过于轻信地相信了那封承诺给她一百万美元的电子邮件。| Synonyms: naively, gullibly, trustingly | Antonyms: skeptically, cynically, suspiciously | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From "over" + "credulous" + "-ly".  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively," "credulous" comes from the Latin "credulus" (believing, trusting), and "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs.
