crowned:/kraʊnd/ | adj. | 戴着王冠的；加冕的；顶部的；圆满完成的 | Having a crown or garland on the head; having achieved a culminating success or accolade. |  The newly crowned king addressed his people. 新加冕的国王向他的人民讲话。| Synonyms:  coroneted, laureate, capped, topped, completed, consummated | Antonyms: uncrowned, bareheaded, incomplete | adjective

crowned:/kraʊnd/ | v. | 为…加冕；使圆满完成；表彰；居…之首 | To place a crown on the head of; to invest with regal dignity and power; to bring to a successful conclusion; to recognize achievement. |  His efforts were crowned with success. 他的努力获得了圆满成功。 | Synonyms: cap, top, complete, consummate, reward, honor | Antonyms: fail, thwart, obstruct | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *crouned*, from Old French *coroner*, from Latin *coronāre*, from *corona* ("crown, garland").
