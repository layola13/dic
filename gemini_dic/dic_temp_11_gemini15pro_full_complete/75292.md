frontier:/ˈfrʌntɪər/ (US) /ˈfrʌnˌtɪər/ (UK)| n. adj.|边界，前沿，国境|a border between two countries, or (especially in the past) a defended area along this border|The settlers pushed further into the frontier. 移民者进一步深入边疆。|近义词：border, boundary, edge|反义词：interior, center|词性:名词

frontier:/ˈfrʌntɪər/ (US) /ˈfrʌnˌtɪər/ (UK)| adj.|前沿的，开拓性的|relating to or characteristic of a frontier; pioneering|Scientists are exploring the frontiers of medicine. 科学家们正在探索医学的前沿。|近义词：pioneering, cutting-edge, advanced|反义词：established, traditional, conventional|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *frontiere "frontier, front" (13c.), from frontiere (adj.) "of or pertaining to the front," from front "forehead, front" (see front (n.)). Originally in a military sense, "front line of an army."
