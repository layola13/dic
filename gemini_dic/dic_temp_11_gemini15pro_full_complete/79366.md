glent:/ɡlɛnt/| n.|峡谷；狭窄的山谷|A deep, narrow valley, especially in Scotland or Ireland.|The hikers followed the stream down the glent.|近义词：ravine, gorge, canyon|反义词：mountain, hill|词性:名词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Scottish Gaelic *gleinn*, from Old Irish *glenn* (“valley, glen”), from Proto-Celtic *glennos*. Cognate with Welsh *glyn* (“valley”), Breton *gleann* (“valley”), Cornish *glyn* (“valley”), Manx *glion* (“valley”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰelH- (“to shine, gleam; green, yellow”).
