asperse:/əˈspɜːrs/ |v. | 诽谤，中伤，污蔑 | To falsely or maliciously attack the reputation of (someone) by spreading negative and often untrue statements. | The politician aspersed his opponent's character during the debate.  这位政治家在辩论中诋毁了对手的品格。|  slander, defame, vilify, calumniate, denigrate | defend, praise, extol | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *aspersus, past participle of aspergere "to sprinkle, scatter," from ad- "to, toward" + spargere "to scatter."  The original meaning referred to sprinkling with holy water, but later evolved to mean sprinkling with something negative, hence the modern sense of "defame."
