noncomplicities:/ˌnɒnkəmˈplɪsɪtiz/ | n. | 不共谋，不串通，不连累 |  The state of not being complicit or involved in wrongdoing; lack of involvement in a crime or harmful act. |  His noncomplicity in the scandal was eventually proven. 他最终被证明没有参与这起丑闻。 | 近义词：innocence, non-involvement, neutrality | 反义词：complicity, collusion, involvement | 词性：名词

词源:
non- (prefix):  Meaning "not," from Latin *non.
complicity (noun): From late Middle English complicité, from late Latin complicitās, from complicit- ‘involved as an accomplice,’ from the verb complicāre.
