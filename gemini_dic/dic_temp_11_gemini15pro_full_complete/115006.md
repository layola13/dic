mandated:/ˈmæn.deɪ.tɪd/
| adj. | 受委托的；强制的 | Officially required (by law, rule, or regulation). |  The government mandated the use of seatbelts. 政府强制要求使用安全带。 | required, compulsory, obligatory | optional, voluntary | adjective

mandate:/ˈmæn.deɪt/
| n. | 授权；命令 | An official order or commission to do something. | The election victory gave the party a clear mandate to govern. 选举的胜利给了该党一个明确的执政授权。 | order, command, instruction | prohibition | noun
| vt. | 授权；命令 | To officially require (something). | The law mandates that all children attend school. 法律规定所有儿童必须上学。 | require, order, direct | forbid | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *mandatum* "something entrusted to one's care," noun use of neuter past participle of *mandare* "to entrust, order," from *manus* "hand" + *dare* "to give."
