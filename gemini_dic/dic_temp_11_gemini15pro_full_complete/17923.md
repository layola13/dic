beeswing:/ˈbiːzˌwɪŋ/| n.|酒渣，酒石，（尤指波尔多葡萄酒中的）悬浮微粒|Small, flaky particles of crystallized tartaric acid and other substances, especially in port and other wines, often considered a sign of age and quality.  These particles sink to the bottom of the bottle or remain suspended in the wine.|The old port had beautiful beeswings.|Synonyms: sediment, deposit, lees|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, perhaps "clarity" in referring to the wine itself)|Noun
