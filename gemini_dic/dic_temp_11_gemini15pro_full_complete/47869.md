deceives:/dɪˈsiːvz/| v.|欺骗，蒙骗|to cause someone to believe something that is not true, typically in order to gain some personal advantage.|He deceived his parents about his grades. 他在成绩上欺骗了他的父母。|misleads, tricks, dupes|tells the truth, is honest|verb

deceiving:/dɪˈsiːvɪŋ/| adj.|欺骗性的，骗人的|giving a false impression; misleading.|Appearances can be deceiving. 外表可能是骗人的。|misleading, deceptive, fallacious|truthful, honest, genuine|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *decevoir*, from Latin *dēcipere* "to ensnare, entrap, beguile, cheat, deceive," from *dē-* "down, away" + *capere* "to take."
