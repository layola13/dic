anaphora:/əˈnæfərə/ | n. | 指代；回指；照应|The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses. |Example: "**We shall** fight on the beaches, **we shall** fight on the landing grounds, **we shall** fight in the fields and in the streets, **we shall** fight in the hills; we shall never surrender." (Winston Churchill)  我们将在海滩上作战，我们将在登陆场作战，我们将在田野和街道上作战，我们将在山岭作战；我们决不投降。 |Synonym: repetition, reiteration |Antonym: cataphora |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin anaphora, from Greek anaphorā  "a carrying back,"  from anapherein "to carry back," from ana- "back" + pherein "to carry."
