lullabying:/ˈlʌləˌbaɪɪŋ/
v.
哼唱摇篮曲；哄，抚慰
soothing or calming by or as if by a lullaby
The mother was lullabying her baby to sleep. 母亲正哼着摇篮曲哄她的宝宝睡觉。
The gentle rocking of the train lullabied me to sleep. 火车的轻柔摇晃让我昏昏欲睡。
近义词: soothing, calming, comforting, quieting
反义词: agitating, disturbing, exciting, rousing
词性: 动词 (present participle)

词源: From lullaby + -ing.  "Lullaby" itself comes from the Middle English "lullaby" and "lullay," probably imitative of soothing sounds, perhaps influenced by "lull" (to calm) and "bye" (an archaic word used to soothe children).
