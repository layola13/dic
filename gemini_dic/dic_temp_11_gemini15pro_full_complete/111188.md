lithographing:/lɪˈθɒɡrɑːfɪŋ/
n.|平版印刷术|The process of printing from a flat stone or metal plate, on which the image areas are chemically treated to retain ink while the non-image areas are treated to repel ink.  |Lithographing was a popular printing technique in the 19th century.  19世纪，平版印刷是一种流行的印刷技术。| 近义词: offset printing, planographic printing | 反义词: intaglio printing, relief printing
词性:名词/动名词

lithograph:/ˈlɪθəɡrɑːf/
n.|平版印刷品|A print made by lithography. | The museum has a collection of rare lithographs. 这家博物馆收藏了一些珍稀的平版印刷品。|近义词: print, artwork | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, could consider digital art as a modern contrast)
词性:名词

lithograph:/ˈlɪθəɡrɑːf/
vt.|平版印刷|To print by lithography. | The artist lithographed his own designs. 这位艺术家用平版印刷技术印制了自己的设计。|近义词: print, reproduce | 反义词: (No direct antonym, but could consider etch or engrave as different printing methods)
词性:动词


词源:  From French *lithographie*, from Ancient Greek *λίθος (líthos, “stone”) + -graphie*  from *γράφειν (gráphein, “to write”)*.
