haematometer:/ˌhiːməˈtɒmɪtər/ (US) /ˌhiːməˈtɒmɪtər/ (UK)| n. |血细胞计数器；血色计|An instrument for measuring the hemoglobin content of the blood. |The doctor used a haematometer to assess the patient's red blood cell count.|Synonyms: hemoglobinometer, hemometer |Antonyms: None|noun

haemocytometer:/ˌhiːməsaɪˈtɒmɪtər/ (US) /ˌhiːməsaɪˈtɒmɪtər/ (UK)| n. |血细胞计数器|A device used to count blood cells. |The lab technician used a haemocytometer to determine the white blood cell count.|Synonyms: hemocytometer, cell counter |Antonyms: None|noun


(Note:  While *haematometer* specifically refers to measuring hemoglobin, *haemocytometer* is more commonly used for counting blood cells generally.  The spelling *hemometer* and *hemocytometer* are more common in American English.)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *haima* (blood) + *metron* (measure)
