calms:/kɑːmz/ /kɑːlmz/ | n. | 平靜，無風狀態 |A state of quiet and peacefulness; absence of wind or rough water. |The lake was glassy smooth in the early morning calms. 清晨，湖面在平静无风的状态下如玻璃般光滑。|  The captain waited for the calms before setting sail. 船长等到风平浪静后再启航。 | synonyms: peace, tranquility, stillness, serenity | antonyms: storm, tempest, turbulence, commotion | noun

calm:/kɑːm/ /kɑːlm/ | v. | 使平静，使镇定 | To make quiet and peaceful; to soothe or pacify. | The mother calmed her crying baby. 母亲使她哭闹的婴儿平静下来。|  Deep breaths calmed his racing heart. 深呼吸使他狂跳的心平静下来。 | synonyms: soothe, pacify, tranquilize, allay | antonyms: agitate, excite, provoke, inflame | verb

calm:/kɑːm/ /kɑːlm/ | adj. | 平静的，镇定的 |  Not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions; (of weather) not windy or stormy. | The sea was calm after the storm. 暴风雨过后，大海平静了下来。|  She remained calm during the emergency. 在紧急情况下，她保持镇定。 | synonyms: peaceful, tranquil, serene, placid | antonyms: agitated, turbulent, stormy, frantic | adjective


Etymology:
From Middle English *calmen, *camen, from Old Norse kalmr (“quiet, still”), from Proto-Germanic *kalmaz (“dull, insensible”). Cognate with Swedish kalm (“calm”), Danish kalm (“calm”).
