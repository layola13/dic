ok:/oʊˈkeɪ/ (US), /əʊˈkeɪ/ (UK)| adj. adv. interj. n.|好的，可以，行|An informal way of expressing agreement, acceptance, or approval.|例句：Ok, I'll do it. 好的，我会做的。|近义词：all right, fine, good, alright|反义词: no, bad, terrible|词性: adjective, adverb, interjection, noun

Etymology:  The origin of "OK" is uncertain, but the most widely accepted theory is that it originated as an abbreviation of "oll korrect," a humorous misspelling of "all correct" popular in the 1830s.
