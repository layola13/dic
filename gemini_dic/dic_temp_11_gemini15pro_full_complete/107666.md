languor:/ˈlæŋɡər/ (US), /ˈlæŋɡə(r)/ (UK)| n.|倦怠，无力，懒散|A pleasant feeling of tiredness or inertia; listlessness.|例句：She felt a pleasant languor steal over her after the long walk.  长时间散步后，她感到一阵惬意的倦怠感袭来。

例句：The afternoon heat induced a languor that made it hard to concentrate. 下午的酷热引起了一种倦怠感，使人难以集中精力。|近义词：lethargy, lassitude, torpor, inertia, listlessness|反义词：energy, vigor, vitality|词性：名词


languors:/ˈlæŋɡərz/ (US), /ˈlæŋɡəz/ (UK)| n.|倦怠，无力，懒散（复数）|Plural form of languor, referring to multiple instances of the feeling of tiredness or inertia.|例句：The summer languors made it difficult to get anything done. 夏日的倦怠使得很难完成任何事情。

例句：He succumbed to the languors of the afternoon and fell asleep. 他屈服于下午的倦怠感，睡着了。|近义词：lethargies, lassitudes, torpors, inertias, listlessnesses|反义词：energies, vigors, vitalities|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *langour*, from Late Latin *languor*, from Latin *languēre* "to be faint, languid," related to *laxus* "loose, slack."
