marsupialia:/mɑːrˌsuːpiˈeɪliə/ | n. |有袋目动物|A taxonomic order of mammals characterized by the presence of a pouch (marsupium) in females, in which the young are carried and nourished.|Kangaroos and koalas are marsupials. 袋鼠和考拉都是有袋动物。|Synonyms: Metatheria (a slightly broader taxonomic group)|Antonyms: None (There's no single antonym for a taxonomic group.  One could contrast them with placental mammals (Eutheria).)|noun
