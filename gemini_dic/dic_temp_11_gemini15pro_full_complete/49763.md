demolition:/ˌdeməˈlɪʃn/ | n. |拆除，摧毁；破坏|The act of demolishing something, especially a building. |The demolition of the old factory made way for a new shopping mall.  旧工厂的拆除为新购物中心的建设腾出了空间。|synonyms: destruction, ruin, razing |antonyms: construction, building, erection |noun

demolition:/ˌdeməˈlɪʃn/ | n. |拆毁的废料（残骸）|The debris or wreckage resulting from demolition. |The demolition was piled high on the site.  拆毁的废料堆积如山。|synonyms: debris, rubble, wreckage |antonyms: N/A |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: from Old French demolition, from Latin demolitio(n-), from the verb demoliri, from de- ‘down’ + moliri ‘to build’ (from moles ‘mass, structure’).
