eviscerate: /ɪˈvɪsəreɪt/ /iˈvɪsəˌret/ | vt. |取出内脏；除去主要部分；掏空；重创|To remove the inner organs of a body, especially those of the abdominal cavity. To deprive something of its essential part. To weaken or damage something severely, as if by removing its vital parts.  |The surgeon eviscerated the fish before grilling it. 外科医生在烤鱼之前先把鱼的内脏掏空了。

The critic's scathing review eviscerated the author's reputation. 评论家尖刻的评论彻底摧毁了作者的名声。 |近义词：disembowel, gut, remove, extirpate, devastate, ruin |反义词: fill, restore, strengthen |词性: 动词


evisceration: /ɪˌvɪsəˈreɪʃn/ /iˌvɪsəˈreʃən/ | n. |取出内脏；除去主要部分；掏空 |The act of removing the inner organs of a body. The act of depriving something of its essential part.  |The evisceration of the animal was performed quickly and efficiently. 对动物的内脏清除进行得快速而有效。

The evisceration of the town's economy was caused by the closure of the factory. 工厂的关闭导致了该镇经济的崩溃。|近义词：disembowelment, gutting, removal, extirpation, devastation |反义词: restoration, strengthening |词性: 名词


词源:  From Latin *evisceratus*, past participle of *eviscerare*  "to disembowel," from *ex-* "out" (see ex-) + *viscera* "internal organs" (see viscera).
