meiji:/ˈmeɪdʒiː/| n. |明治|The Meiji period (明治時代, Meiji jidai) was an era of Japanese history which extended from October 23, 1868 to July 30, 1912.  It represents the first half of the Empire of Japan during which Japanese society moved from being an isolated feudal society to its modern form. Fundamental changes affected its social structure, internal politics, economy, military, and foreign relations.|The Meiji Restoration modernized Japan. 明治维新使日本现代化。|Synonyms: Meiji era, Meiji Restoration|Antonyms: N/A|noun
