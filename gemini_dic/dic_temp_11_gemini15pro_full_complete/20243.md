birlaw:/ˈbɪrlɔː/| n.|乡规民约；村民自治法规|A system of local customary law, especially in Scotland, regulating land use and other matters within a community.|例句：The birlaw of the village dictated that everyone shared the common grazing land. 村里的乡规民约规定每个人共享公共牧场。

例句： Disputes over land boundaries were often settled according to birlaw. 土地边界纠纷通常根据乡规民约解决。|近义词：custom, local law, regulation|反义词:national law, statute|词性:名词

Etymology: From Scots *birlaw*, from Old Norse *býjarlǫg* "town law," from *býr* "town, farm" + *lǫg* "law."
