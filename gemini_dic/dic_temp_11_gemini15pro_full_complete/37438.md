commiserable:/ˈmɪzərəbl/ (US) /ˈmɪzrəbl/ (UK)| adj.|悲惨的，可怜的|In a very unhappy or unfortunate state.|例句：The refugees were living in commiserable conditions. 难民们生活在悲惨的境况中。|近义词：wretched, pathetic, miserable, deplorable|反义词：happy, cheerful, comfortable, fortunate|词性:形容词

commiserable:/ˈmɪzərəbl/ (US) /ˈmɪzrəbl/ (UK)| adj.|令人难受的，糟糕的|Unpleasant and causing discomfort.|例句：The weather was commiserable, cold and rainy. 天气很糟糕，又冷又下雨。|近义词：awful, terrible, dreadful|反义词：pleasant, agreeable, enjoyable|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: from Old French, from late Latin *commiserabilis, from Latin commiserari ‘bewail, pity’, from com- (expressing intensive force) + miserari (from miser ‘wretched’).
