foretoken:/ˈfɔːrˌtoʊkən/ | n. | 预兆，征兆 |A sign or warning of something to come. | The dark clouds were a foretoken of the coming storm.  乌云是暴风雨来临的预兆。| synonyms: omen, portent, presage, harbinger | antonyms: aftermath, consequence, result | noun

foretoken:/fɔːrˈtoʊkən/ | v. | 预示，预兆 |To be a sign or warning of (something to come). |  The raven's appearance foretokened doom. 渡鸦的出现预示着厄运。| synonyms: presage, portend, augur, foreshadow | antonyms:  N/A (Verbs don't typically have antonyms in the same way nouns do) | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fortokenen*, from Old English *foretacn*, from *fore-* (“before”) + *tacen* (“sign, token”).  Cognate with Old Frisian *foretāken* (“foretoken”), Old Saxon *foratecan* (“sign, miracle”), Old High German *furzeichen* (“foretoken”), German *Vorzeichen* (“omen, sign”).
