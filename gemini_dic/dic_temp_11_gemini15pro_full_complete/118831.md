metaphor:/ˈmɛtəfɔːr/ (US), /ˈmɛtəfə(r)/ (UK)| n. | 比喻，隐喻 |A figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable.  A thing regarded as representative or symbolic of something else, especially something abstract. |例句： "The world is a stage" is a common metaphor. “世界是一个舞台”是一个常见的比喻。|近义词：simile, analogy, figure of speech|反义词: literal meaning, plain speech|词性:noun
