distruster:/dɪˈstrʌstər/
n.|不信任者，怀疑者|A person who distrusts or has no confidence in someone or something.|He was a distruster of all politicians. 他不信任所有的政客。|近义词：skeptic, doubter, cynic|反义词：believer, truster|词性:名词

Etymology:
From distrust + -er. Distrust comes from the Old French *destruster (unattested), from des- "dis-" + truster "to trust," from Latin *trūstāre (unattested), from trūstus "protected, safe," past participle of trūdere "to thrust, push."
