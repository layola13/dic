glorifiers:/ˈɡlɔːrɪfaɪərz/ | n. | 颂扬者，赞美者 | People who glorify something or someone; those who praise or extol. | The king's glorifiers filled the court with songs of his greatness. 国王的颂扬者们在宫廷里唱满了歌颂他伟大的歌曲。

The glorifiers of violence in movies often ignore the real-life consequences.  电影中暴力行为的颂扬者常常忽视现实生活中的后果。 | 近义词：praisers, extollers, admirers, eulogizers | 反义词：critics, detractors, denouncers | 词性:Noun


glorify:/ˈɡlɔːrɪfaɪ/ | vt. | 颂扬，赞美，美化 | To describe or represent something as admirable, especially excessively or undeservedly. |  They glorified their leader as a wise and courageous hero.  他们把他们的领袖颂扬成一位英明勇敢的英雄。


 The movie glorifies violence and makes it seem glamorous. 这部电影美化了暴力，使其看起来很迷人。 | 近义词：praise, extol, laud, celebrate, commend | 反义词：criticize, condemn, denounce, disparage | 词性:Verb
