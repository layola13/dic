anticonfederationism:/ˌænti.kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪzəm/ | n. | 反联邦主义 | Opposition to the creation of a strong central government, specifically in the context of the United States Constitution ratification debates (1787-1788).  Anticonfederalists favored states' rights and feared that a powerful federal government would infringe upon individual liberties. | Example: Anticonfederalism played a significant role in the addition of the Bill of Rights to the US Constitution.  反联邦主义在美国宪法权利法案的加入中扮演了重要的角色。

Example:  Historians often debate the motivations behind anticonfederalism. 历史学家经常争论反联邦主义背后的动机。 | 近义词: states' rights advocacy, decentralization | 反义词: federalism, centralization | 词性: noun
