echeloning:/ˈeʃəlɒnɪŋ/ | n. |梯次配置；梯次推进；交错进行| the act of arranging or deploying troops, vehicles, aircraft, or other units in an echelon formation.  A staggered arrangement, especially of troops or ships, to allow each unit an unobstructed line of fire. |The general planned an echeloning of his troops to outflank the enemy. 将军计划梯次配置他的部队以包抄敌人。| synonyms: staggering, layering, phasing, overlapping | antonyms: concentrating, massing | noun

echelon:/ˈeʃəlɒn/ | n. | 梯队；等级；阶层 | a level or rank in an organization, profession, or society. A formation of troops, ships, aircraft, or vehicles in a staggered arrangement. |The upper echelons of management make all the important decisions.  高级管理层做出所有重要决定。| synonyms: level, rank, tier, grade, stratum | antonyms:  none | noun
