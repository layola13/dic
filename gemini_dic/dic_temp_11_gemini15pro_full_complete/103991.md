juggles:/ˈdʒʌɡlz/ | v. (third-person singular simple present juggles, present participle juggling, simple past and past participle juggled) | 杂耍；尽力同时应付（多项工作或活动） |  keeps several objects in motion in the air at the same time by repeatedly throwing and catching them, typically for entertainment. /  tries to do several things at the same time. | He juggles three balls at once. 他一次耍三个球。


She juggles a career and family life. 她兼顾事业和家庭生活。| synonyms: handles, manages, balances, manipulates | antonyms: neglects, abandons, drops, fails | verb

juggler:/ˈdʒʌɡlər/ | n. | 杂耍演员；玩杂耍的人；变戏法的人 | a person who juggles objects, typically for entertainment. / someone who is skilled at keeping several activities, processes, or people in progress or under control at the same time. | The juggler amazed the audience with his skill. 杂耍演员以其精湛的技艺令观众惊叹。 | synonyms: performer, artist, entertainer, manipulator | antonyms: audience, spectator, observer | noun


juggling:/ˈdʒʌɡlɪŋ/ | n. | 杂耍；变戏法 | the action of juggling objects. / the continuous manipulation of several activities or processes at the same time. |  He's skilled at juggling. 他擅长杂耍。 | synonyms: manipulation, handling, balancing | antonyms: inaction, stillness, dropping | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *jogler, from Vulgar Latin *joculārī, frequentative of Latin joculari (“to jest”), from jocus (“jest, joke”).
