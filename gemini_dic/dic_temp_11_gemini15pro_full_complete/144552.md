outwit:/ˌaʊtˈwɪt/ | vt. | 智胜，瞒骗 | To defeat or trick (someone) by being more clever. | He outwitted his opponents by a clever strategy. 他以巧妙的策略智胜了对手。|  She outwitted the police and escaped. 她智胜了警察，逃跑了。| synonyms: outsmart, outfox, outmaneuver | antonyms: be outwitted, be fooled, be tricked | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + wit.  "Out-" acts as a prefix meaning to surpass or exceed. "Wit" refers to intelligence or cleverness. Therefore, "outwit" literally means to surpass someone in wit or cleverness.
