murmurings:/ˈmɜːrmərɪŋz/ | n. | 窃窃私语，低语，怨言 | a low, continuous, indistinct sound, typically made by a group of people speaking quietly or by something like leaves rustling in the wind; also, expressions of discontent or complaint | The murmurings of the crowd grew louder as the speaker stumbled over his words.  人群的低语声随着演讲者结结巴巴的讲话而越来越大。 | synonyms: whispers, mutterings, grumbles, complaints | antonyms: shouts, yells, proclamations | noun

murmur:/ˈmɜːrmər/ | v. | 低语，小声说，发出连续而低沉的声音 | to speak or say something very quietly | He murmured an apology before leaving the room. 他离开房间前低声说了句道歉。 | synonyms: whisper, mutter, mumble | antonyms: shout, yell, proclaim | verb

murmur:/ˈmɜːrmər/ | n. | 低语，低沉的声音 | a low, continuous, indistinct sound | The murmur of the stream was soothing.  小溪的潺潺声令人舒缓。 | synonyms: whisper, mutter, mumble | antonyms: shout, yell, roar | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *murmurer*, from Latin *murmurāre* "to murmur, roar, grumble".
