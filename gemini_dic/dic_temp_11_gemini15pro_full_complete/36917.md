colorlessnesses:/ˈkʌlərləsnəsəz/
noun
无色；缺乏色彩；平淡无奇
The state or quality of being without color; lack of vibrancy or interest.
The colorlessnesses of the winter landscape made her long for spring. 冬日景色的单调乏味让她渴望春天。
近义词: drabness, dullness, monotony, blandness
反义词: vibrancy, colorfulness, brightness, vividness
词性: noun

Etymology:
From colorless + -ness (suffix forming nouns of quality or state) + -es (plural suffix).  "Colorless" itself comes from "color" + "-less" (suffix indicating absence or lack).  The ultimate root, "color," traces back to the Latin "color" and Old French "colur," with cognates in many Indo-European languages.
