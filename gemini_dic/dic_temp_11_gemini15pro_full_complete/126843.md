naphthoquinone:/ˌnæfθəˈkwɪnoʊn/ | n. |萘醌|An organic compound derived from naphthalene, with two carbonyl groups.  A class of organic compounds structurally related to naphthalene.  Many naturally occurring naphthoquinones have important biological properties. | Vitamin K is a type of naphthoquinone.  维生素K是一种萘醌。| synonyms: none (highly specific chemical term) | antonyms: none (highly specific chemical term) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From naphthalene + quinone. Naphthalene from Latin *naphtha (from Greek νάφθα (náphtha, “petroleum”)) + -lene (indicating a hydrocarbon). Quinone from Latin *quin(a) (from Spanish quina, "cinchona bark") + -one (indicating a ketone).
