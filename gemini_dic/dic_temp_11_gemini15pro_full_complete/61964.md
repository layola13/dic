emus:/ˈiːmjuːz/| n.|鸸鹋|Large, flightless Australian birds similar to ostriches.  They are the second-largest living bird by height, after its ratite relative, the ostrich.|We saw a mob of emus while driving through the outback. 我们开车穿过内陆地区时看到了一群鸸鹋。|Near synonyms: ostriches, rheas, cassowaries|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for an animal)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Portuguese *ema*, from Old Provençal *ema*. Ultimately from Arabic *naʿāma*, ostrich.  The application to the emu is due to confusion with the rhea, formerly called *ema* by the Portuguese in Brazil.
