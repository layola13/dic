consciousnesses:/ˈkɒnʃəsnəsɪz/ /ˈkɑːnʃəsnəsɪz/| n.|意识，知觉，觉悟|The state of being aware of and responsive to one's surroundings.|He regained consciousness after the accident. 他在事故后恢复了意识。|Synonyms: awareness, sentience, alertness|Antonyms: unconsciousness, insensibility|noun


Etymology:
From Late Middle English *consciusnes*, from Old French *conscience*, equivalent to *conscient* + *-esse*.  Ultimately from Latin *conscientia* "knowledge within oneself, consciousness," from *consciens*, present participle of *conscire* "to be conscious of," from *com-* "together" + *scire* "to know."
