jitteriness:/ˈdʒɪtərɪnəs/| n. |紧张不安，神经过敏|A state of nervousness or unease, typically characterized by trembling or fidgeting.|例句：The caffeine caused a feeling of jitteriness. 咖啡因引起了一种紧张不安的感觉。|例句：He experienced jitteriness before his big presentation. 在重要的演讲前，他感到紧张不安。|近义词：anxiety, nervousness, unease, restlessness|反义词：calmness, composure, tranquility|词性: noun

jitter:/ˈdʒɪtər/| n. v. |紧张不安，抖动；使紧张，使抖动|n. A state of nervousness or unease. v. To shake slightly and rapidly; tremble.|例句：He had the jitters before the exam. 他在考试前感到紧张。|例句：The screen jittered, then went black. 屏幕抖动了一下，然后变黑了。|近义词：(n.) anxiety, nervousness, unease; (v.) tremble, shake, quiver|反义词：(n.) calmness, composure; (v.) steady, stabilize|词性: noun, verb
