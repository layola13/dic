alveolus:/ælˈviːələs/ (singular)
alveoli: /ælˈviːəlaɪ/ (plural)
|n.|肺泡；小孔；牙槽|A tiny air sac in the lungs where the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide takes place. Also, a small cavity, pit, or socket, such as a tooth socket.|The alveoli are essential for respiration.|Synonyms: air sac, cavity, socket|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, perhaps blockage for the lung context)|Noun

alveolar:/ælˈviːələr/
|adj.|肺泡的；牙槽的|Relating to or resembling alveoli.|Alveolar macrophages are immune cells found in the alveoli.|Synonyms: pulmonary (in some contexts), dental (in some contexts)|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms)|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *alveolus*, diminutive of *alveus* "cavity, trough, tray, tub, boat, ship's hull".
