franchise:/ˈfræntʃaɪz/ | n. |特许经营权；特许经营店；公民选举权|A business model in which a company grants an individual or group the right to operate using its brand, products, and systems.  The individual or group pays fees for this right.  Can also refer to the right to vote.|例句：She opened a fast-food franchise. 她开了一家快餐连锁店。

franchise:/ˈfræntʃaɪz/ | vt. |授予特许经营权|To grant a franchise to someone. |例句：The company franchised its operations throughout the country.  这家公司在全国范围内授权经营。


franchisee:/ˌfræntʃaɪˈziː/ | n. |特许经营人；加盟商|An individual or group that holds a franchise.|例句：The franchisee is responsible for day-to-day operations. 特许经营者负责日常运营。


franchiser:/ˈfræntʃaɪzər/ | n. |特许经营授权者|A company that grants a franchise.|例句：The franchiser provides training and support to its franchisees. 特许人向其特许经营者提供培训和支持。


franchising:/ˈfræntʃaɪzɪŋ/ | n. |特许经营|The system of franchising.|例句：Franchising is a popular business model. 特许经营是一种流行的商业模式。


近义词:license, permit, authorization, concession, right, privilege
反义词:prohibition, restriction


词源: From Old French "franchise" meaning freedom, exemption, privilege, ultimately from Frankish "franko" meaning free.
