backstitches:/ˈbækˌstɪtʃɪz/
n.
回针，倒缝
A sewing stitch made by inserting the needle back through the last stitch and bringing it out a short distance in front.  A method of sewing in which the needle is passed back through the last stitch to create a solid line of stitching, often used for reinforcement or decorative purposes.
She used backstitches to secure the seam. 她用回针加固接缝。

近义词: oversewing, overcasting, whipstitch
