morphon:/ˈmɔːrfɒn/ (UK) /ˈmɔːrfɑːn/ (US)| n. |语素，词素|A minimal meaningful unit in a language.  A morpheme is the smallest unit of language that has meaning. It cannot be divided into smaller meaningful parts. |Example: The word "unbreakable" has three morphemes: "un-", "break", and "-able".|Synonym: morpheme|Antonym: (No direct antonym exists.  Perhaps one could consider a phoneme, which is a unit of sound, as a conceptual opposite, as it pertains to sound rather than meaning.)|Noun
