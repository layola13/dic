cesar:/ˈsiːzər/| n.|凯撒（人名；尤指尤利乌斯·凯撒）; 剖腹产|A Roman general and statesman. Julius Caesar (100-44 BC) played a critical role in the events that led to the demise of the Roman Republic and the rise of the Roman Empire.  A surgical operation for delivering a child by cutting through the wall of the mother's abdomen.|例句：Caesar crossed the Rubicon. 凯撒渡过了卢比孔河。

例句：She had a cesar. 她做了剖腹产。|近义词：emperor (皇帝), ruler (统治者) (for the title); C-section (for the surgical procedure)|反义词: N/A |词性:noun
