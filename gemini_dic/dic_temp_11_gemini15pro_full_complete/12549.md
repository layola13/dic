aspirate:/ˈæspəreɪt/ (US), /əˈspaɪəreɪt/ (UK)| v. n. adj.|吸入，抽出；送气音；渴望，追求|To draw (fluid) by suction from a vessel or cavity. To pronounce (a consonant) with an audible puff of breath.  Something aspirated, especially fluid in the lungs. Characterized by aspiration. |例句：The doctor aspirated the fluid from her lungs. 医生从她的肺部抽出了积液。|近义词：inhale, suck, withdraw, yearn, desire|反义词:exhale, blow, expel|verb

aspirate:/ˈæspərɪt/ (US), /ˈæspərət/ (UK)|n.|送气音；被吸入物|A consonant sound pronounced with an audible puff of breath, such as the English 'p', 't', and 'k'.  Something aspirated, especially fluid in the lungs.|例句：The 'p' in 'pin' is an aspirate. “pin”中的“p”是一个送气音。|近义词：plosive|反义词:none|noun

aspirate:/ˈæspərɪt/ (US), /ˈæspərət/ (UK)|adj.|送气的|Pronounced with an audible puff of breath.|例句：The word "cat" begins with an aspirate consonant. “cat”这个词以送气辅音开头。|近义词：plosive|反义词:unaspirated|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin aspiratus, past participle of aspirare (“to breathe on, blow on, pant after”), from ad- (“to, towards”) + spirare (“to breathe”).
