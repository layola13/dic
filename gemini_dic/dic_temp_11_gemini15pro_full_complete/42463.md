counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/| adj. n.|反事实的，反事实的陈述| relating to or expressing what has not happened or is not the case |例句：Counterfactual thinking can be helpful in learning from mistakes. 反事实思考有助于从错误中学习。|近义词：hypothetical, conjectural, speculative|反义词：factual, real, actual|词性:形容词,名词

counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/| n.|反事实的陈述，反事实条件句|a statement that describes what would have happened if something else had been different|例句：Historians often use counterfactuals to explore alternative possibilities. 历史学家经常使用反事实来探索其他的可能性。|近义词：hypothetical, conjectural, speculative|反义词：factual, real, actual|词性:名词

词源:  From counter- + factual.  "Counter-" indicates opposition or contrast.  "Factual" relates to facts.  Thus, "counterfactual" literally means "against the facts."
