bureaucrats: /ˈbjʊərəˌkræts/ | n. | 官僚 | Officials who work in a bureaucracy; often used negatively to suggest that they are inflexible, inefficient, and concerned with rules and procedures rather than with people or practical results. | The bureaucrats insisted on following every rule, no matter how pointless it seemed.  这些官僚坚持遵守每一条规则，无论它看起来多么无意义。|  officials, administrators, civil servants | reformers, innovators, mavericks | noun

bureaucrat: /ˈbjʊərəˌkræt/ | n. | 官僚 | An official in a government department, especially one perceived as being concerned with procedural correctness at the expense of people's needs. | He was seen as a heartless bureaucrat, more interested in paperwork than helping people. 他被视为一个冷酷无情的官僚，对文书工作比帮助人们更感兴趣。| official, administrator, civil servant | reformer, innovator, maverick | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *bureaucrate*, from *bureau* (desk, office) + *-crate* (from Greek *kratēs*, member of a particular group).
