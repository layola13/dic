chamfering:/ˈtʃæmfərɪŋ/ /ˈtʃæmf(ə)rɪŋ/| n.|倒角，去角|The process of beveling an edge or corner.  This is often done to remove sharp edges, improve aesthetics, or aid in assembly.|The chamfering on the edge of the table prevents splintering.|近义词：beveling, rounding, easing|反义词：sharpening|noun

chamfer:/ˈtʃæmfər/ /ˈtʃæmf(ə)r/| n. vt.|倒角，斜面；给…加倒角|A beveled edge or corner; to cut a chamfer on.|The carpenter chamfered the edges of the shelf.|近义词：bevel, slope, cant|反义词：sharpen|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *chanfraindre (modern French chanfrein), likely from Vulgar Latin *camferinare, from Latin canna "reed, cane" + frangere "to break".  The original sense likely referred to breaking or notching a cane.
