hyperfastidiousness:/ˌhaɪpərˌfæˈstɪdiəsnəs/
|n.|过度讲究；过分挑剔|Excessive fastidiousness; extreme attention to detail and a tendency to find fault.|Her hyperfastidiousness made it difficult for her to enjoy a simple meal out. 她过分挑剔，以至于很难享受一顿简单的外出用餐。|The chef's hyperfastidiousness was evident in the immaculate presentation of every dish.  这位厨师对细节的极致关注体现在每道菜都完美无瑕的呈现上。|近义词: meticulousness, punctiliousness, overscrupulousness|反义词: carelessness, sloppiness, negligence|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From hyper- + fastidious + -ness.  "Hyper-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "over," derived from Greek. "Fastidious" comes from Latin "fastidium" meaning "loathing" or "disgust."  "-ness" is a suffix used to form nouns from adjectives.
