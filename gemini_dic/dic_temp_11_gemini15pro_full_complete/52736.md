dikes:/daɪks/| n.|堤坝，堤防|A long wall or embankment built to prevent flooding from the sea or a river.|The dikes protected the city from the rising waters. 堤坝保护城市免受上涨的洪水侵袭。|近义词：levees, embankments, barriers|反义词:  (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑 breach (决口))|noun


dikes:/daɪks/| v.|筑堤防护|To surround or protect with a dike.|They diked the river to prevent flooding. 他们为河流筑堤以防洪水。|近义词：embank, levee|反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑 demolish (拆除))|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *dīk, from Old Frisian dīk (“dike”), from Proto-West Germanic *dīk, from Proto-Germanic *dīkaz (“ditch, dike”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeyǵ- (“to knead, form, build”). Cognate with West Frisian dyk (“dike”), Dutch dijk (“dike”), German Teich (“pond”), English ditch. The verb is derived from the noun.
