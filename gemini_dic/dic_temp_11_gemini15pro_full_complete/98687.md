insulted: /ɪnˈsʌltɪd/ | adj. | 受辱的，感到侮辱的 | Feeling offended or disrespected. |  She felt insulted by his rude remarks. 她觉得他的粗鲁言论是一种侮辱。| synonyms: offended, affronted, slighted | antonyms: respected, honored, flattered | adjective

insult: /ɪnˈsʌlt/ | vt. n. | 侮辱，辱骂；侮辱，凌辱 | To speak to or treat with disrespect or scorn.  A disrespectful or scornful remark or action. | He insulted her by calling her stupid. 他骂她愚蠢，侮辱了她。 | synonyms: offend, affront, slight | antonyms: compliment, praise, honor | verb, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English insulte, from Old French insulter “to jump on, leap at; insult, taunt,” from Latin insultare “to jump or leap upon, to insult, taunt,” frequentative of insilire “to leap upon,” from in- “on, upon” + salire “to leap, jump.”
