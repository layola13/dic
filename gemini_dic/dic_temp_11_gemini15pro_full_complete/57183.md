dowling:/ˈdaʊlɪŋ/ | n. | 羽绒，绒毛；羽绒制品 | Fine feathers or down.  A filling material, such as down or feathers, used in quilts, pillows, etc. |  The dowling from the geese was used to stuff the pillows.  这些鹅的绒毛被用来填充枕头。 | synonyms: down, fluff, plumage | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for the material itself, but perhaps "synthetic filling") | noun

dowling:/ˈdaʊlɪŋ/ | n. | (澳大利亚俚语) 长木条，板条 | A long, thin piece of wood or timber. | He used a few dowlings to prop up the fence. 他用了几根木条支撑篱笆。 | synonyms: plank, board, strip, lath | antonyms: (No direct antonym) | noun

词源：From Middle English *dunlinge, from Old Norse *dúnlingr, from dúnn (“down”).

注意事项：Dowling is less common than "down" when referring to the soft feathers.  It's more commonly used in Australia to refer to a piece of timber.  Therefore, context is crucial for understanding its meaning.
