词源:
ice (n.) Old English *is "ice," from Proto-Germanic *isa- (source also of Old Norse iss, Old Frisian is, Dutch ijs, German Eis), from PIE root *eis- "cold, ice."

skate (n.) "metal runner for gliding on ice," 1690s, from Dutch schaats "skate," originally "shank, leg," related to Old English sceacan "to go quickly, move rapidly," from Proto-Germanic *skakanan (source also of Old Norse skata "to skate," Middle Dutch and Middle Low German schaten, German schatten "to slide").

-er (suffix)  forms agent nouns from verbs.
