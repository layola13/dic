hurkaru: /hərˈkɑːruː/
n.|传令兵，信使|A messenger, especially one employed by a person of rank or authority.  Historically, a hurkaru was often a runner or horseman in India or the East. |The hurkaru arrived, bearing news from the distant province.|Synonyms: messenger, courier, runner, emissary|Antonyms: recipient, receiver|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Hindi and Urdu *harkāra* (हरकारा, ہرکارہ), ultimately from Persian *harkāra* (هرکاره), meaning "one who does every kind of work," from *har* (هر) "every" + *kār* (کار) "work."
