indecisivenesses:/ɪnˌdɪˈsaɪsɪvnəsɪz/
|n.|优柔寡断，犹豫不决|The state or quality of being indecisive; inability to make decisions quickly and effectively.  A plural form, though rarely used, indicating multiple instances or types of indecisiveness.|His indecisivenesses cost him the opportunity.|近义词：hesitations, waverings, vacillations|反义词：determination, decisiveness|词性:名词

Etymology:
From indecisive + -ness.  Indecisive comes from in- (not) + decisive (from Latin decidere, to decide).
