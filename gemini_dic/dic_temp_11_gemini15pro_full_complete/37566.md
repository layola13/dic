commotion:/kəˈmoʊʃən/ (US), /kəˈməʊʃən/ (UK)| n. |骚动，混乱，喧闹|A state of confused and noisy disturbance.|例句：There was a great commotion when the fire alarm went off. 火警响起时，一片混乱。

例句：The sudden commotion startled the birds. 突然的骚动吓到了鸟儿。


近义词：disturbance, uproar, tumult, chaos, pandemonium|反义词: peace, quiet, calm, order, tranquility|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *commotiōn-, from Latin commovēre "to move violently," from com- "together" + movēre "to move."
