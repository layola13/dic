allegiance:/əˈliːdʒəns/
n.|忠诚，忠贞；拥戴，效忠|loyalty or commitment of a subordinate to a superior or of an individual to a group or cause.|例句：She pledged allegiance to the flag. 她向国旗宣誓效忠。|近义词：loyalty, fidelity, devotion, commitment|反义词：treachery, betrayal, disloyalty|词性:名词
Etymology: From Old French "alegeance"  meaning "loyalty, homage,"  derived from "lige," meaning "liege," ultimately from Frankish "*letig" meaning "free."  The sense shifted from the obligation of a vassal to his lord to a more general sense of loyalty.
