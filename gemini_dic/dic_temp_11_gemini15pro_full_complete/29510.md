catastrophic: /ˌkætəˈstrɒfɪk/ (UK) /ˌkætəˈstrɑːfɪk/ (US) | adj. |灾难性的，毁灭性的 | involving or causing sudden great damage or suffering |例句：The earthquake had catastrophic consequences for the region. 地震给该地区带来了灾难性的后果。|例句：The failure of the dam had a catastrophic impact on the town.  大坝的溃决对这座城镇造成了灾难性的影响。 |近义词：disastrous, calamitous, ruinous, devastating |反义词：beneficial, advantageous, fortunate, auspicious |词性: adjective

catastrophally: /ˌkætəˈstrɒfɪkli/ (UK) /ˌkætəˈstrɑːfɪkli/ (US) | adv. |灾难性地，毁灭性地 | in a way that involves or causes sudden great damage or suffering |例句：The project was catastrophally mismanaged.  这个项目被灾难性地 mismanagement。|例句：The company's finances were catastrophally affected by the recession.  经济衰退灾难性地影响了公司的财务状况。 |近义词：disastrously, calamitously, ruinously, devastatingly |反义词：beneficially, advantageously, fortunately, auspiciously |词性: adverb

词源: From French *catastrophique*, from Ancient Greek καταστροφή (katastrophē, “overturn, sudden turn, overthrow, ruin, catastrophe”), from καταστρέφω (katastrephō, “I overturn, subvert, ruin”), from κατά (kata, “down”) + στρέφω (strephō, “I turn”).
