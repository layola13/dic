ecclesiarch:/ɪˌkliːziɑːrk/ | n. |教会统治者；主教；教会领袖|A ruler of the church, a bishop, or a church leader. | The ecclesiarch decreed a new set of rules for the diocese. 教会统治者为教区颁布了一套新的规章制度。| The ecclesiarch presided over the important religious ceremony. 主教主持了重要的宗教仪式。 | synonym: bishop, prelate, primate, patriarch | antonym: layman, parishioner | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *ecclesiarchēs, from Ancient Greek ἐκκλησιάρχης (ekklēsiárkhēs), from ἐκκλησία (ekklēsíā, “church, assembly”) + ἄρχω (árkhō, “to rule”).
