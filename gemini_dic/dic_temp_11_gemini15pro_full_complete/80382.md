goliard: /ˈɡoʊliɑːrd/ | n. |  放荡不羁的学者；酒色之徒；行吟诗人 | A wandering scholar of the 12th and 13th centuries, typically a cleric or student who wrote satirical Latin poetry often celebrating drinking and sensual pleasures. | The goliards' poems often mocked the Church and its authority.  这些放荡学者的诗歌经常嘲讽教会及其权威。| He lived a goliard life, wandering from town to town, writing verses and drinking wine. 他过着放荡不羁的生活，从一个城镇流浪到另一个城镇，写诗喝酒。 | Synonyms:  reveller, carouser, libertine, bohemian | Antonyms: ascetic, puritan | Noun
