boohooed:/ˌbuːˈhuːd/ | v. | 哭诉，假哭 |  Cried noisily, often in a way that is not sincere. |  He boohooed about losing his job, but he secretly hated it anyway. 他哭诉着丢了工作，但他私下里一直讨厌这份工作。 | Example: The child boohooed loudly when his ice cream fell on the ground.  孩子冰淇淋掉在地上时大声哭了起来。| Synonym: wailed, sobbed, cried, whimpered (sometimes mock-cried, pretended to cry) | Antonym: laughed, rejoiced, cheered | verb

boohoo:/ˌbuːˈhuː/ | n. int. | 哭诉，假哭 (也可做感叹词) | A sound imitating crying, or an expression of disapproval or contempt. | "Boohoo," she said sarcastically, "I'm so sorry you didn't get your way."  “哼哼，”她讽刺地说，“你没能如愿，我真是太遗憾了。” | Example:  He gave a sarcastic "boohoo" when I told him my problems. 当我告诉他我的问题时，他讽刺地“哼哼”了一声。 | Synonym:  (as an interjection) pshaw, fiddlesticks | Antonym:  (as an interjection) hooray, yay | noun, interjection
