nebuchadnezzar:/ˌnɛbjʊkədˈnɛzər/ /ˌnɛbjʊkədˈnɛzər/ | n. |尼布甲尼撒| A Babylonian king who conquered Jerusalem and exiled the Jews.  He is also known for his supposed madness and for the Hanging Gardens of Babylon, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. | Nebuchadnezzar II was the most famous king of the Neo-Babylonian Empire. | 近义词: king, ruler, monarch | 反义词: subject, commoner | 词性: noun

Etymology: From the Hebrew נְבוּכַדְנֶאצַּר‎ (Nəḇūḵaḏneṣṣar), meaning "Nabu, protect the crown prince". Nabu was the Babylonian god of wisdom and writing.
