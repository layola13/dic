anabaptistic:/ˌænəbæpˈtɪstɪk/
adj.|再洗礼派的；激进的改革派的|Relating to or characteristic of Anabaptists, a radical Protestant sect that arose in Europe in the 16th century.  Characterized by  belief in adult baptism, pacifism, and often communal living.|  The community held anabaptistic beliefs, refusing to participate in war and practicing communal ownership.|近义词：radical, sectarian, nonconformist|反义词：orthodox, conventional|词性: adjective

anabaptist:/ˌænəˈbæptɪst/
n.|再洗礼派教徒|A member of a radical Protestant sect that arose in Europe in the 16th century.  Anabaptists believed in adult baptism, pacifism, and often communal living.|He was shunned by his family for becoming an Anabaptist.|近义词：dissenter, heretic|反义词：conformist|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Anabaptista, from Greek anabaptizein 'rebaptize,' from ana- 'again' + baptizein 'baptize.'
