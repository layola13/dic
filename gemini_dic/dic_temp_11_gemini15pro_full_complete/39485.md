connotation:/ˌkɑːnəˈteɪʃn/ /ˌkɒnəˈteɪʃn/| n. |含义; 言外之意; 内涵|An idea or feeling that a word invokes in addition to its literal or primary meaning. |例句：The word "cheap" often has a negative connotation, suggesting poor quality. “便宜”这个词通常有负面含义，暗示质量差。

例句：The word "home" has warm connotations of family and comfort. “家”这个词带有家庭和舒适的温暖含义。|近义词：implication, nuance, undertone, overtone, association|反义词：denotation|词性：名词

connotation:/ˌkɑːnəˈteɪʃn/ /ˌkɒnəˈteɪʃn/| n. |内涵; 隐含意义|The suggesting of a meaning by a word apart from the thing it explicitly names or describes.|例句："Slender" and "skinny" have different connotations, although they both describe someone thin.  “苗条”和“瘦骨嶙峋”有不同的含义，尽管它们都描述某人很瘦。

例句：The connotation of the word "discipline" can be either positive or negative, depending on the context. “纪律”一词的含义可以是正面的，也可以是负面的，取决于上下文。|近义词：implication, suggestion, hint|反义词：denotation, explicit meaning|词性：名词
