ace:/eɪs/ | n. adj. v. |高手；幺点；A牌；一流的；得满分；彻底击败|A playing card, die face, or domino face with a single pip.  A person who excels at something. Very good or excellent. To get a perfect score on (a test, assignment, etc.). To defeat decisively.|He's an ace at tennis. 他是网球高手。| 近义词: expert, master, champion, whiz, star, adept, proficient, skilled, top-notch, first-rate, excellent, superb, outstanding, perfect | 反义词: duffer, novice, beginner, loser, failure, incompetent, mediocre, poor | 词性: 名词, 形容词, 动词

ace:/eɪs/ | 词源: From Old French *as (modern French *as*) “one, unit; die, playing card marked with one spot,” from Latin *as*, assis "unity, a whole, an as (Roman coin)," from Proto-Italic *asos, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ems-o- “one.”| | | 近义词: expert, master, champion, whiz, star, adept, proficient, skilled, top-notch, first-rate, excellent, superb, outstanding, perfect | 反义词: duffer, novice, beginner, loser, failure, incompetent, mediocre, poor | 词性: 名词, 形容词, 动词
