mendicant: /ˈmɛndɪkənt/ | n. adj. | 乞丐；托钵僧；行乞的 | A beggar; someone who lives by asking people for money or food.  Depending on the context, can also refer to a member of a religious order that relies on charity. | The mendicant sat by the roadside, hoping for spare change. 那个乞丐坐在路边，希望能得到一些零钱。| Synonyms: beggar, pauper, supplicant, panhandler | Antonyms: rich person, philanthropist, benefactor | noun

mendicant: /ˈmɛndɪkənt/ | adj. | 乞讨的；行乞的 |  Relating to or characteristic of a beggar; practicing begging. |  He lived a mendicant lifestyle, traveling from town to town. 他过着乞讨的生活，从一个城镇到另一个城镇。| Synonyms: begging, supplicating, imploring | Antonyms: wealthy, affluent, prosperous | adjective
