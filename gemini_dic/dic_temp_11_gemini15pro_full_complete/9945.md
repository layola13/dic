aphaniptera:/əˌfænɪpˈtɪərə/| n. |蚤目；跳蚤|An order of wingless insects, the fleas, that are parasitic on warm-blooded animals. They have laterally compressed bodies and strong legs adapted for jumping.|Fleas, which belong to the order Aphaniptera, are known for their jumping abilities.|近义词:Siphonaptera|反义词:None|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek ἀφανής (aphanḗs, “invisible”) + πτερόν (pterón, “wing”).  Literally "invisible wings".
