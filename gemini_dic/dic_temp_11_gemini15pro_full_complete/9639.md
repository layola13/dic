antistructuralists: /ˈæntiˈstrʌktʃərəlɪsts/ | n. | 反结构主义者 |  Those who oppose or critique structuralism, a theoretical approach that emphasizes the importance of underlying structures in shaping meaning and culture.  Antistructuralists often focus on the instability of meaning and the role of individual agency. |  Many postmodern thinkers could be considered antistructuralists. 许多后现代思想家都可以被认为是反结构主义者。| The antistructuralists challenged the notion of fixed meaning. 反结构主义者挑战了固定意义的概念。 |  poststructuralists, deconstructionists | structuralists | noun
