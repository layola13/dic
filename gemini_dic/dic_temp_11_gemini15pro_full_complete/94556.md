impostures:/ɪmˈpɒstʃəz/ | n. | 欺骗；冒名顶替；诈骗 |  An act of deceiving someone by pretending to be someone else or to have qualities or abilities that you do not have.  | He was eventually convicted of imposture, having pretended to be a qualified doctor for years. 他最终被判犯有欺诈罪，因为他多年来一直假扮合格医生。 | Synonyms: fraud, deception, trickery, sham, charade | Antonyms: honesty, truthfulness, genuineness | noun

imposture:/ɪmˈpɒstʃə(r)/ | n. | 欺骗；冒名顶替；诈骗 | An instance of pretending to be someone else in order to deceive others. | The police quickly discovered the imposture and arrested the man pretending to be a police officer. 警方很快发现了这起冒名顶替事件，并将这名假扮警察的男子逮捕。 | Synonyms: fraud, deception, trickery, sham, charade | Antonyms: honesty, truthfulness, genuineness | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *imposture*, from Latin *impostūra* ("a deception, a fraud"), from *impostus*, past participle of *imponere* ("to impose upon, deceive").
