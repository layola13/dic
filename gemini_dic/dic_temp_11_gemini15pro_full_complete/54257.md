disgracefulness:/dɪsˈɡreɪsfʊlnəs/
noun
耻辱，不光彩
The state or quality of being disgraceful; shamefulness.
His disgracefulness brought shame upon his family. 他的不光彩行为给他的家庭带来了耻辱。
Synonyms: shamefulness, ignominy, dishonor, disreputability
Antonyms: honor, respectability, nobility


Etymology:
From disgrace + -ful + -ness.  Disgrace comes from the Old French *desgrace, from des- (meaning "removal of") + grace.  The suffix -ful transforms the noun into an adjective, and -ness transforms the adjective into a noun denoting a state or quality.
