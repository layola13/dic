engel:/ˈɛŋəl/ | n. | 天使; 安琪儿 | A spiritual being believed to act as an attendant, agent, or messenger of God, conventionally represented in human form with wings and a long robe. |  The Christmas play featured a choir of angels.  圣诞剧里有一群天使组成的唱诗班。 | synonyms: seraph, cherub, archangel | antonyms: devil, demon | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *engel, from Old English engel (also angel, ængel, engel), from Late Latin angelus (“angel, messenger”), from Ancient Greek ἄγγελος (ángelos, “messenger”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂énǵʰelos (“messenger”). Cognate with Latin angulus (“corner, angle”). Doublet of angle.
