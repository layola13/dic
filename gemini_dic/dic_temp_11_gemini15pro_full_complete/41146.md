copula:/ˈkɒpjʊlə/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpjələ/ (US)| n.|系动词|A word used to link the subject of a sentence with a predicate (a noun or adjective phrase that describes the subject), such as "is," "are," "was," "were," "am," "be," "been," "being," "become," "seem," "appear," "feel," "look," "smell," "taste," "sound," "remain," "stay," "get," "grow," "turn," "prove," and "go." | The verb "is" is a copula in the sentence "The sky is blue." 天是蓝色的。|  The flowers are fragrant. 这些花很香。 | synonym: linking verb | antonym: (no direct antonym, but conceptually, action verbs) | noun
