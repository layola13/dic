cannibalic:/ˌkænɪˈbælɪk/| adj.|食人的；同类相食的；残忍的|Relating to cannibalism; characterized by the consumption of one's own species.  Figuratively, it can also mean ruthlessly destructive or exploitative.|The tribe's cannibalic rituals were horrifying.  这个部落的食人仪式令人毛骨悚然。|The company's cannibalic marketing strategy was driving smaller competitors out of business.  这家公司残酷的营销策略正在将规模较小的竞争对手赶出市场。|Synonyms: anthropophagous, man-eating, savage, brutal, ruthless|Antonyms: humane, compassionate, civilized|adjective


cannibalistically:/ˌkænɪbəˈlɪstɪkli/| adv.|食人地；残忍地|In a cannibalic manner; ruthlessly.|The defeated army was cannibalistically destroyed by its own allies.  战败的军队被其盟友无情地摧毁了。|He cannibalistically exploited his employees, paying them low wages and offering no benefits.  他残酷地剥削他的员工，付给他们低工资，并且不提供任何福利。|Synonyms: savagely, brutally, ruthlessly|Antonyms: humanely, compassionately|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *caníbal*, from Carib *caniba*, meaning "Carib," a West Indies tribe reputed to practice cannibalism.  Ultimately from Arawakan.  The term came to be associated with cannibalism through Columbus's reports.
