juxtapose:/ˈdʒʌkstəˌpoʊz/ | v. | 并置，把…并列 | to place close together for comparison or contrast |  The artist juxtaposed light and shadow in the painting. 艺术家在画作中将光明与阴影并置。 | juxtapose images, juxtapose ideas, juxtapose elements, juxtapose texts, juxtapose two things | synonyms: compare, contrast, place side by side, set side by side | antonyms: separate, divide, isolate | verb

juxtaposition:/ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən/ | n. | 并置，并列 | the act or an instance of placing two or more things side by side often to emphasize contrast | The juxtaposition of the two images created a striking effect.  这两幅图像的并置产生了惊人的效果。 | juxtaposition of ideas, juxtaposition of images, juxtaposition of elements | synonyms: comparison, contrast, proximity, adjacency | antonyms: separation, division, isolation | noun
