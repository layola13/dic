constabularies:/kənˈstæbjʊˌlɛriz/| n. |警察队伍，警力；治安制度|A body of constables or other police officers; the police force of a particular district, country, etc. |The city expanded its constabularies to address rising crime rates. 城市扩充了警力以应对不断上升的犯罪率。|Synonyms: police force, law enforcement, police department|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, consider using terms like "lawlessness," "anarchy," or "disorder")|Noun

constabulary:/kənˈstæbjʊˌlɛri/| adj. |警察的，治安的|Relating to or characteristic of a constable or the police. | The constabulary duties included patrolling the streets and maintaining order. 治安职责包括巡逻街道和维持秩序。|Synonyms: police, law enforcement|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym)|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->

From Late Middle English *constablerie*, from Anglo-Norman *cunstablerie*, from Late Latin *constabularia*, from Latin *constabulus* ("constable").
