ossify:/ˈɒsɪfaɪ/ (UK) /ˈɑːsɪfaɪ/ (US)| v. |骨化；硬化；僵化|To become bone; to harden or become rigid like bone; to become inflexible in attitudes, opinions, etc.|
例句：
The cartilage will eventually ossify into bone. 软骨最终会骨化为骨。

His political views began to ossify as he got older. 他的政治观点随着年龄的增长而开始僵化。

近义词：harden, solidify, rigidify, fossilize|
反义词：soften, liquefy|
词性：动词


ossified:/ˈɒsɪfaɪd/ (UK) /ˈɑːsɪfaɪd/ (US)| adj. |骨化的；硬化的；僵化的|Having become bone; hardened or inflexible.|
例句：
The archaeologists discovered ossified remains of the dinosaur. 考古学家发现了恐龙的骨化遗骸。

His ossified thinking prevented him from accepting new ideas. 他僵化的思维使他无法接受新的想法。

近义词：hardened, rigid, inflexible, fossilized|
反义词：flexible, adaptable|
词性：形容词


词源: From Latin *ossificāre, from os (bone) + -ficāre (make).
