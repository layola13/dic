cheerfulnesses:/ˈtʃɪərfəlnəsɪz/
n.
快乐，愉悦，高兴
The state of being cheerful; happiness.
Her cheerfulnesses were infectious, brightening everyone's day. 她的快乐很有感染力，照亮了每个人的一天。
The room was filled with cheerfulnesses. 房间里充满了欢乐。

Synonyms: joy, gaiety, mirth, merriment, glee, lightheartedness
Antonyms: sadness, gloom, melancholy, dejection, depression
词性: 名词(plural)

Etymology:
From cheerfulness + -es (plural suffix).  Cheerfulness itself comes from cheerful + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing a state or quality). Cheerful traces back to Middle English *cherful, from chere "face, countenance" (from Old North French chere, ultimately from Latin cara "face") + -ful "full of."
