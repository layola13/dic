indiligence: /ˈɪndɪlədʒəns/ | n. |勤奋，勤勉 |  The quality of being hard-working and persistent in carrying out tasks. |  Her diligence and attention to detail ensured the project's success. 她勤奋和注重细节确保了项目的成功。|  Near synonyms: assiduity, industry, application | Antonyms: laziness, indolence, negligence | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *indiligence*, from Old French *diligence* (modern French *diligence*), from Latin *diligentia* ("attentiveness, carefulness"), from *diligens*, present participle of *diligere* ("to value highly, esteem, love; to single out, select; to attend to carefully, study"), from *dis-* ("apart, asunder") + *legere* ("to choose, gather, read").
