barricaders:/ˌbærɪˈkeɪdərz/
noun
设置路障的人；阻碍者
People who erect barricades; those who obstruct.
The barricaders held their ground against the advancing police.  设置路障的人抵抗着前进的警察。
Protesters turned into barricaders as they blocked the main road. 抗议者变成了设置路障的人，因为他们封锁了主要道路。
synonyms: protesters, obstructers, blockers, resistors
antonyms: supporters, enablers, assistants
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From the French word "barricade," meaning a barrier or obstruction, and the English suffix "-er," which denotes a person who performs an action. The root of "barricade" comes from the Old French "barrique," meaning "barrel," as barrels were often used to create such barriers.
