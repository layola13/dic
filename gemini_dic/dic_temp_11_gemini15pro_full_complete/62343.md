endenization:/ɛnˌdɛnɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |本土化，地方化|The process of making something more local or native; adapting something to a particular locality or region. This can involve adapting language, customs, or products to fit the local context.|The endenization of the curriculum involved incorporating local history and cultural practices.|近义词：localization, adaptation, indigenization, naturalization|反义词：globalization, internationalization|词性: noun

词源:  From the verb "endenize," which comes from the prefix "en-" (meaning "in" or "into") and the root "denizen" (meaning "inhabitant").  "Denizen" ultimately derives from the Old French word "deinz" (meaning "within").
