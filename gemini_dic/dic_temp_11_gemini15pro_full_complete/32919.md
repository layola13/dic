chooses:/ˈtʃuːzɪz/
| v.|选择，挑选|Third-person singular present tense form of "choose". It indicates the action of selecting something or someone from a group of options.|She chooses to study abroad. 她选择出国留学。|近义词：selects, picks, opts for|反义词: rejects, declines|词性:动词

choose:/tʃuːz/
| v.|选择，挑选|To select from a number of possibilities; to decide on a course of action.|I choose to believe her. 我选择相信她。|近义词：select, pick, opt for|反义词: reject, decline|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cēosan (attested in the Anglian dialect as cēosan), from Proto-Germanic *keusaną, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵews-. Cognates include Old Frisian kiāsa, Middle Dutch kiesen, Dutch kiezen, Old High German kiosan, German kiesen, Gothic 𐌺𐌹𐌿𐍃𐌰𐌽 (kiusan).
