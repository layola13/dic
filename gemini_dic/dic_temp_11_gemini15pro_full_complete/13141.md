asylums: /əˈsaɪləmz/ | n. | 庇护所，收容所；精神病院 |  A place of refuge or safety, especially for the persecuted or unfortunate; formerly, an institution for the care of people with mental illnesses. |  The old asylums were often overcrowded and understaffed. 这些古老的收容所经常人满为患，而且人手不足。 | synonyms: refuge, sanctuary, shelter, haven | antonyms: danger, peril, threat | noun

asylum: /əˈsaɪləm/ | n. | 庇护，避难；收容所，精神病院 |  Protection granted by a nation to someone who has left their native country as a political refugee; an institution offering shelter and support to people who are mentally ill. | He sought political asylum in the United States. 他在美国寻求政治庇护。| synonyms: refuge, sanctuary, shelter, haven | antonyms: danger, peril, threat | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *asile*, from Latin *asylum*, from Greek *ásylon*, neuter of *ásylos* "inviolable," from *a-* "not" + *sylē* "right of seizure."
