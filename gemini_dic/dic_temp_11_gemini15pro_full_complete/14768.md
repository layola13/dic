awkwardness:/ˈɔːkwərdnəs/| n.|尴尬，笨拙，不便|The awkwardness of the situation made everyone uncomfortable. 这种情况的尴尬让每个人都不舒服。|例句：She felt a sense of awkwardness when she realized she had forgotten his name.  当她意识到自己忘记了他的名字时，感到一阵尴尬。

There was an air of awkwardness after he made the inappropriate joke.  在他开了那个不合时宜的玩笑后，气氛变得很尴尬。|近义词：clumsiness, unease, discomfort|反义词:grace, poise, ease|词性:noun

awkward:/ˈɔːkwərd/| adj.|尴尬的，笨拙的，不便的|He's quite awkward at social gatherings. 他在社交场合很不自在。|例句：She felt awkward when she spilled coffee on her boss. 当她把咖啡洒到老板身上时，她感到很尴尬。

He tried to dance, but his movements were awkward and clumsy. 他试着跳舞，但他的动作笨拙而尴尬。 |近义词：clumsy, ungainly, uncomfortable|反义词:graceful, elegant, comfortable|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *awkwarde, from Old Norse *afugr (“turned the wrong way, clumsy”), from *öfugr, from *af (“away from”) + *ugga (“to fear”).  More at of, awe.  The -ward suffix is from Old English -weard.
