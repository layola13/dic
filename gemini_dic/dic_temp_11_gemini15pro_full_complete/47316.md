de-littering: /diːˈlɪtərɪŋ/
n.|清除垃圾，清理垃圾|The act of removing litter from an area.  The de-littering campaign was a huge success.|例句：The de-littering of the park made it much more pleasant.  清理公园里的垃圾让它变得更令人愉快。

例句：Volunteers spent the morning de-littering the beach. 志愿者们早上都在清理海滩上的垃圾。|近义词：cleaning, tidying, clearing up|反义词：littering, polluting|词性：名词/动词化的名词 (Gerund)


de-litter: /diːˈlɪtər/
v.|清除垃圾，清理垃圾|To remove litter from a place.|例句：We need to de-litter the streets. 我们需要清理街道上的垃圾。

例句：The council has employed a team to de-litter the town centre. 市政委员会雇佣了一个团队来清理市中心的垃圾。|近义词：clean, tidy, clear up|反义词：litter, pollute|词性：动词

词源：
"De-" is a prefix meaning "remove" or "reverse." "Litter" refers to trash or garbage carelessly discarded.  Therefore, "de-litter" literally means to remove litter.  The "-ing" form creates a gerund, turning the verb into a noun that represents the act of de-littering.
