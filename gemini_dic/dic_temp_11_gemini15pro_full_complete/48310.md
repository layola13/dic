decrepitude:/dɪˈkrɛpɪˌtjuːd/ /dɪˈkrɛpɪˌtuːd/| n.|衰弱；老朽|His decrepitude prevented him from taking part in the strenuous hike. 他的衰弱使他无法参加艰苦的徒步旅行。|例句：Age and illness had brought him to a state of decrepitude. 年老和疾病使他变得衰弱不堪。|近义词：infirmity, senility, debility|反义词:strength, health, vitality|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *decrepitudo, from Latin decrepitus (“very old, infirm”), past participle of decrepare (“to crackle, creak, rattle, decay”).
