black-out:/ˈblækaʊt/| n.|停电；灯火管制；消息封锁；暂时性失忆|A period of darkness caused by a failure of the electricity supply; a suppression of information, especially news or comment.|例句：The entire city experienced a black-out last night. 昨晚整个城市都停电了。|近义词：power outage, power failure, amnesia (for memory loss)|反义词:illumination, light, memory|词性:noun

black-out:/ˈblækaʊt/| v.|使昏厥；封锁消息|To cause someone to lose consciousness; to suppress information from being released to the public.|例句：The boxer blacked out after the knockout punch.  拳击手被击倒后昏迷了。|近义词：suppress, censor, faint|反义词:reveal, disclose, remember|词性:verb
