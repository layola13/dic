migrators:/ˈmaɪɡreɪtərz/| n. |迁徙者，移民|People or animals that migrate.  Those who move from one place to another, often seasonally or in search of better conditions. |Many birds are migrators, flying south for the winter. 许多鸟类都是迁徙者，冬天飞往南方。|Migratory birds travel thousands of miles each year. 候鸟每年飞行数千英里。|近义词：immigrants, emigrants, travelers, nomads|反义词:residents, settlers|词性:noun

Etymology: From the Late Latin word "migrator," which means "one who migrates". This word is derived from the Latin verb "migrare," meaning "to move from one place to another."
