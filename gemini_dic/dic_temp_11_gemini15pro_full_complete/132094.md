noncredulously:/ˌnɑːnkrəˈdʒuːləsli/
adverb|不轻信地，怀疑地|In a manner that expresses disbelief or skepticism.|He looked at her noncredulously, unable to believe her story.|Synonym: skeptically, doubtfully, distrustfully|Antonym: credulously, trustingly, believingly
word type: adverb

Etymology:
From non- + credulous + -ly.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Credulous" comes from the Latin "credulus," meaning "believing," from "credere," meaning "to believe."  "-ly" is a suffix that forms adverbs.
