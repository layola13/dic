farthing:/ˈfɑːrðɪŋ/ |n.| 四分之一便士（英国旧货币单位，等于四分之一便士）|A farthing was a small amount of money, worth a quarter of an old British penny. |The old woman offered a farthing for the apple.  老妇人出价四分之一便士买苹果。| synonym:  quarter-penny (historical)| antonym:  pound, crown (higher denominations of British currency) | noun

farthing:/ˈfɑːrðɪŋ/ |n.| 微不足道的东西；小事|In modern usage, a farthing can refer to something of very little value. |He wouldn't give a farthing for her opinion. 他对她的意见不屑一顾。| synonym:  jot, trifle, pittance | antonym: fortune, lot (referring to a large amount) | noun
