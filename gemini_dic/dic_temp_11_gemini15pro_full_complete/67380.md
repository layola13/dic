extenuation:/ɪkˌstenjʊˈeɪʃn/ | n. | 减轻；情有可原；辩解 |  The act of attempting to lessen the seriousness of an offense by offering excuses. |  "His youth and inexperience were offered in extenuation of his crime."  他的年轻和缺乏经验被用来为他的罪行辩解。 | synonyms: mitigation, excuse, justification, palliation, apology | antonyms: aggravation, intensification | noun

extenuate:/ɪkˈstenjʊeɪt/ | v. | 减轻；为...辩解；使人原谅 | To make (guilt or an offense) seem less serious or more forgivable. | "The judge took his age into consideration and extenuated his sentence." 法官考虑到他的年龄，减轻了他的刑罚。 | synonyms: excuse, mitigate, lessen, diminish, palliate | antonyms: aggravate, intensify | verb
