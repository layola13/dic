moonshine:/ˈmuːnʃaɪn/ | n. | 月光；私酿酒；空想，妄想 | Illicitly distilled or smuggled liquor. Also, foolish or untrue talk or ideas. | The police raided a moonshine operation in the mountains. 警察突袭了山区的一个私酿酒窝点。


moonshine:/ˈmuːnʃaɪn/ | v. |  从事走私或私酿 | To engage in the illicit distillation or smuggling of liquor. | He moonshined during Prohibition to make extra money.  他在禁酒令期间私酿酒以赚取额外收入。


Etymology: From moon + shine. Originally referring to working by the light of the moon, then to illicit activities done at night, and finally to the illicit liquor itself.


Synonyms: (n.) hooch, white lightning, rotgut; (for foolish talk) nonsense, balderdash, claptrap | Antonyms: (n.) (for liquor) legally produced alcohol; (for foolish talk) sense, truth, reality |


Word type: Noun, Verb
