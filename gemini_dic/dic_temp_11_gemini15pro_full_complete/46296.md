D-Day:/ˈdiː deɪ/
n.
诺曼底登陆日；关键时刻；决战日
The day (June 6, 1944) in World War II on which Allied forces invaded northern France.  Also used to refer to any day scheduled for a decisive operation or event.
The D-Day landings were a turning point in World War II. 诺曼底登陆是二战的转折点。
The CEO announced that the D-day for the merger would be next Monday. 首席执行官宣布合并的决战日是下周一。
synonym:deadline, zero hour, the day of reckoning
antonym:indefinite date, postponement
