circumstantial:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈstænʃəl/| adj.|依情况而定的；详细的；间接的；(证据)旁证的|Relating to, dependent on, or derived from circumstances.  Detailed; giving the full details of an event, etc.  Pointing indirectly to something but not proving it directly.|(of evidence) based on small details and indirect evidence rather than on clear proof.|
The evidence against him was largely circumstantial.
针对他的证据大多是旁证。

His account of the incident was very circumstantial.
他对事件的描述非常详细。


|Synonyms: incidental, contingent, detailed, indirect, inferential|Antonyms: direct, conclusive, essential, inherent|adjective|

circumstantials:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈstænʃəlz/| n.|细节；旁证|The details or facts attending an event, a course of action, etc.  Indirect or inferred evidence. |The police were trying to piece together the circumstantials of the crime.
警方正试图拼凑起这起犯罪的细节。

The jury had to rely on circumstantials as there was no direct evidence.
由于没有直接证据，陪审团不得不依赖旁证。

|Synonyms: details, particulars, facts, circumstances, indirect evidence|Antonyms: direct evidence, proof|noun|
