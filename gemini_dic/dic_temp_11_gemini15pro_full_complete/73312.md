foreclosed:/fɔːrˈkloʊzd/
foreclosed|v. |取消赎取权；排除；阻止；妨碍|To deprive (a mortgagor) of the right to redeem mortgaged property, usually because of failure to make payments.|The bank foreclosed on their house after they missed several mortgage payments. 银行在他们拖欠了几次抵押贷款后取消了他们房屋的赎回权。|
foreclosed|adj. |被取消赎取权的；被阻止的；被排除的|Barred or ruled out; precluded.|They felt foreclosed from any further action. 他们感到被阻止采取进一步行动。|近义词:repossessed, seized, barred, precluded|反义词:redeemed, recovered, allowed, permitted|词性:v. adj.
foreclosure:/fɔːrˈkloʊʒər/
foreclosure|n. |取消赎取权；丧失抵押品赎回权|The action of foreclosing, especially the legal proceeding by which a mortgage is foreclosed.|The family faced foreclosure on their home. 这家人面临房屋被取消赎回权的困境。|近义词:repossession, seizure|反义词:redemption, recovery|词性:n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French forclos, past participle of forclore “to shut out, exclude,” from fors- “outside” (from Latin foris) + clore “to close.”
