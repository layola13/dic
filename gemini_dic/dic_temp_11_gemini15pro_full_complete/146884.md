overingeniousness: /ˌoʊvərɪnˈdʒiːniəsnəs/ | n. | 过于精巧；过分巧妙；自作聪明 |  The quality of being excessively ingenious or clever, often to the point of being impractical or unnecessarily complicated. |  His overingeniousness resulted in a design that was too complex to manufacture. 他的过分巧妙导致设计过于复杂，难以制造。 |  sophistication, complexity, elaborateness |  simplicity, practicality, straightforwardness | noun

overingenious: /ˌoʊvərɪnˈdʒiːniəs/ | adj. | 过于精巧的；过分巧妙的；自作聪明的 | Excessively ingenious or clever, often to the point of being impractical or unnecessarily complicated. | The plot of the movie was overingenious and ultimately confusing. 这部电影的情节过于巧妙，最终令人困惑。 |  clever, intricate, elaborate | simple, practical, straightforward | adjective

ingenious: /ɪnˈdʒiːniəs/ | adj. | 巧妙的；灵巧的；有独创性的 |  (of a person) Clever, original, and inventive. |  She devised an ingenious solution to the problem. 她想出了一个巧妙的解决问题的办法。 | clever, inventive, innovative | uninspired, unimaginative, dull | adjective

Etymology:
From Late Latin *ingeniosus, from Latin ingenium (“natural capacity, talent, intelligence”) + -osus (suffix forming adjectives indicating fullness or abundance).
