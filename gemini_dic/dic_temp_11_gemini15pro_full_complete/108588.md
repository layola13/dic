lead-a-chase:/ˌliːd ə ˈtʃeɪs/| idiom | 引入歧途，误导，兜圈子 | To deliberately mislead or deceive someone, often by sending them on a wild goose chase. |  He led the police a chase all over town before they finally caught him. 他带着警察全城兜圈子，最后才被抓住。

The company led investors a chase with false promises of high returns. 这家公司用高回报的虚假承诺误导了投资者。


|近义词: mislead, deceive, delude, give someone the runaround |反义词: guide, direct, help | idiom
