burse: /bɜːrs/ | n. | 奖学金；助学金；（尤指大学的）资金；（交易所的）交易场所 | A sum of money given to a student at a university or college to help pay for their education.  A treasury, especially of a collegiate or religious institution.  A place where merchants or stockbrokers meet to trade. | He received a burse to study at Oxford. 他获得了牛津大学的奖学金。The burse of the university was significantly depleted. 大学的资金严重枯竭。The New York Stock Exchange is a prominent burse. 纽约证券交易所是一个重要的交易场所。 | 近义词：scholarship, grant, endowment, exchange | 反义词：debt, loan | 词性: 名词

词源: From Middle English *burs, from Old French borse, burse (“purse, money bag”), from Late Latin bursa (“purse”), from Greek βύρσα (búrsa, “hide, leather”), from βύρσης (búrsēs, “ox hide”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰursós (“hide, skin”). Doublet of purse.
