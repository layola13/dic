corpuscle:/ˈkɔːrpəsl/ (US) /ˈkɔːpʌsl/ (UK)| n. |微粒；细胞；血球|A minute particle of matter.  Specifically, a cell, especially a red or white blood cell, or a cell in bone, cartilage, etc.|Red blood corpuscles carry oxygen throughout the body. 红血球将氧气输送到全身。|Synonyms: cell, particle, molecule, atom|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical entity, could consider "mass" or "whole")|noun
