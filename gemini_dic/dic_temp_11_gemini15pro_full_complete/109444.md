lepadoid:/ˈlɛpədɔɪd/| adj. n.| 茗荷状的，茗荷类动物|Resembling or pertaining to barnacles (goose barnacles) of the order Lepadomorpha, which have a fleshy stalk.  They are characterized by a capitulum (body) enclosed by five or more calcareous plates and a stalk (peduncle) by which they attach to the substrate.|The lepadoid barnacles attached to the driftwood. 茗荷状藤壶附着在浮木上。|Synonyms: goose barnacle-like, pedunculate barnacle-like|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps sessile barnacle for contrast)|adjective, noun
