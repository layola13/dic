cuckoo:/ˈkʊkuː/| n. adj. vi.|布谷鸟，杜鹃，傻瓜|A European bird known for its distinctive call and its habit of laying its eggs in the nests of other birds.  Also used informally to mean crazy or foolish. |The cuckoo's call echoed through the woods. 布谷鸟的叫声在树林中回荡。|近义词：fool, simpleton|反义词:genius, wise|noun
cuckoo:/ˈkʊkuː/| adj.|疯狂的，愚蠢的|Informal term meaning crazy or foolish.|He had a cuckoo idea about building a house out of toothpicks. 他有一个愚蠢的想法，要用牙签建造房子。|近义词：crazy, foolish|反义词:sane, sensible|adjective
cuckoo:/ˈkʊkuː/| vi.|发出布谷鸟的叫声|To make the sound of a cuckoo.|The clock cuckooed three times. 时钟发出三声布谷鸟的叫声。|近义词：chirp, call|反义词: (no direct antonym)|verb
