conspirator:/kənˈspɪrətər/| n. |阴谋者，共谋者，同谋者|A person who takes part in a conspiracy; someone who plots with others, usually secretly, to do something wrong, harmful, or illegal.|The conspirator was sentenced to life in prison.  这名阴谋者被判处终身监禁。|synonyms: plotter, schemer, collaborator,  intriguer|antonyms: loyalist, conformist|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French conspirateur, from conspirer ‘conspire,’ from Latin conspirare ‘agree, combine, plot,’ from con- ‘together’ + spirare ‘breathe.’
