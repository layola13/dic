aphorismer:/ˈæfərɪˌzəmər/
n.
aphorist, one who makes or uses aphorisms
格言家；警句家
The aphorismer offered a pithy observation on the nature of time.
这位格言家对时间的本质做了精辟的观察。
Synonyms:  maxim writer, adage maker,  sayer of saws
Antonyms:  prolix writer, verbose speaker


ETYMOLOGY->
From aphorism + -er.  Aphorism comes from Late Latin aphorismus, from Greek aphorismos "definition, concise statement," from aphorizein "to define, limit, distinguish," from apo- "from" + horizein "to bound, limit."
