deriders:/dɪˈraɪdərz/| n.|嘲笑者，嘲弄者|People who mock or ridicule others.  Those who express contempt or scorn.| The deriders laughed at his clumsy attempt to dance. 嘲笑者嘲笑他笨拙的舞姿。|synonyms: mockers, scoffers, scorners|antonyms: admirers, praisers, supporters|noun
Etymology: From the verb "deride," which comes from the Late Middle English "deriden," from the Old French "derider," meaning "to mock, scorn," ultimately from the Latin "deridere," formed from "de-" (down, away) + "ridere" (to laugh).
