catholicalness's: /ˌkæθəˈlɪkəlnəsɪz/ | n. | 普遍性，包容性 | The state or quality of being catholic or universal; universality.  Specifically in a religious context, the quality of being applicable to the whole body of Christians. | The church emphasized the catholicalness's of its doctrines, asserting they applied to all believers. 教会强调其教义的普遍性，声称它们适用于所有信徒。 | 近义词: universality, comprehensiveness, ecumenism | 反义词: particularity, sectarianism, exclusivity | 词性: 名词

Etymology:

From catholicalness + -'s (possessive).  Catholicalness is derived from catholical + -ness.  Catholical comes from Late Latin *catholicalis, from Greek katholikos "universal, general," from kata "according to" + holos "whole."
