melancholic: /ˌmɛlənˈkɒlɪk/ (UK) /ˌmɛlənˈkɑːlɪk/ (US)| adj. n.|忧郁的；忧郁的人|Feeling or expressing pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause.|例句：She had a melancholic expression on her face. 她脸上带着忧郁的表情。|近义词：sad, depressed, wistful, pensive|反义词：happy, cheerful, joyous|词性：形容词

melancholic: /ˈmɛlənˌkɒlɪk/ (UK) /ˈmɛlənˌkɑːlɪk/ (US)| n.|忧郁的人|A person who is habitually melancholic.|例句：He was a melancholic, prone to fits of despair. 他是一个忧郁的人，容易陷入绝望。|近义词：pessimist, brooder|反义词：optimist|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *melancholicus, from Greek melankholikós, from melas, melan- ‘black’ + kholé ‘bile’.  The ancients believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholy.
