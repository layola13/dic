camp-follower:/ˈkæmp ˌfɒləʊər/ (US) /ˈkæmp ˌfɑːləʊər/| n.|随军商贩；营妓；追随者|A person who follows a camp, especially a military camp, to sell goods or services or for other purposes, often of a disreputable nature. Historically, it often referred to prostitutes.|The general ordered the camp-followers away from the battlefield. 将军命令随军商贩离开战场。|Synonyms: hanger-on, parasite, leech, tagalong|Antonyms: leader, pioneer|noun
