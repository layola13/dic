dropstone:/ˈdrɒpˌstəʊn/ (UK), /ˈdrɑːpˌstoʊn/ (US)| n. |坠石；落石|A rock transported by ice (such as a glacier or iceberg) and deposited into finer-grained sediments after the ice melts.  A dropstone is recognizable because it is noticeably different in size and type from the surrounding sediment.|The geologist identified the large boulder in the clay layer as a dropstone.|Synonyms: erratic, glacial erratic|Antonyms: None|Noun
