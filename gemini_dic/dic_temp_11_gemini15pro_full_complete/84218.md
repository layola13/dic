half-ruefully:/ˈhæf ˈruːfəli/ | adv. | 半懊悔地，略带悔意地 |  Partly rueful; expressing slight regret or sorrow, often with a touch of humor or self-deprecation. | He half-ruefully admitted he'd eaten the whole cake. 他半懊悔地承认他把整个蛋糕都吃掉了。 |  He smiled half-ruefully as he recounted the embarrassing incident. 他略带悔意地笑着讲述了那件尴尬的事。 | 近义词: apologetically, regretfully, sheepishly | 反义词: unrepentantly, defiantly | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "half" + "ruefully".  "Rue" comes from Old English *hreowan, from Proto-Germanic *hrewwan, meaning "to make sorry, grieve, regret."  "Fully" is from Old English fullice, meaning "completely."
