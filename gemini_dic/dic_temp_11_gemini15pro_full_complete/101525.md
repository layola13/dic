iron:/ˈaɪərn/ (US) /ˈaɪən/ (UK)| n. vt. adj.|铁；熨斗；坚强的；熨烫|A common metal, often used in construction and manufacturing. It can also refer to an appliance used to smooth clothes or to the act of using that appliance.  It can also be used as an adjective to describe something strong or resilient.|例句：The bridge is made of iron. 这座桥是用铁做的。|近义词：metal, steel (n.); press (v.); strong, resolute (adj.)|反义词:weak, fragile (adj.)|noun
iron:/ˈaɪərn/ (US) /ˈaɪən/ (UK)| vt.|熨烫|To smooth clothes or fabric with an iron.|例句：She ironed her shirt before the interview.  她在面试前熨烫了衬衫。|近义词：press, smooth|反义词:wrinkle, crease|verb
iron:/ˈaɪərn/ (US) /ˈaɪən/ (UK)| adj.|坚强的；钢铁般的|Strong and determined; resilient.|例句：He has an iron will. 他意志坚定。|近义词：strong, resolute, firm, unyielding|反义词:weak, fragile, irresolute|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *īsern, from Proto-Germanic *īsarnaz, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₁ésh₂r̥ "blood". The connection may be due to the reddish color of iron ore or rust, or possibly due to the use of iron weapons in bloodshed.
