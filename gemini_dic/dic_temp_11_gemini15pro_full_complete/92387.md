hypocritical:/ˌhɪpəˈkrɪtɪkəl/| adj.|伪善的，虚伪的|Behaving in a way that suggests one has higher standards or more noble beliefs than is the case.|He condemned the violence, but his own actions were hypocritical. 他谴责暴力，但他自己的行为却很虚伪。|例句：It's hypocritical to preach about honesty and then cheat on your taxes.  宣扬诚实却偷税漏税很虚伪。

例句：She found his concern hypocritical, given his past behavior. 考虑到他过去的行为，她觉得他的关心很虚伪。|近义词：two-faced, insincere, phony, deceitful|反义词：sincere, genuine, honest|词性:adjective

hypocrite:/ˈhɪpəkrɪt/| n.|伪君子，虚伪的人|A person who pretends to have virtues, moral or religious beliefs, principles, etc., that he or she does not actually possess, especially a person whose actions belie stated beliefs.|He's such a hypocrite - he says he cares about the environment but he drives a gas-guzzling car. 他真是个伪君子——他说他关心环境，却开着一辆耗油量大的汽车。|例句：He called her a hypocrite for smoking after telling him it was unhealthy. 他在她告诉他吸烟不健康后又吸烟，便称她为伪君子。


例句：Many people see politicians as hypocrites who don't practice what they preach. 许多人认为政客是言行不一的伪君子。|近义词：phony, charlatan, fraud|反义词：saint|词性:noun
