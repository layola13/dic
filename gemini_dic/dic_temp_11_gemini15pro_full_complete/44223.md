crocket:/ˈkrɒkɪt/ (UK) /ˈkrɑːkɪt/ (US)| n.|装饰物，钩针编织物，（建筑物等的）尖形装饰|A crocket is a decorative element, often found in Gothic architecture, resembling a curled leaf or bud. It is typically carved in stone or wood and placed along the sloping edges of gables, pinnacles, and canopies.|The cathedral's spires were adorned with intricate crockets.  大教堂的尖顶装饰着复杂的钩针编织物。|The craftsman meticulously carved each crocket on the wooden frame.  工匠一丝不苟地在木框架上雕刻每个钩形装饰。|Synonyms: ornament, finial, decoration|Antonyms: plainness, simplicity|Noun
