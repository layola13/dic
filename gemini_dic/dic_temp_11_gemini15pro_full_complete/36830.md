colonised:/ˈkɒlənaɪzd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːlənaɪzd/ (US)| adj.|殖民的；被殖民的|Brought under the control of a foreign power and occupied by settlers from that power.|例句：Many countries in Africa were colonised by European powers. 许多非洲国家都被欧洲列强殖民了。|近义词：settled, occupied, controlled|反义词：independent, liberated|词性：形容词

colonise:/ˈkɒlənaɪz/ (UK) /ˈkɑːlənaɪz/ (US)| vt.|殖民；移居于|To establish a colony in; to settle.|例句：The British colonised Australia in the 18th century. 英国人在18世纪殖民了澳大利亚。|近义词：settle, occupy, populate|反义词：decolonize, liberate|词性：动词

词源:  From the Latin word "colonus" meaning "farmer, husbandman, settler." This evolved into the Late Latin "colonia" meaning "farm, landed estate, settlement," and then into the French "coloniser."  The English verb "colonise" and adjective "colonised" are derived from the French.
