betrayers:/bɪˈtreɪərz/| n. |背叛者，叛徒|A person who betrays someone or something, such as a friend, lover, country, or principle.|例句：The betrayers were punished for their treachery. 叛徒因他们的背叛行为受到了惩罚。

例句：The king felt betrayed by his closest advisors. 国王觉得被他最亲密的顾问背叛了。

近义词：traitors, defectors, turncoats, double-crossers|反义词:loyalists, patriots|词性:名词

betray:/bɪˈtreɪ/| v. |背叛，出卖|To be disloyal to someone or something; to break a promise or violate trust.|例句：He betrayed his country by selling secrets to the enemy. 他向敌人出卖机密，背叛了他的国家。

例句：She felt betrayed by her best friend. 她觉得被她最好的朋友背叛了。


近义词：deceive, mislead, double-cross|反义词:support, defend|词性:动词
