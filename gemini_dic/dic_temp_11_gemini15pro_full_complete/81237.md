grandstander:/ˈɡrændˌstændər/| n.|哗众取宠者，卖弄者|a person who behaves in a way intended to attract attention and applause, especially in a way that is not sincere|The politician was accused of being a grandstander, more interested in photo opportunities than policy.|近义词：show-off, exhibitionist, attention-seeker|反义词:self-effacing, modest|noun

grandstand:/ˈɡrændˌstænd/| v. |哗众取宠，卖弄|to behave in a way intended to attract attention and applause, especially in a way that is not sincere|He was grandstanding for the cameras, pretending to be more upset than he actually was.|近义词：show off, play to the gallery|反义词:behave modestly|verb
