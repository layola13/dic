decrepit:/dɪˈkrɛpɪt/| adj.|破旧的，衰老的|Worn out or ruined and broken down by hard use and age.  Characterized by or showing the effects of old age; feeble.|例句：The decrepit building was condemned by the city. 这栋破旧的建筑被市政府谴责了。

例句：My decrepit car finally gave out on the highway. 我那辆破旧的汽车终于在高速公路上抛锚了。|近义词：ramshackle, dilapidated, feeble, infirm, run-down|反义词：robust, sturdy, sound, strong, vigorous|词性:形容词
词源: From Latin *decrepitus*, past participle of decrepare (“to rattle down, crackle, grow weak”), from de- (“down”) + crepare (“to crack, creak, rattle”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱrep- (“to make a ringing sound”).
