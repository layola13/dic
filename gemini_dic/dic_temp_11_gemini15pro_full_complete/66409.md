executive: /ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv/ (US), /ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv/ (UK) | n. adj. | 高级管理人员，行政人员；执行的，行政的 | A person with senior managerial responsibility in a business organization.  Relating to or having the power to put plans or actions into effect. | The company's executives decided to invest in new technology. 公司的高管们决定投资新技术。|  近义词：manager, administrator, director | 反义词: subordinate, employee | 词性: noun, adjective

executive: /ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv/ (US), /ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv/ (UK) | adj. | 执行的，行政的 | Having the power to put plans, actions, or laws into effect. | She has an executive position in the company. 她在公司担任行政职务。| 近义词：administrative, managerial | 反义词: non-executive, operational | 词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *exécutif, -ive*, from Old French *executif*, from Latin *exsecūtīvus*, from *exsequī* (“to follow out, carry out”), from *ex-* (“out”) + *sequī* (“to follow”).
