alonenesses:/əˈloʊnɪsnɪsɪz/
|n.|孤独，寂寞（复数）|The state of being alone; a period of being alone.  The plural form emphasizes multiple instances or types of loneliness.|
|例句：He experienced profound alonenesses after his wife died.  他妻子去世后，他经历了深深的孤独。|
|例句：The book explores the different alonenesses of city life. 这本书探讨了城市生活中不同类型的孤独。|
|近义词：solitudes, isolations|
|反义词：togetherness, companionship|
|词性：Noun|


aloneness:/əˈloʊnɪsnɪs/
|n.|孤独，寂寞|The state of being alone; a period of being alone.|
|例句：She felt a deep aloneness after her children left home. 孩子们离开家后，她感到深深的孤独。|
|例句：The aloneness of the desert can be both daunting and inspiring. 沙漠的孤寂既令人畏惧，又令人鼓舞。|
|近义词：solitude, isolation|
|反义词：togetherness, companionship|
|词性：Noun|


词源:
From alone + -ness.  "Alone" comes from Old English "all ana" meaning "all one" or "completely by oneself".
