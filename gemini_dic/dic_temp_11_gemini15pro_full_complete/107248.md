laissez-faire:/ˌlɛseɪˈfɛər (US), ˌlɛseɪˈfɛː (UK)| adj. n.|放任主义; 自由放任|A policy or attitude of letting things take their own course, without interfering.|The government adopted a laissez-faire approach to the economy.|Synonyms: noninterventionist, free-market, unregulated|Antonyms: interventionist, regulated, controlled|Adjective, Noun
Etymology->
From French, literally "let (people) do (as they choose)."
