disregards:/ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑːrdz/
| v. (third-person singular simple present disregards, present participle disregarding, simple past and past participle disregarded) | 忽视，漠视，不理会 | to ignore something or treat it as unimportant. | He disregarded medical advice and continued to smoke. 他无视医嘱，继续吸烟。|  近义词：ignores, neglects, overlooks | 反义词：heeds, respects, observes | 词性：动词

disregard:/ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑːrd/
| n. | 忽视，漠视，不理会 | the act of ignoring something or treating it as unimportant. |  She showed a complete disregard for the rules. 她对规则完全漠视。| 近义词：neglect, indifference, contempt | 反义词：regard, respect, attention | 词性：名词


词源:
From dis- + regard.  "Dis-" is a prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of". "Regard" comes from Old French "regarder" meaning "to look at".
