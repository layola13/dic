levered: /ˈliːvərd/ /ˈlevərd/ | adj. | 杠杆化的，举债经营的 |  Using borrowed capital as a source of funding to increase the potential return of an investment. | Example:  The company's levered buyout left it with a significant debt burden. 这家公司的杠杆收购使其背负了沉重的债务。

Example: Levered ETFs can amplify returns, but also losses. 杠杆ETF可以放大收益，但也会放大损失。

Synonym: geared, leveraged

Antonym: unlevered, ungeared

Etymology: From "lever" + "-ed," meaning "equipped with a lever," figuratively used in finance to describe the use of debt to magnify returns.

词性: 形容词
