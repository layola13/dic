farcical:/ˈfɑːrsɪkəl/ | adj. | 滑稽的，荒唐的，闹剧似的 | Relating to or resembling a farce, especially because of absurd or ridiculous aspects. | The whole trial was a farcical display of injustice. 整个审判就是一场荒谬的不公正表演。|The play was so bad, it was almost farcical. 这部戏太糟糕了，简直像闹剧。| ludicrous, absurd, ridiculous, preposterous | serious, realistic, sensible, reasonable | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *farcesque*, from *farce* (“stuffing, forcemeat, farce”), from Late Latin *farsa* (“stuffing”), from Latin *farcire* (“to stuff”).
