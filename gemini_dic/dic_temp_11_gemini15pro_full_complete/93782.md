immensible:/ɪˈmɛnsɪbl/ | adj. | 不能浸入的，不能浸透的 | Not capable of being immersed or steeped; not capable of being dissolved or softened by steeping. |  The philosopher argued that the soul is immensible in the body. 哲学家认为灵魂不能浸入身体。| 近义词：impermeable, impassable | 反义词：permeable, soluble | 词性：形容词

Etymology: From Late Latin immensibilis, from in- "not" + mensibilis "measurable," from mensus, past participle of metiri "to measure."  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *meH- "to measure."
