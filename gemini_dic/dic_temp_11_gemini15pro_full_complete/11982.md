artful:/ˈɑːrtfʊl/ | adj. | 巧妙的；狡猾的；熟练的 |  Clever and skillful, especially in a dishonest way; exhibiting art or skill. | She had an artful way of getting what she wanted. 她有一种巧妙的方法得到她想要的东西。| synonyms: cunning, sly, wily, shrewd, ingenious, adroit | antonyms: clumsy, artless, guileless, naive, honest | adjective

artful:/ˈɑːrtfʊl/ | adj. | 艺术的；精美的 | Showing creative skill or taste. | The artful arrangement of flowers impressed everyone. 精美的插花布置给每个人留下了深刻的印象。| synonyms: artistic, aesthetic, elegant, refined, graceful | antonyms: crude, clumsy, inartistic, tasteless | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *artful*, from *art* + *-ful*.  *Art* in this context originally meant "skill" or "craft," derived from Old French *art*, from Latin *artem* (nominative *ars*), meaning "skill, art, craft". The suffix *-ful* derives from Old English *-ful*, meaning "full of" or "characterized by."
