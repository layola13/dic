implicature:/ɪmˈplɪkətʃər/| n.|含义; 言外之意; 暗示|An implicature is something the speaker suggests or implies with an utterance, even though it is not literally expressed.  It's the intended meaning beyond the literal meaning of what is said.|例句：By saying "It's cold in here," the speaker might be making the implicature that someone should close the window. 说话人说“这里很冷”，其言外之意可能是希望有人关上窗户。

例句：Her implicature was clear, even though she didn't explicitly state her disapproval. 即使她没有明确表示反对，她的言外之意也很清楚。|近义词：insinuation, suggestion, hint, innuendo|反义词:explicit statement, direct statement|词性:noun

implicatures:/ɪmˈplɪkətʃərz/| n.|含义; 言外之意; 暗示（复数）|The plural form of "implicature," referring to multiple implied meanings.|例句：The speaker's implicatures were subtle, but the audience understood them.  演讲者的言外之意很微妙，但听众理解了它们。

例句：Analyzing the implicatures in the conversation revealed the underlying tension. 分析对话中的言外之意揭示了潜在的紧张关系。|近义词：insinuations, suggestions, hints, innuendos|反义词:explicit statements, direct statements|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "to imply" (Latin implicare, "to involve, entangle, enfold"), + "-ature" (a suffix forming nouns denoting an action or result).
