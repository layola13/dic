meddlesomely:/ˈmedəlsəmli/| adv.|爱管闲事地，好干预地|In a way that interferes in other people's affairs without being asked or needed. | She meddlesomely offered her advice, even though no one asked for it. 她多管闲事地提出建议，即使没有人要求她这样做。|例句：He meddlesomely inquired about his neighbor's finances. 他爱管闲事地打听邻居的财务状况。|近义词：intrusively, officiously, interferingly|反义词：respectfully, discreetly|词性:副词

meddle:/ˈmedl/ | v. |干涉，干预，管闲事|To interfere in something that is not one's concern.| Don't meddle in other people's business. 不要干涉别人的事。|例句：He shouldn't meddle in their affairs. 他不应该干涉他们的事情。|近义词：interfere, intrude, tamper|反义词：ignore, neglect|词性:动词

meddlesome:/ˈmedlsəm/ | adj. |爱管闲事的，好干预的|Given to meddling; interfering.| She has a meddlesome aunt who always wants to know everyone's business. 她有一个爱管闲事的阿姨，总是想知道每个人的事。|例句：His meddlesome nature often caused problems. 他爱管闲事的性格经常引起问题。|近义词：interfering, intrusive, officious|反义词：respectful, discreet|词性:形容词
