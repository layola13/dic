headmistress-ships:/ˌhɛdˈmɪstrɪsʃɪps/| n. |女校长职位；女校长身份|The status or position of being a headmistress.  It refers to the collective roles or tenures of headmistresses, especially when considering them as a group or over time.|The conference brought together headmistress-ships from across the country. 这次会议汇集了来自全国各地的女校长。|  Her research focused on the evolving role of headmistress-ships in the 21st century. 她的研究重点是21世纪女校长角色的演变。|Synonyms: headmistress positions, leadership roles (in all-girls schools)|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps consider:  teaching positions, subordinate roles)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
headmistress (n.) + -ship (suffix)

headmistress:
head (n.) + mistress (n.)

head: from Old English *hēafod, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch hoofd and German Haupt.
mistress: from Old French maistresse, feminine of maistre “master,” from Latin magistra, feminine of magister.

-ship: Old English -scipe (cognate with German -schaft), a suffix forming abstract nouns from other nouns and occasionally from adjectives.  It denotes state, condition, or quality.
