equipollency:/ɪˌkwɪˈpɒlənsɪ/ /ɪˌkwɪˈpɑːlənsɪ/
noun
相等；等效；等价
The state of being equipollent; equality of power or force.  The condition of having equal value, significance, or effect.
The treaty aimed to establish equipollency between the two nations. 该条约旨在确立两国之间的平等地位。
The judge stressed the equipollency of the two legal arguments. 法官强调了两种法律论点的等效性。
synonyms: equivalence, parity, equality, sameness
antonyms: disparity, inequality, difference, imbalance
词性: 名词


equipollent:/ɪˌkwɪˈpɒlənt/ /ɪˌkwɪˈpɑːlənt/
adjective
相等的；等效的；等价的
Having equal power, force, or significance.
The two arguments were considered equipollent. 这两个论点被认为是等效的。
The opposing forces were equipollent, leading to a stalemate. 对抗的力量相等，导致了僵局。
synonyms: equivalent, equal, tantamount, commensurate
antonyms: unequal, disparate, dissimilar, different
词性: 形容词


词源:
From Late Latin *aequipollentia, from Latin aequipollens (“equivalent”), from aequi- (“equal”) + pollens (“powerful”).
