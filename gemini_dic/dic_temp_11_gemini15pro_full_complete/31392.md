chargeableness:/ˈtʃɑːrdʒəb(ə)lnəs/

Etymology:
From "chargeable" + "-ness". "Chargeable" comes from Old French "chargable", from "charger" (to load, burden), ultimately from Late Latin "carricare" (to load a car), from "carrus" (cart, wagon). The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
