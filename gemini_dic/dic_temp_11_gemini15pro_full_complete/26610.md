cachet:/ˈkæʃeɪ/ (US), /ˈkæʃeɪ/ (UK)| n. |威望，声望；（尤指正式文件上的）印章，封印|A mark of authenticity, approval, or prestige.  A distinguishing mark or seal, as on a document.| The restaurant had a certain cachet. 这家餐厅有一定的声望。|Synonym: prestige, distinction, status, mark, seal|Antonym: insignificance, obscurity|noun

cachet:/ˈkæʃeɪ/ (US), /ˈkæʃeɪ/ (UK)| n. |（药物的）胶囊|A flat capsule enclosing a dose of unpleasant-tasting medicine.|She took the cachet with a gulp of water. 她用水吞下了胶囊。|Synonym: capsule, tablet, pill|Antonym: N/A|noun
