bootleg:/ˈbuːtˌleɡ/ (US), /ˈbuːtleɡ/ (UK)| adj. n. v. | 私酿的，走私的；私酿酒；走私 | Made, distributed, or sold illegally. |

例句：
He was caught selling bootleg whiskey.
他因贩卖私酿威士忌而被捕。

例句：
The bootleg DVDs were confiscated by the authorities.
盗版DVD被当局没收了。


近义词：illicit, unlawful, contraband, smuggled, pirated |
反义词：legal, legitimate, authorized |
词性：adjective, noun, verb |


bootleg:/ˈbuːtˌleɡ/ (US), /ˈbuːtleɡ/ (UK)| n. | 私酿酒；走私货物 |  Illegally made or distributed goods, especially alcohol. |

例句：
The police raided the warehouse and found a large stash of bootleg cigarettes.
警方突袭了仓库，发现了大量走私香烟。

例句：
He made a fortune selling bootleg recordings of popular bands.
他靠出售流行乐队的盗版唱片发了财。


近义词：contraband, smuggled goods |
反义词：legal goods |
词性：noun |


bootleg:/ˈbuːtˌleɡ/ (US), /ˈbuːtleɡ/ (UK)| v. |  私酿；走私；非法复制 | To make, distribute, or sell illegally. |

例句：
They were bootlegging CDs from their apartment.
他们在公寓里盗版CD。

例句：
The movie was bootlegged and sold on the streets before its official release.
这部电影在正式上映前就被盗版并在街上出售。


近义词：smuggle, pirate, traffic |
反义词：legally produce and distribute |
词性：verb |


词源：From the practice of concealing illicit liquor in the leg of a high boot. |
用法说明：Bootleg can be used as an adjective, noun, or verb.  It typically refers to illicit goods, particularly alcohol, or the act of producing or distributing such goods.  It can also refer to unauthorized recordings of live performances. |
