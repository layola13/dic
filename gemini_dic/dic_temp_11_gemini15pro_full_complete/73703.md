forgoer:/fɔːrˈɡoʊər/
n.
放弃者; 摒弃者
Someone who forgoes something.
例句：He was a forgoer of worldly pleasures. 他是一个放弃世俗享乐的人。

例句：The forgoers of the opportunity later regretted their decision.  那些放弃机会的人后来后悔了他们的决定。


近义词：abstainer, renouncer
反义词：indulger, partaker
词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From forgo + -er.  "Forgo" comes from Old English *forgān (unattested), from for- + gān "to go."  The suffix "-er" is used to form nouns indicating a person who performs an action.
