hole-out:/ˌhəʊlˈaʊt/ | n. vi. | 进洞；一杆进洞 | To sink the ball directly from off the green, typically in approach or recovery shot; the act of completing a hole in golf by hitting the ball into the hole. | He holed-out from the bunker for a birdie. 他从沙坑一杆进洞抓到一只小鸟。 | 近义词: sink, ace (for a hole-in-one) | 反义词: N/A | 词性: 名词, 动词


hole-out:/ˌhəʊlˈaʊt/ | vi. | 一杆进洞 | To complete a hole in golf by hitting the ball into the hole. | She holed-out from 100 yards! 她从100码外一杆进洞！| 近义词: sink, ace (for a hole-in-one) | 反义词: N/A | 词性: 动词


词源:  Compound of "hole" and "out."  "Hole" originates from Old English "hol," meaning "cavity, opening." "Out" comes from Old English "ūt," meaning "outward, outside."
