amalgam:/əˈmælɡəm/ | n. |混合物，汞合金，混杂物 | A mixture or blend of different elements. Specifically, an alloy of mercury with another metal, especially one used for dental fillings.  | The dentist used an amalgam to fill the cavity. 牙医用汞合金填充了蛀牙。| synonyms: mixture, blend, compound, alloy, composite, fusion, combination | antonyms: element, component, ingredient | noun

amalgamate:/əˈmælɡəmeɪt/ | v. |合并，混合，融合 | To combine or unite to form one organization or structure.  | The two companies amalgamated to form a larger corporation. 两家公司合并成了一家更大的公司。| synonyms: merge, combine, unite, consolidate, integrate, fuse | antonyms: separate, divide, split, disintegrate | verb

amalgamation:/əˌmælɡəˈmeɪʃn/ | n. |合并，混合，融合 | The action, process, or result of combining or uniting. | The amalgamation of the two schools created a stronger academic program.  两所学校的合并创造了一个更强大的学术课程。| synonyms: merger, union, combination, consolidation, integration, fusion | antonyms: separation, division, split, disintegration | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *amalgama, from Medieval Latin amalgama, perhaps from Greek malagma "softening agent" or Arabic al-malgham.
