luddism:/ˈlʌdɪzəm/
n.
反对新技术的观点；机器破坏运动
Opposition to new technology; the practice of destroying machines.
The Luddite movement began in the 19th century in England.  卢德运动始于19世纪的英格兰。
Modern-day Luddism is often associated with a fear of artificial intelligence.  现代的卢德主义通常与对人工智能的恐惧有关。
近义词:  anti-technologism, technophobia
反义词:  technophilia, futurism
词性: 名词


luddite:/ˈlʌdaɪt/
n.
反对技术进步的人；（19 世纪初英国）捣毁机器的工人
A person opposed to technological progress; (historical) a member of any of the bands of English workers who destroyed machinery, especially in cotton and woolen mills, that they believed was threatening their jobs (1811–16).
Some people consider him a Luddite because he refuses to use a smartphone.  有些人认为他是个卢德主义者，因为他拒绝使用智能手机。
The Luddites smashed the new weaving machines in protest.  卢德分子砸碎了新的织布机以示抗议。
近义词:  reactionary, saboteur
反义词:  progressive, innovator
词性: 名词
