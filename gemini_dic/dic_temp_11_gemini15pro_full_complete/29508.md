catastasis:/kəˈtæstəsɪs/
n.
灾难的转折点; 剧情发展的转折点; 高潮前的转折点
The climax of a drama, often brought about by a catastasis or a reversal of fortune.
戏剧的高潮，通常是由灾难的转折点或命运的逆转造成的。
The catastasis in the play occurred when the protagonist discovered the villain's true identity.
这部戏的转折点发生在主角发现反派真实身份的时候。
近义词: turning point, climax, crisis, peripeteia
反义词: anticlimax, resolution, denouement
词性: 名词

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek κατάστασις (katástasis, “a standing down, lowering, settling”), from καθίστημι (kathístēmi, “I set down, establish, appoint”), from κατά (katá, “down”) + ἵστημι (hístēmi, “I stand, set”).
