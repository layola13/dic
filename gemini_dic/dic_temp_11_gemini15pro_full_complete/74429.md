franciscanism:/frænˈsɪskənɪzəm/
n.
圣方济各会主义;方济各会精神
The doctrines or principles of St. Francis of Assisi, especially the vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience.
St. Francis embraced a life of poverty and service, and Franciscanism spread rapidly throughout Europe.  圣方济各接受了贫穷和服务的生活，方济各会主义迅速传遍了整个欧洲。
近义词:Franciscan order, Franciscan spirituality
反义词:materialism, worldliness
词性:noun

Etymology:  From Francis + -an + -ism. Ultimately from the Late Latin name *Franciscus*, a nickname meaning "Frenchman," given to Giovanni di Pietro di Bernardone (St. Francis of Assisi).
