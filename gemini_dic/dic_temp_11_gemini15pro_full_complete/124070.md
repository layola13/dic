morphophyly:/ˌmɔːrfəˈfaɪli/
n.|形态谱系|The study of the evolutionary relationships among organisms based on their morphology, or physical form.  It focuses on shared derived characteristics to infer common ancestry.|Morphophyly is an important concept in evolutionary biology, helping us understand how different species are related. 形态谱系是进化生物学中的一个重要概念，它帮助我们理解不同物种之间的关系。|
例句：Researchers used morphophyly to trace the evolution of bird wings. 研究人员利用形态谱系来追踪鸟类翅膀的进化。

例句：The morphophyly of these insects suggests a recent common ancestor. 这些昆虫的形态谱系表明它们有一个共同的祖先。|近义词：cladistics, phylogeny|反义词: (No direct antonym exists, as it represents a specific methodology.  Potentially,  "molecular phylogeny" could be considered a contrasting approach.)|词性:noun

词源：
From morpho- (Greek μορφή morphḗ "form") + -phyly (Greek φυλή phylḗ "tribe, clan, race").
