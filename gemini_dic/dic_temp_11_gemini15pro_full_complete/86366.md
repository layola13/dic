hectorism:/ˈhɛktərɪzəm/| n. |恃强凌弱；威吓；恫吓|Bullying or intimidation through bluster or threats.  Derived from the Trojan warrior Hector, often portrayed as a braggart in later literature, though not in Homer's Iliad.|He engaged in a bit of hectorism to get his way.|近义词：bullying, intimidation, bluster, browbeating, coercion|反义词:  pacifism, appeasement|noun
