intermeddle:/ˌɪntərˈmedl/| v. |干涉，干预，插手|To interfere in something that is not one's concern.|She was constantly intermeddling in her neighbors' affairs. 她经常干涉邻居的事情。|He intermeddled in a family dispute. 他插手了一场家庭纠纷。|近义词：interfere, meddle, intrude|反义词:ignore, abstain|词性:动词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *intermedlen*, from Old French *entremedler*, from *entre-* (between) + *medler* (to mix).  Ultimately from Latin *inter-* (between) + *miscere* (to mix).
