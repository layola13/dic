dispel:/dɪˈspɛl/
verb
驱散，消除
To make something go away, especially a feeling or belief.
The company issued a statement to dispel the rumors. 公司发表声明以驱散谣言。
synonyms: banish, eliminate, disperse, dissipate
antonyms: gather, collect, accumulate
词性: 动词

dispel:/dɪˈspɛl/
词源: From Latin dispellere, from dis- "apart" + pellere "to drive, push."
用法:  Dispel is often used with abstract nouns like doubts, fears, rumors, or illusions. It implies a complete removal or scattering of something unwanted.
