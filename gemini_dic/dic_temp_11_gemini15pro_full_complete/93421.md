ills:/ɪlz/| n.|疾病，弊病，问题|Problems or misfortunes.|The country suffers from many social ills.  这个国家遭受许多社会弊病。|Synonyms: troubles, problems, ailments, diseases, misfortunes|Antonyms: benefits, advantages, remedies, cures|noun

Etymology: From Middle English *illes*, plural of *il* (“evil, harm, disease”), from Old English *yfel* (“evil, ill, bad, wicked”), from Proto-Germanic *ubilaz* (“evil”), from Proto-Indo-European *up-élo-*. Cognate with Scots *ill* (“bad, evil, wicked”), West Frisian *il* (“bad”), Dutch *euvel* (“bad, wrong”), German *übel* (“bad, evil, sick”), Danish *ild* (“bad, evil”), Swedish *illa* (“badly, ill”), Icelandic *illr* (“bad, evil, wicked”).  Related also to Old English *yfelu* (“evil, wickedness”).  The plural form *ills* has become the standard form for the noun meaning "problems or misfortunes".
