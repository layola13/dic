mischanneling:/ˌmɪsˈtʃænəlɪŋ/
|n.|误导，错误引导|The act of directing something, especially resources or energy, in a wrong or inappropriate way.  This implies a failure to utilize something effectively or for its intended purpose.|
|例句：Mischanneling of funds led to the project's failure.  资金的误导导致了项目的失败。|
|例句：His mischanneling of his anger resulted in strained relationships with his colleagues. 他对愤怒的错误引导导致了他与同事关系紧张。|
|近义词：misdirection, misuse, misapplication|
|反义词：proper allocation, appropriate use, correct channeling|
|词性：名词|


mischannel:/ˌmɪsˈtʃænəl/
|v.|误导，错误引导|To direct something, especially resources or energy, in a wrong or inappropriate way.|
|例句：The government was accused of mischanneling public funds. 政府被指控滥用公共资金。|
|例句：He mischanneled his artistic talents into a dead-end job. 他把自己的艺术天赋误用在了一份没有前途的工作上。|
|近义词：misdirect, misuse, misapply|
|反义词：allocate properly, use appropriately, channel correctly|
|词性：动词|
