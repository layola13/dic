breakout:/ˈbreɪkaʊt/| n.|爆发，突破，越狱|A sudden escape from prison.  A sudden increase in the occurrence of something, typically something unpleasant such as a disease or war.  The act or an instance of breaking out. |例句：There was a breakout at the local prison last night. 昨晚当地监狱发生了一起越狱事件。

The breakout of war caused widespread panic. 战争的爆发引起了 widespread 恐慌。|近义词：escape, eruption, outbreak, upsurge|反义词：crackdown, suppression|noun

break out:/breɪk ˈaʊt/| v.|爆发，突然发生|To start suddenly. To escape from a place or situation.  To develop a skin rash or irritation.|例句：A fire broke out in the kitchen. 厨房里突然着火了。

She broke out in a cold sweat. 她突然冒出一身冷汗。|近义词：erupt, emerge, escape, flare up|反义词：subside, die down|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrasal verb "break out," combining "break" (Old English *brecan) and "out" (Old English ūt). The noun sense developed from the verb.
