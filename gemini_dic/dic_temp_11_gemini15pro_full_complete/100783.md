intrenching:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃɪŋ/
adjective
牢固确立的；根深蒂固的
Firmly established and difficult to change.
The government's policies are deeply intrenched in the minds of the people. 政府的政策在人民心中根深蒂固。
intrenched:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃt/
adjective
牢固确立的；根深蒂固的
Firmly established and difficult to change.
He was intrenched in his position and refused to compromise. 他坚守自己的立场，拒绝妥协。
intrench:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃ/
verb
使牢固；确立；巩固
Establish (an attitude, habit, or belief) so firmly that change is very difficult.
Sexism is deeply intrenched in our society.  性别歧视在我们社会根深蒂固。


近义词: ingrained, established, fixed, rooted, entrenched
反义词: superficial, shallow, weak, unstable
词性: adjective (intrenching, intrenched), verb (intrench)


词源: From French "retrancher," meaning "to cut again," from "re-" (again) + "trancher" (to cut).  The original meaning was to dig trenches for defense, which later evolved metaphorically to mean establishing a strong position.
