mincemeat:/ˈmɪnsˌmiːt/ | n. | 肉馅，碎肉; 彻底击败 | A mixture of dried fruit, spices, suet, and sometimes meat, typically baked in a pie or other pastry.  Figuratively, it means to defeat someone decisively. | Example 1:  My grandmother makes the best mincemeat pies for Christmas. 我祖母做的圣诞节肉馅饼最好吃。

Example 2: The opposing team was made mincemeat of in the final game.  对方球队在决赛中被彻底击败了。 | Synonym: ground meat, hash (for the literal meaning); rout, thrashing (for the figurative meaning) | Antonym:  whole, intact (for the figurative meaning) | noun


mince:/mɪns/ | v. | 切碎；委婉地说；装腔作势 | To chop or grind (food, especially meat) into very small pieces. To speak indirectly or evasively. To walk with short, affected steps. | Example 1:  You should mince the garlic finely before adding it to the pan.  你应该把大蒜切碎后再放入锅中。

Example 2:  Don't mince words – just tell me the truth.  不要拐弯抹角——直接告诉我真相。 | Synonym: chop, grind, dice (for food); euphemize, soften (for speech); strut, sashay (for walking) | Antonym:  (for food) leave whole; (for speech) be blunt, be direct | verb
