counterbalancing:/ˌkaʊntərˈbælənsɪŋ/
|n.|平衡，抵消|The experiment used counterbalancing to control for order effects.|实验使用平衡法来控制顺序效应。|
|近义词：offsetting, compensating, neutralizing|反义词：exacerbating, amplifying|词性:名词|
|英文解析:The act of arranging factors in an experiment or study so that any bias introduced by one factor is offset or neutralized by the presence of another factor. This is often used to control for order effects in repeated measures designs.|
|例句：Counterbalancing is essential in research to ensure unbiased results. 平衡在研究中至关重要，以确保结果无偏差。|
|例句：The researchers used counterbalancing to mitigate the potential impact of learning effects on the participants' performance. 研究人员使用平衡法来减轻学习效应对参与者表现的潜在影响。|


counterbalance:/ˌkaʊntərˈbæləns/
|v.|平衡，抵消|The weights counterbalance each other.|砝码相互平衡。|
|近义词：offset, compensate, neutralize|反义词：exacerbate, amplify|词性:动词|
|英文解析:To offset or neutralize the effect of something by introducing an opposing force or influence.|
|例句：The positive aspects of the new policy counterbalance the negative ones. 新政策的积极方面抵消了消极方面。|
|例句：His enthusiasm counterbalanced her pessimism. 他的热情抵消了她的悲观。|
