claw:/klɔː/ (US), /klɔː/ (UK)| n. v. |爪子；抓住；撕扯|A curved, pointed nail on the foot of an animal, especially a bird or mammal.  As a verb, it means to scratch or pull something with claws or fingernails. |The eagle held the fish in its powerful claws.  老鹰用它强有力的爪子抓住鱼。|近义词：talon, nail, grab, scratch|反义词:release, let go|词性:名词, 动词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *clawu, from Proto-Germanic *klawō, from Proto-Indo-European *gleubʰ- (“to cleave, split”).  Cognate with Dutch klauw, German Klaue, Gothic *klau.
