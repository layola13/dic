crackled:/ˈkrækəld/ (US), /ˈkrækld/ (UK)| v. |发出噼啪声；爆裂声| to make a series of short, sharp snapping sounds | The fire crackled merrily in the fireplace. 火在壁炉里欢快地噼啪作响。| The radio crackled with interference. 收音机里发出噼啪的干扰声。| synonyms: snap, pop, crack, sputter | antonyms: whisper, murmur | verb

crackled:/ˈkrækəld/ (US), /ˈkrækld/ (UK)| adj. | 裂纹的; 脆的; 声音沙哑的 | covered with fine cracks; making a series of short, sharp snapping sounds; (of a voice) rough and dry | The old woman's voice was crackled with age. 老妇人的声音因年老而沙哑。| The glaze on the pottery was crackled. 陶器上的釉裂开了。| synonyms: cracked, fractured, broken, hoarse, raspy | antonyms: smooth, clear, resonant | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *craklen, frequentative of craken (“to crack”), of imitative origin.
