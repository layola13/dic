jukebox's:/ˈdʒuːkbɒksɪz/ | n. |自动点唱机（的）|A jukebox is a partially automated music-playing device, usually a coin-operated machine, that will play a patron's selection from self-contained media.|The jukebox's selection was limited, but we still had fun.|Synonym: record player|Antonym: None (lack of a direct antonym for a specific device)|noun
Etymology: "juke" (c. 1900, American English) probably from Gullah "joog," meaning disorderly or wicked + "box" (Old English "box," from Late Latin "buxis," from Greek "pyxos," from "pyxos," boxwood).
