and-how:/ænd haʊ/ | adv. | 以及如何; 以何种方式 |  Used to emphasize the difficulty or complexity of something. |  "I don't know how we're going to finish this project, and-how." 我不知道我们将如何完成这个项目，以及如何完成它。 | 近义词: somehow, by what means | 反义词: easily, simply | 词性: 副词


Etymology:
"And how" is a colloquial expression that originated as an emphatic form of "how."  The "and" adds intensity and implies a sense of difficulty or disbelief.  Its exact origins are difficult to pinpoint, but it likely arose in spoken English before becoming more widely used.
