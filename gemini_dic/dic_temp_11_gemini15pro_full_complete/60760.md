electrography:/ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒɡrəfi/ | n. | 心电描记法;电子照相术 | A method of producing images by electrical means, often used in medical contexts to record the electrical activity of the heart or brain. | Electrography is a useful tool for diagnosing heart conditions. 心电描记法是诊断心脏疾病的有用工具。 | electrocardiography, electroencephalography | photography | noun
etymology->
electro- + -graphy
electro-: from Greek *ēlektron, "amber" (because static electricity was first observed by rubbing amber).
-graphy: from Greek -graphia "writing, description," from graphein "to write, draw, describe,"
近义词->
electrocardiography (for heart activity)
electroencephalography (for brain activity)

反义词->
None (as it's a specific technique, it doesn't have a direct opposite)
