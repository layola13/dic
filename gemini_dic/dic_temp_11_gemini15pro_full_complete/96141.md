indentation:/ɪnˌdɛnˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 缩进；凹陷；锯齿状 |  The act of indenting or the state of being indented.  A cut, notch, or recess formed in an edge or surface. | The indentation of the coastline provides natural harbors. 海岸线的缩进形成了天然的港湾。 | synonyms: notch, recess, indent,  depression | antonyms: projection, protrusion | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *indentaten*, from Middle French *indenter*, from Medieval Latin *indentāre*, from Latin *in-* + *dentāre* (“to furnish with teeth”), from *dens* (“tooth”).
