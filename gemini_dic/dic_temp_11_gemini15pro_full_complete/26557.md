cable-car:/ˈkeɪbl kɑːr/| n.|缆车；索道|A vehicle, usually enclosed, that is suspended from and propelled by a continuously moving cable, typically used for transporting passengers up steep inclines or across spans such as rivers.|The cable-car offered breathtaking views of the city below. 缆车提供了下面城市令人叹为观止的景色。|近义词：ropeway, funicular, lift|反义词:|名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From cable + car.  "Cable" comes from Old North French "cable," ultimately from Latin "capulum" (rope, halter). "Car" comes from Old North French "carre" (wheeled vehicle), from Latin "carrus" (two-wheeled cart).
