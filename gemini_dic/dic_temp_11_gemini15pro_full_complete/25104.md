buccaneered:/ˌbʌkəˈnɪərd/ | v. |  抢劫，掠夺；以海盗方式行事 | acted like a buccaneer; robbed someone |  He buccaneered his way to the top of the company, ruthlessly eliminating his rivals. 他以海盗般的手段爬到了公司最高层，无情地铲除了他的竞争对手。| Synonyms: plundered, pillaged, looted | Antonyms: donated, contributed | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *boucanier*, from *boucan* "a wooden framework for smoking meat," from Tupi *mokam*. Originally referring to Caribbean hunters who smoked meat on boucans, later applied to pirates who preyed on Spanish ships.
