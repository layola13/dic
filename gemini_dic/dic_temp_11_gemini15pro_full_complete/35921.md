cocultivate: /ˌkoʊˈkʌltɪveɪt/
verb
共同培养，协同种植，共同发展
To grow two or more different kinds of organisms together.  In agriculture, it often refers to growing different plant species together that benefit each other. More broadly, it can also refer to fostering the growth or development of something together.
The farmers cocultivate legumes and grains to improve soil fertility.  农民们将豆类和谷物共同种植以提高土壤肥力。

The two companies decided to cocultivate the new technology, sharing resources and expertise.  两家公司决定共同开发这项新技术，共享资源和专业知识。
Synonyms: co-grow, cultivate together, raise together, grow together, develop together
Antonyms: monoculture, separate cultivation, isolate
