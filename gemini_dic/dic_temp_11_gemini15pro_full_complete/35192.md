clubbism:/ˈklʌbɪzəm/
noun
俱乐部主义，社团主义
The tendency to form or join clubs or associations.  A strong attachment to one's club and its members.
He embraced clubbism, joining every organization he could. 他欣然接受了社团主义，加入了每一个他能加入的组织。
The clubbism of the town was evident in the numerous social gatherings.  这个城镇的社团主义在频繁的社交聚会中显而易见。
Synonyms:  sociability, camaraderie, gregariousness
Antonyms:  solitude, isolation, reclusiveness
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From "club" + "-ism," a suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine, etc.  "Club" originates from Old Norse "klubba," meaning a club or cudgel, later evolving to denote a group of people meeting socially.
