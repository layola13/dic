fertilising: /ˈfɜːtɪlaɪzɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈfɜːrtəlaɪzɪŋ/ (US)
| v. |施肥，使肥沃；使受精|The act of making something fertile, typically soil or a female egg.  This can be done through the addition of nutrients to the soil or through the union of sperm and egg. |Farmers are fertilising their fields. 农民们正在给田地施肥。|The flowers are fertilised by bees. 这些花是由蜜蜂授粉的。|近义词：enriching, nourishing, manuring, impregnating|反义词：sterilising, depleting|词性: Verb (present participle)

fertilising: /ˈfɜːtɪlaɪzɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈfɜːrtəlaɪzɪŋ/ (US)
| adj. |施肥的，使肥沃的|Something that has the effect of fertilising.  |The fertilising rains brought new life to the desert. 滋养的雨水给沙漠带来了新的生命。|近义词：enriching, nourishing|反义词：sterilising, depleting|词性: Adjective (present participle used as an adjective)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fertilisen, from Old French fertiliser, from Late Latin fertilisāre, from Latin fertilis (“fertile”) + -āre (suffix forming verbs of the first conjugation).
