depose:/dɪˈpəʊz/ (UK) /dɪˈpoʊz/ (US)| vt.| 免职；罢免；作证；宣誓作证|To remove someone from a position of power, especially a king or queen.  To testify or give evidence under oath. |The king was deposed by his own son. 国王被自己的儿子罢免了。|Synonyms:  oust, overthrow, dethrone, remove, testify, attest |Antonyms: install, reinstate, enthrone|verb


depose:/dɪˈpəʊz/ (UK) /dɪˈpoʊz/ (US)| vt.| 宣誓作证|To make a statement, especially under oath, in a legal proceeding. |She was deposed as a witness in the trial.  她在审判中被宣誓作证。|Synonyms: testify, swear, declare, attest |Antonyms: recant, retract|verb
