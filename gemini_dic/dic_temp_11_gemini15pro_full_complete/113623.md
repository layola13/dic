machiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | adj. n.| 马基雅维利的；权谋的；不择手段的 |  Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian political philosopher whose book "The Prince" described the ruthless methods some rulers use to acquire and maintain power. |  His machiavellian tactics ensured his rise to the top, but he made many enemies along the way.  他那马基雅维利式的策略确保了他登上顶峰，但沿途也树敌众多。|  The young CEO’s machiavellian plan to oust the company founder shocked the board members.  这位年轻CEO用马基雅维利式策略排挤公司创始人的计划震惊了董事会成员。 |Synonyms: cunning, devious, scheming, unscrupulous, artful, wily | Antonyms:  honest, principled, straightforward, ethical, scrupulous | adjective, noun


machiavelli:/ˌmækiəˈveli/ | n. | 尼可罗·马基雅维利（意大利政治哲学家）| Niccolò di Bernardo dei Machiavelli (3 May 1469 – 21 June 1527) was an Italian Renaissance diplomat, philosopher, historian, politician, humanist, writer, playwright and poet. He has often been called the "Father of Modern Political Philosophy and Political Science". He wrote his best-known work The Prince (Il Principe) in 1513. | Machiavelli is often cited as the quintessential example of a political realist. 马基雅维利常被认为是政治现实主义者的典型代表。 | Many consider Machiavelli the father of modern political science. 许多人认为马基雅维利是现代政治学的奠基人。| Synonyms: (for his philosophy) realism, pragmatism | Antonyms: (for his philosophy) idealism, utopianism | noun
