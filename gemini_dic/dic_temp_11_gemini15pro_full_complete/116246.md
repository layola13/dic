mass-observer:/ˈmæs əbˈzɜːrvər/ | n. | 群众观察者；大众观察员 | A person who observes the behavior and opinions of a large group of people, often for market research or social analysis. |  The mass-observer noted a shift in consumer preferences towards sustainable products. 群众观察员注意到消费者偏好转向可持续产品。

| Example: Mass-observers play a crucial role in understanding public opinion.  大众观察员在了解公众舆论方面发挥着至关重要的作用。 | Synonym: market researcher, pollster, social analyst, public opinion researcher | Antonym:  N/A (lack of a direct antonym; perhaps "participant," "insider," or someone actively involved rather than passively observing) | noun
