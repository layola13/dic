ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bolays, *bolas, from Anglo-Norman *beloce, from Old Northern French beloice (“wild plum”), from Vulgar Latin *balātia, neuter plural of Late Latin balātius, from Ancient Greek βαλανωτός (balanōtós, “acorn-shaped”), from βάλανος (bálanos, “acorn”).  The word is related to "ball" and refers to the round shape of the fruit.
