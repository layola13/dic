overadvertised: /ˌoʊvərˈædvərˌtaɪzd/ | adj. | 过度宣传的，广告过度的 |  Excessively advertised; promoted to an excessive degree. overadvertised: /ˌəʊvərˈædvərˌtaɪzd/ | adj. | 过度宣传的，广告过度的 |  Excessively advertised; promoted to an excessive degree.  Often implies that the product or service does not live up to the hype created by the advertising. | The new phone was so overadvertised, I expected it to do my laundry!  这款新手机广告做得太夸张了，我都以为它能帮我洗衣服了！ | Synonyms: hyped, overhyped, overblown, puffed up | Antonyms: underadvertised, understated | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (meaning "excessive") + "advertised" (past participle of "advertise," from "advert" meaning "to turn attention to," ultimately from Latin "advertere").
