dandyish:/ˈdændiɪʃ/| adj.|花花公子的；纨绔子弟的|Resembling or characteristic of a dandy; excessively concerned with or elaborate in dress or manner. |His dandyish demeanor and affected accent grated on her nerves. 他那纨绔子弟般的举止和做作的口音让她很恼火。|Synonyms: foppish, affected, pretentious, ostentatious, vain, narcissistic, self-absorbed|Antonyms: modest, unassuming, humble, down-to-earth|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From dandy + -ish.  "Dandy" originated in the late 18th century, possibly from a Scottish diminutive of Andrew or a pet form of the name Andrew.  The suffix "-ish" is used to form adjectives from nouns, indicating resemblance or characteristic of.
