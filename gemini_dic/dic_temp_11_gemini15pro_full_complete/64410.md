ergativity:/ɜːrɡəˈtɪvɪti/ | n. | 作格性 |  A grammatical system in which the subject of an intransitive verb and the object of a transitive verb are marked in the same way (absolutive case), while the subject of the transitive verb is marked differently (ergative case). This contrasts with nominative-accusative systems like English, where the subject of both intransitive and transitive verbs are marked the same way (nominative case). | The Basque language exhibits ergativity. 巴斯克语展现了作格性。|  Ergativity is a key feature of many languages in Australia. 作格性是澳大利亚许多语言的一个关键特征。| 近义词: (No direct synonym, but related concepts include) absolutive, ergative | 反义词: accusativity | 词性: Noun
