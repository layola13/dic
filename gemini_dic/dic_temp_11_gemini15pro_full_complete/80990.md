grade: /ɡreɪd/ | n. vt. | 等级；成绩；年级；坡度 | a level of quality, size, importance, etc. |  She got good grades in all her exams. 她所有考试都得了好成绩。| level, rank, class | ungraded | noun, verb
grade: /ɡreɪd/ | vt. | 评分；分级；逐步变化 | to arrange in or assign to grades; to evaluate | The teacher graded the essays. 老师给作文评分了。|  assess, evaluate, mark |  -- | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *graden, *greiden (“to prepare, arrange”), from Old French *grader, gredier (“to arrange, prepare, equip”), from Frankish *gradōn, from Proto-Germanic *graþōną (“to prepare, make ready”), from Proto-Indo-European *ghreh₁- (“to prepare”). Cognate with Dutch graden (“to grade, level”), German geraden (“to level, straighten”), Old English geræde (“equipped, ready”).
