flinchers:/ˈflɪntʃərz/ | n. | 退缩者，畏缩者 | People who flinch; those who recoil or shrink back from something difficult, unpleasant, or frightening. |  The flinchers in the group were unwilling to take the necessary risks.  这群人中胆怯的人不愿承担必要的风险。 |  cowards, shirkers, faint-hearted | heroes, daredevils | noun

flinch:/flɪntʃ/ | v. | 畏缩，退缩 | To make a sudden, involuntary movement of the body, typically as a result of pain or fear.  | He flinched when the doctor touched his wound. 当医生触碰到他的伤口时，他畏缩了一下。| wince, recoil, shrink | face, confront | verb
