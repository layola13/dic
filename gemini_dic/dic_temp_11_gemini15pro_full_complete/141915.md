ordinalism:/ˈɔːrdɪnəlɪzəm/| n.|等级主义，序位主义|A doctrine asserting that the bread and wine in the Eucharist become the body and blood of Christ while their appearances remain unchanged, but that this change is not brought about by the words of the priest but by the fact of their being offered as a sacrifice.|The priest explained the concept of ordinalism during the sermon. 牧师在布道中解释了等级主义的概念。|近义词：hierarchy, ranking, stratification|反义词：equality, egalitarianism|noun
