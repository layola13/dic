atrabiliar:/ˌætrəˈbɪliər/| adj.|忧郁的，易怒的，脾气暴躁的|Affected with melancholy; ill-tempered; atrabilious.  Characterized by or arising from black bile, one of the four humors in ancient physiology, believed to cause melancholy and anger. |He had an atrabiliar personality, prone to fits of rage and gloom.|近义词：melancholy, morose, sullen, gloomy, choleric, irascible|反义词：cheerful, jovial, happy, good-natured|词性: adjective
Etymology: From Late Latin *atrabilarius*, from Latin ātra bilis (“black bile”).
