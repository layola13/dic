crosswind:/ˈkrɒsˌwɪnd/ (UK) /ˈkrɑːsˌwɪnd/ (US)| n. adj.|侧风，横风|A wind blowing across or at right angles to the direction of travel (of an aircraft, ship, etc.).|The pilot had to compensate for the strong crosswind during landing. 飞行员在着陆时必须补偿强劲的侧风。|The crosswind made it difficult to control the car. 侧风使汽车难以控制。|近义词:side wind|反义词:headwind, tailwind|词性:名词, 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From cross + wind.  "Cross" in this context means "across" or "at right angles to."  The word "wind" comes from Old English "wind," ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*windaz."
