corncutter:/ˈkɔːrnˌkʌtər/| n. |鸡眼修剪师，江湖郎中|A person who removes corns, especially one who travels from place to place offering this service.  Often used figuratively to refer to a quack doctor or someone who offers dubious remedies.|例句：The old corncutter set up his stall in the market square. 那个老鸡眼修剪师在市场广场上摆起了他的摊子。

例句：He was denounced as a corncutter, peddling false cures. 他被谴责为江湖郎中，兜售虚假疗法。|近义词：chiropodist, podiatrist|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "licensed physician")|词性:noun
