lazaroni:/ˌlɑːtsəˈroʊni/| n.|流浪汉，乞丐（尤指那不勒斯的）|A group of homeless beggars, especially in Naples.|The streets were filled with lazaroni begging for food. 街上到处都是乞丐在乞讨食物。|Synonyms: beggars, vagrants, paupers|Antonyms: rich, wealthy|noun

Etymology->
From Italian *lazzaroni*, plural of *lazzaro* (“beggar”), from Lazarus, the beggar in the biblical parable of Luke 16.
