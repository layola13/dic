dogmatics: /dɒɡˈmætɪks/ | n. | 教条，教义，教条学 |  A set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true.  It refers to the study or body of doctrines or theological tenets of a particular religion or sect.  It often carries a negative connotation, implying rigidity and intolerance of other views. |  His dogmatics alienated many followers who sought a more open-minded approach to faith.  他对教条的坚持疏远了许多寻求更开明信仰的追随者。|  Synonyms: doctrines, tenets, creed, dogma | Antonyms: skepticism, doubt, open-mindedness | noun


dogmatic: /dɒɡˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 教条的，武断的 | Inclined to lay down principles as undeniably true. Asserting opinions in an arrogant manner; dictatorial.  | He was so dogmatic in his views that no one could reason with him. 他观点武断，以至于没有人能和他讲道理。 | Synonyms: assertive, opinionated, peremptory, doctrinaire | Antonyms: open-minded, flexible, receptive | adjective
