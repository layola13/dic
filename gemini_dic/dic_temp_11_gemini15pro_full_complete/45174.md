cuffs:/kʌfs/
n.|袖口，手铐|The shirt's cuffs were frayed.  这件衬衫的袖口磨损了。|例句：The police officer placed the cuffs on the suspect. 警察给嫌疑人戴上了手铐。|近义词：manacles, shackles, restraints|反义词:freedom, liberty|词性:名词
cuffs:/kʌfs/
v.|给…戴上手铐 (cuff的第三人称单数)|He was cuffed and taken away. 他被戴上手铐带走了。|例句：They cuffed the prisoner's hands behind his back.  他们把囚犯的双手铐在背后。|近义词：manacle, shackle, restrain|反义词:release, free|词性:动词

词源: From Old English *cylf, akin to Old Norse kjalfi "jawbone". Originally referred to a glove or mitten covering the lower arm, later narrowing to just the wristband. The meaning "handcuffs" arose from the resemblance to these wristbands.
