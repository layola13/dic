coxswaining:/ˈkɒksweɪnɪŋ (UK), ˈkɑːksweɪnɪŋ (US)| n. | 舵手工作，指挥划艇 | The act of steering and directing a rowboat, especially as the coxswain.  It encompasses navigation, strategy, and motivation of the crew. |  The coxswaining during the race was superb, leading the team to victory.  比赛中的舵手工作非常出色，带领队伍取得了胜利。| synonyms: steering, piloting, navigating | antonyms: drifting, meandering | noun

coxswain:/ˈkɒksən (UK), ˈkɑːksən (US)/| n. v. | 舵手；指挥划艇 | A person who steers a boat, especially the coxswain of a racing crew, or who has a similar function on an aircraft.  As a verb, it means to act as a coxswain. | He coxswained the eight-man crew to a new course record. 他指挥八人划艇队创造了新的赛道纪录. | synonyms: helmsman, steersman, pilot | antonyms: passenger, crew (general) | noun, verb
