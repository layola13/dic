neonomian:/ˌniːoʊˈnoʊmiən/ | n. | 新律法主义者；新律法主义拥护者 | A person who advocates or believes in neonomianism (a doctrine that emphasizes the role of the moral law in the Christian life, especially after justification). |  The neonomian argued that good works were necessary for salvation.  新律法主义者认为善行对于救赎是必要的。| Synonyms: legalist | Antonyms: antinomian | noun
