cultivatable:/ˈkʌltɪveɪtəbl/ |adj.|可耕种的，可培养的|Suitable for growing crops; able to be improved or developed.|The land is fertile and cultivatable. 这片土地肥沃，可以耕种。|Synonyms: arable, tillable, fertile, improvable, developable|Antonyms: barren, infertile, uncultivable, unimprovable|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From cultivate + -able.  Cultivate comes from the Latin *cultivare* "to till, cultivate," from *cultus* "care, cultivation," past participle of *colere* "to till, cultivate, inhabit, protect, worship."
