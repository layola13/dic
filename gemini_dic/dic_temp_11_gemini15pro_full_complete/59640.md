ecdysis: /ˈɛkdɪsɪs/ /ˈekdəsɪs/
noun
蜕皮; 脫皮
The periodic shedding of the cuticle in arthropods or the outer skin in reptiles.
The snake's ecdysis left its old skin draped on a branch.  蛇蜕皮后，旧皮挂在树枝上。

ecdysis: /ˈɛkdɪsɪs/ /ˈekdəsɪs/
noun
(比喻) 抛弃; 摆脱
The ecdysis of old habits.  摆脱旧习惯。
He underwent a personal ecdysis, shedding his former identity. 他经历了一次个人蜕变，摆脱了以前的 identity。


近义词: molting, shedding, sloughing
反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider) growth, development


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *ekdusis*, from *ekduein* "to take off, strip off," from *ek-* "out" + *duein* "to go into, put on."
