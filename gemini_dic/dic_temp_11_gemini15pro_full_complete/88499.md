historiographically:/ˌhɪstɔːriəˈɡræfɪkli/
adverb|史学地，从史学的角度|From the perspective of historiography; in a way that relates to the writing of history.|His work has been historiographically influential. 他的作品在史学方面颇具影响力。|
例句：The events were interpreted historiographically. 这些事件是从史学的角度来解读的。
例句：Historiographically speaking, this period has been neglected. 从史学角度来说，这一时期一直被忽视。
近义词：historically, academically|
反义词：(No direct antonym, but one could consider "ahistorically")|
词性: adverb

Etymology:
historiography (n.) + -ically (adv. suffix)
historiography: from French historiographie, from Greek historiographia "writing of history," from historia "history" (see history) + -graphia "writing," from graphein "to write" (see -graphy).
-ically: adverb-forming suffix, from Middle English -icalli, -ickli, from Old French and Late Latin -icalis, from Latin -icus + -alis (see -al (1)).
