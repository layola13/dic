cavillers:/ˈkævələr z/| n.|吹毛求疵的人，挑剔的人|A caviller is someone who raises petty objections or makes frivolous criticisms.|The proposal was met with resistance from a group of cavillers who found fault with every detail.|Synonyms: carpers, critics, nitpickers, faultfinders|Antonyms: admirers, approvers, praisers|noun

Etymology: From Middle French *cavileur*, from *caviler* "to mock, jest," from Latin *cavillārī* "to jest, banter, argue scoffingly."
