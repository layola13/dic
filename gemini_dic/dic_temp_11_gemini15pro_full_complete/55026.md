disqualify: /dɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪ/ (British) /dɪsˈkwɑːlɪfaɪ/ (American)
|v.|使…没有资格，取消…的资格|To make someone not eligible for something, usually because they have broken a rule or are not suitable.|He was disqualified from the competition for cheating. 他因作弊而被取消比赛资格。|Synonyms: bar, ban, preclude, prohibit|Antonyms: qualify, enable, authorize|verb
disqualification: /dɪsˌkwɒlɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/ (British) /dɪsˌkwɑːlɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/ (American)
|n.|取消资格，不合格|The act of disqualifying someone or something, or the state of being disqualified.|His disqualification from the race was a huge disappointment. 他被取消比赛资格是一个巨大的失望。|Synonyms: ineligibility, rejection, exclusion|Antonyms: qualification, eligibility, acceptance|noun
