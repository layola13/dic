odontophore:/ˈɒdən.tə.fɔːr/ (US) /ˈəʊ.dɒn.tə.fɔːr/ (UK)| n. |齿舌|A ribbon-like structure found in the mouths of most mollusks, except bivalves, used for feeding. It is covered in teeth and is used for scraping, tearing, or cutting food.|The snail extended its odontophore to graze on the algae.  蜗牛伸出齿舌啃食藻类。|Synonym: radula|Antonym: N/A|noun
