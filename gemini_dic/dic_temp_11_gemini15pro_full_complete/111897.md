logrolling:/ˈlɔːɡˌroʊlɪŋ/ | n. | 互相吹捧；相互支持；政治交易 | The practice of exchanging favors, especially in politics by reciprocal voting for each other's proposed legislation. |  The two senators engaged in logrolling to get their respective bills passed. 这两位参议员互相吹捧，以使各自的法案获得通过。

| Example: The politicians were accused of logrolling to ensure their pet projects were funded. 这些政客被指控互相勾结，以确保他们各自的项目获得资助。 | Synonym: back-scratching, quid pro quo, horse-trading | Antonym: impartiality, fairness | noun


logrolling:/ˈlɔːɡˌroʊlɪŋ/ | n. |  滚圆木；(尤指在水上)玩圆木 | The sport or act of balancing on and rolling a floating log, especially as a competitive activity. |  Logrolling was a popular pastime in lumber camps. 滚圆木是伐木场里一项很受欢迎的娱乐活动。

| Example: He demonstrated his impressive logrolling skills at the county fair.  他在县集市上展示了他令人印象深刻的滚圆木技巧。 | Synonym: birling | Antonym: N/A | noun
