demurrage:/dɪˈmɜːrɪdʒ/ | n. |滞期费；延滞费|A charge payable to the owner of a chartered ship on failure to load or discharge the ship within the time agreed.|The charterer had to pay a substantial demurrage charge for the delay.|Synonyms: detention charge, penalty, fine|Antonyms: dispatch money, premium|noun


demurrage:/dɪˈmɜːrɪdʒ/ | n. |逾期费；延误费|A charge payable for the detention of a truck, railroad car, or other cargo conveyance beyond the time allowed for loading, unloading, or both.|The trucking company charged demurrage for the two days the container sat unloaded.|Synonyms: late fee, penalty charge|Antonyms: expedited delivery bonus|noun
