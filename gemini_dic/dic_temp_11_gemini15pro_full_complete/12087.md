artiodactyl:/ɑːtɪoʊˈdæktɪl/| n. |偶蹄动物|An even-toed ungulate mammal.  Artiodactyls are characterized by having an even number of toes (two or four) on each foot, and most species have specialized digestive systems adapted for herbivory.|Cows, sheep, and deer are all artiodactyls.|Synonyms: even-toed ungulate|Antonyms: perissodactyl (odd-toed ungulate)|Noun
