babysit:/ˈbeɪbisɪt/ (US) /ˈbeɪbisɪt/ (UK)| v. n.|照顾婴儿；临时照看小孩|To care for a child or children while the parents are away, usually for a fee.| I babysit my neighbor's kids every Friday night. 我每个周五晚上都照顾邻居家的孩子。| She offered to babysit so we could go out for dinner. 她主动提出帮忙照看孩子，这样我们可以出去吃晚饭。|近义词：childmind, care for, tend|反义词: neglect|词性: verb, noun

babysitter:/ˈbeɪbisɪtər/ (US) /ˈbeɪbisɪtər/ (UK)| n.|临时保姆|A person who babysits.| We hired a babysitter for the evening. 我们晚上雇了一个临时保姆。| My sister is a reliable babysitter. 我妹妹是一个可靠的临时保姆。|近义词：childminder, caretaker|反义词: parent|词性: noun

词源: baby + sit.  The verb form emerged in the 1930s, formed by combining "baby" with "sit".  The noun "babysitter" followed soon after.
