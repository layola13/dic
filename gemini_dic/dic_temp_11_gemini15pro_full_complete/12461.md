asonant:/ˈæsənənt/| adj.|谐音的，assonance的形容词形式|Characterized by assonance, the repetition of vowel sounds in nearby words.|The poet used asonant words like "tide" and "hide" to create a sense of melancholy.|近义词：rhyming, harmonious, euphonious|反义词：dissonant, cacophonous|词性:adjective

Etymology:
From Late Latin *assonans, present participle of assonare "to respond," from ad- "to" + sonare "to sound."  The noun "assonance" entered English in the 16th century, and the adjective "asonant" followed in the 17th century.
