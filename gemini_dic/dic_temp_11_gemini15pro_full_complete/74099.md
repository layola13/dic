fosters:/ˈfɒstəz/ (British) /ˈfɑːstərz/ (American)| v.|促进，培养，鼓励，代养，收养|encourages or promotes the development or growth of something; to bring up (a child that is not one's own by birth)|例句：A good teacher fosters creativity in their students.  一位好老师会培养学生的创造力。

The organization fosters international cooperation. 这个组织促进了国际合作。|近义词：encourages, promotes, cultivates, nurtures, supports, raises, rears|反义词：hinders, discourages, neglects, suppresses|词性:动词

fosters (as a noun - less common): /ˈfɒstəz/ (British) /ˈfɑːstərz/ (American)| n.|代养者，养父母|People who foster children|例句：The fosters provided a loving home for the children.  养父母为孩子们提供了一个充满爱的家。

The child thrived with her fosters.  这个孩子在养父母的照顾下茁壮成长。|近义词：foster parents, caregivers|反义词：biological parents|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fostren*, from Old English *fōstrian* (“to nourish, support, bring up”), from *fōstor* (“nourishment, food, sustenance”), from Proto-Germanic *fōstrą* (“food, nourishment”), from Proto-Indo-European *peh₂- (“to protect, shepherd”). Cognate with Dutch *voesteren* (“to foster”), German *fördern* (“to promote, foster”), Old Norse *fóstra* (“to bring up, educate”).
