disobedience:/ˌdɪsɒˈbiːdiəns/ /ˌdɪsəˈbiːdiəns/| n. |不服从，违抗|The act of disobeying; failure or refusal to obey rules or commands. |His disobedience led to his expulsion from school. 他的不服从导致他被学校开除。|Synonyms: defiance, insubordination, rebellion, noncompliance |Antonyms: obedience, compliance, submission|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French desobeissance, from desobeissant (disobedient), from des- (dis-) + obeissant (obeying), from obeir (to obey), from Latin obedire, from ob- (towards) + audire (to hear, listen).
