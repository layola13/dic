n.|高品位矿石窃贼；偷取高品位矿石的人；精选矿石者|A person who steals high-grade ore from a mine or other source.  A person who sorts through ore to select the pieces with the highest concentration of valuable minerals.|The high-graders were caught trying to smuggle gold out of the mine. 高品位矿石窃贼试图将黄金偷运出矿山时被抓获。

high-graders:/ˈhaɪˌɡreɪdərz/
n.|高品位矿石窃贼；偷取高品位矿石的人；精选矿石者|Someone who picks out the best parts of something, often leaving the rest behind.|The high-graders picked through the pile of gemstones, taking only the largest and most valuable.  这些精选矿石者在成堆的宝石中仔细挑选，只拿走最大最值钱的。

high-grader:/ˈhaɪˌɡreɪdər/
n.|高分学生|A student who consistently receives high grades.|She was a high-grader throughout high school.  她在整个高中阶段都是一名高分学生。


Etymology:  "high-grade" (adjective meaning of superior quality) + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person who performs an action).  The term originated in the mining industry, referring to those who stole the most valuable ore.

Synonyms:  ore thief, pilferer, selector (in the context of picking the best parts)
Antonyms: low-grader (rarely used), discarder
