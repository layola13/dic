ingratiate:/ɪnˈɡreɪʃieɪt/ |v. |讨好，迎合 |To bring oneself into favor with someone by flattering or trying to please them. |He tried to ingratiate himself with the boss by offering to work late. 他试图通过主动加班来讨好老板。

|Synonyms: flatter, charm, win over, curry favor with|Antonyms: alienate, offend, repulse|verb|

ingratiate:/ɪnˈɡreɪʃieɪt/ |v. |使…受欢迎；使…讨人喜欢 | To establish (someone) in the favor or good graces of someone else. (Often used in passive form with "with") |She quickly ingratiated herself with her new colleagues. 她很快就赢得了新同事的喜爱。

|Synonyms: endear, commend|Antonyms: estrange, alienate|verb|
