encephalitogenicity:/ɛnˌsefəˌlaɪtəˌdʒɛnɪˈsɪti/
n.
脑炎原性
The capacity of a substance to cause encephalitis (inflammation of the brain).  This refers to the ability of a substance, such as a virus, bacteria, or chemical, to induce inflammation in the brain.
The encephalitogenicity of the virus was tested in mice.  这种病毒的脑炎原性在小鼠身上进行了测试。
neurovirulence, neurotoxicity
non-encephalitogenic (though this is rarely used)
noun
