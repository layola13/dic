competency:/ˈkɒmpɪtənsi/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmpɪtənsi/ (US)| n.|能力，胜任，称职|(Often interchangeable with competence)  The quality of being competent; adequacy; possession of required skill, knowledge, qualification, or capacity.|He reached a level of competency in French that allowed him to live and work in Paris. 他的法语达到了一定的水平，使他能够在巴黎生活和工作。|近义词：ability, capability, skill, expertise, proficiency|反义词：incompetence, inability, inadequacy|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin competentia "suitableness, agreement," from Latin competens (genitive competentis), present participle of competere "to coincide, agree, be suitable, come together," from com- "together" (see com-) + petere "to strive for, seek, attack" (from PIE root *pet- "to rush, to fly").
