ctenoidei:/ˌtɛnɔɪˈdiːaɪ/
n.
栉鳞鱼类
A large order of bony fishes characterized by having ctenoid scales, which are thin, flexible, and overlapping, with comb-like or toothed posterior margins. These fish are found in a wide range of aquatic habitats, including freshwater and marine environments.
Ctenoidei are a diverse group of fish, including many commercially important species such as perch, bass, and snapper.
近义词: spiny-rayed fishes
词性: noun
