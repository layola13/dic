make-tracks:/ˈmeɪk træks/| idiom | 离开；迅速走开；逃走 | To leave a place quickly. | We'd better make tracks before the storm hits. 我们最好在暴风雨来临之前离开。


He made tracks as soon as he realized the police were after him.  他意识到警察在追他后，就赶紧逃跑了。


Synonym: leave, depart, clear out, run away, escape, flee, bolt, vamoose


Antonym: arrive, stay, remain


Etymology:  Originates from the mid-19th century, referring to the tracks left by animals or vehicles.  The idea is to leave quickly, so as to cover one's tracks.
