ETYMOLOGY->
From the character "Ambrose Philips," a writer ridiculed by Henry Carey in a satirical ballad.  Carey mocked Philips's sentimental children's poems, using the nickname "Namby-Pamby," a childish alteration of "Ambrose." The term then became generalized to describe anyone or anything perceived as excessively sentimental or weak.
