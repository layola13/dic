john-bullishness:/ˈdʒɒn ˈbʊlɪʃnəs/ | n. | 英国式的固执自信; 盲目乐观;  |  A quality of aggressive optimism, stubborn determination, and unwavering belief in one's own country (specifically Britain), often perceived as arrogant or insensitive by others. It stems from the national personification of John Bull. | He displayed a characteristic john-bullishness in his dealings with the foreign diplomats. 他在与外国外交官打交道时表现出典型的英国式固执自信。 |Synonyms:  jingoism, chauvinism, patriotism, nationalism, optimism | Antonyms: pessimism, defeatism, humility, diffidence | noun
