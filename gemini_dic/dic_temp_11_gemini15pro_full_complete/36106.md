coerce:/koʊˈɜːrs/ (US), /kəʊˈɜːs/ (UK)| v. |强迫，胁迫|To persuade (an unwilling person) to do something by using force or threats.|He was coerced into signing the confession.|Synonyms: compel, force, pressure, intimidate|Antonyms: persuade, encourage|verb

coerce:/koʊˈɜːrs/ (US), /kəʊˈɜːs/ (UK)| n. |(罕用)强迫，胁迫（名词形式较少使用）|The act of coercing someone.|The confession was obtained under duress and coerce.|Synonyms: compulsion, force, pressure, intimidation|Antonyms: persuasion, encouragement|noun
