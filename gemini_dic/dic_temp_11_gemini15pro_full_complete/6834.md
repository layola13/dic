anastomotic:/ˌænəstəˈmɒtɪk/ /ˌænəstəˈmɑːtɪk/ | adj. |吻合的；连接的|Relating to or characterized by anastomosis, a connection between two normally separate structures, such as blood vessels or other tubular structures.|The surgeon performed an anastomotic procedure to connect the two sections of the intestine.|Synonyms: connected, joined, linked|Antonyms: separated, disconnected|adjective

anastomose:/əˈnæstəˌmoʊz/ /əˈnæstəˌməʊs/ | v. |吻合；连接|To connect or join by anastomosis, forming a network or system of interconnected channels.|The veins anastomose with each other to form a complex network.|Synonyms: join, connect, link, unite|Antonyms: separate, divide, sever|verb

anastomosis:/əˌnæstəˈmoʊsɪs/ /əˌnæstəˈməʊsɪs/ | n. |吻合；连接|A connection or opening between two things (especially cavities or passages) that are normally diverging or branching, such as between blood vessels, leaf veins, or streams.|The surgeon created an anastomosis between the two arteries.|Synonyms: junction, connection, union, link|Antonyms: separation, division, severance|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *anastōmōsis*, from Ancient Greek ἀναστόμωσις (anastómōsis, “outlet, opening”), from ἀναστομόω (anastomóō, “to furnish with a mouth, to open”), from ἀνά (aná, “up, upon”) + στόμα (stóma, “mouth”).
