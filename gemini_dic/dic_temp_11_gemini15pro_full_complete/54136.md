disenfranchisement:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
|n.|剥夺公民权；剥夺权利|The systematic disenfranchisement of black voters was a major issue during the Jim Crow era.  在吉姆·克劳时代，对黑人选民的系统性剥夺公民权是一个主要问题。|The disenfranchisement of the working class led to social unrest. 工人阶级的权利被剥夺导致了社会动荡。|近义词：deprivation, dispossession|反义词：enfranchisement, empowerment|词性:名词


disenfranchisements:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənts/
|n.|剥夺公民权；剥夺权利（复数）|The disenfranchisements of various minority groups throughout history have had lasting consequences. 历史上对各个少数群体的剥夺公民权都产生了持久的影响。|  Multiple disenfranchisements can lead to a sense of powerlessness and marginalization. 多重权利被剥夺会导致无力感和边缘化。|近义词：deprivations, dispossessions|反义词：enfranchisements, empowerments|词性:名词


词源:

From dis- + enfranchisement,  from en- + franchise + -ment.  "Franchise" ultimately comes from the Frankish word "franko," meaning "free," reflecting the original connection between citizenship and freedom.
