abdal: /ˈæbdɑːl/ | n. | 阿卜达尔（伊斯兰教苏菲派神秘主义者）| A Sufi mystic, especially in North Africa.  A holy man believed to possess supernatural powers (baraka) and considered a saint. Often associated with itinerant lifestyles and unconventional religious practices.  Derived from the Arabic word "badal" meaning "substitute" or "replacement," suggesting they are God's representatives on earth. |  例句：The abdal wandered the desert, seeking enlightenment.  阿卜达尔在沙漠中游荡，寻求启迪。

例句：The villagers revered the abdal for his wisdom and piety. 村民们崇敬这位阿卜达尔的智慧和虔诚。 | 近义词：dervish, fakir, mystic, saint | 反义词: unbeliever, skeptic | 词性: noun
