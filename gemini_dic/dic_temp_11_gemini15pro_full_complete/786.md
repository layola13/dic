absorbedness's:/əbˈzɔːrbdnɪsɪz/
|n.|全神贯注的状态|The state of being completely engrossed or preoccupied; the quality of being absorbed. |Her absorbedness's in the book was so complete she didn't hear the phone ring. 她如此全神贯注地看书，以至于没听到电话响。|近义词: preoccupation, engrossment, immersion |反义词: distraction, inattention|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From absorbed + -ness + -'s (possessive).  "Absorbed" comes from the Latin "absorbēre," meaning "to suck up" or "swallow."  "-ness" is a suffix that forms nouns denoting a state or quality.  "-'s" is the possessive marker.
