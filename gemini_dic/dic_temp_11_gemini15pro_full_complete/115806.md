mark-out:/ˈmɑːrk aʊt/ (US), /ˈmɑːk aʊt/ (UK)| v.|标出；规划；选定；<非正式>注定|To mark lines or boundaries on a surface, often to prepare for building or planting; to select or designate someone or something for a particular purpose or fate.|The builder marked out the foundations of the house.  建筑工人标出了房子的地基。|Synonyms: outline, delineate, designate, earmark|Antonyms: obscure, confuse|Verb

mark-out:/ˈmɑːrk aʊt/ (US), /ˈmɑːk aʊt/ (UK)| n.|划线；规划；<英，非正式>明显的成功|The act of marking something out, or the lines or boundaries that are marked; a clear success.|The mark-out for the new parking lot was clearly visible. 新停车场的划线清晰可见。|Synonyms: delineation, designation, layout|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
