croconate: /ˈkroʊkəneɪt/
noun
 кроконат (Russian),  Croconato (Italian),  Croconato (Spanish),  Croconate (German),  Croconate (French)
a salt or ester of croconic acid.  Croconic acid is a cyclic organic acid with the formula C5H2O5 or (C=O)5H2.  It is named after saffron (Crocus sativus), which contains a yellow pigment related to croconic acid.
Potassium croconate is a yellow crystalline solid.  It is used in analytical chemistry to detect certain metal ions.
Potassium croconate reacts with lead nitrate to form a yellow precipitate.
Croconate salts can be used as electrochemical materials.
