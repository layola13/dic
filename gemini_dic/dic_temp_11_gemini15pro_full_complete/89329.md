homogenizes:/həˈmɒdʒənaɪzɪz/, /həˈmɑːdʒənaɪzɪz/ | v. | 使均匀，使均质化 | to make something uniform or consistent throughout | The milk is homogenized to prevent the cream from separating. 牛奶经过均质化处理以防止奶油分离。| blends, mixes, integrates, standardizes | differentiates, separates, distinguishes | verb

homogenize:/həˈmɒdʒənaɪz/, /həˈmɑːdʒənaɪz/ | v. | 使均匀，使均质化 | to make something uniform or consistent throughout |  Modern society tends to homogenize culture. 现代社会趋向于使文化同质化。| blends, mixes, integrates, standardizes | differentiates, separates, distinguishes | verb


Etymology:
From French *homogénéiser*, from *homogène* (“homogeneous”) + *-iser* (suffix forming verbs).  *Homogène* comes from Ancient Greek *ὁμογενής* (homogenḗs, “of the same kind”), from *ὁμός* (homós, “same”) + *γένος* (génos, “kind, race”).
