high-up:/ˈhaɪ ʌp/| adj. n.|高层的，高位的；高层人士|A high-up person or position is one that is important and involves having control over other people.  A high-up place is literally a high place.|The CEO is a high-up in the company. 首席执行官是公司的高层。|近义词：senior, top-ranking, important, influential|反义词:low-level, junior, subordinate|词性:形容词, 名词
ETYMOLOGY->
From "high" (Old English *hēah) + "up" (Old English up, uppe).  The combined form emphasizes the elevated position, both literally and figuratively.
