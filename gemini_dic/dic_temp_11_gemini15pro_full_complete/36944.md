coloured:/ˈkʌləd/ (UK) /ˈkʌlərd/ (US)| adj.|有色的，彩色的, (尤指人)非白种人的|Having a colour or colours; not black and white.  Often used in the past to refer to people who are not white, especially people who have brown or black skin.  This use is now considered offensive in many contexts.|例句：She wore a brightly coloured dress. 她穿着一件颜色鲜艳的连衣裙。

例句：He grew up in a coloured neighbourhood. (offensive in many contexts) 他在一个有色人种社区长大。（在很多语境下具有冒犯性）|近义词：colorful, pigmented, dyed, tinted, stained (for objects); non-white, Black, Asian, etc. (for people - use with extreme caution and sensitivity)|反义词：colourless, uncoloured, monochrome (for objects); white (for people - use with extreme caution and sensitivity)|词性:形容词
