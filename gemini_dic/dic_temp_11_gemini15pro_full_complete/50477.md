depressing:/dɪˈprɛsɪŋ/| adj.|令人沮丧的，压抑的|Causing or tending to cause sadness and gloom.|The news was so depressing that I cried.|Synonyms: disheartening, discouraging, saddening, dispiriting|Antonyms: uplifting, encouraging, cheering, exhilarating|adjective

Etymology: From Late Middle English depressen, from Old French depresser, from Latin depressare (“to press down, lower, sink”), ultimately from de- (“down”) + premere (“to press”).  The "-ing" suffix transforms the verb into a present participle, which functions as an adjective.
