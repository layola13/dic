introduced: /ˌɪntrəˈdjuːst/ (US) /ˌɪntrəˈdʒuːst/ (UK)
|v. | 介绍，引进，提出，采用 | to make (someone) known to another person by name and often with some information about the person being introduced; to bring something new into use or fashion; to bring something forward for discussion or consideration; to bring (a bill or resolution) into a legislative body |
例句：
He introduced me to his wife.
他把我介绍给他的妻子。

例句：
The company introduced a new product line.
该公司推出了一条新的产品线。

近义词：present, acquaint, launch, implement, propose
反义词：remove, withdraw, discontinue
词性：动词


introduced: /ˌɪntrəˈdjuːst/ (US) /ˌɪntrəˈdʒuːst/ (UK)
|adj. | 被引进的，外来的 | brought in from another place or country |
例句：
The introduced species disrupted the local ecosystem.
引进的物种扰乱了当地的生态系统。

例句：
The introduced changes were met with resistance.
引入的变革遭到了抵制。

近义词：foreign, non-native, exotic
反义词：native, indigenous
词性：形容词


词源: Late Middle English: past participle of introducen, from Latin introducere ‘lead in’, from intro- ‘inwards’ + ducere ‘to lead’.
