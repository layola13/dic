nickel-and-dimed:/ˈnɪkəl ænd daɪmd/ | adj. |  靠微薄收入生活的；锱铢必较的 |  Refers to a situation of being paid very low wages or having to work extremely hard for little money, often involving menial or tedious tasks. It also implies being very frugal or stingy with money.  Derived from "nickel" (a five-cent coin) and "dimed" (a ten-cent coin), emphasizing small amounts of money. |  She felt nickel-and-dimed by her employer, constantly having to account for every penny. 她觉得她的雇主对她锱铢必较，总是让她对每一分钱都交代清楚。|  She worked a nickel-and-dimed job just to make ends meet. 她做了一份收入微薄的工作，只为了维持生计。 | Synonyms:  poorly paid, low-wage, underpaid, exploited | Antonyms:  well-paid, affluent, wealthy | adjective
