manicheism:/ˈmænɪkiːɪzəm/ | n. |摩尼教, 二元论; |A religious or philosophical system based on the belief in two opposing principles, one good and one evil. Also, the tendency to view things in terms of absolute good and evil, or black and white. |例句：His worldview reflected a stark Manicheism, dividing everything into categories of pure good and utter evil. 他的世界观反映了一种鲜明的二元论，将一切都划分为纯粹的善与恶。

例句：The political rhetoric often descends into Manicheism, portraying one side as angelic and the other as demonic. 政治言论常常沦为二元论，将一方描绘成天使，另一方描绘成恶魔。|近义词：dualism, absolutism |反义词:holism, relativism |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Manichaeismus*, from Greek *Mani* (founder of the religion) + *-ismos* (suffix forming nouns of action or condition). Ultimately from Aramaic *Mānī*, meaning "Mani".
