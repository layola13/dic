knots:/nɒts/ |/nɑːts/| n.|结; 难事; 速度单位(节);(树木的)节疤|A knot is a fastening made by looping a piece of string, rope, cloth, etc. on itself and tightening it.  Knots can also refer to a difficult situation or problem, a unit of speed used by ships and aircraft (nautical miles per hour), or a hard lump in wood where a branch once grew.|The sailor tied a knot in the rope. 水手在绳子上打了个结。

The traffic was tied up in knots. 交通拥堵不堪。

The ship was traveling at 20 knots. 船以20节的速度航行。

The wood was full of knots. 木头里全是节疤。|近义词：tangles,  hitches, lumps,  snags, difficulties, problems|反义词: solutions,  smoothness (referring to wood)|词性:名词

knot:/nɒt/ |/nɑːt/| v.|打结; (使)纠结|To tie or fasten something with a knot.  To become tangled or intertwined.|He knotted the rope securely. 他把绳子牢牢地系住。

Her stomach knotted with anxiety. 她焦虑得胃都打结了。|近义词：tie,  fasten,  bind,  tangle|反义词: untie,  unravel, loosen|词性:动词
