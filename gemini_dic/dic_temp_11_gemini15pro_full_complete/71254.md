five-and-dime: /ˈfaɪv ən ˈdaɪm/ | n. | 五分一角店；廉价商店 | A type of store that used to be common in the United States and Canada, selling a variety of inexpensive items, originally priced at five or ten cents.  These stores no longer exist in their original form due to inflation. |  My grandmother used to love browsing the five-and-dime for trinkets.  我祖母以前喜欢在五分一角店里浏览小饰品。

| Example 2: Five-and-dime stores were a fixture of Main Street America. 五分一角店曾是美国主要街道的一道风景线。 | Synonym: variety store, general store | Antonym:  department store, boutique | noun
