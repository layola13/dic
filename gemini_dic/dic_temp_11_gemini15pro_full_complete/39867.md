constative:/ˈkɒnstətɪv/ /ˈkɑːnstətɪv/| adj.|陈述的，描述事实的|Describing or asserting a state of affairs; relating to a statement that can be judged true or false.|例句： "The cat sat on the mat" is a constative sentence. “猫坐在垫子上”是一个陈述句。|近义词：descriptive, declarative, assertive, factual|反义词：performative, non-declarative|词性:形容词

constative:/ˈkɒnstətɪv/ /ˈkɑːnstətɪv/| n.|陈述句|A statement that describes or asserts a state of affairs, and is therefore capable of being true or false.|例句：  J. L. Austin distinguished between performatives and constatives. J. L. 奥斯汀区分了施为句和陈述句。|近义词：statement, assertion, declaration|反义词：performative|词性:名词
