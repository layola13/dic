contusion:/kənˈtjuːʒən/ /kənˈtuːʒn/| n. | 瘀伤，挫伤 | An injury to the body caused by a blunt blow, resulting in discoloration of the skin but no break in the skin. |  She had a nasty contusion on her leg after falling down the stairs. 她从楼梯上摔下来，腿上有一处严重的瘀伤。

contusion:/kənˈtjuːʒən/ /kənˈtuːʒn/| n. | 精神打击 | A feeling of being upset and confused, especially as a result of a shock or a disappointment. | The news of his death came as a terrible contusion. 他去世的消息对他来说是一个沉重的打击。


Etymology: From Latin *contusio*, from contusus, past participle of contundere "to bruise, beat, crush," from com- "together" (see com-) + tundere "to beat".

Synonyms: bruise, injury, lesion, trauma, wound, discoloration

Antonyms: healing, recovery, wellness
