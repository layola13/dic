foxglove:/ˈfɒksɡlʌv/ (UK) /ˈfɑːksɡlʌv/ (US)| n. |毛地黄，洋地黄|A tall, slender plant with clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically purplish or white, and poisonous leaves. It is a source of digitalis, a drug used to treat heart conditions.|The foxglove bloomed beautifully in the garden. 毛地黄在花园里开得很漂亮。|The hillside was covered with foxgloves. 山坡上长满了毛地黄。|Synonyms: digitalis, fairy gloves, fairy bells|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
