buhlwork: /ˈbuːlˌwɜːrk/ (US), /ˈbuːlˌwɜːk/ (UK)| n.|镶嵌细工；金属镶嵌工艺；以镶嵌细工装饰的家具| Decorative work in which pieces of brass, tortoiseshell, or other materials are inlaid into wood, usually forming elaborate patterns.  It's often seen in furniture and decorative boxes.| The antique desk was adorned with intricate buhlwork. 这张古董桌子装饰着复杂的镶嵌细工。|inlay, marquetry| |noun
Etymology-> From the French *boule*, meaning "knob" or "ball," later referring to André-Charles Boulle (1642-1732), a French cabinetmaker who popularized this technique.
