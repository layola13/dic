hob:/hɒb/(UK) /hɑːb/(US)| n.|炉盘，灶台，淘气鬼，妖怪|A flat metal surface on top of a cooker, used for heating pans. Also, a mischievous sprite or goblin.|例句：She put the kettle on the hob. 她把水壶放在炉盘上。

例句：The hobgoblin lurked in the shadows. 那小妖精潜伏在阴影中。|近义词：cooktop, burner, imp, goblin|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


hob:/hɒb/(UK) /hɑːb/(US)| v.|与…密切交往，过…样的生活|To socialize with or spend time with (someone).|例句：He hobbed with the rich and famous. 他与富人和名人密切交往。


例句：She doesn't hob with her neighbours much. 她不怎么和邻居们来往。|近义词：socialize, mingle, consort|反义词:isolate|词性:verb

词源：
From Middle English *hob, hobbe (“clown, rustic, elf”), of uncertain origin. Perhaps related to Rob, a diminutive of Robert. Compare Hobgoblin.  The meaning "part of a fireplace" is first attested in 1557.


注意事项:
hob作为名词，指的是炉盘或灶台，也可以指淘气鬼或妖精。作为动词，指的是与…密切交往。需要注意区分其不同含义和用法。
