cajolements:/kəˈdʒoʊlmənts/
|n.|劝诱；哄骗；甜言蜜语|The child responded to his mother's cajolements and ate his vegetables. 孩子在他的母亲的哄骗下吃了蔬菜。|例句：He used cajolements and flattery to get what he wanted. 他用甜言蜜语和奉承来达到他的目的。

|例句：Her cajolements finally persuaded him to join them for dinner. 她的劝诱最终说服了他和他们一起吃晚饭。
|近义词：persuasion, coaxing, blandishments, flattery|反义词：threats, coercion, intimidation|词性: 名词(plural noun)

Etymology:
From the verb "cajole," which comes from the French word "cajoler," meaning "to chatter like a jay," from "cajol," meaning "jay." The original meaning suggested idle chatter, but the word evolved to mean persuasive talk, often with flattery.
