cottonmouths:/ˈkɑːtnˌmaʊðz/ /ˈkɒtnˌmaʊθz/
n.
棉口蛇（蝮蛇的一种）;胆怯，怯场
A venomous semiaquatic pit viper (Agkistrodon piscivorus) of the southeastern U.S., having a whitish interior of the mouth.  Also refers to a feeling of dryness in the mouth, often due to nervousness.
例句：The swamp was teeming with cottonmouths.  沼泽里到处都是棉口蛇。

例句：I get the cottonmouths every time I have to speak in public. 每次我要在公共场合讲话时，我都会感到口干舌燥。
近义词：water moccasin, copperhead (for the snake); stage fright, anxiety (for the nervousness)
反义词：(No direct antonym for the snake)  confidence, composure (for the nervousness)
词性:名词
