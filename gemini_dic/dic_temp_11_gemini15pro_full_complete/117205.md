measures:/ˈmeʒərz/| n.|措施，方法；尺寸；程度；计量单位|A series of actions taken to achieve a particular purpose or effect.  一系列为达到特定目的或效果而采取的行动。|The government has announced new measures to combat inflation. 政府已经宣布了对抗通货膨胀的新措施。|Synonyms: steps, actions, procedures, methods|Antonyms: inaction, passivity|Noun

measure:/ˈmeʒər/| v.|测量；估量；权衡|To determine the size, amount, or degree of something. 确定某物的大小、数量或程度。|She measured the length of the room. 她测量了房间的长度。|Synonyms: gauge, calculate, assess|Antonyms: guess, estimate|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mesure* (French *mesure*), from Latin *mēnsūra* ("measure, measuring, measurement, extent, size, limit, rule"), from *mēnsus*, past participle of *mētiri* ("to measure").  源自古法语 *mesure*（法语 *mesure*），来自拉丁语 *mēnsūra*（“测量、测量、测量、范围、大小、限制、规则”），来自 *mēnsus*，*mētiri*（“测量”）的过去分词。
