cutout:/ˈkʌtaʊt/| n.|镂空装饰; 剪纸; 切口; (电路)断路器; (引擎等的)自动关闭装置|A shape cut out of paper or other material, or a hole made by cutting something out.|例句：She decorated the windows with paper cutouts. 她用剪纸装饰窗户。

例句：The dress had attractive cutouts at the shoulders. 这件衣服在肩膀处有漂亮的镂空设计。|近义词：silhouette, shape, stencil, template, pattern, hole, gap, opening|反义词: solid, whole|词性:noun


cutout:/ˈkʌtaʊt/| adj.|剪纸的；镂空的|Made by cutting a shape out of something.|例句：Cutout figures decorated the nursery walls.  剪纸图案装饰了育儿室的墙壁。

例句：The artist specialized in cutout animations. 这位艺术家专攻剪纸动画。|近义词：perforated, pierced, openwork|反义词: solid, uncut|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "cut out," the past participle of the verb "cut," meaning to divide or separate with a sharp-edged tool. The noun form originated in the late 19th century.
