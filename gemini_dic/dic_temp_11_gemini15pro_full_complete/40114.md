contempt:/kənˈtempt/ | n. | 轻蔑，蔑视，耻辱 | a feeling that someone or something is not important and deserves no respect |  He looked at her with contempt. 他轻蔑地看着她。 | synonyms: disdain, scorn, disrespect | antonyms: respect, admiration | noun

contemn:/kənˈtem/ | vt. | 鄙视，蔑视 | to consider someone or something as not important and deserving no respect |  She contemned their hypocrisy. 她鄙视他们的虚伪。 | synonyms: despise, scorn, disdain | antonyms: admire, respect | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *contempt*, from Latin *contemptus*, past participle of *contemnere* "to despise, scorn, disdain," from *com-* (intensive prefix) + *temnere* "to slight, scorn."
