blockheadednesses:/ˌblɑːkˈhɛdɪdnɪsɪz/
| n. |愚蠢，迟钝|The state of being blockheaded; stupidity or obtuseness.|His blockheadednesses led him to make the same mistake repeatedly.  他屡次犯同样的错误，真是愚蠢。|近义词：stupidity, obtuseness, dullness, denseness|反义词：intelligence, cleverness, brightness, sharpness|词性:名词

blockheaded:/ˌblɑːkˈhɛdɪd/
| adj. |愚蠢的，迟钝的|Stupid or slow-witted.|He made a blockheaded decision to invest all his money in a single stock. 他把所有的钱都投资在一支股票上，真是个愚蠢的决定。|近义词：stupid, obtuse, dull, dense|反义词：intelligent, clever, bright, sharp|词性:形容词


词源:
blockhead (n.) - late 14c., "wooden head, figurehead," from block (n.1) + head (n.). Figurative sense of "stupid person" is from 1570s.
blockheaded (adj.) - "having a wooden head," hence "stupid," 1580s, from blockhead + -ed.
blockheadedness (n.) - "the state of being blockheaded," from blockheaded + -ness.
