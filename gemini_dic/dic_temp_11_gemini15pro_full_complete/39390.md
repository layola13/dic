conjurator:/ˈkɒndʒərər/ (UK) /ˈkɑːndʒərər/ (US)| n.|魔术师，巫师，召唤者|A person who performs magic tricks or pretends to have magical powers; someone who summons spirits or uses incantations.|The conjurator amazed the audience with his illusions. 魔术师用他的幻术使观众惊叹。|Synonyms: magician, sorcerer, wizard, enchanter|Antonyms: none|Noun

conjure:/ˈkʌndʒər/ (UK) /ˈkɑːndʒər/ (US)| vt.|变戏法，召唤，想象|To perform magic tricks; to summon (a spirit or devil) by incantation or other magic means; to cause (something) to appear or disappear as if by magic; to bring to mind.|He conjured a rabbit out of a hat. 他从帽子里变出一只兔子。  She conjured up images of her childhood. 她想起了她童年的景象。|Synonyms: evoke, summon, invoke, materialize|Antonyms: banish, dispel, exorcise|Verb

词源:
From Late Latin *conjurator, from Latin conjurare "to conspire, swear together, implore earnestly," from com- "together" + jurare "to swear."  The sense shifted from "one who casts spells" to "one who entertains by sleight of hand."
