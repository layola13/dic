intrenchant:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃənt/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；牢固确立的；不容易改变的 | Firmly established and difficult or unlikely to change; ingrained. |  The government's policies have become deeply intrenched over the last decade. 政府的政策在过去十年中已经根深蒂固。|  His views on the subject were so intrenched that he refused to listen to any opposing arguments. 他对这个问题的看法根深蒂固，以至于他拒绝听取任何反对意见。 | synonyms: ingrained, established, fixed, rooted, firm, entrenched | antonyms: flexible, adaptable, changeable, fluid | word type: adjective
