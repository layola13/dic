词源：
From hob or nob, a variant of *hab nab*, from *habben* (“to have”) + *nabben* (“to catch, to seize, to take”).  Originally meaning "to drink together," later "to associate familiarly."  The original sense survives in the Scottish phrase *hob and nob*, “to give and take, share and share alike."
