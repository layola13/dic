grovelers:/ˈɡrɒvələrz/ (US) /ˈɡrɒvələz/ (UK)| n. |卑躬屈膝的人；奴颜婢膝的人|People who grovel; those who behave in a subservient or fawning manner, often to gain favor or avoid punishment.  They abase themselves.|The king was surrounded by grovelers, all vying for his attention. 国王身边围绕着一群阿谀奉承的人，都争着引起他的注意。

The dictator expected his subjects to be grovelers. 独裁者希望他的臣民对他卑躬屈膝。|近义词：sycophants, flatterers, yes-men, toadies|反义词：rebels, dissenters|词性:noun
