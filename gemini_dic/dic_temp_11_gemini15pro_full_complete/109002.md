lieutenant:/luːˈtɛnənt/ /ljuːˈtɛnənt/| n.|中尉；副官；助理；代理人|A commissioned officer in the armed forces who ranks above a second lieutenant and below a captain.  A deputy or subordinate official.|例句：The lieutenant led his platoon into battle. 中尉带领他的排投入战斗。

例句：She served as the lieutenant to the CEO. 她担任CEO的助理。|近义词：officer, deputy, assistant|反义词:superior, commander|词性:名词

词源:
From Old French *luetenant "holding the place of another", present participle of loeir "allow, permit" (from Latin licēre) + tenant "holding", present participle of tenir "to hold" (from Latin tenēre). The word originally denoted a deputy or substitute, later developing the military sense.
