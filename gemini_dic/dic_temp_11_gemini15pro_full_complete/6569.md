anabaptist:/ˌænəˈbæptɪst/| n.|再洗礼派教徒；重浸礼派教徒|A member of a radical Protestant sect that arose in Europe in the 16th century, emphasizing adult baptism and rejecting infant baptism.  They also generally advocated pacifism, separation of church and state, and communal living.|例句：The Anabaptists were persecuted for their beliefs. 再洗礼派教徒因其信仰而受到迫害。

|例句：The Anabaptist movement spread rapidly throughout Europe. 再洗礼派运动迅速蔓延到整个欧洲。|近义词：Baptist, Mennonite|反义词：None|Noun
