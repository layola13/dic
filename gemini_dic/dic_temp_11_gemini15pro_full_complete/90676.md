humblingly:/ˈhʌmbli/ | adv. | 谦逊地，谦恭地 | In a way that shows you are not proud or arrogant. | She humblingly accepted the award, thanking her team for their support. 她谦逊地接受了奖项，感谢团队的支持。|  He humblingly admitted his mistake and apologized. 他谦恭地承认了自己的错误并道歉。 | 近义词: modestly, meekly, submissively | 反义词: arrogantly, proudly, haughtily | 词性: 副词

humble:/ˈhʌmbl/ | adj. | 谦逊的，谦虚的；简陋的，低下的 | Not proud or arrogant; having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance. |  He was a humble man, despite his great achievements. 尽管成就非凡，他仍然是一个谦虚的人。 |  They lived in a humble cottage in the countryside. 他们住在乡下一个简陋的小屋里。 | 近义词: modest, unassuming, meek | 反义词: arrogant, proud, haughty | 词性: 形容词

humble:/ˈhʌmbl/ | v. | 使谦卑，使感到卑微；降低…的身份 |  Lower (someone) in dignity or importance. | The defeat humbled the arrogant champion. 这次失败使傲慢的冠军变得谦逊了。 | The experience humbled him and made him realize his limitations. 这段经历使他感到卑微，并让他认识到自己的局限性。 | 近义词: humiliate, abase, subdue | 反义词: exalt, elevate, aggrandize | 词性: 动词


词源:  From Middle English *humblen, *humilien, from Old French humblier, from Latin humilis (“low, lowly, humble, near the ground”), from humus (“ground, earth”).
