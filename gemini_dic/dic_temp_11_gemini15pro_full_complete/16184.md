bankrupt: /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (US) /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (UK)| adj. n. vt. |破产的，破产者，使破产|Lacking money to pay debts; insolvent.  Unable to function normally or continue in business due to financial difficulties. |The company went bankrupt after the recession.  这家公司在经济衰退后破产了。|Synonyms: insolvent, broke, ruined, impoverished |Antonyms: solvent, affluent, prosperous| adjective

bankrupt: /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (US) /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (UK)|  n. |破产者|A person who is judged by a court to be unable to pay their debts. |He was declared a bankrupt after his business failed. 他在生意失败后被宣布破产。|Synonyms: debtor, insolvent |Antonyms: creditor, millionaire| noun


bankrupt: /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (US) /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (UK)| vt. |使破产|To make someone or something become bankrupt. |The lawsuit bankrupted him. 这场官司让他破产了。|Synonyms: ruin, impoverish, break |Antonyms: enrich, prosper | verb
