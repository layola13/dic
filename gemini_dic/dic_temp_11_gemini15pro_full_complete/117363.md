mediate:/ˈmiːdieɪt/ (US), /ˈmiːdieɪt/ (UK)| v.  n. adj.|调解，斡旋；居中；传达|to act as a negotiator between opposing sides in a dispute; to bring about (an agreement, a settlement, etc.) by intervening between parties; occupying a middle position|The community leader mediated the dispute between the two families. 社区领导调解了两个家庭之间的纠纷.|近义词：intervene, negotiate, arbitrate, conciliate|反义词:aggravate, escalate, incite|verb

mediate:/ˈmiːdieɪt/ (US), /ˈmiːdieɪt/ (UK)| adj.|间接的；居间的|being in a middle position; acting through or by means of an intervening agency; not direct or immediate|The teacher had a mediate influence on the student's decision. 老师对学生的决定有间接的影响。|近义词：indirect, intervening|反义词:direct, immediate|adjective

mediate:/ˈmiːdieɪt/ (US), /ˈmiːdieɪt/ (UK)| n.|媒介物|something that mediates; an intervening agency, means, or instrument|The internet serves as a mediate for communication. 互联网充当了沟通的媒介。|近义词：medium, intermediary, agent|反义词:None|noun

PHRASE->
mediate between = 在...之间调解  Mediate between the two sides. 在双方之间调解。
mediate a dispute = 调解纠纷  The council will mediate the dispute. 委员会将调解纠纷。
mediate a settlement = 调解和解  They are trying to mediate a settlement. 他们正试图调解和解。
mediate a compromise = 调解妥协  He mediated a compromise between the two parties. 他调解了双方之间的妥协。
mediate an agreement = 调解协议  The UN is mediating an agreement. 联合国正在调解一项协议。


词源：From Latin mediatus, past participle of mediare ("to be in the middle, to divide in half, to halve, to occupy the middle place"), from medius ("middle").
