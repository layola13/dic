cross-accent:/ˈkrɒsˌæksənt/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌæksent/ (UK)| n. |交叉重音|A rhythmic pattern in which the natural stress of the words conflicts with the metrical stress of the musical setting.|The composer used cross-accent to create a sense of unease. 作曲家使用交叉重音来营造一种不安的感觉。|synonym: syncopation, shifted accent |antonym: regular accent |noun


cross-accent:/ˈkrɒsˌæksənt/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌæksent/ (UK)| adj. |交叉重音的|Characterized by a rhythmic pattern in which the natural stress of the words conflicts with the metrical stress of the musical setting.|The song has a cross-accent rhythm that is difficult to sing. 这首歌的交叉重音节奏很难唱。|synonym: syncopated |antonym: regular, evenly accented |adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "cross-" (meaning "across" or "contrary") + "accent" (from Old French "accent," ultimately from Latin "accentus" meaning "song added to speech").
