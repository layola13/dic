gallivanted:/ˌɡælɪˈvæntɪd/ | v. | 逍遥；游荡；寻欢作乐 | To go around from one place to another in a lively, enjoyable way, especially looking for pleasure or amusement. |  He gallivanted around Europe for a month after he graduated. 他毕业后在欧洲游荡了一个月。|  She gallivanted from party to party, enjoying herself immensely. 她从一个派对到另一个派对，玩得不亦乐乎。 | 近义词：roam, wander, gad about | 反义词：stay put, remain | 词性：动词

词源:
From the French *gallivander*, of unknown origin.
