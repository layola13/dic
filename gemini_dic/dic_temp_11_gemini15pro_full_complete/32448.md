chimney-pot:/ˈtʃɪmni pɒt/ (UK) /ˈtʃɪmni pɑːt/ (US)| n. |烟囱帽，烟囱管|A cylindrical pipe of earthenware or metal placed on the top of a chimney to increase the draught and prevent smoke from being blown downwards.|The chimney-pot was damaged in the storm.  烟囱帽在暴风雨中受损了。|Synonyms: chimney can, chimney stack, chimney flue, chimney cowl |Antonyms: None|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->

From chimney + pot. Chimney comes from Old French cheminéee "fireplace," from Late Latin caminata "fireplace, forge," from Vulgar Latin caminus "furnace, oven." Pot comes from Old English pott, of uncertain origin.
