impugnabilities:/ɪmˌpʌɡnəˈbɪlɪtiz/| n. |不可 impugn 的特性；无可争辩性|The quality or state of being impugnable; unchallengeability. |The lawyer questioned the impugnabilities of the witness's testimony.|Synonyms: incontestability, irreproachability, invulnerability|Antonyms: contestability, vulnerability, disputability|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin impugnabilitas, from impugnabilis (impugnable) + -itas (-ity). Ultimately from Latin in- (in, into, on, upon, against) + pugnare (to fight).
