词源:
From Late Middle English *confeccioun*, from Old French confection, from Latin confectiō (“a making, preparing, composing”), from perfect passive participle confectus of conficiō (“I make completely, accomplish, finish, complete, perform, execute, bring about, cause, prepare, make ready, compose, put together, bring to an end, kill, destroy, consume, wear out, exhaust”), from con- (“together”) + faciō (“I make, I do”).

用法说明:

"Confectioning" is primarily used as a gerund or verbal noun, referring to the process of making confections (sweets or candies). It's less commonly used as a verb.  The more usual verb form is "to confection."
