amorphism:/əˈmɔːrfɪzəm/
n.
无定形; 非晶形; 无定形性
The lack of a clearly defined shape or form; the state of being amorphous.
Glass is a classic example of amorphism, lacking a regular crystalline structure.  玻璃是无定形的一个典型例子，它缺乏规则的晶体结构。
The artist embraced amorphism in his sculptures, creating abstract forms that defied easy categorization.  这位艺术家在他的雕塑中拥抱了无定形，创造出难以归类的抽象形式。
近义词:amorphousness, formlessness, shapelessness
反义词:crystallinity, structure, form
词性:noun

amorphous:/əˈmɔːrfəs/
adj.
无定形的; 非晶形的; 无定形体的
Lacking a clearly defined shape or form; not crystalline.
Amorphous solids, like glass, do not have a regular atomic arrangement.  像玻璃这样的无定形固体没有规则的原子排列。
The politician's amorphous platform lacked specific policy proposals.  这位政治家的无定形纲领缺乏具体的政策建议。
近义词:formless, shapeless, unstructured
反义词:crystalline, structured, definite
词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek  *ἄμορφος (ámorphos, “shapeless, formless”), from  *ἀ- (a-, “not, without”) +  *μορφή (morphḗ, “form, shape”).
