expropriationist: /ɛksˌproʊpriˈeɪʃənɪst/
noun
征收者；强占者
A person who advocates or practices expropriation, especially of private property by the state.
The expropriationist policies of the new regime led to widespread unrest.  新政权的强占政策导致了广泛的动荡。
近义词: confiscator, seizer
反义词: donor, giver
词性: n.

expropriate: /ɛksˈproʊprieɪt/
verb
征用；强占；没收
(especially of the government) to take property from its owner for public use, with or without payment.
The government expropriated the land for the construction of a new highway. 政府征用了这块土地来修建一条新的高速公路。
近义词: seize, confiscate, commandeer
反义词: return, give back
词性: v.

expropriation: /ɛksˌproʊpriˈeɪʃən/
noun
征用；强占；没收
The act of expropriating something.
The expropriation of land without compensation caused much hardship for the farmers.  未经赔偿的土地征用给农民带来了很多困难。
近义词: confiscation, seizure, appropriation
反义词: restitution, restoration
词性: n.
