corregidor:/ˌkɔːrɪˈhiːdɔːr/ (US), /kəˈrɛxɪdɔː(r)/ (UK)| n.| 地方行政长官；科雷希多岛|From Spanish *corregidor*, from *corregir* "to correct," from Latin *corrigere* "to straighten out, set right," from *com-* "together" + *regere* "to guide, rule."  Originally, a royal official sent to inspect and reform the administration of municipalities.  Later, the title came to signify the chief magistrate of a Spanish town. The island of Corregidor at the entrance to Manila Bay takes its name from the fact that it served as a checkpoint for ships entering the bay. |The corregidor of the town was a just and fair man. 该镇的地方行政长官是一位公正廉明的人。|The Battle of Corregidor was a pivotal moment in World War II. 科雷希多岛战役是二战中的关键时刻。| synonyms: magistrate, governor, administrator| antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps subject/citizen) |noun
