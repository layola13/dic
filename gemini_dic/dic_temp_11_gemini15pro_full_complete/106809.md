labiovelar:/ˌleɪbioʊˈviːlər/ | adj. | 唇软腭音的 |  A sound articulated using both the lips and the soft palate, such as [w] in English.  | Example: The sound /w/ is a labiovelar approximant. /w/音是一个唇软腭近音。 | Example:  Many West African languages have labiovelar consonants. 许多西非语言都有唇软腭辅音。| 近义词:  (No direct synonym, but related terms include: bilabial, velar) | 反义词: (No antonym) | 词性: adjective

labiovelar:/ˌleɪbioʊˈviːlər/ | n. | 唇软腭音 | A consonant articulated using both the lips and the soft palate, such as [w] in English. | Example: The labiovelar /w/ is often confused with the vowel /u/. 唇软腭音/w/经常与元音/u/混淆。 | Example: The language has a series of labiovelars.  这门语言有一系列唇软腭音。| 近义词: (No direct synonym, but related terms include: bilabial, velar) | 反义词: (No antonym) | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From *labio-* (combining form of *labium* "lip") + *velar* (relating to the velum or soft palate).  Ultimately from Latin *labium* "lip" and *velum* "sail, curtain, veil."
