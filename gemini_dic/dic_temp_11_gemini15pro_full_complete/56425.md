dollhouse:/ˈdɑːlˌhaʊs/ (US), /ˈdɒlˌhaʊs/ (UK)| n. |玩具屋；袖珍房屋模型|A small house built as a toy for children, often containing miniature furniture and dolls. |The little girl spent hours playing with her dollhouse. 小女孩花了几个小时玩她的玩具屋。|
dollhouse:/ˈdɑːlˌhaʊs/ (US), /ˈdɒlˌhaʊs/ (UK)| n. |比喻：狭小而封闭的世界|A metaphor for a small, confined world, often suggesting artificiality or control. |Their perfect suburban life felt like a dollhouse, too pristine and unreal. 他们完美的郊区生活感觉像个玩具屋，太原始和不真实了。|
dollhouse:/ˈdɑːlˌhaʊs/ (US), /ˈdɒlˌhaʊs/ (UK)| n. |(少见)妓院| (Rare) A brothel. |The police raided the suspected dollhouse. 警察突袭了疑似妓院的地方。|
