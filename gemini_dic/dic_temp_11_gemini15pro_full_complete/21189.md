blockade: /blɒˈkeɪd/ (British) /blɑːˈkeɪd/ (American)| n. vt.|封锁，阻塞|An act or means of sealing off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving.|The naval blockade prevented any supplies from reaching the besieged city. 海军封锁阻止了任何物资到达被围困的城市。|近义词：siege, barrier, obstruction|反义词: access, opening|词性:名词,动词

blockades: /blɒˈkeɪdz/ (British) /blɑːˈkeɪdz/ (American)| n.|封锁（复数）|The plural form of "blockade," referring to multiple instances of sealing off a place.|Several blockades were put in place during the war. 战争期间实施了几次封锁。|近义词：sieges, barriers, obstructions|反义词: accesses, openings|词性:名词

词源: From French *blocus*, from Dutch *blokhuis* "blockhouse", from *blok* "block" + *huis* "house”. Originally referred to a siege by building fortifications.
