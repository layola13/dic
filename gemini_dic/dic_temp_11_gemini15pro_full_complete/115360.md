manorship: /ˈmænərʃɪp/ | n. | 庄园主的身份，庄园主的权利；采邑 | The position or authority of a lord of a manor; a manor. |  He inherited the manorship from his father. 他从他父亲那里继承了庄园主的身份。| Synonyms: lordship, domain, estate | Antonyms: servitude, subjection | noun

Etymology:  From Middle English *manershipe, equivalent to manor +‎ -ship.  Ultimately from Anglo-Norman maner, from Old French manoir (“to remain, dwell”), from Latin manēre (“to stay”).
