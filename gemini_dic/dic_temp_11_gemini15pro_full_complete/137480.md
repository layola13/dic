nontonal:/nɒnˈtəʊnl/ /nɑːnˈtoʊnl/| adj.|非音调的，无调的|Not depending on variations in pitch to distinguish words; not tonal.  A nontonal language uses other phonetic features like stress or vowel length to differentiate meaning.|Mandarin Chinese is a tonal language, while English is nontonal.|近义词：atonic|反义词：tonal|词性:adjective

Etymology:  non- + tonal.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not" or "without."  "Tonal" relates to tone, specifically the pitch of sounds in speech.
