hobble:/ˈhɒbəl/ (British), /ˈhɑːbəl/ (American)| v. n.|蹒跚; 跛行; 束缚; 难题|To walk with difficulty, typically because of pain from an injury to a leg or foot.  A rope or strap used to tie together the legs of a horse or other animal, preventing it from straying. A problem or difficulty that slows down progress or makes something difficult to do.|例句：The old man hobbled across the street. 老人蹒跚地穿过街道。

The injured horse hobbled back to the stable. 受伤的马一瘸一拐地回到马厩。

近义词：limp, shuffle, stagger, stumble, fetter, shackle, hinder, impede, hamper|反义词:run, sprint, aid, assist|词性:动词, 名词
