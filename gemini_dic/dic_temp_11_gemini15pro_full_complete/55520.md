distressingly:/dɪˈstrɛsɪŋli/
adverb|令人苦恼地，令人担忧地|in a way that causes worry or unhappiness|The poverty in the city is distressingly widespread. 城市的贫困状况令人担忧地普遍存在。|alarmingly, worryingly, concerningly|comfortingly, reassuringly|词性: 副词

distressingly:/dɪˈstrɛsɪŋli/
词源: From distress + -ing + -ly.  Distress ultimately comes from the Latin word "districtus," meaning "drawn apart," implying a state of hardship or suffering.
用法说明: Distressingly is an adverb used to describe something that causes distress or worry. It modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.
