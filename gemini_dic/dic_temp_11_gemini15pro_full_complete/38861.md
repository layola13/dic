confederates:/kənˈfɛdərɪts/ /kənˈfɛdəreɪts/ | n. | 同盟者；共犯；同伙 | A person who joins with others in a league or confederacy; an ally.  A person who helps another person do something, especially something wrong or illegal; an accomplice. |  The general and his confederates planned the coup. 将领和他的同伙策划了政变。| synonyms: ally, accomplice, associate, partner, collaborator | antonyms: opponent, adversary, enemy | noun

confederates:/kənˈfɛdərɪts/ /kənˈfɛdəreɪts/ | v. | 结盟；联合 | To form a league or confederacy; to unite for a common purpose. |  Several small tribes confederated to fight against the larger empire.  几个小部落联合起来对抗更大的帝国。| synonyms: ally, unite, join forces, combine, league | antonyms: separate, divide, disunite | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *confœderatus*, past participle of confœderare (“to unite by treaty”), from Latin con- (“together”) + fœderare (“to make a treaty”), from fœdus (“treaty, league”).
