barbs:/bɑːrbz/
noun
倒钩，尖刺，带刺的评论
Sharp points, often on a wire or fishhook, that prevent easy removal; also, critical or harsh remarks.
The fishhook had barbs, making it difficult to remove. 鱼钩上有倒钩，很难取下来。
His barbs about her dress were unnecessary and cruel. 他对她着装的尖刻评论既不必要又残忍。
Synonyms: prickles, thorns, spikes, criticisms, insults
Antonyms: compliments, praise
n.

barbs:/bɑːrbz/
verb (third-person singular simple present barbs, present participle barbing, simple past and past participle barbed)
装倒钩
To furnish with barbs.
They barbed the wire fence to keep out intruders. 他们在铁丝网上装了倒钩以防止入侵者。
He barbed his comments with sarcasm. 他用讽刺的语气发表了评论。
Synonyms: equip with barbs
Antonyms: remove barbs
v.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English barbe, from Old French barbe, from Late Latin barba (“beard”), from Latin barba (“beard”).  The sense of a pointed part developed from the resemblance of a fishhook barb to a beard's bristles.
