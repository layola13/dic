conceit:/kənˈsiːt/| n.|自负，骄傲自大；奇想，巧妙的构思|An excessive pride in oneself.  A fanciful notion or idea.|He was puffed up with conceit. 他被自负冲昏了头脑。  The architect’s conceit of the building being a ship is reflected in its design.  建筑师把建筑物构想成一艘船，这反映在它的设计中。|近义词：arrogance, vanity, pride, egotism|反义词：humility, modesty|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *conceit* "concept, thought, opinion," from *conceivre*  "to conceive," from Latin *concipere* "to take in, conceive," from *com-* "together" + *capere* "to take."  The meaning shifted from "concept" to "favorable opinion of oneself" in the 16th century.
