bushelful:/ˈbʊʃəlˌfʊl/ | n. | 一蒲式耳 | A quantity that fills a bushel; a large amount. | The farmer harvested a bushelful of apples. 农民收获了一蒲式耳的苹果。|  She brought home a bushelful of fresh produce from the farmer's market. 她从农贸市场带回家满满一蒲式耳的新鲜农产品。 |  heap, abundance, plenty | scarcity, lack | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *bushel* (a unit of dry measure) + *-ful* (suffix indicating fullness). The word "bushel" ultimately comes from Old French *boissiel*, meaning "small box".
