flounderer:/ˈflaʊndərər/| n. |挣扎者，笨拙的人，不知所措的人|A person who struggles or acts clumsily; someone who has difficulty doing or understanding something.|He was a flounderer in social situations, never knowing what to say. 他在社交场合总是很笨拙，不知道该说什么。| 近义词:struggler, blunderer, bungler|反义词:expert, adept, professional|词性:noun

flounder:/ˈflaʊndər/| v. |挣扎，笨拙地行动，不知所措|To struggle or act clumsily; to have difficulty doing or understanding something.|She floundered through the speech, forgetting her lines and stumbling over her words.  她在演讲中磕磕绊绊，忘词，还结结巴巴。|近义词:struggle, blunder, bungle|反义词:succeed, excel, manage|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *flounderen*, frequentative of *floundren*,  "to flounder," probably of Scandinavian origin. Related to Old Norse *flyndra* "flounder" (the fish), perhaps from the fish's clumsy movements.
