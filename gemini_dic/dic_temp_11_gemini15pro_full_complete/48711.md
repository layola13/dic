deficiency:/dɪˈfɪʃənsi/ /dəˈfɪʃənsi/| n.|缺乏，不足，缺陷|a lack or shortage of something essential for health or proper growth|The doctor diagnosed a vitamin D deficiency. 医生诊断出维生素D缺乏症。|近义词：lack, shortage, insufficiency, deficit, shortfall|反义词:surplus, abundance, excess|noun

deficiency:/dɪˈfɪʃənsi/ /dəˈfɪʃənsi/| n.|缺点，弱点|a fault or weakness in something|There are serious deficiencies in the current system. 当前系统存在严重缺陷。|近义词：flaw, defect, weakness, shortcoming, imperfection|反义词:strength, advantage, merit|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dēficientia, from Latin dēficiēns (present participle of dēficiō (“I fail, am wanting, revolt”)), from dē (“down, away from”) + faciō (“I do, make”).
