meiotic:/maɪˈɑːtɪk/| adj.|减数分裂的|Relating to meiosis, a type of cell division that results in four daughter cells each with half the number of chromosomes of the parent cell.|Meiotic division is essential for sexual reproduction.|Synonyms: reductional (less common)|Antonyms: mitotic|Adjective


meiosis:/maɪˈoʊsɪs/| n.|减数分裂|A type of cell division that results in four daughter cells each with half the number of chromosomes of the parent cell, as in the production of gametes and plant spores.|Meiosis is crucial for genetic diversity.|Synonyms: reduction division|Antonyms: mitosis|Noun
