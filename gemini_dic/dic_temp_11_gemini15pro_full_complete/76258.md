fuzz-up:/fʌz ʌp/| n.|混乱，模糊不清; (无线电)干扰|A state of confusion or indistinctness; interference (in radio).|The investigation was a complete fuzz-up.|近义词：mix-up, muddle, confusion, mess|反义词: clarity, order|noun
fuzz-up:/fʌz ʌp/| v.|使模糊，弄乱; 干扰(无线电)|To make unclear or confused; to cause interference (in radio).|He fuzzed up the signal with his transmitter.|近义词：confuse, muddle, obscure, garble|反义词: clarify, organize|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "fuzz" (n.) + "up" (adv.). "Fuzz" originates from Middle English "fusen" meaning "to fly out in small particles," likely imitative.  "Up" as an adverb intensifying the action of the verb.
