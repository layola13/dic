oversecularized: /ˌoʊvərˈsɛkjələrʌɪzd/
| adj. | 过度世俗化的 | Excessively focused on worldly or material concerns, at the expense of spiritual or religious values.  Characterized by a decline or absence of religious influence in society. | The oversecularized society has lost its moral compass.  过度世俗化的社会已经失去了道德指南针。|  The oversecularized educational system fails to address spiritual needs. 过度世俗化的教育体系未能满足精神需求。 | 近义词: secular, worldly, materialistic, irreligious | 反义词: religious, devout, pious, spiritual | 词性: adjective

词源:
over- (prefix): excessive, too much
secular (adjective): relating to worldly things as opposed to religious or spiritual things
-ize (suffix): to make or become; to cause to be or resemble
-ed (suffix): forming the past participle of verbs or adjectives derived from verbs
