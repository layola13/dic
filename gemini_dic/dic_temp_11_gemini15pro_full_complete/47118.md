dauntingness:/ˈdɔːntɪŋnɪs/ | n. | 令人畏惧；使人气馁 | The dauntingness of the task ahead made him hesitate. |  The sheer scale of the project filled him with a sense of dauntingness. | 近义词: formidableness, intimidatingness | 反义词: easiness, simplicity | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From "daunting" + "-ness." "Daunting" comes from the verb "daunt," which originated from Old French "danter" (to tame, subdue), ultimately from Latin "domitare" (to tame).  The suffix "-ness" is used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
