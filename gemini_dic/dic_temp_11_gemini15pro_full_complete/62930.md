enmeshment:/ɪnˈmɛʃmənt/| n. |纠缠，卷入，陷入|A situation in which someone is deeply involved in a difficult or complicated situation, often in a way that makes it hard for them to escape.|例句：Her enmeshment in her family's problems made it difficult for her to have a life of her own. 她深陷于家庭问题之中，难以拥有自己的生活。

例句：The company's enmeshment in legal battles drained its resources.  这家公司卷入法律纠纷，耗尽了资源。|近义词：involvement, entanglement, immersion|反义词: detachment, separation|词性:名词

Etymology: From enmesh + -ment.  Enmesh comes from the Old French en- (in, into) + mesh (net, snare).
