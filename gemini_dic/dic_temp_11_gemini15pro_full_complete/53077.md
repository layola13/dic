dioxins:/ˈdaɪɑːksɪnz/ /ˈdaɪɒksɪnz/| n.|二噁英|A group of highly toxic chemical compounds that are persistent environmental pollutants. They are formed as byproducts of certain industrial processes and the burning of waste. |Exposure to dioxins can cause a variety of health problems, including cancer.  接触二噁英会导致各种健康问题，包括癌症。|synonyms:  polychlorinated dibenzodioxins (PCDDs), polychlorinated dibenzofurans (PCDFs)|antonyms: N/A|noun

dioxin:/ˈdaɪɑːksɪn/ /ˈdaɪɒksɪn/| n.|二噁英|Any of various heterocyclic hydrocarbons that contain two oxygen atoms in a six-membered ring. A specific dioxin, especially a toxic one formed as a byproduct of pesticide manufacture. |Dioxin is a known carcinogen. 二噁英是一种已知的致癌物质。|synonyms:  polychlorinated dibenzodioxin (PCDD), polychlorinated dibenzofuran (PCDF)|antonyms: N/A|noun
