execrate:/ˈɛksɪkreɪt/| vt.|憎恶；咒骂；痛斥|To express great loathing for; denounce.  To feel or express great loathing for.|The citizens execrated the dictator. 市民们痛斥独裁者。|Synonyms: abhor, abominate, detest, loathe|Antonyms: admire, adore, cherish, praise|verb

Etymology: From Latin *exsecrārī*, from *ex-* ("out") + *sacrō* ("I devote, I curse").  Originally meaning "to curse by devoting to evil," later generalized to "denounce vehemently."
