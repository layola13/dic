electroreduction:/ɪˌlɛktrəʊrɪˈdʌkʃən/ /ɪˌlɛktroʊrɪˈdʌkʃən/| n.|电还原|The process of causing a chemical reduction by the addition of electrons supplied by an electric current.|Electroreduction is a key process in the production of many metals.|近义词：electrolytic reduction|反义词: electrooxidation|词性:noun

Etymology: electro- + reduction

electro-: from Greek *ēlektron 'amber', later used for electricity.

reduction: from Latin reducere 'to lead back', from re- 'back' + ducere 'to lead'.
