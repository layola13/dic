loophole:/ˈluːphoʊl/ (US) /ˈluːphəʊl/ (UK)| n. |漏洞，空子|An ambiguity or inadequacy in the law or a set of rules.|The company exploited a loophole in the tax law to avoid paying millions.  这家公司利用税法中的一个漏洞逃税数百万美元。|Synonyms:  gap, opening, flaw, escape clause|Antonyms:  closure, blockage, seal|noun

loophole:/ˈluːphoʊl/ (US) /ˈluːphəʊl/ (UK)| n. |枪眼，射击孔|A narrow opening through which small arms may be fired.|The soldiers fired through loopholes in the fortress walls. 士兵们透过堡垒墙上的射击孔开火。|Synonyms:  aperture, slit, vent|Antonyms:  barrier, wall, obstruction|noun
