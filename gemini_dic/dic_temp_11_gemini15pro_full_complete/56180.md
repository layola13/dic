dodecaphonism: /ˌdoʊdəˈkɑːfənɪzəm/ | n. | 十二音列主义；十二音技法 | A method of musical composition devised by Arnold Schoenberg in which the 12 notes of the chromatic scale are organized into a series, or row, and none is repeated until all the others have been sounded. | Schoenberg's music is characterized by dodecaphonism.  勋伯格的音乐以十二音列主义为特征。| synonym: serialism, twelve-tone technique | antonym: tonality | noun
