liege:/liːdʒ/ | n. | 君主；领主；臣下；封臣 | a lord, sovereign, or master to whom allegiance and service are owed | The knight swore fealty to his liege. 骑士向他的君主宣誓效忠。| synonyms: lord, sovereign, master | antonyms: vassal, subject | noun

liege:/liːdʒ/ | adj. | (封建时代用语)有义务效忠的；受…管辖的 | owing or showing feudal allegiance |  He was a liege man of the king. 他是国王的封臣。| synonyms: loyal, faithful, bound | antonyms: disloyal, rebellious | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *liege (modern French lige), from either Frankish *letigi or from Late Latin *laeticus, both meaning "free from all obligations except to one's lord." Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *leudijaz "free man."
