词源: From Late Latin *dēlīriōsus, from Latin dēlīrāre (“to be out of one's furrow, to rave”), from dē (“from, away”) + līra (“furrow, ridge”).  The plural form, "deliriousnesses," while grammatically correct, is rarely used. "Delirium" is the more common term for multiple instances.
