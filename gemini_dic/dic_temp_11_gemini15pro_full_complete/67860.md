fable:/ˈfeɪbl/| n.|寓言，传说；无稽之谈|A short story, typically with animals as characters, conveying a moral.  A fictitious narrative or statement.|例句：The fable of the tortoise and the hare teaches us that slow and steady wins the race. 龟兔赛跑的寓言告诉我们，缓慢而稳健地才能赢得比赛。

例句：His explanation was a complete fable, designed to protect himself. 他的解释完全是无稽之谈，旨在保护自己。|近义词：story, tale, allegory, myth, legend|反义词：truth, fact, reality|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *fable* "story, tale" (12c., Modern French *fable*), from Latin *fabula* "a story, tale, play, drama," from *fari* "to speak," related to *fama* "fame, report."
