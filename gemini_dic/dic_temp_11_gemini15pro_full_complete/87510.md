heterodactyl:/ˌhɛtərəˈdæktɪl/ | adj. |异趾的，不等趾的 | Having the digits or toes arranged differently than the usual pattern, especially with the outer toe or toes turned backward, as in trogons and woodpeckers. | Example: The heterodactyl arrangement of the woodpecker's toes allows it to climb trees easily.  啄木鸟异趾的排列使它能够轻松地爬树。| Example: The trogon, with its heterodactyl feet, is well-adapted to perching on branches.  咬鹃有着异趾的脚，非常适合栖息在树枝上。| Synonym: zygodactyl (partially) | Antonym: homodactyl (arguably) | Adjective
