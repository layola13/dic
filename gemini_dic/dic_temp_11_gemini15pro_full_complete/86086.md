hearselike:/ˈhɜːrslaɪk/| adj.|像灵车的，阴森的，不祥的|Resembling a hearse, especially in being gloomy, somber, or suggestive of death.  It describes something that evokes the atmosphere or appearance of a hearse, often implying a sense of foreboding or impending doom.|The old house had a hearselike quality, with its darkened windows and overgrown lawn.  这栋老房子有一种像灵车般的气质，窗户昏暗，草坪 overgrown。|近义词：funereal, gloomy, somber, morbid|反义词：cheerful, lively, bright|词性:形容词

词源:
From "hearse" + "-like".  "Hearse" comes from the Old French "herce," meaning a harrow (farming implement), which later came to refer to a framework used to hold candles at a funeral, and eventually the vehicle itself.  "-like" is a common suffix used to form adjectives indicating resemblance.
