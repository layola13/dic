modesty:/ˈmɒdɪsti/ (UK) /ˈmɑːdɪsti/ (US)| n.|谦虚，谦逊；适度，端庄；朴素，简朴|The quality or state of being unassuming or restrained in the estimation of one's abilities or achievements.  A moderate or humble estimate of one's merits, importance, etc.  Behavior, manner, or appearance intended to avoid impropriety or indecency.|She accepted the award with modesty. 她谦虚地接受了奖项。|Synonyms: humility, unpretentiousness, diffidence, reserve, simplicity|Antonyms: arrogance, conceit, boastfulness, ostentation, vanity|noun
