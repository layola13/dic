ducked:/dʌkt/| v.|躲避，闪避，迅速低下头|To lower the head or body suddenly, especially to avoid being seen or hit.  To move quickly downwards to avoid something.| The boy ducked under the table when he saw his mother coming with the medicine.  男孩看到妈妈拿着药来了，赶紧躲到桌子底下。|近义词：dodge, avoid, evade, stoop, crouch|反义词: confront, face|verb

ducked:/dʌkt/| v.| 潜水，浸入水中|To plunge or dip under water briefly.|He ducked his head under the water to cool off. 他把头浸到水里凉快一下。|近义词：dip, immerse, submerge, plunge|反义词: emerge, surface|verb

词源：From Middle English *ducken, from Old English *dūccan, from Proto-Germanic *dūkōną (“to duck, dive, stoop”). Cognate with Dutch duiken (“to dive”), German tauchen (“to dive”).
