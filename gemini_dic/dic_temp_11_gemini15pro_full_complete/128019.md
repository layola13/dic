nepotism:/ˈnɛpətɪzəm/
n.
裙带关系；任人唯亲；徇私
the practice among those with power or influence of favoring relatives or friends, especially by giving them jobs.
He was guilty of nepotism and corruption. 他犯有裙带关系和腐败罪。
近义词:cronyism, favoritism, patronage
反义词:impartiality, fairness, meritocracy
词性:名词

nepotisms:/ˈnɛpətɪzəmz/
n. (plural)
裙带关系；任人唯亲；徇私（复数）
Multiple instances of the practice among those with power or influence of favoring relatives or friends, especially by giving them jobs.
The report highlighted several nepotisms within the organization. 报告强调了该组织内部的几起裙带关系事件。
近义词:cronyisms, favoritisms, patronages
反义词:impartialities, fairnesses, meritocracies
词性:名词


USAGE->

词源: From Late Latin *nepotismus*, from Latin nepos "nephew" (source also of French népotisme, Spanish nepotismo, Italian nepotismo). Originally, the term referred specifically to the practice of popes appointing their nephews to important positions.
