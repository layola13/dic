campaniles: /ˌkæmpəˈniːliːz/ /ˌkæmpəˈniːlez/
| n. |钟楼（尤指独立式钟楼，复数形式）|A tall, freestanding bell tower, especially one associated with a church or other public building.  This word is the plural of "campanile."|The campaniles of Venice are famous landmarks. 威尼斯的钟楼是著名的地标。|Synonyms: bell tower, belfry|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "ground floor" in a figurative sense)|Noun

Etymology:
From Italian *campanile*, from *campana* "bell," from Late Latin *campāna* "bell of Campania" (because bells were originally made there), from *Campānia* "Campania" (a region of Italy).
