cannonading:/ˌkænəˈneɪdɪŋ/
n.
炮击，轰击
A continuous heavy attack with cannons.
The cannonading went on for hours, shaking the very foundations of the city.  炮击持续了几个小时，震动了整个城市的地基。
bombardment, shelling, barrage
(no clear antonym)
noun


cannonading:/ˌkænəˈneɪdɪŋ/
v.
持续炮击，轰击
To attack continuously with cannons.
The enemy forces were cannonading the city walls. 敌军正在炮轰城墙。
bombarding, shelling, battering
(no clear antonym)
verb


Etymology:
From the verb "cannonade," which comes from the French "canonnade" and ultimately from the Italian "cannonata," meaning "a cannon shot."  Derived from "cannone" (cannon).
