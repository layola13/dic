declamatoriness:/dɪˌklæməˈtɔːrɪnɪs/
|n.|雄辩; 慷慨激昂; 演说腔|The quality of being declamatory; a tendency to speak in a pompous or rhetorical manner.|His declamatoriness made the speech feel artificial.  他的演说腔使演讲听起来很做作。|近义词：rhetoric, bombast, grandiloquence|反义词：understatement, simplicity, conciseness|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *declamatorius + -ness.  From Latin declamare (to declaim, practice rhetoric) + -ness.  Declamare from de- (down, from) + clamare (to cry out, shout).
