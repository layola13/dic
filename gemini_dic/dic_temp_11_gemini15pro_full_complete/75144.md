frit:/frɪt/ | n. adj. v. |熔块，玻璃料；油炸的；烧结|  Small pieces of colored glass that are fused together to form a decorative surface, especially on pottery. Can also be an adjective meaning "fried" (especially in Belgian cooking) or a verb meaning "to fuse partially."|  The artist used blue frit to create the sky in the mosaic. 艺术家用蓝色熔块在马赛克中创造了天空。| 近义词: glaze, enamel, fried (as adjective) | 反义词: (no direct antonym) | 词性: noun, adjective, verb

frit:/frɪt/ | n. adj. v. |油炸食品，玻璃料；油炸的；烧结| The frit was applied to the pottery before firing. 在烧制之前，将熔块涂在陶器上。| 近义词: glaze, enamel, fried (as adjective) | 反义词: (no direct antonym) | 词性: noun, adjective, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fritte*, past participle of *frire* "to fry," from Latin *frigere* "to roast, fry, parch." The sense of "partially fused glass" developed from the idea of the material being "fried" or heated.
