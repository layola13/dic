cahoot:/kəˈhuːt/| n. |勾结，串通，同谋|secret partnership or collusion, typically in doing something dishonest or illegal|例句：The two were in cahoots to steal the company's money. 两人串通一气偷公司的钱。

例句：They were suspected of being in cahoots with the enemy. 他们被怀疑与敌人勾结。|近义词：collusion, conspiracy, league, partnership|反义词：opposition, disagreement|词性:名词

词源:Origin uncertain. Perhaps from Cajun French *cahute*, meaning ‘cabin, hut.’
