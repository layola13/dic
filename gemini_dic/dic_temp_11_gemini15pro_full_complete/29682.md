catherine:/ˈkæθ(ə)rɪn/ /ˈkæθrɪn/| n. |凯瑟琳|A female given name, from the Greek Αικατερίνη (Aikaterine), the etymology of which is debated.  It possibly derives from the name of the early Christian martyr Saint Catherine of Alexandria or ultimately from the Greek word καθαρός (katharos), meaning "pure."  |Catherine the Great was a powerful Empress of Russia.|近义词：Katharine, Kathryn, Kathleen|反义词: N/A|名词
