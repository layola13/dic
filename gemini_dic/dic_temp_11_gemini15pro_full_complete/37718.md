comparativenesses:/kəmˈpærətɪvnɪsɪz/
|n.|比较性，可比性|The state or quality of being comparable; the degree to which things are similar or different.|The comparativenesses of the two systems are striking. 这两个系统的可比性很强。|近义词：similarity, comparability, resemblance|反义词：dissimilarity, difference, incomparability|词性: noun

Etymology:
From comparative + -ness.  "Comparative" itself comes from Late Latin *comparativus, from Latin comparare "to compare," from com- "together" + parare "to make ready, prepare."  The suffix "-ness" is used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
