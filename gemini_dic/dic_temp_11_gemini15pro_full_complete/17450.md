bear-down:/ˌber ˈdaʊn/ | phrasal verb | 加强；努力；集中精力；施加压力 | To exert full strength or pressure, often in a difficult situation; to concentrate intensely. | The team bore down in the final minutes to secure the victory.  球队在最后几分钟全力以赴，确保了胜利。

The runner bore down and sprinted to the finish line. 跑步运动员全力冲刺到终点线。 | 近义词:  push, strive, concentrate, focus, persevere | 反义词: relax, give up, slack off | 词性：动词短语

bear-down:/ˌber ˈdaʊn/ | phrasal verb | 对…施加压力；逼迫 | To exert pressure or force on someone or something. | The management bore down on the employees to increase productivity. 管理层向员工施加压力以提高生产力。

The weight of the snow bore down on the roof of the cabin.  雪的重量压在小屋的屋顶上。 | 近义词:  pressure, press, push, force, compel | 反义词: support, alleviate | 词性：动词短语


词源:  "Bear" in this context comes from Old English "beran," meaning "to carry, bring forth, endure."  "Down" intensifies the action, indicating a directed and forceful effort.
