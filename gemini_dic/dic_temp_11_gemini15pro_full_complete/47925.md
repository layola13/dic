deceptivity:/dɪˈsɛptɪvɪti/
n.
欺骗性；迷惑性
The deceptivity of the advertisement led many consumers to believe the product was more effective than it actually was.  广告的欺骗性使许多消费者认为该产品比实际更有效。
The magician's deceptivity was so convincing that the audience was completely fooled. 魔术师的欺骗性如此令人信服，以至于观众完全被愚弄了。
Synonyms: deceitfulness, trickery, guile, deceptiveness
Antonyms: honesty, forthrightness, sincerity, openness
Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From deceptive + -ity.  Deceptive comes from the Latin word "decipere" which means "to deceive, ensnare, or betray."
