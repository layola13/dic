deafening:/ˈdɛfənɪŋ/ | adj. | 震耳欲聋的 | Extremely loud; so loud as to make it difficult to hear anything else. | The music at the concert was deafening.  音乐会上的音乐震耳欲聋。|  The construction noise outside was deafening, making it impossible to concentrate. 外面的建筑噪音震耳欲聋，让人无法集中注意力。 |近义词：loud, earsplitting, thunderous, overpowering | 反义词：quiet, faint, soft, muted | 词性: 形容词

deafening:/ˈdɛfənɪŋ/ | adj. |  使人感到强烈震撼的（引申义）|  Figuratively, something that is so overwhelming that it makes it difficult to think or react. | The silence after the explosion was deafening. 爆炸后的寂静令人窒息。| The news of her sudden death was deafening. 她突然去世的消息令人震惊。 |近义词：overwhelming, stunning, shocking | 反义词：unremarkable, insignificant | 词性: 形容词
