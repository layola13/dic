nosegay: /ˈnoʊzɡeɪ/ (US), /ˈnəʊzɡeɪ/ (UK) | n. | 花束；小花束 | A small bunch of flowers, typically one that is sweet-scented. |  The bride carried a delicate nosegay of white roses and baby's breath.  新娘拿着一束精致的白玫瑰和满天星小花束。

She presented him with a nosegay of fragrant violets. 她送给他一束芳香的紫罗兰。 | 近义词: bouquet, posy, tussie-mussie | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "scattered flowers") | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *nosegay, from nose + gay (adjective, "bright, beautiful"). Originally referred to fragrant flowers held to the nose.
