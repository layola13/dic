malthus:/ˈmælθəs/| n.|马尔萨斯; 马尔萨斯主义; 托马斯·罗伯特·马尔萨斯 (1766-1834，英国经济学家，人口学家);|Thomas Robert Malthus (1766–1834), English economist and demographer, whose theory that population growth would always tend to outrun the food supply and that betterment of humankind is impossible without strict limits on reproduction influenced the thought of Charles Darwin.|The influence of Malthus can be seen in Darwin's theory of natural selection.|近义词:neo-Malthusian|反义词:|n.
