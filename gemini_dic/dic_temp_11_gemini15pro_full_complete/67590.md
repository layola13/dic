extraterritoriality: /ˌekstrətɛrɪˌtɔːriˈælɪti/
n.
治外法权,域外效力
The state of being exempted from the jurisdiction of local law, usually as the result of diplomatic negotiations.  The right of a foreign resident to live under the laws of his or her native country and not under the laws of the host country.
例句：The diplomats enjoyed extraterritoriality, meaning they were immune to the laws of the host country.  外交官享有治外法权，这意味着他们不受所在国法律的约束。

例句：The company argued for extraterritoriality of its contracts, claiming they should be governed by US law, not the laws of the country where the work was performed.  该公司主张其合同的域外效力，声称合同应受美国法律管辖，而不是工作执行所在国的法律。
近义词：immunity, exemption, privilege
反义词：jurisdiction, subjection
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From extra- (outside, beyond) + territoriality (the state of having or relating to territory).  Ultimately from Latin terra (earth, land).
