doublecrosses: /ˈdʌbəlˌkrɒsɪz/ (British) /ˈdʌbəlˌkrɔːsɪz/ (American)
| v. third-person singular present | 背叛，出卖；欺骗 | To betray or deceive someone, especially someone who trusts you. |  She doublecrossed her best friend by revealing her secret. 她泄露了她最好的朋友的秘密，背叛了她。 | synonyms: betrays, deceives, cheats | antonyms: supports, aids, assists | verb

词源:  From the noun "double cross," which originated in the early 20th century, likely from the idea of marking someone with a cross twice, as if to emphasize their betrayal.
