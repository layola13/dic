anathem: /əˈnæθəm/ | n. | 咒逐，诅咒；被咒逐的人（或事物）|A formal curse by a pope or council of the Church, excommunicating a person or denouncing a doctrine.|The rebels were declared anathems to the true faith.| 近义词：curse, denunciation, excommunication, ban | 反义词：blessing, benediction | 词性：名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *anathema*, from Greek *anáthema* "a thing devoted to evil, a curse," literally "a thing set up, set apart," from *ana- "up" + *tithenai* "to put, place."
