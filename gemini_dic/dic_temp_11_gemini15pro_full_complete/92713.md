icequake:/ˈaɪskweɪk/ | n. | 冰震 | A sudden, violent movement of ice, similar to an earthquake, often caused by glacial movement or cracking. |  The icequake caused a loud boom that echoed across the frozen lake. 冰震引起了巨大的轰鸣声，在冰冻的湖面上回荡。

The researchers recorded several icequakes during their expedition to the glacier. 研究人员在冰川探险期间记录了几次冰震。 | 近义词: cryoseism | 反义词:  (No direct antonym exists, but one could consider a geological event like a tremor or earthquake as a contrasting phenomenon in a different medium.) | 词性: noun
