heteropelmous:/ˌhɛtərəˈpɛlməs/
adjective
异掌的；异足的
Having unequal feet, claws, or toes, as in certain birds.  Specifically, refers to a zygodactyl arrangement where the outer toe is directed backward and the inner toe forward, unlike the usual zygodactyl arrangement where two toes point forward and two backward.
Example: The heteropelmous feet of the woodpecker allow it to cling to tree trunks. 啄木鸟异掌的脚可以让它紧紧抓住树干。
Example:  The owl's heteropelmous feet are adapted for grasping prey. 猫头鹰异掌的脚适合抓捕猎物。
Synonym: zygodactyl (in a broader sense)
Antonym:  homopelmous (theoretical opposite, not commonly used)
adjective
