conditionalist:/kənˈdɪʃənəlɪst/ | n. | 条件主义者；条件论者 | Someone who believes in or follows conditionalism, a theological view emphasizing God's love and desire for all people to be saved, but also upholding the necessity of faith in Christ for salvation. |  Many conditionalists believe that those who do not accept Christ will ultimately cease to exist rather than suffer eternal torment. 许多条件主义者相信那些不接受基督的人最终会停止存在，而不是遭受永恒的折磨。 | synonyms: believer in conditional immortality, annihilationist | antonyms: eternalist, proponent of eternal conscious torment | noun

conditionalist:/kənˈdɪʃənəlɪst/ | adj. | 有条件的；条件式的 | Relating to or involving conditions.  (This adjectival use is less common than the noun form.) | The agreement was conditionalist upon both parties fulfilling their obligations. 该协议以双方履行其义务为条件。(This usage is less common.) | synonyms: conditional, contingent, dependent | antonyms: unconditional, absolute, independent | adjective
