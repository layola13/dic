connexive:/kəˈnɛksɪv/
adjective|连接的；有连结关系的|A connexive logic is a kind of logic which allows for the expression of connections between statements, such as "if p then q".|Connexive logics have the property that if p implies q, then not-p implies not-q.|近义词：connected, related, linked, associated|反义词：disconnected, unrelated, separate|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Latin *connexus*, past participle of *connectere* "to connect," from *com-* "together" + *nectere* "to bind."
