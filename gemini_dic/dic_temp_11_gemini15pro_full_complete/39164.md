conge:/ˈkɒndʒeɪ/ (UK), /kɑːnˈʒeɪ/ (US)| n.| 告别，告辞，休假，（尤指法语国家的）冻状甜点|A formal or ceremonious act of leave-taking; farewell. Also, a leave of absence, especially one granted by a superior.  In French cuisine, a conge refers to a thickened sweet dish, often molded and chilled.|例句：He took his conge of the king. 他向国王告别。

例句：She requested a conge from her duties. 她请求休假。|近义词：farewell, leave, departure, adieu, parting, permission, furlough (for leave of absence)|反义词：greeting, arrival, return (for leave of absence)|n.


conge:/ˈkɒndʒeɪ/ (UK), /kɑːnˈʒeɪ/ (US)| v.| 告别，告辞|To take one's leave; to bid farewell.|例句：He congeed to the assembled guests and departed. 他向聚集的宾客告别后离开了。

例句：After a brief chat, she congeed herself and left.  简短交谈后，她告辞离开了。|近义词：depart, leave, withdraw, retire|反义词：greet, arrive, welcome|v.
