lividity:/lɪˈvɪdɪti/
noun
青紫， lividity
The discoloration of the skin caused by the pooling of blood in the dependent parts of the body after death.
The pathologist noted the lividity on the victim's back, indicating he had been lying on his back after death. 尸体背部的青紫表明他死后一直仰卧着。
Synonyms: discoloration, cyanosis, hypostasis
Antonyms: flush, ruddiness
n.

livid:/ˈlɪvɪd/
adjective
青灰色的，铅色的；( informal ) 极愤怒的
Furiously angry.
Discolored, bluish, or grayish.
He was absolutely livid when he found out his car had been stolen. 当他发现他的车被偷了时，他非常愤怒。
The bruise on her arm was livid and painful. 她手臂上的瘀伤呈青灰色，而且很痛。
Synonyms: furious, enraged, indignant, ashen, gray
Antonyms: calm, pleased, rosy, flushed
adj.
