hutzpah:/ˈhʊtspə/| n.| 厚颜无耻；胆大妄为；过分的自信 |Supreme self-confidence or audacity.  It suggests a kind of nerve or arrogance, often in the face of difficulty or opposition.|He had the hutzpah to ask for a raise after only a week on the job. 他竟然敢在工作才一周就要求加薪。|Synonyms: audacity, nerve, gall, chutzpah (variant spelling), temerity|Antonyms: timidity, shyness, humility, modesty|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Yiddish חוצפּה (khutspe), from Hebrew חֻצְפָּה (ḥuṣpā), from Aramaic חֻצְפָּא (ḥuṣpā), meaning "impudence," "insolence."
