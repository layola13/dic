idolatress:/ˈaɪdəl.ə.trɪs/| n. |女偶像崇拜者，女神的女性崇拜者|A woman who worships idols or false gods. |例句：The idolatress knelt before the golden statue.  那个女偶像崇拜者跪在金色雕像前。

例句：She was accused of being an idolatress for her devotion to material possessions. 她因沉迷于物质财富而被指责为拜物主义者。|近义词：worshipper, devotee, admirer|反义词：iconoclast, skeptic|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *idolatresse*, from Old French *idolatrice*, from Late Latin *īdōlātra*, feminine of *īdōlātra* (“idolater”), from Ancient Greek *εἰδωλολάτρης* (*eidōlolátrēs*, “idol-worshipper”), from *εἴδωλον* (*eídōlon*, “idol, image”) + *λάτρης* (*látrēs*, “worshipper”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European **weid- (“to see”).
