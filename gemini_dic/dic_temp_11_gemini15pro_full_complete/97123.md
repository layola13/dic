infiltrate:/ˈɪnfɪltreɪt/| v. |渗透，潜入|To enter or gain access to (an organization, place, etc.) surreptitiously and gradually, especially in order to acquire secret information or cause damage.|The reporter infiltrated the gang to expose their criminal activities. 记者潜入了该团伙以揭露他们的犯罪活动。|Synonyms: penetrate, permeate, infiltrate|Antonyms: withdraw, retreat|verb

infiltration:/ˌɪnfɪlˈtreɪʃn/| n. |渗透，潜入|The act of infiltrating or something that infiltrates.|The infiltration of enemy spies into the government was a serious security breach. 敌方间谍渗透进政府是一个严重的安全漏洞。|Synonyms: penetration, permeation, infiltration|Antonyms: withdrawal, retreat|noun
