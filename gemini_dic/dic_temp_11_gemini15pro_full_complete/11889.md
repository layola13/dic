arrogatingly:/ˈærəɡeɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 傲慢地，专横地 | In an arrogant manner; presumptuously claiming or taking something without justification.  | He arrogatingly dismissed the concerns of his employees. 他傲慢地驳回了员工的担忧。 | She arrogatingly claimed the credit for the project's success, even though she barely contributed. 她傲慢地把项目成功的功劳归于自己，尽管她几乎没有贡献。 | 近义词: presumptuously, haughtily, imperiously, disdainfully | 反义词: humbly, modestly, respectfully | 词性: 副词

arrogate: /ˈærəɡeɪt/ | v. | 篡夺，僭取 | To claim or seize without justification; to take or assume (something, such as a right or privilege) in a way that is not fair or legal. | The king arrogated the right to levy taxes. 国王僭取了征税的权利。|  He arrogated to himself the power to make decisions for the entire group. 他擅自为自己攫取了为整个团队做决定的权力。 | 近义词: usurp, seize, appropriate, commandeer | 反义词: relinquish, surrender, cede | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin arrogatus, past participle of arrogare (“to claim for oneself, appropriate, adopt”), from ad- (“to, towards”) + rogare (“to ask”).
