helm:/hɛlm/ | n. | 舵, 方向盘; 领导地位 | a steering apparatus for a ship or boat; a position of leadership | The ship lost its helm in the storm.  这艘船在暴风雨中失去了舵。| synonyms: wheel, rudder, control, command | antonyms:  powerlessness, subordination | noun

helms:/hɛlmz/ | n. |  舵(helm的复数); 领导地位(helm的复数) | plural of helm |  The helms of the two ships were damaged. 两艘船的舵都损坏了。 | synonyms: wheels, rudders, controls, commands | antonyms: weaknesses, subordinations | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *helma (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *helmaz, from Proto-Indo-European *kel- (“to cover, conceal, protect”). Cognate with Old Saxon helm, Old Frisian helm, Dutch helm, Old High German helm, German Helm, Old Norse hjálmr, Gothic 𐌷𐌹𐌻𐌼𐍃 (hilms). Related also to hall.
