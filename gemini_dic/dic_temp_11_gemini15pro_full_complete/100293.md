interset:/ˌɪntərˈsɛt/ | v. | 相互交叉；互相影响；共有；贯穿 | To intersect is to cross or meet each other, often at a point or line. It can also refer to things sharing common elements or influencing each other. | The two roads intersect just outside the town. 这两条路就在城外交叉。 The interests of the two companies intersect.  两家公司的利益相互交织。 | 近义词：cross, overlap, converge, intertwine | 反义词：diverge, separate | 词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *intersectus*, past participle of *intersecare*, from *inter-* (between) + *secare* (to cut).
