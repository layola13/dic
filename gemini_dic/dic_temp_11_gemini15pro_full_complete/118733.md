metagraphy:/mɛˈtæɡrəfi/ | n. | 后设写作；元写作 | A form of writing that deals with the process of writing itself, often exploring the nature of authorship, the relationship between writer and reader, or the limitations of language.  It can also refer to writing about writing or a description of writing. | Example 1:  The novel's metagraphy made the reader constantly aware of the author's presence. 这部小说的后设写作让读者不断意识到作者的存在。

Example 2: Her thesis focused on the metagraphy of postmodern literature. 她的论文集中研究了后现代文学的后设写作。| Near Synonyms: self-reflexive writing, metafiction | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "straightforward narrative") | Noun
