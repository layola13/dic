after-fact:/ˈæftərˌfækt/
adjective
事后的；马后炮的；亡羊补牢的
happening or done after an event, especially a crime or accident, and often relating to it
例句：After-fact explanations are rarely convincing. 事后诸葛亮的解释很少令人信服。

例句：The investigation revealed the payment to be an after-fact attempt to cover up the crime. 调查显示，这笔款项是事后试图掩盖罪行的举动。

近义词：retrospective, post hoc, ex post facto
反义词：prior, beforehand, anticipatory
词性: adjective

Etymology:
From the Latin phrase "ex post facto," meaning "from a thing done afterward." This phrase was adopted into English legal terminology and eventually shortened to "after-fact" in more general usage.
