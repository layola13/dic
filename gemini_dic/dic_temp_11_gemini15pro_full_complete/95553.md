incognita:/ˌɪnkɒɡˈniːtə/ | n. |未知数，未知的事物|An unknown quantity or person, especially a woman whose name is kept secret.  Something that is unknown or not yet determined.|例句：The identity of the donor remained incognita. 捐赠者的身份仍然未知。|例句：Her past is a complete incognita. 她的过去完全是个谜。|近义词：unknown, mystery, enigma|反义词：known, identified|词性：名词

incognito:/ˌɪnkɒɡˈniːtəʊ/ | adv. adj. |隐姓埋名地，化名地；隐姓埋名的，化名的|In disguise; with one's identity concealed.|例句：The prince travelled incognito. 王子隐姓埋名地旅行。|例句：She registered at the hotel incognito. 她在酒店匿名登记。|近义词：anonymously, disguised, secretly|反义词：openly, publicly|词性：副词，形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *incognita*, feminine of *incognito*, from Latin *incognitus* "unknown, unrecognized," past participle of *incognoscere* "not to know, to fail to recognize," from *in-* "not" + *cognoscere* "to get to know."
