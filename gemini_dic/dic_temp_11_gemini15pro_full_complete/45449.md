curate: /ˈkjʊreɪt/ | n. | 馆长；监护人；助理牧师 | A person who manages a museum, art collection, etc., or a person in charge of selecting and caring for items in such a collection. | The museum curator gave a tour of the new exhibit.  博物馆馆长带领参观了新的展览。 | synonyms: manager, administrator, director, overseer | antonyms: N/A | noun

curate: /ˈkjʊreɪt/ | v. |  精选；策划；组织 | To select, organize, and present online content, such as images, videos, or music, typically using professional or expert knowledge.  | She curates art exhibitions for a living. 她以策划艺术展览为生。 | synonyms: organize, compile, assemble, gather, select | antonyms: disorganize, scatter, disperse | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *curatus, past participle of curare ("to take care of"), from cura ("care").
