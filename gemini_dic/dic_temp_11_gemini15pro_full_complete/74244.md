foxes:/ˈfɒksɪz/ (UK) /ˈfɑːksɪz/ (US)| n.|狐狸（复数）|The plural form of "fox," referring to more than one fox.  It comes from the Old English "fox" and Proto-Germanic "fuhsaz."|The foxes hunted for rabbits in the field. 狐狸在田野里猎捕兔子。|Synonyms: vulpes (formal, scientific), reynards (literary)|Antonyms: N/A (animals don't typically have direct antonyms)|Noun
