neopaganism:/ˌniːoʊˈpeɪɡənɪzəm/ | n. |新异教主义|A modern religious movement that revives or draws inspiration from pre-Christian pagan traditions, especially those of Europe.  It often emphasizes nature worship, polytheism, and goddess worship.|例句：Neopaganism encompasses a wide range of beliefs and practices. 新异教主义包含了广泛的信仰和实践。

Neopaganism is gaining popularity in some parts of the world.  新异教主义在世界某些地区越来越受欢迎。|近义词：paganism, heathenism|反义词:Christianity, monotheism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From neo- + paganism.  "Neo-" is a combining form meaning "new," "recent," "revived," or "modified," used in the formation of compound words. "Paganism" comes from Late Latin pāgānismus, from pāgānus 'pagan'.
