counterfeited:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪtɪd/| adj. |伪造的，仿造的|Made in exact imitation of something valuable or important with the intention to deceive or defraud.|The police found a large number of counterfeited banknotes. 警方发现了大量伪造的钞票。|Synonyms: forged, fake, spurious, bogus|Antonyms: genuine, authentic, real|adjective

counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/| v. |伪造，仿造|To make a fraudulent copy of something valuable or important.|He was arrested for counterfeiting money. 他因伪造货币而被捕。|Synonyms: forge, fake, copy|Antonyms: authenticate, verify|verb

counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/| n. |伪造品，赝品|A fraudulent copy of something valuable or important.|The painting was discovered to be a counterfeit. 这幅画被发现是赝品。|Synonyms: forgery, fake, sham|Antonyms: original, genuine|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French contrefait, past participle of contrefaire “to imitate, copy, forge,” from contre- “against” + faire “to make, do.”
