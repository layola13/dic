importune:/ˌɪmpɔːrˈtjuːn/ /ˌɪmpɔːrˈtuːn/
verb
恳求；不断要求
to ask (someone) for something or to do something in a repeated or annoying way
例句：He importuned her to go to the dance with him. 他不断恳求她和他一起去跳舞。

例句：She was importuned by beggars on the street. 她在大街上被乞丐纠缠。
synonym:beg, beseech, implore, pester, harass
antonym:ignore, neglect, leave alone
词性:v.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *importunen*, from Middle French *importuner*, from Old French *importun*, from Latin *importūnus* (“unfit, troublesome, inconvenient”), from *in-* (“not”) + *portus* (“harbor, port”).  The sense development was from “unseasonable, inconvenient” to “troublesome” to “to trouble, vex.”
