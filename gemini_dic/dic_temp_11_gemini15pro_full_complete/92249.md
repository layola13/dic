hypertropical:/ˌhaɪpərˈtrɒpɪkəl/| adj.|过度热带的，极度炎热的|Excessively tropical; characterized by extreme heat and humidity, often beyond what is typical for a tropical climate.|例句：The hypertropical climate of the region made it difficult for most plants to thrive.  该地区极度炎热的气候使得大多数植物难以生长。

例句：The explorers found the hypertropical conditions in the jungle almost unbearable. 探险者们发现丛林中极度炎热潮湿的环境几乎难以忍受。|近义词：extremely hot, excessively humid, sweltering, torrid|反义词：temperate, mild, frigid|词性:形容词
Etymology:  From hyper- "over, excessive" + tropical.  "Hyper-" is from Greek *hyper* "over, beyond." "Tropical" is from Late Latin *tropicus*, from Greek *tropikos* "of the solstice," from *tropos* "turn."
