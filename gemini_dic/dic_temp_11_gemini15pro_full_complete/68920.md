fata:/ˈfɑːtə/ /ˈfeɪtə/| n. |命运，宿命；仙女 (尤指意大利民间传说中的)|  From Latin *fata (plural), from fatum "fate," literally "what has been spoken," neuter past participle of fari "to speak."  The sense of "fairy" is a later development, perhaps influenced by the notion of a being that determines destiny.|The three Fates spun, measured, and cut the thread of life. 三位命运女神纺织、丈量和剪断生命之线。| synonym: destiny, doom, providence, fairy| antonym: chance, free will | noun
