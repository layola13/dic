consignation:/ˌkɒnsaɪˈneɪʃn/ /ˌkɑːnsaɪˈneɪʃn/| n. |寄售；交付；委托；存放|The act of consigning something to another's care, typically for sale or safekeeping.  The goods were held in consignation until the payment was received.|例句：The paintings were sent to the auction house on consignation. 这些画被寄售到拍卖行。

例句：The consignation of the documents to the lawyer ensured their safekeeping.  这些文件被委托给律师保管，确保了它们的安全。|近义词：deposit, entrustment, commitment|反义词:retrieval, withdrawal|词性:noun

consign:/kənˈsaɪn/ /kənˈsaɪn/| vt. |寄售；交付；委托；打发；放逐|To deliver something to another's care, typically for sale or safekeeping. To assign to an undesirable place or state.|例句：She consigned the letter to the flames. 她把信付之一炬。

例句：He was consigned to a life of poverty. 他被注定过贫困的生活。|近义词：commit, entrust, deliver|反义词:retain, keep|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "consignacion," from Latin "consignatio," from "consignare" meaning "to sign, seal, mark, stamp, register," from "com-" (together) + "signare" (to sign).
