accoutrement: /əˈkuːtərmənt/ (US) /əˈkuːtrəmənt/ (UK)| n. | 装备，服饰；配件| Additional items of dress or equipment, or other items carried or worn by a person or used for a particular activity. |  The soldier checked his accoutrements before going on patrol. 士兵在巡逻前检查了他的装备。| Synonyms: equipment, gear, paraphernalia, trappings | Antonyms:  lack, deficiency | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *acoustrer*, from *a-* (to) + *coustre* (seam), from Latin *consuere* (to sew together).  The original meaning was "to sew onto," hence "to furnish with equipment."
