elevated:/ˈɛləˌveɪtɪd/| adj.|升高的，提高的，高尚的，兴奋的|Raised to a higher position or level, either physically or figuratively; also, of superior quality or character; or, filled with a sense of exhilaration.|例句：The house is situated on an elevated platform. 这座房子坐落在一个高台上。

例句：He spoke in an elevated tone. 他用高亢的语气说话。

近义词：raised, lofty, high, exalted, heightened, improved, superior, refined, noble, exhilarated|反义词：lowered, depressed, base, common, dejected|词性:形容词

elevate:/ˈɛləˌveɪt/| vt.|提升，提高，举起，鼓舞|To raise to a higher position or level, either physically or figuratively; to improve or refine; to lift the spirits of.|例句：The platform elevated the speaker above the crowd.  这个讲台使演讲者高出人群。

例句：Reading good literature can elevate the mind. 阅读优秀的文学作品可以提升思想境界。

近义词：raise, lift, hoist, boost, improve, enhance, uplift, inspire|反义词：lower, depress, degrade|词性:动词

elevation:/ˌɛləˈveɪʃən/| n.|海拔，高度，提升，崇高|The height of something above a given level, especially sea level; the act of raising something or the state of being raised; an elevated place or position; moral or intellectual excellence.|例句：The elevation of the mountain peak is 6,000 meters. 这座山峰的海拔高度为6000米。

例句：His elevation to the presidency was unexpected. 他出乎意料地当选了总统。

近义词：height, altitude, rise, promotion, advancement, loftiness, nobility|反义词：depth, descent, degradation|词性:名词
