fawners:/ˈfɔːnərz/| n. |奉承者，拍马屁的人|People who fawn over others, often in order to gain favor or advantage.  They exhibit excessive flattery and subservience.|The boss was surrounded by fawners who constantly praised his every decision.|近义词：sycophants, flatterers, yes-men|反义词：critics, dissenters|词性:名词

词源: From the verb "fawn," derived from the Old English "fagnian," meaning "to rejoice."  Originally referring to the joyful movements of a young deer (a fawn), the meaning shifted to describe people exhibiting similar submissive behavior to gain favor.
