over-pollinating:/ˌoʊvərˈpɑːləˌneɪtɪŋ/
|v.|过度授粉|The act of pollinating a plant or crop excessively, often resulting in diminished fruit or seed production.|The farmer worried about over-pollinating his apple orchard because it could lead to smaller apples.|over-fertilizing, overwatering|under-pollinating|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
over- (prefix): from Old English *ofer (cognate with German über, Dutch over), meaning "above," "across," "beyond," "excessive"
pollinate (verb): from Latin pollinatus, past participle of pollinare "to sprinkle with fine flour," from pollen "fine flour, dust"
