oceloid:/ˈoʊsəlɔɪd/| n. |眼点；眼状斑|A simple eye or eyespot in invertebrates, especially insects and mollusks, typically pigmented and light-sensitive.  An eye-like marking.|The butterfly's wings were covered in oceloids that resembled large eyes, deterring predators.|近义词：eyespot, simple eye|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps 'blind spot')|noun
