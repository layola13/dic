coot:/kuːt/
noun|小䴙䴘；傻瓜，笨蛋|A small water bird with lobed toes, of the rail family. Also used informally to refer to a foolish or eccentric person.|There were several coots swimming on the lake. 湖面上有几只小䴙䴘在游泳。|Synonyms: moorhen, waterhen (for the bird); fool, idiot, simpleton (for the person)|Antonyms: genius (for the person)|n.

coot:/kuːt/
noun|傻瓜，笨蛋|Informal term for a foolish or eccentric person.|He's a bit of a coot, but he's harmless. 他有点傻，但 harmless. |Synonyms: fool, idiot, simpleton|Antonyms: genius|n.

词源：
The word "coot" comes from the Middle English "cote", likely of imitative origin, referring to the bird's call.  The pejorative sense of "fool" or "simpleton" developed later, possibly due to the bird's perceived clumsiness on land.
