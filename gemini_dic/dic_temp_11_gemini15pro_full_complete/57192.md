down-line: /ˈdaʊnˌlaɪn/ | n. adj. adv.|下线，下游，直销体系|A person or group that is lower than another in a hierarchy or organization, especially in multi-level marketing.  A product or service in a later stage of production or distribution. In a direction away from a central point or authority.|例句：She recruited several new members into her down-line. 她招募了几个新成员加入她的下线组织。|近义词：subordinate, downstream, network|反义词: up-line, upstream|noun

down-line: /ˈdaʊnˌlaɪn/ |adj.|下线的，下游的|Relating to or involved in a down-line. Situated downstream.|例句：The down-line distributor received their products from the regional warehouse. 下线分销商从区域仓库接收他们的产品。|近义词：subordinate, downstream|反义词: up-line, upstream|adjective

down-line: /ˈdaʊnˌlaɪn/ |adv.|向下，下游地|To or at a lower level in a hierarchy or organization. In a direction away from a central point or authority.|例句：Information flows down-line from the head office to the branches. 信息从总部向下流动到分支机构。|近义词：downstream|反义词: up-line, upstream|adverb
