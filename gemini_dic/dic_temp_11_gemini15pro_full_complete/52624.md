diggable:/ˈdɪɡəbl/| adj.|可挖掘的，适于挖掘的|Something that is diggable is able to be dug.  It refers to the quality of earth or other material that allows it to be dug. |The soil in the garden is nice and diggable after the rain. 雨后的花园土壤变得湿润，很容易挖掘。|The ground here is too rocky and not very diggable.  这儿的地面石头太多，不太好挖。|近义词：excavable, workable|反义词：impenetrable, unworkable|词性:adjective
Etymology: From "dig" (verb) + "-able" (suffix). The verb "dig" comes from Old English "dician," meaning "to make a ditch, to dig." The suffix "-able" comes from Old French "-able" and ultimately from Latin "-abilis," meaning "capable of being."
