consequentialist:/ˌkɒnsɪˈkwɛnʃəlɪst/| adj. n.|后果论的，后果论者|Relating to or characteristic of consequentialism; a person who adheres to consequentialism, the ethical theory that the morality of an action is to be judged solely by its consequences.|例句：A consequentialist would argue that lying is acceptable if it leads to a positive outcome. 一个后果论者会认为，如果说谎会导致积极的结果，那么说谎是可以接受的。|近义词：teleological, utilitarian|反义词：deontological|词性：adjective, noun

consequentialism:/kənˌsɛkwənˈʃeɪəlɪzəm/| n.|后果论|The doctrine that the morality of an action is to be judged solely by its consequences.|例句：Consequentialism is often contrasted with deontology. 后果论经常与道义论进行对比。|近义词：teleology, utilitarianism|反义词：deontology|词性：noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From consequence + -ial + -ist.  "Consequence" traces back through Old French to Latin "consequentia" meaning "a following, a conclusion."
