censes:/ˈsɛnsɪz/| n.|人口普查；人口调查|A periodic official count of a country's population including information such as age, sex, occupation, etc.  A process of systematically acquiring and recording information about the members of a given population.|The government conducts a census every ten years. 政府每十年进行一次人口普查。|synonyms: enumeration, poll, survey|antonyms:  None (lack of a direct antonym, perhaps "undercounting" or "misrepresentation" could be considered somewhat opposite in effect)|noun

censes:/ˈsɛnsɪz/| v. (third-person singular simple present censes, present participle censing, simple past and past participle censed)|焚香；用香熏|To perfume with incense; to burn incense, especially as part of a religious ceremony.|The priest censed the altar.  牧师在祭坛上焚香。|synonyms: perfume, fumigate, incense|antonyms:  None (lack of a direct antonym)|verb
