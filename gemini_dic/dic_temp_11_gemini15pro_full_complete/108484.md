lay-waste:/leɪ ˈweɪst/ | vt. | 彻底摧毁，使荒芜 | To completely destroy or devastate a place, leaving it in ruins. | The invading army laid waste to the city, leaving nothing but rubble. 入侵的军队将这座城市夷为平地，只剩下瓦砾。 |  The hurricane laid waste to the coastal towns. 飓风摧毁了沿海城镇。 | synonyms: devastate, ruin, destroy, ravage | antonyms: build, construct, create, restore | verb

lay-waste:/leɪ ˈweɪst/ | n. | 毁灭，荒芜 (较少用作名词) | The state of being completely destroyed or devastated. | The lay-waste of the countryside after the war was heartbreaking. 战后乡村的荒芜令人心碎。 | The earthquake left a scene of utter lay-waste. 地震过后，一片废墟。 | synonyms: devastation, ruin, destruction | antonyms: construction, creation, restoration | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *leien wast*, from Old Norse *leggja í eyði* (“to lay waste”), equivalent to *lay* + *waste*.
