mundic:/ˈmʌndɪk/| n.|黄铁矿，愚人金|A metallic mineral, iron sulfide (FeS₂), often mistaken for gold because of its yellowish color and metallic luster. It is the most common sulfide mineral.|The miners thought they had struck gold, but it was only mundic.|Synonyms: iron pyrite, fool's gold|Antonyms: gold|noun
