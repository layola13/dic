debriefers:/diːˈbriːfərz/
n.
汇报人，询问者
People who conduct debriefings.  Those who question someone, typically after a mission or event, to obtain information.
The debriefers questioned the pilots after their return from the mission.  汇报员在飞行员完成任务返回后询问了他们。
After the exercise, the debriefers helped the team analyze their performance.  演习结束后，汇报员帮助团队分析了他们的表现。
近义词:interviewers, questioners
反义词:briefers, informants
词性:noun


debrief:/diːˈbriːf/
v.
汇报，询问
To question someone, typically after a mission or event, to obtain information.
The team was debriefed after the simulation.  团队在模拟结束后进行了汇报。
The manager debriefed the sales team after their meeting with the client.  经理在销售团队与客户会面后向他们进行了询问。
近义词:interview, question
反义词:brief, inform
词性:verb

词源:
From de- + brief.  The prefix "de-" often signifies reversal or removal.  "Brief" originally meant a concise statement or summary.  Thus, "debrief" implies removing or extracting information through questioning.
