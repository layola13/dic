nonpalpably:/nɒnˈpælpəbli/ | adv. | 不可感知地；难以捉摸地 | Not able to be felt by touch; intangible.  | The tension in the room was nonpalpably thick. 房间里的紧张气氛浓得化不开，却又难以捉摸。 | The fear was nonpalpably present, hanging heavy in the air.  恐惧感无形地存在着，沉重地笼罩在空气中。 | Synonyms: imperceptibly, intangibly, subtly | Antonyms: palpably, tangibly, obviously | adverb

Etymology:
From non- (not) + palpable (able to be touched or felt) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  Palpable comes from Late Latin palpabilis, from Latin palpare (to touch gently).
