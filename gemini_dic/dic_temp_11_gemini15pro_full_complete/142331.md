orthoform:/ˈɔːrθəfɔːrm/| n.|正字形，正写法|A word spelled or pronounced according to accepted usage; the conventional spelling or pronunciation of a word.|The orthoform of "colour" in American English is "color".|近义词：standard spelling, correct spelling|反义词:misspelling, nonstandard spelling|词性:noun
Etymology: From ortho- + -form. Ortho- comes from Greek orthos "straight, right, true." -form comes from Latin forma "form, shape, figure."
