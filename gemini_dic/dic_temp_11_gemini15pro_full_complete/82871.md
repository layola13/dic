gulling:/ˈɡʌlɪŋ/
verb
欺骗，愚弄
the act of deceiving or tricking someone
例句：They were angered by the gulling of the public by the media. 他们对媒体欺骗公众的行为感到愤怒。

例句：He was quite skilled at the art of gulling unsuspecting tourists.  他非常擅长欺骗毫无戒心的游客。

近义词：deceiving, tricking, duping, cheating, defrauding, swindling
反义词：honest, truthful, forthright
词性：Verb (动名词形式)


gull:/ɡʌl/
noun
鸥，笨蛋，易受骗的人
a large white seabird with a long neck and beak, or a person who is easily tricked or cheated
例句：The gulls wheeled overhead, crying plaintively. 海鸥在头顶盘旋，发出哀怨的叫声。

例句：He was a gull to believe her lies. 他真是个傻瓜，竟然相信了她的谎言。

近义词：dupe, fool, sucker, chump
反义词：skeptic, cynic
词性：Noun


gull:/ɡʌl/
verb
欺骗，愚弄
to deceive or trick someone
例句：He gulled them into believing he was a police officer. 他骗他们相信他是警察。

例句：She was gulled out of her life savings. 她被骗走了毕生积蓄。

近义词：deceive, trick, dupe, cheat, defraud, swindle
反义词：be honest with, enlighten
词性：Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gullen*, from Anglo-Norman *guller*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *gaula* (“to howl”) or Old French *guiller* (“to trick, deceive, dupe”).
