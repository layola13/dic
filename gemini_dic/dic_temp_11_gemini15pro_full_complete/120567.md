mired:/maɪərd/| adj.|陷入困境的，深陷泥潭的|stuck or entangled, as if in mud; involved in difficulties|例句：The car was mired in the mud. 汽车陷在泥里了。|例句：The project is mired in bureaucratic delays.  这个项目陷入了官僚主义的拖延之中。|近义词：bogged down, stuck, trapped, entangled|反义词：free, clear, unstuck|词性:形容词

mire:/maɪər/| n.|泥潭，困境|a stretch of swampy or boggy ground; a situation or state of difficulty, distress, or embarrassment|例句：The hikers got lost in the mire.  徒步旅行者在泥潭中迷路了。|例句：He found himself in a mire of debt. 他发现自己陷入了债务的泥潭。|近义词：swamp, bog, quagmire, morass|反义词：solid ground, advantage|词性:名词

mire:/maɪər/| v.|使陷入泥潭，使陷入困境|to cause to stick fast in mire; to involve in difficulties|例句：Heavy rains mired the construction site.  暴雨使建筑工地陷入了泥潭。|例句：The company was mired in legal battles.  这家公司陷入了法律纠纷的泥潭。|近义词：entangle, bog down, embroil|反义词：extricate, free|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *miren*, from Old Norse *myrr*, meaning "bog, swamp."  Cognate with Swedish *myr*.
