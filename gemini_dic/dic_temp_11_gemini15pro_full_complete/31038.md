chamberlain: /ˈtʃeɪmbərlɪn/ (US) /ˈtʃeɪmbəlɪn/ (UK)| n.| 侍从，管家；（市政当局的）财务主管|A chamberlain is an officer who manages the household of a monarch or other high-ranking person.  In some cities, a chamberlain is a treasurer.|The king's chamberlain announced the arrival of the queen. 国王的侍从宣布女王驾到。|The city chamberlain is responsible for managing the city's finances. 市财务主管负责管理城市财政。|synonyms: steward, attendant, treasurer|antonyms: N/A|noun

chamberlain: /ˈtʃeɪmbərlɪn/ (US) /ˈtʃeɪmbəlɪn/ (UK)| n.| 女侍从官|A chamberlain (female) is an officer who attends to a queen or princess.|The queen's chamberlain helped her dress for the coronation. 女王的侍从官帮她穿上了加冕礼服。|The princess dismissed her chamberlain for the evening. 公主让她的侍从官晚上离开了。|synonyms: lady-in-waiting, attendant|antonyms: N/A|noun
