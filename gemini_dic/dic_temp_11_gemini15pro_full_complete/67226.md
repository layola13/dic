expropriable:/ɪksˌprəʊprieɪbəl/ | adj. | 可征用的，可被没收的 |  Able to be expropriated; liable to expropriation.  This refers to property or assets that a government or other authority can legally seize, often for public use, with or without compensation. | The government declared that all land in the area was expropriable for the construction of the new highway. 政府宣布该地区所有土地均可被征用以修建新高速公路。|  The company's assets were deemed expropriable by the foreign government. 该公司的资产被外国政府视为可征用。 | confiscatable, seizable, appropriable | inalienable | adjective

expropriate:/eksˈproʊprieɪt/ | vt. | 征用，没收 | To take (property) from its owner for public use, with or without compensation.  | The government expropriated the land to build a new school. 政府征用了这块地来建一所新学校。 | The regime expropriated the company's assets without any compensation.  该政权没收了该公司的资产，没有给予任何补偿。 | confiscate, seize, appropriate | return, restore | verb
