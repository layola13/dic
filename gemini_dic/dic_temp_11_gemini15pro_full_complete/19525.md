biffin:/ˈbɪfɪn/
noun
苹果
a small, shrivelled apple.
The old tree bore only a few biffins. 这棵老树只结了几个小苹果。
近义词:crab apple, runt
反义词:(None -  lacks a direct antonym.  One could consider a large, perfect apple, but there's no single word for that contrast.)
词性:n.

biffin:/ˈbɪfɪn/
noun
(英国俚语)争吵，小争执
a small quarrel or squabble.
They had a bit of a biffin over the washing up. 他们因为洗碗的事吵了一架。
近义词:spat, tiff, quarrel
反义词:agreement, harmony
词性:n.

词源:
The etymology of "biffin" is uncertain.  For the apple sense, it may be related to "beefing," meaning to fatten or swell, ironically used for the small size.  For the quarrel sense, it may be imitative of a quick, sharp argument.
