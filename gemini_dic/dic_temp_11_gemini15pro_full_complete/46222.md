cystine:/ˈsɪstiːn/| n.|胱氨酸|An amino acid formed by the oxidation of two cysteine molecules joined by a disulfide bond. It is an oxidized dimer of cysteine and is less soluble than cysteine.|Cystine stones are a type of kidney stone. 胱氨酸结石是一种肾结石。|Cystine is important for the formation of hair and skin. 胱氨酸对头发和皮肤的形成很重要。|Near synonyms: dicysteine|Antonyms: cysteine|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cystine*, from Greek *kustis* (bladder, bag), because it was first isolated from bladder stones.  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kewh₂- (to swell).
