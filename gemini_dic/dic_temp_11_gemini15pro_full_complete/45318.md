cumene:/ˈkjuːmiːn/| n.|异丙苯; 枯烯|An aromatic hydrocarbon, C9H12, based on benzene with an isopropyl group attached. Used in the manufacture of phenol, acetone, and other organic compounds.|Cumene is an important intermediate in the production of phenol. 异丙苯是生产苯酚的重要中间体。|Cumene is used as a thinner in paints and lacquers. 异丙苯用作油漆和清漆中的稀释剂。|Synonyms: isopropylbenzene|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cumēnum (cumin) + -ene (suffix denoting unsaturated hydrocarbons).  The name reflects its original preparation from cuminic acid.
