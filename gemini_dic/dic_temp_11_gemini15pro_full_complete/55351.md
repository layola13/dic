distain:/dɪsˈteɪn/
| v. n.|鄙视，轻蔑；蔑视，轻蔑|To regard with contempt or scorn; despise.|She distained his offer of help, believing she could manage on her own. 她鄙视他提供的帮助，认为自己可以应付。|近义词：scorn, despise, contemn, spurn|反义词：admire, respect, esteem, value|verb
distain:/dɪsˈteɪn/
| n.|鄙视，轻蔑|Contempt or scorn.|He looked at the tabloid newspapers with distain. 他轻蔑地看着那些小报。|近义词：scorn, contempt, disdainfulness|反义词：admiration, respect, esteem|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desdaigner*, from *des-* (expressing negation) + *daigner* (“to deign”), from Latin *dignari* (“to consider worthy”). Ultimately from the same source as *dignity*.
