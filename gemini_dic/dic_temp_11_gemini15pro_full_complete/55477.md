distractable:/dɪˈstræktəbl/ | adj. | 易分心的，注意力不集中的 | Easily diverted from one's concentration or focus. |  The open office layout makes some people very distractable. 开放式办公室布局让一些人很容易分心。| synonyms: inattentive, unfocused, easily diverted | antonyms: focused, attentive, concentrated | adjective

distract:/dɪˈstrækt/ | verb | 使分心，分散注意力 | To draw someone's attention away from something. | The noise from the street distracted me from my work. 街上的噪音让我无法专心工作。| synonyms: divert, sidetrack, perturb | antonyms: focus, concentrate | verb

distraction:/dɪˈstrækʃn/ | noun | 分心，分心的事物 | Something that prevents someone from giving their full attention to something else. | Social media can be a huge distraction for students. 社交媒体对学生来说可能是一个巨大的干扰。| synonyms: diversion, interruption, disturbance | antonyms: focus, concentration | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *distractus, past participle of distrahere "to pull apart, divide, separate, distract," from dis- "apart" + trahere "to draw, pull."
