/ˈɡlɒs/ | /ˈɡlɑːs/ | v. |  注释；注解；掩饰；敷衍 | To gloser is to provide a brief explanation or translation of a difficult or unusual word or phrase. It can also mean to try to minimize the importance of something or to explain something away. |  The editor glossed the archaic terms in the footnotes. 编辑在脚注中注释了古代术语。

He tried to gloss over his mistakes, but they were obvious to everyone. 他试图掩盖自己的错误，但这些错误对每个人来说都很明显。 | synonym: annotate, explain, elucidate, gloss over, whitewash | antonym: obscure, confuse, complicate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gloser* "to explain, interpret," from Late Latin *glossare* "to explain a word," from Latin *glossa* "a word needing explanation, a foreign word," from Greek *glōssa* "tongue, language, foreign word."
