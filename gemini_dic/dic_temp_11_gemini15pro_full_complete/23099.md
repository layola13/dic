bountifuls:/ˈbaʊntɪfʊlz/| n. |大量的物品; 丰饶之物|A large amount of things, especially things that are good to eat or that are valuable. This word is not commonly used in modern English and “bounty” is preferred.  "Bountifuls" is grammatically correct as a plural noun but sounds archaic. |The harvest yielded bountifuls of fruits and vegetables this year. 今年的收成带来了大量的水果和蔬菜。|近义词：abundance, plenty, profusion, wealth|反义词：scarcity, dearth, lack, paucity|词性: noun

词源: From Middle English *bountifull, from Anglo-Norman *buntifull, from Old French *buntifull, from *bunti (“goodness, bounty”) + -full.
