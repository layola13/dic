liars:/ˈlaɪərz/| n.|骗子；说谎者|People who tell lies are called liars. 说谎的人被称为骗子。|They caught the liars red-handed. 他们当场抓住了那些骗子。|近义词：deceivers, fibbers, prevaricators|反义词：truth-tellers, honest people|词性:Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lier*, from Old English *lēogere*, agent noun from *lēogan* (“to lie, tell a falsehood”), from Proto-Germanic *leuganą* (“to lie, deny”), from Proto-Indo-European *leugh-* (“to lie, deceive”). Cognate with Scots *liar* (“liar”), West Frisian *liager* (“liar”), Dutch *leugenaar* (“liar”), German *Lügner* (“liar”), Danish *løgner* (“liar”), Swedish *lögnare* (“liar”), Icelandic *ljúgari* (“liar”).
