dibranchiata:/ˌdaɪbræŋˈkiɑːtə/
noun
双鳃类
a subclass of cephalopod mollusks characterized by having two gills, including squid, cuttlefish, and octopuses.
Dibranchiata are exclusively marine animals, known for their intelligence and complex behavior.
近义词:cephalopods
反义词: (No direct antonym, as it's a specific biological classification.  One could consider groups like gastropods or bivalves as contrasting mollusk classes.)
