overgrievously: /ˌoʊvərˈɡriːvəsli/ | adv. | 过度悲伤地，极其沉痛地 |  Excessively grievously; to a degree that causes extreme sorrow or distress. | He mourned overgrievously for the loss of his beloved pet. 他为失去心爱的宠物过度悲伤。| 近义词: excessively sadly, extremely sorrowfully, deeply mournfully | 反义词: joyfully, happily | 词性: 副词

词源:

"over-" (prefix):  Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi (meaning "over, above, beyond"). This prefix intensifies the meaning of the following word.
"grievous" (adjective): From Old French "grevos" (heavy, painful), ultimately from Latin "gravis" (heavy).
"-ly" (suffix):  Forms adverbs from adjectives.
