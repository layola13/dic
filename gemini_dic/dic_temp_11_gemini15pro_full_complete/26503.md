caballing:/kəˈbælɪŋ/
n.
密谋；策劃；陰謀
The act of conspiring or intriguing, typically for political purposes.
例句：The caballing of the opposition parties led to the downfall of the government. 反對黨的密謀導致了政府的垮台。

例句：There was much caballing behind the scenes before the election. 選舉前幕後有很多密謀。

近义词：plotting, scheming, intriguing, conspiring
反义词：cooperation, collaboration

词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the French "cabaler," meaning "to intrigue," and ultimately from the Hebrew "qabbalah," meaning "received tradition." The word gained notoriety in 17th-century England with the Cabal ministry, a group of ministers whose initials coincidentally spelled CABAL.
