jiltish:/ˈdʒɪltɪʃ/| n. | 突然抛弃；负心；被抛弃的状态 | The act of jilting someone; the state of being jilted. |  She suffered the jiltish of her fiancé just weeks before the wedding. 她遭受了未婚夫在婚礼前几周的抛弃。

jiltish:/ˈdʒɪltɪʃ/| adj. | 负心的；被抛弃的 | Of or pertaining to jilting or being jilted. | She felt jiltish and heartbroken after he left. 他离开后，她感到被抛弃，心碎不已。


ETYMOLOGY->
From jilt (v.) + -ish (suffix forming adjectives).  The verb "jilt" comes from "Gill" or "Jill," common nicknames for women, used generically for "sweetheart."  Over time, it evolved to mean "to discard a lover."

SYNONYMS->
(n.) abandonment, desertion, rejection
(adj.) heartbroken, abandoned, deserted

ANTONYMS->
(n.) commitment, loyalty, acceptance
(adj.) loved, cherished, devoted

WORD TYPE-> Noun, Adjective
