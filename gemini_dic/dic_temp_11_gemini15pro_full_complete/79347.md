glee: /ɡliː/
n.
欢乐，高兴，欣喜
A feeling of great happiness, especially from satisfaction or triumph.
例句：
She couldn't hide her glee at winning the lottery.
她无法掩饰自己中彩票的喜悦。

例句：
His eyes sparkled with glee.
他的眼睛里闪烁着喜悦的光芒。

近义词：joy, delight, elation, mirth, happiness
反义词：sorrow, sadness, grief, misery, despair
词性: 名词


glees: /ɡliːz/
n.
欢乐，高兴，欣喜（glee 的复数形式）
Plural form of glee, referring to multiple instances or expressions of great happiness.
例句：
Their glees were infectious, spreading throughout the room.
他们的喜悦很有感染力，弥漫了整个房间。

例句：
The Christmas season was filled with glees and laughter.
圣诞节充满了欢乐和笑声。

近义词：joys, delights, elations
反义词：sorrows, sadnesses, griefs
词性: 名词


词源:  From Middle English *gle, *gleo, from Old English glēo (“glee, mirth, music, entertainment”), from Proto-Germanic *glewją (“joy, amusement”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰlew- (“to shine, be glad”). Cognate with Scots glie (“glee, mirth”), West Frisian glee (“glee, fun”), Dutch glee (“glee”), German Glee (“glee”).
