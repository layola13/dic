ferromagnetism:/ˌfɛroʊˈmæɡnɪˌtɪzəm/| n. |铁磁性|The property of certain materials, such as iron, cobalt, and nickel, that exhibit strong attraction to magnets and can retain their magnetic properties after being magnetized. |Iron displays ferromagnetism. 铁表现出铁磁性。|Synonym: ferrimagnetism (similar, but with different microscopic arrangement)|Antonym: diamagnetism|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From ferro- (combining form meaning "iron") + magnetism.  "Ferro-" ultimately derives from Latin "ferrum" (iron).
