dov:/dʌv/ | n. | 鸽子 | A bird with a stout body and short legs, noted for its soft cooing.  A symbol of peace. | Example: The dov landed gently on the windowsill.  鸽子轻轻地落在窗台上。 | Example:  A white dov is often released at weddings as a symbol of love and peace.  白色的鸽子经常在婚礼上放飞，象征着爱与和平。| Synonym: pigeon, peace dove | Antonym: hawk (figuratively, as a symbol of war) | noun

Etymology: From Old English *dufe, from Proto-Germanic *dubōn, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰubʰ-.  Cognate with Dutch duif and German Taube.
