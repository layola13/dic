caroller's:/ˈkærələrz/ |n.|颂歌者的；唱颂歌的人的|A caroller's song filled the air. 颂歌者的歌声充满了空气。|The caroller's voice was clear and bright. 这位颂歌者的声音清晰而明亮。|近义词：singer's, chorister's|反义词: listener's|词性:名词所有格

词源: From "caroller" + "'s" (possessive suffix). "Caroller" comes from the Middle English "caroler," from Old French "caroller,"  ultimately from Late Latin "carolare" meaning "to sing a carol," derived from "carola," a circular dance and the song accompanying it.

用法说明:
"Caroller's" is the possessive form of "caroller," meaning "belonging to or associated with a person who sings carols." It's used to show ownership or relationship.
