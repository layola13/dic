before-the-fact:/bɪˈfɔːr ðə fækt/
| adj. |事前发生的，预先的；先期约定的|Relating to or occurring before the commission of a crime or act.|The before-the-fact investigation helped prevent the robbery.|
近义词:preliminary, prior, antecedent|
反义词:after-the-fact, subsequent, ex post facto|
词性:adjective


词源:
From the phrase "before the fact," referring to something happening before a significant event, particularly a crime.  "Fact" in this context refers to the act or deed itself.
