echeloned:/ˈeʃəlɒnd/ /ˈɛʃəlɑːnd/| adj.|梯状的，交错排列的|arranged in a series of steps or offset rows, like the steps of a ladder or the formation of troops or ships.|The troops were echeloned along the ridge. 部队沿着山脊交错排列。|近义词：staggered, tiered, layered|反义词：aligned, straight|词性:形容词
Etymology: From French *échelonner* "to form in echelons," from *échelon* "rung of a ladder," from Old French *eschelon*, from Frankish *skala* "ladder, stairs." Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *\*skalō* "something to climb," from Proto-Indo-European *\*kel-* "to be crooked, curved."
