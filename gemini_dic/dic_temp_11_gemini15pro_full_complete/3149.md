aetheogamous:/ˌiːθiˈɒɡəməs/
adjective
异配生殖的
Relating to aetheogamy, a type of sexual reproduction in which the gametes are dissimilar in size and form.  Specifically, it refers to the fusion of a non-motile female gamete (oosphere) with a motile male gamete (antherozoid or spermatozoid). This is a common form of reproduction in algae, fungi, and some plants.
Algae exhibit various forms of reproduction, including aetheogamous reproduction.  藻类表现出多种繁殖形式，包括异配生殖。
