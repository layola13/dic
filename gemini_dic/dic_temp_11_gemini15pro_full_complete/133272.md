nonexistentialisms:/ˌnɑːnɪɡˌzɪˈstɛnʃəlɪzəmz/ | n. | 非存在主义 |  The negation or absence of existentialist principles or beliefs.  A philosophy that rejects or denies the tenets of existentialism. |  His latest book explores themes of nonexistentialisms, arguing against the individual's primary responsibility for creating meaning.  Her art reflects a sense of nonexistentialisms, finding meaning in external sources rather than internal creation.| 近义词: anti-existentialism | 反义词: existentialism | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "non-" (prefix denoting negation) + "existentialism" (a philosophical theory or approach that emphasizes the existence of the individual person as a free and responsible agent determining their own development through acts of the will).
