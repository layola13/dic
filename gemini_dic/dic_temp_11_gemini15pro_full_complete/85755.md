ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English haga, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *hagō (“hedge, enclosure”). Related to Dutch haag (“hedge”), German Hag (“hedge, fenced area”). The meaning shifted from the thorny hedge itself to the fruit of the hawthorn, a common hedge plant.
