legitimatist: /ləˈdʒɪtɪmətɪst/
n.|正统主义者；维护正统王位继承者|A person who supports a legitimate sovereign, especially a monarch, or the principle of hereditary rule, often in opposition to a usurper or a republican government.|The aging legitimatist refused to acknowledge the new republic.  这位年迈的正统主义者拒绝承认新共和国。|The legitimatists plotted to restore the monarchy. 正统派密谋恢复君主制。|royalist, monarchist|republican, revolutionary|noun

legitimate: /ləˈdʒɪtɪmət/
adj.|合法的；正当的；合理的；嫡出的|Conforming to the law or to rules; justifiable; reasonable.|Her claim to the throne was legitimate. 她对王位的要求是合法的。|Is it legitimate to ask for a refund? 要求退款是否合理？|lawful, legal, valid, justifiable|illegal, unlawful, invalid, illegitimate|adjective

legitimate: /ləˈdʒɪtɪmeɪt/
vt.|使合法；证明…为合法|To make lawful; to show or affirm to be justified.|The government legitimized the use of force. 政府使使用武力合法化。|He tried to legitimate his claim to the property. 他试图证明他对这处房产的所有权是合法的。|legalize, validate, justify, authorize|invalidate, illegalize, prohibit, ban|verb
