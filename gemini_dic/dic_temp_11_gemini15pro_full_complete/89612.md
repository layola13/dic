hoodwinking:/hʊdˈwɪŋkɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
欺骗，蒙蔽
deceiving or tricking someone
The salesman was hoodwinking customers by selling them fake diamonds.  这个推销员用假钻石欺骗顾客。
They hoodwinked him into signing the contract. 他们欺骗他签了合同。
近义词: deceiving, tricking, duping, misleading, defrauding, swindling
反义词: enlightening, informing, being honest


hoodwink:/hʊdˈwɪŋk/
verb
欺骗，蒙蔽
to deceive or trick someone
The magician hoodwinked the audience with his sleight of hand. 魔术师用他的手法欺骗了观众。
She hoodwinked her parents into letting her go to the party. 她欺骗了她的父母，让他们同意她去参加聚会。
近义词: deceive, trick, dupe, mislead, defraud, swindle
反义词: enlighten, inform, be honest


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hood" (a covering for the head) + "wink" (to close the eyes briefly). Originally referred to blinding a hawk by covering its head, thus making it easier to control.  源自 "hood"（头部遮盖物）+ "wink"（短暂闭眼）。最初指的是通过遮盖鹰的头部来使其失明，从而更容易控制它。
