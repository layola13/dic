gabardine:/ˈɡæbərdiːn/
n.|茄丁布，华达呢|A tightly woven fabric of worsted, cotton, or other fiber, with a twill weave that makes diagonal ribs or cords.|She wore a classic gabardine trench coat. 她穿着一件经典的华达呢风衣。|Synonyms: twill, worsted|Antonyms: None|Noun
gabardines:/ˈɡæbərdiːnz/
n.|茄丁布（复数）,华达呢（复数）|Plural form of gabardine, referring to more than one piece or type of this fabric.|The store sells gabardines in a variety of colors. 这家商店出售各种颜色的华达呢。|Synonyms: twills, worsteds|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French gabardine, from Spanish gabardina, probably from Old Provençal galvardina, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Old North French wauvardine.
