crankbird: /ˈkræŋkbɜːrd/
noun
古怪的人；脾气坏的人；坏心眼的人
A crankbird is an eccentric or ill-tempered person, often considered disagreeable or difficult to deal with.
例句：He's a real crankbird; he complains about everything. 他真是个怪人，什么事他都要抱怨。

例句：Don't mind her, she's just an old crankbird. 别理她，她就是一个脾气古怪的老太太。
近义词：grump, curmudgeon, grouch
反义词：agreeable person, amiable person
词性：n.


ETYMOLOGY->
crank (n.):  From Old English *cranc, of uncertain origin.  It originally referred to a bend or twist, and later came to mean a machine part with a bent arm.
bird (n.): From Old English brid.
