cold-war:/ˈkoʊld wɔːr/| n. |冷战|a state of political hostility between countries characterized by threats, propaganda, and other measures short of open warfare, in particular that between the US and the Soviet Union and their respective allies after World War II.|The Cold War shaped global politics for decades. 冷战几十年来塑造了全球政治。|近义词：tension, hostility, standoff|反义词：détente, peace, cooperation|名词

cold-war:/ˈkoʊld wɔːr/| adj. |冷战的|relating to or characteristic of the Cold War.|The film depicted the cold-war tensions between the superpowers. 这部电影描绘了超级大国之间的冷战紧张局势。|近义词：hostile, tense|反义词：peaceful, cooperative|形容词
