harpies:/ˈhɑːrpiːz/
n.
鸟身女妖,泼妇,凶恶的女人
In Greek mythology, harpies were winged creatures with the face of a woman and the body of a bird, known for their foul smell and insatiable hunger.
在希腊神话中，哈耳皮埃是长着女人脸和鸟身的有翼生物，以其恶臭和贪得无厌的饥饿而闻名。

The shoppers at the sale became like harpies, fighting over the discounted merchandise.
促销活动的购物者变得像鸟身女妖一样，争抢打折商品。

近义词:shrews, vixens, termagants
反义词:angels


词性:名词(plural noun)

词源:From Late Latin *harpia, from Latin harpyia, from Ancient Greek ἅρπυια (hárpuia, “snatcher”), from ἅρπαξ (hárpax, “robber, plunderer”).
