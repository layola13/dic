ferally:/ˈfɪrəli/ (US), /ˈfɛrəli/ (UK)| adv.|野蛮地，凶猛地|In a wild or untamed manner; resembling a wild animal.|The dog, abandoned in the woods, began to behave ferally. 那只被遗弃在森林里的狗开始表现得像野兽一样。|近义词：wildly, savagely, fiercely|反义词：tamely, domestically, gently|词性:副词
词源: From feral + -ly. Feral comes from Late Middle English, from Latin feralis "of or belonging to wild animals," from fera "wild animal."
