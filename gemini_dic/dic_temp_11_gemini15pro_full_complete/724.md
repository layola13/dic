absentminded:/ˌæbsəntˈmaɪndɪd/| adj.|心不在焉的，健忘的|So lost in thought that one does not realize what one is doing or what is happening around one.  Characterized by a lack of attention to immediate surroundings or business.|He was so absentminded that he left his keys in the car. 他如此心不在焉，以至于把钥匙忘在了车里。|近义词：forgetful, preoccupied, inattentive, distracted|反义词：attentive, alert, mindful|词性:形容词

词源: From absent + minded.  "Absent" traces back to the Latin "absens," meaning "being away," while "minded" is of Germanic origin, relating to the mind or thought processes.  The combination reflects a state of mind being "away" from the present situation.
