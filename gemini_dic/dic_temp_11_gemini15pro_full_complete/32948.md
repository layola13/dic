chopping:/ˈtʃɒpɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈtʃɑːpɪŋ/ (US)| n. v. adj.| 切碎，砍，剁；(n.) 切碎的动作；(adj.) 波涛汹涌的 |The chopping of vegetables is a basic cooking skill. 切菜是一项基本的烹饪技能。| chopping is the present participle of the verb "chop," which means to cut something into pieces with a sharp tool like an axe or knife. It can be used as a noun (gerund), a verb (present participle), or less commonly, as an adjective to describe rough waters.  | She was chopping onions for the stew.  她正在为炖菜切洋葱。| 近义词：cutting, dicing, mincing, hacking | 反义词: joining, connecting |词性: noun, verb (present participle), adjective

chopping:/ˈtʃɒpɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈtʃɑːpɪŋ/ (US)| adj.| 波涛汹涌的 |The sea was chopping and dangerous. 海上波涛汹涌，十分危险。| As an adjective, "chopping" describes a rough sea with short, irregular waves. | The chopping waves made it difficult to sail. 波涛汹涌的海浪使航行变得困难。| 近义词：rough, turbulent, choppy | 反义词: calm, smooth |词性: adjective
