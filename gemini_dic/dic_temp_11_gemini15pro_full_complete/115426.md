mantle:/ˈmæntl/ (US) /ˈmæntəl/ (UK)| n. v.| 地幔，覆盖物；斗篷；责任，职位|A covering of a specified sort.  Something that covers, envelops, or conceals.|The snow mantle covered the fields. 雪覆盖着田野。|Synonyms: cloak, covering, blanket, layer, coat, shroud |Antonyms: uncover, expose|noun

mantle:/ˈmæntl/ (US) /ˈmæntəl/ (UK)| v.|覆盖；披上；脸红|To cover or envelop something.|A blush mantled her cheeks.  她的脸颊泛起了红晕。|Synonyms: cover, envelop, cloak, shroud|Antonyms: uncover, expose|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mantel*, from Latin *mantellum* "cloak, covering," perhaps related to *manus* "hand."  Originally referred to a loose cloak or cape.
