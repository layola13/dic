dirging:/ˈdɜːrdʒɪŋ/ | adj. | 悲叹的，哀悼的 | Singing or speaking in a slow, mournful tone, like a dirge. Expressing grief or sorrow. | The dirging wind whispered through the trees, a mournful sound that echoed the sadness in my heart.  风悲鸣着穿过树林，这哀伤的声音回应着我心中的悲伤。| 近义词: mournful, lamenting, grieving, plaintive | 反义词: joyful, celebratory, cheerful | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From the noun "dirge," which comes from the Middle English "dirige," from the Latin "dirige" (imperative of "dirigere," meaning "to direct"), referring to the opening words of Psalm 5:8, "Dirige, Domine, Deus meus, in conspectu tuo viam meam" ("Direct, O Lord my God, my way in thy sight"), which was part of the Office for the Dead.
