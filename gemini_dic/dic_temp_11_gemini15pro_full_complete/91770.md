hyperendemic:/ˌhaɪpərɛnˈdɛmɪk/ | adj. | 超地方性的 |  Constantly present at a high incidence and/or prevalence rate in a population or geographical area.  It implies a high level of infection or disease that is persistent and widespread within a specific community or region. | Malaria is hyperendemic in some regions of Africa.  疟疾在非洲一些地区是超地方性的。

The prevalence of HIV/AIDS is hyperendemic in several sub-Saharan African countries.  艾滋病毒/艾滋病在几个撒哈拉以南非洲国家非常流行。| 近义词: endemic, widespread | 反义词: sporadic, rare | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:  From hyper- (meaning “over, excessive”) + endemic (meaning “regularly found among particular people or in a certain area”).  Ultimately from Greek *hyper* (over) + *endēmos* (native).
