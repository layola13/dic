baroque's:/bəˈroʊks/| adj. n.|巴洛克式的，巴洛克风格的；巴洛克艺术（或音乐、建筑等）的|Of or relating to a style of European architecture, music, and art of the 17th and 18th centuries that followed mannerism and is characterized by ornate detail.  It's the possessive form of "baroque".  Usually refers to a characteristic or possession of the Baroque period or style. |The palace's decor is pure baroque's. 这座宫殿的装饰是纯粹的巴洛克风格。| The baroque's extravagance is evident in the elaborate carvings. 巴洛克风格的奢华在精美的雕刻中显而易见。 | 近义词: ornate, elaborate, flamboyant | 反义词: simple, plain, austere | 词性:  Possessive adjective (primarily), can also function as a possessive noun when referring to the style itself.

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *baroque* ("irregular pearl"), from Portuguese *barroco* (same meaning), perhaps from Latin *verruca* ("wart"). Originally used to describe pearls that were not perfectly spherical, the term was later applied to the artistic style characterized by its complex and extravagant ornamentation.
