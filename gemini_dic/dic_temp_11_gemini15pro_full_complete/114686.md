malign:/məˈlaɪn/| v. | 诽谤，污蔑；中伤 | to say bad things about someone publicly; to criticize someone unfairly |  The politician was maligned by the press. 这位政治家被媒体诽谤。| synonyms: slander, defame, libel, disparage, vilify | antonyms: praise, commend, laud, extol | verb
malign:/məˈlaɪn/| adj. | 有害的，恶性的 | causing harm; malevolent | The tumor was malign. 肿瘤是恶性的。| synonyms: harmful, evil, malignant, pernicious, baleful | antonyms: benign, beneficial, harmless | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *maligne (malign, wicked), from Latin malignus (ill-disposed, envious, spiteful), from male (badly) + -gnus (born), related to genus (birth, race, stock, kind).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *gen- (to give birth, beget).
