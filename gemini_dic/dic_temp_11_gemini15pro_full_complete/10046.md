aplomb:/əˈplɒm/ (US) /əˈplɑːm/ (UK)| n. |沉着；泰然自若；自信|Self-confidence or assurance, especially when in a demanding situation.|She handled the crisis with remarkable aplomb. 她以惊人的沉着应对了这场危机。|例句：Despite the chaos, she delivered the speech with great aplomb. 尽管现场一片混乱，她仍然泰然自若地发表了演讲。|近义词：poise, composure, assurance, self-possession|反义词：awkwardness, nervousness, anxiety|词性:名词

aplomb:/əˈplɒm/ (US) /əˈplɑːm/ (UK)| n. |垂直；铅直|Vertical position.|The tower stood with perfect aplomb. 这座塔巍然屹立。|例句：The tightrope walker maintained his aplomb despite the strong winds.  走钢丝的人即使在强风中也保持着平衡。|近义词：verticality, perpendicularity|反义词：inclination, slope|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French aplomb (“perpendicularity, equilibrium”), from à plomb (“on the plumb line, vertically”), from a (“to, at”) + plomb (“lead weight, plumb line”).  Ultimately from Latin plumbum (“lead”).
