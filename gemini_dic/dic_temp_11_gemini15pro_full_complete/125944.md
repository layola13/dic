musical: /ˈmjuːzɪkl/ (US), /ˈmjuːzɪkəl/ (UK)| adj. n.|音乐的；悦耳的；音乐剧|Relating to music; melodious; a play or film in which singing and dancing play an essential part.|例句：She has a very musical voice. 她有一副很悦耳的嗓音。|近义词：melodic, harmonious, tuneful|反义词：discordant, unmusical|词性：形容词

musical: /ˈmjuːzɪkl/ (US), /ˈmjuːzɪkəl/ (UK)| n.|音乐剧|A play or film in which singing and dancing play an essential part.|例句：We went to see a Broadway musical. 我们去看了一场百老汇音乐剧。|近义词：show, production|反义词：play (sometimes), drama (sometimes)|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *musical*, from Old French *musical*, from Late Latin *mūsicālis*, from Latin *mūsica* (“music”) + *-ālis* (suffix forming adjectives of relationship).  The Latin *mūsica* comes from Ancient Greek *μουσική* (mousikḗ, “music; any art over which the Muses presided”).
