overbravado:/ˌōvərbrəˈväːdō/
noun
过分的虚张声势；过度的逞能
Excessive bravado; a display of boldness that goes too far.
He showed overbravado in the face of danger, which actually masked his fear. 他在危险面前表现出过分的虚张声势，实际上掩盖了他的恐惧。
Synonyms: swagger, bluster, boastfulness, ostentation
Antonyms: humility, modesty, meekness, diffidence
n.

Etymology:
From over- + bravado.  "Over-" is an English prefix meaning "too much," "excessive," while "bravado" comes from Italian "bravata," meaning "bragging, boasting."
