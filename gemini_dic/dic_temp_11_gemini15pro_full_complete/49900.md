demus:/ˈdiːməs/| n. |人民，民众|(Latin) people, populace.  Specifically, the common people in ancient Rome.  It forms part of words like "demagogue" (a leader who appeals to popular passions and prejudices).|The Roman demus held considerable power in the early Republic.|Synonyms: populace, commoners, masses, plebs|Antonyms: aristocracy, elite, nobility|Noun
