jeremiad:/ˌdʒɛrɪˈmaɪæd/ | n. | 悲叹；哀歌；长篇的抱怨 | A long, mournful complaint or lamentation; a prolonged expression of grief or sorrow. |  The politician's speech was a jeremiad about the state of the economy. 这位政治家的演讲是对经济状况的长篇悲叹。

| Example:  His constant jeremiads about the youth of today are getting tiresome. 他对当今年轻人的不断抱怨令人厌烦。
| Synonyms: lamentation, complaint, moan, wail, diatribe | Antonyms:  celebration, praise, encomium | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Jeremiah, the name of a Hebrew prophet in the Old Testament who lamented the sins of his people and predicted their downfall.  耶利米是《旧约》中一位希伯来先知的的名字，他哀叹他的人民的罪恶并预言他们的垮台。
