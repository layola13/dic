grammaticalization:/ˌɡræmætɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |语法化|The process by which a lexical item or construction becomes grammatical, i.e., part of the grammar of a language.|Example 1: The English word "going" in "going to" has undergone grammaticalization, changing from a verb of motion to a future tense marker.  英语单词“going”在“going to”中经历了语法化，从一个运动动词变成了一个将来时标记。

Example 2: The development of the suffix "-ly" from the word "like" is an example of grammaticalization.  后缀“-ly”从单词“like”发展而来是语法化的一个例子。|近义词:grammaticising |反义词: (None -  This is a process, not a quality with an opposite) |词性:noun
