disapprovers:/ˌdɪsəˈpruːvərz/| n. |不赞成者，反对者|People who express or show disapproval; those who do not approve of something or someone.  Those who withhold approval.|例句：The disapprovers of the new policy voiced their concerns at the meeting.  新政策的反对者在会议上表达了他们的担忧。

例句：The disapprovers made their feelings known through a series of protests.  反对者通过一系列抗议活动表达了他们的感受。|近义词：critics, objectors, dissenters, opponents|反义词：supporters, proponents, approvers|词性:Noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From dis- + approver.  "Dis-" is a prefix indicating negation or opposition. "Approver" comes from the verb "approve," which ultimately derives from the Latin "approbare" (to test, prove, approve).
