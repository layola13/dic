batoidei:/ˈbeɪtɔɪdiːaɪ/
noun
鲼形目；鲼目鱼类
An order of cartilaginous fishes, including rays, skates, and sawfishes, characterized by flattened bodies, enlarged pectoral fins that are fused to the head, and ventral gill slits.
The giant manta ray is a member of the Batoidei order. 巨型蝠鲼是鲼形目中的一员。
synonyms: Rajiformes, Myliobatiformes (sometimes used as separate orders)
(No single, direct antonym for a taxonomic order.  One might consider "non-batoidean fishes" or name a specific contrasting order like "Selachii" (sharks))
