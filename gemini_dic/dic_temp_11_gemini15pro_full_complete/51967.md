diacoustics:/ˌdaɪəˈkuːstɪks/| n. |建筑声学|The branch of acoustics that deals with the qualities that determine the audibility and clarity of sounds in a building.|例句：The diacoustics of the concert hall were superb, allowing every note to resonate beautifully. 音乐厅的建筑声学效果极佳，每个音符都能产生美妙的共鸣。

例句：Poor diacoustics in the classroom made it difficult for students to hear the teacher clearly. 教室糟糕的建筑声学使得学生很难听清老师的声音。|近义词：architectural acoustics|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *dia- "through, across" + acoustics.
