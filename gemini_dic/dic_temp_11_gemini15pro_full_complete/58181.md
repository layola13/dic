drowse: /draʊz/ | n. | 瞌睡 | a light sleep |  He fell into a drowse. 他打起了瞌睡。 | synonyms: doze, nap, slumber | antonyms: awakening, alertness | noun

Etymology:  From Middle English *drousen, from Old English *drūsian (“to become sluggish, grow feeble”), from Proto-Germanic *drūsaną (“to sink, fall, droop”), from Proto-Indo-European *dhrew- (“to flow, run”). Related to dregs.
