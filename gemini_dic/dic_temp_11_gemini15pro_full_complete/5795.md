amarantus:/ˌæməˈræntəs/ | n. | 苋属植物; 苋菜; 不凋花 | A genus of widely distributed herbaceous plants including several weeds, ornamentals, and food plants such as love-lies-bleeding and pigweed.  | Example: Amarantus is a nutritious grain. 苋菜是一种营养丰富的谷物。 | Example:  The ancient Aztecs cultivated amaranthus for its seeds. 古代阿兹特克人种植苋菜以获取种子。 | Synonym: pigweed, love-lies-bleeding, tassel flower | Antonym: N/A | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *amarantus*, from Ancient Greek ἀμάραντος (amárantos, “unfading”), from ἀ- (a-, “not”) + μαραίνω (maraínō, “I wither, fade”).
