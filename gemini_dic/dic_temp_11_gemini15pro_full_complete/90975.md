hurry:/ˈhɜːri/ (US) /ˈhʌri/ (UK)| v. n.| 匆忙，急忙；催促|To move or act with speed, often due to a lack of time or a sense of urgency.  Also, the state of being rushed or the need to move quickly. |例句：
Hurry up, or we'll miss the train! 快点，否则我们会错过火车！
She hurried to finish the report before the deadline. 她急忙赶在截止日期前完成报告。|近义词：rush, hasten, speed up|反义词：delay, linger, dawdle|词性: verb, noun

hurry:/ˈhɜːri/ (US) /ˈhʌri/ (UK)| n.| 匆忙，急忙|The state of being rushed or the need to move quickly.|例句：
We were in a hurry to catch the plane. 我们急着赶飞机。
There's no hurry, take your time.  不用着急，慢慢来。|近义词：rush, haste|反义词：leisure, calmness|词性: noun

词源: From Middle English *hurrien, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to hurtle.
