fragrantness:/ˈfreɪɡrəntnəs/| n. |芬芳，香气|The quality of being fragrant; a sweet or delicate scent.|The fragrantness of the roses filled the air. 玫瑰的芬芳弥漫在空气中。|近义词：aroma, scent, perfume, fragrance|反义词：odor, stench, stink|词性:名词
Etymology: From fragrant + -ness. Fragrant comes from the Old French word "fragrant," which in turn derives from the Latin "fragrans," present participle of "fragrare" meaning "to smell sweet."  The suffix "-ness" is used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
