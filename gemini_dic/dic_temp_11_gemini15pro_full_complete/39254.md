congregationalism: /ˌkɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəlɪzəm/ (US) /ˌkɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəlɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |公理会制，会众制|A system of church government in which each local church is independent and self-governing.|The church follows congregationalism, meaning members elect their own leaders. 这座教堂遵循公理会制，这意味着成员选举自己的领导人。|Synonyms: independency, autonomy|Antonyms: hierarchy, episcopacy|Noun


congregational: /ˌkɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəl/ (US) /ˌkɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəl/ (UK)| adj. |公理会的，会众的|Relating to a congregation or congregationalism.|Congregational singing is an important part of the service.  会众唱诗是礼拜仪式的重要组成部分。|Synonyms: communal, collective|Antonyms: individual, private|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *congregationālis, from Latin congregātiō (“assembly”), from congregō (“collect, assemble”)
