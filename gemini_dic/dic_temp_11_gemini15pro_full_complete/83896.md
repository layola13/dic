half-ashamedly:/ˌhɑːf əˈʃeɪmɪdli/
| adv. |半羞愧地；略带羞愧地|In a way that shows slight shame or embarrassment.  Feeling or showing a little shame or embarrassment.|He half-ashamedly admitted he'd been crying. 他半羞愧地承认他一直在哭。|She half-ashamedly peeked at the letter. 她略带羞愧地偷看了那封信。|近义词：sheepishly, apologetically, guiltily, self-consciously|反义词：proudly, boldly, confidently|词性：副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "half" + "ashamed" + "-ly".  "Half" indicates a partial or incomplete state. "Ashamed" comes from the Old English "æscamod," past participle of "æscamian," meaning "to feel shame."  The suffix "-ly" turns the adjective "ashamed" into an adverb.
