cubisms:/ˈkjuːbɪzəmz/
n.
立体主义
A style of art that stresses abstract structure at the expense of other pictorial elements especially by displaying several aspects of the same object simultaneously in flattened, geometric forms.
Cubism was a revolutionary new approach to representing reality invented in around 1907 by artists Pablo Picasso and Georges Braque.
近义词:  Modernism, Abstract Art
反义词: Realism, Impressionism
词性: Noun
