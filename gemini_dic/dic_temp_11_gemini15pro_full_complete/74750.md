freewheel:/ˈfriːwiːl/| v. n.|自由旋转，滑行；无拘无束地行动；空转；(自行车)飞轮|To move forward in a vehicle without using power or effort, especially by going downhill; to act without concern for rules or consequences; a mechanism that allows a shaft or wheel to rotate freely in one direction but not the other.|The cyclist freewheeled down the hill.  骑自行车的人顺着山坡滑行下去。|Synonyms: coast, drift, glide, roll|Antonyms: pedal, drive, struggle|verb

freewheel:/ˈfriːwiːl/| n.|自由旋转，滑行；无拘无束地行动；空转；(自行车)飞轮|The act of moving forward without using power, especially in a vehicle; a mechanism, typically on a bicycle, that allows the driven wheel to rotate freely while the pedals remain stationary.|The freewheel mechanism allows the bike to coast downhill.  飞轮机构使自行车可以下坡滑行。|Synonyms: coasting, freewheeling (as a noun)|Antonyms: pedaling, driving|noun
