lithotomy: /lɪˈθɒtəmi/ (US) /lɪˈθɒtəmi/ (UK)| n.| 剖石术；膀胱切开术；切石位|Surgical incision for removing a stone, especially from the bladder; the position in which a patient is placed for this operation (legs raised and abducted, knees and hips flexed). |The patient was placed in the lithotomy position for the surgery. 病人被置于截石位进行手术。|Synonyms: cystolithotomy,  lithiasis surgery|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

lithotomy: /lɪˈθɒtəmi/ (US) /lɪˈθɒtəmi/ (UK)| n.| 切石位|The position in which a patient is placed for certain medical examinations or procedures, especially gynecological or urological ones. |The doctor examined the patient in the lithotomy position. 医生以截石位检查病人。|Synonyms: dorsal lithotomy position|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
