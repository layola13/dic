cystoidea:/sɪˈstɔɪdiə/ | n. | 海蕾纲，囊胞纲 | An extinct class of echinoderms, related to blastoids and crinoids, that existed from the Ordovician to the Devonian periods. They were characterized by a sac-like body enclosed in calcareous plates. | Cystoidea were abundant in the Paleozoic seas.  海蕾纲在古生代海洋中很丰富。 | Cystoidea fossils are often found in limestone deposits.  海蕾纲化石经常在石灰岩沉积物中被发现。 | 近义词:  blastoids, crinoids | 反义词: None | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek kystis (“bladder, pouch”) + -oidea.
