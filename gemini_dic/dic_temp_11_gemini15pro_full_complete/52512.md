differentiated:/ˌdɪfəˈrenʃieɪtɪd/ | adj. | 区分的，差异化的 | made or become different in the process of growth or development |  The program offers differentiated instruction to meet the needs of diverse learners. 该项目提供差异化教学，以满足不同学习者的需求。 | differentiated cells 分化的细胞 | synonyms: specialized, distinct, diverse | antonyms: undifferentiated, homogeneous, uniform | adjective

differentiate:/ˌdɪfəˈrenʃieɪt/ | v. | 区分，区别，使不同；构成…间的差异 | 1. to make or become different 2. to recognize or ascertain what makes (someone or something) different |  It's important to differentiate between fact and opinion. 区分事实和观点很重要。| He differentiated himself from his competitors by offering excellent customer service. 他通过提供优质的客户服务使自己与竞争对手区分开来。 | synonyms: distinguish, discriminate, separate | antonyms: confuse, blend, homogenize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin differentiatus, past participle of differentiare "to make different," from Latin different-, differens, present participle of differre "to carry different ways, scatter, differ," from dis- "apart, away" + ferre "to carry, bear."
