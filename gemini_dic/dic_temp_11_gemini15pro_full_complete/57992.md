drivels:/ˈdrɪvəlz/| n.|胡言乱语，废话，口水|Silly, nonsensical talk or writing; saliva running from the mouth.|He was talking such drivels that I just tuned him out. 他一直在说些废话，我干脆就不听了。|近义词：nonsense, twaddle, gibberish, balderdash, saliva, drool|反义词:sense, wisdom|词性:名词

drivel:/ˈdrɪvəl/| v.|说胡话，胡扯，流口水|To talk nonsense; to let saliva run from the mouth.|He driveled on and on about nothing important. 他不停地胡扯些无关紧要的事。|近义词：babble, ramble, drool|反义词:articulate, enunciate|词性:动词

词源: From Middle English *drevelen*, frequentative of *dreven* (“to drive, to push”), from Old Norse *drífa* (“to drift, to drive”).  The sense of "to slaver" arose from the image of saliva being driven or pushed from the mouth.
