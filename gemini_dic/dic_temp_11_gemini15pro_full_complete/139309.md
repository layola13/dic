obloquious: /əˈbloʊkwiəs/ /əˈblɑːkwiəs/ | adj. | 谄媚的，逢迎的 | Excessively compliant and flattering; fawning.  Characterized by obsequious behavior, such as excessive flattery or deference. | He was so obloquious to the boss that it was sickening to watch. 他对老板如此谄媚，让人看着都觉得恶心。

The waiter's obloquious manner, while attentive, felt insincere. 服务员谄媚的态度虽然殷勤，但感觉很不真诚。

| 近义词: servile, subservient, fawning, sycophantic, flattering | 反义词: independent, forthright, assertive, candid | 词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *obloqui*,  meaning "to speak against" or "to contradict," from *ob-* ("against") + *loqui* ("to speak"). The meaning shifted over time to refer to someone who speaks favorably to gain favor, hence "fawning."
