lackadaisically: /ˌlækəˈdeɪzɪkli/ | adv. | 懒洋洋地，无精打采地 | Showing a lack of interest or enthusiasm; listlessly. | He strolled lackadaisically through the park, barely noticing the scenery. 他懒洋洋地在公园里漫步，几乎没有注意到周围的景色。|  She lackadaisically stirred her coffee, lost in thought. 她漫不经心地搅动着咖啡，思绪飘忽。 | 近义词: listlessly, languidly, indolently, apathetically | 反义词: energetically, enthusiastically, vigorously | 词性: 副词

词源:  From lackadaisical (adjective) + -ly (adverb suffix).  Lackadaisical itself comes from "lackaday," an archaic interjection expressing sorrow or regret, often shortened to "alack."  Over time, the meaning shifted from expressing sorrow to indicating a lack of spirit or enthusiasm.
