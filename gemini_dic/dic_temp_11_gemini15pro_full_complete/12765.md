assignations: /ˌæsɪɡˈneɪʃənz/ | n. |约会，幽会；（尤指秘密的）指定；分配，指派|An assignation is a secret meeting, especially one arranged for lovers to meet. It can also refer to the act of assigning something. |例句：The lovers met at their usual assignation. 恋人们在他们通常幽会的地方见面。


例句：The assignation of tasks was done fairly. 任务分配得很公平。|近义词：appointments, rendezvous, trysts, allocations, assignments|反义词: cancellations, withdrawals|词性: 名词(plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *assignacioun*, from Old French *assignacion*, from Late Latin *assignātiō*, from Latin *assignātus*, past participle of *assignāre* (to assign). Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European root *segh-* (to hold, have).
