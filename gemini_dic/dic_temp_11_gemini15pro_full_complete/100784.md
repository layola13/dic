intrenchment: /ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt/ | n. | 壕沟；防御工事；巩固；(权力、习惯等的)牢固确立 |  The act of digging trenches for defense; the state of being entrenched or securely established; a firmly established position or practice. | The army's intrenchment protected them from the enemy attack.  军队构筑的壕沟保护他们免受敌人攻击。| synonyms: fortification, entrenchment, consolidation, establishment | antonyms: weakness, vulnerability | noun

intrenchment: /ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt/ | n. | (尤指削减开支、裁员等)紧缩 |  A defensive measure, especially cutting costs or staff. | The company announced intrenchments to deal with the economic downturn. 公司宣布紧缩开支以应对经济衰退。 | synonyms: cutbacks, reductions, austerity | antonyms: expansion, growth | noun
