ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *more, *mo, from Old English *māra, comparative of *micel (“much, great”), from Proto-Germanic *maizô, comparative of *mikilaz (“great, big”), from Proto-Indo-European *meǵh₂- (“great, big”). Cognate with Scots mair (“more”), West Frisian mear (“more”), Dutch meer (“more”), German mehr (“more”), Danish mere (“more”), Swedish mer (“more”), Icelandic meira (“more”), Latin magnus (“great, large”), Ancient Greek μέγας (mégas, “great, large”).
