draconianism:/dreɪˈkoʊniənɪzəm/| n.|严酷；苛刻；严厉|Excessive harshness or severity in laws or their application.  Derived from Draco, an Athenian law-maker of the 7th century BC, whose code of laws prescribed the death penalty for many minor offenses.|The draconianism of the new regime was evident in the strict curfews and harsh punishments for even minor infractions. 新政权的严酷体现在严格的宵禁和对轻微违规行为的严厉惩罚。|synonyms: severity, harshness, rigor, cruelty, stringency|antonyms: leniency, mercy, clemency, moderation|noun
