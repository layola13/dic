magistrate:/ˈmædʒɪstreɪt/ | n. | 地方法官；治安官 | A civil officer who administers the law, especially one who conducts a court dealing with minor offenses and holds preliminary hearings for more serious ones. |  The magistrate presided over the local court. 地方法官主持了地方的法庭。| synonyms: judge, justice of the peace, adjudicator | antonyms: defendant, accused, plaintiff | noun

magistrate:/ˈmædʒɪstreɪt/ | n. | 行政长官 | A government official, often in a high position. (Less common usage) | The Roman magistrate held considerable power.  这位罗马行政长官拥有相当大的权力。 | synonyms: official, administrator, functionary | antonyms:  citizen, civilian, commoner | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *magistrat*, from Latin *magistrātus* ("magistracy, civil office, public official, magistrate"), from *magister* ("master, chief, director, superintendent"), from *magis* ("more"). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\meǵh₂s* ("great").
