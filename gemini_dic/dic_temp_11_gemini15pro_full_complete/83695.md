haggle:/ˈhæɡəl/| v.|讨价还价，争论|To argue about the terms of a sale or agreement, especially about the price.|例句：They haggled over the price of the car for hours. 他们为了这辆车的价格讨价还价了几个小时。

例句：I managed to haggle the price down by ten percent.  我设法把价格砍了10%。|近义词：bargain, barter, negotiate, dicker|反义词:agree, concede|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *höggva ("to hew, chop"), of Germanic origin. Related to Dutch hakken ("to chop") and German hacken ("to hack, chop").  The original sense was "to chop roughly or clumsily," which evolved to "hack off a price," and then to the current meaning of "bargain."
