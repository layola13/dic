coleslaw:/ˈkoʊlˌslɔː/ (US) /ˈkəʊlˌslɔː/ (UK)
n.|凉拌卷心菜丝|A salad consisting primarily of finely shredded raw cabbage with a salad dressing, commonly either vinaigrette or mayonnaise. |We had burgers and coleslaw for dinner. 我们晚餐吃了汉堡和凉拌卷心菜丝。|Synonyms: cabbage salad|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "hot dish," "cooked vegetable")|Noun


Etymology:
From Dutch *koolsla*, from kool "cabbage" + sla "salad".  First attested in English in the 18th century.
