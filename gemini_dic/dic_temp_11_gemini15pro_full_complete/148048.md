overrun:/ˌoʊvərˈrʌn/ (US), /ˌəʊvəˈrʌn/ (UK)| v. n. adj.| 超过；泛滥；蔓延；侵占 |To spread over or occupy (a place) in large numbers; to exceed (a limit, especially of time or cost).  |例句：The project overran its budget by millions.  这个项目超出预算数百万。|近义词：overwhelm, infest, exceed, surpass|反义词：underestimate, underspend|verb

overrun:/ˌoʊvərˈrʌn/ (US), /ˌəʊvəˈrʌn/ (UK)| n.| 超出；超过限度；泛滥 |An act or instance of overrunning; the amount by which something overruns. |例句：The overrun of the project caused significant delays.  项目的超出导致了严重的延误。|近义词：excess, surplus, overflow|反义词：deficit, shortage|noun


overrun:/ˌoʊvərˈrʌn/ (US), /ˌəʊvəˈrʌn/ (UK)| adj.| 超过的；泛滥的；蔓延的 |Having spread over or occupied (a place) in large numbers. |例句：The garden was overrun with weeds.  花园里杂草丛生。|近义词：overgrown, infested, rampant|反义词：controlled, contained|adjective
