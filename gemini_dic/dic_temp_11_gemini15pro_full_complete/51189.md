destabilizing:/diːˈsteɪbəlaɪzɪŋ/
adjective
使不稳定的，破坏稳定的
tending to make something unstable; undermining stability.
The economic crisis had a destabilizing effect on the region. 经济危机对该地区造成了不稳定的影响。
Synonyms: disrupting, upsetting, undermining, weakening
Antonyms: stabilizing, strengthening, reinforcing
词性: 形容词


destabilize:/diːˈsteɪbəlaɪz/
verb
使不稳定，破坏稳定
to make something unstable; to undermine stability.
The sudden change in leadership destabilized the country. 突然的领导层变动使国家不稳定。
Synonyms: disrupt, upset, undermine, weaken
Antonyms: stabilize, strengthen, reinforce
词性: 动词

destabilization:/diːˌsteɪbələˈzeɪʃən/
noun
不稳定，破坏稳定
the act of making something unstable; the state of being unstable.
The destabilization of the government led to widespread chaos. 政府的不稳定导致了 widespread chaos.
Synonyms: disruption, upheaval, instability
Antonyms: stabilization, stability
词性: 名词


词源:  From de- + stabilize + -ing/-ize/-ation. "Stabilize" comes from Late Latin stabilis "firm, steadfast," ultimately from PIE *sta- "to stand."
