oddballs:/ˈɑːdbɔːlz/| n.|怪人，古怪的人；不合群的人；异类|People who are eccentric or nonconformist are often called oddballs.  Those who don't fit in with the mainstream can be labeled as oddballs.|He was considered an oddball because he wore a top hat every day. 他每天都戴着高顶礼帽，被认为是个怪人。

She's a bit of an oddball, but she's also brilliant. 她有点古怪，但也很聪明。|近义词：eccentric, weirdo, nonconformist, maverick|反义词:conformist, conventional, normal|词性:名词

词源:  A compound of "odd" (meaning strange or unusual) and "ball" (in the informal sense of "person"). The term likely originated in the early 20th century.
