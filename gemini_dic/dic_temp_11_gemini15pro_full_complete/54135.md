disenfranchisement:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/ | n. | 剥夺权利；褫夺公权 |The deprivation of the right to vote or other rights of citizenship. |  Many people were concerned about the disenfranchisement of felons. 许多人关心重罪犯被剥夺公民权的问题。| Synonyms: disqualification, exclusion, deprivation | Antonyms: enfranchisement, empowerment | noun

disenfranchisement:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/ | n. | 丧失权利；无力感 | A feeling of powerlessness or exclusion from society. | The economic crisis led to a sense of disenfranchisement among young people. 经济危机导致年轻人产生了一种无力感。 | Synonyms: alienation, marginalization, helplessness | Antonyms: empowerment, inclusion | noun
