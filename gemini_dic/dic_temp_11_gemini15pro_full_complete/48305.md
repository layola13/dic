decrepitate:/dɪˈkrɛpɪteɪt/| adj. v. |衰老的，破旧的；使衰老，使破旧|Weakened by old age; worn out.  To make decrepit.|例句：The decrepitate building was finally demolished. 这栋破旧的建筑最终被拆除了。|近义词：infirm, frail, dilapidated, feeble, run-down|反义词：robust, sturdy, strong, vigorous|词性:形容词，动词
decrepitate:/dɪˈkrɛpɪteɪt/| adj. v. |衰老的，破旧的；使衰老，使破旧|To cause something to become decrepit.  To weaken or impair with age.|例句：Years of neglect had decrepitated the once grand mansion. 多年的忽视使这座曾经宏伟的宅邸变得破败不堪。|近义词：weaken, deteriorate, enfeeble|反义词：strengthen, rejuvenate, revitalize|词性:形容词，动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *decrepitus*, past participle of *decrepare* "to crackle, rattle," from *de-* "down, completely" + *crepare* "to crack, creak, rattle, break."
