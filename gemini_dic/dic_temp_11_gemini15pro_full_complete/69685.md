fenes:/ˈfiːniːz/| n. |窗户, 窗格 (尤指教堂的)|A transparent part of a building's wall that allows light to pass through, often composed of glass set in a frame, especially in a church.|The stained-glass fenes of the cathedral were breathtaking. 大教堂的彩色玻璃窗令人叹为观止。|Synonyms: window, pane, aperture, casement|Antonyms: wall, barrier, blockage|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *fenester, *fenestre, from Old French fenestre, from Latin fenestra (“window, opening”), from Proto-Italic *fenestrā, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂wenh₁- (“to blow, to fan, to winnow”). Cognate with Spanish fenestra (“window”), Italian finestra (“window”).
