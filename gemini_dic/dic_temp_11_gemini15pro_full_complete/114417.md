make rounds:/meɪk raʊndz/
|v.|查房，巡视，四处走动|To visit patients in a hospital or other medical facility; to make a regular circuit of inspection or visits.|The doctor makes rounds every morning to check on his patients. 医生每天早上查房，查看病人的情况。|Synonyms: visit, inspect, patrol|Antonyms: neglect, ignore|verb

make rounds:/meɪk raʊndz/
|v.|四处走动，传播|To circulate; to spread from person to person.|The rumor made rounds quickly through the office. 谣言很快在办公室里传开了。|Synonyms: circulate, spread, disseminate|Antonyms: suppress, conceal|verb
