hyperexcitements:/ˌhaɪpərɪkˈsaɪtmənts/| n. |过度兴奋，极度兴奋|A state of being excessively or extremely excited.  A plural form of hyperexcitement.|The hyperexcitements of the crowd during the concert were palpable.  演唱会期间人群的极度兴奋 palpable palpable.|近义词：overexcitement, agitation, frenzy|反义词：calmness, tranquility|词性:noun

Etymology:
From hyper- (prefix meaning "over, excessive") + excitement.  "Hyper-" comes from the Greek "hyper" (ὑπέρ) meaning "over, above, beyond". "Excitement" comes from the Late Middle English "excitemente,"  from the Old French "escitement," and ultimately from the Latin "excitare" meaning "to rouse, stir up."
