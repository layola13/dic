inconspicuousness:/ˌɪnkənˈspɪkjuəsnəs/| n. |不显眼，不引人注目|The state or quality of not being easily noticed or attracting attention.|Her inconspicuousness in the crowd made it easy for her to observe unnoticed. 她在一群人中毫不起眼，让她可以不被注意地观察。|近义词：unobtrusiveness, inconspicuousness, anonymity|反义词：conspicuousness, prominence, noticeability|词性:noun

inconspicuous:/ˌɪnkənˈspɪkjuəs/| adj. |不显眼的，不引人注目的|Not clearly visible or attracting attention.|She wore inconspicuous clothing so as not to draw attention to herself. 她穿着不显眼的衣服，以免引起别人的注意。|近义词：unobtrusive, unnoticeable, low-key|反义词：conspicuous, prominent, noticeable|词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *inconspicuus, from in- "not" + conspicuus "visible, striking."  The suffix "-ness" transforms the adjective into a noun denoting the state or quality.
