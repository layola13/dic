canot:/kəˈnɒt/ | n. |小艇，独木舟|A small, light boat, especially one propelled by paddles or oars. |We took a canot out on the lake. 我们在湖上划了一艘小艇。|Synonyms: canoe, skiff, dinghy, rowboat |Antonyms: ship, yacht|noun


Etymology->
From French *canot*, from Canadian French *canot*, borrowed from Algonquian languages (probably Eastern Abenaki *kánú* or Proto-Algonquian *ka·nuhs-*, *ka·now*-). Doublet of *canoe*.
