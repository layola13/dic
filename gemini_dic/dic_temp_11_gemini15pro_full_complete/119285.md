micro-electrode:/ˌmaɪkroʊɪˈlɛktroʊd/ | n. | 微电极 | A very small electrode, especially one used in delicate physiological experiments. |  Micro-electrodes are used to measure the electrical activity of single cells. 微电极用于测量单个细胞的电活动。|Synonyms:  microprobe, microsensor | Antonyms: macroelectrode (less common) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
micro- (prefix): from Greek mikrós "small"
electrode: from electr- + -ode. Electr- from Greek ēlektron "amber" (referring to static electricity). -ode from Greek hodós "way, path".
