granddaddy:/ˈɡrændædi/ (US), /ˈɡrændædi/ (UK)| n.|祖父；鼻祖；元老|A grandfather. Also used to refer to something that is the first or most important of its kind.|My granddaddy used to tell me stories about the war. 我的祖父过去常给我讲战争故事。|近义词：grandfather, ancestor, forefather, progenitor|反义词:grandson, descendant|noun

granddaddy:/ˈɡrændædi/ (US), /ˈɡrændædi/ (UK)| adj.|(informal) large, important, and impressive|Exceptionally large, impressive, or important.|That's a granddaddy of a fish you caught! 你钓到了一条超级大的鱼！|近义词：huge, enormous, massive, gigantic|反义词:small, tiny, minuscule|adjective
