overscrupulous:/ˌoʊvərˈskruːpjələs/| adj.|过分谨慎的，过于拘泥的|Having or showing excessive concern with fine points of behavior, morality, or propriety.|He was so overscrupulous about following the rules that he missed the deadline.|近义词:meticulous, fastidious, conscientious, punctilious|反义词:careless, negligent, unscrupulous, remiss|词性:形容词

词源:
From over- + scrupulous.  "Scrupulous" comes from the Latin "scrupulus," meaning a small sharp stone, hence a source of anxiety or uneasiness.  "Over-" simply intensifies the meaning to excessive.
