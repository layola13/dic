microphonics:/ˌmaɪkrəˈfɒnɪks/ (US) /ˌmaɪkrəˈfɒnɪks/ (UK)| n.|话筒效应；啸叫声；微音器效应|The unwanted noise produced in electronic devices, especially audio equipment, caused by mechanical vibrations being converted into electrical signals.  It's often a low-frequency rumble or howl.|The band's performance was marred by microphonics from the stage monitors. 乐队的演出被舞台监听音箱的啸叫声破坏了。|Synonyms: feedback, ringing, howl|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but consider: silence, quiet)|noun

microphonic:/ˌmaɪkrəˈfɒnɪk/ (US) /ˌmaɪkrəˈfɒnɪk/ (UK)| adj.|对振动敏感的；产生话筒效应的|Susceptible to producing microphonics; exhibiting microphonic effects.|The old vacuum tube was microphonic, creating a humming sound in the amplifier.  这个旧的真空管对振动很敏感，在放大器中产生了嗡嗡声。|Synonyms: sensitive to vibrations|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but consider: vibration-damped, stable)|adjective
