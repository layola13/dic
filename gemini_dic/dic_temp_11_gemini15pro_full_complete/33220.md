chronique: /krɔˈnik/ (UK), /ˈkrɑːnɪk/ (US)| n. |编年史，记事，专栏，（尤指报纸上的）评论|A chronicle is a factual written account of important or historical events in the order of their occurrence. |例句：He kept a chronicle of his life during the war.  他记录了战争期间的生活。|例句：She writes a weekly chronicle for the local newspaper. 她为当地报纸撰写每周专栏。 |近义词：record, history, account, narrative, annals, diary|反义词:fiction|词性:noun

chronique: /krɔˈnik/ (UK), /ˈkrɑːnɪk/ (US)| adj. |慢性的，长期的|Chronic describes something that continues for a long time or keeps occurring. This term is often used in the context of illnesses.|例句：He suffers from chronic back pain. 他患有慢性背痛。|例句：The hospital has a shortage of beds for chronic patients. 这家医院缺少慢性病患者的床位。|近义词：persistent, long-lasting, continuing, constant, inveterate, habitual|反义词:acute, temporary|词性:adjective
