chooks:/tʃʊks/ | n. | 母鸡；(澳大利亚俚语)鸡；(澳大利亚俚语)家禽 | Chickens, especially hens. In Australian slang, it can refer to poultry in general. |例句：The chooks are out scratching in the garden. 母鸡们在花园里扒拉着找食。

例句：He went out to feed the chooks. 他出去喂鸡了。|近义词：chickens, hens, fowl (more general)|反义词: rooster (specifically male chickens) |词性: noun


chook:/tʃʊk/ | v. | (澳大利亚俚语)扔；抛；丢 | To throw something. Primarily used in Australian slang. |例句：Chook it over here! 把它扔过来！

例句：He chooked the ball to his friend. 他把球扔给了他的朋友。|近义词：throw, toss, fling |反义词: catch, receive |词性: verb

词源: Chook likely originates from the British dialect word "chuck," meaning to throw. Its application to chickens is thought to derive from the practice of throwing scraps to poultry.
