ghostwritten:/ˈɡoʊstˌrɪtn/ | adj. | 代笔的，由他人代写的 | Written by someone else, but published under another person's name. | The autobiography was ghostwritten by a famous journalist.  这本自传是由一位著名记者代笔的。|  The politician's speeches were often ghostwritten. 这位政客的演讲稿经常是由他人代写的。| Synonym: ghost-authored | Antonym: self-authored | adjective


ghostwrite:/ˈɡoʊstˌraɪt/ | vt. | 为…捉刀，代笔 | To write something for someone else, who will then put their name on it as if they had written it themselves. | He ghostwrote the singer's autobiography. 他为这位歌手的自传代笔。| She makes a living ghostwriting novels for celebrities. 她以替名人代写小说为生。 | Synonym: ghost-author | Antonym: author | verb


ghostwriter:/ˈɡoʊstˌraɪtər/ | n. | 代笔人，捉刀人 | Someone who writes something for someone else, who will then put their name on it as if they had written it themselves. | The celebrity hired a ghostwriter to work on his memoirs.  这位名人雇佣了一位代笔人来撰写他的回忆录。| Many politicians employ ghostwriters to prepare their speeches. 许多政治家雇用代笔人来准备他们的演讲稿。| Synonym: ghost-author | Antonym: author | noun
