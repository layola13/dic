damn-well:/dæmˈwel/ | adv. | 确实地，绝对地，毫无疑问地 | Used to emphasize that something is true or will definitely happen. It's an informal and slightly emphatic way of saying "certainly" or "definitely," often with a hint of annoyance or defiance.| I damn-well told him not to go! 我确实告诉过他别去！| 近义词: certainly, definitely, absolutely | 反义词: doubtfully, possibly | 词性: 副词
damn:/dæm/ | adj. | 讨厌的，可恶的 | expressing annoyance or anger. | This damn computer keeps crashing! 这该死的电脑老是崩溃！| 近义词: cursed, blasted | 反义词: blessed | 词性: 形容词
well:/wel/ | adv. | 好，很好地 | in a good or satisfactory way. | He plays the piano well. 他钢琴弹得很好。| 近义词: good, fine | 反义词: badly, poorly | 词性: 副词

词源:
damn: From Middle English *damnen, from Old French damner, from Latin damnāre, present participle of damnō (“I condemn, sentence, doom, blame, reject”), from Proto-Italic *dapnō, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰ₂ep- (“to judge”).
well: From Middle English wel, from Old English wel (“well, properly, rightly, very, quite, fully, much, considerably”), from Proto-Germanic *welą (“well”), from Proto-Indo-European *wel- (“good”).

PHRASE->
damn well better:  最好...  You damn well better be there on time. 你最好准时到。
damn well know:  非常清楚  I damn well know what I'm talking about. 我很清楚自己在说什么。
damn well sure: 非常确定 I'm damn well sure I locked the door. 我非常确定我锁了门。
damn well please me:  让我非常高兴 It would damn well please me if you would stop complaining.  如果你停止抱怨，我会很高兴。
not damn well likely:  不太可能  It's not damn well likely that he'll win. 他不太可能赢。

注意事项:
"damn-well" is considered informal and even mildly vulgar. Avoid using it in formal contexts.
