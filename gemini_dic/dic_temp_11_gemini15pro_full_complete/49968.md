denigrate: /ˈdɛnɪɡreɪt/ | v. | 诋毁，诽谤，贬低 | To criticize unfairly; to blacken someone's reputation or belittle someone. | The politician denigrated his opponent's character during the debate.  这位政治家在辩论中诋毁了对手的人格。 | synonyms: belittle, disparage, defame, vilify, slander | antonyms: praise, extol, laud, commend | verb


denigrate: /ˈdɛnɪɡreɪt/ | v. | 诋毁，诽谤，贬低 | To speak damagingly of; criticize unfairly. |  Her work was unfairly denigrated by jealous rivals. 她的作品受到了嫉妒的竞争对手的不公平贬低。 | synonyms: deprecate, detract, malign | antonyms: acclaim, applaud, commend | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *denigrāre*, from *de-* (down, from) + *nigrāre* (to blacken), from *niger* (black).
