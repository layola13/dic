bemock:/bɪˈmɒk/| vt.|嘲笑，嘲弄|To treat with scorn or contempt; ridicule.  To mimic or imitate, often in a mocking or derisive manner.|He bemocked her efforts to learn to play the guitar. 他嘲笑她学习弹吉他的努力。|Synonyms: ridicule, mock, deride, scoff at, jeer at, sneer at, taunt, make fun of|Antonyms: praise, commend, admire, respect|verb

Etymology:
From be- + mock, from Middle English mokken, from Old French mocquer, of uncertain origin.
