dryades:/ˈdraɪ.əˌdiːz/ | n. | 树精; 树仙 |  Wood nymphs, or female tree spirits, in Greek mythology. They were believed to inhabit specific trees and to live and die with those trees. |  Dryades were often depicted as beautiful maidens who danced in the forest.  树精通常被描绘成在森林中跳舞的美丽少女。 | The ancient Greeks believed that harming a tree inhabited by a dryad would bring misfortune. 古希腊人认为，伤害一棵树精居住的树会带来厄运。 | synonyms: wood nymph, tree spirit | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "mortal" or "human") | noun
