exuberance:/ɪɡˈzjuːbərəns/| n. |活力; 丰富; 茂盛;  热情洋溢|The state of being exuberant; overflowing abundance; joyful enthusiasm; flamboyance; lavishness.|Her exuberance and enthusiasm were infectious. 她的活力和热情很有感染力。|Synonyms: ebullience, buoyancy, elation, vivacity|Antonyms: apathy, dullness, lifelessness|Noun

exuberant:/ɪɡˈzjuːbərənt/| adj. |生气勃勃的; 繁茂的; 丰富的;  充满活力的|Filled with or characterized by a lively energy and excitement.|The child showed exuberant joy at opening her presents. 孩子打开礼物时表现出 exuberant 的喜悦。|Synonyms: lively, energetic, enthusiastic, high-spirited|Antonyms: listless, subdued, restrained|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *exuberant- (stem of exuberans), present participle of exuberare "to be abundant, overflow," from ex- "out" + uber "fertile, fruitful, abundant."
