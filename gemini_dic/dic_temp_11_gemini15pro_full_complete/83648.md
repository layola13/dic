haemophilia:/ˌhiːməˈfɪliə/ | n. | 血友病 | A medical condition, usually hereditary, in which the blood's ability to clot is severely reduced, causing the sufferer to bleed excessively when injured. | Haemophilia is a serious genetic disorder. 血友病是一种严重的遗传性疾病。| synonyms: bleeder's disease | antonyms: (No direct antonym for a medical condition) | noun
