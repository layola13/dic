bankruptcy: /ˈbæŋkrʌptsi/ (US) /ˈbæŋkrʌptsi/ (UK)| n. |破产，倒闭|The state of being legally declared unable to pay one's debts.|The company filed for bankruptcy after years of declining sales. 这家公司在多年销售额下降后申请破产。| insolvency, failure, ruin | solvency, prosperity | noun

bankrupt: /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (US) /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (UK)| adj. |破产的，倒闭的|Legally declared unable to pay one's debts; completely lacking in a particular quality or value.|The bankrupt businessman lost everything.  破产的商人失去了一切。|insolvent, ruined, broken | solvent, prosperous | adjective

bankrupt: /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (US) /ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ (UK)| vt. |使破产|To make someone bankrupt.|The recession bankrupted many small businesses. 经济衰退使许多小企业破产。|ruin, impoverish | enrich, save | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian "banca rotta" (broken bench), referring to the practice of breaking the benches of money lenders who were unable to pay their debts.  This evolved into the French "banqueroute" and eventually the English "bankruptcy."
