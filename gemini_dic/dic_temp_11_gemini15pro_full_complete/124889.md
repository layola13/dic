muddle-headed:/ˈmʌdlˌhɛdɪd/ /ˈmʌdlˌhɛdɪd/| adj.|糊涂的，昏头昏脑的|Confused and unable to think clearly.|例句：He's a bit muddle-headed in the mornings before he's had his coffee. 他早上没喝咖啡之前有点糊涂。

例句：She made a muddle-headed decision and now regrets it. 她做了一个糊涂的决定，现在后悔了。|近义词：confused, bewildered, addle-brained, befuddled|反义词：clear-headed, sharp, astute|词性:形容词

Etymology->
From muddle (verb) + -headed.  Muddle itself is of uncertain origin, possibly related to mud. The suffix "-headed" means "having a head of a specific kind," often figuratively relating to mental state.
