neatening:/ˈniːtnɪŋ/ /ˈnitnɪŋ/
|n.|整理；使整洁|The act of making something neat and tidy.  The neatening of the room took longer than expected.|例句：The neatening of his desk made him feel more organized.  整理他的桌子让他感觉更有条理。

例句：The neatening of the garden was a necessary chore. 整理花园是一项必要的琐事。

|近义词：tidying, organizing, straightening, cleaning|反义词：messing, cluttering, disorganizing|词性：名词

neatening:/ˈniːtnɪŋ/ /ˈnitnɪŋ/
|v.|使整洁（neaten的现在分词）|Present participle of "neaten," meaning to make something neat and tidy.|例句：She is neatening the shelves in the living room. 她正在整理客厅的架子。

例句：He was neatening his appearance before the interview. 他在面试前整理自己的仪容。

|近义词：tidying, organizing, straightening, cleaning|反义词：messing, cluttering, disorganizing|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "neaten" + "-ing."  "Neaten" comes from the adjective "neat" + "-en" (suffix forming verbs). "Neat" has its origins in Old French "net" (clean, pure) and ultimately from Latin "nitidus" (shining, bright, elegant).
