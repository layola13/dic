cheerly:/ˈtʃɪrli/| adv. adj.|高兴地，愉快地；兴高采烈的|Cheerly means in a cheerful way. It describes actions or states of being done with happiness and optimism. Although less common now, it retains a slightly archaic or literary feel, evoking a sense of traditional, heartfelt cheer.|He cheerly greeted his friends. 他高兴地迎接他的朋友们。|Near synonyms: cheerfully, merrily, gladly, brightly|Near antonyms: gloomily, morosely, sadly|Adverb

cheerly:/ˈtʃɪrli/| adj.|高兴的，愉快的|Cheerly can also function as an adjective, though this usage is even rarer than the adverbial form.  It describes something that has a cheerful quality or inspires cheer.|She had a cheerly disposition. 她性格开朗。|Near synonyms: cheerful, merry, glad, bright|Near antonyms: gloomy, morose, sad|Adjective
