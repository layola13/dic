chilostomatous:/ˌkaɪləˈstɒmətəs/ | adj. | 唇口目的，具唇口目特征的 | Having an operculum-covered orifice, referring to a specific type of Bryozoan (moss animal) colony structure where the feeding zooids have a hinged lid (operculum) that closes the opening. | Example: The researchers discovered a new species of chilostomatous bryozoan in the deep sea.  研究人员在深海发现了一种新的唇口目苔藓虫。 | Example: Chilostomatous bryozoans are more common in marine environments than their ctenostomatous counterparts.  唇口目苔藓虫在海洋环境中比栉口目苔藓虫更常见。 | Synonym: operculate | Antonym: ctenostomatous | word type: adjective
