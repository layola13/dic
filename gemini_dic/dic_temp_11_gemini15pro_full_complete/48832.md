defoliate:/diːˈfoʊliˌeɪt/
verb
使落叶; 除叶
To cause the leaves of (a plant, tree, or forest) to fall off, especially prematurely, as by the action of a chemical spray, a disease, or an insect.
The strong winds defoliated the trees. 强风使树木落叶。
defoliate:/diːˈfoʊliˌeɪt/
adjective
落叶的
Having lost leaves.
The defoliated trees looked barren and desolate.  落叶的树木看起来光秃秃的，一片荒凉。
Synonyms:strip, denude, bare
Antonyms:leaf out, grow foliage
