bogged:/bɒɡd/ (UK) /bɑːɡd/ (US)| adj.|陷入泥沼的；陷入困境的；停滞不前的|Stuck in mud or wet ground; also used figuratively to mean stuck in a difficult situation or unable to make progress.|The car got bogged down in the mud. 汽车陷在泥里了。|近义词：mired, stuck, stalled|反义词：free, unstuck, progressing|形容词

bogged:/bɒɡd/ (UK) /bɑːɡd/ (US)| v. (past participle)|（使）陷入泥沼；（使）陷入困境|Past participle of the verb "to bog," meaning to become stuck in mud or wet ground, or figuratively to become stuck in a difficult situation.|The project became bogged down by bureaucratic procedures.  这个项目因官僚程序而陷入停滞。|近义词：mired, stuck, stalled|反义词：freed, unstuck, progressed|动词

PHRASE->

词源：From the noun "bog" (meaning a wetland), with the past participle ending "-ed" added to create an adjective describing the state of being stuck in a bog.  来自名词“bog”（意为湿地），加上过去分词词尾“-ed”构成形容词，描述陷入沼泽的状态。
