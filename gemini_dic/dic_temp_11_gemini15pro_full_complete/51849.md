dexterousness:/ˌdɛkˈstɛrəsnəs/| n.| 灵巧，敏捷，熟练| Skill and ease in using one's hands or body; agility.| The pianist's dexterousness was evident in his swift and precise movements.  这位钢琴家的灵巧在他的迅速而精确的动作中显而易见。|synonyms: adroitness, deftness, agility, nimbleness, proficiency, skill, expertise| antonyms: clumsiness, awkwardness, ineptness| noun
dexterity:/dɛkˈstɛrɪti/| n.| 灵巧，敏捷，熟练| Skill and ease in using one's hands or body; agility.| She displayed remarkable dexterity in handling the delicate antique. 她展示了处理精致古董的非凡技巧。|synonyms: adroitness, deftness, agility, nimbleness, proficiency, skill, expertise| antonyms: clumsiness, awkwardness, ineptness| noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *destreté, from Latin dexter "right, skillful," from Proto-Indo-European *deks- "right; south." The connection with "right" likely arose from the fact that most people are right-handed.  The suffix "-ness" is a common English suffix used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
