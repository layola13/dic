enfeebles:/ɪnˈfiːblz/ | vt. | 使衰弱，使无力 | To make someone or something weak and unable to do things effectively. | The illness had greatly enfeebled him.  这种疾病使他非常虚弱。 |  synonyms: weaken, debilitate, incapacitate, enervate | antonyms: strengthen, invigorate, empower | verb

enfeebled:/ɪnˈfiːbld/ | adj. | 衰弱的，无力的 |  Made weak and unable to do things effectively. |  He was old and enfeebled by illness. 他年老体弱，疾病缠身。 | synonyms: weak, debilitated, incapacitated, frail | antonyms: strong, vigorous, robust | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *enfeblir, from en- (“in, into”) + feble (“weak”), from Latin flebilis (“liable to weep, lamentable”), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *pleh₁- (“to flow”).
