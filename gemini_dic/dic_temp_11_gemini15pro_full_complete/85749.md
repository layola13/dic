hawks:/hɔːks/ | n. |鹰; 鹰派人物; 骗子; 咳嗽|Birds of prey, typically having a short, hooked beak, strong claws, and keen eyesight, known for their hunting prowess. Also refers to people who advocate aggressive policies, especially in politics. Can also mean swindlers or a fit of coughing.|
例句：The hawks circled high above the canyon. 鹰在峡谷上空盘旋。

例句：The hawks in the government pushed for military intervention. 政府中的鹰派人物推动军事干预。

近义词：falcon, eagle, predator, hardliner|反义词：dove, pacifist|词性：noun


hawks:/hɔːks/ | v. (third-person singular simple present hawks, present participle hawking, simple past and past participle hawked) |兜售; 咳嗽; 清嗓子|To sell goods by shouting in the street or going from house to house; to clear one's throat noisily.|
例句：He was hawking his wares in the marketplace. 他在市场上兜售他的商品。

例句：She hawked and spat on the ground. 她咳嗽了一声，吐了口痰在地上。

近义词：peddle, vend, cough|反义词：buy|词性：verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hafoc, from Proto-Germanic *habukaz, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kep- "to cut, to scrape." The meaning shifted from "hawk" (the bird) to "hawk" (to sell) due to the perceived aggressiveness of street vendors.
