live-in:/ˈlɪv ɪn/| adj.|同居的，住家的|A person who lives in the place where they work, such as a housekeeper or nanny.  A partner who lives with someone in a long-term relationship but is not married to them.|例句：She employed a live-in nanny to care for her children. 她雇了一个住家保姆来照顾她的孩子。

|例句：They've been live-in partners for five years. 他们已经是五年同居伴侣了。|近义词：resident, cohabiting|反义词：live-out, non-resident|词性:形容词


live-in:/ˈlɪv ɪn/| n.|同居者，住家雇员|A person who lives in the place where they work. A partner who lives with someone in a long-term relationship but is not married to them.|例句：The live-in at the mansion cooked all the meals.  这栋豪宅的住家厨师负责做饭。

|例句：Her live-in is a chef. 她的同居伴侣是个厨师。|近义词：resident, cohabiter, roommate|反义词：commuter, non-resident|词性:名词
