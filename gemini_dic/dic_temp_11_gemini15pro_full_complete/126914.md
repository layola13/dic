nardoo:/ˈnɑːrduː/
n.|卷柏；旱蕨；纳都|A clover fern (Marsilea drummondii) of Australia, whose spores were formerly ground into flour and eaten by Aborigines, but which can be poisonous unless properly prepared.|The explorers were forced to rely on nardoo for sustenance.|Synonyms: clover fern, Marsilea drummondii|Antonyms: None|Noun
