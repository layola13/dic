gratify:/ˈɡrætɪfaɪ/| v. | 使满足，使高兴 | to please someone, or to satisfy a wish or need | It gratified him to see how well his students had done. 看到他的学生们做得这么好，他感到很欣慰。| pleases, satisfies, delights | disappoints, frustrates | verb

Etymology:
From Middle French *gratifier*, from Late Latin gratificārī (“to make agreeable, to oblige”), from Latin gratus (“pleasing, agreeable, thankful”) + -ficāre, akin to facere (“to make, to do”).
