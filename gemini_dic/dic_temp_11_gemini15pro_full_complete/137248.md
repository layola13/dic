nonsustainability:/ˌnɒnsəˌsteɪnəˈbɪləti/ |n.| 不可持续性|The state or quality of not being sustainable; inability to be maintained at a certain rate or level.|The nonsustainability of current farming practices is a major concern.| 近义词:unsustainability| 反义词:sustainability| 词性:名词


Etymology:
non- (prefix):  from Latin *non "not"
sustain: from Old French sustenir "to hold up, support, endure," from Latin sustinere "hold up, hold firm, support, endure," from sub- "up from below" + tenere "to hold."
-ability (suffix): from Middle English -abilite, from Old French -ableté, from Latin -abilitatem (nominative -abilitas), from -abilis "able, capable."
