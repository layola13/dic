memorializations:/məˌmɔːriəlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/| n. |纪念；纪念物；纪念活动|The act or process of memorializing someone or something, or the state of being memorialized.  A formal expression of respect or remembrance, often in the form of a ceremony, monument, or written work.|The memorializations of the war dead were deeply moving. 战争死难者的纪念活动令人深感动容。|近义词：commemorations, remembrances, tributes|反义词: oblivion, forgetting|词性:名词

词源: From the verb "memorialize," which comes from "memorial" + "-ize."  "Memorial" ultimately derives from the Latin "memoria" (memory).
