articulatory:/ɑːrˈtɪkjʊlətɔːri/ /ɑːrˈtɪkjəˌlɛtɔri/
adjective|发音的；清晰的；有关发音的|Relating to the production of speech sounds|The articulatory features of a language are important for understanding its pronunciation.  一种语言的发音特点对于理解其发音很重要。|Example:  The articulatory phonetics course focused on the movement of the tongue and lips.  发音语音学课程的重点是舌头和嘴唇的运动。|Synonyms:  pronunciational, phonetic|Antonyms:  auditory, receptive|词性: adjective

articulate:/ɑːrˈtɪkjʊlət/ /ɑːrˈtɪkjəˌlɛt/
verb|清晰地表达；连接|To express (an idea or feeling) fluently and coherently; to form a joint|He articulated his thoughts clearly and concisely. 他清晰而简洁地表达了他的想法。 |Example: The bones in the hand articulate with each other. 手中的骨头相互连接。|Synonyms:  express, pronounce, enunciate, join, connect|Antonyms:  mumble, slur, disconnect|词性: verb

articulate:/ɑːrˈtɪkjʊlət/ /ɑːrˈtɪkjəˌlɛt/
adjective|善于表达的；有关节的|Having or showing the ability to speak fluently and coherently; having joints or jointed segments|She is an articulate and persuasive speaker. 她是一位口齿伶俐、有说服力的演说家。|Example: The skeleton of a vertebrate is articulate. 脊椎动物的骨骼有关节。|Synonyms:  eloquent, fluent, expressive, jointed|Antonyms:  inarticulate, tongue-tied, unjointed|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *articulatorius, from Latin articulatus (past participle of articulare “to utter distinctly, joint”), from articulus “joint,” diminutive of artus “limb, joint.”
