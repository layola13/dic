claustrophobics:/ˌklɔːstrəˈfəʊbɪks/ | n. | 幽闭恐惧症患者 | People who suffer from claustrophobia, an anxiety disorder characterized by a fear of enclosed spaces. |  Claustrophobics often find it difficult to ride in elevators. 幽闭恐惧症患者通常很难乘坐电梯。|  The design of the MRI machine can be challenging for claustrophobics. 核磁共振仪的设计对于幽闭恐惧症患者来说可能具有挑战性。 |  agoraphobics (some consider this an opposite, but it's more of a different anxiety),  people with anxiety disorders |  non-sufferers,  the general population | noun

claustrophobic:/ˌklɔːstrəˈfəʊbɪk/ | adj. | 幽闭恐惧症的；患幽闭恐惧症的；引起幽闭恐惧的 |  Relating to or suffering from claustrophobia; causing claustrophobia. | I get claustrophobic in small, crowded rooms. 我在狭小拥挤的房间里会感到幽闭恐惧。 | The tiny, windowless office felt claustrophobic.  那个狭小、没有窗户的办公室让人感到幽闭恐惧。| agoraphobic, anxious, apprehensive | comfortable, open, spacious | adjective
