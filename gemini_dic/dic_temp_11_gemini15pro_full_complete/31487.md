charmer:/ˈtʃɑːrmər/| n.|有魅力的人，迷人的人|A person who charms; someone who has an attractive personality and often a flattering manner.  A charmer can be someone who uses their charm to persuade or manipulate others, sometimes in a deceptive way.|例句：He's a real charmer, isn't he? 他真是个万人迷，不是吗？

例句：She's a charmer, but I don't trust her. 她很有魅力，但我并不信任她。|近义词：enchanter, captivator, fascinator, charmer|反义词：bore, repellent|词性：名词

charm:/tʃɑːrm/| n. vt.|魅力，吸引力；符咒；使陶醉，使着迷|The power or quality of pleasing or attracting strongly.  As a verb, it means to delight or attract someone.|例句：The city has a certain charm. 这座城市有一种独特的魅力。

例句：He charmed everyone with his wit and good humor. 他以他的智慧和幽默迷住了所有人。|近义词：allure, charisma, magic, enchantment|反义词：repulsion, unpleasantness|词性：名词，动词
