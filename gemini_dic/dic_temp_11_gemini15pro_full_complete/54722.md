displace:/dɪˈspleɪs/| v. |取代，置换；迫使…离开家园|To force something or someone out of its usual or original position.|The rising floodwaters displaced thousands of people.  上涨的洪水迫使数千人离开家园。|The new software has displaced the old version. 新的软件已经取代了旧版本。|近义词：replace, remove, supplant, oust|反义词: place, reinstate, restore|词性:动词

词源: From Late Latin *displaciare, from Latin dis- + placere "to please."  The original sense was "to displease, offend," later "to remove from a place or office."
