declassified: /diːˈklæsɪfaɪd/ | adj. |解密的，撤销机密的|  No longer officially secret; made public. | The government declassified the documents related to the Cold War. 政府解密了与冷战相关的文件。|  The declassified report revealed new information about the incident. 解密后的报告披露了关于该事件的新信息。|Synonyms:  disclosed, revealed, publicized, unveiled| Antonyms: classified, secret, confidential, restricted | adjective

declassify: /diːˈklæsɪfaɪ/ | vt. |解密，撤销机密| To remove the security classification from (information or documents). | The agency declassified the files after 50 years.  该机构在50年后解密了这些文件。| They decided to declassify the information about the experiment. 他们决定将有关实验的信息解密。| Synonyms: disclose, reveal, publicize, unveil | Antonyms: classify, conceal, hide, protect | verb

declassification: /diːˌklæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/ | n. |解密，撤销机密（的行为）| The act of removing the security classification from something. | The declassification of the documents caused a public outcry. 文件的解密引起了公众的强烈抗议。 | The declassification of these files is a matter of national security. 这些文件的解密事关国家安全。 | Synonyms: disclosure, revelation, publication | Antonyms: classification, concealment, suppression | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- (meaning "remove" or "reverse") + classify + -ed/-ication. The root "classify" comes from the Latin "classis" (class, group) and "-ficare" (to make).
