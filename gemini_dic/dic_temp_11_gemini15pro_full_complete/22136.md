bolt:/boʊlt/ (US), /bəʊlt/ (UK)| n. vt. vi.|螺栓，门闩，闪电，快速移动|A metal pin or rod, usually with a head at one end and a screw thread at the other, used for fastening things together.  Also, a sliding bar used to lock a door or window, a flash of lightning, and the act of moving suddenly and rapidly.|

例句：
He tightened the bolts on the engine.  他拧紧了引擎上的螺栓。

The horse bolted when it heard the gunshot.  那匹马听到枪声后就跑了。


近义词：fastener, latch, dash, sprint, flash|
反义词：unbolt, loosen, dawdle|
词性：名词, 动词|


bolt:/boʊlt/ (US), /bəʊlt/ (UK)| v. | 匆匆吞咽，闩住|To swallow food quickly without chewing it properly. Also, to fasten something with a bolt.|

例句：
He bolted down his breakfast and rushed out the door. 他匆匆吃完早餐，冲出了门。

She bolted the door behind her. 她闩上了身后的门。


近义词：gulp, devour, secure, lock|
反义词：unbolt, unlock|
词性：动词|
