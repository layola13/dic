disintegration:/dɪsˌɪntɪˈɡreɪʃən/| n. | 瓦解，解体，崩溃 |The process of breaking into small parts or pieces; the loss of unity or wholeness.| The disintegration of the Roman Empire was a long and complex process. 罗马帝国的瓦解是一个漫长而复杂的过程。| synonyms: decomposition, decay, breakdown, fragmentation | antonyms: integration, unification, consolidation | noun


disintegrate:/dɪsˈɪntɪˌɡreɪt/| v. | 瓦解，解体，崩溃| To break into small parts or pieces; to lose unity or wholeness.| The old documents had disintegrated into dust.  旧文件已经碎成了灰尘。| synonyms: crumble, decompose, decay, break down | antonyms: integrate, unify, consolidate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (not, apart) + integrate (to make whole).  The word "integrate" comes from Latin "integratus," past participle of "integrare" (to make whole), from "integer" (whole, untouched).
