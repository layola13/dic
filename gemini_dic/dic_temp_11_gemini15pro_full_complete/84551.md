hammer-up:/ˈhæmər ʌp/
|phrasal verb|
|锤高；抬高（价格）；敲定；仓促完成|
|To force (something, such as prices or wages) upward; to finalize or conclude hastily; to complete quickly and often carelessly.|
|They hammered up the price of oil.|他们抬高了石油价格。|
|The deal was hammered up in a matter of hours.|这笔交易在几个小时内就敲定了。|
|They had to hammer up the project to meet the deadline.|他们不得不仓促完成项目以赶上最后期限。|
|近义词: force up, drive up, inflate, rush, hasten|
|反义词: lower, reduce, decrease, delay, postpone|
|词性: 动词短语|


hammer-up:/ˈhæmər ʌp/
|n.|
|(非正式)仓促完成的工作；敲定的协议|
|A hastily completed task or agreement.|
|The agreement was a bit of a hammer-up, but it got the job done.|这项协议有点仓促，但它完成了工作。|
|That paint job looks like a real hammer-up.|那油漆活看起来真是仓促完成的。|
|近义词: rush job, quick fix, botch job|
|反义词: meticulous work, careful planning|
|词性: 名词|
