blighter:/ˈblaɪtər/| n.|家伙，小子，讨厌鬼|A person, typically a man, who is regarded with a mixture of affection and mild disapproval.  A term of endearment, often used playfully or ironically.|That blighter's gone and left the gate open again!  那个家伙又把门开着就走了！|Synonyms: chap, fellow, rascal, rogue|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, as it's a mildly disapproving term of endearment.  Could perhaps use "angel" for humorous contrast.)|Noun


blighter:/ˈblaɪtər/| n.|引起损害的事物|Something that causes damage or harm (less common usage).|The frost was a real blighter for the crops this year. 今年的霜冻对农作物造成了很大的损害。|Synonyms: pest, nuisance, bane|Antonyms:  benefit, boon|Noun
