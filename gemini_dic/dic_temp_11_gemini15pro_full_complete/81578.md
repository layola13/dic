grease-heel:/ˈɡriːsˌhiːl/ /ˈɡriːsˌhil/
n.|拍马屁的人，谄媚者|A person who is seen as obsequious and insincere, often in pursuit of personal gain.  They are often viewed negatively as being overly eager to please those in positions of authority.|He's such a grease-heel, always sucking up to the boss. 他真是个马屁精，总是拍老板的马屁。|Synonyms: sycophant, flatterer, brown-noser, yes-man|Antonyms: rebel, nonconformist|noun

grease-heel:/ˈɡriːsˌhiːl/ /ˈɡriːsˌhil/
v.|拍马屁，谄媚|To behave in an obsequious and insincere manner, typically to gain favor or advantage.|He greased-heeled his way to a promotion. 他靠拍马屁得到了晋升。|Synonyms: fawn, kowtow, toady|Antonyms: defy, challenge|verb
