brace-up:/breɪs ˈʌp/ | phrasal verb | 振作起来；打起精神；做好准备 | To prepare oneself physically or mentally for something difficult or unpleasant.  To make oneself feel more cheerful or determined. |  He braced himself up for the bad news. 他振作精神准备迎接坏消息。| Synonyms:  prepare, steel oneself,  gird (one's loins), summon (up) one's courage, cheer up, perk up | Antonyms: lose heart, despair, give up | verb

brace-up:/breɪs ˈʌp/ | n. | (少见) 支撑物; 振奋 | A support or strengthening element. An act of bracing oneself. | The brace-up gave him the confidence he needed.  这个支撑给了他所需的信心。 (指精神上的支撑) | Synonyms: support, reinforcement, boost | Antonyms: weakening, hindrance | noun
