cash-in-your-chips:/kæʃ ɪn jɔːr tʃɪps/ | idiom | 停止；放弃；死 | To stop doing something, especially something that you have been doing for a long time; to die.  Originates from gambling, where cashing in chips represents ending one's participation. | After twenty years in the business, he decided to cash in his chips and retire to the countryside.  他在这行干了二十年后，决定退休，到乡下去安度晚年。| synonyms: give up, quit, retire, decease | antonyms: continue, persist, start, live | phrase

cash-in-your-chips:/kæʃ ɪn jɔːr tʃɪps/ | idiom | 兑现，套现 | To exchange something for its cash value, often implying taking a profit or cutting losses.  This usage is less common than the "stop/die" meaning. |  He decided to cash in his chips and sell his stock before the market crashed. 他决定在股市崩盘前套现股票。 | synonyms: sell out, liquidate, realize | antonyms: invest, buy, hold | phrase
