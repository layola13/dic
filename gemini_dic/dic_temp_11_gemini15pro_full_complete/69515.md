fell-upon:/fɛl əˈpɒn/ | phrasal verb | 偶然发现，偶然碰到，袭击 | To find or meet something or someone unexpectedly; to attack someone suddenly and violently. |  例句：We fell upon a charming little village during our travels. 我们在旅途中偶然发现了一个迷人的小村庄。

例句：The bandits fell upon the travelers and robbed them. 土匪袭击了旅行者并抢劫了他们。 | 近义词：stumble upon, come across, encounter, attack, assault | 反义词：miss, avoid, overlook, defend, protect | 词性：动词短语

fell:/fɛl/ | v. | 砍倒，击倒；(疾病) 突然致死 |  Past tense of *fall*. To cut down a tree; to knock someone down; to kill suddenly (as a disease). | 例句：The lumberjack felled the giant redwood. 伐木工人砍倒了那棵巨大的红杉。

例句：The boxer felled his opponent with a single punch. 拳击手一拳就把对手击倒了。| 近义词：cut down, knock down, slay | 反义词：raise, erect, revive | 词性：动词

upon:/əˈpɒn/ | prep. | 在…之上；紧接着 | On; immediately after. | 例句：The book is upon the table. 书在桌子上。

例句：Upon hearing the news, she burst into tears. 一听到这个消息，她就放声大哭。 | 近义词：on, atop, after | 反义词：below, beneath, before | 词性：介词
