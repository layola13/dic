digression:/daɪˈɡrɛʃən/ |n.| 离题；偏离；岔开|A temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing.|The speaker made several digressions during his lecture, which confused the audience. 演讲者在演讲过程中多次离题，这让听众感到困惑。|Example:  His essay was full of amusing digressions. 他的文章充满了有趣的题外话。

Example: The professor's digressions were often more interesting than the lecture itself.  教授的跑题往往比讲座本身更有趣。|Synonyms: deviation, detour, tangent, aside, excursus|Antonyms: focus, adherence, concentration|noun
