noncausativeness:/ˌnɒnkɔːzˈətɪvnəs/ | n. | 非致使性 | The property of a verb or predicate that does not imply that the subject causes an action or change of state.  It describes a situation or state that arises without an external agent. | The window broke. (Noncausative - implies the window broke on its own) |  近义词: inactiveness, inertness | 反义词: causativeness, activity | noun
