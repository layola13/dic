neoconservative: /ˌniːoʊkənˈsɜːrvətɪv/ | adj. n. | 新保守主义的；新保守主义者 |  A political ideology that emerged in the United States during the late 20th century, characterized by an emphasis on free-market capitalism, a strong national defense, and a more assertive foreign policy, often including military intervention.  It is rooted in a reaction against the perceived excesses of liberalism and the New Left of the 1960s. | He's known for his neoconservative views on foreign policy. 他以其新保守主义的外交政策观点而闻名。

The neoconservative movement gained prominence during the George W. Bush administration. 新保守主义运动在乔治·W·布什政府时期变得突出。 | 近义词: hawkish, right-wing | 反义词:  liberal, dovish | 词性: adjective, noun


Etymology:  "neo-" (new) + "conservative" (from Latin "conservare," to preserve).
