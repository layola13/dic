mobilizing: /ˈmoʊbəˌlaɪzɪŋ/ (US) /ˈməʊbɪˌlaɪzɪŋ/ (UK)
verb, adjective
动员，调动；动员的，调动的
present participle of mobilize: To organize and make something ready for action, especially for war or a similar activity.
The country is mobilizing its army for war. 这个国家正在动员军队准备战争。
The charity is mobilizing support for the refugees.  这个慈善机构正在动员起来支持难民。
mobilizing troops for deployment 部署动员军队
The government is mobilizing resources to help the victims. 政府正在调动资源来帮助受害者。
synonyms:  assembling, rallying, marshaling, activating, deploying
antonyms: demobilizing, disbanding, dismissing
verb

mobilizing: /ˈmoʊbəˌlaɪzɪŋ/ (US) /ˈməʊbɪˌlaɪzɪŋ/ (UK)
adjective
动员的，调动的
Used to describe something that is in the process of being mobilized.
The mobilizing force is expected to reach 10,000. 预计动员的兵力将达到1万人。
They observed the mobilizing efforts of the enemy troops. 他们观察了敌军的动员工作。
synonyms:  activating, deploying
antonyms: demobilizing, disbanding
adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French mobiliser, from mobile (“movable”) + -iser, from Latin -izāre.
