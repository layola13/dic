mournfuller:/ˈmɔːrnfələr/ | adj. | 更悲伤的，更悲哀的 |  The comparative form of "mournful," meaning exhibiting or expressing sorrow or grief to a greater degree.  It suggests a deeper or more intense sadness than simply "mournful." |  The music grew mournfuller as the funeral procession began.  音乐随着葬礼队伍的开始变得更加悲伤。 |  The poet's later works are mournfuller than his earlier ones, reflecting his personal tragedies.  这位诗人的后期作品比早期作品更悲伤，反映了他的个人悲剧。 | 近义词: sadder, more sorrowful, more doleful | 反义词: happier, more joyful, more cheerful | 词性: 形容词


mournful:/ˈmɔːrnfəl/ | adj. | 悲伤的，悲哀的 | Expressing or suggesting sorrow or grief.  Derived from "mourn" + "-ful." |  The mournful sound of the bagpipes filled the air.  风笛悲伤的声音弥漫在空气中。 |  She wore a mournful expression.  她带着悲伤的表情。 |  近义词: sad, sorrowful, doleful | 反义词: happy, joyful, cheerful | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Middle English *mornful, from Old English *mornfull, from morn (“mourning”) + -full. Equivalent to mourn +‎ -ful.
