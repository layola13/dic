discombobulate:/ˌdɪskəmˈbɑːbəleɪt/ /ˌdɪskəmˈbɒbjʊleɪt/| vt. |使混乱，使困惑，使不知所措|To confuse or disorient someone; to upset or frustrate.|The long flight and time change completely discombobulated me. 长途飞行和时差让我完全不知所措。|近义词：confuse, bewilder, disorient, upset, rattle|反义词:clarify, enlighten, orient, calm, soothe|Verb

discombobulation:/ˌdɪskəmˌbɑːbəˈleɪʃən/ /ˌdɪskəmˌbɒbjʊˈleɪʃən/| n. |混乱，困惑，不知所措|The state of being confused or disoriented.|He was in a state of utter discombobulation after the surprise announcement.  突如其来的消息让他完全不知所措。|近义词：confusion, bewilderment, disorientation|反义词:clarity, composure, orientation|Noun

discombobulated:/ˌdɪskəmˈbɑːbəleɪtɪd/ /ˌdɪskəmˈbɒbjʊleɪtɪd/| adj. |感到混乱的，感到困惑的，感到不知所措的|Feeling confused or disoriented.|I felt completely discombobulated after the meeting. 会议结束后，我感到十分困惑。|近义词：confused, bewildered, disoriented|反义词:clear-headed, oriented, composed|Adjective
