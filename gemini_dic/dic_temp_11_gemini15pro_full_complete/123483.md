monosyllabicity:/ˌmɒnəsɪləˈbɪsɪti/ /ˌmɑːnəsɪləˈbɪsəti/| n. |单音节性|The quality or state of being monosyllabic; the predominance of monosyllables in a language or text.|The monosyllabicity of the poem gives it a certain starkness.|近义词:  monosyllabism|反义词: polysyllabicity|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From mono- (one) + syllable + -icity (state or quality of). Ultimately from Greek monos (single) and syllabē (syllable).
