dictate:/ˈdɪkteɪt/ (US), /dɪkˈteɪt/ (UK)| v. n.|口述；命令；支配|to say words aloud for someone to write down; to give orders with total authority|例句：The boss dictated a memo to his secretary. 老板向他的秘书口述了一份备忘录。

|例句：He dictated the terms of the agreement. 他规定了协议的条款。|近义词：command, order, prescribe, impose|反义词：obey, follow, comply|词性：动词

dictate:/ˈdɪkteɪt/ (US), /dɪkˈteɪt/ (UK)| n.|命令；指示；规定|an order or principle that must be obeyed|例句：The dictates of fashion are constantly changing. 时尚的规则总是在变化。

|例句：The dictates of conscience. 良心的指示。|近义词：command, order, decree, mandate|反义词：request, suggestion|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin dictāre (“to say often, prescribe, dictate”), frequentative of dicō (“I say, speak”).
