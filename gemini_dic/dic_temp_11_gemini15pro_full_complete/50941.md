desight:/dɪˈsaɪt/ | n. | 厌恶，憎恶; 不愉快的事物 | An intense dislike or aversion; something unpleasant or offensive. |  The desight of the battlefield filled him with horror.  战场的惨状使他充满了恐惧。 | 近义词: disgust, abhorrence, loathing, aversion, repugnance | 反义词: delight, pleasure, liking, fondness | 词性: 名词

词源: From Middle English *desight, from Old French despit, from Latin despectus (“a looking down upon, contempt, disdain”), past participle of despicere, from de- (“down”) + specere (“to look”).  Equivalent to de- + sight.
