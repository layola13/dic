disquietment:/dɪsˈkwaɪətmənt/ | n. |不安，忧虑，焦虑 |A feeling of anxiety or worry. | The news of the war caused widespread disquietment among the population.  战争的消息引起了民众普遍的不安。| Near synonyms: uneasiness, anxiety, worry, perturbation, apprehension | Near antonyms: calmness, tranquility, peace, serenity | noun

disquiet:/dɪsˈkwaɪət/ | vt. | 使不安，使忧虑 | To make someone feel anxious or worried. | The strange noises in the night disquieted the children.  夜晚奇怪的声音使孩子们感到不安。| Near synonyms: disturb, upset, worry, bother, perturb | Near antonyms: calm, soothe, reassure, comfort | verb

disquieted:/dɪsˈkwaɪətɪd/ | adj. |  不安的，焦虑的 | Feeling or showing anxiety or worry. | She looked disquieted by the news. 她看起来对这个消息感到不安。| Near synonyms: uneasy, anxious, worried, perturbed, apprehensive | Near antonyms: calm, tranquil, peaceful, serene | adjective
