deploring:/dɪˈplɔːrɪŋ/
adjective
表示悲痛的，表示谴责的
Expressing strong disapproval; lamenting.
The human rights group issued a statement deploring the government's actions. 人权组织发表声明谴责政府的行为。
近义词：lamenting, criticizing, condemning, denouncing
反义词：praising, commending, approving, supporting
词性: adjective

deplorably:/dɪˈplɔːrəbli/
adverb
非常糟糕地，令人遗憾地
In a way that expresses strong disapproval or regret.
The building was deplorably maintained.  这栋楼的维护状况非常糟糕。
近义词：terribly, awfully, regrettably, lamentably
反义词： admirably, excellently, wonderfully
词性: adverb

deplorableness:/dɪˈplɔːrəblnəs/
noun
糟糕，令人遗憾
The state of being deplorable; wretchedness.
The deplorableness of the situation shocked everyone.  情况的糟糕程度震惊了所有人。
近义词：awfulness, terribleness, wretchedness
反义词：excellence, wonderfulness
词性: noun

deplore:/dɪˈplɔːr/
verb
公开谴责，强烈反对；对…深感遗憾
Feel or express strong disapproval of (something).
We deplore all violence against innocent people. 我们谴责所有针对无辜者的暴力行为。
近义词：lament, criticize, condemn, denounce
反义词：praise, commend, approve, support
词性: verb


词源:
From Old French deplorer, from Latin deplorare (“to bewail, lament, weep for”), from de- (“down, completely”) + plorare (“to weep, cry, wail”).
