diplomatic:/ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 外交的；有外交手腕的；策略的 | skilled in dealing with sensitive matters or people; tactful |  She gave a diplomatic answer, not wanting to offend anyone. 她给出了一个外交辞令式的回答，不想冒犯任何人。|  He took a diplomatic approach to the problem, trying to find a solution that would satisfy everyone. 他采取了一种策略性的方法来解决这个问题，试图找到一个让每个人都满意的解决方案。 | 近义词: tactful, politic, discreet, judicious | 反义词: undiplomatic, tactless, clumsy, gauche | 词性: 形容词

diplomatic:/ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk/ | n. | 外交官 |  (rare, usually plural) a diplomat | The diplomats met to discuss the peace treaty. 外交官们会面讨论和平条约。|  Many diplomats attended the state dinner. 许多外交官出席了国宴。 | 近义词: envoy, ambassador, consul | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for the noun form) | 词性: 名词 (rare)

词源：
From French *diplomatique*, from Late Latin *diplomaticus*, from Greek *δίπλωμα* (díplōma, “folded document, letter of recommendation”).  The original meaning referred to official documents, and the sense of "tactful" developed later.
