fossilize:/ˈfɒsəlaɪz/ (British) /ˈfɑːsəlaɪz/ (American)| v. | 使…成化石；使…僵化；使…过时 | to become or make something become a fossil; to become or make something become fixed and unable to change | The dinosaur bones were fossilized over millions of years.  恐龙骨骼经过数百万年变成了化石。

The plant's remains fossilized in the mud.  植物的遗骸在泥中变成了化石。

近义词: petrify, harden, solidify | 反义词: revitalize, modernize | 词性: 动词

fossilization:/ˌfɒsəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (British) /ˌfɑːsəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (American)| n. | 化石化；僵化；过时 | the process of becoming a fossil; the process of becoming fixed and unable to change | The fossilization of the plant took millions of years.  植物的化石化过程耗费了数百万年。

The museum displays exhibits on the fossilization process.  博物馆展出了关于化石化过程的展品。

近义词: petrification, hardening | 反义词: revitalization, modernization | 词性: 名词

fossilized:/ˈfɒsəlaɪzd/ (British) /ˈfɑːsəlaɪzd/ (American)| adj. | 已变成化石的；僵化的；过时的 | having become a fossil; fixed and unable to change | The archaeologists discovered a fossilized skeleton.  考古学家发现了一具变成化石的骨架。

They unearthed a fossilized tree trunk. 他们挖出了一截变成化石的树干。

近义词: petrified, hardened | 反义词: revitalized, modernized | 词性: 形容词
