cankering:/ˈkæŋkərɪŋ/ | adj. | 腐蚀性的，溃烂的，有害的 |  Causing corrosion, decay, or corruption; having a harmful effect. | The cankering effects of jealousy destroyed their friendship.  嫉妒的腐蚀作用破坏了他们的友谊。 | 近义词: corrosive, destructive, malignant, pernicious | 反义词: beneficial, healing, restorative | 词性: 形容词

cankering:/ˈkæŋkərɪŋ/ | n. | 腐烂，溃疡 | The process of decaying or corroding. | The cankering of the wood made it unusable. 木材的腐烂使其无法使用。| 近义词: corrosion, decay, rot | 反义词: growth, repair, restoration | 词性: 名词


词源: From Middle English *cankren, from Old North French cancre, from Latin cancer (“crab, cancer”).  The metaphorical sense of something spreading and destroying like a cancer developed later.
