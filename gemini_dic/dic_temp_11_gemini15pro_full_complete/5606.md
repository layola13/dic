altern:/ˈɔːltərn/ /ˈɑːltərn/ | adj. | 交替的，轮流的 | Occurring or succeeding by turns; every other; alternate. |例句：The flowers are planted in altern rows of red and white.  这些花被交替种植成红色和白色两排。

例句：We take altern turns driving. 我们轮流开车。 |近义词：alternative, rotating, reciprocal |反义词:continuous, constant |词性:形容词

altern:/ˈɔːltərn/ /ˈɑːltərn/ | v. | 交替，轮流 | To occur or cause to occur by turns; interchange regularly. |例句：The sun and moon altern in the sky. 太阳和月亮在天空交替出现。

例句：He alterned between hope and despair. 他在希望和绝望之间交替。 |近义词：rotate, interchange, alternate |反义词:persist, continue |词性:动词


Etymology: From Late Latin *alternare, from Latin alternus (“every other, one after the other”), from alter (“other”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *al- (“beyond”).
