charivari: /ˌʃɪvəˈriː/ (US), /ˌʃɪvəˈriː/ (UK) | n. |喧闹的庆祝活动；粗俗的嘲弄；（旧时为惩罚不孚众望的新婚夫妇而制造的）混战| A noisy, mock serenade, typically performed by a group of people outside the house of a newly married couple, often involving the banging of pots and pans.  It can also refer to any loud, discordant noise or a confused mixture of sounds. |例句：The villagers gave the unpopular newlyweds a raucous charivari. 村民们给不受欢迎的新婚夫妇举行了一场喧闹的庆祝活动。

例句：The charivari of car horns and shouting made it difficult to concentrate.  汽车喇叭和叫喊声的喧闹声让人难以集中注意力。|近义词：hullabaloo, racket, din, commotion |反义词: silence, quiet |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *charibari, from Late Latin *caribaria, of uncertain origin.
