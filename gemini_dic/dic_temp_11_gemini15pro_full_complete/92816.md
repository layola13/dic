iconography: /aɪˈkɒnəɡræfi/ (US) /aɪˈkɒnəɡræfi/ (UK)| n. |图像学;肖像学;圣像画法|The visual images and symbols used in a work of art or the study or interpretation of these.|The iconography of the Renaissance is rich with classical mythology and Christian symbolism.|Synonym: symbolism, imagery|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "literalism")|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *eikon* (image) + *-graphia* (writing, description)
