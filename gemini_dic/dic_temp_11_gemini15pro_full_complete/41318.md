coreferentiality:/ˌkɔːrɛfərɛnʃiˈælɪti/ | n. | 共指性 | The state or condition of referring to the same entity or concept.  It describes the relationship between linguistic expressions that refer to the same thing in the real world or in a text. |  The coreferentiality of "the author" and "Jane Austen" is clear in the sentence "The author, Jane Austen, wrote Pride and Prejudice."  简·奥斯汀写了《傲慢与偏见》这句话中，“作者”和“简·奥斯汀”的共指关系很明确。

The coreferentiality of "it" and "the book" is less clear in this sentence.  这句话中“它”和“书”的共指关系不太明确。 | Synonyms: coreference, co-reference | Antonyms: N/A | noun
