grasp: /ɡræsp/ | v. n. | 抓住，理解，领会 | To take hold of something firmly; to understand something completely. | She grasped the rope tightly. 她紧紧地抓住了绳子。 He quickly grasped the main points of the lecture. 他很快理解了讲座的要点。 | 近义词：grip, seize, comprehend, understand | 反义词：release, misunderstand, miss | 词性: 动词, 名词

grasp: /ɡræsp/ | n. | 抓住，理解，掌握 | The act of grasping something; an understanding of something. | He has a good grasp of the subject. 他对这门学科掌握得很好。  She lost her grasp on the railing and fell. 她没抓住栏杆，摔倒了。| 近义词：grip, hold, comprehension, understanding | 反义词：misunderstanding, ignorance | 词性: 名词

词源: From Middle English *graspen, *grapsen, from Old Norse *grapsa (“to grasp, grope for”), from Proto-Germanic *grapōną (“to grasp, grab, grip”).
