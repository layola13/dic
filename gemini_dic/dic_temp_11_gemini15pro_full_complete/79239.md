glass:/ɡlæs/ /ɡlɑːs/| n. adj.|玻璃；玻璃杯；眼镜|A hard, brittle substance, typically transparent or translucent, made by fusing sand with soda, lime, and sometimes other ingredients. Can also refer to objects made of glass, such as drinking vessels or spectacles.|She dropped the glass and it shattered. 她把玻璃杯掉在地上摔碎了。|近义词：pane, tumbler, beaker|反义词：-|n. adj.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *glæs, from Proto-Germanic *glasam, from Proto-Indo-European *ghel- (to shine, gleam).  The word originally referred to the material itself, and later extended to objects made from it.
