disfavour:/dɪsˈfeɪvər/ (UK), /dɪsˈfeɪvər/ (US)| vt.|不喜欢；冷待；亏待|To disfavour means to regard or treat with disfavour; to put at a disadvantage.|The new policy disfavours small businesses. 新政策对小企业不利。|He disfavoured his younger son. 他不喜欢他的小儿子。|近义词：dislike, oppose, reject, discriminate against|反义词：favour, support, prefer, encourage|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desfavor, from des- "dis-" + favor "favor." Ultimately from Latin dis- "apart, away, not" + favor "favor, goodwill."
