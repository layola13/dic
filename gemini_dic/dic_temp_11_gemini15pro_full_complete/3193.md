affect:/əˈfekt/ | v. | 影响；感染；假装；感动 | to have an influence on someone or something, or to cause a change in something | The weather can affect our moods. 天气会影响我们的心情. | influence, impact, touch | leave unaffected, be immune to | verb

affect:/ˈæfekt/ | n. | 情感；感情；（尤指脸上表现出的）情绪 |  a person's feelings or emotions, especially as shown on the face | He displayed little affect. 他几乎没有表露任何情感。 | emotion, feeling, sentiment | apathy, indifference | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *affectus, past participle of afficere (“to do something to; act on; influence, attack”), from ad + facere (“to do, make”).
