dishonorary: /ˌdɪsˈɒnərəri/ (UK) /ˌdɪsˈɑːnəreri/ (US)| adj.|不光彩的，耻辱的|Conferring no honor; humiliating; shameful.  Used of a position or title given as a mark of disapproval or punishment.|He received a dishonorary discharge from the army for his misconduct. 他因行为不端被不光彩地开除军籍。| 近义词: shameful, disgraceful, ignominious, humiliating| 反义词: honorable, respectable, creditable| 词性: adjective

dishonorary: /ˌdɪsˈɒnərəri/ (UK) /ˌdɪsˈɑːnəreri/ (US)| n.|不光彩的头衔或职位|A title or position that brings shame or disgrace.|Being stripped of his captaincy and given a dishonorary role was a public humiliation. 被剥夺队长职位并给予不光彩的角色是一种公开的羞辱。|近义词: disgrace, shame, stigma | 反义词: honor, accolade, distinction | 词性: noun (rare usage)

词源：
