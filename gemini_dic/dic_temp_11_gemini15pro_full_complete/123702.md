mooners:/ˈmuːnərz/
n.
暴露狂，露阴癖者
People who expose their buttocks in public as a prank or gesture of disrespect.
A group of mooners disrupted the ceremony. 一群暴露狂扰乱了仪式。
近义词:flashers, exhibitionists
反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider words like "prudes" in a loose sense, but not a true opposite)
词性:noun


moon:/muːn/
n.
月亮
The natural satellite of the Earth, visible (chiefly at night) by reflected light from the sun.
The moon is full tonight. 今晚的月亮是满月。
近义词:lunar orb, satellite
反义词:(No direct antonym)
词性:noun

moon:/muːn/
vi.
闲逛；虚度光阴
To spend time idly or dreamily.
He mooned around the house all day. 他整天在家里闲逛。
近义词:dawdle, daydream, loiter
反义词:hustle, work diligently
词性:verb

moon:/muːn/
vt.
对着...做鬼脸
To make a face expressing disapproval or contempt.
The child mooned at his teacher. 孩子对着老师做鬼脸。
近义词:grimace, scowl
反义词:smile, grin
词性:verb
