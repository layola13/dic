demonstratory:/dɪˈmɒnstrətɪv (British), /ˌdɛmənˈstreɪtɔːri (American)| adj.|说明性的，证明性的；公开表露感情的|Serving as conclusive evidence of something; exhibiting clearly. Also, given to or marked by the open expression of emotion.|例句：The experiment provided demonstrative proof of the theory.  这个实验提供了该理论的证明。

例句：She is not a demonstrative person, but she cares deeply. 她不是一个善于表达感情的人，但她很关心别人。


近义词：illustrative, expository, expressive, affectionate|反义词：unexpressive, reserved, undemonstrative|词性:形容词


demonstration:/ˌdɛmənˈstreɪʃən/| n.|示范，演示；游行示威|A public display of group opinion, often a march or rally. Also, an explanation of how something works or how to do something.|例句：The students held a demonstration to protest against tuition fees. 学生们举行示威抗议学费。

例句：The teacher gave a demonstration of the new software. 老师演示了新的软件。


近义词：display, exhibition, protest, rally|反义词：concealment, suppression|词性:名词

demonstrate:/ˈdɛmənˌstreɪt/| v.|证明；示范，演示；表露；游行示威|To show or prove something clearly. Also, to show how to do something or how something works.  Also, to take part in a public display of opinion.|例句：The study demonstrates the link between smoking and lung cancer. 这项研究证明了吸烟和肺癌之间的联系。

例句：He demonstrated how to use the new machine. 他演示了如何使用这台新机器。


近义词：prove, show, exhibit, display, manifest|反义词：refute, disprove, conceal|词性:动词
