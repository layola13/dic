CFCs:/ˌsiːɛfˈsiːz/| n.|氟氯化碳|Chlorofluorocarbons, synthetic compounds containing carbon, fluorine, and chlorine, formerly used widely as refrigerants and propellants in aerosols. Their use has been greatly restricted because of their damaging effect on the ozone layer.|CFCs were once commonly used in refrigerators. 氟氯化碳曾经普遍用于冰箱。|近义词: Freon (trade name), halocarbons|反义词:  (No direct antonym, but alternatives include HFCs and HCFCs)|词性: noun
