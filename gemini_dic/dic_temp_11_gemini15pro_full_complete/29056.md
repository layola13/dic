carucate: /ˈkærəkət/ | n. | 卡鲁凯特（一种中世纪的土地面积单位） | A unit of land area used in medieval England, notionally the amount that could be ploughed by a team of eight oxen in a year, varying in size from about 120 to 180 acres. |  The lord of the manor held several carucates of land.  领主拥有几卡鲁凯特的土地。| synonyms: hide, ploughland | antonyms: N/A | noun
