ceremony: /ˈserɪməni/ (US) /ˈserɪməni/ (UK)| n. |仪式，典礼|A formal act or series of acts performed on a special occasion.|The wedding ceremony was beautiful. 婚礼很美。|Synonyms: ritual, rite, observance|Antonyms: informality, casualness|noun

ceremonial: /ˌserɪˈməʊniəl/ (US) /ˌserɪˈməʊniəl/ (UK)| adj. |仪式的，正式的|Relating to or used in a ceremony.|The ceremonial robes were impressive.  仪式用的长袍令人印象深刻。|Synonyms: formal, ritualistic, conventional|Antonyms: informal, casual, unofficial|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *ceremonie*, from Old French *ceremonie*, from Latin *caerimonia* ("sacred rite, religious worship, reverence, formality"), of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Latin *caeremonia* ("holiness, sanctity").
