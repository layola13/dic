offhand:/ˌɔːfˈhænd/ /ˌɑːfˈhænd/| adj. adv.|随便的，即兴的；无礼的|done without preparation or forethought; casual; unceremonious|I can't tell you the figures offhand, but I can look them up for you. 我无法立刻告诉你这些数字，但我可以帮你查一下。|近义词：extempore, impromptu, ad-lib, casual, informal, unceremonious|反义词:premeditated, prepared, formal, courteous|词性:形容词/副词

offhand:/ˌɔːfˈhænd/ /ˌɑːfˈhænd/| n.|即席的评论；无礼|an offhand remark or action|His offhand manner offended some people. 他随便的态度冒犯了一些人。|近义词：improvisation|反义词:premeditation|词性:名词

词源: From off + hand, originally meaning 'with the hand not employed; not busy; at leisure'.  The current meaning developed from the idea of speaking without pausing to consider or reflect. 来自 off + hand，最初意思是“用手没有被雇用；不忙；闲暇时”。目前的含义是从不暂停思考或反思而说话的想法发展而来的。
