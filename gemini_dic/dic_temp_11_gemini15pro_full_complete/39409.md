conk:/kɒŋk/ (British), /kɑːŋk/ (American)| n. v.|鼻子；(引擎)突然熄火；昏倒|A nose, especially a large one.  To suddenly stop working (of an engine or machine). To faint or collapse.|

例句：
He had a prominent conk. = 他有一个突出的鼻子。

The engine suddenly conked out. = 引擎突然熄火了。


近义词：nose, beak, hooter (for nose); fail, stall (for engine); collapse, faint (for faint) |反义词: start, run (for engine)|词性: 名词，动词


conks:/kɒŋks/ (British), /kɑːŋks/ (American)| n. v. (plural)|鼻子(复数)；(引擎)突然熄火(复数)；昏倒(复数)|Plural form of "conk," referring to multiple noses, instances of engines stalling, or fainting events.|

例句：
Their conks were red from the cold. = 他们的鼻子因为寒冷而发红。

All the engines conked out at the same time. = 所有引擎同时熄火了。


近义词：noses, beaks, hooters (for noses); failures, stalls (for engines); collapses, faints (for faints) |反义词: starts, runs (for engines)|词性: 名词，动词


词源:  Possibly imitative of the sound of a sudden stoppage.  The meaning "nose" is likely slang, possibly from resemblance to a conch shell.
