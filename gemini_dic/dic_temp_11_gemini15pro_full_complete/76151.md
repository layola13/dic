fuse:/fjuːz/ (UK) /fjuːz/ (US)| n. | 保险丝; 导火线, 引信 | A safety device that protects an electric circuit by melting and breaking the circuit if the current becomes too strong.  A thin piece of material that burns quickly and is used to set off an explosion. | The fuse blew and the lights went out. 保险丝烧断了，灯灭了。| synonyms: safety device, circuit breaker, detonator | antonyms: connector, conductor | noun

fuse:/fjuːz/ (UK) /fjuːz/ (US)| v. | 熔合, 融合; （使）保险丝烧断 | To join or blend to form a single entity. To melt (a fuse or circuit breaker) by passing too much current through it.  | The two companies fused to create a larger corporation.  两家公司合并成了一家更大的公司。| synonyms: merge, combine, blend, melt | antonyms: separate, divide, disconnect | verb


Etymology: From Old French *fuser (unattested), from Latin *fūsus, past participle of fundere (“to pour, melt”).
