bind:/baɪnd/ /baɪnd/ |v. n.|捆绑，约束，装订，结合，凝固|To tie or fasten something tightly; to obligate someone to do something.|The prisoner's hands were bound behind his back. 囚犯的双手被绑在背后。

The contract binds me to work for them for two years. 这份合同约束我为他们工作两年。|tie, fasten, secure, obligate, restrict|release, free, loosen, untie|verb

bind:/baɪnd/ /baɪnd/ |n.|困境，窘境|A difficult situation.|I'm in a bit of a bind - I've promised to help two different people at the same time. 我有点进退两难——我答应同时帮助两个人。|predicament, dilemma, quandary|advantage, solution|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bindan, of Germanic origin, from Proto-Germanic *bindaną, from Proto-Indo-European *bhendh- "to bind."  Cognates include Dutch binden and German binden.
