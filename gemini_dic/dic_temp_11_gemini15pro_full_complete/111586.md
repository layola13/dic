lobotomize: /ləˈbɒtəmaɪz/ (UK), /ləˈbɑːtəmaɪz/ (US)
|v.|切除脑叶；使丧失活力|To perform a lobotomy on; to reduce a person's mental capacity, especially by means of a lobotomy.  To make someone emotionally unresponsive or lethargic. |The doctor lobotomized the patient to alleviate his severe mental illness. 医生对病人进行了脑叶切除术以减轻他的严重精神疾病。

|The controversial procedure effectively lobotomized her, leaving her docile and apathetic.  这项备受争议的手术实际上切除了她的脑叶，使她变得温顺和冷漠。
|近义词：disable, incapacitate, dull, deaden|反义词：invigorate, stimulate, enliven|词性：动词

lobotomy: /ləˈbɒtəmi/ (UK), /ləˈbɑːtəmi/ (US)
|n.|脑叶切除术|A surgical operation involving incision into the prefrontal lobe of the brain, formerly used to treat mental illness.|The lobotomy left the patient with permanent brain damage. 脑叶切除术使病人永久性脑损伤。

|Lobotomies were a common practice in the mid-20th century but are now considered barbaric.  脑叶切除术在20世纪中期是一种常见的做法，但现在被认为是野蛮的。
|近义词：psychosurgery|反义词：(No direct antonym, but could use terms like "therapy," "counseling," or "medication")|词性：名词

词源:  From lobo- (Greek lobos "lobe") + -tomy (Greek -tomia "cutting").
