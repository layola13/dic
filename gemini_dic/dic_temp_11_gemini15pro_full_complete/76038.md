furled:/fɜːrld/ | adj. | 卷起的，收拢的 | Rolled up tightly; neatly and compactly gathered together, especially a flag or sail. | The captain ordered the sails furled as the storm approached. 船长命令在暴风雨来临之际收起船帆。| The furled flag lay in the corner. 卷起的旗帜放在角落里。 | synonyms: folded, rolled, gathered, tucked, bunched | antonyms: unfurled, spread, open, extended | adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *furlynge, *ferlynge, verbal noun from *furle, *ferle (“to furl, roll up”), from Old French ferler (“to furl a sail”), of uncertain origin, probably from Frankish *furljan (“to roll”), from Proto-Germanic *furþilōną (“to roll”), from Proto-Indo-European *pʷért- (“to turn, whirl”).
