mystic:/ˈmɪstɪk/ | adj. n. | 神秘的；神秘主义者 | Relating to mystics or mysticism; inspiring a sense of mystery and wonder. |  The mystic rituals were performed in secret.  神秘的仪式是秘密进行的。| synonyms: mysterious, esoteric, occult, enigmatic | antonyms: mundane, ordinary, clear, obvious | adjective

mystic:/ˈmɪstɪk/ | n. | 神秘主义者 | A person who seeks by contemplation and self-surrender to obtain unity with or absorption into the Deity or the absolute, or who believes in the spiritual apprehension of truths that are beyond the intellect. | The mystic spent years in meditation seeking enlightenment.  这位神秘主义者花了数年时间冥想以寻求启迪。| synonyms: spiritualist, seer, visionary | antonyms: materialist, realist, skeptic | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mysticus, from Greek mystikos “of mysteries, secret, mystic,” from mystēs “one initiated into mysteries,” from myein “to close the eyes or mouth, initiate into mysteries,” from PIE root *mu- “to shut, close.”
