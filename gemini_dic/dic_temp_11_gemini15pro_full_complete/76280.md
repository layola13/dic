/wɒt/ (UK) /wʌt/ (US) | pron. det. adv. int.| 什么，多么 | Used to ask for information specifying something. | What is your name? 你叫什么名字？ | which, who |  N/A | pronoun
What color is the sky? 天空是什么颜色的？

/wɒt/ (UK) /wʌt/ (US) | pron. det. adv. int.| 多么 | Used to express surprise or admiration. | What a beautiful day! 多么美好的一天！ | how | N/A | adverb
What a lovely surprise! 多么美好的惊喜！


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English _hwæt_, neuter singular nominative and accusative of _hwā_  "who, what,"  from Proto-Germanic *_hwat_ (source also of Old Saxon _hwat_, Old Frisian _hwet_, Middle Dutch _wat_, Dutch _wat_, Old High German _waz_, German _was_, Old Norse _hvat_), from PIE *_kwod_, neuter singular nominative and accusative of *_kwis_ "who, what."
