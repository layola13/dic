interconfounding: /ˌɪntərˌkɑːnˈfaʊndɪŋ/ | adj. |相互混杂的，互相干扰的 |  A situation in statistical analysis where the effects of two or more confounding variables are intertwined and difficult to separate. This makes it hard to determine the true causal relationship between the independent and dependent variables.  It describes a situation where multiple confounding factors are present and their individual effects are hard to disentangle from one another.  |  The study's results were difficult to interpret due to the interconfounding of socioeconomic status and access to healthcare. 这项研究的结果很难解释，因为社会经济地位和医疗保健的可及性相互混杂。 | 近义词:  intertwined, intermixed, confounded, interacting | 反义词: independent, separate, distinct | 词性: adjective

词源： "inter-" (prefix meaning "between, among") + "confound" (verb meaning "to mix up, to confuse")  The word "confound" itself comes from the Old French "confondre," ultimately from the Latin "confundere," meaning "to pour together, to mix up."
