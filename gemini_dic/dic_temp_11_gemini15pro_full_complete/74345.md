fragmentist:/ˈfræɡmənˌtɪst/| n.|碎片主义者；片段化思维者|A person who focuses on small parts or details rather than the whole, often in an intellectual or artistic context.|The critic dismissed the artist as a mere fragmentist, unable to synthesize his ideas into a cohesive whole.  这位评论家认为这位艺术家只是一个碎片主义者，无法将他的想法综合成一个有凝聚力的整体。|近义词:atomist, reductionist|反义词:holist, synthesist|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From fragment + -ist. Fragment comes from Latin *fragmentum*, from the verb frangere "to break."
