fundamental:/ˌfʌndəˈmentl/ | adj. n. | 基本的；根本的；基础；基本原理 | Forming a necessary base or core; of central importance. |A fundamental change is needed in our educational system.  我们的教育体制需要进行根本性的变革。| basic, essential, primary | secondary, superficial | adjective

fundamental:/ˌfʌndəˈmentl/ | n. | 基本原则；基本原理 | A central or primary rule or principle on which something is based. |The fundamentals of physics are taught in high school.  物理学的基本原理在高中教授。| basics, rudiments, principles | complexities, intricacies | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *fundamentale*, from Late Latin *fūndāmentālis* (“pertaining to a foundation”), from Latin *fūndāmentum* (“foundation”), from *fūndāre* (“to found, lay the bottom, establish”), from *fundus* (“bottom, base”).
