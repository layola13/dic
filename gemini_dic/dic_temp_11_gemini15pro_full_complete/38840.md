confarreation:/ˌkɒnfəˈreɪʃən/ |n.|结婚仪式，共食婚|An ancient Roman form of marriage, the most sacred of the three forms, involving a ceremony featuring the sharing of a cake made of spelt (far) by the bride and groom.|The confarreation ceremony was reserved for patricians and considered highly sacred.|近义词：wedding, marriage, matrimony|反义词：divorce, annulment|词性: 名词

Etymology: From Latin *confarreatio*, from *confarreare* "to marry by sharing spelt cake," from *com-* "together" + *farreus* "of spelt," from *far* "spelt."
