over-optimism: /ˌoʊvərˈɑːptɪmɪzəm/ | n. | 过分乐观 | Excessive optimism; a tendency to expect the best possible outcome or dwell on the most hopeful aspects of a situation. |  His over-optimism about the project led to unrealistic deadlines and ultimately, failure.  他对这个项目过于乐观，导致设定了不切实际的截止日期，最终失败了。| synonyms:  excessive optimism,  unrealistic hopefulness,  rose-tinted view,  Pollyannaism | antonyms: pessimism,  realism,  cynicism | noun

over-optimistic: /ˌoʊvərˌɑːptɪˈmɪstɪk/ | adj. | 过分乐观的 | Excessively optimistic; tending to expect the best possible outcome or dwell on the most hopeful aspects of a situation. | He was over-optimistic about his chances of winning the lottery. 他对他赢得彩票的机会过于乐观。 | synonyms: excessively hopeful,  unrealistically positive,  rose-tinted,  Pollyannaish | antonyms: pessimistic,  realistic,  cynical | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + optimism. "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much."  "Optimism" comes from the Late Latin "optimus" meaning "best."
