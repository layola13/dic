adiaphorite:/ˌeɪdiˈæfəˌraɪt/
n.
不关心道德的人；认为道德无关紧要的人
a person who is indifferent to moral questions; one who holds that some things are neither right nor wrong.
Adiaphorites believed that certain religious practices were neither commanded nor forbidden by God.  不关心道德的人认为某些宗教习俗既不是上帝命令的，也不是上帝禁止的。
The king, an adiaphorite in matters of religion, allowed both Catholics and Protestants to worship freely. 在宗教问题上不关心道德的国王允许天主教徒和新教徒自由 worship。
近义词:moral relativist, indifferentist, unconcerned
反义词:moralist, puritan, zealot
词性:noun

adiaphoristic:/ˌeɪdiˌæfəˈrɪstɪk/
adj.
与不关心道德相关的；认为道德无关紧要的
relating to the view that some things are neither right nor wrong; indifferent to moral questions.
His adiaphoristic stance on the issue angered both sides of the debate. 他对这个问题不关心道德的立场激怒了辩论的双方。
The adiaphoristic nature of the new law made it difficult to enforce. 新法律不关心道德的性质使其难以执行。
近义词:neutral, non-judgmental, impartial
反义词:moralistic, judgmental, partisan
词性:adjective

adiaphory:/ˌeɪdiˈæfəri/
n.
道德中立；道德无关紧要的事物
something that is neither morally right nor wrong; a matter of indifference.
The question of whether to eat meat or not was considered an adiaphory by some religious groups. 一些宗教团体认为是否吃肉是一个道德中立的问题。
The council declared that the wearing of vestments was an adiaphory.  委员会宣布穿着法衣是道德无关紧要的事情。
近义词:non-essential, indifferent matter
反义词:moral imperative, ethical obligation
词性:noun


词源: From Greek *adiáphoros* "indifferent," from *a-* "not" + *diáphoros* "different."
