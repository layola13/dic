obversion:/əbˈvɜːrʒən/
noun
反面说法；换质位
The process of transforming a categorical proposition into an equivalent proposition that has the opposite quality (affirmative to negative, or vice versa) but the same subject and predicate terms, while replacing the predicate term with its complement.
No men are immortal is the obversion of All men are mortal.
所有的人都是凡人，反过来说就是没有人是不朽的。
equivalent statement, converse, contrapositive
obverse

ETYMOLOGY->
Late Middle English: from late Latin obversio(n-), from the verb obvertere ‘turn towards’, from ob- ‘towards’ + vertere ‘to turn’.
