brockish:/ˈbrɒkɪʃ/| adj.|沼泽的，污浊的|Resembling or characteristic of a brook, especially a small, muddy, or weedy one; marshy; boggy.  Often implying a sense of unpleasantness or stagnation.|The brockish water was stagnant and smelled foul. 这片沼泽的水停滞不前，散发着恶臭。|The air hung heavy and brockish around the neglected pond. 被忽视的池塘周围空气沉闷而污浊。|近义词：marshy, boggy, swampy, miry|反义词：clear, pristine, flowing, clean|词性:形容词

Etymology: From "brock," a now dialectal word for a badger, likely referring to the damp and muddy habitats badgers prefer.  Brock + -ish (suffix indicating resemblance or characteristic).
