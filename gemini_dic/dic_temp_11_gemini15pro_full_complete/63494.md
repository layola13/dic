entwinement:/ɪnˈtwaɪnmənt/ | n. |交织，缠绕，纠缠|The act of entwining or the state of being entwined; a close interweaving. |例句：The entwined branches of the trees created a canopy overhead.  树木交织的枝条在头顶形成了一个树冠。

例句：The entwining plot kept the viewer guessing what would happen next.  环环相扣的情节让观众不断猜测接下来会发生什么。|近义词：intertwining, interweaving, entanglement, embrace |反义词：separation, detachment, disentanglement |词性: noun

词源: From Middle English *entwynement, from entwine + -ment.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *twīnaz ("two-ply, double").
