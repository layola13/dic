acauline:/eɪˈkɔːlaɪn/| adj.|无茎的，贴地生长的|Lacking a stem or growing close to the ground; stemless.  Referring to plants that appear stemless, either because the stem is very short or because the leaves arise directly from a rootstock or rhizome.|The acauline dandelion hugged the earth, its bright yellow flower a stark contrast to the bare ground.|近义词：stemless, sessile|反义词：caulescent, stemmed|词性:形容词
Etymology:  From a- (without) + Latin caulis (stem, stalk). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱewl- (tree trunk).
