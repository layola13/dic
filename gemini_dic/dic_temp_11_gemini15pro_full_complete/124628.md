mousy:/ˈmaʊsi/ (US), /ˈmaʊzi/ (UK)| adj.|鼠灰色的，胆怯的，羞怯的|Resembling a mouse, especially in being quiet and timid.|She has mousy brown hair. 她有一头鼠灰色的棕色头发。|The new assistant was mousy and hardly spoke.  新来的助理很胆小，几乎不说话。|近义词：timid, shy, meek, retiring|反义词：bold, assertive, confident|词性:形容词
Etymology: From *mouse* + *-y*.  The color sense refers to the dull grayish-brown color of the fur of some mice. The figurative sense of "timid" likely arose from the perceived quiet and unobtrusive nature of mice.
