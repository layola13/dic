intercalibration: /ˌɪntərˌkælɪˈbreɪʃn/ | n. | 相互校准，比校，互校 |The process of comparing and adjusting the measurements or values of two or more instruments or systems to ensure consistency and accuracy.  |  例句：Intercalibration of the instruments is essential for accurate data comparison. 仪器的相互校准对于精确的数据比较至关重要。

例句：The intercalibration exercise helped to identify systematic biases between the different laboratories. 比校练习帮助识别了不同实验室之间的系统偏差。 | 近义词：cross-calibration, comparison, standardization | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "incompatibility" in a broad sense) | 词性: noun

intercalibrate: /ˌɪntərˈkælɪbreɪt/ | v. | 相互校准，比校，互校 |To compare and adjust the measurements or values of two or more instruments or systems to ensure consistency and accuracy. | 例句：We need to intercalibrate these thermometers to ensure accurate readings. 我们需要相互校准这些温度计以确保读数准确。

例句：The researchers intercalibrated their data with that of other studies. 研究人员将他们的数据与其他研究的数据进行了比校。| 近义词：cross-calibrate, compare, standardize | 反义词:  (No direct antonym) | 词性: verb


词源:  "inter-" (Latin, meaning "between") + "calibration" (from "caliber," ultimately from Arabic "qālib," meaning "mold").
