fo'c'sle: /ˈfoʊksəl/ (US), /ˈfɔːksəl/ (UK) | n. | 前甲板，船首楼 | The forecastle is the forward part of a ship's upper deck, traditionally used as the crew's living quarters. | The sailors gathered on the foc'sle to watch the sunset. 水手们聚集在前甲板上看日落。 |  foredeck, bow | stern | noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *forcastel, from Old French forcastel, from fors "outside" + castel "castle".  Originally referring to a raised structure at the front of a ship, often used for defensive purposes.
