drumlinoid:/ˈdrʌmlɪnɔɪd/ | adj. | 似鼓丘的，鼓丘状的 | Resembling or suggesting a drumlin in form.  A landform that looks like a drumlin but doesn't fully meet the geological criteria to be classified as one. | The landscape featured several drumlinoid hills, hinting at past glacial activity.  这些景观以一些鼓丘状的小山为特征，暗示着过去的冰川活动。 | 近义词: drumlin-like, elliptical | 反义词: (No direct antonym; could use terms describing contrasting landforms, e.g., irregular, jagged) | 词性: adjective

Etymology:  From drumlin + -oid.  "Drumlin" itself is of Irish origin, from *druimnín, diminutive of *druimm "ridge, back," of unknown ultimate origin.  The suffix "-oid" means "resembling," "having the form of."
