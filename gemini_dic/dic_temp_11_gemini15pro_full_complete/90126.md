hot-wire:/ˈhɑːtˌwaɪər/ (US) /ˈhɒtˌwaɪə(r)/ (UK)| v. n. |热启动，热线启动（汽车），热线（名词） | To start a vehicle without using the ignition key, typically by connecting wires.  As a noun, it can refer to the wire used in this process, or more generally to a wire carrying electric current that becomes hot. | Example (verb): The thieves hot-wired the car and sped off.  小偷们热启动了汽车，飞驰而去。

Example (noun):  He used a hot-wire to bypass the ignition. 他用一根热线绕过了点火装置。| Synonym (verb): jump-start, bypass | Antonym (verb):  N/A (no direct antonym for the action of hot-wiring) | Verb

hot-wire:/ˈhɑːtˌwaɪər/ (US) /ˈhɒtˌwaɪə(r)/ (UK)| n. |热线，热丝 | A wire heated by an electric current, used for cutting or burning.  | Example:  He used a hot-wire to cut through the foam. 他用热线切割泡沫。| Synonym: heated wire, filament | Antonym: N/A | Noun
