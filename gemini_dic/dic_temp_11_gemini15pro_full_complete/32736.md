chlorometer:/ˌklɔːˈrɒmɪtər/ (US) /ˌklɔːrˈɑːmɪtər/| n.|氯量计；测氯计|An instrument used to measure the amount of chlorine or chlorine compounds in a substance, especially in water.|The chlorometer showed high levels of chlorine in the pool water. 池水中的氯含量很高。|
chlorometer:/ˌklɔːˈrɒmɪtər/ (US) /ˌklɔːrˈɑːmɪtər/| n.|叶绿素计|An instrument used to measure the amount of chlorophyll in plants.|Researchers used a chlorometer to assess the health of the plants. 研究人员用叶绿素计来评估植物的健康状况。|
chlorometer:/ˌklɔːˈrɒmɪtər/ (US) /ˌklɔːrˈɑːmɪtər/| n.|颜色计|An instrument used to measure the intensity of color.|The artist used a chlorometer to ensure the colors in the painting were consistent. 艺术家用颜色计来确保画作中的颜色一致。


Etymology:  From chloro- (Greek khlōros, "pale green, yellowish") + -meter (from Greek metron, "measure").

Synonyms:  chlorimeter, colorimeter (for color intensity)

Antonyms:  N/A (No direct antonyms for a measuring instrument)

Word Type: Noun
