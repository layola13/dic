faddishly:/ˈfædɪʃli/
adjective
赶时髦地；一时流行地；
In a faddish manner; according to the latest fad or fashion.
She dressed faddishly, always chasing the latest trends. 她穿着很时髦，总是追逐最新的潮流。
He faddishly adopted the new diet, even though he didn't understand its principles. 他赶时髦地采用了新的饮食方式，即使他不了解其原理。
近义词: trendily, fashionably, stylishly
反义词: classically, timelessly, traditionally
词性: adverb

fad:/fæd/
noun
一时流行的狂热；时尚；
An intense and widely shared enthusiasm for something, especially one that is short-lived and without basis in the object's qualities; a craze.
The latest fad among teenagers is collecting vintage sneakers.  青少年中最新的流行趋势是收集复古运动鞋。
He dismissed the new health drink as just another fad. 他认为这种新的健康饮品只是另一种一时流行的玩意儿。
近义词: craze, trend, vogue
反义词: tradition, classic
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"fad" (n.) is of uncertain origin, first recorded 1868, perhaps a shortened form of "fadaise" (nonsense, trifle), which comes from Old French.  "faddish" (adj.)  and "faddishly" (adv.) are derived from "fad".
