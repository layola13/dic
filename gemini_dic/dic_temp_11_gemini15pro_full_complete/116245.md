mass-observe:/ˌmæs əbˈzɜːrv/ | v. | 大规模观察；群体观察 | To observe something on a large scale, often involving many people or automated systems. |  Scientists are mass-observing the behavior of bees to understand their decline. 科学家正在大规模观察蜜蜂的行为以了解它们数量下降的原因。

The project involves mass-observing traffic patterns in the city. 该项目包括对城市交通模式的大规模观察。 | 近义词: monitor, survey, track | 反义词: ignore, neglect | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
"mass-" + "observe"
"mass-" comes from Greek "mazza," meaning "lump of dough," later evolving to indicate a large quantity or aggregate.
"observe" comes from Latin "observare," meaning "to watch, heed, comply with," from "ob-" (toward) + "servare" (to keep, watch).
