overgreatly:/ˌoʊvərˈɡreɪtli/| adv.| 过度地，过分地 |  Meaning "to an excessive degree," formed by combining "over" (meaning excessively) and "greatly" (meaning to a large extent). | He overgreatly exaggerated the importance of his role in the project. 他过分夸大了他在项目中的作用。| 近义词: excessively, unduly, inordinately | 反义词: moderately, slightly, reasonably | 词性: 副词

Etymology->
From "over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi, meaning "above, beyond") + "greatly" (from "great" (Old English grēat, from Proto-Germanic *grautaz, meaning "big, large, thick") + "-ly" (adverbial suffix)).
