intoxicate:/ɪnˈtɑːksɪkeɪt/ /ɪnˈtɒksɪkeɪt/| vt. |使陶醉；使喝醉；使中毒|To excite or stupefy by alcohol or a drug, especially to the point of losing control over one's actions or thinking; to elate or exhilarate.|The music intoxicated the audience. 音乐使观众陶醉。|Synonyms: inebriate, exhilarate, thrill, enchant|Antonyms: sober, bore, depress|verb

intoxicated:/ɪnˈtɑːksɪkeɪtɪd/ /ɪnˈtɒksɪkeɪtɪd/| adj. |陶醉的；喝醉的；中毒的|Affected by alcohol or drugs especially to the point of losing control over one's actions or thinking; exhilarated.|He was intoxicated by the beauty of the sunset. 他被日落的美丽陶醉了。|Synonyms: drunk, inebriated, exhilarated, thrilled|Antonyms: sober, clear-headed|adjective


intoxication:/ɪnˌtɑːksɪˈkeɪʃn/ /ɪnˌtɒksɪˈkeɪʃn/| n. |陶醉；醉酒；中毒|The state of being intoxicated, especially by alcohol.|The driver's intoxication was the cause of the accident.  司机的醉酒是事故的原因。|Synonyms: drunkenness, inebriation, exhilaration, euphoria|Antonyms: sobriety, clarity|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *intoxicatus*, past participle of intoxicare "to poison," from Latin in- "in" + toxicum "poison"
