affrontedly:/əˈfrʌntɪdli/
adverb
冒犯地，被侮辱地
In a manner that shows offense or resentment at having been insulted or wronged.
She stared affrontedly at him when he suggested she was lying.  她被他暗示说谎后，冒犯地瞪着他。
He turned and walked away affrontedly. 他生气地转身走开了。
近义词: indignantly, resentfully, offensively
反义词: politely, respectfully, humbly
词性: adverb

词源:
From affront + -ed + -ly. Affront comes from the Old French afronter, meaning "to strike on the forehead," from a- "to" + front "forehead."  The meaning shifted to "insult" over time.
