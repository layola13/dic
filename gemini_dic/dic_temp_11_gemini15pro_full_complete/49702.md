demobilising:/ˌdiːˈməʊbɪlaɪzɪŋ/
verb
复员；遣散（军队）
The process of discharging soldiers from military service, typically after a war or conflict.
The government is demobilising thousands of soldiers. 政府正在遣散数千名士兵。
The army began demobilising its troops after the ceasefire. 停火后军队开始复员。
近义词:discharging, releasing, mustering out
反义词:mobilising, recruiting, enlisting
词性:Verb (present participle)

demobilise:/ˌdiːˈməʊbɪlaɪz/
verb
复员；遣散
To discharge from military service.
Thousands of soldiers were demobilised after the war. 战后数千名士兵复员。
The government plans to demobilise a large number of troops. 政府计划遣散大量的军队。
近义词:discharge, release, muster out
反义词:mobilise, recruit, enlist
词性:Verb

demobilisation:/ˌdiːˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
noun
复员；遣散
The act or process of demobilising.
The demobilisation of the army was a complex process. 军队复员是一个复杂的过程。
The rapid demobilisation of troops led to social unrest. 部队的快速复员导致了社会动荡。
近义词:discharge, release
反义词:mobilisation, recruitment
词性:Noun
