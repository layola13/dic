confined:/kənˈfaɪnd/ | adj. | 受限的，狭窄的，封闭的 | Restricted in area or volume; limited to a certain space or place; unable to leave a place, especially because of illness or disability. | The patient was confined to bed after the surgery. 病人手术后被限制在床上。|  She felt confined by her small town upbringing. 她觉得她的小镇成长环境限制了她。 | 近义词: restricted, limited, cramped, narrow, imprisoned | 反义词: free, open, unrestricted, spacious, liberated | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Late Latin *confinatus*, past participle of *confinare* "to border on, have a common boundary," from Latin *com-* "together" (see com-) + *finis* "boundary, end" (see finish (v.)).  The original sense was of places having a common border; sense of "restrict, imprison" developed in Latin.
