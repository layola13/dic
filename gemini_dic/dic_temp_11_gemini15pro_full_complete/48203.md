deconcoct:/ˌdɪkənˈkɒkt/ (US), /ˌdiːkənˈkɒkt/ (UK)| vt.| 捏造，编造，虚构；调制，配制（尤指饮料） |To invent or devise (a story, plan, etc.), especially fraudulently; to prepare by combining various ingredients, especially a drink or medicine.|He deconcocted a story about being robbed to avoid going to work. 他编造了一个被抢劫的故事以逃避上班。|She deconcocted a delicious soup from leftover vegetables. 她用剩下的蔬菜调制了一碗美味的汤。| synonyms: fabricate, invent, concoct, devise, formulate, contrive | antonyms: discover, reveal, expose | verb

deconcoction:/ˌdiːkənˈkɒkʃən/ (UK), /ˌdɪkənˈkɑːkʃən/ (US)| n.| 捏造的故事；调制品 |A story or plan that has been invented or devised, especially fraudulently; a mixture of various ingredients, especially a drink or medicine.|Her alibi was a complete deconcoction. 她的不在场证明完全是捏造的。|The deconcoction he created tasted surprisingly good. 他调制的饮料尝起来出奇地好。| synonyms: fabrication, invention, concoction, mixture, brew | antonyms: reality, truth, fact | noun
