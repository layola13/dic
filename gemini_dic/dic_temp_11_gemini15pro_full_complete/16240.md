banshee:/ˈbænʃiː/| n.|女妖，报丧女妖|A female spirit in Irish folklore whose wailing warns of an impending death in a family.|Legend says that if you hear a banshee's cry, someone in your family will soon die.|Synonyms: spirit, wraith, phantom|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a supernatural being.  Could consider "human," "mortal," or "living being" as contrasting concepts.)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Irish *bean sídhe* "woman of the fairy mound," from Old Irish *ben side*.
