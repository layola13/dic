capuchins:/ˈkæpʊtʃɪnz/ | n. |卷尾猴； капуцины (俄语)|A type of New World monkey, known for their intelligence and often trained to assist people with disabilities. They are easily recognized by their capuchin-like hoods of fur.|Capuchins are highly intelligent primates. 卷尾猴是非常聪明的灵长类动物。| Capuchins have been observed using tools in the wild.  人们观察到卷尾猴在野外使用工具。|Synonyms: monkey, primate|Antonyms: None|Noun

capuchin:/ˈkæpʊtʃɪn/ | n. | капуцин (俄语);卡布奇诺咖啡;卡布奇诺修士;卷尾猴; |A type of coffee made with espresso and steamed milk, often topped with foamed milk; a member of a branch of the Franciscan order; a capuchin monkey.|I ordered a capuchin with extra foam. 我点了一杯加奶泡的卡布奇诺。|The capuchin monk dedicated his life to helping the poor.  这位卡布奇诺修士一生致力于帮助穷人。|Synonyms: (for coffee) latte, macchiato; (for monk) Franciscan friar |Antonyms: None|Noun, (rarely) Adjective (referring to the order)
