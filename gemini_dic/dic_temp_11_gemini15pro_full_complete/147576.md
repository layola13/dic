overpersuade:/ˌōvərˌpərˈswād/ | v. | 过度劝说；说得服服帖帖 | To persuade someone excessively, often to the point of causing them to regret their decision. | He overpersuaded her into investing in his risky venture, and she lost all her money. 他过度劝说她投资他的风险企业，结果她赔光了所有的钱。| synonyms: coerce, browbeat, pressure, railroad | antonyms: dissuade, deter | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + persuade.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much."  "Persuade" comes from the Latin word "persuadere," meaning "to advise thoroughly" or "to convince."
