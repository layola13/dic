falconoid:/ˈfɔːlkənɔɪd/| adj. n.|像隼的，隼类的；隼类动物|Resembling a falcon; pertaining to or characteristic of falcons.  A falconoid bird. |例句：The kestrel has a falconoid appearance. 红隼有像隼一样的外貌。

例句：The falconoid swooped down and caught its prey.  这只隼类猛禽俯冲下来抓住了猎物。|近义词：falcon-like, raptorial|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider prey)|词性: adjective, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From *falcon* + *-oid*.  *Falcon* comes from Old French *faucon*, from Late Latin *falco*, ultimately from Latin *falx* "sickle," referring to the bird's curved talons. *-oid* is a suffix meaning "resembling," "having the form of," from Greek *-oeidēs*.
