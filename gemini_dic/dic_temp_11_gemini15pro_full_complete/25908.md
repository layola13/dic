burn-out:/ˈbɜːrnaʊt/ (US) /ˈbɜːnaʊt/ (UK)| n.|精疲力竭，倦怠|A state of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion caused by prolonged or excessive stress.|She's suffering from burn-out after working too many hours. 她因工作时间过长而感到精疲力竭。|
burn-outs:/ˈbɜːrnaʊts/ (US) /ˈbɜːnaʊts/ (UK)| n. (plural)|精疲力竭，倦怠（复数）|Multiple instances of a state of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion.|The company has seen a rise in burn-outs among its employees.  这家公司员工的倦怠感有所上升。|
burn-out:/ˈbɜːrnaʊt/ (US) /ˈbɜːnaʊt/ (UK)| adj.|燃尽的，烧坏的|Relating to or caused by burn-out.|He took a break from his burn-out job. 他辞去了让他精疲力竭的工作。|


近义词:exhaustion, fatigue, breakdown|
反义词:energy, vigor, rejuvenation|
词性: noun, adjective


词源:  "Burn-out" originated from the literal sense of something being completely consumed by fire.  This meaning was later extended metaphorically to describe a state of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion.
