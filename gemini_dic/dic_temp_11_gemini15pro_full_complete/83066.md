gurnard: /ˈɡɜːrnərd/ (UK), /ˈɡɜːrnɑːrd/ (US) | n. |  鲂鮄；红娘鱼 |  Any of various marine fishes of the family Triglidae, typically having a large head encased in bony plates, a tapering body, and fingerlike pectoral fin rays used for crawling on the seabed. |  The gurnard is a bottom-dwelling fish found in coastal waters.  鲂鮄是一种生活在沿海水域的底栖鱼类。 |  synonyms: sea robin, tub fish | antonyms: None (no direct opposite for a specific fish type) | noun
