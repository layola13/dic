disorderliness:/dɪsˈɔːrdərlɪnəs/
n.| 混乱，无序 |The disorderliness of his room reflects his chaotic mind. 他的房间的凌乱反映了他混乱的思维。|
英文解析:The state or quality of being disorderly; lack of order or regularity.
例句：The disorderliness of the protest made it difficult for police to control. 抗议活动的混乱使得警方难以控制。
例句：She was appalled by the disorderliness of the house. 她对房子的杂乱无章感到震惊。
近义词：chaos, disarray, mess, confusion, untidiness
反义词：order, tidiness, neatness, organization
词性: 名词

disorderly:/dɪsˈɔːrdərli/
adj.| 混乱的，无序的，妨碍治安的 |The disorderly crowd became violent. 混乱的人群变得暴力起来。|
英文解析: Characterized by disorder; lacking order or regularity; unruly or disruptive.
例句：His disorderly conduct led to his arrest. 他的扰乱治安行为导致他被捕。
例句：The teacher struggled to control the disorderly classroom. 老师努力控制混乱的教室。
近义词：chaotic, messy, untidy, unruly, disruptive
反义词：orderly, tidy, neat, organized, peaceful
词性: 形容词

词源:
From Middle English *disordelynesse, from disordely +‎ -nesse, equivalent to disorderly +‎ -ness.  Disorderly itself comes from dis- +‎ orderly, with order ultimately from Latin ordo.
