dervish: /ˈdɜːvɪʃ/ (UK) /ˈdɜːrvɪʃ/ (US)| n. |托钵僧；苦行僧|A member of a Muslim (specifically Sufi) religious order who has taken vows of poverty and austerity, often known for their ascetic practices and sometimes for their ecstatic dancing or whirling.|The dervish whirled in a trance-like state. 托钵僧在恍惚的状态下旋转。|Synonym: ascetic, mystic, monk|Antonym: materialist, hedonist|noun

dervish: /ˈdɜːvɪʃ/ (UK) /ˈdɜːrvɪʃ/ (US)| n. |狂热者；极端分子 (figurative, less common)|Someone who behaves with extreme passion or zeal, often in a slightly uncontrolled or frenzied manner.|He's a dervish when it comes to politics.  谈到政治，他就像个狂热分子。|Synonym: fanatic, zealot, extremist|Antonym: moderate, pacifist|noun
