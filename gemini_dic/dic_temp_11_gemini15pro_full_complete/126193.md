muzzle:/ˈmʌz.əl/ (US), /ˈmʌz.əl/ (UK)| n. vt.|口套，枪口，钳制言论|A covering placed over an animal's snout to prevent it from biting or eating, or the projecting part of an animal's head including the nose and mouth.  As a verb, it means to put a muzzle on or to silence or restrain someone. |例句：The dog was fitted with a muzzle to prevent it from biting. 这只狗被戴上了口套以防止它咬人。

例句：The government tried to muzzle the press. 政府试图压制新闻界。|近义词：gag, silence, restrain, suppress|反义词:free, unleash, liberate|词性: noun, verb

muzzles:/ˈmʌz.əlz/ (US), /ˈmʌz.əlz/ (UK)| n. vt.|口套（复数），枪口（复数），钳制言论（用于复数主语）|Plural form of muzzle.|例句：The dogs' muzzles were removed after the training session. 训练结束后，狗的口套被取了下来。

例句：The reporters felt their muzzles tightening as the government increased censorship. 随着政府加强审查，记者们感到他们的言论受到越来越多的限制。|近义词：gags, silences, restraints|反义词:freedoms, releases|词性: noun, verb
