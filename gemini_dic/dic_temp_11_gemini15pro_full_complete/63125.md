enslaver:/ɪnˈsleɪvər/| n. |奴役者，使成奴隶的人|Someone who enslaves another person.  The enslavers profited greatly from the forced labor. |例句：The enslavers treated their captives with unspeakable cruelty.  奴隶主以难以言喻的残忍对待他们的俘虏。

The enslaver was eventually brought to justice for his crimes.  奴隶主最终因其罪行被绳之以法。|近义词：oppressor, tyrant, subjugator |反义词:liberator, emancipator |词性:noun
enslave:/ɪnˈsleɪv/| vt. |奴役，使成奴隶|To make someone a slave; to deprive someone of their freedom. |例句：The company was accused of enslaving its workers with unfair contracts and low wages.  该公司被指控以不公平的合同和低工资奴役其工人。

Warlords often enslave conquered populations. 军阀经常奴役被征服的人口。|近义词：subjugate, oppress, dominate |反义词:liberate, free, emancipate |词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From en- + slave + -er.  "En-" acts as a prefix meaning "to put into" or "to cause to be," while "-er" denotes the agent performing the action. The root word "slave" comes from the Medieval Latin word "sclavus" referring to the Slavic peoples who were often enslaved during that period.
