kineses: /kɪˈniːsiːz/ /kaɪˈniːsiːz/
| n. |趋性，趋动性|A kineses is a non-directional movement of an organism in response to a stimulus. It is characterized by random turning or change in speed. |例句：The pillbugs exhibited kineses in response to the light, moving more rapidly in brightly lit areas and slowing down in darker areas.  药丸虫对光表现出趋性，在明亮的地方移动得更快，在黑暗的地方则减慢速度。
