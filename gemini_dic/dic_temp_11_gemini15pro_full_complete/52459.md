dieresis:/daɪˈɛrɪsɪs/ /daɪˈɪərɪsɪs/
|n.|分音符|A diacritical mark ( ¨ ) placed over a vowel to indicate that it is pronounced in a separate syllable, as in naïve, Brontë, or Zoë.|The dieresis in "naïve" indicates that the "i" is pronounced separately from the "a".|近义词:diaeresis, umlaut|反义词: (No direct antonym exists)|noun
