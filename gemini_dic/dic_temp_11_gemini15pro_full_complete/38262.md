comtism: /ˈkɒmtɪzəm/ (US) /ˈkɒmtɪɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |孔德主义，实证主义|A philosophy founded by Auguste Comte, emphasizing the importance of scientific observation and classification in understanding the world and advocating a social system based on this philosophy.| Comte's positivism, later known as Comtism, influenced many thinkers of his time. 孔德的实证主义，后来被称为孔德主义，影响了他那个时代的许多思想家。|近义词: positivism,  Auguste Comte's philosophy|反义词: metaphysics, idealism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Auguste Comte (French philosopher) + -ism.  Derived from the name of the French philosopher Auguste Comte, the founder of positivism.  The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, belief, or system.  Therefore, Comtism literally means the doctrine or system of Comte.
