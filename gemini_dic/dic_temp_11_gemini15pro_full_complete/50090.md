denticete:/ˈdɛntɪˌsiːt/ | n. |齿鲸|A toothed whale, belonging to the suborder Odontoceti, characterized by having teeth and a single blowhole. |Dolphins and porpoises are examples of denticetes. 海豚和鼠海豚是齿鲸的例子。|近义词：toothed whale |反义词:baleen whale (须鲸)|名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dentatus* ("having teeth") +‎ *-cētus* (“whale, sea monster”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₁dónts* (“tooth”) and *ḱētos* (“whale, sea monster”).
