cucullus:/kʊˈkʌləs/| n.|兜帽，头巾，风帽|A hood or cowl, especially one attached to a cloak or monastic habit. |The monk pulled his cucullus over his head to shield himself from the rain.  僧侣拉起兜帽遮雨。|Synonyms: hood, cowl, cape, cloak|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "uncovered," "bareheaded")|noun

cucullus:/kʊˈkʌləs/| n.|(解剖学) 喙状突|A beak-shaped anatomical structure.|The cucullus of the cerebellum is involved in motor control. 小脑的喙状突参与运动控制。|Synonyms: beak, rostrum (in anatomical contexts)|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym in this context)|noun
