bloodlettings: /ˈblʌdˌletɪŋz/
noun
放血；瀉血；冲突；流血事件
The practice of letting blood as a medical treatment; bloodshed, often in a conflict.
In the past, bloodlettings were a common medical practice. 过去，放血是一种常见的医疗方法。
The bloodlettings of the revolution left the country scarred. 革命的流血事件给这个国家留下了伤痕。
Synonyms: bleeding, phlebotomy, bloodshed, carnage, slaughter
Antonyms: healing, transfusion
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English blodletting, equivalent to blood + letting.  "Letting" here refers to the act of allowing something to flow out.  Ultimately from Old English *blōdlǣtan.
