caprice:/kəˈpriːs/| n.|反复无常，怪想，奇想|A sudden and unaccountable change of mood or behavior.|She quit her job on a caprice. 她一时心血来潮辞掉了工作。|近义词：whim, fancy, impulse|反义词：constancy, predictability|词性:名词
ETYMOLOGY->
From French caprice "whim, fancy," from Italian capriccio "sudden start, shuddering fear, whim," of unknown origin, perhaps from capo "head" + riccio "hedgehog" (referring to hair standing on end from fright), or from Late Latin *capra "goat" (referring to a goat's frisky behavior).
