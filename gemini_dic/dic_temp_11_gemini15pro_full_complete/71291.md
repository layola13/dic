fixer-upper:/ˈfɪksər ˈʌpər/ | n. |待修房屋，需要装修的房子|A house or other property in need of repairs and renovations, often bought at a low price with the intention of improving it. |例句：They bought a fixer-upper in the countryside and spent months renovating it. 他们在乡下买了一栋需要装修的房子，花了几个月的时间翻新。

例句：That fixer-upper has great potential, but it needs a lot of work. 那栋待修的房子很有潜力，但需要大量的工作。|近义词：handyman's special, renovation project, doer-upper |反义词：turnkey property, move-in ready |词性:名词

fixer-uppers:/ˈfɪksər ˈʌpərz/ | n. |待修房屋（复数）|Plural form of "fixer-upper," referring to multiple houses or properties in need of repairs and renovations. |例句：They specialize in flipping fixer-uppers for a profit. 他们专门以盈利为目的翻新待修房屋。

例句：We looked at several fixer-uppers before deciding on this one. 我们看了几处需要装修的房子才决定买这处。|近义词：handyman's specials, renovation projects, doer-uppers |反义词：turnkey properties, move-in ready |词性:名词
