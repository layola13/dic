co-ordination:/koʊˌɔːrdɪˈneɪʃən/ /kəʊˌɔːdɪˈneɪʃn/| n.|协调，调和|The act of organizing different parts of something so that they work together efficiently.|The co-ordination of the rescue effort was remarkable. 救援工作的协调非常出色。|近义词：collaboration, cooperation, harmony, integration, synchronization|反义词：discord, disorganization, conflict|noun

co-ordinate:/koʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt/ /kəʊˈɔːdɪneɪt/| v.|协调，调和|To make different parts of something work together efficiently.|We need to co-ordinate our efforts to achieve the best results. 我们需要协调努力以取得最佳结果。|近义词：harmonize, integrate, synchronize, organize, arrange|反义词：disorganize, disrupt|verb

co-ordinate:/koʊˈɔːrdɪnət/ /kəʊˈɔːdɪnət/| adj.|同等的，并列的|Of the same order or degree; equal in rank or importance.|The two projects are co-ordinate. 这两个项目是同等级的。|近义词：equal, equivalent, parallel, corresponding|反义词：subordinate, unequal|adjective

co-ordinates:/koʊˈɔːrdɪnəts/ /kəʊˈɔːdɪnəts/| n.|坐标|A set of numbers or letters used to show the exact position of something on a map or graph.|Please give me the GPS co-ordinates of your location. 请告诉我你所在位置的GPS坐标。|近义词：position, location|反义词: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Late Latin *coōrdinātiōn*, from *coōrdinātus*, past participle of *coōrdināre*, from *co-* (together) + *ōrdināre* (to arrange, put in order)
