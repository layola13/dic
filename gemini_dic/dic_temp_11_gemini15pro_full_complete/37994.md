complicities: /kəmˈplɪsɪtiz/ | n. | 共谋，串通，同谋关系 | involvement in wrongdoing; the state of being an accomplice |  The investigation revealed the complicities of several government officials in the scandal. 调查揭露了几位政府官员在这起丑闻中的共谋关系。 | synonyms: collusion, conspiracy, connivance | antonyms: innocence, non-involvement | noun

complicity: /kəmˈplɪsɪti/ | n. | 共谋，串通，同谋 | the state of being involved with others in an illegal activity or wrongdoing | His complicity in the robbery was undeniable. 他参与抢劫是不可否认的。| synonyms: collusion, collaboration, connivance | antonyms: innocence, non-involvement | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *complicitatem, from Latin complicit-, past participle stem of complicare "to fold together," from com- "together" + plicare "to fold."
