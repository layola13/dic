blackishness:/ˈblækɪʃnəs/| n. |黑暗； blackness|The blackness of the night sky was breathtaking. 夜空的黑暗令人叹为观止。|近义词：darkness, obscurity |反义词:brightness, lightness| noun
Etymology: From black + -ish + -ness.  Black from Old English *blæc "dark," from Proto-Germanic *blakaz "burned" (source also of Old Saxon blak, Old Frisian blak, Middle Dutch blac, Dutch blak, Old High German blah, German bleich "pale," Old Norse blakkr "dark," Swedish black "ink"). -ish and -ness are common suffixes indicating a quality or state.
