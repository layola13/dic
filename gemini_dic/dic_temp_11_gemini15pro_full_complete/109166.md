legitimating: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmeɪtɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
使合法化; 赋予合法性
The process of legitimating power often involves complex rituals and ceremonies.  赋予权力合法性的过程通常涉及复杂的仪式和典礼。
Making changes to the constitution requires legitimating them through a public referendum. 修改宪法需要通过公投使其合法化。
近义词: validating, justifying, authorizing, sanctioning
反义词: invalidating, delegitimizing, condemning
词性: verb (present participle of legitimate)

legitimate: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət/
adjective
合法的；正当的；合理的
The government is a legitimate authority. 政府是一个合法的权威机构。
He had a legitimate reason for being late. 他迟到是有正当理由的。
近义词: legal, valid, lawful, justifiable
反义词: illegal, invalid, unlawful, illegitimate
词性: adjective

legitimate: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmeɪt/
verb
使合法化；赋予合法性
The new law legitimized the sale of alcohol. 新法律使酒类的销售合法化。
The president sought to legitimate his position by holding democratic elections. 总统试图通过举行民主选举来使其地位合法化。
近义词: validate, justify, authorize, sanction
反义词: invalidate, delegitimize, condemn
词性: verb

词源: From Late Latin legitimatus, past participle of legitimare ‘to make lawful,’ from Latin legitimus ‘lawful’ (see legitimate (adj.)).
