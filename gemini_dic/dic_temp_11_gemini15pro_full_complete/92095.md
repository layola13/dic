hyperresonant:/ˌhaɪpərˈrɛzənənt/ | adj. | 过度共鸣的，亢进的 | Characterized by an exaggerated resonance, often indicating an abnormal condition in the lungs or chest cavity.  A hyperresonant sound is lower-pitched and louder than a resonant sound, and has a longer duration. |  Example: The doctor detected a hyperresonant sound when percussing the patient's chest, suggesting a pneumothorax. 医生在叩诊病人的胸部时发现了过度共鸣的声音，提示可能有气胸。

Example:  Hyperresonant percussion notes are common in patients with emphysema.  肺气肿患者常见过度共鸣的叩诊音。 | 近义词: booming, resonant | 反义词: dull, flat | 词性: adjective

词源: hyper- (prefix meaning "over, excessive") + resonant (from Latin resonare, "to resound")
