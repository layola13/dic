ichthyophthira:/ɪkθiɒfˈθɪərə/ | n. | 鱼虱;鱼虱属 |A genus of parasitic copepods that infest fish.  These parasites attach themselves to the skin, fins, or gills of fish and feed on their blood and tissues.| Example: The ichthyophthira infestation caused significant mortality in the fish farm.  鱼虱的感染导致了渔场的大量死亡。|Synonym: fish louse, sea louse |Antonym: None (no direct opposite for a parasitic organism) | Noun
