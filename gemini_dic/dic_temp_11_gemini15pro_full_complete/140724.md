omniparity:/ɒmˈnɪpærɪti/ | n. | 杂食性 | The ability to obtain energy and nutrients from more than one trophic level.  This means an organism can function as both an herbivore and a carnivore, consuming a wide range of food sources including plants, animals, algae, and fungi. |  Example 1: Bears exhibit omniparity, feeding on berries and fish. 熊表现出杂食性，以浆果和鱼类为食。

Example 2: Human omniparity allows us to thrive in diverse environments. 人类的杂食性使我们能够在不同的环境中繁衍生息。 | Synonym: polyphagy | Antonym: monophagy, stenophagy | noun
