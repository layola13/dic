lapsable:/ˈlæpsəbəl/| adj.|可失效的，可终止的|Subject to lapse; allowing something to lapse or become void.  Able to lapse, expire, or become void, especially of rights, privileges, or agreements.|例句：The offer is lapsable after 30 days.  这个提议在30天后失效。|例句：The insurance policy is lapsable if premiums are not paid. 如果不支付保费，保险单将失效。|近义词：expiring, terminable, voidable, forfeitable|反义词：permanent, perpetual, non-expiring|词性:形容词
lapse:/læps/| n. v.|失效，终止；流逝；过失|n. an interval or passage of time; a temporary failure of concentration, memory, or judgment; a decline from a higher state. v. (of a right, privilege, or agreement) become invalid because it is not used, renewed, or enforced; (of time) pass or elapse.|词源: From Latin lapsus, past participle of labi "to slip, glide, fall, sink."|
