incriminate: /ɪnˈkrɪmɪneɪt/ | vt. | 控告；暗示…有罪；牵连 | To make someone appear guilty of a crime or wrongdoing; to show evidence of someone's guilt. | The evidence found at the scene incriminated the suspect.  现场发现的证据表明嫌疑犯有罪。|  The witness's testimony incriminated the defendant. 目击者的证词牵连了被告。 | synonyms: accuse, implicate, charge, indict | antonyms: exonerate, acquit, absolve | verb

incriminate: /ɪnˈkrɪmɪneɪt/ | vt. |  使负罪；连累 | To cause someone to appear guilty or to involve them in a crime or wrongdoing. | His careless words incriminated him in the scandal.  他粗心的话使他卷入了丑闻。|  Don't say anything that might incriminate yourself. 不要说任何可能让你自己负罪的话。| synonyms: entangle, compromise | antonyms: vindicate, clear | verb
