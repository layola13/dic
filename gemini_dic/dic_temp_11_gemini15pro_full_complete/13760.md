audacity:/ɔːˈdæsəti/ /ɑːˈdæsəti/| n. |大胆；鲁莽；厚颜无耻|A willingness to take bold risks.  The state or quality of being audacious.  Rude or disrespectful behavior; impudence.|She had the audacity to ask for a raise after only two months on the job. 她才工作两个月就敢要求加薪，真是大胆。| 近义词: boldness, daring, nerve, temerity, chutzpah | 反义词: timidity, shyness, diffidence, humility | 词性: noun

Etymology: From late Middle English (in the sense ‘boldness, courage’): from Old French audacite, from Latin audacitas, from audax, audac- ‘bold’.
