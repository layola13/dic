cowpock:/ˈkaʊpɒk/ (UK) /ˈkaʊpɑːk/ (US)| n. |牛痘|A mild infectious disease of cows, caused by a poxvirus that is closely related to the vaccinia and variola viruses. Cowpox can be transmitted to humans, producing localized skin lesions. Historically, exposure to cowpox was observed to confer immunity to smallpox, leading to the development of the smallpox vaccine.|The farmer contracted cowpox from milking an infected cow.|Synonym: vaccinia|Antonym: N/A|Noun
