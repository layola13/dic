brakes:/breɪks/
n.|刹车|A device for slowing or stopping a moving vehicle, typically by applying friction to the wheels.|The car's brakes failed, and it crashed into a tree. 汽车的刹车失灵了，撞到了一棵树上。|synonyms: decelerators, stoppers|antonyms: accelerators|noun

brakes:/breɪks/
v.|刹车; 抑制|To slow or stop a moving vehicle or process.|He braked suddenly to avoid hitting the deer. 他突然刹车以避免撞到鹿。|synonyms: decelerate, halt, stop|antonyms: accelerate|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *braken*, from Old Norse *breka* (to break, crack), related to Old English *brecan* (to break). The sense development is from the idea of breaking or interrupting motion.
