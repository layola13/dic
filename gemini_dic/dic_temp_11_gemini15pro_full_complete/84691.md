handful:/ˈhændfʊl/ (US), /ˈhændfʊl/ (UK)| n.|一把；少量；难以控制的人（或动物）|A small number or quantity that can be held in one hand.  Also, a person or animal that is difficult to control.|例句：She grabbed a handful of berries from the bush. 她从灌木丛中抓起一把浆果。

例句：He's a real handful, that child. 那孩子真是个小捣蛋。|近义词：few, smattering, small amount, troublemaker|反义词:lot, many, large amount|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *handfull, from hand + full.  Literally "hand full."
