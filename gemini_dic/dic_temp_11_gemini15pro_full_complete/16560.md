barnabite:/ˈbɑːrnəbaɪt/| n.|巴纳拜特教士；巴纳拜会会员|A member of a Roman Catholic religious order of regular clerks founded in Milan in 1530, formally known as the Clerks Regular of St. Paul.|He was a Barnabite priest known for his charitable works. 他是一位以慈善工作而闻名的巴纳拜特教士。|近义词: cleric, priest |反义词: layman|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *Barnabita*, from *Sant'Ambrogio ad Nemus* (St. Ambrose at the Woods), the location of their first church in Milan, later dedicated to St. Barnabas.
