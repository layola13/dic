discriminating:/dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ/| adj.|有识别力的；有鉴赏力的；歧视的|Showing fine taste or judgment. Making a clear distinction. Treating a person or group differently from another in an unfair way.|例句：She has a discriminating palate for wine. 她对葡萄酒有很高的鉴赏力。|近义词：discerning, selective, perceptive, prejudiced (negative), biased (negative)|反义词：undiscriminating, indiscriminate, uncritical, impartial, unbiased|词性:形容词

discriminating:/dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ/| adj.|歧视的|Treating a person or group differently from another in an unfair way.|例句：The company was accused of discriminating against women. 这家公司被指控歧视女性。|近义词：prejudiced, biased|反义词：impartial, unbiased, fair|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin discriminat-, past participle stem of discriminare "to separate, distinguish," from Latin discrimen "interval, difference," from discernere "to separate, distinguish."
