convivialists:/kənˈvɪviəlists/
| n. |好交际的人，宴饮者|People who are sociable and enjoy the company of others, especially while eating and drinking.|The convivialists gathered around the table, laughing and sharing stories.  好交际的人们围坐在桌子旁，笑着分享故事。|
|例句：The party was full of convivialists, all enjoying the food, drink, and conversation.  派对上挤满了喜欢交际的人，他们都在享受美食、美酒和谈话。|
|例句：The convivialists stayed late into the night, enjoying each other's company.  这些好交际的人们深夜才离开，享受彼此的陪伴。|
|近义词：socializers, minglers, extroverts, partygoers|
|反义词：introverts, recluses, loners|
|词性：noun|


convivial:/kənˈvɪviəl/
| adj. |欢乐的，好交际的|Friendly, lively, and enjoyable.|The atmosphere at the party was convivial. 派对上的气氛很欢乐。|
|例句：We enjoyed a convivial evening with our friends. 我们和朋友们度过了一个愉快的夜晚。|
|例句：The restaurant had a convivial atmosphere. 这家餐厅的气氛很热闹友好。|
|近义词：sociable, jovial, festive, merry|
|反义词：unsociable, dour, somber|
|词性：adjective|


conviviality:/kənˌvɪviˈælɪti/
| n. |欢乐，好交际|The quality of being friendly, lively, and enjoyable.|The conviviality of the gathering made everyone feel welcome. 聚会的欢乐气氛让每个人都感到宾至如归。|
|例句：The conviviality of the host made the party a success. 主人的热情好客使派对取得了成功。|
|例句：We were impressed by the conviviality of the local people.  当地人的热情好客给我们留下了深刻的印象。|
|近义词：sociability, joviality, festivity, merriment|
|反义词：unsociability, gloom, somberness|
|词性：noun|
