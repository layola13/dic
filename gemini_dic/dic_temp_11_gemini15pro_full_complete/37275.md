commandant:/ˌkɒmənˈdɑːnt/ /ˌkɑːmənˈdænt/| n.| 司令官；指挥官； commandant (especially in the US) an officer who is in charge of a military school, prison, or camp|The commandant of the Marine Corps base addressed the troops.  海军陆战队基地司令向部队发表了讲话。|Synonyms: commander, officer, chief, director, superintendent|Antonyms: subordinate, follower|noun

commandant:/ˌkɒmənˈdɑːnt/ /ˌkɑːmənˈdænt/| n.| 院长；系主任；(especially in some European and Asian countries) an officer in charge of a particular group of soldiers or a particular place|The commandant of the police academy welcomed the new recruits. 警察学院院长欢迎新学员。|Synonyms: director, head, principal, dean|Antonyms: subordinate, follower|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French commandant, present participle of commander "to command," from Late Latin commandare.
