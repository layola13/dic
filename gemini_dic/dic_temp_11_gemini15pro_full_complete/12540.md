asphyxia:/æsˈfɪksiə/ | n. | 窒息，缺氧 |A condition arising when the body is deprived of oxygen, causing unconsciousness or death. | The victim died of asphyxia. 受害者死于窒息。|  suffocation, choking | ventilation, oxygenation | noun

asphyxiate: /æsˈfɪksieɪt/ | vt. | 使窒息，使闷死 | To cause someone to become unconscious or die by preventing them from breathing. | The fumes from the fire asphyxiated the family. 火灾产生的烟雾使这家人窒息而死。| suffocate, choke, smother | resuscitate, ventilate | verb

asphyxiated: /æsˈfɪksieɪtɪd/ | adj. | 窒息的，闷死的 |  Having been deprived of oxygen, resulting in unconsciousness or death. | The asphyxiated child was rushed to the hospital. 窒息的孩子被紧急送往医院。 | suffocated, choked, smothered | resuscitated, ventilated | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *asphyxie*, from Greek *asphyxia* "stopping of the pulse," from *a-* "without" + *sphyxis* "pulse."
