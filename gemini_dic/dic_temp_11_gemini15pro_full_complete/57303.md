downstage:/ˌdaʊnˈsteɪdʒ/ | adj. adv.| 舞台前部的，靠近观众席的；在舞台前部 |  Relating to or situated in the front part of a stage, nearest to the audience. | The actor moved downstage to deliver his monologue.  演员走到舞台前部，发表了他的独白。 | 近义词: frontstage | 反义词: upstage | 词性:形容词/副词

downstages:/ˌdaʊnˈsteɪdʒɪz/ | v.| 将…移至舞台前部；使…处于次要地位；抢…的镜头 |  To move (something or someone) downstage; to relegate to a less important position; to upstage. | The director downstages the supporting characters to emphasize the protagonist. 导演将配角移到舞台后方，以突出主角。 | 近义词: overshadows, upstages | 反义词: highlights, features | 词性:动词
