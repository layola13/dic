black-jacking:/ˌblækˈdʒækɪŋ/ | n. | 敲诈勒索；强迫；以暴力脅迫 | The act of extorting or coercing someone, often through violence or threats. |  They resorted to black-jacking the store owner to get protection money. 他们敲诈勒索店主以获取保护费。| Synonyms: extortion, coercion, intimidation | Antonyms: persuasion, cooperation | noun

black-jack:/ˈblækˌdʒæk/ | n. | 橡皮棍；二十一点（一种纸牌游戏） | A short, heavy club; a card game. | The police officer carried a black-jack. 警察带着一根橡皮棍。 | Synonyms: cosh, truncheon (for the club); twenty-one (for the game) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for the object; for the game perhaps "losing") | noun

black-jack:/ˌblækˈdʒæk/ | vt. | 胁迫；强迫；用棍棒打 | To extort or coerce someone, often through violence or threats; to hit with a blackjack. | They black-jacked him into signing the contract.  他们胁迫他签署了合同。| Synonyms: coerce, extort, bludgeon | Antonyms: persuade, cooperate | verb
