natica:/ˈnætɪkə/ | n. |玉螺; 月壳螺 |A genus of predatory sea snails, typically having a globular shell with a short spire and a large aperture.  They are known for their ability to drill holes into the shells of other mollusks to feed on them. | The natica drilled a neat hole in the clam shell. 玉螺在蛤壳上钻了一个整齐的洞。| The beachcomber found a beautiful natica shell. 海滩拾荒者发现了一个美丽的玉螺壳。|synonyms: moon snail, necklace shell | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a specific genus of snail. Perhaps, "prey" as opposed to "predator" in its ecological role.) | noun
