discomfit:/dɪsˈkʌmfɪt/ | v. | 使不安；使窘迫；挫败 | To make someone feel uneasy, awkward, or embarrassed; to thwart or defeat. | The unexpected question discomfited her, and she stammered a reply.  突如其来的问题让她感到不安，她结结巴巴地回答。

Her failure to secure the promotion discomfited her deeply. 她未能获得晋升，这使她深感不安。 | 近义词:  unsettle, disconcert, embarrass, frustrate, thwart | 反义词: comfort, reassure, encourage | 词性: 动词

discomfited: /dɪsˈkʌmfɪtɪd/ | adj. |  不安的；窘迫的；挫败的 | Feeling uneasy, awkward, or embarrassed; thwarted. | He looked discomfited when I challenged his assumptions. 当我质疑他的假设时，他看起来很不安。

The discomfited army retreated after the surprise attack. 挫败的军队在突袭后撤退了。| 近义词: uneasy, embarrassed, frustrated | 反义词: comfortable, confident, assured | 词性: 形容词

词源:
From Old French desconfit, past participle of desconfire  "to defeat, rout, undo," from des- "expressing reversal" + con- "with, together" + faire "to do, make."  The meaning shifted from "defeat" to "disconcert" and "make uneasy."
