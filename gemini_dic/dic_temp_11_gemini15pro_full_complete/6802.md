anaptotic:/ˌænəˈpɒtɪk/| adj.|无格变化的，无屈折变化的|Lacking inflection; uninflected.  Relating to or characterized by the absence of inflectional variations.|A language with an anaptotic case system would not change the form of nouns to mark their grammatical function.  The researcher studied the anaptotic nature of certain adverbs.|近义词：uninflected, invariable|反义词: inflected, declinable|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἀνάπτωτος (anáptōtos, “uninflected”), from ἀν- (an-, “not”) + πτῶσις (ptôsis, “fall, case”).
