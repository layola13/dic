insulator:/ˈɪnsjəleɪtər/ (US), /ˈɪnsjʊleɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n.|绝缘体；隔离者；隔热体|A material that does not allow heat, electricity, or sound to travel through it easily.  A person or thing that insulates.|例句：Rubber is a good insulator. 橡胶是良好的绝缘体。

例句：The electrician wrapped the wires with an insulator. 电工用绝缘体包裹电线。|近义词：nonconductor, dielectric|反义词：conductor|词性：名词


insulate:/ˈɪnsjəleɪt/ (US), /ˈɪnsjʊleɪt/ (UK)| v.|使绝缘；使隔离；隔热|To cover or protect something with a material that prevents heat, electricity, or sound from passing through.|例句：They insulated the attic to reduce heat loss. 他们在阁楼做了隔热，以减少热量损失。

例句：The walls are insulated with fiberglass. 墙壁用玻璃纤维隔热。|近义词：protect, shield, isolate|反义词：expose|词性：动词


insulating:/ˈɪnsjəleɪtɪŋ/ (US), /ˈɪnsjʊleɪtɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|绝缘的；隔热的|Preventing heat, electricity, or sound from passing through.|例句：Insulating materials are used in building construction. 绝缘材料用于建筑施工。

例句：An insulating layer of fat keeps the penguins warm. 一层隔热的脂肪使企鹅保持温暖。|近义词：protective, shielding|反义词：conductive|词性：形容词
