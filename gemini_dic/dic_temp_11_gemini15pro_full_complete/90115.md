hot-pot:/ˈhɒtˌpɒt/ (UK) /ˈhɑːtˌpɑːt/ (US)| n.|火锅，砂锅|A cooking pot, typically made of metal, used for cooking food in liquid over direct heat, especially one used for communal meals at the table.  Also refers to the meal itself, especially a Chinese fondue.|We had hot-pot for dinner last night.  我们昨晚吃了火锅。|Synonyms: fondue, casserole, stew|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for the object itself.  Could consider "cold dish" as an antonym for the meal type.)|noun

hot-pot:/ˈhɒtˌpɒt/ (UK) /ˈhɑːtˌpɑːt/ (US)| n.|热点，热门话题|A subject or issue that is currently receiving a lot of public attention or discussion.|The economy is a real hot-pot at the moment. 经济是目前的热点话题。|Synonyms: hot topic, burning issue, talking point|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "unimportant issue" or "niche topic")|noun
