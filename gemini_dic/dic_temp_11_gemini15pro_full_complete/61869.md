empower:/ɪmˈpaʊər/ (US) /ɪmˈpaʊə(r)/ (UK)| vt.|授权；使能够|To give someone the authority or power to do something; to make someone stronger and more confident, especially in controlling their life and claiming their rights.|例句：Education empowers people to make informed decisions. 教育使人们能够做出明智的决定。|近义词：authorize, enable, strengthen|反义词: weaken, disempower|词性:动词

empowerment:/ɪmˈpaʊəmənt/ (US) /ɪmˈpaʊəmənt/ (UK)| n.|授权，赋能|The process of becoming stronger and more confident, especially in controlling one's life and claiming one's rights.|例句：Women's empowerment is crucial for social progress. 女性赋权对社会进步至关重要。|近义词：enablement, authorization|反义词: disempowerment|词性:名词

词源:  From en- + power.  "En-" acts as a prefix meaning "to put into" or "to cause to be."  So, "empower" literally means "to put into power."
