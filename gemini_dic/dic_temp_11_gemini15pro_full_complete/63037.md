enraptures:/ɪnˈræptʃərz/
|v.|使狂喜，使入迷
|fills with delight; transports with pleasure.
|The beauty of the sunset enraptures me every evening.  每天傍晚，日落的美丽都让我陶醉。
|The performance so enraptured the audience that they gave a standing ovation. 表演如此令人着迷，以至于观众起立鼓掌。
|captivates, enthralls, charms, fascinates
|bores, repels, disgusts
|verb

enraptured:/ɪnˈræptʃərd/
|adj.|狂喜的，入迷的
|filled with delight; transported with pleasure.
|She had an enraptured look on her face as she listened to the music.  她听着音乐，脸上露出欣喜若狂的表情。
|The enraptured audience couldn't take their eyes off the stage.  着迷的观众无法将目光从舞台上移开。
|delighted, ecstatic, thrilled, captivated
|bored, unimpressed, apathetic
|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From en- (meaning "to cause to be in" or "to put into") + rapture (from Latin *raptura, from rapere "to seize, carry off").  So, literally, "to put into a state of rapture."
