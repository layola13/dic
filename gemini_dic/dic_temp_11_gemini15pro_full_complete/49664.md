demised:/dɪˈmaɪzd/ | adj. |  去世的，已故的；转让的（尤指财产）|  Transferred or conveyed by will or lease.  Primarily used in legal contexts to refer to the passing of an estate or interest in property upon death.  It can also mean simply "dead." | The demised premises shall be used solely for residential purposes.  (已故的房产将仅用于居住目的。)  | Synonyms: deceased, late, bequeathed, conveyed | Antonyms: living, extant, inherited (when referring to the recipient) | adjective


demised:/dɪˈmaɪz/ | v. |  遗赠，转让（财产）；死亡 | To convey or transfer (property) by will or lease. Also, a more formal term for "to die." |  He demised his estate to his children. (他把他的财产遗赠给了他的孩子们。) | Synonyms: bequeath, transfer, convey, die | Antonyms: inherit, receive, live, survive | verb
