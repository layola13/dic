ground-breaking: /ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ/ | adj. | 开创性的；突破性的 | Introducing new ideas or methods; innovative. |  The scientist made a ground-breaking discovery in the field of medicine.  这位科学家在医学领域取得了突破性的发现。 |  innovative, pioneering, revolutionary, seminal |  unoriginal, conventional, traditional | adjective

ground-breaking: /ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ/ | n. |  破土动工；奠基 | The ceremony of breaking the ground to formally begin construction. | The ground-breaking ceremony for the new hospital was attended by the mayor. 新医院的奠基仪式由市长主持。 | commencement, inauguration, initiation | completion, conclusion | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "ground" (referring to the earth's surface) + "breaking" (the act of creating a rupture or opening).  The literal sense of breaking ground for construction evolved metaphorically to mean making a new and significant beginning in any field.
