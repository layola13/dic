melancholy: /ˈmɛlənˌkɒli/ (UK) /ˈmɛlənˌkɑːli/ (US) | n. adj.|忧郁，悲哀；忧郁的|A feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause.  Causing or expressing pensive sadness.|例句：A deep melancholy settled over him.  一种深深的忧郁笼罩着他。|近义词：sadness, depression, gloom, dejection|反义词：happiness, joy, elation|词性: 名词, 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *melancholie*, from Late Latin melancholia, from Ancient Greek μελαγχολία (melankholía, “melancholy, sadness”), from μέλας (mélas, “black, dark”) + χολή (kholḗ, “bile”).  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholy.
