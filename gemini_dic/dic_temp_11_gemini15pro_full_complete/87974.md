high-hats:/ˈhaɪˌhæts/| n.|踩镲, 高帽钹 (plural)|A pair of cymbals mounted on a stand operated by a foot pedal.  They are used in drum kits and other percussion setups.|The drummer's high-hats were incredibly precise and added a lot of energy to the song.  鼓手的踩镲非常精确，为歌曲增添了很多活力。|Synonyms: cymbals|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a musical instrument)|noun

high-hat:/ˈhaɪˌhæt/| adj.|高傲的，自命不凡的|Snobbish, condescending, thinking oneself superior to others.|She had a very high-hat attitude and looked down on everyone else. 她态度非常高傲，瞧不起其他人。|Synonyms: snobbish, arrogant, haughty, condescending, supercilious|Antonyms: humble, modest, down-to-earth|adjective
