heteromyaria:/ˌhɛtərəʊˈmɪəriə/
| n. |异鼠亚目|A suborder of rodents that includes kangaroo rats, pocket mice, and pocket gophers, characterized by external fur-lined cheek pouches.|Kangaroo rats belong to the suborder Heteromyaria. 袋鼠鼠属于异鼠亚目。|Synonyms: none (it's a scientific classification)|Antonyms: none (it's a scientific classification)|noun

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek ἕτερος (héteros, “other”, “different”) + μῦς (mûs, “mouse”) + -aria (New Latin taxonomic suffix denoting a suborder).  Essentially meaning "different mice" referring to their distinctive cheek pouches.
