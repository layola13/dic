ambrotype: /ˈæmbrəˌtaɪp/ | n. | 银版照相，蛋白照片 | An early type of photograph made by placing a glass negative against a dark background, typically a piece of black velvet, creating a positive image. | Example 1: The museum's collection included several ambrotypes from the Civil War era.  博物馆的收藏品中包括几张内战时期的银版照片。

Example 2:  He found an ambrotype of his great-grandmother in the attic. 他在阁楼里找到了一张他曾祖母的银版照片。 | Near synonyms:  tintype, ferrotype, daguerreotype | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "digital photograph" in a modern context) | noun
