mezzotint:/ˌmetsoʊˈtɪnt/ | n. | 铜版雕刻法；(用此法制成的)铜版画 | A method of engraving on copper or steel by scraping and burnishing a roughened surface to produce light and shade. A print made using this method. |  Example: The artist created a haunting mezzotint of a moonlit landscape. 艺术家用铜版雕刻法创作了一幅令人难忘的月光风景画。 | Example: The gallery displayed a collection of 18th-century mezzotints.  画廊展出了一批18世纪的铜版画。 | Synonym: engraving, print | Antonym: (No direct antonym exists, perhaps "photograph" as a contrasting modern method of image reproduction) | Noun


mezzotinter:/ˌmetsoʊˈtɪntər/ | n. | 铜版雕刻家 | An engraver who uses the mezzotint method. | Example: The skilled mezzotinter meticulously burnished the plate.  这位技艺娴熟的铜版雕刻家一丝不苟地打磨着印版。| Example:  Famous mezzotinters of the 18th century often reproduced famous paintings. 18世纪著名的铜版雕刻家经常复制名画。 | Synonym: engraver, printmaker | Antonym: (No direct antonym exists) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *mezzotinto*, literally "half-tint," from *mezzo* ("half") + *tinto* ("tinged" or "dyed").
