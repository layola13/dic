acclimatization:/əˌklaɪmətaɪˈzeɪʃn/ |n.| 适应，驯化|The process of adapting to a new climate, environment, or situation.|  The climbers spent a few days at base camp for acclimatization to the altitude.  登山者们在大本营待了几天以适应高海拔。|Synonyms: adaptation, adjustment, acclimation|Antonyms: maladaptation|noun

acclimatize:/əˈklaɪmətaɪz/ |v.| 适应，驯化|To become accustomed to a new climate, environment, or situation.| It took me a while to acclimatize to the hot and humid weather.  我花了一段时间才适应了湿热的天气。|Synonyms: adapt, adjust, acclimate|Antonyms: maladapt|verb

Etymology: From French *acclimatation* or *acclimatement*, from *acclimater* "to accustom to a climate," from *a-* (from Latin *ad-*) "to" + *climat* "climate."
