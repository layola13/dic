embolic:/ɪmˈbɑːlɪk/ | adj. | 栓塞的；栓子引起的 | Relating to or caused by an embolus (a blood clot, air bubble, or other foreign matter) that has been transported by the bloodstream to lodge in a vessel and obstruct it. |  The patient suffered an embolic stroke due to a blood clot. 病人因血凝块而遭受了栓塞性中风。

The doctor suspected an embolic event after observing the patient's symptoms. 医生观察到病人的症状后怀疑是栓塞事件。 | 近义词: thrombotic, obstructive | 反义词: unobstructed, patent | 词性: 形容词

embolus:/ˈɛmbələs/ | n. | 栓子 | A blood clot, air bubble, piece of fatty deposit, or other foreign matter that has been transported by the bloodstream to lodge in a vessel and obstruct it. | The embolus traveled to his brain, causing a stroke. 栓子游走到他的大脑，导致了中风。

Doctors worked quickly to remove the embolus before it caused further damage. 医生迅速采取行动清除栓子，以免造成进一步损害。| 近义词: clot, thrombus | 反义词: None | 词性: 名词
