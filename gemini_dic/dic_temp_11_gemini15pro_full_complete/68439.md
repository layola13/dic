falterings:/ˈfɔːltərɪŋz/| n. |踌躇，迟疑，支吾；摇晃，蹒跚|Repeated or hesitant steps; moments of wavering or hesitation in speech, purpose, or action.  A plural form of "faltering."|His falterings on the stage showed his nervousness. 他在舞台上的踌躇显示了他的紧张。|近义词：hesitations, waverings, uncertainties, stumbles|反义词：certainties, assurances, firmness, steadiness|词性:名词

Etymology: From the verb "falter," which comes from the Middle English "falteren," frequentative of "fallen" (to fall), from Old English "feallan."  The "-ing" suffix turns it into a gerund or present participle, and the "-s" makes it plural, referring to multiple instances of faltering.
