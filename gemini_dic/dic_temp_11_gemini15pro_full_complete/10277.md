appanage:/ˈæpənɪdʒ/ | n. | 俸禄，遗产；附属物 | Something, such as property or a privilege, that is set aside for the use and benefit of a person, especially a member of a royal family. | The prince received a generous appanage from the king. 王子从国王那里得到了丰厚的俸禄。| synonyms: allowance, endowment, provision, grant, stipend | antonyms: debt, liability | noun


appanage:/ˈæpənɪdʒ/ | n. | 附属物，附属品 | Something incidental or added on. | The library was an appanage of the university. 图书馆是大学的附属设施。| synonyms: adjunct, accessory, appendage, attachment | antonyms: core, essence | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *apanage, from *apanoier "to provide with bread," from pan "bread," from Latin panis.
