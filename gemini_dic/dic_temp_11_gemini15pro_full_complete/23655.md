brandishing:/ˈbrændɪʃɪŋ/ | v. |挥舞，炫耀 | waving something (especially a weapon) in a threatening or excited way | The knight was brandishing his sword. 骑士挥舞着他的剑。| 近义词：waving, flourishing, flaunting | 反义词：concealing, hiding | 词性: 动词 (present participle of brandish)

brandish:/ˈbrændɪʃ/ | vt. |挥舞，炫耀 | to wave or flourish (something, especially a weapon) as a threat or in anger or excitement | He brandished a knife at them. 他向他们挥舞着刀。| 近义词：wave, flourish, flaunt | 反义词：conceal, hide | 词性: 动词

词源：From Old French *brandiss-, present participle stem of brandir "to brandish," from Frankish *brand "sword" (source also of Old High German brant, German Brand "firebrand, sword"). The original notion in both French and Germanic was "to move quickly, swing."
