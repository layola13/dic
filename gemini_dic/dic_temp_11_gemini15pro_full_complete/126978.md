narwhal:/ˈnɑːrwəl/ (US), /ˈnɑːwəl/ (UK)| n.|独角鲸，一角鲸|A medium-sized toothed whale (Monodon monoceros) of Arctic waters, having a long, spirally twisted tusk extending forward from the upper jaw of the male.|The narwhal's tusk can grow up to ten feet long. 独角鲸的獠牙可以长到十英尺长。|Synonyms: unicorn whale, sea unicorn|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

Etymology: From Middle Dutch *narhwal, from Old Norse *náhvalr, from nór "corpse" + hvalr "whale" (so called because of the mottled gray color resembling a drowned sailor).
