desolateness:/ˈdɛsələtnəs/ | n. | 荒凉，凄凉，孤寂 | The desolateness of the landscape was overwhelming.  这片景色的荒凉令人难以承受。 |  A feeling of utter desolateness swept over her. 一种彻底的孤寂感席卷了她。| synonyms: bleakness, barrenness, loneliness, desolation | antonyms: vibrancy, liveliness, cheerfulness, comfort | noun

desolate:/ˈdɛsələt/ | adj. | 荒凉的，凄凉的，孤寂的 | The house stood desolate and empty. 这房子孤零零地空着，一片荒凉。| She felt desolate after her husband died. 丈夫去世后，她感到十分孤寂。 | synonyms: bleak, barren, lonely, deserted | antonyms: vibrant, lively, cheerful, populated | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *desolatnes, from Anglo-Norman or Old French desolat, past participle of desoler (“to desolate”), from Latin dēsōlātus, past participle of dēsōlāre (“to abandon, forsake, lay waste”), from dē- (“thoroughly”) + sōlāre (“to make alone”), from sōlus (“alone”).
