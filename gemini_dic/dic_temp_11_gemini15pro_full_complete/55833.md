dividend: /ˈdɪvɪdend/ (US), /ˈdɪvɪdənd/ (UK) | n. | 股息；红利；回报；被除数 | A sum of money paid regularly (typically quarterly) by a company to its shareholders out of its profits (or reserves).  A benefit, reward, or advantage that is gained as a result of something. The number being divided in a division problem. | Example 1: The company declared a dividend of $0.50 per share. 公司宣布每股派息0.50美元。

Example 2:  Education pays rich dividends. 教育带来丰厚的回报。

Example 3: In the equation 10 ÷ 2 = 5, 10 is the dividend. 在等式 10 ÷ 2 = 5 中，10是被除数。| Synonym:  payout, return, yield, bonus, gain, profit | Antonym: loss, debit, expense | noun
