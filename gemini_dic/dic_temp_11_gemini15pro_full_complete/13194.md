at loose ends:/æt luːs ɛndz/| adj.|无所事事，闲散无聊|Being at loose ends means having nothing to do and feeling restless or unsettled.  It describes a state of boredom and a lack of direction.|例句：He was at loose ends after retiring from his job.  他退休后无所事事。

例句：With the children away, she found herself at loose ends. 孩子们不在家，她觉得很无聊。|近义词：idle, restless, bored, unoccupied|反义词：busy, occupied, engaged|词性:adjective


Etymology->
"Loose ends" originally referred to literal threads or ropes that were untied or unfastened.  This nautical term gradually evolved into a figurative expression for unfinished business or unresolved matters. By the late 18th century, the phrase "at loose ends" began to be used to describe a person's state of being unsettled and without purpose.
