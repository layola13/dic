hyoscine:/ˈhaɪəˌsiːn/ (US), /ˈhaɪəˌsiːn/ (UK)| n.|东莨菪碱;莨菪宁|A drug made from a plant of the nightshade family. It is used to treat nausea, vomiting, and motion sickness, and to dilate the pupil of the eye.|She was given hyoscine to prevent travel sickness.|Synonyms: scopolamine|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a specific drug, but one could consider drugs with opposite effects, such as stimulants if hyoscine is used as a sedative)|noun
