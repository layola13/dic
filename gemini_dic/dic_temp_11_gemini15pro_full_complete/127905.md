neolithically:/ˌniːəˈlɪθɪkli/ | adv. | 新石器时代地 | In a way relating to the Neolithic period, the later part of the Stone Age. | They lived neolithically, farming the land and raising livestock. 他们过着新石器时代的生活，耕种土地，饲养牲畜。 | 近义词: primitively | 反义词: modernly | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Neolithic + -ally.  Neolithic comes from Neo- (new) + -lithic (stone). Ultimately from Greek *neos* (new) and *lithos* (stone).
