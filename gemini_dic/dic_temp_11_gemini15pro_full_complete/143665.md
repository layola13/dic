outlander:/ˈaʊtlændər/| n.|外国人，外地人；异乡人|A person from a foreign country, especially one who is not familiar with the customs and language of the place they are visiting.  A stranger.|例句：The outlander struggled to understand the local dialect.  这个外地人很难理解当地的方言。

例句：She felt like an outlander in her new school.  她在新学校里感觉像个外乡人。|近义词：foreigner, stranger, alien, newcomer|反义词：native, local, resident|词性: noun

outlandish:/aʊtˈlændɪʃ/| adj.|古怪的，奇异的；异国情调的|Looking or sounding bizarre or unfamiliar.|例句：She wore an outlandish outfit to the party. 她穿着一套古怪的服装去参加聚会。

例句：He told an outlandish story about his travels. 他讲述了一个关于他旅行的奇异故事。|近义词：bizarre, strange, eccentric, peculiar, unusual, exotic|反义词：ordinary, conventional, normal, common|词性: adjective
