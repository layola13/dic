词源:  "Loopy" comes from "loop," referring to something circular or circuitous.  The "-y" suffix transforms it into an adjective.  "Loopiest" is the superlative form, meaning "most loopy."  The original sense likely related to convoluted thinking, eventually extending to general eccentricity.
