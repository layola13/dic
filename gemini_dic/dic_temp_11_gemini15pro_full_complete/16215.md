bannister:/ˈbænɪstər/ (US) /ˈbænɪstə(r)/ (UK)| n. |栏杆，扶手|A railing at the side of a staircase or balcony to prevent people from falling.|She slid her hand along the smooth, polished bannister as she descended the stairs. 她下楼时，手沿着光滑的扶手滑下去。|Synonyms: railing, handrail, balustrade|Antonyms: None|Noun

bannisters:/ˈbænɪstəz/ (plural)| n. |(复数)栏杆，扶手|The handrails and supporting posts at the side of a staircase.|The bannisters need a fresh coat of paint. 扶手需要重新粉刷一下。|Synonyms: railings, handrails, balustrades|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French "banister" or "balustre," from Italian "balaustro," ultimately from Greek "balaustion" (pomegranate flower), because of the resemblance of the turned posts of a balustrade to the shape of a half-open pomegranate flower.
