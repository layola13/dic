antilynching:/ˌæntiˈlɪntʃɪŋ/
adjective
反私刑的
Opposed to the practice of lynching.
The NAACP led the fight for antilynching legislation.  全国有色人种协进会领导了反私刑立法的斗争。
Antilynching laws were finally passed after decades of struggle.  经过几十年的斗争，反私刑法终于获得通过。
近义词:anti-mob, against vigilantism
反义词:pro-lynching (This is a highly sensitive and potentially offensive term, use with extreme caution)
词性:adjective


Etymology:
anti- (prefix) + lynching (noun)

"anti-" is a prefix of Greek origin meaning "against," "opposite of," "opposed to."

"lynching" comes from the late 18th century, American English,  likely derived from the name of Charles Lynch (1736-1796), a Virginia planter and justice of the peace who headed an extralegal court during the American Revolution.
