dissembling:/dɪˈsɛmblɪŋ/
| adj. | 伪装的，掩饰的 | concealing one's true motives, feelings, or beliefs |  He had a dissembling smile that made it hard to trust him. 他脸上挂着伪善的笑容，让人难以信任他。 |
| v. | 掩饰，假装 | to conceal one's true motives, feelings, or beliefs | She dissembled her anger with a forced smile. 她强颜欢笑以掩饰她的愤怒。|
|近义词: feigning, pretending, masking, cloaking, disguising|
|反义词:  revealing, disclosing, expressing, being honest, being truthful|
|词性: adjective, present participle of dissemble (verb)|

词源:
From Middle English *dissemblen, from Old French dissimuler, from Latin dissimulāre (“to disguise, conceal, dissemble”), from dis- (“not, un-”) + simulāre (“to resemble, simulate, feign, copy”), from similis (“like, resembling”).
