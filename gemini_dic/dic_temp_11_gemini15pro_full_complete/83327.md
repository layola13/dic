gypsies:/ˈdʒɪpsiz/ | n. |吉普赛人；流浪者；居无定所的人|A traditionally nomadic ethnic group, believed to have originated in the Indian subcontinent, known for their rich culture, music, and fortune-telling traditions.  This term is now considered offensive by many and the preferred term is Roma/Romany.  |The gypsies traveled across the country in caravans. 吉普赛人乘坐大篷车穿越全国。

The gypsies were known for their vibrant music. 吉普赛人以其充满活力的音乐而闻名。|近义词：Roma, Romany, travellers, nomads|反义词：settlers|词性:noun


gypsy:/ˈdʒɪpsi/ | adj. |吉普赛的；流浪的；居无定所的|Relating to or characteristic of the Roma/Romany people. This term is now considered offensive by many. |She lived a gypsy life, traveling from place to place. 她过着吉普赛般的生活，四处旅行。

He had a gypsy soul, always yearning for adventure. 他有一颗流浪的心，总是渴望冒险。|近义词：nomadic, itinerant, wandering|反义词：settled, sedentary|词性:adjective


词源: From Middle English *gipcyan, from Egyptian (by way of Greek), a popular but erroneous name for the Roma as they were thought to have originated from Egypt.
