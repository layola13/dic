legion:/ˈliːdʒən/
n.
军团，众多，大量
A large group of soldiers, especially in ancient Rome. A great number of people or things.
Caesar led his legions into Gaul. 凯撒率领他的军团进入高卢。
There are legions of fans who would disagree with you. 有大批粉丝会不同意你的观点。
synonyms: army, host, multitude, throng, horde
antonyms: few, handful, minority, squad
