newspaperwoman: /ˈnjuːzˌpeɪpərˌwʊmən/ (British), /ˈnuːzˌpeɪpərˌwʊmən/ (American)
noun
女报人；女记者
A woman who works for a newspaper, typically as a reporter or writer.
The newspaperwoman interviewed the mayor about the new policy. 女报人采访了市长关于新政策的问题。

近义词: reporter, journalist, newswoman
反义词: None (No direct opposite for a profession)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From newspaper + woman. "Newspaper" itself comes from "news" (from Middle English "newes," plural of "newe," meaning "new thing") + "paper" (from Old French "papier," ultimately from Latin "papyrus").
