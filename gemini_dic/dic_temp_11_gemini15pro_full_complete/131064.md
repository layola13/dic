nonbarbarously: /ˌnɒnbɑːrˈbərəsli/ (US) /ˌnɒnbɑːˈbærəsli/ (UK) | adv. | 非野蛮地，文明地 | In a way that is not barbarous; civilly. | The prisoners of war were, surprisingly, treated nonbarbarously.  令人惊讶的是，战俘受到了文明的对待。|  Synonyms: humanely, civilly, kindly, compassionately | Antonyms: barbarously, cruelly, savagely, inhumanely | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + barbarously.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "barbarously" is the adverbial form of "barbarous," meaning "extremely cruel or unpleasant."  "Barbarous" ultimately comes from the Greek word "bárbaros," meaning "foreign, strange."
