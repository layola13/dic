gloms:/ɡlɑmz/ | v. (gloms, glomming, glommed) | 抓住，攫取；理解，明白 | To seize or grab; to understand or catch on to something. | He glommed onto the first taxi he saw. 他一把抓住他看到的第一辆出租车。

gloms:/ɡlɑmz/ | v. (gloms, glomming, glommed) | 抓住，攫取；理解，明白 |  She quickly glommed onto the main idea of the lecture. 她很快理解了讲座的中心思想。

gloms:/ɡlɑmz/| n. (informal, rare) |  A grasping or seizing; an understanding. | (noun usage is rare)  |  His glom of the situation was surprisingly accurate. 他对情况的理解出奇地准确。


gloms: /ɡlɑmz/ | adj. (informal, very rare) |  Grasping, avaricious. | (adjective usage is extremely rare) | He had a gloms attitude towards money. 他对金钱的态度很贪婪。


Etymology: Origin uncertain, possibly from Yiddish *glemen or German schlimm.

Synonyms: grab, seize, snatch, grasp, comprehend, understand, latch onto

Antonyms: release, let go, misunderstand, misinterpret

Word Type: Primarily a verb, rarely a noun or adjective.
