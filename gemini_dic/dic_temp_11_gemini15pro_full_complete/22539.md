boor:/bʊr/| n.|粗人，不雅的人|A rude, insensitive, and clumsy person.  A boor is someone who is ill-mannered and insensitive to the feelings of others.|He behaved like a complete boor at the dinner party, insulting the host and spilling wine on the tablecloth. 他在晚宴上表现得像个十足的粗鲁之人，侮辱主人，还把酒洒在桌布上。|Synonyms: lout, churl, oaf, yahoo, brute|Antonyms: gentleman, lady, sophisticate|noun

Etymology->
From Middle English *bore*, *boor*, from Middle Dutch *boere*, *boer* ("farmer, peasant").  The word originally referred to a peasant and later acquired the negative connotation of rudeness and insensitivity.
