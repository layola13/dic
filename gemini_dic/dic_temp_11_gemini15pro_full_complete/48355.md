decubitus:/dɪˈkjuːbɪtəs/ | n. | 褥疮；卧位 | A chronic ulcer of the skin caused by pressure over bony prominences, especially those on the back, buttocks, and heels, occurring in bedridden or otherwise immobile patients. |  The nurse checked the patient regularly for signs of decubitus. 护士定期检查病人是否有褥疮的迹象。

The patient developed a decubitus ulcer on his heel. 病人的脚后跟长了一个褥疮。 | 近义词：bedsore, pressure sore, pressure ulcer | 反义词：None | 词性：名词

decubitus:/dɪˈkjuːbɪtəs/ | adj. | 卧位的 |  Relating to or characterized by the posture of lying down.  | The decubitus position can put pressure on certain areas of the body.  卧位会对身体的某些部位施加压力。

The doctor examined the patient in the decubitus position. 医生在卧位检查了病人。 | 近义词：recumbent, prostrate, supine, prone  | 反义词： erect, upright, standing | 词性：形容词
