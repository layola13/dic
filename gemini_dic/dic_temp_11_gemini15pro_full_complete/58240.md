drum:/drʌm/| n. vi. vt.|鼓; 鼓状物; 鼓声; 石首鱼科|A cylindrical musical instrument played by striking with sticks or the hands, typically having a taut membrane stretched over one or both ends.  A similar cylindrical container, especially one for storing liquids. To play a drum or drums. To make a rhythmic sound by hitting something repeatedly.|例句：He plays the drum in a band. 他在一个乐队里敲鼓。

例句：She drummed her fingers on the table nervously. 她紧张地用手指敲着桌子。|近义词：beat, percussion, barrel, cylinder|反义词:silence, quiet|词性:名词, 动词

词源:
From Middle English *dromme, from Middle Dutch dromme, tromme, from Old French *drombe, from Frankish *trumma (“trumpet”), from a Germanic source akin to Old High German trumba (“trumpet”), Old English truma (“troop”).  Related to dream. The verb is derived from the noun.
