gyration:/dʒaɪˈreɪʃən/ | n. |旋转，回旋，旋转运动|The rapid gyration of the dancer made me dizzy. 舞蹈演员快速的旋转让我感到头晕。|A spinning motion; a circular or spiral movement. The gyration of the planets around the sun. 行星绕太阳的旋转运动。|例句：

The gyration of the top fascinated the child.
陀螺的旋转让孩子着迷。

The gyration of the dancers was mesmerizing.
舞者们的旋转令人着迷。

|近义词：rotation, whirling, spinning, revolution|反义词：stillness, immobility|词性：名词

gyrate:/ˈdʒaɪˌreɪt/ | v. |旋转，回旋，盘旋|The leaves gyrated in the wind. 树叶在风中旋转。|To move in a circle or spiral; to rotate. The gyroscope gyrated smoothly. 陀螺仪平稳地旋转。|例句：

The planets gyrate around the sun.
行星绕太阳旋转。

The dancer gyrated wildly to the music.
舞者随着音乐疯狂地旋转。


|近义词：rotate, spin, whirl, revolve|反义词：stop, halt|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin gyrationem, accusative of gyratio (“a turning round”), from Latin gyro (“I turn in a circle”), from Ancient Greek γυρόω (guróō, “I turn round”).
