huskily:/ˈhʌskɪli/| adv.|沙哑地；粗声地；声音低沉地|In a husky voice; hoarsely.  It describes a voice that sounds rough, deep, and often quiet, usually due to illness, strong emotion, or smoking.|She spoke huskily, her voice thick with tears. 她沙哑地说着，声音哽咽着泪水。

He coughed huskily and cleared his throat. 他沙哑地咳嗽了一声，清了清嗓子。|近义词：hoarsely, roughly, gruffly|反义词：clearly, smoothly, melodically|词性：副词

huskily:/ˈhʌskɪli/| adj.|（尤指男人）健壮的，强壮的|Strong and powerfully built (especially of a man). It describes someone who is physically strong and well-built.|He was a huskily built man with broad shoulders. 他是一个身材魁梧、肩膀宽阔的男人。

The boxer was huskily built and looked intimidating.  这位拳击手身材健壮，看起来很吓人。|近义词：strong, sturdy, brawny, muscular|反义词：slim, thin, frail, weak|词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From husk + -ly.  "Husk" refers to the dry outer covering of certain fruits or seeds.  The connection to voice likely comes from the image of a voice being rough and dry, like a husk.
