doppler:/ˈdɒplər/ (UK), /ˈdɑːplər/ (US)| n. adj.|多普勒，多普勒的|Relating to the change in frequency of a wave (such as light or sound) as the source and observer move towards or away from each other.  It is named after Christian Doppler, an Austrian physicist.|The Doppler effect is used in radar to measure the speed of moving objects. 多普勒效应用于雷达测量移动物体的速度。|synonyms: Doppler shift, Doppler's principle|antonyms: N/A|noun, adjective
