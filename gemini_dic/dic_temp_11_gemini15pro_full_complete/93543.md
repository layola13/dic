imagination:/ɪˌmædʒɪˈneɪʃn/| n.|想象力，想象; 虚构的事物|The faculty or action of forming new ideas, or images or concepts of external objects not present to the senses.|She has a vivid imagination. 她有丰富的想象力。| synonyms: fantasy, creativity, ingenuity| antonyms: reality, actuality| noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French imaginacion, from Latin imaginatio(n-), from imaginari ‘picture to oneself’, from imago, imagin- ‘image’.
