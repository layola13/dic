mean-well:/ˌmiːnˈwɛl/ | adj. | 善意的，好心的 | Having good intentions, but often ineffectual or clumsy. |  He's a mean-well guy, but he always seems to mess things up. 他是个好心人，但他似乎总是把事情搞砸。|  例句：She meant well, but her advice wasn't very helpful. 她本意是好的，但她的建议不是很有帮助。| 近义词: well-meaning, kind-hearted, benevolent | 反义词: malicious, malevolent, ill-intentioned | 词性: 形容词

mean-well:/ˌmiːnˈwɛl/ | n. | 好心人 | A person with good intentions, often ineffectual or clumsy. | He's a bit of a mean-well, always trying to help but often making things worse. 他有点好心，总是想帮忙，但常常把事情弄得更糟。| 例句：The mean-wells of the world often get taken advantage of. 世界上的好心人常常被利用。| 近义词: do-gooder (often used ironically) | 反义词: malefactor, villain | 词性: 名词
