brahmans: /ˈbrɑːmən, ˈbrɑːmɪn/ | n. | 婆罗门（印度种姓）| A member of the highest Hindu caste, traditionally the priestly caste. |  The brahmans performed the religious ceremonies. 婆罗门执行宗教仪式。|  Brahmins performed sacred rituals and were responsible for teaching and preserving the Vedas.  婆罗门举行神圣的仪式，并负责教授和保存吠陀经。 | synonyms: Brahmin, priest | antonyms: outcaste, pariah | noun

brahman: /ˈbrɑːmən/ | n. | 梵（印度教哲学中的终极实在）| The ultimate reality in Hindu philosophy, the impersonal, divine ground of all existence. |  The concept of Brahman is central to Hinduism. 梵的概念是印度教的核心。|  Hindus believe that Brahman is the source and sustainer of all things. 印度教徒相信梵是一切事物的起源和维持者。 | synonyms: Absolute, ultimate reality, God | antonyms: Maya (illusion) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Sanskrit *brāhmaṇa*, meaning "priest, prayer, one who prays," derived from the root *bṛh-* meaning "to grow, expand, swell."  This relates to the concept of Brahman as the expanding, all-encompassing cosmic principle.
