holy: /ˈhoʊli/ (US) /ˈhəʊli/ (UK)| adj.|神圣的，圣洁的，虔敬的|dedicated or consecrated to God or a religious purpose; sacred.| The Holy Bible is a sacred text for Christians. 圣经是基督徒的圣典。| The monks lived a holy life dedicated to prayer.  僧侣们过着虔诚的祈祷生活。|近义词：sacred, divine, pious, religious|反义词：unholy, profane, secular|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hālig, from Proto-Germanic *hailagaz ("holy, healthy, whole"), from Proto-Indo-European *kailo- ("whole, uninjured, of good omen"). Cognate with Old Norse heilagr, Old High German heilag, Gothic 𐌷𐌰𐌹𐌻𐌰𐌲𐍃 (hailags). Related to whole. The semantic development from "whole, healthy" to "holy" is paralleled in several other Indo-European languages.
