ascidian: /əˈsɪdiən/ | n. | 海鞘 | A marine animal of the subphylum Urochordata, having a sac-like body and two siphons for taking in and expelling water.  Ascidians are filter feeders and are typically found attached to rocks or other hard surfaces. |  Example: The ascidian clung to the pier piling, filtering plankton from the water.  海鞘紧紧地附着在码头的桩子上，从水中过滤浮游生物。| Example: Researchers are studying the regenerative properties of certain ascidian species. 研究人员正在研究某些海鞘物种的再生特性。 |  Synonyms: sea squirt, tunicate | Antonyms: (No direct antonyms for a specific animal) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek "askidion,"  meaning "small bag or wineskin," diminutive of "askos," meaning "bag or wineskin."  This refers to the sac-like body of the animal.
