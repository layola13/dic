goth: /ɡɒθ/ (British) /ɡɑːθ/ (American) | n. adj. | 哥特人，哥特式的 | A member of a Germanic people who invaded and settled in the Roman Empire from the 3rd to the 5th centuries. Also, relating to or characteristic of the Goths or their language.  A person who follows the gothic subculture. |  例句：She's a goth. 她是一个哥特族。| 近义词：dark, gloomy, morbid | 反义词：cheerful, bright, preppy | 词性: 名词，形容词

goth: /ɡɒθ/ (British) /ɡɑːθ/ (American) | adj. | 哥特式的 | Relating to a style of rock music characterized by dark and morbid themes. | 例句：He likes goth music. 他喜欢哥特音乐。 | 近义词：darkwave, deathrock | 反义词：pop, mainstream | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Old English *Gota (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *Gutōz.
