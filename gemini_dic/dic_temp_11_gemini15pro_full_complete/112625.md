low-key:/ˌloʊˈkiː/| adj. |低调的，不张扬的|Not intended to attract a lot of attention; understated. |例句：They had a low-key wedding ceremony with only close family and friends. 他们举行了一个低调的婚礼，只有亲朋好友参加。

例句：She wanted to keep the party low-key, so she only invited a few people. 她想让派对保持低调，所以只邀请了几个人。|近义词：understated, subtle, quiet, restrained|反义词：ostentatious, flamboyant, showy|词性:形容词

Etymology: From the lighting term "low-key," referring to a scene lit with a single, dim light source, creating a shadowy and subdued effect.  This sense of being understated and not drawing attention was then applied figuratively to other contexts.
