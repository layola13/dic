mystic:/ˈmɪstɪk/| adj. n.|神秘的；神秘主义者| Relating to mystics or mysticism; inspiring a sense of mystery and wonder.|例句：The mystic rituals of the ancient tribe were fascinating.  古代部落的神秘仪式令人着迷。|近义词：spiritual, esoteric, occult, enigmatic|反义词：mundane, ordinary, clear|词性:形容词

mystic:/ˈmɪstɪks/| n.|神秘主义者；神秘家|A person who seeks by contemplation and self-discipline to obtain consciousness of God or the ultimate realities.|例句：The mystics lived a life of prayer and contemplation.  这些神秘主义者过着祈祷和冥想的生活。|近义词：seer, visionary, spiritualist|反义词：materialist, rationalist|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mistik*, from Old French *mistique*, from Late Latin *mysticus*, from Greek *μυστικός (mystikós)*, meaning "secret, initiated," from *μύστης ( mystēs)* "initiate," from *μύω (myō)* "I close, initiate."  The original sense referred to secret religious rites.
