effectuations:/ɪˌfɛktʃuˈeɪʃənz/ | n. | 实现，实施，生效 | The process of bringing something into existence or operation; the act of carrying out or accomplishing something. |  The effectuation of the new policy will begin next month.  新政策的实施将于下个月开始。 | Synonyms:  realization, implementation, execution, accomplishment | Antonyms:  failure, inaction, neglect | noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *effectuationem, noun of action from past participle stem of effectuare "to effect," from Latin effectus, past participle of efficere "to work out, accomplish," from ex- "out" + facere "to make, do."
