dreadlocks: /ˈdredˌlɒks/ (UK) /ˈdredˌlɑːks/ (US) | n. |  dreadlocks; 脏辫 |  A hairstyle in which the hair is washed infrequently and allowed to mat together in long, rope-like strands.  | Example: She wore her dreadlocks piled high on her head. 她把她的脏辫高高地盘在头上。 | Example: He spent hours meticulously maintaining his dreadlocks. 他花了几个小时精心打理他的脏辫。 | Synonym: locs, braids (loosely) | Antonym:  straight hair,  loose curls | noun

dreadlockses:  This is not a standard English word.  The plural of "dreadlocks" is simply "dreadlocks."  The suffix "-es" is not used.
