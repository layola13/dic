enticed:/ɪnˈtaɪst/ |v. |诱惑；吸引；怂恿|To attract someone to a particular place or activity by offering something pleasant or advantageous.|The aroma of freshly baked bread enticed me into the bakery.  新鲜出炉的面包的香味把我吸引进了面包店。|Synonyms: tempt, lure, allure, attract, seduce |Antonyms: repel, deter, discourage|verb

enticed:/ɪnˈtaɪst/ |adj. |被诱惑的 (This is less common and usually used in a passive sense, implying the state of being enticed.)| Feeling a strong desire to do something, usually because someone has offered you something pleasant or advantageous. |He was enticed by the promise of a large reward. 他被巨额赏金的承诺所诱惑。 |Synonyms: tempted, lured, allured |Antonyms: uninterested, indifferent|adjective
