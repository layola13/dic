goose-steps:/ˈɡuːs stɛps/ | n. | 正步，鹅步 | A marching step in which the legs are swung high and kept straight.  Characterized by an exaggeratedly stiff-legged, high-stepping march, often associated with military parades and totalitarian regimes. | The soldiers performed goose-steps in the parade. 士兵们在阅兵式上走了正步。| The soldiers goose-stepped across the square. 士兵们正步走过广场。 | synonyms:  lockstep, parade march, stiff march | antonyms: casual walk, stroll | noun


goose-step:/ˈɡuːs stɛp/ | v. | 走正步 | To march with stiff legs and high steps. | The soldiers goose-stepped past the reviewing stand. 士兵们正步走过检阅台。 | The troops were ordered to goose-step. 部队被命令走正步。 | synonyms: march stiffly, parade | antonyms: stroll, saunter | verb
