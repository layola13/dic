impersonate:/ɪmˈpɜːrsəneɪt/ | v. | 扮演；模仿；假冒 | To assume the character or appearance of; pretend to be. | He was arrested for impersonating a police officer. 他因假冒警察而被捕。

impersonate:/ɪmˈpɜːrsəneɪt/ | v. | 扮演；模仿；假冒 | To mimic (a person or manner) for satirical effect. | She impersonated her teacher, much to the amusement of the class. 她模仿她的老师，逗得全班哈哈大笑。

impersonation:/ɪmˌpɜːrsəˈneɪʃn/ | n. | 扮演；模仿；冒充 | The act of impersonating someone or something. | His impersonation of the president was hilarious. 他对总统的模仿滑稽极了。

impersonator:/ɪmˈpɜːrsəneɪtər/ | n. | 模仿者；演员；假冒者 | A person who impersonates someone or something. | He's a talented impersonator who can mimic dozens of celebrities. 他是一位才华横溢的模仿者，可以模仿几十位名人。
