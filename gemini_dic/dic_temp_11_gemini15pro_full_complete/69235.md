ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *fesable*, from Old French *faisable* "that can be done", from *faire* "to do" (from Latin *facere*) + *-able*. The ending *-ity* comes from Old French *-ité* (from Latin *-itatem*). The apostrophe + s indicates the possessive form of "feasibility".
