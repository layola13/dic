dampen:/ˈdæmpən/ (US), /ˈdæmpən/ (UK)| v. |使潮湿；抑制；减弱；使沮丧|To make something slightly wet. To make something less strong or intense. |例句：The rain dampened the ground. 雨水使地面潮湿。


例句：The bad news dampened his spirits.  坏消息使他沮丧。


近义词：damp, moisten, stifle, suppress, restrain, curb, moderate, diminish, reduce, weaken|反义词:dry, encourage, intensify, strengthen|词性:动词


dampening:/ˈdæmpənɪŋ/ (US), /ˈdæmpənɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |抑制；减幅；阻尼|The act of making something damp, less strong, or less intense.  Also refers to the reduction of oscillations or vibrations, often through friction or resistance.|例句：The dampening of the vibrations reduced the noise.  振动阻尼降低了噪音。


例句：The dampening effect of the news on the market was immediate.  这消息对市场的抑制作用是立竿见影的。


近义词：damping, suppression, reduction, moderation|反义词:intensification, amplification|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From damp + -en.  The word "damp" itself comes from Middle English dampe, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Old English *damp, "vapor."
