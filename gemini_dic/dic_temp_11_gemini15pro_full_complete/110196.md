life:/laɪf/| n. |生命，生活；一生；生物；活力，生气|The condition that distinguishes animals and plants from inorganic matter, including the capacity for growth, reproduction, functional activity, and continual change preceding death.  |例句：Life is precious. 生命是宝贵的。|例句：He dedicated his life to science. 他毕生致力于科学。|近义词：existence, being, survival|反义词：death, demise|词性:noun

life:/laɪf/| adj. |一生的；终身的；生命的|Relating to or characteristic of life; lifelong. |例句：He received a life sentence. 他被判处无期徒刑。|例句：It was a life-changing experience. 这是一次改变人生的经历。|近义词：lifelong, lasting|反义词：temporary, short-lived|词性:adjective

词源:
From Middle English lif, from Old English līf (“life, lifetime; a living being; way of life, condition”), from Proto-Germanic *lībam (“life, body”), from Proto-Indo-European *leyp- (“to remain, adhere, stick”). Cognate with Scots life (“life”), West Frisian libben (“life”), Dutch leven (“life”), German Leben (“life”), Danish liv (“life”), Swedish liv (“life”), Icelandic líf (“life”). Related also to Old English libban (“to live”).
