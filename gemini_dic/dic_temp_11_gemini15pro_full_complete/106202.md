ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English knolle, from Old English cnol (“hilltop, summit, top, knoll”), from Proto-Germanic *knūllaz (“clod, lump, ball”), from Proto-Indo-European *genw- (“to swell, to bend”). Cognate with Dutch knol (“tuber, bulb”), German Knolle (“tuber, lump, clod”), dialectal Swedish knall (“rock, cliff”), Icelandic knollur (“hilltop”).
