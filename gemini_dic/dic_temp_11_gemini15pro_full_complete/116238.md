masques:/mɑːsks/ | n. |面具；化装舞会|A plural form of masque, referring to more than one mask used for disguise or in theatrical performances.  Masques were especially popular during the Renaissance and Baroque periods.|The courtiers entertained themselves with elaborate masques and balls.  宫廷大臣们用精心制作的面具和舞会来娱乐自己。

The theatre company created stunning masques for their production of Romeo and Juliet.  这家剧团为他们制作的《罗密欧与朱丽叶》创作了令人惊叹的面具。 |Near Synonyms: masks, disguises, veils|Antonyms: faces, identities|Noun
