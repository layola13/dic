glorified:/ˈɡlɔːrɪfaɪd/| adj.|美化的；吹嘘的；赞美的；荣耀的|Made to seem more important or better than it really is, often when this is not true.  Elevated to a higher status or position, often undeservedly.|He has a glorified job as a clerk. 他有一个被美化了的职员工作。|近义词：exalted, aggrandized, romanticized, idealized|反义词：degraded, diminished, belittled|词性:形容词
词源: From the past participle of glorify, from Late Latin glorificare "to glorify," from Latin gloria "glory" + -ficare,  combining form of facere "to make."
