epidictical: /ˌɛpɪˈdɪktɪk(ə)l/
adjective
说教的；夸耀的；表演的
Intended to display eloquence or to win applause; demonstrative.  Relating to or characteristic of a formal speech delivered on a special occasion, designed primarily to display the speaker's rhetorical skill and to inspire or entertain the audience.
Example: His commencement speech was highly epidictical, filled with soaring rhetoric and inspiring anecdotes. 他的毕业典礼演讲非常具有说教性，充满了高昂的修辞和鼓舞人心的轶事。

Example: The politician's epidictical address focused more on emotional appeals than on concrete policy proposals.  这位政治家的说教性讲话更多地集中在情感诉求上，而不是具体的政策建议。

近义词: demonstrative, rhetorical, oratorical, declamatory
反义词: plain, simple, unadorned, informal
词性: adjective

词源: From Greek *epideiktikos* "fit for display," from *epideiknynai* "to show off, exhibit," from *epi-* "upon, forth" + *deiknynai* "to show."
