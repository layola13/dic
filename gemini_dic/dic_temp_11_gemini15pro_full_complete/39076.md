confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/ (US), /kənˈfaʊnd/ (UK)| v.|使困惑；使惊讶；挫败；混淆|To cause surprise or confusion in (someone), especially by acting against their expectations. To prove (a theory, expectation, or prediction) wrong.|Her sudden recovery confounded the doctors. 她突然康复，使医生们感到困惑。|Synonyms: amaze, astonish, baffle, bewilder, confuse, disconcert, dumbfound, flummox, nonplus, perplex, puzzle|Antonyms: clarify, enlighten, explain|verb

confounded:/kənˈfaʊndɪd/ (US), /kənˈfaʊndɪd/ (UK)| adj.|困惑的；惊讶的；讨厌的|Used to express anger or annoyance.|I can't find my confounded keys! 我找不到我那该死的钥匙了！|Synonyms: annoyed, irritated, vexed|Antonyms: pleased, delighted|adjective
