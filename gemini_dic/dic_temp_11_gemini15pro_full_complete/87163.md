harborough:/ˈhɑːrbərə/ |n.| 港口；避難所；海港|A place on the coast where ships may moor in shelter, especially one protected from rough water by piers, jetties, and other artificial structures.|The ships sailed into the harborough at dawn. 船只在黎明时分驶入港口。|Synonyms: port, haven, harbor, anchorage|Antonyms: open sea|noun


harbour:/ˈhɑːrbər/ |n. vt.| 港口，避難所；窩藏；懷有|A place on the coast where ships may moor in shelter; to give shelter or refuge to.|The police suspected that she was harbouring an escaped convict. 警方怀疑她窝藏了一名逃犯。|Synonyms: port, haven, shelter, conceal|Antonyms: expose, evict|noun, verb
