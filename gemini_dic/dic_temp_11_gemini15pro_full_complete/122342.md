ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mixen*, from Old English *meoxen*, dative of *meox* (“dung, manure”), from Proto-Germanic *mikstaz (“mixed, dung”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)mik- (“to mix”). Cognate with Dutch *mest* (“manure”), German *Mist* (“manure”), Old Norse *myki* (“dung”).
