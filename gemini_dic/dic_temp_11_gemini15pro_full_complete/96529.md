indolency:/ˈɪndələnsɪ/ /ˈɪndələnsɪ/| n. |懒惰，惰性|The state of being indolent; aversion to labor or exertion; laziness; idleness.|His indolency prevented him from achieving his goals. 他的懒惰使他无法实现目标。|近义词：laziness, idleness, sloth, inertia|反义词：diligence, industry, activity|词性: noun

Etymology:
From French *indolence*, from Latin *indolentia* "freedom from pain; insensibility," figuratively "indifference, apathy," from *in- "not" (from PIE root *en* "in") + *dolens* (genitive *dolentis*), present participle of *dolēre* "to feel pain," of uncertain origin.
