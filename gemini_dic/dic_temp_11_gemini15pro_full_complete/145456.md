overcivilization: /ˌoʊvərˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 过度文明 | The state of a society believed to be suffering from the negative effects of having become too civilized, often characterized by excessive complexity, artificiality, and a loss of connection with nature.  |  Modern urban life can sometimes feel like overcivilization, with its constant noise and demands. 现代都市生活有时会让人感觉过度文明，充满持续的噪音和各种要求。 |  Overcivilization has led to a decline in traditional skills and crafts. 过度文明导致了传统技能和工艺的衰落。 | 近义词: decadence, sophistication | 反义词: primitiveness, simplicity | 词性: noun


Etymology: "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "civilization" (from French "civilisation," ultimately from Latin "civis" meaning "citizen").
