merrys:/ˈmɛriz/ | n. | 欢乐, 快乐 (古语，现已少用) |  An archaic or dialectal word meaning "mirth" or "gaiety."  Derived from Middle English *meryes*, plural of *merye* (merry). The modern plural is "merries." |  The hall echoed with the sound of merrys.  大厅里回荡着欢乐的声音。(例句较生硬，因为该词已少用) | 近义词: mirth, gaiety, jollity, glee | 反义词: sadness, gloom, melancholy | 词性: 名词


词源:

From Middle English *meryes*, plural of *merye*, from Old English *myrġe* (“pleasant, sweet, delightful, merry, joyful, agreeable”), from Proto-Germanic *murgijaz (“short, brief, pleasant”), from Proto-Indo-European *mréǵʰu- (“short, small”). Cognate with Old Frisian *merī*, *mergī* (“agreeable, sweet”), Old Saxon *murgio* (“pleasant, agreeable”), Middle Dutch *merghe* (“marrow, pith”), Old High German *murgi*, *murwi* (“short, brief”), German *mürbe* (“tender, mellow”), Gothic *𝗺𝗮𝘂𝗿𝗴𝗷𝗮𝗻* (maurgjan, “to shorten, abridge”).
