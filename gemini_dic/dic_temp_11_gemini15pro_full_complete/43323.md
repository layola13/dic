crammed:/kræmd/| adj.|塞满的，拥挤的，（时间）紧迫的|Filled to capacity; packed tightly; having studied intensively over a short period.|The bus was crammed with passengers. 公共汽车里挤满了乘客。|Synonyms: packed, crowded, jammed, stuffed, full|Antonyms: empty, spacious, deserted|adjective

crammed:/kræmd/| v. (past participle of cram)|塞满，填塞，（为考试）临时死记硬背|Past participle of the verb "cram," meaning to force something into a small space or to study intensively over a short period.|He crammed all his clothes into the suitcase. 他把所有的衣服都塞进了手提箱。|Synonyms: stuff, pack, jam, force|Antonyms: empty, unpack, remove|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *crammian* meaning "to stuff, press," of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old Norse *kram* "goods, wares."
