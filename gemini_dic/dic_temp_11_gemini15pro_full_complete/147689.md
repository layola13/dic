overpracticed:/ˌoʊvərˈpræktɪst/| adj.|过度练习的，练习过度的|Having practiced excessively, often to the point of diminishing returns or negative effects.  The pianist's performance was stiff and mechanical, suggesting an overpracticed piece.|The gymnast's overpracticed routine lacked spontaneity. 体操运动员过度练习的套路缺乏自发性。|overtrained, overrehearsed|underpracticed|adjective
ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + practiced, the past participle of practice.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much."  "Practice" comes from the Middle French "practiquer" and ultimately from the Late Latin "practicare," meaning "to perform, to do."
