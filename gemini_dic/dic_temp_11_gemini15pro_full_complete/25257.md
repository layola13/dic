buffering:/ˈbʌfərɪŋ/ /ˈbʌfərɪŋ/| n. |缓冲，缓存|The process of temporarily storing data in a buffer, typically to compensate for differences in the rate of data flow or to avoid interruptions.|The video kept buffering, making it impossible to watch. 视频一直在缓冲，无法观看。|synonyms: caching, loading, preloading|antonyms: N/A|noun
buffer:/ˈbʌfər/ /ˈbʌfər/| n. v. |缓冲器，缓冲，缓冲剂|1. (n.) a person or thing that prevents something unpleasant or harmful from happening. 2. (v.) to lessen or moderate the impact of something.|1. (n.) He served as a buffer between the two warring factions. 他在两个交战派别之间充当缓冲器。 2. (v.)  The government is trying to buffer the economy against a recession. 政府正在努力缓冲经济衰退的影响。|synonyms: cushion, shield, protect (v.)|antonyms: worsen, aggravate (v.)|noun, verb
