colonizationist: /ˌkɑːlənɪˈzeɪʃənɪst/ /ˌkɒlənɪˈzeɪʃənɪst/
n.|殖民主义者|A person who supports or participates in colonization, the policy or practice of acquiring full or partial political control over another country, occupying it with settlers, and exploiting it economically.|The colonizationist believed in the superiority of their culture and sought to impose it on the indigenous population.|近义词: colonizer, imperialist, settler|反义词: anti-colonialist, native, indigenous|词性: 名词

colonizationist: /ˌkɑːlənɪˈzeɪʃənɪst/ /ˌkɒlənɪˈzeɪʃənɪst/
adj.|殖民主义的|Relating to or characteristic of colonization or colonizationists.|The colonizationist policies of the 19th century had a devastating impact on many indigenous populations.|近义词: colonial, imperialistic|反义词: anti-colonial, indigenous|词性: 形容词
