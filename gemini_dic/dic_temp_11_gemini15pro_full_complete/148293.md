oversoften:/ˌoʊvərˈsɒfən/ /ˌoʊvərˈsɑːfən/| v. |过度软化；过度弱化|To make a sound softer than it normally is, especially in pronunciation.  This often involves changing a plosive sound (like /p/, /t/, /k/) to a fricative or approximant (like /f/, /s/, /θ/, /ð/, /x/, /h/, /w/, /j/).|Many German dialects oversoften the 'k' sound to a 'ch' sound. 许多德语方言会把“k”音过度软化成“ch”音。|synonyms: lenite, weaken|antonyms: harden, strengthen|verb

oversoftening:/ˌoʊvərˈsɒfənɪŋ/ /ˌoʊvərˈsɑːfənɪŋ/ | n. |过度软化；过度弱化|The process of making a sound softer than it normally is.|The oversoftening of consonants is a common feature of this dialect. 辅音的过度软化是这种方言的一个共同特征。|synonyms: lenition, weakening|antonyms: hardening, strengthening|noun
