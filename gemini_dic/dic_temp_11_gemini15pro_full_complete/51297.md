detachment:/dɪˈtætʃmənt/| n.|分离，超然，冷漠，分遣队|a state of being objective or aloof.|例句：He spoke with detachment about the accident. 他以一种超然的态度谈论那场事故。

例句：She felt a sense of detachment from the world around her. 她感到与周围的世界格格不入。|近义词：indifference, aloofness, separation, objectivity, impartiality|反义词：involvement, attachment, engagement, passion|词性:noun


detachment:/dɪˈtætʃmənt/| n.|分遣队|a group of soldiers, vehicles, etc. sent away from the main group on a special mission.|例句：A detachment of soldiers was sent to guard the bridge.  一支分遣队被派去守卫桥梁。

例句：The detachment consisted of two tanks and a platoon of infantry.  这支分遣队由两辆坦克和一个步兵排组成。|近义词：unit, squad, troop|反义词：main body, whole|词性:noun

词源:
From Old French "detachement", from "detachier"  "to detach," from "des-" (expressing reversal) + "attachier" "to attach." Ultimately from Frankish "*stakjan" "to stick, fix."
