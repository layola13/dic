dozily:/ˈdoʊzɪli/| adv.|懒洋洋地，昏昏沉沉地|In a lazy, sleepy manner. Characterized by inactivity and lack of alertness.|She dozily stretched and yawned, reluctant to get out of bed. 她懒洋洋地伸了个懒腰，打了个哈欠，不愿起床。|Synonyms: sleepily, drowsily, sluggishly, lethargically|Antonyms: energetically, actively, alertly|词性:副词

doze:/doʊz/| v. n.|打瞌睡，小睡|To sleep lightly and briefly, often unintentionally. A short, light sleep.|The old man dozed in his armchair by the fire. 老人坐在火炉旁的扶手椅上打瞌睡。|Synonyms: nap, slumber, drowse|Antonyms: awaken, stay awake|词性:动词/名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From doze (v.) + -ly (adv. suffix).  The verb "doze" comes from Old English *dūsian "become stupefied, be dazed," related to Old High German tusen "be still, be silent."
