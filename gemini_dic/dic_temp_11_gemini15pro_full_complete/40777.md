convincingly:/kənˈvɪnsɪŋli/ | adv. | 令人信服地，有说服力地 | in a way that makes someone believe that something is true | He argued convincingly that the project was viable. 他令人信服地论证了该项目是可行的。 | persuasively, cogently, effectively | unconvincingly, implausibly | adverb

convince:/kənˈvɪns/ | vt. | 使确信，使信服 | persuade (someone) that something is true | I managed to convince him that the investment was sound. 我设法使他相信这项投资是可靠的。| persuade, assure, satisfy | dissuade, deter | verb

Etymology: From Late Middle English *convincen, from Anglo-Norman convincre, from Old French convaincre and Latin convincō (“I overcome, convict, prove wrong, refute”), from com- (“together, with”) + vincō (“I conquer, overcome”).
