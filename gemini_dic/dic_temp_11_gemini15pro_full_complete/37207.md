comet:/ˈkɒmɪt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːmɪt/ (US)| n. |彗星|A celestial object consisting of a nucleus of ice and dust and, when near the sun, a “tail” of gas and dust particles pointing away from the sun.|Halley's Comet is visible from Earth every 76 years. 哈雷彗星每76年从地球上可见。|Synonym: blazing star|Antonym: N/A|noun

comet:/ˈkɒmɪt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːmɪt/ (US)| n. |(喻)突然出现并引起轰动的人(或物)|Someone or something that appears suddenly and attracts a lot of attention, often disappearing again quickly. |The new band was a comet, blazing brightly for a short time and then fading away.  这支新乐队就像一颗彗星，短暂地闪耀了一下就消失了。|Synonym: meteor, phenomenon|Antonym: N/A|noun
