adjudicatory:/əˈdʒuːdɪkətɔːri/ | adj. | 审判的；裁决的 | Relating to the process of making a formal judgment or decision, especially in a legal or administrative context. | The adjudicatory hearing will determine the defendant's guilt or innocence. 听证会将裁定被告是否有罪。|  The adjudicatory body reviewed the evidence and made a decision. 裁决机构审查了证据并做出了决定。 |Synonyms: judicial, legal, jurisdictional | Antonyms: non-judicial, informal | adjective

adjudicate:/əˈdʒuːdɪkeɪt/ | v. | 裁决；判决 | To make a formal judgment or decision about a dispute or problem. | The judge will adjudicate the case next week. 法官将于下周审理此案。 |  The panel adjudicated the dispute between the two companies.  专家组裁决了两家公司之间的争议。 | Synonyms: judge, decide, arbitrate, determine, rule | Antonyms: mediate, conciliate | verb

adjudication:/əˌdʒuːdɪˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 裁决；判决 | The act of making a formal judgment or decision. | The adjudication of the case took several months.  该案件的审理耗时数月。 | The adjudication resulted in a guilty verdict. 裁决结果是有罪判决。 | Synonyms: judgment, decision, ruling, determination, decree | Antonyms: negotiation, mediation | noun
