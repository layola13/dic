nonsobernesses:/nɒnˈsoʊbərnəsɪz/| n. |非清醒状态；醉酒状态（复数）|The plural form of nonsoberness, referring to multiple instances or occurrences of a lack of sobriety.  This could refer to repeated episodes of intoxication or a general state of being frequently not sober. |His frequent nonsobernesses were a concern for his family. 他的频繁醉酒让家人很担心。|近义词：intoxications, drunkennesses|反义词：sobriety, temperance|词性:名词

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  Meaning "not," from Latin.
sober: From Old French sobre, from Latin sobrius, meaning “temperate, moderate, sensible, calm, serious.”
-ness (suffix):  Forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
