contester:/kənˈtɛstər/| n.| 竞争者，质疑者，抗辩者 |A person who contests something; someone who formally objects to or disputes something. |The contester argued that the election results were fraudulent. 这位质疑者认为选举结果是欺诈性的。|Synonyms: challenger, disputant, opponent, objector, litigant|Antonyms: supporter, advocate, proponent|noun

contested:/kənˈtɛstɪd/ | adj.| 有争议的，竞争激烈的 |Disputed or challenged; subject to contention or competition.| The election results were highly contested. 选举结果备受争议。|Synonyms: disputed, challenged, debatable, controversial|Antonyms: undisputed, unchallenged, agreed upon|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "contester" (to contend with, dispute, oppose, contest), from Latin "contestari" (to call to witness, invoke as witness, attest, prove, assert), from "com-" (together) + "testari" (to bear witness), from "testis" (witness).
