allomerism:/əˈlɒmərɪzəm/| n. |同质异晶|The existence of different crystalline forms of the same substance, or of two or more substances of similar chemical composition but different crystalline form.|Diamond and graphite are a classic example of allomerism, as they are both composed of pure carbon but have vastly different structures and properties.|近义词:polymorphism|反义词:isomorphism|词性:noun
