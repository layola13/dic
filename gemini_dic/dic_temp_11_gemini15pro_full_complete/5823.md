amateurishly: /ˌæməˈtɜːrɪʃli/ | adv. | 业余地；笨拙地 | In a manner characteristic of an amateur; lacking professional skill or expertise. | He played the piano amateurishly, hitting wrong notes frequently. 他弹钢琴很业余，经常弹错音符。|  She amateurishly attempted to fix the leaky faucet, making the problem worse. 她笨手笨脚地试图修理漏水的水龙头，结果反而使问题更糟。 | clumsily, ineptly, unskillfully | professionally, expertly, skillfully | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From amateur + -ish + -ly.  "Amateur" comes from the French "amateur," meaning "lover of," from the Latin "amator," also meaning "lover."  The suffix "-ish" forms adjectives meaning "somewhat like" or "having the quality of," while "-ly" forms adverbs.
