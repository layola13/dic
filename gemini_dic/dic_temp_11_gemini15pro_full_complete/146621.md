overhandicapping: /ˌoʊvərˈhændɪˌkæpɪŋ/ | n. | 过度让分，故意示弱 | The act of deliberately giving oneself a disadvantage in a competition or other situation, often as a way to avoid pressure or to make a victory seem more impressive. | He admitted to overhandicapping himself by staying up late the night before the match. 他承认比赛前一晚熬夜是故意让自己处于劣势。| 近义词: sandbagging, downplaying | 反义词: boasting, exaggerating | noun


overhandicapping: /ˌoʊvərˈhændɪˌkæpɪŋ/ | v. |  故意示弱，过度让分 |  To deliberately give oneself a disadvantage. | He overhandicapped himself by not practicing before the tournament. 他在比赛前没有练习，故意让自己处于劣势。 |近义词: sandbagging, downplaying | 反义词: boasting, exaggerating | verb
