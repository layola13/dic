overreprimands: /ˌoʊvərˈrɛprɪmændz/
| v. |过度责备，过分申斥| To reprimand excessively or too harshly. | The teacher overreprimanded the student for a minor infraction. 老师因为一件小事过度责备了这个学生。|  The manager overreprimands his employees for even small mistakes, creating a tense work environment.  经理甚至会因为小错误而过度责备他的员工，造成了紧张的工作氛围。 |  rebuke, scold, chastise, berate |  praise, commend, compliment | Verb
Etymology: "over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi) + "reprimand" (from French réprimander, from re- + primander "to take to task," from Latin premere "to press")
