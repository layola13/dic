hocus-pocus:/ˌhoʊkəsˈpoʊkəs/| n. |戏法，胡言乱语，欺骗|Meaningless words or actions used to deceive; sleight of hand; trickery. |The magician amazed the children with his hocus-pocus. 魔术师用他的戏法使孩子们惊叹不已。|Synonyms: trickery, deception, sleight of hand, magic, conjuring, wizardry|Antonyms: reality, truth, honesty|noun

hocus:/ˈhoʊkəs/| n. |戏法，胡言乱语|A form of conjuring or trickery. |He vanished in a puff of smoke, pure hocus. 他在一缕烟雾中消失了，纯粹是戏法。|Synonyms: trickery, deception, sleight of hand, magic, conjuring|Antonyms: reality, truth|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From pseudo-Latin, perhaps an alteration of *hoc est corpus meum ("this is my body"), the words spoken by a priest during the Eucharist. The phrase was used mockingly to refer to meaningless ritualistic words.
