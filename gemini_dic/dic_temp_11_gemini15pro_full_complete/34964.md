cloddish:/ˈklɒdɪʃ/ (US) /ˈklɑːdɪʃ/ (UK)| adj.|笨拙的，愚钝的，粗俗的|Lacking grace, refinement, or sensitivity; awkward and clumsy in behavior or appearance; stupid or insensitive.|He made a cloddish remark about her weight. 他对她体重说了一句很粗俗的话。|近义词：awkward, clumsy, gauche, oafish, boorish|反义词：graceful, elegant, refined, sophisticated|词性:形容词

clod:/klɒd/ (US) /klɒd/ (UK)| n.|土块，笨蛋|A lump of earth or clay; a stupid person.|He's a bit of a clod. 他有点笨。|近义词：lump, chunk, dolt, idiot|反义词：genius|词性:名词

词源: From clod + -ish.  The word "clod" comes from Old English *clod-, of Germanic origin, related to Dutch klod and German Klotz.
