misinform:/ˌmɪsɪnˈfɔːrm/ | vt. | 误导，提供错误信息 | To give someone wrong information about something. | The newspaper misinformed the public about the cause of the accident. 这家报纸误导了公众关于事故原因的报道。

The government deliberately misinformed the people about the severity of the crisis. 政府故意向人民隐瞒了危机的严重性。

| 近义词：deceive, mislead, misguide | 反义词：inform, enlighten, educate | 词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (meaning "wrong" or "badly") + inform (meaning "to give knowledge of something").  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old French "mes-" which ultimately derives from Latin "minus" meaning "less." "Inform" comes from Old French "enformer" from Latin "informare" meaning "to shape, train, instruct, educate."
