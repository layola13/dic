discombobulating:/ˌdɪskəmˈbɒbjuleɪtɪŋ/
adjective
令人困惑的，使人糊涂的
Causing someone to feel confused, upset, or disconcerted.
例句：The discombobulating news left her speechless. 令人困惑的消息让她 speechless。

例句：The sudden change in plans was quite discombobulating.  计划的突然改变令人十分困惑。

近义词：confusing, bewildering, unsettling, disconcerting, perturbing
反义词：calming, soothing, reassuring, comforting, clarifying
词性：adjective


discombobulate:/ˌdɪskəmˈbɒbjuleɪt/
verb
使困惑，使糊涂
To confuse or disconcert someone.
例句：The complex instructions completely discombobulated him. 复杂的指令让他完全糊涂了。

例句：I was discombobulated by the unexpected questions. 我被突如其来的问题弄糊涂了。


近义词：confuse, bewilder, unsettle, disconcert, perturb
反义词：calm, soothe, reassure, comfort, clarify
词性：verb


discombobulation:/ˌdɪskəmˌbɒbjuˈleɪʃn/
noun
困惑，糊涂
The state of being confused, upset, or disconcerted.
例句：He was in a state of utter discombobulation after the accident. 事故发生后，他处于极度困惑的状态。

例句：The new software caused widespread discombobulation among the staff. 新软件在员工中引起了普遍的困惑。

近义词：confusion, bewilderment, disorientation
反义词：clarity, composure, calmness
词性：noun
