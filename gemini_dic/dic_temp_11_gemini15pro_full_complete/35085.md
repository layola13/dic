clothe:/kloʊð/ (US) /kləʊð/ (UK)| v. |给…穿衣；覆盖|To dress someone, especially in particular clothes; to cover something.|She clothed her children in warm coats for the winter. 她给孩子们穿上暖和的外套过冬。|dress, attire, garb|undress, uncover|verb

clothe:/kloʊð/ (US) /kləʊð/ (UK)| v. |赋予，披上（某种特质）|To provide something with a particular quality.|The mountains were clothed in mist. 群山笼罩在薄雾中。|invest, endow, imbue|divest, strip|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *clāþian, from Proto-Germanic *klāþijaną, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)klei- (“to stick, adhere”). Cognate with Dutch kleden, German kleiden, Old Norse klæða. Related to cloth.
