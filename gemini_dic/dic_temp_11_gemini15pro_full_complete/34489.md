clays: /kleɪz/
noun
粘土; 泥土
a stiff, sticky fine-grained earth, typically yellow, red, or bluish-gray in color and often forming an impermeable layer in the soil. It can be molded when wet, and is dried and fired to make bricks, pottery, and ceramics.
The potter shaped the clay on the wheel. 陶艺家在轮子上塑造粘土。

近义词: mud, earth, soil
反义词:  (No direct antonym for a physical substance like clay)


词性: noun (plural of clay)
词源: From Old English *clæg (attested in compounds), from Proto-Germanic *klajjaz, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kley- (“to stick, smear”). Cognate with Middle Low German klei, Dutch klei, German Klei, Old Norse leir.
