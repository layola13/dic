imaginableness:/ɪˈmædʒɪnəbəlnəs/
|n.|可想象性，可想象的程度|The imaginableness of the scenario made it all the more terrifying.  这个场景的可想象性使它更加可怕。|
|Etymology: From imaginable + -ness.  "Imaginable" comes from the Late Middle English "imaginable," from Old French "imaginable," ultimately from Latin "imaginari" (to imagine). The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.|
|Synonyms: conceivability, thinkableness|
|Antonyms: unconceivability, unthinkableness|
|Word type: Noun|
