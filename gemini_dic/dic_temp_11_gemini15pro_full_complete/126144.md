mutinies:/ˈmjuːtɪniz/ | n. |叛乱，哗变|An open rebellion against constituted authority, especially by soldiers or sailors against their officers. |The captain was killed during the mutinies. 船长在叛乱中被杀。|Synonyms: rebellions, uprisings, revolts |Antonyms: obedience, submission | Noun

mutiny:/ˈmjuːtɪni/ | n. |叛乱，哗变|An open rebellion against constituted authority, especially by soldiers or sailors against their officers. |The crew staged a mutiny against the cruel captain.  船员发动了反对残暴船长的叛乱。|Synonyms: rebellion, uprising, revolt |Antonyms: obedience, submission | Noun

mutiny:/ˈmjuːtɪni/ | v. |叛乱，哗变|To engage in mutiny; revolt. |The soldiers mutinied against their commanding officer. 士兵们反抗他们的指挥官。|Synonyms: rebel, revolt, rise up |Antonyms: obey, submit | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mutinie (modern French mutinerie), from mutin ("mutinous"), from Italian mutino ("rebellious"), possibly from Middle Dutch muiten ("to mutiny").  Ultimately of uncertain origin.
