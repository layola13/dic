diplomas:/dɪˈploʊməz/ (US), /dɪˈpləʊməz/ (UK)
n.|文凭，毕业证书|A certificate awarded by an educational establishment to show that someone has successfully completed a course of study.|She received her diploma in nursing last year. 她去年获得了护理文凭。|certificate, degree, qualification|  (No direct antonym, consider lack of diploma/certification)|noun
diplomas:/dɪˈploʊməz/ (US), /dɪˈpləʊməz/ (UK)
n.|执照，许可证|(less common) A document conferring some privilege, honor, or power.|He presented his diplomat's diploma to the ambassador. 他向大使递交了他的外交执照。|license, permit, authorization| (No direct antonym, consider revocation of license/permit) |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin diploma, from Greek δίπλωμα (díplōma, “folded paper, letter”), from the verb διπλόω (diplóō, “I double, fold”), from διπλόος (diplóos, “double”).  The original diplomas were official documents written on two folded tablets.
