chambers:/ˈtʃeɪmbərz/ /ˈtʃeɪmbər/ | n. |房间，室；腔；会所；议院；(尤指律师的)办公室|A room used for a particular purpose, especially by a judge or group of judges, or by lawyers or politicians. |The judge retired to his chambers to consider the verdict.  法官退到自己的办公室去考虑判决。| synonyms: room, compartment, chamber, office, hall | antonyms: exterior, outside | noun

chambers:/ˈtʃeɪmbərz/ /ˈtʃeɪmbər/ | n. |(枪膛内的)弹膛；药室|(the part of a gun where the bullet is loaded) |He loaded the chambers of the revolver. 他给左轮手枪装上子弹。| synonyms: cartridge, magazine, cylinder | antonyms:  N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *chambre*, from Latin camera "vault, arched roof". Ultimately from Greek καμάρα (kamára) "vaulted chamber".
