hang-over:/ˈhæŋˌoʊvər/ (US) /ˈhæŋˌəʊvə(r)/ (UK)| n.|宿醉，残留物，遗留问题|A hang-over is the unpleasant physical effects following the heavy consumption of alcohol, such as a headache and nausea.  It can also refer to something (usually unpleasant) remaining from a past event or period.|I had a terrible hang-over after the party last night. 昨晚派对后我宿醉得厉害。|The economic hang-over from the recession is still being felt. 经济衰退的遗留问题仍然存在。|近义词: after-effects, hangover (informal), repercussions|反义词:  (No direct antonym, consider using phrases like "feeling great," "full recovery")|词性:noun
