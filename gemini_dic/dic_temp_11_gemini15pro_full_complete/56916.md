dotcom:/ˈdɑːtˌkɑːm/ /ˈdɒtˌkɒm/ | n. |网络公司；互联网公司|A company that does most of its business on the internet. |  Example 1: Many dotcoms went bankrupt during the early 2000s. 许多网络公司在21世纪初破产了。

Example 2:  He invested heavily in several dotcom startups. 他在几家互联网初创公司投入了巨资。| 近义词: internet company, online business, e-business | 反义词: brick-and-mortar store, offline business | 词性: 名词

dot-com:/ˈdɑːtˌkɑːm/ /ˈdɒtˌkɒm/ | adj. |互联网的；网络公司的 | Relating to or characteristic of a dotcom company. | Example 1: The dotcom boom of the late 1990s created many millionaires. 20世纪90年代末的互联网热潮造就了许多百万富翁。

Example 2: He made his fortune in the dotcom era. 他在互联网时代发了财。 | 近义词: internet, online | 反义词: offline, traditional | 词性: 形容词


词源:  A blend of "dot" (referring to the "." in a web address) and "com" (the top-level domain for commercial websites). 由“dot”（指网址中的“.”）和“com”（商业网站的顶级域名）混合而成。
