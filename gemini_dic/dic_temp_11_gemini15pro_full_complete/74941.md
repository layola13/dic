freudian slip:/ˈfrɔɪdiən slɪp/ | n. |  弗洛伊德式失言；口误；| An unintentional error in speech, memory, or physical action that is interpreted as occurring due to the interference of an unconscious subdued wish or internal train of thought.  It is named after Sigmund Freud, the founder of psychoanalysis. |  Example: He meant to say "I'm glad to meet your wife," but in a Freudian slip, he said, "I'm glad to meet your mother." 他本想说“很高兴见到你的妻子”，但由于弗洛伊德式口误，他说成了“很高兴见到你的母亲”。

Example:  She called her new boyfriend by her ex-boyfriend's name - a classic Freudian slip. 她用前男友的名字称呼了现任男友——一个典型的弗洛伊德式口误。 | 近义词: slip of the tongue, gaffe, blunder | 反义词:  (No direct antonym;  one might consider "intentional utterance/action") | 词性: noun
