coquimbite:/koʊˈkɪmbaɪt/| n. |水铁矾|A hydrous iron sulfate mineral, Fe₂(SO₄)₃·9H₂O, occurring as granular masses or hexagonal prisms, typically white to pale violet in color.  It is formed by the oxidation of pyrite and other sulfide minerals in arid or semi-arid environments.|Coquimbite forms as a crust on pyrite-rich rocks in dry climates.  水铁矾在干燥气候下形成于富含黄铁矿的岩石上。|Synonyms: none specifically, but other hydrated iron sulfates exist (e.g., melanterite, rozenite).|Antonyms: N/A (not applicable for a mineral name).|noun
