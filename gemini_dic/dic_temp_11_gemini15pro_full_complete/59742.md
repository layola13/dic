ecog:/ˈiːkɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈiːkɑːɡ/ (US)| n. |脑皮层电图|Electrocorticography (ECoG) is a type of electrophysiological monitoring that uses electrodes placed directly on the exposed surface of the brain to record electrical activity from the cerebral cortex. |The patient underwent an ECoG to identify the seizure focus.  病人接受了脑皮层电图检查以确定癫痫病灶。|The ECoG revealed abnormal brain activity in the temporal lobe. 脑皮层电图显示颞叶异常脑活动。|Synonyms: intracranial EEG (iEEG)|Antonyms: N/A|noun
