facsimiled:/ˈfækˈsɪmɪld/
v.|传真（过去式和过去分词）|A document or image that has been copied by a method (such as fax) that reproduces the exact form of the original. | He facsimiled the signed contract to the head office. 他把签好的合同传真到总部。|近义词：faxed, copied, reproduced|反义词：original|词性:动词

facsimile:/ˈfækˈsɪmɪli/
n.|摹本，复制品，传真|An exact copy or reproduction of something, such as a document, book, or painting. |The museum displayed a facsimile of the Declaration of Independence. 博物馆展出了《独立宣言》的复制品。|近义词：copy, replica, reproduction|反义词：original|词性:名词

facsimile:/ˈfækˈsɪmɪli/
adj.|摹写的，复制的|Made to look exactly like something else. |The art dealer was caught selling facsimile paintings.  那个艺术品经销商被抓到卖复制画。|近义词：copied, replicated, reproduced|反义词：original|词性:形容词
