out-group: /ˈaʊtˌɡruːp/ | n. | 外群体；圈外人 | A social group with which an individual does not identify. |  Example 1:  He felt like an out-group, excluded from the popular clique. 他感觉自己像个圈外人，被排除在受欢迎的小圈子之外。

Example 2:  The study examined how out-groups are perceived by members of the in-group. 这项研究调查了圈内人如何看待圈外人。 | 近义词: outsider, non-member | 反义词: in-group | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + group.  "Out-" is an adverb and preposition meaning "outside" or "away from." "Group" refers to a number of people or things that are located close together or are considered or classed together.  The term "out-group" gained prominence in social psychology.
