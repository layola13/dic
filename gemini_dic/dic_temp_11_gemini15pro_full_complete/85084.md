hardback:/ˈhɑːrdbæk/ (US) /ˈhɑːdbæk/ (UK)| n. adj.|精装本；精装的|A book with a stiff cover, typically made of cardboard covered with cloth, leather, or other material.|例句：I prefer hardback books because they are more durable. 我更喜欢精装书，因为它们更耐用。

例句：The hardback edition is more expensive than the paperback. 精装版比平装版更贵。|近义词：hardcover, casebound|反义词：paperback, softback, softcover|词性: 名词/形容词
词源: hard + back. 'Hard' refers to the stiff cover, and 'back' refers to the spine and covering of a book.  The term emerged in the late 19th century as a descriptive term for books bound with stiff covers, contrasting with earlier practices of flexible bindings or paperback formats.
