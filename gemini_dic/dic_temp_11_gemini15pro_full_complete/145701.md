overcount:/ˌoʊvərˈkaʊnt/ | v. | 多算；重复计算；过高估计 | To count something more times than appropriate, resulting in a higher total than the actual amount. | I overcounted the apples and thought I had more than I actually did. 我多算了苹果，以为比实际拥有的多。|  The cashier overcounted the money and gave me too much change. 收银员多算了钱，多找了我零钱。 | 近义词: overestimate, exaggerate | 反义词: undercount, underestimate | 词性: 动词

overcount:/ˈoʊvərˌkaʊnt/ | n. | 多算；重复计算；过高估计 | An amount or instance of counting something more times than appropriate, resulting in a higher total than the actual amount. | There was an overcount of the votes, and the election results had to be recalculated. 投票计数过多，选举结果必须重新计算。 | The inventory overcount led to a discrepancy in the stock records. 库存多算导致了库存记录的差异。 | 近义词: overestimation, exaggeration | 反义词: undercount, underestimation | 词性: 名词

词源: "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much") + "count" (verb meaning "to determine the number of")
