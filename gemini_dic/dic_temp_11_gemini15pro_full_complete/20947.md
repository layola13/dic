bleeding-edge:/ˈbliːdɪŋ ɛdʒ/| adj.|尖端的，最前沿的|Extremely modern and advanced; at the very forefront of technological development.|Example 1:  They are using bleeding-edge technology in their new smartphones. 他们正在他们的新智能手机中使用最先进的技术。

Example 2:  The company invests heavily in bleeding-edge research. 这家公司在最前沿的研究上投入巨资。|Synonyms: cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, advanced, leading-edge|Antonyms: outdated, obsolete, antiquated, old-fashioned|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->

From "bleeding" (referring to the idea of being so new it's still "raw" or "untested") + "edge" (referring to the forefront or cutting edge).  The term likely arose by analogy with "cutting-edge," emphasizing the even newer and potentially riskier nature of the technology.
