budding:/ˈbʌdɪŋ/| adj. n.| 萌芽的，发芽的；新兴的；发展中的；初露头角的 |Beginning to develop or show signs of future success in a particular area.  A small part of a plant that develops into a flower or leaf. |例句：She's a budding artist with a lot of potential. 她是一位很有潜力的新兴艺术家。|近义词：emerging, nascent, developing, burgeoning, sprouting |反义词：declining, waning, fading, mature, established |词性:形容词

budding:/ˈbʌdɪŋ/| n. | 发芽，萌芽 |The formation of buds. | 例句：The budding of the trees in spring is a beautiful sight. 春天树木发芽的景象很美。|近义词：sprouting, germination |反义词：wilting, withering |词性:名词

Etymology: From the noun "bud" + "-ing" suffix.  "Bud" comes from Middle English *budde*, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Low German *but*.
