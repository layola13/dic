incrustate:/ɪnˈkrʌsteɪt/ | vt. adj.| 镶嵌；包壳；覆盖 | To cover or decorate with a hard crust or layer; to embed something within a surface. |
The artist incrusted the box with jewels.  艺术家在盒子上镶嵌了珠宝。

The walls were incrusted with salt from the sea spray.  墙壁上覆盖着海浪溅起的盐。| 近义词：encrust, coat, cover, embed, inlay | 反义词: uncover, expose, strip | 词性: 动词, 形容词

incrustation: /ɪnˌkrʌˈsteɪʃən/ | n. | 镶嵌；包壳；覆盖物 | A hard crust or layer; something that covers or decorates a surface. |
The plumber removed the incrustation of limescale from the pipes.  水管工清除了管道上的石灰垢。

Over time, an incrustation of dirt and grime built up on the statue.  随着时间的推移，雕像上积聚了一层污垢和尘垢。| 近义词：coating, covering, crust, layer, deposit | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but consider "bare surface") | 词性: 名词

词源： From Latin "incrustare," from "in-" (in, on) + "crusta" (crust, shell).
