mercantilist:/ˈmɜːrkəntɪlɪst/ | n. adj. | 重商主义者；重商主义的 | A believer in mercantilism; relating to mercantilism.  Mercantilism is an economic theory and practice common in Europe from the 16th to the 18th century that promoted governmental regulation of a nation's economy for the purpose of augmenting state power at the expense of rival national powers. It was the economic counterpart of political absolutism. |  The mercantilist policies of the 17th century aimed to increase national wealth through exports. 17世纪的重商主义政策旨在通过出口增加国家财富。 |  The East India Company was a powerful mercantilist organization.  东印度公司是一个强大的重商主义组织。 | 近义词:protectionist, nationalist | 反义词:free trader, laissez-faire | 词性: 名词, 形容词 | 词源: From French *mercantiliste*, from Italian *mercantile* "merchant," from *mercante* "merchant," from Latin *mercari* "to trade, traffic, earn."
