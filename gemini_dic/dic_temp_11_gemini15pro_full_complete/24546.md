broadsheet:/ˈbrɔːdʃiːt/| n.|大报；单张印刷品|A newspaper printed on large sheets of paper, typically regarded as more serious and less sensationalist than tabloids.  A large sheet of paper printed on one side only, used for advertising or proclamations.|I prefer reading broadsheets to tabloids. 我更喜欢读大报而不是小报。|The town crier posted a broadsheet announcing the king's arrival.  传令官张贴了一张布告，宣布国王的到来。|近义词：newspaper, journal, daily|反义词：tabloid|词性:noun

Etymology:
From "broad" (wide) + "sheet" (of paper).  Originally referred to single sheets printed on one side, often used for proclamations or ballads.  Later came to refer to newspapers printed on large-size paper.
