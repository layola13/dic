intoxicated:/ɪnˈtɑːksɪkeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 喝醉的；陶醉的；极其兴奋的 | Affected by alcohol or drugs to the point of losing control over one's faculties;  filled with excitement and enthusiasm. | He was so intoxicated that he couldn't stand up straight. 他醉得站都站不稳了。|  The crowd was intoxicated by the team's victory.  人群为球队的胜利而兴奋不已。| 近义词：drunk, inebriated, exhilarated | 反义词：sober, clear-headed | 词性: 形容词

intoxicate:/ɪnˈtɑːksɪkeɪt/ | v. | 使喝醉；使陶醉；使极其兴奋 | To make someone drunk; to fill with excitement. | The music intoxicated the dancers. 音乐使舞者陶醉。| The victory intoxicated the fans. 胜利让球迷们欣喜若狂。 | 近义词： inebriate, exhilarate | 反义词： sober | 词性: 动词


词源: From Late Latin *intoxicatus, past participle of intoxicare "to poison," from Latin toxicum "poison" (see toxic).  Originally meaning "to poison," the sense shifted to "make drunk" in the 16th century.
