apartheid:/əˈpɑːrtaɪt/
|n.|种族隔离|A system of institutionalised racial segregation and discrimination that existed in South Africa and South West Africa (now Namibia) from 1948 until the early 1990s.|Nelson Mandela fought against apartheid in South Africa.  纳尔逊·曼德拉在南非与种族隔离作斗争。|Synonyms: segregation, discrimination, prejudice|Antonyms: integration, equality, acceptance|Noun

apartheids:/əˈpɑːrtaɪts/
|n.|种族隔离（复数，罕用）|Plural form of "apartheid," referring to multiple instances or systems of racial segregation.  This form is rarely used as the concept of apartheid is generally considered singular in its historical South African context, but theoretically could refer to similar systems elsewhere.|Historians might analyze the different apartheids that have existed throughout history. 历史学家可能会分析历史上存在的不同种族隔离制度。|Synonyms: segregations, discriminations (rarely used in this context)|Antonyms: integrations, equalities (rarely used in this context)|Noun
