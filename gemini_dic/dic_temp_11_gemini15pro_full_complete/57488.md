drag-on:/ˈdræɡɒn (UK) /ˈdræɡɑːn (US)| n.|龙; 拖累; 令人厌烦的人(或事)|A mythical monster like a giant reptile with wings and claws and often breathes out fire. Something that is very difficult or unpleasant to deal with. A person or thing that is annoying or causes problems.|例句：The film featured a fierce fire-breathing dragon. 这部电影以一条凶猛的喷火龙为主角。

例句：The project has become a real drag-on. 这个项目已经变成了一个真正的拖累。|近义词：monster, beast, nuisance, burden|反义词:blessing, asset|词性:名词


drag-on:/ˈdræɡ ɒn/| vi.|拖延; 缓慢费力地行进|To continue for longer than you want or think is necessary. To move slowly and with difficulty.|例句：The meeting dragged on for hours. 会议拖了好几个小时。

例句：She dragged herself out of bed, exhausted. 她疲惫不堪地从床上爬起来。|近义词：linger, continue, persist|反义词:expedite, hasten|词性:动词
