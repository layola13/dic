PHRASE->

(While less common, "congeal" can be used figuratively and thus form phrases like these:)

Fear congealed in his veins. 恐惧在他血管中凝结。（意思是极度恐惧）
His blood congealed with horror.  他吓得血液都凝固了。 (Meaning: He was terrified.)
Her smile congealed on her face. 她的笑容僵在了脸上。(Meaning: Her expression became forced and unnatural.)
The atmosphere in the room congealed. 房间里的气氛凝固了。(Meaning: The atmosphere became tense and awkward.)
His thoughts congealed into a plan. 他的想法凝结成一个计划。(Meaning: His thoughts became organized and formed a plan.)


词源：From Old French congeler, from Latin congelare "to freeze," from com- "together" + gelare "to freeze," related to gelu "frost."


注意事项： "Congealabilities" is a very rarely used plural form. The singular "congealability" is much more common.  The verb "congeal" is generally preferred in most contexts.
