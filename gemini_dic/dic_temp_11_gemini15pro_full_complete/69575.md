felony:/ˈfɛləni/| n. |重罪，重型罪|A felony is a serious crime, typically one involving violence, and is usually punishable by imprisonment for more than one year or by death.|He was convicted of a felony.|近义词：crime, offense, serious crime|反义词:misdemeanor, petty offense|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Anglo-French *felonie, from Old French felon, felun “traitor, rebel, villain, scoundrel,” from Medieval Latin fellonem (nominative fello) “evil-doer, scoundrel,” perhaps ultimately of Germanic origin (compare Old High German fillo “rogue”).
