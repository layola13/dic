draft:/drɑːft/ /dræft/| n. vt. adj.|草稿，汇票；征兵；通风； draught|A preliminary version of a piece of writing.|例句：The first draft of the novel was completed in three months. 这部小说的初稿三个月就完成了。

例句：She drafted a letter to her lawyer. 她起草了一封给律师的信。|近义词：sketch, outline, version, draught|反义词：final, finished|词性:名词,动词,形容词

draft:/drɑːft/ /dræft/| n. vt. adj.| draught；通风；气流|A current of cool air in a room or other enclosed space.|例句：She felt a cold draft and closed the window. 她感到一阵冷风，就把窗户关上了。

例句：The chimney created a strong draft. 烟囱产生了一股强劲的气流。|近义词：breeze, current|反义词：stuffiness|词性:名词

draft:/drɑːft/ /dræft/| n. vt. adj.| draught；(尤指啤酒的) 一饮|The act of drinking or inhaling.|例句：He took a long draft of beer. 他喝了一大口啤酒。

例句：She took a deep draft of fresh air. 她深深地吸了一口新鲜空气。|近义词：gulp, swig|反义词：sip|词性:名词

draft:/drɑːft/ /dræft/| n. vt. adj.|征兵，选秀|Compulsory recruitment for military service.|例句：He was drafted into the army during the war. 他在战争期间被征召入伍。

例句：The draft was reinstated during the Vietnam War.  在越南战争期间重新开始了征兵。|近义词：conscription|反义词：discharge|词性:名词,动词

draft:/drɑːft/ /dræft/| n. vt. adj.|汇票|A written order for payment of money.|例句：The company issued a draft for the amount owed.  公司开出了一张汇票来支付欠款。

例句：He presented the draft to the bank for payment. 他向银行提交了汇票以获得付款。|近义词：bill of exchange, check|反义词：deposit|词性:名词
