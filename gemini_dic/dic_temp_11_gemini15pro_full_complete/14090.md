autarch:/ˈɔːtɑːrk/| n.| 独裁者，专制君主|A ruler who has absolute power. A self-governing state or person.|例句：The autarch ruled his small island nation with an iron fist.  这位独裁者用铁腕统治着他小小的岛国。

例句：She declared herself autarch of the newly formed republic. 她宣布自己为新成立共和国的独裁者。|近义词：dictator, autocrat, despot, tyrant|反义词：democrat|词性: 名词

autarchy:/ˈɔːtɑːrki/| n.| 独裁统治；自给自足|Rule by a single person with unlimited power; the state of being self-sufficient.|例句：The country’s autarchy ended with the dictator's death.  这个国家的独裁统治随着独裁者的死亡而结束。

例句：The small community strived for autarchy, growing their own food and generating their own power. 这个小社区力求自给自足，自己种植粮食，自己发电。|近义词：absolutism, autocracy, self-sufficiency, independence|反义词：democracy, dependence|词性: 名词

autarchical:/ɔːˈtɑːrkɪkəl/| adj.| 独裁的；自给自足的|Relating to autarchy; self-governing; independent.|例句：The autarchical regime suppressed all dissent. 独裁政权压制了所有异议。

例句：Their autarchical lifestyle allowed them to live off the grid. 他们自给自足的生活方式使他们能够脱离电网生活。|近义词：autocratic, despotic, self-sufficient, independent|反义词：democratic, dependent|词性: 形容词


词源: From Greek *autarkēs* "self-sufficient, independent," from *autos* "self" + *arkein* "to suffice."
