goggle-box:/ˈɡɒɡl bɒks/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːɡl bɑːks/ (US)| n. |电视机|A television set.  It refers to the act of watching television, often implying excessive viewing.|例句：We spent the evening parked in front of the goggle-box. 我们一晚上都坐在电视机前。

例句：He's always glued to the goggle-box. 他总是目不转睛地盯着电视。|近义词：television, TV, telly (informal, UK), boob tube (informal, US)|反义词: None (no direct opposite, perhaps a book or outdoor activities)|词性:noun


goggle-box:/ˈɡɒɡl bɒks/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːɡl bɑːks/ (US)| n. |电视机|The term originated in the 1950s in Britain, possibly referencing the early bulky TV sets and the wide-eyed stare of viewers. |词源: From "goggle" (to stare with wide eyes) + "box" (referring to the television set).
