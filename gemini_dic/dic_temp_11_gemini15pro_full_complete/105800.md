kiss-off:/ˈkɪs ɒf/ (US), /ˈkɪs ɒf/ (UK)| n.| 拒绝; 解雇; 告别; 打发;| An abrupt dismissal or rejection.  A signal to end a transmission or communication.| He gave her a kiss-off and walked away. 他把她打发走然后离开了。|synonyms: brush-off, rejection, dismissal, send-off, farewell| antonyms: welcome, acceptance, hiring| noun

kiss-off:/ˈkɪs ɒf/ (US), /ˈkɪs ɒf/ (UK)| adj.|  (informal) serving as a final, often perfunctory, farewell; dismissive.|  A kiss-off letter. 一封告别信。|synonyms: final, parting, valedictory, dismissive| antonyms: welcoming, introductory| adjective
