bosom:/ˈbʊzəm/ | n. adj. | 胸部，胸怀；内心；中间 |  The part of the human body between the neck and the abdomen, especially the breasts of a woman; the chest considered as the seat of emotions and feelings; a close or intimate relationship. | She held the child to her bosom. 她把孩子抱在怀里。 | 近义词：chest, breast, heart, embrace | 反义词：back | 词性: 名词, 形容词


bosom:/ˈbʊzəm/ | adj. | 亲密的，知心的 | Closely associated or intimate. |  They were bosom friends. 他们是知心朋友。| 近义词：close, intimate, dear | 反义词：distant, estranged | 词性: 形容词


词源:  From Old English *bōsm, from Proto-Germanic *bōsmaz ("breast, lap, bosom"). Cognate with Old Frisian bōsem, Old Saxon bōsm, Old High German buosam, German Busen, Old Norse bōss ("udder, teats of a cow"), Gothic 𐌱𐌰𐌿𐍃𐌰𐌽𐍃 (baúsans, "breast").
