caricature: /ˈkærɪkətʃər/ (US), /ˈkærɪkətʃʊə(r)/ (UK)| n. vt.|漫画；讽刺画；滑稽模仿|A picture, description, or imitation of a person or thing in which certain striking characteristics are exaggerated in order to create a comic or grotesque effect.|The politician was easily identified by the caricature of him in the newspaper.  这位政治家很容易从报纸上的漫画中被认出来。|He caricatured the president's mannerisms perfectly. 他完美地模仿了总统的举止。|近义词：cartoon, parody, satire, lampoon|反义词：portrait, likeness|词性: noun, verb

caricature: /ˈkærɪkətʃər/ (US), /ˈkærɪkətʃʊə(r)/ (UK)| vt.|用漫画表现；滑稽地模仿|To represent or imitate someone or something in a caricature.|The comedian caricatured the singer's high-pitched voice.  这位喜剧演员滑稽地模仿了这位歌手的尖嗓子。|The play caricatured the pretensions of the upper class. 这部戏剧讽刺了上流社会的虚荣。|近义词：mock, mimic, satirize|反义词：portray, depict|词性: verb
