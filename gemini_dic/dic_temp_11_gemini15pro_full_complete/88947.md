hole-and-corner:/ˌhəʊl ən ˈkɔːnə(r)/ /ˌhoʊl ən ˈkɔːrnər/ | adj. | 秘密的，偷偷摸摸的，鬼鬼祟祟的 | Done or made secretly and not openly, often because it is dishonest or illegal. |  They held a hole-and-corner meeting to discuss the plan. 他们秘密开会讨论这个计划。| synonyms: clandestine, secretive, surreptitious, furtive, covert, underhand | antonyms: open, public, overt | adjective

hole-and-corner:/ˌhəʊl ən ˈkɔːnə(r)/ /ˌhoʊl ən ˈkɔːrnər/ | n. | 秘密活动，鬼鬼祟祟的行为 |  A secret and often dishonest or illegal activity. | The whole affair was a hole-and-corner business. 整个事件都是鬼鬼祟祟的。 | synonyms: secrecy, clandestine activity, covert operation | antonyms: openness, transparency | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From the late 16th century. Originally referring to a remote or obscure place (literally, a corner with a hole in it), the meaning shifted to describe activities conducted in such places, implying secrecy and often something illicit.
