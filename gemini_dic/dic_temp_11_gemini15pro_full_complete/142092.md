orientalist: /ˌɔːriˈentəlɪst/ /ˌɔːrˈiːɛntəlɪst/ | n. adj. | 东方学家；东方通；对东方文化有兴趣的人；东方情调的 | A person who studies or is an expert in the cultures of the East.  Someone who shows special knowledge of or interest in Eastern cultures.  Having qualities or characteristics perceived as typical of the cultures of the East. |  例句：He is a renowned orientalist specializing in ancient Chinese literature. 他是一位著名的汉学家，专攻中国古代文学。

例句：She decorated her home with orientalist art and furnishings. 她用东方艺术和家具装饰她的家。


近义词：Asianist, scholar of Asian studies | 反义词：Occidentalist | 词性：noun, adjective

orientalist: /ˌɔːriˈentəlɪst/ /ˌɔːrˈiːɛntəlɪst/ | adj. | 东方情调的 | Having qualities or characteristics perceived as typical of the cultures of the East. | 例句：The building had an orientalist design, with intricate carvings and vibrant colors. 这座建筑具有东方情调的设计，带有复杂的雕刻和鲜艳的色彩。

例句：Her orientalist paintings depicted scenes from life in the Middle East. 她的东方主义绘画描绘了中东生活场景。


近义词：Eastern, exotic | 反义词：Western | 词性：adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *orientaliste*, from *oriental* ("eastern") + *-iste* (suffix forming agent nouns and adjectives). *Oriental* is from Late Latin *orientalis*, from Latin *oriens* ("east, rising sun"), present participle of *orior* ("to rise").
