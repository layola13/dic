conflagration:/ˌkɑːnfləˈɡreɪʃn/ | n. | 大火灾，战争 | a large and destructive fire that causes a lot of damage | The conflagration destroyed several city blocks. 这场大火摧毁了几个街区。 | synonyms: inferno, blaze, firestorm | antonyms: drizzle, shower, deluge | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin conflagratio (“a burning up”), from conflagrare (“to burn completely”), from con- (“together”) + flagrare (“to burn, blaze”).
