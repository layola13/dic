overgratification:/ˌoʊvərˌɡrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/| n.|过度满足，过分满足|The overgratification of a child can lead to a sense of entitlement. 过度满足孩子会导致他们产生一种权利感。|例句：Overgratification of desires can lead to unhappiness in the long run.  长期来看，欲望的过度满足会导致不快乐。

|例句：The constant overgratification of his appetite led to health problems. 他不断地过度满足食欲导致了健康问题。|近义词：overindulgence, pampering, spoiling|反义词: deprivation, self-denial|词性:名词


Etymology->

From over- + gratification.  "Over-" signifies "excessive" or "too much." "Gratification" comes from the Late Latin gratificātiōn- (stem of gratificātiō), meaning "pleasing, gratifying," from the past participle stem of gratificārī "to gratify," from gratus "pleasing, agreeable, thankful" + -ficāre, combinative form of facere "to do, make."
