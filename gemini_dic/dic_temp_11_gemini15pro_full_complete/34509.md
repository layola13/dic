Etymology:
clean (adjective) + -able (suffix) + -ity (suffix)

"Clean" comes from Old English "clæne," meaning "free from dirt or defilement."
"-able" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "capable of being," "worthy of being," or "tending to."
"-ity" is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, often denoting a state or quality.
