mows:/moʊz/ (US), /məʊz/ (UK)| v. |割(草坪); 刈(草); |cuts down (grass) with a machine or tool with a sharp blade | He mows the lawn every Saturday. 他每个星期六都修剪草坪。| synonyms: cuts, trims, shears | antonyms: grows, cultivates | verb (third-person singular simple present mows, present participle mowing, simple past mowed, past participle mowed or (rare, dialectal) mown)

mows:/moʊz/ (US), /məʊz/ (UK)| n. |(干草的)堆,垛 | a pile of hay or other similar material, especially one built in a field for storage | The farmer stored the hay in mows. 农民把干草堆放在草垛里。 | synonyms: stacks, piles, heaps | antonyms:  (This noun form doesn't have a direct antonym) | noun (plural mows)


Etymology: From Old English *māwan (attested in the past participle māwen), from Proto-Germanic *mēaną (“to mow”), from Proto-Indo-European *meh₁-  (“to mow, reap”).
