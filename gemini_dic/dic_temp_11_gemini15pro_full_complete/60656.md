electively:/ɪˈlɛktɪvli/ | adv. | 选修地；可选择地 | By choosing; of one's own free will.  | Students can electively take advanced courses. 学生可以选择修读高级课程。|  He electively chose to stay home and study. 他选择留在家学习。 | 近义词: optionally, voluntarily | 反义词: obligatorily, compulsorily | 词性: 副词

词源: From "elective" + "-ly."  "Elective" comes from Late Latin "ēlectivus" meaning "chosen, select," from past participle stem of "ēligere" "to choose out, elect," from "ē-" (variant of "ex-") "out" + "legere" "to choose, gather, read."
