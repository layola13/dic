hegelianism: /ˌheɪɡəˈliːənɪzəm/ /ˌhiːˈɡeɪliənɪzəm/ | n. | 黑格尔主义; 黑格尔哲学 |  The philosophy of Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel, which can be summed up by the dictum that "the rational alone is real", which means that all reality is capable of being expressed in rational categories. His goal was to reduce reality to a more synthetic unity within the system of absolute idealism. |  Hegelianism has been a major influence in Western philosophy. 黑格尔主义对西方哲学产生了重大影响。|  The professor's lectures focused on the intricacies of Hegelianism.  教授的讲座集中讨论了黑格尔主义的复杂性。 | synonyms: absolute idealism, Hegelian philosophy | antonyms: materialism, empiricism | noun
