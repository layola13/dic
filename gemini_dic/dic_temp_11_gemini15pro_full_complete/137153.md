nonsubtleness:/ˌnɒnsʌtltnəs/ |n.| 不微妙; 粗糙; 粗俗; 不精细 |Lack of subtlety; the quality of being obvious or heavy-handed.| Her nonsubtleness in asking for a raise was embarrassing. 她毫不掩饰地要求加薪，令人尴尬。| synonyms: obviousness, bluntness, crudeness, directness, explicitness, artlessness, lack of finesse| antonyms: subtlety, nuance, finesse, delicacy, tact, discretion | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + subtlety + -ness (forming nouns expressing a state or quality).  Subtlety itself comes from Old French *subtilité*, from Latin *subtilitas*, from *subtilis* "fine, thin, delicate," perhaps related to *sub-*, "under" + *tela* "web."
