intershading:/ˈɪntərˌʃeɪdɪŋ/
n.
相互遮蔽; 交错遮蔽;  互相遮蔽
The act or process of shading one part of a surface so that it appears darker than another part, creating the illusion of depth or three-dimensionality. This effect is often achieved through the use of varying colors, tones, or values within a given area.
The artist used subtle intershading to create a realistic portrait. 艺术家运用精妙的相互遮蔽创作了一幅逼真的肖像画。

近义词: shading, gradation, blending
反义词: highlighting

词性: noun

词源:  "inter-" (prefix meaning "between, among") + "shading" (from "shade," ultimately from Old English "sceadu").
