intimate:/ˈɪntɪmeɪt/
verb
暗示，提示
to make known subtly and indirectly; hint
He intimated that he would resign if he didn't get a raise. 他暗示如果得不到加薪就会辞职。

intimate:/ˈɪntɪmət/
adjective
亲密的，密切的
closely acquainted; familiar; private and personal
They have an intimate understanding of each other's feelings. 他们对彼此的感受有着深刻的理解。

intimate:/ˈɪntɪmeɪt/
noun
至交，密友
a close friend or associate
She was surrounded by her intimates. 她被她的密友们包围着。

Etymology:  From Late Latin intimatus, past participle of intimare "to announce, to make known," from Latin intimus "inmost," superlative of in "in."

Synonyms (verb): suggest, imply, hint, allude to
Antonyms (verb): declare, announce, proclaim
Synonyms (adjective): close, personal, private
Antonyms (adjective): distant, impersonal, public
