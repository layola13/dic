incommensurability:/ɪnkəmɛnʃərəˈbɪlɪti/
|n.|不可通约性；无法比较性|The state or quality of being incommensurable; lacking a common standard of measurement.|There is an incommensurability between the two theories, making it difficult to compare them directly.  这两种理论之间存在不可通约性，因此很难直接比较它们。|incompatibility, disparity, difference, incongruity|commensurability, similarity, compatibility|noun


incommensurable:/ɪnkəmɛnʃərəbl/
|adj.|不可通约的；不能比较的|Not commensurable; having no common measure or standard of comparison.|The values of the two societies are incommensurable. 这两个社会的价值观是不可通约的。|incompatible, disparate, different, incongruous|commensurable, similar, compatible|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *incommensurabilis, from in- "not" + commensurabilis "commensurable," from com- "together" + mensurabilis "measurable," from mensurare "to measure," from mensura "measure."
