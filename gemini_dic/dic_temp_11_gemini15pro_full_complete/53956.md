discredit:/dɪˈskredɪt/| v. n.|败坏名声，怀疑，使不可信|To refuse to believe; to cause doubt about the truth or accuracy of something; loss or damage to someone's reputation.|例句：The scandal discredited the politician.  这桩丑闻败坏了这位政治家的名声。|近义词：disgrace, dishonor, defame, slander|反义词：credit, honor, praise|词性: 动词, 名词

discredit:/dɪˈskredɪt/| n.|名誉丧失，不信，怀疑|Loss or lack of reputation or credibility; the state of being discredited.|例句：The report cast discredit on the company's practices.  这份报告使该公司 practices 的可信度受到质疑。|近义词：disgrace, dishonor, disrepute|反义词：credit, honor, prestige|词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *discreditare, from dis- "not, opposite of" + creditare "to trust, believe," from Latin creditum, past participle of credere "to believe, trust."
