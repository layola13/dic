mainstreams:/ˈmeɪnstriːmz/| n.|主流，主流思想|The idea quickly entered the mainstream.  这个想法迅速成为主流。|例句：Her music is now firmly in the mainstream. 她的音乐现在完全进入了主流。

例句：The mainstream media often ignores minority viewpoints. 主流媒体经常忽视少数群体的观点。|近义词：trend, norm, standard, convention|反义词:alternative, fringe, unorthodox, unconventional|词性:名词

词源: "mainstream" comes from "main" (meaning principal or chief) and "stream" (a steady flow).  So it literally means the principal current of a river or sea. Figuratively, it refers to the prevailing current of thought, influence, or activity.
