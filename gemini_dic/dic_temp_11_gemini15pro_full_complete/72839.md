folios:/ˈfoʊliˌoʊz/ (US), /ˈfəʊliəʊz/ (UK)
|n.|对开纸；页码；（尤指书籍的）一大张纸；帐簿|Large sheets of paper folded once to make two leaves or four pages of a book or manuscript. Also, a page number, especially in a manuscript or early printed book.|The archivist carefully examined the ancient folios.|Synonyms: leaves, pages, sheets|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical object. Could consider "digital document" as a conceptual opposite.)
|noun|


folios:/ˈfoʊliˌoʊz/ (US), /ˈfəʊliəʊz/ (UK)
|n.|（尤指财务的）一组记录、文件或帐目；（尤指律师事务所或社会服务部门的）客户档案|A collection of records, documents, or accounts, especially of a financial nature. Also, a client's file, especially in a law firm or social services department.|The lawyer reviewed the folios related to the case.|Synonyms: files, records, dossiers|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a collection of information.  "Disarray" or "chaos" could describe the opposite state of organization.)
|noun|
