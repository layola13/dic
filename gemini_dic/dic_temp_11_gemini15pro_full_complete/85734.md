hawked:/hɔːkt/ | v. |叫卖；兜售；散播；咳出(痰) |To sell goods by calling out in public; to promote or publicize something aggressively; to cough something up from the throat. |He hawked his wares in the marketplace. 他在市场上叫卖他的商品。|synonyms: peddled, vended, touted, promoted, coughed up |antonyms: bought, purchased, suppressed, concealed |verb

hawked:/hɔːkt/ | adj. |(指鼻子)钩状的|Having a curved or hooked shape, like a hawk's beak. |He had a hawked nose. 他长着一个鹰钩鼻。|synonyms: hooked, aquiline, curved |antonyms: straight, flat |adjective
