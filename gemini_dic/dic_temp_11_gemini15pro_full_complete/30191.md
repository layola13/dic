celestials:/səˈlɛstiəlz/
| n.|天神，仙人；天体|Inhabitants of the heavens; divine beings; heavenly bodies.|The ancients believed that celestials controlled their destinies.|Synonyms: deities, gods, divinities, heavenly bodies, stars|Antonyms: mortals, humans|Noun


celestial:/səˈlɛstiəl/
| adj.|天的，天空的； celestial|Relating to the sky or the heavens; divinely good or beautiful.|The celestial bodies, such as stars and planets, have always fascinated mankind.|Synonyms: heavenly, ethereal, divine, otherworldly|Antonyms: earthly, terrestrial, mundane|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *celestial*, from Old French *celestiel*, from Latin *caelestis* (“heavenly”), from *caelum* (“sky, heaven”).
