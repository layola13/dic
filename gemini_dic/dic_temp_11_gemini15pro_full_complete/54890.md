dispondee:/dɪˈspɒndiː/ /dɪˈspɑːndiː/
n.|长短短格，双长音节|A metrical foot consisting of two long syllables or, in accentual-syllabic verse, two stressed syllables.|The poet used several spondees to emphasize certain words in the line.|近义词: molossus (less common)|反义词: pyrrhic, iamb|词性: noun
Etymology: From Latin *spondeus*, from Ancient Greek σπονδεῖος (spondeîos, “of a spondee, of a libation”), from σπονδή (spondḗ, “libation”).  Spondeic hymns were chanted during libations.
