deputization:/ˌdɛpjʊtaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 代理; 授权; 委任 | The act of appointing a deputy or deputies; the state of being deputized.  The authority given to a deputy. |  The deputization of the vice president allowed the president to undergo surgery. 副总统的代理授权使得总统能够接受手术。| The deputization of teachers to administer medication is a common practice.  授权教师管理药物是一种常见的做法。|  appointment, delegation, commission, authorization | revocation, dismissal | noun

deputize:/ˈdɛpjʊtaɪz/ | vt. | 授权; 委任; 指定…为代理 | To appoint someone as a deputy; to give someone the authority to act on one's behalf. |  The sheriff deputized several citizens to help search for the missing child. 警长授权几名市民协助搜寻失踪儿童。|  He was deputized to sign the documents in the manager's absence.  他被授权在经理不在时签署文件。| appoint, delegate, commission, authorize, empower | dismiss, revoke | verb
