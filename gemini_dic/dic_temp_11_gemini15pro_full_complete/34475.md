claws:/klɔːz/
n.
爪子，钳子
A sharp, curved nail on the foot of an animal, especially a mammal or bird.  A curved pointed appendage of a crustacean or arachnid, similar to a claw.
The cat sharpened its claws on the tree. 猫在树上磨爪子。
The lobster's claws are powerful. 龙虾的钳子很有力。
synonyms: talons, nails, pincers
antonyms: paws, pads
noun

claw:/klɔː/
v.
(用爪子)抓，撕
To scratch or tear something with claws or fingernails. To grip something tightly with claws or hands.
The cat clawed at the furniture. 猫抓家具。
He clawed his way up the cliff. 他用手扒着爬上了悬崖。
synonyms: scratch, tear, rip, grasp, clutch
antonyms: release, let go
verb

PHRASE->
claw back: 
收回，追回
The government is trying to claw back some of the money it has spent. 政府正试图收回部分已花费的资金。

claw one's way:
艰难地爬行/前进
He clawed his way to the top of the mountain. 他艰难地爬到了山顶。

claw (something) out of (someone):
费力地从某人那里得到(信息/钱财等)
The police had to claw the information out of him. 警察不得不费力地从他那里得到信息。

get one's claws into (someone/something):
控制住，抓住
She's determined to get her claws into that promotion. 她决心要得到那个晋升机会。

sink one's claws into (someone/something):
牢牢抓住，控制
The company has sunk its claws into the local market. 该公司牢牢控制了当地市场。
