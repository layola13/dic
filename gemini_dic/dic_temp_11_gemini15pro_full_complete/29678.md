cathedrated:/kəˈθiːdreɪtɪd/
adjective
有主教座位的；设有主教座堂的
Having a cathedral; possessing a cathedral church.
The newly cathedrated bishop addressed the congregation. 新任主教在主教座堂向会众发表讲话。
Near Antonyms: non-cathedrated
Synonyms: episcopal (related to bishops, but not specifically having a cathedral)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cathedratus*, past participle of *cathedrare* "to install in a cathedral," from Latin *cathedra* "seat," especially "bishop's throne," from Greek *kathedra* "seat, chair, base," from *kata* "down" + *hedra* "seat, chair, face of a geometric solid."
