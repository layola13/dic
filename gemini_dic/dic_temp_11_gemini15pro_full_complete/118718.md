metafictionists:/ˌmetəˈfɪkʃənɪsts/
|n.|元小说家|Writers of metafiction, a type of fiction that self-consciously addresses the devices of fiction.  It draws attention to its status as a literary work, often by blurring the lines between fiction and reality.|John Barth and Kurt Vonnegut are considered influential metafictionists. |Synonyms: postmodernists, experimental writers |Antonyms: realist writers, traditional novelists|noun
