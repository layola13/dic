lurks:/lɜːrks/ /lɝːks/| v. |潜伏，埋伏；隐藏；(危险、疑虑、恐惧等)暗藏；悄悄地移动|To wait hidden and out of sight, typically with a sinister or predatory motive; to be present but not readily visible or noticeable; to move stealthily or furtively.| The danger still lurks. 危险仍然潜伏着。 |synonyms: skulk, prowl, slink, loiter, linger | antonyms: appear, emerge, show, leave |verb

lurking:/ˈlɜːrkɪŋ/ /ˈlɝːkɪŋ/ | adj. |潜伏的；隐藏的| Existing but not yet noticed or obvious; hidden.| There's a lurking suspicion that he's not telling the truth.  他没说实话的疑虑暗藏心中。|synonyms: hidden, concealed, latent, dormant | antonyms: obvious, apparent, evident, manifest |adjective
