outhold:/ˈaʊthəʊld/| n.|住户;家庭;户;|A group of people living together in a single dwelling, typically a family.|例句：The survey revealed that most outholds in the area owned a car. 这项调查显示，该地区大多数家庭拥有一辆汽车。

例句：The government provided financial aid to low-income outholds. 政府向低收入家庭提供经济援助。|近义词：household, family, home|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ūthold (unattested), from *ūt "out" + *hold "a holding, house," related to healdan "to hold."  The prefix *ūt suggests a dwelling set apart or outside a larger community.  While "outhold" appears obsolete in modern usage, its components are preserved in words like "outbuilding" and "household."
