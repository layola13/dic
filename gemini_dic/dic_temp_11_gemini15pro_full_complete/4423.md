alarum:/əˈlɑːrəm/ | n. | 警报，闹钟 |A loud noise or signal that warns people of danger or summons them to action. | The fire alarm went off. 火灾警报响了。| synonyms: alert, warning, signal | antonyms: silence, peace | noun

alarum:/əˈlɑːrəm/ | v. |  发出警报，惊动 | To give a warning or signal of danger. |  The news alarmed the public.  这消息使公众震惊。| synonyms: warn, alert, frighten | antonyms: reassure, comfort | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *alarme, from Old French alarme, from Italian all'arme ("to arms!"), from a (from Latin ad) + le (plural definite article) + arme ("arms"). Ultimately from Latin arma ("weapons").
