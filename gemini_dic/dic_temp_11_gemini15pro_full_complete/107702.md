lansquenet:/ˈlænskwɪnɪt/| n.|雇佣兵，步兵，纸牌游戏|A German mercenary foot soldier, especially of the 15th and 16th centuries. Also, a card game resembling piquet.|The lansquenets were feared for their ruthlessness.  那些雇佣兵因为他们的残忍而令人恐惧。  He lost all his money playing lansquenet. 他玩纸牌游戏输光了所有的钱。|Synonyms: mercenary, soldier, foot soldier, pikeman (for the soldier); card game (for the game) |Antonyms: civilian (for the soldier)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From French *lansquenet*, from German *Landsknecht*, literally “land servant,” from *Land* (“land”) + *Knecht* (“servant, knight”).
