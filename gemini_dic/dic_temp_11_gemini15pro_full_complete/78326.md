gerrymanderer:/ˈdʒɛrimændərər/ | n. | 不公正划分选区的人；操纵选区的人 |  Someone who gerrymanders. A person who manipulates the boundaries of an electoral constituency so as to favor one party or class. |  The gerrymanderer was accused of trying to disenfranchise minority voters.  这个划分选区的人被指控试图剥夺少数族裔选民的权利。| synonym: manipulator, cheat | antonym: impartial arbiter, fair dealer | noun

gerrymander:/ˈdʒɛrimændər/ | v. | 不公正地划分选区；操纵选区 | To divide (a geographic area) into voting districts so as to give unfair advantage to one party in elections. | The state legislature gerrymandered the districts to ensure their party's victory. 州议会不公正地划分选区以确保他们党派的胜利。 | synonym: manipulate, rig | antonym: redistrict fairly, apportion equitably | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Elbridge Gerry + (sala)mander.  Elbridge Gerry (1744-1814) was the governor of Massachusetts when a redistricting plan was passed in 1812 that created a district resembling a salamander in shape.  This practice of manipulating district boundaries for political advantage became known as gerrymandering.
