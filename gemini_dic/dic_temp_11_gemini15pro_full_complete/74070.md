fossilising:/ˈfɒsəlaɪzɪŋ/ (British), /ˈfɑːsəlaɪzɪŋ/ (American)
verb (present participle)
使…变成化石；僵化；使…墨守成规
Becoming a fossil; preserving something so that it remains unchanged.
The dinosaur bones were fossilising over millions of years.  恐龙骨骼在数百万年的时间里变成了化石。
Bureaucracy is fossilising the company's innovative spirit.  官僚主义正在扼杀公司的创新精神。
近义词:petrifying, ossifying
反义词:modernizing, evolving
词性:v.

fossilising:/ˈfɒsəlaɪzɪŋ/ (British), /ˈfɑːsəlaɪzɪŋ/ (American)
adjective
(rare)  使…变成化石的；僵化的
Causing or tending to fossilize.
These fossilising resins preserve insects perfectly. 这些石化树脂完美地保存了昆虫。
The fossilising effects of tradition can stifle progress.  传统的僵化效应会扼杀进步。
近义词:petrifying, ossifying
反义词:modernizing, evolving
词性:adj.
