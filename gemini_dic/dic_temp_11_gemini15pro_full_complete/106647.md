kurofune:/ˌkʊroʊˈfuːneɪ/ /ˌkʊrəʊˈfuːneɪ/
noun
黑船 (Black Ships)
Japanese term for the Western, primarily American, steamships that arrived in Japan in the mid-19th century, forcing the end of Japan's isolationist sakoku policy.  The term literally translates to "black ships" and refers to the black paint and coal smoke of the vessels.
The arrival of the kurofune marked a turning point in Japanese history.
近义词: Western ships, American ships (in the context of the 19th century)
反义词: None (Specific historical term)
词性: Noun


Etymology:
From Japanese 黒船 (kurofune), from 黒 (kuro, “black”) + 船 (fune, “ship”).
