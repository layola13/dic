hieroglyphed:/ˌhaɪərəˈɡlɪft/ | adj. | 象形文字的，用象形文字写的 | Covered or inscribed with hieroglyphs; resembling hieroglyphs. | The tomb was hieroglyphed with ancient symbols. 墓穴上刻满了古老的符号。 | The weathered stone was hieroglyphed with strange markings that no one could decipher.  风化的石头上刻着奇怪的符号，没有人能 decipher. | Synonym: inscribed, carved, marked | Antonym: plain, unmarked | Word type: adjective

hieroglyph:/ˈhaɪərəɡlɪf/ | n. | 象形文字 | A character used in a system of pictorial writing, especially that of the ancient Egyptians. | The Rosetta Stone helped scholars decipher Egyptian hieroglyphs. 罗塞塔石碑帮助学者们破译了埃及象形文字。 |  Ancient Egyptian scribes used hieroglyphs to record important events. 古埃及的抄写员使用象形文字记录重要事件。 | Synonym: symbol, character, ideogram | Antonym: letter, alphabet | Word type: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *hiéroglyphe* (now *hiéroglyphe*), from Latin *hieroglyphicus*, from Greek *hieroglyphikos*, from *hieroglyphein* "to carve sacred characters," from *hieros* "sacred" + *glyphein* "to carve".
