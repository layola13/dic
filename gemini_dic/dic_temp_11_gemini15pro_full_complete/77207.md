gasteropod:/ˈɡæstrəpɒd/ (US) /ˈɡæstrəpɑːd/ (UK)| n. |腹足纲动物；腹足类动物|A mollusk of the large class Gastropoda, such as a snail, slug, or whelk, having a single shell (often coiled) or no shell at all, a distinct head bearing tentacles, and a broad, flat foot.|Snails and slugs are common gasteropods.  蜗牛和蛞蝓是常见的腹足纲动物。|Synonyms: univalve, snail, slug|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps bivalve for a contrasting mollusk group)|noun
