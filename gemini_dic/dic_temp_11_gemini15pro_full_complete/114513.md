maladroit:/ˌmæləˈdrɔɪt/
| adj. |笨拙的；不灵巧的|Lacking skill or dexterity; awkward and clumsy. |She was maladroit in social situations. 她在社交场合很笨拙。|Synonyms: clumsy, awkward, inept, ungainly|Antonyms: deft, skillful, adroit, graceful
|词性:形容词


词源:
From French *maladroit*, from *mal* ("badly") + *adroit* ("skillful, dexterous").  Ultimately from Latin *dexter* ("right, right-hand").  The prefix *mal-* signifies "bad" or "wrong".
