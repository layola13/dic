instep:/ˈɪnstep/ | n. | 脚背；鞋面 | The top part of the foot between the toes and the ankle/The part of a shoe or boot that covers this part of the foot. |  She hurt her instep playing football. 她踢足球时伤到了脚背。 |  The instep of his boot was scuffed. 他靴子的鞋面被擦破了。 | synonyms: arch (of foot), whamp  | antonyms: (no direct antonym, perhaps sole) | noun


instep:/ˈɪnstep/ | n. | (拱的)内曲线；拱腹 | The inner curve of an arch. (less common usage) | The instep of the bridge was elegantly designed.  桥拱的内曲线设计得很优雅。 | The architect paid close attention to the instep of each archway. 建筑师密切关注每个拱门的拱腹。| synonyms: soffit, intrados | antonyms: extrados | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *instep*, equivalent to *in* + *step*.  Cognate with Dutch *instap*, German *Inst*, Swedish *insteg*.  Literally, the "in-step," where the foot steps into a shoe.
