harridan:/ˈhærɪdən/| n. |泼妇，悍妇|A scolding, vicious, or shrewish woman; a hag.|The old harridan yelled at the children playing in her yard.  那个老泼妇对着在她院子里玩耍的孩子们大喊大叫。|近义词：shrew, virago, termagant, vixen|反义词:angel, sweetheart|词性:名词

harridan:/ˈhærɪdən/| n. |老巫婆|An unpleasant or cruel older woman.|The shopkeeper was a real harridan, always complaining and never smiling.  那个店主真是个老巫婆，总是抱怨，从不微笑。|近义词：witch, hag, crone|反义词:youth, ingenue|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *haridegan*, possibly from Anglo-French *haride*, a tired-out horse.  The semantic shift from a worn-out horse to a shrewish woman likely occurred due to the perceived unpleasantness and bad temper of both.
