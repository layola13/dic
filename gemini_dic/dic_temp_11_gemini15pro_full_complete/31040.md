chamberlainship: /ˈtʃeɪmbərlɪnʃɪp/
n.
侍从长之职; 内务大臣的职位
The office or dignity of a chamberlain.  The period of time during which someone holds this position.
He held the chamberlainship for many years, serving three different monarchs. 他担任侍从长多年，侍奉过三位不同的君主。
Synonyms: stewardship, administratorship
Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, could consider subordination, subjection if referring to the power dynamic)
词性: 名词

词源:
From chamberlain + -ship.  Chamberlain comes from Old French chambrelenc, ultimately from Late Latin camerarius, "chamber attendant," from camera, "chamber."  The suffix -ship denotes office, state, or condition.
