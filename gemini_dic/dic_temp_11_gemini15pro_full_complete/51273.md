desultory: /ˈdesəlˌtɔːri/ (US), /ˈdezəltəri/ (UK) | adj. | 散漫的，断断续续的，不连贯的 | lacking a plan, purpose, or enthusiasm; occurring randomly or occasionally |  He made a desultory attempt to tidy his room. 他漫不经心地尝试整理房间。|  She flipped through the channels, watching desultory snippets of different shows. 她不停地转换频道，断断续续地看了些不同节目的片段。 | 近义词: aimless, haphazard, random, erratic, sporadic | 反义词: methodical, systematic, focused, diligent | 词性: 形容词
词源:  From Latin *desultorius* "leaping down from," from *desilire* "to leap down," from *de* "down" + *salire* "to leap."  Originally referred to a rider in a Roman circus who leaped from one horse to another.  The sense of "lacking constancy" developed later.
