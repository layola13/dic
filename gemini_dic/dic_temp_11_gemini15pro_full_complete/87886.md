hield: /hiːld/
n.|盾牌；保护；保护者|A broad piece of metal or another suitable material, held by straps or a handle attached to one side, used as a protection against blows or missiles.|The knight raised his shield to deflect the blow. 骑士举起盾牌挡开攻击。|近义词: buckler, aegis, protection, defense|反义词: vulnerability, exposure|词性: 名词

hield: /hiːld/
v.|保护；遮蔽|To protect from danger or harm.|He shielded his eyes from the sun. 他用手遮住眼睛挡住阳光。|近义词: protect, defend, guard, safeguard|反义词: expose, endanger|词性: 动词


词源: From Old English *sċeld or *scield, from Proto-Germanic *skelduz, meaning "shield, board."  Cognate with Old Norse skjöldr, Old High German scilt, and Gothic skildus.
