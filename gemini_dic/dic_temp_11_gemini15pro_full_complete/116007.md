marshbanker:/ˈmɑːrʃˌbæŋkər/ | n. | 沼泽银行家；沼泽地区的地主 | A person who owns or manages land in a marsh area, especially for the purpose of exploiting its resources (e.g., peat, reeds, fish). | The marshbanker carefully surveyed his land, assessing the potential for peat harvesting. 沼泽银行家仔细勘察了他的土地，评估了泥炭收割的潜力。 | 近义词：landowner, marshland owner | 反义词：N/A | 词性: noun

词源: marsh + banker.  "Marsh" refers to a wetland, and "banker" traditionally refers to someone who owns or controls a bank, often implying financial resources and control over a valuable asset.  In this context, "banker" is extended to mean someone who controls and exploits the resources of a marsh.
