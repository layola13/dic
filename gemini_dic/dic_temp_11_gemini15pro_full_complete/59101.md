dying-down:/ˈdaɪɪŋ daʊn/
adjective
逐渐平息的；逐渐减弱的
Becoming less strong or active; gradually decreasing.
The applause was dying down when he began to speak.  掌声在他开始讲话时逐渐平息下来。
Synonyms: subsiding, fading, lessening, weakening, abating
Antonyms: intensifying, increasing, strengthening, escalating
词性:形容词

dying-down:/ˈdaɪɪŋ daʊn/
verb phrase
逐渐平息；逐渐减弱
To become less strong or active; to gradually decrease.
The wind is dying down. 风逐渐减弱了。
Synonyms: subsiding, fading, lessening, weakening, abating
Antonyms: intensifying, increasing, strengthening, escalating
词性:动词短语

词源:  "dying" comes from Old English "dyġan," meaning "to die." "Down" comes from Old English "dūn," meaning "from a higher to a lower place."  The combination signifies a gradual decrease or weakening, like something fading away.
