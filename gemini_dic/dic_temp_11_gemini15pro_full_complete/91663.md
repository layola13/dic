hypercautiousnesses:/ˌhaɪpərˈkɔːʃəsnəsɪz/| n. |过度谨慎，极度小心|  The state of being excessively cautious, characterized by extreme or exaggerated carefulness and a reluctance to take risks.|  His hypercautiousnesses prevented him from investing in the stock market.  他过度谨慎，以至于不敢投资股市。|近义词：timidity, wariness, apprehension|反义词：recklessness, boldness, audacity|词性:名词

Etymology:
From "hyper-" (Greek, meaning "over, beyond") + "cautious" (from Latin "cautus," meaning "careful, wary") + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
