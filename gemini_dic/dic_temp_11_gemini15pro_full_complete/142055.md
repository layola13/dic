organum:/ˈɔːrɡənəm/ (US) /ˈɔːɡənəm/ (UK)| n. |奥尔加农,  管风琴, 器官|A form of early polyphony in which a new voice or voices are added to an existing plainchant melody.|The Notre Dame school of polyphony was famous for its development of the organum.|近义词：polyphony, counterpoint|反义词: monophony|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *organum*, from Latin organum "instrument, tool, organ (musical instrument or of the body)," from Ancient Greek ὄργανον (órganon, “tool, instrument, organ”). Doublet of organ.
