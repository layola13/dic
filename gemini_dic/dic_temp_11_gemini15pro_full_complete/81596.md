ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *greet, gret, from Old English *grēat (“big, large, thick, coarse, tall, long; pregnant; intense, severe; important, chief, principal; noble, powerful, proud; high-ranking; advanced (in age); grown up, adult”), from Proto-Germanic *grautaz (“great, big”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵhrewh₂- (“to grow, become green”). Cognate with West Frisian great (“great”), Dutch groot (“large, big, great”), German groß (“large, big, great”), Danish grov (“coarse, rough”), Swedish grov (“coarse, rough”), Icelandic gróinn (“grown”).
