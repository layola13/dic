nanoparticle:/ˈnænoʊˌpɑːrtɪkəl/| n. |纳米颗粒，纳米粒子|A microscopic particle whose size is measured in nanometers (typically 1-100 nanometers).  Nanoparticles exhibit unique properties due to their small size and large surface area to volume ratio.|Scientists are exploring the use of nanoparticles in medicine for targeted drug delivery.|Synonyms: nanomaterial, ultrafine particle|Antonyms: bulk material|noun
