desolate: /ˈdɛsələt/ (US) /ˈdɛsəlɪt/ (UK)| adj. v. | 荒凉的，无人烟的；使孤寂，使凄凉 | Characterized by emptiness and loneliness; to make someone feel sad and alone. | The desolate landscape stretched for miles.  荒凉的景色绵延数英里。| synonyms: barren, bleak, deserted, forlorn | antonyms: populous, cheerful, lively | 词性: 形容词, 动词

desolates: /ˈdɛsəleɪts/ (US) /ˈdɛsəleɪts/ (UK)| v. | 使荒芜，使孤寂 | Third-person singular present tense form of "desolate," meaning to make a place empty and lonely or to make someone feel sad and alone. | The war desolates the once vibrant city. 战争使这座曾经充满活力的城市变得荒芜。| synonyms: devastates, ruins, ravages | antonyms: populates, enlivens, cheers | 词性: 动词

词源: From Late Latin *desolatus, past participle of desolare (“to abandon, forsake”), from Latin dē- + sōlāre (“to leave alone”), from sōlus (“alone”).
