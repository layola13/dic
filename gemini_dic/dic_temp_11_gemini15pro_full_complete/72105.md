flock:/flɑːk (US) /flɒk (UK)| n. vi.| 群;聚集;大量;涌向|A large number of birds, especially of one kind, feeding, resting, or traveling together.  A group of people with a common interest or purpose.|例句：A flock of sheep grazed in the field. 一群羊在田野里吃草。|例句：A flock of tourists descended on the small town.  一群游客涌入小镇。|近义词：group, herd, swarm, crowd, throng|反义词: scattering, dispersal |词性:noun, verb


flock:/flɑːk (US) /flɒk (UK)| vi.|聚集;成群而行;涌向|To gather or move in a flock; to congregate.|例句：People flocked to the beach on the hot summer day.  炎热的夏日，人们涌向海滩。|例句：Birds of a feather flock together. 物以类聚，人以群分。|近义词：gather, congregate, assemble|反义词: disperse, scatter|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *flocc, from Proto-Germanic *flokkaz. Related to Old High German floc, and Old Norse flokkr.  The original meaning referred specifically to a company of people.
