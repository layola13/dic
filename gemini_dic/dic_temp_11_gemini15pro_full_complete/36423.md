col:/kɒl/ (British) /kɑːl/ (American)| n.| 山口，关隘，鞍部；（气象学）低气压区，低压槽；（植物）叶柄间隙|A high mountain pass between two peaks. A col is a low point on a ridge between two higher peaks.|We reached the col just before sunset. 我们在日落前到达了山口。|synonyms: pass, saddle, gap, notch|antonyms: peak, summit|noun

col:/kɒl/ (British) /kɑːl/ (American)| n.| （蔬菜的）心，菜心|The heart of a vegetable, such as cabbage or lettuce.|The col of the lettuce was crisp and sweet. 生菜心脆甜可口。|synonyms: heart, core|antonyms: outer leaves|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *col*, from Latin *collum* "neck", related to *collis* "hill". The sense of "mountain pass" developed from the idea of a "neck" between two peaks.
