imperatives:/ɪmˈpɛrətɪvz/| n.|命令，指令；祈使语气（的动词）|A command or instruction; verbs in the imperative mood. |例句：The general issued several imperatives to his troops. 将军向他的部队发出了几条指令。

例句：Use imperatives sparingly in formal writing. 在正式写作中谨慎使用祈使句。


近义词：commands, orders, directives|反义词:requests, suggestions|词性:noun

一般将来时 (Simple Future):

*While less common, you can imply an imperative in the future tense with context:*

Don't forget to lock the door when you leave. = 你走的时候别忘了锁门。
Make sure you submit the report by Friday. = 确保你在星期五之前提交报告。

虚拟语气:
*Imperatives themselves are not used in the subjunctive, but they can be the subject of a subjunctive clause:*

It is essential that everyone follow the instructions. = 每个人都必须遵守指示。
It is important that you be there on time. = 你准时到那里很重要。


被动语态:
*Imperatives are not typically used in the passive voice, though the sense of an imperative can be conveyed passively:*

The door is to be closed. = 门要关上。
Silence is to be maintained. = 要保持安静.


疑问句:
*Imperatives can be softened into questions:*

Could you please close the door? = 你能关上门吗？
Would you mind being quiet? = 你介意安静点吗？



否定句:
Don't open the window. = 不要开窗户。
Do not disturb. = 请勿打扰。
