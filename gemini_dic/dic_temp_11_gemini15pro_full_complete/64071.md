epitrope:/ɪˈpɪtrəʊp/ /əˈpɪtrəˌpi/ | n. | 允许；让步；委托；特许 |  The act of conceding or yielding a point or privilege. Permission granted by an authority, especially a government or church. |  The government granted an epitome to the company to build a new factory. 政府特许该公司建造一座新工厂。 | 近义词：concession, allowance, permission, grant | 反义词：prohibition, refusal, denial | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *epitropia*, from Greek *epitropē* "committal, commission, superintendence, administration, power of attorney," from *epitropous* "entrusted with the care of something," from *epi* "over, upon" + *tropos* "turn, direction, manner."
