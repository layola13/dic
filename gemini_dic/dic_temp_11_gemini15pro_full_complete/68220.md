fairies:/ˈfeəriz/| n. |仙女，小精灵|A small, imaginary being with magical powers, often depicted with wings and a wand.  They are often associated with nature and folklore.|例句：The fairies danced in the moonlight. 仙女在月光下跳舞。

例句：She believed in fairies when she was a child. 她小时候相信有仙女。


近义词：sprites, pixies, elves|反义词:monsters, demons|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fairie*, from Old French *faerie* (“fairyland, enchantment”), from *fae* (“fairy”), from Vulgar Latin *fata* (“fate, destiny; fairy”), from Latin *fātum* (“fate”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*bhā-*.
