frocking:/ˈfrɒkɪŋ/ /ˈfrɑːkɪŋ/ | n. |  授予圣职；任命仪式；(尤指女童穿的)连衣裙 |  The act of ordaining someone into the Christian ministry; a ceremony at which this happens. Also, a dress, typically a woman's or girl's, with a close-fitting bodice and a full skirt. | The bishop will be frocking five new priests this Sunday.  主教这个星期天将要任命五位新牧师。

She wore a pretty pink frocking for the party. 她穿着漂亮的粉红色连衣裙参加派对。 | 近义词：ordination (for the religious sense), dress, gown | 反义词：defrocking (for the religious sense) | 词性：名词

词源:  From the noun "frock," meaning a dress or robe, particularly a monastic or clerical one.  The "-ing" suffix transforms it into a noun representing the act of putting on such a garment, hence the association with ordination.  "Frock" itself comes from Old French "fro(c)," of Germanic origin.
