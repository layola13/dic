carmine:/ˈkɑːrmɪn/ /ˈkɑːrmaɪn/ | n. adj.|洋红色，胭脂红；深红色|A vivid crimson pigment made from cochineal.  A rich, deep red color.|例句：She wore a dress of carmine silk. 她穿着一件胭脂红的丝绸连衣裙。|近义词：crimson, scarlet, vermilion|反义词：pale, light|词性:名词，形容词

carmine:/ˈkɑːrmɪn/ /ˈkɑːrmaɪn/ | adj.|洋红色的，胭脂红的；深红色的|Having a vivid crimson color.|例句：The sunset painted the sky in carmine hues.  日落把天空染成了深红色。|近义词：crimson, scarlet, vermilion|反义词：pale, light|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *carmin (modern French carmin), from Medieval Latin carminium, from Arabic qirmiz (“kermes”), from Sanskrit krimiga (“insect producing a red dye”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kʷr̥mis (“worm”).  Doublet of crimson and kermes.
