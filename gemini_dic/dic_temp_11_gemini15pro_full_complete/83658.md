haemosiderin:/ˌhiːməˈsɪdərɪn/| n. |含铁血黄素|An iron-storage complex. It is formed by the breakdown of red blood cells and is usually found within cells (especially macrophages) in tissues such as the bone marrow, liver, and spleen.  It appears as golden-yellow to brown granules that stain blue with Prussian blue dye.|Hemosiderin accumulation can be seen in the liver in cases of iron overload. 在铁过载的情况下，可以在肝脏中看到含铁血黄素的积聚。|Synonyms: siderin|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

haemosiderin:/ˌhiːməˈsɪdərɪn/| n. |含铁血黄素|Excessive haemosiderin deposition can cause organ damage. 过多的含铁血黄素沉积会导致器官损伤。|Synonyms: siderin|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
