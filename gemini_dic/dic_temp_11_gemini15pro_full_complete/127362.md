nda:/ˌɛn diːˈeɪ/ | n. |保密协议; 不公开协议| A non-disclosure agreement (NDA), also known as a confidentiality agreement or confidential disclosure agreement (CDA), is a legal contract between at least two parties that outlines confidential material, knowledge, or information that the parties wish to share with one another for certain purposes, but wish to restrict access to or by third parties. |Example:  Both companies signed an NDA before beginning negotiations.  两家公司在开始谈判前签署了保密协议。

Example: We need to sign an NDA before we can discuss the details of the project. 我们需要在讨论项目细节之前签署一份保密协议。 | 近义词：confidentiality agreement, secrecy agreement | 反义词: disclosure agreement | 词性: noun
