falernian: /fɔːlˈɜːrniən/ | adj. n. | 法勒尼安酒（的） | An ancient Roman white wine, originally made from grapes grown on the slopes of Mount Falernus in Campania, Italy.  It was highly regarded and celebrated in Roman literature. |  The poet Horace praised the qualities of Falernian wine. 贺拉斯诗人赞美了法勒尼安酒的品质。 | The host served a rare vintage of Falernian. 主人端上了一瓶珍稀的法勒尼安葡萄酒。 | 近义词: wine, vintage | 反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider non-alcoholic beverages) | 词性: adjective, noun

Etymology: From Latin *Falernus*, referring to Mount Falernus in ancient Italy.
