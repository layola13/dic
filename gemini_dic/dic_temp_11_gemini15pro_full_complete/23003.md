| n. | 大树枝；主枝 | a main branch of a tree | The monkey swung from bough to bough. 猴子从一根树枝荡到另一根树枝。 | branch, limb | trunk, root | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bow, boghe, from Old English bōg (“bough, branch, shoulder, bow”), from Proto-Germanic *bōgz (“bow, shoulder, branch”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰewg- (“to bend, curve”). Cognate with Scots bough (“bough, branch”), West Frisian boech (“bow”), Dutch boeg (“bow, prow”), German Bug (“bow, bend, curve, shoulder, haunch”), Danish bug (“bend, curve”), Swedish bug (“bend, curve”), Icelandic bogi (“bow, arch”). Related to bow (v.).
