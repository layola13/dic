adumbration:/ˌædʌmˈbreɪʃn/ | n. |  预示，略述，勾勒 |A faint indication or outline of something; a foreshadowing or prefiguration. |The architect's initial sketches provided a rough adumbration of the finished building.  建筑师最初的草图大致勾勒出了竣工后的建筑物。|Synonyms: outline, sketch, foreshadowing, prefiguration, intimation, suggestion, hint |Antonyms: clarification, detail, elaboration, specification| noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *adumbratio*, from Latin *adumbrare* "to sketch, represent faintly," from *ad-* "to" + *umbrare* "to shade," from *umbra* "shade, shadow."
