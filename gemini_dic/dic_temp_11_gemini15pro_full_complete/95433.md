incing:/ˈɪnsɪŋ/
verb
怂恿;煽动;激起
Encouraging or urging someone to do something, often something wrong or unwise.
He was incing his friend to steal the car. 他一直在怂恿他的朋友去偷那辆车。
The crowd was incing the fighters to hit each other harder.  人群煽动战士们更用力地互相攻击。


近义词: goading, urging, prompting, egging on, inciting, instigating
反义词: discouraging, dissuading, deterring
词性: verb (present participle of *ince*)

词源:
"Incing" is the present participle of the verb "ince," which is now considered obsolete or archaic.  It likely derives from the now also obsolete verb "incite" meaning "to stir up; excite," from the Latin "incitāre," frequentative of "inciēre," meaning "to set in motion; begin."
