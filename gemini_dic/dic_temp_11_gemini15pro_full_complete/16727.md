bars:/bɑːrz/|/bɑːrz/| n. |酒吧；条，杆；栅栏；障碍；音乐小节 |A place where alcoholic drinks are served over a counter. Also, a long, rigid piece of metal or wood; a barrier or obstacle; a measure in music. |例句：We went to a few bars last night. 昨晚我们去了几家酒吧。

The prison bars were thick and strong.  监狱的栏杆又粗又壮。


|近义词：pubs, taverns, saloons (for drinking establishments); rods, poles, rails (for physical bars); obstacles, hindrances, impediments (for barriers) |反义词: openings, gaps (for physical bars)|词性:noun


bars:/bɑːrz/|/bɑːrz/| v. |禁止；阻拦 |To prevent or forbid something.  |例句：He was barred from entering the club. 他被禁止进入俱乐部。

The heavy snow barred the mountain pass. 大雪封住了山口。


|近义词：prevent, prohibit, obstruct, block |反义词:allow, permit, aid, assist|词性:verb



ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *barre, from Late Latin *barra "bar, crossbar," of uncertain origin, possibly from Gaulish.
