mediocritization:/ˌmiːdiˈɑːkrɪtɪˌzeɪʃən/ | n. | 平庸化；庸俗化；中等化 | The process of becoming mediocre; a decline in quality or standards to a mediocre level.  | The mediocritization of education is a serious concern. 教育的平庸化是一个严重的问题。 | The mediocritization of art is often attributed to commercial pressures. 艺术的庸俗化通常归因于商业压力。 | 近义词：decline, deterioration, degeneration | 反义词：improvement, enhancement, elevation | 词性: noun

Etymology: From mediocre + -ization.  Mediocre comes from the Latin word "mediocris," meaning "middling," from "medius" (middle) + "ocris" (mountainous, rugged).  The suffix "-ization" denotes the process of making or becoming something.
