coulda:/ˈkʊdə/|modal verb (informal contraction)|可能已经，本来可以|Contraction of "could have". Used informally to express possibility in the past or a missed opportunity.|例句：I coulda gone to the party, but I was tired. 我本来可以去参加派对的，但我太累了。|近义词：could have, might have|反义词:couldn't have|词性:modal verb

虚拟语气:
N/A - while "could have" is used in the conditional mood, the informal "coulda" is generally avoided in these more complex grammatical constructions.

被动语态:
N/A - "Could have" can be used passively (e.g., "It could have been done differently"), but "coulda" is not used this way.

疑问句:

Coulda they done it any better? (informal) 他们本来可以做得更好吗？

否定句:
I couldn't a believed it if I hadn't seen it with my own eyes. (informal, double contraction - could not have) 如果我没有亲眼看到，我简直不敢相信。

PHRASE->
(Phrases with "coulda" are generally very informal and often involve other contractions)
You coulda told me! 你本来可以告诉我的!
I coulda swore I saw him there. 我本来发誓我看到他了.
He coulda done better. 他本来可以做得更好。
They coulda warned us. 他们本来可以警告我们的。
She coulda at least tried. 她至少可以尝试一下。


注意事项:
"Coulda" is extremely informal and should be avoided in formal writing or speech.  It represents a casual pronunciation and is not considered standard English. Its use might be perceived as uneducated or careless in formal contexts.
