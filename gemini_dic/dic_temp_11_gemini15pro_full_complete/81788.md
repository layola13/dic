grenade:/ɡrəˈneɪd/ | n. | 手榴弹; 榴弹 | A small bomb thrown by hand or launched mechanically. | The soldier pulled the pin and threw the grenade. 士兵拔掉保险销，扔出了手榴弹。|  bomb, explosive |  peace, treaty | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *grenade* (pomegranate), from Old Provençal *granada*, from Latin *grānātum* (having many seeds), neuter of *grānātus* (seeded), from *grānum* (grain, seed).  The weapon was so named because of its resemblance to a pomegranate, both in shape and in the scattering of its contents.
