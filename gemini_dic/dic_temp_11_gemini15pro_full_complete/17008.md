bata:/ˈbɑːtə/ | n. | 巴塔鞋，帆布鞋 | A type of canvas shoe, often rubber-soled, commonly worn in parts of Africa and South America, and formerly in Eastern Europe. It's derived from the Czech company Bata Shoes, which popularized the style. | Example: He wore his old bata shoes to the market. 他穿着他的旧巴塔鞋去市场。 | Example: Bata shoes are durable and affordable. 巴塔鞋耐穿且价格实惠。| Synonym: plimsolls, sneakers, canvas shoes | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but could be contrasted with) leather shoes, dress shoes | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the name of the Czech company Bata Shoes, founded by Tomáš Baťa in 1894.
