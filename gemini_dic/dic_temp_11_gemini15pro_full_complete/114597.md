词源:  From French *chauvinisme*, ultimately from Nicolas Chauvin, a legendary, excessively patriotic soldier in Napoleon's army. The term originally referred to excessive or blind patriotism, later broadening to include excessive belief in the superiority of one's own group, especially men over women.
