ETYMOLOGY->
"Down" and "out" are both common English words. "Down" meaning low in spirits or position and "out" meaning without, lacking. The combined phrase "down-and-out" emphasizes a state of complete destitution and hopelessness. It gained popularity in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, particularly in the context of economic hardship and the Great Depression.
