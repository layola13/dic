bedfellowship:/ˈbɛdˌfɛləʊʃɪp/| n.|伙伴关系，盟友关系；同床共枕|The state of being bedfellows; the sharing of a bed.  Figuratively, it refers to an alliance, especially one that is temporary or unexpected.|例句：Strange bedfellowship indeed – a socialist and a conservative working together.  真是奇怪的伙伴关系——一个社会主义者和一个保守派一起工作。|例句：Their bedfellowship in this project was born of necessity, not shared ideals.  他们在该项目中的合作是出于需要，而非共同的理想。|近义词：alliance, partnership, association, collaboration|反义词：enmity, antagonism, rivalry|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From bed + fellow + ship.  "Bedfellow" dates back to the late 14th century, meaning "one who shares a bed." "Bedfellowship" arose later, signifying the state of being bedfellows.  The figurative meaning of "alliance" emerged in the 16th century.
