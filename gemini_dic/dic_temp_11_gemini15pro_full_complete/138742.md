numbness:/ˈnʌmnɪs/| n.|麻木，麻痹|A lack of sensation in a part of the body, typically caused by cold or pressure.|My foot went to sleep and I experienced a tingling numbness. 我的脚麻了，感觉一阵刺痛的麻木。|Synonyms: insensibility, deadness, anesthesia, paralysis|Antonyms: sensation, feeling|noun

numbnesses:/ˈnʌmnɪsɪz/ |n. |麻木（numbness的复数形式）| Multiple instances of a lack of sensation in parts of the body.|He complained of numbnesses in his fingers and toes. 他抱怨说手指和脚趾麻木。|Synonyms: insensibilities, deadnesses, anesthesias |Antonyms: sensations, feelings|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *nome*, *numbe*, from Old English *numen* ("taken, seized, captured; numb, torpid"), from Proto-Germanic *numaną* ("to take, seize, receive"), from Proto-Indo-European *nem- ("to take, distribute, allot"). Related to German *nehmen* ("to take"). The ending *-ness* is a common suffix used to form nouns denoting a state or quality.
