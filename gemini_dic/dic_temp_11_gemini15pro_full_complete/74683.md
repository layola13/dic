freedom:/ˈfriːdəm/| n.|自由，自主，免除，特权|The state of not being imprisoned or enslaved.  The power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants without hindrances.|Birds symbolize freedom. 鸟象征着自由。|liberty, independence, autonomy|restriction, confinement, captivity|noun
etymology->
From Middle English *fredom*, from Old English *frēodōm* (“freedom, liberation, immunity, franchise, free will; generosity, liberality”), from Proto-Germanic *friþōmiz* (“freedom”), from Proto-Indo-European *prei- (“to love, to like”). Cognate with Old Frisian *fridom* (“freedom”), Old Saxon *friodom* (“freedom”), Old High German *frīheit* (“freedom”), Old Norse *frjáls* (“free”), Gothic *𐍆𐍂𐌴𐌹𐍃* (freis, “free”).  The meaning "political or national independence" is from late Middle English.
