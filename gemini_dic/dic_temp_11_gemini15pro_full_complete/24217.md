bridgehead:/ˈbrɪdʒˌhɛd/| n.|桥头堡，据点|A position secured in enemy territory from which an advance can be made.  A foothold.|例句：The troops established a bridgehead across the river. 部队在河对岸建立了桥头堡。

例句：The company's new product gave them a bridgehead in the competitive market.  公司的新产品让他们在竞争激烈的市场中获得了立足点。|近义词：foothold, base, lodgement|反义词:rear, retreat|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From bridge + head.  Originally referred to a fortified position protecting the end of a bridge.
