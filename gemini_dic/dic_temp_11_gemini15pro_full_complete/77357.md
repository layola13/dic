gaudygreen:/ˈɡɔːdi ɡriːn/
adjective
俗艳的绿色; 花里胡哨的绿色
A bright, showy, and often tasteless shade of green.  Characterized by being excessively bright, showy, or ornamented, typically in a way that is considered vulgar or tasteless.
例句：She wore a gaudygreen dress to the party, which clashed with everyone else's attire. 她穿了一件俗艳的绿色连衣裙去参加聚会，与其他人的着装格格不入。

例句：The gaudygreen decorations made the room look like a child's birthday party. 花里胡哨的绿色装饰使房间看起来像孩子的生日派对。

近义词：garish green, lurid green, flashy green
反义词：subtle green, muted green, understated green
词性：adjective

词源：
"Gaudy" comes from the Middle French "gaudir" meaning "to rejoice" or "be showy."  Over time, it evolved to describe something excessively ornamented. "Green" is a common color term with Germanic roots.
