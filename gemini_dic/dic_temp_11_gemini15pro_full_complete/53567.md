disbeliever:/ˌdɪsbɪˈliːvər/ | n. |不信者，怀疑者 |Someone who does not believe something, especially someone who does not believe in a particular religion. |例句：He was a disbeliever in organized religion.  他不相信任何有组织的宗教。

例句：The disbelievers scoffed at the idea of life after death.  不信者对死后生命的说法嗤之以鼻。|近义词：skeptic, doubter, cynic |反义词：believer, adherent, devotee |词性: 名词

Etymology:
From dis- + believer, from believe + -er.  "Dis-" is a prefix indicating negation or reversal. "Believe" comes from Old English "belīefan" meaning "to have confidence in."  "-er" is a suffix denoting a person who performs a particular action.
