counter-mart:/ˈkaʊntərˌmɑːrt/ | n. | 反超市，廉价超市 | A counter-mart is a smaller, less expensive grocery store, often specializing in basic staples and discounted items.  It's positioned as a counterpoint to larger, more conventional supermarkets. |  Example 1: The counter-mart down the street has great deals on canned goods. 街角那家廉价超市的罐头食品很划算。

Example 2: We saved a lot of money by shopping at the counter-mart instead of the big supermarket. 我们在廉价超市而不是大型超市购物，省了很多钱。| Synonym: discount grocery, budget grocery, no-frills grocery | Antonym: supermarket, hypermarket | noun
