interclusion:/ˌɪntərˈkluːʒən/ | n. | 阻生; 闭塞; 包含 | The act of shutting up or enclosing; the state of being shut up or enclosed.  The inclusion of something within something else. |  The dentist diagnosed the interclusion of the wisdom tooth.  牙医诊断出智齿阻生。| 近义词：inclusion, enclosure, occlusion | 反义词：exposure, release | 词性: 名词

词源: From Latin *interclusus, past participle of intercludere (“to shut in, enclose, block”), from inter- (“between, among, amid”) + claudere (“to shut, close”).
