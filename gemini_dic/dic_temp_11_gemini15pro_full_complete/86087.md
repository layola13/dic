hearses:/ˈhɜːrsɪz/ | n. |灵车；柩车 |A vehicle for conveying a coffin to a church or cemetery. |  The hearse slowly followed the procession to the graveyard. 灵车缓缓地跟着队伍前往墓地。

| Example: Two hearses were parked outside the funeral home. 两辆灵车停在殡仪馆外面。

| Synonyms: coffin carrier, funeral car, funeral coach | Antonyms: N/A | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French herse "harrow," later "framework for candles," then "catafalque," from Late Latin hercia "harrow." The meaning shifted to "carriage for a coffin" in the 17th century.
