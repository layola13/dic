distressed:/dɪˈstrɛst/ /dɪˈstrɛst/| adj.|痛苦的，忧虑的，困苦的，仿旧的|Suffering from anxiety, sorrow, or pain.  Also, (of things)  giving the appearance of being old and worn.|例句：The distressed family waited for news of their missing child.  焦急的一家人等待着失踪孩子的消息。

例句：She wore a distressed leather jacket. 她穿着一件仿旧的皮夹克。|近义词：upset, worried, anxious, troubled, suffering, poverty-stricken, shabby, worn|反义词：calm, peaceful, happy, content, prosperous, pristine|词性:形容词

词源: From Old French destresse, from Vulgar Latin *districtia, from Latin districtus, past participle of distringere "to draw tight, stretch out," from dis- "apart" + stringere "to draw tight." The original meaning referred to physical strain or suffering, and the sense of mental anguish developed later.
