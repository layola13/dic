nonbuttressed:/ˌnɒnˈbʌtrɪst/ | adj. | 无扶壁的，无支撑的 | Not supported by buttresses. A buttress is an architectural structure built against or projecting from a wall which serves to support or reinforce the wall.  | The nonbuttressed wall eventually crumbled under the pressure.  这面没有扶壁的墙最终在压力下倒塌了。 | unsupported, unreinforced | buttressed, supported | adjective

Etymology->

From non- + buttressed, the past participle of buttress.  "Buttress" comes from Old French "boterez," a plural noun meaning "flying buttresses."  This ultimately derives from "bouter," meaning "to thrust, push."
