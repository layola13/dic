bifurcately: /baɪˈfɜːrkɪtli/ /baɪˈfɝːkɪtli/
| adv. |分叉地，成两叉地|In a bifurcated manner; dividing into two branches or forks. |The river bifurcately flowed around the island. 这条河分叉流经小岛。| The path bifurcately split into two trails leading up the mountain. 这条小路分叉成两条小径通向山上。|Synonyms: forkedly, branching, diverging|Antonyms: converging, joining, merging|词性:副词

bifurcate: /ˈbaɪfərˌkeɪt/ /ˈbaɪfɝːˌkeɪt/
| v. |分叉，分成两支|To divide into two branches or forks.|The road bifurcates at the next intersection. 这条路在下一个十字路口分叉。|The river bifurcates several times before reaching the sea. 这条河在入海前分叉了好几次。|Synonyms: fork, divide, split|Antonyms: converge, join, merge|词性:动词

bifurcation: /ˌbaɪfərˈkeɪʃən/ /ˌbaɪfɝːˈkeɪʃən/
| n. |分叉，分歧|The point or action of forking or dividing into two branches.|The bifurcation of the road led to two different destinations. 道路的分叉通向两个不同的目的地。|The bifurcation in the party created two opposing factions.  党内的分歧造成了两个对立的派别。|Synonyms: fork, division, split|Antonyms: convergence, junction, merger|词性:名词
