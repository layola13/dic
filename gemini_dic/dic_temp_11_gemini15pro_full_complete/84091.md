half-ingenuously:/ˌhɑːf ɪnˈdʒenjuəsli/
| adv. | 半坦率地，半真诚地 | Appearing or claiming to be ingenuous, but not entirely so.  Meaning something said or done is only partly innocent or naive, often suggesting a hidden motive or awareness. | He half-ingenuously confessed that he had lost the money. 他半真半假地承认他把钱弄丢了。|  He half-ingenuously asked if she was seeing anyone else, knowing full well she was. 他明知故问她是否跟别人约会，装作天真烂漫的样子。 | Synonyms: artfully, slyly, dissemblingly | Antonyms: frankly, honestly, openly | adverb
