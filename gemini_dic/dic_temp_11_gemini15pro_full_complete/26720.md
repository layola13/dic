cadew: /ˈkæd.uː/ (Welsh pronunciation)
noun
gift; present
A cadew is something given voluntarily without payment in return, often to express affection or celebrate a special occasion.
Example: He gave her a cadew for her birthday. 他送给她一份生日礼物。

Example: The children were excited to open their cadeaux on Christmas morning. 孩子们很兴奋地在圣诞节早晨打开他们的礼物。

Synonyms: present, gift, offering
Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a gift, perhaps consider:  taking, receiving, purchase)
词性: 名词

词源:
From Welsh *cadew, from cadw (“to keep, preserve”).  The original sense was "something kept, something precious".  Over time, the meaning shifted to "something given as a token of affection or esteem".
