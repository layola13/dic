modernisation:/ˌmɒdərnɪˈzeɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌmɑːdərnɪˈzeɪʃn/ (US)| n.|现代化|The process of adapting something to modern needs or standards, by making improvements and updating it.|例句：The modernisation of the factory increased productivity. 工厂的现代化提高了生产力。

例句：The government invested heavily in the modernisation of the railway system. 政府大力投资铁路系统的现代化。|近义词：updating, upgrading, improvement, development, innovation|反义词：stagnation, obsolescence, decline, regression|词性:noun


modernize:/ˈmɒdənaɪz/ (UK) /ˈmɑːdərnaɪz/ (US)| v.|使现代化|To make something more modern by adopting new methods, ideas, or equipment.|例句：They plan to modernize the old telephone system. 他们计划对旧的电话系统进行现代化改造。

例句：The company modernized its production line to improve efficiency.  公司对其生产线进行了现代化改造以提高效率。|近义词：update, upgrade, improve, develop, innovate|反义词：preserve, maintain, keep, retain|词性:verb


modern:/ˈmɒdən/ (UK) /ˈmɑːdərn/ (US)| adj.|现代的|Relating to the present time, or recent times; not ancient or traditional.|例句：Modern technology has made our lives easier. 现代科技使我们的生活更加便捷。

例句：We live in a modern society. 我们生活在一个现代社会。|近义词：contemporary, current, present-day, up-to-date|反义词：ancient, old, traditional, outdated|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *modernisation*, from moderniser, from moderne (“modern”), from Late Latin modernus, from Latin modo (“just now, recently”).
