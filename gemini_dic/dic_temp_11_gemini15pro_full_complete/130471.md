nonabstracted:/ˌnɒnæbˈstræktɪd/ | adj. | 非抽象的，具体的，未被概括的 | Not abstracted; concrete; specific.  Existing in a physical or concrete form; not summarized or generalized. | The artist preferred nonabstracted paintings, focusing on realistic depictions of everyday life.  这位艺术家更喜欢非抽象的绘画，专注于对日常生活的逼真描绘。 | concrete, specific, tangible, real, literal | abstract, theoretical, conceptual | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + abstracted.  "Abstracted" comes from the Latin "abstractus," the past participle of "abstrahere," meaning "to draw away."  The prefix "non-" simply negates the meaning of "abstracted."
