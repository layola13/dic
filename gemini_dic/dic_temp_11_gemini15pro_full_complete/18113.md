behest:/bɪˈhest/| n.| 命令，指示，要求|A person's behest is an order or command given by that person. |The soldiers carried out the general's behest. 士兵们执行了将军的命令。|近义词：command, order, instruction, directive|反义词：request, plea|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *beheste*, from Old English *behǣs* (“vow, promise, command”), from *be-*(“by, about”) + *hǣs* (“command, behest”), from Proto-Germanic *haisaz* (“command, order”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱeyH-* (“to incite, set in motion”). Cognate with Old Frisian *hest* (“command”), Middle Dutch *heet* (“command”), Dutch *heet* (“command”), Old High German *heiz* (“command”), German *Geheiß* (“command”), Gothic *haitan* (“to call, command”).
