canoness:/ˈkænənɪs/| n. | 女修道院院长; 女教士 | A woman who is the head of a community of nuns, especially in certain Roman Catholic orders. |  The canoness presided over the ceremony. 女修道院院长主持了仪式。| Synonyms: prioress, abbess | Antonyms: monk, priest | Noun

Etymology: From Middle English *canonesse*, from Old French *canonesse*, feminine form of *canone*, from Late Latin *canonicus* (“canon”), from Greek *κανόνικος* (kanonikos, “according to rule”), from *κανών* (kanōn, “rule, canon”).
