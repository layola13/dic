laboring:/ˈleɪbə(r)ɪŋ/ | adj. | 辛苦工作的，费力的 | working hard, especially physically |  The laboring classes were hit hardest by the recession. 劳动阶级受经济衰退的打击最大。| 近义词: toiling, struggling, striving | 反义词: resting, relaxing | 词性: 形容词

laboring:/ˈleɪbə(r)ɪŋ/ | v. |  labored的现在分词形式，辛苦工作，费力地做 | present participle of "labor," meaning to work hard, especially physically  | He was laboring under the misapprehension that he was popular. 他误以为自己很受欢迎，因而费尽心思。 | 近义词: toiling, struggling, striving | 反义词: resting, relaxing | 词性: 动词

词源: From Middle English *labouren, *laburen, from Old French labourer (“to till, cultivate, plough”), from Latin labōrāre (“to work, toil, exert oneself”), from labor (“work, toil, hardship”).
