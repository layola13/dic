insinuation:/ɪnˌsɪn.juˈeɪ.ʃən/ | n. | 暗示；含沙射影；影射 |An indirect and usually unpleasant hint or suggestion of something bad. | He resented the insinuation that he had lied. 他对别人暗示他撒谎感到愤慨。 | synonyms: implication, suggestion, hint, innuendo, allusion | antonyms: direct statement, explicit declaration | noun

insinuate:/ɪnˈsɪn.ju.eɪt/ | v. | 暗示；含沙射影；影射 | To suggest or hint something bad or unpleasant in an indirect and subtle way. | She insinuated that he was not telling the truth. 她暗示他没有说实话。 | synonyms: imply, suggest, hint, intimate | antonyms: state directly, declare explicitly | verb
