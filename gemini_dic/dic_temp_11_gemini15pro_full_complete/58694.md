dumpily:/ˈdʌmpɪli/| adv.|矮胖地，笨拙地|In a short, thick, and clumsy manner.  Describes actions or movements, not physical appearance itself.|He walked dumpily across the room, his shoulders hunched. 他笨拙地走过房间，肩膀弓着。|近义词：clumsily, awkwardly|反义词：gracefully, elegantly|词性: 副词

Etymology: From the adjective "dumpy" + "-ly" (adverbial suffix). "Dumpy" originated in the late 18th century, possibly a variant of "dump," referring to something short and thick.
