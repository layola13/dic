billionaire:/ˌbɪljəˈnɛər/ | n. | 亿万富翁，巨富 | A person who has assets worth at least one billion units of a given currency (e.g., dollars, euros, etc.). |  Example 1: The billionaire invested in renewable energy.  这位亿万富翁投资了可再生能源。

Example 2:  Many billionaires donate to charity. 许多亿万富翁向慈善机构捐款。 | Synonym: multimillionaire, tycoon, magnate | Antonym: pauper | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From billion + -aire (a suffix borrowed from French, denoting a person connected with something). Billion itself comes from French billion, from bi- (meaning "two") + million.  Originally, a billion meant a million million (1,000,000,000,000) in British English, but is now standardized as a thousand million (1,000,000,000).
