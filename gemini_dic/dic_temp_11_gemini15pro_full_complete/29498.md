cataractic:/ˌkætəˈræktɪk/| adj.|瀑布般的；白内障的；激烈的；突发的|Relating to or resembling a cataract, especially in being a sudden, overwhelming downpour or rush.  Can also refer to a medical cataract.|The rain was cataractic, making driving nearly impossible.  暴雨如注，几乎无法开车。|近义词：torrential, overwhelming, cascading, flooding|反义词：trickling, drizzling, gentle|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Late Latin *cataracta*, from Greek *kataraktēs* "down-rushing," from *katarassein* "to dash down," from *kata-* "down" + *arassein* "to strike, dash."
