bluster:/ˈblʌstər/| n. vi. vt.|咆哮，怒吼；狂风；喧闹|a loud, aggressive, or indignant talk or roar; to roar, as wind; to be loud, noisy, or swaggering; to utter with noisy threats|The wind blustered through the trees. 风咆哮着穿过树林。|近义词：roar, bellow, rant, rave, storm|反义词：whisper, murmur, be quiet|词性:名词, 动词

bluster:/ˈblʌstər/| v. |咆哮，怒吼；虚张声势|to talk in a loud, aggressive, or indignant way with little effect; to act in a noisy, swaggering manner|He blustered about how he would get revenge, but everyone knew it was empty talk.  他咆哮着说要报仇，但大家都知道那只是空话。|近义词：roar, bellow, rant, rave, storm|反义词：whisper, murmur, be quiet|词性:动词

Etymology:  From Middle English *blustren, probably of imitative origin.
