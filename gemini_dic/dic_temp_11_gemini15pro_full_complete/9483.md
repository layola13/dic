antiritualisms:/ˌæntiˈrɪtʃuəlɪzəmz/
| n. |反儀式主義，反禮儀主義|Opposition or aversion to rituals, especially in religion or social life.  The rejection of established forms or ceremonies.|The growing antiritualisms of the younger generation is challenging traditional practices. 年轻一代日益增长的反仪式主义正在挑战传统习俗。|近义词：iconoclasm, nonconformity, unorthodoxy|反义词: ritualism, formalism, traditionalism|词性:noun

Etymology:
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to (from Greek)
ritual (noun): a religious or solemn ceremony consisting of a series of actions performed according to a prescribed order. (from Latin ritualis, "relating to rites")
-ism (suffix):  a distinctive practice, system, or philosophy, typically a political ideology or an artistic movement.
