cognizable:/ˈkɒɡnɪzəbl/ (US) /ˈkɑːɡnɪzəbl/| adj.|可认知的；可认识的；（案件）可受理的|Something that is cognizable is perceptible, noticeable, or able to be known or understood, especially by the mind or senses. In legal contexts, it refers to a case that can be legally tried or examined by a court.|The change in her demeanor was barely cognizable. 她神态的变化几乎难以察觉。|Synonyms: perceptible, discernible, noticeable, appreciable, detectable|Antonyms: imperceptible, indiscernible, unnoticeable, impalpable|adjective


cognizable:/ˈkɒɡnɪzəbl/ (US) /ˈkɑːɡnɪzəbl/| adj.|（罪行）可由治安法官审理的|In law, a cognizable offense is one that can be tried by a magistrate or judge without needing a grand jury to indict the accused.|Theft is a cognizable offense. 盗窃罪是可由治安法官审理的罪行。|Synonyms: actionable, triable, justiciable|Antonyms: non-cognizable|adjective
