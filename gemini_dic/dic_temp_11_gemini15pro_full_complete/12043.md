articulatenesses:/ɑrˌtɪkjʊlətˈnɛsɪz/
|n.|清晰度，表达能力|The articulateness of her speech impressed everyone. 她的讲话清晰度给每个人留下了深刻的印象。|The lawyer presented his case with great articulateness. 律师非常清晰地陈述了他的案件。|近义词：eloquence, fluency, clarity|反义词：inarticulacy, incoherence|词性:名词

articulate:/ɑrˈtɪkjʊlət/
|adj.|清晰的，善于表达的|She gave a very articulate presentation. 她做了一个非常清晰的演讲。|He is an articulate and persuasive speaker. 他是一位口齿伶俐、有说服力的演说家。|近义词：eloquent, fluent, clear|反义词：inarticulate, incoherent|词性:形容词

articulate:/ɑrˈtɪkjʊlət/
|v.|清晰地表达，清楚地吐字|She articulated her thoughts clearly and concisely. 她清晰而简明地表达了她的想法。|He struggled to articulate his feelings. 他难以表达自己的感受。|近义词：express, pronounce, enunciate|反义词：mumble, slur|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin articulatus, past participle of articulare (“to utter distinctly, joint”), from Latin articulus (“a joint, a small connecting part”), diminutive of artus (“joint, limb”). The notion of “jointed” is related to distinctness of pronunciation.
