cauterised:/ˈkɔːtəraɪzd/| adj.|烧灼的，烙的|Burned to prevent infection or bleeding, typically using a hot iron or electric current.|The wound was cauterised to stop the bleeding.  伤口被烧灼以止血。|Synonyms:  seared, branded, burned|Antonyms:  unburned, untreated|adjective

cauterise:/ˈkɔːtəraɪz/| v.|烧灼，烙|To burn (a wound or body tissue) to stop bleeding or prevent infection.|The doctor cauterised the wound. 医生烧灼了伤口。|Synonyms: sear, brand, burn|Antonyms: heal|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French cauteriser, from late Latin cauterizare, from Greek kauteriazein, from kautērion ‘branding iron’, from kaiein ‘to burn’.
