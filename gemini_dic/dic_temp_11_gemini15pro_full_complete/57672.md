drawhead: /ˈdrɔːhɛd/ (US), /ˈdrɔːhed/ (UK)| n.|牵引头，车钩|A coupling device at the front of a locomotive or railcar used to connect it to other rolling stock.  It absorbs pushing and pulling forces between rail vehicles and transmits these forces to the car body.|The drawhead of the locomotive was damaged in the collision. 火车头的牵引头在碰撞中受损了。|The train was delayed because the drawhead failed. 火车延误是因为牵引头故障了。|Synonyms: coupler, coupling|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
