mezzotinto:/ˌmetsəˈtɪntoʊ/ | n. | 铜版腐蚀制版法；(用此法制成的)版画 | A method of engraving on copper or steel by roughening the entire surface and then scraping away the roughness where light tones are desired.  A print made by this process. | Example 1: The artist created a stunning mezzotinto portrait of his mother.  这位艺术家创作了一幅令人惊叹的母亲肖像铜版画。

Example 2: Mezzotint is known for its rich, velvety blacks. 铜版腐蚀制版法以其丰富的、天鹅绒般的黑色而闻名。 | synonym: engraving, etching | antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "lithograph" as a different printmaking method) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *mezzotinto* "half-tinted," from *mezzo* "middle, half" + *tinto* "tinted," past participle of *tingere* "to dye, tint."  The term describes the tonal range achieved in the process.
