iniquity: /ɪˈnɪkwɪti/ | n. | 邪恶，罪恶，不公正 | Immoral or grossly unfair behavior. |  The iniquity of the slave trade is undeniable. 奴隶贸易的罪恶是不可否认的。

The judge condemned the iniquity of the defendant's actions. 法官谴责了被告行为的邪恶。

| 近义词: wickedness, sin, evil, injustice, wrongdoing | 反义词: righteousness, virtue, justice, goodness | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *iniquité*  (Modern French *iniquité*), from Latin *iniquitās*, from *inīquus* (“unequal, unjust”), from *in-* (“not”) + *aequus* (“equal, level, just”).
