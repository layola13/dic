lobbyists:/ˈlɒbiɪsts/ (UK) /ˈlɑːbiɪsts/ (US)| n. |说客，游说者|People who try to influence legislation or government policy on behalf of a particular group or cause.|  Lobbyists spent millions trying to influence the outcome of the election. 说客们花费了数百万美元试图影响选举结果。|
The lobbyists worked tirelessly to persuade senators to vote in their favor.  这些说客不知疲倦地游说参议员们投票支持他们。 | synonyms: advocates, campaigners, influencers, pressure groups | antonyms: opponents, adversaries | noun

词源:
From lobby + -ist.  "Lobby" originally referred to the entrance hall of a building where people could meet and discuss matters. Over time, it came to mean attempting to influence legislators in such spaces. The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who practices or engages in a particular activity.
