masqueraded:/ˌmæskəˈreɪdɪd/
|v.|伪装的，乔装的|appeared or behaved in a way that was intended to deceive people about one's true identity or intentions|He masqueraded as a waiter to get into the party. 他伪装成服务员混进了派对。|近义词：disguised, posed, impersonated|反义词:revealed, unmasked|词性:verb (past tense of masquerade)

masquerade:/ˌmæskəˈreɪd/
|n.|伪装，化装舞会|an action or appearance that is designed to deceive someone; a ball at which masks and other disguises are worn.|The charity ball was a masquerade, with everyone in elaborate costumes.  慈善舞会是一个化装舞会，每个人都穿着精致的服装。|近义词：disguise, pretense, charade|反义词:reality, truth|词性:noun

masquerade:/ˌmæskəˈreɪd/
|v.|伪装，乔装|pretend to be someone one is not|She masqueraded as a doctor to gain access to the hospital. 她伪装成医生以进入医院。|近义词：pose, impersonate, feign|反义词:reveal, unmask|词性:verb
