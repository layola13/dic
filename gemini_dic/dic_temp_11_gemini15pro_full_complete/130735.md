nonambiguity:/ˌnɒnæmbɪˈɡjuːɪti/ | n. | 无歧义性，清晰性 | The nonambiguity of the instructions was crucial for the success of the experiment.  The nonambiguity of the contract terms prevented any misunderstandings. | clarity, precision, explicitness | ambiguity, vagueness | noun

Etymology:
From non- + ambiguity.  Ambiguity comes from Late Latin ambiguitās, from ambiguus "doubtful," from ambigere "to be undecided," from ambi- "around" + agere "to drive, lead, act."
