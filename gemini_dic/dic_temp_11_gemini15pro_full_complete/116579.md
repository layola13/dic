matriarchalisms:/ˌmeɪtriˈɑːrkəlɪzəmz/ | n. | 母权制；母系社会 |  The doctrines or principles of a social system in which the mother or a female elder is head of the family or tribe, and descent and kinship are reckoned through the female line.
