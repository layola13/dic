crystallized:/ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 结晶的；明确的；固定的 |  Formed into crystals; made clear and definite; fixed and settled. | The honey had crystallized in the jar.  蜂蜜在罐子里结晶了。| The plan crystallized in her mind.  这个计划在她脑海中逐渐清晰。 | 近义词: solidified, concretized, defined, settled | 反义词: fluid, amorphous, vague, unsettled | 词性: 形容词

crystallize:/ˈkrɪstəlaɪz/ | v. | 结晶；使明确；使固定 |  To cause to form crystals or assume crystalline form; to make something clear and definite; to cause something to become fixed and settled. |  The cold temperatures caused the water to crystallize into ice. 低温使水结晶成冰。|  The discussion helped to crystallize his thoughts. 讨论使他的想法更加明确。 | 近义词: solidify, concretize, define, settle | 反义词: melt, dissolve, obscure, destabilize | 词性: 动词

词源: From French *cristallisé*, past participle of *cristalliser*, from Medieval Latin *crystallisare*, from Latin *crystallus* "crystal," from Greek *krystallos* "ice, crystal."
