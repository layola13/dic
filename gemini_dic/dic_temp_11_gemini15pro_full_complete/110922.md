lipoprotein:/ˌlɪpoʊˈproʊtiːn/, /ˌlɪpəˈprəʊtiːn/ | n. | 脂蛋白 |  A biochemical assembly that contains both proteins and lipids, bound to the proteins, which allow fats to move through the water inside and outside cells. The lipids may be triglycerides, phospholipids, cholesterol, or cholesterol esters. | High levels of certain lipoproteins are associated with an increased risk of atherosclerosis. 高水平的某些脂蛋白与动脉粥样硬化的风险增加有关。|  Lipoproteins transport cholesterol and other lipids in the blood. 脂蛋白在血液中转运胆固醇和其他脂质。 | synonym: lipid-protein complex | antonym:  (No direct antonym, but a deficiency might be considered an opposite state) | noun
