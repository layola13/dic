forfeiting:/ˈfɔːrfɪtɪŋ/
n.|丧失，罚款，没收; 放弃|The act of losing or surrendering something as a penalty for a crime, fault, or breach of contract.  It can also refer to the financing technique where an exporter sells their receivables to a third party at a discount.|He risked forfeiting his property. 他冒着丧失财产的风险。|Synonyms: loss, surrender, sacrifice, relinquishment|Antonyms: gain, acquisition, retention|noun

forfeit:/ˈfɔːrfɪt/
n.|罚金，没收物|Something that is lost or surrendered as a penalty.|He had to pay a forfeit for breaking the rules. 他因违反规定而不得不支付罚款。|Synonyms: penalty, fine, mulct|Antonyms: reward, prize|noun
v.|丧失，被没收|Lose or be deprived of (property or a right or privilege) as a penalty for wrongdoing.|He forfeited his right to a trial. 他丧失了受审的权利。|Synonyms: lose, surrender, sacrifice|Antonyms: gain, acquire, retain|verb
adj.|丧失了的，被没收的|Lost or surrendered as a penalty for wrongdoing.|His claim was forfeit. 他的主张被没收了。|Synonyms: lost, surrendered, sacrificed|Antonyms: gained, acquired, retained|adjective
