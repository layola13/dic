jeffersonian: /ˌdʒɛfərˈsoʊniən/ | adj. n. |杰斐逊主义的；杰斐逊主义者|Relating to or characteristic of Thomas Jefferson, his political philosophy, or his era; a supporter of Thomas Jefferson or his principles.  |  Example: He admired the Jeffersonian ideals of limited government and individual liberty.  他钦佩杰斐逊关于有限政府和个人自由的理想。

|Example:  She considered herself a Jeffersonian Democrat. 她认为自己是一位杰斐逊主义的民主党人。| 近义词: democratic, republican (in the historical sense) | 反义词: federalist, authoritarian | 词性: 形容词, 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
Derived from the name of Thomas Jefferson (1743-1826), the third president of the United States. The suffix "-ian" is used to form adjectives and nouns denoting affiliation or adherence to a person, place, or doctrine.
