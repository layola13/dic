monopoler:/məˈnɒpələr/ | n. | 垄断者，独占者 | A person or company that has a monopoly.  Someone who controls the entire supply of a particular commodity or service in a given market. |  Example 1: The monopoler controlled the price of diamonds.  这名垄断者控制着钻石的价格。

Example 2: The government investigated the monopoler for unfair business practices. 政府调查了该垄断者是否存在不正当商业行为。 |  Near Synonyms: monopolist, trust, cartel | Antonyms: competitor, rival | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "mono-" (Greek, meaning "one, single") + "-pole" (from Greek "polein," meaning "to sell") + "-er" (agent suffix).  Essentially, "one seller."
