disfranchisement:/dɪsˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/| n. |剥夺权利，褫夺公权|The act of depriving someone of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote. |Example: The disfranchisement of black voters in the South was a major civil rights issue. 南方黑人选民的被剥夺选举权是一个主要的民权问题。

Example:  The new law resulted in the disfranchisement of many ex-convicts. 新法律导致许多有前科的人被剥夺了公民权。|近义词：disenfranchisement, deprivation, forfeiture|反义词:enfranchisement, empowerment|词性:名词


disfranchise:/dɪsˈfræntʃaɪz/| vt. |剥夺权利，褫夺公权|To deprive someone of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote. |Example:  The government disfranchised all citizens who opposed the new regime. 政府剥夺了所有反对新政权的公民的权利。

Example:  Many felt disfranchised by the new voting laws. 许多人感到新的选举法剥夺了他们的权利。|近义词：disenfranchise, deprive, strip|反义词: enfranchise, empower|词性:动词
