irascibly:/ɪˈræsɪbli/ | adv. | 易怒地，暴躁地 | Characterized by or arising from anger easily provoked; irritable. | He irascibly slammed the door and stomped up the stairs. 他暴躁地摔门，跺着脚上了楼。| Example:  The old man irascibly shooed the children away from his lawn. 老人怒气冲冲地把孩子们从他的草坪上赶走。| Synonyms: angrily, irritably, cantankerously, churlishly, peevishly | Antonyms: calmly, patiently, placidly, affably | word property: adverb

irascible:/ɪˈræsɪbl/ | adj. | 易怒的，暴躁的 | Having or showing a tendency to be easily angered. | My grandfather becomes increasingly irascible as he gets older. 我的祖父随着年龄的增长越来越易怒。| Example:  She was known for her irascible temper and sharp tongue. 她以脾气暴躁和说话刻薄而闻名。| Synonyms: irritable, short-tempered, touchy, testy, choleric | Antonyms: calm, patient, good-natured, even-tempered | word property: adjective


Etymology:
From Late Latin *irascibilis, from Latin irasci (“to grow angry”), from ira (“anger”).
