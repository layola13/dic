ill-informed:/ˌɪlɪnˈfɔːrmd/ | adj. | 消息不灵通的；缺乏信息的；无知的 | Not having enough information or knowledge to make good judgments; poorly informed. |  Many people are ill-informed about the dangers of smoking. 很多人对吸烟的危害缺乏了解。

The politician made an ill-informed decision based on inaccurate data. 这位政治家根据不准确的数据做出了一个不明智的决定。 | 近义词: misinformed, uninformed, ignorant, uneducated | 反义词: well-informed, knowledgeable, educated, informed | 词性: 形容词

ill-: /ɪl/ | prefix |  badly; wrongly; un-; not | 糟糕地；错误地；不；非 |  ill-advised (不明智的), ill-conceived (构思欠佳的), ill-fated (不幸的) |词性: 前缀

-informed: /ɪnˈfɔːrmd/ | suffix | having or showing knowledge or information about something | 了解…的；对…知情的 |  well-informed (消息灵通的), informed decision (明智的决定) | 词性: 后缀

词源：
From ill + informed.  "Informed" comes from the Old French "enformer" meaning "to train, instruct, teach." The prefix "ill-" adds the sense of "badly" or "wrongly" to the meaning of "informed."
