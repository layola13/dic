near-miss:/nɪr ˈmɪs/| n.|侥幸脱险，险些发生的意外|An event or situation that almost caused an accident or failure but did not.  A near-miss serves as a warning of potential danger.|The car swerved at the last minute, avoiding a near-miss with a pedestrian. 汽车在最后一刻突然转向，避免了与行人险些发生的碰撞。|near thing, close call|accident, disaster|noun


near-miss:/nɪr ˈmɪs/| adj.|险些发生的，差一点发生的|Describing an event or situation that almost caused an accident or failure but didn't.|It was a near-miss situation; the plane almost crashed. 这是一次险些发生的事故；飞机差点坠毁。|near, close|far, distant|adjective
