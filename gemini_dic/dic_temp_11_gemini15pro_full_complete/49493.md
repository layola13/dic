词源: From Latin dēlīrium (“going out of the furrow, madness”), from dē (“out of, away from”) + līra (“furrow, ridge between furrows”).  Originally an agricultural metaphor, referring to a plow going out of the furrow.


注意事项:
"Delyria" is an archaic plural form of "delirium".  It is rarely used in modern English.  The singular "delirium" is the standard and preferred form.
