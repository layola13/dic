hackles:/ˈhækəlz/ (US), /ˈhækəlz/ (UK)| n. |颈毛；(因恐惧或愤怒而竖起的)颈背部的毛；|The hairs on the back of an animal's neck, especially those of a dog, that are erected when the animal is angry or frightened. |The dog's hackles rose when the stranger approached.  这只狗在陌生人靠近时竖起了颈毛。|Synonyms: fur, hair, bristles|Antonyms: None (lack of hackles isn't a directly opposing concept)|Noun


hackles:/ˈhækəlz/ (US), /ˈhækəlz/ (UK)| n. |(比喻)怒气，敌意；|A feeling of anger and animosity. (Figurative usage, often used in the phrase "make someone's hackles rise")|His condescending tone made my hackles rise. 他居高临下的语气让我很生气。|Synonyms: ire, resentment, animosity|Antonyms: calmness, composure, friendliness|Noun
