cleopatra: /ˌkliːəˈpætrə/ /ˌkliːəˈpɑːtrə/ | n. | 克利奥帕特拉（埃及艳后） |  The name "Cleopatra" comes from the Greek name Κλεοπάτρα (Kleopatra), meaning "glory of the father".  It refers specifically to Cleopatra VII Philopator (69-30 BC), the last active ruler of the Ptolemaic Kingdom of Egypt.  She is famous for her relationships with Julius Caesar and Mark Antony, and her role in the Roman civil wars. | Cleopatra was a powerful and intelligent queen.  克利奥帕特拉是一位强大而聪慧的女王。

Cleopatra's beauty was legendary.  克利奥帕特拉的美貌是传奇。| 近义词: queen, ruler | 反义词: (No direct antonym for a proper noun) | 词性: noun
