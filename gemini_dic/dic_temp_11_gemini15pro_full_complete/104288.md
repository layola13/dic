justicoat: /ˈdʒʌstɪˌkəʊt/
n.
正义外套; 公正之袍; 法袍 (figurative, humorous)
A justicoat refers to a judge's robe, often used humorously or satirically to represent the judiciary or the concept of justice.  It blends "justice" and "coat."  There's no established etymology beyond this obvious portmanteau.

The satirical newspaper depicted the politician as tripping over his own justicoat in his eagerness to condemn the opposition.  这家讽刺报纸把这位政客描绘成急于谴责反对派，结果被自己的法袍绊倒了。

The blogger jokingly referred to the internet forum moderators as the "justicoats of the digital realm."  这位博主开玩笑地将互联网论坛版主称为“数字领域的正义使者”。


近义词: robe, gown (referring to the garment itself), judge (referring to the person wearing it)
反义词:  (No direct antonym; one might consider concepts like "injustice" or "tyranny" as opposing concepts, but not direct antonyms for the word itself)
词性: noun
