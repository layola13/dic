anchoret:/ˈæŋkərɪt/ |n.|隐士，遁世者|A person who lives in seclusion, typically for religious reasons.  A hermit. |The anchoret lived in a small cave, praying and meditating.| 近义词:hermit, recluse, ascetic|反义词:socialite, extrovert|名词

词源:
From Middle English *ancrete, from Old French ancrete, from Late Latin anachōrēta, from Ancient Greek ἀναχωρητής (anachōrētḗs, “one who retires, recluse”), from ἀναχωρέω (anachōréō, “I withdraw, retire”), from ἀνά (aná, “up, back”) + χωρέω (chōréō, “I go, depart”).
