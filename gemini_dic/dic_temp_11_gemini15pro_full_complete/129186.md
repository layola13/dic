nobel:/ˈnoʊbəl/| adj. n.|高尚的，贵族的；诺贝尔奖|Of a noble or elevated character; impressive and magnificent.  Relating to Alfred Nobel or the prizes established by him. |The king displayed a noble gesture by pardoning the thief. 国王展现了高尚的姿态，赦免了小偷。|Synonyms: honorable, virtuous, dignified, aristocratic, stately, grand, magnificent|Antonyms: ignoble, base, vile, common, vulgar|adjective, noun

nobel:/ˈnoʊbəl/| n.|诺贝尔奖获得者|A person who has been awarded a Nobel Prize. |Marie Curie was a Nobel in both Physics and Chemistry.  玛丽·居里是物理学和化学领域的诺贝尔奖获得者。|Synonyms: Nobel laureate, Nobel Prize winner|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *nobele (unattested), from Latin nobilis (“well-known, famous, celebrated, high-born, of noble birth”), from noscere (“to come to know”). Doublet of noble.
