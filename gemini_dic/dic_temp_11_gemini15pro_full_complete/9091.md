antimodern:/ˌæntiˈmɑːdərn/ |/ˌæntiˈmɒdərn/ | adj. |反现代的；反对现代化的| Opposed to or rejecting modernism, often favoring traditional values and practices. | The antimodern movement gained traction in the early 20th century. 反现代主义运动在20世纪初获得了发展。| His antimodern sentiments were evident in his writings. 他的反现代情绪在他的作品中很明显。| 近义词：traditional, conservative |反义词: modern, progressive | adjective


Etymology:
anti- (prefix) : against, opposed to (from Greek "anti-")
modern (adjective/noun): relating to the present or recent times as opposed to the remote past (from Old French "moderne," ultimately from Latin "modernus")
