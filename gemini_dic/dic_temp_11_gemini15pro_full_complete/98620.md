instrumentalism:/ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəlɪzəm/
n.|工具主义，实用主义|A doctrine that ideas are instruments or tools for action rather than accurate representations of reality; the view that the value of scientific theories is determined by their usefulness rather than their truth or falsity.|Many scientists adopt an instrumentalist view of scientific theories, valuing their predictive power over their correspondence to reality.|近义词：pragmatism, utilitarianism|反义词：realism, idealism|词性:noun


Etymology:
From instrument + -al + -ism.  "instrument" comes from Old French instrument, from Latin instrumentum "tool, implement, instrument,"  "-al" forms adjectives from nouns, and "-ism" forms nouns meaning a doctrine, belief, or practice.
