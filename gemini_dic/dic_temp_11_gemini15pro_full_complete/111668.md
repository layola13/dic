lock-down: /ˈlɒkdaʊn/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkdaʊn/ (US) | n. | 封锁，禁闭 |A state of isolation or restricted access imposed as a security measure or to control the spread of something, typically a disease. | The government imposed a strict lockdown to control the spread of the virus. 政府实施了严格的封锁以控制病毒的传播。| 近义词：quarantine, isolation, confinement, shutdown | 反义词：opening, reopening, freedom | 词性: 名词

lock-down: /ˈlɒkdaʊn/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkdaʊn/ (US) | adj. | 封锁的，禁闭的 | Relating to or denoting a state of isolation or restricted access. | The lockdown measures were difficult for many businesses.  封锁措施对许多企业来说很困难。 | 近义词：confined, restricted, isolated | 反义词：open, free, unrestricted | 词性: 形容词


词源：Compound of "lock" and "down."  "Lock" refers to securing something, while "down" emphasizes the restrictive nature of the action.
