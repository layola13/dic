chilopod:/ˈkaɪləpɒd/ (US) /ˈtʃɪləpɒd/ (UK)| n. | 蜈蚣 | A predatory myriapod invertebrate with a flattened body composed of numerous segments, each bearing a single pair of legs.  A centipede. |  例句：The chilopod darted across the forest floor.  蜈蚣飞快地穿过森林地面。

例句：She recoiled in disgust at the sight of the chilopod. 她一看到蜈蚣就厌恶地退缩了。| 近义词：centipede | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

Etymology: From New Latin Chilopoda, from Greek kheilos "lip" + pous "foot."  The "lip-foot" refers to the modified pair of legs on the first body segment that are used as venomous fangs.
