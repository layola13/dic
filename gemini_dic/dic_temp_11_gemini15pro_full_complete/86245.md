heave-ho:/ˈhiːv ˈhoʊ/
noun|解雇，抛弃，丢弃|the act of dismissing someone from a job; the act of throwing something away or getting rid of something.|例句：He was given the heave-ho after just three months. 他只干了三个月就被解雇了。

例句：We gave the old sofa the heave-ho. 我们把旧沙发扔掉了。|近义词：dismissal, rejection, ejection|反义词：retention, acceptance|词性:n.


heave-ho:/ˈhiːv ˈhoʊ/
verb|解雇，抛弃，丢弃|to dismiss someone from a job; to throw something away or get rid of something.|例句：They heaved-ho the old furniture. 他们把旧家具扔掉了。

例句：He was heaved-ho for being late. 他因为迟到被解雇了。|近义词：dismiss, reject, eject|反义词：retain, accept|词性:v.

ETYMOLOGY->
From the nautical phrase "heave ho," a rhythmic chant used by sailors when hauling ropes or other heavy objects.  The repetitive nature of the chant suggests effort to get rid of something cumbersome, which evolved into the metaphorical meaning of getting rid of a person or thing.
