bordered:/ˈbɔːrdərd/ | adj. |有边界的，毗邻的|Having a border or edge; being next to another country or area.| The garden is bordered by a tall hedge.  花园四周环绕着高高的树篱。| synonyms: edged, bounded, neighboring, adjacent | antonyms: boundless, unlimited, unconfined | adjective


bordered:/ˈbɔːrdərd/ | v. |加边于，与…接壤|To form a border around something; to share a border with another area. | France borders Germany to the east. 法国东部与德国接壤。 | synonyms: adjoin, abut, neighbor | antonyms: separate, divide, detach | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *border* (modern French *border*), from Frankish or other Germanic source, akin to Old English *bord* "board, plank."  The meaning "to furnish with a border" arose in the 16th century.
