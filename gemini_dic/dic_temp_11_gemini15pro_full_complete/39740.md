consociation:/kənˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən/
|n.|联合，结合，联盟；伙伴关系；公会，协会|An association or alliance, especially of churches or other organizations; the act of associating. |The consociation of churches worked together to help the homeless. 教堂联盟共同努力帮助无家可归者。|Synonyms: alliance, federation, union, league, coalition|Antonyms: separation, division, disunity|noun

consociation:/kənˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən/
|n.|共治；联合统治|A power-sharing arrangement in a divided society. |The consociation government ensured representation for all ethnic groups. 联合政府确保所有族裔群体都有代表。|Synonyms: power-sharing, joint rule, co-governance|Antonyms: autocracy, dictatorship|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *consociatio, from Latin consociare ("to associate together"), from con- ("together") + sociare ("to associate"), from socius ("companion").
