leucoplastid:/ˈluːkəˌplæstɪd/| n. |白色体|A colorless plastid found in plant cells, especially in roots and tubers, that stores starch, oil, or protein.|Leucoplasts, such as amyloplasts, store starch in potatoes. 白色体，例如淀粉体，在马铃薯中储存淀粉。|Synonyms: plastid, amyloplast (when storing starch), elaioplast (when storing oil), proteinoplast (when storing protein)|Antonyms: chloroplast, chromoplast|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From  *leuko-* (Greek: white) + *-plast* (Greek: formed, molded)
