common:/ˈkɒmən/ (UK), /ˈkɑːmən/ (US)| adj. n.| 普通的，常见的；共有的；公共的；粗俗的；平民 |Something that is common is found in large numbers or it happens often.  A common room is one that is shared by a number of people. |  Colds are common in winter. 感冒在冬天很常见。| synonyms: usual, ordinary, frequent, prevalent, shared, mutual, public, general | antonyms: rare, unusual, uncommon, scarce, private, individual | adjective

common:/ˈkɒmən/ (UK), /ˈkɑːmən/ (US)| n.| 公共用地；平民；普通的事物 |Land that belongs to or is used by the public.  Ordinary or common people. | The sheep grazed on the common. 羊群在公共草地上吃草。| synonyms: commonplace, generality | antonyms: rarity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French comun, from Latin communis "held in common, public, general, shared by all or many," from com "with, together" + munis "performing services, ready to serve, obligated," related to munia "duties, offices."
