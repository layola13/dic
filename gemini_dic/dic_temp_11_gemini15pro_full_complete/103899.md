judaizers:/ˈdʒuːdeɪˌaɪzərz/
|n.|犹太化者；主张遵守犹太律法的人|People who advocated or practiced the adoption of Jewish laws and customs, especially by non-Jews.|The early church struggled with the influence of judaizers who insisted on circumcision for Gentile converts.|Synonyms: legalists, traditionalists|Antonyms: Hellenizers, liberals|noun


judaize:/ˈdʒuːdeɪˌaɪz/
|v.|犹太化；使…犹太化；皈依犹太教|To convert to Judaism or to cause someone to adopt Jewish customs and beliefs.|Some early Christians attempted to judaize Gentile converts by requiring them to follow Jewish dietary laws.|Synonyms: proselytize, convert|Antonyms: secularize, Hellenize|verb


judaizing:/ˈdʒuːdeɪˌaɪzɪŋ/
|adj.|犹太化的；主张遵守犹太律法的|Relating to or promoting the adoption of Jewish laws and customs.|Paul strongly condemned the judaizing tendencies within the early Christian communities.|Synonyms: legalistic, traditionalist|Antonyms: Hellenistic, liberal|adjective
