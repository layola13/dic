disaster: /dɪˈzæstər/ (US) /dɪˈzɑːstər/ (UK) | n. | 灾难，灾祸 | A sudden event, such as an accident or a natural catastrophe, that causes great damage or loss of life. | The earthquake was a major disaster.  地震是一场大灾难。| catastrophe, calamity, tragedy | triumph, success | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Italian *disastro, from dis- “bad” + astro “star”. Originally referred to an unfavorable position of a star or planet, later broadened to mean any great misfortune.
