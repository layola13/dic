bioscientist: /ˈbaɪoʊˌsaɪənˌtɪst/ | n. | 生物学家 | A scientist who studies living organisms, their life processes, and their relationships with each other and the environment. |  Example: The bioscientist dedicated her life to researching the effects of climate change on marine ecosystems.  这位生物学家毕生致力于研究气候变化对海洋生态系统的影响。

Example:  Bioscientists are working on developing new drugs to combat antibiotic-resistant bacteria. 生物学家正在努力开发新的药物来对抗抗生素耐药细菌。 | 近义词: biologist, life scientist | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From bio- + scientist. "Bio-" comes from the Greek "bios," meaning "life." "Scientist" comes from the Latin "scientia," meaning "knowledge," combined with the suffix "-ist," which denotes a person who practices or is skilled in something.
