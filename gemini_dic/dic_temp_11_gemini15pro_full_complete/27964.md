cantors:/ˈkæntərz/| n.|歌手，领唱者|A cantor is a person who leads the singing in a synagogue or church, especially in Jewish religious services.|The cantor's voice filled the synagogue with beautiful melodies. 领唱者的声音充满了犹太教堂，优美的旋律回荡其中。| The cantor led the congregation in prayer. 领唱者带领会众祈祷。 |synonyms: singer, vocalist, musician, chorister| antonyms: listener, audience| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cantor, from Latin cantō (“I sing”), frequentative of canō (“I sing”).
