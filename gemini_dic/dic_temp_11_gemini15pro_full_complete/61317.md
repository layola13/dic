embarrassment: /ɪmˈbærəsmənt/ (US) /ɪmˈbærəsmənt/ (UK)| n.| 尴尬，窘迫，难堪|A feeling of self-consciousness, shame, or awkwardness.|例句：Her face flushed with embarrassment. 她的脸因尴尬而涨得通红。|近义词：shame, humiliation, mortification, awkwardness|反义词: composure, confidence, pride|词性: noun

embarrass: /ɪmˈbærəs/ (US) /ɪmˈbærəs/ (UK)| vt.| 使尴尬，使窘迫，使难堪|To cause someone to feel self-conscious, ashamed, or awkward.|例句：He was embarrassed by his child's behavior. 他被他孩子的行为弄得很尴尬。|近义词：shame, humiliate, mortify|反义词: encourage, support|词性: verb
