brinkmanship: /ˈbrɪŋkmənʃɪp/ | n. | 边缘政策 | The practice, especially in politics, of pursuing a dangerous policy to the limits of safety before stopping, typically in hopes that the other party will yield first. |  The two nations engaged in a dangerous game of brinkmanship, each threatening nuclear war. 这两个国家进行了一场危险的边缘政策游戏，彼此都威胁要发动核战争。

He brought the company back from the brink of failure through skillful brinkmanship in negotiations with creditors. 他通过与债权人谈判时的巧妙边缘政策，使公司从破产边缘起死回生。 | 近义词：calculated risk, daredevilry, gamble | 反义词：caution, prudence, diplomacy | 词性: noun
