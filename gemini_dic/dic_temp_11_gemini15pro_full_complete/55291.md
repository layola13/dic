dissociative:/dɪˈsoʊʃiːətɪv/ /dɪˈsəʊʃɪətɪv/| adj.|分离性的，游离性的|Characterized by or causing dissociation, especially a mental process involving the separation of emotions, thoughts, memories, or other mental processes from conscious awareness.|He experienced dissociative amnesia after the trauma. 他在遭受创伤后经历了分离性失忆。|近义词:disconnected, detached, separate|反义词:associated, connected, integrated|词性:形容词

dissociation:/dɪˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən/ /dɪˌsəʊʃɪˈeɪʃən/| n.|分离，解离|The act of separating or the state of being separated.|The dissociation of the company into smaller units was completed last year.  公司分解成更小的单位的工作在去年完成了。|近义词:separation, disconnection, detachment|反义词:association, connection, integration|词性:名词

dissociate:/dɪˈsoʊʃieɪt/ /dɪˈsəʊʃɪeɪt/| v.|分离，使分离|To separate or become separated.|He tried to dissociate himself from the scandal. 他试图把自己与丑闻撇清关系。|近义词:separate, disconnect, detach|反义词:associate, connect, integrate|词性:动词
