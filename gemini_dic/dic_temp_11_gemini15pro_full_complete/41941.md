cosmopolitanism: /ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtənɪzəm/ /ˌkɑːzməˈpɑːlɪtənɪzəm/ | n. | 世界主义；世界性；四海一家 | The ideology that all human beings belong to a single community, based on a shared morality. |  New York is known for its cosmopolitanism.  纽约以其国际化而闻名。| synonyms: internationalism, globalism, universality | antonyms: parochialism, provincialism, insularity | noun

cosmopolitan: /ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtən/ /ˌkɑːzməˈpɑːlɪtən/ | adj. | 世界性的；全球的；国际化的 |  Including people from many different countries. | London is a very cosmopolitan city. 伦敦是一个非常国际化的城市。| synonyms: international, global, multicultural | antonyms: provincial, local, insular | adjective

cosmopolitan: /ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtən/ /ˌkɑːzməˈpɑːlɪtən/ | n. | 世界公民；四海为家的人 | A person who is free from local, provincial, or national prejudices. | She considers herself a cosmopolitan. 她认为自己是一个世界公民。| synonyms: internationalist, global citizen | antonyms: provincial, local | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
