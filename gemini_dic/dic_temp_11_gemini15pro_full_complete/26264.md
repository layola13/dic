buttoned-up:/ˈbʌtnd ʌp/ | adj. | 拘谨的，保守的，一本正经的 | Appearing conventional and somewhat reserved or repressed in manner; emotionally controlled. | He's a very buttoned-up kind of guy, not very spontaneous. 他是一个非常拘谨的人，不是很自然。 |  She always seems so buttoned-up at work.  她在工作中总是显得一本正经。 | 近义词: reserved, restrained, formal, conventional, uptight, prim, proper | 反义词: relaxed, informal, casual, open, expressive, spontaneous | 词性: 形容词

词源: This term originated in the late 19th century, likely referring to the literal act of buttoning up one's clothing, particularly a high-collared shirt or jacket, which could create a stiff and formal appearance.  Over time, the meaning extended to describe a person's demeanor.
