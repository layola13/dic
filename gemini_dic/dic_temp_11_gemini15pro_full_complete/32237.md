chicken:/ˈtʃɪkɪn/ (US), /ˈtʃɪkɪn/ (UK)| n. adj.|小鸡，鸡肉，胆小鬼|A domestic fowl kept for its eggs or meat, or the meat of this bird.  Also used informally to describe someone who is cowardly.|例句：The farmer raises chickens for their eggs.  这位农民养鸡是为了获取鸡蛋。|近义词：fowl, poultry (for the animal); coward, wimp (for the informal meaning)|反义词：brave, courageous (for the informal meaning)|noun

chicken:/ˈtʃɪkɪn/ (US), /ˈtʃɪkɪn/ (UK)| adj.|胆小的，懦弱的|Easily frightened; cowardly.|例句：He was too chicken to ask her out. 他太胆小了，不敢约她出去。|近义词：timid, fearful|反义词：brave, courageous|adjective
