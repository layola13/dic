backscratching: /ˈbækˌskræʧɪŋ/
| n.|互相吹捧；相互奉承；挠背|The politicians engaged in a lot of backscratching to get their bills passed. 这些政客们为了让他们的法案通过，进行了大量的互相吹捧。|英文解析: The act of reciprocating favors, often with an implication of something slightly shady or self-serving.|例句：Their relationship was based on mutual backscratching. 他们的关系建立在相互奉承的基础上。|近义词：logrolling, quid pro quo, favor-trading|反义词: altruism, selflessness|词性:noun

backscratcher: /ˈbækˌskræʧər/
| n.|挠痒痒的工具；帮助别人的人|I can't reach that itch on my back – could you hand me the backscratcher? 我够不到后背上的痒处——你能把挠痒痒的工具递给我吗？|英文解析: A tool with a long handle and a curved or clawed end, used for scratching one's back.  Can also refer to a person who helps another, often with the expectation of a favor in return.|例句：He’s a real backscratcher – always willing to help out if he thinks he’ll get something in return. 他真是个爱帮忙的人——如果他认为自己会得到回报，他总是乐于助人。|近义词：helper, assistant (for the person sense)|反义词: - |词性:noun
