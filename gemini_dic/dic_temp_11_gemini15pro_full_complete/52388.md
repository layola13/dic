didacticism:/ˌdɪdˈæktɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 教训主义，说教主义 | Designed or intended to teach; intended to convey instruction and information as well as pleasure and entertainment, often excessively. |  The didacticism of the novel, while meant to be instructive, often detracts from the story itself. 这部小说的教训主义，虽然意在指导，但往往会削弱故事本身。 | synonyms:  moralizing, preaching, instruction, edification | antonyms:  amusement, entertainment, diversion, non-instructional | noun

didactic:/daɪˈdæktɪk/ | adj. |  教诲的，说教的 | Intended to teach, particularly in having moral instruction as an ulterior motive.  | The professor's lectures were didactic, focusing on conveying specific knowledge and principles.  教授的讲座很具有教诲性，侧重于传授具体的知识和原则。 | synonyms: instructive, educational, pedagogical, preceptive | antonyms:  uninformative,  entertaining, non-instructive | adjective
