imperilments:/ɪmˈpɛrɪlmənts/ | n. | 危及，危害，危险 | The act of imperiling or the state of being imperiled; danger. |  The company's financial imperilments forced it to lay off workers.  该公司面临的财务危机迫使其裁员。 | Synonyms: dangers, hazards, jeopardies, risks, threats | Antonyms: safety, security | Noun

Etymology: From Old French *empareillier (“to put in peril”), from en- (“in, into”) + parel (“peril, danger”), from Latin periculum.
