actualist:/ˈæktʃuəlɪst/
n.|现实主义者；实际主义者|Someone who believes in or emphasizes the importance of dealing with the actual facts or realities of a situation, rather than theories or ideals.|The actualist refused to speculate about the future, focusing only on present circumstances.|近义词: pragmatist, realist|反义词: idealist, theorist|词性: 名词

actualist:/ˈæktʃuəlɪst/
adj.|现实主义的；实际主义的|Relating to or characteristic of actualism.|He took an actualist approach to the problem, considering only the practical solutions.|近义词: pragmatic, realistic|反义词: idealistic, theoretical|词性: 形容词


词源: From the Latin word "actualis" meaning "active, practical,"  derived from "actus" meaning "act, deed."  The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or belief.
