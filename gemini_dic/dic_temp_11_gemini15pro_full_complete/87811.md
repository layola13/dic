hiccup:/ˈhɪkʌp/ (US) /ˈhɪkʌp/ (UK)| n. v.|打嗝|a sudden, involuntary contraction of the diaphragm muscle, causing a sharp intake of breath and a characteristic sound.|例句：The baby had the hiccups after feeding. 婴儿喂奶后打嗝了。

hiccup:/ˈhɪkʌp/ (US) /ˈhɪkʌp/ (UK)| n. v.|小问题；暂时性挫折|a small problem or temporary setback.|例句：We experienced a few hiccups during the project launch.  项目启动过程中，我们遇到了一些小问题。

hiccuping:/ˈhɪkʌpɪŋ/ (US) /ˈhɪkʌpɪŋ/ (UK)| n.  v. (present participle)|打嗝; 打嗝的行为|the act of hiccuping|例句：He couldn't stop hiccuping after drinking the soda too fast. 他喝汽水太快，停不下来地打嗝。|近义词：hiccoughing|反义词: (No direct antonym for the action itself)
