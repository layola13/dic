out-of-town:/ˌaʊt əv ˈtaʊn/
adjective
外地的；城外的
Located or originating away from a town or city one is discussing or in which one lives.
We have a lot of out-of-town visitors for the festival.
我们有很多外地游客来参加节日。
The out-of-town guests stayed at a nearby hotel.
外地来的客人们住在附近的一家酒店。
近义词:nonresident, non-local
反义词:local, in-town
词性:adjective

Etymology:
"out of" + "town". "Out of" indicates location outside of a specified place. "Town" refers to a populated area, smaller than a city.
