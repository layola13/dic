barricade: /ˈbærɪkeɪd/ (US) /ˈbærɪkeɪd/ (UK)| n. vt.|路障，障碍物；设路障，阻挡|A barrier hastily constructed, as in a street, to obstruct the passage of traffic or troops. |The protesters built a barricade across the street. 抗议者在街上筑起了路障。|Synonyms: barrier, obstacle, blockade, obstruction|Antonyms: opening, passage|noun
barricade: /ˈbærɪkeɪd/ (US) /ˈbærɪkeɪd/ (UK)| vt.|设路障，阻挡；封锁|To obstruct or fortify with a barricade. |They barricaded the doors and windows to protect themselves from the storm.  他们用障碍物封住了门窗以抵御风暴。|Synonyms: block, obstruct, fortify, secure|Antonyms: open, expose|verb
