antiperistatic:/ˌæntɪˌpɛrɪˈstætɪk/
| adj. |逆蠕动的，反蠕动的；【修辞学】增强对立面的（指通过强调对立面来增强某一观点或情感）| Relating to the supposed increase in the intensity of a quality or state, especially of activity or motion, by the presence of an opposing quality, force, or influence.  Historically used in rhetoric to describe a figure of speech in which one intensifies an emotion or idea by dwelling on its opposite. | The speaker used an antiperistatic argument, claiming that the very opposition he faced proved the strength of his cause. 演讲者使用了一个反蠕动的论点，声称他面临的反对恰恰证明了他的事业的力量。|  contrasting, opposing, antithetical | supporting, aiding, synergistic
|adjective
