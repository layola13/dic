good-temperedness:/ˌɡʊdˈtɛmpərdnəs/| n.|好脾气，温和|The good-temperedness of the teacher made her popular with the students. 老师的好脾气使她深受学生喜爱。|English Explanation: The state or quality of having a good temper; being even-tempered and not easily angered. |Example: Her good-temperedness is one of her most admirable qualities. 她的好脾气是她最令人钦佩的品质之一。|Synonym: amiability, affability, equanimity, placidity|Antonym: irascibility, irritability, cantankerousness|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "good-tempered" + "-ness".  "Good-tempered" comes from "good" (meaning positive or desirable) and "tempered" (from the verb "temper," meaning to moderate or soften).  The suffix "-ness" forms nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition.
