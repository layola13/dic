forebodings:/fɔːrˈboʊdɪŋz/ | n. | 预感；预兆 |A feeling that something bad is going to happen. | Example 1: She had forebodings about the journey. 她对这次旅行有不祥的预感。

Example 2:  Dark clouds filled the sky, giving me forebodings of a storm.  乌云密布天空，让我预感到暴风雨即将来临。 | 近义词: premonitions, apprehensions, misgivings | 反义词: hopes, assurances | 词性: 名词 (plural)


foreboding:/fɔːrˈboʊdɪŋ/ | n. | 预感；预兆 |A feeling that something bad is going to happen. | Example 1: A sense of foreboding hung over the town. 小镇笼罩着一种不祥的预感。

Example 2: He had a foreboding that the interview wouldn't go well. 他预感到面试不会顺利。 | 近义词: premonition, apprehension, misgiving | 反义词: hope, assurance | 词性: 名词 (singular)


foreboding:/fɔːrˈboʊdɪŋ/ | adj. | 不祥的；预兆的 |Suggesting that something unpleasant is going to happen. | Example 1: The dark, foreboding clouds promised a storm.  黑暗不祥的云预示着暴风雨。

Example 2: There was a foreboding silence before the announcement.  宣布之前是一片不祥的寂静。| 近义词: ominous, portentous, threatening | 反义词: promising, auspicious | 词性: 形容词


词源:  From Middle English *forboding, from Old English *forbōdung, equivalent to for- + bōdian (“to announce, proclaim, foretell”).
