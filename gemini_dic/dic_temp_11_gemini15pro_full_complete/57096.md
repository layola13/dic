doughtren: /ˈdɔːtrən/ | n. | 女儿 | Archaic form of "daughter."  A female child in relation to her parents. |  And he had three doughtren, the which were faire. | 近义词: daughter | 反义词: son | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *doughtren*, *doughter*, from Old English *dohtor*, from Proto-Germanic *dukhtēr*, from Proto-Indo-European *dhugh₂tḗr*. Cognate with Dutch *dochter*, German *Tochter*, Gothic *𐌳𐌰𐌿𐌷𐍄𐌰𐍂* (*dauhtar*), Latin *filia*, Greek *θυγάτηρ* (*thugátēr*), Sanskrit *दुहितृ* (*duhitṛ́*), Old Irish *ingen*.  The -en ending is a remnant of an older plural form.
