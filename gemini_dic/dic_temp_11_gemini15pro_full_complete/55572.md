disturbedly:/dɪˈstɜːbɪdli/
| adv. |不安地，心烦意乱地|In a manner that shows worry, anxiety, or mental distress.  Characterized by disruption of peace, quiet, or order.|He paced the room disturbedly. 他不安地在房间里踱步。|近义词：anxiously, nervously, worriedly, uneasily|反义词：calmly, peacefully, serenely|词性:副词

Etymology:
From "disturbed" + "-ly".  "Disturbed" comes from the verb "disturb," which originates from the Old French "destorber," meaning "to throw into disorder," ultimately from Latin "disturbare,"  formed from "dis-" (apart, asunder) + "turbare" (to disturb, confuse).
