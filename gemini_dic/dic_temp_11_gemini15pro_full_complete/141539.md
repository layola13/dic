opportunism: /ˌɒpətˈjuːnɪzəm/ (UK) /ˌɑːpərˈtuːnɪzəm/ (US) | n. |机会主义；投机主义|The act of taking advantage of opportunities without regard for moral principles or the interests of others.|He seized the opportunity with a mixture of opportunism and desperation. 他带着机会主义和绝望的心情抓住了这个机会。| 近义词: exploitation, expediency, pragmatism | 反义词: principle, integrity, scrupulousness | 词性: noun

Etymology: From French opportunisme, from opportun (opportune) + -isme (-ism).  The word "opportune" comes from Latin opportunus "fit, suitable, convenient, favorable," from ob- "toward" + portus "harbor." The original sense was of a wind blowing toward a harbor, hence "favorable."
