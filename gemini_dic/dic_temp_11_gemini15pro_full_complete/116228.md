masoretic:/ˌmæsəˈrɛtɪk/ |adj.|马索拉的，指犹太圣经文本的传统发音和注音系统|Relating to the Masoretes, Jewish scholars who worked between the 6th and 10th centuries CE to standardize the Hebrew Bible, adding vowel points, accent marks, and other annotations.|The masoretic text is considered the authoritative version of the Hebrew Bible.|近义词: traditional, authoritative|反义词: unpointed, unvocalized|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Masora*, from Hebrew *masōrāh* "tradition", from *masar* "to hand down".
