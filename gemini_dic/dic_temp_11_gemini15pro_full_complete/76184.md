fussily:/ˈfʌsɪli/ | adv. | fussily | in a fussy manner; with excessive attention to minor details | She fussily rearranged the flowers in the vase. 她不停地摆弄花瓶里的花。| meticulously, fastidiously | carelessly, casually | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From fussy + -ly.  "Fussy" originates from the now obsolete verb "fust" (meaning to grow moldy or stale), implying a sense of agitated impatience. Over time, the meaning shifted to describe someone overly concerned with minor details.
