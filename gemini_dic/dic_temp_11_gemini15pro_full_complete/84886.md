hangover:/ˈhæŋoʊvər/ (US) /ˈhæŋəʊvə(r)/ (UK)| n.|宿醉，残留物，遗留物|The unpleasant physical effects following the heavy consumption of alcohol, such as a headache and nausea.|I had a terrible hangover after last night's party. 我昨晚聚会后宿醉得很厉害。|

近义词: after-effects,  sequelae|
反义词:  (No direct antonym, but one could consider "feeling fresh" or "well-rested" as contrasting states)|
词性: noun|


hangover:/ˈhæŋoʊvər/ (US) /ˈhæŋəʊvə(r)/ (UK)| n.|遗留物，残留物（非酒精方面）|Something (usually unpleasant) remaining from a past event or experience.|The hangover from the economic crisis is still being felt. 经济危机的遗留问题仍然存在。|

近义词: legacy, remnant, residue|
反义词:  (No direct antonym in this sense)|
