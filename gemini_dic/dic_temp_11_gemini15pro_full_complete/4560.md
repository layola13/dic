alchemists:/ˈælkɪmɪsts/
n.
炼金术士; 点金家
A person who practices alchemy.  Alchemy was a philosophical tradition practiced throughout history and across the world.  It aimed to purify, mature, and perfect certain objects.
The alchemists of old sought to turn lead into gold. 古代的炼金术士试图将铅变成金。
近义词:chemist, sorcerer, magician
反义词:scientist
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *alkemiste*, from Old French *alquemiste*, from Medieval Latin *alchimista*, from Arabic *al-kīmiyā’* (alchemy) + *-ista* (suffix forming agent nouns). The Arabic term is derived from the Greek word *khēmeía* (χημεία), whose ultimate origin is uncertain.
