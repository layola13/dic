ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin consonantia "agreement in sound," from Latin consonantem (nominative consonans) "sounding together, agreeing in sound," present participle of consonare "to sound together," from com- "together" (see com-) + sonare "to sound" (see sound (v.)).
