consorts:/kənˈsɔːrts/ |kənˈsɔːrtz/
n. |配偶；伙伴；同事；盟友|A wife, husband, or companion, especially of a reigning monarch.  A ship accompanying another, especially for protection. A group of musicians performing together.| Queen Elizabeth II and her consort, Prince Philip, visited Canada.  The warship had two consorts. The consort of viols played a beautiful piece.|近义词：spouse, partner, companion, colleague, ally|反义词：enemy, opponent|词性:noun

consorts:/kənˈsɔːrts/ |kənˈsɔːrtz/
v. |(与…)交往；(与…)结交|Habitually associates with (someone), typically with the disapproval of others. To accord with or agree with.|He consorts with known criminals.  His lifestyle doesn't consort with his stated values.|近义词：associate, fraternize, mingle|反义词：avoid, shun|词性:verb
