corbeling: /ˈkɔːrbəlɪŋ/ (US), /ˈkɔːbəlɪŋ/ (UK) | n. | 叠涩, 突出部分 | A construction technique where each course of masonry projects slightly beyond the one below it, creating a stepped overhang. | The corbeling of the arch is a remarkable feat of engineering.  拱门的叠涩是一项非凡的工程壮举。 | Synonyms: oversailing, projecting | Antonyms: receding, indented | noun

corbel: /ˈkɔːrbəl/ (US), /ˈkɔːbəl/ (UK) | n. vt. |  (n.) 叠涩, 突出部分, 托架; (vt.) 用叠涩法建造 | (n.) A projecting architectural member used to support a weight; (vt.) To build using the corbeling technique. | The gargoyle perched on a corbel high above the street. 石像鬼栖息在街道上方高高的托架上。  They corbeled the roof to create a shaded area. 他们用叠涩法建造屋顶以创建一个阴凉区域。 | Synonyms: bracket, support, console (n.); project, overhang (vt.) | Antonyms: recess (n., vt.) | noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *corbel (modern French corbeau), diminutive of corb "raven," because of the resemblance of the projecting masonry to a raven's beak. Ultimately from Late Latin corvus "raven."
