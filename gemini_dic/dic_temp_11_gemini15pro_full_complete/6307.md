amortising:/ˈæmərtaɪzɪŋ/ (US), /ˈæmɔːtaɪzɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|摊销的，分期偿还的|The process of gradually reducing a debt or cost by making regular payments over a period of time.|The company is amortising the cost of new equipment over five years.  这家公司正在五年内分期偿还新设备的成本。|Synonyms: depreciating, expensing, writing off|Antonyms: appreciating, accruing|adjective


amortise:/ˈæmərtaɪz/ (US), /ˈæmɔːtaɪz/ (UK)| v.|摊销，分期偿还|To gradually reduce a debt or cost by making regular payments over a period of time.|The loan will be amortised over 15 years.  这笔贷款将在15年内分期偿还。|Synonyms: depreciate, expense, write off|Antonyms: appreciate, accrue|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *amortir "to deaden, extinguish," from a- "to" (from Latin ad) + morir "to die" (from Latin mori).  The financial sense developed from the idea of "killing off" a debt.
