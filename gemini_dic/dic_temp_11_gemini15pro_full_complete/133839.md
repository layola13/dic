nongracefulness: /ˌnɒnˈɡreɪsfʊlnəs/ | n. | 不优雅，笨拙 |  The state or quality of lacking grace or elegance in movement, form, or behavior.  It describes something that is awkward, clumsy, or ungainly. | Her nongracefulness on the dance floor was embarrassing.  她在舞池里的笨拙令人尴尬。 | 近义词: awkwardness, clumsiness, ungainliness, inelegance | 反义词: gracefulness, elegance, poise, refinement | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  "non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "gracefulness" (noun derived from "graceful" + "-ness" suffix). "Graceful" ultimately comes from the Latin "gratia" meaning "grace, favor, thanks."
