fact's:/ˈfækts/| n.|事实，真相|The fact's (fact is) that we don't have enough money. 事实是我们没有足够的钱。|近义词：reality, truth|反义词：fiction, falsehood|名词

词源:
From Old French *fait, later spelled fait (modern French fait), from Latin factum "something done, deed, act, achievement, circumstance, event," noun use of neuter of factus, past participle of facere "to do, make"

注意事项:

"fact's" is a colloquial contraction of "fact is" and is generally considered informal. It's more common in spoken English than in written English.  While grammatically correct, it's generally recommended to use "the fact is" in formal writing.
