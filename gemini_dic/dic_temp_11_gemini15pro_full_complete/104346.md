juxtapose:/ˌdʒʌkstəˈpoʊz/ | v. | 并列； juxtaposition:/ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən/ | n. | 并置，并列 | To place close together for contrasting effect. 将…并置，以形成对比效果。|  The exhibition juxtaposes Picasso's early works with his later pieces.  展览将毕加索的早期作品与后期作品并列展出。 | synonyms: compare, contrast, place side by side | antonyms: separate, divide | verb

juxtaposition:/ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən/ | n. | 并置，并列 | The act or an instance of placing two or more things side by side often to compare or contrast or to create an interesting effect.  将两个或多个事物并排放置的行为或实例，通常是为了比较或对比或创造一种有趣的效果。 | The juxtaposition of the two images creates a striking visual effect. 这两幅图像的并置创造了一种惊人的视觉效果。 | synonyms: comparison, contrast, proximity | antonyms: separation, distance, division | noun
