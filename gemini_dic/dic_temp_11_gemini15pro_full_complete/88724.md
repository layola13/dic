hobnailed:/ˈhɒbneɪld/ (UK) /ˈhɑːbneɪld/ (US)| adj.|带钉的；粗糙的；乡土气的|Covered or reinforced with hobnails, short nails with thick heads, typically used on the soles of boots.|例句：He wore hobnailed boots. 他穿着带钉的靴子。|例句：The farmer's hobnailed boots clattered on the stone floor. 农夫的钉靴在石地板上发出咔哒咔哒的声音。|近义词：studded, spiked, rough, rustic|反义词:smooth, refined|词性:adjective

词源:
From hobnail + -ed.  "Hobnail" itself comes from "hob" (a projection or peg) + "nail."  "Hob" is likely related to Middle Low German "hobbe" (a hob, a hobgoblin), perhaps referring to a rough, clumsy object.
