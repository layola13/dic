kelpy:/ˈkɛlpi/| n. |水鬼，凯尔派|A mythological water spirit of Scottish folklore, typically taking the form of a horse, said to lure people to their deaths by drowning.|The kelpy is said to inhabit the lochs and rivers of Scotland.  据说凯尔派栖息在苏格兰的湖泊和河流中。|Near synonyms: water horse, each-uisge, nixie|Near antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for a mythological creature. Could consider terrestrial creatures or benevolent spirits)|noun
