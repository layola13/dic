at my heels:/æt mɑɪ hiːlz/ | adv. phrase | 紧跟着我，寸步不离 |  This expression describes someone or something following very closely behind, often in a persistent or annoying way. It originates from the image of someone's heels being followed so closely that they are almost being stepped on. | The paparazzi were at my heels all day. 狗仔队整天都紧跟着我。|  close behind, following closely, dogging, shadowing, hounding | far behind, distant, ahead | adverbial phrase
