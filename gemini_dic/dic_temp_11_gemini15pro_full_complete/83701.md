hagiocracy:/ˌhæɡiˈɒkrəsi/| n. |圣人统治；伪善统治|A government ruled by religious leaders or a system where those perceived as holy have undue influence.  A system of government where people are overly reverent to those in power, treating them as saints.|The country's descent into hagiocracy stifled dissent and critical thinking.  这个国家沦为圣人统治，扼杀了异议和批判性思维。

The CEO cultivated a hagiocracy within the company, making it difficult to challenge his decisions.  这位CEO在公司内部营造了一种伪善统治的氛围，使得挑战他的决定变得困难。|近义词：theocracy, hierocracy|反义词：secularism, democracy|词性: noun

hagiographic:/ˌhæɡiəˈɡræfɪk/| adj. |圣徒传记的；过分赞美的|Relating to the writing of the lives of saints; excessively flattering and idealized.|The biography was criticized for its hagiographic portrayal of the president, ignoring his flaws and controversies.  这部传记因其对总统的圣徒传记式描写而受到批评，因为它忽略了他的缺点和争议。

The article offered a hagiographic account of the company's founder, glossing over the unethical practices that led to his success. 这篇文章对公司创始人的描述过于赞美，掩盖了导致他成功的那些不道德行为。|近义词：laudatory, adulatory|反义词：critical, objective|词性: adjective

hagiolatry:/ˌhæɡiˈɒlətri/| n. |圣徒崇拜；偶像崇拜|Excessive devotion to or reverence for someone or something.|The fan's hagiolatry of the pop star bordered on obsession.  粉丝对这位流行歌星的崇拜近乎痴迷。

The cult leader encouraged hagiolatry of himself, demanding absolute obedience and adoration from his followers.  邪教头目鼓励对自己的偶像崇拜，要求信徒绝对服从和崇拜。|近义词：idolatry, worship|反义词：disdain, contempt|词性: noun
