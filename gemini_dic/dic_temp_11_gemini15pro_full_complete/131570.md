noncommunities:/ˌnɑːnkəˈmjuːnətiz/
|n.|非共同体，缺乏共同利益或联系的群体|A group of people lacking shared interests, values, or identity, typically characterized by social fragmentation and limited interaction.|The rise of social media has led to the formation of online noncommunities, where individuals interact superficially without developing meaningful connections.|近义词:disorganized groups, fragmented societies|反义词:communities, societies|词性:名词

Etymology:
non- (prefix): from Latin "non" meaning "not"
community (noun): from Old French "comunité," ultimately from Latin "communitatem" (nominative "communitas") meaning "fellowship, community of relations or feelings," from "communis" meaning "common"
