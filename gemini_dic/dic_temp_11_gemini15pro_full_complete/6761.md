anam:/ˈɑːnəm/| n.|灵魂，生命|A soul or life, especially in Irish contexts.|例句：The anam of the forest was felt by all who entered. 所有进入森林的人都感受到了森林的灵魂。|例句：He believed his anam would live on after death. 他相信他的灵魂会在死后继续存在。|近义词：soul, spirit, life, essence|反义词：body, corpse|词性：名词

词源: From Irish *anam, from Old Irish anim, from Proto-Celtic *anamā (“soul”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂enh₁- (“to breathe”). Cognate with Welsh enaid, Breton ene.
