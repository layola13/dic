inoculative:/ɪˈnɒkjʊlətɪv/ | adj. | 接种的；灌输的 | Introducing or tending to introduce something, such as an idea or attitude, into the mind of another; implanting. Relating to or produced by inoculation. |  The teacher's inoculative methods helped the students develop a love for learning. 老师的循循善诱的教学方法帮助学生培养了学习的热爱。| 近义词:  instilling, implanting, infusing, imbuing,  communicable | 反义词: eradicative,  extractive | 词性: adjective

inoculation:/ɪˌnɒkjuˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 接种；灌输 | The act or process of inoculating; introduction of a microorganism, serum, or vaccine into a living organism to induce immunity. The act of introducing something, such as an idea or attitude, into the mind of another; implantation. |  The doctor recommended annual flu inoculations. 医生建议每年接种流感疫苗。  The constant inoculation of fear by the media can lead to public anxiety. 媒体不断灌输恐惧会导致公众焦虑。 | 近义词: vaccination, immunization, indoctrination, injection | 反义词: none | 词性: noun
