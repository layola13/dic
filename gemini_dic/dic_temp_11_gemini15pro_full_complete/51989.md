diaglyph: /ˈdaɪɡlɪf/ | n. | 双字形，二合字母 | A diaglyph is a ligature, a group of two letters treated as a single glyph. It can also refer to two different forms of the same letter or symbol. |  The Old English letter "æ" is a diaglyph representing the sounds "a" and "e".  |  Example:  The diaglyph "fi" is often used in typography to avoid collision between the dot of the "i" and the hook of the "f".  | 近义词: ligature, digraph | 反义词:  (No direct antonym) | noun
