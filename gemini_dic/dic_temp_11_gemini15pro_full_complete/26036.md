bushelings:/ˈbʊʃəlɪŋz/
|n.|蒲式耳计量的人或物（通常指人，尤指儿童）|People or things, especially children, measured by the bushel. This term often carries a connotation of smallness or insignificance, as a bushel is a large unit of measurement for dry goods.  It suggests the people are being treated as commodities.|The farmer referred to the children gleaning the fields as "bushelings," implying their small size and the meagerness of their harvest.|Near synonyms:  urchins, waifs, ragamuffins|Antonyms: giants, adults|noun
