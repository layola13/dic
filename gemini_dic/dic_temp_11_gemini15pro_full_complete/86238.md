heatproof:/ˈhiːtpruːf/| adj.|耐热的，隔热的|Able to withstand high temperatures without being damaged or melting.|例句：The scientist used heatproof gloves to handle the hot materials. 科学家用耐热手套来处理高温材料。|例句：I need a heatproof mat for my hot pot. 我需要一个耐热的垫子来放我的火锅。|近义词：fireproof, heat-resistant, thermostable|反义词：flammable, combustible|词性:形容词

词源:
heat (n.) + proof (adj.)

heat (n.): From Old English *hætu (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *haitō (source also of Old Saxon hēta, Old Frisian hēte, Old Norse hiti, Danish hede, Swedish hetta, Dutch hitte, German Hitze "heat"), from Proto-Indo-European *kayd-o- "heat."
proof (adj.):  From Old French prouf, prove "proof, test; capable, of proven value," from Latin probatus "tried, tested, approved," past participle of probare "to try, test, approve, find good," from probus "honest, upright," from PIE root *pro- "forward."
