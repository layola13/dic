chopsticks: /ˈtʃɒpstɪks/ (UK) /ˈtʃɑːpstɪks/ (US)| n.|筷子|Two thin sticks held together in one hand, used as eating utensils in East Asian countries.|She skillfully maneuvered the chopsticks to pick up a single grain of rice.|近义词: eating utensils, cutlery|反义词: fork, knife, spoon|词性: Noun


chopstick: /ˈtʃɒpstɪk/ (UK) /ˈtʃɑːpstɪk/ (US)| n.|一根筷子|One of the two sticks that make up a pair of chopsticks.|He dropped a chopstick and had to reach down to pick it up.|近义词: stick|反义词: N/A|词性: Noun


词源:  From Pidgin English, from *chop "quick" + stick.  "Chop chop" was a common phrase used to urge speed, and chopsticks were seen as enabling quick eating.
