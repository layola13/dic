glucogen:/ˈɡluːkədʒən/ | n. |胰高血糖素|A hormone formed in the pancreas that promotes the breakdown of glycogen to glucose in the liver.|  Example: Glucagon helps regulate blood sugar levels. 胰高血糖素有助于调节血糖水平。| Example:  The body releases glucagon when blood sugar is low. 当血糖低时，身体会释放胰高血糖素。|Synonym: hyperglycemic factor| Antonym: insulin | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From gluco- (glucose) + -gen (producing).  Ultimately from Greek *gleukos (sweet) and -genes (born, produced).
