carnegie: /ˈkɑːrnɪɡi/ | n. | 卡内基 |  Refers to Andrew Carnegie (1835-1919), a Scottish-American industrialist, philanthropist, and business magnate.  It can also refer to institutions named after him, such as Carnegie Hall or Carnegie Mellon University. | He donated a large sum of money to build a public library, a Carnegie library.  他捐了一大笔钱建造一座公共图书馆，一座卡内基图书馆。| synonym: philanthropist, benefactor | antonym: miser | noun
