foster:/ˈfɒstər/ (British) /ˈfɑːstər/ (American)| v. adj.|培养，促进；收养的|To encourage the development or growth of something; to bring up a child that is not one's own by birth.|例句：The teacher fostered a love of reading in her students. 老师培养了学生对阅读的热爱。|近义词：encourage, promote, nurture, cultivate, adopt|反义词: neglect, hinder, discourage|词性:动词

foster:/ˈfɒstər/ (British) /ˈfɑːstər/ (American)| adj.|收养的，寄养的|Relating to the fostering of a child.|例句：They are foster parents to three children. 他们是三个孩子的养父母。|近义词：adoptive|反义词: biological|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fostren*, from Old English *fostrian* (“to nourish, support, bring up, educate”), from Proto-Germanic *fōstrijaną (“to foster”), from *fōstrą (“food, nourishment”), from Proto-Indo-European *pā- (“to protect, feed”). Cognate with Old Frisian *fosteria* (“to feed, nourish, bring up”), Old Saxon *fostrian* (“to nourish, educate”), Dutch *voesteren* (“to nurse, foster”), German *fördern* (“to promote, advance”).
