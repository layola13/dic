nonsociableness:/ˌnɒnˈsəʊʃəb(ə)lnəs/
|n.|不善交际；孤僻|The state or quality of being unsociable; aversion to society or companionship.|His nonsociableness made it difficult for him to make friends. 他的不善交际使他很难交到朋友。|unsociability, introversion, reclusiveness|sociability, gregariousness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + sociable + -ness.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Sociable" comes from the Latin "sociabilis," meaning "companionable."  "-ness" is a suffix used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
