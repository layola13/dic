nativist: /ˈneɪtɪvɪst/ | n. adj. | 本土主义者；本土主义的 | A person who favors those born in his country and is opposed to immigrants.  A policy of favoring native inhabitants over immigrants. |  Example: The nativist movement gained traction during periods of economic hardship.  本土主义运动在经济困难时期获得了关注。

Example:  Nativist sentiments were evident in the rhetoric of some politicians. 一些政客的言论中明显带有本土主义情绪。| Synonym: xenophobe, chauvinist | Antonym: cosmopolitan, internationalist | noun, adjective

Etymology:  From Latin *nativus* "native, born," from *natus*, past participle of *nasci* "to be born."
