antidilution:/ˌæntiˌdaɪˈluːʃn/ | n. |反稀释|  A provision in an agreement or contract, typically in finance and investment, that protects an investor's ownership stake from being diminished by the issuance of additional shares or equity.  It aims to maintain the investor's percentage ownership in a company. | Example: The antidilution clause in the contract protected the investor's stake when the company issued new shares. 合同中的反稀释条款在公司发行新股时保护了投资者的股份。

Example:  Antidilution rights are crucial for early-stage investors. 反稀释权对早期投资者至关重要。 | 近义词: preemptive right, subscription right | 反义词: dilution | 词性: 名词
