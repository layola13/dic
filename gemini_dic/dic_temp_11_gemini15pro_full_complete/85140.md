hardly:/ˈhɑːrdli/| adv.|几乎不，简直不，刚刚|Used to say that something is almost not true or almost does not happen.  It emphasizes the smallness or insignificance of something.|例句：I hardly know her. 我几乎不认识她。|近义词：barely, scarcely|反义词:easily, definitely|词性:副词
词源:From Old English heardlice "severely, harshly, boldly, bravely, with difficulty, scarcely," from heard "hard" + -lice, adverbial suffix. The meaning softened to "with difficulty" and then to "scarcely."
