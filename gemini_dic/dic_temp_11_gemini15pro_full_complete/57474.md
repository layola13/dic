draftsman:/ˈdræftsmən/ /ˈdrɑːftsmən/| n. |起草人；制图员；绘图员|A person who draws plans and sketches, especially of machinery or buildings.|例句：The draftsman meticulously rendered the blueprints for the new skyscraper.  制图员一丝不苟地绘制了新摩天大楼的蓝图。

例句：He worked as a draftsman before becoming an architect. 他在成为建筑师之前做过绘图员。|近义词：draughtsman, designer, architect, engineer|反义词:|词性:noun


draftswoman:/ˈdræftsˌwʊmən/ /ˈdrɑːftsˌwʊmən/| n. |女起草人；女制图员；女绘图员|A female draftsman.|例句：The draftswoman carefully outlined the design for the new bridge.  女制图员仔细地勾勒出了新桥的设计。

例句：She was a skilled draftswoman with years of experience. 她是一位经验丰富、技术娴熟的女绘图员。|近义词：draughtswoman, designer, architect, engineer|反义词:|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From draft + -s- + man.  Draft in this sense means a preliminary version of a piece of writing or a plan or drawing.
