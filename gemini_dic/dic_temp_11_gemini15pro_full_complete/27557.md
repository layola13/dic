canakinumab:/ˌkænəˈkɪnjuːmæb/| n. |卡纳单抗|A monoclonal antibody medication used to treat certain types of arthritis and autoinflammatory diseases. It works by blocking the activity of interleukin-1 beta, a protein that plays a key role in inflammation.|Example: Canakinumab has shown promise in reducing the risk of heart attacks. 卡纳单抗在降低心脏病发作风险方面已显示出希望。|Synonym: IL-1 blocker|Antonym: N/A|Noun
