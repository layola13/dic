eme:/iːm/ | n. | uncle | A term of respect and endearment used to address or refer to an older male relative or friend, especially a maternal uncle, in some cultures, particularly in parts of Africa and the African diaspora. | He called his elder cousin "eme" out of respect. 他出于尊重称他的堂兄为“eme”。 | 近义词: uncle, elder | 反义词: None | 词性: Noun

词源:
The word "eme" originates from Igbo language, a language spoken in southeastern Nigeria, where it means "maternal uncle." Its usage has spread through the African diaspora and can be found in various communities.
