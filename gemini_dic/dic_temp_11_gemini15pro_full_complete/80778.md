gossips:/ˈɡɒsɪps/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːsɪps/ (US)| n. vi.|闲话，流言蜚语；说闲话|Casual or unconstrained conversation or reports about other people, typically involving details that are not confirmed as being true. |They were gossiping about the new neighbors. 他们正在谈论新邻居的闲话。|Synonyms: rumors, whispers, tattle, chatter|Antonyms: facts, truth|noun

gossips:/ˈɡɒsɪps/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːsɪps/ (US)| vi.|说闲话，传播流言|To talk about other people's private lives. |She gossips about everyone she knows. 她对她认识的每个人都嚼舌根。|Synonyms:  chatter, tattle, whisper, spread rumors|Antonyms: withhold information|verb
