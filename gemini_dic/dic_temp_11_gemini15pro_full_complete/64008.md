epistrophe:/ɪˈpɪstrəfi/ /ɪˈpɪstroʊfi/| n. |首语重复法；尾词重复|Repetition of a word or phrase at the end of successive clauses, sentences, or verses.| "Of the people, by the people, for the people" is a famous example of epistrophe.| 近义词: epiphora, anaphora (somewhat) | 反义词: anaphora (strictly speaking) | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἐπιστροφή (epistrophḗ, “return, turning about”), from ἐπί (epí, “upon, on, at”) + στροφή (strophḗ, “a turning”).

PHRASE->
No specific phrases exist using "epistrophe" directly.  It's a rhetorical device, not a word used in set phrases.  However, here are examples of phrases *containing* epistrophe:

When I was a child, I spoke as a child, I understood as a child, I thought as a child.
当我还是个孩子的时候，我像孩子一样说话，像孩子一样理解，像孩子一样思考。

Government of the people, by the people, for the people, shall not perish from the earth.
民有、民治、民享的政府，永世长存。
