bell-hopping:/ˈbelˌhɑːpɪŋ/
noun
搬运行李；当侍者
The act of working as a bellhop, carrying luggage and performing other services for hotel guests.
He spent a summer bell-hopping to earn money for college. 他整个夏天都在做侍者赚钱上大学。
近义词: bell service, luggage handling
反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "self-service")
词性: noun


bell-hopping:/ˈbelˌhɑːpɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
搬运行李；当侍者
Working as a bellhop.
He was bell-hopping at the Grand Hotel. 他在大酒店当侍者。
近义词: working as a bellhop
反义词: (No direct antonym)
词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
bell + hop
bell (n.) - Old English belle, from Proto-Germanic *bellan "to ring," related to *belljo "bell"
hop (v.) - Old English hoppian "to jump, leap," of uncertain origin.
