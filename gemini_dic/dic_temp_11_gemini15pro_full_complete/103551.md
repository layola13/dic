jog:/dʒɒɡ/ (British) /dʒɑːɡ/ (American)| v. n.|慢跑，轻推|To run at a slow, steady pace, typically for exercise.  Also, a slight push or nudge.|例句：I like to jog in the park every morning. 我喜欢每天早上在公园里慢跑。|例句：She gave him a jog to wake him up. 她轻轻推了他一下，把他叫醒。|近义词：run, trot, canter|反义词:sprint, stand, sit|词性:动词, 名词

trotted:/ˈtrɒtɪd/ (British) /ˈtrɑːtɪd/ (American)| v.|（马）小跑，快步走|The past tense and past participle of "trot," meaning to move faster than a walk, especially of a horse lifting each diagonal pair of legs alternately.|例句：The horse trotted around the field. 马绕着田野小跑。|例句：He trotted down the street to catch the bus. 他沿着街道快步走，去赶公共汽车。|近义词：jogged, ran, hurried|反义词:walked, strolled, ambled|词性:动词
