legitimatize: /ləˈdʒɪtɪmaɪz/ | v. | 使合法化；使正当化；给…以合法地位 | To make something that was previously illegal or unacceptable legal or acceptable. | The government has moved to legitimize the status of immigrant workers. 政府已经采取行动使移民工人的身份合法化。

The company tried to legitimize its questionable practices by claiming they were industry standard.  这家公司试图通过声称其可疑做法是行业标准来使其合法化。 | synonyms: validate, justify, authorize, legalize | antonyms: criminalize, outlaw, prohibit, condemn | verb

legitimate: /ləˈdʒɪtɪmət/ | adj. | 合法的；正当的；合理的 | Conforming to the law or to rules. |  He had a legitimate reason for being late. 他迟到是有正当理由的。

Is it legitimate to ask for a refund? 要求退款是否合理？ | synonyms: lawful, legal, valid, justifiable | antonyms: illegal, unlawful, invalid, illegitimate | adjective

legitimacy: /ləˈdʒɪtɪməsi/ | n. | 合法性；正统性 | The quality or state of being legitimate. |  The legitimacy of the election results is being questioned. 选举结果的合法性受到了质疑。

The new government's legitimacy was quickly recognized by other countries. 新政府的合法性很快得到了其他国家的认可。 | synonyms: lawfulness, legality, validity | antonyms: illegitimacy, invalidity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin  legitimatus, past participle of legitimare  "to make lawful," from Latin legitimus  "lawful," from lex (genitive legis) "law."
