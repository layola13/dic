bowab:/ˈboʊwɑːb/ (US) /ˈbəʊwæb/ (UK)| n.|厕所管理员，守门人|(from Hindi बावर्ची bāvarchī, meaning "cook," via Anglo-Indian) Originally referred to a cook or head servant, but later evolved to mean a doorkeeper or concierge, especially in India. Now often used humorously or archaically. |The bowab unlocked the gate for the residents.  守门人为居民打开了大门。|Synonym: doorkeeper, concierge, gatekeeper, janitor|Antonym: none|noun

bowab:/ˈboʊwɑːb/ (US) /ˈbəʊwæb/ (UK)| n.|厕所，洗手间|(Less common usage, derived from the association of the bowab with maintaining the building, including restrooms)  A rather dated and regional term.|The bowab was down the hall. 厕所就在走廊尽头。|Synonym: toilet, restroom, lavatory, WC|Antonym: none|noun
