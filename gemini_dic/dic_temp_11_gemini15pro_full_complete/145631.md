overconstructing: /ˌoʊvərˈkənˌstrʌktɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
过度解读；过度诠释；想得太多
Analyzing something in an excessively detailed or complicated way, often to find meanings that are not intended.
She was overconstructing his casual remark and read far too much into it. 她过度解读了他随意的评论，想得太多了。
Overanalyzing, overthinking, overinterpreting
Underestimating, simplifying, overlooking
词性: 动词(现在分词)


overconstruct: /ˌoʊvərˈkənˌstrʌkt/
verb
过度解读；过度诠释；想得太多
To analyze something in an excessively detailed or complicated way, often to find meanings that are not intended.
Don't overconstruct his words; he meant it as a compliment. 不要过度解读他的话；他本意是赞美。
Overanalyze, overthink, overinterpret
Underestimate, simplify, overlook
词性: 动词

词源：
"Over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "construct" (from Latin "construere," meaning "to build together").  The meaning evolved from the literal sense of building something too elaborately to the figurative sense of building up an interpretation that is too complex.
