haggles:/ˈhæɡlz/ (British) /ˈhæɡəlz/ (American)| v. (third-person singular simple present haggles, present participle haggling, simple past and past participle haggled)|讨价还价，争论价格|To dispute or bargain persistently, especially over the cost of something.|She haggled with the street vendor over the price of the scarf. 她和街头小贩就围巾的价格讨价还价。|近义词：bargain, barter, negotiate|反义词:acquiesce, concede|词性:动词

haggle:/ˈhæɡl/ (British) /ˈhæɡəl/ (American)| n.|讨价还价，争论|The act of haggling.|After much haggle, they finally agreed on a price. 经过一番讨价还价，他们最终达成了价格协议。|近义词：bargaining, bartering, negotiation|反义词:agreement, concession|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *höggva (“to hew, chop”), from Proto-Germanic *haggōną (“to hack, chop”).  Originally referred to chopping or hacking, later extended to mean cutting back on a price, and finally to bargaining.
