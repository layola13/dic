gougers:/ˈɡaʊdʒərz/
n.
敲诈者，奸商，哄抬物价的人
People who exploit situations to charge excessively high prices for goods or services.
During the hurricane, some gougers were selling bottled water for $10 each. 在飓风期间，一些奸商以每瓶10美元的价格出售瓶装水。
synonyms: profiteers, racketeers, extortionists
antonyms: philanthropists, donors


gouge:/ɡaʊdʒ/
v.
敲竹杠；诈骗； gouge out 挖出
To overcharge; to extort. To scoop or force something out roughly or brutally.
The mechanic gouged me on the car repairs.  那个机修工在汽车修理费上敲了我竹杠。
The child gouged out a chunk of the birthday cake with his fingers. 孩子用手指抠出一大块生日蛋糕。
synonyms: overcharge, extort, swindle, scoop, excavate
antonyms: undercharge, donate, give


gouge:/ɡaʊdʒ/
n.
凿子； gouge wound 凿伤
A chisel with a concave blade, used for scooping or cutting holes. A deep wound or groove.
He used a gouge to create the intricate design on the wooden door. 他用凿子在木门上雕刻出复杂的图案。
The falling debris left a gouge in the car's hood. 掉落的碎片在汽车引擎盖上留下了一个凹痕。
synonyms: chisel, groove, indentation
antonyms: protrusion, bulge
