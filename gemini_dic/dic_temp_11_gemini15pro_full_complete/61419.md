embolden:/ɪmˈboʊldən/| v. |使大胆，使有胆量；鼓励|to give someone the courage to do something|例句：His success emboldened him to try again. 他的成功鼓舞他再次尝试。|例句：Emboldened by the support of her family, she decided to start her own business. 在家人的支持下，她鼓起勇气决定自己创业。|近义词：encourage, hearten, inspire|反义词：discourage, intimidate, daunt|词性:动词
ETYMOLOGY->
From en- + bold + -en.  "En-" is a prefix meaning "to put into" or "cause to be." "Bold" means courageous and daring.  "-en" is a suffix used to form verbs from adjectives. Therefore, embolden literally means "to make bold."
