gibbet:/ˈdʒɪbɪt/| n. v. |绞刑架; 绞死|A gallows.|
The highwayman's body was left hanging from the gibbet as a warning to others.|
They gibbeted the traitor after the rebellion.|
近义词：gallows, scaffold |反义词: None |词性: noun, verb

gibbet:/ˈdʒɪbɪt/| v. |公开谴责，嘲笑|To expose to public scorn or ridicule.|
The newspapers gibbeted the politician for his corrupt dealings.|
His foolish mistakes were gibbeted by his colleagues.|
近义词：denounce, ridicule, pillory |反义词: praise, commend |词性: verb

词源:
From Old French *giber "cage for poultry," of uncertain origin.  The meaning shifted from "cage" to "gallows" likely due to the cage-like structure sometimes surrounding the platform of the gallows.
