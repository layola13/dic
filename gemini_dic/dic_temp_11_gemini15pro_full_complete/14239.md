autocratism:/ˈɔːtəˌkrætɪzəm/
noun
专制主义，独裁主义
A system of government in which supreme power is concentrated in the hands of one person, whose decisions are subject to neither external legal restraints nor regularized mechanisms of popular control.
The country suffered under the king's autocratism for decades.  这个国家在国王的专制统治下受苦了几十年。
His leadership style veered towards autocratism, silencing any dissent. 他的领导风格走向了独裁主义，压制了任何异议。
近义词:absolutism, despotism, dictatorship, tyranny
反义词:democracy, liberalism
词性:n.

autocratic:/ˌɔːtəˈkrætɪk/
adjective
专制的，独裁的
Relating to or characteristic of an autocrat; exercising unlimited power.
The autocratic regime suppressed all forms of protest.  这个专制政权压制了所有形式的抗议。
He made an autocratic decision without consulting his team. 他没有咨询他的团队就做出了一个独裁的决定。
近义词:despotic, tyrannical, dictatorial, authoritarian
反义词:democratic, egalitarian
词性:adj.

autocrat:/ˈɔːtəkræt/
noun
独裁者，专制君主
A ruler who has absolute power.
The autocrat demanded complete obedience from his subjects.  独裁者要求臣民完全服从。
She ruled as an autocrat, making all decisions without consultation. 她以独裁者的身份进行统治，所有决定都不进行协商。
近义词:dictator, despot, tyrant
反义词:democrat
词性:n.


词源:
From Ancient Greek αὐτοκράτωρ (autokrátōr, “self-ruler, emperor”), from αὐτός (autós, “self”) + κρατέω (kratéō, “to rule, be strong”).
