lustre:/ˈlʌstər/ (US) /ˈlʌstə(r)/ (UK)| n.|光泽，光彩；光辉|A gentle sheen or soft glow, especially that of a partly reflective surface. |The polished wood had a beautiful lustre.  抛光后的木头有着美丽的光泽。|Synonyms: sheen, gloss, shine, gleam, glimmer, radiance, brilliance|Antonyms: dullness, matteness|noun
lustre:/ˈlʌstər/ (US) /ˈlʌstə(r)/ (UK)| n.|荣誉，荣耀|Glory or distinction.|His career achieved a certain lustre after he won the Nobel Prize.  他获得诺贝尔奖后，他的职业生涯增添了一定的光彩。|Synonyms: glory, renown, prestige, distinction, honor|Antonyms: disgrace, dishonor, ignominy|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *lustre* (modern French *lustre*), from Italian *lustro*, from Latin *lūstrare* "to brighten, purify, illuminate," perhaps from a lost noun *lūstrum* related to *lūx* "light."
