dodging:/ˈdɒdʒɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈdɑːdʒɪŋ/ (US)| n. v.|躲避，闪避|The act of evading or avoiding something.|He was adept at dodging the paparazzi. 他很善于躲避狗仔队。|Synonyms: evading, avoiding, eluding, circumventing|Antonyms: confronting, facing, meeting|Noun/Gerund

dodging:/ˈdɒdʒɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈdɑːdʒɪŋ/ (US)|v.|躲避，闪避|To move quickly and suddenly in order to avoid being hit or caught.|She dodged the snowball thrown by her brother. 她躲开了她弟弟扔的雪球。|Synonyms: evading, avoiding, eluding, circumventing|Antonyms: confronting, facing, meeting|Verb/Present Participle


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *doggen, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to a similar word in Middle Dutch.
