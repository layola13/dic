esquire:/ɪˈskwaɪər/ /ˈɛskwaɪər/| n.|先生；律师|A title of courtesy, respect, or formality appended to a man's name. It is often used for lawyers. |Example 1: John Smith, Esquire. 约翰·史密斯先生。

Example 2:  The letter was addressed to David Jones, Esquire. 这封信寄给大卫·琼斯先生。|近义词：Mr., gentleman, attorney (for lawyers)|反义词:None|词性:noun


esquire:/ɪˈskwaɪər/ /ˈɛskwaɪər/| n.| 地主;乡绅 |Historically, a man of gentle birth, next in rank below a knight.  |Example 1:  In the past, the local esquire owned most of the land. 过去，当地的乡绅拥有大部分土地.

Example 2: He lived the life of a country esquire. 他过着乡绅般的生活。|近义词：landowner, gentleman|反义词:None|词性:noun
