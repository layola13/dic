impregnate:/ɪmˈprɛɡneɪt/ /ɪmˈprɛɡˌneɪt/ | vt. adj.| 使充满；使怀孕；浸渍；灌输| To fill something completely with a particular quality or substance; to make a woman or female animal pregnant. |

例句：
The air was impregnated with the smell of pine.
空气中充满了松树的气味。

The cloth was impregnated with a fire-retardant chemical.
布料浸透了防火化学物质。

He impregnated his wife.
他使他的妻子怀孕了。

The writer’s works are impregnated with his political beliefs.
这位作家的作品充满了他的政治信仰。


近义词：saturate, permeate, infuse, imbue, fertilize, inseminate | 反义词: drain, empty | 词性: verb, adjective

impregnate:/ɪmˈprɛɡneɪt/ /ɪmˈprɛɡˌneɪt/ | adj.| 充满的；浸渍的| Filled or saturated with something. |

例句：
The air was thick and impregnate with the smell of woodsmoke.
空气又浓又充满着木柴燃烧的烟味。

The old walls were impregnate with history.
古老的墙壁充满了历史的痕迹。


近义词：saturated, permeated, infused, imbued | 反义词: empty, drained | 词性: adjective


词源：Late Middle English: from Latin impregnare, from in- ‘into’ + pregnans, pregnant- ‘pregnant’.
