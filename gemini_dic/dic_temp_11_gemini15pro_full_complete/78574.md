giambeux:/ʒɑ̃ˈbø/| n.|护胫甲，腿甲|Armor for the legs, specifically below the knee. It can refer to a single piece or a pair.|The knight polished his giambeux until they gleamed. 骑士把他的护胫甲擦得闪闪发光。|The museum displayed a set of 15th-century giambeux.  博物馆展出了一套15世纪的护胫甲。|近义词：greaves, jambeau (singular)|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps consider unprotected legs)|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "giambeau," ultimately from Italian "gambale," meaning "legging" or "boot," derived from "gamba" meaning "leg."  The word reflects the purpose of the armor, which is to protect the leg.
