monothelite:/məˈnɒθəlaɪt/ | n. adj. | 一志论者，一志说的 |A person who believed in Monothelitism, a Christological doctrine that held that Christ had only one will, the divine will, despite his two natures, divine and human.|  The controversy surrounding the Monothelites caused significant division within the early Church. 一志论者引起的争论在早期教会内部造成了严重的分裂。 | Near Synonyms: Monophysite  | Antonym:  Dyothelite | noun, adjective

monothelite:/məˈnɒθəlaɪt/ | adj. | 一志论的，一志说的 | Relating to Monothelitism, a Christological doctrine that held that Christ had only one will, the divine will, despite his two natures, divine and human. | The monothelite doctrine was eventually condemned as heretical. 一志论最终被定为异端邪说。| Near Synonyms: Monophysite  | Antonym:  Dyothelitic | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Monothelitae, from Greek μονόθελητος (monothēlētos) 'having one will', from μόνος (monos) 'one, alone' + θέλητος (thēlētos) 'willed, wished'.
