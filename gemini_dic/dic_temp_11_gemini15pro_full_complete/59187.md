dysmorphia:/dɪsˈmɔːrfɪə/| n. |躯体变形障碍|A mental disorder characterized by the obsessive idea that some aspect of one's own body part is severely flawed and warrants exceptional measures to hide or fix it.  It is a preoccupation with an imagined defect in appearance or a markedly excessive concern about a slight physical anomaly that causes significant distress or impairment in social, occupational, or other important areas of functioning. |She suffers from body dysmorphia and is constantly worried about her nose. 她患有躯体变形障碍，总是担心自己的鼻子。

She was diagnosed with muscle dysmorphia, a type of body dysmorphia that causes people to obsess over their muscularity. 她被诊断出患有肌肉变形障碍，这是一种导致人们痴迷于自己肌肉发达程度的躯体变形障碍。|近义词:body image distortion, body dysmorphic disorder (BDD)|反义词:body acceptance|词性:noun
