cross-bencher:/ˈkrɒsˌbɛntʃər/ (US) /ˈkrɔːsˌbɛntʃər/ (UK)| n.|交叉议员；中立议员|A member of the House of Lords (UK upper house of Parliament) or other legislative body who is not formally aligned with any political party and sits on the crossbenches, which are the seats provided for such members.|Lord Smith, a respected cross-bencher, often cast the deciding vote.|Synonym: independent, non-partisan|Antonym: partisan, party loyalist|Noun

cross-benches:/ˈkrɒsˌbɛntʃɪz/ (US) /ˈkrɔːsˌbɛntʃɪz/ (UK)| n.|交叉议席|The seats in the House of Lords or other legislative body occupied by members who are not formally aligned with any political party.|The cross-benches were full during the debate on the controversial bill.|Synonym: neutral seats|Antonym: government benches, opposition benches|Noun
