overpartialities: /ˌoʊvərˌpɑrʃiˈælɪtiz/ | n. | 过分的偏袒，过度的偏爱 | Excessive favoritism or bias, often to the detriment of others. |  Her overpartialities towards her youngest child created resentment among her other children. 她对她最小孩子的过分偏爱引起了其他孩子的怨恨。 |  bias, favoritism, prejudice, unfairness | impartiality, fairness, objectivity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over" (excessive) + "partiality" (bias, favoritism). "Partiality" comes from Old French "partialité,"  from Late Latin "partialitas," from "partialis" (partial), ultimately from Latin "pars" (part).
