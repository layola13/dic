commercialized: /kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 商业化的，商品化的 | To make something commercial; to exploit something for profit. | The festival has become increasingly commercialized in recent years. 近年来，这个节日越来越商业化了。 | The once quiet beach has been completely commercialized with hotels and restaurants. 曾经安静的海滩已经被酒店和餐馆彻底商业化了。 |  Synonyms: marketed, merchandized, monetized | Antonyms: non-commercial, uncommercialized | adjective

commercialize: /kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪz/ | v. | 商业化，使…商业化 | To make something commercial; to exploit something for profit. |  They commercialized the invention and made a fortune. 他们将这项发明商业化，赚了一大笔钱。 | The company commercialized the new technology quickly.  这家公司迅速将新技术商业化。| Synonyms: market, merchandise, monetize | Antonyms:  decommercialize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From commercial + -ize.  "Commercial" comes from the Late Latin "commercialis," meaning "relating to trade," from "commercium" meaning "trade, commerce." The suffix "-ize" comes from the Greek suffix "-izein," meaning "to make, to do."
