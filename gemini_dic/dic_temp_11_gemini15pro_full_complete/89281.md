homodermy:/ˈhɒməʊˌdɜːmi/ /ˈhoʊmoʊˌdɜːrmi/ | n. |恒温性；同温性|The condition of maintaining a relatively constant body temperature, regardless of the surrounding environment.  This is achieved through internal physiological processes.|Birds and mammals exhibit homodermy. 鸟类和哺乳动物表现出恒温性。|近义词：homeothermy, endothermy|反义词：poikilothermy, ectothermy|词性: noun
Etymology: homo- (Greek: same) + -derm (Greek: skin, hide) + -y (suffix forming abstract nouns)  Indicates "same temperature" related to the body.  The term homeothermy is more commonly used.
