macerate:/ˈmæsəreɪt/| v. |浸渍；软化；消瘦；憔悴 |To soften or break down by soaking in liquid; to cause to become thin and weak.|The chef macerated the strawberries in sugar and balsamic vinegar. 厨师将草莓浸泡在糖和香醋中。|The illness macerated his body. 疾病使他的身体消瘦。|近义词: soak, steep, soften, weaken|反义词: harden, strengthen|verb

macerated:/ˈmæsəreɪtɪd/| adj. |浸渍的；软化的；消瘦的；憔悴的 |Softened or broken down by soaking; thin and weak.|The macerated fruit was perfect for the jam.  浸软的水果非常适合做果酱。|His macerated appearance worried his family. 他憔悴的样子令家人担忧。|近义词: soaked, steeped, softened, weakened|反义词: hardened, strengthened|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *maceratus, past participle of macerare "to soften, soak," from Latin macer "lean, thin."
