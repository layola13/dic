decommercializing: /diːkəˈmɜːʃəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 去商业化 | The process of removing commercial influences or characteristics from something, often with the aim of making it more accessible, less profit-driven, or focused on public good rather than private gain. | The government is decommercializing public broadcasting. 政府正在将公共广播去商业化。|  The museum is decommercializing its gift shop, focusing on locally made crafts instead of mass-produced souvenirs.  博物馆正在将其礼品店去商业化，专注于当地制造的手工艺品，而不是批量生产的纪念品。 | 近义词: demonetizing, deprivatizing | 反义词: commercializing, monetizing | 词性: 动词 (present participle)

commercialization: /kəˌmɜːʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 商业化 | The process of introducing a new product or production method into commerce—making it available on the market. | The commercialization of the internet has led to a boom in online businesses. 互联网的商业化导致了在线业务的蓬勃发展。| The commercialization of space travel is becoming a reality. 太空旅行的商业化正在成为现实。 | 近义词: monetization, commodification | 反义词: decommercialization | 词性: 名词

commercial: /kəˈmɜːʃəl/ | adj. n. | 商业的；商业广告 | Related to commerce or trade; an advertisement broadcast on television or radio. | The commercial success of the movie surprised everyone. 这部电影的商业成功让所有人感到惊讶。|  I saw a funny commercial on TV last night. 昨晚我在电视上看到一个有趣的广告。| 近义词: mercantile, business-oriented | 反义词: non-commercial, non-profit | 词性: 形容词，名词
