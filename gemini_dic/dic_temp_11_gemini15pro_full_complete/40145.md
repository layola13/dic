contempt:/kənˈtempt/ | n. | 轻蔑，蔑视；耻辱，不光彩；【律】藐视法庭（或国会） | The feeling that a person or a thing is beneath consideration, worthless, or deserving scorn. |  He showed utter contempt for his job. 他对自己的工作表现出极端的蔑视。 | synonyms: scorn, disdain, disrespect, disregard | antonyms: respect, admiration, reverence | noun

contemptuous:/kənˈtemptʃuəs/ | adj. | 轻蔑的，蔑视的 | Showing contempt; scornful. | She gave him a contemptuous look. 她轻蔑地看了他一眼。| synonyms: scornful, disdainful, disrespectful | antonyms: respectful, admiring, reverent | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French contempt, from Latin contemptus, past participle of contemnere "to scorn, despise," from com- intensive prefix + temnere "to slight, scorn."
