overdogmatism:/ˌoʊvərˈdɒɡməˌtɪzəm/| n. |过度教条主义; 过于武断|The state of being excessively dogmatic; adhering rigidly to doctrines or beliefs, often without sufficient evidence or consideration of alternative perspectives.|His overdogmatism alienated many of his followers. 他的过度教条主义疏远了他的许多追随者。|近义词：dogmatism, rigidity, inflexibility|反义词：open-mindedness, flexibility, tolerance|词性:名词

Etymology:
From 'over-' (prefix meaning "excessive") + 'dogmatism' (from Greek 'dogma', meaning "opinion, belief, tenet").
