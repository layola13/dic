outshaming:/aʊtˈʃeɪmɪŋ/
|n.|公开羞辱|The act of publicly shaming someone, especially online, for their perceived flaws or mistakes.  This often involves sharing personal information or photos without consent.|Her outshaming of her colleague on social media resulted in disciplinary action.|近义词: public shaming, humiliation, denigration|反义词: praising, commending|词性: Noun (Gerund)
Etymology:  A combination of "out" (meaning to reveal or expose) and "shaming" (the act of making someone feel ashamed).  "Shaming" itself comes from the Old English word "sceamu" meaning shame or disgrace.
