overindividualization: /ˌoʊvərˌɪndɪˌvɪdʒuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
| n. |过度个体化|The excessive emphasis on individual needs and desires, often at the expense of collective or societal well-being.  It refers to a societal trend where individual preferences and self-expression are prioritized over shared values and communal responsibilities.|The trend towards overindividualization in modern society can lead to social fragmentation.|近义词：atomization, fragmentation, isolation, excessive individualism|反义词：collectivism, communitarianism|词性: noun

Etymology:
"over-" (prefix meaning "excessive, too much") + "individualization" (noun from "individualize" + "-ation" suffix)
"individualize" comes from "individual" (from Latin "individuus" meaning "indivisible") + "-ize" (suffix meaning "to make or become").
