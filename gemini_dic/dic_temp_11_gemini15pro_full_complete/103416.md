jitterbugger:/ˈdʒɪtərbʌɡər/ | n. | 紧张不安的人；坐立不安的人 | A person who is nervous, restless, or fidgety. | He was a real jitterbugger before the exam, pacing back and forth and chewing his nails. 他考试前真是个紧张不安的人，来回踱步，咬着指甲。|  The child was a jitterbugger, unable to sit still for even a minute.  这孩子坐立不安，连一分钟都坐不住。 |  近义词: fidget, worrier, nervous Nellie | 反义词: calm person, relaxed individual | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  A blend of "jitterbug" (a fast, acrobatic dance popular in the 1930s and 40s, also referring to someone who does this dance) and "bugger" (a mildly vulgar term used as a general intensifier).  The word suggests someone so nervous they are like a frantic jitterbug dancer.
