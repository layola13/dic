fawningness:/ˈfɔːnɪŋnɪs/| n. |奉承；谄媚；奴颜婢膝|The excessive fawningness of the employee towards the boss was obvious to everyone. |例句：His fawningness towards the wealthy patrons was sickening. 他对有钱顾客的阿谀奉承令人作呕。

例句：The dog greeted its owner with enthusiastic tail wags and playful fawningness. 这只狗摇着尾巴，欢快地、谄媚地迎接它的主人。|近义词：servility, obsequiousness, sycophancy|反义词：independence, assertiveness, dignity|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From fawn (v.) + -ing + -ness.  The verb "fawn" comes from the Old English "fāgnian," meaning "to rejoice," later evolving to mean "to show affection" like a dog, and eventually taking on the negative connotation of excessive flattery or subservience.
