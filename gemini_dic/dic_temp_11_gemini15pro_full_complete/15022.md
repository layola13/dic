babism:/ˈbɑːbɪzəm/ | n. | 巴比教 | A religious movement founded in Persia in 1844 by Sayyid `Alí Muhammad, who proclaimed himself the Báb (Arabic for "gate") and a forerunner of a promised messianic figure.  It emphasized social reform and attracted many followers, leading to persecution by the established Shi`a clergy.  Many of its tenets were later incorporated into the Baha'i Faith. | The rise of Babism in 19th-century Persia challenged traditional religious authority. 巴比教在19世纪的波斯兴起，挑战了传统的宗教权威。

Babism spread rapidly despite government suppression. 尽管政府压制，巴比教仍然迅速传播。| 近义词: Baha'i Faith (in later development),  | 反义词:  Islam (specifically Twelver Shi'a Islam which it broke away from) | 词性: noun
