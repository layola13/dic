fulsomely:/ˈfʊlsəmli/| adv.|过分地，谄媚地，令人作呕地|Characterized by abundance, copiousness, or excess, often to the point of being offensive or insincere.  Originally, "fulsome" meant abundant or plentiful, but over time it acquired negative connotations.|He fulsomely praised the dictator, which made everyone uncomfortable. 他过分地赞扬了独裁者，这使每个人都感到不舒服。|近义词：excessively, profusely, effusively, insincerely|反义词: moderately, sparingly, sincerely|词性:副词
词源: From Middle English *fulsom, from Old English *fūlsum (“foul, loathsome, excessive”), equivalent to foul +‎ -some.  The semantic shift from "offensive, disgusting" to "excessive, overdone" and then to "insincere, flattering" occurred over several centuries.
