civilisable:/ˈsɪvɪlaɪzəbl/ /ˈsɪvɪlɪzəbl/
adjective
可开化的，可教化的
Capable of being civilized; susceptible to civilizing influences.
Many anthropologists believed that all human societies were civilisable. 许多人类学家认为所有人类社会都是可开化的。
The tribe, though primitive, proved to be civilisable and quickly adapted to modern ways.  虽然这个部落很原始，但事实证明他们是可以教化的，并且很快适应了现代生活方式。
近义词: educable, teachable,  reclaimable,  refinable
反义词: uncivilized, barbaric, savage
词性: adjective

词源:
From civilize + -able.  "Civilize" comes from French *civiliser*, from Latin *civilis* "relating to a citizen,"  ultimately from *civis* "citizen."
