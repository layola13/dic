monograph:/ˈmɒnəɡrɑːf/ (US) /ˈmɒnəɡræf/ (UK)| n. | 专著；专题论文 | A detailed written study of a single specialized subject or an aspect of it. |  Example 1: He wrote a monograph on the history of jazz. 他写了一本关于爵士乐历史的专著。

Example 2: Her monograph on butterflies of the Amazon rainforest is considered the definitive work on the subject. 她关于亚马逊雨林蝴蝶的专著被认为是该主题的权威著作。 | synonym: treatise, dissertation, study, paper | antonym:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps "overview," "summary," or "compendium" which suggest a broader, less focused treatment) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *monos* "single, alone" + *graphein* "to write"
