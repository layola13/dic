cloven:/ˈkloʊvən/| adj.|劈开的，分裂的|Divided into two parts, especially at the tip; split.|The devil is often depicted with cloven hooves. 魔鬼通常被描绘成有偶蹄。|近义词：split, cleft, forked|反义词:whole, undivided|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Middle English *cloven*, past participle of *cleven* (“to cleave”), from Old English *clēofan* (“to cleave, split”), from Proto-Germanic *kleubaną* (“to cleave”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵlewbʰ- (“to cleave, peel”). Cognate with Dutch *kloven* (“to cleave”), German *klieben* (“to cleave”), Gothic *𐌺𐌻𐌴𐌹𐌱𐌰𐌽* (kleiban, “to adhere, stick to”).
