confessional: /kənˈfɛʃənl/ (US), /kənˈfɛʃnəl/ (UK)
| n. adj.|忏悔室，忏悔的|A private booth where a person can confess their sins to a priest.  Relating to confession or the act of confessing.|例句：He entered the confessional to seek forgiveness. 他进入忏悔室寻求宽恕。|近义词：penitential, contrite|反义词：unrepentant, defiant|词性: 名词, 形容词
confessional: /kənˈfɛʃənl/ (US), /kənˈfɛʃnəl/ (UK)
| adj.|忏悔的，坦白的|Characterized by or relating to confession, especially of private matters or sins.|例句：Her memoir was deeply confessional, revealing intimate details of her life. 她的回忆录非常坦白，揭露了她生活的私密细节。|近义词：frank, candid, revealing|反义词：secretive, reserved|词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *confessionale*, from Medieval Latin *confessionale*, from Latin *confessio* (“confession”), from *confiteri* (“to confess”).
