cross-hatch:/ˈkrɒsˌhætʃ/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌhætʃ/ (UK)| v. n.|交叉影线；交叉排线|To mark with intersecting parallel lines, often at right angles.  As a noun, it refers to the pattern created by this action.|例句：He cross-hatched the area on the map to indicate rough terrain.  他在地图上用交叉影线标出崎岖的地形。

例句：The artist used cross-hatching to create a sense of depth in the drawing.  这位艺术家用交叉影线在画作中营造出一种纵深感。|近义词：shade, hatch|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly "smooth" for the resulting texture) |词性: verb, noun

cross-hatch:/ˈkrɒsˌhætʃ/ (US) /ˈkrɒsˌhætʃ/ (UK)| adj.|交叉影线的|Marked with intersecting parallel lines.|例句：The cross-hatch pattern on the fabric added a subtle texture.  织物上的交叉影线图案增添了微妙的质感。

例句：The architect specified a cross-hatch design for the metal grating.  建筑师指定了金属格栅的交叉影线设计。|近义词：hatched, crosslined|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly "plain" or "unmarked") |词性: adjective
