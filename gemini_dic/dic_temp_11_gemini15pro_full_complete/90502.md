huckster:/ˈhʌkstər/| n.|叫卖的小贩；推销员；骗子|A person who sells small items, either door-to-door or from a stall, often using aggressive and persuasive sales techniques.  Someone who promotes or sells something aggressively or dishonestly.|The huckster at the fair sold overpriced trinkets.  集市上的小贩兜售着价格过高的廉价饰品。|synonyms: peddler, hawker, vendor, salesman, charlatan, fraud, con artist|antonyms: customer, buyer, philanthropist|noun

huckster:/ˈhʌkstər/| v.|兜售；叫卖；推销|To promote or sell something aggressively or dishonestly.|He was huckstering his wares on the street corner. 他在街角兜售他的货物。|synonyms: peddle, hawk, tout, promote, plug|antonyms: buy, purchase|verb
