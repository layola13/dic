carbonyl:/ˈkɑːrbənɪl/ /ˈkɑːrbənl/ | n. |羰基|A divalent radical consisting of a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom (C=O). This group is characteristic of aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids, esters, amides, and other compounds. |The carbonyl group in formaldehyde is highly reactive.| 近义词:  oxo group (less common)|反义词: N/A |noun
