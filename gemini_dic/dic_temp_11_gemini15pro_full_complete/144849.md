over-officious: /ˌoʊvər əˈfɪʃəs/ | adj. | 过于殷勤的，多管闲事的 | Excessively eager in offering unwanted services or advice; interfering.  Characterized by excessive and unnecessary zeal in performing duties, especially in a way that is annoying or intrusive.  |  The over-officious waiter kept interrupting our conversation to offer more wine.  那个过于殷勤的服务员不断打断我们的谈话，推销更多葡萄酒。 | Synonyms: officious, interfering, meddlesome, intrusive, pushy | Antonyms: helpful, respectful, unobtrusive, discreet, reserved | Adjective

Etymology:
From "over-" (excessive) + "officious" (offering unwanted services). "Officious" comes from Latin "officiosus," meaning "dutiful, obliging," from "officium" (duty, service).
