albatross:/ˈælbətrɒs/ (British), /ˈælbatross/ (American)| n. |信天翁|A large seabird with long, narrow wings, known for its ability to fly long distances.  Also, something that hinders or burdens someone.|例句：The sailor considered the albatross an omen of good luck.  水手认为信天翁是好运的预兆。

例句：His past mistakes are an albatross around his neck. 他过去的错误是他沉重的负担。


近义词：seabird, pelagic bird, burden, impediment|反义词: (For the bird sense, no direct antonym, perhaps "land bird". For the figurative sense, perhaps "advantage", "boon")|词性:Noun (Singular)


词源: From Portuguese *alcatraz (ultimately from Arabic al-qaṭṭās “the pelican,” influenced by Latin albus “white”), originally referring to frigatebirds. The application to albatrosses arose from sailors' confusion between the two kinds of birds.
