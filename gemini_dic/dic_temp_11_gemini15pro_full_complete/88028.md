highball:/ˈhaɪbɔːl/| n. v.|高杯酒（一种鸡尾酒），以过高的价格估价，漫天要价|A mixed alcoholic drink consisting of a spirit and a mixer, such as ginger ale or soda water, served in a tall glass.  To estimate something at too high a price.|例句：He ordered a highball. 他点了一杯高杯酒。|例句：The contractor highballed the estimate for the renovations. 承包商对翻修工程的估价过高。|近义词：overcharge, overestimate, inflate|反义词：undercharge, underestimate, lowball|词性：名词，动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the railroad practice of raising a ball high on a pole to signal a clear track ahead, thus allowing trains to proceed at full speed (high ball).  This idea of "full speed ahead" was then transferred to drinking quickly, and then to the tall glass in which such drinks were served.
