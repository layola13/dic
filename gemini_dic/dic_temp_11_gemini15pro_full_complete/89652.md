hookworm:/ˈhʊkwɜːrm/| n.|钩虫|A parasitic nematode worm that inhabits the intestines of humans and other mammals, attaching itself to the intestinal wall and sucking blood. It can cause anemia and other health problems. |The doctor diagnosed him with a hookworm infection. 医生诊断他感染了钩虫。|Synonyms: Ancylostoma duodenale, Necator americanus|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From hook + worm.  "Hook" refers to the hooked shape of the worm's mouthparts, and "worm" refers to its elongated, worm-like body.
