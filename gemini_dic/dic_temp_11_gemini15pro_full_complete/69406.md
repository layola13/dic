feel-goodism: /ˈfiːlˌɡʊdɪzəm/
|n.| 欣快主义； feel-good;令人感觉良好的|A focus on positive news and happy endings, often at the expense of a realistic portrayal of problems.|The movie was pure feel-goodism, offering little substance but plenty of heartwarming moments.| 近义词: optimism, Pollyannaism | 反义词: pessimism, cynicism, realism | 词性: noun

Etymology:  A blend of "feel-good" (referring to something that evokes positive emotions) and "-ism" (a suffix forming nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, or practices).  "Feel-good" itself emerged in the mid-20th century.
