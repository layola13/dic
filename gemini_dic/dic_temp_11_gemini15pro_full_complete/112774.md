lubric:/ˈluːbrɪk/| adj.|光滑的; 润滑的;|Having a smooth or slippery surface.  Relating to or used for lubrication. |The engine needs lubric oil. 发动机需要润滑油。|Synonyms: slippery, smooth, oily, greasy|Antonyms: rough, dry, coarse|adjective


lubricate:/ˈluːbrɪkeɪt/| vt.|润滑; 使…光滑;|To apply a substance such as oil or grease to (something) in order to reduce friction between its moving parts.  To make (something) smooth or slippery.|He lubricated the hinges with oil. 他用油润滑了铰链。|Synonyms: oil, grease, anoint|Antonyms: dry, desiccate|verb


lubricant:/ˈluːbrɪkənt/| n.|润滑剂; 润滑油;|A substance, such as oil or grease, used to reduce friction between moving parts.|The mechanic applied a lubricant to the gears. 技工在齿轮上涂了润滑剂。|Synonyms: oil, grease, emollient|Antonyms: desiccant, abrasive|noun


lubrication:/ˌluːbrɪˈkeɪʃn/| n.|润滑; 润滑作用;|The action of applying a lubricant to something.|Regular lubrication is essential for the smooth running of the engine. 定期润滑对于发动机的平稳运行至关重要。|Synonyms: oiling, greasing|Antonyms: drying|noun
