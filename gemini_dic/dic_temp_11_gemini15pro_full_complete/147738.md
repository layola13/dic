overprocrastination:/ˌoʊvərproʊˌkræstɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. |过度拖延|The act of delaying tasks to the point of excessive stress and negative consequences, often involving elaborate planning and preparation that never leads to actual execution.| She suffered from overprocrastination, spending weeks researching the perfect productivity app but never actually using any of them. 她过度拖延，花了数周时间研究完美的效率应用程序，但从未真正使用过其中的任何一个。| 近义词:procrastination, analysis paralysis | 反义词: proactiveness, decisiveness | 词性: noun

词源: "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "procrastination" (from Latin "procrastinare," meaning "to put off until tomorrow").
