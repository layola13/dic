balaam: /ˈbeɪləm/ | n. | 巴兰 | A Mesopotamian prophet (diviner) in the Book of Numbers of the Hebrew Bible, hired by Balak, king of Moab, to curse the Israelites. Balaam is prevented by God from cursing the Israelites and instead blesses them. | Example: The story of Balaam is a cautionary tale about the dangers of greed and disobedience. 巴兰的故事是一个警示性的故事，讲述了贪婪和不服从的危险。 | Example: Like Balaam, he started out intending to do one thing, but ended up doing the opposite.  像巴兰一样，他开始打算做一件事，但最终却做了相反的事。| Synonym: prophet, soothsayer, diviner | Antonym:  (No direct antonym; consider "follower" or "believer" in the context of his eventual obedience to God) | Noun
