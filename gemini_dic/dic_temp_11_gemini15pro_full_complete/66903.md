expeditionary:/ˌɛkspɪˈdɪʃənɛri/| adj.|远征的，探险的|Relating to or forming an expedition.  Characterized by exploration or discovery.|The expeditionary force landed on the beach. 远征军登陆了海滩。|近义词：exploring, pioneering|反义词:domestic, settled|adjective

expeditionary:/ˌɛkspɪˈdɪʃənɛri/| n.|远征军士兵|A member of an expeditionary force.|The expeditionaries were well-equipped for their journey. 远征军士兵们装备精良，为他们的旅程做好了准备。|近义词：explorer, adventurer|反义词:civilian, resident|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *expeditionarius, from Latin expeditio (“preparation, arrangement, military expedition”), from expedire (“to set free, prepare, make ready”), from ex- (“out”) + pes (“foot”).  The original sense was related to freeing one's feet for a journey.
