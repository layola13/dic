outrider:/ˈaʊtraɪdər/| n.|骑兵侦察员，前驱，护卫，|a person in a group of vehicles, etc. who travels ahead or to the side to check for danger or give warning of its approach; a person, vehicle, etc. that forms part of a protective escort; a person, company, or thing that is ahead of or detached from the main group or force|The President's limousine was preceded by a phalanx of motorcycle outriders.|近义词：scout, escort, vanguard, forerunner|反义词:follower, rearguard|词性:noun

outrider:/ˈaʊtraɪdər/| n.|位于边缘或偏远地区的公司、团体或个人|a person, company, or thing that is situated on the edge or in a remote area|The small independent bookstore was an outrider in the literary world.|近义词：outsider, peripheral|反义词:insider, central|词性:noun
