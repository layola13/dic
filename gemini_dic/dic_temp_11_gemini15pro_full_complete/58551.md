dui: /dweɪ/ (Mandarin pronunciation - no direct English equivalent)|interjection, noun|对 (duì)|Used in Mandarin Chinese, "dui" has a wide range of meanings depending on context.  As an interjection, it signifies agreement, similar to "yes," "right," or "correct." As a noun, it can mean "opposite," "pair," or "versus."  It can also be used as a particle to indicate correctness or emphasis.|例句：他说得对。 (Tā shuō de duì.) He is right.


|近义词：(As interjection) yes, right, correct, exactly|反义词：(As interjection) no, wrong, incorrect|词性：Interjection, Noun

这对他来说很重要 (zhè duì tā lái shuō hěn zhòngyào) = This is very important to him.
你对这件事怎么看? (nǐ duì zhè jiàn shì zěnme kàn?) = What do you think about this matter?
这对我们来说是一个挑战 (zhè duì wǒmen lái shuō shì yīgè tiǎozhàn) = This is a challenge for us.
