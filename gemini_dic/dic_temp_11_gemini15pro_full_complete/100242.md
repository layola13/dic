interrupted: /ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪd/ (US) /ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪd/ (UK)| adj. v. | 中断的，被打断的 |  Stopped or broken the continuity of something. |  The meeting was interrupted by a phone call. 会议被一个电话打断了。 |  disrupted, disturbed, broken | continuous, uninterrupted | adjective, verb

interrupted: /ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪŋ/ (US) /ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪŋ/ (UK)| v. | 打断，中断 | To stop the continuous progress of an activity or process. | He interrupted me while I was speaking. 他在我讲话时打断了我。 |  disturb, disrupt, intrude | continue, proceed | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin interruptus, past participle of interrumpere (“to break between, interrupt”), from inter (“between”) + rumpere (“to break”).
