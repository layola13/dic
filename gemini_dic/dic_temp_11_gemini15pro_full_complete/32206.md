chicane:/ʃɪˈkeɪn/ | n. v. | 欺骗；诡计；刁难；（赛车道上的）S弯，减速弯 | A chicane is a deceptive scheme or artifice. As a verb, it means to deceive or trick someone. In motorsports, it refers to a sequence of tight turns, usually in an S shape, designed to slow down racers. |  The lawyer used legal chicanery to get his client off. 律师运用法律上的诡计帮他的客户脱罪。 | Synonyms: artifice, ruse, trickery, subterfuge, deception, fraud, wile, guile, machination | Antonyms: honesty, forthrightness, straightforwardness | noun

chicane:/ʃɪˈkeɪn/ | v. | 欺骗；刁难 | To chicane someone is to obstruct or delay them by using trickery or unfair tactics. | The company tried to chicane its competitors by spreading false rumors. 这家公司试图通过散布谣言来刁难竞争对手。 | Synonyms: deceive, trick, cheat, dupe, outwit, defraud, swindle | Antonyms: be honest, be straightforward | verb
