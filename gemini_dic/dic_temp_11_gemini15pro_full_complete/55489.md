distractible:/dɪˈstræktɪbl/| adj.|易分心的，注意力不集中的|Easily distracted; having one's attention readily drawn to other things.|The child was very distractible and couldn't focus on his homework.  这个孩子很容易分心，无法集中精力做作业。|Synonyms: inattentive, unfocused, scatterbrained|Antonyms: focused, attentive, concentrated|adjective

distractibility:/dɪˌstræktɪˈbɪləti/| n.|易分心，注意力不集中|The quality of being easily distracted.|His distractibility made it difficult for him to succeed in school. 他容易分心，这使他很难在学校取得成功。|Synonyms: inattentiveness, flightiness|Antonyms: focus, attention, concentration|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *distractus (past participle of distrahere, "to pull apart, divide, distract"), from dis- ("apart") + trahere ("to draw, pull").  The suffix -ible signifies "capable of being."
