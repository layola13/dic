outsiderness:/ˌaʊtˈsaɪdənəs/
|n.|
|局外人的身份; 外来性;  被排斥感|
|The state or quality of being an outsider; alienation or detachment from a particular group or society.|
|She felt a profound sense of outsiderness in the elite social circle.  她在精英社交圈里感到一种强烈的局外人感。|

|He wrote about the outsiderness of the immigrant experience. 他写了关于移民经历的局外性。|
|近义词：alienation, detachment, isolation, foreignness, otherness|
|反义词：belonging, inclusion, insiderness, acceptance|
|词性: noun|


outsider:/ˌaʊtˈsaɪdər/
|n.|
|局外人; 外行;  非会员|
|A person who does not belong to a particular group, organization, or society; someone who is not familiar with a particular subject or area of expertise.|
|He was treated as an outsider by the close-knit community. 他被这个紧密团结的社区视为局外人。|

|As an outsider, she had a fresh perspective on the issue. 作为一名局外人，她对这个问题有一个全新的视角。|
|近义词：stranger, alien, nonmember, foreigner|
|反义词：insider, member, native|
|词性: noun|


词源:
From outsider + -ness.  Outsider is from out + sider, with sider being related to the verb "sit".  Essentially, someone who "sits outside" the group.
