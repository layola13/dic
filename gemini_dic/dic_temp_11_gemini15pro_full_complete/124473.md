mouille:/mwiˈjeɪ/ (US), /muːˈjeɪ/ (UK)| adj. n.|湿润的，柔软的；法语中表示软腭音化|A mouillé sound is a palatalized consonant, typically involving movement of the tongue towards the hard palate.  As a noun, it can refer to this sound itself, or in older usage, a bog or marsh.|The 'gn' in French 'oignon' (onion) represents a mouillé sound.|近义词：(for sound) palatalized, softened; (for older meaning) bog, marsh, swamp|反义词：(for sound) hard, unpalatalized|词性: adjective, noun

Etymology: From Old French *moillier, from Latin *molliare, from mollis "soft."
