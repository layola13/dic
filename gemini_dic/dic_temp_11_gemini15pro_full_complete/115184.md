manichean:/ˌmænɪˈkiːən/ | adj. n. | 摩尼教的；二元论的；摩尼教徒 | Of or relating to Manichaeism, a religion founded in the 3rd century AD by Mani, characterized by dualism, especially regarding good and evil. Also, a believer in Manichaeism.  |  例句：His worldview was starkly Manichean, dividing everything into good and evil. 他的世界观是明显的二元论，把一切都分为善恶。

例句：The Manicheans believed in a cosmic struggle between light and darkness. 摩尼教徒相信光明与黑暗之间存在着宇宙间的斗争。 | 近义词：dualistic, gnostic | 反义词：monistic, unified | 词性: adjective, noun

manicheans:/ˌmænɪˈkiːənz/ | n. | 摩尼教徒 |  Plural form of Manichean, referring to followers of Manichaeism. | 例句：The Manicheans were persecuted in many parts of the world. 摩尼教徒在世界许多地方遭到迫害。

例句：Historians debate the precise beliefs and practices of the Manicheans.  历史学家们对摩尼教徒的确切信仰和实践争论不休。 | 近义词：dualists, gnostics | 反义词：N/A (No direct antonym for a group of people) | 词性: noun
