deliquesce:/ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛs/ | v. |  潮解，溶解 | To become liquid by absorbing moisture from the air. | The salt deliquesced in the humid air.  盐在潮湿的空气中潮解了。| melt, dissolve, liquefy | solidify, harden | verb

deliquescent:/ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛsənt/ | adj. |  潮解的，易潮解的 |  Tending to deliquesce; readily absorbing moisture from the air and becoming liquid. | Calcium chloride is a deliquescent substance. 氯化钙是一种易潮解的物质。|  hygroscopic, absorbent | non-hygroscopic, waterproof | adjective

deliquescence:/ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛsəns/ | n. | 潮解 | The process of deliquescing; the state of being deliquescent. | The deliquescence of the salt caused a mess. 盐的潮解造成了一片混乱。| melting, dissolving, liquefaction | solidification, hardening | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *deliquescere, from Latin deliquescere (“to melt away”), from de- (“down, away”) + liquescere (“to become liquid, melt”), from liquidus (“liquid”).
