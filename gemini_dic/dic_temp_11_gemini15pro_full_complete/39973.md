consubstantially:/ˌkɒnsəbˈstænʃəli/ /ˌkɑːnsəbˈstænʃəli/| adv.|同体的，同质的，本质上相同的|Sharing the same substance or essence; existing within one another in a unified way.  Relating to the doctrine of consubstantiation.|例句：The divine and human natures are believed to be consubstantially united in Christ.  人们相信神性和人性在基督里同质合一。

例句：The two elements are thought to exist consubstantially.  这两种元素被认为是同质存在的。|近义词：essentially, inherently, intrinsically|反义词:differently, separately|词性:副词

consubstantiation:/ˌkɒnsəbˌstænʃiˈeɪʃən/ /ˌkɑːnsəbˌstænʃiˈeɪʃən/| n.|同体说，同质说|The doctrine, especially in Lutheran belief, that the substance of the body and blood of Christ is present together with the substance of the bread and wine in the Eucharist.|例句：Consubstantiation differs from transubstantiation, the Catholic doctrine. 同质说不同于天主教的化质说。

例句：The debate over consubstantiation continues to this day. 关于同质说的争论至今仍在继续。|近义词：(none - this is a specific theological term)|反义词:transubstantiation|词性:名词

词源: From Late Latin *consubstantialis*, from con- ‘together with’ + substantia ‘substance’.
