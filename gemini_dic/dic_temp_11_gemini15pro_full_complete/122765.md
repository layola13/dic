moither:/ˈmɔɪðə(r)/ (British), /ˈmɑːðər/ (American)| v. n.|烦扰，纠缠；麻烦，困境|To bother, annoy, or pester someone; to be in a state of worry or confusion.|例句：Stop moithering me! 别烦我！|近义词：pester, bother, annoy, harass, trouble, worry|反义词:soothe, calm, please|词性:verb

moither:/ˈmɔɪðə(r)/ (British), /ˈmɑːðər/ (American)| n.|麻烦，困境|A state of worry or confusion; a troublesome or annoying situation.|例句：I'm in such a moither about this exam. 我对这次考试感到非常烦恼。|近义词：predicament, dilemma, quandary, trouble|反义词:peace, tranquility, calm|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *moyther, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *meyða (“to injure, hurt”).
