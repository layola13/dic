balancing:/ˈbælənsɪŋ/| n. v. adj.|平衡，均衡，权衡|The act of keeping something in a steady position so it does not fall.|She maintained her balancing act on the tightrope.|近义词：stabilizing, equalizing, counterbalancing|反义词:destabilizing, upsetting|noun

balancing:/ˈbælənsɪŋ/| n. v. adj.|平衡，均衡，权衡|The process of making something equal or fair.|The government is trying to balancing the budget.|近义词：adjusting, regulating, moderating|反义词:unbalancing, biasing|verb

balancing:/ˈbælənsɪŋ/| n. v. adj.|平衡的，均衡的|Relating to or used for balancing.|The balancing act of a unicyclist is impressive.|近义词：stabilizing, equalizing|反义词:unbalancing, destabilizing|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "balancer" (to sway, waver), from Late Latin "bilanx" (having two scales), from Latin "bis" (twice) + "lanx" (plate, dish, scale of a balance).
