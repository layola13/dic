deviability:/ˌdiːviːəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可偏差性，偏差度，变异性 | The deviability of the results indicates a problem with the experimental setup.  | 例句：The deviability of the test scores was high, suggesting a wide range of student abilities. 测试分数的偏差度很高，表明学生的能力范围很广。

例句：The study examined the deviability of the data from the expected norm. 这项研究检验了数据与预期标准的偏差。 | 近义词：variation, variability, divergence, fluctuation | 反义词：stability, consistency, uniformity | 词性: noun

词源: deviate (v.) + -ability (suffix)
From Late Middle English: *deviaten, from Latin deviatus, past participle of deviare ‘turn aside’, from de- ‘aside’ + via ‘way’. The noun is formed from the adjective deviated with the addition of the suffix -ability.
