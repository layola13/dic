chausses:/ˈʃɔːsɪz/
|n.|
|chausses (plural noun): (historical) Tight-fitting hose or leggings covering the legs and feet, often made of chainmail or other protective material, worn as part of medieval armor.|
|These chausses, crafted from finely linked mail, provided excellent protection for the knight's legs.|这些由精细连接的链甲制成的护腿，为骑士的腿部提供了极好的保护。|
|近义词: leggings, hose, stockings|
|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider loose-fitting garments like trousers or breeches)|
|词性: noun|


Etymology: From Old French "chausses," plural of "chausse," meaning "hose" or "stocking," derived from Late Latin "calcia" meaning "shoe" or "covering for the foot," ultimately from Latin "calceus" meaning "shoe."
