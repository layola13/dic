mispraise:/ˌmɪsˈpreɪz/| n. vt.|诽谤，诋毁；错误地称赞|An act of expressing disapproval of someone or something; undeserved or inappropriate praise.|He heaped mispraise on the new policy. 他对新政策大加批评。|近义词：criticize, condemn, censure, denigrate, disparage, slander, libel|反义词: praise, commend, laud, extol|词性:noun, verb

mispraise:/ˌmɪsˈpreɪz/| n. vt.|错赞，不恰当的赞扬|Praise that is undeserved or inappropriate.|She received mispraise for her role in the scandal. 她因在丑闻中扮演的角色受到了不恰当的赞扬。|近义词：misjudge, misrepresent|反义词: praise appropriately|词性:noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (wrongly) + praise.  The "mis-" prefix is from Old French mes- from Latin minus ("less"). "Praise" comes from Old French preisier from Latin pretiare ("to value").
