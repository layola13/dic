ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *æftan stearn "behind the steering oar," from æftan "behind" (from Proto-Germanic *aftan, from PIE *apo- "off, away") + stearn "steering oar, stern" (from Proto-Germanic *sturniz, from PIE *ster- "stiff").  The sense shifted from "behind the steering oar" to "at the back of the boat."
