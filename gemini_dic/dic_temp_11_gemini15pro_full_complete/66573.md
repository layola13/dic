exhilarates:/ɪɡˈzɪləreɪts/ | v. | 使高兴，使兴奋 | Makes someone feel very happy, animated, or elated. | The news of their victory exhilarated the entire team. 他们的胜利消息让整个团队兴奋不已。

The roller coaster ride exhilarated the children. 过山车让孩子们兴奋不已。


Synonyms: thrills, excites, stimulates, invigorates, enlivens
Antonyms: depresses, saddens, bores, discourages, weakens
Word type: Verb (third-person singular simple present)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin exhilaratus, past participle of exhilarare (“to gladden, cheer”), from ex- (“out, from”) + hilarare (“to make cheerful”), from hilaris (“cheerful”).
