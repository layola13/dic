overdoctrinaire: /ˌoʊvərˌdɑːktrɪˈnɛər/ | adj. | 过于教条的，过于理论化的 | Excessively doctrinaire; adhering rigidly to a doctrine or theory without regard to practical considerations.  Characterized by excessive adherence to a doctrine or set of beliefs, often in a rigid or inflexible manner. |  The government's overdoctrinaire approach to economic policy alienated many voters. 政府在经济政策上过于教条的做法疏远了许多选民。 |  dogmatic, rigid, inflexible, unpragmatic, doctrinaire | pragmatic, flexible, adaptable, open-minded, practical | adjective

Etymology:
From "over-" (meaning "excessively") + "doctrinaire" (from French "doctrinaire," meaning "theoretical," ultimately from Latin "doctrina," meaning "teaching").
