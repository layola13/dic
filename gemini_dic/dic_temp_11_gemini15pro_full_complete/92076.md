hyperpyrexia:/ˌhaɪpərpaɪˈrɛksiə/| n.|极度高热，超高热|An extremely high fever, typically above 41.5°C (106.7°F).  It is a medical emergency and can be life-threatening.|The patient presented with hyperpyrexia and seizures.|近义词: hyperthermia|反义词: hypothermia|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek ὑπέρ (hupér, “over, above”) + πῦρ (pûr, “fire”) + -exia
