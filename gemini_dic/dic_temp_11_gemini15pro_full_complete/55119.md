disruptions:/dɪsˈrʌpʃənz/
noun
中断，扰乱
a situation in which something is prevented from continuing in its usual way
例句：The storm caused major disruptions to train services.  暴风雨对火车服务造成了重大干扰。

例句：The construction work caused disruptions to traffic flow. 施工造成了交通中断。

近义词：interruptions, disturbances, disruptions
反义词：continuities, order, stability
词性：noun (plural)


disruption:/dɪsˈrʌpʃən/
noun
中断，扰乱
an interruption in the usual way that a system, process, or event works
例句：The strike caused widespread disruption to industry. 罢工对工业造成了广泛的破坏。

例句：The pandemic caused significant disruption to global supply chains.  疫情对全球供应链造成了重大干扰。


近义词：interruption, disturbance, upheaval
反义词：continuity, order, stability
词性：noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *disrupcioun, from Latin disruptiō (“a breaking asunder, bursting”), from disrumpō (“break asunder, burst”), from dis- (“apart, asunder”) + rumpō (“break, burst”).
