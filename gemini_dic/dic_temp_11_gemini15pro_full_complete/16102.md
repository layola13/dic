bandwagon:/ˈbændˌwæɡən/| n. |花车，潮流，浪潮|A wagon used for carrying a band in a parade, or figuratively, a popular activity or cause that attracts growing support.|例句：Many politicians jump on the bandwagon of popular opinion. 许多政客跟风赶时髦。

例句：The environmental movement has gained momentum, and more people are jumping on the bandwagon. 环保运动势头强劲，越来越多的人加入其中。|近义词：trend, fad, movement|反义词：unpopular, declining|词性:名词


bandwagons:/ˈbændˌwæɡənz/| n. |花车（复数），潮流（复数），浪潮（复数）|Plural form of "bandwagon," referring to multiple wagons used for carrying bands or multiple popular activities or causes.|例句：There are many political bandwagons during election season.  选举季节有很多政治潮流。

例句：Several bandwagons emerged in the music industry last year. 去年音乐界出现了好几种潮流。|近义词：trends, fads, movements|反义词：unpopular things, declining things|词性:名词
