loquaciousnesses:/ˌloʊkweɪʃəsnəsəz/
|n.|多话，饶舌|The state or quality of being very talkative.|Her loquaciousnesses during the meeting annoyed several colleagues. 她开会时的喋喋不休惹恼了几位同事。|Synonyms: talkativeness, garrulity, volubility|Antonyms: reticence, taciturnity, silence|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *loquācitās, from Latin loquāx (“talkative”) + -itās (“-ity”).  Loquāx is from loquor (“to speak”).
