carotid: /kəˈrɒtɪd/ (UK) /kəˈrɑːtɪd/ (US)| n.|颈动脉|Either of the two main arteries that carry blood to the head and neck.|Blockage of the carotid arteries can lead to stroke. 颈动脉阻塞会导致中风。|Synonyms:  (When referring to the artery itself) neck artery|Antonyms: None.|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek karotides (plural), literally "causing stupor," from karos "deep sleep."  Pressure on the carotid arteries can cause unconsciousness.  This came into Late Latin as carotis, then into Middle French and finally into English.
