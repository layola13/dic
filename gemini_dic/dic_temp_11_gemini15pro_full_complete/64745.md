escorts: /ˈeskɔːrts/ (US) /ˈeskɔːts/ (UK)| n. |陪同人员，护送者；护航舰；护卫队|People who accompany someone to give them protection or guidance, or ships or planes that accompany another vehicle to protect it.|The president's car was surrounded by police escorts. 总统的座驾被警察护送着包围着。|The battleship had a destroyer escort.  这艘战列舰有一艘驱逐舰护航。|近义词：guards, attendants, companions, convoy|反义词：none (no single direct antonym)|noun

escorts: /ɪˈskɔːrts/ (US) /ɪˈskɔːrts/ (UK)| vt. |护送，陪同|To accompany someone to give them protection or guidance.|He escorted her to the theatre. 他护送她去剧院。|Security escorted the protesters out of the building.  保安把抗议者护送出了大楼。|近义词：accompany, guide, usher, convoy|反义词：abandon, leave|verb
