acaleph: /əˈkælɪf/ | n. |水母；海蜇 | A free-swimming marine coelenterate with a gelatinous bell- or saucer-shaped body that is typically transparent and has stinging tentacles around the edge. |  The acaleph pulsed gently through the clear water.  水母在清澈的水中轻轻地搏动。

| The beach was closed due to an influx of acalephs.  由于大量水母涌入，海滩关闭了。 |Synonyms: jellyfish, medusa | Antonyms: None | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *acalēphe, from Ancient Greek ἀκαλήφη (akalḗphē, “sea-nettle”), perhaps related to κνίδη (knídē, “nettle”).
