demarcating:/ˌdiːˈmɑːrkeɪtɪŋ/
verb
划分界限; 标明界限
marking the limits or boundaries of something.
例句：The surveyor is demarcating the boundary between the two properties. 测量员正在标明两处房产之间的界限。

例句：The river demarcates the border between the two countries. 这条河是两国之间的边界。

近义词：separating, distinguishing, delimiting, outlining
反义词：connecting, joining, uniting, merging
词性: verb (现在分词)


demarcate:/ˈdiːmɑːrkeɪt/
verb
划分界限; 标明界限
to mark or establish the limits of something.
例句：The fence demarcates the boundary of the property. 篱笆标明了这块地产的界限。

例句：The new policy clearly demarcates the roles and responsibilities of each department. 新政策明确划分了每个部门的角色和职责。
近义词：define, delineate, separate, distinguish
反义词：unite, merge, combine, connect
词性: verb

demarcation:/ˌdiːmɑːrˈkeɪʃn/
noun
划分界限; 标定界限; 界限
the action of fixing the boundary or limits of something.
例句：The demarcation of the border was a complex process. 边界的划分是一个复杂的过程。

例句：There is a clear demarcation between the two groups. 这两个群体之间有明显的界限。
近义词：boundary, limit, separation, division
反义词：union, merger, connection, integration
词性: noun


词源：From the French *demarquer*, from *de-* (expressing removal) + *marquer* "to mark," from Germanic origin.
