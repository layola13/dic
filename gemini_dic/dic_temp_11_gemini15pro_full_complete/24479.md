brittle's:/ˈbrɪtlz/| adj.|脆的，易碎的|Possession form of "brittle", indicating something belonging to or associated with something brittle.  It's less common than using "of a brittle material/substance" etc. but grammatically correct.  Typically used before a noun related to the brittle object.|The brittle's edge crumbled easily. 这易碎物品的边缘很容易破碎。|Near synonyms: fragile's, breakable's (these are also uncommon and better replaced with "of a fragile/breakable material")|Near antonyms: resilient's, tough's (also uncommon and better replaced)|adjective (possessive form)
