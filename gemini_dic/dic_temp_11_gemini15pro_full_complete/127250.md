nautiloid: /ˈnɔːtɪlɔɪd/ | n. adj. | 鹦鹉螺；鹦鹉螺类的 | A mollusk of the class Cephalopoda, having a spiral, chambered shell, such as a nautilus.  A nautiloid is a type of cephalopod mollusk related to octopuses and squid, but characterized by its external shell. |  The pearly nautilus is a beautiful nautiloid.  珍珠鹦鹉螺是一种美丽的鹦鹉螺。| The fossil record shows a diverse array of extinct nautiloids. 化石记录显示了各种各样的已灭绝的鹦鹉螺。 | 近义词: cephalopod,  ammonite (for extinct forms) | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "octopus" or "squid" as contrasting cephalopods without external shells) | 词性: noun, adjective

nautiloid: /ˈnɔːtɪlɔɪd/ |  adj. |  鹦鹉螺似的 | Resembling or characteristic of a nautilus.  | The shell had a distinct nautiloid shape.  这个壳有一个明显的鹦鹉螺形状。 | The creature exhibited nautiloid characteristics.  这个生物展现出鹦鹉螺的特征。| 近义词:  spiral, chambered (referring to the shell) | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek "nautilos" (sailor) + "-oid" (resembling).  The name refers to the nautilus's supposed ability to sail by using its shell as a boat.  This is a misconception, although the nautilus can control its buoyancy and move vertically in the water column.
