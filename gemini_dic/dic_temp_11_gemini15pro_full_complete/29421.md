catafalque: /ˈkætəfɔːlk/ | n. | 棺架，灵柩台 | A decorated platform, often raised, on which a coffin or a body in an open coffin rests in state or is carried during a funeral procession. | The king's body lay in state on a magnificent catafalque in the cathedral. 国王的遗体安放在大教堂里一个华丽的棺架上供人瞻仰。

The catafalque was draped in black velvet and adorned with white lilies. 棺架上覆盖着黑色天鹅绒，并装饰着白色的百合花。 | synonyms: bier, hearse | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *catafalco*, from Old Italian *cadafalco*, probably from Vulgar Latin *catafalicum* (unattested), perhaps from *catta* "cat" (unattested) + *falco* "falcon". The original sense may have been a raised wooden structure used in siege warfare, from which the later sense of a funeral platform developed.
