depolish:/diːˈpɒlɪʃ/ | vt. | 除光，磨光，使失去光泽 | To remove the polish or shine from something; to make dull. | The constant handling has depolished the antique silver.  不断的搬运使古董银器失去了光泽。 | Synonyms: dull, tarnish, mat, roughen | Antonyms: polish, burnish, shine, gloss | verb

depolish:/diːˈpɒlɪʃ/ | vt. |  剥夺优雅，使粗俗 | To deprive of refinement or elegance; to vulgarize. |  His time in prison seemed to have depolished his manners. 他在监狱的时光似乎使他的举止变得粗俗。 | Synonyms: coarsen, vulgarize, degrade | Antonyms: refine, polish, civilize | verb
