chapped:/ˈtʃæpt/| adj.|皴裂的，裂开的|rough, cracked, and sore, especially from cold weather|My lips are chapped from the cold wind. 我的嘴唇被冷风吹裂了。|近义词:cracked, dry, rough, sore|反义词:smooth, soft|词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *chappen, from Old English *ceapian (“to buy, sell, bargain”), from Proto-Germanic *kaupijaną (“to buy”).  The original meaning was "to bargain," and the sense development is uncertain. It might be related to the idea of something becoming worn or damaged through use or exposure, as in bartering.
