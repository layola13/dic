falteringly:/ˈfɔːltərɪŋli/ | adv. | 犹豫地，支吾地，颤抖地 | In a hesitant or unsteady manner, often due to nervousness or lack of confidence. | She spoke falteringly, her voice barely above a whisper. 她说话断断续续，声音 едва слышно。| synonyms: hesitantly, uncertainly, waveringly, tentatively | antonyms: confidently, resolutely, firmly, decisively | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "falter" + "-ing" (present participle) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix). "Falter" comes from the Middle English "falteren," likely of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse "faltrask" (to stumble).
