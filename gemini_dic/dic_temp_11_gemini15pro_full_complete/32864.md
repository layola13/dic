cholochrome:/ˌkoʊləˈkroʊm/ | n. | 色洛西宾，一种虚构的致幻剂 | A fictional psychedelic drug from the novel "A Scanner Darkly" by Philip K. Dick, said to cause vivid hallucinations and time distortion.  It is not a real drug. | In "A Scanner Darkly," the protagonist's addiction to cholochrome leads to a fractured sense of self. 在《黑暗扫描仪》中，主角对色洛西宾的上瘾导致了自我意识的分裂。| Synonym: (in the context of the novel) Substance D, hallucinogen | Antonym:  sobriety drug | noun
