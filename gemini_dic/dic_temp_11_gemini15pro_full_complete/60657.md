electiveness:/ɪˈlɛktɪvnəs/| n. |选择性，选修性|The quality of being elective; the fact of being chosen by election.|The electiveness of the position ensured a lively campaign.  这个职位的选举性确保了一场激烈的竞选。|Synonyms: optionality, selectivity |Antonyms: obligatoriness, compulsion|noun


electiveness:/ɪˈlɛktɪvnəs/| n. |可选性，随意性|The quality of being optional or non-compulsory.|The electiveness of the course allows students to tailor their studies. 这门课程的可选性允许学生定制他们的学习内容。|Synonyms: optionality, voluntariness |Antonyms: requirement, necessity|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From elect + -ive + -ness.  Elect (verb) comes from Latin electus, past participle of eligere "to choose out, pick out," from ex- "out" + legere "to choose, read."  -ive forms adjectives, and -ness forms nouns denoting a state or quality.
