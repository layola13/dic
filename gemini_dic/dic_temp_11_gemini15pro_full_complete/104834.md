keelage: /ˈkiːlɪdʒ/
noun
船底费，停泊费
A charge payable for mooring a boat.
The harbor master charges £5 per day for keelage. 港务长每天收取 5 英镑的停泊费。
Synonyms: mooring fee, dockage, wharfage
Antonyms: N/A
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From keel + -age.  The suffix "-age" forms nouns denoting a charge or fee.  The word "keel" refers to the main structural beam of a ship, and "keelage" originally referred to a charge based on the length of a ship's keel.
