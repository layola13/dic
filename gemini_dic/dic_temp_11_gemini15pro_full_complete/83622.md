haemochromogen:/ˌhiːməˈkroʊmədʒən/ | n. |血色原|A coloured compound formed by the combination of haem with a nitrogenous base, such as globin or denatured globin.|The haemochromogen test is used in forensic science to detect the presence of blood.|Synonyms: heme compound, haemochrome |Antonyms: None |noun


haemochromogen:/ˌhiːməˈkroʊmədʒən/ | n. |血色素原|A compound formed by the combination of heme with a nitrogenous base.
