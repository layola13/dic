dissentaneous:/ˌdɪsenˈteɪniəs/
| adj. |
| 不同意的；不一致的；有分歧的 |
| Lacking agreement in sentiment or opinion; disagreeing.  Characterized by dissent. |
| The committee was dissentaneous on the proposed budget cuts. 委员会对提议的预算削减意见不一。|
| The dissentaneous voices grew louder as the meeting progressed. 随着会议的进行，反对的声音越来越大。|
| discordant, disagreeing, dissident, at odds, conflicting, divided |
| unanimous, agreeing, concordant, harmonious, of one mind |
| adjective |

Etymology:
From Latin "dissentire" (to disagree) + "-aneous" (forming adjectives).  "Dissentire" is formed from "dis-" (apart, not) + "sentire" (to feel, think).
