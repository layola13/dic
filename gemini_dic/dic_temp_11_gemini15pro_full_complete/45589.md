currant: /ˈkʌrənt/ (US), /ˈkʌrənt/ (UK)| n. |醋栗；无核小葡萄干|A small dried seedless grape, especially a black Corinth grape, used in baking. Also, a small berry from various shrubs related to the gooseberry. |例句：I added currants to the muffin batter. 我在松饼面糊里加了无核小葡萄干。

例句：The currant bushes in the garden are laden with fruit. 花园里的醋栗树上果实累累。|近义词：raisin (for dried currant), berry (for fresh currant)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

currant: /ˈkɜːrənt/ (US), /ˈkʌrənt/ (UK)| adj. |现在的；流通的|Belonging to the present time; happening or being used or done now.  Primarily used in the phrase "current events". While technically an adjective, its usage is almost exclusively within this fixed phrase. |例句：She keeps up with current events by reading the newspaper. 她通过读报纸来了解时事。

例句：The current political climate is very tense. 当前的政治气氛非常紧张。|近义词：present, contemporary|反义词: past, former, outdated|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English and Anglo-Norman *coraunt, variant of Old French corin (Modern French raisin de Corinthe), referring to grapes from Corinth, Greece. The spelling with two "r"s emerged in the 16th century, possibly influenced by the unrelated word "current" (flowing).
