groggier:/ˈɡrɑːɡiər (US)/ /ˈɡrɒɡɪər (UK)/| adj.|更昏沉的，更迷糊的|More groggy; more dazed and confused, especially from sleepiness, intoxication, or illness.|例句：I felt even groggier after that second cup of coffee.  喝了第二杯咖啡后，我感觉更昏沉了。

例句：The medication made him groggier than usual.  药物使他比平时更昏沉。|近义词：drowsier, sleepier, woozier, dazed, confused|反义词：alert, awake, clear-headed|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From groggy + -er (comparative suffix).  Groggy itself comes from "grog," a diluted rum drink named after Admiral Vernon, nicknamed "Old Grog" because of his grogram cloak.  The association with grog likely stems from the intoxicating effects of the drink.
