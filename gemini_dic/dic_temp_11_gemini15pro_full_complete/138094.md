normatively:/ˈnɔːrmətɪvli/| adv.|规范地；合乎规范地；按规范|In a way that relates to or is based on norms or standards.  It describes actions or situations that are considered standard or acceptable according to a particular set of rules or beliefs.|The question of whether people behave normatively is a complex one.  人们是否按照规范行事是一个复杂的问题。|The law normatively defines the rights and duties of citizens.  法律规范地界定了公民的权利和义务。|近义词：typically, standardly, usually, conventionally|反义词：abnormally, unconventionally, atypically|词性:副词
Etymology: From the adjective "normative" + the adverbial suffix "-ly". "Normative" derives from the noun "norm" (meaning "standard, pattern, rule"), which itself comes from the Latin "norma" (meaning "carpenter's square, rule, pattern").
