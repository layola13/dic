antiphrastic:/ˌæntiˈfræstik/| adj.|反讽的，用反语的|Using words in a sense opposite to their proper meaning; ironically.  Employing antiphrasis.|例句：Calling a tall person "Shorty" is an antiphrastic nickname.  把一个高个子叫做“矮子”是一个反讽的绰号。

例句：Her comment "Oh, brilliant!" after he spilled the coffee was clearly antiphrastic. 她在他洒了咖啡后说的“哦，太棒了！”显然是反讽。|近义词：ironic, sarcastic, sardonic|反义词：literal, sincere, straightforward|词性:adjective
ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἀντιφραστικός (antiphrastikós, “expressing the opposite”), from ἀντίφρασις (antiphrasis, “use of an opposite word”), from ἀντί (anti, “against, opposite”) + φράσις (phrasis, “diction, way of speaking”).
