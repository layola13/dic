abdicators:/ˈæbdɪˌkeɪtərz/| n. |退位者，放弃者|A person who abdicates, or renounces a throne, right, power, claim, responsibility, or the like. |The abdicators lived a quiet life after leaving the throne. 退位者离开王位后过着平静的生活。|The abdicators faced public criticism for their decision.  退位者因他们的决定而面临公众批评。|近义词：resigners, relinquishers|反义词：successors, inheritors|词性:noun

abdicate:/ˈæbdɪˌkeɪt/| v. |退位，放弃|To renounce or relinquish a throne, right, power, claim, responsibility, or the like, especially in a formal manner.  |例句：The king abdicated the throne in favor of his son. 国王退位，让位给他的儿子。|The CEO abdicated his responsibility and left the company in chaos. 首席执行官放弃了他的责任，使公司陷入混乱。|近义词：resign, relinquish, renounce|反义词：assume, usurp, retain|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *abdicatus*, past participle of *abdicare*  "to disown, renounce, reject," from *ab* "off, away from" + *dicare* "to proclaim, dedicate."
