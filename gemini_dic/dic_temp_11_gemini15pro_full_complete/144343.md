outstare:/ˌaʊtˈsteər/
| vt. | 瞪视；以目光压倒 | To stare at someone longer or more intensely than they stare at you, until they look away. | He outstared his opponent, refusing to yield. 他以目光压倒了他的对手，拒绝屈服。 |  I tried to outstare him, but he wouldn't look away. 我试图瞪着他，但他不肯移开视线。 | gaze down, stare down, glare at | look away, avert one's gaze | verb

From out- + stare.  "Out-" acts as an intensifier, implying exceeding or surpassing in the act of staring.
