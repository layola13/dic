free-float:/ˈfriː floʊt/ | n. | 自由流通股；自由浮动 | The proportion of a company's shares that are readily available for trading on the stock market. | Example: The company's free-float is relatively small, making it more susceptible to price volatility. 该公司的自由流通股相对较少，因此更容易受到价格波动的影响。| Example:  A large free-float generally contributes to higher liquidity.  大量的自由流通股通常有助于提高流动性。| Synonyms: floating stock, publicly traded shares | Antonyms: restricted shares, closely held shares | noun
