kosher:/ˈkoʊʃər/ (US), /ˈkəʊʃə(r)/ (UK)| adj. n.| 符合犹太教规的，犹太教的，可接受的，合法的 |  (adj.)  Fit or allowed for consumption or use according to Jewish dietary laws; (n.)  Food that is kosher. | Example (adj.): The restaurant serves kosher meals. 这家餐厅提供符合犹太教规的餐食。

Example (n.):  We bought some kosher for the Sabbath. 我们为安息日买了一些符合犹太教规的食物。 |  Synonyms: (adj.) permissible, acceptable, legitimate, lawful; (n.) kosher food | Antonyms: (adj.) non-kosher, treif (specifically for food), forbidden; (n.) treif (specifically for food) | 词性: adjective, noun

kosher:/ˈkoʊʃər/ (US), /ˈkəʊʃə(r)/ (UK)| n. | 犹太教规食品 | Food that fulfills the requirements of Jewish dietary law. | Example:  They only eat kosher. 他们只吃符合犹太教规的食物。 | Synonyms: kosher food | Antonyms: treif | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Yiddish כּשר‎ (kosher), from Hebrew כָּשֵׁר‎ (kashér, “fit, proper”).
