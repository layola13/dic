orthogeosynclinal: /ˌɔːrθoʊˌdʒiːoʊˈsɪŋklɪnəl/ /ˌɔːθəʊˌdʒiːəʊˈsɪŋklɪnəl/
adjective | 正地槽的 | Relating to or designating a geosyncline that develops along the margin of a continent, characterized by volcanic activity and intense folding and faulting. | The orthorgeosynclinal theory explains the formation of mountain ranges. 正地槽理论解释了山脉的形成。 | geosynclinal | None | adjective
etymology: ortho- (Greek orthos, "straight, right, true") + geosyncline (geo- "earth" + syncline "a downward fold in rock strata")
