give up the ghost: /ɡɪv ʌp ðə ɡoʊst/ | v. | 死，断气，放弃 | To die; to expire.  Also, to cease operating; to break down. |  例句：The old car finally gave up the ghost.  这辆旧车终于报废了。


例句：After a long illness, he finally gave up the ghost.  久病之后，他终于去世了。 | 近义词：die, expire, perish, pass away, kick the bucket | 反义词：live, survive | 词性：动词短语

give up the ghost: /ɡɪv ʌp ðə ɡoʊst/ | n. | 死亡，断气 | The act of dying.  (Less common usage.) | 例句：The give up the ghost of the old king marked the end of an era. 老国王的驾崩标志着一个时代的结束。


例句：The sudden give up the ghost of the company shocked everyone.  这家公司的突然倒闭震惊了所有人。 | 近义词：death, demise, passing | 反义词：birth, life | 词性：名词短语 (Less common)


词源: This idiom originates from the Bible (King James Version), specifically from the Book of Genesis (25:8), describing Abraham's death: "Then Abraham gave up the ghost, and died in a good old age, an old man, and full of years; and was gathered to his people."  The "ghost" here refers to the spirit or soul leaving the body.
