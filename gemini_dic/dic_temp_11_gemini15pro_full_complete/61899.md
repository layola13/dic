empty-headednesses:/ˌɛmptiˈhɛdɪdnɪsɪz/| n. |愚蠢，轻浮，头脑空虚|The state of being empty-headed; lack of intelligence or good sense.|Her empty-headednesses led her to make a series of poor decisions.|近义词：foolishness, silliness, stupidity, vacuity|反义词：intelligence, wisdom, sense, sagacity|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From empty-headed + -ness.  Empty meaning "containing nothing" comes from Old English *æmettig, from Germanic *amtijaz, related to Old Norse auðr "empty, desolate."  Headed from head, from Old English hēafod.  -ness is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being."
