embroilments: /ɪmˈbrɔɪlmənts/
n.
纠纷，纷争，卷入
The state of being embroiled in a conflict or difficulty; involvement in a complicated or unpleasant situation.
The politician found himself in a series of embroilments over campaign finance. 这位政治家发现自己卷入了一系列关于竞选资金的纠纷。
近义词:  complications, entanglements, involvements, disputes, conflicts
反义词:  agreements, harmonies, resolutions, solutions
词性: 名词 (plural)

embroilment: /ɪmˈbrɔɪlmənt/
n.
纠纷，纷争，卷入
The state of being embroiled in a conflict or difficulty; involvement in a complicated or unpleasant situation.
His embroilment in the scandal ruined his career. 他卷入丑闻毁了他的事业。
近义词:  complication, entanglement, involvement, dispute, conflict
反义词:  agreement, harmony, resolution, solution
词性: 名词 (singular)


词源:
From Middle French *embrouiller (meaning "to confuse, mix up, entangle"), from en- ("in") + brouiller ("to mix, stir up"). The English word "embroil" originally meant "to confuse or perplex," but its meaning shifted to "involve in conflict" in the 17th century.
