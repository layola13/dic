caddies:/ˈkædiz/ (US), /ˈkædiz/ (UK)| n.|球童；球袋；茶叶罐|A person who carries a golfer's clubs and offers advice.|The caddie offered advice on which club to use. 球童就该用哪根球杆提出了建议。|Synonyms: golf caddy, bag carrier|Antonyms: golfer (in a sense)|noun

caddies:/ˈkædiz/ (US), /ˈkædiz/ (UK)| v. (caddies, caddied, caddying)|当球童；(为…)背包；运送|To work as a caddie for a golfer.|He caddied for Tiger Woods when he was younger. 他年轻时曾为老虎伍兹当过球童。|Synonyms: bag, carry, tote|Antonyms: play (golf) (in a sense)|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Scottish Gaelic *caddie*, perhaps related to French *cadet*.
