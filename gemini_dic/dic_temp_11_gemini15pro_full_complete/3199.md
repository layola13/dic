affectation:/ˌæfɛkˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 做作；矫揉造作；装模作样 |  An affectation is behavior, speech, or writing that is artificial and designed to impress. | She spoke with a ridiculous affectation of a French accent. 她说话时故意装出法国口音，十分可笑。 | synonyms: pretense, artificiality, mannerism, pose, airs | antonyms: naturalness, genuineness, sincerity | noun

affectation:/ˌæfɛkˈteɪʃən/ | n. |  (尤指对某事物的)喜爱，钟爱 |  A particular liking or fondness for something.  | He had a peculiar affectation for loud ties. 他特别喜欢颜色鲜艳的领带。 | synonyms: fondness, liking, preference, penchant, partiality | antonyms: dislike, aversion | noun
