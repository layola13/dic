gangplank:/ˈɡæŋˌplæŋk/| n. |跳板；梯板|A movable bridge-like structure used for boarding or disembarking a ship or boat.|The pirates ran across the gangplank and onto the ship. 海盗们跑过跳板，上了船。|Synonyms: ramp, walkway, bridge, gangway|Antonyms: None|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "gang" (walkway, passage) + "plank" (a long, flat piece of timber).  The word "gang" in this sense comes from Old Norse "gangr" (a going, way).
