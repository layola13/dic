impishnesses:/ˈɪmpɪʃnɪsɪz/| n. |顽皮；淘气；恶作剧|The quality of being impish; mischievous playfulness.|His impishnesses were tolerated because of his youth. 他的顽皮行为因为他年纪小而被容忍了。|近义词：mischief, pranks, naughtiness|反义词：obedience, seriousness|词性:名词

词源: From impish + -ness.  "Impish" comes from "imp," originally meaning a small demon or evil spirit.  Over time, the meaning softened to mischievous child.  The suffix "-ness" turns the adjective "impish" into the noun "impishnesses."
