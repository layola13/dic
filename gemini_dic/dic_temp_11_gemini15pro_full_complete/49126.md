deism:/ˈdiːɪzəm/ /ˈdeɪɪzəm/| n. |自然神论；自然神教|The belief in a God who created the universe and then abandoned it, assuming no further control over it or human affairs.  It emphasizes reason and observation of the natural world over scripture or religious revelation. |例句：Deism was a popular philosophy among intellectuals in the 18th century. 18世纪，自然神论在知识分子中很流行。

例句：He subscribed to the tenets of deism, believing in a creator God but rejecting organized religion. 他赞同自然神论的原则，相信造物主上帝，但拒绝有组织的宗教。|近义词：theism, rationalism|反义词:atheism, agnosticism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *déisme*, from Latin *deus* ("god") + *-ismus* (suffix forming nouns of action, condition, doctrine).
