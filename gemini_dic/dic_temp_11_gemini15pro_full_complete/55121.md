disruptively:/dɪsˈrʌptɪvli/
| adv. |扰乱地，破坏性地|Causing or tending to cause disruption.|The new technology disruptively impacted the traditional industry.  这项新技术对传统产业产生了颠覆性的影响。|Example: The comedian disruptively interrupted the politician's speech.  这位喜剧演员扰乱了政治家的演讲。|Synonyms: disturbingly, destructively, radically|Antonyms: constructively, harmoniously, smoothly|词性:副词

disrupt:/dɪsˈrʌpt/
| vt. |扰乱，中断，破坏|Interrupt (an event, activity, or process) by causing a disturbance or problem.|The loud noise disrupted my concentration. 巨大的噪音打断了我的注意力。|Example: The storm disrupted the power supply to the entire city.  暴风雨中断了整个城市的电力供应。|Synonyms: interrupt, disturb, upset|Antonyms: organize, arrange, order|词性:动词

disruption:/dɪsˈrʌpʃən/
| n. |扰乱，中断，破坏|A disturbance or problem which interrupts an event, activity, or process.|The construction work caused a major disruption to traffic. 施工造成了严重的交通中断。|Example:  There was a disruption in the train service due to the snow. 由于下雪，火车服务中断了。|Synonyms: interruption, disturbance, disorder|Antonyms: order, organization, stability|词性:名词
