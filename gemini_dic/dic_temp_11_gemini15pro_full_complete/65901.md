evulgation:/ˌiːvʌlˈɡeɪʃən/ |n.| 泄露，暴露，传播 |The evulgation of the secret caused a scandal. 这个秘密的泄露引发了一场丑闻。|例句：The evulgation of the company's financial troubles led to a drop in stock prices. 公司财务困境的泄露导致股价下跌。

The evulgation of his private life by the media caused him great distress. 媒体对他私生活的曝光给他带来了巨大的痛苦。


|近义词：disclosure, revelation, divulgence, exposure|反义词：concealment, suppression|词性: 名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *evulgationem, accusative singular of evulgatio (“a publishing abroad, divulging”), from evulgare (“to publish, divulge”) + -tio.  Evulgare is from ex- (“out”) + vulgare (“to make common, publish”), from vulgus (“the common people, crowd”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *wel- (“to turn, roll”).
