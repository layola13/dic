drylander:/ˈdraɪˌlændər/| n.|陆地人，旱地人|A person who lives on dry land, especially one who is not used to or comfortable with being on or near water.|The drylander struggled to adapt to life on the island.|近义词：landsman, landlubber|反义词:seaman, mariner|词性:noun

Etymology:
From *dry* + *land* + *-er*.  Dry meaning arid or lacking moisture; land referring to the solid part of the earth's surface; and -er denoting a person or thing associated with a particular activity or quality.
