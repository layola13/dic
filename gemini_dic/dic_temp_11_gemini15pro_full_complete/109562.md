let fly:/lɛt flaɪ/
verb phrase
放飞; 发射; 突然说出; 猛烈攻击
To release something suddenly and with force, often referring to a projectile or a verbal attack.  Also, to give free expression to (one's feelings, etc.).
He let fly an arrow at the target. 他向目标射了一箭。
She let fly a string of curses at the driver who cut her off. 她对加塞的司机破口大骂。
近义词: unleash, release, discharge, fire,  vent
反义词: restrain, suppress, hold back
词性: 动词短语

let:/lɛt/
verb
让，允许
To allow or permit.
Let me help you with that. 让我帮你吧。
Let him go!  放他走！
近义词: allow, permit
反义词: forbid, prevent
词性: 动词

fly:/flaɪ/
verb
飞，飞行
To move through the air using wings.
The birds fly south for the winter. 鸟儿冬天飞往南方。
The plane will fly to Paris tomorrow. 飞机明天将飞往巴黎。
近义词: soar, flit, wing
反义词: land, descend
词性: 动词

fly:/flaɪ/
noun
苍蝇
A small insect with two wings.
There are too many flies in this room. 这个房间里苍蝇太多了。
A fly landed in my soup. 一只苍蝇掉进了我的汤里。
近义词: insect, pest
反义词: (无明显的反义词)
词性: 名词

词源:
"Let fly" originates from the literal act of releasing a bird or other projectile. "Let" in this context means "to allow" and "fly" refers to the action of flying.  Over time, the phrase evolved to encompass other forms of sudden release, including verbal expressions.
