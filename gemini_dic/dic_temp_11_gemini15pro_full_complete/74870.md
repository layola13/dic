frescoes:/ˈfrɛskoʊz/ (US), /ˈfrɛskoʊz/ (UK)| n. |壁画（尤指湿壁画，复数形式）|A painting done rapidly in watercolor on wet plaster on a wall or ceiling, so that the colors penetrate the plaster and become fixed as it dries. |The Italian Renaissance is famous for its frescoes. 意大利文艺复兴以其壁画而闻名。|The church walls were adorned with vibrant frescoes. 教堂的墙壁上装饰着充满活力的壁画。|synonyms: mural, painting, wall painting|antonyms: sculpture, statue|noun


frescos:/ˈfrɛskoʊ/ (US), /ˈfrɛskoʊ/ (UK)| n. |壁画（尤指湿壁画，单数形式）|A painting done rapidly in watercolor on wet plaster on a wall or ceiling, so that the colors penetrate the plaster and become fixed as it dries. |The artist spent years perfecting the fresco.  这位艺术家花费了数年时间完善这幅壁画。|Michelangelo's frescoes in the Sistine Chapel are masterpieces. 米开朗基罗在西斯廷教堂的壁画是杰作。|synonyms: mural, painting, wall painting|antonyms: sculpture, statue|noun


词源: From Italian *fresco* "fresh," ultimately from Frankish *frisk* "fresh."
