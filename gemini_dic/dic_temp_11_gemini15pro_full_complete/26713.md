cadet:/kəˈdɛt/| n.|学员，军校学生|A young person who is training to become an officer in the armed forces or police.|Example 1: The cadet was eager to begin his training at the naval academy.  这个学员渴望开始他在海军学院的训练。

Example 2:  The cadets marched in perfect formation. 学员们排着整齐的队形行进。|Synonyms: trainee, student, recruit|Antonyms: veteran, graduate, instructor|noun

Etymology: From French *cadet*, meaning younger son, ultimately from Gascon *capdèt*, diminutive of *cap*, meaning head, chief. The term originally referred to younger sons of noble families who, being ineligible to inherit the family estate, often pursued military careers.
