anachronistic:/əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪk/ | adj. |时代错误的；年代失调的 | Belonging or appropriate to an earlier period, especially so as to seem conspicuously old-fashioned. |  The writer included anachronistic details, like mentioning cell phones in a story set in the 1920s.  作者加入了一些时代错误的细节，比如在一个设定在20世纪20年代的故事中提到了手机。 | anachronous, outdated, old-fashioned | contemporary, modern, current | adjective

anachronism:/əˈnækrəˌnɪzəm/ | n. |时代错误；年代失调 | A thing belonging or appropriate to a period other than that in which it exists, especially a thing that is conspicuously old-fashioned. | The movie was full of anachronisms, like knights using machine guns.  这部电影充满了时代错误，比如骑士使用机关枪。 | misplacement, misdating, solecism |  accuracy, correctness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *anachronique*, from Ancient Greek *ἀνά* (aná, “against, back”) + *χρόνος* (khrónos, “time”).
