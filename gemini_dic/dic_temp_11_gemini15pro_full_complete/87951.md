high-commands:/ˌhaɪ kəˈmændz/ | n. | 高级命令，高级指令 | Commands issued by those in positions of authority, especially in military or organizational hierarchies. | The high-commands ordered a full-scale retreat. 高级指挥部下令全面撤退。 | The high-commands decided to launch a new offensive. 高级指挥部决定发动新的攻势。 | 近义词: directives, orders, instructions | 反义词: requests, suggestions | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
"high" (adjective) from Old English "hēah," meaning "tall, lofty, elevated, proud, noble."
"command" (noun) from Old French "comandement" and directly from Late Latin "commandamentum,"  from "commandare" meaning "to entrust, commit, order."
