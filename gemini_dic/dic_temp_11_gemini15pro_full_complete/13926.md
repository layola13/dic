aulnager:/ˈɔːlnədʒər/| n.| 检尺员，量布员|An official who inspected and measured cloth.  A person appointed to examine and seal cloth, ensuring it met the required standards of quality and measure.|The aulnager ensured the wool was of the correct weight and length.|Synonym: inspector, examiner, gauger|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "cheat" or "fraudster" in the context of dishonest practices)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *aulnage*, from Old French *aulnage*, from *aune* (meaning "ell," a unit of measurement), from Latin *ulna* (meaning "elbow, forearm," also a unit of measurement).
