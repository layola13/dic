drunk:/drʌŋk/| adj.|喝醉的，醉酒的|Affected by alcohol to the extent of losing control of one's faculties or behavior.|He was so drunk he couldn't stand up straight. 他醉得站都站不稳了。|近义词：intoxicated, inebriated|反义词:sober|词性:形容词

drunk:/drʌŋk/| n.|醉汉，醉鬼|A person who is intoxicated.|The police arrested a drunk who was causing a disturbance. 警察逮捕了一个正在闹事的醉汉。|近义词：alcoholic, inebriate|反义词:teetotaler|词性:名词

drunk:/drʌŋk/| v. (past tense of drink) |喝，饮|Past participle of "drink".  Has mostly archaic usage except in the phrase "drunk dry". |He had drunk all the wine. 他把所有的酒都喝光了. (Archaic, more common: He had *drunken* all the wine.) The well was drunk dry. 井里的水都喝干了。|近义词：imbibed, swallowed|反义词: - (No direct antonym for the past tense verb)|词性:动词

词源: From Old English *druncen*, past participle of *drincan* "to drink," from Proto-Germanic *drenkanan*, from PIE root *po- "to drink."
