adjure:/əˈdʒʊr/| v.|恳求；告诫；命令|To urge or request (someone) solemnly or earnestly to do something.  To command or enjoin solemnly, often under oath or the threat of a penalty.|The judge adjured the witness to tell the truth. 法官告诫证人要说实话。|Synonyms: beseech, entreat, implore, exhort, command|Antonyms: allow, permit|Verb

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *adjuren*, from Old French *adjurer*, from Latin *adiūrāre* (“to swear to, take an oath to”), from *ad-* (“to”) + *iūrāre* (“to swear”).
