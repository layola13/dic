detrition:/dɪˈtrɪʃən/
n.
磨损；损耗；消耗
the process of gradually reducing the strength or effectiveness of someone or something through sustained attack or pressure.
例句：The company suffered from employee attrition due to low salaries.  由于工资低，该公司员工不断流失。

例句：Years of wind and rain caused attrition of the stone monument.  多年的风雨侵蚀了石碑。

近义词：erosion, abrasion, weakening, decline, depletion
反义词：growth, accretion, accumulation, strengthening
词性：名词


detrition:/dɪˈtrɪʃən/
n.
(尤指牙齿的)磨损，磨耗
the process of wearing away by friction; abrasion.
例句：Dentists study tooth attrition patterns. 牙医研究牙齿磨损模式。

例句：The attrition of the grinding stones makes them need replacement regularly. 磨石的磨损使得它们需要定期更换。

近义词：abrasion, wear, erosion
反义词: accretion, buildup
词性：名词
