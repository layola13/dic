词源：
From Old English cristen (adj.) "Christian," from Latin Christianus, Greek Christianos, name applied to followers of Jesus Christ, said to have been first used in Antioch (Acts xi.26) about A.D. 43.  Ultimately from Greek Khristos "anointed," translation of Hebrew mashiah (see Messiah).
