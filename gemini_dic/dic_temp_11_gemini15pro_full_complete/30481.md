centrist:/ˈsɛntrɪst/| n. adj.|中间派，中间派的|A person who holds moderate political views, especially one who avoids extreme left-wing or right-wing doctrines.  A centrist position.|例句：The candidate positioned himself as a centrist to appeal to a wider range of voters.  这位候选人将自己定位为中间派，以吸引更广泛的选民。

例句：She is known for her centrist views on economic policy. 她以其在经济政策上的中间派观点而闻名。|近义词：moderate, neutral|反义词:extremist, radical|词性:noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From center + -ist.  Center from French centre, from Latin centrum, from Greek kentron "sharp point, stationary point of a pair of compasses, center."
