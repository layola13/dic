blinkering:/ˈblɪŋkərɪŋ/| n. |狭隘，蒙蔽，眼界狭窄|The act of limiting someone's perspective or understanding, often by preventing them from seeing alternative viewpoints or possibilities.  It can also refer to the use of blinkers on a horse to restrict its vision to the path ahead.|His blinkering prevented him from seeing the bigger picture.|近义词：narrowing, limiting, restricting, blinding|反义词: broadening, expanding, enlightening|noun

blinker:/ˈblɪŋkər/| n. |（马的）眼罩；转向灯|A piece of leather or plastic fitted to a horse's bridle to prevent it from seeing sideways. Also, a flashing light on a vehicle that indicates the driver's intention to turn.|The horse wore blinkers to keep it focused on the track.  The driver turned on his left blinker to signal a lane change.|近义词：(for horse blinkers) blinders, winkers; (for vehicle blinkers) indicator, turn signal|反义词: N/A|noun

blinkered:/ˈblɪŋkərd/| adj. |狭隘的，蒙蔽的，眼界狭窄的|Having a limited outlook or understanding, often due to being prevented from seeing alternative viewpoints or possibilities.|Her blinkered view of the world prevented her from accepting new ideas. |近义词：narrow-minded, limited, parochial, insular|反义词: open-minded, broad-minded, receptive|adjective
