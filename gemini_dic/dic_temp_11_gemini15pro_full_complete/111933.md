lollard:/ˈlɒlərd/ (British), /ˈlɑːlərd/ (American)| n. | 罗拉德教徒；宗教改革先驱 | A follower of John Wycliffe in the 14th and 15th centuries; a pre-Reformation religious reformer who advocated translating the Bible into English and challenged the authority of the Catholic Church. |  The Lollards were persecuted for their beliefs. 罗拉德教徒因其信仰而受到迫害。|Synonyms: heretic, dissenter, reformer, nonconformist | Antonyms: conformist, orthodox | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *lollaerd (“mumbler, mutterer”), from lollen (“to mumble”).  The term was originally derogatory, implying that the Lollards were unintelligible in their prayers and teachings.  It is related to the modern Dutch lullen (“to chatter, babble”).
