hypermetropia: /ˌhaɪpərˈmɛtrəʊpiə/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpərˈmɛtroʊpiə/ (US)| n.|远视|A condition in which close objects appear blurred because the eyeball is too short or the refractive power of the lens is too weak, so that the image is focused behind the retina.|She wears glasses to correct her hypermetropia.|近义词: farsightedness, longsightedness|反义词: myopia, nearsightedness|词性: noun
hypermetropic: /ˌhaɪpərˈmɛtrɒpɪk/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpərˈmɛtrɑːpɪk/ (US)| adj.|远视的|Relating to or affected with hypermetropia.|He has hypermetropic eyes.|近义词: farsighted, longsighted|反义词: myopic, nearsighted|词性: adjective

Etymology:  From Greek *hupérmetr*o*s*  "measuring over," from *hupér* "over" + *métron* "measure" + *-ia*
