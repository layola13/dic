hecatompedon:/ˌhekəˈtɒmpɪdən/| n.|百步殿，百尺殿|A temple, especially in ancient Greece, measuring 100 feet (approximately 30 meters) in length or width.  It often refers specifically to the ancient temple of Athena on the Acropolis in Athens.|The Parthenon, although larger, was sometimes referred to as a hecatompedon.  帕台农神庙虽然更大，但有时也被称为百步殿。|Synonyms: temple, shrine, sanctuary|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, could consider profane place)|noun

hecatompedon:/ˌhekəˈtɒmpɪdən/| adj.|百英尺长的，百英尺宽的|Measuring 100 feet in length or width.|The archaeologists discovered the remains of a hecatompedon structure. 考古学家发现了一座百英尺长的建筑遗迹。|Synonyms: hundred-foot|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|adjective
