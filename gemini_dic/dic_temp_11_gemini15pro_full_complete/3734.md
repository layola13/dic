agha:/ˈɑːɣə/ | n. |老爷，先生，长官 (尤指土耳其、伊朗等地对有地位男子的尊称)| A title of respect used in Turkey, Iran, and some other parts of the Middle East, especially formerly, for a man of high social standing, especially a landowner or military officer. |  The agha welcomed us to his home.  老爷欢迎我们到他家。|  The villagers sought the agha's advice. 村民们征求老爷的意见。| synonyms: sir, master, lord, chief | antonyms: servant, slave | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Turkish *ağa*, from Old Turkic *aγa* (“elder brother, paternal uncle, father, lord, master, senior member of a tribe”). Ultimately from Proto-Turkic *‎*aɣa* (“elder brother”).
