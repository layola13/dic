incidentalness:/ˌɪnsɪˈdɛntəlnəs/| n. | 附带性；偶然性；非必要性 |The incidentalness of the discovery made it even more exciting.  这个发现的偶然性使它更加令人兴奋。| incidentalness refers to the quality of being incidental, something that is not essential or a necessary part of something else. It describes something that occurs merely as a byproduct or an unintended consequence of something else. | The incidentalness of his remark did not escape my notice.  他话语的随意性我没有忽略。 |  incidentality,  nonessentiality, secondariness |  essentiality,  importance, necessity | noun
incidental:/ˌɪnsɪˈdɛntəl/| adj. | 附带的；偶然的；非必要的 |The incidental expenses of the trip added up.  旅行的杂费加起来了。| incidental describes something that is not essential or a necessary part of something else. It can refer to something that occurs merely as a byproduct or an unintended consequence of something else. It can also mean minor or of lesser importance. |  The meeting covered the main points, with only a brief mention of incidental matters. 会议涵盖了要点，只简要提到了次要事项。 |  minor,  secondary,  nonessential, accidental, casual |  essential,  important, necessary, crucial | adjective
