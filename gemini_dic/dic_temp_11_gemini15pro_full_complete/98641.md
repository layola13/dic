insubstantialities: /ɪnˌsəbˌstænʃiˈælɪtiz/ | n. | 无关紧要的事物；空洞无物；虚无缥缈之事 |  Lack of substance; things that are unimportant, trivial, or lacking meaning. |  He filled his speeches with insubstantialities, avoiding any real discussion of policy.  他讲话空洞无物，回避任何真正的政策讨论。| Synonyms: trifles, frivolities, trivialities | Antonyms: essentials, necessities, realities | Noun

insubstantiality: /ɪnˌsəbˌstænʃiˈælɪti/ | n. | 无关紧要；空洞无物；虚无缥缈 | The state or quality of lacking substance, importance, or meaning. | The insubstantiality of his arguments became clear upon closer examination.  仔细审查后，他论点的空洞无物变得显而易见。 | Synonyms: immateriality, emptiness,  flimsiness | Antonyms: substance, solidity, concreteness | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *insubstantialitas, from insubstantial + -itas.  Insubstantial is from in- "not" + substantial.  Substantial from Late Latin substantialis "pertaining to substance," from substantia "being, essence, material," from substare "stand firm, exist," from sub "under" + stare "to stand."
