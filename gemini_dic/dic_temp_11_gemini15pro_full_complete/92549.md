hyrst: /hɜːrst/ | n. | 小树林，树丛 | A small wood; a copse. |  He wandered through the hyrst, enjoying the dappled sunlight. 他漫步在小树林里，享受着斑驳的阳光。| Example: The birds were singing in the hyrst at dawn.  黎明时分，鸟儿们在树林里歌唱。| Synonym: copse, thicket, grove | Antonym: field, plain | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *hirst, *hurst, from Old English *hyrst, *hurst (“wood, thicket, forest, hill, sandbank”), from Proto-Germanic *hurstaz (“bush, thicket”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱers- (“to rise, stand out”). Cognate with West Frisian horst (“bush, thicket”), Dutch horst (“high place, shrub, mound”), German Horst (“eyrie, aerie, nest (of bird of prey), thicket”), Norwegian dialectal hurst (“juniper bush”). Related to harslet.
