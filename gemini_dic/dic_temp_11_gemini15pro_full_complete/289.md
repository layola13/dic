aber:/ˈɑːbər/| prep. conj.|但是，然而，可是|Used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or qualifies something that has just been said.|例句：He wanted to go, aber he didn't have time. 他想去，但是他没有时间。|近义词：but, however, yet, still, though, nevertheless|反义词: (No direct antonym for a conjunction.  Consider words like "therefore," "consequently" that indicate agreement rather than contrast.)|词性: conjunction, preposition

aber:/ˈɑːbər/| prep.|除了，除…之外|Except for; apart from.|例句：Aber einem kleinen Kratzer ist das Auto in Ordnung. 除了一个小刮痕，这辆车还不错。|近义词：except, apart from, besides, excluding|反义词: including|词性: preposition


Etymology:
From Middle High German *aber, from Old High German *abir, from Proto-Germanic *afar.  Ultimately related to English "off."  The original meaning was more spatial ("away from," "off"), which developed into the contrastive sense.
