invasivenesses:/ɪnˈveɪsɪvnəsɪz/| n. |侵略性，侵袭性|The quality or state of being invasive; the tendency to spread or intrude aggressively.  It refers to the ability of a species, disease, or other agent to establish itself in a new environment and spread rapidly, often to the detriment of native species or ecosystems.|The invasivenesses of certain plant species pose a serious threat to biodiversity.  某些植物物种的入侵性对生物多样性构成了严重威胁。| Synonyms: aggressiveness, intrusiveness, pervasiveness| Antonyms: docility, harmlessness|noun
