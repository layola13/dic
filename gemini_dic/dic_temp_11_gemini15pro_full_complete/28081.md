capillary:/ˈkæpɪˌlɛri/ /kəˈpɪləri/| n. adj.|毛细管；毛细血管；毛细作用的|A very thin tube, especially one of the many tubes that carry blood between the arteries and veins in the body. Also used as an adjective to describe things related to capillaries.|例句：The capillaries are the smallest blood vessels in the body. 毛细血管是人体中最小的血管。

|例句：Capillary action causes the water to rise in the tube. 毛细作用使水在管中上升。|近义词：blood vessel, tube|反义词:|n. adj.
capillary:/ˈkæpɪˌlɛri/ /kəˈpɪləri/| n. adj.|毛细作用|The ability of a liquid to flow in narrow spaces without the assistance of, or even in opposition to, external forces like gravity.|例句：Water moves through the soil by capillary action. 水通过毛细作用在土壤中移动。

|例句：The capillary action of the towel absorbed the spilled water. 毛巾的毛细作用吸收了溢出的水。|近义词：capillarity|反义词:|n. adj.
