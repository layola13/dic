buccaneer: /ˌbʌkəˈnɪr/ | n. | 海盗，掠夺者 | A pirate, especially one operating in the Caribbean during the 17th and 18th centuries, originally one who hunted wild oxen and pigs for their hides.  They were known for their ruthlessness and daring raids on Spanish ships and settlements. | The buccaneers sailed the Caribbean, searching for Spanish galleons laden with gold.  那些海盗在加勒比海航行，搜寻满载黄金的西班牙大帆船。 | Synonyms: pirate, corsair, privateer, freebooter | Antonyms: lawman, officer, sailor | noun
