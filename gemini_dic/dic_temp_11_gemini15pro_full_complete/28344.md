caraon: /ˈkærən/ | n. | 卡龙 (一种法式小圆饼)| A small, round, brightly coloured macaroon biscuit with a meringue-like consistency, typically made from ground almonds, sugar, and egg whites, and filled with buttercream, ganache, or jam. | She bought a box of colourful caraons from the bakery. 她从面包店买了一盒五颜六色的卡龙。| synonym: macaroon, macaron | antonym: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *carron*, ultimately from Italian *macarone* (see macaroon).  The spelling *caraon* appears to be a less common variant, possibly influenced by pronunciation or a misspelling.
