descry:/dɪˈskraɪ/ | v. | 发现；看出；辨认出 | To catch sight of something that is distant or obscure. |  From a distance, they descried a small boat on the horizon. 从远处，他们看到地平线上有一艘小船。

| Example 1:  Through the fog, I could barely descry the approaching ship.  透过浓雾，我几乎看不清驶来的船只。

| Example 2:  With a powerful telescope, he was able to descry details on the moon's surface.  借助强大的望远镜，他能够辨认出月球表面的细节。

| Synonym:  detect, perceive, spot, discern, espy | Antonym: overlook, miss | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *descrier "to make a proclamation; to cry out," from des- "down" + crier "to cry." The meaning shifted in English to "discover by looking" because of association with describe.
