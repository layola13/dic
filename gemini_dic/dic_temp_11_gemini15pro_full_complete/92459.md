hyposplenism:/ˌhaɪpoʊˈspliːnɪzəm/ /ˌhaɪpəʊˈspliːnɪzəm/
n.
脾功能减退;脾功能不全
Decreased or absent splenic function, resulting in a reduced ability to filter blood and remove damaged or abnormal red blood cells, and a decreased immune response to certain bacteria.
Hyposplenism can be caused by a number of factors, including surgical removal of the spleen, certain diseases, and some medications.  It increases the risk of serious infections.

Hyposplenism is a condition in which the spleen doesn't work properly. This can increase your risk of serious infections.
脾功能减退是一种脾脏功能不正常的疾病。这会增加您患严重感染的风险。

People with hyposplenism are more susceptible to infections.
脾功能低下的人更容易受到感染。

近义词:asplenia (完全没有脾脏功能)
反义词: hypersplenism (脾功能亢进)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
hypo- (prefix): from Greek, meaning "under," "deficient," or "less than normal."
splen- (root): from Greek "splēn," meaning "spleen."
