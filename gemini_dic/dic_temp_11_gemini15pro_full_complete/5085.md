allantois:/əˈlæntoɪs/
|n.|尿囊|A membranous sac that develops from the posterior part of the alimentary canal in the embryos of mammals, birds, and reptiles. It forms part of the placenta in mammals and plays a role in the respiration and excretion of the embryo.|The allantois is a crucial structure for embryonic development in many vertebrates. 尿囊是许多脊椎动物胚胎发育的关键结构。|The allantois helps the embryo exchange gases and handle waste products. 尿囊帮助胚胎交换气体和处理废物。|Synonyms: allantoic sac, allantoic vesicle|Antonyms: N/A|noun


allantoidea:/ˌælənˈtɔɪdiə/
|n.|尿囊类|A subclass of vertebrates characterized by the presence of an allantois during embryonic development.|Birds, reptiles, and mammals belong to the subclass Allantoidea. 鸟类、爬行动物和哺乳动物都属于尿囊类。|The allantois is a defining feature of the Allantoidea subclass. 尿囊是尿囊类亚纲的一个定义特征。|Synonyms: Allantoidia, Amniota|Antonyms: Anamniota|noun


allantoidian:/ˌælənˈtɔɪdiən/
|adj.|尿囊的|Relating to or characteristic of the allantois.|The allantoidian membrane plays a vital role in gas exchange. 尿囊膜在气体交换中起着至关重要的作用。|The allantoidian vessels are involved in nutrient transport. 尿囊血管参与营养物质的运输。|Synonyms: allantoic|Antonyms: N/A|adjective
