dives:/daɪvz/ | v. | 潜水；俯冲；跳水；急剧下降 |  goes down quickly, usually into water, or drops suddenly in amount or value | The plane dived steeply. 飞机急速俯冲。| synonyms: plunges, descends, drops, falls | antonyms: climbs, ascends, rises | verb (third-person singular simple present dives, present participle diving, simple past and past participle dived or dove)


dives:/daɪvz/ | n. |  潜水；俯冲；跳水 |  the act of going down quickly, usually into water, or a sudden drop in amount or value | She took a deep dive into the pool. 她在泳池里来了个深潜。| synonyms: plunges, descents, drops, falls | antonyms: climbs, ascends, rises | noun (plural dives)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dīfan (attested only in past participle gedifen "immersed, dipped"), from Proto-Germanic *dībaną ("to dip, dive"). Cognate with Old Norse dýfa ("to dip, dive"), Middle Dutch dūven ("to dive, duck, stoop"), Old High German tūfan ("to dip, immerse, baptize"), German taufen ("to baptize").
