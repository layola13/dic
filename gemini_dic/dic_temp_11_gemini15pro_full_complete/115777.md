marinism:/ˈmærɪnɪzəm/ | n. | 海事主义，马里尼主义 | A style of poetry and prose popular in 17th-century Italy and France, characterized by elaborate conceits, extravagant metaphors, and a focus on sensual and often erotic themes, especially those related to the sea. |  Marinism, named after the Italian poet Giambattista Marino, was a highly influential literary movement. 海事主义，以意大利诗人吉安巴蒂斯塔·马里诺的名字命名，是一个极具影响力的文学运动。|  conceitism, Gongorism, baroque |  classicism, simplicity | noun
