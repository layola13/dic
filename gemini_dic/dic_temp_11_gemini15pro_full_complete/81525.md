gravitate-toward:/ˈɡrævɪˌteɪt təˈwɔːrd/
verb
被吸引；倾向于；受吸引而转向
to be drawn toward something; to tend toward something
Young people tend to gravitate toward the cities in search of job opportunities. 年轻人往往为了寻找工作机会而涌向城市。
The conversation gravitated toward politics.  谈话渐渐转向了政治。
近义词: be drawn to, incline towards, tend towards, be attracted to
反义词: avoid, shun, repel from
词性: verb (phrasal verb)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *gravitatus*, past participle of *gravitare* "to weigh down, press heavily upon," from *gravis* "heavy."
