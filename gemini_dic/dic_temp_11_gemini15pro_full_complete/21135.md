blithenesses:/ˈblaɪðnɪsɪz/
n.
愉快，高兴，欢乐
The state of being blithe; happiness; cheerfulness.
Her blithenesses were infectious, brightening everyone's day. 她的快乐很有感染力，照亮了每个人的一天。
The children's blithenesses filled the air. 孩子们的欢声笑语充满了空气。
近义词：happiness, joy, gaiety, cheerfulness, merriment
反义词：sadness, sorrow, gloom, melancholy
词性:名词(plural)

blithe:/blaɪð/
adj.
愉快的；快乐的；无忧无虑的
Showing a casual and cheerful indifference considered to be callous or improper.
He spoke with blithe ignorance of the true situation. 他在谈话中对真实情况表现出轻松愉快的无知。
She walked with a blithe step. 她步伐轻快地走着。
近义词：cheerful, happy, merry, joyous, lighthearted
反义词：sad, unhappy, sorrowful, gloomy, melancholy
词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *blith, from Old English blíðe (“blithe, happy, kind, friendly, gracious, merciful, gentle, pleasant”), from Proto-Germanic *blīþiz (“mild, friendly, happy”). Cognate with West Frisian bliid (“glad, happy”), Dutch blijde (“happy, glad”), German blide (“cheerful, kind, gentle, pleasant”), Old Norse blíðr (“happy, friendly”). More at blithe.
