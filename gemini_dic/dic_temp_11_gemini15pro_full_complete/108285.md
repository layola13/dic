laurasia:/lɔːˈreɪʒə/ | n. | 劳亚古陆 | A large continental mass in the Northern Hemisphere that existed from the late Paleozoic Era to the Mesozoic Era, comprising present-day North America, Greenland, Europe, and Asia north of the Himalayas. It separated from Gondwana in the Mesozoic Era. | The breakup of Laurasia had significant impacts on the evolution of life on Earth.  劳亚古陆的分裂对地球上生命的进化产生了重大影响。 |  Fossil evidence suggests dinosaurs migrated freely across Laurasia. 化石证据表明恐龙在劳亚古陆上自由迁徙。| 近义词: Pangaea, supercontinent | 反义词: Gondwana, Gondwanaland | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From Laur- (combining form of Laurentia, the North American craton) + -asia (Asia).
