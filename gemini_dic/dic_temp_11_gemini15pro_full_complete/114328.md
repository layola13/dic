maister:/ˈmeɪstər/ (Archaic)| n.|大师; 老师; 先生|A skilled practitioner of a particular art or craft; a teacher or instructor; a form of address for a man, similar to "mister".|例句：The maister craftsman carefully shaped the wood. 大师级工匠小心翼翼地塑造着木头。

例句：The young apprentice learned much from his maister.  年轻的学徒从他的师傅那里学到了很多东西。|近义词：master, teacher, sir, mister|反义词:apprentice, student, pupil|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *maister*, from Old French *maistre*, from Latin *magister* ("master, chief, director, teacher, superintendent"), from *magis* ("more").  Cognate with Spanish *maestro*, Italian *maestro*, Portuguese *mestre*, and English *master*.
