ceremonials: /ˌserɪˈmoʊniəlz/ | n. |仪式，典礼|A set of formal acts, often religious or traditional, performed on a special occasion.|The ceremonials surrounding the coronation were elaborate and impressive. 加冕典礼的仪式非常精心和令人印象深刻。|Synonyms: rites, rituals, observances |Antonyms: informalities, casualness| noun

ceremonial: /ˌserɪˈmoʊniəl/ | adj. |仪式的，礼仪的|Relating to or used in a ceremony.|The ceremonial robes were richly embroidered. 礼仪长袍绣工精美。|Synonyms: formal, ritualistic, conventional |Antonyms: informal, casual, everyday| adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *ceremonial*, from Old French *ceremonial*, from Late Latin *ceremoniālis*, from Latin *cerimōnia* ("religious rite, ceremony").
