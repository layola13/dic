expiatory:/ɪkˈspiːətɔːri/
expiatory|adj.|赎罪的，补偿的|Relating to or intended to make amends or atone for a sin or wrongdoing.|例句：He performed an expiatory ritual. 他举行了赎罪仪式。|例句：She made an expiatory offering to the gods. 她向神明献上了赎罪祭品。|近义词：atonement, propitiatory, cleansing|反义词：unrepentant, unforgiving|词性:形容词
expiate:/ˈekspiːeɪt/
expiate|vt.|赎罪，补偿|To make amends or atone for (a sin or wrongdoing).|例句：He sought to expiate his sins through charitable work. 他试图通过慈善工作来赎罪。|例句：She expiated her guilt by confessing to the crime. 她通过坦白罪行来赎清自己的罪责。|近义词：atone, redeem, redress|反义词：commit, sin|词性:动词
expiation:/ˌekspiˈeɪʃn/
expiation|n.|赎罪，补偿|The act of making amends or atoning for a sin or wrongdoing.|例句：He sought expiation for his past misdeeds. 他寻求对其过去罪行的赎罪。|例句：The ceremony was an act of expiation for the community's collective guilt.  这个仪式是对社区集体罪恶的赎罪行为。|近义词：atonement, redemption, redress|反义词：sin, transgression|词性:名词
