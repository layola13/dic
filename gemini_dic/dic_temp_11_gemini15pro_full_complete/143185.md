outcompassed:/ˌaʊtkəmˈpæst/
Verb
超过，胜过，包围
To surpass in compass, extent, or scope; to exceed or outdo.
The Roman Empire outcompassed many smaller nations. 罗马帝国的疆域超过了许多小国家。

outcompassed:/ˌaʊtkəmˈpæst/
Verb
包围，环绕
To encircle or enclose completely.
The enemy army outcompassed the city, cutting off all supplies. 敌军包围了城市，切断了所有补给。


ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + compass (v.), from Old French compasser "to go around, measure," from com- "together" + passer "to pace, step."

SYNONYMS->
surpass, exceed, outdo, outstrip, outmaneuver, eclipse, circumvent, surround, enclose, encompass

ANTONYMS->
