monolith: /ˈmɒnəlɪθ/ (UK) /ˈmɑːnəlɪθ/ (US)| n. | 巨石，独块巨石; 庞然大物，整体; 巨大建筑物 | A large single upright block of stone, especially one shaped into a pillar or monument.  A large, powerful, and indivisible entity, such as a large company or a system of government.  |  例句：The company is a monolith in the software industry. 这家公司是软件行业的巨头。 例句：Ancient peoples erected monoliths as religious symbols. 古代人民竖立巨石作为宗教象征。| 近义词：pillar, obelisk, column, edifice, giant, behemoth | 反义词: fragment, piece | 词性: 名词

monolithic: /ˌmɒnəˈlɪθɪk/ (UK) /ˌmɑːnəˈlɪθɪk/ (US) | adj. | 巨大的，庞大的; 整体的，单一的; 坚不可摧的 | Formed of a single large block of stone. Large, powerful, and indivisible. | 例句：The government's monolithic control of the media stifled dissent. 政府对媒体的全面控制压制了异议。 例句：The monolithic structure of the company hindered innovation. 公司的整体结构阻碍了创新。| 近义词：massive, huge, immense, unified, solid, inflexible | 反义词: fragmented, diverse, flexible | 词性: 形容词
