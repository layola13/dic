imblazon:/ɪmˈbleɪzən/
verb
中文翻译：纹章，用徽章装饰；宣扬，颂扬
英文解析：To adorn or decorate with heraldic arms or ensigns; to display prominently or vividly; to celebrate or extol.
例句：
The knight's shield was imblazoned with a golden lion. 骑士的盾牌上饰有金色的狮子。

The story of their heroism was imblazoned across the newspapers.  他们的英雄事迹被各大报纸广泛宣扬。

近义词：decorate, adorn, embellish, proclaim, celebrate
反义词：obscure, conceal, hide
词性：v.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *emblaisoner, from en- "in, on" + blason "coat of arms," from Old High German blaso "horn signal, blast of a horn" (from Proto-Germanic *blas-, from PIE root *bhle- "to blow"). The sense evolution is from "to sound a horn signal announcing the arrival of a nobleman" to "to display his coat of arms" to "to decorate with bright colors."
