logical-empiricism:/ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ɛmˈpɪrɪˌsɪzəm/ (US) /ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ɛmˈpɪərɪˌsɪzəm/ (UK)| n.|逻辑经验主义|A philosophical movement that emphasizes the importance of empirical evidence and logical analysis in the development of scientific knowledge. It combines empiricism, the view that all knowledge comes from sensory experience, with a belief in the power of logical analysis to clarify concepts and theories.|Logical empiricism was a dominant force in philosophy during the first half of the 20th century. 逻辑经验主义是20世纪上半叶哲学中的主导力量。|synonyms: logical positivism, neopositivism|antonyms: idealism, rationalism|noun

logical-empiricisms:/ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ɛmˈpɪrɪˌsɪzəmz/ (US) /ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ɛmˈpɪərɪˌsɪzəmz/ (UK)| n.|逻辑经验主义（复数）|Plural form of logical empiricism, referring to multiple instances or variations of the philosophy.|The various logical empiricisms of the 20th century shared a commitment to empirical evidence.  20世纪的各种逻辑经验主义都致力于经验证据。|synonyms: logical positivisms, neopositivisms|antonyms: idealisms, rationalisms|noun
