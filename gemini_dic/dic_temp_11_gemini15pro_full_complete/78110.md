geological:/ˌdʒiːəˈlɒdʒɪkəl/ (US) /ˌdʒiːəˈlɒdʒɪkl/| adj.|地质的；地质学的|Relating to the study of the earth, especially its rocks, soil, and minerals, and its history and development. |例句：Geological surveys are essential for understanding the earth's structure. 地质调查对于了解地球的结构至关重要。

例句：The geological formations in this area are fascinating.  这个地区的地质构造令人着迷。

近义词：earth science, geomorphic|反义词:|词性:adjective

geologist:/dʒiˈɒlədʒɪst/ (US) /dʒiˈɑːlədʒɪst/| n.|地质学家|A scientist who studies geology. |例句：The geologist examined the rock samples carefully. 地质学家仔细检查了岩石样本。

例句：Geologists play a vital role in exploring for natural resources. 地质学家在勘探自然资源方面发挥着至关重要的作用。


近义词：earth scientist|反义词:|词性:noun

geology:/dʒiˈɒlədʒi/ (US) /dʒiˈɑːlədʒi/| n.|地质学|The science that deals with the earth's physical structure and substance, its history, and the processes that act on it. |例句：She studied geology at university. 她在大学学习地质学。

例句：Geology helps us understand the Earth's past and predict its future. 地质学帮助我们了解地球的过去并预测它的未来。

近义词：earth science|反义词:|词性:noun
