esqr:/ˈɛskwʌɪər/ | n. | Esquire (先生，律师)| A title of courtesy, formerly used for a man of some rank below a knight or gentleman, now chiefly given to lawyers. | Example: John Smith, Esqr.  约翰·史密斯先生 | Synonym: Mr., gentleman | Antonym: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French esquier (shield bearer, squire), from Late Latin scutarius (shield bearer), from scutum (shield). Originally denoted a squire, a young nobleman acting as an attendant to a knight. Later, it became a courtesy title, particularly for lawyers.
