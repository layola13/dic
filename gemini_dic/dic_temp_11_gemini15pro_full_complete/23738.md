brattinesses: /ˈbrætɪnɪsɪz/
noun
顽皮任性；娇蛮的行为（复数）
The state of being a brat; spoiled, childish behavior.  Specifically, it refers to multiple instances or displays of such behavior.
Her constant brattinesses exhausted her parents. 她不断的娇蛮行为让她的父母筋疲力尽。
The twins' brattinesses were on full display during the car ride.  双胞胎在坐车的时候把娇气任性表现得淋漓尽致。
synonyms:  tantrums,  spoiled behavior, childishness, petulance,  naughtiness
antonyms:  obedience, maturity, politeness,  respectfulness
n.

Etymology:
From brat + -iness + -es.  "Brat" originates from Old English "bratt" meaning "rag, cloth," later evolving to refer to a child, often in a derogatory sense.  "-iness" is a suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality. "-es" is the plural suffix.
