cleverishly: /ˈklɛvərɪʃli/
| adv. | 巧妙地，机灵地，精明地 | In a clever, sometimes excessively clever or showy way.  It often implies a slightly negative connotation, suggesting that the cleverness is superficial or used for deceitful purposes. | He cleverlyishly evaded the question. 他巧妙地回避了这个问题。 |  cleverly, artfully, ingeniously, shrewdly, slickly | stupidly, clumsily, awkwardly, foolishly | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From clever + -ish + -ly.  "Clever" comes from Middle English *clever, of uncertain origin. "-ish" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "somewhat like" or "having the quality of." "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives.
