gladful:  (This word is not standard English and is rarely used. It likely stems from a combination of "glad" and "full".) /ˈɡlædfʊl/ | adj. | 充满喜悦的；非常高兴的 |  A non-standard word combining "glad" and "full", suggesting a state of being very happy or filled with joy. While not incorrect, it's uncommon and might be considered informal or childish. |  She felt gladful upon receiving the good news. (她收到好消息后感到非常高兴。) |Near synonyms: joyful, delighted, elated, jubilant | Antonyms: sorrowful, unhappy, dejected | Adjective
