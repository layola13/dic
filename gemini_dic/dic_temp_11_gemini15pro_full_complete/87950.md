high-class:/ˈhaɪ klɑːs/| adj.|高级的，上等的|Of superior quality or prestige; intended for or used by wealthy or fashionable people.|They stayed in a high-class hotel. 他们住在一个高级酒店。|The restaurant offers a high-class dining experience. 这家餐厅提供高档的用餐体验。|近义词：luxurious, upscale, exclusive, posh|反义词：low-class, cheap, inferior|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From high + class.  "High" meaning elevated in rank, status, or quality, and "class" referring to a social group sharing similar economic, social, or cultural status. The combination emphasizes a class of high social standing and, by extension, the goods and services associated with that status.
