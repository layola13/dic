harlequinisms:/ˈhɑːrlɪkwɪnɪzəmz/
n.
丑角症；滑稽动作；小丑姿态
A pattern of congenital skin lesions distributed in a mosaic pattern, typically involving one side of the body.  This is caused by a genetic mutation affecting pigment cells during embryonic development.
Harlequinisms are often associated with other developmental abnormalities. 丑角症通常与其他发育异常有关。
Synonym:  mosaicisms, chimerism
Antonym:  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific medical condition)
