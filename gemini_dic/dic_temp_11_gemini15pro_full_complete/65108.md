ethereal:/ɪˈθɪəriəl/ /iˈθɪrɪəl/ | adj. | 天上的，非尘世的；飘逸的，轻盈的；精致的，超凡脱俗的 | Extremely delicate and light in a way that seems too perfect for this world. | Her ethereal beauty captivated everyone in the room. 她飘逸的美丽吸引了房间里所有的人。 | The music had an ethereal quality that seemed to transport the listener to another realm. 这音乐有一种空灵的特质，似乎把听众带到了另一个境界。 | 近义词：airy, celestial, delicate, heavenly, otherworldly, spiritual, sublime | 反义词：earthly, mundane, substantial, tangible | 词性: 形容词

ethereous:/ɪˈθɪəriəs/ /iˈθɪrɪəs/ | adj. | 天上的，非尘世的；飘逸的，轻盈的；精致的，超凡脱俗的 |  Characteristic of heaven; celestial.  | She possessed an ethereous beauty, as if she were a celestial being. 她拥有一种超凡脱俗的美，仿佛她是天上的生灵。 | The ethereous music filled the cathedral, inspiring a sense of awe. 空灵的音乐充满了大教堂，激发了一种敬畏感。| 近义词：airy, celestial, delicate, heavenly, otherworldly, spiritual, sublime | 反义词：earthly, mundane, substantial, tangible | 词性: 形容词


词源:  From Late Latin aethereus, from Latin aether + -eus. Ultimately from Ancient Greek αἰθήρ (aithḗr, “upper air; bright, purer air”).
