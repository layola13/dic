last-ditch:/ˌlæst ˈdɪtʃ/ | adj. | 最后的；孤注一掷的；绝望的 | Made as a final effort, especially when other methods have failed. |  The government made a last-ditch attempt to save the economy. 政府为挽救经济做了最后的努力。| Near synonyms: desperate, final, last-gasp, last-resort | Near antonyms: initial, first, opening | adjective


last-ditch:/ˌlɑːst ˈdɪtʃ/ | n. | 最后防线；最后手段 | A final line of defense or last resort.  |  Negotiations have failed, and the union is preparing for a last-ditch fight.  谈判失败了，工会正准备进行最后的斗争。 | Near synonyms: last resort, final stand | Near antonyms: first step, initial action | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From military fortifications, referring to the ditch at the back of a fortification, the last line of defense.  The figurative sense developed from this literal meaning.  "Ditch" comes from Old English "dīc" meaning "ditch, moat."
