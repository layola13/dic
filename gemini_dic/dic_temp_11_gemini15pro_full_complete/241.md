abdicator:/ˈæbdɪˌkeɪtər/| n. |退位者，放弃者|A person who abdicates a throne, right, power, claim, responsibility, or the like. | King Edward VIII was an abdicator of the British throne. 爱德华八世是英国王位的放弃者。| The CEO, facing mounting pressure, became an abdicator of his position. 面对越来越大的压力，这位CEO放弃了他的职位。|近义词：renouncer, relinquisher, forsaker|反义词：successor, heir, inheritor|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin abdicator, from Latin abdicatus, past participle of abdicare ("to renounce, abdicate").  From ab- ("away, from") + dicare ("to proclaim, dedicate, devote"). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *deik- ("to show, point out").
