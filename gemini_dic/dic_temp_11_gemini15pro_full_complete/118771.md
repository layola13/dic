metallochrome: /məˈtæləkroʊm/
n.
金属指示剂
A compound that forms colored complexes with metal ions, used in analytical chemistry to indicate the presence or concentration of these ions.
The metallochrome changed color, indicating the presence of calcium ions in the solution. 金属指示剂变色，表明溶液中存在钙离子。
synonyms: indicator, chelating agent
antonyms: N/A
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "metallo-" (relating to metals) + "-chrome" (color).  "Metallo-" ultimately derives from Greek "metallon" (metal). "-chrome" derives from Greek "chroma" (color).
