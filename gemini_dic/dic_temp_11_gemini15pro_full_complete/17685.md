bed:/bed/ | n. | 床，床铺，河床，苗床 | A piece of furniture for sleep or rest. | I made the bed this morning.  我今天早上整理了床铺。| synonym: cot, bunk, berth | antonym:  (No direct antonym for a physical object, could be considered conceptually opposite to "awake" or "active") | noun


bed:/bed/ | vt. | 安置，把…栽入苗床 | To put something in a bed, especially plants. | We bedded the seedlings in peat. 我们把幼苗栽种在泥炭里。| synonym: plant, embed | antonym: uproot, remove | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bedd*, from Proto-Germanic *badjaz*, from Proto-Indo-European *bhedh- (“to dig, pierce”). Cognate with Dutch bed, German Bett, Gothic 𐌱𐌰𐌳𐌹 (badi). The verb senses are derived from the noun.
