jacquerie:/ʒɑːkˈriː/
n.
农民起义；暴动
A revolt or uprising by peasants.
The jacquerie of 1381 in England, also known as the Peasants' Revolt, was a major uprising.
1381年英格兰的农民起义，也被称为农民起义，是一次重大的暴动。

The French jacquerie of 1358 was a brutal and bloody affair.
1358年的法国农民起义是一场残酷而血腥的事件。

近义词：rebellion, uprising, revolt, insurrection
反义词：pacification, suppression

词性: noun

词源: From French *jacquerie*, from *Jacques Bonhomme*, a derogatory nickname for a peasant.  The name *Jacques* was a common peasant name, and *Bonhomme* meant "good fellow."  The term became associated with the peasant revolts in France in the 14th century.
