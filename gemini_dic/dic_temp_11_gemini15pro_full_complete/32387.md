ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ciele, cele (“cold, coldness, chill”), from Proto-Germanic *kaliz (“cold, cool”). Cognate with West Frisian kjeld (“cold”), Dutch kil (“chill”), German Kiel (“keel, quill”). More distant cognates include Latin gelū (“frost”) and Ancient Greek χάλαζα (khálaza, “hail”).  The verb senses derive from the noun.
