词源：From fire + -er.  "fire" comes from Old English "fȳr," ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*fūr-" and Proto-Indo-European "*peh₂wr-," both meaning "fire." The suffix "-er" is used to form agent nouns, indicating someone or something that performs the action of the verb.
