hierarchy: /ˈhaɪərɑːrki/ (US) /ˈhaɪərɑːki/ (UK) | n. | 等级制度，层级，统治集团 | A system or organization in which people or groups are ranked one above the other according to status or authority. |  The corporate hierarchy is very rigid. 公司的等级制度非常森严。| synonyms: ranking, pecking order, chain of command | antonyms: equality, democracy | noun

hierarchy: /ˈhaɪərɑːrki/ (US) /ˈhaɪərɑːki/ (UK) | n. | 等级体系，层次结构 |  An arrangement or classification of things according to relative importance or inclusiveness. | The hierarchy of needs is a motivational theory in psychology. 需求层次理论是心理学中的一个动机理论。| synonyms: order, classification, system | antonyms: disorder, chaos | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *hierarchie*, from Late Latin *hierarchia*, from Greek *hierarkhia* "rule of a high priest," from *hierarkhes* "high priest," from *hieros* "sacred" + *arkhein* "to rule."
