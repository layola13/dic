cahenslyism: /ˈkeɪnzliɪzəm/ | n. | 凯恩斯主义 |  An economic theory advocating government intervention, through public spending and tax policies, to stimulate demand and stabilize the economy, especially during recessions. It is based on the ideas of British economist John Maynard Keynes. | The government's stimulus package is a clear example of Cahenslyism in action. 政府的经济刺激计划是凯恩斯主义的典型例子。|  Keynesian economics, fiscal policy, demand-side economics | Laissez-faire economics, supply-side economics | noun

Etymology:  Derived from the surname of John Maynard Keynes (1883-1946), a British economist.
