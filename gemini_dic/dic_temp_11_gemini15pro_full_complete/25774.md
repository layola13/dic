ETYMOLOGY->
From burden (n.) + -some (suffix forming adjectives meaning 'characterized by or tending to'). Burden comes from Old English byrðen "load, weight, duty, responsibility," from Proto-Germanic *burdinjo- (source also of Old Norse byrðr "birth, burden, load," Old Saxon burthina "burden," Middle Dutch borden, Dutch borden "plank," Old High German burdi "burden," German Bürde), from PIE root *bher- (1) "to carry," also "to give birth to."
