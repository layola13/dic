carmine:/ˈkɑːrmɪn/ /ˈkɑːrmaɪn/| n. adj. |胭脂红; 深红色; 洋红色|A vivid crimson or purplish-red color.  Can also be used as an adjective to describe something of this color.|The artist used carmine paint for the flowers. (n.)  The cardinal's robes were a vibrant carmine. (adj.)|近义词：crimson, scarlet, ruby, vermilion|反义词:pale, light|词性:名词, 形容词

词源: From Middle French *carmin*, from Medieval Latin *carminium*, from Arabic *qirmiz* (“kermes”), from Sanskrit *kṛmi* (“worm, insect”).  The dye was originally made from the dried bodies of the kermes insect.
