mutine:/ˈmjuːtɪn/ /ˈmjuːtɪnaɪ/| v. | 叛变，哗变 | To refuse to obey the orders of a person in authority, especially by sailors or soldiers against their officers. | The sailors mutinied against the captain.  水手们反抗船长。| 近义词：rebel, revolt, rise up | 反义词: obey, comply | verb

mutiny:/ˈmjuːtɪni/| n. | 叛乱，兵变 | Open rebellion against constituted authority, especially by sailors or soldiers against their officers. | The mutiny was quickly suppressed. 叛乱很快就被镇压了。| 近义词：rebellion, revolt, uprising | 反义词: obedience, compliance | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mutiner, from mutin (“rebellious”), from Italian mutino, diminutive of muto (“mute, silent”), from Latin mutus (“mute”).  The notion is perhaps of refusing to speak or communicate with the captain.
