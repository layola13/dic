obsoleting:/ˈɒbsəˌliːtɪŋ/
verb
使过时；淘汰
The new technology is obsoleting traditional methods.  新技术正在淘汰传统方法。
词性: v.
英文解析:present participle of obsolete, meaning to make something out of date or no longer in use.
例句：New technology is rapidly obsoleting existing computer systems. 新技术正在迅速淘汰现有的计算机系统。
例句：The company is obsoleting its older models of phones. 公司正在淘汰其旧款手机。
近义词：replacing, superseding, phasing out, retiring
反义词：updating, modernizing, reviving


obsolete:/ˈɒbsəˌliːt/
adjective
过时的；废弃的
This type of computer is now obsolete.  这种类型的计算机现在已经过时了。
词性: adj.
英文解析:no longer produced or used; out of date.
例句：Many traditional skills have become obsolete in the modern world. 许多传统技能在现代世界已经过时了。
例句：The old factory equipment is now obsolete. 旧的工厂设备现在已经过时了。
近义词：outdated, antiquated, archaic, old-fashioned
反义词：modern, contemporary, current, up-to-date


obsolescence:/ˌɒbsəˈles(ə)ns/
noun
过时；废弃
Planned obsolescence is a controversial marketing strategy.  计划性报废是一种有争议的营销策略。
词性: n.
英文解析:the process of becoming obsolete or outdated.  the state of being obsolete or outdated.
例句：The rapid obsolescence of technology is a challenge for many businesses.  技术的快速过时对许多企业来说是一个挑战。
例句：The product's short lifespan is a result of planned obsolescence.  该产品寿命短是计划性报废的结果。
近义词：decline, decay, disuse, superannuation
反义词：modernization, renewal, update
