expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/| adj. n.|权宜之计的，方便的；权宜之计|Something expedient is useful or helpful for a particular purpose, but it may not be morally right.|例句：It might be expedient not to tell her the whole truth just yet.  暂时不告诉她全部真相或许是权宜之计。|例句：The government chose the most politically expedient solution. 政府选择了政治上最权宜的解决方案。|近义词：convenient, advantageous, practical, resourceful|反义词：inconvenient, disadvantageous, impractical, unwise|词性:形容词,名词

expedients:/ɪkˈspiːdiənts/| n.|权宜之计|Methods used to achieve something, especially something that is not morally right.|例句：They resorted to various expedients to raise the necessary funds. 为了筹集必要的资金，他们采取了各种权宜之计。|例句：The company is accused of using illegal expedients to gain a competitive advantage.  该公司被指控使用非法手段来获得竞争优势。|近义词：measures, tactics, strategies, schemes|反义词：principles, morals, ethics|词性:名词
