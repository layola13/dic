obeisances: /oʊˈbeɪsənsiz/ /əˈbiːsənsiz/
n.
敬礼，鞠躬，尊崇
a gesture expressing deference or homage, such as a bow or curtsy

The knight made his obeisances to the king.  骑士向国王行礼。

The villagers paid their obeisances to the local deity. 村民们向当地神灵致敬。

Synonyms: bows, curtsies, respects, homage, tributes
Antonyms: disrespect, defiance, insolence
词性: 名词(plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *obeisance "obedience, submission," from obeissant "obedient," present participle of obeir "to obey" (from Latin obedire "to obey, be obedient to,")
