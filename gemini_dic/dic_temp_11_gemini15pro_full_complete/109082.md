legation:/lɪˈɡeɪʃən/
n.
外交使团；使节馆舍
A diplomatic mission to a foreign country, typically one headed by a minister.
The French legation in London has issued a statement. 法国驻伦敦使节团发表了一份声明。

legation:/lɪˈɡeɪʃən/
n.
派遣；委派（现在罕用）
The act of sending someone as a representative.
His legation to the conference was unexpected. 他被派去参加会议出乎意料。


Etymology:
From Late Latin *legatio, from Latin legatus (“envoy”), past participle of legare (“to send as an envoy, to depute, to bequeath”), from lex, legis (“law”).

Synonyms:
embassy, mission, consulate

Antonyms:
N/A (No direct antonym for a physical place or group of diplomats)
