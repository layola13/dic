impecuniously:/ˌɪmpɪˈkjuːniəsli/| adv.|贫困地，拮据地|In a way that suggests having little or no money.|He lived impecuniously, often relying on the generosity of friends. 他生活贫困，经常依赖朋友的慷慨。|Synonyms: poorly, indigently, penuriously |Antonyms: richly, wealthily, affluently|Adverb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *impecuniōsus, from Latin in- (“not”) + pecūnia (“money”).
