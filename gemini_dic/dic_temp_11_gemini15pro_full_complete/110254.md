lifestring:/ˈlaɪfˌstrɪŋ/| n.|生命线; 命脉; 救命索;|A vital lifeline or source of sustenance, often metaphorical, representing something essential for survival or success.  It can also refer to a literal rope or line used for safety.|The internet has become a lifestrin for many small businesses during the pandemic.  疫情期间，互联网已成为许多小企业的生命线。

The climber's lifestrin kept him from falling. 登山者的救命索防止他掉下去。|近义词：lifeline, support, mainstay, anchor|反义词: weakness, vulnerability|词性:noun

词源:  A compound word formed from "life" and "string."  "Life" refers to existence or survival, and "string" suggests a thin cord or line.  The combination implies a crucial connection to life or survival.
