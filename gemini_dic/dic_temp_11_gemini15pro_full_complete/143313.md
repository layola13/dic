outdwell:/ˌaʊtˈdwɛl/| v.| 居住得比…久；比…活得长 | To live longer than; to outlive.  | He outdwelled his wife by ten years. 他比妻子多活了十年。 | 近义词: outlive, survive | 反义词: predecease | 词性: 动词

Etymology:  From "out-" (meaning exceeding or surpassing) + "dwell" (to reside or live in a place).  The word "dwell" comes from Old English *dwellan, from Proto-Germanic *dweljaną (“to delay, hinder, lead astray, deceive”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰwel- (“to wander, go astray, err”).
