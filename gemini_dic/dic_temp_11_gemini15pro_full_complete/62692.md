engine:/ˈɛndʒɪn/|n.|引擎，发动机|A machine with moving parts that converts energy into motion.|The car's engine sputtered and died.|motor, machine|brake, obstacle|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French engin "skill, cleverness; war machine," from Latin ingenium "inborn quality, talent; clever invention," from in- "in" + gignere "to beget."  The sense shifted from "device" to "machine" in the late 14th century.
