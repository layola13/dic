hawebake: /ˈhɔːbeɪk/ | n. | 烤面包片; 烤面包 | A slice of bread that has been toasted, often spread with butter, jam, or other toppings. |  I had a hawebake for breakfast this morning. 我今天早上吃了一片烤面包。 | 近义词: toast, grilled bread | 反义词: (no direct opposite, perhaps "uncooked bread" or "dough") | 词性: noun

Etymology:  'Hawebake' is a Scottish and Northern English dialect word. It likely originates from the Middle English "haveke," meaning "baked oatcake."  Over time, its meaning shifted to encompass toasted bread.
