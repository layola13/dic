loudhailer:/ˌlaʊdˈheɪlər/ | n. |扩音器；喇叭|A device that amplifies the voice, used for addressing a crowd.|The police used a loudhailer to address the protesters. 警察用扩音器向抗议者喊话。|Synonyms: megaphone, bullhorn, amplifier|Antonyms: microphone (opposite function, not strictly an antonym)|noun


loudhailer:/ˌlaʊdˈheɪlər/ | n. |扬声器；喇叭|An electronic device that converts electrical signals into sound, often used to project the voice over a distance.|The captain addressed the passengers over the ship's loudhailer. 船长通过船上的扬声器向乘客讲话。|Synonyms: speaker, loudspeaker, PA system|Antonyms:  (no direct antonym, perhaps silencer/mute button for the opposite function)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "loud" + "hailer" (one who hails or calls loudly).  "Hail" comes from the Old Norse "heill" meaning "healthy" or "whole," later evolving to mean "to greet" or "call out."
