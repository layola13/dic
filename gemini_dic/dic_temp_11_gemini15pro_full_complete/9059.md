antimetabole:/ˌænti məˈtæbəli/ | n. |逆转反复, 交错配列法 |A rhetorical device that involves repeating a phrase or sentence in reverse order. |"Ask not what your country can do for you; ask what you can do for your country."  – John F. Kennedy (不要问你的国家能为你做什么，问问你能为你的国家做什么)|Synonyms: chiasmus, inversion, ananym | Antonyms: N/A | noun

PHRASE->
There's no real set phrase with "antimetabole" as it's a rhetorical device.  Instead, it’s identified by its structure.  Here are a few examples of famous antimetaboles that are commonly quoted:

"Eat to live, not live to eat." =  吃饭是为了活着，不是活着为了吃饭。
"Mankind must put an end to war – or war will put an end to mankind." – John F. Kennedy = 人类必须结束战争，否则战争将结束人类。 – 约翰·肯尼迪
"One should eat to live, not live to eat." – Socrates = 人应该为了活着而吃，而不是为了吃而活。 – 苏格拉底
"I know what I like, and I like what I know."  = 我知道我喜欢什么，我喜欢我所知道的。
"Fair is foul, and foul is fair" - Shakespeare (Macbeth) =  美好的是丑恶的，丑恶的是美好的 - 莎士比亚（麦克白）
