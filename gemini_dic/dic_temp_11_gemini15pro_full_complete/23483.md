brahans:/ˈbrɑːmənz/ /ˈbrɑːmɑːnz/ | n. | 婆罗门，梵天 |  Brahmans are the highest caste in Hinduism, traditionally priests and scholars.  They are considered to be at the top of the varna system.  The word is derived from the Sanskrit word "brāhmaṇa," meaning "one who has realized Brahman" (the ultimate reality).| Example: The Brahmans performed the sacred rituals. 婆罗门执行神圣的仪式。

Example:  He was born into a Brahman family. 他出生在一个婆罗门家庭。 | Synonym: Brahmin | Antonym:  (No direct antonym; perhaps consider lower castes like Kshatriya, Vaishya, or Shudra depending on the context.) | noun
