disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/ /dɪsˈdeɪn/
verb|鄙视，蔑视|To consider someone or something as unworthy of one's respect or attention.|She disdained to answer his rude question. 她不屑于回答他粗鲁的问题。|近义词：scorn, despise, contemn|反义词：admire, respect, esteem|词性:v.
disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/ /dɪsˈdeɪn/
noun|鄙视，蔑视|A feeling of contempt or scorn.|He looked at her with disdain. 他带着鄙视的眼神看着她。|近义词：contempt, scorn, derision|反义词：admiration, respect, esteem|词性:n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desdeigner*, from *des-* (expressing negation) + *deigner* "to deem worthy," from Latin *dignari* "to deem worthy," from *dignus* "worthy."
