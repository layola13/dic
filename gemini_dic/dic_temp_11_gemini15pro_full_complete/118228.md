mercaptide:/mərˈkæptaɪd/| n. |硫醇盐；硫醇化物|A compound formed by replacing the hydrogen atom of a thiol group (-SH) with a metal, typically an alkali metal or a heavy metal.  It's the sulfur analog of an alkoxide.|Sodium ethanethiolate is a mercaptide used in organic synthesis.|Synonym: thiolate|Antonym: N/A|noun
