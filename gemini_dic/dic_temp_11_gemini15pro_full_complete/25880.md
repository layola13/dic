burlesque:/bərˈlɛsk/ | n. adj. |滑稽模仿；滑稽讽刺作品；滑稽剧； |A burlesque is a literary or dramatic work that makes fun of something by imitating it in a humorous way. As an adjective, it describes something that is intended to be humorous, especially by imitating something serious in a playful way. | The play was a burlesque of Shakespearean tragedies. 这部戏是对莎士比亚悲剧的滑稽模仿。

The comedian's burlesque of the president was hilarious.  这位喜剧演员对总统的滑稽模仿令人捧腹。| 近义词：parody, satire, lampoon, caricature | 反义词：serious, respectful | 词性：noun, adjective

burlesque:/bərˈlɛsk/ | vt. | 滑稽地模仿；讽刺 |To burlesque something means to represent it in a ludicrous or mocking way. | The actors burlesqued the pompous politician.  演员们滑稽地模仿了那位自负的政客。

They burlesqued the opera with a funny rendition. 他们用滑稽的表演来讽刺这部歌剧。 | 近义词：mock, parody, satirize | 反义词：honor, praise | 词性：verb


词源: Italian *burlesco*, from *burla* 'joke, mockery'. Ultimately from Vulgar Latin *burra* 'wool, trifle'.
