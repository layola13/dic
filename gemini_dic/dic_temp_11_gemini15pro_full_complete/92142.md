hypersensitivity:/ˌhaɪpərˌsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪti/| n. |过敏症，过度敏感|Excessive or abnormal sensitivity, as of the skin or a sense organ, to certain stimuli.|She has a hypersensitivity to sunlight. 她对阳光过敏。|近义词:allergy, intolerance|反义词:hyposensitivity, insensitivity|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- + sensitivity, from sensitive + -ity.  "Hyper-" is a Greek prefix meaning "over, above, beyond." "Sensitive" comes from Late Latin sensitivus, from sentire "to feel." "-ity" is a suffix forming nouns of quality or state.
