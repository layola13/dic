misperceiving:/ˌmɪspərˈsiːvɪŋ/
|v.|错误地感知；误解|
|The public is misperceiving the government's intentions. 公众误解了政府的意图。|
|misinterpret, misunderstand, misconstrue|
|perceive correctly, understand correctly, interpret correctly|
|verb|


ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (prefix meaning 'wrongly' or 'badly') + perceiving (present participle of the verb 'perceive', from Latin percipere 'seize entirely, feel, understand', from per- 'thoroughly' + capere 'to take').
