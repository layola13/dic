cracker:/ˈkrækər/ (US) /ˈkrækə(r)/ (UK)| n.|薄脆饼干；鞭炮；解密高手|A thin, crisp biscuit/cookie, often eaten with cheese; a firework that makes a sharp cracking sound when exploded; a person who breaks into computer systems.|

例句：
I like to eat cheese and crackers. 我喜欢吃奶酪和饼干。

He lit a cracker on the Fourth of July. 他在七月四日点燃了一个鞭炮。

The cracker bypassed the security system. 这个解密高手绕过了安全系统。


近义词：biscuit, cookie (for food); firecracker (for firework); hacker (for computer cracker)|
反义词: (No direct antonym) |
词性: noun|


cracker:/ˈkrækər/ (US) /ˈkrækə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|一流的；出色的 (Mostly US, informal)|Excellent; first-rate.|

例句：
He's a crackerjack tennis player. 他是一名一流的网球运动员。

That was a cracker of a goal! 那是一个精彩的进球！

近义词：excellent, superb, first-rate, top-notch|
反义词: terrible, awful, poor|
词性: adjective|
