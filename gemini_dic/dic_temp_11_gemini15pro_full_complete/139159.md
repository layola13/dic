obfuscation: /ˌɒbfʌsˈkeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌɒbfʌsˈkeɪʃn/ (UK)| n. | 混淆，模糊，困惑 | The act of making something unclear, obscure, or unintelligible. |  The government's obfuscation of the facts made it difficult to understand the true situation. 政府对事实的混淆使得很难理解真实情况。

The lawyer's obfuscation of the issue confused the jury. 律师对问题的混淆让陪审团感到困惑。

Synonym: confusion, obscurity, mystification, bafflement

Antonym: clarification, elucidation, explanation, simplification

词性: noun


obfuscations: /ˌɒbfʌsˈkeɪʃənz/ (US) /ˌɒbfʌsˈkeɪʃnz/ (UK) | n. | 混淆(复数)，模糊(复数)，困惑(复数) | Plural form of "obfuscation," referring to multiple instances of making something unclear or unintelligible. | The politician's obfuscations about his campaign finances led to calls for an investigation.  这位政治家对其竞选资金的含糊其辞导致了调查的呼声。

Her deliberate obfuscations about her past made it hard to trust her. 她对她过去的有意隐瞒让人很难相信她。

Synonym: confusions, obscurities, mystifications, bafflements

Antonym: clarifications, elucidations, explanations, simplifications

词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *obfuscatio, from Latin obfuscare "to darken," from ob- "over" + fuscare "to darken," from fuscus "dark."
