ordovician: /ɔːr.dəˈvɪʃ.ən/ | adj. n. | 奥陶纪的；奥陶纪 |  Relating to or denoting the second period of the Palaeozoic era, between the Cambrian and Silurian periods, or the system of rocks deposited during it.  The name comes from the Ordovices, an ancient Celtic tribe of Wales where rocks of this age were first studied. |  The Ordovician period saw a great diversification of marine life. 奥陶纪见证了海洋生物的大规模多样化。| Silurian, Cambrian | Precambrian, Devonian | adjective, noun
