Etymology->
From Old English *ēast* (also *east,* *ēastan,* *ēastor,* *ēostor*), from Proto-Germanic *austaz* (“east”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ews- (“to shine,” “dawn”). Cognate with Old Frisian *āst,* Old Saxon *ōstan,* Old High German *ōstan* (German *Osten*), Old Norse *austr* (Swedish *öster,* Danish *øster,* Icelandic *austur*), Gothic *𐌰𐌿𐍃𐍄𐍂* (*austr*). Related to aurora.
