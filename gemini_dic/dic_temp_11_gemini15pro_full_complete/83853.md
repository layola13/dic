halcyon: /ˈhælsiən/ (US), /ˈhælsiən/ (UK)| adj. n.| 平静的；宁静的；太平的；翠鸟| characterized by happiness, great success, and prosperity; denoting a mythical bird identified with the kingfisher and believed to nest at sea about the winter solstice, charming the wind and waves into calm |例句：The halcyon days of summer. 夏日平静的日子。|近义词：peaceful, tranquil, serene, calm, quiet|反义词：turbulent, chaotic, stormy|词性:形容词, 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *alcyon, from Ancient Greek ἀλκυών (alkuṓn, “kingfisher, halcyon”).  The word entered English via Middle French alcyon.  The ancient Greeks believed that the halcyon (kingfisher) built its nest on the sea and calmed the waves while it incubated its eggs.
