dalmania:/dælˈmeɪniə/| n. |达尔曼虫属；达尔曼狂热|A genus of extinct trilobites, common in the Ordovician and Silurian periods.  Also used, often humorously, to describe an intense enthusiasm for something.|例句：His dalmania for collecting stamps bordered on obsession. 他对集邮的狂热近乎痴迷。

例句：The museum's new exhibit on dalmania fossils drew large crowds.  博物馆新展出的达尔曼虫化石吸引了大批观众。|近义词：passion, craze, obsession, enthusiasm|反义词：apathy, indifference|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Adam Sedgwick, a British geologist, who named the genus after John Dalman, a Swedish palaeontologist.
