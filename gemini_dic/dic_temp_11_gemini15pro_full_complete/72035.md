flipper:/ˈflɪpər/| n.|鳍状肢，脚蹼|A broad, flat limb adapted for swimming, as in seals, whales, or turtles.  Also, a rubber fin worn on the foot as an aid in swimming. |The penguin used its flippers to propel itself through the water.  企鹅用它的鳍状肢在水中推动自己前进。

The diver put on his flippers before entering the ocean.  潜水员在进入海洋前戴上了脚蹼。|近义词：fin, paddle|反义词:(No direct antonym)|noun


flipper:/ˈflɪpər/| n.|（游戏机等的）弹球按钮|A button or lever used to operate the flippers of a pinball machine.|He expertly used the flippers to keep the ball in play. 他熟练地使用弹球按钮使球保持在游戏中。

She pressed the flipper at just the right moment to score big points. 她在恰当的时刻按下弹球按钮，获得了高分。|近义词：button, lever|反义词:(No direct antonym)|noun


flipper:/ˈflɪpər/| n.|（尤指旧式的）房子，（尤指为了快速获利而）倒卖的房子|A house, especially one bought and sold quickly for profit (often used in older contexts).|He made a fortune as a flipper in the booming housing market. 在蓬勃发展的房地产市场中，他作为一名倒卖房屋的人发了大财。

That flipper they bought needs a lot of work. 他们买的那个房子需要大量的维修。|近义词：speculator, house trader|反义词:(No direct antonym)|noun
