flirt:/ˈflɜːrt/ (US), /ˈflɜːt/ (UK)| n. vi. |调情，打情骂俏|1. (n.) An act of flirting; a brief, playful romantic encounter. 2. (vi.) To behave in a way that suggests a playful romantic interest, often without serious intentions.|She had a brief flirt with him at the party.  她在派对上和他短暂地调情了一下。|He's always flirting with the waitresses. 他总是和女服务员调情。|近义词：tease, dally, trifle|反义词：ignore, shun|词性: Noun, Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Old French "fleureter" meaning "to touch lightly with flowers," later evolving to mean "to act amorously without serious intent."  The word entered English in the 16th century.
