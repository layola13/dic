melancholiness:/ˈmɛlənkɒlɪnəs/ (British), /ˌmɛlənˈkɑːlinəs/ (American)| n. |忧郁，悲伤，愁绪|A state of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause.|She felt a deep melancholiess wash over her as she watched the sunset.|近义词：sadness, gloom, dejection, depression, wistfulness|反义词：happiness, cheerfulness, joy, elation|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *mélancolie* (modern French *mélancolie*), from Late Latin *melancholia*, from Greek *melankholía*, from *melas* "black" + *kholē* "bile".  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholia.
