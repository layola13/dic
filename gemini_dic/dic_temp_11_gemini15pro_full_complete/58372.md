dualist:/ˈduːəlɪst/| n.|二元论者|A person who believes that two basic principles or forces, such as good and evil or mind and matter, govern the universe.|Descartes was a dualist, believing in the separate existence of mind and body.  笛卡尔是一位二元论者，相信心灵和身体的独立存在。|synonyms: dichotomist |antonyms: monist|noun

dualist:/ˈduːəlɪst/| adj.|二元论的|Relating to or advocating dualism.|He holds a dualist view of the world, seeing everything in terms of opposites. 他持二元论的世界观，用对立的观点看待一切。|synonyms: dichotomous |antonyms: monistic|adjective
