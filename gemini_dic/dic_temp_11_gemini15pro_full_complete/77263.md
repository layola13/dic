gastropods:/ˈɡæstrəpɑːdz/ | n. | 腹足纲动物；腹足类 |  A large class of mollusks, typically having a single, often spirally coiled shell and a muscular foot for locomotion.  They include snails, slugs, limpets, and whelks. | Snails and slugs are common gastropods.  蜗牛和蛞蝓是常见的腹足纲动物。 | synonyms: univalves,  | antonyms: (no direct antonym, but could consider bivalves or cephalopods as contrasting groups) | noun
