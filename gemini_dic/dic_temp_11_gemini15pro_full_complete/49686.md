demiurge:/ˈdɛmiˌɜːrdʒ/| n. |造物主，创造者|A being responsible for the creation of the universe, in particular a lesser god who creates the physical world in subordination to a higher being.|Plato believed in a demiurge who fashioned the material world after the forms.|近义词：creator, maker, architect, author|反义词：destroyer, annihilator|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin demiurgus, from Greek dēmiourgos "craftsman, artisan," from dēmios "belonging to the people" (from dēmos "people") + -ergos "working," from ergon "work."
