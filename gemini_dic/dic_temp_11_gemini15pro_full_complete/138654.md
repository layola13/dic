nudger:/ˈnʌdʒər/| n.|轻推者；推动者；用肘轻推的人|Someone who nudges.  A nudger encourages or persuades someone to do something in a gentle way, often by implying what they should do rather than by telling them directly.|He's a bit of a nudger when it comes to getting people to donate to charity.  在让人们向慈善机构捐款方面，他有点像个推动者。|近义词：prompter, encourager, persuader|反义词:deterrent, obstacle|词性:noun

nudger:/ˈnʌdʒər/| n.|（尤指政府）促使…做…的人；引导者|Someone who influences people’s behaviour in a particular direction, especially by using gentle persuasion rather than force or direct instruction. This term is often used in the context of government policies.|The government has become a nudger, encouraging healthier lifestyles through subtle incentives. 政府已经成为一个引导者，通过一些微妙的激励措施鼓励更健康的生活方式。|近义词：influencer, guide|反义词: dictator, controller|词性:noun
