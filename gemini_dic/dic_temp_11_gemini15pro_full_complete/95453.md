incisively:/ɪnˈsaɪsɪvli/| adv.|深刻地，尖锐地，敏锐地|In a way that is clear, brief, and forceful; sharply and intelligently.|Her questions were incisive and to the point. 她的问题尖锐而切中要害。|近义词：acutely, sharply, keenly, piercingly|反义词: vaguely, obtusely, dully|词性:副词
Etymology: From incisive + -ly. Incisive comes from Late Latin incisivus "cutting, piercing," from Latin incis-, past participle stem of incidere "to cut into," from in- "into" + caedere "to cut."
