clear-off:/ˌklɪər ˈɒf/ (British), /ˌklɪr ˈɔːf/ (American)| phrasal verb | 走开，离开；放晴 | To leave a place, often abruptly or as an order; for weather to improve and become sunny. |"Clear off!" he shouted. “走开！”他喊道。| synonyms: get lost, go away, beat it, scram, leave, depart | antonyms: arrive, stay, remain | verb

clear-off:/ˌklɪər ˈɒf/ (British), /ˌklɪr ˈɔːf/ (American)| n. |  (天气)放晴 | A period of improving weather, especially when clouds disperse and the sun appears. | The forecast predicts a clear-off later this afternoon. 预报说今天下午晚些时候天气会放晴。| synonyms: brightening, clearing | antonyms: clouding over, worsening weather | noun

词源： "Clear" originates from the Old French word "cler" meaning "bright, clear." "Off" is an adverb indicating separation or departure. The combination "clear off" developed as a phrasal verb with both literal and figurative meanings related to becoming clear or going away.
