coup:/kuː/ (US) /kuː/ (UK)
|n.|政变；妙计；出乎意料的成功|a sudden, violent, and illegal seizure of power from a government|The military staged a coup and overthrew the government. 軍方發動政變，推翻了政府。|synonyms: overthrow, takeover, putsch|antonyms:  surrender, concession|noun
coup:/kuː/ (US) /kuː/ (UK)
|n.|意外的成功；妙计|a brilliantly executed plan or strategy; a clever maneuver|Pulling off that deal was a real coup for the small company.  做成那笔交易对这家小公司来说真是一个妙计。|synonyms: triumph, feat, masterstroke|antonyms: failure, blunder|noun

Etymology:
From French *coup*, from Late Latin *colpus* "blow, strike", from Latin *colaphus* "a cuff, box on the ear" ultimately from Greek *κόλαφος* (kolaphos) "a blow, cuff".
