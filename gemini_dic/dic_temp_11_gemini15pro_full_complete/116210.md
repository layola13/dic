masochism:/ˈmæsəkɪzəm/ | n. | 受虐狂，受虐癖 |  The derivation of "masochism" comes from the name of Leopold von Sacher-Masoch, an Austrian writer whose novels explored themes of sexual gratification derived from pain and humiliation.  It refers to the practice of deriving pleasure, especially sexual gratification, from one's own pain or humiliation. | He explored the complexities of masochism in his latest novel.  他最新的小说探讨了受虐狂的复杂性。 | Synonyms: flagellation, self-abuse, self-torture | Antonyms: sadism | noun
