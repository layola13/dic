expedientness:/ɪkˈspiːdiəntnəs/| n.|权宜之计，方便，利己|The quality of being expedient; suitability for achieving a particular end in a given circumstance, especially one that is convenient or advantageous rather than fair or just. |The expedientness of the decision was questioned by many.  许多人质疑该决定的权宜之计。|近义词:convenience, advantage, practicality, suitability, usefulness|反义词:disadvantage, impracticality, unsuitability, uselessness|词性:名词
expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/| adj.|权宜的，方便的，有利的|(of an action) convenient and practical although possibly improper or immoral.|(adj.)  It was expedient to silence the witness.  让证人闭嘴是权宜之计。 (n.) Using a calculator is an expedient way to solve complex math problems. 使用计算器是解决复杂数学问题的一种权宜之计。|近义词:advantageous, convenient, practical, suitable, useful|反义词:disadvantageous, inconvenient, impractical, unsuitable, useless|词性:形容词, 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French *expedient, or from late Latin expedient- ‘extricating, developing’, from the verb expedire (see expedite).
