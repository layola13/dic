lernaeacea:/ˌlɜːrniˈeɪsiə/ | n. | 锚头鳋科 |A family of parasitic copepods, commonly known as anchor worms. They infest fish, attaching themselves to the skin, fins, or gills.|The lernaeacea infestation decimated the fish population in the lake.  锚头鳋科的感染使湖中的鱼类数量锐减。|近义词:copepod family (更广义), parasite (更广义) |反义词:host (从寄生关系的角度)|词性:noun

Etymology:  From Modern Latin, derived from the genus *Lernaea*, named after the Lernaean Hydra, a mythical multi-headed serpent.
