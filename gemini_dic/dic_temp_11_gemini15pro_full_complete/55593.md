disunited:/ˌdɪsjuːˈnaɪtɪd/
adj.
分裂的，不团结的
Lacking unity; divided or separated.
The disunited states could not agree on a common strategy.  分裂的各州无法就共同战略达成一致。
The team was disunited and performed poorly.  团队不团结，表现很差。
近义词: divided, fragmented, separated, at odds
反义词: united, unified, harmonious, cohesive
词性: adjective


disunite:/ˌdɪsjuːˈnaɪt/
v.
使分裂，使不团结
To cause disunity or division; separate.
Political differences disunited the country.  政治分歧使国家分裂。
The scandal disunited the members of the club.  丑闻使俱乐部成员之间产生了隔阂。
近义词: divide, separate, alienate, estrange
反义词: unite, join, connect, reconcile
词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- + unite.  "Dis-" is a prefix meaning "apart," "asunder," "reversal," or "negation."  "Unite" comes from the Old French "unir," ultimately from the Latin "unus," meaning "one."  Therefore, "disunite" literally means to make not one, or to separate.
