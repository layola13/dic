Mennonite:/ˈmɛnəˌnaɪt/ | n. |门诺派教徒|A member of a Protestant Christian sect originating in the 16th century, emphasizing pacifism, simplicity, and community.  | Example: The Mennonite community is known for its pacifist beliefs. 门诺派社区以其和平主义信仰而闻名。| Example:  Many Mennonites are farmers and live a simple life. 许多门诺派教徒是农民，过着简朴的生活。| Synonym: Anabaptist, pacifist | Antonym: militarist | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Menno Simons (1496–1561), a Dutch Anabaptist religious leader + -ite.
