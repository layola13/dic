overextrapolation: /ˌoʊvərɪkˈstræpəˌleɪʃən/ | n. | 过度外推，过度推断 | The act of inferring something beyond the range of known data, often leading to inaccurate or unrealistic conclusions. |  His prediction of a million-dollar profit in the first year was pure overextrapolation.  他预测第一年就能盈利一百万美元，纯粹是过度推断。| 近义词：overgeneralization, overestimation | 反义词: understatement, interpolation | 词性: noun

overextrapolation: /ˌoʊvərɪkˈstræpəˌleɪʃən/ | n. | 过度外推，过度推断 | Basing a prediction on too little data, extending a trend beyond the region where it is supported by evidence. |  The company's overextrapolation of initial sales figures led them to believe they would dominate the market, a prediction that proved false.  该公司对初期销售数据的过度推断，让他们相信自己将主导市场，而这一预测最终被证明是错误的。| 近义词：projection, conjecture | 反义词: measured estimation | 词性: noun
