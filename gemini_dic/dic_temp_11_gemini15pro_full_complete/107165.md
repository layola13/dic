lagena:/ləˈdʒiːnə/| n.| 拉金纳瓶, 瓶状器官|A flask-shaped anatomical structure, particularly the part of the cochlea of the inner ear that resembles a flask. Also, a unit of liquid volume used in ancient Rome, approximately equal to a quart.  |The base of the cochlea, the lagena, contains sensory cells important for hearing. 基底的耳蜗，拉金纳，包含对听力重要的感觉细胞。

The Roman merchant measured the wine in lagenae.  罗马商人用拉金纳瓶测量葡萄酒。|Synonyms:  flask, bottle, ampulla (anatomy), quart (unit of volume)|Antonyms:  None (for anatomical sense), none (for unit of volume sense)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *lagena*, meaning "flask" or "bottle."  Ultimately from Greek *λάγηνος (lagēnos)*, meaning "flask."
