miz:/mɪz/ | n. | 女士，小姐（非正式，主要用于美国南部） |  A dialectal variant of Mrs., used especially in the southern U.S., as a courtesy title prefixed to a married woman's name. It is a more informal term of address than "Ma'am". | Example: Miz Scarlet, how are you today?  斯佳丽特女士，你今天好吗？| Synonym: Mrs., ma'am (more formal) | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but contrasts with Mr. for men) | Noun
