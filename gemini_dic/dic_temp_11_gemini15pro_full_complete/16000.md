bamboozling:/bæmˈbuːzlɪŋ/
| adj.  v. (present participle)|欺骗的，迷惑的；欺骗，迷惑|To deceive or confuse someone, often by tricking them.|The magician bamboozled the audience with his incredible illusions. 魔术师用他不可思议的幻觉迷惑了观众。|Synonyms: deceiving, tricking, misleading, confounding, perplexing|Antonyms: enlightening, clarifying, explaining|verb/adjective

bamboozle:/bæmˈbuːzl/
| vt.|欺骗，迷惑|To deceive or confuse someone, often by tricking them.|He bamboozled me into giving him my money. 他骗走了我的钱。|Synonyms: deceive, trick, mislead, confound, perplex|Antonyms: enlighten, clarify, explain|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain, possibly from dialectal English *bam, "to hoax, cheat," and *foozle, "to manage clumsily." First recorded in the early 18th century.
