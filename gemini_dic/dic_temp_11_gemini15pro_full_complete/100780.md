intrenched:/ɪnˈtrɛnʃt/ | adj. | 坚固的，根深蒂固的，牢固确立的 | Firmly established and difficult to change. |  The enemy was deeply intrenched. 敌人已牢固地构筑了工事。 | 近义词: established, ingrained, rooted, fixed, fortified | 反义词: weak, vulnerable, exposed, precarious | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French "retrancher" (to cut again, to fortify), from "re-" (again) + "trancher" (to cut).  The English "intrench" (and its variant "entrench") originally meant to dig trenches for defense, and later evolved to mean establishing something firmly.
