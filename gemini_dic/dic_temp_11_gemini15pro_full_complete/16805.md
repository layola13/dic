baselard: /ˈbæsəlɑːrd/ (US), /ˈbæsələrd/ (UK) | n. | 基拉德, 一种短剑 | A type of dagger with an I-shaped cross-section blade, popular in the 14th and 15th centuries. It was primarily used as a civilian weapon for self-defense or dueling. | He carried a baselard concealed beneath his cloak. 他在外衣下藏着一把基拉德。 | Synonyms: dagger, poniard, dirk | Antonyms: N/A | noun
