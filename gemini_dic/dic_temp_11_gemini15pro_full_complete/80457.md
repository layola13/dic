gongorism: /ˈɡɒŋɡərɪzəm/ (US) /ˈɡɒŋɡərɪzəm/ (UK)| n. | 贡戈拉主义；夸饰主义 | An affectedly ornate style of writing or speaking, marked by convoluted syntax, obscure allusions, and extravagant metaphors, named after the 17th-century Spanish poet Luis de Góngora y Argote.  |  The writer's prose was so filled with gongorism that it was almost incomprehensible.  这位作家的散文充满了贡戈拉主义，几乎难以理解。| synonyms:  euphuism,  bombast,  purple prose | antonyms:  simplicity,  clarity,  conciseness | noun
