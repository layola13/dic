Bukowski: /bʊˈkaʊski/ | n. | 布考斯基 |  Charles Bukowski (1920-1994), a German-American poet, novelist, and short story writer known for his gritty portrayals of urban life and his often-provocative subject matter. | He was influenced by the naturalism of John Fante and is often compared to other Beat Generation writers. Bukowski's work often features themes of alienation, alcohol, gambling, and the struggles of the working class. |  "Bukowski's writing is not for the faint of heart." | 近义词:  Fante,  Beat writers | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun (proper noun)
