cinematography: /ˌsɪnɪməˈtɒɡrəfi/ (UK) /ˌsɪnɪməˈtɑːɡrəfi/ (US)
n.
电影摄影术；电影摄影
The art of making motion pictures, including both the technical aspects (such as lighting, camera angles, and lens choices) and the artistic aspects (such as composition, framing, and visual storytelling).
The cinematography in that film was breathtaking, capturing the vastness of the landscape perfectly. 那部电影的摄影令人叹为观止，完美地捕捉了景色的广阔。
近义词:  filming, camerawork
反义词:  N/A
词性: noun

cinematography:
Etymology:  From French *cinématographie*, from *cinéma* ("cinema") + *-graphie* ("-graphy"). Ultimately from Ancient Greek κίνημα (kínēma, “movement”) + γράφω (gráphō, “to write, record”).
词源：来自法语 *cinématographie*，由 *cinéma*（“电影”）+ *-graphie*（“-graphy”）组成。最终源自古希腊语 κίνημα (kínēma, “运动”) + γράφω (gráphō, “书写，记录”)。
