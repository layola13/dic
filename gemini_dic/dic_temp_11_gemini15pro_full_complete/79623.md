glossanthrax:/ˌɡlɒsænˈθræks/ | n. |舌炭疽; 牛舌炭疽|A disease of cattle, sheep, and other ruminants, characterized by the formation of dark carbuncles on the tongue and other mucous membranes. It's caused by the bacterium *Clostridium chauvoei*.|The veterinarian diagnosed the cow with glossanthrax after observing characteristic lesions on its tongue.|Synonym: blackleg, quarter evil|Antonym: (No direct antonym for a specific disease.  One could consider "health" or "wellness" as a general antonym.)|noun
