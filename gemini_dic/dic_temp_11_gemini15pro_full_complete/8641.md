anticonfederationist: /ˌænˌtɪkənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪst/ | n. adj. | 反联邦主义者；反对联邦制的 |  A person who opposed the adoption of the United States Constitution in 1787-1788.  They feared that a strong central government would infringe on states' rights and individual liberties. | Example: Anticonfederationists argued that the Constitution lacked a bill of rights. 反联邦主义者认为宪法缺乏权利法案。

Example:  Many Anticonfederationists later joined the Democratic-Republican Party. 许多反联邦主义者后来加入了民主共和党。 | 近义词:States' rights advocate, opponent of federalism| 反义词:Federalist, supporter of the Constitution | 词性:名词，形容词
