canvasing:/ˈkænvəsɪŋ/| n. |游说，拉票|The act of soliciting votes, opinions, or support, typically by going door-to-door or making phone calls.|Example: The candidates spent weeks canvassing the district before the election.  候选人在选举前花了几个星期在该地区游说。|近义词：soliciting, campaigning, polling|反义词: abstaining|词性:名词

canvasing:/ˈkænvəsɪŋ/| v. |游说，拉票|To solicit votes, opinions, or support, typically by going door-to-door or making phone calls.|Example: They canvassed the neighborhood for donations to the local charity. 他们在附近一带游说，为当地慈善机构募捐。|近义词：solicit, campaign, poll|反义词: abstain|词性:动词

词源：From canvas (n.) "close inspection," from Anglo-French canbas "canvas, close inspection." The original sense was of tossing someone in a canvas sheet as a punishment. This developed into a sense of tossing ideas about and finally, soliciting.
