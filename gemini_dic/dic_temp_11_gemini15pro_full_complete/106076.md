knick:/nɪk/
noun
小伤口；刻痕；缺口
a small cut or notch
He had a small knick on his chin. 他的下巴上有个小伤口。

knick:/nɪk/
noun
(非正式)监狱
(informal) a prison
He spent a few nights in the knick. 他在监狱里待了几个晚上。


knick:/nɪk/
verb
使有缺口；刻痕
to make a small cut or notch in something
I knicked my finger while chopping vegetables. 我切菜的时候割破了手指。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *knikken, frequentative of knikken (“to bend, crack”), from Proto-Germanic *knakkijaną (“to crack, snap”). Cognate with German knicken (“to bend, crack”), knacken (“to crack, snap, click”).
