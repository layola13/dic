hybridised:/ˈhaɪbrɪdaɪzd/ |v. |杂交的，混合的 |Combined or mixed from two different things, especially breeds or varieties.|The orchid is a hybridised variety.  这种兰花是一个杂交品种。|例句：Many plants in gardens are hybridised for improved color or hardiness.  花园里的许多植物都是为了改良颜色或增强抗逆性而杂交的。

例句：Scientists have hybridised the two species to create a new, disease-resistant strain. 科学家们将这两个物种杂交，创造了一种新的抗病菌株。|近义词：crossbred, mixed, blended|反义词：purebred, unmixed|词性:动词(过去分词)


hybridized:/ˈhaɪbrɪdaɪzd/ |adj. |杂交的，混合的 |Combined or mixed from two different things, especially breeds or varieties.|The hybridized rose had a unique fragrance.  这种杂交玫瑰有一种独特的香味。|例句：The hybridized corn produced higher yields than the traditional variety. 这种杂交玉米比传统品种产量更高。

例句：Hybridized animals are sometimes bred for specific traits. 杂交动物有时是为了特定的性状而培育的。|近义词：crossbred, mixed, blended|反义词：purebred, unmixed|词性:形容词


词源: From hybrid + -ize + -ed.  "Hybrid" comes from Latin "hybrida" meaning "offspring of a tame sow and a wild boar."
