clutch:/klʌtʃ/| n. vt.|抓住，离合器，掌控，关键时刻|A tight grasp or hold.|She clutched her purse tightly. 她紧紧地抓着她的钱包。|近义词：grasp, grip, seize, hold|反义词:release, loosen|noun

clutch:/klʌtʃ/| n. vt.|一窝（蛋），一组（人或物）|A group of eggs, or a number of people or things.|A clutch of tourists gathered around the guide.  一群游客聚集在导游周围。|近义词：group, bunch, cluster|反义词:individual|noun

clutch:/klʌtʃ/| vi. vt.|（在困难情况下）突然抓住，（因害怕、疼痛等）抓紧|To grasp or seize suddenly, especially in fear or pain.|He clutched at the railing as he slipped. 他滑倒时抓住了栏杆。|近义词：grab, snatch|反义词:release|verb

clutch:/klʌtʃ/| adj.|关键的，重要的|Decisive; critical.|He made a clutch hit in the bottom of the ninth inning. 他在第九局下半场打出了关键的一击。|近义词：crucial, critical, decisive|反义词:insignificant, unimportant|adjective
