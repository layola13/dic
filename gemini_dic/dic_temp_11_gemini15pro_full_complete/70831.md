fining:/ˈfaɪnɪŋ/
n.
罚款
the act of imposing and paying a fine
例句：The fining of the company for environmental violations sent a strong message.  该公司因违反环境法规而被罚款，发出了强烈的信号。

例句：The city increased the fining for illegal parking. 该市提高了对违章停车的罚款金额。
近义词：penalty, sanction
反义词：reward, compensation
词性：名词

fining:/ˈfaɪnɪŋ/
v.
(fine的现在分词) 罚款
present participle of fine (verb) - to punish by making someone pay a sum of money
例句：The judge is fining the defendant $500 for contempt of court. 法官因被告藐视法庭而对其处以500美元的罚款。

例句：They are fining drivers who exceed the speed limit. 他们正在对超速驾驶的司机处以罚款。
近义词：penalizing, sanctioning
反义词：rewarding, compensating
词性：动词


词源: From Middle English *finen*, from Old French *finer* "to end, settle, pay," from Latin *finis* "end, boundary, limit."  The noun sense "monetary penalty" developed from the verb.
