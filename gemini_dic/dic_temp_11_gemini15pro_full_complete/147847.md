overqualifying:/ˌoʊvərˈkwɒlɪfaɪɪŋ/ | adj. |资历过高的，能力过剩的 | Having qualifications or skills that are too high or advanced for a particular job or position.  This can sometimes be a disadvantage, as employers may worry that the candidate will quickly become bored or leave for a better opportunity. |  She was overqualifying for the entry-level position. 她资历过高，不适合这个入门级的职位。|  He was concerned about being perceived as overqualifying during the interview. 他担心在面试中被认为资历过高。 | 近义词: overqualified | 反义词: underqualified | 词性:形容词

overqualify:/ˌoʊvərˈkwɒlɪfaɪ/ | v. | 使资历过高；使资格过剩 | To possess qualifications or skills that exceed the requirements of a particular job or position. | He overqualified himself by earning a PhD before applying for the administrative assistant role. 他在申请行政助理职位之前获得了博士学位，使自己资历过高。 |  She felt she had overqualified herself for the position and was worried about being rejected. 她觉得自己资历过高，不适合这个职位，担心会被拒绝。 | 近义词: - | 反义词: underqualify | 词性:动词


词源: "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "qualify" (verb meaning "to make competent or eligible")
