demoralizations:/diːˌmɒrəlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/
n.
士气低落，泄气，道德败坏
The act of demoralizing; the state of being demoralized.  A reduction of morale.
The team's string of losses led to widespread demoralizations among the players.  该队的一连串失利导致球员普遍士气低落。
近义词: discouragement, dejection, depression, despair
反义词: encouragement, inspiration, upliftment
词性: noun


demoralization:/diːˌmɒrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
n.
士气低落，泄气，道德败坏
The act of demoralizing; the state of being demoralized. A reduction of morale.
Constant criticism can lead to demoralization.  不断的批评会导致士气低落。
近义词: discouragement, dejection, depression
反义词: encouragement, inspiration
词性: noun


Etymology:
From French *démoralisation*, from *démoraliser* (“to demoralize”), from *dé-* (from Latin *dis-* (“apart, away”)) + *moral* (“moral”) + *-iser* (from Latin *-izāre*, from Ancient Greek *-ίζειν* (*-ízein*)).  Ultimately from Latin *mōrēs* (“customs, manners, morals”).
