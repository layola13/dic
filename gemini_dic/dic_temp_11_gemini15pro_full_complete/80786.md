got-to:/ˈɡɑːtə/ (US), /ˈɡɒtə/ (UK)| modal verb (informal contraction)|必须，不得不|A contraction of "have got to," expressing obligation or necessity.|I got to go to work now. 我现在得去上班了。|近义词：have to, must, need to|反义词: needn't, don't have to|词性:modal verb (contraction)

got-to:/ˈɡɑːtə/ (US), /ˈɡɒtə/ (UK)|modal verb (informal contraction)|必须，不得不|Used informally to express a strong desire or intention.|I got to see that new movie. 我得去看那部新电影。|近义词: have to, must, need to, be bound to |反义词: needn't, don't have to|词性:modal verb (contraction)

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
I gotta go to school every day.  我每天都得去上学。
He's gotta work hard to support his family. 他必须努力工作来养家。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
I'm gotta be leaving now. 我现在得走了。 (Note: This usage is less common and might sound awkward)
He's gotta be kidding me! 他一定是在跟我开玩笑！（Note: This expresses disbelief rather than ongoing obligation）

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
I've gotta finish this report before I leave. 我得在离开前完成这份报告。(Note: This implies the obligation exists now, resulting from a past action)
She's gotta have forgotten our appointment. 她一定是忘记了我们的约会。(Note: This expresses a deduction about a past event)


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
I've gotta been working on this project for weeks. 我已经在这个项目上工作好几个星期了。(Note: This construction is unusual and generally avoided)
He's gotta been thinking about quitting his job. 他一定一直在考虑辞职。(Note: This emphasizes the duration of the thought process, but is not standard usage)

She gotta leave early for her appointment. 她得早点离开去赴约。(Note:  Again, "had to" is the correct past tense form.)
