Farmingdale: /ˈfɑːrmɪŋdeɪl/ (US), /ˈfɑːmɪŋdeɪl/ (UK)| n. | 农庄谷; 纽约州的一个村庄 | A village on Long Island, New York, and a census-designated place (CDP) within the Town of Oyster Bay in Nassau County, New York, United States. It is also the location of two major public universities, Farmingdale State College and part of SUNY Old Westbury.| He grew up in Farmingdale, New York. 他在纽约州的农庄谷长大。 | 近义词: village, town | 反义词: city, metropolis | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  "Farmingdale" is likely derived from the combination of "farming" and "dale," indicating a valley or area suitable for agriculture.  This type of place name is common, reflecting the historical importance of farming in the area.
