dilapidated:/dɪˈlæpɪdeɪtɪd/| adj.|破旧的，失修的|Falling into ruin or decay, usually through neglect or misuse.  In a state of disrepair or ruin because of age or neglect.|The dilapidated house stood on a deserted street.  这栋破旧的房子坐落在一条荒凉的街道上。|The old barn was dilapidated and in need of repair.  这间旧谷仓破旧不堪，需要修理。|近义词：ramshackle, derelict, ruined, tumbledown|反义词：pristine, renovated, restored|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Latin *dilapidatus*, past participle of *dilapidare* "to squander, scatter (stones)," from *dis-* "apart, asunder" + *lapidare* "to throw stones," from *lapis* "stone."  Originally applied to buildings made of stone, the meaning broadened to include structures of other materials.
