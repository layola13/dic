bunodonta:/ˌbjuːnəˈdɒntə/ | n. |丘齿兽类; 低冠齿兽| A group of extinct mammals characterized by low-crowned, rounded teeth, adapted for crushing and grinding vegetation.  They were prevalent during the Paleogene and Neogene periods. |  Bunodonta were a successful group of herbivores. 丘齿兽类是成功的食草动物群体。|  Fossil evidence suggests bunodonta diversified rapidly after the Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction event. 化石证据表明，丘齿兽类在白垩纪-古近纪灭绝事件后迅速多样化。|  Synonyms:  None (specific taxonomic group)| Antonyms: None (specific taxonomic group) | Noun
