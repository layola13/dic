bustle:/ˈbʌsəl/ | n. vi. | 喧闹，忙碌，裙撑 | a period of hurried activity; a frame worn under a skirt to puff it out |  The market was filled with the bustle of shoppers. 市场里挤满了熙熙攘攘的购物者。| synonyms: activity, hustle, flurry, commotion | antonyms: calm, quiet, stillness | noun

bustle:/ˈbʌsəl/ | vi. | 奔忙，忙乱 | to move about energetically; to teem | She bustled around the kitchen, preparing dinner. 她在厨房里忙来忙去，准备晚餐。| synonyms: rush, hurry, dash, scurry | antonyms: dawdle, linger, stroll | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bustelen*, frequentative of *busten* "to prepare, make ready," probably of Scandinavian origin.  Related to Old Norse *búask* "to prepare oneself, get ready." The noun sense developed from the verb, referring to the noisy activity associated with hurried preparation.
