digged-out:/ˌdɪɡd ˈaʊt/| adj.|挖出的，挖掘出来的|excavated; unearthed;  having been dug out of the ground or another material.  Describes something that has been removed from a place by digging.|例句：The archaeologists discovered a  digged-out  chamber. 考古学家发现了一个挖出的房间。

例句：They found a  digged-out  canoe buried in the mud. 他们在泥里发现了一艘挖出来的独木舟。|近义词：excavated, unearthed, exhumed|反义词:buried, interred|词性:形容词

词源: From the verb "dig out," which is formed by combining the verb "dig" (from Old English *dician, "to ditch, to make a trench") and the adverb/preposition "out" (from Old English ūt).
