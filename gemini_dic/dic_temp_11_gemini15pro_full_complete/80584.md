goofing:/ˈɡuːfɪŋ/| n. | 愚弄，胡闹，开玩笑 | the act of goofing around; acting silly or playful |  He spent the afternoon goofing off with his friends. 他下午和朋友们一起闲逛瞎混。 | 近义词：fooling around, messing around, playing around, joking, kidding | 反义词：working, being serious | 词性: 名词 (Gerund/Present Participle)

goof:/ɡuːf/| n. | 愚蠢的错误；傻瓜 | a silly or stupid mistake; a foolish person |  I made a real goof by forgetting her birthday. 我把她的生日忘了，真是犯了个愚蠢的错误。| 近义词：mistake, blunder, error, fool, idiot | 反义词：genius, expert | 词性: 名词

goof:/ɡuːf/| vi. |  犯愚蠢的错误；闲逛；混日子 | to make a silly mistake; to spend time idly or foolishly | I goofed and locked myself out of the house. 我犯了个傻，把自己锁在门外了。 | 近义词：blunder, err, mess up, fool around, idle | 反义词：succeed, excel | 词性: 动词

词源:  "goof" likely originated in the late 19th or early 20th century, possibly as a variant of "goff" or "gaff," both meaning "fool" or "clown." "Goofing" is the present participle form of the verb "goof."
