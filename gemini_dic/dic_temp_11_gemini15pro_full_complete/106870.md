labrus: /ˈleɪbrəs/ | n. | 濑鱼 | A genus of fish in the family Labridae, commonly known as wrasses.  These are typically brightly colored fish found in coral reefs and rocky coastal areas. |  The labrus swims gracefully among the coral.  濑鱼在珊瑚间优雅地游动。

| The divers spotted a colorful labrus near the reef. 潜水员在珊瑚礁附近发现了一条色彩艳丽的濑鱼。 | Synonyms: wrasse | Antonyms: None | Noun

Etymology: From Latin 'labrus', meaning 'lip' or 'mouth', referring to the thick lips characteristic of these fish.
