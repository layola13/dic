dreads:/drɛdz/| n.|恐惧，担忧；令人恐惧的事物；脏辫|A feeling of fear or apprehension.|She had deep dreads about the future. 她对未来充满了恐惧。|Synonyms: fears, anxieties, apprehensions, worries, terrors|Antonyms: hopes, joys, comforts|noun

dread:/drɛd/| v.|害怕，恐惧；担心|To feel extremely worried or frightened about something that is going to happen or that might happen.|He dreaded going to the dentist. 他害怕去看牙医。|Synonyms: fear, be afraid of, worry about, be anxious about|Antonyms: look forward to, anticipate with pleasure|verb

dread:/drɛd/| adj.|可怕的，令人恐惧的；令人敬畏的|Causing fear or dread; inspiring awe.|The dread day finally arrived.  可怕的日子终于来了。|Synonyms: terrible, awful, frightful, fearsome, awesome|Antonyms: wonderful, delightful, pleasant|adjective
