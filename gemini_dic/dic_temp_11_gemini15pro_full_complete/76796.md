gamma-globulins:/ˈɡæmə ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪnz/ /ˈɡæmə ˈɡlɑːbjʊlɪnz/ | n. | γ-球蛋白 | A class of serum proteins, including most antibodies, responsible for humoral immunity. They are produced by plasma cells and are involved in fighting infections. |  High doses of gamma-globulins are sometimes prescribed for people with weakened immune systems.  高剂量的γ-球蛋白有时会开给免疫系统较弱的人。 | synonyms: immunoglobulins, antibodies | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but immunosuppressants have opposing function) | noun
