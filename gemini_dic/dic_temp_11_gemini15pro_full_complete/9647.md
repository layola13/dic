antisubsidy:/ˌæntiˈsʌbsɪdi/ | adj. | 反补贴的 |  Opposing or designed to counteract subsidies.  A subsidy is a form of financial aid or support extended to an economic sector (or institution, business, or individual) generally with the aim of promoting economic and social policy. Anti-subsidy measures aim to level the playing field by counteracting the effects of these subsidies. | The government imposed antisubsidy duties on imported steel. 政府对进口钢材征收反补贴税。 |  The World Trade Organization (WTO) allows countries to impose antisubsidy measures under certain conditions.  世界贸易组织（WTO）允许各国在一定条件下采取反补贴措施。 | 近义词: countervailing | 反义词: pro-subsidy | 词性: adjective
