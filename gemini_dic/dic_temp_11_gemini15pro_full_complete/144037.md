outrigger:/ˈaʊtrɪɡər/ | n. | 支腿，舷外浮材 | A projecting frame, spar, or float fastened to and extending from the side of a canoe or other boat to increase its stability.  | The outrigger helped stabilize the small boat in the rough seas.  舷外浮材帮助小船在波涛汹涌的海面上保持稳定。 | synonyms: stabilizer, float, support | antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps instability) | noun


outrigger:/ˈaʊtrɪɡər/ | n. | 吊臂，伸杆 | A beam or spar projecting from a main structure for support. | The crane's outrigger provided extra stability for lifting heavy loads. 起重机的吊臂为提升重物提供了额外的稳定性。 | synonyms: boom, jib, cantilever | antonyms: (No direct antonym) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *outrigger, from out- + riggen (“to equip, fit”).  Ultimately from Old Norse *út- + *rigga.
