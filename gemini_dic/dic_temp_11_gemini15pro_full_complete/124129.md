mortgage: /ˈmɔːrɡɪdʒ/ (British) /ˈmɔːrɡɪdʒ/ (American)| vt.|抵押|To convey or pledge (property) as security for a loan.|He mortgaged his house to start a business. 他抵押了房子来创业。|近义词：pledge, pawn|反义词:redeem|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mort gage* "dead pledge," from *mort* "dead" (from Latin *mortuus*) + *gage* "pledge" (from Frankish *wadiare* "to pledge"). So called because the pledge "dies" when the debt is paid or the property is forfeited.
