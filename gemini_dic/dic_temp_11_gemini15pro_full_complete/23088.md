bounds:/baʊndz/ | n. | 界限，范围；跳跃 |  Limits or boundaries of something; leaps or springs. | The ball went out of bounds.  球出界了。 | synonyms: limits, boundaries, confines, restrictions, leaps, jumps | antonyms: limitlessness, boundlessness, freedom,  | noun

bounds:/baʊndz/ | v. | 限制，约束；跳跃，弹回 | To set limits on; to move by leaping or springing. |  His ambition knows no bounds. 他的雄心壮志无限。| synonyms: restrict, confine, limit, restrain, leap, jump, spring | antonyms: free, release, liberate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *boundes*, plural of *bounde*, from Anglo-Norman *boundes*, *bones*, plural of *bunde*, *bone* "boundary, limit," from Old North French *bodne*, *bonne* (compare Old French *bodne*, *bonne*), from Late Latin *bodina* "boundary," of uncertain origin.  The verb is derived from the noun.
