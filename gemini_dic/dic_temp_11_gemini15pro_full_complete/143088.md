outbreak:/ˈaʊtbreɪk/| n.|爆发，突然发生|A sudden occurrence of something unwelcome, such as war or disease.|例句：The outbreak of war caused widespread panic. 战争的爆发引起了 widespread 恐慌。|例句：There was an outbreak of measles in the school. 学校爆发了麻疹。|近义词：eruption, explosion, burst, flare-up|反义词:suppression, containment|词性:名词

词源: From "out-" (meaning "forth, outwards") + "break" (meaning "to burst forth, erupt").  The original sense was a bursting forth or eruption, later applied metaphorically to sudden occurrences of undesirable events.
