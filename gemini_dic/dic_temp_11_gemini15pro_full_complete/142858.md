out-guard:/ˈaʊtɡɑːrd/
noun|前哨；外卫；外围警卫|A soldier or group of soldiers stationed at a distance from the main body of troops to guard against surprise attack. |The out-guard spotted the enemy approaching. 前哨发现了敌人正在靠近。|近义词: picket, sentinel, sentry|反义词: main force, garrison
verb|担任前哨；担任外围警卫|To act as an out-guard. |They out-guarded the camp during the night. 他们在夜间担任营地的外围警卫。|近义词: patrol, guard, protect|反义词: undefend
