impugn:/ɪmˈpjuːn/| v. |质疑，抨击，攻击(…的诚实或名誉)|To challenge the truth, validity, or honesty of (a statement or motive); call into question.|The lawyer impugned the witness's testimony. 律师质疑证人的证词。|Synonyms: challenge, question, dispute, contradict, deny, oppose, attack, discredit, disparage|Antonyms: support, uphold, defend, endorse, confirm, corroborate|verb

impugn:/ɪmˈpjuːn/| v. |非难，指责|To criticize (a person's character, intentions, etc.) by suggesting that they are not honest and should not be trusted.|His motives were impugned by his political rivals. 他的动机受到了政敌的非难。|Synonyms: criticize, censure, condemn, denounce, reproach, rebuke, reprimand, castigate|Antonyms: praise, commend, laud, extol, applaud|verb
