antispiritualists:/ˌæntiˈspɪrɪtʃuəlɪsts/
|n.|反唯灵论者，反对唯灵论的人|People who oppose spiritualism, a system of belief based on the idea that spirits of the dead exist and can communicate with the living.|Many antispiritualists viewed the séances of the Victorian era as fraudulent.|Synonyms: materialists, skeptics|Antonyms: spiritualists, believers|noun


antispiritualist:/ˌæntiˈspɪrɪtʃuəlɪst/
|adj.|反唯灵论的|Opposed to spiritualism.|He held an antispiritualist view of the world, believing only in what could be scientifically proven.|Synonyms: materialistic, skeptical|Antonyms: spiritualistic, believing|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix) + spiritualist

"anti-" from Greek "anti" meaning "against" or "opposite"

"spiritualist" from "spiritual" + "-ist"

"spiritual" from Late Latin "spiritualis" meaning "of the spirit," from Latin "spiritus" meaning "breath, spirit"

"-ist" a suffix forming nouns that refer to a person who practices or is skilled in something, or who adheres to a particular doctrine or system.
