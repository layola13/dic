brocade:/broʊˈkeɪd/| n. |锦缎，织锦|a rich fabric woven with a raised pattern, typically with gold or silver thread|The queen wore a gown of crimson brocade. 女王穿着一件深红色的锦缎礼服。|近义词：tapestry, damask|反义词:muslin, calico|noun

brocade:/broʊˈkeɪd/| v. |用锦缎装饰|to decorate with or as if with brocade|The walls were brocaded with intricate designs. 墙壁上装饰着复杂的锦缎图案。|近义词：adorn, embellish|反义词:strip, bare|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *broccato*, past participle of *broccare* "to prick, spur, stitch," from *brocco* "a pointed instrument, sprout," probably of Germanic origin.
