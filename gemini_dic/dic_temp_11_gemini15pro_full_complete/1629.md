acidy: /ˈæsɪdi/ | adj. | 酸的，酸性的；尖刻的 | Something that is acidy tastes sour, or related to acid.  Figuratively, it can also refer to a sharp or biting tone or manner. | The lemon juice was incredibly acidy. 柠檬汁酸得不得了。| The critic's acidy remarks stung the author.  评论家尖刻的言辞刺痛了作者。 |  sour, tart, sharp, biting | bland, mild, sweet | adjective

Etymology:
From acid + -y.  "Acid" comes from the Latin word "acidus," meaning "sour" or "sharp."
