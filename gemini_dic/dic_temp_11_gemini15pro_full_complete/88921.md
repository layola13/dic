hold-with:/hoʊld wɪð/| phrasal verb |同意，赞同；容忍，忍受|To agree with or approve of something; to tolerate something.|例句：I don't hold with his methods. 我不赞同他的方法。|近义词：agree with, approve of, condone, tolerate|反义词:disagree with, disapprove of, condemn|词性:动词短语

Etymology:  "Hold" comes from Old English "haldan," meaning "to grasp, keep, retain." "With" comes from Old English "wiþ," meaning "against, opposite, toward, near."  The combined meaning developed over time to express agreement or tolerance.
