continentally: /ˌkɒntɪˈnɛntəli/ /ˌkɑːntɪˈnɛntəli/
| adv. | 大陆式地；在大陆方面；从大陆观点 | In a continental manner; from a continental perspective. |  She dresses continentally.  她穿衣打扮具有欧洲大陆风格。| 近义词: Europeanly (especially in American English) | 反义词: insularly | 词性: 副词


Etymology:
From continental + -ly.  "Continental" comes from Late Middle English, from Old French continental, from Medieval Latin continentalis, from Latin continens, present participle of continēre "to hold together, contain."  "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives.
