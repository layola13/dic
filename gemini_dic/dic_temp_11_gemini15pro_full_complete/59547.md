eavesdropper:/ˈiːvzdrɒpər/ (UK) /ˈiːvzdrɑːpər/ (US)| n. |偷听者，窃听者|a person who secretly listens to a conversation|例句：The eavesdropper was hiding behind the curtain. 偷听者躲在窗帘后面。|例句：She accused him of being an eavesdropper. 她指责他是个偷听者。|近义词：listener, snooper|反义词：None|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ēavesdrip, from ēaves + drip. Originally referred to the dripping of water from the eaves of a house, and later to the area under the eaves where one could stand and listen to conversations inside.  The verb sense "to listen secretly" developed from this.
