impetuousnesses:/ɪmˈpɛtʃʊəsnɪsɪz/| n. |冲动，鲁莽|The state of being impetuous; characterized by sudden action or emotion without thought or care.|His impetuousnesses led him to make several rash decisions he later regretted. 他的冲动让他做出了几个后来后悔的轻率决定。|Synonyms: rashness, impulsiveness, recklessness, hastiness|Antonyms: caution, prudence, deliberation, forethought|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *impetuosnes*, from Anglo-Norman *impetuosité*, from Late Latin *impetuōsitās*, from Latin *impetuōsus* (“impetuous”) + *-itās* (suffix forming abstract nouns of quality).  *Impetuōsus* is from *impetus* (“attack, assault, onset, impulse, impetus”) (from *in-* (“in, on, upon, against”) + *petere* (“to seek, aim at, attack, go to”)) + *-ōsus* (suffix forming adjectives meaning “full of, prone to”).
