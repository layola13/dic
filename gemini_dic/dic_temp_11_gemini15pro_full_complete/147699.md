overpreciseness:/ˌoʊvərprɪˈsaɪsnɪs/| n.| 过于精确，过分讲究细节|The overpreciseness of his language made him sound pedantic. 他过于精确的语言让他听起来像个学究。|近义词：meticulousness, fastidiousness, punctiliousness|反义词：sloppiness, carelessness, inaccuracy|词性:名词
Etymology:  From "over" + "precise" + "-ness".  "Over-" is an Old English prefix meaning "excessive"; "precise" comes from the Old French "precis" and ultimately from the Latin "praecīsus", meaning "cut short, abridged, precise"; "-ness" is an Old English suffix forming nouns denoting quality, state, or condition.
