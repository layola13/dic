agued:/ˈeɪɡjud/ | adj. | 发烧的，患疟疾的，颤抖的 |  Affected with an ague; shivering; feverish.  Derived from "ague," meaning a fever marked by chills. | He felt agued and weak after being caught in the rain. 他淋雨后感到发烧和虚弱。 | 近义词: feverish, shivering, chilled, ill | 反义词: healthy, well | 词性: adjective

词源: From Middle English *ague*, from Old French *ague* (modern French *aiguë*, feminine of *aigu* (“sharp, acute”)), from Latin *acūta* (sc. *febris*), feminine of *acūtus* (“sharp”), from *acuō* (“I sharpen”), from *acus* (“needle”).  The original meaning referred to the sharp, piercing pains associated with the fever.
