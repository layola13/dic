flunkers:/ˈflʌŋkərz/
noun
不及格的人；失败者
People who fail a test or course.  Those who are unsuccessful.
The flunkers had to retake the exam.  那些不及格的人不得不重考。
近义词:failures, losers
反义词:passers, winners
词性:n.

From the verb "flunk," which means to fail an exam or course. "Flunk" likely originated as student slang in the mid-19th century in American English. Its exact origins are uncertain, but it may be related to similar-sounding words implying a heavy fall or failure.
