alamire:/ˈæləˌmɪər/| n.|A (now obsolete) name for the lowest note of the gamut; the note A below middle C.|The alamire was a fundamental note in medieval music.|In medieval music theory, the alamire served as a crucial reference point.|近义词: lowest note, A (musical note)|反义词: highest note|词性:noun

词源: From Medieval Latin *a la mi re, referring to the note A in the hexachord system of Guido d'Arezzo.  "Mi" was sung on A, and the Gamma (Greek letter gamma, corresponding to G) was often written with a symbol resembling A, hence "A la mi re" meaning "A with mi".
