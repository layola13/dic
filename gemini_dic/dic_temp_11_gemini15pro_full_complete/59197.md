dyspeptically:/dɪsˈpɛptɪkli/ | adv. | 消化不良地；脾气坏地 |  In a manner relating to or suffering from dyspepsia (indigestion); also, figuratively, in a bad-tempered or irritable way. | He complained dyspeptically about the rich food. 他抱怨油腻的食物让他消化不良。 |  grumpily, irritably, peevishly, morosely, sourly | cheerfully, pleasantly, good-humoredly | adverb

Etymology:
From dyspepsia + -ally.  Dyspepsia comes from Greek *dyspepsia, from dys- (bad, difficult) + pepsis (digestion) from peptein (to digest).
