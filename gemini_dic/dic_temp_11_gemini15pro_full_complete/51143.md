despoliation:/ˌdɛspɔɪˈleɪʃən/ /ˌdiːspɔɪˈleɪʃən/
noun
掠夺，破坏，损毁
The act of despoiling; plundering; robbery; the act of stripping of what constitutes its value, beauty, or usefulness.
The despoliation of the countryside caused by the new road was devastating.  新修的公路对乡村的破坏是毁灭性的。
近义词：plunder, pillage, looting, devastation, destruction
反义词：preservation, conservation, restoration
词性：n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *despoliatio, from Latin despoliare "to plunder, rob," from de- "down, away" + spoliare "to strip, plunder."
