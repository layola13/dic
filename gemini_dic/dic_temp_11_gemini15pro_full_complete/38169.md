compromising:/ˈkɒmprəmaɪzɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmprəmaɪzɪŋ/ (US)| adj.| 妥协的，让步的；有损名誉的，不名誉的|  damaging to one's reputation or moral standing; involving a compromise| Example: The photos were compromising and could ruin his career. 这些照片有损他的名誉，可能会毁了他的事业。|Synonyms: incriminating, damaging, discrediting, embarrassing, jeopardizing |Antonyms:  flattering, commendable, honorable, respectable| adjective

compromising:/ˈkɒmprəmaɪzɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmprəmaɪzɪŋ/ (US)| v.| 妥协，让步；危及，损害 (现在分词)|present participle of  *compromise* meaning to expose to danger, suspicion, scandal, or disrepute| Example: By accepting the bribe, he was compromising his integrity.  通过接受贿赂，他损害了自己的正直。|Synonyms: endangering, jeopardizing, imperiling, undermining|Antonyms: protecting, safeguarding, securing, upholding| verb (present participle)

Etymology: From the Late Latin *compromissum*,  past participle of *compromittere*  "to make a mutual promise," from *com-* "together" + *promittere* "to promise."  The negative sense developed from the idea of placing oneself in a position of vulnerability or suspicion through compromise.
