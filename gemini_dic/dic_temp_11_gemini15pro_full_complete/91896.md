hyperlethargy: /ˌhaɪpərˈlɛθərdʒi/ | n. | 极度嗜睡，极度倦怠 | A state of extreme lethargy, characterized by excessive sleepiness or sluggishness and lack of energy. |  The patient exhibited hyperlethargy and confusion after the surgery.  手术后，病人表现出极度嗜睡和神志不清。|  Near synonyms: somnolence, stupor, lassitude | Antonyms:  energy, vigor, alertness | noun


Etymology:  From hyper- (Greek: 'over, excessive') + lethargy (from Greek lēthargia, from lēthargos 'forgetful,' from lēthē 'forgetfulness' + argos 'idle').
