gold-plated:/ˈɡoʊld ˈpleɪtɪd/| adj.|镀金的；虚饰的，华而不实的|Covered with a thin layer of gold; superficially attractive but lacking real value.|例句：She received a gold-plated pen as a retirement gift. 她收到了一支镀金钢笔作为退休礼物。

例句：The company's promises turned out to be gold-plated; they didn't deliver on anything. 该公司的承诺结果证明是华而不实的；他们什么也没兑现。|近义词：gilded, gold-covered, showy, ostentatious|反义词：plain, simple, unadorned|词性:形容词

gold-plate:/ˈɡoʊld ˈpleɪt/| v.|镀金；虚饰，粉饰|To cover with a thin layer of gold; to make something appear better than it really is.|例句：The company gold-plates its products to make them seem more luxurious.  这家公司给它的产品镀金，让它们看起来更奢华。

例句：He tried to gold-plate his resume with exaggerated achievements. 他试图用夸大的成就来粉饰他的简历。|近义词：gild, embellish, exaggerate|反义词：expose, reveal, understate|词性:动词
