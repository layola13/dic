bad-mouther: /ˈbædˌmaʊðər/ (US), /ˈbædˌmaʊðə/ (UK)
n. | 诽谤者，说坏话的人 | Someone who says bad things about another person or thing. | He's such a bad-mouther; he's always criticizing people behind their backs. 他真是个爱说人坏话的家伙，总是在背后批评别人。|  slanderer, gossipmonger |  supporter, admirer | Noun

Etymology:
"bad" + "mouth" + "-er" (agent suffix).  "Bad" meaning unfavorable or unpleasant, "mouth" referring to speaking, and "-er" indicating the person who performs the action.  Essentially, someone who speaks badly of others.
