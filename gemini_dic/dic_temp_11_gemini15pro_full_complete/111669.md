lock-in:/ˈlɑːk ɪn/ (US) /ˈlɒk ɪn/ (UK)| n.|锁定；套牢；限制|The process of becoming unable to change to a different supplier, system, etc. because it would be too expensive or complicated.|The company's lock-in strategy makes it difficult for customers to switch providers.|近义词：trap, tie-in, commitment|反义词：freedom, flexibility|词性:名词

lock-in:/ˈlɑːk ɪn/ (US) /ˈlɒk ɪn/ (UK)| adj.|锁定的；套牢的|Relating to a lock-in period or situation.|The lock-in contract prevents customers from leaving for two years.|近义词：tied, committed, fixed|反义词：free, flexible, uncommitted|词性:形容词
