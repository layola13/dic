aggregate: /ˈæɡrɪɡət/ (US), /ˈæɡrɪɡeɪt/ (UK)| n. adj. v. |总数，合计；聚集体；骨料|A whole formed by combining several separate elements.|The aggregate score of both teams was 20-18.  两队的总分是20比18。|synonyms: total, sum, combined, collective|antonyms: individual, separate, single|noun

aggregate: /ˈæɡrɪɡət/ (US), /ˈæɡrɪɡeɪt/ (UK)|adj.|总的，合计的；聚集的|Formed or calculated by the combination of several separate elements; total.|The aggregate demand for consumer goods has fallen sharply. 消费品的总需求急剧下降。|synonyms: overall, total, combined, gross|antonyms: individual, separate, single|adjective

aggregate: /ˈæɡrɪɡeɪt/ (US), /ˈæɡrɪɡeɪt/ (UK)|v.|聚集，集合；总计|Form or group into a class or cluster.|The butterflies aggregate in dense clouds. 蝴蝶聚集在一起形成密集的云状。|synonyms:  gather, collect, assemble, amass|antonyms: disperse, scatter, separate, divide|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin aggregatus, past participle of aggregare ("to add to"), from ad- ("to") + gregare ("to herd"), from grex ("flock, herd").
