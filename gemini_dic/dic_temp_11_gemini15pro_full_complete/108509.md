layoffs:/ˈleɪɒfs/ (UK) /ˈleɪˌɔːfs/ (US)| n.|裁员；临时解雇|a temporary or permanent discharge of a worker or workers.|The company announced layoffs of 100 employees. 公司宣布裁员100人。|Many workers are facing layoffs due to the economic downturn. 由于经济低迷，许多工人面临裁员。|近义词：redundancies, downsizing, job cuts|反义词：hiring, recruitment|词性:noun

词源: layoff (n.) 1895, American English, from lay (v.) + off (adv.). Originally "a period of inactivity," especially in sports; meaning "discharge from employment, usually temporary" is from 1921.
