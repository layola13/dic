manipulate: /məˈnɪpjʊleɪt/
| vt. | 操纵，控制；操作，处理 | To control or influence someone or something, often in a skillful or devious way; to handle or operate something skillfully. | He manipulated the stock market to his advantage. 他操纵股市为自己谋利。| Synonyms: control, influence, exploit, maneuver | Antonyms: assist, help, support | verb

manipulate: /məˈnɪpjʊleɪt/
| vt. | (熟练地)操作，运用 | To handle or operate something skillfully. | She manipulated the controls of the aircraft with ease. 她轻松地操作飞机的控制装置。| Synonyms: operate, handle, control, manage | Antonyms: misuse, mishandle | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *manipulatus*, past participle of *manipulare* "to handle, manage," from Latin *manipulus* "handful, bundle, company of soldiers," from *manus* "hand" + *-pulus* diminutive suffix.
