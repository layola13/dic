deadend:/ˈdedˌend/| n. adj.|死胡同，尽头；僵局|A street or passage closed at one end.  A situation in which no further progress can be made. |例句：
We drove into a deadend. 我们开车进了死胡同。

The negotiations have reached a deadend. 谈判陷入了僵局。

|近义词：impasse, stalemate, blind alley, cul-de-sac|反义词:continuation, breakthrough|名词，形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "dead" (meaning "closed off, blocked") + "end".  First recorded use as a noun in the late 19th century, originally referring to a street closed at one end.  The figurative meaning of a situation with no progress developed later.
