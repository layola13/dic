kernel:/ˈkɜːrnl/ (US) /ˈkɜːnəl/ (UK)| n. |仁，核心，内核|The central, most important part of something. |例句：The kernel of the argument is that free trade benefits everyone.  这论证的核心是自由贸易对每个人都有利。

例句：He ate the apple, but discarded the kernel. 他吃了苹果，但扔掉了果核。|近义词：core, heart, center, nucleus, gist, essence|反义词：periphery, edge, surface|词性:noun


kernel:/ˈkɜːrnl/ (US) /ˈkɜːnəl/ (UK)| n. |（谷物）粒，籽|A grain or seed, as of wheat or corn.|例句：The chickens pecked at the scattered kernels of corn.  鸡啄食散落在地的玉米粒。

例句：The farmer saved the best kernels for next year's planting.  农民把最好的种子留到明年播种。|近义词：grain, seed|反义词：-|词性:noun


kernel:/ˈkɜːrnl/ (US) /ˈkɜːnəl/ (UK)| n. |（操作系统）内核|The central part of an operating system.|例句：The Linux kernel is open source.  Linux内核是开源的。

例句：A kernel panic can cause the system to crash. 内核崩溃会导致系统崩溃。|近义词：core, nucleus|反义词：-|词性:noun
