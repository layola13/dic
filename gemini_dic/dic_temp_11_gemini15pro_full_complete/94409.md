implication:/ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. |含义;暗示;牵连;卷入|Something that is implied or suggested, or the act of implying or suggesting something.  The conclusion that can be drawn from something although it is not explicitly stated.|例句：The implication of his statement was that he was resigning. 他的话暗示着他要辞职。

例句：The company's expansion has implications for local employment. 公司的扩张对当地就业有影响。|近义词：consequence, effect, significance, suggestion, inference|反义词：explicit statement, direct statement|词性:noun

implication:/ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. |牵涉，牵连;包含|The fact or state of being involved in or connected to something (such as a crime).|例句：His implication in the robbery was never proven. 他参与抢劫的指控从未被证实。

例句：The implication of several high-ranking officials in the scandal shocked the nation. 几位高级官员卷入丑闻震惊了全国。|近义词：involvement, connection|反义词：exclusion, non-involvement|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin implicātiōn- (stem of implicātiō ), from Latin implicare (to involve, entangle), from in- (in, into) + plicare (to fold).
