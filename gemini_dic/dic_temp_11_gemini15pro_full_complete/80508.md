good-naturedly:/ˌɡʊdˈneɪtʃərdli/
| adv. | 好脾气地，和蔼地 | In a pleasant, friendly, and easygoing manner.  | He good-naturedly agreed to help her with the move. 他和蔼地答应帮她搬家。|  She good-naturedly laughed at his jokes, even though they weren't very funny. 即使他的笑话并不好笑，她也和善地笑了。 | 近义词: amiably, affably, genially, pleasantly | 反义词: grumpily, irritably, churlishly | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "good-natured" + "-ly".  "Good-natured" is formed from "good" + "natured". "Natured" comes from the noun "nature" with the suffix "-ed" indicating having a certain nature or disposition.  "Good" traces back to Old English "gōd". "Nature" comes from Old French "nature" ultimately derived from Latin "nātūra".  "-ly" is a common suffix forming adverbs.
