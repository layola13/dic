catwalks:/ˈkætˌwɔːks/ | n. |T台，狭窄的过道 |A narrow, elevated walkway used by models to display clothes at a fashion show, or a similar walkway used for maintenance or inspection purposes. |The models strutted confidently down the catwalk. 模特们自信地在T台上昂首阔步。

The technician used a catwalk to access the top of the machinery. 技术员使用狭窄的过道到达机器顶部。|近义词：runway, ramp, walkway |反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "floor" in some contexts) |词性:noun


catwalk:/ˈkætˌwɔːk/ | v. |在T台上走秀 |To walk on a catwalk, typically as a fashion model.  |She catwalks with grace and poise. 她优雅而沉着地在T台上走秀。

He practiced his catwalk for hours before the audition. 他在试镜前练习了几个小时的T台走秀。|近义词：strut, sashay, model |反义词: (No direct antonym) |词性:verb

词源:  "catwalk" originally referred to a narrow walkway used by cats, perhaps on ships or in other confined spaces.  This meaning was later extended to similar walkways used for industrial purposes, and eventually to the raised platform used in fashion shows.
