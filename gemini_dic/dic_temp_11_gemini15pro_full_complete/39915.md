constrainedly:/kənˈstreɪnɪdli/ | adv. |拘谨地，勉强地|In a manner that is restricted or limited, often due to external factors or social pressures.  Showing a lack of naturalness or spontaneity.|She smiled constrainedly, clearly uncomfortable with the attention.|近义词:stiffly, awkwardly, reservedly, hesitantly|反义词:freely, openly, naturally, spontaneously|词性:副词

Etymology:  From constrain + -ed + -ly.  "Constrain" comes from Old French "constraindre," ultimately from Latin "constringere" meaning "to bind tightly together."
