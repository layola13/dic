downhaul:/ˈdaʊnˌhɔːl/| n.|下曳索；下拉索|A rope used to pull something down, especially the sail on a sailboat.|The downhaul was tightened to flatten the sail. 下曳索被拉紧以展平帆。|近义词：downward pull, outhaul (in some contexts)|反义词: topping lift|noun

downhaul:/ˈdaʊnˌhɔːl/| v.|向下拖拉；用下曳索拉紧|To pull something down using a downhaul.|He downhauled the sail to reduce its power. 他用下曳索拉紧帆以减少其力量。|近义词：lower, pull down|反义词: hoist, raise|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "down" + "haul".  "Down" indicates direction, and "haul" means to pull with force.
