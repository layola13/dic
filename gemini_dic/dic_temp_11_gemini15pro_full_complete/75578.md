fuddy-duddy:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi/ | n. | 老顽固，守旧的人 | A person who is old-fashioned and fussy, especially about their appearance or habits. |  例句：He's such a fuddy-duddy, he still wears a bow tie every day. 他真是个老顽固，每天都戴着领结。

例句：My grandfather is a bit of a fuddy-duddy when it comes to technology. 我祖父对科技有点守旧。| 近义词：conservative, traditionalist, diehard, stick-in-the-mud | 反义词：modernist, progressive, innovator | 词性: 名词


fuddy's: /ˈfʌdiz/ | n. | 老顽固的（东西/行为/观点）; (非正式) 指老顽固拥有的东西 | Belonging to or associated with a fuddy-duddy; (informally) the possessions or domain of a fuddy-duddy.  | 例句：That's just fuddy's talk; no one believes that anymore. 那只是老顽固的说法，现在没人相信了。

例句：Don't touch that; it's fuddy's! 别碰那个，那是老顽固的东西！| 近义词：old-fashioned, outdated, antiquated | 反义词：modern, contemporary, trendy | 词性: 名词 (通常用作所有格)


词源:  "fuddy-duddy" 的起源不确定，但它可能模仿了重复的音节来表示琐碎或过时的想法。 最早记录的用法是在20世纪初。 "fuddy's" 则是从 "fuddy-duddy" 派生出来的所有格形式。
