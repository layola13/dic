oversimplification:/ˌoʊvərˌsɪmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 过度简化 |The act of making something seem simpler than it really is, often distorting or misrepresenting it in the process. | Historians often caution against oversimplification when studying complex historical events. 历史学家经常告诫人们在研究复杂的历史事件时不要过度简化。

The explanation of the economic crisis was an oversimplification, leaving out many important factors. 对经济危机的解释过于简化，忽略了许多重要因素。

| 近义词：reductionism, distortion, misrepresentation | 反义词：nuance, complexity, elaboration | 词性: noun

oversimplify:/ˌoʊvərˈsɪmplɪfaɪ/ | v. | 过度简化 |To describe or explain something in a way that makes it seem simpler than it really is, often leaving out important details. | The teacher oversimplified the complex scientific concept, leading to confusion among the students. 老师过度简化了复杂的科学概念，导致学生们感到困惑。

Politicians often oversimplify complex issues to appeal to a wider audience. 政客们经常过度简化复杂的问题以吸引更广泛的受众。

| 近义词：distort, misrepresent, trivialize | 反义词：elaborate, clarify, specify | 词性: verb
