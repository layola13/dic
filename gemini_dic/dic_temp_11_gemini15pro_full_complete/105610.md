kinematics: /ˌkɪnəˈmætɪks/ | n. |运动学|The branch of mechanics concerned with the motion of objects without reference to the forces which cause the motion.|Example 1:  The kinematics of the robot arm were carefully calculated to ensure smooth movement.  机器人手臂的运动学经过仔细计算，以确保运动流畅。

Example 2:  Understanding the kinematics of the human body is essential for athletes and physical therapists. 了解人体的运动学对运动员和物理治疗师至关重要。|Synonyms: motion study,  movement analysis |Antonyms: statics, dynamics (in some contexts as dynamics often includes kinematics) |Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cinématique*, from Greek *kínēma* "movement, motion," from *kineîn* "to move."
