mercurialize:/mɜːrˌkjʊəriəlaɪz/ | v. | 使变化无常，使反复无常 | To make (someone or something) subject to sudden or unpredictable changes of mood or mind. |  His mercurial personality made it difficult to predict his reactions. 他的反复无常的性格让人很难预测他的反应。| The stock market has been mercurialized by the recent economic downturn. 最近的经济衰退使股票市场变得变化无常。 | 近义词: destabilize, unsettle, fluctuate | 反义词: stabilize, steady |词性:动词

mercurial:/mɜːrˈkjʊəriəl/ | adj. |  变化无常的，反复无常的；水银的 | Subject to sudden or unpredictable changes of mood or mind; containing or caused by mercury. | Her mercurial temperament made her difficult to work with. 她反复无常的脾气让她很难相处。 |  He suffered from mercurial poisoning. 他患有汞中毒。 | 近义词: volatile, erratic, fickle | 反义词: stable, steady, constant |词性:形容词


词源:  From Latin *mercuriālis*, from Mercurius (“Mercury”), the Roman god of commerce, eloquence, messages/communication (including divination), travelers, boundaries, luck, trickery and thieves; equivalent to the Greek god Hermes.  The connection to mood swings likely comes from the perceived swiftness and unpredictability of the messenger god.
