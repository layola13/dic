antipatriot: /ˌæntiˈpeɪtriət/ /ˌæntiˈpeɪtriɑːt/
| n. |不爱国者|A person who does not love or support their country. |He was labeled an antipatriot for his outspoken criticism of the government.  由于他公开批评政府，他被贴上了不爱国者的标签。|
|Example 1:  The protesters were called antipatriots by some members of the media. 一些媒体成员称抗议者为不爱国者。|
|Example 2: He was accused of being an antipatriot for revealing classified information. 他因泄露机密信息而被指控为不爱国者。|
|近义词: traitor, dissident, rebel|
|反义词: patriot, loyalist|
|词性: 名词|


词源:
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to (来自希腊语 anti-)
patriot: a person who vigorously supports their country and is prepared to defend it against enemies or detractors (来自法语 patriote, 最终来自希腊语 patrios, meaning "of one's fathers")
-ic (suffix): relating to; having the character or form of (来自希腊语 -ikos)
-ally (suffix): in a ... manner (来自古英语 -lice)
