indulge: /ɪnˈdʌldʒ/ (US) /ɪnˈdʌldʒ/ (UK)| v. |放纵；沉溺；满足（欲望、爱好等）；纵容；迁就|To allow oneself or another person to enjoy something desired, especially something considered wrong or not good for you.  To satisfy a desire.|

例句：
She indulged in a long hot bath.
她尽情享受了一次长时间的热水澡。

He indulged his passion for collecting stamps.
他沉溺于集邮的爱好。


近义词：pamper, spoil, gratify, humor, cater to|
反义词：deny, restrict, abstain|
词性：动词 (verb)|

indulged: /ɪnˈdʌldʒd/ (US) /ɪnˈdʌldʒd/ (UK)| v. (past tense/past participle) |放纵；沉溺；满足；纵容；迁就 (过去式/过去分词)|Past tense and past participle of "indulge".|

例句：
They indulged themselves with a weekend at a spa.
他们在水疗中心享受了一个周末。


She had indulged in too much chocolate and felt sick.
她吃了太多的巧克力，感到恶心。


近义词：pampered, spoiled, gratified, humored, catered to|
反义词：denied, restricted, abstained|
词性：动词 (past participle/past tense)|


词源: From Old French *indulgier, from Latin indulgere "to be kind or tender to, yield to, comply with," from in- "in" + dulcis "sweet."
