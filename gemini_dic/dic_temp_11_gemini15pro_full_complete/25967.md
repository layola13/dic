burrowers:/ˈbɜːroʊərz/ (US) /ˈbʌrəʊəz/ (UK)
n. |穴居动物；挖洞者|Animals that dig and live in burrows. |例句：Burrowers, such as rabbits and moles, play an important role in soil aeration.  穴居动物，例如兔子和鼹鼠，在土壤通气中起着重要作用。

例句：The burrowers had created an intricate network of tunnels beneath the ground. 这些挖洞者在地下建造了一个复杂的隧道网络。
|近义词： diggers, excavators, tunnelers |反义词: surface dwellers
词性: 名词 (plural noun)

Etymology:
From the verb "burrow" + "-er" (agent suffix).  "Burrow" comes from the Old English "burg" (fortress, fortified place) and later evolved to mean a hole dug in the ground.
