apposed:/əˈpoʊzd/ | adj. | 并列的，并置的，紧靠的 | Placed side by side or in close proximity; juxtaposed. | Example: The two buildings were apposed, sharing a common wall.  两栋建筑并排而立，共用一堵墙。| Example:  The artist apposed contrasting colors to create a striking effect. 艺术家将对比鲜明的颜色并置，以创造引人注目的效果。 |Synonyms: juxtaposed, adjacent, contiguous | Antonyms: separated, distant, apart | adjective

appose:/əˈpoʊz/ | v. | 并置，把…并列 | To place side by side or in close proximity; juxtapose. | Example: She apposed the two photographs to compare the differences. 她把两张照片并排放置以便比较差异。| Example: The chef carefully apposed the different ingredients on the plate. 厨师小心翼翼地将不同的食材摆放在盘子上。| Synonyms: juxtapose, place together, set side by side | Antonyms: separate, divide, detach | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *apponere, from Latin ad- + ponere "to put, place."  The word "appose" shares its root with words like "pose," "position," and "apposition."
