ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *ciborium*, from Greek *κιβώριον* (kibōrion), meaning "Egyptian bean pod," then "cup-shaped vessel."  The meaning shifted to refer to a canopy-like structure resembling an inverted cup, and later to the vessel used to hold the Eucharist, possibly due to its shape.
