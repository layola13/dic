anacoenosis:/ænəsɪˈnoʊsɪs/ | n. | 反问; 设问 | A rhetorical device in which a speaker poses a question to the audience, often with an implied answer.  It's used to engage the audience and make them think about the speaker's point of view. | Example 1: "Don't you think we deserve better?" (难道我们不值得更好吗？)

Example 2: "Is it not obvious that this policy is flawed?" (难道这项政策的缺陷还不明显吗？) | Near synonyms: rhetorical question, interrogation, appeal | Antonyms: statement, assertion | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *anacoenosis, from Ancient Greek ἀνακοινόω (anakoinóō, “I communicate, consult”), from ἀνά (aná, “up”) + κοινόω (koinóō, “I make common, communicate”).
