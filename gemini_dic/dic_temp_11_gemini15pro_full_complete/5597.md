altercate: /ˈɔːltərkeɪt/ (US) /ˈɒltəkeɪt/ (UK)| vi.| 争吵，口角| To argue or quarrel heatedly; to dispute angrily. | The two neighbours altercated loudly about the property line.  两个邻居大声地争论着地产界限。| synonyms: argue, quarrel, squabble, bicker, wrangle, dispute, contend | antonyms: agree, concur, harmonize, compromise | verb

altercation: /ˌɔːltərˈkeɪʃn/ (US) /ˌɒltəˈkeɪʃn/ (UK) | n. | 争吵，口角 | A noisy or angry argument or disagreement. | The altercation between the two drivers caused a traffic jam. 两个司机之间的争吵造成了交通堵塞。| synonyms: argument, quarrel, squabble, disagreement, dispute, clash | antonyms: agreement, harmony, concord, accord | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *altercatio, from Latin altercari "to quarrel, dispute, wrangle," frequentative of altercare "to contend with another" from alter "other" + -care "to take care."
