nosier:/ˈnɔɪ.zi.ər/ (US), /ˈnɔɪ.zi.ə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更吵闹的，更多管闲事的|Comparative form of noisy: producing a lot of noise. Also, more inquisitive than one should be; prying.|例句：The street market was nosier than usual today. 今天的街市比往常更吵。

例句：My neighbor is getting nosier as she gets older. 我的邻居年纪越大就越爱管闲事。|近义词：louder, more inquisitive, prying, meddlesome|反义词:quieter, less inquisitive, discreet|词性:adjective

nosey:/ˈnəʊ.zi/ (UK), /ˈnoʊ.zi/ (US)| adj.|爱管闲事的，好打听的|Inquisitive about other people's affairs; prying.|例句：She's a bit nosey, always wanting to know everyone's business. 她有点爱管闲事，总是想知道每个人的事。

例句：Don't be so nosey! It's none of your concern. 别这么八卦！这不关你的事。|近义词：inquisitive, curious, prying, meddlesome|反义词:uninterested, indifferent, discreet|词性:adjective


词源:From Middle English *nosy, from nose + -y.  The sense of "inquisitive" arose from the image of someone poking their nose into other people's business.
