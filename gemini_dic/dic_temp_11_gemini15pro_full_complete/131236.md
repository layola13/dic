noncapriciously: /ˌnɒnkəˈprɪʃəsli/ | adv. | 不反复无常地，稳定地 | In a manner that is not characterized by sudden, unpredictable changes in mood or behavior; steadily. |  The judge noncapriciously applied the law to all cases. 法官对所有案件都稳定地运用法律。| The company noncapriciously invested in long-term growth strategies. 公司坚持不懈地投资于长期增长战略。 |近义词：steadily, consistently, reliably, dependably | 反义词：capriciously, erratically, whimsically, unpredictably | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
non- (prefix): from Latin, meaning "not"
capricious (adjective): from Italian "capriccioso," ultimately from "capro" (goat), referring to the goat's tendency to leap and jump erratically.
-ly (suffix): forms adverbs from adjectives.
