astipulate:/əˈstɪpjʊleɪt/| v. |规定；约定；明确要求|To specify or arrange something formally or clearly, especially as a condition of agreement.|The contract stipulates that the work must be completed within six months. 合同规定，这项工作必须在六个月内完成。|近义词：specify, prescribe, require, demand|反义词：imply, suggest|词性:动词

astipulation:/əˌstɪpjʊˈleɪʃən/| n. |规定；约定；明确要求|A condition or requirement that is specified or demanded as part of an agreement.|One of the stipulations of the contract was that the goods must be delivered within 24 hours. 合同的其中一项规定是货物必须在 24 小时内交付。|近义词：requirement, condition, provision, specification|反义词：implication, suggestion|词性:名词

词源：From Latin *stipulātus, past participle of stipulārī (“to demand a formal promise, make a bargain”), from stipula (“stalk, straw; agreement”), perhaps related to stipare (“to press, pack, stuff”).  来自拉丁语 *stipulātus，stipulārī（“要求正式承诺，达成协议”）的过去分词，来自 stipula（“茎，稻草；协议”），可能与 stipare（“按压，包装，填充”）相关。
