fiery:/ˈfaɪəri/ (US), /ˈfaɪəri/ (UK)| adj.|火热的，炽热的；暴躁的；激烈的|Consisting of fire or burning strongly; easily provoked to anger; showing strong emotion, especially anger or excitement.|The fiery sunset painted the sky in vibrant hues. 火红的夕阳将天空染成了鲜艳的色彩。|Synonyms: burning, blazing, passionate, fervent, intense, heated|Antonyms: calm, cool, peaceful, impassive, subdued|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fieri*, *fyry*, from Old English *fȳren* (“fiery, on fire”), from Proto-Germanic *fūrijaną* (“fiery”), from Proto-Indo-European *péh₂wr̥* (“fire”). Cognate with West Frisian *fierich* (“fiery”), Dutch *vurig* (“fiery”), German *feurig* (“fiery”), Danish *fyrig* (“fiery”). Related to fire.
