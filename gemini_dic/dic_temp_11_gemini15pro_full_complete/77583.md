gedd:/ɡɛd/ | n. | (Scottish) pike (fish) | A freshwater fish with a long, slender body and sharp teeth. | Example: The gedd is a common fish in Scottish lochs.  这种梭子鱼在苏格兰湖泊中很常见。 | Example: He caught a large gedd while fishing. 他钓鱼时钓到了一条大梭子鱼。 | Synonym: pike, pickerel | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a specific fish) | noun

etymology->
From Scots *ged, from Middle English gedde, from Old English *gedd, from Proto-Germanic *gaddiz. Cognate with Dutch geep (“garfish”), German Hecht (“pike”).
