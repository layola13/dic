hang-it-up:/ˌhæŋ ɪt ˈʌp/ | phrasal verb | 放弃，停止，戒掉 | To quit or stop doing something, often due to frustration or realizing it's no longer worth the effort.  It can also mean to retire from a profession or activity. | Example: After years of trying to make it as a musician, he finally decided to hang it up.  多年来一直试图成为一名音乐家后，他最终决定放弃。

Example:  The team was down by 20 points and decided to hang it up. 球队落后20分，决定放弃比赛。 | Synonym: give up, quit, stop, retire, cease | Antonym: continue, persist, persevere | verb phrase


hang-it-up:/ˌhæŋ ɪt ˈʌp/ | idiom | 放弃，停止，戒掉 | To quit or stop doing something.  Often used informally. | Example:  "I'm tired of arguing, let's just hang it up."  “我厌倦了争吵，我们还是就此打住吧。”

Example: After failing the exam for the third time, she decided to hang it up.  在第三次考试不及格后，她决定放弃。 | Synonym: give up, quit, stop | Antonym: continue, persist | idiom
