eat crow:/iːt kroʊ/
verb phrase
认错；收回前言；承认错误
To admit one's mistake or defeat, especially when one has predicted or promised otherwise.  To be forced to do something humiliating.
He boasted that his team would win, but he had to eat crow when they lost badly. 他吹嘘他的球队会赢，但当他们惨败时，他不得不承认错误。
The politician was forced to eat crow after his scandalous affair was revealed.  这位政客在丑闻曝光后被迫承认错误。
近义词: admit defeat, back down, recant, swallow one's pride
反义词: gloat, boast, brag
词性: verb phrase

eat crow:/iːt kroʊ/
idiom
认错；收回前言；承认错误
To admit one's mistake or defeat, especially when one has predicted or promised otherwise.  To be forced to do something humiliating.
