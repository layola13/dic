bowerbirds:/ˈbaʊərbɜːrdz/
noun
园丁鸟
A family of birds known for their elaborate courtship rituals, where males build and decorate structures called bowers to attract females.
Male bowerbirds build elaborate bowers to attract females. 雄性园丁鸟建造精致的凉亭来吸引雌性。
Bowerbirds are found in Australia and New Guinea. 园丁鸟分布在澳大利亚和新几内亚。
synonyms:  bird of paradise (similar behavior)
antonyms:  (none specific, could use a bird known for simple courtship)
