monodynamism:/ˌmɒnəˈdaɪnəmɪzəm/ | n. |单一动力论，单一驱动论|The theory that all phenomena are ultimately reducible to a single active principle or substance.| The philosopher argued for monodynamism, claiming that all natural processes could be explained by a single underlying force.  哲学家主张单一动力论，声称所有自然过程都可以用单一潜在力来解释。|近义词: monism |反义词: pluralism |词性: noun

Etymology:  From mono- (one) + dyna- (power) + -ism (doctrine).
