mendicant: /ˈmɛndɪkənt/ | n. adj.| 乞丐；托钵僧；行乞的 | A beggar, especially one who lives by asking for alms.  Also used as an adjective to describe someone who begs or something related to begging. |  The mendicant sat on the corner, silently holding out his cup.  那个乞丐坐在角落里，默默地伸出他的杯子。| Synonyms: beggar, pauper, supplicant | Antonyms: giver, philanthropist, benefactor | Noun, Adjective

mendicant: /ˈmɛndɪkənt/ | adj.| 行乞的；乞讨的 | Relating to or characteristic of a beggar. | The mendicant friar relied on the kindness of strangers.  托钵僧依靠陌生人的好意。| Synonyms: begging, supplicating | Antonyms: giving, donating | Adjective
