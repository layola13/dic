bollocks's:/ˈbɒləksɪz/ | n. | 胡说八道；废话；睾丸（粗俗） |  "Bollocks" is a vulgar British slang term primarily used to express disbelief, disagreement, or annoyance.  The possessive form "bollocks's" is grammatically unusual and rarely used, and would imply ownership of something related to the aforementioned meanings.  It's more common to see phrases like "a load of bollocks" or simply "bollocks." | It's highly unlikely to encounter "bollocks's" in a natural sentence due to its grammatical awkwardness and vulgarity.  A contrived example could be: "The politician's bollocks's impact on the public discourse was significant," but this is highly unnatural and better expressed as "The politician's lies/nonsense had a significant impact on the public discourse." |  Near synonyms: rubbish, nonsense, crap, lies | Antonyms: truth, facts | noun
