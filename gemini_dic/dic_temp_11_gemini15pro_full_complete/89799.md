hormones:/ˈhɔːrmoʊnz/ (US) /ˈhɔːməʊnz/ (UK)| n. |激素|A regulatory substance produced in an organism and transported in tissue fluids such as blood or sap to stimulate specific cells or tissues into action.|The doctor checked her hormone levels. 医生检查了她的激素水平。|synonyms: secretions, endocrine secretions|antonyms: (No direct antonyms for hormones, but one could consider inhibitors or blockers as functional opposites)|noun
