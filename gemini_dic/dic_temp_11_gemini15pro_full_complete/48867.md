deforming:/dɪˈfɔːrmɪŋ/
verb, adjective|使变形；损毁；丑化|changing the shape of something, usually by making it worse; making something ugly or spoiled|The heat was deforming the plastic.  高温使塑料变形了。|synonyms: distorting, warping, twisting, contorting|antonyms:  perfecting, improving, enhancing|verb
deforming:/dɪˈfɔːrmɪŋ/
adjective|变形的；畸形的|having an altered or misshapen form|The deforming effects of the disease were clearly visible. 这种疾病造成的畸形影响清晰可见。|synonyms: distorted, misshapen, warped|antonyms:  perfect, symmetrical, unblemished|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "deform," which comes from the Old French "deformer," from the Latin "deformare," meaning "to disfigure, distort."  "De-" is a prefix meaning "down, away from," and "formare" means "to form, shape."
