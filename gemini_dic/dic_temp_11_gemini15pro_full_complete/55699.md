diuretics:/ˌdaɪjʊˈrɛtɪks/ | n. | 利尿剂 | Substances that promote the production of urine.  They increase the excretion of water and salts from the body. |  The doctor prescribed diuretics to help reduce the fluid buildup in his legs. 医生开了利尿剂来帮助减少他腿部的积液。

Diuretics are often used to treat high blood pressure. 利尿剂常用于治疗高血压。 | synonyms: water pills | antonyms: antidiuretics | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *diurétique* (now *diurétiques*), from Ancient Greek *διουρητικός (diourētikós)*, from *διά (diá, “through”)* + *οὖρον (oûron, “urine”)*. Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ews- (“to urinate”)*.
