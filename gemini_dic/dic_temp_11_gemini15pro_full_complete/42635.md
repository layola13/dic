counterseal: /ˈkaʊntərˌsiːl/ (US), /ˈkaʊntəˌsiːl/ (UK)| n. vt.| 副印，加盖副印；反封，反签|A seal affixed to a document already bearing another seal, typically as confirmation or validation. It can also refer to the act of affixing such a seal.|The king countersealed the treaty, making it official.|近义词: co-sign, endorse, ratify|反义词:  (No direct antonym, consider "reject", "invalidate")|词性: noun, verb

Etymology: From counter- + seal.  "Counter-" signifies "against, opposite, corresponding," reflecting the idea of an additional seal applied in opposition or response to the original. "Seal" comes from Old French "seel" ultimately from Latin "sigillum" meaning "small image, figure, seal."
