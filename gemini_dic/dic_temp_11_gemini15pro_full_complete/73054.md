footmanship:/ˈfʊtmənʃɪp/| n.|步法；仆人的工作；（尤指）男仆的职责；|The butler's footmanship was impeccable, moving silently and efficiently through the grand house.  这位管家的步法无可挑剔，在豪宅中悄无声息、高效地走动。|例句：His footmanship was so quiet, you barely knew he was there. 他的脚步如此轻盈，你几乎感觉不到他的存在。

例句：The old butler's footmanship was a testament to years of dedicated service. 老管家的步法证明了他多年来尽职尽责的服务。|近义词：service, attendance, valeting|反义词:clumsiness, awkwardness|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From footman + -ship.  Footman (1570s) comes from foot (n.) + man (n.).  The suffix -ship denotes "state, condition, quality, skill, office, or art."
