nominative: /ˈnɒmɪnətɪv/ | adj. n. | 主格的，主格 |  Relating to or denoting a case of nouns, pronouns, and adjectives (in some inflected languages) used for the subject of a verb.  A noun or other part of speech in the nominative case. |  The nominative case marks the subject of a verb.  |  Example: In the sentence "She gave him the book," "she" is in the nominative case. 在句子“她给了他这本书”中，“她”是主格。 | Synonyms: subjective | Antonyms: objective | Part of Speech: adjective, noun
