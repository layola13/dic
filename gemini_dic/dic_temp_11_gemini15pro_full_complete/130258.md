non-stationary:/ˌnɒnˈsteɪʃənəri/ (US) /ˌnɒnˈsteɪʃənri/ (UK)| adj.|非平稳的，非静态的|Not remaining constant over time, especially referring to statistical properties.  Changing or varying over time.|The data exhibited a non-stationary pattern, making it difficult to predict future values.|近义词：unstable, dynamic, fluctuating, volatile|反义词: stationary, static, constant|词性:形容词

Etymology:
non- + stationary
non-: prefix meaning "not" (from Latin "non")
stationary:  from Late Latin "stationarius," meaning "standing in a fixed place"
