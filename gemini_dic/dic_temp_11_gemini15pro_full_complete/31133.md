chancer:/ˈtʃɑːnsər/ (UK), /ˈtʃænsər/ (US)| n.|投机分子；碰运气的人；冒险家|Someone who takes risks, especially in business or financial matters, hoping to gain an advantage; someone who exploits situations, often in a slightly dishonest way; someone who tries to achieve something by relying on luck rather than skill or hard work.|He's a bit of a chancer, always looking for a quick way to make money. 他有点投机取巧，总想找捷径赚钱。|Synonyms: gambler, risk-taker, opportunist, speculator |Antonyms: cautious person, careful planner, strategist|noun
