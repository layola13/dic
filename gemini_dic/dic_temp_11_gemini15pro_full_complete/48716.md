deficit:/ˈdefɪsɪt/ | n. |赤字，亏损，不足 |A deficit is the amount by which something, especially a sum of money, is too small. | The government is facing a huge budget deficit. 政府面临巨额预算赤字。| shortfall, deficiency, shortage | surplus, excess | noun

deficits:/ˈdefɪsɪts/ | n. |赤字（deficit的复数形式）, 亏损，不足 | Deficits are the plural form of deficit, referring to multiple instances of something being too small, especially sums of money. |  The company has been running deficits for the past three years.  这家公司过去三年一直在亏损。 | shortfalls, deficiencies, shortages | surpluses, excesses | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin dēficit, third-person singular present active indicative of dēficere “to fail, desert, abandon, give out, run short, be wanting,” from de- “down, away” + facere “to do, make.”
