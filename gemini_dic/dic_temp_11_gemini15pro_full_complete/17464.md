beard-the-lion-in-its-den:/bɪrd ðə ˈlaɪən ɪn ɪts den/| idiom |直捣黄龙；在太岁头上动土；敢于冒险|To confront a powerful or dangerous person on their own territory, showing great courage.  This idiom implies taking a significant risk by challenging someone in a place where they have the most control and power.|例句：He decided to beard the lion in its den and confront his boss directly about the unfair treatment. 他决定直捣黄龙，直接和老板对峙不公平待遇。

例句：She knew it was risky, but she bearded the lion in its den and presented her controversial proposal to the board of directors. 她知道这很冒险，但她还是在太岁头上动土，向董事会提出了她备受争议的提案。|近义词：confront, challenge, take the bull by the horns|反义词：avoid, evade, shy away from|phrase
