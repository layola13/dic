hydragogue:/ˈhaɪdrəɡɒɡ/ (US) /ˈhaɪdrəɡɒɡ/ (UK)| n. |利尿剂，泻药|A medicine that increases the discharge of watery fluids from the body, especially one used as a purgative.|The doctor prescribed a hydragogue to relieve the patient's edema.|近义词:  purgative, cathartic, aperient, diuretic|反义词: antidiuretic|词性: noun
Etymology: From Greek ὕδωρ (hýdōr, “water”) + ἀγωγός (agōgós, “leading, drawing”).
