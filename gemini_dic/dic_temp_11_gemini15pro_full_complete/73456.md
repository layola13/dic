forensic:/fəˈrɛnsɪk/ | adj. | 法医的；法庭的；辩论的 | relating to or denoting the application of scientific methods and techniques to the investigation of crime. |  Forensic evidence was used to convict the murderer. 法医证据被用来给凶手定罪。| synonyms: legal, judicial, medicolegal | antonyms:  non-legal, informal | adjective

forensics:/fəˈrɛnsɪks/ | n. | 法医学；辩论术 | scientific tests or techniques used in connection with the detection of crime. |  He studied forensics in college. 他在大学学习了法医学。 | synonyms:  criminology, crime scene investigation | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *forensis*, meaning "of the forum, of the market place," from *forum* "public place, market."  Originally, forensic referred to legal proceedings conducted in public.  Over time, the meaning shifted to focus on the scientific methods used in legal investigations.
