isopod:/ˈaɪsəpɒd/ (US) /ˈaɪsəpɒd/ (UK)| n.|等足类动物|An isopod is a crustacean that has a flattened, segmented body and seven pairs of legs.  Isopods are found in a wide variety of habitats, including marine, freshwater, and terrestrial environments.|Woodlice are common terrestrial isopods.  潮虫是常见的陆生等足类动物。|Synonyms:  woodlouse (terrestrial), pill bug, sow bug|Antonyms: None (as a taxonomic group)|noun
