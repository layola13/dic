nestle:/ˈnesəl/ (US), /ˈnesl/ (UK)| v.  n.|依偎；舒适地坐；安置；偎依；巢；窝|To settle down comfortably and snugly; to lie close and warm against something.|The child nestled in her mother's arms. 孩子依偎在母亲的怀里.|Synonyms: cuddle, snuggle, curl up|Antonyms: separate, detach|verb

nestle:/ˈnesəl/ (US), /ˈnesl/ (UK)| n.|巢；窝|A snug or cozy place; the place where a bird lays its eggs.|The birds built their nestle in the tree. 鸟儿在树上筑了巢。|Synonyms: nest, haven, retreat|Antonyms: exposure, openness|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *nestelen, frequentative of nestlen  "to build a nest," from Old English nestlian "to make a nest," from nest "nest."
