ephemerae's:/ɪˈfɛmərəz/ |n. |蜉蝣的；短暂的 |Possession form of ephemerae, meaning "belonging to ephemerae" or "of the ephemerae."  It refers to something that belongs to or is characteristic of mayflies, which are known for their short lifespan. |The ephemerae's delicate wings shimmered in the sunlight. 蜉蝣那纤细的翅膀在阳光下闪闪发光。|Near synonyms: mayfly's, short-lived's (though these don't capture the specific insect connection).|Antonyms:  long-lasting's, permanent's|noun (possessive)

ephemera:/ɪˈfɛmərə/ | n. |蜉蝣；短暂的事物 |Things that exist or are used or enjoyed for only a short time.|The museum has a fascinating collection of ephemera from the 1920s, including playbills, posters, and flyers.  这家博物馆收藏了20世纪20年代引人入胜的短暂时效物，包括节目单、海报和传单。|Near synonyms:  transients, ephemerals|Antonyms:  permanents, durables|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek  ἐφήμερος (ephēmeros, “lasting for a day”), from ἐπί (epí, “on, upon”) + ἡμέρα (hēméra, “day”).
