bitterwort:/ˈbɪtərwɜːrt/ | n. | 苦草, 龙胆 | A European perennial herb (Gentiana lutea) with intensely bitter roots, used medicinally and as a flavoring. |  The alpine meadows were dotted with the yellow flowers of bitterwort.  高山草甸上点缀着黄色的苦草花。| synonyms: gentian, yellow gentian | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a plant name, could use "sweet herb" for contrast) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *biterwyrt, from biter "bitter" + wyrt "herb, plant."  The "wort" element is common in names of plants used medicinally, such as St. John's wort.
