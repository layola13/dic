apprehensive:/ˌæprɪˈhɛnsɪv/ | adj. | 忧虑的，担心的 | Feeling or showing anxiety or dread about something that might happen in the future. |  I'm apprehensive about the upcoming exam. 我对即将到来的考试感到忧虑。

apprehensive:/ˌæprɪˈhɛnsɪv/ | adj. | 理解的，领悟的 (obsolete, rarely used) |  Quick to learn or understand. | He was an apprehensive student, quickly grasping the complex concepts.  他是一个理解力很强的学生，能很快掌握复杂的知识。(这个用法比较古老，现在很少使用)

apprehensive:/ˌæprɪˈhɛnsɪv/ | adj. | 知晓的，意识到的 (obsolete, rarely used) | Aware or conscious of something. |  He was apprehensive of the danger he was in. 他意识到自己身处险境。(这个用法比较古老，现在很少使用)


Etymology: Late Middle English: from late Latin apprehensivus, from apprehendere ‘seize, grasp’. The original sense was ‘quick to grasp or understand’, later ‘anticipating something, especially with anxiety’.

近义词: anxious, worried, concerned, uneasy, nervous, fearful, frightened, afraid
反义词: confident, calm, relaxed, composed, assured

词性: adjective
