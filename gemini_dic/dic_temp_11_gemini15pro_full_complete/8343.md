anti-platonism:/ˌæntiˈpleɪtənɪzəm/ | n. | 反柏拉图主义 |  A philosophical position that rejects or opposes the central tenets of Platonism, particularly the Theory of Forms. It asserts that abstract objects like numbers, beauty, and justice do not have an independent, objective existence. |  Anti-platonism has gained traction in recent philosophical discussions. 反柏拉图主义在最近的哲学讨论中获得了关注. |  nominalism, materialism |  Platonism, idealism | noun
