kinders:/ˈkɪndərz/| n.|孩子们|An informal, often affectionate term for children, especially young ones. Primarily used in American English, particularly in the context of kindergarten.  It's derived from the German word "Kinder" meaning "children".  It's not grammatically correct as a plural form in standard English.|The kinders were excited about the field trip. 孩子们对这次实地考察感到很兴奋。|Synonyms: children, kids, youngsters, tots|Antonyms: adults, grown-ups|noun
