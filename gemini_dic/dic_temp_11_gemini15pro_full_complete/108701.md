lean:/liːn/
verb
倾斜，倚靠
To incline or bend from a vertical position.
The tower leaned precariously in the wind.  塔在风中摇摇欲坠地倾斜。
He leaned against the wall, exhausted. 他精疲力尽地靠在墙上。
incline, bend, tilt
stand, straighten

verb
依赖，依靠
To depend on someone or something for support.
She leaned on her friends for emotional support during the difficult time. 在困难时期，她依靠朋友们获得情感支持。
depend, rely
independent

adjective
瘦的，无脂肪的
Thin, not fat.
He had a lean and muscular physique. 他身材精瘦，肌肉发达。
thin, slim, slender
fat, obese, plump

adjective
简洁的，高效的
Containing little or no fat; efficient and without waste.
The company adopted lean manufacturing principles to improve productivity. 公司采用精益生产原则来提高生产力。
efficient, streamlined
wasteful, inefficient

noun
瘦肉
Meat without much fat.
The butcher trimmed the lean from the pork. 屠夫把猪肉上的瘦肉切下来。
meat (without fat)
fat


Etymology:
From Old English *hlēonian, from Proto-Germanic *hlinōną (“to lean, bend”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ley- (“to bend, lean”). Cognate with Dutch leunen (“to lean”), German lehnen (“to lean”).
