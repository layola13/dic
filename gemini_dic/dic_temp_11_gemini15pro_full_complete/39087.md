confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/ (US), /kənˈfaʊnd/ (UK)| vt.|使困惑；使惊讶；挫败；混淆|To cause surprise or confusion in (someone), especially by acting against their expectations.|Her recovery confounded the doctors. 她的康复使医生们感到困惑。|Synonyms: amaze, astonish, baffle, bewilder, confuse, dumbfound, nonplus, perplex, puzzle|Antonyms: clarify, enlighten, explain|verb

confound:/ˈkɒnfaʊndz/ (UK)| n. (archaic)| 诅咒|An expression of annoyance or anger.|A confounded nuisance!  真是个讨厌的家伙！|Synonyms: curse, damnation|Antonyms: blessing|noun
