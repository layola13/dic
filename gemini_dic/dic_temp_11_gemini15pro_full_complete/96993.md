infantilizing: /ˈɪnfəntɪˌlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 幼稚化，婴儿化 | To treat someone as if they were a child, often by not allowing them to make decisions or by talking to them in a condescending way. | He resented being infantilized by his parents even though he was in his twenties. 他憎恨父母即使在他二十多岁时还把他当孩子看待。

She felt infantilized when her boss explained simple tasks to her as if she were a child. 当她的老板像对待孩子一样向她解释简单的任务时，她感到自己被幼稚化了。 | 近义词: patronizing, condescending, demeaning | 反义词: empowering, respecting, validating | 词性: 动词

词源: From infant (婴儿) + -ize (动词后缀，表示“使成为…”) + -ing (现在分词后缀)
