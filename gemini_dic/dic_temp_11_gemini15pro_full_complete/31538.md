chartering:/ˈtʃɑːrtərɪŋ/
n.|租赁；包租；特许|The act of hiring or renting a vehicle, especially an aircraft or ship, for a particular purpose.  The process of granting a charter, which is a document that establishes an organization or company and defines its rights and privileges.|
例句：The chartering of private jets has become increasingly popular. 包租私人飞机越来越流行。

例句：The company is specializing in the chartering of cargo ships. 这家公司专门从事货船的租赁业务。
近义词：hiring, renting, leasing, commissioning
反义词：selling, purchasing
词性: noun (gerund or present participle)


chartering:/ˈtʃɑːrtərɪŋ/
v.|租赁；包租；特许|To hire or rent a vehicle, especially an aircraft or ship, for a particular purpose. To grant a charter, which is a document that establishes an organization or company and defines its rights and privileges.|
例句：They chartered a bus to take them to the concert. 他们租了一辆公共汽车去听音乐会。

例句：The king chartered the company to trade in the East Indies. 国王特许该公司在东印度群岛进行贸易。
近义词：hire, rent, lease, commission
反义词：sell, purchase
词性: verb (present participle)


ETYMOLOGY->
From "charter" + "-ing."  "Charter" comes from Old French "chartre" (written document), from Latin "charta" (papyrus leaf, document), from Greek "khartes" (papyrus leaf).
