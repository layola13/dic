damper:/ˈdæmpər (US), ˈdæmpə(r) (UK)| n. |减震器，阻尼器；抑制因素；挡板；闷音器；(澳大利亚)一种面包|A device that reduces vibrations or oscillations, such as in a piano or a car's suspension; something that subdues or restrains; a metal plate that regulates the draft in a stove or fireplace; a mute for a musical instrument; (in Australia) a type of bread.|The damper on the piano pedal softens the sound. 钢琴踏板上的制音器使声音柔和。|Synonyms: muffler, mute, suppressor, control, restraint|Antonyms: amplifier, stimulator|noun

damper:/ˈdæmpər (US), ˈdæmpə(r) (UK)| vt. |抑制，减弱；使潮湿|To reduce the amplitude or intensity of something; to make damp or moist.|The bad news put a damper on the celebration.  这个坏消息给庆祝活动泼了冷水。|Synonyms: subdue, restrain, curb, dampen|Antonyms: intensify, enhance, stimulate|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "damp," meaning to reduce the amplitude or intensity of something, ultimately from Middle Low German "dampen."  The noun sense developed from the device used to damp vibrations.
