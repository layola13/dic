hexametrize:/ˈheksəˌmɪtraɪz/ | v. | 使成六音步, 以六音步写 | To write or compose something in hexameter (a line of verse consisting of six dactyls or spondees, especially in classical Greek and Latin poetry). | He hexametrized the epic poem, giving it a classical feel.  他将史诗改写成六音步诗，赋予它古典气息。|  Homer hexametrized the Iliad and the Odyssey. 荷马用六音步写了《伊利亚特》和《奥德赛》。 | versify, write in verse |  | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *hexametros* (consisting of six measures) < *hexa-* (six) + *metron* (measure) + *-ize* (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make into," "to cause to be," etc.)
