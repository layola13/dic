oblivions:/əˈblɪvɪənz/| n. |遗忘；湮没；被遗忘的状态|The state of being completely forgotten, especially for a long time; the state of being unaware or unconscious of what is happening.|  His work was consigned to the oblivions of history. 他的作品被历史所遗忘。|  She sank into a blissful oblivion. 她陷入了幸福的遗忘之中。|近义词：forgetfulness, amnesia, unconsciousness|反义词：remembrance, memory, awareness|词性:名词

词源: From Late Latin *oblīviōn-, from Latin oblīviō (“forgetfulness”), from oblīvīscor (“I forget”).
