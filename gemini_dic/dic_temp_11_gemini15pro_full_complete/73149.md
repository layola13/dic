forager:/ˈfɔːrɪdʒər/ (US), /ˈfɒrɪdʒə(r)/ (UK)| n. |觅食者，搜寻者|A person or animal that searches widely for food or provisions.|例句：The forager returned to the camp with a basket full of berries. 觅食者带着满满一篮子的浆果回到了营地。

例句：Squirrels are natural foragers, constantly searching for nuts and seeds. 松鼠是天生的觅食者，不断地寻找坚果和种子。

|近义词：hunter, gatherer, searcher, scrounger|反义词：provider, supplier|词性：名词


forage:/ˈfɔːrɪdʒ/ (US), /ˈfɒrɪdʒ/ (UK)| v. n. |觅食，搜寻；饲料| (v.) To search widely for food or provisions; (n.) Food for animals, especially farm animals, or the act of searching for it.|例句：The pigs were foraging in the woods for acorns. 猪在树林里觅食橡子。

例句：We spent the afternoon foraging for mushrooms. 我们下午都在寻找蘑菇。

|近义词：(v.) search, hunt, rummage, scavenge; (n.) fodder, feed|反义词：(v.) provide, supply|词性：动词，名词
