canopies:/ˈkænəpiz/ | n. | 天篷；遮篷；树冠层 |A protective covering of canvas or other material supported by a frame and used over a bed, throne, entrance, etc., or as a shelter from the sun or rain.  A layer of something that spreads over an area like a roof. | The market stalls were covered with brightly colored canopies.  市场摊位上覆盖着色彩鲜艳的遮阳篷。

The rainforest canopies teem with life. 雨林的树冠层充满了生机。 | 近义词: awnings, coverings, shelters, roof, ceiling | 反义词: floor, base, ground | 词性: noun


canopy:/ˈkænəpi/ | v. |  覆盖；遮蔽 |To cover or spread over something like a canopy. | Lush foliage canopied the path. 茂盛的树叶遮蔽了小路。

Clouds canopied the sky. 云层遮蔽了天空。 | 近义词: cover, overspread, shade, shelter | 反义词: expose, uncover | 词性: verb


Etymology: From Old French *canopie "mosquito net," from Medieval Latin canopeum "mosquito net, canopy over a bed," from Greek kōnōpeion "bed with mosquito netting," from kōnōps "mosquito, gnat."
