cardiograph: /ˈkɑːrdioʊɡræf/ | n. | 心电图仪；心动描记器 | An instrument that graphically records the movements of the heart. | The doctor used a cardiograph to monitor the patient's heart rate. 医生用心电图仪监测病人的心率。 | electrocardiograph, ECG machine |  | noun
Etymology:  From cardio- (relating to the heart) + -graph (an instrument for recording).  "Cardio-" comes from Greek kardia "heart." "-graph" comes from Greek -graphos "writing."
