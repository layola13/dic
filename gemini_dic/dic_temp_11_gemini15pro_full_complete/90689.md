humbugging:/hʌmˈbʌɡɪŋ/
| n. |欺骗；哄骗；虚伪的行为|The act of deceiving or misleading someone, often in a playful or harmless way.  It can also refer to the act of pretending to be something one is not.|
|He accused the company of humbugging consumers with false advertising. 他指责该公司用虚假广告欺骗消费者。|
|deception, trickery, fraud, hoax|
|honesty, sincerity, truthfulness|
|noun|


humbug:/ˈhʌmbʌɡ/
| n. |骗子；欺骗；废话|A person who deceives or tricks others.  Also refers to something that is intended to deceive; a hoax or sham.  Can also be used as an exclamation of disbelief or annoyance.|
|He's a humbug who pretends to be a psychic. 他是个骗子，假装自己是通灵者。|
|fraud, sham, hoax, imposter, charlatan|
|truth, reality, genuineness|
|noun|


humbug:/ˈhʌmbʌɡ/
| vt. |欺骗；哄骗|To deceive, trick, or hoax someone.|
|They humbugged him out of his life savings. 他们骗走了他一生的积蓄。|
|deceive, trick, mislead, dupe, defraud|
|be honest, tell the truth|
|verb|


humbug:/ˈhʌmbʌɡ/
| interj. |胡说！|Used to express disbelief or annoyance.|
|"Humbug!" he exclaimed, throwing the newspaper down. “胡说！”他叫道，把报纸扔了下去。|
|nonsense, rubbish, balderdash|
|truth, fact|
|interjection|
