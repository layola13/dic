harp:/hɑːrp/ | n. v. |竖琴；喋喋不休地说 |A large, stringed musical instrument played by plucking the strings with the fingers. To talk or write persistently and tediously on a particular topic. |例句：She played a beautiful melody on the harp. 她用竖琴演奏了一曲优美的旋律。|例句：He kept harping on about the unfairness of it all. 他不停地抱怨这一切的不公平。|近义词：(n.) lyre, lute; (v.) dwell on, nag |反义词: (v.) drop, ignore |词性: 名词，动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hearpe, from Proto-Germanic *harpo, from Proto-Indo-European *kerp- (“to pluck, gather”). Cognate with Old Norse harpa, Old High German harpha, and Latin carpere.
