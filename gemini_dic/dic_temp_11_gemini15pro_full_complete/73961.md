fortifications:/ˌfɔːrtɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/
|n.|防御工事，防御设施|Defensive walls, ditches, and other structures designed to protect a place from attack.|The city's fortifications held against the siege. 这座城市的防御工事抵挡住了围攻。|Synonyms: defenses, ramparts, bulwarks,  |Antonyms: vulnerabilities, weaknesses|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: fortificacioun, from Old French fortification, from Late Latin fortification-, fortificatio, from Latin fortificare ‘make strong’, from fortis ‘strong’.
