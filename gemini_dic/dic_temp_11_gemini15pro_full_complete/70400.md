fig:/fɪɡ/|n.|无花果；无花果树；少量；不值钱的东西|A small, soft, sweet fruit with many tiny seeds, typically purple or green, or the tree that produces this fruit.  Also used informally to mean a small amount or something of little value.|例句：She doesn't care a fig about what they think. 她一点也不在乎他们怎么想。|例句：I haven't got a fig to wear to the party. 我没有一件像样的衣服可以穿去参加派对。|近义词：bit, scrap, jot, iota|反义词：lots, abundance|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fige*, from Old English *fic*, from Latin *ficus*, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeigʷ-*. Cognate with Dutch *vijg*, German *Feige*, French *figue*, Spanish *higo*, Italian *fico*.
