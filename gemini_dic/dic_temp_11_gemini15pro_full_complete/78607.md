gibbets:/ˈdʒɪbɪts/| n.|绞刑架; 耻辱柱|A structure for hanging criminals, typically consisting of a wooden post with a projecting arm or crossbar.|The bodies of the executed pirates were left hanging from the gibbets as a warning to others.|Synonyms: gallows, scaffold|Antonyms: freedom, liberation|noun


gibbet:/ˈdʒɪbɪt/| vt.|绞死; 吊死; 暴露…于公众的嘲笑|To hang (someone) on a gibbet; to expose to public scorn or ridicule.|They gibbeted the traitor in the town square.|Synonyms: hang, execute, pillory|Antonyms: pardon, exonerate|verb
