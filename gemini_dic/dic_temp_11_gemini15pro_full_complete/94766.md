improvisors:/ˈɪmprəvaɪzərz/ | n. | 即兴表演者；即兴创作的人 | People who create and perform something spontaneously, without preparation. | The improvisors were quick-witted and entertaining. 这些即兴表演者机智幽默，很有娱乐性。|  The improvisors captivated the audience with their spontaneous performance.  这些即兴表演者以其自发的表演吸引了观众。 | synonyms: ad-libbers, extemporizers, improvisers | antonyms: planners, preparers | noun

improvise:/ˈɪmprəvaɪz/ | v. | 即兴创作；临时凑合 | To create and perform (music, drama, or verse) spontaneously or without preparation. |  We improvised a meal from the leftovers in the fridge. 我们用冰箱里的剩菜做了一顿饭。|  He had to improvise a speech at the last minute. 他不得不在最后一刻即兴演讲。 | synonyms: ad-lib, extemporize, wing it | antonyms: plan, prepare, rehearse | verb
