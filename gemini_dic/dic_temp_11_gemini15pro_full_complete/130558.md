nonacquisitiveness:/ˌnɒnˌækwɪˈzɪtɪvnəs/
n.
不贪婪，无占有欲
Lack of desire to acquire possessions or gain wealth; contentment with what one has.
Her nonacquisitiveness was remarkable in a society obsessed with material possessions.  她在一个痴迷于物质财富的社会里，不贪婪的品质非常引人注目。
Synonyms:  unselfishness, detachment, generosity, contentment, asceticism
Antonyms:  acquisitiveness, greed, avarice, materialism, covetousness
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + acquisitive + -ness.  Non- is a prefix meaning "not." Acquisitive comes from the Latin *acquisitīvus*, meaning "inclined to acquire," from *acquīsītus*, the past participle of *acquīrere*, "to acquire."  The suffix -ness forms nouns from adjectives.
