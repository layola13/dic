mucus:/ˈmjuːkəs/ (US), /ˈmjuːkəs/ (UK)| n. |粘液；黏液|A slimy substance, typically secreted by mucous membranes, that lubricates or protects body surfaces.|The cold caused him to have a lot of mucus in his throat. 感冒使他的喉咙里有很多粘液。|Synonyms: phlegm, slime, snot|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical substance like mucus)|noun

mucous:/ˈmjuːkəs/ (US), /ˈmjuːkəs/ (UK)| adj. |粘性的；黏液的|Relating to, resembling, or consisting of mucus.|The mucous membranes of the nose produce mucus. 鼻子的粘膜会产生粘液。|Synonyms: slimy, viscous, sticky|Antonyms: dry|adjective
