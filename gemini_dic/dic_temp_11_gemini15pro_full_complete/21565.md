bluesier:/ˈbluːziər/| adj.|更忧郁的，更悲伤的|Comparative form of bluesy: more melancholy and mournful; more tinged with or evocative of the blues (a style of music).|The singer's voice was bluesier than usual, reflecting his recent heartbreak. 歌手的声音比平时更忧郁，反映了他最近的心碎。

The music had a bluesier feel in the second set. 第二组的音乐感觉更忧郁了。|近义词：sadder, more melancholic, more mournful|反义词：happier, more cheerful|词性:形容词

Etymology: From "bluesy" + "-er" (comparative suffix). "Bluesy" originates from "blues," referring to a state of melancholy or depression, and later to the blues music genre characterized by its mournful melodies and themes.
