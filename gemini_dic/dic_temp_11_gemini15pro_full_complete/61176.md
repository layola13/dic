eluctation:/ˌiːlʌkˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 不情愿，勉强 | The eluctation in her voice was evident as she agreed to the proposal. |  Her agreement to the plan was marked by considerable eluctation. | 近义词：reluctance, hesitation, unwillingness | 反义词：eagerness, willingness, alacrity | 词性: 名词

词源: From Latin *eluctatio*, from eluctari (“to struggle out, to extricate oneself”), from e- (“out of”) + luctari (“to wrestle, struggle”).
