molehills:/ˈmoʊlˌhɪlz/ | n. |鼹鼠丘，小土堆；小事，琐事|A small mound of earth thrown up by a mole burrowing underground. Also used figuratively to refer to minor problems or obstacles.|
例句：
The field was covered in molehills.  田野上布满了鼹鼠丘。

Don't make a mountain out of a molehill.  别小题大做。


近义词：mounds, bumps, trifles, trivialities|
反义词：mountains, major issues|
词性: noun


词源：
From Middle English *molhil, from Old English *moldhyll, from mold (“earth, soil”) + hyll (“hill”).  The word is a compound of "mole" and "hill".
