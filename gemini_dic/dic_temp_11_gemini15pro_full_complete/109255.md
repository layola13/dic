leman:/ˈlɛmən/| n.|情人，爱人；(古)甜心，宝贝|A sweetheart or lover, especially a secret or illicit one.  The word carries a connotation of romance and passion, often with a hint of secrecy or forbidden love.|例句：He whispered sweet nothings to his leman. 他对着他的情人低声说着甜言蜜语。

例句：In medieval romances, the knight often had a secret leman. 在中世纪的浪漫故事中，骑士通常有一个秘密情人。|近义词：lover, sweetheart, paramour, mistress|反义词:enemy, foe|词性:noun

词源:
From Middle English *leman, from Old English lēofman (“lover, dear one, man”), from lēof (“dear, beloved”) + man (“man, person”). Cognate with German Liebmann. The original meaning included both men and women, but in later use it primarily referred to a woman, often a mistress.
