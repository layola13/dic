accost: /əˈkɒst/ (UK), /əˈkɔːst/ (US) | vt. | 搭讪；上前攀谈；(唐突或具侵略性地) 走近；质问 | To approach and speak to someone boldly or aggressively, often in a way that seems rude or unwelcome. |  A stranger accosted me on the street and asked for money. 一个陌生人在街上搭讪我要钱。

The beggar accosted passersby for spare change. 乞丐上前向路人乞讨零钱。 | 近义词: approach, confront, address, hail | 反义词: avoid, shun, evade | 词性: 动词
