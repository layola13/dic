overgeniality:/ˌoʊvərˌdʒiːniˈælɪti/ | n. | 过分热情，过分友好 | Excessive geniality; being too friendly or amiable, often to the point of insincerity or inappropriateness. | He greeted me with such overgeniality that I suspected he wanted something. 他如此热情地跟我打招呼，让我怀疑他有所求。 |  Near Synonyms:  effusiveness, exuberance, excessive friendliness | Near Antonyms: coldness, aloofness, unfriendliness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + geniality.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much." "Geniality" comes from the Latin "genialis," meaning "festive, cheerful, pertaining to marriage," ultimately from "genius," meaning "guardian deity or spirit."
