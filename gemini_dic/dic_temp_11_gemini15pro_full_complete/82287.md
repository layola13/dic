groundwater:/ˈɡraʊndˌwɔːtər/ (US) /ˈɡraʊndˌwɔːtə(r)/ (UK)| n. |地下水|Water held underground in the soil or in pores and crevices in rock.|The groundwater level has dropped significantly due to the drought. 由于干旱，地下水位显著下降。|近义词：subsurface water, underground water|反义词: surface water|noun


groundwater:/ˈɡraʊndˌwɔːtər/ (US) /ˈɡraʊndˌwɔːtə(r)/ (UK)| n. |地下水|Water held underground in the soil or in pores and crevices in rock.|Farmers rely heavily on groundwater for irrigation. 农民们严重依赖地下水进行灌溉。|近义词：subsurface water, underground water|反义词: surface water|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From ground + water.  "Ground" comes from Old English "grund," meaning "ground, soil, bottom." "Water" comes from Old English "wæter," from Proto-Germanic "*watar,"  from Proto-Indo-European "*wod-or," meaning "water."
