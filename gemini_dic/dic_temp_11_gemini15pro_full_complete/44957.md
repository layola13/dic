cryptozoon:/ˈkrɪptoʊˌzoʊɒn/ | n. |隐生动物；化石藻类|An organism of uncertain affinity, whose fossil remains suggest an animal but which may be plant, for example, a stromatolite.  A fossil evidence of primitive life, especially such as algae. |Example 1: The researchers discovered a large cryptozoon in the ancient rock formation. 研究人员在古老的岩层中发现了一个大型隐生动物。

Example 2:  Cryptozoa are fascinating because they offer clues about early life on Earth. 隐生动物很吸引人，因为它们提供了关于地球早期生命的线索。|Synonym: fossil algae, stromatolite |Antonym:  N/A (No direct antonym for a fossilized organism) | Noun
