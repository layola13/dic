axiomatically:/ˌæksiəˈmætɪkli/ | adv. | 公理地；不言自明地 | In a way that relates to or is based on axioms; self-evidently. |  It is axiomatically true that governments must protect their citizens. 政府必须保护其公民，这本身是不言而喻的。|  It's axiomatically accepted that good health requires exercise and a balanced diet.  拥有良好的健康需要锻炼和均衡的饮食，这是公认的。 | synonym: self-evidently, obviously, undoubtedly, indisputably | antonym: questionably, dubiously | 词性: 副词

axiomatic:/ˌæksiəˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 公理的；不言自明的 | Relating to or containing axioms; self-evident; taken as a given. | It is axiomatic that good health requires exercise. 良好的健康需要锻炼，这是不言而喻的。| The principle of free speech is axiomatic in a democracy. 言论自由的原则是民主制度中的公理。| synonym: self-evident, obvious, a priori | antonym: questionable, debatable | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *axiomaticus*, from Ancient Greek ἀξιωματικός (axiōmatikós, “worthy, of axioms”), from ἀξίωμα (axíōma, “that which is thought worthy or fit, that which commends itself as evident, axiom”).
