haemocytometer:/ˌhiːməsaɪˈtɒmɪtər/ (US) /ˌhiːməsaɪˈtɑːmɪtər/ (UK)| n. |血细胞计数器|An instrument for visual counting of cells, especially blood cells, per unit volume.  It consists of a thick glass microscope slide with a rectangular indentation that creates a chamber of precise volume.  This chamber is etched with a grid of perpendicular lines.|The lab technician used a haemocytometer to count the white blood cells in the patient's blood sample.|Synonyms:  hemacytometer, counting chamber|Antonyms: None|Noun
