balustraded:/ˌbæləˈstreɪdɪd/| adj. |有栏杆的，有围栏的 |Having a balustrade or balustrades.  A balustrade is a railing supported by balusters, especially an ornamental parapet on a balcony, bridge, or terrace.| The balustraded balcony offered a stunning view of the city.  有栏杆的阳台可以欣赏到令人惊叹的城市景观。|Near Synonyms: railed, fenced, barricaded |Antonyms: open, unenclosed| adjective

balustrade:/ˌbæləˈstreɪd/| n. |栏杆，围栏 |A railing supported by balusters, especially an ornamental parapet on a balcony, bridge, or terrace.|  We leaned against the balustrade and watched the sunset. 我们靠在栏杆上看日落.|Near Synonyms: railing, banister, parapet |Antonyms: none (lack of a specific antonym)| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *balaustrata*, from *balaustro* "baluster," likely from Late Latin *balaustium* "flower of the wild pomegranate," from Greek *balaustion*.  The shape of the baluster resembled the flower.
