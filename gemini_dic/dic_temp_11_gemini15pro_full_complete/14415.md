autotropism: /ɔːˈtɒtrəˌpɪzəm/ (UK) /ɔːˈtɑːtrəˌpɪzəm/ (US)
noun
向性运动; 自养性
The involuntary orientation of an organism or other entity in response to a stimulus, specifically the directional growth response of a plant to an external stimulus.
Plants exhibit autotropism by growing towards light. 植物通过朝光生长表现出自养性。
The sunflower's movement is an example of autotropism. 向日葵的运动是向性运动的一个例子。
近义词: tropism, phototropism (specifically for light), gravitropism (specifically for gravity)
反义词: (No direct antonym, but one could consider random growth or movement)
词性: n.

Etymology:
From Greek autos "self" + tropos "a turn" + -ism.
