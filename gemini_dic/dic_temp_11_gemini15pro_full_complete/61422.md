emboldening:/ɪmˈboʊldənɪŋ/ | adj. | 使大胆的，使有勇气的 | Making someone feel more confident and daring; encouraging someone to be brave and assertive. |  The coach's words had an emboldening effect on the team. 教练的话对球队起到了鼓舞士气的作用。

The success of the first protest emboldened the activists to organize another. 第一次抗议的成功鼓舞了活动家们组织另一次抗议。| 近义词: encouraging, inspiring, empowering | 反义词: discouraging, intimidating, weakening | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From embolden + -ing. Embolden comes from em- (variant of en-, meaning "to cause to be in" or "to put into") + bold.  The suffix -ing transforms the verb "embolden" into a present participle, which can function as an adjective.
