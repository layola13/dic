cold-turkeying: /kəʊld ˈtɜːkiɪŋ/ | n. |  突然戒断，尤指突然戒除毒瘾或坏习惯| The act of abruptly stopping a habit, such as smoking or drug use, without tapering off or using any aids or substitutes. | He tried cold-turkeying his caffeine addiction, but the headaches were unbearable. 他尝试突然戒掉咖啡因瘾，但头痛难忍。| He quit smoking cold turkey. 他突然戒掉了烟瘾。 |  近义词: abrupt cessation, withdrawal | 反义词: gradual reduction, tapering off | 词性: 名词 (derived from the adjective "cold turkey")


cold turkey: /kəʊld ˈtɜːki/ | adj. adv. n. | 突然的；突然戒断的；突然戒断 | Abrupt and complete; suddenly and without preparation or tapering off; the act of abruptly stopping a habit.  | He went cold turkey off heroin. 他突然戒掉了海洛因。|  The withdrawal symptoms were intense after he quit cold turkey.  在他突然戒断后，戒断症状非常强烈。 | Going cold turkey can be difficult. 突然戒断很困难。 | 近义词: abrupt, sudden, immediate | 反义词: gradual, tapered | 词性: 形容词, 副词, 名词
