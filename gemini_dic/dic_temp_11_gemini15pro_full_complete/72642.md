flysch:/ˈflɪʃ/| n. |复理石；飞施；浊积岩|A sedimentary rock formed from alternating layers of sandstone and shale, typically deposited in a deep marine environment during periods of tectonic activity.|The geologists studied the flysch formations in the Alps. 地质学家研究了阿尔卑斯山的复理石地层。|Synonyms: turbidite, sedimentary rock|Antonyms: igneous rock|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Swiss German *flysch, from *flisschen "to flow," related to German fliessen.  The term was originally used in the Alps to describe rapidly eroding, unstable slopes.
