invadable:/ɪnˈveɪdəbl/| adj.|可入侵的，易受攻击的|Susceptible to invasion or attack; capable of being invaded.|例句：The country's weak defenses made it invadable. 这个国家薄弱的防御使其易受入侵。

例句：The small island nation, with its limited military, was considered invadable.  这个军事力量有限的小岛国被认为容易被入侵。|近义词：vulnerable, susceptible, accessible|反义词：invincible, impregnable, secure|词性:形容词
词源: From "invade" + "-able".  "Invade" comes from Latin "invadere" (to go into, enter, attack), from "in-" (in, into) + "vadere" (to go). "-able" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "capable of being," from Old French "-able" and Latin "-abilis."
