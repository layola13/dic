accustomably:/əˈkʌstəməbli/ | adv. | 习惯地，通常地 | In a customary manner; habitually. | He accustomably rises early in the morning. 他习惯早起。 | She accustomably drinks tea in the afternoon. 她习惯下午喝茶。 | 近义词: usually, habitually, typically, customarily, ordinarily | 反义词: unusually, rarely, seldom | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From *accustomed* + *-ly*.  *Accustomed* comes from Old French *acoustumé* (past participle of *acoustumer*), from Vulgar Latin *accostumare*, from Latin *consuescere* "to accustom," from *con-* "together" + *suescere* "to become accustomed."
