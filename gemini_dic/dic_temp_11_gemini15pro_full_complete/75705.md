full-bloodedness:/ˌfʊlˈblʌdɪdnɪs/ | n. | 纯血统; 血统纯正;  热情奔放 | The state or quality of being full-blooded, especially of being of unmixed ancestry. It can also refer to a passionate and vigorous nature. |  His full-bloodedness was evident in his fiery speeches and unwavering dedication. 他的热情奔放体现在他慷慨激昂的演讲和坚定不移的奉献精神中。 | Synonyms: pure-bloodedness, thoroughbred, passion, zeal, fervor | Antonyms: mixed-blood, half-blooded, apathy, indifference | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "full-blooded" + "-ness". "Full-blooded" means having or showing strong emotions and a vigorous nature, or of unmixed ancestry.  "-ness" is a suffix used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state, quality, or condition.
