free: /friː/
adjective|自由的，免费的|Not under the control or in the power of another; costing nothing.|The birds were set free. 鸟儿被放飞了。|independent, liberated, gratis, complimentary|restricted, confined, captive, costly
adjective|空闲的，无事的|Not occupied or in use.|Are you free this evening? 你今晚有空吗？|available, unoccupied, spare|busy, occupied, engaged
verb|释放，使自由|Release from captivity or confinement.|They freed the prisoners. 他们释放了囚犯。|release, liberate, emancipate|confine, imprison, restrict
adverb|免费地|Without cost or payment.|Children under five travel free. 五岁以下儿童免费乘车。|gratis, for nothing|expensively
noun|自由，自主|The state of not being imprisoned or enslaved.|We have the freedom to choose. 我们有选择的自由。|liberty, independence, autonomy|restriction, confinement, captivity


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *frēo (attested in compounds only), from Proto-Germanic *frijaz (“free”), from Proto-Indo-European *priH-yos (“dear; beloved; own; free”). Cognate with Old Frisian frī (“free”), Old Saxon frī (“free”), Old High German frī (“free”), Old Norse frīr (“free”), Gothic 𐍆𐍂𐌴𐌹𐍃 (freis, “free”), Latin prīvus (“one's own, private”), Sanskrit प्रिय (priya, “own, dear, beloved”).
