backwards-and-forwards:/ˈbækwərdz ənd ˈfɔːrwərdz/
adverb|来回地，反复地|Moving first in one direction and then in the opposite direction repeatedly.|She rocked backwards and forwards in her chair. 她坐在椅子上来回摇晃。| back and forth, to and fro| stationary, still
词性:副词

backwards-and-forwards:/ˈbækwərdz ənd ˈfɔːrwərdz/
adjective|来回的，反复的|Characterized by repeated movement in opposite directions.|The backwards-and-forwards motion of the swing made her dizzy. 秋千来回摆动让她头晕。|reciprocating, oscillating|  stationary, still
词性:形容词


词源:  This phrase is a compound of two adverbs: "backwards" and "forwards". "Backwards" comes from the Old English "bacweardes", meaning "towards the back". "Forwards" comes from the Old English "forweardes", meaning "towards the front".  The combination emphasizes repeated movement in opposite directions.
