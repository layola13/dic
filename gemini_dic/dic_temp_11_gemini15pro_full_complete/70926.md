firebrand:/ˈfaɪərˌbrænd/| n.|煽动者，激进分子，燃烧的木块|A person who is very passionate and determined about their beliefs, especially political beliefs, and who often causes trouble or violence.  Also, a piece of wood or other material that is burning and can be used to start a fire. |例句：She was a firebrand in her youth, always protesting against injustice. 她年轻时是一个激进分子，总是抗议不公正。

例句：The arsonist used a firebrand to ignite the building. 纵火犯用燃烧的木块点燃了大楼。|近义词：agitator, radical, incendiary, troublemaker, hothead |反义词:moderate, pacifist, conformist|词性:noun

firebrand:/ˈfaɪərˌbrænd/| adj.|煽动性的，激进的|Used to describe someone who is very passionate and determined about their beliefs, and who often causes trouble.|例句：His firebrand rhetoric roused the crowd. 他煽动性的言辞激怒了人群。

例句：The firebrand preacher was known for his controversial sermons.  这位煽动性的传教士以其有争议的布道而闻名。|近义词：inflammatory, provocative, incendiary |反义词:conciliatory, appeasing, moderate|词性:adjective
