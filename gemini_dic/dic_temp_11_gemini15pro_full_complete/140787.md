omphalos: /ˈɒmfələs/ (UK) /ˈɑːmfəˌloʊs/ (US) | n. | 中心点，肚脐，世界中心 |  A central point, especially one considered as the center of the world or a very important place.  It derives from the Greek word "ὀμφαλός" (omphalos), literally meaning "navel". In ancient Greece, the omphalos at Delphi was considered the center of the world. | The ancient Greeks believed Delphi was the omphalos of the world. 古希腊人认为德尔斐是世界的中心。| synonyms: hub, center, nucleus, focal point | antonyms: periphery, edge, margin | noun

omphalos: /ˈɒmfələs/ (UK) /ˈɑːmfəˌloʊs/ (US) | adj. | 中央的，中心的 | Situated at or pertaining to the center. |  The omphalos position of the temple signified its importance.  寺庙的中心位置表明了它的重要性。| synonyms: central, middle | antonyms: peripheral, outer | adjective
