co-defendant:/ˌkoʊdɪˈfɛndənt/| n. |共同被告|A person or entity charged with the same crime as another person in the same legal case.|例句：The co-defendant pleaded guilty to a lesser charge. 共同被告对较轻的罪名认罪。|例句：She and her co-defendant will be tried together. 她和她的共同被告将一起受审。|近义词：fellow defendant, joint defendant|反义词:plaintiff, complainant|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From co- + defendant.  Co- is a prefix meaning "together with," "jointly," or "mutually."  Defendant comes from Old French defendaunt, present participle of defendre "to defend," ultimately from Latin defendere "to ward off, protect."
