gnarlier: /ˈnɑːrliər/ (US), /ˈnɑːlɪə(r)/ (UK)
| adj. | 更崎岖的，更粗糙的，更复杂的 | More gnarled, twisted, or complex; more difficult or challenging. | The trail became gnarlier as we ascended the mountain.  这条小路随着我们上山变得更加崎岖。 | Synonyms: knottier, rougher, tougher, more challenging | Antonyms: smoother, easier, simpler | adjective

gnarlier: /ˈnɑːrliər/ (US), /ˈnɑːlɪə(r)/ (UK)
| adj. | 更粗犷的，更狂野的（尤指冲浪、滑板等极限运动） | More rugged, wild, or extreme (especially in surfing, skateboarding, and other extreme sports). | The waves were gnarlier today than they were yesterday.  今天的海浪比昨天更狂野。 | Synonyms:  heavier, wilder, more extreme | Antonyms:  calmer, milder, tamer | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "gnarly" + "-er" (comparative suffix). "Gnarly" originated in the 1970s surfing slang, from "gnarled" meaning "knotted, twisted."
