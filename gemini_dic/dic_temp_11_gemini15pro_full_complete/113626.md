machiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/ |adj. n.| 马基雅维利主义的；权谋主义的；不择手段的 | Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  |例句：His machiavellian tactics helped him climb the corporate ladder quickly. 他的权谋手段帮助他很快地爬上了公司的阶梯。

例句：She was a machiavellian businesswoman, always putting her own interests first. 她是一个不择手段的女商人，总是把自己的利益放在第一位。|近义词：cunning, devious, manipulative, unscrupulous, artful, shrewd, wily |反义词：honest, principled, ethical, scrupulous|词性: 形容词, 名词


machiavellians:/ˌmækiəˈveliənz/ |n.| 马基雅维利主义者；权谋家 | People who are cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing their careers. |例句：The company was full of machiavellians, all vying for power.  这家公司充满了权谋家，都在争夺权力。


例句：Machiavellians often use manipulation and deceit to achieve their goals.  马基雅维利主义者经常使用操纵和欺骗来达到他们的目的。|近义词：schemers, manipulators, intriguers |反义词：moralists, idealists|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Niccolò di Bernardo dei Machiavelli (1469-1527), Italian Renaissance diplomat, philosopher, historian, humanist, writer, playwright and poet. His most famous work is *The Prince* (Il Principe), a political treatise.
