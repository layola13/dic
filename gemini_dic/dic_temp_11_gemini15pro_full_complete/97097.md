infest:/ɪnˈfɛst/| v. |侵扰，骚扰；寄生于；大批出没于|To be present (of pests, etc.) in large numbers, typically so as to cause damage or disease.|Rats infested the ship. 老鼠横行于船上。|近义词：overrun, plague, swarm, teem|反义词：clear, cleanse|词性:动词
Etymology: From Latin infestare "attack, annoy, trouble, infest," from infestus "hostile, unsafe, disturbed," related to in- "not, opposite of" (see in- (1)) + festus "festive," from PIE root *dhes- "to do, perform." Originally "to attack in a hostile manner," sense of "to swarm in or over" is first recorded 1610s.
