desolative: /ˈdesələtɪv/ | adj. | 荒凉的，凄凉的，使人感到孤独的 | Causing or marked by desolation; creating a scene of barrenness and emptiness.  Making someone feel sad and lonely. | The desolative landscape stretched for miles.  荒凉的景象绵延数英里。

The empty swing set in the overgrown yard gave the house a desolative air.  杂草丛生的院子里空荡荡的秋千架让这房子显得格外凄凉。| 近义词: bleak, desolate, barren, dreary, forlorn | 反义词: cheerful, comforting, lively, vibrant, populous | 词性: adjective

desolate: /ˈdesələt/ | adj. | 荒凉的，无人居住的 | (of a place) deserted of people and in a state of bleak and dismal emptiness. | The town was left desolate after the hurricane.  飓风过后，小镇一片荒凉。

She felt desolate after her husband died.  丈夫去世后，她感到十分孤独。


词源: Late Middle English: from Latin desolatus ‘abandoned, forsaken’, past participle of desolare, from de- ‘thoroughly’ + solus ‘alone’.
