fending:/ˈfɛndɪŋ/ | v. | 照顾；保护；抵挡 | Looking after and providing for oneself, especially in the face of difficulty. | He was left fending for himself at a young age. 他很小就被迫独立生活。| 近义词: managing, coping, defending, protecting | 反义词: succumbing, yielding | 词性: 动词 (present participle of "fend")

词源:  From Middle English *fenden, from Old English *fēndan, from Proto-Germanic *fanþjaną (“to seize, grasp, try to get”).  Cognate with Dutch vatten (“to catch, seize”), German fangen (“to catch”).  Originally meant "to try to get something," later evolving to mean "to provide for oneself."
