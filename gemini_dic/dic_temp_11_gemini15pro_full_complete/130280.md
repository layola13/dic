non-substitution:/ˌnɒnˌsʌbstɪˈtjuːʃən/ | n. |非替代，非置换|The principle of non-substitution emphasizes the unique value of certain resources.| 例句：The architect insisted on the non-substitution of the original materials.  这位建筑师坚持不替换原始材料。|例句：Non-substitution is a key concept in conservation of historic buildings.  非替代是历史建筑保护中的一个关键概念。|近义词：non-replacement, irreplaceability|反义词: substitution, replacement|词性:名词

Etymology->
From non- + substitution.  "Substitution" comes from Late Latin *substitutionem, from Latin substitutus, past participle of substituere "to substitute".  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not".
