consecraters:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪtəz/ (UK), /ˈkɑːnsɪkreɪtərz/ (US)| n. |祝圣者，授圣职者|People who perform the act of consecration, dedicating something to a sacred purpose.  They officially declare a person, place, or thing sacred through a religious ceremony. |The consecraters gathered around the altar to bless the new church. 祝圣者聚集在祭坛周围为新教堂祈福。|Synonyms: ordainers, dedicators|Antonyms: desecrators, profaners| Noun

consecrate:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːnsɪkreɪt/ (US)| vt. |祝圣，奉献|To make or declare something sacred; dedicate formally to a religious or divine purpose.|The bishop consecrated the new church building. 主教祝圣了新的教堂建筑。|Synonyms: sanctify, hallow, bless|Antonyms: desecrate, profane| Verb
