dresser: /ˈdrɛsər/ (US) /ˈdrɛsə(r)/ (UK)| n.|梳妆台；碗柜；穿着讲究的人|A piece of furniture with drawers, often with a mirror, used for storing clothes and other personal items. Also, a person who dresses carefully or smartly. |She keeps her jewelry in her dresser. 她把她的珠宝放在梳妆台里。|Synonyms: bureau, chest of drawers, vanity (for storing items); dandy, fop (for a person)|Antonyms: slob, mess (for a person)|noun

dresser: /ˈdrɛsər/ (US) /ˈdrɛsə(r)/ (UK)| n.| (医院)敷料员|A person who applies dressings to wounds, especially in a hospital. |The dresser cleaned and bandaged the wound. 敷料员清洗并包扎了伤口。|Synonyms: wound dresser, medical assistant|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *drecier, from Vulgar Latin *directiare "to straighten, arrange," from Latin directus "straight, direct."  The meaning shifted from "one who sets things in order" to "one who dresses (others or oneself)" and eventually to "a piece of furniture used in dressing."
