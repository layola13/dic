mights:/maɪts/ | v. | 可能；也许；可以 | Expresses possibility or permission, less definite than "may."  It's the past tense form of "may" used in reported speech or subjunctive mood. |  He said he might come. 他说他可能会来。| synonyms: may, could, perhaps | antonyms: cannot, will not | verb

USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
He might come to the party. 他可能会来参加派对。
She might know the answer. 她可能知道答案。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
He might be coming to the party later. 他可能晚点会来参加派对。
She might be thinking about it. 她可能正在考虑这件事。

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
He might have already left. 他可能已经离开了。
She might have finished the book. 她可能已经读完了这本书。

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
He might have been working on it all day.  他可能一整天都在做这件事。
She might have been waiting for you. 她可能一直在等你。


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
He said he might go. 他说他可能会去。
She thought it might rain. 她认为可能会下雨。

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
He said he might be going later. 他说他可能晚点会去。
She thought it might be raining. 她认为可能正在下雨。


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
He said he might have gone already. 他说他可能已经去了。
She thought it might have rained earlier. 她认为可能早些时候下过雨了。

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
He said he might have been working on it. 他说他可能一直在做这件事。
She thought it might have been raining for hours. 她认为可能已经下了好几个小时的雨了。


一般将来时 (Simple Future):
He might come tomorrow. 他明天可能会来。
She might know the answer by then. 到那时她可能就知道答案了。

将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
He might be coming tomorrow afternoon. 他明天下午可能会来。
She might be working on it next week. 她下周可能正在做这件事。


将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
He might have finished by next week. 他下周可能就完成了。
She might have left by then. 到那时她可能已经离开了。

将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
He might have been working on it for a month by then.  到那时他可能已经做了一个月了。
She might have been living there for five years by next year. 到明年，她可能已经在那里住了五年了。


虚拟语气:
I might go if I had the time. 如果我有时间，我可能会去。
She might have helped if you had asked. 如果你问了，她可能会帮忙。


被动语态 (Passive Voice):
It might be done tomorrow.  明天可能会完成。
It might have been finished yesterday.  昨天可能已经完成了。


疑问句:
Might he come tomorrow?  他明天会来吗？
Might she have known the answer?  她可能知道答案吗？


否定句:
He might not come tomorrow. 他明天可能不会来。
She might not have known the answer. 她可能不知道答案。

词源：From Middle English *mihte, from Old English *mihte (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *mahtē (“might, could”), from Proto-Indo-European *mégh₂te (“might, could”), the aorist of *mégh₂ti (“to be able, to have power”). Cognate with West Frisian michte (“might”), Dutch mocht (“might”), German mochte (“liked, might”), Icelandic mætti (“might”).  Related to may.
