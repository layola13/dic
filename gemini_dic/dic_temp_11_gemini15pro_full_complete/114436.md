make-works:/ˈmeɪkˌwɜːrks/ | n. | 虚工（复数）|  Work created especially to keep people employed, rather than because it is needed (plural form). | The make-works assigned to the interns were mostly filing and data entry. 分配给实习生的虚工主要是归档和数据录入。| 近义词: busyworks, boondoggles | 反义词: essential works, necessary works | 词性: 名词

词源: "make-work" is a compound word formed from the verb "make" and the noun "work".  It signifies work that is "made" or created artificially, often for the sole purpose of keeping people occupied.
