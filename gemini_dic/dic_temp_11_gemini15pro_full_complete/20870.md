blavatsky:/bləˈvætski/
proper noun
布拉瓦茨基
Helena Petrovna Blavatsky (1831-1891), Russian occultist and writer, co-founder of the Theosophical Society.  She is best known for her works *Isis Unveiled* and *The Secret Doctrine*.
Blavatsky's writings significantly influenced the development of modern esotericism.
Helena Blavatsky founded the Theosophical Society with Henry Steel Olcott and William Quan Judge.
