nova:/ˈnoʊvə/| n. |新星|A star that suddenly becomes much brighter and then gradually returns to its original brightness.  A nova is caused by a nuclear explosion on the surface of a white dwarf star.|Astronomers observed a nova in the Andromeda galaxy.|Synonyms: exploding star, cataclysmic variable star|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
