moderationist:/ˌmɒdəˈreɪʃənɪst/| n. |温和主义者；稳健派|A person who favors moderate views, especially in politics.| The moderationist  argued for a compromise between the two extreme positions. 温和主义者主张在两个极端立场之间寻求妥协。|synonyms: moderate, centrist|antonyms: extremist, radical|noun

moderate:/ˈmɒdərət/| adj. |温和的；适度的|Average in amount, intensity, quality, or degree.|The hike was moderately difficult.  这次徒步旅行难度适中。|synonyms: reasonable, temperate|antonyms: extreme, excessive|adjective

moderate:/ˈmɒdəreɪt/| v. |使缓和；主持|Make or become less extreme, intense, rigorous, or violent.|He moderated his tone when he realized he was speaking to the boss.  当他意识到自己正在和老板说话时，他缓和了语气。|synonyms: lessen, mitigate|antonyms: intensify, exacerbate|verb
