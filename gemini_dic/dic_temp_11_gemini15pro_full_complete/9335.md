antipragmatically:/ˌæntipræɡˈmætɪkli/
adverb
反实用主义地，不切实际地
Acting in a way that disregards or goes against practical considerations or the achievement of effective action.  Prioritizing theoretical purity or ideological consistency over practical results.
He antipragmatically insisted on using a horse-drawn carriage, even though a car would have been much faster and more efficient. 他反实用主义地坚持使用马车，即使汽车会更快、更高效。
The artist antipragmatically destroyed his most successful painting, claiming it was too commercial. 这位艺术家反实用主义地毁掉了自己最成功的画作，声称它太商业化了。
Near Antonyms: pragmatically, practically, realistically
Near Synonyms: impractically, unrealistically, idealistically
词性: 副词
