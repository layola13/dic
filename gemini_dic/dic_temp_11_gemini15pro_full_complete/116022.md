marsipobranchia:/ˌmɑːrsɪpəˈbræŋkiə/ | n. |  圆口纲 |A superclass of eel-like aquatic vertebrates lacking jaws and paired fins, comprising the lampreys and hagfishes.  They are characterized by a cartilaginous skeleton, a round sucking mouth, and seven or more pairs of gill pouches.| Lampreys and hagfishes belong to the Marsipobranchia. 七鳃鳗和盲鳗属于圆口纲。 | synonyms: Cyclostomata | antonyms: Gnathostomata | noun
