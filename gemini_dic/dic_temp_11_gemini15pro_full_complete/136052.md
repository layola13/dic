nonrecalcitrances: /ˌnɒnˌrɪkælˈsɪtrənsɪz/ | n. |  不顽固，顺从，服从 |  The state or quality of not being recalcitrant; compliance, obedience.  It signifies a willingness to cooperate and a lack of resistance. |  The team's nonrecalcitrance made the project run smoothly. 团队的顺从使项目进展顺利。 |  compliance, acquiescence, obedience, docility, submissiveness | recalcitrance, resistance, defiance, obstinacy | noun

Etymology:
From non- (prefix meaning "not") + recalcitrance (from Latin recalcitrāns, present participle of recalcitrāre "to kick back," from re- "back" + calcitrāre "to kick," from calx "heel").
