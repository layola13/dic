encapsulation:/ɪnˌkæpsjʊˈleɪʃn/ | n. |封装，包封|The act of enclosing something in a capsule or other container; the state of being enclosed in a capsule or other container. In computer science, it refers to the bundling of data with the methods that operate on that data, or the restricting of direct access to some of an object's components.|The company specializes in the encapsulation of liquid medicines. 这家公司专门从事液体药物的封装。|近义词：enclosure, containment, packaging|反义词: exposure, revelation | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin "incapsulatio," from "in" (in) + "capsula" (small box), diminutive of "capsa" (box).
