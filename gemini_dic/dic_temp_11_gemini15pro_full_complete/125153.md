Etymology->

From multi- + armed.  "Multi-" is a combining form meaning "many," "multiple," deriving from Latin "multus" (much, many). "Armed" comes from the Old English "earm" (arm) and the suffix "-ed," indicating possession of the quality described by the preceding element.
