calotype: /ˈkælətaɪp/ | n. |卡罗式摄影法，卡罗版|An early photographic process in which a negative image is made on paper sensitized with silver iodide and from which positive prints can be made. |The museum displayed a collection of stunning calotypes from the 19th century.  博物馆展出了一批19世纪令人惊叹的卡罗式照片。|Synonyms: talbotype |Antonyms: (No direct antonym, could consider modern digital photography)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *kalos* "beautiful" + *typos* "impression".
