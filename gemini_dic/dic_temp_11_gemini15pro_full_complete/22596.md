bootstrap:/ˈbuːtˌstræp/ | n. v. | 引导程序；自举；解靴带 | a loop of leather sewn at the side or rear top of a boot, enabling one to pull it on. Also (as a verb): to pull oneself up by one’s bootstraps (originally a humorous or impossible action). Now frequently figurative, of improving one’s position by one’s own efforts, especially in the absence of outside help. | He pulled on the bootstraps of his boots. 他拉起靴子上的靴带。|She bootstrapped her business from a small market stall. 她从小摊位白手起家建立了自己的生意。 | synonyms: start-up, launch, initiate | antonyms: discontinue, cease, halt | noun, verb
