monadic:/məˈnædɪk/ /moʊˈnædɪk/| adj.|单子的，一元的|Relating to or involving a single unit or element; having the nature of a monad.  Characterized by or acting upon only one operand or argument (in computer science).|例句：A monadic predicate takes only one argument.  一个一元谓词只接受一个参数。

例句：Monadic logic deals with single entities or individuals. 单子逻辑处理单个实体或个体。|近义词：singular, unary, single|反义词：dyadic, binary|词性:adjective

monad:/ˈmɒnæd/ /ˈmoʊnæd/| n.|单子，单元|A single unit; an indivisible entity. In philosophy, a monad is a simple substance that constitutes the ultimate unit of reality. In computer science, a monad is a type of abstract data type.|例句：Leibniz believed the universe was composed of monads.  莱布尼茨认为宇宙是由单子组成的。

例句：In Haskell, monads are used to manage side effects. 在Haskell中，单子用于管理副作用。|近义词：unit, element, atom|反义词：dyad, binary|词性:noun
