galumphs:/ɡəˈlʌmfs/ | v. | 笨拙地跳跃，欢快地蹦跳 | To move in a clumsy, heavy-footed, and often noisy way, typically with a suggestion of excitement or boisterousness. | The children galumphed through the puddles. 孩子们笨拙地在水坑里跳来跳去。 | He galumphed across the field, clearly delighted with his new boots.  他穿着新靴子兴高采烈地蹦蹦跳跳地穿过田野。 | 近义词: lumber, stomp, clump, bound | 反义词: tiptoe, creep, slink | 词性: 动词

词源:  Coined by Lewis Carroll in "Through the Looking-Glass" (1871), probably a blend of "gallop" and "triumph."
