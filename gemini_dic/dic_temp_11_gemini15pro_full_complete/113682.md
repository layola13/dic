mackerel:/ˈmæk(ə)rəl/ | n. | 鲭鱼；马鲛鱼 | A fast-swimming, edible fish of the North Atlantic, important commercially because of its oily flesh. | We had grilled mackerel for dinner. 我们晚餐吃了烤鲭鱼。 |  例句：The mackerel were jumping out of the water. 鲭鱼不断从水中跃出。| 近义词：tuna, herring | 反义词: (No direct antonym for a specific fish) | 词性:noun

mackerel:/ˈmæk(ə)rəl/ | adj.(informal, often derogatory) | (指女人)打扮俗艳的，轻浮的 |  (of a woman) Showily dressed and made up, typically in a way considered vulgar. | She was wearing a rather mackerel dress. 她穿着一件相当俗艳的连衣裙。| 例句: He described her as a mackerel tart. 他把她描述成一个打扮俗艳轻浮的女人。| 近义词：tarty, flashy, gaudy | 反义词: modest, understated | 词性:adjective
