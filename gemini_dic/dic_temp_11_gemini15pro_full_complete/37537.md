commemorative: /kəˈmɛmərətɪv/ (US) /kəˈmɛmərətɪv/ (UK)
| adj. | 纪念的，庆祝的 | Serving to commemorate; intended as a memorial. |  This commemorative stamp celebrates the 100th anniversary of the founding of the city. 这枚纪念邮票是为了庆祝建市一百周年。 |  The museum houses a commemorative plaque dedicated to the war heroes.  博物馆里有一块纪念战争英雄的牌匾。 | celebratory, memorial, remembrance |  n/a | adjective

commemorate: /kəˈmɛməreɪt/ (US) /kəˈmɛməreɪt/ (UK)
| v. | 纪念，庆祝 | Recall and show respect for (someone or something). | They commemorated the victory with a parade. 他们举行游行庆祝胜利。 | The ceremony commemorated the soldiers who died in the war.  这个仪式是为了纪念在战争中牺牲的士兵。 | celebrate, observe, memorialize | forget, ignore | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin commemorativus, from commemorare "to call to mind, remember," from com- "together" + memorare "to bring to mind," from memor "mindful."
