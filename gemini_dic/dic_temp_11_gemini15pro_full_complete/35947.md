coddle:/ˈkɒd(ə)l/ (UK) /ˈkɑːdl/ (US)| v.| 娇惯；溺爱；悉心照料 |To treat someone with excessive care and attention, often to the point of spoiling them.  It also means to cook something gently in liquid just below boiling point.|例句：She coddles her youngest child. 她溺爱她最小的孩子。

例句：The eggs were coddled to perfection.  这些鸡蛋被煮得恰到好处。|近义词：pamper, indulge, spoil, baby, cosset |反义词: neglect, ignore |词性: verb

This is simply the third-person singular simple present form of the verb "coddle".|例句: He coddles his plants and talks to them every day. 他悉心照料他的植物，每天都跟它们说话。

例句:  She coddles her dog with gourmet treats and a warm bed. 她用美味的食物和温暖的床溺爱她的狗。 |近义词：pampers, indulges, spoils, babies, cossets |反义词: neglects, ignores |词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "caudle," meaning a warm drink given to invalids, which in turn comes from the Old French "chaudel," ultimately from the Latin "calidus" meaning "warm." The sense shifted from warming something physically to treating someone with excessive warmth and care.
