incur:/ɪnˈkɜːr/ (US), /ɪnˈkəː(r)/ (UK)| v. |招致，遭受，引起|To become subject to something unwelcome or unpleasant, often as a result of one's own actions or behavior.|例句：The company incurred heavy losses due to the economic downturn. 公司因经济衰退而遭受了严重的损失。|近义词：suffer, sustain, experience, obtain, acquire|反义词：avoid, prevent, avert|词性:动词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "encourir" (to run into, meet with), from Latin "incurrere" (to run into, run against, attack), from "in-" (in, into, against) + "currere" (to run).
