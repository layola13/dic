nonabsolutists:/ˌnɒnˈæbsəluːtɪsts/ | n. |非绝对主义者| People who do not believe in absolute principles or standards, often in a moral, ethical, or political context.  They believe that context and circumstances are important considerations. |  The nonabsolutists in the group argued for a more flexible approach.  小组中的非绝对主义者主张采取更灵活的方法。|  relativists, pragmatists | absolutists, dogmatists | noun
Etymology:  "non-" (Latin prefix meaning "not") + "absolute" (from Latin "absolutus" meaning "free from restriction, complete") + "-ist" (suffix indicating a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or belief)
