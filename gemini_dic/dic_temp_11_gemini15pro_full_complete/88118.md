hijacking:/ˈhaɪdʒækɪŋ/| n.|劫持，挟持|The act of illegally seizing an aircraft, vehicle, or ship while in transit and forcing it to go to a different destination or using it for one's own purposes.  It can also refer to the unauthorized takeover of a website or online account.|The pirates were arrested for hijacking the cargo ship. 海盗因劫持货船而被捕。|synonyms: seizing, commandeering, taking over|antonyms: releasing, surrendering|noun

hijack:/ˈhaɪdʒæk/| vt.|劫持，挟持|To illegally seize an aircraft, vehicle, or ship while in transit and force it to go to a different destination or use it for one's own purposes. It can also refer to the unauthorized takeover of a website or online account.|Terrorists hijacked the plane and demanded a ransom. 恐怖分子劫持了飞机并索要赎金。|synonyms: seize, commandeer, take over|antonyms: release, surrender|verb
