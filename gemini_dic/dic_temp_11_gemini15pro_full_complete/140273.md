offuscate:/ˈɒfəskeɪt/ (US) /ɒfʌsˈkeɪt/ (UK)| v. |使模糊，使混乱，使昏暗|To make something unclear or difficult to understand.|The fog offuscated the view of the mountains. 浓雾模糊了山景。|The jargon in the legal document offuscated its meaning. 法律文件中使用的术语使其含义变得模糊不清。|近义词：obscure, confuse, bewilder, muddle, cloud|反义词：clarify, elucidate, illuminate|词性:动词

offuscation:/ˌɒfəsˈkeɪʃn/ (US) /ˌɒfʌsˈkeɪʃn/ (UK)| n. |模糊，混乱，昏暗|The act of making something unclear or difficult to understand.|The lawyer's offuscation of the facts made it difficult for the jury to reach a verdict. 律师对事实的模糊化使得陪审团难以做出裁决。|His deliberate offuscation of the issue annoyed everyone. 他对这个问题的故意含糊其辞惹恼了所有人。|近义词：obscurity, confusion, bewilderment|反义词：clarity, elucidation, illumination|词性:名词

词源: From Late Latin *offuscatus, past participle of offuscare "to darken," from Latin ob "against, toward" + fuscare "to make dark," from fuscus "dark."
