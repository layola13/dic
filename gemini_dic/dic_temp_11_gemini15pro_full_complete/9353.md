antiprohibitionists:/ˌæntiˌproʊɪˈbɪʃənɪsts/
|n.| 反禁酒主义者|People who opposed the prohibition of alcohol, particularly in the United States during the period of national prohibition (1920-1933).|The antiprohibitionists argued that banning alcohol would lead to increased crime and a loss of personal freedom.|近义词:wets, legalizers|反义词:prohibitionists, drys|词性:Noun

Etymology:
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to (from Greek *anti-)
prohibition: the action of forbidding something, especially by law (from Latin prohibitus, past participle of prohibere 'to prevent, prohibit')
-ist: (suffix) denoting a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system, or who performs a particular action.
