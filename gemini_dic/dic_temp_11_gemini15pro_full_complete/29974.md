cavil:/ˈkævəl/| v. n.|吹毛求疵，无端指摘；苛责；反对；挑剔；（吹毛求疵的）批评，反对意见|To raise trivial objections; find fault unnecessarily.|He cavilled at the price of the meal. 他对这顿饭的价格吹毛求疵。|近义词：nitpick, quibble, carp, criticize|反义词：praise, commend, approve|词性:动词,名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *caveler*, frequentative of *caver* "to separate chaff from grain," from Latin *cavillārī* "to jest, mock, banter," perhaps related to *callēs* "hard skin." The sense shift from "jest, mock" to "raise trivial objections" occurred in French.
