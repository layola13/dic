mozarab:/ˈmoʊzəræb/ | n. adj.| 莫萨拉布人，莫萨拉布式的 | A Christian living in Muslim-controlled Spain during the Middle Ages who adopted some Arabic customs but retained their own Romance language and religion.  A mixture of Arabic and Romance languages spoken by Mozarabs. |  例句：The Mozarab community preserved their unique culture for centuries. 莫萨拉布族群保存了他们独特的文化数个世纪。

例句：Mozarabic art and architecture show a fascinating blend of Islamic and Christian influences.  莫萨拉布艺术和建筑展现了伊斯兰教和基督教影响的迷人融合。 | 近义词：Musta'rib (Arabic term) | 反义词:  N/A | 词性: noun, adjective


Etymology: From Spanish *mozárabe*, from Arabic مستعرب *mustaʿrib*, meaning "Arabized".
