offtake:/ˈɒfteɪk/ (UK) /ˈɔːfteɪk/ (US)| n.| 消耗量，承购量，销售量，支取，提货|The act of taking off or away something; the amount of something that is taken or bought from a supplier over a particular period of time.  A guaranteed minimum purchase of a product or service, usually in bulk, over a specified period.|The offtake of electricity has increased with the hot weather.  电力消耗量随着炎热的天气而增加。|Synonyms:  consumption, sales, purchase, demand, uptake|Antonyms: supply, production|noun

offtake:/ˈɒfteɪk/ (UK) /ˈɔːfteɪk/ (US)| n.| 分支道，支流|A channel or pipe that branches off from a larger one.|The irrigation system has several offtakes from the main canal. 灌溉系统从主干渠分出几条支流。|Synonyms: branch, outlet, tributary|Antonyms: main channel, source|noun
