hot-wiring:/ˈhɒtˌwaɪərɪŋ/ | n. | 偷车；热启动 | The act of starting a car engine without using the ignition key, typically by connecting wires. | He got arrested for hot-wiring a car. 他因偷车而被捕。| synonyms: jumpstarting, bypassing the ignition | antonyms: legal starting, using the key | noun


hot-wire:/ˌhɒtˈwaɪə(r)/ | vt. |  偷车；热启动 | To start (a car engine) without using the ignition key, typically by connecting wires. | The thieves hot-wired the car and sped away. 小偷们偷了车，飞驰而去。| synonyms: jumpstart, bypass the ignition | antonyms: legally start, use the key | verb

Etymology:  "hot" (referring to the live electrical wire) + "wire" (referring to the manipulation of wires).  This term originated in the early 20th century with the increasing popularity of automobiles.
