bletonism: /ˈblɛtənɪzəm/
n.
色盲, 色弱
the inability to distinguish certain colors, especially between red and green.
He suffers from a mild form of bletonism. 他患有轻度色盲。

近义词: color blindness, daltonism
反义词: (No direct antonym for a medical condition)

词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *bletonisme*, coined by physician Jules Chéron in 1872, after his patient, a Monsieur Bleton, who exhibited this condition.
