iricism: /ˈaɪrɪsɪzəm/
noun
爱尔兰主义, 爱尔兰特色
A distinctive characteristic of Ireland or its people, especially in literature, art, or music.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Irish + -ism.  "Irish" ultimately derives from Old Irish *Ériu, the name of a goddess. The suffix "-ism" indicates a characteristic or quality.
