commonplacer:/ˈkɒmənˌpleɪsə(r)/ (British) /ˈkɑːmənˌpleɪsər/ (American)| n. adj.| 平凡的事物；陈词滥调；老生常谈；平凡的；陈腐的|Something ordinary or commonplace; a trite or obvious remark.|

例句：
His speech was filled with commonplacers about the importance of hard work.
他的演讲充满了关于努力工作重要性的陈词滥调。

例句：
Air travel has become a commonplace.
乘飞机旅行已成为司空见惯的事情。


近义词：banality, cliché, platitude, truism, bromide|
反义词：novelty, originality, rarity|
词性：noun, adjective|


词源:
From common + place + -er.  The noun sense arose from the adjectival use.  Ultimately from Latin communis (common) and locus (place).
