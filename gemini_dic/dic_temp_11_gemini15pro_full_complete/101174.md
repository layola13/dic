invigorate:/ɪnˈvɪɡəreɪt/| vt.|使充满活力，使精神焕发，激励|To give strength and energy to.  To fill with life and energy.|The cold shower invigorated me after the long run.  跑完长跑后，冷水澡让我恢复了活力。|energize, revitalize, refresh, stimulate|weaken, exhaust, tire, enervate|verb
invigorating:/ɪnˈvɪɡəreɪtɪŋ/| adj.|使人精神焕发的，令人振奋的|Making one feel strong, healthy, and full of energy.|A brisk walk in the crisp morning air is invigorating.  在清爽的早晨空气中 brisk walking 令人精神焕发。|refreshing, stimulating, energizing, revitalizing|exhausting, tiring, weakening, enervating|adjective
invigoration:/ɪnˌvɪɡəˈreɪʃən/| n.|鼓舞，激励，使充满活力|The act of invigorating; the state of being invigorated.|The invigoration I felt after the yoga class was amazing.  瑜伽课后我感到精神焕发，感觉棒极了。|renewal, revitalization, refreshment, stimulation|exhaustion, fatigue, weakness, enervation|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *invigorāre, from in- (“in, into”) + vigor (“strength, vigor”).
