lowball:/ˈloʊbɔːl/ (US) /ˈləʊbɔːl/ (UK)| adj.|低估的；低价的|Ridiculously low in price or estimate.|That's a lowball offer. 那是一个低得离谱的报价。|近义词：underestimated, undervalued|反义词：overestimated, overvalued|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the game of poker, referring to a deceptively low first bet intended to lure other players into the pot.  "Low ball" as a noun phrase meaning a low pitch in baseball dates back to the 1890s.  The figurative and broader sense of making a deceptively low offer emerged in the mid-20th century.
