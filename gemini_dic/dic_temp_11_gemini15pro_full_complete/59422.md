earthlight:/ˈɜːrθlaɪt/
noun
地球反照，地光
The faint illumination of the dark part of the moon by sunlight reflected from the earth's surface and atmosphere.
The astronauts could see the earthlight illuminating the lunar landscape. 宇航员可以看到地球反照照亮月球表面。

The earthlight was strong enough to read by. 地球反照亮到足以阅读。

Synonym: earthshine
Antonym: (No direct antonym, but consider "darkness" in context)
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From earth + light.  "Earth" comes from Old English eorþe, meaning "ground, soil, dry land." "Light" comes from Old English lēoht, meaning "illumination, radiance."
