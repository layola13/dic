bail:/beɪl/| n. vt. vi.|保释金，保证金；桶；杓；紧急跳伞|Money paid to release someone from jail until their trial.  To remove water from a boat. To jump out of a plane or other vehicle with a parachute because of an emergency. |The judge set bail at $10,000. 法官将保释金定为1万美元。|He bailed the boat to keep it from sinking. 他往外舀水以免船沉没。|The pilot was forced to bail out of the plane when the engine failed.  发动机故障后，飞行员被迫跳伞。|近义词：(n.) bond, security, guarantee,  (v.) scoop, ladle, rescue, abandon|反义词: (v.) imprison, confine, remain |词性: noun, verb


bail:/beɪl/| n.| 栏杆，横木|A horizontal bar or a series of bars. |The cows were kept in a pen with wooden bails.  牛被关在一个有木栏杆的围栏里。|近义词: railing, bar, fence |反义词: none |词性: noun
