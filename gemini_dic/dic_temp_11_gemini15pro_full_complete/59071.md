dwindle:/ˈdwɪndl/| v. |减少，缩小，衰落|To become smaller and smaller; to decrease gradually.|The town's population has dwindled to a mere few hundred.  这个镇的人口已经减少到只有几百人。

The company's profits dwindled in the last quarter.  公司上个季度的利润下降了。|近义词：decrease, diminish, shrink, decline|反义词:increase, grow, expand|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dwindlen, frequentative of dwine (“to waste away”), from Old English dwīnan (“to vanish, perish, dwindle, pine away”), from Proto-Germanic *dwīnaną (“to disappear, fade, waste away”), from Proto-Indo-European *dwey- (“to lack, want”). Cognate with Dutch *dwijnen (“to faint”), German schwinden (“to disappear, vanish”), Icelandic dvīna (“to vanish, perish, dwindle”).
