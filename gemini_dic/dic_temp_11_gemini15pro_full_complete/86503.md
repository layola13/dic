heired:/ɛrd/
|v.|继承（财产、头衔等）|To receive or acquire (property, a title, etc.) as an heir.|He heired a fortune from his grandfather. 他从祖父那里继承了一笔财富。|
|近义词：inherited, acquired, succeeded to|反义词：forfeited, relinquished|词性:动词

heired:/ɛrd/
|adj.|被继承的|Having been received or acquired as an heir.|The heired estate was vast and impressive.  被继承的遗产广阔而令人印象深刻。|近义词：inherited|反义词：acquired (through one's own efforts)|词性:形容词

词源：From Old French *eirer (unattested) or directly from Latin haerēre "to stick, cling, adhere; to be attached or connected with; to hang, depend; to remain, stay, continue."  The meaning shifted from "to stick to, cling to" to "to be attached to (an estate) as an heir."
