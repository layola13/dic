equivocation:/ɪˌkwɪvəˈkeɪʃn/
n.|模棱两可，含糊其辞，诡辩|The politician's equivocation on the issue angered many voters.  这位政客在这个问题上的含糊其辞激怒了许多选民。|例句：His equivocation about his whereabouts last night made me suspicious. 他对昨晚行踪的含糊其辞让我起了疑心。|近义词：ambiguity, prevarication, evasion, double-talk|反义词：clarity, directness, honesty, forthrightness|词性:名词

equivocate:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪt/
v.|含糊其辞，支吾其词，说模棱两可的话|He equivocated when asked about his plans. 当被问及他的计划时，他含糊其辞。|例句：She equivocated when asked directly about her involvement in the scandal. 当被直接问及她是否卷入丑闻时，她支吾其词。|近义词：prevaricate, evade, hedge|反义词：be forthright, be direct, be honest|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin aequivocatio, from aequivocus (“ambiguous, having two meanings”), from Latin aequi- + vocare.  Equi- means “equal,” and vocare means “to call.”  Thus, an equivocation is like calling something by two equal (i.e., equally valid) names, leading to ambiguity.
