malthusianism:/mælˈθuːziənɪzəm/ | n. | 马尔萨斯主义 | The theory that population growth will eventually outstrip food supply, leading to famine, disease, and war. | The economist argued that malthusianism is still relevant in some developing countries. 这位经济学家认为马尔萨斯主义在一些发展中国家仍然适用。 | synonyms: overpopulation theory, Malthusian theory | antonyms: cornucopianism | noun


malthusian:/mælˈθuːziən/ | adj. | 马尔萨斯的；马尔萨斯主义的 | Relating to Malthus or his theory. |  The malthusian predictions of widespread famine have not yet come to pass.  马尔萨斯关于大范围饥荒的预测还没有实现。 | synonyms: neo-Malthusian | antonyms:  anti-Malthusian | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Thomas Robert Malthus (1766–1834), English economist and demographer + -ian + -ism.
