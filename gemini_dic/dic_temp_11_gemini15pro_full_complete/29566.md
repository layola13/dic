catchpenny:/ˈkætʃˌpɛni/| adj. n.|粗制滥造的，骗钱的；骗钱的商品|Something designed to sell quickly and cheaply, often of low quality; something intended to appeal to undiscriminating customers.| The market was flooded with catchpenny toys.  市场上充斥着粗制滥造的玩具。| The author churned out catchpenny novels to make a quick buck.  这位作家粗制滥造地写小说，就为了快速赚钱。|近义词：cheap, tawdry, shoddy, gimmicky|反义词：high-quality, valuable, well-made|词性:adjective, noun

catchpenny:/ˈkætʃˌpɛni/| n.|骗钱货，廉价商品|A product or item designed to be eye-catching and appealing but of low quality, often intended for quick profit.|He tried to sell me some catchpenny at the fair. 他试图在集市上卖给我一些廉价商品。|The street vendor hawked catchpennies to the tourists.  街头小贩向游客兜售廉价商品。|近义词：trinket, knick-knack, bauble|反义词：treasure, heirloom, valuable|词性:noun
