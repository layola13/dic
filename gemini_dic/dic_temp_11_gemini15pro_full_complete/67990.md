factionalism:/ˈfækʃənəlɪzəm/
n.
派系主义；党派之争
The tendency to form or promote factions; the state of being divided into factions.
The factionalism within the party threatened to tear it apart.  党内的派系主义有可能导致党的分裂。

The constant factionalism made it difficult to get anything done.  持续不断的派系斗争使得什么事情都难以完成。
cliquishness, sectarianism, infighting
unity, cooperation, solidarity
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From faction + -alism.  Faction comes from Late Latin factionem, meaning "a making, a doing, a party, a faction," from Latin facere "to make, do."
