logical-empiricism:/ˈlɒdʒɪkəl ɛmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ /ˈlɑːdʒɪkəl ɛmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ | n. |逻辑经验主义|A philosophical movement that emphasizes the importance of experience and observation in the formation of knowledge, and holds that metaphysical speculation is meaningless.  It combines logic and empiricism, asserting that only statements verifiable through direct observation or logical proof are meaningful. |Logical empiricism was a dominant force in philosophy during the early 20th century. 逻辑经验主义是20世纪早期哲学中的主导力量。|Synonym: logical positivism, neopositivism |Antonym: metaphysics, idealism |noun
