overobjects:/ˈoʊvərˌɒbdʒɪkts/ | n. | 超对象，过度对象 | Philosophical concept referring to objects so massively distributed in time and space that they defy traditional notions of objecthood.  These objects are often technological, social, or ecological in nature and challenge our ability to comprehend their totality. | The internet could be considered an overobject, as its vastness and constant evolution make it difficult to fully grasp. | 近义词: hyperobjects, mega-objects | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "local object" or "discrete object") | 词性: noun


overobjects:/ˈoʊvərˌɒbdʒɪkts/ | n. | 超对象，过度对象 |  A concept from object-oriented ontology (OOO) describing entities vastly distributed in time and space, exceeding human comprehension and control, often with significant ecological or social implications. | Climate change is a prime example of an overobject, its effects rippling across the globe and impacting generations to come. | 近义词: hyperobjects | 反义词: (No direct antonym, consider "local phenomena" or "bounded entities") | 词性: noun
