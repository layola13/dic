interdictory:/ˌɪntərˈdɪktəri/| adj.|禁止的，禁令的|Having the nature of or imposing an interdict; prohibitory.|The judge issued an interdictory order preventing the company from selling the product.|Near Synonyms:  prohibitory, forbidding, restrictive, prohibitive, proscriptive|Near Antonyms:  permissive, allowing, authorizing|adjective

interdict:/ˈɪntərˌdɪkt/| n. vt.|禁令，禁止| (n.) An authoritative prohibition; a decree that forbids something. (vt.) To prohibit or forbid officially.|The church imposed an interdict on the king. 教会禁止国王。|The king interdicted trade with neighboring countries. 国王禁止与邻国贸易。|Near Synonyms: prohibition, ban, injunction, proscription|Near Antonyms: permission, allowance, sanction|noun, verb
