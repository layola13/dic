ensear:/ɛnˈsɪr/ |v. |套住；缠住；使…陷入圈套；使…落入陷阱|To enclose, entrap, or ensnare.|The hunter enseared the rabbit in a net. 猎人用网套住了兔子。|近义词: entrap, ensnare, trap|反义词: release, free|词性: 动词


Etymology: From en- (make, cause to be) + sear (to burn, dry up).  While the modern meaning relates to trapping, the original sense likely referred to enclosing something as if by burning or drying around it.
