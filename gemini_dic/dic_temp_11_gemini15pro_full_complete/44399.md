cross-grainedness:/ˌkrɒsˈɡreɪndnɪs/ | n. | 脾气暴躁；别扭；难以相处 | The state or quality of being cross-grained; perverseness or irritability of temper. |  His cross-grainedness made it difficult to work with him. 他的暴躁脾气让他很难与人共事。 | synonyms: irascibility, cantankerousness, grumpiness, churlishness, ill-temper, peevishness, fractiousness | antonyms: affability, amiability, agreeableness, good nature, pleasantness | noun


Etymology:
From cross-grained + -ness.  Cross-grained itself comes from cross (meaning "contrary, opposing") + grained (referring to the direction of the grain in wood, which if irregular can make it difficult to work with).
