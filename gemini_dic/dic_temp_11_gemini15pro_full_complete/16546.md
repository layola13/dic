barmaids:/ˈbɑːmeɪdz/| n. |酒吧女招待；女调酒师|A woman who serves drinks in a bar. |The barmaids were busy serving the thirsty customers. 酒吧女招待们正忙着招待口渴的顾客。|Example 2:  The two barmaids knew all the regulars by name.  两位酒吧女招待知道所有常客的名字。|近义词：bartenders, waitresses|反义词:customer, patron|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "bar" (meaning a counter where drinks are served) + "maid" (a female servant).  The word "bar" ultimately comes from the Old French "barre," meaning "bar" or "barrier." "Maid" comes from Old English "mægden," meaning "virgin" or "young woman."
