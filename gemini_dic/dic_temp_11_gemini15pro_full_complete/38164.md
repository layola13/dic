compromise:/ˈkɒmprəmaɪz/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmprəmaɪz/ (US)| n. v.|妥协，折衷；妥协方案，折衷方案；损害，危害|An agreement or a settlement of a dispute that is reached by each side making concessions.|例句：They reached a compromise over the price. 他们在价格上达成了妥协。|近义词：agreement, settlement, concession, bargain|反义词：intransigence, disagreement, refusal|词性:名词,动词

compromise:/ˈkɒmprəmaɪz/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmprəmaɪz/ (US)| v.|妥协，折衷；损害，危害|To settle a dispute by mutual concession.|例句：We are not prepared to compromise on safety standards. 我们不愿在安全标准上妥协。|近义词： concede, negotiate, settle, yield|反义词：resist, refuse, reject|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin compromissum, from Latin compromittere "to make a mutual promise," from com- "together" + promittere "to promise."
