fallacy:/ˈfæləsi/ (US), /ˈfæləsi/ (UK)| n.|谬误，谬论|A mistaken belief, especially one based on unsound argument.|Many people believe the fallacy that money brings happiness. 很多人相信金钱带来幸福的谬论。|Synonyms: misconception, delusion, error, myth|Antonyms: truth, fact, reality|noun

fallacy:/ˈfæləsi/ (US), /ˈfæləsi/ (UK)| n.|谬误，谬论|An unsound argument that appears superficially correct.|The politician's argument was based on a logical fallacy.  这位政客的论点基于逻辑谬误。|Synonyms:  sophism, false reasoning, faulty logic|Antonyms: valid argument, sound reasoning|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *fallācia* "deceit, trick," from Latin *fallāx* (genitive *fallācis*) "deceptive, deceitful," from *fallere* "to deceive, trip, cause to fall."
