demise:/dɪˈmaɪz/| n.|死亡，终止，转让|A death, especially of a person in a high social position. Also, the end or failure of an enterprise or institution. Can also refer to the transfer of an estate by lease or will. |例句：The demise of the company was caused by poor management.  这家公司的倒闭是由管理不善造成的。

例句：The king's demise led to a power struggle. 国王的逝世导致了一场权力斗争。

|近义词：death, decease, end, termination, expiration, transfer, conveyance|反义词：birth, beginning, start, commencement, inception|词性:noun


demise:/dɪˈmaɪz/| vt.|遗赠，转让（财产）|To transfer (real property) by will or lease. |例句：He demised his estate to his nephew. 他把他的财产遗赠给了他的侄子。

例句：The property was demised to the tenant for a period of 99 years.  该房产租给租户99年。


|近义词：bequeath, transfer, convey|反义词：inherit, receive|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *demis, past participle of metre "to put, place, send," from Latin mittere "to send, let go."  The legal sense "to convey or grant (an estate) by will or lease" led to the meaning "transfer of sovereignty or dominion (especially by death)."
