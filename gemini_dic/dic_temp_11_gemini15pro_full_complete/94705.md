impromptu:/ɪmˈprɒmptjuː/ (UK), /ɪmˈprɑːmptuː/ (US)| adj. adv. n.|即兴的，即席的；即兴表演；即兴曲|Done without being planned or rehearsed.|She gave an impromptu performance at the party. 她在聚会上进行了即兴表演。|近义词：offhand, extemporaneous, unrehearsed, spontaneous|反义词：planned, prepared, rehearsed|词性: adjective, adverb, noun|词源: From Italian *all'improvviso*, meaning "in the unforeseen," from *improvviso* "unforeseen," from Latin *in- "not" + *provisus* "foreseen."


impromptu:/ɪmˈprɒmptjuː/ (UK), /ɪmˈprɑːmptuː/ (US)| adv.|即兴地，即席地|Done without being planned or rehearsed.|He spoke impromptu at the meeting. 他在会议上即兴发言。|近义词：extemporaneously, offhandedly|反义词：deliberately, planned|词性: adverb


impromptu:/ɪmˈprɒmptjuː/ (UK), /ɪmˈprɑːmptuː/ (US)| n.|即兴表演；即兴曲|A short piece of music, poem, etc., that is performed without preparation.|The pianist played an impromptu. 钢琴家演奏了一首即兴曲。|近义词：improvisation|反义词：preparation, rehearsal|词性: noun
