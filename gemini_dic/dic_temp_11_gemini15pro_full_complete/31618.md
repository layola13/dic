chasuble: /ˈtʃæzəbəl/ (US), /ˈtʃæzjubəl/ (UK)
noun
祭披; 主祭的法衣
A sleeveless outer vestment worn by a priest celebrating Mass.
The priest wore a richly embroidered chasuble.  神父穿着一件绣工精美的祭披。

The chasuble was a deep shade of purple, signifying penitence.  这件祭披是深紫色的，象征着忏悔。

近义词: vestment, robe
反义词: (No direct antonym for a specific garment)


词性: n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French chasuble, from Late Latin casubula, from Latin casula "small house, hut, cloak," diminutive of casa "house, cottage, hut."  The original meaning referred to a hooded outer garment.
