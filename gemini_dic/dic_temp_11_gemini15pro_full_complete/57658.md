drawbore:/ˈdrɔːbɔːr/
noun|榫钉孔|A hole drilled offset from a primary hole, used in conjunction with a peg to draw two pieces of timber tightly together.|The carpenter used a drawbore to secure the joint. 木匠用榫钉孔来固定接头。|Synonyms: dowel joint,  mortise and tenon|Antonyms: N/A|n.
drawbore:/ˈdrɔːbɔːr/
verb|用榫钉固定|To fasten two pieces of timber together using a drawbore and peg.|He drawbored the two beams together. 他用榫钉把两根梁固定在一起。|Synonyms: peg, pin, fasten|Antonyms: loosen, unfasten, separate|v.


ETYMOLOGY->
From "draw" (Old English *dragan, to pull) + "bore" (Old English borian, to pierce).  The term refers to the action of the peg drawing the timber pieces together.
