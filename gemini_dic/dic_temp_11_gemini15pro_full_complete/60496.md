ekebergite:/ˈɛkəˌbɜːrɡaɪt/
noun
埃克伯格岩
A rare scapolite-group mineral, a sodium calcium chloro-aluminosilicate, typically found in igneous and metamorphic rocks.  It is named after the locality Ekeberg, Norway, where it was first discovered.
Ekebergite crystals were found embedded in the syenite.  埃克伯格岩晶体被发现嵌在正长岩中。
Ekebergite is a relatively rare mineral. 埃克伯格岩是一种相对稀有的矿物。
Synonyms: scapolite, meionite, mizzonite
