illiberalize:/ɪˈlɪbərəlaɪz/
verb
使不自由；限制；变得不宽容
To make less liberal; to restrict freedoms or become less tolerant.
The government's new policies illiberalize the press. 政府的新政策限制了新闻自由。
illiberalized:/ɪˈlɪbərəlaɪzd/
verb (past tense)
使不自由化了；限制了；变得不宽容了
Made less liberal; restricted freedoms or became less tolerant.
The education system was illiberalized under the previous regime.  教育体系在前政权下变得不自由。
illiberalizing:/ɪˈlɪbərəlaɪzɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
使不自由化中；限制中；变得不宽容中
Making less liberal; restricting freedoms or becoming less tolerant.
The illiberalizing policies are causing concern among human rights groups. 这些限制自由的政策引起了人权组织的担忧。
illiberalization:/ɪˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
noun
不自由化；限制；变得不宽容
The process of becoming less liberal; the restriction of freedoms or a decrease in tolerance.
The illiberalization of society is a worrying trend. 社会不自由化的趋势令人担忧。

近义词: restrict, repress, constrain, curtail
反义词: liberalize, free, emancipate, empower
词性: verb, noun (derived forms)


ETYMOLOGY->
From il- (not) + liberal + -ize (to make or become).  The prefix "il-" is a variant of "in-" which becomes "il-" before "l". "Liberal" comes from Latin "liberalis" meaning "of freemen". "-ize" is a suffix used to form verbs meaning "to make or become".
