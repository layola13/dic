nanyang:/ˈnɑːnˈjɑːŋ/ | n. |南洋|A term historically used to refer to the geographical region of Southeast Asia, particularly the Malay Archipelago, including present-day Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, Brunei, and the Philippines. It translates literally to "South Sea" in Chinese. |  Early Chinese traders and migrants referred to Southeast Asia as Nanyang. 早期的中国商人和移民将东南亚称为南洋。| synonyms: Southeast Asia, Malay Archipelago | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a geographical location) | noun
