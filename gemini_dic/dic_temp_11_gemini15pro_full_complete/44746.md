crumble:/ˈkrʌmbəl/ (US), /ˈkrʌmbl/ (UK)| v. n.|崩溃，破碎，瓦解；碎屑|To break or fall apart into small fragments, or to cause something to do this.  The building crumbled after the earthquake.  The cookies crumbled as I picked them up. |The empire crumbled under the weight of its own corruption.  帝国在其自身腐败的重压下崩溃了。|Near synonyms: disintegrate, collapse, fall apart|Near antonyms: solidify, strengthen|Verb, Noun

crumbled:/ˈkrʌmbəld/ (US), /ˈkrʌmbəld/ (UK)| adj.|破碎的，崩溃的|Broken or fallen apart into small fragments. The crumbled walls of the ancient city stood as a testament to time. |The cake was overbaked and crumbled easily.  蛋糕烤过头了，很容易碎。|Near synonyms: broken, fragmented, disintegrated|Near antonyms: whole, intact, solid|Adjective
