decentralising: /diːˈsɛntrəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 分散化，去中心化 | The process of distributing power or control away from a central authority. | The company is decentralising its operations to give more autonomy to regional offices.  公司正在将其业务分散化，以便赋予区域办事处更大的自主权。 | distributing, dispersing | centralising, consolidating | verb

decentralise: /diːˈsɛntrəlaɪz/ | v. | 分散化，去中心化 | To distribute power or control away from a central authority. | The government decided to decentralise control of education. 政府决定将教育控制权下放。 | distribute, disperse | centralise, consolidate | verb

decentralised: /diːˈsɛntrəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 分散的，去中心化的 | Having power or control distributed away from a central authority. | They have a decentralised system of management. 他们有一个分散的管理系统。 | distributed, dispersed | centralised, consolidated | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- (meaning "removal or reversal") + centralise (from centre + -ise).  The word "centre" ultimately derives from the Greek word "kéntron" meaning "sharp point, spur, stationary point of a pair of compasses."
