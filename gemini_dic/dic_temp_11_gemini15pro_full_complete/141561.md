opposite:/ˈɒpəzɪt/ (UK) /ˈɑːpəzɪt/ (US)| adj. n. prep. adv.|相反的，对面的；对立面，反面；在…的对面|A situation, quality, or feeling that is as different as possible from another one.|例句：My brother and I have opposite personalities. 我和我哥哥性格截然相反。|近义词：contrary, reverse, inverse, converse|反义词：same, similar, alike|词性:形容词

opposite:/ˈɒpəzɪt/ (UK) /ˈɑːpəzɪt/ (US)| n.|对立面，反面|Something that is the opposite of something else.|例句：Hot is the opposite of cold.  热是冷的反面。|近义词：contrary, reverse, antithesis|反义词：same, equivalent|词性:名词

opposite:/ˈɒpəzɪt/ (UK) /ˈɑːpəzɪt/ (US)| prep.|在…的对面|Across from or facing something.|例句：The bank is opposite the post office. 银行在邮局对面。|近义词：across from, facing|反义词：behind, beyond|词性:介词

opposite:/ˈɒpəzɪt/ (UK) /ˈɑːpəzɪt/ (US)| adv.|在对面|On the other side; across.|例句：He lives opposite. 他住在对面。|近义词：across, facing|反义词：behind, beyond|词性:副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *oposite, from Latin oppositus (“placed against, opposed”), past participle of opponere (“to oppose, set against”), from ob- (“against”) + ponere (“to put, place”).
