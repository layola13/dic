jump-ship:/ˌdʒʌmp ˈʃɪp/ | v. | 跳槽，背叛，离开 | To abandon a job, cause, or enterprise, often to join a competing one. |  He jumped ship after only six months with the company to work for their biggest rival.  他只在公司工作了六个月就跳槽到他们最大的竞争对手那里去了。

| 近义词: defect, desert, abandon | 反义词: stay, remain, support | 词性: 动词

jump-ship:/ˌdʒʌmp ˈʃɪp/ | n. | 跳槽行为 | The act of abandoning a job, cause, or enterprise, often to join a competing one. | The company suffered a wave of jump ships after the new CEO took over.  新任首席执行官上任后，公司经历了一波跳槽潮。

| 近义词: desertion, defection | 反义词: loyalty, commitment | 词性: 名词
