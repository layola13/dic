americanizers:/əˈmɛrɪkəˌnaɪzərz/
n.
美国化者；使美国化的人
People who promote or impose American culture, values, or customs on others.
The americanizers sought to assimilate new immigrants quickly.  美国化者试图迅速同化新移民。
The americanizers distributed pamphlets promoting American values.  美国化者分发了宣传美国价值观的宣传册。
近义词:assimilators, westernizers
反义词:traditionalists, nationalists
词性:名词(plural)

americanizer:/əˈmɛrɪkəˌnaɪzər/
n.
美国化者；使美国化的人
A person who promotes or imposes American culture, values, or customs on others.
He was seen as an americanizer by the local community. 他被当地社区视为美国化者。
She worked as an americanizer for the government, helping new immigrants adapt to American life.  她为政府担任美国化者，帮助新移民适应美国生活。
近义词:assimilator, westernizer
反义词:traditionalist, nationalist
词性:名词(singular)

词源:
From Americanize + -er.  Americanize itself comes from American + -ize.  The suffix "-ize" means "to make or become," while "-er" denotes a person who performs an action.  Ultimately derived from "America,"  named after Amerigo Vespucci.
