confounder:/kənˈfaʊndər/ | n. | 混杂因素，干扰因素 |  A confounder is a variable that influences both the dependent variable and independent variable, causing a spurious association.  |  Smoking is a confounder in the relationship between coffee drinking and heart disease. 吸烟是咖啡饮用与心脏病之间关系的混杂因素。| synonym: extraneous variable, lurking variable, confounding factor | antonym: controlled variable | noun


confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/ | vt. | 使困惑，使惊讶，挫败 | To cause surprise or confusion in (someone), especially by acting against their expectations. | The sudden drop in sales confounded the company's projections. 销售额的突然下降让公司的预测感到困惑。| synonym: confuse, bewilder, perplex, frustrate | antonym: clarify, enlighten | verb
