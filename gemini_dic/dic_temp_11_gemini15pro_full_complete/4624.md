aldermanly:/ˈɔːldərmənli/ | adj. | 市議員般的；像市議員的；有市議員風範的 |  Like or befitting an alderman; dignified and portly, often implying a sense of self-importance or pomposity.  |  He had an aldermanly air about him, with his large belly and booming voice. 他有着一股市議員般的氣派，大腹便便，聲音洪亮。 | synonyms: dignified, portly, magisterial, imposing, pompous | antonyms: undignified, humble, unassuming, modest | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From alderman + -ly.  Alderman comes from Old English ealdorman "chieftain, governor," from ealdor "elder, chief, prince" (from Proto-Germanic *aldiz "old, grown up") + man "man."
