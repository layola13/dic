corsair:/ˈkɔːrseər/ (US) /ˈkɔːsɛː(r)/ (UK)| n. |海盗，私掠船|A pirate, especially one of the Barbary Coast, or their ship.  Historically, a privateer authorized by a North African ruler to attack the ships of Christian nations.|The corsairs plundered merchant ships in the Mediterranean.  那些海盗在地中海掠夺商船。|Synonyms: pirate, buccaneer, privateer, rover|Antonyms: merchant, sailor (in lawful trade)|noun
