liberties:/ˈlɪbərtiz/
n.|自由，自由权；放肆，失礼|The fundamental liberties guaranteed by the Constitution. 宪法保障的基本自由。|
例句：They fought for their liberties. 他们为自由而战。
例句：He took the liberty of borrowing your car without asking. 他擅自借用了你的车，没有事先征求你的同意。
近义词：freedoms, rights, privileges
反义词：restrictions, constraints
词性:名词(plural)


liberties:/ˈlɪbərtiz/
n.|特权，优惠；(复数)大胆的举动，冒昧的行为|He took unwarranted liberties with the text. 他对文本进行了不必要的篡改。|
例句：I hope you don't mind me taking the liberty of calling you by your first name. 希望你不介意我冒昧地直呼你的名字。
例句：The scriptwriter took some liberties with the original story. 编剧对原著故事进行了一些改编。
近义词：presumption, audacity, boldness
反义词：respect, restraint
词性:名词(plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "liberté,"  from Latin "libertas" ("freedom"), derived from "liber" ("free").
