ETYMOLOGY->
From "grass" (referring to the availability of women for casual sexual encounters in open areas like fields) + "widowhood" (the state of being a widow). The term originally carried derogatory connotations, implying that the separated wife might be more sexually available, but this implication has largely faded in modern usage.
