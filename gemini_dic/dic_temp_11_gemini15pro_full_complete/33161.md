chromatophore:/ˈkrəʊmətəfɔːr/ (US) /ˈkroʊmətəfɔːr/ (UK)| n. |色素体；载色体|A pigment-containing cell or group of cells, especially one that can change color, found in some animals such as amphibians, reptiles, fish, and cephalopods.|Chameleons change color by controlling their chromatophores. 变色龙通过控制它们的色素体来改变颜色。|
The octopus uses its chromatophores for camouflage. 章鱼利用色素体进行伪装。|
Synonyms: pigment cell, melanophore, iridophore|
Antonyms: N/A|
