non-metaphorical:/ˌnɒn ˌmetəˈfɒrɪkəl/ /ˌnɑːn ˌmetəˈfɔːrɪkəl/| adj.|非比喻的，字面的|Literally true, not metaphorical or figurative.  Relating to the explicit meaning of a word or phrase, rather than a symbolic or implied meaning.|Her response was non-metaphorical; she meant exactly what she said. 她的回答是字面意思；她就是她说的意思。|literal, direct, plain, explicit|figurative, metaphorical, symbolic|adjective
Etymology->
non-:  Prefix meaning "not." From Latin *nōn*.
metaphorical:  From metaphor + -ical.  Metaphor comes from Greek *metaphora*, meaning "transfer."
