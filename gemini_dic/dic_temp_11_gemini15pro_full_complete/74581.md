fray:/freɪ/
n.|磨损，争吵，冲突|a situation of intense activity, typically one incorporating elements of aggression or competition|The edges of the carpet were starting to fray.|磨损：地毯的边缘开始磨损。|近义词：fight, quarrel, clash, scuffle|反义词：peace, harmony|词性:名词

fray:/freɪ/
v.|（使布料、绳索等）磨损，磨破；（使）烦躁，恼火；（神经等）紧张，（耐心）逐渐消失|to (cause to) become worn away or unravel at the edge|The cuffs of his jacket were fraying.|磨损：他的夹克袖口磨损了。|近义词：unravel, wear, erode|反义词：mend, repair|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *freien, from Old French *freier, *froeier (“to rub”), from Frankish *frijōn (“to rub”), from Proto-Germanic *frijōną (“to rub”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰrī́yeti (“to rub”). Cognate with Old High German *frīen (“to rub, stroke”). Related to fret.
