genipap: /ˈdʒɛnɪpæp/ | n. | 染料木; 染料木的果实 | A tropical American tree (Genipa americana) of the madder family, cultivated for its edible fruit and for a dye obtained from the fruit.  The unripe fruit yields a black dye traditionally used for body painting and tattoos by indigenous peoples. The ripe fruit is edible and used to make drinks, jams, and desserts. |  Example 1: Indigenous people used genipap to create intricate body paintings for ceremonies.  原住民使用染料木为仪式创作复杂的彩绘。

Example 2:  Genipap juice makes a refreshing drink in the summer heat. 夏天酷暑里，染料木汁做成的饮料清凉爽口。 | 近义词: jagua | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for a specific plant. Could be contrasted with other dye sources like "indigo" or "woad" depending on context.) | 词性: 名词
