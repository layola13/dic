blustery:/ˈblʌstəri/ (US) /ˈblʌstəri/ (UK)| adj.|狂风大作的；寒冷的；喧闹的|Characterized by strong winds and usually cold weather; noisy and boisterous.|
例句：It was a blustery day, perfect for flying a kite.  这是一个刮风的日子，非常适合放风筝。

例句：The blustery wind made it difficult to walk.  狂风使得行走困难。

近义词：windy, gusty, stormy, tempestuous, boisterous|
反义词：calm, still, peaceful, quiet|
词性: adjective


bluster:/ˈblʌstər/ (US) /ˈblʌstər/ (UK)| n. vi.|咆哮；怒吼；虚张声势|To talk in a loud, aggressive, or indignant way with little effect; to blow in loud gusts.|
例句：His bluster didn't scare anyone. 他的咆哮并没有吓到任何人。

例句：The wind blustered through the trees. 风呼啸着穿过树林。

近义词：rant, rave, roar, swagger, boast|
反义词：whisper, murmur, be quiet|
词性: noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *blustren, frequentative of *blusten, "to blow loudly, to puff," likely from Old Norse *blysja, "to blaze, glow, flicker."  The sense of noisy, empty threats developed later.
