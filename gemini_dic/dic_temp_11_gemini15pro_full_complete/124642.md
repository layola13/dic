mouthier:/ˈmaʊðiər/ (US), /ˈmaʊθɪə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更爱说话的，更直言不讳的，更爱顶嘴的|Comparative form of 'mouthy': More inclined to speak freely, often in a bold or disrespectful way.  It suggests excessive talking, often in a rude or argumentative manner.|例句：He's gotten a lot mouthier since he joined the debate team.  自从他加入辩论队后，他变得更爱说话了。

例句：She was much mouthier with her parents after she went to college.  她上大学后对父母更加顶嘴了。|近义词：talkative, outspoken, impudent, sassy, cheeky|反义词：quiet, reserved, respectful, polite|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "mouth" + "-y" (suffix forming adjectives meaning "full of" or "characterized by") + "-er" (comparative suffix).  The word "mouth" comes from Old English "mūþ," from Proto-Germanic "*munþaz," from Proto-Indo-European "*ment-."
