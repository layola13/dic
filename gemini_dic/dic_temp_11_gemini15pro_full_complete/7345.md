anhydrite:/ænˈhaɪdraɪt/ | n. |无水石膏|A mineral consisting of anhydrous calcium sulfate, CaSO4.  It is commonly found in evaporite deposits.|The anhydrite was mined and used in the production of plaster.|Synonyms:  calcium sulfate, anhydrous gypsum|Antonyms: gypsum|noun


anhydrous:/ænˈhaɪdrəs/ | adj. |无水的| (of a substance, especially a crystalline compound) containing no water of crystallization.|Anhydrous copper sulfate is white.|Synonyms:  waterless, desiccated, dehydrated|Antonyms:  hydrous, hydrated|adjective
