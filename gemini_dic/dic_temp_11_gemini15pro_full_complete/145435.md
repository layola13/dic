overcheap:/ˌoʊvərˈtʃiːp/| adj.|过于便宜的，价格过低的|Too cheap; cheaper than is reasonable or normal.  It implies that the low price may be suspicious or suggest poor quality.|The overcheap souvenirs looked flimsy and likely to break.|近义词：inexpensive, cheap, bargain, cut-rate|反义词：expensive, overpriced, costly|词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *overchep, from Old English *ofercēap (“over bargain, over purchase”), from ofer (“over”) + cēap (“bargain, purchase, price”).  The sense shifted over time from "buying too much" to "too cheap".
