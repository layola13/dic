egregiously: /ɪˈɡriːdʒəsli/ | adv. | 惊人地；过分地；极其恶劣地 |  in a very bad and shocking way |  He behaved egregiously, insulting the host and several other guests.  他表现得极其恶劣，侮辱了主人和其他几位客人。| 近义词: shockingly, outrageously, flagrantly, appallingly | 反义词: mildly, slightly, insignificantly | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *egregius* "distinguished, excellent, remarkable,"  from *ex* "out of" + *greg* (root of *grex*) "flock, herd." The original meaning was "standing out from the herd," which later evolved to mean "exceptional," and eventually, through ironic usage, "exceptionally bad."
