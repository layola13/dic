collectivization:/kəˌlɛktɪvɪˈzeɪʃən/| n.|集体化|The process of organizing something, such as agriculture or industry, on the basis of collective ownership or management.|Example 1: The collectivization of farms in the Soviet Union led to widespread famine. 苏联的农场集体化导致了大范围的饥荒。

Example 2:  The government's push for collectivization met with resistance from many farmers. 政府推行的集体化遭到了许多农民的抵制。|近义词：communization, nationalization, socialization|反义词: privatization, individualization|词性:noun


collectivize:/kəˈlɛktɪvaɪz/| v.|集体化|To organize something, such as agriculture or industry, on the basis of collective ownership or management.|Example 1: The government collectivized the farms. 政府将农场集体化了。

Example 2: They plan to collectivize the production of certain crops. 他们计划将某些作物的生产集体化。|近义词：communize, nationalize, socialize|反义词: privatize, individualize|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From collective + -ize + -ation.  "Collective" comes from the Latin "collectivus," meaning "gathered together." "-ize" is a suffix used to form verbs meaning "to make or become." "-ation" is a suffix used to form nouns from verbs, indicating the process or result of the action.
