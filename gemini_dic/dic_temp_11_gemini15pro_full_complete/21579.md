bluff:/blʌf/| n. v. adj.|虚张声势，悬崖，吓唬；欺骗；陡峭的|A high, steep bank or cliff. To deceive someone by pretending to be stronger or more confident than one really is.  Rough and blunt in manner.|He climbed up the bluff overlooking the ocean. 他爬上了可以俯瞰大海的悬崖。|近义词：cliff, pretense, deceive, intimidate, gruff |反义词:valley, honesty, sincerity, gentle, courteous|词性:名词, 动词, 形容词|词源:Probably from Dutch *bluffen “to boast, brag,” of uncertain origin.|
