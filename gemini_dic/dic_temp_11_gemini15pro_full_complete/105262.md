khedive: /kəˈdiːv/ | n. |总督，赫迪夫（埃及总督的头衔）| A viceroy or governor of Egypt, especially from 1867 to 1914.  Formerly a Turkish title, it later became hereditary under the Ottoman Empire. |  The khedive ruled Egypt with considerable autonomy. 埃及总督拥有相当大的自治权。

The khedive commissioned the building of the Suez Canal. 总督下令建造苏伊士运河。 | Near synonyms: viceroy, governor, ruler | Antonyms: subject, citizen | Noun
