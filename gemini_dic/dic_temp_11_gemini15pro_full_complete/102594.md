jailbird:/ˈdʒeɪlbɜːrd/
n.
囚犯，惯犯
A person who has spent a lot of time in prison.
He’s a jailbird who’s been in and out of prison for years. 他是个惯犯，多年来一直进进出出监狱。

The notorious jailbird was finally apprehended.  这个臭名昭著的惯犯终于被逮捕了。

近义词:convict, prisoner, inmate, criminal, felon
反义词:law-abiding citizen
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From jail + bird.  First recorded in the 1670s.  The "bird" element likely refers to a caged bird.
