hardboiled:/ˈhɑːrdbɔɪld/| adj.|煮老的，强硬的，冷酷的| (of an egg) cooked until the yolk and white are solid; (of a person) tough and unsentimental; (of a detective story) dealing with crime and violence in a realistic and unsentimental way|  The detective in the novel was a hardboiled character. 小说中的侦探是个冷酷的角色。|近义词：tough, callous, cynical|反义词：soft, sentimental, compassionate|词性:形容词
Etymology:  "hard" + "boiled".  The original literal meaning referred to eggs cooked until solid.  The figurative meaning, referring to a tough, unsentimental person, developed in the early 20th century, likely influenced by the image of a hard-boiled egg being difficult to crack.
