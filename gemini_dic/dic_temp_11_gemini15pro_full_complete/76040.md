furler's:/ˈfɜːrlərz/
noun
卷帆索的；收帆装置的
belonging to or associated with a furler (a device for rolling up a sail)
The furler's line snapped during the storm.  暴风雨中，卷帆索断了。
近义词:sail-rolling gear's, reefing gear's
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "unfurler's" if such a device existed)
词性:noun (possessive form)


Etymology:
From "furler" + "-'s" (possessive suffix). "Furler" comes from the verb "furl," meaning to roll up (a sail). "Furl" originates from the Old French word "ferler," and ultimately from the Frankish "*firlan."
