fieldwork: /ˈfiːldwɜːrk/ | n. | 野外工作；实地考察 | Practical work conducted by a researcher in the natural environment, rather than in a laboratory or office. |  Geologists conduct fieldwork to study rock formations. 地质学家进行野外工作来研究岩层。

fieldwork: /ˈfiːldwɜːrk/ | n. | 田野调查；现场工作 | Work done in the field, such as social research or marketing surveys. | The anthropologist conducted extensive fieldwork in remote villages.  这位人类学家在偏远村庄进行了广泛的田野调查。


ETYMOLOGY->
From "field" + "work". "Field" comes from Old English "feld" meaning open country. "Work" comes from Old English "worc" meaning activity involving mental or physical effort done in order to achieve a purpose or result.

SYNONYMS->
field study, field research, ground truthing, practical work, on-site work, location shooting, hands-on experience, outdoor work

ANTONYMS->
lab work, office work, theoretical study, desk research, indoor work
