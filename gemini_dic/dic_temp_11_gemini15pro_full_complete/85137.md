hardline:/ˈhɑːrdlaɪn/ | adj. n. |强硬路线的，强硬派的；强硬路线，强硬派|uncompromising; adhering rigidly to a dogma, policy, or opinion|例句：He took a hardline stance against the protestors. 他对示威者采取了强硬立场。|近义词：inflexible, uncompromising, unyielding, firm, resolute|反义词：moderate, flexible, compromising, conciliatory|词性：形容词/名词

hard-liner:/ˈhɑːrdˌlaɪnər/ | n. |强硬派人士|a person who adheres rigidly to a dogma, policy, or opinion|例句：Hard-liners in the party opposed any compromise. 党内的强硬派人士反对任何妥协。|近义词：extremist, zealot, diehard|反义词：moderate, centrist|词性：名词

词源:  "hard" + "line."  The "line" refers to a policy or position.  The term gained prominence during the Cold War.
