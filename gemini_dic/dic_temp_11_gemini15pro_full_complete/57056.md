doubting-thomases:/ˈdaʊtɪŋ ˈtɑməsəz/| n.|怀疑者，不相信的人|A doubting Thomas is someone who habitually or instinctively doubts or questions. This term originates from the biblical story of Thomas the Apostle, who refused to believe that Jesus had resurrected until he had seen and touched his wounds.|Many were present at the demonstration, but the doubting Thomases remained unconvinced.|近义词：skeptics, cynics, doubters|反义词:believers, trusting individuals|noun
