d-glucoses:/diːˈɡluːkoʊsiz/| n. |D-葡萄糖（复数）|A simple sugar that is an important energy source in living organisms and is a component of many carbohydrates.  It's the dextrorotatory isomer of glucose.  The "d-" prefix indicates the configuration of the molecule.  Often just called "glucose".|D-glucoses are the primary source of energy for the brain. D-葡萄糖是大脑主要的能量来源。|
D-glucoses are essential for cellular respiration.  D-葡萄糖对细胞呼吸至关重要。|
Synonyms: dextrose, grape sugar|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a specific sugar, but perhaps consider "non-carbohydrate" in certain contexts) |noun
