onboard:/ˈɒnbɔːd/ (UK), /ˈɑːnbɔːrd/ (US)| adj. adv.| 在船上，在飞机上，在火车上；加入，融入|On a vehicle such as a ship, aircraft, or train. Also means integrated or part of a group or team.|例句：The passengers were all safely onboard the plane. 所有乘客都安全登机了。|近义词：aboard, on, in|反义词：offboard, ashore, disembarked|词性:形容词/副词

onboard:/ˈɒnbɔːd/ (UK), /ˈɑːnbɔːrd/ (US)| vt.| 使上船/飞机/火车，使加入|To take on board a vehicle or to integrate into a group.|例句：We will onboard the new employees next week. 我们下周将让新员工入职。|近义词：enroll, integrate, induct|反义词：disembark, discharge|词性:动词

词源：on + board.  "Board" originally referred to the side of a ship.  Thus, "on board" literally meant on the side of the ship, and by extension, on the ship itself.  The meaning later broadened to include other vehicles and then metaphorically to joining a group or enterprise.
