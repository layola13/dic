dandy:/ˈdændi/ | n. | 花花公子，纨绔子弟 | A man who is excessively concerned with his appearance and clothes. | He was such a dandy, always impeccably dressed and groomed. 他真是个花花公子，总是衣着光鲜，打扮得一丝不苟。 | synonyms: fop, coxcomb, popinjay | antonyms: slob, ragamuffin | noun

dandy:/ˈdændi/ | adj. |  极好的，一流的 | Excellent; first-rate. (informal) |  That's a dandy idea! 那真是个好主意！| synonyms: excellent, great, superb, splendid, marvelous | antonyms: awful, terrible, dreadful | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->

Origin uncertain. Possibly from the Scottish and Northern English dialect noun *dandiprat*, meaning "small or insignificant fellow," of unknown origin.  First recorded in the late 18th century.
