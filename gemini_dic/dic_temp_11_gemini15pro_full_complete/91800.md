hyperfederalists:/ˌhaɪpərˈfɛdərəlɪsts/| n.|极端联邦主义者|Supporters of a strong central government and advocates for greater federal power over states' rights.  Those who favored the adoption of the U.S. Constitution and a strong national government.|The hyperfederalists believed that a powerful central government was essential for the stability and prosperity of the new nation.|近义词：Federalists, Nationalists|反义词: Anti-Federalists, States' rights advocates|Noun
