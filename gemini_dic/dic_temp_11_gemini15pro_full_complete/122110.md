mistletoe:/ˈmɪsltəʊ/ (UK), /ˈmɪsəlˌtoʊ/ (US)| n. |槲寄生|A leathery-leaved parasitic plant that grows on apple, oak, and other broadleaf trees and bears white glutinous berries in winter.  It is traditionally hung at Christmas, and people kiss under it.|We hung mistletoe in the doorway.|Synonyms:  None (specific plant)|Antonyms:  None|Noun

mistletoes:/ˈmɪsltəʊz/ (UK), /ˈmɪsəlˌtoʊz/ (US)| n. |槲寄生（复数）|Plural form of mistletoe.|There were several bunches of mistletoes hanging in the hall.|Synonyms: None (specific plant, plural)|Antonyms: None|Noun
