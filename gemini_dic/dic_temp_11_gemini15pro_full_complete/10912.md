aramaism:/ˈærəˌmeɪɪzəm/ | n. | 阿拉姆语 | An ancient Semitic language, closely related to Hebrew, that was the lingua franca of much of the Middle East from the 8th century BC to the 7th century AD. |  Example: Aramaism was the language Jesus likely spoke.  耶稣可能说阿拉姆语。 | Example: Scholars study Aramaism to understand ancient texts. 学者们研究阿拉姆语以理解古代文献。 | Synonym: Syriac (in some contexts) | Antonym: N/A | noun
