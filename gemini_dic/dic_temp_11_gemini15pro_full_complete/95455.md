incisivenesses:/ɪnˈsaɪsɪvnɪsɪz/
|n.|深刻，敏锐，犀利|The quality of being incisive; penetrating, clear, and sharp, especially in thought or expression.|Her incisivenesses in the debate impressed everyone. 她在辩论中的犀利给每个人留下了深刻的印象。|近义词：acuteness, sharpness, penetration, acuity|反义词：vagueness, dullness, obtuseness|词性:名词

词源:
From incisive + -ness.  Incisive comes from Late Latin incisivus "cutting, sharp," from Latin incis-, past participle stem of incidere "to cut into," from in- "into" + caedere "to cut."
