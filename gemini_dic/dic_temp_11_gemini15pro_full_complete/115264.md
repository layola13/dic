manipulus:/məˈnɪpjʊləs/| n. |一把；一小队；一队士兵；一捆；一束|A handful, a small band, a company of soldiers, a bundle, a sheaf.  It originally referred to a handful of hay used as a standard, later applied to a company of soldiers in the Roman army and also to a bundle or sheaf.|The centurion commanded a manipulus of soldiers. 百夫长指挥着一队士兵。|Synonyms: handful, band, company, detachment, bundle, sheaf|Antonyms: multitude, horde|noun

manipulus:/məˈnɪpjʊləs/| n. | (在古罗马) 一组士兵（约60至120人）| A unit of soldiers in the ancient Roman army, numbering from 60 to 120 men. This is the most common historical meaning of the word. | Caesar divided his legion into several manipuli. 凯撒把他的军团分成几队士兵。 | Synonyms: cohort, century, company | Antonyms: legion, army | noun
