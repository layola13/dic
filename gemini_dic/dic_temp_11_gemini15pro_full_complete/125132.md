multi-partism: /ˈmʌltiˈpɑːtɪzəm/ | n. | 多党制 | A system in which multiple political parties have the capacity to gain control of government offices, separately or in coalition. |  India is a classic example of multi-partism, with numerous national and regional parties vying for power. 印度是多党制的典型例子，许多国家和地区政党都在争夺权力。

Multi-partism can lead to more representative governments, but it can also create political instability. 多党制可以导致更具代表性的政府，但也可能造成政治不稳定。 | Synonyms: multi-party system, pluralistic system | Antonyms: one-party system, single-party state | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From multi- (many) + parti (party) + -ism (system, doctrine).  The "multi-" prefix is from Latin multus "much, many."  "Parti" comes from Old French partie "part, share, side," ultimately from Latin pars "part." "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine.
