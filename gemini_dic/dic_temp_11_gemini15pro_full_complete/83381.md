gyrus:/ˈdʒaɪrəs/| n. |脑回|A convoluted ridge between anatomical grooves.|The hippocampus is located in the medial temporal lobe, and consists of the dentate gyrus, the hippocampus proper (Cornu Ammonis), and the subiculum.|近义词:convolution, ridge|反义词:sulcus|词性:noun

gyri:/ˈdʒaɪraɪ/| n. |脑回（复数）|Plural of gyrus; convoluted ridges between anatomical grooves.|The gyri and sulci of the brain increase the surface area of the cerebral cortex.|近义词:convolutions, ridges|反义词:sulci|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin gyrus, from Ancient Greek γῦρος (gûros, “circle, ring”), from Proto-Indo-European *gew- (“to bend, curve”).
