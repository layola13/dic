overreported:/ˌoʊvərɪˈpɔːrtɪd/ | adj. | 过度报道的，夸大报道的 | reported more than is true or reasonable; exaggerated in reporting | The effects of the new policy were overreported in the media.  媒体过度报道了新政策的效果。|Synonyms: exaggerated, inflated, overstated, overblown | Antonyms: underreported, downplayed, minimized | word type: adjective

overreported:/ˌoʊvərɪˈpɔːrtɪd/ | v. | 过度报道，夸大报道 (past participle) | having reported more than is true or reasonable | The incident was overreported by several news outlets.  这起事件被几家新闻媒体过度报道了。|Synonyms: exaggerated, inflated, overstated, overblown | Antonyms: underreported, downplayed, minimized | word type: verb (past participle)


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + reported, the past participle of the verb report.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively," "too much." "Report" comes from the Old French word "reporter," meaning "to carry back," from the Latin "reportare."
