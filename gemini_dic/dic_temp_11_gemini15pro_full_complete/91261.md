hydrofoil:/ˈhaɪdrəfɔɪl/ | n. | 水翼船；水翼 | A boat that rises out of the water on hydrofoils when it reaches a certain speed. | The hydrofoil skimmed across the waves. 水翼船掠过波浪。 | synonyms: hydrofoil boat, surface-effect ship | antonyms: submarine, submersible | noun

hydrofoil:/ˈhaɪdrəfɔɪl/ | n. | 水翼 | A wing-like structure on a boat that lifts the hull out of the water at high speeds, reducing drag.  |  The hydrofoils lifted the boat out of the water. 水翼将船抬出了水面。 | synonyms: wing, foil, fin | antonyms: hull, keel | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From hydro- (water) + -foil (leaf, blade), from French feuille (leaf). Ultimately from Latin folium (leaf).
