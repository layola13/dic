mediocrist:/ˌmiːdiˈɒkrɪst/ | n. | 平庸的人；庸才 | A person of mediocre abilities or achievement, especially one who is satisfied with mediocrity. | He was just a mediocrist, content with a mundane job and a simple life. 他只是一个平庸的人，满足于平凡的工作和简单的生活。|Synonyms: nonentity, nobody, cipher | Antonyms: genius, prodigy, expert | Noun

mediocrity:/ˌmiːdiˈɒkrɪti/ | n. | 平庸；平凡；普通 | The quality or state of being mediocre; of only moderate quality; not very good. | His performance was marked by mediocrity. 他的表现很平庸。| Synonyms: ordinariness, averageness, commonplaceness | Antonyms: excellence, brilliance, superiority | Noun
