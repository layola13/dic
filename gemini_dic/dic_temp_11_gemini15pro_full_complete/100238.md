interrupted: /ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪd/ | v. adj. | 中断，打断；被打断的 | stop the continuous progress of (an activity or process). | The loud noise interrupted my concentration.  喧闹的声音打断了我的注意力。 | synonyms: disrupted, disturbed, broken, ceased, hindered | antonyms: continued, resumed, proceeded, encouraged, facilitated | verb

interrupted: /ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪd/ | adj. | 中断的，被打断的 | having been disrupted or stopped. |  My sleep was interrupted by a phone call. 我的睡眠被一个电话打断了。 | synonyms: disrupted, disturbed, broken, incomplete, unfinished | antonyms: continuous, uninterrupted, complete, finished, whole | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *interruptus, past participle of interrumpere (“to break off, interrupt”), from Latin inter (“between, among”) + rumpere (“to break”).
