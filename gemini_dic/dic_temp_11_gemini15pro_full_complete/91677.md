hyperclassicality:/ˌhaɪpərˌklæsɪˈkælɪti/ | n. | 超古典主义 |  A heightened or exaggerated form of classicism, often characterized by extreme adherence to traditional forms and principles, sometimes to the point of artificiality or sterility.  |  The composer's later works displayed a marked hyperclassicality, eschewing any hint of modernism. 作曲家后期的作品表现出明显的超古典主义，避免任何现代主义的痕迹。

The architect's hyperclassicality resulted in a building that felt more like a museum piece than a functional space.  这位建筑师的超古典主义导致这座建筑感觉更像一件博物馆藏品，而不是一个功能性空间。|  classicism, formalism, traditionalism | modernism, avant-gardism | noun
