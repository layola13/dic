dibbler:/ˈdɪblər/| n.|袋食蚁兽; 点播机; 偷窃者|A small, nocturnal marsupial native to Australia, known for its long snout and pointed ears.  It primarily feeds on insects and other invertebrates. Also, a tool used for making small holes in the ground for planting seeds or bulbs.  Figuratively, it can refer to someone who pilfers or steals in small quantities.|The dibbler is an endangered species. 袋食蚁兽是一种濒危物种。

The gardener used a dibbler to plant the tulip bulbs. 园丁用点播机种植郁金香球茎。

He was known as a dibbler, always taking small amounts of money from the cash register. 他被称为小偷，总是从收银机里偷少量钱。|近义词：thief, pilferer, planter (tool)|反义词:donor, giver (figurative)|词性:noun
