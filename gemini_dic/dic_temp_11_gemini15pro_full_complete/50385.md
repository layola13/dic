deportment:/dɪˈpɔːtmənt/ /dɪˈpɔːrtmənt/| n. |举止，行为，风度； deportments (plural) |A person's behavior or manners.  The way someone stands and walks, especially in a formal situation. |She was praised for her graceful deportment. 她因举止优雅而受到称赞。|synonym: bearing, conduct, demeanor, manner, posture|antonym: impropriety, rudeness|noun

deportment:/dɪˈpɔːtmənt/ /dɪˈpɔːrtmənt/| n. |(旧)部门，系，(尤指大学的)|A department, especially in a university. (archaic usage) |The Deportment of Biology had several distinguished professors. 生物系有几位杰出的教授。|synonym: department, faculty, division|antonym: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *deportement*, from *deporter* "to behave, carry oneself," from Latin *deportare* "to carry away," from *de-* "away" + *portare* "to carry." The meaning shifted from the act of carrying oneself to the manner of carrying oneself.
