forebodingness:/fɔːrˈboʊdɪŋnɪs/| n. |预感, 厄运感, 不祥之兆|The quality of being foreboding; a feeling that something bad is going to happen.|A sense of forebodingness hung over the town after the mayor's mysterious disappearance. 市长神秘失踪后，一种不祥的预感笼罩着整个小镇。|Synonyms: premonition, apprehension, presentiment|Antonyms: calmness, tranquility, peace|noun

foreboding:/fɔːrˈboʊdɪŋ/| adj. n. |预感的, 不祥的; 预感|adjective: predicting something bad; ominous.  noun: a feeling that something bad will happen.  |The dark clouds were a foreboding sign of the storm to come. 乌云是不祥之兆，预示着暴风雨即将来临。  He had a foreboding that something terrible was about to happen. 他有一种不祥的预感，觉得可怕的事情即将发生。 |Synonyms: ominous, portentous, inauspicious (adj.); premonition, presentiment (n.)|Antonyms: promising, auspicious, encouraging (adj.); calmness, tranquility (n.)|adjective, noun
