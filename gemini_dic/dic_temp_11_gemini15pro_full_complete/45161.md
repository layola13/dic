cue someone in:/kjuː ˈsʌmwʌn ɪn/| phrasal verb | 通知某人；使某人了解情况 | To give someone information about something so that they are aware of what is happening. |例句：Could you cue me in on what's been happening while I was away?  你能告诉我我不在的时候发生了什么吗？

例句：Before the meeting, she cued her colleagues in on the client's expectations.  会议前，她向同事们介绍了客户的期望。|近义词：inform, fill in, brief, update |反义词: leave out, exclude |词性: 动词短语
