double-talk:/ˈdʌbəl tɔːk/ (US), /ˈdʌbəl tɔːk/ (UK)| n.|模棱两可的话，含糊其辞，胡说八道|Deliberately ambiguous or evasive language, designed to mislead or confuse.|He gave us a lot of double-talk, but no real answers. 他说了很多模棱两可的话，但没有真正的答案。|Synonyms: equivocation, ambiguity, prevarication, evasion, obfuscation|Antonyms: clarity, directness, honesty, straightforwardness|noun

double-talk:/ˈdʌbəl tɔːk/ (US), /ˈdʌbəl tɔːk/ (UK)| v.|说模棱两可的话，含糊其辞，胡说|To speak in a deliberately ambiguous or evasive way.|The politician was accused of double-talking his way through the interview.  这位政客被指责在采访中含糊其辞。|Synonyms: equivocate, prevaricate, evade, obfuscate|Antonyms: clarify, be direct, be honest, be straightforward|verb
