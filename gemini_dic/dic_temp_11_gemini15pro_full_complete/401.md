abjectednesses:/əˈbʒɛktɪdnəsɪz/| n. |卑躬屈膝， abject spirits|The abjectednesses of the refugees touched the hearts of those who witnessed their plight.  难民们的卑躬屈膝触动了那些目睹他们困境的人的心。|Synonyms: degradation, humiliation, servility|Antonyms: dignity, pride, nobility|Noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *abjectednes, from Old French abjecteté, from Late Latin abjectitās ("abjection, vileness"), from Latin abjectus ("cast down, downtrodden"), past participle of abicere ("to throw away, cast off"), from ab- ("away, from") + jacere ("to throw").
