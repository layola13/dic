override: /ˌōvərˈrīd/ (US), /ˌəʊvəˈraɪd/ (UK)| v. | 推翻，否决，覆盖，凌驾 | To use one's authority to reject or cancel (a decision, view, etc.).  To replace or supersede. | The president used his veto to override the decision of Congress. 总统用他的否决权推翻了国会的决定。 |  The emergency override system allows pilots to take manual control of the aircraft. 紧急覆盖系统允许飞行员手动控制飞机。 | synonyms: overrule, overturn, supersede, cancel, nullify, counteract | antonyms: uphold, sustain, confirm, ratify, approve | verb

override: /ˈōvərˌrīd/ (US), /ˈəʊvəˌraɪd/ (UK)| n. |  否决，覆盖，凌驾 | The act of overriding; an instance of overriding. | The chairman's override of the committee's decision caused a lot of dissent. 主席否决委员会的决定引起了很多异议。 | The system has a manual override in case of power failure. 系统在断电的情况下有手动覆盖。| synonyms: veto, cancellation, nullification | antonyms: approval, confirmation | noun
