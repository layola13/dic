jesuitisms:/ˈdʒɛzjuɪtɪzəmz/
|n.| 耶稣会主义；诡辩；遁辞|equivocation or mental reservation, especially with the intent to deceive; casuistry, often applied to the doctrines of the Jesuits.|He was accused of using Jesuitisms to avoid answering the question directly. 他被指控使用耶稣会主义来避免直接回答问题。|
|近义词:casuistry, sophistry, equivocation, quibbling|
|反义词:frankness, honesty, candor, straightforwardness|
|词性:noun|


jesuitry:/ˈdʒɛzjuɪtri/
|n.| 耶稣会主义；诡辩；遁辞|Jesuitry, like Jesuitism, refers to the use of casuistry and subtle, sometimes deceptive reasoning, often associated (rightly or wrongly) with the Jesuits.|His argument was full of jesuitry, making it difficult to trust his conclusions. 他的论点充满了诡辩，让人难以相信他的结论。|
|近义词:casuistry, sophistry, equivocation, Jesuitisms|
|反义词:frankness, honesty, candor, straightforwardness|
|词性:noun|
