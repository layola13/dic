mannered:/ˈmænərd/| adj.|有礼貌的，矫揉造作的，做作的|Having or showing a particular manner, especially an artificial or affected one.|His mannered speech and overly formal attire made him seem aloof. 他矫揉造作的讲话和过于正式的着装让他显得 aloof（冷漠，高傲）。|近义词：affected, artificial, stilted, studied, contrived|反义词：natural, unaffected, genuine, artless|词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *manered*, from *manere* (“manner”) + *-ed* (suffix forming adjectives).  Ultimately from Latin *manus* (“hand”). Originally referring to having "hands," later broadened to encompass behavior, bearing, and style.
