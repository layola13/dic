mocked: /mɑːkt/ (US) /mɒkt/ (UK) | v. | 嘲笑，讥讽 | To tease or laugh at someone in a scornful or contemptuous manner.  | He mocked her attempts at singing. 他嘲笑她唱歌的尝试。

| They mocked his accent mercilessly. 他们无情地嘲笑他的口音。

|  近义词: ridiculed, derided, taunted, jeered, scoffed at | 反义词: praised, complimented, admired | 词性: 动词

mocked: /mɑːkt/ (US) /mɒkt/ (UK) | adj. | 被嘲笑的，仿制的 |  Something that is designed to imitate or resemble something else, often in a simplified or inferior way. |  A mocked-up version of the website was used for testing.  一个仿制的网站版本被用于测试。

| The exam included a mocked-up crime scene. 考试包括一个模拟的犯罪现场。

| 近义词: imitated, simulated, feigned, sham | 反义词: genuine, authentic, real | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mokken*, frequentative of *moken* (“to mock”), of uncertain origin. Perhaps related to Old French *mocquer* (“to mock, deride, scoff at”).
