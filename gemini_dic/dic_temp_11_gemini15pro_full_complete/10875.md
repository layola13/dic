arachnidans:/əˈræknɪdənz/| n.|蛛形纲动物|A class of joint-legged invertebrate animals (Arthropoda) that includes spiders, scorpions, mites, and ticks. They are characterized by having eight legs, no antennae, and a body divided into two parts: a cephalothorax and an abdomen.|Spiders, scorpions, mites, and ticks are all arachnidans.|Near synonyms: arachnids, spider family|Antonyms: None (no direct opposite for a biological classification)|noun
