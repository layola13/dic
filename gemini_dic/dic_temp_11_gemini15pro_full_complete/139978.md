odorisi: /ˌoʊdəˈriːsi/ | n. | 踊り字 (Odoriji) | A type of Japanese wordplay involving the substitution of kanji characters with others that have the same or similar pronunciation but different meanings, often for humorous or poetic effect.  It's sometimes translated as "dancing characters." | Example: Using 歌 (uta - song) in place of  唄 (uta - song, more archaic). | Synonym:  gikun (when referring to phonetic substitution),  dajare (when referring to the punning aspect) | Antonym:  N/A | noun
