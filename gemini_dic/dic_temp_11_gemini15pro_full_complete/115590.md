marauding: /məˈrɔːdɪŋ/ | adj. | 掠夺的，抢劫的 |  (of people or animals) going around a place in search of things to steal or people to attack.  |  Marauding gangs of youths terrorized the town.  掠夺成性的青年帮派恐吓着小镇。 | Synonyms: pillaging, plundering, raiding, looting | Antonyms: protecting, guarding, defending | Adjective

marauder: /məˈrɔːdər/ | n. | 掠夺者，抢劫者 | A person who marauds; a raider. | The marauders stole everything of value. 掠夺者偷走了所有值钱的东西。 | Synonyms: raider, plunderer, pillager, looter | Antonyms: protector, guardian, defender | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *marauder*, from *maraud* "rogue, vagabond," of uncertain origin.
