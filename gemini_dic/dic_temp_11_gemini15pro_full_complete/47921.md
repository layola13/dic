deceptive:/dɪˈsɛptɪv/| adj.|欺骗性的，误导性的|Giving an appearance or impression different from the true one; misleading.|Her deceptively simple question masked a deeper meaning. 她看似简单的问题掩盖了更深层的含义。|The calm water was deceptive; strong currents flowed beneath the surface. 平静的水面是骗人的；水面下水流湍急。|近义词：misleading, deceitful, fallacious, treacherous, illusory|反义词：honest, truthful, genuine, sincere, frank|词性:形容词

词源:
From Late Middle English *deceptyf, from Old French deceptif, from Latin dēceptīvus (“fit to deceive, deceptive”), from dēcept-, perfect passive participle stem of dēcipiō (“I deceive, ensnare, beguile, betray”), from dē- (“away, down, from”) + capiō (“I take, seize”).
