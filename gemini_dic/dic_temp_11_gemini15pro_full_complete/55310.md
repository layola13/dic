dissolve:/dɪˈzɒlv/ (UK), /dɪˈzɑːlv/ (US)| v. n.|溶解，解散，消失|To become incorporated into a liquid so as to form a solution; to cause to pass into solution; to break up or terminate (something, such as a parliament, business enterprise, or marriage); to fade away; to be overcome with emotion.|例句：The sugar dissolved quickly in the hot water. 糖在热水中很快就溶解了。|近义词：melt, liquefy, disintegrate, disperse, vanish|反义词: solidify, freeze, congeal, coalesce|词性:动词, 名词

dissolve:/dɪˈzɒlv/ (UK), /dɪˈzɑːlv/ (US)|n.|溶解, 解散|The act or process of dissolving; the state of having been dissolved.|例句：The dissolve of the company left many people unemployed. 公司的解散让很多人失业了。|近义词：disintegration, dissolution|反义词: solidification, coalescence|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin dissolvere, from Latin dis- "apart" + solvere "to loosen, release, solve".
