inductility:/ˌɪndʌkˈtɪləti/ | n. |延展性，韧性 |The property of a material that allows it to be drawn into wires or hammered into thin sheets without breaking.  It describes a material's ability to deform permanently under tensile stress. |例句：Copper has high inductility, which makes it ideal for wiring.  铜具有很高的延展性，这使得它非常适合用于电线。

例句：The inductility of the metal allowed it to be shaped into intricate designs.  这种金属的延展性使其可以被塑造成复杂的图案。|近义词：malleability, ductility, plasticity |反义词: brittleness, fragility |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *inductilitas, from Latin inductus, past participle of inducere (“to lead in, bring in”), from in- + ducere (“to lead”).  Equivalent to induct + -ility.
