nonnobilities: /ˌnɒnnoʊˈbɪlɪtiz/ | n. | 非贵族, 平民 | Lack of noble qualities; the state of not belonging to the nobility.  The common people; those who are not of noble birth. | The French Revolution aimed to overthrow the nonnobilities and establish a republic. 法国大革命旨在推翻非贵族，建立共和国。| The rise of the merchant class challenged the traditional power of the nonnobilities. 商人阶层的兴起挑战了传统非贵族的权力。 | 近义词: commoners, plebians, populace | 反义词: nobility, aristocracy, elite | 词性: 名词

词源:  Formed from "non-" (not) + "nobility" (the quality of being noble).  "Nobility" comes from Old French "noblete" and ultimately from Latin "nobilitas,"  derived from "nobilis" (noble, well-known).
