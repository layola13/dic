carapace:/ˈkærəpeɪs/ | n. | 甲壳，外壳 | A hard outer covering or shell, typically of a crustacean, turtle, or arachnid.  | The turtle retreated into its carapace for protection. 海龟缩进它的壳里寻求保护。 |  The crab's carapace was brightly colored.  螃蟹的甲壳颜色鲜亮。| synonyms: shell, covering, exoskeleton | antonyms: underside, interior | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *carapace*, from Spanish *carapacho* "shell, carapace," probably from *cara* "face" (from Latin *cara*) + *pacho* (of uncertain origin, possibly related to *paja* "straw").
