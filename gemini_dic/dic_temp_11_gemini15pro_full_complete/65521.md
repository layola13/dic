eurypteroidea:/jʊˌrɪptəˈrɔɪdiə/ | n. | 广翅鲎目 | An extinct order of arthropods, related to arachnids and horseshoe crabs, that lived in aquatic environments during the Paleozoic Era. They are often called "sea scorpions," although not all were marine. |  Example: Eurypteroidea fossils have been found on every continent. 广翅鲎化石在每个大陆都被发现。| Example: Some eurypteroidea grew to enormous sizes, over two meters long.  一些广翅鲎体型巨大，超过两米长。 | Synonym: Sea scorpions (informal), Gigantostraca (obsolete) | Antonym:  N/A | noun
