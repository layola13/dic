loggerheads:/ˈlɒɡəhedz/ (UK) /ˈlɔːɡərˌhɛdz/ (US)| n.| 争吵，意见不合；蠵龟|a state of disagreement or conflict:|例句：The two countries are at loggerheads over trade. 这两个国家在贸易问题上意见不合。


例句：They’ve been at loggerheads with the management for months. 他们和管理层已经僵持了好几个月了。


|近义词：disagreement, dispute, conflict, quarrel|反义词：agreement, harmony, accord|词性: 名词


loggerhead:/ˈlɒɡəhed/ (UK) /ˈlɔːɡərˌhɛd/ (US)| n.| 笨蛋，傻瓜；蠵龟|a foolish person (archaic); a large marine turtle with a reddish-brown shell:|例句：He's such a loggerhead, he always forgets his keys. 他真是个笨蛋，总是忘记带钥匙。 (archaic usage - meaning foolish person)


例句：The loggerhead turtle is an endangered species.  蠵龟是一种濒危物种。


|近义词：(for foolish person) fool, idiot, dunce; (for turtle) Caretta caretta|反义词：(for foolish person) genius, smart person|词性: 名词
