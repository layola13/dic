mixedness:/ˈmɪkstnɪs/ | n. | 混合性，混杂性 | The state or quality of being mixed; the degree to which something is mixed. | The mixedness of the group, with people from all walks of life, created a vibrant atmosphere. 这个群体人员混杂，来自各行各业，营造了充满活力的氛围。|  heterogeneity, diversity, variety | homogeneity, uniformity | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From mixed + -ness.  "Mixed" comes from the Middle English "mixt," from the past participle of "mixen," which in turn comes from the Old English "mixan," of uncertain origin.  "-ness" is a common suffix used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
