destabilize:/diːˈsteɪbəlaɪz/
v.
使不稳定，动摇
To make something unstable or unsteady.
The new policy destabilized the economy. 新政策使经济不稳定。
近义词: undermine, weaken, disrupt, unsettle
反义词: stabilize, strengthen, secure
词性: 动词

destabilizing:/diːˈsteɪbəlaɪzɪŋ/
adj.
不稳定的，动摇的
Causing instability or unsteadiness.
The destabilizing effects of the war were felt throughout the region. 战争带来的不稳定影响波及整个地区。
近义词: disruptive, unsettling
反义词: stabilizing
词性: 形容词

destabilization:/diːˌsteɪbələˈzeɪʃən/
n.
不稳定，动摇
The process of making something unstable or unsteady.
The destabilization of the government led to widespread chaos. 政府的不稳定导致了 widespread 的混乱。
近义词: disruption, instability
反义词: stabilization
词性: 名词


词源:  From de- + stabilize, from stable (adj.) ultimately from Latin stabilis "firm, steadfast,"
