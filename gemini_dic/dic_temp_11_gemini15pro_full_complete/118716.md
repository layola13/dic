metafictionality: /ˌmetəfɪkʃəˈnælɪti/ | n. | 元小说性 | The quality of a work of fiction that self-consciously draws attention to its status as fiction.  It often involves the blurring of the lines between fiction and reality, with the author or characters acknowledging the fictional nature of the narrative. | The novel's metafictionality is evident in the narrator's frequent addresses to the reader, breaking the fourth wall and reminding us that we are engaged with a constructed story.  |  self-reflexivity, self-consciousness |  realism, naturalism | noun
