fawns:/fɔːnz/
fawn (singular):/fɔːn/| n.|幼鹿|A young deer, especially one less than a year old.|例句：The fawn hid in the tall grass. 小鹿躲在高高的草丛里。|近义词：calf, yearling|反义词: adult deer|Noun
fawn (plural):/fɔːnz/| n.|幼鹿（复数）|Young deer, especially ones less than a year old.|例句：Several fawns grazed peacefully in the meadow. 几只小鹿在草地上安静地吃草。|近义词：calves, yearlings|反义词: adult deer|Noun
fawn:/fɔːn/| v.|奉承，讨好|To seek favor or attention by flattery and obsequious behavior.|例句：The courtiers fawned over the king. 朝臣们向国王献媚。|近义词：flatter, grovel|反义词: criticize, ignore|Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fauen*, *fawne*, from Old French *faon* (modern French *faon*), from Vulgar Latin *fetō*, from Latin *fetus* (“offspring, bringing forth young, pregnancy”).  Doublet of *fetus*.
