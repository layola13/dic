honky-tonk:/ˈhɑːŋki tɑːŋk/ /ˈhɒŋki tɒŋk/| n. |低级酒吧；喧闹的场所；乡村音乐|A cheap, noisy bar or dance hall, often featuring country music.  A style of often fast and lively country music typically played in such bars. |例句：The band played all night at the local honky-tonk.  这支乐队在当地一家低级酒吧演奏了一整夜。

例句：She loved the lively atmosphere of the honky-tonk. 她喜欢低级酒吧里热闹的气氛。|近义词：bar, saloon, dive, roadhouse, juke joint |反义词: upscale bar, restaurant, club |词性:noun

honky-tonker:/ˈhɑːŋki tɑːŋkər/ /ˈhɒŋki tɒŋkər/| n. |低级酒吧的常客；乡村音乐演奏者|A person who frequents honky-tonks. A musician who plays honky-tonk music. |例句：He was a well-known honky-tonker, playing in bars across the country. 他是一位著名的乡村音乐演奏者，在全国各地的酒吧演奏。

例句：The honky-tonkers danced and drank the night away. 低级酒吧的常客们整晚跳舞喝酒。|近义词：musician, barfly, regular |反义词:teetotaler, abstainer |词性:noun
