offshore:/ˌɒfˈʃɔːr/ /ˌɔːfˈʃɔːr/| adj. adv.|近海的，离岸的；在海外的，在国外的|Situated or operating at some distance from the coast; located or based outside one's national borders for reasons of lower costs or less stringent regulations.|例句：Offshore drilling is becoming increasingly controversial. 近海钻探正变得越来越有争议。|近义词：overseas, foreign|反义词：onshore, domestic|词性:形容词/副词

offshore:/ˌɒfˈʃɔːr/ /ˌɔːfˈʃɔːr/| n.|近海，离岸|The area of sea relatively close to the shore.|例句：The wind was blowing from the offshore. 风从近海吹来。|近义词：coast, seaside|反义词：inland|词性:名词

offshore:/ˌɒfˈʃɔːr/ /ˌɔːfˈʃɔːr/| vt.|转移到海外|To move (funds or a business) to a foreign country, often to avoid taxes or regulations.|例句：Many companies offshore their manufacturing operations to reduce costs. 许多公司将制造业务转移到海外以降低成本。|近义词：relocate, transfer|反义词：repatriate|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From off + shore.  "Off" indicates separation or distance, and "shore" refers to the land bordering a body of water.
