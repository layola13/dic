eutrophication: /ˌjuːtrəfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 富营养化 |  The excessive richness of nutrients in a lake or other body of water, frequently due to runoff from the land, which causes a dense growth of plant life and death of animal life from lack of oxygen. | The algal bloom was a clear sign of eutrophication in the lake.  湖泊中藻类的大量繁殖是湖泊富营养化的明显迹象。| 近义词: hypertrophication | 反义词: oligotrophication | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From eu- (good, well) + -troph- (nourishment) + -ication (process).  Comes from Greek *eutrophos* meaning "well-nourished".
