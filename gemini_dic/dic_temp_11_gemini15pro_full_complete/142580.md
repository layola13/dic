osteocranium: /ˌɒstioʊˈkreɪniəm/ | n. | 骨颅，脑颅 | The bony portion of the cranium enclosing the brain.  The osteocranium forms by intramembranous ossification. | The osteocranium protects the brain and provides attachment points for facial muscles. |  Synonyms: neurocranium, braincase | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps viscerocranium which refers to the facial skeleton) | noun
