as fit as a fiddle:/æz ˈfɪt əz ə ˈfɪdəl/| adj.|非常健康，精神饱满|Meaning "in perfect health."  The origin of "fiddle" in this phrase refers not to the musical instrument, but is thought to be a corruption of "fittle," an old word meaning energetic or lively.|My grandfather is 90 years old, but he's still as fit as a fiddle.|Synonyms: healthy, well, strong, robust|Antonyms: ill, sick, weak, frail|adjective
