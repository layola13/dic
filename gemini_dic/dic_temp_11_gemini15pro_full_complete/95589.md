incommensurable: /ˌɪnkəˈmɛnʃərəbl/ | adj. n. | 不可公约的，不能比较的 | Not able to be judged by the same standard; having no common standard of measurement. |

例句：
The two cultures were so different as to be incommensurable.
这两种文化截然不同，无法比较。

Their values were incommensurable with ours.
他们的价值观与我们的无法相提并论。


近义词：incomparable, disparate, dissimilar, incompatible, conflicting |
反义词：commensurable, comparable, similar, compatible |
词性：adjective, noun


incommensurables: /ˌɪnkəˈmɛnʃərəblz/ | n. | 不可公约数（量）| Things that cannot be measured or compared using the same standard. |

例句：
The incommensurables of love and loss are difficult to quantify.
爱与失去的无法衡量性很难量化。

The philosopher discussed the incommensurables of human experience.
这位哲学家讨论了人类经验中不可比较的东西。


近义词：imponderables |
反义词：commensurables |
词性：noun


词源：Late Middle English: from late Latin incommensurabilis, from in- ‘not’ + commensurabilis (see commensurable).
