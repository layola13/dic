bigot's:/ˈbɪɡəts/| n.|偏执者的（东西）|Something belonging to or associated with a bigot.  It usually refers to the bigot's views, opinions, or prejudices. |The politician's speech was filled with bigot's rhetoric, alienating many voters. 这位政客的演讲充满了偏执者的言辞，疏远了许多选民。|近义词：prejudiced person's, racist's, chauvinist's|反义词:tolerant person's, open-minded person's|词性:名词所有格

词源: From Middle French *bigot*, perhaps from *bigod* (by God), or from a Germanic source.  The original meaning referred to a religious hypocrite, particularly a Norman.  Over time, the meaning broadened to encompass intolerance and prejudice in general.
