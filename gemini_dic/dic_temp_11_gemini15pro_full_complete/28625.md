caretook:/ˈkɛərtʊk/ | v. | 照顾；看管 | acted as a caretaker; looked after |  He caretook the property while the owner was away. 他在主人不在的时候照看房子。|  supervised, oversaw, managed, tended, guarded | neglected, abandoned | verb

Etymology:  From "care" + "take". "Caretaker" is the noun form, and "caretook" is the simple past tense of the verb form.  The word "care" comes from Old English "caru,"  meaning anxiety or sorrow. Later, it evolved to mean attention or heed.  "Take" comes from Old English "tacan," meaning to grasp or seize.
