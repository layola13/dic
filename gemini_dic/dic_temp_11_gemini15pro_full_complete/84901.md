hankers:/ˈhæŋkərz/ (third-person singular simple present)| v. |渴望，热切希望| To have a strong desire for something. |She hankers after a simpler life in the countryside. 她渴望在乡村过上更简单的生活。|
hankers:/ˈhæŋkərz/| v. |渴望，热切希望|He hankers for the days of his youth. 他渴望回到青春岁月。|近义词：yearns, longs, craves, desires|反义词：dislikes, hates, abhors|词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hankeren, frequentative of hanken (“to hang”), from Old Norse hangra (“to dangle, hang”), from Proto-Germanic *hangōną (“to hang”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kengʰ- (“to hang”).
