dilettantism:/ˌdɪləˈtænˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 业余爱好；浅尝辄止；玩票 | The superficial pursuit of an art or activity, especially for amusement, rather than professionally or seriously.  | His dilettantism in painting never progressed beyond the level of a hobby. 他对绘画的浅尝辄止从未超越业余爱好者的水平。 |  Example 2: Her dilettantism in music was evident in her lack of formal training. 她在音乐上的浅尝辄止从她缺乏正规训练就可以看出来。 | 近义词：amateurism, dabbling, superficiality | 反义词：professionalism, expertise, mastery | 词性: noun

dilettante:/ˌdɪləˈtænti/ | n. adj. | 业余爱好者；浅尝辄止的人 | A person who cultivates an area of interest, such as the arts, without real commitment or knowledge. | He was a dilettante artist, painting only when the mood struck him. 他是个业余画家，只有心血来潮时才画画。 | Example 2: She considered herself a dilettante chef, experimenting with recipes but never mastering any. 她自认为是一个业余厨师，尝试各种食谱，但从未精通任何一种。 | 近义词：amateur, dabbler | 反义词：professional, expert, master | 词性: noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *dilettante*, from *dilettare* "to delight," from Latin *delectare*.
