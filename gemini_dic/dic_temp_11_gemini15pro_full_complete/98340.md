insinuation:/ɪnˌsɪnjuˈeɪʃn/ | n. |暗示，暗讽|The act of suggesting something indirectly, especially something unpleasant or disapproving. |Her insinuation that I was lying made me angry. 她暗示我在说谎，这让我很生气。|Synonyms: implication, suggestion, innuendo, hint |Antonyms: direct statement, explicit declaration |noun

insinuate:/ɪnˈsɪnjuˌeɪt/ | v. |暗示，暗讽|To suggest or hint something indirectly, especially something unpleasant or disapproving. |He insinuated that she had cheated on the test. 他暗示她在考试中作弊了。|Synonyms: imply, suggest, intimate, hint |Antonyms: state directly, declare explicitly |verb
