dicynodont:/daɪˈsaɪnəˌdɒnt/ | n. | 二齿兽 | An extinct group of mammal-like reptiles characterized by a pair of tusks and a beak-like mouth. They flourished during the Permian and Triassic periods. |  Dicynodonts were herbivores that lived in a variety of environments. 二齿兽是生活在各种环境中的食草动物。|  Fossil evidence suggests that some dicynodonts lived in burrows. 化石证据表明，一些二齿兽生活在洞穴中。 | synonyms:  therapsid (a broader group),  lystrosaurus (a specific genus of dicynodont) | antonyms:  None (as it's an extinct group, there's no direct opposite) | noun
