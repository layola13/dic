dismissiveness:/dɪsˈmɪsɪvnəs/| n. |轻蔑；不屑一顾；不予理会|The dismissiveness of her tone made him angry. 她的语气中带着轻蔑，这让他很生气。|例句：He treated my suggestion with dismissiveness. 他对我的建议不屑一顾。

She showed a dismissiveness towards the concerns of the employees. 她对员工的担忧表现出不屑一顾的态度。|近义词：contempt, disdain, scorn, indifference|反义词：respect, attentiveness, consideration|词性:名词

dismiss:/dɪsˈmɪs/| v. |解散；解雇；驳回；不予理会|The case was dismissed due to lack of evidence.  由于缺乏证据，该案被驳回。|例句：He dismissed the idea as ridiculous. 他认为这个想法很荒谬，不予理会。

The teacher dismissed the class early. 老师提前下课了。|近义词：reject, disregard, ignore, fire|反义词：accept, consider, heed, hire|词性:动词

dismissive:/dɪsˈmɪsɪv/| adj. |轻蔑的；不屑一顾的|He gave a dismissive wave of his hand. 他轻蔑地挥了挥手。|例句：She was dismissive of my concerns. 她对我的担忧不屑一顾。

His dismissive attitude offended many people. 他轻蔑的态度冒犯了很多人。|近义词：contemptuous, disdainful, scornful|反义词：respectful, attentive, considerate|词性:形容词
