craniognomy: /ˌkreɪniˈɒɡnəmi/ | n. |颅相学|The study of the shape and size of the skull as a supposed indication of character and mental abilities.  It is now considered pseudoscience. |  Phrenology, a related pseudoscience, attempted to link specific areas of the brain to personality traits, using craniognomy as one of its foundations. 颅相学，一门相关的伪科学，试图将大脑的特定区域与人格特质联系起来，并以颅相学作为其基础之一。

His interest in craniognomy led him to believe he could assess a person's intelligence just by looking at their head shape. 他对颅相学的兴趣使他相信，只需观察一个人的头部形状，就能评估其智力。| 近义词: phrenology | 反义词: None | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek "kranion" (skull) + "gnomon" (judge, interpreter).
