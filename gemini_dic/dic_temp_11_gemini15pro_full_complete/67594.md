extravagant: /ɪkˈstrævəɡənt/| adj.| 奢侈的，浪费的；过度的，放肆的|  Given to lavish or profuse expenditure; exceeding the bounds of reason or moderation.  | She has extravagant tastes in clothes. 她对服装的品味很奢侈。| He made extravagant claims about the product's effectiveness. 他对该产品的效果做出了夸大的宣传。| 近义词: lavish, profuse, luxurious, immoderate | 反义词: frugal, thrifty, economical, moderate | 词性: 形容词

extravagance: /ɪkˈstrævəɡəns/| n.| 奢侈，浪费；过度，放肆|  Excessive or unnecessary expenditure or outlay of money;  an instance of excess or prodigality. |  His extravagance led him into debt. 他的奢侈使他负债累累。| The extravagance of the wedding was the talk of the town. 婚礼的奢华是全城的谈资。| 近义词: lavishness, profusion, luxury, excess | 反义词: frugality, thrift, economy, moderation | 词性: 名词

extravagantly: /ɪkˈstrævəɡəntli/| adv.| 奢侈地，浪费地；过度地，放肆地| In an extravagant manner; excessively. |  They lived extravagantly, spending money on lavish parties and expensive cars. 他们生活奢侈，把钱花在奢华的派对和昂贵的汽车上。| The room was extravagantly decorated with flowers. 房间里装饰着大量的鲜花。| 近义词: lavishly, profusely, luxuriously, immoderately | 反义词: frugally, thriftily, economically, moderately | 词性: 副词


词源:  From Latin *extravagans*, present participle of extravagari "to wander outside," from extra- "outside" + vagari "to wander."
