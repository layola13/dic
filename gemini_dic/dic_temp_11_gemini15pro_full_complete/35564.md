coalitionism:/ˌkoʊəˈlɪʃənɪzəm/| n. |联合主义，联盟主义|The act or principle of forming or participating in a political coalition.  The advocacy or practice of different political parties or groups cooperating. |The country's history is marked by periods of coalitionism, where multiple parties share power.|近义词：alliance, partnership, cooperation|反义词:division, fragmentation, opposition|词性:noun

Etymology:
From coalition + -ism.  "Coalition" comes from Late Latin "coalitio," from "coalescere"  "to grow together," from "com-" (together) + "alescere" (to grow). "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine, etc.
