microcrystalline:/ˌmaɪkroʊˈkrɪstəlɪn/| adj.|微晶的；由微小晶体组成的|Composed of minute crystals, too small to be seen individually by the naked eye but detectable by other means such as X-ray diffraction.|The rock is microcrystalline and difficult to identify without a microscope. 这块岩石是微晶的，没有显微镜很难识别。|近义词:fine-grained, cryptocrystalline|反义词:macrocrystalline, coarsely crystalline|词性:adjective
Etymology:  "micro-" (small) + "crystalline" (composed of crystals). "Micro-" comes from Greek "mikros" (small). "Crystalline" comes from Latin "crystallinus" (of crystal), ultimately from Greek "krystallos" (ice, crystal).
