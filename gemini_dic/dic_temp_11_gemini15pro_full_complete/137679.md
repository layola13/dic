nonuniversally:/ˌnɒnˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəli/
adv.|非普遍地；并非普遍地|Not in every case; not generally.|例句：The law is nonuniversally applied. 这项法律并非普遍适用。|近义词：not universally, partially, locally|反义词：universally, generally, globally|词性:副词

Etymology:
non- (prefix) + universally (adverb)
"non-"  from Latin *non "not".
"universally" from "universal" + "-ly".
"universal" from Late Middle English: from Old French universel or late Latin universalis, from Latin universus ‘whole, entire’ (see universe).
"-ly" from Old English -līce, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch -lijk and German -lich.
