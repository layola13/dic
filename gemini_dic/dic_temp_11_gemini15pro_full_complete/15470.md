badger:/ˈbædʒər/ (US), /ˈbædʒə(r)/ (UK)| n. v. |獾; 纠缠, 烦扰|A heavily built omnivorous nocturnal mammal of the weasel family, typically having a grey and black coat.  To pester or annoy persistently.|The badger dug a sett under the tree. 獾在树下挖了一个洞穴。 | He badgered her into agreeing to the proposal. 他缠着她，直到她同意这个提议。|近义词：(n.) brock, (v.) harass, pester, hound|反义词：(v.) soothe, appease|词性:名词, 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bagier, from Old French *bagier, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to French *bêche "spade," referring to the badger's digging habits.
