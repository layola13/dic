desensationalizing:/diːˌsɛnsəˈʃənəlaɪzɪŋ/
verb
使不轰动；使不耸人听闻；降低…的轰动效应
To deprive of sensational qualities; to make something seem less exciting or shocking.
The media tried to desensationalize the story by focusing on the facts. 媒体试图通过关注事实来降低该故事的轰动性。
The government attempted to desensationalize the crisis. 政府试图淡化危机的轰动性。

近义词: downplay, minimize, de-emphasize, understate
反义词: sensationalize, dramatize, exaggerate, hype up
词性: verb (present participle)

词源:  From de- (prefix meaning "remove" or "reduce") + sensationalize (verb meaning "to present something in a way that is intended to provoke public interest and excitement").  Ultimately from Latin *sensus* "feeling".
