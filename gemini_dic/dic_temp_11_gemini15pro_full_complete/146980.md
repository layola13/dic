overjoyousness:/ˌōvərˈdʒɔɪəsnəs/
n.|极度的喜悦，狂喜|The state of being overwhelmingly joyful. |Her overjoyousness at the news was infectious. 她听到这个消息欣喜若狂，很有感染力。|  Near Synonyms:  ecstasy, rapture, bliss, elation | Antonyms: despair, misery, sorrow | Noun

overjoyed:/ˌoʊvərˈdʒɔɪd/
adj.|欣喜若狂的，极度高兴的|Extremely happy; filled with great joy. |They were overjoyed at the birth of their first child.  他们第一个孩子的出生让他们欣喜若狂。| Near Synonyms: delighted, thrilled, ecstatic | Antonyms:  dejected, miserable, heartbroken | Adjective


overjoy:/ˌoʊvərˈdʒɔɪ/
v.|使欣喜若狂，使非常高兴|To fill with great joy; to make extremely happy. |The news of her acceptance to the university overjoyed her. 她被大学录取的消息让她欣喜若狂。 |Near Synonyms: delight, thrill, enrapture| Antonyms: sadden, depress, devastate | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + joy + -ous + -ness. "Over-" intensifies the meaning of "joy".  "-ous" turns the noun "joy" into an adjective "joyous", meaning "full of joy".  Finally, "-ness" transforms the adjective "joyous" into the noun "overjoyousness", representing the state of being overjoyed.
