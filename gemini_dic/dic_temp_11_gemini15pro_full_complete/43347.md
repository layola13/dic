cramps:/kræmps/| n. |痉挛，抽筋|A painful, involuntary contraction of a muscle or muscles.|I got leg cramps after running the marathon. 我跑完马拉松后腿抽筋了。|Synonyms: spasms, contractions|Antonyms: relaxation, looseness|noun


cramp:/kræmp/| vt. |限制，束缚|To restrict or confine.|Lack of funding cramped our research efforts. 资金短缺限制了我们的研究工作。|Synonyms: restrict, confine, hinder|Antonyms: facilitate, aid, assist|verb

cramp:/kræmp/| adj. |狭窄的，拥挤的|Confined and uncomfortable.|The room was small and cramp. 房间又小又挤。|Synonyms: confined, cramped, restricted|Antonyms: spacious, roomy, comfortable|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cramp or from Old North French crampe, ultimately from a Germanic base meaning "to press together".
