morbidity:/mɔːrˈbɪdɪti/ /mɔːrˈbɪdəti/| n. | 病态，病态心理; 发病率; 患病状态|An abnormally gloomy or unhealthy preoccupation with death and disease; the rate of incidence of disease.| The morbidity rate from lung cancer is increasing. 肺癌的发病率正在上升。| 近义词：unhealthiness, sickness, prevalence, incidence | 反义词: health, wellness | 词性: noun

morbid:/ˈmɔːrbɪd/ /ˈmɔːrbɪd/| adj. | 病态的，不健康的; (对死亡等)病态地感兴趣的; 恐怖的; 令人毛骨悚然的 | Characterized by or appealing to an abnormal and unhealthy interest in disturbing and unpleasant subjects, especially death and disease. | He has a morbid fascination with death. 他对死亡有一种病态的迷恋。 | 近义词：macabre, gruesome, unhealthy, ghoulish | 反义词: healthy, cheerful | 词性: adjective


词源:
From Late Latin morbiditās (“sickliness, diseased state”), from Latin morbidus (“diseased, sickly”), from morbus (“disease”),  akin to Old English mearþ (“tender, delicate, frail”).
