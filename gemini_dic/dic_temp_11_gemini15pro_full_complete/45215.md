culls:/kʌlz/| n.|剔除物，淘汰品|Something rejected as inferior or worthless.  The act of selecting and removing weak or inferior members from a group.|例句：The farmer culled the weakest lambs from the flock. 农民从羊群中剔除了最弱的小羊。

例句：These books are culls from the library's collection. 这些书是从图书馆的藏书中剔除出来的。|近义词：rejects, discards, refuse|反义词: selections, choices|词性:noun (plural)

cull:/kʌl/| v.|剔除，淘汰|To select and remove weak or inferior members from (a group of animals, plants, etc.). To select from a large quantity; obtain from a variety of sources.|例句：The herd was culled to eliminate disease.  为了消除疾病，牛群被进行了剔除。

例句：She culled information for her report from various sources. 她从各种来源收集信息来撰写报告。|近义词：select, choose, pick, glean|反义词: gather, collect, accumulate|词性:verb
