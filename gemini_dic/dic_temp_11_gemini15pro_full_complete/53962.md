discrediting:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪtɪŋ/
verb
使失去信誉；败坏名声
damaging the reputation of someone or something; causing someone or something to be no longer trusted or respected
The lawyer tried discrediting the witness by showing he had lied in the past. 律师试图通过证明证人过去撒过谎来败坏他的名声。
synonyms: disparaging, defaming, denigrating, smearing, maligning, vilifying, traducing, aspersing
antonyms: praising, commending, lauding, extolling, acclaiming
v.

discredit:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪt/
noun
丧失名誉；耻辱
loss or damage to someone's reputation; disgrace
The scandal brought discredit on the whole political party.  这桩丑闻给整个政党带来了耻辱。
synonyms: disgrace, dishonor, disrepute, ignominy, opprobrium, shame, stigma
antonyms: honor, respect, esteem, prestige, acclaim
n.

discredit:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪt/
verb
使失去信誉；败坏名声
damage the reputation of; cause to be no longer trusted or respected
His theories have been discredited by recent research.  他的理论被最近的研究成果推翻了。
synonyms: disprove, refute, invalidate, debunk, explode
antonyms: prove, validate, confirm, corroborate, substantiate
v.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French *descredire (unattested), from Latin discredere ‘disbelieve, distrust’, from dis- ‘not’ + credere ‘believe’.
