assassinative:/əˈsæsɪˌneɪtɪv/ | adj. | 暗杀的；行刺的；像刺客的；阴险的 | Characteristic of or resembling an assassin; designed or likely to assassinate. |  His assassinative gaze made her uncomfortable. 他那刺客般的眼神让她很不舒服。 | The dictator used assassinative tactics to eliminate his rivals.  这位独裁者使用暗杀手段来消灭他的对手。 | 近义词：murderous, homicidal, deadly | 反义词：peaceful, harmless, benevolent | 词性：形容词

Etymology: From Late Latin *assassinativus, from assassinatus, past participle of assassinare "to assassinate," from Arabic hashīshīyīn "hashish-eaters," plural of hashīshīy "hashish-eater," referring to a Muslim sect in Syria and Persia in the 11th-13th centuries known for murdering their enemies, often for political reasons.  The word eventually lost its connection to hashish and came to refer to any murderer.
