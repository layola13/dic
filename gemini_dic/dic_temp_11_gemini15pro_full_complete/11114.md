archdeaconess:/ˌɑːrtʃˈdiːkənɪs/
n.
女执事长，女总铎
a senior female official in certain Christian churches, especially the Anglican Church, who assists a bishop or archdeacon.
The archdeaconess visited the parish to confirm the new members. 女执事长访问了教区，为新成员进行坚振礼。
Synonyms: churchwoman, female cleric
Antonyms: layman, layperson
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin archidiaconissa, from Greek arkhi- (“chief”) + diakonos (“servant, minister”).
