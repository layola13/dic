conservers:/kənˈsɜːrvərz/| n. |保护者，保存者|A person who protects something from harm or destruction, especially something such as a natural resource or an animal or plant species.|例句：The conservers are working hard to protect the endangered species. 保护者们正在努力保护濒危物种。

例句：The museum conservers carefully restored the ancient artifact. 博物馆的文物保护者仔细地修复了这件古代文物。

|近义词：preservationists, protectors, guardians, custodians|反义词：destroyers, wasters, squanderers|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *conserver, from Anglo-Norman conserver, from Latin conservare (“to preserve, maintain, keep”), from con- (“together”) + servare (“to keep, guard”).
