feudalist:/ˈfjuːdəlɪst/ | n. | 封建主义者；封建制度拥护者 | A person who supports or advocates feudalism, a social system in which land ownership and political power are based on a hierarchy of lords and vassals. | The king rewarded his loyal feudalist with a large estate. 国王用一大块领地奖励了他忠诚的封建主义者。|  The duke, a staunch feudalist, refused to grant the peasants more rights. 这位公爵，一位坚定的封建主义者，拒绝给予农民更多的权利。 | synonyms:  lord, nobleman, vassal (in some contexts) | antonyms: commoner, peasant, revolutionary | noun

feudalistic:/ˌfjuːdəˈlɪstɪk/ | adj. | 封建的；封建主义的 | Relating to or characteristic of feudalism. |  The feudalistic society was strictly hierarchical. 封建社会等级森严。| The peasants revolted against the feudalistic oppression. 农民反抗封建压迫。 | synonyms:  feudal, manorial, medieval (in some contexts) | antonyms: democratic, egalitarian, modern | adjective
