frankpledge: /ˈfræŋkˌpledʒ/ | n. | 十人联保制，互相担保 | A system in medieval England in which groups of ten households, called tithings, were responsible for the good behavior of their members. Each member was pledged to uphold the law and bring any lawbreakers within the tithing to justice. |  The frankpledge system was a key element of social order in Anglo-Saxon England.  十人联保制是盎格鲁-撒克逊时期英国社会秩序的一个关键要素。

The frankpledge system declined in importance after the Norman Conquest.  诺曼征服后，十人联保制的重要性下降了。 | Synonyms: collective security, mutual guarantee | Antonyms: individual responsibility | noun
