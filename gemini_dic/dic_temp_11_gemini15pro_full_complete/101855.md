isagoge:/ˌaɪsəˈɡoʊdʒi/ | n. |  入门，导论 | An introduction or preliminary explanation to a subject, especially a philosophical or religious one. |  The professor's lecture served as an isagoge to the complex theories of quantum physics. 教授的讲座是对复杂量子物理理论的入门介绍。 |  introduction, preface, prelude, prolegomenon |  epilogue, conclusion, postscript | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *isagoge*, from Ancient Greek εἰσαγωγή (eisagōgḗ, “introduction, entrance”), from εἰσάγω (eiságō, “I lead into”), from εἰς (eis, “into”) + ἄγω (ágō, “I lead”).
