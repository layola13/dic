gastropod:/ˈɡæstrəpɒd/ (US) /ˈɡæstrəpɑːd/ (UK)| n. | 腹足纲动物；腹足类 |A mollusk of the large class Gastropoda, such as a snail, slug, or whelk, having a single shell or no shell at all, a flattened muscular foot for locomotion, and eyes and feelers located on a distinct head.| Snails are common gastropods.  蜗牛是常见的腹足纲动物。|Synonyms: univalve, snail, slug|Antonyms: None|Noun


gastropod:/ˈɡæstrəpɒd/ (US) /ˈɡæstrəpɑːd/ (UK)| adj. | 腹足纲的 |Of or relating to the Gastropoda.|  Gastropod shells are often collected by beachcombers. 腹足纲动物的壳经常被海滩拾荒者收集。|Synonyms: univalve|Antonyms: None|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek γαστήρ (gastḗr, “belly, stomach”) + πούς (poús, “foot”).
