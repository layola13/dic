callousing: /ˈkæləsɪŋ/ | adj. |  使生老茧的；使麻木的；变得冷酷无情的 |  Becoming hardened or insensitive, especially to emotion or suffering. |  Years of working in the ER had a callousing effect on the doctor. 多年在急诊室工作使这位医生变得麻木。 |近义词: hardening, toughening, desensitizing | 反义词: softening, sensitizing | 词性: 形容词

callous: /ˈkæləs/ | adj. |  无情的；冷淡的；硬结的；起老茧的 | Showing or having an insensitive and cruel disregard for the feelings of others. (adj.);  A thickened and hardened part of the skin or soft tissue, especially in an area that has been subjected to friction. (n.) | His callous disregard for her feelings hurt her deeply. 他对她的感情漠不关心，深深地伤害了她。/ The guitarist had callouses on his fingertips. 吉他手的指尖长了老茧。| 近义词: insensitive, unfeeling, hard, indifferent | 反义词: compassionate, kind, sensitive, soft | 词性: 形容词, 名词

callus: /ˈkæləs/ | n. | 老茧；硬皮；胼胝 | A thickened and hardened part of the skin or soft tissue, especially in an area that has been subjected to friction. |  He developed calluses on his hands from years of manual labor. 他多年体力劳动，手上长出了老茧。 | 近义词:  corn, callosity | 反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑用描述皮肤柔软的词) soft skin | 词性: 名词


词源:  Late Middle English: from Latin callus, callosus ‘hard skin’.
