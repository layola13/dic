bluenose:/ˈbluːnoʊz/| n.|禁酒主义者；(尤指)严谨的人；新斯科舍省人|A person, especially from Nova Scotia, or one who is rigidly moral, especially regarding alcohol.|例句：He's a bit of a bluenose, he disapproves of drinking even on special occasions. 他有点古板，即使在特殊场合也反对喝酒。

例句：My grandmother was a real bluenose; she wouldn't even let us play cards on Sundays. 我的祖母非常古板；她甚至不让我们在星期天玩纸牌。|近义词：puritan, moralist, killjoy|反义词：libertine, hedonist|词性:noun

bluenose:/ˈbluːnoʊz/| adj.|禁酒的；严谨的；新斯科舍省的|Relating to Nova Scotia, or rigidly moral, especially regarding alcohol.|例句：The bluenose laws in the town made it difficult to get a drink.  镇上禁酒的法律使得很难买到酒。

例句：She gave him a bluenose stare when he suggested they open another bottle of wine.  当他建议再开一瓶酒时，她严厉地瞪了他一眼。|近义词：puritanical, strict, austere|反义词：permissive, liberal|词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From the nickname for people from Nova Scotia, Canada, possibly referring to the cold winters that might cause noses to turn blue, or perhaps from a type of potato grown there.  来自加拿大新斯科舍省人的绰号，可能指的是寒冷的冬天可能会导致鼻子变蓝，或者也可能来自那里种植的一种土豆。
