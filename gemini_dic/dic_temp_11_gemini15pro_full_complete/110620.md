limpet:/ˈlɪmpɪt/| n.|帽贝；顽固的人；坚持不懈的人|A marine mollusk with a low conical shell, which clings tightly to rocks.  A person who stubbornly refuses to leave a place or position.|The limpets clung to the rocks despite the crashing waves.  这些帽贝尽管波涛汹涌，仍然紧紧地 clinging to the rocks.|近义词：barnacle, clingfish, stick-in-the-mud (figurative)|反义词: nomad, wanderer|词性:noun

limpet:/ˈlɪmpɪt/| adj.|紧附的，粘住的|Clinging tightly; stubbornly persistent.|His limpet-like grip on power finally loosened. 他那紧抓着不放的权力终于松动了。|近义词：tenacious, clinging, persistent|反义词: loose, yielding|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lempedu, from West Germanic *lamprēdo, from Proto-Germanic *lamprēdaz, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)lēp-, meaning "to stick, adhere." Related to the word "slime."
