n.
黄昏鸟
an extinct, flightless, toothed bird of the Late Cretaceous period, known from fossils found in North America. It was a strong swimmer and diver, and probably fed on fish.
The Hesperornis, with its powerful legs and reduced wings, was well-adapted to life in the water.  黄昏鸟凭借其强壮的腿和退化的翅膀，非常适应水中生活。

The discovery of Hesperornis fossils provided important evidence for the evolution of birds from reptilian ancestors.  黄昏鸟化石的发现为鸟类从爬行动物祖先进化提供了重要的证据。
