knarled:/nɑːrld/ | adj. | 粗糙的，多节的，扭曲的 | Covered with knots; twisted and rough; (of a person) rugged and weather-beaten, especially through age and experience. | The old man's gnarled hands showed a lifetime of hard work. 老人的双手粗糙多节，显示出他一生辛勤劳作。|  The gnarled branches of the oak tree reached towards the sky. 橡树粗糙扭曲的树枝伸向天空。| 近义词: knotted, twisted, rough, rugged, wizened | 反义词: smooth, straight | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Middle English *knorred, *knurled (“knotted, gnarled”), equivalent to knor +‎ -ed. Cognate with Dutch knorrig (“gnarly, grumpy”), German knorrig (“gnarled, knobbly”).  The word is related to "knot" and implies a twisted, knotted appearance.
