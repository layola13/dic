heavying:/ˈhɛviɪŋ/| v. |加重，使沉重；使苦恼；使变得严重|Making something heavier or more serious; becoming heavier or more serious. |The rain was heavying the load on the roof. 雨水加重了屋顶的负荷。|Synonyms: burdening, weighting, aggravating, intensifying|Antonyms: lightening, easing, alleviating|Verb

Etymology->

From heavy + -ing.  The suffix "-ing" transforms the adjective "heavy" into a present participle, which can function as a verb.  "Heavy" itself has Old English roots (hefīg) meaning "weighty," "grievous," or "serious."
