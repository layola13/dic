ended:/ˈɛndɪd/| v.| 结束，终止 |The meeting ended at 5 pm. 会议在下午5点结束。|The movie ended with a cliffhanger. 电影以悬念结尾。|近义词：finished, concluded, terminated|反义词:began, started, commenced|verb (past tense of "end")

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *enden*, from Old English *endian* (“to end, finish, put an end to”), from Proto-Germanic *andijaną* (“to end”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ent- (“front, forehead; beginning, end”).  Cognate with Old Frisian *enda*, Old Saxon *endian*, Old High German *enten*, Old Norse *enda*, Gothic *andin* (“end”).

USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
The show ends at 10 pm every night.  演出每晚10点结束。
He always ends his emails with "Best regards". 他总是以“此致敬礼”结束他的邮件。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
The movie is ending now. 电影现在要结束了。
The concert is ending soon.  音乐会即将结束。

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
The game has ended. 比赛结束了。
The rain has finally ended. 雨终于停了。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
The negotiations have been ending smoothly. 谈判一直在顺利结束。  (This usage is less common.  Usually, "have been progressing" or similar would be used instead.)
They have been ending their relationship gradually. 他们一直在逐渐结束他们的关系。(This usage is also less common)


一般过去时 (Simple Past):

The war ended in 1945. 战争于1945年结束。

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):

We were ending our conversation when he arrived. 他到达时，我们正要结束谈话。


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
The movie had ended before we arrived.  在我们到达之前，电影已经结束了。
The meeting had ended by the time I got there. 我到那儿的时候，会议已经结束了。


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):

He had been ending his sentences with prepositions, which annoyed his editor. 他一直在用介词结束句子，这惹恼了他的编辑。（This usage is less common)
