consumerization:/kənˌsjuːməraɪˈzeɪʃən/ |n.|消费化|The process by which products, services, or technologies that were originally designed for businesses or specific industries are adopted and adapted for use by individual consumers.|例句：The consumerization of IT has led to employees bringing their own devices to work. 消费化IT导致员工自带设备上班。

例句：The consumerization of healthcare is empowering patients to take more control of their own well-being. 医疗保健的消费化正在赋予患者更多自主掌控自身健康的权力。|近义词：commoditization, popularization|反义词: specialization, niche marketing|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From consume (v.) + -er (suffix forming nouns denoting agents or instruments) + -ize (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become") + -ation (suffix forming nouns from verbs).  The word began to gain popularity in the early 2000s, particularly in the context of information technology.
