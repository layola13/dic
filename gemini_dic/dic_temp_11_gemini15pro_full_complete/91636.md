hyperbole:/haɪˈpɜːrbəli/ (US), /haɪˈpɜːbəli/ (UK)| n. |夸张法；夸张的语句|A figure of speech that uses extreme exaggeration to create emphasis or humor. It is not meant to be taken literally.|例句：He said he was so hungry he could eat a horse.  他饿得可以吃下一匹马。（这是夸张的说法）

例句：I've told you a million times to clean your room! 我已经告诉过你一百万次要打扫你的房间了！（这也是夸张的说法）|近义词：exaggeration, overstatement|反义词:understatement, litotes|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hyperbola, from Greek hyperbolḗ, from hyperballein "to throw over, overshoot," from hyper- "beyond" + ballein "to throw."
