nonplussed: /ˌnɑːnˈplʌst/ (US) /ˌnɒnˈplʌst/ (UK)| adj.| 困惑的，不知所措的，非常惊讶的| So surprised and confused that you are unsure how to react.|例句：She looked nonplussed when I told her the news. 当我告诉她这个消息时，她看起来很困惑。|近义词：stunned, bewildered, perplexed|反义词：expectant, prepared, unsurprised|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *non plus*, literally "no more," i.e., at a loss what more can be said or done.  It originally meant "brought to a standstill,"  but the modern meaning developed in the 17th century.
