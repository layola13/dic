interdicting:/ˌɪntərˈdɪktɪŋ/
|v.|禁止，阻止，阻断|The government interdicted the sale of the drug. 政府禁止销售这种药物。|例句：The court interdicted the company from selling the faulty product. 法院禁止该公司销售有缺陷的产品。

例句：They were interdicting the flow of illegal arms into the country.  他们正在阻止非法武器流入该国。|近义词：prohibiting, forbidding, banning, barring|反义词：permitting, allowing, authorizing|词性：动词
Etymology: From Latin *interdictus*, past participle of *interdicere* (“to forbid, prohibit, interdict”), from *inter-* (“between”) + *dicere* (“to say, speak”).
