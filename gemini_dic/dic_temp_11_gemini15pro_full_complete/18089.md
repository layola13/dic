behaviorist:/bɪˈheɪvjərɪst/| n. |行为主义者|A psychologist who believes that external factors shape behavior and that only observable actions should be studied.  |例句：The behaviorist observed the rat's response to the stimulus. 行为主义者观察了老鼠对刺激的反应。|近义词：learning theorist|反义词:cognitivist|noun


behaviorist:/bɪˈheɪvjərɪst/| adj. |行为主义的|Relating to behaviorism. |例句：The behaviorist approach to psychology focuses on observable actions. 行为主义心理学方法侧重于可观察的行为。|近义词：learning|反义词:cognitive|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From behavior + -ist. Behavior from Old French *behaveor “to handle, manage, conduct oneself,” borrowed from Anglo-French behauour, alteration (by influence of Old French avoir “to have”) of Anglo-French aveir, ahaveir “to have,” from Latin habēre. -ist from -iste, from Latin -ista.
