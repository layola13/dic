objector:/əbˈdʒektər/ | n. | 反对者；抗议者；拒服兵役者 | A person who expresses or feels disapproval or opposition to something. |  例句：Several objectors voiced their concerns at the public hearing.  几位反对者在公开听证会上表达了他们的担忧。

例句：The objector to the new highway cited environmental concerns.  反对修建新公路的人列举了环境方面的担忧。| 近义词：dissenter, protestor, opponent | 反义词：supporter, proponent | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *objectour*, from Anglo-Norman *objectour, obeitour*, and Old French *obgetour*, from Medieval Latin *ōbjectōrem* (nominative *ōbjector*), from Latin *obiectāre* ("to throw against, oppose"), from *ob-* ("against") + *iactare* ("to throw repeatedly, toss, hurl").
