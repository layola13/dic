everythings:/ˈɛvriˌθɪŋz/ |  n. (nonstandard) | 所有事物；一切 | This word is a nonstandard form of "everything". It's often used informally, especially in spoken language, to emphasize the totality of things.  It can sound childish or uneducated in formal writing or speech. |  "Everythings gonna be alright." (Informal)  一切都好。 | 近义词: everything, all | 反义词: nothing | 词性: pronoun (nonstandard)

虚拟语气:
If everything were different, I would be happier. 如果一切都不同，我会更快乐。
I wish everything were easier. 我希望一切都更容易。


被动语态:
(Not commonly used in the passive voice.  One might say something like: "Everything is considered..." but it is not a frequent construction.)


疑问句:
Is everything alright? 一切都好吗？
Is everything ready? 一切都准备好了吗？


否定句:
Not everything is perfect.  并非一切都是完美的。
Everything isn't going according to plan.  并非一切都按计划进行。


PHRASE-> (Note: These phrases use "everything" not "everythings")
Everything is under control. 一切尽在掌握。
Everything but the kitchen sink.  几乎所有东西（除了厨房水槽以外的所有东西）。
Everything is going to be okay. 一切都会好起来的。
Everything is coming up roses.  一切都很顺利。
Everything is going swimmingly.  一切都很顺利。


词源:
"Everything" comes from Old English *æfre þing, meaning "ever thing".  "Everythings" is a nonstandard, colloquial variant that arises from the tendency to add "-s" to make plurals, even when not grammatically correct.  It doesn't have a separate etymology.


注意事项:

Avoid using "everythings" in formal contexts.  Stick with "everything" for standard English.
While "everythings" might be used for emphasis in casual speech, it's generally perceived as incorrect.
