blockage:/ˈblɒkɪdʒ/ (UK) /ˈblɑːkɪdʒ/ (US)| n.|阻塞；堵塞|An obstruction that prevents passage or progress. |The blockage in the artery caused a heart attack. 动脉阻塞导致了心脏病发作。|Synonyms: obstruction, barrier, impediment, obstacle|Antonyms: opening, clearing, access|noun

blockage:/ˈblɒkɪdʒ/ (UK) /ˈblɑːkɪdʒ/ (US)| n.|封锁；阻挠|The act of blocking something.|The police set up a blockage to prevent the protesters from entering the building. 警察设置了路障以阻止抗议者进入大楼。|Synonyms: blockade, barricade, obstruction|Antonyms: assistance, aid|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "block" + "-age" (suffix forming nouns of action or result). "Block" comes from Middle French "bloc" meaning "a block of wood." Ultimately from a Germanic source.
