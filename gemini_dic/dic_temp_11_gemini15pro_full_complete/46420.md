dager:/ˈdeɪɡər/| n.|匕首，短剑|A short knife used as a weapon. |He drew his dagger and lunged. 他拔出匕首刺了过去。|Synonyms: knife, blade, stiletto|Antonyms: shield, armor|noun

Etymology->
From Middle English *dager*, from Old French *dagiere*, *daiere*, from Frankish **daggar**. Ultimately from Proto-Germanic **dagaz** (“day”).  Relates to the shape of the blade resembling a ray of sunlight.
