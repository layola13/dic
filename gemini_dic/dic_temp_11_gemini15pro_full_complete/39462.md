conning:/ˈkɒnɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnɪŋ/ (US)| n. v. adj.|欺骗，诈骗；骗术；操舵，掌舵|The act of tricking or deceiving someone.  Also, the act of directing the steering of a ship or boat.  As an adjective, it can describe something deceitful or sly.|

例句：
He made his living by conning elderly people out of their savings.
他靠骗取老年人的积蓄为生。

The captain was conning the ship through the narrow channel.
船长正在引导船只通过狭窄的航道。

She had a conning smile that made everyone trust her.
她有着让人信服的狡猾笑容。


近义词：swindling, deceiving, defrauding, manipulating, steering, piloting|
反义词：honest, truthful, forthright, guiding (as a verb)|
词性: noun, verb, adjective|


con:/kɒn/ (UK) /kɑːn/ (US)| v. n.| 欺骗；学习；操舵；精读；背诵|To persuade (someone) to do or believe something, typically by use of a deception.  Also, to study or learn something carefully, or to direct the steering of a ship or boat. As a noun, it refers to the act of swindling or deceiving.|

例句：
He conned her into giving him her bank details.
他骗取了她的银行信息。

She spent hours conning her notes before the exam.
考试前，她花了几个小时仔细研读笔记。

The con artist pulled off a complex con.
这个骗子完成了一个复杂的骗局。

近义词：trick, deceive, swindle, study, learn, steer, pilot|
反义词：be honest, be truthful|
词性: verb, noun|


词源: From Middle English *connen, from Old English *cunnan (“to know, know how to, be able to”), from Proto-Germanic *kunnaną (“to know, be able to”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵneh₁- (“to know”). Related to can (v.). The nautical sense "direct the steering of" is from 1902, probably a shortened form of conning tower.|
