incidentally:/ˌɪnsɪˈdɛntəli/| adv.|顺便说一句，偶然地，附带地|Used to introduce a new topic or piece of information that is not strictly part of the main subject of conversation but is related to it in some way.  It indicates that the speaker is adding a supplementary remark or thought.|I went to the library to return some books and, incidentally, to see if they had any new biographies. 我去图书馆还了一些书，顺便看看他们有没有新的传记。|Synonyms: by the way, by the bye, in passing, parenthetically|Antonyms: purposely, intentionally, deliberately|词性:副词
