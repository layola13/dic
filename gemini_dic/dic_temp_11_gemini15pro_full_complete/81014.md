gradualism: /ˈɡrædʒuəlɪzəm/ | n. | 渐变；渐进主义 | The theory that change occurs slowly and incrementally, rather than suddenly or dramatically. |  The company adopted a policy of gradualism, slowly introducing changes to avoid upsetting the staff. 公司采取了渐进主义的政策，缓慢地引入变革，以避免员工不安。

Gradualism is often contrasted with revolution. 渐进主义常与革命对比。 | 近义词:  incrementalism,  evolution, progression | 反义词: revolution, suddenness, abruptness | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  From "gradual" + "-ism."  "Gradual" comes from Late Latin "gradualis"  meaning "pertaining to steps or degrees,"  from Latin "gradus" meaning "step, degree."  "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine, principle, etc.
