neocolonization:/ˌniːoʊˌkɑːlənaɪˈzeɪʃn/ | n. | 新殖民主义 | The use of economic, political, cultural, or other pressures to control or influence other countries, especially former dependencies.  This often involves exploitation and perpetuates inequalities similar to traditional colonialism. |  Example 1: Critics argue that neocolonization through unfair trade practices keeps developing nations in a cycle of debt. 批评人士认为，通过不公平贸易行为进行的新殖民主义使发展中国家陷入了债务循环。

Example 2: The influence of multinational corporations is often cited as a form of neocolonization. 跨国公司的影响力经常被认为是一种新殖民主义的形式。 |  Synonym: neo-imperialism, economic imperialism | Antonym: decolonization, independence | noun
