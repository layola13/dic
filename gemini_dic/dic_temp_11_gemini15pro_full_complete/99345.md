interdeceiving:/ˌɪntərdiˈsiːvɪŋ/
adj.
相互欺骗的
Engaging in mutual deception; deceiving each other.
The two spies were locked in an interdeceiving game, each trying to outwit the other. 两个间谍陷入了相互欺骗的游戏中，每个人都试图智胜对方。
近义词:mutually deceptive, reciprocally misleading
反义词:honest, truthful
词性:形容词

词源:
"Inter-" (prefix): between, among, mutually. From Latin "inter" (between).
"Deceive": to cause to believe something that is not true, typically in order to gain some personal advantage. From Old French "decevoir," from Latin "decipere" (to ensnare, beguile).
"-ing" (suffix): forms present participles and gerunds.
