liquidize: /ˈlɪkwɪdaɪz/ | v. | 液化；清算；消灭 | To convert (something) into a liquid form; to settle the affairs of (a business) by converting its assets into cash; to kill or destroy. |  The chef liquidized the strawberries for the sauce. 厨师把草莓打成汁做酱。 The company was forced to liquidize its assets to pay off its debts. 公司被迫清算资产以偿还债务。 The dictator ordered his troops to liquidize the opposition. 独裁者命令他的军队消灭反对派。 | 近义词: liquefy, dissolve, kill, eliminate | 反义词: solidify, freeze, establish | 词性: 动词

liquidize: /ˈlɪkwɪdaɪz/ | n. |  液化；清算 | The act of converting something into liquid form; the process of settling the affairs of a business. | The liquidize of the company's assets took several months. 公司资产的清算花了几个月的时间。 The recipe called for the liquidize of the fruit. 食谱要求将水果液化。 | 近义词: liquefaction, dissolution, liquidation | 反义词: solidification, freezing | 词性: 名词

词源: From Latin *liquidus*, meaning "liquid," from *liquere*, meaning "to be fluid."
