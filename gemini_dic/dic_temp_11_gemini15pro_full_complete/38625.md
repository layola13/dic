concrimination:/kənˌkrɪmɪˈneɪʃən/
n.
相互指责，互相criminating
the act of accusing someone of something, especially when you are also guilty of the same thing
The politicians engaged in bitter concrimination, each blaming the other for the country's economic problems. 政治家们互相指责，每个人都把国家的经济问题归咎于对方。
近义词: accusation, recrimination, countercharge, mutual accusation
反义词: exoneration, acquittal, vindication
词性: noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *concriminatio*, from Latin concriminari (“to accuse jointly”), from com- (“together”) + criminari (“to accuse”), from crimen (“accusation, crime”).
