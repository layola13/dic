comport:/kəmˈpɔːrt/ | v. |  表现；相称；符合 | To behave in a particular manner; to be in agreement or harmony with. | She comported herself with dignity at the funeral. 她在葬礼上举止庄重。| synonyms: behave, conduct, accord, agree | antonyms: misbehave, clash, disagree | verb

comport with:/kəmˈpɔːrt wɪð/ | phrasal verb |  与...相符；与...一致 | To be in agreement, harmony, or conformity with something; to behave in a way that is appropriate to something. |  His actions do not comport with his words. 他的行为与他的言辞不符。| synonyms: accord with, agree with, conform to, be consistent with | antonyms: clash with, disagree with, be inconsistent with | phrasal verb

词源：
From Old French comporter "to bear, endure, tolerate; behave," from Latin comportare "to carry or bring together," from com- "together" + portare "to carry."  The meaning "to behave" developed in French and was adopted into English.
