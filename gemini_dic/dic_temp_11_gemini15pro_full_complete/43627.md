creatine:/ˈkriːətiːn/ /ˈkriːətɪn/| n.|肌酸|A nitrogenous organic acid that occurs naturally in vertebrates and helps to supply energy to all cells in the body, primarily muscle.  It is synthesized from amino acids mainly in the liver and kidneys.|Creatine supplements are popular among athletes. 肌酸补充剂在运动员中很受欢迎。|Synonyms:  phosphocreatine|Antonyms: None|Noun

creatinine:/kriˈætɪniːn/ /kriˈætɪˌnin/| n.|肌酐|A compound formed in vertebrate muscle tissue as the end product of creatine metabolism.  It is routinely measured in blood and urine tests as an indicator of kidney function.|High creatinine levels can indicate kidney problems. 肌酐水平高可能表明肾脏有问题。|Synonyms: None|Antonyms: None|Noun
