gerrymandered:/ˈdʒɛrimændərd/ | adj. | 不公正划分的；(指选区)为一方利益非法改划的 |Manipulated the boundaries of (an electoral constituency) so as to favor one party or class.  A district whose boundaries have been gerrymandered. |例句：The state was gerrymandered to give the ruling party an advantage.  这个州的选区被不公正地划分，以利于执政党。

例句：The opposition claimed the new electoral map was gerrymandered. 反对党声称新的选举地图是不公正划分的。|近义词：manipulated, rigged, biased |反义词：fair, impartial |词性: 形容词

gerrymander:/ˈdʒɛrimændər/ | vt. n.| (为一方利益)非法改划(选区)；不公正的选区划分 | To divide (a geographic area) into voting districts so as to give unfair advantage to one party in elections. The act of gerrymandering. | 例句：The politicians gerrymandered the districts to ensure their re-election. 政客们为了确保自己连任而非法改划选区。

例句：The gerrymander gave the Republicans a significant advantage.  这种不公正的选区划分给了共和党很大的优势。| 近义词：manipulate, rig | 反义词：n/a (verb); fair districting (noun) |词性: 动词, 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the name of Elbridge Gerry (1744–1814), governor of Massachusetts, + salamander, because the shape of a new voting district created while he was in office (1812) was thought to resemble a salamander.
