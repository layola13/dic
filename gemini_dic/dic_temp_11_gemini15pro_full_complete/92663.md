ibuprofen: /ˌaɪbjuːˈproʊfən/ | n. | 布洛芬 | A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to reduce fever and treat pain or inflammation caused by conditions such as headache, toothache, back pain, arthritis, menstrual cramps, or minor injury. |  I took some ibuprofen for my headache. 我吃了些布洛芬来治疗头痛。 | Synonyms: Advil, Motrin, Nuprin | Antonyms:  N/A (no direct antonym for a medication) | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From *iso-butyl-propanoic-phenolic acid*, a chemical description of the drug's structure.
