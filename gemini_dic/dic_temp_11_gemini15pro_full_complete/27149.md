call-in-sick:/ˌkɔːl ɪn ˈsɪk/| phrasal verb | 打电话请病假 | To telephone one's workplace to inform them that one is unable to come to work due to illness. | Example: I had a terrible headache this morning, so I called in sick.  今天早上我头疼得厉害，所以我打电话请病假了。| Example: He called in sick for the third time this month.  这个月他已经是第三次打电话请病假了。 | 近义词: report sick, phone in sick | 反义词: come to work, show up |词性: 动词短语

call-in:/ˈkɔːl ɪn/| n. | 打电话; 插播电话; 召集|A phone call made to a radio or television show, usually to give an opinion or ask a question. | Example: The radio show host took several call-ins from listeners.  电台节目主持人接听了几个听众打来的电话。| Example: There was a call-in for expert advice on the topic.  有一个电话征求关于这个话题的专家意见。 | 近义词: phone-in | 反义词:  (No direct antonym) |词性: 名词

sick:/sɪk/| adj. | 生病的；恶心的；厌恶的 | Affected by physical or mental illness; feeling nausea; disgusted. | Example:  She's been sick with the flu for a week. 她得了流感，病了一个星期了。| Example: I felt sick after eating that spoiled food. 我吃了那变质的食物后感到恶心。 | 近义词: ill, unwell, nauseated, disgusted | 反义词: healthy, well |词性: 形容词
