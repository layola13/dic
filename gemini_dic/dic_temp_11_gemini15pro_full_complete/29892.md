cauterises:/ˈkɔːtəraɪzɪz/
|v.|烧灼；烙；使麻木|To burn the skin or flesh of (a wound) with a heated instrument or caustic substance, typically to stop bleeding or prevent infection.  It can also mean to make insensitive; deaden.|The doctor cauterised the wound to stop the bleeding. 医生烧灼伤口以止血。|Synonyms: sear, burn, scorch|Antonyms: heal, soothe|Verb (Third-person singular simple present)
