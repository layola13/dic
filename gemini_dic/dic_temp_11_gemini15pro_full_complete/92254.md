hypervariability:/ˌhaɪpərˌvɛəriəˈbɪləti/| n.|高度变异性，高度可变性|The quality or state of being hypervariable; extreme variability or diversity, especially in a gene sequence.|The hypervariability of this region of the genome makes it a useful target for DNA fingerprinting.  这个基因组区域的高度变异性使其成为DNA指纹识别的有用目标。|近义词：variability, diversity, heterogeneity|反义词：uniformity, homogeneity|词性:名词

Etymology:
hyper- (prefix): from Greek, meaning "over, above, beyond"
variable (adjective): from Late Middle English, from Old French, from Latin variabilis, from variare "to change, vary"
-ity (suffix): from Latin -itas, used to form abstract nouns from adjectives
