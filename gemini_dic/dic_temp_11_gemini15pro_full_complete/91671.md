hyperchromatism:/ˌhaɪpərˈkrəʊməˌtɪzəm/
noun|深染色，增色性|Increased absorption of ultraviolet light by a biological macromolecule, especially DNA, due to a loss of ordered structure.|The hyperchromatism of the DNA sample indicated denaturation.|近义词: hyperchromicity|反义词: hypochromatism|词性:n.
Etymology:  From hyper- + -chromatism, from Greek χρῶμα (khrôma, “color”).
