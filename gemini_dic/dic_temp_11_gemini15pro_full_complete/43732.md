creekfish: /ˈkrɪːkfɪʃ/ | n. |溪鱼，小河鱼|A fish that lives in a creek.  A small freshwater fish inhabiting creeks or streams.|The creekfish darted among the rocks in the shallow water. 溪鱼在浅水中的岩石间快速游动。|Example: We caught several creekfish while fishing in the mountain stream. 我们在山间小溪钓鱼时抓到了几条小河鱼。|Synonyms: minnow, darter, small fry|Antonyms: ocean fish, saltwater fish|Noun


Etymology:
'Creek' comes from Old Norse 'kriki' meaning "bend, corner." 'Fish' comes from Old English 'fisc.'  The word is a compound noun formed by combining these two words.
