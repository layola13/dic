internalized:/ɪnˈtɜːrnəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 内化的，内在化的 |  Made a part of one's nature by learning or unconscious assimilation.  Absorbed and integrated into one's own beliefs, values, and behavior. |  He had internalized his parents' values. 他已经将父母的价值观内化了。 | Example:  She had internalized the societal pressure to be thin. 她已经内化了社会上要求苗条的压力。| Synonyms: integrated, assimilated, incorporated, absorbed | Antonyms: externalized, rejected, repudiated | adjective

internalize:/ɪnˈtɜːrnəlaɪz/ | v. | 内化 | To make (attitudes or behavior) part of one's nature by learning or unconscious assimilation. | She internalized her anger. 她把愤怒藏在心里。 | Example: He internalized the criticism and used it to improve his performance. 他听取了批评，并用它来改进自己的表现。 | Synonyms: incorporate, assimilate, absorb, integrate | Antonyms: externalize, reject, repudiate | verb
