dactylics:/dækˈtɪlɪks/| n.| 抑扬格诗, 抑扬格诗句 |A system of versification based on the rhythmic pattern of one long or stressed syllable followed by two short or unstressed syllables ( ¯ ˘ ˘ ).  A dactylic refers to a metrical foot consisting of one stressed syllable followed by two unstressed syllables.|The poet employed dactylics to create a flowing, rhythmic effect. 诗人运用抑扬格来营造流畅的节奏效果。|Synonyms: dactyls|Antonyms: None|Noun
