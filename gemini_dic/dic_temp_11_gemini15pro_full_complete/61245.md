emanatist:/ˈɛmənətɪst/| n.|流溢说信徒，流溢论者|A believer in emanationism, the doctrine that the universe derives from God by a series of emanations or descending steps.|The Neoplatonists were emanatists who believed the world was a series of progressively less perfect emanations from the One.|近义词：monist, idealist|反义词：materialist, creationist|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *emanatista, from emanare (“to flow out”), from ex- (“out of”) + manare (“to flow”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *men- (“to think”).
