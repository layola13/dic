Fehling:/ˈfeɪlɪŋ/ | adj. n.|费林, Fehling试剂/溶液|Fehling's solution is a chemical reagent used to differentiate between water-soluble carbohydrate and ketone functional groups, and as a test for reducing sugars and non-reducing sugars, supplementary to the Tollens' reagent test.  It was developed by German chemist Hermann Fehling in 1849.| Fehling's solution is used to test for the presence of glucose in urine.  费林溶液用于测试尿液中是否存在葡萄糖。|  Benedict's, Barfoed's (reagents/tests) | Tollens' (reagent/test)| adjective, noun

Etymology: Named after Hermann von Fehling, a German chemist who developed the test in the 19th century.
