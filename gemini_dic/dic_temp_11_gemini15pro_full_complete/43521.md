cravingnesses:/ˈkreɪvɪŋnɪsɪz/
n.
渴望，热望，瘾
The state of having strong desires or cravings, especially for something unhealthy.
He displayed alarming cravingnesses for sweets. 他对甜食表现出惊人的渴望。
Synonyms: desires, yearnings, hankerings, longings, lusts
Antonyms: indifference, apathy, aversion
Noun


Etymology:
From craving + -ness.  "Craving" comes from the Old English "crafian" meaning "to beg, demand, implore." The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
