expurgate:/ˈekspərɡeɪt/ | v. | 净化；删节；删除（不当之处） | To remove objectionable or offensive parts from a book, text, or other form of media.  |  The editor expurgated the novel before its publication, removing several sexually explicit passages.  编辑在小说出版前将其净化，删除了几个露骨的性描写段落。| synonyms: censor, bowdlerize, purge, cleanse | antonyms:  include, insert, add, retain | verb

expurgate:/ˈekspərɡeɪt/ | v. | 净化；删节；删除（不当之处） | To cleanse or purify something, often in a moral or religious sense. (less common usage) |  The priest sought to expurgate the temple of its unholy influences.  神父试图净化寺庙中不神圣的影响。| synonyms: purify, cleanse, sanitize | antonyms: contaminate, pollute, defile | verb
