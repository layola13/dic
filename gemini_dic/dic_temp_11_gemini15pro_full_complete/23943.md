breastbone:/ˈbrɛstˌbəʊn/ (UK) /ˈbrɛstˌboʊn/ (US)| n. |胸骨|The breastbone, also known as the sternum, is a long flat bone located in the center of the chest. It connects to the ribs via cartilage and forms the front of the rib cage, thus helping to protect the heart, lungs, and other vital organs.|The doctor checked his breastbone after the accident. 医生在事故后检查了他的胸骨。|Synonyms: sternum|Antonyms: None|Noun

breastbones:/ˈbrɛstˌbəʊnz/ (UK) /ˈbrɛstˌboʊnz/ (US)| n. |胸骨（复数）|Plural form of breastbone, referring to multiple sterna (although typically a person only has one).  This is more commonly used in a biological or zoological context when discussing multiple organisms.|The breastbones of birds are adapted for flight. 鸟类的胸骨是为了飞行而适应的。|Synonyms: sterna|Antonyms: None|Noun
