anisodactyl:/ˌænɪsəˈdæktɪl/ | adj. n.| 异趾型的；异趾动物 |Having unequal toes, specifically having three toes pointing forward and one pointing backward, characteristic of most perching birds.  Also, a bird with such feet.| Example: The robin is an anisodactyl, allowing it to perch easily on branches. 知更鸟是一种异趾鸟，这使得它可以很容易地栖息在树枝上。| Example:  Anisodactyl feet are an adaptation for arboreal life.  异趾足是适应树栖生活的一种特征。 |Synonym: zygodactyl (sometimes, but technically different),  |Antonym:  pamprodactyl (all toes pointing forward)|adjective, noun
