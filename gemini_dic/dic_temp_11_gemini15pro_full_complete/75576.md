fuddler:/ˈfʌdlər/| n.|笨手笨脚的人；糊涂的人；醉鬼|Someone who acts clumsily or confusedly, or a drunkard. |He was such a fuddler, he spilled his drink all over himself. 他真是个笨手笨脚的人，把饮料洒得自己一身都是。|Synonyms: blunderer, klutz, bungler, drunk, inebriate|Antonyms: adept, expert, skilled, sober|noun

fuddle:/ˈfʌdəl/| v.|使糊涂；灌醉|To confuse or make drunk. |The strong liquor quickly fuddled his senses. 烈酒很快让他神志不清了。|Synonyms: confuse, bewilder, intoxicate, inebriate|Antonyms: clarify, enlighten, sober|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fudlen, frequentative of Old English *fudian ("to feed, nourish").  The sense of "confuse" likely developed from the idea of being "stuffed" or "overfed" to the point of mental dullness.  The connection with alcohol likely arose from the effects of excessive drinking.
