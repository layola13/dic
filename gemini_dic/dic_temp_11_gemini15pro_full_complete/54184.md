disespouse:/ˌdɪsɪˈspaʊz/ /ˌdɪsɪˈspaʊs/ | vt. | 摒弃，抛弃（信仰、观点等）| to reject or abandon (a belief, cause, or person) publicly. |  She disespoused her earlier radical views. 她摒弃了她早先的激进观点。| 近义词：reject, renounce, abandon, repudiate | 反义词：adopt, embrace, support, uphold | 词性：动词

词源:
From dis- "apart, away" + espouse "to marry," figuratively "to take up, adopt."  Ultimately from Latin spondere "to promise solemnly, engage oneself."
