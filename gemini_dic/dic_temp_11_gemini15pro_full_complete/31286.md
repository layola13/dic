chaperonages:/ˈʃæpərəʊnɪdʒɪz/
n.
陪伴；监护；伴护
The act of chaperoning someone, or the instances of being chaperoned.  A young lady in the 19th century would require chaperonages to many social events. 十九世纪的年轻女士需要有人陪同参加许多社交活动。
例句：The chaperonages provided by her aunt felt more like surveillance than support.  她阿姨提供的陪伴与其说是支持，不如说是监视。

例句：Her memories of school dances were filled with awkward chaperonages and stilted conversations. 她对学校舞会的记忆充满了尴尬的陪伴和生硬的对话。
近义词：escorting, supervision, guidance
反义词：neglect, abandonment
词性: 名词(plural)

chaperonage:/ˈʃæpərəʊnɪdʒ/
n.
陪伴；监护；伴护
The act of chaperoning someone, or the instance of being chaperoned. A young lady in the 19th century would require chaperonage to many social events. 十九世纪的年轻女士需要有人陪同参加许多社交活动。
例句：She resented the chaperonage of her older brother. 她讨厌哥哥的陪伴。

例句：The strict chaperonage imposed on young women in those days severely limited their freedom.  那时候对年轻女性施加的严格陪伴严重限制了她们的自由。
近义词：escort, supervision, guidance
反义词：neglect, abandonment
词性: 名词(singular)


词源: From French chaperonage, from chaperon (hood, chaperone). 最初指一种带帽子的斗篷，后来引申为戴这种斗篷的年长妇女陪伴年轻女性参加社交活动，最终演变为“陪伴”的含义。
