cycloganoidei:/ˌsaɪkloʊɡəˈnɔɪdiːaɪ/
|n.|  圆鳞鱼类|An extinct group of primitive, heavily armored fishes characterized by thick, rhomboid scales that were hinged together. They lived from the Devonian to the Permian periods.|The cycloganoidei were an important group of fishes in the Paleozoic era.|近义词:  palaeonisciformes (more general, encompassing a wider group)|反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific extinct group. Could consider "modern fish" as a broad contrast.)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From cyclo- (circular) + ganoid (type of scale) + -ei (plural suffix).  The "cyclo-" refers to the arrangement of the scales. "Ganoid" refers to the hard, enamel-like substance ganoin that covered the scales.
