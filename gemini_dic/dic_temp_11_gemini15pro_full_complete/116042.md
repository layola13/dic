martens:/ˈmɑːrtənz/ /ˈmɑːrtn̩z/ | n. | 马丁靴; (尤指) 德国产的马丁靴 | A type of sturdy work boot, often with a thick, air-cushioned sole, originally made in Germany. | She wore her favorite pair of Dr. Martens. 她穿了她最喜欢的马丁靴。|  近义词: boots, work boots | 反义词: sandals, slippers | noun

martens:/ˈmɑːrtənz/ /ˈmɑːrtn̩z/ | n. |貂;貂皮|The fur of a marten, or a garment made from it. (This meaning is less common than the boot meaning)|The queen wore a luxurious martens coat. 女王穿着一件奢华的貂皮大衣。|近义词: sable, mink | 反义词: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the name of Dr. Klaus Märtens, a German doctor who developed the air-cushioned sole during World War II. The boots were initially popular among workers and later became a fashion item associated with various subcultures.  For the animal meaning, from Middle English *'Marten', from Old French 'martre', ultimately of Germanic origin.
