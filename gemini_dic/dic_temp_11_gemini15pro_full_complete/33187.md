chromolithography:/ˌkroʊməlɪˈθɑːɡrəfi/ | n. | 彩色石版印刷 | A method of printing in color from a series of stone or zinc plates, each carrying a different ink for a separate color. | Example: Chromolithography allowed for the mass production of vibrant and detailed images in the 19th century.  19世纪，彩色石版印刷使得大量制作鲜艳而精细的图像成为可能。| Example:  The antique book contained beautiful illustrations created using chromolithography. 这本古董书包含了用彩色石版印刷术制作的精美插图。 | Synonym: color lithography, chromo | Antonym:  N/A | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From *chromo-* (color) + *-litho-* (stone) + *-graphy* (writing).  Ultimately from Greek *chrōma* (color), *lithos* (stone), and *graphein* (to write).
