allomorphic:/ˌæləˈmɔːrfɪk/ | adj. | 同质异形的；同质异象的 |  Relating to or being an allomorph (a variant form of a morpheme).  A morpheme is the smallest grammatical unit in a language. Allomorphs are different forms of the same morpheme that occur in different contexts. | Example: The English plural morpheme has several allomorphs, including "-s," "-es," and "-en."  英语复数词素有几个同质异形体，包括“-s”、“-es”和“-en”。

Example: The past tense marker in English has allomorphic variants like "-ed," "-t," and vowel changes (e.g., sing/sang). 英语过去时标记有同质异形变体，如“-ed”、“-t”和元音变化（例如，sing/sang）。| 近义词: variant, alternative | 反义词: invariant, unchanging | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From allo- (Greek 'other') + morph- (Greek 'form') + -ic (adjective-forming suffix).
