brasher:/ˈbræʃər/| adj. n.|傲慢的；自以为是的；喧闹的；浮夸的；爱吹嘘的人；自以为是的人|Someone who is brash is noisy, self-assertive, and too confident. A brash person is loud, confident, and aggressive in a way that other people may find annoying. |例句：He was brash, cocky, and arrogant. 他傲慢、自大、狂妄。

例句：The brash young executive ignored the advice of his colleagues.  这个傲慢的年轻主管无视了他同事的建议。|近义词：bold, cocky, arrogant, impudent, brazen, audacious, forward, pushy|反义词：humble, modest, diffident, timid, reserved|词性:形容词,名词

brashness:/ˈbræʃnɪs/| n.|傲慢；自以为是；喧闹；浮夸|The quality of being brash; noisy, self-assertive confidence.|例句：His brashness annoyed many of his coworkers. 他的傲慢惹恼了许多同事。

例句：Her initial brashness gave way to a more subdued manner as she grew older.  她年轻时的傲慢随着年龄的增长而变得更加内敛。|近义词：audacity, boldness, arrogance, impudence|反义词：humility, modesty, diffidence|词性:名词

词源：
From Middle English *brasch, *brask (“rash, impetuous, brittle”), perhaps from Old French *brasque, from Old Occitan *brasca (“embers, twigs”), possibly related to Italian frasca (“branch, twig”).  Influenced by or related to brash (“rubbish, refuse”).
