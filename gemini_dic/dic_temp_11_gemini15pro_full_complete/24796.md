brotherhoods:/ˈbrʌðərhʊdz/| n.|兄弟情谊；手足之情；同业公会；兄弟会|An association of men for a particular purpose or support, often characterized by strong bonds of fellowship and loyalty.  A feeling of community and fellowship.|例句：The brotherhoods of firefighters are known for their camaraderie. 消防员的兄弟会以其友谊而闻名。

例句：The ancient brotherhoods held secret rituals. 古老的兄弟会举行秘密仪式。|近义词：fraternities, associations, guilds, unions, fellowships, societies|反义词:rivalry, antagonism, hostility|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *brōðorhād, from brōðor (“brother”) +‎ -hād (“-hood”). Cognate with Dutch broederschap, German Bruderschaft.
