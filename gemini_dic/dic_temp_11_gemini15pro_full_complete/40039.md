consummate:/ˈkɑːnsəmˌeɪt/ /ˈkɒnsəmeɪt/| v. adj.|完成；使完美；圆房|To complete (something, such as a business deal, agreement, or marriage) perfectly or legally; to make (something) complete or perfect|例句：The deal was consummated with a handshake. 这笔交易以握手成交。|近义词：complete, finalize, conclude, perfect, fulfill|反义词:begin, start, initiate, ruin, spoil|词性:动词/形容词

consummate:/ˈkɑːnsəmɪt/ /ˈkɒnsəmɪt/| adj.|完美的；圆满的；技艺高超的|Complete or perfect in every respect; supremely skilled|例句：He's a consummate professional. 他是一位技艺高超的专业人士。|近义词：perfect, complete, accomplished, superb, masterful|反义词:imperfect, incomplete, flawed, unskilled, amateur|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin consummatus, past participle of consummare "to sum up, complete, perfect," from Latin com-, intensive prefix (see com-), + summa "sum, total" (see sum (n.2)). The original sense was "to sum up, summarize," hence "to bring to an end, finish, complete."
