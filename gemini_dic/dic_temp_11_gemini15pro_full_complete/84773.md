hands-down:/ˌhændz ˈdaʊn/| adv. adj.|轻松地，毫无疑问地；明显的，无可争议的|Originally a horse racing term, referring to a jockey so far ahead that they can drop their hands and loosen the reins.  Now used figuratively to describe something achieved easily or something that is clearly the best.|She won the election hands-down. 她轻松地赢得了选举。|Synonyms: easily, effortlessly, decisively, undoubtedly, clearly|Antonyms: barely, narrowly, with difficulty|adverb

hands-down:/ˌhændz ˈdaʊn/| adj.|轻松的，毫无疑问的；明显的，无可争议的|Used as an adjective before a noun to describe something that is clearly the best.|It was a hands-down victory. 这是一场毫无疑问的胜利。|Synonyms: clear, decisive, undisputed, obvious|Antonyms: doubtful, questionable, uncertain|adjective
