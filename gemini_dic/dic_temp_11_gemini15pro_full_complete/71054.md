first-past-the-post:/ˌfɜːrst pɑːst ðə ˈpoʊst/ |/ˌfɜːst pɑːst ðə ˈpəʊst/| adj. n.|领先者当选制；简单多数当选制|An electoral system in which the candidate who receives more votes than any other candidate wins the election.  The winner does not need a majority of the total votes cast, only a plurality.|The UK uses a first-past-the-post system for general elections.|Synonym: winner-take-all, plurality voting system|Antonym: proportional representation|adjective, noun
