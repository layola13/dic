backstabbing: /ˈbækˌstæbɪŋ/ | n. adj. | 背后捅刀子；暗箭伤人 | The act of criticizing someone unfairly behind their back.  It can also be used as an adjective to describe such behavior. |  He was accused of backstabbing his colleagues to get ahead.  他被指控为了晋升而在背后捅同事刀子。 |  She felt betrayed by her friend's backstabbing comments. 她觉得被朋友背后捅刀子的评论出卖了。 | 近义词: treacherous, deceitful, disloyal, two-faced | 反义词: loyal, supportive, honest | 词性: noun, adjective

backstab: /ˈbækˌstæb/ | v. | 背后捅刀子；暗箭伤人 | To criticize someone unfairly behind their back. | He backstabbed his best friend for a promotion. 他为了升职而背后捅了他最好的朋友一刀。 |  Don't backstab your colleagues; it will only create a toxic work environment. 不要在背后捅同事刀子，这只会造成有害的工作环境。 | 近义词: betray, double-cross, undermine | 反义词: support, defend, champion | 词性: verb

词源:  "Back" (referring to the back of a person) + "stab" (to wound with a pointed weapon).  The term is metaphorical, referring to a betrayal of trust that feels like a physical attack from behind.
