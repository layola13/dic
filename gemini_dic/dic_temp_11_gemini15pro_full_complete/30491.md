centrosome: /ˈsɛntrəˌsoʊm/ (US), /ˈsɛntrəˌsəʊm/ (UK)| n. |中心体|An organelle near the nucleus of a cell which contains the centrioles (in animal cells) and from which the spindle fibers develop in cell division.|The centrosome plays a crucial role in cell division. 中心体在细胞分裂中起着至关重要的作用。|Synonyms: microtubule organizing center (MTOC)|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

centrosome: /ˈsɛntrəˌsoʊm/ (US), /ˈsɛntrəˌsəʊm/ (UK)| n. |中心体|A small region of cytoplasm adjacent to the nucleus containing the centrioles and acting as the main microtubule-organizing center of the animal cell.|The position of the centrosome influences the shape of the cell. 中心体的位置会影响细胞的形状。|Synonyms: microtubule organizing center (MTOC)|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
