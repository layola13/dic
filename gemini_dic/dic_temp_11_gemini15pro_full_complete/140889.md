on your guard:/ɒn jɔː(r) ɡɑːd/
|adj.|警惕的，提防的|Being watchful and prepared for danger or trouble. |Example 1:  Stay on your guard when walking alone at night.  晚上一个人走的时候要保持警惕。

Example 2:  The police warned residents to be on their guard against burglars. 警察警告居民要提防窃贼。|Synonyms:  wary, cautious, vigilant, alert, watchful|Antonyms:  careless, heedless, unguarded, unwary|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the French phrase "en garde," meaning "on guard" or "on watch." This ultimately derives from the Germanic root *wardon, meaning "to watch" or "to guard."
