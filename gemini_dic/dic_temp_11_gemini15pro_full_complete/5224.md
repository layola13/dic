alliteration:/əˌlɪtəˈreɪʃn/| n.|头韵，押头韵|The repetition of the same sounds—usually initial consonants of words or of stressed syllables—in any sequence of neighboring words.|Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers is a classic example of alliteration.|consonance, assonance (vowel sounds)|  |noun|
