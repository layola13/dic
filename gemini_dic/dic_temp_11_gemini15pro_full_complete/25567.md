bailiff: /ˈbeɪlɪf/| n.|法警；执行官；地主代理人|A bailiff is an official of a court of law who is employed to seize property, evict tenants, etc. as ordered by the court, or to sell possessions in order to pay back a debt.|The bailiffs arrived to evict the family from their home.|Synonyms: sheriff, officer, process server|Antonyms: N/A|noun

bum-bailiff: /ˈbʌm ˌbeɪlɪf/| n.|私设法警；非法收债人；恶棍|A bum-bailiff is a derogatory term for a bailiff, especially one perceived as unscrupulous or acting illegally, or someone who impersonates a bailiff to collect debts.|He hired a couple of bum-bailiffs to intimidate his tenants into paying.|Synonyms: thug, goon, enforcer, strong-arm man|Antonyms: N/A|noun
