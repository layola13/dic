oversecularizations:/ˌoʊvərˌsɛkjələrɪˈzeɪʃənz/
|n.|过度世俗化|The process of becoming excessively secular, often to the point of neglecting or rejecting religious or spiritual values.|The rapid oversecularizations of some societies have led to a decline in traditional moral values. 一些社会快速过度世俗化导致了传统道德价值观的衰落。

The oversecularizations of public education has been a subject of much debate. 公共教育的过度世俗化一直是备受争议的话题。|近义词:secularization|反义词:sacralization|词性:名词


词源:
"Over-" (prefix): From Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond, excessively."
"Secularization": From Late Latin "saecularis," meaning "worldly, belonging to an age or generation," ultimately from Latin "saeculum," meaning "age, generation, world."  The suffix "-ization" denotes the process of becoming or making something.
