noticers:/ˈnoʊtɪsərz/ | n. | 注意者；观察者；察觉者 | People who are particularly observant and attentive to details, often noticing things that others miss. |  The noticers in the group quickly spotted the error in the report.  小组中细心的人很快发现了报告中的错误。| 近义词: observers, perceivers, detectors | 反义词: ignorers, oblivious individuals | 词性: Noun

Etymology: From the verb "notice" + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person who performs the action of the verb). "Notice" comes from Old French "noticer" (to note, to make known), from Latin "notitia" (knowledge, acquaintance).
