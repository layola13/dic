antirealist:/ˌæntiˈriːəlɪst/ | n. adj.|反实在论者，反实在论的|A person who believes that reality is not independent of our minds or that we cannot have true knowledge of reality.|例句：The antirealist argued that scientific theories are just useful tools, not descriptions of the real world. 反实在论者认为，科学理论只是有用的工具，而不是对现实世界的描述。|近义词：idealist, subjectivist, relativist|反义词：realist|词性：名词，形容词

antirealism:/ˌæntiˈriːəlɪzəm/ | n.|反实在论|The philosophical doctrine that reality is not independent of our minds or that we cannot have true knowledge of reality.|例句：Antirealism challenges the traditional view that science can provide an objective picture of the world. 反实在论挑战了科学可以提供客观世界图景的传统观点。|近义词：idealism, subjectivism, relativism|反义词：realism|词性：名词

词源：anti- (against) + realism.  "Anti-" comes from Greek "anti" meaning "against, opposite of". "Realism" derives from Late Latin "realis" (relating to things) from Latin "res" (thing).
