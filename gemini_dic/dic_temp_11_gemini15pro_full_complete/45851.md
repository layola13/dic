cut-away:/ˈkʌtəˌweɪ/ (US), /ˈkʌtəweɪ/ (UK)
n.|剖面图；剖视图；插播镜头；燕尾服|A view or diagram that shows the internal structure of something, as if it has been cut open. A brief scene inserted into a film or television programme that shows something that happened earlier or elsewhere.|The textbook included a cut-away of the human heart. 这本教科书包含了一张人体心脏的剖面图。|synonyms: cross-section, sectional view, cutaway drawing|antonyms: N/A
词性: 名词

cut-away:/ˈkʌtəˌweɪ/ (US), /ˈkʌtəweɪ/ (UK)
adj.|剖开的；挖空的；燕尾服式的|Having a part cut away to reveal the interior or to create a specific shape.  Often used to describe clothing, especially formal wear.|She wore a stunning cut-away gown. 她穿着一件惊艳的燕尾服式长袍。|synonyms: sectioned, dissected, revealing|antonyms: closed, whole, covered
词性: 形容词


词源:  From the verb phrase "cut away," meaning to remove a part by cutting.  The noun and adjective forms evolved from this original meaning.
