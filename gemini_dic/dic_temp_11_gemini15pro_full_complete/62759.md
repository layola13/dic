engraff:/ɪnˈɡræf/ /ɛnˈɡræf/ | v.  n. |嫁接，接枝；移植；插入；（思想、习惯等）灌输|To insert or implant (a shoot, bud, or scion) as a graft. Also, to transplant or introduce (something) into something else, as an idea or habit.|He carefully engrafted the apple scion onto the rootstock. 他小心翼翼地把苹果枝嫁接到砧木上。|Synonyms: graft, implant, insert, transplant, inculcate|Antonyms: remove, extract, excise|verb

engraff:/ɪnˈɡræf/ /ɛnˈɡræf/ | n. |嫁接，接枝|The act or process of engrafting.|The engraff was successful, and the new branch began to grow. 嫁接成功了，新的枝条开始生长。|Synonyms: graft, implantation, insertion|Antonyms: removal, extraction|noun
