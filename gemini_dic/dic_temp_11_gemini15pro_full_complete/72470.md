flunkeyiana:/ˌflʌŋkiˈɑːnə/
noun
谄媚行为；奴才习气
The excessive flattery and subservience displayed by the staff was pure flunkeyiana.
员工表现出的过度奉承和卑躬屈膝纯粹是奴才习气。
Etymology: From "flunkey" (a liveried servant or one who behaves like one) + "-iana" (a suffix used to form nouns indicating things related to or characteristic of a person or place).  "Flunkey" itself is of uncertain origin, possibly from Scottish or Northern English dialect.
Synonyms: servility, obsequiousness, sycophancy, toadyism
Antonyms: independence, assertiveness, self-respect
