glucosinolate: /ˌɡluːkəˈsɪnəleɪt/ | n. | 芥子油苷 | A class of organic compounds that contain sulfur and nitrogen and are derived from glucose and an amino acid. They are found in many plants, particularly cruciferous vegetables like broccoli, cabbage, and kale, and are responsible for their characteristic pungent taste. |  Broccoli and other cruciferous vegetables are rich in glucosinolates.  西兰花和其他十字花科蔬菜富含芥子油苷。 | synonym: thioglucoside | antonym: N/A | noun
