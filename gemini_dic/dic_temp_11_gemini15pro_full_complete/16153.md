baniya: /ˈbɑːniːə/ (US), /ˈbʌnɪə/ (UK) | n. | 商人，店主 (尤指印度教种姓中的商人) | A merchant, trader, or shopkeeper, especially a member of a Hindu trading caste. |  例句：The baniya opened his shop early in the morning.  商人一大早就开了店。| 近义词：merchant, trader, shopkeeper, businessman | 反义词: customer, buyer | noun

baniya: /ˈbɑːniːə/ (US), /ˈbʌnɪə/ (UK) | n. | (贬义) 斤斤计较的人，守财奴 | (derogatory) A person who is perceived as being overly concerned with money and profits; a miser. | 例句：He's such a baniya, always haggling over the price. 他真是个守财奴，总是讨价还价。| 近义词：miser, skinflint, tightwad | 反义词: spendthrift, philanthropist | noun
