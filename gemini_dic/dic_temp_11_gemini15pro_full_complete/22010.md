boiling-point:/ˈbɔɪlɪŋ pɔɪnt/| n.|沸点|The temperature at which a liquid boils and changes to a vapor.|The boiling point of water is 100 degrees Celsius.  水的沸点是100摄氏度。|synonyms: boiling temperature|antonyms: freezing point|noun

Etymology:
From boil + -ing + point.  "Boil" comes from Old French "boillir," ultimately from Latin "bullire" (to bubble). "Point" comes from Old French "point," ultimately from Latin "punctum" (a prick, a point).
