declaratives:/dɪˈklærətɪvz/| n.|陈述句|Statements that declare or assert something; grammatical mood used for statements of fact, opinion, etc. |例句：Declaratives are the most common type of sentence. 陈述句是最常见的句子类型。

例句：He used declaratives to express his opinion. 他用陈述句表达他的观点。|近义词：statements, assertions, pronouncements|反义词：questions, interrogatives, imperatives|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *declaratif, from Late Latin dēclarātīvus, from Latin dēclārō (“I make clear, reveal, explain; I declare, proclaim”), from dē (“down, completely”) + clārō (“I make clear, brighten”).

PHRASE->
There are not many common phrases using "declaratives" directly.  It's a grammatical term more than a word used in everyday phrases.  However, here are examples of declarative sentences showing different sentiments:

The sky is blue. = 天是蓝色的。
I love ice cream. = 我喜欢冰淇淋。
I am tired. = 我累了。
He is a good friend. = 他是一个好朋友。
She works hard. = 她工作努力。
