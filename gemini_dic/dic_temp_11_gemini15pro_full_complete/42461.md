counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/ | adj. n. |反事实的；反事实的陈述| relating to or expressing what has not happened or is not the case; a claim, hypothesis, or other proposition that asserts or implies something contrary to fact. |例句：Historians sometimes engage in counterfactual history, speculating on what might have happened if events had unfolded differently. 历史学家有时会进行反事实的历史研究，推测如果事件以不同的方式展开会发生什么。

例句：It's a counterfactual statement to say that the South would have won the Civil War if it had better generals. 说如果南方有更好的将军就能赢得内战，这是一个反事实的陈述。 | 近义词：hypothetical, conjectural, speculative, unreal, untrue | 反义词：factual, real, actual | 词性：adjective, noun

counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/ | n. |反事实的陈述| a claim, hypothesis, or other proposition that asserts or implies something contrary to fact. |例句：The author explored the counterfactual of a world without the internet. 作者探讨了一个没有互联网的反事实世界。

例句：Considering counterfactuals can help us understand the importance of certain historical events. 考虑反事实可以帮助我们理解某些历史事件的重要性。 | 近义词：hypothesis, conjecture, speculation | 反义词：fact, reality | 词性：noun
