old-worldliness:/ˌoʊld ˈwɜːrldlinəs/
|n.|旧世界的风格；旧式；古朴|The room was furnished with an air of old-worldliness. 房间布置得古色古香。|English Explanation:  Characteristic of or adhering to the customs, values, or styles of an earlier time, especially those of Europe before the modern era.  It suggests a charm or quaintness associated with the past.|例句：Her old-worldliness was charming. 她的古朴气质很迷人。

例句：He maintained an air of old-worldliness in his dress and manners. 他在衣着和举止上保持着旧世界的风格。
|近义词： quaintness, traditionalism, formality, old-fashionedness|反义词： modernity, contemporary, modernism|词性: 名词

old-worldly:/ˌoʊld ˈwɜːrldli/
|adj.|旧世界的；旧式的；古朴的|She had an old-worldly charm about her. 她身上有一种旧世界的魅力。| English Explanation: Having the characteristics of the old world; old-fashioned; quaint.  It often implies a charming or nostalgic quality associated with past times.|例句：The hotel had an old-worldly atmosphere. 这家酒店有一种旧世界的氛围。

例句：His old-worldly manners were refreshing. 他那老派的举止令人耳目一新。
|近义词：quaint, traditional, formal, old-fashioned|反义词：modern, contemporary, newfangled|词性: 形容词


词源:
From "old world" + "-liness" (suffix forming abstract nouns denoting a state or quality). "Old world" refers to Europe, Asia, and Africa, especially before the discovery of America.
