counteractively:/ˌkaʊntərˈæktɪvli/
adv.
抵消地，对抗地
acting in opposition to something; in a way that prevents something from having its intended effect.
例句：He added sugar counteractively to neutralize the bitterness of the coffee. 他反作用地加了糖来中和咖啡的苦味。

例句：Her efforts to improve communication were counteracted by her colleagues' reluctance to share information. 她为改善沟通所做的努力被同事们不愿分享信息的举动抵消了。


近义词：adversely, opposingly, antagonistically
反义词：supportively, constructively, synergistically
词性：adverb

词源:
"counter-" (against) + "act" (to do something) + "-ive" (forming adjectives) + "-ly" (forming adverbs).  "Counter-" comes from the Old French "contre-" and ultimately from the Latin "contra" (against). "Act" comes from the Latin "actus" (a doing, a driving).
