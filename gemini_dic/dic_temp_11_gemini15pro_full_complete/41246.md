corban:/ˈkɔːrbæn/ (US), /ˈkɔːbɑːn/ (UK)| n. | 奉献物，禁献物 | Something dedicated to God; a gift devoted to religious purposes; also, by extension, anything prohibited or forbidden.  It derives from the Hebrew word קָרְבָּן (qorbān), meaning "offering."  In the New Testament, Jesus criticized the Pharisees for using "corban" as a way to avoid their responsibilities to their parents.  |  "He declared all his possessions 'corban' so he wouldn't have to support his aging parents." 他宣布他所有的财产都是“奉献物”，这样他就不必赡养年迈的父母了。 |  Example: The temple received many corbans during the festival.  寺庙在节日期间收到了许多奉献物。|  Synonyms: offering, oblation, sacrifice, dedication | Antonyms: desecration, profanation | noun
