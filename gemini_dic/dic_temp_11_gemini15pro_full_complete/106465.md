kowtow:/ˌkaʊˈtaʊ/| n. vi.|叩头，磕头;  卑躬屈膝，唯命是从|An act of kneeling and touching the ground with the forehead in worship or submission.  A servile, submissive act.|例句：The emperor demanded a kowtow from all foreign emissaries. 皇帝要求所有外国使节叩头。|近义词：grovel, fawn, prostrate oneself|反义词：defy, resist|词性:名词, 动词

kowtow:/ˌkaʊˈtaʊ/| vi.|叩头，磕头;  卑躬屈膝，唯命是从|To kneel and touch the ground with the forehead in worship or submission. To act in a servile or submissive manner.|例句：She refused to kowtow to the unreasonable demands of her boss. 她拒绝向老板的无理要求卑躬屈膝。|近义词：grovel, fawn, prostrate oneself|反义词：defy, resist|词性:动词
