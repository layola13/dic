metathetically:/ˌmɛtəˈθɛtɪkli/| adv.|换位地，语音转位地|Relating to or characterized by metathesis, the transposition of sounds or letters in a word.|The word "aks" for "ask" is a common example of metathesis, formed metathetically.|近义词: transposedly|反义词: regularly|词性:副词

Etymology:
From metathesis + -al + -ly.  Metathesis comes from Greek *metáthesis*  "transposition," from *metatithénai*  "to transpose," from *metá* "change" + *tithénai* "to put, place."
