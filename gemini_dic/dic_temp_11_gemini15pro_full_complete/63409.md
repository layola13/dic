entrainment:/ɪnˈtreɪnmənt/| n.|夹带，带动，同步|The entrainment of air bubbles in the concrete weakened its structure.  混凝土中气泡的夹带削弱了其结构。|例句：The entrainment of air in the flowing water increased the oxygen levels. 流动的水中夹带的空气增加了氧气含量。

例句：The musician's rhythmic drumming caused an entrainment of the dancers' movements.  音乐家有节奏的鼓声带动了舞者的动作。|近义词：synchronization, coupling, incorporation|反义词: decoupling, separation|词性:名词

entrainment:/ɪnˈtreɪnmənt/| n.|输送，运送(较少用)|The entrainment of sediment by the river created a delta at its mouth. 河流对沉积物的输送在其河口形成了一个三角洲。|例句：The entrainment of troops by train was quicker than by truck.  用火车运送部队比用卡车快。|近义词：transport, conveyance|反义词: N/A|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French entraîner (to drag along, carry away), from en- (in, into) + traîner (to drag, pull). Ultimately from Latin trahere (to draw, pull).
