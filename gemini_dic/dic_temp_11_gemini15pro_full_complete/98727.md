insurgently:/ɪnˈsɜːdʒəntli/| adv.|叛乱地，暴动地；反抗地|In a rebellious or uprising manner; characteristic of an insurgent.  |The crowd reacted insurgently to the announcement of the new tax. 群众对新税的宣布反应强烈，表示反抗。|The soldiers fought insurgently against the oppressive regime. 士兵们奋起反抗压迫政权。|近义词：rebelliously, mutinously, seditiously|反义词：obediently, submissively|词性:副词

insurgent:/ɪnˈsɜːdʒənt/| n. adj.|叛乱者，起义者；反抗的，暴动的|n. A person who rises in forcible opposition to lawful authority, especially a person who engages in armed resistance to a government or its laws. adj. Rising in revolt; rebellious. |The insurgents stormed the government building. 叛乱分子袭击了政府大楼。|The insurgent activity threatened the stability of the region. 叛乱活动威胁着该地区的稳定。|近义词：rebel, revolutionary, mutineer|反义词：loyalist, conformist|词性:名词，形容词

insurgency:/ɪnˈsɜːdʒənsi/| n.|叛乱，起义，暴动|An act or instance of rising in revolt, rebellion, or insurrection against civil authority or an established government.|The government struggled to quell the insurgency. 政府努力平息叛乱。|The insurgency was fueled by widespread discontent. 叛乱是由普遍不满情绪引发的。|近义词：rebellion, revolt, uprising|反义词：obedience, submission|词性:名词


词源:  From Late Latin *insurgentem (nominative insurgens), present participle of insurgere "to rise up," from in- "up, upon" + surgere "to rise."
