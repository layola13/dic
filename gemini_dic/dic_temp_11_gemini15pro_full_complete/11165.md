archeus:/ˈɑːrkiəs/| n.|活力；精气；元气|A supposed life force or principle of the universe, formerly believed to be the ultimate cause of all vital phenomena.|The alchemists believed that the archeus was the vital force that animated all matter.|近义词：vital principle, life force, anima,  élan vital|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "inertness", "lifelessness")|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *archeus*, from Greek *arkhḗ* meaning "beginning, origin, principle, rule, sovereignty".
