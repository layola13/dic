dock:/dɑːk (US) /dɒk (UK)| n.|码头，船坞；被告席；尾巴的骨肉部分；酸模|A dock is a structure built on the shore of or projecting into a harbor, river, etc., so that vessels may moor alongside for loading and unloading or repairs.|The ship is in dock for repairs. 这艘船停靠在码头进行维修。|近义词：pier, wharf, harbor|反义词:|词性:n.


dock:/dɑːk (US) /dɒk (UK)| vt.|使（船）入坞；扣除（工资）；剪短（尾巴）|To bring or come into dock; to deduct from (wages); to cut short (a tail).|They docked the ship for repairs. 他们把船停靠在码头进行维修。|近义词：berth, moor|反义词:|词性:vt.


docks:/dɑːks (US) /dɒks (UK)| n.|码头，船坞（dock 的复数形式）|Plural form of "dock," referring to multiple structures for mooring vessels.|The docks were busy with activity. 码头上熙熙攘攘。|近义词：piers, wharves, harbors|反义词:|词性:n.


词源: From Middle Dutch *docke "cage, enclosed space," probably related to Old English *docca "pit, trench."
