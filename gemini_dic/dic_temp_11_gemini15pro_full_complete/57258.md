downlook: /ˌdaʊnˈlʊk/ | v. | 蔑视，轻视，瞧不起 | To look down on someone or something; to regard with contempt or disdain. | Example:  He tends to downlook people who haven't had a college education. 他倾向于瞧不起没有受过大学教育的人。| Example: The wealthy businessman downlooked the struggling artist.  这位富有的商人轻视这位苦苦挣扎的艺术家。 | Synonyms: despise, disdain, scorn, condescend to, patronize | Antonyms: admire, respect, esteem, value | verb


ETYMOLOGY->

The word "downlook" is a combination of the adverb "down" and the verb "look."  "Down" in this context implies a position of superiority, while "look" suggests the act of observing. The combination creates the meaning of looking down upon someone with disdain.
