honeysuckle:/ˈhʌnɪˌsʌkəl/ (US), /ˈhʌniˌsʌkl/ (UK)| n.|金银花；忍冬|A shrub or climbing plant with fragrant flowers, typically cream or pink in color.  The flowers produce a sweet nectar that attracts hummingbirds and other pollinators.|The air was thick with the scent of honeysuckle.|Synonyms: woodbine, Lonicera|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a plant.  Could consider plants with unpleasant smells, like stinking hellebore.)|noun
