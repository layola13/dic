operculiferous: /ˌɒpərˈkjuːlɪfərəs/
adjective
有鳃盖的；具有鳃盖的
Bearing or furnished with an operculum (a bony flap that covers and protects the gills).
Example: Many operculiferous snails are found in freshwater habitats. 许多有鳃盖的蜗牛生活在淡水栖息地。
Example:  The scientist studied the operculiferous fish to understand its respiratory system.  科学家研究了这种有鳃盖的鱼，以了解它的呼吸系统。
Synonym: operculate
Antonym: (No direct antonym, but could consider "without an operculum")
Word type: Adjective


Etymology:

From Latin *operculum (cover, lid) + -fer (bearing).  The Latin *operculum is related to operire (to cover).
