epoophoron:/ˌɛpoʊˈɒfərɒn/ | n. | 附睾旁管 | A rudimentary organ that lies in the mesosalpinx between the ovary and the uterine tube, representing the remains of the tubules of the Wolffian body or mesonephros of the embryo. It consists of a number of small rudimentary tubules that converge into a longitudinal duct which may open into the duct of Gartner. | The epoophoron is a vestigial structure in females. 附睾旁管是女性体内的一个退化结构。| Synonyms: parovarium, organ of Rosenmüller | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for an anatomical structure) | noun
