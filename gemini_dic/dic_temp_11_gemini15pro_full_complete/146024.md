overdramatized: /ˌōvərˈdræməˌtīzd/ | adj. | 过于戏剧化的，夸张的 |  Exaggerated the drama or emotional intensity of a situation or event.  It implies making something seem more significant or impactful than it actually is, often for effect. | She overdramatized the story of her minor fall, making it sound like a near-death experience. 她把轻微摔伤的故事夸大了，说得好像九死一生似的。 |  exaggerated, melodramatic, overblown, theatrical | understated, downplayed, realistic | adjective

overdramatize: /ˌōvərˈdræməˌtaɪz/ | v. |  过分戏剧化，夸大 | To portray something in an overly dramatic or exaggerated way. |  He tends to overdramatize his problems, turning small setbacks into major crises. 他倾向于夸大自己的问题，把小挫折变成大危机。 | exaggerate, embellish, amplify | understate, downplay | verb

Etymology:
"over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi) + "dramatize" (from "drama" + "-ize")
