disjunctively:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋktɪvli/ | adv. | 分离地，不相连地 | In logic, it refers to connecting propositions with "or," where the compound proposition is true if at least one of the individual propositions is true.  In other contexts, it means separately or distinctly. | The two issues must be considered disjunctively. 这两个问题必须分开考虑。| Synonyms: separately, individually, distinctly | Antonyms: conjunctively, jointly, together | adverb

disjunct:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋkt/ | adj. | 分离的，不相连的 | Characterized by separation or disunity. | The plot felt disjunct, jumping between unrelated scenes.  情节感觉不连贯，在不相关的场景之间跳跃。 | Synonyms: separate, disconnected, disunited | Antonyms: connected, joined, united | adjective

disjunction:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋkʃən/ | n. | 分离，分裂 | The act of separating or the state of being separated.  In logic, it's a compound proposition formed by joining two or more propositions with "or." | There is a growing disjunction between the rich and the poor. 贫富差距越来越大。 | Synonyms: separation, division, disunity | Antonyms: connection, union, unity | noun
