hermit:/ˈhɜːmɪt/ |n.|隐士；遁世者|A person who lives in solitude, often for religious reasons. |The hermit lived in a cave in the mountains. 这位隐士住在山里的一个洞穴里。|Synonyms: recluse, anchorite, solitary|Antonyms: socialite, extrovert|noun

hermitage:/ˈhɜːmɪtɪdʒ/ |n.|隐居处；修道院|The dwelling of a hermit; a secluded retreat. |The hermitage was nestled deep within the forest. 这座隐居处坐落在森林深处。|Synonyms: retreat, sanctuary, monastery|Antonyms: city, metropolis|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *ermite*, from Late Latin *eremita*, from Greek *erēmítēs* (desert dweller), from *erēmos* (desert, solitary). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *er- (to move, set in motion), perhaps with the sense of "going apart."
