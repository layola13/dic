determined: /dɪˈtɜːmɪnd/ (UK) /dɪˈtɜːrmɪnd/ (US)| adj.| 坚决的；有决心的；已确定的| Showing or having a firm intention or purpose; resolute.|  She was determined to finish the race, even with a sprained ankle. 她决心完成比赛，即使脚踝扭伤了。|例句：He was determined to win the championship. 他决心赢得冠军。|近义词：resolute, firm, decisive, persistent|反义词：hesitant, indecisive, wavering|词性:形容词

determined: /dɪˈtɜːmɪnd/ (UK) /dɪˈtɜːrmɪnd/ (US)| v. | （determine的过去式和过去分词）决定；确定；测定| (past tense and past participle of determine) To fix or establish conclusively and authoritatively; to decide.| The scientists determined the cause of the outbreak. 科学家们确定了疫情爆发的原因。|例句：They determined to travel to Europe next summer. 他们决定明年夏天去欧洲旅行。|近义词：decided, resolved, established, ascertained|反义词：undecided, unresolved|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *determinen*, from Latin dētermināre (“to limit, fix, bound, define”), from dē (“from, down from, away from, out of”) + termināre (“to limit, bound”), from terminus (“boundary, limit, end”).
