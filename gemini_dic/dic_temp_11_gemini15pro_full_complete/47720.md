decadent:/ˈdekədənt/ /ˈdɛkədənt/ | adj. n. | 衰落的，颓废的；堕落的(人) |Characterized by or reflecting a state of moral or cultural decline.  A person who is morally or culturally declined. | The decadent Roman Empire.  衰落的罗马帝国。 | luxurious, self-indulgent, corrupt, immoral | austere, wholesome, moral, virtuous | adjective, noun

decadent:/ˈdekədənt/ /ˈdɛkədənt/ | n. | 颓废派(成员)，堕落者 | A person who is morally or culturally declined. |  He lived a decadent life, indulging in every excess. 他过着颓废的生活，沉溺于各种放纵。 | degenerate, profligate, libertine | puritan, ascetic | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *décadent*, present participle of *décadire* (“to decay, decline”), from Latin *dēcadere* (“to fall down, fall away, decay”), from *de-* (“down, away from”) + *cadere* (“to fall”).
