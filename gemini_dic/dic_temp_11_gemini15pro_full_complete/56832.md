dormy:/ˈdɔːrmi/ (US), /ˈdɔːmi/ (UK)| adj.|领先洞数的; 占优势的|Being in a position in match play golf where one is as many holes up as there are holes remaining to be played, thus guaranteeing victory.|例句：He was dormy three going into the 15th hole. 他在进入第15洞时领先3洞。|近义词：winning, advantageous|反义词:losing, disadvantaged|词性:形容词

dormy:/ˈdɔːrmi/ (US), /ˈdɔːmi/ (UK)| n.|宿舍; 寝室 (主要用于英国英语，尤指学校的)|A bedroom for several people, especially in a school or institution.|例句：The students were assigned to different dormies. 学生们被分配到不同的宿舍。|近义词：dormitory, dorm, residence hall|反义词: N/A|词性:名词

词源:  "Dormy" comes from the French word "dormir" meaning "to sleep," reflecting its original meaning as a sleeping room. In golf, it's believed the term may have arisen from the idea that a player who is "dormy" can effectively "sleepwalk" to victory.
