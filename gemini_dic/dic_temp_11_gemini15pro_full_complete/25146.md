buckle:/ˈbʌk(ə)l/ (US) /ˈbʌkl/ (UK)| v.|扣住，系上；弯曲，变形|To fasten or be fastened with a buckle; to bend, warp, or give way under pressure or strain.|The intense heat buckled the railroad tracks. 酷热使铁轨变形了。|近义词：fasten, secure, bend, warp|反义词:unbuckle|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "bocle" (buckle, boss of a shield), from Vulgar Latin *buccula (strap, cheek-strap of a helmet), diminutive of Latin bucca (cheek, mouth).
