chagatai:/tʃəˈɡɑːtaɪ/
noun
察合台语，察合台文
A Turkic language of Central Asia, spoken in the 15th and 16th centuries. It was the literary language of the Timurid Empire and is considered an ancestor of Uzbek and Uyghur.
Babur wrote his memoirs, the Baburnama, in Chagatai. 巴布尔用察合台文写了他的回忆录《巴布尔回忆录》。
Synonyms:  Chaghatay, Jagatai
Etymology: From Chagatai Khan (d. 1242), second son of Genghis Khan.
