overcommercialize: /ˌoʊvər kəˈmɜːrʃəˌlaɪz/ |v. |过度商业化|To exploit or promote (something) excessively for profit, often to the detriment of its intrinsic value.|The town has been overcommercialized, losing its original charm.|Synonyms: overexploit, oversaturate|Antonyms: preserve, conserve|Verb

overcommercialized: /ˌoʊvər kəˈmɜːrʃəˌlaɪzd/ |adj. |过度商业化的|Excessively exploited or promoted for profit.|The once-peaceful beach is now overcommercialized and crowded.|Synonyms: overexploited, oversaturated|Antonyms: unspoiled, pristine|Adjective

overcommercialization: /ˌoʊvər kəˌmɜːrʃəˌləˈzeɪʃən/ |n. |过度商业化|The act or process of overcommercializing something.|The overcommercialization of Christmas has led to a focus on material goods rather than spiritual meaning.|Synonyms: overexploitation, oversaturaion|Antonyms: preservation, conservation|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From "over-" (meaning "excessive") + "commercialize" (from "commercial" + "-ize," meaning "to make commercial").  The root "commercial" comes from the Latin "commerciālis,"  meaning "pertaining to trade."
