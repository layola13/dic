consecrated:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪtɪd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsɪkreɪtɪd/ (US)| adj.| 神圣的；被奉献的；被祝圣的|Dedicated to a sacred purpose; made holy.  Having been made sacred by a religious ceremony. |The bishop consecrated the new church building. 主教祝圣了新的教堂建筑。|Synonyms: hallowed, sacred, blessed, sanctified|Antonyms:  profane, secular, unconsecrated|Adjective

consecrate:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːnsɪkreɪt/ (US)| vt.| 使神圣；奉献；祝圣|To dedicate to a sacred purpose; to make holy. To declare or dedicate something to a particular purpose.|The ground was consecrated as a cemetery. 这块地被祝圣为墓地。|Synonyms:  hallow, sanctify, bless, dedicate|Antonyms: desecrate, profane|Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *consecrātus*, past participle of consecrāre (“to dedicate, devote, consecrate”), from Latin com- (“together”) + sacrāre (“to make sacred”), from sacer (“sacred”).
