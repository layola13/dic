handles:/ˈhændlz/ | n. | 把手；柄；(尤指成对的)拉手，把手；(车辆的)操纵装置；(软件界面的)控制部件；(非正式)(人的)姓名，称呼；(非正式)优势，有利条件 | Handles are parts of objects designed for holding, manipulating, or controlling. handles:/ˈhændlz/ | v. (third-person singular simple present handles, present participle handling, simple past and past participle handled) |  处理；搬运；操作；驾驭；管理；(用手)触摸，抚摸；(买卖)经销，经营；(体育)带球，触球；控制(感情)；对付；应付(人或事物) | To handle something means to deal with, manage, or manipulate it. It can refer to physical objects, situations, emotions, or even people.  |  She handles pressure well. 她能很好地应对压力。

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *handelen, from Old English *handlian, from hand (“hand”). Cognate with West Frisian hannelje (“to handle, to trade”), Dutch handelen (“to act, to trade”), German handeln (“to act, to deal, to trade”).
