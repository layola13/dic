high-mindedly: /ˌhaɪˈmaɪndɪdli/
| adv. | 高尚地，怀着高尚的动机地 |  In a manner that is characterized by noble ideals and principles; with elevated thoughts or motives. |  He high-mindedly refused the bribe, even though he desperately needed the money. 他高尚地拒绝了贿赂，即使他非常需要钱。 |  nobly, virtuously, ethically |  basely, selfishly, meanly | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "high-minded" + "-ly". "High-minded" comes from "high" (meaning elevated, noble) + "minded" (having a specified kind of mind or disposition).
