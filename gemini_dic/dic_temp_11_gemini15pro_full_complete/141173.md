ooezoa: /ˌoʊiˈzoʊə/ | n. | 卵生动物 |  A taxonomic group (now obsolete) that included all animals whose eggs developed outside the mother's body, encompassing birds, reptiles, fishes, and monotremes. |  Ooezoa was a classification used in the past, but it's no longer considered valid. |  No longer in scientific use, so no modern example sentence.  Older texts might contain something like "Birds belong to the Ooezoa." |  Synonyms (in older classifications): Exozooa |  Antonyms (in older classifications):  Endozooa | noun
