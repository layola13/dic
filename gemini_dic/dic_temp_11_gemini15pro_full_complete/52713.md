digressively:/daɪˈɡrɛsɪvli/
adv. | 枝节性地；离题地；散漫地 | in a manner that strays from the main topic; in a rambling or discursive way |  He spoke digressively, touching on several unrelated subjects. 他说话很散漫，谈到了几个不相关的主题。 | synonym: ramblingly, discursively, circuitously | antonym: concisely, directly, succinctly |词性: 副词

digression:/daɪˈɡrɛʃən/
n. |  枝节话；离题；脱轨 | a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing | The professor's digression about his childhood was interesting but irrelevant. 教授关于他童年的离题很有趣，但不相关。 | synonym: deviation, detour, aside | antonym: adherence, focus, relevance |词性: 名词

digress:/daɪˈɡrɛs/
v. | 离题；跑题 | to deviate or wander away from the main topic or purpose in speaking or writing |  The speaker digressed from his prepared remarks to tell a joke. 演讲者偏离了他准备好的讲话，讲了个笑话。 | synonym: deviate, wander, ramble | antonym: focus, stay on topic, adhere |词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *digressio, from Latin digressus, past participle of digredi "to go away, depart," from dis- "apart, away" + gradi "to step, walk."
