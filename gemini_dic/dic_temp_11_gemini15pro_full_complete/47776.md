decant:/dɪˈkænt/| v.| 轻轻倒出；倾注 |To pour (liquid, especially wine) from one container into another, typically in order to separate out sediment.| He carefully decanted the wine into a carafe. 他小心翼翼地将酒倒入玻璃水瓶中。|例句：She decanted the wine into a crystal decanter. 她把酒倒进了一个水晶醒酒器。|近义词：pour, transfer, siphon|反义词: N/A|词性: 动词
decant:/dɪˈkænt/| n.| 倾析；移注 |The act of decanting a liquid.| The decant of the vintage port was a special moment.  将年份波特酒倾析是一个特别的时刻。|近义词：pouring, transfer, siphoning|反义词: N/A|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *décanter*, from *dé-* (indicating removal) + *canter* (to sing, chant).  The original sense related to pouring off wine gently so as not to disturb the sediment, likened to separating a clear voice (the wine) from the background noise (the sediment).
