chloralamide:/ˌklɔːrəlˈæmaɪd/ | n. |氯醛酰胺|A sedative and hypnotic drug, a combination of chloral hydrate and formamide.  It is less irritating to the stomach than chloral hydrate.|Chloralamide was once used as a sleep aid, but it is rarely prescribed now due to its potential side effects. 氯醛酰胺曾被用作安眠药，但由于其潜在的副作用，现在很少开处方。|Synonym: chloral hydrate,  noctal|Antonym: stimulant |noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From chloral + amide.  Chloral from chlor(o)-  (Greek khlōros, pale green) + -al (aldehyde suffix). Amide from ammonia + -ide (chemical suffix).
