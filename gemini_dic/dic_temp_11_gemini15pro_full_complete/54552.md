dislocate: /dɪsˈloʊkeɪt/ (US) /dɪsˈləʊkeɪt/ (UK)
|v. |使脱臼；使错位；扰乱|To put out of its usual place or order; to disturb the normal function of|
|例句：He dislocated his shoulder in the accident. 他在事故中肩膀脱臼了。|


|例句：The war dislocated the country's economy. 战争扰乱了国家的经济。|


|近义词：displace, disrupt, unsettle|
|反义词：locate, order, organize|
|词性：动词|



dislocated: /dɪsˈloʊkeɪtɪd/ (US) /dɪsˈləʊkeɪtɪd/ (UK)
|adj. |脱臼的；错位的；混乱的|Out of its usual place or order; disturbed from its normal function|
|例句：He had a dislocated shoulder. 他的肩膀脱臼了。|


|例句：The refugees came from dislocated communities. 这些难民来自混乱的社区。|


|近义词：displaced, disordered, disrupted|
|反义词：ordered, organized, in place|
|词性：形容词|



ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dislocatus, past participle of dislocare "to displace," from dis- "apart, away" + locare "to place" (from locus "place").
