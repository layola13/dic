affirm:/əˈfɜːrm/ | v. | 肯定；断言；证实 | state emphatically or publicly that something is true or that one believes it to be true |  The suspect affirmed that he had been at home all night. 嫌疑犯声称他整晚都在家。| synonyms: assert, declare, maintain, confirm | antonyms: deny, refute, contradict | verb

affirm:/əˈfɜːrm/ | v. |  支持；赞成；认可 |  to support and strengthen a belief, feeling, or idea | The court affirmed the lower court's decision. 法院维持了初审法院的判决。 | synonyms: uphold, endorse, ratify, approve | antonyms: oppose, reject, overrule | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French afermer, based on Latin affirmare, from ad- ‘to’ + firmare ‘strengthen’ (from firmus ‘firm’).
