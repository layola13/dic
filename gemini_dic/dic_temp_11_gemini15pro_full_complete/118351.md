merino:/məˈriːnoʊ/
n.|美利奴羊；美利奴羊毛|A breed of fine-wooled sheep originally from Spain, or the fine wool from these sheep.|The merino wool is incredibly soft.|近义词:lamb, fleece|反义词:(无直接反义词, 可用coarse wool)|词性:名词

merinos:/məˈriːnoʊz/
n.|美利奴羊（复数）|Plural form of merino, referring to multiple merino sheep.|The farmer sheared all his merinos.|近义词:sheep, lambs|反义词:(无直接反义词)|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *merino*, meaning "overseer," "judge," "royal official,"  possibly from Late Latin *majorinus* "superior officer," derived from Latin *maior* "greater."  The precise connection between the sheep and the word is uncertain, but it may refer to a type of official who oversaw sheep pastures.
