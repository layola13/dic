appositive:/əˈpɒzɪtɪv/ | adj. n. |同位语的，同位语 |A word or phrase placed next to another word or phrase to explain or identify it. |Example: My best friend, Sarah, is a doctor.  我的最好的朋友，莎拉，是一个医生。|Synonyms:  explanatory, equivalent,  restrictive, non-restrictive |Antonyms: N/A | Word Class: adjective, noun

appositive:/əˈpɒzɪtɪv/ | adj. n. |同位语的，同位语 |An appositive noun or noun phrase follows another noun or noun phrase and refers to the same person or thing. It adds information or details. |Example:  The capital of France, Paris, is a beautiful city. 法国的首都，巴黎，是一个美丽的城市。|Synonyms:  explanatory, equivalent,  restrictive, non-restrictive |Antonyms: N/A | Word Class: adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *appositivus, from Latin appositus (“placed near, applied”), past participle of appōnō (“apply, place near”). Equivalent to ad- +‎ positive.
