fibbing:/ˈfɪbɪŋ/| n. vi.|撒小谎，说瞎话|The act of telling small lies, often to avoid trouble or embarrassment.  It implies a relatively harmless form of lying, not a serious deception.|He got caught fibbing about his age. 他谎报年龄被抓到了。|近义词：lying, prevaricating, exaggerating|反义词:truth-telling, honesty|词性:noun, verb (present participle)

fib:/fɪb/| vi. n.|撒小谎，说瞎话；小谎|To tell a small lie, often to avoid trouble or embarrassment.  It implies a relatively harmless form of lying, not a serious deception.  As a noun, it refers to the lie itself.|She fibbed about why she was late. 她为迟到编了个理由。|Don't tell fibs. 不要撒谎。|近义词：lie, prevaricate, exaggerate|反义词:tell the truth, be honest|词性:verb, noun
