bolshevik: /ˈbɒlʃəvɪk/ (UK) /ˈboʊlʃəvɪk/ (US)| n. adj.| 布尔什维克，布尔什维克的| A member of the majority faction of the Russian Social Democratic Party, which seized power in the October Revolution of 1917.  Also used as an adjective to describe things related to Bolshevism.| Example:  Lenin was a prominent Bolshevik. 列宁是一位著名的布尔什维克。| Example: The Bolshevik Revolution changed the course of history. 布尔什维克革命改变了历史进程。|Synonym: communist, revolutionary, Marxist, Leninist|Antonym: Tsarist, Menshevik|Noun, Adjective

bolshevist: /ˈbɒlʃəvɪst/ (UK) /ˈboʊlʃəvɪst/ (US)| n. adj.| 布尔什维克主义者，布尔什维克主义的|  Often used interchangeably with "Bolshevik," but sometimes implies a more general adherence to Bolshevik principles, especially outside Russia. Can also be used adjectivally.| Example:  Many European intellectuals were sympathetic to Bolshevist ideals after World War I.  一战后，许多欧洲知识分子同情布尔什维克主义的理想。| Example:  The Bolshevist uprising was swiftly suppressed. 布尔什维克起义被迅速镇压。|Synonym: communist, revolutionary, Marxist, Leninist|Antonym: Tsarist, Menshevik|Noun, Adjective
