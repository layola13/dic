jabbered:/ˈdʒæbərd/ | v. | 急促而不清楚地说；喋喋不休 | to talk quickly and excitedly but not very clearly | He jabbered excitedly about the game. 他兴奋地叽里咕噜地谈论着比赛。| synonyms: babble, chatter, prattle, ramble | antonyms: articulate, enunciate | verb

jabbering:/ˈdʒæbə(r)ɪŋ/ | n. | 急促而不清楚的说话；喋喋不休 | rapid, unclear, and excited speech | The jabbering of the monkeys was deafening. 猴子叽叽喳喳的声音震耳欲聋。| synonyms: babble, chatter, prattle | antonyms: articulation, enunciation | noun

jabbering:/ˈdʒæbə(r)ɪŋ/ | adj. | 急促而不清楚地说；喋喋不休的 | talking quickly and excitedly but not very clearly | The jabbering crowd was hard to understand.  叽叽喳喳的人群很难听懂。| synonyms: babbling, chattering, prattling | antonyms: articulate, enunciating | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *jaberen*, of imitative origin.
