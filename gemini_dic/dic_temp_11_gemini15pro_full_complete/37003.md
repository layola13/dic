columbine: /ˈkɒləmbaɪn/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləmbaɪn/ (US) | n. | 耧斗菜;  鸽子般的人（尤指少女） | A plant with attractive, spurred flowers, typically of a purple, pink, or white colour.  Also, a dove-like person, especially a young girl. | The columbines in her garden were a vibrant shade of purple. 她花园里的耧斗菜呈现出鲜艳的紫色。| Synonyms: aquilegia (botanical name), dove (symbolic), maiden (symbolic) | Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a flower.  Could use contrasting flower, e.g., sunflower) | noun

columbine: /ˈkɒləmbaɪn/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləmbaɪn/ (US) | adj. |  鸽子般的;  与耧斗菜有关的 | Resembling a dove; relating to the columbine flower.  | She had a columbine innocence about her. 她身上有一种鸽子般的纯真。 | Synonyms: dovelike, innocent, gentle | Antonyms:  aggressive, harsh | adjective
