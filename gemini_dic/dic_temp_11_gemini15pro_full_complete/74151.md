founding:/ˈfaʊndɪŋ/| n. adj.|建立，成立；创始；熔铸|The act of establishing something, especially an institution or organization. The founding members of the club are still active.  俱乐部的创始成员仍然活跃。|例句：The founding of the United States was a momentous occasion. 美国的成立是一个重要时刻。

例句：The founding fathers of the nation are revered.  国家的开国元勋受到尊敬。|近义词：establishment, creation, institution, inauguration|反义词：destruction, demolition, dissolution|词性:noun, adjective

founding:/ˈfaʊndɪŋ/| adj.|创始的，成立的|Relating to the establishment or beginning of something. The founding principles of the company are based on integrity.  公司的创始原则是建立在诚信之上的。|例句：The founding members of the organization were all women.  该组织的创始成员都是女性。

例句：He played a founding role in the development of the new technology. 他在新技术的发展中扮演了创始性的角色。|近义词：initial, original, primary, inaugural|反义词：later, subsequent|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *foundinge*, verbal noun from founden, from Old French *fonder* (to found, establish), from Latin *fundāre* (to lay the bottom or foundation of, to ground, establish, secure), from *fundus* (bottom, base, foundation).
