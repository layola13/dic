mercantilistic: /ˌmɜːrkənˈtɪlɪstɪk/ | adj. | 重商主义的 | Relating to or characteristic of mercantilism (an economic theory prevalent in Europe from the 16th to the 18th centuries, holding that prosperity depended on a large supply of gold and silver and that the total volume of trade was unchangeable). | The government's mercantilistic policies aimed to maximize exports and minimize imports. 政府的重商主义政策旨在最大限度地增加出口，减少进口。| The East India Company was a powerful force in promoting mercantilistic trade. 东印度公司是推动重商主义贸易的一股强大力量。 | 近义词: protectionist, nationalist | 反义词: free-trade, laissez-faire | 词性: 形容词 | 词源: From French *mercantiliste*, from Italian *mercantilista*, from *mercante* "merchant".
