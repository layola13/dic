cryonic:/kraɪˈɒnɪk/ (US) /kraɪˈɑːnɪk/ (UK)| adj.|低温的，冷冻的|Relating to or involving the freezing of a dead body or animal in the hope that it can be brought back to life in the future when medical science has advanced.|例句：Cryonic preservation is a controversial practice.  人体冷冻保存是一项有争议的做法。

例句：He invested in a cryonic facility hoping to be revived in the future. 他投资了一个人体冷冻设施，希望将来能复活。|近义词：freezing, subzero|反义词: warm, heated|词性: adjective

cryonics:/kraɪˈɒnɪks/ (US) /kraɪˈɑːnɪks/ (UK)| n.|人体冷冻学|The practice or technique of deep-freezing the bodies of people who have just died, in the hope that scientific advances may allow them to be revived in the future.|例句：Cryonics is still considered to be an experimental field. 人体冷冻学仍然被认为是一个实验性领域。

例句：The ethics of cryonics are frequently debated. 人体冷冻学的伦理问题经常受到争论。|近义词：cryopreservation, cryogenics (related but distinct)|反义词: None (no direct opposite)|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *kryos* meaning "icy cold, frost" + "-ic" (adjective forming suffix).
