dry-nurse:/ˈdraɪˌnɜːrs/| v. n.|试运行，预演；排练；代为照顾（婴儿）|To rehearse or practice something before it is done for real, or to care for a baby in place of its mother.|例句：The team dry-nursed the software before launching it to the public.  团队在软件公开发布前进行了试运行。|近义词：rehearse, practice, trial run, test, babysit|反义词:implement, execute, perform|词性:动词,名词

dry-nurse:/ˈdraɪˌnɜːrs/| n.|代乳母|A woman who breastfeeds another woman's baby.|例句：She hired a dry-nurse for her baby while she was working. 她工作期间雇了一个代乳母照顾她的孩子。|近义词：wet-nurse|反义词:mother|词性:名词

词源： "dry" (not providing milk) + "nurse" (one who cares for a child or sick person).  The term originated in the 16th century, referring to a woman who cares for a baby but does not breastfeed it. Later, it expanded to mean a rehearsal or practice run.
