non-evidential:/ˌnɒn ɛvɪˈdɛnʃəl/ | adj. | 非证据性的；非基于证据的 | Not based on or supported by evidence; expressing something not directly witnessed or known through personal experience. |  The story he told was completely non-evidential, based entirely on hearsay. 他讲的故事完全是非证据性的，完全基于传闻。

The shaman's pronouncements were considered non-evidential by the scientifically minded villagers.  萨满的声明被具有科学意识的村民们认为是非证据性的。 | 近义词: conjectural, speculative, hypothetical, unsubstantiated, anecdotal | 反义词: evidential, empirical, factual, proven, corroborated | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
non- (prefix): from Latin *non "not"
evidential (adjective): from evidence + -ial (suffix forming adjectives)
evidence (noun): from Old French evidence, from Latin evidentia "distinctness, clearness," from evident-, present participle stem of evidēre "to be evident," from ex- "out" + vidēre "to see"
