irresolutely:/ɪˈrɛzəluːtli/ /ˌɪrɪˈzɑːlətli/| adv.|优柔寡断地，犹豫不决地|In a manner showing hesitation or uncertainty; without firmness of purpose.|He irresolutely paced the floor, unsure of what to do next. 他犹豫不决地在房间里踱步，不知道下一步该做什么。|Synonyms: hesitantly, indecisively, waveringly, doubtfully|Antonyms: resolutely, decisively, firmly, confidently|词性:副词

Etymology->
From the adjective "irresolute" + "-ly" (adverb suffix).  "Irresolute" comes from the Latin "irresolutus,"  meaning "undecided," from "in-" (not) + "resolutus" (resolved, determined), the past participle of "resolvere" (to loosen, dissolve, resolve).
