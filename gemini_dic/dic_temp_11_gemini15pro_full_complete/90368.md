how-the-wind-blows:/haʊ ðə wɪnd bləʊz/ |idiom| 看情况；随机应变；见机行事|To see what happens; to be flexible and adapt to changing circumstances; to play it by ear.  This idiom refers to being adaptable and responsive to changing circumstances, much like a sailboat adjusts its course according to the wind.| We'll have to see how the wind blows before we make any firm plans. 我们得看看情况再做决定。|Synonyms: play it by ear, see how things go, wait and see, go with the flow |Antonyms:  have a fixed plan, be inflexible, be rigid| idiom
