bitterly:/ˈbɪtərli/| adv.|痛苦地，愤恨地，残酷地|In a bitter manner; with great resentment, disappointment, or sadness.|She wept bitterly when she heard the news. 她听到这个消息后痛哭流涕。|Synonyms: resentfully, angrily, acrimoniously, fiercely|Antonyms: happily, joyfully, sweetly|Adverb

bitterly:/ˈbɪtərli/| adv.|极其，非常|Extremely; to a great extent (used to emphasize unpleasant qualities).|It was bitterly cold that winter. 那年冬天极其寒冷。|Synonyms: extremely, intensely, exceedingly|Antonyms: mildly, slightly|Adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *biterli*, from Old English *bitrelice* "bitterly," from *biter* "bitter" (from Proto-Germanic *bitraz*, from Proto-Indo-European *bheyd- "to split, cleave, bite") + *-lice* adverbial suffix (cognate with -ly).
