helot:/ˈhɛlət/| n. |农奴，奴隶，受压迫的人|A person in a subjugated class, especially a serf or slave.  A helot is someone who is forced to work for another person and has very few rights.  The term originally referred to the state-owned serfs in ancient Sparta.|The Spartans treated the helots cruelly. 斯巴达人残酷地对待农奴。|Synonyms: serf, slave, bondsman, thrall, peon|Antonyms: freeman, master, lord|noun

helot:/ˈhɛlət/| adj. |奴隶的，农奴的，受压迫的|Of or relating to helots; servile. Describing someone or something characteristic of a helot, often implying a state of servitude or oppression.|The helot population outnumbered the Spartans. 农奴人口超过了斯巴达人。|Synonyms: servile, enslaved, oppressed, subjugated|Antonyms: free, liberated, independent|adjective
