dazy:/ˈdeɪzi/ | adj. | 昏沉的，恍惚的；迷乱的 | Feeling confused and unable to think clearly, often because of tiredness, heat, or drugs. |  The long car journey left me feeling dazed and confused. 长途汽车旅行让我感到昏昏沉沉，思绪混乱。|  lightheaded, foggy, bewildered, confused | clearheaded, alert, focused | adjective

daze:/deɪz/ | n. | 昏沉；恍惚；迷乱 | A state of being confused and unable to think clearly. |  He was in a daze after being hit on the head. 他头部被击中后处于昏沉状态。| stupor, confusion, bewilderment | clarity, alertness | noun

daze:/deɪz/ | vt. | 使茫然；使昏沉；使惊呆 | To stun or confuse someone. | The sudden news dazed her. 突如其来的消息把她惊呆了。| stun, bewilder, confuse | enlighten, clarify | verb
