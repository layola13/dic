dissipated: /ˈdɪsɪpeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 放荡的，挥霍的，消散的 |  indulging in pleasure to a degree perceived to be morally harmful; wasted; squandered; dispersed or scattered. | He led a dissipated life, squandering his fortune on gambling and drinking. 他过着放荡的生活，把自己的财产都挥霍在赌博和酗酒上。 | synonyms: debauched, profligate, dissolute, extravagant, squandered, dispersed | antonyms:  temperate, moderate, frugal, collected, concentrated | adjective

dissipate: /ˈdɪsɪpeɪt/ | v. |  消散，驱散，浪费 | (with reference to a feeling or other intangible thing) disappear or cause to disappear; squander or fritter away (money, energy, or resources). | The fog began to dissipate as the sun rose. 随着太阳升起，雾开始消散。| synonyms: disperse, scatter, squander, waste, deplete | antonyms: gather, collect, accumulate, conserve | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin dissipatus, past participle of dissipare (“to scatter, disperse”), from dis- (“apart, asunder”) + sipare (“to throw, scatter”).
