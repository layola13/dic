akram:/ˈækrəm/ | adj. n. |最慷慨的，最尊贵的；阿克拉姆（人名）|  Derived from the Arabic word "karam" (كرم), meaning generosity, nobility, or honor. Akram is the superlative form, meaning "most generous," "most noble," or "most honorable." It can be used as an adjective or a name. | He is known for his akram nature, always helping those in need. 他以慷慨的本性而闻名，总是帮助那些需要帮助的人。| He was named Akram after his grandfather. 他以祖父的名字命名为阿克拉姆。 |Synonyms: generous, noble, honorable, magnanimous | Antonyms: stingy, selfish, ignoble, dishonorable | adjective, noun
