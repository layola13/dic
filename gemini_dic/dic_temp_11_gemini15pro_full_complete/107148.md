laemodipodous:/ˌliːməˈdɪpədəs/| adj.|薄板状足的；侧面扁平的|Having laterally compressed feet, typically adapted for swimming.|例句：The laemodipodous crustacean darted through the water.  这种薄板状足的甲壳类动物在水中飞快地游动。

例句：Laemodipodous adaptations are common in aquatic animals. 薄板状足的适应性在水生动物中很常见。|近义词：flattened, compressed|反义词：rounded, cylindrical|词性: adjective

Etymology: From Greek *laimos* (λαιμός) "throat, gullet" + *pous* (πούς) "foot" + *-ous* (adjective suffix). The "throat" part likely refers to the slender, compressed shape of the foot, reminiscent of a throat or gullet.
