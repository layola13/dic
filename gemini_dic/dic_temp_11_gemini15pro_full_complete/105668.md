kingmakers:/ˈkɪŋˌmeɪkərz/
n.
造王者；拥立国王的人；幕后操纵者
A person or group that has great influence on the selection of a leader, but does not hold power themselves.
The media is often considered the kingmaker in modern elections, influencing public opinion and shaping the narrative around candidates. 媒体通常被认为是现代选举中的造王者，他们影响公众舆论并塑造围绕候选人的叙事。
Lobbyists can be powerful kingmakers, wielding influence behind the scenes to shape policy and determine who gets elected.  说客可以成为强大的造王者，在幕后施加影响以制定政策并决定谁当选。
近义词: power broker, influencer, manipulator
反义词: pawn, puppet, figurehead
词性: 名词 (plural)

kingmaker:/ˈkɪŋˌmeɪkər/
n.
造王者；拥立国王的人；幕后操纵者
A person or group that has great influence on the selection of a leader, but does not hold power themselves.
Historically, wealthy families acted as kingmakers, choosing who ascended to the throne. 从历史上看，富裕的家庭充当造王者，选择谁登上王位。
The union leader became a kingmaker in the local elections, delivering crucial votes to the winning candidate. 工会领导人在地方选举中成为造王者，为获胜的候选人提供了关键选票。
近义词: power broker, influencer, manipulator
反义词: pawn, puppet, figurehead
词性: 名词 (singular)
