overornamentality: /ˌoʊvərˌɔːrnəˌmɛnˈtælɪti/ | n. | 过度装饰；过于华丽 | The state or quality of being excessively ornamented.  An overabundance of decoration. | The overornamentality of the building made it look gaudy rather than grand. 这栋建筑的过度装饰使其看起来很俗气而不是宏伟。| 近义词：excessiveness, elaborateness, ostentation, flamboyance | 反义词：simplicity, minimalism, austerity, plainness | 词性：名词

overornamentality: | 词源: over- (prefix meaning "excessive") + ornament (noun meaning "decoration") + -ality (suffix forming abstract nouns) |  "Over-" comes from Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond." "Ornament" comes from Latin "ornare," meaning "to equip, adorn." "-ality" is a suffix of Latin origin used to form nouns denoting qualities or states. |  The word suggests a level of decoration that surpasses what is considered tasteful or appropriate, often resulting in a cluttered or gaudy appearance. | The overornamentality of the Victorian era is evident in the architecture and fashion of the time. 维多利亚时代的过度装饰在当时的建筑和时尚中显而易见。 | 近义词：excessiveness, elaborateness, ostentation, flamboyance | 反义词：simplicity, minimalism, austerity, plainness | 词性：名词
