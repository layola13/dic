circumgyratory:/ˌsərkəmˈdʒaɪrəˌtɔːri/ /ˌsərkəmˈdʒaɪrəˌtɔri/
noun
旋转，绕圈
The act of moving in a circle or spiral. (Less common usage)
The circumgyratory of the bird in flight was graceful.  鸟儿在空中盘旋的动作很优美。

Etymology:
From Latin circum- "around" + gyrat- "turned, rotated," past participle stem of gyrare "to turn, whirl," from Greek gyros "circle, ring."
