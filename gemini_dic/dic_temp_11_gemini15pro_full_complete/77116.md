garum:/ˈɡɑːrəm/ | n. | 鱼酱，鱼露 | A fermented fish sauce used as a condiment in ancient Roman and Greek cuisine.  It was made by fermenting the entrails of fish, especially mackerel and tuna, with salt. | The garum added a pungent, salty flavor to many Roman dishes.  罗马人会在许多菜肴中加入鱼酱，增添一种辛辣的咸味。|  Example: Garum was a staple condiment in ancient Rome, much like ketchup is today.  鱼酱在古罗马是一种主要的调味品，就像今天的番茄酱一样。 |Synonyms: fish sauce, liquamen |Antonyms: N/A | noun
