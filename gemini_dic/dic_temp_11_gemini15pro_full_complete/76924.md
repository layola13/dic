gaoler:/ˈdʒeɪlər/ (US) /ˈɡeɪlər/ (UK)| n.|狱卒；看守|A person in charge of a jail or prison.|The gaoler brought the prisoner his meal. 狱卒给囚犯送来了饭。|Synonyms: jailer, prison guard, warden, turnkey|Antonyms: prisoner, inmate|noun

gaolbird:/ˈdʒeɪlbɜːrd/ (US) /ˈɡeɪlbɜːrd/ (UK)| n.|囚犯；惯犯|A person who has spent a lot of time in prison.|The old gaolbird knew all the tricks of the trade.  这个老囚犯知道所有的伎俩。|Synonyms: convict, prisoner, inmate, criminal|Antonyms: law-abiding citizen|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French gaiolier, from gaiole "cage, jail," from Latin caveola "small cage, cavity," diminutive of cavus "hollow."
