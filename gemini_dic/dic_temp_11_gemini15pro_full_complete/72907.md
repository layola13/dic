foment:/fəˈment/ /ˈfoʊment/ | v. |煽动，激起；助长；热敷 | to instigate or stir up (an undesirable or violent sentiment or course of action); to promote the growth or development of; to apply warm water or medicated liquid to (a part of the body) | The politician's fiery speech fomented unrest in the crowd.  这位政客的激烈演讲煽动了人群中的骚乱。|Synonyms: incite, instigate, provoke, abet, encourage | Antonyms: quell, suppress, pacify, soothe, allay | verb

fomented:/fəˈmentɪd/ /ˈfoʊmentɪd/ | v. (past tense & past participle) | 煽动的，激起的 |  past tense and past participle of "foment" | The unrest was fomented by a group of agitators.  骚乱是由一群煽动者激起的。 | Synonyms: incited, instigated, provoked | Antonyms: quelled, suppressed, pacified | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French fomenter ‘apply a poultice to,’ from Latin fomentare ‘bathe, cherish,’ from fomentum ‘lotion, poultice.’ The sense ‘stir up’ dates from the mid 17th century.
