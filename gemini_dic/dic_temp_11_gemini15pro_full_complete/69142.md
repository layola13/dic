favourableness:/ˈfeɪvərəblnəs/ | n. | 有利；适合；赞同；好感 | The favourableness of the weather encouraged us to go for a picnic.  天气宜人，我们便去野餐了。 |  The favourableness of the market conditions led to a surge in investment.  有利的市场条件导致投资激增。 | favorableness (US),  advantageousness,  propitiousness, auspiciousness | unfavourableness, disadvantageousness, inauspiciousness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *favourable* + *-ness*.  *Favourable* comes from Old French *favorable*, from Latin *favorabilis* ("winning favor, propitious"), from *favor* ("favor, goodwill").  *-ness* is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, denoting a state or quality.
