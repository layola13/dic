diamide:/ˈdaɪəˌmaɪd/ | n. |二酰胺|A type of organic compound containing two amide groups.  Specifically, it often refers to compounds with the general formula R1C(O)NHNHC(O)R2, where R1 and R2 can be various organic groups.|Diamide herbicides are effective against certain weeds. 二酰胺类除草剂对某些杂草有效。|Synonyms:  diacylhydrazine (more technically precise)|Antonyms:  (No direct chemical antonym)|noun
