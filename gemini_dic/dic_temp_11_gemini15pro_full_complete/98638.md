insubordinate:/ˌɪnsəˈbɔːrdɪnət/ | adj. |不服从的，反抗的 | Not submitting to authority; disobedient. | The insubordinate soldier refused to follow orders.  不服从的士兵拒绝服从命令。|  insolent, rebellious, defiant, unruly | obedient, compliant, submissive | adjective

insubordination:/ˌɪnsəˌbɔːrdɪˈneɪʃn/ | n. |不服从，反抗 | Defiance of authority; refusal to obey orders. |  His insubordination led to his dismissal from the army. 他的不服从导致他被军队开除。| disobedience, defiance, rebellion, mutiny | obedience, compliance, submission | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *insubordinatus, from in- "not" + subordinatus "subordinate," past participle of subordinare "to subordinate," from sub- "under" + ordinare "to arrange, order."
