mazarine:/ˈmæzərɪn, ˈmæzəriːn/| adj. n.|深蓝色,  马萨林蓝|A rich deep blue, similar to the color of Cardinal Mazarin's robes.|The dress was a stunning mazarine, the perfect complement to her dark hair.|近义词:azure, sapphire, cobalt blue|反义词:pale blue, light blue|词性:形容词, 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mazarin*, ultimately from Italian *Mazarino*, referring to Cardinal Jules Mazarin (1602-1661), a powerful French statesman during the reign of Louis XIV. The color became fashionable during his time.
