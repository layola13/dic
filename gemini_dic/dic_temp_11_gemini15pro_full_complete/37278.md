commandatory:/kəˈmændəˌtɔːri/ /ˈkɒmənˌdeɪtəri/| adj.|强制性的，命令式的|Holding an obligatory or commanding position; pertaining to a command or mandate.  Relating to or of the nature of a command; expressing a command. |The king issued a commandatory order to his subjects. 国王向他的臣民发布了强制性命令。|Synonyms: obligatory, compulsory, mandatory, imperative|Antonyms: optional, voluntary, discretionary|adjective

commandatory:/kəˈmændəˌtɔːri/ /ˈkɒmənˌdeɪtəri/| n.|指挥官，司令官 (古用法，现已罕见)|A commander; one in command. |He was appointed the commandatory of the fort.  他被任命为堡垒的指挥官。(古用法)|Synonyms: commander, commandant, chief|Antonyms: subordinate, follower|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *commandatorius, from Latin commandare "to entrust, command," from com- "together" + mandare "to entrust."
