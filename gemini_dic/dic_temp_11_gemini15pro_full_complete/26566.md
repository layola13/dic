cables:/ˈkeɪblz/| n.|电缆，缆绳|Thick cables snaked across the floor. 粗大的电缆在地板上蜿蜒而过。|The bridge is supported by strong cables. 这座桥由坚固的缆绳支撑。|近义词：wires, cords, ropes, lines|反义词: wireless (as an adjective describing technology)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *cable, from Late Latin *capulum, from Latin capere "to take, seize." Originally referred to a rope or chain, especially one used for mooring a ship. The modern sense of "electrical cable" developed in the 19th century.
