canicula:/kəˈnɪkjʊlə/ /kəˈnɪkjuːlə/| n.|天狼星; 夏季酷暑; 三伏天|The period of greatest heat in the summer, usually between early July and early September.  It originally referred to the rising of Sirius, the Dog Star. |The canicula brought scorching temperatures and drought.  三伏天带来了酷热和干旱。

The farmers were worried about their crops during the canicula.  农民们担心三伏天期间的农作物。|近义词：dog days, heatwave, summer solstice|反义词:winter solstice|词性:noun
Etymology->
From Latin canicula (“little dog, puppy”), diminutive of canis (“dog”).  Refers to Sirius, the "Dog Star," in the constellation Canis Major.  The hottest part of the summer coincided with the heliacal rising of Sirius in the Northern Hemisphere.
