lituite:/ˈlɪtʊˌaɪt/| n. |直角石|An extinct, slender, straight-shelled cephalopod mollusk of the Ordovician and Silurian periods, characterized by a complex internal siphuncle.|The museum displayed a well-preserved lituite fossil. 博物馆展出了一块保存完好的直角石化石。|Synonyms: orthoceras (similar but not identical), orthocone (general term for straight-shelled cephalopods)|Antonyms: None (as it's a specific extinct creature, no direct opposite exists)|Noun
