boracous: /bɒˈreɪʃəs/ | adj. | 好吹牛的，爱炫耀的 | Given to boasting; bragging.  | He's always been a bit boracous, telling everyone about his supposed achievements. 他一直有点爱吹牛，逢人就讲他所谓的成就。 |  He made boracous claims about his wealth and connections. 他吹嘘自己拥有财富和人脉。| 近义词：boastful, bragging, vainglorious, ostentatious | 反义词：humble, modest, unassuming | 词性：形容词

词源:  From Latin *borax*, "boaster" + -ous.  (This Latin form is reconstructed and not directly attested.) The term is likely related to the word "borax," originally referring to a type of white mineral, possibly due to its inflated perceived value or the exaggerated claims made about its properties in the past.
