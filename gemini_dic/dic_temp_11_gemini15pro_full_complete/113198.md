lurk's:/lɜːrks/ | n. | 潜伏者的，潜伏物的 |  Possessive form of "lurk," indicating something belonging to or associated with someone who lurks.  This is less common than using "of lurking" or a similar construction. | The lurk's shadow stretched across the alley. 潜伏者的影子穿过小巷。 | 近义词:  (For the verb "lurk") skulk, prowl, sneak | 反义词: (For the verb "lurk")  appear, emerge | 词性: Noun (possessive)


lurk:/lɜːrk/ | v. | 潜伏，埋伏 | To stay hidden, usually with an intent to do something harmful or illicit. To exist unobserved or unsuspected.  | A crocodile lurked in the murky water. 一条鳄鱼潜伏在浑浊的水中。| The danger still lurks. 危险依然潜伏着。 | 近义词: skulk, prowl, sneak | 反义词: appear, emerge | 词性: Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lurken, from Old Norse *lūrka (“to lie in wait, lurk”), from Proto-Germanic *lūrkōną (“to crouch, bend down, lie hidden”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)lewg- (“to bend, curve”).
