birthroot:/ˈbɜːrθˌruːt/| n. | 根状茎；延龄草；三叶延龄草 | A perennial herb (Trillium erectum) of eastern North America, having a whorl of three broad leaves and a solitary, usually dark purple, three-petaled flower. Its rootstock is used in herbal medicine. |  The Native Americans used birthroot to aid in childbirth. 美国原住民使用延龄草来帮助分娩。| synonym: beth root, trillium erectum, wake-robin | antonym: N/A | noun
