lingo's: /ˈlɪŋɡoʊz/ | n. | 行话，术语 |  A language, especially one that is not easily understood because it is used by a particular group of people.  Refers to the specialized vocabulary or jargon of a particular field, profession, or group. It often implies informality or even a slightly negative connotation, suggesting that the language is obscure or exclusive. The apostrophe and 's' indicate possessive form, meaning "belonging to a lingo" or "of a lingo." |  He doesn't understand the lingo's of computer programmers. 他不懂计算机程序员的行话。| synonyms: jargon, argot, cant, vernacular, idiom | antonyms: standard language, formal language | noun
