delirancy:/dɪˈlɪrənsi/| n.|谵妄，神志不清|A state of mental disturbance characterized by incoherent thought, speech, and hallucinations.|The patient experienced delirancy due to high fever.|近义词：madness, insanity, psychosis|反义词：lucidity, sanity|词性:noun

delirious:/dɪˈlɪriəs/| adj.|谵妄的，神志不清的|Affected by or characteristic of delirium.|He became delirious from lack of sleep.|近义词：mad, insane, frenzied|反义词：lucid, sane, rational|词性:adjective

词源:
Late Middle English: from Old French delirance, from delirer ‘rave’, from Latin delirare, from de- ‘away from’ + lira ‘furrow’ (hence ‘go out of the furrow, deviate’).
