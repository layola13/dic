epidemic:/ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk/ | n. adj. | 流行病，传染病；流行的，盛行的 | A widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time. | 例句：The city was gripped by a flu epidemic.  这座城市爆发了流感。| 近义词：outbreak, plague, pandemic | 反义词：sporadic, isolated | 词性: 名词, 形容词

epidemic:/ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk/ |  adj. | 流行性的，盛行的，普遍的 | Affecting or tending to affect a disproportionately large number of individuals within a population, community, or region at the same time. | 例句：There's an epidemic of cheating in the school.  学校里作弊成风。| 近义词：widespread, rampant, prevalent | 反义词：rare, uncommon | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *epidēmicus, from Ancient Greek ἐπιδημία (epidēmíā, “staying in one place, sojourning; prevalence of an epidemic disease”), from ἐπί (epí, “upon”) + δῆμος (dêmos, “people, district”).
