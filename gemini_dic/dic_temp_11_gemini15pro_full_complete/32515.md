chinse:/tʃaɪns/ | v. | 塞入；填塞；嵌入| to force or stuff something into a small space | He chinsed the last few clothes into his suitcase. 他把最后几件衣服塞进了手提箱。| 近义词： cram, stuff, jam, wedge | 反义词： extract, remove | 词性：动词

chinse:/tʃaɪns/ | n. | 裂缝；缝隙 (主要用于航海)| a caulking material, or a crack or seam caulked with such material (chiefly nautical) |  The sailors used oakum to chinse the seams of the deck. 水手们用麻絮填塞甲板的缝隙。| 近义词： crack, crevice, fissure, gap, seam | 反义词： closure, seal | 词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->

The origin of "chinse" is uncertain. One theory suggests it might be related to the Old French word "chasser" meaning "to chase" or "drive," implying the action of driving something into a gap. Another theory connects it to the Dutch word "ketsen" meaning "to calk."
