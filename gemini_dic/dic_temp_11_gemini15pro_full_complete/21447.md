blowlamp: /ˈbləʊˌlæmp/ (UK) /ˈbloʊˌlæmp/ (US) | n. | 喷灯，吹管 | A tool that produces a very hot flame by mixing air with a flammable gas, typically paraffin (kerosene), used for burning paint off surfaces, joining metal pipes, etc. |  He used a blowlamp to solder the pipes together. 他用喷灯焊接管道。|  The workman used a blowlamp to strip the paint from the door. 工人用喷灯去除门上的油漆。 | synonyms: blowtorch, torch | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From blow + lamp.  "Blow" refers to the action of air being forced into the flame to increase its intensity, and "lamp" refers to the device producing light and heat.
