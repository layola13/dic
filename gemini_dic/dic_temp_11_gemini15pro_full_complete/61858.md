emplunge: /ɪmˈplʌndʒ/ /ɛmˈplʌndʒ/ | v. | 投入；使陷入；使突然进入 | To thrust or plunge into something, especially a liquid; to involve deeply in something difficult or unpleasant. | He emplunged his hands into the icy water. 他把手猛地 plunge into icy water. | 近义词：immerse, submerge, plunge, dive | 反义词：extricate, withdraw | 词性: 动词

Etymology:
From en- + plunge.  "Plunge" comes from Old French "plongier," from Late Latin "plumbicare" meaning "to sound with a plummet," from Latin "plumbum" (lead).  The prefix "en-" (or "em-" before "p, b, m") acts as an intensifier.
