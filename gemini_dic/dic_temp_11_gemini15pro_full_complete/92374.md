hypocist: /ˈhaɪpəsɪst/
noun
膀胱冲洗器;  灌肠器
A hypocist is a historical medical instrument used for injecting fluids into the bladder or rectum, essentially a type of syringe or clyster.
The physician used a hypocist to administer a cleansing enema.  医生用膀胱冲洗器进行灌肠。
The museum displayed a collection of antique hypocists and other medical tools. 博物馆展出了一系列古代的膀胱冲洗器和其他医疗工具。
Synonyms: clyster, syringe, enema syringe
Antonyms: None (This is a specific medical instrument; there isn't a direct opposite)
Etymology: From Late Latin *hypocista, from Greek ὑποκυστίς (hypokystis), from ὑπό (hypo, “under”) + κύστις (kystis, “bladder, bag”).
