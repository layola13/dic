mass-markets:/ˌmæs ˈmɑːrkɪts/ | n. | 大众市场 | Markets for goods that are produced and sold in large quantities to a wide range of consumers. |  Example: The company is trying to break into the mass-markets with its new product. 该公司正试图用其新产品打入大众市场。 | Example: Mass-markets are often targeted by large advertising campaigns.  大众市场经常成为大型广告宣传的目标。 | Synonyms: consumer markets, general public, mainstream markets | Antonyms: niche markets, specialized markets | noun

mass-market:/ˌmæs ˈmɑːrkɪt/ | adj. | 大众市场的 | Relating to or intended for the mass market. | Example: The company adopted a mass-market approach to selling its products. 这家公司采用了大众市场销售产品的方法。 | Example: Mass-market products are usually affordable and widely available. 大众市场产品通常价格实惠且容易买到。 | Synonyms: general, mainstream, popular, commercial | Antonyms: niche, specialized, exclusive, limited | adjective

mass-market:/ˌmæs ˈmɑːrkɪt/ | v. | 向大众市场销售 | To market a product or service to the mass market. | Example: The company plans to mass-market its new line of clothing. 这家公司计划向大众市场销售其新的服装系列。 | Example: They are mass-marketing the product through television commercials and social media. 他们通过电视广告和社交媒体向大众市场推广该产品。 | Synonyms: popularize, commercialize, distribute widely | Antonyms: specialize, target a niche market | verb

词源:  "mass-" + "market". "Mass" refers to a large quantity or number, and "market" refers to the activity of buying and selling goods or services.  The term "mass market" arose with the development of mass production in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
