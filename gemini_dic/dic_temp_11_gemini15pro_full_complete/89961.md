horseshoe:/ˈhɔːrsʃuː/ (US), /ˈhɔːsʃuː/ (UK)| n.|马蹄铁；马蹄形的东西|A U-shaped piece of iron nailed to the bottom of a horse's hoof to protect it from wear and tear.|The blacksmith carefully shaped the horseshoe.|近义词：crescent (for shape), farrier (for the maker/process)|反义词: None|词性: noun

Etymology:
From Old English *hors-scōh* ("horse-shoe"), composed of *hors* ("horse") and *scōh* ("shoe"). Cognate with Old Norse *hrossskór*, Old High German *huofiskōh* and Dutch *hoefijzer*.
