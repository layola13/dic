disinvestiture:/dɪsɪnˈvɛstɪtʃər/
n.|撤资，剥离|The act of selling off assets, such as stocks, bonds, or property, or the process of withdrawing investment from a particular area or sector.|The government announced a disinvestiture of its stake in the national airline. 政府宣布撤出其在国家航空公司的股份。|divestment, divestiture, liquidation|investment, acquisition|noun
disinvestiture:/dɪsɪnˈvɛstɪtʃər/
n.|放弃，退出|The act of giving up or relinquishing something, such as power, control, or responsibility.|The disinvestiture of colonial power led to the formation of several independent nations.  殖民权力的放弃导致了几个独立国家的形成。|renunciation, abandonment, relinquishment|acquisition, assumption|noun

词源: dis- (not, opposite of) + invest (to put money into something) + -ure (noun suffix)  来自拉丁语 investire（包围，覆盖），后来引申为投资。dis- 前缀表示相反的动作，因此 disinvest 表示撤资。-ure 后缀构成名词，表示行为或结果。
