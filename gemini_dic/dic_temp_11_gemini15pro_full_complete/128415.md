词源:  "Newfangled" comes from the Middle English word "newefangel,"  a compound of "new" and "fangle" (from "fangen," meaning "to seize or take").  It originally meant "eager for novelty," but the meaning shifted to refer to the novelties themselves, often with a negative connotation.
