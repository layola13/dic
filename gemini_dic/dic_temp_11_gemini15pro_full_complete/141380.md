opercular:/oʊˈpɜːrkjʊlər/ | adj. | 鳃盖的，盖状的 |Relating to or resembling an operculum; a lid or covering, especially the gill cover of a fish. | The fish's opercular movements increased as it struggled to breathe. 这条鱼挣扎着呼吸时，鳃盖的活动增加了。| The opercular bones protect the delicate gills of the fish. 鳃盖骨保护着鱼脆弱的鳃。 | synonyms: covering, lid-like, protective | antonyms: uncovered, exposed | adjective

operculum:/oʊˈpɜːrkjʊləm/ | n. | 鳃盖，盖 | A lid or covering, especially the gill cover of a fish; any lid-like or covering structure. | The operculum of the snail sealed its shell tightly. 蜗牛的盖子紧紧地封住了它的壳。 |  The surgeon removed the operculum to access the inner ear.  外科医生取下了盖子以便进入内耳。| synonyms: cover, lid, flap | antonyms: opening, aperture | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *operculum* "lid, cover," diminutive of *operis* "work, effort, deed" (related to *opus* "work").
