counterculturalist: /ˌkaʊntərˈkʌltʃərəlɪst/ | n. | 反文化者，反主流文化者 | A person who opposes or rejects the dominant values and norms of society. |  Example: The counterculturalists of the 1960s challenged traditional social and political structures.  60年代的反文化主义者挑战了传统的社会和政治结构。

Example:  He was a counterculturalist who embraced alternative lifestyles and philosophies. 他是一个反文化主义者，信奉另类的生活方式和哲学。 | Synonym: nonconformist, rebel, dissident, iconoclast | Antonym: conformist, traditionalist | Noun


counterculture: /ˈkaʊntərˌkʌltʃər/ | n. | 反文化 |  A way of life and set of attitudes opposed to or at variance with the prevailing social norm. | Example: The counterculture of the 1960s embraced peace, love, and personal liberation.  60年代的反文化运动倡导和平、爱和个人解放。

Example: The punk rock movement was a form of counterculture. 朋克摇滚运动是一种反文化形式。| Synonym: subculture, underground | Antonym: mainstream culture | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"counter-" (prefix): from Latin "contra" meaning "against"
"culture": from Latin "cultura" meaning "cultivation," figuratively "care, culture, refinement"
"-ist" (suffix): forms agent nouns, denoting a person who practices or is associated with something
