cravenness:/ˈkreɪvənnəs/
n.
懦弱，胆怯
The state of being craven; abject cowardice.
His cravenness in the face of danger was shocking.  他在危险面前的懦弱令人震惊。

近义词:cowardice, pusillanimity, timidity
反义词:bravery, courage, valor
词性:noun

Etymology: From craven + -ness. Craven (adjective) comes from Middle English cravant, from Old French cravanté, past participle of cravanter "to crush, oppress," perhaps from crever "to break."
