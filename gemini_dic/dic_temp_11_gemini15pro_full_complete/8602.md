anticlinorium:/ˌæntiklaɪˈnɔːriəm/ | n. |复斜构造，背斜群|A large anticlinal structure, composed of many smaller folds, extending over a considerable area.  It is a regional upwarping of stratified rocks in which the individual folds constituting it are commonly asymmetric and overturned and the axes of the folds plunge towards the center of the structure.|The Appalachian Mountains are a classic example of an anticlinorium.|Synonym: arch, uplift |Antonym: synclinorium|noun

anticlinorium:/ˌæntiklaɪˈnɔːriəm/ | n. |复斜构造，背斜群|A broad uplift resembling an anticline but composed of many smaller folds.|Geologists identified the structure as a complex anticlinorium.|Synonym: mega-anticline,  composite anticline |Antonym: synclinorium|noun
