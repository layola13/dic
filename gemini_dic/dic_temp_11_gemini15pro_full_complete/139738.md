ochre:/ˈoʊkər/| n. adj.|赭石，赭色；赭色的|An earthy pigment containing hydrated iron oxide, varying in color from light yellow to deep orange or brown; of the color ochre.|The artist mixed ochre with linseed oil to create a rich, earthy tone.  艺术家将赭石与亚麻籽油混合，创造出一种丰富的泥土色调。 The ochre cliffs glowed in the setting sun. 赭色的悬崖在夕阳下闪闪发光。|Synonyms: umber, sienna,  brownish-yellow, yellowish-brown|Antonyms: pale, light, white|noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French ocre, from Latin ōchra, from Ancient Greek ὤχρα (ṓkhra, “pale yellow”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₁weh₂ḱ- (“pale yellow”).
