ill-treating: /ɪlˈtriːtɪŋ/
|v.|虐待; 苛待|The act of treating someone cruelly or badly.|He was accused of ill-treating his animals. 他被指控虐待动物。|Synonyms: abusing, mistreating, maltreating|Antonyms: cherishing, nurturing|Verb (present participle)

ill-treat: /ɪlˈtriːt/
|v.|虐待; 苛待|To treat someone cruelly or badly.|The RSPCA investigates claims of owners ill-treating their pets. 皇家防止虐待动物协会调查主人虐待宠物的指控。|Synonyms: abuse, mistreat, maltreat|Antonyms: cherish, nurture|Verb

Etymology:
From Middle English ille-treten, from ille (“ill, badly”) + treten (“to treat”).  The word "ill" in this context means "badly" or "poorly," and "treat" retains its basic meaning of "to act or behave towards."
