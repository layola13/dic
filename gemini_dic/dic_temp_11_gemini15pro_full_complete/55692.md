diureide:/ˈdaɪjʊrɪˌaɪd/ | n. | 利尿酰胺，尿酸盐 | A compound derived from uric acid by replacing two of its hydrogen atoms with acyl groups.  A nitrogenous waste product often found in the excreta of birds and reptiles. |  Birds excrete nitrogenous waste as diureide. 鸟类以利尿酰胺的形式排出含氮废物。| synonyms: urate | antonyms: N/A | noun

diuretic:/ˌdaɪjʊˈrɛtɪk/ | adj. n. | 利尿的，利尿剂 | (adj.) Increasing the volume of urine excreted. (n.) A substance that promotes the production of urine. | The doctor prescribed a diuretic to reduce the swelling in his legs. 医生开了利尿剂来减轻他腿部的肿胀。  She's on a diuretic to control her blood pressure. 她服用利尿剂来控制血压。 | synonyms:  (adj.) urinary, natriuretic; (n.) water pill | antonyms:  antidiuretic | adjective, noun
