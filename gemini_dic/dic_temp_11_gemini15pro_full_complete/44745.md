crumble:/ˈkrʌmbəl/ (US), /ˈkrʌmbl/ (UK)| v. n.|崩溃，破碎，弄碎|To break or fall apart into small fragments; to cause something to break or fall apart into small fragments.  The empire began to crumble after the war.  帝国在战后开始崩溃。|The cake crumbled as I tried to cut it.  蛋糕在我试图切它的时候碎了。|近义词：disintegrate, collapse, fall apart|反义词：build, construct, strengthen|词性：动词/名词

crumble:/ˈkrʌmbəl/ (US), /ˈkrʌmbl/ (UK)| n.|碎屑，面包屑|Small fragments of something that has broken or been crumbled. She sprinkled the crumble topping over the fruit.  她在水果上撒了面包屑。|The building was reduced to rubble and crumble. 这座建筑变成了一堆瓦砾和碎屑。|近义词：fragments, crumbs, pieces|反义词：whole, intact|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *crumblen, frequentative of crumen “to crumble,” from Old English cruma “crumb.”
