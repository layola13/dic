bohemian: /bəʊˈhiːmiən/ (British) /boʊˈhiːmiən/ (American) | n. adj. | 波西米亚人，放荡不羁的艺术家，波西米亚风格的 |  A person, especially an artist or writer, who lives and acts in an unconventional way, ignoring social rules and conventions.  As an adjective, it describes a lifestyle or appearance that is unconventional, artistic, and free-spirited. | She led a bohemian life, travelling from country to country and living as she pleased. 她过着波西米亚式的生活，从一个国家旅行到另一个国家，随心所欲地生活。 | synonyms: unconventional, nonconformist, artistic, free-spirited | antonyms: conventional, conformist, bourgeois | noun

bohemian: /bəʊˈhiːmiən/ (British) /boʊˈhiːmiən/ (American) | adj. | 波西米亚风格的，放荡不羁的 |  Having an unconventional lifestyle, especially one associated with artistic or literary circles. Characterized by a lack of concern for conventional rules of behavior. |  Her bohemian style was reflected in her flowing clothes and eclectic jewelry. 她的波西米亚风格体现在她飘逸的衣服和不拘一格的珠宝上。 | synonyms: unconventional, nonconformist, artistic, free-spirited | antonyms: conventional, conformist, bourgeois | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *bohémien*, "Gypsy," because Gypsies were mistakenly thought to have come from Bohemia (a region in the Czech Republic).
