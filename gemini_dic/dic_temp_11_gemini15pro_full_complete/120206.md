mineralizer:/ˈmɪnərəlaɪzər/
n.|矿化剂|A substance that promotes the crystallization of minerals or facilitates the formation of a desired mineral assemblage during rock formation.|The presence of a mineralizer like fluorine can significantly influence the size and shape of crystals.|Synonym: flux, catalyst|Antonym: inhibitor|noun

mineralizer:/ˈmɪnərəlaɪzər/
n.|矿化器|A device used to add minerals to water or other liquids.|Some mineralizers are designed to add specific minerals like calcium or magnesium to drinking water.|Synonym: mineralizer|Antonym: demineralizer|noun
