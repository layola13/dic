conjuror:/ˈkʌndʒərər/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒərə/ (UK)| n. |魔术师；巫师；术士|A person who performs magic tricks to entertain an audience, or who claims to have magical powers. |The conjuror pulled a rabbit out of a hat. 魔术师从帽子里拉出一只兔子。|Synonyms: magician, illusionist, sorcerer, wizard|Antonyms: none|noun


conjure:/ˈkʌndʒər/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒə(r)/ (UK)| v. |变戏法；使…像变魔术般出现；恳求；使想起|To perform magic tricks; to make something appear as if by magic; to implore; to evoke a memory or feeling.|He conjured a dove out of thin air. 他凭空变出一只鸽子。  She conjured up images of her childhood. 她想起了童年的景象。|Synonyms: evoke, summon, implore, beseech|Antonyms: banish, dispel|verb
