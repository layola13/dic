glyphography: /ɡlɪˈfɒɡrəfi/
noun
雕版印刷术；电刻术
A process of etching designs on a copper plate by electrolysis, for printing.  A relief etching process in which the image is drawn on a wax-coated metal plate, which is then electroplated and used for printing.
Glyphography was a popular printing method in the 19th century.  雕版印刷术在19世纪是一种流行的印刷方法。
The museum has a collection of glyphographs from Victorian times.  博物馆收藏了一些维多利亚时代的雕版印刷品。
近义词: electrotyping, engraving
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps hand-drawing, lithography as alternative methods)
词性: n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *glyphē* (γλυφή, "carving") + *-graphia* (-γραφία, "writing, description").
