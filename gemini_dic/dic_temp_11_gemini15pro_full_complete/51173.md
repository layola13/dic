desquamative:/dɪˈskwɒmətɪv/| adj.|脱屑的，落屑的|Relating to or characterized by desquamation; shedding or peeling of the outer layer of skin.|The patient presented with desquamative rash on his hands and feet. 病人的手脚出现了脱屑性皮疹。|Synonyms: exfoliative, peeling, shedding|Antonyms: adherent, intact|adjective

desquamate:/dɪˈskweɪmeɪt/| v.|脱落，剥落|To shed or peel off in scales or flakes, especially the outer layer of skin.|The skin on his sunburn began to desquamate. 他晒伤的皮肤开始脱落。|Synonyms: peel, shed, exfoliate|Antonyms: adhere, stick|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin desquamare (“to scale off”), from de- (“from, away from”) + squama (“scale”).  最终源自原始印欧语 *skwel- (“to cut, split”).
