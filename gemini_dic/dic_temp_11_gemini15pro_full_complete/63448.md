entrenched:/ɪnˈtrentʃt/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；牢固确立的 | Firmly established and difficult to change. |  The company's position in the market is now deeply entrenched.  这家公司在市场上的地位现在根深蒂固。

The idea of individual liberty is entrenched in the American constitution. 个人自由的理念深深植根于美国宪法之中。


| Synonym: established, fixed, ingrained, rooted | Antonym:  weak, unstable, superficial, precarious | 词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French "entrencher" (to dig a trench, to establish firmly), from "en-" (in) + "trencher" (to cut).  Ultimately from Latin "truncare" (to cut off, shorten).
