improperation:/ˌɪmprəˈpreɪʃən/ | n. | 不当行为，不得体的言行，失礼 | An act or remark that is unsuitable or inappropriate, especially in being rude or improper. |  His loud laughter during the ceremony was considered an impropriety.  他在仪式上的大笑被认为是一种不当行为。|  Synonyms:  misconduct, indecorum, unseemliness, impropriety, gaffe | Antonyms:  decorum, propriety, seemliness | noun

impropriety:/ˌɪmprəˈpraɪəti/ | n. | 不适当;不得体 | The state or quality of being improper; an improper act or remark. | There was a hint of impropriety in his dealings with the client. 他与客户的交易中有些不当之处。 | Synonyms:  misconduct, indecorum, unseemliness, impropriation, gaffe | Antonyms:  decorum, propriety, seemliness | noun
