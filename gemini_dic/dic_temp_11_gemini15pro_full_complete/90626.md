humanisms:/ˈhjuːmənɪzəmz/| n. |人文主义（复数）|A Renaissance intellectual movement that focused on human potential and achievements, emphasizing classical studies and valuing human dignity and worth.  It often refers to a variety of ethical stances that emphasize human reason, ethics, and justice, while sometimes rejecting the importance of traditional religion.  In the plural, it can refer to different historical or contemporary expressions of this thought.|例句：Many modern humanisms are secular. 许多现代人文主义是世俗的。

例句：Her work reflected the humanisms of the Renaissance. 她的作品反映了文艺复兴时期的人文主义。|近义词：humanitarianism, liberal arts, Renaissance humanism|反义词:spiritualism, asceticism|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *humanismus*, from Latin humanus (“human”) + -ismus (“-ism”).
