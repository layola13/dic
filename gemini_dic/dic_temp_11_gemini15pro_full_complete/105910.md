kitza: /ˈkɪtsə/ | n. | 小猫 (尤指犹太教饮食规条许可食用的动物) | A kitza is a young cat, specifically one that is considered kosher according to Jewish dietary laws.  It refers to an animal that is permissible to eat, but the term is rarely used in modern contexts and may be considered archaic or humorous. |  The rabbi joked about blessing the family's new kitza. 拉比开玩笑说要祝福这家人新养的小猫（可食用的）。|  Kitten, young cat |  (No direct antonym, potentially "adult cat") | noun
