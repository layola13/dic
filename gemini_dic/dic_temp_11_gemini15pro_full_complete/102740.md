janizary: /ˈdʒænɪˌzɛri/ | n. | 近卫军，禁卫军 (尤指旧时土耳其或某些其他东方国家的) |  A member of the Turkish infantry forming the Sultan's guard between the 14th and 19th centuries, known for their military skill and loyalty (until they became a powerful and unruly force).  Origin: from Turkish *yeniçeri*, literally ‘new troop’. | The janizaries were once the elite fighting force of the Ottoman Empire.  近卫军曾经是奥斯曼帝国的精锐部队。 |  The janizaries revolted against the Sultan in the 19th century.  近卫军在19世纪反抗了苏丹。 | Synonyms: bodyguard, guardsman, soldier | Antonyms: civilian, commoner | noun
