make amends:/meɪk əˈmendz/
|verb phrase|
|弥补；赔罪；赔偿|
|To make amends is to compensate or make up for a wrongdoing or injury. It involves taking action to right a wrong, often by expressing remorse, offering an apology, or providing restitution.|
|He tried to make amends for his past mistakes by donating to charity. 他试图通过向慈善机构捐款来弥补过去的错误。|

|She made amends to her friend by buying her flowers and apologizing sincerely. 她通过给朋友买花并真诚地道歉来弥补。|

|近义词：atone, compensate, apologize, rectify|
|反义词：worsen, damage, offend|
|词性：verb phrase|
