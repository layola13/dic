maladdress:/ˌmæləˈdrɛs/ | vt. |  处理不当，笨拙地处理；词不达意 | To handle clumsily or ineptly; to address incorrectly or inappropriately. | He maladdressed the situation, making it worse. 他处理情况不当，使情况变得更糟。

maladdress:/ˌmæləˈdrɛs/ | vt. |  写错地址 | To write the wrong address on (a letter, package, etc.).  |  The package was returned because it was maladdressed. 包裹被退回来了，因为地址写错了。

maladdress:/ˌmæləˈdrɛs/ | n. |  拙劣的处理；词不达意 | Clumsy or inept handling; an inappropriate or incorrect address. |  His maladdress of the issue led to further complications. 他对这个问题的拙劣处理导致了进一步的复杂化。



Etymology:
From mal- (badly) + address (to direct).  来源于 mal-（糟糕地）+ address（处理，指向）。


Synonyms:
mismanage, mishandle, bungle, fumble, blunder, botch


Antonyms:
manage well, handle competently


Part of Speech:
Verb (primarily), Noun (less common)
