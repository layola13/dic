limerick: /ˈlɪmərɪk/ | n. | 打油诗，五行诗 | A humorous, frequently bawdy, verse of three long and two short lines rhyming aabba. | Example 1:  He wrote a limerick about a flea. 他写了一首关于跳蚤的打油诗。

Example 2: The limerick made everyone laugh. 这首打油诗让大家都笑了。 | synonym:  nonsense verse, jingle, rhyme | antonym:  epic, sonnet | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Limerick, a county in Ireland, though the precise connection is uncertain.  Possibly related to a parlor game involving singing and composing rhyming lines, or to the chorus "Will you come up to Limerick?"
