intercut: /ˌɪntərˈkʌt/ (US) /ˌɪntəˈkʌt/ (UK)
verb
插入；穿插；交切
To insert (shots from other scenes, flashbacks, etc.) into the main narrative of a film.
The director intercut scenes of the war with images of the family at home. 导演将战争场面与家人在家的画面穿插在一起。
interweave, intersperse, interpose
