dissimulation's:/dɪˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃənz/| n.|伪装，掩饰，虚伪|The act of concealing one's true feelings or intentions.  It's the possessive form of "dissimulation," indicating ownership or association.|His dissimulation's effectiveness surprised everyone; no one suspected his true motives. 他的伪装如此有效，让每个人都感到惊讶；没有人怀疑他的真实动机。|Synonyms: deception, pretense, feigning|Antonyms: honesty, candor, openness|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dissimulatio*, from Latin dissimulare (“to disguise, conceal”), from dis- (“not, un-”) + simulare (“to resemble, imitate, feign”).
