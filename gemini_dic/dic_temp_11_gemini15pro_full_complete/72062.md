flit:/flɪt/| v.|轻快地飞;掠过，迅速闪过|To move swiftly and lightly.  The butterfly flitted from flower to flower. 蝴蝶在花丛中飞来飞去。|近义词：dart, flutter, flash|反义词:lumber, plod|verb

flit:/flɪt/| n.|飞掠; 闪现|A quick, light movement.  I caught a flit of a bluebird in the garden. 我在花园里瞥到了一只蓝知更鸟。|近义词：flutter, glimpse|反义词:stay, linger|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *flytja "to convey, transport," causative of *flita "to hasten, be busy," from Proto-Germanic *flitan "to strive, contend," from PIE root *plei- "to flow."  The original sense was "to remove, shift," later "to move quickly and lightly."
