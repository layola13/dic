foreday:/ˈfɔːrdeɪ/ | n. |黎明，破晓|The time just before dawn; the very beginning of the day.|We set off at foreday to avoid the heat. 我们在黎明出发以避开炎热。|近义词：dawn, daybreak, sunrise|反义词：sunset, dusk, nightfall|词性:名词

Etymology: From Middle English *foreday, from Old English *foredæg ("preceding day, eve, vigil"), from fore- + dæg ("day").  Cognate with Scots foreday ("daybreak"). The sense shifted from "preceding day" to "beginning of the day" over time.
