loger:/ˈloʊɡər/ | n. |寄宿者；房客|A person who rents a room in someone else's house.|例句：The lodger paid his rent weekly.  房客每周支付房租。|例句：The family had a lodger to help with the bills. 这家人有一个房客来帮忙付账单。|近义词：tenant, roomer, boarder|反义词: landlord, landlady|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *loggere*, from Old French *logier*, "to lodge, accommodate," from Frankish *lauʒōn* (“to lodge”), from Proto-Germanic *lauʒōną* (“to lie, to lodge, to marry”). Cognate with Dutch *legeren* (“to lodge, to camp”), German *lagern* (“to camp, to store, to layer”), and English *lay*.
