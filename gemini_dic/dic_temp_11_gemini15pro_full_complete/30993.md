chalk:/tʃɔːk/ (UK), /tʃɑːk/ (US)| n. v. |粉笔；白垩；用粉笔写|A soft white, gray, or buff limestone composed chiefly of the shells of minute marine organisms.  Used for writing or drawing, especially on blackboards or other surfaces.|She wrote the answer on the board in chalk. 她用粉笔在黑板上写下了答案。|Synonyms:crayon, pastel|Antonyms:eraser|Noun, Verb


chalk up:/tʃɔːk ʌp/ (UK), /tʃɑːk ʌp/ (US)| v. |记下；归因于|To record or attribute something, often a success or failure, to a particular cause.|They chalked up another victory. 他们又取得了一场胜利。|Synonyms:record, attribute|Antonyms:ignore, disregard|Verb
