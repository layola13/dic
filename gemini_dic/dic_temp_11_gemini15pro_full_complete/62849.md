enjoining:/ɪnˈdʒɔɪnɪŋ/
verb
命令；嘱咐；规定
To instruct or urge (someone) to do something.
The judge enjoined the defendant from contacting the victim. 法官命令被告不得联系受害人。
近义词: commanding, ordering, directing, instructing, urging, admonishing
反义词: allowing, permitting, authorizing


enjoining:/ɪnˈdʒɔɪnɪŋ/
adjective
命令的；嘱咐的；规定的 (This form is less common but can be used as a present participle acting as an adjective)
Referring to the act of instructing or urging someone to do something.
The enjoining words of the king resonated throughout the kingdom. 国王的命令之词响彻整个王国。
近义词: commanding, ordering
反义词: permitting


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French enjoindre, from Latin iniungere "to join on, attach, fasten to; impose, inflict; enjoin, order," from in- "in, on" + iungere "to join."
