friends:/frendz/
n.|朋友；友人；支持者；赞助者|People whom one knows and with whom one has a bond of mutual affection, typically exclusive of sexual or family relations.|例句：She's going out with her friends tonight. 她今晚要和朋友们出去。|例句：He made many friends in college. 他在大学里交了很多朋友。|近义词：companions, pals, buddies|反义词：enemies, foes|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *frendes*, plural of *frend*, from Old English *frēond* (“friend, lover; relative, kinsman”), from Proto-Germanic *frijōndz* (“friend, lover”), from Proto-Indo-European *prih₂- (“to love, to like”). Cognate with West Frisian *freon* (“friend”), Dutch *vriend* (“friend, boyfriend”), German *Freund* (“friend”), Old Norse *frændi* (“relative, kinsman”), Gothic *𐍆𐍂𐌹𐌾𐍉𐌽𐌳𐍃* (frijōnds, “friend”). Related also to Old English *frēon* (“to love, to free”).
