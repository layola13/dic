ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *desirable*, from Old French *desirable*, from Latin *dēsīderābilis* (“worthy of being desired”), from *dēsīderō* (“long for, desire, wish for, demand, require, expect, miss, regret”), from *de* (“from, down from, out of”) + *sīderō* (“consider, contemplate a star, observe the stars in order to foretell something, be affected by the influence of a star”).
