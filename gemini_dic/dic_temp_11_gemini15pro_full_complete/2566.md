admittatur:/ˌædmɪˈteɪtər/ | v. |  may he/she/it be admitted | Let it be admitted that the evidence is inconclusive.  让大家承认证据尚无定论。| English explanation: The third-person singular present active subjunctive form of the Latin verb "admitto," meaning "I admit" or "I allow to enter."  It is used in formal or legal contexts to express a wish, possibility, or hypothetical situation. |  例句：Admittatur persona illa in hospitium.  Let that person be admitted into the guesthouse. | 近义词: be allowed, be admitted | 反义词: be denied, be excluded | 词性: Verb (Subjunctive)
