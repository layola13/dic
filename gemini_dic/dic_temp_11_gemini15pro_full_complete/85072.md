hard-liner: /ˈhɑːrdˌlaɪnər/ (US) /ˈhɑːdˌlaɪnə/ (UK)| n.|强硬派；强硬路线者|A person who adheres rigidly to a set of beliefs or policies, especially political ones, and is unwilling to compromise. |The hard-liners in the party refused to consider any changes to their platform. 党内的强硬派拒绝考虑对他们的纲领做出任何修改。|synonyms:  extremist, diehard, zealot,  ultraconservative, inflexible person| antonyms: moderate, compromiser, pragmatist| noun


hard-liner: /ˈhɑːrdˌlaɪnər/ (US) /ˈhɑːdˌlaɪnə/ (UK)| adj.|强硬路线的；强硬派的|Relating to or characteristic of a hard-liner. |The government took a hard-line stance on immigration. 政府在移民问题上采取了强硬立场。|synonyms: uncompromising, inflexible, rigid, unyielding| antonyms: flexible, moderate, compromising, yielding| adjective
