abominate:/əˈbɒmɪneɪt/ | v. | 憎恶，厌恶，痛恨 | To hate something very much or find it disgusting. | He abominates cruelty to animals. 他憎恶虐待动物。 | 近义词: detest, loathe, abhor, despise | 反义词: admire, love, cherish | verb

abominated:/əˈbɒmɪneɪtɪd/ | v. | 憎恶，厌恶，痛恨（过去式/过去分词） |  Past tense and past participle of "abominate". |  Slavery was a practice that many people abominated. 奴隶制是一种许多人憎恶的做法。 | 近义词: detested, loathed, abhorred, despised | 反义词: admired, loved, cherished | verb

词源:
From Latin abominari "deprecate as an ill omen," from ab- "away from" (see ab-) + ominari "forebode, prophesy," related to omen (see omen). The sense evolved to "abhor, detest."
