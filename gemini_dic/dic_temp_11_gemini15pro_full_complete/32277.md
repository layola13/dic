chidon:/ˈtʃaɪdən/| n.|斥责，责骂; 责骂的对象|A rebuke or scolding; a person who is rebuked or scolded. |She received a chidon from her mother for staying out too late. 她因为在外面待得太晚而受到了母亲的责骂。|Synonyms: rebuke, scolding, reprimand, reproach, reproof|Antonyms: praise, commendation, compliment|noun


etymology->
From Middle English *chiden*, from Old English *cīdan* (“to chide, rebuke, reproach, blame, complain, quarrel, dispute, contend”), from Proto-Germanic *kīdaną* (“to quarrel, contend, complain”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱeyd- (“to worry, be anxious; to hate”). Cognate with Scots chide (“to scold, rebuke”), West Frisian kide (“to scold”), Dutch kijven (“to quarrel, squabble”), German schelten (“to scold”), Danish kide (“to tickle”), Swedish kida (“to tickle”), Icelandic kída (“to tickle”).
