amorite:/ˈæməˌraɪt/
n.
亚摩利人；亚摩利语
a member of a Semitic people who invaded Mesopotamia c. 2000 BC and built a large empire, which lasted until c. 1600 BC.  Also: the language of this people.
The Amorites conquered much of Mesopotamia. 亚摩利人征服了美索不达米亚的大部分地区。
synonyms:  Canaanite (sometimes used interchangeably, though not strictly synonymous)
antonyms:  (No direct antonym, as it refers to a specific group of people. Could potentially use terms like "native Mesopotamians" in a specific context to denote the people they displaced.)
noun
