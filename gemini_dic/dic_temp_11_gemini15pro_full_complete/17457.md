bearabilities:/ˌbɛərəˈbɪlɪtiz/| n. |可忍受性；可承受性|The plural form of "bearability," referring to the qualities or aspects of something that make it bearable.  It highlights the multiple factors contributing to something being tolerable. |The bearabilities of the situation were few, but they kept her going.  这种情况下的可忍受性很少，但它们支撑着她继续下去。|近义词:tolerabilities, manageabilities|反义词:intolerabilities, unmanageableness|词性:名词

词源: From "bearable" + "-ities" (suffix forming plural nouns). "Bearable" comes from "bear" (to endure) + "-able" (suffix meaning "capable of being").  The root "bear" traces back to Old English "beran."
