haemorrhaged:/ˈhɛmərɪdʒd/| v.| 出血；大量流失|To lose a large amount of something, especially money or resources, rapidly or suddenly.|The company haemorrhaged money in the first quarter. 该公司在第一季度大量亏损。|Synonyms: bleed, lose, deplete|Antonyms: gain, accumulate|verb

haemorrhage:/ˈhɛmərɪdʒ/| n.| 出血；大量流失|A large loss of something, especially money or resources, rapidly or suddenly; a heavy discharge of blood from a ruptured blood vessel.|The government's policies led to a haemorrhage of jobs. 政府的政策导致了大量的工作岗位流失。|Synonyms: bleeding, loss, outflow|Antonyms: gain, influx|noun
