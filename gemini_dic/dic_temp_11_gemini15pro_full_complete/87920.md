hierolatry:/ˌhaɪərˈɒlətri/ | n. | 圣物崇拜；偶像崇拜 | The worship of sacred things. Excessive reverence for tradition or ritual. |  The museum's exhibit on ancient Egypt explored the intricacies of their hierolatry, showcasing the reverence they held for various deities and sacred objects.  The tribe's hierolatry involved elaborate rituals and offerings to appease the spirits of their ancestors. | 近义词: idolatry, iconolatry, fetishism | 反义词: iconoclasm | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek ἱερός (hierós, “holy, sacred”) + -λατρεία (-latreia, “worship, service”).
