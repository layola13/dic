colter:/ˈkəʊltər/ (British), /ˈkoʊltər/ (American)| n. |犁刀; 开沟器|A cutting device attached to the beam of a plow, used to make a vertical cut in the soil ahead of the plowshare.|The farmer attached a new colter to his plow before starting the spring tilling.  农民在开始春季耕作之前在他的犁上安装了一个新的犁刀。|The colter sliced through the tough sod. 犁刀切开了坚硬的草皮。|近义词：knife, blade|反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps 'blunt edge')|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *culter, from Latin culter (“knife, plowshare”), related to colere (“to cultivate”).
