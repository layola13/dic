orthopteran: /ɔːˈθɒptərən/ (UK), /ɔːrˈθɑːptərən/ (US) | n. | 直翅目昆虫 | An insect of the order Orthoptera, such as grasshoppers, crickets, and locusts. ETYMOLOGY->
From the Greek words "orthos" (straight) and "pteron" (wing), referring to the straight, leathery forewings characteristic of many orthopterans.
