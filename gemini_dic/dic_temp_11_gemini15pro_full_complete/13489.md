attach:/əˈtætʃ/| v.| 附加，系上，贴上；使依恋；使依附 |To fasten or join one thing to another.  To connect oneself to something, such as an organization or group. To believe that something has importance or significance. |I attached a photo to my application. 我把照片附在了我的申请表上。

|Synonyms: affix, append, connect, join, link, fasten, adhere |Antonyms: detach, remove, separate, disconnect|verb


attach:/əˈtætʃ/| n.| 附件 |Something attached to something else. (Usually used in the plural form "attachments") |Please find the attachments for your reference. 请查看附件供您参考。

|Synonyms: appendix, supplement, addendum, accessory |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for the noun form) |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "atachier"  meaning "to fasten, tie, attach,"  from "a-" (to) + "tachier" (to fasten).  "Tachier" comes from a Frankish or other Germanic source related to Old High German "zahhen" (to pull, draw).
