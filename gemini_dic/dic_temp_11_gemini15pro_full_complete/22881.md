botcher:/ˈbɒtʃə(r)/ | n. | 笨手笨脚的人；拙劣的工人 | A person who does something clumsily or badly; a bungler. | He's a real botcher in the kitchen, always burning things. 他在厨房里真是个笨手笨脚的人，总是把东西烧糊。|  Near synonyms: bungler, muddler, duffer | Antonyms: expert, professional | Noun

botch:/bɒtʃ/ | vt. | 笨拙地弄糟；拙劣地修理 | To spoil something by doing it clumsily or badly. | He botched the repair job so badly that the car was worse than before. 他把修理工作搞得一塌糊涂，以至于车子比以前更糟了。| Near synonyms: bungle, mess up, ruin | Antonyms: perfect, master | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bocchen*, probably of imitative origin.  Relates to words suggesting clumsy or awkward movement.
