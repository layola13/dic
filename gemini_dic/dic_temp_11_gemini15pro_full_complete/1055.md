accent:/ˈæksent/ (US), /ˈæksənt/ (UK)| n.|口音，腔调；重音；重音符号|a distinctive way of pronouncing a language, especially one associated with a particular country, area, or social class; emphasis given to a particular syllable or word in speech; a mark on a letter or word to indicate stress, pronunciation, etc.|She speaks with a Southern accent. 她说话带有南方口音。|synonym: pronunciation, intonation, inflection|antonym: monotone|noun


accent:/əkˈsɛnt/ (US), /əkˈsɛnt/ (UK)| vt.|强调；重读|to emphasize something; to pronounce a syllable or word with stress|The lighting accented the beauty of the sculpture. 灯光突出了雕塑的美。|synonym: emphasize, highlight, stress|antonym: downplay, de-emphasize|verb
