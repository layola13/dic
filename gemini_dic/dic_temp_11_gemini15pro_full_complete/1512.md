acetonemia: /ˌæsɪtəˈniːmiə/ | n. | 丙酮血症 | A medical condition characterized by the presence of excessive amounts of acetone in the blood.  |  Example: The doctor diagnosed the patient with acetonemia due to uncontrolled diabetes. 医生诊断该病人患有因糖尿病失控引起的丙酮血症。 | Synonym: ketoacidosis, ketosis | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, consider "normal blood ketone levels") | Noun
