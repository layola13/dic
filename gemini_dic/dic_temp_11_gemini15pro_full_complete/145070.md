overanalytically:/ˌoʊvərˌænəˈlɪtɪkli/
adverb|过度分析地|in a way that involves too much analysis; excessively analytically|She tends to overanalytically dissect every conversation she has, searching for hidden meanings.|Synonyms: excessively, too much, overmuch|Antonyms: superficially, cursorily|词性:副词

Etymology:
"over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi "over, above") + "analytically" (from "analyze" + "-tically"). "Analyze" comes from French *analyser*, from Late Latin *analysare*, from Greek *analyein* "to loosen up, dissolve," from *ana- "up"* + *lyein* "to loosen."
