cnidoblast:/ˈnaɪdəblæst/| n. |刺细胞|A specialized cell containing a nematocyst, found in the tentacles of cnidarians (such as jellyfish and corals) and used for capturing prey and defense.|Jellyfish use their cnidoblasts to sting and paralyze their prey.|Synonyms: stinging cell, nematocyte|Antonyms: None|Noun
