halisauria:/ˌhælɪˈsɔːriə/
n.
halisaurians (plural)
海蜥亚目
A suborder of extinct marine reptiles belonging to the order Sauropterygia, characterized by small heads, long necks, and paddle-like limbs.  They lived during the Triassic period.
The fossil remains of a halisaurian were discovered in China. 在中国发现了海蜥亚目动物的化石遗骸。
Near Synonyms:  sauropterygian, nothosaur (more general)
No real antonyms for a taxonomic classification.
