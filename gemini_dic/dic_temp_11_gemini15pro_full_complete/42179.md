couldn't-care-less:/ˈkʊdnt ˈkɛər ˈlɛs/| adj.|毫不在乎的，漠不关心的|Indicates a complete lack of concern or interest.  It's often used ironically, where the speaker actually cares very much.| "He said he couldn't care less about the promotion, but I know he really wants it." 他说他根本不在乎晋升，但我知道他真的很想要。 |近义词：indifferent, apathetic, uninterested |反义词:concerned, interested, caring|词性:adjective

could-care-less (often mistakenly used as couldn't-care-less):/ˈkʊd ˈkɛər ˈlɛs/| adj.|毫不在乎的，漠不关心的 (Technically incorrect, but common usage often treats it the same as couldn't-care-less.)| Although logically this phrase should mean that someone *could* care less (implying they do care somewhat), it’s commonly used interchangeably with "couldn't care less" to express apathy.| "She said she could care less about the gossip, but her tone suggested otherwise." 她说她对这些流言蜚语毫不在乎，但她的语气却暗示并非如此。|近义词：indifferent, apathetic, uninterested (though technically incorrect)|反义词:concerned, interested, caring|词性:adjective
