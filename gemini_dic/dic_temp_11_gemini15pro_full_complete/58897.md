durhams:/ˈdɜːrəmz/ | n. | 杜伦（货币单位，现已不再使用）;  （指复数）少量钱 |A former English silver penny, first minted in Durham during the reign of King Stephen (1135-1154).  Also informally used to refer to a small amount of money. | He earned a few durhams for his work. 他工作挣了几便士（少量钱）。 | 近义词: pennies, coins (in older contexts) | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, could consider large sums of money like "fortune") | noun
