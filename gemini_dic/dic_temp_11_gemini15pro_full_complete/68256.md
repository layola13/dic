faithist:/ˈfeɪθɪst/| n.|信仰主义者；宗教主义者|A person who emphasizes faith as the primary basis for belief, sometimes to the exclusion of reason or evidence.|He's a faithist, believing in things without any proof. 他是一个信仰主义者，不经任何证明就相信事物。|近义词：believer, devotee|反义词：skeptic, atheist|词性:noun


Etymology:
From "faith" + "-ist" (suffix forming nouns denoting adherents of a doctrine or system). "Faith" comes from Old French "feid," ultimately from Latin "fides" (trust, belief).
