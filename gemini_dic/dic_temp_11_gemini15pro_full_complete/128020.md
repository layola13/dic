nepotist:/ˈnɛpətɪst/| n. |裙带关系者；任人唯亲者|A person who favors relatives or friends, especially by giving them jobs. |He was accused of being a nepotist when he promoted his nephew over more qualified candidates. 他提拔侄子而不是更有资格的候选人，因此被指责为任人唯亲。|Synonyms: crony, favorite |Antonyms: impartial person, meritocrat |noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *népotisme*, from Italian *nepotismo*, from *nepote* “nephew”, from Latin *nepōtem* (nominative *nepōs*) “grandson, nephew”, from Proto-Italic *nepots*, from Proto-Indo-European *népōts* “descendant, sister’s son”.  The term originated in the Middle Ages, referring to the practice of popes appointing their nephews to important positions in the Catholic Church.
