moth-eaten:/ˈmɔːθˌiːtn/ | adj. | 虫蛀的，破旧的 | Damaged or worn away, as if by moths. |  The old house was filled with moth-eaten furniture.  这栋老房子里摆满了虫蛀的家具。 | moth-bitten, dilapidated, shabby, worn | pristine, immaculate | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "moth" (Old English "moððe") + "eaten" (past participle of "eat," Old English "etan").  The word describes the appearance of fabric or other materials that have been damaged by moth larvae.
