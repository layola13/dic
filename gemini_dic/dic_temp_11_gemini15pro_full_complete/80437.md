Gondwana:/ɡɒndˈwɑːnə/ /ɡɑːndˈwɑːnə/
noun
冈瓦纳大陆
A vast southern supercontinent that existed from the late Precambrian to the Jurassic. It comprised present-day South America, Africa, Arabia, Madagascar, India, Australia, and Antarctica.
Gondwana began to break up around 180 million years ago. 冈瓦纳大陆在大约1.8亿年前开始分裂。

The breakup of Gondwana led to the formation of the Atlantic and Indian Oceans. 冈瓦纳大陆的分裂导致了大西洋和印度洋的形成。

近义词:supercontinent 超级大陆
反义词: (No direct antonym, but one could consider Laurasia, the northern supercontinent, a counterpart)

Etymology: From Gondwana, a region of central India, inhabited by the Gond people.
