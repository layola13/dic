词源:  The word is formed by the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") added to "retarded" (an outdated and offensive term meaning "slowed or delayed in development").  The term "retarded" itself comes from the Latin "retardare," meaning "to slow down."  While "nonretarded" was previously used as a neutral medical term, it is now also considered outdated and potentially offensive.  More appropriate and respectful terms include "typical," "normal," "average," or "neurotypical."
