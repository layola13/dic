inoculation:/ɪˌnɒkjʊˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 接种，灌输 | The act or process of inoculating; the introduction of a serum, vaccine, or antigenic substance into the body of a person or animal, especially to produce immunity to a specific disease; also, the introduction of a microorganism into a culture medium. |  The inoculation against polio has saved millions of lives.  脊髓灰质炎疫苗的接种挽救了数百万人的生命。 | immunization, vaccination | infection, contamination | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin inoculatus, past participle of inoculare "to graft, implant," from in- "in" + oculus "eye, bud" (see ocular).  Originally referred to grafting buds onto plants. The medical sense of "introducing a virus or germ into the body" is from the mid-18th century.
