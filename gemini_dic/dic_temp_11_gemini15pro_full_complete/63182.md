entangle:/ɪnˈtæŋɡl/ | v. | 纠缠，卷入，使…复杂化 | To cause something to become twisted together with something else in a complicated way; to involve someone in a difficult situation. | The fishing line became entangled in the seaweed. 鱼线缠在了海藻里。

The US became entangled in the conflict. 美国被卷入了这场冲突。 | 近义词：tangle, snarl, involve, implicate | 反义词：disentangle, extricate, free | 词性：动词

entanglement:/ɪnˈtæŋɡlmənt/ | n. | 纠缠，缠绕，牵连 | The state of being entangled; a complicated or compromising relationship or situation. |  The entanglement of the wires caused a short circuit. 电线的缠绕导致了短路。

He tried to avoid any entanglement with the media. 他试图避免与媒体有任何牵连。 | 近义词：tangle, complication, involvement | 反义词：disentanglement, separation | 词性：名词
