knock-around:/ˈnɒkəˌraʊnd/ (UK) /ˈnɑːkəˌraʊnd/ (US)| adj. n.|随便的，非正式的；随便穿的衣服；闲逛；殴打|A knock-around is something suitable for informal or casual use.  As a noun, it can also refer to casual clothes or an act of wandering or loafing around. In less common usage, it can also mean a beating or thrashing.|He wore his knock-around clothes to the barbecue. 他穿着他的便服去烧烤。|Synonyms: casual, informal, everyday, comfy, lounging |Antonyms: formal, dressy, elegant, sophisticated |adjective, noun

knock-around:/ˈnɒkəˌraʊnd/ (UK) /ˈnɑːkəˌraʊnd/ (US)| n.|闲逛；殴打|An act of wandering or loafing around, or a beating or thrashing.|He spent the afternoon having a knock-around in town. 他整个下午都在城里闲逛。|Synonyms: wander, loaf, stroll, roam |Antonyms: stay, remain, settle |noun
