anchors:/ˈæŋkərz/ (US), /ˈæŋkəz/ (UK)| n.|锚; 依靠; 主持人; 给(电视或广播节目)当主持人|A heavy object attached to a ship by a cable or chain and used to moor the ship to the sea bottom, typically having a metal shank with a ring or shackle at one end for the cable and a pair of curved and pointed arms at the other.|The ship dropped anchor in the bay. 这艘船在海湾抛锚了。|近义词：fastener, mainstay, support, host, presenter|反义词: instability, detachment|noun


anchors:/ˈæŋkərz/ (US), /ˈæŋkəz/ (UK)| v.|抛锚; 使固定; 使稳固; 主持(电视或广播节目);|To secure (a vessel) by an anchor.|She anchored the boat to the dock. 她把船停泊在码头上。|近义词：fasten, secure, moor, host, present|反义词: detach, loosen|verb
