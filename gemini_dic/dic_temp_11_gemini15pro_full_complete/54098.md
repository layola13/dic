disembogue:/ˌdɪsɪmˈboʊɡ/ /ˌdɪsɛmˈbəʊɡ/| v. |流入，注入；排放|To flow or discharge (itself) into a larger body of water, such as a sea or lake. |The river disembogues into the Pacific Ocean. 这条河流注入太平洋。|近义词：discharge, empty, flow into|反义词:receive, absorb|词性:动词

disembogue:/ˌdɪsɪmˈboʊɡ/ /ˌdɪsɛmˈbəʊɡ/| n. |河口，出海口|The mouth of a river or stream where it flows into a larger body of water.  (This noun usage is less common.)|The disembogue of the river was wide and sandy.  这条河的河口宽阔且多沙。|近义词：mouth, outlet|反义词:source, origin|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Spanish *desembocar* "to flow into the sea," from *des-* "dis-" + *em-* "in" + *boca* "mouth." Ultimately from Latin *bucca* "cheek, mouth."
