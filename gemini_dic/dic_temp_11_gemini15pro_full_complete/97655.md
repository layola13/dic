inhibit:/ɪnˈhɪbɪt/| vt.|抑制，阻止|To restrain, hinder, or prevent the activity or development of something.|Drinking coffee inhibits sleep. 喝咖啡会抑制睡眠。|The drug inhibits the growth of cancer cells.  这种药物抑制癌细胞的生长。|近义词：restrain, hinder, suppress, prevent|反义词:encourage, promote, stimulate|verb

inhibition:/ˌɪnhɪˈbɪʃən/| n.|抑制，阻止|The act of inhibiting or the state of being inhibited.|Social inhibitions can prevent people from speaking up. 社交禁忌会阻止人们畅所欲言。|He overcame his inhibitions and joined the dance class. 他克服了自己的拘谨，参加了舞蹈班。|近义词：restraint, hindrance, suppression|反义词:encouragement, promotion, stimulation|noun

inhibitor:/ɪnˈhɪbɪtər/| n.|抑制剂|A substance that inhibits a chemical reaction or other process.|This enzyme inhibitor can help control blood sugar levels. 这种酶抑制剂可以帮助控制血糖水平。|A corrosion inhibitor was added to protect the metal.  添加了腐蚀抑制剂来保护金属。|近义词：suppressant, blocker|反义词:activator, catalyst|noun
