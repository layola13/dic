custodial: /kʌˈstoʊdiəl/ | adj. | 监护的；保管的；拘留的 | Relating to or involving the legal right to keep and care for someone, especially a child after a separation or divorce of the parents; relating to the safekeeping of something valuable or to the holding of someone in prison. |  例句：The custodial parent is responsible for the child's daily care. 监护人负责孩子的日常照顾。

例句：The museum has a custodial responsibility to preserve the artifacts. 博物馆有保管文物的责任。 | 近义词：protective, guardian,  caretaking,  detaining | 反义词: non-custodial, releasing | 词性: adjective

custodials: /kʌˈstoʊdiəlz/ | n. |  (通常指机构、公司等使用的) 清洁用品；清洁服务 | Cleaning products or services, typically those used by institutions or companies. | 例句:  The school ordered new custodials for the upcoming semester. 学校为即将到来的学期订购了新的清洁用品。

例句: The company outsourced its custodials to a third-party provider.  这家公司将其清洁服务外包给第三方供应商。 | 近义词：cleaning supplies, cleaning services, janitorial supplies | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun (plural)
