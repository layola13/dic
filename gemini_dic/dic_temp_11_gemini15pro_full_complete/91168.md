hydramide:/ˈhaɪdrəmaɪd/ | n. | 酰胺 | An amide, especially one formed from ammonia.  Specifically, a crystalline compound formed by the action of ammonia on an aldehyde. |  The hydramide formed from benzaldehyde is called hydrobenzamide. 由苯甲醛形成的酰胺称为氢苯甲酰胺。 | synonym: amide | antonym:  (No direct antonym for a chemical compound) | noun
