carried-away:/ˈkærid əˈweɪ/ | adj. |忘乎所以的，过度激动的|Overly enthusiastic; transported with emotion; so excited that one doesn't act or think as one normally would.|She got so carried away with the music that she started dancing on the table.|近义词：overexcited, enthusiastic, exuberant, passionate|反义词：calm, composed, restrained|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From the past participle of "carry away," meaning to transport or move something from one place to another.  Figuratively, it evolved to describe a state of being emotionally transported or overwhelmed.
