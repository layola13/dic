lumbrical: /ˈlʌmbrɪk(ə)l/ | adj. n. | 蚓状的；蚓肌 | Relating to or resembling a worm; (noun) any of the four small skeletal muscles in the palm of the hand, associated with the tendons of the flexor digitorum profundus, or any of the similar muscles in the sole of the foot. |  The lumbrical muscles help with fine finger movements.  蚓状肌有助于精细的手指运动。 | Near synonyms:  vermiform, wormlike | Antonyms:  (none for anatomical term) | noun, adjective
