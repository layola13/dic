cavalierisms:/ˌkævəˈlɪərɪzəmz/| n.|傲慢的态度；轻蔑的言行；漫不经心的举动|An arrogant or offhand manner; a haughty disregard of others or of normal procedures. |He dismissed her concerns with a series of cavalierisms. 他以一系列傲慢的态度驳回了她的担忧。|The CEO's cavalierisms about employee safety ultimately led to his downfall. 首席执行官对员工安全的轻蔑态度最终导致了他的垮台。|近义词：arrogance, haughtiness, disdain, superciliousness|反义词：humility, modesty, respect, deference|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cavalierisme*, from *cavalier* ("knight, horseman"), from Late Latin *caballarius* ("horseman"), from *caballus* ("horse").  The sense of "haughtiness" developed from the stereotypical image of a proud, disdainful knight.
