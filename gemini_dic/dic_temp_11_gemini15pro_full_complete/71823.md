fleet:/fliːt/
n.|舰队，船队；机群；车队|A group of ships, aircraft, or vehicles, especially one owned or controlled by a single company or organization.|The company owns a fleet of delivery trucks. 这家公司拥有一支送货卡车车队。|synonyms: armada, flotilla, squadron|antonyms: single, individual|noun

fleet:/fliːt/
adj.|快速的，敏捷的；转瞬即逝的|Moving swiftly; rapid.|A fleet horse. 一匹飞奔的骏马。|synonyms: swift, rapid, nimble|antonyms: slow, sluggish|adjective

fleet:/fliːt/
v.|快速移动，疾驰；掠过|To move swiftly; to flit.|Time fleets by. 时光飞逝。|synonyms: dart, dash, flit|antonyms: dawdle, linger|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *flēotan "to float, swim, flow," related to Old Norse flota "ship, fleet," and Old High German flioȥan "to flow." The original meaning referred to a group of ships moving together, and later extended to other groups of vehicles or aircraft.
