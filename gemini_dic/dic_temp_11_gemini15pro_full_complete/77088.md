garrisoned:/ˈɡærɪs ənd/| adj.| 被驻防的，驻守的 |  Provided with a body of troops stationed in a fort or town to defend it.  | The city was heavily garrisoned during the war.  战争期间，这座城市驻扎了重兵。|  occupied, fortified, defended, manned | abandoned, undefended | adjective

garrison:/ˈɡærɪsən/| n. vt.| 驻军，守备部队；驻防，派兵驻守 | A body of troops stationed in a fortified place; to station troops in (a place) to defend it. | The garrison held the fort for months. 守军坚守堡垒数月。 The general decided to garrison the town with two thousand soldiers. 将军决定派两千名士兵驻守该镇。 | troops, soldiers, force, contingent |  withdraw, evacuate | noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *garir* "to protect, defend,"  and eventually from Frankish *warjan* "to defend, protect". The sense shifted from "protection" to the "place of protection" and then to the "troops stationed at such a place."
