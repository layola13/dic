laddering:/ˈlædərɪŋ/ (US) /ˈlædərɪŋ/ (UK)
n.|阶梯式提问, 逐步深入的询问|A research technique where a series of questions proceeds from product characteristics to user characteristics.  It is used to uncover the underlying emotions and values associated with product choices.|The interviewer used laddering to understand why the customer preferred that particular brand of coffee.|近义词:probing, drilling down, in-depth questioning|反义词:superficial questioning
v.|进行阶梯式提问|The act of asking a series of increasingly personal questions in order to uncover motivations and values.|The market researcher laddered the participants to explore their brand loyalty.|近义词:probe, delve, investigate|反义词:skim, glance over
