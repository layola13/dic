counterpoint:/ˈkaʊntərpɔɪnt/ | n. | 对位法；对比；反论 |  A contrasting element, item, or theme, typically one that is complementary to another. | The spicy peppers were a fiery counterpoint to the creamy avocado. 辣椒的辛辣与牛油果的柔滑形成了鲜明的对比。| synonyms: contrast, antithesis, foil | antonyms: harmony, agreement, conformity | noun

counterpoint:/ˈkaʊntərpɔɪnt/ | v. | 形成对照；构成对比 | To set in contrast or opposition; to create a counterpoint. | The director counterpointed the light and shadow to create a dramatic effect. 导演将光影进行对比，营造出戏剧性的效果。 | synonyms: juxtapose, contrast, oppose | antonyms: harmonize, blend, unify | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *contrepoint*, from Medieval Latin *contrapunctum*, from *contra* ("against") + *punctum* ("point").  Originally a musical term referring to a composition with independent melodic lines. The broader sense of "contrast" developed from this musical meaning.
