fire:/ˈfaɪər/ (US), /ˈfaɪə(r)/ (UK)| n. v. adj.|火，火灾，射击，解雇|A state, process, or instance of combustion in which fuel or other material is ignited and combined with oxygen, giving off light, heat, and flame.|The campfire kept us warm. 篝火让我们保持温暖。|近义词：blaze, flame, inferno|反义词：water, ice, extinguisher|noun

fire:/ˈfaɪər/ (US), /ˈfaɪə(r)/ (UK)| v. |点燃，开火，解雇|To ignite or cause to ignite.|He fired the wood in the fireplace. 他点燃了壁炉里的木头。|近义词：ignite, kindle, dismiss|反义词：extinguish, hire|verb

fire:/ˈfaɪər/ (US), /ˈfaɪə(r)/ (UK)| adj. |火红的，充满激情的|Relating to or resembling fire.|Her fire-red dress was stunning. 她火红色的裙子非常惊艳。|近义词：fiery, passionate, ardent|反义词：calm, cool, impassive|adjective
