hold-down:/ˈhoʊldˌdaʊn/| n.|压制；紧固装置；抑制；(尤指艰难时期后的)保有(工作);|A device or system for keeping something securely in place.  A job, especially one that is difficult to get or keep. |The engineer designed a hold-down to secure the satellite during launch. 工程师设计了一个紧固装置来固定发射过程中的卫星。|synonyms: clamp, fastener, restraint, anchor, secure, retain |antonyms: release, loosen, detach|noun
hold-down:|/ˈhoʊldˌdaʊn/| adj.|抑制的; 保有的;|Tending to suppress or restrain. Keeping a job, especially during difficult times. |The government implemented hold-down policies to control inflation. 政府实施了抑制政策来控制通货膨胀。  He was lucky to have a hold-down job during the recession. 他很幸运在经济衰退期间保住了一份工作。|synonyms: restraining, suppressive,  secure |antonyms: stimulating, promoting, releasing|adjective
