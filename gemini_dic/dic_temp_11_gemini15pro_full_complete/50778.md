descant:/ˈdɛskænt/ /dɪˈskænt/ | n. vi.|高音部；详述；评论|A descant is an additional melody sung above the principal melody.|The choir sang a beautiful descant during the hymn.|近义词：counterpoint, commentary, elaboration|反义词: silence, refrain |noun

descant:/ˈdɛskænt/ /dɪˈskænt/ | vi.|演唱高音部；详述；评论|To descant is to speak or write at length on a subject.|He descanted on the merits of organic farming.|近义词：elaborate, expound, discourse|反义词: summarize, abridge |verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *deschant*, from *deschanter* "to sing a descant, disenchant," from *des-* "dis-" + *chanter* "to sing," from Latin *cantare* "to sing."  The meaning shifted from "a part sung counter to the plainsong" to "discourse, comment" in the 16th century.
