incorrigiblenesses:/ɪnˈkɒrɪdʒɪblənɪsɪz/
|n.|无可救药，积习难改|The incorrigibleness of his behavior frustrated his parents.  他的无可救药的行为让他的父母感到沮丧。|
|incorrigibleness is the noun form of incorrigible, meaning "not able to be corrected or improved." It refers to a state of being beyond reform or correction.  incorrigibleness是incorrigible的名词形式，意思是“无法纠正或改进的”。它指的是一种无法改革或纠正的状态。|
|例句：His incorrigiblenesses were evident from a young age. 他从小就表现出无可救药的顽劣。|

|例句：The teacher despaired at the incorrigiblenesses of some of her students. 老师对一些学生的无可救药感到绝望。|
|近义词：incurability, hopelessness, depravity|
|反义词：correctability, reformability|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: *incorrigible + -ness.  Ultimately from Latin *in- ‘not’ + corrigibilis (from corrigere ‘to correct’).
源自中世纪晚期英语：*incorrigible + -ness。最终来自拉丁语 *in- “不” + corrigibilis（来自 corrigere “纠正”）。
