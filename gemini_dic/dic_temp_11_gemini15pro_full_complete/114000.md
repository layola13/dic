magistrate:/ˈmædʒɪstreɪt/| n.| 地方法官；治安官 |A civil officer or lay judge who administers the law, especially one who conducts a court dealing with minor offenses and holds preliminary hearings for more serious ones. |The magistrate presided over the traffic court. 地方法官主持了交通法庭。|Synonyms: judge, justice, official, arbiter|Antonyms: defendant, accused, plaintiff|noun

magistrate:/ˈmædʒɪstreɪt/| n.| 地方行政官 |A local official with administrative and sometimes judicial powers. |In ancient Rome, magistrates held considerable power. 在古罗马，地方行政官拥有相当大的权力。|Synonyms: administrator, governor, official, functionary|Antonyms: citizen, resident, commoner|noun
