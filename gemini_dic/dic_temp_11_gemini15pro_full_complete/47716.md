decadence:/ˈdekədəns/ /dɪˈkeɪdəns/| n. |颓废，衰落；堕落| moral or cultural decline as characterized by excessive indulgence in pleasure or luxury.  A state of decay or decline, especially in morals or art; unrestrained or excessive self-indulgence.|The Roman Empire collapsed after a long period of decadence. 罗马帝国在经历了长期的颓废之后崩溃了。

The decadence of modern society is a common theme in his novels.  现代社会的颓废是他小说中的一个常见主题。|近义词：decline, decay, degeneration, corruption, dissipation|反义词：growth, development, improvement, virtue, morality|词性:noun

decadence:/ˈdekədəns/ /dɪˈkeɪdəns/| adj. |颓废的 (rarely used as an adjective) |Characterized by decadence; declining, decaying.|  While not commonly used, one might describe a decadent lifestyle. 尽管不常用，但人们可能会描述一种颓废的生活方式。|近义词：declining, decaying, degenerate, corrupt, dissipated|反义词：growing, developing, improving, virtuous, moral|词性:adjective (rare)


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *décadence*, from Late Latin *decadentia*, from *decadēns*, present participle of *decadere* "to decay, fall down," from *de-* "down" + *cadere* "to fall."
