melanin: /ˈmɛlənɪn/ (US), /ˈmɛlənɪn/ (UK)| n. |黑色素|A dark brown to black pigment occurring in the hair, skin, and iris of the eye in people and animals. It is responsible for tanning of skin exposed to sunlight.|例句：Melanin protects the skin from the harmful effects of UV radiation. 黑色素保护皮肤免受紫外线辐射的 harmful effects.|近义词：pigment|反义词: None|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mélanine*, from Greek *μέλας* (mélas, “black, dark”).
