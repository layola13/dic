biting:/ˈbaɪtɪŋ/| adj.| 刺骨的，辛辣的，尖刻的|Causing a sharply painful or stinging sensation; critical or sarcastic.|The biting wind made my eyes water. 刺骨的寒风使我的眼睛直流泪。|Synonyms: sharp, stinging, piercing, harsh, acrid, caustic, sarcastic|Antonyms: mild, gentle, soothing, bland, complimentary|adjective

biting:/ˈbaɪtɪŋ/| n.| 咬，叮|The act of gripping or cutting something with the teeth.|The dog's biting caused a deep wound. 狗咬造成了很深的伤口。|Synonyms: gnawing, nipping, chewing|Antonyms: releasing, letting go|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bītende, present participle of bītan (“to bite”), from Proto-Germanic *bītaną (“to bite”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰeyd- (“to split, cleave, bite”).
