languages:/ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒɪz/ | n. | 语言 | The method of human communication, either spoken or written, consisting of the use of words in a structured and conventional way. |  Many languages are spoken in Africa. 非洲使用很多种语言。| synonyms: tongues, dialects | antonyms: silence, quiet | noun

languages:/ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒɪz/ | n. | (艺术等的)表达方式，表达能力 | A particular style of verbal or artistic expression. | The film is remarkable for its visual language. 这部电影的视觉语言非同凡响。| synonyms: style, expression | antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French langage (“language, speech, words, discourse”), from Vulgar Latin *linguaticum, from Latin lingua (“tongue; language, speech”), of uncertain origin.
