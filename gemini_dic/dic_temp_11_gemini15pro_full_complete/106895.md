laccolite: /ˈlækəˌlaɪt/ | n. | 岩盖,  岩床 | A sheet-like intrusion of igneous rock injected between layers of sedimentary rock, forcing the overlying strata upwards to form a dome-like shape. |  The geologists discovered a large laccolite during their field expedition.  地质学家们在野外考察中发现了一个巨大的岩盖。

The Henry Mountains are known for their prominent laccoliths. 亨利山脉以其突出的岩盖而闻名。| 近义词: sill, intrusion | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Greek *lákko- ‘cistern, pond’ + -lite ‘stone’
