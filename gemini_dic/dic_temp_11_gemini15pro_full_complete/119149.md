mewed:/mjuːd/ /mjud/
Verb
喵喵叫
(of a cat) make a characteristic high-pitched crying sound.
The kitten mewed pitifully, lost and hungry. 小猫可怜地喵喵叫着，又饿又迷路。

Verb
(of a gull) make a sound resembling a mew.
Gulls mewed overhead. 海鸥在头顶喵喵叫着。

Etymology:
From Middle English *meuen, from Old English mēawian (“to mew, meow”), from Proto-Germanic *mawōną (“to mew”), from Proto-Indo-European *meh₁w- (“to meow, bleat”). Cognate with West Frisian miauwe (“to mew”), Dutch miauwen (“to mew”), German miauen (“to mew”), Swedish mjaua (“to mew”).

Synonym:
miaow, meow, caterwaul (for prolonged mewing)

Antonym:
(No direct antonym for the sound a cat makes, but one could consider silence or other animal sounds like barking or chirping as contrasting sounds)
