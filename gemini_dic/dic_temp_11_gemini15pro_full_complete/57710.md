dray:/dreɪ/
noun|货车，运货马车|a low, strong cart without sides, used for carrying heavy loads|The farmer used a dray to haul the hay.|cart, wagon|truck, lorry
verb|用货车运|to transport by dray|They drayed the equipment to the construction site.|transport, haul|carry, lift

drays:/dreɪz/
noun|货车（复数）, 运货马车（复数）|plural of dray|The drays were lined up ready to be loaded.|carts, wagons|trucks, lorries

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dræge, from Proto-Germanic *dragiją (“act of drawing, something drawn”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰregʰ- (“to draw, pull”). Cognate with Dutch draagbaar (“handbarrow”), German Trage (“stretcher, litter”), Swedish dråg (“tow, pull”).
