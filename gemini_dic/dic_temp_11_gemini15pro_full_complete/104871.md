keening:/ˈkiːnɪŋ/ | n. | 哀号，恸哭 | A prolonged, high-pitched wail of grief, usually at a funeral. | Example: The keening of the mourners filled the air. 哀悼者的恸哭声响彻空中。 | Example:  The wind seemed to make a keening sound as it blew through the trees. 风吹过树林，似乎发出呜咽的声音。 | Synonyms: wailing, lamenting, mourning | Antonyms: rejoicing, celebrating | Noun

keening:/ˈkiːnɪŋ/ | adj. | 哀伤的，恸哭的; 尖锐的 | Expressing grief or sorrow in a prolonged, high-pitched wail; sharp, piercing. | Example: A keening cry echoed through the valley. 山谷里回荡着一声凄厉的哭喊。| Example: The keening wind cut through their thin coats. 刺骨的寒风穿透了他们单薄的外套。 | Synonyms: mournful, plaintive, sharp, piercing | Antonyms: joyful, happy, dull | Adjective
