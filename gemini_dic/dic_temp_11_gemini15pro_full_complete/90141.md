hotcockles:/ˈhɒtkɒkəlz/ (UK) /ˈhɑːtkɑːkəlz/ (US)| n.| 热屁股游戏；蒙眼猜人游戏|A children's game in which a blindfolded player guesses who has struck him or her.  A player is blindfolded and kneels down, placing their head in another player's lap.  The other players take turns hitting the blindfolded player, who has to guess who hit them. |We used to play hotcockles at parties when we were kids. 我们小时候在派对上常玩热屁股游戏。|The children shrieked with laughter during a boisterous game of hotcockles. 孩子们在喧闹的热屁股游戏中尖叫着大笑。|近义词: blind man's buff, buff|反义词: None|词性: noun

词源:
The origin of "hotcockles" is uncertain, but it's believed to be related to a similar game played in ancient Rome called "aluta".  The "hot" likely refers to the stinging sensation of being slapped, and "cockles" may refer to the cupped hands used to strike.
