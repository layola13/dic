latch-on:/ˈlætʃ ɒn/ (US), /ˈlætʃ ɒn/ (UK)| phrasal verb |理解；抓住（机会）；依赖；缠住|To understand something, often after a period of confusion; to seize an opportunity eagerly; to become attached or dependent on someone; to cling tightly to something or someone. |The baby latched on to the breast immediately. 婴儿立即吸住了乳房。|synonyms: grasp, cling to, understand, seize, attach|antonyms: let go, release, misunderstand, miss, detach|verb phrase

latch-on:/ˈlætʃ ɒn/ (US), /ˈlætʃ ɒn/ (UK)| n. |吸附；理解；抓住（机会）|The act of latching on, such as a baby to a breast or someone grasping an idea.|The baby's latch-on was strong. 婴儿的吸附力很强。|synonyms: grasp, grip, understanding, seizure, attachment|antonyms: release, detachment|noun
