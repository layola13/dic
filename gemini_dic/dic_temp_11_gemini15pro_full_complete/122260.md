mithras: /ˈmɪθrəs/ | n. | 密特拉 |  An Indo-Iranian deity of covenant, light, and oath.  Mithras is a prominent figure in Mithraism, a Roman mystery religion.  His worship spread throughout the Roman Empire. |  The cult of Mithras was popular among Roman soldiers.  密特拉教在罗马士兵中很流行。

The mysteries of Mithras were never fully understood. 密特拉的奥秘从未被完全理解。 | 近义词:  Sol Invictus (Unconquered Sun),  Helios | 反义词: None | 词性: Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Mithrās*, from Greek *Μίθρας (Míthras)*, from Old Persian *Miθra*, from Proto-Indo-Iranian *Mítra*, meaning "contract, covenant, treaty, oath, promise". Cognate with Sanskrit *Mitra* (friend, ally).
