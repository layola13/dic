The word "choic" doesn't exist in standard English.  It's likely a misspelling of "choice."  Therefore, the following analysis is for the word "choice."


choice: /tʃɔɪs/ | n. adj. | 选择，选择权；精选品；入选者；供选择的种类 |  An act of selecting or making a decision when faced with two or more possibilities. Can also refer to something that has been chosen or the range of things from which a choice can be made. |  She had to make a difficult choice between her career and her family. 她不得不在事业和家庭之间做出艰难的选择。| synonyms: option, alternative, selection, preference | antonyms: obligation, necessity | noun
choice: /tʃɔɪs/ | adj. |  上等的，精选的 | Of very good quality. |  We only use choice ingredients in our restaurant. 我们的餐厅只使用精选食材。| synonyms: superior, prime, select, excellent | antonyms: inferior, poor, substandard | adjective
