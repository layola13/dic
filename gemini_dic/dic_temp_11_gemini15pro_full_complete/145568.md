overcomplex:/ˌoʊvərˈkɒmplɛks/ /ˌoʊvərˈkɑːmplɛks/| adj.| 过于复杂的，过于繁复的|Excessively complex; more complex than necessary.|The system is overcomplex and difficult to use. 这个系统过于复杂，难以使用.|The rules are overcomplex and confusing. 这些规则过于复杂，令人困惑.|近义词：complicated, convoluted, intricate, elaborate|反义词：simple, straightforward, basic|词性:形容词

Etymology:
"over-" (prefix) + "complex" (adjective)

"over-" from Old English "ofer," meaning "above," "across," "beyond."  It signifies excess or going beyond a limit.

"complex" from Latin "complexus," past participle of "complecti," meaning "to embrace, comprise, surround." It refers to something made up of many interconnected parts.
