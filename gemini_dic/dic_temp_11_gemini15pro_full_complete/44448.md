cross-selling:/ˈkrɔːsˌsɛlɪŋ/
n.|交叉销售|The act of selling an additional product or service to an existing customer.|The bank teller suggested a credit card to the customer, a classic example of cross-selling.|近义词:add-on selling, suggestive selling|反义词:N/A|词性:名词

Etymology:
From "cross-" (meaning across or combined) + "selling" (the act of exchanging goods or services for money).  The term gained popularity in the late 20th century with the rise of relationship marketing.
