fallow:/ˈfæləʊ/ | adj. n. v. |休耕的；休闲的； fallow land 休耕地 |left uncultivated or unplanted; plowed but left unseeded during a growing season |The farmer left the field fallow this year. 今年农民让这块地休耕。|Synonyms: uncultivated, idle, dormant, inactive |Antonyms: cultivated, active, productive |词性:形容词/名词/动词

fallows:/ˈfæləʊz/ | n. |休耕地；休耕期 |plural of fallow, land that is plowed and harrowed but left unsown for a period in order to restore its fertility as part of a crop rotation or to avoid surplus production. |The farmer rotated his crops, leaving some fields as fallows each year. 农民轮作作物，每年都留下一些田地休耕。|Synonyms: fallow land, idle land |Antonyms: cultivated land |词性:名词

词源:
From Old English *fealg, from Proto-Germanic *falwaz ("pale, yellowish"), from Proto-Indo-European *pol- ("pale; to plough"). Cognate with Scots fallow ("pale yellow, fawn-coloured"), West Frisian fal ("pale, faded"), Dutch vaal ("pale, faded"), German fahl ("pale, fallow"), Danish fal ("pale yellow"), Swedish falkfärgad ("fallow"). Related also to Old English fealgian ("to fallow").
