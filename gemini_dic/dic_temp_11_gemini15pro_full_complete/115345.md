manoeuvre:/məˈnuːvər/ (UK) /məˈnuːvər/ (US)| n. v.|策略，调动，操控|A movement or series of moves requiring skill and care.  A carefully planned or cunning scheme or action.|The ship executed a series of complex manoeuvres to avoid the rocks. 这艘船进行了一系列复杂的操控以避开岩石。|synonyms: tactic, strategy, operation, scheme, ploy, maneuver (US)|antonyms: inaction, stillness, idleness|noun

manoeuvre:/məˈnuːvər/ (UK) /məˈnuːvər/ (US)| v.|操纵，调动，策划|Move skillfully or carefully.|I managed to manoeuvre the car into a tight parking space. 我设法把车停在一个狭小的停车位里。|synonyms: manipulate, guide, steer, navigate, maneuver (US)|antonyms: neglect, abandon, mismanage|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *manœuvrer*, from Italian *manovrare*, from *mano* "hand" and *opera* "work". Ultimately from Latin *manus* "hand" and *opera* "work".
