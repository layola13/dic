mosaism:/ˈmoʊzeɪˌɪzəm/| n. |镶嵌画，镶嵌工艺，嵌合体|A condition in which an individual has two or more genetically different sets of cells in their body.  This can result in patches of different colored skin or hair, or different blood types in different parts of the body. |The doctor diagnosed the patient with mosaicism after observing patches of differently pigmented skin.|近义词：chimerism|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider "homogeneity" in a genetic context)|词性:noun
