ephor: /ˈɛfɔːr/ (US), /ˈiːfɔːr/ (UK)| n. | 督政官；民政官 | A magistrate or supreme officer in ancient Sparta, having extensive powers of control over the kings and the community. |  The ephors held significant power in Spartan society.  斯巴达的督政官在社会中拥有重要的权力。

The ephors could even impeach the Spartan kings. 督政官甚至可以弹劾斯巴达国王。| 近义词: magistrate, overseer, official | 反义词: subordinate, subject | 词性: noun

ephor:  /ˈɛfɔːr/ (US), /ˈiːfɔːr/ (UK)| n. | (古希腊斯巴达)民政官，监察官|  An official in ancient Sparta who was elected annually and who exercised considerable power. |  The five ephors of Sparta acted as a check on the power of the kings. 斯巴达的五位民政官对国王的权力起到制衡作用。

The ephors were responsible for overseeing the education of Spartan youth. 民政官负责监督斯巴达青年的教育。 | 近义词: magistrate, official, inspector | 反义词: king, ruler | 词性: noun
