contradicters:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪktəz/ | n. |反驳者，驳斥者|People who contradict or deny the truth of something. |  Those contradicters refused to accept the overwhelming evidence. 那些反驳者拒绝接受压倒性的证据。| The contradicters questioned the validity of the research. 反驳者质疑研究的有效性。 | 近义词: opponents, dissenters, challengers | 反义词: supporters, proponents, advocates | 词性: Noun (plural)

Etymology:
From the verb "contradict," which comes from the Latin "contradicere," meaning "to speak against."  "Contra" means "against" and "dicere" means "to say."  The "-er" suffix turns the verb into a noun indicating a person who performs the action.
