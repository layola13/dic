nominal: /ˈnɒmɪnl/ (British) /ˈnɑːmɪnl/ (American)| adj. n.|名义上的，象征性的；名词性的；名词|Relating to or constituting a name or names; existing in name only, not real; (of a price or charge) very small.|The fee was nominal.|Synonyms: insignificant, trifling, token, titular, in name only|Antonyms: real, actual, substantial|adjective

nominal: /ˈnɒmɪnl/ (British) /ˈnɑːmɪnl/ (American)| n.|名词|A word or group of words that functions as a noun.|“Cat” is a nominal.|Synonyms: noun|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a grammatical term)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English nominal, from Old French nominal, from Latin nōminālis (“of or belonging to a name”), from nōmen (“name”).
