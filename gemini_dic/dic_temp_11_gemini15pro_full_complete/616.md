abraid:/əˈbreɪd/| vt.|磨损，擦伤；责骂，申斥|To rub or wear away by friction; to scold or criticize harshly.|The ropes were abraided by the constant friction.  绳子由于不断的摩擦而磨损。|近义词：chafe, graze, erode, scold, berate|反义词：soothe, mollify|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *abreden*, from Old French *abreer*, from Vulgar Latin *abradere*, from Latin *abradere* ("to scrape off"), from *ab* ("off, away from") + *radere* ("to scrape").
