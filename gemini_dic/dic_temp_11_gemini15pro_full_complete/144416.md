outsucked:/ˌaʊtˈsʌkt/ | v. | 被超越，被比下去（尤指在糟糕的方面） | To be surpassed or outdone by someone or something, especially in a negative or undesirable way.  This term emphasizes being worse than someone else in a particular aspect, often related to poor performance or a difficult situation. It's informal and somewhat vulgar. |  "The other team completely outsucked us in the second half - we couldn't even make a single basket."  另一队在下半场完全把我们比下去了——我们连一个球都投不进。| Synonym: outdone, surpassed, eclipsed (in a negative context) | Antonym: excelled, triumphed, outperformed | verb

outsucked:/ˌaʊtˈsʌkt/ | adj. | 被超越的，被比下去的（尤指在糟糕的方面）| Being surpassed or outdone by someone or something, especially in a negative or undesirable way. | "Their performance was truly outsucked; I've never seen anything so bad." 他们的表现真是太差劲了；我从未见过如此糟糕的。 | Synonym: outdone, surpassed, eclipsed (in a negative context) | Antonym: excellent, triumphant, superior | adjective
