cashback:/ˈkæʃbæk/| n.|现金返还，返现|A percentage of the purchase price returned to the buyer, typically by the retailer or a credit card company.|例句：I got 20% cashback on my new laptop. 我买新笔记本电脑获得了20%的返现。

例句：This credit card offers 1% cashback on all purchases. 这张信用卡所有购物都提供1%的返现。|近义词：rebate, refund|反义词: surcharge, extra charge|词性:noun


cashback:/ˈkæʃˌbæk/| adj.|提供返现的|Describing a service or product that offers cashback.|例句：This is a cashback credit card. 这是一张提供返现的信用卡。

例句：They are running a cashback offer on selected items. 他们对选定的商品提供返现优惠。|近义词：rebate-offering, refunding|反义词: N/A|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "cash" + "back". "Cash" meaning money in the form of coins or notes, from Middle French "casse" (money box), ultimately from Latin "capsa" (box). "Back" meaning returning to an original position, from Old English "bæc".
