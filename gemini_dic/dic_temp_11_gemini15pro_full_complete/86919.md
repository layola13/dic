hemoglobin:/ˈhiːməˌɡloʊbɪn/ (US), /ˈhiːməˌɡləʊbɪn/ (UK)| n.|血红蛋白|A red protein responsible for transporting oxygen in the blood of vertebrates.|A low hemoglobin level can lead to anemia. 低血红蛋白水平会导致贫血。|synonyms:  haemoglobin (chiefly British), red blood cell pigment|antonyms: None (Hemoglobin is a specific protein; it doesn't have a direct opposite.)|noun
