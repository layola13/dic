fovea:/ˈfoʊviə/ (US) /ˈfəʊviə/ (UK)| n.|中央凹，视网膜中央凹|The fovea is the central part of the retina responsible for sharp central vision.|The eagle uses its fovea to spot prey from high altitudes.  老鹰用它的中央凹从高空发现猎物。|近义词: macular lutea |反义词: (no direct antonym, perhaps peripheral retina) |词性:noun
Etymology: From Latin *fovea (“pit, small hollow”), from Proto-Italic *foweā, from Proto-Indo-European *gʷṓbʰeh₂ (“deep place, pit”). Cognate with Old Irish góbaimm (“I take away, carve”), Old English giefu (“gift”).
