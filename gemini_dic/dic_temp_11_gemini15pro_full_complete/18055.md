begrudges:/bɪˈɡrʌdʒɪz/
verb
吝惜；嫉妒；勉强给
envies the possession or enjoyment of something by another person.  Resents giving or allowing something.
He begrudges her every penny she earns. 他对她挣的每一分钱都耿耿于怀。
She begrudges him his success. 她嫉妒他的成功。
近义词:envy, resent, grudge
反义词:grant, allow, give freely
词性:verb

begrudged:/bɪˈɡrʌdʒd/
verb
吝惜；嫉妒；勉强给 (过去式)
envied the possession or enjoyment of something by another person. Resented giving or allowing something.
She begrudged the time she spent on housework. 她不情愿地把时间花在家务上。
He never begrudged his children anything. 他从不吝啬给孩子们任何东西。
近义词: envied, resented, grudged
反义词: granted, allowed, given freely
词性:verb

begrudging:/bɪˈɡrʌdʒɪŋ/
adjective
不情愿的；吝啬的
given or allowed reluctantly or resentfully.
He gave a begrudging apology. 他很不情愿地道歉。
She offered a begrudging smile. 她勉强挤出一丝笑容。
近义词:reluctant, unwilling, resentful
反义词:willing, generous, enthusiastic
词性:adjective

begrudgingly:/bɪˈɡrʌdʒɪŋli/
adverb
不情愿地；吝啬地
in a reluctant or resentful manner.
He begrudgingly agreed to help. 他很不情愿地同意帮忙。
She begrudgingly handed over the money. 她不情愿地交出了钱。
近义词:reluctantly, unwillingly, resentfully
反义词:willingly, generously, enthusiastically
词性:adverb


词源:
From Middle English *begrucchen, from Old French be- + grouchier (“to grumble”), from Frankish *krukjan (“to croak”), from Proto-Germanic *krōkaz (“hook, crook”).
