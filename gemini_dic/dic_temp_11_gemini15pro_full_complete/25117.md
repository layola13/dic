Buchenwald:/ˈbuːxənˌvɑːld/ | n. | 布痕瓦尔德集中营 |  Buchenwald was a Nazi concentration camp established near Weimar, Germany, in 1937.  It was one of the largest concentration camps within Germany's 1937 borders. Prisoners were used for forced labor and subjected to horrific conditions, including medical experimentation and extermination.  | Buchenwald was liberated by American forces in April 1945. 布痕瓦尔德于1945年4月被美军解放。| Synonym: concentration camp, death camp | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but concepts like freedom, liberation, sanctuary represent opposing ideas) | Noun
