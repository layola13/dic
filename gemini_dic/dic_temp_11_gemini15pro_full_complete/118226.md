mercaptal:/mərˈkæptəl/| n. |硫缩醛|A sulfur analog of an acetal, formed by the reaction of a thiol with an aldehyde or ketone.|The chemist synthesized a mercaptal by reacting ethanedithiol with acetone.|近义词:thioacetal|反义词:acetal|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *mercaptan* (containing the *merc-* root from Latin *mercurius* "mercury," due to mercury's affinity for sulfur compounds) + *-al* (suffix forming nouns denoting derivatives).
