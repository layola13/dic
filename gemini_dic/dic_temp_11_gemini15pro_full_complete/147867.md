overrationalizing: /ˌoʊvərˈræʃənəˌlaɪzɪŋ/
|v.|过度合理化|The act of creating excessive or illogical rationalizations for one's own or others' behavior, often to avoid facing uncomfortable truths or accepting responsibility.|He was overrationalizing his failure, blaming everyone but himself. 他在过度合理化自己的失败，责怪所有人，除了他自己。|
|近义词: overthinking, justifying, excusing, rationalizing|
|反义词: accepting, confronting, acknowledging|
|词性: 动名词 (gerund) or present participle|
|词源:  "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "rationalizing" (from "rationalize," from Latin "rationalis" meaning "reasoning")|
