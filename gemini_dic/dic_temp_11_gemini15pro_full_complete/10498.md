appoint:/əˈpɔɪnt/| vt. |任命；指定；约定|to choose someone officially for a job or responsibility; to arrange or decide on a time or place for something to happen|The president will appoint a new cabinet member soon. 总统很快会任命一位新的内阁成员。

The committee appointed a time for the next meeting. 委员会指定了下次会议的时间。|近义词：nominate, designate, assign, choose, select|反义词:dismiss, fire, discharge|词性:动词

appoint:/əˈpɔɪnt/| n. |任命；约定|An act of appointing; an appointment.|His appoint to the board was met with approval. 他被任命为董事会成员受到了赞同。（注意：appoint作为名词使用较少见，appointment更常用）|近义词：appointment, nomination, designation|反义词:dismissal, discharge|词性:名词
