interconfound: /ˌɪntərˌkənˈfaʊnd/
verb
相互混淆，混为一谈
to confuse two or more elements or factors in a way that makes it difficult to distinguish their separate effects.
Researchers must be careful not to interconfound the effects of different variables in their experiments. 研究人员必须小心，不要在实验中混淆不同变量的影响。
The study interconfounds age and experience, making it difficult to isolate the impact of each factor. 这项研究混淆了年龄和经验，使得难以区分每个因素的影响。
近义词:confuse, conflate, mix up, muddle
反义词:distinguish, differentiate, separate
词性:verb

词源:
"inter-" (Latin, meaning "between, among") + "confound" (from Old French "confondre," ultimately from Latin "confundere," meaning "to pour together, mix up").
