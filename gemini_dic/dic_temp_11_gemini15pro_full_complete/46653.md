damningnesses:/ˈdæmɪŋnɪsɪz/
noun
定罪性；非难性；该死程度
The quality or state of being damning; the degree to which something is damning.
The damningnesses of the evidence presented in court led to a swift conviction.  法庭上提出的证据的非难性导致了迅速定罪。
The various damningnesses of the accusations against him made it hard to defend him.  针对他的各种指控的该死程度使他难以为自己辩护。
Synonyms: conclusiveness, incriminating nature
Antonyms: exculpatory nature, exonerating quality
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From damning + -ness.  "Damning" originates from the verb "damn," which comes from the Old French "damner," ultimately derived from the Latin "damnare" (to condemn, inflict loss upon).  The suffix "-ness" is used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state, quality, or condition.
