gelatinate:/dʒəˈlætɪneɪt/ | v. | 凝胶化，使…成胶状 | To convert into gelatin or a substance like gelatin; to become gelatinous. | The mixture will gelatinate as it cools.  这种混合物冷却后会凝胶化。 | The protein began to gelatinate, forming a thick gel. 蛋白质开始凝胶化，形成浓稠的凝胶。 |  近义词: congeal, solidify, thicken | 反义词: liquefy, melt, dissolve | 词性: 动词

gelatinate:/dʒəˈlætɪneɪt/ | n. | 胶状物 | A gelatinous substance. |  The gelatinate formed a protective layer over the wound. 胶状物在伤口上形成了一层保护膜。 | The scientist analyzed the gelatinate found at the scene. 科学家分析了在现场发现的胶状物。 | 近义词: gel, jelly | 反义词: liquid, solution | 词性: 名词

gelatinous:/dʒəˈlætɪnəs/ | adj. | 胶状的，像胶的 | Having the nature of or resembling gelatin; jellylike. | The soup had a gelatinous consistency. 汤汁呈胶状。 |  The jellyfish's body is gelatinous and transparent. 水母的身体呈胶状且透明。 | 近义词: jelly-like, viscous, gummy | 反义词: watery, runny, fluid | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Latin *gelatinatus*, past participle of *gelatinare* "to freeze, congeal," from *gelatina* "jelly, gelatin" (see gelatin).
