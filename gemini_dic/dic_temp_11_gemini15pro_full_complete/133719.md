nonfrugalness:/ˌnɒnˈfruːɡəlnəs/ | n. | 不节俭，奢侈 | Lack of frugality; extravagance.  The state of being characterized by excessive spending or consumption. | His nonfrugalness led him into debt. 他的不节俭让他负债累累。|  Example: Her nonfrugalness was evident in her designer wardrobe. 她的不节俭体现在她满是名牌的衣橱里。 |Synonyms: extravagance, lavishness, prodigality, spendthriftiness | Antonyms: frugality, thrift, economy, parsimony | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + frugal (thrifty) + -ness (state of being).  "Frugal" comes from the Latin "frugalis," meaning "economical," related to "frux," meaning "fruit" (in the sense of profit or benefit).
