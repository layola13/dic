dissimulation:/dɪˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 掩饰，假装 | The act of concealing one's true feelings or intentions.  | He practiced dissimulation to hide his guilt. 他假装来掩盖他的罪行。 |  She maintained a calm demeanor, a masterful act of dissimulation. 她保持着平静的风度，这是一种巧妙的掩饰。 | 近义词：deception, pretense, feigning | 反义词：honesty, candor, openness | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *dissimulātiōn- (stem of dissimulātiō), from dissimulāt(us), past participle of dissimulāre ‘disguise, conceal,’ from dis- ‘not’ + simulāre ‘to copy, represent, feign.’
