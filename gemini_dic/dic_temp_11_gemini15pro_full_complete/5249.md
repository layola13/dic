allochroous:/əˈlɒkrəs/ | adj. | 異色的，變色的 | Having a different color; exhibiting allochroism.  Allochroism is the property of exhibiting different colors by transmitted light than by reflected light.  It's often due to impurities or defects within the crystal structure of a mineral. | The alexandrite gemstone is famously allochroous, appearing green in daylight and red under incandescent light.  | Synonyms: iridescent, opalescent | Antonyms: monochromatic, homochromatic | adjective
