misbalances:/ˌmɪsˈbælənsɪz/
noun
不平衡，失衡
lack of balance or symmetry; disproportion.
The current misbalances in the global economy are a cause for concern.
当前全球经济的不平衡令人担忧。

misbalances:/ˌmɪsˈbælənsɪz/
verb
使不平衡，使失衡
to cause to be out of balance; to make disproportionate.
The sudden influx of refugees misbalanced the demographics of the small town.
难民的突然涌入打破了小镇的人口结构平衡。


Etymology:
mis- (prefix):  Old English, from Germanic *missa-, meaning "wrong," "bad," or "amiss."  Related to Old High German missi "wrongly," Gothic missa-deds "misdeed."
balance (noun/verb):  From Old French balance (noun), balancer (verb), from Late Latin bilanx, from Latin bis "double" + lanx "plate, scale, pan of a balance."

Synonym:
imbalance, disparity, inequality, disproportion, asymmetry

Antonym:
balance, equilibrium, symmetry, parity, proportion
