ethnic: /ˈeθnɪk/ (US) /ˈɛθnɪk/ (UK)| adj. |种族的，民族的；具有民族特色的|Relating to a population subgroup (within a larger or dominant national or cultural group) with a common national or cultural tradition. |例句：Ethnic tensions have been rising in the region.  该地区的种族紧张局势一直在加剧。|近义词：racial, cultural, national|反义词: none |词性: adjective

词源:
From French *ethnique*, from Late Latin ethnicus “heathen, pagan, Gentile,” from Greek ethnikos “of the nations, Gentile, heathen,” from ethnos “nation, people, tribe; a multitude, swarm, class, caste, kind, species.”  The Latin word was used in the Vulgate (translation of the Bible) to translate Greek ethnos, Hebrew goyim, referring to non-Jews, non-Christians.
