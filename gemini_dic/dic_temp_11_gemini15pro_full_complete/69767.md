ferly:/ˈfɜːrli/| n. adj. adv.|奇迹，奇观；奇妙的；奇异地|Something unusual, marvelous, or wondrous.  A marvel; a wonder.  Strange; wonderful.  In a wonderful or marvelous manner.|例句：The aurora borealis is a true ferly. 北极光是真正的奇观。|近义词：wonder, marvel, miracle, prodigy|反义词：ordinariness, commonness|词性:名词,形容词,副词
Etymology: From Middle English *ferli, *ferly, from Old English *fearlīc (“sudden, unexpected, dangerous, terrible”), from Proto-Germanic *fairlīkaz (“sudden”), from Proto-Indo-European *per- (“to try, risk”). More at perilous.  The sense development appears to have been from “unexpected” to “remarkable,” “wonderful.”  Related to fear.
