keeping's:/ˈkiːpɪŋz/ |n.|保管；保持；饲养（动物）；遵守；要塞|Something that is kept; the cost of maintaining something.  The action of continuing to have something; the fact of something continuing to exist. |The keeping's of the castle were expensive. 城堡的维护费用很高。|近义词：maintenance, upkeep, preservation, support, observance |反义词：neglect, abandonment|词性:名词

词源: From Middle English *keping(e)*, from Old English *cēping*, meaning "market, purchase, act of buying." The meaning shifted to "the act or state of keeping" in Middle English.  The possessive form *keeping's* is a relatively modern development, indicating "something belonging to or associated with the act of keeping."
