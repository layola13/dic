ealderman:/ˈɔːldərmən/| n.|市议员，市政官|A member of a municipal council or corporation, especially in England, historically having various administrative and judicial functions.|The ealderman proposed a new park for the town.  市政官提议为镇上建一个新公园。|Example:  The ealderman was known for his honesty and integrity.  这位市政官以诚实正直闻名。|Synonym: councilor, councilman|Antonym: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ealdormann* "chief, leader, head of a shire," composed of *eald* "old" (related to "elder") + *mann* "man."  The term originally referred to a high-ranking nobleman or official, and its meaning evolved over time to refer to a member of a municipal council.
