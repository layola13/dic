musket:/ˈmʌskɪt/| n.|滑膛枪|A smoothbore long gun fired from the shoulder.|The soldiers were armed with muskets.|Synonyms: firearm, gun, rifle|Antonyms: peace (abstract), disarmament (abstract)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *mousquet*, diminutive of *mousquette* " sparrowhawk," from Old Italian *moschetto*, diminutive of *mosca* "fly."  The name originally referred to a type of crossbow bolt, then a handgun, and finally the long gun we know today.
