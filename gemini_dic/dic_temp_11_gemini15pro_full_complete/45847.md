cut a figure:/kʌt ə ˈfɪɡjər/
|idiomatic phrase|
|炫耀；引人注目；给人留下深刻印象|
|To make a striking or remarkable impression by appearance or behavior.|
|He cut a dashing figure in his new suit. 他穿着新西装，显得十分潇洒。|
|make an impression, stand out, attract attention, be noticeable|
|go unnoticed, blend in|
|phrase|

cut a fine figure:/kʌt ə faɪn ˈfɪɡjər/
|idiomatic phrase|
|给人留下好印象；显得出色|
|To make a particularly impressive or admirable appearance.|
|She cut a fine figure on the dance floor. 她在舞池中显得格外优美。|
|make a good impression, look good, impress, shine|
|look bad, make a poor impression|
|phrase|

cut a poor/sorry figure:/kʌt ə pʊr/ˈsɒri ˈfɪɡjər/
|idiomatic phrase|
|给人留下不好的印象；显得糟糕|
|To make an unimpressive or embarrassing appearance.|
|He cut a sorry figure after losing the race. 输掉比赛后，他显得十分狼狈。|
|make a bad impression, look bad, embarrass oneself, fail to impress|
|look good, make a good impression|
|phrase|
