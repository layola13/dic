anaesthetize:/əˈniːsθətaɪz/ (UK) /əˈnɛsθəˌtaɪz/ (US)| vt.|麻醉|To administer an anesthetic to (a person or animal), especially so as to induce a loss of consciousness.|The vet anaesthetized the cat before the operation. 兽医在手术前麻醉了猫。|Synonyms: numb, drug, sedate|Antonyms: stimulate, awaken|Verb

anaesthesia:/ˌænɪsˈθiːʒə/ (UK) /ˌænɪsˈθiːʒə/ (US)| n.|麻醉|Loss of feeling or sensation, especially resulting from the administration of a drug.|The patient was given general anaesthesia for the surgery. 病人接受了全身麻醉进行手术。|Synonyms: numbness, insensibility|Antonyms: sensation, feeling|Noun

anesthetic:/ˌænɪsˈθɛtɪk/ (UK) /ˌænɪsˈθɛtɪk/ (US)| n. adj.|麻醉剂；麻醉的|A substance that produces insensitivity to pain.|The dentist injected a local anesthetic before filling the cavity. 牙医在补牙前注射了局部麻醉剂。|Synonyms: narcotic, opiate|Antonyms: stimulant, analgesic (Note: analgesic relieves pain without causing numbness)|Noun/Adjective
