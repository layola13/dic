Belleau Wood:/ˈbɛloʊ wʊd/| n.|贝洛森林|A forest in France, the site of a major battle in World War I.|The Marines fought bravely in Belleau Wood.|Synonyms: battlefield, woods|Antonyms: peaceful place|noun

Etymology: Named after the nearby village of Belleau, the name of which is derived from the Old French word "bell" meaning "beautiful" and "eau" meaning "water."
