noncausatively:/ˌnɒnˈkɔːzətɪvli/ | adv. | 非因动地，非使役地 |  In a way that does not involve causing something to happen.  It describes an action that happens on its own, without an external force or agent making it happen. | The door opened noncausatively.  门自动开了。| The balloon deflated noncausatively. 气球自行瘪了。 | 近义词：automatically, spontaneously, naturally | 反义词: causatively, deliberately, intentionally | 词性: adverb

词源:  Formed from "non-" (not) + "causative" (causing something) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).  "Causative" ultimately comes from the Latin "causa" (cause).
