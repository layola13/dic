cantingly:/ˈkæntɪŋli/ | adv. | 伪善地，虚伪地 | In a hypocritical or insincere manner, often with a suggestion of affected piety or religious zeal. | He spoke cantingly about the importance of charity, while hoarding his own wealth. 他虚伪地谈论慈善的重要性，却囤积着自己的财富。 |  She cantingly praised the virtues of humility while boasting about her own accomplishments. 她虚伪地赞扬谦逊的美德，同时吹嘘自己的成就。 | 近义词: hypocritically, sanctimoniously, pietistically | 反义词: sincerely, genuinely, honestly | 词性: 副词

词源:
From "cant" (n.), meaning hypocritical and sanctimonious talk, typically of a moral, religious, or political nature, + "-ing" (forming a present participle, often used adjectivally) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).  The noun "cant" likely originates from the Latin "cantus" (song, chant), referring to the singsong tone often adopted by preachers or those speaking insincerely.
