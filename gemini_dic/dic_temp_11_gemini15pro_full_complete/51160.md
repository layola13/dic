despotical:/dɪˈspɒtɪkəl/ /dɪˈspɑːtɪkəl/| adj.|专制的，暴虐的|Of or relating to a despot; tyrannical. Characterized by absolute power and arbitrary or cruel exercise of power.|The despotical ruler showed no mercy to his subjects.  这个专制的统治者对他的臣民毫不留情。|The king's despotical reign ended with a revolution. 国王的暴虐统治以一场革命告终。|近义词：tyrannical, authoritarian, dictatorial, autocratic|反义词：democratic, liberal, egalitarian|词性：形容词

despot:/ˈdespɒt/ /ˈdespɑːt/| n.|暴君，专制君主|A ruler or other person who holds absolute power, typically one who exercises it in a cruel or oppressive way.|The despot ruled the country with an iron fist.  这个暴君用铁腕统治着国家。|History is full of despots who abused their power. 历史上充满了滥用权力的暴君。|近义词：tyrant, dictator, autocrat|反义词：democrat|词性：名词

despotism:/ˈdespətɪzəm/| n.|专制统治，暴政|The exercise of absolute power, especially in a cruel and oppressive way.|The people lived in fear under the king's despotism.  人民生活在国王的专制统治的恐惧之下。|The country was finally free from the despotism of the dictator. 这个国家终于摆脱了独裁者的专制统治。|近义词：tyranny, dictatorship, autocracy|反义词：democracy, freedom, liberty|词性：名词


词源:
From French *despotique*, from Late Latin *despoticus*, from Greek *despotikos*, from *despotes* "master, lord," from *des- "house" + *pot-* "capable of, powerful."
