alkalies: /ˈælkəlaɪz/ | n. | 碱; 碱金属 |  A substance that has a pH of more than 7 and that forms a salt when it reacts with an acid.  A strong base, especially one that dissolves in water to form hydroxide ions. Refers to the alkali metals (lithium, sodium, potassium, rubidium, cesium, and francium) or their hydroxides. |  Alkalies are commonly used in cleaning products. 碱通常用于清洁产品中。| Synonyms: bases, hydroxides | Antonyms: acids | noun

alkali: /ˈælkəlaɪ/ | n. | 碱; 碱金属 | Any of the soluble hydroxides of the alkali metals, such as sodium hydroxide or potassium hydroxide.  A substance that has a pH of more than 7 and that forms a salt when it reacts with an acid.  Can also refer to an alkali metal (lithium, sodium, potassium, rubidium, cesium, and francium). |  Sodium hydroxide is a strong alkali. 氢氧化钠是一种强碱。| Synonyms: base, hydroxide | Antonyms: acid | noun
