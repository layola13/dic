accretion:/əˈkriːʃən/ | n. | 增加，积累，积聚; 添加生长; 冲积层 | the process of growth or increase, typically by the gradual accumulation of additional layers or matter. | The accretion of sediments in the riverbed caused the water level to rise. 河床沉积物的积聚导致水位上升。 |  The fund was increased by the accretion of new capital. 这笔资金因新增资本而增加。 | synonyms: accumulation, growth, increase, buildup, expansion | antonyms: decrease, reduction, diminution, erosion, loss | noun

accretion:/əˈkriːʃən/ | n. |  (天体)吸积 | the coming together and cohesion of matter under the influence of gravitation to form larger bodies. |  Planetary accretion is the process by which planets form from dust and gas. 行星吸积是行星由尘埃和气体形成的过程。 | The accretion disk around a black hole is incredibly hot. 黑洞周围的吸积盘非常热。 | synonyms: accumulation, agglomeration, aggregation | antonyms: dispersion, dissipation | noun
