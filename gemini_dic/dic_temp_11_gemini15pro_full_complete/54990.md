disprovers:/dɪsˈpruːvərz/
|n.|反驳者，证明…有误的人|People who disprove a theory or statement are disprovers.|The scientist's experiment produced results that attracted many disprovers.|近义词:refuters, challengers, contradictors|反义词:supporters, advocates|词性:名词
Etymology: dis- + prover.  "Dis-" is a prefix of Latin origin meaning "apart," "asunder," "away," expressing negation, reversal, or undoing. "Prover" comes from the verb "prove," ultimately from Latin "probare" (to test, approve). Thus, a "disprover" is someone who negates or undoes a proof.
