nontransitiveness:/ˌnɒntrænˈzɪtɪvnəs/ /ˌnɑːntrænˈzɪtɪvnəs/| n. |非及物性；非传递性|The property of a relation that is not transitive.  A relation is nontransitive if it's not always the case that if a relates to b, and b relates to c, then a relates to c.|The nontransitiveness of the "is a friend of" relationship is evident: if John is a friend of Mary, and Mary is a friend of Peter, it doesn't necessarily follow that John is a friend of Peter.|近义词:intransitivity|反义词:transitivity|词性:noun
