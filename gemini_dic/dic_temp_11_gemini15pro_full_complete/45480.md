curbstone:/ˈkɜːrstəʊn/ (UK) /ˈkɜːrbstoʊn/ (US)| n. |路缘石，镶边石|A stone or concrete block forming the edge of a pavement next to a roadway.|The car scraped against the curbstone. 汽车擦到了路缘石。|Synonyms: kerbstone (UK), curb, edging|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, possibly roadway, street)|noun

curb:/kɜːrb/ (UK) /kɜːrb/ (US)| n. vt. |路缘，(马的)衔铁；控制，抑制|A stone or concrete border along a street or path; a check or restraint on something.|1.  He parked his car close to the curb. 他把车停在了靠近路缘的地方。 2.  We need to curb our spending. 我们需要控制开支。|Synonyms: restrain, control, check, limit|Antonyms: unleash, release, free, encourage|noun, verb
