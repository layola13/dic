deep-seated:/ˌdiːpˈsiːtɪd/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；深层的 | Firmly established at a deep or profound level. | Example 1:  He has a deep-seated fear of spiders. 他对蜘蛛有一种根深蒂固的恐惧。

Example 2: The problem is deep-seated and will require a long-term solution. 这个问题根深蒂固，需要长期解决方案。 | Synonyms: ingrained, entrenched, deep-rooted, inherent, intrinsic | Antonyms: superficial, shallow, surface | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:  From "deep" + "seated," meaning firmly fixed or established. "Deep" traces back to Old English "dēop," while "seated" comes from the past participle of "seat," from Old French "seoir," ultimately from Latin "sedēre" (to sit).
