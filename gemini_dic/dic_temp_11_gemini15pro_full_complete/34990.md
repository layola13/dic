cloister:/ˈklɔɪstər/ (US), /ˈklɔɪstə/ (UK)| n. vt.|修道院, 回廊;隔离,隐居|A covered walkway, especially in a religious institution, forming a quadrangle or garth.  To seclude or confine, as in a monastery.|The monks walked slowly around the cloister.  僧侣们绕着回廊慢慢地走。 He cloistered himself in his study for months to write his novel.  他把自己关在书房里几个月，就为了写他的小说。|近义词:monastery, abbey, convent, seclusion, retreat|反义词:world, outside, public|词性:noun, verb

词源: From Old French *cloistre*, from Latin *claustrum* "lock, bolt, bar, barrier," from *claudere* "to close."
