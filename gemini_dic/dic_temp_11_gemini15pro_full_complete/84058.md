half-frowning:/ˌhæf ˈfraʊnɪŋ/
adj.
半皱眉的，略带不悦的
A facial expression showing slight displeasure or disapproval, characterized by a partial frown.
She gave him a half-frowning look when he suggested they go hiking. 她在他建议去远足时给了他一个半皱眉的表情。
He listened with a half-frowning expression. 他带着略带不悦的表情听着。
Near Synonyms: displeased, disapproving, skeptical, dubious
Near Antonyms: smiling, beaming, joyful, approving
Word Origin:  A compound adjective formed from "half" and "frowning," both of Old English origin. "Half" traces back to "healf," and "frowning" comes from "fruncian," meaning "to wrinkle."
