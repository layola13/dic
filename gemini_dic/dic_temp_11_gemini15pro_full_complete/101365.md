ionize:/ˈaɪənaɪz/ | v. | 电离，离子化 | To convert (an atom, molecule, or substance) into an ion or ions, typically by removing one or more electrons. |  The intense heat from the sun ionizes the upper atmosphere. 太阳的强烈热量使高层大气电离。

The radiation ionized the gas, creating a plasma. 辐射使气体电离，形成了等离子体。 | 近义词: charge, electrify | 反义词: deionize, neutralize | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *ioniser*, from ion + -ize. Ultimately from Ancient Greek ἰόν (ion, “going”), neuter present participle of εἶμι (eimi, “I go”).
