haul-up:/ˈhɔːl ʌp/| n. & v.|停顿，检查；拖拽；逮捕|A haul-up is a stop or check, often by authorities. As a verb, it means to pull or drag something, often a net or a vehicle, or to arrest someone. |The police hauled up the suspect for questioning. 警察逮捕了嫌疑人进行审问。|Synonyms: arrest, detain, pull up, drag, heave|Antonyms: release, let go, push, lower|noun & verb

haul-up:/ˈhɔːl ʌp/| n. & v.|拖上岸；拉起来|The act of hauling something up, such as a boat onto land or a net from the water. |They hauled up the fishing nets full of salmon. 他们把装满鲑鱼的渔网拉了上来。|Synonyms: pull up, hoist, raise, lift|Antonyms: lower, drop, sink|noun & verb
