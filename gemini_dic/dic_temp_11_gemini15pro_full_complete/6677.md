anaka: /ɑːˈnɑːkə/ | n. |  elder brother (in some Polynesian languages) | A respectful term for an older male relative, especially a brother, in some Polynesian languages, particularly Hawaiian.  | Example: He greeted his anaka with a warm embrace. 他热情地拥抱了他的哥哥(anaka)。 | Example: The anaka offered wise counsel to his younger siblings.  这位哥哥(anaka)向他的弟弟妹妹提供了明智的建议。 | Synonym: older brother, senior male relative | Antonym: younger brother | Part of Speech: Noun
