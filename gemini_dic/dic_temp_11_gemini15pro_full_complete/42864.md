courtesy:/ˈkɜːrtəsi/ (US), /ˈkɜːtɪsi/ (UK)| n.| 礼貌；好意；殷勤；客气话|Politeness, respect, and consideration for others; a polite gesture or remark.|She treated everyone with courtesy and respect. 她对每个人都以礼貌和尊重相待。|synonyms: politeness, civility, good manners, respect, kindness, consideration, graciousness|antonyms: rudeness, impoliteness, disrespect, discourtesy|noun

courtesy:/ˈkɜːrtəsi/ (US), /ˈkɜːtɪsi/ (UK)| adj. (only before noun)| 礼貌的；客气的 (仅用于名词前)|Given or done as a polite gesture; free of charge.| We were given courtesy tickets to the concert. 我们得到了音乐会的赠票。|synonyms: complimentary, free, gratis|antonyms: paid, purchased|adjective


Etymology: From Old French "curteisie," meaning "courtesy, courtliness," derived from "curteis," meaning "courteous," ultimately from Latin "cohors," meaning "enclosure, retinue, cohort."
