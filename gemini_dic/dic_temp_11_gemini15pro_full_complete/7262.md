anglophiles:/ˈæŋɡləfaɪlz/ | n. | 亲英派；英国文化爱好者 | A person who greatly admires or favors England, its people, and its customs. |  Many anglophiles enjoy drinking tea and watching British television shows. 许多亲英派喜欢喝茶和看英国电视节目。 | 近义词：Britophile | 反义词：Anglophobe | 词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Anglo- (combining form meaning "English") + -phile (suffix meaning "lover of"). "Anglo-" ultimately derives from the Angles, one of the Germanic peoples who settled in Britain. "-phile" comes from the Greek word "philos," meaning "loving" or "dear."
