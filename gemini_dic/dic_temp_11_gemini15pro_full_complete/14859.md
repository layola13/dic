axon:/ˈæksɒn/ (UK), /ˈæksɑːn/ (US)| n. |轴突|A long, slender projection of a nerve cell, or neuron, that typically conducts electrical impulses away from the neuron's cell body.|The axon transmits signals to other neurons, muscles, or glands.|例句：The axon is a vital part of the neuron. 轴突是神经元的重要组成部分。

axones:/ˈæksɒnz/ (plural)| n. |轴突（复数）|The plural form of axon, referring to multiple long, slender projections of nerve cells that conduct electrical impulses.|Axones are essential for communication within the nervous system.|例句：The axones transmit signals across long distances. 轴突长距离传输信号。
