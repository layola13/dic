condemners:/kənˈdɛmərz/
|n.|谴责者；责难者|People who condemn someone or something; those who express strong disapproval or pronounce judgment against something or someone.|The condemners of the new policy were very vocal.  新政策的谴责者声音很大。|Synonyms: critics, detractors, censurers|Antonyms: supporters, advocates, defenders|noun


condemn:/kənˈdɛm/
|v.|谴责；责备；宣告…不适用|To express strong disapproval of; to pronounce judgment against; to declare unfit for use.|The judge condemned the thief to prison. 法官判决小偷入狱。|Synonyms: denounce, criticize, censure|Antonyms: praise, commend, approve|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French condemner, from Latin condemnare  "to condemn, sentence, doom, blame, disapprove," from com- "together" (see com-) + damnare "to inflict loss upon, condemn, damage," from damnum "loss, hurt, damage" (see damage (n.)).
