frowns:/fraʊnz/
| n. | 皱眉；不悦的表情 | An expression of displeasure or disapproval characterized by a furrowing of one's brows. | She greeted the news with a frown. 她皱着眉头回应了这个消息。| synonyms: scowl, grimace | antonyms: smile, grin | noun

frowns:/fraʊnz/
| v. | 皱眉；表示不悦 | To contract one's brows, typically as an expression of displeasure or concentration. | He frowned at the complicated instructions. 他对着复杂的说明皱起了眉头。| synonyms: scowl, glare | antonyms: smile, beam | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *frounen*, from Old French *frogner* (to wrinkle), of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Frankish *frūgnōn* (to wrinkle the forehead).
