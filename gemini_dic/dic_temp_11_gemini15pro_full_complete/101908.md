isinglass:/ˈaɪzɪŋɡlæs/ /ˈaɪzɪŋɡlɑːs/
n.
鱼胶，明胶
a translucent, whitish, odorless, and tasteless gelatin obtained from the air bladders of certain fish, especially the sturgeon, and used as a clarifying agent for food and beverages, as an adhesive, and in making isinglass.
The winemaker used isinglass to clarify the wine.  酿酒师用鱼胶来澄清葡萄酒。

近义词:gelatin, agar, collagen
反义词:(No direct antonym, could consider substances that cloud liquids)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *huysenblas (attested as huysenblaes), literally "sturgeon's bladder," from huis "sturgeon" + blas "bladder."  This was later reinterpreted in English as "isinglass," probably influenced by ice and glass due to its appearance.
