jollier:/ˈdʒɒliər/ (US) /ˈdʒɒlɪər/ (UK)| adj.|更快乐的，更愉快的|Comparative form of jolly: more jolly.  Used to describe someone or something that is more cheerful, merry, or festive than another.|He seemed jollier after the good news. 他听到好消息后似乎更快乐了。|Synonyms: merrier, cheerier, more joyful, more festive|Antonyms: gloomier, sadder, more depressed|adjective

jolly:/ˈdʒɒli/ (US) /ˈdʒɒli/ (UK)| adj.  n. adv.|快乐的，愉快的； (n.) 英国水兵对海军陆战队队员的称呼; (adv.) 非常|Happy, cheerful, and friendly; (n.) A British sailor's familiar term of address to a Royal Marine; (adv.) Very|He's a jolly good fellow. 他是个非常不错的人。|Synonyms: cheerful, merry, joyful, mirthful|Antonyms: sad, gloomy, miserable, depressed|adjective, noun, adverb
