apsis: /ˈæpsɪs/ (US), /ˈæpsɪs/ (UK)| n. |远地点；近地点|The point in an orbit, of a planet or comet around the sun or of a satellite around a planet, at which it is closest to or furthest from the body that it orbits.|The comet reached its aphelion, the apse farthest from the Sun.|近义词: apocenter (when referring to the furthest point), pericenter (when referring to the closest point)|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *apsis*, from Ancient Greek ἀψίς (*apsís*) meaning "arch, loop, vault".  Ultimately related to ἅπτειν (*háptein*) meaning "to fasten, join".
