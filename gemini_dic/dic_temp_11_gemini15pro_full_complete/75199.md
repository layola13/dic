frocked:/frɒkt/ /frɑːkt/| adj.|穿着法衣的，穿…衣服的|Dressed in a frock (a long, loose garment, especially a woman's or girl's dress, or a clerical robe).|The priest, frocked in white, began the ceremony.  穿着白色法衣的牧师开始了仪式。|The little girl, frocked in a pink dress, skipped happily through the park. 穿着粉色连衣裙的小女孩在公园里快乐地跳来跳去。|近义词：robed, gowned, clad|反义词: undressed, unclothed|词性:形容词

词源:
From the Old English *frocc, meaning "frock, garment," which is related to the Old High German froccho "monk's cowl."  The original meaning referred to a monk's habit, hence the association with clerical robes.  Over time, the term broadened to include various types of long, loose garments.
