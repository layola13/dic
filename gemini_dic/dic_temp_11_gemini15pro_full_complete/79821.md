glycosaminoglycan:/ˌɡlaɪkəʊsəˌmiːnəˈɡlaɪkæn/ /ˌɡlaɪkoʊsəˌmiːnoʊˈɡlaɪkæn/| n.| 糖胺聚糖 |A long unbranched polysaccharide consisting of a repeating disaccharide unit. One of the two sugars is always an amino sugar (N-acetylglucosamine or N-acetylgalactosamine) and the other is usually a uronic acid. Glycosaminoglycans are major components of connective tissue and are also found as components of some glycoproteins and glycolipids. |Hyaluronic acid is a glycosaminoglycan found in the synovial fluid of joints.|Synonym: mucopolysaccharide|Antonym:  (No direct antonym exists, as it's a specific biochemical class)|Noun
