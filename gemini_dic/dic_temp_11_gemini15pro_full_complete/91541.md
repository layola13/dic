hype-up:/ˈhaɪp ʌp/ | n. | 炒作；宣传；兴奋剂 | An attempt to generate excitement or interest, often through exaggerated publicity. | The media hype-up before the fight was intense.  媒体在比赛前的炒作非常激烈。 | 近义词: promotion, publicity, buildup, ballyhoo | 反义词: downplay, understate | 词性: noun

hype-up:/ˈhaɪp ʌp/ | vt. | 大肆宣传；使兴奋；使激动 | To promote or publicize excessively, often with exaggerated claims. | They hyped up the new product as revolutionary. 他们把新产品吹捧成革命性的。 | 近义词: promote, publicize, boost, exaggerate | 反义词: downplay, understate | 词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hype," short for "hypodermic," referring to injecting something, plus "up," indicating an increase or intensification. Originally referred to injecting drugs, later extended to injecting enthusiasm or excitement.
