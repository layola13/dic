cross-questioned:/ˌkrɒsˈkwɛstʃənd/ /ˌkrɑːsˈkwɛstʃənd/ | v. |盘问，反复询问|To question (someone) closely, often aggressively, on a particular subject, especially in order to expose inconsistencies or falsehoods.|The police cross-questioned the suspect for hours. 警方盘问了嫌疑人几个小时。|The lawyer cross-questioned the witness about his alibi. 律师反复询问了证人关于他不在场证明的事情。|Synonyms: interrogate, grill, examine, quiz|Antonyms:  evade, avoid|verb

cross-question:/ˌkrɒsˈkwɛstʃən/ /ˌkrɑːsˈkwɛstʃənd/| n. |盘问，反复询问|The act of cross-questioning someone.|The witness faced a rigorous cross-question.  证人面临着严厉的盘问。|The defense lawyer's cross-question revealed inconsistencies in the witness's testimony.  辩护律师的盘问揭露了证人证词中的矛盾之处。|Synonyms: interrogation, grilling, examination, quizzing|Antonyms: avoidance, evasion|noun
