exoneration: /ɪɡˌzɑːnəˈreɪʃən/ /ɪɡˌzɒnəˈreɪʃən/
| n. | 免罪，赦免 |The act of officially absolving someone from blame or guilt; vindication.| He sought exoneration for the crime he did not commit. 他寻求为他没犯的罪获得免罪。 | 近义词:acquittal, absolution, clearance, vindication | 反义词:condemnation, conviction | 词性:名词

词源: Late Middle English: from Old French exoneracion, from late Latin exoneratio(n-), from exonerare ‘relieve of a burden’ (see exonerate).
