overbleached:/ˌoʊvərˈbliːtʃt/| adj.|过度漂白的|Excessively bleached; bleached to an undesirable degree, often resulting in a damaged or weakened state.|The overbleached jeans looked faded and worn.|近义词:overwhitened, faded, washed-out|反义词:undyed, unbleached|词性:形容词

词源:
"over-" (prefix) + "bleach" (verb)

"over-"  means "excessively" or "too much".

"bleach" comes from Old English *blēacian "to make pale," from Proto-Germanic *blaikijaną "to become pale," from the root *blaikaz "pale, bright, shining."
