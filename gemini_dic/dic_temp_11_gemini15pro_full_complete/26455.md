byssus:/ˈbɪsəs/| n. | 海丝，足丝 | A fine, silky, thread-like filament secreted by certain bivalve mollusks, especially mussels, used to attach themselves to rocks or other surfaces.  A yellowish-brown cloth made from the silky filaments of certain Mediterranean bivalves. | The mussel anchors itself to the rock with its byssus.  贻贝用它的足丝把自己固定在岩石上。|  近义词：fibers, threads | 反义词: None | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin byssus, from Ancient Greek βύσσος (bússos), of Semitic origin. Related to Hebrew בוץ (butz, "white linen").
