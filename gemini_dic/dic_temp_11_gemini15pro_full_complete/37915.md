complementers:/ˈkɒmplɪˌmentəz/ /ˈkɑːmplɪˌmentərz/ | n. | 补语 |A word or phrase that completes the meaning of a verb or adjective.  A complementer can be a noun phrase, an adjective phrase, or a clause.  It provides information required by the verb or adjective to express a complete thought.| The sentence "She is happy" requires a complementer like "to be here" to become "She is happy to be here." |近义词: complementizers |反义词:  (No direct antonym; the absence of a complementer results in an incomplete sentence.) |词性: noun

注意事项:  Complementers introduce complement clauses.  They are sometimes referred to as complementizers.  While they look like conjunctions, they don't link two independent clauses. Instead, they introduce a clause that acts as the subject or object of the main verb.
