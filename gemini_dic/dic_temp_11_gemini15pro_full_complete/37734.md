compartmentalising:/kəmˌpɑːtmɛntəlaɪzɪŋ/
compartmentalize:/kəmˌpɑːrtˈmɛntəlaɪz/
| v. | 分隔；划分；区分；把…分成各自独立的部分 |  The process of separating something into isolated compartments or categories, often to avoid emotional distress or to focus on specific tasks. |  She compartmentalizes her work life and her personal life to avoid stress. 她把工作和生活区分开来以避免压力. | categorize, classify, separate, divide, isolate | integrate, combine, unify | verb

compartmentalisation:/kəmˌpɑːtmɛntəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
compartmentalization:/kəmˌpɑːrtˌmɛntələˈzeɪʃən/
| n. | 分隔；划分；区分 | The act or process of compartmentalizing.  |  The compartmentalization of the company into smaller units increased efficiency.  将公司分成更小的部门提高了效率。 | categorization, classification, separation, division | integration, combination, unification | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From compartment + -alize/-ise.  Compartment ultimately comes from the Latin word compartiri meaning "to divide, share."
