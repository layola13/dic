aiyo:/ˈaɪjəʊ/ | int. | 哎呀; 天哪; 我的媽呀 | An exclamation expressing surprise, dismay, frustration, or exasperation.  It is commonly used in Singaporean and Malaysian English, and also sometimes in Indian English. | Aiyo, I forgot my keys! 哎呀，我忘记带钥匙了！ | Near synonyms: oh dear, oh no, good heavens, gosh | Antonyms: (No direct antonyms for an interjection) | interjection

注意事项:

The word "aiyo" originates from Southern Chinese dialects (e.g., Hokkien, Cantonese).  Its usage is primarily informal.  It carries a strong emotional weight and is less common in formal written English.
