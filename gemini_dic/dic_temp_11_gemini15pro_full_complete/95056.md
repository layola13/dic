inadequacy:/ɪnˈædɪkwəsi/ | n. | 不足；不适当；不胜任 |  The inadequacy of the existing system is apparent. 现有系统不足之处显而易见。 |例句：He felt a deep sense of inadequacy. 他感到深深的不足感。|例句：The inadequacy of the resources hampered the project. 资源的不足阻碍了项目进展。| 近义词：insufficiency, deficiency, shortcoming, incompetence | 反义词：sufficiency, adequacy, competence | 词性: 名词

inadequate:/ɪnˈædɪkwət/ | adj. | 不充分的；不适当的；不胜任的 | Not enough or good enough for a particular purpose. | 例句：The food supply was inadequate to feed the refugees. 食物供应不足以养活难民。|例句：Her qualifications were inadequate for the job. 她的资历不足以胜任这项工作。| 近义词：insufficient, deficient, lacking, incompetent | 反义词：sufficient, adequate, competent | 词性: 形容词

词源: Late 16th century: from French *inadéquat, from inadequate, from in- ‘not’ + adaequatus (see adequate).
