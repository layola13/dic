conciliate: /kənˈsɪlieɪt/
verb
安抚，调解，平息
to stop someone from being angry or discontented; placate; to reconcile, pacify
例句：The company tried to conciliate the angry customers by offering them refunds. 公司试图通过提供退款来安抚愤怒的顾客。

例句：The mediator conciliated the two warring factions. 调解员调解了两个交战派别。

近义词：appease, placate, pacify, soothe, mollify
反义词：aggravate, antagonize, provoke, incite
词性：v.

conciliating: /kənˈsɪlieɪtɪŋ/
adjective
安抚的，调解的
having the intention or effect of making someone less angry or more friendly
例句：She had a conciliating manner that made people feel at ease. 她有一种安抚人的态度，让人感到轻松自在。

例句：He offered a conciliating smile, hoping to diffuse the tension. 他 conciliating 露出一个微笑，希望能缓和紧张气氛。

近义词：appeasing, placatory, pacifying, soothing, mollifying
反义词：aggravating, antagonistic, provoking, inciting
词性：adj.

conciliation: /kənˌsɪliˈeɪʃən/
noun
安抚，调解
the action of stopping someone from being angry or discontented; placation
例句：Conciliation is often the best way to resolve disputes. 调解通常是解决争端的最佳方式。

例句：The union and management reached an agreement through conciliation. 工会和管理层通过调解达成了协议。

近义词：appeasement, placation, pacification, propitiation
反义词：aggravation, antagonism, provocation, incitement
词性：n.

词源: From Latin conciliatus, past participle of conciliare "to bring together, unite, win over," from concilium "council."
