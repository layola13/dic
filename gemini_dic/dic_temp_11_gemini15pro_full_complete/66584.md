exhort:/ɪɡˈzɔːrt/ | v. | 劝告；敦促；力劝 | To strongly encourage or urge (someone) to do something. | The coach exhorted his team to play harder. 教练激励他的团队更加努力地比赛。 | urge, encourage, implore | discourage, dissuade | verb

exhort:/ɪɡˈzɔːrt/ | v. | 劝告；敦促；力劝 | To advise earnestly. |  She exhorted her son to study diligently for the upcoming exams. 她劝告儿子为即将到来的考试努力学习。 | advise, counsel, recommend | deter, hinder | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *exhorten*, from Old French *exhorter*, from Latin *exhortārī* (“to encourage, urge strongly”), from *ex- (“out of, from”) + *hortārī (“to urge, encourage, exhort”), from *hortor (“I urge”). Related to *hortaor* (“encourager”).
