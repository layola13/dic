counterstatements:/ˈkaʊntərˌsteɪtmənts/
noun|反驳，反陈述|Statements made to contradict or oppose other statements.|The lawyer presented counterstatements to refute the witness's testimony. 律师提出了反驳，以反驳证人的证词。|synonyms: rebuttals, contradictions, denials|antonyms: confirmations, agreements, corroborations|n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From "counter-" (prefix meaning "against" or "opposite") + "statement" (a declaration or assertion).  "Counter-" comes from the Old French "contre-" and ultimately from the Latin "contra" (against). "Statement" comes from the Old French "estat" (state, condition) and the Latin "status" (position, condition).
