metaphors:/ˈmɛtəfɔːrz/ (US), /ˈmɛtəfɔːz/ (UK)| n.|隐喻，比喻|A figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable.  A comparison without using like or as.|例句：Her eyes were metaphors for the ocean. 她的眼睛如同大海。|近义词：similes, analogies, figures of speech|反义词:literal language|Noun
