deixis:/ˈdaɪksɪs/ | n. |指示语|The use of a word or expression whose meaning is dependent on the context in which it is used, such as who is speaking, the time of speaking, and the location of the speaker and listener.  It encompasses personal, spatial, and temporal deixis.|Example: "I am here now" is an example of deixis, as the meaning of "I," "here," and "now" depends entirely on who is speaking, where they are, and when they are speaking.|Synonym: indexicality|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, the concept itself is either present or absent in language) | noun

deixises: /ˈdaɪksiːsiːz/ | n. |指示语（复数）| Plural form of deixis, referring to multiple instances of using words or expressions whose meaning depends on the context.|Example:  The sentence "You went there yesterday" contains several deixises.|Synonym: indexicals (plural) |Antonym: (No direct antonym) | noun
