burrowing:/ˈbɜːroʊɪŋ/ (US), /ˈbʌrəʊɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.  n.|挖洞的，穴居的；挖洞，穴居|The act of making a burrow or tunnel, or the state of living in one.  Characteristic of an animal that digs burrows. |The rabbits were burrowing under the fence. 兔子正在篱笆下挖洞。|近义词：excavating, tunneling, digging|反义词: surfacing, emerging|词性: adjective, noun (gerund/present participle)

burrow:/ˈbɜːroʊ/ (US), /ˈbʌrəʊ/ (UK)| n. v.|洞穴，地道；挖洞，钻入|A hole or tunnel dug by an animal as a dwelling. To dig a burrow or tunnel. |The badger retreated to its burrow. 獾退回到它的洞穴里。|近义词：den, hole, tunnel|反义词: mound, hill|词性: noun, verb

Etymology: From Old English *burhrow "fortification made of boards," from *burg "fortress" (see borough) + *hrow "heap, pile" (see raw).  The sense shifted from "fortified place" to "hole, burrow."
