ethnography:/ɛθˈnɒɡrəfi/ (US) /eθˈnɒɡrəfi/ (UK) | n. | 人种志，民族志 | The scientific description of the customs of individual peoples and cultures. |  The ethnography of the remote tribe revealed their unique rituals and social structure. 这偏远部落的民族志揭示了他们独特的仪式和社会结构。 |  近义词: anthropology, cultural study | 反义词: None | 词性: noun

ethnographic:/ˌɛθnəˈɡræfɪk/ (US) /ˌeθnəˈɡræfɪk/ (UK) | adj. | 人种志的，民族志学的 | Relating to the scientific description of the customs of individual peoples and cultures. |  Her ethnographic research focused on the role of women in the community. 她的人种志研究集中于女性在社区中的角色。 | 近义词: anthropological | 反义词: None | 词性: adjective

ethnographer:/ɛθˈnɒɡrəfər/ (US) /eθˈnɒɡrəfər/ (UK) | n. | 人种志学者，民族志学家 | A person who studies ethnography. | The ethnographer spent years living with the tribe to understand their culture. 这位人种志学者花了数年时间与该部落一起生活，以了解他们的文化。 | 近义词: anthropologist | 反义词: None | 词性: noun

USAGE->

词源: From the French "ethnographie," from Greek "ethnos" (nation, people) + "graphein" (to write).
