flyover:/ˈflaɪˌoʊvər/ (US) /ˈflaɪˌəʊvə(r)/ (UK)| n.|立交桥；天桥；空中掠过|A bridge that carries a road, railroad, etc., over another road, railroad, etc.  A low flight by aircraft over a specific location, often as a tribute or ceremonial display.|例句：We took the flyover to avoid the traffic. 我们走立交桥避开了交通堵塞。

例句：The Air Force did a flyover for the national holiday. 空军在国庆节进行了空中飞行表演。|近义词：overpass, bridge, fly-past|反义词:underpass|词性:名词


flyover:/ˈflaɪˌoʊvər/ (US) /ˈflaɪˌəʊvə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|飞越的|Relating to or involving a flyover.|例句：The flyover ceremony was impressive.  飞越仪式令人印象深刻。

例句：The flyover traffic was heavy this morning. 今早立交桥上的交通很拥堵。|近义词：aerial|反义词:N/A|词性:形容词
