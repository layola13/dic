mercurialist:/mərˈkjʊəriəlɪst/ |n.| 性格多变的人；反复无常的人 |A person whose mood or behavior changes quickly and unpredictably. |He's such a mercurialist; one minute he's happy, the next he's furious. 他真是个反复无常的人；一会儿高兴，一会儿又暴怒。|Synonyms: capricious person, fickle person, volatile person, erratic person |Antonyms:  stable person, steady person, consistent person |Noun


mercurial:/mərˈkjʊəriəl/ |adj.| 善变的；反复无常的；水银的 |Characterized by rapid and unpredictable changeableness of mood or behavior; of or containing mercury. |Her mercurial temperament made it difficult to predict her reactions. 她善变的性情让人难以预测她的反应。 The ancient alchemists were fascinated by mercurial compounds. 古代炼金术士对汞化合物非常着迷。|Synonyms:  temperamental, volatile, erratic, fickle, capricious |Antonyms: stable, steady, constant, predictable |Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Mercurialis*, from Mercurius (Mercury), the Roman god of commerce, eloquence, messages, travelers, trickery, and thieves.  Mercury was also associated with the planet Mercury, known for its swift movement, which likely influenced the meaning related to quick and unpredictable changes.
