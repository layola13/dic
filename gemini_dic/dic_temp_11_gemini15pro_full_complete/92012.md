hyperotreta:/ˌhaɪpərəˈtriːtə/ | n. | 盲鳗纲 |  A class of jawless, eel-shaped marine animals, also known as hagfish, characterized by a cartilaginous skeleton, a single nostril, and the production of copious slime. | Hagfish, classified as Hyperotreti, are known to tie themselves in knots. 盲鳗，归类为盲鳗纲，以能把自己打成结而闻名。 | 近义词: Myxini (another scientific name for the same group) | 反义词:  (No direct antonym; could consider Gnathostomata - jawed vertebrates as a contrasting group) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ὑπέρ (hupér, “over, above”) + τρῆμα (trêma, “hole, opening”), referring to the single nostril located above the mouth.
