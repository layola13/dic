lynchs:/lɪntʃɪz/| n.|私刑处死（lynch的第三人称单数形式）|The act of killing someone without a legal trial, usually by hanging, often by an angry mob. | The newspapers reported several lynchs in the South. 报纸报道了南方发生的几起私刑事件。 | 近义词: executions, killings | 反义词: pardons, acquittals |词性:名词

lynch:/lɪntʃ/| v. |私刑处死|To kill someone without a legal trial, usually by hanging, often by an angry mob. |They threatened to lynch him if he didn't leave town.  他们威胁说如果他不离开镇子就对他处以私刑。|近义词: execute, kill illegally | 反义词: pardon, acquit |词性:动词


词源: From Lynch's Law, named after Charles Lynch (1736–1796), a Virginia planter and justice of the peace who headed an extralegal court during the American Revolution.
