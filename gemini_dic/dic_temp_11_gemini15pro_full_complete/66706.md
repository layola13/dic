exonerators:/ɪɡˈzɑːnəreɪtərz/ | n. | 免罪者，证明无罪者 |  A person or thing that exonerates someone.  Someone who clears another person of blame or removes a burden. | The exonerators worked tirelessly to prove his innocence. 免罪者孜孜不倦地工作以证明他的清白。| The DNA evidence served as a powerful exonerator for the wrongly accused man. DNA证据有力地证明了被冤枉的人的清白。 | synonyms: vindicator, absolver, liberator, defender, champion | antonyms: accuser, prosecutor, incriminator, condemner | noun

exonerate:/ɪɡˈzɑːnəreɪt/ | v. | 免除…的责任；证明…的清白 |  To clear from a charge or accusation; to prove innocent. |  The evidence exonerated him from all suspicion. 证据洗清了他所有的嫌疑。 |  The report exonerated the company from any wrongdoing. 报告证明了该公司没有任何不当行为。 | synonyms: acquit, absolve, clear, vindicate, exculpate | antonyms: accuse, convict, incriminate, blame | verb
