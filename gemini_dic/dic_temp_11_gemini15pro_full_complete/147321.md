overmoralizingly: /ˌoʊvərˈmɔːrəlaɪzɪŋli/
| adv. | 过分道德化地 | In a way that expresses excessive moral judgments or imposes moral standards too rigidly. | He spoke overmoralizingly about the dangers of modern music.  他过分道德化地谈论了现代音乐的危害。| 近义词: sanctimoniously, preachily, moralistically | 反义词: amorally, immorally | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From "over" (excessive) + "moralize" (to express moral judgments) + "-ing" (present participle forming an adjective) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).  The root "moral" comes from Latin "moralis," meaning "custom, manner, conduct."
