bullist: /ˈbʊlɪst/
n.|恃强凌弱者; 欺负人的人|A person who habitually intimidates or persecutes weaker people.|He was a bullist in school, always picking on smaller kids. 他在学校是个恃强凌弱者，总是欺负比他小的孩子。|bully, intimidator, oppressor|victim, target|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "bully" (n.) + "-ist" (suffix forming agent nouns). "Bully" originally meant "sweetheart," "lover," then shifted to "fine fellow," "blusterer," and finally to its modern meaning of "one who uses superior strength or influence to intimidate others."  The "-ist" suffix denotes a person who practices or advocates something.
