hippies:/ˈhɪpiz/| n.|嬉皮士|A person, especially of the late 1960s, who rejected established social customs (especially regarding dress and drugs) and advocated love, peace, and simple living.|The hippies gathered in San Francisco during the Summer of Love.|Synonyms: flower children, bohemians, counterculturalists|Antonyms: conformists, traditionalists|noun

hippie:/ˈhɪpi/| n. adj.|嬉皮士；嬉皮风格的|A person, especially of the late 1960s, who rejected established social customs (especially regarding dress and drugs) and advocated love, peace, and simple living. / Characteristic of a hippie.|The hippie wore tie-dye and flowers in her hair. | She had a very hippie style.|Synonyms: flower child, bohemian, counterculturalist |Antonyms: conformist, traditionalist|noun, adjective
