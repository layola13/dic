garroter:/ɡəˈrɒtər/ (UK), /ɡəˈrɑːtər/ (US)| n.| 绞杀者；扼杀者|A person who garrotes someone, typically a robber or murderer who strangles their victim.|The garroter was apprehended by the police within days of the murder.|Synonyms: strangler, killer, assassin, murderer|Antonyms: rescuer, savior|noun

garrote:/ɡəˈrɒt/ (UK), /ɡəˈrɑːt/ (US)| n. vt.| 绞索；勒死；用绞索勒死|A wire, cord, or other length of material used to strangle someone.|The victim was garroted with a piece of piano wire.  |He attempted to garrote the guard.|Synonyms: strangle, choke, throttle|Antonyms: release, free|noun, verb

Etymology: From Spanish *garrote*, from *garrota* "cudgel," perhaps of pre-Roman origin.
