aorta:/eɪˈɔːrtə/ /eɪˈɔrdə/| n. |主动脉|The largest artery in the body, carrying oxygenated blood from the left ventricle of the heart to the rest of the body. |The surgeon carefully repaired the damaged aorta.  外科医生小心翼翼地修复了受损的主动脉。|Synonym:  main artery|Antonym: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *aorta*, from Ancient Greek ἀορτή (aortḗ, “the great artery”), from ἀείρω (aeírō, “I lift, raise”).  The original meaning may have been "something hung up," referring to the way the aorta appears suspended in the chest cavity.
