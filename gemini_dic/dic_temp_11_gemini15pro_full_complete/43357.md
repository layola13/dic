craned:/kreɪnd/ | v. | 伸长（脖子），(为看得更清楚)探头探脑 | to stretch one's neck, especially in order to see something |  She craned her neck to see over the fence. 她伸长脖子想看看篱笆那边。 | synonyms: stretched, extended, peered | antonyms: ducked, lowered, crouched | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cranen*, from Old English *cranian* (“to crave, beg”), from *cran* (“crane”).  The sense shifted from the bird's characteristic posture to the human action of imitating it.
