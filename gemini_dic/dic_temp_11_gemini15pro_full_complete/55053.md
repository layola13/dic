disquisitorial:/ˌdɪskwɪzɪˈtɔːriəl/
adjective
学究式的，好辩论的，好钻研的
Inclined to prolonged and detailed discussion or argument; excessively analytical or argumentative.
例句：The professor's disquisitorial lectures often went over the allotted time. 教授好钻研的讲座经常超过规定的时间。

例句：He launched into a disquisitorial examination of the evidence. 他开始对证据进行一番学究式的审查。

近义词：analytical, argumentative, critical, scholarly, pedantic
反义词：concise, succinct, brief, practical

词性: adjective

词源: From Late Latin *disquisitorius*, from Latin disquisitio "inquiry, investigation," from disquirere "to seek out, investigate," from dis- "apart, thoroughly" + quaerere "to seek, ask."
