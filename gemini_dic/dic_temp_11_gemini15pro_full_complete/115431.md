mantlewise:/ˈmæntlˌwaɪz/ | adv. | 像斗篷一样; 披着斗篷般地; 以覆盖的方式 | In a manner resembling a mantle; like a mantle covering something. | The snow fell mantlewise over the fields. 雪像斗篷一样覆盖着田野。| Similar words: cloak-like, covering, enveloping | Opposite words: uncovered, exposed |  Part of Speech: Adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "mantle" (a loose sleeveless garment worn over other clothes) + "-wise" (suffix indicating manner or direction). "Mantle" comes from Old French "mantel" and ultimately from Latin "mantellum." "-wise" comes from Old English "-wīs."
