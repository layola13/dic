disenfranchised:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzd/ | adj. | 被剥夺权利的；失去公民权的；被剥夺选举权的 | Deprived of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.  |  Many young people feel disenfranchised and don't bother to vote. 许多年轻人感到被剥夺了权利，所以懒得去投票。 | synonyms: deprived, marginalized, dispossessed, alienated | antonyms: empowered, enfranchised, privileged | adjective


disenfranchisement:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃɪzmənt/ | n. | 剥夺权利；剥夺公民权；剥夺选举权 | The act of depriving someone of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote. |  The disenfranchisement of black voters was a major issue in the civil rights movement.  黑人选民被剥夺选举权是民权运动中的一个主要问题。 | synonyms: deprivation, marginalization, dispossession | antonyms: empowerment, enfranchisement | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (meaning "not" or "opposite of") + enfranchise (meaning "to grant the right to vote").  Ultimately from Frankish *frankisk, meaning "free," as only free men originally had the right to vote.
