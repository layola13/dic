diatom: /ˈdaɪətɒm/ (US) /ˈdaɪətəm/ (UK)| n. |硅藻|A single-celled alga that has a cell wall of silica. Diatoms are a major group of algae, and are among the most common types of phytoplankton.|Diatoms are responsible for 20% of the world's oxygen production. 硅藻负责全球20%的氧气产量。

Diatomaceous earth is made from the fossilized remains of diatoms. 硅藻土是由硅藻的化石遗骸制成的。|近义词:phytoplankton (浮游植物), alga (藻类) |反义词: N/A |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Modern Latin *diatoma, from Ancient Greek διάτομος (diátomos, “cut in half”), from διά (diá, “through”) + τέμνω (témnō, “I cut”).  Refers to the two-part structure of the diatom's silica shell.
