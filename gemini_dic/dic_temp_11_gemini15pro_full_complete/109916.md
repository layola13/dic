libel:/ˈlaɪbl/| n. vt.|诽谤，中伤；诽谤性文字|a published false statement that is damaging to a person's reputation|The article was a vicious libel against him. 这篇文章对他恶意诽谤。|synonyms: slander, defamation, calumny|antonyms: praise, commendation|noun

libel:/ˈlaɪbl/| vt.|以文字诽谤，中伤|publish a libel against (someone)|He was libelled in the newspapers. 他在报纸上被诽谤了。|synonyms: slander, defame, malign|antonyms: praise, commend|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *libelle* (meaning "little book," specifically a formal written statement, request, or complaint), from Latin *libellus*, diminutive of *liber* (meaning "book"). The sense of "defamatory writing" developed from the use of such documents in legal proceedings.
