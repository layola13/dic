cordon-off:/ˈkɔːrdən ɒf/ |v.| 封锁，隔离 |To enclose an area with a rope or barrier to prevent people from entering.  |Police cordoned off the area after the explosion. 警察在爆炸后封锁了该地区。|The street was cordoned off while they filmed the movie scene.  他们在拍摄电影场景时封锁了街道。|近义词：block off, close off, seal off, shut off|反义词: open, allow access|词性: 动词

cordon:/ˈkɔːrdən/ |n.|警戒线，封锁线|A line or circle of police, soldiers, or guards preventing access to or from an area or building.|The police set up a cordon around the crime scene. 警方在犯罪现场周围设置了警戒线。|There was a heavy police cordon around the embassy. 大使馆周围有严密的警察警戒线。|近义词：barrier, barricade, fence|反义词: opening|词性: 名词
