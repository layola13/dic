one-sidedly: /ˌwʌnˈsaɪdɪdli/ | adv. | 片面地，单方面地 |  In a way that shows only one side of an issue or situation; unfairly. | The issue was presented one-sidedly, ignoring the perspectives of the other party.  这个问题被片面地呈现，忽略了另一方的观点。|  The media reported the conflict one-sidedly, focusing only on the government's narrative. 媒体对这场冲突进行了片面的报道，只关注了政府的说法。 |  unilaterally, biasedly, unfairly |  fairly, objectively, impartially | adverb

Etymology:
From "one-sided" + "-ly".  "One-sided" comes from "one" + "side" + "-ed".  "Side" comes from Old English "sīde".
