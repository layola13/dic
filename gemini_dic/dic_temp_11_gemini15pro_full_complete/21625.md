blurbist:/ˈblɜːrbɪst/| n.|吹捧者；广告撰稿人；简介作者|A blurbist is someone who writes blurbs, short promotional pieces of text, often found on the back of book covers.|The blurbist crafted a compelling summary that made me want to buy the book.|近义词: publicist, promoter, copywriter|反义词: critic, detractor|词性:noun

Etymology:  From "blurb" (a short promotional description) + "-ist" (a suffix denoting a person who does something).  "Blurb" itself is of uncertain origin, possibly imitative of a sound, coined by American humorist Gelett Burgess in 1907.
