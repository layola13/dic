datives:/ˈdeɪtɪvz/ | n. | 与格 | A grammatical case used to indicate the noun or pronoun that is the recipient or beneficiary of the action of the verb.  It answers the question "to whom?" or "for whom?". | The delivery driver gave the package to me.  "Me" is in the dative case (indirect object). | Synonyms: indirect objects | Antonyms: N/A | noun

dative:/ˈdeɪtɪv/ | adj. | 与格的 | Of, relating to, or constituting a grammatical case marking the indirect object of a verb. | The word "to" often marks a dative construction in English. | Synonyms: indirect | Antonyms: N/A | adjective
