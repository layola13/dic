conductivity:/ˌkɒndʌkˈtɪvɪti/ (US: /ˌkɑːndʌkˈtɪvɪti/)| n.|电导率，导电性，传导率|The ability of a substance to transmit heat, electricity, or sound.|Copper has high electrical conductivity. 铜具有高导电性。|近义词: conductance, transmittance|反义词: resistivity, insulator|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *conductivitās*, from conductīvus (“serving to lead or guide”), from conducere (“to lead or guide”), from con- (“together”) + dūcere (“to lead”).
