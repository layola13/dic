epochal: /ˈɛpəkəl/ (US), /ɪˈpɒkəl/ (UK) | adj. | 划时代的，开创新纪元的 | Forming or marking a new epoch; of great significance. |  The invention of the printing press was an epochal event. 印刷术的发明是一个划时代的事件。

The moon landing was an epochal achievement for humankind. 登月是人类一项划时代的成就。

| 近义词: momentous, historic, groundbreaking, landmark, pivotal, seminal | 反义词: insignificant, trivial, unimportant | 词性: adjective


Etymology:
From Late Latin *epochalis, from Latin epocha (“epoch”) + -alis.  Ultimately from Ancient Greek ἐποχή (epokhḗ, “pause, fixed point of time”).
