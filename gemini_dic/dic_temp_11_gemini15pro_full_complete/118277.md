merchet:/ˈmɜːrtʃɪt/
noun
领主权；初夜权；结婚税
A fine paid to the lord of a manor by a tenant's daughter upon her marriage.  It was a feudal custom, prevalent especially during the Middle Ages.
The lord demanded merchet from any serf who wished to marry his daughter outside the manor.  领主要求任何想把女儿嫁到庄园外的农奴缴纳结婚税。
Synonyms: fine, levy, tax, impost, duty
Antonyms:  reimbursement, refund, reward (These are not perfect antonyms, but represent the opposite concept of payment *to* the lord)
