crap-up:/ˈkræp ʌp/| v. |  突击复习；临时抱佛脚 | To study intensively in a short period, usually just before an examination. | He was crapping up for his finals. 他正在为期末考试突击复习。| 近义词: cram | 反义词: (No direct antonym, consider "study consistently") | 词性: 动词


词源:  "Crap" (meaning nonsense or rubbish) + "up" (intensifying adverb).  The meaning likely evolved from the idea of quickly filling one's head with information, even if it's not well-understood or retained long-term.
