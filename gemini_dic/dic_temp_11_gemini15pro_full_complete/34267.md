claptrap:/ˈklæptræp/| n. | 废话，空话，哗众取宠的言辞 |  Showy language or actions designed to impress, but lacking real meaning or value.  | The politician's speech was full of claptrap about freedom and democracy, but he had no real intention of implementing any reforms.  这位政客的演讲充满了关于自由和民主的空话，但他并没有真正打算实施任何改革。

The advertisement was full of claptrap about the product's amazing benefits.  这个广告充满了关于产品神奇功效的废话。 | 近义词：nonsense, drivel, balderdash, twaddle | 反义词：sense, truth, substance | 词性: 名词
