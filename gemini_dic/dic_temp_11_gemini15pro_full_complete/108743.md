leaps:/liːps/| n. v. |跳跃，飞跃；激增|A sudden, energetic movement upwards or forwards.  A sudden large change or increase.|The athlete leaps over the hurdle. 运动员跳过栏架。|近义词：jumps, bounds, springs, surges|反义词:falls, declines, drops|词性:名词复数; 动词单数第三人称形式
Etymology: From Old English *hlēapan, from Proto-Germanic *hlaupaną (“to run, jump, leap”), from Proto-Indo-European *kleub- (“to stick, adhere; to bend, curve”). Cognate with Dutch lopen (“to walk, run”), German laufen (“to run”), Icelandic hlaupa (“to run, leap”).
