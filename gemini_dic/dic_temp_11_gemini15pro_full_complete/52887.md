diminutively:/dɪˈmɪnjʊtɪvli/ | adv. | 微小地，极小地 | in a way that makes something seem smaller than it really is |  She spoke diminutively, as if trying not to be overheard. 她低声细语，好像生怕被人听到。| synonym: slightly, tinily, minutely | antonym: largely, enormously | Adverb

diminutiveness:/dɪˈmɪnjʊtɪvnɪs/ | n. | 微小，细小 | the state or quality of being extremely small |  The diminutiveness of the hummingbird is fascinating. 蜂鸟的微小令人着迷。| synonym: smallness, tininess, minuteness | antonym: largeness, enormity | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *diminutifly*, from Old French diminutif + -ly. Ultimately from Latin dēminuere (to lessen, diminish).
