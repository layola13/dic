epanalepsis:/ˌɛpənəˈlɛpsɪs/| n. |首语重复法; 头尾重复; |A rhetorical device that repeats the beginning word or clause of a sentence at the end of that same sentence or clause.  |Example: "Rejoice in the Lord always: and again I say, Rejoice." (Philippians 4:4)  快乐地侍奉主吧：我再说一次，快乐地侍奉主吧。

Example: "Blood hath bought blood, and blows have answer'd blows."  血债要用血来还，以牙还牙，以眼还眼。|Synonyms: repetition, anadiplosis |Antonyms: N/A |noun
