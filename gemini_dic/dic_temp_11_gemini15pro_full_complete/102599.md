jailbroke:/ˈdʒeɪlbroʊk/
v.
越狱（指从监狱中逃脱）;  (computing) 对（电子设备）进行越狱
Removed restrictions imposed by the manufacturer or operator, typically of a mobile device or video game console.
He jailbroke his iPhone to install unauthorized apps. 他对自己的iPhone进行了越狱，以便安装未经授权的应用程序。
近义词：escaped, liberated (for the prison sense); unlocked, modified, hacked (for the computing sense)
反义词：imprisoned, confined (for the prison sense); restricted, locked (for the computing sense)
词性：动词(past tense of jailbreak)


jailbreak:/ˈdʒeɪlbreɪk/
n.
越狱
An escape from jail.
The prisoners planned a daring jailbreak. 囚犯们计划了一次大胆的越狱。
近义词：escape, breakout
反义词：imprisonment, confinement
词性：名词

jailbreak:/ˈdʒeɪlbreɪk/
v.
越狱;  (computing) 对（电子设备）进行越狱
To escape from jail.  To remove software restrictions imposed by a manufacturer.
They jailbreak their phones to access unauthorized apps. 他们对手机进行越狱以访问未经授权的应用程序。
近义词：escape, liberate (for the prison sense); unlock, modify, hack (for the computing sense)
反义词：imprison (for the prison sense); restrict, lock (for the computing sense)
词性：动词

词源：jail + break.  "Jail" from Old French "jaiole," ultimately from Latin "cavea" (cage). "Break" from Old English "brecan."  The computing sense emerged in the late 2000s with the rise of the iPhone.
