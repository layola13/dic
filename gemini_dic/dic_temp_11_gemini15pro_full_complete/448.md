ableists:/ˈeɪblɪsts/
n.|歧视残疾人士者；健全中心主义者|People who discriminate against disabled people, often by assuming that typical abilities are superior.|Many accessibility advocates point out how certain architectural designs are inherently ableists and exclude people with mobility devices. 许多无障碍倡导者指出某些建筑设计本质上是歧视残疾人士的，并将行动不便的人排除在外。|近义词：bigots, discriminators|反义词：inclusionists, allies|词性:名词

词源：able + -ist.  From able, meaning having the power, skill, means, or opportunity to do something. The suffix -ist denotes a person who practices or is skilled in something, or who adheres to a particular doctrine or system. In this case, it takes on a negative connotation, referring to someone who subscribes to the belief that able-bodied individuals are superior to disabled people.
