ealdorman:/ˈɔːldərmən/| n.|郡长；市议员|A high-ranking official in Anglo-Saxon England, originally a subordinate to the king but later developing into the chief officer of a shire or county.|The ealdorman presided over the shire court. 郡长主持郡法院。|The ealdorman collected taxes for the king. 郡长为国王收税。|Synonyms: magistrate, governor, chief|Antonyms: subordinate, commoner|noun
