notches:/ˈnɒtʃɪz/ (UK) /ˈnɑːtʃɪz/ (US)
|n.|刻痕，槽口；等级；成就|A V-shaped cut in a surface or edge, typically one of a series, used for keeping a record or making adjustments. Also, a level or degree of something, or an achievement.|
|例句：He made a notch in the stick for each day he spent on the island.  他在棍子上每过一天就刻一个刻痕。|
|例句：She had reached a new notch in her career. 她在事业上达到了一个新的高度。|
|近义词：marks, grooves, indentations, levels, degrees, ranks, achievements|
|反义词：smoothness (for physical notches)|
|词性: noun (plural)|

notch:/ˈnɒtʃ/ (UK) /ˈnɑːtʃ/ (US)
|v.|刻痕；赢得|To cut a V-shaped indentation in something. Also, to achieve or score something.|
|例句：He notched the wood to mark the measurement. 他在木头上刻了个刻痕来标记尺寸。|
|例句：She notched up another victory. 她又赢得了一场胜利。|
|近义词：score, achieve, win, mark, cut, carve|
|反义词：erase (for physical notches)|
|词性: verb|
