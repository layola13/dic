outpray:/ˌaʊtˈpreɪ/ | v. | 胜过祈祷 | To pray more fervently or effectively than someone else. | She outprayed her rival for the healing of her child. 她为孩子的康复比她的对手祈祷得更虔诚。| 近义词: surpass in prayer | 反义词: be outprayed | 词性: 动词

词源: out- + pray

"out-"  prefix meaning "exceeding," "surpassing," "outside," related to Old English "ūt," "out."

"pray" from Old French "preier" (11c., Modern French "prier"), from Late Latin "precare" "to ask earnestly, beg, entreat," from Latin "precari" "to pray, entreat, ask earnestly," related to "prex" (genitive "precis") "prayer, request," from PIE root "*prek-" "to ask, entreat."
