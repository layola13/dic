blester: /ˈblɪstər/| n. |水泡；气泡；(使)起泡；(使)起水泡|A thin vesicle on the skin containing watery matter, as from a burn or other injury. |例句：I got a blister on my heel from my new shoes. 我的新鞋把我脚后跟磨出了水泡。

|例句：The paint on the wall started to blister. 墙上的油漆开始起泡了。


|近义词：bubble, vesicle|反义词: none|词性:noun, verb


blester: /ˈblɪstər/ | v. |(使)起泡；(使)起水泡|To form blisters or cause blisters to form.|例句：The sun blistered the paintwork.  太阳把油漆晒得起了泡。


|例句：My hands blistered after hours of gardening.  园艺工作几小时后，我的手起了水泡。


|近义词：scald, burn|反义词: none|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *blester, probably from Old French blestre "pustule, blister," or a similar source, ultimately of Germanic origin. Related to Old Norse blastr "blast, gust." The original sense likely referred to a swelling caused by a blast of air or heat.
