knock-on:/ˈnɒkɒn/ (UK) /ˈnɑːkɑːn/ (US)| adj. n.|连锁的，连锁反应；撞击|A knock-on effect is a secondary, indirect, or unintended consequence of an action or event.|The closure of the factory had a knock-on effect on local businesses.  这家工厂的关闭对当地企业产生了连锁反应。|indirect, secondary, consequential|direct, primary|adjective, noun

knock-on:/ˈnɒkɒn/ (UK) /ˈnɑːkɑːn/ (US)| n.|撞击|A knock-on is an instance of hitting or striking something.|The knock-on damaged the car's bumper. 撞击损坏了汽车的保险杠。|impact, collision|avoidance|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "knock" + "on," literally meaning a knock that continues onward, implying a chain reaction.
