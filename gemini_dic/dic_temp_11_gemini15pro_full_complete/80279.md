going-up:/ˈɡəʊɪŋ ʌp/ (UK) /ˈɡoʊɪŋ ʌp/ (US)| adj. |上升的，上涨的，增长的|Relating to something that is increasing in value, level, or position.  It can also describe the act of ascending or the process of being constructed.|例句：The going-up ceremony marked the completion of the building's steel frame.  封顶仪式标志着建筑钢框架的竣工。

例句：Going-up costs for raw materials are affecting the final price of goods.  原材料价格上涨正在影响商品的最终价格。|近义词：rising, increasing, ascending, escalating|反义词：going-down, decreasing, descending, falling|词性:形容词


going-up:/ˈɡəʊɪŋ ʌp/ (UK) /ˈɡoʊɪŋ ʌp/ (US)| n. |上升，上涨，增长|The act of ascending or increasing, or the process of being built.|例句：The going-up of the new skyscraper changed the city skyline. 新摩天大楼的建成改变了城市的天际线。

例句：There has been a significant going-up in property prices this year. 今年房地产价格大幅上涨。|近义词：rise, increase, ascent, escalation|反义词：decline, decrease, descent, fall|词性:名词
