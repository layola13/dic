objurgate: /ˈɒbdʒəɡeɪt/ (UK) /ˈɑːbdʒərˌɡeɪt/ (US) | v. |  严厉斥责，痛骂 | to rebuke severely; scold |  The teacher objurgated the student for cheating on the exam. 老师严厉斥责了考试作弊的学生。 | Synonyms: reprimand, rebuke, censure, castigate, upbraid, lambaste, scold | Antonyms: praise, commend, laud, compliment | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *objurgatus*, past participle of *objurgare* (“to scold, rebuke, chide”), from *ob-* (“against, toward”) + *jurgare* (“to quarrel, scold”).
