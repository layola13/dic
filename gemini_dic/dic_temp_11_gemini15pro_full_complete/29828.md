caulk:/kɔːk/| v. n.|填塞；堵缝；填缝料；|To seal (a joint, seam, or crack) with a waterproof filler.|He caulked the windows to keep out the drafts. 他给窗户填缝以防穿堂风。|近义词：seal, fill, pack|反义词:open, unseal|词性:动词/名词
caulked:/kɔːkt/|v.|填塞；堵缝；|Past tense and past participle of "caulk".|The ship's hull was caulked and ready for launching. 船体已经填缝完毕，准备下水。|近义词：sealed, filled, packed|反义词:opened, unsealed|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old North French *cauquer, calquer (“to press, to tread”), from Latin calcare (“to tread, to press”), from calc- (“heel”).
