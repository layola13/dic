flunkeyisms: /ˈflʌŋkiɪzəmz/ | n. | 谄媚行为；奴才作风 | Excessive or servile flattery and attentiveness; the characteristics of a flunkey. | The politician's flunkeyisms alienated many voters who valued sincerity. 这位政客的谄媚行为疏远了许多重视真诚的选民。 |  servility, obsequiousness, sycophancy | independence, assertiveness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From flunkey + -ism.  "Flunkey" originally referred to a liveried servant, especially a footman.  It later acquired the pejorative sense of someone who is excessively subservient.  The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, practice, or characteristic.
