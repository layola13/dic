overjoy:/ˌoʊvərˈdʒɔɪ/| v.|使 overjoyed,欣喜若狂|To fill with great joy; delight exceedingly.|Her unexpected visit overjoyed the children.|Near synonyms: thrill, enchant, elate|Near antonyms: sadden, depress|Verb

overjoyed:/ˌoʊvərˈdʒɔɪd/| adj.|欣喜若狂的，非常高兴的|Extremely happy; delighted.|I was overjoyed to hear about your engagement.|Near synonyms: elated, thrilled, jubilant|Near antonyms: miserable, dejected|Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + joy.  "Over-" acts as an intensifier, meaning "excessively" or "completely".  "Joy" comes from Old French "joie," ultimately from Latin "gaudia," plural of "gaudium" meaning "joy, delight."
