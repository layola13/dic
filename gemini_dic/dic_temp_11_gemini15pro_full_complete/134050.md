nonidiomatic:/ˌnɒnɪdiəˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 非惯用的，不符合语言习惯的 | Not conforming to the natural or usual way in which words and phrases are used in a language.  It refers to language that is grammatically correct but sounds unnatural or awkward to native speakers because it doesn't follow the typical patterns of the language. |  His English is grammatically correct, but sometimes sounds nonidiomatic. 他的英语语法正确，但有时听起来不自然。 | synonyms: unnatural, awkward, stilted, unidiomatic| antonyms: idiomatic, natural, fluent, colloquial | adjective
