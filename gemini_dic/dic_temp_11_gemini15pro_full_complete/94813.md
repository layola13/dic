impulsivity:/ɪmˌpʌlˈsɪvɪti/| n. |冲动，冲动性|The tendency to act on a whim, displaying behavior characterized by little or no forethought, reflection, or consideration of the consequences.|His impulsivity often led him into trouble. 他的冲动经常给他带来麻烦。|Synonyms: impetuousness, rashness, recklessness|Antonyms: caution, prudence, deliberation|noun

impulsive:/ɪmˈpʌlsɪv/| adj. |冲动的|Acting or done without forethought.|She made an impulsive decision to quit her job. 她一时冲动决定辞掉工作。|Synonyms: spontaneous, hasty, rash|Antonyms: cautious, planned, deliberate|adjective

impulsiveness:/ɪmˈpʌlsɪvnəs/| n. |冲动|The quality of being impulsive.|Her impulsiveness often got her into trouble. 她的冲动经常使她陷入困境。|Synonyms: impetuousness, rashness|Antonyms: caution, prudence|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *impulsivus, from Latin impulsus (“impulse”), past participle of impellō (“to push, strike against”). Equivalent to impulse +‎ -ive.
