blockader:/blɒˈkeɪdə(r)/ /blɑːˈkeɪdər/ | n. |封锁者，阻塞者|A person or a country that blockades a place, especially a port.|The British blockaders prevented supplies from reaching the enemy.  英国封锁者阻止补给到达敌人手中。|近义词：besieger, investor|反义词：liberator|词性:名词

blockade:/blɒˈkeɪd/ /blɑːˈkeɪd/ | n. vt. |封锁，阻塞|The isolating, closing off, or surrounding of a place, such as a port, harbor, or city, by enemy forces to prevent entrance or exit; any obstruction that hinders passage or progress. From Old French *bloquer "to obstruct, block."  封锁一个地方，例如港口、海港或城市，以阻止人员或物资进出；任何阻碍通行或进展的障碍物。源自古法语 *bloquer “阻碍，阻塞”。|The navy enforced a blockade of the enemy port. 海军对敌方港口实施封锁。|The protesters formed a blockade at the entrance to the building. 抗议者在建筑物入口处设置了路障。|近义词：siege, obstruction|反义词：opening, access|词性:名词,动词
