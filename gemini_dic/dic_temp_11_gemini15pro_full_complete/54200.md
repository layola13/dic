disfancy:/dɪsˈfænsi/ | n. | 不喜爱，厌恶 |  A feeling of no longer liking or being interested in something that you used to like. | I used to love collecting stamps, but now I feel a disfancy for it. 我曾经热爱集邮，但现在我对它感到厌烦。 | 近义词：dislike, aversion, distaste, disaffection | 反义词：fancy, liking, fondness, affection | 词性: 名词

词源:  "dis-" (expressing negation) + "fancy" (liking, inclination).  The prefix "dis-" negates the meaning of "fancy," creating the sense of no longer liking something.
