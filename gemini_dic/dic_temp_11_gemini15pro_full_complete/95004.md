in-waiting:/ɪn ˈweɪtɪŋ/| adj.|等待中的，即将上任的|Someone or something in waiting is ready and available to be used or to take a particular role when needed.  It usually implies a formal or official role. |The heir in-waiting is preparing for his coronation. 等待继位的继承人正在准备他的加冕典礼。|近义词：expectant, forthcoming, prospective|反义词:current, incumbent|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From "in" + "waiting"
"in" (preposition) - indicating position or state
"waiting" (present participle of "wait") - remaining in a place until something expected happens
