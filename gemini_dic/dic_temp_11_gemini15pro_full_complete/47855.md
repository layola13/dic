deceitfully:/dɪˈsiːtfəli/
adverb
欺骗地，诡诈地
In a way that is intended to deceive; dishonestly.
He deceitfully claimed to be a qualified doctor. 他欺骗性地声称自己是一名合格的医生。
She smiled deceitfully, hiding her true intentions. 她诡诈地笑了笑，掩盖了她的真实意图。
近义词:dishonestly, fraudulently, deceptively, craftily
反义词:honestly, truthfully, sincerely, frankly
词性:adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *deceitfulli*, from deceit + -fully.  Deceit itself comes from Old French *deceite*  (Modern French *déceite*), from *decevoir* "to deceive," from Latin *dēcĭpĕre* "to ensnare, deceive, beguile, cheat."
