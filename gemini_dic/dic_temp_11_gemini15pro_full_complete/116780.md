maquiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | adj. | 马基雅维利主义的；不择手段的；狡猾的 | Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian political philosopher known for his book "The Prince," which advocated pragmatism and amorality in political leadership. |  His maquiavellian tactics ensured he won the election, even though he lost the respect of many. 他不择手段的策略确保他赢得了选举，尽管他失去了很多人的尊重。 | synonyms: cunning, devious, scheming, unscrupulous, artful, wily | antonyms: honest, principled, straightforward, ethical, scrupulous | adjective
