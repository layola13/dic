frisking:/ˈfrɪskɪŋ/ /ˈfrɪskɪŋ/
| n. |搜身，轻快的跳跃|The act of quickly running your hands over someone's clothes to search for hidden weapons or other illegal items. Also, playful skipping or leaping.|
| The police officer was frisking the suspect for weapons. 警察正在搜查嫌疑犯身上的武器。|
|近义词：searching, patting down, inspecting, gambolling, frolicking|
|反义词：ignoring, neglecting|
|词性：noun (derived from the verb "frisk")|

词源:  From the Old French *frisque* "fresh, lively," from Frankish *frisc* related to the Old English *fersc* "fresh." The sense of "to search (someone) for concealed weapons" arose in the late 16th century.
