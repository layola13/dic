deflate:/dɪˈfleɪt/ | v. | 放气；泄气；使缩小；贬低；使灰心 | to release air or gas from something, such as a tire; to make something smaller or less important; to reduce someone's confidence or enthusiasm |  The mechanic deflated the tire. 技工给轮胎放了气。| synonyms: collapse, shrink, depress | antonyms: inflate, expand, encourage | verb

deflate:/dɪˈfleɪt/ | v. |  使灰心；使泄气 | to make someone feel less confident or enthusiastic |  The harsh criticism deflated him. 严厉的批评使他灰心丧气。| synonyms: discourage, dishearten, demoralize | antonyms: encourage, hearten, inspire | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *deflāre, from de- (“down, away”) + flāre (“to blow”).
