expropriate: /ɛksˈproʊprieɪt/ | v. | 征用，没收 | To take property from its owner for public use, often with compensation. | The government expropriated the land for the construction of a new highway. 政府征用了这块土地来修建一条新的高速公路。|  The regime expropriated the farmer's land without compensation. 该政权没收了农民的土地，没有给予任何补偿。 | synonyms: seize, confiscate, appropriate, commandeer | antonyms: return, restore, give back | verb

expropriated: /ɛksˈproʊprieɪtɪd/ | adj. | 被征用的，被没收的 | Having been deprived of property by the government or other authority. | The expropriated landowners were given minimal compensation. 被征收土地的所有者只得到了很少的补偿。 |  The family lived in an expropriated mansion that once belonged to royalty. 这家人住在一座被征用的豪宅里，这座豪宅曾经属于皇室。 | synonyms: seized, confiscated, appropriated | antonyms: returned, restored | adjective
