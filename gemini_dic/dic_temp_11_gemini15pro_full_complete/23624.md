branching:/ˈbrɑːntʃɪŋ/ | n. adj.| 分支，分叉；树枝状的 |The act of splitting into two or more parts, like the branches of a tree.  It can also describe something shaped like branches. | The company's branching into new markets is a sign of growth.  公司拓展新市场是增长的标志。| synonyms: diverging, forking, ramifying | antonyms: converging, uniting | noun

branching:/ˈbrɑːntʃɪŋ/ | adj.| 分支的，分叉的 | Having branches; resembling branches. | The branching structure of the organization allows for specialized departments.  该组织的分支结构允许设立专门部门。| synonyms: branched, forked, dendritic | antonyms: straight, unbranched | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *braunching, derived from branch + -ing.  Ultimately from Old French branche.
