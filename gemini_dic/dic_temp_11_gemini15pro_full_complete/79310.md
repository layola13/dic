gleam:/ɡliːm/| n. vi.|微光，闪光；闪现，流露|A faint or brief light, or a brief or faint showing of something (such as emotion).|The gleam of the headlights caught her eye.  车前灯的微光引起了她的注意。|The swords gleamed in the sunlight. 剑在阳光下闪闪发光。|近义词：shine, glimmer, flash, sparkle, glint|反义词：dullness, darkness|词性:名词,动词

gleam:/ɡliːm/| vi.|发出微光；闪现，流露|To shine faintly or briefly; to appear briefly or faintly.|A hope gleamed in his eyes.  一丝希望在他眼中闪现。|Her eyes gleamed with amusement. 她的眼中流露出愉悦的光芒。|近义词：shine, glimmer, flash, sparkle, glint|反义词：fade, dim|词性:动词

词源: From Old English *glæm, from Proto-Germanic *glæmaz. Cognate with Old Norse gleima (“to gleam”).
