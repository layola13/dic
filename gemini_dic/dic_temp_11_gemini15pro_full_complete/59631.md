ecclesiasticism:/ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪsɪzəm/
n.
教会主义，教权主义
Excessive emphasis on the importance of church tradition, doctrine, and authority, especially in matters of faith and morals.
The novel critiques the ecclesiasticism of the Victorian era. 这部小说批判了维多利亚时代的教会主义。
近义词: clericalism, dogmatism, sectarianism
反义词: secularism, liberalism, nonconformity
词性: noun

Etymology:  From Late Latin *ecclēsiasticismus*, from ecclēsiasticus (“ecclesiastical”) + -ismus.  Ultimately from Ancient Greek ἐκκλησιαστικός (ekklēsiastikós, “of the assembly, pertaining to the church”), from ἐκκλησία (ekklēsía, “assembly, church”).
