disfiguringly:/dɪsˈfɪɡərɪŋli/
| adv. | 毁容地，丑陋地 | In a way that spoils the appearance of something or someone; so as to cause disfigurement. | The burn scars disfiguringly marked his face. 烧伤疤丑陋地在他脸上留下了痕迹。| 近义词：hideously, grotesquely, repulsively | 反义词：beautifully, attractively | 词性: 副词

disfigure:/dɪsˈfɪɡər/
| vt. | 毁容，损毁…的外形 | To spoil the appearance of something or someone. | The accident disfigured him for life.  这场事故使他终身毁容。| 近义词：deform, mar, mutilate | 反义词：beautify, adorn | 词性: 动词

figure:/ˈfɪɡjər/
| n. | 数字；人物；身材；图形 | A numerical symbol; a person; the shape of a human body; a diagram or illustrative drawing. |  She has a good figure. 她身材很好。| 近义词：shape, form, number | 反义词：ground, background (for "figure" as in a prominent person) | 词性: 名词
