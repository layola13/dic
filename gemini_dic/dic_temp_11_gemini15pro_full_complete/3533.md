against-her-grain:/əˈɡenst hər ɡreɪn/| idiom |违背她的意愿，让她不舒服|Going against the grain means doing something that is difficult or unpleasant because it contradicts one's natural inclinations or beliefs.  Specifically, "against *her* grain" refers to something that is contrary to a female's natural inclination. | Asking her to lie went against her grain.  要求她撒谎违背了她的意愿。 | against one's principles, contrary to one's nature, reluctantly |with one's principles, willingly, naturally |idiom
