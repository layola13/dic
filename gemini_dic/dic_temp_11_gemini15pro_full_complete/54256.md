disgracefully: /dɪsˈɡreɪsfəli/ | adv. | 耻辱地，丢脸地，不光彩地 | in a way that causes disgrace or shame. | He behaved disgracefully at the party, embarrassing his family. 他在聚会上的行为很不光彩，让他的家人很尴尬。 |  The team played disgracefully and lost the match. 这个队比赛打得很丢脸，输了比赛。 | 近义词: shamefully, ignominiously, dishonorably | 反义词: honorably, respectably, creditably | 词性: 副词

disgraceful: /dɪsˈɡreɪsfəl/ | adj. | 耻辱的，丢脸的，不光彩的 | causing disgrace or shame. | His behavior was disgraceful. 他的行为很丢脸。 | It's disgraceful that so much food is wasted.  这么多食物被浪费真是可耻。 | 近义词: shameful, ignominious, dishonorable | 反义词: honorable, respectable, creditable | 词性: 形容词

grace: /ɡreɪs/ | n. |  优美，优雅；恩典，慈悲；宽限，缓期 | elegance or beauty of form, manner, motion, or action;  unmerited divine assistance given humans for their regeneration or sanctification; a short period of time allowed as a delay before some obligation must be met | She moved with grace and elegance. 她举止优雅。| He asked for a grace period to pay the loan. 他要求延期偿还贷款。| 近义词: elegance, charm, kindness, favor | 反义词: awkwardness, clumsiness, disfavor | 词性: 名词

词源: From Middle English *disgracen, from Old French desgrecier, disgracier (“to put out of favor, deprive of grace”), from des- + grace (“grace”).  Disgracefully is the adverbial form of disgraceful, which is formed by adding the suffix -ful to disgrace, and then -ly to create the adverb.
