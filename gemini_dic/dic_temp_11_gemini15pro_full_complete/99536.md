intergossiped:/ˌɪntərˈɡɒsɪpt/ | v. |互相八卦，私下议论|To gossip among themselves; to spread rumors or talk about others' private lives within a group.| They intergossiped about their coworkers during lunch. 他们在午餐时互相八卦同事。|The neighbors intergossiped about the new family on the block. 邻居们私下议论着新搬来的那家人。|近义词：gossiped, chatted, whispered,rumored|反义词：remained silent, kept secrets|词性：动词

Etymology:
"inter-" (prefix): Latin, meaning "between, among"
"gossip":  From Old English godsibb "sponsor at baptism," from god "God" + sibb "relative." The meaning shifted to "close friend," then to "idle chatter," and finally to "rumor or talk about others' private lives."
