dampen:/ˈdæmpən/ (US), /ˈdæmpən/ (UK)| v. |使潮湿；抑制；减弱；使沮丧|To make something slightly wet or to reduce the intensity of something (like enthusiasm, sound, or light).|The rain dampened the ground. 雨水使地面潮湿了。|synonyms: moisten, dampen, stifle, mute, soften, restrain, curb, check, moderate, diminish, lessen, reduce, lower, weaken|antonyms: dry, intensify, encourage, heighten|verb

dampened:/ˈdæmpənd/ (US), /ˈdæmpənd/ (UK)| adj. |潮湿的；沮丧的|Made slightly wet or reduced in intensity.|His dampened spirits made it difficult to enjoy the party. 他沮丧的情绪让他很难享受派对。|synonyms: damp, moist, depressed, dejected, disheartened|antonyms: dry, elated, cheerful|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->

From damp + -en (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become").  The original sense was literally "to make damp," with the figurative senses of "suppress" and "depress" developing later.
