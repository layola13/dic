deliquesce:/ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛs/| v. |潮解，溶解|To absorb moisture from the air and dissolve.|The salt deliquesced in the humid air.  盐在潮湿的空气中潮解了。|The crystals deliquesced quickly in the damp environment. 晶体在潮湿的环境中迅速潮解。|近义词：dissolve, melt, liquefy|反义词：dry, solidify, harden|词性：动词

deliquescent:/ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛsənt/| adj. |潮解的，易潮解的|Tending to deliquesce; readily absorbing moisture from the air and dissolving.|Calcium chloride is a deliquescent substance. 氯化钙是一种易潮解的物质。|The deliquescent nature of the chemical made it difficult to store. 这种化学品的潮解性使其难以储存。|近义词：hygroscopic|反义词：non-hygroscopic|词性：形容词

deliquescence:/ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛsəns/| n. |潮解，溶解|The process of deliquescing; the state of being deliquesced.|Deliquescence is a problem for some hygroscopic materials. 潮解对某些吸湿性材料来说是一个问题。|The deliquescence of the salt caused a puddle to form. 盐的潮解导致形成了一个水坑。|近义词：dissolution, melting, liquefaction|反义词：drying, solidification, hardening|词性：名词

词源：From Latin *deliquescere*, from *de-* (down, away) + *liquescere* (to become liquid), from *liquidus* (liquid).
