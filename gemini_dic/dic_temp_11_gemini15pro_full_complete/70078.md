feudatory:/ˈfjuːdətɔːri/ (US), /ˈfjuːdətəri/ (UK)| n. adj.|封臣，封建附庸；封地的，附庸的|A person who holds land from a lord in return for allegiance and service; relating to or characteristic of a feudal vassal.|The duke's feudatories were expected to provide troops in times of war. 公爵的封臣们在战时要提供军队。|Synonym: vassal, liegeman, tributary|Antonym: lord, sovereign|Noun

feudatory:/ˈfjuːdətɔːri/ (US), /ˈfjuːdətəri/ (UK)| adj.|封地的，附庸的|Relating to or characteristic of a feudal vassal.|The feudatory system was complex and often led to disputes. 封建附庸制度很复杂，经常导致纠纷。|Synonym: vassal, dependent, subservient|Antonym: independent, sovereign|Adjective
