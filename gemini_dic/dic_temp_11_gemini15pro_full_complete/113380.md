lycopodite:/laɪˈkɒpəˌdaɪt/| n. |石松植物；石松孢子|A fossil lycopod, especially one from the Carboniferous period.  A spore from a lycopodium plant.|例句：Lycopodites are common fossils in coal deposits. 石松植物化石在煤矿床中很常见。

例句：The lycopodite spores were used in early photography. 石松孢子在早期摄影中被使用。|近义词：club moss, ground pine|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From  *lycopodium* + *-ite* (suffix forming nouns denoting fossils). *Lycopodium* comes from  Greek *lýkos* (wolf) + *pódion* (little foot), referring to the appearance of some species.
