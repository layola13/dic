outspying:/ˌaʊtˈspaɪɪŋ/
verb
监视，侦查，胜过…的间谍活动
The act of spying more effectively than someone else.
例句：They were accused of outspying their rivals. 他们被指控胜过对手的间谍活动。

例句：The agency focused on outspying enemy nations during the Cold War.  这个机构在冷战期间专注于胜过敌国的间谍活动。

近义词：surveilling, monitoring,  espionage, exceeding in espionage
反义词：N/A
词性：v.


outspying:/ˌaʊtˈspaɪɪŋ/
noun
胜过…的间谍活动
The act of spying more effectively than someone else, resulting in superior intelligence gathering.
例句：Their outspying gave them a significant advantage. 他们的优秀间谍活动给了他们显著的优势。

例句：The government invested heavily in outspying during the war.  政府在战争期间大力投资间谍活动。


近义词：superior espionage, surveillance, monitoring
反义词：N/A
词性：n.


词源:  "out-" (prefix meaning "exceeding, surpassing") + "spying" (present participle of "spy")
