acantha: /əˈkænθə/| n.|刺状突起；莨苕；（建筑）叶形装饰|A prickly spine or a plant of the genus *Acanthus*, especially the *Acanthus mollis* (bear's breeches), the leaves of which are represented in stylized form on Corinthian and Composite capitals.|The acanthus leaf is a common motif in classical architecture.|Synonyms: spine, prickle, thorn, bear's breeches|Antonyms: smoothness, softness|noun
