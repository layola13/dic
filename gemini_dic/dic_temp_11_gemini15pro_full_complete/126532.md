nabobical:/ˌneɪˈbɒbɪk(ə)l/| adj.|富豪似的，奢华的，像土王的|Resembling or characteristic of a nabob, especially in wealth or ostentation.  Characterized by or suggestive of great wealth and a lavish lifestyle, often associated with someone who acquired their fortune in the East Indies.|He led a nabobical lifestyle, complete with a mansion, servants, and exotic pets. 他过着富豪般的生活，拥有豪宅、仆人和奇异的宠物。|Synonyms: opulent, luxurious, lavish, extravagant, sumptuous|Antonyms: poor, impoverished, modest, humble|adjective

nabob:/ˈneɪbɒb/| n.|土王，富豪|A person, especially a European, who became rich in India or elsewhere in the East, typically during the period of British rule. A person of great wealth.|He lived like a nabob, throwing lavish parties and traveling the world. 他像个土豪一样生活，举办奢华的派对，周游世界。|Synonyms: tycoon, magnate, millionaire, billionaire|Antonyms: pauper|noun
