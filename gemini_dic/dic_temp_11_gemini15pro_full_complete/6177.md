amiss:/əˈmɪs/| adj. |错误的，不正常的，不对劲的|Something is wrong; not as it should be.  Something is out of place.|例句：She sensed something was amiss when she saw the broken window. 她看到窗户破了，感觉有什么不对劲。|例句：He noticed that the books on the shelf were amiss. 他注意到书架上的书放错了地方。|近义词：wrong, faulty, improper, awry|反义词：right, correct, proper, in order|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *amis*, from Old Norse *á mis*, literally "on amiss," from the preposition *á* "on" and the noun *mis*, "error, fault." Ultimately from the Proto-Germanic *missa* "loss, damage, error."
