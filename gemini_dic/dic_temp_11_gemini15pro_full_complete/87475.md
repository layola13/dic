hesychast:/ˈhɛsɪkæst/| n. | 静修者，默祷者 | A person who practices Hesychasm, a mystical tradition of contemplative prayer in the Eastern Orthodox Church.  This involves focusing the mind by using techniques such as repetitive prayer and controlled breathing to achieve a state of inner stillness and union with God. |  Example 1: The hesychast retreated to his cell for hours of prayer. 静修者退到他的小屋里祈祷数小时。

Example 2:  The life of a hesychast is dedicated to prayer and contemplation. 静修者的生活致力于祈祷和冥想。 |  Synonyms: contemplative, mystic, ascetic | Antonyms:  materialist, hedonist | noun
