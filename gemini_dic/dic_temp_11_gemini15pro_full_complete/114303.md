mainstreamed:/ˈmeɪnstriːmd/| adj.|主流化的，融入主流的|placed into the main stream of society or education; integrated.|The school has successfully mainstreamed many students with special needs.  这所学校已经成功地将许多有特殊需要的学生融入主流教育。|近义词：integrated, incorporated, included|反义词：segregated, isolated|词性:形容词

Etymology:
mainstream (n.) + -ed (suffix forming adjectives from nouns)

mainstream (n.):
main (adj.) + stream (n.)

main (adj.):  From Middle English *main, *maine (“principal, chief, most important”), from Old French maine (“hand”), from Latin manus (“hand”). The semantic development is from "held in the hand" to "principal."

stream (n.): From Old English strēam (“stream, current, river”), from Proto-Germanic *straumaz (“stream, current”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)rew- (“to flow”).
