discursist:/dɪˈskɜːsɪst/| n.| 演讲者，论述者，雄辩家 | A person who engages in discourse, especially formal and lengthy discussion or debate. |  The discursist captivated the audience with his eloquent arguments.  这位雄辩家以其雄辩的论点吸引了观众。| synonyms: speaker, orator, lecturer, rhetorician | antonyms: listener, audience | noun

discursive:/dɪˈskɜːsɪv/| adj.| 散漫的，离题的，推理的 | Proceeding by argument or reasoning rather than intuition. Digressing from subject to subject. | His discursive style made it difficult to follow his train of thought.  他散漫的风格使得人们很难跟上他的思路。 The professor's lecture was discursive, covering various related topics. 教授的讲座很散漫，涵盖了各种相关的主题。 | synonyms: rambling, digressive, meandering, analytical, reasoned | antonyms: concise, succinct, focused, intuitive | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *discursor, from Latin discursus (“a running about”), past participle of discurrere (“to run about, run to and fro, run different ways”), from dis- (“apart, in different directions”) + currere (“to run”).
