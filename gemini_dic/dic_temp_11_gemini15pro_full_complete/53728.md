discodactylous: /ˌdɪskoʊdækˈtɪləs/ | adj. | 盘趾的，吸盘指（趾）的 | Having adhesive discs on the toes, typically for climbing. This adaptation is often seen in tree frogs and geckos. |  Example: The discodactylous gecko easily scaled the smooth glass.  盘趾壁虎很容易爬上光滑的玻璃。

Example: Discodactylous feet are an advantage for arboreal species.  吸盘指（趾）的脚对树栖物种来说是一个优势。| 近义词: zygodactylous (partially), adhesive-toed | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but could consider non-adhesive, or claw-bearing) | 词性: adjective

Etymology: From Greek *diskos* "disk" + *daktylos* "finger, toe" + *-ous* (adjective suffix).
