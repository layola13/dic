accosting: /əˈkɒstɪŋ/ (UK) /əˈkɑːstɪŋ/ (US)
| v. | 搭讪；上前攀谈；(唐突或具侵略性地)走近谈话 | approaching and speaking to someone boldly or aggressively, especially in order to ask for or demand something |  The beggar accosted me for money. 乞丐上前向我讨钱。 |近义词：approaching, confronting, addressing, hailing, buttonholing |反义词: avoiding, ignoring, shunning |词性: 动名词 (gerund or present participle)
词源:  From Old French *acoster (meaning "come alongside"), from coste ("rib, side, coast"), from Latin costa ("rib, side"). The sense evolution was from "come alongside" to "approach" to "address."
