deforciant:/dɪˈfɔːrsiənt/| n.|强迫放弃者，非法剥夺者|A person who wrongfully withholds property from another.|The deforciant was ordered to return the stolen land.|Synonym: usurper, disseisor|Antonym: rightful owner|Noun

Etymology:
From Old French *deforçant*, present participle of *deforcer* "to force out, dispossess," from *de-* (expressing removal) + *force* "strength."
