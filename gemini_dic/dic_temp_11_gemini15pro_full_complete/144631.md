outwits:/ˌaʊtˈwɪts/| vt.|智胜，瞒过，比…更聪明|to get an advantage over someone by acting more cleverly and quickly than they do|She completely outwitted her opponents and won the contest. 她彻底智胜了对手，赢得了比赛。|
近义词: outsmart, outmaneuver, trick, deceive|
反义词: be outwitted, be fooled|
词性: 动词


词源:
From out- + wit.  "Out-" prefix indicating exceeding or surpassing. "Wit" originally meant "knowledge, understanding," later evolving to "mental capacity, intelligence, cleverness."  Thus, "outwit" means to surpass someone in cleverness.
