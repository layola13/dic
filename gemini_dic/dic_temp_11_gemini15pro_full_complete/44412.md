cross-lots:/ˈkrɔːs lɒts/ (US), /ˈkrɒs lɒts/ (UK)| adv., adj.| 抄近路；斜穿地块 | Going cross-lots means taking a shortcut across an area, typically a field or piece of land, rather than following a designated path or road. It can also be used adjectivally to describe a path or route that cuts across an area. | We took a cross-lots path to get to the river. 我们抄近路穿过一块地到达了河边。| The cross-lots route saved us a lot of time. 这条抄近路的路线节省了我们很多时间。 | Synonyms: shortcut, direct, diagonal | Antonyms: roundabout, circuitous | adverb, adjective
