courtesanship: /ˈkɔːrtiənʃɪp/ /ˈkɔːrtəʃɪp/ | n. | 交际花；奉承；求爱；求婚 | The art or practice of a courtier; courtly behavior.  Also, the behavior of a person who seeks favor, especially by flattery.  Furthermore, the process of courting or wooing someone with the intent of marriage or a romantic relationship. |  In the 18th century, courtesanship was a common way for women to gain social and economic power. 在18世纪，做交际花是女性获得社会和经济权力的一种常见方式。

courtesan: /ˈkɔːrtɪzən/ /ˈkɔːrtəzən/ | n. | 交际花；妓女 | A prostitute, especially one with wealthy or upper-class clients.  Historically, a courtesan could also be a woman who attended the court of a monarch or other powerful figure, often with considerable social and political influence. | The courtesan was known for her wit and beauty.  这位交际花以她的智慧和美貌而闻名。
