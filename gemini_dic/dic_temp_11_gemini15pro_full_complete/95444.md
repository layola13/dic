incise:/ɪnˈsaɪz/| v. |雕刻，切割，刻入|To cut into a surface, especially precisely and with a sharp instrument.|The surgeon incised the skin with a scalpel.  外科医生用手术刀切开了皮肤。|
The artist incised a delicate pattern onto the silver plate. 艺术家在银盘上刻了一个精美的图案。|Synonyms: carve, engrave, etch, cut|Antonyms: join, unite, fuse|verb


incision:/ɪnˈsɪʒn/| n. |切口， incision, 雕刻|A cut made into a surface, especially precisely and with a sharp instrument. Also, the act of making such a cut.|The surgeon made a small incision to drain the abscess. 外科医生做了一个小切口来排出脓肿。|
The incisions on the ancient stones told a story of a forgotten civilization. 古代石头上的雕刻讲述了一个被遗忘的文明的故事。|Synonyms: cut, gash, slit, score|Antonyms: closure, suture|noun
