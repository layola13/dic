intimidating: /ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人胆怯的，吓人的 | Inspiring fear or awe, especially in order to make someone feel less confident. |  The wrestler's huge size was intimidating.  这个摔跤手的巨大体型令人胆怯。 |  The courtroom can be an intimidating place for young lawyers.  法庭对于年轻律师来说可能是一个令人胆怯的地方。 | 近义词: frightening, menacing, daunting, formidable | 反义词: comforting, reassuring, encouraging | 词性: 形容词

intimidate: /ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪt/ | vt. | 恐吓，威胁 | To frighten or overawe (someone), especially in order to make them do what one wants. | The boss intimidated his employees into working overtime. 老板恐吓员工加班。 |  They were intimidated into silence by the threat of violence.  他们在暴力威胁下被吓到不敢出声。| 近义词: frighten, threaten, browbeat, bully | 反义词: encourage, embolden, reassure | 词性: 动词

intimidation: /ɪnˌtɪmɪˈdeɪʃn/ | n. | 恐吓，威胁 | The action of intimidating someone, or the state of being intimidated. |  The government condemned the intimidation of voters. 政府谴责对选民的恐吓行为。| The use of intimidation is not acceptable in the workplace.  在工作场所使用恐吓手段是不可接受的。 | 近义词: threat, coercion, bullying, menace | 反义词: encouragement, reassurance | 词性: 名词


词源: Late Middle English: from late Latin intimidat- ‘made timid’, from the past participle of intimidare, from Latin timidus ‘timid’.
