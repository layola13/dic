opposed:/əˈpoʊzd/ | adj. | 反对的，对抗的 | feeling or showing dislike or disapproval |  She was strongly opposed to the new policy. 她强烈反对这项新政策。|  against, contrary, hostile, averse |  in favor of, supporting, agreeing | adjective

opposed:/əˈpoʊzd/ | adj. | 截然不同的，对立的 | completely different |  They held opposed views on the subject. 他们在这一问题上持有截然不同的观点。| contrasting, differing, conflicting, dissimilar | similar, alike, same, identical | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *opposed, from Old French *opposé*, past participle of *opposer* (“to oppose”), from Latin *oppōnere* (“to set against, oppose”), from *ob* (“against, toward”) + *pōnere* (“to put, place”).
