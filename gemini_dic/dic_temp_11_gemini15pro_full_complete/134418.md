noninvinciblenesses:/ˌnɒnɪnˈvɪnsɪbəlɪsnɪsɪz/ | n. | 不可战胜性（的复数） |  The state or quality of not being invincible; vulnerability or susceptibility to defeat or harm.  This word emphasizes the multiple instances or aspects of vulnerability. |  His recent defeats have exposed his noninvinciblenesses. 他最近的失败暴露了他的弱点。| Synonyms: vulnerabilities, weaknesses, frailties | Antonyms: invincibility, impregnability | noun

Etymology:
From non- + invincible + -ness + -es. "Invincible" comes from Old French "invincible," from Latin "invincibilis," from "in-" (not) + "vincibilis" (conquerable), from "vincere" (to conquer).
