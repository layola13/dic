inoculate:/ɪˈnɒkjʊleɪt/ (UK) /ɪˈnɑːkjəleɪt/ (US)| vt.|接种，灌输|To introduce something, such as a serum, vaccine, or an idea, into the mind or body.|The doctor inoculated the children against measles. 医生给孩子们接种了麻疹疫苗。|近义词：vaccinate, immunize, inject, infuse|反义词: infect, contaminate|词性: verb

inoculation:/ɪˌnɒkjuˈleɪʃn/ (UK) /ɪˌnɑːkjuˈleɪʃn/ (US)| n.|接种，灌输|The act or process of inoculating.|The inoculation against polio has been very successful.  脊髓灰质炎疫苗接种非常成功。|近义词：vaccination, immunization, injection|反义词: infection, contamination|词性: noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin inoculātus, past participle of inoculāre  "to engraft, implant,"  from in- "in" + oculus "eye, bud" (figuratively, "a cutting").  Originally referred to grafting plants by inserting a bud from one plant into another. The medical sense of introducing a serum or vaccine developed later.
