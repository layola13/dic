acephalocyst: /ˌeɪsefˈæləsɪst/ /əˌsefələˈsɪst/ | n. | 无头蚴，无头囊虫 | A larval stage of certain tapeworms, especially *Echinococcus*, characterized by a large fluid-filled sac without a head or scolex.  It is the stage that typically forms hydatid cysts in the intermediate host. | The acephalocyst was found in the liver of the sheep.  这只羊的肝脏中发现了无头囊虫。 | Synonym: hydatid cyst | Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps the adult tapeworm) | noun
