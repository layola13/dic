antagonizes:/ænˈtæɡəˌnaɪzɪz/
|v.| 对抗；引起…的敌意；激怒|To act in opposition to; to provoke the hostility of.|His aggressive behavior antagonizes his coworkers. 他的侵略性行为激怒了他的同事。|irritates, provokes, alienates|appeases, conciliates|verb

antagonizing:/ænˈtæɡəˌnaɪzɪŋ/
|adj.|对抗性的；引起敌意的|Acting in opposition; provoking hostility.|His antagonizing remarks made everyone angry. 他对抗性的言论让每个人都很生气。|irritating, provoking|soothing, conciliating|adjective

antagonized:/ænˈtæɡəˌnaɪzd/
|adj.|被激怒的；敌对的|Feeling hostility or opposition as a result of being provoked.|She felt antagonized by his constant criticism. 她被他不断的批评激怒了。|irritated, offended|pleased, appeased|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *antagonizārī, from Ancient Greek ἀνταγωνίζεσθαι (antagōnízesthai) "to struggle against," from ἀντί- (antí-) "against" + ἀγωνίζεσθαι (agōnízesthai) "to contend for a prize," from ἀγών (agṓn) "contest."
