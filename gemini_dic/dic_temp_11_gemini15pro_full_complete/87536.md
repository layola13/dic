heterogeneity:/ˌhetərədʒəˈniːɪti/ | n. |异质性，多样性 |The heterogeneity of the group made it difficult to reach a consensus. |例句：The heterogeneity of the student population enriches campus life. 学生群体的多样性丰富了校园生活。

例句：The heterogeneity of the data made it challenging to analyze.  数据的异质性使其难以分析。|近义词：diversity, variety, dissimilarity, difference |反义词：homogeneity, uniformity, similarity, sameness |词性: noun

heterogeneous:/ˌhetərəˈdʒiːniəs/ | adj. |异质的，由不同成分组成的 | The city is a heterogeneous mix of cultures and ethnicities. |例句：The heterogeneous mixture was difficult to separate. 这种异质混合物很难分离。

例句：The study sample was heterogeneous, including people of different ages and backgrounds.  研究样本是异质的，包括不同年龄和背景的人。|近义词：diverse, varied, dissimilar, mixed |反义词：homogeneous, uniform, similar, unmixed |词性: adjective

词源: From Ancient Greek  ἕτερος (héteros, “other, different”) + γένος (génos, “kind, race, stock”).
