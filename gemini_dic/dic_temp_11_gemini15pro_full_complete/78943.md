girding:/ˈɡɜːrdɪŋ/ /ˈɡɜːrdɪŋ/| n. v.|准备，束腰，(尤指)准备行动|The act of girding or encircling something, especially with a belt or band; preparing oneself for action.|He was girding himself for a fight. 他正准备战斗。|近义词：preparing, bracing, equipping|反义词:relaxing, resting|词性:n./v.

girding:/ˈɡɜːrdɪŋ/ /ˈɡɜːrdɪŋ/| n. v.|腰带，带子|A belt or band worn around the waist.|The knight wore a girding of leather. 骑士戴着一条皮腰带。|近义词：belt, sash, girdle|反义词: N/A|词性:n.

词源: From Old English *gyrdan, from Proto-Germanic *gurdjaną, from Proto-Indo-European *gher- (“to enclose, grasp”). Cognate with Old Norse gyrða (“to gird”), Old High German gurtan (“to gird”), Latin hortus (“garden”).
