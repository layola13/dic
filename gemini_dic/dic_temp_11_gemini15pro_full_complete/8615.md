anticoherer:/æntiˈkoʊˈhɪərər/
noun
反相干器
A device used in early radio receivers to detect radio waves. It consisted of a tube containing loose metal filings, whose electrical resistance decreased when radio waves were applied, allowing a current to flow and activate a relay or other device.  It required a tap to restore its high resistance state.
The anticoherer was a key component in early wireless telegraphy. 反相干器是早期无线电报中的一个关键部件。
The anticoherer was superseded by more reliable detectors like the crystal detector. 反相干器被更可靠的探测器如晶体探测器所取代。
Synonyms:  radio wave detector, coherer restorer (though technically slightly different functions)
Antonyms: coherer (in terms of its electrical behavior during wave detection)
