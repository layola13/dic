arteries:/ˈɑːrtəriːz/ (US), /ˈɑːtəriz/ (UK)| n. |动脉|Blood vessels that carry oxygenated blood away from the heart to the rest of the body.|The arteries carry oxygen-rich blood from the heart.  动脉将富含氧气的血液从心脏输送到全身。|The surgeon repaired the damaged artery.  外科医生修复了受损的动脉。|近义词:blood vessels|反义词:veins|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English arterie, from Old French artaire, from Medieval Latin arteria, from Greek artēria, originally meaning "windpipe," later "artery," perhaps from airein "to raise" (as air). The original meaning reflects the ancient belief that arteries carried air.
