cunctator:/kʌŋkˈteɪtər/ | n. | 拖延者，迟缓者 |  Someone who delays or is slow to act.  Derived from the Latin verb *cunctari*, meaning "to delay." Famous example is Quintus Fabius Maximus Verrucosus, the Roman general given the agnomen "Cunctator" for his delaying tactics against Hannibal. |  The general, known as a cunctator, carefully planned every move.  这位将军以拖延战术闻名，他仔细地计划着每一步。 | synonyms: procrastinator, delayer, loiterer | antonyms: expediter, doer, go-getter | noun
