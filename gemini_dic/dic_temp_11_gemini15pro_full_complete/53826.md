disconnective:/ˌdɪskəˈnɛktɪv/
adjective
断开的，分离的
serving or tending to disconnect; disconnecting.  A disconnective conjunction signals a contrast or choice between two ideas.
The power outage caused a disconnective effect on the internet service for the entire neighborhood. 停电导致整个社区的互联网服务中断。
Logic gates perform disconnective operations, creating a break in the circuit. 逻辑门执行断开操作，在电路中产生断路。
近义词: disconnecting, separative, divisive
反义词: connective, joining, uniting
词性: adjective

disconnective:/ˌdɪskəˈnɛktɪv/
noun
断开词，分离词
A word or phrase that disconnects or separates clauses or sentences, such as "but," "or," "however."
"But" is a common disconnective used to contrast two ideas.  “但是”是用来对比两种想法的常见断开词。
The lecturer used disconnectives like "however" and "on the other hand" to signal shifts in argument. 演讲者使用“然而”和“另一方面”等断开词来表示论点的转变。
近义词: conjunction, adverb
反义词:  N/A (反义词通常不适用于语法术语)
词性: noun


词源:  From disconnect + -ive. Disconnect comes from the prefix dis- (meaning "apart" or "reversal") and connect (from Latin connectere, meaning "to bind together").
