divet:/ˈdaɪvɪt/| n. |跳水; (尤指从跳板上)跳入水中;  潜入; 猛冲; 俯冲|A sudden plunge into water, especially headfirst from a diving board or other height. A sharp swift descent; a swoop. |She took a deep breath and performed a perfect divet. 她深吸一口气，完成了一次完美的跳水。| The hawk made a sudden divet towards its prey.  老鹰突然俯冲向它的猎物。 |近义词：plunge, dive, leap |反义词: ascent, rise |词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *diven, from Old Norse *dȳfa ("to dive, dip, duck"), from Proto-Germanic *dūbijaną ("to dive, dip, duck"), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰewb- ("deep, hollow").  Cognate with Danish dykke ("to dive"), Swedish dyka ("to dive"), German tauchen ("to dive").  The noun form likely developed from the verb.
