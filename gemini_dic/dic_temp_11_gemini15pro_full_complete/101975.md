isocephalism: /ˌaɪsoʊˈsɛfəlɪzəm/ | n. | 等头式，等高人像式 |  The arrangement of figures in a composition, especially a frieze, so that the heads are approximately on the same level.  It is a common feature in classical art. |  The Parthenon frieze displays a remarkable degree of isocephalism, making the procession of figures appear unified and harmonious.  The artist employed isocephalism to create a sense of order and stability in the composition. | synonyms:  alignment, leveling | antonyms: disorder, asymmetry | noun
