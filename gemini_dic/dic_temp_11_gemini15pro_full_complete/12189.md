as-it-were:/ˌæz ɪt ˈwɜːr/
adverb
仿佛；可以说；好像
Used to indicate that a word or phrase is not exactly accurate but is close enough to the truth for current purposes.  It implies a slight reservation or hesitancy about the word or phrase used.
My brother, as it were, adopted the stray dog. 我的哥哥，可以说是收养了那只流浪狗。
Synonyms: so to speak, in a manner of speaking, sort of, kind of
Antonyms: literally, precisely, exactly
词性: 副词

PHRASE->
No real set phrases exist with "as it were," as it's a discourse marker. However, it's often used within sentences containing phrases like:

1. so to speak  (这么说)
He is, so to speak, the leader of the pack. 他可以说是这群人的领袖。

2. in a manner of speaking (可以说)
She is, in a manner of speaking, the boss.  她可以说是老板。

3. kind of/sort of (有点像，某种程度上)
He was, kind of, in charge. 他有点像负责人。
She felt, sort of, responsible. 她感到有点责任。

4.  you could say (你可以说)
He is, you could say, the life of the party. 你可以说他是派对的灵魂人物。

5. loosely speaking (粗略地说)
They are, loosely speaking, related. 他们粗略地说是有关系的。
