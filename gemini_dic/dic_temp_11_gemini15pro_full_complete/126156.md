muttered:/ˈmʌtərd/ (US), /ˈmʌtəd/ (UK)| v. |咕哝；低声说|To speak in a low, indistinct voice, often expressing discontent or uncertainty. |He muttered something about being late and hurried off. 他咕哝着说自己要迟到了，然后匆匆离去。|murmured, mumbled| shouted, yelled|verb

muttered:/ˈmʌtərd/ (US), /ˈmʌtəd/ (UK)| adj. |咕哝的；低声说的 (This is less common, usually used as past participle)|Spoken in a low, indistinct voice.|"Muttered complaints filled the room."  房间里充满了低声的抱怨。|low, quiet|loud, clear|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mutteren*, of uncertain origin. Perhaps imitative of the sound of muttering.
