autarchy:/ˈɔːtɑːrki/| n.|专制，独裁；自给自足|A system of government in which supreme power is concentrated in the hands of one person, whose decisions are subject to neither external legal restraints nor regularized mechanisms of popular control.  A country, state, or society that is economically independent.|North Korea is often described as an autarchy.| 近义词: dictatorship, despotism, absolutism, tyranny, totalitarianism, autocracy|反义词: democracy, republic|词性: noun

autarkic:/ɔːˈtɑːrkɪk/| adj.|专制的，独裁的；自给自足的|Relating to or denoting autarchy; self-sufficient.|The country aimed to become completely autarkic in food production.|近义词: self-sufficient, independent, autonomous|反义词: dependent, interconnected|词性: adjective

autarky:/ˈɔːtɑːrki/| n.|专制，独裁；自给自足|The same as autarchy. See above.|The small island nation achieved a state of autarky.|近义词: autarchy, self-sufficiency, independence|反义词: dependence, interdependence|词性: noun
