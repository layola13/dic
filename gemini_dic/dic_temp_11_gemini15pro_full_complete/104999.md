kenning:/ˈkɛnɪŋ/| n.|比喻, 隐喻|A kenning is a figure of speech, a type of circumlocution, that uses a compound expression in place of a more common noun, especially in Old Norse and Old English poetry.  It is a compressed metaphor, often a two-word phrase, that describes an object or person in a roundabout way.|The phrase "whale-road" is a kenning for the sea.|Synonym: metaphor, circumlocution, periphrasis|Antonym: literal expression|noun
