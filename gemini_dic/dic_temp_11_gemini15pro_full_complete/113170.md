词源：
From Middle English *lurchen, *lorken (“to lie hidden, skulk”), from Old North French *lurcher (“to tug, pull”), from Frankish *lurkjan (“to pull, drag”). Cognate with Dutch lurken (“to lurk”).  The original sense was to "move furtively," hence the modern sense of "sway suddenly."
