lay down the law:/leɪ daʊn ðə lɔː/
verb phrase
颁布法律；规定规则；下命令
To assert one's authority, typically by stating rules or commands in a forceful and uncompromising way.
The boss laid down the law about being late. 老板制定了关于迟到的规定。
My parents laid down the law about having friends over. 我父母制定了关于带朋友回家的规矩。
Synonyms: dictate, decree, command, order, impose, prescribe
Antonyms: negotiate, compromise, suggest, request

lay down the law:/leɪ daʊn ðə lɔː/
verb phrase
中文翻译：发号施令；严加管束；下命令
英文解析：To tell people what they must do in a forceful way, like a strict rule or law.  It implies an authoritarian and often inflexible approach.
例句：
The coach laid down the law about training schedules, and no one dared to question him. 教练对训练时间做了规定，没有人敢质疑他。

She laid down the law to her children about their screen time. 她给孩子们规定了使用电子屏幕的时间。

近义词：dictate, decree, command, order, impose
反义词：negotiate, compromise, suggest, request
