myxocystodea:/ˌmɪksoʊsɪˈstoʊdiə/ | n. | 粘菌纲 | A class of slime molds characterized by the formation of a fruiting body containing spores embedded in a mass of hardened slime.  Formerly considered to be fungi, they are now classified as protists. |  Myxocystodea are fascinating organisms that exhibit both animal-like and fungal-like characteristics. 粘菌纲是兼具动物和真菌特征的迷生物种。 |  Synonyms: Myxomycetes, slime molds | Antonyms: None (no direct opposite for a biological classification) | Noun
