inadvisably:/ˌɪnədˈvaɪzəbli/| adv.|不明智地，不妥当地|In a way that is not wise or sensible; unwisely.|He inadvisably invested all his money in one stock. 他不明智地把所有钱都投资在一支股票上。|She inadvisably chose to ignore her doctor's advice. 她不明智地选择了无视医生的建议。|unwisely, foolishly, imprudently|wisely, prudently|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From in- (not) + advisable + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Advisable" comes from the Old French "avisable," meaning "worthy of being advised," ultimately from Latin "ad-" (to) + "visum" (past participle of "videre," to see).
