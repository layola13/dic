nuraghe:/nuˈraːɡe/ | n. |努拉吉|A type of megalithic edifice found in Sardinia, Italy, from the Bronze Age.  These are truncated conical towers built of dry-laid stones. |Example:  The nuraghe of Barumini is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.  巴鲁米努的努拉吉是联合国教科文组织世界遗产。|Example: Archaeologists are still studying the purpose of the nuraghe. 考古学家仍在研究努拉吉的用途。|Synonym: megalith, tower|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps modern building) | noun
