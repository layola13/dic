hyperalert:/ˌhaɪpərəˈlɜːrt/| adj.|高度警觉的，过度警觉的|Extremely alert and watchful; unusually sensitive to stimuli.|The soldier on night watch was hyperalert, scanning the perimeter for any sign of movement.|近义词：watchful, vigilant, observant, attentive|反义词:inattentive, oblivious, unaware|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From hyper- + alert.  "Hyper-" is a prefix meaning "over, above, beyond," derived from Greek "hyper" (over, beyond). "Alert" comes from Italian "all'erta" (on the watch), from "erta" (watch), ultimately from Latin "erigere" (to erect, raise).
