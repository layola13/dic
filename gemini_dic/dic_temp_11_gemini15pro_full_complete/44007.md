criminaloid:/ˈkrɪmɪnəlɔɪd/ | n. | 犯罪类人物；准犯罪人 | A person who projects a respectable, law-abiding image, but engages in criminal behavior, often of a white-collar nature.  They operate on the fringes of legality, exploiting loopholes and technicalities to avoid prosecution. |  The CEO, though never convicted, seemed like a criminaloid, always pushing ethical boundaries. 首席执行官虽然从未被定罪，但看起来像个准犯罪人，总是挑战道德底线。 |  Near synonyms:  near-do-well,  rascal, rogue, scoundrel | Antonyms: saint, law-abiding citizen | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From *criminal* + *-oid*  (*-oid*  meaning "resembling" or "like").  Coined by the Italian criminologist Cesare Lombroso in the late 19th century.
