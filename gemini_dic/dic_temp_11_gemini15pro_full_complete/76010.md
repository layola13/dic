furciferous:/fɜːrˈsɪfərəs/| adj.|叉状的，分叉的|Having a forked or branching shape, resembling a fork.  Derived from Latin "furca" (fork) and "-ferous" (bearing).|The furciferous branches of the lightning bolt illuminated the sky.  叉状的闪电照亮了天空。|Synonyms: forked, branched, bifurcated|Antonyms: straight, unbranched|adjective

furciferous:/fɜːrˈsɪfərəs/| adj.|（俚语）争吵的，爱吵架的| (Informal, humorous) Quarrelsome, argumentative. This usage likely stems from the image of a forked tongue, often associated with snakes and deceit, and by extension, arguments.|He had a reputation for being furciferous and difficult to get along with.  他以好争吵且难以相处而闻名。|Synonyms: quarrelsome, argumentative, contentious|Antonyms: agreeable, amiable, peaceable|adjective
