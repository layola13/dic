convictively:/kənˈvɪktɪvli/ | adv. | 令人信服地，有说服力地 |  In a manner that produces or tends to produce conviction or belief. |  She argued her case convictively, swaying the jury to her side. 她令人信服地陈述了她的案情，说服了陪审团支持她。|  persuasively, convincingly, compellingly | unconvincingly, weakly | adverb

Etymology:
From convict (verb) + -ive (adjective suffix) + -ly (adverb suffix).  The verb "convict" comes from the Latin "convincere," meaning "to overcome, conquer, prove wrong."
