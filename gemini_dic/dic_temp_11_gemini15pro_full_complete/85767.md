haybote:/ˈheɪboʊt/| n.|拾柴权；为修理房屋和篱笆而砍伐树木的权利|The tenant had the right of haybote on the landlord's estate.  房客在房东的庄园里拥有拾柴权。|The ancient deed granted haybote, allowing the tenant to gather wood for repairs.  这份古老的契约授予了拾柴权，允许租户收集木材进行维修。|近义词：estovers, right of common|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

Etymology->
From Anglo-French *heybote, from Old French *hesbote, from Frankish *hagibōt (“hedge-repairing”), from *hag (“hedge, enclosure”) + *bōt (“remedy, improvement, profit”). Cognate with Dutch haag (hedge), German Hag (hedge).  The second element is related to Old English bōt (“remedy, help, advantage, profit”), from Proto-Germanic *bōtiz (“improvement, betterment”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰedʰ- (“to dig”).
