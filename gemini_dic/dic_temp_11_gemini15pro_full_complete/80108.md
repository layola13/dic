gobbling:/ˈɡɒblɪŋ/ (US) /ˈɡɒblɪŋ/ (UK)| v. |狼吞虎咽；贪婪地抓住|The act of eating quickly and greedily. |He was gobbling down his dinner. 他正狼吞虎咽地吃着晚饭。|devouring, guzzling, wolfing down|savoring, nibbling|verb
gobbling:/ˈɡɒblɪŋ/ (US) /ˈɡɒblɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |(火鸡等的)咯咯叫声|The characteristic sound made by a turkey. |The gobbling of the turkeys could be heard across the farm. 火鸡的咯咯叫声在整个农场都能听到。|clucking, chirping|silence|noun
gobbling:/ˈɡɒblɪŋ/ (US) /ˈɡɒblɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |贪婪的；狼吞虎咽的|Characterized by eating quickly and greedily. |He had a gobbling appetite. 他胃口很大。|voracious, ravenous|moderate|adjective

Etymology: From the verb "gobble," which is of imitative origin, echoing the sound made by turkeys.
