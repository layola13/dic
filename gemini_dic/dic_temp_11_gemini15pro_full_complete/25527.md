bullrush:/ˈbʊlˌrʌʃ/ | n. | 香蒲，灯心草 | A tall marsh plant (Typha latifolia or T. angustifolia) having long flat leaves and a dark brown, compact, cylindrical head of many minute flowers. |  We saw bullrushes growing along the edge of the lake. 我们看到湖边长着香蒲。| Synonyms: reedmace, cattail, bulrush | Antonyms: N/A | noun

bullrush:/ˈbʊlˌrʌʃ/ | vt. | 急速通过，冲过 | To force or hurry (someone) into doing something.  | The police bullrushed the suspect into confessing. 警察迫使嫌疑人招供。 | Synonyms: hustle, rush, hurry, push | Antonyms: delay, stall, procrastinate | verb
