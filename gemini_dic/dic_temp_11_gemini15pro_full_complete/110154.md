ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lēof, from Proto-Germanic *lēubaz (“dear, beloved, agreeable”), from Proto-Indo-European *lewbʰ- (“to care, desire, love”). Cognate with Old Frisian liāf (“dear, beloved”), Old Saxon liof, Old High German liob (German lieb), Old Norse ljūfr (“pleasant, sweet, agreeable”), Gothic 𐌻𐌹𐌿𐍆𐍃 (liufs, “dear, beloved”). More at love.
