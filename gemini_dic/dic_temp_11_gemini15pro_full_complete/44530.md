crosstown:/ˈkrɒsˈtaʊn/ /ˈkrɑːsˈtaʊn/| adj.  adv.|跨城的，横穿城市的；|Relating to or involving travel across a city.|The crosstown bus is always crowded.  跨城巴士总是很拥挤。|近义词：citywide, interurban|反义词:  local, neighborhood|词性: adjective, adverb

Etymology:  "cross" + "town"  The word "cross" comes from Old English "cros" and ultimately from Proto-Germanic "krusaz" and Proto-Indo-European "*kreu-".  "Town" comes from Old English "tun,"  meaning an enclosure or settlement.
