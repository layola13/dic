espadrilles:/ˌɛspəˈdriːlz/
n.
帆布鞋, 草编鞋
Lightweight canvas shoes with rope soles, originating in the Pyrenees.
She wore a pair of bright red espadrilles. 她穿着一双鲜红色的帆布鞋。

espadrilles:/ˌɛspəˈdriːlz/
n.
便鞋
A type of casual shoe, typically made of canvas or cotton fabric and having a flexible sole made of rope or rubber.
He packed his espadrilles for his beach vacation. 他把帆布鞋打包好，准备去海滩度假。


Synonyms:
plimsolls, sneakers, canvas shoes, flats

Antonyms:
boots, heels, formal shoes

Word origin:
From Catalan *espardenya*, from esparto, a type of grass used to make rope.
