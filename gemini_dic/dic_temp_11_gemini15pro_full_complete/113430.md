lymphomas:/lɪmˈfoʊməz/ | n. | 淋巴瘤 |  A type of cancer that affects the lymphatic system, which is part of the body's germ-fighting network. |  Example:  Hodgkin's and non-Hodgkin's lymphomas are the main types. 霍奇金淋巴瘤和非霍奇金淋巴瘤是主要类型。| Example:  Lymphomas can develop in any part of the body where lymph tissue is found. 淋巴瘤可以在身体任何有淋巴组织的地方发生。 | Synonym: lymphoid cancers,  lymphatic cancers | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a disease) | Noun

Etymology:  From Modern Latin lympha (lymph) + -oma (tumor, swelling)
