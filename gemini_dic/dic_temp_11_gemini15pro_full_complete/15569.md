bagwig: /ˈbæɡwɪɡ/ | n. | 包式假发 | A wig having the back hair enclosed in a bag of silk.  A large, curled wig, worn chiefly by men in the 17th and 18th centuries, with the bottom part gathered and tied at the nape of the neck and often enclosed in a silk bag. |  The judge wore a ceremonial bagwig. 法官戴着仪式用的包式假发。| synonyms: wig, periwig | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps natural hair) | noun
