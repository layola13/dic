nitpicking: /ˈnɪtˌpɪkɪŋ/ | n. | 吹毛求疵；挑剔；找茬儿 | The act of finding faults in details that are minor or unimportant. |  He's always nitpicking about my work. 他总是对我的工作吹毛求疵。 |  carping, caviling, faultfinding, quibbling | meticulousness, thoroughness | noun

Etymology:  From nit + pick, meaning to pick out nits (lice eggs) from hair, a tedious and meticulous task.
