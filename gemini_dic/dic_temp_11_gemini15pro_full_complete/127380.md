neanderthal:/niˈændərˌθɔːl/ /niˈændərˌtɑːl/ | n. |尼安德特人；(尤指)思想僵化的人| An extinct species of human that was widely distributed in ice-age Europe between *c*. 120,000–35,000 years ago, with a receding forehead and prominent brow ridges. The Neanderthals were associated with the Mousterian flint industry of the Middle Paleolithic. Can also be used figuratively to describe someone who is perceived as outdated or unintelligent. |例句：Neanderthals lived in Europe during the last Ice Age. 尼安德特人生活在欧洲的最后一个冰河时代。

例句：He called him a Neanderthal for refusing to use a computer.  他因为拒绝使用电脑而称他为尼安德特人。|近义词：caveman, hominid |反义词:modern human|词性:noun
