emancipated: /ɪˈmænsɪpeɪtɪd/
adjective
解放了的，摆脱束缚的
Free from legal, social, or political restrictions; liberated.
例句：The emancipated slaves started new lives in the North.  获得解放的奴隶们在北方开始了新生活。

例句：She felt emancipated after leaving her controlling parents.  离开控制欲强的父母后，她感到获得了自由。

近义词：liberated, freed, unrestrained, independent
反义词：constrained, restricted, enslaved, dependent
词性：adjective

emancipate: /ɪˈmænsɪpeɪt/
verb
解放，释放
Set free, especially from legal, social, or political restrictions.
例句：Lincoln emancipated the slaves. 林肯解放了奴隶。

例句：The new law emancipated women from the constraints of outdated customs.  新法律将妇女从过时的习俗的束缚中解放出来。

近义词：liberate, free, release
反义词：enslave, constrain, restrict
词性：verb

emancipation: /ɪˌmænsɪˈpeɪʃn/
noun
解放，释放
The fact or process of being set free from legal, social, or political restrictions; liberation.
例句：The emancipation of the serfs marked a turning point in Russian history.  农奴的解放标志着俄罗斯历史的一个转折点。

例句：She celebrated her emancipation from a toxic relationship. 她庆祝自己从一段有害的感情中解脱出来。

近义词：liberation, freedom, release
反义词：enslavement, constraint, restriction
词性：noun


词源: From Latin emancipatus, past participle of emancipare (“to free from paternal power, set free, liberate”), from e (“ex, out of”) + mancipium (“ownership, legal ownership of a person, slave”).
