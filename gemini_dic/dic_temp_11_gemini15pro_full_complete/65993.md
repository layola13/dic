exalts:/ɪɡˈzɔːlts/| v.| 提升，赞扬，使兴奋 | To raise in rank, power, or character; to praise highly; to glorify. | The king exalted his loyal knight to a higher position. 国王将他的忠诚骑士提升到更高的职位。

The poem exalts the beauty of nature. 这首诗赞美了大自然的美丽。| 近义词：praise, elevate, glorify, commend | 反义词：debase, degrade, condemn, criticize | 词性：动词

exalted:/ɪɡˈzɔːltɪd/ | adj. | 高贵的，崇高的，兴奋的 | Raised in rank, character, or status; noble; dignified. |  She felt an exalted sense of joy after winning the competition. 赢得比赛后，她感到无比喜悦。

He holds an exalted position in the company. 他在公司里地位显赫。 | 近义词：noble, lofty, elevated, dignified | 反义词：lowly, humble, base, degraded | 词性：形容词

exaltation:/ˌɛɡzɔːlˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 提升，赞扬，兴奋 | The act of exalting or the state of being exalted; elation. | The exaltation of the new king was a grand ceremony. 新国王的登基仪式非常隆重。

She felt a sense of exaltation after finishing the marathon. 完成马拉松后，她感到一阵兴奋。| 近义词：elevation, glorification, praise, elation | 反义词：degradation, humiliation, condemnation | 词性：名词


词源: From Old French *exalter, from Latin exaltare "to lift up, elevate," from ex- "up" + altus "high."
