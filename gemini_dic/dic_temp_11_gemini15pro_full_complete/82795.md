guineafowls: /ˈɡɪnɪˌfaʊlz/ | n. |珍珠鸡|A bird of the family Numididae, native to Africa, characterized by spotted plumage and a fleshy wattles or helmet on the head.  They are often domesticated for their meat and eggs. | We saw a flock of guineafowls foraging in the field. 我们看到一群珍珠鸡在田里觅食。|The guineafowls made a loud, chattering noise. 珍珠鸡发出大声的喋喋不休的声音。|近义词：fowl, poultry |反义词: None |词性: Noun

词源: From *guinea fowl*, from *Guinea* + *fowl*.  Originally referring to birds imported from the Guinea coast of West Africa.
