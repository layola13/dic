montem: /ˈmɒntem/ | n. |  a procession of schoolboys begging for money, especially formerly in England |  A traditional custom where students would go out and ask for money, often for charitable purposes or school activities.  | Example: The annual montem at Eton College was a grand spectacle. 伊顿公学的年度募捐游行是一场盛大的场面。 | Example:  The boys participating in the montem wore elaborate costumes. 参加募捐游行的男孩们穿着精致的服装。 | Synonym:  fundraiser, collection | Antonym: donation (arguably, as montem is *soliciting* funds, while a donation is *giving* funds) | noun
