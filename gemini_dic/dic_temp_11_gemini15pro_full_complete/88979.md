holinesses:/ˈhoʊlinɪsɪz/
n.
神圣, 圣洁(复数)
The state of being holy; sanctity.  The holinesses of various saints are celebrated in the church. 教堂庆祝各种圣徒的神圣。
The holinesses attributed to the ancient relics drew pilgrims from afar.  归因于古代遗物的神圣性吸引了远方的朝圣者。
sanctity, sacredness, piety
profane, impiety
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *holynesse*, from Old English *hālignes*, from *hālig* (“holy”) + *-nes* (suffix forming abstract nouns of quality).  Cognate with Scots *haliness* (“holiness”), West Frisian *hilligens* (“holiness”), Dutch *heiligheid* (“holiness, sanctity”), German *Heiligkeit* (“holiness, sanctity”), Swedish *helighet* (“holiness, sanctity”).
