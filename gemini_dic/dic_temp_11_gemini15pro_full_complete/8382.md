anti-regulatory:/ˌæntiˈreɡjələtɔːri/
anti-regulatory| adj.| 反监管的，反对监管的| Opposing or intended to reduce or eliminate government regulation, especially in business or industry.| The politician ran on an anti-regulatory platform, promising to cut red tape for businesses.  这位政治家以反监管的纲领竞选，承诺减少企业的繁文缛节。|Example: Many businesses support anti-regulatory policies. 许多企业支持反监管政策。|Synonyms: deregulationist, laissez-faire, non-interventionist|Antonyms: regulatory, interventionist, pro-regulation|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix): from Greek anti- "against, opposite of"
regulatory (adjective): from regulate + -ory. Regulate from Late Latin regulatus, past participle of regulari "to control by rule, direct," from Latin regula "rule."
