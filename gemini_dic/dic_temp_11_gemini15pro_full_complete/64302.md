equivocacy:/ɪˈkwɪvəkəsi/ | n. | 模棱两可；含糊其辞；歧义 | The equivocacy of the politician's statement made it impossible to determine his true position.  这位政客的声明模棱两可，让人无法确定他的真实立场。| Example: The lawyer used equivocacy to confuse the witness. 律师用模棱两可的话语迷惑证人。 | Synonyms: ambiguity, vagueness, double entendre, prevarication | Antonyms: clarity, precision, directness | noun

equivocate:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪt/ | v. | 含糊其辞；支吾其词 |  He equivocated when asked about his involvement in the scandal. 当被问及他与丑闻的关系时，他含糊其辞。| Example:  She equivocated about her plans for the weekend. 她对周末的计划含糊其辞。 | Synonyms: prevaricate, hedge, evade, fudge | Antonyms: be forthright, be direct, speak plainly | verb
