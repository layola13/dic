coalface:/ˈkəʊlfeɪs/ (UK) /ˈkoʊlfeɪs/ (US)| n. |煤层; (尤指)最艰苦的工作场所; |The part of a coal mine where the coal is cut from the seam.  Figuratively, it refers to any challenging or demanding work environment, especially one where the most difficult or unpleasant tasks are performed. |The miners worked long hours at the coalface. 矿工们在煤层工作很长时间。|  He's a politician who rarely leaves Westminster and never sees the coalface of real life. 他是一个很少离开威斯敏斯特、从未见过现实生活艰辛的政治家。|近义词:mine, front line, trenches|反义词:headquarters, office|词性:名词
