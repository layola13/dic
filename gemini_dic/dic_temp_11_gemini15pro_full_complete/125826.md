murphy:/ˈmɜːrfi/ (US) /ˈmɜːfi/ (UK)| n.|墨菲; 墨菲床; 马铃薯; |An Irish surname, anglicized from Ó Murchadha. Also used, often humorously, to refer to Murphy's law.|Murphy's law states that anything that can go wrong will go wrong. 墨菲定律指出，任何可能出错的事情都会出错。|The Murphys are coming over for dinner tonight. 墨菲一家今晚要过来吃晚饭。|synonyms: potato, spud (for potato); difficulty, problem, snag (for Murphy's law)|antonyms: solution, ease (for Murphy's law)|noun
