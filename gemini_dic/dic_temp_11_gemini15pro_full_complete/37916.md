complementing:/ˈkɒmplɪmentɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkɑːmplɪmentɪŋ/| v. |补充；完善；使更具吸引力|Adding something to something else in a way that improves or enhances it, or makes it more attractive or effective. |Her scarf complemented her outfit perfectly. 她的围巾与她的服装完美搭配。|Synonyms: enhancing, improving, perfecting, supplementing|Antonyms: clashing, detracting, worsening|Verb

complementing:/ˈkɒmplɪmentɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkɑːmplɪmentɪŋ/| adj. |补充的；完善的|Serving to complete or make perfect.|The complementing colors made the painting vibrant. 互补的色彩使这幅画充满活力。|Synonyms: supplementary, completing|Antonyms: detracting, clashing|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin complementum "that which fills up or completes," from Latin complere "to fill up, complete," from com- "together" + plere "to fill."  The meaning shifted from "completing" to "expressing praise or admiration" in the 17th century.
