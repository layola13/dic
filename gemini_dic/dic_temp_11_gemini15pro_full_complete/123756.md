moored:/mʊrd/ | adj. | 停泊的，系泊的 | secured in place with ropes or chains, typically a boat | The boat was moored to the dock.  这艘船停泊在码头。 | synonyms: anchored, docked, berthed | antonyms: adrift, afloat | adjective

moored:/mʊrd/ | v. | 停泊，系泊（moor的过去式和过去分词）| to secure (a boat) in place with ropes or chains | We moored the yacht in the harbor. 我们把游艇停泊在港口。 | synonyms: anchored, docked, berthed | antonyms: unmoor, cast off | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *moren, from Old Norse *mōra (“to delay, hinder, moor a ship”), from Proto-Germanic *mōrijaną (“to hinder, delay, moor”).  Cognate with Old English mōrian (“to moor”), Old Frisian mōria (“to hinder, moor”), Dutch meren (“to moor”), German mauern (“to wall up”).
