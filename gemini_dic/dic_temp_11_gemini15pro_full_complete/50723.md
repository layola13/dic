derogate:/ˈdɛrəˌɡeɪt/ /ˈdɪrəˌɡeɪt/
verb
贬低，损害
to cause something to seem less important or valuable
He derogated his colleagues by calling them incompetent. 他称同事们不称职，贬低了他们。

derogate:/ˈdɛrəˌɡeɪt/ /ˈdɪrəˌɡeɪt/
verb
违反，背离（法律、习俗等）(Formal, less common)
to deviate from a rule, custom, etc.
Such conduct derogates from acceptable social norms. 这种行为违背了可接受的社会规范。

derogate:/dɪˈrɒɡət(ə)ri/ /dɪˈrɑːɡətɔːri/
adjective
贬损的，诽谤的
showing a critical or disrespectful attitude
He made derogatory remarks about her appearance. 他对她的外貌发表了贬损的言论。


Etymology:
From Late Latin *dērogātus, past participle of dērogāre (“to repeal partly, diminish, detract from”), from dē- (“from, away from”) + rogāre (“to ask, propose a law”).

Synonyms (verb):
belittle, disparage, diminish, detract from, depreciate

Antonyms (verb):
enhance, elevate, praise, extol, laud

Synonyms (adjective):
disparaging, belittling, denigrating, uncomplimentary, pejorative

Antonyms (adjective):
complimentary, flattering, praising, laudatory
