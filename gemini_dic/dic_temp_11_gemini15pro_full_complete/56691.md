doom-and-glooms:/ˈduːm ən ˈɡluːmz/
n.|悲观主义者，悲观情绪|Pessimists, or an atmosphere of pessimism and negativity.  It refers to people who constantly predict or focus on negative outcomes.|The meeting was filled with doom-and-glooms who predicted the company's imminent failure.|近义词:pessimists, naysayers, killjoys|反义词:optimists, positive thinkers|词性:名词

词源:  Compound formed from "doom" (Old English *dōm "judgment, statute, law; destiny, fate") and "gloom" (Middle English gloumen "to become dark or dim," of uncertain origin).  The pairing emphasizes the negativity of both concepts.
