outreddened:/ˌaʊtˈrɛdənd/
|adj.|
|比...更红，使...相形见绌|
|Made redder than something else; surpassed in redness.  It implies a more intense or striking redness than the object of comparison.|
|The sunset outreddened the vibrant hues of the painter's palette.|夕阳比画家调色板上的鲜艳色彩更红。
|The maple leaves in autumn outreddened the nearby Virginia creeper.|秋天的枫叶比附近的弗吉尼亚爬山虎更红。
|surpassed, exceeded, outshone|
|paler, less vibrant|
|adjective|

ETYMOLOGY->
From "out-" (a prefix meaning exceeding or surpassing) + "redden" (to make or become red). "Redden" comes from the Old English "read" (red) + "-en" (a suffix forming verbs).
