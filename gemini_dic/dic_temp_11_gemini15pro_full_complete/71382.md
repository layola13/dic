flagitation:/ˌflædʒɪˈteɪʃən/
|n.|鞭打；鞭挞；责难；恳求|The act of whipping or flogging; severe criticism or denunciation; earnest entreaty or supplication.|He endured flagitation as punishment for his crimes. 他因罪行遭受了鞭打。|近义词：flogging, whipping, castigation, condemnation, supplication|反义词: praise, commendation|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin flagitatio, from Latin flagitare ("to demand urgently, importune"), frequentative of flagrare ("to burn").  The sense development is from "demanding repeatedly" to "insisting violently" to "flogging."
