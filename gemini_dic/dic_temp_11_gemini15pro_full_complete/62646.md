enfranchise:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ | vt. | 给予选举权；赋予权力；解放 | To grant the right to vote; to give someone the freedom to do something. | The Nineteenth Amendment enfranchised women in the United States.  第十九修正案赋予美国妇女选举权。

The company's new policy enfranchises employees to work from home. 公司的新政策赋予员工在家工作的自由。 | 近义词: empower, liberate, free | 反义词: disenfranchise, restrict, oppress | 词性: 动词

enfranchises:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzɪz/ | vt. | 给予选举权；赋予权力；解放 (第三人称单数) | Third person singular present tense form of "enfranchise," meaning to grant the right to vote; to give someone the freedom to do something. |  The new law enfranchises all citizens over the age of 18. 新法律赋予所有18岁以上的公民选举权。

He believes that education enfranchises people to make better choices. 他相信教育能赋予人们做出更好选择的能力。 | 近义词: empowers, liberates, frees | 反义词: disenfranchises, restricts, oppresses | 词性: 动词
