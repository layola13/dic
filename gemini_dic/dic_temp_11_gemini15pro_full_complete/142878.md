out-of-pocket:/ˌaʊt əv ˈpɒkɪt/ (UK) /ˌaʊt əv ˈpɑːkɪt/ (US)| adj.|自掏腰包的；实际支出的|involving personal expense; paid for with one's own money.|例句：The insurance company covered most of the costs, but I still had some out-of-pocket expenses.  保险公司承担了大部分费用，但我仍然有一些自付费用。

例句：Medical expenses can be very high, so it's important to have good insurance to reduce your out-of-pocket costs. 医疗费用可能很高，因此拥有良好的保险以减少自付费用非常重要。|近义词：unexpended, uncompensated, personal expense |反义词: reimbursed, covered, compensated|词性:形容词


out-of-pocket:/ˌaʊt əv ˈpɒkɪt/ (UK) /ˌaʊt əv ˈpɑːkɪt/ (US)| n.|自付费用|expenses paid from one's own money.|例句：Her out-of-pocket for the trip was minimal. 她这次旅行的自付费用很少。

例句：I keep all my receipts so I can calculate my total out-of-pocket. 我保留所有收据，以便计算我的总自付费用。|近义词：expenses, costs |反义词: reimbursement, compensation|词性:名词

词源:  This phrase originates from the idea of having to reach into one's own pocket to pay for something, as opposed to having it covered by another source.  它源于必须把手伸进自己的口袋里付钱的想法，而不是由其他来源支付。
