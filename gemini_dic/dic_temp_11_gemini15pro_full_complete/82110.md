groggiest:/ˈɡrɑːɡiɪst/| adj.|最昏昏沉沉的，最迷糊的|Superlative form of groggy: most groggy.  It describes someone experiencing the highest degree of drowsiness, disorientation, and unsteadiness.|Example: After the surgery, I felt groggiest I've ever been.  手术后，我感觉是我这辈子最昏昏沉沉的时候。|Example: He was the groggiest of the bunch after the long flight.  长途飞行之后，他是那一群人中最迷糊的。|近义词：sleepiest, most dazed, most confused|反义词: most alert, most awake|词性:形容词

groggy:/ˈɡrɑːɡi/| adj.|昏昏沉沉的，迷糊的|Dazed, weak, or unsteady, especially from lack of sleep, intoxication, or illness.|Example: I felt groggy after taking the medication.  服药后我感到昏昏沉沉的。|Example:  She was still groggy from the anesthetic. 她麻醉后仍然昏昏沉沉。|近义词：dazed, sleepy, woozy|反义词: alert, awake|词性:形容词

词源:
Likely a variant of the earlier word "groggy," which referred to a sailor's unsteady gait caused by drinking too much "grog" (a mixture of rum and water).
