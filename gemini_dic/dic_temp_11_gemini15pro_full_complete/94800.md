impel:/ɪmˈpɛl/ (US), /ɪmˈpel/ (UK)| vt.|推动；驱使；激励|To drive or urge forward; to motivate or stimulate to action.|His conscience impelled him to confess. 他的良心驱使他坦白。|synonyms: urge, drive, compel, force, motivate, prompt|antonyms: restrain, hinder, discourage|verb

impulse:/ˈɪmpʌls/ (US), /ˈɪmpʌls/ (UK)| n.|冲动；推动；刺激；脉冲|A sudden, strong and unreflective urge or desire to act; a driving or motivating force; a sudden surge of electrical or other energy.|She bought the dress on impulse. 她一时冲动买了那条裙子。|synonyms: urge, whim, fancy, motivation, stimulus|antonyms: hesitation, deliberation, premeditation|noun
