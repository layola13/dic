modulation:/ˌmɒdʒʊˈleɪʃn/ |/ˌmɑːdʒəˈleɪʃn/| n.|调制；调节；变调|the process of varying the characteristics of a carrier wave, such as its amplitude, frequency, or phase, in order to transmit information.|The modulation of the radio signal allowed for the transmission of music.|近义词：variation, adjustment, alteration|反义词：constancy, uniformity|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *modulatio, from Latin modulor (“to measure rhythmically, regulate, tune”), from modulus (“measure, rhythm”).
