appreciation: /əˌpriːʃiˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 欣赏; 感激; 理解; 评估; 增值 | An increase in value over time. The act of recognizing the good qualities of something.  Understanding of a situation and realizing why it is important.  A feeling of thankfulness. |  I expressed my appreciation for her help. 我对她的帮助表示感谢。 The painting has increased in value due to market appreciation. 由于市场升值，这幅画的价值有所增加。 He has a deep appreciation for classical music. 他对古典音乐有着深刻的欣赏。  My appreciation of the situation is that we need to act quickly. 我对形势的理解是，我们需要迅速采取行动。 | Synonyms: gratitude, recognition, understanding, valuation, increase, rise | Antonyms: depreciation, ingratitude, disregard, criticism, decline, fall | noun

词源:  Late Middle English: from late Latin appretiatio(n-), from the verb appretiare ‘set a price on’, from ad- ‘to’ + pretium ‘price’.  The original sense was ‘estimation of the worth of something’, later ‘recognition of the value of something’.
