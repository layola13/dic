mads:/mædz/ | adj. | 发疯的；疯狂的；着迷的；非常恼火的 | insane; extremely angry; very enthusiastic |  He was mad with grief. 他悲痛欲绝。| 近义词: crazy, insane, furious, passionate | 反义词: sane, rational, calm | 词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *mad, from Old English ġemād (“foolish, insane”), from Proto-Germanic *gamadaz (“foolish, stunned”). Cognate with Scots mad (“mad”), West Frisian mâd (“tired, exhausted”), Dutch mat (“faint, weak, checkmated”), German matt (“checkmated, faint, exhausted”). More at mate.  The current sense developed from the original meaning of "foolish" or "insane".
