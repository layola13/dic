aggrievedness:/əˈɡriːvdnɪs/| n. |委屈，冤屈，愤懑|The state of feeling wronged or unjustly treated.|Her aggrievedness was palpable after the unfair decision.  她受到不公平的待遇后，委屈之情溢于言表。|Synonyms: resentment, indignation, umbrage|Antonyms: contentment, satisfaction|noun


Etymology:
From aggrieved + -ness.  Aggrieved comes from the Old French agrever, meaning "to aggravate, make worse," ultimately from Latin gravare, "to burden." The suffix -ness forms nouns denoting a state or quality.
