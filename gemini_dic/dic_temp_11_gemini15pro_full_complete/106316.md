koch:/kɔːx/ | n. | 科赫 | Robert Koch (1843-1910), a German physician and microbiologist. He is considered one of the main founders of modern bacteriology. He identified the specific causative agents of tuberculosis, cholera, and anthrax and gave experimental support for the concept of infectious disease. | Robert Koch discovered the bacterium that causes tuberculosis. 罗伯特·科赫发现了导致肺结核的细菌。| synonyms: none (as it's a proper noun) | antonyms: none (as it's a proper noun) | proper noun

koch:/kɔːx/ | n. | (烹飪)燉菜 | A stew or casserole, often containing meat and vegetables. (Primarily used in German and other European languages.) |  We had a delicious koch for dinner last night.  昨晚我们吃了一顿美味的炖菜。| synonyms: stew, casserole | antonyms: none | noun
