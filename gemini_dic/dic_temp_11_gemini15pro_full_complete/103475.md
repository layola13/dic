jobbish:/ˈdʒɒbɪʃ/| adj.|庸俗的，平庸的，缺乏想象力的|Characteristic of or resembling a job; mundane, unimaginative, concerned with routine or small details.  Often used to describe writing or art that is uninspired and formulaic.|His latest novel was criticized for being derivative and jobbish.|Synonyms: pedestrian, commonplace, unimaginative, humdrum, prosaic, banal, trite, hackneyed, stale, formulaic|Antonyms: imaginative, creative, original, inspired, innovative|adjective
