Suffix: -inent
Meaning:  Prominent, projecting, extending outwards.  Often implies a quality that is readily apparent or noticeable.
Origin: Latin, "-inens," present participle of "inere" (to go in).  Related to the more common suffix "-ent."
Etymology: From Latin *inens (present participle of ineo (“I enter, begin, commence”)), from in (“in, into, on, upon, against”) + eo (“I go”).
