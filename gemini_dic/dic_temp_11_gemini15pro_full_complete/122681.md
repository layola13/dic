modulus:/ˈmɒdʒʊləs/ (US) /ˈmɑːdʒələs/| n. |模数，系数，模量|A quantity by which two given quantities can be divided to leave the same remainder; the absolute value of a complex number.  A number by which another number is to be divided. In general, a number that gives the size of something.|The modulus of 7 divided by 3 is 1. |Synonyms: absolute value, magnitude, size, value|Antonyms: N/A|noun


modulus (plural moduli):/ˈmɒdʒʊlaɪ/ (US) /ˈmɑːdʒəlaɪ/| n. |模数，系数，模量| (Architecture)  A fixed unit of measurement, used as a basis for the dimensions of the components of a building. A constant factor or ratio. |The architect used a modulus of 10 cm for the design. |Synonyms: unit, measure, standard|Antonyms: N/A|noun
