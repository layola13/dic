colt:/koʊlt/| n.|小马；新手；轻机枪|A young male horse, typically less than four years old.  A beginner or novice, especially in a particular activity. A type of firearm.|例句：The farmer proudly showed off his new colt. 农民自豪地展示了他的新小马驹。

例句：He's a young colt in the world of finance. 他是金融界的新手。|近义词：foal, yearling, beginner, novice, rookie|反义词：mare, stallion, expert, veteran|词性:noun

词源:  From Old English *colt, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch and German Kalt, from an Indo-European root shared by cold.  Originally referring specifically to a young male horse, the sense ‘inexperienced person’ dates from the late 16th century.
