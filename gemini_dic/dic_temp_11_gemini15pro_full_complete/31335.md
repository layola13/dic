charactering:/ˈkærəktəraɪzɪŋ/
adj.
赋予…特色的，具有…特征的
describing the distinctive nature or features of something or someone.
Example: The soft light was charactering of a pleasant summer evening.  柔和的光线是宜人夏夜的特征。

Example:  His charactering smile made everyone feel welcome. 他标志性的笑容让每个人都感到宾至如归。
近义词:  typical, distinctive, characteristic, representative
反义词: atypical, unusual, uncommon
词性: adjective


characterization:/ˌkærəktəraɪˈzeɪʃn/
n.
描绘，刻画
the act of describing the distinctive nature or features of someone or something.
Example: The author's characterization of the protagonist was vivid and complex. 作者对主人公的刻画生动而复杂。

Example: The film's characterization of historical events has been criticized for its inaccuracies.  这部电影对历史事件的描述因其不准确性而受到批评。
近义词: portrayal, depiction, description
反义词: misrepresentation, distortion
词性: noun


characterize:/ˈkærəktəraɪz/
v.
描绘，刻画…的特征
describe the distinctive nature or features of.
Example: The report characterizes the current economic situation as unstable.  报告将目前的经济形势描述为不稳定。

Example:  He characterized the new policy as a disaster.  他将这项新政策描述为一场灾难。
近义词: describe, depict, portray, define
反义词: misrepresent, distort
词性: verb



词源:
From the Late Latin word "characterizare,"  meaning "to mark with a character," derived from the Greek "kharaktēr," meaning "engraved mark" or "distinguishing feature."
