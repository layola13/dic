electroshocks: /ɪˈlɛktrəʃɒks/ (UK) /ɪˈlɛktroʊʃɑːks/ (US)
electroshocks|n.|电击|The sudden passage of an electric current through the body, often used in medicine or as a form of torture.|The patient was given a series of electroshocks to treat his severe depression. 患者接受了一系列电击治疗以治疗他的严重抑郁症。|synonyms:  electroconvulsive therapy (ECT), shock therapy, shocks|antonyms: grounding, earthing|noun
electroshock|n.|电击|A single instance of passing an electric current through the body.|The electroshock caused the patient's muscles to contract violently. 电击导致病人的肌肉剧烈收缩。|synonyms: jolt, shock, zap|antonyms: grounding, earthing|noun
