metaplasm:/ˈmɛtəˌplæzəm/| n. |变形，转写; 语音变化|A change in a word, especially one involving alteration of the spelling, pronunciation, or grammatical form.|The poet employed metaplasm to create a rhyming effect.|近义词：metamorphosis, transformation, alteration|反义词:stasis, immutability|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *metaplasma, from Ancient Greek μεταπλα­σμός (metaplasmós, “transformation, remolding”), from μεταπλάσ­σω (metaplássō, “to transform, remodel”), from μετά (metá, “change”) + πλάσ­σω (plássō, “to mold”).
