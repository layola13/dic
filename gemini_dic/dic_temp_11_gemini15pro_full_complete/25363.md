buk:/bʊk/ | n. | 雄鹿; 公鹿(尤指未满五岁的雄鹿) | A male deer, especially a fallow deer less than five years old. | He saw a young buk grazing in the meadow. 他看到一只年轻的雄鹿在草地上吃草。|  The buk bounded across the field. 雄鹿跳跃着穿过田野。 | synonyms: stag, hart, buck | antonyms: doe, hind | noun

Etymology->
From Middle English *bukke*, from Old English *bucca* (“male goat, he-goat”), from Proto-Germanic *bukkô* (“he-goat”), from Proto-Indo-European *bhug* (“to swell, grow”). Cognate with Dutch *bok* (“he-goat, ram”), German *Bock* (“he-goat, ram, billy goat”), Icelandic *bukkr* (“ram, he-goat”).  The meaning shifted from "male goat" to "male deer" over time.
