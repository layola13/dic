canonicity:/ˌkænəˈnɪsɪti/
n.
正典性；规范性
The quality or state of being canonical; the fact of being included in a list of sacred books or other authoritative works.
The canonicity of the Gospels is a central issue in Christian theology. 福音书的正典性是基督教神学中的核心问题。
近义词：authenticity, authority, legitimacy
反义词：spuriousness, illegitimacy, apocryphality
词性：名词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *canonicalitas, from canonicus (canonical) + -itas (-ity).  Ultimately from Greek kanōn (rule, standard).
