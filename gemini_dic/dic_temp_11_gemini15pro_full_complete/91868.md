hyperingenuity:/ˌhaɪpərˌɪndʒɪˈnjuːɪti/
n.|超级精巧; 过度的巧妙|Extreme ingenuity or cleverness, often to the point of being excessive or impractical.|The artist's hyperingenuity was evident in the intricate details of his miniature sculptures, but some critics found it overwhelming.|近义词: over-ingenuity, excessive cleverness, over-elaboration|反义词: simplicity, artlessness|词性:名词

Etymology:
From hyper- + ingenuity.  "Hyper-" is a prefix derived from Greek meaning "over," "above," or "excessive." "Ingenuity" comes from the Latin "ingenium," meaning "inborn quality, talent, natural capacity."
