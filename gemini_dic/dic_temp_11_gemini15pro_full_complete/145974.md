overdiversities:/ˌoʊvərdaɪˈvɜːrsɪtiz/ | n. | 过度多样化; 过分多元化 |  The state of having too much diversity, often leading to complexity or difficulty in management.  It can refer to an excessive range of products, services, or options within a business, or an overrepresentation of different groups in a social or political context, sometimes perceived as challenging cohesion or creating friction. | The company's overdiversities in product offerings confused customers and diluted its brand identity. 该公司产品种类过度多元化，让顾客感到困惑，并削弱了其品牌形象。

The overdiversities of opinions within the committee made it difficult to reach a consensus. 委员会内部意见过度分歧，难以达成共识。| 近义词: excess diversity, overabundance of diversity | 反义词: lack of diversity, homogeneity, uniformity | 词性: 名词

词源: "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much") + "diversity" (from Latin "diversitas," meaning "difference").
