adrift:/əˈdrɪft/ | adj. | 漂流的，漂泊的；漫无目的的 | Floating freely, not moored or anchored; without purpose or guidance. | The boat was adrift at sea. 这艘船在海上漂流。|  The young man felt adrift in the big city.  这个年轻人在大城市里感到茫然。| near: afloat, drifting, aimless, lost | near: anchored, moored, grounded, directed | adjective

adrift:/əˈdrɪft/ | adv. | 漂流地；漂泊地 | In a drifting state; without anchor or mooring. |  The survivors were adrift for days. 幸存者们漂流了好多天。| The project is currently adrift, with no clear direction.  该项目目前漫无目的，没有明确的方向。| near: afloat, drifting, aimlessly | near: anchored, moored, grounded | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From a- + drift.  "A-" is a prefix meaning "on" or "in." "Drift" comes from Old Norse "drifa" meaning "to drive."  So, "adrift" literally means "on the drift."
