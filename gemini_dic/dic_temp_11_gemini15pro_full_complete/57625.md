draughtiness:/ˈdrɑːftinəs/ | n. | 通风；透风；有穿堂风 |The draughtiness of the old house made it difficult to heat. 这栋老房子的通风状况使得它很难被加热。| Example: The draughtiness of the cabin made it uncomfortably cold. 小屋的通风使它冷得不舒服。 |Synonyms: airiness, chilliness, ventilation | Antonyms: airtightness, closeness, stuffiness | noun

draughty:/ˈdrɑːfti/ | adj. | 通风的；有穿堂风的 | The draughty windows let in a cold breeze.  通风的窗户让冷风吹进来。| Example: We had to block up the draughty gaps under the door. 我们不得不堵住门下透风的缝隙。 | Synonyms: airy, breezy, chilly | Antonyms: airtight, close, stuffy | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From *draught* + *-iness*. *Draught* comes from Old English *droht*  meaning "act of drawing, pulling," related to *dragan*  "to draw, pull." The suffix *-iness* forms nouns denoting a state or quality.
