geotextile:/ˌdʒiːoʊˈtɛkstɪl/| n.|土工织物|A permeable fabric which, when used in association with soil, has the ability to separate, filter, reinforce, protect, or drain.  It is typically made from synthetic polymers such as polypropylene or polyester.|The geotextile prevented soil erosion on the slope.|Synonyms: geofabric, filter fabric|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps natural materials like clay or rock in some applications)|noun
