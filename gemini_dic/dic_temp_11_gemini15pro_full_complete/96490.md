indivisibles:/ˌɪndɪˈvɪzəblz/ | n. | 不可分割的事物；极小量 | Something that cannot be divided further; an infinitesimal quantity.  |  The ancient Greeks believed that atoms were indivisibles. 古希腊人认为原子是不可分割的。|  These rights are indivisibles and cannot be taken away. 这些权利是不可分割的，不能被剥夺。 | synonyms: atoms, units, elements, fundamentals | antonyms: fractions, parts, segments | noun

indivisible:/ˌɪndɪˈvɪzəbl/ | adj. | 不可分割的 | Not able to be divided. | Liberty and justice are indivisible. 自由与正义是不可分割的。|  The nation's security is indivisible from its economic strength.  国家的安全与其经济实力密不可分。| synonyms: inseparable, unbreakable, whole, entire | antonyms: divisible, separable, fractional | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *indivisibilis, from in- "not" + divisibilis "divisible," from dividere "to divide."
