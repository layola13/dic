overprolixity:/ˌoʊvərproʊˈlɪksɪti/ | n. | 过于冗长，啰嗦 |  The overprolixity of his writing made it difficult to follow his argument.  他的写作过于冗长，很难理解他的论点。| Example: The speaker's overprolixity bored the audience. 演讲者的冗长让听众感到厌烦。|Synonyms: verbosity, wordiness, prolixity | Antonyms: conciseness, brevity, succinctness | Noun

Etymology: From "over" (excessive) + "prolixity" (the state of being prolix). "Prolixity" comes from Late Latin *prolixitatem, from Latin prolixus "extended, long," from pro- "forth" + -lixus (akin to liqui "to flow").  Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European root *lei- "to flow, pour."
