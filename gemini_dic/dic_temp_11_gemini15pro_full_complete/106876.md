labyrinth:/ˈlæbərɪnθ/ (US), /ˈlæbərɪnθ/ (UK)| n.|迷宫，曲径，错综复杂的事物|A complex and confusing arrangement of passages or paths, often thought of as a maze.  It can also refer to a complex and confusing situation or state of mind.|The old house was a labyrinth of dark corridors and hidden rooms. 这座老房子就像一个黑暗走廊和密室组成的迷宫。|Synonyms: maze, tangle, web, network|Antonyms: simplicity, clarity|noun


labyrinthine:/ˌlæbəˈrɪnθaɪn/ (US), /ˌlæbəˈrɪnθaɪn/ (UK)| adj.|迷宫似的，复杂的，曲折的|Resembling a labyrinth; intricate and confusing.|The labyrinthine plot of the novel was difficult to follow. 这部小说的复杂情节很难理解。|Synonyms: intricate, complex, convoluted, tortuous|Antonyms: simple, straightforward, clear|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *labyrinthus*, borrowed from Ancient Greek λαβύρινθος (labúrinthos), of pre-Greek origin.
