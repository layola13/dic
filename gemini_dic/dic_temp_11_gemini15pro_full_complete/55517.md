distresses:/dɪˈstrɛsɪz/
|n.|痛苦，忧虑；贫困，危难|Extreme poverty and hardship cause great distress.  极度的贫困和艰难造成了巨大的痛苦。|近义词: anxieties, worries, troubles, hardships, agonies, sufferings|反义词: comforts, joys, reliefs, eases|noun

distresses:/dɪˈstrɛsɪz/
|v.|使痛苦，使忧虑；使贫困|The news of his illness distressed his family greatly. 他生病的消息使他的家人非常痛苦。|近义词: worries, troubles, upsets, grieves, pains, bothers|反义词: comforts, soothes, pleases, relieves|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French destresse (“hardship, oppression, anxiety”), from Vulgar Latin *districtia, from Latin districtus (“drawn apart, tight, narrow”), past participle of distringō (“draw tight, bind fast”), from dis- (“apart”) + stringō (“draw tight, bind”). Doublet of distraught.
