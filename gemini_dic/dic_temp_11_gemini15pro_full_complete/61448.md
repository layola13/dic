embouchure: /ɒ̃ˈbuːʃə(r)/ (British), /ɑːmbuˈʃʊr/ (American)| n. |口型; 吹奏乐器的嘴型|The way in which a musician applies their mouth to a musical instrument, especially a wind instrument.  It refers to the shape and position of the lips, tongue, and teeth, which are crucial for producing the desired tone and pitch.|

例句：
His embouchure was perfect, allowing him to hit every note with precision.
他的口型完美，让他能够精准地演奏每一个音符。

Her embouchure weakened after hours of practice.
练习几个小时后，她的口型变弱了。

近义词：mouthpiece placement, lip position
反义词: N/A
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *embouchure*, from *emboucher* "to put into the mouth," from *en-* "in" + *bouche* "mouth," from Latin *bucca* "cheek, mouth."
