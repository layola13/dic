disepalous: /daɪˈsɛpələs/ | adj. | 二萼片的 | Having two sepals.  |  The disepalous flower was a unique find.  这朵二萼片的花是一个独特的发现。 | Synonym: bisepalous | Antonym: polysepalous | adjective

Etymology:  From di- (two) + sepal + -ous (adjectival suffix). "Sepal" comes from New Latin *sepalum, coined by Noël Martin Joseph de Necker in 1790, possibly from Greek σκέπη (skepē, “covering”).
