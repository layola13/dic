eggar:/ˈɛɡə(r)/| n.| 蛾类的一种；刺激者，煽动者 |A person who incites or urges others to action, especially to unlawful or undesirable action.  A moth of the family Lasiocampidae, the larvae of which are hairy caterpillars.|He was an eggar of the rebellion, stirring up the crowd with fiery speeches.|近义词：agitator, instigator, provoker |反义词:pacifier, conciliator |词性:noun
Etymology: From Middle English *egger, from Old Norse eggja (“to incite, urge”), from Proto-Germanic *agjōną (“to sharpen, incite”).
