clownish:/ˈklaʊnɪʃ/| adj.|滑稽的，小丑般的|Resembling or characteristic of a clown, especially in being foolishly playful or boisterous.|He wore a clownish outfit to the party. 他穿着一身滑稽的小丑服装去参加了派对。|Synonyms: comical, ludicrous, farcical, buffoonish|Antonyms: serious, solemn, dignified|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From clown + -ish.  "Clown" comes from Middle English *cloune*, likely of Scandinavian origin, related to a word meaning "clumsy lout." The suffix "-ish" forms adjectives from nouns and means "having the qualities of" or "somewhat like."
