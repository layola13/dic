candids:/ˈkændɪdz/| n.|抓拍照片；偷拍照片|Photographs taken informally, especially without the subject's knowledge.|例句：The photographer captured some wonderful candids of the wedding guests. 摄影师捕捉到了一些婚礼宾客的精彩抓拍照片。|例句：She prefers candids to posed portraits. 她更喜欢抓拍而不是摆拍的肖像。|近义词：snapshots, informal photos|反义词：posed photos, formal portraits|词性:名词

词源:  From "candid" (adjective), meaning "frank" or "unposed," derived from Latin "candidus," meaning "white," "shining," or "pure."  The sense evolved to mean "straightforward" and then "unposed" in photography.
