momentum:/məˈmɛntəm/ (US), /məˈmɛntəm/ (UK)| n. |动量，势头，冲力|Force or speed of movement; impetus, as of a physical object or course of events.|The car gained momentum as it rolled down the hill. 汽车顺着山坡向下滚动，速度越来越快。|synonyms: impetus, drive, force, power, thrust, velocity, energy|antonyms: inertia, stagnation|noun

momentum:/məˈmɛntəm/ (US), /məˈmɛntəm/ (UK)| n. |动力，势头|The strength or force that something has when it is moving.|The campaign is gaining momentum. 这场运动的势头越来越猛。|synonyms: impetus, drive, force, power, thrust|antonyms: inertia, stagnation|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *movimentum*, from movēre "to move."
