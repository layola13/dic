closemouthed:/ˌkloʊzˈmaʊðd/| adj.|闭口不言的，沉默寡言的|Not inclined to speak; reticent.|He was closemouthed about his plans.|近义词：reticent, taciturn, uncommunicative|反义词：talkative, loquacious, garrulous|词性:形容词
Etymology: close (adj.) + mouthed (adj.).  "Close" in this sense means "shut," from Old French clos, past participle of clore "to shut, enclose."  "Mouthed" simply refers to having a mouth, but in combination with "close" it takes on the meaning of the manner in which the mouth is held.
