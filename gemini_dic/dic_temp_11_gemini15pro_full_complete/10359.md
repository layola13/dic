appellative: /əˈpɛlətɪv/ | adj. n. | 称呼的；称谓的；名字；称号 |  Relating to or denoting a name or title; a name or title.  Derived from Late Latin appellativus, from appellare ‘to address, call upon’, from ad- ‘to’ + pellere ‘to drive, push’.  Originally meant "calling upon" and later evolved to mean a name by which someone is called. |  "King" is an appellative noun. 国王是一个称谓名词。 | Synonyms: name, title, designation, denomination, sobriquet, epithet | Antonyms: anonymous, nameless | adjective
