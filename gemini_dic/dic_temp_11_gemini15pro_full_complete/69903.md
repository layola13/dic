fertilization:/ˌfɜːrtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ /ˌfɜːrtələˈzeɪʃən/
n.|受精，施肥|The process of combining the male and female gametes to form a zygote, or the act of enriching soil with nutrients.|例句：The fertilization of the egg by the sperm is the first step in the creation of new life. 精子和卵子的结合是新生命诞生的第一步。|近义词：insemination, impregnation, enrichment|反义词:sterility|词性:名词

fertilize:/ˈfɜːrtɪlaɪz/ /ˈfɜːrtəlaɪz/
vt.|使受精，施肥|To cause an egg or ovum to develop a new individual by uniting with a sperm or male gamete, or to enrich soil with nutrients.|例句：Farmers fertilize their fields to increase crop yields. 农民给田地施肥以提高作物产量。|近义词：enrich, nourish, inseminate|反义词:sterilize|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *fertilisatio*, from fertilis (fertile) + -atio (suffix forming nouns of action).  The root "fertilis" comes from Latin "ferre" (to bear, carry, produce).
