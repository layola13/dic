grippingnesses:/ˈɡrɪpɪŋnɪsɪz/
|n.|扣人心弦，引人入胜（通常指复数形式，但实际使用较少）|The grippingnesses of the novel's plot kept me reading all night. 这部小说的情节引人入胜，让我读了一整夜。|例句：The multiple grippingnesses of the stories made the anthology a bestseller.  这些故事的多重引人入胜之处使这部选集成为畅销书。|近义词：captivation, fascination, enthrallment|反义词：tedium, boredom|词性:名词

词源:
From gripping (adjective) + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing a state or quality).  Gripping itself comes from grip (verb) + -ing (suffix forming present participles and adjectives).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *gripaną (“to grip”).

注意事项:
"Grippingnesses" is grammatically correct but very rarely used.  It's more natural to use "gripping quality" or simply "grippingness" (singular).
