counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/ | adj. n. |反事实的；反事实命题| Relating to or expressing what has not happened or is not the case |Historians sometimes create counterfactual histories, speculating on what might have been. 历史学家有时会创造反事实的历史，推测可能发生的事情。| Counterfactual thinking can be helpful in learning from mistakes. 反事实思考有助于从错误中学习。| synonyms: hypothetical, conjectural, speculative, imaginary | antonyms: factual, real, actual | adjective
counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/ | n. |反事实命题；反事实假设| A counterfactual conditional statement (e.g. *if kangaroos had no tails, they would topple over*) |The counterfactual that Lincoln had lived might have led to a quicker Reconstruction period.  如果林肯还活着，可能会导致更快的重建时期。|Considering counterfactuals can help us understand the causes of events. 考虑反事实可以帮助我们理解事件的起因。| synonyms: hypothesis, conjecture, speculation | antonyms: fact, reality, truth | noun
