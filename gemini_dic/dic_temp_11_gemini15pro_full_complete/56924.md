dotes:/doʊts/| v. (third-person singular simple present dotes, present participle doting, simple past doted, past participle doted)| 溺爱；过分喜爱 |To be excessively fond of; to adore.| She dotes on her grandchildren. 她溺爱她的孙子孙女们。|近义词: adores, cherishes, loves, treasures|反义词: neglects, ignores, dislikes |verb

dotes:/doʊts/| n. (plural noun)|傻瓜，呆子 (古语，现已少用)|A foolish or simple person (archaic).|  He's a dote for believing that story. 他真是个傻瓜，竟然相信那个故事。|近义词: fool, idiot, simpleton |反义词: genius, sage |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *doten*, from Old English *dōtian* (“to be foolish, rave, be delirious”), of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Dutch *doten* (“to be silly, dote, be in one's dotage”), German *töricht* (“foolish”).
