ashoka:/əˈʃoʊkə/ | n. | 阿育王，阿育树 | An Indian emperor of the Maurya Dynasty, who reigned from 268 to 232 BCE. Also refers to the Ashoka tree (Saraca asoca), a sacred tree in Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism. | Example 1: Emperor Ashoka converted to Buddhism after the Kalinga War. 阿育王在羯陵伽战争后皈依了佛教。

Example 2: The Ashoka tree is known for its beautiful fragrant flowers. 阿育树以其美丽的芬芳花朵而闻名。 | Synonym: (for the emperor)  ruler, monarch, sovereign; (for the tree) Saraca indica | Antonym: (for the emperor) subject, commoner | Noun
