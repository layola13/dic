coercive:/kəʊˈɜːsɪv/ /koʊˈɜːrsɪv/| adj.|强制的，胁迫的|Relating to or using force or threats.|The government used coercive measures to quell the protests. 政府采取强制措施平息抗议活动。|compulsory, forced, mandatory|persuasive, voluntary|adjective

Etymology->

From Late Latin *coercitivus*, from Latin coercere "to restrain, confine, curb, repress," from com- "together" (see com-) + arcere "to ward off, confine, enclose," from Proto-Italic *arkē- "to hold, enclose, protect," from PIE *h₂erk- "to enclose, to hold."
