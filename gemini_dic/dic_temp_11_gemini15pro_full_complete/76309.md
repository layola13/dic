gabeler:/ˈɡeɪblər/ (US), /ˈɡeɪblə(r)/ (UK)| n.|叉子; (尤指)餐叉; |A gabeler is a fork, especially one used for eating. |He picked up his gabeler and began to eat. 他拿起叉子开始吃。|Synonym: fork|Antonym: spoon|noun


gabeler:/ˈɡeɪblər/ (US), /ˈɡeɪblə(r)/ (UK)| n.|（旧）收税官；税吏|A gabeler was historically a tax collector, especially one who collected salt tax. |The gabeler was not a popular figure in the village.  这个收税官在村里不受欢迎。|Synonym: tax collector|Antonym: taxpayer|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gabeleur, from gabler "to levy a tax," from gabele "tax, tribute," ultimately from Frankish *gaba.  The meaning "fork" is a later development, perhaps by analogy to the prongs of a fork resembling the fingers of a tax collector taking money.
