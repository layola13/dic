boycotting:/ˈbɔɪkɒtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈbɔɪkɑːtɪŋ/ (US)| n. v.|抵制，联合抵制|The act of refusing to buy, use, or participate in something as a way of protesting. |Many consumers are boycotting products tested on animals. 许多消费者抵制用动物做实验的产品。|Synonyms: embargo, blacklist, sanction, shun, ostracize |Antonyms: patronize, support, endorse|gerund/present participle

boycott:/ˈbɔɪkɒt/ (UK) /ˈbɔɪkɑːt/ (US)| n. vt.|抵制，联合抵制|A refusal to buy, use, or participate in something as a way of protesting. |They called for a boycott of the company's products. 他们呼吁抵制这家公司的产品。|Synonyms: embargo, blacklist, sanction, shun, ostracize |Antonyms: patronize, support, endorse|noun/verb

词源:  From Captain Charles Boycott, a land agent in Ireland who was ostracized in 1880 for refusing to reduce rents.  来源于查尔斯·博伊科特船长，一位爱尔兰的地产经纪人，他在1880年因拒绝降低租金而被排斥。
