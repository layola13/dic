##oxymoronic: /ˌɒksɪˈmɔːrɒnɪk/ | adj. | 矛盾的；似是而非的|figure of speech where apparently contradictory terms appear in conjunction. The effect is often striking and thought-provoking, highlighting the complex or ironic nature of the subject.**例句:**，也是忠诚的社会主义者。, self-contradictory, incongruous, inconsistent"sharp," and μωρός (moros), meaning "foolish" or "dull."  The combination originally referred to a figure of speech using seemingly contradictory terms.  The "-onic" suffix denotes an adjective form.  Therefore, "oxymoronic" describes something characterized by possessing the qualities ofan oxymoron.
