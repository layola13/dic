abovedeck:/əˈbʌvˈdɛk/| adj. adv.|甲板上的，在甲板上|On or above the deck of a ship; in the open air on the deck.|例句：The passengers were allowed abovedeck after the storm.  风暴过后，乘客被允许到甲板上。|例句：We spent the morning abovedeck, enjoying the sunshine. 我们在甲板上度过了一个上午，享受着阳光。|近义词：on deck, topside|反义词:below deck|词性:形容词，副词

Etymology->
From above + deck. "Above" traces back to Old English *abufan, from *a- "off, away from" + *bufan "above." "Deck" comes from Middle Dutch dec "covering," related to decken "to cover."  The combined term "abovedeck" literally means "above the covering (of the ship)."
