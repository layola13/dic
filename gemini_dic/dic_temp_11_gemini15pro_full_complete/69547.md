fellow-traveller:/ˈfeləʊ ˈtrævələ(r)/ (UK) /ˈfeloʊ ˈtrævlər/ (US)| n.| 同路人；同行者；同道；（尤指过去）共产主义同情者|A person who sympathizes with the aims and policies of a political organization, such as the Communist Party, without being a member, especially in the past; a companion on a journey.|例句1：He was known as a fellow-traveller during the Cold War.  他被认为是冷战时期的同路人。

例句2：She found a fellow-traveller on the long train journey.  她在漫长的火车旅途中找到了一个同行者。|近义词：sympathizer, supporter, companion, associate|反义词：opponent, adversary, enemy|词性：noun

词源： "fellow" + "traveller".  "Fellow" indicating companionship or shared purpose, and "traveller" referring to one who journeys. The political sense emerged in the early 20th century, particularly in relation to communism.
