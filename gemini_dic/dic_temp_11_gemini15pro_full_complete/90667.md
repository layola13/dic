humblehead: /ˈhʌmbəlˌhɛd/
| adj. | 谦逊的，不张扬的 |  Describing someone who is modest and unassuming, not boastful or arrogant, and often downplays their own achievements or abilities.  | He remained humblehead despite his great success.  他即使功成名就，仍然保持谦逊。 |  modest, unassuming, down-to-earth, self-effacing |  boastful, arrogant, conceited, pompous | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "humble" (meaning having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance) + "head" (referring to the mind or intellect). The combination suggests a mind focused on humility.
