freaks:/friːks/
| n. |怪人，畸形人；狂热爱好者；反常现象；(尤指吸毒引起的)幻觉；(非正式)突发奇想；惊吓；恐慌|A person or animal that is markedly unusual or deformed, typically as a result of a genetic mutation; A very enthusiastic person; A strange or unexpected event or situation; (informal) A sudden and transient strong emotion, such as fear or excitement.|
|例句：The circus freaks drew a large crowd. 马戏团的怪胎吸引了大批观众。|

|例句：He's a fitness freak. 他是个健身狂。|


|近义词：eccentrics, weirdos, oddballs, enthusiasts, fanatics, anomalies, aberrations, hallucinations, whims, fears, panics|
|反义词：normals, regulars, conformists|
|词性：名词|

freak:/friːk/
| v. |(使)处于极度兴奋状态；(使)震惊；(使)恐慌；(非正式)(吸毒后)产生幻觉|To become or cause someone to become extremely excited, especially in a way that is not controlled; To shock or surprise someone very much; To suddenly feel or make someone feel very worried, upset, or afraid.|
|例句：The news freaked me out. 这消息把我吓坏了。|

|例句：He freaked when he saw the spider. 他看到蜘蛛时吓坏了。|


|近义词：excite, thrill, shock, surprise, scare, frighten, panic, hallucinate|
|反义词：calm, soothe, reassure|
|词性：动词|
