glamourized: /ˈɡlæməraɪzd/ | adj. | 迷人的，富有魅力的，被美化的 |  Made to seem more attractive or exciting than it really is. | The movie glamourized the life of a rock star. 这部电影美化了摇滚明星的生活。 |  Synonyms: romanticized, idealized, glorified | Antonyms: deglamorized, unglamorous, realistic | adjective

glamorize: /ˈɡlæməraɪz/ | v. | 使...有魅力，美化 | To make something seem more attractive or exciting than it really is. | The media tends to glamorize violence. 媒体倾向于美化暴力。| Synonyms: romanticize, idealize, glorify | Antonyms: deglamorize, expose | verb

词源:  From glamour + -ize.  "Glamour" originally referred to a magic spell, later evolving to mean enchanting beauty or allure.  The suffix "-ize" turns a noun into a verb meaning "to make or become like."
