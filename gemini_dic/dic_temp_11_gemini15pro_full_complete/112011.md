long-drawn-out: /ˌlɔːŋˈdrɔːnˈaʊt/ | adj. |冗长的，拖延的|Prolonged to a tedious degree.  Characterized by extending over a longer period than necessary or desired. |The meeting was long-drawn-out and unproductive. 会议冗长而没有成果。| The peace talks were long-drawn-out and frustrating. 和平谈判旷日持久，令人沮丧。|近义词: protracted, lengthy, prolonged, extended, tedious |反义词: brief, concise, short, quick |词性: adjective
词源:  A compound adjective formed from "long," referring to duration, "drawn," the past participle of "draw" suggesting something stretched or extended, and "out," emphasizing the continuation or extension of the action or process.
