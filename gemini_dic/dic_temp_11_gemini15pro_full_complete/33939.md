cirripedia:/sɪrɪˈpiːdiə/| n. |藤壶，蔓足类|A subclass of marine crustaceans, commonly known as barnacles, that are typically sessile as adults and have a shell of calcareous plates.|Barnacles, which belong to the Cirripedia subclass, are common sights on rocky shores. 藤壶，属于蔓足亚纲，是岩石海岸的常见景象。|近义词:barnacles|反义词:None|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "cirrus" (curl of hair) + "ped" (foot), referring to the feathery, leg-like appendages (cirri) they use for filter feeding.
