coincident:/koʊˈɪnsɪdənt/| adj.|一致的，同时发生的，巧合的|Happening at the same time by accident; similar or the same|The two events were coincident. 这两件事同时发生。|Synonyms: simultaneous, concurrent, synchronous|Antonyms: separate, distinct|adjective

coincidence:/koʊˈɪnsɪdəns/| n.|巧合，一致，符合|The fact of two or more things happening at the same time by chance, or in a way that seems to be planned or arranged|It was pure coincidence that we met. 我们相遇纯属巧合。|Synonyms: chance, accident, fluke|Antonyms: intention, plan, design|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *coincidens*, present participle of *coincidere* ("to coincide"), from *co-* ("together") + *incidere* ("to fall into, happen, befall"), from *in-* ("in, on, upon") + *cadere* ("to fall").
