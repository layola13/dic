countersecure:/ˈkaʊntər səˈkjʊr/| adj. |反安全的，易受攻击的|Making a system countersecure means making it more vulnerable to attack.  This might be done intentionally, for example, by inserting a backdoor, or unintentionally, through poor design or implementation.|The supposedly secure system was found to be countersecure due to a flaw in its encryption algorithm.  据说安全的系统由于其加密算法中的缺陷而被发现是反安全的。|Near Synonyms: vulnerable, insecure, compromised, exposed|Antonyms: secure, safe, protected, fortified|Adjective

countersecure:/ˈkaʊntər səˈkjʊr/| v. |使反安全，使易受攻击|To countersecure a system is to make it less secure and more susceptible to attack.  This can be done deliberately or accidentally.|The hacker managed to countersecure the server by exploiting a known vulnerability.  黑客利用已知漏洞成功地使服务器变得不安全。|Near Synonyms: weaken, undermine, compromise, jeopardize|Antonyms: secure, strengthen, protect, fortify|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
"counter-" (prefix meaning "against, opposite") + "secure" (from Latin "securus" meaning "free from care, safe"). The term implies an action or state that is the opposite of secure, resulting in vulnerability.
