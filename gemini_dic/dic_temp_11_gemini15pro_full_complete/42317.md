counter-question:/ˈkaʊntərˌkwɛstʃən/ | n. |反问; 反诘|A question asked in response to another question, often to avoid answering the original question. | "When I asked him about his alibi, he responded with a counter-question about my own whereabouts." 当我问他不在场证明时，他反问我自己的行踪。| synonyms: retort, rejoinder, riposte | antonyms: answer, reply | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + question.  "Counter-" acts as a prefix meaning "against" or "in opposition to."  "Question" comes from the Old French "question" and ultimately from the Latin "quaestio," meaning "inquiry" or "investigation."
