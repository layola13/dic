colliquation:/ˌkɒlɪˈkweɪʃən/ | n. | 熔化，液化；(病理)组织的溶解, (尤指坏疽) |The process of becoming liquid; abnormal discharge of body fluids.  Specifically in pathology: the degeneration of tissue into a liquid or semi-liquid state, especially by necrosis.|The intense heat caused the colliquation of the metal.  高温导致金属熔化。|Synonyms: melting, liquefaction, dissolution, deliquescence |Antonyms: solidification, freezing, coagulation | noun
