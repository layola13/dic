lisped:/lɪspt/| v. adj.|说话口齿不清，咬舌不清;口齿不清的，咬舌不清的|To speak with a lisp, pronouncing "s" and "z" sounds like "th".  Characterized by a lisp.|She lisped slightly when she was nervous. 她紧张的时候说话有点口齿不清。|Synonyms: slur, mispronounce, mutter|Antonyms: articulate, enunciate|verb, adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lispen, from Old English *wlispian (“to lisp”), from Proto-Germanic *wlispijaną (“to lisp”), possibly imitative of the sound. Cognate with Old Norse lespa (“to lisp”).
