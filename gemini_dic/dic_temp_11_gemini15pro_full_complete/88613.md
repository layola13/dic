hiving:/ˈhaɪvɪŋ/| n. |入蜂巢；分蜂；蜂拥而至|The process of bees entering a hive, especially a new colony establishing itself; a swarming of bees; a crowding together like bees in a hive.|例句：The beekeeper observed the hiving of the new swarm. 养蜂人观察了新蜂群入巢的情况。

例句：The hiving of shoppers into the mall on Black Friday was overwhelming. 黑色星期五，购物者蜂拥而至商场，人山人海。|近义词：swarming, thronging, crowding|反义词：dispersing, scattering|词性:名词

hiving:/ˈhaɪvɪŋ/| adj. |蜂拥的，拥挤的 (较少用)|Characterized by a swarming or crowding together like bees in a hive.|例句：The hiving crowds made it difficult to navigate the street. 拥挤的人群使得在街上行走困难。

例句：The hiving activity of the bees indicated they were preparing to swarm. 蜜蜂蜂拥的活动表明它们正准备分蜂。|近义词：swarming, crowded, teeming|反义词：sparse, empty, deserted|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From hive + -ing.  "Hive" itself comes from Old English *hīfe, related to Old High German hūba.  The "-ing" suffix forms a noun or adjective from a verb, indicating the action or process of the verb, or the result of that action.
