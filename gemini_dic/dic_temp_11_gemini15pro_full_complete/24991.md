brutalism: /ˈbruːtəlɪzəm/| n.|野兽派；粗野主义|A style of architecture characterized by massive, blocky, concrete structures, often with exposed building materials and a minimalist aesthetic.  It flourished from the 1950s to the mid-1970s, having descended from the modernist architectural movement of the early 20th century.|The Barbican Centre in London is a famous example of brutalism.  伦敦的巴比肯中心是野兽派建筑的著名例子。|Synonyms:  modernism, functionalism|Antonyms:  ornateness, embellishment|Noun
