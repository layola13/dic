colossus's:/kəˈlɒsəsiz/ |n.| 巨像的；巨人的 |Something that belongs to or is associated with a colossus (a gigantic statue or thing).  It is the possessive form of "colossus." | Example: The colossus's foot was larger than a car. 巨像的脚比汽车还大。
| Example:  The museum displayed the colossus's head. 博物馆展出了巨像的头。 | Synonyms: giant's, titan's, behemoth's | Antonyms: miniature's, tiny's, dwarf's |  Possessive Noun

colossus:/kəˈlɒsəs/ |n.| 巨像；巨人；庞然大物 | A gigantic statue or thing; a person or thing of enormous size, importance, or influence. | Example: The Colossus of Rhodes was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.  罗德岛巨像是古代世界七大奇迹之一。
| Example: The company is a colossus in the tech industry. 这家公司是科技行业的巨头。 | Synonyms: giant, titan, behemoth | Antonyms: miniature, dwarf, midget | Noun
