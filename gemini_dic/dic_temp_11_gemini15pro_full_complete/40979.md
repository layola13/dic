coordinate: /koʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt/ (US), /kəʊˈɔːdɪneɪt/ (UK) | n. | 坐标；配套服装；同等重要的人或物 | a set of values that show an exact position |  The coordinates of the point are (3, 4).  这个点的坐标是(3, 4). | synonyms: position, location | antonyms:  disorganize,  scatter | noun

coordinate: /koʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt/ (US), /kəʊˈɔːdɪneɪt/ (UK) | v. | 协调；配合 | to make many different things work effectively as a whole | We need to coordinate our efforts to achieve our goal. 我们需要协调我们的努力来实现我们的目标. | synonyms: harmonize, synchronize, integrate | antonyms:  conflict,  clash | verb

coordinate: /koʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt/ (US), /kəʊˈɔːdɪneɪt/ (UK) | adj. | 同等的；并列的 | equal in importance or rank |  The two departments have coordinate status. 这两个部门地位相同. | synonyms: equal, equivalent, parallel | antonyms: subordinate, unequal | adjective


Etymology: From late Middle English *coordinat*, from Latin *coōrdinātus*, past participle of *coōrdināre* (“to arrange, regulate”), from *co-* (com-) (“together”) + *ōrdināre* (“to arrange, order”), from *ōrdō* (“order, rank”).
