awkwardish:/ˈɔːkwərdɪʃ/| adj.|略显尴尬的，有点笨拙的|Somewhat awkward; slightly clumsy or uncomfortable.|例句：His awkwardish dancing made everyone laugh. 他略显笨拙的舞姿让大家都笑了。|近义词：clumsy, ungainly, gawky|反义词:graceful, elegant|词性:形容词
Etymology:  From awkward + -ish (a suffix forming adjectives from adjectives, with the sense "somewhat," "rather," "having somewhat the character of").  Awkward itself comes from Old Norse *afgorðr "turned the wrong way," from *af- "away" + *gorðr "prepared."
