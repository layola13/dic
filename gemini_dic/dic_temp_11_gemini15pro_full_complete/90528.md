huff:/hʌf/| n. vi. vt.|生气，怒气；发脾气|A fit of anger or resentment.|She left in a huff after the argument.  她争吵后怒气冲冲地离开了。|Synonyms: rage, fury, tantrum, outburst|Antonyms: calmness, composure|noun

huff:/hʌf/| vi. vt.|吹气；喘息；恫吓|To breathe heavily; to blow; to bully or intimidate.|He huffed and puffed as he climbed the stairs. 他爬楼梯时气喘吁吁。|Synonyms: puff, pant, bluster|Antonyms: soothe, calm|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *huffen*, probably imitative of the sound of heavy breathing. Related to puff.
