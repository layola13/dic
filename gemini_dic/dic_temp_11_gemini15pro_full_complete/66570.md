exhilarant:/ɪɡˈzɪlə.rənt/ | adj. | 令人振奋的；使人高兴的 | Making one feel very happy, animated, or elated; thrilling. | The music was exhilarating and made everyone want to dance.  音乐振奋人心，让每个人都想跳舞。| Synonyms: invigorating, stimulating, uplifting, exciting, thrilling | Antonyms: depressing, boring, tedious, monotonous | adjective

exhilarate:/ɪɡˈzɪlə.reɪt/ | v. | 使高兴；使振奋 | Make (someone) feel very happy, animated, or elated. | The fresh air and sunshine exhilarated her.  新鲜的空气和阳光让她精神焕发。| Synonyms: invigorate, stimulate, uplift, excite, thrill | Antonyms: depress, bore, tire | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin exhilaratus, past participle of exhilarare (“to gladden, cheer”), from ex- (“out, from”) + hilarare (“to cheer, make cheerful”), from hilaris (“cheerful”).
