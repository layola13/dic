burggrave:/ˈbɜːrɡɡreɪv/
n.
城主；城堡伯爵
A count having jurisdiction over a town or district, especially in medieval Germany.
The burggrave ruled the town and surrounding lands in the name of the emperor. 城主以皇帝的名义统治着城镇及其周围的土地。
The powerful burggrave controlled access to the vital trade routes.  这个强大的城主控制着重要的贸易路线。
近义词：castellan, governor
反义词：（无明显的反义词，可以考虑citizen, resident, commoner等表示普通居民的词以示对比）
词性：noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle High German *burcgreve, from burc (“castle, fortified town”) + greve (“count”). Cognate with Dutch burggraaf.
