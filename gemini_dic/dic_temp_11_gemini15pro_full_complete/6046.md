amenders:/əˈmɛndərz/
|n.|修改者；修正者；改进者|People who amend something, typically a legal document or piece of legislation.  Those who make corrections or improvements.|The amenders of the constitution worked tirelessly to ensure fairness.|近义词: revisers, correctors, modifiers, editors|反义词: damagers, destroyers|词性: Noun


Etymology:
From the verb "amend," which comes from the Old French "amender" (to correct, improve), derived from the Latin "emendare"  (to free from faults, correct).  The suffix "-er" denotes a person who performs the action of the verb.
