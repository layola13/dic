ideographic:/ˌaɪdiəˈɡræfɪk/ | adj. n.|表意文字的，表意符号；表意文字|A character or symbol representing an idea or a thing without expressing the sounds that form its name, especially a character in the writing system of Chinese, Japanese, and Korean.|Chinese characters are ideographic. 汉字是表意文字。|The ideographic nature of Egyptian hieroglyphs makes their interpretation challenging.  埃及象形文字的表意性质使得它们的解释具有挑战性。|近义词：symbolic, pictorial|反义词:phonetic, alphabetic|词性:形容词，名词

ideographs:/ˌaɪdiəˈɡræfs/ | n. |表意文字|Characters or symbols representing ideas or things without indicating the sounds used to pronounce them.|Many East Asian languages utilize ideographs. 许多东亚语言使用表意文字。|Researchers study the evolution of ideographs across different cultures. 研究人员研究不同文化中表意文字的演变。|近义词：symbols, pictograms|反义词:phonograms, alphabets|词性:名词
