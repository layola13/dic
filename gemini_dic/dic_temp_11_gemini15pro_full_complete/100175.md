interregnal: /ˌɪntərˈrɛɡnəm/ | adj. n. | 间歇的，空位期间的；空位期，过渡期 |  Relating to a period of time between the end of one reign or regime and the beginning of the next. | The interregnal period was marked by political instability. 过渡时期政治不稳定。|  The museum highlighted art from the interregnal years between the two dynasties.  博物馆重点展出了两个朝代之间的过渡时期的艺术。 | 近义词: transitional, interim | 反义词:  reigning, permanent | 词性: adjective, noun

interregnum: /ˌɪntərˈrɛɡnəm/ | n. | 空位期，过渡期 | A period when normal government is suspended, especially between successive reigns or regimes. |  The interregnum lasted for several years after the king's death. 国王死后，空位期持续了数年。 |  The revolution led to a brief interregnum before the new government was established. 革命导致在新政府成立之前出现了一个短暂的过渡期。 | 近义词: transition, interim | 反义词:  reign, incumbency | 词性: noun


词源: From Latin *interregnum*, from *inter-* (between) + *regnum* (reign).
