interownerships:/ˌɪntərˈoʊnərʃɪps/
n.
相互持股，交叉持股
The situation where two or more companies own shares in each other.  This can be a strategy for companies to gain influence over each other, create stability, or foster collaboration.
Example: The complex interownerships between these corporations make it difficult to determine who ultimately controls the market. 这些公司之间复杂的交叉持股关系使得难以确定谁最终控制着市场。
Example: Interownerships can sometimes lead to reduced competition in a market. 交叉持股有时会导致市场竞争减少。
Synonyms: cross-holdings, mutual ownership, reciprocal ownership
Antonyms: sole proprietorship, independent ownership
