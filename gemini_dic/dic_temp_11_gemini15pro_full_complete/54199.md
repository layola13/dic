disfame:/dɪsˈfeɪm/
v. |诽谤，中伤|To attack the reputation of; slander or libel.|The newspaper disfamed the politician with false accusations. 这家报纸用虚假的指控诽谤了这位政治家。|
近义词:slander, libel, defame, malign, vilify
反义词:praise, commend, laud, extol
词性:verb

disfame:/dɪsˈfeɪm/
n. |诽谤，中伤|The act of harming someone's reputation; slander or libel.|The disfame spread quickly through the town, ruining her chances of election.  诽谤迅速传遍了整个城镇，毁了她当选的机会。|
近义词:slander, libel, defamation, calumny
反义词:praise, commendation, acclaim
词性:noun


词源: From Middle English *disfamen*, from Old French *desfamer* (meaning "to defame," "to insult"), from *des-* (meaning "opposite of," "away," similar to English *dis-*), and *famer* (meaning "to speak ill of," from Latin *fāma* "fame, reputation").
