dramatis personae:/ˌdrɑːməˈtɪs pərˈsoʊniː/ | n. | 剧中人；角色 | The characters or actors in a play.  A list of the characters or actors in a play. | Example 1: The dramatis personae of Shakespeare's Hamlet includes the prince himself, Ophelia, and Claudius.  莎士比亚的《哈姆雷特》中的剧中人包括王子本人、奥菲莉亚和克劳狄斯。

Example 2: The program listed the dramatis personae in order of appearance. 节目单按出场顺序列出了剧中人物。 | Synonyms: characters, cast, actors, players | Antonyms: audience, spectators | noun


dramatis:/drəˈmætɪs/ | adj. | 戏剧的；剧本的 | Relating to drama or the theater.  (Usually used in the phrase "dramatis personae") | Example 1: The dramatis aspect of the poem was highlighted by the professor. 教授强调了这首诗的戏剧性方面。

Example 2: While not a play, the novel had a strong dramatis quality. 这部小说虽然不是戏剧，但具有很强的戏剧性。| Synonyms: theatrical, dramatic | Antonyms: undramatic, nondramatic | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "dramatis personae," literally "persons of the drama."  "Dramatis" is the genitive plural of "drama" (play), and "personae" is the plural of "persona" (mask, character).
