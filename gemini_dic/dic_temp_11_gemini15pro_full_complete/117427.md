meding:/ˈmiːdɪŋ/ | n. | 修补；治疗；弥合 | The act of repairing or healing something, especially something abstract like a relationship. |  The couple sought counseling in hopes of meding their broken marriage. 这对夫妇寻求咨询，希望能弥合破裂的婚姻。|  repairing, mending, healing, fixing | breaking, damaging, worsening | noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *meden, from Old English *mēdian (“to heal, cure”), from Proto-Germanic *maidjaną (“to change, alter”), from Proto-Indo-European *moyd- (“to change, move”).  Related to Old Frisian mēdia, Old Saxon mēdian, Old High German meiten, Old Norse meiða (“to injure”), Gothic 𐌼𐌰𐌹𐌳𐌾𐌰𐌽 (maidjan, “to change, alter”).  The sense development is from "to change (for the better)" to "heal, cure".
