hypocritely:/ˌhɪpəˈkrɪtɪkli/
| adv. | 伪善地，虚伪地 |  In a way that shows hypocrisy; insincerely. | He spoke hypocritically about the importance of honesty. 他虚伪地谈论诚实的重要性。| 近义词: disingenuously, falsely, deceptively | 反义词: sincerely, genuinely, honestly | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *ipocritishli*, from Old French *ypocritement*, from *ypocrite* (“hypocrite”) + *-ment* (suffix forming abstract nouns denoting action, result, or means). *Ypocrite* comes from Latin *hypocrita*, from Ancient Greek *ὑποκριτής (hupokritḗs)*, from *ὑποκρίνομαι (hypokrínomai, “I answer, I interpret, I play a part”)*.
