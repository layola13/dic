cannibalized:/ˈkænɪbəlaɪzd/| v. |拆用（配件），调拨（资金、人员），蚕食，内部竞争|To take parts from one machine or system in order to repair another, or to take from one thing to make or improve another.  To reduce the sales of one product by introducing a similar product.|例句：The company cannibalized its own product line by releasing a new, cheaper version.  这家公司通过发布一款新的、更便宜的版本来蚕食自己的产品线。|例句：He cannibalized parts from an old radio to build his own transmitter. 他拆了一台旧收音机的零件来组装自己的发射机。|近义词：strip, dismantle, salvage, appropriate, undermine|反义词:assemble, construct, build, support|词性:动词

cannibalization:/ˌkænɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/| n. |拆用，调拨，蚕食，内部竞争|The act of cannibalizing something.|例句：The cannibalization of the older model hurt the company's profits. 旧型号的拆用损害了公司的利润。|例句：Cannibalization of resources from other departments led to resentment.  从其他部门调拨资源导致了怨恨。|近义词：appropriation, reallocation, undermining|反义词:construction, development|词性:名词
