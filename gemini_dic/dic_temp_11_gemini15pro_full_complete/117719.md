A state of being melancholic; sadness or depression of the spirits.|She felt a deep melancholy after her dog died. 她心爱的狗死后，她感到深深的忧郁。|Synonyms: sad, depressed, gloomy, dejected, wistful|Antonyms: happy, cheerful, joyful, elated|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *melancolie*, from Old French *melancholie*, from Latin *melancholia*, from Ancient Greek *μελαγχολία* (*melankholía*), from *μέλας* (*mélas*, “black, dark”) + *χολή* (*kholḗ*, “bile”).  The word literally refers to "black bile," one of the four humors in ancient medicine, believed to cause sadness.
