bridgeable:/ˈbrɪdʒəbl/| adj.|可架桥的，可弥合的|Able to be bridged or overcome.  Something that can be connected or joined, often referring to a gap or difference.|例句：The gap between the two buildings is bridgeable. 两座建筑物之间的间隙是可以架桥的。|近义词：spannable, connectable, surmountable|反义词：insurmountable, impassable|词性:形容词
Etymology: From bridge (n.) + -able.  "Bridge" comes from Old English "brycg," from Proto-Germanic "*brugjo," meaning "bridge, structure."  "-able" is a suffix of Latin origin meaning "capable of, worthy of, fit for, tending to, given to."
