cataclysm: /ˈkætəklɪzəm/ (US) /ˈkætəklɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |灾难；大灾变；剧变|A cataclysm is a sudden, violent, or devastating upheaval, often referring to a natural disaster or a social or political upheaval that causes widespread destruction and change.|The earthquake caused a cataclysm in the region. 地震给这个地区带来了灾难。|近义词：disaster, catastrophe, upheaval, calamity|反义词:blessing, boon, miracle|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cataclysme*, from Late Latin *cataclysmus*, from Ancient Greek *κατακλυσμός* (kataklysmós, “deluge, flood”), from *κατά* (katá, “down”) + *κλύζω* (klýzō, “I wash, I dash over”).  Originally referred to the Great Flood described in the Bible.
