affricative: /ˈæfrɪkətɪv/ | n. adj. | 摩擦音 | A consonant sound that begins as a stop and releases as a fricative, such as the sounds represented by the letters "ch" and "j" in English. | Example: The "ch" sound in "church" is an affricative.  教堂里的"ch"音是一个摩擦音。

Example: The "j" sound in "judge" is also an affricative.  法官中的"j"音也是一个摩擦音。 | 近义词: none (as a specific phonetic term) | 反义词: none (as a specific phonetic term) | 词性: noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *affricātīvus, from Latin affricāre (“to rub against”), from ad (“to, toward”) + fricāre (“to rub”).
