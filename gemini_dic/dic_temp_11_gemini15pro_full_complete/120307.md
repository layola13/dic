minicomputer:/ˈmɪniˌkəmpjuːtər/ (US), /ˈmɪniˌkəmˌpjuːtə(r)/ (UK)| n.|小型计算机|A computer of medium power, smaller and less expensive than a mainframe but more powerful than a microcomputer.  It emerged in the 1960s and bridged the gap between mainframes and personal computers.|The company used a minicomputer to control the manufacturing process.|Synonym: midrange computer, mini|Antonym: mainframe, supercomputer|noun

minicomputers:/ˈmɪniˌkəmpjuːtərz/ (US), /ˈmɪniˌkəmˌpjuːtəz/ (UK)| n. (plural)|小型计算机（复数）|Plural form of minicomputer.|Many businesses relied on minicomputers before the advent of personal computers.|Synonym: midrange computers|Antonym: mainframes, supercomputers|noun
