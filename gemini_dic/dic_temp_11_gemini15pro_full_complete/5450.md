almshouse: /ˈɑːmzˌhaʊs/ (US), /ˈɑːmzhaʊs/ (UK)| n. |救济院，贫民院|A house built and endowed for the lodging and support of poor people, especially elderly people.|The old woman spent her last years in the almshouse.|Synonyms: poorhouse, workhouse|Antonyms: mansion, palace|noun


almshouse: /ˈɑːmzˌhaʊs/ (US), /ˈɑːmzhaʊs/ (UK)| n. |救济院，贫民院|A house built and endowed for the lodging and support of poor people, especially elderly people.|Many almshouses were established in the Victorian era.|Synonyms: charitable institution, hospice|Antonyms: private residence, luxury home|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *almeshous*, from Old English *ælmesshūs*, from *ælmesse* (“alms”) + *hūs* (“house”).  *ælmesse* comes from Late Latin *eleemosyna*, from Greek *ἐλεημοσύνη* (eleēmosunē, “pity, alms”), from *ἐλεήμων* (eleēmōn, "merciful").
