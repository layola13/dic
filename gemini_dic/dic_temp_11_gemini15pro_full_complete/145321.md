overbrood:/ˈoʊvərbruːd/
verb
过度思虑，沉思; (尤指)担忧
to think anxiously for a long time about something, especially something unpleasant
She overbrooded her son's future. 她过度担忧她儿子的未来。


overbrood:/ˈoʊvərbruːd/
adjective
(尤指不愉快的事情)萦绕心头的，挥之不去的
(especially of something unpleasant) filling one’s thoughts and difficult to forget
An overbrooding sense of doom hung over the town. 一种挥之不去的厄运感笼罩着整个小镇。


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + brood.  "Over-" acts as an intensifier, meaning "excessively" or "too much."  "Brood" comes from Old English "brōd," meaning "to sit on eggs," and later developed the metaphorical meaning of "to ponder moodily or anxiously."


SYNONYMS->
(v.) worry, fret, agonize, dwell on, obsess over
(adj.) oppressive, overwhelming, haunting, pervasive


ANTONYMS->
(v.) ignore, dismiss, forget, neglect
(adj.) lighthearted, carefree, uplifting
