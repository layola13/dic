monopolistically:/ˌmɒnəpəˈlɪstɪkli/ /ˌmɑːnəpəˈlɪstɪkli/| adv.|垄断地，独占地|In a manner that creates or maintains a monopoly.  Characterized by having exclusive control of a commodity or service in a particular market.|The company acted monopolistically, driving out all competitors.|近义词：exclusively, solely|反义词: competitively|词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From monopoly + -istically.  "Monopoly" comes from the Greek "monos" (alone, single) and "polein" (to sell).  "-istically" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives ending in "-istic."
