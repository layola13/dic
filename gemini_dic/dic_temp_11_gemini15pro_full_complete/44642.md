crows:/kroʊz/ | n. |乌鸦；<非正式>鱼尾纹；|A large, black bird with a harsh cry, often seen as a symbol of ill omen.  A crow is known for its intelligence and adaptability. / The wrinkles at the corners of someone's eyes, resembling the feet of a crow. | The crows were circling overhead. 乌鸦在头顶盘旋。/  She laughed, and I noticed the crows' feet around her eyes. 我注意到她笑的时候眼角的鱼尾纹。| 近义词: raven, rook (for the bird) | 反义词:  None (no single opposite for a crow) | 词性: noun

crows:/kroʊz/ | v. |（公鸡）啼叫；自鸣得意；| (of a rooster) make a loud, shrill cry, typically to announce the dawn. / To boast or brag loudly. | The rooster crows at dawn. 公鸡在黎明啼叫。/ He's always crowing about his achievements. 他总是吹嘘自己的成就。 | 近义词: boast, brag, gloat | 反义词:  be modest, downplay  | 词性: verb
