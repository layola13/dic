intellectualized:/ˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəˌlaɪzd/ | adj. | 理性化的，理智化的，过度分析的 |  Made excessively intellectual; overanalyzed or over-thought.  Characterized by the excessive use of intellect, often at the expense of emotion or intuition.  |  He presented an intellectualized version of the events, devoid of any emotional content. 他讲述了一个理性化的事件版本，没有任何情感内容。

The intellectualized debate lacked practical solutions. 这场理性化的辩论缺乏切实可行的解决方案。 | 近义词: cerebral, abstract, theoretical, analytical | 反义词: emotional, intuitive, instinctive, visceral | 词性: 形容词

词源: From the verb "intellectualize," which is formed from "intellect" + "-ize."  The word "intellect" comes from the Latin "intellectus," meaning "understanding, perception, comprehension."
