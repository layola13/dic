organicity:/ɔːrɡəˈnɪsəti/ | n. | 有机性；有机组织 | The state or quality of being organic; the degree to which something is organized as a whole. | The organicity of the community was evident in the way everyone worked together.  这个社区的有机性体现在每个人共同努力的方式上。|  coherence, unity, integration | artificiality, disorganization | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From organic + -ity.  "Organic" comes from Late Latin *organicus, from Latin organum "instrument, tool, organ," from Greek organon "instrument, implement, tool; organ of the body," from PIE *werg-ano-, from root *werg- "to work, do."  The suffix "-ity" forms abstract nouns from adjectives, and comes from French -ité and Latin -itas.
