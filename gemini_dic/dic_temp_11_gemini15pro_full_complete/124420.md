motorcaded:/ˈmoʊtərˌkeɪdɪd/| adj.|车队护送的，列队行进的|A procession of vehicles, typically carrying and escorting an important person.|The president was motorcaded to the airport.|近义词：escorted, chauffeured, conveyed in a procession|反义词：unescorted|词性:形容词
Etymology: motor + cavalcade. "Motor" refers to a vehicle's engine, while "cavalcade" historically refers to a procession of riders or horse-drawn carriages. The combination signifies a procession of motorized vehicles.
