ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *closen, *closin, from Anglo-Norman clos, closer,  from Old French clos, past participle of clore (“to close, shut, enclose”), from Latin claudere (“to shut, close”), from Proto-Italic *klawdō, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱléw- (“to hook, bend”).
