landgrave: /ˈlændɡreɪv/ | n. | 伯爵；地方统治者 | A German nobleman of a high rank, historically ruling a territory.  A count having jurisdiction over a territory. |  The landgrave ruled over a vast and prosperous region.  这位伯爵统治着一个广阔而繁荣的地区。| synonym: count, earl | antonym: peasant, serf | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle High German *lantgrāve, from lant (“land”) + grāve (“count”).  Cognate with Dutch landgraaf.
