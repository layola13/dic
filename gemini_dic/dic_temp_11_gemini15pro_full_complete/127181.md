naturalizing:/ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪzɪŋ/
verb
使入籍；归化；移植（动植物）；吸纳
the process of becoming a citizen of a country that you were not born in. Also, to introduce and establish (a plant or animal) in a region where it previously did not exist.
Many immigrants are naturalizing as American citizens. 许多移民正在归化为美国公民。
The process of naturalizing can take several years. 归化过程可能需要数年时间。

近义词:acclimatizing, adapting, assimilating, integrating
反义词:denaturalizing, expatriating

naturalize:/ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪz/
verb
使入籍；归化；移植（动植物）；吸纳
To admit (a foreigner) to the citizenship of a country. Also, to introduce and establish (a plant or animal) in a region where it previously did not exist.
He was naturalized as a British subject. 他被归化为英国公民。
They successfully naturalized the plant in their garden. 他们在花园里成功地移植了这种植物。

近义词:acclimatize, adapt, assimilate, integrate
反义词:denaturalize, expatriate

naturalization:/ˌnætʃrələˈzeɪʃn/
noun
入籍；归化；移植
the process of becoming a citizen of a country that you were not born in. Also, the introduction of plants or animals to a region where they are not native.
She applied for naturalization after living in the country for five years.  她在该国居住五年后申请入籍。
The naturalization of non-native species can have negative consequences. 非本地物种的移植可能会产生负面影响。

近义词:citizenship, integration, assimilation
反义词:expatriation
