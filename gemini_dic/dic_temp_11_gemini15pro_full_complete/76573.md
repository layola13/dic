galliardness:/ˈɡæliərdnɪs/| n.|快乐；欢快；轻快|The state or quality of being galliard; cheerfulness, merriment, liveliness.|His galliardness was infectious, spreading joy to everyone around him. 他的快乐很有感染力，把快乐传播给周围的每个人。|Synonyms: cheerfulness, merriment, gaiety, lightheartedness, joviality, exuberance|Antonyms: gloominess, sadness, melancholy, dejection, moroseness|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *galliardise*, from Old French *galiart*, meaning "valiant, high-spirited, joyful," likely of Germanic origin.
