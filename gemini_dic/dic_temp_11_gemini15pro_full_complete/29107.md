casbah's:/kæzˈbɑːz/ | n. | 城堡,  (尤指北非城市的)旧城区 |  A casbah is the citadel of a North African city, typically one that is walled. It often refers to the older part of a city, with narrow, winding streets and traditional buildings.  It comes from the Arabic word  قصبة (qaṣabah), meaning "citadel".| We wandered through the casbah's labyrinthine alleys. 我们在城堡迷宫般的小巷中漫步。 | Synonym: citadel, fortress, old town, walled city | Antonym: new town, suburb | noun
