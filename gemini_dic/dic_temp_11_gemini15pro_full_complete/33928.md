cirrhosis:/sɪˈroʊsɪs/ |n.|肝硬化|A chronic disease of the liver marked by degeneration of cells, inflammation, and fibrous thickening of tissue. It is typically a result of alcoholism or hepatitis.| He developed cirrhosis of the liver due to years of heavy drinking.  他由于多年的酗酒患上了肝硬化。|Synonyms:  liver damage, hepatic fibrosis|Antonyms:  liver health|noun
