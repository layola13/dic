droving:/ˈdroʊvɪŋ/
n.
驱赶牲畜；赶牲畜
The act of driving livestock over a distance, usually to market.
例句：Droving cattle across the plains was a common sight in the old West.  在古老的西部，赶着牛群穿过平原是常见的景象。

例句：The annual droving of sheep to the highlands is a tradition that continues to this day.  每年将羊群赶到高地是一项延续至今的传统。

近义词：herding, driving
反义词：N/A
词性：名词


drove:/droʊv/
n.
畜群；人群
A group of animals, especially sheep, cattle, or pigs, driven or moving together; a large crowd of people.
例句：A drove of cattle blocked the road. 一群牛挡住了路。

例句：A drove of tourists descended upon the small town.  一大群游客涌入小镇。

近义词：herd, flock, crowd
反义词：N/A
词性：名词


drove:/droʊv/
v. (past tense of drive)
驾驶；驱赶
Past tense of "drive": operated and controlled the direction and speed of a vehicle or other conveyance.  Also, compelled or urged (animals or people) to move in a particular direction.
例句：He drove the cattle to market. 他把牛赶到市场。

例句：She drove her car to the store. 她开车去商店。

近义词：operated, steered, herded
反义词：rode, walked
词性：动词
