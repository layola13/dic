disjunctive:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋktɪv/ | adj. | 分离的，析取的 | Characterized by disjunction; separating or dividing.  Marking a choice between two mutually exclusive possibilities. |The lawyer presented two disjunctive options to her client: settle out of court or go to trial. 律师向她的客户提出了两个相互排斥的选择：庭外和解或上法庭。 | synonyms: dividing, separating, alternative, contrasting | antonyms: conjunctive, connecting, uniting | adjective

disjunction:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋkʃən/ | n. | 分离，析取 | The act of disjoining or the state of being disjoined; separation. A statement presenting two mutually exclusive alternatives. | There is a clear disjunction between the two schools of thought. 两种思想流派之间存在明显的区别。 | synonyms: separation, division, disunion, split | antonyms: conjunction, union, connection | noun
