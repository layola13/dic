lividnesses:/lɪˈvɪdnɪsɪz/
noun
尸斑，瘀斑
The discoloration of the skin in the dependent parts of a corpse, resulting from the settling of the blood after death.
The pathologist noted the lividnesses on the victim's back, indicating he had been lying on his back after death.  病理学家注意到受害者背部的尸斑，表明他死后一直仰卧着。

Synonym: hypostasis, postmortem staining
Antonym:  (No direct antonym for this medical term)
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From lividity + -ness.  Lividity comes from Middle English lividite, from Old French lividité, from Latin lividitāt-, lividitās, from lividus (“bluish”), perhaps from līvēre (“to be bluish”).
