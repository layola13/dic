buccinator:/ˈbʌkɪneɪtər/ | n. | 颊肌；吹号手 | A thin, flat muscle in the cheek, forming the fleshy wall of the cheek. Also, a person who plays a buccina, an ancient Roman wind instrument. | The buccinator muscle aids in chewing and blowing.  颊肌有助于咀嚼和吹气。| The Roman army employed buccinator to signal troop movements.  罗马军队雇用吹号手来指挥部队行动。| synonyms: cheek muscle, trumpeter | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for an anatomical muscle) | noun
