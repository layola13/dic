half-intellectually:/ˌhæf ˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəli/| adv.| 半智力地；不完全用智力地| Engaging with a topic half-intellectually implies a superficial or incomplete understanding, driven by something other than rigorous thought. It suggests a lack of deep intellectual engagement. | He approached the problem half-intellectually, relying more on intuition than logic. 他处理这个问题的方式并不完全理智，更多地依赖直觉而不是逻辑。|  She listened to the lecture half-intellectually, her mind wandering to other things. 她心不在焉地听着讲座，思绪飘到了别处。| 近义词: superficially, cursorily, shallowly | 反义词: intellectually, thoroughly, rigorously | 词性: 副词
