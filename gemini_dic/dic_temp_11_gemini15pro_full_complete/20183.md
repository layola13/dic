birch:/bɜːrtʃ/ (US) /bɜːtʃ/ (UK)| n. |桦树；桦木|A slender tree with thin, papery bark, typically peeling in horizontal strips, and small, toothed leaves.  Birch wood is close-grained and hard.|The birch trees swayed gently in the breeze. 桦树在微风中轻轻摇曳。|Synonyms: Betula (genus name)|Antonyms: N/A|noun

birch:/bɜːrtʃ/ (US) /bɜːtʃ/ (UK)| adj. |桦木制的|Made of birch wood.|He crafted a beautiful birch canoe. 他制作了一艘漂亮的桦木独木舟。|Synonyms:  birchen|Antonyms: N/A|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *birce, from Proto-Germanic *birkjō, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰerHǵós, meaning "bright, shining" (referring to the bark). Cognates include German Birke, Dutch berk.
