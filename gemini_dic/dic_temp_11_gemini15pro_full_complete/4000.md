ahaz:/ˈeɪhæz/| n.|亚哈斯 (犹大王)|Ahaz was a king of Judah, mentioned in the Hebrew Bible.  He reigned from c. 735 to c. 715 BCE and is described as a wicked king who engaged in idolatrous practices.|Ahaz made his son pass through the fire, according to the abominations of the heathen. 亚哈斯仿照外邦人的可憎恶俗，叫他的儿子经火。

Ahaz refused to ask for a sign from God. 亚哈斯拒绝向上帝求一个兆头。|Synonym: king (in this context)|Antonym: N/A|Proper noun
