fallownesses:/ˈfæloʊnəsɪz/
n.
休耕地；闲置状态
The state of being fallow; inactivity or neglect.
The fallownesses of the fields allowed the soil to regenerate.  田地的休耕使土壤得以再生。
近义词:inactivity, idleness, dormancy
反义词:activity, productivity, cultivation
词性:名词

fallow:/ˈfæloʊ/
adj.
休耕的；闲置的
Left uncultivated or unplanted for a season or more; inactive.
The farmer left the field fallow this year. 今年农民让这块地休耕。
近义词:uncultivated, idle, dormant
反义词:cultivated, active, productive
词性:形容词

fallow:/ˈfæloʊ/
v.
休耕；使闲置
To leave (land) uncultivated or unplanted for a season or more; to plow but not seed.
They decided to fallow the land to improve its fertility. 他们决定休耕土地以提高肥力。
近义词:rest, unplant
反义词:cultivate, plant
词性:动词

词源:
From Old English *fealh, from Proto-Germanic *falwaz (“pale, yellowish”). Related to Old High German falaw (“pale yellow, fallow”), Old Norse folr (“pale yellow, dun”), Latin pallidus (“pale”).  The semantic shift from "pale" to "fallow" likely arose from the pale color of plowed but unsown land.
