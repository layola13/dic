backstaff:/ˈbækˌstæf/ | n. | 背竿，反照仪 | An instrument formerly used for taking the altitude of the sun or stars, consisting of a graduated staff with a vane that slides along it. The observer's back was turned to the sun, hence the name. |  Sailors used a backstaff to navigate by the sun. 海员使用背竿依靠太阳导航。|  The captain used the backstaff to determine the ship's latitude. 船长使用背竿确定船的纬度。| synonym: quadrant, astrolabe | antonym: N/A | noun
