disincline:/ˌdɪsɪnˈklaɪn/| vt.|使不情愿，使不愿|To make someone unwilling or reluctant to do something.|His poor health disinclined him to travel. 他的健康状况不佳，使他不愿旅行。|The rain disinclined me to go out. 这场雨让我不想出门。|近义词：deter, discourage, dissuade|反义词：incline, encourage, motivate|词性: verb (transitive)
Etymology: From Late Latin *disinclināre, from dis- (“not, un-”) + inclināre (“to bend, incline”).
