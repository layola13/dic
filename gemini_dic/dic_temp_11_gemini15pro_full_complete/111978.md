Londinium: /lɒnˈdɪnɪəm/ | n. | 伦敦 (古罗马时期) |  The ancient Roman name for London, referring to a settlement established in the first century AD. It is derived from a Celtic word, possibly meaning "place belonging to a man called *Londinos*". |  Londinium was a major trading hub in Roman Britain.  伦敦是罗马不列颠的主要贸易中心。 |  Example: Archaeologists have uncovered many Roman artifacts in Londinium. 考古学家在伦敦出土了许多罗马文物。| Synonym: Roman London, Augusta (occasionally) | Antonym: N/A | noun
