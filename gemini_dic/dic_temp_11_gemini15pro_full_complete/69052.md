faulcon:/ˈfɔːlkən/ | n. |猎鹰|A bird of prey with long pointed wings and a notched beak, known for its swift flight and keen eyesight.  It is often trained for hunting.|例句：The faulcon soared high above the mountains. 猎鹰在群山上空翱翔。

例句：The falconer released the faulcon to hunt the pheasant.  猎鹰人放出猎鹰去捕猎野鸡。


|近义词：hawk, falcon (Note: "falcon" is both the primary term and a synonym, as some hawks are considered falcons.) |反义词:prey |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *faucon*, from Late Latin *falconem*, accusative of *falco*. Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *falkaz* ("falcon, hawk"), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)pel- ("to tear, split").  Related to "fallow".
