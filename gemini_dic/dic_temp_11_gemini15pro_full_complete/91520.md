hyoliths:/ˈhaɪ.ə.lɪθs/
n.
hyoliths（复数）; 海奥利斯生物（已灭绝的一类海洋生物）
An extinct group of marine animals characterized by a conical shell, an operculum, and two curved spines known as helens.  They lived from the Cambrian to the Devonian periods.
Hyoliths are an enigmatic group of extinct Paleozoic lophotrochozoans. 海奥利斯生物是一类已经灭绝的古生代触手冠动物。

Hyoliths were abundant in the Cambrian oceans. 海奥利斯生物在寒武纪海洋中数量众多。
