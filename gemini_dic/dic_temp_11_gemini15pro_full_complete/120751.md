misanthrope: /ˈmɪsənˌθroʊp/ (US), /mɪˈsænθrəʊp/ (UK)| n.|厌世者，愤世嫉俗者|A person who dislikes humankind and avoids human society. |例句：He was a misanthrope who lived alone in the woods. 他是一个厌世者，独自住在树林里。|例句：Her misanthropic tendencies made it difficult for her to maintain friendships. 她厌世的倾向让她很难维持友谊。|近义词：cynic, pessimist, recluse|反义词：philanthropist, humanitarian|词性: noun


misanthropic: /ˌmɪsənˈθrɑːpɪk/ (US), /ˌmɪsænˈθrɒpɪk/ (UK)| adj.|厌世的，愤世嫉俗的|Relating to or characteristic of a misanthrope; hating or distrusting humankind.|例句：He had a misanthropic view of the world. 他对世界持厌世的态度。|例句：Her misanthropic comments alienated her colleagues. 她厌世的评论疏远了她的同事。|近义词：cynical, pessimistic, world-weary|反义词：philanthropic, humanitarian|词性: adjective


misanthropy: /mɪˈsænθrəpi/| n.|厌世，愤世嫉俗|A dislike of humankind.|例句：His misanthropy grew with each passing year. 他的厌世情绪逐年加深。|例句：The novel explored the themes of isolation and misanthropy. 这部小说探讨了孤独和厌世这两个主题。|近义词：cynicism, pessimism|反义词：philanthropy, humanism|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek μισάνθρωπος (misánthrōpos, “hater of mankind”), from μῖσος (mîsos, “hatred”) + ἄνθρωπος (ánthrōpos, “human being”).
