desolate:/ˈdesələt/ (US) /ˈdɛsələt/ (UK)| adj. | 荒凉的，无人烟的；孤寂的，凄凉的 | Making a place empty or bare; deserted and lonely; feeling or showing misery, unhappiness, or loneliness. | The war left the city desolate.  战争使这座城市变得荒凉。| synonyms: barren, bleak, deserted, lonely, forlorn | antonyms: populous, cheerful, lively | adjective

desolate:/ˈdesəleɪt/ (US) /ˈdɛsəleɪt/ (UK)| vt. | 使荒芜，使孤寂 | To make a place empty or bare; to make someone feel utterly wretched and unhappy. | The hurricane completely desolated the coastal region.  飓风彻底摧毁了沿海地区。| synonyms: devastate, ruin, destroy, sadden | antonyms: build, construct, cheer, comfort | verb
