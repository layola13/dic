midlands's:/ˈmɪdləndzɪz/ | n. |英格兰中部地区（尤指工业区）的；英格兰中部地区的人的| The Midlands's industrial heritage is rich. 英格兰中部地区的工业遗产非常丰富。| Midlands' economy has been declining. 英格兰中部地区的经济一直在衰退。|近义词：central England|反义词:|词性:名词所有格

词源:
From Midlands + -'s (possessive suffix).  "Midlands" itself comes from Old English *middel land*  meaning "middle land."

用法说明:
"Midlands's" is the possessive form of "Midlands," a proper noun referring to the central region of England. Although somewhat awkward, it is grammatically correct.  More commonly, you would rephrase to avoid this construction, using "of the Midlands" instead. For example, "the economy of the Midlands" is preferred over "the Midlands's economy."
