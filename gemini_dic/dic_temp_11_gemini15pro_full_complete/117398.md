medicate:/ˈmedɪkeɪt/| v. |治疗；用药；服药|to treat (a person or a condition) with medicine |The doctor medicated the patient for pain. 医生给病人开了止痛药。|近义词：treat, dose, drug |反义词: - |verb

medicate:/ˈmedɪkeɪt/| v. |缓解；减轻（不快的情绪）| to make (something, such as an unpleasant feeling or situation) less painful or severe |He medicated his grief with alcohol. 他用酒精来麻痹自己的悲伤。|近义词：alleviate, soothe, relieve|反义词: - |verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin medicatus, past participle of medicare "to heal, treat medically," from Latin medicus "physician," from medeor "I heal."
