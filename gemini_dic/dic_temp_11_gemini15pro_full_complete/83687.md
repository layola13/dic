haggardnesses:/ˈhæɡərdnəsəz/| n. |憔悴，枯槁，疲惫不堪|The state of being haggard; gauntness, weariness, or exhaustion, often due to prolonged stress, worry, or lack of sleep.|Her face showed the haggardnesses of a long illness.|近义词：gauntness, weariness, exhaustion, fatigue|反义词:robustness, healthiness, vigor|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hagardnes, from hagard + -nes.  Haggard itself comes from Old French hagard, meaning "wild, untamed," especially of a hawk.  This sense evolved to describe a wild or worn appearance.
