crystallization:/ˌkrɪstələˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 结晶，具体化，明确化 | The process by which a solid forms, where the atoms or molecules are highly organized into a structure known as a crystal. Also refers to the act of making something definite or clear. |  The crystallization of his ideas into a viable business plan took months.  他的想法最终形成了一个可行的商业计划，这花费了几个月的时间。

The slow crystallization of the honey made it difficult to pour.  蜂蜜缓慢结晶，难以倒出。| 近义词：solidification, concretion, formation, development | 反义词：dissolution, melting, liquefaction | 词性: noun


crystallize:/ˈkrɪstəlaɪz/ | v. | 结晶，具体化，明确化 | To cause to form crystals or assume crystalline form; to make something definite or clear. |  The solution crystallized overnight, forming beautiful patterns.  溶液一夜之间结晶，形成了美丽的图案。

His vague ideas began to crystallize into a concrete plan. 他模糊的想法开始具体化为一个具体的计划。 | 近义词：solidify, concretize, formulate, develop | 反义词：dissolve, melt, liquefy | 词性: verb
