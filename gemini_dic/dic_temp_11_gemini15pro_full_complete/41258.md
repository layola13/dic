corbiestep:/ˈkɔːrbiˌstɛp/| n.|乌鸦步，指一种踮着脚尖走路的方式|A corbiestep is a stealthy, tiptoe kind of gait, often associated with mischievous or secretive behavior.  It derives from the image of a crow (or raven, traditionally called a "corbie" in Scotland) hopping and stepping lightly.|He took a corbiestep towards the door, trying not to wake the baby. 他踮着脚尖走向门口，尽量不唤醒宝宝。|Synonyms: tiptoe, stealthy step, light step|Antonyms: stomp, heavy tread|noun
