crimson: /ˈkrɪmzən/ (US) /ˈkrɪmzən/ (UK) | n. adj. v. | 深红色; 深红色的;  使…变成深红色 | A rich deep red color inclining to purple. / Of a rich deep red color. / Become deep red in color. | The dress was a vibrant crimson. 这件连衣裙是鲜艳的深红色。 | Her face flushed crimson with embarrassment. 她的脸因尴尬而涨成了深红色。 | The leaves crimsoned in the autumn. 秋天树叶变成了深红色。| 近义词:  scarlet, ruby, vermilion | 反义词: pale, light | 词性: 名词，形容词，动词


词源: From Middle English *crimsin, from Old Spanish cremesín (“crimson”), from Medieval Latin cremesinus (“of or belonging to kermes”), from Arabic قِرْمِز‎ (qirmiz, “kermes”), from Middle Persian *kirmiz (“red dye, kermes”), from Sanskrit कृमिज (kṛmija, “produced by worms”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kʷŕ̥mis (“worm”). Doublet of kermes.
