conjurer:/ˈkʌndʒərər/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒərə(r)/ (UK)| n.|魔术师；巫师；召唤者|A person who performs magic tricks; a magician. Also, someone who seems to make things appear or happen in a surprising or unexpected way, or someone who calls upon or summons spirits.|The conjurer pulled a rabbit out of his hat. 魔术师从帽子里变出一只兔子。|Synonyms: magician, illusionist, sorcerer, wizard|Antonyms: skeptic, realist|noun

conjure:/ˈkʌndʒər/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒə(r)/ (UK)| v.|变戏法；召唤；想象；恳求|To perform magic tricks; to make something appear or happen in a surprising or unexpected way; to call upon or summon spirits; to implore or earnestly request.|He conjured a feast out of thin air. 他凭空变出一桌盛宴。|Synonyms: evoke, summon, invoke, implore|Antonyms: banish, dispel, dismiss|verb
