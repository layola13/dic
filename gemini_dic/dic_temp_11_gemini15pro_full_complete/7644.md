annuloida:/ˌænjuˈlɔɪdə/ | n. |环节动物门（已废弃）|A former phylum or other high-level grouping of invertebrates, including annelids, echinoderms, and others, considered to have a segmented or ringed body plan. This classification is now obsolete and no longer used in modern taxonomy.| Example:  Annuloida was once considered a major grouping in the animal kingdom. 环节动物门曾被认为是动物界的一个主要类别。| Synonym: (No direct synonym as the term is obsolete; use specific phyla like Annelida or Echinodermata) | Antonym: (Not applicable as the term represents a taxonomic group) | Noun
