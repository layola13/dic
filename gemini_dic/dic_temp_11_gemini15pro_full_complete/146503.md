overgeneralised:/ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛnərəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 过度概括的，过分概括的 | Characterized by the application of a grammatical rule or pattern in a broader context than is appropriate.  Applying a general rule too broadly. |  He made an overgeneralised statement about all teenagers being lazy. 他对所有青少年都很懒惰的说法过于概括。| Synonyms: oversimplified, sweeping, blanket | Antonyms: nuanced, specific, precise | adjective

overgeneralize:/ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz/ | v. | 过度概括，过分概括 |  To draw a conclusion or make a statement about a group or category based on limited or insufficient evidence, often leading to inaccurate or unfair assumptions. | Young children often overgeneralize grammatical rules, like saying "goed" instead of "went."  幼儿经常过度概括语法规则，比如用“goed”而不是“went”。 | Synonyms: oversimplify, stereotype, extrapolate | Antonyms: specify, differentiate, particularize | verb
