eardrum:/ˈɪərdrʌm/ (US), /ˈɪədrʌm/ (UK)| n. |耳膜；鼓膜|A thin membrane that separates the outer ear from the middle ear, vibrating in response to sound waves.|She ruptured her eardrum in a diving accident. 她在一次潜水事故中弄破了耳膜。|Synonym: tympanic membrane|Antonym: N/A|noun
