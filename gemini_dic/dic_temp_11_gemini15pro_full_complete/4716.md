alewife: /ˈeɪlˌwaɪf/ | n. | 北美大西洋沿岸的一种鲱鱼; 泼妇，悍妇 | A small anadromous fish (Alosa pseudoharengus) of the herring family, found along the Atlantic coast of North America.  A woman who is perceived as a scold or shrew. | 例句：The alewife swim upstream to spawn.  鲱鱼逆流而上产卵。| 近义词：herring, shad (for the fish); shrew, nag (for the woman) | 反义词: (No direct antonym for the fish);  pleasant, agreeable (for the woman) | 词性: noun


alewife: /ˈeɪlˌwaɪf/ | n. |  女店主，酒店女老板 (古语) |  A woman who keeps an alehouse.  (archaic) | 例句：The alewife served a tankard of ale.  酒店女老板端上一大杯麦芽酒。 | 近义词：barmaid, landlady | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *alewif, from Old English *ealuwīf (“ale-woman”), equivalent to ealu (“ale”) + wīf (“woman”). The pejorative sense developed later.
