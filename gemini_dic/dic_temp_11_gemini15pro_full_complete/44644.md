crowstone:/ˈkroʊˌstoʊn/| n.|界石，鸦石|A large stone, often one of a series, placed to mark a boundary, especially in or near water. Crowstones often mark the limits of a manor, parish or port. In some locations, particularly Essex, England, they also mark fishing rights or oyster beds. |The crowstone marked the furthest point of the king's jurisdiction. 这块鸦石标志着国王管辖范围的最远点。

The crowstone near Southend-on-Sea marks the limit of the City of London's jurisdiction over the River Thames.  绍森德海边的鸦石标志着伦敦市对泰晤士河管辖范围的界限。

|近义词：boundary stone, landmark, marker|反义词: N/A|词性:noun
