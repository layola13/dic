chromogen:/ˈkroʊmədʒən/ | n. | 色原；生色基；色素原 |A substance that can be readily converted into a dye or other colored compound.| The pathologist used a chromogen to stain the tissue sample so that specific cells could be visualized under a microscope. 病理学家使用色原对组织样本进行染色，以便在显微镜下观察特定细胞。 | synonyms: precursor, substrate | antonyms: dye, pigment | noun

chromogenic:/ˌkroʊməˈdʒɛnɪk/ | adj. | 生色的；色原的 | Producing color or pigment; pertaining to a chromogen. |  The chromogenic reaction allowed the researchers to identify the presence of the bacteria. 生色反应使研究人员能够识别细菌的存在。The chromogenic substrate was added to the enzyme solution. 将生色底物添加到酶溶液中。 | synonyms: color-producing, pigment-forming | antonyms: achromatic | adjective
