go-around:/ˈɡoʊ əˌraʊnd/| n.| 复飞，绕行，争吵 |A maneuver in which an aircraft interrupts its approach to landing and climbs back up for another attempt. Also, a detour or argument.| The pilot had to execute a go-around because another aircraft was on the runway. 飞行员不得不复飞，因为另一架飞机在跑道上。| synonyms: overshoot, second attempt, detour, argument | antonyms: landing, agreement, straight path | noun

go-around:/ˈɡoʊ əˌraʊnd/| n.| 分配，一份 |A share or portion of something.| Everyone got a go-around of the cake. 每个人都分到了一块蛋糕。| synonyms: share, portion, allotment | antonyms: whole, entirety | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrasal verb "go around," meaning to circulate or make a detour. The noun usage likely developed from the aviation context, where a "go-around" is a standard procedure.
