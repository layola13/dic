disinformation:/ˌdɪsɪnfərˈmeɪʃən/ | n. | 虚假信息，假情报 | The spread of disinformation can have serious consequences. |  The government accused the media of spreading disinformation about the war. | 近义词: misinformation, propaganda, lies, falsehoods | 反义词: truth, facts, accuracy | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + information.  The prefix "dis-" comes from Latin, meaning "apart," "asunder," "away," "utterly," or having a privative, negative, or reversing force. "Information" comes from Old French "informacion"  meaning "formation, instruction, education," from Latin "informationem"  meaning "outline, concept, idea."
