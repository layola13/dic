gridlock: /ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk/ (British) /ˈɡrɪdˌlɑːk/ (American)| n. |交通堵塞；僵局|A situation where streets are so blocked by traffic that vehicles cannot move.|例句：The accident caused a gridlock on the highway. 事故导致高速公路交通堵塞。

例句：The negotiations reached a gridlock, with neither side willing to compromise. 谈判陷入僵局，双方都不愿妥协。|近义词：traffic jam, standstill, deadlock, stalemate|反义词: free flow, movement|词性: 名词

gridlocks: /ˈɡrɪdˌlɒks/ (British) /ˈɡrɪdˌlɑːks/ (American)| v. (third-person singular simple present gridlocks, present participle gridlocking, simple past and past participle gridlocked) |造成交通堵塞；使陷入僵局|To cause a traffic jam; to bring to a standstill.|例句：Heavy snow gridlocked the city centre. 大雪导致市中心交通瘫痪。

例句：The political process was gridlocked by partisan bickering.  政治进程因党派纷争而陷入僵局。|近义词：blocks, jams, stalls|反义词:expedites, facilitates|词性: 动词
