daguerreotypist:/dəˈɡɛrəˌtaɪpɪst/ | n. | 达盖尔银版照相师 | A person who takes daguerreotypes, an early type of photograph. | Example 1: The daguerreotypist carefully polished the silver plate. 达盖尔银版照相师仔细地擦拭着银板。

Example 2:  Finding a skilled daguerreotypist was difficult in those days. 在那个年代，找到一位熟练的达盖尔银版照相师很困难。 | 近义词: photographer | 反义词:  (No direct antonym) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *daguerréotypiste*, from *daguerréotype* (daguerreotype) + *-iste* (a suffix denoting a person practicing a particular art or profession).  *daguerréotype* is named after Louis-Jacques-Mandé Daguerre, the inventor of the process.
