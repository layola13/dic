knock-about:/ˈnɒkəˌbaʊt/ (UK) /ˈnɑːkəˌbaʊt/ (US)| adj. n.|耐用的，随便的；闹剧；|A knockabout comedy is one with lots of slapstick humour and rough, noisy action.  A knockabout person is boisterous and enjoys rough-and-tumble activities.  As a noun, it can refer to a small, open sailing boat, often used for casual sailing or racing.|例句：They put on a knockabout farce that had the audience in stitches. 他们上演了一出滑稽闹剧，让观众笑破肚皮。

The children enjoyed a knockabout on the beach. 孩子们在沙滩上尽情玩耍。|近义词：slapstick, boisterous, rambunctious, rumbustious, farcical (for the adjective); romp, frolic (for the noun) |反义词:refined, sophisticated, gentle, quiet|词性:adjective, noun

knock-about:/ˈnɒkəˌbaʊt/ (UK) /ˈnɑːkəˌbaʊt/ (US)| n.|耐用的物品；随便的衣服；|Something, especially a boat or a piece of clothing, that is designed to withstand rough usage. |例句：He wore his old knockabout clothes for gardening. 他穿着他那套耐穿的旧衣服做园艺。
