caper:/ˈkeɪpər/| n. vi.|跳跃，雀跃；滑稽古怪的举动；(常指非法的)冒险，(尤指)抢劫；刺山柑花蕾；|A playful skipping movement. An illegal or questionable act or escapade, especially one with an element of risk or daring. The pickled flower bud of the caper bush, used as a seasoning. |The children were capering about in the garden. 孩子们在花园里蹦蹦跳跳。He was involved in a caper that ended badly. 他参与了一场以悲剧收场的冒险活动。Pasta with lemon, butter and capers.  柠檬黄油刺山柑意面。|近义词：frolic, gambol, prank, escapade, antic, adventure, exploit|反义词:trudge, plod|词性:名词, 动词

词源: From Middle English *capre, from Anglo-Norman capre, Old North French capri (“goat; leap, jump”), from Latin caper (“goat”). Doublet of chievre.
