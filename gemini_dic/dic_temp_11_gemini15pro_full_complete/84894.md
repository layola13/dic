hanker:/ˈhæŋkər/| v. |渴望，热切希望|To have a strong desire for or to long for something. |She hankered for a return to her homeland. 她渴望回到故土。|近义词：yearn, crave, long for, desire|反义词: dislike, hate|verb

Etymology->
From Middle Dutch *hankeren "to hang around," probably frequentative of hangen "to hang."  The sense evolution is perhaps from "linger" to "linger in expectation" to "long for."
