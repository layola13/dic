hacking:/ˈhækɪŋ/| n. | 黑客行为，非法入侵电脑系统 | The act of gaining unauthorized access to a computer system. |  He was arrested for hacking into government databases. 他因入侵政府数据库而被捕。

hacking:/ˈhækɪŋ/| n. | 砍，劈; 骑马远足 | The act of cutting or chopping something roughly. / A long ride on horseback. | The gardener was hacking away at the overgrown bushes. 园丁正在砍伐 overgrown 的灌木丛。 /  They went hacking through the forest trails. 他们骑马穿越森林小径。


hacking:/ˈhækɪŋ/| adj. | 烦人的，恼人的(主要用于英式英语) | Annoying, irritating. |  She had a hacking cough that kept her up all night. 她一直咳嗽，整晚没睡。


Etymology: From the verb "hack," which originally meant "to cut or chop roughly."  The computing sense developed from its use in the phrase "to hack one's way through something," meaning to solve a problem in a clever or unorthodox way.

Synonyms:  (for the computing sense) cracking, cyberattack, cyber intrusion, data breach, infiltration
Antonyms:  (for the computing sense)  protecting, securing, defending
