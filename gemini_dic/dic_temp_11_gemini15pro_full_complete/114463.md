making-for:/ˈmeɪkɪŋ fɔːr/| phrasal verb |走向，导致，促成| contributes to; results in; heads towards |例句：The dark clouds are making for a storm. 乌云预示着暴风雨即将来临。|近义词：heading towards, contributing to, resulting in, leading to|反义词：diverting from, preventing, hindering|词性：动词短语

Etymology:  "Make" comes from Old English "macian" (to make, do, perform). "For" comes from Old English "for," meaning "toward," "before," "on account of," or "during." The combination "making for" developed its idiomatic sense of movement and contribution over time.
