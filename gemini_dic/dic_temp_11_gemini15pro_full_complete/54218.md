disfigurers:/dɪsˈfɪɡjərərz/ | n. | 毁容者，损毁者 |  People or things that cause disfigurement, making something less beautiful or aesthetically pleasing by damaging its appearance. | The acid attack left her with permanent scars, making the perpetrators disfigurers of her beauty. 酸性袭击给她留下了永久的疤痕，使肇事者成为她美丽的毁容者。

The vandals, spraying graffiti all over the historical monument, acted as disfigurers of our cultural heritage.  这些破坏者在历史遗迹上到处喷涂鸦，就像是在毁坏我们的文化遗产。| 近义词：vandals, spoilers, damagers | 反义词：restorers, beautifiers | 词性: 名词(复数)

词源:
From the verb "disfigure" + "-er" (agent suffix).  "Disfigure" comes from "dis-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of") + "figure" (meaning "shape" or "form"). Ultimately from Latin "dis-" and "figura".
