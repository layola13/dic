capriciousnesses:/kəˈprɪʃəsnəsɪz/
n.
反复无常；任性；善变
The capriciousnesses of the weather made it difficult to plan a picnic.
天气反复无常，很难计划野餐。
capriciousnesses:  The state or quality of being capricious; unpredictable changes in mood or behavior.  This refers to multiple instances of such behavior.
Her capriciousnesses were legendary, one moment demanding silence, the next throwing a lavish party.  她的反复无常是出了名的，一会儿要求安静，一会儿又举办奢华派对。
近义词:whims, vagaries, fickleness, unpredictability
反义词:steadiness, consistency, stability
词性:noun (plural)

capricious:/kəˈprɪʃəs/
adj.
反复无常的；任性的；善变的
The capricious wind made it difficult to sail.
反复无常的风使得航行困难。
capricious:  Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior.
He's such a capricious boss, I never know what to expect.  他是个反复无常的老板，我从来不知道会发生什么。
近义词:whimsical, fickle, erratic, unpredictable
反义词:steady, consistent, stable
词性:adjective

Etymology: From French *capricieux*, from Italian *capriccioso*, from *capriccio* "a sudden start or motion," from *capro* "goat" + *-iccio* (suffix forming adjectives).  The image is of a goat's unpredictable leaping or frisking.
