intrench/ɪnˈtrɛntʃ/ /ɪnˈtrɛnʃ/ | v. | 壕堑；挖壕固守；牢固确立 | to establish (an attitude, habit, or belief) so firmly that change is very difficult or unlikely; to dig trenches for defense | The enemy intrenched themselves behind the river. 敌人在河后挖壕固守。 | 近义词：entrench, fortify, establish, secure | 反义词：expose, weaken, undermine | 词性：动词

intrenched/ɪnˈtrɛntʃt/ /ɪnˈtrɛnʃt/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；牢固确立的 | firmly established and difficult or unlikely to change | The government's policies are deeply intrenched. 政府的政策根深蒂固。 | 近义词：established, ingrained, rooted, fixed | 反义词：unstable, precarious, weak | 词性：形容词
