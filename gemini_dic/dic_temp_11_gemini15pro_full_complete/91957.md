hypermodernity:/ˌhaɪpərmoʊˈdɜːrnəti/ |n.| 超现代性 |A cultural condition characterized by an intensification of modernity, often including features such as globalization, technological advancement, media saturation, and a sense of fragmentation and instability.|  The architecture of Dubai embodies hypermodernity with its dizzying skyscrapers and artificial islands. 迪拜的建筑以其令人眼花缭乱的摩天大楼和人工岛屿体现了超现代性。|  Hypermodernity is reflected in the constant bombardment of information we receive through social media.  超现代性体现在我们通过社交媒体不断接收到的信息轰炸中。 | 近义词: postmodernity, late modernity | 反义词: tradition, pre-modernity | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From hyper- (meaning "excessive" or "beyond") + modernity.  "Hyper-" comes from the Greek "hyper" (ὑπέρ), meaning "over," "above," or "beyond." "Modernity" comes from the Late Latin "modernitas,"  from "modernus" ("modern").
