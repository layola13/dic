obstreperosity:/əbˌstrepəˈrɑːsəti/
n.|喧闹, 吵闹, 骚动|The state of being noisy and difficult to control.  Characterized by loud, unruly, and boisterous behavior.|The teacher was unable to conduct the class due to the students' obstreperosity.|Synonyms: boisterousness, clamor, tumult, uproar, racket|Antonyms: quiet, calmness, peacefulness, tranquility|noun


obstreperous:/əbˈstrepərəs/
adj.|喧闹的，吵闹的，任性的|Noisy and difficult to control.|The obstreperous child was finally removed from the restaurant.|Synonyms: unruly, boisterous, clamorous, vociferous, rambunctious|Antonyms: quiet, well-behaved, docile, obedient|adjective


Etymology:
From Latin obstrepere ("to make a noise against, oppose noisily"), from ob- ("against") + strepere ("to make a noise").
