fantasizing: /ˈfæntəsaɪzɪŋ/ | n. | 幻想，空想 | The act of creating imaginary scenarios in one's mind. |  She spent hours fantasizing about winning the lottery. 她花了几个小时幻想自己中了彩票。| synonyms: daydreaming, imagining, visualizing | antonyms: facing reality, being practical | gerund

fantasize: /ˈfæntəsaɪz/ | v. | 幻想，想象 | To create imaginary scenarios in one's mind. | He often fantasizes about traveling the world. 他经常幻想环游世界。| synonyms: daydream, imagine, visualize | antonyms: face reality, be practical | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *phantasia, from Ancient Greek φαντασία (phantasía, “appearance, imagination, phantom”), from φαντάζω (phantázō, “I make visible, I reveal”), from φαίνω (phaínō, “I show, I bring to light”).
