deframed:/diːˈfreɪmd/ | adj. | 去框的，脱离框架的 | Removed from a frame, either literally or figuratively; freed from constraints or limitations. | The artist deframed the painting to give it a more modern look. 艺术家将这幅画从画框中取出，使其更具现代感。 | The deframed narrative challenged conventional storytelling.  脱离框架的叙事挑战了传统的叙事方式。 | framed, restricted, confined | unrestricted, liberated, open | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
de- (prefix):  From Latin, meaning "down, away from, off, reversal, intensive."  Indicates removal, separation, or negation.
frame (noun/verb): From Old French "frem" (framework, structure).  Refers to a rigid structure that surrounds or encloses something.
