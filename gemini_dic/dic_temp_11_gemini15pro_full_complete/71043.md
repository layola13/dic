first-class:/ˌfɜːrst ˈklæs/ (US) /ˌfɜːst ˈklɑːs/ (UK)| n.|头等舱；头等邮递|The most expensive and luxurious class of accommodation and service (especially on a train, ship, or plane).|例句：He booked a first-class ticket to London. 他预订了一张去伦敦的头等舱机票。|近义词：luxury class, premium class|反义词：economy class, second class|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "first" + "class."  "First" meaning foremost or highest in rank or importance, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *furistaz, superlative of *fur- "before." "Class" from Old French classe, from Latin classis "a division of the people," originally "a summoning," from calare "to call out."
