overperemptorily:/ˌoʊvərˌpɛrˈɛmptərɪli/| adv.|专横地，断然地，不容置疑地|In a manner that is overly assertive, decisive, and final, leaving no room for debate or opposition.  It suggests an authoritarian and often arrogant tone. |The chairman overperemptorily dismissed the suggestion. 主席断然否决了这项建议。|Synonyms: dictatorially, arbitrarily, dogmatically, imperiously|Antonyms: hesitantly, tentatively, submissively|Word Type: Adverb

overperemptory:/ˌoʊvərˈpɛrəmptɔːri/| adj.|专横的，断然的，不容置疑的|Excessively assertive, decisive, and final, leaving no room for debate or opposition.  It suggests an authoritarian and often arrogant tone. |He gave an overperemptory refusal. 他断然拒绝。|Synonyms: dictatorial, arbitrary, dogmatic, imperious|Antonyms: hesitant, tentative, submissive|Word Type: Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *overperemptorie, from Anglo-Norman *overperemptorie, from Medieval Latin *overperemptōrius, from Latin peremptōrius (“destructive, decisive, final”), from perimō (“I destroy, abolish, kill”).  The prefix over- intensifies the meaning.
