hitchhiker:/ˈhɪtʃˌhaɪkər/| n.|搭便车的人；免费搭车旅行的人|A person who travels by getting free rides from passing vehicles.|Example: The hitchhiker stood by the road, thumbing a ride.  那个搭便车的人站在路边，竖起大拇指请求搭车。|Example:  She picked up a hitchhiker on her way to San Francisco.  她在去旧金山的路上载了一名搭便车的人。|Synonym: free rider, thumb-twiddler|Antonym: driver, passenger|noun

hitchhikers:/ˈhɪtʃˌhaɪkərz/| n.|搭便车的人们；免费搭车旅行的人们|Plural form of hitchhiker, referring to multiple people who travel by getting free rides.|Example: The group of hitchhikers were dropped off at the next town.  这群搭便车的人在下一个城镇下车了。|Example: Hitchhikers are often seen on long stretches of highway.  在长长的公路上经常能看到搭便车的人。|Synonym: free riders|Antonym: drivers, passengers|noun
