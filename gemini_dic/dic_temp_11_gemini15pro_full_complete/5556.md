al-Qaeda:/ælˈkaɪdə/ | n. |基地组织| A militant Sunni Islamist multi-national organization founded in 1988 by Osama bin Laden and several other Arab volunteers who fought against the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan in the 1980s.  It has been designated as a terrorist group by the United Nations Security Council, the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO), the European Union, and various countries.| Al-Qaeda claimed responsibility for the 9/11 attacks. 基地组织声称对911事件负责。|Synonyms: terrorist group, militant organization | Antonyms: peacekeeping force, humanitarian organization | Noun
