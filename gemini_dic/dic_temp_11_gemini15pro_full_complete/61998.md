enamelar:/ɪˈnæmələr/
n.|搪瓷匠；瓷釉工人|A worker who applies enamel to metal, glass, or pottery.|The enamelar carefully painted the intricate design onto the copper plate.|近义词: enameller|反义词: 无|词性: 名词

enamelar:/ɪˈnæmələr/
v.|涂瓷釉；上瓷漆|To coat or decorate with enamel.|He enamelared the brooch with a delicate floral pattern.|近义词: glaze, varnish|反义词: strip, remove|词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *enamelen*, from Old French *esmaillier* (modern French *émailler*), from *esmail* (“enamel”), from Frankish *smalt*, from Proto-Germanic *smaltą* (“smalt, enamel”), from Proto-Indo-European *smēlt- (“to melt, smelt”).  Cognate with Old High German *smalto* (“enamel”), Old English *smeltan* (“to melt, refine”). The -ar suffix indicates a person who performs the action of the verb.
