longspur: /ˈlɔːŋspɜːr/ (US), /ˈlɒŋspɜːr/ (UK)| n.| 长刺歌雀|A type of small, ground-dwelling songbird found in open grasslands, typically with long hind claws.  These birds are known for their elaborate courtship displays and melodious songs.|The longspur sang a cheerful tune from its perch on a fence post. 长刺歌雀在篱笆桩上唱着欢快的曲调。|Synonyms: lark, pipit|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, could consider birds of prey as predators of longspurs)|noun
