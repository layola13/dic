carmagnole: /ˌkɑːrməˈnjɔːl/ | n. | 卡马尼奥尔舞; 卡马尼奥尔歌曲; 欢快的歌曲或舞蹈 | A lively song and dance popular during the French Revolution. It was often performed in the streets and public squares as a celebration of revolutionary fervor.  | The revolutionaries danced the carmagnole in the streets after the storming of the Bastille.  革命者攻占巴士底狱后在街上跳起了卡马尼奥尔舞。|  The band played a rousing carmagnole to celebrate the victory. 乐队演奏了一首激动人心的卡马尼奥尔舞曲来庆祝胜利。 | Synonyms: dance, song, celebration | Antonyms: lament, dirge | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *carmagnole*, from Carmagnola, a town in northern Italy, where a distinctive style of clothing associated with the dance originated.
