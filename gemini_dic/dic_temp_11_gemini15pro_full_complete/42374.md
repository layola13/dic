counterbalanced:/ˌkaʊntərˈbælənst/ | adj. | 平衡的，抵消的 | made equal or steady by adding an opposite force or weight; having different parts or aspects that balance each other | The experiment was carefully counterbalanced to avoid bias.  这个实验经过精心平衡以避免偏差。 |  The design of the bridge is counterbalanced to withstand strong winds.  桥梁的设计是为了抵御强风而平衡的。 | synonyms: offset, compensated, neutralized | antonyms: unbalanced, unequal, biased | adjective

counterbalance:/ˌkaʊntərˈbæləns/ | n. | 平衡力，抵消因素 | a weight or force that balances another | The heavy counterbalance kept the crane stable.  沉重的平衡物使起重机保持稳定。 |  Her natural optimism served as a counterbalance to his pessimism.  她天生的乐观精神抵消了他的悲观情绪。 | synonyms: offset, counterpoise | antonyms: imbalance | noun

counterbalance:/ˌkaʊntərˈbæləns/ | v. | 平衡，抵消 | to have an equal or opposite effect on something | The advantages and disadvantages of the new system appear to counterbalance each other.  新系统的优缺点似乎相互抵消。 |  The government's spending on education is counterbalanced by cuts in other areas. 政府在教育上的支出被其他领域的削减所抵消。 | synonyms: offset, compensate, neutralize | antonyms: unbalance | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- (against, opposite) + balance (equilibrium). The word "balance" ultimately comes from the Latin "bilanx" (having two scales), from "bi-" (two) + "lanx" (plate, dish, scale).
