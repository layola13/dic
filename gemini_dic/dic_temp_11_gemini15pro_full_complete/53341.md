disadvantageable:/ˌdɪsədˌvænˈteɪdʒəbl/
adjective
处于劣势的，处于不利地位的
Being in a position of disadvantage, especially with regard to social or economic factors.
Students from disadvantageable backgrounds often face greater challenges in accessing quality education.  来自弱势背景的学生在获得优质教育方面往往面临更大的挑战。
The program aims to support disadvantageable communities.  该计划旨在支持弱势群体。
近义词: disadvantaged, underprivileged, deprived
反义词: advantaged, privileged, affluent
词性: adjective

disadvantage:/ˌdɪsədˈvæntɪdʒ/
noun
不利条件，劣势
A condition or circumstance that puts someone in a worse position than others.
One disadvantage of living in the city is the high cost of housing.  住在城市的一个缺点是住房成本高。
His lack of experience was a disadvantage in applying for the job.  他缺乏经验是他申请这份工作的劣势。
近义词: drawback, handicap, detriment
反义词: advantage, benefit, asset
词性: noun
