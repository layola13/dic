elicitors:/ɪˈlɪsɪtərz/ | n. | 引出物，引发剂 | Something that elicits a particular response or reaction. |  The researchers used various pictures as elicitors to provoke emotional responses. 研究人员使用各种图片作为引发剂来激发情绪反应。|  The comedian's jokes were effective elicitors of laughter.  这位喜剧演员的笑话是有效的笑声引发剂。 |  stimuli, triggers, cues | inhibitors, suppressants | noun

elicitor:/ɪˈlɪsɪtər/ | n. | 引出物，引发剂 | Something that elicits a particular response or reaction. | The question served as an elicitor for a lively discussion. 这个问题引发了热烈的讨论。| Bright colors are often used as elicitors of attention in advertising.  鲜艳的颜色经常在广告中被用作吸引注意力的诱因。 | stimuli, triggers, cues | inhibitors, suppressants | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "elicit" + "-or" (agent suffix). "Elicit" comes from the Latin "elicere," meaning "to draw out" or "to entice."
