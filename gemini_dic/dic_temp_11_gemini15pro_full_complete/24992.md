brutalisms: /ˈbruːtəlɪzəmz/ | n. |野兽主义（建筑风格）,粗野主义| a style of architecture that flourished from the 1950s to the mid-1970s, characterized by massive, imposing structures of exposed concrete and austere geometric designs. | The Barbican Centre in London is a famous example of brutalism.  伦敦的巴比肯中心是野兽主义的著名例子。| synonyms:  concrete architecture, modernist architecture | antonyms: ornate architecture, decorative architecture | noun

Etymology: From French *brutalisme*, from *brut* (“raw”), referring to the use of raw concrete.  源自法语 *brutalisme*，来自 *brut*（“生的”），指的是使用未经加工的混凝土。
