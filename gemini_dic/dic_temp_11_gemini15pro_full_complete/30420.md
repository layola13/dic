centralise:/ˈsentrəlaɪz/ (UK) /ˈsɛntrəˌlaɪz/ (US)| v. | 集中；中央集权 | To organize the control of a country, organization, or system so that everything is done or decided in one place. |例句：The government decided to centralise the education system. 政府决定集中教育系统。


例句：The company centralised its operations in London.  公司将其业务集中在伦敦。

近义词：consolidate, concentrate, unify | 反义词:decentralise, distribute, disperse |词性:动词

centralization:/ˌsɛntrələˈzeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌsentrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (UK)| n. | 集中化；中央集权化 | The act or process of centralising something. | 例句：The centralization of power led to increased efficiency. 权力集中导致效率提高。


例句：Many argue against the centralization of political power. 许多人反对政治权力集中。

近义词：consolidation, concentration, unification | 反义词:decentralization, distribution, dispersion |词性:名词
