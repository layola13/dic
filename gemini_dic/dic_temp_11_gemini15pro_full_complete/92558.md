hysterias:/hɪˈstɪəriəz/ | n. |歇斯底里，癔症发作(复数) | A fit of uncontrollable laughter or crying.  A state of extreme emotional excitement, fear, or anger. |  The hysterias that followed the announcement of the celebrity's death were widespread.  这位名人去世的消息传出后，歇斯底里的情绪蔓延开来。 | synonyms: frenzies, outbursts, fits, paroxysms | antonyms: calmness, composure, tranquility | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hysteria, from Latin hystericus (“of the womb; hysterical”), from Ancient Greek ὑστερικός (husterikós, “of the womb”), from ὑστέρα (hustéra, “womb”).  Originally believed to be a female affliction caused by disturbances of the uterus.
