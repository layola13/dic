broach:/broʊtʃ/| n. |胸针；烤肉叉；拉刀 |A decorative pin worn on clothing.  A pointed tool for roasting meat. A tool for enlarging or shaping a hole. |She wore a beautiful antique broach on her lapel.  The chef used a long broach to turn the roasting pig. The mechanic used a broach to enlarge the hole in the metal plate.|近义词：(n.) pin, brooch, spit, reamer|反义词: None|词性:n.
broach:/broʊtʃ/| vt. |提出；开启；钻孔；打破(沉默) |To raise a subject for discussion. To open or begin something. To pierce or tap a cask or other container in order to draw out liquid. To break the surface of something.|He decided to broach the subject of a pay raise with his boss.  They broached a new bottle of wine to celebrate.  The workers broached the pipeline to drain the remaining oil.  She finally broached the silence by asking about his family.|近义词：(v.) raise, introduce, open, tap, pierce|反义词: close, conceal|词性:v.

词源: From Old French *broche, from Vulgar Latin *brocca "pointed instrument", from Gaulish *brocca, *broga "point, awl".
