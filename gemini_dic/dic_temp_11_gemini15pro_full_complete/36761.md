collude:/kəˈluːd/| v.|串通，共谋|To act together secretly or illegally in order to deceive or cheat someone.|The two companies colluded to fix prices.  这两家公司串通操纵价格。|Synonyms: conspire, plot, scheme|Antonyms: cooperate fairly, compete honestly|verb

colluded:/kəˈluːdɪd/| v. (past tense of collude)|串通，共谋（过去式）|The companies had colluded for years before they were caught.  这些公司串通多年才被抓获。|Synonyms: conspired, plotted, schemed|Antonyms: cooperated fairly, competed honestly|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "colludere" (to play together, to conspire), from "com-" (together) + "ludere" (to play).  The original meaning was broader, encompassing both playful collaboration and deceitful plotting.  Over time, the negative connotation of secret plotting became dominant.
