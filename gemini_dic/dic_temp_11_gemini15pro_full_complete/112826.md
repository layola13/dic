lucifer:/ˈluːsɪfər/| n.|路西法；金星；启明星；火柴|Lucifer, originally a name for the planet Venus, became associated with the devil after a passage in Isaiah 14:12, which speaks of the "morning star" being cast down from heaven. This passage was later interpreted by some Christian writers as referring to Satan's fall from grace. In modern usage, "Lucifer" is often used as a proper name for Satan, particularly in literature and popular culture.|例句：Lucifer, the fallen angel, is a common figure in mythology. 路西法，堕落天使，是神话中常见的形象。

例句：He struck a lucifer to light his cigarette. 他划了一根火柴点燃香烟。|近义词：Satan, Devil, the Morning Star (when referring to the planet Venus)|反义词：God, Jesus (in a theological context)|词性：名词
