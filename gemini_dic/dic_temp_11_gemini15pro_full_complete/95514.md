incloister: /ɪnˈklɔɪstər/ | vt. n. | 使禁闭，修道院 | To confine within or as if within a cloister; seclude. A place of religious seclusion, such as a monastery or convent. | The writer incloistered himself in his study for months to finish his novel.  作家把自己关在书房里好几个月来完成他的小说。| The monks lived in the incloister of the monastery.  僧侣们住在修道院的回廊里。 | 近义词: confine, seclude, isolate, monastery, convent | 反义词: free, liberate, release | 词性: 动词, 名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *incloistren*, from Anglo-Norman encloistrer, from Old French encloistre (“cloister”), from Vulgar Latin *inclaustrium*, from Latin inclūsus (“shut in”), past participle of inclūdere, from in- + claudere (“to shut, to close”).
