motion:/ˈməʊʃən/ (UK) /ˈmoʊʃən/ (US)| n. vi.| 运动，移动；提议，动议|A change of position of a body with respect to time and relative to a reference point.  The act of proposing something, typically formally in a meeting. |The car was in motion. 汽车处于运动状态。 He made a motion to adjourn the meeting. 他提议休会。|近义词：movement, action, proposal|反义词：stillness, inaction|词性: noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mociun*  (modern French *motion*), from Latin *mōtiōn*, *mōtiō* "a moving, motion," from *mōtus*, past participle of *movēre* "to move."
