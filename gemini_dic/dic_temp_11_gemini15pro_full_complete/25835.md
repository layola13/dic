burgle:/ˈbɜːrɡəl/| v.|盗窃，非法闯入|to enter a building illegally with intent to commit a crime, especially theft.|Someone burgled the office and stole two computers. 有人闯入办公室偷走了两台电脑。|近义词：rob, steal, thieve|反义词:protect, secure|词性:Verb

词源:  From the noun "burglar," which comes from the Old French word "burgleor" meaning "housebreaker."  Ultimately, it traces back to Germanic roots related to the concept of breaking or bursting.
