cotillion: /kəˈtɪljən/ | n. | 方块舞；社交舞会；正式舞会 | A formal ball, especially one at which debutantes are presented to society.  A type of social dance popular in the 18th and 19th centuries. |  The debutantes made their grand entrance at the cotillion.  初次参加社交活动的年轻女子在正式舞会上隆重登场。|  They learned the steps of the cotillion. 他们学习了方块舞的舞步。 | synonyms: ball, formal, prom, dance | antonyms: informal gathering, casual party | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cotillon*, a kind of petticoat, from Old French *cote*, tunic. The connection is likely due to the elaborate dresses worn at such events.
