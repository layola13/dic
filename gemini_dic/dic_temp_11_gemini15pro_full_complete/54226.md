disfranchise:/ˌdɪsˈfræntʃaɪz/ | vt. |剥夺公民权；剥夺选举权|To deprive of a franchise, of a legal right, or of some privilege or immunity; especially : to deprive of the right to vote.|例句：The new law disfranchised many poor people. 新法律剥夺了许多穷人的选举权。

例句：They were disfranchised for not paying their taxes. 他们因未缴税而被剥夺了选举权。|近义词：deprive, divest|反义词：enfranchise|词性：动词

disfranchise:/ˌdɪsˈfræntʃaɪz/ | n. |剥夺权利|The act of disfranchising or the state of being disfranchised.|例句：The disfranchisement of black voters was a major issue in the civil rights movement. 黑人选民被剥夺选举权是民权运动中的一个主要问题。

例句：The group protested against the disfranchisement of their members. 该团体抗议剥夺其成员的权利。|近义词：deprivation, divestiture|反义词：enfranchisement|词性：名词
