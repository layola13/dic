concoct:/kənˈkɒkt/ (UK), /kənˈkɑːkt/ (US)| v.|调制，配制；编造，捏造|To create or devise something, often in a skillful or ingenious way, especially food or drink by mixing various ingredients, or a story or plan.|She concocted a delicious soup from leftover vegetables. 她用剩菜 concoct 出一碗美味的汤。|近义词：devise, invent, fabricate, formulate, create, prepare, mix, blend|反义词：discover, find, destroy, ruin|词性: 动词

concocter:/kənˈkɒktər/ (UK), /kənˈkɑːktər/ (US)| n.|调制者；捏造者|A person who concocts something, especially food or drink, or a story or plan.|He was the chief concocter of the marketing campaign. 他是这次营销活动的主要策划者。|近义词：inventor, creator, fabricator, author|反义词：discoverer, finder|词性: 名词

词源:
From Late Latin *concoctus, past participle of concoquere (“to boil together, digest, mature”), from con- (“together”) + coquere (“to cook”).
