crippledom: /ˈkrɪp(ə)ldəm/ | n. | 残疾; 残疾状态; 残疾人 |The state of being crippled; disability.  A collective term for crippled people.|  The war left many soldiers in crippledom. 战争使许多士兵残废。| 近义词: disability, infirmity, handicap, impairment | 反义词: health, ability, wellness | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From cripple + -dom.  "Cripple" comes from Old English "crypel"  meaning "to creep,"  influenced by Old Norse "krypill,"  both ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*krupilaz."  "-dom" is an Old English suffix meaning "state or condition of being."
