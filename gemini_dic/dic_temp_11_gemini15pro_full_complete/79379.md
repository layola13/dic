glibnesses: /ˈɡlɪbnəsɪz/ | n. |油嘴滑舌，流利但不真诚| The politician's glibnesses made the audience distrust him. |  A smooth, fluent, and insincere way of speaking, often used to deceive or impress.  | 例句：His glibnesses about the economy couldn't hide the real problems. 他对经济的油嘴滑舌掩盖不了真正的问题。

| 近义词：smooth talk, slickness, superficiality, volubility | 反义词：sincerity, earnestness, genuineness | 词性：noun (plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From glib + -ness.  Glib itself comes from Middle Low German glibberig "slippery," related to Dutch glibberig and German glibberig. Originally referring to a slippery surface, the meaning extended to smooth and fluent speech, and eventually acquired the negative connotation of insincerity.
