outsource:/ˈaʊtsɔːrs/| v.|外包，外购；|To obtain (goods or a service) from an outside or foreign supplier, especially in place of an internal source.|Many companies outsource their IT operations to specialized firms. 许多公司将他们的IT业务外包给专业公司。|例句：The company decided to outsource its customer service.  这家公司决定将其客户服务外包。

例句：They outsourced the manufacturing of the components. 他们把零件的制造外包出去了。|近义词：subcontract, contract out, delegate, farm out|反义词：insource|词性：动词

outsourcing:/ˈaʊtsɔːrsɪŋ/| n.|外包，外购；|The practice of obtaining goods or a service from an outside or foreign supplier, especially in place of an internal source.|Outsourcing can help companies reduce costs. 外包可以帮助公司降低成本。|例句：The company's outsourcing strategy has saved them millions. 这家公司的外包策略为他们节省了数百万美元。

例句：Outsourcing of certain jobs can lead to job losses in the home country.  某些工作的外包会导致本国的工作岗位流失。|近义词：subcontracting, offshoring|反义词：insourcing|词性：名词
