intrigued: /ɪnˈtriːɡd/ | adj. | 感兴趣的，好奇的，被迷住的 |  Feeling a strong desire to know or learn more about something; fascinated.  Origin: From the past participle of the verb *intrigue*.  Late 16th century: past participle of intrigue, from French intriguer, from Italian intrigare ‘entangle, perplex,’ from Latin intricare ‘entangle.’ |  She was intrigued by his story. 她被他的故事迷住了。 |  curious, fascinated, interested, captivated, absorbed | bored, apathetic, indifferent, uninterested | adjective
