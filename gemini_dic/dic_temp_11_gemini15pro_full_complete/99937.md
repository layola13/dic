internments:/ˈɪnˌtɜːnmənts/ |/ɪnˈtɜːrnmənts/| n. |拘留；扣留；收容|The act of interning or confining, especially during wartime.  The state of being interned or confined.|例句：The internments of Japanese Americans during World War II was a dark chapter in American history.  二战期间对日裔美国人的拘留是美国历史上黑暗的一章。

例句：The government ordered the internments of all enemy aliens. 政府下令拘留所有敌国侨民。|近义词：detention, confinement, imprisonment|反义词：release, liberation|词性: 名词

词源: From the French word "internement," derived from the verb "interner," meaning "to intern." Ultimately from Latin "internus," meaning "internal, inward."
