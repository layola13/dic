cachexia:/kəˈkek.si.ə/ | n. |恶病质；羸弱；衰弱|A condition of physical wasting away due to the loss of weight and muscle mass that occurs in patients with diseases such as advanced cancer or AIDS.|The patient's advanced cancer resulted in severe cachexia.|Synonyms: wasting, emaciation, atrophy, decline|Antonyms: robustness, vigor, health|Noun
