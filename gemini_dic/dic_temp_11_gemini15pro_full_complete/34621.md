cleft:/klɛft/ | adj. n. | 裂开的，劈开的；裂缝，裂口 | split, divided, or partially divided; a split or crack in something, especially in rock or the earth | The river flowed through a deep cleft in the mountains.  河流流经群山中一道深深的裂缝。| 近义词: split, cracked, fissured, gap, crevice | 反义词: joined, whole, sealed | 词性: 形容词, 名词

cleft:/klɛft/ | adj. | （下巴、上唇等）有裂的，裂开的 | (of a chin, upper lip, etc.) having a vertical indentation in the middle | He had a strong jaw with a cleft chin. 他有着轮廓分明的下巴和一个有裂的下巴。 | 近义词: split, divided | 反义词: smooth, undivided | 词性: 形容词


词源:
From Old English *cleft (attested only in the past participle clofen), from Proto-Germanic *kluft-, from Proto-Indo-European *gleub-, *gloub- (“to carve, hollow out, cleave”). Cognate with Dutch kluft (“cleft, ravine”), German Kluft (“chasm, gorge, abyss”).  Related also to cleave.
