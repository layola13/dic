antennule:/ænˈtɛnjuːl/ /ænˈtɛnˌjuːl/ | n. |小触角，触须|A small antenna, typically one of a pair on the head of a crustacean, used for sensing. |The lobster uses its antennules to detect chemicals in the water. 龙虾用它的小触角探测水中的化学物质。|Synonym: antenna, feeler |Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "body" in a very broad sense) |noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Modern Latin *antennula, diminutive of Latin antenna "sail yard."  The resemblance of the crustacean's antennules to the yardarms of a ship gave rise to the name.
