idealized:/aɪˈdiːəlaɪzd/ | adj. |理想化的，完美的|Made to seem perfect or better than reality; romanticized.|例句：She had an idealized view of marriage. 她对婚姻抱有理想化的看法。|例句：The movie presented an idealized version of life in the countryside. 这部电影呈现了乡村生活的理想化版本。|近义词：romanticized, glorified, perfect, utopian|反义词：realistic, pragmatic, flawed, imperfect|词性:形容词

idealize:/aɪˈdiːəlaɪz/ | v. |理想化，将…理想化|To regard or represent as perfect or better than in reality.|例句：He tends to idealize his childhood. 他倾向于将自己的童年理想化。|例句：Don't idealize the past; it wasn't perfect. 不要把过去理想化；它并不完美。|近义词：romanticize, glorify, exalt|反义词：denigrate, disparage, vilify|词性:动词

idealization:/aɪˌdiːələɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |理想化|The action of regarding or representing something as perfect or better than in reality.|例句：His idealization of country life led him to move to a farm. 他对乡村生活的理想化使他搬到了一个农场。|例句：The book is a pure idealization of love and marriage. 这本书是对爱情和婚姻的纯粹理想化。|近义词：romanticization, glorification|反义词：realism|词性:名词
