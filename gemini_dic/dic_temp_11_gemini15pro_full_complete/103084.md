jeopardy:/ˈdʒɛpərdi/ (US), /ˈdʒɛpədi/ (UK)| n. |危险，风险；危难|A state of being exposed to loss, harm, death, or injury.|The hiker's life was in jeopardy after he fell into the ravine.  徒步旅行者掉进峡谷后生命垂危。|Synonyms: danger, peril, risk, hazard|Antonyms: safety, security|noun

jeopardies:/ˈdʒɛpərdiz/ (US), /ˈdʒɛpədiz/ (UK)| n. |危险，风险；危难（jeopardy的复数形式）|Multiple situations of being exposed to loss, harm, death, or injury.  It is rare, but used.  Think of it as multiple, simultaneous instances of jeopardy.|The ongoing drought put the crops and livestock in several jeopardies.  持续的干旱使农作物和牲畜面临多种危险。 |Synonyms: dangers, perils, risks, hazards|Antonyms: safeties, securities|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *jeu parti "divided game, game of chance," from jeu "game" (from Latin jocus "joke, game") + parti "divided" (past participle of partir "to divide," from Latin partire "to divide, share, distribute"). Originally a term in chess for a situation where a piece could be captured on the next move.
