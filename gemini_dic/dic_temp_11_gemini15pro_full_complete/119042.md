metic:/ˈmɛtɪk/| adj.|一丝不苟的，小心翼翼的；过分注意细节的|Characterized by extreme or excessive care in the consideration or treatment of details.|He's very metic about his appearance. 他对自己的外表非常一丝不苟。|近义词：fastidious, scrupulous, meticulous, punctilious|反义词：careless, sloppy, negligent|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *métique* "timid, fearful," from Latin *meticulosus* "fearful, timid," from *metus* "fear, dread."  The semantic shift from "fearful" to "overly careful" likely arose from the idea of being afraid to make a mistake.
