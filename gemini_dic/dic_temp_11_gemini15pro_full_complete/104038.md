ETYMOLOGY->

From Old English "Geol," which in turn comes from Proto-Germanic "*jeχwlan," meaning "Yule." The meaning shifted to "July" later, likely due to the influence of the Roman calendar and the renaming of months after Roman figures.  The month was originally named after Julius Caesar.
