allomorph:/ˈæləmɔːrf/ | n. | 同位异形 | One of two or more variant forms of a morpheme.  A morpheme is the smallest grammatical unit in a language. Allomorphs are different forms of the same morpheme that occur in different phonetic environments. | Example 1: The English plural morpheme has three allomorphs: /-s/ (cats), /-z/ (dogs), and /-ɪz/ (dishes).  例句1：英语复数词素有三种同位异形：/-s/ (cats)、/-z/ (dogs) 和 /-ɪz/ (dishes)。
