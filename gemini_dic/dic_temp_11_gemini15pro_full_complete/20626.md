blackleg:/ˈblækˌleɡ/ | n. |  骗子；破坏罢工的人；黑腿病（牛羊疾病） | A person who continues to work when fellow workers are on strike. Also, a disease affecting cattle and sheep. |  Example 1: The strikers branded him a blackleg for crossing the picket line.  罢工者称他为破坏罢工者，因为他越过了警戒线。

Example 2: The farmer was worried about the spread of blackleg among his cattle. 这位农民担心黑腿病在他的牛群中蔓延。 | 近义词: scab, strikebreaker, traitor | 反义词: striker, unionist | 词性: 名词

词源：
From black and leg. Apparently from the dark discoloration of the leg muscles in affected cattle. This sense is first recorded in the late 18th century. The figurative sense referring to strikebreakers dates from the 1830s.
