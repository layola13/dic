fork-out:/fɔːrk ˈaʊt/ | phrasal verb | 付出（尤指一大笔钱，不情愿地）| To pay (a sum of money), typically a large one and unwillingly. |  Example: I had to fork out $500 for the car repairs. 我不得不支付500美元的汽车修理费。

Example:  She forked out a small fortune for her daughter's wedding. 她为女儿的婚礼花了一大笔钱。| 近义词: pay up, shell out, cough up | 反义词: earn, receive | 词性: 动词短语

fork-out:/fɔːrk aʊt/ | n. |  支出，花费 (尤指一大笔钱，不情愿地) | The act of paying out money, especially a large sum unwillingly. | Example: The fork-out for the new equipment was considerable. 新设备的花费相当可观。

Example: That was a painful fork-out, but it was necessary. 那是一笔很痛苦的支出，但是很必要。| 近义词: expense, outlay, expenditure | 反义词: income, profit | 词性: 名词
