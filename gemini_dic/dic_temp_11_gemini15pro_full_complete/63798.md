epidemiography:/ˌɛpɪˌdiːmiˈɒɡrəfi/ | n. | 流行病学史; 流行病描述; |The study of the geographical distribution of disease and its history.  It describes the patterns of disease occurrence and spread in populations, often focusing on specific time periods or geographical areas. | Example 1: The epidemiography of cholera in the 19th century reveals much about sanitation and public health practices of the time. 19世纪霍乱的流行病学史揭示了当时的卫生和公共卫生实践。

Example 2:  The epidemiography of HIV/AIDS has changed significantly since the virus was first identified. 自病毒首次被发现以来，艾滋病毒/艾滋病的流行病学史发生了显著变化。 | 近义词: descriptive epidemiology, disease mapping | 反义词:  None | 词性: noun


Etymology:  From "epi-" (Greek: upon, among) + "demos" (Greek: people) + "-graphy" (Greek: writing, description).
