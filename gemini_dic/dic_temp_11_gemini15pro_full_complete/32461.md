chin-up:/ˈtʃɪn ʌp/ (US), /ˈtʃɪn ʌp/ (UK)| n.|引体向上；振作精神|An exercise in which you pull yourself up to a bar by your hands, with your palms facing towards you. Also used figuratively to mean to become more cheerful and determined.|例句：He can do ten chin-ups. 他可以做十个引体向上。

例句：Give yourself a chin-up; things will get better.  振作起来，事情会好转的。|近义词：pull-up (exercise), cheer up (figurative)|反义词: (no direct antonym) |词性:noun

chin-up:/ˈtʃɪn ʌp/ (US), /ˈtʃɪn ʌp/ (UK)| v.|振作起来|To make an effort to be more cheerful and determined.|例句：Chin up! It'll all be over soon. 振作起来！一切很快就会结束。

例句：She told herself to chin up and face the challenge. 她告诉自己要振作起来，迎接挑战。|近义词：cheer up, buck up, perk up|反义词: (no direct antonym) |词性:verb

词源:  "Chin-up" comes from the physical action of pulling oneself up to a bar until the chin is above it. The figurative meaning developed from the idea of needing strength and determination to raise one's chin in a difficult situation.
