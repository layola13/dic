adducer:/əˈdʌktər/ | n. | 内收肌 | A muscle that draws a part of the body toward the median line or toward the axis of a limb. |  The adductor muscles of the thigh bring the legs together. 大腿内收肌使双腿并拢。 | synonyms: adductor muscle, converging muscle | antonyms: abductor | noun

adduct:/əˈdʌkt/ | v. | 内收 | To draw (a limb) toward the median line of the body or toward the axis of a limb. | She adducted her arm to show the scar. 她内收手臂以展示伤疤。 | synonyms: draw in, pull in | antonyms: abduct | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *adducere*, from *ad-* ("to, toward") + *ducere* ("to lead, draw").
