fantique:/fænˈtɪːk/ | n. |古董, 值钱的旧东西;  狂热爱好者 (尤指收集者) | An object, typically an article of furniture or work of art, that is valued because it is old.  Also, a person with a strong interest in a particular subject or activity, especially collecting. |  例句：She found a real fantique in the attic. 她在阁楼里发现了一件真正的古董。

例句：He's a fantique about old cars. 他是个老车迷。 | 近义词：antique, collector, enthusiast, aficionado | 反义词：modern, contemporary | 词性: 名词


词源:  Probably a humorous alteration of *fanatique*, from French *fanatique* "fanatical," from Late Latin *fanaticus* "inspired by a deity, frenzied," from Latin *fanum* "temple." The meaning shifted from "fanatic collector" to "object of their collecting."
