hallucinators:/həˈlusɪneɪtərz/ | n. | 幻觉者；产生幻觉的人 |  A person who experiences hallucinations.  A person who sees, hears, smells, tastes, or feels things that are not really there. | The researchers studied the brain activity of hallucinators to understand the mechanisms behind these experiences. 研究人员研究了幻觉者的脑活动，以了解这些经历背后的机制。

The new drug reduced the frequency of hallucinations in the hallucinators. 这种新药减少了幻觉者的幻觉频率。 |  Synonyms: visionaries, fantasists | Antonyms: realists | noun
