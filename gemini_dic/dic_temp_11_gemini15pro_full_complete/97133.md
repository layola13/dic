infiltrating: /ˈɪnfɪltreɪtɪŋ/
adjective
渗透的; 潜入的
Entering or gaining access to a place or organization surreptitiously, especially for hostile purposes.
The infiltrating soldiers disguised themselves as civilians. 渗透的士兵把自己伪装成平民。
The company's security system was breached by infiltrating hackers. 该公司的安全系统被渗透的黑客攻破了。
Synonyms: permeating, penetrating, invading, insinuating
Antonyms: withdrawing, retreating, extracting


infiltrate: /ˈɪnfɪltreɪt/
verb
渗透; 潜入
1. (of a liquid) permeate (something) by filtration.
2. Enter or gain access to (an organization, place, etc.) surreptitiously and gradually, especially in order to acquire secret information.
Rainwater infiltrated the soil. 雨水渗透了土壤。
Enemy agents infiltrated the government. 敌方特工渗透了政府。
Synonyms: permeate, penetrate, pervade
Antonyms:  extract, withdraw, retreat
noun
渗透物; 潜入者
Something that infiltrates, especially a group of people.
The infiltrates were quickly discovered and arrested. 渗透者很快被发现并逮捕。
Synonyms:  invader, intruder
