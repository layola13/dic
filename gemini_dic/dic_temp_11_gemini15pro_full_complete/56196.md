dodger:/ˈdɑːdʒər/ (US), /ˈdɒdʒər/ (UK)| n.| 逃避者，躲避者，骗子|Someone who avoids something, especially a duty or responsibility, or someone who cheats or tricks people.|He's a tax dodger. 他是个逃税者。| The dodger quickly disappeared into the crowd. 那个骗子迅速消失在人群中。|synonyms: evader, shirker, cheat, swindler|antonyms: conformist, honest person|noun


dodgy:/ˈdɑːdʒi/ (US), /ˈdɒdʒi/ (UK)| adj.| 可疑的，狡猾的，危险的，不牢靠的|Dishonest, unreliable, or risky.|The whole deal sounded a bit dodgy to me.  整个交易听起来有点可疑。|The stairs looked a bit dodgy, so I took the elevator. 楼梯看起来不太稳固，所以我坐了电梯。|synonyms: suspicious, shady, risky, precarious|antonyms: reliable, trustworthy, safe, secure|adjective
