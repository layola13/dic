deadheads:/ˈdɛdˌhɛdz/
n.|无效部分；空车返回的行程；（尤指）乐队“感恩而死”的歌迷|A non-revenue-producing portion of a transportation run; a return trip with no load or paying passengers. Also, especially, a devoted fan of the band the Grateful Dead.|The truck driver had to make a long deadhead back to the depot. 卡车司机不得不空车返回仓库。|Synonyms: non-revenue trip, empty run, dead run|Antonyms:  paying trip, loaded run|noun
deadheads:/ˈdɛdˌhɛdz/
n.|（从植物上）剪掉的花（尤指枯萎的花）；枯枝|A flower, especially a faded one, removed from a plant; a dead or withered part of a plant.|She deadheaded the roses to encourage new blooms. 她剪掉了玫瑰上的枯萎花朵，以促进新的花朵生长。|Synonyms: faded flower, withered flower, spent bloom|Antonyms: fresh flower, blooming flower|noun
