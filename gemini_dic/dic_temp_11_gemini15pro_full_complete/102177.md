itacism: /ˈɪtəˌsɪzəm/ | n. | iota主义，过度使用i音|  A pronunciation style characterized by the frequent use or repetition of the "ee" sound, typically represented by the Greek letter iota (ι), especially in excess or where another vowel sound would be more appropriate.  It can also refer to the misspelling of a word by substituting an "i" for another vowel. | The speaker's itacism made it difficult to understand which words he was using.  演讲者的iota主义使得很难理解他使用的单词。| synonyms:  lisp,  mispronunciation | antonyms: - | noun
