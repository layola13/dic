mincings:/ˈmɪnsɪŋz/| n. |切碎；矫揉造作的行为；装腔作势|The act of mincing something, especially food, into very small pieces; affectedly dainty or delicate behaviour.|She disliked the mincings of the other guests at the party. 她不喜欢聚会上其他客人装腔作势的样子。|近义词：affectations, posturings, pretenses|反义词：naturalness, sincerity, genuineness|词性:名词

词源: From mince (v.) + -ing + -s (plural).  Ultimately from Old French "mincier" (to cut into small pieces), from Vulgar Latin *minutiare, from Latin "minutus" (small).
