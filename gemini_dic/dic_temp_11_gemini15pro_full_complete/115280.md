manky:/ˈmæŋki/| adj.|肮脏的，破旧的，令人不快的|Unpleasant, dirty, or in bad condition, often due to age or neglect.  It can also be used to describe something that is of poor quality or generally undesirable.|That old teddy bear is getting a bit manky. 那只旧泰迪熊有点脏了。|Synonyms: dirty, grimy, shabby, scruffy, grotty|Antonyms: clean, pristine, immaculate|adjective


manky:/ˈmæŋki/| adj.|糟糕的，差劲的|(informal, chiefly British) Bad, of poor quality, or unsatisfactory.|He did a manky job of painting the fence. 他把篱笆漆得很糟糕。|Synonyms: awful, terrible, lousy, rubbish|Antonyms: excellent, wonderful, superb|adjective
