embolus:/ˈɛmbələs/ (US), /ˈɛmbələs/ (UK)| n.|栓子；栓塞|A mass (such as a blood clot, air bubble, or piece of fatty deposit) that travels through the bloodstream and lodges in a vessel, obstructing it.|The doctor feared the embolus would travel to his lungs.|Synonym: clot, plug, thrombus|Antonym: None|Noun
