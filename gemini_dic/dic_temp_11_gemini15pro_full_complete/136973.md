词源： "nonspeculativenesses" comes from "speculative", which has its roots in the Latin word "speculativus," meaning "contemplative" or "theoretical."  The prefix "non-" negates the meaning of "speculative," resulting in "nonspeculative," meaning "not based on speculation."  The suffix "-ness" turns the adjective into a noun, indicating the state or quality of being nonspeculative. Finally, the suffix "-es" creates the plural form.
