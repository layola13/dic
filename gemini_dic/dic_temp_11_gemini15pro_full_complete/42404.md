counterclaimants:/ˈkaʊntərˌkleɪmənts/ | n. | 反诉人，反索赔人 |  A person or entity who files a counterclaim against another party in a legal proceeding.  This means they are not just defending themselves but also making their own claim against the original claimant. | The counterclaimants argued that they were owed money by the original plaintiff. 反诉人辩称，原告欠他们钱。| The counterclaimants presented compelling evidence to support their claim. 反诉人提供了令人信服的证据来支持他们的主张。 |  Synonyms: counter-plaintiffs, respondents (in some contexts) | Antonyms: claimants, plaintiffs | Noun
