exorbitance:/ɪɡˈzɔːrbɪtəns/ /ɪɡˈzɔːr-/| n.| 过度；过高；极度|The exorbitance of the hotel prices shocked us.  酒店价格之高让我们震惊。| English Explanation:  The state or quality of being exorbitant; excessiveness, especially in amount or price.|例句：The exorbitance of the CEO's salary caused outrage among the employees.  首席执行官过高的薪水激怒了员工。

例句：The exorbitance of the fees charged by the lawyer was questioned by the client.  客户质疑律师收取的过高费用。|近义词：excess, extravagance, immoderateness|反义词: moderation, reasonableness|词性: noun

exorbitant:/ɪɡˈzɔːrbɪtənt/ /ɪɡˈzɔːr-/| adj.| 过度的；过高的；极端的|The prices at the resort were exorbitant.  度假村的價格過高。| English Explanation: Exceeding the bounds of custom, propriety, or reason, especially in amount or price; excessive.|例句：They were charged an exorbitant price for a simple repair.  他们被收取了简单的修理费，价格过高。

例句：The restaurant's exorbitant prices kept most people away.  这家餐馆过高的价格让大多数人望而却步。|近义词：excessive, outrageous, unreasonable|反义词: reasonable, moderate, affordable|词性: adjective
