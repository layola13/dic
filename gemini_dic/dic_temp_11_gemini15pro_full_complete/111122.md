literarinesses:/ˌlɪtəˈrerɪnəsɪz/
|n.| 文学性（复数）|The quality or state of being literary; the characteristics that make a work of writing considered literary.  It often refers to the use of literary devices, elevated language, and a focus on aesthetic qualities rather than purely informational or practical purposes.|His prose is marked by its literarinesses, employing intricate metaphors and allusions. 他的散文以其文学性为标志，运用了复杂的隐喻和典故。|
|Synonyms: artistry, refinement, sophistication, eloquence|
|Antonyms: artlessness, simplicity, plainness|
|noun|
