conspiring:/kənˈspaɪrɪŋ/| v.|密谋，共谋，协力|acting or working together toward the same result or goal, typically a harmful or illegal one. |例句：They were accused of conspiring to overthrow the government. 他们被指控密谋推翻政府。

例句：The weather seemed to be conspiring against us; it rained every day of our trip.  天气似乎在和我们作对，我们旅行的每一天都下雨。|近义词：plotting, scheming, colluding|反义词:cooperating (in a positive sense), dissenting|词性:Verb (present participle)

Etymology:
From Latin *conspirare* "to agree, unite, plot," from *com-* "together" + *spirare* "to breathe." The original sense was of people whispering together, then plotting together.
