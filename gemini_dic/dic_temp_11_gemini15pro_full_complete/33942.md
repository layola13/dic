cirrocumulus:/ˌsɪroʊˈkjuːmjələs/ | n. |卷积云|A type of high-altitude cloud characterized by small, white, flaky patches arranged in a regular pattern, often resembling fish scales or ripples.  These clouds are made of ice crystals and are often a precursor to unsettled weather.| Example: The cirrocumulus clouds dotted the sky, hinting at the approaching storm. 卷积云点缀着天空，预示着即将来临的暴风雨。|Example:  We saw beautiful cirrocumulus formations during our flight. 在我们的飞行过程中，我们看到了美丽的卷积云层。|Synonym: mackerel sky (informal),  |Antonym: clear sky | Noun
