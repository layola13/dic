melancholias:/ˌmɛlənˈkəʊliəz/ | n. | 忧郁，愁绪 | A state of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause.  A collection or series of melancholic states or works of art. |  Her melancholias were a constant companion. 她的忧郁情绪一直伴随着她。|  The artist's collection of melancholias explored themes of loss and longing.  这位艺术家的忧郁作品集探索了失落和渴望的主题。 | synonyms: sadness, dejection, depression, gloom, sorrow | antonyms: happiness, joy, elation, cheerfulness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin melancholia, from Greek melankholía, from melas (black) + kholé (bile), based on the ancient belief that an excess of black bile caused melancholia.
