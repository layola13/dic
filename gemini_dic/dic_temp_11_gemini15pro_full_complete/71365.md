flagellata:/ˌflædʒəˈleɪtə/ | n. |  鞭毛虫 |A large group of single-celled organisms characterized by the presence of one or more whip-like appendages called flagella, which are used for locomotion. | Example 1: Flagellata are a diverse group, including both free-living and parasitic species.  鞭毛虫种类繁多，包括自由生活的和寄生的种类。

flagellate:/ˈflædʒəleɪt/ | vt. | 鞭打，鞭笞 | To whip or flog someone.  | Example 1:  He was flagellated for his crimes. 他因犯罪而被鞭打。

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *flagellatus, past participle of flagellare (“to whip”), from Latin flagellum (“whip, scourge”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *fleg- (“to strike”).
