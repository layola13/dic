feudalism:/ˈfjuːdəlɪzəm/| n. |封建主义；封建制度|A social system that existed in Europe during the Middle Ages in which people worked and fought for nobles who gave them protection and the use of land in return.|The history professor lectured on the complexities of feudalism in medieval Europe. 欧洲史教授讲授了中世纪欧洲封建制度的复杂性。|Feudalism was a defining characteristic of medieval society. 封建主义是中世纪社会的一个典型特征。|近义词：manorialism|反义词: democracy, capitalism|词性: noun

Etymology:  From Medieval Latin *feudālismus*, from *feudālis* (“feudal”) + *-ismus*.  Ultimately from Frankish *fehu* (“cattle, property”)
