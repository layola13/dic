bey:/beɪ/
n.|贝伊（旧时对总督、官员或先生的尊称，尤用于奥斯曼帝国）|A title of respect formerly used in the Ottoman Empire for governors, officials, or gentlemen. |He was addressed as "Bey." 他被称为“贝伊”。|近义词:sir, mister, esquire|反义词: (No direct antonym exists, as it's a title)|词性:名词

词源: From Turkish *bey*, from Old Turkic *beg*. Ultimately from Proto-Turkic *bäg* (“leader, ruler”).
