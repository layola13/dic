lychnoscope: /ˈlɪknəˌskoʊp/ | n. | 窥视孔，小观察窗 | A small window or opening in a church wall, often near the altar, through which lepers or other excluded persons could view the service.  |  Example: The leper peered through the lychnoscope, hoping to catch a glimpse of the communion. 麻风病人透过窥视孔，希望能瞥见圣餐仪式。

Example: The lychnoscope provided a way for those outside the church to participate in the service. 窥视孔为教堂外的人提供了一种参与礼拜的方式。 | Synonym: squint, hagioscope | Antonym: N/A | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *λυχνοσκόπιον (lychnoskopion) < λύχνος (lychnos, “lamp”) + σκοπέω (skopeō, “to watch, look at”).  Originally thought to be for watching a lamp or vigil light, but later associated with lepers.
