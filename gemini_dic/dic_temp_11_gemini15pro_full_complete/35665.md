cobalts:/ˈkoʊbɔːlts/| n.|钴（金属元素），钴颜料|A brittle, hard, lustrous, silver-gray metal, a chemical element (symbol Co) with atomic number 27.  It is found in various minerals and is used to make alloys, blue glass, and pigments.|The cobalts in the painting gave it a vibrant hue.|近义词: (as a pigment) ultramarine, azure|反义词:  (None for the element itself.  For the color, perhaps) drab, muted|词性:noun
