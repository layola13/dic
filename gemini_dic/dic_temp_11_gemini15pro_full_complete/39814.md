conspicuity:/kənˌspɪkˈjuːɪti/| n.|显著，显眼，引人注目|The conspicuity of the building made it a landmark. 这座建筑的显眼使它成为地标。|例句：The error was of such conspicuity that it could not be missed.  这个错误如此显眼，不可能被忽略。

例句：Her bright red dress ensured her conspicuity at the party. 她鲜红色的连衣裙确保她在派对上引人注目。|近义词：prominence, noticeability, visibility|反义词：inconspicuousness, obscurity, unnoticeability|词性:名词

conspicuous:/kənˈspɪkjuəs/| adj.|显著的，显眼的，引人注目的|He was conspicuous by his absence.  他的缺席很引人注目。|例句：She wore a conspicuous red hat. 她戴着一顶显眼的红色帽子。

例句：The advertisement was placed in a conspicuous position. 广告被放在一个显眼的位置。|近义词：prominent, noticeable, visible|反义词：inconspicuous, unnoticeable, obscure|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *conspicuitas, from Latin conspicuus (“visible, striking”), from conspicio (“I catch sight of, observe”), from con- (“together”) + specio (“I look, see”).
