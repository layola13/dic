chronologist:/krəˈnɒlədʒɪst/| n.|年代学家；年表编者|A person who studies chronology (the science of arranging events in their order of occurrence in time).  A scholar who specializes in establishing the dates of past events.|The chronologist meticulously documented the dynasty's reign, creating a detailed timeline of their rule.|近义词：historian, annalist|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "fabricator" in a context where the chronologist's work is intentionally inaccurate)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *chronologista*, from Ancient Greek *χρονολόγος* (khronológos, “narrator of events in time”), from *χρόνος* (khrónos, “time”) + *λόγος* (lógos, “word, speech, account”).
