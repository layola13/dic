blish: /blɪʃ/
Verb
装腔作势；炫耀；虚张声势
To talk in a pompous or self-important way; to boast or brag.
He's always blishing about his connections. 他总是吹嘘自己的人脉。
Synonyms: boast, brag, swagger, show off
Antonyms: be modest, downplay

Etymology:  Origin uncertain.  Possibly related to "blush" or "polish."  First recorded use in the late 19th century.
