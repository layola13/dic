mooring's:/ˈmʊərɪŋz/| n.|系泊设备；停泊处|A mooring is a place where a boat or ship is secured.  It can refer to the physical equipment used to secure the vessel (anchors, chains, ropes, buoys) or the designated location for mooring.  It is the possessive form of "mooring," indicating ownership or association.|The ship returned to its moorings after the voyage.  船只航行结束后返回其泊位。|Synonyms: anchorage, berth, dock|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "sailing," "voyage," "at sea")|noun
