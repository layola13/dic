confoundingly:/kənˈfaʊndɪŋli/
adverb|令人困惑地，使人难懂地|in a way that causes surprise or confusion, especially by not behaving in the way that you expect|The results were confoundingly inconclusive. 结果令人困惑地没有定论。|Synonyms: puzzlingly, bewilderingly, perplexingly, bafflingly|Antonyms: clearly, understandably, plainly|词性: 副词


confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/
verb|使困惑，使混淆|1. to cause surprise or confusion in (someone), especially by acting in a way that they do not expect. 2. (often passive) to prove (someone or something) wrong; to defeat (someone or something)|His behavior confounded everyone. 他的行为让每个人都感到困惑。|Synonyms: confuse, bewilder, perplex, baffle|Antonyms: clarify, enlighten, explain|词性: 动词

Etymology:
From Middle English *confounden*, from Old French *confondre*, from Latin *confundere* (“to pour together, mix, mingle, confuse”), from *con-* (“together”) + *fundere* (“to pour”).
