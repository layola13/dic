antimodernist:/ˌæntiˈmɒdənɪst/ /ˌæntiˈmɑːdərɪnɪst/| n. adj.|反现代主义者，反现代主义的|Someone who opposes modernism, especially in the arts, and prefers traditional forms and styles.  A person who is averse to modern trends, ideas, or practices.|
The gallery showcased the work of several prominent antimodernists.  这家画廊展出了几位著名的反现代主义者的作品。
Her antimodernist views were reflected in her preference for classical music. 她的反现代主义观点体现在她对古典音乐的偏爱上。
|近义词：traditionalist, conservative|反义词: modernist, avant-garde|词性: noun, adjective

antimodernism:/ˌæntiˈmɒdəˌnɪzəm/ /ˌæntiˈmɑːdərˌnɪzəm/| n.|反现代主义|Opposition to modernism, especially in the arts, favoring traditional forms and styles.|
Antimodernism gained traction in the early 20th century.  反现代主义在20世纪初获得了关注。
The architecture of the building reflected a strong sense of antimodernism. 这座建筑的建筑风格体现了强烈的反现代主义色彩。
|近义词：traditionalism, conservatism|反义词: modernism, avant-gardism|词性: noun


Etymology:  "anti-" (against) + "modernist" (a supporter of modernism).  "Modernism" itself derives from "modern" (from Old French "moderne," ultimately from Latin "modernus").
