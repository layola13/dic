abseiling: /ˈæbseɪlɪŋ/ (UK) /ˌæpˈseɪlɪŋ/ (US)
n.|绳降;游绳下降|The act of descending a rock face or other near-vertical surface by using a doubled rope coiled around the body and fixed at a higher point.|We went abseiling down the cliff face. 我们沿着悬崖面进行了绳降。|近义词:rappelling (US), roping down|反义词:climbing, ascending|词性:名词

abseil: /ˈæbseɪl/ (UK) /ˌæpˈseɪl/ (US)
v.|绳降;游绳下降|To descend a rock face or other near-vertical surface by using a doubled rope coiled around the body and fixed at a higher point.|She abseiled down the building. 她从大楼上绳降下来。|近义词:rappel (US), rope down|反义词:climb, ascend|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *abseilen*, from *ab-* (down) + *seil* (rope).  First used in English in the early 20th century.
