giggling: /ˈɡɪɡəlɪŋ/ | n.  v. | 咯咯笑，傻笑 |  The act of laughing lightly and repeatedly in a silly or nervous way.  Derived from the verb "giggle," which comes from the Middle English "giglen," probably imitative of the sound of laughter. |  She couldn't stop giggling at his jokes. 她忍不住咯咯地笑他的笑话。 |  laughing, chuckling, tittering | frowning, crying | v.
giggling: /ˈɡɪɡəlɪŋ/ | adj. |  咯咯笑的，傻笑的| Describes someone who is laughing lightly and repeatedly in a silly or nervous way. Derived from the verb "giggle." | The giggling children were enjoying the game. 咯咯笑着的孩子们正在享受游戏。| laughing, chuckling, tittering | frowning, crying | adj.
