dismay:/dɪsˈmeɪ/| n. vt.|沮丧，惊慌；使沮丧，使惊慌|A feeling of unhappiness and disappointment.|The news of her death filled us with dismay. 她去世的消息使我们悲痛万分。|近义词：disappointment, consternation, alarm, shock|反义词：joy, delight, happiness|n.
dismay:/dɪsˈmeɪ/| vt.|使沮丧，使惊慌|To cause (someone) to feel discouraged or disappointed.|The team was dismayed by their loss in the final. 球队因决赛失利而感到沮丧。|近义词：discourage, dishearten, daunt|反义词：encourage, hearten, cheer|vt.
