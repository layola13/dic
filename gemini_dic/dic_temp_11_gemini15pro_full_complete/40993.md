coot:/kuːt/| n.| 傻瓜，笨蛋；(水鸟) 䴙䴘|A foolish or eccentric person.  A small water bird of the rail family, with lobed toes and short wings.|The old coot next door is always yelling at the squirrels. 隔壁的老傻瓜总是对着松鼠大喊大叫。|The American coot is a common sight on many lakes and ponds. 美洲骨顶鸡在许多湖泊和池塘中很常见。|Synonyms: fool, idiot, simpleton, nincompoop, (for the bird) grebe|Antonyms: genius, sage, wise person (no direct antonym for the bird)|noun


coot:/kuːt/| adj.(informal, chiefly North American)|疯狂的, 精神错乱的| crazy, mentally unstable |"He went coot and started throwing furniture out the window." 他疯了，开始把家具扔出窗外。 | Synonyms: crazy, mad, insane, demented | Antonyms: sane, rational, lucid |adjective
