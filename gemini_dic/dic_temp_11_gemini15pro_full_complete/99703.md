interlocutory:/ˌɪntərˈlɒkjʊtəri/ (US) /ˌɪntərˈlɑːkətɔːri/| adj.|中间的，暂时的，非最终判决的|Of, relating to, or made during the progress of a legal action and not final or definitive.|The judge issued an interlocutory injunction to prevent the company from selling the property until the lawsuit was resolved. 法官发布了一项中间禁令，以阻止该公司在诉讼解决之前出售该房产。|Synonyms: provisional, temporary, interim|Antonyms: final, conclusive, definitive|adjective

interlocutory:/ˌɪntərˈlɒkjʊtəri/ (US) /ˌɪntərˈlɑːkətɔːri/| n.|中间裁决，非最终判决|An interlocutory decree or judgment, especially one issued provisionally.|The interlocutory order required the defendant to provide certain documents to the plaintiff. 中间裁决要求被告向原告提供某些文件。|Synonyms: interim order, provisional judgment|Antonyms: final decree, definitive judgment|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *interlocutorius*, from Latin interlocūtor ("one who speaks between or in the midst of others"), from interloquor ("I speak between"), from inter ("between") + loquor ("I speak").
