brattishing:/ˈbrætɪʃɪŋ/
adjective
幼稚的，孩子气的，惹人讨厌的
Behaving in a childish, spoiled, or annoying way, often by complaining or demanding attention.
He was brattishing about not getting the toy he wanted. 他因为没有得到想要的玩具而耍孩子气。
synonyms: childish, immature, spoiled, petulant, churlish
antonyms: mature, grown-up, well-behaved, polite

词源:  "Brattishing" comes from the noun "brat," which has its roots in Old English "brætt" meaning "rag" or "piece of clothing," later evolving to refer to a child, especially a spoiled or badly behaved one.  The "-ish" suffix adds the sense of behaving like a brat.
