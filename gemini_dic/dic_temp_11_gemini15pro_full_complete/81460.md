gratuities: /ɡrəˈtjuːɪtiz/ /ɡrəˈtuːɪtiz/
| n. |小费，赏钱|A sum of money customarily given by a client or customer to certain service workers such as waiters, baggage porters, and taxi drivers, in addition to the basic price.|The restaurant automatically adds a 15% gratuity to the bill for large parties.  这家餐厅会自动将15%的小费添加到大型聚会的账单中。|Synonyms: tip, perk, bonus|Antonyms: deduction, penalty|noun

gratuities: /ɡrəˈtjuːɪtiz/ /ɡrəˈtuːɪtiz/
| n. |赠品，礼物|Something given freely or without recompense.|The company offered gratuities to its employees during the holiday season. 公司在节日期间向员工赠送礼物。|Synonyms: gift, present, donation|Antonyms:  fine, forfeiture|noun
