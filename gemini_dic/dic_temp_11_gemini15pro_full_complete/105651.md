kingfishers:/ˈkɪŋfɪʃərz/ | n. |翠鸟（复数）|A family of small to medium-sized, brightly colored birds known for their fish-hunting skills. |Example 1:  Kingfishers are often seen near rivers and lakes. 翠鸟经常在河流和湖泊附近被看到。

Example 2: We saw several kingfishers diving for fish. 我们看到几只翠鸟潜入水中捕鱼。|近义词:Alcedinidae (family name) |反义词:N/A |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "king's fisher," referring to its skill in fishing.  Originally, the name likely referred to the common kingfisher, later extended to the whole family.  "Fisher" comes from Old English "fiscere" from "fisc" (fish).
