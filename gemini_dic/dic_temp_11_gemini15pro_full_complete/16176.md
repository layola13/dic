banknotes:/ˈbæŋknəʊts/ (US) /ˈbæŋknoʊts/| n. | 纸币；钞票 | Pieces of paper money issued by a bank or government, used as a form of currency. | Example: The cashier counted out the banknotes carefully.  收银员仔细地数着钞票。

Example:  Counterfeit banknotes are a serious problem for the economy. 假钞对经济来说是一个严重的问题。 | Synonyms: bills, currency, notes, paper money | Antonyms: coins | noun

banknote:/ˈbæŋknəʊt/ (US) /ˈbæŋknoʊt/| n. | 纸币；钞票 | A single piece of paper money. | Example: He pulled a crumpled banknote from his pocket. 他从口袋里掏出一张皱巴巴的钞票。

Example: The banknote was so worn that it was barely recognizable. 这张钞票太破旧了，几乎认不出来了。 | Synonyms: bill, note, paper money | Antonyms: coin | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "bank" + "note."  Originally, banknotes were promissory notes issued by banks, promising to pay the bearer a specified amount of gold or silver.  Over time, these notes became a form of currency in their own right.
