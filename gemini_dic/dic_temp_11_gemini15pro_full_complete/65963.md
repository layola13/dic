exaggeratedly:/ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪtɪdli/
| adv. |夸张地；夸大地|in an exaggerated manner; to an excessive degree|She laughed exaggeratedly at his joke. 她夸张地大笑他的笑话。|近义词：overly, excessively, dramatically|反义词：moderately, subtly, understatedly|词性:副词

exaggerate:/ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪt/
| v. |夸大；夸张|represent (something) as being larger, greater, better, or worse than it really is.|Don't exaggerate - it wasn't that bad. 不要夸大其词——没有那么糟糕。|近义词：overstate, amplify, embellish|反义词：understate, downplay, minimize|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin exaggeratus, past participle of exaggerare "to heap up, increase," from ex- "out" + aggerare "to heap up," from agger "heap, mound."
