distractible:/dɪˈstræktɪbl/| adj.|易分心的，注意力不集中的|Easily diverted from one's original focus of attention; easily distracted.|Example: He's very distractible and has trouble focusing on his studies. 他很容易分心，很难集中精力学习。|Example: The open office environment proved too distractible for her to get any work done. 开放式办公环境对她来说太容易分心了，以至于无法完成任何工作。|近义词：inattentive, unfocused, scatterbrained|反义词：focused, attentive, concentrated|词性:形容词

distract:/dɪˈstrækt/| v.|使分心，转移(注意力)|To draw someone's attention away from something; divert.|Example: The noise from the street distracted him from his reading. 街上的噪音使他无法专心读书。|Example:  Don't distract her while she's driving. 她开车的时候不要打扰她。|近义词：divert, sidetrack|反义词：focus, concentrate|词性:动词

distraction:/dɪˈstrækʃn/| n.|分心，分心的事物|Something that prevents someone from giving their full attention to something else.|Example: The constant distractions made it difficult to concentrate.  不断的干扰使得很难集中注意力。|Example: Social media can be a major distraction for students. 社交媒体对学生来说可能是一个主要的干扰。|近义词：interruption, disturbance|反义词：focus, concentration|词性:名词
