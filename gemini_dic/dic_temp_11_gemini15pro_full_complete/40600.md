convection:/kənˈvekʃən/| n.|对流，传送，|The transfer of heat through a fluid (liquid or gas) caused by molecular motion.  It occurs when warmer areas of a liquid or gas rise to cooler areas in the liquid or gas.|Heat is transferred through the water by convection. 热量通过对流在水中传递。|近义词：circulation, transmission|反义词: conduction, radiation|词性:noun

convective:/kənˈvektiv/| adj.|对流的，传送的|Relating to, caused by, or characterized by convection.|Convective clouds often bring heavy rain. 对流云常常带来暴雨。|近义词：circulatory|反义词: conductive, radiative|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin convectio, convectiōn-,  "a carrying or bringing together," from Latin convect-, past participle stem of convehere "to carry or bring together," from com- "together" (see com-) + vehere "to carry" (see vehicle).
