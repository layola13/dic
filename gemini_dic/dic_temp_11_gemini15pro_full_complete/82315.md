grout: /ɡraʊt/ | n. v. | 水泥浆；灌浆；填缝料；用灰浆涂 | A thin mortar used to fill spaces between tiles or bricks.  To fill spaces with grout. |  The tiler carefully grouted the spaces between the new tiles.  瓦工小心地将新瓷砖之间的缝隙用水泥浆填满。 |  They grouted the foundations of the building. 他们给建筑物的地基灌浆。 | 近义词: cement, mortar, filler | 反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑 gap, opening 表示未填补的状态) | 词性: 名词, 动词


grout: /ɡraʊt/ | 词源:  From Middle English *gruten, *grouten, from Old French grote, grute (“coarse meal, grout”), from Frankish *grūt (“grit, groats”), from Proto-Germanic *grutą (“grit, groats”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵrew- (“to grind, rub”). Cognate with Dutch grut (“groats, grit”), German Grütze (“grit, groats”). |
