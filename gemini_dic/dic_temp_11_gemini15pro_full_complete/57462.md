draftees:/ˌdræfˈtiːz/| n. |被征召者；应征入伍者|People who are drafted into military service.|Many young draftees were sent to Vietnam during the war. 许多年轻的应征入伍者在战争期间被派往越南。|The draftees were nervous about their upcoming deployment. 这些被征召者对即将到来的部署感到紧张。|Synonyms: conscripts, recruits|Antonyms: volunteers|Noun


Etymology:
From draft + -ee.  "Draft" comes from the Middle English *draught*, meaning "act of drawing, pulling," from Old Norse *dráttr*, from *draga* "to draw, pull." The suffix "-ee" denotes one who undergoes or is affected by the action of the verb.
