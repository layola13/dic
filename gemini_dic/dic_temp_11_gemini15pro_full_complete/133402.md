nonfactually:/ˌnɒnfækˈtʃʊəli/ (US) /ˌnɑːnfækˈtʃuːəli/ (UK)| adv.| 非事实地；不真实地|  In a way that is not based on or concerned with facts.  It describes something said, written, or done without regard for factual accuracy.  Often implies a deliberate disregard for the truth, but not necessarily with malicious intent.| He spoke nonfactually about the incident, blending fiction with reality.| 近义词：inaccurately, falsely, untruthfully, fictitiously| 反义词：factually, truthfully, accurately| 词性: adverb
