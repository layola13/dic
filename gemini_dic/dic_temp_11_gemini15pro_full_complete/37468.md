commissioners:/kəˈmɪʃənərz/
n.
委员；专员；行政长官
A person appointed to an official position, often in a government or other organization, with responsibility for a particular area of activity.
The city commissioners voted to approve the new budget. 市政委员投票通过了新的预算。
The commissioners of the European Union met to discuss trade policy. 欧盟委员会委员们开会讨论贸易政策。
synonyms: officials, representatives, delegates, administrators, authorities, board members
antonyms: subordinates, employees, staff

Etymology:
From Old French comissioner, from Late Latin commissionarius, from Latin commissio "commission," from committere "to commit, entrust."
