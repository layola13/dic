jacobinism:/ˈdʒækəbɪnɪzəm/
n. |雅各宾主义，激进主义|The principles and practices of the Jacobins, especially during the French Revolution; extreme radicalism or violence in politics.|  The Reign of Terror was a dark chapter of Jacobinism in France.  法国大革命期间的恐怖统治是雅各宾主义的黑暗篇章。|synonyms: radicalism, extremism, militancy|antonyms: conservatism, moderation, pacifism|noun

jacobin:/ˈdʒækəbɪn/
n. |雅各宾派（成员）|A member of a radical political club during the French Revolution.| He was a Jacobin who advocated for the overthrow of the monarchy. 他是一位主张推翻君主制的雅各宾派成员。|synonyms: revolutionary, radical, extremist|antonyms: royalist, conservative, moderate|noun
jacobin:/ˈdʒækəbɪn/
adj. |雅各宾派的，激进的|Relating to or characteristic of the Jacobins or their principles.|His jacobin views led him to support the execution of the king. 他的雅各宾观点使他支持处决国王。|synonyms: radical, extremist, revolutionary|antonyms: conservative, moderate, reactionary|adjective
