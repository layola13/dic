astronomy: /əˈstrɒnəmi/ (UK), /əˈstrɑːnəmi/ (US) | n. | 天文学 | The scientific study of celestial objects (such as stars, planets, comets, and galaxies), space, and the physical universe as a whole. |例句：She has always been fascinated by astronomy. 她一直对天文学着迷。|例句：He's studying astronomy at university. 他在大学学习天文学。|近义词：astrophysics |反义词: N/A |词性: noun

astronomical: /ˌæstrəˈnɒmɪkl/ (UK), /ˌæstrəˈnɑːmɪkl/ (US)| adj. | 天文学的；庞大的 | Relating to astronomy; extremely large. |例句：The astronomical cost of the project caused it to be cancelled.  该项目的天文数字般的成本导致它被取消。|例句：The chances of winning the lottery are astronomical.  赢得彩票的几率微乎其微。|近义词：huge, immense, vast |反义词: tiny, small |词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἀστρονομία (astronomía)  'science of the stars', from ἄστρον (ástron, 'star') + -νομία (-nomía, 'law, custom, usage', related to νέμω (némō), 'distribute, allocate').
