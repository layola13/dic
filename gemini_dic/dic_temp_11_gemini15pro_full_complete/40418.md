contrapuntally:/ˌkɒntrəˈpʌŋktəli/ | adv. | 对位地，复调地 | In a manner resembling counterpoint; with independent but harmonizing melodies.  It describes something, often music or writing, that has interwoven, independent elements that work together harmoniously. | The two themes are developed contrapuntally. 这两个主题以对位法展开。| 近义词: polyphonically, harmoniously | 反义词: monophonically, homophonically | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *contrapunctum*, from Italian *contrappunto*, from *contra* ("against") + *punto* ("point," as in a musical note).  The English word "counterpoint" is a direct borrowing of the Italian term.  "Contrapuntally" is the adverbial form of "counterpoint."
