full-on:/ˌfʊlˈɒn/ | adj. | 全面的，十足的，彻底的 |  Complete, absolute, and without reservation. Often used to describe something intense or extreme. |  例句：It was a full-on blizzard.  那是一场十足的暴风雪。

例句：She went full-on into her new career. 她全心全意地投入到她的新事业中。

近义词：intense, extreme, complete, total, absolute, all-out | 反义词：mild, moderate, restrained, half-hearted | 词性：形容词

词源:  A combination of "full" and "on," suggesting something operating at maximum capacity or intensity.  "Full" has its roots in Old English "full," meaning "filled." "On" comes from Old English "on," indicating a state of being in operation or activity.
