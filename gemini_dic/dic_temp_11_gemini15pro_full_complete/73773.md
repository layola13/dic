formalisms:/ˈfɔːrməlɪzəmz/ | n. | 形式主义；形式系统；规范方法 | A set of rules and conventions used to describe or represent something, especially in a precise or abstract way.  Formalisms can be found in various fields like mathematics, logic, computer science, and linguistics. |  These mathematical formalisms can be applied to a variety of problems. 这些数学形式可以应用于各种问题。 | synonyms: conventions, systems, frameworks, methods, procedures, protocols, rules, structures, notations, representations | antonyms: informality,  imprecision, vagueness | noun

formalism:/ˈfɔːrməlɪzəm/ | n. | 形式主义；拘泥形式；形式体系 | Excessive adherence to prescribed forms or conventions, often at the expense of substance or meaning. Also refers to a system of formal rules and principles used to represent or describe something, especially in mathematics, logic, or computer science. | He disliked the formalism of organized religion. 他不喜欢有组织宗教的繁文缛节。| synonyms: ritualism, conventionalism, rigidity, dogmatism, strictness | antonyms: informality, flexibility, pragmatism, substance | noun
