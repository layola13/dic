exclusioners:/ɪksˈkluːʒənərz/ | n. | 排斥者；被排除者 | People who are excluded from a group or society. |  The exclusioners felt marginalized and ostracized. 被排除者感到被边缘化和排斥。|  Those denied access became the exclusioners in this social experiment.  在这个社会实验中，那些被拒绝进入的人成了被排斥者。| 近义词: outcasts, pariahs | 反义词: insiders, included | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
From exclude + -er + -s. Exclude comes from the Latin "excludere," meaning "to shut out," from "ex-" (out) + "claudere" (to close).
