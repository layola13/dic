acclimatation:/əˌklaɪməˈteɪʃn/ | n. | 适应，驯化 | The process of becoming accustomed to a new climate or environment. |  The climbers spent a few days at base camp for acclimatation to the altitude. 登山者们在大本营待了几天以适应高海拔。 | acclimatization, adaptation, adjustment | - | noun

acclimation:/əˌklɪˈmeɪʃn/ | n. | 适应，驯化（尤指气候） | The process of adapting to a new environment, especially to a new climate. | The acclimation of the plants to the desert climate was successful.  这些植物成功地适应了沙漠气候。 | acclimatization, adaptation, adjustment | - | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French acclimatation, from acclimater “to acclimatize,” from à “to” + climat “climate,” from Latin clima “region, slope of the earth,” from Greek klima "inclination, slope, zone," from klinein "to lean, slope."
