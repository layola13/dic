appreciatory:/əˈpriːʃiətɔːri/ /əˈpriːʃiˌeɪtɔːri/ | adj. |欣赏的，感激的| Showing or expressing gratitude or pleasure.|  She gave him an appreciatory smile. 她感激地对他笑了笑。|Synonyms: grateful, thankful, appreciative| Antonyms: ungrateful, unappreciative | adjective

appreciate:/əˈpriːʃieɪt/ | v. | 欣赏；感激；增值|1. To recognize the full worth of. 2. To be grateful for. 3. To increase in value.| I appreciate your help. 我感谢你的帮助. | Synonyms: value, cherish, admire, acknowledge, recognize| Antonyms:  disparage, depreciate, undervalue, criticize, condemn | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *appretiatus*, past participle of *appretiare* "to set a price to, appraise," from *ad-* "to" + *pretium* "price."  The sense shifted from "to estimate the value of" to "to value highly, appreciate" in the 17th century.
