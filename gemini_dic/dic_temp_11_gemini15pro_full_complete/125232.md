multiculture:/ˈmʌltiˌkʌltʃər/| n.|多元文化|The presence of, or support for the presence of, several distinct cultural or ethnic groups within a society.|The city is known for its vibrant multiculture.|近义词:pluralism, diversity|反义词:monoculture|词性:名词

multicultural:/ˌmʌltiˈkʌltʃərəl/| adj.|多元文化的|Relating to or constituting several cultural or ethnic groups within a society.|The university has a multicultural student body.|近义词:diverse, pluralistic|反义词:homogeneous, monocultural|词性:形容词

multiculturalism:/ˌmʌltiˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm/| n.|多元文化主义|The policy or process whereby the distinctive identities of the cultural groups within such a society are maintained or supported.|Multiculturalism is a complex and controversial topic.|近义词:cultural diversity, pluralism|反义词:assimilation, monoculturalism|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
"multi-" (from Latin "multus" meaning "many, much") + "culture" (from Latin "cultura" meaning "cultivation, culture").  The term "multiculturalism" emerged in the late 20th century.
