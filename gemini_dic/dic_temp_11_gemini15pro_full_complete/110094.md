lichwort:/ˈlɪçˌvɔːrt/ (German pronunciation)| n. |誓言，誓约，诅咒|A word or phrase used as an oath or curse, especially in Middle High German literature.  It refers to a word or phrase imbued with magical power, often used in spells or charms.  It can also mean a solemn promise or vow.|Example: In ancient Germanic cultures, a lichwort was believed to carry significant weight and could even influence fate.  在古代日耳曼文化中，lichwort 被认为具有重要的分量，甚至可以影响命运。|近义词：oath, vow, curse, imprecation, pledge|反义词: blessing, benediction|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle High German *līchwort, from Old High German *līhwort, from Proto-Germanic *līkworđą (“body-word, charm, spell”). Equivalent to modern German Leichwort.  *līk- is related to English like (body).  *-wort is cognate with English word.
