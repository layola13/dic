burh: /bʊrx/ (similar to modern 'borough') | n. | 城堡，设防城镇，市镇 | A fortified town, often a major administrative centre.  From Old English, meaning "fortress, fortified place, castle." Cognate with German *burg.  During the Anglo-Saxon period, burhs were often built as defensive structures against Viking raids. |  The king ordered the construction of several burhs along the coast. 国王下令沿海岸建造几座设防城镇。| synonym: fortress, stronghold, town | antonym: village, countryside | noun
