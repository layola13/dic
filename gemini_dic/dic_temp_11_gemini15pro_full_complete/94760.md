improviser: /ˈɪmprəvaɪzər/ (US), /ˈɪmprəvaɪzə(r)/ (UK) | n. | 即兴演奏者，即兴创作的人 | A person who improvises, especially in music, drama, or speech. |  He's a talented improviser on the piano. 他是一位才华横溢的钢琴即兴演奏家。| synonyms: extemporizer, ad-libber, impromptu performer | antonyms: planner, preparer | noun

improvise: /ˈɪmprəvaɪz/ (US), /ˈɪmprəvaɪz/ (UK) | v. | 即兴创作，临时凑合 | Create and perform (music, drama, or verse) spontaneously or without preparation.  To make or do something using whatever is available. |  I hadn't prepared a speech so I had to improvise. 我没有准备演讲，所以不得不即兴发挥。  We improvised a shelter from branches and leaves. 我们用树枝和树叶临时搭建了一个遮蔽处。 | synonyms: extemporize, ad-lib, wing it, make do | antonyms: plan, prepare, rehearse | verb
