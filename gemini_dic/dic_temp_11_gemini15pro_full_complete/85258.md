harmon-muted:/ˈhɑːrmən mjuːtɪd/ | adj. | 和声弱化的，弱音化的 |  Having the harmony softened or subdued, often by using a mute or similar device.  This term is typically used to describe musical sounds, particularly in jazz or orchestral music. It suggests a mellower, less vibrant tone. | The trumpet player produced a harmon-muted sound that was both haunting and beautiful. 小号手奏出了一个既令人难忘又优美的弱音和声。| The harmon-muted melody drifted through the smoky club. 弱音化的旋律飘荡在烟雾弥漫的俱乐部里。| 近义词：soft, subdued, muted, mellow | 反义词：bright, loud, resonant | 词性：形容词

Etymology:

* **harmon-**:  Derived from "harmony," ultimately from Greek "harmonia" meaning "joint, agreement, concord."  Relates to the combination of musical notes to produce chords and a pleasing sound.
* **muted**:  From "mute," meaning silenced or softened. From Old French "mut" meaning "silent, dumb."
