monopolylogue:/məˈnɒpəliˌlɒɡ/ | n. | 独白;垄断性独白 | A long speech by one person, especially one that prevents other people from speaking or expressing their views.  A speech resembling a monologue delivered by a character in a play. |  The CEO's annual address to the employees was more of a monopolylogue than a dialogue. 首席执行官对员工的年度讲话与其说是对话，不如说是一场独白。

He delivered a lengthy monopolylogue about the importance of his work, ignoring everyone else's contributions. 他发表了冗长的独白，讲述他的工作的重要性，而忽视了其他人的贡献。 |  monologue, soliloquy | dialogue, conversation | noun
