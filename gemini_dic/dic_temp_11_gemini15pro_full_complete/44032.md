crimsoned:/ˈkrɪmzənd/ | adj. | 深红色的，染成深红色的 | Turned deep red, as if stained with blood. | The setting sun crimsoned the clouds. 落日把云染成了深红色。 | Synonyms: blood-red, scarlet, ruby, vermilion | Antonyms: pale, bleached, ashen | word type: adjective

Etymology: From crimson + -ed.  "Crimson" itself comes from the Old Spanish "cremesin," ultimately derived from the Arabic "qirmiz," referring to the kermes insect, from which a red dye was obtained.
