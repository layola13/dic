muddlement:/ˈmʌdəlmənt/| n.|困惑，糊涂，混乱|A state of confusion or bewilderment.|He was in a state of muddlement after the long flight. 长途飞行后，他感到昏昏沉沉。|近义词：confusion, bewilderment, disorientation|反义词：clarity, lucidity, coherence|词性:名词

muddled:/ˈmʌdld/| adj.|困惑的，糊涂的，混乱的|Confused and unable to think clearly.|His explanation was muddled and unclear. 他的解释混乱不清。|近义词：confused, bewildered, disoriented|反义词：clear, lucid, coherent|词性:形容词

muddle:/ˈmʌdl/| v. |使混乱，弄混；胡乱应付|To make something untidy or disorganized; to cope with something clumsily or inefficiently.|He muddled the accounts and couldn't balance the books. 他把账目弄乱了，无法结清账目。|近义词：confuse, mix up, jumble|反义词：organize, arrange, clarify|词性:动词
