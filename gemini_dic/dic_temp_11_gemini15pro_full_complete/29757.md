词源：Catrina originally referred to a zinc etching by Mexican printmaker José Guadalupe Posada. It depicted a female skeleton dressed in elegant European attire, satirizing the adoption of European fashions by the Mexican upper class. Over time, the image became associated with the Day of the Dead and evolved into a broader symbol of the holiday.
