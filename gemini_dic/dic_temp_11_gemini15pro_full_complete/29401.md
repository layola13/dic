catachrestic:/ˌkætəˈkrɛstɪk/ | adj. |  滥用的，比喻不当的 | The use of a word in a way that is not strictly correct but is still understandable, often for rhetorical effect.  It involves a misuse or strain of a word’s meaning, applying it in a context where it doesn’t quite fit, but creating a novel or striking effect. |  His catachrestic use of the word "obfuscate" made his meaning even more confusing. 他对“obfuscate”一词的滥用使他的意思更加令人困惑。| 近义词: misused, inappropriate, strained, forced, far-fetched, improper, inaccurate | 反义词: appropriate, correct, proper, accurate, apt | 词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *catachresticus*, from Greek katakhrēstikos “using catachresis,” from katakhresthai “to misuse, abuse,” from kata- “down, against” + khresthai “to use.”
