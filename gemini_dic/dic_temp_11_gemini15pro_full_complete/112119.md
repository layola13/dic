longtail:/ˈlɒŋteɪl/ (UK) /ˈlɔːŋteɪl/ (US)| adj. n.|长尾的，长尾（理论/效应）|Relating to or involving a business strategy that allows companies to realize significant profits by selling a low volume of hard-to-find items to many customers, instead of only selling a high volume of a reduced number of popular items.|Many online retailers have adopted the longtail strategy, selling a wide variety of niche products.|近义词：niche, specialized|反义词: mainstream, popular|词性:形容词,名词

longtail:/ˈlɒŋteɪl/ (UK) /ˈlɔːŋteɪl/ (US)| n.|长尾（理论/效应）|The portion of a distribution curve representing a large number of products or customers that each contribute a small amount to the total but in aggregate represent a significant portion.|The longtail of the book market consists of titles that individually sell few copies but collectively represent a substantial market share.|近义词：niche market|反义词: mass market|词性:名词
