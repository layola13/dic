cowardice:/ˈkaʊərdɪs/| n. |懦弱，胆怯|Lack of courage to face danger, difficulty, or pain; fearfulness.|His cowardice prevented him from speaking up. 他的懦弱使他不敢开口说话。|近义词：timidity, pusillanimity, fearfulness|反义词：bravery, courage, valor|noun

coward:/ˈkaʊərd/| n. |懦夫，胆小鬼|A person who lacks courage to face danger, difficulty, or pain; a timid or easily intimidated person.|He was called a coward for refusing to fight. 他因为拒绝战斗而被称为懦夫。|近义词：craven, poltroon, dastard|反义词：hero, brave, valiant|noun

cowardly:/ˈkaʊərdli/| adj. |懦弱的，胆怯的|Lacking courage; characteristic of a coward.|It was a cowardly act to abandon his friends. 抛弃他的朋友是一个懦弱的行为。|近义词：timid, fearful, pusillanimous|反义词：brave, courageous, valiant|adjective
