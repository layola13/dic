bibliothec:/ˌbɪbliəˈθɛk/ | n. | 图书馆，藏书室 | A building or room containing a collection of books, periodicals, and other materials for reading, study, or reference.  It comes from the Greek words "biblion" (book) and "theke" (case, box).  Originally referred to a case or chest for storing books, later evolved to mean a collection of books and ultimately the building housing them. |  The bibliothec houses a vast collection of medieval manuscripts.  这座图书馆收藏了大量中世纪手稿。 | synonyms: library, archive | antonyms: None (There isn't a direct opposite for a place storing books.) | noun
