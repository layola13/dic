disjoint:/dɪsˈdʒɔɪnt/ | adj. | 不相交的，脱节的，分离的 | Having no elements in common; separated. | The two sets of numbers are disjoint, meaning they share no common elements. 这两组数字是不相交的，意味着它们没有共同的元素。 |  The plot felt disjoint and confusing.  情节感觉脱节和混乱。 | 近义词: separate, distinct, unconnected, non-overlapping | 反义词: connected, intersecting, overlapping, joint | 词性: adjective

disjoint:/dɪsˈdʒɔɪnt/ | v. |  使分离，使脱节 | To separate or disconnect. | The author disjointed the narrative, making it difficult to follow. 作者把叙述割裂开来，使之难以理解。 | The company's restructuring disjointed several departments. 公司的重组使几个部门脱节了。 | 近义词: separate, disconnect, disunite | 反义词: connect, unite, join | 词性: verb
