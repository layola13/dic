forager:/ˈfɔːrɪdʒər/ (US) /ˈfɒrɪdʒə(r)/ (UK)| n. |觅食者，采集者|A person or animal that searches widely for food or provisions.|Squirrels are natural foragers, burying nuts for later retrieval. 松鼠是天生的觅食者，它们会把坚果埋起来以便日后取回。|The forager returned to camp with a basket full of berries. 觅食者带着满满一篮子浆果回到了营地。|synonyms: hunter-gatherer, scavenger, collector, seeker|antonyms: farmer, cultivator|noun

forage:/ˈfɔːrɪdʒ/ (US) /ˈfɒrɪdʒ/ (UK)| v. |觅食，搜寻| (of a person or animal) search widely for food or provisions.|The pigs foraged in the woods for acorns. 猪在树林里觅食橡子。|She foraged in her handbag for her keys. 她在手提包里翻找钥匙。|synonyms: search, hunt, rummage, scavenge|antonyms: plant, cultivate|verb
