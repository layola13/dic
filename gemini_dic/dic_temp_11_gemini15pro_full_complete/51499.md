detractory:/dɪˈtræk.tər.i/ | adj. | 贬低的，减损的 | Tending to detract; disparaging. |  Her detractory remarks about his work hurt his feelings. 她对他作品的贬低性评论伤害了他的感情。| The review contained several detractory comments about the film's plot. 这篇评论包含了几条对电影情节的贬损性评论。|  derogatory, disparaging, belittling, depreciatory, negative |  complimentary, flattering, laudatory, positive | adjective

detract:/dɪˈtrækt/ | verb | 减损，降低 |  Reduce the worth or value of (something). | The scandal detracted from the candidate's image.  这桩丑闻损害了候选人的形象。|  Constant interruptions detracted from her ability to concentrate.  不断的打扰降低了她集中注意力的能力。| diminish, lessen, reduce, take away from | enhance, increase, augment, improve | verb


Etymology:
From Late Latin *dētractōrius, from Latin dētractus, past participle of dētrahere (“to draw away, take away”), from dē (“from, away”) + trahere (“to draw, pull”).
