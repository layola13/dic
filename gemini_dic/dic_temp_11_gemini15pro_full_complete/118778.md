metalloid:/ˈmɛt(ə)lɔɪd/ | n. | 类金属 | An element (e.g. arsenic, antimony, or tin) whose properties are intermediate between those of metals and nonmetals. |  Silicon is a classic example of a metalloid. 硅是类金属的典型例子。

Boron is a metalloid used in semiconductors. 硼是一种用于半导体的类金属。 | 近义词: semimetal | 反义词: metal, nonmetal | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From meta- + -oid.  "Meta-" comes from Greek, meaning "after, among, with," while "-oid" means "resembling" or "having the form of."  Therefore, "metalloid" literally means "resembling a metal."
