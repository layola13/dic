delirious:/dɪˈlɪriəs/| adj.|神志不清的，精神错乱的；极度兴奋的|Being in a state of delirium, characterized by mental confusion and often accompanied by hallucinations; extremely excited.|例句：He became delirious from the high fever. 他因为高烧而神志不清。

例句：She was delirious with joy when she won the lottery. 她中了彩票高兴得发狂。|近义词：incoherent, irrational, frenzied, ecstatic|反义词：lucid, rational, calm|词性:形容词

delirate:/dɪˈlɪreɪt/| vi.|精神错乱，语无伦次|To talk or act in a way that shows a temporary state of mental confusion.|例句：He delirated for hours before falling into a deep sleep. 他语无伦次地说了好几个小时才沉沉睡去。

例句：The patient began to delirate after the surgery. 病人在手术后开始精神错乱。|近义词：rave, ramble|反义词：speak coherently|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dēlīrāre* "to go out of the furrow," from *dē-* "out of" + *līra* "furrow." Figuratively, it means to deviate from a straight path or to be mentally off track.
