miniaturizing:/ˈmɪnɪətʃəˌraɪzɪŋ/
v. |小型化；微型化|The process of making something much smaller than its usual size.
|They are miniaturizing electronic components for use in space. 他们在将电子元件小型化以用于太空。|
|miniaturise, shrink, reduce, compress|
|enlarge, expand, magnify|
verb

miniaturization:/ˌmɪnɪətʃəraɪˈzeɪʃən/
n. |小型化；微型化|The process of making something much smaller than its usual size.
|The miniaturization of computers has revolutionized the electronics industry. 计算机的小型化彻底改变了电子工业。|
|miniaturisation, shrinking, reduction, compression|
|enlargement, expansion, magnification|
noun


Etymology:
From miniature + -ize + -ing. Miniature comes from the Italian word miniatura, meaning "a painting in miniature."  The "-ize" suffix is used to form verbs meaning "to make or become like" something.  The "-ing" suffix creates the present participle form of the verb.
