amender:/əˈmɛndər/| n.|修改者；修正者|A person who amends something, especially a legal document.  Someone who corrects or improves something, typically a text or piece of legislation.|The amender proposed several changes to the original bill.|近义词：corrector, reviser, editor, modifier|反义词：corrupter, damager|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *amendour*, from amender (“to amend”), from Latin ēmendāre, present active infinitive of ēmendō (“I correct, free from fault”), from ex (“out”) + mendum (“fault”).
