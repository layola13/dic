fast-lane: /ˈfæst leɪn/ (US) /ˈfɑːst leɪn/ (UK)| n. | 快车道，捷径，快速通道 | A route or track that is specially designated for vehicles traveling at higher speeds, often used metaphorically for a quicker way to achieve success. |  Driving in the fast lane can be dangerous. 在快车道上开车很危险。 | He's living life in the fast lane. 他过着快节奏的生活。 | 近义词: express lane, inside track, shortcut | 反义词: slow lane, long haul | 词性: 名词

词源:  Compound of "fast" (meaning quick) and "lane" (a narrow road).  The literal meaning refers to a highway lane for faster traffic. The figurative meaning developed from this, suggesting a quicker route to success or a lifestyle characterized by speed and excitement.
