labour: /ˈleɪbər/ (US) /ˈleɪbə/ (UK) | vi. | 劳动；努力；艰难地行进；分娩 | to work hard; to exert oneself; to move with difficulty; to be in childbirth | She laboured for hours in the garden. 她在花园里辛勤劳作了几个小时。 | synonyms: toil, strive, struggle | antonyms: rest, relax, idle | verb

Etymology: From Middle English *labour*, from Old French *labour* (Modern French *labeur*), from Latin *labor* (“labor, toil, hardship, fatigue, exertion”).
