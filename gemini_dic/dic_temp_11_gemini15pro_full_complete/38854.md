confederate:/kənˈfɛdərət/ (US) /kənˈfɛdərɪt/ (UK)| adj. n. v.| 同盟的；联邦的；同盟者；结盟；联合| joined by an agreement or treaty |The Confederate States of America fought against the Union in the American Civil War. | 美利坚联盟国在南北战争中与联邦作战。| synonyms: allied, federated, united | antonyms: separate, independent, divided | adjective

confederate:/kənˈfɛdərət/ (US) /kənˈfɛdərɪt/ (UK)| n.| 同盟者；联邦成员| a person, group, or nation that is united with others for a common purpose |He was a confederate of the notorious robber. | 他是那个臭名昭著的强盗的同伙。| synonyms: ally, partner, associate | antonyms: enemy, opponent, adversary | noun

confederate:/kənˈfɛdərət/ (US) /kənˈfɛdərɪt/ (UK)| v.| 结盟；联合| to unite for a common purpose |The small tribes confederated to defend themselves against the larger empire. | 小部落联合起来对抗更大的帝国。| synonyms: ally, unite, join forces | antonyms: separate, divide, disunite | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *confœderatus*, past participle of confœderāre (“to join by treaty, ally”), from Latin con- (“together”) + fœderāre, from fœdus (“treaty, league, covenant”). Doublet of federate.
