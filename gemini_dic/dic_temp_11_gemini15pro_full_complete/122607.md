modernizers:/ˈmɑːdərnaɪzərz/ | n. | 现代化者，革新者 | People or groups who bring something up to date, making it more modern.  Those who advocate for or implement modernization. | The modernizers pushed for reforms in education and industry. 现代化者推动了教育和工业的改革。

The government praised the modernizers for their contributions to the economy. 政府赞扬了现代化者对经济的贡献。 | 近义词: reformers, innovators, progressives | 反义词: traditionalists, conservatives | 词性: 名词(plural)

ETYMOLOGY->
From modernize + -er (agent suffix).  Modernize itself comes from modern + -ize (verb-forming suffix). "Modern" ultimately derives from Latin "modernus," meaning "of the present mode or fashion."
