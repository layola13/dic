defrocking:/diːˈfrɒkɪŋ/
n.|剥夺圣职；革除教职|The act of officially removing a priest or other member of the clergy from their position and privileges.|The defrocking of the priest shocked the community.|dismissal, removal, expulsion|reinstatement, investiture|noun

defrock:/diːˈfrɒk/
vt.|剥夺圣职；革除教职|To officially remove a priest or other member of the clergy from their position and privileges.|The bishop defrocked the priest for his misconduct.|dismiss, remove, expel|reinstate, invest|verb
Etymology: From de- + frock (a monk's habit), ultimately from Late Latin *froccus.
