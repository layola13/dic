droven:/ˈdroʊvən/| adj.|受到驱使的，被迫的|Driven describes someone who is highly motivated, determined, and works very hard to achieve their goals. It implies a strong inner force or external pressure that compels them forward.  It's the past participle of "drive" used as an adjective.|He was a driven man, determined to succeed at all costs. 他是一个充满动力的人，决心不惜一切代价取得成功。|The refugees were driven from their homes by the war. 难民因战争被迫离开家园。|近义词：motivated, ambitious, determined, compulsive, pressured|反义词：lazy, unmotivated, apathetic|词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *driven, *dreven, from Old English *drīfen, past participle of drīfan (“to drive, urge, compel”), from Proto-Germanic *drībananą (“to drive”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰreybʰ- (“to push, drive”). Cognate with West Frisian driven, Dutch gedreven, German getrieben.
