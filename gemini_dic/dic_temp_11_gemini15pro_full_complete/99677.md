interlingualisms:/ˌɪntərˈlɪŋɡwəlɪzəmz/ | n. |语际词；跨语言借词|Words or phrases that are adopted from one language into another with little or no modification, and are understood by speakers of both languages. These words often fill lexical gaps or express concepts not easily translated. |Example: The word "computer" is an interlingualism, used in many languages with minimal changes.| Synonyms: loanwords, borrowings | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps neologisms - newly coined words within a language) | Noun
