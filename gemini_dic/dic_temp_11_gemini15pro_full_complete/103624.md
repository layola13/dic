joist:/dʒɔɪst/| n.|托梁，桁条|A horizontal beam supporting a floor or ceiling.|The floor joists were spaced 16 inches apart.  地板托梁間隔 16 英寸。|Synonyms: beam, girder, support|Antonyms: none|noun

joist:/dʒɔɪst/| v. |用托梁支撑|To support with joists.|They joisted the ceiling before adding the drywall.  他们在安装干墙之前用托梁支撑天花板。|Synonyms: support, brace, reinforce|Antonyms: weaken, undermine|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *giste (modern French gîte “lodging, resting place”) from Vulgar Latin *jacita, feminine past participle of jacēre "to lie down," from PIE root *key- "to lie down."  The sense evolution is from "lying beam" to "supporting beam."
