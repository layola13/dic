archipterygium: /ˌɑːrkɪpˈtɛrɪdʒiəm/ | n. |  原始鳍; 翅基; 辐鳍 | The basic type of vertebrate paired fin, having a central axis with side branches.  A fin skeleton, such as that of sharks and rays, that consists of a series of radiating cartilaginous or bony rods extending from a basal element.|  The fossil shows a well-preserved archipterygium. 这块化石展现了一个保存完好的原始鳍。|  Synonyms: fin,  primitive fin | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could consider evolved fin structures like those in teleost fish) | noun
