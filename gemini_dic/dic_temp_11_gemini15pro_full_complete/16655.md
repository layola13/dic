barracoon:/ˌbærəˈkuːn/| n. |奴隶营，奴隶围栏|A barracoon was an enclosure, often a fortified building, used for temporarily holding enslaved people before they were transported across the Atlantic. |The barracoon was a grim reminder of the horrors of the slave trade.  奴隶营是奴隶贸易恐怖的残酷提醒。|The captives were held in a barracoon for weeks before being shipped to America.  俘虏们在被运往美洲之前被关在奴隶营里数周。 |Synonyms: slave pen, slave quarters, holding pen|Antonyms:  freedom, liberty|noun

barracoon:/ˌbærəˈkuːn/| n. |临时兵营，棚屋|A barracoon can also refer to a simple, roughly built hut or shelter.  |The soldiers erected a barracoon to shelter from the rain. 士兵们搭建了一个棚屋来躲雨。|The workers lived in barracoons near the construction site. 工人们住在工地附近的棚屋里。 |Synonyms: hut, shack, shelter|Antonyms: mansion, palace|noun
