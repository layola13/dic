inchoating:/ɪnˈkoʊeɪtɪŋ/
v. |开始，着手，创始|beginning something; commencing|The project is just inchoating, but we have high hopes for its success.  这个项目才刚刚开始，但我们对它的成功抱有很高的期望。|例句：The artist was inchoating a new masterpiece.  这位艺术家正在着手创作新的杰作。|近义词：beginning, commencing, initiating, starting|反义词：finishing, completing, ending|词性：动词

inchoate:/ɪnˈkoʊeɪt/
adj. |刚开始的，未完成的，不成熟的|just begun and so not fully formed or developed; rudimentary|The inchoate democracy struggled to find its footing.  这个新生的民主国家艰难地站稳脚跟。|例句：His ideas were still inchoate and needed further development. 他的想法还不成熟，需要进一步发展。|近义词：undeveloped, rudimentary, nascent, incipient|反义词：developed, mature, complete|词性：形容词

词源:  From Latin *incohātus, past participle of incohāre (“to begin, commence”), from in- (“in, on”) + cohāre (“to stick together, cling, cohere”).


注意事项: inchoate 更常用作形容词，而 inchoating 虽然是动词，但使用频率相对较低，很多情况下可以用begin, start, commence 等更常见的词代替。
