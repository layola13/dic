functionalism:/ˈfʌŋkʃənəlɪzəm/| n.|功能主义|A theory that explains something based on its purpose or role, especially in social sciences, architecture, and design. It emphasizes the practical aspects rather than aesthetics or other considerations.|Functionalism in architecture prioritizes practicality and efficiency. 建筑中的功能主义优先考虑实用性和效率。|The design of the building reflects the principles of functionalism.  这座建筑的设计体现了功能主义的原则。|synonyms: pragmatism, utilitarianism|antonyms: formalism, aestheticism|noun

functionalist:/ˈfʌŋkʃənəlɪst/| n. adj.|功能主义者，功能主义的|A person who believes in or follows the principles of functionalism; relating to or characteristic of functionalism.|He was a strong functionalist in his approach to design. 他在设计方法上是一个坚定的功能主义者。|The functionalist design of the furniture emphasized practicality.  家具的功能主义设计强调实用性。|synonyms: pragmatist, utilitarian|antonyms: formalist, aestheticist|noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the word "function" + "-ism" (a suffix forming nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, or theories). "Function" comes from Late Latin *functionem* "performance, execution," from *functus*, past participle of *fungi* "perform, execute."
