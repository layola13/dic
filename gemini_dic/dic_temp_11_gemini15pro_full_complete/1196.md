accoast: /əˈkɒst/ (UK) /əˈkɔːst/ (US)| vt.| 搭讪；上前和…说话；（尤指官员）走近（船只）检查| To approach and speak to someone, often aggressively or in a way that seems bold or intrusive. Also, to approach and board a ship, especially for official inspection.|  例句：A stranger accosted me on the street and asked for money. 一个陌生人在街上跟我搭讪，要钱。

例句：The coast guard accosted the suspicious vessel. 海岸警卫队上前检查那艘可疑的船只。

|近义词：approach, address, confront, hail|反义词：avoid, shun, evade|词性：动词(verb)

词源:
