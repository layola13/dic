machine-gunned:/məˈʃiːnˌɡʌnd/ | v. | 用机关枪扫射；|To shoot with a machine gun, typically in a rapid and indiscriminate manner.| The retreating soldiers were machine-gunned by enemy aircraft. 撤退的士兵遭到敌机机关枪扫射。 | Synonyms: strafe, riddle (with bullets), mow down | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, consider "protected," "defended") | Verb (past participle)

machine-gunned:/məˈʃiːnˌɡʌnd/ | adj. | 被机关枪扫射的 |  Having been shot with a machine gun. |  The machine-gunned car was riddled with bullet holes.  被机关枪扫射的汽车布满了弹孔。| Synonyms: riddled, bullet-ridden | Antonyms:  intact, undamaged | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "machine gun" + "-ed." "Machine gun" itself combines "machine," from Old French "machine" (ultimately from Greek "mēkhanē," meaning "contrivance, machine"), and "gun," from Old Norse "gunna" (a woman's name, originally applied to a catapult).
