craters:/ˈkreɪtərz/ (US), /ˈkreɪtəz/ (UK)| n. |火山口，陨石坑|A large, bowl-shaped cavity in the ground or on the surface of a planet or moon, typically one caused by an explosion or the impact of a meteorite or other celestial body. |The surface of the moon is covered in craters. 月球表面布满了陨石坑。|Synonyms: depressions, hollows, cavities, pits|Antonyms: mounds, hills, peaks|Noun
