double-blind:/ˌdʌbəl ˈblaɪnd/| adj.|双盲的|A double-blind experiment is one in which neither the participants nor the experimenters know who is receiving a particular treatment. This procedure is used to prevent bias in research results.|The study was conducted double-blind to eliminate bias. 这项研究以双盲方式进行，以消除偏差。|近义词:unbiased, objective|反义词:biased, subjective|词性:形容词

Etymology:
double (from Old French *dobl- < Latin duplus "twofold") + blind (from Old English blind)
