affirmativeness:/əˈfɜːrmətɪvnəs/
|n.|肯定；坚定；确认|The quality or state of being affirmative; positive assertion.  A mood or mode of expression conveying assent or agreement.|She spoke with great affirmativeness, leaving no doubt about her decision. 她说话非常肯定，对自己的决定毫不怀疑。|近义词：positiveness, certainty, assurance, confidence|反义词：negativity, uncertainty, doubt, hesitation|词性:名词

affirmative:/əˈfɜːrmətɪv/
|adj.|肯定的；同意的|Expressing agreement or consent; positive. |He gave an affirmative answer to the question. 他对这个问题给出了肯定的回答。|近义词：positive, approving, consenting|反义词：negative, dissenting, opposing|词性:形容词

affirm:/əˈfɜːrm/
|v.|断言；肯定；证实|State as a fact; assert strongly and publicly. To validate or confirm.|He affirmed his belief in the project. 他肯定了他对这个项目的信心。|近义词：assert, declare, confirm, endorse|反义词：deny, refute, contradict, negate|词性:动词
