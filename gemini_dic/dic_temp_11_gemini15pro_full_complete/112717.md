loyaler: /ˈlɔɪələr/ | adj. | 更忠诚的 | Comparative form of loyal: showing firm and constant support or allegiance to a person or institution.  More loyal than someone or something else. |  He was a loyaler friend than I deserved. 他是一位比我应得的更忠诚的朋友。 |  more faithful, truer, more devoted, more steadfast | less loyal, faithless, disloyal, treacherous | adjective


Etymology:
From Middle English *loyaler, *lealer, comparative of *loyal, *leal, from Anglo-Norman leal, loial, from Old French leial, loial (“legal, loyal, faithful”), from Latin lēgālis (“legal”), from lēx (“law”).  The -er suffix denotes the comparative form.
