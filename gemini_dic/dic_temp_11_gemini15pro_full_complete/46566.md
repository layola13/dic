dalmatic: /dælˈmætɪk/
n.
达尔马提卡长袍
A long, wide-sleeved tunic worn by deacons in the Roman Catholic and some other Christian churches.
The deacon wore a richly embroidered dalmatic.  执事穿着一件绣工精美的达尔马提卡长袍。
vestment, robe
None
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dalmatica vestis (“Dalmatian garment”), from Latin Dalmaticus (“of Dalmatia”). Dalmatia is a historical region on the eastern shore of the Adriatic Sea.
