hieromartyr:/ˌhaɪəroʊˈmɑːrtər/ | n. | 圣殉道者；殉教圣徒 | A person who is killed for their religious beliefs, especially a bishop or other high-ranking religious figure. |  Example 1:  St. Stephen is considered a hieromartyr because he was stoned to death for his faith.  圣斯德望被认为是一位圣殉道者，因为他因信仰而被石头砸死。

Example 2:  Many early Christians became hieromartyrs during the Roman persecutions.  许多早期的基督徒在罗马迫害期间成为了圣殉道者。 |  Synonyms: martyr, saint | Antonyms: apostate, heretic | noun
