fugue:/fjuːɡ/
n.
赋格曲；神游症；朦胧状态
A polyphonic composition based on a short theme (the subject) that is introduced successively in different voices, each voice then continuing by interweaving the subject and other melodic material (countersubject) in imitative counterpoint.  A disturbed state of consciousness in which the one affected seems to perform acts in full awareness but upon recovery cannot recollect the acts performed.
The composer's last work was a complex fugue.  作曲家的最后一部作品是一首复杂的赋格曲。
He experienced a fugue state after the accident and couldn't remember anything. 事故发生后，他经历了一段神游状态，什么都不记得了。
近义词：composition, amnesia, flight
反义词：awareness, lucidity
词性：名词


Etymology:
From French *fugue*, from Italian *fuga* (“flight, escape”), from Latin *fuga* (“flight, running away”), from *fugiō* (“I flee, run away”).
