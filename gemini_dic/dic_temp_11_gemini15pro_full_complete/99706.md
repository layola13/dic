interlope:/ˈɪntərloʊp/| v. n.|干涉；闯入；干预；入侵者；中间人；私贩|To intrude or interfere in a domain where one is not welcome or has no right to be; a person who does this.|Example 1:  Several new companies have interloped on their traditional market.  几家新公司侵入了他们的传统市场。

Example 2:  The company was accused of interloping in the affairs of a sovereign nation.  该公司被指控干涉一个主权国家的内政。|近义词：intrude, interfere, meddle, encroach|反义词：respect boundaries, abstain|词性:verb, noun
