chutzpah:/ˈhʊtspə, ˈxʊtspə/| n.|厚颜无耻；胆大包天；无礼|Supreme self-confidence; audacity.  It suggests a kind of nerve or arrogance, often in the face of authority or social norms.|He had the chutzpah to ask for a raise after only a week on the job. 他竟然有胆量在工作才一周后就要求加薪。|Synonyms: audacity, nerve, gall, temerity, boldness, effrontery|Antonyms: timidity, shyness, humility, modesty|noun

chutzpahs:/ˈhʊtspəz, ˈxʊtspəz/| n.|厚颜无耻；胆大包天；无礼 (plural of chutzpah)|Plural form of chutzpah, referring to multiple instances of extreme self-confidence or audacity.|The chutzpahs of some politicians are truly astounding. 一些政客的厚颜无耻真是令人震惊。|Synonyms: audacities, nerves, galls, temerities, boldnesses, effronteries|Antonyms: timidities, shynesses, humilities, modesty|noun
