distorted:/dɪˈstɔːrtɪd/ | adj. | 扭曲的，歪曲的 | Pulled or twisted out of shape; misrepresented; altered from the true meaning. | The media presented a distorted view of the event. 媒体对事件的报道歪曲了事实。|  The heat distorted the image on the screen.  高温使屏幕上的图像变形了。| synonyms: twisted, warped, deformed, misrepresented, falsified | antonyms: accurate, true, undistorted, correct | adjective

distort:/dɪˈstɔːrt/ | v. | 扭曲，歪曲 | Pull or twist out of shape; give a misleading or false account or impression of. | The loud music distorted the sound of her voice.  吵闹的音乐使她的声音失真。| He distorted the facts to fit his theory. 他歪曲事实以符合他的理论。| synonyms: twist, warp, deform, misrepresent, falsify | antonyms: clarify, represent truthfully | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *distortus*, past participle of *distorquēre*  ("to twist different ways, distort, deform"), from *dis-* ("apart, asunder") + *torquēre* ("to twist").
