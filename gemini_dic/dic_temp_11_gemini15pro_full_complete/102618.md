jainist:/ˈdʒeɪnɪst/
n./adj.|耆那教徒；耆那教的|A follower of Jainism, an ancient Indian religion. Also used as an adjective to describe things related to Jainism.|She is a devout Jainist. 她是一位虔诚的耆那教徒。|The Jainist temple is beautifully ornate. 这座耆那教寺庙装饰精美。|synonyms:  follower of Jainism, adherent of Jainism|antonyms: (No direct antonym, could be contrasted with followers of other religions e.g., Buddhist, Hindu)|noun/adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Jain + -ist.  Jain comes from the Sanskrit *jina* meaning "victor," referring to spiritual conquerors who have triumphed over the cycle of rebirth.
