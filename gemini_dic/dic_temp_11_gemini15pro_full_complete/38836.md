confabulate:/kənˈfæbjʊleɪt/ /kənˈfæbjəleɪt/
| v. | 虚构；编造；闲聊 | To talk informally; chat. To fabricate imaginary experiences as compensation for loss of memory.  |
例句：He confabulated a story about being a war hero. 他编造了一个关于自己是战争英雄的故事。

例句：The old friends sat in the cafe, confabulating about their youth. 老朋友们坐在咖啡馆里，闲聊着他们的青春。


|近义词：fabricate, invent, chat, gossip |
|反义词：recollect, remember |
|词性：动词|

confabulation:/kənˌfæbjʊˈleɪʃən/ /kənˌfæbjəˈleɪʃən/
| n. | 虚构；编造；闲聊 | The act of confabulating; a fabricated story. An unconscious filling-in of gaps in memory by imagined or untrue experiences due to amnesia. |
例句：Her account of the event was a complete confabulation. 她对这件事的描述完全是虚构的。

例句：The patient's confabulations made it difficult for doctors to assess his true condition. 病人的虚构使得医生难以评估他的真实病情。

|近义词：fabrication, invention, chat, gossip |
|反义词：recollection, memory |
|词性：名词|
