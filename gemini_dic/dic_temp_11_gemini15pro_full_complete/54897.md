disport:/dɪˈspɔːrt/ /dɪˈspɔrt/
verb|嬉戏，玩耍；炫耀，卖弄|To amuse oneself in a lighthearted way; to display or show off.|They disported themselves in the pool all afternoon. 他们在游泳池里玩了一下午。|近义词:frolic, play, cavort, gambol|反义词:work, toil, labor|词性:v.
disport:/dɪˈspɔːrt/ /dɪˈspɔrt/
noun|娱乐，消遣|Amusement or diversion.|The king and his court enjoyed hunting as a disport. 国王和他的廷臣以狩猎为乐。|近义词:pastime, recreation, entertainment|反义词:work, toil, labor|词性:n.

词源: From Middle English disporten, from Old French desporter, from des- (expressing separation, removal) + porter (to carry, bear).  The original sense was "to divert oneself from serious occupations."
