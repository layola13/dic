geodesic:/ˌdʒiːəˈdɛsɪk/ /ˌdʒiːoʊˈdɛsɪk/ | n. adj. |测地线，测地线的|A curve representing the shortest path between two points on a mathematically defined surface (such as a sphere or ellipsoid) or a Riemannian manifold. As an adjective, relating to or involving such curves.  |Example: The airplane follows a geodesic route to minimize travel time. 飞机沿着测地线飞行以最大限度地减少旅行时间。

Example: Geodesic domes are known for their structural strength. 测地线穹顶以其结构强度而闻名。|近义词：shortest path, shortest route|反义词:longest path|词性:noun, adjective

geodesics:/ˌdʒiːəˈdɛsɪks/ /ˌdʒiːoʊˈdɛsɪks/ | n. |测地线（复数）|Plural form of "geodesic," referring to multiple shortest paths between points on a surface or manifold. |Example:  The architect studied the geodesics of the dome's structure. 建筑师研究了穹顶结构的测地线。

Example: Understanding geodesics is crucial in navigation and general relativity. 理解测地线对于导航和广义相对论至关重要。|近义词：shortest paths, shortest routes|反义词:longest paths|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Greek "geodaisia" (γεωδαισία), meaning "division of the earth," composed of "geo" (γῆ) meaning "earth" and "daiein" (δαίειν) meaning "to divide."  It was initially related to surveying and determining the Earth's shape and size.
