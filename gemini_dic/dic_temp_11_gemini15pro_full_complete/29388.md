casus: /ˈkɑːsəs/ (US), /ˈkeɪsəs/ (UK)| n. |情况，案例，格（语法）|A specific instance or situation; a case, especially in grammar or law.  In linguistics, it refers to the grammatical function of a noun or pronoun. |例句：The lawyer presented a compelling casus to the jury. 律师向陪审团提出了一个令人信服的案例。

例句：The genitive casus in Latin indicates possession. 拉丁语中的属格表示所有格。|近义词：case, instance, situation, example|反义词: N/A |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *casus*, literally "a falling", from *cadere* "to fall".  The grammatical sense developed from the idea of a noun "falling" into a particular form depending on its function in the sentence.
