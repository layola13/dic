disertitude:/ˈdɪsərˌtɪtjuːd/ /ˈdɪsərˌtuːd/
n.
学识; 博学; 论文; 专著
A lengthy formal treatise, especially one written by a candidate for a doctoral degree.  A learned discourse.
例句：Her dissertation was a profound study of 18th-century literature. 她的论文是对18世纪文学的深入研究。

例句：He displayed great disertitude on the subject of ancient history. 他在古代历史方面展现了渊博的学识。
近义词：thesis, treatise, dissertation, discourse, erudition, scholarship
反义词：ignorance, illiteracy
词性：noun


Etymology:
From Late Latin *disertitudo, from Latin disertus (“skilled in speaking, eloquent”), past participle of disserere (“to discuss, debate, examine, explain, scatter seeds”), from dis- (“apart, asunder”) + serere (“to join, link, connect, interweave, arrange, sow, plant”).  Doublet of dissert.
