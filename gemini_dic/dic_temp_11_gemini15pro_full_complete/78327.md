gerrymanderers:/ˈdʒɛriˌmændərərz/
n.
不公正划分选区的人; 操纵选区者
People who manipulate the boundaries of an electoral constituency so as to favor one party or class.
The gerrymanderers carved up the city to ensure their party's victory.
这些划分选区者把城市分割开来，以确保他们政党的胜利。

近义词:manipulators, riggers
反义词:reformers

词性:noun (plural)

词源:From the name of Elbridge Gerry, governor of Massachusetts (1810–12), whose signature appeared on a bill creating a salamander-shaped voting district designed to favor his party + -mander.
