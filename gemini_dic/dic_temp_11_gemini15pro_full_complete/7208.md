anglers:/ˈæŋɡlərz/| n. |钓鱼者；垂钓者|A person who fishes with a rod and line.|例句：The anglers were casting their lines into the river. 钓鱼者们把鱼线抛进河里。

例句：Many anglers enjoy the peace and quiet of fishing. 许多钓鱼者享受钓鱼时的宁静。|近义词：fishermen, fisherwomen|反义词: None (No direct opposite for a person engaging in a hobby)|词性:Noun
Etymology->
From Middle English *angler, from Anglo-Norman angleur, from Old French angleor, from Late Latin angulārius (“angler, fisherman”), from Latin angulus (“angle, corner, hook, fishhook”).
