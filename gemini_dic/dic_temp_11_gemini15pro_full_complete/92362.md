hypochondriacs:/ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪæks/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪæks/ (UK)| n. |疑病症患者；忧郁症患者|A person who is abnormally anxious about their health. |例句：He's a real hypochondriac, always convinced he's dying. 他真是个疑病症患者，总是确信自己快要死了。

例句：She's become a hypochondriac since her husband's illness. 自从她丈夫生病以来，她就变成了一个疑病症患者。

|近义词：valetudinarian, worrier|反义词: optimist|词性: noun


hypochondriac:/ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪæk/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪæk/ (UK)| adj. |疑病的；患疑病症的|Excessively anxious about one's health; relating to hypochondria. |例句：He was prone to hypochondriac anxieties. 他容易患疑病症。

例句：She dismissed his complaints as hypochondriac. 她认为他的抱怨是疑病症的表现。

|近义词：valetudinarian, neurotic|反义词: healthy|词性: adjective


词源: From Late Latin *hypochondriacus, from Ancient Greek ὑποχόνδριος (hupochóndrios, “hypochondriac”), from ὑπό (hupó, “under”) + χόνδρος (khóndros, “cartilage of the breastbone”). The ancient Greeks believed that melancholy and other emotional disorders originated in the organs beneath the cartilage of the breastbone.
