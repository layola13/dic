overornamentally: /ˌoʊvərˈɔːrnəˈmɛntəli/ | adv. | 过度装饰地 | Excessively ornamented; decorated to an excessive or tasteless degree.  It describes something that has too much ornamentation, making it look cluttered or gaudy. | The cake was overornamentally decorated with sugary flowers and figurines. 这个蛋糕装饰得过于花哨，上面堆满了糖花和小雕像。 | The house was overornamentally furnished, with gilded mirrors and plush velvet drapes everywhere. 这房子装修得过于华丽，到处都是镀金的镜子和豪华的天鹅绒窗帘。| 近义词: excessively, elaborately, ostentatiously | 反义词: simply, plainly, minimally | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
over- + ornament + -al + -ly

"over-" is a prefix meaning "too much" or "excessive."

"ornament" comes from the Latin word "ornamentum," meaning "decoration" or "embellishment."

"-al" is a suffix that forms adjectives from nouns.

"-ly" is a suffix that forms adverbs from adjectives.
