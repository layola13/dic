chloralism: /ˈklɔːrəlɪzəm/ | n. |氯醛中毒；氯醛成瘾|A state of intoxication or addiction caused by the excessive or habitual use of chloral hydrate, a sedative and hypnotic drug.|She suffered from chloralism after years of using chloral hydrate to treat her insomnia. 她因多年服用水合氯醛治疗失眠而患上氯醛中毒。| 近义词: sedation, hypnotic addiction | 反义词: sobriety | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From chloral + -ism.  Chloral itself comes from chlor- (from Greek khlōros "pale green") + -al (chemical suffix denoting an aldehyde).  The suffix -ism denotes a state or condition.
