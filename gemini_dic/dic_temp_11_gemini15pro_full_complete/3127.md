aesopian:/ˌiːsəˈpiːən/ |adj.|寓言式的，隐晦的，带有讽刺意味的|Conveying meaning through allegory, fable, or symbolic language, often with a veiled or critical subtext; especially applicable to literature produced under censorship or political repression.|The writer used aesopian language to criticize the government without being directly censored.  这位作家用寓言式的语言批评政府，从而避免了直接的审查。|Example:  The play was filled with aesopian references to the current political climate. 这部戏剧充满了对当前政治气候的寓言式指涉。|Synonyms: allegorical, symbolic, metaphorical, oblique, veiled, allusive, indirect|Antonyms: direct, explicit, literal, overt|Adjective
