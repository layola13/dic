inveterateness:/ɪnˈvɛtərɪtnəs/
n.|根深蒂固，由来已久|The inveterateness of their feud made any reconciliation unlikely.  他们由来已久的世仇使得任何和解都不太可能。|例句：The inveterateness of his smoking habit made it difficult for him to quit.  他根深蒂固的吸烟习惯使他很难戒掉。|近义词：entrenchment, ingrainedness, fixedness, permanence|反义词：transience, impermanence|词性:名词
Etymology: From Late Latin inveteratus (aged, chronic), past participle of inveterare (to age, become chronic), from in- (in, upon) + vetus (old).
