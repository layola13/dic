caper:/ˈkeɪpər/| v. n.|雀跃，嬉戏；滑稽动作|To leap or skip about in a playful way; a playful skipping movement.|The children capered about in the garden. 孩子们在花园里嬉戏玩耍。|近义词：frolic, gambol, frisk|反义词：mope, sulk|词性:动词, 名词

caper:/ˈkeɪpər/| n.|刺山柑花蕾；恶作剧|The unopened flower bud of a Mediterranean shrub, pickled and used as a seasoning; an illicit or mischievous adventure; a prank.|The dish was garnished with capers. 这道菜用刺山柑装饰。  They were involved in a daring caper. 他们参与了一场大胆的恶作剧。|近义词：(for prank) escapade, stunt|反义词：(for prank) none|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *capre*, from Italian *cappero*, ultimately from Latin *capparis*, from Greek *kápparis*. The verb sense "to leap or skip playfully" arose from the association with goats (known for their playful leaping), which also enjoy eating the caper plant.
