exclusivist: /ɪkˈskluːsɪvɪst/ | n. adj. | 排外主义者；排他的 | A person who favors a policy of excluding certain people or groups, especially from a country or organization.  Also, relating to or characterized by such a policy. |  The country club had a reputation for being exclusivist and only accepting members from a select few families.  这个乡村俱乐部因排外而闻名，只接受来自少数几个家庭的成员。

The exclusivist policies of the regime led to widespread unrest. 该政权的排外政策导致了广泛的动荡。 | 近义词：elitist, sectarian, parochial, restrictive | 反义词：inclusive, egalitarian, open, welcoming | 词性: noun, adjective
Etymology: From Late Latin exclusus (past participle of excludere, "to shut out") + -ist.
