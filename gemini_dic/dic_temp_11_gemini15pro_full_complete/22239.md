bonders:/ˈbɒndəz/ (UK) /ˈbɑːndərz/ (US)| n.| 保税人；担保人；结合者；粘合剂|People or things that create bonds, connections, or attachments. Can refer to people involved in bonding processes (like in finance or construction), or substances used for adhesion.|The bonders ensured the new wing adhered seamlessly to the old building. 粘合剂确保了新翼与旧建筑无缝连接。|近义词：linkers, connectors, joiners, adhesives|反义词：separators, dividers|词性:noun


bonders:/ˈbɒndəz/ (UK) /ˈbɑːndərz/ (US)| n.| 保税货物|Goods stored in a bonded warehouse until duties are paid.|The bonders were inspected by customs officials. 海关官员检查了保税货物。|近义词：bonded goods, dutiable goods|反义词：duty-paid goods|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "bond" (meaning tie, connection) + "-er" (agent suffix). "Bond" ultimately comes from Old English "band," meaning "band, bond, fetter."
