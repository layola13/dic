disinfection's:/ˌdɪsɪnˈfekʃənz/ | n. | 消毒（作用）|  The process of cleaning something, especially with a chemical, in order to destroy bacteria.  It is the possessive form of "disinfection". | The disinfection's effectiveness depends on the concentration of the chemical used.  这种消毒的效果取决于所用化学品的浓度。| synonyms: sterilization, sanitization | antonyms: contamination, infection | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English disinfeccioun, from Late Latin dīsinfictiō, dīsinfectiōn- (“a cleansing, purification”), from dīsinficiō, dīsinficere (“to cleanse from infection”), from dis- (“reversal of an action, removal”) + inficiō, inficere (“to dip into, stain, dye, infect”), from in- (“in, into”) + faciō, facere (“to make, do”).  Equivalent to disinfect + -ion.
