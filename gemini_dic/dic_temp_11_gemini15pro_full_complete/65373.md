eunuchism:/ˈjuːnəkɪzəm/ | n. | 宦官主义；阉割状态 |The state of being a eunuch, or the practice of castration.  The effects, physical and psychological, of castration. | Eunuchism was common in some ancient courts.  宦官制度在一些古代宫廷中很常见。| The historical novel explored the psychological effects of eunuchism on young boys. 这部历史小说探讨了宦官制度对年轻男孩的心理影响。 | 近义词: castration, emasculation | 反义词: virility, masculinity | 词性: noun

词源: Late Middle English: from Old French eunuquisme, via late Latin from Greek eunouchismos, from eunouchos ‘eunuch.’
