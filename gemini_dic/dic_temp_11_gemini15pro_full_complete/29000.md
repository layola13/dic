carthusian:/kɑːrˈθjuːʒən/ | adj. n. | 卡尔特教团的；禁欲苦行的；卡尔特教团的修士 | Relating to or characteristic of the Carthusian order, a strict monastic order.  A member of the Carthusian order. | The monastery followed a Carthusian rule of silence and solitude. 这座修道院遵循卡尔特教团的沉默和独居的戒律。| He lived a Carthusian existence, devoting himself to prayer and contemplation. 他过着卡尔特教团式的生活，致力于祈祷和沉思。 | 近义词: ascetic, austere, monastic | 反义词: hedonistic, worldly | 词性: adjective, noun

Etymology: From Late Latin Carthusiensis, from Cartusia, the Latin name for La Grande Chartreuse, the first Carthusian monastery, near Grenoble, France. Ultimately from a pre-Roman substrate word.
