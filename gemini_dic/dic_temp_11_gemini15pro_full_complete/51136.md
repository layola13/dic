despoiler:/dɪˈspɔɪlər/| n.|掠夺者，破坏者|A person who steals or violently removes valuable possessions from a place; a person who ruins or destroys something.|The despoilers looted the ancient city, taking all its treasures.|Synonyms: plunderer, looter, raider, pillager, ravager, vandal|Antonyms: protector, guardian, preserver|noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *despoilen, *dispoilen, from Anglo-Norman despoiller, dispeiller, from Old French despoillier, from des- (expressing removal) + spolier (“to strip, spoil”).
