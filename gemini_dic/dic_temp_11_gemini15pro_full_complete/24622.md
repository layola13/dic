broken-down:/ˈbroʊkən daʊn/ (US) /ˈbrəʊkən daʊn/ (UK)| adj.| 损坏的，破旧的，衰弱的|Out of working order; not functioning.  Also, in a weakened state, either physically or mentally.  Derived from the verb "break down," meaning to stop functioning.  The past participle "broken" combined with the adverb "down" emphasizes the state of disrepair or weakness.|例句：The car is broken-down. 汽车坏了。|例句：He was feeling broken-down after working for so long. 长时间工作后，他感到很疲惫。|近义词：worn-out, dilapidated, exhausted, debilitated|反义词：functioning, new, strong, healthy|词性:形容词

broken-down:/ˈbroʊkən daʊn/ (US) /ˈbrəʊkən daʊn/ (UK)| adj.| 详细分析的，分解的|Analyzed in detail; itemized.  Derived from the verb "break down," meaning to separate something into smaller parts for analysis.|例句：The budget was broken-down into categories. 预算被分解成几个类别。|例句：She gave a broken-down account of the events. 她详细叙述了事情的经过。|近义词：itemized, detailed, analyzed|反义词：summarized, general|词性:形容词
