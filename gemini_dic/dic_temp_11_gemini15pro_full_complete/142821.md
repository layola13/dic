out-and-out:/ˌaʊt ən ˈaʊt/| adj.|彻头彻尾的，十足的，完全的|Complete; thoroughgoing.  Used to emphasize the nature of something, usually something bad.|He's an out-and-out liar. 他是个彻头彻尾的骗子。|Synonyms: complete, total, absolute, thorough, utter, downright, thoroughgoing|Antonyms: partial, incomplete, slight|adjective

Etymology:
From "out and out," originally meaning "thoroughly," "completely," from the notion of something extending from one end to the other.  First recorded in the late 16th century.
