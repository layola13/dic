cliquey: /ˈkliːki/ | adj. | 小集团的，派系的 | Tending to form or associate in cliques; exclusive.  Characterized by the presence or influence of cliques. |  "The office was cliquey and it was hard to make friends." 办公室里小集团林立，很难交到朋友。 | synonyms: exclusive, clannish, snobbish, elitist | antonyms: inclusive, welcoming, open, friendly | 词性: adjective

cliquey: /ˈkliːki/ | adj. | 排外的，抱团的 |  Excluding those not considered part of the group; tending to stick together in small, exclusive groups.  | "The high school students were very cliquey and didn't welcome newcomers."  高中生们非常抱团，不欢迎新来的人。 | synonyms: insular, closed, restricted | antonyms: broad-minded, tolerant, accepting | 词性: adjective

词源:
From clique + -y.  "Clique" originated in French, from the verb "cliquer" meaning "to click, clink," referring to the sound of dice. It later came to mean a group of people who click or get along well together, and eventually acquired its negative connotation of exclusivity.
