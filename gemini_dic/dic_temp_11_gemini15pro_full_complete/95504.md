incline:/ɪnˈklaɪn/ (US), /ɪnˈklaɪn/ (UK)| n.| 倾斜，倾向；斜坡，斜面 | A slope or hill; a tendency or preference towards something. | The house is built on an incline. 这房子建在斜坡上。| Synonyms: slope, slant, gradient | Antonyms: decline, level, flat | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *enclinen, *inclinent, from Old French incliner, from Latin inclināre (“to bend, bow, lean”), from in- (“in, into, on, upon, towards, against”) + clināre (“to bend, lean”).
