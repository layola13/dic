harboured:/ˈhɑːbəd/ (US) /ˈhɑːbəd/ (UK)| v. |窝藏，庇护；怀有（想法或感情）|To give shelter or refuge to. To hold (a thought or feeling) secretly.|The fugitive was harboured by sympathetic villagers.  这名逃犯得到了同情他的村民的庇护。|Synonyms: shelter, conceal, harbor, entertain|Antonyms: expose, expel, evict|verb

harboring:/ˈhɑːbərɪŋ/ (US) /ˈhɑːbərɪŋ/ (UK)| v. |窝藏，庇护；怀有（想法或感情）|(Present participle of harbour)  |He was harboring a fugitive in his basement. 他在地下室窝藏一名逃犯。|Synonyms: sheltering, concealing, harboring, entertaining|Antonyms: exposing, expelling, evicting|verb
