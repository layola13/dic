chanam:/ˈtʃɑːnəm/| n.|灰泥，熟石灰，石灰浆|A lime-based plaster traditionally used in construction, particularly in South and Southeast Asia.  It is made by burning seashells or limestone and mixing the resulting lime with water and sometimes other ingredients like sand or jaggery.|The walls of the old temple were built with chanam.|Synonyms: lime plaster, mortar|Antonyms: N/A|noun
