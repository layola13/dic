mellowing:/ˈmeləʊɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈmeloʊɪŋ/ (US)| adj. |变得柔和的，成熟的，醇厚的|Becoming softer and gentler, less harsh or intense; ripening to full flavor and sweetness. |The harsh tannins in the wine are mellowing with age.  这酒中涩的单宁随着年份的增长而变得柔和了。|The autumn sunlight mellowed the landscape. 秋日的阳光使景色变得柔和。|Synonyms: softening, maturing, ripening, calming, relaxing|Antonyms: hardening, intensifying, worsening, agitating|Word Type: Adjective

mellow:/ˈmeləʊ/ (UK) /ˈmeloʊ/ (US)| adj.  v. |(adj.) 柔和的，成熟的，醇厚的；(v.) 使…柔和，使…成熟| (adj.) Soft, ripe, and full-flavored; smooth and pleasant; relaxed and good-humored. (v.) To make or become mellow.|The mellow tones of the cello filled the room.  大提琴柔和的音调充满了房间。|The years have mellowed him. 岁月使他变得成熟了。|He mellowed over time, becoming less angry and more tolerant. 随着时间的推移，他变得温和了，不那么易怒，也更宽容了。|Synonyms: (adj.) soft, ripe, smooth, gentle, pleasant; (v.) soften, mature, ripen|Antonyms: (adj.) harsh, unripe, rough, sharp, unpleasant; (v.) harden, intensify|Word Type: Adjective, Verb
