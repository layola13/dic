oversimplifying:/ˌoʊvərˈsɪmplɪfaɪɪŋ/ | v. | 过度简化 | Presenting something in a way that makes it seem less complex or nuanced than it actually is. |  The report oversimplifies the issue and ignores important factors.  这份报告过度简化了这个问题，忽略了重要因素。| Synonyms: simplifying excessively, dumbing down, trivializing | Antonyms: complicating, elaborating, nuancing | Verb (present participle)

oversimplify:/ˌoʊvərˈsɪmplɪfaɪ/ | v. | 过度简化 | To make something seem simpler or less complicated than it really is. | He oversimplified the explanation so much that it became misleading. 他过度简化了这个解释，以至于它变得误导人了。 | Synonyms:  simplify excessively, dumbing down, trivializing | Antonyms: complicate, elaborate, nuance | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + simplify, from simple + -fy.  "Over-" indicates an excess or going too far. "Simplify" comes from "simple" meaning easy to understand or do, and "-fy" meaning to make or cause to be.
