equivocation:/ɪˌkwɪvəˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 模棱两可，含糊其辞，诡辩 | The use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or to avoid committing oneself; prevarication. |  He answered the journalist's questions with equivocation. 他含糊其辞地回答了记者的提问。| synonyms: ambiguity, prevarication, evasion, double-talk | antonyms: clarity, directness, honesty, forthrightness | noun


equivocate:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪt/ | v. |  含糊其辞，支吾其词，说模棱两可的话 | To use ambiguous language so as to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself. | When asked directly for his position, the politician equivocated. 当被直接问到他的立场时，这位政客含糊其辞。 | synonyms: prevaricate, hedge, evade, fudge | antonyms:  be forthright, be direct, be honest | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin aequivocatio, from aequivocus ‘having two or more meanings’ (see equivocal).
