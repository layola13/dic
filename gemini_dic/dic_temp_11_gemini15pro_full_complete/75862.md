functionalists: /ˈfʌŋkʃənəlɪsts/
| n. |功能主义者|People who believe in or follow functionalism, a theory that explains social phenomena in terms of the functions they perform.|Functionalists believe that every part of society has a role to play in maintaining social order. | 社会学家认为，社会的每个部分都在维持社会秩序方面发挥着作用。|
| | Functionalists often study the role of institutions like family and education in society. | 功能主义者经常研究家庭和教育等机构在社会中的作用。|
| | adherents, supporters, proponents | critics, opponents, detractors | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "functional" + "-ist"
"functional":  Late 18th century.  From French fonctionnel, from fonction ('function') + -el.
"-ist":  A suffix forming agent nouns and nouns and adjectives denoting adherence to a system or principle.  From French -iste and its source Italian -ista, from Latin -ista.
