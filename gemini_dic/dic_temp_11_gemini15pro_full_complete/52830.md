diluvialist:/ˌdaɪˈluːviəlɪst/
n.
洪积论者，灾变论者
A person who believes in or studies the diluvial theory, which attributes geological formations to the effects of a great flood (especially Noah's flood).
The diluvialist argued that the canyon was carved by a massive flood. 洪积论者认为峡谷是由一场大洪水雕刻而成的。

近义词:catastrophist
反义词:uniformitarian

词源: From Latin *diluviālis (“of a flood”), from diluvium (“flood, deluge”) + -ist.
