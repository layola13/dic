ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *blind, from Proto-Germanic *blindaz, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰlendʰ- (“weak, pale, confused”). Cognates include German blind, Dutch blind, and Swedish blind.  The original sense may have been "dazzled," rather than completely sightless.
