emotivity:/ɪˌmoʊˈtɪvɪti/ | n. | 情感性，情绪化 | The quality or state of being emotional; emotional responsiveness.  | Her emotivity made her prone to tears. 她的情感丰富，容易落泪。 |  He displayed a great deal of emotivity during his speech. 他在演讲中表现出强烈的情感。| 近义词：emotionality, sensitivity, sentimentality | 反义词：apathy, indifference, coldness | 词性: noun

Etymology: From French *émotivité*, from *émotif* (“emotional”) + *-ité* (suffix forming abstract nouns). *Émotif* is from *émotion* (“emotion”) + *-if* (suffix forming adjectives). Ultimately from Latin *ēmovēre* (“to move out, remove, stir up, excite”), from *ex-* (out of) + *movēre* (to move).
