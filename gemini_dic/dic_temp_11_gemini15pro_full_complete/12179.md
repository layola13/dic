as-big-as-life:/əz ˌbɪɡ əz ˈlaɪf/
|adj.|
|栩栩如生；活灵活现；千真万确；本人；亲自|
|Appearing in person, typically unexpectedly.  Also used to emphasize that someone or something seemed completely real or genuine, even if surprising or unbelievable.|
|例句1：And then, as big as life, my old school friend walked into the room. 然后，我的老同学活生生地走进了房间。|
|例句2：There she was, as big as life, on the front page of the newspaper. 她就在那里，千真万确地登上了报纸的头版。|
|近义词：in person, in the flesh, real, genuine|
|反义词：unreal, imaginary|
|词性: adjective|


词源:
This idiom likely originated in the 18th century, emphasizing the surprising or striking presence of someone.  "Life" here refers to living, breathing reality, highlighting the unexpected physical appearance of the person.
