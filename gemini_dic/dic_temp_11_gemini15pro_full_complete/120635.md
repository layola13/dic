mis-frames:/ˌmɪsˈfreɪmz/ | v. | 误读；曲解；歪曲 | To present or interpret (something, such as a situation or an event) inaccurately or unfairly, often deliberately.  | The media mis-framed the politician's comments, making them seem more controversial than they were. 媒体歪曲了这位政治家的评论，使它们看起来比实际更有争议性。 | The lawyer argued that the prosecution had mis-framed the evidence. 律师辩称，控方歪曲了证据。| misrepresents, distorts, misconstrues | accurately represents, correctly interprets | verb
