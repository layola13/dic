carolingians: /ˌkærəˈlɪndʒiənz/ | n. | 卡洛林王朝；卡洛林家族|  A member or descendant of the Frankish dynasty founded by Charlemagne.  The Carolingians ruled as kings of the Franks and emperors of the Romans from 751 to 987. |  Charlemagne was the most famous of the Carolingians.  查理曼大帝是最著名的卡洛林王朝成员。

The Carolingian Renaissance was a period of intellectual and cultural revival.  卡洛林文艺复兴时期是一个知识和文化复兴的时期。| 近义词: Carolingian dynasty, Frankish dynasty| 反义词: None (specific dynasty, no direct opposite) | 词性: Noun (plural)
