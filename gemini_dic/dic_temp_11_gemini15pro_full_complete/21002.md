blesseder: /ˈblesɪdər/ (assuming a pronunciation similar to "bless-ed-er") | adj. (comparative) | 更受祝福的，更幸福的 |  Someone or something that is more blessed than another.  It's the comparative form of "blessed."  While "blesseder" is grammatically correct, "more blessed" is generally preferred in modern English. |  "Of the two brothers, John was the blesseder." (Less common usage)  "John was more blessed than his brother." (More common usage) |  Near Synonyms: more fortunate, luckier, more favored | Antonyms: more cursed, less fortunate, unluckier | Word type: Adjective
