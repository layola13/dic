ligated:/ˈlaɪɡeɪtɪd/
adjective
结扎的，绑扎的
Tied or bound off, especially by means of a ligature.  Specifically used in medicine to refer to the tying off of a blood vessel or duct.
The surgeon ligated the artery to stop the bleeding.  外科医生结扎了动脉以止血。
近义词:tied, bound, constricted
反义词:untied, unbound, open
词性:adjective


ligate:/ˈlaɪɡeɪt/
verb
结扎，绑扎
To tie or bind off, especially by means of a ligature.  Often used in a surgical context.
The doctor will ligate the blood vessels during the surgery. 医生将在手术期间结扎血管。
近义词:tie, bind, constrict
反义词:untie, unbind, open
词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *ligatus*, past participle of *ligare* "to bind," from Proto-Italic *ligare*, from Proto-Indo-European *\*leyǵ- "to bind."
