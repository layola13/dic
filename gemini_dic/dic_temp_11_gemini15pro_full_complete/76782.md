gamiest:/ˈɡeɪmiɪst/
adjective
最玩世不恭的；最放荡的；最不正经的
Having the characteristics of game, especially pluck, spirit, or tolerance of hardship.  Implies a spirited, often roguish, or even disreputable, kind of zest or gusto. Often used in a slightly ironic or humorous way.
例句：He told the gamiest stories about his time in the navy. 他讲述了他在海军服役期间最放荡的故事。

例句：She had a gamiest glint in her eye as she planned the prank.  当她计划恶作剧时，她的眼中闪过一丝顽皮的光芒。

近义词：racy, spicy, risqué, naughty, mischievous
反义词：prim, proper, respectable, decent
词性：adjective

词源：From "game" (spirited, plucky) + "-est" (superlative suffix).  The word "game" in this sense comes from Old English "gamen" (game, sport, amusement).
