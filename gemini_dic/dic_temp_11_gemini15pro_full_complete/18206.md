belchers:/ˈbɛltʃərz/ | n. | 打嗝的人；爱抱怨的人 | People who belch frequently; people who complain a lot. | The belchers at the back of the bus were disrupting everyone. 公交车后座那些打嗝的人打扰了所有人。|  The constant belchers in the office are so annoying. 办公室里不断抱怨的人真烦人。 |近义词：complainers, whiners |反义词: (For belching) none; (For complaining) optimists, enthusiasts | noun

belch:/bɛltʃ/ | n. vi. vt. | 打嗝；喷出 |  A noisy expulsion of gas from the stomach through the mouth; to emit something violently. | He let out a loud belch after drinking the soda. 他喝完汽水后打了个响亮的嗝。| The volcano belched ash and smoke into the sky. 火山向天空喷出火山灰和烟雾。| He belched the smoke from his cigar. 他吐出了雪茄的烟。| 近义词：burp, eructate, emit, spew | 反义词: (For belching) none; (For emitting) absorb | noun, verb, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *belchen, from Old English *bealcian (“to belch”), of imitative origin. Cognate with Dutch balken (“to bellow”), German bälken (“to bellow”).
