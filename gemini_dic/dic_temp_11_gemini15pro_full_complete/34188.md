clamours:/ˈklæmərz/ (US) /ˈklæməz/ (UK)| n. |喧闹，吵闹声；大声的要求 | A loud outcry; a vehement demand or protest, often from a large group of people. | The prime minister faced clamours for his resignation after the scandal.  首相在丑闻曝光后面临着要求他辞职的呼声。| synonyms: uproar, outcry, commotion, hubbub, din, racket | antonyms: silence, quiet, hush, stillness | noun

clamor:/ˈklæmər/ (US) /ˈklæmə(r)/ (UK)| v. |喧闹，大声要求 | To shout loudly and insistently; to make a vehement demand or protest. | The crowd clamored for an encore.  人群大声要求再来一次。| synonyms: yell, shout, demand, insist, clamor | antonyms: whisper, murmur, be quiet | verb
