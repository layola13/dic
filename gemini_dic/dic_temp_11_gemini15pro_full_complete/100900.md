intuitionist:/ˌɪn.tuˈɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ | n. | 直觉主义者；直觉论者 |  A person who believes in or follows intuitionism, a philosophy that emphasizes the role of intuition in knowledge and morality.  |  The intuitionist argued that mathematical truths are not discovered but created.  直觉主义者认为，数学真理不是被发现的，而是被创造的。

|  He's an intuitionist when it comes to investing; he relies more on gut feeling than market analysis.  在投资方面，他是一个直觉主义者；他更多地依赖直觉而不是市场分析。 |  instinctivist,  gut thinker | rationalist, empiricist | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From intuition + -ist.  Intuition from Late Latin *intuitiōn-, from intuitī 'contemplated,' past participle of intueri 'to look at attentively,' from in- 'in, on' + tueri 'to watch, guard.'
