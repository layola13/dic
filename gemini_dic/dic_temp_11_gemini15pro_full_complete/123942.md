ETYMOLOGY->
From French *morgue* "mortuary," originally "the inner wicket of a prison where prisoners were viewed," from Old French *morguer* "to look at solemnly, defy, scoff at," of uncertain origin.  The sense shifted in the 18th century to refer specifically to a viewing room for the dead.
