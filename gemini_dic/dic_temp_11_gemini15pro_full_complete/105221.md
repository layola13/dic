keystone:/ˈkiːstoʊn/| n.|基石，楔石，关键|A central stone at the summit of an arch, locking the whole together.  Something on which other things depend.|The keystone of their policy is full employment.  他们的政策的基石是充分就业。|cornerstone, linchpin, centerpiece|none|noun
Etymology->
From Middle English *keye ston*, from Old English *cǣgstan* (“key stone”), equivalent to *cǣg* (“key, peg”) + *stān* (“stone”). Cognate with Middle Dutch *keysteen*, Middle Low German *kēsten*, German *Keilstein* (“keystone”).  The sense shifted from "wedge-shaped stone" to "something essential" in the 18th century.
