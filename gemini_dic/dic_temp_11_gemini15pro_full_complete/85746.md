hawkishnesses:/ˈhɔːkɪʃnəsɪz/
noun
鹰派作风；强硬态度
The quality of being hawkish, i.e., advocating aggressive or warlike policies, especially in foreign affairs.
His hawkishnesses on defense spending worried some voters.  他对国防开支的强硬态度让一些选民感到担忧。
近义词: aggressiveness, belligerence, militancy
反义词: dovishness, pacifism
词性: noun

词源: From hawkish + -ness.  Hawkish itself derives from hawk (n.), referring to the bird of prey, known for its aggressive hunting behavior.  The figurative use of "hawk" to describe a person who favors war or aggressive policies emerged in the mid-20th century.
