Confucius: /kənˈfjuːʃəs/ | n. | 孔子 | A Chinese philosopher and politician of the Spring and Autumn period who is traditionally considered the paragon of Chinese sages. | Confucius's teachings emphasized personal and governmental morality, correctness of social relationships, justice, kindness, and sincerity. |  Confucius is revered as a symbol of Chinese culture and wisdom. | 近义词: sage, philosopher, teacher | 反义词: fool, ignoramus | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Latin *Confucius*, from earlier *Confutius*, based on Chinese *Kǒng Fūzǐ* (孔夫子), meaning "Master Kong".
