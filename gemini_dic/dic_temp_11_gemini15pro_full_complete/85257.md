harmon:/ˈhɑːrmən/ | n. | 和声；泛音；谐波 |A sound that is made when another sound is played, and that has a higher frequency (= number of sound waves per second) than the other sound. Also, any of a series of musical notes that are played or sung above a basic note, which combine to form a pleasing sound; a component frequency of an oscillation or wave.| The violin's tone is rich in harmonics. 小提琴的音调富含谐波。| synonyms: overtone, partial | antonyms: fundamental | noun
