eremitage: /ˈɛrəmɪtɪdʒ/ | n. | 隐居处，隐士住所；僻静的地方 | A secluded dwelling or retreat, especially one occupied by a hermit. |  He sought solace in his eremitage high in the mountains. 他在山上的隐居处寻求慰藉。| synonyms: hermitage, retreat, sanctuary, seclusion, solitude | antonyms: city, metropolis, urban area | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *ermitage*, from Late Latin *eremitagium*, from Latin *eremita* (“hermit”), from Greek *ἐρημίτης* (*erēmítēs*, “desert dweller”).
