decadent:/ˈdɛkədənt/| adj. n.|颓废的；堕落的；放纵的人|Characterized by or reflecting a state of moral or cultural decline.  A person who is luxuriously self-indulgent.|The decadent rich gave lavish parties. 那些颓废的富人举办奢华的派对。|Synonyms: corrupt, degenerate, dissipated, profligate|Antonyms: austere, moral, upright|adjective


decadent:/ˈdɛkədənt/| n.|颓废者；放纵的人|A person who is luxuriously self-indulgent.|He lived the life of a decadent. 他过着颓废的生活。|Synonyms: voluptuary, sybarite|Antonyms: ascetic, puritan|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *décadent*, present participle of *déchoir* ("to decay, decline"), from Latin *cadere* ("to fall").
