boogie-down:/ˈbuːɡi daʊn/| adj. n.| 尽情跳舞的；热闹的；与嘻哈文化相关的；布朗克斯区（尤指其嘻哈文化） |  Referring to a lively, exciting atmosphere, often associated with hip-hop culture and dance, especially originating in the Bronx borough of New York City.  Can also refer to the Bronx itself. |  The party was a real boogie-down affair.  派对真是热闹非凡。|  funky, groovy, hip, happening | dull, boring | adjective, noun
boogie-down:/ˈbuːɡi daʊn/| v.| 尽情跳舞 | To dance enthusiastically, especially to hip-hop or funk music. |  They were boogieing-down all night long.  他们整晚都在尽情跳舞。|  groove, get down, dance, party | sit still, be quiet | verb

词源: "Boogie" comes from boogie-woogie, a style of blues piano music. "Down" intensifies the action, suggesting enthusiastic participation.  "Boogie-down" gained popularity in the 1980s hip-hop scene, particularly associated with the Bronx.
