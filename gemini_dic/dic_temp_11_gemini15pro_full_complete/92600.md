iamb's:/ˈaɪæmz/| n.|抑扬格(的)|A metrical foot consisting of one unstressed syllable followed by one stressed syllable, or a line of verse made up of such feet.  A poem written using iambs is said to be written in iambic meter. |Example:  "The CURfew TOLLS the KNELL of PARting DAY" (Thomas Gray, "Elegy Written in a Country Churchyard") is a line of iambic pentameter. |近义词: foot (in poetry)|反义词: trochee|词性:noun
