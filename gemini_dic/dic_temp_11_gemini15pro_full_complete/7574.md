annotationist:/ˌænəˈteɪʃənɪst/ | n. |注释者，注解者| A person who adds annotations or notes to a text, image, or other piece of information. | Example: The annotationist meticulously documented every detail of the historical manuscript. 这位注释者一丝不苟地记录了这份历史手稿的每一个细节。

Example:  The annotationist provided helpful explanations for the complex technical terms. 注释者对复杂的专业术语提供了有益的解释。

|Synonym: commentator, annotator, expounder, elucidator | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps obfuscator - someone who makes things unclear) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From annotate + -ist.  "Annotate" comes from the Latin "annotatus," the past participle of "annotare," meaning "to mark" or "to note." "Annotare" is formed from "ad-" (to, towards) + "notare" (to mark). "-ist" is a suffix indicating a person who performs a particular action or has a particular skill.
