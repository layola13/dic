bolter:/ˈboʊltər/| n.| 逃跑者，脱离者；(尤指)叛逃者；脱缰的马；筛子 |A person or animal that bolts, especially a horse. A person who abruptly abandons a political party or other organization. A sieve or sifter.|The senator was a bolter from the Republican Party.  那位参议员是共和党的叛逃者。|Synonyms: deserter, runaway, rebel, renegade |Antonyms: loyalist, adherent|noun


bolter:/ˈboʊltər/| v.| (使)脱缰；(使)逃跑；快速冲出；筛(面粉等) |To run away suddenly. To sift or separate something.|The horse bolted at the sound of the gunshot.  枪声一响，马就脱缰而逃了。|Synonyms: flee, escape, dash, sift |Antonyms: stay, remain|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bolten*, from Old Norse *bolta* "to bolt".
