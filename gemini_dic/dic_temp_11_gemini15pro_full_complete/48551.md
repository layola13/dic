defamation:/ˌdefəˈmeɪʃn/ | n. | 诽谤，中伤 | The act of communicating false statements about a person that injure the person's reputation. | The newspaper article was a blatant act of defamation against the politician. 这篇报纸文章是对这位政治家公然的诽谤。 |  slander, libel, calumny, vilification |  praise, commendation | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French diffamacion, or from late Latin diffamatio(n-), from the verb diffamare ‘spread ill reports’, from dis- (expressing reversal) + fama ‘reputation’.
