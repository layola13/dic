jaundices: /ˈdʒɔːndɪsɪz/ | n. | 黄疸 | A medical condition in which the skin, whites of the eyes, and mucous membranes turn yellow because of a high level of bilirubin in the blood. | The baby was born with jaundice.  这个婴儿出生时患有黄疸。|  The doctor diagnosed the patient with jaundice due to liver problems. 医生诊断该病人因肝脏问题患有黄疸。 | synonyms: icterus | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a medical condition. One could consider "healthy liver function" a contrasting state.) | noun
