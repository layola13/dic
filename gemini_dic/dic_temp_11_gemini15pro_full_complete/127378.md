neanderthal: /niˈændərˌθɔːl/ (British) /niˈændərˌtɑːl/ (American)| n. adj.|尼安德特人；粗鲁的人|An extinct species of human that was widely distributed in ice-age Europe between *c*. 120,000 and 35,000 years ago, with a receding forehead and prominent brow ridges.  Figuratively, a Neanderthal is a person who is perceived as being primitive, unintelligent, or uncultured.|例句：Neanderthals were skilled hunters. 尼安德特人是熟练的猎人。

|例句：He called him a Neanderthal for his outdated views. 他因为他过时的观点称他为尼安德特人。|近义词：caveman, hominid (as noun), primitive, crude, backward (as adjective)|反义词：modern human, sophisticated, cultured (as adjective)|词性: noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Neander Thal (Neander Valley), near Düsseldorf, Germany, where the first recognized fossil was found.  "Thal" is German for "valley."
