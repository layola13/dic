mossbanker:/ˈmɒsˌbæŋkər/| n. |苔藓银行家; 在苔藓覆盖的河岸上钓鱼的人|A person who fishes from moss-covered riverbanks.| He's a keen mossbanker, spending hours by the river. 他是一个热衷于在苔藓覆盖的河岸钓鱼的人，会在河边待上几个小时。|近义词:angler, fisherman|反义词:None|词性:名词

Etymology:
moss (n.) + bank (n.) + -er (suffix forming agent nouns)

moss (n.): Old English mos, from Proto-Germanic *mussa-, from PIE root *meu- "moist."
bank (n.): Old Norse bakki "ridge, bank," from Proto-Germanic *bankiz "slope, bench," from PIE root *bheg- "to bend, bow."
-er (suffix): from Old English -ere, from Proto-Germanic *-arjaR, agentive suffix of verbs, from PIE *-teros.
