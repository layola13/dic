inner-circle:/ˈɪnər ˈsɜːrkl/ | adj. n. |核心圈子，核心集团；内部人士 |A small group of people within a larger group who have a lot of power or influence.  A group of close associates or confidants.| He's in the inner circle of the president's advisors. 他是总统顾问的核心成员。| The inner circle made all the important decisions. 核心集团做出了所有重要决定。 |近义词：clique, core group, inner ring |反义词：outer circle, periphery |词性: 形容词, 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "inner" (meaning inside or internal) + "circle" (a round shape).  "Inner" comes from Old English "innera" (comparative of "inne," meaning within).  "Circle" comes from Old French "cercle" and ultimately from Latin "circulus" (diminutive of "circus," meaning ring).
