landlock: /ˈlændlɒk/ (British) /ˈlændlɑːk/ (American)
| vt. adj.|陆锁，被陆地包围|To surround by land; to prevent access to the sea.|例句：The country is landlocked and has no access to the sea.  这个国家是内陆国家，没有出海口。

例句：High tariffs landlocked the country's economy, stifling trade and growth.  高额关税使该国经济陷入困境，扼杀了贸易和增长。|近义词：enclosed, surrounded, inland|反义词:coastal, maritime|词性:verb, adjective

landlocked: /ˈlændlɒk/ (British) /ˈlændlɑːk/ (American)
| adj.|被陆地包围的，内陆的|Surrounded entirely or almost entirely by land; having no coastline or seaport.|例句：Switzerland is a landlocked country in Europe.  瑞士是欧洲的一个内陆国家。

例句：The landlocked nation relies heavily on its neighbors for access to ports. 这个内陆国家严重依赖其邻国获得港口通道。|近义词：inland, interior|反义词:coastal, maritime|词性:adjective
