microburin:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌbʊərɪn/ | n. | 微石锛; 微型雕刻器 | A small, chisel-like flint tool fragment, typically a byproduct of blade production, often exhibiting a characteristic angled break.  It is considered a diagnostic artifact of certain prehistoric cultures. | Archaeologists found numerous microburins at the site, indicating a Late Upper Paleolithic occupation. 考古学家在该遗址发现了大量的微石锛，表明该遗址属于晚期旧石器时代晚期。 | The presence of microburins suggests the use of a specific blade-making technique. 微石锛的存在表明使用了一种特定的刀片制作技术。 | synonyms: burin spall (though not perfectly interchangeable) | antonyms: N/A | noun
