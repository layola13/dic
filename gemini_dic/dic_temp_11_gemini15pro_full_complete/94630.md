imprescriptibly:/ɪmprɪˈskrɪptɪbli/
adverb
不可抗拒地；无法取消地；不因时效而作废地
in a way that cannot be taken away or made invalid, especially by the passage of time.
The right to life is imprescriptibly enshrined in the constitution. 生命权不可磨灭地载入宪法。
近义词: indefeasibly, permanently, inalienably
反义词: revocably, conditionally
词性: adverb

词源:
From Late Latin *imprescriptibilis, from in- (“not”) + praescribere (“to prescribe, to lay down as a law”) + -ibilis (suffix forming adjectives). Equivalent to im- +‎ prescript +‎ -ible.
