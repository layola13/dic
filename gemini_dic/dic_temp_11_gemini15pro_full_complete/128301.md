neurosis:/nʊˈroʊsɪs/ /njʊˈrəʊsɪs/| n. |神经症；神经官能症|A relatively mild mental illness that is characterized by symptoms of stress (depression, anxiety, obsessive behavior, hypochondria) but not a radical loss of touch with reality.|She suffers from acute neurosis.|近义词：mental disorder, emotional disturbance, psychological disorder|反义词: mental health, stability|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek νεῦρον (neûron, “sinew, tendon, nerve”) +‎ -ωσις (-ōsis, abstract noun suffix).
