as-a-matter-of-fact:/æz ə ˈmætər əv fækt/| idiom|事实上，其实|Used to introduce a statement that corrects or contradicts something previously said, or adds to it in an important way.  It often emphasizes the truth of the statement being made.|例句：I thought he was from Spain. As a matter of fact, he's from Portugal. 我以为他来自西班牙。事实上，他来自葡萄牙。|近义词：in fact, actually, in reality, to tell the truth|反义词:theoretically, hypothetically|词性 idiom
