outlawed:/ˈaʊtˌlɔːd/ | adj. | 非法的，被禁止的 |  Declared illegal or prohibited. Something that is outlawed is no longer legal or allowed. |  Dog fighting is outlawed in most countries. 斗狗在大多数国家都是非法的。|  illegal, prohibited, banned, forbidden | legal, permitted, allowed | adjective

outlaw:/ˈaʊtˌlɔː/ | n. | 歹徒，逃犯 | A person who has broken the law and is hiding or running away from the authorities.  A person excluded from the benefit or protection of the law. | The outlaw was finally captured after years on the run.  这名逃犯在逃亡多年后终于被抓获。 | criminal, fugitive, bandit, robber | law-abiding citizen | noun

outlaw:/ˈaʊtˌlɔː/ | vt. | 宣布…为不合法，禁止 | To make something illegal or prohibited.  To deprive of the benefit and protection of the law. |  The government outlawed the sale of certain drugs. 政府禁止销售某些药物。| ban, prohibit, forbid, illegalize | legalize, permit, allow | verb
