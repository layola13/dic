groin:/ɡrɔɪn/| n.|腹股沟；棱线；交叉拱|The area of the body where the thigh joins the abdomen; the junction of two intersecting vaults, especially in architecture; a structure built out from a shore to protect against erosion.|He pulled a muscle in his groin. 他拉伤了腹股沟肌肉。 |The groin vaulting of the church was impressive. 教堂的交叉拱顶令人印象深刻。|Synonyms: (anatomical) inguinal region; (architectural)  rib, vault|Antonyms: (For the architectural sense, no direct antonym. groin:/ɡrɔɪn/| vt.| 装棱线；使成穹棱|To build or construct groins (architectural structures).|They groined the ceiling to support the weight above. 他们用拱顶支撑上面的重量。|Synonyms: arch, vault|Antonyms: flatten, level|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *groigne ("snout, projecting part"), from Vulgar Latin *grunia, of uncertain origin. Related to groin (n. "junction of vaults"). The anatomical sense emerged later, perhaps influenced by the resemblance of the inguinal region to the architectural feature.
