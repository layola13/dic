keel:/kiːl/
n.|龙骨; (船的) 底部; 平底船;
|The ship's keel was damaged in the collision.  这艘船的龙骨在碰撞中受损了。
|The keel of the sailboat sliced through the water. 帆船的龙骨划破水面。
|近义词:bottom, hull
|反义词: (没有明确的反义词)
|词性:名词

keel:/kiːl/
v.|（使）倾覆; （使）翻倒;  (使)晕倒;
|The small boat keeled over in the strong wind. 小船在强风中倾覆了。
|He keeled over from exhaustion. 他累得晕倒了。
|近义词:capsize, overturn, faint
|反义词:right, stabilize
|词性:动词

词源：
From Middle English *kēle, from Old English cēol (“ship, keel”), from Proto-Germanic *kēlaz (“ship”), from Proto-Indo-European *keh₂l- (“ship, boat”). Cognate with West Frisian kiel (“keel”), Dutch kiel (“keel”), German Kiel (“keel”), Swedish köl (“keel”), Icelandic kjöl (“keel”). Doublet of keelson.
