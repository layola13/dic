muckers:/ˈmʌkərz/ | n. |伙伴，朋友；捣乱者，搞破坏的人| Close friends or companions, especially those involved in rough or boisterous activities; those who make a mess or cause trouble. |  They've been muckers since they were kids.  他们从小就是好朋友。 | synonyms: pals, mates, buddies, partners, comrades; troublemakers, mischief-makers | antonyms: enemies, foes;  goody-goodies | noun

muckers:/ˈmʌkərz/ | n. |笨蛋，傻瓜|  Foolish or inept people. | Don't be such a mucker!  别这么傻！| synonyms: fools, idiots, dolts, nincompoops | antonyms: geniuses,  smart people | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From British slang, likely related to "muck" (dirt, filth), implying those who work with or are involved in messy activities.  The sense of "friend" may have developed from shared experiences in such activities.
