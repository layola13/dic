haemorrhages:/ˈhɛmərɪdʒɪz/ (UK) /ˈhɛmərɪdʒəz/ (US)| n. v.| 出血；大量流失|A haemorrhage is a medical term referring to the escape of blood from a ruptured blood vessel, especially when profuse. It can also be used figuratively to describe a rapid and uncontrolled loss of something valuable, such as money or resources. |The patient was suffering from a severe haemorrhage. 病人患有严重的出血。|synonyms: bleeding, loss of blood, effusion, outflow|antonyms: clotting, coagulation|noun


haemorrhage:/ˈhɛmərɪdʒ/ (UK) /ˈhɛmərɪdʒ/ (US)| v.| 出血；大量流失|To haemorrhage means to bleed profusely or to lose a large amount of something valuable rapidly.|The company haemorrhaged money after the failed investment.  公司在投资失败后大量失血。|synonyms: bleed profusely, lose heavily|antonyms: gain, accumulate|verb
