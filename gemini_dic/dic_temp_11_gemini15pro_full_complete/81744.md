greenwashing:/ˈɡriːnwɒʃɪŋ/ (US) /ˈɡriːnwɒʃɪŋ/ (UK)| n.|漂绿|The dissemination of misleading information about a company's environmental performance, often to present a more environmentally responsible public image.|Many companies are accused of greenwashing to attract environmentally conscious consumers.|近义词：eco-posturing, green sheen|反义词：genuine environmental responsibility|词性:名词

Etymology:
A blend of "green" (referring to environmentalism) and "whitewash" (meaning to gloss over or cover up something bad).  The term was coined in the 1980s by environmentalist Jay Westerveld.
