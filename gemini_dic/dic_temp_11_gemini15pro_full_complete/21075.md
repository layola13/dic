blindsided:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪdɪd/
verb|使措手不及，出其不意地打击|To surprise someone, usually with bad news or some other unpleasant event, especially when they are unprepared for it.|The news of his dismissal completely blindsided him. 他被解雇的消息完全打了他个措手不及。|
近义词:surprised, shocked, stunned, ambushed|
反义词:forewarned, prepared|
词性:verb

blindsided:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪdɪd/
adjective|措手不及的，出乎意料的|Surprised and unprepared, especially by something unpleasant.|I was completely blindsided by their decision. 他们的决定让我完全措手不及。|
近义词:unprepared, unsuspecting, caught off guard|
反义词:prepared, expecting|
词性:adjective


词源:
From "blind side," referring to the side of a person that cannot be seen, and therefore vulnerable to attack.
