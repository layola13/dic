naffer:/ˈnæfə(r)/
adjective
笨拙的，无能的
A person, especially a man, who is foolish or incompetent.
He's a complete naffer when it comes to DIY.  他在DIY方面完全是个笨蛋。
近义词:klutz, clumsy, awkward, inept
反义词:competent, skilled, adept
词性:adjective

词源:
Possibly from a dialectal variant of "knacker" (meaning to tire out or exhaust), implying someone who is worn out and thus incompetent.  Its origins are uncertain and likely informal.
