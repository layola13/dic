coups:/kuːz/ (plural of coup)
| n.|政变；突然成功的行动|A sudden, violent, and illegal seizure of power from a government.|例句：The military coup resulted in the overthrow of the democratically elected government. 军事政变导致民选政府被推翻。

| | | |例句：Several coups have taken place in the country in recent years.  近年来，该国发生了几次政变。
| | | |近义词：overthrow, takeover, rebellion, revolt, insurrection, putsch|反义词: restoration, reinstatement|noun (plural)


coup:/kuː/
| n.|政变；妙计；出乎意料的成功|A sudden, violent, and illegal seizure of power from a government; a brilliant and unexpected manoeuvre; a notable or successful stroke or move.|例句：The coup was swiftly and brutally executed. 政变迅速而残酷地执行了。

| | | |例句：Pulling off that deal was a real coup for the company.  完成那笔交易对公司来说真是一个妙计。
| | | |近义词：overthrow, takeover, rebellion, revolt, insurrection, putsch, triumph, feat, masterstroke|反义词: failure, defeat|noun
