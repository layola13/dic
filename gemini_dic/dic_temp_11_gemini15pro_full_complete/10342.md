appeasement: /əˈpiːzmənt/ | n. | 绥靖；姑息；平息 | The act of giving in to an aggressor's demands in order to avoid conflict.  |  Example: The policy of appeasement towards Hitler in the 1930s ultimately failed to prevent war. 20世纪30年代对希特勒的绥靖政策最终未能阻止战争。 | Synonyms: conciliation, pacification, concession | Antonyms: confrontation, resistance, defiance | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *apaisement "pacification," from apaiser "to pacify, appease," from a- "to" (see ad-) + pais "peace," from Latin pacem (nominative pax) "peace," related to pangere "to fix, fasten."
