gigue:/ʒiːɡ/ | n. | 吉格舞曲；快步舞；急速的旋转 | A lively dance in triple time, popular in the 17th and 18th centuries.  A fast, lively piece of music, typically in triple time. | The orchestra played a lively gigue.  乐队演奏了一首活泼的吉格舞曲。 |  The final movement of the suite was a brilliant gigue.  组曲的最后一个乐章是一首精彩的吉格舞曲。 | synonyms: jig, reel, hornpipe | antonyms:  slow waltz, adagio | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *gigue*, from Old French *giguer* "to frolic, jig," perhaps of imitative origin.
