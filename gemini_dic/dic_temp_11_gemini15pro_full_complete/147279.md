overmixed:/ˌoʊvərˈmɪkst/| adj.|过度混合的|Mixed too much; excessively blended.|The cake was dense and heavy because the batter was overmixed.|Synonyms: overbeaten, overprocessed|Antonyms: undermixed|adjective
overmix:/ˌoʊvərˈmɪks/| v.|过度混合|To mix ingredients for too long, typically resulting in a tough or dense texture.|Be careful not to overmix the dough, or the bread will be tough.|Synonyms: overbeat, overprocess|Antonyms: undermix|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + mix.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "too much" or "excessively". "Mix" comes from the Old French "mixer," ultimately from the Latin "miscere," meaning "to mix."
