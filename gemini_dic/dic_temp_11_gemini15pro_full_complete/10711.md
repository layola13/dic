apron: /ˈeɪprən/ | n. |围裙; (舞台的)台口; (飞机跑道尽头的)停机坪 |A garment of cloth or leather or plastic that is tied about the waist and worn to protect the clothing while working. |She wore an apron while she cooked. 她做饭时穿着围裙。|Synonyms: pinafore, coverall, bib |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but one could consider "formal wear") |noun

apron: /ˈeɪprən/ | n. |(飞机跑道尽头的)停机坪 |A paved area for parking aircraft. |The plane taxied to the apron. 飞机滑行到停机坪。|Synonyms: tarmac, ramp |Antonyms: runway (arguably) |noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *naperon "small tablecloth," diminutive of nape "tablecloth," from Latin mappa "napkin, cloth."  The initial "n" was lost through misdivision of "a napron" as "an apron."
