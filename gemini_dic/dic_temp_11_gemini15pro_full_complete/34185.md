clamoured:/ˈklæmərd/ (US) /ˈklæməd/ (UK)| v.|大声疾呼，喧嚷，吵闹|To demand something noisily or insistently.|The crowd clamoured for an encore.  人群大声喊着要求再来一个节目。|Synonyms: shouted, yelled, shrieked, roared, bayed|Antonyms: whispered, murmured, muttered|Verb

clamor:/ˈklæmər/ (US) /ˈklæmə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.|喧闹声，吵闹；大声疾呼|A loud and confused noise, especially that of people shouting vehemently.|The clamor of the protesters filled the streets. 抗议者的喧闹声充满了街道。|Synonyms: uproar, din, hubbub, racket, commotion|Antonyms: silence, quiet, stillness|Noun, Verb
