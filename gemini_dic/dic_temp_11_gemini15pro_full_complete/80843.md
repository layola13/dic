gould:/ɡuːld/| n. |古尔德; 黃雀;|A surname, notably associated with the ornithologist John Gould and the pianist Glenn Gould.  It also refers to a type of finch. |John Gould was a renowned ornithologist.  约翰·古尔德是一位著名的鸟类学家。

Glenn Gould's piano playing was highly distinctive. 格伦·古尔德的钢琴演奏非常独特。|近义词：(For the bird) finch, canary |反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *golde, from Old English golde, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *gulþaz (“gold”).  The surname likely originated as a nickname for someone with golden hair or a wealthy person.  The bird's name comes from the same root, referring to its yellow plumage.
