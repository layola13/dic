cohabiters:/koʊˈhæbɪtərz/| n. |同居者|People who live together, especially couples who are not married.|Example: The apartment building had a mix of families and cohabiters. 这栋公寓楼里住着各种家庭和同居伴侣。|Example:  Many cohabiters eventually choose to get married. 许多同居者最终选择结婚。|近义词：housemates, roommates, partners, live-in lovers|反义词: spouses, married couples|词性:noun

cohabiter:/koʊˈhæbɪtər/| n. |同居者|A person who lives with another, especially a partner in a romantic relationship without being married.|Example: My cohabiter and I share the rent and household chores. 我和我的同居伴侣分担房租和家务。|Example:  It's becoming increasingly common for young cohabiters to buy property together. 年轻的同居伴侣一起买房越来越普遍。|近义词：housemate, roommate, partner, live-in lover|反义词: spouse, married person|词性:noun

词源:
From co- + habit + -er.  Co- means "together," habit refers to "dwelling," and -er denotes a person who performs an action.  因此，cohabiter 的意思是“住在一起的人”。
