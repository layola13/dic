dotage:/ˈdoʊtɪdʒ/| n. |老朽; 衰老; 昏聩|The state of being old and weak, especially mentally; senility. |His grandfather is in his dotage and often forgets things. 他的祖父年事已高，经常忘记事情。| 近义词:senility, feebleness, decrepitude |反义词:youth, vigor, prime|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *dotage, from Old French *dotage, from doten (“to be foolish, infatuated”), from dot (“foolish, infatuated”), from Frankish *dōt, from Proto-Germanic *dauþaz (“stupid, foolish”). Cognate with Dutch dot (“foolish, simple”), German Tölpel (“fool, lout”). Ultimately from the same Proto-Indo-European root as death.
