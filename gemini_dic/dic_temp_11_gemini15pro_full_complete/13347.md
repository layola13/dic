atlantides:/ətˈlæntɪdiːz/
n.
亚特兰蒂斯（复数）
A legendary island, first mentioned by Plato, said to have been swallowed by the sea.  It is often portrayed as an advanced civilization that represents an ideal of human perfection.
The atlantides were said to be a powerful and advanced civilization.  据说亚特兰蒂斯是一个强大而先进的文明。
atlantis, atlantean
--
n.
词性
Etymology: From Ancient Greek Ἀτλαντὶς νῆσος (Atlantìs nêsos, “island of Atlas”).
