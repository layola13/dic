japhetic:/dʒəˈfɛtɪk/
| adj.|雅弗的，印欧语系的|Relating to Japheth, one of the sons of Noah, or his descendants, traditionally considered to be the ancestors of the Indo-European peoples.|The term "japhetic" is now considered outdated and potentially offensive, as it reflects an outdated understanding of linguistic and ethnic origins.  It was once used to describe languages now classified as Indo-European.|This theory posited a "Japhetic" origin for many European languages.  这个理论假设许多欧洲语言起源于“雅弗”。|Near synonyms (though usage differs significantly): Indo-European|Antonyms:  None (the term is outdated and no longer used in a way that allows for direct antonyms)|Adjective
