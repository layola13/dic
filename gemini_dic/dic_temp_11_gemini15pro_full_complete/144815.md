over-humble:/ˌoʊvərˈhʌmbəl/ | adj. | 过分谦逊的，过分谦卑的 | Excessively humble, to a degree that may seem insincere or affected.  Characterized by excessive humility, often to the point of self-deprecation. | He gave an over-humble acceptance speech, downplaying his significant contributions to the project. 他发表了过分谦逊的获奖感言，淡化了他对该项目的重大贡献。|  She was over-humble about her achievements, attributing her success to luck. 她对自己的成就过分谦逊，把成功归因于运气。 | 近义词: excessively modest, self-effacing, unassuming | 反义词: arrogant, conceited, boastful | 词性: adjective

over-humble:| /ˌoʊvərˈhʌmbəl/ | adj. | 过分谦逊的，过分谦卑的 | Etymology:  "over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi, meaning "above, over") + "humble" (from Old French umble, from Latin humilis "low, lowly, humble," literally "on the ground," from humus "ground, earth").  The prefix "over-" intensifies the meaning of "humble" to suggest an excessive or affected degree of humility. |  He made an over-humble apology, even though it wasn't entirely his fault. 他做了一个过分谦卑的道歉，即使那并不完全是他的错。 | He was over-humble in his response to the praise. 他对赞扬的回应过分谦逊。 | 近义词: excessively modest, self-deprecating | 反义词: arrogant, proud | 词性: adjective
