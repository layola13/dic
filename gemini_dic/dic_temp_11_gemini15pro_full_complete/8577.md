anticlassicalism:/ˌæntiˈklæsɪsɪzəm/| n.|反古典主义|A movement or tendency in art, literature, music, or architecture that rejects or reacts against the principles of classicism. It often emphasizes individuality, imagination, and emotional expression over adherence to traditional rules and forms.|The Romantic movement in literature was a strong expression of anticlassicalism, rejecting the formality and restraint of the preceding Neoclassical era.|近义词:romanticism, modernism|反义词:classicism, neoclassicism|noun
