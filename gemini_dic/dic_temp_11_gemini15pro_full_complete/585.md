about-turn:/əˈbaʊt tɜːrn/| n.|向后转，彻底改变|A sudden change of opinion or policy.  The government did an about-turn on its tax policy.| The company made an about-turn on its decision to close the factory.  公司彻底改变了关闭工厂的决定。|近义词：U-turn, reversal, volte-face|反义词:continuation, persistence|词性:noun
about-face:/əˈbaʊt feɪs/| n.|向后转，彻底改变|(US) Same meaning as about-turn. The general ordered the troops to about-face.| The senator did an about-face on the issue after pressure from his constituents. 这位参议员在其选民的压力下，在这个问题上来了个一百八十度大转弯。|近义词：U-turn, reversal, volte-face, about-turn|反义词:continuation, persistence|词性:noun

词源： From the military command "About turn!", instructing soldiers to turn 180 degrees.


注意事项:  "about-turn" and "about-face" are essentially synonymous, with "about-face" being more common in American English.  While primarily a noun, "about-turn" (and less commonly "about-face") can sometimes function adjectivally, e.g., "an about-turn decision."
