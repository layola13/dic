ikaro:/ˈɪkɑːroʊ/| n. |伊卡洛斯; 伊卡洛|Icarus, in Greek mythology, the son of Daedalus, who attempted to escape from Crete with wings made of feathers and wax.  Icarus flew too close to the sun, the wax melted, and he fell into the sea and drowned.|Daedalus fashioned wings for himself and Icarus, warning his son not to fly too high nor too low. Icarus, ignoring his father's warning, flew too close to the sun. The wax holding his wings together melted and he plunged into the sea and drowned.|近义词:none (Proper noun)|反义词:none (Proper noun)|名词
