long-standing:/ˌlɔːŋˈstændɪŋ/ |adj.| 长期存在的，由来已久的 |Having existed or continued for a long time.|  We have a long-standing agreement with the company. 我们与该公司有一项长期协议。| The two families have a long-standing feud. 这两个家族之间有着由来已久的世仇。| 近义词:long-term, enduring, established,  chronic | 反义词:recent, new, short-lived, temporary | 词性:形容词

Etymology:
"long" (Old English "lang," meaning "long") + "standing" (present participle of "stand," from Old English "standan," meaning "to stand").  The combined meaning implies something that has endured or persisted for a considerable period.
