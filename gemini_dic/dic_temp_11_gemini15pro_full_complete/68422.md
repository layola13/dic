falsified: /ˈfɔːlsɪfaɪd/ |v. | 篡改；伪造；歪曲 | to make something false; to change information so that it is not true |  The documents were falsified to hide the truth.  这些文件被篡改以掩盖真相。| synonyms: forged, fabricated, counterfeited, misrepresented, distorted | antonyms: verified, authenticated, validated | verb
falsify: /ˈfɔːlsɪfaɪ/ |v. | 篡改；伪造；歪曲 | to make something false; to change information so that it is not true | He was accused of falsifying financial records. 他被指控伪造财务记录。| synonyms: forge, fabricate, counterfeit, misrepresent, distort | antonyms: verify, authenticate, validate | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *falsificāre, from Latin falsus (“false”) + -ficāre (from facere (“to make, to do”)).
