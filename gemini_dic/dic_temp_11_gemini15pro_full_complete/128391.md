new lease on life:/nuː liːs ɒn laɪf/ (US) /njuː liːs ɒn laɪf/ (UK)
|n.|新生，重获新生|A fresh start or renewed energy and enthusiasm, often after a period of difficulty or illness.|After his surgery, he felt like he had a new lease on life.  手术后，他感觉自己重获新生。|Synonyms: fresh start, second chance, revival, rejuvenation|Antonyms: decline, deterioration|Noun

Etymology:  The phrase originated in the legal context of renewing a lease on a property, extending its use. This meaning was then figuratively applied to life, signifying a renewal of vigor and vitality.  这个短语起源于法律语境，指续签房产租赁合同，延长其使用期限。后来这个意思被比喻地应用于生命，表示活力和生机的恢复。
