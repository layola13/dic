harrowers:/ˈhæroʊərz/
noun
耙地者；使痛苦的人；掠夺者
People who harrow, either literally (with a farm implement) or figuratively (causing distress).
The harrowers worked tirelessly in the field.  耙地者在田里不知疲倦地工作。
The tax collectors were seen as harrowers of the poor.  税吏被看作是穷人的掠夺者。
synonyms: tormentors, oppressors, pillagers
antonyms: comforters, protectors, benefactors
n.

harrow:/ˈhæroʊ/
verb/noun
耙地；使痛苦；掠夺
To draw a harrow over (land); to inflict great distress on; to pillage or plunder.
The farmer harrowed the field after plowing.  农夫耕地后耙地。
The memories of the war harrowed him.  战争的记忆使他痛苦不堪。
synonyms: distress, torment, ravage
antonyms: comfort, soothe, protect
v./n.
