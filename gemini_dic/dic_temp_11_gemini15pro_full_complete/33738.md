circuiter:/ˈsɜːrkɪtər/| n.|巡回者，巡回演出的人；流动推销员|A person who travels a circuit, especially a judge or a performer.|The circuiter visited several towns during his tour.  这位巡回演出者在他的巡回演出期间访问了几个城镇。|Synonyms: traveler, itinerant,  salesperson|Antonyms: resident, settler|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "circuit" + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person or thing associated with a particular activity or place).  "Circuit" comes from Old French "circuit" and ultimately from Latin "circuitus," past participle of "circumire"  ("to go around"), from "circum" ("around") + "ire" ("to go").
