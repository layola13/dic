inrush:/ˈɪnrʌʃ/| n.|涌入，浪涌；（电流等的）突入，涌流|A sudden, powerful flow of something, especially of a liquid, gas, or electricity.|The inrush of cold air when the door opened made me shiver. 门打开时冷空气涌入，让我打了个寒颤。|synonyms: surge, influx, flood, rush|antonyms: outflow, efflux, trickle|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From in- + rush.  The prefix "in-" indicates direction or movement into something. "Rush" refers to a rapid, powerful movement.
