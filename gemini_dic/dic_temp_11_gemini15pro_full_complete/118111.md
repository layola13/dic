mennonites:/ˈmɛnəˌnaɪts/ | n. |门诺派教徒| Members of a Protestant sect originating in the 16th century from the Anabaptist movement. They are known for their pacifism, simple living, and emphasis on community. |  The Mennonites in our town are known for their beautiful quilts. 我们镇上的门诺派教徒以他们漂亮的被子而闻名。|  The Mennonites hold a church service every Sunday. 门诺派教徒每个星期天都举行教堂礼拜。|Synonyms: Amish, Brethren | Antonyms: None (It's difficult to have a direct antonym for a religious group). | Noun

Etymology: Named after Menno Simons (1496-1561), a Dutch priest who became a leader of the Anabaptist movement.
