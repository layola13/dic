landsturm:/ˈlɑːndʃtʊrm/| n. |地方守备队，国民自卫队|The last reserve of the German army, consisting of men between the ages of 17 and 45 not already in the army or its reserves.|During World War I, the Landsturm was called upon to defend German territories.|Synonym: militia, home guard|Antonym: regular army|noun
landsturm:/ˈlɑːndʃtʊrm/| n. |地方守备队，国民自卫队|A local defense force, typically made up of older reservists or civilians, called upon in times of emergency.|The Swiss Landsturm played a crucial role in defending the country's neutrality during World War II.|Synonym: reserve force, territorial army|Antonym: standing army|noun
