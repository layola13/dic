harebells:/ˈhɛərˌbɛlz/ | n. | 野风信子；蓝铃花 | A plant with slender stems and bell-shaped blue flowers, typically found in meadows and woodlands.  A common wildflower in many parts of the northern hemisphere. | Example 1: The harebells swayed gently in the breeze. 野风信子在微风中轻轻摇曳。

Example 2: A field of harebells carpeted the hillside. 一片野风信子覆盖了山坡。 | Near synonyms: bluebells, campanula | Antonyms: N/A | Noun
