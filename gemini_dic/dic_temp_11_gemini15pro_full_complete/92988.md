/ˈɪdiəm/ | n. | 成语，习语 | A group of words established by usage as having a meaning not deducible from those of the individual words (e.g., over the moon, see eye to eye). | Example: "Over the moon" is an idiom that means extremely happy.  “欣喜若狂”是一个习语，意思是极其高兴。 | Synonym: expression, phrase, saying | Antonym: literal (language) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Late 16th century: from French idiome, or via modern Latin from Greek idiōma ‘peculiarity’, from idios ‘own, private’.
