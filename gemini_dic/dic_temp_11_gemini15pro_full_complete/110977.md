liquidation:/ˌlɪkwɪˈdeɪʃn/ | n. | 清算；清偿；清除；（尤指生意的）停业 |The act of selling assets to pay debts.  The process of bringing a business to an end and distributing its assets to claimants. The killing of someone, typically by violent means. | The company went into liquidation after failing to secure new funding.  这家公司在未能获得新资金后进入清算阶段。

The liquidation of the company's assets took several months.  公司资产的清算花了几个月的时间。

The gang leader ordered the liquidation of his rivals.  该帮派头目下令清除他的对手。 | 近义词：dissolution, winding-up, clearance, elimination | 反义词：establishment, founding | 词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *liquidationem, noun of action from past-participle stem of liquidare (“to make liquid, clarify, pay off”), from Latin liquidus (“liquid, clear, evident”).
