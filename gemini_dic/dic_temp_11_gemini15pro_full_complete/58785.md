dunny:/ˈdʌni/
noun
厕所，茅坑
A toilet, especially an outhouse.
He went out back to the dunny. 他去后面的茅坑了。
近义词:toilet, lavatory, restroom, outhouse, bathroom
反义词:(None -  lacks a direct opposite)
词性:n.

dunny:/ˈdʌni/
noun
厕所，茅坑 (Australian and New Zealand slang)
A toilet, particularly one that is outside the main house.
The dunny was a bit smelly.  那个茅坑有点臭。
近义词:toilet, lavatory, restroom, outhouse, bathroom
反义词:(None - lacks a direct opposite)
词性:n.

词源:  The word "dunny" is thought to derive from the British dialect word "dunnekin," meaning a dung heap or privy. This evolved into "dunnyken" and eventually shortened to "dunny." It gained popularity in Australia and New Zealand in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
