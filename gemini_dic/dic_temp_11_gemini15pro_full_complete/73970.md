fortin:/ˈfɔːrtɪn/ |n.|小堡垒，小型要塞|A small fort.  A fortified place, especially a small fort.  Often refers to a fortified trading post, particularly in the context of the fur trade in North America.|The Hudson's Bay Company established a fortin along the river.  哈德逊湾公司沿着河边建立了一个小堡垒。|Synonyms: outpost, stronghold, fortification|Antonyms: vulnerability, weakness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fortin*, diminutive of *fort* (fort), from Old French *fort*, from Latin *fortis* (strong).
