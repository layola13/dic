overcomplacence:/ˌoʊvərˈkəmˈpleɪsəns/| n. |过度自满，沾沾自喜|A state of smug satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements, often to the point of failing to recognize potential danger or defect.|The team's overcomplacence after their initial victory led to their downfall in the final. 该队在最初的胜利后沾沾自喜，导致了他们在决赛中的失败。|近义词：smugness, complacency, self-satisfaction|反义词：anxiety, humility, insecurity|词性:名词

Etymology:
From over- + complacence.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much". "Complacence" comes from the Late Latin complacentia, from complacere "to be very pleasing," from com- "with" + placere "to please."
