haggishly:/ˈhæɡɪʃli/| adv.|像老巫婆一样地；丑陋地；令人讨厌地|In a way that resembles a hag; in an unpleasant or ugly manner.  Resembling or characteristic of a hag, especially in being ugly, bad-tempered, or cruel.|She stared haggishly at the children playing in her yard. 她像老巫婆一样盯着在她院子里玩耍的孩子们。|Synonyms: shrewishly, vixenishly,  maliciously, spitefully|Antonyms: kindly, pleasantly, graciously|词性:副词

haggish:/ˈhæɡɪʃ/| adj.|像老巫婆一样的；丑陋的；令人讨厌的|Resembling or characteristic of a hag, especially in being ugly, bad-tempered, or cruel.|She had a haggish appearance with a hooked nose and warts. 她有着老巫婆般的外表，鹰钩鼻和满脸的疣。|Synonyms: witchlike, cronelike, unpleasant, repulsive|Antonyms: beautiful, attractive, pleasant|词性:形容词

hag:/hæɡ/| n.|老巫婆；丑老太婆|An ugly old woman, especially one considered to be a witch.|The children were frightened by the story of the old hag who lived in the woods. 孩子们被住在森林里的老巫婆的故事吓坏了。|Synonyms: witch, crone, harridan|Antonyms: beauty, maiden|词性:名词
