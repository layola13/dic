humbler:/ˈhʌmblər/ (US), /ˈhʌmblə(r)/ (UK)| adj.| 更谦逊的，更卑微的 |  Comparative form of humble: more humble.  Indicates a greater degree of humility or lower status. |  He became a humbler man after the experience.  他经历了这件事后变得更加谦逊了。|  The new employee was humbler than the CEO. 新员工比CEO更谦逊。 |  meeker, more modest, less proud | prouder, more arrogant | adjective

humble:/ˈhʌmbl/ (US), /ˈhʌmbl/ (UK)| adj.  v.| 谦逊的，谦卑的；卑微的；使谦卑，使卑下 |  Having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance; of low social, administrative, or political rank. As a verb, it means to lower in dignity or importance. | He is a humble man, despite his great achievements. 尽管他取得了巨大的成就，他仍然是一个谦虚的人。 |  She humbled herself before the queen.  她在女王面前自贬身份。| modest, unassuming, meek | arrogant, proud | adjective, verb
