gothicism:/ˈɡɒθɪsɪzəm/ (US) /ˈɡɑːθɪsɪzəm/ (UK)| n.|哥特式风格，哥特式特征|The qualities or characteristics associated with the Gothic style, particularly in literature and architecture, such as darkness, mystery, and the supernatural.|The film's use of gothicism created a suspenseful and eerie atmosphere.  这部电影对哥特式风格的运用营造了一种悬疑而怪诞的氛围。|近义词：Gothic style, Gothic elements|反义词：Classical style, Enlightenment ideals|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Gothic + -ism.  "Gothic" originally referred to the Goths, an East Germanic people.  During the Renaissance, the term "Gothic" became associated with the medieval architectural style that was viewed as barbaric and crude by Renaissance thinkers.  Later, "Gothic" expanded to encompass literature and art that evoked similar feelings of darkness, mystery, and the supernatural.
