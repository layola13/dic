interpenetration:/ˌɪntərˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən/| n. |相互渗透，互相 проникновение|The interpenetration of the two cultures enriched both societies. 两种文化的相互渗透丰富了这两个社会。|例句：The interpenetration of light and shadow created a mesmerizing effect. 光影交错创造了一种迷人的效果。  The interpenetration of political and economic power is a complex issue. 政治权力和经济权力的相互渗透是一个复杂的问题。|近义词：intermingling, fusion, blending, integration|反义词:separation, division, segregation|词性:noun

interpenetrate:/ˌɪntərˈpɛnɪtreɪt/| v. |互相渗透，互相 проникновение|The two religions interpenetrated each other, sharing beliefs and practices.  这两种宗教相互渗透，共享信仰和习俗。|例句：The roots of the trees interpenetrated the soil. 树根相互渗透在土壤中。 The different musical styles interpenetrated to create a unique sound. 不同的音乐风格相互渗透，创造出独特的声音。|近义词：intermingle, fuse, blend, integrate|反义词:separate, divide, segregate|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *interpenetrationem,  from interpenetrat- ‘interpenetrated’, from the verb interpenetrare, from inter- ‘between’ + penetrare ‘to penetrate’.
