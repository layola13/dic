chauffeur:/ʃoʊˈfɜːr (US), ʃəʊˈfɜː (UK)| n. v.|司机；驾驶；|A person employed to drive a private or rented automobile.  A person who chauffeurs someone.|例句：The chauffeur drove them to the airport. 司机开车送他们去了机场。

例句：He chauffeurs his boss around town. 他开车载着他的老板在城里转。|近义词：driver, operator|反义词:passenger|词性:名词, 动词

词源:
From French chauffeur "stoker, fireman," literally "heater," from chauffer "to heat," from Old French chauffer (see calefaction). Sense of "driver of an automobile" is from 1899, so called because early cars were steam-powered.
