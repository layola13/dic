interweavement:/ˌɪntərˈwiːvmənt/ | n. |交织，交错，交融|The interweavement of the plot lines kept the readers guessing. 情节线的交织让读者们不断猜测。|例句：The interweavement of light and shadow created a dramatic effect. 光影交错营造出戏剧性的效果。

例句：The interweavement of different cultures in the city makes it a vibrant place to live. 城市中不同文化的交融使它成为一个充满活力的地方。|近义词：intertwining, interlacing, blending, fusion|反义词：separation, division, disentanglement|词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From interweave + -ment.  Interweave comes from the Old English "interweafan,"  meaning "to weave together," with "inter-" signifying "between" or "among" and "weafan" meaning "to weave."  The suffix "-ment" forms nouns denoting an action, process, or result.
