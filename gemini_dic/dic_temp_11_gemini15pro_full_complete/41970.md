cosset: /ˈkɒsɪt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːsɪt/ (US)| n. v. |宠儿，宝贝；娇惯，溺爱|To treat someone as a cosset is to pamper or spoil them, often excessively.  As a noun, a cosset is a person who is treated this way.|She cossets her dog like a child. 她像对待孩子一样溺爱她的狗。|近义词：pamper, indulge, spoil, coddle |反义词: neglect, ignore |词性: noun, verb
Etymology: From Middle English *cosse, probably from a Scandinavian source akin to Old Norse *kot-siðr "bedfellow," from kot "cottage" + siðr "custom, habit." Originally referred to a lamb brought up in the house.
