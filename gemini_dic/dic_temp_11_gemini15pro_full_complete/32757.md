cho:/tʃoʊ/ (US) /tʃəʊ/ (UK)| determiner, pronoun| 这，那 (用于韩语，指代事物，相当于英文的 "this" 或 "that") | Used in Korean to refer to things, similar to "this" or "that" in English.  It can be used before nouns or on its own. |例句：Cho chaek eun nae geoya. = This book is mine.  这本书是我的。|例句：Choge mwoyeyo? = What is that? 那是什么？ | 近义词：(In Korean) igeo (this), jeogeo (that) | 反义词: N/A (Determiners don't typically have antonyms) | 词性: Determiner, Pronoun

疑问句:
Choge mwoyeyo? = What is that? 那是什么？
Igeo cho yeppuji 않아요? = Isn't this pretty? 这个不是很漂亮吗?

否定句:
Cho anieyo. = That's not it. / That isn't it.  那不是。
Cho geos-eun nae geos-i anieyo. = That thing isn't mine. 那东西不是我的。

注意事项:

"cho" is primarily used in spoken Korean.  The more formal written equivalent is "jeo (저)".


While some tense examples are given, it's important to understand that Korean tenses don't map directly onto English tenses.  The examples provided show how the meaning might be conveyed, but the grammatical structure is often different.


Korean relies heavily on context and particles.  The meaning of "cho" can shift slightly depending on the situation.
