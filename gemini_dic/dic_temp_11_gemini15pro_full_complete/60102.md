eerily:/ˈɪərɪli/| adv.|怪异地；令人毛骨悚然地；可怕地|In a strange and frightening manner, suggesting the presence of ghosts or supernatural forces.|The wind howled eerily through the empty streets. 风怪异地呼啸着穿过空荡荡的街道。|Synonyms: spookily, creepily, uncannily, weirdly|Antonyms: normally, ordinarily, naturally|adverb

Etymology:
From eerie + -ly.  "Eerie" comes from the Middle English *eri*, meaning "fearful, timid," likely influenced by the Old English *earg*, meaning "cowardly."
