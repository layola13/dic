laissez-faireisms:/ˌlɛseˈfɛrɪz(ə)mz/ | n. | 自由放任主义（尤指政府对经济事务不加干预的政策）|  The doctrine or practice of laissez-faire, especially government noninterference in economic affairs. It often implies an extreme or doctrinaire adherence to such a policy. |  The candidate's speech was full of laissez-faireisms, promising minimal government regulation and intervention.  这位候选人的演讲充满了自由放任主义，承诺最小限度的政府监管和干预。 |  The economist warned against the dangers of unchecked laissez-faireisms in developing economies.  这位经济学家警告说，发展中经济体不加控制的自由放任主义是危险的。 |Synonyms:  free-market principles, deregulation, noninterventionism | Antonyms:  regulation, interventionism, state control | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *laissez faire* ("let do"), imperative of *laisser* ("to let, to leave") + *faire* ("to do").  The "-ism" suffix denotes a doctrine, belief system, or practice.
