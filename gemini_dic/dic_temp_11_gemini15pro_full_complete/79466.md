gloar: /ɡlɔːr/
Noun|凝视，注视|A fixed look, stare.|He gave her a gloar across the room. 他在房间的另一头狠狠地瞪了她一眼。|近义词: glare, stare, scowl|反义词: glance, glimpse|词性:n.

Etymology: Likely a variant or alteration of "glare," influenced by words like "gloom" and "lower."  The connection to "glare" is evident in the shared sense of a fixed, intense look, often with negative connotations.  The influence of words like "gloom" and "lower" may contribute to the sense of menace or disapproval often associated with a gloar.
