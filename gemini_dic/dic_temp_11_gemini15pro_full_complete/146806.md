overimitations:/ˌoʊvərɪmɪˈteɪʃənz/| n.|过度模仿|The act of copying every detail of a demonstrated action, even those that are irrelevant to achieving the intended outcome.|Young children often engage in overimitation, meticulously copying every step of an adult's actions, even if some steps are clearly unnecessary.|近义词：mimicry, emulation, copying|反义词: innovation, originality|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
"Over-" (prefix meaning "excessive" or "beyond") + "imitation" (from Latin "imitari," meaning "to copy, imitate").
