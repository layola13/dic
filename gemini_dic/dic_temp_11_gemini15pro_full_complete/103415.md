jitterbugged:/ˈdʒɪtərbʌɡd/ | v. | 跳吉特巴舞 | danced the jitterbug; acted nervously or excitedly. |  They jitterbugged the night away. 他们整晚都在跳吉特巴舞。| Synonyms: danced, pranced, bounced | Antonyms: stood still, sat, rested | Verb

jitterbugged:/ˈdʒɪtərbʌɡd/ | adj. |  紧张不安的，兴奋的 |  Describing someone acting nervously or excitedly, like the fast movements of the jitterbug dance. | He was jitterbugged before the big presentation.  他在重要的演讲前非常紧张。| Synonyms: nervous, agitated, fidgety | Antonyms: calm, relaxed, composed | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From jitterbug (n.) + -ed (suffix forming the past tense and past participle of verbs).  "Jitterbug" itself likely originated in the 1930s, combining "jitters" (nervousness) with "bug" (an enthusiasm or obsession).
