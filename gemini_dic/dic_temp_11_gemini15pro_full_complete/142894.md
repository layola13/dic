out-of-turn:/ˌaʊt əv ˈtɜːrn/ | adj. | 不合时宜的；越俎代庖的；不按顺序的 | Done or said at a time that is not appropriate or expected, interrupting the usual order. |  He made an out-of-turn remark that offended the host. 他说了一句不合时宜的话，冒犯了主人。| synonyms: inappropriate, untimely, ill-timed, premature, unsuitable, awkward | antonyms: appropriate, timely, suitable, opportune, proper | adjective

out-of-turn:/ˌaʊt əv ˈtɜːrn/ | adv. | 不合时宜地；越俎代庖地；不按顺序地 | In a way that is not appropriate or expected, interrupting the usual order. | She spoke out-of-turn and revealed a secret.  她不合时宜地说话，泄露了一个秘密。| synonyms: inappropriately, untimely, prematurely | antonyms: appropriately, timely, opportunely | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
"Out" (from Old English "ūt") meaning outside, beyond, or away from.  "Turn" (from Old English "turnian") meaning to rotate, revolve, or change direction. The combined phrase "out-of-turn" signifies something happening outside of its designated or expected sequence or time.
