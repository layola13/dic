amphibiology:/æmˈfɪbiˈɒlədʒi/
n.|两栖动物学|The study of amphibians, a class of vertebrate animals that live both in water and on land, including frogs, toads, salamanders, and newts.|Amphibiology is a fascinating field, encompassing the study of creatures adapted to both aquatic and terrestrial environments.|近义词:herpetology (partly)|反义词:---|词性:名词

amphibian:/æmˈfɪbiən/
n.|两栖动物|A cold-blooded vertebrate animal of a class that comprises the frogs, toads, newts, salamanders, and caecilians. They are distinguished by having an aquatic gill-breathing larval stage followed (typically) by a terrestrial lung-breathing adult stage.|The frog is a common amphibian.|近义词:frog, toad, newt, salamander|反义词:---|词性:名词

amphibian:/æmˈfɪbiən/
adj.|两栖的|Relating to or characteristic of amphibians; able to live both on land and in water.|Amphibian vehicles are designed for both land and water travel.|近义词:aquatic, terrestrial|反义词:---|词性:形容词
