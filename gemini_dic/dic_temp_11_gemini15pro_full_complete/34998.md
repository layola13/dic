cloister:/ˈklɔɪstər/ (US), /ˈklɔɪstə(r)/ (UK)| n. |修道院,迴廊；隔离,隐居|A covered walkway, especially in a religious institution, or a place of religious seclusion.|The monks walked slowly around the cloister. 修道士们绕着修道院慢慢地走。|Synonyms: monastery, abbey, convent, sanctuary|Antonyms: city, public, open|noun


cloister:/ˈklɔɪstər/ (US), /ˈklɔɪstə(r)/ (UK)| vt. |使隔离,使隐居|To seclude or confine, as in a monastery.|She cloistered herself in her study for the entire weekend. 她整个周末都把自己关在书房里。|Synonyms: seclude, isolate, confine, shut away|Antonyms: expose, reveal, open|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *cloistre*, from Latin *claustrum* ("lock, bolt, bar, barrier"), from *claudere* ("to close, shut").
