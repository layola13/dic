elocutionary: /ˌɛləˈkjuːʃəneri/ | adj. |雄辩的；演说的；表达清晰的 | Relating to the way in which something is said, and the effect it has on the listener, rather than the literal meaning of the words.  It focuses on the speaker's intention and the act of speaking itself.  |  Her elocutionary force was undeniable; she captivated the audience with her speech. 她雄辩的力量不可否认；她的演讲吸引了所有的观众。 |  articulate, expressive, fluent | inarticulate, unclear, mumbled | adjective

elocution: /ˌɛləˈkjuːʃən/ | n. | 演说术；演讲风格；口才 | The art of clear and expressive speech, especially of distinct pronunciation and articulation.  | Her elocution was impeccable, every word delivered with precision and clarity. 她的演讲技巧无可挑剔，每个字都清晰而准确。 |  pronunciation, articulation, delivery | stammering, mumbling, inarticulation | noun
