blaming:/ˈbleɪmɪŋ/| v.|责备，指责|The act of assigning responsibility for a fault or wrong.|He kept blaming me for the accident, even though it wasn't my fault. 他一直把事故的责任归咎于我，即使那不是我的错。|近义词：accusing, reproaching, criticizing, condemning|反义词：praising, commending, forgiving, excusing|verb (present participle)


blaming:/ˈbleɪmɪŋ/| adj.|责备的，归咎的|Used to describe something or someone that assigns responsibility for a fault or wrong.|His blaming tone made me feel uncomfortable. 他责备的语气让我感到不舒服。|近义词：accusatory, reproachful|反义词：complimentary, laudatory|adjective


词源: From the Middle English verb *blamen*, derived from Old French *blasmer*, ultimately from Late Latin *blasphemare* "to blaspheme," which later broadened in meaning to "to blame."
