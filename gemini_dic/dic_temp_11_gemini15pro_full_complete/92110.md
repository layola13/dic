hyperromanticisms:/ˌhaɪpərroʊˈmæntɪsɪzəmz/ | n. |过度浪漫主义，极端浪漫主义 |  An excessive or exaggerated expression of romantic ideals, often characterized by sentimentality, idealism, and a focus on passionate love and emotional intensity. | Her poetry was full of hyperromanticisms, with every line proclaiming undying love and eternal devotion. 她的诗充满了极端浪漫主义，每一行都宣告着永恒的爱和无限的奉献。|  Exaggerated romanticism, excessive sentimentality | Realism, cynicism | noun

Etymology:
From hyper- (prefix meaning "excessive" or "beyond") + romanticism.  "Hyper-" comes from the Greek "hyper" (ὑπέρ) meaning "over," "above," or "beyond." "Romanticism" comes from the Old French "romaunce" (a romantic tale) and ultimately from the Latin "Romanus" (Roman).
