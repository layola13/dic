chide:/tʃaɪd/| v. |责骂，斥责|To scold or rebuke someone gently or mildly.  To express disapproval.|例句：She chided him for his carelessness. 她责备他粗心大意。|例句：The teacher chided the student for being late. 老师斥责学生迟到。|近义词：rebuke, scold, reprimand, reproach|反义词:praise, commend|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cīdan, from Proto-Germanic *kīdaną (“to complain, accuse, lament”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰeyd- (“to call, proclaim, incite”). Cognate with Old Frisian chīda (“to quarrel, chide”), Old Saxon kīdan (“to complain, lament”), Old High German kīdan (“to complain, lament”), Old Norse kveða (“to say, speak, tell”), Gothic 𐌵𐌹𐌸𐌰𐌽 (qiþan, “to say”).
