overconservatively: /ˌoʊvər kənˈsɜːrvə tɪvli/ | adv. | 过于保守地 |  In a way that is excessively cautious and avoids risks or change. |  The company invested overconservatively, missing out on several lucrative opportunities.  公司投资过于保守，错过了几个赚钱的机会。 | Example: He dressed overconservatively for the party, wearing a suit and tie when everyone else was in casual attire. 他穿着过于保守地参加了聚会，穿着西装打领带，而其他人则穿着便装。| Synonyms: cautiously, timidly, prudently (to an excessive degree) | Antonyms:  boldly, aggressively, rashly | Part of Speech: Adverb

From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "conservatively" (adverb form of "conservative," from Latin "conservare" - to preserve).
