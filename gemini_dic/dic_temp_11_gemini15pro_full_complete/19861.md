bine:/baɪn/ | n. | 藤蔓；蛇管；啤酒花 | A climbing plant, especially a hop vine. Also, a flexible tube, often used for conveying liquids. | The hops bine was heavy with flowers.  啤酒花藤蔓上开满了花。| synonyms: creeper, tendril, liana, hose, tube | antonyms: N/A | noun

bine:/baɪn/ | v. | 用藤蔓捆绑；缠绕 | To bind or fasten with bines. | They bined the branches together to make a shelter. 他们用藤蔓把树枝绑在一起做了一个棚子。| synonyms: bind, tie, fasten | antonyms: untie, loosen, unbind | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bīgan, from Proto-Germanic *bīną (“to bend, curve”). Cognate with Dutch binden (“to bind”), German biegen (“to bend”), Old Norse beygja (“to bend”). The meaning “hop vine” arose from the plant's climbing habit.
