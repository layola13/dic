emulsifiability's:/ɪˌmʌlsɪˌfaɪəˈbɪlɪtiz/| n. |乳化性，乳化能力|The emulsifiability of a substance refers to its ability to form or be formed into an emulsion.  一种物质的乳化性是指它形成或被形成为乳液的能力。|The emulsifiability's of this new oil is excellent. 这种新油的乳化性非常好。|近义词：emulsibility|反义词:|noun

emulsify:/ɪˈmʌlsɪfaɪ/| v. |乳化|To combine two immiscible liquids, like oil and water, into a stable mixture, often with the aid of an emulsifying agent. 将两种不混溶的液体，如油和水，结合成稳定的混合物，通常借助乳化剂。|The egg yolk emulsifies the oil and vinegar in the dressing. 蛋黄使调料中的油和醋乳化。|近义词：blend, mix|反义词:separate|verb

emulsifiable:/ɪˈmʌlsɪfaɪəbl/| adj. |可乳化的|Capable of being emulsified. 能够被乳化的。|This type of wax is emulsifiable in water. 这种蜡可以在水中乳化。|近义词：mixable, blendable|反义词:immiscible|adjective

词源:  From emulsifiable + -ity + -'s (possessive).  来自可乳化的 + -ity（名词后缀）+ -'s（所有格）。
