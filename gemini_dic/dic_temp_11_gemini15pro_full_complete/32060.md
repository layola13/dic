chersonese:/ˈkɜːrsəniːz/ /ˈkɜːrsəˌniːs/| n. |半岛，海角|A peninsula or headland, especially one extending into the sea. |The ancient Greeks established many colonies on the chersonese.|近义词：peninsula, headland, promontory|反义词:isthmus, strait, bay|名词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *chersonesus*, from Greek *χερσόνησος* (khersónēsos), from *χέρσος* (khérsos, “dry land”) + *νῆσος* (nêsos, “island”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰers- (“to dry up, become rough”).
