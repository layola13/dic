gates:/ɡeɪts/ | n. | 大门，出入口；关卡；闸门 | A barrier used to close an opening in a wall, fence, or hedge, typically consisting of a hinged frame of wood or metal. |  The gates of the castle were opened. 城堡的大门打开了。

gates:/ɡeɪts/ | n. |  (复数) 比喻义：入口，途径 |  The university is seen as the gates to a successful career. 大学被视为通往成功职业的途径。


gates:/ɡeɪts/ | n. | (复数) 比喻义：控制，权力 | The company controls the gates of the market. 这家公司控制着市场的入口。


Etymology: From Middle English *yates, *zates, from Old English geat (plural gatu), from Proto-Germanic *gatan, from Proto-Indo-European *ghedh- (“to join, enclose”). Cognate with West Frisian gat (“gate, hole”), Dutch gat (“hole, gap, opening”), German Gasse (“lane, alley”), Gothic 𐌲𐌰𐍄𐍅𐍉 (gatwo, “street”).


Synonyms: entrance, doorway, portal, barrier, opening, access

Antonyms: exit, closure, blockage

Word Type: Noun
