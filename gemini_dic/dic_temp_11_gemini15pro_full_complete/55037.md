disquieting:/dɪsˈkwaɪətɪŋ/
adjective
令人不安的；令人担忧的
Causing anxiety or uneasiness; disturbing.
The news from the hospital was disquieting.  医院传来的消息令人不安。
There was a disquieting silence after he spoke. 他说完话后，一阵令人不安的沉默。
近义词: disturbing, unsettling, troubling, worrisome, alarming
反义词: calming, soothing, reassuring, comforting, peaceful
词性: adjective

disquiet:/dɪsˈkwaɪət/
noun
不安；焦虑；担忧
A feeling of anxiety or worry.
The disquiet among the staff was palpable.  员工们心中的不安很明显。
Public disquiet about the government's policies is growing. 公众对政府政策的担忧日益加剧。
近义词: unease, anxiety, worry, concern, apprehension
反义词: calm, peace, tranquility, composure, serenity
词性: noun

disquiet:/dɪsˈkwaɪət/
verb
使不安；使担忧
Make (someone) worried or anxious.
The sudden change in plans disquieted her. 突然改变计划让她感到不安。
The economic downturn disquieted investors. 经济下滑使投资者感到担忧。
近义词: disturb, unsettle, trouble, worry, alarm
反义词: calm, soothe, reassure, comfort, pacify
词性: verb


词源:  From Old French *desquiete (“inquietude,” “unease”), from des- + quiet (“quiet”).
