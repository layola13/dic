foreprize:/fɔːrˈpraɪz/ | v. | 预先给予奖励; 预先酬赏; 预购 | To seize beforehand; to intercept. To anticipate or forestall. To buy up provisions or goods before they reach the market, especially with the intent to resell at a higher price. |  He foreprized the goods before they reached the market. 他在货物上市前就预购了。| Synonyms: anticipate, forestall, intercept, monopolize | Antonyms: relinquish, forfeit | verb

foreprize:/fɔːrˈpraɪz/ | n. | 预购; 垄断 | The act of foreprizing; the act of seizing or intercepting beforehand.  | The foreprize of the grain supply caused a shortage in the city.  粮食供应的预购导致了城市短缺。| Synonyms: monopoly, cornering the market | Antonyms:  release, distribution | noun
