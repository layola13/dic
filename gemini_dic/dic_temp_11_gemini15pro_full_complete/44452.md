cross-subsidy:/ˈkrɒs ˈsʌbsɪdi/ /ˈkrɔːs ˈsʌbsədi/ | n. | 交叉补贴 | The practice of charging higher prices for one product or service in order to subsidize the cost of another, often making the latter available at a lower price or even free. |  The postal service uses cross-subsidies, charging more for express delivery to subsidize the cost of standard mail. 邮政服务使用交叉补贴，对快递收费更高，以补贴标准邮件的成本。

The company uses cross-subsidies between its profitable products and its less successful ones to keep the latter afloat.  公司在其盈利产品和不太成功的产品之间进行交叉补贴，以维持后者的运营。 | 近义词：internal subsidy, price discrimination | 反义词:  N/A | 词性: noun


cross-subsidize:/ˌkrɒs ˈsʌbsɪdaɪz/ /ˌkrɔːs ˈsʌbsəˌdaɪz/ | v. | 交叉补贴 | To finance (one activity) by diverting funds from another. |  The city council decided to cross-subsidize public transportation by increasing parking fees. 市议会决定通过提高停车费来交叉补贴公共交通。

The company cross-subsidizes its loss-making products with the profits from its more popular items. 公司用其更受欢迎产品的利润来交叉补贴亏损产品。| 近义词：underwrite, support, finance | 反义词:  N/A | 词性: verb


词源: "cross-" + "subsidy". "Cross-" means across or between, indicating the transfer of funds between different products or services. "Subsidy" comes from the Latin "subsidium", meaning "assistance" or "support".
