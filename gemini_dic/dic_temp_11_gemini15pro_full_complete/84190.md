half-reasonably:/ˌhɑːfˈriːzənəbli/ | adv. | 半合理地；还算合理地 |  Moderately reasonably; to a limited extent reasonably.  It implies that something is reasonable to a certain degree, but not entirely or fully so. | The film was half-reasonably entertaining, but I wouldn't rush to see it again. 这部电影还算有趣，但我不会急着再看一遍。| Near synonyms:  moderately, tolerably, fairly, somewhat reasonably | Antonyms:  unreasonably, irrationally, completely unreasonably | Part of Speech: Adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
"half-" (Old English healf) + "-reasonably" (from "reasonable" - Late Middle English: from Old French raisonable, from raison ‘reason’)
