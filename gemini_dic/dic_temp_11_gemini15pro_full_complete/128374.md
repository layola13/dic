neven:/ˈnɛvən/| adj.|从未; 并非|never; not ever|He had neven been to China before. 他以前从未去过中国。|近义词:never|反义词:always|词性:形容词
词源:From Middle English *neven, *nevere, from Old English næfre (“never”), from Proto-Germanic *nawiz (“never”), from Proto-Indo-European *ne (“not”) + *h₂eiw- (“vital force, eternity”). Cognate with Scots nevir (“never”), Saterland Frisian nai (“never”), West Frisian nea (“never”), Dutch nooit (“never”), German nie (“never”), Danish, Norwegian and Swedish aldrig (“never”), Icelandic aldrei (“never”).

PHRASE->

*Neven* doesn't really form phrases in the same way "what" does. It acts primarily as an adverb.  Since *neven* is archaic and rarely used, it's better to use *never*. It's simpler and more natural in modern English.
