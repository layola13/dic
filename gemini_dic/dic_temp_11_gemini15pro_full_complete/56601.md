donatistic:/ˌdoʊnəˈtɪstɪk/
adjective
多纳图斯的；多纳图斯派的
Of or relating to Donatism, a schismatic North African Christian sect of the 4th and 5th centuries that held that the validity of the sacraments depended on the moral character of the minister.
The Donatist controversy was a major schism in the early Church. 多纳图斯之争是早期教会的一次重大分裂。
The Donatist view on the sacraments was rejected by the mainstream Church. 主流教会拒绝了多纳图斯派关于圣礼的观点。
Synonyms: schismatic, heretical, sectarian
Antonyms: orthodox, catholic
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Donatus, the name of the bishop who led the schism.  Ultimately from Latin *donatus* "given", past participle of *donare* "to give".
