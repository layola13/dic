misqualifying:/ˌmɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪɪŋ/
adjective
使失去资格的；取消资格的
Causing someone or something to no longer meet the requirements or be eligible for something.
His use of performance-enhancing drugs was a misqualifying offense. 他的使用兴奋剂的行为导致他失去了参赛资格。
The athlete's positive drug test was misqualifying.  这名运动员的药检呈阳性，导致他失去了参赛资格。
近义词:disqualifying, invalidating
反义词:qualifying, enabling
词性:adjective

misqualify:/ˌmɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪ/
verb
使失去资格；取消…的资格
To cause someone or something to no longer meet the requirements or be eligible for something.
The judge misqualified the boxer for hitting below the belt.  裁判因拳击手击打腰带以下部位而取消了他的比赛资格。
One mistake can misqualify you from the competition. 一个错误就可能让你失去参赛资格。
近义词:disqualify, bar, exclude
反义词:qualify, admit, include
词性:verb


词源:
From mis- (meaning "wrongly" or "badly") + qualify.  由mis-（表示“错误地”或“糟糕地”）+ qualify组成。
