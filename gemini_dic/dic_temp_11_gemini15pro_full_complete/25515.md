bullishness:/ˈbʊlɪʃnəs/| n. |看涨；乐观；牛市|The state of being bullish; optimistic about the future, especially in the financial markets; characterized by rising prices.|
例句：There is a general bullishness about the company's future prospects. 大家普遍看好这家公司未来的发展前景。

例句：Investor bullishness drove the stock market to record highs. 投资者的乐观情绪将股市推至历史新高。

近义词：optimism, confidence, buoyancy|
反义词：bearishness, pessimism, negativity|
词性:noun


bullish:/ˈbʊlɪʃ/| adj. |看涨的；乐观的；牛市的|Optimistic about the future, especially in the financial markets; characterized by rising prices.  Also, resembling a bull in strength and stubbornness.|
例句：Investors are bullish on the stock market. 投资者看好股市。

例句：He has a bullish personality, always charging ahead with his ideas. 他性格乐观，总是充满干劲地推进自己的想法。

近义词：optimistic, confident, positive|
反义词：bearish, pessimistic, negative|
词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From bull (n.) + -ish (suffix).  The term originally referred to the aggressive upward attack of a bull, and this imagery was later applied to the stock market.
