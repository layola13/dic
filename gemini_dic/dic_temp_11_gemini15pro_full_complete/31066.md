chamlet:/ˈtʃæmlɪt/| n.|小房间，小室；茅屋；陋室|A small, mean, or humble dwelling; a hut or cottage. |He lived in a chamlet on the edge of the forest. 他住在森林边缘的一个小茅屋里。|Synonyms: hovel, shack, shanty, cabin|Antonyms: mansion, palace|noun

Etymology:  From Old French *chamelette, diminutive of chamel, of uncertain origin, perhaps from Late Latin *calamellus, diminutive of calamus "reed," referring to a hut made of reeds.
