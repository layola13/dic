affricating:/ˈæfrɪkeɪtɪŋ/| adj. | 摩擦的，塞擦音化的 | The process of changing a sound, typically a stop consonant, into an affricate. This involves adding a fricative element to the sound. |  The sound change in some dialects where "t" before "yod" becomes "ch" is an example of affricating.  一些方言中“t”在“yod”之前变成“ch”的声音变化是塞擦音化的一个例子。| Near Synonyms:  fricativizing, spirantizing | Antonyms:  leniting, weakening | adjective

affricate:/ˈæfrɪkət/| n. | 塞擦音 | A consonant sound that begins as a stop and releases as a fricative, such as the "ch" in "chair" or the "j" in "judge". | The "ch" sound is a common affricate in English. “ch”音是英语中常见的塞擦音。| Near Synonyms:  affricated consonant | Antonyms:  pure stop, pure fricative | noun
