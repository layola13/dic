overrationalizations: /ˌoʊvərˌræʃənələˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. | 过度合理化；过度解释 |  The act of creating excessive or elaborate rationalizations, often to justify actions or beliefs that are not entirely logical or justifiable.  This often involves inventing complex or convoluted explanations to avoid facing uncomfortable truths or admitting fault. | He engaged in a series of overrationalizations to explain why he missed the deadline, blaming everything but his own procrastination. 他进行了一系列过度合理化来解释为什么他错过了截止日期，把一切都归咎于除了他自己的拖延症之外的原因。 |  Overexplanations, justifications, excuses | Understatements, admissions of guilt | noun
