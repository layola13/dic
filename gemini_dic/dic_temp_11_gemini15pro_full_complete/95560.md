incognizant:/ɪnˈkɒɡnɪzənt/ /ɪnˈkɑːɡnɪzənt/| adj.|未察觉的，不知道的，未意识到的|Not aware of something; lacking knowledge or consciousness of something.|例句：He was completely incognizant of the danger he was in. 他完全没有意识到自己身处险境。|例句：She seemed incognizant of the fact that she was interrupting. 她似乎没有意识到她在打断别人。|近义词：unaware, oblivious, ignorant, unconscious|反义词：aware, conscious, mindful|词性:形容词

incognizance:/ɪnˈkɒɡnɪzəns/ /ɪnˈkɑːɡnɪzəns/| n.|未察觉，不知情，无意识|Lack of awareness, knowledge, or consciousness.|例句：Her incognizance of the situation was surprising. 她对情况的不知情令人惊讶。|例句：The judge cited the defendant's incognizance of the law as a mitigating factor. 法官引用被告对法律的不知情作为减轻处罚的因素。|近义词：unawareness, obliviousness, ignorance|反义词：awareness, consciousness, mindfulness|词性:名词

词源:  From Late Latin *incognizant-, from Latin in- ‘not’ + cognoscent- ‘knowing,’ present participle of cognoscere ‘get to know.’
