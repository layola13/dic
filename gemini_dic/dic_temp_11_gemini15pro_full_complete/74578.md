fray:/freɪ/
n.|争吵，磨损，(战斗的)开始|A noisy quarrel or fight.  The edge of the cloth is starting to fray. The fray began at dawn.|例句：The debate turned into a nasty fray. 辩论变成了一场激烈的争吵。

例句：The hem of her dress was starting to fray. 她连衣裙的褶边开始磨损了。|近义词：fight, brawl, scuffle, clash, unravel, wear|反义词：peace, harmony, mend, repair|词性:名词

fray:/freɪ/
v.|磨损，使烦躁，(神经等)紧张|To wear away or unravel the edges of cloth, rope, etc. To strain or irritate (nerves, temper, etc.)|例句：The constant stress began to fray his nerves.  持续的压力开始让他神经紧张。

例句：The ropes had frayed where they rubbed against the rock. 绳子在与岩石摩擦的地方磨损了。|近义词：unravel, wear, erode, chafe, irritate|反义词：mend, repair, soothe, calm|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *freien, from Old French *freier, *froier (“to rub, fray”), from Vulgar Latin *fricare, from Latin fricare (“to rub”).  Cognate with French frayer (“to rub, fray”), Italian fregare ("to rub, wear"). The noun is derived from the verb.
