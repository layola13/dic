outcast:/ˈaʊtkæst/| n. adj.|被驱逐者，被遗弃者；被抛弃的，被排斥的|A person who has been rejected by society or a particular group.  Someone or something that is rejected or excluded.|例句：He lived as an outcast from society. 他像个被社会遗弃的人一样活着。|例句：She felt like an outcast in her new school.  她在新学校感觉自己像个局外人。|近义词：pariah, exile, reject, outsider|反义词: insider, accepted, included|词性:名词、形容词
outcurse:/ˌaʊtˈkɜːrs/| vt.| 诅咒，咒骂|To curse vehemently; to utter curses against.|例句：He outcursed the gods for his misfortune. 他咒骂众神给他带来的厄运。|例句：She outcursed her rival in a fit of rage.  她盛怒之下咒骂她的对手。|近义词：curse, damn, execrate|反义词: bless, praise|词性:动词
outcurst:/ˌaʊtˈkɜːrst/| adj.| 被诅咒的|Under a curse; accursed.|例句：The outcurst land yielded no crops. 这片被诅咒的土地颗粒无收。|例句：He wandered as an outcurst soul.  他像一个被诅咒的灵魂一样四处游荡。|近义词：cursed, damned, accursed|反义词: blessed, sacred|词性:形容词
