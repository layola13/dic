nomads:/ˈnoʊmædz/ /ˈnɒmædz/| n.|游牧民；流浪者|People who travel from place to place to find fresh pasture for their animals, or who move from place to place in search of food, work, etc.|The nomads travelled across the desert seeking water. 这些游牧民穿越沙漠寻找水源。|
Synonyms: wanderers, rovers, migrants, travelers
|Antonyms: settlers, residents|noun

nomadic:/noʊˈmædɪk/ /nəˈmædɪk/| adj.|游牧的；流浪的|Relating to or characteristic of nomads.|They led a nomadic life, moving from place to place. 他们过着游牧生活，居无定所。|Synonyms: wandering, roving, migratory, itinerant|Antonyms: settled, sedentary, stationary|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *nomade*, from Latin nomas (genitive nomadis), from Ancient Greek νομάς (nomás, “roaming, wandering”), from νέμω (némō, “I distribute, pasture, graze”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *nem- (“to allot, to take”).
