legitimizer:/ləˈdʒɪtɪˌmaɪzər/ | n. |合法化者；使…合法的人（或物）|Someone or something that legitimizes something. |The election was seen as a legitimizer of the new government.  这次选举被视为新政府合法化的标志。| The UN resolution served as a legitimizer for the military intervention. 这项联合国决议成了军事干预的合法化依据。 |  近义词: validator, ratifier, authorizer | 反义词: invalidator, denier | 词性: noun

legitimate:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmət/ | adj. |合法的；正当的；合理的|lawful, rightful, reasonable| It's not legitimate to ask for that information.  要求提供该信息是不合法的。| He had a legitimate claim to the throne. 他对王位有合法的要求。|近义词: legal, valid, lawful | 反义词: illegal, illegitimate, invalid | 词性: adjective

legitimately:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmətli/ | adv. |合法地；正当地；合理地|in a legitimate manner| The business was legitimately run. 该企业是合法经营的。 | She is legitimately concerned about her safety. 她对自己的安全有合理的担忧。 | 近义词: legally, validly, lawfully | 反义词: illegally, illegitimately, invalidly | 词性: adverb

legitimize:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmaɪz/ | v. |使合法化；使正当化；合理化|to make something legal or acceptable|The government has legitimized the use of force. 政府已经将使用武力合法化。 | They tried to legitimize their actions by claiming they were following orders. 他们试图通过声称自己是在服从命令来使自己的行为合法化。 | 近义词: validate, ratify, authorize | 反义词: invalidate, condemn, denounce | 词性: verb
