词源：From Middle English *narow, from 古英语 nearu (“narrow, constricted, close; straightened, severe; small, scanty, few; oppressive, grievous”), from Proto-Germanic *narwaz (“narrow, close, pressed”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰnerH- (“to draw together, to tie, to knot”). Cognate with Scots narro (“narrow”), West Frisian nau (“narrow”), Dutch nauw (“narrow”), German eng (“narrow, tight”), Danish and Norwegian nær (“near, close”), Swedish nära (“near, close”), Icelandic nær (“near”).
