non-testable:/ˌnɒnˈtestəbl/ | adj. | 不可测试的 | Not able to be tested or assessed.  This often applies to theories or concepts that are difficult to quantify or measure empirically. | The philosopher's claims about the nature of reality were interesting but ultimately non-testable. 哲学家关于现实本质的主张很有趣，但最终是不可检验的。|  The effectiveness of the new security measures is non-testable until a real threat occurs. 新安全措施的有效性在真正的威胁出现之前是无法测试的。 | verifiable, demonstrable, measurable | unprovable, theoretical, abstract | adjective

Etymology:
non- (prefix) from Latin *non*, meaning "not"
testable (adjective) from Middle English *testable*, from Old French *testable*, from Late Latin *testabilis*, from Latin *testari* "to bear witness, testify," from *testis* "witness"
