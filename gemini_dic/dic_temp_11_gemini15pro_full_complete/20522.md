bizantine:/bɪˈzæn.taɪn/ /ˈbɪz.ən.tin/| adj.|拜占庭式的，错综复杂的|Characterized by intrigue, complexity, deviousness, and elaborate ornamentation, often in a political or organizational context.  Often used to describe something excessively complicated, secretive, and inflexible.|The company's byzantine structure made it difficult to get anything done. 这家公司错综复杂的结构使得很难完成任何事情。|The plot of the novel was so byzantine that I couldn't follow it. 这部小说的情节如此复杂，以至于我无法理解。|近义词：complex, complicated, convoluted, intricate, labyrinthine|反义词：simple, straightforward, clear|词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Byzantium, the ancient name of Istanbul, the capital of the Byzantine Empire (Eastern Roman Empire).  The empire was known for its complex political and religious systems.
