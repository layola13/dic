jitterbug:/ˈdʒɪtərbʌɡ/| n.|吉特巴舞者；紧张不安的人；（旧）摇摆舞爱好者|A person who dances the jitterbug, a fast and energetic dance popular in the 1940s; someone who is nervous or restless; (dated) a swing music enthusiast.|例句：The jitterbugs were showing off their impressive moves on the dance floor.  吉特巴舞者在舞池里炫耀着他们令人印象深刻的舞步。

例句：He's such a jitterbug before every performance. 他每次演出前都紧张不安。|近义词：dancer, enthusiast, worrier, fidget|反义词:calm person, relaxed individual|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "jitters" (nervousness) + "bug" (enthusiast, as in "firebug").  The term likely originated in the 1930s or 1940s.
