emulsioned:/ɪˈmʌlʃənd/ | adj. | 乳化的 | Made into an emulsion; combined into a uniform mixture of two unblendable liquids, such as oil and water.  | The mayonnaise was perfectly emulsioned, smooth and creamy. 蛋黄酱完全乳化，光滑细腻。| The photographer used emulsioned paper for the prints. 摄影师使用乳化纸进行打印。 | blended, mixed, homogenized | separated, immiscible | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "emulsify," which comes from the Latin word "emulgere" meaning "to milk out," combined with the suffix "-ify" which transforms a word into a verb. The "-ed" suffix turns the verb "emulsify" into the adjective "emulsioned."
