exhilarator: /ɪɡˈzɪləˌreɪtər/ | n. | 兴奋剂；使人兴奋的事物 | Something that exhilarates; something that makes one feel invigorated, refreshed, or excited. | The roller coaster was an exhilarating experience. 过山车是一次令人兴奋的经历。 | The crisp mountain air was a natural exhilarator. 清新的山间空气是天然的兴奋剂。 | Synonyms: stimulant, tonic, boost, pick-me-up | Antonyms: depressant, sedative, downer | noun

exhilarate: /ɪɡˈzɪləˌreɪt/ | v. | 使高兴；使振奋 | To make someone feel very happy, animated, or elated; to invigorate or stimulate. | The news of their victory exhilarated the entire team. 胜利的消息让整个团队都兴奋不已。 | The fresh air and sunshine exhilarated her after being indoors all day.  在室内待了一整天后，新鲜的空气和阳光让她精神焕发。 | Synonyms: thrill, excite, enliven, invigorate | Antonyms: depress, sadden, discourage, enervate | verb

exhilarating: /ɪɡˈzɪləˌreɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人兴奋的；使人振奋的 | Making one feel very happy, animated, or elated; invigorating or stimulating. | The exhilarating ride left them breathless and laughing.  刺激的旅程让他们气喘吁吁，开怀大笑。 |  The view from the mountaintop was exhilarating. 从山顶望去的景色令人振奋。 | Synonyms: thrilling, exciting, stimulating, invigorating | Antonyms: depressing, boring, tedious, monotonous | adjective
