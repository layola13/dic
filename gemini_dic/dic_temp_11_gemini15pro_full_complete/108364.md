lavishment:/ˈlævɪʃmənt/ | n. | 挥霍，铺张，奢华 | The act of being lavish; extravagance; profusion. |  The lavishment of praise on the young artist was overwhelming.  对这位年轻艺术家的过度赞扬令人不知所措。| synonyms: extravagance, profusion, luxuriance, prodigality | antonyms: frugality, thrift, economy, austerity | noun


lavish:/ˈlævɪʃ/ | adj. | 奢华的，丰富的，慷慨的 |  (1) expended, bestowed, or occurring in profusion. (2) using or giving in great amounts; profuse. |  (1) They threw a lavish party for their anniversary.  他们为他们的周年纪念举办了一场奢华的派对。(2) She is lavish with her praise. 她不吝啬赞美。 | synonyms: extravagant, luxurious, opulent, profuse, generous | antonyms: frugal, thrifty, economical, meager, sparse | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lavisshen*, from Old French *laver* (to wash), from Latin *lavāre* (to wash). The original sense was to "drench" or "wash," later evolving to mean "bestow profusely" and then to describe something extravagant or profuse.
