glibly:/ˈɡlɪbli/ | adv. | 流利地，油嘴滑舌地 | Speaking or spoken in a smooth, fluent, and easy manner, often in a way that suggests insincerity or superficiality.  It implies a quickness and readiness of speech that may lack depth or genuineness. | He glibly explained away the inconsistencies in his story. 他油嘴滑舌地解释了他故事中的矛盾之处。 | Synonyms: smoothly, fluently, volubly, readily, effortlessly | Antonyms: haltingly, awkwardly, hesitantly | adverb

Etymology: From glib + -ly.  "Glib" likely comes from Low German *glibberig "slippery," related to Middle High German glibberich. The original sense of "smooth" or "slippery" evolved to describe smooth-talking.
