joggling:/ˈdʒɑːɡlɪŋ/ | n. | 杂耍；（尤指）抛接多个物体 | The act of keeping several objects in motion in the air at the same time by repeatedly tossing them up and catching them, typically for entertainment. | He entertained the crowd with his impressive juggling skills. 他用他令人印象深刻的杂耍技巧娱乐了人群。 | synonyms: juggling, toss, keep up, balance | antonyms: dropping, failing, neglecting | noun

joggling:/ˈdʒɑːɡlɪŋ/ | v. | 杂耍；（尤指）抛接多个物体；使难以兼顾；尽力应付 |  To keep several objects in motion in the air at the same time by repeatedly tossing them up and catching them. To try to deal with or manage several things at once. | She was juggling three balls and a club. 她同时抛接三个球和一根棍子。
He was juggling a full-time job and family responsibilities. 他尽力兼顾一份全职工作和家庭责任。 | synonyms: manage, handle, cope with, balance | antonyms: neglect, ignore, drop, fail | verb
