mouldier:/ˈmoʊldiər (US), ˈməʊldiər (UK)| adj.|更发霉的，更陈腐的|Covered with or smelling of mould, especially to a greater degree than something else.  More stale or outdated.|例句：The bread in the back of the cupboard was mouldier than the loaf on the counter.  橱柜后面的面包比柜台上的面包更发霉。

例句：His ideas about education were rather mouldier than I expected. 他关于教育的观点比我想象的要更陈腐。|近义词：mustier, fustiest, stalier, damper|反义词：fresher, newer|词性：形容词

mouldy:/ˈmoʊldi (US), ˈməʊldi (UK)| adj.|发霉的，陈腐的|Covered with or smelling of mould; stale or outdated.|例句：The cheese had gone mouldy.  奶酪发霉了。

例句：His jokes were all mouldy and unfunny. 他的笑话都很陈腐，不好笑。|近义词：musty, fusty, stale, damp|反义词：fresh, new|词性：形容词

词源: From Middle English *mowledy, from mould +‎ -y.  Ultimately from Latin *mucidus, from mucus (“mucus”).
