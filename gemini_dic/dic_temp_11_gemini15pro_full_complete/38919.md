confessionals: /kənˈfɛʃənəlz/ | n. | 忏悔室; 忏悔录; (尤指天主教的)告解亭; 告解席 |  a small enclosed compartment in a church where a priest hears confessions. Also, a piece of writing or speech in which someone admits to something they have done wrong. |  The priest sat in the confessional, listening to the penitent's confession. 神父坐在忏悔室里，听着忏悔者的忏悔。 |  synonyms: confession booths, confession boxes, private rooms | antonyms:  public forums, open discussions | noun


confessional: /kənˈfɛʃənəl/ | adj. | 忏悔的; 坦白的; | relating to or used for confession, especially in the context of religion.  Can also describe something that reveals private thoughts and feelings. | The novel was deeply confessional, revealing the author's innermost fears. 这部小说非常坦诚，揭示了作者内心深处的恐惧。 | synonyms: revealing, intimate, personal | antonyms: secretive, hidden, guarded | adjective
