deserver:/dɪˈzɜːrvər/ |n.|应得者；值得拥有者|Someone who deserves something; a worthy recipient. |She is a deserver of praise. 她是一个值得称赞的人。|The team were deserver of the championship after all their hard work.  这支队伍在他们所有的辛勤工作之后，值得拥有冠军。 |近义词:recipient, beneficiary, meriter|反义词:undeserving, unworthy|词性:名词

Etymology: From the verb "deserve" + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person or thing that performs a specified action).  "Deserve" comes from Old French "deservir" (to deserve, merit), from Latin "deservire" (to serve well, merit), from "de-" (intensive prefix) + "servire" (to serve).
