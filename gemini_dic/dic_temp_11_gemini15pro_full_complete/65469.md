eurocommunism:/ˌjʊərəʊˈkɒmjunɪzəm/ | n. | 欧洲共产主义 | A revisionist form of communism that emerged in Western Europe in the 1970s, characterized by a rejection of Soviet authority and an emphasis on democratic principles and social reforms. |  The Italian Communist Party was a prominent advocate of eurocommunism. 意大利共产党是欧洲共产主义的突出倡导者。|Synonyms: revisionist communism, Western communism | Antonyms: Stalinism, Soviet communism | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From euro- + communism.  Euro- is a combining form representing Europe or European. Communism comes from the French communisme, from commun common.
