kwela:/ˈkwɛlə/| n. |快乐的爵士乐；一种南非街头音乐|A style of upbeat South African township music featuring a pennywhistle or similar wind instrument.|Example: The band played a lively kwela tune that got everyone dancing. 乐队演奏了一首活泼的快乐的爵士乐曲，让每个人都跳起舞来。|Example: Kwela music originated in the 1950s. 快乐的爵士乐起源于20世纪50年代。|近义词：marabi, mbaqanga|反义词:|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Zulu *khwela* meaning "climb," "get up," or "get on," referring to the police vans ("kwela-kwela") used to transport arrested people.  The vans often had loudspeakers playing music, and the association eventually shifted to the music itself.
