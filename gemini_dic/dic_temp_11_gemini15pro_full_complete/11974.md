arterioscleroses: /ɑːrˌtɪəriəʊskləˈroʊsiːz/ (US) /ɑːˌtɪəriəʊskləˈrəʊsiːz/ (UK)
| n. | 动脉硬化 | A group of diseases characterized by thickening and hardening of the walls of the arteries. | Arterioscleroses can lead to heart attacks and strokes. 动脉硬化会导致心脏病发作和中风。 | Synonyms: hardening of the arteries | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a disease process, but one might consider "vascular health" as a contrasting concept) | Noun
