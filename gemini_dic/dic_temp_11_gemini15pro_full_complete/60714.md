electro-oculograph:/ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈɒkjʊləɡrɑːf/
n.|眼电图仪|An instrument for measuring the potential difference between the front and back of the eyeball, especially as an indicator of eye movement during sleep or when reading.|The electro-oculograph recorded rapid eye movements during the REM sleep phase.|Synonyms: EOG|Antonyms: None|noun

electro-oculography:/ɪˌlɛktrəʊˌɒkjʊˈlɒɡrəfi/
n.|眼电图描记法|The technique of recording eye movements by measuring changes in the electrical potential between the front and back of the eyeball.|Electro-oculography is used to diagnose certain eye disorders.|Synonyms: EOG recording|Antonyms: None|noun

electro-oculographic:/ɪˌlɛktrəʊˌɒkjʊləˈɡræfɪk/
adj.|眼电图的|Relating to or involving electro-oculography.|The electro-oculographic data revealed unusual eye movement patterns.|Synonyms: EOG|Antonyms: None|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
electro- (from Greek ēlektron, meaning "amber," referring to static electricity) + oculo- (from Latin oculus, meaning "eye") + -graph (from Greek graphē, meaning "writing" or "record")
