caraway:/ˈkærəweɪ/ | n. | 香菜，葛缕子 | An aromatic biennial herb (Carum carvi) of the parsley family, native to Europe and Asia, having finely divided leaves, small white flowers, and tiny, ridged, aromatic fruits (caraway seeds) used in cooking and baking. |  I added some caraway seeds to the rye bread. 我在黑麦面包里加了一些香菜籽。| Synonyms: meridian fennel, Persian cumin | Antonyms: N/A | noun
