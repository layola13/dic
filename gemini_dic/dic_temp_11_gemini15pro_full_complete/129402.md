nominalist:/ˈnɒmɪnəlɪst/ (US) /ˈnɑːmɪnəlɪst/| n. |唯名论者；名义主义者|A person who believes that universals or abstract concepts have no objective reality but exist only as names or labels.|The philosopher was a strict nominalist, arguing that abstract concepts like "justice" and "beauty" are merely convenient labels.|近义词:conceptualist|反义词:realist|词性:noun

nominalistic:/ˌnɒmɪˈnælɪstɪk/ (US) /ˌnɑːmɪˈnælɪstɪk/| adj. |唯名论的；名义主义的|Relating to or characteristic of nominalism.|His nominalistic approach to philosophy led him to reject the existence of abstract entities.|近义词:conceptualistic|反义词:realistic|词性:adjective
