chioppine:/tʃɒˈpiːneɪ/ /tʃɑːˈpiːneɪ/| n.|高底木屐；矮子乐|A type of women's shoe with a very thick, often wooden sole, fashionable in Renaissance Italy.  They elevated the wearer, sometimes to a considerable height. | Venetian women wore chioppine to display their wealth and status.  威尼斯妇女穿着高底木屐来展示她们的财富和地位。| Near synonyms: platform shoes, pattens |Antonyms: flats, slippers|noun
