coppice: /ˈkɒpɪs/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpɪs/ (US) | n.  |矮林，灌木丛; 砍伐矮林|An area of woodland where trees or shrubs are periodically cut back to ground level to stimulate growth.|The farmer harvested wood from his coppice every few years. 农民每隔几年就从他的矮林里收获木材。|Synonyms: thicket, brushwood, undergrowth | Antonyms: forest, woodland (mature trees)| noun


coppice: /ˈkɒpɪs/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpɪs/ (US) | vt. |修剪矮林，将…作成矮林|To cut back (trees or shrubs) to ground level to stimulate growth.|He coppiced the hazel trees to encourage new shoots. 他修剪榛树以促进新芽生长。|Synonyms: pollard, trim, cut back| Antonyms: plant, grow, cultivate| verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *colpis (recorded in the derivative colper "to cut"), from Vulgar Latin *colpare, variant of culpa "knife" (compare French couper "to cut"), from Latin colpus "blow with the fist, cuff," from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kelp- "to hit, strike."
