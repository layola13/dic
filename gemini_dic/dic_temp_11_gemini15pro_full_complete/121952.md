missionary: /ˈmɪʃənəri/ (US), /ˈmɪʃənəri/ (UK) | n. adj.| 传教士，传教的 | A person sent on a religious mission, especially one sent to promote Christianity in a foreign country.  A missionary can also refer to something related to or characteristic of religious missions. |  例句：The missionaries traveled to Africa to spread the Gospel.  传教士前往非洲传播福音。

例句：She worked as a missionary in India for many years. 她在印度做了多年的传教士。| 近义词：apostle, evangelist, preacher | 反义词: nonbeliever, atheist | 词性: noun, adjective


missionary: /ˈmɪʃənəri/ (US), /ˈmɪʃənəri/ (UK)| adj.| 传教的，传教士的 | Relating to or characteristic of religious missions. |  例句：He has a strong missionary zeal. 他有很强的传教热情。

例句：They participated in missionary work in remote villages. 他们参与了偏远村庄的传教工作。| 近义词：evangelical, apostolic | 反义词: secular, non-religious | 词性: adjective


词源：Late Middle English: from late Latin *missionarius, from Latin missio(n-) ‘sending’ (see mission).
