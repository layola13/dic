computerizations:/kəmˌpjuːtəraɪˈzeɪʃənz/
|n.|计算机化; 电脑化|The process of equipping something with or converting something to computers; the process of automating something by the use of a computer or computer system.|The computerizations of the banking industry have led to faster transactions.  银行产业的计算机化导致了更快的交易。|computerisation, automation|manual operation|noun

computerization:/kəmˌpjuːtəraɪˈzeɪʃən/
|n.|计算机化; 电脑化|The process of equipping something with or converting something to computers; the process of automating something by the use of a computer or computer system.|The computerization of the library catalog made searching for books much easier. 图书馆目录的计算机化使查找书籍变得更加容易。|computerisation, automation|manual operation|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From computerize + -ation.  Computerize is from computer + -ize.  Computer comes from the Latin 'computare' meaning "to compute, calculate, sum up, count."
