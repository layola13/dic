jeopardize:/ˈdʒɛpərdʌɪz/ | vt. | 危及，损害 | To put (something) in danger of loss or injury. |  Leaving the window open jeopardized the security of the house.  把窗户开着危及了房屋的安全。 | endanger, threaten, compromise, imperil | protect, safeguard | verb
jeoparder:/ˈdʒɛpərdʌɪzər/ | n. | 危害者，使陷于危险者 | A person or thing that jeopardizes something. | The reckless driver was a jeoparder of public safety.  这个鲁莽的司机是公共安全的危害者。 | endangerer, threat | protector, guardian | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *jeu parti, literally "divided game," referring to a situation in chess where the next move could result in either winning or losing. This evolved into the English word "jeopardy" meaning "danger," and "jeopardize" is the verb form derived from "jeopardy."
