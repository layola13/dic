nonpalatability:/ˌnɒnpælətəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 不适口性；难吃；令人厌恶 |The state or quality of being unpleasant or unacceptable to the taste or mind. | The nonpalatability of the medicine made it difficult for the child to swallow.  这种药很难吃，孩子很难吞下去。| Synonyms: unpalatableness, distastefulness, unpleasantness, aversiveness | Antonyms: palatability, tastiness, deliciousness | noun

nonpalatable:/ˌnɒnˈpælətəbl/ | adj. | 不适口的；难吃的；令人厌恶的 |Unpleasant or unacceptable to the taste or mind. | The burnt food was completely nonpalatable. 烧焦的食物完全不能入口。| Synonyms: unpalatable, distasteful, unpleasant, aversive | Antonyms: palatable, tasty, delicious | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + palatable.  "Palatable" comes from Old French "palatable"  (meaning "agreeable to the palate"),  from "palat" (palate) + "-able" (suffix meaning "capable of being").  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not".
