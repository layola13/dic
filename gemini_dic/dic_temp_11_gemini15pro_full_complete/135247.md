nonpalpable:/nɒnˈpælpəbl/ /nɑːnˈpælpəbl/
adjective
不可触知的；难以捉摸的
Not able to be felt by touch; intangible.
The tension in the room was thick and nonpalpable, yet everyone felt it. 房间里的气氛紧张得令人窒息，虽然摸不着，但每个人都能感觉到。
The benefits of meditation are often nonpalpable, making it hard for some to grasp its value. 冥想的好处通常难以捉摸，使得有些人难以理解它的价值。
近义词: intangible, imperceptible, impalpable
反义词: palpable, tangible, perceptible
词性: adjective

词源:
From non- + palpable.  "Palpable" comes from Late Middle English, from Old French, from Late Latin *palpabilis, from Latin palpare "to touch gently, stroke."  The prefix "non-" simply negates the meaning.
