cirrhosis:/sɪˈroʊsɪs/
noun
肝硬化
A chronic disease of the liver marked by degeneration of cells, inflammation, and fibrous thickening of tissue. It is typically a result of alcoholism or hepatitis.
The patient was diagnosed with cirrhosis of the liver.  这位病人被诊断出患有肝硬化。
近义词:  liver damage, hepatic fibrosis
反义词:  healthy liver


ETYMOLOGY->
From New Latin *cirrhōsis, from Ancient Greek κιρρός (kirrhós, “tawny, orange-colored”), referring to the orange-yellow appearance of the cirrhotic liver, + -osis.
