anthonys:/ˈænθəniz/| n. |安东尼斯（人名复数）|Anthony's is a possessive form, indicating something belonging to or associated with multiple people named Anthony. It could also be a plural form referring to multiple individuals named Anthony.  Derived from the Greek name "Αντώνιος" (Antōnios), meaning "priceless" or "highly praiseworthy".|Example: The Anthonys are coming for dinner tonight. 安东尼斯一家今晚要来吃晚饭。|Example: We visited the Anthonys' house last weekend.  我们上周末去了安东尼斯家。 |Synonym: (As a plural) Anthonies|Antonym: N/A|noun
