croustade:/kruːˈstɑːd/ (US), /kruːˈstɑːd/ (UK)| n.|酥皮盒，油炸面包块；(盛放馅料的)酥皮壳|A croustade is a small, hollow, shell made of pastry or fried bread, often used as a container for savory fillings such as meat, vegetables, or cheese. |The chef prepared a delicate seafood croustade.  厨师准备了一份精致的海鲜酥皮盒。

The croustades were filled with a creamy mushroom ragout.  这些酥皮盒里填满了奶油蘑菇炖菜。|近义词：tartlet, vol-au-vent|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *croustade*, ultimately from Provençal *crostada*, from *crostar* "to crunch", from Latin *crustata*, meaning "encrusted".  It shares its etymology with the English word "crust".
