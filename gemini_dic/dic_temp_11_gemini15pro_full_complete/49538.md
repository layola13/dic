demarkation:/ˌdiːmɑːrˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 界限；分界线 | A line or boundary separating two areas, or the act of establishing such a boundary. | The demarcation between the two properties was clearly marked with a fence. 两处房产的界限用栅栏清楚地标明了。| synonyms: boundary, division, separation, limit | antonyms: connection, union, integration | noun

demarkation:/ˌdiːmɑːrˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 区分；划定 | The act of setting apart or distinguishing something. |  The demarcation of roles and responsibilities within the team was essential for success.  团队内部角色和职责的划分对于成功至关重要。 | synonyms: distinction, differentiation, delimitation | antonyms: confusion, blending, merging | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *demarcacion, from Old French demarcation, and directly from Medieval Latin dēmarcātiō (“boundary, limit”), from Latin dēmarcātus, past participle of dēmarcāre (“to mark off, to delimit”), from dē (“off, down from, away from”) + marcāre (“to mark, to border”).
