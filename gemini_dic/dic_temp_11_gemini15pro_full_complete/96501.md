indo-european:/ˌɪndoʊˌjʊərəˈpiːən/
adjective|印欧语系的|Relating to the family of languages spoken over the greater part of Europe and Asia as far as northern India.|例句：English is an Indo-European language. 英语是一门印欧语系语言。|近义词：Indo-Germanic|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly non-Indo-European)|词性:adjective
indo-european:/ˌɪndoʊˌjʊərəˈpiːən/
noun|印欧语系|A family of languages spoken over the greater part of Europe and Asia as far as northern India.|例句：The Indo-European language family is vast and complex. 印欧语系庞大而复杂。|近义词：Indo-Germanic|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly non-Indo-European)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *Indo- + European.  "Indo-" refers to India, reflecting the easternmost extent of the family's historical range. "European" reflects its presence in Europe. The term acknowledges the geographical distribution of languages descended from a common ancestor, Proto-Indo-European (PIE).
