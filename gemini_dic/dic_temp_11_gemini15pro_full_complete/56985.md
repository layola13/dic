double-crosses: /ˈdʌbəlˌkrɒsɪz/ (British) /ˈdʌbəlˌkrɔːsɪz/ (American)
| n. | 背叛；出卖；欺骗 | The act of betraying or deceiving someone, especially someone who trusts you. | He double-crossed his partner and stole all the money. 他出卖了他的伙伴，偷走了所有的钱。 |
double-crosses: /ˈdʌbəlˌkrɒsɪz/ (British) /ˈdʌbəlˌkrɔːsɪz/ (American)
| v. | 背叛；出卖；欺骗 | To betray or deceive someone, especially someone who trusts you. | She double-crossed him by revealing his secret to the police. 她向警方透露了他的秘密，从而背叛了他。 | Synonyms: betray, deceive, cheat | Antonyms: support, help, assist | verb, noun

PHRASE->
double-cross someone = 背叛某人
be double-crossed by someone = 被某人背叛
a double-cross =  背叛行为
double-crossing = 背叛；欺骗 (名词形式，表示这个行为)
He pulled a double-cross on his best friend. 他对他的最好的朋友耍了两面派。


词源： double + cross.  cross原意有阻碍，反对的意思，double cross 指双重阻碍，也就是背后捅刀子的意思。
