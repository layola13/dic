also-ran:/ˈɔːlsoʊ ræn/ | n. | 落选者，失败者 |  A person who loses in a race or other competition, especially someone who is not considered to have a serious chance of winning.  |  Many of the also-rans in the presidential primaries have now endorsed the nominee.  许多在总统初选中落败的候选人现在都支持被提名人。 | synonyms: loser, failure, non-winner, underdog | antonyms: winner, victor, champion | noun

also-ran:/ˈɔːlsoʊ ræn/ | n. | 次要人物，无关紧要的人 | A person or thing that is not important or successful. | He felt like an also-ran in the company, never getting the recognition he deserved. 他觉得自己在公司里是个无足轻重的人物，从未得到应有的认可。 | synonyms: nobody, nonentity, insignificant person | antonyms: VIP, celebrity, somebody | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From horse racing terminology.  Originally referred to a horse that finished a race but did not win, place, or show (finish in the top three).  The term later broadened to include any loser or non-winner in a competition. 源于赛马术语。最初指的是完成比赛但没有赢、位列第二或第三（前三名）的马。该术语后来扩展到包括比赛中的任何输家或非赢家。
