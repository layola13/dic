disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/ /dɪsˈdeɪn/
n.|轻蔑，鄙视|The feeling that someone or something is unworthy of one's consideration or respect.|He looked at me with disdain. 他轻蔑地看着我。
v.|鄙视，蔑视|Consider to be unworthy of one's consideration or respect.|She disdained to answer his rude remarks. 她不屑回答他粗鲁的言论。
近义词:contempt, scorn, derision
反义词:respect, admiration, esteem
词性:noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desdeignier*, from *des-* (expressing reversal) + *deignier* "to deem worthy," from Latin *dignari* "to deem worthy," from *dignus* "worthy."
