discerption:/dɪˈsɜːrpʃən/ | n. |识别；辨别；洞察力|The act of perceiving or recognizing something; keenness of insight and judgment.|She demonstrated great discerption in choosing her investments. 她对投资的选择展现出非凡的洞察力。|例句：He had the discerption to see that the plan was flawed. 他有洞察力看出这个计划有缺陷。


例句：With remarkable discerption, she identified the true culprit. 她以非凡的洞察力，识别出真正的罪魁祸首。


近义词：insight, perception, acumen, judgment, discrimination|反义词：ignorance, blindness, obtuseness|词性:名词

词源: Late Middle English: from Old French discerpcion, from Latin discerptio(n-), from discerpere ‘tear apart’, from dis- ‘apart’ + carpere ‘pluck’.
