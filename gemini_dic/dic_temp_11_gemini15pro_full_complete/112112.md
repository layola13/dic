longshore:/ˈlɔːŋʃɔːr/ | adj. n.| 沿岸的，海岸边的；码头工人 | relating to or working on the shore, especially loading and unloading ships. |  The longshore drift is gradually changing the coastline. 海岸的漂沙正在逐渐改变海岸线。 | synonyms: coastal, shoreline, seaside, waterfront, dockside | antonyms: inland, offshore | adjective

longshore:/ˈlɔːŋʃɔːr/ | n.| 码头工人 | a dockworker; someone who loads and unloads ships. | The longshoremen were on strike for better wages. 码头工人为了争取更高的工资而罢工。| synonyms: dockworker, stevedore, lumper | antonyms: None | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "long" + "shore."  "Long" in this context refers to the length of the shore. The term began to be used in the late 16th century.
