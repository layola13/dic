blackbirded: /ˌblækˈbɜːrdɪd/ | v. |  诱骗；挖走（员工）；秘密劫持（飞机） | to entice (a person, esp. an entertainer or sports player) away from a commitment or contract, typically by offering more money; to hijack (an aircraft) clandestinely. |  They blackbirded the engineer from their competitor by offering double the salary. 他们以双倍的薪水从竞争对手那里挖走了工程师。 | Synonyms: poached, lured, enticed, headhunted | Antonyms: released, freed | verb

blackbirding: /ˈblækˌbɜːrdɪŋ/ | n. |  诱骗；挖人；劫持 | The act of enticing someone away from a commitment, especially an employee. Also refers to the historical practice of kidnapping people to work as forced laborers. | The company engaged in aggressive blackbirding of top talent. 这家公司大肆挖竞争对手的顶尖人才。 | Synonyms: poaching, headhunting, abduction | Antonyms: recruitment (within ethical boundaries) | noun
