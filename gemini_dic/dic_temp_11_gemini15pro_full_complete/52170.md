diapir:/ˈdaɪəpaɪər/ | n. |底辟，挤入构造|A dome-shaped geologic structure formed when a mobile mass of rock, such as salt or igneous rock, is forced upward through denser overlying rock layers.|The diapir pushed through the overlying sedimentary layers, forming a salt dome.|Near synonyms: intrusion, dome, pluton|Antonym: (No direct antonym, could consider "basin" in certain geological contexts)|Noun


diapirs:/ˈdaɪəpaɪərz/ | n. |底辟（复数）|Plural form of diapir, referring to multiple dome-shaped geologic structures formed by upward intrusion of mobile rock masses.|The region is characterized by numerous salt diapirs.|Near synonyms: intrusions, domes, plutons|Antonym: (No direct antonym)|Noun
