hardball: /ˈhɑːrdbɔːl/ | n.  | 强硬手段，强硬政策；硬式棒球 |  Uncompromising and ruthless methods or dealings, especially in politics or business. |  The mayor is known for playing hardball with his opponents. 市长以对他的对手采取强硬手段而闻名。 | synonyms:  tough tactics, no-holds-barred, aggressive negotiation, bare-knuckle fighting | antonyms:  compromise, negotiation, diplomacy, soft approach | noun

hardball: /ˈhɑːrdbɔːl/ | adj.  | 强硬的，不妥协的 |  Uncompromising and ruthless. | They played hardball politics to get their way. 他们采取强硬的政治手段来达到目的。 | synonyms: tough, uncompromising, ruthless, aggressive | antonyms:  compromising, lenient, flexible, soft | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"Hardball" originates from the literal term for baseball played with a hard ball, as opposed to softball.  The figurative meaning, referring to aggressive and uncompromising tactics, emerged in the late 20th century, likely influenced by the competitive nature of the sport.
