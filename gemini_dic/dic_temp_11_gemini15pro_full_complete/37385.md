commentator:/ˈkɒmənteɪtə(r) (UK), ˈkɑːmənteɪtər (US)| n.|评论员，解说员|A person who comments on events or programs, especially on radio or television.|The commentator described the goal in vivid detail.  这位评论员生动地描述了进球的过程。|近义词：analyst, pundit, announcer, reporter|反义词:listener, viewer|noun

commentary:/ˈkɒmənt(ə)ri/ (UK), /ˈkɑːmənteri/ (US) | n.|评论，解说|A series of comments, explanations, or interpretations.|The political commentary was insightful. 这篇政治评论很有见地。|近义词：analysis, exposition, explanation|反义词:silence|noun

commentate:/ˈkɒmənˌteɪt/ (UK), /ˈkɑːmənˌteɪt/ (US) | v.|评论，解说|To provide a spoken description of an event as it happens.|He commentated on the boxing match. 他解说了这场拳击比赛。|近义词：report, narrate, describe|反义词:listen, watch|verb

comment:/ˈkɒment/ (UK), /ˈkɑːment/ (US) | n. v.|评论，意见；评论，发表意见|An expression of opinion or reaction.|She made a helpful comment about my work. 她对我的工作提出了一条有益的意见。  He refused to comment on the allegations. 他拒绝评论这些指控。|近义词：remark, observation, note|反义词:silence|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *commentari*, from commentor "devise, write notes," frequentative of comminiscor "think upon, contrive."
