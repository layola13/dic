offenses:/əˈfɛnsɪz/
n.
犯罪，违法行为；冒犯；攻击
An act that breaks a law or rule; something that causes a person to be upset, angry, or hurt.
The new law lists several serious traffic offenses.  这项新法律列出了几项严重的交通违法行为。
His words were an offense against good manners. 他的话违反了良好的礼仪。

近义词: crimes, misdeeds, violations, transgressions, insults, affronts
反义词:  obedience, compliance, compliments, praises

词性: 名词 (Plural of offense)

词源:  From Old French *ofense, from Latin offensa “offense, injury, hurt feelings,” noun use of feminine past participle of offendere “to strike against, stumble, hurt, displease”
