catfish:/ˈkætˌfɪʃ/ (US) /ˈkætˌfɪʃ/ (UK)| n. |鲶鱼；欺骗感情的人|A freshwater or marine fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth, typically bottom-dwelling.  Figuratively, someone who creates a false persona online to trick or deceive others.|例句：We caught a huge catfish in the river. 我们在河里钓到了一条大鲶鱼。

例句：He was catfished by someone pretending to be a model. 他被一个假扮模特的人欺骗了感情。|近义词：swindler, fraudster (figurative)|反义词: honest person (figurative)|词性: 名词

词源：
The word "catfish" originated from the whisker-like barbels around the fish's mouth, which resemble a cat's whiskers.  The figurative meaning, referring to online deception, became popularized in the 2010 documentary film "Catfish."
