desperate:/ˈdɛspərɪt/ /ˈdɛspərət/ | adj. | 绝望的，不顾一切的，极度渴望的 | Feeling or showing a hopeless sense that a situation is so bad as to be impossible to deal with; reckless or dangerous because of despair, hopelessness, or desperation; extremely wanting or needing something |  例句：He made a desperate attempt to save the drowning child. 他拼命地试图救起溺水的孩子。 | 近义词：frantic, hopeless, dire, critical, urgent | 反义词：calm, hopeful, composed, patient | 词性：形容词

desperate:/ˈdɛspərɪt/ /ˈdɛspərət/ |  adj. | 危急的，严峻的 | Extremely serious or dangerous | 例句：The situation is desperate - we need to act now.  情况危急——我们现在需要行动。 | 近义词：critical, dire, serious, grave | 反义词：safe, secure, stable | 词性：形容词

词源: From Latin *dēsperātus* (past participle of *dēsperāre* “to despair, give up hope”), from dē- “from” + sperāre “to hope.”
