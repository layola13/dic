hunkerism:/ˈhʌŋkərɪzəm/| n. |闭关自守；保守主义；墨守成规|A conservative political ideology characterized by a strong emphasis on traditional values, self-reliance, and a resistance to change, often associated with a desire to maintain the status quo, particularly in times of economic hardship.|During the Great Depression, hunkerism became a prevalent attitude as people focused on protecting their own resources and families.|近义词：conservatism, traditionalism, isolationism|反义词：progressivism, liberalism, radicalism|词性:名词

Etymology:
From "hunker down" (to squat or crouch down) + "-ism" (suffix forming nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, or practices).  The term gained popularity during the Great Depression in the 1930s.
