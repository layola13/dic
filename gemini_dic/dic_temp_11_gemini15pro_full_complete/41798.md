cortege:/kɔːrˈteɪʒ/ /kɔːrˈtɛʒ/| n. |送葬队伍；行列；随行人员|A solemn procession, especially for a funeral; a group of people or things. |例句：The funeral cortege slowly made its way to the cemetery. 送葬队伍缓缓前往墓地。

例句：A cortege of luxury cars followed the president. 一列豪华轿车跟在总统后面。|近义词：procession, retinue, entourage, train |反义词: N/A |词性:noun

cortege:/kɔːrˈteɪʒ/ /kɔːrˈtɛʒ/| n. |随从；仪仗队|A group of attendants or followers. |例句：The queen's cortege included several ladies-in-waiting. 女王的随从中包括几位侍女。

例句：The ambassador arrived with a small cortege. 大使带着一小队随从抵达。|近义词：retinue, entourage, suite|反义词: N/A |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cortège*, from Italian *corteo*, from *corta* "short," from Latin *cohors*, *cohort-*, meaning "enclosed yard, company of soldiers." The sense evolution likely involves the idea of a compact, organized group.
