outmaneuver: /ˌaʊtməˈnuːvər/ /ˌaʊtməˈnuːvər/
Verb
智胜，以策略取胜
to defeat or gain an advantage over someone by being more clever or skillful in using tactics or strategy.
The general outmaneuvered his opponent by flanking his army. 将军通过侧翼包抄敌军，智胜了对手。
The company outmaneuvered its competitors by releasing a new product first.  这家公司通过率先发布新产品，战胜了竞争对手。
Synonyms: outwit, outsmart, outflank, circumvent
Antonyms: be outmaneuvered, be outsmarted, be outwitted
词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + maneuver.  "Out-" prefix means to surpass or exceed. "Maneuver" comes from French "manœuvrer," ultimately from Latin "manu operari" meaning "to work with the hand." Therefore, outmaneuver literally means to surpass someone in maneuvering or handling a situation.
