cave-ins:/ˈkeɪvɪnz/| n.|塌方；坍塌；倒塌|A sudden collapse or sinking of land, a roof, or other structure.|The heavy rains caused several cave-ins along the highway.  暴雨导致公路沿线发生了几起塌方。|Synonyms: collapses, subsidence, sinkholes|Antonyms: constructions, building|noun

cave-in:/ˈkeɪvɪn/| n.|屈服；让步|The act of yielding or giving in under pressure.|After weeks of protest, the government finally caved in to the demands of the workers.  经过数周的抗议，政府最终屈服于工人的要求。|Synonyms: surrender, concede, give in|Antonyms: resist, defy, stand firm|noun

PHRASE->
cave in on someone:  向某人施压，迫使某人让步 The pressure caved in on him, and he confessed.  压力迫使他坦白。
cave in to pressure:  屈服于压力  He finally caved in to pressure and resigned. 他最终屈服于压力而辞职。
a cave-in of the roof: 屋顶的坍塌 The cave-in of the roof trapped the miners. 屋顶的坍塌困住了矿工们。
the cave-in of a mine: 矿井的坍塌 The cave-in of the mine caused several deaths. 矿井的坍塌造成数人死亡。
cave in (on something): 放弃（某事）  She was going to refuse, but she caved in and accepted the offer. 她本来打算拒绝，但她最终还是妥协了，接受了这个提议。
