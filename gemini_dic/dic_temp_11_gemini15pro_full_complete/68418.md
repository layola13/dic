falsifiable:/ˈfɔːlsɪfaɪəbl/ /ˈfæl.sə.faɪ.ə.bəl/| adj.|可证伪的，可检验的|Able to be proven false by observation or experiment.  A statement, hypothesis, or theory is falsifiable if it can be logically contradicted by an empirical test.|例句：A good scientific theory must be falsifiable. 一个好的科学理论必须是可证伪的。|例句：His claims were not falsifiable, so they couldn't be considered scientific. 他的说法是不可证伪的，因此不能被认为是科学的。|近义词：testable, verifiable, refutable|反义词：unfalsifiable, unverifiable|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From falsify (v.) + -able.  Falsify comes from Old French falsifier, from Late Latin falsificare "to counterfeit," from Latin falsus "false" + facere "to make."
