|n.|共同摄政，共同执政|A period of time when two or more people rule together.|The co-regency of William and Mary marked a turning point in British history.|joint reign, shared rule|sole reign|noun

|n.|共同摄政，共同执政|A period of time when two or more people rule together.|The co-regency of William and Mary marked a turning point in British history.|joint reign, shared rule|sole reign|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From co- (together) + regency (rule).  The "co-" prefix comes from the Latin "com-" meaning "together" or "with." "Regency" comes from the Old French "regence" and ultimately from the Latin "regere" meaning "to rule."
