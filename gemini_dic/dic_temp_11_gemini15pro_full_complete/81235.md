grandstand: /ˈɡrændstænd/ (US), /ˈɡrændstænd/ (UK) | n. v. | 大看台；炫耀；哗众取宠 | a large raised structure for spectators at a sporting event or public performance; an act of displaying one's abilities or opinions in an exaggerated or ostentatious way to impress onlookers. |  The president's visit was just a grandstand play for the media. 总统的访问不过是一场做给媒体看的表演。| Synonyms: show off, boast, swagger | Antonyms: be modest, be humble | noun

grandstand: /ˈɡrændstænd/ (US), /ˈɡrændstænd/ (UK) | v. | 炫耀；哗众取宠 | to perform or speak in a showy or ostentatious manner in an attempt to impress onlookers. | The politician was accused of grandstanding to gain votes.  这位政客被指责哗众取宠以赢得选票。| Synonyms: show off, boast, swagger | Antonyms: be modest, be humble | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From grand + stand.  "Grand" meaning large or impressive, and "stand" referring to a place to stand or sit.
