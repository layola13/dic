idealogue: /ˈaɪdiəlɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈaɪdiəlɔːɡ/ (US)| n. |理论家；空想家；意识形态的拥护者|A person who strongly believes in a particular set of ideas, especially political ideas, and tries to persuade other people to accept them.  Someone who is an advocate of a particular ideology.|He's a staunch idealogue who refuses to compromise.|近义词：theorist, doctrinaire, zealot, partisan|反义词：pragmatist, realist|词性: noun

Etymology: From French *idéologue*, from *idée* (“idea”) + *-logue* (“-logist”). *Idée* is from Latin *idea*, from Ancient Greek *ἰδέα* (idéa, “form, appearance, nature”). *-logue* is from Ancient Greek *-λόγος* (-lógos, “one who speaks (in a certain manner)”, a combining form of *λόγος* (lógos, “word, speech, discourse”)).
