over-sparingnesses: /ˈoʊvərˈspɛərɪŋnɪsɪz/ | n. | 过度节俭，过度吝啬 |  Excessive frugality or stinginess; the quality of being too unwilling to spend money or use resources. |  His over-sparingnesses bordered on miserliness. 他的过度节俭近乎吝啬。| Synonyms: parsimony, frugality, thrift, stinginess | Antonyms: generosity, extravagance, lavishness | Noun

Etymology:

* "over-" (prefix): From Old English "ofer," meaning "above," "across," "beyond," related to Old High German "ubar" and Latin "super."  Indicates excess or going beyond a limit.
* "sparing": From Middle English "sparynge," derived from Old English "sparian" meaning "to refrain from," "to spare." Related to Old High German "sparon" and Old Norse "spara."
* "-ness" (suffix):  Forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state, quality, or condition.
