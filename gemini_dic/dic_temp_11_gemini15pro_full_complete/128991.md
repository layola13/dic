nippingly:/ˈnɪpɪŋli/ | adv. | 刺骨地；尖刻地；辛辣地 | In a nipping or biting manner; sharply, keenly.  It describes something that is cold, sharp, or biting, often referring to the weather or a critical remark. | The wind blew nippingly across the frozen lake. 寒风刺骨地吹过冰冻的湖面。|  The critic nippingly remarked on the author's lack of originality.  评论家尖刻地评论了作者缺乏原创性。 | 近义词:bitingly, sharply, keenly, piercingly | 反义词:gently, mildly, warmly | 词性:副词

词源: From "nip" (verb) meaning "to pinch or bite sharply" + "-ing" (present participle suffix) + "-ly" (adverb suffix).  The word "nip" itself has roots in Old English and Proto-Germanic words related to pinching or biting.
