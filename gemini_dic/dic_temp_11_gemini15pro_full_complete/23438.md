bradawl:/ˈbrædɔːl/| n.|锥子；小锥|A small hand tool with a sharp point used for making holes in wood or leather before inserting screws or nails.|He used a bradawl to make a pilot hole for the screw. 他用锥子钻了一个导向孔，以便拧入螺丝。|Synonyms: awl, pricker, gimlet|Antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *brad-al, equivalent to brad (small nail) + al (awl).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *brōd- (point) and *aliz (awl).
