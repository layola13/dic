mispurchasing:/ˌmɪsˈpɜːrtʃəsɪŋ/
n.|错误采购，采购失误|The act of buying something incorrectly, either the wrong item, the wrong quantity, or at the wrong price.|Due to a mispurchasing error, the company ended up with 10,000 staplers instead of 1,000. 由于采购失误，公司最终收到了 10,000 个订书机而不是 1,000 个。|Synonym: misbuying, wrong purchase|Antonym: correct purchasing, appropriate procurement
词性:名词

mispurchase:/ˌmɪsˈpɜːrtʃəs/
v.|错误地购买，采购失误|To buy something incorrectly, either the wrong item, the wrong quantity, or at the wrong price.|The department mispurchased a large quantity of outdated software. 该部门错误地购买了大量过时的软件。|Synonym: misbuy, buy wrongly|Antonym: purchase correctly, procure appropriately
词性:动词

词源:
The word "mispurchase" is formed by the prefix "mis-" (meaning "wrong" or "badly") and the verb "purchase" (meaning "to buy").  The prefix "mis-" is of Old English and Germanic origin.  "Purchase" comes from Old French "purchaser".
