mellow:/ˈmeloʊ/ (US) /ˈmɛləʊ/ (UK)| adj. n. v. |柔和的，成熟的，醇厚的；成熟，柔和；使成熟，使柔和|

**(adjective) Soft, gentle, and pleasant.  Ripe and sweet. Relaxed and calm.**

例句：
The mellow light of the setting sun bathed the room.
夕阳柔和的光线洒满了房间。

The wine has a mellow flavor.
这酒味道醇厚。

He was in a mellow mood after a few drinks.
几杯酒下肚后，他的心情变得轻松愉快。


近义词：soft, gentle, mild, smooth, calm, relaxed, ripe, mature
反义词：harsh, rough, immature, unripe, agitated


词性: adjective


mellow:/ˈmeloʊ/ (US) /ˈmɛləʊ/ (UK)| n. |成熟，柔和|

**(noun)  A mellow state or quality.**

例句：
The cheese had reached its perfect mellow.
奶酪已经达到了最佳的成熟度。

The mellow of the evening light was beautiful.
傍晚柔和的光线很美。


近义词：softness, gentleness, mildness, smoothness, calmness, ripeness, maturity
反义词：harshness, roughness, immaturity


词性: noun


mellow:/ˈmeloʊ/ (US) /ˈmɛləʊ/ (UK)| v. |使成熟，使柔和|

**(verb) To become or make something soft, gentle, and pleasant. To become or make something ripe and sweet.**

例句：
The years have mellowed him.
岁月使他变得成熟。

Age and experience had mellowed his temper.
年龄和阅历使他的脾气变得温和。


近义词：soften, gentle, mature, ripen
反义词：harden, roughen


词性: verb


词源：From Old English *mearu*  "soft, tender, ripe," related to Old High German *maro* "soft, tender," and ultimately from Proto-Germanic *mazwjaz*.
