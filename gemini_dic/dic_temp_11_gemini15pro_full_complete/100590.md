inthrall:/ɪnˈθrɔːl/ (US), /ɪnˈθrɔːl/ (UK)| v.t.| 迷住，吸引住|to hold the complete attention of (someone) as if by magic; fascinate|The magician enthralled the audience with his tricks. 魔术师用他的戏法迷住了观众。

The storyteller's vivid descriptions enthralled the children.  讲故事人生动的描述吸引了孩子们。

|近义词：captivate, fascinate, mesmerize, charm, enchant|反义词：bore, repel, disgust|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *enthrallen*, from en- + thrallen (“to enslave”), from Old Norse *þræll* (“slave”).  The original meaning was "to enslave," but the meaning shifted to "captivate" over time.
