invalidates:/ɪnˈvælɪdeɪts/
v. |使无效；使作废|To make something officially unacceptable or no longer usable. |The judge invalidated the contract.|Synonyms: nullify, negate, cancel, void, annul, overrule, disallow|Antonyms: validate, confirm, ratify, authorize, approve|verb

invalidating:/ɪnˈvælɪdeɪtɪŋ/
adj. |无效的；使人丧失信心的|Making someone feel unimportant or useless.|Having my ideas constantly rejected was an invalidating experience.|Synonyms: demoralizing, discouraging, disheartening|Antonyms: validating, encouraging, empowering|adjective

invalidation:/ɪnˌvælɪˈdeɪʃən/
n. |失效；作废|The act of making something invalid.|The invalidation of the election results caused widespread protests.|Synonyms: nullification, cancellation, revocation|Antonyms: validation, confirmation, ratification|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin invalidare, from Latin invalidus "weak, infirm," from in- "not" + validus "strong."
