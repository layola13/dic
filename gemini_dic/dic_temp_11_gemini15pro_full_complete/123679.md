moodishly:/ˈmuːdɪʃli/| adv.|喜怒无常地，闷闷不乐地|In a manner that shows changing moods; sullenly.|She walked moodishly around the room, refusing to speak. 她闷闷不乐地在房间里走来走去，拒绝说话。|近义词：sullenly, gloomily, morosely, grumpily|反义词：cheerfully, happily, brightly|词性:副词

词源:
From moody + -ly.  "Moody" comes from Old English *modig "proud, arrogant," later "brave, high-spirited," from Proto-Germanic *modigas "angry, spirited," from PIE root *med- "to take appropriate measures." The meaning shifted to "subject to changing moods" in Middle English.
