overlusciousnesses: /ˌoʊvərˈlʌʃəsnɪsɪz/ | n. | 过分甜美; 过分华丽; 过分丰盛 |  The quality of being excessively luscious; an overabundance of richness or sweetness, often to the point of being cloying or excessive. | The overlusciousnesses of the dessert made it difficult to finish. 这道甜点过于甜腻，让人难以吃完。|  excessiveness, richness, sweetness, opulence | plainness, simplicity, austerity | noun

Etymology:
"Over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi, meaning "above, over, beyond") + "luscious" (from Middle English "lucius," from Old French "luscious," meaning "delicious, delightful," ultimately from Latin "luxuria," meaning "luxury, abundance") + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns of quality or state).
