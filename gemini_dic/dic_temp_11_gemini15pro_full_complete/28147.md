capitulator:/kəˈpɪtʃʊˌleɪtər/ | n. |投降者，屈服者 |A person who surrenders or gives in to an enemy or opponent. |例句：The capitulator signed the treaty, ending the war. 投降者签署了条约，结束了战争。

例句：The general refused to be a capitulator and vowed to fight to the death. 将军拒绝投降，誓死战斗。|近义词：surrenderer, defeatist, quitter |反义词：victor, conqueror, resister |词性:noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *capitulator, from Latin capitulatus, past participle of capitulare "to draw up in chapters, arrange conditions, surrender on terms," from capitulum "little head, chapter (of a book)," diminutive of caput "head."
