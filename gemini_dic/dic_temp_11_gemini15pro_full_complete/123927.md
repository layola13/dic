mores:/ˈmɔːreɪz/| n. |风俗，习惯，道德|The customs and conventions embodying the fundamental values of a group or society.|Many older people complain about the decline in mores. 许多老年人抱怨世风日下。|近义词：customs, conventions, norms, traditions|反义词： taboos (in some contexts) |词性:noun

Etymology: From Latin *mōrēs* (plural), meaning "customs," "manners," "character," "conduct."  Related to *mos* (singular), meaning "custom," "manner," "habit," and ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\mos-,* meaning "custom."
