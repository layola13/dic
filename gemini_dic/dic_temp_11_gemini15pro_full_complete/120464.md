minors:/ˈmaɪnərz/| n.|未成年人; 副修科目; 小调; |Subjects studied in less depth than a major are called minors.  学习深度低于主修科目的科目被称为副修科目。|例句：She's majoring in Biology with a minor in Chemistry. 她主修生物，辅修化学。


minors:/ˈmaɪnərz/| n.|未成年人; 副修科目; 小调; |A musical key characterized by minor intervals, generally conveying a sad or melancholic mood.  以小音程为特征的音调，通常传达悲伤或忧郁的情绪。|例句：The piece was composed in A minor. 这首曲子是用A小调创作的。

minors:/ˈmaɪnərz/| v. (minor in something) |辅修|To study a subject as a secondary area of specialization.  将一门学科作为次要专业领域来学习。|例句：He minored in history while majoring in economics. 他主修经济学，辅修历史。
