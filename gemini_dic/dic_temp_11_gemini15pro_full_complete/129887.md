non-interventionist:/ˌnɒnɪntəˈvɛnʃənɪst/ | adj. n. |不干涉主义的；不干涉主义者 |  Relating to or characteristic of a policy of not intervening in the affairs of other countries.  |  The senator was a staunch non-interventionist, believing that the country should focus on domestic issues.  这位参议员是一位坚定的不干涉主义者，认为国家应该专注于国内问题。 | Synonyms: isolationist, noninterfering | Antonyms: interventionist, imperialist | adjective

non-interventionist:/ˌnɒnɪntəˈvɛnʃənɪst/ | n. |不干涉主义者 | A person who advocates or practices a policy of non-intervention. | The non-interventionists argued against sending troops overseas.  不干涉主义者反对向海外派兵。 | Synonyms: isolationist | Antonyms: interventionist, imperialist | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + intervention + -ist.  "Intervention" comes from the Late Latin "interventio," meaning "a coming between."
