multistakeholder:/ˌmʌltiˈsteɪkˌhoʊldər/ | adj. n. | 多方利益相关者的，多利益攸关方的 | involving or participated by representatives of several different groups, organizations, etc, each having an interest in the matter at hand. | The conference adopted a multistakeholder approach to internet governance.  这次会议采用了多方利益相关者参与的互联网治理方法。| The project requires multistakeholder collaboration for its success.  这个项目需要多方利益相关者的合作才能成功。 | Synonyms: multilateral, participatory, collaborative | Antonyms: unilateral, exclusive | adjective, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
multi- + stakeholder
multi-: prefix meaning "many," "multiple," from Latin multus "much, many."
stakeholder:  a person or group that has an investment, share, or interest in something, as a business or industry. From stake + holder. Stake means "an interest or share in an undertaking," from Old English staca "stake, post." Holder means "one that holds something," from hold + -er.
