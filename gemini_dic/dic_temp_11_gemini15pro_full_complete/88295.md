hip-pier:/ˈhɪpiər/ (US), /ˈhɪpɪə(r)/ (UK)| n. |嬉皮士；嬉皮风格的人|A person, typically young, who rejects established social customs and values and adopts a bohemian lifestyle characterized by long hair, colorful clothes, and often the use of drugs.|He lived like a hippie in a commune in the 1970s. 他在20世纪70年代像个嬉皮士一样生活在公社里。|近义词：bohemian, counter-culturalist, nonconformist|反义词: conformist, traditionalist|词性:名词

hip-pier:/ˈhɪpiər/ (US), /ˈhɪpɪə(r)/ (UK)| adj. |嬉皮的；嬉皮风格的|Characteristic of a hippie or their lifestyle.|She wore a hippie dress and sandals. 她穿着嬉皮风格的连衣裙和凉鞋。|近义词：bohemian, counter-cultural, unconventional|反义词: conventional, traditional, mainstream|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
Shortened from hipster (1940s), which referred to jazz aficionados.  The term "hipster" later evolved to describe the Beat Generation, and then in the 1960s, "hippie" emerged to describe the counter-culture movement.  Ultimately, from "hip," meaning "aware," "in the know."
