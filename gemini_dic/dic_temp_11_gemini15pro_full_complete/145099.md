overappraising: /ˌoʊvərəˈpreɪzɪŋ/
verb
高估
Estimating the value of something too highly.
She was overappraising her abilities when she applied for the senior management position. 她申请高级管理职位时高估了自己的能力。
He was overappraising the value of his old car, hoping to sell it for a much higher price than it was worth. 他高估了自己旧车的价值，希望能以比其实际价值高得多的价格卖掉它。
近义词: overestimating, overvaluing
反义词: undervaluing, underestimating
词性: verb (present participle)

overappraise: /ˌoʊvərəˈpreɪz/
verb
高估
To estimate the value of something too highly.
The real estate agent overappraised the house, leading the buyer to pay more than it was worth. 房地产经纪人高估了房子的价值，导致买家支付的价格高于其实际价值。
Many people overappraise their own importance. 很多人高估了自己的重要性。
近义词: overestimate, overvalue
反义词: undervalue, underestimate
词性: verb


词源:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "appraise" (from Old French "apreser" meaning "to set a price on").
