discontented:/ˌdɪskənˈtɛntɪd/ | adj. | 不满的，不满足的 | Feeling or showing dissatisfaction or unhappiness with one's circumstances. | She seemed discontented with her new job. 她似乎对她的新工作不太满意。 |  dissatisfied, malcontent, disgruntled | content, satisfied, happy | adjective

discontent:/ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt/ | n. | 不满，不满足 | A feeling of wanting something more or different than what one has. | There is widespread discontent among the workers. 工人们普遍存在不满情绪。 | dissatisfaction, unhappiness, restlessness | contentment, satisfaction, happiness | noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *discontent*, borrowed from Old French *descontent*, from *des-* (prefix indicating negation) + *content* ("content, satisfied"), from Latin *contentus*, past participle of *continēre* ("to hold together, contain, restrain"), from *com-* ("together, with") + *tenēre* ("to hold").
