blindsides:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪdz/ | v. | 使措手不及，攻其不备 | To attack or surprise someone from an unexpected direction or position, especially when they are vulnerable. | The news of his resignation completely blindsided us.  这个消息让我们措手不及。

The team was blindsided by their opponent's unexpected strategy.  这支队伍被对手出其不意的策略打了个措手不及。 | 近义词: ambush, surprise, catch off guard | 反义词: forewarn, prepare | 词性: 动词

blindsides:/ˈblaɪndˌsaɪdz/ | n. |  弱点，盲点 | A person's vulnerable side or weakness. |  His opponents attacked him through his blindsides. 他的对手攻击了他的弱点。

Exploiting someone's blindsides is considered unfair. 利用某人的弱点被认为是不公平的。 | 近义词: weakness, vulnerability, soft spot | 反义词: strength, forte | 词性: 名词
