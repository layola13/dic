back-door:/ˌbækˈdɔːr/ /ˌbækˈdɔr/
adjective|后门的；秘密的；不正当的|Relating to or involving a method that is not the usual or official one; indirect or secret.|The company gained access to the market through a back-door arrangement with a distributor.|Synonyms: clandestine, covert, secret, surreptitious, underhand|Antonyms: open, overt, public, aboveboard|adjective

back-door:/ˌbækˈdɔːr/ /ˌbækˈdɔr/
noun|后门；秘密通道|A door at the back of a building.|We went in through the back-door.|Synonyms: rear entrance, back entrance, tradesman's entrance|Antonyms: front door, main entrance|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From back + door.  "Back" meaning the rear part, and "door" referring to a hinged barrier for closing an entrance.
