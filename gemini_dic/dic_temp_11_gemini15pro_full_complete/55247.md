dissimulate:/dɪˈsɪmjʊleɪt/
verb
假装;掩饰;隐藏(感情)
To conceal or disguise one's true thoughts, feelings, or intentions.
He dissimulated his fear with a smile.  他用微笑掩饰他的恐惧。
She dissimulated her anger by pretending to be calm. 她假装镇定来掩饰她的愤怒。
近义词: feign, pretend, mask, conceal, hide
反义词: reveal, expose, disclose
词性: verb

dissimulation:/dɪˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃən/
noun
掩饰;假装
The act of concealing or disguising one's true thoughts, feelings, or intentions.
Her dissimulation was so perfect that no one suspected her true motives. 她的掩饰如此完美，以至于没有人怀疑她的真正动机。
The politician's dissimulation about his involvement in the scandal was eventually exposed.  这位政治家对卷入丑闻的掩饰最终被揭露。
近义词: pretense, deception, feigning
反义词: honesty, openness, candor
词性: noun

词源: From Latin dissimulare, from dis- "not" + simulare "to simulate, copy, represent," from similis "like."
