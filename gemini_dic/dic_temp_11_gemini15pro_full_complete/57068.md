doucet:/ˈduːsɪt/, /ˈduːseɪ/ | adj. | 甜的；可口的；悦耳的 |  Pleasant to the taste; sweet. Also used figuratively to describe something pleasant or agreeable. Derived from Old French "doucet" (modern French "douce"), ultimately from Latin "dulcis" meaning "sweet." | The doucet tones of the harp lulled the baby to sleep.  竖琴甜美的声音使婴儿入睡了。| Synonyms: sweet, pleasant, agreeable, melodious, delightful | Antonyms: sour, bitter, unpleasant, harsh, disagreeable | Adjective
