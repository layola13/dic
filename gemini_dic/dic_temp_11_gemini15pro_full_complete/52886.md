diminutive:/dɪˈmɪnjətɪv/| adj. n.|小的，微小的；爱称|Something that is diminutive is extremely small.  A diminutive word, name, or form of a word is made shorter and/or cuter. |The bonsai tree is a diminutive version of the full-sized tree. 盆栽树是全尺寸树的缩小版。|She prefers to be called by the diminutive "Suzy." 她更喜欢被叫做昵称“Suzy”。|synonyms: tiny, small, miniature, petite, compact|antonyms: large, huge, enormous, gigantic|adjective, noun

diminutive:/dɪˈmɪnjətɪv/| n.|爱称，昵称|A shortened and/or cuter form of a name, often used affectionately.|He always called his wife by her diminutive, "Honey." 他总是用她的昵称“Honey”来称呼他的妻子。|synonyms: nickname, pet name, sobriquet|antonyms: full name, formal name|noun
