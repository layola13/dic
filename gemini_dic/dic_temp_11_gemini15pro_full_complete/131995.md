noncoordinating:/ˌnɒnkəʊˈɔːrdɪneɪtɪŋ/
adjective
非并列的，非对等的
not coordinating or coordinating poorly; not of equal rank, importance, or function.  Referring to conjunctions (like "but," "or," "and," "so," "for," "nor," "yet") that connect grammatical elements of equal rank.  A noncoordinating conjunction connects a subordinate clause to a main clause.
Example: "Because" is a noncoordinating conjunction, connecting a subordinate clause that explains the reason for the main clause. 因为是一个非并列连词，连接一个解释主句原因的从句。

Example: Noncoordinating conjunctions, such as "although" and "since," introduce dependent clauses. 像“尽管”和“自从”这样的非并列连词引出从属子句。


近义词:subordinating, dependent
反义词:coordinating, independent
词性:adjective

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
The noncoordinating conjunction "although" introduces a contrast.  非并列连词“尽管”引出对比。
He goes to work every day although he is retired.  尽管他已经退休了，他还是每天去上班。

Because it rained, we stayed inside. 因为下雨了，我们待在室内。
She sings beautifully because she practices every day. 她唱歌优美动听，因为她每天练习。
