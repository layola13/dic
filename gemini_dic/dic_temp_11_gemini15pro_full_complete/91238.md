hydrocorallia:/ˌhaɪdrəʊkəˈreɪliə/ | n. | 水螅珊瑚 | An order of colonial marine cnidarians, often mistaken for true corals due to their calcareous skeletons. They are found in tropical and subtropical waters. | Example: The divers observed a vibrant colony of hydrocorallia on the reef. 潜水员在珊瑚礁上观察到了一群充满活力的水螅珊瑚群。Example:  Some species of hydrocorallia can cause painful stings.  有些种类的水螅珊瑚会引起疼痛的刺痛。 | 近义词: fire coral (specifically Millepora), lace coral | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific biological order) | noun
