fanfare:/ˈfænfeə(r)/| n.|号角齐鸣；夸耀；炫耀；吹捧|A short loud flourish of trumpets or other brass instruments, typically to announce the arrival of someone important. Also, a showy display, especially designed to impress.|The king's arrival was announced by a fanfare of trumpets.|Synonyms: flourish, blast, peal, trumpet call, blare, announcement, proclamation, heraldry, pomp, pageantry|Antonyms: silence, quiet, hush, stillness|Noun

fanfares:/ˈfænfeəz/| n.|号角齐鸣（fanfare的复数形式）; 夸耀；炫耀；吹捧（fanfare的复数形式）|Plural form of fanfare.  Refers to multiple instances of short, loud flourishes of trumpets or other brass instruments, or multiple instances of showy displays.|Fanfares and fireworks marked the opening of the Olympic Games.|Synonyms: flourishes, blasts, peals, trumpet calls, blares, announcements, proclamations|Antonyms: silences, quiets, hushes, stillnesses|Noun

From Italian *fanfara*, from Arabic *fanfār*.
