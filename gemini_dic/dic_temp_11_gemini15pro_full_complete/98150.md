insatiably:/ɪnˈseɪʃəbli/ | adv. |  贪得无厌地，不知足地 | in a way that cannot be satisfied | He read insatiably, devouring books one after another. 他如饥似渴地阅读，一本书接着一本书地吞噬。 | She insatiably pursued knowledge, always eager to learn more. 她不知疲倦地追求知识，总是渴望学习更多。 | 近义词: voraciously, ravenously, unappeasably | 反义词: moderately, temperately | 词性: 副词

insatiable:/ɪnˈseɪʃəbl/ | adj. | 贪得无厌的，不知足的 |  having a strong desire for something, especially food, that cannot be satisfied  |  His insatiable appetite for power led him to betray his closest allies. 他对权力贪得无厌的欲望使他背叛了他最亲密的盟友。 |  She had an insatiable curiosity about the world and a thirst for adventure. 她对世界有着永不满足的好奇心和对冒险的渴望。 | 近义词: greedy, ravenous, voracious | 反义词: satisfied, content | 词性: 形容词

词源:  Late Middle English: from Old French insatiable, from Latin insatiabilis, from in- ‘not’ + satiabilis (from satiare ‘satisfy’).
