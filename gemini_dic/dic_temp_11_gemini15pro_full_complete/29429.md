catalepsy:/ˈkætəˌlɛpsi/ | n. | 僵住症，木僵状态 | A medical condition characterized by a trancelike state, often with loss of voluntary motion and feeling.  It is a nervous disorder marked by muscular rigidity and fixity of posture, as well as reduced sensitivity to pain. | During a catalepsy, the person may remain in any position they are placed in. 在僵住症发作期间，患者可能会保持在他们被放置的任何姿势。| Synonym: catalepsies, trance, rigidity | Antonym: flexibility, mobility | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *catalepsis, from Ancient Greek κατάληψις (katálēpsis, “seizure”), from καταλαμβάνω (katalambánō, “I seize”), from κατά (katá, “down”) + λαμβάνω (lambánō, “I take”).
