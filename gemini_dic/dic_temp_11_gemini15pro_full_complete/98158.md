inscapes:/ˈɪnskeɪps/ | n. | 内景，心灵景象 |  A term coined by the poet Gerard Manley Hopkins to describe the unique inner nature of a thing, its distinctive design, the features that make it what it is.  It often refers to the inner landscape of the mind or spirit. | Hopkins’s poetry is full of vivid inscapes. 霍普金斯的诗歌充满了生动的内景描写。 | synonyms: inner landscape, inner world, mindscape, soulscape | antonyms: outscape, exterior | noun
