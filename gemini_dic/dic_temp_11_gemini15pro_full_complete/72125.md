flood:/flʌd/| n. v. |洪水，泛滥；大量，涌入|A large amount of water covering an area that is usually dry; an overwhelming quantity of something.|The town was devastated by the flood.  这个城镇被洪水摧毁了。|近义词：deluge, inundation, torrent|反义词:drought, ebb|noun

flood:/flʌd/| v. |淹没，泛滥；大量涌入|To cover or fill something with water; to arrive or go somewhere in large numbers.|The river flooded the fields. 河水淹没了田野。|近义词：inundate, overwhelm, deluge|反义词:drain, recede|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *flōd (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *flōduz, from Proto-Indo-European *plewd-, meaning "to flow, swim". Cognate with Old Norse flōð, Dutch vloed, German Flut.
