accustomations:/əˌkʌstəˈmeɪʃənz/
n.
习惯，适应; 惯例
the process of becoming familiar with something or making something familiar
例句：
He quickly made the necessary accustomations to his new life in the city.  他很快就适应了在城市的新生活。

The accustomations of the local tribe were fascinating to the anthropologist.  当地部落的习俗让人类学家着迷。

近义词：adaptations, adjustments, habits, customs, practices
反义词：unfamiliarity, strangeness


词源:
From Late Middle English: from Old French acostumer, from a- (expressing direction) + costumer ‘accustom’, based on Latin consuetudo ‘custom’.
