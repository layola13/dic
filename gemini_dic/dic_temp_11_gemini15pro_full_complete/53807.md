disconfirmable:/ˌdɪskənˈfɜːrməbl/ | adj. | 可证伪的，可反驳的 |  Able to be proven false; capable of being refuted by evidence. | The hypothesis that all swans are white is disconfirmable, as the existence of black swans proves it wrong.  这个所有天鹅都是白色的假设是可证伪的，因为黑天鹅的存在证明了它是错的。 | refutable, falsifiable |  unfalsifiable, irrefutable | adjective

disconfirmable:/ˌdɪskənˈfɜːrməbl/ | adj. | 可证伪的，可反驳的 | Able to be disproven or shown to be false.  |  The scientist presented a disconfirmable theory, inviting others to test its validity.  这位科学家提出了一个可证伪的理论，邀请其他人来检验其有效性。 | testable, verifiable |  incontestable,  indubitable | adjective
