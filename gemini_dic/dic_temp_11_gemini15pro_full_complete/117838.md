melliphagan:/ˌmelɪˈfæɡən/ | n. | 食蜜鸟 | A bird of the family Meliphagidae, characterized by a long, curved bill and a brush-tipped tongue adapted for feeding on nectar.  These birds are found mainly in Australia and New Guinea.|  Example: The melliphagan flitted from flower to flower, sipping nectar. 食蜜鸟在花丛间飞来飞去，吸食花蜜。| Synonym: honeyeater | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, could consider insectivore) | Noun
