concreture:/ˈkɒŋkrɪtʃə(r)/ /kɑːnˈkriːtʃər/ | n. |凝结物；具体化；凝固|Something that has concreted or solidified; a hardened mass.  The process of becoming concrete or solid.  A concrete concept or idea.|The wound healed into a hard concreture. 伤口愈合成硬块。|近义词：coagulation, concretion, solidification|反义词: solution, dissolution|词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *concretura*, from Latin *concretus*, past participle of *concrescere* "to grow together," from *com-* "together" + *crescere* "to grow."
