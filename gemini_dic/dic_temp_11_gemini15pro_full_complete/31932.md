/ˈkaɪroʊˌθɪəriəm/ | n. | 手兽；手兽足迹 | An extinct genus of reptiles from the Triassic period, known primarily from its distinctive hand-shaped footprints. |  Example:  The cheirotherium footprints found in the sandstone provided evidence of Triassic life.  手兽足迹在砂岩中的发现提供了三叠纪生命存在的证据。 | Example:  Scientists debated whether Cheirotherium was a bipedal or quadrupedal animal. 科学家们争论手兽是两足动物还是四足动物。| Synonym:  Chirotherium (variant spelling) | Antonym:  N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *kheir* (χειρ) "hand" + *thērion* (θηρίον) "beast," literally "hand-beast."
