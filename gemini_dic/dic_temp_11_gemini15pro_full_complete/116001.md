marshals:/ˈmɑːrʃəlz/ (US) /ˈmɑːʃlz/ (UK)| n. v.| 元帅；执法官；司仪；引导员|A high-ranking officer in the armed forces, or a law enforcement officer, or someone who arranges and directs a ceremony or event.| The marshals escorted the president. 元帅护送总统。 | synonyms: officer, director, organizer, guide | antonyms: follower, subordinate | noun

marshals:/ˈmɑːrʃəlz/ (US) /ˈmɑːʃlz/ (UK)| v.|排列；引导；整理；指挥| To arrange or assemble in order; to lead or guide; to gather and organize.|  The band marshaled the parade. 乐队指挥游行队伍。 | synonyms: arrange, assemble, conduct, guide, direct | antonyms: scatter, disorganize | verb
