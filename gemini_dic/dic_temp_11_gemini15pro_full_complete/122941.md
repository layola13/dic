momentous:/moʊˈmɛntəs/| adj.|重大的，重要的|Of great importance or significance, especially in its bearing on the future.|例句：The signing of the peace treaty was a momentous occasion. 签署和平条约是一个重大事件。

例句：It was a momentous decision that changed the course of history. 这是一个改变历史进程的重大决定。

近义词：important, significant, historic, consequential, pivotal|反义词：trivial, insignificant, unimportant|词性：形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *momentōsus*  "full of importance," from *momentum*  "movement, moving power" (from *movēre* "to move") + *-ōsus*  "full of."  The sense shifted from "weighty, of consequence" to "important" in the 18th century.
