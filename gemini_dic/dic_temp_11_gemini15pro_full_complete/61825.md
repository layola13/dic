empiricism:/ɪmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 经验主义;经验论 | The theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience. Stimulated by the rise of experimental science, it developed in the 17th and 18th centuries, expounded in particular by John Locke, George Berkeley, and David Hume. |  Modern science is based on empiricism. 现代科学建立在经验主义的基础上。| synonyms: experientialism,  sensationalism | antonyms: rationalism,  intellectualism | noun
