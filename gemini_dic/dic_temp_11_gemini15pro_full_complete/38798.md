conductor:/kənˈdʌktər/ | n. | 指挥家；售票员；导体 | a person who directs the performance of an orchestra or choir.  A person who collects fares on a bus or train. A substance that allows heat or electricity to pass through it. | The conductor led the orchestra in a powerful performance of Beethoven's Fifth Symphony. The conductor asked to see our tickets. Copper is a good conductor of electricity. | 近义词: director, leader, guide (for people); wire, cable (for electricity) | 反义词: insulator (for electricity and heat) | 词性: noun


conductor:/kənˈdʌktər/ | n. | 导体 | A material or device that transmits heat, electricity, or sound. | Copper wires are good conductors of electricity.  The metal pot handle was a poor conductor of heat, burning my hand. | 近义词: transmitter | 反义词: insulator | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *conductour*, from Anglo-Norman *conduettour*, from Late Latin *conductor*, from Latin *conducere* ("to lead together, bring together, assemble, hire"), from *com-* ("together") + *ducere* ("to lead").
