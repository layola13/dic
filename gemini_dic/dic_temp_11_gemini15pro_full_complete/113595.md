macedonianism: /ˌmæsɪˈdoʊniənɪzəm/ | n. | 马其顿主义; 马其顿民族主义 |  A policy, especially that of the nineteenth and twentieth centuries, promoting the political unification of Macedonia (a region in southeastern Europe) or the creation of a Greater Macedonia.  It can also refer to the doctrine of a separate Macedonian Orthodox Church, distinct from the Serbian, Bulgarian, and Greek Orthodox Churches. |  The rise of Macedonianism in the late 19th century complicated the already tense Balkan politics. 马其顿主义在19世纪后期的兴起使本已紧张的巴尔干政治更加复杂。 |  nationalism, irredentism |  anti-nationalism, particularism | noun
