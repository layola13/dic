fiord:/ˈfjɔːrd/ (US) /fjɔːd/ (UK)| n. |峡湾|A long, narrow, deep inlet of the sea between high cliffs, typically formed by submergence of a glaciated valley.|We sailed through the majestic Norwegian fjords. 我们航行穿过雄伟的挪威峡湾。|近义词：inlet, bay, cove |反义词: (没有明确的反义词)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Norwegian *fjǫrðr*, from Old Norse *fjǫrðr*, from Proto-Germanic *ferþuz* (“fjord, firth”), from Proto-Indo-European *pertu- (“ford, crossing”). Cognate with Scottish Gaelic *fiord*.
