hell-on-wheels:/ˌhɛl ɒn ˈwiːlz/
adjective
风风火火的；精力充沛的；快速移动的；非常棒的
Something or someone that is hell-on-wheels is very fast, exciting, or effective.
例句：That new sports car is hell-on-wheels. 那辆新的跑车真是棒极了。

例句：She's a hell-on-wheels fundraiser. 她是一位非常出色的筹款人。

近义词：fast, dynamic, energetic, impressive, excellent, terrific
反义词：slow, sluggish, ineffective

词性: adjective
词源:  This idiom originated in the American West, likely in the late 19th century.  While the exact origin is uncertain, some believe it initially referred to a fast-moving stagecoach or wagon.  The "hell" part emphasizes the speed and power, suggesting something almost demonic in its intensity.
