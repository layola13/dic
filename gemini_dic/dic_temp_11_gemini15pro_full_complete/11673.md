ammoniac:/əˈmoʊniæk/ | n. |氨; 氨水| A pungent colorless gaseous alkaline compound of nitrogen and hydrogen that is very soluble in water and can easily be condensed to a liquid by cold and pressure.  It is used in many industrial and laboratory processes, and occurs naturally in decaying organic matter.|  The strong smell of ammoniac filled the air. 空气中弥漫着刺鼻的氨水味。| synonyms: ammonia,  NH3 | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a chemical compound) | noun
