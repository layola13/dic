bid:/bɪd/| n. vt. vi.|出价，投标；努力争取；命令，吩咐|An offer of a particular amount of money for something that is being sold.  To offer a particular amount of money for something that is being sold. To attempt to achieve or obtain something.| He made a bid of $1,000 for the painting. 他出价1000美元买这幅画。| synonyms: offer, tender, proposal | antonyms: withdrawal, refusal | noun/verb

bid:/bɪd/| n. vt. vi.|邀请，恳求|An invitation to somebody to do something.| She made a bid for freedom. 她争取自由。| synonyms: appeal, plea, request | antonyms:  demand, command | noun/verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *biddan, from Proto-Germanic *bidjaną (“to ask, request, pray”), from Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰedʰ- (“to ask, pray”). Cognate with Dutch bidden (“to pray”), German bitten (“to ask, request”).
