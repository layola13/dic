outpriced:/ˌaʊtˈpraɪst/ | v. | 定价过高，价格超过; 使…因价格过高而无人问津 |  To price something higher than someone else or higher than the market can bear, making it too expensive to be purchased. | The smaller stores were outpriced by the larger chains.  小商店的价格比连锁店高，失去了竞争力。 |  Example 1:  The local bookstore was outpriced by online retailers and had to close down.  本地书店的价格比在线零售商高，不得不关门了。

Example 2:  Their luxury cars are outpriced for the average consumer.  他们的豪华车对普通消费者来说价格太高了。 | Synonyms: overprice, price out, undersell (antonym for the seller) | Antonyms: underprice, undersell (for the seller) | verb (past tense: outpriced, past participle: outpriced, present participle: outpricing, third-person singular present: outprices)
