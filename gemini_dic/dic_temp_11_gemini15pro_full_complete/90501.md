hucksterisms: /ˈhʌkstərɪzəmz/
| n. |叫卖；兜售；花言巧语；夸大其词的广告宣传|Aggressive salesmanship or advertising, especially using exaggerated or dubious claims.|The politician's speech was full of hucksterisms, promising everything but delivering nothing. 这位政客的演讲充满了花言巧语，承诺了一切，却什么也没兑现。|近义词：hype, ballyhoo, puffery, claptrap|反义词: understatement, sincerity, honesty|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From huckster + -ism.  "Huckster" comes from Middle Dutch "hokester" (peddler, retailer), related to "hoken" (to peddle).  The "-ism" suffix denotes a doctrine, practice, or characteristic.
