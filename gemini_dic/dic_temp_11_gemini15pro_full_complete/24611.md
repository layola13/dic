broil:/brɔɪl/| v. n.|烧烤，烤焦，(被)灼热，争吵|To cook something by exposing it to direct, intense heat, or to be cooked in this way. Also, to become extremely hot, or to cause something to become extremely hot. Can also refer to a noisy quarrel or commotion.|例句：I broiled the steak for five minutes on each side. 我把牛排两面各烤了五分钟。

例句：The sun broiled the desert sand. 太阳炙烤着沙漠里的沙子。|近义词：grill, roast, scorch, bake, burn, sear|反义词：freeze, chill, cool|词性:动词,名词

broil:/brɔɪl/| n.|烧烤，烤焦，灼热|The act of broiling or the state of being broiled. Also, a noisy quarrel or commotion.|例句：The chicken was cooked to perfection under the broil.  鸡肉在烤架下烤得恰到好处。

例句：The two families were in a constant broil. 这两个家庭一直争吵不休。|近义词：grilling, roasting, scorching, baking, burning, searing, quarrel, commotion|反义词：freezing, chilling, cooling, peace, tranquility|词性:名词

词源: From Old French *bruiller (to roast, burn), probably of Germanic origin.
