enfranchise:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ /ɛnˈfræntʃaɪz/| vt.|赋予…公民权（或选举权）；给予…特许权|To grant the privileges of citizenship, especially the right to vote; to empower. |The Nineteenth Amendment enfranchised women in the United States.  第十九修正案赋予美国妇女选举权。

The company was enfranchised to operate a ferry service. 该公司被授予经营渡轮服务的特许权。|近义词：empower, liberate, free, authorize|反义词: disenfranchise, oppress, restrict|词性:verb

enfranchiser:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzər/ /ɛnˈfræntʃaɪzər/| n.|赋予公民权（或选举权）的人；授予特许权的人|One who enfranchises another. |The enfranchisers of the 19th century fought tirelessly for voting rights. 19世纪的那些赋予公民权的人为投票权不懈地奋斗。

The city council acted as the enfranchiser for the new cable company. 市议会充当了新有线电视公司的授权者。|近义词：liberator, emancipator|反义词: oppressor, tyrant|词性:noun
