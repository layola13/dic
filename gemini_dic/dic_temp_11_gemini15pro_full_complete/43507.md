craven:/ˈkreɪvən/| adj. n.|懦弱的，胆小的；懦夫|Contemptibly lacking in courage; cowardly.  A cowardly person.|例句：He was too craven to stand up to the bully. 他太懦弱了，不敢反抗恶霸。

例句：The craven soldier deserted his post.  这个胆小的士兵擅离职守。|近义词：cowardly, timid, pusillanimous, spineless|反义词：brave, courageous, valiant, bold|词性:形容词,名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cravant*, from Old French *cravante*, past participle of *cravanter* (“to crush, oppress”), from *cravant* (“defeated, crushed”), of uncertain origin.
