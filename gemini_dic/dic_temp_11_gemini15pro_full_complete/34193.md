clampdown:/ˈklæmpdaʊn/| n.|压制，取缔，打击|A sudden action taken by a government or other authority to stop an activity, usually one that it considers illegal or harmful.|The government imposed a clampdown on illegal immigration. 政府对非法移民进行了打击。|近义词：crackdown, suppression, curb|反义词:tolerance, leniency|词性:名词


clampdowns:/ˈklæmpdaʊnz/| n.|压制，取缔，打击（复数）|Sudden actions taken by a government or other authority to stop activities, usually ones that it considers illegal or harmful.|The government has initiated several clampdowns on online gambling this year. 政府今年已经对网络赌博进行了多次打击。|近义词：crackdowns, suppressions, curbs|反义词:tolerances, leniencies|词性:名词


Etymology:  The word "clampdown" is a compound noun formed from the verb "clamp" and the adverb "down." "Clamp" originates from the Middle Dutch word "klampe" meaning "clasp" or "fastening." "Down" emphasizes the forceful and restrictive nature of the action.
