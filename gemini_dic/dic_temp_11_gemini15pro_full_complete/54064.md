disease:/dɪˈziːz/ | n. |疾病，病害 |A disorder of structure or function in a human, animal, or plant, especially one that produces specific signs or symptoms or that affects a specific location and is not simply a direct result of physical injury.|Heart disease is the leading cause of death in many countries. 心脏病是许多国家的主要死因。|近义词：illness, sickness, ailment, malady |反义词：health, wellness |词性: noun

Etymology: From Old French *desaise "discomfort, uneasiness, trouble," from des- "opposite of, away" (from Latin dis-) + aise "ease, comfort" (from Latin adjacens, present participle of adjacēre "to lie near").  The spelling was influenced by the unrelated word "ease."
