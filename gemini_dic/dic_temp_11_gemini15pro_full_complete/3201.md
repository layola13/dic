affectations:/ˌæfɛkˈteɪʃənz/
n.
做作；矫揉造作；装腔作势
An unnatural form of behavior meant to impress others.
She spoke with a ridiculous affectation of a French accent. 她说话时矫揉造作地模仿法国口音。
近义词:pretense, artificiality, mannerism
反义词:naturalness, genuineness, sincerity
词性:名词(plural)

affectation:/ˌæfɛkˈteɪʃən/
n.
喜爱；钟爱(的事物)
Something that someone likes or is interested in.  (Less common usage)
He had a peculiar affectation for collecting vintage postcards. 他有个怪癖，喜欢收集老式明信片。
近义词: fondness, liking, preference
反义词: dislike, aversion
词性:名词(singular)
