mucked:/mʌkt/ | v. | 弄脏，搞砸 | to make something dirty or messy |  He mucked his shoes walking through the mud. 他在泥里走，把鞋子弄脏了。 | messed up, soiled, dirtied | cleaned, tidied | verb

mucked:/mʌkt/ | adj. (informal) | 糟糕的，混乱的 | in a mess; ruined or spoiled |  "Things got a bit mucked up with the arrangements." 安排有点乱了套。 | messed up, chaotic, ruined | organized, tidy | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *mukken*, frequentative of *muken* (“to dung, manure”), of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *myki* (“dung, manure”).
