hyperintense:/ˌhaɪpərɪnˈtɛns/| adj.|高信号的，高强度的|Appearing unusually bright on a medical image due to increased signal intensity, often indicating a specific condition or abnormality.  The term is commonly used in radiology, particularly in MRI scans.|The MRI showed a hyperintense lesion in the brain. 核磁共振显示大脑中有一个高信号病灶。|近义词：bright, prominent, intense|反义词：hypointense, dark|词性:形容词
Etymology: "hyper-" (Greek: over, above) + "intense" (Latin: stretched out, strained)
