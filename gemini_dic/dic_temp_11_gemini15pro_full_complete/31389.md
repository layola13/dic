charge-with:/ˌtʃɑːrdʒ ˈwɪð/ | vt. | 控告；指控；使承担；赋予；使充满 | to accuse someone formally of a crime; to give someone a responsibility or task; to fill something with a particular quality or feeling |  The police charged him with theft. 警方指控他盗窃。 | synonyms: accuse, indict, entrust, imbue | antonyms: acquit, exonerate | verb

charge-with:/ˌtʃɑːrdʒ ˈwɪð/ | vt. |  使承担；赋予 | to give someone a responsibility or task | The committee was charged with finding a solution. 委员会被赋予寻找解决方案的任务。 | synonyms: entrust, task, assign | antonyms:  N/A | verb

charge-with:/ˌtʃɑːrdʒ ˈwɪð/ | vt. | 使充满 | to fill something with a particular quality or feeling | The air was charged with excitement.  空气中充满了兴奋。| synonyms: fill, imbue, permeate | antonyms: empty, drain | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "chargier" (to load, burden), from Late Latin "carricare" (to load a car), from "carrus" (wagon, cart). The legal sense of "accuse" developed later.
