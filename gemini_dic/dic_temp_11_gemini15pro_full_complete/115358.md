manorialization:/mænɔːriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
n.|庄园化|The process of reorganizing an estate or region into a system of manors, characterized by the concentration of land ownership in the hands of lords and the establishment of serfdom.|The manorialization of medieval England transformed the rural landscape and social structure. 中世纪英国的庄园化改变了乡村景观和社会结构。|
近义词: feudalization, enserfment
反义词: (No direct antonym, consider: liberalization, modernization)
词性: noun

manorialize:/mænɔːriəlaɪz/
v.|庄园化|To convert (land or a region) into a manor or manorial system.|The Norman conquerors manorialized much of England after 1066.  诺曼征服者在1066年后将英格兰的大部分地区庄园化。|
近义词: feudalize
反义词: (No direct antonym)
词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Medieval Latin *manerialis*  + -ization. *Manerialis* derives from Latin *manerium* (manor).  The suffix "-ization" denotes the process of making or becoming.
