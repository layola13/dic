obscurantists:/əbˌskjʊˈræntɪsts/ | n. | 蒙昧主义者；故弄玄虚者 |  A person who deliberately makes matters unclear or difficult to understand, especially in order to prevent people from having knowledge or insight. | The obscurantists tried to suppress the new scientific discoveries. 蒙昧主义者试图压制新的科学发现。 |  The professor was accused of being an obscurantist by his colleagues, who felt he intentionally made his lectures overly complex.  这位教授被他的同事指责为蒙昧主义者，他们觉得他故意把他的讲座弄得过于复杂。 | 近义词: dogmatists, sophists, charlatans | 反义词: educators, elucidators, clarifiers | 词性: 名词 (Noun)

obscurantist:/əbˌskjʊˈræntɪst/ | adj. | 蒙昧主义的；故弄玄虚的 | Relating to or characterized by obscurantism; deliberately unclear or abstruse. |  The group's obscurantist beliefs hindered progress and open discussion.  该团体的蒙昧主义信仰阻碍了进步和公开讨论。 |  He used obscurantist language to confuse his opponents. 他使用故弄玄虚的语言来迷惑他的对手。 | 近义词: mysterious, esoteric, ambiguous | 反义词: clear, straightforward, lucid | 词性: 形容词 (Adjective)


词源 (Etymology):

From Late Latin obscurans, present participle of obscurare (“to darken”),  from Latin obscurus (“dark, obscure”).
