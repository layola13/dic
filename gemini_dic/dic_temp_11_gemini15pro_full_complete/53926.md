discourses:/ˈdɪskɔːrsɪz/ (US), /ˈdɪskɔːsɪz/ (UK)| n.| 言语，论述，演讲; (正式的)谈话，讨论| A formal and often lengthy discussion of a subject, either written or spoken.  |The professor gave a series of discourses on modern philosophy. 教授做了一系列关于现代哲学的演讲。|近义词：discussions, talks, conversations, lectures, speeches|反义词：silence, quiet|词性:名词

discourse:/ˈdɪskɔːrs/ (US), /ˈdɪskɔːs/ (UK)| n.| 言语，论述，演讲; (正式的)谈话，讨论| A formal and often lengthy discussion of a subject, either written or spoken.  |The discourse on climate change is becoming increasingly urgent.  关于气候变化的讨论变得越来越紧迫。|近义词：discussion, talk, conversation, lecture, speech|反义词：silence, quiet|词性:名词

discourse:/dɪˈskɔːrs/ (US), /dɪˈskɔːs/ (UK)| vi.| (正式) 演讲，论述| To speak or write formally and at length about a subject. |He discoursed at length on the history of the region. 他详细地讲述了该地区的历史。|近义词：speak, talk, lecture, expound|反义词：be silent|词性:动词


词源: From Middle English *discours*, from Old French *discours* (“reasoning, speech, discourse”), from Late Latin *discursus* (“a running about”), from Latin *discurrere* (“to run about, run to and fro, run different ways”), from *dis-* + *currere* (“to run”).
