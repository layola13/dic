disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/ | n. vt. | 幻灭，失望 |  A feeling of disappointment resulting from the discovery that something is not as good as one believed it to be. |  The election result brought a sense of disillusion to many voters. 选举结果使许多选民感到失望。 | synonyms: disenchantment, disappointment, disillusionment | antonyms: hope, optimism, idealism | noun
disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/ | vt. | 使…幻想破灭 | To free from illusion or false ideas. |  The harsh realities of war disillusioned many young soldiers. 战争的残酷现实使许多年轻士兵的幻想破灭。| synonyms: disenchant, undeceive, disabuse | antonyms: enchant, deceive, delude | verb
