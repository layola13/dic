incapacious:/ˌɪnkəˈpeɪʃəs/
adj.
容量小的，狭小的；无能力的，不能胜任的
Not spacious; lacking capacity; not large enough to contain something.  Unable to hold or contain much; not roomy.  Lacking the ability or power to do, experience, or understand something.
The incapacious suitcase couldn't hold all of her clothes for the trip.  这个容量小的行李箱装不下她旅行的所有衣服。
Her incapacious mind struggled to grasp the complex philosophical concepts. 她狭隘的心智难以理解复杂的哲学概念。
Synonyms: small, cramped, limited, narrow, restricted, inadequate, insufficient
Antonyms: capacious, spacious, roomy, large, ample, vast, extensive
Etymology: From Late Latin *incapaciosus, from in- + capaciosus (“capable, roomy”), from Latin capax (“able to hold, capacious”).
Word Type: Adjective
