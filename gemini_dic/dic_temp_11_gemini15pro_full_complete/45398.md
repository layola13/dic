cupful:/ˈkʌpfʊl/| n.|一满杯|The amount that a cup can hold.  A cupful of flour is needed for this recipe.|She added a cupful of sugar to the batter. 她往面糊里加了一杯糖。|cup, scoop|handful|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "cup" + "-ful" (suffix indicating fullness). "Cup" comes from Old English "cuppe," from Late Latin "cuppa," of uncertain origin, possibly related to "cupa" (tub, cask).  "-ful" comes from Old English "-full," from Proto-Germanic "*fullaz" (full).
