amphipneust: /æmˈfɪpnjuːst/ | n. | 两栖呼吸动物 | An animal capable of breathing both in air and in water, or with both lungs and gills. |  The mudskipper is an example of an amphipneust, able to survive both in and out of water. 弹涂鱼是两栖呼吸动物的一个例子，能够在水中和陆地上生存。

The axolotl is another amphipneust, retaining its gills into adulthood.  蝾螈是另一种两栖呼吸动物，成年后仍保留着鳃。| 近义词: air-breathing fish, bimodal breather | 反义词: obligate aquatic breather, obligate air breather | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Greek *ἀμφί* (amphi, “both, on both sides”) + *πνεῦμα* (pneuma, “breath, spirit”).
