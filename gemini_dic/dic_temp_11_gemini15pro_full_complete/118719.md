metafictions:/ˌmetəˈfɪkʃənz/ | n. | 后设小说；元小说 |  Fiction that consciously and systematically draws attention to its status as an artifact in order to pose questions about the relationship between fiction and reality. It explores the nature of fiction itself, often blurring the lines between the story and the act of storytelling. |  "The French Lieutenant's Woman" is a famous example of metafiction, with the author inserting himself into the narrative.  | 近义词: postmodern fiction, self-reflexive fiction | 反义词: realist fiction | 词性: noun


metafiction:/ˌmetəˈfɪkʃən/ | n. | 后设小说；元小说 | Fiction that explores the nature of fiction itself, often blurring the lines between the story and the act of storytelling. |  Many of Borges's short stories are considered metafiction because they often feature stories within stories and self-referential elements. | 近义词: postmodern fiction, self-reflexive fiction | 反义词: realist fiction | 词性: noun
