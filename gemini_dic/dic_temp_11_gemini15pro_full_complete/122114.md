mistradition:/ˌmɪstrəˈdɪʃən/ | n. | 错误的传统，谬传 | An inherited or established custom, belief, or story, popularly regarded as traditional but in fact not accurately reflecting the past. |  Many people believe that groundhogs can predict the weather, but this is a mistradition. 很多人相信土拨鼠可以预测天气，但这只是一个谬传。|  The idea that Vikings wore horned helmets is a common mistradition.  维京人戴着角盔的想法是一个常见的谬传。 |  misconception, fallacy, erroneous belief | truth, fact, accurate history | noun
