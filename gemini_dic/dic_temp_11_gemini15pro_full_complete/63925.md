epiplexis:/ˌɛpɪˈplɛksɪs/
n.
 rhetorical question
A rhetorical question asked in order to rebuke or reproach rather than to elicit an answer.
"But what is government itself, but the greatest of all reflections on human nature?" is an example of epiplexis.
rebuke, reproach, question
praise
noun
Etymology: From Ancient Greek ἐπίπληξις (epíplēxis, “rebuke”), from ἐπιπλήττω (epiplḗttō, “I strike at, rebuke”).
