Faramir:/ˈfæ.rə.mɪər/| n. |法拉米尔|A fictional character in J.R.R. Tolkien's The Lord of the Rings. He is the younger brother of Boromir and son of Denethor II, the Steward of Gondor. |Example: Faramir is a noble and wise character. 法拉米尔是一个高贵而睿智的角色。|Synonym:  captain, soldier, warrior|Antonym: coward |noun

Etymology: Invented by J.R.R. Tolkien.  Likely derived from elements in Tolkien's invented languages, possibly related to Quenya *fara- meaning "sufficient, enough."  The exact etymology is not explicitly stated by Tolkien.
