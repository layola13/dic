nonphilosophy:/ˌnɒn fɪˈlɒsəfi/ | n. |非哲学|A term coined by François Laruelle designating a rigorously non-philosophical thought, a thought that does not take philosophy as its central focus or starting point. It aims to describe the Real without relying on philosophical concepts or methods.|Laruelle's non-philosophy attempts to break free from the constraints of philosophical thinking. 拉鲁埃尔的非哲学试图摆脱哲学思维的束缚。|Near Synonyms: non-standard philosophy, anti-philosophy |Antonyms: philosophy| noun
