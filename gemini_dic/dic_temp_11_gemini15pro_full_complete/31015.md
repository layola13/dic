challenged: /ˈtʃælɪndʒd/ | adj. |受到挑战的；残疾的；能力不足的 |Facing a difficult task or situation; having a disability that limits physical or mental abilities.|  He felt challenged by the difficult climb. 他觉得这次艰难的攀登对他来说是一次挑战。| The school has a special program for challenged children. 这所学校有针对残疾儿童的特殊项目。 |近义词：disabled, handicapped, impaired, struggling, tested | 反义词：able, capable, competent, gifted, talented | 词性：形容词

challenged: /ˈtʃælɪndʒd/ | v. | 挑战；质疑；考验 | To question something or someone; to invite someone to compete or take part in a contest; to test someone's abilities or determination. | He challenged me to a game of chess. 他向我挑战下棋。 | The lawyer challenged the witness's testimony. 律师质疑证人的证词。 |近义词：question, dispute, contest, oppose, defy  | 反义词：accept, concede, surrender, submit, yield | 词性：动词


词源: From the Old French "chalenge," meaning accusation, derived from the Latin "calumnia," meaning false accusation.
