blacklisting:/ˈblæklɪstɪŋ/
n.
列入黑名单，禁止
the act of putting someone or something on a blacklist
例句：The company faced blacklisting after the scandal.  这家公司在丑闻曝光后被列入黑名单。

例句：Blacklisting certain websites can prevent employees from accessing inappropriate content.  将某些网站列入黑名单可以防止员工访问不适当的内容。

近义词：banning, barring, excluding, ostracizing
反义词：whitelisting, approving, accepting
词性：名词

blacklist:/ˈblæklɪst/
n.
黑名单
a list of people or things that are disapproved of or are to be avoided or punished
例句：His name was on the blacklist. 他的名字在黑名单上。

例句：The government maintains a blacklist of suspected terrorists. 政府维护着一份可疑恐怖分子的黑名单。
近义词：prohibition list, banned list
反义词：whitelist, approved list
词性：名词

blacklist:/ˈblæklɪst/
vt.
将…列入黑名单
to put someone or something on a blacklist
例句：The actor was blacklisted for his political views.  这位演员因其政治观点而被列入黑名单。

例句：Several companies were blacklisted for violating environmental regulations.  几家公司因违反环境法规而被列入黑名单。

近义词：ban, bar, exclude, ostracize
反义词：whitelist, approve, accept
词性：动词
