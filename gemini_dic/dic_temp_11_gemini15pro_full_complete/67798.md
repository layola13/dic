eyeservant:/ˈaɪˌsɜːrvənt/
noun
眼奉阴违的人；只做表面功夫的人
A person who pretends to work hard only when being watched by their employer.
The new employee seemed eager at first, but soon proved to be an eyeservant, slacking off whenever the boss was out.  这个新员工起初看起来很热情，但很快就被证明是一个眼力见儿，只要老板不在就偷懒。
He was an eyeservant, always trying to impress the boss. 他是个马屁精，总是试图给老板留下好印象。
