monosaccharide: /ˌmɒnəˈsækəraɪd/ (US) /ˌmɒnəˈsækəˌraɪd/ (UK)| n. |单糖|A simple sugar that constitutes the building blocks of more complex carbohydrates. It cannot be hydrolyzed to simpler sugars.|Glucose is a monosaccharide and an important source of energy. 葡萄糖是一种单糖，是重要的能量来源。|Synonyms: simple sugar, single sugar|Antonyms: polysaccharide|noun
