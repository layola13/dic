blady: /ˈbleɪdi/ | adj. | 血腥的；残忍的；非常的 |  Bloody is a British English adjective used to emphasize something, sometimes as a swear word. It can also mean covered in blood. |  "It's bloody cold today!" (今天真他妈冷！)  The battlefield was a bloody mess. (战场上一片血腥狼藉。) | 近义词：gory, sanguinary, extremely, damned | 反义词: clean, bloodless | 词性: adjective

词源:  From Old English *blodig, from blod (blood) + -ig (suffix forming adjectives).  The intensive use likely developed from a blasphemous association with "Christ's blood," becoming a general intensive in the 17th century.
