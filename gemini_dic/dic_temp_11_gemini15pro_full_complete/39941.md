constructibility:/kənˌstrʌktɪˈbɪləti/
n.
可构造性，可建性
The capability of being constructed or the ease with which something can be constructed.
The constructibility of the bridge was questioned due to the difficult terrain.  这座桥的可建性由于地形复杂而受到质疑。
constructability:/kənˌstrʌktɪˈbɪləti/
n.
可构造性，可建性
The state or quality of being constructible.
The architect considered the constructibility of the design before finalizing the plans.  建筑师在最终确定方案之前考虑了设计的可施工性。


Synonyms: feasibility, practicability, buildability
Antonyms: impossibility, impracticability

Etymology:  From construct + -ibility.  "Construct" comes from Latin  "constructus," the past participle of "construere,"  meaning "to heap up, build." "-ibility" is a suffix forming nouns denoting quality or capability.
