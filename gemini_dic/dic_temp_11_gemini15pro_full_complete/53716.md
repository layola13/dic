disclosure:/dɪˈskloʊʒər/ (US), /dɪˈskləʊʒə(r)/ (UK)| n.|披露；揭露；公开；被披露的事情|The disclosure of the secret documents caused a scandal. 秘密文件的披露引发了一场丑闻。|Example: The company made full disclosure of its financial status.  公司充分披露了其财务状况。

Example:  The whistleblower's disclosure led to a criminal investigation. 告密者的揭露导致了刑事调查。

近义词: revelation, divulgence, unveiling, exposure
反义词: concealment, secrecy, suppression
词性: noun

词源: Late Middle English: from Anglo-Norman French disclosur, from the verb disclos- ‘unclosed’, from the prefix dis- (expressing reversal) + clos ‘closed’.
