ellipses:/ɪˈlɪpsiːz/
n.
省略号，省略
omission of a word or phrase which is grammatically necessary but can be deduced from the context.  A series of dots that indicates an intentional omission of a word, sentence, or whole section from a text without altering its original meaning.
Example: The ellipses in the quote suggested a longer, more complex statement had been made.  引文中的省略号表明曾说过更长、更复杂的陈述。

Example:  She trailed off, her voice fading into ellipses. 她渐渐不说话了，声音消失在省略号中。
近义词: omission, deletion, gap
反义词: inclusion, addition, completeness
词性: noun


ellipsis:/ɪˈlɪpsɪs/
n.
省略，省略法
The omission from speech or writing of a word or words that are superfluous or able to be understood from contextual clues.  A set of three periods (...) indicating an omission.
Example: The use of ellipsis can create a sense of mystery or suspense. 使用省略号可以营造一种神秘感或悬念感。

Example:  The ellipsis in the sentence made its meaning unclear. 句子中的省略号使其含义不清楚。
近义词: omission, deletion, gap
反义词: inclusion, addition, completeness
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin ellipsis, from Greek élleipsis "a falling short, defect, ellipse," from elleipein "to fall short, leave out," from ek- "out" + leipein "to leave."
