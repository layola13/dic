outguard:/ˈaʊtɡɑːrd/
noun
前哨，外卫
A soldier or group of soldiers stationed at a distance from the main body of an army to guard against surprise attack.
The outguard spotted the enemy approaching. 前哨发现了敌人正在靠近。
Synonyms: picket, sentinel, sentry
Antonyms: main force, rearguard
