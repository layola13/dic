meretriciously:/ˌmɛrɪˈtrɪʃəsli/| adv.|俗艳地；华而不实地；虚有其表地|In a way that appears attractive but has no real value or integrity.  Showing off in a vulgar or flashy way, but lacking real merit. |She dressed meretriciously, hoping to attract attention. 她穿着俗艳，希望能吸引眼球。|Synonyms: gaudily, flashily, tawdrily, garishly, ostentatiously|Antonyms: simply, plainly, subtly, unpretentiously, modestly|词性:副词

Etymology:
From Latin *meretricius* "of or pertaining to prostitutes," from *meretrix* "prostitute," from *merēre* "to earn, deserve, gain, acquire."
