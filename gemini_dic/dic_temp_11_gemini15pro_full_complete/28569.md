careen:/kəˈriːn/| vi.|倾斜，摇晃；狂奔，疾驰|To lean, sway, or tip to one side, especially while in motion; to rush headlong or carelessly.|The ship careened violently in the storm. 这艘船在暴风雨中剧烈地摇晃。|近义词：tilt, lean, lurch, sway, rush, speed, career|反义词：steady, stabilize|词性:动词

careen:/kəˈriːn/| vt.|使倾斜，使摇晃|To cause (a ship or boat) to lean over on one side for cleaning or repair.|They careened the ship to repair the hull. 他们使船倾斜以便修理船体。|近义词：tilt, heel, list|反义词：right, straighten|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cariner*, "to lay a ship on its side," from Italian *carina*, "keel," from Late Latin *carina*, from Latin *carus*, "dear, loved."  The semantic shift likely occurred through the association of the keel with the action of leaning a ship to expose it.
