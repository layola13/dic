balustrade:/ˌbæləˈstreɪd/| n. |栏杆，扶手|A railing supported by balusters, especially one forming an edge to a balcony, bridge, or terrace.|The balustrade of the balcony was intricately carved.  阳台的栏杆雕刻精美。|Synonyms: railing, banister, parapet|Antonyms: None|Noun

balustrade:/ˌbæləˈstreɪd/| n. |栏杆，扶手|A structure like a fence made of wood, stone, or metal, that is built along the edge of stairs, a balcony, etc. to prevent people from falling.|They leaned against the balustrade and looked out over the city. 他们倚着栏杆，俯瞰着整个城市。|Synonyms: handrail, guardrail|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *balustrade*, from Italian *balaustrata*, from *balaustro* "baluster," of uncertain origin, perhaps from Late Latin *balaustium* "flower of the wild pomegranate," from Greek *balaustion*.  The shape of a baluster is thought to resemble the half-open flower of a pomegranate.
