impropriation:/ɪmˌproʊpriˈeɪʃən/
|n.|挪用，私吞；（尤指教会财产的）世俗化|The act of taking something, especially church property, for one's own use, typically without permission or right.|The impropriation of church lands during the Reformation enriched many noble families.|近义词：misappropriation, embezzlement, seizure, confiscation|反义词：restitution, return|词性:名词

词源:
From Late Latin *impropriatio*, from impropriare ‘to make one’s own, convert to private use’, from in- ‘into’ + proprius ‘one’s own’.
