idealizations:/ˌaɪdiːələˈzeɪʃənz/| n. |理想化；理想化的事物|The process of making something seem perfect or better than it really is, or the result of this process. |Her book presents an idealization of rural life. 她的书呈现了对乡村生活的理想化。|近义词：glorifications, romanticizations, abstractions|反义词：realities, facts|词性:noun

idealization:/ˌaɪdiːələˈzeɪʃən/| n. |理想化；理想化的事物|The process of making something seem perfect or better than it really is, or the result of this process. |His childhood memories were an idealization of the past. 他童年记忆是对过去的理想化。|近义词：glorification, romanticization, abstraction|反义词：reality, fact|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From idealize + -ation.  Idealize comes from ideal + -ize. Ideal traces back to the Late Latin ideālis, meaning "pertaining to an idea," from Latin idea "idea, form, semblance," from Greek idéa "form, pattern," from idein "to see."
