contingently:/kənˈtɪndʒəntli/
| adv. | 依情况而定地，偶然地 | depending on something else that might or might not happen | The trip is contingently scheduled for next week, depending on the weather.  旅行暂定于下周，取决于天气情况。 | Example:  The deal will go ahead contingently on approval by the board. 这笔交易能否进行取决于董事会的批准。| Synonyms: conditionally, provisionally, subject to | Antonyms: unconditionally, absolutely, definitely |词性: 副词

contingent:/kənˈtɪndʒənt/
| adj. | 依情况而定的，偶然发生的；可能的；有条件的| dependent for existence, occurrence, character, etc., on something not yet certain; conditional | Our plans are contingent on the weather. 我们的计划取决于天气。| Example:  The success of the project is contingent upon securing funding. 该项目的成功取决于能否获得资金。| Synonyms: dependent, conditional, subject to | Antonyms: independent, unconditional, absolute | 词性: 形容词

contingent:/kənˈtɪndʒənt/
| n. | 偶然事件；小分队；代表团 | a group of people representing a larger group | The university sent a large contingent to the conference. 大学派出了一个大型代表团参加会议。| Example: The French contingent was smaller than usual this year. 今年法国代表团比往年规模小。| Synonyms: group, detachment, delegation | Antonyms: whole, entirety | 词性: 名词
