conservatist: /kənˈsɜːrvətɪst/ | n. | 保守主义者；保守派人士 | A person who is averse to change and holds to traditional values and attitudes, typically in politics. | Example: He's a staunch conservatist who believes in limited government intervention. 他是一位坚定的保守主义者，信奉有限的政府干预。 | Example: The conservatists opposed the proposed reforms. 保守派人士反对提议的改革。| Synonym: traditionalist, right-winger, reactionary | Antonym: liberal, progressive, radical | Noun


conservatism: /kənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/ | n. | 保守主义 | A political philosophy based on tradition and social stability, emphasizing established institutions and preferring gradual development to abrupt change. | Example:  Conservatism is a dominant force in many Western societies. 保守主义是许多西方社会的主导力量。| Example:  Her conservatism prevented her from embracing new ideas. 她的保守主义使她无法接受新思想。 | Synonym: traditionalism, orthodoxy | Antonym: liberalism, radicalism | Noun


conservative: /kənˈsɜːrvətɪv/ | adj. | 保守的；谨慎的 | Holding to traditional attitudes and values and cautious about change or innovation, typically in relation to politics or religion. | Example: She has a conservative approach to investment. 她对投资持保守态度。 | Example:  He's a conservative dresser, always in a suit and tie.  他穿着保守，总是西装革履。 | Synonym: traditional, cautious, prudent | Antonym: liberal, radical, adventurous | Adjective
