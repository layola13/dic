ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *glisteren, *glistren, from Old English *glisian (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *glisjaną (“to shine, gleam, glisten”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵleys- (“to shine”). Cognate with Scots glister (“to glitter, glisten”), West Frisian glisterje (“to glitter”), Dutch glinsteren (“to glitter, sparkle”), German glimmern, glinstern (“to glimmer, glitter”), Swedish glimma (“to gleam, glitter”), Icelandic glita (“to glitter”). Related to glide.
