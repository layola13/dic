flurried:/ˈflɜːrid/ (US), /ˈflʌrid/ (UK)| adj.|慌乱的，慌张的；激动不安的；(雪)纷飞的|Characterized by a sudden burst of activity or emotion; agitated and confused.|She was flurried by the unexpected guests. 她被突如其来的客人弄得慌乱不堪。

The actress was flurried by the paparazzi.  这位女演员被狗仔队弄得心烦意乱。|近义词：agitated, nervous, rushed, hectic, flustered|反义词：calm, composed, tranquil|词性:形容词

flurry:/ˈflɜːri/ (US), /ˈflʌri/ (UK)| n.|一阵，一 gusts; a brief period of excitement or activity|A flurry of snow fell.  下了一阵雪。

There was a flurry of activity in the kitchen as the chefs prepared for the dinner rush.  厨师们为晚餐高峰做准备，厨房里一阵忙碌。|近义词：burst, rush, spate, flutter|反义词：lull, calm|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "flurry," which originated in the late 18th century.  It is likely of imitative origin, perhaps related to words like "flutter" and "flitter," suggesting quick, light movements.
