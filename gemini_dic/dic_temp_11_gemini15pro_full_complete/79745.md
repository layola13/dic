glumelle:/ɡluːˈmɛl/| n.|颖片，外稃|One of two empty bracts (glumes) below the floret in grasses.  It is the lower of the two bracts that enclose a grass floret. It's often larger and coarser than the palea (the upper bract).|The glumelle of this grass species has a distinctive awn.|近义词:lemma, lower bract|反义词:palea, upper bract|词性:noun
Etymology: From French glumelle, diminutive of glume (glume), ultimately from Latin glūma (husk, chaff).
