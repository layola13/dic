Bábism: /ˈbɑːbɪzəm/ | n. | 巴比教 | A religious movement founded in Persia (Iran) in 1844 by Siyyid `Alí Muḥammad Shírází, who took the title of the Báb (meaning "the Gate"). It emphasized spiritual and moral reform and paved the way for the Baháʼí Faith. | The Bábism attracted a significant following in Persia despite facing persecution. 巴比教在波斯吸引了大量的追随者，尽管面临迫害。 |  The Báb's writings are considered sacred scriptures by Bábís and Baháʼís. 巴孛的著作被巴比教徒和巴哈伊教徒视为圣典。 | Synonyms: Bábí faith, Bayání faith | Antonyms: None | Noun
