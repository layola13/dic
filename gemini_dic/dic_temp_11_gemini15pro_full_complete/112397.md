lost-out: /lɒst ˈaʊt/ (British) /lɔːst ˈaʊt/ (American) | phrasal verb | 输掉; 错失良机; 未能得到 | to not get something you wanted or expected, or to be defeated in a competition |  He lost out on a promotion. 他错失了升职的机会。 |  missed out on, failed to secure | secured, won | verb phrase

lost-out: /lɒst ˈaʊt/ (British) /lɔːst ˈaʊt/ (American) | adjective |  失败的; 亏损的 (less common usage) | Describes someone who has experienced a loss or setback. | He felt lost-out after the deal fell through.  交易失败后，他感到很失落。| defeated, unsuccessful | successful, winning | adjective
