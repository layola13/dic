hither-and-yon:/ˈhɪðər ənd ˈjɒn/ (British), /ˈhɪðər ən ˈjɑːn/ (American)| adv.|到处；四处； hither and thither|Moving hither and yon, the restless child never stayed in one place for long.  到处乱跑，这个好动的孩子在一个地方待不久。|The butterflies flitted hither and yon among the flowers. 蝴蝶在花丛中飞来飞去。|近义词：here and there, everywhere, all over, back and forth|反义词: nowhere, in one place|词性:副词

Etymology:
From Middle English *hider and yon*, equivalent to *hider* (“hither”) + *and* + *yon* (“yon”). *Hider* is from Old English *hider*, from Proto-Germanic *hidrē,* from Proto-Indo-European *ḱi-dʰr̥-é,* meaning "to this place." *Yon* is from Old English *ġeon*, from Proto-Germanic *jainaz,* of uncertain origin.
