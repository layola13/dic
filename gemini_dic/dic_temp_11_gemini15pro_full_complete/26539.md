cabin:/ˈkæbɪn/ (US), /ˈkæbɪn/ (UK)| n.|小屋，船舱，机舱|A small, simple house, especially one built of wood; a private room on a ship or boat; the area for passengers in an aircraft.|例句：They spent the summer in a cabin by the lake. 他们在湖边的小屋里度过了夏天。|近义词：cottage, hut, shed, compartment, berth|反义词: mansion, palace|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *cabine*, diminutive of *cape*, from Late Latin *capa* "cloak, hooded cape." The sense evolution was from "small room," then to "hut," and finally to "cabin."
