atomisms:/ˈætəmɪzəmz/ | n. |原子论；原子主义|The doctrine that all reality is composed of simple, indivisible units called atoms, or that reality can be explained in terms of such units. It can refer to physical atomism (the view that physical matter is composed of atoms) or to social or political atomism (the view that society or the state is composed of individuals who are fundamentally separate and independent).|He subscribed to a strict atomism, believing that all complex phenomena could be reduced to their basic components.|近义词: individualism, particularism |反义词: holism, collectivism |词性: noun

atomism:/ˈætəmɪzəm/ | n. |原子论；原子主义|The theory that all matter is made up of tiny indivisible particles called atoms.  It can also refer to a social or political philosophy emphasizing individual rights and self-reliance.|Her political philosophy leaned towards atomism, emphasizing individual liberty above all else.|近义词: individualism, reductionism |反义词: holism, organicism |词性: noun
