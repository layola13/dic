maunder:/ˈmɔːndər/ (British), /ˈmɑːndər/ (American)| v. n.| 闲逛；抱怨；絮叨；低沉地说|To talk in a rambling, indistinct way; to wander slowly and idly; to grumble.| He maundered on about his childhood. 他絮絮叨叨地讲起了他的童年。|近义词：ramble, mutter, grumble, complain, wander, meander|反义词：articulate, speak clearly, be decisive, be direct|词性：动词, 名词

maunders:/ˈmɔːndərz/ (British), /ˈmɑːndərz/ (American)| n.| 闲逛；抱怨；絮叨|Plural form of "maunder," referring to instances of rambling or indistinct talk, or aimless wandering.|  His maunders about the weather were becoming tiresome. 他对天气的絮叨越来越烦人。|近义词： ramblings, mutterings, grumbles, complaints|反义词： pronouncements, declarations, clear statements|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->

From probably a dialectal variant of *maunder*, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to obsolete *mome*, meaning "fool," or to *maund*, meaning "basket," suggesting aimless wandering like a beggar with a basket.
