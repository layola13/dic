dread:/drɛd/| n. v. adj.|恐惧，害怕；令人恐惧的事物；担忧，焦虑|A feeling of great fear and anxiety.|例句：She felt a sense of dread as she approached the abandoned house. 她走近那栋废弃的房子时感到一阵恐惧。|近义词：fear, terror, anxiety, apprehension|反义词：courage, bravery, calmness, composure|词性：名词

dread:/drɛd/| v. |害怕，恐惧；担忧，焦虑|To feel extremely worried or frightened about something that is going to happen or that might happen.|例句：He dreaded going to the dentist. 他害怕去看牙医。|近义词：fear, be afraid of, worry about|反义词：look forward to, anticipate with pleasure|词性：动词

dread:/drɛd/| adj. |可怕的，令人恐惧的|Causing fear or dread; extremely unpleasant.|例句：The dread moment had finally arrived.  可怕的时刻终于到来了。|近义词：terrible, awful, dreadful, frightful|反义词：wonderful, pleasant, delightful|词性：形容词


词源：From Old English *drǣdan "to advise, counsel, urge; to fear, respect," from Proto-Germanic *drōzaną (source also of Old Saxon drādian "to threaten, fear," Old Frisian drēda, Middle Dutch and Dutch draden "to threaten," Old High German trātan "to threaten, be afraid," German drohen "to threaten"), from PIE root *dhrēgh- "to disturb, trouble."
