fractures:/ˈfræktʃərz/ (US), /ˈfræktʃəz/ (UK)
n.|骨折；断裂；裂缝|The act or process of breaking or the state of being broken.  A crack or break, especially in a bone or other hard material.|
例句：He suffered multiple fractures in the accident.  他在事故中多处骨折。
例句：The earthquake caused fractures in the earth's crust. 地震导致地壳断裂。
近义词：breaks, cracks, ruptures
反义词：joins, mends, heals
词性: noun (plural)

fracture:/ˈfræktʃər/ (US), /ˈfræktʃə(r)/ (UK)
n.|骨折；断裂；裂缝|The act or process of breaking or the state of being broken.  A crack or break, especially in a bone or other hard material.|
例句：The X-ray revealed a hairline fracture in his wrist. X光显示他的手腕有 hairline fracture(细微的裂缝)。
例句：Stress fractures can occur in athletes.  运动员可能发生应力性骨折。
近义词：break, crack, rupture
反义词：join, mend, heal
词性: noun (singular)

fracture:/ˈfræktʃər/ (US), /ˈfræktʃə(r)/ (UK)
v.| (使)断裂，(使)折断|To break or crack (something hard, such as a bone). To (cause something to) separate into pieces as a result of a blow, shock, or strain.|
例句：He fractured his leg in a skiing accident. 他在一次滑雪事故中摔断了腿。
例句：The impact fractured the glass. 撞击使玻璃破碎。
近义词：break, crack, shatter
反义词：mend, repair, heal
词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *fracturen*, from Latin *fractūra* ("a breach, break"), from *fractus*, past participle of *frangere* ("to break").
