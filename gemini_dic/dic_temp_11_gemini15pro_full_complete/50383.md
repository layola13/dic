deporting:/dɪˈpɔːtɪŋ/
verb
驱逐出境; 将…递解
the act of forcing someone to leave a country, usually because they have broken the law or their permission to stay there has ended
The government is deporting illegal immigrants. 政府正在驱逐非法移民。

deporting:/dɪˈpɔːtɪŋ/
Verb
递解; 驱逐
The act of officially forcing someone to leave a country
They are deporting him back to his own country. 他们正将他驱逐回国。

deporting:/dɪˈpɔːtɪŋ/
Gerund or present participle
驱逐出境；递解
The action of forcing someone to leave a country, especially because they do not have the legal right to be there.
The authorities are deporting illegal immigrants. 当局正在驱逐非法移民。


Synonyms:expelling, banishing, extraditing, removing
Antonyms:admitting, welcoming, naturalizing
Etymology: From Old French *deporter, from Latin deportare (“to carry away”)
