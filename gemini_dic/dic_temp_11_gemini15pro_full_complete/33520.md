ciceronianism: /ˌsɪsəˈroʊniənɪzəm/ | n. | 西塞罗主义；雄辩的风格 | A style of writing or speaking characterized by elaborate, flowing periods, balanced constructions, and copious use of classical allusions, reminiscent of the Roman orator Cicero. |  His speeches were filled with Ciceronianism, impressing the audience with their eloquence. 他的演讲充满了西塞罗式的雄辩，其口才给听众留下了深刻的印象。| synonyms: eloquence, rhetoric, oratory | antonyms: terseness, conciseness | noun
