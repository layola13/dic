gallows:/ˈɡæləʊz/ (US) /ˈɡæləʊz/ (UK)| n.|绞刑架；危险的境地|A structure for hanging criminals.|The condemned man was led to the gallows.|scaffold, gibbet|n/a|noun
gallows:/ˈɡæləʊz/ (US) /ˈɡæləʊz/ (UK)| adj.|(古语，罕用) 灾难性的；不祥的|Causing or deserving death by hanging; ill-omened.|He had a gallows humor about his situation.|inauspicious, ominous|auspicious, fortunate|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *galga, from Proto-Germanic *galgon, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰolgos ("pole, stick"). Cognate with Old Norse galgi ("gallows"), Dutch galg ("gallows"), German Galgen ("gallows").
