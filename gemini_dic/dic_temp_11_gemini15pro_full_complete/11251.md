archpriest:/ˈɑːrtʃˌpriːst/ | n. |大教长，首席司祭|A chief priest; a priest of high rank. |The archpriest presided over the important ceremony. 大教长主持了这项重要的仪式。| The archpriest delivered a moving sermon. 大教长发表了一篇感人的布道。 |Synonyms: chief priest, high priest, primate |Antonyms: layman |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *archepreest*, from Old French *archeprestre*, from Medieval Latin *archipresbyter*, from Ancient Greek *ἀρχιπρεσβύτερος (arkhipresbúteros)*, from *ἀρχι- (arkhi-, “chief”) + πρεσβύτερος (presbúteros, “elder, priest”)*.
