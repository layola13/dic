immunize:/ˈɪmjʊnaɪz/ | v. |免疫，使免疫|To make someone immune to a particular disease, usually by injecting them with a vaccine.|例句：The doctor immunized the children against measles. 医生给孩子们接种了麻疹疫苗。|例句：They are campaigning to immunize children against the disease. 他们正在开展运动，为儿童接种疫苗以预防这种疾病。 |近义词：vaccinate, inoculate|反义词: infect, expose|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin immunis "exempt, free, not paying a share" + -ize.  The original meaning related to exemption from taxes or public service.  The medical sense of making resistant to disease developed later.
