crack:/kræk/
n.|裂缝，裂纹|A line on the surface of something where it has broken or split, but not into separate parts.|There was a crack in the window. 窗户上有一道裂纹。
n.|破裂声，爆裂声|A sudden sharp or explosive noise.|The crack of a whip. 鞭子的噼啪声。
n.|俏皮话，笑话|A joke, typically a critical or unkind one.|He made a crack about her weight. 他拿她的体重开玩笑。
n.|尝试，努力|An attempt.|I'll have a crack at fixing it. 我会试着修理它。
v.|（使）破裂，（使）裂开|To break or cause to break without complete separation of the parts.|The ice cracked as I stepped on it. 我一踩冰就裂开了。
v.|（使）发出爆裂声|To make a sudden sharp or explosive noise.|The whip cracked. 鞭子发出噼啪声。
v.|破解，攻破|To find the solution to a problem or code.|They cracked the code. 他们破解了密码。
v.|（精神或身体）崩溃，垮掉|To become mentally and emotionally stressed, especially to the extent of losing control.|She cracked under the pressure. 她承受不住压力崩溃了。
adj.|一流的，顶尖的|Excellent, first-rate.|He's a crack shot. 他是个神枪手。

词源:From Middle English crakken, of uncertain origin. Possibly from Old English *cracian (“to crack, creak”).

近义词:(n.) fissure, split, fracture, burst, joke, witticism, attempt, try; (v.) break, snap, split, explode, decipher, solve, collapse, crumble; (adj.) excellent, superb, top-notch
反义词:(n.) mend, repair; (v.) fix, join, heal; (adj.) poor, inferior, mediocre

词性: 名词, 动词, 形容词
