courtroom:/ˈkɔːrtruːm/ |ˈkɔːrtˌrum/| n. |法庭，审判室|A room in which a court of law is held.|The courtroom was packed with reporters.|court, chamber, tribunal| N/A |noun
Etymology:  From 'court' (meaning a judicial assembly) + 'room'.  'Court' ultimately derives from the Latin 'cohors', meaning 'enclosed yard', which evolved to refer to the king's court and then to judicial assemblies.
