balky:/ˈbɔːlki/ | adj. | 倔强的，不愿合作的，不肯动的 | Refusing to move or act as desired; stubborn. | The balky mule refused to move. 这头倔强的骡子拒绝移动。| The old engine was balky and difficult to start.  这台旧引擎很难启动，总是不听使唤。 | 近义词: stubborn, obstinate, uncooperative, recalcitrant, refractory | 反义词: cooperative, compliant, willing, amenable | 词性: adjective

词源:
From balk (v.) + -y (suffix forming adjectives).  Balk (v.) comes from Middle English balken "to leave unplowed," from Old Norse *balkr "ridge of land between furrows," related to Old English balca "ridge." The sense development is from the idea of stopping at a ridge or obstacle, to refusing to proceed.
