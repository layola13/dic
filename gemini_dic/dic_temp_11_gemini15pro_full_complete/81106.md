grammarianism: /ˌɡræˈmɛəriənɪzəm/ | n. | 语法癖; 语法学究; 语法至上主义 | Excessive concern with the rules of grammar or a belief that strict adherence to prescribed grammar is essential for good writing.  It often refers to a pedantic or overly rigid approach to language. | Her grammarianism made it difficult for her to enjoy casual conversation. 她对语法的执着让她很难享受轻松的对话。 |  He corrected every minor grammatical error, his grammarianism on full display.  他纠正了每一个细微的语法错误，充分展现了他的语法癖。 |  pedantry, purism, formalism | informality, flexibility | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From grammar + -ian + -ism.  "Grammar" comes from Old French "gramaire," ultimately from Greek "gramma" meaning "letter."  "-ian" is a suffix denoting "one skilled in" or "adherent of." "-ism" is a suffix indicating a doctrine, belief, or practice.
