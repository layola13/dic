epanodos:/ɪˈpænədɒs/ | n. | 回环往复，反复 |A rhetorical figure of repetition in which the same word or phrase is repeated at the beginning and middle and end of a clause or sentence. |Example 1:  “Rejoice in the Lord always: and again I say, Rejoice.” (Philippians 4:4) 总是要欢喜快乐。我再说，要欢喜快乐。(腓立比书 4:4)

Example 2: "Nothing is worse than doing nothing."  没有什么比什么都不做更糟糕的了。|Synonym: repetition, reiteration, anadiplosis |Antonym: brevity, conciseness | noun

(Epanodos doesn't typically form set phrases in the same way other words do because it's a rhetorical device.  It's more about the structure within a sentence than a phrase itself.  Therefore, instead of phrases, I am providing examples of its structural usage within a sentence):


1.  "The king is dead, long live the king!" 国王死了，国王万岁！（虽然不是严格意义上的epanodos，但体现了重复的核心概念）
2. "Fair is foul, and foul is fair."  美好的是丑陋的，丑陋的是美好的。(麦克白)
3. "Blood will have blood."  血债血偿。(麦克白)
4. "An eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth." 以眼还眼，以牙还牙。（虽然起源于古代法律，但体现了重复的修辞效果）
5.  "Love is love." 爱就是爱。（现代用法，强调包容性，也体现了重复）
