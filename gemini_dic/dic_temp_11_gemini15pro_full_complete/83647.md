haemophiliac:/ˌhɪːməˈfɪliæk/ /ˌhiməˈfɪliæk/ | n. | 血友病患者 | A person who has haemophilia, a medical condition in which the blood does not clot normally. | Example: The haemophiliac had to be careful to avoid injuries.  这位血友病患者必须小心避免受伤。| Example:  Haemophiliacs require regular treatment to manage their condition. 血友病患者需要定期治疗以控制病情。 | Synonym: bleeder | Antonym: (No direct antonym for a person. The antonym for the condition would be normal blood clotting) | Noun

haemophilic:/ˌhɪːməˈfɪlɪk/ /ˌhiməˈfɪlɪk/ | adj. | 血友病的 | Relating to or affected with haemophilia. | Example: Haemophilic patients are at risk of excessive bleeding. 血友病患者有过度出血的风险。 | Example:  She inherited the haemophilic gene from her mother. 她从母亲那里遗传了血友病基因。| Synonym:  bleeding (informal) | Antonym: (No direct antonym. The opposite condition would be having normal blood clotting) | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *haima* "blood" + *philia* "loving."  Refers to the tendency to bleed (love of blood).
