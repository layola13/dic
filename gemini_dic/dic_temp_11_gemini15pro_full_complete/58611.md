dulls:/dʌlz/| v.| 使变钝; 使迟钝; 减轻; 变得迟钝; 失去光泽| Makes less sharp, intense, or bright; becomes less sharp, intense, or bright. | The rain dulled the shine on my car. 雨水使我的车失去了光泽。|近义词：blunts, weakens, dims, fades|反义词:sharpens, brightens, intensifies|词性:动词

dull:/dʌl/| adj.| 钝的; 不锋利的; 迟钝的; 阴暗的; 枯燥的; 无聊的| Not sharp; slow to understand; lacking brightness or interest; boring.| The knife was too dull to cut the bread. 这把刀太钝了，切不了面包。|近义词：blunt, obtuse, boring, drab|反义词:sharp, bright, interesting, exciting|词性:形容词

Etymology: From Old English *dol (attested only in compounds), from Proto-Germanic *dulaz. Cognate with Old High German tulli "dull," Dutch dol "mad, foolish," German toll "mad, amazing, great." The original sense was probably "foolish, slow-witted."
