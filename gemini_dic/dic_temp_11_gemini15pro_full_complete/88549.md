hitch:/hɪtʃ/| n. vt. vi.|故障，障碍；猛拉，系住；搭便车|A temporary problem or difficulty that causes a short delay.  To fasten something to something else with a rope, hook, etc. To get a free ride in someone else's vehicle.|The project hit a hitch when funding was withdrawn.  项目在资金撤回时遇到了障碍。 He hitched the trailer to his car. 他把拖车挂在他的车上。 She hitched a ride to the next town. 她搭便车去了下一个城镇。|近义词：problem, snag, obstacle (n.); fasten, attach, tie (vt.);  thumb a ride (vi.)|反义词：solution, advantage (n.); unfasten, detach (vt.)|词性:名词,动词
Etymology: From Old English *hiccian "to jerk, move convulsively," related to Dutch hikken "to hiccup," German hicken "to hop."  The meaning shifted from "jerk" to "fasten with a jerk" and then to the various modern senses.
