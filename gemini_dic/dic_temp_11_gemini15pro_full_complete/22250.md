bondslave:/ˈbɒndsleɪv/ | n. | 奴隶，契约奴隶 | A person held in servitude, especially one bound by contract or law. |  The king treated his captives as bondslaves, forcing them to work in the mines. 国王把俘虏当作奴隶，强迫他们在矿井里干活。 |  slave, serf, captive | freeman, citizen | noun

Etymology:
From bond + slave.  "Bond" in this context refers to a binding agreement or contract.  "Slave" refers to a person who is the legal property of another and is forced to obey them.
