chuck-out:/ˈtʃʌk aʊt/| n.|扔掉的东西；逐出；清扫|Something discarded; an ejection; a clearing out|The annual chuck-out of old clothes raised money for charity.  年度旧衣清理为慈善机构筹集了资金。|Synonyms: discard, ejection, eviction, clearance|Antonyms: keeping, acceptance, admission|noun


chuck-out:/ˈtʃʌk aʊt/| vt.|丢弃；驱逐|To discard; to expel|They chucked out the old furniture. 他们把旧家具扔掉了。|Synonyms: discard, throw away, eject, evict|Antonyms: keep, retain, admit|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
"Chuck" originates from the Old North French word "choquer," meaning "to jolt or shake." This evolved into the Middle English "chuken," meaning "to throw."  The "out" particle simply adds the sense of outward motion or removal.

PHRASE->
chuck something out the window:  放弃某事，不再考虑  He chucked the whole project out the window after the funding fell through.  资金不足后，他放弃了整个项目。
chuck someone out (of somewhere):  把某人赶出去 The bouncer chucked him out of the club for being too drunk.  保镖把他赶出了俱乐部，因为他喝得太醉了。
be chucked out: 被解雇或开除 He was chucked out of his job for misconduct. 他因为行为不端而被解雇了。
chuck it in: 放弃，停止做某事  I'm chucking it in - I can't take this stress anymore. 我不干了——我再也受不了这种压力了。
