downtowner:/ˌdaʊnˈtaʊnər/| n.|都市人，市中心居民|A resident of the downtown area of a city, often implying a sophisticated or fashionable lifestyle.|She's a real downtowner; she loves the hustle and bustle of city life. 她是个真正的都市人；她热爱都市生活的喧嚣。|近义词：city dweller, urbanite|反义词：suburbanite, rural resident|词性:noun

Etymology:  "downtown" + "-er" (suffix forming agent nouns). "Downtown" itself originated in the late 19th century in American English, referring to the central business district of a city, often situated lower geographically than the surrounding residential areas.
