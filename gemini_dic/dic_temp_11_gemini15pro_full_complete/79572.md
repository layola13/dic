gloomier: /ˈɡluːmiər/ (US) /ˈɡluːmiə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更阴沉的，更忧郁的|Comparative form of gloomy: more gloomy.  It describes something that is dark, depressing, or pessimistic.|The weather is gloomier today than yesterday. 今天的天气比昨天更阴沉。|The economic forecast is gloomier than expected. 经济预测比预期的更悲观。|近义词：darker, drearier, more depressing, more pessimistic|反义词：brighter, cheerier, more optimistic|词性:形容词

gloomily: /ˈɡluːmɪli/ | adv. |阴郁地，忧郁地|In a gloomy manner. It describes how something is done or how someone behaves in a sad or depressing way.|He stared gloomily out the window. 他忧郁地盯着窗外。|She walked gloomily through the rain. 她沮丧地在雨中走着。|近义词：sadly, dejectedly, despondently|反义词：happily, cheerfully, brightly|词性:副词

gloominess: /ˈɡluːminəs/ | n. |阴沉，忧郁|The state or quality of being gloomy.  It refers to a dark, depressing, or pessimistic atmosphere or feeling.|The gloominess of the weather reflected his mood. 天气的阴沉反映了他的心情。|The gloominess of the economic situation is concerning. 经济形势的黯淡令人担忧。|近义词：darkness, dreariness, depression, pessimism|反义词：brightness, cheerfulness, optimism|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *glome, from Old English *glom, meaning “twilight, dusk.” Related to Old High German glāmo (“twilight”). The sense of "depression, despondency" developed later.
