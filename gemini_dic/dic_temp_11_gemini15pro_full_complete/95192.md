inaugural:/ɪˈnɔːɡjərəl/| adj. n.|就职的，开幕的；就职典礼|Marking the beginning of an institution, activity, or period; relating to a ceremony in which someone formally takes a position of authority. |The president's inaugural address was inspiring. 总统的就职演说鼓舞人心。|inaugural ball 就职舞会|Synonyms: opening, initial, first, introductory|Antonyms: concluding, final, closing|adjective

inaugural:/ɪˈnɔːɡjərəl/| n.|就职演说，开幕词|An inaugural speech or ceremony. |The inaugural was held in the grand hall. 就职典礼在大会堂举行。|inaugural address 就职演说|Synonyms:  initiation, commencement, debut|Antonyms: conclusion, finale|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin inauguralis, from Latin inaugurare (“to inaugurate”), from in- (“in, on”) + augurare (“to practice augury, to foretell”), from augur (“augur, diviner”).  The original sense related to taking auspices before entering office.
