interpolation:/ˌɪnˈtɜːrpoʊˈleɪʃn/ /ɪnˌtɜːpəˈleɪʃn/ | n. |插入；插值；窜改|The act of inserting something of a different nature into something else.  The insertion of something spurious into a text or conversation.|He interpolates a comment into the conversation. 他在谈话中插了一句。| 近义词:insertion, addition, interjection|反义词:deletion, removal, omission|词性:名词

etymology->
From Late Latin interpolātiō, from interpolāre (“to refurbish, polish up, alter, falsify”), from inter- (“between”) + polīre (“to polish”).
