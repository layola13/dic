have a crow to pluck with someone:/hæv ə kroʊ tə plʌk wɪð ˈsʌmwʌn/ | idiom | 与某人有争论；与某人算账 | To have a grievance with someone that needs to be discussed, often angrily. It implies a need to confront someone about something they have done wrong. |  I have a crow to pluck with you about the damage you did to my car. 我要和你谈谈你对我车造成的损害。| 近义词: have a bone to pick with someone, have it out with someone, confront someone, settle a score | 反义词: be on good terms with someone, be friends with someone | phrase

PHRASE->
have a crow to pluck with someone = 与某人有争论
a crow to pluck =  一件需要解决的事情（通常指不愉快的事情）
pluck a crow =  解决争端
not have a crow to pluck with someone = 与某人和睦相处
have more than one crow to pluck with someone =  与某人有多个争端


词源:  The idiom's origin is uncertain, but it likely refers to the tedious and unpleasant task of plucking the feathers from a crow, which was considered a worthless bird. This tedious task is likened to the unpleasant confrontation implied by the idiom.
