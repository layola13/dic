buccaneering:/ˌbʌkəˈnɪərɪŋ/
noun|海盗行为；冒险精神；大胆进取|The buccaneering spirit of the early explorers drove them to discover new lands. 早期探险家的冒险精神驱使他们发现新的土地。|
adjective|海盗似的；冒险的；大胆进取的|His buccaneering business tactics led to rapid growth but also considerable controversy. 他大胆冒险的商业策略带来了快速增长，但也引起了相当大的争议。|
Etymology: From buccaneer (海盗), from French boucanier, from boucan (烤肉架), referring to the pirates' practice of smoking meat on a boucan.  源自海盗（buccaneer），来自法语 boucanier，来自 boucan（烤肉架），指的是海盗在烤肉架上熏肉的做法。|
Synonyms: adventurous, daring, enterprising, audacious, swashbuckling, piratical|
Antonyms: cautious, timid, conservative, conventional|
Word Type: Noun, Adjective
