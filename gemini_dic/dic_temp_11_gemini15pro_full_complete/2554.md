admissiblenesses:/ədˌmɪsɪˈbɪlɪtɪz/| n. |可采纳性；可接纳性；可容许性|The quality or state of being admissible, especially in a court of law.  This often refers to the suitability of evidence or testimony to be considered in a legal proceeding.|The admissibleness of the evidence was questioned by the defense attorney.  辩护律师对证据的可采纳性提出了质疑。|Synonyms: acceptability, allowability|Antonyms: inadmissibility, unacceptability|noun
