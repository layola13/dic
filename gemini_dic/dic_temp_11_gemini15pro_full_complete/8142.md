anti-choice:/ˌæntiˈtʃɔɪs/| adj.| 反堕胎的，反对选择权的| Opposed to the legal right to have an abortion.  Describes individuals or organizations that advocate against a woman's right to choose whether or not to have an abortion. |  The anti-choice movement has gained momentum in recent years.  反堕胎运动近年来势头强劲。|  The politician's anti-choice stance alienated many voters. 这位政客的反堕胎立场疏远了许多选民。|近义词：pro-life|反义词: pro-choice|词性:形容词


Etymology: "anti-" (against) + "choice" (the act of selecting or making a decision).  The term gained prominence in the late 20th century as a label for those opposing abortion rights.
