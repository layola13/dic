brutalist: /ˈbruːtəlɪst/ | adj. |野兽派的，粗野主义的 | Relating to a style of architecture characterized by massive, imposing concrete structures, often with exposed surfaces and a lack of ornamentation. |  Example: The Barbican Centre in London is a famous example of brutalist architecture.  伦敦的巴比肯中心是野兽派建筑的著名例子。

Example:  Many people find brutalist buildings ugly and oppressive. 很多人觉得野兽派建筑丑陋而压抑。 | 近义词: austere, harsh, severe | 反义词: ornate, decorated, embellished | 词性: adjective

词源: From French *brutaliste*, from *brut* (“raw, crude”) (from Latin *brutus* (“dull, senseless, irrational, brute”)).  Coined by the British architectural critic Reyner Banham.
