eclectic:/ɪˈklɛktɪk/
adjective
折衷的，兼容并蓄的
deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.
She has an eclectic taste in music, enjoying everything from classical to hip-hop. 她对音乐的品味很广泛，从古典到嘻哈都喜欢。
synonyms: diverse, varied, broad, wide-ranging, comprehensive
antonyms: narrow, limited, restricted, specific, homogenous
词性: 形容词


eclectic:/ɪˈklɛktɪk/
noun
折衷主义者
a person who derives ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.
He's an eclectic, drawing inspiration from various philosophies and religions. 他是一个折衷主义者，从各种哲学和宗教中汲取灵感。
synonyms: collector, connoisseur, enthusiast
antonyms: specialist, purist


词源:
From Late Latin *eclecticus, from Ancient Greek ἐκλεκτικός (eklektikós, “selective, picking out”), from ἐκλεκτός (eklektós, “picked out, select”), from ἐκλέγω (eklégō, “I pick out, select”), from ἐκ (ek, “out”) + λέγω (légō, “I gather, choose”).
