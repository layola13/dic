half-demolished:/ˌhɑːf dɪˈmɒlɪʃt/
half-demolished| adj.|半拆除的，部分拆毁的|partly demolished; torn down or wrecked to a partial extent.  It describes something that has been partially destroyed through demolition.|The half-demolished building stood as a stark reminder of the war.|Synonyms: partially demolished, partly wrecked, half-ruined|Antonyms: intact, complete, fully constructed
half-demolished|词性|形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "half" (Old English healf) + "demolished" (from French démolir, from Latin demoliri, from de- "down" + moliri "to build," related to moles "mass, structure").
