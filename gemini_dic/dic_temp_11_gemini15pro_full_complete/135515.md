nonplausibility:/ˌnɑːnˌplɔːzəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 不可信，难以置信 |The nonplausibility of his alibi made the police suspicious. |  His story lacked plausibility and was ultimately dismissed. | Synonyms: implausibility, incredibility, unbelievability | Antonyms: plausibility, credibility, believability | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + plausibility.  "Plausibility" comes from Late Latin plausibilis "deserving applause, acceptable," from Latin plausus, past participle of plaudere "to clap, applaud."  The prefix "non-" simply negates the meaning.
