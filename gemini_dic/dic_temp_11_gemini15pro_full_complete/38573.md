concomitancy:/kənˈkɒmɪtənsi/ /kənˈkɑːmɪtənsi/
noun
伴随，并发
The state or quality of existing or occurring together with something else.
The concomitancy of economic growth and environmental protection is a key challenge for governments.  经济增长与环境保护的并存是各国政府面临的关键挑战。
The study explored the concomitancy of stress and illness. 这项研究探讨了压力与疾病的伴随关系。
近义词: coexistence, concurrence, accompaniment
反义词:  dissociation, separation

concomitant:/kənˈkɒmɪtənt/ /kənˈkɑːmɪtənt/
adjective
伴随的，并发的
Naturally accompanying or associated.
Weight gain is a concomitant symptom of this medication. 体重增加是这种药物的伴随症状。
The rise in crime was concomitant with increasing unemployment. 犯罪率的上升伴随着失业率的上升。
近义词:  concurrent, accompanying, attendant, associated
反义词:  unrelated, separate, independent

concomitant:/kənˈkɒmɪtənt/ /kənˈkɑːmɪtənt/
noun
伴随物，并发物
Something that accompanies or is collaterally connected with something else.
Loss of appetite is a frequent concomitant of chemotherapy. 食欲不振是化疗的常见伴随症状。
One of the concomitants of old age is often a decline in physical strength.  衰老的伴随物之一通常是体力下降。
近义词:  accompaniment, associate, corollary
反义词:  opposite
