de-democratized:/diː-dɪˈmɒkrətaɪzd/
|v. |去民主化；非民主化|To reverse the process of democratization; to make a system or institution less democratic.|The military coup effectively de-democratized the country, stripping citizens of their voting rights and silencing dissent.|近义词: undermined, weakened, subverted, authoritarianized|反义词: democratized, liberalized, empowered|词性:动词


de-democratized:/diː-dɪˈmɒkrətaɪzd/
|adj. |去民主化的；非民主化的|Having undergone a process of de-democratization; characterized by a reduction in democratic principles or practices.|The de-democratized state controlled all aspects of public life, suppressing free speech and assembly.|近义词: undemocratic, authoritarian, totalitarian|反义词: democratic, liberal, free|词性:形容词

词源:

"de-" (prefix):  From Latin, meaning "down, away from, off, reversal."  Indicates negation or reversal of an action.
"democratize" (verb): From "democracy," ultimately from Greek "dēmokratia," meaning "rule by the people."
