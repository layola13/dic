halfliberally: /ˌhæfˈlɪbərəli/
adjective|半自由地; 不完全自由地|Acting or thinking in a way that is only partially liberal, combining liberal principles with more conservative or traditional ones.|The government adopted a halfliberally policy on immigration, welcoming skilled workers but restricting family reunification.|近义词: moderately liberal, partially liberal|反义词: illiberal, completely liberal|词性:形容词

Etymology:
half- + liberally
half: From Old English *healfh, from Proto-Germanic *halbaz, from Proto-Indo-European *sel-.
liberally: From Latin līberālis (“of freedom, pertaining to freedom, befitting a free man”), from līber (“free”).
