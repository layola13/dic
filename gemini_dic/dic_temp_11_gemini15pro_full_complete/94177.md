imperate:/ɪmˈpɛreɪt/ | v. | 命令，指示 | To give an authoritative command; order. | The king imperated his soldiers to attack. 国王命令他的士兵进攻。

The law imperates that all citizens pay taxes. 法律规定所有公民都要纳税。| 近义词: command, order, direct, dictate | 反义词: request, plead, beg | verb

imperative:/ɪmˈpɛrətɪv/ | adj. | 必要的，紧急的，命令式的 | Absolutely necessary or required; expressing a command. | It is imperative that we act now. 我们现在必须采取行动。

"Stop!" he shouted in an imperative tone. “停下！”他用命令的语气喊道。| 近义词: crucial, essential, vital, urgent, mandatory | 反义词: optional, unnecessary, unimportant | adjective

imperative:/ɪmˈpɛreɪtɪv/ | n. | 命令，指示，必要的事 | A command; something that is essential or urgent. | The general issued an imperative to his troops. 将军向他的部队下达了命令。

Helping the homeless is a moral imperative. 帮助无家可归者是一种道德义务。| 近义词: command, order, directive, necessity | 反义词: option, choice | noun


词源: From Latin *imperatus*, past participle of *imperare* "to command," from *in-* "in, on" + *parare* "to prepare, procure."
