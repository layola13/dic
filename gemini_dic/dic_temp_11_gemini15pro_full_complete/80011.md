go-over-the-hill:/ˌɡoʊ oʊvər ðə ˈhɪl/| adj.|过气的；走下坡路的；年老的|To decline in strength, vigor, or appeal, especially because of age.  This idiom often implies that someone is past their prime or no longer as effective as they once were.|例句：He felt he was going over the hill when he couldn't keep up with the younger players.  他觉得自己跟不上年轻球员了，开始走下坡路了。

例句：The band's popularity went over the hill after their lead singer left.  主唱离开后，乐队的受欢迎程度就一落千丈了。|近义词：declining, aging, waning, fading, past one's prime|反义词：improving, flourishing, thriving|词性：形容词

go-over-the-hill (as a noun phrase - less common):/ˌɡoʊ oʊvər ðə ˈhɪl/| n.|衰退期；下坡路|The state of declining in strength, vigor, or appeal.|例句：His career is on the go-over-the-hill. 他的职业生涯正在走下坡路。

例句：Many athletes fear the go-over-the-hill. 很多运动员都害怕走下坡路。|近义词：decline, downturn|反义词：upturn, ascent|词性：名词短语
