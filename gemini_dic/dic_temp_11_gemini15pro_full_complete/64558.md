erroneous:/ɪˈroʊniəs/ | adj. | 错误的，不正确的 | Containing or based on error; incorrect. |例句：The report contained several erroneous statements. 报告中包含几个错误的陈述。|例句：His erroneous assumptions led to a disastrous outcome. 他错误的假设导致了灾难性的结果。|近义词：incorrect, mistaken, false, inaccurate |反义词：correct, accurate, true, right |词性:形容词

err:/ɜːr/ (British) /er/ (American) | v. | 犯错，出错 | To make a mistake or be incorrect. |例句：To err is human, to forgive divine. 人孰无过？|例句：He erred in his judgment of the situation. 他对形势的判断失误了。|近义词：mistake, blunder, slip up, lapse |反义词：succeed, achieve, triumph |词性:动词

errone:/əˈroʊn/ | n. |  错误；过失 (古语，现已很少使用) | A mistake or error (archaic). |例句：His errones were many and costly. 他的错误很多，代价也很大。(古语用法)|例句：Forgive us our errones.  宽恕我们的过错。(古语用法)|近义词：mistake, error, fault, blunder |反义词：correctness, accuracy |词性:名词

注意事项:  "errone" 作为名词已经非常古老，现在几乎不用了。  "err" 作为动词比 "erroneous" 作为形容词更常见。
