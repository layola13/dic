anesthesia: /ˌænɪsˈθiːʒə/ (US) /ˌænɪsˈθiːziə/ (UK)| n.|麻醉|A loss of feeling or awareness, especially so that surgery can be performed.|The patient was given general anesthesia before the operation. 病人在手术前进行了全身麻醉。|Synonyms: numbness, unconsciousness|Antonyms: sensation, consciousness|noun

anesthetic: /ˌænɪsˈθɛtɪk/ (US) /ˌænɪsˈθɛtɪk/ (UK)| n. adj.|麻醉剂；麻醉的|A substance that causes anesthesia.|The dentist used a local anesthetic to numb the patient's gums. 牙医使用局部麻醉剂来麻醉病人的牙龈。  The surgery was performed using a general anesthetic. 手术是在全身麻醉下进行的。|Synonyms: narcotic, painkiller (n.); numbing (adj.)|Antonyms: stimulant (n.); stimulating (adj.)|noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Modern Latin anaesthaesia, from Greek anaisthēsia "lack of sensation," from an- "without" + aisthēsis "sensation."
