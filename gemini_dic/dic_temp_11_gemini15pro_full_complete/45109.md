cuckooed:/ˈkʊkuːd/ | v. | 布谷鸟叫；鸠占鹊巢；强加于 | To make the sound of a cuckoo; to deposit (as an egg) in the nest of another bird as the cuckoo does; to foist (something unwanted) on someone else. | The cuckoo cuckooed loudly in the early morning.  清晨，布谷鸟大声地叫着。| Synonyms: imposed, foisted, planted | Antonyms: removed, withdrawn | Verb

cuckooed:/ˈkʊkuːd/ | adj. | (非正式)疯狂的，愚蠢的 | (informal) Crazy, foolish. |  He had some cuckooed idea about starting a business selling pet rocks. 他有一些关于开始销售宠物石头的疯狂想法。 | Synonyms: crazy, foolish, daft, barmy | Antonyms: sane, sensible, rational | Adjective
