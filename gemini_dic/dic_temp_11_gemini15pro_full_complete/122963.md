monachism: /ˈmɒnəkɪzəm/ (US) /ˈmʌnəkɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |修道主义，僧侣主义|The renunciation of worldly pursuits to fully devote oneself to spiritual work.  It involves living apart from society, often in monasteries or convents, and adhering to religious vows such as poverty, chastity, and obedience.|  Early Christian monachism flourished in the deserts of Egypt. 早期基督教的修道主义在埃及的沙漠中蓬勃发展。|  Monasticism is a central aspect of many religions. 修道主义是许多宗教的核心内容。 |synonyms: asceticism, monastic life, religious seclusion| antonyms: worldliness, secularism| noun
