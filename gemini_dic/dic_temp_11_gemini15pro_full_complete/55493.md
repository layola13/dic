distraction's:/dɪˈstrækʃənz/| n.|分心的事物；娱乐|Something that prevents someone from giving their attention to something else. |The TV was a constant distraction's.  电视一直让人分心。|Synonyms: interruption, disturbance, diversion|Antonyms: concentration, focus|noun
Etymology-> From Late Middle English distraccion, from Late Latin distractiō (“a pulling apart, separation”), from Latin distractus, past participle of distrahere (“to pull apart, separate, distract”), from dis- (“apart”) + trahere (“to pull, draw”).
