internalise:/ɪnˈtɜːnəlaɪz/ (UK) /ɪnˈtɜːrnəlaɪz/ (US)| v. |内化；使…内在化|To make (attitudes or beliefs) part of one's nature by learning or unconscious assimilation.|She had internalized her parents' values. 她已经内化了她父母的价值观。|Synonyms: assimilate, absorb, incorporate, integrate|Antonyms: externalize, reject, repudiate|verb

internalized:/ɪnˈtɜːnəlaɪzd/ (UK) /ɪnˈtɜːrnəlaɪzd/ (US)| adj. |内化的|Existing or occurring within the mind or consciousness; subjective.|He struggled with deeply internalized anger. 他与内心深处内化的愤怒作斗争。|Synonyms: ingrained, inherent, intrinsic|Antonyms: external, extrinsic, outward|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From internal + -ize.  The suffix "-ize" forms verbs meaning "to make or cause to be," "to subject to," "to become," "to behave like," or "to follow the example of."  "Internal" comes from Late Middle English, from late Latin *internālis, from Latin internus ‘inward’, from in- ‘into’ + -nus (forming adjectives).
