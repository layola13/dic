driftier: /ˈdrɪftiər/ | adj. | 更漂流的，更容易漂流的 | More prone to drifting; more subject to being carried along by currents of air or water. | The driftier the boat, the more difficult it is to control.  船越容易漂流，就越难控制。| The fog made the air driftier, and sounds seemed to come from nowhere.  雾使空气更加飘忽不定，声音似乎不知从何而来。 | 近义词: aimless, afloat, adrift, drifting, rootless, unsettled | 反义词: anchored, fixed, moored, stable, steady | 词性: 形容词

词源: drift (n.) + -ier (comparative adjective suffix)

drift: from Old Norse *drifa, from Proto-Germanic *drifan "to push, drive, urge on"

-ier:  A suffix forming the comparative degree of adjectives and adverbs, meaning "more."
