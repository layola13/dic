fades:/feɪdz/
verb
消失; 褪色; 衰退
(of a colour, image, or sound) gradually becomes less clear or loud until it disappears.

The photograph had faded badly.
这张照片已经严重褪色了。

The music faded away as the band left the stage.
乐队离开舞台时，音乐逐渐消失了。

近义词:disappears, diminishes, weakens, pales
反义词:brightens, intensifies, strengthens, deepens
词性:v.


fades:/feɪdz/
noun
淡入淡出
a gradual decrease in the strength of a sound or image.

The film ended with a slow fade to black.
电影以缓慢的淡入黑色结束。

There was a brief fade in the radio signal.
无线电信号短暂地减弱了。

近义词:dissolve, transition
反义词:cut, jump
词性:n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *faden, from Old French fader, from Frankish *fadōn, from Proto-Germanic *fadōną (“to arrange, order, fit, adapt”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁- (“to put, place, set”). Cognate with Old High German fadam (“thread, string, fathom”), Old English fæðm (“fathom”). More at fathom.
