overscrupulousness:/ˌoʊvərˈskruːpjələsnəs/| n.| 过分谨慎；过于拘泥；过分小心| The state of being excessively scrupulous, characterized by extreme attention to detail and a tendency to worry about even minor moral or ethical considerations.  It implies an excessive concern with correctness and propriety, often to the point of being counterproductive.| Her overscrupulousness prevented her from making a quick decision. 她的过分谨慎让她无法快速做出决定。|近义词：meticulousness, fastidiousness, punctiliousness|反义词：carelessness, negligence, recklessness|词性: noun

Etymology:
From over- + scrupulous + -ness.  "Over-" indicates "excessive" or "too much." "Scrupulous" comes from the Latin "scrupulus," meaning "a small sharp stone," which figuratively represents a source of anxiety or doubt.  "-ness" is a suffix that forms nouns denoting a state or quality.
