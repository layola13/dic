concoctive:/kənˈkɒktɪv/ | adj. | 调制的，捏造的，虚构的 | Having the power to concoct; devising; inventive, especially in a negative way, suggesting fabrication or manipulation. |  He had a concoctive mind, always dreaming up elaborate schemes. 他有着爱编造故事的头脑，总是虚构出复杂的阴谋。|  inventive, fabricating, imaginative, creative | truthful, factual, genuine | adjective

concoct:/kənˈkɒkt/ | v. | 调制，调合；捏造，编造 | To prepare or make by combining ingredients, especially in cookery; to devise, invent, or fabricate, often fraudulently or deceptively. | The chef concocted a new dish using seasonal ingredients. 厨师用时令食材调制了一道新菜。| create, devise, invent, fabricate | destroy, dismantle, ruin | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *concoctus, past participle of concoquere "to cook together, digest," from com- "together" + coquere "to cook."
