karus: /ˈkɑːrʊs/ | n. | 卡鲁斯（一种已灭绝的史前有袋类动物） | An extinct marsupial animal that lived in Australia during the Pleistocene epoch.  It was related to the wombat and the koala. |  The karus was a large herbivore. 卡鲁斯是一种大型食草动物。 |  Fossils of karus have been found in caves in Naracoorte, South Australia.  在南澳大利亚纳拉库特的洞穴中发现了卡鲁斯的化石。|  Synonyms:  Diprotodon (a related, larger extinct marsupial),  giant wombat (a less precise but evocative term) | Antonyms: N/A (extinct animals don't have direct antonyms) | noun
