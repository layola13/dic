herder:/ˈhɜːrdər/ (US) /ˈhɜːdə(r)/ (UK)| n.|牧人；牧羊人|A person who takes care of a herd of animals, especially sheep or cattle.|The herder led his flock to the high pastures for the summer. 牧羊人夏天把羊群带到高高的牧场。|Synonyms: shepherd, herdsman, stockman, cowherd, goatherd|Antonyms: None (lack of a direct opposite)|Noun

herder:/ˈhɜːrdər/ (US) /ˈhɜːdə(r)/ (UK)| n.|牧人；牧羊人|One who keeps or looks after livestock.|The young herder skillfully guided the cattle across the stream.  年轻的牧民熟练地引导牛群过河。|Synonyms: shepherdess, rancher, drover|Antonyms: predator|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hierde (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *hirdijaz, from *herdoną "to herd". Cognate with German Hirt "shepherd".
