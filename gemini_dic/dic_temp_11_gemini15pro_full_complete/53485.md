disarraying: /dɪsəˈreɪɪŋ/
| adj. |  使混乱的，扰乱的 | Throwing clothes on the floor is disarraying the room.  把衣服扔在地板上会使房间凌乱。| The sudden news was disarraying to her calm composure.  突如其来的消息扰乱了她平静的沉着。| 近义词: disordering, confusing, disrupting | 反义词: ordering, organizing, arranging |词性: 形容词

disarray: /ˌdɪsəˈreɪ/
| n. | 混乱，紊乱 | The room was in disarray after the party. 派对结束后，房间一片狼藉。|  His financial affairs were in complete disarray. 他的财务状况完全混乱。| 近义词: disorder, chaos, confusion | 反义词: order, organization, neatness |词性: 名词

disarray: /ˌdɪsəˈreɪ/
| vt. | 使混乱，扰乱 | The loud noise disarrayed the performers.  巨大的噪音扰乱了表演者。| The unexpected announcement disarrayed their plans.  突如其来的通知打乱了他们的计划。|近义词: disorder, confuse, disrupt | 反义词: order, organize, arrange |词性: 动词


词源:  From dis- "apart, asunder" + array "to arrange, set in order".  源自 dis- “分开，分离” + array “排列，整理”。
