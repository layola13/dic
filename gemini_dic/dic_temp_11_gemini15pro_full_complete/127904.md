neolithic's:/ˌniːəˈlɪθɪks/ | n. | 新石器时代的 |  Of or relating to the Neolithic period, also known as the New Stone Age, a period in the development of human technology, beginning about 10,200 BC and ending between 4,500 and 2,000 BC, characterized by the development of agriculture and the making of polished stone implements.  It's the possessive form, but rarely used correctly.  It's more common and accurate to say "of the Neolithic period" or "Neolithic era's". |  Incorrect usage: The neolithic's tools were advanced for their time. | Correct usage: The tools of the Neolithic period were advanced for their time.| 近义词: New Stone Age | 反义词: Paleolithic | 词性: noun (possessive, though usage is usually incorrect)


neolithic:/ˌniːəˈlɪθɪk/ | adj. | 新石器时代的 | Of or relating to the Neolithic period, also known as the New Stone Age. | Neolithic farming practices led to settled communities.  新石器时代的农业实践导致了定居的社区。| 近义词: New Stone Age | 反义词: Paleolithic | 词性: adjective
