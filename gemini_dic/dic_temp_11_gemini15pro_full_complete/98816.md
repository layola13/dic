intellectualist: /ˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəlɪst/ | n. adj. | 知识分子；智力主义者；理智主义者 |  Someone who places a high value on or pursues intellectual pursuits. As an adjective, it describes something related to intellectualism. |  He was known as an intellectualist, always engaged in deep thought and philosophical discussions. 他被称为知识分子，总是沉浸在深刻的思考和哲学讨论中。| Synonyms: intellectual, highbrow, thinker | Antonyms: philistine, lowbrow, anti-intellectual | noun, adjective

intellectualist: /ˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəlɪst/ | adj. | 智力主义的；理智主义的 |  Relating to or characterized by intellectualism.  | The intellectualist movement emphasized reason and logic over emotion and intuition. 智力主义运动强调理性逻辑胜过情感和直觉。| Synonyms: cerebral, rational, abstract | Antonyms: emotional, intuitive, instinctive | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *intellectualiste*, from *intellectuel* (intellectual) + *-iste* (-ist).  Ultimately from Latin *intellectus* (understanding).
