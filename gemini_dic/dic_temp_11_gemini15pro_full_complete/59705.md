eclampsia:/ɪˈklæmpsɪə/| n. |子痫|A severe complication of pregnancy characterized by seizures and high blood pressure, typically occurring after the 20th week of gestation.  It is a progression from pre-eclampsia, a condition involving high blood pressure and protein in the urine.|The pregnant woman was diagnosed with eclampsia and required immediate medical attention.|近义词:toxemia (older term), convulsions|反义词: (No direct antonym, consider normalcy in pregnancy)|词性:noun
