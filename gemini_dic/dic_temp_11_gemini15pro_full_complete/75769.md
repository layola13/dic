fulsome:/ˈfʊlsəm/| adj.|过分恭维的，令人生厌的，过多的，过饱的|Characterized by excessive flattery or praise; sickeningly excessive; overabundant; cloying.|例句：His fulsome praise made me uncomfortable. 他过分的赞美让我感到不舒服。

例句：The meal was a fulsome feast, leaving everyone feeling stuffed. 这顿饭菜过于丰盛，每个人都感到吃撑了。|近义词：excessive, effusive, overdone, cloying, sickening|反义词:meager, scant, reserved, understated|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Middle English *fulsom, from Old English *fullsum (“abundant, plentiful, copious”), equivalent to full +‎ -some.  The negative connotations developed later, likely due to the excessive nature implied by the word.
