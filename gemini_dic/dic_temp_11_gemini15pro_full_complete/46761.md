danegelt:/ˈdeɪnɡɛlt/| n.|丹麦金，贡金|A tax levied in England from 991 to the 12th century to buy off Danish invaders or to pay for defenses against them.  It refers to any payment extorted by force or threat.|The king levied a heavy danegeld on the people to appease the Viking raiders.|近义词：tribute, ransom, protection money|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "reward")|noun
