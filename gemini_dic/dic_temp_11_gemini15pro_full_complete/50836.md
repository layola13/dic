desiccate:/ˈdɛsɪkeɪt/ |v. |使干燥，使脱水；使枯竭|To remove the moisture from something, typically in order to preserve it or to make it smaller and lighter.  |The hot desert sun desiccated the plants.  炎热的沙漠太阳使植物枯萎了。| The prolonged drought desiccated the land, turning fertile fields into barren wastelands.  长期的干旱使土地干涸，把肥沃的田野变成了贫瘠的荒地。 | synonyms: dry, dehydrate, parch, wither | antonyms: hydrate, moisten, soak |verb

desiccated:/ˈdɛsɪkeɪtɪd/ |adj. |干燥的，脱水的；枯竭的|Having had moisture removed; dried up.  |The desiccated landscape offered little in the way of food or shelter. 干燥的土地几乎没有食物或住所。| The archaeologists discovered desiccated remains of ancient plants and animals in the cave.  考古学家在洞穴里发现了古代动植物的干尸。 | synonyms: dried, dehydrated, parched, withered | antonyms: hydrated, moisten, soaked |adjective
