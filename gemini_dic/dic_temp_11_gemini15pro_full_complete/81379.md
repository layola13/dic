grasp:/ɡræsp/ /ɡrɑːsp/ | n. vt. | 抓住，掌握，理解 |  The act of seizing and holding firmly; a firm hold; understanding or comprehension of something. |  She grasped the rope tightly. 她紧紧地抓住绳子。

He quickly grasped the main points of the lecture. 他很快掌握了讲座的要点。 | 近义词：grip, seize, clutch, comprehend, understand | 反义词：release, misunderstand, let go | 词性: 名词, 动词

Etymology: From Middle English *graspen, *grapsen, from Old Norse *grapsa (“to grasp, grope for”), from Proto-Germanic *grapōną (“to grope, grasp”), from Proto-Indo-European *grebʰ- (“to seize, grasp”).
