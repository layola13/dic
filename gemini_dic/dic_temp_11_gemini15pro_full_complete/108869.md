lecithin:/ˈlesɪθɪn/ /ˈlɛsɪθɪn/
noun
卵磷脂
A group of fatty substances found in animal and plant tissues, essential for the proper functioning of cell membranes.  It is also used in food processing as an emulsifier.
Lecithin is found in many foods, such as soybeans and egg yolks. 卵磷脂存在于许多食物中，例如大豆和蛋黄。
Lecithin is used as an emulsifier in margarine. 卵磷脂在人造黄油中用作乳化剂。
synonym: phosphatidylcholine
antonym: (No direct antonym, as it's a specific substance.  One could consider substances that inhibit its function, but those would be context-specific.)
