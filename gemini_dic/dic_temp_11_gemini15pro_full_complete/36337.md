coign:/ˈkɔɪn/| n.|角落，突出部，有利地位|A projecting corner or angle of a wall or building; a corner or angle from which something can be observed.|From his coign of vantage, he could see the entire battlefield. 从他的有利位置，他可以俯瞰整个战场。|Synonyms: vantage point, viewpoint, outlook, corner, niche|Antonyms: center, interior, middle|noun

coign:/kɔɪn/| n.|楔形物|A wedge; a quoin.|The builders used a coign to level the foundation stones. 建筑工人用楔子来调平基础石。|Synonyms: wedge, chock, block|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *coigne (modern French coin), from Latin cuneus (“wedge”).  The sense of "projecting corner" arose from the image of a wedge.
