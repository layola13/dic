dietetics:/ˌdaɪəˈtɛtɪks/ | n. |营养学；饮食学|The science or art of applying the principles of nutrition to the feeding of individuals or groups, especially in health and disease.  It involves the study of foods and their effects on the body, as well as the planning and preparation of meals to meet specific dietary needs.| She studied dietetics in college and now works as a registered dietitian. 她大学学习营养学，现在是一名注册营养师。| synonyms: nutrition, nutriment, nourishment | antonyms: malnutrition, starvation | noun


dietitian:/ˌdaɪəˈtɪʃən/ | n. |营养学家；营养师| A health professional who is trained to assess the nutritional needs of individuals and groups and to develop and implement dietary plans to meet those needs. | The dietitian recommended a low-sodium diet for the patient with hypertension. 营养师建议高血压患者采用低钠饮食。| synonyms: nutritionist | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "unhealthy food advocate") | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *diaetetica, from Greek diaitētikē (tekhnē) “(the art/science) pertaining to diet/regimen,” from diaita “manner of living, diet.”
