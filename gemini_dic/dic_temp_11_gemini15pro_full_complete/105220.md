keyseat: /ˈkiːˌsiːt/ | n. | 键槽 | A groove or channel cut in a shaft or hub to receive a key, which prevents relative rotation between the shaft and a component mounted on it. |  The keyseat in the motor shaft was too shallow for the key. 电机轴上的键槽对于键来说太浅了。| synonym: keyway, slot | antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps a smooth, unbroken surface) | noun

keyseat: /ˈkiːˌsiːt/ | v. |  键槽加工；开键槽 | To cut a keyseat in a shaft or hub. |  The machinist keyseated the shaft to accommodate the gear.  机械师在轴上加工了键槽以适应齿轮。 | synonym:  cut a keyway, slot | antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps fill a keyway) | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "key" + "seat".  "Key" in this context refers to a mechanical key used to prevent rotation.  "Seat" refers to a place or surface where something rests or fits.
