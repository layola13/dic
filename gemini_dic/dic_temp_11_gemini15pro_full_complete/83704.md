hagiographer:/ˌhæɡiˈɒɡrəfər/ | n. |圣徒传记作者；吹捧者|A writer of hagiographies; someone who idealizes or praises another excessively.|Example 1: The hagiographer portrayed the politician as a saint, ignoring all his flaws.  圣徒传记作者将这位政治家描绘成圣人，忽略了他所有的缺点。

Example 2:  He's not a journalist; he's a hagiographer for the CEO. 他不是记者；他是首席执行官的吹捧者。|Synonym: biographer, eulogist, panegyrist, idolater |Antonym: critic, detractor, iconoclast|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hagiographus, from Greek hagiographos "writing about saints," from hagios "holy" + -graphos "writing."
