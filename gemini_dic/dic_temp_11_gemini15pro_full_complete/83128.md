gut:/ɡʌt/| n. v. adj.|肠道；内脏；胆量；直觉|The intestines or stomach, especially of a large animal.  Courage, determination, or resolve.  Fundamental, basic, or essential.|
He had a gut feeling that something was wrong. 他有一种直觉，觉得事情不对劲。|
He gutted the fish and threw the guts to the cat.  他把鱼的内脏掏出来扔给了猫。|
The gut issue here is a lack of funding.  这里的核心问题是缺乏资金。|
近义词：intestines, bowels, courage, nerve, instinct, fundamental, basic|
反义词：exterior, superficial|词性: 名词, 动词, 形容词


gut:/ɡʌt/| n. v. adj.|勇气；决心；毅力|Courage and determination.|She had the guts to stand up to the bully. 她有勇气对抗恶霸。|近义词：courage, bravery, nerve, determination|反义词：cowardice, fear|词性: 名词


gut:/ɡʌt/| n. v. adj.|基本的；根本的；本质的|Relating to the most basic or essential aspects of something.|The gut reaction of most people was one of shock. 大多数人的本能反应是震惊。|近义词：fundamental, basic, essential, core|反义词：superficial, peripheral|词性: 形容词


gut:/ɡʌt/| v. |取出内脏；摧毁（建筑物）内部|To remove the internal organs of an animal or fish.  To destroy the inside of a building.|They gutted the building before demolishing it. 他们在拆除建筑物之前摧毁了它的内部。|近义词：eviscerate, disembowel, devastate, destroy|反义词：repair, renovate|词性: 动词


词源: From Middle English gutte, from Old English guttas (“bowels, entrails”), of uncertain origin.
