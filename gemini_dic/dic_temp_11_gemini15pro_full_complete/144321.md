outspun:/ˌaʊtˈspʌn/
verb (past participle of outspin)
超越宣传；胜过…的宣传
having surpassed someone in the dissemination of propaganda or information, especially in a political context.
The candidate outspun his rival with a clever social media campaign.  这位候选人凭借一场巧妙的社交媒体宣传活动，在宣传方面胜过了他的对手。
Synonym: outmaneuvered, outsmarted, outflanked
Antonym: outdone, surpassed


outspun:/ˌaʊtˈspʌn/
adjective
过度宣传的；宣传过度的
excessively or elaborately spun; overspun.
The outspun narrative eventually lost credibility with the public.  过度宣传的叙事最终失去了公众的可信度。
Synonym: overblown, exaggerated, hyped
Antonym: understated, downplayed


outspun:/ˌaʊtˈspʌn/
adjective
(of yarn or thread)  纺成的；拉细的
drawn out into a thread; spun out.
The outspun silk was incredibly fine and delicate.  纺成的丝绸非常精细而精致。
Synonym: spun, drawn, extended
Antonym: unspun, raw

Etymology:  "out-" (prefix indicating exceeding or surpassing) + "spun" (past participle of "spin," meaning to draw out and twist fibers into thread, or to tell a story, especially with a particular bias).
