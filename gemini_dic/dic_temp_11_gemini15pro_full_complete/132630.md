nondiscerning: /ˌnɒndɪˈsɜːnɪŋ/ /ˌnɑːndɪˈsɜːrnɪŋ/ | adj. | 无辨别力的；不加区分的 | Lacking or exhibiting a lack of discernment; unable or unwilling to distinguish between things. |  He has nondiscerning taste in music. 他对音乐的品味不怎么挑剔。| synonyms: undiscriminating, indiscriminate, uncritical, accepting | antonyms: discerning, discriminating, selective, critical | adjective

nondiscern: /ˌnɒndɪˈsɜːn/ /ˌnɑːndɪˈsɜːrn/ | verb | 不辨别；不区分 | Fail to distinguish or differentiate. | The study showed that babies can nondiscern between different sounds at an early age.  研究表明，婴儿在很小的时候就无法区分不同的声音。 | synonyms: not distinguish, not differentiate, confound | antonyms: discern, distinguish, differentiate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + discerning.  Discerning comes from the Old French discerner, from Latin discernere "to separate, distinguish," from dis- "apart" + cernere "to distinguish, separate, sift."
