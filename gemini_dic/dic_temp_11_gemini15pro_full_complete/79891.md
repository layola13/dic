gnathostoma:/næθəˈstoʊmə/ | n. |颚口线虫属|A genus of parasitic nematodes (roundworms) that can infect humans and other animals.  They are typically found in raw or undercooked freshwater fish, frogs, and snakes. |Example: Gnathostoma spinigerum is a species of Gnathostoma that causes gnathostomiasis. 颚口棘唇线虫是一种引起颚口线虫病的颚口线虫。|Example: Infection with Gnathostoma can lead to serious health problems. 感染颚口线虫会导致严重的健康问题。|Synonym:  (No direct synonym for the genus, but related terms include nematode, roundworm, parasite) |Antonym: (No antonym for a genus) | noun
