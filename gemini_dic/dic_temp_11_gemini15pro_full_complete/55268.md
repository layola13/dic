dissipatednesses:/dɪˈsɪpeɪtɪdnəsɪz/
noun
放荡；挥霍；消散
The state of being dissipated; excessive indulgence in pleasure or vice.  A collection or aggregation of instances of dissipation.
His dissipatednesses ultimately led to his ruin. 他的放荡最终导致了他的毁灭。
The dissipatednesses of the aristocracy were legendary.  贵族的挥霍无度是传奇性的。
Synonyms: debauchery, profligacy, dissoluteness, extravagance, dissipation
Antonyms: moderation, temperance, frugality, abstinence, restraint
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From dissipated + -ness.  Dissipated from Latin dissipatus, past participle of dissipare "to scatter," from dis- "apart" + sipare "to throw, scatter."
