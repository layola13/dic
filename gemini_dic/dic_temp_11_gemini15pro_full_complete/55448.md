distinguishabilities:/dɪˌstɪŋɡwɪʃəˈbɪlətiz/| n. |可区分性；可辨别性|The quality or state of being distinguishable; the ability to be distinguished.  The state of objects when they are different or distinct in such a way that they can be separated or recognized as not the same.|The subtle distinguishabilities between the two wines were evident to the sommelier.| 近义词: differences, distinctions, disparities, variances | 反义词: similarities, likenesses, identities, indistinguishabilities | 词性: 名词(plural)


Etymology:
From distinguishable + -ity.  Distinguishable is derived from distinguish (from Old French distinguer) + -able.
