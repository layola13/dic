library: /ˈlaɪbrɛri/ | n. |图书馆| A library is a building or room containing collections of books, periodicals, and sometimes films and recorded music for people to read, borrow, or refer to. The word comes from Old French "librairie" meaning "bookstore", ultimately from Latin "librarius" meaning "bookseller," derived from "liber," meaning "book." |  I often spend my weekends at the library. 我经常在图书馆度过周末。|  The library has a vast collection of historical documents. 图书馆收藏了大量的历史文献。| Synonyms: archive, collection, depository | Antonyms: None (a direct opposite for a place holding knowledge is unusual) | Noun
