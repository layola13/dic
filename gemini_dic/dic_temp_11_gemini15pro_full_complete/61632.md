emigre:/ˈemɪɡreɪ, ˈemɪɡri/ /ˈemɪɡreɪ, ˈemɪɡri/
noun
(political) emigrant; refugee
a person who has left their own country in order to settle in another, usually for political reasons.
Many emigres from the former Soviet Union settled in the United States.  许多前苏联的移民定居在美国。
synonyms: exile, refugee, expatriate, deportee, displaced person
antonyms: immigrant, native, resident
n.

emigre:/ˈemɪɡreɪ, ˈemɪɡri/ /ˈemɪɡreɪ, ˈemɪɡri/
adjective
relating to or characteristic of an emigre.
The emigre community maintained its cultural traditions.  移民社区保持了其文化传统。
synonyms: exiled, refugee, expatriate, displaced
antonyms: native, indigenous

ETYMOLOGY->
From French émigré, past participle of émigrer ‘to emigrate’, from Latin emigrare, from e- (variant of ex-) ‘out of’ + migrare ‘to migrate’.
