all-there:/ˌɔːlˈðɛər/ | adj. |  神志清醒的；机智的；精明的；正常的 | Mentally alert; sane; intelligent; normal. |  He's not quite all there. 他有点不太正常。 | 近义词: sane, lucid, intelligent, sharp | 反义词: insane, crazy, mad, out of it | 词性: 形容词

词源:  This term originated in the late 19th century.  "All there" refers to having all one's mental faculties present and functioning correctly. It likely arose from the idea that if someone isn't "all there," some part of their mind is missing or not working properly.
