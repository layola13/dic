crozier:/ˈkroʊʒər/ (US), /ˈkrəʊziə(r)/ (UK)| n. |权杖，牧杖，主教杖|A ceremonial staff carried by a bishop or abbot as a symbol of pastoral office.|The bishop held his crozier aloft as he addressed the congregation. 主教高举着牧杖向会众讲话。|Synonyms: staff, pastoral staff, crosier|Antonyms: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *crocier, from Medieval Latin *crociarius, from Latin crocia "shepherd's crook," of uncertain origin.
