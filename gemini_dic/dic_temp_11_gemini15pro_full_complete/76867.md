ganglion:/ˈɡæŋɡliən/ (US), /ˈɡæŋɡlɪən/ (UK)| n. |神经节；腱鞘囊肿|A structure containing a number of nerve cell bodies, typically linked by synapses, and often forming a swelling on a nerve fibre.|The surgeon located the ganglion and carefully removed it. 外科医生找到了神经节并小心地将其移除。|She developed a ganglion cyst on her wrist. 她的手腕上长了一个腱鞘囊肿。|Synonym: nerve center, node|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, could consider individual nerve fibers)|noun
