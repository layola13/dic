ojibwa:/oʊˈdʒɪbwə/ /əˈdʒɪbweɪ/| n. adj.|奥吉布瓦人；奥吉布瓦语|A member of an Algonquian people of the Great Lakes region. Also, the Algonquian language of the Ojibwa.  A person of Ojibwa descent; the language spoken by the Ojibwa people.|例句：The Ojibwa tribe has a rich history. 奥吉布瓦部落有着悠久的历史。

例句：He is studying the Ojibwa language. 他正在学习奥吉布瓦语。|近义词：Chippewa, Anishinaabe|反义词: N/A|词性:名词/形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ojibwe auto-onym *očipowa (plural *očipowak) meaning “to roast/toast (something) till it puckers/plists,” referring to their puckered-toe moccasins.  The term Chippewa comes from an English corruption of Ojibwa.
