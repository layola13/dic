omosternum: /ˈoʊmoʊstɜːrnəm/ | n. | omosternum (Anatomy) | A bony process projecting forward from the anterior border of the sternum of many amphibians and reptiles. | The omosternum provides an attachment point for muscles involved in tongue protrusion and swallowing.  |  Synonym:  episternum (in some contexts), presternum (sometimes used interchangeably but can also refer to other structures) | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific anatomical feature) | noun

Etymology:  "omo-" (Greek, meaning shoulder) + "sternum" (Latin, meaning breastbone).  The term reflects its location and connection to shoulder girdle muscles.
