eccentric:/ɪkˈsɛntrɪk/ /ɛkˈsɛntrɪk/| adj. n.|古怪的，反常的；古怪的人|Deviating from the recognized or customary character, practice, etc.; peculiar; odd;  a person who has an unusual or odd personality.|例句：He was known for his eccentric habits, like wearing mismatched socks. 他以古怪的习惯而闻名，比如穿不配对的袜子。|例句：She's a bit of an eccentric, but she's very kind. 她有点古怪，但她很善良。|近义词：peculiar, odd, strange, unusual, quirky, unconventional|反义词：conventional, normal, ordinary, typical|词性:形容词,名词

eccentric:/ɪkˈsɛntrɪk/ /ɛkˈsɛntrɪk/| n.|古怪的人|A person who has an unusual or odd personality.|例句：The old woman was a beloved eccentric in the neighborhood. 这位老妇人是邻居中一位受人爱戴的怪人。|例句：He was a brilliant but eccentric scientist. 他是一位才华横溢但古怪的科学家。|近义词：character, crank, oddball|反义词：conformist|词性:名词
