intimidate:/ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪt/| v. |恐吓，威胁|To frighten or threaten someone, usually in order to persuade them to do something that you want them to do.|The bully tried to intimidate the smaller children. 恶霸试图恐吓较小的孩子。|Synonyms: frighten, menace, threaten, browbeat, cow, bully|Antonyms: encourage, embolden, reassure|verb

intimidation:/ɪnˌtɪmɪˈdeɪʃn/| n. |恐吓，威胁|The action of intimidating someone, or the state of being intimidated.|The witness refused to testify due to intimidation.  由于受到恐吓，证人拒绝作证。|Synonyms: coercion, bullying, menace, threat|Antonyms: encouragement, reassurance|noun
