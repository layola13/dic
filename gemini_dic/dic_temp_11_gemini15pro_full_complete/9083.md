antimodernisms: /ˌæntiˈmɒdərnɪzəmz/ /ˌæntiˈmɑːdərˌnɪzəmz/
|n.|反现代主义（复数）|Opposition to modernism, especially in the arts, architecture, or literature.  Often characterized by a preference for traditional forms and values.|Many architectural antimodernisms emerged in the late 20th century, embracing classical and vernacular styles.|近义词: traditionalism, conservatism|反义词: modernism, avant-gardism|词性: 名词

Etymology:
anti- + modernism + -s

anti-:  prefix meaning "against," "opposite of," from Greek anti "against, opposite of, instead of, in return for."

modernism:  from modern + -ism.  Modern from Old French moderne "modern, recent," from Late Latin modernus "of the present mode or fashion," from Latin modo "just now, only, lately," ablative of modus "measure, manner, fashion."

-s: plural suffix.
