civilisations:/ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/| n.|文明（复数）|The complex societies and cultures developed by human beings, characterized by advanced development of intellectual, material, and social aspects, including cities, government, writing, art, and technology.|The study of ancient civilisations is fascinating.  研究古代文明令人着迷。|近义词：cultures, societies|反义词: barbarity, savagery|noun

civilisation:/ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n.|文明（单数）|The stage of human social development and organization that is considered most advanced.|Ancient Egypt was a remarkable civilisation. 古埃及是一个非凡的文明。|近义词：culture, society|反义词: barbarity, savagery|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: from Old French, or from late Latin *civilisatio(n-), from civilis ‘relating to citizens’ (see civil).
