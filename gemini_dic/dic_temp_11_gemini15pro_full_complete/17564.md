beaucoup:/boʊˈkuː/ (US), /bəʊˈkuː/ (UK)| adv.|很多，大量|A French word adopted into English, meaning "a lot" or "much." It is used informally and often considered slightly pretentious. |例句：There were beaucoup people at the concert. 音乐会上人山人海。

例句：We had beaucoup de plaisir at the party. 我们在派对上玩得很开心。|近义词：many, much, a lot of, plenty of, lots of|反义词：few, little|词性: adverb

词源: From French, literally "much good," from beau ("beautiful, fine, great") + coup ("blow, stroke"). Originally meant "a great deal of good," later simply "a great deal" or "much."

注意事项:
Avoid using "beaucoup" in formal writing. While understood, it's considered informal and might be perceived as affected.  避免在正式写作中使用“beaucoup”。虽然可以理解，但它被认为是非正式的，可能会被认为是做作的。
