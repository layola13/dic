capal: /ˈkeɪpəl/ | adj. | capable | able to do something; having the skill, power, or other qualities that are needed to do something | She's a very capable lawyer. 她是一位非常有能力的律师。|  synonyms: competent, able, skilled, proficient | antonyms: incompetent, incapable, unskilled, inept | adjective

Etymology:  From Late Latin *capabilis, from Latin capere "to take, seize, grasp, hold, contain, comprehend."  The notion is of "being able to take or hold."
