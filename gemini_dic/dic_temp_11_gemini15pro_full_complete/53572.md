disbelievingly:/ˌdɪsbɪˈliːvɪŋli/| adv.|怀疑地，不相信地|In a manner that shows one does not believe something.  Expressing disbelief.|She looked at him disbelievingly. 她怀疑地看着他。|Synonyms: skeptically, doubtfully, incredulously|Antonyms: trustingly, believingly, credulously|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From disbelieve + -ing + -ly.  "Dis-" prefixes words to indicate negation or reversal, from Latin. "Believe" comes from Old English "belīefan," meaning "to believe, trust."  "-ing" forms a present participle or gerund, and "-ly" forms adverbs.
