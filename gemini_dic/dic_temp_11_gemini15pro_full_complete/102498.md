jacobite:/ˈdʒækəˌbaɪt/| n. adj.|詹姆斯党人；詹姆斯党人的|A supporter of James II of England after he was deposed in 1688, or of his son, the Old Pretender, or of his grandson, Bonnie Prince Charlie.  A Jacobite could also be described as someone who supports the restoration of the Stuart line to the British throne.|例句：The jacobite uprising of 1745 was ultimately unsuccessful. 1745年的詹姆斯党人起义最终失败了。

例句：He was a staunch Jacobite, refusing to swear allegiance to the new king. 他是一个坚定的詹姆斯党人，拒绝向新国王宣誓效忠。|近义词：Stuart supporter, Tory (in the 18th century context)|反义词：Whig (in the 18th century context), Hanoverian|词性: 名词，形容词
