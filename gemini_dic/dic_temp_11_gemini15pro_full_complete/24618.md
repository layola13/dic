broke-down:/ˈbrəʊkˈdaʊn/ (UK)| adj.| 故障的，破旧的，垮掉的|  Something that is broke-down is not working or in very bad condition.  It can also refer to a person who is emotionally or physically exhausted.|  She was feeling completely broke-down after the long journey.  长途旅行后，她感到筋疲力尽。|近义词：broken, ruined, dilapidated, exhausted, overwhelmed|反义词:working, functional, new, repaired, healthy|词性:形容词


词源:  "Broke down" originates from the literal sense of something breaking and falling down.  Over time, the meaning extended to include mechanical failures and emotional collapse.
