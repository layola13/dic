decays:/dɪˈkeɪz/| v. (third-person singular simple present decays, present participle decaying, simple past decayed, past participle decayed)|腐烂，腐朽；衰败，衰退|to rot or decompose; to decline in health, strength, or vigor|The fruit quickly decayed in the heat. 水果在高温下迅速腐烂。|deteriorates, rots, decomposes|flourishes, thrives|verb
decay:/dɪˈkeɪ/| n.|腐烂，腐朽；衰败，衰退|the state or process of rotting or decomposition; a decline in health, strength, or vigor|Tooth decay can be prevented with proper dental hygiene.  蛀牙可以通过适当的牙齿卫生来预防。|deterioration, decline, decomposition|growth, improvement|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *decair, from Latin *cadere "to fall," related to caducus "falling, frail."
