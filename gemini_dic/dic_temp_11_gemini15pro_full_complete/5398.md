almahad:/ˌæl.məˈhæd/ | n. | 阿尔摩哈德 | Member of a Berber Muslim movement and dynasty that ruled much of North Africa and southern Spain in the 12th and 13th centuries. | The Almohads unified much of the Iberian Peninsula under their rule.  阿尔摩哈德王朝统一了伊比利亚半岛的大部分地区。| synonym: Almohade | antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps consider Christian kingdoms of the time, like Castile or Aragon) | noun

etymology->
From Arabic الموحدون (al-Muwaḥḥidūn, “the Unitarians, those who affirm the unity of God”).
