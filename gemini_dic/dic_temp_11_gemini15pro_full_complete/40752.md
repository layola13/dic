convict:/kənˈvɪkt/ (verb) /ˈkɒnvɪkt/ (noun)| v. 证明…有罪；宣判…有罪； n. 罪犯|
(verb) To prove or declare someone guilty of a crime, usually after a legal trial. (noun) A person who has been found guilty of a crime and is serving a sentence. |
(verb) The jury convicted him of murder. 陪审团裁定他谋杀罪名成立。
(noun) The escaped convict was apprehended by the police. 逃犯被警方逮捕。|
(verb) condemn, sentence |
(verb) acquit, exonerate |
verb

convict:/ˈkɒnvɪkt/ (noun)| n. 罪犯 | A person who has been found guilty of a crime and is serving a sentence. | The convict was sentenced to ten years in prison.  这名罪犯被判处十年监禁。
The convicts rioted in the prison yard. 罪犯们在监狱的院子里发生了暴乱。 | prisoner, inmate, felon | law-abiding citizen | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *convict*, from Latin *convictus*, past participle of *convincere* ("to overcome, convict"), from *con-* ("together, with") + *vincere* ("to conquer, overcome").
