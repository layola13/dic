laggards:/ˈlæɡərdz/| n.|落后者，迟缓者|a person or group that makes slow progress and falls behind others.  Those who are slow to adapt to new technologies are often referred to as laggards.|The laggards in the race finally crossed the finish line hours after the winner.  比赛中的落后者最终在获胜者数小时后才越过终点线。|synonyms: stragglers, dawdlers, slowpokes|antonyms: leaders, pioneers, innovators|noun
laggard:/ˈlæɡərd/| n. adj.|落后者，迟缓的|a person or group that makes slow progress and falls behind others; slow or reluctant to act or respond.  He's a bit of a laggard when it comes to housework.  说到家务事，他有点拖拖拉拉。|The laggard student finally turned in his assignment.  迟缓的学生终于交上了作业。|synonyms: straggler, dawdler, slowpoke|antonyms: leader, pioneer, innovator|noun, adjective
