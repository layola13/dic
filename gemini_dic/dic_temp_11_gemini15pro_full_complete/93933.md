immunizers:/ˈɪmjʊnaɪzərz/
noun
免疫接种者，免疫者
People who administer immunizations (vaccines) to others.
The immunizers visited the school to vaccinate the children against measles.  免疫接种员到学校为孩子们接种麻疹疫苗。
近义词:vaccinators, inoculators
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps consider) anti-vaxxers (those who oppose vaccination)
词性:n.


immunize:/ˈɪmjʊnaɪz/
verb
使免疫；接种疫苗
To make someone immune to a disease, typically by vaccination.
They immunized the children against polio. 他们为孩子们接种了小儿麻痹症疫苗。
近义词:vaccinate, inoculate
反义词: (No direct antonym, consider the effect) expose, infect
词性:v.

immunization:/ˌɪmjʊnɪˈzeɪʃən/
noun
免疫；免疫接种
The process of making someone immune to a disease, typically by vaccination.
Childhood immunization is crucial for preventing many diseases. 儿童免疫接种对预防许多疾病至关重要。
近义词:vaccination, inoculation
反义词: (No direct antonym, consider the result) susceptibility, vulnerability
词性:n.

immune:/ɪˈmjuːn/
adjective
免疫的；不受影响的
Protected from or resistant to a particular disease or the like.
He seems to be immune to colds. 他似乎对感冒免疫。
近义词:resistant, unsusceptible
反义词:susceptible, vulnerable
词性:adj.
