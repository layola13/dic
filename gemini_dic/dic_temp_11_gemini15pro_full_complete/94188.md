imperator:/ɪmˈpɛrətɔːr/ (Classical Latin), /ˈɪmpəreɪtər/ (Ecclesiastical Latin) | n. |皇帝,指挥官,统治者|A commander, especially a commander-in-chief in ancient Rome; a title bestowed upon victorious generals by their troops, later becoming synonymous with emperor.|The imperator addressed his legions before the battle. 皇帝在战前向他的军团发表了讲话。|Synonyms: emperor, ruler, sovereign, commander, leader|Antonyms: subject, follower, subordinate|Noun
