ostrogoth:/ˈɒstrəˌɡɒθ/ (US) /ˈɑːstrəˌɡɑːθ/| n. |东哥特人|A member of the eastern branch of the Goths, who established a kingdom in Italy in the 5th century.|The Ostrogoths, under Theodoric the Great, ruled Italy for several decades.|近义词: Goth, barbarian (in a historical context)|反义词: Roman (often presented as their adversaries)|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Ostrogothi, from Greek *Ὀστρογότθοι (Ostrogóthoi), a compound of *ὄστρος (óstros, “east”) + *Γότθοι (Gótthoi, “Goths”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *Austrō- *Guþōz, from *austrō (“east”) + *guþą (“Goth”).
