deconstructionism:/ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪzəm/
n.
解构主义
A philosophical movement and theory of literary criticism that questions traditional assumptions about certainty, identity, and truth; asserts that words can only refer to other words; and attempts to demonstrate how statements about any text subvert their own meanings.
Deconstructionism challenges the idea of a fixed meaning in a text.  解构主义挑战文本中固定意义的概念。
Deconstructionism has had a profound impact on literary studies. 解构主义对文学研究产生了深远的影响。
近义词: post-structuralism
反义词: structuralism
词性: noun


deconstruct:/ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkt/
vt.
解构
To analyze (a text) by deconstruction. Also, to take apart or examine (something) in order to reveal the basis or composition often with the intention of exposing biases, flaws, or inconsistencies.
The professor deconstructed the poem to reveal its hidden meanings. 教授解构了这首诗，揭示了其隐藏的含义。
They deconstructed the argument to show its weaknesses. 他们解构了这个论点以展示其弱点。
近义词: analyze, dismantle
反义词: synthesize, construct
词性: verb
