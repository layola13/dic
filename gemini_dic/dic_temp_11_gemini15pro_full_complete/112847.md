luckier:/ˈlʌkiər/| adj.|更幸运的|Comparative form of lucky: more fortunate.  Having more good fortune than someone or something else.|例句：He was luckier than me and won the lottery. 他比我幸运，中了彩票。|例句：She felt luckier than ever to have such supportive friends. 她觉得自己比以往任何时候都幸运，拥有如此支持她的朋友。|近义词：more fortunate, more blessed, happier|反义词：unluckier, less fortunate|词性:形容词
词源:From Middle English *lukker, from Old English *luccu (“good fortune, luck”), from Proto-Germanic *lukō (“good fortune, luck”).  Related to Dutch geluk (“happiness, luck”) and German Glück (“fortune, luck”). The -ier ending is the standard comparative suffix.
