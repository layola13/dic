buttercup:/ˈbʌtərˌkʌp/ | n. | 毛茛；金凤花 | A small, bright yellow wildflower with cup-shaped petals. |  Example 1: The field was covered in buttercups.  田野里开满了毛茛。

Example 2: She picked a bouquet of buttercups. 她摘了一束毛茛。|Synonyms: crowfoot, kingcup, goldcup | Antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *buttercuppe*, from butter + cup, referring to the shape and color of the flower. Some sources erroneously suggest a connection to cows eating buttercups and producing yellow butter, which is untrue as buttercups are toxic to cattle.
