blockade: /blɒˈkeɪd/ (UK) /blɑːˈkeɪd/ (US)| vt.|封锁，阻塞|To seal off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving.|They blockaded the city to prevent supplies from reaching the rebels. 他们封锁了这座城市，以防止补给到达叛军手中。|近义词：besiege, obstruct, barricade|反义词：open, free|词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *bloquer* "to block," from Middle Dutch *blokken* "to block," from *blok* "log."  Originally referred to obstructing with logs.
