gild the lily:/ɡɪld ðə ˈlɪli/ | idiom | 画蛇添足；多此一举 | To try to improve something that is already perfect, thus making it worse.  | The dress was beautiful, but the extra sequins just gilded the lily. 这件衣服已经很漂亮了，但额外的亮片只是画蛇添足。 | over-egg the pudding, overdo it | understate, simplify | idiom

gild:/ɡɪld/ | vt. | 镀金；修饰；使光彩夺目 | To cover something with a thin layer of gold. |  They gilded the frame of the painting. 他们给画框镀了金。| embellish, adorn, enhance | tarnish, dull | verb

lily:/ˈlɪli/ | n. | 百合花；百合属植物 | A type of flowering plant with large, prominent flowers, often white or brightly colored. | The lilies in the garden were in full bloom. 花园里的百合花盛开了。| flower, blossom | - | noun
