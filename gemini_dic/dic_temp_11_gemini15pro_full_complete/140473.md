old-mannish:/ˌoʊldˈmænɪʃ/| adj.|老气横秋的，老成的|Having qualities or characteristics considered typical of an old man, especially in a disapproving way.  Appearing or behaving older than one's years, often in a fussy or crotchety way.|He had an old-mannish way of clearing his throat before speaking. 他讲话前总要像个老头子似的清清嗓子。|Synonyms: elderly, aged, mature (in a negative context), crotchety, fussy, cantankerous|Antonyms: youthful, young-at-heart, energetic, spirited|adjective
