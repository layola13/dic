gerundive:/ˈdʒɛrənˌdaɪv/ | adj. n.| 动名词的; 将来的，必须做的 |A gerundive is a verbal adjective that indicates necessity or obligation.  It is formed from the present stem of a verb. In Latin, it often expresses the idea of "ought to be done." |The agenda included discussing the *gerundive* clause.  议程包括讨论动名词从句。|The *gerundive* form of "laudare" (to praise) is "laudandus" (to be praised/deserving of praise).  “laudare”（赞美）的动名词形式是“laudandus”（应受赞美/值得赞美）。|近义词:obligatory, necessary, required |反义词:optional, unnecessary, forbidden|词性:形容词, 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *gerundivus*, from *gerundium* (gerund) + *-ivus* (forming adjectives). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵer- (to carry, bear).

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
The gerundive form expresses an action that should be done generally. 动名词形式表达了通常应该完成的动作。
The book is worth reading. 这本书值得一读。

This task requires doing carefully. 这项任务需要认真完成。
