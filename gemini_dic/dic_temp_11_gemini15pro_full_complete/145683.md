overcopious: /ˌoʊvərˈkoʊpiəs/ | adj. | 过于丰富的，过多的 | Excessively abundant; more than sufficient or required.  Derived from "over" (excessive) + "copious" (abundant).  The word "copious" comes from the Latin "copiosus," meaning "plentiful." |  The writer's overcopious use of adjectives made the prose feel cluttered.  作者过度使用形容词，使散文显得杂乱无章。 | synonyms: excessive, profuse, superabundant, overflowing | antonyms: scarce, meager, insufficient, scanty | adjective
