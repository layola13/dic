insition: /ɪnˈsɪʃən/ | n. | 切口；嵌入；开始 | An act of inserting something; the state of being inserted; a cut, especially one made during surgery. | The surgeon made a small incision to access the affected area.  外科医生做了一个小切口以进入患处。 | Synonyms: cut, gash, slit, insertion, penetration | Antonyms: closure, suture, healing | noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *incīsio(n-), from Latin incīsus, past participle of incīdere ‘to cut into’, from in- ‘into’ + caedere ‘to cut’.
