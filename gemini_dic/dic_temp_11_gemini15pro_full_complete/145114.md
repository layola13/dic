overaptness:/ˌoʊvərˈæptnɪs/ | n. | 过于贴切；过分恰当 |  The overaptness of the metaphor made it sound contrived.  这个比喻过于贴切，听起来很做作。 | English Explanation: The quality of being excessively or inappropriately apt or suitable; an instance of this.  It often implies that something is too perfect or convenient, making it seem artificial or forced.  | Example: His overaptness in quoting scripture made him seem sanctimonious. 他引用圣经过于贴切，显得他假装虔诚。 | Synonyms:  appropriateness (with a negative connotation),  excessiveness,  contrivance,  artificiality | Antonyms:  inappropriateness,  unsuitability,  naturalness | Noun
