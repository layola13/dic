moor:/mʊr/ (UK) /mɔːr/ (US)| n. | 荒野，沼泽；停泊处 | A tract of open, uncultivated upland, typically covered with heather and bracken.  A place where a ship or boat is moored. | The boat was moored to the dock. 这艘船停泊在码头。  | synonyms: heath, wasteland, bog, anchorage | antonyms: meadow, grassland, harbor | noun

moor:/mʊr/ (UK) /mɔːr/ (US)| vt. |  停泊（船） | To secure (a boat or ship) to the shore or to the bottom of a body of water by cables or anchors. | We moored the yacht in the harbor. 我们把游艇停泊在港口。 | synonyms: anchor, berth, dock | antonyms: unmoor, cast off | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *more*, from Old English *mōr* (“moor, swamp, waste land”), from Proto-Germanic *mōraz* (“bog, marsh”), from Proto-Indo-European *mērs-* (“swamp, sea”). Cognate with Dutch *moer* (“moor, peat bog”), German *Moor* (“moor, bog”), Icelandic *mýri* (“bog, swamp”).
