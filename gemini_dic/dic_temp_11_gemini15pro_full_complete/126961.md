narrow-mindedness:/ˌnæroʊˈmaɪndɪdnəsɪz/| n.|心胸狭窄，偏狭|The state of being narrow-minded; unwillingness to consider different perspectives or opinions.|His narrow-mindedness prevented him from seeing the other side of the argument. 他的心胸狭窄使他看不到争论的另一面。|近义词：intolerance, bigotry, prejudice|反义词:open-mindedness, tolerance, broad-mindedness|词性:名词

Etymology:
From narrow + minded + -ness.  "Narrow-minded" first appears in the early 17th century, combining the literal sense of "narrow" with "minded" to mean having limited intellectual or mental scope. The suffix "-ness" transforms the adjective into a noun, denoting the state or quality of being narrow-minded.
