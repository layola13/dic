cyrenian:/sɪˈriːniən/ | adj. n. | 古利奈人;  |  A native or inhabitant of Cyrene, an ancient Greek city in North Africa (modern Libya).  Relating to Cyrene or its people.  Also used figuratively to refer to someone who helps another bear a burden, alluding to Simon of Cyrene who helped Jesus carry the cross. |  He was a Cyrenian merchant who traded in spices and silks. 他是一位从事香料和丝绸贸易的古利奈商人。

Simon of Cyrene, a Cyrenian, was compelled to carry the cross.  古利奈人西门被迫背负十字架。| 近义词:  North African, Libyan (in a historical context), helper, assistant | 反义词: None | 词性: adjective, noun

Etymology: From Latin *Cyrenaeus*, from Greek *Κυρηναῖος (Kurēnaios)*, meaning "of Cyrene". Cyrene itself is derived from the Greek word *Κυρήνη (Kurēnē)*, a nymph in Greek mythology.
