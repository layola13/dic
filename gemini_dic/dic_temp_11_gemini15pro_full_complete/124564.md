mour:/mɔːr/ | v. | 哀悼，忧伤 | To feel or express sorrow or grief. | She mourned the loss of her beloved pet. 她哀悼她心爱宠物的去世。

| The entire nation mourned the death of their leader.  举国哀悼他们领袖的逝世。

| 近义词: grieve, lament, sorrow | 反义词: rejoice, celebrate | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mouren*, from Old French *mordre*, from Latin *mordēre* (to bite, sting, grieve), from Proto-Indo-European *merd-  (to rub, crush).  The sense development from "bite" to "grieve" likely occurred through the association of grief with physical pain.
