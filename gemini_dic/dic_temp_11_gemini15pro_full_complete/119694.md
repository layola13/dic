midlifes:/ˈmɪdˌlaɪvz/| n.|中年危机，中年生活|The period of life between approximately 45 and 65 years of age, often associated with significant life changes and transitions.|She's going through a difficult time in her midlifes.|近义词：middle age, midlife crisis|反义词:youth, childhood|词性:名词

词源:  midlife (n.) + -s (plural suffix)

midlife: From mid- + life.  Attested from 1868 as an adjective ("being in middle life"); as a noun from 1958.

-s:  Old English plural suffix -as, from Proto-Germanic *-ōs, *-iz, *-ez (also source of Old Frisian, Old Saxon -as, Old Norse -ar, -ir, Dutch -s, -en, German -e, -er, Gothic -os, -eis).
