baron: /ˈbærən/ (US) /ˈbærən/ (UK)| n.| 男爵；大亨；巨头 | A nobleman of varying rank, historically the lowest rank of the peerage. Also, a powerful or influential person in a particular industry or activity. | He was elevated to the rank of baron. 他被提升为男爵爵位。| synonyms: nobleman, peer, lord, magnate, tycoon | antonyms: commoner, peasant | noun

baron: /ˈbærən/ (US) /ˈbærən/ (UK)| adj. (rare)| 贫瘠的; 荒芜的 | (Archaic) Barren, unfruitful.  This usage is now rare. | The baron landscape offered little sustenance.  这片贫瘠的土地提供的食物很少。| synonyms: barren, sterile, unproductive | antonyms: fertile, productive | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *baron*, from Late Latin *baro*, a word of Germanic origin meaning "man," "free man."
