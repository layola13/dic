distaffs:/ˈdɪstæfs/| n.|纺杆；女系亲属；女性的职责或领域|A distaff is a stick or spindle on which wool or flax is wound for spinning.  It also refers to the female side of a family or to areas of activity traditionally associated with women.|She learned to spin using a distaff from her grandmother. 她从祖母那里学会了用纺锤纺线。|The distaff side of the family was known for its strong women. 这个家族的母系以其坚强的女性而闻名。|synonyms: spindle,  female lineage, women's domain|antonyms: male lineage, men's domain|noun
