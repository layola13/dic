fliest:/flaɪɪst/ /flaɪst/| v.|飞; (fly的第二人称单数现在时)|This archaic form is rarely used in modern English.  It's the second-person singular present indicative form of the verb "to fly."  It means "you fly."|Thou fliest through the air like a bird. (你像鸟儿一样在空中飞翔).  This is an archaic usage.  Modern English would use "You fly."|近义词：soar, flit, wing|反义词:fall, descend|动词

注意事项:
*  "Fliest" is an archaic form and is rarely used in modern English.  The modern equivalent is "fly."
*  The examples provided for the continuous tenses (present continuous, past continuous, future continuous, present perfect continuous, past perfect continuous, future perfect continuous) are used to express actions in progress at a specific time or over a period of time.  The simple tenses (simple present, simple past, simple future) are used for habitual actions, completed actions, or future actions.  The perfect tenses (present perfect, past perfect, future perfect) are used to express completed actions with a connection to the present, past, or future.
