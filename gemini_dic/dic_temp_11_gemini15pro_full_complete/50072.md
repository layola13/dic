dentalities: /dɛnˈtælɪtiz/
n.
牙列；齿系；牙系
The arrangement, type, and number of teeth in a given species or individual.  The study of dentition.
The paleontologist examined the fossil's dentalities to determine its species.  古生物学家检查了化石的齿系以确定其种类。
近义词: teeth, dentition,  dental arrangement
反义词:  (No direct antonym exists. One might consider "edentulousness" as a contrasting concept, referring to the lack of teeth.)
词性: noun (plural)

Etymology:
From Late Latin *dentalitas, from Latin dentalis (“of teeth”), from dens, dent- (“tooth”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *dónts (“tooth”).
