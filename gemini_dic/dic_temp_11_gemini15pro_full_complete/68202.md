fair-traded:/ˌferˈtreɪdɪd/
| adj.|公平交易的|Produced or involving trade that ensures that producers in developing countries receive a fair price for their goods.|例句：I always try to buy fair-traded coffee. 我总是尽量购买公平交易的咖啡。|例句：She is a strong advocate for fair-traded products. 她是一位公平交易产品的坚定拥护者。|近义词：ethical, responsible, sustainable|反义词：exploitative, unethical|词性:形容词
词源:  "fair" + "traded" (past participle of "trade"). "Fair" meaning just and equitable, and "trade" referring to the exchange of goods and services. The term gained prominence in the latter half of the 20th century with the rise of consumer awareness about the working conditions and pay of producers in developing countries.
