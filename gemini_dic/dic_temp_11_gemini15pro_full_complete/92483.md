hypothalamus:/ˌhaɪpoʊˈθæləməs/| n. |下丘脑|A region of the forebrain below the thalamus that coordinates both the autonomic nervous system and the activity of the pituitary, controlling body temperature, thirst, hunger, and other homeostatic systems, and involved in sleep and emotional activity.|The hypothalamus plays a crucial role in regulating body temperature.|近义词: None (It's a specific anatomical structure)|反义词: None|词性: noun

Etymology:  From hypo- + Greek *thalamos* inner chamber, bedroom.
