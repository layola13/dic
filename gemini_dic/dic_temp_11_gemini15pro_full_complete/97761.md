initiative: /ɪˈnɪʃətɪv/ (US) /ɪˈnɪʃiətɪv/ (UK)| n.|倡议，主动性，积极性| the power or opportunity to act or take charge before others do |The government announced a new initiative to reduce unemployment. 政府宣布了一项减少失业的新举措。|synonyms: action, plan, scheme, enterprise, drive, leadership|antonyms: apathy, indifference, inaction|noun

initiative: /ɪˈnɪʃətɪv/ (US) /ɪˈnɪʃiətɪv/ (UK)| adj.|起始的，初步的| serving to initiate; introductory |an initiative step in a negotiation  谈判中的第一步|synonyms: introductory, preliminary, beginning|antonyms: concluding, final|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English iniciativa, from Late Latin initiātīvus (“of or pertaining to a beginning”), from Latin initiātus (“initiated”).
