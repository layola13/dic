hyperaltruisms:/ˌhaɪpərˈæltruɪzəmz/
|n.|极度利他主义|Extreme altruism, often involving self-sacrifice to an extent that seems irrational from an evolutionary perspective.|He displayed remarkable hyperaltruism by donating a kidney to a complete stranger.|近义词: extreme altruism, selflessness, abnegation|反义词: selfishness, egoism|词性: Noun

Etymology:
hyper- (prefix): from Greek, meaning "over, beyond, excessive"
altruism: from French altruisme, coined by Auguste Comte from autrui "other people," from Old French autre "other"
