linocut: /ˈlaɪnəʊkʌt/ | n. | 版画，油毡版画 | A print made from a design cut in a piece of linoleum. | Example:  The artist displayed a series of linocuts depicting local landscapes. 艺术家展出了一系列描绘当地风景的油毡版画。

Example: She's taking a linocut workshop this summer. 她今年夏天要参加一个油毡版画工作室。 | 近义词: woodcut, block print | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, could be considered opposite to digital print) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From lino(leum) + cut.  First recorded in the early 20th century.  "Lino" is a shortened form of "linoleum," which comes from Latin "linum" (flax) + "oleum" (oil).
