booted:/ˈbuːtɪd/| adj.|穿着靴子的；启动的|Wearing boots; having been started (especially of a computer).|He wore a booted and spurred look. 他穿着靴子，一副跃跃欲试的样子。|The system is booted and ready to use. 系统已启动，可以使用。|近义词：booted up (for computers), shod|反义词:barefoot, unshod|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From boot (n.) + -ed.  The original sense referred to wearing boots.  The computing sense arose by analogy with "pulling oneself up by one's bootstraps" – a difficult feat requiring effort.
