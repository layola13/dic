axinomancy:/ˈæksɪnəˌmænsi/| n.|占斧卜|Divination by means of an axe or hatchet, observing its movements or the manner in which it falls when thrown.  It involved throwing an axe head up into the air and interpreting the way it landed to foretell the future.|The villagers practiced axinomancy to determine the best time for planting their crops.|近义词：divination, augury, omen|反义词:None|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἀξίνη (axínē, “axe”) + μαντεία (manteía, “divination”).
