crack-up:/ˈkrækʌp/ (US), /ˈkrækʌp/ (UK)| n. |崩溃，精神崩溃，(汽车、飞机等的)撞毁，大笑|A sudden collapse or failure, especially of mental or emotional stability; a crash; a fit of laughter.|He had a complete crack-up after losing his job and his wife. 他在失去工作和妻子后彻底崩溃了。|synonyms: breakdown, collapse, crash, nervous breakdown, hysteria, fit of laughter|antonyms: recovery, stability|noun


crack-up:/ˈkrækʌp/ (US), /ˈkrækʌp/ (UK)| v. (informal) |(使)崩溃，(使)精神崩溃，(使)撞毁，大笑|To cause to crash; to suffer a mental or emotional breakdown; to burst into laughter.|The pressure cracked him up. 压力让他崩溃了。|synonyms: break down, collapse, crash|antonyms: recover, stabilize|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrasal verb "crack up," meaning to break into pieces, which originated in the early 19th century. The figurative sense of mental breakdown emerged in the early 20th century.
