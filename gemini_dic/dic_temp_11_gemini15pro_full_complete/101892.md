PHRASE->
(ises is not commonly used in phrases.  It's an incorrect conjugation.  The following phrases use the correct form "is".)

What is going on? = 发生了什么事？
What is the matter? = 怎么了？
What is your name? = 你叫什么名字？
What is this? = 这是什么？
What is that? = 那是什么？


注意事项:

The word "ises" is not standard English.  It might be a dialectal variation, but it's generally considered incorrect.  The correct third-person singular present tense form of "be" is "is."
