clause:/klɔːz/ (US) /klɔːz/ (UK)| n.|从句；条款；句子|A group of words containing a subject and a predicate and forming part of a compound or complex sentence; a distinct article or provision in a contract, treaty, or other formal agreement.|The contract contains a clause that allows either party to terminate the agreement.|Synonyms: stipulation, provision, article, section, condition|Antonyms: whole, entirety|Noun


clause:/klɔːz/ (US) /klɔːz/ (UK)| v.|规定；限定|To specify or restrict by or as if by a clause.|The contract clauses the payment terms in detail.|Synonyms: stipulate, specify, restrict, limit|Antonyms: free, liberate|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *clause (Modern French close), from Latin clausa, feminine past participle of claudere "to close, shut."  Originally meaning "the close or end of a sentence or period."
