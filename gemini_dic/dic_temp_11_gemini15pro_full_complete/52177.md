diorama:/ˌdaɪəˈrɑːmə/ /ˌdaɪəˈræmə/
noun
立体模型；西洋景
A three-dimensional miniature or life-size scene, often enclosed in a glass showcase, typically showing historical events, nature scenes, or fictional situations.
The museum featured several dioramas depicting life in prehistoric times.  博物馆展出了几个描绘史前生活的立体模型。
miniature, model, exhibit, display, scene
antonym (This word doesn't have a direct opposite.  One might consider "abstraction" or "summary" as conceptually opposite, but not true antonyms.)
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *diorama*, from Greek *dia* ("through") + *orama* ("that which is seen, a sight").  Coined by Louis Daguerre and Charles Marie Bouton in 1822.
