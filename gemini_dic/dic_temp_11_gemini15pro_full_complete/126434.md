mysticism:/ˈmɪstɪsɪzəm/| n.|神秘主义，神秘性|A belief in the possibility of attaining communion with or knowledge of ultimate reality through direct, immediate intuition or insight, rather than through rational thought or inference.  It often involves practices like meditation or contemplation.|She was drawn to the mysticism of Eastern religions. 她被东方宗教的神秘主义所吸引。|近义词：spiritualism, occultism, esotericism|反义词：rationalism, empiricism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mysticus (“mystical”), from Ancient Greek μυστικός (mustikós, “of or pertaining to secret rites”), from μύστης (mústēs, “an initiate into the mysteries”), from μύω (múō, “I close, I initiate”).
