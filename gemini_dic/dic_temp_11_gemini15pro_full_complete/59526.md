eat dirt:/ˌiːt ˈdɜːrt/ /ˌiːt ˈdɝːt/
| idiom | 忍辱负重；低声下气；承认错误 | To be humiliated or forced to retract a statement; to admit one is wrong. |  He made a lot of boastful claims, but when challenged, he had to eat dirt. 他吹嘘了很多，但受到挑战时，他不得不承认错误。| Synonyms: swallow one's pride, eat humble pie, back down, apologize, retract | Antonyms: stand one's ground, hold one's head high, be unrepentant | idiom
eat dirt:/ˌiːt ˈdɜːrt/ /ˌiːt ˈdɝːt/
| v. | 忍辱负重；低声下气；承认错误 | To be humiliated or forced to retract a statement; to admit one is wrong.  This verb phrase is always used figuratively and never literally. | After losing the election, he had to eat dirt and congratulate his opponent.  选举失利后，他不得不忍辱负重并祝贺他的对手。 | Synonyms: swallow one's pride, eat humble pie, back down, apologize, retract | Antonyms: stand one's ground, hold one's head high, be unrepentant | verb
