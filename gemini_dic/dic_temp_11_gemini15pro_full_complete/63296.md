enthusiast:/ɪnˈθuːziæst/ (US), /ɪnˈθjuːziæst/ (UK)| n.|爱好者，热心者|A person who is very interested in and involved with a particular activity or subject.|He's a real car enthusiast. 他是一个真正的汽车爱好者。|近义词：fan, aficionado, devotee, zealot|反义词：critic, detractor, skeptic|noun

enthusiastic:/ɪnˌθuːziˈæstɪk/ (US), /ɪnˌθjuːziˈæstɪk/ (UK)| adj.|热情的，热心的|Having or showing intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval.|She was enthusiastic about the new project. 她对这个新项目充满热情。|近义词：eager, ardent, fervent, passionate|反义词：apathetic, indifferent, unenthusiastic|adjective

enthusiastically:/ɪnˌθuːziˈæstɪkəli/ (US), /ɪnˌθjuːziˈæstɪkəli/ (UK)| adv.|热情地，热心地|In a way that shows intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval.|The audience applauded enthusiastically. 观众热情地鼓掌。|近义词：eagerly, ardently, fervently, passionately|反义词：apathetically, indifferently, unenthusiastically|adverb



ETYMOLOGY->
From French *enthousiaste*,  from Greek *enthousiastes* "inspired," from *enthousiazein* "to be inspired or possessed by a god, be enthusiastic," from *en* "in" + *theos* "god".
