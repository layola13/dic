antiromantics:/ˌæntiɹoʊˈmæntɪks/
|n.|反浪漫主义者；反对浪漫主义的人|People who reject or are opposed to romanticism, often seen as cynical or pragmatic.| The antiromantics scoffed at the idea of love at first sight. 反浪漫主义者嘲笑一见钟情的想法。|cynics, pragmatists, realists|romantics, idealists|noun


antiromantic:/ˌæntiɹoʊˈmæntɪk/
|adj.|反浪漫的；反对浪漫主义的|Characterized by a rejection of romanticism, often emphasizing practicality or cynicism.|Her antiromantic view of relationships stemmed from her parents' messy divorce. 她对两性关系的反浪漫主义观点源于她父母糟糕的离婚经历。|cynical, pragmatic, realistic|romantic, idealistic|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix): from Greek anti- "against, opposite"
romantic: from Late Latin romanticus "of the Romanic languages," later associated with tales of chivalry and adventure.
