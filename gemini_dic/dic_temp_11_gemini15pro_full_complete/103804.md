jovial: /ˈdʒoʊviəl/ | adj. | 愉快的，高兴的 | Cheerful and friendly. | He had a jovial personality that made him popular. 他性格开朗，很受欢迎。| The jovial atmosphere at the party was infectious. 派对上欢乐的气氛很有感染力。| 近义词: merry, cheerful, happy, joyful | 反义词: sad, gloomy, morose | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Late Middle English *jovial, from Old French joival, and directly from Late Latin Joviālis (“of or pertaining to the planet Jupiter; born under the planet Jupiter”). From Latin Jovis (“Jupiter (the planet)”), genitive of Jupiter (“Jupiter (the Roman god)”).  The association with the planet Jupiter in astrology traditionally indicated happiness and good cheer.
