luminary: /ˈluːmɪnɛri/ (US), /ˈljuːmɪnəriː/ (UK)| n. |杰出人物；发光体| a person who inspires or influences others, especially one prominent in a particular sphere; a natural light-giving body, especially the sun or moon |He was a luminary in the field of astrophysics. 他是天体物理学领域的杰出人物。|The moon is a luminary of the night sky. 月亮是夜空中一个发光体。 |Synonyms: notable, dignitary, leading light, star, VIP |Antonyms: unknown, nobody| Noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English luminarie, from Old French luminarie, from Latin luminārium (“that which gives light; lamp, torch”), from lūmin- (stem of lūmen (“light”)), from PIE *lewk- (“light, brightness”).
