cap-in-hand:/ˌkæp ɪn ˈhænd/
|adj. adv.| 谦恭地，低声下气地；恳求地| humbly, respectfully, usually when asking for a favor; submissively. |He went cap in hand to the bank manager to ask for another loan. 他低声下气地去找银行经理请求再贷一笔款。| 近义词: humbly, submissively, beseechingly, pleadingly | 反义词: arrogantly, haughtily, demandingly | 词性: adjective, adverb


cap-in-hand:/ˌkæp ɪn ˈhænd/
|n.| 恳求；乞求|The act of going cap in hand; a humble request.|His cap-in-hand approach to the negotiations irritated his colleagues. 他在谈判中低声下气的态度激怒了他的同事们。| 近义词: supplication, plea, entreaty | 反义词: demand, ultimatum | 词性: noun

词源：
This idiom dates back to the 16th century.  The image is of a beggar holding his cap out for coins.  Over time, the phrase evolved to describe anyone making a humble request, even if they aren't literally holding a cap.
这个习语可以追溯到16世纪。其形象是一个乞丐拿着帽子讨钱。随着时间的推移，这个短语演变为描述任何提出谦卑请求的人，即使他们并没有真的拿着帽子。
