maltose: /ˈmɔːltoʊs/ (US) /ˈmɔːltəʊs/ (UK)| n. |麦芽糖|A white crystalline sugar formed by the action of diastase (an enzyme) on starch. It is a disaccharide consisting of two linked glucose units.|Maltose is produced during the germination of barley, a key step in beer making. 麦芽糖在大麦发芽过程中产生，这是啤酒酿造的关键步骤。|Maltose is less sweet than table sugar (sucrose). 麦芽糖不如蔗糖甜。|Synonyms: malt sugar|Antonyms: None|Noun
