msx:/ˌɛm ɛs ˈɛks/
noun|MSX|MSX is a standardized home computer architecture, announced by Microsoft and ASCII Corporation on June 16, 1983. It was initially conceived by Kazuhiko Nishi, then vice-president at Microsoft Japan and director at ASCII Corporation. Nishi conceived the project as an attempt to create unified standards among various hardware makers of the period, in much the same fashion as the VHS standard for videocassette recorders. |The MSX standard was popular in Europe, South America, the Middle East, and Asia, especially Japan, where it received strong support from companies like Yamaha, Sony, and Panasonic. It was less popular in North America. |Synonyms: home computer, personal computer|Antonyms: mainframe, supercomputer|n.
