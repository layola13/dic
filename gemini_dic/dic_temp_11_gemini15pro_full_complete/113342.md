luxuriating:/lʌɡˈʒʊərieɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 沉溺；尽情享受；| indulging oneself in something pleasant, especially something considered unnecessary or decadent |  She was luxuriating in a hot bath after a long day. 她劳累了一天后，正享受着热水澡。| 近义词：revelling, indulging, basking, wallowing | 反义词：abstaining, refraining, suffering | verb

词源: From Latin *luxuriari*, present active infinitive of *luxuriō* (“to be luxuriant, abound, be wanton, riot, indulge oneself”), from *luxuria* (“luxury, abundance, excess”).
