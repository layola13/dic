ethyl:/ˈɛθəl/ |adj. |乙基的|A univalent hydrocarbon radical, C2H5, derived from ethane by removing one hydrogen atom. It forms the basis of ethyl alcohol and many other organic compounds.| Ethyl alcohol is commonly used as a solvent. 乙醇通常用作溶剂。|近义词: None (as a specific chemical group)|反义词: None|词性: Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From *ether* + *-yl*, a suffix used in chemistry to denote a radical. Ultimately from Greek *aithēr* (upper air, bright sky, ether).
