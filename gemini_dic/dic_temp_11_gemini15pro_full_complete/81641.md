greedy: /ˈɡriːdi/ | adj. | 贪婪的，贪心的 | Having or showing an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power. |  He was greedy for power and wealth. 他贪图权力和财富。 |  The greedy child ate all the cookies.  这个贪婪的孩子把所有的饼干都吃光了。 | avaricious, covetous, grasping | generous, selfless | adjective

Etymology->
From Old English grǣdig "greedy, gluttonous," from grǣd "greed," from Proto-Germanic *grēdiz (source also of Old Saxon grētig, Old Frisian gredig, Dutch gretig "eager, greedy," Old High German grātag, German gierig, Old Norse grāðigr), from PIE root *ghre- "to desire, like."
