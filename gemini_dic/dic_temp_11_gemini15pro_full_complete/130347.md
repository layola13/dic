non-trivially:/nɒnˈtrɪviəli/ | adv. | 非平凡地，不寻常地 |  In a way that is not simple or obvious; significantly.  It implies a level of complexity, importance, or difficulty beyond the ordinary. | The theorem is non-trivially true for all prime numbers.  该定理对所有素数非平凡地成立。

The problem was non-trivially solved using advanced calculus.  这个问题是用高等微积分非平凡地解决的。 | 近义词: significantly, importantly, substantially, meaningfully | 反义词: trivially, simply, easily | 词性: 副词

词源:  Formed by combining the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") with the adjective "trivial" (from Latin *trivialis*, meaning "belonging to the crossroads," hence "commonplace" or "of little importance").
