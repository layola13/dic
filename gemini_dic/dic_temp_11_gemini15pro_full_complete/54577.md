dismay:/dɪsˈmeɪ/ | n. vt. |沮丧，惊慌；使沮丧，使惊慌 | Dismay is a feeling of unhappiness and disappointment.  |  The news of the layoffs filled the workers with dismay. 裁员的消息使工人们感到沮丧。 | 近义词: consternation, alarm, distress, discouragement | 反义词: encouragement, hope, joy, delight |词性: 名词；动词

dismay:/dɪsˈmeɪ/ |  vt. | 使沮丧，使惊慌 | To dismay someone means to cause them to feel discouraged or alarmed. | The sudden storm dismayed the picnickers. 突如其来的暴风雨使野餐的人们感到惊慌。| 近义词: appall, daunt, discourage, perturb | 反义词: encourage, hearten, reassure, comfort |词性: 动词
