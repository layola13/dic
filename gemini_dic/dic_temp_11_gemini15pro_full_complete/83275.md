gymnosperms:/ˈdʒɪmnəˌspɜːrmz/
| n. |裸子植物|A group of seed-producing plants that includes conifers, cycads, ginkgoes, and gnetophytes.  They are characterized by having seeds that are not enclosed in an ovary (fruit).|Conifers, such as pine and spruce trees, are common examples of gymnosperms.|Synonyms:  none (as a distinct botanical group)|Antonyms:  angiosperms|Noun
