mislocations:/ˌmɪsləʊˈkeɪʃənz/
| n. |错位，位置错误|The mislocations of inventory caused significant delays in fulfilling orders. 库存位置错误导致订单履行出现严重延误。|A mislocation is an instance of something being in the wrong place. |The frequent mislocations of important files led to a new filing system being implemented. 重要文件频繁错位导致实施了新的归档系统。|近义词：misplacements, errors, inaccuracies|反义词: correct placements, accuracy|词性:名词

词源:
mis- (prefix): from Old English, meaning "wrongly," "badly," or "unsuitably."
location (noun): from Old French locacion and Late Latin locatio, from Latin locare "to place," from locus "place."
