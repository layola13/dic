obrok: /ˈɔbrɔk/ | n. | 膳食，餐食，(尤指)定量口粮 | A measured portion of food, especially a ration. |  The soldiers received their daily obrok of bread and water. 士兵们领到了他们每日的面包和水口粮。| synonyms: ration, allowance, portion, allotment | antonyms: feast, abundance, surplus | noun

Etymology:  From Serbian оброк (obrok), from Proto-Slavic *ob-rokъ, from *ob- + *rokъ (“term, time, fate”).  Cognate with Russian оброк (obrok, “quitrent, tribute”), Polish obroka, Czech obrok. Originally referred to a feudal obligation to provide food or other goods to a lord.
