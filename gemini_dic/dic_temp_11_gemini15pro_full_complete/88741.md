hoc:/hɑːk/ /hɒk/| pronoun, adverb|这个，此|This; used to refer to something close at hand or readily available.  It comes from the Latin word "hic," meaning "this."  In modern English, it's primarily used in academic or legal contexts.|Hoc est corpus meum. (This is my body.)|近义词：this, the present, the latter|反义词:that, the former|pronoun

hoc:/hɑːk/ /hɒk/| adverb|为了这个目的；为此|For this purpose; to this end.  Again, derived from the Latin "hic."  Often seen in phrases like "ad hoc."|A committee was formed ad hoc to address the issue. (A committee was formed specifically to address the issue.)|近义词：for this purpose, to this end|反义词:generally, broadly|adverb
