dungy:/ˈdʌndʒi/ | adj. | 肮脏的，昏暗的，破败的 | Gloomy and dirty; grimy. | The room was small and dungy, with peeling wallpaper and a musty smell.  这个房间又小又脏，墙纸剥落，还有一股霉味。| The abandoned house had a dungy and neglected appearance. 这栋废弃的房子显得肮脏破败。 | synonyms: grimy, dirty, filthy, squalid, sordid, shabby, dingy, murky, gloomy | antonyms: clean, bright, pristine, immaculate | adjective

dungy:/ˈdʌndʒi/ | n. | 地牢 | (archaic) A dungeon. |  He was thrown into the dungy for his crimes. 他因犯罪被投入地牢。(古语用法，现代很少使用) |  The prisoner was kept in a dark, damp dungy.  囚犯被关在一个黑暗潮湿的地牢里。(古语用法，现代很少使用) | synonyms: dungeon, cell, jail | antonyms: palace, mansion | noun (archaic - this noun usage is rare)
