coelacanth: /ˈsiːləˌkænθ/ | n. | 腔棘鱼 | A rare order of fish (Coelacanthiformes) that includes two extant species and was thought to have been extinct since the Cretaceous Period.  They are known as "living fossils." |  The coelacanth, once believed extinct, was rediscovered in 1938.  腔棘鱼曾被认为已经灭绝，但在1938年被重新发现。 | Example:  The discovery of the coelacanth astonished the scientific world. 腔棘鱼的发现震惊了科学界。 | Synonym:  living fossil  | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, could use extinct species) | noun
