achatina:/ˌækəˈtiːnə/ | n. | 非洲大蝸牛 | A large air-breathing land snail of the genus Achatina, native to Africa, now established in many tropical areas worldwide and considered an invasive species. |  Example: The achatina can grow to be quite large, sometimes exceeding eight inches in length. 非洲大蝸牛可以长得相当大，有时超过八英寸长。

Example: The introduction of achatina to this ecosystem has had devastating consequences for native snail populations.  非洲大蝸牛引入这个生态系统对本地蜗牛种群造成了毁灭性的后果。| 近义词: giant African land snail | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, potentially small snail/native snail depending on context) | 词性: noun


Etymology: From Modern Latin *Achatinus, derived from Ancient Greek ἀχάτης (akhátēs, “agate”).  The shell patterns of some species resemble agate.
