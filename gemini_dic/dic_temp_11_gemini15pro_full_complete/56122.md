doctrinarianism:/ˌdɒktrɪˈnɛːrɪənɪzəm/ | n. | 教条主义；学理主义 |  The tendency to adhere rigidly to a set of beliefs or doctrines, often disregarding practical considerations or the opinions of others. |  His doctrinarianism made him unwilling to compromise on even minor points. 他的教条主义使他不愿在即使是细枝末节上妥协。 | synonyms: dogmatism, rigidity, orthodoxy | antonyms: pragmatism, flexibility, open-mindedness | noun
