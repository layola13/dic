notably:/ˈnəʊtəbli/ (UK) /ˈnoʊtəbli/ (US)| adv.|尤其；特别；显著地|Especially; particularly; significantly.  It modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb, highlighting a specific point or example. |Many species of tree are affected, notably the oak. 许多树种都受到了影响，尤其是橡树。|Synonyms: especially, particularly, in particular, specifically, significantly, chiefly, mainly, principally, primarily|Antonyms: insignificantly, generally, commonly|adverb

notable:/ˈnəʊtəbl/ (UK) /ˈnoʊtəbl/ (US)| adj.|值得注意的；显著的；著名的|Worthy of attention or notice; remarkable; distinguished. It describes a person or thing that deserves attention or recognition because of their importance or quality. |a notable achievement  一项显著的成就|Synonyms: remarkable, outstanding, noteworthy, significant, important, prominent, distinguished, eminent, famous, celebrated, renowned, well-known|Antonyms: insignificant, unremarkable, unknown, obscure|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *notable*, from Old French, from Latin *notabilis* "remarkable," from *notare* "to note, mark."
