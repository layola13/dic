illude:/ɪˈluːd/| v. | 欺骗，迷惑，使产生错觉 | to deceive or trick someone, often by making them believe something that is not true | The magician illuded the audience into thinking the rabbit had disappeared. 魔术师让观众误以为兔子消失了。 | mislead, deceive, delude | enlighten, disabuse | verb
Etymology: From Latin *illudere, from in- (“in, into, on, upon, against”) + ludere (“to play”).  The notion is of "mocking" or "playing tricks on."
