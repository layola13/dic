hummer:/ˈhʌmər/ (US) /ˈhʌmə(r)/ (UK)| n.|悍马; 嗡嗡声; 振翅声; 哼歌的人; 唱轻快歌曲的人|A large, powerful vehicle originally designed for military use, but now also available as a civilian vehicle; a low, continuous vibrating sound; the sound made by the rapid flapping of wings; someone who hums a tune.

例句：He drives a Hummer. 他开一辆悍马。
例句：The hummer of the bees filled the air. 蜜蜂的嗡嗡声充满了空气。


近义词：SUV (for the vehicle), drone, buzz, croon (for the sound)
反义词：whisper (for the sound)
词性：noun

hum:/hʌm/ (US) /hʌm/ (UK)| v. & n.|哼唱; 嗡嗡作响; 忙碌; 顺利进行; 哼唱声; 嗡嗡声|To sing with closed lips; to make a low, continuous vibrating sound; to be busy and active; to progress smoothly; the sound of humming.

例句：She hummed a cheerful tune. 她哼着一首欢快的曲子。
例句：The refrigerator is humming loudly. 冰箱嗡嗡作响。


近义词：drone, buzz, sing, croon
反义词：be silent, be still
词性：verb & noun


ETYMOLOGY->

"Hummer" (the vehicle) is derived from the High Mobility Multipurpose Wheeled Vehicle (HMMWV), pronounced "Humvee". This military vehicle was adapted for civilian use and became known as the "Hummer".

"Hum" originates from the imitative word "hummen" (Middle English), likely echoing the sound itself.
