intertextuality:/ˌɪntərˌtɛkstʃuˈælɪti/| n.|互文性|The shaping of a text's meaning by another text. It can refer to an author’s borrowing and transformation of a prior text or to a reader’s referencing of one text in reading another.|Example 1: The novel's intertextuality with classic mythology enriched its meaning. 这部小说与古典神话的互文性丰富了它的意义。

Example 2:  The poem's intertextuality with other works by the same author creates a sense of continuity. 这首诗与同一作者其他作品的互文性创造了一种连续感。|近义词：interrelationship, interconnectedness|反义词: N/A|词性: noun
