disconcertingly: /ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrtɪŋli/ /ˌdɪskənˈsɝːtɪŋli/
| adv. | 令人不安地，令人窘迫地 | In a manner that causes one to feel unsettled, slightly confused, or embarrassed. |  He smiled disconcertingly, making her feel uneasy. 他令人不安地笑了笑，让她感到不舒服。| Synonyms: disturbingly, unsettlingly, unnervingly,  | Antonyms: reassuringly, calmingly, comfortably | adverb

disconcert: /ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrt/ /ˌdɪskənˈsɝːt/
| vt. | 使不安，使窘迫 | To disturb the composure of; unsettle. | The sudden question disconcerted her. 突如其来的问题让她不知所措。| Synonyms: unsettle, disturb, ruffle, perturb | Antonyms: reassure, comfort, calm | verb


Etymology:
From French *disconcerter*, from *dis-* ("not") + *concerter* ("to arrange, to agree upon").  Ultimately from Latin *con-* ("together") + *serere* ("to join").
