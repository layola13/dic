lag:/læɡ/ | n. vi. vt.|滞后，延迟；落后|a period of time between one event and another|例句：There is a time lag between infection and the appearance of symptoms. 感染和症状出现之间有一段时间的滞后。|近义词：delay, slowdown, lapse|反义词：lead, advance|noun

lag:/læɡ/ | n. vi. vt.|滞后，延迟；落后|to fail to keep up with another or others in movement or development|例句：We lagged behind the others on the walk. 我们在散步时落在了别人后面。|近义词：trail, fall behind|反义词：lead, outpace|verb

lag:/læɡ/ | n. vi. vt.|滞后，延迟；落后|to cover (pipes, a tank, etc.) with lagging|例句：The pipes were lagged with insulation to prevent heat loss. 管道上覆盖了隔热材料以防止热量散失。|近义词：insulate|反义词：-|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *lagg, meaning "something laid down or low-lying, a low-lying piece of ground, something that hangs down."
