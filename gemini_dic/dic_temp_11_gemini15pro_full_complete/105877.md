kitschier:/ˈkɪtʃiər/ | adj. | 更庸俗的，更媚俗的 | More kitsch; having more qualities of kitsch.  Showing bad taste because it is trying too hard to be popular or artistic. |  Her apartment was filled with kitschier decorations than I'd ever seen, including a velvet Elvis painting and a ceramic poodle lamp.  她的公寓里摆满了比我见过的任何东西都更庸俗的装饰品，包括一幅天鹅绒的猫王画像和一盏陶瓷贵妇犬灯。| synonyms: tackier, cheesier, campier, gaudier | antonyms: tasteful, refined, sophisticated, understated | adjective

kitschier:/ˈkɪtʃiər/ | adj. | 更低劣的，更粗俗的 |  More representing inferior, crude, or tasteless artistic or literary work.  |  The movie's plot became progressively kitschier, culminating in a musical number featuring dancing hot dogs.  这部电影的情节越来越低俗，最后以一段以跳舞的热狗为特色的音乐剧告终。| synonyms: tawdrier, schlocky, trashy | antonyms: highbrow, sophisticated, artistic, refined | adjective
