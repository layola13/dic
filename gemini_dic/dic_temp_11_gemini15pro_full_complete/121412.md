misinference:/ˌmɪsˈɪnfrəns/| n.|错误推断，误推|A conclusion that is not logically justified by the evidence.|He made a misinference about her intentions based on a single comment. 他基于一句评论错误地推断出她的意图。|近义词：misinterpretation, fallacy, misjudgment|反义词：correct inference, deduction|词性:名词

Etymology:
mis- (prefix): wrong, bad, wrongly
infer (verb): deduce or conclude (information) from evidence. From Latin inferre "bring into, carry in, introduce, infer," literally "bring in."
-ence (suffix): forming nouns of action, state, or quality.
