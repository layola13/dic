liripipe: /ˈlɪrɪpaɪp/ | n. | 长尾帽,垂带;  | A long tail or tippet hanging from a hood, especially in medieval times.  It could be wound around the head for warmth or left to hang down as a decorative element. | The nobleman wore a velvet cloak with a liripipe hanging from the hood. 贵族穿着一件天鹅绒斗篷，兜帽上垂着一条长尾帽。|  The scholar's liripipe swayed as he walked. 学者的长尾帽在他走路时摇摆。 | synonym: tippet, tail, streamer | antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps something like "hood without a liripipe") | noun
