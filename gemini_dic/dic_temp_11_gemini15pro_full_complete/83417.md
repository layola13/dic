habid:/ˈhæbɪd/| adj.| 习惯的，惯常的| accustomed; usual. | He is habid to getting up early. 他习惯早起。|Example: The habid smoker coughed incessantly.  老烟枪不停地咳嗽。| 近义词: accustomed, customary, usual, habitual | 反义词: unaccustomed, unusual, rare | 词性: adjective
词源: From Middle English *habid, from Old French *habid, from Latin *habitus (“accustomed, usual”), past participle of habeō (“I have, hold, possess”), from Proto-Italic *habeō, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰabʰ- (“to seize, take, hold”).
