demobilized: /ˌdiːˈmoʊbəˌlaɪzd/ | adj. | 复员的，遣散的 |  Discharged from military service or no longer mobilized.  Having been released from military service or active duty, especially after a war. |  Many soldiers were demobilized after the war ended.  战争结束后，许多士兵复员了。| synonyms: discharged, released, mustered out | antonyms: mobilized, enlisted, drafted | adjective

demobilize: /ˌdiːˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz/ | vt. | 复员，遣散 | To release from military service or to disband (troops, an army, etc.). | The government plans to demobilize thousands of soldiers next year. 政府计划明年复员数千名士兵。 | synonyms: discharge, release, disband | antonyms: mobilize, enlist, draft | verb

demobilization: /ˌdiːˌmoʊbɪləˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 复员，遣散 | The act of demobilizing or the state of being demobilized. | The demobilization of the army was a complex process. 军队复员是一个复杂的过程。 | synonyms: discharge, release, disbandment | antonyms: mobilization, enlistment, draft | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From de- + mobilize, from French mobiliser, from mobile (“movable”) + -iser (suffix forming verbs).  Ultimately from Latin mobilis (“movable”).
