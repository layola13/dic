mandarinship: /ˈmændərɪnʃɪp/
|n.|
|官僚作风; 官僚主义;  高级官员的职位|
|The position or rank of a mandarin (a high-ranking official, especially in the past, in China or other Asian countries).  It often carries a connotation of bureaucracy and elitism.|
|The pervasive mandarinship stifled innovation and creativity within the government.  这种普遍存在的官僚作风扼杀了政府内部的创新和创造力。|

|His mandarinship gave him considerable power, but also isolated him from the concerns of ordinary people. 他的高官职位赋予他相当大的权力，但也使他脱离了普通民众的关切。|
|近义词: bureaucracy, officialdom, elitism|
|反义词: democracy, egalitarianism|
|词性: noun|
