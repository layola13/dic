drag:/dræɡ/| v. n.|拖，拉；缓慢而费力地移动；硬拽；（用鼠标）拖动|To pull something along the ground or other surface, usually with difficulty.  Also, to move something on a computer screen using a mouse.|She dragged the heavy suitcase across the floor. 她吃力地把沉重的行李箱拖过地板。| synonyms: pull, haul, tow, trail | antonyms: push, shove, carry | verb

drag:/dræɡ/| n.|令人厌烦的人或事；阻力；吸，抽（香烟等）|Something or someone that is boring or causes difficulty. Also, an act of inhaling smoke from a cigarette, etc.|The meeting was a real drag. 这次会议真是令人厌烦。| synonyms: bore, nuisance, burden | antonyms: delight, pleasure | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *draga "to draw, pull, drag," from Proto-Germanic *draganą (source also of Old English dragan "to draw, drag, protract," Old Saxon dragan, Old Frisian draga, Dutch dragen "to carry, wear," Old High German tragan, German tragen "to carry, bear, wear," Gothic dragan "to draw").
