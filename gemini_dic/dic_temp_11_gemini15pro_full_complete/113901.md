madrigalesque: /ˌmædrɪˈɡælɛsk/ | adj. | 牧歌式的，牧歌风的 |  Resembling or characteristic of a madrigal, especially in being light, delicate, or elaborate in style.  Evoking the style or mood of a madrigal, particularly its lyrical and often pastoral themes. | Her voice was light and madrigalesque, perfectly suited to the Renaissance music. 她的声音轻盈而富有牧歌风格，非常适合文艺复兴时期的音乐。 | The composer's new piece had a distinctly madrigalesque quality, full of intricate harmonies and playful melodies.  这位作曲家的新作品具有鲜明的牧歌风格，充满了复杂的和声和俏皮的旋律。 | 近义词: lyrical, melodious, pastoral, idyllic | 反义词: harsh, discordant, cacophonous | 词性: adjective

Etymology: From Italian *madrigale* (madrigal) + *-esque* (in the style of). Ultimately from Late Latin *matricale* (song sung in mother tongue).
