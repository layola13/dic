artifice:/ˈɑːrtɪfɪs/| n.|技巧；诡计；巧妙的装置；人为事物|skill in designing and making something; clever trickery or deception|例句：The whole legal system is an artifice designed to control people. 整个法律体系都是一种旨在控制人民的精心设计。

例句：He spoke without artifice. 他说话很真诚。|近义词：trickery, deception, cunning, guile, subterfuge, ruse, stratagem, contrivance, maneuver, scheme, plot, device, expedient|反义词：honesty, sincerity, candor, openness, genuineness, artlessness, naturalness|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French artifice "skill, craft," from Latin artificium "a trade, craft, profession; a work of art; a skillful contrivance," from artifici- (stem of artifex "craftsman, artist," from ars "art" + -fex "maker") + -ium (suffix forming nouns). The sense of "trickery" developed in French.
