amadeus:/ˌæməˈdeɪəs/ | n. | 阿玛迪斯; 天神的宠儿 |A given name, from Latin, meaning "love of God," or "loved by God." It is composed of the Latin words "amare" (to love) and "deus" (God). It was popularized by the play and film "Amadeus" about Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart. | Mozart's middle name was Amadeus. 莫扎特的中间名是阿玛迪斯。 | Near Synonyms: Theophilus, Gottlieb | Antonyms: None | Noun


amadeus:/ˌæməˈdeɪəs/ | adj. | 神赐的; 天才的 | Inspired by God; divinely gifted. This usage is less common than the noun form but can be used to describe someone with exceptional talent. | He has an amadeus touch when it comes to music composition. 他在音乐创作方面有着神赐的天赋。 | Near Synonyms: Gifted, Talented, Inspired, Divine | Antonyms: Untalented, Mediocre | Adjective
