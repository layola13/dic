abide: /əˈbaɪd/ (US), /əˈbaɪd/ (UK)| v. |忍受，容忍；遵守，遵循；居留，逗留| to tolerate or accept something or someone unpleasant or difficult; to obey or follow a rule, decision, or instruction; to remain or continue in a particular state or place|例句：She couldn't abide the thought of leaving her family. 她无法忍受离开家人的想法。|近义词：tolerate, bear, endure, obey, follow, comply with, stay, remain|反义词：disobey, reject, leave|词性：动词

abide: /əˈbaɪd/ (US), /əˈbaɪd/ (UK)| v. |（用于疑问句或否定句）等待，停留| (used in questions or negative sentences) to wait; to stay|例句：He couldn't abide there any longer. 他再也无法在那里待下去了。|近义词：stay, wait|反义词：leave, go|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ābīdan, from ā- (intensifying prefix) + bīdan (“to wait, remain, dwell”). Cognate with Old Frisian abīda (“to await, expect”), Old Saxon abīdan, Old High German arbītan (“to wait for, expect”), German erbeuten (“to seize, capture”).
