mony: /ˈmʌni/ | n. | money |  A medium of exchange in the form of coins and banknotes; wealth reckoned in terms of money.  From Old French *moneie*, from Latin moneta ‘mint, money’, originally a surname of Juno, in whose temple in Rome money was coined. |  He doesn't have much money. 他没有很多钱。 |  cash, currency, funds | poverty, destitution | noun
