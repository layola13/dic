demurrant:/dɪˈmɜːrənt/
| n.|抗辩者，提出异议者|A person who demurs; someone who objects or raises doubts.|The demurrant argued that the contract was invalid. 抗辩者认为合同无效。|Synonyms: objector, dissenter, protestor|Antonyms: supporter, endorser, approver|noun


demur:/dɪˈmɜːr/
| v. |提出异议，反对；犹豫|To raise doubts or objections or show reluctance.|She demurred at the idea of working late on Friday. 她对周五加班的想法表示反对。|Synonyms: object, protest, hesitate|Antonyms: agree, accept, consent|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *demourer* (to linger, tarry), from Latin *demorari* (to delay, linger), from *de-* (down, from) + *morari* (to delay).  The legal sense of "to raise an objection" developed from the sense of "delaying proceedings".
