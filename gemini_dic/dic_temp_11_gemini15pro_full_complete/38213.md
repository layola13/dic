compurgation:/ˌkɒmpərˈɡeɪʃən/ |n.|宣誓清除罪名；同誓辩护|The practice of justifying or clearing oneself of a charge by producing a number of compurgators, or oath-helpers, who swear to one's innocence.|  In medieval law, compurgation was a common way to avoid trial by combat. 在中世纪法律中，同誓辩护是避免比武审判的常见方式。

|Synonym: exculpation, vindication, acquittal|Antonym: accusation, condemnation, conviction|noun|
