jeopardize: /ˈdʒɛpərdaɪz/ (US) /ˈdʒɛpədaɪz/ (UK)| vt.| 危及，损害，使处于危险境地 | to put something such as a plan or system in danger of being harmed or damaged |例句：His health was jeopardized by overwork. 他的健康因过度工作而受到损害。|例句：The company's future is jeopardized by the new regulations. 新的规章制度危及了公司的未来。|近义词：endanger, threaten, imperil, risk|反义词：protect, safeguard, secure|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French jeu parti "divided game, game with even chances," from jeu "game" (from Latin jocus "jest, game") + parti "divided" (from Latin partitus, past participle of partire "to divide"). Originally a term in chess, referring to a situation where a player must make a move that could lead to loss.  The sense evolved to "put in jeopardy" in the 17th century.
