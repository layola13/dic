dukedoms: /ˈdjuːkdəmz/
|n.|公爵领地；公爵爵位（复数）|The area ruled by a duke.  It can also refer to the rank or position of a duke, especially when considering multiple dukes.|The king bestowed several dukedoms upon his loyal followers. 国王将几个公爵领地赐给了他忠诚的追随者们。|Synonyms: duchies, fiefs, domains|Antonyms: N/A
|Noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *duchédom*, from duc (“duke”) + -dom, from Old English -dōm (“state, condition, dignity, office, domain”). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *dōmaz (“judgment, decree”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁- (“to set, put”).
