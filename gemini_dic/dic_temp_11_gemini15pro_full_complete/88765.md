hocus-pocus:/ˈhoʊkəs ˈpoʊkəs/| n.|戏法；花招；胡言乱语|Meaningless words or activity, often used in magic or to deceive. It refers to a formula used in conjuring or sleight of hand, or to any trickery or deception.|The magician muttered some hocus-pocus and pulled a rabbit out of the hat. 魔术师念叨了几句咒语，从帽子里变出一只兔子。|Synonyms: trickery, deception, sleight of hand, magic, mumbo jumbo|Antonyms: reality, truth, honesty|noun
hocus-pocusses:/ˈhoʊkəs ˈpoʊkəsəz/| n.|戏法；花招；胡言乱语（复数）|The plural form of "hocus-pocus".  It refers to multiple instances of meaningless words or activity used in magic or to deceive.|The charlatan used all sorts of hocus-pocusses to convince people of his powers.  这个骗子用各种各样的花招让人们相信他的力量。|Synonyms: trickery, deceptions, sleight of hand, magic, mumbo jumbo|Antonyms: realities, truths, honesties|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From late 16th century, possibly a mock Latin phrase invented by stage magicians.  Its origin is obscure, but it may be a parody of religious liturgical formulas.
