Hittite: /ˈhɪtaɪt/ | n. adj. |  赫梯人；赫梯人的；赫梯语的 |  A member of an ancient people who established an empire in Asia Minor and Syria in the second millennium BC.  Relating to the Hittites or their language. | The Hittite empire was a powerful force in the ancient Near East. 赫梯帝国是古代近东的一股强大力量。 | Synonyms: Anatolian (for the language) | Antonyms:  N/A | noun

Hittite: /ˈhɪtaɪt/ | adj. | 赫梯人的；赫梯语的 | Relating to the Hittites or their language.  |  Hittite art often depicts scenes of hunting and warfare. 赫梯艺术经常描绘狩猎和战争的场景。 | Synonyms: Anatolian (for the language) | Antonyms: N/A | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Hittiter*, from Hebrew *חִתִּים* (*ḥittîm*), plural of *חִתִּי* (*ḥittî*) “Hittite”, from *חֵת* (*ḥēṯ*). Ultimately related to Akkadian *𒄩𒋾𒅔𒌝* (*ḫatti*)
