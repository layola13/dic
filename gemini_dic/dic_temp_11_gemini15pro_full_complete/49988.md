denization:/ˌdiːnaɪˈzeɪʃən/ |/ˌdɛnɪˈzeɪʃən/|
n.|入籍；赋予公民权；许可居住|The act of granting the rights of citizenship to a foreigner; admission to residence and certain rights in a country. |The denization of immigrants is a complex process. 移民的入籍是一个复杂的过程。|
Synonyms: naturalization, citizenship, incorporation|
Antonyms: expatriation, deportation, banishment|
noun

denization:/ˌdiːnaɪˈzeɪʃən/ |/ˌdɛnɪˈzeɪʃən/|
n.| 居住权；特许居住权|The granting of certain rights to a foreigner, short of full citizenship, allowing them to live and work in a country.|He was granted denization after living in the country for five years.  在该国居住五年后，他获得了居住权。|
Synonyms:  residence permit, right of abode|
Antonyms:  expulsion, exile|
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *denizacion, from Anglo-Norman denisein, denizein, Old French denezein, denisein (“inhabitant, citizen”), from denzier (“to make a denizen, naturalize”), from deniz, deinz (“within, inside”), from Latin de intus (“from within”).
