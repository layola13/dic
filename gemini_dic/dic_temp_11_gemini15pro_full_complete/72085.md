floaters:/ˈfloʊtərz/ (US) /ˈfləʊtəz/ (UK)| n.|飞蚊症，漂浮物|Small spots or specks that drift across your field of vision, often caused by debris in the vitreous humor of the eye.|I've been seeing floaters in my vision lately. 我最近视野里一直看到漂浮物。|synonyms: specks, spots, cobwebs|antonyms: (No direct antonym)|noun

float:/floʊt/ (US) /fləʊt/ (UK)| v. n.|漂浮，浮动；提出，提议|1. To rest or move on or near the surface of a liquid without sinking. 2. To suggest an idea or plan.|The boat floated gently on the lake. 小船在湖面上轻轻地漂浮着。|synonyms: drift, waft, hover|antonyms: sink, submerge|verb & noun
