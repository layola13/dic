marinaded:/ˈmærɪneɪtɪd/
| adj. | 腌制的 |  Soaked in a marinade.  A marinade is a seasoned liquid in which food is soaked before cooking. | The marinaded chicken was delicious. 腌制的鸡肉很美味。 | Example:  The chef prepared marinaded tofu for the vegetarian guests.  厨师为素食客人准备了腌制豆腐。| Synonyms: pickled, soaked, steeped | Antonyms: unmarinated, fresh, raw | Word type: Adjective

marinade:/ˈmærɪneɪd/
| n. vt. | 腌泡汁，腌制 | A mixture, typically of oil, vinegar, spices, and herbs, in which meat, fish, or vegetables are soaked before cooking in order to flavor or soften them.  As a verb, it means to soak food in a marinade. |  The steak was flavorful because of the marinade. 牛排因为腌泡汁味道鲜美。  She marinaded the pork overnight. 她把猪肉腌制了一夜。 | Example: The marinade gave the fish a tangy flavor. 腌泡汁给鱼增添了浓郁的风味。  Example: He decided to marinade the lamb for several hours. 他决定把羊肉腌几个小时。 | Synonyms: sauce, dressing, brine (for pickling) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for the noun) | Word type: Noun, Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *marinade*, from *mariner* "to pickle in sea brine," from *marin* "sea," from Latin *marinus* "of the sea," from *mare* "sea."
