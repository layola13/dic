homesteader:/ˈhoʊmsteɪdər/| n.|自耕农；农场主|A person who settles on land granted by the government, especially under the Homestead Act of 1862.|例句：The homesteaders faced many hardships on the Great Plains.  自耕农在大平原上面临许多困难。

例句：Many homesteaders were immigrants hoping for a better life. 许多自耕农是移民，希望过上更好的生活。|近义词：settler, pioneer, farmer|反义词:landlord, tenant|词性:noun

homesteading:/ˈhoʊmsteɪdɪŋ/| n.|自耕农场；宅地垦殖|The act of establishing a homestead.|例句：Homesteading was a popular way to acquire land in the 19th century.  在19世纪，自耕农场是获得土地的流行方式。


例句：Homesteading requires hard work and dedication. 宅地垦殖需要辛勤的工作和奉献精神。|近义词：settling, pioneering, farming|反义词:renting, leasing|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From home + stead + -er.  "Stead" means a place or location, often a farm or estate.  "-er" is a suffix denoting a person who performs an action or occupies a certain role.
