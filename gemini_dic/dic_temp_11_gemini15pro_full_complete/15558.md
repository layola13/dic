bagpipe:/ˈbæɡpaɪp/| n. |风笛|A wind instrument consisting of a leather bag that is inflated by the player blowing into it or by bellows, and several pipes, including a chanter with finger holes and one or more drones that produce a continuous note.|The sound of the bagpipes filled the air. 风笛声响彻空中。|synonyms: pipes |antonyms: None|noun
