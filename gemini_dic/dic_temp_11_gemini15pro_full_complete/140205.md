officership: /ˈɒfɪsərʃɪp/ (UK), /ˈɑːfɪsərʃɪp/ (US)
n.
军官职务；军官身份；军官素质
The state or quality of being an officer, especially in the armed forces.  It encompasses the skills, knowledge, and character required for leadership.
He demonstrated exceptional officership during the campaign.  他在战役中展现了非凡的军官素质。
近义词: command, leadership, rank
反义词: subordination,  (No direct antonym for officership, these represent opposite concepts)
词性: noun

Etymology:
From "officer" + "-ship."  "Officer" comes from Old French "officier,"  ultimately from Latin "officium" meaning "duty, service." "-ship" is a suffix denoting state, quality, or condition.
