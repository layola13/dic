neoconservatism:/ˌniːoʊkənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/
n.|新保守主义|A political ideology that emerged in the United States in the 1970s, characterized by an emphasis on free-market capitalism, a strong national defense, and a more assertive foreign policy, often including military intervention.  It also often involves socially conservative stances.|Neoconservatism gained prominence during the Reagan administration. 里根政府时期新保守主义开始崭露头角。

Neoconservatism influenced the decision to invade Iraq. 新保守主义影响了入侵伊拉克的决定。
|近义词：right-wing conservatism, hawkishness|反义词: liberalism, progressivism|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"neo-" (new) + "conservatism"  The prefix "neo-" indicates a renewed or modified form of conservatism.
