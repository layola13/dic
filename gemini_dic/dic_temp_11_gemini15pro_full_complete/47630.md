debilitating:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪŋ/
adjective
使人衰弱的；使身体虚弱的
making someone or something physically weak
Her debilitating illness prevented her from working. 她的衰弱性疾病使她无法工作。

debilitating:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪŋ/
adjective
削弱的; 损伤的
weakening, impairing
The country's economy was severely debilitated by the war.  战争严重削弱了该国的经济。


Etymology:  From Late Latin *dēbilitāre, from Latin dēbilis "weak" + -itāre (frequentative suffix).

Synonyms: weakening, enervating, incapacitating, crippling, disabling
Antonyms: strengthening, invigorating, empowering, enabling
