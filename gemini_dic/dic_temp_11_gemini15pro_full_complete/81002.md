gradiated:/ˈɡreɪdieɪtɪd/| adj.|分级的，渐变的，刻度的|Arranged in grades, stages, or degrees of intensity or magnitude; marked with degrees or a scale.|The beaker contained a gradiated solution, changing from light blue to deep indigo. 烧杯里装着一种渐变的溶液，颜色从浅蓝色变为深靛蓝色。|Synonyms: graduated, tiered, progressive, measured|Antonyms: uniform, consistent, homogenous|adjective

gradiate:/ˈɡreɪdieɪt/| v.|分级，渐变，授予学位|To arrange in grades or stages; to mark with degrees or a scale; to confer an academic degree or diploma upon.|The artist gradiated the colors in the painting to create a sense of depth. 艺术家将画作中的颜色分级，以营造出一种深度感。|Synonyms: grade, graduate, tier, measure|Antonyms: homogenize, equalize|verb
