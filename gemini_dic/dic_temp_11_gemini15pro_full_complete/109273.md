lemniscus:/lɛmˈnɪskəs/
noun
束，带，薄片 (解剖学)
A ribbon-shaped tract of nerve fibers, especially in the brain.
The medial lemniscus is a pathway important for touch and proprioception. 内侧丘系是触觉和本体感觉的重要通路。
The spinal lemniscus carries sensory information from the spinal cord to the thalamus. 脊髓丘系将感觉信息从脊髓传递到丘脑。
tract, fasciculus, pathway

From Latin *lemniscus, diminutive of lēmniscus (“ribbon, fillet”), from Ancient Greek λῆμνισκος (lēmniskos, “woolen ribbon, bandage”).
