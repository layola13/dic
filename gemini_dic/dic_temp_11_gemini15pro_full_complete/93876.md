immolating: /ˈɪməˌleɪtɪŋ/
verb
献祭；牺牲；烧死
killing something or someone, especially as a sacrifice, by burning
The ancient Mayans would immolate captives to appease their gods. 古代玛雅人会烧死俘虏来安抚他们的神。
近义词: sacrificing, burning
反义词: saving, rescuing
词性: verb (present participle)

immolate: /ˈɪməˌleɪt/
verb
献祭；牺牲；烧死
to kill something or someone, especially as a sacrifice, by burning
The monk immolated himself in protest against the war.  这位僧侣自焚以抗议战争。
近义词: sacrifice, burn
反义词: save, rescue
词性: verb


Etymology:  From Latin *immolatus*, past participle of *immolare* (“to sprinkle with sacrificial meal”), from *in-* + *mola* (“sacrificial meal, salt cake”).
