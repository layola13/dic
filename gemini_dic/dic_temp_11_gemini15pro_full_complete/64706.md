escarpment:/ɪˈskɑːrpmənt/| n. |悬崖，陡坡|A long, steep slope, especially one at the edge of a plateau or separating areas of land at different heights.|例句：The escarpment offered a stunning view of the valley below. 悬崖提供了山谷底下令人惊叹的景色。

例句：The hikers carefully navigated the treacherous escarpment.  徒步旅行者小心翼翼地穿过险峻的悬崖。|近义词：cliff, precipice, bluff|反义词:slope, incline|词性:名词

escarpments:/ɪˈskɑːrpmənts/| n. |悬崖，陡坡（复数）|Plural form of escarpment, referring to multiple long, steep slopes.|例句：The region is characterized by dramatic escarpments and canyons. 该地区以壮观的悬崖和峡谷为特征。

例句：Erosion has carved intricate patterns into the escarpments. 侵蚀在悬崖上雕刻出复杂的图案。|近义词：cliffs, precipices, bluffs|反义词:slopes, inclines|词性:名词
