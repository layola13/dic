heterogynous:/ˌhɛtərˈɒdʒɪnəs/ | adj. | 异雌性的，雌雄异型的 | Having females of two or more distinct forms.  Referring to species in which females exist in two or more forms, especially differing in reproductive capacity or behavior. | Example 1: The heterogynous ant colony had both worker and queen ants, each with distinct roles.  异雌性的蚂蚁群体既有工蚁，也有蚁后，每一种都有不同的角色。

Example 2: Heterogynous reproduction is an adaptation found in some social insects.  异雌性繁殖是某些社会性昆虫的一种适应性。 | Near Synonyms: polymorphic, dimorphic (when specifically two forms) | Antonyms:  monomorphic, homogynous | adjective
