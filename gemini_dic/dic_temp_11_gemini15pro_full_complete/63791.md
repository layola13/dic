epideictic:/ˌɛpɪˈdaɪktɪk/
adjective
仪式性的；夸示的；表演的
Ceremonial, designed for display or show, often used to describe a type of rhetoric focused on praise or blame.
The president delivered an epideictic speech commemorating the fallen soldiers. 总统发表了一篇仪式性演讲，纪念阵亡的士兵。
Her commencement address was highly epideictic, full of praise for the graduating class. 她的毕业典礼演讲非常具有表演性，充满了对毕业生的赞扬。
近义词:ceremonial, demonstrative, rhetorical
反义词:informative, instructional, didactic
词性:adjective

epideictic:/ˌɛpɪˈdaɪktɪk/
noun
夸示性修辞
A type of rhetoric focused on praise or blame, often used in ceremonial occasions.
The professor's lecture explored the nuances of epideictic in ancient Greek oratory.  教授的讲座探讨了古希腊演说中夸示性修辞的细微之处。
His eulogy was a moving example of epideictic. 他的悼词是一个感人的夸示性修辞的例子。
近义词:rhetoric, oratory
反义词:N/A
词性:noun


词源: From Greek *epideiktikos* "fit for display," from *epideiknynai* "to show off, display," from *epi-* "upon" + *deiknynai* "to show."
