eudaemonia: /ˌjuːdɪˈmoʊniə/ | n. | 幸福; 福乐;  福祉; |  A state of happiness and flourishing, especially as understood by Aristotle, involving living a life of virtue and fulfilling one's potential. |  He found eudaemonia not in wealth or power, but in the pursuit of knowledge and the practice of virtue. 他发现幸福不在于财富或权力，而在于追求知识和实践美德。
