bluffnesses:/ˈblʌfnəsɪz/
n.
虚张声势，假装勇敢，佯装自信
an instance of pretending to be stronger or braver than one is, or the quality of a person who does this.
His bluffnesses didn't fool anyone. 他的虚张声势骗不了任何人。
近义词:pretenses, dissembling, feigning
反义词:honesty, sincerity, frankness
词性:名词


bluffnesses:/ˈblʌfnəsɪz/
n.
悬崖峭壁
a steep, high cliff or bank.  (Less common usage)
The bluffnesses overlooking the sea were breathtaking.  俯瞰大海的悬崖峭壁令人叹为观止。
近义词:cliffs, precipices, escarpments
反义词:valleys, plains, lowlands
词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From bluff (n.) + -ness (suffix forming nouns of quality or state).  Bluff (n.) originally referred to a steep cliff or headland, later developing the sense of "deceiving by pretense of strength, etc." probably from the idea of a bold front.
