mandlestone: /ˈmændlstəʊn/ (UK) /ˈmændlstˌoʊn/ (US) | n. | 杏仁石 | A concretion found in the intestines of certain animals, especially ruminants, supposedly having medicinal properties.  Historically believed to be a bezoar formed around a swallowed almond (or other seed), but now understood to be primarily composed of mineral salts.  |  He kept a mandlestone in his pocket, believing it would protect him from illness. 他口袋里放着一块杏仁石，相信它能保护自己不生病。 |  Synonym: bezoar | Antonym: N/A | Noun
