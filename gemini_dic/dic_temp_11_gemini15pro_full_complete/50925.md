desiccate:/ˈdɛsɪkeɪt/ | v. |使干燥，使脱水；使枯萎|To remove the moisture from something, typically in order to preserve it. |The hot desert sun desiccated the plants.  炎热的沙漠太阳使植物枯萎了。| The process desiccates the fruit, leaving it dry and chewy. 这个过程使水果脱水，使其干燥且耐嚼。 |近义词: dry, dehydrate, parch, wither |反义词: moisten, hydrate, saturate |词性:动词

desiccated:/ˈdɛsɪkeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 干燥的，脱水的；枯萎的| Having had moisture removed; dried up. | The desiccated landscape offered little sign of life. 干燥的土地上几乎没有生命迹象。 |  The desiccated flowers crumbled in my hand. 干枯的花朵在我手中碎裂了。 | 近义词: dry, dehydrated, parched, withered |反义词: moisten, hydrated, saturated |词性:形容词

desiccation:/ˌdɛsɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |干燥，脱水；枯萎|The process of removing moisture. | Desiccation is a common method of food preservation. 干燥是常见的食物保存方法。 | The desiccation of the lake bed led to the formation of salt flats. 湖床的干燥导致了盐滩的形成。 |近义词: drying, dehydration |反义词: hydration |词性:名词


词源: From Latin *desiccatus*, past participle of desiccare "to dry up," from de- "thoroughly" + siccare "to dry," from siccus "dry."
