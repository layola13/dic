eskimo:/ˈɛskɪmoʊ/ | adj. |爱斯基摩人的 (现被认为带有贬义)|Relating to the Inuit people or their language and culture. This term is now considered outdated and often offensive. The preferred term is "Inuit."|Example 1: The eskimo language is complex and fascinating. (Outdated usage - 爱斯基摩语复杂而迷人。)

Example 2: They studied eskimo traditions and customs. (Outdated usage - 他们研究了爱斯基摩人的传统和习俗。)| 近义词: Inuit | 反义词: None (referring to a specific group of people)| 词性: adjective


词源:  The origin of "eskimo" is uncertain, but it is believed to come from an Algonquian word meaning "eaters of raw meat."  This etymology is now disputed by some linguists.  The term has a history of colonial imposition and is therefore considered offensive.
