amortisement: /əˈmɔːtɪzmənt/ (UK), /əˈmɔːrtɪzment/ (US)| n.|摊销；分期偿还|The gradual reduction of a loan or debt through periodic payments.  It also refers to the spreading out of capital expenses for intangible assets over a specific period (useful life).|The company's balance sheet showed the annual amortisation of its intangible assets.|Synonyms: depreciation, write-off|Antonyms: appreciation, accrual|noun

amortize: /əˈmɔːtaɪz/ (UK), /əˈmɔːrtaɪz/ (US)| v.|摊销；分期偿还|To gradually reduce a loan or debt through periodic payments. It also refers to the spreading out of capital expenses for intangible assets over a specific period (useful life).|The company will amortize the cost of the new equipment over five years.|Synonyms: depreciate, write off|Antonyms: appreciate, accrue|verb
