babbitts:/ˈbæbɪts/| n.|巴比特金属，庸俗的市侩|A soft, white, nonferrous alloy of tin, copper, and antimony, used as a bearing metal.  Figuratively, a narrow-minded, self-satisfied businessman or bourgeois philistine, especially one who is uninterested in literature or the arts.  The term originates from Sinclair Lewis's novel "Babbitt."|The engine bearings are made of babbitts.  发动机轴承由巴比特金属制成。

He's such a babbitt, only interested in golf and making money. 他真是个庸俗的市侩，只对高尔夫球和赚钱感兴趣。|近义词:philistine, materialist|反义词:intellectual, aesthete|词性:名词

词源:From the surname of George F. Babbitt, the protagonist of Sinclair Lewis's 1922 satirical novel *Babbitt*.  Babbitt is portrayed as a complacent, materialistic, and conformist businessman.
