aconitum:/ˌækəˈnɑɪtəm/ | n. | 乌头，附子 | A poisonous plant of the genus Aconitum, also called monkshood or wolfsbane.  It is known for its distinctive hooded flowers and highly toxic properties. | Aconitum is a beautiful but dangerous plant. 乌头是一种美丽但危险的植物。| The ancient Greeks used aconitum as a poison for hunting wolves, hence the name wolfsbane. 古希腊人用乌头作为毒药来猎狼，因此得名狼毒。 | synonyms: monkshood, wolfsbane | antonyms: antidote (in terms of effect) | noun

aconite:/ˈækənaɪt/ | n. | 乌头碱 | A poisonous alkaloid derived from aconitum plants. | Aconite is used in some traditional medicines but is highly toxic and should only be handled by experts. 乌头碱用于一些传统药物，但毒性很强，只能由专家处理。|  Exposure to even small amounts of aconite can be fatal. 即使少量接触乌头碱也可能致命。 | synonyms: aconitin | antonyms:  None (no direct chemical antonym) | noun
