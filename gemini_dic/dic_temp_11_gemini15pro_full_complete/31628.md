chatelaine:/ˌʃætəˈleɪn/ | n. | 女管家；（旧时挂在女管家腰带上的）钥匙串；女主人 | A woman in charge of a large house; a set of keys or other small items formerly worn by women at their waist. |  The chatelaine directed the servants to prepare the dining room.  女管家指示仆人们准备餐厅。

The chatelaine jingled her keys as she walked. 女管家走动时钥匙串叮当作响。| Near synonyms: housekeeper, matron, mistress | Antonyms: servant, maid | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *chastelaine, from castel 'castle' (from Latin castellum 'fort') + -aine (suffix forming feminine nouns).
