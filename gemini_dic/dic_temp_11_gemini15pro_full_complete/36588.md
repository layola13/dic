collateral: /kəˈlætərəl/ | n. adj.| 抵押品，担保品；附属的，次要的 |Something pledged as security for repayment of a loan, to be forfeited in the event of a default.  (n.) Accompanying something else but not a major part of it; secondary or subordinate. (adj.) |  The bank required collateral for the loan. (n.) 银行要求贷款提供抵押品。|  His collateral duties included attending fundraising events. (adj.)  他的附属职责包括参加筹款活动。| synonyms: security, guarantee, pledge (n.); secondary, subsidiary, ancillary (adj.) | antonyms:  unsecured (adj.) | noun, adjective


collaterals: /kəˈlætərəlz/ | n.| 抵押品，担保品（复数） | Securities or other assets pledged as security for repayment of a loan. | The borrower offered several collaterals, including real estate and stocks. 借款人提供了几项抵押品，包括房地产和股票。| synonyms: securities, guarantees, pledges | antonyms: N/A | noun


Etymology: From Late Latin *collateralis "placed side by side," from com- "together" + lateralis "of the side."
